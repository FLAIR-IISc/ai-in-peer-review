- Paper studies recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from MNAR data given an explicit m-graph.  
- Main contributions: generalized sequential factorization allowing R-variables in separating sets.  
- Main contributions: new joint-recovery formula for semi-Markovian models using R partitions and Markov blankets.  
- Main contributions: first explicit non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions.  
- Main contributions: recovery of causal effects P(y|do(x)) even when associated probabilistic quantities are non-recoverable.  
- Main contributions: necessary and sufficient conditions for recovery under simple attrition.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) to handle edges among R variables and semi-Markovian structure.  
- Complements and contrasts with MAR/MCAR methods and model-blind ML/EM approaches by showing MNAR cases where those fail.  
- Theorems and proofs appear technically substantial and sound; examples and do-calculus illustration aid understanding.  
- Non-recoverability criteria are particularly valuable and novel.  
- Several assumptions need clarification: explicit statement of where strict positivity over complete cases is required.  
- Central constraint “R variables may not be parents of V ∪ U” must be justified or presented as a simplifying modeling restriction.  
- Define “neighbor” precisely (does adjacency include bidirected edges?).  
- Clarify whether V* variables are part of the canonical m-graph or treated separately and how they enter estimands.  
- Notational and presentation fixes needed: move Figure 1 explanation to main text and fully label observed/partially observed variables and R nodes.  
- Example 1 and notation corrections: fix conditional independence notation, RHS of example, and consistent R′/R(i) superscripts.  
- Define collider and inducing path where used and emphasize “minimality” requirement before it is used critically.  
- Provide a reference for the variable-level recoverability/positivity definition cited in Footnote 2.  
- Discuss practical applicability: how to learn/validate m-graphs from data and robustness to misspecification (sensitivity analysis).  
- Clarify when standard ML/EM can be applied and why they fail in the MNAR cases studied; discuss using recovery formulas in estimation.  
- Suggested additions: pseudo-code for recovery procedures and complexity notes.  
- Strength/novelty: high originality and strong theoretical significance, especially for attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Limitation: practical impact limited unless guidance on obtaining/validating m-graphs is provided.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying assumptions, fixing notation, and expanding discussion on learning/robustness.
