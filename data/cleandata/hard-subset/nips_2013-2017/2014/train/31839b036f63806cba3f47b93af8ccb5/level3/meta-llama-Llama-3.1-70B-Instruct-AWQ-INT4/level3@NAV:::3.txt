**Review**

The paper "Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data" presents a novel approach to handling missing data in graphical models. The authors extend previous work by introducing a more general missingness mechanism and providing conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the concepts and techniques used.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper addresses the problem of recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data in graphical models. The authors introduce a new concept called "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) to represent the missingness process. They provide conditions for recovering probabilistic queries, such as joint and conditional distributions, and causal queries, such as P(y|do(x)). The authors also apply their results to problems of attrition and provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents a novel approach to handling missing data in graphical models.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of the concepts and techniques used.
* The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work.
* The results are interesting and have potential applications in various fields.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical machine learning applications.
* The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently, and the authors should clarify whether V* variables are being omitted and why.
* The paper forbids R variables as parents of V or U, but the authors should justify this restriction or state it is for convenience.
* The term "neighbor" is used without a definition and should be defined.
* The paper could benefit from more examples and illustrations to help readers understand the concepts and techniques used.
