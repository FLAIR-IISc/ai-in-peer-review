- Paper extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to determine recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Focuses on graphical conditions for recoverability (e.g., P(y|x), P(y|do(x))) rather than traditional MCAR/MAR statistical assumptions.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous theorems and clear proofs (notably Theorem 3 on non-recoverability).  
- Some results appear incremental or closely related to prior work (Theorem 2/Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability weighting; Theorem 1 echoes Richardson 2009).  
- Theorem 1 is hard to understand and feels disconnected from the rest of the paper.  
- Sections 5–6 contain the most original and valuable contributions, showing causal queries can be recoverable when probabilistic ones are not (e.g., Theorem 4).  
- Contradiction with Garcia (2013) needs clarification: are prior results incorrect or using a different framework?  
- Major clarity issues: inconsistent and confusing notation (e.g., V, Vi, Vo, Vm), poorly defined concepts (e.g., R^part), and illegible subscripts.  
- Presentation problems: Corollary 1 poorly presented, numerous grammatical errors, and casual language that should be formalized.  
- Structural issues: Section 3’s flow is orthogonal to the rest, and premature references to Vo and Vm must be fixed.  
- Definition 4 / Theorem 4 require clarification on "graphs formed by severing edges."  
- Paper is too long for a conference (13-page appendix); better suited for journal submission after revision.  
- Strengths: strong causal query results, clear illustrative examples (e.g., Example 3), and relevant application to attrition.  
- Weaknesses: poor accessibility due to clarity and notation problems, incremental aspects, and lack of a unifying framework.  
- Overall verdict: technically strong but poorly communicated; recommend rejection for NIPS with encouragement to revise substantially and resubmit to a journal.
