- Tackles identification of estimable queries under missing data, extending Mohan et al. [2013] to more general missingness mechanisms.  
- Derives a novel identification formula and shows causal queries can be recoverable even when the joint distribution is not.  
- Builds on Pearl-style causal inference and refines the sequential factorization theorem with new recoverability conditions.  
- Theorem 4 is a notable contribution, demonstrating causal query recovery with inseparable Y and Ry.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and clear presentation of extensions to existing identification frameworks.  
- Connects to related areas like attrition analysis and highlights limitations of common approaches (e.g., imputation, Garcia et al.).  
- Major practical concern: the method assumes full knowledge of the missingness process (R variables), which is often unrealistic.  
- Needs discussion of sensitivity to misspecification of the missingness model and practical applicability.  
- Figure 1 is illustrative but its crucial details should be moved into the main text.  
- Some assumptions (e.g., excluding R as parents of V or U) lack justification and feel arbitrary.  
- Missing definition of “neighbor” and several other minor technical/notation errors need correction.  
- Clarify relationship to Garcia et al.’s rectification, provide precise explanation of MLE applicability, and fix missing references.  
- Section 2’s implicit assumption of a known full population limits real-world relevance and should be addressed.  
- Overall: technically solid and interesting, but requires significant clarifications, sensitivity analysis, and revisions before acceptance.
