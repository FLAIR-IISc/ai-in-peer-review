- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to give more general conditions for recovering probabilistic (P(y|x), P(y,x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries from missing data.
- Shows causal queries can be recoverable even when joint distribution P(y,x) is not, notably when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.
- Provides technically sound theorems and proofs, representing a significant theoretical advance over prior work.
- Extends sequential factorization (Theorem 1) to handle interacting R variables, which is a valuable contribution.
- Gives necessary and sufficient conditions for joint distribution recoverability (Theorem 2).
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for causal query recoverability (Theorem 4), introducing d*-separation to recover causal effects even with inducing paths between Y and Ry.
- Demonstrates practical relevance to longitudinal attrition problems, correcting misconceptions in Garcia (2013) (Theorem 6).
- The requirement for knowing missingness processes (R variables) is insufficiently contextualized and may be impractical for many ML applications.
- Implicit assumption of knowing the full population (Section 2) excludes population size estimation and should be acknowledged.
- Inconsistent use of "mgraph" notation (sometimes including V* variables, sometimes not) creates confusion; authors should clarify V* usage.
- The term "neighbor" is undefined and needs a formal definition to avoid ambiguity.
- The restriction that R variables cannot be parents of V or U is unexplained; authors should justify or state it's for convenience.
- Relation to Garcia (2013) and "rectification" needs clarification, especially concerning positivity assumptions.
- Claim that "maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR" (Section 8) is too vague; authors should explain MLE applicability to their missingness processes.
- Several minor technical errors: inconsistent notation (R^{(N)} vs. R^{(j)}), error in Example 1 (RHS should be P(Y|X,Z)), and a missing partially observed variable in Corollary 1.
- Recommend moving more detail from Figure 1 into the main text for clarity.
- Overall significance is high for causal inference and missing-data theory; theoretical contributions warrant acceptance pending revisions.
- Suggested decision: accept with minor revisions to address clarity, assumptions about missingness mechanisms, and consistency of terminology.
