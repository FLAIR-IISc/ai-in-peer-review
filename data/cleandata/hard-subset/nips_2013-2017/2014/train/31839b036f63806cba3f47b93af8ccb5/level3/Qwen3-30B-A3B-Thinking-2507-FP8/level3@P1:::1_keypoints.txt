- Extends Mohan et al.’s m-graph framework to recover probabilistic and causal queries under missing data, emphasizing causal effect identification when probabilistic queries are non-recoverable.
- Generalizes prior results on joint/conditional distribution recovery (Section 3).
- Introduces novel graphical non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3).
- Shows causal queries like P(y | do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness R_y are inseparable (Section 6).
- Example 3 (Section 6) compellingly demonstrates recoverable causal effect despite non-recoverable joint/conditional distributions via d*-separation and do-calculus.
- Provides an attrition application (Section 7, Theorem 6) giving necessary/sufficient conditions for causal effect recovery in longitudinal studies and correcting Garcia (2013).
- Strength: High technical quality with clear, interpretable non-recoverability conditions filling a literature gap.
- Strength: Sections 5–7 deliver significant, practical results for MNAR data practitioners.
- Weakness: Notation is inconsistent and many symbols (V, V_o, V_m, R^{part}, etc.) are referenced before definition.
- Weakness: Corollary 1 and other results are opaque due to undefined sets and unclear partition semantics.
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s sequential factorization feels disconnected, lacks intuition, and assumes Richardson (2003/2009) background.
- Weakness: Paper flow is disjoint — Sections 3–4 are overly technical and distract from stronger causal contributions in Sections 5–7.
- Weakness: Missing definitions (e.g., graphs after severing edges) and underdeveloped connections to related work (IPW/Markov blankets, Garcia) weaken coherence.
- Presentation concern: 13-page appendix and dense formalism make the manuscript more suitable for a journal than NIPS without substantial pruning.
- Recommendation: Major revision to clarify notation, streamline/reauthor Sections 3–4, and emphasize the causal recoverability insight to enhance accessibility and impact.
