- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from datasets with missing entries using m-graphs.  
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and extends the m-graph framework to more general settings.  
- Provides general graphical conditions for recoverability of probabilistic queries (e.g., P(y|x), P(y,x)).  
- Derives graphical conditions for recoverability of causal queries P(y|do(x)).  
- Shows a causal query can be recoverable even when the corresponding joint/conditional distributions are not.  
- Introduces novel graphical criteria for proving non-recoverability of queries.  
- Applies theoretical results to attrition in longitudinal studies, giving necessary and sufficient recovery conditions.  
- Significance: tackles ubiquitous MNAR problems and decouples causal from probabilistic recoverability.  
- Originality: Theorem 1 generalizes sequential factorization to dependent missingness mechanisms.  
- Originality: Theorems 2–3 introduce new recoverability/non-recoverability conditions using collider paths and Markov blankets.  
- Originality: Theorem 4 characterizes when causal effects are recoverable despite non-recoverable joint distributions.  
- Quality: technically sound with formal theorems and proofs; useful illustrative examples.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, and consistent notation with effective graphical illustrations.  
- Weakness: entirely theoretical with no empirical validation or experiments.  
- Weakness: relies on the strong assumption of a correctly specified m-graph; sensitivity to misspecification is not discussed.  
- Weakness: omits discussion of computational complexity for finding admissible sequences, partitions, and Markov blankets.  
- Overall: strong, original theoretical contribution with a main shortcoming of lacking empirical validation; strong candidate for acceptance.
