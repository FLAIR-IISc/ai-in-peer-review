- Proposes novel necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for identifiability of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing observations.  
- Extends prior work by explicitly incorporating the missing-data generating mechanism into the causal diagram (m-graphs).  
- Refines and generalizes the sequential factorization theorem from Mohan et al. (2013) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Introduces new graphical criteria that identify when joint and conditional distributions are unrecoverable.  
- Derives general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y, x), and causal queries P(y|do(x)).  
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when some factors in their identifying estimands are not recoverable.  
- Provides graphical conditions for recovering P(y|do(x)) when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Applies results to attrition problems and characterizes recovery of causal effects from attrition-corrupted data.  
- Demonstrates that the approach overturns some prevailing claims in the literature about non-recoverability under simple attrition.  
- Paper is well-written, focused, and builds coherently on existing literature with clear explanations and illustrative examples.  
- Technically rigorous and theoretically sound; claims are supported by analysis and examples.  
- Results are significant and have important implications for researchers and practitioners handling missing data.  
- Weakness: assumes high familiarity with graphical models and causal analysis, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Weakness: heavy theoretical focus may reduce appeal to readers seeking practical applications.
