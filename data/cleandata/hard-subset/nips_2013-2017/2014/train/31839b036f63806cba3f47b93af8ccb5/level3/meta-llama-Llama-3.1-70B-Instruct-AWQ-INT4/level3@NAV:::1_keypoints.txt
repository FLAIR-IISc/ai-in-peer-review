- Tackles recoverability of causal and probabilistic queries from missing data, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Technical quality is high; results are involved and appear correct.  
- Theorem 3 gives clear, interpretable necessary conditions for joint distribution recoverability.  
- Provides useful examples, including recoverability when conditional distributions are unavailable.  
- Demonstrates applicability to attrition problems.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are strong, offering clear answers and interesting causal-query results.  
- Some results are incremental and do not form a larger unifying picture.  
- Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest of the paper; Theorem 1 is hard to comprehend.  
- Main weakness is clarity: text is dense and the flow is poor.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent, undermining readability.  
- Authors should aim for a general identifiability criterion instead of piecemeal results.  
- Clarify connections to prior work (e.g., Richardson 2003, 2009).  
- Revise notation and present results more clearly and concisely.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.
