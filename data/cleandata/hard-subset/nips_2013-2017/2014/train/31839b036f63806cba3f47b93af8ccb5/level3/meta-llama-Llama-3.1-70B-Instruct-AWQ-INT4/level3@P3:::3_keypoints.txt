- Significant extension of Mohan et al. [2013] on recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using graphical models.  
- Introduces a novel identification formula and conditions for recovering causal queries even when the joint distribution is not recoverable.  
- Develops a more general missingness mechanism than prior work, enabling recovery under broader conditions.  
- Provides a new graphical condition for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Paper is technically sound and results are well-supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Well-organized presentation with clear writing, well-defined notation, and illustrative examples.  
- Minor errors and inconsistencies (wording, figure qualifications, notation) need correction.  
- Strength: builds on earlier work to offer a more comprehensive framework and applicability to attrition problems.  
- Limitation: requires knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may be unavailable in typical ML settings.  
- Limitation: assumes the full population is known, excluding problems like population size estimation.  
- Suggestions: move more figure details into main text, clarify the term "mgraph" and define "neighbor".  
- Needs clarification on relation to Garcia et al.'s "rectification" regarding positivity assumptions and on the ML consistency claim under MAR.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques with meaningful new insights and significant advancement of the state of the art.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address errors and inconsistencies.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
