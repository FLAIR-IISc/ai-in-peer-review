- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using "missingness graphs" (m-graphs).  
- Introduces m-graphs that explicitly incorporate the missing-data generating mechanism into causal diagrams.  
- Provides novel necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability of probabilistic queries (P(y|x), P(y,x)).  
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability of causal queries (P(y|do(x))).  
- Refines the sequential factorization theorem to handle cases where missingness mechanisms influence each other.  
- Establishes new graphical criteria for non-recoverability that complement prior sufficient conditions.  
- Demonstrates that causal queries can be recoverable even when probabilistic relations are not (e.g., Y and Ry not d-separable).  
- Validates theoretical results with examples and applies them to attrition problems in longitudinal studies.  
- Technically rigorous: formal definitions, theorems, and proofs provided (appendix).  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with consistent notation enabling expert reproducibility.  
- Original contribution advancing prior work (Mohan et al. 2013) by giving necessary and sufficient conditions and new insights.  
- Significant practical relevance for researchers diagnosing bias from missing data in causal inference.  
- Strengths: fills a critical gap, clear presentation, novel insight on causal recoverability, rigorous proofs, comprehensive attrition treatment.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of practical implementation (estimating missingness mechanisms), lack of analysis of computational complexity, and limited connection to practical statistical methods.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical contribution with minor practical limitations; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
