- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) graphical framework for missing data with more general recoverability conditions and a refined sequential factorization.
- Presents novel non-recoverability criteria and shows causal queries can be recoverable even when corresponding conditional distributions are not.
- Supplies useful examples and an application to attrition in experimental studies.
- Tackles a significant, challenging problem with high technical quality and sound results.
- Sections 5 and 6 are particularly valuable, especially Theorem 3 on non-recoverability and results on causal-query recovery.
- Main weakness is clarity: manuscript is dense, covers too much, and has poor flow for a conference paper.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of V, unclear Vi vs Vo; Vo and Vm not defined before use).
- Corollary 1 and Theorem 1 are hard to comprehend; Theorem 1 feels incremental and lacks a unifying recoverability picture.
- Connections to prior literature are insufficiently explicit (ties to Richardson 2003/2009, inverse-probability weighting, Markov blankets).
- Would benefit from aiming for a general recoverability calculus analogous to do-calculus.
- Specific fixes needed: define notation for graphs with severed edges (Definition 4), clarify the critique of Garcia (2013), and correct minor notational issues (e.g., use \leftrightarrow for bidirected edges).
- Overall recommendation: important and technically solid work but better suited for a journal submission after substantial clarification and reorganization.
