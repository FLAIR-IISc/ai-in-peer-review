- Presents a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Introduces "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) as a formal representation of the missingness process.  
- Defines the notion of recoverability and gives sufficient conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions.  
- Extends results to causal query recovery and demonstrates applicability to attrition problems.  
- Theorem 3 gives a clear, interpretable condition for non-recoverability—highlighted as a notable contribution.  
- Includes useful illustrative examples, e.g., causal identifiability when conditional distributions are unavailable.  
- Technical quality is high and results appear correct.  
- Some results (notably Theorem 1) are incremental and lack a larger unifying theory.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of capital V) and corollaries are poorly presented.  
- Paper is dense, poorly organized, and hampered by a lengthy 13-page appendix.  
- Would benefit from a more general identifiability criterion analogous to causal-effect identifiability.  
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and situates itself relative to prior work (Garcia 2013’s status is unclear).  
- Sections 5 and 6, especially Theorem 4, contain potentially significant results addressing an important problem.  
- Acceptance argument: important problem, useful approach, and significant results in later sections; rejection argument: presentation/notation issues, density, and incremental scope.
