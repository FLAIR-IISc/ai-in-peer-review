- Presents a novel approach for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using graphical models.  
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) to represent the missingness process and derive recoverability conditions.  
- Provides conditions for recovering probabilistic queries (joint and conditional distributions) and causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))).  
- Applies results to attrition problems and gives necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of concepts and techniques.  
- Thorough discussion of related work and technically sound results.  
- Results are interesting and have potential applications across machine learning, statistics, and epidemiology.  
- Assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical ML settings.  
- Inconsistent use of the term "mgraph" and unclear whether V* variables are omitted and why.  
- Forbids R variables as parents of V or U without justification or explanation if for convenience.  
- Uses the term "neighbor" without providing a formal definition.  
- Could benefit from more examples and illustrations to aid reader understanding.  
- Original contributions include the m-graph concept and new recoverability conditions.  
- Quality is high technically but clarity would improve with additional examples and fixed terminology.  
- Recommends clarifying mgraph usage and defining "neighbor," justifying R-parent restriction, and adding examples/illustrations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
