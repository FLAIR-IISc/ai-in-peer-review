- Paper extends Mohan et al. (2013) to study recoverability of P(y, x), P(y|x) and P(y|do(x)) under missingness graphs.
- Main technical contributions: (i) generalized sequential factorization allowing R-variables in admissible orders for interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Main technical contributions: (ii) graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions.
- Main technical contributions: (iii) conditions showing P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when the full joint is not (using do-calculus and dormant independences).
- Main technical contributions: (iv) necessary and sufficient results for “simple attrition,” clarifying and correcting Garcia (2013).
- Theoretical development is careful, technically sound, and well-connected to identification, Markov blankets, and collider paths.
- Novel and important result: causal effects (P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable despite non-recoverability of P(V).
- Key assumption needing clarification: the paper assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process—discuss how this is obtained in typical ML workflows and add elicitation/diagnostic guidance.
- Request to move more of Figure 1 specification into main text and clarify Figure 1 in captions/text.
- Section 2 ambiguity: appears to assume a full population including entirely unobserved individuals—clarify implications and whether population size estimation is excluded.
- Notational/clarity issues: inconsistent use of “m-graph” (sometimes includes V*), standardize and state when V* omitted and why.
- Justify forbidden edges R → V or R → U since R are treated as substantive—state if restriction is causal or technical.
- Define “neighbor” when first used (it is pivotal for Theorems 2 and 3).
- On p.8, clarify relation to Garcia’s “rectification” with explicit positivity/overlap assumptions and whether Garcia assumes the same or stronger overlap.
- On p.8, the claim that MLE is consistent under MAR is too general—explain applicability of standard ML/EM to the MNAR mechanisms studied.
- Minor corrections: wording (“can bias outcomes” → “can bias estimates”), qualify Figure 1 in text, fix Example 1 RHS to P(Y|X,Z), replace Y ⫫ R with Y ⫫ RY, fix R^(N) vs R^(j) notation, Corollary 1 omission of partially observed variable, add citation for variable-level definition in Footnote 2.
- Originality/significance: novel extensions beyond Mohan et al. and non-recoverability criteria likely to influence causal inference with missing data.
- Applicability concern: reliance on a known missingness graph limits practicality—discuss elicitation, diagnostics, and broader ML relevance.
- Pros: strong theory, novel causal-recovery insight, clear graphical conditions, useful attrition results.
- Cons: reliance on known missingness process, clarity/notation issues, ambiguous positivity and MLE assumptions, limited guidance for typical ML applications.
