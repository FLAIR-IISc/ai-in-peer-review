- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by developing graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using m-graphs.  
- Contribution: general conditions for recovering joint/conditional distributions beyond sequential factorization.  
- Contribution: shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness Ry are not d-separable (e.g., Example 3).  
- Contribution: provides necessary and sufficient conditions for causal effect recovery under attrition.  
- Builds on foundational missing-data literature (Rubin 1976; Pearl 2009) and corrects misconceptions in Garcia (2013) about attrition.  
- Technical quality and originality are high; Theorems 1–6 are rigorously proven.  
- Example 3 compellingly demonstrates causal recoverability despite probabilistic non-recoverability.  
- Theorem 3’s non-recoverability criteria are practically valuable for practitioners.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-structured theorems, intuitive examples (Figures 2–4), and precise definitions.  
- Application to attrition (Section 7) shows practical significance for longitudinal studies.  
- Main weakness: lacks empirical validation or comparisons to existing methods (e.g., multiple imputation).  
- Scope constraints: Theorem 4 requires |Vm| = 1 and Section 4’s “generalization” lacks concrete implementation guidance.  
- Limitations discussion is adequate but should better address computational feasibility (e.g., collider path restrictions).  
- Related work is comprehensive but misses some recent causal-missing-data methods (e.g., Van der Laan & Robins, 2003).  
- Overall assessment: highly significant and novel; well-suited for NIPS with recommendation for acceptance, pending added empirical validation and computational discussion.
