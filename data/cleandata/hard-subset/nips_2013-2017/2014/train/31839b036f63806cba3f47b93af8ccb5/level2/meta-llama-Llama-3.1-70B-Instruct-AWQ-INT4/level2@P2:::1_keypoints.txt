- Presents a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries using graphical models of the missingness process.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by providing more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries and causal queries P(y | do(x)).  
- Extends the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Introduces new graphical criteria that preclude recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Derives necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects in attrition problems.  
- Demonstrates practical usefulness by applying results to attrition, a common real-world missingness challenge.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with a logical flow and helpful illustrative examples.  
- Bridges theory and practice, highlighting the role of graphical models in deciding recoverability.  
- Technically sound with thorough analysis and proofs provided in the appendix.  
- Minor issues: some definitions/notations (e.g., "general ordered factorization" and "minimal set") need clarification.  
- Minor issues: several examples could be expanded or rephrased for non-expert accessibility.  
- Minor issue: assumes substantial background in graphical models and missing data, which may limit accessibility.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, rating 9/10, accept with minor revisions.
