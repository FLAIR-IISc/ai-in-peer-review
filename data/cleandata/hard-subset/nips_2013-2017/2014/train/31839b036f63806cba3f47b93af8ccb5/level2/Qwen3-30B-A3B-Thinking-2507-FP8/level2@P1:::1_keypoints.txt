- Extends Missingness Graphs to more general recoverability conditions and interdependent missingness mechanisms.  
- Introduces graphical criteria for recovering joint/conditional distributions (Theorems 1–3).  
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic relations are not (Theorem 4).  
- Demonstrates recoverability of P(y|do(x)) despite Y not d-separated from its missingness R_y.  
- Applies results to attrition, providing necessary and sufficient conditions and correcting prior misconceptions (Theorems 5–6).  
- Technical contributions are rigorous with proofs and meaningful examples (e.g., Figure 3); non-recoverability criterion fills a literature gap.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear definitions and useful discussion of related work.  
- Novel and significant insight: causal recoverability need not follow probabilistic recoverability—advances missing-data theory.  
- Weakness: d*-separation is under-motivated and needs clearer justification and contrast with standard d-separation.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation or synthetic experiments demonstrating practical utility.  
- Weakness: Corollary 2’s condition about intermediate nodes being in X is ambiguous and needs a concrete example.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical contribution outweighs addressable weaknesses.
