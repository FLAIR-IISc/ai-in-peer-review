- Problem and contribution: determines when causal or probabilistic queries are recoverable from data with missing entries and introduces Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to model missingness and dependencies.  
- Extension of prior work: extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Main claim 1: provides sufficient conditions for recovering probabilistic queries (joint and conditional distributions) via general ordered factorization.  
- Main claim 2: introduces graphical conditions to recover causal queries, including cases where some factors in identifying estimands are not recoverable.  
- Main claim 3: applies results to attrition problems, characterizing recovery of causal effects under attrition.  
- Theoretical support: presents theorems and examples (notably Theorem 1 for recoverability of Q and Theorem 4 for P(y|do(x)) when Y and Ry have an inducing path).  
- Related work: situates contributions relative to deletion-based, maximum likelihood, and inverse probability weighted methods.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations and illustrative examples (gender wage-gap, simple attrition).  
- Strengths: introduces a more general framework for missing data, provides significant sufficient conditions for causal recovery, and addresses attrition use-cases.  
- Weaknesses: notation and terminology are complex for non-experts, lacks empirical evaluation, and gives limited discussion of the approach's limitations.  
- Recommendation summary: significant contribution with potential broad applicability; suitable for top-tier venues pending addressing notation clarity, empirical validation, and fuller discussion of limitations.
