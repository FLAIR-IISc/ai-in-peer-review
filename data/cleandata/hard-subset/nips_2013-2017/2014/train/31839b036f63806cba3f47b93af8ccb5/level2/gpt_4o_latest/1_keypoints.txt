- Problem: Determines recoverability of causal and probabilistic queries from datasets with missing data given a missingness model (m-graph).  
- Main contribution: Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general graphical conditions and algorithms for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Key insight: Shows causal queries can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.  
- Application: Derives necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects in attrition (longitudinal dropout) problems.  
- Novelty/significance: Substantially advances recoverability theory, handling interacting missingness mechanisms previously unaddressed.  
- Theoretical rigor: Presents formal graphical definitions, theorems, proofs, and introduces concepts like general ordered factorization.  
- Practical relevance: Graphical criteria provide actionable guidance for researchers dealing with missing data in causal inference.  
- Clarity of contributions: Well-organized progression and illustrative examples (e.g., sequential factorization, causal recoverability).  
- Weakness — experiments: Lacks empirical validation or simulations to show practical utility and robustness.  
- Weakness — accessibility: Dense presentation assumes strong background in causal inference and missing data literature.  
- Weakness — limitations discussion: Insufficient discussion of practical challenges, e.g., constructing accurate m-graphs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretical novelty, soundness, and valuable extensions to attrition and non-recoverability criteria.  
- Arguments against acceptance: No empirical results and limited accessibility could hinder practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance based on novelty and rigor, with a request to add empirical validation and expanded discussion of practical challenges in revisions.
