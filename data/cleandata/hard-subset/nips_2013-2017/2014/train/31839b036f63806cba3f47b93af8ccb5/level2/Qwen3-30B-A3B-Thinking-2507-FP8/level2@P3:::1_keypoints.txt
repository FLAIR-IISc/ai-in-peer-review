- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by giving graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Shows causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness Ry are not d-separated (Theorem 4).  
- Introduces a generalized sequential factorization theorem for interacting missingness mechanisms (Theorem 1).  
- Provides non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).  
- Corrects necessary/sufficient conditions for attrition problems, fixing errors in Garcia (2013) (Theorem 6).  
- Uses missingness graphs (m-graphs) to formalize recoverability and close a gap left by prior separability requirements.  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous with detailed proofs and clear links to prior work; writing and structure are strong.  
- Clarifies limitations of MAR/MCAR assumptions and situates contributions within missing data literature.  
- Counterintuitive Example 3 (recovering P(y|do(z)) despite non-separability) is well-justified via do-calculus and d*-separation.  
- Attrition application is practically valuable and corrects influential prior analyses (e.g., collider-path explanation in Figure 4a).  
- Definition 2’s minimality requirement lacks intuitive justification and needs a brief explanatory discussion.  
- Theorem 4’s use of inducing paths should be more clearly linked to existing causal graph concepts (e.g., Verma & Pearl 1991).  
- No empirical validation or synthetic experiment included to demonstrate practical impact—an opportunity missed.  
- Section 7.2 focuses on a single partially observed variable (|Vm|=1); generalization to multiple missing variables should be stated as open.  
- Essential revisions: clarify minimality in Definition 2, explicitly state the |Vm|=1 limitation in Theorems 5–6, and optionally add a synthetic example to illustrate Theorem 4.
