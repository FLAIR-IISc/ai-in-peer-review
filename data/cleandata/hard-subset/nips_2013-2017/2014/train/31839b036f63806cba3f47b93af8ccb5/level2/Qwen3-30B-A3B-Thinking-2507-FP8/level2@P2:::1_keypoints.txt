- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to derive conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when related probabilistic relations (P(y,x), P(y|x)) are not.  
- Introduces new graphical criteria for recoverability, including cases where Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are not d-separable.  
- Extends the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Formal definitions (recoverability, d*-separation) are well-motivated and carefully presented.  
- Examples (notably Example 3) effectively illustrate the key theoretical insights.  
- Applies the framework to attrition problems and provides necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects (Theorem 6).  
- Corrects a misconception in Garcia (2013) regarding attrition, demonstrating practical relevance.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and links effectively to prior work (Mohan et al., Pearl).  
- Main strength: strong theoretical contribution and clear distinction between probabilistic vs causal recoverability.  
- Main weakness: no empirical validation—no simulations, case studies, or real-data demonstrations.  
- Lacks practical implementation guidance; needs a step-by-step application example to show usability.  
- Originality somewhat limited by direct extension of Mohan et al., though the causal recoverability results are novel.  
- Insufficient discussion of connections to CAR and related missing-data frameworks (Heitjan & Rubin, Robins & Rotnitzky).  
- Minor issues: "admissible sequence" definition is confusing and minimality not well motivated.  
- Presentation issues: proof of Theorem 4 is only in the appendix and should be summarized in the main text; notation for missingness mechanisms is dense.  
- Overall: valuable and rigorous theoretical advance, but its impact is reduced by lack of concrete applications and clearer practical guidance.
