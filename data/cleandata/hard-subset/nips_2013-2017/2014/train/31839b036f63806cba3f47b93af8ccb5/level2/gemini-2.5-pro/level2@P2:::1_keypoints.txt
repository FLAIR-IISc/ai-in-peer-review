- Generalized sequential factorization theorem allowing dependencies between missingness mechanisms, making it applicable to complex settings like longitudinal studies.
- New theorem for recovering joint distributions when factorization fails by partitioning the missingness mechanisms.
- Novel graphical criteria for non-recoverability to identify when modeling assumptions are insufficient.
- Key insight that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when probabilistic queries P(y,x) are not.
- Application to attrition, providing necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects in simple attrition models.
- Significance: addresses MNAR missingness and moves beyond MAR/MCAR, with important implications for empirical research.
- Originality: substantial novel contributions, notably the non-recoverability criteria and the distinction between probabilistic and causal recoverability using do-calculus.
- Quality: technically sound with clear theorems, illustrative examples, proofs, and a correction to prior literature.
- Clarity of new concepts: definitions for Theorem 2 (collider path, R(part), Mb) are dense and need a step-by-step example and intuition for the key condition.
- Assumed background: paper assumes strong familiarity with graphical models and do-calculus; a brief recap in Section 6 would improve accessibility.
- From identification to estimation: lacks discussion on practical estimation from finite samples; add commentary on estimators and challenges (efficiency, dimensionality).
- Overall: strong, novel, and rigorous paper with minor clarity and scope issues; suitable for NIPS.
