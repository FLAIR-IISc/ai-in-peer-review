## Review of “Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data”

This paper extends prior work on recoverability of queries from data with missing values, represented using “Missingness Graphs” (m-graphs). The authors build upon Mohan et al. [2013] by presenting more general conditions for recovering both probabilistic (P(y|x), P(y,x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries. A key contribution lies in demonstrating that causal effects can be recoverable even when the underlying probabilistic relationships are not, specifically when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable. The paper applies these results to the problem of attrition, offering nuanced conditions for recovering causal effects in this common scenario.

The work is technically sound and builds logically on the existing literature. The authors provide rigorous theoretical results (Theorems 1-6 and Corollaries 1-2) and clearly define necessary concepts like general ordered factorization and d*-separation.  The extension of sequential factorization (Theorem 1) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms is a notable improvement. The discussion of recovering causal queries even when Y and Ry are inseparable (Example 3 and Theorem 4) is particularly insightful and addresses a limitation of previous approaches.  The connection to attrition (Section 7) provides a practical application and illustrates the benefits of the proposed framework.

However, the paper's clarity suffers somewhat from dense notation and a rapid presentation of theoretical results. While mathematically rigorous, the explanations could be more intuitive, particularly for readers less familiar with causal inference.  The related work section is extensive, but could benefit from a more critical assessment of the existing literature. For example, the discussion of MAR and ML estimation feels somewhat cursory given its prominence in the field.  The paper would also be strengthened by including a small simulation study or real-world example demonstrating the practical impact of the proposed recovery conditions.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound extension of recoverability results for missing data.
*   Addresses an important problem with practical implications (e.g., attrition).
*   Demonstrates a significant advancement in understanding when causal queries can be recovered despite missing data challenges.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Dense notation and rapid theoretical presentation may hinder understanding.
*   Lack of empirical validation (simulation or real-world data).
*   Related work section could be more critically engaged with the field.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of missing data and causal inference. While the density of the presentation and lack of empirical validation are weaknesses, the theoretical contributions are significant. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically focusing on improving clarity and considering the inclusion of a small-scale empirical demonstration.
