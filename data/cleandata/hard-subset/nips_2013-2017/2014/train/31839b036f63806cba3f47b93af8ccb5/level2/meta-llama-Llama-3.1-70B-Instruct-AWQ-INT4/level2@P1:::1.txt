This paper presents a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using graphical models. The authors extend the results of Mohan et al. [2013] by providing more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries and introduce new graphical criteria for recovering causal queries. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear overview of the missingness graphs and the notion of recoverability.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a general ordered factorization theorem that allows for the recovery of probabilistic queries. The authors also introduce a new graphical condition for recovering causal queries, which is based on the concept of d*-separation. The paper includes several examples and applications, including the recovery of causal effects under simple attrition.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a clear and comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.
* The paper includes several examples and applications that demonstrate the usefulness of the proposed framework.
* The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and highlight the contributions of their paper.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes a strong background in graphical models and causal inference, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* Some of the proofs and technical results are relegated to the appendix, which may make it difficult for readers to fully appreciate the technical contributions of the paper.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential pitfalls of the proposed framework.
