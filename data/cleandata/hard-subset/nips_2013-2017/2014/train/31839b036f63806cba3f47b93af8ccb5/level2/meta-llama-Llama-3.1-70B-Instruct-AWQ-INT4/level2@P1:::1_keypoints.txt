- Presents a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using graphical models.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries.  
- Introduces a general ordered factorization theorem enabling recovery of probabilistic queries.  
- Proposes a new graphical condition for recovering causal queries based on d*-separation.  
- Provides several examples and applications, including recovery of causal effects under simple attrition.  
- Well-organized presentation with clear overview of missingness graphs and recoverability.  
- Thorough discussion of related work and clear statement of contributions.  
- Assumes strong background in graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Some proofs and technical results are placed in the appendix, which may hinder appreciation of technical contributions.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential pitfalls of the proposed framework.  
- Reviewer view: significant contribution to graphical models and causal inference.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add limitations discussion and more background for non-experts.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
