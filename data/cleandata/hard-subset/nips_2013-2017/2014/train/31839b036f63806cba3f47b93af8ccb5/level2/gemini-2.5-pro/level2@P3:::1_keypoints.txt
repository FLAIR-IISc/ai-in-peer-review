- Formal graphical framework for recoverability under MNAR built on the m-graph representation.
- Generalizes sequential factorization theorem to handle dependencies between missingness mechanisms.
- Proposes a novel recovery algorithm using partitioning of missingness variables when sequential factorization fails.
- Introduces the first formal graphical criteria for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates that causal effects (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic quantities (e.g., P(y,x)) are not.
- Applies theoretical results to attrition in experiments, yielding necessary and sufficient conditions and correcting prior analyses.
- High significance and originality: tackles a fundamental MNAR problem and provides important new tools.
- Strong technical quality: rigorous theorems, clear graphical definitions, and illustrative examples.
- Well-structured and clear presentation with effective figures that link probabilistic and causal results.
- Presentation is technically dense and may limit accessibility to non-experts.
- Theorem 2 constructs (R(part), Mb) need more intuitive explanation to clarify why they ensure recoverability.
- The connection to d*-separation/dormant independence in Section 6 is abrupt and would benefit from a brief explanatory clause.
- The minimality condition in Definition 2 needs expanded rationale to clarify its necessity for valid factorization paths.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contributions, first non-recoverability criteria, deep causal–probabilistic connection, technical soundness, and practical relevance.
- Argument against acceptance: dense presentation and high prerequisite knowledge may limit immediate accessibility.
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, technically solid work with high potential impact.
