- Extends the m-graph framework to recover probabilistic and causal queries from datasets with missing entries.  
- Introduces a refined sequential factorization theorem that handles dependencies between missingness mechanisms.  
- Provides graphical criteria to recover joint distributions in cases where factorization fails.  
- Proposes novel graphical criteria to establish non-recoverability of queries.  
- Shows that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when the corresponding probabilistic queries P(y|x) are not.  
- Significant and original: decouples recoverability of causal and probabilistic queries, a non-obvious and impactful insight.  
- Offers practical tools: formal graphical criteria for non-recoverability useful for researchers.  
- Technically strong: rigorous theorems and sound arguments based on graphical models and do-calculus.  
- Relevant application: addresses attrition and corrects misconceptions in existing literature.  
- Clear presentation: well-organized, systematic extensions with motivating examples and defined terminology.  
- Main weakness: entirely theoretical with no empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Accessibility concern: dense exposition relying on prior work; a more self-contained recap of m-graph concepts would help broader readership.
