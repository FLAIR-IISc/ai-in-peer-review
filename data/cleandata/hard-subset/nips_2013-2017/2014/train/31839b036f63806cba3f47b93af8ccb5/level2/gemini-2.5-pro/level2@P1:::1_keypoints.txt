- Presents a formal graphical framework (m-graphs) for determining recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries with missing data.  
- Generalizes sequential factorization to handle interdependent missingness mechanisms.  
- Introduces a new theorem for joint distribution recovery using collider paths and Markov blankets of missingness indicators.  
- Provides novel graphical criteria for non-recoverability, enabling identification of fundamentally unsolvable queries under given assumptions.  
- Shows that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic queries (e.g., P(y,x)) are not.  
- Applies the general results to attrition in experiments, deriving necessary and sufficient recovery conditions and correcting prior work.  
- Significance/originality: makes strong, original contributions to missing data and causal inference, notably the non-recoverability criteria and the causal/probabilistic decoupling.  
- Technical quality: theorems are rigorous, build on d-separation/do-calculus, and include formal proofs; application to attrition demonstrates practical relevance.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with effective small graphical examples illustrating concepts and theorems.  
- Major weakness: entirely theoretical with no empirical evaluation or simulations to demonstrate estimator behavior or consistency.  
- Practicality concern: framework assumes a correctly specified m-graph, but model specification challenges and sensitivity to misspecification are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Presentation issue: some complex definitions (e.g., collider path, R(part), Mb(R(i))) are introduced after theorems that rely on them, reducing clarity.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper with important contributions and recommendation for acceptance, but would benefit from simulation studies and more discussion on model specification.
