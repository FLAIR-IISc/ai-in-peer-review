- Paper extends Mohan et al. (2013) to allow interactions among missingness mechanisms via a generalized sequential factorization (Theorem 1).  
- Introduces partition-based criterion and practical corollary to recover joint distributions when no admissible sequence exists (Theorem 2).  
- Presents graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2) to prevent misuse.  
- Shows certain causal effects are recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic factors are not, using dormant independences/d∗-separation (Theorem 4).  
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for joint and causal recovery in single missing-variable (|Vm|=1) attrition settings (Theorems 5–6).  
- Builds on do-calculus and clarifies connections to prior work in causal inference and MNAR literature.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with formal theorems and proofs and illustrative examples.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation (simulations or case studies) to assess finite-sample behavior and robustness to graph misspecification.  
- Exposition is generally clear but dense, with heavy notation and some inconsistent references.  
- Recommends adding explicit pseudo-code for admissible sequence/partition construction and a worked example for Theorem 4.  
- Suggests clearly restating key assumptions (semi-Markovian, positivity, R not parents of V) up front.  
- Originality rated high: generalized R-factorization, partition-based recovery, and negative criteria fill gaps since Mohan et al.  
- High significance for researchers on MNAR, causal inference under missingness, and longitudinal attrition, offering actionable graphical checks.  
- Suggested additions: algorithmic procedures with complexity guarantees, simulations vs. MAR/IPW/MI, sensitivity to m-graph misspecification, and a validator tool.  
- Pros: strong theory, novel non-recoverability results, important causal vs probabilistic insight, clear attrition characterization.  
- Cons: no experiments, limited practical guidance on constructing m-graphs, minor clarity and citation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept after addressing presentation and adding evaluation.
