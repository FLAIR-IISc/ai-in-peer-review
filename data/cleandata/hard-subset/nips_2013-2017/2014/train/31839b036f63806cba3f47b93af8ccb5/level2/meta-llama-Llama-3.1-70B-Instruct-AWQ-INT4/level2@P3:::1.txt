**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper addresses the problem of deciding whether a causal or probabilistic query is estimable from data corrupted by missing entries, given a model of the missingness process. The authors extend the results of Mohan et al. [2013] by presenting more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries and causal queries. They introduce a graphical representation called Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to explicitly portray the missingness process and dependencies among variables. The authors provide sufficient conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions, as well as causal queries, and demonstrate the applicability of their results to problems of attrition.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon the work of Mohan et al. [2013], who introduced the concept of Missingness Graphs and derived conditions for recovering probabilistic queries. The authors also draw upon the work of Pearl and Mohan [2013], who discussed the notion of recoverability and its importance in handling missing data. Additionally, the paper references various other works on handling missing data, including deletion-based methods, maximum likelihood estimation, and imputation-based methods.
