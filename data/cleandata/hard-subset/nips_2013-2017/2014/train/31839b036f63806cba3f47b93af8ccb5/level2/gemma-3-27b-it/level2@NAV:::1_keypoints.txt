- Extends prior m-graph recoverability work (Mohan et al. 2013) to more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relationships are not (e.g., Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are not d-separable).  
- Introduces formal tools and rigorous results (Theorems 1–6, Corollaries 1–2) and defines concepts like general ordered factorization and d*-separation.  
- Extends sequential factorization (Theorem 1) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Example 3 and Theorem 4 provide key insight that causal queries can be recovered despite inseparability between Y and Ry.  
- Applies framework to attrition, offering nuanced, practically relevant recovery conditions (Section 7).  
- Technically sound and a significant theoretical contribution to missing data and causal inference.  
- Presentation suffers from dense notation and rapid theoretical exposition, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Related work section is extensive but lacks critical engagement (discussion of MAR and ML estimation is cursory).  
- Lacks empirical validation—no simulation or real-world example to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on improved clarity and inclusion of a small empirical demonstration.
