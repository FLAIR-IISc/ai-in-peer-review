- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to provide more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Main theoretical advance: sequential factorization theorem (Theorem 1) extended to interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Novel and surprising result (Theorem 4 & Example 3): causal queries can be recoverable even when required probabilistic queries are not.  
- Corrects a prior misconception in the literature about attrition (addresses Garcia, 2013).  
- Rigorous technical development with detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Clear organization, definitions, theorems, and illustrative examples (Section 6 and Example 3 especially strong).  
- Effective use of m-graphs to visualize and communicate missingness assumptions.  
- Comprehensive related work coverage (MCAR, MAR, MNAR, graphical and causal approaches).  
- Significant practical relevance for fields facing missing data (e.g., epidemiology, social sciences) and for attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Main weakness: lacks empirical validation or simulation studies to demonstrate practical applicability of the theorems.  
- Conditions for recoverability (particularly Theorem 4) are complex and may be difficult for practitioners to apply directly.  
- Would benefit from implementation guidance (simple algorithm or checklist) to aid practical use.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, well-executed theoretical contribution that merits acceptance.
