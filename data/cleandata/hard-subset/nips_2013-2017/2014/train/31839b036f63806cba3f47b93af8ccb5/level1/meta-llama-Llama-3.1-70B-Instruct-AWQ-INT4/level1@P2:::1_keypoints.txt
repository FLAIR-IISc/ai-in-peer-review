- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Introduces missingness graphs (m-graphs) and defines recoverability as computing consistent estimates as if no data were missing.  
- Presents theorems and corollaries giving sufficient conditions to recover joint, conditional, and causal distributions.  
- Introduces new graphical conditions that establish non-recoverability for joint and conditional distributions.  
- Gives a sufficient condition to recover causal queries P(y|do(x)) even when Y and Ry are not d-separable.  
- Applies the theoretical results to attrition problems and states necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for recovering causal effects in those settings.  
- Paper is comprehensive, rigorous, and technically sound with well-proven theorems.  
- Well-organized with clear definitions, explanations, and helpful examples/illustrations.  
- Thoroughly referenced and situates itself well in the existing literature.  
- Assumes high technical expertise in graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Some proofs and derivations are highly technical and require careful reading.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation or experiments to demonstrate practical applicability.  
- Overall judged a significant and original contribution with potential wide applicability.  
- Ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous, original methods, well-presented and potentially widely applicable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, technical density, and no empirical validation.
