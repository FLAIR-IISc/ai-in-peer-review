This paper addresses the problem of recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data, given a model of the missingness process. The authors extend the results of Mohan et al. [2013] by presenting more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries of the form P(y|x) and P(y, x) as well as causal queries of the form P(y|do(x)). They also apply their results to problems of attrition and characterize the recovery of causal effects from data corrupted by attrition.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of missing data and the concept of missingness graphs (m-graphs). They also provide a clear explanation of the recoverability of queries and the conditions under which they can be recovered.

The main contribution of the paper is the extension of the sequential factorization theorem to handle cases where the missingness mechanisms interact with each other. The authors also introduce new graphical criteria that preclude recoverability of joint and conditional distributions. They show that causal queries may be recoverable even when the associated probabilistic relations are not recoverable.

The paper also includes a thorough discussion of related work and a clear explanation of the differences between their approach and existing methods. The authors also provide a detailed example of how their results can be applied to problems of attrition.
