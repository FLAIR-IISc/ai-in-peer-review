- Presents a comprehensive graphical-model framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries.  
- Introduces new graphical criteria for recovering causal queries.  
- Well-organized with clear overview of missingness graphs and recoverability notions.  
- Technically sound with thorough analysis of recoverability conditions.  
- Uses examples and illustrations to clarify concepts and improve accessibility.  
- Original contribution in extending prior work and adding causal recovery criteria.  
- Significant potential to improve inference accuracy from missing data and handle attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Implications for more robust missing-data methods in machine learning and statistics.  
- Writing, notation, and explanation of assumptions/limitations are clear and consistent.  
- Assumes high level of expertise in graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility.  
- Lacks a detailed empirical or methodological comparison with other missing-data approaches.  
- Recommends acceptance for publication (NIPS 2014) due to significance and technical contribution.
