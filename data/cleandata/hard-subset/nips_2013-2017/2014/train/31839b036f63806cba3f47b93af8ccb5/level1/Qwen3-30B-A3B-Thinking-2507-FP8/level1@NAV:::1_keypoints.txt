- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by deriving more general graphical conditions for recovering queries from missing data using m-graphs.  
- Demonstrates that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when related probabilistic relations (e.g., P(y,x)) are not, especially when Y and its missingness are not d-separable.  
- Introduces Theorems 1–6 characterizing recoverability of joint/conditional distributions and causal effects, with application to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Significant theoretical advance: handles complex missingness mechanisms where missingness processes interact.  
- Novel insight: causal recoverability can circumvent separability requirements needed for probabilistic recovery.  
- Technically sound: complete proofs, well-organized, and clearly motivated by causal-inference problems.  
- Relevant application: addresses attrition, a common and important issue in longitudinal studies.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation beyond illustrative examples.  
- Weakness: does not discuss computational complexity of applying the proposed conditions.  
- Weakness: needs more concrete guidance for practical implementation on real datasets.  
- Originality: clearly differentiates from prior work (Mohan et al., Garcia) and corrects Garcia’s claim about necessary conditions for attrition.  
- Significance: provides a formal framework for diagnosing causal recoverability from missing data with broad scientific implications.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality theoretical contribution that meets NIPS criteria; recommend acceptance.
