- Presents a formal graphical framework extending m-graphs to determine recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries with missing data.
- Introduces a generalized sequential factorization theorem handling dependencies between missingness mechanisms for more general probabilistic recovery.
- Proposes novel graphical criteria for non-recoverability, filling a gap often overlooked in prior work.
- Shows that a causal query P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when the probabilistic distribution P(y,x) is not, due to variable–missingness dependencies.
- Applies theoretical results to derive necessary and sufficient conditions for recovery in attrition problems, correcting prior results.
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental, practical problem and advances beyond MAR/MCAR assumptions; the causal-vs-probabilistic recoverability insight is particularly significant.
- Strength: Original and non-trivial extension of prior m-graph work, including the new non-recoverability criteria.
- Strength: Technically sound with formal theorems, corollaries, and illustrative graphical examples.
- Weakness: Notationally dense and assumes familiarity with m-graphs; would benefit from a brief, intuitive recap and earlier definition of terms like R(part).
- Weakness: Entirely theoretical with no simulation or empirical validation to demonstrate practical performance.
- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution with clear significance and originality; main issues are presentation clarity and lack of empirical evaluation.
