- Paper studies when P(V), P(y|x), and P(y|do(x)) are recoverable from data with missing entries given a causal m-graph, extending Mohan et al. (2013).

- Contribution 1: Introduces "general ordered factorization" allowing R nodes in admissible factorizations and gives Theorem 1 with sufficient conditions for estimating conditional factors from the manifest distribution.

- Contribution 2: For cases with no admissible sequence, Theorem 2 uses partitions of R and Markov blankets via collider paths to give sufficient conditions and constructive formulas to recover P(V).

- Contribution 3: Theorem 3 and Corollary 2 provide necessary graphical non-recoverability criteria for P(V) and P(y|x) based on adjacencies and all-collider paths between variables and their R indicators.

- Contribution 4: Shows some causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recovered even when P(V) or P(y|x) are not, formalized in Theorem 4 using do-calculus and d*-separation; Theorems 5–6 give tight characterizations for |Vm|=1.

- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) by broadening admissible factorizations, adds non-recoverability criteria, and leverages identifiability/do-calculus results.

- Technical soundness: Theorems are precise, graph-theoretic, checkable, and constructive formulas align with m-graph semantics under positivity; proofs are in the appendix and appear coherent.

- Completeness: Provides sufficient (not complete) criteria for general semi-Markovian graphs; a full decision procedure for arbitrary |Vm| remains open and algorithmic complexity isn't provided.

- Empirics: Purely theoretical work with no simulations or case studies to assess finite-sample performance or estimator variance.

- Clarity: Generally well-written with consistent notation and illustrative definitions, though some figures and references need clearer elaboration and formatting fixes.

- Suggested clarifications: Add pseudocode/checklists to construct admissible sequences and R partitions, and clarify latent confounding scope in Theorem 2 vs Corollary 1 with examples.

- Originality: Novel inclusion of R nodes in factorization, new non-recoverability criteria, demonstration of recoverable causal effects despite non-recoverable probabilistic quantities, and corrected tight results for simple attrition.

- Significance: Important for MNAR settings in causal inference and ML; provides actionable graphical criteria and constructive estimands but lacks full decision procedures and empirical validation.

- Strength: Extends prior work with broader applicability, offers both positive and negative diagnostic criteria, and provides insightful causal recovery results.

- Weakness: No experimental validation, limited practical guidance/automation for finding admissible sequences/partitions, reliance on correct m-graph specification, and some notation/figure issues.

- Open questions for authors: Provide an explicit algorithm and complexity for R partitions/Mb(R(i)); systematic search procedure for admissible sequences; sensitivity to positivity violations; and a simulated example under MNAR.

- Arguments for acceptance: Substantive theoretical novelty, practical checkable criteria, constructive estimands, and relevance to common MNAR problems.

- Arguments against acceptance: Lack of empirical demonstrations and algorithms with complexity guarantees, incomplete general decision procedure, and dependence on correct m-graphs without sensitivity analysis.

- Overall: Solid theoretical contribution with meaningful extensions over prior work, offering useful graphical criteria and formal results but limited by lack of empirical validation and automation details.
