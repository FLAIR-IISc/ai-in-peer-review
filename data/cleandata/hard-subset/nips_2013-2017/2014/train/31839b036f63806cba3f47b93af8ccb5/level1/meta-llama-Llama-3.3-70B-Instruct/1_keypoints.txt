- Problem: Determines when causal or probabilistic queries are estimable from data with missing entries given a model of the missingness process.
- Extension: Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) by providing more general conditions for query recovery.
- Method: Introduces Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to represent the missingness process and variable dependencies.
- Contribution 1: Proposes general ordered factorization including R variables to broaden recoverability of probabilistic queries.
- Contribution 2: Derives graphical conditions for recovering causal effects P(y|do(x)) when Y and its missingness are not d-separable.
- Contribution 3: Applies results to attrition problems, characterizing recovery of causal effects under attrition.
- Writing/organization: Well-written with clear introduction to m-graphs and recoverability; technical sections contain detailed proofs and illustrative examples.
- Strength: Novel and powerful framework for handling missing data with wide potential applicability.
- Strength: Provides clear, intuitive graphical conditions for causal-effect recovery.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical relevance via application to attrition scenarios.
- Weakness: Assumes reader familiarity with graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility.
- Weakness: Results rely on assumptions about the missingness process that may not always hold in practice.
- Weakness: Lacks a comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed methods’ performance.
- Pro-accept: Novel framework, clear graphical recovery conditions, and useful application to attrition argue for acceptance.
- Con-accept: Accessibility issues, strong/maybe-unrealistic assumptions, and insufficient empirical validation argue against acceptance.
- Recommendation: Paper makes a significant contribution and should be accepted, with suggested additions of more explanations/examples and broader empirical evaluation.
