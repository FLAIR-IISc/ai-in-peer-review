- Problem: recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data given a model of the missingness process.  
- Extension of prior work: generalizes Mohan et al. (2013) to recover P(y|x), P(y,x) and P(y|do(x)).  
- Main technical contribution: extends the sequential factorization theorem to interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- New tools: introduces graphical criteria that preclude recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Causal vs probabilistic: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.  
- Application: characterizes recovery under attrition and provides a detailed example.  
- Writing/organization: paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction to missing data and m-graphs.  
- Recoverability analysis: provides a thorough explanation of conditions under which queries can be recovered.  
- Related work: includes a detailed discussion and clearly distinguishes the approach from existing methods.  
- Strength: clear exposition, strong organization, and a detailed attrition example.  
- Weakness: assumes high familiarity with graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: some proofs and technical details are relegated to the appendix, hindering standalone readability.  
- Weakness: could benefit from additional examples and illustrations to clarify concepts.  
- Originality: novel extension of sequential factorization and new graphical criteria for recoverability.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with significant implications for analysis of data with missing values, especially causal inference.  
- Quality: overall high-quality writing and organization with thorough technical treatment.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, new criteria, and demonstration that causal recovery can succeed when probabilistic recovery fails.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical prerequisites for readers and reliance on appendix for key proofs.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance.
