- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to derive more general recoverability conditions for probabilistic and causal queries with missing data.
- Addresses the core problem of determining when queries can be consistently estimated from data with missing entries.
- Refines the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Introduces new graphical criteria for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions, complementing existing sufficient conditions.
- Shows that causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations (e.g., P(y,x), P(y|x)) are not.
- Applies results to attrition in longitudinal studies, providing necessary and sufficient conditions for causal effect recovery under simple attrition.
- Theoretical development is rigorous, with precise definitions, theorems, and proofs (appendix-referenced).
- Examples (e.g., Example 1 and 3) effectively illustrate theorem application.
- Clearly distinguishes between probabilistic and causal recoverability, a significant conceptual contribution.
- Demonstrates deep engagement with and proper positioning within missing data and causal inference literature.
- Rectifies previous misconceptions about attrition, showing Garcia (2013)'s necessary condition for non-recoverability is incorrect.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses consistent, well-defined notation.
- Main limitation: lacks empirical validation (simulations or real-world applications) to demonstrate practical utility.
- Could provide more practical guidance on constructing m-graphs from real data.
- Original and significant contribution that meaningfully extends prior work on m-graphs and recoverability.
- Technically sound and complete as a theoretical contribution, though empirical testing would strengthen impact.
- High significance for practitioners dealing with missing data and causal inference, recommending acceptance.
