- Extends m-graph framework with more general recoverability conditions and shows causal queries can be recoverable even when probabilistic queries are not.  
- Extends sequential factorization (Theorem 1) to allow interactions between missingness mechanisms.  
- Introduces Theorem 2 to handle cases where no admissible sequence exists, broadening applicability.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance via detailed attrition examples (Sections 7.1–7.2).  
- Theoretical results are rigorous and significant, offering novel insights for missing-data inference.  
- Presentation is dense, heavily notational, and often lacks intuitive explanations in the main text.  
- Proofs are in the appendix, but core arguments need more illustrative examples and intuition.  
- Connection between d*-separation and causal recoverability (Theorem 4) is abrupt and under-explained.  
- Related work section is extensive but reads like a catalog; needs focused comparison to MAR estimation, multiple imputation, etc.  
- Over-relies on Mohan et al. and Mohan & Pearl, making the paper less accessible to readers unfamiliar with those works.  
- Non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3, Corollary 2) are useful but lack practical guidance for real datasets.  
- Terminology (e.g., "inducing path," "dormant independence") introduced without sufficient context for newcomers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, emphasizing improved clarity, more examples, streamlined notation, and stronger related-work positioning.
