- Presents a framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using graphical models (m-graphs).  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for probabilistic query recovery and new graphical conditions for causal query recovery.  
- Well-organized with a clear introduction to missing data and the need for formal missingness representation.  
- Introduces Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) as a novel approach, with thorough notation and terminology.  
- Main theoretical contributions: sequential factorization theorem, non-recoverability criteria for joint/conditional distributions, and recoverability results for causal queries.  
- Includes several illustrative examples, e.g., recovering causal effects under attrition.  
- Strength: provides a clear, comprehensive framework for query recovery from missing data.  
- Strength: novel and well-explained use of m-graphs.  
- Strength: examples improve accessibility and demonstrate application.  
- Weakness: assumes high familiarity with graphical models and causal inference, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: many proofs and technical details are in the appendix, which can obscure main arguments.  
- Weakness: could include more discussion of limitations and potential applications.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to add background on graphical models/causal inference and expand discussion of limitations and applications.
