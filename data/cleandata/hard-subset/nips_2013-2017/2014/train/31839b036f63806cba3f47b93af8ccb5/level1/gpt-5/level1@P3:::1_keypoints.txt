- Extends missingness graphs (m-graphs) to decide recoverability of probabilistic (P(y,x), P(y|x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries with missingness.
- Generalizes ordered factorization to include missingness indicators R in the ordering (Theorem 1), enabling recovery when missingness mechanisms interact.
- Gives a new sufficient condition for joint distribution recovery in semi-Markovian models without admissible sequences via R-partitions and Markov blankets along collider paths (Theorem 2).
- Provides graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic factors are not, using do-calculus and dormant independences (Example 3, Theorem 4).
- Derives necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability in simple attrition (|Vm|=1) (Theorems 5–6).
- Theorems are clearly stated with proofs in the appendix and Example 1 illustrates the importance of minimality in factorization.
- Theorem 2’s R-partition/Markov blanket construction appears workable and consistent with ADMG separation rules.
- Non-recoverability criteria align with intuition about selection bias via colliders.
- The insight that conditioning on Ry=0 can yield unbiased causal estimands when dormant independences hold is particularly strong.
- Some conditions are intricate and may be hard for practitioners to check in practice.
- The "general algorithms" claim reads more as constructive criteria rather than a complete decision procedure; pseudocode would strengthen the paper.
- Positivity over complete cases is assumed and extensions beyond strict positivity are only briefly mentioned.
- Theorem 4’s conditions are powerful but abstract; more guidance on graphical verification is needed.
- No empirical or synthetic demonstrations are provided; small simulations would improve accessibility.
- Notation is heavy and occasionally ambiguous (e.g., same symbols for different graph operations, nonstandard X′ and R′x usage).
- Dense condition statements (e.g., Theorem 1) would benefit from a graphical checklist and one-line intuitions per theorem.
- Reference formatting has inconsistencies that should be fixed.
- Contributions are original and advance beyond Mohan et al. (2013) in multiple ways, including interacting R mechanisms and unified graphical treatment.
- Results are significant for MNAR and causal inference practice, especially the attrition characterization.
- Adoption may be limited by technical complexity; tool support or decision procedures would broaden impact.
- Suggested improvements: provide explicit algorithms for finding admissible sequences, constructing R-partitions, and verifying Theorem 4 graphically.
- Clarify assumptions about edges among R and consistency with restrictions on R→V∪U edges.
- Discuss complexity/scalability of recovery procedures, sensitivity to positivity violations, and tests for model misspecification.
- Pros: strong theoretical advances, clear non-recoverability criteria, novel causal recoverability insight, practical attrition results, good fit for venue.
- Cons: heavy notation/ambiguities, lack of unified decision procedure and empirical validation, Theorem 4 may seem niche without more guidance.
- Recommendation: Accept.
