- Adds selective attention gating to CNNs and trains gating parameters to improve classification, addressing adaptive reconfiguration under resource/task limits.
- Classification results are impressive but the method functions as a black box with little explanation of why it works.
- Analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters (mean and variance) is inadequate and uninformative.
- Paper is an important step beyond fixed-parameter feed-forward networks despite interpretability shortcomings.
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need clearer explanation.
- Major suggestion: explore connections to dynamical systems—model appears closer to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning because reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic.
- Need deeper analysis of internal dynamics (e.g., why layer‑1 activations increase on first iteration, effect of global scaling, evolution of gating variables in local image regions).
- Recommend analyzing hard cases (images misclassified by standard Maxout but corrected by the proposed model) and testing effects of constraining learned theta statistics.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: h_M is undefined and it’s ambiguous whether F[i] and Θ[i] are collected per image or overwritten.
- Equation issues: Eq.11’s summation index is unclear and Eq.8 may need an image index j.
- Notation problem: symbols x and d after Eq.11 are not defined.
- Question the necessity and justification of theta regularization; unclear how the final gradient updates are computed or whether they adjust posterior hyperparameters.
- Overall: interesting and effective recurrent-style approach with strong accuracy gains but needs better interpretability and theoretical/algorithmic clarity.
