The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that incorporates top-down processing via an attention-based feedback mechanism. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets.

This is a well-written and interesting paper that demonstrates novelty in its model design and application to computer vision. The approach of integrating both bottom-up and top-down signals to train a CNN is a forward-looking and valuable contribution. I particularly appreciate the evaluation of the model's performance over time and the use of a gating mechanism to constrain the learned representations. Since the quality of these representations hinges on the attention policy, the paper would be more compelling if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail.

The experiments are insightful, and the authors provide helpful visualizations that offer a better understanding of the model's behavior. However, my main concern is the lack of evaluation on a more challenging, large-scale dataset. Given that the proposed method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, its scalability is a critical question. The paper would be significantly strengthened by including results on a dataset like ILSVRC to convincingly demonstrate the model's effectiveness on larger images and a greater number of classes.

In summary, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs results in a compelling model that is certainly worthy of further study. However, the contribution would be more impactful with practical insights drawn from experiments on larger datasets.
