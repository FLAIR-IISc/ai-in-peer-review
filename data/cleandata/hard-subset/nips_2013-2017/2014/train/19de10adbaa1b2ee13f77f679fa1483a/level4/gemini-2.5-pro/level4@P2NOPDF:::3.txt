This paper introduces a novel convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that integrates top-down processing through an attention-based feedback mechanism. The proposed model is shown to achieve state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets.

The paper is well-written and presents a model that is novel in its design and application. The core contribution—combining bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN—is a conceptually significant and forward-looking idea. I particularly commend the specific architectural choices, such as the use of a gated signal to constrain the learned representations and the evaluation of the model's performance across multiple time steps. The experiments are insightful, and the inclusion of visualizations is particularly helpful for understanding the model's internal dynamics and the behavior of the attention mechanism.

My primary concern, however, relates to the experimental validation. The proposed architecture is considerably more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN. While the results on CIFAR are strong, demonstrating the model's scalability and practical utility on a larger and more complex dataset, such as ILSVRC, would substantially strengthen the paper's claims. An evaluation on a dataset with larger images and a greater number of classes is crucial for understanding how such a complex feedback mechanism performs under more demanding conditions. The addition of these results would provide more practical insights and make a more compelling case for the model's general applicability.

Additionally, the paper could be improved by including a discussion of the model's limitations. Since the quality of the learned representations is heavily dependent on the attention policy, an analysis or demonstration of potential failure cases would provide a more complete and nuanced understanding of the algorithm's capabilities and robustness.

In summary, this is an interesting paper that introduces a promising model architecture. The combination of attention-based feedback with CNNs is a novel concept that is certainly worthy of further investigation. Although the current results are encouraging, the paper's impact would be significantly greater if the authors could demonstrate the model's effectiveness on a large-scale dataset.
