- Proposes dasNet: feedback connections that modulate convolutional filter activations to implement visual attention, trained with reinforcement learning.  
- Combination of existing components is novel and effective.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the architecture, RL training, and experiments.  
- Provides thorough analysis with visualizations of attention mechanisms and network dynamics.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Strengths: novelty of approach, clarity of presentation, and significant empirical results.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art visual attention methods.  
- Weakness: does not discuss potential limitations such as computational training cost and attention interpretability.  
- Recommendation: paper is a significant contribution and deserves publication.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on larger, more challenging datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to further validate effectiveness.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel/effective approach, SOTA results, clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: individual components not new, missing comparisons, missing limitations discussion.
