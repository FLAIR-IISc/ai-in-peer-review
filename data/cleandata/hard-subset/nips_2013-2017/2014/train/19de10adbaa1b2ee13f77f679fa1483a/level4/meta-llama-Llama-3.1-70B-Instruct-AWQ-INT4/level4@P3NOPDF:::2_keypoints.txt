- Presents an innovative method for selective attention in CNNs that adaptively reconfigures processing under resource limits.  
- Trains gating parameters to optimize classification performance and achieves strong image recognition results.  
- Method is a “black box” with limited insight into why it works, especially regarding learned theta distribution statistics.  
- Analysis of image representations fails to clarify the role of mean and variance of theta in performance.  
- Paper advances beyond fixed-parameter feed-forward networks by incorporating recurrent/adaptive computation.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equations that need clarification or typo corrections.  
- Major: Authors should better connect the approach to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than reinforcement learning.  
- Major: Need deeper analysis of model behavior (e.g., why layer 1 activations increase at iteration 1 and whether that affects the objective).  
- Major: Recommend focused local analysis of gating variable evolution and study of hard cases where the model outperforms baseline.  
- Major: Suggest constraining or probing learned theta (mean/variance) to understand its impact on performance.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 is unclear—h_M is undefined and it’s ambiguous whether F[i]/Θ[i] are stored or overwritten per image.  
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation is unclear and Equation 8 may need an image index (j).  
- Minor: Variables 'x' and 'd' after Equation 11 are used without explicit definitions.  
- Minor: Theta regularization seems unnecessary; the final gradient-update step is poorly explained and lacks theoretical justification (how gradients are computed and whether this adjusts posterior hyperparameters).  
- Conclusion: Promising demonstration that recurrent computation helps recognition, but requires more interpretability and analytical justification.
