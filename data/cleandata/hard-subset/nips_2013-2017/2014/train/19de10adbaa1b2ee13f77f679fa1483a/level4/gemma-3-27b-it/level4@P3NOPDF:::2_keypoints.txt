- Introduces selective attention gates for CNNs and a method to learn gating parameters to improve classification under resource/task constraints.
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing as an alternative to large fixed feed-forward networks.
- Reports strong classification improvements, showing recurrent computation can aid feature extraction.
- Method functions largely as a “black box” with limited insight into why it works.
- Analysis of image representations is not revealing; roles of learned theta mean and variance remain unclear.
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need clarification.
- Major: Explore framing the method as a dynamical system / recurrent neural network rather than reinforcement learning for principled optimization.
- Major: Current analysis of the example cat image is insufficient; unexplained initial increase in layer-1 activations needs investigation.
- Major: Recommend focused analyses (small image region, monitoring local gating evolution) and studying hard cases misclassified by baseline but fixed by the model.
- Major: Probe learned parameters directly by constraining theta mean/variance to understand their effects.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 omits definition of h_M and has ambiguous indexing/array overwriting for image fitness values.
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation and indexing (per-image vs overall) are unclear.
- Minor: Variables 'x' and 'd' after Equation 11 are undefined and need clarification.
- Minor: Unclear necessity of regularizing theta given its prior; motivate or justify regularization.
- Minor: Final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling/theta fitness eval) lacks explanation and theoretical justification—are these updating hyperparameters or a posterior over policies?
