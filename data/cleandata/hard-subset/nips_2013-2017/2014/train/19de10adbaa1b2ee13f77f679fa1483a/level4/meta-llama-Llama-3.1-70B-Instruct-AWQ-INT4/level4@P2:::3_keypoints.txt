- Proposes dasNet, a CNN architecture with attention-based top-down feedback to dynamically modify convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Enables iterative internal attention focusing on discriminative image features.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, detailing architecture components and the reinforcement learning policy for attention.  
- Thorough related-work analysis highlighting top-down processing parallels with human vision.  
- Experimental results on CIFAR-10/100 show state-of-the-art performance with ~6% relative improvement over a vanilla CNN.  
- Provides insightful visualizations of dynamic filter activations and attention patterns over time.  
- Strength: novel model design combining bottom-up and top-down signals.  
- Strength: forward-looking approach and use of gated signals to constrain representations.  
- Strength: evaluation across time demonstrating dynamic behavior of the network.  
- Main limitation: no experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ILSVRC) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Unclear how dasNet will perform on larger images and more complex, realistic scenarios.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss potential failure cases/limitations and include experiments on larger datasets.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong CIFAR results, useful visualizations, and integration of top-down/bottom-up signals.  
- Rejection arguments: limited evaluation scope, lack of scalability evidence, and unclear real-world performance.  
- Overall: compelling and well-executed idea with promising small-scale results but needs larger-scale validation.
