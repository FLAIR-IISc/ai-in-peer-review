The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that incorporates top-down processing via attention-based feedback. The results on the CIFAR datasets demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.

This paper is generally interesting and exhibits novelty in both its model design and its application to computer vision. It is also forward-looking to consider both bottom-up and top-down signals when training a CNN. I particularly appreciate the evaluation of the model’s behavior over time and the use of gated signals to constrain the learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations is heavily dependent on the attention policy, it would be insightful if the authors could discuss or demonstrate scenarios where the algorithm might struggle or fail.

The experiments are compelling, and the authors effectively use visualization techniques to aid understanding of the model. However, because the method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, I believe the paper would be strengthened by results on the ILSVRC dataset. This would convincingly demonstrate its scalability to a larger dataset with higher-resolution images. This is my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and a CNN creates an interesting model that warrants further investigation, and more practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be highly valuable.
