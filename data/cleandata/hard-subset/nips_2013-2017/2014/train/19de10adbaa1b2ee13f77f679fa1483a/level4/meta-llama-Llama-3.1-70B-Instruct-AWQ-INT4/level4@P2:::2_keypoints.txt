- Proposes a novel selective-attention mechanism via feedback connections and trains gating parameters to improve DNN classification.  
- Shows impressive classification gains, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition.  
- Analysis of image representations is uninformative; the roles of the learned theta mean and variance are unclear.  
- Connection to dynamical systems is underexplored; the method appears closer to recurrent nets than RL (immediate reward, deterministic policy) and implications should be discussed.  
- The cat-image processing analysis is not insightful; more focused analysis (small regions, gating evolution) is needed.  
- Suggest analyzing difficult cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected by proposed model) to reveal strengths/weaknesses.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: where/how is h_M defined (is it the observation collection step)?  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: are F[i] and Θ[i] stored per-image or overwritten inside the image loop?  
- Eqn 11 ambiguity: unclear what the summation index is and whether Eqn 8 should be indexed by image j.  
- Notation ambiguity after Eqn 11: undefined variables 'x' and 'd'.  
- Regularization of θ is not clearly justified given θ is sampled from a prior and may already trend small.  
- The final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling/evaluating θs) is poorly explained; gradients computation and theoretical justification are needed.  
- Overall, an interesting recurrent approach that likely helps extract/focus complex features, but the work remains a black box lacking mechanistic explanation.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear writing.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak representation analysis, insufficient dynamical-systems discussion, unhelpful image analysis, and unresolved methodological/notation issues.
