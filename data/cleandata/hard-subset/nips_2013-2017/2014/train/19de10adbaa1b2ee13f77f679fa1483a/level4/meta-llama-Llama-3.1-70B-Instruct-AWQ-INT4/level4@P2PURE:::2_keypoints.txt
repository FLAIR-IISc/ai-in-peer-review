- Proposes selective attention via trainable gating parameters in CNNs to optimize classification performance.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and varying tasks.  
- Reports impressive classification accuracy but treats the method as a black box with unclear underlying mechanisms.  
- Provides little insight into why the approach is effective; analysis of image representations is not revealing.  
- The roles of learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are not well understood.  
- Represents an important step beyond fixed-parameter, feed-forward networks by introducing recurrent/adaptive computation.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos and areas needing further explanation.  
- Major: The method is more closely related to recurrent/dynamical systems than to reinforcement learning; training looks like traversing recurrent policy-parameter space.  
- Major: The cat-image analysis fails to reveal how new model features aid recognition; layer-1 activation increases and global scaling effects are unexplained.  
- Suggestion: Analyze gating evolution in a localized image region, inspect hard cases (errors by baseline but corrected by model), and probe effects of constraining learned theta parameters.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 ambiguous—definition of h_M unclear and it’s unknown whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored or overwritten per iteration.  
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation and indexing are unclear; Equation 8 may need image index j; variables 'x' and 'd' are undefined.  
- Minor/theoretical: The need for regularizing theta is not justified, and the final optimization step (sampling thetas, evaluating fitness, then gradient updates) lacks explanation and theoretical grounding.  
- Overall: Recurrent computation appears useful for extracting complex features, but the approach lacks transparency and mechanistic explanation.
