The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that incorporates top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. The results on CIFAR datasets demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.

This paper is generally interesting and exhibits novelty in both model design and its application to the vision problem. Considering both bottom-up and top-down signals for CNN training is a forward-looking approach. I particularly appreciate the evaluation of the model's dynamics over time and the use of gated signals to constrain the learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations is heavily dependent on the attention policy, it would be beneficial if the authors discussed or demonstrated potential failure cases for the algorithm.

The experiments are well-executed and include visualizations to aid understanding. However, given the increased complexity of this method compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, the paper would be strengthened by results on ILSVRC. This would more convincingly demonstrate the scalability of the approach to a larger dataset with higher-resolution images. This remains my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs creates a compelling model worthy of further investigation. More practical insights derived from experiments on larger datasets would be highly valuable.
