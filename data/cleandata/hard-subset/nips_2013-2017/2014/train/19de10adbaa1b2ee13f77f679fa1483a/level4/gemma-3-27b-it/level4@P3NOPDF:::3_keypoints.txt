- Introduces a CNN architecture with top-down processing via attention-based feedback.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR datasets.  
- Novel model design combining bottom-up and top-down signals is a promising advance for computer vision.  
- Temporal evaluation and gated signals are effective for constraining learned representations.  
- Quality of representations depends on the attention policy; discuss potential failure modes and challenging scenarios.  
- Experimental analysis is well-executed with helpful visualizations clarifying model behavior.  
- Increased architectural complexity raises scalability concerns compared to feedforward CNNs.  
- Absence of ImageNet (ILSVRC) results is a primary concern; demonstrating performance on larger, higher-resolution datasets is needed.  
- Overall a strong, interesting contribution contingent on addressing scalability and broader evaluation.
