- Summary: Proposes dasNet, a Maxout CNN augmented with recurrent feedback that gates internal feature maps over multiple passes; policy learned with SNES yields state-of-the-art unaugmented results on CIFAR-10/100.  
- Novelty: Main novelty is directly modulating internal CNN feature maps with a large-scale NES-trained policy rather than glimpse-based or input-level attention.  
- Relation to prior work: Consistent with recurrent/top-down vision and RL attention literature, but differs by gating channels (feature-wise) and using black-box SNES for a high-dimensional policy.  
- Technical soundness: Gating mechanism, observation design, and scaled-softmax policy are straightforward and well-justified.  
- Training: Misclassification upweighting and training procedure are reasonable; dynamics remain stable beyond training horizon T.  
- Empirical results: Shows consistent improvements over base Maxout CNN and prior unaugmented baselines on CIFAR-10/100; qualitative gating analyses are informative.  
- Missing baselines/ablations: Lacks comparisons to multi-pass without feedback, confidence-based ensembling, end-to-end/backprop or policy-gradient gate learning, larger-capacity baselines, and layer-wise ablations.  
- Practicality concerns: Computational overhead from multiple passes and SNES training noted but not quantified (no wall-clock, sample-efficiency, or latency/accuracy trade-offs reported).  
- Clarity: Paper is well written; action/observation and training algorithm clear, but exact dimensionalities, hyperparameters, and softmax-scaling rationale should be specified.  
- Error analysis: Needs more explicit per-class/confusion breakdowns and where feedback helps most.  
- Originality assessment: Components are known, but their combination at scale (feature-map gating via NES) is a novel, pragmatic contribution with moderate conceptual novelty.  
- Significance: Demonstrates learned feedback can improve strong baselines on CIFAR and motivates iterative/conditional computation, but broader impact needs larger-scale experiments.  
- Pros summary: Biologically motivated, practical interface to existing CNNs, demonstrated gains and stable multi-pass dynamics, class-relevant gate behavior.  
- Cons summary: Incremental novelty, limited to CIFAR, missing comparisons to simpler/differentiable alternatives, and unquantified SNES/compute costs.  
- Suggestions/questions: Provide layer-wise ablations, compare SNES to gradient-based/end-to-end training, report compute budgets and latency vs. passes, analyze failure cases and per-class gains, and explore spatial or hybrid attention and larger benchmarks.  
- Recommendation: Solid, well-executed paper with convincing CIFAR results; accept despite incremental novelty.
