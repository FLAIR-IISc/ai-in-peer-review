This paper augments a convolutional neural network with selective attention and proposes a training method for attention gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It tackles an important problem in computer vision: how to adaptively reconfigure deep processing when resources are limited and tasks vary. The classification results are strong, but the approach remains a black box—useful as a direction to pursue, yet not illuminating why it works. The representation analysis offers little insight, and the roles of the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are unclear. Despite this, the work moves beyond the prevailing paradigm of large, purely feed-forward networks with fixed parameters after training. The exposition is generally clear, though several equations appear to contain typos or require better explanation.

Major comments:
- Please explore the connection to dynamical systems more thoroughly. The method appears closer to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning. The reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic; training amounts to traversing the space of recurrent “policy” parameters theta to maximize the observed objective.
- The analysis using the cat image does not clarify how the new mechanisms aid recognition. Why are all layer-1 activations increased in the first iteration? Does global scaling affect the objective, or is the output invariant to it? A more focused analysis on a small image region, tracking the evolution of the local gating variables, could be informative. It would also help to examine challenging cases—images misclassified by the standard Maxout network but corrected by the proposed model—and to disentangle the impact of learned parameters (mean and variance of theta) by imposing additional constraints or ablations.

Minor comments:
- Algorithm 1: Where is h_M defined? Is it the step where observations are collected? Also, F[i] and Θ[i] appear inside the loop over images (j); are fitness values stored per image (e.g., in an array indexed by j) or overwritten each iteration?
- Equation 11: What is the summation index? Is Equation 8 defined per image and therefore should be indexed by j?
- Text after Equation 11: Please define the symbols x and d.
- It is unclear why explicit regularization of theta is necessary if theta is sampled from a prior that already encourages small values. Moreover, the final optimization step—gradient updates on parameters after sampling thetas and evaluating fitness—is insufficiently specified and theoretically unmotivated. How are the gradients computed? Are you effectively adjusting hyperparameters of a posterior over policies?

Overall assessment:
An interesting approach to constructing deep recurrent networks for recognition. The superior accuracy relative to standard baselines suggests that recurrent computation can help extract or focus on more complex image features. However, the current black-box treatment provides limited understanding of why the method is effective. Further analytical and methodological clarification would significantly strengthen the contribution.
