This work presents a convolutional network incorporating feedback connections for learning visual attention, implemented through gated filter activations. The model is trained using a reinforcement learning framework applied to a convolutional network base. While individual components aren’t groundbreaking, the overall approach is a strong and promising direction for research in this area. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset, providing further evidence supporting the potential of feedback and attention mechanisms in visual processing. The manuscript is well-written and clear. This is a valuable contribution demonstrating a compelling vision technique – convolutional networks enhanced with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention – and delivers impressive results. Future work could benefit from exploration of this approach on larger, more complex datasets such as ImageNet.
