- Proposes a novel CNN architecture integrating top-down attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100.  
- Explicitly models bidirectional information flow, combining bottom-up feature extraction with top-down attention refinement.  
- Conceptually fresh and well-motivated, notably using temporal evaluation and gated signal constraints to stabilize representation learning.  
- Analysis of attention policies gives valuable theoretical insight; accompanying visualizations effectively clarify attention dynamics.  
- Thorough CIFAR experiments and temporal evaluation demonstrate how feedback mechanisms iteratively refine features.  
- Main limitation: no validation on large-scale benchmarks (e.g., ImageNet), leaving scalability and real-world applicability unproven.  
- Lacks exploration of failure modes (ambiguous boundaries, domain shifts, occlusions); needs failure-case studies or targeted ablations.  
- Omits discussion and metrics for computational cost (inference time, memory) of the attention feedback loop, important for deployment.  
- Recommendation: add ImageNet (or scaled-down ImageNet) experiments or a detailed scaling discussion, plus failure analysis to strengthen impact.  
- Overall: promising, biologically plausible contribution with strong CIFAR results but requires key validations to realize broader significance.
