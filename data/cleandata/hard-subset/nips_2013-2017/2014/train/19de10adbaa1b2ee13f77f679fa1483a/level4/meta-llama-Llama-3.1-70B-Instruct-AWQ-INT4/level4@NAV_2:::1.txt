This paper introduces a novel approach to deep neural networks by incorporating internal selective attention through feedback connections, dubbed Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet). The authors draw inspiration from the human visual cortex, where feedback connections play a crucial role in object recognition. By allowing the network to iteratively focus its internal attention on specific features, dasNet achieves state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology. The introduction provides a thorough background on the limitations of traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and the benefits of incorporating feedback connections. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work in the field, highlighting the importance of feedback and attention in visual processing.

The technical soundness of the paper is evident in the authors' use of reinforcement learning to train the dasNet model. The application of Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to optimize the policy parameters is a novel approach that allows for efficient exploration of the large parameter space. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of dasNet in improving classification performance, particularly on difficult cases.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance technical detail with high-level insights. The authors provide a clear explanation of the dasNet architecture and its components, making it accessible to readers with a basic understanding of deep learning. At the same time, they delve into the technical aspects of the model, including the use of SNES and the policy parameterization.

The significance of the paper lies in its contribution to the field of computer vision. By demonstrating the effectiveness of dasNet on challenging datasets, the authors provide strong evidence for the importance of feedback and attention in visual processing. The paper's results have implications for future research in deep learning, particularly in the development of more robust and efficient models for image classification.

Some potential areas for improvement include exploring the application of dasNet to larger datasets, such as ImageNet, and investigating the use of more complex policies to control the attention mechanism. Additionally, the authors could provide more analysis on the dynamics of the dasNet model, including the behavior of the attention mechanism over time.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, demonstrating the effectiveness of dasNet in improving image classification performance. The authors' approach is well-motivated, and their results are well-supported by experimental evidence. The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the technical details, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to deep neural networks, incorporating internal selective attention through feedback connections.
* The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.
* The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology.
* The technical soundness of the paper is evident in the authors' use of reinforcement learning and SNES to optimize the policy parameters.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper may benefit from additional analysis on the dynamics of the dasNet model.
