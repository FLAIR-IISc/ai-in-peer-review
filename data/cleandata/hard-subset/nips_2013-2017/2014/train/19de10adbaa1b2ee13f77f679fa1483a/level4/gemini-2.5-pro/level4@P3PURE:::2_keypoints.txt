- Introduces a selective attention gating mechanism in a CNN and trains gating parameters to optimize classification.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited computation or multiple tasks.  
- Classification results are impressive but the method remains a "black box" with little explanation of why it works.  
- Analysis of learned image representations is not revealing; unclear how policy parameters (mean and variance of theta) affect behavior.  
- Represents an important step beyond fixed feed-forward networks toward recurrent/adaptive computation.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains typographical errors and unclear equations.  

Major points:
- The framework appears more related to dynamical systems / RNNs than to reinforcement learning and should be reframed accordingly.  
- Because reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic, training can be seen as traversing recurrent policy-parameter space to directly maximize the objective.  
- The presented analysis (e.g., the cat image) is not informative about how novel components aid recognition; observed layer-wide activation changes lack interpretation.  
- Recommend finer-grained analyses: track gating evolution in small image regions, inspect cases where the model corrects baseline errors, and constrain policy parameters during training to probe their effects.  

Minor points:
- Algorithm 1 notation is ambiguous: what is h_M, and are F[i] and Θ[i] overwritten or collected across images?  
- Equation 8 likely needs j indexing if it applies per image.  
- Equation 11 is missing the summation index.  
- Variables 'x' and 'd' are used without definition after Equation 11.  
- Motivation for regularizing theta is unclear given sampling from a prior; need clarification.  
- The final optimization step (updating policy parameters via gradients after sampling thetas) is poorly explained—how are gradients computed and what is the theoretical justification (e.g., relation to posterior hyperparameter updates)?  

- Overall: recurrent computation appears beneficial for recognition, but the paper fails to explain why the approach is effective.
