- Introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections and a learned RL policy to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities during inference.  
- Targets post-training adaptation to improve classification on challenging instances, inspired by recurrent biological vision.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 for unaugmented data.  
- Novelty: combines feedback in deep CNNs with SNES-based reinforcement learning to learn attentional policies.  
- Strong motivation linking limitations of static feedforward networks to human visual processing.  
- Generally well-written and logically organized, though some equations need clarification.  
- Major weakness: learned attentional policy is a “black box” with limited interpretability and analysis.  
- The method resembles recurrent/dynamical systems; the paper should explore this connection and theoretical framing more thoroughly.  
- Limited empirical analysis: example analyses (e.g., the cat image) are unconvincing and require deeper investigation (misclassifications, sensitivity to scalings, policy constraints).  
- Several technical details need clarification (Algorithm 1’s h_M, storage of F[i]/Θ[i], indexing in equations 8 and 11, definitions of x and d, and Equation 1 variables).  
- Regularization of θ is weakly justified; sampling from a prior and post-sampling updates lack clear theoretical grounding.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong benchmark improvements and promising research direction.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor interpretability, insufficient theoretical/dynamical-system analysis, and multiple minor technical ambiguities.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify technical details and expand analysis and interpretability of the learned policies.
