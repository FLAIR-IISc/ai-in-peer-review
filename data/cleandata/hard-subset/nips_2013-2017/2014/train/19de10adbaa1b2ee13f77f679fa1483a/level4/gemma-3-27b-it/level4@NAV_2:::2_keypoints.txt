- Introduces dasNet: a deep CNN with internal selective attention via feedback and gating parameters trained by reinforcement learning.  
- Addresses adapting deep processing to handle diverse vision tasks and shows improved CIFAR-10/100 performance.  
- Results are promising but the mechanisms driving improvements are opaque and require deeper analysis.  
- Exposition is generally clear, but some equations need clarification and contain typographical errors.  
- Major suggestion: explore connections to dynamical systems and recurrent network interpretations for theoretical insight.  
- The deterministic policy and immediate reward signal suggest training navigates a recurrent policy parameter space.  
- The cat image example analysis is not sufficiently informative and raises concerns about sensitivity to global scaling.  
- Recommend focused analyses: inspect smaller image regions, track gating evolution, and study cases corrected after initial misclassification.  
- Suggest systematic perturbation experiments (e.g., constrain mean/variance of θ) to probe robustness and mechanisms.  
- Algorithm 1: function h(M) is undefined and should be clarified (observation step?).  
- Algorithm 1: clarify whether accumulated F[i] and Θ[i] are summed or overwritten per image loop.  
- Equation 11: specify the range/index of the summation; Equation 8 likely needs an image index ‘j’.  
- Define variables ‘x’ and ‘d’ following Equation 11.  
- Clarify need for regularizing θ given sampling from a prior that biases smaller values.  
- Explain how gradients are computed after sampling and fitness evaluation and provide theoretical justification (e.g., posterior over policies).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel integration of recurrence/attention into CNNs, demonstrated CIFAR improvements, tackles adaptive feature selection, generally clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of theoretical understanding, limited analysis of learned dynamics, ambiguities in algorithm/equations, RL connection appears tenuous.  
- Overall: technically sound with supported experimental claims but requires more analysis and clarification to fully explain why it works.
