- Proposes dasNet: internal selective attention via feedback that dynamically alters convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Trains attention gating parameters with reinforcement learning using scalable natural evolution strategies (SNES).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented datasets).  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explains CNN limitations and benefits of feedback connections.  
- Choice of SNES is interesting and the algorithm and its advantages are thoroughly explained.  
- Experimental results are impressive and include a detailed per-image analysis illustrating the attentional mechanism.  
- Needs deeper analysis of learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) and their role in attention.  
- Connection to dynamical systems and relationship to recurrent networks is underexplored and should be expanded.  
- The reinforcement learning reward function is not fully justified and requires more explanation.  
- Minor clarity issues: explicitly define function h_M and clarify the roles of F[i] and Θ[i] in the algorithm loop.  
- Equation 11 needs clarification (specify the sum) and variables 'x' and 'd' should be explicitly defined.  
- Regularization of θ should be theoretically justified and gradient computations in the optimization step explained in more detail.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound and novel; could be stronger with additional analysis and clarifications.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
