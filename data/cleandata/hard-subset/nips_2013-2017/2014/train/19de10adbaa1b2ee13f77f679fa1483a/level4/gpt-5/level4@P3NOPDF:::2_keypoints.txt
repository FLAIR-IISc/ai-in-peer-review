- Paper augments a convolutional network with learned gating for selective attention and trains gating parameters to improve classification; empirical results are strong but the method is presented as a black box.
- Strength: tackles adaptive computation in deep networks, showing impressive gains over strong feed-forward baselines.
- Strength: clear high-level narrative and connection to selective attention.
- Strength: potentially impactful toward deep recurrent networks for recognition.
- Weakness: limited interpretability—little insight into what learned gates do or why performance improves.
- Weakness: RL framing seems misaligned; setup appears closer to a deterministic recurrent/dynamical system with immediate reward.
- Weakness: key training details (gradient estimation, regularization, dataset objective) are underspecified.
- Weakness: analysis examples (e.g., cat image) are unconvincing and fail to reveal the attention mechanism’s contribution.
- Major: clarify framing—explain why RL is necessary versus viewing the method as a recurrent dynamical system optimizing parameters via BPTT or other optimizers.
- Major: precisely describe gradient estimation and the final optimization step—reparameterization, likelihood-ratio/REINFORCE, or gradient-free method.
- Major: state theoretically what is being optimized—hyperparameters of a policy posterior or expected supervised loss under a parametric distribution over θ.
- Major: justify the role of regularization and priors on θ, show sensitivity to prior mean/variance, and provide ablative results removing the regularizer.
- Major: improve interpretability—provide localized gate trajectories, spatial/feature-wise visualizations, and analyses of cases corrected by the model versus baseline.
- Major: probe learned distribution parameters (mean/variance of θ) by constraining/fixing them and reporting effects on accuracy and attention patterns.
- Major: report performance vs. number of recurrent steps and compute budget (FLOPs/wall-clock), and compare deterministic vs. stochastic gating at train/test.
- Major: clarify parameter counts and inference-time overhead relative to baselines for fair comparison.
- Minor: fix Algorithm 1 notation—define h_M, clarify indexing for F[i], Θ[i], and whether fitness values are stored per image or overwritten.
- Minor: index Eq. 8/11 properly (e.g., per image j, sum over images j and/or time t) and state dataset vs. minibatch optimization.
- Minor: define x and d where first introduced (inputs/images and desired labels).
- Minor: provide exact regularization penalty term and coefficient and explain interaction with the prior over θ.
- Minor: correct notation/typos and align symbols across text, pseudocode, and equations for readability.
- Overall: promising and compelling approach, but needs clearer dynamical-systems or RL justification, precise gradient/regularization documentation, and targeted analyses/ablations to move beyond a black-box result.
