This paper presents a convolutional neural network (convnet) with feedback connections to learn visual attention. The feedback mechanism is implemented by gating the activation of certain filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of a convnet. While no individual component is particularly novel, the approach taken is, in my opinion, a promising direction and the right way to proceed. As expected, the authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which significantly confirms the effectiveness of incorporating feedback and attention mechanisms. The quality and clarity of the paper are good. Overall, the approach of using convnets with RL-driven feedback attention for vision tasks is very good and yields impressive results. If possible, I suggest that the authors consider applying their method to more challenging problems, such as ImageNet, to further demonstrate its capabilities.
