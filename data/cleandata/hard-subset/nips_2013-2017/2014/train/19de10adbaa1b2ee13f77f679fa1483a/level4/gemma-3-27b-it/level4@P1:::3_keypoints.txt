- Proposes dasNet: a deep CNN with top-down, attention-based feedback achieving state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
- Novel, forward-looking integration of bottom-up and top-down signals with temporal evaluation and gated representations.
- Clear method explanation and helpful visualizations that illuminate model behavior.
- Performance heavily depends on the learned attention policy; lacks analysis of failure cases and limitations.
- Primary concern: no evaluation on large-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet), leaving scalability and computational cost unaddressed.
- Experiments are well-presented but do not demonstrate applicability to larger images or more complex scenes.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel top-down feedback, SOTA CIFAR results, clear exposition and visualizations, tackles recurrent processing in deep learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing ImageNet-scale evaluation and limited discussion of potential failure modes.
