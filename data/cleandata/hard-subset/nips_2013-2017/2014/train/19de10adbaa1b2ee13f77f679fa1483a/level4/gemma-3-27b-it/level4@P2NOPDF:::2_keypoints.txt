- Introduces selective attention in CNNs with a novel method for learning gating parameters to optimize classification.
- Addresses adaptive configuration of deep processing under resource constraints and task variability.
- Reports compelling classification results that exceed standard methods.
- Method is largely opaque: paper lacks insight into why the approach works.
- Analysis of image representations is unrevealing; the role of learned parameters (theta mean and variance) is unclear.
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation errors and needs clarification in places.
- Major: investigate connection to dynamical systems and recurrent networks—method resembles RNNs more than Reinforcement Learning given immediate reward and deterministic policy.
- Major: training may amount to navigating recurrent “policy” parameter space (theta) to maximize the objective; authors should analyze this perspective.
- Major: analysis of the cat image fails to explain how attention improves recognition; unexplained initial increase in layer-1 activations and possible invariance to global scaling.
- Major: recommend focused analyses—track gating variables in a small image region, study hard cases misclassified by baseline but corrected by the model, and systematically probe effects of theta mean/variance (e.g., via constraints).
- Minor: Algorithm 1 missing definition of h_M and unclear roles of F[i] and Θ[i] in the image loop (accumulation vs. overwrite).
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation is undefined; indexing of Equation 8 relative to images is unclear.
- Minor: Symbols ‘x’ and ‘d’ following Equation 11 are not explained.
- Minor: Regularization of theta is not justified (theta sampled from a prior); final gradient update step after sampling lacks explanation and theoretical grounding (how gradients are computed and what is being optimized).
- Summary: promising, recurrent attention-based approach with improved accuracy, but poor interpretability and incomplete analysis hinder understanding of why it succeeds.
