- Introduces dasNet: adds learned top-down feedback to CNNs to modulate feature maps over multiple passes for selective internal attention.  
- Policy network observes activation summaries and class probs, outputting gating actions that scale convolutional filter maps.  
- Policy trained post hoc with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) due to high-dimensional action/observation and parameter spaces.  
- Built on Maxout networks with dropout and achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100, especially on commonly confused classes.  
- Novel integration of high-capacity CNNs with learned feedback distinguishes it from prior recurrent/attention models.  
- Well-specified action/observation design, gating mechanism, and multi-pass inference; softmax-normalized gates maintain activation stability.  
- Empirical gains are consistent and analyses show the policy can correct initial misclassifications and produce stable dynamics across steps.  
- Visualizations of map emphasis/de-emphasis enhance interpretability of how feedback alters representations.  
- Positions feedback as a biologically inspired, general tool to improve CNN robustness and iterative refinement.  
- Limited to CIFAR-10/100 — lacks large-scale evaluation (e.g., ILSVRC) to demonstrate scalability and practical utility.  
- Multi-pass inference plus a policy network adds computational and latency overhead; inference/training runtime costs are not reported.  
- Missing targeted ablations to identify critical observation components, sensitivity to T/gating normalization, and policy architecture choices.  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes, including cases where feedback degrades performance or robustness to adversarial/OOD inputs.  
- Comparisons are limited; broader baselines including augmentation/committee methods or augmentation experiments would improve context.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and promising, but requires large-scale validation, runtime profiling, ablations, and failure analyses to strengthen claims.
