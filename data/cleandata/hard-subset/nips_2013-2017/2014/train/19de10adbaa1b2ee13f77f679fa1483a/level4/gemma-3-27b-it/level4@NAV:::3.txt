The authors present a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture integrating top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. Performance on CIFAR datasets is reported as state-of-the-art.

This work is a compelling and novel contribution to the field, both in its model design and application to computer vision. The consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signaling pathways for CNN training is a particularly forward-looking aspect. I found the evaluation of the model’s dynamics over time, and the use of gated signals to regulate representation learning, to be especially insightful. Given the reliance of learned representations on the attention policy, exploring and discussing potential failure modes of the algorithm would strengthen the paper. 

The experimental analysis is well-executed and includes visualizations that aid understanding. However, given the increased complexity relative to standard feedforward CNNs, a key improvement would be to demonstrate scalability with results on a larger, more challenging dataset such as ILSVRC. This would be my primary concern. Overall, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs results in a promising model deserving of further investigation, and practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be highly valuable.
