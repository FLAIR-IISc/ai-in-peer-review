- Introduces dasNet: feedback-enabled CNNs with an RL-learned policy that modulates filter activations across multiple inference passes.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to evolve a high-dimensional policy controlling filter emphasis without changing pre-trained CNN weights.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 with ~6% relative improvement by iteratively refining attention to correct hard examples.  
- Draws biological inspiration from cortical top-down feedback (Felleman & Essen, 1991) and builds on prior RL-for-vision work.  
- Technical execution is robust: SNES motivation, clear ablation studies, and layer-specific filter dynamics effectively shown (Fig. 3).  
- Experimental design isolates the attention mechanism’s impact, improving ambiguous cases without degrading easy-example performance (Table 1).  
- Regularization, boosting terms, and computational feasibility (4-day training on GTX 560 Ti) are appropriately addressed.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized; key equations, Algorithm 1, and figures provide intuitive understanding.  
- Originality: novel application of SNES to learn filter-level attention policies in pre-trained CNNs, enabling post-training adaptability.  
- Significance: shows feedback-driven attention is practically effective and broadly applicable to enhance existing CNNs.  
- Main weakness: evaluation limited to small datasets (CIFAR-10/100); scalability to ImageNet or detection tasks is not demonstrated.  
- Revision should better emphasize why SNES was chosen over standard RL and correct the draft’s understatement of the novelty.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revision to address scalability concerns and clarify SNES rationale.
