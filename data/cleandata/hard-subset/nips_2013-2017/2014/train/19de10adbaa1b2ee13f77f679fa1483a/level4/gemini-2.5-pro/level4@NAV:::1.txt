This paper presents a Convolutional Neural Network that incorporates feedback connections to learn a visual attention mechanism. The feedback is implemented by gating the activations of specific convolutional filters, and this attention policy is trained using a reinforcement learning framework.

While the individual components of the model are not fundamentally novel, their synthesis and the overall approach are compelling and represent a promising direction for the field. The effectiveness of this method is demonstrated by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR benchmark. This is a significant finding, as it provides strong empirical evidence that feedback and attention mechanisms are a valuable path for advancing computer vision models.

The quality of the paper and the clarity of the presentation are both high. In summary, this work details a well-executed approach to vision, successfully integrating ConvNets with an RL-driven feedback attention mechanism to yield impressive results. As a direction for future work, the authors are encouraged to investigate the performance and scalability of their model on more complex, large-scale datasets such as ImageNet.
