The authors propose a CNN that incorporates top-down processing via attention-based feedback, and report state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR datasets.

This is an engaging paper with clear novelty in both model design and its application to vision. It is forward-looking in jointly leveraging bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN. I particularly appreciate the temporal evaluation of the model and the use of gated signals to constrain representations. Because the quality of the learned representations depends critically on the attention policy, it would be helpful if the authors could suggest or demonstrate scenarios in which the algorithm might fail, or discuss its sensitivity to suboptimal attention policies.

The experiments are interesting and the visualizations aid understanding. However, given that the method is more complex than a standard feedforward CNN, the paper would be significantly strengthened by results on ILSVRC, to convincingly demonstrate its scalability to larger datasets with higher-resolution images. This is my main (and possibly only) concern.

Overall, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs yields a compelling model that merits further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger-scale datasets would be especially valuable.
