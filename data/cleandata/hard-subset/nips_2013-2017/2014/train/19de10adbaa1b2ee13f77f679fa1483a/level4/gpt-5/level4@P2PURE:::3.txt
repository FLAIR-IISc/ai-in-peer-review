The authors propose a CNN that integrates top-down processing via attention-based feedback. The results on the CIFAR datasets show state-of-the-art performance.

Overall, the paper is engaging and presents novelty in both model design and its application to vision. It is also forward-looking to incorporate both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN. I particularly appreciate the evaluation across time and the use of a gated signal to constrain the representation. Because the quality of the learned representations depends on the attention policy, it would be more informative if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail.

The experiments are interesting, and the model is examined in several useful ways, including visualizations that aid interpretation. However, given that the method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, the paper would be stronger with results on ILSVRC to convincingly demonstrate its scalability to a larger dataset with higher-resolution images. That is my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and a CNN is compelling and merits further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferred.
