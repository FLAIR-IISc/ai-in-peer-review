

The authors propose a CNN integrating top-down processing through attention-based feedback mechanisms. Their model achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR datasets.  

This paper presents a compelling contribution with novel model design and application for vision tasks, effectively incorporating both bottom-up and top-down signals during training. The evaluation across temporal dimensions using gated signals to constrain representations is particularly noteworthy. Given that representation quality depends on the attention policy, the authors would strengthen their work by providing specific failure cases or failure analysis.  

The experimental section is well-executed, featuring insightful visualizations to elucidate model behavior. However, as the method introduces greater complexity than standard feedforward CNNs, the paper would be significantly strengthened by including results on ILSVRC to demonstrate scalability to larger datasets with higher-resolution images. This represents the primary (and potentially sole) concern. The integration of attention-based feedback with CNNs yields an intriguing architecture that warrants further investigation; practical insights derived from larger-scale experiments would be highly valuable.
