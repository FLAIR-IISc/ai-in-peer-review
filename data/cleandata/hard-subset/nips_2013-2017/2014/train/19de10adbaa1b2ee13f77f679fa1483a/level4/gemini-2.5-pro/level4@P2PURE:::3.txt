This paper introduces a novel CNN architecture that incorporates top-down processing via an attention-based feedback mechanism. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR datasets.

The paper is interesting and demonstrates novelty in its model design and application to computer vision. The approach of combining bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN is forward-looking. A particular strength is the evaluation of the model over time, along with the use of a gated signal to constrain the learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations hinges on the attention policy, the paper would be significantly strengthened by an analysis or demonstration of the algorithm's potential failure modes.

The experimental section is solid, featuring insightful visualizations that aid in understanding the model's behavior. However, the primary concern is the model's scalability. As the proposed method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, its evaluation on only the CIFAR datasets is a limitation. To convincingly demonstrate the method's practical viability and scalability, experiments on a large-scale, high-resolution dataset such as ILSVRC are necessary. Despite this concern, the combination of attention-based feedback with a CNN is an interesting direction worthy of further study, but the paper would be more impactful with practical insights derived from experiments on larger datasets.
