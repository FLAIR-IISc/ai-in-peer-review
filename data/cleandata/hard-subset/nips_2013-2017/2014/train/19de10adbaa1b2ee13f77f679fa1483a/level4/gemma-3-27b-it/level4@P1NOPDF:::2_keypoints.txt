- Introduces selective attention gating in a CNN and trains gating parameters to optimize classification performance.  
- Tackles adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and varying tasks.  
- Achieves impressive classification results but the method largely remains a black box—lacks explanation of why it works.  
- Analysis of image representations is not insightful; learned parameters (mean/variance of θ) lack clear interpretation.  
- Represents an important step beyond fixed, feed-forward networks by incorporating adaptive/recurrent computation.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos and sections needing further explanation.  
- Major recommendation: explore connection to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than framing as Reinforcement Learning.  
- Note that the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic, so training traverses recurrent policy parameters θ.  
- Criticism of the cat-image analysis: it does not clarify how model features contribute to recognition (e.g., why layer-1 activations rise).  
- Suggest analyzing a smaller image region and tracking gating-variable evolution to reveal mechanisms.  
- Recommend studying hard cases (misclassified by Maxout but corrected by this model) to understand gains.  
- Suggest probing or constraining learned θ mean/variance to interpret their role.  
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: undefined h_M, ambiguous observation-collection step, and unclear handling of F[i] and Θ[i] (accumulation vs overwrite).  
- Equation 11 and surrounding text are unclear: unspecified summation, possible missing index j for Equation 8, and undefined variables ‘x’ and ‘d’.  
- Question necessity of θ regularization given prior sampling, and unclear explanation/theoretical justification for final gradient-update optimization step.
