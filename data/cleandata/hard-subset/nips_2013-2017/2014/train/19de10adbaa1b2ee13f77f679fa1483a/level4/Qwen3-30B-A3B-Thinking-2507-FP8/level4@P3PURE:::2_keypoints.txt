- Introduces selective attention in CNNs by training gating parameters to optimize classification, enabling adaptive reconfiguration under resource constraints.  
- Classification results are strong, but the method functions largely as a black box with insufficient explanation of why it works.  
- Analysis of image representations is uninformative; the roles of learned parameters (mean/variance of θ) remain unclear.  
- Represents an important advance beyond large, fixed-parameter feedforward networks.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains typographical errors and some equations need better explanation.  
- Major: The paper should rigorously explore the connection to dynamical systems—method resembles recurrent networks more than reinforcement learning given immediate, deterministic rewards.  
- Major: Training can be viewed as traversing recurrent “policy” parameter space θ to maximize an objective; this perspective requires deeper discussion.  
- Major: The presented cat-image analysis is unhelpful—specifically, why do layer-1 activations uniformly increase at the first iteration?  
- Major: It is unclear whether the observed global scaling of activations affects the objective or whether outputs are invariant to such scaling.  
- Major: Recommend analyzing a smaller image region and the local evolution of gating variables to gain insight.  
- Major: Recommend examining misclassified cases (e.g., corrected by the proposed model but not baseline) and constraining mean/variance of θ to isolate their effects.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 needs definitions (e.g., h_M) and clarification on whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated or overwritten per image j.  
- Minor: Equation 11 lacks a specified summation index; Equation 8 should likely be indexed by image j.  
- Minor: Clarify definitions of variables x and d after Equation 11.  
- Minor: Theta regularization is not justified—θ is sampled from a prior that already biases toward small values; the final gradient update after sampling lacks theoretical justification and a clear gradient-computation explanation.
