- Proposes dasNet: feedback-based selective attention for CNNs that iteratively refines classifications by modulating filter sensitivities using a learned policy (SNES).
- Trains a policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to modulate convolutional filter weights across multiple passes, enabling the network to "rethink" its output.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on CIFAR-10/100 (≈6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs, new SOTA for unaugmented data).
- Builds on Maxout networks and connects to reinforcement-learning style policy learning and biological recurrent/top-down visual processing.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough (ablation on number of passes, dynamics analysis, policy information verification) with clear exposition and helpful figures.
- Major weakness: paper lacks interpretability and does not explain why or how the attention mechanism improves classification (e.g., cat-dog example gives no meaningful insight).
- Technical ambiguities undermine credibility: undefined observation function h(M) in Algorithm 1, inconsistent fitness handling (F[i], Θ[i]), and unclear notation in Equation 11 (undefined x and d).
- Regularization for θ (λL2∥θ∥2) is unjustified given the sampling scheme, and the SNES implementation/theoretical basis and natural gradient updates are not explained.
- Reviewer recommends reframing the approach as a dynamical/recurrent processing system rather than standard reinforcement learning due to deterministic policy and immediate rewards.
- Suggested fixes: rigorously analyze spatial/local activation changes and their link to classification, clarify θ/µ/Σ relationship, define h(M) and fitness, justify regularization and optimization, and discuss dynamical-systems theory.
- Overall: promising and empirically strong contribution but insufficient theoretical/analytical explanation; needs substantial revisions before acceptance.
