The authors propose a novel convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that incorporates top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets. This paper is engaging to read and showcases innovation in model design and application for computer vision problems. The consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN is forward-thinking, and the evaluation of the model over time using gated signals to constrain the representation is particularly noteworthy.

The quality of the representations learned hinges on the attention policy, making it intriguing to explore cases where the algorithm may fail. While the experiments are compelling, with the authors providing various visualizations to facilitate understanding, the method's complexity compared to traditional feedforward CNNs warrants additional evaluation. Specifically, results on the ILSVRC dataset would be beneficial in demonstrating the model's scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is my primary concern, as the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs presents an interesting model worthy of further investigation.

The paper's strengths include its originality, as it introduces a new approach to CNN design, and its significance, as it achieves state-of-the-art performance on challenging datasets. The writing is generally clear, although some sections could benefit from more detailed explanations. The use of visualizations and supplementary material enhances the reader's understanding of the model and its performance.

However, the paper could be improved by addressing the following points:

* Provide more in-depth analysis of the attention policy's impact on the model's performance and potential failure cases.
* Include results on larger datasets, such as ILSVRC, to demonstrate the model's scalability.
* Offer more detailed explanations of the model's components, particularly the attention mechanism and the reinforcement learning approach.
* Discuss the computational requirements and potential limitations of the model.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and promising approach to CNN design, and with some additional evaluation and analysis, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* Originality: The paper introduces a new approach to CNN design, incorporating top-down processing and attention-based feedback.
* Significance: The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on challenging datasets.
* Clarity: The writing is generally clear, and the use of visualizations and supplementary material enhances the reader's understanding.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* Limited evaluation: The paper could benefit from additional evaluation on larger datasets to demonstrate the model's scalability.
* Complexity: The method's complexity compared to traditional feedforward CNNs may limit its applicability.
* Lack of analysis: The paper could benefit from more in-depth analysis of the attention policy's impact on the model's performance and potential failure cases.
