The authors propose a CNN that incorporates a form of top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback, yielding state-of-the-art performances on the CIFAR datasets.

This paper is generally engaging to read and showcases novelty in terms of model design and application to vision problems. Its forward-thinking approach to combining both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN is particularly noteworthy. I appreciate the aspect of evaluating the model over time and using gated signals to constrain the representation. However, since the quality of the learned representations heavily relies on the attention policy, it would be more insightful if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail.

The experiments are well-designed, with the authors providing various demonstrations of the model, including visualizations to facilitate better understanding. Nevertheless, given that the method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I believe the paper would benefit from results on ILSVRC to truly demonstrate its scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs presents an intriguing model worthy of further investigation. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferable.
