- Introduces selective attention gating in CNNs and a training method for gating parameters to improve classification under resource constraints.  
- Tackles adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing stages for diverse tasks, a significant computer vision challenge.  
- Classification results are strong, indicating recurrent computation may enhance feature extraction or attention.  
- Methodology is opaque: paper does not explain why the approach succeeds or how learned mechanisms work.  
- Analysis of image representations is weak and lacks insight into what features are learned.  
- Role and interpretation of learned θ parameters (mean and variance) are unclear and not analyzed.  
- Should frame the approach as related to dynamical/recurrent systems rather than Reinforcement Learning, since rewards are immediate and policies deterministic.  
- Need rigorous exploration of the recurrent/dynamical systems connection and optimization over θ as recurrent policy parameters.  
- Cat image analysis fails to show how novel features improve recognition; global layer-1 activation scaling is unexplained.  
- Recommend focusing on localized image regions, tracking gating variable evolution, and testing output invariance to global scaling.  
- Suggest analyzing misclassified cases where baseline fails but the model succeeds, and performing constrained experiments isolating θ’s impact.  
- Algorithm 1 needs clarifications: define h_M and whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored or overwritten during the image loop.  
- Equation 11 and surrounding text need indexing and definitions: specify summation index and define variables x and d.  
- θ regularization rationale is unclear given prior sampling; the final gradient updates on θ lack theoretical justification and a clear gradient computation explanation.  
- Overall: meaningful advancement beyond fixed feed-forward networks with clear exposition, but typographical errors in equations and insufficient theoretical/empirical analysis limit understanding.
