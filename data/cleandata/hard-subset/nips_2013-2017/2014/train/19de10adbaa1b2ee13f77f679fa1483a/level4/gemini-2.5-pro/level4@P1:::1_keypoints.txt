1. Introduces dasNet: feedback connections that iteratively modulate convolutional filter activations to implement internal selective attention.  
2. Trains the attentional control policy with reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) on top of a pre-trained Maxout CNN.  
3. Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, improving classification of ambiguous images.  
4. Significance: well-motivated integration of top-down attentional processing into deep CNNs, inspired by human vision.  
5. Originality: novel and effective synthesis of CNNs, attention, and RL; SNES is a practical choice for the large policy space.  
6. Experimental quality: strong baseline, clear performance gains, analyses of policy dynamics, and evidence that gates carry class-relevant information.  
7. Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and includes useful algorithmic descriptions and visualizations.  
8. Scalability concern: evaluation limited to small CIFAR datasets, leaving effectiveness on large-scale sets (e.g., ImageNet) uncertain.  
9. Computational cost: RL policy training is expensive (≈ four days on a GPU), posing a barrier to scalability and adoption.  
10. Interpretability: limited qualitative insight into learned attentional strategies and how gates correct specific inter-class confusions.  
11. Overall: solid, well-motivated contribution showing benefits of feedback attention in CNNs, with open questions on scale and interpretability.
