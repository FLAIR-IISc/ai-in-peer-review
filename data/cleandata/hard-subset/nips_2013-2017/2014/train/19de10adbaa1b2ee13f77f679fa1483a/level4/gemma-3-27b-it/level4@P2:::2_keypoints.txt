- Introduces feedback-based selective attention in CNNs with trained gating parameters to improve classification.
- Tackles adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing for limited resources and diverse tasks.
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, showing tangible effectiveness.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to scale training of many policy parameters.
- Generally clear exposition, though some equations need clarification or correction.
- Learned attention mechanism is a “black box”; the paper lacks insight into why the method works.
- Analysis of image representations is superficial and does not explain observed activation changes.
- Mean and variance of the policy distribution and other learned parameters remain poorly understood.
- Connection to recurrent neural networks and dynamical systems is noted but under-explored.
- Reward structure and deterministic policy implications for optimization dynamics are not analyzed.
- Several equations and algorithm steps lack context or contain minor inconsistencies (e.g., Equation 8, Equation 11).
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: h_M undefined, use of F[i] and Θ[i] inside loops ambiguous, fitness accumulation unclear.
- Variables ‘x’ and ‘d’ after Equation 11 are undefined, harming reproducibility.
- SNES gradient computation and the theoretical basis for updating SNES parameters are not explained.
- Regularization of policy parameters (θ) is insufficiently justified given prior sampling already biases toward small values.
- Related work section is comprehensive but fails to sufficiently link to RNN/attention literature.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, improved accuracy, addresses CNN adaptability, scalable training.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of interpretability, weak analysis, under-explored related connections, and clarity issues.
- Overall: promising and novel contribution but requires deeper analysis, better interpretability, and clearer technical details.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing interpretability, analysis, related-work connections, and equation/algorithm clarity.
