- Introduces a novel CNN architecture that integrates top-down processing via an attention-based feedback mechanism.
- Core contribution: combining bottom-up and top-down signals for training CNNs is conceptually significant and forward-looking.
- Specific architectural choices (gated signal, multi-time-step evaluation) are well-motivated and commendable.
- Experiments are insightful and visualizations help explain internal dynamics and attention behavior.
- Primary concern: experimental validation is limited to CIFAR despite the model's greater complexity than standard feedforward CNNs.
- Need for scalability evaluation on larger, more complex datasets (e.g., ILSVRC) to demonstrate practical utility and general applicability.
- Paper should include a discussion of limitations and analyses of potential failure cases, given dependence on the attention policy.
- Overall: promising and interesting work that would be significantly strengthened by large-scale evaluation and explicit robustness/limitation analysis.
