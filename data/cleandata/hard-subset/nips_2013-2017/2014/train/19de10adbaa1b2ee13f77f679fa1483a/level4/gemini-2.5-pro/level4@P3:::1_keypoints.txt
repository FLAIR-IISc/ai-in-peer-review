- Proposes dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback modulatory gates that iteratively refine classifications over multiple passes.  
- Uses a two-stage training: supervised training of a base Maxout CNN, then reinforcement learning (policy network) to set filter gates.  
- Employs Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to optimize a very high-dimensional policy (over a million parameters).  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 and corrects initial misclassifications on hard examples.  
- Strength: advances a more dynamic, biologically plausible vision model showing practical benefits of feedback/attention.  
- Strength: original synthesis of CNNs, feedback, and RL with a clever black-box optimization for high-dimensional control.  
- Strength: solid technical execution, clear methodology, convincing experiments, and useful analyses/visualizations.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly motivated, with a good connection to neuroscience and vision literature.  
- Weakness: unclear scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) given growing action space and heavy compute costs.  
- Weakness: computational cost—training the attentional policy required four days on a GPU in addition to base training.  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies—sensitivity to observation vector components, comparisons across different numbers of passes (T), and role of λ_boost are not fully explored.  
- Weakness: interpretability remains limited; it’s unclear why the policy emphasizes specific filters for particular ambiguous images.  
- Final assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with solid empirical support; recommended for acceptance.  
- Suggestion: add discussion of scalability and potential solutions (e.g., policy parameter sharing, spatial-region attention) for larger-scale problems.
