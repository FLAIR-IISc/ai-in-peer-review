- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that learn selective internal attention.  
- Core idea: modulate convolutional filter activations via learned gating to iteratively refine processing.  
- Attentional policy learned with reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Combines pre-trained Maxout convolutional network with SNES-driven feedback; components individually familiar but combination is sound.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (on unaugmented data).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the work within relevant literature.  
- Methodology and experiments are described in sufficient detail for expert reproducibility; SNES implementation details are in the supplement.  
- Originality stems from integrating SNES-driven feedback with a Maxout network and modulating internal filters rather than controlling gaze/actions.  
- Use of SNES is pragmatic for handling the high-dimensional policy space of large networks.  
- Significance: demonstrates learned feedback/attention can improve visual recognition and iterative refinement helps ambiguous cases.  
- Visualization (Figure 3) provides preliminary insight into filter activation changes during iterations.  
- Dynamics analysis (Figure 2) suggests learned attention patterns generalize beyond training iterations.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong empirical results and clear, well-motivated approach combining RL and feedback.  
- Argument for acceptance: clear writing and demonstration of iterative internal attention’s potential.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited evaluation scope—needs experiments on larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Argument against acceptance: analysis of learned attention policies is limited; deeper understanding needed.  
- Argument against acceptance: the link to biological feedback in visual cortex could be more explicitly justified.  
- Overall judgment: solid, well-executed contribution; recommends acceptance.
