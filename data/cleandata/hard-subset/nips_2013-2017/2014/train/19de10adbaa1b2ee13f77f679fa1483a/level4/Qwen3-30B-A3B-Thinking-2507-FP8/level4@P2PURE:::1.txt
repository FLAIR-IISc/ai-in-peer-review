

This paper introduces a convolutional neural network (CNN) enhanced with feedback connections for learning visual attention, implemented through filter gating. The model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of a standard CNN architecture. While no individual component is novel, the overall approach represents a strong and well-executed methodology that aligns with the most promising direction for attention mechanisms in vision. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which effectively validates the efficacy of feedback-driven attention as a critical next step in vision research. The quality of presentation and clarity of the work are excellent. This constitutes a compelling approach to vision with robust empirical results: CNNs leveraging reinforcement learning for feedback-driven attention. If not already addressed, the authors should consider extending the evaluation to larger-scale benchmarks such as ImageNet to further substantiate the approach's generalizability.
