This paper presents a convolutional neural network with feedback connections designed to learn visual attention. The feedback mechanism is implemented through gating the activations of selected filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of the convolutional network. While no single component of this work is particularly novel, the overall approach is strong and represents a promising direction for research. As demonstrated, the method achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which provides significant support for the potential of feedback and attention mechanisms in computer vision. The quality of the writing and the clarity of the presentation are both good. In summary, this is a well-executed approach to visual attention using reinforcement learning to guide feedback within a convolutional network, yielding compelling results. The authors are encouraged to explore the scalability of this method to larger and more complex datasets, such as ImageNet, if they have not already done so.
