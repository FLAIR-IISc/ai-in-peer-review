

This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) augmented with feedback connections to model visual attention, implemented through gating the activation of specific filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning (RL) on top of a standard CNN architecture. While no single component of the approach is highly novel, the integration of RL-driven feedback for attention mechanisms represents a strong and well-motivated direction for vision research. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10, which serves as significant empirical validation that feedback-based attention is a promising path forward for visual perception tasks. The quality of presentation and technical clarity are excellent, and the results effectively support the core thesis. The work presents a compelling case for attention-driven feedback in CNNs, achieving robust performance with a principled implementation. As a next step, the authors should consider extending the evaluation to larger-scale benchmarks such as ImageNet, if not already explored in their current framework.
