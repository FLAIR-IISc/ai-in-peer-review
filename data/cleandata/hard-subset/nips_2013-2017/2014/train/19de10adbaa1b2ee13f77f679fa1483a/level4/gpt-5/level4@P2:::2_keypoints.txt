- Summary: Adds iterative, feedback-controlled multiplicative gates to a Maxout CNN, trained with SNES after supervised pretraining, improving CIFAR-10/100 performance.  
- Quality: Method is technically sound and empirically effective on CIFAR-10/100 with a clear two-stage training protocol.  
- Quality: Paper is largely a black box—limited insight into why sequential gating helps or what gates represent.  
- Quality: RL framing is superficial; the approach resembles direct search for recurrent parameters rather than classical delayed-reward RL.  
- Clarity: Exposition is generally clear but some equations and algorithmic details are ambiguous or contain typos.  
- Clarity: The rationale for L2 regularization on θ given SNES’s distributional prior needs clarification.  
- Originality: Modulating internal CNN activations via learned top-down signals is timely and distinguishes the work from feedforward-only models.  
- Originality: Using SNES to optimize a million-parameter attention policy inside a CNN is an original combination.  
- Significance: Iterative feedback shows measurable gains on CIFAR-10/100 and can help ambiguous cases.  
- Significance: If made interpretable, the approach could influence integrating feedback/attention into CNNs.  
- Major: Recast method as a dynamical/recurrent system (Mt+1 = f(Mt, a(ot))) and discuss relation to unrolled BPTT vs. black-box search.  
- Major: Improve interpretability—analyze spatial/temporal gate evolution, study corrected-but-previously-misclassified images, and test behavior beyond trained horizon T.  
- Major: Probe policy distribution (mean/variance) effects via ablations or freezing to understand their roles.  
- Major: Clarify SNES objective and update rules, and explain interaction between L2 penalty and the Gaussian search distribution.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 needs explicit definition of h(M), clearer indexing for F[i]/Θ[i], and per-image/per-policy loss notation.  
- Minor: Equations 8/11 should be indexed by image and batch sums; define all symbols after equations (x, d, etc.).  
- Minor: Justify L2 penalty on θ—MAP interpretation or numerical stability—given adaptive Gaussian sampling.  
- Minor: Fix typos and inconsistent notation (layer/map indices, softmax scaling, dimensionalities for O and A).  
- Pros: Simple, clear mechanism to add learned feedback to CNNs.  
- Pros: Strong empirical gains on CIFAR-10/100 without test-time augmentation.  
- Pros: Bridges neuroscience-inspired top-down modulation with deep CNN architectures.  
- Pros: SNES enables scalable black-box optimization for very high-dimensional controllers.  
- Cons: Limited interpretability and analysis of learned dynamics; approach remains a black box.  
- Cons: Weak RL framing; effectively direct search over recurrent parameters.  
- Cons: Algorithmic details and equations need clearer definitions and indexing.  
- Cons: Computational cost and practical guidance for stability/generalization are under-discussed.  
- Overall: Promising and empirically strong approach that would be strengthened by clearer dynamical framing, deeper gate analysis, and tighter algorithmic presentation.
