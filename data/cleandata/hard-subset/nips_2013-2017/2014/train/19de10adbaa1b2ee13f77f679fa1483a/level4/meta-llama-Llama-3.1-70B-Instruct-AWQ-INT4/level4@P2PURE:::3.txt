The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that incorporates top-down processing through attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets. This paper is engaging to read and showcases novelty in model design and application to vision problems. It is forward-thinking in its consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN. The evaluation of the model over time and the use of gated signals to constrain representation are particularly noteworthy aspects. However, since the quality of the learned representations heavily relies on the attention policy, it would be more insightful if the authors could provide or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail.

The experiments are interesting, with the authors using visualization to facilitate a deeper understanding of the model. Nevertheless, given the increased complexity of the method compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, I believe the paper would benefit from results on ILSVRC to demonstrate its scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs yields an intriguing model worthy of further investigation. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferable.
