The authors propose a novel approach to deep neural networks, incorporating internal selective attention through feedback connections. This approach is inspired by the human brain, which uses feedback connections to dynamically alter its processing of visual information. The proposed Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet) architecture consists of a standard convolutional neural network (CNN) with additional feedback connections that modulate the activity of the convolutional filters. These feedback connections are learned through reinforcement learning, specifically using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES), to focus the network's attention on the most discriminative features in the input image.

The authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach, including the underlying CNN architecture, the reinforcement learning framework, and the SNES algorithm used to learn the feedback connections. The experimental evaluation of dasNet is conducted on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, where it achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented data.

The strengths of this paper include its novelty, clarity, and significance. The approach taken by the authors is innovative and well-motivated, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the technical details.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the individual components of the approach are not particularly novel. However, the way in which they are combined and applied to a challenging problem is significant and demonstrates the potential of this approach for computer vision tasks.
