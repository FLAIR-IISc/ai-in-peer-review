The authors present a convolutional neural network (CNN) that integrates top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. Their results on the CIFAR datasets achieve state-of-the-art performance. 

This paper is generally compelling and introduces a novel model design and application to the computer vision domain. The consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signals for CNN training is a forward-looking approach. I particularly appreciate the evaluation of the model’s temporal dynamics and the use of gated signals to constrain the learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations is directly tied to the attention policy, it would be valuable for the authors to discuss or demonstrate potential failure cases of the algorithm.

The experiments are insightful, and the authors employ visualizations effectively to aid understanding. However, given the increased complexity of this method compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, the paper would be strengthened by results on the ILSVRC dataset. This would convincingly demonstrate the scalability of the approach to larger datasets and higher-resolution images – this represents my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs yields an interesting model deserving of further investigation, and practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be highly beneficial.
