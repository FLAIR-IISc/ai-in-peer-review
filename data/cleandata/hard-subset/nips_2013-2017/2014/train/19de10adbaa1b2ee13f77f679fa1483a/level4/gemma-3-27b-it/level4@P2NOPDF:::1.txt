This paper introduces a convolutional neural network architecture incorporating feedback connections to learn visual attention. The feedback mechanism is implemented through gating the activations of selected filters, and the entire model is trained using reinforcement learning. While none of the individual components – convolutional networks, reinforcement learning, or attention mechanisms – are novel in isolation, the combination and application are compelling and represent a promising direction for research. 

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. This performance is significant as it provides further evidence supporting the potential of feedback and attention mechanisms to improve visual recognition systems. The quality of the writing is good, and the paper is generally clear in its presentation. 

In summary, this work presents a strong and well-executed approach to vision, leveraging reinforcement learning to guide feedback attention within a convolutional network. The results are convincing, and the architecture appears well-motivated. 

A potential direction for future work would be to explore the scalability of this approach to larger and more complex datasets, such as ImageNet. Investigating performance on these benchmarks would further solidify the impact of the presented method and demonstrate its potential for broader applicability.
