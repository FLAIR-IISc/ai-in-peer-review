- Introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections and a SNES-learned policy to adapt filter sensitivities over multiple passes.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 (~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs).  
- Shows iterative attention refinement can correct misclassifications by emphasizing/de-emphasizing filters.  
- Aligns with neuroscience ideas of top-down processing and addresses CNNs’ inability to reconfigure representations post-training.  
- Strength: compelling technical contribution demonstrating that simple feedback boosts performance on challenging tasks.  
- Strength: robust, well-validated SOTA results on standard benchmarks.  
- Strength: generally clear exposition and practical implementation details (e.g., T=5, training time).  
- Weakness: interpretability gap — paper shows attention helps but fails to explain why particular filter adjustments correct errors.  
- Weakness: superficial analysis of examples (e.g., cat/dog) without linking activation changes to classification outcomes.  
- Weakness: RL framing is misleading — deterministic policies, immediate rewards, static images make it closer to recurrent nets/dynamical systems.  
- Weakness: technical ambiguities — undefined observation function h(M), unclear summation/indexing in Eq. 11, unexplained L2 regularization, and unclear natural gradient update.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of learned policy parameters (mean/covariance reported but not interpreted or constrained).  
- Recommendation: prioritize interpretability by analyzing specific misclassified examples and visualizing gating/filter patterns.  
- Recommendation: reframe or clarify the RL connection and relate the method to recurrent/dynamical systems literature.  
- Recommendation: clarify technical details (define h(M), fix indexing, justify regularization and optimization steps) and simplify/adopt standard loss instead of ad hoc boosting.  
- Overall: empirically valuable and practical, but interpretability and framing issues limit scientific contribution; borderline accept conditional on addressing these gaps.
