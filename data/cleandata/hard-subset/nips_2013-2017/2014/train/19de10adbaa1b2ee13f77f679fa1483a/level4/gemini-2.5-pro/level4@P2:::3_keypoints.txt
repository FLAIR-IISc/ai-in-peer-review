- Proposes dasNet: a feedforward CNN augmented with feedback connections that iteratively modulate convolutional filters to refine predictions.
- Trains the attentional control policy with reinforcement learning (Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies) to handle a million-dimensional parameter space.
- Applied to a Maxout network and achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.
- Conceptually novel by combining bottom-up feature extraction with top-down, human-vision-inspired attentional processing.
- Technically original in merging deep CNNs with RL and using SNES to cope with very high-dimensional policy learning.
- Strong qualitative analysis: visualizations show meaningful gate-driven emphasis/suppression of filters and informative final gate values.
- Scalability concerns: evaluated only on small (32x32) images, unclear if benefits and cost scale to large, high-resolution datasets (e.g., ILSVRC).
- Computational and practical cost: separate RL training phase and multiple forward passes increase complexity and inference time.
- Insufficient analysis of failure modes: lacks study of cases where iterative attention degrades performance or fixates on wrong features.
- Training complexity: two-stage training raises questions about hyperparameter sensitivity and overall training stability.
- Overall: well-written, original, and effective on CIFAR, but limited evaluation leaves scalability and practicality unresolved.
