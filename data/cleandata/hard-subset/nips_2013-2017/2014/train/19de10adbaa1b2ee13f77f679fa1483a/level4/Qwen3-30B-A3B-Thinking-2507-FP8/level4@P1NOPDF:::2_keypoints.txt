- Introduces selective attention in CNNs by training gating parameters to optimize classification performance.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under resource constraints for diverse tasks.  
- Achieves impressive classification results compared to standard methods.  
- Method remains a black box with little explanation of why it works.  
- Analysis of image representations is uninformative.  
- No insight into the role of learned θ parameters (mean and variance).  
- Represents an important advance beyond fixed-parameter, feed-forward networks.  
- Exposition is clear but some equations contain typos or need better explanation.  

Major comments:
- Should explore connection to dynamical systems; method resembles recurrent networks more than reinforcement learning.  
- Training can be seen as traversing space of recurrent “policy” parameters θ to maximize the objective, which could clarify theory and mechanics.  
- Cat-image analysis fails to show how novel features improve recognition.  
- Unanswered: why do layer-1 activations uniformly increase at first iteration?  
- Unanswered: does global scaling of activations affect the objective or is the output invariant?  
- Recommend focus on smaller image regions and track local evolution of gating variables.  
- Recommend analyzing misclassified examples where baseline fails but proposed model succeeds.  
- Recommend isolating the effects of θ’s mean and variance (e.g., by constraining them) to gain insight.  

Minor comments / implementation questions:
- Algorithm 1: what is h_M? Is it the observation collection step?  
- Algorithm 1: are F[i] and Θ[i] stored per image or overwritten each iteration?  
- Equation 11: what is the summation index/scope?  
- Should Equation 8 (single-image expression) be indexed by j?  
- Text after Equation 11: what do variables 'x' and 'd' denote?  
- Why is θ regularized if θ is sampled from a prior that biases toward small values?  
- Final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling θ and evaluating fitness) is unclear and lacks theoretical justification—are posterior hyperparameters being adjusted?  

Overall conclusion:
- Promising approach suggesting recurrent computation improves feature extraction, but interpretability and technical-implementation gaps must be resolved for broader impact.
