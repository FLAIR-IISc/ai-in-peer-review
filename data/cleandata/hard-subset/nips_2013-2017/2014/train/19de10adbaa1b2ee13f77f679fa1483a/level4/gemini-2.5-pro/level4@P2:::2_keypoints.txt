- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback that iteratively modulates convolutional filters via a learned control policy.
- Control policy learned with reinforcement learning using scalable natural evolution strategies (SNES) after supervised CNN training.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, showing iterative refinement can correct misclassifications.
- Strength: significant and original attempt to move beyond static feed-forward architectures toward biologically inspired adaptive processing.
- Strength: impressive empirical performance validating the recurrent attentional approach on challenging benchmarks.
- Strength: generally clear exposition and well-motivated core idea.
- Weakness: analysis is superficial — the method is treated as a "black box" with little insight into why attention improves performance.
- Weakness: illustrative analyses (e.g., Figure 3) are not illuminating and fail to explain specific modulation patterns or correction logic.
- Weakness: no discussion of what the learned policy parameters (mean/covariance of θ) represent or how they guide attention.
- Weakness: conceptual framing as reinforcement learning is tenuous — system resembles a deterministic recurrent network or discrete-time dynamical system.
- Weakness: missed opportunity to explore connections to recurrent networks and alternative training schemes beyond black-box optimization.
- Weakness: methodological details unclear or potentially erroneous — e.g., h(M) undefined in Algorithm 1 and fitness/parameter updates appear mis-placed in inner loop.
- Weakness: Equation 11 missing summation index and likely incorrect indexing of loss terms.
- Weakness: unclear motivation for L2 regularization on θ given SNES already optimizes a Gaussian distribution; needs justification.
- Weakness: SNES update mechanics are not explained; brief overview of natural gradient computation would aid clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture combining state-of-the-art CNNs with feedback, strong empirical results, and important conceptual direction.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient analysis of mechanisms, unconvincing RL framing, and ambiguities that hinder reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance due to novelty and strong results, but strongly urge deeper analysis and clarification of methodological details in the final version.
