

A convolutional neural network incorporating feedback connections for visual attention, implemented through filter gating mechanisms. The model is trained using reinforcement learning integrated with the base convolutional architecture. While no individual component demonstrates significant novelty, the overall approach is well-conceived and represents a sound methodology for integrating attention mechanisms into vision systems. The authors achieve state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10, providing strong validation that feedback-driven attention is a promising direction for future vision research. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented. This constitutes a strong, well-executed approach to vision with compelling empirical results. Should the authors not have already addressed this, extending the evaluation to larger-scale benchmarks such as ImageNet would further strengthen the contribution.
