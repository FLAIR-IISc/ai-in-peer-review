This paper proposes a convolutional network with feedback connections to learn visual attention. The feedback operates by gating the activation of selected filters, and the attention policy is trained with reinforcement learning on top of the convnet.

While no individual component is particularly novel, the integrated approach is, in my view, very strong and the right direction for vision. As expected, the authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which is significant in supporting the claim that feedback/attention is a promising next step.

The paper’s quality and clarity are good. Overall, this is a very good approach to vision—convnets with RL-driven feedback attention—backed by solid results. If possible, the authors should also evaluate on larger-scale problems like ImageNet and consider going deeper, if they have not already.
