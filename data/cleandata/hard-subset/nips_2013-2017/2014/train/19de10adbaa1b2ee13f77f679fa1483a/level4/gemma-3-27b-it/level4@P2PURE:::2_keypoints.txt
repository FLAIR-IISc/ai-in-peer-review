- Introduces selective attention gating in CNNs with learnable gating parameters and reports strong classification results.
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep pipelines under resource constraints and diverse tasks; important advance beyond fixed feed-forward nets.
- Method is opaque—lacks mechanistic insight into why it works despite promising performance.
- Analysis of image representations is uninformative; the meaning of learned theta mean and variance is unclear.
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain errors or need clarification.
- Major: Recommend exploring connections to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than framing as Reinforcement Learning (deterministic policy, immediate reward).
- Major: Analysis of the cat image is unconvincing; need focused analysis of gating variables (e.g., regional tracking) and explanation for global layer-1 activation increases.
- Major: Suggest examining difficult cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected by the model) and systematically probing theta mean/variance effects with constrained experiments.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 missing definition of h_M and unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated or overwritten across images.
- Minor: Eqn 11’s summation is undefined and indexing (e.g., per-image Eqn 8) is unclear.
- Minor: Clarify the meaning of symbols ‘x’ and ‘d’ referenced after Eqn 11.
- Minor: Necessity of regularizing theta is unclear; final gradient-updates after theta sampling lack explanation and theoretical justification (what is being optimized/updated).
- Summary: Promising recurrent computation that improves accuracy, but the approach remains a black box without sufficient mechanistic explanation.
