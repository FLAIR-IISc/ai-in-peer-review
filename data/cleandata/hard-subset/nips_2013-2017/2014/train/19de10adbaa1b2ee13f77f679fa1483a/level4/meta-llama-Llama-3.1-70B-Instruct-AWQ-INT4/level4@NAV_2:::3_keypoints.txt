- Proposes dasNet: a deep neural network with internal selective attention implemented via feedback connections.  
- Inspired by human visual cortex top-down modulation to iteratively focus processing.  
- Architecture enables each layer to influence others over successive image passes to highlight discriminative features.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Introduction outlines CNN limitations and motivates top-down processing.  
- Provides comprehensive related work on recurrence and attention in vision.  
- Technical content is sound with a detailed description of dasNet components.  
- Novel use of reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to train the attention policy.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- dasNet outperforms prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR datasets.  
- Visualizations aid interpretation of the attention mechanism.  
- Lacks experiments on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ILSVRC) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Potential risk of overfitting due to increased parameters and attention complexity.  
- Authors show some evidence the attention policy learns meaningful representations and use regularization (dropout).  
- Strengths: novel architecture, clear technical exposition, thorough experiments, promising results.  
- Weaknesses: limited to smaller datasets and potential overfitting.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, promising approach with sound technical and experimental support.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient large-scale evaluation and overfitting concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but encourage future work on scalability to larger datasets.
