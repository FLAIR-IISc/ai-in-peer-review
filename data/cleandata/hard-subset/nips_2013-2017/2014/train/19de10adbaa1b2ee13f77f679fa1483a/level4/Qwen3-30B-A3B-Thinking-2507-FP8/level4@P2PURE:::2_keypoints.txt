- Introduces selective attention via gating in CNNs and a method to train gating parameters to optimize classification.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under resource constraints for diverse tasks.  
- Achieves impressive classification results but the method remains largely opaque.  
- Paper suggests promising directions but fails to explain mechanistically why the approach works.  
- Analysis of image representations (e.g., the cat image) is unilluminating and lacks insight.  
- The roles of learned parameters (mean and variance of the θ distribution) are not clarified.  
- Represents an important advancement beyond large, fixed-parameter feed-forward networks.  
- Exposition is clear overall but contains typographical errors and equations needing better explanation.  
- Major methodological concern: the approach aligns more with recurrent/dynamical systems than reinforcement learning and the connection should be rigorously explored.  
- Training viewed as traversing recurrent “policy” parameter space θ—clarify this deterministic policy/reward setup.  
- Specific analysis requested: explain why layer-1 activations uniformly increase at first iteration and whether global scaling affects the objective.  
- Recommend focusing on a smaller image region and tracing gating variable evolution for clearer insight.  
- Suggest analyzing hard cases corrected by the model and isolating effects of mean/variance of θ by constraining them.  
- Minor clarifications needed in Algorithm 1 (define h_M; specify storage/overwriting of F[i] and Θ[i]).  
- Equation issues: specify summation indices, index per-image equations, and define variables x and d.  
- Question the necessity of θ regularization given prior sampling and small θ tendency.  
- Final optimization step is unclear: how gradients are computed after sampling/evaluating θ and what is being optimized/theoretically justified.  
- Overall: recurrent computation appears to improve feature extraction/focusing and yields high accuracy, but the method’s opacity prevents understanding why.
