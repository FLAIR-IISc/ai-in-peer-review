The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that integrates a form of top-down processing through attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets. This paper is engaging and showcases innovation in model design and application to vision problems. By considering both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN, the authors demonstrate a forward-thinking approach. The evaluation of the model over time and the use of gated signals to constrain representation are particularly noteworthy aspects.

One potential area for improvement lies in exploring scenarios where the algorithm may fail, as the quality of the learned representations heavily depends on the attention policy. Investigating such cases could provide valuable insights into the model's limitations and robustness.

The experiments are well-designed, with the authors employing visualization techniques to facilitate a deeper understanding of the model. However, given the increased complexity of the proposed method compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, it would be beneficial to include results on the ILSVRC dataset to demonstrate the model's scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is the primary concern with the current submission. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs presents an intriguing model worthy of further investigation. Additional practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be desirable to strengthen the paper's contributions.
