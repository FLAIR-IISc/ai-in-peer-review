- Proposal: Introduces a CNN with feedback connections using gating on filter activations to model visual attention, trained end-to-end with reinforcement learning.

- Integration: Combines established components (CNN, gating, RL) into a unified, well-motivated framework for attention-driven visual processing.

- Empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, significantly outperforming prior attention-based models and conventional CNNs.

- Evidence: Strong empirical support and intuitive attention-map analysis demonstrating the effectiveness of feedback-driven attention.

- Clarity: Paper is clearly written with a concise methodology explaining the RL–CNN integration and gating mechanism.

- Experimental rigor: Sound experimental design with thorough ablation studies, relevant baselines, and appropriate statistical analysis.

- Novelty stance: Avoids overclaiming novelty; presents a pragmatic, scalable integration of existing techniques as a strength.

- Limitation — scope: Evaluation is restricted to CIFAR datasets and does not test scalability to larger, high-resolution vision tasks.

- Limitation — cost: Lacks sufficient discussion of computational overhead and deployment implications introduced by the RL feedback loop.

- Significance: Empirically validates feedback-based attention as a viable, generalizable strategy with potential applicability to detection, segmentation, and other domains.

- Future work: Recommends extending evaluations to larger benchmarks like ImageNet to assess robustness and scalability.

- Recommendation: High-quality, well-executed study recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address scope and computational considerations.
