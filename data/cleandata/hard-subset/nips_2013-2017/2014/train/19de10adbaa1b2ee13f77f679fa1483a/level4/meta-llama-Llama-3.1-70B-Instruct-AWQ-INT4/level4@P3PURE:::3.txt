The authors propose a CNN that incorporates a form of top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets.

This paper is generally interesting to read and demonstrates novelty in terms of model design and application to vision problems. It is also forward-looking in considering both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN. A notable aspect of the model is its evaluation across time, using gated signals to constrain the representation. Since the quality of the learned representations hinges on the attention policy, it would be more interesting if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail.

The experiments are well-designed, with the authors demonstrating the model in various ways, including visualization to facilitate better understanding. However, given that the method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I believe the paper could be improved by including results on ILSVRC to truly demonstrate its scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is my primary concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs makes for an interesting model, worthy of further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferable.
