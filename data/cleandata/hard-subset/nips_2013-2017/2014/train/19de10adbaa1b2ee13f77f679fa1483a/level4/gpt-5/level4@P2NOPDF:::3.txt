The paper proposes a CNN that integrates top-down processing through attention-based feedback and reports state-of-the-art results on CIFAR. The work is engaging, with clear novelty in both model design and its application to visual recognition. It is forward-looking to jointly leverage bottom-up and top-down signals during training. I particularly appreciate the temporal evaluation of the model and the use of gated signals to constrain the representation. Because the quality of the learned representations depends heavily on the attention policy, it would be valuable to discuss or demonstrate potential failure modes.

The experiments are compelling, and the visualizations aid interpretability. However, the method is more complex than a standard feedforward CNN. To convincingly establish scalability to larger images and datasets, results on ILSVRC would strengthen the paper considerably; this is my primary concern. Overall, the combination of attention-based feedback with a CNN is promising and merits further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger-scale datasets would be preferable.
