This paper integrates selective attention into a convolutional neural network and introduces a training procedure for attention-gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It tackles an important set of computer vision problems—how to adaptively reconfigure deep, complex processing stages when resources are limited and multiple tasks must be addressed. The classification results are impressive, but the approach remains largely a black box: it points to promising directions without clarifying why it works. The analysis of the learned image representations is not particularly illuminating, and it remains unclear what the learned parameters (the mean and variance of the theta distribution) are actually doing. Nonetheless, this is a meaningful step beyond the current paradigm of large, purely feed-forward networks with parameters fixed after training. The exposition is generally clear, though several equations contain typos or require better explanation.

Major comments:
- Please explore more thoroughly the connection to dynamical systems. The method appears closer to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning. Because the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic, training effectively becomes a search through the space of recurrent “policy” parameters theta to maximize the observed objective.
- The analysis of the network processing the cat image did not clarify how the new model features aid recognition. Why are all layer-1 activations increased at the first iteration? Does this influence the objective, or is the output invariant to global scalings? Focusing on a smaller image region and analyzing the evolution of gating variables there could be more informative. It would also be useful to examine challenging cases—examples misclassified by a standard Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model—or to isolate the effect of the learned model parameters (mean and variance of theta) by further constraining them.

Minor comments:
- Algorithm 1: Where is h_M defined? Is this the observation-collection step? Since F[i] and Theta[i] appear inside the loop over images (j), are the per-image fitness values stored in an array or overwritten each iteration?
- Equation 11: What is the index of summation? Is Equation 8 intended for a single image and should it be indexed by j?
- Text after Equation 11: What do “x” and “d” denote?
- The need for regularizing theta is unclear, given that theta is sampled from the prior and should already tend toward small values. Moreover, the final optimization step—performing gradient updates on parameters after sampling and evaluating the fitness of thetas—is not well explained (how are these gradients computed?) or theoretically justified (are you adjusting hyperparameters of the posterior over policies?).

Overall, this is an interesting approach to constructing deep recurrent networks for recognition. The strong classification performance relative to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation helps extract or focus on more complex image features, but the black-box nature of the approach does not explain why it is effective.
