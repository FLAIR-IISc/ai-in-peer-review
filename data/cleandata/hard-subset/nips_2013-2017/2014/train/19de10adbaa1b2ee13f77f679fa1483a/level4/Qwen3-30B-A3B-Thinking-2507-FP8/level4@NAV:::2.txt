

This paper introduces selective attention into convolutional neural networks and proposes a training method for gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It tackles a significant challenge in computer vision: adaptively reconfiguring deep processing stages under resource constraints for diverse tasks. While the classification results are compelling, the approach remains largely opaque—it suggests promising directions without elucidating why the method succeeds. The analysis of image representations lacks insight, and the role of learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) is not clarified. Despite this, the work represents a meaningful advancement beyond fixed-parameter, feed-forward deep networks. The exposition is clear, though some equations contain typographical errors or require better explanation.

Major comments:  
The authors should rigorously explore the connection to dynamical systems. The framework aligns more closely with recurrent networks than Reinforcement Learning, as the reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic. Training thus entails optimizing over a space of recurrent "policy" parameters θ to maximize the objective function.  
The analysis of the cat image fails to demonstrate how the model’s novel features enhance recognition. Specifically: Why do layer 1 activations uniformly increase at the first iteration? Does this scaling affect the objective, or is the output invariant to global scaling? Focusing on localized image regions and tracking gating variable evolution would be more instructive. Additionally, examining misclassified cases (where standard Maxout fails but the proposed model succeeds) or isolating the impact of learned θ parameters (e.g., via constrained experiments) would strengthen the analysis.

Minor comments:  
- Algorithm 1: Define h_M (is it the observation collection step?). Clarify whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored in an array or overwritten during the image loop (j).  
- Equation 11: Specify the summation index. If Equation 8 applies to a single image, it should be indexed (e.g., j).  
- Post-equation 11 text: Define 'x' and 'd'.  
- The necessity of θ regularization is unclear, as θ is already sampled from a prior and naturally small. The final optimization step (gradient updates on θ post-sampling) lacks theoretical justification and explanation of gradient computation.  

This work presents an interesting path toward deep recurrent networks for recognition. The high classification accuracy compared to standard baselines suggests recurrent computation enhances feature extraction or focus, but the opaque methodology prevents understanding its efficacy.
