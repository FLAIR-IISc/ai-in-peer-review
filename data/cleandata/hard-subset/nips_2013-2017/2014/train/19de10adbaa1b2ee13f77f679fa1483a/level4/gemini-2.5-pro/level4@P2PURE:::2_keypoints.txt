- Introduces a selective attention mechanism in a CNN with trainable gating parameters to optimize classification.
- Tackles adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under resource constraints and varying tasks.
- Produces impressive classification results but the model’s internal workings remain opaque.
- Promising research direction but provides little explanation for why the method is effective.
- Analysis of learned image representations is uninformative; role of theta distribution parameters (mean, variance) is unclear.
- Advances beyond fixed, feed-forward networks; paper is generally well-written but contains typographical/errors in equations.
- Major: Needs stronger connection to dynamical systems; method seems closer to RNNs than RL since reward is immediate and policy deterministic.
- Major: Example analyses (e.g., the “cat” image) do not clarify how novel components aid recognition; questions about global activation scaling and its effect on the objective.
- Major: Recommend focused analyses on small image regions, evolution of gating variables, and hard cases where the model corrects standard network errors.
- Major: Suggest experiments constraining theta mean/variance to determine their specific effects.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 issues — h_M undefined; unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] updates store per-image fitness or overwrite values.
- Minor: Equation 11 missing summation index; Equation 8 may need per-image indexing (j).
- Minor: Variables x and d are undefined following Equation 11.
- Minor: Motivation for regularizing theta is unclear; final gradient updates on distribution parameters lack explanation and theoretical justification (how gradients are computed; whether adjusting posterior hyperparameters).
- Overall: Recurrent computation appears to improve recognition, but the “black box” nature and lack of mechanistic explanation are significant limitations.
