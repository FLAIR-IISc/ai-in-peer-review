- Proposes dasNet: CNNs with trainable feedback gates that dynamically reweight convolutional filters for iterative inference.  
- Trains attention policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle >1M policy parameters.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100, reporting ~6% relative improvement.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and connects to reinforcement-learning–style policy learning and recurrent processing literature.  
- Conceptually novel: adds internal attention without redesigning base CNN architecture.  
- Strong technical execution: well-motivated SNES choice, thorough experiments, ablation studies, and dynamics analysis.  
- Clear training procedure (Algorithm 1) and illustrative examples (e.g., cat/dog Fig. 3) demonstrating mechanism.  
- Clarity generally good, though some equations (Eq. 5, 11) need minor clarification.  
- Major weakness: poor interpretability — paper does not explain why specific gates improve recognition or which features are important.  
- Underdeveloped theoretical grounding: insufficient discussion of relation to recurrent/dynamical systems despite iterative process.  
- Methodological details lacking: unjustified L2 regularization (Eq. 11), unclear natural-gradient computation in Algorithm 1, and ambiguous notation (x vs. x_j, d vs. d_j).  
- Related-work gap: misses recent papers on interpretable attention (e.g., Zeiler & Fergus 2014) that could frame interpretability issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, reproducible advance that merits publication despite interpretability and minor methodological gaps.  
- Revision requests: quantify gating–accuracy correlations, expand dynamical-systems/RNN framing, justify regularization/optimization choices, and correct ambiguous notation.
