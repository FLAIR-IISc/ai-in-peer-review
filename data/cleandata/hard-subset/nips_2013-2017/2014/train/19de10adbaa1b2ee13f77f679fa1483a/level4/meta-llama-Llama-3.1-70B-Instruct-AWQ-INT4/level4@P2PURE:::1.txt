This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (convnet) with feedback connections to learn visual attention through reinforcement learning. The feedback mechanism is implemented by gating the activation of certain filters. Although the individual components of the model are not particularly novel, the approach as a whole is well-motivated and represents a promising direction for future research, in my opinion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset, which provides significant evidence that incorporating feedback and attention mechanisms is a fruitful area of investigation. The quality and clarity of the paper are also commendable. Overall, the approach of using convnets with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention for vision tasks is well-executed and yields impressive results. A potential direction for future work could be to apply this method to more challenging datasets, such as ImageNet, if the authors have not already done so.
