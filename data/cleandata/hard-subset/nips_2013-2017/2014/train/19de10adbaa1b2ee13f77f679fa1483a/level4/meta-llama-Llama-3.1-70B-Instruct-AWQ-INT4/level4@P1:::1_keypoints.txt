- Proposes dasNet: a CNN augmented with internal selective attention via feedback connections inspired by the brain.  
- Feedback connections modulate convolutional filter activity and are learned using reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies, SNES).  
- Paper provides thorough explanations of the CNN architecture, the RL framework, and the SNES implementation.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, achieving state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented data.  
- Strengths: novel, well-motivated approach with clear exposition and significant empirical results.  
- Weakness: constituent components are not individually novel.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the learned feedback connections and their specific role in performance.  
- Argument for acceptance: innovative method, strong results, and clear presentation with potential for broader impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited novelty at the component level and insufficient introspection of learned mechanisms.  
- Suggestion: apply the approach to larger, more challenging datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Suggestion: provide a deeper analysis of the learned feedback connections and how they affect network behavior.  
- Suggestion: explore other reinforcement learning algorithms to improve learning efficiency and effectiveness.  
- Overall: strong paper that, with minor revisions addressing analysis and scalability, could make a significant contribution.
