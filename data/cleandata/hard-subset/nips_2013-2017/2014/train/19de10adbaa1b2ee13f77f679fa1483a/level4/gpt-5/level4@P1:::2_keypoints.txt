- Paper augments a Maxout CNN with learned feedback gates that adapt filter sensitivities across multiple passes, trained as a deterministic policy.
- The policy is large (~1M parameters) and trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) instead of standard RL due to scale.
- Evaluated on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100, showing notable accuracy gains over a strong Maxout baseline and claiming state-of-the-art for unaugmented data.
- Positions itself relative to biologically inspired feedback, recurrent iterative refinement, and attention mechanisms, building on Maxout and dropout.
- Novelty: learns feedback that scales internal feature maps in a modern deep CNN and trains the controller via black-box evolution.
- Strength: addresses feedforward CNN limitations by enabling adaptive, sequential processing.
- Strength: demonstrates practical, non-trivial accuracy improvements on standard benchmarks without data augmentation.
- Strength: implementation is conceptually simple (per-map gating) and compatible with strong CNN architectures.
- Weakness: method operates as a black box—unclear why, when, or how learned feedback helps or what learned parameters represent.
- Weakness: qualitative analyses (e.g., cat example) are uninformative and do not elucidate mechanisms.
- Weakness: algorithmic and notational details contain typos/ambiguities that hinder reproducibility.
- Weakness: RL framing is superficial; training is effectively black-box optimization of a recurrent controller with immediate rewards.
- Major: explicitly connect to dynamical systems/recurrent networks and clarify why RL terminology is used and whether delayed credit assignment exists.
- Major: provide more localized and systematic analyses (hard cases, ablations on θ distribution, variance/mean constraints) to reveal mechanism.
- Major: report learned SNES mean and variance to show whether gates are near identity, sparse deviations, or layer-consistent up/down-weighting.
- Minor: define h(Mt) in Algorithm 1 and clarify the observation collection step.
- Minor: clarify Algorithm 1 indexing and whether per-image losses are accumulated or overwritten when computing fitness.
- Minor: fix Equation (11) and Eq. (8) indexing, and clarify x and d after Eq. (11) (input and label).
- Minor: justify explicit L2 regularization on θ given SNES search distribution contraction and clarify SNES natural-gradient updates and whether distribution hyperparameters are adjusted instead of policy parameters.
- Evaluation — Quality: technically plausible with strong results but limited by clarity and lack of diagnostic ablations.
- Evaluation — Clarity: generally readable but equations/indices contain ambiguities and interpretability analysis is weak.
- Evaluation — Originality: novel combination of learned internal attention via feedback gates and SNES training.
- Evaluation — Significance: performance gains suggest iterative, attentive computation can improve recognition and may influence future feedback integrations.
- Pros for acceptance: measurable SOTA improvements, advances adaptive iterative processing, and a simple general mechanism.
- Cons for acceptance: limited insight into mechanisms, presentation/reproducibility issues, and potentially overstated RL framing.
- Overall: promising, high-performing approach that currently reads as a black box; stronger technical clarity and deeper analysis of learned dynamics are needed.
