Review- This work augments a convolutional neural network with selective attention and introduces a training procedure for attention gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It tackles an important challenge in computer vision: how to adaptively reconfigure deep, complex processing stages under resource constraints and varying task demands. The classification outcomes are strong, but the approach remains largely a black box—it points to promising directions without explaining why it works. The representation analysis is not very illuminating, and the roles of the learned parameters (the mean and variance of the theta distribution) are not clarified. Even so, the paper marks a noteworthy step beyond the prevailing paradigm of large, purely feed-forward networks with frozen parameters post-training. The presentation is generally clear, though several equations contain typos or would benefit from clearer exposition.

major comments:
I encourage the authors to seriously investigate the connection to dynamical systems. The method appears closer to recurrent networks than to Reinforcement Learning. Rewards are available immediately and the policy is deterministic, so training amounts to traversing the space of recurrent “policy” parameters theta to maximize the observed objective.

The analysis of the network’s processing of the cat image did not help me understand how the new model components improve recognition. Why are all layer-1 activations increased in the first iteration? Does this influence the objective, or is the output invariant to global scaling? It may be more informative to focus on a smaller image region and analyze the evolution of the gating variables there. Also consider examining difficult cases—those misclassified by the baseline Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model—or isolating the contribution of the learned parameters (theta’s mean and variance) by further constraining them.

minor comments:
Algorithm 1 description: Where is h_M defined? Is that the observation-collection step? Since F[i] and Θ[i] appear inside the loop over images (j), are the per-image fitness values stored in an array or overwritten each iteration?

Eqn 11: What is the summation index? Is Eqn 8 defined per image and should it be indexed by j?

Text after Eqn 11: What do “x” and “d” denote?

I do not see why additional regularization of theta is needed, given that theta is sampled from the prior and should already bias toward small values. Moreover, the final optimization step (performing gradient updates on the parameters after sampling and evaluating thetas) is not well explained (how are the gradients computed?) or theoretically justified (are you updating hyperparameters of the posterior over policies?).

Overall, this is an interesting route toward building deep recurrent recognition systems. The strong accuracy relative to standard baselines suggests that recurrent computation helps extract or emphasize more complex image features, but the black-box nature of the method leaves its effectiveness insufficiently explained.
