This paper introduces selective attention to a convolutional neural network and develops a method for training gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It addresses an important problem in computer vision – how to adaptively reconfigure complex, deep processing stages when resources are limited and different tasks need to be solved. The classification results are impressive, though the underlying mechanisms remain largely opaque. While the work suggests promising directions, it doesn't fully explain *why* the method works. The analysis of image representation isn’t particularly insightful, and there's limited understanding of what the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) represent. Nevertheless, this work is an important step beyond the current paradigm of large, feedforward networks with fixed parameters post-training. The exposition is generally clear, although some equations require clarification or correction.

**Major Comments:**

I encourage the authors to explore the connection to dynamical systems. The approach feels closer to recurrent networks than to Reinforcement Learning. The reward is observed immediately, and the policy is deterministic. Therefore, training can be framed as a traversal through the space of recurrent "policy" parameters, theta, to maximize the observed objective function.

I found the analysis of the cat image processing less informative than it could be. Why are layer 1 activations universally increased in the first iteration? Does this affect the objective function, or is the output invariant to global scalings? Perhaps focusing on a smaller image region and analyzing the evolution of the gating variables within that region could provide more insights. Additionally, investigating difficult cases – those misclassified by the standard Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model – or teasing out the effect of the learned model parameters (mean and variance of theta) through further constraints would be valuable.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Algorithm 1 Description:** Where is *h(M)* defined? Is this the step of collecting observations? *F[i]* and *Θ[i]* are within the loop over images (j); are image fitness values accumulated in an array or overwritten with each iteration?
*   **Equation 11:** What is the summation over? Is Equation 8 calculated for a single image and, if so, should it be indexed by *j*?
*   **Text After Equation 11:** What do ‘x’ and ‘d’ refer to?
*   I'm unclear on the necessity of regularizing theta, given it’s already sampled from a prior distribution and will naturally tend towards smaller values. Furthermore, the final step of optimization – gradient updates on the parameters after sampling and evaluating fitness – isn’t well explained (how are the gradients computed?) or theoretically justified (are you adjusting hyperparameters of the posterior over policies?).

Overall, this is an interesting approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition. The high classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation aids in extracting or focusing on more complex image features. However, the "black box" nature of the approach limits our understanding of its effectiveness.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
*   Introduces a novel approach to selective attention in deep networks.
*   Successfully leverages Reinforcement Learning (SNES) to train gating parameters.
*   Presents a potentially significant step towards more adaptable and robust deep learning models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of interpretability: the mechanisms driving performance improvements are not fully understood.
*   Limited analysis of the learned gating parameters and their impact on image representation.
*   The connection to dynamical systems is unexplored, potentially providing a more principled understanding.
*   Some technical details and explanations require clarification.
