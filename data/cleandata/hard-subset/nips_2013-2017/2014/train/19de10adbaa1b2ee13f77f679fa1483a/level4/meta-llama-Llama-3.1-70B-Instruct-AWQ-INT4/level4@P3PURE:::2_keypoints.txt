- Introduces selective attention via gating in CNNs to optimize classification under limited resources; results are impressive but method is a black box.
- Important contribution beyond fixed, feed-forward networks; proposes adaptive reconfiguration for different tasks.
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos and areas needing better explanation.
- Should explore connections to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than framing as Reinforcement Learning, since rewards are immediate and policy is deterministic.
- Training resembles searching over recurrent policy parameters θ to maximize the objective; this connection needs elaboration.
- Analysis of the cat-image processing is not insightful; it fails to explain how new model features aid recognition.
- Need to explain why layer-1 activations increase on the first iteration and whether global scaling affects the objective or output invariance.
- Suggest focusing analysis on a small image region and tracking gating-variable evolution for more instructive insight.
- Recommend examining hard cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected by the model) to understand what the model learns.
- Propose constraining or probing learned θ statistics (mean, variance) to reveal their effect on performance.
- Algorithm 1 has unclear variables: h_M is undefined and it's ambiguous whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored or overwritten per image.
- Equation 11’s summation is unclear; Equation 8 may need image indexing (j); symbols 'x' and 'd' are used without definition.
- Regularization on θ is not well-justified since θ is sampled from a prior and tends to small values; rationale needed.
- Final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling/evaluating θ) lacks explanation of how gradients are computed and theoretical justification (e.g., whether posterior hyper-parameters are adjusted).
- Overall, recurrent computation plausibly improves feature extraction and accuracy, but lack of transparency makes the reasons for success unclear.
