- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that uses reinforcement learning to dynamically gate filter activations across multiple passes.  
- Core method: supervised pre-training of a CNN followed by training a policy with SNES to modulate convolutional filters iteratively.  
- Motivated by recurrent processing in biological vision and the need for iterative refinement to resolve visual ambiguity.  
- Situates work within existing CNN, reinforcement learning, and attention literature with appropriate citations.  
- Strength: elegant, compelling combination of attention and feedback that improves classification performance.  
- Strength: pragmatic two-stage training (supervised then RL) that scales to large control spaces.  
- Strong empirical results: reports state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, especially on difficult examples.  
- Writing and organization are clear; Figure 3 effectively visualizes filter weighting changes.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in individual components; main contribution is their combination which needs clearer justification.  
- Weakness: choice of SNES is reasonable but lacks deeper discussion of trade-offs versus other RL methods.  
- Weakness: experiments restricted to CIFAR-10/100; evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) needed for claims of scalability/generalizability.  
- Weakness: dynamics analysis (Figure 2) is limited and qualitative analysis of learned attention patterns is insufficient.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on expanding experiments to larger datasets and deeper analysis of learned attention in future work.
