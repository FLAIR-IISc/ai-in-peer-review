- Introduces selective attention via gating in a CNN and a method to train gating parameters to optimize classification.
- Addresses an important problem: adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and diverse tasks.
- Achieves impressive classification results compared to standard methods.
- Method operates largely as a black box; lacks explanation for why it works effectively.
- Analysis of image representations is limited and not particularly revealing.
- Learned parameter meanings (mean and variance of theta) remain obscure.
- Represents a significant step beyond large, fixed-parameter feed-forward networks.
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos and needs more explanation in places.
- Suggests exploring connections to dynamical systems, as the approach resembles recurrent networks more than RL.
- Training appears to traverse recurrent “policy” parameter space (theta) with immediate reward and deterministic policy.
- The network analysis (e.g., cat image) fails to show how model features contribute to recognition; observed effects (like layer 1 scaling) are unexplained.
- Recommends more focused analysis on local image regions and evolution of gating variables.
- Suggests investigating difficult cases misclassified by baseline but corrected by the model to understand mechanisms.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: undefined variables (h_M) and ambiguous indexing/array handling for F[i] and Θ[i].
- Equations are ambiguous: Eqn 11 summation unclear, Eqn 8 likely needs image indexing, and variables 'x' and 'd' are undefined after Eqn 11.
- Questions necessity and justification of theta regularization and lacks explanation/theory for final gradient updates on parameters.
