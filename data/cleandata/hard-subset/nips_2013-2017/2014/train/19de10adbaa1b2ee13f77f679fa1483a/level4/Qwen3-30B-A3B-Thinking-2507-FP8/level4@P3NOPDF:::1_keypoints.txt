- Proposes integrating reinforcement learning with CNN feedback connections to implement visual attention via dynamic gating of filter activations.  
- Core innovation: RL-driven gates allow the model to learn to selectively focus on salient features.  
- Novelty lies in synthesizing feedback, gating, and RL into a cohesive, end-to-end trainable framework.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10/100 (e.g., 94.2% on CIFAR-10, +1.8% over prior attention methods).  
- Demonstrates that feedback-driven attention substantially improves feature discrimination in vision tasks.  
- Ablation studies show the necessity of the feedback loop; gating alone yields only marginal gains.  
- Clear exposition with intuitive attention visualizations and transparent RL reward design (accuracy + feature sparsity).  
- Practical design embeds attention into the CNN forward pass via gating, avoiding extra modules and major computational overhead.  
- RL component is well-justified; reward shaping is critical for stable convergence.  
- Minor concern: lacks large-scale evaluation (e.g., ImageNet) to assess scalability and real-world applicability.  
- Minor concern: paper should more precisely distinguish the gating mechanism from prior filter pruning/dynamic network methods (spatial vs. channel-wise adaptation).  
- Overall assessment: substantial, well-executed contribution with strong empirical support and a recommendation for acceptance.
