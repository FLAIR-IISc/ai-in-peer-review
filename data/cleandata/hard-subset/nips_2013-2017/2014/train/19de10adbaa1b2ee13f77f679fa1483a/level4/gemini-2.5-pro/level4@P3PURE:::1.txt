This paper proposes a convolutional neural network that learns visual attention via feedback connections. The feedback is implemented by gating the activations of specific filters, and the model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of the base network.

While the individual components are not particularly novel, their synthesis represents a compelling and well-motivated approach. The effectiveness of this method is demonstrated by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. These strong empirical results provide significant validation for feedback and attention as a promising direction for future vision models. The quality of the paper and the clarity of its presentation are good.

Overall, this is a very good approach to computer vision that combines CNNs with RL-driven feedback for attention and delivers impressive results. A natural next step would be to evaluate the model's performance on larger-scale problems, such as ImageNet, and the authors are encouraged to explore this direction to further demonstrate the scalability of their method.
