- Clear idea: use feedback-driven gating to focus computation on informative convolutional features.
- Sound methodology: employ reinforcement learning to learn the attention policy on top of a standard convnet.
- Empirical impact: achieves state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR, showing feedback/attention can improve convnet accuracy.
- Presentation: paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Novelty moderate: components (convnets, gating, RL) are known; main contribution is their integration and validation.
- Limited scale: results are compelling on CIFAR but unclear how the approach generalizes to larger, more complex datasets.
- Efficiency and stability concerns: computational overhead and RL training stability/convergence are not fully discussed.
- Significance: supports the view that learned feedback and attention are promising directions for vision models.
- Suggestion — scale tests: add experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to test scalability and generalization.
- Suggestion — ablations: provide ablation studies isolating the contribution of feedback/gating versus baseline convnets.
- Suggestion — interpretability: analyze learned attention patterns (which filters/layers are gated) to explain behavior.
- Suggestion — costs and training: report computational costs (training time, inference overhead) and discuss RL stability/variance.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, empirically validated, and timely work; recommendation to accept with encouragement to extend evaluation.
