This paper introduces a novel architecture called Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet) that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections, allowing the network to adaptively alter its convolutional filter sensitivities during classification. The authors demonstrate that dasNet outperforms the previous state-of-the-art model on unaugmented datasets, specifically CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.

The main idea behind dasNet is to harness the power of sequential processing to improve classification performance by allowing the network to iteratively focus its internal attention on some of its convolutional filters. This is achieved through a feedback structure that modulates the activity of the convolutional filters, enabling the network to correct its initial guesses via additional internal "thoughts".

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. However, the analysis of the network's processing of the cat image could be more insightful. It would be helpful to explore the connection to dynamical systems, as the work seems closer to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning. The reward is observed immediately, and the policy is deterministic, so training involves a traversal through the space of recurrent "policy" parameters theta to maximize the observed objective function.

Some minor comments include the need for clarification on the definition of h_M in Algorithm 1, the collection of observations, and the indexing of Eqn 8. Additionally, the text after Eqn 11 could be clearer on what 'x' and 'd' refer to. The regularization of theta is also not well-explained, as theta is already sampled from the prior and will tend to small values anyway.

The paper's approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition is interesting, and the high classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation helps extract or focus on more complex features of the images. However, the black box approach does not explain why it's effective.

**Strengths:**

* Novel architecture that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections
* Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented datasets
* Clear explanation of the approach

**Weaknesses:**

* Lack of insight into why the method works
* Analysis of image representation is not very revealing
* Connection to dynamical systems is not explored
* Minor clarifications needed in the text and equations
