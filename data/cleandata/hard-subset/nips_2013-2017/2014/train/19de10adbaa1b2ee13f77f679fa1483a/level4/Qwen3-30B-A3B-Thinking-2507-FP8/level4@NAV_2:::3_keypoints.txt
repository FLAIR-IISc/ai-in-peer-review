- Introduces dasNet: a CNN architecture with feedback-based selective attention that iteratively refines filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Biologically inspired by recurrent processing in visual systems, contrasting with stationary feedforward CNNs.  
- Extends Maxout networks by learning a policy (via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies, SNES) to dynamically weight convolutional filters across multiple passes.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100, ~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs.  
- Originality: novel internal attention mechanism using feedback, distinct from prior recurrent or saccade-control approaches.  
- Technical contribution: applying SNES to scale policy learning in a high-dimensional control space.  
- Experimental quality: rigorous design with ablation studies (T steps, gate info) and visualizations demonstrating corrected misclassifications.  
- Significance: tangible performance gains without augmentation and potential for “corrective thinking” in vision tasks.  
- Clarity: well-structured paper with clear algorithmic descriptions and persuasive biological-technical linkage.  
- Weakness: no results on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet), limiting assessment of generalizability.  
- Weakness: lacks systematic failure analysis showing when dasNet cannot correct errors.  
- Weakness: justification for using SNES over standard RL methods could be better contextualized beyond the high-dimensionality argument.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and novel; acceptable for NIPS given strong CIFAR results, but should address scalability and failure analysis in future work.
