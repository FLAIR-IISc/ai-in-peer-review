The authors have proposed a CNN that incorporates a form of top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback. Results on the CIFAR dataset demonstrate cutting-edge performance.

This paper is generally interesting to read and shows novelty in the context of model design and application to a vision problem. It is also promising for considering both bottom-up and top-down signals to train CNNs. I particularly like the aspect of using gated signals to evaluate the model over time and limit representation. Since the quality of learned representations depends on the attention policy, it would be more interesting if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm might fail.

The experiments are interesting, with the authors demonstrating the model in various ways, such as visualization to help better understand it. However, since this method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I think the paper could be improved if results were available on ILSVRC, so that its scalability with large images in truly large datasets could be demonstrated. This would be my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs makes for an interesting model, which warrants further study. More practical insights from experiments on large datasets will be prioritized.
