This paper introduces a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that integrates top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR datasets.

Overall, this is a compelling and novel contribution. The work is interesting to read and presents a significant advance in model design for computer vision, moving beyond purely bottom-up processing to explicitly consider top-down signals during training. I particularly appreciate the temporally-aware evaluation of the model and the use of gated signals to constrain learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations is inherently tied to the attention policy, it would strengthen the paper to discuss or illustrate scenarios where the proposed algorithm might encounter difficulties or limitations. Exploring potential failure modes would provide valuable insight.

The experimental analysis is thorough and includes visualizations that aid in understanding the model's behavior. However, the increased complexity of this approach compared to traditional feedforward CNNs raises a crucial question regarding scalability. To convincingly demonstrate the practical applicability of this work, I believe results on the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) dataset are necessary. Evaluating performance on a larger, more challenging dataset with higher-resolution images would significantly bolster the claims of the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of its capabilities. This remains my primary concern.

In conclusion, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs yields a promising and potentially impactful model worthy of further investigation. I would welcome the inclusion of experiments on larger datasets to provide more practical insights and solidify the demonstrated benefits.
