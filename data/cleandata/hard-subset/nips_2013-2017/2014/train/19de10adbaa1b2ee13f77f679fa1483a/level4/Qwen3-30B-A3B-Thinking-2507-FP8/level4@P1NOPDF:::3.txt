

The authors propose a novel CNN architecture incorporating top-down attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100. The work presents a compelling model design that integrates both bottom-up and top-down signals during training—a forward-looking approach for vision tasks. I particularly appreciate the temporal evaluation of the model's representation evolution and the use of gated signals to constrain feature learning. Since the quality of learned representations critically depends on the attention policy, the authors could further strengthen the analysis by providing concrete examples where the model fails, offering deeper insight into its limitations.

The experimental section is well-executed, with helpful visualizations that clarify the model's behavior. However, as the proposed method introduces significant complexity beyond standard feedforward CNNs, the primary concern is the lack of evaluation on a larger-scale benchmark. Demonstrating scalability on ILSVRC-2012 (ImageNet) with higher-resolution images would substantially bolster the paper's claims. Without such results, the paper's contribution remains largely validated on smaller datasets, leaving open questions about its applicability to more challenging, real-world vision problems. This gap represents the most significant limitation requiring attention before publication.

The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs presents a promising direction for future research. While the current results on CIFAR are impressive, the work would be substantially more impactful with evidence of robust performance on larger, more diverse datasets. The authors should prioritize including ILSVRC-2012 results to address scalability concerns and provide more concrete insights into the model's practical utility. The core novelty and technical contribution are valuable, but the current evaluation scope limits the paper's broader significance. With the addition of larger-scale experiments, this work would warrant serious consideration for publication.
