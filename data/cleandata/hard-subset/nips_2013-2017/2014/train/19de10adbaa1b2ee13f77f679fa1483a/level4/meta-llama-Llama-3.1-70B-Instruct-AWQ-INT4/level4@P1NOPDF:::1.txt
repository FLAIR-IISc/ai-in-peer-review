A convolutional network (convnet) with feedback connections to learn visual attention is proposed. The feedback mechanism is implemented by gating the activation of certain filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of a convnet. While no individual component is particularly novel, the approach taken is, in my opinion, a promising direction and a suitable way forward. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method with state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. This achievement is significant, as it confirms that incorporating feedback and attention mechanisms is a viable path for future research. The quality and clarity of the submission are good. Overall, the authors present a compelling approach to vision tasks, achieving good results by combining convnets with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention. To further demonstrate the potential of their method, the authors may consider applying it to more challenging problems, such as ImageNet, if they have not already done so.
