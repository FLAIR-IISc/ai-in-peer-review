- Introduces dasNet: feedback-enabled architecture that applies learned gating to convolutional filters across sequential image passes.  
- Gating mechanism (eq.5) dynamically modulates filter activations; attention policy learned with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Addresses limitation of stationary feedforward CNNs by modeling recurrent, top-down feedback inspired by biological vision.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains: state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10/100 with ~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs without augmentation or retraining.  
- Effectively corrects misclassifications by selectively enhancing/de-emphasizing features (e.g., disambiguating cat/dog examples).  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous execution, SNES scales to million-dimensional policy space, and thorough ablation/dynamics analyses.  
- Clarity is excellent: precise math (eqs.1–11), clear algorithm (Alg.1), and insightful visualizations (Fig.3).  
- Strong biological motivation linking feedback processing to improved classification (Section 5).  
- Originality: novel use of evolutionary policy search for filter-level gating in pre-trained CNNs, distinct from prior RL/saccade work.  
- Significance: shows feedback-driven internal attention is a viable path to improving CNN robustness and sets new SOTA for unaugmented CIFAR.  
- Main weakness: validation limited to CIFAR-10/100; scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) remains untested.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, well-executed contribution with minor scope limitation.
