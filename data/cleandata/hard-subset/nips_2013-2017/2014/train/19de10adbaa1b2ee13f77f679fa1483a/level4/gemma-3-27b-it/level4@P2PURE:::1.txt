This paper presents a convolutional neural network incorporating feedback connections to learn visual attention, implemented through gating the activations of selected filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning applied on top of the convolutional network. While no single component is groundbreaking, the overall approach is strong and represents a promising direction for research. As demonstrated, the method achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which supports the hypothesis that feedback and attention mechanisms are a valuable path forward. The paper is well-written and clear. In summary, this is a strong approach to visual recognition with compelling results – a convolutional network with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention. The authors are encouraged to explore the scalability of this method to more challenging datasets, such as ImageNet, if they haven’t already done so.
