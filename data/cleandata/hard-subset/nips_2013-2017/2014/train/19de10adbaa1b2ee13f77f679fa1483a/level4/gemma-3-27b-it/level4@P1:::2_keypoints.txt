- Introduces selective attention via gating in CNNs and trains gating parameters to improve classification performance.
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources for multiple tasks.
- Achieves impressive classification results but lacks clear explanation of underlying mechanisms.
- Analysis of image representations is weak and provides limited insight into learned parameters (mean/variance of theta).
- Work is an important step beyond fixed-parameter feedforward networks, proposing more adaptive architectures.
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations and notations need clarification or correction.
- Major: recommend exploring connections to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than RL, since reward is immediate and policy is deterministic.
- Major: further analysis needed on why layer-1 activations increase universally in the first iteration and whether outputs are invariant to global scaling.
- Major: suggest focusing on smaller image regions and examining gating evolution, and analyzing cases where the method corrects Maxout failures.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 unclear — definition of h(M), and whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated or overwritten across images.
- Minor: Equation 11 unclear — summation index and whether Equation 8 should be indexed by image j.
- Minor: Undefined notation after Equation 11 — what do ‘x’ and ‘d’ refer to?
- Minor: Question the necessity of regularizing theta and request clarification of gradient updates after sampling (how gradients are computed and theoretical justification).
- For acceptance: demonstrates state-of-the-art CIFAR-10/100 results and introduces a novel selective-attention approach.
- For acceptance: successfully applies SNES/RL to train gating parameters and advances adaptable, robust deep models.
- Against acceptance: poor interpretability and limited understanding of how gating parameters affect representation and performance.
- Against acceptance: unexplored dynamical-systems perspective and several technical/explanatory gaps needing clarification.
