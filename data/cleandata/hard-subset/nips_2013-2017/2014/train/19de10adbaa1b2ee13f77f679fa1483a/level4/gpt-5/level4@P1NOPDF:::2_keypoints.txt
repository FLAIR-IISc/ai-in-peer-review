- Proposes augmenting CNNs with selective attention via learnable gating and a training method for gating parameters to optimize classification.
- Tackles the important problem of adaptively reconfiguring deep processing under limited resources and varying tasks.
- Classification results are impressive and show high accuracy relative to standard methods.
- The method is presented largely as a black box and offers little insight into why it works.
- Representation analysis is uninformative and the roles of the learned theta mean and variance are unclear.
- The work is an important step beyond fixed-parameter, purely feed-forward networks by introducing recurrent/adaptive computation.
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need better explanation.
- Major: The method should be framed and explored as a dynamical/recurrent system rather than as reinforcement learning to clarify learning signals and stability.
- Major: The single-image (cat) analysis fails to explain how new features aid recognition; unclear why layer-1 activations increase and whether output is invariant to global scaling.
- Major: Recommend focused analysis on small image regions, evolution of gating variables, hard examples corrected by the model, and ablation/constraining of theta mean/variance.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 is unclear—h_M is undefined and it’s ambiguous whether per-image fitness values are stored or overwritten.
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation index is missing, and Equation 8 may need an image index j.
- Minor: Variables x and d referenced after Equation 11 are not defined.
- Minor/theoretical: The need for regularizing theta is unclear; the final gradient-based optimization after sampling is not explained—how gradients are computed and what theoretical justification supports adjusting these parameters.
