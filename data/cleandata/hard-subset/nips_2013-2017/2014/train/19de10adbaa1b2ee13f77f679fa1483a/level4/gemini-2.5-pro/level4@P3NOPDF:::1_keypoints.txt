- Introduces a model that incorporates visual attention into CNNs via feedback connections.  
- Learns to selectively gate convolutional filter activations to allocate resources to salient features.  
- Trains the attention mechanism with a reinforcement learning policy for attending to visual cues.  
- Elegant synthesis of existing components (CNNs, feedback, RL) yielding a principled approach.  
- Strong empirical validation: achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR benchmark.  
- Provides evidence that top-down attentional feedback is important beyond pure feed-forward architectures.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, model description, and result interpretation.  
- Primary limitation is scalability; results are limited to CIFAR and need testing on larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Authors should discuss potential challenges and computational considerations for ImageNet-scale application.  
- Overall: a high-quality proof of concept for attention and feedback in vision models; recommend acceptance.
