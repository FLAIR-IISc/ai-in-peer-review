The authors have proposed a CNN that incorporates a form of top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback. The results of the cipher dataset show state-of-the-art performance.

This paper is generally readable and demonstrates innovation in model design and vision problem solving. It is forward-looking in considering both bottom-up and top-down signals for a CNN training. I particularly liked the aspect of using gated signals to limit model evaluation and representation over time. Since the quality of the learned representations depends on the attention principle, it would be more interesting if the authors could provide or demonstrate some examples where the algorithm might fail.

The experiments are interesting, and the authors have demonstrated the model in various ways, such as visualization for better understanding. However, since the method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I think this paper could be further improved if ILSVRCs were available, so that its scalability for larger datasets and larger images can truly be demonstrated. That would be my main (and probably only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs creates an interesting model, which is worthy of further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets will be prioritized.
