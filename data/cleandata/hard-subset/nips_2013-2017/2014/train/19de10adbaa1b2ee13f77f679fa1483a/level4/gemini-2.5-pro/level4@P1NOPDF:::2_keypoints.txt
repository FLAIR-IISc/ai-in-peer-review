- Introduces a selective attention mechanism in CNNs and trains gating parameters to optimize classification performance.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep networks for limited computation or multiple tasks.  
- Achieves impressive classification accuracy, suggesting benefits of recurrent computation over fixed feed-forward models.  
- Method remains a black box with little insight into why it is effective.  
- Analysis of learned image representations is superficial and does not explain the role of theta's mean and variance.  
- Represents an important step beyond fixed parameter feed-forward networks despite limited interpretability.  
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need further explanation.  
- Major comment: framework is more related to dynamical systems/RNNs than to reinforcement learning given immediate reward and deterministic policy.  
- Major comment: training may be direct optimization of the objective w.r.t. recurrent policy parameters rather than RL-style learning.  
- Major comment: analysis of example (cat image) is uninformative—why do layer 1 activations increase and does that affect the objective or is output invariant to scaling?  
- Major comment: suggest focused analyses—track gating evolution for a small image region and study hard cases where proposed model corrects baseline errors.  
- Major comment: propose experiments constraining theta’s mean/variance to reveal their effect.  
- Minor comment: Algorithm 1 uses h_M without definition and has unclear handling of F[i] and Θ[i] across loop iterations.  
- Minor comment: Eq. 11’s summation index is unclear.  
- Minor comment: Eq. 8 may need indexing by image (j).  
- Minor comment: Variables 'x' and 'd' after Eq. 11 are undefined.  
- Minor comment: Motivation for regularizing theta is unclear since theta is sampled from a prior.  
- Minor comment: Final optimization step and gradient computation are not well-explained or theoretically justified; unclear if adjusting hyperparameters of a posterior over policies.
