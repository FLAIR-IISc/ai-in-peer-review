- Introduces a selective attention mechanism in a CNN with trainable gating parameters to optimize classification.  
- Tackles adaptive configuration of deep networks for resource-limited or dynamic tasks.  
- Demonstrates strong classification performance, showing recurrent computation can improve feature discrimination.  
- Mechanism remains opaque—paper lacks clear explanation of why the method is effective.  
- Exposition mostly clear but several equations and algorithmic descriptions need clarification.  

- Novelty: adaptive, recurrent attention in feed-forward CNNs is a significant and timely contribution.  
- Empirical strength: high classification accuracy relative to standard methods validates the approach.  

- Conceptual framing: method is closer to RNNs/dynamical systems than RL; authors should discuss this connection.  
- Interpretability: paper provides limited insight into internal dynamics; deeper analysis is needed to explain model behavior.  
- Suggested analyses: trace gating variables over time in local regions; analyze cases where the model corrects baseline errors; ablate/constrain learned theta distribution parameters.  
- Training clarity: unclear how gradients w.r.t. policy parameters are computed after sampling theta; final optimization step needs detailed explanation.  
- Regularization motive: the need for additional regularization of theta is not justified—clarify whether the update is tuning posterior hyperparameters.  

- Algorithm 1: `h_M` undefined; unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated or overwritten in the inner loop.  
- Equation 11: summation index missing.  
- Equation 8: unclear if it applies per image—should be indexed (e.g., j).  
- Text after Eq.11: variables `x` and `d` used without definition.
