- Proposes Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), adding internal selective attention via feedback connections to deep neural nets.  
- Draws biological inspiration from feedback mechanisms in the human visual cortex for object recognition.  
- Enables iterative internal attention focusing on features, improving classification performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a thorough introduction and related-work review.  
- Clearly motivates limitations of traditional CNNs and benefits of feedback/attention.  
- Uses reinforcement learning to train dasNet, applying Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to optimize policy parameters.  
- SNES application is a novel, efficient approach for exploring a large policy parameter space.  
- Balances technical detail and high-level explanation, making the method accessible to readers.  
- Demonstrates technical soundness with experimental evidence, notably on difficult cases.  
- Significance: supports the importance of feedback and attention in visual processing and impacts future deep-learning research.  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate dasNet on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Suggested improvement: explore more complex policies to control the attention mechanism.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of dasNet dynamics and attention behavior over time.
