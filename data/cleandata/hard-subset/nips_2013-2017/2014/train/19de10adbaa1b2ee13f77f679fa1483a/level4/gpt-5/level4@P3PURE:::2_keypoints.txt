- Proposes augmenting CNNs with selective attention gates and training gating parameters to optimize classification; tackles adaptive reconfiguration under limited resources and multitask settings with impressive accuracy.
- Approach acts largely as a black box: it shows promising performance but offers little insight into why it works or what the model is learning.
- Analysis of learned image representations is uninformative and there is no clear interpretation of the learned theta parameters (mean and variance).
- Paper should more seriously explore connections to dynamical systems and recurrent computation, since the framework resembles recurrent nets more than reinforcement learning (immediate reward, deterministic policy).
- Analyze the “cat” image more tightly: explain why layer-1 activations increase in the first iteration, whether global scaling affects the objective, and track gating evolution in a small region.
- Examine hard cases (e.g., examples misclassified by standard Maxout but corrected by this model) and perform ablations or constraints to isolate the contribution of mean and variance of theta.
- Exposition is generally clear but contains typos and equations that require clearer explanations.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: define h_M, clarify if it collects observations, and specify whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored per-image or overwritten inside the loop.
- Equation 11 needs the domain of summation clarified; Equation 8 may need an image index (j) if intended per-image.
- Symbols after Equation 11 (“x” and “d”) are undefined and should be explained.
- The need for regularizing theta is unclear given prior sampling; the final gradient-based optimization after sampling/evaluation is poorly explained—how are gradients computed and what is the theoretical justification (e.g., are hyperparameters of a posterior being adjusted?).
- Overall: an interesting, meaningful step toward recurrent deep recognition with strong empirical results, but requires clearer explanation of learning dynamics, gating parameter roles, and ties to recurrent/dynamical system theory.
