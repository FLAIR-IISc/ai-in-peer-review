This paper proposes a convolutional neural network that incorporates top-down processing via attention-based feedback. The model iteratively modulates convolutional filter responses across time using learned gates, allowing the network to refine its internal representations over multiple passes on the same image. Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, it achieves state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented data.

The paper is engaging and presents a novel model design and application to vision. It is forward-looking in combining bottom-up and top-down signals within a CNN. I particularly appreciate the temporal evaluation of the model and the use of gated signals to constrain the representation. Because the quality of the learned representations depends critically on the attention policy, it would be more informative if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm fails.

The experiments are interesting, and the visualizations help interpret the model’s behavior. However, since the method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, the paper would be stronger with results on ILSVRC, to convincingly demonstrate its scalability to larger datasets and higher-resolution images. This is my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs is compelling and merits further study. Practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferred.
