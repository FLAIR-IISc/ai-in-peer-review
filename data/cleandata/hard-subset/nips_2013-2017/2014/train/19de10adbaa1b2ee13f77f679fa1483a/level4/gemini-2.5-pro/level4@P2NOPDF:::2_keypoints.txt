- Introduces a method for selective attention in CNNs by training gating parameters to optimize classification, addressing adaptive reconfiguration under resource/task constraints.  
- Empirical results show notable improvement in classification accuracy and advance beyond static feed-forward networks.  
- Exposition is generally clear and easy to follow.  
- Primary weakness: model operates as a "black box" with limited insight into mechanisms behind its success.  
- Analysis of learned image representations is uninformative and the roles of theta's mean and variance are unclear.  
- Major comment 1: The RL framing is tenuous — the method is better viewed as a dynamical/recurrent systems optimization rather than standard reinforcement learning.  
- Major comment 2: Insufficient analysis of model behavior — example: why do layer-1 activations uniformly increase in the first iteration and does this affect the objective?  
- Suggestion: visualize gating evolution over a focused image region to reveal attention dynamics.  
- Suggestion: analyze cases misclassified by the baseline but corrected by the proposed model to illustrate specific advantages.  
- Suggestion: ablate or constrain learned mean/variance of theta to isolate their contributions.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 ambiguous — undefined h_M and unclear intent of F[i] and Θ[i] updates (accumulate vs overwrite).  
- Minor: Equation 11 missing summation index — clarify what the sum ranges over.  
- Minor: Equation 8 appears defined for a single image but should be indexed by j to match algorithm.  
- Minor: Variables 'x' and 'd' are used without definition after Equation 11.  
- Minor: Motivation for additional regularization on theta is unclear given sampling from a prior already biases values.  
- Minor: Gradient computation for updating distribution parameters after sampling is insufficiently explained and lacks theoretical justification (e.g., are these hyperparameter/posterior updates?).
