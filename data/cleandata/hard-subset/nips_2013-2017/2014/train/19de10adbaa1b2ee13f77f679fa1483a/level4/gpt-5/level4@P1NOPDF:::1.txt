This paper proposes a convolutional network with feedback connections to learn visual attention, implemented by gating the activations of selected filters. The attention policy is trained with reinforcement learning on top of the convnet.

While no single component is particularly novel, the overall approach is well conceived and, in my view, represents the right direction for advancing vision models. The authors substantiate this claim with state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, providing strong empirical evidence that feedback and attention mechanisms are a promising next step.

The paper is of good quality and clearly written. Overall, this is a strong approach to vision: convnets augmented with RL-driven feedback attention yield compelling results.

I encourage the authors to push further on larger-scale problems such as ImageNet, if they have not already, to demonstrate scalability and broader impact.
