- Proposes a CNN with feedback connections that gate convolutional filters to implement visual attention.  
- Uses reinforcement learning to train the attention/gating mechanism on top of a standard convolutional backbone.  
- Individually the components are not novel, but their combination and application is compelling and promising.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR datasets, validating the feedback-driven attention concept.  
- Empirical results convincingly support that feedback-guided attention can improve convolutional network performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture, training procedure, and experiments.  
- Strength is the pragmatic, well-executed integration of RL and CNN that yields demonstrable improvements.  
- Suggests scaling to larger, more complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to test generalizability and behavior.  
- Recommends investigating the computational cost of feedback connections and methods for efficient implementation.  
- Overall judgment: strong contribution that warrants acceptance and advances visual attention research.
