- Introduces dasNet: CNN with feedback connections that modulate convolutional filter activations via learned gating for internal selective attention.  
- Policy controlling gates is optimized with reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) due to large action space.  
- Motivated by biological visual processing and recurrent/dynamic computation in the brain.  
- Implemented on top of a pre-trained Maxout network and demonstrated state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented).  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation, method, and experiments.  
- Use of SNES is justified and the algorithm is adequately described.  
- Experimental evaluation focuses on challenging classification instances and provides clear comparisons to prior work.  
- Visualizations of filter activation changes (Figure 3) are insightful, though interpreting them remains an open problem.  
- Strength: Novel combination of deep CNNs with RL-driven feedback attention, showing empirical gains.  
- Strength: Conceptually sound and clearly written with thorough experimental validation.  
- Weakness: Related-work discussion on recurrent neural networks and attention mechanisms is not comprehensive.  
- Weakness: Reinforcement learning phase is computationally expensive (≈4 days on a GTX 560 Ti) without discussion of mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to small datasets (CIFAR); extending to larger datasets like ImageNet is needed to strengthen claims.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
