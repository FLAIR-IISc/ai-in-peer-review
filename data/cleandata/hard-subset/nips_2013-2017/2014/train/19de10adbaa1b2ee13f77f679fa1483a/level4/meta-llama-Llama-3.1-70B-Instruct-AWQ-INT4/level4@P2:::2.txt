This paper proposes a novel approach to deep neural networks by introducing selective attention through feedback connections. The authors develop a method for training gating parameters to optimize the network's classification performance, which is an important problem in computer vision. The classification results are impressive, demonstrating a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art model on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the exposition is clear. However, the analysis of image representation is not very revealing, and it is unclear what the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are doing. The authors could provide more insight into how the model works and why it is effective.

One major concern is that the connection to dynamical systems is not thoroughly explored. The work seems closer to recurrent networks than to Reinforcement Learning, as the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic. The authors could discuss the implications of this connection and how it relates to the model's performance.

The analysis of the network processing the cat image is not very helpful in understanding how the new features of the model help recognition. The authors could provide more detailed analysis, such as focusing on a smaller region of the image and analyzing the evolution of the gating variables in that region. Additionally, examining difficult cases, such as those misclassified by the standard Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model, could provide more insight into the model's strengths and weaknesses.

Some minor comments and questions include:

* In Algorithm 1, where is h_M defined? Is that the step of collecting observations?
* In Algorithm 1, F[i] and \Theta[i] are inside the loop over images (j), but it is unclear if the image fitness values are collected in an array or overwritten on each iteration.
* In Eqn 11, what is the sum over? Is Eqn 8 for a single image, and should be indexed j?
* In the text after Eqn 11, what do 'x' and 'd' refer to?
* The regularization of theta is not clearly justified, as theta is already sampled from the prior and will tend to small values anyway. The authors could provide more explanation for this choice.
* The last step of the optimization (gradient updates on the parameters after having sampled and evaluated the fitness of thetas) is not well-explained. The authors could provide more detail on how the gradients are computed and justify this step theoretically.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition. The high classification accuracy suggests that recurrent computation helps extract or focus on more complex features of the images. However, the black box approach does not provide a clear understanding of why the method is effective.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to deep neural networks with selective attention through feedback connections.
* The classification results are impressive, demonstrating a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art model on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.
* The paper is well-written, and the exposition is clear.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The analysis of image representation is not very revealing, and it is unclear what the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are doing.
* The connection to dynamical systems is not thoroughly explored.
* The analysis of the network processing the cat image is not very helpful in understanding how the new features of the model help recognition.
* Some minor comments and questions, such as the definition of h_M and the regularization of theta, are not clearly addressed.
