- Introduces dasNet: a CNN extended with learned top-down feedback that iteratively modulates convolutional filters to refine predictions.  
- Implements internal selective attention as a policy trained with scalable natural evolution strategies (SNES) to handle a >1M-dimensional policy space.  
- Biologically motivated by recurrent and attentional processing in human visual cortex for disambiguation.  
- Applied on a strong Maxout baseline, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Highly original and significant idea: operationalizes sequential “internal thoughts” and dynamic recurrent processing in vision models.  
- Technically sound; choice of SNES is well-justified and experiments are thorough and convincing.  
- Clear presentation with helpful pseudocode and qualitative visualizations (e.g., filter activation changes) illustrating attention-driven corrections.  
- Analysis indicates gate values are informative, supporting that the policy learns meaningful control strategies.  
- Major weakness: scalability not demonstrated—no large-scale/high-resolution results (e.g., ImageNet) and CIFAR training already takes ~4 GPU days.  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes—no systematic examination of when attention fails or turns correct predictions incorrect.  
- Missing ablation study on policy observation components (average map activations, intermediate outputs, class probabilities) to justify design choices.  
- Overall: strong, novel contribution worthy of acceptance, tempered by concerns about scalability and broader practicality.
