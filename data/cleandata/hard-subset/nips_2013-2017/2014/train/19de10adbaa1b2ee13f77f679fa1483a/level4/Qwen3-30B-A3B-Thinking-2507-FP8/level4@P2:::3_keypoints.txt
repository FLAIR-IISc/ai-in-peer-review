- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with top-down feedback and iterative attention modulation during inference.  
- Uses a reinforcement learning policy optimized via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to adjust filter sensitivities across multiple passes.  
- Demonstrates ability to correct initial misclassifications by enhancing/suppressing features, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 without data augmentation.  
- Experimental quality is strong: 6% relative improvement, ablation on number of passes (T=5), attention visualizations, and gate-based information validation (40.7% 15-NN).  
- SNES implementation is technically sound but computationally expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and not contextualized against standard training costs.  
- Lacks failure analysis showing cases where dasNet cannot correct errors, weakening claims about policy robustness.  
- Clarity is generally good with effective figures, but Algorithm 1 and the observation function h(·)/ot construction need clearer specification for reproducibility.  
- Writing occasionally overly technical in places, which may impede broader readability.  
- Originality is strong: post-training SNES-learned feedback for attention modulation is novel relative to prior recurrent CNNs.  
- Significance is limited by missing large-scale validation (no ILSVRC/ImageNet results), leaving scalability and practical impact unproven.  
- Pros: novel mechanism, strong CIFAR results, insightful visualizations, biologically motivated, rigorous RL implementation.  
- Cons: no ImageNet validation, insufficient failure analysis, computational cost not benchmarked, and algorithmic details unclear.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on adding ImageNet results or justification, including failure cases, and clarifying algorithmic details for reproducibility.
