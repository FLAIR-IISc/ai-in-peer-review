- Proposes a convnet with feedback connections that gate filter activations to implement visual attention.  
- Feedback mechanism enables selective focusing on relevant features via filter gating.  
- Uses reinforcement learning on top of the convnet to learn adaptive attention policies.  
- Novelty lies in the combination of feedback, gating, and RL rather than in new individual components.  
- Approach is well-motivated, effectively implemented, and compelling.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, supporting the method's efficacy.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow, with solid technical exposition.  
- Results strongly support the authors' claims and suggest promise for complex vision tasks.  
- Suggests future work applying the method to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to test scalability.
