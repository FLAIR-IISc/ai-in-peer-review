- Novel CNN architecture integrating top-down attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
- Model actively incorporates both bottom-up and top-down signals during training, offering a forward-looking approach to vision tasks.
- Strong evaluation framework with temporal analysis of representation evolution and gated signals to constrain feature learning.
- Visualizations enhance interpretability and make the feedback mechanism more accessible.
- Combining attention-driven feedback with CNNs is novel and well-motivated, addressing a gap in current vision models.
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of model limitations, particularly the attention policy’s reliability and failure scenarios.
- Quality of learned features depends on the attention mechanism; identifying cases where it fails would inform robustness.
- Absence of results on large-scale benchmarks (e.g., ILSVRC-2012) is a notable gap and raises scalability concerns.
- The method introduces significant complexity over feedforward CNNs, and scalability to high-resolution, large-scale datasets is unverified.
- Authors should report ILSVRC performance and provide ablation studies on attention robustness to strengthen claims.
- Writing is clear and technical contributions are well-articulated; the work merits publication after addressing scalability and attention limitations.
- The model is a meaningful step toward more biologically plausible, adaptive vision systems, but real-world viability remains speculative without larger-scale results.
