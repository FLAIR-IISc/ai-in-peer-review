This paper introduces a selective attention mechanism to a convolutional neural network and proposes a method for training gating parameters to optimize the network's classification performance. The work addresses a crucial problem in computer vision, namely, how to adaptively reconfigure complex, deep processing stages when resources are limited and different tasks need to be solved. The classification results are impressive, but the approach is largely a black box, providing useful directions for future research without revealing the underlying reasons for its effectiveness. The analysis of image representation is not particularly insightful, and it is unclear what the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) represent.

Despite these limitations, the work is an essential step beyond the current paradigm of large, feed-forward networks with fixed parameters after learning. The exposition is clear, although some equations contain typos or require further explanation.

Major Comments:

The authors should explore the connection to dynamical systems in more depth. The work appears to be more closely related to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning, as the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic. Training involves traversing the space of recurrent "policy" parameters theta to maximize the observed objective function. A more thorough analysis of this connection could provide valuable insights into the proposed method.

The analysis of the network processing the cat image is not particularly helpful in understanding how the new features of the model contribute to recognition. For instance, why are layer 1 activations all increased at the first iteration? Does this affect the objective function, or is the output invariant to global scalings? A more informative approach might be to focus on a smaller region of the image and analyze the evolution of the gating variables in that region. Additionally, examining difficult cases, such as those misclassified by the standard Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model, or teasing out the effect of the learned model parameters (mean and variance of theta) by constraining them further, could be instructive.

Minor Comments:

In Algorithm 1, the definition of h_M is missing. Is this the step where observations are collected? Furthermore, F[i] and \Theta[i] are inside the loop over images (j), but it is unclear whether the image fitness values are collected in an array or overwritten on each iteration.

Equation 11 requires clarification on the sum. Is Equation 8 for a single image and should be indexed j? The text following Equation 11 is also unclear, as the variables 'x' and 'd' are not defined.

The regularization of theta is not well-justified, as theta is already sampled from the prior and tends to small values. The last step of the optimization, involving gradient updates on the parameters after sampling and evaluating the fitness of thetas, is not well-explained and lacks theoretical justification. Specifically, it is unclear how the gradients are computed and whether the approach is equivalent to adjusting hyper-parameters of the posterior over policies.

The proposed approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition is interesting, and the high classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation can help extract or focus on more complex features of images. However, the lack of transparency in the approach makes it difficult to understand why it is effective.
