

This paper introduces selective attention into convolutional neural networks by developing a method to train gating parameters that optimize classification performance. It addresses a significant challenge in computer vision: adaptively reconfiguring complex, deep processing stages under resource constraints for diverse tasks. The classification results are impressive, but the method remains largely a black box—it suggests promising directions yet fails to explain why it works. The analysis of image representations provides little insight, and the roles of the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) remain unclear. Nevertheless, this work represents an important advancement beyond the current paradigm of large, fixed-parameter feedforward networks. The exposition is generally clear, though some equations contain typographical errors or require better explanation.

**Major Comments:**  
1.  The authors should rigorously explore the connection to dynamical systems. The approach more closely resembles recurrent network paradigms than reinforcement learning, as the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic. Training involves traversing the space of recurrent "policy" parameters θ to maximize the objective function, a perspective that merits deeper discussion.  
2.  The analysis of the cat image processing is unhelpful for understanding the model's improvements. Specifically:  
    - Why do layer 1 activations uniformly increase at the first iteration?  
    - Does this global scaling affect the objective function, or is the output invariant to such scaling?  
    - Focusing on a smaller image region and analyzing the evolution of gating variables within it could be instructive.  
    - Examining misclassified cases (e.g., examples correctly classified by the proposed model but not by the standard Maxout network) or constraining learned parameters (mean/variance of θ) to isolate their effects would strengthen the analysis.

**Minor Comments:**  
- **Algorithm 1:** Define $h_M$ (is this the step for collecting observations?). Clarify whether $F[i]$ and $\Theta[i]$ are accumulated in an array or overwritten per image $j$ within the loop.  
- **Equation 11:** Specify the summation index. Should Equation 8 (for a single image) be indexed as $j$?  
- **Text after Equation 11:** Define the roles of $x$ and $d$.  
- **Theta regularization:** The necessity of regularization is unclear, as $\theta$ is already sampled from a prior that induces small values. Additionally, the final optimization step (gradient updates on parameters after sampling/evaluating $\theta$) lacks theoretical justification and a clear explanation of gradient computation.  

This is an interesting approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition. The high classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests recurrent computation enhances feature extraction or focus, but the lack of explanatory power prevents understanding why the method is effective.
