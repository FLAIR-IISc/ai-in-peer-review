This paper proposes a CNN that incorporates top-down processing via attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets. The work is engaging and novel in both model design and its application to vision. It is forward-looking in its integration of bottom-up and top-down signals for training, and I particularly appreciate the temporal evaluation of the model and the use of gated signals to constrain the learned representations.

Because the quality of the learned representations hinges on the attention policy, it would be valuable for the authors to suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm may fail—e.g., scenarios where the attention mechanism misguides the network or where the feedback does not improve (or even harms) performance. Such analysis would clarify the model’s limitations and robustness.

The experiments are interesting and include helpful visualizations that improve interpretability. However, the method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, and the paper would be significantly strengthened by results on ILSVRC to demonstrate scalability to larger datasets and higher-resolution images. This is my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs is compelling and worth further study, but more practical insights from experiments on larger-scale data would make the contribution more convincing.
