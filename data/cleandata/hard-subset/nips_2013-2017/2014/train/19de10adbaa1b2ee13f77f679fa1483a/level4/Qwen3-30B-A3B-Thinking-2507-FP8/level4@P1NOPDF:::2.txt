

This paper introduces selective attention into convolutional neural networks by developing a method to train gating parameters that optimize classification performance. It tackles a significant challenge in computer vision: adaptively reconfiguring complex deep processing stages under resource constraints for diverse tasks. While the classification results are impressive, the approach remains largely a black box—it suggests promising directions but fails to explain why it works. The analysis of image representations proves unilluminating, and there is no insight into the role of the learned parameters (mean and variance of the θ distribution). Nevertheless, this work represents an important advance beyond the current paradigm of fixed-parameter, feed-forward networks. The exposition is clear, though some equations contain typos or require better explanation.

**Major Comments:**  
The authors should rigorously explore the connection to dynamical systems. The work bears stronger resemblance to recurrent networks than to reinforcement learning, as the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic. Training entails traversing the space of recurrent "policy" parameters θ to maximize the objective function. This perspective could clarify the method's mechanics and theoretical foundation.  

The analysis of the cat image does not elucidate how the model's novel features improve recognition. Specifically:  
- Why do layer 1 activations uniformly increase at the first iteration?  
- Does this scaling affect the objective function, or is the output invariant to global scaling?  
Focusing on a smaller image region and tracking gating variable evolution there would be instructive. Additionally, examining misclassified examples (where standard Maxout fails but the proposed model succeeds) or isolating the effect of θ’s mean/variance by constraining them would provide deeper insights.  

**Minor Comments:**  
- **Algorithm 1:** What is h_M? Is it the observation collection step? F[i] and Θ[i] are defined inside the image loop (j)—are fitness values stored in an array or overwritten per iteration?  
- **Equation 11:** What is the summation over? Should Equation 8 (for a single image) be indexed by j?  
- **Text after Equation 11:** What do 'x' and 'd' denote?  
- **Regularization of θ:** Why is regularization necessary if θ is already sampled from a prior and thus tends toward small values? The final optimization step (gradient updates on parameters after sampling θ and evaluating fitness) lacks clear explanation and theoretical justification—e.g., are hyperparameters of the posterior over policies being adjusted?  

This is a promising approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition. The superior classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests recurrent computation enhances feature extraction or focus on complex image structures. However, the black-box nature of the method prevents understanding *why* it is effective, limiting its broader impact. The work’s value hinges on resolving these interpretability gaps while clarifying the technical implementation details outlined above.
