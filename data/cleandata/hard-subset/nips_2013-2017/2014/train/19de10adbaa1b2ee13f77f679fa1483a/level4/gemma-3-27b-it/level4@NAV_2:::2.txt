This paper introduces a deep convolutional neural network architecture, dasNet, incorporating internal selective attention via feedback connections, and a novel approach to training the associated gating parameters using reinforcement learning to improve classification performance. It addresses a significant problem in computer vision: adapting complex, deep processing stages to efficiently handle diverse tasks. The reported classification results are promising, demonstrating improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets. However, the mechanisms driving these improvements remain largely opaque; while the approach is a valuable step beyond static, feedforward networks, further analysis is needed to understand *why* it works. The exposition is generally clear, though some equations require further clarification and a few appear to contain typographical errors.

**Major Comments:**

The authors should consider exploring connections to dynamical systems theory. The proposed method shares characteristics with recurrent neural networks, particularly given the deterministic policy and immediate reward signal, suggesting training involves navigating a space of recurrent “policy” parameters to maximize an objective function. Framing the work within this context could offer valuable insights. 

The analysis of the cat image classification example is not particularly illuminating. The initial uniform increase in layer 1 activations raises questions about the robustness of the system to global scalings and its impact on the objective function. A more focused analysis, perhaps examining a smaller image region and tracking the evolution of gating variables within that region, could provide a clearer understanding of the network’s behavior. Investigating challenging cases—those initially misclassified but subsequently corrected—or systematically perturbing the learned model parameters (e.g., constraining the mean and variance of θ) could also offer valuable insights.

**Minor Comments:**

Algorithm 1 description: The function h(M) is undefined; clarify its purpose (is it the observation collection step?). The loop over images (j) accumulates fitness values F[i] and policy parameters Θ[i]. Confirm whether these represent cumulative values or are overwritten in each iteration.

Equation 11: Clarify the summation; over what is it performed? Equation 8 appears to represent a loss for a single image and should potentially be indexed by ‘j’.

Text following Equation 11: Define the variables ‘x’ and ‘d’.

The necessity of regularizing θ is unclear, given that it is already sampled from a prior distribution which inherently favors smaller values. The gradient updates on the parameters following sampling and fitness evaluation are not adequately explained—specify how gradients are computed and provide a theoretical justification for this step (e.g., are these adjustments to hyperparameters of a posterior distribution over policies?).

Overall, this is an interesting approach to building deep recurrent networks for image recognition. The improved classification accuracy compared to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation enables the extraction or focusing on more intricate image features. However, the largely "black box" nature of the method hinders a complete understanding of its effectiveness.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to introducing recurrence and attention mechanisms into CNNs.
*   Demonstrated performance improvements on standard datasets (CIFAR-10/100).
*   Addresses an important open problem in computer vision: adaptive feature selection.
*   Clear and generally well-written exposition.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of theoretical understanding of *why* the method works.
*   Limited analysis of learned parameters and network dynamics.
*   Some ambiguity in the algorithmic description and equations.
*   The connection to reinforcement learning feels somewhat tenuous; exploring alternative frameworks (e.g., dynamical systems) might be more fruitful.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, although further analysis and clarification are needed. Claims are supported by experimental results, but a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms is lacking. The work represents a substantial contribution, but is not yet a complete, fully explained solution.

**Clarity:** The paper is largely well-written and organized, but some equations and algorithmic steps need further explanation.

**Originality:** The combination of feedback connections, selective attention, and reinforcement learning for CNNs is novel and represents a significant departure from conventional approaches.

**Significance:** The results are important and have the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in image recognition. The ability to adaptively focus attention within a deep network could have broad implications for other computer vision tasks.
