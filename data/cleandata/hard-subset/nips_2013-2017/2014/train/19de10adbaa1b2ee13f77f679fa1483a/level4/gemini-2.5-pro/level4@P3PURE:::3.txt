The authors propose a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) that incorporates top-down processing in the form of attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets.

This paper is interesting, well-written, and demonstrates novelty in its model design and application to computer vision. The approach of integrating both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN is forward-looking. I particularly appreciate the evaluation of the model over time and the use of a gated signal to constrain the learned representations. The experiments are insightful, and the inclusion of visualizations helps to better understand the model's behavior.

The paper could be strengthened by a discussion of the model's limitations. Since the quality of the learned representations hinges on the attention policy, it would be more interesting if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm might fail.

My primary concern is the model's demonstrated scalability. Given that the proposed architecture is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, its performance on the CIFAR datasets alone is not sufficient. To truly demonstrate the model's scalability and practical utility, the paper would be significantly improved by including results on a larger and more complex dataset, such as ILSVRC.

In summary, the combination of attention-based feedback and a CNN makes for an interesting model that is worthy of further study. However, more practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would make the contribution more compelling.
