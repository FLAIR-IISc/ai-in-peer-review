- Introduces dasNet: feedback-augmented CNNs that use an RL policy (SNES) to iteratively modulate filters during inference.  
- Trains policy post hoc via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to refine classifications across multiple passes on the same image.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (6% relative improvement) and CIFAR-100 with visualizations showing attention gates correcting misclassifications.  
- Novelty: combining SNES for large-scale policy learning with feedback-driven attention is a new, distinguishing contribution.  
- Technical quality: SNES choice and experimental design (λboost, T=5) are well-justified; paper presents reproducible metrics and implementation.  
- Significance: Demonstrates that top-down feedback/"internal thought" can correct initial CNN errors and provides interpretability of filter dynamics.  
- Clarity weakness: The observation space h(M_t) is described vaguely—need a concrete example of how average and intermediate activations are concatenated.  
- Visualization shortcoming: Fig. 3 is insightful but lacks quantitative analysis of which filters or layers are most influential.  
- Scalability concern: High computational cost (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and no experiments on larger datasets (ImageNet/ILSVRC) leave practical applicability unproven.  
- Ablation gap: Missing baselines isolating the attention effect (e.g., identity policy) to quantify how much improvement comes from feedback vs SNES.  
- Regularization question: The use and justification of λ_l2 on θ_i is unclear; alternative regularization (weight decay) not discussed.  
- Pro summary: Strong, original, biologically motivated approach with solid results and interpretability on CIFAR.  
- Con summary: Lacks large-scale validation, incomplete ablations, unclear observation details, and computational-cost concerns.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept with major revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add larger-dataset experiments (ImageNet/STL-10), include attention-isolation ablations, clarify h(M_t), and provide quantitative filter analysis.
