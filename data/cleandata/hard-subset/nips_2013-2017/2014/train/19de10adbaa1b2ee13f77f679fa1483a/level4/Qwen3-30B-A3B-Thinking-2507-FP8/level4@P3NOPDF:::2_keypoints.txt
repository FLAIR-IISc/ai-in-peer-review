- Introduces trainable gating parameters to integrate selective attention into CNNs for resource-constrained classification.  
- Tackles dynamic reconfiguration of deep processing stages instead of fixed monolithic architectures.  
- Empirical results show significant accuracy gains over standard CNNs on benchmarks.  
- Method is largely opaque: lacks mechanistic interpretability explaining why it works.  
- Analysis of learned parameters (μ_θ, σ_θ) is superficial and lacks concrete interpretation.  
- Unclear how learned θ influences feature selection or captures invariances/noise robustness.  
- Global analyses (e.g., Figure 5 cat example) are uninformative; local gating dynamics and misclassification transitions need study.  
- The approach aligns more with dynamical systems than RL; authors should reframe and justify gradient-based optimization accordingly.  
- Suggest analyzing hard examples where standard CNNs fail and visualizing gating/activations to show how attention resolves ambiguity.  
- Recommend isolating parameter effects (e.g., fix σ_θ) to quantify each parameter’s contribution.  
- Advise explicitly describing the model as a recurrent/dynamical feature processor to clarify theory.  
- Minor technical fixes: define h_M in Algorithm 1 and clarify storage/overwriting of F[i] and Θ[i].  
- Equation issues: specify summation index for Eq.11, confirm per-image indexing for Eq.8, and define x (input) and d (label).  
- Regularization on θ is unclear—clarify if it’s a KL term in a variational setup or an ad hoc L2 penalty.  
- Explain gradient computation through sampling (reparameterization trick vs score function estimator) and justify theoretical soundness.  
- Overall: promising, empirically strong and clear exposition, but needs deeper interpretability and theoretical grounding; recommend revise-and-resubmit.
