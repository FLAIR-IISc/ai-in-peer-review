- Integrates selective attention into a CNN by training gating parameters to improve classification performance.
- Addresses important problem of adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under resource constraints and task demands.
- Achieves impressive classification results but operates largely as a black box with little explanation of why it works.
- Analysis of learned image representations is uninformative; the roles of theta’s mean and variance are unclear.
- Represents an important step beyond fixed, feed-forward networks toward recurrent/adaptive computation.
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos and sections that need better explanation.
- Major: The method appears closer to dynamical/recurrent systems than to reinforcement learning and should be analyzed as such.
- Major: Because reward is immediate and policy deterministic, training effectively searches the space of recurrent policy parameters (theta).
- Major: The cat-image analysis is not insightful; need focused analyses (e.g., local image regions and gating evolution) to explain how features aid recognition.
- Major: Study challenging cases (misclassified by Maxout but corrected by this model) to reveal what the model gains.
- Major: Constrain or ablate theta’s mean and variance to determine their specific effects on behavior.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 is unclear—h_M is undefined and it’s not specified whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored or overwritten per image.
- Minor: Equation 11’s summation index is unclear; Equation 8 likely should be indexed by image j.
- Minor: Symbols after Equation 11 (x and d) are undefined and need clarification.
