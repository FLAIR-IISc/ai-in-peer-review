

This paper proposes a CNN with feedback connections for learning visual attention, implemented through filter-level gating of activations. The model integrates reinforcement learning on top of a standard CNN architecture to optimize attention mechanisms. While the individual components (CNN backbone, RL training, gating mechanism) are not novel, the integration represents a compelling and well-executed approach to modeling attention in vision. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, significantly supporting the premise that feedback-driven attention is a promising direction for future vision systems. The technical presentation is clear and well-structured, with results convincingly validating the core hypothesis. The methodology is sound, and the results provide strong empirical evidence that RL-driven feedback mechanisms effectively enhance visual attention. The work is a solid contribution that effectively bridges reinforcement learning and attention modeling in CNNs. While the current results on CIFAR are impressive, extending the approach to larger-scale benchmarks like ImageNet would further strengthen the case for its broader applicability. If not already explored, this extension would be a valuable next step to assess performance on more complex visual recognition tasks. Overall, this is a high-quality, well-motivated study that delivers strong empirical results and offers a clear, actionable path forward for attention-based vision models.
