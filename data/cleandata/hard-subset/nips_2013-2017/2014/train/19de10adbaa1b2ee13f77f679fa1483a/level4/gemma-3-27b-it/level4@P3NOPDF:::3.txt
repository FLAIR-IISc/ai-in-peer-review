This paper introduces a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture incorporating top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets, representing a significant contribution to the field.

The work is generally compelling and demonstrates both novelty in model design and a promising application to computer vision. The consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signals for CNN training is a forward-looking approach, and I particularly appreciate the use of temporal evaluation and gated signals to constrain learned representations. Given that the quality of these representations is directly tied to the attention policy, a discussion of potential failure modes – scenarios where the algorithm might struggle or produce suboptimal results – would strengthen the paper. Exploring such limitations would provide a more nuanced understanding of the method’s capabilities.

The experimental analysis is well-executed and insightful, with visualizations effectively aiding comprehension of the model’s behavior. However, the increased complexity of this approach compared to traditional feedforward CNNs raises questions about its scalability. While the CIFAR results are strong, the paper would be significantly improved by demonstrating performance on the ImageNet (ILSVRC) dataset. This would provide crucial evidence of the model's ability to handle larger, more complex datasets with higher-resolution images, and better justify the added architectural complexity. 

Currently, the absence of ILSVRC results is my primary concern. Demonstrating scalability is essential for establishing the practical utility of this method. Despite this, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs creates a genuinely interesting model deserving of further investigation. Expanding the experimental evaluation to include larger datasets would yield more practical insights and further solidify the contribution. Overall, this is a strong paper with the potential to significantly impact the field, contingent on addressing the scalability concern.
