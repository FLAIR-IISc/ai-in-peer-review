A convolutional network augmented with feedback connections to implement internal selective attention. The feedback operates by gating the activations of selected filters. The attention mechanism is trained via reinforcement learning on top of a pretrained convnet. While no single component is especially novel, the overall formulation is, in my view, compelling and the right direction to pursue. As expected, the method achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR. This is meaningful evidence that feedback/attention is a promising next step. The paper’s quality and clarity are strong. In sum, this is a solid vision approach—convnets with RL-driven feedback attention—that delivers good performance. If possible, the authors should extend the evaluation to larger benchmarks such as ImageNet.
