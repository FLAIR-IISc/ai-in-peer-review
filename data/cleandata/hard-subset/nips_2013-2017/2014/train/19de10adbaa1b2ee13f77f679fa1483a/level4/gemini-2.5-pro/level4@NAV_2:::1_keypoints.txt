- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback connections that dynamically modulate convolutional filter activity over multiple passes as an internal selective attention mechanism.  
- Uses a pre-trained Maxout CNN as the base and trains a high-dimensional attention policy via reinforcement learning with Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Motivated by recurrent and top-down processing in primate visual cortex to move beyond purely feedforward vision models.  
- Significance: addresses the static, single-pass limitation of standard CNNs by enabling iterative refinement and biologically-plausible attention.  
- Performance: achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation, validating the approach.  
- Approach: novel and well-executed synthesis of a pre-trained CNN with an RL-based attention policy; SNES is a practical choice for million+ parameter policies.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly motivates the problem, explains the architecture/training, and ties to neuroscience.  
- Computational cost: iterative processing and separate RL training add substantial overhead and raise scalability concerns for larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Limited interpretability: lacks deeper quantitative analysis of learned attention policies (e.g., which layers/filters are modulated per class).  
- Overall: high-quality, effective, and promising work; next important step is demonstrating scalability to larger, more complex vision problems.
