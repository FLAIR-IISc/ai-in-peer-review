- Proposes a selective attention gating mechanism in a CNN and a method to train gating parameters to improve classification performance.  
- Tackles the important problem of adaptively reconfiguring deep processing under resource/task constraints.  
- Classification results are strong but the method behaves like a black box with little explanation for its effectiveness.  
- Analysis of image representations is weak and not particularly insightful.  
- The learned parameter meanings (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are unclear.  
- The work is an important step beyond fixed, feed-forward networks.  
- Exposition is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need further explanation.  
- Major methodological connection to dynamical/recurrent systems is underexplored; framing as RL seems questionable.  
- Training appears to traverse recurrent “policy” parameters deterministically; this connection needs deeper analysis.  
- The cat-image analysis is unhelpful—questions remain (e.g., why layer-1 activations all increase) and whether outputs are scale-invariant.  
- Recommend analyzing local image regions, gating variable evolution, and cases where the model corrects standard-network errors.  
- Suggest experiments constraining or probing the learned theta mean/variance to reveal their roles.  
- Algorithm 1 has omissions (h_M undefined) and unclear handling/storage of F[i] and Θ[i] across images.  
- Equations need clarification (sum in Eq.11, indexing in Eq.8, undefined variables x and d).  
- Regularization of θ and the final gradient-update step lack theoretical justification; unclear how gradients are computed or if this adjusts posterior hyper-parameters.  
- Overall: recurrent computation for recognition is promising, but the lack of transparency limits understanding and interpretability.
