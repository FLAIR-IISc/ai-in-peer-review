- Paper introduces a selective attention mechanism in a CNN and a novel training procedure for attention-gating parameters.  
- Addresses adaptive configuration of deep processing under resource constraints and varying tasks.  
- Reports strong classification performance but provides limited explanation of underlying mechanisms.  
- Analysis of image representations is shallow and does not clarify how new features aid recognition.  
- Roles of learned parameters (mean and variance of theta) are unclear and unexplored.  
- Represents a meaningful advance beyond static feed-forward architectures.  
- Presentation is generally good but several equations contain typos or need clarification.  

Major comments:
- Recommend investigating links to dynamical systems and framing the method as recurrent rather than RL given deterministic, immediate rewards.  
- Training can be seen as navigating a space of recurrent “policy” parameters (theta) to maximize the objective.  
- The cat-image analysis fails to show how novel features contribute; the uniform increase in layer‑1 activations needs investigation (impact on objective or output sensitivity).  
- Suggest focused analysis tracking gating variables in a small image region over time.  
- Recommend examining hard cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected by this model) to illustrate benefits.  
- Propose systematic tests constraining mean/variance of theta to evaluate their effects.  

Minor comments:
- Algorithm 1: h_M is undefined; role of F[i] and Θ[i] inside the image loop is ambiguous (accumulated or overwritten?).  
- Equation 11: summation scope unclear; Equation 8 appears written for a single image and should be indexed by j.  
- Text after Equation 11: referents for ‘x’ and ‘d’ are undefined.  
- Unclear why theta needs regularization given its prior; final gradient-update step after sampling lacks explanation or theoretical justification.  

- Overall: intriguing recurrent approach with improved accuracy, but limited interpretability leaves the reason for its success unresolved.
