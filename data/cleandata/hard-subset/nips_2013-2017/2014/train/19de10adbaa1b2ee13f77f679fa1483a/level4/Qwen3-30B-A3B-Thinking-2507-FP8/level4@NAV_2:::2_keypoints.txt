- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that modulate lower-layer filter sensitivities via a policy learned with SNES to enable iterative selective attention during classification.  
- DasNet refines predictions over multiple passes without changing CNN weights by emphasizing/suppressing convolutional filters.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 (un-augmented), outperforming prior methods by ~6%.  
- Builds on prior work (Maxout networks, feedback-driven vision) but uniquely applies scalable evolutionary optimization to learn attention policies in high-dimensional spaces.  
- Strength: significant empirical improvement in challenging cases without data augmentation, advancing benchmark performance.  
- Strength: rigorous experiments with clear ablation (e.g., T=5) and quantitative comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Strength: architecture and training procedure are generally well explained, aside from minor notation issues.  
- Weakness: originality limited by under-theorized reliance on SNES instead of RL and insufficient discussion of connections to dynamical/recurrent systems.  
- Weakness: policy is a "black box" with limited interpretability; analyses (e.g., cat/dog example) do not explain why filter changes improve recognition.  
- Weakness: key notational/definition gaps (meaning of "d" in Eq.10, summation index in Eq.11, necessity of L2 regularization for θ) need clarification.  
- Major: method resembles recurrent/dynamical processing more than standard RL; paper should discuss links to dynamical systems and recurrent CNN literature.  
- Major: require quantitative analyses linking layer-specific gating variations to accuracy gains, testing loss-invariance of global activation scaling, and analyzing corrected misclassifications.  
- Major: the role of the policy distribution parameters (mean/variance θ) is unclear; constrained analyses (e.g., fix variance) are needed to show they encode meaningful attention.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 omits definition of observation function h(·); specify its components (Avg(y_j), ȳ_j, v_t).  
- Minor: Eq.11 sum index should be over images (j), not policy samples (i).  
- Minor: Eq.8 “d” denotes correct class label and should be clarified.  
- Minor: L2 regularization on θ lacks theoretical justification given θ is sampled from a prior; explain its necessity to prevent overfitting in policy space.  
- Overall: dasNet is a significant empirical contribution demonstrating scalable feedback attention improves classification, but lacks mechanistic insight into how the policy enhances recognition.
