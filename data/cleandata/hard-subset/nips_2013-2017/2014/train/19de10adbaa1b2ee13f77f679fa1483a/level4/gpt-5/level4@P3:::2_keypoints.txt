- Introduces dasNet: a Maxout CNN augmented with multiplicative feature-map gates adjusted over multiple passes via a learned feedback policy.  
- Policy maps observations (per-layer activation summaries + class probs) to gating actions and is trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Demonstrates measurable gains on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline and reports state-of-the-art unaugmented test-time results.  
- Novelty: internal, feature-map-level attention applied to a high-performing CNN at CIFAR scale, with SNES used for million-parameter policy learning.  
- Strength: clear and interesting idea of learned feedback re-weighting features over time to fix initial mistakes.  
- Strength: technically straightforward augmentation that integrates cleanly with existing CNNs.  
- Strength: strong empirical results without test-time ensembling or multi-view augmentation.  
- Strength: practical policy search via SNES for a very high-dimensional controller; simple training procedure.  
- Weakness: limited interpretability—policy dynamics and what is learned remain largely a black box.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in the learning signal—closer to deterministic policy optimization than standard RL, not fully explored.  
- Weakness: missing ablations comparing simpler controllers, gradient-based policy learning, and end-to-end BPTT through feedback.  
- Weakness: algorithmic details need clarification (indexing, definitions, gradient updates, role of regularization).  
- Weakness: computational cost vs. gain not quantified (extra passes, wall-clock overhead, parameter counts).  
- Quality: approach is sound and motivated; SNES choice is reasonable given parameter count but comparisons to gradient methods are lacking.  
- Quality: results support claims, and some dynamics analysis is shown, but deeper diagnostics (gate convergence, stability, per-layer contributions) are missing.  
- Clarity: paper reads well but several equations/algorithms need fixes; h(·) should be defined when introduced.  
- Originality: advancing recurrent/attention-driven CNNs by combining internal attention with SNES at CIFAR scale is novel.  
- Significance: shows learned recurrence/attention can improve strong feedforward baselines and likely influences recurrent CNN research.  
- Suggestion: recast dasNet as a recurrent system and analyze stability, fixed points, and sensitivity (Jacobian), plus gate statistics.  
- Suggestion: improve interpretability with aggregate analyses on corrected examples, per-layer gate norms vs. confidence change, and layer importance.  
- Ablation suggestions: compare policy capacities (linear vs. hidden layer), SNES vs. gradient-based learning, subset observations, and alternative action constraints.  
- Baseline suggestions: compare to a post-hoc logit corrector MLP and to fine-tuning the base CNN with matched parameter budgets.  
- Minor fixes: explicitly define h(M), clarify Algorithm 1 fitness accumulation, fix equation indexing, and clarify symbols after Eq. 11.  
- Minor fixes: explain L2 regularization rationale, detail the SNES update variant and fitness shaping, discuss action normalization effects, and report computational overhead.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, effective integration of learned feedback; SOTA CIFAR results; timely contribution on recurrence in vision.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited interpretability/analysis, missing ablations and gradient-baseline comparisons, and insufficient quantification of computational trade-offs.
