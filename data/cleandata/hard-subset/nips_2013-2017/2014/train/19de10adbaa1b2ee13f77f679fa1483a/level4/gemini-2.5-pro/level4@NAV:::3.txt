This work introduces a novel CNN architecture that integrates attention-based feedback to facilitate top-down processing. On the CIFAR datasets, the proposed model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance.

The paper is well-written, and its contribution is novel in both its model architecture and its application to computer vision. The conceptual framework, which considers both bottom-up and top-down signals for training, represents a promising research direction. The temporal evaluation of the model and the use of a gated signal to constrain learned representations are particularly compelling aspects. To further strengthen the analysis, it would be beneficial for the authors to discuss potential failure modes, especially since the quality of the representations is critically dependent on the attention policy.

The experimental validation is thorough and includes insightful visualizations that aid in understanding the model's behavior. However, a primary concern is the model's scalability. Given its increased complexity relative to standard feedforward networks, the paper would be significantly improved by including results on a large-scale benchmark like ILSVRC. This would be necessary to truly validate its effectiveness on larger datasets with higher-resolution images and represents my main reservation. Nonetheless, the combination of attention-based feedback with CNNs is an interesting model that warrants further investigation, though more practical insights from experiments on larger-scale data are needed.
