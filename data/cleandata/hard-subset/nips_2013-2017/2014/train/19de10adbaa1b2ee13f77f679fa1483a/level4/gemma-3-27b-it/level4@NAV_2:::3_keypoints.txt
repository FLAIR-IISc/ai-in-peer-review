- Proposes dasNet: a novel CNN with top-down, attention-based feedback that dynamically adjusts filter sensitivities for selective attention.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the attentional policy, addressing large parameter-space challenges.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Visualizations (Figure 3) show how filter activations change over time, providing insight into network behavior.  
- Dynamic analysis (Figure 2) demonstrates the network can correct misclassifications via attention.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and reinforcement-learning ideas, and aligns with biologically-inspired attention literature.  
- Strength: bridges biologically plausible attention mechanisms with practical CNN improvements, enhancing robustness and interpretability.  
- Major weakness: limited experimental scale—no results on larger datasets like ImageNet to demonstrate scalability/generalizability.  
- Missing detailed analysis of computational cost and memory requirements introduced by feedback connections.  
- Lacks discussion of potential failure modes or situations where the learned attention policy may be harmful.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing scalability concerns through additional experiments or deeper theoretical analysis.
