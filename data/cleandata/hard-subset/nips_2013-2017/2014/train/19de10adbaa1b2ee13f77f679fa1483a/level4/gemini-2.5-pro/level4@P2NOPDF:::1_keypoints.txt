- Proposes a CNN architecture with feedback/top-down connections that dynamically gate convolutional filters to implement visual attention.
- Uses a reinforcement learning agent on top of the convolutional base to learn a policy for controlling the attention gates.
- Strength lies in an elegant, principled integration of convolutional networks, attention, and RL to provide goal-driven top-down attention in typically feed-forward vision systems.
- Strong empirical validation: achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR datasets, supporting the effectiveness of the approach.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed method.
- Main limitation/suggestion: needs evaluation on larger, more diverse datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to test scalability and generalizability.
- Overall, a high-quality, valuable contribution highlighting the importance of feedback and attention beyond purely feed-forward architectures.
