This paper presents a convolutional neural network that incorporates feedback connections to learn visual attention. The model is trained using a reinforcement learning framework, where the feedback mechanism operates by gating the activations of specific filters.

While the individual components are not novel in isolation, their synthesis into a cohesive system for visual attention is a well-conceived and promising approach. The effectiveness of this method is demonstrated by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. This strong empirical performance is significant, offering compelling evidence that integrating feedback and attention is a valuable direction for advancing deep vision models.

The paper's quality and clarity are good. To further strengthen the contribution, it would be beneficial for the authors to explore the scalability of their approach on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet.
