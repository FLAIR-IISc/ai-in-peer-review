- Introduces dasNet, a CNN augmented with feedback that iteratively re-processes images by modulating convolutional filter activations for internal selective attention.  
- Built on a pre-trained Maxout network; a control policy is trained on the frozen base using reinforcement learning and Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle a million-dimensional policy space.  
- The iterative "re-thinking" refines initial predictions and corrects misclassifications, helping on ambiguous or difficult cases.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Effectively combines ideas from neuroscience (recurrent processing), reinforcement learning, and deep learning.  
- Significance: Provides strong empirical evidence that moving beyond purely feedforward architectures yields tangible performance gains and will likely spur research on dynamic/attentional models.  
- Quality: Sound technical execution with a pragmatic two-stage training process, justified use of SNES, and supportive analyses (filter modulation visualizations, action information).  
- Clarity: Well-written and structured, with sufficient background and a clear Algorithm 1 summarizing training.  
- Originality: Components are individually known; main novelty lies in the non-trivial synthesis of existing methods into an effective system.  
- Scalability: Experiments restricted to CIFAR; concerns about scaling to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet), training time, and increased inference cost.  
- Further analysis needed: More comprehensive study on performance versus number of attention steps (T) and an ablation isolating the boosting scheme’s impact.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated, and effective contribution; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses and the paper is a strong candidate for acceptance.
