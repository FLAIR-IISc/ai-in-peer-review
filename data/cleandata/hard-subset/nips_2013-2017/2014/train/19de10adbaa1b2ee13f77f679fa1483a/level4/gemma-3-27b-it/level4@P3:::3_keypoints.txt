- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections implementing internal selective attention trained via SNES.  
- Novel architecture departs from purely feedforward designs, inspired by biological vision.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, especially on challenging instances.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and aligns with recent recurrent and attention-based vision work.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to train a high-dimensional attention policy, enabling scalable optimization.  
- Visualizations of filter activations across iterations provide insight into internal decision-making.  
- Lacks evaluation on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet), limiting claims about scalability and practical impact.  
- Paper does not analyze failure modes or scenarios where dasNet might struggle (including adversarial examples).  
- Adds architectural and training complexity; computational cost analysis compared to standard CNNs is insufficient.  
- Analysis of the gates’ information content is preliminary; deeper investigation of what gates encode is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong CIFAR results, practical SNES solution, and useful visualizations.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing large-scale evaluation and limited discussion of limitations/failure cases.  
- Overall: strong and innovative work with promising results but notable concerns about scalability and analysis of limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing scalability (add large-dataset results or justify infeasibility) and expanding failure-mode analysis.
