- Proposes dasNet: CNNs with internal selective attention via feedback connections that dynamically alter convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Uses reinforcement learning (Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies, SNES) to train the feedback/attentional policy.  
- Provides detailed architecture description including Maxout network, convolutional/pooling layers, and the RL framework.  
- Iterative attention mechanism lets the network focus on discriminative features to improve classification on difficult cases.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show effectiveness, with ~6% relative improvement over a vanilla CNN.  
- Offers thorough analysis and discussion of experimental results, including strengths and weaknesses.  
- Novel contribution: integrating feedback-based internal selective attention into deep CNNs.  
- Novel methodological angle: applying reinforcement learning to train internal attentional policies in CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Limited evaluation scope: tested only on CIFAR-10/100, lacking large-scale benchmarks like ImageNet.  
- Complexity concern: dasNet architecture and RL training may be difficult to implement and reproduce.  
- Recommendation: accept, with a suggestion to validate the approach on more challenging datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
