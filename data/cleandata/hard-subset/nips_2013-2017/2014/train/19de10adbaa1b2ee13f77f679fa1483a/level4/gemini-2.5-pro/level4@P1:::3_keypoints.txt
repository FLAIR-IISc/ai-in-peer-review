- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with top-down attention-based feedback that iteratively refines predictions by modulating convolutional filters.  
- Attention policy is trained with reinforcement learning (Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies) to correct difficult misclassifications.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Novel, well-written work that bridges feedforward CNNs and recurrent/top-down biological vision mechanisms.  
- Creative combination of a strong base model (Maxout CNN) with an RL-trained internal attention policy.  
- Dynamic, time-dependent design uses gated signals over multiple steps to refine representations.  
- Experiments include insightful analyses and visualizations (e.g., correcting "dog" → "cat" and filter activation maps).  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes—no discussion of when the policy reinforces wrong guesses or becomes unstable.  
- Major concern about scalability: multiple forward passes and costly evolutionary training raise doubts about applicability to large-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Needs experiments on higher-resolution, large-scale benchmarks to establish practical significance beyond CIFAR.
