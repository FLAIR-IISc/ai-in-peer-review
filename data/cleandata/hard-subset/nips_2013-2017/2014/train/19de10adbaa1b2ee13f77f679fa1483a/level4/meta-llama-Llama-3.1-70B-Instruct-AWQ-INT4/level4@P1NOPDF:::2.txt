This paper introduces selective attention to a convolutional neural network and develops a method for training gating parameters for attention to optimize the network's classification performance. It addresses an important set of problems in computer vision, namely, how to adaptively reconfigure complex, deep processing stages when resources are limited and different tasks need to be solved. The classification results are impressive, but the overall approach remains somewhat of a black box – it suggests useful directions to pursue but does not reveal why the method works. The analysis of image representation is not particularly revealing, and there is no clear sense of what the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are doing. Nevertheless, the work is an important step beyond the current paradigm of large, feed-forward networks with parameters fixed after learning. The exposition is quite clear, although some of the equations contain typos (or could be explained more thoroughly).

Major comments:

I would like to see the authors seriously explore the connection to dynamical systems. The work appears to be more closely related to recurrent networks than to Reinforcement Learning, as the reward is observed immediately and the policy is deterministic. Training involves traversing the space of recurrent "policy" parameters theta to maximize the observed objective function.

I did not find the analysis of the network processing the cat image particularly helpful in understanding how the new features of the model aid recognition. Why are layer 1 activations all increased at the first iteration? Does this affect the objective function, or is the output invariant to global scalings? Perhaps focusing on a smaller region of the image and analyzing the evolution of the gating variables in that region could be more instructive. Additionally, examining difficult cases – those misclassified by the standard Maxout network but correctly labeled by the proposed model – or teasing out the effect of the learned model parameters (mean and variance of theta) by constraining them further would be interesting.

Minor comments:

In the description of Algorithm 1, where is h_M defined? Is that the step of collecting observations? F[i] and Θ[i] are inside the loop over images (j); are the image fitness values collected in an array or overwritten on each iteration?

In Eqn 11, what is the sum over? Is Eqn 8 for a single image and should be indexed by j? The text after Eqn 11 refers to 'x' and 'd', but it is unclear what these variables represent.

I am not clear on why regularization of theta is necessary, since theta is already sampled from the prior and will tend to small values anyway. Furthermore, the last step of the optimization (gradient updates on the parameters after having sampled and evaluated the fitness of thetas) is not well-explained (how are the gradients computed) or justified theoretically (are you adjusting hyper-parameters of the posterior over policies)?

This approach to building deep recurrent networks for recognition is interesting. The high classification accuracy relative to standard methods suggests that recurrent computation helps extract or focus on more complex features of the images, but the black box approach does not explain why it is effective.
