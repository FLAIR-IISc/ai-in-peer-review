This paper introduces a novel approach to deep convolutional neural networks by incorporating selective attention through feedback connections, and develops a method for training the associated gating parameters to optimize classification performance. It addresses a crucial challenge in computer vision: adaptively reconfiguring deep processing stages for limited resources and diverse tasks. The reported classification results are promising, demonstrating performance exceeding the current state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets. However, the mechanism remains largely opaque; while the work identifies a promising direction, it lacks insight into *why* the method succeeds. The analysis of image representation is superficial, and the learned parameters – specifically the mean and variance of the policy distribution – remain poorly understood. Despite this, the work represents a significant step beyond the conventional paradigm of large, feedforward networks with fixed post-training parameters. The exposition is generally clear, though some equations require clarification or correction.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of feedback connections and learned attention mechanisms within a CNN architecture is a valuable contribution. It moves beyond purely feedforward approaches.
*   **Performance:** The reported results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 demonstrate a tangible improvement over existing state-of-the-art methods, indicating the effectiveness of the approach.
*   **Scalability:** The use of Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) allows for training a large number of parameters, making the approach feasible for complex networks.
*   **Clear Writing (Generally):** The core concepts are presented in a relatively accessible manner, allowing for comprehension of the proposed architecture and training procedure.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Interpretability:** The “black box” nature of the learned attention mechanism hinders understanding of *how* and *why* the network achieves its performance gains.  The authors provide limited analysis of the learned gating parameters or the resulting changes in feature representations.
*   **Limited Analysis:** The analysis of the cat image example is not particularly insightful.  The observed increase in layer 1 activations in the first iteration lacks explanation regarding its impact on the objective function or its invariance to global scalings. 
*   **Connection to Dynamical Systems Under-explored:**  The work shares strong similarities with recurrent neural networks and dynamical systems, but this connection is not thoroughly explored. The reward structure and deterministic policy suggest an optimization process in the space of recurrent policy parameters, akin to traversing a landscape to maximize an objective function.
*   **Equation Clarity & Minor Errors:** Several equations lack sufficient context or contain minor inconsistencies (see specific comments below).
*   **Justification of Regularization:** The necessity for regularizing the policy parameters (θ) is not fully justified, given that sampling from a prior distribution already encourages smaller values. The gradient computation and theoretical basis for updating these parameters are also insufficiently explained.



**Specific Comments & Questions:**

*   **Algorithm 1:** The definition of *h<sub>M</sub>* is missing. Is it the observation collection step? The use of *F[i]* and *Θ[i]* inside the inner loop (over images *j*) is unclear. Are fitness values accumulated in an array or overwritten with each iteration?
*   **Equation 11:** The summation is not defined. What is being summed over?
*   **Equation 8:**  Is this equation for a single image, and should it be indexed by *j*?
*   **Text after Equation 11:** The variables ‘x’ and ‘d’ are undefined.
*   **SNES Gradient Calculation:** The process of computing gradients for updating the SNES parameters is not explained.
*   **Related Work:** While the related work section is comprehensive, it doesn’t fully connect the present work to existing research on recurrent neural networks or attention mechanisms within those frameworks.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to incorporating attention into CNNs.
*   Demonstrated improvement in classification accuracy on standard datasets.
*   Addresses a key limitation of standard CNNs (lack of adaptability).
*   Scalable training procedure using SNES.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of interpretability and limited analysis.
*   Under-explored connection to relevant work in recurrent neural networks and dynamical systems.
*   Minor clarity issues in equations and algorithm description.
*   Insufficient justification for certain design choices (e.g., regularization).




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising, albeit imperfect, approach to incorporating attention mechanisms into deep convolutional neural networks. While the lack of interpretability and in-depth analysis are significant drawbacks, the demonstrated performance improvements and the novelty of the approach warrant consideration.  The paper would benefit substantially from a more detailed investigation of the learned parameters, a more thorough discussion of the connection to related work in recurrent networks, and a clearer explanation of the technical details. I believe the paper has the potential to contribute to the field, but addressing the weaknesses outlined above is crucial.

**Recommendation:** Weak Accept. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the points raised regarding interpretability, analysis, and clarity. A more detailed response to the specific comments would also strengthen the paper considerably.
