- Introduces selective attention gates in CNNs to optimize classification under resource constraints, yielding strong accuracy but limited interpretability.  
- Approach is largely a black box: paper shows that it works but fails to explain why or how.  
- Analysis of learned image representations is superficial; roles of key parameters (mean/variance of θ distribution) are unclear.  
- The method resembles recurrent/dynamical systems more than RL—reward is immediate and policy deterministic—so the RL framing is inappropriate.  
- Authors should adopt a dynamical systems perspective and analyze training as traversal of recurrent policy parameter space.  
- The cat-image analysis (Section 4.1) is uninformative—need focused tracking of gating variable evolution and mechanistic explanation (e.g., why layer 1 activations increase).  
- Examine failure/success cases (where Maxout fails but proposed model succeeds) and probe effects of constraining θ’s mean/variance for mechanistic insight.  
- Algorithm 1 needs clarification: define h_M and state whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated per image or overwritten.  
- Equation issues: Equation 11 needs explicit summation index; Equation 8 should be indexed per image (e.g., j).  
- Clarify notation after Equation 11: x = input and d = target labels.  
- Regularization of θ is questionable—θ is sampled from a prior favoring small values, so the extra regularization and the post-sampling gradient update lack theoretical justification.  
- Overall: promising and significant idea with strong empirical results, but limited impact until deeper theoretical grounding, dynamical analysis, and targeted interpretability studies are provided.
