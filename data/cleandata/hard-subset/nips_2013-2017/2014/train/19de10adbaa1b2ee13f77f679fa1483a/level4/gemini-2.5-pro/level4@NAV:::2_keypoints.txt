- Proposes selective attention gating in CNNs to train gating parameters and improve classification.  
- Tackles adaptive reconfiguration of deep architectures for limited resources and multiple tasks.  
- Achieves strong classification results but the internal mechanics and reasons for effectiveness are opaque.  
- Analysis of learned image representations is weak and offers little interpretability.  
- Role of learned theta distribution parameters (mean and variance) is unexplained.  
- Manuscript is clear overall but contains typographical errors and poorly explained equations.  

Major points:
- Needs a deeper exploration of connections to dynamical systems; method resembles recurrent networks more than RL.  
- Training appears to be a search over deterministic policy parameters (theta) rather than a typical RL setup.  
- The single-image (cat) analysis is uninformative; must analyze local gating dynamics and evolution of gating variables.  
- Should examine difficult cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected here) to clarify how the model helps.  
- Should isolate and test effects of theta’s mean and variance through constraints or ablations.  

Minor points:
- Algorithm 1: h_M is undefined; unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are collected or overwritten per image.  
- Equation 11: summation index missing.  
- Equation 8: appears to lack image index j if intended for a single image.  
- Text after Eq. 11: variables x and d are used without definition.  
- Unclear why theta requires additional regularization given sampling from a prior.  
- Final optimization step (gradient update after sampling thetas) is not described or theoretically justified.
