This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (convnet) with feedback connections to learn visual attention through reinforcement learning. The key innovation lies in the use of gated activation for certain filters, allowing the model to selectively focus on relevant features. While individual components of the model are not particularly novel, the overall approach is well-justified and effective. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their method by achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. This achievement is significant, as it validates the use of feedback and attention mechanisms in computer vision tasks. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and good quality overall. The proposed approach, combining convnets with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention, is a promising direction for vision research. A potential next step could be to apply this method to more challenging datasets, such as ImageNet, to further demonstrate its capabilities.
