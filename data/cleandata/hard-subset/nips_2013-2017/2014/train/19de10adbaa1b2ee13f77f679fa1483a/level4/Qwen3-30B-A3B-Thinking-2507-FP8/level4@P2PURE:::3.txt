

The authors propose a CNN incorporating top-down processing via attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR datasets. The paper is generally engaging and demonstrates novelty in model design and application for vision tasks, particularly in integrating both bottom-up and top-down signals during training. The evaluation across time using gated signals to constrain representations is especially compelling. However, as the quality of learned representations depends critically on the attention policy, it would be more informative if the authors could suggest or demonstrate specific failure cases for the algorithm. The experiments are well-executed, including helpful visualizations, but the method's complexity relative to standard feedforward CNNs warrants stronger validation. The primary concern—my main (and potentially only) critique—is the absence of results on ILSVRC to demonstrate scalability to larger datasets with higher-resolution images. The attention-based feedback architecture is promising and merits further study; more practical insights from experiments on larger-scale data would significantly strengthen the contribution.
