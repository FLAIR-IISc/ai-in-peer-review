

The authors propose a CNN incorporating attention-based top-down feedback for enhanced visual processing, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR datasets. The work is generally interesting and demonstrates meaningful novelty in model design for vision tasks, particularly through its integration of both bottom-up and top-down signals during training. The evaluation across temporal steps and the use of gated signals to constrain representations are especially commendable. However, since the quality of the learned representations critically depends on the attention policy, it would be more insightful if the authors could identify or demonstrate specific failure cases for their algorithm.

The experimental section is engaging, with effective visualizations aiding interpretation. Nevertheless, given the method's increased complexity relative to standard feedforward CNNs, the paper would benefit significantly from results on ILSVRC to demonstrate scalability to larger datasets and higher-resolution images. This represents the paper's primary concern. The integration of attention-based feedback with CNNs presents a promising direction worthy of further investigation, and additional practical insights from larger-scale experiments would substantially strengthen the contribution.
