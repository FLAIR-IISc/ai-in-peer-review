The authors present a novel CNN architecture that integrates top-down processing via attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR datasets.

This manuscript is engaging and showcases innovation in model design and application to vision problems, demonstrating a forward-thinking approach by incorporating both bottom-up and top-down signals in CNN training. The evaluation of the model over time, along with the utilization of gated signals to constrain representations, is particularly noteworthy. However, as the quality of the learned representations heavily relies on the attention policy, it would be intriguing to explore or demonstrate scenarios where the algorithm may fail, providing a more comprehensive understanding of its limitations.

The experimental design is compelling, with the authors employing various methods, such as visualization, to facilitate a deeper understanding of the model. Nevertheless, considering the increased complexity of this approach compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, the inclusion of results on the ILSVRC dataset would significantly enhance the paper by demonstrating its scalability to larger datasets with larger images, which is my primary concern. The synergy between attention-based feedback and CNNs gives rise to an intriguing model worthy of further investigation, and more practical insights gained from experiments on larger datasets would be highly valuable.
