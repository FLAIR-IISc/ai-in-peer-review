- Paper integrates selective attention gating into a CNN and proposes a training method that optimizes classification by adjusting gating parameters.
- Addresses dynamic reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and diverse tasks, an important CV challenge.
- Classification results are strong, indicating recurrent computation or attention improves performance over standard feed-forward models.
- Approach is opaque: mechanisms underlying its effectiveness are not elucidated, especially how gating yields improved recognition.
- Analysis of image representations is shallow; the role of learned parameters (e.g., mean and variance of theta) is unclear.
- The work is a significant step beyond fixed-parameter feed-forward networks, despite limited mechanistic insight.
- Presentation is generally clear but contains typographical errors and places needing further explanation.
- Major critique: the method should be framed and analyzed as a dynamical/recurrent system rather than primarily as reinforcement learning.
- Training appears to navigate recurrent policy parameters deterministically with immediate rewards; this perspective needs elaboration.
- The single-image analysis (cat example) is uninformative: unexplained universal increase in layer-1 activations at iteration 1 and unknown effect on objective.
- Recommend focus on smaller image regions, tracking evolution of gating variables, and analyzing cases where the model corrects baseline misclassifications.
- Suggest constraining learned parameters or examining challenging examples to reveal how parameters affect behavior.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: h_M is undefined and the handling of F[i] and Θ[i] across image loop needs clarification (array vs. overwrite).
- Equation issues: Eq.11’s summation is unspecified; Eq.8 may need indexing by image j; variables 'x' and 'd' after Eq.11 are not explained.
- Question the need for theta regularization given sampling from a prior and small theta values, and clarify/justify the final gradient-based optimization step and how posterior hyper-parameters are adjusted.
