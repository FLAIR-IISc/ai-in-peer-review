- Proposes selective attention via gating in a CNN and trains gating parameters to improve classification performance.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep networks under resource constraints and multi-task needs.  
- Reports impressive classification results but presents the method as a black box without explaining why it works.  
- Analysis of image representations is not revealing; learned parameters (mean and variance of theta) lack clear interpretation.  
- Advances beyond fixed feed-forward networks by introducing recurrent/adaptive computation.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains equation typos or underspecified derivations.  
- Major suggestion: explore connections to dynamical systems and recurrent networks rather than framing as Reinforcement Learning.  
- Major suggestion: analyze local gating dynamics (e.g., a small image region) and evolution of gating variables for better insight.  
- Major suggestion: examine hard cases—images misclassified by baseline but corrected by the model—to understand improvements.  
- Major suggestion: probe the role of learned theta parameters by constraining or varying their mean/variance.  
- Minor comment: Algorithm 1 is unclear—where is h_M defined and are F[i], Θ[i] stored or overwritten?  
- Minor comment: Eqn. 11’s summation and Eqn. 8’s indexing (per-image j) are ambiguous; variables x and d are undefined.  
- Minor comment: unclear why theta regularization is needed since sampling from the prior should bias small values.  
- Minor comment: gradient updates after sampling thetas are not well-explained or theoretically justified—unclear if hyperparameters of a posterior are being adjusted.  
- Overall: approach is interesting and suggests recurrent computation benefits, but lacks mechanistic explanation of why it improves accuracy.
