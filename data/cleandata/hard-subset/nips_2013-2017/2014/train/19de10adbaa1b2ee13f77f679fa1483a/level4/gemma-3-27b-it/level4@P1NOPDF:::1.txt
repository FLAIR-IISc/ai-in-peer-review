This paper presents a convolutional network with feedback connections designed to learn visual attention. The feedback mechanism is implemented by gating the activations of specific filters. The model is trained using reinforcement learning on top of a convolutional network. While no single component is particularly novel, the overall approach is strong and, in my opinion, a promising direction for future research. As expected, the authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, which significantly strengthens the argument for the efficacy of feedback and attention mechanisms in vision.

The quality and clarity of the paper are good. This is a well-executed approach to visual processing, achieving strong results by combining convolutional networks with reinforcement learning-driven feedback attention. 

My primary suggestion for future work would be to explore the approach on larger, more challenging datasets such as ImageNet, if this has not already been done. This would further demonstrate the scalability and generalizability of the proposed method.
