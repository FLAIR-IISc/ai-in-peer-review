- Proposes augmenting a CNN with selective internal attention via feedback and trains gating variables to modulate filter responses for improved classification.  
- Addresses an important problem: reconfiguring deep processing under limited computation and varied discriminative needs.  
- Reports strong accuracy gains but treats the model largely as a black box without explaining mechanisms.  
- Representational analysis is uninformative and gives little insight into what learned policy parameters (e.g., mean/variance of θ) do.  
- Paper is a meaningful step beyond feedforward architectures by introducing recurrent/adaptive computation.  
- Writing is generally clear, but several equations appear to contain typos or need clearer exposition.  
- Major methodological concern: the approach resembles recurrent neural networks/dynamical systems more than conventional reinforcement learning.  
- Because rewards are immediate and the policy deterministic, training effectively searches over recurrent policy parameters θ rather than performing standard RL.  
- The qualitative cat example fails to explain how new components aid recognition; unexplained global increase in layer-1 activations on the first iteration.  
- Need analysis of whether uniform scaling of activations affects the objective or is invariant.  
- Recommend focused analyses: localized image regions, time-course of gating variables, and genuinely hard cases corrected by the method.  
- Suggest probing the role of the learned policy by constraining or examining the mean/variance of θ.  
- Algorithm 1 queries: where is h_M defined, and are per-image fitness values stored or overwritten?  
- Equation/text queries: clarify summation index in Eq. (11), index loss by image j if appropriate, and define x and d after Eq. (11).  
- Question the necessity of explicit regularization on θ given its prior, and clarify how the final optimization updates distribution parameters—how gradients are computed and the theoretical justification (are hyperparameters of a posterior being tuned?).  
- Overall verdict: interesting and promising approach with empirical gains, but lacking clarity on mechanisms and theoretical grounding.
