- Augments a CNN with selective attention and trains attention-gating parameters to optimize classification under resource/task constraints.  
- Tackles an important problem of adaptively reconfiguring deep processing, with strong classification outcomes.  
- Method remains largely a black box—paper shows it works but does not explain why.  
- Representation analysis is uninformative and the roles of learned parameters (theta mean and variance) are unclear.  
- Marks a noteworthy advance beyond large feed‑forward networks with frozen parameters.  
- Presentation is generally clear but several equations contain typos or need clearer exposition.  

Major comments:
- Recommend investigating connection to dynamical systems; approach seems closer to recurrent networks than to Reinforcement Learning.  
- Training appears to traverse recurrent “policy” parameters theta with immediate rewards and deterministic policy, not a typical RL setup.  
- The analysis of the cat image is not illuminating—unclear why layer‑1 activations increase initially and whether global scaling affects the output.  
- Suggest analyzing gating evolution on a smaller image region, examining difficult cases (baseline failures corrected by the model), and isolating contributions of theta’s mean/variance.  

Minor comments:
- Algorithm 1: h_M is undefined; unclear whether per‑image fitness F[i], Θ[i] are stored or overwritten.  
- Eqn 11: summation index missing; check whether Eqn 8 should be indexed per image (j).  
- Text after Eqn 11: symbols “x” and “d” are undefined.  
- Question the need for additional regularization on theta (prior should bias small values) and the unclear final optimization step—how gradients are computed and what is being updated/theoretically justified.  

- Overall: recurrent computation appears beneficial, but the method’s black‑box nature leaves its effectiveness insufficiently explained.
