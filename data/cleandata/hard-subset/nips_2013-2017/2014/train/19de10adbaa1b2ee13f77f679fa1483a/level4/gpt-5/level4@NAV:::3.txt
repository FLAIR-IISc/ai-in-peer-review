The authors present a CNN that integrates a form of top-down processing via attention-based feedback. The results on the CIFAR datasets achieve state-of-the-art performance.

This paper is generally engaging and shows novelty in both model design and its application to a vision problem. It is forward-looking to incorporate both bottom-up and top-down signals when training a CNN. I particularly appreciate the evaluation across time and the use of a gated signal to constrain the representation. Since the quality of the learned representations depends on the attention policy, it would be more informative if the authors could suggest or demonstrate scenarios where the algorithm might fail.

The experiments are interesting, with the authors showcasing the model in several ways, including visualizations that aid understanding. However, because the method is more complex than a traditional feedforward CNN, the paper would be strengthened by results on ILSVRC to clearly demonstrate its scalability to a larger dataset with larger images. That is my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and a CNN results in an intriguing model worthy of further study. More practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferred.
