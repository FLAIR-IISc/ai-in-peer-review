- Introduces dasNet: adds iterative feedback to a pre-trained CNN to modulate convolutional filters and refine classification over multiple steps.  
- Trains the attentional control policy with Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) on top of a Maxout network.  
- Novelty/significance: moves beyond static feedforward CNNs by learning a dynamic internal attention policy.  
- Performance: achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 versus a strong baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and explains the dasNet architecture clearly.  
- Interpretability: method is a black box with little insight into why the learned policy improves performance.  
- Analysis is weak: the provided cat-image example and explanations of learned parameters (SNES mean/variance) are not revealing.  
- Theoretical framing: labeling the approach as reinforcement learning is questionable because the policy is deterministic and rewards are immediate—closer to training an RNN/dynamical system.  
- Methodological clarity: several training and objective details (Algorithm 1, loss indexing, SNES updates) are under-specified or unclear.  
- Suggest deeper analysis: track gating variables over T steps in ambiguous image regions and examine cases where dasNet corrects baseline errors.  
- Suggest linking to dynamical systems/RNN literature and comparing to BPTT to justify SNES choice for large parameter spaces.  
- Specific methodological questions: define h(Mt) in Algorithm 1 and clarify whether F[i]/Θ[i] are accumulated or overwritten across images.  
- Ask to correct Equation 11 indexing (sum over images) and justify L2 regularization on θ given SNES sampling.  
- Request brief explanation of SNES update mechanics (how fitness values update µ and Σ) for self-containment.
