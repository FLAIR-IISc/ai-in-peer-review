- Integrates selective attention into a CNN and trains attention-gating parameters to optimize classification.
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and multi-task conditions.
- Classification performance is strong, but the approach is a black box with little explanation of why it works.
- Analysis of learned image representations is uninformative; the roles of theta’s mean and variance are unclear.
- Paper is a meaningful step beyond fixed feed-forward networks, but several equations contain typos or need clearer explanation.
- Major: Method seems closer to recurrent dynamical systems than reinforcement learning given immediate reward and deterministic policy.
- Major: Training appears to be a search over recurrent “policy” parameters theta to maximize observed objective.
- Major: The cat-image analysis fails to show how new model features aid recognition—e.g., why do all layer-1 activations increase initially?
- Major: Recommend analyzing gating evolution in a smaller region, examining hard cases corrected by the model, or constraining theta to isolate effects.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 omits definition of h_M and is unclear whether per-image fitness values F[i], Theta[i] are stored or overwritten.
- Minor: Equation 11 has an unclear summation index; Equation 8 may need indexing by image j.
- Minor: Symbols “x” and “d” after Equation 11 are undefined.
- Minor: Need justification for regularizing theta since sampling from the prior should bias small values.
- Minor: Final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling and fitness evaluation) is not explained—how are gradients computed and what is the theoretical basis (e.g., adjusting posterior hyperparameters)?
- Overall: Recurrent computation likely helps extract complex features, but the black-box nature leaves the mechanism unexplained.
