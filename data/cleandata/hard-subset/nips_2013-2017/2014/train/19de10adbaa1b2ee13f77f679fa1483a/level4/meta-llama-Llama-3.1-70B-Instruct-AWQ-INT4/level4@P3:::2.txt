This paper proposes a novel approach to deep neural networks by introducing internal selective attention through feedback connections, which enables the network to dynamically alter its convolutional filter sensitivities during classification. The authors develop a method for training gating parameters for attention using reinforcement learning, specifically through scalable natural evolution strategies (SNES). The proposed Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet) architecture achieves state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and the potential benefits of incorporating feedback connections. The use of SNES for training the attentional policy is an interesting choice, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of the algorithm and its advantages.

The experimental results are impressive, with dasNet outperforming the previous state-of-the-art model on unaugmented datasets. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the network's behavior on a specific image, which helps to illustrate the effectiveness of the attentional mechanism.

However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the learned parameters and their role in the attentional mechanism. The authors mention that the learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are not well-understood, and further exploration of this topic could provide valuable insights into the workings of the model.

Additionally, the connection to dynamical systems is not fully explored, and the authors could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between dasNet and recurrent networks. The reward function used in the reinforcement learning framework is also not fully justified, and the authors could provide more explanation for their choice of reward function.

Some minor comments and suggestions for improvement:

* The algorithm description could be improved by explicitly defining the function h_M and clarifying the role of F[i] and \Theta[i] in the loop over images.
* Equation 11 could be clarified by specifying the sum over which the equation is taken.
* The text after equation 11 could be improved by explicitly defining the variables 'x' and 'd'.
* The regularization of theta could be justified theoretically, and the computation of gradients in the optimization step could be explained in more detail.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and novel approach to deep neural networks, and the experimental results are impressive. With some additional analysis and clarification, the paper could be even stronger.
