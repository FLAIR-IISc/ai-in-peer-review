- Proposes dasNet, a novel CNN that uses top-down attentional feedback to dynamically modulate filter sensitivities across multiple classification passes.  
- Trains feedback connections with reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to handle a million-dimensional policy space.  
- Iterative processing lets the network correct initial misclassifications through successive reweighting of internal activations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation including visualizations of activation changes and analysis of dynamics across iterations.  
- Validates the learned policy’s information content via gate-value classification and shows stable behavior beyond trained iterations.  
- Conceptually grounded in neuroscience, linking cortical feedback mechanisms to bidirectional processing in vision.  
- Novel architectural contribution bridging recurrent processing and attention in deep learning.  
- Technical approach (SNES) is well-justified and avoids pitfalls of conventional RL methods for large parameter spaces.  
- Main empirical limitation: no experiments on larger benchmarks like ImageNet, leaving scalability unproven.  
- Computational cost noted (4 days on a GTX 560 Ti) and feasibility for modern large-scale tasks is unclear.  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes where attention might misdirect or fail to improve performance.  
- Not benchmarked against contemporary architectures (e.g., ResNet) in terms of compute or scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, clearly written, significant contribution deserving acceptance, with future work needed on scalability and failure analysis.
