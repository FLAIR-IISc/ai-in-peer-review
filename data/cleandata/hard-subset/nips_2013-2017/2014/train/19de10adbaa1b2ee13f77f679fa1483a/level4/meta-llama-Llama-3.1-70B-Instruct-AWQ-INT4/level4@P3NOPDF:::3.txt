The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) that incorporates a form of top-down processing through attention-based feedback, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR datasets. This paper is engaging to read, demonstrating novelty in both model design and application to vision problems. Its forward-thinking approach considers both bottom-up and top-down signals for training a CNN, a notable strength.

A particularly interesting aspect of the model is its evaluation across time and the use of gated signals to constrain representation. The quality of the learned representations heavily depends on the attention policy, making it intriguing to consider scenarios where the algorithm might fail. Providing such analysis or demonstrations would further enrich the paper.

The experiments are well-designed, with the authors using visualization techniques to facilitate a deeper understanding of the model. However, given the increased complexity of the method compared to traditional feedforward CNNs, results on the ILSVRC dataset would be beneficial in demonstrating scalability to larger datasets with larger images. This is a key concern, as it would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the model's capabilities.

The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs presents an interesting model worthy of further study. Additional practical insights gained from experiments on larger datasets would be valuable, providing a more robust evaluation of the proposed method. Overall, the paper contributes a unique perspective to the field, and with further experimentation on more extensive datasets, it has the potential to make a significant impact.
