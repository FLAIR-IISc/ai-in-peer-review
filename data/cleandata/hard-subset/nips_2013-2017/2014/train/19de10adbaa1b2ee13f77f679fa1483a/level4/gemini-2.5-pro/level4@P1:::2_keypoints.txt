- Introduces dasNet: a selective attention mechanism in a CNN that modulates filter activations over successive passes.  
- Trains gating parameters via a policy network using reinforcement learning and natural evolution strategies (SNES).  
- Frames adaptive, recurrent top-down processing as a way to reconfigure deep architectures beyond static feed-forward models.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, showing recurrent passes can correct baseline misclassifications.  
- Main weakness: presented as a “black box” with little insight into why the method works or how internal decisions are made.  
- Analysis (e.g., cat image visualization) is uninformative and does not reveal how attention improves recognition.  
- Unclear how the learned policy distribution (mean and covariance) actually guides attention decisions.  
- Paper would benefit from discussing the connection to dynamical systems and unrolled RNNs, since training resembles direct optimization of an RNN.  
- Suggest analyzing hard cases (images misclassified by baseline but corrected by dasNet) to isolate attention effects.  
- Recommend tracking gating variable evolution for salient image regions across steps to clarify mechanism.  
- Propose investigating the learned policy distribution parameters (µ, Σ) by constraining or analyzing their structure.  
- Algorithm 1: h(M_t) (observation construction) is not clearly defined in the main text.  
- Algorithm 1: fitness and policy updates appear wrongly placed inside the image loop and should occur after the inner loop.  
- Equation issues: Equation 11’s summation index is incorrect and Equation 8 likely needs image-indexing (j); notation should be fixed.  
- SNES update procedure and how the natural gradient is computed/applied are not well explained; add detail or clearer references.  
- Question the necessity of L2 regularization on sampled policy parameters θ given SNES’s Gaussian sampling—motivation for this term should be clarified.
