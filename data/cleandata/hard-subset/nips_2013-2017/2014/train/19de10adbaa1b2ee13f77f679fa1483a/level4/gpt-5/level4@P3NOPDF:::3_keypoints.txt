- Proposes a CNN with recurrent, gated, attention-based top-down feedback that modulates intermediate representations over time.  
- Shows state-of-the-art results on CIFAR, supporting the claim that top-down attention improves recognition.  
- Novel integration of bottom-up and top-down signals via multi-step recurrent processing and gating mechanisms.  
- Qualitative visualizations and temporal analyses increase interpretability relative to feedforward baselines.  
- Main concern: scalability to large-scale vision (e.g., ILSVRC/ImageNet) given added complexity.  
- Suggestion: include large-scale or proxy ImageNet experiments (or ImageNet-100/200 subsets) to establish scalability.  
- Suggestion: analyze failure modes of the attention policy (background focus, occlusion, clutter, adversarial textures, class-specific errors).  
- Suggestion: perform ablations/controls (remove/randomize attention, vary feedback steps, gated vs. ungated, isolate iteration vs. attention effects).  
- Suggestion: report computational costs—parameter counts, FLOPs, wall-clock training/inference time, memory, and per-step cost for T steps.  
- Suggestion: clarify training/stability details for the attention policy, loss weighting, convergence, curricula, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: evaluate generalization and robustness on additional datasets and simple perturbation tests (noise, occlusion, jitter).  
- Suggestion: expand visualizations—temporal attention/class sequences, counterfactual masking, and comparison to human saliency.  
- Minor: clarify architecture with a concise diagram and layer specs, ensure matched baselines and augmentations, fix minor writing errors.  
- Minor: releasing code or pretrained models would aid reproducibility.  
- Overall: promising, well-motivated contribution with clear CIFAR gains; large-scale validation, ablations, and computational analysis are needed to assess practical impact.
