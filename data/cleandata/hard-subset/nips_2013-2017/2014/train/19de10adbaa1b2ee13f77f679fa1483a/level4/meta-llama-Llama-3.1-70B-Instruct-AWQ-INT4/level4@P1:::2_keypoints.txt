- Proposes dasNet: selective attention via inter-layer modulation of convolutional filters using a learned control policy trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of traditional CNN limitations and benefits of selective attention.
- Using SNES to learn the high-dimensional control policy is an interesting and appropriate choice.
- Experimental results are strong: dasNet outperforms prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
- Visualization of the network processing (cat image) aids intuition about how the model works.
- Analysis of image representations is weak; no clear insight into what the learned theta mean/variance are doing.
- Framing as Reinforcement Learning is questionable—model appears closer to recurrent/dynamical systems since reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic.
- Authors should analyze gating variable evolution in small image regions to better explain how attention improves recognition.
- Recommend examining difficult cases (misclassified by baseline but corrected by dasNet) and constraining/analyzing theta distribution effects.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear; needs definitions for h_M and clarification of F[i] and Θ[i] in image loop.
- Equation 11 is unclear; specify summation, index Equation 8 per image (j), and define variables x and d referenced afterward.
- Regularization of theta is poorly explained; need justification for regularization and details on how gradients are computed.
- Overall: interesting and effective recurrent approach for recognition, but currently a black box—more analysis required to understand strengths and limitations.
