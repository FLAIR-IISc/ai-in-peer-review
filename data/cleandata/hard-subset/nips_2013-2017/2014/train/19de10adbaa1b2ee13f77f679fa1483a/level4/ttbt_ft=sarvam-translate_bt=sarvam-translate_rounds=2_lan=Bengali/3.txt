The authors propose a CNN that incorporates a top-down processing approach in the form of attention-based feedback. The results of the cipher dataset demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.

This paper generally demonstrates the readability and innovation in model design and vision problem solving. It is forward-looking, considering both bottom-up and top-down signals for a CNN training. I particularly liked the aspect of model evaluation using gated signals and limiting the presentation over time. Since the quality of the learning presentation depends on the attention principle, it would be more appealing if the authors could provide or demonstrate some examples where the algorithm might fail.

The experiments are interesting, and the authors have demonstrated the model in various ways, such as visualization for better understanding. However, since the method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I believe this paper could be further improved if ILSVRCs were available, so that its scalability could truly be demonstrated for larger datasets and larger images. That would be my main (and probably only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs creates an interesting model, which is worthy of further study. More practical insights from testing on larger datasets will be prioritized.
