- Proposes a CNN that integrates top-down processing via attention-based feedback.
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR.
- Clear novelty in both model design and its application to visual recognition.
- Forward-looking approach that jointly leverages bottom-up and top-down signals during training.
- Temporal evaluation and gated signals are valuable for interpretability and constraining representations.
- Quality of learned representations depends heavily on the attention policy; discussion/demonstration of failure modes is needed.
- Experiments are compelling and visualizations aid interpretability.
- Method is more complex than a standard feedforward CNN.
- Primary concern is scalability—results on ILSVRC or larger images/datasets would strengthen the paper.
- Overall promising and merits further study, with more large-scale experiments preferred for practical insights.
