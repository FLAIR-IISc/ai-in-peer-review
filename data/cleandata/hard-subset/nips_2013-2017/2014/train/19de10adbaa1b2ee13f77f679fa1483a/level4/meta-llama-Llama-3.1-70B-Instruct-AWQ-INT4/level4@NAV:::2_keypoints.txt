- Proposes selective attention gating in CNNs by training gating parameters to optimize classification performance.  
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration of deep processing under limited resources and diverse tasks in computer vision.  
- Achieves impressive classification accuracy but offers little insight into the underlying mechanisms.  
- Analysis of image representations is limited and not revelatory.  
- Role and interpretation of learned parameters (mean and variance of the theta distribution) are unclear.  
- Represents progress beyond large feed-forward networks with fixed post-training parameters.  
- Exposition is generally clear, though some equations need correction or clarification.  
- Major suggestion: explore connections to dynamical systems; method seems closer to recurrent networks than reinforcement learning.  
- Training resembles traversing recurrent “policy” parameter space (theta) to maximize observed objectives.  
- Empirical analysis (cat image) was not informative about the benefits of new model features for recognition.  
- Recommend investigating why layer-1 activations increase on first iteration and whether outputs are invariant to global scaling.  
- Suggest focusing on smaller image regions and tracking gating-variable evolution for clearer insights.  
- Propose analyzing hard cases (misclassified by Maxout but corrected by the model) and constraining theta mean/variance to test their effects.  
- Algorithm 1: definition of h_M is unclear and needs specification (is it observation collection?).  
- Implementation detail: F[i] and Θ[i] inside image loop—unclear if fitness values are stored or overwritten.  
- Equations: clarity issues with Equation 11’s sum, Equation 8 indexing (per image), and undefined variables x and d.  
- Question the necessity of regularizing theta given prior sampling and tendency toward small values.  
- Final optimization step (gradient updates after sampling/evaluation) lacks explanation of gradient computation and theoretical justification.  
- Overall: approach is intriguing and suggests recurrent computation aids recognition, but lack of transparency limits understanding of effectiveness.
