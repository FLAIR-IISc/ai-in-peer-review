- Introduces dasNet, a novel architecture adding internal selective attention via feedback to adapt convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.
- Uses sequential/iterative processing so the network can refocus internal attention and correct initial guesses through additional internal "thoughts."
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.
- Well-written manuscript with a clear explanation of the approach.
- Analysis of the network’s processing (e.g., the cat image) is shallow and could be more insightful.
- The work seems closer to recurrent/dynamical systems than to reinforcement learning, a connection that should be explored.
- Reward signal and deterministic policy imply training traverses recurrent policy parameter space, which is not fully discussed.
- Black-box approach: high accuracy shown, but lacks explanation of why the method is effective.
- Minor clarifications needed: definition of h_M in Algorithm 1, details on observation collection, and indexing in Eqn 8.
- Ambiguity after Eqn 11 about the meanings of 'x' and 'd' needs clarification.
- Regularization of theta is poorly explained given theta is sampled from a prior and tends to small values.
- Originality rated 8/10: novel architecture that builds on existing ideas.
- Significance rated 8/10: strong results but limited to specific datasets.
- Quality rated 8/10: overall well-written with some minor issues.
- Clarity rated 8/10: generally clear but some equations and text require clarification.
