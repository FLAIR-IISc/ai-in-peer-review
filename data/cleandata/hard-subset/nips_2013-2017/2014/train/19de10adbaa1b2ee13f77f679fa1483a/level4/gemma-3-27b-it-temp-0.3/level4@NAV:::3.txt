This paper presents a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture integrating top-down processing via attention-based feedback connections. The reported results on CIFAR datasets achieve state-of-the-art performance.

The work is compelling and introduces a novel model design and application to computer vision. The consideration of both bottom-up and top-down signal integration for CNN training is a particularly promising direction. I found the temporal evaluation and use of gated signals to constrain representations to be strong aspects of this work. To further strengthen the manuscript, exploring potential failure modes or scenarios where the attention policy limits performance would be valuable. 

The experimental analysis is well-executed, with visualizations aiding in model understanding. However, given the increased complexity compared to standard feedforward CNNs, demonstrating scalability to larger datasets is crucial. Results on ILSVRC would significantly address this point and provide a more comprehensive evaluation. This remains my primary concern. Overall, the combination of attention-based feedback and CNNs creates a noteworthy model deserving of further investigation, and additional experimental insights from larger-scale datasets would be highly beneficial.
