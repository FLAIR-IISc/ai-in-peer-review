- Proposes dasNet: a Maxout CNN augmented with learned multiplicative per-feature-map feedback gates applied over multiple passes.  
- Policy is learned via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle a million-parameter policy space.  
- Policy observations are averaged map activations, pre-softmax activations, and class probabilities; outputs are positive per-map gains via scaled softmax.  
- Uses a boosting-style loss to upweight misclassified examples during policy learning.  
- Reports consistent improvements over a strong Maxout baseline on CIFAR-10/100 and state-of-the-art single-model results on “unaugmented” datasets.  
- Provides qualitative analyses (per-layer changes, stability across steps) and experiments varying recurrent steps.  
- Builds on prior CNN, attention, and RL-based selective attention literature; novelty lies in high-dimensional black-box policy search for internal feedback.  
- Method description is sound: gating integration, observation/action definitions, and simple policy mapping are well explained.  
- SNES is a reasonable choice given policy dimensionality and the boosting loss focuses learning on hard cases.  
- Experimental evidence shows improvements but some claims need clarification (e.g., what “unaugmented” means).  
- Baselines are limited; missing comparisons to backprop-trained recurrent/gated CNNs, simple linear gating, or policy-gradient methods.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing, per-class improvements, and detailed error analysis (confusion matrices).  
- Computational cost is a concern: repeated passes and large policy imply overhead; training/inference compute and wall-clock times are not reported.  
- Clarity suggestions: resolve augmentation terminology, report exact observation/action dimensions, and briefly describe SNES updates.  
- Notation needs tightening in Equations (5)–(7) for index and activation consistency.  
- Originality: novel combination of per-feature-map feedback gating with high-dimensional evolutionary policy search applied to strong CNNs.  
- Significance: supports learned feedback for improving recognition on ambiguous inputs, but scalability to larger datasets/tasks is untested.  
- Pros: clear formulation of internal attention, pragmatic learning scheme, consistent baseline improvements, and informative qualitative analyses.  
- Cons: limited baseline comparisons, ambiguous augmentation claims, unquantified compute/parameter overhead, and no empirical comparison to differentiable alternatives.  
- Suggestions: clarify augmentation/ensemble usage, add backprop-trained recurrent baselines and simpler gating ablations, and report compute/FLOPs/latency.  
- Further requests: provide per-class/confusion analyses, ablate observation components and policy capacity, and explore scaling to ImageNet and spatial attention.  
- Recommendation: Accept — well-executed integration of feedback attention with deep CNNs yielding credible CIFAR gains and promising direction for learned internal feedback.
