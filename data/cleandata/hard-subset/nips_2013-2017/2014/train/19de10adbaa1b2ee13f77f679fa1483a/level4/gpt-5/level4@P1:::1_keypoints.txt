- Proposes a convolutional network augmented with feedback gating that selectively gates filter activations over multiple inference passes, controlled by an RL policy (SNES) on top of a pretrained Maxout CNN.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, validating the approach empirically.  
- Approach aligns with biological recurrent/top-down processing and prior ML work on attention and RL.  
- Strength: compelling and well-motivated idea to add RL-driven internal attention to a strong feedforward CNN.  
- Strength: careful implementation on a competitive Maxout architecture with clear empirical gains.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and technically sound; gating and SNES choices are sensible.  
- Weakness: individual components (CNNs, attention/gating, RL) are not novel; contribution is mainly their combination.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to CIFAR-scale datasets; scalability to larger benchmarks (e.g., ImageNet) is not shown.  
- Quality: technically sound integration of multiplicative gating and justified use of SNES for high-dimensional policy learning.  
- Clarity: well organized description of problem setup, gating mechanism, policy, training loop, and results.  
- Originality: limited conceptual novelty, but meaningful for its effective integration and empirical validation.  
- Significance: provides empirical evidence that feedback/attention can improve modern CNN classification and motivates further scaling.  
- Pros: strong empirical performance, clear architecture and motivation, high paper quality.  
- Cons: primarily integrative novelty and restricted experimental scale.  
- Recommendation: a very good paper that convincingly validates the approach; would be strengthened by experiments on larger datasets like ImageNet.
