The authors present a CNN architecture that incorporates top-down processing via attention-driven feedback connections. On the CIFAR datasets, the method achieves state-of-the-art performance.

The paper is generally engaging and shows novelty in both architectural design and its application to a vision task. It is forward-looking to integrate bottom-up and top-down signals within a CNN. I particularly appreciate the evaluation across time and the use of gated signals to constrain the representation. Because the quality of the learned representation depends heavily on the attention policy, it would be helpful if the authors could propose or illustrate scenarios where the approach might fail.

The experiments are interesting, including visualizations that aid interpretability. However, given that the method is more complex than a conventional feedforward CNN, the paper would be stronger with results on ILSVRC to convincingly demonstrate scalability to larger, higher-resolution datasets. This is my main (and likely only) concern. The combination of attention-based feedback with a CNN is compelling and merits further study; additional practical insights from experiments on larger datasets would be preferable.
