- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with top-down modulatory feedback that dynamically adjusts convolutional filters over multiple passes.  
- Implements the attentional mechanism as a control policy (neural network) trained with reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies).  
- Draws inspiration from recurrent and top-down pathways in human visual cortex and aims to scale those principles to deep models.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Strength — Originality: novel combination of high-performing feedforward CNN and RL-trained iterative internal modulation.  
- Strength — Significance: enables “internal thoughts” to refine predictions and offers biologically plausible, adaptive processing with potential broad impact.  
- Strength — Analysis quality: strong experiments, useful visualizations of filter modulation, dynamics-over-time analysis, and gate-value information tests.  
- Weakness — Scalability: experiments limited to small 32x32 CIFAR images; unclear feasibility and computational cost on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) given multiple passes and large policy.  
- Weakness — Failure analysis: lacks systematic examination of failure modes or cases where attention degrades performance versus the feedforward baseline.  
- Overall: well-written, novel, and compelling work with strong CIFAR results, but scalability concerns temper the claims despite strengths outweighing weaknesses.
