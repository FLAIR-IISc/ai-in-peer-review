This paper proposes a novel approach to visual attention in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by introducing feedback connections that modulate the activation of convolutional filters. The Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet) architecture is trained using reinforcement learning to selectively focus on the most discriminative features in an image. While the individual components of the approach are not new, the way they are combined is innovative and effective.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the dasNet architecture, the reinforcement learning algorithm used to train it, and the experimental results on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the performance of dasNet, including visualizations of the attention mechanisms and the dynamics of the network.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the approach, the clarity of the writing, and the significance of the results. The authors demonstrate that dasNet achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, which is a significant contribution to the field of computer vision.

The weaknesses of the paper are minor. One potential issue is that the authors do not provide a detailed comparison of dasNet with other state-of-the-art approaches to visual attention in CNNs. Additionally, the authors do not discuss the potential limitations of their approach, such as the computational cost of training dasNet and the difficulty of interpreting the attention mechanisms.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a significant contribution to the field of computer vision and deserves to be published. The approach taken by the authors is innovative and effective, and the results are impressive. I would recommend that the authors consider applying their approach to more challenging datasets, such as ImageNet, to further demonstrate its effectiveness.
