The authors have proposed a CNN that incorporates a form of top-down processing as a focus-based response. The results on the CIFAR dataset demonstrate cutting-edge performance.

This paper is generally interesting to read and shows novelty in the context of model design and application to a vision problem. It is also promising to consider both bottom-up and top-down signals to train the CNN. I particularly like the aspect of using gated signals to evaluate the model over time and limit representation. Since the quality of the learned representation depends on the focus policy, it would be more interesting if the authors could suggest or demonstrate cases where the algorithm might fail.

The experiments are interesting; the authors have demonstrated the model in various ways, such as through visualization to help better understand it. However, since this method is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, I think the paper could be improved if results were available on ILSVRCs, so that its scalability with large images on truly large datasets could be demonstrated. This would be my main (and possibly only) concern. The combination of focus-based response and CNN creates an interesting model, warranting further study. More practical insights from experiments on large datasets will be prioritized.
