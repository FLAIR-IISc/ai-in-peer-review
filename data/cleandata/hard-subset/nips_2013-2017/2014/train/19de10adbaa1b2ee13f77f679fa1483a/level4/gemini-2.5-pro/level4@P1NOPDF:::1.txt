This paper presents a convolutional neural network (ConvNet) that incorporates feedback connections to learn visual attention. The feedback mechanism is implemented by gating the activations of specific filters, and the model is trained using a reinforcement learning framework built upon the ConvNet architecture.

While the individual components—convolutional networks, feedback mechanisms, and reinforcement learning—are not novel in themselves, the authors' synthesis of these elements into a cohesive system is highly effective and compelling. In my view, the proposed approach represents a significant and promising direction for the field.

The strength of this method is convincingly demonstrated by its state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR dataset. This achievement is significant, as it provides strong empirical validation that integrating feedback and attention is a critical path forward for advancing computer vision models.

The quality of the writing and the clarity of the presentation are both high. In summary, this is a very strong contribution that introduces an elegant and effective approach to vision, combining ConvNets with reinforcement learning-driven feedback for attention. As a suggestion for future work, it would be highly valuable to see this model scaled and evaluated on more complex, large-scale datasets such as ImageNet, if the authors are not already pursuing this.
