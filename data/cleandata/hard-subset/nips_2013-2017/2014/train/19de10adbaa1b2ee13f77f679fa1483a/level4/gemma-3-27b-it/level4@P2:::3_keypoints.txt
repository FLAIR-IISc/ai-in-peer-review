- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with top-down attention-based feedback to modulate filters.  
- Novelty: combines feedback connections and reinforcement learning to control convolutional filters.  
- Uses sequential processing and gated signals to iteratively refine representations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Visualizations of filter activations and attention weights effectively illuminate learned mechanisms.  
- Paper clearly explains the architecture and the reinforcement-learning training of the attention policy.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to relatively small datasets (CIFAR), raising scalability concerns.  
- Missing results on larger, more complex benchmarks (e.g., ImageNet) to validate generalizability.  
- Lacks empirical analysis of failure modes and limitations despite mentioning potential complex failure scenarios.  
- Computational cost (4 days on a GTX 560 Ti) should be acknowledged and contextualized for practical use.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, potentially impactful approach with promising empirical results and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope and insufficient analysis of failure cases.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; accept if authors address scalability concerns via more experiments or a thorough limitations discussion.
