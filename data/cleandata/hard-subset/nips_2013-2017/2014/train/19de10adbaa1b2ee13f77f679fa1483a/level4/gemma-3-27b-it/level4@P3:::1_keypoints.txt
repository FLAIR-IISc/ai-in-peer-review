- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that iteratively modulates filter sensitivities via a policy learned with SNES, achieving SOTA on CIFAR-10/100.  
- Builds on established CNN, maxout, and reinforcement learning techniques; combines SNES with filter-level attention as a novel contribution.  
- Properly cites related iterative/recurrent attention and biological vision literature.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrating clear improvements on CIFAR benchmarks.  
- Novel and well-executed integration of reinforcement learning (SNES) to learn filter-level attention.  
- Clear motivation with biological plausibility and accessible writing; helpful visualization of filter activations (Figure 3).  
- Good scalability argument: SNES is a sensible choice given large policy parameter counts.  
- Experiments are limited to CIFAR datasets, leaving generalizability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) uncertain.  
- Analysis complexity: more in-depth investigation of what the policy attends to is needed.  
- Dependence on Maxout networks raises questions about applicability to other architectures (e.g., ResNet).  
- Choice of SNES hyperparameters lacks sufficient justification or ablation studies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with compelling empirical results and strong motivation.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited experimental scope and insufficient analysis of learned attention mechanisms.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but encourage extending experiments to larger datasets and deeper analysis of attention behavior.
