This paper augments a convolutional neural network with selective internal attention via feedback and proposes a training procedure for gating variables that adaptively modulate filter responses to improve classification. It targets an important problem in computer vision: how to reconfigure deep processing stages on the fly when computational resources are constrained and different discriminative cues are needed. The reported accuracy is strong, yet the approach remains largely a black box—pointing to promising directions without clarifying the underlying mechanisms. The representational analysis is not very enlightening, and there is little insight into what the learned policy parameters (e.g., mean and variance of the θ distribution) are doing. Even so, the work represents a meaningful step beyond purely feedforward architectures with fixed post-training parameters. The writing is generally clear, though several equations appear to contain typos or would benefit from clearer exposition.

Major comments:
I encourage the authors to more thoroughly examine the link to dynamical systems. The method seems closer to recurrent neural networks than to conventional reinforcement learning. Because rewards are obtained immediately and the policy is deterministic, training effectively performs a search over recurrent “policy” parameters θ to optimize the observed objective.
The qualitative analysis of the cat example does not illuminate how the new components aid recognition. Why are layer-1 activations globally increased on the first iteration? Does a uniform scaling influence the objective, or is the output invariant to such rescaling? A more focused analysis on a localized image region and the time-course of the gating variables there could be instructive. Additionally, examining genuinely hard cases—instances misclassified by the base Maxout model but corrected by the proposed system—or probing the role of the learned policy (e.g., constraining the mean/variance of θ) would be informative.

Minor comments:
Algorithm 1: Where is h_M defined? Is this the observation-collection step? Also, since F[i] and Θ[i] appear inside the loop over images j, are per-image fitness values stored or overwritten each iteration?
Equation (11): Please clarify the summation index. If Equation (8) is the loss for a single image, should it be indexed by j?
Text following Equation (11): Please define x and d explicitly.
It is unclear why explicit regularization of θ is needed, given that θ is sampled from a prior that already biases toward small values. Moreover, the final optimization step (updating distribution parameters after sampling/evaluating θ) needs clearer explanation: how are gradients computed, and what is the theoretical justification? Are you effectively tuning hyperparameters of a posterior over policies?

Overall, this is an interesting approach to constructing deep recurrent recognition systems. The performance gains over strong baselines suggest that recurrent computation can help extract or emphasize more complex features, but the current black-box treatment does not yet clarify why it works.
