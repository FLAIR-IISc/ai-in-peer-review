- Summary: Proposes dasNet, a CNN with top-down attention that dynamically adjusts filter sensitivities and uses SNES to train the attention policy, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100.

- Novelty and Innovation: Introduces attention-based feedback in CNNs, combining reinforcement learning and deep learning to model selective attention.

- State-of-the-Art Results: Outperforms prior methods on CIFAR datasets and corrects ambiguous misclassifications.

- Evaluation Across Time: Uses iterative multi-step processing and gated signals to analyze dynamics and refine representations.

- Visualization: Provides visualizations of filter emphasis changes and class probability dynamics to aid interpretability.

- Biologically Inspired Design: Aligns architecture with neuroscience principles like feedback connections and selective attention.

- Scalability: Does not demonstrate or discuss applicability to larger, more complex datasets (e.g., ILSVRC).

- Failure Cases: Lacks analysis of failure modes and limitations of the learned attention policy.

- Computational Cost: Training, especially the reinforcement learning component, is computationally intensive with no proposed optimizations.

- Broader Applicability: Unclear practical usefulness beyond CIFAR datasets; more diverse or larger-scale experiments are needed.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance contingent on future work addressing scalability, failure cases, and computational efficiency.
