- Introduces dasNet: a recurrent-style CNN with feedback connections and RL to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains, achieving state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Motivation is clear: builds on CNN successes and human-like iterative visual refinement.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the attentional policy due to high-dimensional parameter space.  
- Major interpretability problem: the reasons for dasNet’s success remain a black box and analyses are insufficient.  
- Analysis of learned theta parameters (mean/variance) is uninformative; claiming “complex patterns” is inadequate.  
- Dynamics analysis is limited; needs deeper study of gating variable evolution in local image regions and effects of global scaling.  
- Recommend examining hard cases (images corrected by dasNet but misclassified by baseline) to reveal attention strategies.  
- Weak connection to existing literature: framed as policy search but is arguably closer to recurrent/dynamical systems than traditional RL.  
- Suggest constraining learned theta statistics (e.g., sparsity) and studying impact to elucidate model behavior.  
- Algorithmic and notational issues: Algorithm 1 is poorly defined (undocumented h_M, ambiguous F[i]/Θ[i] accumulation).  
- Multiple equation ambiguities and typos (unclear summation indices, missing image indices, unexplained symbols x and d).  
- Theta regularization and final gradient-update procedures are unjustified and unclearly described.  
- Pros: novel architecture combining feedback and RL, addresses limits of static feedforward nets, strong empirical results.  
- Cons: lack of interpretability, inadequate internal analysis, notation/algorithmic ambiguities, weak theoretical grounding, equation errors.  
- Overall recommendation: promising but requires major revisions for clarity, interpretability, theoretical grounding, and reproducibility; accept with major revisions.
