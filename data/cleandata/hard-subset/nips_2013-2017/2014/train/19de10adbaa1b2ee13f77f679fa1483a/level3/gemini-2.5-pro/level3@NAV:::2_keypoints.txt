- Introduces dasNet: adds recurrent feedback and selective attention to pretrained feed-forward CNNs.  
- Uses gating parameters to modulate filter activations across successive passes.  
- Trains gates with reinforcement learning (Natural Evolution Strategies) to correct initial misclassifications.  
- Novelty: applies iterative internal modulation/attention to a state-of-the-art classification model.  
- Strong empirical results: significant accuracy gains on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Conceptual contribution: moves beyond fixed-parameter, single-pass networks toward dynamic adaptive models.  
- Main weakness: poor interpretability — model is a black box with superficial analysis of learned parameters and representations.  
- Major suggestion: reframe/relate the approach to dynamical systems or recurrent neural networks rather than pure reinforcement learning.  
- Request deeper analysis: explain internal dynamics (e.g., why layer-1 activations increase, effect of global softmax scaling) and inspect hard cases corrected by dasNet.  
- Clarity issues in manuscript: Algorithm 1 ambiguous (undefined h_M, unclear collection/overwriting of F[i] and Θ[i]).  
- Several equation problems: unspecified summation index in Eq.11, missing image index in Eq.8, undefined symbols x and d after Eq.11.  
- Unclear justification for theta regularization.  
- Conclusion: promising and effective method but acceptance should require substantial revisions addressing interpretability and multiple clarity/notation errors.
