- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections to modulate convolutional filter sensitivities and model selective attention.  
- Uses Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn a control policy that adjusts filters over multiple image passes.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Motivation is biologically inspired, drawing parallels to iterative human visual processing and top-down feedback.  
- Related work is discussed thoroughly, situating dasNet among recurrent and reinforcement-learning approaches in vision.  
- Novelty: learned feedback to modulate filters is a significant contribution.  
- Strong visualizations (Figure 3) and dynamics experiments (Figure 2) provide insight into internal attention behavior.  
- Pragmatic use of SNES to handle large policy parameter spaces is a sensible solution.  
- Analysis includes gate information content but could be expanded in some technical details.  
- Writing is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Major weakness: no results on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability and real-world applicability.  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases and instances where dasNet does not improve classification.  
- Some methodological details need clarification (regularization selection; why action vectors average to 1 rather than sum to 1).  
- Unclear whether gains justify added complexity versus simply training larger feedforward networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting scalability experiments/discussion and deeper failure-case analysis.
