- Paper adds learned multiplicative channel-wise feedback gating across all layers over multiple passes, using a deterministic policy trained with SNES, improving single-model CIFAR-10/100 accuracy.  
- Situates work within top-down/recurrent vision, attention/RL for vision, and iterative inference literature.  
- Empirical gains are noteworthy for a single model without test-time augmentation/ensembles; dynamics appear stable beyond training horizon.  
- Control mechanism is simple and well-motivated (positive multiplicative gates centered at 1).  
- Key algorithmic and notation details are ambiguous, and the paper offers limited analysis of why/when feedback helps.  
- Using SNES for ~10^6-parameter policies is pragmatic but raises questions about sample efficiency and optimization quality versus BPTT or policy gradients.  
- Ablations and diagnostics are insufficient; systematic tests (random/identity gates, layer-restricted gating, observation variants, L2 effects, normalizations) are missing.  
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: h(Mt) undefined; ambiguous whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated per candidate or overwritten; lines appear redundant.  
- Equations 8–11 have unclear indexing; loss summation indices, and variables x and d are used without definition at the point of use.  
- Notation conflates base network parameters and policy parameters (suggest separating Mφ and πθ and letting Mt denote gated instances).  
- “Gradient updates” wording is misleading: SNES updates the search distribution (µ, Σdiag), not θ directly; clarify what is updated and what is frozen (φ).  
- Originality: feedback/modulation is not novel per se, but the combination of channel-wise gating across layers with a deterministic policy and large-scale black-box optimization is a novel, pragmatic contribution.  
- Significance: results are significant and could influence CNN designs with feedback if scalability and learned dynamics are better understood.  
- Strengths: biologically inspired motivation, simple compatible mechanism, strong benchmark results, evidence of stable dynamics, and a practical high-dimensional SNES training recipe.  
- Weaknesses: limited interpretability and analysis of learned dynamics, several notation/algorithmic errors, RL framing is unnecessary and potentially misleading.  
- Missing justification for θ regularization (L2) and effect of global softmax normalization; no ablation on these choices.  
- Suggestions: clarify Algorithm 1 and equations, explicitly state SNES updates (µ, Σdiag) and that φ is frozen.  
- Suggested ablations: T=0/1 baselines and variable T at test time, identity/random vs. learned gates, per-layer gating, normalization schemes, remove/alter L2, and replace SNES with BPTT or policy-gradient training.  
- Suggested analyses: dynamical-systems perspective (fixed points, stability, Jacobian spectral radius), layer-wise activation trajectories, disentangle global gain vs selective modulation.  
- Suggested interpretability work: gate statistics per class/layer, PCA/MIs of gates, gate trajectories on corrected hard examples, sparsity/variance constraints on θ and performance effects.  
- Computational suggestions: report training/eval cost vs baseline, population size sensitivity, diagonal Σ implications, and selective multi-pass strategies for uncertain samples.  
- Pros: measurable accuracy gains, scalable feedback for modern CNNs, stable dynamics, sensible architectural choices.  
- Cons: unclear theory/algorithms and notation, method remains a black box with limited analysis, RL framing adds distraction.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — advances SOTA but needs clearer presentation and deeper analysis of learned dynamics to maximize impact.
