- Proposes dasNet: a feedforward Maxout CNN augmented with iterative, top-down per-feature-map gating controlled by a learned policy.
- Policy observes aggregated layer activations and class probabilities and outputs positive gates to modulate filters over multiple passes.
- Policy trained after supervised CNN training using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle high-dimensional action/observation spaces.
- Reports state-of-the-art single-model accuracy on CIFAR-10/100 (unaugmented) by correcting ambiguous cases via iterative modulation.
- Connects to prior work on recurrent processing, attention, and RL-based glimpse models while performing internal channel-wise attention.
- Strength: clear formulation combining bottom-up recognition with learned top-down attention and sequential training.
- Strength: conceptual novelty in high-dimensional, per-feature-map gating conditioned on internal activations.
- Strength: empirical improvements concentrated on ambiguous cases, with temporal analysis showing stability beyond training horizon.
- Strength: interpretability via visualizations of evolving map activations and qualitative disambiguation examples.
- Strength: careful action parameterization (positive gates via scaled softmax) to maintain stability.
- Weakness: no experiments on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ILSVRC) to assess scalability, compute/memory overhead, and optimization stability.
- Weakness: added complexity — million-parameter policy, multiple test-time passes, and evolutionary training — with no compute/latency quantification.
- Weakness: attention granularity limited to spatially averaged feature-map gates; lacks spatial attention for complex scenes.
- Weakness: missing baselines/ablations (e.g., linear gating, gradient-based training, BPTT, top-layer-only gates) to isolate gains.
- Weakness: failure modes not documented (e.g., gate amplification of spurious features, confirmation bias, over-correction).
- Weakness: clarification needed about “unaugmented” claim versus base model data augmentation during training.
- Weakness: SNES choice argued mainly on dimensionality; lacks comparison to policy-gradient or diagonal natural gradient alternatives.
- Quality: technically sound with coherent algorithm and stable behavior, but empirical study would benefit from large-scale validation and ablations.
- Clarity: generally well-written; request clarification on “unaugmented” phrasing and more SNES settings/stopping details.
- Originality: high — novel integration of modern CNNs with learned per-map top-down gating trained via SNES.
- Significance: moderate-to-high, contingent on demonstrated scalability to larger datasets.
- Suggestions: run larger-dataset experiments, report wall-clock/memory costs, and provide detailed ablations (policy types, gate scope, observation components).
- Suggestions: quantify accuracy vs compute trade-offs, consider adaptive stopping, document failure cases, and explore spatial attention variants.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled top-down feedback integration, SOTA CIFAR results, and informative temporal/visual analyses.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of scalability evidence, missing ablations/baselines, and insufficient analysis of failure modes and deployment costs.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing scalability and ablation concerns in future work.
