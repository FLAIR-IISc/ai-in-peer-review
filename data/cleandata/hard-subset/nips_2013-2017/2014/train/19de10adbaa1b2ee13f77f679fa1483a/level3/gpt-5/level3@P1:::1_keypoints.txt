- Proposal: dasNet augments a Maxout CNN with multiplicative gates on feature maps and a recurrent policy that modulates them across multiple passes.  
- Policy learning: uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to directly search for a high-dimensional policy (millions of parameters).  
- Observation/action design: observations concatenate average map activations, pre-softmax activations, and class probabilities; a linear softmax policy outputs scaled gates.  
- Training protocol: starts from a trained Maxout CNN and runs T=5 recurrent passes; loss emphasizes misclassified examples.  
- Main results: improves over the base CNN and reports state-of-the-art accuracy on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without test-time augmentation.  
- Dynamics: learned recurrent dynamics are stable even when run for more steps than trained.  
- Gate informativeness: gate vectors carry label-relevant information (good NN and logistic regression performance).  
- Technical contribution: integration of feature-wise gating, top-down feedback, and policy search at scale—novelty is primarily integrative rather than component-wise.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on Maxout/dropout and recurrence/attention literature; differs from foveal/glimpse models by keeping input fixed and modulating internal features.  
- SNES choice: unconventional for deep learning but reasonable for scaling to million-parameter policy search.  
- Missing baseline 1: no comparison to end-to-end gradient-based controller training (e.g., BPTT through T steps).  
- Missing baseline 2: no fixed-gate or random-gate multi-pass baseline to isolate the benefit of learned recurrence.  
- Missing baseline 3: no compute-matched comparison (larger feedforward network or ensemble) to control for extra compute.  
- Missing analyses: lacks per-class/confusion-matrix breakdowns and explicit identification of which classes benefit.  
- Ambiguity: unclear statements about “unaugmented” datasets vs. training with data augmentation—needs clarification for fair SOTA claims.  
- Computational cost: training/inference FLOPs, wall-clock time, and policy parameter counts are underreported.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written with clear algorithm and hyperparameters; observation design loses spatial structure which should be noted.  
- Reproducibility: should report policy size, wall-clock cost, and more implementation details.  
- Originality assessment: moderate—components have precedents but the scale/integration and empirical gains are timely.  
- Significance: moderate-to-high; empirical support for feedback/attention in vision and potential to inspire differentiable-controller follow-ups and spatial attention extensions.  
- Suggestions: clarify augmentation and comparisons, add BPTT/fixed/random-gate/compute-matched baselines, report compute vs. accuracy, analyze per-class effects, explore spatial observations/gates.  
- Pros: clear, well-executed integration with empirical gains, informative dynamics/gate analyses, conceptually simple and broadly compatible.  
- Cons: limited novelty in core components, missing key baselines and compute quantification, evaluation limited to small images, augmentation ambiguity.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—solid empirical case but needs stronger comparisons and scaling experiments to increase impact.
