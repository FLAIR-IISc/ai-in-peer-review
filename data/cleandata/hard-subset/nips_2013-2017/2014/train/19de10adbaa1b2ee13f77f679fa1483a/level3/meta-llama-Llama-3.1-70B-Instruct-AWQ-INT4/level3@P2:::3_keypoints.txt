- Proposes dasNet: a deep network with attention-based top-down feedback to improve image classification.  
- Draws inspiration from human visual cortex feedback connections for object recognition.  
- Combines a standard Maxout network with iterative feedback allowing the network to focus internal attention.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Introduction gives a thorough background and highlights feedforward CNN limitations.  
- Training framed as reinforcement learning; uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to evolve the policy.  
- Technical presentation is strong with detailed description of architecture and training algorithm.  
- Experimental evaluation on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 is thorough and reports state-of-the-art performance.  
- Novel and original approach that integrates bottom-up and top-down signals and uses gated signals to constrain representations.  
- Visualizations help interpret the model and illustrate how the attention policy influences performance.  
- Method is more complex than feedforward CNNs, raising practical adoption concerns.  
- Lack of results on larger datasets (e.g., ILSVRC) raises scalability questions.  
- Representation quality depends heavily on the learned attention policy; authors should show failure cases.  
- Paper needs deeper analysis of dasNet dynamics, including how different values of T affect performance.  
- Overall assessment: promising, technically sound, and worth further study if scalability and failure-mode analyses are addressed.
