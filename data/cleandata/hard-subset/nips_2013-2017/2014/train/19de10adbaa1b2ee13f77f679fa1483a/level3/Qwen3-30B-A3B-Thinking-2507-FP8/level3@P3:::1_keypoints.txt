- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with internal selective attention via feedback connections that dynamically modulates filter activations during classification.  
- Uses a reinforcement learning policy (learned via SNES) to gate filter activations across multiple passes rather than a fixed attention mechanism.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and is biologically inspired by feedback/attention in visual cortex (Felleman & Essen; Lamme).  
- Key innovation: learning the attention policy with RL to iteratively refine predictions, differentiating it from prior hard-coded or simplified attention approaches.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (≈6% relative improvement over vanilla CNN).  
- Provides a clear illustrative example (Figure 3) showing how the network corrects misclassifications by emphasizing/suppressing filters.  
- Ablation/dynamics studies (varying number of passes) show robustness of the iterative attention mechanism.  
- Paper is technically sound with appropriate experiments and analysis.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with a helpful algorithm presentation (Algorithm 1) and strong related work discussion.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future work on scaling to larger datasets.  
- Main limitation: experiments limited to CIFAR datasets; no demonstration on large-scale datasets like ImageNet.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of why specific filters are emphasized or suppressed, which would give insight into learned attention.  
- Significant contribution: empirical evidence that learned feedback/attention improves CNN performance even with modest compute resources.  
- Evaluation: high quality and clarity, moderate originality (combination of existing techniques), and significant empirical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong technical execution and results, though somewhat incremental and limited in scale.
