- Proposes dasNet: a Maxout-based neural architecture with feedback gating for selective attention learned via reinforcement learning (SNES), showing SOTA on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.
- Architecture builds on Maxout and RL, inspired by visual cortex feedback connections.
- Novel use of reinforcement learning to learn gating parameters that iteratively focus network attention.
- Significant departure from feedforward nets by incorporating feedback to improve classification.
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness on challenging datasets.
- Paper lacks clear explanation of why dasNet is effective; representation and learned-theta analyses are inadequate.
- Several equations contain typos or need clearer explanation.
- Algorithm description is unclear in places and notation is not well-defined.
- Necessity and justification of theta regularization are unclear.
- Final gradient updates and their justification are not well-explained.
- Originality is strong: novel architecture and novel RL-based gating approach.
- Potential significance: could improve classification and be applicable across vision tasks; represents a shift from pure supervised learning.
- Pro-acceptance argument: novel, effective approach with strong empirical performance.
- Con-acceptance argument: insufficient theoretical/analytical justification, clarity, and methodological detail.
- Recommendation: provide clearer explanation of effectiveness and deeper analysis of image representations and learned theta.
- Recommendation: correct typos and clarify equations and notation.
- Recommendation: justify theta regularization and clarify final gradient computations.
- Recommendation: explore dynamical-systems connections and analyze internal network dynamics.
- Recommendation: analyze hard cases and test effects of constraining learned-theta statistics.
