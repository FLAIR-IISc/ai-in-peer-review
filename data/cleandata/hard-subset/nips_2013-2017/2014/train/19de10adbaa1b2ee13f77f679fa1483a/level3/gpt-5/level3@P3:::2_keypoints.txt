- Paper adds multiplicative per-feature-map gates to a Maxout CNN, updated over multiple passes via a learned top-down policy to adapt inference at test time.  
- Policy is a deterministic neural network mapping pooled activations and class probs to gates; optimized with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (≈1M params).  
- Shows consistent accuracy gains on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline and set new single-model results at submission.  
- Clear problem framing: enables adaptive reconfiguration of a trained CNN at test time under resource/task limits.  
- Sensible design choices: positive gates via scaled softmax and emphasis on misclassified examples via λboost.  
- Main weakness: black-box training with limited analysis — why and when the gating policy succeeds is largely unexplained.  
- Analysis of representations and learned policy θ is superficial (mean/variance only); deeper statistics and mechanisms are needed.  
- Algorithmic clarity issues: hM (h(·)) is not precisely defined — missing exact statistics, ordering, and dimensions.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored per sampled policy and how they’re reduced/aggregated.  
- Notation problems: several equations have ambiguous/incorrect indices (e.g., Eq. 8, Eq. 10, Eq. 11) and reused symbols (i, d) that hinder reproducibility.  
- Eq. 8 likely needs vT[d] or an image index; Eq. 10 must unambiguously define d ∈ {1,…,C}; Eq. 11 needs a distinct summation index and batch-averaging clarity.  
- Regularization question: why L2-regularize θ inside sampled fitness rather than regularizing µ directly given NES updates distribution params (µ, Σ)?  
- Need explicit statement that SNES gradients are with respect to distribution parameters (µ, Σ), not θ itself.  
- Interpretability lacking: visualizations are anecdotal; require systematic analysis of gate dynamics, layer effects, and class-specific motifs.  
- Suggest analyzing hard cases: images corrected by dasNet vs base Maxout with confusion-pair breakdowns, gate trajectories, and affected layers/features.  
- Suggest experiments constraining θ statistics (fix mean gate to 1, bound per-layer variance, tie gates) to study performance/robustness trade-offs.  
- Conceptual positioning: method resembles learned recurrent dynamical inference more than standard RL; a dynamical-systems analysis (stability, fixed points, Jacobian) would clarify why it works.  
- Clarity improvements needed: fix equation typos, fully specify Algorithm 1, reintroduce variables, and list SNES hyperparameters (step-size, damping, variance schedule).  
- Suggested ablations: vary T (0/1/2/5), random/frozen gates, per-layer vs per-filter gating, alternative optimization (policy gradients), and λboost/λL2 effects.  
- Report computational overhead: SNES training time, inference slowdown ≈T, and memory footprint for gates/observations.  
- Explore stability: sensitivity to gate perturbations and behavior beyond trained T (fixed-point behavior).  
- Pros: strong empirical improvements, principled and modular mechanism, well-motivated by neuroscience and attention literature.  
- Cons: black-box method with limited insight, notation/algorithmic ambiguities, missing ablations and in-depth policy/dynamics analysis.  
- Overall: interesting and effective recurrent-style approach worthy of acceptance if authors fix clarity issues and substantially deepen analysis; otherwise borderline.
