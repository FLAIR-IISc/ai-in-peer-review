- Proposal: Introduces dasNet, a CNN with attention-based top-down feedback that multiplicatively gates intermediate feature maps across recurrent passes.  
- Training: Policy mapping layer summaries and logits to gates is learned via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Results: Improves over a strong Maxout baseline and achieves state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to recurrent/attention vision models and policy-search RL, but is distinct in gating internal CNN representations with learned top-down signals.  
- Technical quality: Method is technically sound, with coherent objective, observation/action design, and SNES optimization.  
- Evidence: Visualizations support that feedback meaningfully alters representations; temporal evaluation (varying T) is informative.  
- Scalability concern: No ILSVRC/ImageNet experiments and compute/latency overheads are not quantified.  
- Baselines limited: Lacks comparisons to other recurrent/feedback CNNs, glimpse/attention models, and supervised/backprop-trained gating.  
- Failure analysis missing: Dependence on the attention policy acknowledged but failure cases and sensitivity are not analyzed.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and organized with clear algorithmic details.  
- Reproducibility gaps: Missing exact observation dimensionality, gating ranges, SNES hyperparameters, compute budget, and compute–accuracy trade-offs across T.  
- Originality: Novel and interesting combination of internal multiplicative selective attention learned via black-box policy search on a state-of-the-art CNN.  
- Significance: CIFAR improvements and interpretability suggest potential impact, but impact would grow with demonstrated scalability and stronger baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel bottom-up/top-down integration; SOTA CIFAR results with informative visualizations; careful temporal evaluation showing semantic gates.  
- Cons against acceptance: No large-scale results or compute quantification; missing comparisons to alternative attention/recurrent methods and supervised gating; no systematic failure-case analysis.  
- Suggestions: Add large-scale experiments or partial ImageNet results, quantify overhead vs accuracy across T, compare to backprop-trained gates and recurrent baselines, and include failure-case taxonomy and policy ablations.  
- Overall: Paper is promising and worthy of further study.
