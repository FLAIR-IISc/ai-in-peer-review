- Introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback that dynamically modulate filter sensitivities via an RL policy (SNES) across multiple passes to correct misclassifications without retraining.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and neurobiology-inspired top-down feedback; achieves state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 (≈6% relative improvement).  
- Technical execution is robust: scalable policy learning over a million-dimensional space and thorough experimental validation.  
- Experiments include clear visualizations and dynamics analysis (Figures 1–3) showing meaningful attention behavior and gate informativeness.  
- Ablation (T=5) and boosting strategy for misclassified examples are well-motivated and effective.  
- Novelty lies in integrating Maxout, feedback, and SNES for selective attention in large CNNs, differing from prior RL and RNN-attention work.  
- Paper is very well written with a precise algorithm and intuitive explanations; only minor notation inconsistencies.  
- Significance: provides compelling evidence that feedback-driven iterative refinement improves CNN performance and advances biologically plausible modeling.  
- Limitation: evaluation confined to CIFAR-10/100 — scalability to ImageNet or larger datasets is not demonstrated.  
- Missing analysis of number of passes T beyond T=5, so saturation or degradation with more iterations is unclear.  
- Methodological gap: no comparison to standard RL methods (e.g., policy gradients) to justify SNES choice; claims about not forgetting need stronger analysis.  
- Related work could better engage recent attention literature and more clearly distinguish internal attention from spatial/external attention mechanisms.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing T ablation, RL method comparison, and discussion of ImageNet scalability.
