- Proposes dasNet, a novel deep network that adds attention-based top-down feedback to standard CNNs.  
- Trains dasNet using supervised learning plus a reinforcement-learning attention policy via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Architecture inspired by human visual cortex, with layers learning progressively abstract representations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Evaluates model behavior across time using gated signals to constrain representations.  
- Provides visualizations that aid interpretation of dasNet’s dynamics.  
- Does not evaluate on larger datasets (e.g., ILSVRC), leaving scalability untested.  
- Model complexity is higher than feedforward CNNs, potentially making implementation and training harder.  
- Representation quality relies heavily on the learned attention policy, which may be suboptimal in some cases.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient experimental detail for reproduction.  
- Recommends experiments on larger datasets to assess scalability and practical utility.  
- Suggests reporting or analyzing failure cases and exploring further applications and extensions of dasNet.
