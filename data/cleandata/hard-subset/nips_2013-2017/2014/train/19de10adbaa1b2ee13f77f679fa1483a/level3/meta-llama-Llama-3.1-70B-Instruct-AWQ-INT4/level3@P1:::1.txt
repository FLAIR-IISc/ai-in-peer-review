The paper "Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections" presents a novel deep neural network architecture, called Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The authors propose a reinforcement learning approach to train the attention policy on top of a pre-trained convolutional neural network (CNN). The key idea is to allow the network to iteratively focus its internal attention on some of its convolutional filters, enabling it to correct its initial guesses and improve classification performance.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the dasNet architecture, the reinforcement learning approach, and the experimental results. The presentation is good, with a logical flow of ideas and a clear structure. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work in the field of computer vision and reinforcement learning.

The novelty of the paper lies in the combined approach of using feedback connections and reinforcement learning to implement internal selective attention in a deep neural network. While individual components of the approach may not be highly novel, the way they are combined and executed is well-chosen and effective.

The experimental results demonstrate that dasNet achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, outperforming previous models on unaugmented datasets. The results provide empirical support for the idea that feedback and attention are promising directions for vision models.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A well-designed and effective architecture for implementing internal selective attention in a deep neural network.
* A thorough and well-executed experimental evaluation, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on challenging datasets.
* A clear and well-written presentation, making the paper easy to follow and understand.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The approach may be computationally expensive, requiring significant resources to train and evaluate the network.
* The scalability of the approach to larger problems and datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is not demonstrated in the paper.

To further improve the paper, the authors could consider scaling the approach to larger problems and datasets, exploring the application of dasNet to other computer vision tasks, and providing a more detailed analysis of the attention policy learned by the network.

In terms of originality, the paper scores high, as it presents a novel combination of techniques to implement internal selective attention in a deep neural network. The significance of the paper is also high, as it provides empirical support for the idea that feedback and attention are promising directions for vision models.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.

Arguments for acceptance:

* Novel and effective approach to implementing internal selective attention in a deep neural network.
* State-of-the-art performance on challenging datasets.
* Clear and well-written presentation.

Arguments against acceptance:

* Computational expense of the approach.
* Limited scalability to larger problems and datasets.
* Lack of detailed analysis of the attention policy learned by the network.

Recommendations for future work:

* Scale the approach to larger problems and datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
* Explore the application of dasNet to other computer vision tasks.
* Provide a more detailed analysis of the attention policy learned by the network.
