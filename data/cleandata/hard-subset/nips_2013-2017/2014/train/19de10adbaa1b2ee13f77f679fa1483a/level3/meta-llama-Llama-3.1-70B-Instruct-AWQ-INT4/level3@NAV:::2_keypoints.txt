- Paper proposes dasNet, a CNN variant with internal selective attention via feedback connections and a reinforcement-learned control policy.  
- DasNet allows layers to influence other layers across successive image passes.  
- Empirical results: dasNet outperforms prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (≈6% relative improvement).  
- Strength: novel, innovative architecture addressing CNN limitations by adding internal selective attention.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition that is easy to follow.  
- Strength: thorough experimental analysis, including effects of different T values on performance.  
- Weakness: method behaves like a black box with little explanation of why or how attention decisions are made.  
- Weakness: inadequate analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters; details on their learning and updates are lacking.  
- Weakness: unclear notation and equations (e.g., ambiguous summation index in Eq.11 and missing image index in Eq.8).  
- Weakness: theta regularization is used without sufficient justification or explanation of its effect on gradients.  
- Weakness: algorithm presentation is unclear with undefined variables and ambiguous notation.  
- Originality: approach is original in implementing internal selective attention within CNNs despite attention being a known concept.  
- Significance: results are significant and could impact CNN performance across tasks like recognition, segmentation, and parsing.  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims, but analysis of internal representations needs improvement.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but some equations and notation require clarification.  
- Recommendations: provide deeper insight into internal workings and attention learning, improve representation/theta analyses, clarify equations/indices, justify theta regularization, and fix algorithm notation.
