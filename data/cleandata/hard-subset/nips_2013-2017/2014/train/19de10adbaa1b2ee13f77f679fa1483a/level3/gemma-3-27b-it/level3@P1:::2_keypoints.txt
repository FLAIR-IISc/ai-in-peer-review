- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that dynamically adjusts convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Core idea: iterative, attention-driven refinement of feature selection to mimic non-stationary brain processing.  
- Trains the attentional policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle the large parameter space.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, notably on difficult examples.  
- Situates work within prior CNN, neuroscience (feedback in vision), RL-based attention, and recurrent image-processing literature.  
- Strength: introduces adaptability into CNNs, enabling iterative “thinking” about images in a biologically plausible way.  
- Strength: SNES is a pragmatic solution for high-dimensional policy learning.  
- Strength: improved performance on unaugmented CIFAR datasets is a significant empirical contribution.  
- Weakness: the mechanism behind performance gains is largely a black box with insufficient analysis of the learned policy.  
- Weakness: internal network dynamics (e.g., early layer activation changes) are under-explained.  
- Weakness: the RL framing feels tenuous—deterministic policy with immediate rewards resembles a recurrent network more than standard RL.  
- Suggestion: exploring dynamical systems theory may be a more appropriate analytical framework.  
- Clarity issues: several equations contain errors or ambiguities (e.g., Eq.11 summation index, Eq.8 image index) and unexplained symbols.  
- Algorithmic description problems: undefined function h_M and unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated per image or overwritten.  
- Methodological concern: justification for theta regularization is weak and gradient update details are sparse.  
- Originality: novel combination of SNES-driven policy learning with dynamic filter weighting in CNNs.  
- Significance: results are promising for more robust, adaptable image classification, but limited by lack of interpretability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, novel architecture addressing CNN adaptability, and successful SNES application.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient interpretability, reproducibility and notation issues, weak design justifications, and superficial RL connection.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on clarifying algorithm/notation, improving interpretability of learned policies, and addressing analytical framing (e.g., dynamical systems).
