- Proposes dasNet, a novel deep neural architecture that adds attention-based top-down feedback to improve image classification.
- Builds on a standard Maxout network with iterative feedback connections that focus attention on discriminative image features.
- Uses reinforcement learning to train feedback connections, specifically evolving the policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of the architecture and components.
- Experimental results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show improved classification performance, particularly on difficult instances.
- Provides visualizations of the attentional policy and analyses of classification performance.
- Strength: clear intuition and thorough explanation of the novel RL-based feedback training.
- Strength: thorough explanation of the SNES algorithm and how it evolves the policy.
- Limitation: no experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet), leaving scalability unclear.
- Limitation: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale tasks.
- Limitation: does not analyze representation quality of learned features and its effect on generalization.
- Limitation: builds on prior attentional and RL work and could present a deeper related-work comparison.
- Recommendation: evaluate dasNet on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
- Recommendation: analyze representation quality and provide computational complexity measurements.
- Recommendation: provide a more thorough related-work analysis and comparisons.
- Overall assessment: potentially significant contribution to computer vision and deep learning.
- Reviewer score: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
