- Introduces dasNet: augments a feedforward Maxout CNN with feedback connections that gate convolutional filter activations for internal selective attention.  
- Attention policy is learned (not hard-coded) using reinforcement learning (Natural Evolution Strategies) on top of a pre-trained convnet.  
- Motivated by recurrent processing in human vision and builds on state-of-the-art CNNs.  
- Strength: achieves new state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10/100 (no augmentation), showing feedback/attention improve performance.  
- Strength: technically sound approach with well-designed experiments, especially effective on ambiguous classification cases.  
- Strength: analysis shows final gate activations contain substantial class-relevant information.  
- Strength: paper is very well-written and clearly organized with sufficient methodological detail.  
- Weakness: individual components (CNNs, feedback, RL for attention) are not novel; novelty lies in their combination.  
- Weakness: evaluated only on small datasets; scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is unclear.  
- Weakness: training time (4 days on CIFAR) indicates potential computational bottlenecks; authors should discuss complexity and scaling.  
- Recommendation: accept; encourage authors to scale dasNet to larger datasets like ImageNet to validate generalizability.
