This paper introduces dasNet, a Maxout CNN augmented with multiplicative, per-feature-map gates that are iteratively updated via top-down feedback. At each pass over an image, a deterministic policy maps summary statistics of internal activations and class probabilities to a new set of gates, enabling selective amplification/suppression of filters. The policy is trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to avoid the prohibitive dimensionality of gradient-based RL. On CIFAR-10/100 (unaugmented), dasNet outperforms prior baselines. The work connects to recurrent processing in vision and selective attention via policy search/glimpse models, but differs by manipulating internal channel sensitivities rather than spatial glimpses, and by using black-box optimization for the controller.

Quality: Empirical gains are solid and the iterative dynamics appear stable. However, the approach remains largely a black box: the analysis of internal representations and of the learned SNES distribution parameters (mean/variance) is shallow and uninformative. The RL framing is debatable: reward is immediate and the policy is deterministic; the model seems closer to a recurrent dynamical system (or iterative inference) than to RL. Algorithmic clarity needs work: Algorithm 1 leaves hM undefined; it is unclear whether F[i] and Θ[i] are accumulated per image or overwritten; Eq. 11’s summation index is ambiguous; Eq. 8 appears to need an image index j; symbols x and d after Eq. 11 are undefined. The necessity and effect of θ-regularization (λL2) are not justified, and it is unclear how “final gradient updates” are computed in SNES (are µ and Σ interpreted as posterior hyperparameters?).

Clarity: Exposition is generally good, but several equations contain typos or require clearer derivations and notation.

Originality: A thoughtful step beyond fixed-parameter feedforward nets, combining internal attention with evolutionary policy search. While related ideas exist in recurrent/feedback models, the specific gating mechanism and training pipeline are interesting.

Significance: The accuracy gains and demonstration of adaptive reconfiguration under resource/task limits are valuable; improved interpretability would increase impact.

Suggestions:
- Recast and analyze the model as a dynamical system (fixed points, stability, relation to RNNs) rather than RL.
- Provide deeper probes of internal dynamics: why do layer-1 activations increase on the first iteration? effect of the global softmax scaling on stability? trajectories of gates in local image regions.
- Systematically analyze “hard cases” corrected by dasNet; test constraining θ statistics (e.g., variance penalties, gate bounds).
- Clarify Algorithm 1 and all equation/notation issues; ablate λboost and λL2; compare against backprop-through-time recurrent baselines.

Pros:
- Strong empirical improvements
- Simple, general modification to CNNs
- Addresses adaptive reconfiguration

Cons:
- Limited interpretability
- Algorithmic/notation ambiguities
- Questionable RL framing; missing theoretical analysis

Overall: Interesting and promising recurrent-style approach; recommend acceptance only if clarity and analysis issues are substantially addressed.
