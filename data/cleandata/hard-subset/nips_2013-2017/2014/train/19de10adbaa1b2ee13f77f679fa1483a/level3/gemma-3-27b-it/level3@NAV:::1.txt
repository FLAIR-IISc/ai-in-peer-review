## Review of Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections

This paper introduces dasNet, a convolutional neural network augmented with feedback connections to implement internal selective attention. The core idea is to allow the network to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities after an initial feedforward pass, guided by a policy learned through reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES). The motivation stems from observations in human vision, where iterative processing and attentional mechanisms play a crucial role in complex perception. 

The approach builds upon existing work in Maxout networks and combines them with the concept of recurrent processing and reinforcement learning – none of which are individually novel. However, the authors successfully integrate these components to create a functioning system that demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented data. This is particularly noteworthy given the computational challenge of applying RL to networks of this scale, which the authors address using SNES. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR is a significant accomplishment and validates the effectiveness of the approach.
*   **Well-Chosen Combination of Techniques:** The integration of feedback connections, reinforcement learning, and Maxout networks is a clever and well-executed combination.
*   **Effective Use of SNES:** Addressing the large parameter space of the attentional policy with SNES demonstrates technical proficiency and practicality.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the methodology and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope:** The experiments are currently limited to the CIFAR datasets. Scaling the approach to larger, more complex datasets (like ImageNet) is crucial to assess its generalizability and potential real-world impact.
*   **Interpretability:** While the authors visualize filter changes, a deeper understanding of *what* the network is attending to and *why* would be valuable. More detailed analysis of the learned attention policies is needed.
*   **Computational Cost:** The training time (4 days on a GTX 560 Ti) is considerable, and further work could explore methods to improve efficiency.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel combination of existing techniques that yields strong empirical results.
*   It provides empirical evidence supporting the potential benefits of feedback and attention mechanisms in deep vision models.
*   The work is technically sound and the presentation is clear.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The limited scope of the experiments restricts the generalizability of the findings.



**Overall Recommendation:** 

I recommend **acceptance** with the strong encouragement that the authors scale their approach to larger datasets like ImageNet. Addressing the interpretability of the learned attention mechanisms would also significantly strengthen the contribution. While no single component is groundbreaking, the combined approach is well-motivated, effectively executed, and demonstrates promising results.
