- Introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback-based selective attention trained via SNES to iteratively modulate filter sensitivities.  
- Achieves ~6% relative improvement over Maxout baselines on CIFAR-10/100, setting a new unaugmented state-of-the-art.  
- Positioned as a step toward more biologically plausible, adaptable networks with post-training iterative processing.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical gains on challenging datasets demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Conceptually interesting idea: selective attention via feedback addresses a key limitation of fixed feedforward CNNs.  
- Technical contribution: applying reinforcement learning/SNES to very high-dimensional policy spaces (~1M+ parameters).  
- Demonstrates qualitative corrections of misclassifications by reweighting filters (e.g., cats vs dogs).  

Weaknesses:
- Lack of interpretability: analysis of internal representations and learned parameters is superficial and uninformative.  
- Algorithmic/notation issues: Algorithm 1 and several equations (h_M, indexing, undefined symbols) are confusing or ambiguous.  
- Inadequate theoretical/dynamical analysis: connections to recurrent/dynamical systems and internal dynamics are not explored.  
- Questionable/unclear regularization: role and justification of theta regularization and gradient/posterior update details are missing.  

Recommendations:
- Explore and clarify connections to dynamical/recurrent systems given immediate rewards and deterministic policy.  
- Provide deeper analysis of internal dynamics (layer activation changes, gating evolution, local effects).  
- Analyze failure/hard cases where Maxout fails but dasNet succeeds and test constraints on learned theta statistics.  
- Fix and clarify algorithmic exposition and equation indexing for reproducibility.  

Overall assessment:
- Interesting and effective recurrent-style approach with solid empirical evidence, but limited scientific insight due to black-box interpretability issues.  
- Technically sound and worthy of acceptance pending revisions to improve interpretability, theoretical clarity, and exposition.
