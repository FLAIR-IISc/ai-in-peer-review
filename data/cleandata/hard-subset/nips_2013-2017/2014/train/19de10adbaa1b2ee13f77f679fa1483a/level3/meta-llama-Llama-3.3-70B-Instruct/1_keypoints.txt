- Novel framework: constructs priors for structured variables via maximum-entropy information projection of a base distribution onto constraint sets.  
- Sparse-case solution: addresses intractable optimal prior with a family of parameterized approximations indexed by subsets.  
- Theoretical guarantees: provides thorough analysis and proofs, including a greedy forward selection with (1-1/e) approximation.  
- Empirical performance: outperforms baselines (Lasso, Spike-and-Slab) on support recovery and predictive accuracy in simulations.  
- Real-data validation: applied to functional neuroimaging data, achieving positive R2 and good predictive performance.  
- Clarity and exposition: paper is well-written, clear, and accessible with a concise methodology description.  
- Originality: novel combination of maximum-entropy principle and sparse structured priors with a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Main weakness: potential scalability issues for very large datasets due to expensive normalization-constant computation.  
- Mitigation: authors propose a practical greedy procedure that empirically alleviates scalability concerns.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significance and solid empirical/theoretical support.
