- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with learned top-down feedback connections.  
- Inspired by recurrent processing in biological vision, deviating from purely feedforward approaches.  
- Uses reinforcement learning to train a policy that modulates convolutional filters over multiple sequential passes.  
- Allows iterative refinement of internal representations and selective focus on discriminative features.  
- Builds on state-of-the-art Maxout networks by adding a dynamic, filter-level attention mechanism.  
- Strength: original idea combining bottom-up features with learned top-down attention.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Strength: visualizations and temporal analysis provide insight into the learned policy's behavior.  
- Main weakness: scalability concerns due to increased complexity and computational cost versus feedforward CNNs.  
- Missing large-scale experiments (e.g., ILSVRC), leaving practical viability for large problems unclear.  
- Paper could be improved by analyzing attention-policy failure cases and robustness limitations.  
- Overall: technically sound, clearly written, significant results for tested datasets, recommended for acceptance and likely to inspire further research.
