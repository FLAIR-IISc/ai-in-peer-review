- Proposal: dasNet — a CNN with feedback connections that dynamically modulate filter activations to implement internal attention, with the feedback policy learned via SNES; achieves state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Novelty: Combines convolutional networks, learned internal (filter-level) attention, and reinforcement learning (SNES) in a way not previously explored.  
- Motivation: Biologically inspired by recurrent/feedback processing in vision; convincingly argued as useful for difficult classification cases.  
- Empirical strength: Clear performance gains and new SOTA results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented).  
- Method clarity: Well-written, well-organized paper with helpful visualizations (e.g., filter activation changes).  
- Related work: Thorough and appropriately positions the contribution among CNN, attention, and recurrent-processing literature.  
- Scalability concern: Lacks detailed discussion or experiments on extending to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) and the computational cost of SNES.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient ablation on RL and SNES hyperparameters (e.g., reward weights, population size) and robustness.  
- Interpretability gap: Limited analysis of what the learned policy attends to and why—Figure 3 is helpful but not sufficient.  
- Architecture dependence: Uses Maxout as backbone, which may limit generalizability to modern architectures (e.g., ResNet).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective approach with strong empirical results, solid motivation, and a practical solution (SNES) for high-dimensional policies.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Unclear scalability, limited policy interpretability, and possible dependence on Maxout architecture.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, technically sound contribution that outweighs weaknesses; recommends acceptance with suggestions to address scalability and deeper policy analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept, and encourage future work on scalability, interpretability, and testing with other base architectures.
