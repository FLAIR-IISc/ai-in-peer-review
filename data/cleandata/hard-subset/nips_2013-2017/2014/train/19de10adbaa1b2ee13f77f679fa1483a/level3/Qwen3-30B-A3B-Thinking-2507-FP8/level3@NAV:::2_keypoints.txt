- Proposes Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet): CNNs with feedback-based selective attention gating for iterative, post-training focus on relevant features.  
- Frames dasNet as a non-stationary CNN adapted after training via reinforcement learning (SNES) to modulate filter sensitivities.  
- Claims conceptual advance enabling sequential “thinking” about ambiguous classifications beyond fixed feed-forward networks.  
- Strong empirical result: ~6% relative improvement over state-of-the-art CNNs on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Architecture is biologically motivated and the exposition is generally clear.  
- Major interpretability issue: method treated as a black box with little explanation of why it works.  
- Inadequate analysis of learned theta (mean/variance) — results are uninformative and don’t explain which filter weightings help classification.  
- Multiple typos and unclear equations (e.g., Eq.11 summation index error; Eq.8 likely needs image index j).  
- Algorithm 1 is unclear: h_M is undefined and it’s ambiguous whether F[i] and Θ[i] are stored per image or overwritten.  
- Notation problems persist (symbols x and d undefined after Eq.11).  
- Lacks deeper analysis of internal dynamics (e.g., why layer-1 activations spike on first iteration, impact of global scaling, local gating evolution).  
- Should analyze hard cases (images misclassified by standard Maxout but corrected by dasNet) to illustrate mechanism.  
- Needs experiments constraining learned theta statistics and investigation of whether theta regularization is necessary.  
- Unclear how final gradient updates are computed or how posterior hyperparameters are adjusted.  
- Originality: novel application of SNES to attention gating, but theoretical grounding and algorithmic clarity are insufficient for top-tier acceptance.  
- Overall: interesting and effective with strong accuracy gains, but interpretability, theoretical justification, and presentation issues must be addressed before acceptance.
