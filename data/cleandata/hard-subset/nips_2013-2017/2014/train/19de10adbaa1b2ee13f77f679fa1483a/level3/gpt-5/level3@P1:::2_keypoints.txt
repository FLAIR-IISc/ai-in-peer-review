- Proposes dasNet: a Maxout CNN augmented with per-channel multiplicative gates and a deterministic feedback policy that iteratively modulates gates over multiple passes.  
- Policy maps averaged layer feature maps, classification activations, and class probabilities to positive, unity-mean channel gains via a scaled softmax.  
- Policy has ~10^6 parameters and is optimized with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) due to high dimensionality.  
- On CIFAR-10/100, T=5 recurrent passes improve top-1 accuracy over a strong Maxout baseline and corrects ambiguous-class mistakes.  
- Positions dasNet as selective attention inspired by cortical feedback and related to recurrent/iterative vision and RL-based attention models.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and connects to recurrent/top-down modulation, glimpse/attention RL models, and NES/SNES optimization literature.  
- Distinct from glimpse-based attention: performs global, channel-wise internal modulation rather than saccades or spatial glimpses.  
- Strength: simple, well-motivated mechanism that yields state-of-the-art unaugmented CIFAR performance at the time and stable multi-step dynamics.  
- Strength: gate vectors contain class-relevant information and SNES is a practical optimizer for the large policy.  
- Weakness: method is largely a black box with limited analysis of learned dynamics and sensitivity by layer.  
- Weakness: RL framing is weak—deterministic policy, single-step reward at T, and no explicit temporal credit assignment via gradients.  
- Weakness: some algorithmic details, regularization choices, boosting weights, and SNES update specifics are unclear or unjustified.  
- Weakness: test-time computational overhead from multiple passes is not quantified.  
- Clarity issues: ambiguous notation in Algorithm 1, undefined h(M), unclear accumulation of fitness across images, and inconsistent indexing in equations.  
- Clarity issues: need explicit indexing for images in Eqs. 8–9 and fix summation index collision in Eq. 11; define x_j and d_j near usage.  
- Clarity issues: explain and justify scaled softmax action mapping and clarify “unaugmented” SOTA claim versus training augmentation.  
- Clarity issues: provide SNES pseudo-code and all hyperparameters (population, learning rates, initial Σ).  
- Originality: novel internal, learned channel-wise attention across recurrent passes and unusual use of SNES for million-dimensional policy search.  
- Significance: meaningful accuracy gains and a promising direction for dynamic test-time reconfiguration of CNNs via internal feedback.  
- Recommendation for analysis: treat model as deterministic RNN, analyze fixed points/stability, and relate to recurrent batch-normalization-like scalings.  
- Suggested experiments: per-layer gate distributions over time, spatial effects on activation maps, and systematic analysis of corrected hard cases.  
- Ablation suggestions: test T sensitivity, per-layer vs all-layer gating, softmax scaling removal, stochastic vs deterministic policy, λ_boost and λ_L2 effects, and smaller policy capacities.  
- Policy-distribution suggestions: study effects of clamping or narrowing Σ and priors on µ to assess dependence on variance/exploration.  
- Regularization concerns: justify including λ_L2 in SNES fitness, clarify which covariance parameters are updated, and detail posterior hyperparameters.  
- Baseline suggestions: compare to backprop-through-time training of the gating policy (or reduced-capacity variants) and simple heuristics (e.g., one extra linear pass).  
- Reproducibility: provide random seeds, exact preprocessing, learned θ statistics (µ, diag(Σ)), and full hyperparameter/specification details.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple effective mechanism, empirical gains, demonstrates value of feedback/recurrent processing, and scalable black-box optimization.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited interpretability/theory, notation/algorithm ambiguities, overstated RL framing, missing ablations/runtime analysis, and “unaugmented” inconsistency.  
- Overall: compelling and effective recurrent-style approach that advances performance and suggests fruitful follow-up, but needs clearer algorithmic specification and deeper dynamics analysis.
