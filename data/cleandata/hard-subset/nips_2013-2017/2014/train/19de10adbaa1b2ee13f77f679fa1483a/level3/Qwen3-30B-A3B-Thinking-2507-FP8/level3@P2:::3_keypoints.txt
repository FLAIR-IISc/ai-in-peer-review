- Paper introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback connections that iteratively reweights convolutional filters via a learned policy (SNES) to improve ambiguous classifications.  
- Method uses dynamic filter weighting/gating across multiple passes on a pre-trained Maxout CNN, trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies.  
- Experimental result: ~6% relative improvement over baseline on CIFAR-10/100, claiming new state-of-the-art for unaugmented data.  
- Originality: novel fusion of RL-based policy learning and feedback in deep networks, inspired by biological cortical feedback.  
- Novel application: first to apply such feedback-driven attention directly to state-of-the-art CNNs for classification without simplifying datasets.  
- Significance: bridges neuroscience and deep learning and shows iterative internal attention can correct misclassifications (e.g., cat vs. dog).  
- Strength — Technical quality: SNES effectively handles the million-dimensional policy space and experiments (including ablations) are well-designed.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: gating mechanism shown informative (40.7% 15-NN accuracy) and visualizations effectively illustrate attention dynamics.  
- Strength — Novelty of approach: refines a pre-trained network via feedback rather than retraining recurrently, integrating Maxout with iterative gating.  
- Weakness — Scalability: no results on larger datasets like ImageNet and 4-day single-GPU training on CIFAR raises practical concerns.  
- Weakness — Missing failure analysis: paper lacks examples where feedback worsens performance or robustness evaluations.  
- Weakness — Complexity vs. benefit: added SNES training and gated architecture may not justify the modest CIFAR gains without cost-benefit comparisons to simpler methods.  
- Pro for acceptance: first RL-learned feedback policy improving SOTA CNNs, strong CIFAR results, biologically motivated and technically sound.  
- Con for acceptance: absent ImageNet validation, missing failure/scalability analyses, and unclear computational trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: technically solid and novel but not yet broadly validated — accept with reservations pending scalability/ImageNet experiments or deeper complexity analysis.
