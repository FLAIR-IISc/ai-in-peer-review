- Summary: Proposes dasNet—a Maxout CNN with recurrent, multiplicative per-feature-map gates controlled by a learned policy (SNES) over T sequential test passes, improving CIFAR-10/100 performance to SOTA for unaugmented setups.  
- Technical soundness: Gating mechanism, observation/action spaces, boosted loss, and use of SNES are well-defined and justified; dynamics analyses support that feedback is effective.  
- Empirical support: Consistent gains and qualitative diagnostics versus a strong Maxout baseline, but key ablations (gating only top layers, linear policy, BPTT-trained policy) are missing.  
- Reproducibility: Architecture and hyperparameters are described, yet exact policy parameter counts and test-time compute/FLOP costs are not reported.  
- Clarity: Paper is clear and well-structured; needs to clarify what “unaugmented datasets” means given base-model data augmentation.  
- Originality: No single component is novel, but the specific combination (per-map multiplicative gating + high-dim SNES policy on Maxout CNN) and empirical demonstration are the main contributions.  
- Significance: Provides concrete evidence that internal feature-level feedback improves CNNs, though scope is limited to small-resolution benchmarks; scalability to larger datasets/tasks is open.  
- Strengths: Principled feedback formulation, sensible use of SNES for large policies, strong empirical gains, informative dynamics diagnostics, and good writing/positioning.  
- Weaknesses: Evaluation limited to CIFAR-10/100, missing critical ablations/comparisons, coarse global (non-spatial) gating, unquantified multi-pass compute/latency, and limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Key ablations requested: (1) gate only last conv layer; (2) linear policy baseline; (3) supervised BPTT for policy; (4) policy-free iterative-refinement baseline.  
- Reporting requests: Clarify SOTA comparisons (single-model vs ensembles/augmentations), provide parameter counts for base CNN and policy, and give per-image FLOPs/time for T passes and adaptive-T behavior.  
- Further analyses suggested: Per-class error improvements, error-mode analysis, and exploration of spatially structured/per-region gating.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Demonstrates feedback/attention improves strong CNNs to SOTA, is simple/applicable, and includes thoughtful dynamics analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Results confined to small datasets, missing ablations leave causality uncertain, and compute/latency trade-offs are unquantified.  
- Recommendation: Leaning accept; advise adding larger-scale experiments, key ablations, compute trade-off analyses, and exploring more expressive/adaptive gating for camera-ready and future work.
