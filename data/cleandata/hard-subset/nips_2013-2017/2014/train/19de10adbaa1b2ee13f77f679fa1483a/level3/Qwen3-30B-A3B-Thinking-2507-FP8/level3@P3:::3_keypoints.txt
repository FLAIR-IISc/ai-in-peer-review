- Proposes dasNet: a CNN architecture with top-down feedback that iteratively adjusts filter sensitivities (internal selective attention) across multiple passes.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn a high-dimensional attention policy, avoiding standard RL limitations.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and integrates feedback into pre-trained feedforward CNNs rather than creating entirely new architectures.  
- Distinguishes itself by focusing on internal selective attention within an existing network instead of learning new visual features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100, ~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs on unaugmented data.  
- Offers compelling visualizations showing dynamic emphasis/de-emphasis of filters that correct misclassifications.  
- Includes ablation studies (dynamics, information content of gates) that support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: novel, biologically inspired model design that elegantly combines bottom-up and top-down signals.  
- Strength: thorough temporal evaluation and interpretability through gated signals and visualizations.  
- Weakness: no experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet), leaving scalability unclear.  
- Weakness: added complexity and computational cost relative to standard feedforward CNNs are not sufficiently justified.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases and guidance for improving the attention policy when it fails.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; authors should discuss scalability to larger datasets (ImageNet) in the final version.
