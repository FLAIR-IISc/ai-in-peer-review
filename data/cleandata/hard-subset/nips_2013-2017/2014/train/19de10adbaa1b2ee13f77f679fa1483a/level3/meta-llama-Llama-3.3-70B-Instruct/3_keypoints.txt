- Proposes a novel max-entropy prior defined as the information projection of a base measure onto constraint-supported distributions.  
- Focuses on sparse structured variables and shows approximate inference over convex subsets is equivalent to maximizing a submodular function with cardinality constraints.  
- Introduces an efficient greedy forward selection procedure with a (1 − 1/e) approximation guarantee.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and thorough explanation of the approach.  
- Significant technical contributions demonstrated through experiments on simulated and high-dimensional neuroimaging data.  
- Strong experimental results, but experiments are limited to a single dataset and may not generalize.  
- Assumes strong background in Bayesian inference and information theory, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of limitations and potential avenues for future work.
