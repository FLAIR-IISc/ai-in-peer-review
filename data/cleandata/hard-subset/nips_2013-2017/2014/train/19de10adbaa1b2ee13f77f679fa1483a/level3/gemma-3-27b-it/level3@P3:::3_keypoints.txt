- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with attention-based top-down feedback learned via reinforcement learning (SNES) to iteratively refine classifications.  
- Novel combination of CNNs and learned attention feedback, moving beyond feedforward architectures and adding biological plausibility.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Leverages prior work in CNNs, Maxout networks, RL, and neuroscience-inspired recurrent/top-down processing.  
- Distinguishes itself by learning the attention policy through RL rather than using predefined attention mechanisms.  
- Provides interpretability via visualizations showing how filter activations and attention evolve across iterations.  
- Analyses model dynamics across iterations and shows stability of the learned attention policy.  
- Main weakness: unclear scalability — no results on larger datasets like ImageNet and potential high computational cost of SNES.  
- Lacks thorough failure-case analysis and insights into when or why attention does not help.  
- Policy sensitivity is a concern; paper offers little guidance on policy design or initialization strategies.  
- Increased complexity (additional RL phase and larger parameter space) may limit practical applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, strong CIFAR results, insightful visualizations, and addresses a key CNN limitation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, limited failure analysis, and increased complexity hindering adoption.  
- Overall assessment: promising and strong paper deserving acceptance, but needs future work on scalability, failure analysis, and policy sensitivity.
