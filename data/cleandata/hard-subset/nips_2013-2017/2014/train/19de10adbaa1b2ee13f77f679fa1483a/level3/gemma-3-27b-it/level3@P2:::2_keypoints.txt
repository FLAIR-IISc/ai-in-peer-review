- Introduces DasNet: CNN with learned feedback connections that iteratively modulate filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Uses reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to learn a control policy for feedback.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, with a reported ~6% relative improvement on unaugmented data.  
- Builds on prior CNN and recurrent/attention work but uniquely modulates internal filter activations rather than spatial attention or external loops.  
- Novelty: learned feedback within a standard CNN is a genuine and noteworthy contribution.  
- Performance: empirical gains on standard benchmarks are compelling and significant.  
- Conceptual appeal: biologically inspired iterative attention rationale is well-motivated.  
- Practicality: implemented and evaluated on standard datasets, showing feasibility despite added complexity.  
- Addresses limitation of static CNNs by enabling adaptive reconfiguration for ambiguous inputs.  
- Interpretability: learned attention policy is a black box with insufficient explanation of why it works.  
- Limited analysis: internal dynamics, global scaling effects, and spatial evolution of gating variables are underexplored.  
- Algorithmic clarity: Algorithm 1 is ambiguous (undefined h_M, unclear updates for F[i] and Θ[i]).  
- Equation issues: several equations contain ambiguous indices or undefined symbols (e.g., Eq. 8, Eq. 11, later x and d).  
- Theoretical justification: weak rationale for theta regularization and missing gradient/posterior hyperparameter details.  
- Missed framing: authors do not adequately connect DasNet to dynamical systems / RNN perspectives despite recurrent-like behavior.  
- Paper quality: technically sound overall, but equation and algorithm issues detract from rigor.  
- Clarity: generally clear exposition but hampered by the specific ambiguities noted.  
- Originality: approach is highly original and departs meaningfully from traditional CNNs.  
- Significance: results are important and could spur further research into feedback/attention in CNNs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong benchmark performance, novel architecture, and biologically plausible motivation.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of interpretability, insufficient internal analysis, and algorithmic/equation ambiguities.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify Algorithm 1 and equations, strengthen theoretical justification, and provide deeper analysis of learned attention dynamics.
