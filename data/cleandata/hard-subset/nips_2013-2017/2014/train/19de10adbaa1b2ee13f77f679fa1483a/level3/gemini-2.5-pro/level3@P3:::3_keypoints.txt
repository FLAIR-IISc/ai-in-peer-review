- Introduces dasNet, which augments a feedforward CNN with learned top-down feedback to enable iterative attentional refinement.  
- Mimics human vision's fast feedforward pass followed by slower recurrent attentional processing.  
- Uses a pre-trained Maxout CNN for initial classification, then a policy network gates convolutional filters over multiple time steps.  
- Trains the attention policy with reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to emphasize or suppress features.  
- Novel integration of biological feedback concepts and RL-based attention into a modern deep CNN architecture.  
- Technically sound implementation with empirical validation on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, outperforming a strong Maxout baseline.  
- Clear writing and useful visualizations that show how filter activations are modulated to correct misclassifications.  
- Additional experiments analyze temporal dynamics and the information content of learned gates, supporting meaningful policy behavior.  
- Main weakness: lack of evaluation on large-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet), raising scalability and practical deployment concerns.  
- Computational complexity concern due to iterative inference (T=5 passes) and separate RL training phase.  
- Needs deeper analysis of the attention policy’s failure modes and robustness (e.g., amplifying noise or forgetting correct initial predictions).  
- Choice of SNES is justified by high dimensionality but lacks comparison to or discussion of gradient-based RL alternatives and sample efficiency.  
- Recommendation: accept — novelty and empirical gains outweigh scalability concerns; promising direction for future research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: highly novel architecture, state-of-the-art CIFAR results, iterative internal attention is forward-looking, strong analyses/visualizations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability/computational cost on large datasets and insufficient analysis of potential policy failure modes.
