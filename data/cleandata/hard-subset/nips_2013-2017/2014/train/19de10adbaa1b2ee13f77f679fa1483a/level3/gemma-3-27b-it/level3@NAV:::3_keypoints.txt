- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with top-down feedback attention that iteratively adjusts filter sensitivities after supervised training using reinforcement (SNES).  
- Novelty: attention learned post-training and distinct from standard forward-pass attention mechanisms.  
- Biological inspiration: recurrent processing in visual cortex motivates the design.  
- Empirical result: achieves state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- SNES chosen as a pragmatic method to search the large attention-policy parameter space.  
- Useful visualizations show filter activation changes and class-probability shifts, aiding interpretability.  
- Dynamics experiments indicate stable performance beyond trained iteration counts.  
- Major weakness: scalability not demonstrated—no ImageNet results and unclear computational cost for larger datasets.  
- Analysis gap: limited examination of failure cases and lack of systematic study on why dasNet succeeds where standard CNNs fail.  
- Suggested future work: evaluate on larger datasets, analyze failure modes, and explore different attention policies and their effect on representations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally—novel and promising but needs scalability testing and deeper attention analysis.
