**Paper Review: Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections**

**Summary:**
This paper introduces Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a novel architecture that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. DasNet builds upon traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by allowing each layer to influence all other layers on successive passes over an image. The weights of these special connections implement a control policy that is learned through reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate that dasNet outperforms the previous state-of-the-art model on unaugmented datasets, specifically CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.

**Strengths:**

* DasNet is a novel and innovative approach that addresses the limitations of traditional CNNs by incorporating internal selective attention.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized exposition of their work, making it easy to follow and understand.
* The experimental results are impressive, with dasNet achieving a relative improvement of 6% with respect to the vanilla CNN on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the dynamics of dasNet, including the effect of different values of T on the performance of the network.

**Weaknesses:**

* The method functions as a black box, with little explanation of why it works. The authors could provide more insight into the internal workings of dasNet and how it learns to focus its attention.
* The analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters is inadequate and uninformative. The authors could provide more details on how the network learns to represent images and how the theta parameters are updated.
* The notation and equations could be improved for clarity. For example, Equation 11's summation index is unclear, and Equation 8 may need an image index j.
* The authors could provide more justification for the use of theta regularization and how it affects the final gradient updates.
* The algorithm is unclear, with undefined variables and ambiguous notation.

**Originality:**
DasNet is a novel approach that builds upon traditional CNNs. While the idea of incorporating internal selective attention is not new, the authors' implementation and application to CNNs is innovative and original.

**Significance:**
The results of this paper are significant, as they demonstrate a new state-of-the-art result for unaugmented datasets. The authors' approach has the potential to improve the performance of CNNs on a wide range of tasks, including image recognition, segmentation, and scene parsing.

**Quality:**
The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and experimental results. However, the analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters could be improved.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-written and easy to follow. However, some of the notation and equations could be improved for clarity.

**Recommendations:**

* Provide more insight into the internal workings of dasNet and how it learns to focus its attention.
* Improve the analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters.
* Clarify the notation and equations, including Equation 11's summation index and Equation 8's image index j.
* Justify the use of theta regularization and how it affects the final gradient updates.
* Improve the algorithm by defining undefined variables and clarifying ambiguous notation.

Overall, this paper is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, and with some improvements, it has the potential to be a seminal work in the area of internal selective attention in CNNs.
