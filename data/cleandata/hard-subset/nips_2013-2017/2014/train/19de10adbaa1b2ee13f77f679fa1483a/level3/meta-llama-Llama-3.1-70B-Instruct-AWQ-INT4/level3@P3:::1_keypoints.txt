- Proposes dasNet: adds feedback connections to a Maxout CNN to iteratively focus internal attention via modulatory connections controlled by a learned policy.  
- Introduces a non-stationary CNN that can adapt post-training through feedback, inspired by bidirectional connections in the visual cortex.  
- Learns the control policy with Separable NES (SNES) because standard RL is impractical given the large number of control parameters.  
- Evaluated on ambiguous cases in CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100; achieves a ~6% relative improvement over prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with helpful figures and tables.  
- Empirically supports that feedback and attention are promising directions for vision models and can improve CNN performance.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the learned policies: representation, behavior, and update dynamics are not clearly explained.  
- Does not sufficiently discuss computational cost or provide detailed resource/efficiency analysis (training cited as ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).  
- Overall contribution is significant, novel, and technically sound, meriting acceptance with improvements.  
- Quality: 9/10 — technically sound and experimentally supported, but could analyze policies further.  
- Clarity: 9/10 — clear writing, but needs more detail on learned policies.  
- Originality: 9/10 — novel approach, though feedback connections are not entirely new; more related-work context desirable.  
- Significance: 9/10 — strong results and motivation, but would benefit from deeper policy and cost analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with a request to provide more detailed analysis of learned policies and computational costs in a revision.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient policy analysis, insufficient computational-cost discussion, and limited contextualization of related feedback-connection work.
