- Proposes a convnet with multiplicative, per-channel feedback gates learned via SNES on top of a Maxout CNN, enabling iterative internal attention and achieving SOTA on CIFAR-10/100 (unaugmented).  
- Method is technically sound with a concrete training algorithm and principled policy objective; dynamics analysis and CIFAR results are strong.  
- Evaluation needs stronger baselines and broader ablations (e.g., backprop-through-time for gates, supervised per-channel gating, multi-pass ensembling, curriculum/boosting).  
- Should report compute/latency overhead for T>1, sensitivity to number of passes T, and run multiple seeds/significance tests.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; interaction of observations and actions is explained, but a diagram clarifying gating granularity and injection points would help.  
- Individual components aren’t novel, but the combined channel-wise feedback attention with black-box policy search on a strong CNN is well chosen and convincingly executed.  
- Biological feedback connections are appropriately motivated and cited.  
- Empirical gains and correction of ambiguous cases indicate recurrent attention is promising, but impact would be strengthened by scaling to larger datasets and testing robustness to distribution shift.  
- Pros: simple coherent integration, clear exposition, pragmatic use of SNES at scale.  
- Cons: limited baselines/ablations, unclear compute/latency trade-offs, evaluation confined to CIFAR, no comparison of black-box RL vs gradient-based recurrent training.  
- Recommendation: Promising and well executed; encourage acceptance with requests to expand baselines and explore scaling to larger datasets.
