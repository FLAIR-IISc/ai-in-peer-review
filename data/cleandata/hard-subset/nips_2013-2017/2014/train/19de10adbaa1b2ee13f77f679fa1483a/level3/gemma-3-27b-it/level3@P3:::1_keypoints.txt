- Introduces dasNet: CNN with feedback connections trained by reinforcement learning to adapt filter activations for better classification.
- Combination of known components (CNNs, feedback, RL) is well-executed and novel in integration.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100, supporting the value of feedback/attention in CNNs.
- Strong grounding in prior biological and computer vision literature with a comprehensive related work section.
- Sound technical approach: pragmatic use of SNES to handle high-dimensional policy space.
- Clear presentation and organization; helpful visualizations (e.g., activation changes).
- Insightful analysis of dynamics and gate information content showing how attention is used.
- Motivation for feedback connections is well-justified by biological inspiration.
- Scalability concerns: experiments limited to CIFAR-10/100 — ImageNet-scale feasibility untested.
- Computational cost is high (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), raising practical concerns for larger datasets.
- Interpretability issues: learned policy remains largely a black box despite some visualizations.
- SNES adds complexity; trade-offs versus other RL methods (e.g., policy gradients) need more discussion.
- Limited ablation studies; more analysis of hyperparameters and architectural choices would strengthen claims.
- Technically sound with well-supported claims; more than a position paper.
- Originality stems from the effective combination of existing techniques rather than novel individual components.
- Significant contribution: empirical evidence that feedback/attention can improve vision models and spur further research.
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA results, promising attention mechanism, clear writing, strong analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability, interpretability, and computational cost concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability strategies, policy interpretability, computational complexity, and expanded ablations; encourage ImageNet-scale future work.
