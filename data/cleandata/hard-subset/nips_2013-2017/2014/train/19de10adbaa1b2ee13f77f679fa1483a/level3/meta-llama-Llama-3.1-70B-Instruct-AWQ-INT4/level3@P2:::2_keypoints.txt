- Introduces dasNet: a CNN architecture that adds selective attention via feedback connections to let layers modulate each other across successive image passes.  
- Implements attention by learning a control policy with reinforcement learning after supervised CNN training.  
- Extends Maxout networks and uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to evolve a deterministic policy represented by a neural network.  
- Policy is deterministic: same action for a given observation.  
- Experiments focus on ambiguous cases in CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Reports a 6% relative improvement over prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets.  
- Provides visualizations showing the network selectively focuses on image features.  
- Claims a significant contribution by integrating selective attention into CNNs to improve classification.  
- Analysis of internal network dynamics is inadequate and lacks explanation of why the method works.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of learned policy parameters (mean and variance).  
- Notation and equations are sometimes unclear; there are typos and ambiguities.  
- Exposition is generally clear but some equations, SNES implementation, and policy network details need more explanation.  
- Suggests deeper analysis of dynamics (e.g., why layer-1 activations increase on first iteration) and effects of global scaling.  
- Recommends analyzing learned theta statistics and providing implementation details for SNES and the policy network.  
- Suggests exploring connections to dynamical systems / recurrent networks rather than only reinforcement learning framing.  
- Advises testing constraints on theta statistics and investigating hard cases corrected by dasNet but missed by Maxout.  
- Acceptance hinges on clear explanation of dasNet, SNES algorithm, and well-defined notation; rejection points include lack of mechanistic explanation and unclear parameter analysis.  
- Overall rating: 7/10 — significant contribution but needs more analysis and clarity.
