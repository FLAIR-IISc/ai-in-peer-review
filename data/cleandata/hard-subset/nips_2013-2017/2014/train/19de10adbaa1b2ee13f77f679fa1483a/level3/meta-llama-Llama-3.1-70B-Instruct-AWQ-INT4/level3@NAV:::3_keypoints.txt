- Proposes dasNet, a novel CNN architecture with top-down feedback for internal selective attention.  
- Draws inspiration from human visual cortex top-down modulation.  
- Uses iterative attention to focus internal features and disambiguate difficult cases.  
- Trains the attention policy via reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 with ~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and well-organized.  
- Experiments are thorough and visualizations effectively demonstrate the attention mechanism.  
- Novel and original approach distinct from prior attention work.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and clearly distinguishes the contribution.  
- Lacks evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet), raising scalability concerns.  
- Unclear how the approach generalizes to other tasks such as object detection or segmentation.  
- Recommendation to accept, with a suggestion to include larger-scale results and clarify generalizability.
