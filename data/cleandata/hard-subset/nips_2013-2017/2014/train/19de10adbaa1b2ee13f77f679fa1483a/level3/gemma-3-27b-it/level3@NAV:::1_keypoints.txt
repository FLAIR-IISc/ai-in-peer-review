- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback connections for internal selective attention that dynamically adjusts filter sensitivities after a feedforward pass.  
- Attentional policy is learned via reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle a large parameter space.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and recurrent processing; the novelty is in the integration rather than individual components.  
- Empirically improves performance, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Strength: strong empirical results that validate the effectiveness of feedback and attention mechanisms.  
- Strength: well-chosen and effectively executed combination of feedback, reinforcement learning, and Maxout networks.  
- Strength: practical and effective use of SNES to train the large attentional policy.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation and methodology.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—only evaluated on CIFAR datasets, needs scaling to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Weakness: limited interpretability—insufficient analysis of what the network attends to and why.  
- Weakness: significant computational cost (training time ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).  
- Argument for acceptance: novel integration yields strong empirical evidence and is technically sound.  
- Argument against acceptance: restricted experimental scope limits claims of generalizability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with strong encouragement to scale to larger datasets and improve interpretability of learned attention.
