- Introduces dasNet: a CNN architecture with feedback connections that iteratively modulates filter activations during classification.
- Uses reinforcement learning (SNES) to learn an attention policy that adjusts filter sensitivities over multiple passes.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, improving accuracy over baseline CNNs.
- Motivated by biological, feedback-driven visual processing to handle ambiguous cases.
- Execution is robust, well-grounded, and the paper is clearly written with enough detail for reproducibility.
- SNES is a reasonable choice for high-dimensional policy optimization but is not a novel algorithmic contribution.
- Experimental scope is limited to relatively small datasets, leaving scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) untested.
- The approach is incrementally novel, building on existing feedback and attention concepts rather than introducing fundamentally new techniques.
- Interpretability of the learned attention policies is insufficiently analyzed; qualitative insights are provided but lack rigorous evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: effective feedback+RL application, significant empirical gains, clear presentation and foundation for future work.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, restricted experimental scale, and weak interpretability analysis.
- Recommendation: accept the paper, with suggestions to evaluate on larger datasets and provide deeper analysis of attention dynamics.
