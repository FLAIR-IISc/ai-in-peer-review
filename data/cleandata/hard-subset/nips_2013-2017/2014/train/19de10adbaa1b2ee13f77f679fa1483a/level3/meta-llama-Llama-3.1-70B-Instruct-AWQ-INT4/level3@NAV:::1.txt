**Paper Review: Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections**

This paper introduces a novel deep neural network architecture, Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet), that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) with feedback connections that gate the activation of some filters to implement visual attention. The gating/attention policy is learned using reinforcement learning on top of the CNN.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper presents a new approach to deep learning by introducing feedback connections in a CNN to implement internal selective attention. The authors argue that this approach is inspired by the human visual cortex, where feedback connections play a crucial role in visual processing. The dasNet architecture is designed to improve classification performance by allowing the network to iteratively focus its internal attention on some of its convolutional filters.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on CNNs, reinforcement learning, and visual attention. The authors cite several papers on CNNs, including the work of Krizhevsky et al. on ImageNet classification and Goodfellow et al. on maxout networks. They also draw inspiration from the human visual cortex and the work of Felleman and Essen on the hierarchy of visual cortical areas.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to deep learning by introducing feedback connections in a CNN to implement internal selective attention.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented datasets.
* The paper provides a clear and well-organized presentation of the dasNet architecture and its training algorithm.

Weaknesses:

* The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the dasNet architecture and its training algorithm.
* The authors do not compare their approach to other state-of-the-art methods for visual attention and image classification.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations and examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the dasNet architecture.

**Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance**

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its significance.

Originality: The paper presents a novel approach to deep learning by introducing feedback connections in a CNN to implement internal selective attention.

Significance: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the dasNet architecture on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented datasets. The approach has the potential to improve image classification performance in a variety of applications.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication in NIPS. The paper presents a novel approach to deep learning that has the potential to improve image classification performance in a variety of applications. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach, and the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the dasNet architecture on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

* Provide a more thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the dasNet architecture and its training algorithm.
* Compare the approach to other state-of-the-art methods for visual attention and image classification.
* Provide more visualizations and examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the dasNet architecture.
* Consider scaling the approach to larger problems/datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
