- Introduces dasNet, a CNN architecture with feedback connections that gate filter activations to implement internal selective attention.  
- Gating/attention policy is learned on top of the CNN using reinforcement learning.  
- Architecture is biologically inspired, drawing on feedback mechanisms in the human visual cortex.  
- Demonstrates improved classification performance, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Paper is technically sound and clearly written with a well-organized presentation of the architecture and training algorithm.  
- Builds on prior work in CNNs, reinforcement learning, and visual attention (cites Krizhevsky, Goodfellow, Felleman & Essen).  
- Lacks a thorough analysis of computational complexity for dasNet and its training procedure.  
- Does not compare directly to other state-of-the-art visual attention or image classification methods.  
- Paper would benefit from more visualizations and examples to illustrate how internal attention operates.  
- Scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is not evaluated and should be considered.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS due to novelty, clarity, and empirical gains.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze complexity, add comparisons to SOTA methods, include more visualizations, and test on larger-scale datasets.
