- Introduces dasNet: a CNN variant that uses iterative feedback to modulate convolutional filter sensitivities and improve classification, optimized with SNES and evaluated on CIFAR-10/100.  
- Innovative approach: departs from feedforward CNNs by adding a biologically inspired adaptive feedback mechanism.  
- Performance gains: achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on unaugmented CIFAR datasets and corrects ambiguous misclassifications.  
- Relevance and originality: well-motivated work that applies evolutionary strategies (SNES) to optimize a large gating parameter space.  
- Clarity of exposition: generally well-written with Algorithm 1 and detailed experiments aiding reproducibility.  
- Black-box nature: limited analysis of why dasNet works; learned gating variables and parameters lack interpretability.  
- Unclear theoretical justifications: regularization and gradient updates for θ are not well-explained or theoretically grounded.  
- Missing analysis of difficult cases: no in-depth examination of images misclassified by standard CNNs but corrected by dasNet.  
- Untapped connection to dynamical systems: paper doesn’t explore links between the feedback mechanism and recurrent/dynamical system frameworks.  
- Ambiguous visualizations: Figure 3 and related activation changes are described without meaningful interpretation.  
- Typos and ambiguities: some equations (e.g., Eqn. 11) and notations (terms like 'x' and 'd') are unclear or undefined.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed analysis of gating variable evolution, especially in localized image regions.  
- Suggestion: study challenging cases qualitatively to explain dasNet’s advantages over standard CNNs.  
- Suggestion: explore theoretical connections to dynamical systems and recurrent networks to strengthen the framework.  
- Suggestion: clarify ambiguous equations and fix typos to improve readability.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance — promising originality and performance but needs improved interpretability and theoretical grounding.
