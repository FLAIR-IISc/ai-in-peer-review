**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture called Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), which incorporates selective attention through feedback connections to improve classification performance. The dasNet architecture is based on a Maxout network, which is augmented with a gating mechanism that allows the network to iteratively focus its attention on specific features in the input image. The gating parameters are learned through reinforcement learning using a scalable natural evolution strategy (SNES). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of dasNet on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented data.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The dasNet architecture builds upon previous work on Maxout networks and reinforcement learning. The authors draw inspiration from the human visual cortex, which uses feedback connections to modulate the activity of neurons in response to visual stimuli. The use of reinforcement learning to learn the gating parameters is novel and allows the network to adapt its behavior in response to changing inputs.

**Strengths**

* The dasNet architecture is a significant departure from traditional feedforward neural networks and demonstrates the potential of incorporating feedback connections to improve classification performance.
* The use of reinforcement learning to learn the gating parameters is a novel approach that allows the network to adapt its behavior in response to changing inputs.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of dasNet on challenging datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on unaugmented data.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper lacks a clear explanation of why the dasNet architecture is effective, and the analysis of image representations and learned theta parameters is inadequate and uninformative.
* The exposition is generally clear, but several equations contain typos or need clearer explanation.
* The algorithm is unclear in some places, and the notation is not well-defined.
* The necessity and justification of theta regularization are unclear, and the final gradient updates are not well-explained.

**Originality**

* The dasNet architecture is a novel approach that incorporates selective attention through feedback connections to improve classification performance.
* The use of reinforcement learning to learn the gating parameters is a novel approach that allows the network to adapt its behavior in response to changing inputs.

**Significance**

* The dasNet architecture has the potential to improve classification performance on challenging datasets and could be applied to a wide range of computer vision tasks.
* The use of reinforcement learning to learn the gating parameters is a significant departure from traditional supervised learning approaches and could have implications for the development of more adaptive and robust neural networks.
