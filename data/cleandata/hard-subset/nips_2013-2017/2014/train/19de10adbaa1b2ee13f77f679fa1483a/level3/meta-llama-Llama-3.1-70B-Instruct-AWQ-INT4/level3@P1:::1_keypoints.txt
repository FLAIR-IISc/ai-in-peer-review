- Introduces dasNet: a deep network with internal selective attention via feedback connections trained with reinforcement learning on top of a pre-trained CNN.  
- Core idea: iterative focusing on convolutional filters to correct initial predictions and improve classification.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture, RL approach, and experiments.  
- Thorough related work review and logical presentation flow.  
- Novelty resides in combining feedback connections and reinforcement learning to implement internal selective attention.  
- Experimental results show state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Provides empirical evidence that feedback and attention are promising directions for vision models.  
- Strength: well-designed and effective architecture for internal selective attention.  
- Strength: thorough and well-executed experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: clear and accessible presentation.  
- Weakness: approach may be computationally expensive to train and evaluate.  
- Weakness: scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is not demonstrated.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the learned attention policy.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Suggested future work: scale to larger datasets, apply dasNet to other vision tasks, and analyze the learned attention policy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective approach, SOTA results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited demonstrated scalability, insufficient policy analysis.
