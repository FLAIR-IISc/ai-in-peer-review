- Introduces dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback to perform multiple passes and dynamically modulate convolutional filter activations via learned multiplicative gates.  
- Policy maps internal state (average maps, intermediate activations, class probs) to gating actions, trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Motivated by recurrent selective attention in biological vision and aims to fix hard classification cases beyond a single feed-forward pass.  
- Empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, improving ~6% relative over a Maxout baseline.  
- Strength: Significant and original idea to move from static deep networks to dynamic, input-adaptive processing via learned internal policies.  
- Strength: Strong practical performance demonstrating that recurrent attentional processing yields real gains.  
- Strength: Clear high-level exposition and biologically motivated framing.  
- Weakness: Model presented as a black box with insufficient analysis of why it works or the learned attentional strategy.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper visualizations and analyses, especially on hard cases corrected by dasNet, showing gate sequences and feature-map changes.  
- Suggestion: Analyze learned policy parameters (SNES mean/variance) and perform controlled experiments (e.g., variance constraints) to probe sensitivity.  
- Weakness: Framing as reinforcement learning is questionable — setup is deterministic with immediate reward and resembles an RNN/dynamical system.  
- Suggestion: Reframe/discuss relation to RNNs and consider alternative training (e.g., backprop through time) as a comparison.  
- Weakness: Technical clarity and reproducibility issues — undefined functions/variables in Algorithm 1, ambiguous fitness accumulation, and indexing errors in equations.  
- Weakness: Unjustified L2 regularization on policy and unclear description of how SNES updates use the regularized fitness.  
- Overall: Interesting and effective approach with important contributions, but paper needs deeper interpretability analyses, clearer technical details, and stronger connections to recurrent-network literature.
