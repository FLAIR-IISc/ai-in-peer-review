- Introduces dasNet: integrates feedback connections with CNNs to implement selective visual attention via filter gating.  
- Augments Maxout networks with reinforcement-learning-trained attention policies that modulate filter activations across multiple image passes.  
- Builds on established neural-network and neuroscience ideas, representing a well-executed combination rather than a highly novel component.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Demonstrates that iterative attention refinement can correct misclassifications, with visualizations showing changing filter activations (e.g., cat vs. dog).  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train a million-dimensional policy effectively, but lacks explanation for choosing SNES over other RL methods.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, thorough experiments, and strong empirical validation that attention mechanisms improve CNN performance.  
- Supports the hypothesis that feedback connections are a promising direction for vision models.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation scope (only CIFAR datasets) and incremental contribution compared to prior work.  
- Suggests scaling to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) and better distinguishing dasNet from prior recurrent-vision work.  
- Overall: high-quality paper recommended for acceptance, with a caveat that future work should address scalability.
