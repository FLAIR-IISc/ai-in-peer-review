- Introduces dasNet, a novel architecture adding biologically inspired top-down selective attention via feedback connections to deep CNNs.  
- Dynamically modulates convolutional filter sensitivities during inference using a learned RL policy to iteratively refine classifications.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the high-dimensional policy, avoiding infeasibility of standard RL.  
- Builds on Maxout networks with dropout and adds gated attention mechanisms for layer-level filter weighting across sequential passes.  
- Policy is explicitly trained to correct misclassified samples using a hindsight-style boosting objective.  
- Demonstrates a 6% relative improvement over vanilla Maxout on CIFAR-10/100, achieving new SOTA for unaugmented data.  
- Provides clear visualizations showing how attention shifts across layers and analysis confirming stability of dynamics beyond training steps.  
- Offers thorough ablation studies (e.g., gate information content via 15-NN) and careful handling of misclassified samples (λboost).  
- Acknowledges complex suppression/enhancement patterns in high-level features as a limitation of interpretability.  
- Lacks experiments on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ILSVRC), leaving scalability to real-world tasks unproven.  
- Relies on global gating rather than spatially localized attention, limiting applicability to tasks like object detection.  
- Does not include failure-case analysis (e.g., occluded or texture-dominated classes) to assess robustness.  
- Reports substantial training time (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) but does not quantify inference-time costs or compare computational trade-offs to efficient alternatives.  
- Recommendation: strong accept, with suggested minor revisions to add scalability discussion/ILSVRC results, include failure-case analysis, and clarify computational trade-offs.
