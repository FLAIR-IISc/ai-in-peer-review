- Introduces dasNet: an architecture adding attention-based top-down feedback to CNNs.
- Uses iterative refinement where a pre-trained CNN dynamically modulates filter sensitivities via learned attention policies.
- Draws biological inspiration from top-down feedback in vision systems.
- Extends Maxout networks with gated signals to enable multiple "rethink" passes over the same input.
- Learns the attention policy using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle high-dimensional control parameters.
- Shows state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 with a ~6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs.
- Provides visualizations demonstrating how successive passes alter internal representations and correct misclassifications.
- Conceptually novel in combining reinforcement learning-style attention with CNNs to add internal selective attention.
- Strengths: clear biological motivation, thorough experiments on challenging datasets, and compelling visual evidence.
- Well-grounded in prior neuroscience and recurrent processing literature (Felleman & Essen; Lamme & Roelfsema).
- Weakness: no evaluation on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ILSVRC), limiting demonstrated scalability.
- Weakness: lacks presentation of failure cases where attention fails to correct errors.
- Weakness: relatively high computational cost (four days on a single GPU) compared to feedforward nets.
- Overall contribution is significant and technically sound; acceptance warranted with recommendation to address scalability and failure-case analysis in future work.
