- Paper introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections that iteratively adjust filter sensitivities via a learned attention policy.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the attention policy because standard RL is infeasible for the high-dimensional parameter space.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation, showing practical value.

- Strength: Achieves ~6% relative improvement over the Maxout baseline, establishing new unaugmented SOTA.
- Strength: Novel architecture adding internal selective attention via feedback, moving beyond fixed feedforward networks.
- Strength: Shows feasibility of learning large-scale attention policies using SNES.

- Weakness: Lacks interpretability — acts as a black box with superficial analysis of why it works or how gating affects classification.
- Weakness: Technical and algorithmic clarity problems (undefined symbols like h_M, ambiguous Algorithm 1 implementation details).
- Weakness: Equations contain typos and unclear notation (missing indices, undefined symbols), weakening reproducibility.
- Weakness: The RL framing is tenuous; behavior resembles recurrent processing with immediate rewards rather than true sequential decision-making.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of learned policy parameters (mean/variance of theta), gating dynamics, global scaling effects, and hard-case corrections.

- For acceptance: Significant empirical improvements on challenging datasets support publication.
- For acceptance: Conceptual advance toward adaptive, attention-based neural networks with novel feedback idea.
- For acceptance: Opens promising new research directions in internal selective attention.

- Against acceptance: Fails to explain mechanistically why the approach works, limiting scientific contribution.
- Against acceptance: Weak theoretical justification for using an RL approach; connection to RL is superficial.
- Against acceptance: Multiple technical issues (typos, undefined symbols, unclear algorithm) reduce credibility.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient internal-dynamics analysis undermines the paper's claims.

- Suggestion: Clarify distinction between reinforcement learning and recurrent network interpretations; adjust framing accordingly.
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of internal dynamics (why layer-1 activations increase initially, effects of global scaling, spatial evolution of gates).
- Suggestion: Fix equation typos, define all symbols, and clarify Algorithm 1 implementation (per-image vs. overwriting variables).
- Suggestion: Thoroughly analyze hard cases and show how attention corrections arise from learned policies.
- Suggestion: Justify theta regularization and clearly explain how gradient updates are computed.

- Overall: Interesting and effective approach with strong empirical gains, but technical clarity and interpretability issues leave the paper borderline — needs substantial revision to be a strong NIPS contribution.
