- Proposal: dasNet augments a Maxout CNN with iterative top-down, channel-wise multiplicative gates controlled by a learned policy, running T passes per image to refine predictions.  
- Training: base CNN trained normally; a policy (mapping layer averages, pre-softmax activations, class probs to per-map gains) is learned with Separable NES, emphasizing misclassified examples.  
- Empirical results: shows competitive/state-of-the-art improvements on CIFAR-10/100 versus a strong Maxout baseline, with performance peaking around the trained T and remaining stable.  
- Conceptual novelty: introduces an implementable mechanism for internal, iterative attention over feature maps, distinct from glimpse-based or purely feedforward attention.  
- Interpretability: provides visualizations of gate dynamics and an information-content study indicating gate vectors themselves carry discriminative signal.  
- Biological and engineering framing: aligns with recurrent/top-down neuroscience ideas while fitting standard CNN pipelines.  
- Objective design: uses a boosting-style objective that focuses the policy on correcting hard/misclassified examples.  
- Scalability concern: experiments are limited to CIFAR-10/100, leaving applicability to ImageNet/high-resolution, large-vocabulary tasks untested.  
- Compute complexity: adds T forward passes at test time and a large policy trained via population-based SNES; lacks reported test-time latency and training cost comparisons.  
- Policy learning choice: SNES is used due to dimensionality, but no comparison to gradient-based alternatives (policy gradients, BPTT, or simpler linear policies) is provided.  
- Ambiguity on augmentation: claims about “unaugmented” SOTA are unclear because base model M used augmentation; training/testing protocol needs clarification.  
- Missing failure analysis: paper shows success cases but lacks systematic failure modes (e.g., reinforcing wrong hypotheses, instability beyond T, or uniform gating collapse).  
- Lack of ablations: unclear contribution of design choices (which layers gated, role of class probs in observations, boosting scheme, gates per layer).  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and well-described, but would benefit from additional baselines and causal evidence for learned attention.  
- Additional evaluation needed: recommend reporting latency, compute, robustness to distribution shift, per-class improvements, and confusion reduction.  
- Clarity issues: writing is clear overall, but detail the policy architecture, observation normalization, gating ranges, and provide run variance/confidence intervals.  
- Originality: novel to combine channel-wise multiplicative feedback gates with a learned SNES-trained policy on modern CNNs.  
- Significance conditional: approach could influence adaptive test-time representation methods if scalability and runtime are addressed.  
- Suggested experiments/improvements: add ImageNet or larger-scale tests, run ablations (top-layer only, exclude class probs, SGD-trained/simple policies, BPTT), report compute/accuracy trade-offs, show failure cases, and analyze gate sparsity/layer contributions.  
- Acceptance argument: novel, well-motivated architecture with solid CIFAR gains and insightful visualizations.  
- Rejection argument: lacks large-scale validation, key ablations/comparisons for policy learning, and clarity on augmentation and failure modes.  
- Verdict: promising and recommend accept if authors clarify protocols, add key ablations, and discuss compute/latency and failures; otherwise borderline accept.
