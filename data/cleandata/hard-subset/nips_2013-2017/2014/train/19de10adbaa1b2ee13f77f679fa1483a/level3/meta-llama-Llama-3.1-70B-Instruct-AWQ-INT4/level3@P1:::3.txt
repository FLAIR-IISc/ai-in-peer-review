**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel deep neural network architecture, called Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet), which incorporates attention-based top-down feedback to improve classification performance. The architecture is designed to mimic the human visual cortex, where each layer learns progressively more abstract representations of the input data. The dasNet model is trained using a combination of supervised learning and reinforcement learning, where the attention policy is learned through a variant of Natural Evolution Strategies (NES) called Separable NES (SNES). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of dasNet on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented data.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel Model Design**: The paper proposes a novel deep neural network architecture that incorporates attention-based top-down feedback, which is a departure from traditional feedforward CNNs.
2. **State-of-the-Art Performance**: The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, which is a significant achievement in the field of computer vision.
3. **Interesting Vision Application**: The paper presents a forward-looking approach to combining bottom-up and top-down signals for training, which is a promising direction for future research in computer vision.
4. **Evaluations Across Time**: The authors evaluate the model across time, using gated signals to constrain representations, which provides a more comprehensive understanding of the model's behavior.
5. **Visualizations**: The paper includes visualizations that help interpret the model, which is essential for understanding the complex dynamics of the dasNet architecture.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of ILSVRC Results**: The paper does not provide results on larger datasets, such as ILSVRC, which is a concern for scalability and practical insights.
2. **Complexity of the Model**: The dasNet model is more complex than traditional feedforward CNNs, which may make it more challenging to implement and train.
3. **Representation Quality**: The representation quality of the model hinges on the attention policy, which may not always be optimal. The authors should suggest or demonstrate failure cases to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the model's limitations.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel deep neural network architecture that incorporates attention-based top-down feedback, which is a significant departure from traditional feedforward CNNs. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, which is a significant achievement in the field of computer vision. The paper's forward-looking approach to combining bottom-up and top-down signals for training is a promising direction for future research in computer vision.
