- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections and learned selective attention to iteratively refine classifications.  
- Demonstrates improved performance and sets new state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 using Maxout networks.  
- Novel idea: enables post-training adaptation of CNN behavior, inspired by iterative human vision processing.  
- Builds on prior RNN/RL work but scales the concept to state-of-the-art architectures, showing feasibility.  
- Strength: clear and impressive empirical gains, offering a promising direction for adaptive, robust image classification.  
- Major weakness: interpretability is poor—mechanisms behind performance improvements are largely opaque.  
- Analysis of image representations and learned gating parameters (theta) is inadequate; Figures showing activations are insufficient.  
- Unclear necessity and role of theta regularization; gradient update details appear underspecified.  
- Technical issues: Algorithm 1 ambiguous about variable scope (per-image vs overwritten).  
- Equations 8 and 11 contain errors or need clarification; symbols x and d after Eq. 11 are undefined.  
- Framing as reinforcement learning is questionable given the immediate reward and deterministic policy; a dynamical-systems viewpoint may fit better.  
- Requested further analyses: explain initial increase in layer-1 activations, effects of global scaling on gating, inspect misclassified examples, and test constrained theta statistics.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing interpretability, technical clarifications, and the suggested deeper analyses.
