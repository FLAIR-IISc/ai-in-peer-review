- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that iteratively modulates filter sensitivities to implement internal selective attention.  
- Learns a control policy via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to adjust convolutional filter activations across iterations.  
- Builds on Maxout networks as the base architecture and integrates ideas from recurrent visual processing and attention literature.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, outperforming previous models.  
- Practical contribution: shows reinforcement learning can scale to a high-dimensional policy (over 1M parameters) using SNES.  
- Biological motivation: models iterative refinement similar to human visual disambiguation and recurrent cortical processing.  
- Strong empirical evidence and sound methodology combining well-established CNNs with an RL-based attention mechanism.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail and visualizations (e.g., filter activations) for reproducibility.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluation restricted to CIFAR datasets; lacks tests on larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Insufficient analysis of the learned attentional policy—more insight into what and why the network attends to is needed.  
- Computational cost is high (training took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti); discussion of resource requirements and optimization strategies is lacking.  
- Limited direct comparisons to alternative attention-based models reduce clarity on novelty and relative effectiveness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-executed integration of attention into CNNs, clear performance gains, theoretical and biological grounding, reproducible.  
- Arguments for rejection: small-scale experiments, significant computational expense, and inadequate analysis of the attentional policy.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggestions to scale to larger datasets and provide deeper analysis of the learned attentional policy.
