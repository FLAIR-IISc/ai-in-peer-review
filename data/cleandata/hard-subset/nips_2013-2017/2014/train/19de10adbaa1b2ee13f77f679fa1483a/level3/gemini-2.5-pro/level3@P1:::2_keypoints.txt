- Paper introduces dasNet: a pre-trained Maxout CNN augmented with modulatory gates and feedback to enable sequential internal attention over T=5 passes.
- Policy network observes CNN internal state and outputs gate values a_t to iteratively refine representations and classification.
- Policy is trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES), a black-box optimizer, using a reward that heavily penalizes misclassifications.
- Motivation is drawn from human vision: fast feed-forward pass plus slower top-down attention.
- Empirical results: dasNet outperforms baseline Maxout and achieves state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and strong results on CIFAR-100.
- Strength: original and significant idea—moving beyond static feed-forward networks to sequential feedback-based attention.
- Strength: strong empirical performance, notably correcting difficult examples and improving a strong baseline.
- Strength: generally clear exposition and well-argued neuroscience motivation.
- Weakness: lacks interpretability—insufficient analysis of why the method works and how gates evolve on hard vs. easy cases.
- Weakness: missing analysis of learned policy parameters (mean µ and variance Σ) and whether a small subset of parameters drives behavior.
- Weakness: limited investigation of internal dynamics (why performance stabilizes after T, effect of softmax scaling, trajectories of state vector o_t).
- Weakness: conceptual framing as reinforcement learning is tenuous; model is closer to an unrolled RNN/dynamical system and alternative training (BPTT) should be discussed.
- Weakness: technical clarity and reproducibility issues—Algorithm 1 omissions, ambiguous notation and indices in Eqs. 8 and 11, and unclear use of symbols like d and vT.
- Weakness: L2 regularization on policy parameters is unexplained; role, sensitivity, and interaction with SNES updates are not detailed.
- Recommendation: accept but strongly urge authors to improve interpretability, clarify conceptual framing, and fix technical presentation errors.
