- Introduces dasNet, a feedback-augmented CNN that iteratively modulates convolutional filter activations to "re-think" predictions.  
- Built on a Maxout network; gates weight convolutional output maps and a control policy maps observations to gate values.  
- Observation vector constructed from intermediate activations (including averaged output maps), collapsing spatial information.  
- Policy parameter space exceeds one million dimensions, necessitating scalable optimization.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the high-dimensional policy as a black-box optimizer.  
- Achieves a new state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 (no augmentation) and strong results on CIFAR-100.  
- Novel and well-motivated synthesis of CNNs, reinforcement learning, and feedback attention with neuroscience inspiration.  
- Technical execution is solid; experiments include dynamics analysis and show gate values contain class-relevant information.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and organized with illustrative qualitative examples.  
- Major weakness: scalability is unclear — experiments only on CIFAR, unclear viability on ImageNet-scale tasks.  
- Computational cost is substantial (policy training ≈4 days on GPU; iterative evaluations increase expense).  
- Observation representation may be suboptimal due to spatial averaging; alternative representations not fully explored.  
- Lacks thorough ablation studies (e.g., effects of varying the number of iterative steps T).  
- Overall assessment: strong, original work with significant empirical gains; recommended for acceptance, with scalability and ablation studies suggested for future work.
