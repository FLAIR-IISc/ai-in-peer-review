- Proposes dasNet: adds feedback-driven modulatory gates to a pre-trained Maxout CNN to iteratively refine classifications over multiple passes.  
- Attention policy is learned with reinforcement learning; uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to optimize a >1M-parameter policy.  
- Motivated by recurrent and top-down processing in primate vision to handle complex recognition beyond a single feedforward sweep.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, outperforming the Maxout baseline.  
- Training pipeline: supervised pre-training followed by a separate RL phase; policy training is compute-intensive (~4 days).  
- Provides useful analyses: dynamics over time, visualizations of filter modulation, and evidence that final gate activations are class-discriminative.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-described; algorithmic details (Algorithm 1) and explanations are clear.  
- Originality: novel integration of top-down attention and RL into a modern CNN architecture.  
- Significance: introduces a promising direction showing gains from recurrent/attentional mechanisms and high-dimensional policy learning.  
- Main weakness: evaluation restricted to small-scale CIFAR datasets; no large-scale benchmark (e.g., ILSVRC) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Practical concerns: added model and training complexity may limit adoption; uncertain how approach scales to many classes and larger images.  
- Missing analysis: limited discussion of failure modes—whether recurrent processing can degrade initially correct predictions.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong, original contribution; authors should address scalability and failure-mode analysis in the final version.
