- Introduces dasNet: augments a feedforward CNN with a feedback mechanism that performs multiple internal passes to apply selective attention by gating convolutional filter activations.  
- Uses a two-stage training process: train a strong baseline Maxout CNN first, then freeze it and learn a gating policy with reinforcement learning.  
- Represents the RL agent’s state using internal network activations and actions as the vector of filter gating values.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) for direct policy search to handle the policy’s very high-dimensional parameter space (>1M).  
- Demonstrates empirical improvement and achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented) at the time.  
- Connects to biological inspiration and prior work on feedback/recurrent vision and attention, integrating these into a deep architecture.  
- Strength: significant and novel integration of CNN, feedback, and RL showing iterative refinement improves difficult/ambiguous classifications.  
- Strength: technically sound experiments, clear presentation, useful analysis of dynamics and class-discriminative information in gates.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—SNES training is computationally intensive (four days on GPU for CIFAR) and may not scale to deeper networks or ImageNet.  
- Weakness: increased inference cost due to multiple forward passes (T=5), trading accuracy for higher runtime that may be impractical for real-world applications.  
- Weakness: qualitative analysis of learned attention is limited; more systematic visualization across classes/confusing pairs would strengthen insights.  
- Overall: high-quality, well-motivated contribution that opens a promising line of research, but needs further work on scaling and deeper interpretability.
