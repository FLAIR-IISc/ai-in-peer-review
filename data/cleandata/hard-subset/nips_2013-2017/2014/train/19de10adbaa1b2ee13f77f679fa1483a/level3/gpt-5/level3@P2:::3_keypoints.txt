- Proposal: dasNet augments a Maxout CNN with multiplicative feature-map gates updated by a top-down policy over multiple passes, trained with SNES, yielding refined predictions at test time.
- Empirical claim: ~6% relative improvement over a strong Maxout baseline on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 and visualizations showing gate dynamics and stability beyond training horizon.
- Novelty: RL-trained, high-dimensional gating policy operating on internal feature-map channels is new compared to concurrent attention models and recurrent CNNs.
- Soundness: Mechanism is straightforward and differentiable; actions constrained by a scaled softmax (average 1) for stability; misclassification-boosted loss focuses policy on hard cases; SNES is reasonable but sample-inefficient.
- Missing controls/ablations: No per-layer vs per-map gating study, no analysis of policy complexity or observation design, and no comparison to end-to-end BPTT despite differentiability.
- Missing baselines: Lacks comparisons to deeper/wider Maxout, test-time ensembling/multi-crop/MC-dropout, simple logits reweighting, and standard recurrent CNNs trained by gradient methods.
- Compute/latency gap: Paper does not quantify compute cost, T-pass inference overhead, policy size, training time, or comparison to ensembling/multi-crop trade-offs.
- Clarity: Generally well written with a clear training loop; should include SNES update details, compute metrics, and standardized visualization methodology.
- Significance: Could influence design of recurrent attention-enabled CNNs for hard-example refinement, but limited experimental scope (only CIFAR) leaves scalability and practical impact uncertain.
- Strengths: Biologically inspired selective internal attention, reproducible CIFAR gains, insightful visualizations, and stable action constraints.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: Evaluate on larger datasets, add missing baselines, compare SNES to gradient-based training, perform detailed ablations, provide failure analysis, and report reproducibility statistics and hyperparameters.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, well-motivated method with solid empirical gains and analyses that are broadly applicable.
- Rejection arguments: Limited to CIFAR, missing critical comparisons and scalability/compute quantification, and unclear sample-efficiency of SNES for large policies.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: High in deep learning/attention/recurrent models; moderate in NES variants.
