- Paper introduces dasNet: a Maxout CNN with multiplicative per-feature-map gates iteratively updated by a deterministic top-down policy trained with SNES.  
- At each pass the policy maps activation summaries and class probabilities to gates that amplify/suppress filters; SNES avoids high-dimensional gradient-based RL.  
- Empirical: dasNet yields solid accuracy improvements on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 and shows stable iterative dynamics.  
- Analysis is shallow: internal representations and learned SNES distribution parameters (mean/variance) are not meaningfully examined.  
- RL framing is questionable: rewards are immediate and policy deterministic, making the model closer to a recurrent dynamical system or iterative inference than to RL.  
- Major algorithmic/notation issues: Algorithm 1 leaves hM undefined; unclear accumulation/overwriting of F[i] and Θ[i]; Eq. 11 summation index ambiguous; Eq. 8 needs an image index; symbols x and d after Eq. 11 undefined.  
- Regularization and training details unclear: necessity/effect of θ-regularization (λL2) not justified; unclear how “final gradient updates” are computed in SNES and whether µ/Σ are treated as posterior hyperparameters.  
- Exposition is generally good but multiple equations contain typos or need clearer derivations and notation.  
- Originality: a thoughtful extension beyond fixed feedforward nets by combining internal attention with evolutionary policy search; gating mechanism and pipeline are interesting despite related prior recurrent/feedback work.  
- Significance: accuracy gains and adaptive reconfiguration are valuable, but greater interpretability would boost impact.  
- Suggestions: recast/ analyze as a dynamical system (fixed points, stability, RNN relation) rather than RL.  
- Suggestions: provide deeper probes of internal dynamics (e.g., why layer‑1 activations rise on first iteration, effect of global softmax scaling, gate trajectories).  
- Suggestions: systematically analyze hard cases corrected by dasNet; test constraints on θ statistics (variance penalties, gate bounds).  
- Suggestions: fix Algorithm 1 and equation/notation issues; ablate λboost and λL2; compare to backprop-through-time recurrent baselines.  
- Pros: strong empirical improvements; simple, general modification to CNNs; addresses adaptive reconfiguration.  
- Cons: limited interpretability; algorithmic/notation ambiguities; questionable RL framing and missing theoretical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising but recommend acceptance only if clarity and analysis issues are substantially addressed.
