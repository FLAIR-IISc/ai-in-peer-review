- Introduces dasNet, a CNN with top-down feedback that dynamically adjusts convolutional filter sensitivities via a learned attention policy.  
- Mimics iterative attentional processing from human visual cortex by refining initial assessments through recurrent processing.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn the high-dimensional control policy post hoc on a fully trained CNN.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation, with a reported ~6% relative improvement.  
- Builds on and appropriately cites prior work in CNNs, reinforcement learning, and biologically inspired vision, situating the contribution well.  
- Novelty: learning a dynamic attention policy after CNN training via direct policy search is a creative and original contribution.  
- Interpretability: visualizations of filter activations and gate information provide insight into how the attention mechanism operates.  
- Sound methodology: SNES is justified for the large parameter space, and the ablation on iteration steps shows stable dynamics.  
- Biologically inspired: architecture and motivation draw convincingly from human visual system principles.  
- Scalability concern: no experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet), leaving practical relevance and scalability unproven.  
- Lacks analysis of failure cases and robustness; unclear which images or classes still cause persistent errors.  
- Complexity: dasNet and SNES training introduce significant computational and architectural complexity that is not fully quantified.  
- Limited policy analysis: the learned policy’s structure and specific features it attends to are not deeply analyzed.  
- Overall: strong, novel work with impressive CIFAR results and useful visualizations, but limited by scalability and analysis gaps.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, strong empirical results, and insightful visualizations that could advance biologically inspired deep learning.  
- Rejection arguments: absent large-scale scalability evidence, insufficient failure-case/robustness analysis, and increased complexity without broader real-world demonstration.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add (even limited) larger-scale experiments, analyze failure cases, and provide deeper analysis of the learned attention policy.
