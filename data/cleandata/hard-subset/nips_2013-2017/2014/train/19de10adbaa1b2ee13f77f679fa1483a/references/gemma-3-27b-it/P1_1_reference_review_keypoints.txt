- Introduces dasNet, a CNN augmented with learned feedback connections that modulate filter activations over multiple passes using reinforcement learning.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategy (SNES) to learn a high-dimensional policy that controls internal attention.  
- Demonstrates improved performance and state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, especially correcting difficult misclassifications.  
- Builds on prior work in deep CNNs and Maxout networks and is inspired by biological recurrent/feedback processing.  
- Distinguishes itself by applying RL-based internal attention directly to a state-of-the-art CNN rather than simpler models or external attention.  
- Novel contribution: learning internal attentional policies that refine CNN activations post-training.  
- Strong empirical claim: ~6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100 with qualitative examples (e.g., correcting cat vs. dog).  
- Provides a clear justification for using SNES given the large policy dimensionality and scalability concerns with traditional RL.  
- Analysis shows the policy learns non-trivial suppression/enhancement patterns rather than uniform amplification of activations.  
- Paper is well written with sufficient explanations of Maxout networks and SNES for readers.  
- High computational cost: training took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti, raising scalability concerns for larger datasets/architectures.  
- Limited exploration of policy dynamics: convergence behavior, sensitivity to hyperparameters, and dynamics across T values need deeper analysis.  
- Action space design is somewhat arbitrary—the softmax scaling to an average of 1 lacks detailed justification or alternatives.  
- Observation space (average activations, intermediate activations, class probs) appears ad-hoc and may not be optimal for the policy.  
- Generalizability is unproven: experiments are restricted to CIFAR-10/100 and not tested on larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong empirical gains and potential impact on dynamic CNNs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited policy-dynamics analysis, and unclear generalizability to complex datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong and promising paper with weaknesses that do not substantially detract from its contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, expand dynamics analysis, consider alternative action scaling, and discuss scaling to datasets like ImageNet.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
