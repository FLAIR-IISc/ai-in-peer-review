- Proposes DasNet: a CNN with multiplicative per-channel gates applied across multiple recurrent passes, controlled by a policy network trained with SNES.  
- Policy maps per-map average activations, intermediate classifier activations, and class probabilities to channel gains; only final pass is scored with boosted loss.  
- Shows empirical improvements on CIFAR-10/100 over a Maxout baseline and provides qualitative visualizations and stability analysis.  
- Situates work at intersection of CNNs, attention/feedback, and RL; novelty is internal channel-wise, multi-layer attention in a full-frame classifier.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, positive mean-1 multiplicative gains that integrate easily into CNNs.  
- Strength: lightweight sequential loop (small T) that can correct initial misclassifications on ambiguous inputs.  
- Strength: empirical gains on benchmarks and diagnostics (visualizations, dynamics analysis).  
- Strength: bridges communities by combining internal attention with evolutionary optimization.  
- Main weakness: lacks critical baselines, especially end-to-end gradient-based training (BPTT) for the recurrent loop.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablations on observation components (per-map means vs logits vs probabilities).  
- Weakness: no comparison to simple post-hoc calibrators (MLP on logits) or static learned per-map gains and single-pass policies.  
- Quantitative reporting is incomplete: missing absolute error rates, standard deviations, and clear evaluation protocol regarding augmentation.  
- Choice of SNES is unjustified; likely sample-inefficient and no evidence provided that gradient methods fail.  
- Overfitting risk: SNES objective boosts misclassified instances but paper lacks validation-based stopping or overfitting monitoring.  
- Architectural wording is misleading: gates are external multiplicative scalars, not dense learned top-down synapses; action is spatially global, not spatial attention.  
- Analysis is shallow: gate-based classifiers and stability claims are under-explained and possibly inconclusive.  
- Clarity is generally good but missing implementation details (O/A dimensionalities, SNES hyperparameters, batch size n) and has notation inconsistencies.  
- Originality is moderate: known components combined in a new way, with potential value if gains are robust.  
- Significance is conditional: potentially impactful but limited until stronger baselines and reporting are provided.  
- Suggested improvements: add full quantitative tables, ablations (observation components, T=1 vs T>1, static gains), compare SNES to BPTT, use validation for SNES, and include per-class/confusion analyses.  
- Reasons to accept: simple mechanism, empirical improvements, and cross-disciplinary appeal.  
- Reasons to reject: missing key baselines/ablations, incomplete reporting, and unjustified use of SNES.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger empirical evidence and additional experiments.
