- Overall: Novel and well-motivated approach to internal attention via feedback in CNNs that yields improved CIFAR-10/100 performance but lacks thorough analysis and broader evaluation; reviewer recommends weak accept with major revisions.
- Core idea: dasNet augments a pre-trained CNN with learned feedback policies that modulate filter activations post-training to refine predictions.
- Method: Policy learned with Reinforcement Learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle high-dimensional action space.
- Novelty: Combining internal selective attention, recurrent feedback, and RL inside a pre-trained deep network is a genuinely new direction.
- Motivation: Strong analogy to human visual recurrent processing; iterative “thinking” over an image is compelling and well-justified.
- Empirical result: Reports ~6% relative improvement on challenging CIFAR-10/100 instances, supporting the central hypothesis.
- SNES explanation: Paper offers a clear, concise justification and explanation for using SNES given the action dimensionality.
- Dynamics: Experiments show stable behavior even when running more iterations than trained for, indicating robust dynamics.
- Information analysis: Final gate values contain class-relevant information, suggesting the policy learns meaningful modulation.
- Limited policy analysis: Paper shows that performance improves but provides superficial visualizations and little systematic insight into how policies operate.
- Narrow evaluation: Experiments are restricted to CIFAR-10/100 and a Maxout architecture, leaving generalizability to larger datasets and other architectures untested.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No analysis of sensitivity to learning rates, regularization, SNES sample size, or other key hyperparameters.
- Computational cost: Training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), raising concerns about scalability and practicality.
- Lack of ablations: Missing experiments isolating components (e.g., remove top-down connections, alternative RL algorithms) to quantify contributions.
- Overstated claims: Claiming a “new state-of-the-art” is slightly overstated given the incremental improvement and limited benchmarks.
- Recommendations: Authors should expand policy analysis, run broader experiments (datasets and architectures), perform hyperparameter and ablation studies, and address computational efficiency.
- Question for authors: How dependent are results on the Maxout choice, and would dasNet work similarly with ResNet/VGG or on ImageNet?
- Acceptance pros: Novel, well-motivated, positive CIFAR results, sound methodology and presentation.
- Acceptance cons: Insufficient analysis of learned policies, narrow experimental scope, high compute cost, missing ablations, and somewhat inflated claims.
