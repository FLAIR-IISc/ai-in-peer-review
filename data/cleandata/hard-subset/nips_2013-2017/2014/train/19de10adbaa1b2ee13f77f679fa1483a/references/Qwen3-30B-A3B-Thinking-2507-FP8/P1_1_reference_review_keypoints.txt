- Paper introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned internal selective attention via feedback and RL to iteratively refine classification across multiple passes.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to learn a policy that modulates filter weights in a pre-trained Maxout network.
- Empirical claim: ~6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100, establishing new state-of-the-art for those datasets.
- Strength: Methodology is well-motivated and technically sound, addressing difficult classification cases with a plausible RL-based solution.
- Strength: Novelty in applying RL to learn internal (filter-level) attention across multiple passes rather than input-space attention.
- Strength: Strong empirical validation with quantitative gains, qualitative analysis, and an ablation study on number of passes (T).
- Strength: Well-grounded biological motivation linking to top-down processing in visual cortex.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing and helpful algorithm description.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to other attention mechanisms (e.g., image-captioning attention, transformers); claim of novelty needs stronger justification.
- Weakness: Computational cost is high (training ~4 days on GTX 560 Ti) with no fair runtime comparison to the base Maxout network.
- Weakness: Limited generalization analysis—experiments only on CIFAR-10/100, no ImageNet or other datasets.
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (attention mechanism vs. boosting strategy).
- Weakness: Absolute improvement is modest despite relative gain, raising questions about practical significance given added complexity.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with reasonable experiments, but comparisons to other attention methods could be stronger.
- Clarity assessment: Paper is accessible and well-explained overall, though some notation (observation space) needs clarification.
- Originality assessment: Novel use of RL for internal selective attention; differentiates from prior attention work but needs deeper comparison.
- Significance assessment: Limited by modest absolute gains and computational overhead; broader impact uncertain without larger-scale evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel RL-based internal attention, solid benchmark results, neuroscience motivation, clear methodology.
- Arguments against acceptance: Modest absolute improvement, limited evaluation, high computational cost, insufficient comparisons and ablations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing comparisons, runtime reporting, and ablation analyses.
- Specific suggestions: Add detailed comparisons to recent attention methods (e.g., Xu et al., transformers).
- Specific suggestions: Provide a table comparing dasNet training cost to base Maxout training.
- Specific suggestions: Include ablation study separating attention mechanism and boosting contributions.
- Specific suggestions: Discuss limitations explicitly (modest gains, cost) and consider broader evaluations (e.g., ImageNet).
- Specific suggestions: Clarify observation space construction (Equation 7) and detail observation vector formation.
- Specific suggestions: In conclusion, state practical limitations and propose future directions to address them.
