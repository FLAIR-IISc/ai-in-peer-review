- Proposal: dasNet augments a Maxout CNN with per-feature-map gating and recurrent feedback, running T passes with a policy mapping observations to gates learned via SNES.
- Novelty: introduces internal, feature-map-level attention learned by evolution inside a CNN, distinct from spatial saccade/glimpse models and reconstruction-focused feedback.
- Mechanism: gating via a scaled softmax that averages to 1 is simple, sensible, and stabilizes dynamics; training loop and loss boosting for misclassified examples are coherent.
- Optimization choice: using SNES (black-box evolution) avoids brittle credit assignment in very high-dimensional policy learning.
- Empirical support: shows consistent improvements on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline, claims unaugmented SOTA, and provides qualitative visualizations and dynamics analyses.
- Dynamics finding: performance peaks near the trained T and remains stable for longer rollouts; final gate values encode class-relevant information.
- Missing baselines: no backprop/REINFORCE or simple supervised policy comparison to test if NES is necessary.
- Missing controls: no random or fixed-gate baselines to quantify learned attention value.
- Missing ablations: lacks per-layer/partial observation studies and exploration of T and boosting weight effects.
- Missing comparisons: no experiment showing whether fine-tuning the base Maxout CNN alone could yield similar gains.
- Statistical reporting: no mean±std over runs or significance testing; variance across runs not reported.
- Computational cost: runtime, latency, energy, and sample-efficiency of SNES and the policy are not quantified.
- Reproducibility gaps: underspecified observation/action dimensionalities, unclear policy architecture (implicit linearity), dropout treatment during recurrent passes, and policy validation/early-stopping protocol.
- Inconsistency: claim of “state-of-the-art for unaugmented data” conflicts with earlier mention of data augmentation for the Maxout baseline.
- Clarity: paper is readable and well-motivated, but ambiguous on policy definition, training/validation protocol, and lacks full result tables and absolute error rates.
- Gates-as-features ambiguity: reported 40–46% accuracy for gate-based classification lacks clear comparison baseline and dataset specification.
- Originality nuance: the integration of gating, iterative inference, and large-scale evolution is novel, though individual components are known.
- Significance limits: gains are modest (~6% relative), scalability to higher-resolution or spatial tasks is unclear, and adoption may be limited by compute/sample costs.
- Pros: biologically motivated, simple and attachable mechanism, stable dynamics, consistent benchmark improvements, and capability to correct ambiguous feedforward errors.
- Cons: missing key baselines/ablations and stats, experimental ambiguities, unquantified compute/sample costs, and limited comparisons to other recurrent/feedback methods.
- Suggestions: add random/fixed and supervised/REINFORCE baselines, fine-tune-only baseline, report absolute error±std and confusion matrices, clarify protocols and dimensions, quantify runtime overhead, analyze T sensitivity and decision changes, and consider constrained policy parameterizations.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — interesting, credible contribution likely to stimulate follow-ups, but empirical evaluation needs stronger baselines and clearer reporting.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
