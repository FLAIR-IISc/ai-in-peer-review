- Proposes dasNet: CNNs with learned feedback connections enabling internal selective attention and multiple forward passes to iteratively refine predictions.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn an attention policy after supervised pretraining of a Maxout CNN.  
- Demonstrates performance improvements and a new state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, with qualitative filter-change visualizations (Figure 3).  
- Novelty: combines learned feedback and reinforcement learning for internal attention, distinct from prior recurrent/attention work.  
- Strong biological motivation: iterative refinement mirrors human visual processing and addresses CNN inflexibility post-training.  
- Technical implementation is clearly described and SNES choice is justified given the large parameter space.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, situating the contribution within recurrent processing, attention, and RL literature.  
- Lacks deep analysis of what the attention mechanism is actually attending to and why filter changes correct specific misclassifications.  
- Reliance on SNES may limit accessibility and raises questions about computational cost and alternative RL methods.  
- Evaluation is limited to CIFAR datasets, leaving generalizability to larger/complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet) untested.  
- No sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters (T, λcorrect, λmisclassified, λL2), so robustness is unclear.  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., Maxout base, RL algorithm, number of passes).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful idea with promising empirical gains, strong motivation, and technical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of the learned attention, limited experimental scope, and potential accessibility issues due to SNES.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on revisions—reviewer requests substantial additional analyses and experiments (inconsistently labeled as minor vs. major revision).  
- Requested revisions: deeper interpretation/visualization of attention (e.g., activation maximization), discussion of generalizability and limitations, hyperparameter sensitivity study, ablation experiments, and a clearer, more accessible discussion of SNES vs. alternatives.
