- Proposes dasNet: add multiplicative per-feature-map gates to a trained Maxout CNN and iteratively refine predictions via a policy over T passes.
- Policy observes mean map activations, pre-softmax activations, and class probabilities to produce gating actions; trained with SNES as a black-box optimizer.
- Reported improvements on CIFAR-10/100 versus a Maxout baseline, claiming ~6% relative gain and “state-of-the-art” for unaugmented data.
- Novelty: internal, global per-feature-map gating over multiple passes, distinguishing it from saccade/glimpse attention and classical recurrent CNNs.
- Method is simple and technically sound; gates are positive, normalized, and maintain average activation.
- Observation vector plausibly sufficient but discards spatial information, limiting attention to global channel reweighting.
- Using SNES to train a ~10^6-parameter linear policy is reasonable but unexplained and not compared to gradient-based alternatives like backprop-through-time or policy gradients.
- Boosted loss upweights misclassified examples appropriately; stability analysis shows dynamics remain stable beyond training T.
- Experimental reporting is weak: absolute error rates, standard deviations, and full tables are missing; only relative improvements reported.
- The “unaugmented” SOTA claim is confusing and inconsistent since the Maxout baseline used augmentation; needs clarification and fair baseline comparisons.
- Missing key ablations: per-layer vs. all-layer gating, T=1 vs. multi-pass, nonlinear policies, backprop-trained gating, top-layer-only gates, and observation component effects.
- Efficiency metrics are absent: inference cost scales with T, but wall-clock, throughput, and training time details (e.g., 4 days SNES) are not reported.
- Robustness analysis to random seeds and per-class/confusion matrix improvements are missing to substantiate claims about fixing ambiguous cases.
- Baselines are incomplete: no comparisons to ensembling, test-time augmentation, deeper/wider CNNs, or multi-crop evaluation.
- Qualitative analysis is anecdotal (single cat/dog example); more systematic visualization of gate behavior and correlations with corrections are needed.
- Several implementation details are omitted: exact action/observation dimensionalities, policy architecture, SNES hyperparameters, learning schedule, early stopping, and generation counts.
- Paper is generally readable and well organized but contains typos, equation index issues, and missing referenced figures/tables.
- Originality is moderate: iteratively reweighting internal features is a novel twist, but components are related to existing attention and recurrent methods.
- Significance is conceptual but practical impact is uncertain due to modest gains, lack of strong baselines, and added inference cost.
- Pros: biologically inspired formulation, simple general mechanism, demonstrated improvements over a strong baseline, and a stability/info-content check.
- Cons: insufficient empirical evaluation, unjustified SNES choice, spatial information discarded in observations, and unreported test-time compute trade-offs.
- Key questions for authors: provide absolute test errors with confidence intervals; clarify “unaugmented” claim; specify action/observation sizes and policy architecture; report runtime overhead and accuracy vs. T; compare to backprop-trained gating, top-layer gating, ensembling, and test-time augmentation; and give per-class confusion changes.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — compelling idea but insufficient experimental evidence and comparisons for acceptance; stronger empirical work could change the verdict.
