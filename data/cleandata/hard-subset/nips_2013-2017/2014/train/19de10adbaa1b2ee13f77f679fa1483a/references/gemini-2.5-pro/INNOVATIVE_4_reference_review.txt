**Summary**

This paper introduces Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a novel architecture that augments a standard, pre-trained convolutional neural network with feedback connections. The core idea is to enable a form of internal, selective attention where the network can iteratively refine its predictions on a given input image over multiple passes. Unlike a standard feedforward pass, dasNet learns a control policy that modulates the activations of convolutional filters at each layer. This policy is learned via reinforcement learning (specifically, using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies, or SNES) after the base CNN has been trained via supervised learning. The authors frame this as mimicking human visual processing, which involves a rapid initial feedforward pass followed by slower, more deliberate attentional processing for difficult cases. The method is evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, where it improves upon a state-of-the-art Maxout network baseline, particularly on ambiguous examples.

**Strengths**

The primary strength of this paper is its outstanding novelty and conceptual boldness. The idea of decoupling the feature extraction (the pre-trained CNN) from a dynamic control policy (the RL agent) that modulates internal representations is a significant departure from the standard, static inference paradigm in deep learning. This introduces a notion of "computational effort" or "thinking" that can be allocated to more challenging inputs post-training.

1.  **High Novelty and Significance:** The proposed framework is highly original. While recurrence and attention are not new to NIPS, applying them as a distinct, RL-trained control layer on top of a frozen, state-of-the-art feedforward model is a powerful and new concept. It opens up an exciting research direction into networks that can adapt their inference process on-the-fly, which has profound implications for efficiency and accuracy. This is not an incremental improvement but a potential paradigm shift in how we view inference.

2.  **Strong Motivation:** The work is well-motivated by compelling parallels to the human visual system, specifically the distinction between a fast, pre-attentive phase and a slower, attentional phase. This provides a strong conceptual grounding for the architectural choices.

3.  **Impressive Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates a tangible benefit, achieving a new state-of-the-art result on CIFAR-100 at the time of publication. The fact that this performance gain is achieved by "fixing" the mistakes of an already powerful baseline model validates the core hypothesis that iterative refinement can resolve ambiguities that a single feedforward pass cannot.

4.  **Bold Methodological Choice:** Using reinforcement learning to learn the control policy is ambitious. While the choice of SNES might be debated (see weaknesses), the successful application of RL in this high-dimensional internal state space of a deep network is a noteworthy achievement.

**Weaknesses**

The paper's strengths in novelty and ambition naturally lead to some methodological aspects that are less polished and could be improved in future work. However, in my view, these are acceptable trade-offs for the significance of the core contribution.

1.  **Use of Black-Box Optimization:** The policy is learned using SNES, a black-box, gradient-free optimization method. While the authors justify this by the high dimensionality of the parameter space, it feels somewhat brute-force. A differentiable, gradient-based RL approach (e.g., policy gradients) would be more elegant and potentially more sample-efficient, though admittedly much harder to implement in this context. This stands as the main methodological weakness, but it is a practical choice that enables the exploration of the core idea.

2.  **Ad-hoc State Representation:** The observation vector for the RL agent is a concatenation of averaged map activations, intermediate layer outputs, and final class probabilities. While this seems to work, it is a heuristic. There is little analysis of why this is a sufficient state representation or exploration of alternatives. A more principled approach to defining the network's internal state could lead to better policies.

3.  **Limited Analysis and Interpretability:** The authors provide an interesting qualitative example of the cat/dog classification, but as they note, a deep understanding of what the policy is *doing* remains elusive. The analysis confirming the information content of the final gate values is a good sanity check but does not provide deep insight into the learned strategy. A deeper analysis of the dynamics over more time steps and across different classes would strengthen the paper, but its absence does not invalidate the results.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Introduces a highly novel and conceptually exciting idea of dynamic, iterative inference controlled by an RL agent.
*   The approach has the potential to inspire a significant amount of follow-up research on adaptive computation in neural networks.
*   Achieves strong, state-of-the-art empirical results on a challenging benchmark, validating the proposed method.
*   The work is ambitious and pushes the boundaries of current deep learning paradigms.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
*   The RL methodology relies on a black-box optimizer (SNES), which is less elegant than a gradient-based alternative.
*   The state representation for the policy is heuristic and not deeply analyzed.
*   The analysis of the learned attentional policies is preliminary.

**Recommendation:** Accept

This paper is a quintessential NIPS paper. It presents a bold, creative, and thought-provoking idea that challenges standard assumptions about inference in deep networks. While the methodology has some unrefined aspects, such as the use of SNES and the heuristic state representation, these are minor flaws in the context of the work's novelty and significance. The paper successfully demonstrates that its ambitious approach yields tangible performance gains on a competitive benchmark. I strongly believe this work will be influential and will open up new avenues of research. The prioritization of a groundbreaking concept over minor methodological perfection makes this a clear and enthusiastic accept.
