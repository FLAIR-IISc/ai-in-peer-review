- Overall assessment: promising, ambitious approach but lacks sufficient detail; reviewer leans to weak reject with invitation to resubmit after fixes.
- Core idea: iteratively refine CNN classifications via learned feedback that modulates filter activations using reinforcement learning.
- Motivation: well-motivated by human visual processing and recurrent attention literature.
- Summary: dasNet augments a Maxout CNN with feedback policy learned by Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).
- Reported result: ~6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100 and ability to correct misclassifications via iterative refinement.
- Novelty: learning an internal attentional mechanism post-training to modulate filters is a novel contribution.
- SNES choice: reasonable justification for SNES given the large parameter space.
- Qualitative insight: visualizations show targeted changes in filter activations rather than uniform amplification.
- Missing policy details: architecture, layer sizes, activations, and training specifics of the policy network are not provided.
- Observation design unclear: chosen observation features (average/intermediate activations, class probs) are arbitrary and lack justification or ablation.
- Action scaling unexplained: rationale and effect of scaling softmax outputs to average 1 are not sufficiently discussed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: no analysis of sensitivity to SNES learning rate, population size, or reward-shaping weights.
- Computational cost: training takes 4 days on a GTX 560 Ti, raising scalability concerns.
- Limited ablations: insufficient studies on effects of number of iterative passes (T) and other components.
- Generalizability limited: experiments only on CIFAR-10/100; unclear performance on larger datasets like ImageNet.
- For acceptance: introduces dynamic attention to CNNs, shows measurable gains, and is conceptually well-motivated.
- Against acceptance: methodological details lacking, approach feels ad-hoc, high compute cost, and limited experimental scope.
- Requested actions: provide detailed policy architecture, justify/expand observation features, explain softmax scaling, run hyperparameter sensitivity and ablation studies, address compute/scalability, evaluate on larger datasets, and detail SNES implementation and optimizations.
