- Proposes dasNet: a recurrent feedback policy that multiplicatively gates convolutional feature maps across T test-time passes to refine a frozen Maxout CNN.  
- Policy observes compact statistics (global average per feature map, intermediate classifier activations, class probabilities) and outputs per-feature gates for the next pass.  
- Only the feedback policy is learned using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES); base CNN weights remain frozen.  
- Reports empirical improvements on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline, claiming state-of-the-art for single unaugmented models and qualitative fixes of ambiguous cases.  
- Situates work in biological top-down modulation, iterative inference, and RL-based attention; differs by performing global channel-wise reweighting rather than spatial glimpses.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, implementable mechanism for feature-wise top-down modulation across layers.  
- Strength: clean separation of supervised feature learning from post hoc control learning with a strong baseline integration.  
- Strength: empirical gains and qualitative evidence that multi-pass feedback corrects ambiguous classifications.  
- Strength: stability analysis showing peak performance near trained T and mild degradation beyond it.  
- Strength: compact observation design is computationally efficient.  
- Weakness: Choice of SNES over backprop/BPTT or deterministic policy gradients is unconvincing and lacks comparative evidence on sample-efficiency and generalization.  
- Weakness: Notation and loss clarity issues (ambiguous definition of d, boosted loss, and potential off-by-one in pass indexing) need correction.  
- Weakness: Action parameterization (scaled softmax enforcing positive gates with mean 1) restricts expressiveness by preventing uniform amplification/damping; needs justification or alternatives.  
- Weakness: Observation design discards spatial information, making the method channel-wise rather than spatially selective and potentially misleadingly labeled “selective attention.”  
- Weakness: Experimental reporting is thin—missing exact error rates, confidence intervals, number of seeds, and statistical significance for claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: Missing baselines/ablations—compare to single-pass learned reweighting, gradient-based policy training, fine-tuning base CNN, layer-restricted gating, and standard ensembling/augmentation.  
- Weakness: Risk of overfitting by focusing loss on misclassified examples; need validation curves, early stopping, and multi-seed results.  
- Clarity: Provide exact dimensionalities of A and O, policy architecture details, and all SNES hyperparameters.  
- Originality: Learned internal channel-wise iterative feedback is novel and distinct from spatial/glimpse attention; use of large-scale black-box evolution is unusual but not well-justified.  
- Significance: If robust, demonstrates that iterative top-down modulation can improve recognition on small images and may inspire feature-wise modulation research.  
- Suggestions: Add comprehensive quantitative results with error bars, ablations, head-to-head comparison of SNES vs gradient methods, analysis of where corrections occur, alternative action parameterizations, and clearer training/validation protocols.  
- Pros: Clear novel mechanism, improvements over a strong baseline, biologically plausible and simple to implement.  
- Cons: Insufficient ablations, weak SNES justification with scalability/sample-efficiency concerns, notation ambiguities, and limited experimental detail.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject without stronger empirical evidence and optimizer justification; could be acceptable if camera-ready addresses ablations and clarifies results.
