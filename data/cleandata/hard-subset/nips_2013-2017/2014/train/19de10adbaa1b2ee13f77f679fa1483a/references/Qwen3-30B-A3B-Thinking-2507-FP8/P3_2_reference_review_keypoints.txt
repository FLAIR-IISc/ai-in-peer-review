- Summary: Proposes dasNet, which uses an RL-trained policy (SNES) to iteratively modulate CNN filter activations across multiple passes to refine classifications; shows modest gains on CIFAR-10/100 with visualizations.

- Strength: Novel application of RL to control internal network dynamics in a post-training phase.

- Strength: Biologically motivated by human iterative visual processing.

- Strength: Practical choice of SNES to handle high-dimensional policy space.

- Strength: Clear experimental examples and visualizations illustrating the attention mechanism.

- Strength: Uses a reasonable baseline (vanilla Maxout network).

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why RL-based internal attention should outperform standard CNNs.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison and contextualization with recent attention mechanisms (e.g., CBAM, SE) and iterative refinement works.

- Weakness: Modest performance gains (~6% relative) and unclear improvement over modern SOTA architectures.

- Weakness: No analysis of inference-time computational cost due to multiple passes.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation only on small datasets (CIFAR-10/100); no large-scale validation (e.g., ImageNet).

- Weakness: Unclear overall contribution beyond applying RL to modulate filters.

- Quality assessment: Methodologically sound but experimental evaluation is limited and claims about state-of-the-art are misleading.

- Clarity issues: Missing exact dimensions for observation/action spaces and vague description of observation vector; some figures lack sufficient explanation.

- Originality assessment: Idea is somewhat novel but not clearly differentiated from existing recurrent/attention approaches.

- Significance assessment: Limited by small gains, computational overhead, and lack of large-scale results; practical impact is questionable.

- Recommendation: Reject — needs substantial revisions and stronger empirical and comparative evidence.

- Required improvements suggested: compare to modern CNNs (ResNet/EfficientNet), analyze inference cost, expand literature review vs. attention methods, and justify benefits over single-pass attention.

- Pros summary: Biologically motivated, creative RL application, informative visualizations, practical SNES implementation.

- Cons summary: Small gains, poor comparison to SOTA, no inference-cost analysis, weak contextualization, limited datasets, weak theoretical justification.
