- Summary: Proposes dasNet—additive multiplicative gates per feature map modulated across repeated passes by a learned policy (trained with SNES) using layer-summary observations; improves over Maxout on CIFAR-10/100 and yields interpretable corrections.

- Relation to prior work: Biologically motivated, connects to recurrent/top-down feedback and RL-attention literature, but novel in modulating internal feature maps and using SNES for a large control policy.

- Strength: Clear, biologically inspired idea that minimally augments strong CNN baselines to enable top-down modulation.

- Strength: Simple mechanism compatible with standard CNNs and a Maxout baseline.

- Strength: Empirical gains on CIFAR-10/100 and qualitative examples showing multi-pass feedback corrects confusions.

- Strength: Dynamics appear stable around trained step count T and final gate values encode class-relevant information.

- Weakness: Missing important baselines—end-to-end recurrent/gated CNN trained with BPTT, single-pass conditional gating (T=1), static per-map scaling, curriculum/hard-example fine-tuning, and random/heuristic or smaller policies.

- Weakness: Policy architecture and capacity unclear—need explicit dim(O), dim(A), total θ, and justification for a large linear-softmax policy vs. overfitting.

- Weakness: Placement of gates relative to Maxout pooling and architectural details are underspecified; a per-layer schematic/table is needed.

- Weakness: Claim of “state-of-the-art for unaugmented data” is inconsistent with augmentation used; augmentation and comparison regime must be clarified.

- Weakness: Optimization/RL framing is stretched—objective is supervised final-step cross-entropy optimized by SNES, with no sequential reward or credit-assignment discussion and no comparison to policy-gradient or differentiable alternatives.

- Weakness: Validation and early stopping for SNES are not described; risk of overfitting and lack of protocol are concerning.

- Weakness: Experiments lack ablations (which observation components matter, per-layer gate importance, effect of T on test performance) and per-class/confusion analyses to support ambiguity claims.

- Weakness: Runtime, memory, and wall-clock overheads are not quantified beyond a single GPU runtime statement.

- Quality: Technically plausible and implementable, but empirical evaluation is thinner than expected and several hyperparameters and SNES details are underspecified.

- Clarity: Paper is readable but needs explicit dimensions, architecture tables, exact error rates with standard deviations, SNES hyperparameters, and minor reference/notation fixes.

- Originality: Novel combination of per-map gating, iterative multi-pass processing, and large-scale SNES training for a control policy.

- Significance: Moderate—interesting concept with potential, but practical impact depends on simpler gradient-based alternatives, compute overhead, and scalability to larger datasets.

- Key questions for authors: report dim(O)/dim(A)/#gates/#θ, validation/early-stopping protocol for SNES, absolute CIFAR improvements with/without augmentation and competing methods, T=1 comparison, feasibility/performance of end-to-end backprop, and test-time cost multiplier for T=5.

- Pros (summary): Elegant, biologically motivated mechanism; demonstrable accuracy gains; interpretable and stable iterative behavior.

- Cons (summary): Missing strong baselines and ablations; insufficient methodological details for reproducibility; SNES choice not well justified; test-time compute not quantified.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (borderline)—interesting and timely idea but requires stronger comparative experiments and clearer reporting.

- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
