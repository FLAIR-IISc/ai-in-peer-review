- Paper proposes dasNet: add feedback connections and an RL-learned policy to iteratively modulate CNN filter activations across multiple passes for improved attention on ambiguous images.

- Strength: Novel conceptual framework linking iterative internal attention in CNNs to human visual processing and biological evidence.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation highlighting CNNs’ static behavior and the need for a mechanism to adapt across multiple passes.

- Strength: Experimental design focuses on ambiguous cases and includes useful visualizations of attention dynamics.

- Strength: Practical implementation that augments existing Maxout networks with attention gates without changing core architecture.

- Strength: Analysis shows stable behavior across different numbers of passes and insights into temporal dynamics.

- Weakness: Choice of SNES for policy learning is weakly justified and lacks comparison to gradient-based RL methods (e.g., policy gradients).

- Weakness: No theoretical justification or analysis of convergence, required number of passes, or relation between passes and task complexity.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons to true state-of-the-art models (e.g., ResNet, EfficientNet, transformers) making the 6% relative gain hard to contextualize.

- Weakness: Insufficient experimental details (policy architecture, full hyperparameters) and high computational cost versus modest accuracy gain.

- Weakness: Mischaracterizes related iterative/attention work and understates prior art that achieved improvements on benchmarks.

- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (SNES choice, observation features, number of passes).

- Quality: Technically sound implementation and results support claims, but methodological choices limit impact.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized with clear methodology and visualizations, though RL rationale needs clarification and supplementary details.

- Originality: Novel within CNNs to use internal selective feedback attention, but incremental relative to broader attention literature (SE, CBAM, transformers).

- Significance: Interesting idea with limited practical significance due to modest gains, high compute cost, and unclear scalability to larger datasets.

- For acceptance: Novel biologically motivated idea, solid implementation, insightful visualizations, and addresses static behavior of CNNs.

- Against acceptance: Poor justification for SNES, weak SOTA comparisons, lack of ablations, high computational cost, and weak theoretical grounding.

- Overall recommendation: Weak reject — promising concept but execution and evaluation insufficient for acceptance.

- Specific improvements: Compare to gradient-based RL, evaluate against modern SOTA baselines, provide full implementation/hyperparameter details, add ablation studies, reframe SOTA claims, and better contextualize related work.
