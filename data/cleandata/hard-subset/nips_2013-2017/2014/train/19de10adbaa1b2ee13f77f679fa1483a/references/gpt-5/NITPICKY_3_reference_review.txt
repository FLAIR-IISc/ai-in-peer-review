Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a recurrent augmentation to a pretrained Maxout CNN that iteratively modulates the strength of each convolutional feature map via multiplicative gates over T passes of the same image. A deterministic policy πθ maps observations (concatenated averages of intermediate feature-map activations, classification-layer activations, and class probabilities) to an action vector of gates a, with a softmax scaled so the average gate is 1 and all gates are positive. The policy parameters (≈1M) are optimized with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) using a boosted negative log-likelihood loss on the final pass. Experiments on CIFAR-10/100 claim improvements over a vanilla Maxout baseline and qualitative evidence that the network corrects ambiguous mistakes via selective emphasis/de-emphasis of maps.

The work relates to recurrent and attentional models in vision and to RL for attention; unlike prior approaches, it modulates internal CNN filters directly and learns a high-dimensional policy via black-box optimization rather than end-to-end backprop through time. The paper cites relevant biological and ML attention literature, Maxout/dropout, and NES.

Strengths
- Conceptually simple, general mechanism: per-map gates that can be applied to many CNNs without architectural redesign.
- Clear intuition and plausible biological motivation (top-down modulatory feedback).
- Use of SNES is pragmatic for million-parameter policies where conventional RL may be impractical.
- Empirical results claim nontrivial gains on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline; qualitative examples are compelling.
- Stability study suggests the dynamics do not explode when run for more steps than trained on.

Weaknesses and methodological concerns
- Clarity/notation issues in core objective: Li = −λboost d log(vT) is ambiguous (likely −λboost log vT[d]). Eq. (10) defines λboost = λcorrect if d = ||vT||∞; comparing a class index (d) to a probability magnitude is nonsensical. Presumably the condition is argmax(vT) = d. These errors cast doubt on the exact training signal.
- Insufficient architectural/training details to reproduce:
  - Maxout CNN: missing numbers of maps per layer, kernel sizes, strides, spatial pooling specifics, maxout block sizes, learning rate schedule, optimizer, epochs.
  - Observation vector o: exact dimensionality and which layers are included; how averages are computed (over x,y only? pre/post nonlinearity?).
  - Action space A: precise mapping from action indices to layer maps; whether biases are present in πθ; any normalization/clipping of gates beyond positivity/mean-1.
  - SNES: stopping criterion, learning rates, fitness shaping, batch size effects.
- Baselines are thin. Comparisons are only to the base CNN. Missing:
  - A “static gates” ablation (per-map scalars learned once by supervised training).
  - A supervised recurrent baseline trained end-to-end with backprop through time using the same observation/action design.
  - A simple second-pass refinement network without feedback.
  - Alternative attention mechanisms (e.g., layer-wise reweighting conditioned on the same observations) trained by standard SGD.
- Claims of state-of-the-art are under-supported. Table 1 is referenced but not shown; “relative improvement of 6%” lacks absolute numbers and CIs. The text also says “for unaugmented data” while the method section states that data augmentation was used. This contradiction must be resolved.
- Overfitting/validation protocol unclear. T, λ values, and SNES stopping were “experimentally determined”; was a validation set used? With a 1M-parameter policy and boosted loss emphasizing misclassified training samples, overfitting risk is significant. No learning curves, no ablation of λcorrect/λmisclassified, no report of validation performance.
- Computational cost is not quantified at test time. The method runs T passes (T=5), plus policy evaluations, which may be substantial. No throughput or latency numbers are reported; no analysis of accuracy vs. compute trade-off by varying T.
- The “gates carry information” analysis is weak: reporting 40.70%/45.74% accuracy for kNN/logistic regression on CIFAR-100 as “similar to dasNet” is implausible given typical Maxout baselines; precise baselines are missing, and methodology (train/test split, features normalization) is not described.
- Design justification is limited:
  - Why average feature-map activations suffice as observations (vs. richer statistics)?
  - Why a linear policy (θo followed by softmax) rather than a deeper controller?
  - Why SNES rather than policy gradients or evolution strategies with richer covariance?
- Biological framing overreaches: the method does not implement explicit top-down feature computations or spatial attention; it is a global per-map gain modulation. “Both bottom-up and top-down special connections” is misleading; the feedback is an external controller gating existing feedforward activations.
- Notation and exposition problems hinder understanding:
  - Conflation of spatial pooling and Maxout across channels; Eq. (2) uses “Pooling Layer” to denote Maxout over b maps but earlier describes spatial pooling.
  - Equation indices and layer symbols are inconsistent; biases and nonlinearities are sometimes omitted.
  - Algorithm 1 has off-by-one issues (loop t=0..T computes aT+1).
- References are inconsistent and incomplete (missing years/pages; duplicates; nonstandard formatting). Several key related works on recurrent CNNs and attention (e.g., end-to-end attention models) are not discussed comparatively.

Questions for the authors
- Provide exact architecture, training settings, and the full Table 1 with absolute accuracies, standard deviations, and whether augmentation was used.
- Clarify Eqs. (8)–(10): what exactly is the loss and the boosting condition?
- Did you use a validation set to select T and λ values? How sensitive are results to these choices?
- What is the test-time compute overhead (FLOPs or images/sec) vs. the baseline for T=1..5?
- Results for static learned gates, supervised recurrent training, and a policy with hidden layers?

Minor comments/formatting
- Multiple typos and grammar issues (e.g., “used to reduced,” “In this work we use the Maxout networks”; inconsistent spacing/symbols).
- Figures/Tables are referenced but not included; ensure captions quantify improvements.
- Clarify whether yℓj in Eq. (5) is pre- or post-activation and pre- or post-spatial pooling.

Recommendation
Reject. The idea is interesting and potentially impactful, but the paper contains multiple notation errors in the core loss, insufficient experimental detail, weak and incomplete baselines, and contradictory claims about augmentation/SOTA. With clearer exposition, rigorous comparisons, and a reproducible experimental section, this could become a strong submission.

Reviewer confidence: high.
