- Overall: Novel, well-motivated approach adding learned post-training internal attention to CNNs; achieves SOTA on CIFAR-10/100 and merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: dasNet adds feedback connections and learns a policy via SNES to iteratively modulate filter activations after initial supervised training.  
- Positioning: Appropriately related to prior CNN, attention, and neuroscience literature and distinguished from prior RL-for-vision work by applying to strong CNNs.  
- Novelty: Learning a post-training attentional policy to modulate internal filter activations is a unique contribution.  
- Performance: Demonstrates a 6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10, establishing new state-of-the-art results.  
- Biological plausibility: Iterative feedback and refinement are inspired by neuroscience, improving interpretability.  
- Motivation and exposition: Clear motivation and relatively comprehensive description of architecture, RL training (SNES), and experiments.  
- Dynamics analysis: Investigation of attention dynamics across different T values provides useful behavioral insights.  
- Computational cost: Training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), raising concerns about scalability to larger datasets/networks.  
- Limited generalization: Experiments confined to CIFAR-10/100, unclear performance on datasets like ImageNet.  
- Policy interpretability: Visualization of gate changes is a start but the reasons behind policy decisions remain unclear.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Number of steps T was chosen experimentally but lacks systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- SNES choice: SNES is reasonable given parameter scale but lacks stronger justification versus other RL methods.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with supported results, but cost and generalization concerns temper evaluation.  
- Clarity assessment: Well-written overall, though SNES explanation could be simplified for broader readership.  
- Originality assessment: Highly original application of RL to internal CNN activations.  
- Significance assessment: Significant potential impact due to performance gains and increased robustness/interpretability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel effective method, SOTA results, biological framing, and thorough methodology.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High computational cost, limited generalization analysis, interpretability challenges, and missing hyperparameter studies.  
- Recommendation and revisions: Accept with minor revisions—expand discussion of computational cost/scalability and alternative RL options, add sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (e.g., T), deepen analysis of the learned policy, and discuss generalization to other datasets.
