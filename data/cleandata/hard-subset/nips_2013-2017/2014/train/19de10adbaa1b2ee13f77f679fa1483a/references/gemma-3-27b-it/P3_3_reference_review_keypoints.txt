- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections using SNES to iteratively modulate internal filter activations, improving classification on CIFAR-10/100, especially ambiguous cases.  
- Situates work within CNN, RL, and biological attention literature and emphasizes focus on internal attention rather than external mechanisms.  
- Differentiates contribution by applying RL-based internal attention to a large-scale, state-of-the-art CNN and demonstrating tangible performance gains.  
- Novelty: learning a feedback policy to modulate internal filters is a significant and original contribution.  
- Biological plausibility: motivated by human visual iterative processing and top-down influences in cortex.  
- Strong empirical results: measurable improvement over Maxout baseline and insightful ablation analyses (dynamics, gate info).  
- Technical soundness: sensible combination of Maxout networks and SNES with clear justification for SNES on high-dimensional control.  
- Clear writing and organization make complex components accessible.  
- Scalability concern: SNES and training cost are substantial (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), limiting practicality for larger models/datasets.  
- Policy interpretability is limited; visualizations don’t fully explain why actions are taken.  
- Limited analysis of failure cases; paper emphasizes successes without deeply exploring where dasNet fails.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not analyzed; choices (λcorrect, λmisclassified, λL2, T) may heavily affect results.  
- Dependence on Maxout networks may limit generalizability to other architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, biologically motivated, and empirically effective on benchmarks.  
- Arguments for rejection: high computational cost, limited interpretability, and reliance on a specific CNN architecture.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Revision recommendations: discuss scalability and mitigation strategies, analyze failure cases, test applicability to other CNN architectures, and include a hyperparameter sensitivity study.
