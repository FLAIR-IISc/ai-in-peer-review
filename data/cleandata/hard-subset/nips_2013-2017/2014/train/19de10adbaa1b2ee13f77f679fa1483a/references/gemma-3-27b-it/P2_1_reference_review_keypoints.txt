- Paper introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections that modulate filters via an RL policy (SNES) to iteratively refine classifications.  
- Novelty: Learning post-training which filters to emphasize/de-emphasize via reinforcement learning is a unique contribution beyond fixed attention/gating.  
- Significance: Mimics iterative human-like vision and shows practical improvement on difficult examples, addressing one-shot classifier limitations.  
- Soundness: Experiments on CIFAR-10/100 with a strong Maxout baseline show meaningful gains (6% on CIFAR-10); filter activation analysis offers initial insight.  
- Clarity: Well-written, organized paper with helpful explanatory diagrams.  
- Computational cost: Training dasNet is significantly more expensive than the baseline; SNES and large parameter space increase runtime and complexity.  
- Limited analysis: Explanations for why dasNet corrects misclassifications are superficial; more rigorous analysis of the learned attention policy is needed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient detail on selection of hyperparameters (T, SNES learning rates, lambda), raising reproducibility and robustness concerns.  
- Related work: Related work section lacks focused discussion on dynamic networks and learned modulatory/recurrent connections that modify behavior post-training.  
- Overall assessment: Promising and novel approach with encouraging results, but drawbacks include computational cost, limited analysis, and hyperparameter issues.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel method addressing CNN limitations, meaningful experimental improvement, clear writing, and potential to inspire further research.  
- Arguments for rejection: High computational cost, superficial policy analysis, and inadequate hyperparameter discussion impacting reproducibility.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (7/10) — strengths outweigh weaknesses, but authors should address analysis, hyperparameter selection, and scalability.
