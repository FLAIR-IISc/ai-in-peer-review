- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections using SNES to iteratively refine classifications post-supervised training.  
- Reviewer leans toward weak accept, contingent on addressing clarity and justification issues.  
- Novelty: post-hoc reinforcement-learned attention policy is a new angle compared to end-to-end attention.  
- Demonstrated improvement: reported gains on CIFAR-10/100, especially on hard examples, with useful filter activation visualizations.  
- Relevance: tackles CNN limitations on ambiguous cases and draws motivation from human visual attention.  
- Practical choice: SNES is reasonable for the large policy parameter space.  
- Clarity problems: vague description of observation h(Mt), unclear averaging of activations, and insufficient detail on action modulation (eq.5).  
- Hyperparameter opacity: T=5 chosen experimentally but selection process not described.  
- Design justification weak: unclear why Maxout networks were used and whether approach generalizes to ReLU or other activations.  
- Boosting parameters (λcorrect, λmisclassified, λL2) lack motivation or tuning rationale.  
- Limited ablations: no exploration of SNES population/learning rates or sensitivity to initial supervised training.  
- Poor positioning relative to prior attention work: insufficient comparison to spatial transformer networks, channel attention, and end-to-end methods.  
- Scalability concerns: no discussion or experiments on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) or computational cost of iterative refinement.  
- Quality: technically sound SNES implementation but weakened by missing details and analyses.  
- Clarity overall is a significant concern for reproducibility.  
- Originality: combining post-hoc RL with feedback attention is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: potential to improve difficult-case performance, but practical impact depends on scalability and further validation.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, empirical gains, and relevance to image classification.  
- Rejection arguments: lack of methodological clarity, insufficient justification for choices, and limited ablation/scalability analysis.  
- Recommendation and fixes: weak accept conditional on clarifying observation/action details, justifying Maxout and boosting params, adding ablation studies, comparing to other attention methods, and discussing scalability.
