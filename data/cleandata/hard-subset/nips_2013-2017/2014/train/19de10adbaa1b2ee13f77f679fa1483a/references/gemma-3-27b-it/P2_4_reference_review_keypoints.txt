- Summary: Proposes dasNet, using reinforcement-learned feedback to modulate CNN filter activations over multiple passes, improving classification on challenging CIFAR images.
- Novelty: Learning internal selective attention via RL inside a pre-trained CNN is a novel and compelling idea.
- Theoretical motivation: Well-motivated by parallels to human recurrent visual processing and the idea of “thinking longer” on hard cases.
- Empirical results: Demonstrates clear performance gains on CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100 and shows qualitative filter-activation changes.
- Addressing hard cases: Directly targets ambiguous/ambivalent examples, improving robustness.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with helpful Maxout network details.
- Computational cost: SNES-based RL training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), limiting scalability.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Paper lacks analysis of SNES hyperparameter robustness (population size, learning rate, regularization).
- Limited generalization: Experiments confined to CIFAR-10/100; unclear if policies generalize to other datasets/architectures.
- Interpretability: Learned attention policies remain largely a black box despite activation visualizations.
- SNES justification: No empirical comparison to simpler RL methods to justify choosing SNES.
- Ablation studies: Missing detailed ablations (e.g., number of passes T, components of observation vector o_t).
- Quality score: Technically sound with supported claims, but concerns about compute and generalization (Score: 7/10).
- Clarity score: Clear and organized presentation (Score: 8/10).
- Originality score: Novel integration of internal attention, feedback, and RL (Score: 8/10).
- Significance score: Promising results but tempered by computational cost and limited analysis (Score: 7/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel internal attention approach, demonstrated improvements, cognitive motivation, clear writing.
- Arguments for rejection: High computational cost, limited generalization, lack of ablations, and interpretability issues.
- Overall assessment: Solid, potentially impactful contribution but needs more analysis on cost, generalization, and interpretability.
- Recommendation: Weak accept with minor revisions, requesting cost-reduction discussion, one additional dataset, ablation studies, and deeper analysis of learned policies.
