- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections that modulate internal filters via an attentional policy trained with SNES after supervised training.  
- Enables iterative refinement over multiple passes to emphasize/suppress filters and improve difficult classifications.  
- Demonstrates modest but state-of-the-art improvements on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, with visualizations of filter weight changes.  
- Motivated by biological recurrent/feedback processing and situated within attention and reinforcement-learning literature.  
- Novel contribution: learning a post-training internal attentional policy rather than integrating attention during feedforward training.  
- Uses SNES as a pragmatic black-box optimizer to handle the large policy parameter space.  
- Strength: clear motivation, thorough related work, and technically sound methodology with reproducible claims.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why specific filters are modulated; interpretability of high-level filters is not deeply explored.  
- Weakness: substantial computational cost (4 days on GTX 560 Ti), raising scalability concerns for larger datasets/models.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity (T, SNES population, boosting parameters) is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Weakness: SNES is a black-box with potential for local optima and limited insight into learned policies.  
- Weakness: the impact and necessity of the boosting loss are not explicitly demonstrated or ablated.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, though SNES implementation details could be expanded.  
- Originality: combination of CNNs, feedback, and post-training attentional policy is novel and valuable.  
- Significance: promising direction, but limited by small datasets, computational cost, and modest gains; scaling remains to be shown.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, empirical gains, clear presentation, and relevance to improving robustness on hard cases.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited policy analysis, high computational cost, hyperparameter sensitivity, and modest improvement magnitudes.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept; recommended to address analysis and computational-cost concerns in revision.
