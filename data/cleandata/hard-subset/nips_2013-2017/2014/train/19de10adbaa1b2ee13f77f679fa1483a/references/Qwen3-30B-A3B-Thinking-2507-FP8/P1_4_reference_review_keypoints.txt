- Proposes dasNet: adds internal selective attention via feedback to CNNs, using SNES to learn a policy that modulates filters across multiple passes.  
- Demonstrates a reported 6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100 and claims state-of-the-art for those datasets.  
- Technically sound combination of reinforcement learning with CNNs; SNES is reasonable for the very high-dimensional action space.  
- Experimental design evaluates "difficult" cases where the base CNN errs, but overall evaluation is weak.  
- Improvement is modest compared to recent CNN advances and may not justify the "state-of-the-art" claim.  
- Fails to compare against modern attention modules (SE, CBAM) and architectures (ResNet, EfficientNet).  
- Explanation of attention mechanism is insufficient and lacks analysis of why specific filters are modulated.  
- Ambiguity in key technical details: whether modulation multiplies filters or activations (eq.5).  
- Observation vector description is vague, so it's unclear what information the policy uses (eq.9).  
- Training procedure is unclear (Algorithm 1), especially why a batch is used instead of per-image iterative processing.  
- Unclear differentiation from prior recurrent/recurrent-attention work and whether the learned attention is fundamentally new.  
- Scalability and computational cost are concerns; reported training time (~4 days on old GPU) suggests practical limitations.  
- Overall assessment: interesting idea but modest gains, insufficient comparisons, and unclear mechanism weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; suggests more thorough evaluation, clearer explanations, and comparisons before acceptance.  
- Specific suggested improvements: compare to SE/CBAM and modern CNNs, analyze filter modulation effects, clarify eq.5/eq.9 and Algorithm 1, address scalability, and show per-case base CNN vs dasNet performance.
