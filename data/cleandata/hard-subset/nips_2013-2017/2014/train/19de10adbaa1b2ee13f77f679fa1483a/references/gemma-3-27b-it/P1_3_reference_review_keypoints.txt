- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned internal feedback connections that iteratively emphasize/de-emphasize filters across multiple passes to mimic visual attention.
- Uses reinforcement learning (SNES) to learn a filter-level attention policy within the deep network.
- Demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, outperforming a strong Maxout CNN baseline, especially on challenging examples.
- Provides analysis of filter activation changes to illustrate how iterative attention corrects misclassifications.
- Situates the work well within prior literature on CNNs, recurrent vision, top-down processing, and RL-based attention mechanisms.
- Novel contribution: fine-grained, filter-level internal attention learned via RL inside a deep CNN.
- Practical scalability addressed by using SNES to handle a large policy parameter space.
- Clear and well-structured writing that makes the method and results easy to follow.
- Major weakness: high computational cost (training took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti), limiting accessibility.
- Limited evaluation of generalization: no experiments on other datasets or different CNN backbones.
- Learned attention policy remains largely a black box despite some activation analyses; interpretability is limited.
- Paper lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for SNES and the number of attention iterations (T).
- Insufficient justification or comparison for choosing SNES over alternative large-scale RL algorithms.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for more discussion of computational cost, some generalization experiments, hyperparameter analysis, and rationale for SNES.
