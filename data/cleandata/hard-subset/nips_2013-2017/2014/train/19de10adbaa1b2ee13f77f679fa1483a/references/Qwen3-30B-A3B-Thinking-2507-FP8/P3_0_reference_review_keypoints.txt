- Proposes dasNet: augment pre-trained Maxout CNNs with feedback-based internal attention that iteratively modulates filter weights across multiple passes to refine classification.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn a high-dimensional attention policy; evaluated on CIFAR-10/100 with ~6% relative improvement on difficult cases.
- Technically sound implementation with clear explanation and sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Experiments are well-designed, focusing on ambiguous/confused class pairs and including useful ablations (dynamics, gate information).
- Appropriate baselines against vanilla CNNs; claims of outperforming prior work are present but somewhat vague.
- No theoretical justification for using reinforcement learning over supervised alternatives; rationale is limited to practical high-dimensionality concerns.
- Limited ablation of key design choices (e.g., fixed T=5 passes) and unclear causes of performance drop after T steps.
- Weak theoretical analysis and underdeveloped connection to biological visual processing.
- Evaluation limited to CIFAR-10/100 and does not compare to modern architectures (e.g., ResNet, EfficientNet).
- Paper is well-structured and figures effectively illustrate attention dynamics.
- Terminology is ambiguous (feedback vs. top-down), hindering clarity about the connectivity semantics.
- Insufficient implementation details about the observation space h(M) and normalization, affecting assessment of scalability.
- Inconsistent notation for attention weights adds confusion.
- Novel in combining Maxout, RL, and feedback to modulate internal filters rather than spatial/external attention.
- Some overlap with prior iterative/attention work (Neural Abstraction Pyramid, Deep Recurrent Attention Networks); distinctions from related methods are not clearly drawn.
- Modest empirical gains (6% relative) compared to recent advances; added complexity may not justify benefit.
- Computational cost concerns: training time reported, but inference cost and scalability to larger models/datasets are not addressed.
- No discussion of broader limitations (scalability, other tasks like segmentation, generalization).
- Recommendations: compare to modern state-of-the-art models, provide theoretical justification for RL, clarify relation to prior work, report computational costs, detail observation construction, ablate T, and discuss limitations/scalability.
- Final recommendation: reject due to limited novelty, weak theoretical grounding, modest performance gains, and insufficient comparisons to contemporary baselines.
