- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with learned internal feedback gates that modulate per-feature-map activations across multiple passes to correct feedforward mistakes.
- Policy maps summary observations (feature-map averages and class probs) to multiplicative gates and is trained post hoc using Separable NES due to high policy dimensionality (~10^6).
- Demonstrates improvements over a Maxout baseline on CIFAR-10/100 and claims state-of-the-art (for unaugmented data at the time).
- Differs from spatial glimpse attention by focusing on channel-wise internal attention and relates to top-down/recurrent vision work, but omits some gradient-based alternatives.
- Strength: clear, appealing idea of iterative internal attention with a simple, CNN-compatible gating mechanism and sequential decision formulation.
- Strength: empirical analyses show multiple passes help ambiguous cases and gates carry informative dynamics.
- Weakness: weak justification for NES—model is differentiable and could be trained with BPTT; no comparison to gradient-based training.
- Weakness: limited baselines and ablations (no supervised channel-wise gating, post-hoc calibrators, or ensembling comparisons).
- Weakness: inference cost increases by factor T; runtime/latency and compute–accuracy trade-offs are not quantified.
- Weakness: observations discard spatial information (only map averages), so “attention” is strictly channel-wise and spatial attention is unexplored.
- Weakness: reporting lacks statistical rigor (no confidence intervals or multiple runs) and has notational ambiguities (e.g., definition of d, λboost).
- Weakness: evaluation limited to CIFAR; no larger-scale benchmarks to assess scalability and robustness.
- Technical quality: method is plausible and coherent, but NES for a million-parameter policy raises sample-efficiency and scalability concerns.
- Empirical support: gains and dynamics analysis are shown, but stronger baselines, variance estimates, and per-class analyses are missing.
- Clarity: generally readable with helpful algorithm box, but some notational imprecision and missing SNES/policy details hinder reproducibility.
- Originality: novel combination of internal channel-wise feedback attention and evolutionary policy learning for deep nets.
- Significance: conceptually influential and shows practical gains, but scalability and absence of gradient-based baselines limit immediate impact.
- Key questions for authors: why not train via BPTT, runtime overhead per image, sensitivity to observation design, variance over runs, and comparisons to simpler policies.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-motivated idea with demonstrated empirical gains and insightful dynamics analyses.
- Cons for acceptance: missing strong baselines, scalability/runtime concerns, and reporting/notation/statistical rigor issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept (7/10) with reviewer confidence 3/5.
