- Introduces dasNet: a method that iteratively refines CNN classifications by learning an attentional policy to modulate internal filter activations via feedback.  
- Uses reinforcement learning (SNES) to learn the high-dimensional control policy that weights filter activations after supervised training.  
- Demonstrates performance improvements over a baseline Maxout CNN on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, especially on difficult instances.  
- Positions work within attention and recurrent processing literature, citing biological feedback inspiration and prior RL/recurrence efforts.  
- Appropriately uses Maxout networks as a competitive base architecture.  
- Novelty: learning attentional modulation post-training is distinct from jointly learned attention mechanisms.  
- Addresses a real problem by targeting ambiguous/difficult instances where feedforward CNNs struggle.  
- Empirical results and visualizations (e.g., filter activation changes) provide intuitive evidence of the mechanism’s effect.  
- Scalability: SNES is a practical choice to handle the policy’s high dimensionality.  
- Paper is well-written with clear equations and a structured algorithm.  
- Computational cost is high (training took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti), limiting accessibility.  
- Limited analysis of why specific filters are emphasized or suppressed; deeper policy interpretability is lacking.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (T, SNES learning rates, λL2) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Generalizability is uncertain since experiments are confined to CIFAR-10/100 and not larger real-world datasets.  
- Information-content analysis of gates feels ad-hoc and doesn’t fully explain what gates encode.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with empirical gains and a scalable RL solution, and reproducible presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, insufficient hyperparameter exploration, shallow policy analysis, and limited dataset generalization.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: explicitly discuss computational cost and mitigation strategies, perform a more thorough hyperparameter study, deepen analysis of the learned policy/gates, and discuss challenges scaling to larger datasets.
