- Proposes dasNet: iterative internal selective attention in CNNs via feedback that modulates filter activations to refine ambiguous classifications.
- Uses Maxout networks as the base architecture and learns a feedback policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) due to a large parameter space.
- Demonstrates a 6% relative improvement over vanilla CNNs on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, establishing new state-of-the-art without data augmentation.
- Neuroscience motivation: models top-down feedback connections in visual cortex to enable “thinking” about ambiguous inputs.
- Technical quality is strong: clear algorithm, well-justified SNES choice, solid experimental design with ablations and qualitative filter analyses.
- Paper is technically complete and provides sufficient detail for replication, though some implementation specifics could be moved to supplementary material.
- Clarity is generally good and well-structured, but the construction of the observation vector (what and how components are concatenated/weighted) needs clearer explanation.
- Original contribution: applies reinforcement learning to control internal filter weights (not spatial attention), directly on state-of-the-art CNNs rather than toy models.
- Distinguishes itself from prior attention work and connects compellingly to neuroscience findings about feedback prevalence.
- Significance: addresses CNNs’ limitation on ambiguous cases and shows meaningful performance gains, with potential applicability beyond classification.
- Qualitative results show the method can disambiguate difficult examples (e.g., cat vs. dog) by focusing on specific features.
- Strengths: strong neuroscience motivation, measurable state-of-the-art improvement, novel RL-controlled internal attention, qualitative insights, and justified optimization choice.
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of computational/inference cost and runtime overhead.
- Weaknesses: lacks comparison with other recurrent or attention-based approaches (e.g., transformers, memory networks).
- Weaknesses: unclear justification for using reinforcement learning rather than end-to-end training of feedback connections.
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of which misclassification types are corrected and no discussion of scaling to larger datasets/tasks.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution that meaningfully advances state-of-the-art despite some analysis gaps.
- Recommendation: accept.
