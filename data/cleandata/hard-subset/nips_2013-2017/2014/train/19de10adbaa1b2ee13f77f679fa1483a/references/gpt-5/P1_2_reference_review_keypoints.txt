- Proposal: DasNet adds learned, multiplicative per-feature-map gates to a trained Maxout CNN and runs T iterative inference passes to modulate internal features via a learned policy.
- Mechanism: Observations (per-map averages, intermediate activations, class probs) feed a deterministic policy that outputs next-step gates; policy optimized with SNES and a boosted loss to upweight misclassified examples.
- Empirical claim: Reports improved accuracy on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline, with qualitative examples showing corrected initial misclassifications and gate patterns carrying class information.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to neuroscience and ML attention/feedback literature but differs from glimpse-based and reconstruction-focused recurrent CNNs by applying global per-channel modulation to a discriminative CNN.
- Training choice: Uses black-box Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to train a large continuous policy (~10^6 parameters) rather than differentiable alternatives.
- Technical soundness: Mechanism is simple, well-specified, with stabilization constraints on gates and a clear SNES setup including boosting.
- Empirical shortcomings: Exact quantitative results, error bars, and Table 1 entries are missing, making the claimed 6% relative improvement hard to assess.
- Limited dynamics analysis: Dynamics and gate analyses are interesting but fairly limited in scope.
- Missing baselines: No comparisons to test-time ensembling/augmentation, larger base CNNs, end-to-end BPTT recurrence, policy-gradient RL, or image-independent per-map scalars.
- Missing ablations: No ablation of observation vector components, gated layer scope, or sensitivity analyses for T.
- Computational costs: T× inference cost and SNES training compute/sample efficiency are not quantified or compared to alternatives.
- Overfitting risk: Boosted loss on misclassified samples could overfit; validation, early stopping, and generalization diagnostics are not described.
- Clarity issues: Request to include full Table 1 with exact scores, stddevs, and augmentation/committee settings.
- Ambiguity: Clarify inconsistency about data augmentation for base CNN vs. claim of “unaugmented” SOTA.
- Missing details: Specify numbers of gated maps, whether fully connected units are gated, observation dimensionality, and full policy architecture.
- SNES details lacking: Provide batch size per generation, learning rates, stopping criteria, and validation protocol for SNES.
- Originality: Novel combination of per-map iterative gain modulation, a global feedback policy conditioned on internal activations, and SNES training atop a strong CNN baseline.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if gains hold—simple, architecture-agnostic way to add test-time recurrence—but practical benefits vs. conventional alternatives are unclear.
- Strengths: Clear mechanism, integration with existing CNNs, empirical improvements on CIFAR, and neuroscience-inspired motivation.
- Weaknesses: Incomplete quantitative reporting, limited baselines/ablations, expensive training/inference, potential overfitting, and large linear policy with questionable parameter efficiency.
- Suggested experiments: Report full results with CIs, add baselines (wider/deeper CNNs, augmentation, BPTT, RL), ablate observation/action choices, and measure compute/time tradeoffs.
- Suggested model fixes: Consider reducing policy dimensionality (low-rank/grouped gates), adding nonlinearity, or testing image-independent scalars as controls.
- Acceptance case: Novel, simple mechanism with promising gains and good motivation bridging neuroscience and deep nets.
- Rejection case: Evaluation is incomplete by conference standards, practicality and attribution of gains unclear; accept only if substantial additional results and baselines are provided.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on providing full quantitative results and stronger baselines/ablations; otherwise borderline.
