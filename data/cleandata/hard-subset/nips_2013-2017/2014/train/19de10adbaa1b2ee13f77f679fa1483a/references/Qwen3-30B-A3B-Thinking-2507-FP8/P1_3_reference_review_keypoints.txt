- Introduces dasNet: use of feedback connections and RL to iteratively refine a pre-trained CNN's predictions across multiple passes.  
- Core mechanism: a learned policy (trained with SNES) adjusts filter weights per pass to implement selective attention.  
- Uses Maxout networks as the base and achieves a 6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100, setting a new state-of-the-art.  
- Method targets difficult/ambiguous classification cases rather than broad uniform improvements.  
- Experimental validation is thorough, methodology clearly described, and reproducible details are provided.  
- Use of SNES is well-justified to manage the large/high-dimensional policy space.  
- Computational cost is high (training dasNet took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti), limiting large-scale practicality.  
- Lacks a thorough theoretical analysis explaining why the approach works.  
- Some notation and descriptions are unclear, notably attention weight notation and the observation-to-action mapping.  
- Construction of the observation vector (h(M_t)) needs more justification and explanation.  
- Novelty: RL-driven dynamic adjustment of filters across passes is a distinct and original contribution.  
- The paper under-discusses connections/comparisons to recent attention mechanisms (e.g., transformers) and related CNN attention work.  
- Significance: generalizable approach with meaningful benchmark gains, but scalability to larger networks/datasets is not addressed.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-executed, and a meaningful advance; suggested improvements include clarifying observations, deeper theoretical analysis, comparisons to transformer-style attention, and discussion of scaling.
