- Proposal: dasNet adds recurrent, per-feature-map multiplicative gates to a Maxout CNN to implement internal selective attention via feedback.  
- Policy: A policy maps concatenated global observations to gate actions using a scaled softmax to keep average gate near 1.  
- Training: Policy learned post hoc with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) for scalability in high-dimensional parameter space.  
- Loss: Training emphasizes misclassified samples via a boosted cross-entropy-like loss.  
- Results: Reports improvements on CIFAR-10/100 and claims a new state-of-the-art for unaugmented single-model results.  
- Dynamics: Learned recurrent dynamics are stable beyond the training horizon and final gate values contain class-predictive information.  
- Novelty: Novel formulation of layer-wise feature-map gating trained with large-scale black-box optimization, building on prior RL/attention and recurrent CNN work.  
- Soundness: Architectural modification is straightforward and likely stable due to positive, normalized gates; SNES is reasonable for black-box policy search.  
- Empirical reporting issues: Lacks exact error rates, standard deviations, and clear context for the reported “6% relative” improvement.  
- Augmentation ambiguity: Conflicting statements about “unaugmented” results versus base Maxout being trained with augmentation; needs clarification on augmentation/ensembling.  
- Missing baselines: No comparisons to fine-tuning, simple calibration heads, end-to-end differentiable gating, or REINFORCE-based policies.  
- Observation design concern: Observation vector uses global averages and discards spatial information; observation design not studied.  
- Reproducibility gaps: Important implementation details (dimensions of O and A, per-layer map counts, gate initialization, loss indexing, stopping/validation criteria) are missing.  
- Algorithmic ambiguities: Equations and pseudo-code (Li definition, λboost criterion, indexing) are unclear or possibly misstated.  
- Clarity issues: Notation overloading (e.g., M vs Mt, θ reused) and conflation of augmentation settings hurt clarity.  
- Originality assessment: Moderately novel—idea is not fully new but the specific scaling and per-map gating formulation is new.  
- Significance concerns: Conceptually interesting but practical significance limited by lack of rigorous baselines, ablations, and scalability analysis.  
- Strengths: Biologically motivated, simple architectural hook, decoupled policy training, stability beyond training horizon, and informative visualizations.  
- Weaknesses: Insufficient experimental detail, absent ablations, high-dimensional action space vs limited signal, unquantified computational overhead, and notation ambiguities.  
- Key author questions: Request exact CIFAR error rates (mean±std), dimensions of O/A and gate constraints, inference-time overhead for T=5, comparisons to backprop/REINFORCE, and specific ablation results.  
- Suggested improvements: Clarify equations/pseudo-code, add rigorous baselines and ablations, report absolute error rates/variance and compute costs, explore spatial or group gating, and discuss scalability.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept (leaning weak accept) pending improved experimental reporting and stronger baselines.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
