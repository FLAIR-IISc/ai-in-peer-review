- Overall assessment: technically sound, interesting approach; lean toward Accept with minor revisions.

- Core idea: dasNet adds feedback connections to CNNs to iteratively modulate filter activations and refine classifications.

- Training/control: uses reinforcement learning (SNES) to learn a policy that modulates internal activations over multiple passes.

- Biological motivation: approach is inspired by iterative human visual processing and cortical feedback.

- Positioning: situates work among CNN, maxout, RL-based attention, and recurrent processing literature, distinguishing dasNet’s full inter-layer modulation.

- Empirical result: tangible improvement on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, ~6% relative improvement, especially on ambiguous cases.

- Interpretability: provides some visualizations (filter activation changes) that give partial insight into attention adjustments.

- Strength — novelty: novel combination of post-training RL adaptation and fully connected internal attention within a deep CNN.

- Strength — technical soundness: methodology is well-defined, Maxout baseline is appropriate, SNES choice is justified for large parameter space.

- Strength — application focus: addresses realistic limitation of CNNs by targeting ambiguous/difficult instances.

- Weakness — limited policy analysis: insufficient exploration of how the learned attention policies operate or consistent filter-class patterns.

- Weakness — SNES scalability/justification: limitations, computational cost, and scalability to larger networks are not fully addressed.

- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: no detailed analysis of sensitivity to SNES learning rate, iteration count (T), or regularization.

- Weakness — missing ablations: lacks studies on the effect of restricting feedback to certain layers or other architectural variants.

- Weakness — limited dynamic analysis: dynamics experiment (Figure 2) is shallow; needs convergence, stability, and longer-horizon analysis.

- Weakness — reward clarity: boosting reward function (Equation 8) and lambda choices are not clearly explained.

- Pro acceptance points: novel internal attention, measurable dataset improvements, sound methodology, clear algorithm presentation.

- Con acceptance points: limited policy analysis, missing hyperparameter/ablation studies, and SNES scalability concerns.

- Recommendations: expand learned-policy analysis, run ablation studies (e.g., layer-specific feedback), analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, clarify boosting reward intuition, and discuss SNES computational limitations.
