- Proposes dasNet: iterative internal selective attention via feedback that reweights CNN filters across multiple passes using an RL policy (SNES) on Maxout nets.  
- Motivated by biological two-stage visual processing to improve classification on difficult/ambiguous cases.  
- Reports ~6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10/100 and new SOTA for unaugmented CIFAR-10; qualitative examples show corrected misclassifications.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to learn a very large policy (over 1M parameters) as a practical solution for high-dimensional policy learning.  
- Strength: novel, biologically plausible idea that’s technically clear and implementable on top of existing CNNs.  
- Strength: solid experimental design focusing on ambiguous cases and ablation study on number of passes (T).  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from other attention mechanisms (e.g., Squeeze-and-Excitation, non-local nets, transformers).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational cost—especially inference cost (T=5 implies ~5× slower inference).  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent SOTA methods (EfficientNet, Vision Transformers, other attention architectures).  
- Weakness: incomplete SNES methodological details and hyperparameters, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases or scenarios where dasNet is not beneficial.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-written but needs more analysis on costs and comparisons to strengthen claims.  
- Originality: novel combination of Maxout networks with RL-based internal attention, but needs clearer distinction from prior attention work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address differentiation, computational cost, SOTA comparisons, and SNES details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful biological motivation, significant CIFAR improvements, clear implementable framework.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient differentiation, missing computational cost analysis, lack of recent SOTA comparisons, and missing methodological details.
