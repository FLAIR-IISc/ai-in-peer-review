- Proposes dasNet: a CNN augmented with learned per-feature-map multiplicative gates updated by a policy across multiple inference passes, trained with SNES.
- Gating mechanism and recurrent inference are conceptually simple and technically sound; SNES is a pragmatic choice for large parameter vectors.
- Observation design (per-map means, penultimate activations, class probabilities) is plausible for guiding the controller.
- Reports CIFAR-10/100 improvements over a Maxout baseline and claims SOTA for unaugmented data, but exact error rates and comparisons are missing.
- Ablations are limited: no comparison to end-to-end backprop through the unrolled model, no study of policy size, no ablation of observation features, and no comparison to simpler feedback.
- Methodological ambiguities: loss/boosting definition (Eq. 8–10) is unclear and the indicator logic needs clarification.
- Reproducibility issues: policy network architecture, action dimensionalities per layer, and full Maxout hyperparameters are underspecified.
- Regularization and constraints on gates (e.g., clipping) are not discussed beyond softmax scaling.
- Scope is narrow (32×32 CIFAR); claims about selective attention need stronger, systematic error and per-class analyses.
- Paper is well written with clear motivation and intuitions, but notation issues and missing experimental details reduce reproducibility.
- Originality lies in combining per-map multiplicative feedback, sequential re-evaluation, and large-scale black-box policy optimization on a modern CNN.
- Significance could be high if gains are validated, but current evidence is weakened by incomplete quantitative reporting and missing ablations.
- Computational concerns: inference cost increases by factor T and SNES sample complexity/training cost are not analyzed.
- Pros: biologically motivated idea, simple mechanism easy to integrate, demonstrated CIFAR gains, sensible observation design, useful qualitative visualizations.
- Cons: missing quantitative details and variance, lack of key comparisons, ambiguous loss and omitted architecture details, unassessed compute/scalability, no spatial selectivity.
- Suggestions: provide full results table with baselines and stddevs, compare SNES to backprop and simpler controllers, clarify loss and policy specs, report compute overhead, ablate observations and T, give confusion matrices and robustness analyses, and release code.
- Overall: promising and relevant but currently lacks crucial quantitative details and ablations; preliminary rating weak accept if additional analyses confirm clear gains, otherwise borderline.
