- Proposes dasNet: add feedback-based internal selective attention to a pre-trained Maxout CNN to iteratively modulate filter weights across multiple passes.  
- Learns the attention policy with SNES (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to handle very large parameter space (>1M) where standard RL is infeasible.  
- Demonstrates qualitative attention dynamics and achieves a 6% relative improvement on CIFAR-10, claimed as new state-of-the-art.  
- Methodology is technically sound and clearly differentiates dasNet from standard recurrent/attention models.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous with proper baselines, ablation studies (varying T), and reproducible hyperparameters.  
- Paper is well-written, structured, and uses effective figures; biological motivation is clearly integrated.  
- Novel idea: using RL to learn internal selective attention post-training rather than altering core architecture during training.  
- Practical significance: can improve existing networks without full retraining, useful when retraining is expensive.  
- Major drawback: high computational cost (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and unclear whether gains justify cost versus scaling depth/size.  
- Insufficient comparison to other attention mechanisms (spatial, self-attention) and lack of exploration of integrating dasNet during training.  
- Limited theoretical justification beyond biological analogy.  
- Overall positive assessment with recommendation for acceptance.
