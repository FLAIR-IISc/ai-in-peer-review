- Summary: Proposes dasNet—CNNs with feedback-modulated internal attention learned via RL (SNES) to iteratively adjust feature-map strengths across multiple passes; reports ~6% relative improvement on difficult CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100 cases.

- Strength: Addresses improving classification on ambiguous cases without retraining the entire network.

- Strength: Biologically plausible inspiration from human visual feedback mechanisms.

- Strength: Methodology is technically sound with a justified choice of SNES for high-dimensional policies.

- Strength: Experiments include a compelling visualization of the attention mechanism and acknowledge computational costs.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—similar feedback/iterative-processing ideas exist (e.g., Neural Abstraction Pyramid, recurrent processing) and differentiation is insufficient.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical explanation for why feedback improves performance and when it helps.

- Weakness: Limited experimental validation—only CIFAR-10/100, no ImageNet-scale tests or comparisons to recent SOTA methods.

- Weakness: Ambiguous implementation details—unclear normalization/weighting of observation vector and precise observation-to-action mapping.

- Weakness: Questionable practicality—multiple passes (T=5) increase inference time ~5x with no discussion of real-time applicability.

- Recommendation: Expand related work and explicitly differentiate dasNet from prior feedback/iterative-processing models.

- Recommendation: Add theoretical analysis or empirical analysis of feature-activation changes to explain the mechanism.

- Recommendation: Broaden experiments to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) and include ablation studies and SOTA comparisons.

- Recommendation: Clarify implementation details (observation vector normalization, weighting, and action mapping).

- Recommendation: Discuss computational trade-offs and potential adaptations for real-time or resource-constrained settings.

- Final assessment: Technically sound but incremental with weak theory and limited validation; not novel/impactful enough for NeurIPS.

- Scores & decision: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 5/10, Significance 6/10 — Overall recommendation: Reject.
