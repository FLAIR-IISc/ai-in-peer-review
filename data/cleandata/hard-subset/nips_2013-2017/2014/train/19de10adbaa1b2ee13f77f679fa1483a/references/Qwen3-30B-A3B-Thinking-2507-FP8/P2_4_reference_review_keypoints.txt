- Paper introduces dasNet: iterative internal selective attention using reinforcement learning to modulate CNN filters across multiple passes.  
- Uses Maxout base networks and learns attention policies with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle large policy parameter space.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10/100, reporting ~6% relative improvement, especially on ambiguous classification cases.  
- Methodology is technically sound and builds on established concepts; experimental design focuses appropriately on ambiguous cases.  
- Choice of SNES is reasonable given the high-dimensional policy search.  
- Improvement is modest and may not justify added complexity and iterative inference cost.  
- Comparisons are limited: mainly against “vanilla CNN” and lacking against recent attention mechanisms (e.g., Transformers) or iterative CNN methods.  
- No statistical significance testing presented for the reported gains.  
- Paper omits important architectural and SNES implementation details, hindering reproducibility.  
- The authors acknowledge multiple passes but do not quantify the trade-off between performance gains and increased inference time.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, with a strong related-work section linking neuroscience and computer vision.  
- Originality is strong: novel use of reinforcement learning for internal selective attention and post-training modulation of filter activations.  
- Significance is limited by modest gains, lack of large-scale evaluations (e.g., ImageNet), and unclear practical advantages over larger networks or ensembles.  
- Recommendation: borderline acceptance with major revisions — request broader SOTA comparisons, ImageNet or larger-dataset evaluation, statistical tests, computational cost analysis, and fuller architectural/implementation details.
