- Proposes Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet): learned feedback gates reweight CNN feature maps across T iterative passes via a policy network trained with SNES.
- Policy observes per-feature-map averages, classification pre-activations, and class probabilities and outputs multiplicative per-channel gates normalized to be positive with mean ~1.
- Trains policy after standard supervised Maxout CNN training; optimizes a weighted log-loss emphasizing misclassified examples.
- Reports consistent improvements on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline and claims state-of-the-art for “unaugmented data.”
- Novelty: internal, channel-wise attentional modulation across passes (vs. prior spatial glimpse attention or recurrent feedback approaches).
- Distinctive use of Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) for high-dimensional policy optimization instead of gradient-based RL or BPTT.
- Mechanism is straightforward, differentiable, and the observation/action parametrizations and gate normalization are sensible for stability.
- SNES description is correct but is a heuristic with limited covariance modeling; choice over gradient methods is not fully justified.
- Empirical results show improvements but omit critical details: exact error rates, confidence intervals/variance across runs, and full Table 1 contents.
- Ablation studies are limited (T sweeps only on a small model); missing per-layer gating, observation-design analysis, and main-model dynamics experiments.
- Missing key baselines: fine-tuning on hard examples, end-to-end recurrent BPTT with shared gates, gradient-based policy training (REINFORCE/BPTT), and cheaper gating variants (e.g., only higher layers).
- Inference cost is roughly T× (T=5) baseline runtime but runtime/accuracy trade-off and latency analysis are not provided.
- Ambiguity on augmentation: paper inconsistently states training with augmentation but claims SOTA for “unaugmented” data—needs clarification.
- Clarity is generally good with helpful algorithm box, but lacks details on architectures, hyperparameters, SNES settings, observation/action dimensionality, and full numerical results.
- Originality is moderate-to-high: novel combination of learned internal per-channel gating, feedback across passes, and SNES optimization in modern CNNs.
- Significance is moderate: method is general and shows gains, but scope limited to small datasets and usefulness is unclear without stronger analysis of causes and cost.
- Strengths: well-motivated internal attention architecture, consistent empirical gains on benchmarks, simple stable gating mechanism, and informative qualitative analyses.
- Weaknesses: incomplete baselines/ablations, insufficient reporting of variance and exact results, unquantified computational overhead, limited evaluation of observation design and layer contributions, and no justification for SNES over BPTT.
- Questions for authors: report exact error rates and stds, clarify augmentation setup and “unaugmented” claim, report attempts at gradient-based policy training, provide runtime overhead, and supply ablations (layer gating, obs components, T, policy size).
- Pros for acceptance: novel, timely idea with empirical improvements and broad applicability.
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete experimental analysis, unclear necessity of SNES, missing baselines, unquantified overhead, and reporting ambiguities.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept conditional on clarifying results and providing stronger baselines/ablations to meet conference standards.
