- Proposes dasNet: a CNN augmented with feedback connections that iteratively modulate convolutional filter activations for selective internal attention.
- Operates by a fast feedforward pass followed by several learned recurrent processing steps to refine classification on the same input.
- Builds on a Maxout network: train the base CNN first, then learn a control policy (state from internal activations, action = per-filter weights) to modulate filters.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to train the high-dimensional (1M+ params) policy instead of traditional RL due to scalability concerns.
- Demonstrates corrective behavior on ambiguous inputs and achieves state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 (also evaluated on CIFAR-100).
- Methodology is technically sound; two-stage training and Algorithm 1 are clearly described and justified.
- Provides analyses of system dynamics over extended time steps and an information-theoretic study of attention gates showing class-relevant learning.
- Paper is very clear, well-organized, and includes sufficient details (hyperparameters, figures) to support reproducibility.
- Highly original: novel combination of a modern CNN with an evolutionary-trained policy to control internal computations post-training.
- Significant: yields practical performance gains, challenges the purely feedforward paradigm, and opens avenues for dynamic instance-specific computation.
- Strengths: novelty, empirical performance, neuroscience-inspired motivation, clarity, and potential for future impact.
- Main weaknesses: high computational cost (policy training ≈ 4 days), limiting accessibility and adoption.
- Main weaknesses: reliance on SNES is a black-box approach that provides less insight than gradient-based methods.
- Main weaknesses: limited analysis of the general strategies learned by the policy beyond illustrative examples.
- Overall conclusion: outstanding and significant contribution with a strong recommendation for acceptance despite the noted computational and analysis limitations.
