- Introduces dasNet: CNN with feedback connections trained via SNES to iteratively modulate filter sensitivities for refined classification.
- Biologically motivated design mimics iterative, attention-like processing in human visual cortex.
- Builds on strong prior work (CNNs, Maxout) and situates contribution within recurrent connections and RL-for-attention literature.
- Uses Maxout base network and SNES to handle the high-dimensional control problem, making RL feasible for large networks.
- Demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, achieving state-of-the-art on unaugmented data.
- Visualization (Figure 3) and dynamics study (Figure 2) provide some insight into activation changes and stability.
- Novelty: feedback + RL for dynamic filter modulation is a unique approach to CNN design.
- Scalability: SNES addresses optimization challenges for many parameters, enabling practical training.
- Complexity: architecture and SNES training significantly increase model complexity and computational cost (4 days on GTX 560 Ti).
- Limited ablation study: unclear contribution of individual components (feedback, SNES, Maxout) to overall gains.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: fixed hyperparameters reported without justification or selection procedure.
- Qualitative analysis is shallow: filter activation visualizations lack deeper interpretation of emphasized/suppressed features.
- SNES explanation is insufficient for unfamiliar readers; supplementary material is not adequate.
- Reproducibility concerns: detailed hyperparameters and code are not provided.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with significant empirical improvements and practical RL solution.
- Arguments against acceptance: added complexity/cost, weak ablation and qualitative analysis undermine claims about component contributions.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution warranting acceptance but requiring further investigation and clarification.
- Recommendation (accept with minor revisions): add ablation studies, justify hyperparameters, expand qualitative interpretation, provide SNES implementation details, and release code/hyperparameters.
