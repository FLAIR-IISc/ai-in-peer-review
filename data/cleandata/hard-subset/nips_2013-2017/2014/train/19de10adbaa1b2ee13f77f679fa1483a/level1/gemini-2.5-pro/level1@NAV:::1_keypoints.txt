- Introduces dasNet: augments feedforward CNNs with feedback to iteratively modulate convolutional filters for selective internal attention.  
- Grounded in neuroscience: positions approach as biologically plausible recurrent visual processing.  
- Novelty: first to apply RL to learn a high-dimensional policy that gates internal filters of a state-of-the-art classifier.  
- Uses SNES to optimize a million-dimensional policy; choice is well-justified for scalability.  
- Strong empirical results: establishes new state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and situates itself within deep learning and computational neuroscience.  
- State representation concerns: the policy state vector (averaged map activations, etc.) appears ad-hoc and needs stronger justification or ablation.  
- Reproducibility and cost: RL training is computationally expensive and complex, potentially hindering replication and adoption.  
- Analysis limitations: qualitative attention visualizations are insightful but preliminary and lack systematic validation across classes/errors.  
- Practical significance unclear: performance gains may not justify the added complexity and computational overhead for many applications.
