**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a novel deep neural network architecture called Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet) that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The architecture is designed to mimic the human visual cortex, which uses feedback connections to modulate the activity of feature detectors. The dasNet architecture uses a reinforcement learning approach to learn a control policy that selectively focuses the attention of the network on the most discriminative features in an image. The policy is learned through a variant of Natural Evolution Strategies (NES) called Separable NES (SNES), which is more efficient and scalable than standard NES.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and innovative approach to deep neural networks that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections.
* The use of reinforcement learning to learn a control policy that selectively focuses the attention of the network is a significant contribution to the field.
* The experimental results show that dasNet outperforms the previous state-of-the-art model on unaugmented datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper is quite long and dense, making it challenging to follow at times.
* The explanation of the SNES algorithm and its application to the dasNet architecture could be clearer and more concise.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to two datasets (CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100), and it would be beneficial to see the results on more datasets to further demonstrate the generalizability of the approach.
