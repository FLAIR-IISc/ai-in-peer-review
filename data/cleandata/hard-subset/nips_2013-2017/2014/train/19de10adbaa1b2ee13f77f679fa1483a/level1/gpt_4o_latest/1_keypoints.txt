- Introduces dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections that use reinforcement learning (SNES) to iteratively adjust filter sensitivities during evaluation.  
- Novelty: Combines reinforcement learning and feedback/attention in CNNs, offering a biologically inspired selective-attention mechanism.  
- Technical rigor: Methodology is well-founded, uses SNES to handle high-dimensional policy learning, and includes reproducible algorithmic details.  
- Significance: Improves classification accuracy on difficult cases and outperforms state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments restricted to CIFAR-10/100; lacks testing on larger/diverse datasets like ImageNet.  
- Computational cost: Training with SNES and multiple image passes is computationally intensive, potentially limiting practicality.  
- Interpretability: Lacks sufficient visualization or qualitative analysis of learned attentional policies and high-level feature changes.  
- Comparison to related work: Related work coverage is comprehensive but needs clearer performance contrasts with other feedback/attention models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach with compelling experimental results likely to spur further research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited evaluation scope, high computational demands, and unresolved interpretability issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with encouragement to expand evaluation and improve interpretability in future work.
