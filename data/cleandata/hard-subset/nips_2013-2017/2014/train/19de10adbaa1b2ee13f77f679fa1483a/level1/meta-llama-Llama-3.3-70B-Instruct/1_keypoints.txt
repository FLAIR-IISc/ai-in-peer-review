- Proposes DasNet, a novel architecture combining CNNs with sequential processing and reinforcement learning to enable post-training adaptation.
- Introduces a non-stationary CNN with feedback that modulates convolutional filter activity to iteratively focus internal attention and correct initial classifications.
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to evolve the attention policy, with a detailed explanation of the algorithm.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, reporting a ~6% relative improvement over a vanilla CNN.
- Provides visualizations of filter emphasis/de-emphasis that offer insight into the network’s decision-making.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the architecture and training procedure.
- Major weakness: training is computationally expensive (4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).
- Major weakness: fixed number of steps (T=5) chosen experimentally, which may not generalize or be optimal across datasets.
- Major weakness: insufficient analysis of the dasNet policy’s information content, relying only on simple nearest-neighbor and logistic regression tests.
- Pro acceptance: novel and effective approach, innovative use of SNES, and strong experimental results on challenging datasets.
- Con acceptance: high computational cost may limit scalability and lack of deeper policy analysis weakens interpretability.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but authors should address computational demands and provide a more detailed analysis of the policy in future work.
