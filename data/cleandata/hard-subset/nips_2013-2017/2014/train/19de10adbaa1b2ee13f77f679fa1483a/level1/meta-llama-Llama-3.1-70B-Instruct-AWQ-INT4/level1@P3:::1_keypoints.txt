- Proposes dasNet: a deep network with feedback-based internal selective attention that dynamically alters convolutional filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Learns an attention-controlling policy with reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) applied to a pre-trained Maxout network.  
- Builds on existing feedback/recurrent and attention work, but the policy-learning control over attention is a novel contribution.  
- Novel architecture: dasNet introduces an innovative mechanism for internal selective attention in deep networks.  
- Effective policy learning: SNES is shown to successfully learn policies that improve network performance.  
- Empirical results: dasNet achieves state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Computational cost: training dasNet is resource- and time-intensive, limiting practicality.  
- Limited analysis: the paper provides insufficient insight into the learned policy and how it affects network behavior.  
- Limited comparisons: lacks evaluation against other attention mechanisms and reinforcement learning approaches.  
- Technical quality: method and descriptions are sound, but experimental analysis is not thorough enough.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear figures and tables that support the presentation.  
- Originality: combining dasNet architecture with SNES-based policy learning is an original contribution.  
- Significance: has potential to advance image classification and attention/RL methods in deep learning.  
- Pro-acceptance: innovative architecture and effective policy learning that advance the field and improve results.  
- Con-acceptance: high computational cost and inadequate analysis/comparisons weaken practical impact.  
- Recommendation — analysis: provide deeper analysis of the learned policy and its effect on performance.  
- Recommendation — baselines: compare dasNet to other attention mechanisms and RL-based control methods.  
- Recommendation — efficiency: explore ways to reduce training cost (more efficient optimizers, parallelization, etc.).
