**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections" proposes a novel deep neural network architecture called Deep Attention Selective Network (dasNet). DasNet incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections, allowing the network to dynamically alter its convolutional filter sensitivities during classification. This is achieved by training a policy using reinforcement learning to focus the attention of the network on the most discriminative features in the image. The policy is learned using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) and is applied to a pre-trained Maxout network.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The idea of incorporating feedback connections in deep neural networks is not new, but the authors' approach to learning a policy to control the attention of the network is novel. The use of SNES for policy learning is also a significant contribution. The authors' work is related to previous research on recurrent neural networks, attention mechanisms, and reinforcement learning.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel architecture**: DasNet's architecture is innovative and has the potential to improve the performance of deep neural networks on image classification tasks.
2. **Effective policy learning**: The authors' use of SNES for policy learning is effective in learning a policy that improves the performance of the network.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: DasNet achieves state-of-the-art results on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational cost**: Training dasNet is computationally expensive, requiring significant resources and time.
2. **Limited analysis**: The authors' analysis of the learned policy and its effects on the network's performance is limited.
3. **Lack of comparison to other methods**: The authors do not compare dasNet to other attention mechanisms or reinforcement learning methods.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear description of their architecture and methodology. However, the analysis of the results could be more thorough, and the authors could provide more insights into the learned policy and its effects on the network's performance.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of their work. The figures and tables are also well-organized and help to illustrate the authors' points.

**Originality**

DasNet's architecture and the use of SNES for policy learning are novel contributions to the field of deep learning.

**Significance**

DasNet has the potential to improve the performance of deep neural networks on image classification tasks, and the authors' work contributes to the development of more effective attention mechanisms and reinforcement learning methods.
