This paper introduces Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a novel architecture that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The authors propose a non-stationary convolutional neural network (CNN) that can adapt its behavior post-training, and a process that decides how to adapt the CNN's behavior through reinforcement learning. The dasNet architecture is based on the Maxout network, which is augmented to allow the filters to be weighted differently on different passes over the same image. The weights of these special connections implement a control policy that is learned through reinforcement learning after the CNN has been trained in the usual way via supervised learning.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the dasNet architecture and its components. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the reinforcement learning process used to train the policy, and the experiments conducted to evaluate the performance of dasNet.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a novel architecture that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of dasNet on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets, achieving a relative improvement of 6% with respect to the vanilla CNN. The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the dynamics of dasNet, including the effect of the number of steps on the performance of the network.
