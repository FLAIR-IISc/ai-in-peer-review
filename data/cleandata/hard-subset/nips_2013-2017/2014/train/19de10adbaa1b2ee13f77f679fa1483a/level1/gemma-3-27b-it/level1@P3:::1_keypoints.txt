- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections via SNES to iteratively modulate filter activations for improved classification.
- Demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 versus a Maxout baseline, especially on difficult examples.
- Well-motivated biologically, relating feedback to human visual processing and prior recurrent/feedback work.
- Thorough related work coverage and sensible choice of SNES to handle large policy parameter space.
- Novelty: learning to modulate filter sensitivities across layers via reinforcement learning is unique.
- Technical soundness: clear methodology, explanation of Maxout, RL framework, and SNES; experiments are well-defined.
- Useful analyses: filter activation visualizations and dynamics plots provide insight into dasNet behavior.
- Major scalability concern: SNES training is computationally expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and unclear how this scales to larger datasets/architectures.
- Limited interpretability: visualizations are helpful but do not fully explain why specific filters are up-/down-weighted.
- Potentially redundant action space: per-filter weight outputs may be high-dimensional; structured or grouped actions might be more efficient.
- Insufficient ablation studies: missing analyses on effects of augmentation, number of iterations (T), and other design choices.
- SNES hyperparameter sensitivity is not discussed; choice and robustness of SNES settings are unclear.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, SOTA results, and informative analyses that advance attention/biologically inspired work.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational cost/scalability, limited ablations, and restricted interpretability of learned policy.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on scalability discussion, more ablations, deeper policy interpretability, and SNES sensitivity reporting.
