- Introduces dasNet, a novel CNN architecture adding internal selective attention via feedback connections.  
- Proposes a non-stationary CNN that can adapt its behavior post-training using a learned control policy.  
- Builds on Maxout networks, allowing filter weights to be modulated across multiple passes over the same image.  
- Learns the control policy via reinforcement learning after standard supervised training of the CNN.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the architecture and training procedures thoroughly.  
- Provides a detailed explanation of the reinforcement learning method used to train the policy.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show a relative improvement of ~6% over the vanilla CNN.  
- Offers a thorough analysis of dasNet dynamics, including how performance varies with the number of steps.  
- Strength: novel architecture integrating feedback-based selective attention.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments that yield insights into network behavior.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art attention-based architectures.  
- Weakness: limited interpretability analysis — role of feedback and the learned policy is not deeply explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity, parameter counts, and training/testing cost.  
- Significance: promising results and potential applicability to other vision tasks (detection, segmentation).  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing weaknesses—add SOTA comparisons, interpretability insights, and complexity analysis.
