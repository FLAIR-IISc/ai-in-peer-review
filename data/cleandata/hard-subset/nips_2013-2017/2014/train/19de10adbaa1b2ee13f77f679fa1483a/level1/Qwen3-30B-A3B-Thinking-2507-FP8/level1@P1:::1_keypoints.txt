- Contribution: Introduces dasNet, a CNN extension with feedback connections that iteratively modulate filter sensitivities via a learned policy for internal selective attention.  
- Learning method: Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to learn a high-dimensional attention policy over multiple passes.  
- Biological motivation: Inspired by top-down feedback in biological vision to correct misclassifications by emphasizing or suppressing filters.  
- Empirical results: Shows clear improvements on CIFAR-10/100 (≈6% relative) and compelling ablations on ambiguous examples (e.g., cat vs. dog).  
- Reproducibility: Architecture and training procedure are described in sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Theoretical gap: Lacks a strong theoretical explanation for why and when the attention modulation mechanism works.  
- Relation to prior work: Does not sufficiently differentiate or compare to existing attention methods (e.g., Bahdanau attention, self-attention).  
- Computational cost: Training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and not justified by the modest improvement.  
- Scalability concerns: Unclear whether the approach scales to larger datasets or more complex tasks.  
- Use-case limitation: Trained on top of an already-trained CNN to avoid catastrophic forgetting, limiting scope and analysis of benefits.  
- Writing and clarity: Generally well-written and organized; algorithm and figures are clear, though the biological-to-technical mapping could be clarified.  
- Originality: SNES for attention policy is novel, but feedback-based selective attention is incremental relative to prior work.  
- Significance: Improvement is meaningful but not transformative; practical impact is limited by cost and scope.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel feedback incorporation, strong empirical bench results, biologically plausible motivation, and a practical SNES solution.  
- Cons for acceptance: Modest gains vs. cost, weak theoretical grounding, insufficient differentiation from existing attention mechanisms, and limited scalability discussion.  
- Recommendation: Technical soundness but incremental contribution; reviewer recommends rejection absent stronger theory, broader comparisons, or more substantial improvements.
