- Introduces dasNet: a novel deep network with internal selective attention via feedback connections inspired by the visual cortex.  
- Employs Maxout networks as the base model and trains the attentional policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the dasNet architecture and components.  
- Iterative attention mechanism lets the network focus on discriminative features, improving classification performance.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 and provides qualitative examples of corrected misclassifications.  
- Novel contribution: departs from traditional feedforward CNNs and uniquely applies reinforcement learning to attention.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, significance, and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Major weakness: high computational cost for training (e.g., ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).  
- Lacks a more detailed analysis and direct comparisons of the attentional policy with other attention mechanisms.  
- Builds on existing methods (Maxout, SNES), so the novelty is significant but still grounded in prior work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to address computational cost and deepen policy analysis.
