- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections for internal selective attention that iteratively refines classification by modulating filter sensitivities.  
- Learns attentional policy with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle a >1M-parameter policy space instead of standard RL.  
- Builds on Maxout networks and draws inspiration from biological recurrent/top-down visual processing.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10/100 show a ~6% relative gain on CIFAR-10 and corrected ambiguous misclassifications, claiming new state-of-the-art for unaugmented data.  
- Methodology is technically sound; attention gates and SNES-based policy learning are clearly described.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees explaining why the approach should outperform alternatives.  
- Experimental scope is limited—only two datasets and no comparisons to other attention mechanisms (e.g., spatial/channel attention, SE, CBAM).  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized with useful visualizations of attention dynamics.  
- Insufficient discussion of computational cost and training time relative to base CNNs and other attention methods.  
- Novel in applying learned feedback via RL to deep CNNs, but does not adequately distinguish from related or similarly named prior work.  
- Significant in demonstrating the value of recurrent processing for correcting errors, but unclear how it scales to larger datasets or modern architectures (batch norm, ResNets).  
- Strengths: novel attention mechanism, empirical improvements, focused evaluation on ambiguous cases, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: missing theory, limited comparisons, poor discussion of computational scalability, no integration plan with contemporary DL techniques.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address theoretical justification, related-work comparisons, and computational/scalability analysis.
