- Introduces dasNet: a novel CNN variant that adds internal selective attention via feedback connections.  
- Argues standard CNNs are limited by stationary, feedforward processing and can benefit from feedback.  
- Implements dasNet by augmenting a Maxout network with feedback that modulates convolutional filters.  
- Learns the feedback control policy with reinforcement learning after supervised CNN training.  
- Provides a thorough description of architecture, RL framework, and training algorithm.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show dasNet outperforms previous state-of-the-art on unaugmented data.  
- Strength: genuinely novel architecture that departs from traditional CNN designs.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation, including comparisons and dynamics analysis.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation.  
- Weakness: high computational cost (training required ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).  
- Weakness: limited in-depth analysis of the attention mechanism and its effect on performance.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparisons to other attention mechanisms.  
- Originality: novel use of reinforcement learning to train an internal attention/control policy.  
- Significance: achieves state-of-the-art results and could impact multiple computer-vision tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept—novel approach, strong results, and thorough evaluation despite some limitations.
