- Proposes dasNet, a deep network with feedback-based internal selective attention, trained via reinforcement learning to modulate feature detectors.  
- Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to efficiently learn the attention control policy.  
- Strength: novel architecture combining feedback attention with RL for selective feature focus.  
- Strength: empirical improvement over prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Weakness: paper is long and dense, making parts hard to follow.  
- Weakness: explanation of SNES and its application to dasNet lacks clarity and conciseness.  
- Weakness: experimental validation limited to only two datasets, reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Quality: technically sound and well written overall, with clear descriptions of main ideas.  
- Originality: significant and innovative use of feedback connections and RL in deep nets.  
- Significance: potential to improve DNN performance broadly, though broader evaluation is needed.  
- Pro acceptance: novelty, RL-based attention mechanism, and strong empirical results.  
- Con acceptance: clarity issues, dense presentation, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall: meaningful contribution to computer vision that would benefit from clearer exposition and wider empirical validation.
