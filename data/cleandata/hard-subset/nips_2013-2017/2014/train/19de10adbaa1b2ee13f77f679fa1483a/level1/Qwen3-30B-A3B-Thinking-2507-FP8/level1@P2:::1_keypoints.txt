- Proposes dasNet: augmenting feedforward CNNs with feedback connections to iteratively adjust internal attention during classification.  
- Uses reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to learn modulation of filter sensitivities across multiple passes.  
- Demonstrates improved classification on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, achieving state-of-the-art for unaugmented data.  
- Connects approach to biological feedback in visual cortex and prior attention/vision work.  
- Technically sound architecture with clear description of how feedback modifies filter activations.  
- SNES is a reasonable choice to handle the large policy parameter space.  
- Experimental setup is appropriate and implementation details (e.g., T=5, λcorrect=0.005) allow reproducibility.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why the feedback mechanism improves performance beyond empirical evidence.  
- Claim that the network “corrects itself” is supported qualitatively but not rigorously analyzed.  
- Description of the reinforcement learning observation vector is insufficiently detailed or motivated.  
- Novelty: introducing feedback to dynamically adjust filters is a meaningful departure from standard feedforward CNNs.  
- Insufficient comparison or positioning relative to other recent attention mechanisms (channel/spatial attention).  
- Significant empirical gain (6% relative on CIFAR-10), showing effectiveness on challenging benchmarks.  
- Computational cost is substantial (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) and scalability to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is not addressed.  
- Shows that boosting misclassified samples can focus attention effectively on difficult cases.  
- Pro-accept: biologically motivated novelty, strong benchmark gains without augmentation, quantitative and qualitative analyses, well-structured and motivated.  
- Con-accept: limited theoretical grounding, improvement magnitude may be modest in DL context, insufficient differentiation from other attention methods, no scalability analysis to larger architectures/datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the RL observation design and better contextualize contributions within attention literature.  
- Paper opens avenues for future work on recurrent/feedback processing in deep networks despite computational and theoretical gaps.
