- Proposes dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections that iteratively adjust filter sensitivities via a learned policy for internal selective attention.  
- Inspired by biological top-down feedback; builds on Maxout networks and uses SNES to learn the policy.  
- Enables multiple passes through the network to refine ambiguous classifications.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10/100 and reports a 6% relative improvement over the vanilla Maxout baseline.  

- Strength: Clear, well-structured technical approach with precise equations and a detailed training algorithm.  
- Strength: Provides ablation studies and qualitative analyses showing how attention evolves across iterations.  
- Strength: Justifies use of SNES to handle a very large policy parameter space (>1M).  
- Strength: Exceptionally well-written and organized; supplementary material aids reproducibility.  
- Strength: Demonstrates significance by improving state-of-the-art without extra training data and offers a practical way to enhance existing CNNs.  

- Weakness: Does not sufficiently differentiate from prior recurrent/feedback vision work and lacks thorough comparison to related literature.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for key design choices, including architecture and choice of SNES over other RL methods.  
- Weakness: Does not justify the chosen observation space or analyze computational complexity versus other attention mechanisms.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to relatively small datasets (CIFAR-10/100); scalability to larger datasets like ImageNet is unaddressed.  
- Weakness: Training is computationally expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) with no discussion of cost-benefit tradeoffs.  

- Overall: Technically sound, clearly presented, and yields meaningful benchmark improvements; meets conference quality standards.  
- For acceptance: 6% improvement, novel practical extension, solid math and analysis, detailed attention dynamics.  
- For rejection: Limited prior-work comparison, weak theoretical rationale, small-scale evaluation, and substantial computational cost.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limited comparison to prior feedback/recurrence literature.
