- Paper introduces dasNet: a CNN with learned feedback connections via SNES to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities during classification.
- Motivated by human visual processing using multiple feedforward passes for ambiguous cases.
- Demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 compared to a baseline Maxout network.
- Well-structured paper with clear motivation, detailed architecture description, and helpful Algorithm 1.
- Appropriately cites related work in CNNs, reinforcement learning, and visual attention.
- Using Maxout as a base network provides a solid foundation for experiments.
- Novelty: combining feedback within CNNs learned via reinforcement learning (SNES) is a significant contribution.
- Performance: outperforms prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR datasets, showing effectiveness.
- Clear explanation: architecture and training process are explained clearly and accessibly.
- Justification: parallels to human vision provide a plausible rationale for the approach.
- Thorough experimentation: dynamic study shows stable performance over iterations and gate analysis indicates useful information.
- Weakness — computational cost: training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) with little discussion of mitigation strategies.
- Weakness — limited feedback analysis: visualizations are superficial; deeper analysis of what feedback learns is needed.
- Weakness — SNES details: hyperparameter choices for SNES are not thoroughly discussed, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness — limited comparisons: evaluation only against Maxout; unclear performance relative to ResNets/DenseNets.
- Weakness — generalizability: experiments limited to CIFAR-10/100; no results on larger datasets like ImageNet.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with clear performance gains and practical training strategy for high-dimensional RL.
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited analysis of feedback, lack of modern baselines, and limited dataset scope.
- Overall assessment: solid and potentially significant contribution; weaknesses are addressable and do not outweigh strengths.
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to improve feedback analysis and discuss computational costs.
