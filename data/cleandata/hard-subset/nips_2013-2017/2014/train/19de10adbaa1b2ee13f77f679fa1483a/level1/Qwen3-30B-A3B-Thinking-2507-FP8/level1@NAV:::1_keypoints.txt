- Introduces dasNet: a CNN architecture with feedback connections enabling internal selective attention via sequential processing.  
- Allows iterative adjustment of filter sensitivities during classification to improve handling of ambiguous cases.  
- Builds on Maxout networks as the base architecture and trains feedback with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to manage large parameter spaces.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, achieving a ~6% relative improvement and new state-of-the-art for unaugmented datasets.  
- Positions work within prior literature (Goodfellow et al. Maxout, Behnke’s Neural Abstraction Pyramid) and distinguishes from recurrent approaches by focusing on post-training internal attention.  
- Strength: strong technical execution with thorough experimental validation and insightful visualizations of attention dynamics.  
- Strength: SNES implementation is well-justified for the high-dimensional feedback parameter space.  
- Strength: ablation studies thoughtfully address potential limitations.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational efficiency and practicality (training took ~4 days on a GTX 560 Ti).  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of generalization and scalability to larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Weakness: underdeveloped connection and comparison to recent transformer-style attention mechanisms.  
- Clarity: well-written with excellent visualizations, but some SNES technical details need clearer exposition.  
- Overall: novel and significant contribution with strong empirical results; reviewer recommends acceptance.
