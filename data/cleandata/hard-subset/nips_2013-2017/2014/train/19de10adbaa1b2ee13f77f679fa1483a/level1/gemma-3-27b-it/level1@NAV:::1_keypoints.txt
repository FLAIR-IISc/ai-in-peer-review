- Proposes dasNet, a CNN with feedback connections that iteratively modulates filter sensitivities for internal selective attention.  
- Uses reinforcement learning (Separable Natural Evolution Strategies) to learn a policy that adjusts internal filter activations.  
- Motivated by biological visual processing and iterative attention mechanisms.  
- Demonstrates improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, claiming state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets.  
- Distinguishes from prior work by applying RL to internal filter modulation rather than external input attention.  
- Visualization (Figure 3) and dynamics study (Figure 2) offer insight into how filter activations change over iterations.  
- Novelty is a major strength, introducing a fresh architecture and learning approach.  
- Paper is well-written and situates itself within relevant literature on CNNs and biological feedback.  
- System complexity and substantial computational overhead (4-day training) are significant weaknesses.  
- Lacks sufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (iterations, SNES hyperparameters, etc.).  
- Limited generalizability: experiments confined to small datasets (CIFAR-10/100), no evaluation on larger benchmarks like ImageNet.  
- Observation space for the RL policy is limited (average activations and classifier outputs), potentially restricting policy effectiveness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: genuine novelty, promising empirical results, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited dataset scope, and insufficient analysis; recommends minor revisions to address these.
