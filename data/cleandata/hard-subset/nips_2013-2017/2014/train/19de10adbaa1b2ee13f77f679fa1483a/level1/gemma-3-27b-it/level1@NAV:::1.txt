## Review of “Deep Networks with Internal Selective Attention through Feedback Connections”

This paper introduces Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a novel CNN architecture incorporating feedback connections to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities during classification. The core idea is to enable a form of internal attention, allowing the network to iteratively refine its understanding of an image by modulating filter activations through a reinforcement learning (RL) policy learned via Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES). This is motivated by observations of human visual processing, which often involves multiple passes and attentional mechanisms. The authors demonstrate improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 compared to previous state-of-the-art models on unaugmented datasets.

This work builds on a significant body of research in deep learning, particularly CNNs [1, 7, 8] and attention mechanisms [40, 41].  It differentiates itself by applying RL to *internal* filter modulation within a standard CNN, rather than external attention on input features. The use of SNES to handle the high-dimensional policy space is a pragmatic approach, given the challenges of standard RL algorithms [23, 24].  The paper effectively situates itself within the context of biologically-inspired computation, referencing work on visual cortex feedback connections [28, 29, 30] and iterative processing [31, 36].

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of learning internal attention via feedback connections, coupled with the use of SNES, is a fresh perspective in CNN architecture design.
* **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100, even modestly, demonstrates the potential of the proposed method.
* **Motivation:** The biological inspiration is well-articulated and provides a compelling rationale for the work.
* **Analysis:** The visualization of filter activation changes (Figure 3) and the dynamics study (Figure 2) provide insights into the network's behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The overall system is complex, adding significant computational overhead. The 4-day training time is substantial.
* **Ablation Studies:** While the dynamics study is helpful, more detailed ablation studies are needed to understand the contribution of each component (e.g., number of iterations, SNES parameters).
* **Generalizability:** The experiments are limited to CIFAR-10/100. Evaluating dasNet on more complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet) would strengthen the findings.
* **Limited Observation Space:** The observation space used for the RL policy (average activations and classification layer outputs) may be limited, hindering the policy's ability to fully understand the network’s state.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The work introduces a genuinely novel approach to CNN architecture design.
* The experimental results are promising, demonstrating an improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology and results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The computational cost is high, potentially limiting practical application.
* The scope is limited to relatively small datasets.
* Further analysis and ablation studies are needed to fully understand the benefits and limitations of the approach.

**Overall:** I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field. While there are areas for improvement, the novelty of the approach and the promising results justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above regarding ablation studies, generalizability, and discussion of computational cost.
