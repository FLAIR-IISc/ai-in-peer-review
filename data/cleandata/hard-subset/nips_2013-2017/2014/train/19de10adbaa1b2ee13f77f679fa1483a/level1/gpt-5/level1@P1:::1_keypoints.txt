- Proposal: dasNet adds learned internal feedback gating to a trained Maxout CNN, modulating convolutional feature maps over T iterative passes via a policy learned with SNES.
- Test-time operation: each pass computes observations (per-map averages, pre-softmax activations, class probs) and a policy outputs positive multiplicative gates to reweight maps for the next pass.
- Training: policy search (SNES) optimizes a boosted negative log-likelihood on the final pass after standard supervised CNN training.
- Relation to prior work: motivated by biological feedback and related to recurrence/attention and Maxout CNNs, but performs internal channel-wise attention rather than spatial glimpses.
- Architectural strength: clear, composable multiplicative per-map gating driven by internal activations.
- Empirical strength: reports improvements on CIFAR-10/100 over a strong Maxout baseline with qualitative correction examples.
- Pragmatic design: decouples feature learning from policy learning, uses simple observations and positive gates to avoid destabilization.
- Scalability: SNES enables handling very high-dimensional policy parameters without BPTT and shows stable dynamics beyond training horizon.
- Biological grounding: well-motivated with appropriate citations for feedback and top-down modulation.
- Missing gradient comparison: does not attempt or compare to BPTT/gradient-based training for the recurrent/gating pathway; SNES justification is plausible but not fully demonstrated.
- Lacking baselines/ablations: no comparisons to single-pass image-conditioned gating, gating only top layer, linear logit reweighting, or a shallow SGD-trained recurrent layer.
- Misleading RL framing: policy is optimized via supervised loss without temporal credit assignment or discounting; should be acknowledged as black-box supervised optimization.
- Computational cost omitted: inference cost is T× higher (T=5 used) but no timing, throughput, energy, or latency measurements are provided.
- Reporting issues: absolute error rates, confidence intervals, and standardized protocol details (augmentation, ensembles) are missing or unclear.
- Observation limitation: using only per-map averages discards spatial information and may limit gains; no comparisons to richer observations are provided.
- Action constraint concerns: positive normalized gates (scaled softmax) enforce competition and preclude strong suppression or negative modulation; design choices lack analysis.
- Qualitative evaluation: method is sound and novel, but ES vs gradient choice is not empirically justified and experiments are limited to CIFAR without robustness tests.
- Clarity issues: main idea clear but some SNES/hyperparameter details deferred and the “unaugmented” claim is ambiguous given base CNN augmentation.
- Originality: novel combination of internal channel-wise attention learned via policy search, distinct from spatial attention/glimpse models.
- Significance: promising but practical impact tempered by added inference cost and absent comparisons to gradient-based recurrent training.
- Pros for acceptance: elegant biologically inspired mechanism, empirical improvements, timely attention/feedback theme, demonstrates scalable black-box policy search.
- Cons against acceptance: missing strong baselines/ablations, no gradient-based comparison, limited computational/sensitivity analysis, ambiguous SOTA claim.
- Minor requests: report absolute CIFAR-10/100 error rates with/without dasNet, provide inference-time costs for T=1..5, visualize gate trajectories and class-conditional distributions, fix typos and notation.
- Overall verdict: compelling and timely idea with encouraging results but needs stronger experimental comparisons and clearer reporting; reviewer leans weak accept.
