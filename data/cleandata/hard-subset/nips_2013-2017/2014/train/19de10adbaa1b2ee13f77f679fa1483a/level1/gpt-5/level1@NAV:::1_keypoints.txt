- Contribution: Proposes dasNet, a recurrent extension of Maxout CNNs that uses a learned top-down policy to multiplicatively gate feature maps during inference.  
- Training: Trains feedforward Maxout first, then optimizes a high-dimensional deterministic policy mapping internal summaries to gates using SNES.  
- Observation/action design: Policy inputs are averaged feature-map activations, classification pre-activations, and softmax outputs; outputs are gates over all feature maps.  
- Optimization scale: Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to handle ~10^6 policy parameters.  
- Empirical results: Iteratively refines predictions over T passes and reports improved accuracy on CIFAR-10/100, claiming state-of-the-art on unaugmented data.  
- Biological/related work: Positions method against feedforward CNNs and links to biological recurrent/top-down modulation and RL-driven attention literature.  
- Technical quality: Approach is well-motivated and technically sound, with explicit algorithm and boosting-style loss to emphasize hard cases.  
- Analysis: Experiments show consistent gains, stability beyond the trained horizon, and informative analysis of learned gates.  
- Missing ablations: No tests on which layers need gating, gate dimensionality choices, or comparison of SNES vs simpler optimizers or end-to-end backprop for the policy.  
- Missing sensitivity/overhead analysis: Compute/latency overhead from T passes and sensitivity to T, population size, and regularization λ are not quantified.  
- Statistical rigor: Lacks significance testing and variance reporting across runs.  
- Clarity issues: Generally clear but underspecified details include exact architecture sizes, SNES hyperparameters, validation protocol for selecting T, observation vector dimensionality, and memory footprint.  
- Originality: Novel combination of internal feature-wise attention learned via black-box policy search applied at scale, distinct from spatial-attention/saccade models.  
- Significance: Demonstrates feedback benefits for recognition and a practical recipe for high-dimensional control, but impact is moderate without stronger baselines and broader datasets.  
- Pros: Biologically inspired internal attention mechanism, clear performance gains and dynamics analysis, scalable treatment of million-parameter policies via SNES.  
- Cons: Limited baselines and ablations, unclear necessity of SNES vs gradient methods, unquantified deployment overhead, SOTA claim limited to unaugmented data, and missing significance/variance analyses.
