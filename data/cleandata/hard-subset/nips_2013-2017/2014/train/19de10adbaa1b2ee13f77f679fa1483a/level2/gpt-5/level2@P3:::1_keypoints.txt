- Summary: Proposes dasNet, adding recurrent top-down feedback via per-feature-map multiplicative gates learned with SNES to improve CNN predictions over multiple passes.  
- Summary: Trains a base Maxout CNN supervised, then evolves a high-dimensional policy conditioned on compact observations to modulate feature maps across T steps, showing gains on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to biological top-down/recurrent vision and to attention/feedback models, differing by internal channel-wise attention trained with large-scale black-box policy search.  
- Quality: Methodologically coherent with well-placed gating, reasonable observations, and magnitude-stabilizing action normalization; separates base CNN training from policy evolution.  
- Quality: Experiments show consistent improvements, stability beyond trained steps, and useful qualitative visualizations.  
- Quality: Missing important experimental details—absolute errors, variances, strong baseline comparisons (augmentation/ensembles), computational costs, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Quality: SNES is reasonable for million-parameter policies, but analysis of sample efficiency, convergence, and smaller/structured policies is lacking.  
- Clarity: High-level idea, algorithm loop, and notations are mostly clear, though minor typos and some architectural specifics (O/A dims, layer counts) are omitted.  
- Originality: Novel instantiation of internal feature-map selective attention with top-down feedback and large-scale NES optimization yielding empirical gains.  
- Significance: If robust, shows lightweight recurrent feedback can complement feedforward CNNs; practical impact depends on the accuracy–compute trade-off as T grows.  
- Strength: Biologically motivated, clear formulation of top-down feedback for CNNs.  
- Strength: Scalable policy optimization via SNES for large controllers.  
- Strength: Demonstrated improvements on CIFAR-10/100 with qualitative and stability analyses.  
- Strength: Simple, clean channel-wise gating mechanism easily integrated with existing CNNs.  
- Weakness: Incomplete experimental reporting (errors, variance, compute, validation protocol).  
- Weakness: Limited ablations—no breakdown of observation components, policy structure, or gate placements.  
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to unaugmented data and lacks comparisons to strong contemporary baselines.  
- Weakness: Policy appears linear in observation; representational adequacy and capacity concerns unaddressed.  
- Weakness: Risk of policy overfitting due to unclear validation/early-stopping during SNES.  
- Requested clarification: Provide exact test error rates with confidence intervals and details of the compared “previous state-of-the-art.”  
- Requested clarification: State whether data augmentation was used or evaluated and compare to augmented models/committees.  
- Requested clarification: Supply full O and A dimensions, policy architecture, and precise gate placements.  
- Requested clarification: Describe SNES validation/stopping criteria and measures to prevent overfitting.  
- Requested clarification: Quantify training/inference costs and show accuracy–compute trade-off vs T.  
- Suggestions (essential): Report precise performance with variability, compare to augmented/ensemble baselines, quantify computational overhead, and run ablations on T, policy capacity, observations, and gate placement.  
- Suggestions (optional): Explore structured/lower-rank policies, contrast SNES with gradient-based or hybrid policy learning, extend to spatial attention, and add systematic visualizations linking gates to class disambiguation.  
- Accept argument: Novel, well-motivated feedback attention integrated into CNNs with demonstrated benchmark improvements and scalable high-dimensional policy optimization.  
- Reject argument: Empirical evaluation lacks necessary details, ablations, and strong baseline comparisons, and practical compute costs are unquantified, limiting assessment of impact.
