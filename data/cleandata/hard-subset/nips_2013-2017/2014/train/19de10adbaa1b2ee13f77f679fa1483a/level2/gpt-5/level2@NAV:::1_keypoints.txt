- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with learned top-down multiplicative gating applied over multiple passes to modulate feature-map activations.  
- Policy for gating is learned post-supervised training using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle million-parameter policies.  
- Demonstrates improved accuracy on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 versus a Maxout baseline and reports SOTA for single models without augmentation/ensembles.  
- Claims feedback via a learned policy can correct initial misclassifications by reweighting feature maps across iterative passes.  
- Claims SNES can efficiently train very high-dimensional gating policies.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100.  
- Method is valid, clearly described at a high level, with explicit equations and training loop.  
- Empirical support includes accuracy gains, dynamics showing stability over T steps, and evidence that gate activations encode class information.  
- Lacks statistical significance reporting and has limited ablation studies (policy complexity, per-layer gating, effect of T, simpler baselines).  
- Compute and parameter overheads (inference cost for T passes) are not quantified.  
- Paper is well organized and readable but omits implementation details important for reproducibility (exact architectures/hyperparameters, SNES update pseudocode/code).  
- Combining global feature-map gating with black-box optimization in a modern CNN is novel relative to prior spatial-attention/RL approaches.  
- Framing as internal selective attention is biologically compelling and practically interesting.  
- If robust, approach offers a practical way to add iterative refinement to feedforward models, but validation is limited to small benchmarks.  
- Suggested comparisons: end-to-end differentiable recurrent CNNs (ConvRNN/GRU), simple gated feedback, and policy gradients/REINFORCE for the same policy class.  
- Suggested ablations: number of passes, policy size, per-layer vs per-map gating, and effect of boosting weights.  
- Suggested reporting: parameter counts, inference-time cost, accuracy vs latency trade-offs, tests with/without data augmentation, and comparisons to ensembles and contemporary baselines.  
- Suggested analyses: per-class confusion matrices and more systematic visualizations.  
- Pros: clear biologically motivated idea, simple integration with CNNs, demonstrated improvements, stable dynamics, and novel use of SNES.  
- Cons: limited ablations and statistical rigor, restricted benchmarks, unclear compute overhead, and missing comparisons to strong recurrent/differentiable alternatives.
