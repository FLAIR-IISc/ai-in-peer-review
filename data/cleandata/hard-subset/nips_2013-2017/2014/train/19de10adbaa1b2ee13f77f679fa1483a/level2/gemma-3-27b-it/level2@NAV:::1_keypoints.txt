- Proposes dasNet: CNNs with feedback connections that dynamically adjust filter sensitivities during classification.  
- Motivation grounded in neuroscience: models iterative, attention-driven processing like the human visual cortex.  
- Trains feedback policy with Scalable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) due to high-dimensional control space.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without data augmentation.  
- Visualization of filter activations shows some insight into iterative attention changes.  
- Demonstrates stability of the network across multiple iterative processing steps.  
- Related work section is extensive but under-engages with modern attention methods (self-attention, attention gates).  
- Practical computational costs are not adequately discussed; SNES training is expensive (4 days on GTX 560 Ti).  
- Explanation of what the network actually learns to attend to is vague; activation maps are insufficient.  
- Use of Maxout networks feels dated and may limit generalizability to newer architectures.  
- Lacks direct comparative analysis with existing attention mechanisms within deep learning.  
- Strengths: novelty of feedback-trained attention, strong empirical results, and clear neuroscientific motivation.  
- Weaknesses: limited interpretability of learned attention, scalability/computational concerns, and architectural choices.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add comparisons to modern attention mechanisms, discuss computational trade-offs, and provide deeper analysis of learned attention.
