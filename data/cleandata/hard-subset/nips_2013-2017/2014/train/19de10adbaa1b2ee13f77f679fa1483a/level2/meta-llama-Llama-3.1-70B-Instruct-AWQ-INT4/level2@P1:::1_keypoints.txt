- Summary: Proposes dasNet, a CNN with internal selective attention via feedback, trained with SNES reinforcement learning, achieving state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10/100 (unaugmented).
- Strength: Novel architecture that integrates internal selective attention into deep CNNs, departing from feedforward designs.
- Strength: Uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) for scalable reinforcement learning of the attentional policy.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented).
- Strength: Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.
- Weakness: High computational cost, which may limit applicability to larger datasets or tasks.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of the learned attentional policies and their effect on classification performance.
- Weakness: No empirical comparison to other existing attention mechanisms.
- Quality: Technically sound with clear explanations and thorough experiments, but would benefit from deeper analysis of attention behavior.
- Clarity: Clear, concise writing with a good balance of technical detail and high-level explanation.
- Originality: Novel integration of internal selective attention into CNNs and novel use of SNES for the attentional policy.
- Significance: Demonstrates potential to improve CNN performance on attention-requiring tasks, validated by SOTA CIFAR results.
- Arg for acceptance: Introduces a novel internal-attention CNN architecture.
- Arg for acceptance: Empirically strong SOTA performance on CIFAR benchmarks.
- Arg for acceptance: Well-presented and readable manuscript.
- Arg for rejection: Computational demands may hinder broader applicability.
- Arg for rejection: Insufficient analysis of the learned attention mechanisms.
- Arg for rejection: Lacks comparison to other attention methods, limiting contextual evaluation.
