- Introduces dasNet, an architecture with feedback connections for selective internal attention in deep networks.
- Main claim: iterative internal attention via feedback improves classification performance.
- Empirical support: outperforms prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of architecture and components.
- Uses reinforcement learning to learn feedback connections, a novel and interesting approach.
- Experiments are well-designed and comprehensive.
- Achieves a notable ~6% relative improvement over a vanilla CNN.
- Strengths: originality, clarity, significance, and thorough related-work analysis.
- Limitation: dasNet is complex and may be difficult to train and optimize.
- Limitation: policy evolution via Natural Evolution Strategies (NES) may be inefficient or suboptimal.
- Limitation: lacks deeper analysis and visualization of learned feedback connections and their behavioral impact.
- Overall recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance despite limitations.
