- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that dynamically adjusts filter sensitivities via a policy learned with SNES, improving CIFAR-10/100 performance on unaugmented data.  
- Well-motivated by prior work and human visual cortex literature, but needs clearer differentiation from related recurrent/attention models.  
- Novelty: learning a post-training policy to modulate filter activations represents a departure from static CNNs.  
- Practical solution: uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies to handle the high-dimensional policy search.  
- Empirical strength: reports state-of-the-art results on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Analysis: includes visualizations of filter activation changes and dynamics showing stable iterative processing.  
- Writing: paper is generally clear and easy to follow.  
- Limited evaluation: experiments are confined to CIFAR datasets, reducing generalizability to more complex datasets like ImageNet.  
- Policy interpretability: the learned policy is a black box and lacks analysis connecting filter adjustments to image features.  
- Computational cost: training (4 days on GTX 560 Ti) is high and may hinder adoption; trade-offs/optimizations not discussed.  
- SNES sensitivity: lacks thorough hyperparameter/sensitivity analysis for the SNES optimization.  
- Comparison to attention mechanisms: insufficient comparison to modern attention methods (e.g., Squeeze-and-Excitation).  
- Recommendation: overall promising and technically sound; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to analyze the policy, address computational trade-offs, and compare to existing attention mechanisms.
