- Proposes dasNet: feedback connections in CNNs to dynamically adjust filter sensitivities using reinforcement learning (SNES).  
- Motivation: models iterative human perception and attention allocation.  
- Claims improved performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.  
- Strength: novel integration of feedback-based attention and RL in CNNs.  
- Strength: compelling biological motivation and practical application of SNES to large parameter spaces.  
- Strength: reported performance gains and preliminary visualization of filter activation changes.  
- Weakness: baseline model for comparison is not clearly specified, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: unclear whether baseline used data augmentation, affecting fairness of comparisons.  
- Weakness: reports a 6% relative improvement but omits absolute improvement values.  
- Weakness: observation space (concatenated average activations and class probabilities) is ad-hoc and unjustified.  
- Weakness: hyperparameters (λcorrect, λmisclassified, λL2) chosen empirically without sensitivity analysis or ablation.  
- Weakness: visualization of filter changes is vague (metric unspecified) and lacks quantitative support.  
- Weakness: related work section omits recent attention mechanisms (e.g., SENet, CBAM) and lacks comparative discussion.  
- Weakness: insufficient connection between SNES-learned policy and biological neural mechanisms.  
- For acceptance: novelty, encouraging CIFAR results, and clever use of SNES argued in favor.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient baseline details, weak justification of design choices, and superficial analysis/visualization argued against.  
- Revision 1: provide full description of baseline architecture, training procedure, and data augmentation details.  
- Revision 2: report both relative and absolute performance improvements.  
- Revision 3: justify and analyze the observation space’s information content.  
- Revision 4: perform hyperparameter sensitivity analysis or ablation studies.  
- Revision 5: improve filter-change visualizations with clear metrics and examples of influential filters.  
- Revision 6: expand related work to include recent CNN attention modules and contrast approaches.  
- Revision 7: deepen discussion of biological plausibility and how the learned policy maps to neural mechanisms.
