- Contribution: Proposes DasNet, adding feedback connections to CNNs to enable internal selective attention by dynamically modulating convolutional filter sensitivities across multiple passes using a policy evolved with SNES.  
- Architecture detail: Builds on Maxout networks with attention gates (a^ℓ_j) that scale filter weights during iterative inference.  
- Optimization: Uses Separable NES to learn a very high-dimensional policy (>1M parameters) as a pragmatic way to apply RL-like control to CNNs.  
- Empirical results: Reports ~6% relative improvement over vanilla Maxout on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 and claims new SOTA for unaugmented Maxout networks.  
- Interpretability: Qualitative visualizations show the network corrects misclassifications by selectively enhancing or suppressing filters across layers.  
- Strength — motivation: Neuroscience-inspired top-down feedback motivation is compelling and well-justified.  
- Strength — method: SNES to handle high-dimensional policy space is a practical, well-motivated technical choice.  
- Strength — analysis: Ablations (varying T, gate-value classification) and visualizations provide strong evidence the attention mechanism is functional.  
- Weakness — novelty: The concept of iterative feedback/refinement isn’t novel; paper underemphasizes differences from prior iterative/recurrent attention works and lacks comparisons to them.  
- Weakness — methodological rigor: Loss weighting (λ_misclassified > λ_correct) is heuristic without ablation; SNES hyperchoices (p=50, diagonal Σ) lack justification and no comparison to other RL/optimization baselines.  
- Weakness — clarity: Algorithm 1 and notation are confusing (e.g., initialization vs. role of a^0), observation h(M_t) is vaguely defined, and the claim of a deterministic policy conflicts with using softmax.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly contrast DasNet with prior iterative methods (e.g., [48]) and clarify scalability claims.  
- Suggestion: Add ablation comparing T=1 (no feedback) vs T>1 to quantify iterative benefit.  
- Suggestion: Clarify policy/action design (if continuous, avoid softmax; consider linear+tanh) and justify SNES/parameter choices or compare alternatives.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost (training time, GPU) and compare to alternatives like larger models or ensembles.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound with convincing empirical and interpretability results, but novelty claims are somewhat overstated and methodological/clarity issues should be addressed.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to address comparisons, ablations, methodology justification, and exposition.
