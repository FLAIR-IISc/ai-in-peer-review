- Introduces dasNet: a CNN with feedback that performs additional processing steps to modulate convolutional filters via internal selective attention.  
- Core idea: after a feedforward pass, a learned control policy selectively emphasizes or suppresses filter activations to improve classification of ambiguous images.  
- Training is two-stage: first train a strong Maxout CNN, then add multiplicative gates and train a policy with reinforcement learning to minimize classification loss.  
- Policy search uses Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle the million-plus dimensional parameter space.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10/100 show improved accuracy over the baseline and achieved state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets at the time.  
- Analysis shows performance peaks at the trained number of steps T and gate activations contain class-relevant information, supporting the learned policy’s effectiveness.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-motivated by biological recurrent/attentional processing, and uses a plausible mechanism for filter modulation.  
- Weakness: analysis of what the policy actually learns is high-level and could be deeper (e.g., systematic patterns for commonly confused classes).  
- Clarity: well-written with clear figures and algorithm, but lacks key implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Missing reproducibility details include base Maxout architecture specifics (filters, kernels), dimensionality of observation and action vectors, and baseline vanilla Maxout performance.  
- Originality: highly original integration of internal attention via learned policy and combination of pre-trained deep nets with evolution-based policy search.  
- Significance: important departure from purely feedforward vision models, demonstrating benefits of sequential dynamic processing and likely inspiring follow-up work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical results, significant contribution to the field, and technically sound methodology.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient reproducibility details and limited depth in analysis of learned policies.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
