- Introduces dasNet, a CNN augmented with feedback connections that performs multiple sequential passes to iteratively modulate convolutional filter activations for refined classification.
- Frames internal selective attention as a reinforcement learning problem where a policy network enhances or suppresses filter activations based on the previous pass.
- Trains the policy with Scalable Natural Evolution Strategy (SNES) due to the high dimensionality of the parameter space.
- Originality/significance: proposes a novel, biologically motivated internal attentional mechanism that departs from purely feedforward models and could inspire adaptive architectures.
- Empirical quality: strong results claiming state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (unaugmented) and analysis showing gate activations contain class-relevant information.
- Clarity/justification weakness: insufficient comparison or detailed justification for choosing SNES over conventional policy-gradient methods.
- Evaluation weakness: qualitative analysis of filter changes is speculative and would benefit from quantitative metrics (e.g., consistent confidence improvement across T steps, especially for initially misclassified samples).
- Scalability weakness: high computational cost (four additional days of training) with unclear scalability and trade-offs for larger datasets like ImageNet.
- Overall: high-quality, well-executed and significant contribution that integrates feedback and RL into deep networks; weaknesses are avenues for future work rather than fatal flaws.
