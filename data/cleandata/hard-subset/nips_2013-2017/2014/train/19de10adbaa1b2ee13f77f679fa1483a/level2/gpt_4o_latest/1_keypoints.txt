- Paper: Proposes dasNet — adds feedback to CNNs and trains an internal attention policy with SNES to iteratively modulate filter sensitivities; shows SOTA on unaugmented CIFAR-10/100 and can correct misclassifications.

Strengths:
- Novelty: Introduces biologically inspired feedback into CNNs, a clear departure from static feedforward models.
- Technical soundness: SNES for high-dimensional policy learning is well-motivated and the experimental setup is robust.
- Significance: Demonstrates meaningful SOTA improvements on CIFAR and better handling of ambiguous cases.
- Clarity: Well-organized exposition with detailed architecture/training descriptions and helpful visualizations.
- Relevance: Aligns with efforts to make networks more adaptive and biologically plausible.

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation: Only tested on relatively small CIFAR datasets; lacks validation on larger/diverse datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
- Computational overhead: Iterative attention and SNES introduce significant cost and practicality concerns; efficiency trade-offs not discussed.
- Interpretability challenges: Hard to qualitatively analyze high-level filter changes; more policy/impact visualization needed.
- Missing comparisons: No direct comparison to other feedback/recurrent/attention-based computer vision methods.

Pro: Novel, biologically inspired method with SOTA results and clear methodology.
Con: Limited-scale evaluation and substantial computational cost may hinder practical adoption.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — address computational efficiency, expand evaluation to larger datasets, and compare to related feedback-based approaches.
