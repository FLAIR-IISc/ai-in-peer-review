This paper introduces Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet), a novel deep neural network architecture that incorporates internal selective attention through feedback connections. The authors propose a method to dynamically alter the convolutional filter sensitivities during classification, allowing the network to iteratively focus its internal attention on the most discriminative features in the image being processed.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the dasNet architecture and its components. The use of feedback connections and reinforcement learning to control the attention mechanism is a novel approach that sets dasNet apart from traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The authors also provide a thorough review of related work in the field, including the use of recurrent connections and reinforcement learning in computer vision.

The experimental evaluation of dasNet on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets shows promising results, with dasNet outperforming the previous state-of-the-art model on unaugmented datasets. The authors also provide a qualitative analysis of the effect of the alterations made by dasNet, showing that the network is able to correct image misclassifications produced by a fully trained feedforward Maxout network.
