- Proposes Deep Attention Selective Networks (dasNet): a non-stationary CNN with feedback-based internal selective attention that can adapt post-training.  
- Attentional policy is learned via reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear motivation explaining limitations of traditional CNNs.  
- Provides a detailed, technically sound description of dasNet’s architecture, feedback structure, and training algorithm.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show improved classification performance, particularly on difficult instances.  
- Presentation is clear; figures and tables are well-organized and explanations are concise.  
- High originality: novel use of internal selective attention through feedback and RL in CNNs.  
- High significance: potential to improve performance across computer vision tasks and advance understanding of attention in deep nets.  
- For acceptance: novelty, empirical effectiveness, clear exposition, and contribution to understanding attention mechanisms.  
- Against acceptance: relies on the assumption that SNES-based RL will learn effective policies in all scenarios.  
- Against acceptance: CIFAR-10/100 experiments may not generalize to other tasks or datasets.  
- Against acceptance: lacks detailed analysis of computational cost and scalability for large-scale applications.  
- Overall conclusion: paper is well-written, technically sound, original, and recommended for acceptance.
