- Introduces dasNet: a novel architecture adding feedback to pre-trained CNNs that iteratively modulates internal filter activations via a learned policy.  
- Policy is trained with Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) to handle the million-dimensional parameter space, after supervised training of the base CNN.  
- Motivation grounded in biological vision: a fast feedforward pass followed by slower top-down attentional processing.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100; achieves a new state-of-the-art on unaugmented CIFAR-10.  
- Technically sound: method is well-defined and experimentally supported.  
- Use of SNES is pragmatic and justified for the high-dimensional control problem.  
- Qualitative analyses and dynamics experiments strengthen the claim that the learned policy is meaningful and stable.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides helpful algorithmic and architectural descriptions.  
- Highly original: novel combination of RL-learned internal filter modulation inside a deep CNN.  
- High significance: demonstrates that iterative, guided computation improves performance and suggests a new research direction.  
- Suggestion: motivate and ablate the observation vector (why average activations, and importance of each component).  
- Suggestion: provide error vs. number of passes (T) to show trade-offs and saturation point.  
- Suggestion: discuss computational cost, training time, and scalability to larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, SOTA result, opens a significant research direction, and is technically sound.  
- Arguments against acceptance: substantial computational requirements and experiments limited to CIFAR datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong case for acceptance—novel, significant, and well-executed work.
