- Introduces dasNet, a novel CNN architecture with internal selective attention via feedback connections.  
- Dynamically alters convolutional filter sensitivities during classification to iteratively focus on discriminative features.  
- Uses reinforcement learning to control the attention mechanism, distinguishing it from traditional CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture and components.  
- Thorough related-work review covering recurrent connections and reinforcement learning in vision.  
- Experimental evaluation on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 is promising, outperforming prior state-of-the-art on unaugmented datasets.  
- Qualitative analysis shows dasNet can correct misclassifications made by a fully trained feedforward Maxout network.  
- Strength: novel architecture incorporating internal selective attention through feedback.  
- Strength: novel combination of feedback connections and reinforcement learning for attention control.  
- Strength: strong experimental results on benchmark datasets.  
- Strength: comprehensive review of related literature.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of dasNet architecture and component roles.  
- Weakness: insufficient reporting of training details (e.g., number of iterations, learning rate).  
- Weakness: qualitative analysis of attention effects is limited and could be more extensive.  
- Weakness: missing information on computational resources required for training/evaluation.  
- Originality: feedback+reinforcement learning attention mechanism and dasNet architecture are new contributions.  
- Significance: demonstrates potential to improve image classification and suggests broader applicability in computer vision.  
- Quality: overall well-written with thorough experimental work and qualitative examples.  
- Clarity: well-organized presentation with helpful figures and tables.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel architecture, strong CIFAR results, and solid related-work context.  
- Argument for rejection: insufficient architectural and training-detail analysis limits reproducibility and assessment.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper architectural analysis and component ablations.  
- Recommendation: report full training hyperparameters (iterations, learning rates) and optimization details.  
- Recommendation: expand qualitative analysis of attention effects across more examples.  
- Recommendation: include computational resource and training-time requirements.
