- Proposes dasNet: a CNN with feedback connections that iteratively modulates convolutional filters over multiple passes to refine classification.
- Builds on a pre-trained Maxout network and adds modulatory gates per feature map controlled by a learned policy.
- Policy maps a state vector (derived from network activations) to actions that scale filter activities for the next pass.
- Trains the policy with reinforcement learning using Separable Natural Evolution Strategies (SNES) as a black-box optimizer.
- Reward is designed to emphasize correcting misclassifications from the initial feedforward pass.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 (no data augmentation), outperforming the baseline Maxout network.
- Well-motivated by recurrent and attentional processing in biological vision and connects to prior computational work.
- Strong technical contribution with solid empirical results that support the claims.
- Novel framing: treating internal feature modulation as a sequential decision-making problem solved with RL.
- Original training paradigm: pre-train deep network supervised, then train an RL-based attention policy on top.
- Paper includes useful analysis of network dynamics and information content of learned gate activations.
- Major methodological question: choice of SNES needs stronger justification over conventional policy gradient methods or lower-dimensional policy architectures.
- The policy appears to be a single linear layer, which may be overly restrictive given the large parameter count.
- Observation vector construction (average map activations, intermediate activations, class probs) seems ad-hoc and requires a rationale.
- Key implementation details are missing for reproducibility, e.g., exact Maxout architecture specifications and dimensionalities of state/action/parameter spaces.
- Training is computationally expensive (RL phase ~4 days), which is a practical limitation and should be discussed with potential efficiency improvements.
- Scalability concern: experiments limited to small CIFAR images, unclear how method scales to larger datasets like ImageNet.
- Recommendation: lean towards acceptance due to novelty, performance, and significance, but concerns about cost, methodological justification, and scalability remain.
