- Proposes dasNet, a novel CNN architecture with feedback connections to model selective attention and iteratively focus filters for better classification.
- Demonstrates technical soundness with claims supported by experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.
- Provides clear explanations of dasNet architecture, feedback mechanisms, and the reinforcement learning policy training.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly structured.
- Novel use of feedback connections and reinforcement learning to model selective attention in deep CNNs.
- Shows state-of-the-art performance on unaugmented CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, with the policy able to correct misclassifications over time.
- Strength: Introduces a novel architecture that models selective attention in deep CNNs.
- Strength: Delivers improved performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets.
- Strength: Offers insights into the dynamics and behavior of the dasNet policy.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the dasNet policy’s behavior across different datasets.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited; needs more datasets and comparisons to other state-of-the-art models.
- Weakness: Lacks details on computational resources required for training and evaluation.
- Argument for acceptance: Novel and significant contribution with demonstrated performance gains and clear presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: Needs deeper analysis, broader experimentation, and more resource usage details.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, conditional on addressing the noted weaknesses in the final version.
