- Studies which locations in static and dynamic images attract human attention using a variational energy-based model.
- Model built from three principles: harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance term.
- Derives a cost function and differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Presents a novel, formal approach to saliency and bottom-up eye-movement modeling valuable for vision and cognitive science.
- Paper is poorly written and hard to follow; needs proofreading and clearer exposition.
- Authors should expand and define abbreviations and explicitly define every variable/function used (especially in Section 3 and equations).
- Recommend moving the two-page appendix to supplementary material and using freed pages to define variables/functions in the main text.
- Suggest connecting and comparing results to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussing potential integration.
