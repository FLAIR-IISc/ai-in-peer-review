- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model combined with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces novel "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies a Least Action Principle to drive scanpaths.  
- Affordances appear somewhat ad hoc and insufficiently motivated.  
- The Least Action Principle derivation should be connected to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab work).  
- Biological motivation is limited, which is less critical given benchmark-driven deep models but still noteworthy.  
- Model performance is mixed and generally worse compared to benchmark deep saliency models.  
- Paper lacks main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths (only reports overall statics); more dynamic/scanpath analyses are needed.  
- Overall enjoyable paper with interesting ideas but needs stronger motivation, biological grounding, and empirical demonstration of claimed dynamics.
