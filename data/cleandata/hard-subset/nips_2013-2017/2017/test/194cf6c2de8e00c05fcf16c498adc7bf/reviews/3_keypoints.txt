- Proposes a new approach (EYMOL) to modeling eye movements.
- Reviewer notes authors correctly summarize current state of the art (reviewer not an expert).
- Current models are saliency-map based with fixation selection via optimization/maximization.
- EYMOL differs by modeling gaze trajectories directly using a "Least Action Principle."
- Some mathematical details (especially appendix) were beyond the reviewer's physics background to evaluate.
- Despite math gaps, reviewer understood the key approach and how it differs from prior models.
- Model is evaluated on real eye-movement datasets and compared to several alternative models.
- Results are favorable overall but not dominant on every metric, supporting the model's validity.
- Paper likely of interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees).
- Reviewer is less convinced it fits NIPS significance but defers to more expert reviewers.
- Novel theory that could inspire further empirical research and has merit for perception sciences.
