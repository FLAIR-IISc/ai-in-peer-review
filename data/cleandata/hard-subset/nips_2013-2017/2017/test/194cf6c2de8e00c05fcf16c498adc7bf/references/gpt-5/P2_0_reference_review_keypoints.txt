- Proposal: EYMOL — a dynamical variational model that generates scanpaths by minimizing an action functional; saliency maps obtained from visit counts over multiple runs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on classic saliency, information/surprise models, scanpath simulators, and optical-flow constraints; novelty is unifying curiosity and fixation/tracking via a least-action principle.
- Technical soundness: Variational derivation is mostly coherent and detailed, but several modeling choices are heuristic and under-justified.
- Kinetic energy inconsistency: Equation mismatch (K(ẋ) = 1/m ẋ^2 vs. use of mẍ) needs correction/clarification.
- Curiosity term concerns: Uses bx^2 and px^2 with deterministic cos^2/sin^2 modulation; choice of ω, schedule, and anti-trapping rationale lack analysis or ablation.
- Brightness-invariance term concerns: Treated as a velocity-dependent potential; stability/damping consequences are not analyzed.
- Empirical support: Reports competitive metrics but omits top deep saliency baselines (e.g., DeepGaze II, SALICON, SAM/MLNet); no component ablations.
- Video evaluation weak: Uses small SFU set (12 clips); tracking claims are anecdotal with no scanpath/kinematic metrics.
- Evaluation design bias: Strong center bias injected via central Gaussian and central initial conditions; grid-tuned blur/center may inflate scores and confound dynamics.
- Reproducibility gaps: Missing/ scattered hyperparameters and implementation details (m, k, ω, integrator, time step, SA schedule/acceptance, number of runs).
- Clarity issues: Manuscript readable but has typos, incomplete references, inconsistent equation numbering, and unquantified claims like “real-time.”
- Originality: Novel formulation casting bottom-up attention and fixation/tracking in a unified variational framework; alternation between local and peripheral drives is interesting.
- Significance: Conceptually appealing as an interpretable dynamic model, but practical impact limited without comparisons to SOTA deep models and scanpath validation.
- Strengths: Unified interpretable dynamical formulation, explicit equations, saliency emergent from dynamics, competitive against several classic baselines, applies to images and videos.
- Weaknesses: Missing comparisons to top deep models; heavy reliance on tuned center bias and central initialization; lack of ablations and scanpath validation; questionable kinetic-energy term; small parameter-fitting set.
- Suggested experiments/analyses: Ablate center bias and initial conditions; disable/modify curiosity and brightness terms; test fixed vs time-modulated C; sensitivity analysis over ω, ηb, ηp, λ, m, integrator step; add stability analysis beyond λ bound.
- Suggested evaluations: Compare to strong deep baselines and report leaderboard ranks; add scanpath metrics (saccade amplitudes, fixation durations, string-edit distances) and video tracking metrics; quantify runtime for single scanpath and for full saliency generation.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, interpretable variational framework with clear math; saliency emerges from dynamics; competitive vs classic baselines.
- Rejection arguments: Empirical evaluation incomplete vs modern deep models; central-bias confounds; lack of ablations, scanpath-level validation, and reproducibility details.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject pending stronger comparisons, thorough ablations, clarified math/implementation, scanpath validation, and reproducibility improvements.
