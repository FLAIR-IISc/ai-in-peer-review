- Summary: Proposes a variational, physics-inspired ODE model for gaze combining boundedness, gradient-based “curiosity,” and brightness constancy, producing saliency from simulated scanpaths.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to energy/dynamical attention models and optical-flow regularization, but lacks adequate comparison to recent deep saliency methods.
- Strength: Conceptual elegance and unified, interpretable formulation for saccades, fixation, and tracking.
- Strength: Euler–Lagrange derivation is technically sound at a high level with attention to solvability conditions.
- Strength: Simple, efficient dynamics that yield saliency maps naturally and can run in real time.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on center bias (input weighting, center initialization, post-hoc tuning) likely inflates reported metrics.
- Weakness: Evaluation protocol issues — possible overfitting on SFU videos, no held-out split described, and missing tables for verification.
- Weakness: Comparisons omit top-performing 2016–2017 deep models, weakening claims of competitiveness.
- Weakness: Periodic cos^2/sin^2 alternation between local and peripheral terms is ad hoc and lacks justification or empirical support.
- Weakness: Omits key oculomotor constraints (saccade velocity profiles, refractory periods, IOR) reducing biological plausibility.
- Weakness: Lacks sensitivity analyses, ablations of the three terms, scanpath-level validation, and robustness checks to noise/contrast.
- Weakness: Important implementation details and hyperparameter values (ω, m, λ, ηb, ηp, integration steps, stopping criteria) are missing.
- Quality: Variational derivation mostly correct, but empirical section and metric reliability are weak due to center bias and evaluation gaps.
- Clarity: Readable overall but needs consistent equation numbering, complete references, parameter reporting, and grammatical fixes.
- Originality: Novel integration of known terms into an ODE/least-action framework; periodic alternation is new but ad hoc.
- Significance: Potentially valuable if rigorously validated, but current evaluation shortcomings limit impact.
- Questions for authors: Report ω and sensitivity, quantify performance without center-bias components, provide held-out SFU evaluation, give ablations, and compare scanpath statistics to humans.
- Suggestions: Remove/control center bias, add ablations and sensitivity analyses, include stronger baselines, justify or replace periodic weighting with adaptive/learned scheme, publish code and full parameter details.
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; resubmit after stronger, bias-controlled evaluations, ablations, and scanpath validation.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
