- Proposes a novel variational principle for visual attention based on boundedness, curiosity (image gradients), and brightness invariance, producing an action functional and Euler–Lagrange scanpath dynamics.  
- Frames saliency maps as a byproduct of explicit scanpath modeling rather than the central goal, contrasting with mainstream saliency-map approaches.  
- Demonstrates model performance on static (MIT300, CAT2000) and dynamic (SFU) datasets, showing competitive empirical results despite not being tailored for video saliency.  
- Positions the work within prior attention literature (Koch & Ullman, Itti et al., CNN saliency) and connects to control/least-action theoretical frameworks and cognitive-science debates (scene interpretation vs. attention).  
- Novelty: introduces a distinctive trajectory-based, variational formulation of attention and derives differential equations for scanpaths.  
- Theoretical foundation: conceptually clear, biologically plausible principles with detailed mathematical derivations (provided in appendix).  
- Unified modeling: attempts to capture both saccadic movements and object tracking within a single framework.  
- Empirical strength: competitive benchmark performance on both images and video, encouraging given model design.  
- Emphasis on scanpaths offers a refreshing alternative perspective to saliency-centric methods.  
- Parameter sensitivity concern: reliance on simulated annealing for estimation with insufficient exploration of parameter influence on behavior.  
- Brightness invariance may be too strong an assumption for complex scenes; limited discussion of failure cases and impacts.  
- Peripheral processing modeled with simple Gaussian blur, which may be an oversimplification needing further exploration.  
- Limited biological validation: lacks direct neurophysiological comparisons or biologically relevant metrics.  
- Appendix clarity: mathematical appendix is long and dense, potentially inaccessible without better formatting or explanations.  
- Lack of systematic ablation studies to quantify the individual contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper with high potential but areas needing improvement.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting deeper parameter-sensitivity analysis, systematic ablations, and a brief discussion of limitations and future work.
