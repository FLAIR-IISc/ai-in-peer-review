- Paper proposes a variational (least-action) dynamical systems model (EYMOL) for gaze/scanpath generation with kinetic energy and three potential-like terms producing saliency maps from trajectory visits.
- Euler–Lagrange derivation and handling of the velocity-dependent brightness term appear technically sound and correctly constrained.
- Curiosity term uses periodic alternation C(t,x)=|∇b|^2 cos^2(ωt)+|∇p|^2 sin^2(ωt) but ω and the alternation lack theoretical justification or analysis.
- Boundary potential uses indicator functions causing non-smooth forces at edges, with no discussion of integration stability.
- Parameters are tuned on a 100-image subset via simulated annealing and center-bias/blur are grid-searched, potentially inflating performance.
- Empirical evaluation focuses on saliency-map metrics across MIT1003/MIT300/CAT2000/SFU but omits scanpath-level comparisons to human data.
- Important metrics that control for center bias (sAUC, shuffled NSS) are not reported, and center-biased initialization likely boosts AUC/NSS.
- Ablation studies are limited (only exact vs. approximate brightness term); contributions of curiosity, brightness invariance, center bias, and initialization are not quantified.
- Runtime claims are unsupported by actual timing measurements.
- Reproducibility is weak: missing key details (ω, m, k, parameter ranges, solver tolerances, preprocessing zone detection) and code availability is not stated.
- Presentation is generally readable with derivations in an appendix but contains grammatical errors, inconsistent references, and vague descriptions of some procedures.
- Novelty is moderate: combining curiosity and brightness constancy in a Lagrangian scanpath model is interesting but components are largely hand-crafted and related prior dynamical models exist.
- Significance is limited until center-bias-controlled evaluation and scanpath-level validation are provided, given strong modern deep saliency baselines.
- Strength: elegant variational formulation with closed-form EL equations and unified treatment of fixation and motion tracking via brightness invariance.
- Strength: saliency maps emerge naturally as a byproduct of explicit scanpath dynamics and reported benchmark competitiveness despite model simplicity.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on hand-crafted features, ad hoc periodic alternation, and tuned center bias without justification.
- Weakness: absence of scanpath-level validation (e.g., saccade amplitudes, MultiMatch, fixation durations) and limited ablation/sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior potential-field/dynamical saliency approaches.
- Suggested improvement: report sAUC, shuffled NSS, CC, SIM, KL, IG and show results with/without center bias and varied initializations.
- Suggested improvement: add scanpath-level validation (MultiMatch, trajectory similarity, saccade/fixation statistics).
- Suggested improvement: perform ablations (remove curiosity, remove brightness term, vary ω and key parameters) and analyze sensitivity.
- Suggested improvement: justify or learn ω and consider state-dependent switching instead of fixed periodic alternation.
- Suggested improvement: clarify numerical integration details, parameter units/ranges, stability, runtime, and release code for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: borderline reject — interesting modeling proposal but empirical validation and justification are insufficient for acceptance.
