- Summary: Proposes EYMOL, a variational, differential-equation-based model of visual attention integrating curiosity and brightness invariance, evaluated on static and dynamic saliency benchmarks.  
- Novelty & Theoretical Foundation: Variational least-action formulation provides a novel, principled alternative to data-driven saliency maps.  
- Unified Framework: Combines curiosity and brightness invariance into a single, biologically-plausible framework for fixation and tracking.  
- Dynamic Scene Handling: Naturally applicable to dynamic scenes without major modifications.  
- Competitive Performance: Achieves competitive results on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000 and SFU, matching or exceeding some complex methods.  
- Clear Mathematical Formulation: Presents derivation of Euler–Lagrange equations and a clear mathematical description (appendix contains detailed derivations).  
- Parameter Sensitivity & Tuning: Results depend heavily on simulated annealing for parameter estimation; sensitivity and robustness not fully explored.  
- Simplifications and Assumptions: Uses simplifications (Gaussian blur, center bias, brightness invariance approximation) whose impact on generalizability and plausibility is under-discussed.  
- Limited Biological Validation: Lacks strong neurophysiological comparisons and direct scanpath-level validation against human eye-tracking beyond saliency metrics.  
- Clarity of Appendix: Appendix derivations need clearer explanation and more concise notation.  
- Lack of Ablation Studies: No ablation experiments to quantify individual contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.  
- Quality Assessment: Mathematically sound and experiments clear, but robustness concerns (parameters, ablations) weaken confidence.  
- Clarity Assessment: Main text is readable; mathematical details require careful study and appendix could be improved.  
- Originality Assessment: The variational approach is original and a significant contribution despite prior work on individual components.  
- Significance Assessment: Promising alternative approach with potential for real-time and dynamic applications, contingent on further validation.  
- Arguments For Acceptance: Novel theory-driven approach, competitive benchmark performance, handles dynamics well, and clear math formulation.  
- Arguments Against Acceptance: Parameter sensitivity, restrictive assumptions, limited biological grounding, and missing ablation analyses.  
- Overall Assessment: Valuable and original contribution but needs further validation on parameters, assumptions, and biological plausibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Requested Revisions: perform parameter sensitivity analysis (ηb, ηp, λ); run ablation studies for core principles; discuss modeling assumptions and limitations; expand biological plausibility and scanpath comparisons to human data.
