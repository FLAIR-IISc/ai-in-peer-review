- Overall: Novel variational/Lagrangian scanpath model, but reviewer leans “weak reject” due to limited empirical advantage and clarity issues.  
- Core idea: Proposes EYMOL that derives scanpaths from a Lagrangian combining boundedness, curiosity (image gradients), and brightness invariance.  
- Contrast to prior work: Departs from saliency-map approaches and relates to feature-integration, information-maximization, and surprise-based attention models.  
- Theoretical contribution: Euler–Lagrange derivation provides a solid mathematical foundation and links attention to the least-action principle.  
- Novelty: Lagrangian formulation is conceptually original and unifies multiple attention principles.  
- Scanpath generation: Produces scanpaths directly rather than relying on precomputed saliency maps.  
- Real-time potential: Authors claim the model can be implemented in real time.  
- Empirical evaluation: Limited and not convincingly better than state-of-the-art deep learning methods; gains are marginal where present.  
- Parameter tuning: Use of simulated annealing lacks detail; parameter search space and sensitivity analysis are insufficient.  
- Approximation issues: Brightness invariance approximation (Eq. 6) is acknowledged but its impact is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Presentation clarity: Mathematical notation and explanations are dense; essential derivations relegated to the appendix.  
- Overstated claims: Calling the saliency map a “byproduct” is misleading since maps are computed from generated scanpaths for evaluation.  
- Missing analyses: No ablation studies to quantify contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance terms.  
- Significance vs. practicality: High originality but unclear practical benefits; combining with deep feature extraction is speculative.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; revise by providing stronger comparisons to deep baselines, clearer exposition, and deeper analysis of parameters and ablations.
