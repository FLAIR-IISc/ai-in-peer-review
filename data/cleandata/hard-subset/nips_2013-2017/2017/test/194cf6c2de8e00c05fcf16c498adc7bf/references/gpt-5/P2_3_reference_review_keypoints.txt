- Paper proposes a physics-inspired variational model of visual attention combining boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance into a Lagrangian yielding ODEs for scanpaths and saliency maps.
- Model generates scanpaths by integrating Euler–Lagrange equations and produces saliency by aggregating visits over multiple random runs.
- Parameters are tuned via simulated annealing and grid search; center bias and blur are added in preprocessing.
- Evaluated on MIT1003/MIT300/CAT2000 (static) and SFU (video) and compared to classic and some learning-based baselines.
- Connects to classic saliency, information/surprise models, optical flow, and dynamical saccade models; novelty lies in unifying curiosity and brightness invariance in a variational action.
- The kinetic energy term and EL derivation contain a basic inconsistency (missing 1/2 factor) and algebraic/notation typos that undermine theoretical claims.
- The treatment of velocity-dependent, non-conservative brightness invariance as a Lagrangian potential lacks justification; a dissipative framework might be more appropriate.
- The curiosity alternation term using cos^2/sin^2(ωt) is heuristic and ω is unspecified and unjustified.
- Strong center bias is introduced twice (central Gaussian preprocessing and centered initializations) and further tuned, risking inflated metrics without bias-controlled evaluations.
- Important bias-controlled metrics (sAUC, shuffled-NSS, IG) are not reported and an ablation without center bias is missing.
- Choices like number of runs (199) and 1s integration time are arbitrary with no sensitivity analysis provided.
- Ablation studies are minimal; missing tests for curiosity-only vs brightness-only, multi-scale alternation effects, blur, boundary term, and center bias.
- Comparisons omit several stronger deep baselines (e.g., DeepGaze II/IIE, SALICON variants, MLNet, EML-NET), weakening claims of competitive performance.
- Video evaluation is qualitative with no quantitative scanpath metrics (e.g., MultiMatch, string edit distance, saccade stats).
- Reproducibility is poor: many hyperparameters, solver settings, SA schedule, and preprocessing details are missing and no code is released.
- Paper is readable at a high level but marred by derivation errors, ambiguous notation, and an appendix needing careful proofreading.
- Originality: the variational framing and deriving attention dynamics from a unified action is a fresh, interpretable contribution.
- Significance: conceptually valuable as a lightweight, interpretable baseline, but limited practical impact without stronger, bias-controlled empirical validation.
- Pros: coherent, interpretable model; produces both scanpaths and saliency; efficient and unsupervised; evaluated on multiple datasets.
- Cons: technical derivation issues, heuristic/unjustified design choices, heavy reliance on center bias, missing strong baselines, incomplete reporting, and reproducibility concerns.
- Key suggestions: correct EL derivation, justify or reframe brightness term, report bias-controlled metrics and fuller ablations, include stronger baselines and scanpath metrics, and release code and full hyperparameters.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending corrected derivations, stronger bias-controlled experiments, comprehensive ablations, and improved reproducibility.
