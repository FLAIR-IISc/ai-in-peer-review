Summary
This paper proposes a dynamical systems formulation of visual attention in dynamic scenes. The authors define a Lagrangian action whose stationary point yields Euler–Lagrange equations governing the scanpath of gaze. The Lagrangian contains: (i) a boundary potential that keeps the trajectory within the retinal/image bounds; (ii) a “curiosity” term that alternates between local detail (||∇b||^2) and peripheral/blurred detail (||∇p||^2) via a cos^2/sin^2 temporal modulation; and (iii) a brightness invariance term (optical-flow constraint ḃ = b_t + ∇b·ẋ ≈ 0), which the authors argue underlies fixation and motion tracking. The resulting second-order ODEs (EYMOL) define scanpaths in real time; saliency maps are produced as a byproduct by counting visitation frequencies. Parameters are fitted via simulated annealing on a small training subset. On MIT1003/CAT2000/MIT300 for images and SFU for videos, the model is competitive with several baselines and sometimes best on standard metrics.

Relation to prior work
The paper positions itself against global saliency-map-centric pipelines (Itti & Koch; information maximization; Bayesian surprise; learned CNN predictors), and prior scanpath simulators that use winner-take-all plus heuristics (inhibition of return, similarity/proximity). The brightness invariance connects to classical optical flow (Horn–Schunck). The variational perspective links to physics-informed models; related ideas exist in active vision/dynamical fields, and, more broadly, in the free-energy/variational control literature, but an explicit least-action derivation for attention scanpaths that unifies curiosity and fixation/tracking is relatively rare in the saliency community.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A unified variational principle yields both saccadic exploration and (arguably) fixation/tracking behavior, with saliency emerging as a byproduct rather than being the central quantity. This is elegant and bold.
- Clear mechanistic interpretation: Each Lagrangian term has a plausible perceptual role (boundedness, detail-seeking, brightness constancy), and the alternation between local and peripheral fields is a simple, interpretable way to avoid detail traps.
- Derivation and analysis: The appendix provides explicit Euler–Lagrange equations, conditions for well-posedness (positivity of the “mass” matrix mI − 2λ∇b∇b^T), and a simplified static-image variant (upper bound on ḃ^2).
- Practicality: The ODE can be integrated efficiently; the method runs in real time and produces scanpaths—useful beyond saliency evaluation.
- Empirical signal: With minimal features (grayscale + blur + center bias), the model achieves competitive performance on multiple benchmarks and sometimes tops baselines.

Weaknesses and detailed comments
Quality
- Evaluation breadth and rigor: The reported comparisons are limited to a handful of (mostly older) baselines; modern deep models (e.g., deep saliency networks for images and video) are not systematically compared. For MIT300/CAT2000, specific numbers/tables are referenced but not shown; for SFU (12 clips), the scope is small. Include sAUC and other center-bias-robust metrics prominently, given the explicit center bias injection.
- Parameter tuning and generalization: Simulated annealing on a 100-image subset risks overfitting; it is unclear whether a single configuration is used across datasets or tuned per dataset. Please report cross-dataset generalization without re-tuning, and ablations (remove curiosity, remove brightness invariance, vary λ/η_b/η_p) to substantiate the mechanistic claims.
- Center bias: Multiplying inputs by a central Gaussian and later optimizing blur/center parameters can inflate AUC/Judd and NSS. sAUC/HFHD or a “debiased” evaluation should be emphasized.
- Scanpath validation: A central promise is generating scanpaths; yet evaluation is only via saliency maps. Please include scanpath metrics (e.g., MultiMatch, string-edit distance, time-to-first-fixation) and qualitative trajectory comparisons, including tracking behavior in video.
- Some technical rough edges: The boundary potential as a piecewise quadratic with Iverson brackets introduces discontinuities in gradients at borders; a smooth barrier may be preferable. The frequency ω governing the curiosity alternation is not specified or validated; how sensitive are results to ω? The derivation’s matrix determinant D contains apparent typos; a careful pass would improve confidence. Finally, the system is undamped; explaining the effective damping (if any) induced by the ḃ term or adding an explicit damping/noise term could stabilize trajectories and better match saccadic variability.

Clarity
- The main ideas are presented clearly, and the appendix helps. However, some notation is overloaded (bx denotes gradient, then squared norms appear without explicit notation). Specify all hyperparameters (ω, Gaussian σ for p, center-bias σ), integration details (step size, solver), and runtime. Provide a schematic of the energy terms and resultant force fields.
- Tables/metrics: Include full quantitative tables in the submission, with statistical significance and confidence intervals.

Originality
- High on conceptual originality within saliency/attention modeling. Related lines in optimal control/active inference/dynamical neural fields should be more fully discussed to situate the contribution (e.g., Friston’s free-energy principle, dynamic field models of saccades).

Significance
- If strengthened, this could influence how we think about attention modeling—moving from map-based predictors to principled dynamical laws that yield scanpaths and saliency jointly. Even if not SOTA on benchmarks, the framework is generative and extensible (e.g., adding top-down terms as potentials).

Questions for the authors
- How is ω chosen? Is its value dataset-independent? What happens if the alternation is removed?
- Do you use the same parameter triplet (η_b, η_p, λ) across all image datasets and video, or re-tune? Please report performance when fixing a single configuration.
- Can you report sAUC and center-bias-free metrics prominently and include ablations for the center-bias preprocessing?
- Do trajectories converge/fixate without an explicit damping term? Any stochasticity/noise added between runs beyond initial conditions?

Suggestions
- Add scanpath-level evaluations and qualitative videos.
- Expand comparisons to recent deep saliency models (both image and video).
- Provide ablations and sensitivity analyses (ω, σ_blur, σ_center).
- Explore smoother boundary potentials and/or explicit damping/noise.
- Release code and parameter defaults to facilitate adoption.

Recommendation: Weak Accept
Rationale: The paper presents a fresh, principled, and unifying variational framework for attention that generates scanpaths and saliency as an emergent property. While the empirical evaluation and clarity can be strengthened, the conceptual contribution is substantial and could stimulate new research directions. I am willing to overlook some evaluation incompleteness in favor of the novelty and potential impact. Confidence: Medium.
