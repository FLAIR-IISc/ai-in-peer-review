- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a novel variational model of visual attention using a Lagrangian whose Euler-Lagrange equations generate scanpaths.
- Frames attention as interplay between “curiosity” (high-detail regions) and “brightness invariance,” deriving saliency maps as byproducts of scanpath dynamics.
- Evaluates the model on static (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000) and dynamic (SFU) saliency benchmarks with competitive results.
- Positions the work against saliency-map-centric approaches and learning-based methods, claiming a first-principles (least action) unifying framework.
- Could strengthen connections to related physics-inspired and active inference literature.
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded variational formulation that departs from empirical hand-crafted models.
- Strength: unified treatment of saccadic movements and object tracking within a single model.
- Strength: incorporation of brightness invariance links attention to optical-flow and computer vision principles.
- Strength: competitive experimental performance achieved without directly optimizing for saliency maps.
- Strength: clear mathematical presentation with key derivations provided in the appendix.
- Weakness: reliance on simulated annealing for parameter estimation is computationally expensive and lacks justification.
- Weakness: model lacks direct neurophysiological comparisons, limiting biological validation.
- Weakness: simplified brightness signal b(t,x) may not handle real-world illumination, textures, and shadows robustly.
- Weakness: use of Gaussian blur and center bias preprocessing introduces artificiality and limited justification.
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies to quantify contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.
- Overall assessment: promising and original approach; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- For acceptance: novelty of framework, competitive results, and deriving saliency from scanpath modeling.
- Against acceptance: expensive parameter search, limited biological plausibility, and reliance on preprocessing.
- Required revisions: justify/replace simulated annealing, strengthen biological validation, add ablation studies, and expand discussion of limitations.
