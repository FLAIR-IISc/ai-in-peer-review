- Paper proposes a mechanistic variational model of overt visual attention using a Lagrangian with retinal confinement, curiosity (local/peripheral gradient alternation), and brightness-invariance, producing scanpaths via Euler–Lagrange ODEs and saliency maps from occupancy counts.  
- Parameters are tuned by simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN to maximize NSS; evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU video with claims of competitive results and emergent tracking.  
- Positions itself against global saliency-map methods and cites classical and recent work, but omits comparisons to many strong contemporary deep saliency models (e.g., DeepGaze II/III, SALICON, SAM-ResNet, EML-NET).  
- Variational setup is reasonable but contains at least one apparent error: kinetic term K(ẋ) inconsistently written as (1/m) ẋ^2 vs. (1/2) m ẋ^2, affecting the inertial term.  
- The curiosity term’s periodic modulation (cos^2, sin^2) and choice of ω are ad hoc and insufficiently justified.  
- Empirical reporting is incomplete: referenced tables and exact numbers/statistical tests are missing from the provided text.  
- Use of center bias (multiplying b and p by a Gaussian) and tuning it on training data can inflate metrics and confound attribution of improvements to the dynamics.  
- Simulated annealing (SA) methodology is underspecified (acceptance probability, ranges, ω, m, λ, ηb, ηp, solver step sizes).  
- Evaluation focuses on saliency-map metrics but omits scanpath-level quantitative metrics (e.g., MultiMatch, string-edit distance, temporal NSS), weakening support for the unified scanpath claim.  
- Paper is readable but has notational issues, typos, missing hyperparameters, and insufficient implementation details that hinder reproducibility.  
- Moderately novel: the variational formulation and EL dynamics are conceptually appealing and distinctive, though related dynamical/biophysical models exist.  
- Practical significance for saliency prediction is unclear due to missing comparisons with modern baselines and possible reliance on tuned center bias.  
- Pros: principled dynamical formulation linked to least action; unifies saccade generation and fixation/tracking in one ODE system; generates real-time scanpaths with saliency as a byproduct.  
- Cons: incorrect/missing formulas and specification gaps; heavy reliance on center bias and post-hoc tuning; lack of scanpath/tracking metrics; absence of comparisons to strong recent baselines; curiosity design is ad hoc with no ablations.  
- Requested fixes: correct kinetic term and derivations, provide all hyperparameters and SA details, fully report standard saliency metrics with CIs and modern baselines, include ablations (center bias, curiosity, brightness term, ω), and add scanpath/tracking quantitative evaluations.  
- Also requested: analyze robustness and computational cost, perform sensitivity analyses, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Overall rating: 4 (weak reject) — promising theoretical contribution but currently lacking technical clarity, empirical rigor, and reproducibility to verify claims.
