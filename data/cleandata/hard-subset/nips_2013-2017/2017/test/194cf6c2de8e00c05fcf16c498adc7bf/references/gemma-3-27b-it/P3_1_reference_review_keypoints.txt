- Overall assessment: novel variational approach with potential, but requires major revisions; recommendation: weak accept pending fixes.  
- Paper proposes EYMOL: attention dynamics from variational principles (bounded trajectories, curiosity, brightness invariance) leading to differential equations.  
- Evaluation: saliency maps from simulated scanpaths tested on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU; results competitive despite not being optimized for saliency.  
- Strength: unique theoretical framework linking attention to least-action principles.  
- Strength: unified modeling of saccadic movements and object tracking in one framework.  
- Strength: promising empirical performance relative to state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: clear motivation and mathematically rigorous derivations (appendix).  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks crucial details (simulated annealing parameters, initialization, decay).  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for parameter choices and theoretical bounds.  
- Weakness: over-reliance on quantitative metrics without deeper analysis of successes/failures on specific stimuli.  
- Weakness: near absence of qualitative visualizations comparing model and human scanpaths.  
- Weakness: implications of approximation in Eq. (6) are not discussed or analyzed.  
- Weakness: disconnect between elegant theory and pragmatic simulated annealing application; parameter-principle links feel tenuous.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational cost and real-time performance claims.  
- For acceptance: novelty, rigorous framework, competitive results, unified saccade/tracking model.  
- Against acceptance: inadequate experimental validation, weak parameter interpretation, lack of qualitative analysis, dense writing.  
- Major suggested revisions: fully describe simulated annealing (domain, init temp, cooling schedule, iterations, batch size).  
- Suggested revision: perform ablation study isolating contributions of ηb, ηp, λ.  
- Suggested revision: include visual scanpath overlays comparing model vs. human fixations.  
- Suggested revision: analyze and justify approximation in Eq. (6) and interpret parameters intuitively.  
- Suggested revision: quantify computational cost, especially numerical integration time.  
- Suggested revision: relate model predictions to biological constraints (saccade amplitude, velocity).  
- Related work: cite more recent deep-learning attention models (RNNs, transformers) and discuss limits of saliency-map approaches.  
- Conclusion: promising and mathematically solid but incomplete; major experimental, interpretative, and presentation improvements needed.
