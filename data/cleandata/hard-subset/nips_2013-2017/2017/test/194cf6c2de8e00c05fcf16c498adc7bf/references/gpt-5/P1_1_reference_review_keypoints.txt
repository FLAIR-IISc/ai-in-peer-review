- Proposes a variational/least-action formulation of visual attention: scanpaths as particle trajectories under action S = ∫(K − U)dt with potential U comprising boundary, curiosity, and brightness-invariance terms.  
- Euler–Lagrange yields second-order ODEs (EYMOL); static images use an upper-bound simplification; parameters fitted by simulated annealing to maximize NSS.  
- Saliency maps are produced as visitation heatmaps from multiple stochastic runs; evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU video with competitive results versus classical baselines.  

Strengths:
- Provides a principled, compact unification of curiosity-driven exploration and fixation/tracking via least-action mechanics.  
- Saliency naturally emerges from aggregated simulated scanpaths rather than being modeled directly.  
- Single framework handles both static and dynamic inputs; optical-flow constraint offers a natural motion-tracking bias.  
- Empirically competitive with simple hand-crafted model and claimed real-time feasibility.  

Weaknesses / Technical concerns:
- Mathematical errors: kinetic energy K is mis-specified, derivative inconsistencies, determinant derivation drops λ² term, and repeated typos make derivations unreliable.  
- Many implementation hyperparameters and choices are unspecified (ω, m, k, λ ranges, SA schedule, ODE solver/timestep, blur/center-bias σ, initialization, etc.), preventing reproducibility.  
- Strong center bias (central Gaussian and center init) likely inflates metrics like NSS and AUC, confounding claims about dynamics.  
- Parameter fitting on CAT2000 subset risks overfitting; no cross-validation or controls to separate dynamics from priors.  
- Evaluation limited to saliency maps; no scanpath similarity, saccade stats, fixation durations, or quantitative pursuit/tracking metrics.  
- Comparisons to modern deep saliency models are limited or insufficiently detailed.  
- Clarity issues: typos, malformed/incomplete equations, missing references, and absent tables hinder readability.  

Quality assessment:
- Interesting central idea but technical correctness is compromised and stability/ODE claims are not currently trustworthy.  
- Empirical methodology lacks key controls and ablations to support claims.  

Clarity request:
- Paper is not reproducible as written; authors must fully specify hyperparameters, solver details, initial conditions, and dataset splits, and correct derivations.  

Originality:
- Novel and elegant use of variational least-action framework for attention dynamics, but related work (dynamic neural fields, active vision, RL attention) needs fuller coverage.  

Significance:
- Potentially impactful if derivations are fixed and evaluations control for center bias and include scanpath analyses; current empirical significance is limited.  

Suggested improvements:
- Correct the Lagrangian and all derivations (include λ² term) and fix typos.  
- Fully specify and release implementation details and code for reproducibility.  
- Run ablations: remove center bias, vary initialization, remove curiosity/brightness terms, probe ω, compare exact vs approximate B.  
- Evaluate scanpath statistics and pursuit metrics quantitatively; compare to strong contemporary static/video deep models and center-bias baselines.  
- Report computational costs and real-time benchmarks; expand related-work discussion.  

Acceptance pros:
- Conceptually elegant unification, lightweight, real-time potential, promising initial performance.  

Acceptance cons:
- Mathematical errors, heavy tuning/center-bias reliance, missing details, limited evaluation of scanpaths/tracking.  

Recommendation:
- Weak reject; idea is promising but requires substantial technical corrections and stronger, controlled empirical validation.
