- Proposes a physics-inspired, bottom-up model of gaze using three principles: border “spring” potential, periodic curiosity for detailed regions, and brightness-invariance for fixation/tracking.  
- Defines a Lagrangian from these principles and derives Euler–Lagrange equations yielding a second-order dynamical system for scanpaths.  
- Produces saliency maps by aggregating trajectory visit counts over multiple random-initialization runs.  
- Parameters tuned by simulated annealing on 100 CAT2000 images; evaluated on MIT1003/300, CAT2000, and SFU video datasets with competitive metrics claimed.  
- Contrasts with saliency-map-first literature and echoes active-vision/control perspectives; omits engagement with recent active sensing/RL and predictive-coding/free-energy models.  
- Conceptual strengths: simple, interpretable, mathematically transparent model that unifies saccades, fixation, and tracking.  
- Applicability strength: extends to video via brightness-constancy term and appears computationally feasible for real-time.  
- Empirical strength: competitive results on multiple datasets despite minimal features.  
- Major technical issue: kinetic energy uses K = (1/m)ẋ^2 instead of standard (1/2)m||ẋ||^2 — needs justification/correction.  
- Velocity-dependent potential B is handled informally; should be positioned correctly (velocity-dependent potentials or Rayleigh dissipation) and implications discussed.  
- Boundary potential uses Iverson brackets causing non-differentiability at borders, conflicting with smooth EL assumptions; suggest smooth barrier instead.  
- Appendix derivations contain typos/inconsistencies; determinant algebra and the λ < m/4 stability bound appear incorrect or unjustified.  
- Periodic curiosity term (cos^2/sin^2 alternation) lacks biological/algorithmic justification and sensitivity analysis for ω; multi-scale alternative may be preferable.  
- Experimental fairness concern: strong center bias injected twice (input multiplication and post-hoc grid search), potentially inflating metrics sensitive to center bias.  
- Missing sAUC (shuffled AUC) and unclear separation of results with/without center-bias tuning; this undermines claims that dynamics drive performance.  
- Parameter tuning on small subset risks overfitting; unclear if parameters fixed across datasets or re-tuned — report values, CIs, and sensitivity (especially ηb, ηp, λ, ω).  
- Lacks crucial ablations (curiosity-only, brightness-only, no border term, no center bias, no post-hoc blur) to validate each principle’s contribution.  
- Video evaluation is under-specified: absent details on bt computation, time step, integration scheme, and no quantitative tracking/scanpath metrics provided.  
- Reproducibility limited: code and many implementation choices (ω, integration steps, runtime) are not provided.  
- Presentation issues: many typos, incomplete references, equation/index errors, and unclear figures/tables — needs careful editing.  
- Center bias treated as pre/post-processing rather than integrated into the Lagrangian; recommend making it an explicit prior term.  
- Originality: interesting variational approach and unified scanpath dynamics, but impact limited by bias tuning and lack of ablations/controlled evaluations.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject; require corrected derivations, bias-controlled and ablated experiments with reported parameters/code, and stronger scanpath evaluations for revision.
