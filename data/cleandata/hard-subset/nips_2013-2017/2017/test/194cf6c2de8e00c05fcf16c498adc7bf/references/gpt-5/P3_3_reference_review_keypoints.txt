- Proposes a variational dynamical-systems model of scanpaths using a Lagrangian L = K − U with explicit Euler–Lagrange ODEs for gaze.  
- Model components: boundary potential V(x), time-modulated curiosity C(t,x) switching local/peripheral gradients, and brightness-invariance penalty B(t,x,ẋ).  
- For static images, proposes an upper-bound approximation to simplify the brightness penalty.  
- Parameters tuned by simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN; saliency maps obtained by visit counts over many simulated center-initialized scanpaths.  
- Evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU video and claims competitive performance vs a few classical baselines and anecdotal tracking evidence.  
- Strength: conceptually novel, interpretable unified variational formulation tying curiosity and brightness constancy.  
- Strength: derivation is worked out to produce continuous-time scanpaths and is computationally light.  
- Strength: saliency emerges naturally from simulated dynamics rather than being hand-crafted.  
- Strength: tested on standard image/video datasets with some MIT benchmark metrics reported.  
- Major technical inconsistency: kinetic energy expression is contradictory (1/m vs 1/2 m), affecting derived motion equations and λ condition.  
- Mathematical derivation errors: determinant for λ condition appears to drop terms and contains indexing mistakes, undermining well-posedness claims.  
- Modeling choices are ad hoc and under-justified (cos^2/sin^2 modulation, piecewise boundary potential, applying center bias to brightness).  
- Empirical methodology concerns: center bias and blur tuned to metrics, center initialization and many runs may inflate center-sensitive metrics, no ablations reported.  
- Incomplete evaluation: missing referenced tables, exclusion of modern deep baselines for images and video, and no quantitative tracking metrics for video.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing values/ranges for hyperparameters, integration method/step size, bt estimation, stopping rules, and train/val/test specifics; no code provided.  
- Clarity issues: typos, indexing errors, inconsistent notation, and incomplete derivation for static-image approximation; experimental settings and full results absent.  
- Originality: variational second-order gaze dynamics are interesting, but constituent principles are classical and related prior work in active vision/oculomotor models is insufficiently discussed.  
- Significance: promising interpretable model, but limited impact without stronger empirical evidence, ablations, and corrected technical issues; efficiency reduced by needing many stochastic runs.  
- Key suggestions: fix kinetic energy and λ derivation, provide stability/attractor analysis, justify/ablate curiosity modulation and ω, remove/treat center bias principledly, and include oculomotor constraints.  
- Experimental suggestions: run full ablations, report results vs modern baselines (DeepGaze II, SALICON, SAM/MLNet/DeepFix, recent video models), include sAUC/CC/KL/SIM and scanpath metrics, and show performance without center bias.  
- Practical suggestions: provide parameter values, integration details, bt estimation/smoothing for video, sensitivity analysis, and release code/data.  
- Pros summary: elegant interpretable formulation, continuous scanpaths, lightweight computation.  
- Cons summary: derivation errors, reliance on tuned center bias/blur, limited and outdated comparisons, missing ablations and experimental details, and unquantified tracking claims.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; promising idea but needs technical fixes, stronger empirical validation, and improved clarity/positioning.
