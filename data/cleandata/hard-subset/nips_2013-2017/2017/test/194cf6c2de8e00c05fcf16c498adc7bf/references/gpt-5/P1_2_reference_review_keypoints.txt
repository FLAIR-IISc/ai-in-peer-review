- Summary: Proposes EYMOL, a variational, physics-inspired model that generates scanpaths as particle trajectories under a Lagrangian combining boundary, curiosity (local+peripheral gradients), and brightness-invariance terms, producing saliency maps from visitation heatmaps.

- Relation to prior work: Positions against centralized saliency maps and saccadic simulators, connects to optical-flow brightness constancy and generative gaze models, but could discuss deep saliency and scanpath models more comprehensively.

- Theory — kinetic energy: Kinetic term is dimensionally suspect (missing 1/2 factor); units/constants are not justified.

- Theory — velocity-dependent potential: B depends on velocity so is not a true potential; implications for Euler–Lagrange derivation and stability need clearer treatment (e.g., generalized forces/dissipation).

- Theory — determinant and bounds: Derivation of determinant D and λ < m/4 has typos, unstated normalizations, and appears heuristic; rigorous existence/uniqueness/stability analysis is needed.

- Theory — alternating curiosity term: The cos^2/sin^2 temporal mixing is ad hoc; ω choice lacks perceptual justification and analysis of trapping/escape effects.

- Experiments — reporting: Numerical results, tables, and statistical tests are insufficiently presented here, making competitiveness vs deep baselines hard to verify.

- Experiments — center bias: Model uses a strong center bias twice (input weighting and center initialization) plus tuning, raising fairness concerns and requiring sAUC and no-center ablations.

- Experiments — parameter tuning and robustness: SA tuning on 100 images with five repeats is limited; sensitivity, cross-dataset reproducibility, and initialization robustness are not reported.

- Experiments — ablations and implementation details: No ablations of B/C/peripheral/boundary terms, and integrator/time-step/parameter values are not fully specified for reproducibility.

- Experiments — videos and scanpaths: Claims of tracking are anecdotal; need quantitative scanpath/motion-tracking metrics (e.g., MultiMatch).

- Reproducibility: Paper should release code, seeds, full parameter settings, and preprocessing/postprocessing details.

- Clarity: High-level story and appendix derivations are helpful but contain typos, notation inconsistencies, and skipped assumptions; key parameter roles (ω, m, λ, k) and discretization are unclear.

- Originality: Variational framing unifying curiosity and brightness invariance is novel, though ingredients are standard and similar ODE-based scanpath generation exists.

- Significance: Could be impactful if matching deep baselines without training, offering interpretability, but current evidence is insufficient and may be inflated by center bias.

- Pros: Elegant variational formulation, unified dynamic generation of scanpaths and saliency, interpretable simple ingredients, explicit equations.

- Cons: Technical derivation errors/ambiguities, heavy reliance on center bias and tuning, missing quantitative details and ablations, heuristic components lacking validation, no released code.

- Suggestions: Fix derivations (kinetic factor, determinant), provide stability proofs, present full metrics with CIs and deep-baseline comparisons with center-bias controls, run comprehensive ablations and scanpath analyses, report integrator and parameter settings, and release code.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject — intriguing and potentially impactful but currently lacks rigorous technical, empirical, and reproducibility support.
