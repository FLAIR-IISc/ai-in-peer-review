- Introduces EYMOL, a variational least-action model where attention emerges from boundedness, curiosity (image detail), and brightness invariance; outputs scanpaths and derives saliency maps as a byproduct.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior saliency work by directly generating scanpaths rather than optimizing saliency maps; links brightness invariance to optical flow estimation.  
- Novel variational formulation offers a fresh, principled perspective on visual attention.  
- Provides a unified framework that models both saccadic eye movements and object tracking.  
- Mathematical foundations are solidly derived via Euler-Lagrange equations.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on static (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000) and dynamic (SFU) benchmarks despite model simplicity.  
- Produces emergent saliency maps from scanpath generation, suggesting a more holistic attention model.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-written with accessible mathematical notation.  
- Parameter estimation relies on simulated annealing, which is computationally expensive and potentially sensitive to initialization; search-space description is vague.  
- Uses an approximation for brightness invariance that simplifies computation but may affect performance; needs deeper analysis.  
- Ablation studies are limited (only two model variants); more experiments needed to isolate each principle’s contribution.  
- Lacks strong neurological grounding or explicit links to known brain mechanisms of attention.  
- Dynamic-scene evaluation relies on the small SFU dataset; claims would benefit from larger, more diverse video datasets.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 4.5/5, Originality 4/5, Significance 3.5/5.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and principled approach, competitive results, clear presentation, and potential broader impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost for parameter tuning, limited ablations, and weak neurological justification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: provide detailed parameter search-space information, perform additional ablation studies for each principle, and discuss connections to neurological attention mechanisms.
