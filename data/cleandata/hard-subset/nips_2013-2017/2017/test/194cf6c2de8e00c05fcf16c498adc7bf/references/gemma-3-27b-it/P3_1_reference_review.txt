## Review of "Variational Laws of Visual Attention for Dynamic Scenes"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents an interesting and novel approach to modeling visual attention, framing it as a problem of variational optimization. The attempt to unify curiosity-driven exploration and brightness invariance within a single theoretical framework is commendable. However, while the core idea is appealing, the paper lacks sufficient detail in its experimental validation and suffers from a somewhat disconnected presentation of the mathematical formulation and the empirical results. I believe the paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution, but requires significant revisions to address my concerns. My recommendation is **weak accept**, contingent on major revisions addressing the points detailed below.

**1. Summary of the Paper:**

The authors propose a new model of visual attention, termed EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), based on principles of bounded trajectories, curiosity (high detail regions), and brightness invariance. These principles are formalized through a variational approach, leading to differential equations describing the focus of attention.  The model is evaluated on static image datasets (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000) and a dynamic scene dataset (SFU) by computing saliency maps derived from simulated scanpaths and comparing them to human fixations. The key innovation lies in deriving attention dynamics from first principles rather than directly optimizing for saliency map prediction.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novel Theoretical Framework:** The variational approach is a unique and potentially powerful way to model visual attention. Linking attention to concepts from physics (least action principle) offers a fresh perspective.
* **Unified Model:**  The simultaneous modeling of both saccadic eye movements and object tracking within a single framework is a significant strength.
* **Promising Results:** The experimental results show competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, especially considering the model isn’t explicitly designed for saliency map prediction.
* **Clear Motivation:** The introduction clearly articulates the complexities of visual attention and the limitations of existing approaches, justifying the need for a new model.
* **Mathematical Rigor:** The appendix provides a detailed derivation of the Euler-Lagrange equations, demonstrating a commitment to mathematical precision.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The experimental section lacks crucial details. The specific parameter settings used for the simulated annealing (SA) are not fully explained, making it difficult to assess their impact. A more thorough ablation study is needed to understand the contribution of each parameter (ηb, ηp, λ).
* **Limited Justification for Parameter Choices:** The rationale behind the chosen parameters for the SA algorithm (domain D, initialization, temperature decay) is not clearly articulated. What theoretical bounds are considered?
* **Over-reliance on Existing Metrics:** While the use of established metrics (AUC-Judd, NSS) is helpful, the paper would benefit from a deeper analysis of *why* the model succeeds or fails on specific images/videos. Simply reporting numbers doesn’t provide sufficient insight.
* **Lack of Qualitative Analysis:** There's a near complete absence of visual examples comparing the generated scanpaths with human scanpaths. Including such visualizations would significantly strengthen the paper.
* **Simplifications and Approximations:** The use of the approximation in Eq. (6) is presented as a simplification, but the implications of this approximation on the model’s behavior are not fully discussed.
* **Disconnected Presentation:** There's a disconnect between the theoretical elegance of the model and the somewhat pragmatic, almost “black box,” application of simulated annealing.  The connection between the parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) and the underlying principles of curiosity and brightness invariance feels tenuous.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** The paper mentions real-time performance, but a more detailed discussion of the computational cost (especially for the numerical integration of the differential equations) would be valuable.



**4. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a genuinely novel approach to a challenging problem.
* The mathematical framework is well-developed and rigorous.
* The experimental results, while not overwhelming, are competitive.
* The unified model of saccades and tracking is a valuable contribution.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental validation is insufficient and lacks crucial details.
* The connection between the theoretical parameters and the underlying principles is weak.
* The paper lacks qualitative analysis and visual examples.
* The writing is occasionally dense and could benefit from clearer explanations.




**5. Specific Suggestions & Questions:**

* **Simulated Annealing Details:** Provide a full description of the SA process: Domain D, initial temperature, cooling schedule (α), number of iterations, and the batch size used for evaluation.
* **Ablation Study:** Conduct an ablation study to assess the individual contribution of each parameter (ηb, ηp, λ) to the model’s performance.
* **Qualitative Results:** Include visualizations of scanpaths generated by the model, overlaid on the input images/videos, alongside corresponding human scanpaths.
* **Approximation Analysis:** Discuss the potential impact of the approximation in Eq. (6) on the model’s behavior. Is there a theoretical justification for its validity?
* **Parameter Interpretation:** Provide a more intuitive explanation of how the parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) relate to the underlying principles of curiosity and brightness invariance.
* **Computational Cost:**  Quantify the computational cost of the model, especially the time required for numerical integration.
* **Connection to Biological Data:** Discuss how the model's predictions align with known biological constraints on eye movements (e.g., saccade amplitude, velocity).




**6. Related Work:**

The paper cites relevant work in visual attention and saliency modeling. However, it could benefit from acknowledging more recent advances in deep learning-based approaches to attention, such as those based on recurrent neural networks or transformers. Also a little bit more discussion on the limitations of current saliency-map based approaches would be helpful to better justify the proposed new approach.

**7. Conclusion:**

This paper presents a promising, but incomplete, approach to modeling visual attention. The novelty of the variational framework and the unified model are significant strengths. However, the lack of experimental detail, limited justification for parameter choices, and absence of qualitative analysis are major weaknesses.  Addressing these concerns would significantly improve the paper and strengthen its contribution to the field. I recommend **weak accept** pending major revisions.
