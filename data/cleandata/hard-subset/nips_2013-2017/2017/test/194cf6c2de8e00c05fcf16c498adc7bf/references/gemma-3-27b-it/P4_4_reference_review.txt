The paper proposes a novel model of visual attention, termed EYMOL, based on a variational approach and a least action principle. The core idea is that attention emerges from balancing curiosity for detailed regions and the need for brightness invariance, driving both saccadic eye movements and object tracking. This contrasts with many existing saliency models that rely on global computations and post-hoc scanpath generation. The model is mathematically formulated and its dynamics are described by Euler-Lagrange equations. Experimental evaluation is performed on static images (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000) and dynamic scenes (SFU) using standard saliency detection metrics.

This work builds upon the established feature integration theory [14, 9, 10] but diverges by moving away from centralized saliency map computation and instead defining attention as an emergent property of underlying principles. Recent deep learning approaches [26, 18] have achieved strong performance in saliency prediction, but EYMOL offers a different perspective, grounded in variational calculus, aiming for a more biologically plausible mechanism. The variational approach is reminiscent of work in motor control and perception [28], although the application to visual attention in this specific manner appears novel.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The variational approach to modeling visual attention is a fresh perspective, offering a fundamentally different mechanism compared to traditional saliency map based methods. The combination of curiosity and brightness invariance as driving principles is insightful.
* **Mathematical Rigor:** The model is clearly and mathematically defined, using established principles of variational calculus. The derivation of the Euler-Lagrange equations provides a solid theoretical foundation.
* **Unified Framework:** The model elegantly handles both saccadic movements and object tracking within the same framework, which is a significant advantage.
* **Competitive Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate that the model achieves competitive performance on standard datasets, despite its relative simplicity compared to deep learning-based approaches.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** While the mathematical formalism is present, the paper does a reasonable job of explaining the key concepts and the intuition behind the model.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The model relies on several parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) that need to be tuned using simulated annealing. The sensitivity of the results to these parameters is not thoroughly investigated, and the process of parameter estimation could be more transparent.
* **Computational Cost:** Although presented as real-time capable, the numerical integration of the differential equations might become computationally expensive for high-resolution images or complex scenes. The paper mentions this but does not provide detailed performance benchmarks.
* **Limited Biological Validation:** While the authors claim biological plausibility, there is a lack of direct comparison with specific neurophysiological data or detailed modeling of neural mechanisms. The connection to actual biological processes could be strengthened.
* **Simplifying Assumptions:** The model makes several simplifying assumptions (e.g., grayscale images, Gaussian blur for peripheral processing) that may limit its applicability to real-world scenarios. The impact of these assumptions on the model's performance is not fully explored.
* **Insufficient Ablation Studies:** While two versions of the model are compared (with and without the approximation in brightness invariance), a more comprehensive ablation study to analyze the contribution of each component (boundedness, curiosity, brightness invariance) would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to modeling visual attention, offering a compelling alternative to existing methods.
* The mathematical framework is well-developed and provides a clear and concise description of the model's dynamics.
* The experimental results demonstrate that the model can achieve competitive performance on standard datasets.
* The unified treatment of saccades and tracking is a significant strength.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The parameter sensitivity and computational cost are potential limitations.
* The lack of strong biological validation raises questions about the model's relevance to actual human visual attention.
* The simplifying assumptions could limit the model's generalizability.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of visual attention modeling. While some limitations exist, the novelty of the approach, the mathematical rigor, and the competitive performance warrant acceptance. The work opens up new avenues for research and could potentially lead to more biologically plausible and efficient attention mechanisms.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
