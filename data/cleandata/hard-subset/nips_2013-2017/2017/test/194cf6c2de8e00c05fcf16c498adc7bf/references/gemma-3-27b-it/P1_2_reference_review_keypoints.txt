- Overall: Proposes a novel variational approach to visual attention (EYMOL); promising results but lacks deeper analysis — recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Derives scanpaths from a Lagrangian combining boundedness, curiosity (image gradients), and brightness invariance, producing saliency maps as a byproduct.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on static images and dynamic videos, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art saliency methods.  
- Novelty: Variational framing of attention is a distinct and compelling departure from saliency-map paradigms.  
- Mathematical rigor: Derivation of Euler–Lagrange equations is sound and well-presented (with technical details in the Appendix).  
- Unified framework: Elegantly combines curiosity and brightness invariance, suggesting a deeper connecting principle.  
- Practicality: Model demonstrates potential for real-time application.  
- Main shortcoming: Paper does not convincingly explain why the chosen principles are effective or their neural/cognitive plausibility.  
- Parameter issues: Simulated annealing parameter estimation lacks justification; sensitivity and search domain are not thoroughly explored.  
- Ablation missing: No ablation studies to isolate contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness-invariance terms.  
- Biological comparison lacking: Limited comparison to biologically inspired saccade/attention models and neural mechanisms.  
- Evaluation tension: Although saliency maps are framed as byproducts, evaluation still relies heavily on saliency-map metrics instead of direct scanpath metrics.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but mathematical appendix may be difficult for non-experts; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Originality vs. significance: Highly original approach, but practical/significant impact is currently moderate and not clearly demonstrated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorous, competitive performance, offers new perspective, feasible derivation of scanpaths from first principles.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient explanation of mechanism, parameter sensitivity not addressed, lack of ablations, limited demonstrated significance.  
- Recommended revisions: Expand discussion of biological plausibility and neural links.  
- Recommended revisions: Conduct ablation studies to quantify each Lagrangian term’s contribution.  
- Recommended revisions: Provide thorough parameter sensitivity analysis and clarify search domain.  
- Recommended revisions: Include alternative evaluations focusing on scanpath qualities (e.g., length, revisits) and strengthen motivation for the approach.
