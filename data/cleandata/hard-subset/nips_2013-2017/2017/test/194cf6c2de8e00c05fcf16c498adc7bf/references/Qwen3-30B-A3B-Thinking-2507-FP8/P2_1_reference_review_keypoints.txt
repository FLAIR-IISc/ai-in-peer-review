- Proposes EYMOL: a variational model of eye movements where attention emerges from bounded trajectories, curiosity to detail, and brightness invariance, producing scanpaths with saliency maps as byproducts.  
- Mathematical derivation is technically sound with clear Euler–Lagrange exposition and reproducible appendix.  
- Full model with parameter estimation via simulated annealing, well-motivated to fit human fixation patterns.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough on standard image and video datasets using NSS and AUC-Judd, showing competitive results.  
- Acknowledges limitations: saliency map is a byproduct and model not specifically designed for saliency prediction.  
- Insufficient comparison to other variational attention approaches; differentiation from prior work is weak.  
- Curiosity term (Eq. 4) lacks strong justification; the chosen local/peripheral and trigonomic form is not well-motivated or validated.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis of generated scanpaths; only quantitative saliency metrics reported.  
- No timing or runtime data to support claims of real-time performance.  
- Some technical details (e.g., brightness invariance approximation) need clearer explanation for non-expert readers.  
- Relationship between scanpaths and the resulting saliency map should be explained more clearly.  
- Conceptually novel: unified variational framework combining curiosity and brightness invariance, and focus on scanpath generation rather than direct saliency.  
- Intellectual appeal in relating attention to physical laws and offering a fresh perspective on eye-movement modeling.  
- Practical value: simple, computationally efficient model that yields realistic scanpaths and competitive benchmark performance.  
- Limitations in impact: unclear integration path into larger vision systems and limited demonstration of addressing unique CV/neuroscience challenges.  
- Overall assessment: novel, theoretically sound, competitive empirically; main weaknesses are insufficient differentiation from related work and limited qualitative/implementation details.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed comparisons with other variational models, show qualitative scanpath examples vs humans, provide runtime measurements, justify/ablate the curiosity term, and compare to recent deep saliency models.
