Summary
This paper proposes a variational formulation of visual attention in which scanpaths arise as solutions to Euler–Lagrange equations derived from an action functional. The Lagrangian includes: (i) a boundary potential that confines motion to the “retina”; (ii) a “curiosity” term that alternates between local gradient energy and a blurred (peripheral) gradient energy (via cos^2/sin^2 modulation); and (iii) a brightness invariance term inspired by the optical flow constraint bt + bx ẋ = 0, enforced softly as a squared residual. Saliency maps are obtained as byproducts by aggregating visited locations over many simulated trajectories. Parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) are tuned via simulated annealing on a subset of CAT2000-TRAIN; blur and center-bias parameters are further tuned by grid search. Results on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU video are reported as competitive with selected baselines.

Strengths
- Conceptual originality: a unified variational perspective that derives attention dynamics and scanpaths directly from an action, with saliency maps as a byproduct.
- Attempt to connect fixation/brightness constancy and curiosity in one mathematical framework.
- Real-time, lightweight dynamics; uses only grayscale gradients and basic preprocessing.
- Includes both static and dynamic scenes; claims object tracking behavior in some cases.

Weaknesses
Technical correctness and derivations:
- Kinetic energy definition appears incorrect/inconsistent: Eq. (8) K(ẋ) = 1/m ẋ^2 contradicts later use of d/dt ∂K/∂ẋ = m ẍ (consistent with K = (m/2)|ẋ|^2). Dimensional inconsistency here propagates to the equations of motion.
- Several derivation steps in the Appendix are wrong or sloppy:
  - In the determinant simplification (Eq. 41–42), terms are mistyped (bx1 repeated) and the simplification D = m(m − 2λ(…)) is incorrect. The bound λ < m/4 is therefore not justified.
  - The derivative of V with Iverson brackets ignores distributional effects at boundaries; a smooth barrier or proper handling of non-differentiability is needed.
  - The “generalized potential” B depends on velocity; treating it as a potential without discussing non-conservative forces and Rayleigh dissipation is non-standard and confusing.
- The periodic curiosity modulation C(t,x) = |bx|^2 cos^2(ωt) + |px|^2 sin^2(ωt) is ad hoc; ω is not specified or learned, and no ablation is provided to justify the alternation vs. fixed weights.

Experimental design and fairness:
- Heavy center bias: images are multiplied by a central Gaussian pre-filter and initial conditions are near the center; then an additional post-hoc blur/center bias is tuned by grid search to optimize AUC-Judd/NSS. This risks attributing center-bias gains to the proposed dynamics. Ablations removing these biases are essential.
- Parameter tuning via simulated annealing uses only 100 images, with unspecified neighbor proposals and acceptance function P(·), and unspecified parameter domain D. Important hyperparameters (ω, m, k, integration step/solver) are not described or tuned.
- Implementation details missing for reproducibility: ODE solver choice, step size, time discretization, “1 second” mapping to numerical steps, how bt is computed in videos, frame rates, interpolation, random initialization distribution, and velocity magnitude.
- Evaluation: Missing tables/metrics in the text provided (e.g., “Table 2/3/4”) prevents assessment. Claims like “best in half of the cases” cannot be verified. No statistical testing details, no ablations (remove B, remove C, static vs. dynamic, with/without periodic modulation, with/without blurred term p).
- Fairness to baselines unclear; baselines may not be retuned on the same train splits or endowed with extra center bias/blur.

Scope and claims:
- The model does not convincingly capture saccades (ballistic, with characteristic amplitude and duration distributions). The continuous ODE dynamics with quadratic penalties lack an account of saccade generation, inhibition-of-return, or fixation durations. No scanpath-level metrics (e.g., saccade amplitude/direction distributions, sequence similarity) are reported, despite scanpath modeling being central to the contribution.
- The “least action” narrative is largely aesthetic; there is limited justification for the specific Lagrangian form beyond convenience, and no theoretical guarantees (e.g., stability, existence/uniqueness given the velocity-dependent term).
- The brightness-invariance “approximation” (Eq. 6) is used in main experiments, but support for its equivalence is based on missing tables. Also, when bt = 0 for static images, B̄ reduces to a speed penalty weighted by |bx|^2, which can trivially slow motion in textured areas—behavioral consequences and sensitivity are not analyzed.

Clarity and presentation:
- Numerous typos, grammatical errors, and formatting glitches (e.g., “st Conference on…”, “interwound” vs. “intertwined”, “eggs-chicken”, “psedo-code”), unclear notation (bx vs. |∇b|, vector vs. scalar squares), inconsistent indices, and missing reference details (years/venues). Several equations are misnumbered or contain copy-paste errors (e.g., repeated bx1).
- The paper omits essential algorithmic details (acceptance probability, neighbor generation, hyperparameter values). Reproducibility is currently unattainable.

Originality and related work:
- While the variational framing is interesting, there is limited discussion of prior physics/variational or control-theoretic models of gaze/active vision, or recent deep saliency models (e.g., DeepGaze II/III, SALICON, MLNet, GBVS variants). The novelty relative to dynamic saliency and reinforcement learning-based gaze control is underdeveloped.

Pro/Con arguments
Pros:
- Elegant, unified variational formulation of attention dynamics.
- Simple, fast, and potentially interpretable.
- Extends to dynamic scenes; promising qualitative tracking behavior.

Cons:
- Multiple technical errors/inconsistencies in the core derivations and bounds.
- Heavy reliance on center bias and ad hoc periodic modulation; no ablations.
- Insufficient experimental detail, missing tables, unclear baseline fairness.
- Lack of scanpath-level evaluations; saccade modeling not credible.
- Poor clarity and numerous typographical/notation issues; not reproducible.

Questions for the authors
1. Please correct Eq. (8) and the derivations in the Appendix; provide a consistent, dimensionally correct Lagrangian and re-derive the EL equations. Clarify the proper condition on λ (with a correct D expression).
2. Specify ω, m, k, solver, step size, and initialization distributions; describe computation of bt for videos.
3. Provide ablations: remove center bias (both preprocessing and post-hoc), remove blur term p, remove periodic modulation, remove B, and report impacts on MIT1003/CAT2000/SFU with statistical tests.
4. Report scanpath-level metrics and compare to human distributions.
5. Ensure fair baseline tuning and include strong modern baselines (e.g., DeepGaze II/III, SALICON, SAM) with the same center-bias handling.

Recommendation
Reject. The idea is intriguing, but the paper in its current form has substantial technical inaccuracies, incomplete and potentially biased evaluation, missing essential details, and clarity issues. With corrected derivations, rigorous ablations, fair comparisons, and improved writing, this work could be much stronger in a future submission.
