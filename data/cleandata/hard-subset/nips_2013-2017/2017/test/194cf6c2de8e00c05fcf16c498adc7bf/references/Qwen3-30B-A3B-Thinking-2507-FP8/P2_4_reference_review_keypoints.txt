- Proposes a novel variational framework (EYMOL) modeling visual attention as particle motion in a potential field governed by least-action principles.  
- Models attention dynamics via three principles: trajectory boundedness (retina constraints), curiosity-driven attraction to high-detail gradients, and brightness invariance for fixation/tracking.  
- Derives Euler-Lagrange equations that govern scanpath generation, with saliency maps produced as a byproduct rather than the primary output.  
- Rigorous mathematical derivation with sufficient appendix detail for reproducibility.  
- Evaluation on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000 (images) and SFU (video) using standard metrics (NSS, AUC-Judd) shows competitive performance.  
- Experimental design is sound and parameters are estimated via simulated annealing.  
- Writing and organization are exceptionally clear; mathematical development is accessible and well-motivated.  
- Original and elegant theoretical contribution, offering a physics-inspired alternative to saliency-focused models.  
- Limited comparison set—omits recent deep learning/transformer-based saliency models.  
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate the contributions of individual principles (curiosity vs. brightness invariance).  
- Practical impact is modest: competitive but not superior to state-of-the-art deep learning methods; parameter estimation could be more efficient.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution and clear presentation, with suggested improvements on comparisons, ablations, and discussion of integration with neural approaches.
