- Proposes EYMOL: a physics-inspired variational framework modeling eye movements as particle trajectories via Euler-Lagrange equations.  
- Identifies three driving principles: boundedness, curiosity (high gradients), and brightness invariance.  
- Produces scanpaths directly, with saliency maps as a byproduct.  
- Evaluated on standard datasets (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU) using NSS and AUC-Judd, showing competitive performance.  
- Technically rigorous with clear mathematical derivations and reproducible implementation details (appendix, simulated annealing, SciPy).  
- Lacks a comprehensive ablation study to isolate the impact of curiosity, brightness invariance, and boundedness.  
- Well-written and organized, effectively explaining the variational/physics connection.  
- Original contribution by applying variational principles to attention dynamics, unifying static and dynamic scene processing.  
- Distinguishes itself from saliency-first models by directly modeling attention dynamics.  
- Could better cite and compare to recent deep learning attention approaches.  
- Real-time capability is a practical advantage over many deep models.  
- Results are competitive but not state-of-the-art, limiting transformative impact.  
- Limited qualitative analysis of generated scanpaths and saliency maps.  
- Needs deeper discussion linking the model to human visual attention mechanisms beyond math.  
- Overall recommendation: accept at NIPS for a novel, technically sound contribution with minor suggested improvements.  
- Suggested improvements: add ablation study, include qualitative scanpath comparisons to humans, expand related work with recent deep learning methods, discuss real-world applications of real-time capability, and analyze failure cases.
