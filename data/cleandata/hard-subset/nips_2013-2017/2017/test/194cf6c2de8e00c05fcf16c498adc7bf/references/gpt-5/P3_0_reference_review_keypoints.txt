- Proposes a variational/least-action formulation of bottom-up visual attention where scanpaths follow Euler–Lagrange dynamics from an action with retina, curiosity, and brightness-invariance terms.  
- Saliency maps are obtained as visit-density aggregates from multiple randomized scanpath runs; parameters tuned via simulated annealing on CAT2000 subset.  

- Strength: Elegant dynamical/unified perspective casting saccades, fixation, and tracking as stationary points of a variational principle.  
- Strength: Brightness-invariance term links to optical-flow constraints and offers an interpretable mechanism for edge-following and tracking.  
- Strength: Producing scanpaths first and saliency as visit densities aligns with active-vision/trajectory-based accounts and is a refreshing contrast to end-to-end models.  
- Strength: Lightweight, grayscale-only model with derivations for exact and upper-bounded B terms and claimed real-time integration.

- Kinetic energy appears incorrectly written (K = 1/m ẋ^2 vs standard (m/2)||ẋ||^2), causing unit/scaling and stability-condition inconsistencies.  
- Appendix contains algebraic/notation errors (duplicated indices, scalar/vector inconsistencies, missing factors) that impede reproducibility.  
- Use of Euler–Lagrange with velocity-dependent potentials is valid but derivations must be carefully checked and clarified.  
- The alternation C(t,x)=||∇b||^2 cos^2(ωt)+||∇p||^2 sin^2(ωt) is ad hoc; choice of ω, sensitivity, and rationale for deterministic scheduling are unanalyzed.

- Preprocessing (grayscale conversion, Gaussian center weighting before gradient computation, central initializations) and post-hoc grid search for blur/center bias can inflate metrics and obscure the dynamics’ contribution.  
- Parameter tuning via simulated annealing on 100 images and per-dataset grid search risks overfitting; train/val/test separation is unclear.  
- Quantitative results are insufficiently documented in text; claims like “best score in half of the cases” lack full metric reporting across standard measures and baselines.  
- Video claims (tracking moving objects) are qualitative; quantitative tracking proxies or dynamic saliency metrics are missing.

- Brightness-invariance plus curiosity may produce edge-following/oscillations rather than semantically meaningful fixations; stability analysis and trajectory visualizations are needed.  
- Ignoring color and mid/high-level features limits competitiveness with deep-feature saliency models and requires rigorous benchmarking.  
- Implicit center bias via preprocessing undermines the claim that saliency maps are emergent; shuffled AUC and removing center weighting are necessary controls.

- Limited discussion of related dynamical/optimal-control/active-vision and saccade-generation literature (ideal observer models, dynamic neural fields, RL for gaze) weakens positioning.  
- No connection drawn between the Lagrangian terms and information-theoretic objectives (e.g., information gain, Fisher information), which could strengthen theoretical motivation.

- Core idea is sound and potentially impactful, but derivational inconsistencies and experimental confounds limit current quality and reproducibility.  
- Paper is readable but marred by typos, incomplete references, and notation issues; Appendix and algorithmic details need careful editing.  
- Originality: variational least-action perspective is relatively novel, though components are heuristic and need stronger grounding.  
- Significance depends on rigorous validation; current reliance on center bias limits practical impact.

- Suggested: Fix kinetic energy expression, re-derive stability conditions, and clean up all equations/notation.  
- Suggested: Provide full quantitative results with standard metrics (AUC, sAUC, NSS, CC, KL) and strong baselines including no-center-bias and shuffled AUC.  
- Suggested: Ablations for C and B terms, ω variation, removal of image-level center weighting, and varied initializations.  
- Suggested: Report SA-learned parameter values, sensitivity analyses, and cross-dataset generalization without retuning center/blur.  
- Suggested: For videos, add quantitative tracking analyses and compare scanpath statistics (saccade amplitudes, fixation durations) to human data.  
- Suggested: Discuss connections to information-theoretic objectives and active-vision/optimal-control literature.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject in current form; fix derivations, strengthen/fairify experiments, and provide richer scanpath analyses for a solid contribution.
