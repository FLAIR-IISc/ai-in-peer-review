Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a dynamical systems/variational formulation for bottom-up visual attention. A particle (focus of attention) evolves under an action S = ∫ (K − U) dt, where U comprises: (i) a boundary “retina” potential to keep trajectories in-bounds, (ii) a curiosity term that favors large spatial gradients of brightness and of a blurred image (alternated via cos^2/sin^2 weights), and (iii) a brightness invariance term enforcing the optical-flow constraint bt + bx·ẋ ≈ 0. The Euler–Lagrange (EL) equations yield a second-order ODE in x(t). Saliency maps are obtained by accumulating visits from many runs with randomized initial conditions; parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) are tuned by simulated annealing to maximize NSS on a subset of CAT2000. Experiments on MIT1003/MIT300/CAT2000 and SFU video report competitive saliency metrics.

This connects to classic saliency (Itti & Koch; Bruce & Tsotsos; Itti & Baldi), scanpath simulation (winner-take-all + IOR), dynamical/saccadic models (e.g., Le Meur & Liu), and optical-flow/brightness constancy (Horn–Schunck). The idea of deriving an attention law via least action is interesting, but there are significant technical and experimental issues.

Quality (technical soundness)
Major technical inconsistencies:
- Kinetic energy: K(ẋ) is given as 1/m · ẋ^2 (Eq. 8). Standard mechanics K = (m/2) ||ẋ||^2. The subsequent EL term d/dt ∂L/∂ẋ = m ẍ (Eq. 12) assumes K = (m/2)||ẋ||^2, not 1/m. As written, Eq. 8 is inconsistent with the derivations.
- Determinant/bounds: In the Appendix, the determinant D simplifies to m^2 − 2mλ(||bx||^2), because the 4λ^2(ac − b^2) term cancels. The text alternates between b^2x1 and b^2x2 and duplicates terms, then concludes λ < m/4 without a clear bound on ||bx||^2. The algebra, inequalities, and the final bound (Eqs. 40–47) are inconsistent and contain typos that undermine the well-posedness claim.
- Notation/derivatives: bx denotes spatial gradient, but bxx also appears (Hessian?) in Eq. 15; sometimes “bxẋ” denotes bx·ẋ. This ambiguity makes the EL derivation hard to follow and potentially incorrect.
- Smoothness: V(x) uses Iverson brackets, making V non-C2 at boundaries, yet EL derivations assume C2 functions. The consequences (e.g., discontinuous forces at borders) are not addressed.
- Brightness invariance approximation: The inequality in Eq. 6 is fine, but the resulting approximate L does not match the EL derived for the exact constraint. The equivalence of V1/V2 is asserted empirically, but the approximation’s effect on dynamics is not analyzed.
- Missing damping: The ODE has no explicit damping/viscosity; stability and realism rely on the velocity-dependent “potential” B(t,x,ẋ), yet the paper reports “numerically unstable and unrealistic trajectories” for some η, without analyzing stability or providing conditions.

Experimental and reproducibility issues:
- Center bias is injected twice: (a) the raw image b and blurred p are multiplied by a centered Gaussian blob, and (b) a post hoc grid search tunes blur radius and center σ on training to optimize AUC-Judd/NSS. This strongly favors metrics sensitive to center bias. Shuffled-AUC/sAUC (center-bias-controlled) is not reported.
- Initial conditions are “almost at the center” with small velocities, introducing additional center bias that is not ablated.
- SA details are insufficient: domain D, α, number of iterations, neighbor definition, acceptance probability P(·), and ω choice are not specified; only three parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) are tuned; mass m, ω, Gaussian blur levels for p are unspecified. No code is released.
- Video: How bt is computed (temporal derivatives, smoothing), frame rate, and any temporal filtering are omitted. Claims of real-time operation lack timing benchmarks.
- Evaluation: Tables are referenced but not shown. Metrics used are not comprehensive; stronger baselines (modern deep models) are underrepresented; dataset splits and avoidance of test-set tuning are unclear (MIT300 is blind; CAT2000 train/test are used, but the tuning/external validation protocol is not crisp).
- Scanpath claims (“tracking moving objects”) are anecdotal; no scanpath-level metrics (e.g., saccade amplitude distributions, fixation durations, scanpath similarity) are reported.

Clarity
The manuscript contains many typos and formatting issues (e.g., “st Conference…”, footnotes misnumbered, “eggs-chicken,” “interwound”), incomplete/inconsistent references (years/venues missing), and overloaded notation (bx, bxx). Key hyperparameters (m, ω, Gaussian σ), numerical solver details (step size, tolerances), and preprocessing specifics are missing. The Appendix contains algebraic errors and duplicated symbols that impede verification.

Originality
The least-action framing is a neat perspective, and unifying curiosity (spatial gradients) with a brightness-constancy constraint in one variational model is appealing. However, dynamic attention models with control/differential equations and fixation/IOR mechanisms exist, and brightness constancy is standard in optical flow. The conceptual novelty is moderate and would benefit from deeper positioning against prior dynamical/variational models of attention/active vision.

Significance
Given the technical inconsistencies and the heavy reliance on center bias and post-processing to obtain competitive AUC/NSS, the current evidence does not convincingly demonstrate advances over state of the art. The potential is there, especially for generating scanpaths and linking fixation/tracking, but stronger, bias-controlled evaluations and corrected derivations are needed.

Pros
- Elegant, unified variational formulation for attention dynamics.
- Derives scanpaths; saliency is a byproduct.
- Works on images and videos within one framework.
- Simple ingredients; interpretable terms.

Cons
- Key mathematical inconsistencies (kinetic energy; determinant bounds; notation).
- Incomplete/unclear hyperparameters and solver details; poor reproducibility.
- Double center bias and post-hoc tuning; no sAUC/CC/IG; limited baselines.
- Small/limited video evaluation; no scanpath-level validation.
- Many typos, formatting, and reference issues.

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix the Lagrangian: use K = (m/2)||ẋ||^2; re-derive EL cleanly with unambiguous vector notation; correct determinant algebra and parameter bounds; justify smoothness or regularize V near borders.
- Add damping and analyze stability; provide conditions on (ηb, ηp, λ, m, ω).
- Specify all parameters (m, ω, blur scales), SA protocol, derivative computations (bt), and solver settings; release code.
- Remove the input Gaussian center bias; report sAUC, CC, NSS, IG; include modern deep baselines; perform ablations (with/without post-blur/center bias, with only curiosity vs only brightness term).
- Evaluate scanpaths quantitatively against human data (saccade length distributions, fixation durations, scanpath similarity).
- Improve writing, notation, and references to NeurIPS standards.

Recommendation: Reject (in current form)
While the idea is promising, the paper has substantial technical and experimental deficiencies and clarity issues. A revised version that corrects the derivations, removes confounds, strengthens evaluation, and improves presentation could be of interest.
