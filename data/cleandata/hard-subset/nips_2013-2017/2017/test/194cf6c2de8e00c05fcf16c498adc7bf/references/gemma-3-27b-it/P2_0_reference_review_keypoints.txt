- Proposes EYMOL, a variational least-action model where attention balances “curiosity” and “brightness invariance” producing scanpaths via differential equations.  
- Saliency maps are computed as a byproduct of simulated scanpaths and evaluated on static (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000) and dynamic (SFU) benchmarks.  
- Positions itself against feature-integration and learning-based saliency models, offering a less common variational perspective inspired by infant attention.  
- Novelty: introduces a genuinely new variational framework for visual attention.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: derives Euler-Lagrange equations and connects attention modeling to least-action principles.  
- Unified framework: combines curiosity and brightness invariance to explain saccades and tracking within one model.  
- Competitive empirical performance on standard saliency benchmarks despite model simplicity.  
- Clear presentation and well-explained mathematical formulations (with some derivations in the appendix).  
- Performance depends on careful parameter tuning via simulated annealing; sensitivity and robustness of parameters are insufficiently analyzed.  
- Limited biological plausibility: connections to neurophysiology and neural correlates of proposed principles are underdeveloped.  
- Incomplete ablation: comparison of V1 vs V2 is narrow; contribution of individual Lagrangian terms is not thoroughly assessed.  
- Simplifying assumptions (grayscale inputs, center bias) may limit generalizability and are not fully explored.  
- Lacks qualitative scanpath visualizations to compare generated eye movements to human behavior.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound, clear, highly original, and potentially significant, but needs more analysis to strengthen claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical novelty, competitive results, clear derivations, and potential impact on attention modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter sensitivity, weak biological grounding, and insufficient ablation/sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add thorough ablation studies, discuss limitations and simplifying assumptions, include scanpath visualizations, elaborate neural correlates, and justify simulated annealing choices and convergence.
