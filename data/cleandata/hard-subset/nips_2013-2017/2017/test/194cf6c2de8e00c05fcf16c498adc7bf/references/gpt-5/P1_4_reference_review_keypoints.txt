- Paper proposes a variational Lagrangian formulation whose Euler–Lagrange ODEs generate scanpaths and saliency via trajectory visits in images/videos.
- Energy terms: image-bounding potential, “curiosity” attracting high-detail regions (time-varying cos^2/sin^2 mix of local and peripheral gradients), and a brightness-invariance penalty for optical-flow constancy.
- Saliency maps are obtained by integrating trajectories multiple times; parameters tuned by simulated annealing to maximize NSS.
- Strength: conceptually elegant unified dynamics combining curiosity and brightness constancy into a single model producing full scanpaths.
- Strength: model is simple, low-parameter, numerically tractable and reportedly real-time, evaluated across image and video datasets.
- Novelty: Lagrangian unification for scanpaths is interesting, though component ideas (gradient attraction, brightness constancy) are classical.
- Major technical error: inconsistency in kinetic energy—Eq.(8) vs. EL derivation implies wrong K(ẋ) and incorrect derived dynamics.
- Stability bound concern: determinant/stability derivation uses b∈[0,1] but depends on ∥∇b∥, so claimed λ < m/4 bound may not ensure D>0 in practice.
- Curiosity term is ad hoc: the cos^2/sin^2 alternation and unspecified ω lack principled justification or ablation evidence.
- Reproducibility issues: simulated annealing details (acceptance P, neighborhood, domain D, α) and many hyperparameters (m, k, ω, blur σ, integration step, initial variance) are unspecified.
- Strong center bias introduced twice (multiplying b,p by central Gaussian and center-biased initialization), with blur/center bias tuned to maximize metrics, risking inflated scores on center-biased datasets.
- Evaluation gaps: missing comparisons to strong modern deep saliency models, limited metrics, no ablations (e.g., without center bias), and no scanpath-level or quantitative tracking analyses.
- Video-specific methods (bt estimation) lack description; numerical differentiation and noise robustness not addressed.
- Clarity: generally readable but contains typos, ambiguous/incorrect equations, missing implementation and runtime details, and incomplete references/tables.
- Originality qualified: interesting unified derivation but relies on classical components and heuristic alternation; related dynamic saliency/variational work needs fuller discussion.
- Significance conditional: could be useful if corrected and rigorously evaluated, but current inconsistencies and evaluation choices reduce confidence and impact.
- Key author requests: fix kinetic energy and EL derivation; report exact L used in code and all hyperparameters/sensitivity analyses.
- Key author requests: explain ω, m, k, Gaussian center-bias choices and provide ablations for center bias, alternation schedule, and fixed combinations.
- Key author requests: detail bt computation for videos and test robustness to temporal noise.
- Key author requests: provide quantitative scanpath comparisons, tracking metrics, and expanded baselines (DeepGaze/SALICON/MLNet/GBVS) with standard metrics (AUC variants, NSS, CC, KL, SIM).
- Pros summary: elegant variational framework, direct scanpath output, simple and fast ODE integration, claimed competitive results.
- Cons summary: mathematical inconsistencies, heavy/tuned center bias, missing details affecting reproducibility, limited baselines and lacking scanpath/tracking evaluation.
- Overall assessment: borderline reject—idea promising but needs corrected mathematics, full implementation details, stronger/fairer evaluations, and ablations to be a solid contribution.
