- Overall assessment: novel and promising variational model of attention but insufficient experimental rigor; lean Reject with encouragement to revise.  
- Core idea: frames visual attention as emergent from a Lagrangian combining curiosity, brightness invariance, and retinal boundedness.  
- Novelty: unique least-action/variational approach unifying saccades and tracking within one model.  
- Theoretical foundation: provides Euler-Lagrange derivation and parameter estimation via simulated annealing.  
- Potential: claims real-time scanpath generation potential and emergent saliency as a byproduct.  
- Limited comparison: evaluation against state-of-the-art saliency models is superficial and incomplete.  
- Lack of ablations: no experiments isolating the contributions of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.  
- Parameter issues: simulated annealing details, search ranges, runtime, and theoretical/biological justification for parameters are missing.  
- Reproducibility gaps: insufficient implementation details (integration scheme, time step, initial conditions).  
- Overstated claims: asserting the model “captures mechanisms” of saccades is too strong given limited validation.  
- Clarity issues: some equation derivations (appendix) are hard to follow and need clearer exposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, promising benchmark performance, and potential to bridge physics-inspired and cognitive models.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak state-of-the-art comparison, missing ablations, reproducibility and parameter-justification gaps, and overstated claims.  
- Suggested revisions: add thorough comparisons (including deep models), ablation studies, parameter analysis, reproducibility details, qualitative scanpath visuals, and discussion of biological plausibility.
