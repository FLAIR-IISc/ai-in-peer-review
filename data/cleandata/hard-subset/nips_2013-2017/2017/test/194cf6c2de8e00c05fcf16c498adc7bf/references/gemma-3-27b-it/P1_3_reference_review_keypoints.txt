- Proposes EYMOL, a variational model of visual attention that unifies curiosity and brightness invariance and models scanpaths via Euler–Lagrange dynamics; saliency maps emerge as a byproduct.
- Positions the work relative to classic and ML saliency approaches, emphasizing a dynamic, physics-inspired scanpath generation and linking to least-action principles and developmental attention.
- Novel variational framework offering a different, potentially insightful perspective on attention modeling.
- Unified formalism intended to handle both static fixation and dynamic motion tracking in one model.
- Conceptually appealing idea that saliency emerges from scanpath dynamics rather than being explicitly optimized.
- Competitive performance on standard benchmarks despite the model’s relative simplicity and no explicit saliency training.
- Clear and reasonably detailed mathematical derivation of the model and Euler–Lagrange equations.
- Theoretical justification for choosing curiosity and brightness invariance is limited and could be better grounded in neural or cognitive evidence.
- Model relies on parameter tuning (simulated annealing, grid search) with unclear robustness and insufficient detail on parameter exploration.
- Use of heuristics (blurred brightness signal, Gaussian center bias) lacks strong theoretical grounding and their effects need deeper analysis.
- Ablation studies are limited; comparison between V1 and V2 is useful but insufficient to isolate each Lagrangian component’s contribution.
- Lacks qualitative analysis and visualization of generated scanpaths to compare behavior with human fixations.
- Claims about runtime feasibility are not supported by explicit computational timing or cost details.
- Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, competitive results, unified static/dynamic framework, and clear math.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification, reliance on parameter tuning/heuristics, and missing qualitative scanpath analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: expand theoretical justification for chosen principles; detail parameter tuning and include sensitivity analysis; add qualitative scanpath visualizations and comparisons with human data; report computational cost and runtimes.
