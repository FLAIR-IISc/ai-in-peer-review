This paper presents a novel model of visual attention, termed EYMOL, grounded in variational principles of boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance. The core idea is to model attention as the result of minimizing an action integral, leading to differential equations that govern scanpath generation. This contrasts with many existing saliency models that rely on global computation of saliency maps, and aims to directly model eye movements rather than inferring them from saliency. The paper includes experimental validation on both static images and dynamic scenes, comparing against established state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-motivated, contextualizing the proposed approach within the longstanding debate regarding bottom-up and top-down attention mechanisms and highlighting the limitations of current saliency map-based approaches. The mathematical formulation of the model is rigorous, and the derivation of the Euler-Lagrange equations is detailed (albeit in the Appendix). The experimental setup is reasonable, employing standard datasets (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU) and evaluation metrics (AUC-Judd, NSS). The inclusion of both static image and video experiments strengthens the evaluation.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty of Approach:** The variational formulation of visual attention is a fresh perspective, moving beyond traditional saliency map computation. This offers a potentially more biologically plausible and theoretically grounded model.
* **Mathematical Rigor:** The derivation of the model and the Euler-Lagrange equations are mathematically sound and clearly presented (in the Appendix).
* **Unified Framework:** The model elegantly combines principles of curiosity and brightness invariance within a single, coherent framework.
* **Direct Scanpath Modeling:**  The approach focuses on generating scanpaths directly, potentially capturing more nuanced dynamics of eye movements than saliency map-based methods.
* **Competitive Performance:** The model achieves competitive results on standard datasets, demonstrating its practical viability.
* **Clear Writing (mostly):** The introduction and conclusions are well-written and provide a clear overview of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The model relies on several parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) that require careful tuning using simulated annealing. The justification for the specific parameter search space and the algorithm itself could be expanded upon. The discussion of numerical stability issues related to parameter selection is also somewhat cursory.
* **Complexity vs. Benefit:** While mathematically elegant, the model’s complexity introduces computational overhead compared to simpler saliency map methods. The practical benefits of this complexity, especially in real-time applications, are not fully demonstrated.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:**  The comparison between versions V1 and V2 (approximated vs. exact brightness invariance) is useful, but further ablation studies to assess the individual contributions of each principle (boundedness, curiosity, brightness invariance) would strengthen the analysis.
* **Lack of Detailed Scanpath Analysis:** While the model generates scanpaths, the paper primarily focuses on saliency map comparisons. A more in-depth analysis of the generated scanpaths themselves – comparing them to human scanpaths in terms of features like saccade length, fixation duration, and scanpath smoothness – would provide stronger evidence of the model's biological plausibility.
* **Clarity in Some Sections:** The mathematical sections (especially in the Appendix) can be dense and challenging to follow for readers not deeply familiar with variational calculus.  More intuitive explanations and visual aids could improve readability.
* **Limited Discussion of Failure Cases:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of scenarios where the model performs poorly and potential reasons for these failures.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to visual attention modeling.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on standard datasets.
* The work has the potential to advance our understanding of the underlying mechanisms of visual attention.
* The unified framework provides a compelling alternative to existing approaches.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The model’s complexity and parameter sensitivity may limit its practical applicability.
* The lack of detailed scanpath analysis and ablation studies weakens the empirical validation.
* The mathematical sections may be inaccessible to a broad audience.
* While competitive, the performance gains over existing methods are not dramatic enough to warrant immediate acceptance given the increased complexity.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a promising paper that introduces a novel and mathematically rigorous approach to visual attention modeling. While the model demonstrates competitive performance, its complexity and limited analysis of scanpath dynamics raise concerns. The paper would benefit from more detailed ablation studies, a more in-depth scanpath analysis, and improved clarity in the mathematical sections. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly focusing on simplifying the model (if possible), conducting more thorough ablation studies, and providing a more detailed analysis of the generated scanpaths. If these improvements are made, the paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to the field.
