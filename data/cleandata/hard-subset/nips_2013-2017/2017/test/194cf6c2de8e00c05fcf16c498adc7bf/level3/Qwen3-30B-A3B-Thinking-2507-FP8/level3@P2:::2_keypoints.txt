- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention based on the Least Action Principle, modeling attention as a particle trajectory under boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.
- Derives Euler–Lagrange equations for attention dynamics and numerically solves them to produce scanpaths, with saliency maps emerging as a byproduct.
- Evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000 (images) and SFU (video), showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.
- Theoretical contribution: reframes attention modeling using physics-inspired variational principles, unifying bottom-up attention and object tracking.
- Technical quality: mathematically rigorous derivation but presentation and explanation of the model are unclear in places.
- Clarity issues: many variables/functions (e.g., C(t,x), B(t,x,ẋ)) are introduced without definition and the exposition is hard to follow.
- Parameter estimation uses simulated annealing, but the paper lacks details on the search procedure and justification of chosen values.
- The claim that the brightness invariance approximation suffices for static images is stated without adequate supporting analysis.
- Originality is high, but the paper omits relevant psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
- Strengths: novel physics-inspired framework, competitive benchmark performance, computational efficiency, unified static/dynamic treatment, saliency as emergent property.
- Weaknesses: poor writing and organization, insufficient variable definitions, overly long appendix in the main paper, missing psychological citations, and some unsupported claims.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on major revisions to address clarity, define terms, move appendix to supplementary material, connect to psychological literature, and improve writing.
