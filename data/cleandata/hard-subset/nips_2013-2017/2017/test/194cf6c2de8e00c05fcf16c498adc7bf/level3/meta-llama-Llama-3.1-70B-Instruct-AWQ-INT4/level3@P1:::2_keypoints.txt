- Presents a novel variational energy-based model for visual attention combining a harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance term.
- Model is derived from a generalized least-action principle and yields differential equations predicting eye movements from initial position and velocity.
- Evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets with performance comparable to state-of-the-art models.
- Offers a unified, formal perspective valuable for vision and cognitive science and saliency/ bottom-up eye-movement modeling.
- Strength: captures underlying mechanisms of visual attention within a unified framework and provides thorough experimental analysis.
- Strength: variational-principle derivation gives a solid theoretical foundation.
- Weakness: poor writing and organization make the paper hard to follow.
- Weakness: explanations of the model and its components are unclear; many abbreviations and variables/functions are undefined.
- Weakness: appendix is overly lengthy and should be moved to supplementary material to free space in the main text.
- Weakness: limited discussion connecting results to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
- Originality: variational approach is novel, but energy-based prediction of eye movements is not entirely new and the relationship to prior work should be clarified.
- Significance: potential for substantial impact across vision science, HCI, robotics, and neuroscience if revisions are made.
- Recommendations: proofread and reorganize, define all abbreviations/variables, move technical derivations to supplementary material, and place results in context of existing theories/models.
