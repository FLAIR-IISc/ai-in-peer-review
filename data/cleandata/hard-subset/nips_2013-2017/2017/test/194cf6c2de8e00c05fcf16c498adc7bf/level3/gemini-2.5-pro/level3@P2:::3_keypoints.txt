- Introduces EYMOL, a physics-inspired model generating eye-movement scanpaths via a variational Least Action Principle.  
- Models scanpath dynamics directly rather than using saliency-then-selection heuristics.  
- Constructs a Lagrangian with kinetic energy and a generalized potential combining boundary constraint, curiosity (image gradients), and brightness-invariance.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange differential equations to produce attention trajectories.  
- Provides algorithm for parameter estimation and produces saliency maps by aggregating simulated visitations.  
- Validated on standard eye-tracking datasets (MIT300, CAT2000, SFU) for images and video.  
- Quantitative results show competitive performance against representative models on several metrics.  
- Theoretical framework is coherent and technically solid at a high level; some derivation details in the appendix are hard to independently verify.  
- Paper is well written, clearly organized, and motivates the approach effectively despite mathematical complexity.  
- Highly original: applies Least Action Principle to unify saccades and smooth pursuit within one framework.  
- Potentially significant for vision science and computational neuroscience, offering a new foundational theory for eye-movement generation.  
- Possible weaknesses: uncertain fit for the general ML/NIPS audience and complex physics derivations that may be hard for reviewers to verify.
