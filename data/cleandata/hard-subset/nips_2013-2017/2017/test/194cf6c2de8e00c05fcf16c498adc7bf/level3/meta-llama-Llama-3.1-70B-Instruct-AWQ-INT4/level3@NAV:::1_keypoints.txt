- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL): a variational, least-action model combining bottom-up and top-down attention to generate scanpaths.
- Introduces "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) to drive scanpath dynamics.
- Derives differential equations of visual attention from a generalized Lagrangian framework.
- Novel and interesting fusion of physics and cognitive science principles for attention modeling.
- Variational framework is a unique contribution that differentiates the approach.
- Model is simple yet achieves competitive saliency-detection results against state-of-the-art methods.
- Affordances appear ad hoc and lack sufficient motivation or justification.
- Least Action Principle derivation should be connected to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).
- Biological motivation is limited and underdeveloped.
- Overall performance is mixed and generally worse than benchmark deep saliency models in some respects.
- Paper lacks main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths and needs more dynamic/scanpath analyses.
- Technically sound and well-organized with clear mathematical notation and presentation.
- Claims are supported by theory and experiments but the model’s limitations and potential biases need fuller discussion.
- Recommendations: better motivate affordances biologically, link derivation to eye-movement literature, add dynamic/scanpath analyses, and discuss limitations/biases.
- Recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions pending the above improvements.
