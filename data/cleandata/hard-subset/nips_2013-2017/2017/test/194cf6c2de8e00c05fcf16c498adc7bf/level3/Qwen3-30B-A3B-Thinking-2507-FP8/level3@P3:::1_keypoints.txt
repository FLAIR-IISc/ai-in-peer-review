- Proposes a novel variational model framing eye movements via a Least Action Principle, modeling attention as a particle in a potential from three principles: trajectory boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.  
- Mathematical formulation and Euler–Lagrange derivation are technically sound and clearly presented (appendix).  
- Conceptually interesting unification of curiosity and brightness invariance into a single variational framework.  
- Brightness invariance enables handling of dynamic scenes, offering promise for video attention modeling.  
- Parameter estimation using simulated annealing is reasonable and justified.  
- The proposed affordances (curiosity, brightness invariance) lack strong biological or cognitive motivation and feel ad hoc.  
- Fails to connect the Least Action Principle derivation to existing eye-movement/optimal-control literature (e.g., Wolpert et al.).  
- Empirical results are mixed: competitive on some metrics (best in half of MIT300 cases) but generally underperform relative to state-of-the-art deep models.  
- Crucially missing scanpath visualizations and quantitative scanpath comparisons to human data, so core claim about capturing scanpath dynamics is unvalidated.  
- Object-tracking capability in dynamic scenes is not convincingly demonstrated beyond brief mention.  
- Paper is well-written and organized but would benefit from moving key visualizations into the main text.  
- Original application of variational principles is novel, but the formulation needs stronger grounding in cognitive science and clearer distinction from prior optimal-control work.  
- Practical significance is limited by weaker performance and insufficient evidence that saliency maps naturally emerge from the model.  
- Recommendation: strengthen motivation for affordances, include scanpath visualizations, compare model scanpaths to human data, and connect to eye-movement literature.  
- Overall: promising theoretical framework and technically sound, but requires substantial empirical and conceptual revisions before being compelling for NIPS.
