- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a novel visual-attention model based on a generalized Least Action Principle for dynamic scenes.
- Model driven by three principles: harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance term.
- Authors derive a cost function and Euler–Lagrange differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.
- Paper presents a thorough, rigorous mathematical formulation of the model.
- Experimental results validate the model on saliency detection for static and dynamic scenes, showing competitive performance.
- Strength: provides a novel physics-inspired perspective combining computer vision and theoretical principles.
- Strength: mathematically rigorous and well-developed foundation.
- Strength: promising empirical results that can compete with state-of-the-art.
- Weakness: writing is often unclear and convoluted, making arguments difficult to follow.
- Weakness: key variables and functions are not explicitly defined, especially in Section 3 and equations.
- Weakness: two-page appendix should be moved to supplementary material to free space for clearer definitions in the main text.
- Weakness: paper lacks sufficient connections and comparisons to psychological literature on optimal eye movements and integration possibilities.
- NIPS scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, definitions, and literature integration.
