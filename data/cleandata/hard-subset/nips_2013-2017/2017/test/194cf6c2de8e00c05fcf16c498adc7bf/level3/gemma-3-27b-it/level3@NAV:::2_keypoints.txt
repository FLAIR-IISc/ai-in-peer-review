- Proposes a novel variational/energy-based model of visual attention that models scanpaths by minimizing an action integral with terms for retinal boundaries (harmonic oscillator), curiosity (multi-scale brightness changes), and brightness invariance.  
- Derives differential equations governing eye movements from this principled, physics-inspired framework.  
- Validated quantitatively on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets, achieving performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: creative connection to the Least Action Principle and a formal alternative to feature- or learning-based saliency models.  
- Builds on, but differs from, classic saliency models (Itti et al., Judd et al.) by emphasizing theoretical grounding rather than empirical optimization.  
- Major weakness: paper is poorly written and organized; Section 3 and mathematical derivations are dense and hard to follow.  
- Many abbreviations and variables are introduced without clear definitions, hindering comprehension.  
- Recommendation to move the two-page appendix to supplementary material to free space for clearer explanations in the main text.  
- Missing discussion/connection to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008); authors should compare and contrast with those findings.  
- Overall verdict: promising idea but underdeveloped — reject with encouragement to substantially rewrite for clarity, completeness, and contextualization before resubmission.
