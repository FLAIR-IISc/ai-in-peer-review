- Proposes EYMOL: a dynamical, bottom-up visual-attention model from a variational least-action formulation producing scanpaths and saliency maps by visitation counts.
- Lagrangian includes kinetic term, retinal confinement potential, a “curiosity” term alternating attraction to local/peripheral gradients, and a brightness-invariance term from optical flow.
- Euler–Lagrange equations generate scanpaths; saliency maps derived from many simulated trajectories and parameters tuned via simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN subset.
- Evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU video dataset; approximate and exact brightness-invariance variants perform similarly on static images.
- Performance is competitive with classic baselines and one CNN on some metrics/datasets but generally lags modern deep saliency methods on others.
- Contrasts with saliency-map-centric and learning-based approaches; loosely related to dynamic scanpath generators but lacks connection to optimal control/motor-control literature (Wolpert/Todorov).
- Strength: principled dynamical formulation unifying fixation, saccades, and tracking within a single equation set.
- Strength: technically careful derivation with Appendix clarifying implicit ẍ dependence and well-posedness conditions.
- Strength: brightness-invariance term connects to classic optical flow and plausibly explains contour fixation and motion tracking.
- Strength: empirical coverage of static and dynamic datasets; V1 vs V2 comparison indicates approximate invariance works for static scenes.
- Strength: computationally light and potentially suitable for real-time use.
- Weakness: “affordance” components (curiosity alternation, brightness invariance) appear ad hoc and lack biological grounding, ablations, or supporting data.
- Weakness: limited engagement with established eye-movement optimality principles (effort/noise tradeoffs) despite least-action framing.
- Weakness: unconventional kinetic term K(ẋ)=1/m||ẋ||² departs from standard form, flipping interpretation of m and requiring dimensional/stability clarification.
- Weakness: evaluation emphasizes saliency scores but omits detailed scanpath analyses (saccade amplitudes, fixation durations, MultiMatch/string-edit metrics, temporal correlations).
- Weakness: center-bias and blur tuned to inflate AUC-Judd/NSS may bias results; fairness versus learned baselines is not fully addressed.
- Weakness: comparisons omit several state-of-the-art deep models (e.g., DeepGaze II, SALICON, ML-Net/SAM), and overall performance is mixed below top deep methods.
- Weakness: simulated annealing details (acceptance function, proposal, bounds) and sensitivity analyses are sparse; parameter stability issues (ηb, ηp) are not quantified.
- Quality: mathematical development is mostly sound but kinetic energy choice and parameter scaling need units analysis and ablation to ensure they do not drive results.
- Quality: well-posedness condition λ < m/4 is derived under specific normalizations; practical enforcement and interaction with ω and blur should be documented.
- Quality: learning/tuning pipeline risks overfitting to a small training subset; cross-validation and variance across runs should be reported.
- Clarity: paper needs figures of representative trajectories, temporal fixation heatmaps, and histograms comparing saccade amplitudes and fixation durations to human data.
- Clarity: SA procedure should be clarified with acceptance probability, neighbor generation, α schedule, and pseudocode with concrete settings.
- Clarity: notation should be consolidated (consistent vector norms) and minor typos corrected.
- Originality: least-action formulation and periodic mixing of local vs peripheral gradients are novel contributions to saliency literature.
- Originality caveat: paper should better differentiate itself from existing dynamical saliency/optimal control models with explicit comparisons and citations.
- Significance: offers a mechanistic, interpretable alternative to black-box models with potential, but impact is limited without stronger scanpath analyses and SOTA benchmarks.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to ω/σ/center-bias, effect of removing curiosity alternation, scanpath metrics (e.g., MultiMatch), and robustness of tracking with noisy bt or multiple moving objects.
- Suggestions: compare to stronger deep models, provide comprehensive scanpath characterizations and visualizations, justify kinetic term and relate to oculomotor optimality, and include failure cases.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled interpretable dynamical framework, neat variational derivation, plausible brightness-invariance mechanism, and competitive performance vs classic baselines with low compute.
- Arguments against acceptance: mixed empirical performance, ad hoc components lacking biological/optimal-control grounding, missing scanpath-level evaluation and ablations, and ambiguity around kinetic term and parameter scaling.
