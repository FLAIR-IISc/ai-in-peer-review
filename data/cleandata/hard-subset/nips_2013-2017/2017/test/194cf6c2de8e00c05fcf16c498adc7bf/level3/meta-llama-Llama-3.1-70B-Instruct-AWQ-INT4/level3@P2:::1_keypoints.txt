- Proposes a novel paradigm for visual attention based on variational laws and a generalized Least Action Principle, deriving Euler–Lagrange equations for eye-movements.  
- Model is driven by three principles: boundedness of trajectory, curiosity-driven exploration, and brightness invariance.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with understandable mathematical derivations.  
- Offers a unified framework that models scanpath dynamics, improving over traditional saliency-only approaches.  
- Performance on benchmarks is mixed but generally competitive with state-of-the-art deep saliency models.  
- Introduction of "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) feels ad hoc and insufficiently motivated or justified.  
- Biological motivation and connection to existing eye-movement literature are limited and should be strengthened.  
- Lacks main-paper figures and dynamic analyses characterizing generated scanpaths, making model dynamics hard to visualize and assess.  
- Needs more explicit comparisons to existing models and a clearer roadmap for future work.  
- Overall an interesting, original contribution with potential to inspire follow-up research despite empirical and motivation weaknesses.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
