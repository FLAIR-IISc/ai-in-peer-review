**Paper Review: Variational Laws of Visual Attention for Dynamic Scenes**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel model of visual attention based on the principle of least action, which combines bottom-up and top-down processes to capture scanpath dynamics. The model, called EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), introduces "affordances" such as curiosity and brightness invariance to drive scanpaths. The authors derive differential equations of visual attention using a generalized Lagrangian and apply the model to saliency detection tasks on images and videos.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents a novel and interesting approach to modeling visual attention, combining concepts from physics and cognitive science.
* The use of a variational framework to derive the differential equations of visual attention is a unique and valuable contribution.
* The model's performance on saliency detection tasks is competitive with state-of-the-art models, despite its simplicity.

**Weaknesses**

* The "affordances" introduced in the model, such as curiosity and brightness invariance, seem somewhat ad hoc and insufficiently motivated.
* The Least Action Principle derivation should be connected to existing eye-movement literature, such as the work of the Wolpert lab.
* The biological motivation of the model is limited, which is less critical given the benchmark-driven deep models, but still noteworthy.
* The model's performance is mixed and generally worse compared to benchmark deep saliency models.
* The paper lacks main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths, and more dynamic/scanpath analyses are needed.

**Originality**

* The paper presents a novel approach to modeling visual attention, combining concepts from physics and cognitive science.
* The use of a variational framework to derive the differential equations of visual attention is a unique and valuable contribution.

**Significance**

* The paper addresses a difficult problem in visual attention modeling, providing a new perspective on the underlying mechanisms.
* The model's performance on saliency detection tasks is competitive with state-of-the-art models, despite its simplicity.
* The paper has the potential to inspire new research in visual attention modeling and cognitive science.

**Quality**

* The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-organized presentation of the model and its application.
* The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* However, the model's limitations and potential biases should be more thoroughly discussed.

**Clarity**

* The paper is clearly written, with a well-organized structure and adequate explanations of the model and its application.
* The use of mathematical notation is clear and consistent throughout the paper.

**Recommendations**

* The authors should provide more thorough explanations of the "affordances" introduced in the model and their biological motivation.
* The Least Action Principle derivation should be connected to existing eye-movement literature.
* More dynamic/scanpath analyses are needed to fully evaluate the model's performance.
* The authors should discuss the model's limitations and potential biases more thoroughly.

**Acceptance**

* Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
* The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above and provide more thorough explanations and analyses to support their claims.
