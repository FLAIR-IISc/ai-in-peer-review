- Paper proposes a variational, energy-based scanpath generator (EYMOL) producing saliency maps as a byproduct via Euler–Lagrange equations from a generalized action.
- Lagrangian terms: harmonic boundary potential, alternating local/peripheral curiosity term, and brightness-invariance (optical-flow) term for fixation/motion tracking.
- Authors derive explicit second-order differential equations, estimate parameters with simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN, and compute saliency via visit-count aggregation.
- Method attains comparable performance to baselines on MIT1003/CAT2000/MIT300 and SFU using only grayscale and simple features.
- Contrasts with saliency-first models by making saliency emergent from mechanistic scanpath dynamics.
- Relation to prior work: inverts saliency-driven scanpath pipelines and connects brightness-invariance to Horn–Schunck optical flow.
- Should better situate within optimal/active sensing literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler) to explain when least-action approximates optimal behavior.
- Technical soundness: Euler–Lagrange derivation is careful, with a well-posedness condition λ < m/4.
- Major technical issue: kinetic energy term appears nonstandard (uses 1/m ||ẋ||^2 instead of 1/2 m ||ẋ||^2); units and interpretation of m must be corrected.
- Unjustified modelling choices: cosine/sine modulation in C(t,x) and selection of ω lack justification and sensitivity/stability analysis.
- Brightness-invariance approximation for static images is only empirically validated; needs error bounds or clearer equivalence conditions.
- Experimental design concern: parameter tuning (SA and grid search for blur/center bias) on same dataset risks inflating center-biased metrics (AUC, NSS).
- Request to report results without added center bias and with fixed blur, and to clarify video hyperparameter tuning (held-out subset?).
- Multiple initializations near image center induce center bias; quantify their effect.
- Missing ablations: test ηb/ηp/λ and provide term-removal studies (V only; V+C; V+B; V+C+B) and vary ω.
- Evaluation scope limited: focus on saliency metrics while core output is scanpaths — add scanpath-level metrics (saccade amplitude, fixation durations, MultiMatch, string-edit) and motion-tracking success.
- Clarity issues: manuscript needs proofreading, consistent notation, and earlier definition of symbols (bx, px, bt, ω, m, λ, ηb, ηp, li).
- Suggest moving lengthy derivations to supplement and use space to explain modeling choices, units, parameter ranges, initial/boundary conditions, and numerical integration details (step size, solver, runtime).
- Preprocessing should be explicitly described and discussed (grayscale conversion, Gaussian peripheral channel, multiplicative center bias) including interactions with evaluation metrics.
- Originality: novel least-action formulation unifying curiosity and brightness-invariance; alternating local/peripheral gradient is an interesting trap-avoidance mechanism.
- Significance: interpretable, lightweight model producing continuous-time scanpaths with competitive saliency performance, though limited by grayscale/simple features and tuned center bias.
- Suggested improvements: ablations, results with/without center bias and blur, justify/correct kinetic energy term and units, add scanpath metrics and qualitative trajectory analyses.
- Recommend situating work in active sensing/RL/Bayesian surprise literature and releasing code + parameters for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: novel variational attention dynamics, interpretable equations and stability conditions, competitive benchmarks with a simple fast model.
- Cons for acceptance: clarity and notation errors, probable kinetic-energy typo, heavy reliance on center bias/blur, limited ablations, and evaluation focused on saliency not scanpaths.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing clarity, ablations, scanpath analyses, kinetic-energy correction, and hyperparameter-tuning transparency.
