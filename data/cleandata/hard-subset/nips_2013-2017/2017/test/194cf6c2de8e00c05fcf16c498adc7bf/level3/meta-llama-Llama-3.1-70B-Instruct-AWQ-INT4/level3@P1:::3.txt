This paper, "Variational Laws of Visual Attention for Dynamic Scenes," proposes a novel approach to modeling eye movements, dubbed EYMOL (EYe MOvement Laws). The authors provide a thorough review of the current state of the art in visual attention modeling, correctly summarizing the key concepts and existing approaches. The reviewer, while not an expert in the field, found the summary to be accurate and helpful in understanding the context of the proposed model.

The EYMOL approach differs significantly from existing saliency-map-based models, which typically rely on optimization or maximization techniques to select fixation points. Instead, EYMOL models gaze trajectories directly using a "Least Action Principle," which is a generalization of the principle used in physics. The mathematical details of the model, particularly in the appendix, were challenging for the reviewer to evaluate due to their limited physics background. However, the reviewer was able to grasp the key concepts and how they differ from prior models.

The authors evaluate their model on real eye-movement datasets and compare its performance to several alternative models. The results are generally favorable, although not dominant on every metric. This suggests that the model is valid and has merit for the perception sciences.

The reviewer believes that this paper is likely of interest to the vision science community, such as attendees of the Vision Sciences Society (VSS) conference. However, the reviewer is less convinced that the paper fits the significance criteria for NIPS, but defers to more expert reviewers for a final assessment.

Overall, the paper presents a novel theory that could inspire further empirical research in the field of visual attention. The reviewer's main criticism is that the paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the mathematical concepts, particularly for readers without a strong physics background.
