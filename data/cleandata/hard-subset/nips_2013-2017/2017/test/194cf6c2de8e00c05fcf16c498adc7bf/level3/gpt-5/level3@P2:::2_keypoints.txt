- Proposes a variational, energy-based dynamical model for focus of attention (FOA) in static and dynamic scenes, deriving Euler–Lagrange equations for FOA trajectories.
- Lagrangian combines a boundary potential, a time-oscillating curiosity term (cos²/sin² on gradients of raw vs. blurred images), and a brightness-invariance term akin to optical flow.
- Parameters tuned via simulated annealing on CAT2000; saliency maps formed by counting pixel visitations over many simulated scanpaths.
- Empirical results on MIT1003/CAT2000 (images) and SFU (videos) are competitive with several classical and recent baselines.
- Conceptual novelty: a physics-inspired, interpretable variational formalization unifying saccadic exploration and tracking.
- Provides derivations for static and dynamic scenes and a well-posedness condition (λ < m/4).
- Saliency emerges as a byproduct of FOA dynamics rather than optimized directly, which is scientifically appealing.
- Writing and clarity are poor: many symbols/functions undefined and the presentation needs proofreading and consolidation.
- Strong center bias likely introduced by multiplying inputs with a center Gaussian and initializing runs near the center; this may inflate AUC-Judd and NSS.
- Missing evaluation metrics and baselines: should report sAUC, Information Gain against center-prior, full metrics (AUC, sAUC, NSS, CC, KL, IG) with CIs and compare to modern deep models.
- Experimental transparency issues: tables referenced but values omitted; need to include exact numbers and baselines for videos.
- No ablation or sensitivity analyses for key design choices (curiosity term, brightness invariance, cos²/sin² alternation, ω, Gaussian center weighting, number/duration of runs, parameters ηb, ηp, λ, m).
- Scanpath claims unsupported: lacks quantitative scanpath analyses (saccade amplitude/direction, main sequence, fixation durations, MultiMatch/string-edit distances, tracking benchmarks).
- Simulated annealing procedure under-specified (α, proposals, acceptance, stopping); potential overfitting to CAT2000-TRAIN without cross-validation.
- Numerical implementation underspecified: spatial derivative estimation, solver choice/tolerances, step sizes, runtime, and stability analysis not detailed.
- Some mathematical choices (non-smooth boundary penalty, time-oscillating curiosity) feel ad hoc and need justification or ablation.
- Positioning within cognitive/optimal vision literature is shallow; should better relate to Najemnik & Geisler and other active vision work.
- Reproducibility plausible in principle but requires more specifics and code release.
- Recommendations: improve exposition, define all symbols, report sAUC/IG and center-prior baseline, provide full ablations and scanpath analyses, justify cos²/sin², smooth boundary penalty, detail SA/ODE settings, and release code.
- Key open questions for authors: exact parameter values/ranges (m, λ, ηb, ηp, ω), sensitivity to these, contribution of center bias, whether trajectories match human saccade statistics, number of runs needed for saliency convergence, and runtime/solver details for videos.
- Pros: original, interpretable variational model; unified static/dynamic treatment; competitive performance without heavy learning.
- Cons: clarity/definition issues, potential center-bias evaluation confounds, missing ablations and scanpath validation, and under-specified optimization/numerical details.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline (leaning weak accept) conditional on addressing clarity, evaluation metrics, and ablations.
