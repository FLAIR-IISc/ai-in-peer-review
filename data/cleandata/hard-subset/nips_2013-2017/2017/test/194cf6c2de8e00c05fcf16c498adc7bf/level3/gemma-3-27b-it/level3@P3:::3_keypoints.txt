- Proposes EYMOL, a novel eye-movement model using the Least Action variational principle to directly model gaze trajectories.  
- Contrasts with prior models that rely on saliency maps and fixation selection optimization.  
- Core idea is original and represents a significant departure from conventional approaches.  
- Unified framework attempts to combine curiosity-driven movements and brightness invariance.  
- Evaluated on multiple real eye-tracking datasets (MIT300, CAT2000, SFU) with comparisons to several baselines.  
- Experimental results are generally favorable, supporting the model’s validity though not uniformly superior across all metrics.  
- Mathematical formalism is rigorous but dense, reducing accessibility for readers without a physics background.  
- Appendix contains crucial but hard-to-follow derivations; needs more intuitive explanations and visual aids.  
- Parameter estimation via simulated annealing is standard but lacks detailed justification for parameter ranges and annealing schedule.  
- Technically sound based on reviewer’s understanding, with claims supported by experiments.  
- Paper is complete and well-structured, but clarity suffers from mathematical complexity.  
- Originality is the paper’s strongest attribute; literature is adequately cited.  
- Significance to the NIPS community is questionable—more directly relevant to vision science and cognitive neuroscience.  
- Potential to inspire further empirical and computational research despite limited immediate impact on core NIPS topics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theoretical foundation, comprehensive evaluation, favorable results, and potential to spur future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, unclear NIPS relevance, inconsistent improvement over existing methods, and insufficient parameter-selection justification.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance for originality and potential, but defer definitive judgment on mathematical details to expert reviewers; improve accessibility and clarify impact.
