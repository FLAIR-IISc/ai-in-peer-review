- Proposes a novel variational model of scanpath generation using a Least Action Principle and a Lagrangian formulation.  
- Lagrangian includes terms for trajectory boundedness, curiosity (image detail), and brightness invariance.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange equations to model attentional dynamics in a biologically plausible, computationally tractable way.  
- Emphasizes modeling the dynamic process of scanpath creation rather than static saliency maps.  
- Validated on image and video datasets and compared to established benchmarks.  
- Core idea is intriguing and could offer a unifying theoretical framework for attention.  
- Inclusion of affordances like curiosity and brightness invariance is a commendable attempt to inject meaningful principles.  
- Justification for chosen affordances feels ad hoc and lacks deeper theoretical or physiological grounding.  
- Weak connection to existing literature on optimal control and Least Action Principle in motor control (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Performance is mixed and generally lags behind deep learning–based saliency models.  
- Evaluation focuses on aggregate metrics (NSS, AUC) without detailed analysis of generated scanpaths.  
- Major omission: no visualizations or examples showing dynamic scanpath trajectories and temporal behavior.  
- Biological motivation is limited and could be strengthened with neurophysiological links.  
- Recommendation: interesting and thought-provoking but requires substantial revision; currently not acceptable—encouraged to resubmit after addressing theoretical, empirical, and presentation weaknesses.
