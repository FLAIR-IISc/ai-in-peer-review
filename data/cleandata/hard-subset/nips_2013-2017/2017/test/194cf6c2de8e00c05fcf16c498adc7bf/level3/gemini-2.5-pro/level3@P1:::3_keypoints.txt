- Proposes EYMOL, a physics-inspired variational model that models eye movement trajectories by minimizing a generalized action functional.  
- Lagrangian comprises kinetic energy plus a potential with three principles: boundedness (stay inside image), curiosity (attract to high image gradients), and brightness invariance (encourage fixation/tracking).  
- Scanpaths are generated by numerically solving Euler–Lagrange differential equations; saliency maps are produced by accumulating trajectory density.  
- Frames the work as a fundamental departure from static saliency-map + winner-take-all approaches.  
- High originality and theoretical elegance: unifies saccades, fixation, and tracking under a variational principle.  
- Produces generative, dynamic scanpaths, addressing temporal dynamics often ignored by standard saliency models.  
- Strong empirical validation: competitive results on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU despite no end-to-end learning.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; main ideas are accessible though mathematical derivations are dense.  
- Technical accessibility/verifiability concern: calculus-of-variations derivations require specialized physics knowledge; reviewer couldn’t fully verify correctness.  
- Questionable fit/significance for NIPS: more aligned with cognitive/vision science than mainstream ML; not learning-based and not clearly advancing state-of-the-art ML practice.  
- Model complexity and hyperparameter tuning are somewhat heuristic (simulated annealing on a small subset); oscillatory curiosity terms may be ad-hoc and need more justification.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance for novelty but flags audience/fit concerns and defers final judgment to others with relevant expertise.
