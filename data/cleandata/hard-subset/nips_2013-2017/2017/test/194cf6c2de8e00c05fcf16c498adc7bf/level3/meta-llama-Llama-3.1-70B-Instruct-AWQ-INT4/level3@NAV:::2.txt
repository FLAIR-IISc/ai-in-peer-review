This paper, "Variational Laws of Visual Attention for Dynamic Scenes", proposes a novel model of visual attention based on a generalized view of the Least Action Principle in physics. The model, called EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), is driven by three fundamental principles: harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance term. The authors derive a cost function and differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.

The paper presents a thorough mathematical description of the model, including the Euler-Lagrange equations of motion that describe attention dynamics. The authors also provide experimental results to validate the model in tasks of saliency detection on both static and dynamic scenes.

The strengths of this paper are:

* The model is novel and provides a fresh perspective on visual attention, combining principles from physics and computer vision.
* The mathematical formulation is rigorous and well-developed, providing a solid foundation for the model.
* The experimental results are promising, showing that the model can compete with state-of-the-art models in saliency detection tasks.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The writing style is often unclear and convoluted, making it difficult to follow the authors' arguments.
* The paper could benefit from more explicit definitions of variables and functions, especially in Section 3 and the equations.
* The two-page appendix could be moved to supplementary material, and the freed pages could be used to define variables and functions in the main text.
* The authors could provide more connections and comparisons to psychological literature on optimal eye movements and discuss potential integration.

In terms of the NIPS criteria, I would evaluate this paper as follows:

* Quality: 7/10 (the model is technically sound, but the writing style and clarity could be improved)
* Clarity: 6/10 (the paper is well-organized, but the writing style is often unclear)
* Originality: 8/10 (the model is novel and provides a fresh perspective on visual attention)
* Significance: 8/10 (the results are promising, and the model has the potential to contribute to the field of computer vision and cognitive science)

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
