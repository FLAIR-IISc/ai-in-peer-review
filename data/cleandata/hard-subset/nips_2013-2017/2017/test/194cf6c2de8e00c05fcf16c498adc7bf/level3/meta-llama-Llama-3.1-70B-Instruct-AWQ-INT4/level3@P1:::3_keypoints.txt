- Proposes EYMOL, a novel model that directly generates gaze trajectories using a Least Action Principle rather than saliency-map optimization.  
- Provides a thorough, accurate review of existing visual attention models and situates the contribution well.  
- Mathematical formulation is substantial and novel, but detailed physics-based derivations are hard to evaluate for non-experts.  
- Evaluation on real eye-movement datasets yields generally favorable results, though the model is not dominant across all metrics.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and free of major errors.  
- Lacks sufficient intuitive explanations of mathematical concepts for readers without a strong physics background.  
- The approach represents a significant departure from saliency-map methods and is an original application of physical principles.  
- Potentially of strong interest to the vision science community and likely to inspire further empirical research.  
- Reviewer questions whether the work meets NIPS-level significance, deferring that judgement to more expert reviewers.  
- Main strengths: novelty, thorough literature review, empirical validation, and potential to spur new research.  
- Main weaknesses: mathematical accessibility for non-experts and mixed performance across evaluation metrics.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel theory with supportive empirical results and community relevance; rejection arguments: challenging math accessibility and unclear top-tier significance.
