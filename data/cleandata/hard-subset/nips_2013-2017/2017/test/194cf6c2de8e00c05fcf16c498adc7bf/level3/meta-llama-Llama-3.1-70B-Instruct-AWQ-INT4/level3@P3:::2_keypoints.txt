- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a variational attention model using a generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Model built from three principles: harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance term.  
- Derives a cost function and differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.  
- Well-motivated paper with a clear overview of state-of-the-art visual attention modeling.  
- Thorough mathematical description, with clear presentation of equations.  
- Experimental results are promising and comparable to state-of-the-art on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets.  
- Provides detailed analysis of model strengths and weaknesses.  
- Writing and organization are poor: text is dense, sentences overly long, and structure needs clearer headings and shorter paragraphs.  
- Mathematical derivations lack explicit assumptions and contextual explanations, making them hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Parameter estimation is under-detailed; simulated annealing is mentioned but not clearly explained or justified.  
- Originality is strong — variational approach and brightness-invariance term are novel contributions.  
- Discussion of related work is insufficient; needs deeper comparison to existing approaches.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and significant but requires revisions for clarity, exposition, and methodological detail.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions addressing writing, derivation clarity, related work, and parameter-estimation details.
