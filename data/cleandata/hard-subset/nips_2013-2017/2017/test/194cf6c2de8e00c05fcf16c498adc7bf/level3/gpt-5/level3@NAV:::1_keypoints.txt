- Proposes EYMOL: a continuous-time, bottom-up dynamical model where scanpaths minimize a generalized action (Lagrangian with kinetic + potential terms).
- Potential terms include boundary confinement, a curiosity term switching between local and peripheral gradient energy, and a brightness-invariance term from the optical-flow constraint.
- Saliency maps are generated as visit histograms from multiple simulated trajectories; experiments on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU show mixed performance against baselines.
- Lacks explicit connections to eye-movement and motor-control literature on least-action/optimal control (e.g., Harris & Wolpert; Todorov & Jordan) and smooth pursuit control.
- Strength: elegant dynamical-systems formulation unifying saccades, fixations, and tracking in one variational framework.
- Strength: produces scanpaths directly with saliency maps as a byproduct, offering a conceptually different map-first pipeline.
- Strength: simple ODEs that are real-time integrable, attractive for simulation and robotics applications.
- Weakness: affordance terms (curiosity switching, brightness invariance) feel ad hoc and lack biological or information-theoretic motivation.
- Weakness: externally injected center bias undermines the claim of purely bottom-up behavior.
- Weakness: Least Action derivation misses links to established eye-movement optimality accounts and could be better framed via constrained optimization/Lagrange multipliers.
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited—performance is generally below modern deep saliency models.
- Weakness: no scanpath-level analyses provided (e.g., MultiMatch, saccade amplitude/angle distributions, TTFF, temporal NSS) and no main-paper trajectory figures.
- Weakness: parameter tuning via simulated annealing is weakly justified and risks overfitting to NSS and sensitivity to initial conditions.
- Quality: mathematically sound but missing theoretical links and comprehensive empirical validation.
- Clarity: generally well written but needs main-text trajectory figures and an expanded related-work section.
- Originality: novel synthesis of least-action principles with saliency, but conceptually close to optimal control traditions.
- Significance: promising as a generative scanpath model, but current results do not advance state of the art.
- Pros for acceptance: fresh, unifying dynamical perspective with potential for generative modeling and robotics.
- Cons against acceptance: ad hoc affordances, limited biological grounding, mixed empirical performance, insufficient temporal/scanpath analyses, and weak connections to optimal control and eye-movement theory.
