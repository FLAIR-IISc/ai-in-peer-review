- Proposes EYMOL, a model that generates continuous gaze trajectories rather than the standard saliency-map + WTA approach.  
- Frames eye movements via a variational Least Action Principle with a Lagrangian (kinetic + potential energy).  
- Potential energy combines three terms: boundedness, curiosity (image gradients), and brightness invariance (motion tracking).  
- Scanpaths are obtained by solving Euler–Lagrange differential equations; saliency maps are a byproduct of aggregated trajectories.  
- Implemented and evaluated on MIT1003/300, CAT2000, and SFU (images and videos), showing competitive performance.  
- Theoretical framework is coherent, well-motivated, and technically sound.  
- Mathematical derivations are complex and may be hard to verify for readers without analytical mechanics background.  
- Parameters are estimated pragmatically using simulated annealing.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; model principles are explained intuitively before formalization.  
- Highly original: unifies saccades and smooth pursuit in a single physics-based model.  
- Significant for vision science and computational neuroscience; less directly impactful for core ML/NIPS audience.  
- Performance is competitive but not clearly superior to recent deep learning saliency methods.  
- Pros for acceptance: originality, theoretical elegance, solid empirical evaluation, potential to inspire new research.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited fit for NIPS focus, non-dominant performance, and heavy mathematical complexity in the appendix.  
- Overall: high-quality, thought-provoking work that may be better suited to specialized vision science venues but deserves consideration.
