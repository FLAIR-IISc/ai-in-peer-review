- Proposes a novel, principled model (EYMOL) that predicts human visual attention scanpaths via a variational Principle of Least Action.  
- Defines a Lagrangian for attention combining three principles: bounding potential (harmonic oscillator), curiosity (multi-scale image gradients), and brightness invariance (for motion tracking).  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange differential equations to generate scanpath dynamics; saliency maps produced by aggregating simulated scanpaths.  
- Evaluated on MIT300, CAT2000 (images) and SFU (video), achieving competitive or superior performance to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Contrasts with prior work by modeling attention dynamics from first principles rather than building static saliency maps or using data-driven deep nets.  
- Originality: elegant, highly original unifying mathematical framework for attraction, fixation, and tracking.  
- Significance: offers a principled explanation for scanpath emergence and potential broad impact across vision, neuroscience, and cognitive science; supports real-time dynamics.  
- Quality: technical derivation appears sound and experimental results support claims despite no deep learning or heavy feature engineering.  
- Weakness — clarity: manuscript is dense, with grammatical errors and hard-to-follow exposition.  
- Weakness — undefined terms: many variables/functions (e.g., p(t,x)) and abbreviations (e.g., NSS) are not clearly defined on first use.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: experimental section is rushed and omits key details (final parameter values from simulated annealing not reported).  
- Weakness — organization: lengthy derivations in the two-page appendix crowd the main text; main text needs space for clearer presentation.  
- Literature connection: should better relate the "curiosity" principle to cognitive theories (e.g., optimal information gathering, Najemnik & Geisler).  
- Suggestion: major revision for clarity—proofread, rewrite, and explicitly define all mathematical variables and functions.  
- Suggestion: expand experimental methodology to include all details necessary for reproducibility (parameter settings, optimization details).  
- Suggestion: move appendix to supplementary materials to free space for motivation, clearer definitions, and expanded experiments.  
- Suggestion: broaden discussion to connect variational principles to psychology/neuroscience concepts like information maximization or optimal foraging.  
- Summary pro-accept: highly novel, principled approach with unified framework and strong benchmark validation.  
- Summary con-accept: poor presentation and insufficient experimental detail hinder understanding and reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on a major revision addressing clarity, organization, and reproducibility.
