- Novel physics-inspired variational model for predicting bottom-up visual attention scanpaths in static and dynamic scenes.
- Model formulated via a Lagrangian combining a harmonic oscillator, a curiosity term attracted to multi-scale brightness changes, and a brightness-invariance term.
- Applies the principle of least action to derive differential equations that govern scanpath dynamics.
- Generates saliency maps as a byproduct of simulated scanpaths and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art on standard datasets.
- Strength: original, principled, and unifying framework linking saccades and smooth pursuit as emergent from fundamental principles.
- Strength: technical approach appears sound and experimental validation shows competitive results, indicating high potential significance.
- Weakness: manuscript clarity is poor—writing is difficult to follow and obscures novel contributions.
- Weakness: many variables, functions, and abbreviations are used without clear, upfront definitions, harming reproducibility.
- Suggestion: require major revision with thorough proofreading and rewrite to improve exposition.
- Suggestion: explicitly define every variable and function at first appearance.
- Suggestion: move the lengthy two-page derivation to supplementary material and use the freed space to clearly define model components and parameters.
- Suggestion: strengthen connection to psychological literature on optimal eye movement strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discuss potential integration.
