This paper proposes a novel approach to modeling eye movements, dubbed EYMOL (EYE MOvement Laws), which differs from current saliency-map based models by directly modeling gaze trajectories using a "Least Action Principle." The authors provide a thorough review of the current state of the art in visual attention modeling, correctly summarizing the key concepts and approaches.

The proposed model is based on three fundamental principles: boundedness of the trajectory, curiosity-driven principle, and brightness invariance. These principles are mathematically formulated using a generalized Lagrangian, which leads to the Euler-Lagrange equations of motion that describe attention dynamics. The model is evaluated on real eye-movement datasets and compared to several alternative models, with favorable results overall.

The strengths of this paper include its originality, clarity, and significance. The authors provide a novel and well-motivated approach to modeling eye movements, which is supported by experimental results. The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for readers with a basic understanding of physics and computer vision.

However, there are some weaknesses to note. Some of the mathematical details, especially in the appendix, may be challenging for non-experts to follow. Additionally, while the model performs well on several metrics, it is not dominant on every metric, which may indicate some limitations.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a significant contribution to the field of visual attention modeling and is likely to be of interest to the vision science community. While I am less convinced of its significance for NIPS, I defer to more expert reviewers in this area.
