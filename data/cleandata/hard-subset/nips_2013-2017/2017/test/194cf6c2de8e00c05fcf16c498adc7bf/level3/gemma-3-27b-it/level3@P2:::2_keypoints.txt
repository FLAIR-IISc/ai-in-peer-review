- Novel variational model frames eye movements via a Least Action Principle, offering a direct, dynamic alternative to saliency-map approaches.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in physics enables a mathematically rigorous formulation.  
- Integrates curiosity, brightness invariance, and boundedness into a unified attention framework.  
- Experimental results on static and dynamic datasets are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Authors claim potential for real-time application.  
- Paper is often unclear and hard to follow; dense math and poor exposition hinder comprehension.  
- Key variables and functions (e.g., ηb, ηp, λ, ḃ, bxx) are insufficiently defined, impeding reproducibility.  
- Extensive derivations belong in supplementary material rather than the main text.  
- Weak connection to psychological literature on eye movements and optimal scanpaths.  
- Parameter estimation via simulated annealing raises concerns about sensitivity to initial conditions and search space.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theory, competitive results, and real-time potential.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity/readability, undefined variables, weak psychological grounding, and parameter sensitivity.  
- Overall: promising and interesting contribution but requires substantial revisions for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; encourage revision and resubmission addressing notation, explanations, and psychological links.
