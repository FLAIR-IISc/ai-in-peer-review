- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention based on generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Model built from three principles: boundedness of trajectory, curiosity-driven movement, and brightness invariance.  
- Introduces corresponding Lagrangian terms and derives differential equations governing eye movements.  
- Provides a unified interpretation linking curiosity-driven movements and brightness invariance for fixation and tracking.  
- Novel use of a variational/least-action approach not common in visual attention literature.  
- Relates to prior work (feature integration theory, saliency maps) but remains distinct in formulation.  
- Paper is well-organized with generally clear explanations of model and principles.  
- Writing is sometimes unclear and needs proofreading and editing for readability.  
- Some variables and functions in the equations are not explicitly defined, hindering mathematical clarity.  
- Experimental results are promising: performance comparable to state-of-the-art on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets.  
- Experimental setup and evaluation metrics lack sufficient detail and should be expanded.  
- Significant potential impact on visual attention and cognitive science by offering a new explanatory framework.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty of approach, principled variational formulation, and competitive empirical results.  
- Rejection concerns: unclear writing, undefined mathematical notation, and insufficient experimental details.  
- Recommended fixes: proofread/edit, explicitly define all variables/functions (esp. Section 3), move long appendix to supplementary material, and connect results to psychological literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
