- Proposes a novel variational energy-based model for predicting eye movements grounded in a generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Derives a cost function and differential equations from three principles: harmonic-oscillator eye-movement constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change, and brightness-invariance.  
- Generates saliency maps as a byproduct of simulated scanpaths, providing a unified framework for attention and eye-movement modeling (vs. centralized saliency maps).  
- Evaluated on MIT1003, CAT2000 (static) and SFU (dynamic) datasets, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strong technical quality: well-constructed theoretical framework, solid experimental validation, and an original variational approach.  
- Significant contribution: offers a more biologically plausible mechanism and bridges cognitive science with computational modeling.  
- Major clarity issues: key variables/functions (especially in Section 3 and equations) are undefined, making derivations hard to follow.  
- Suggests moving the two-page appendix to supplementary material to free space for essential definitions and explanations.  
- Writing is convoluted with grammatical errors and unclear exposition that detracts from the contribution.  
- Needs explicit connection to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and integration with cognitive theories.  
- Overall judgment: technically strong but undermined by poor clarity; recommend rejection with strong encouragement to resubmit after substantial revision.
