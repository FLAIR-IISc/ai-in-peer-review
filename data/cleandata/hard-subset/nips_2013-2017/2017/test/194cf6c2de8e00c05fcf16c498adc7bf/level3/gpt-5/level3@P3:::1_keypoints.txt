- Proposes a bottom-up dynamical-systems model of visual attention where gaze follows Euler–Lagrange equations from a Least-Action Lagrangian.
- Lagrangian includes a boundary potential, a “curiosity” potential mixing local and blurred gradients via cos2/sin2 oscillation, and a brightness-invariance term to induce fixation/tracking.
- Scanpaths are generated by integrating second-order ODEs and saliency maps are visitation heatmaps over multiple runs; parameters fit with simulated annealing and some post-hoc blur/center-bias tuning.
- Novel framing: applying a Lagrangian/Least-Action perspective to scanpath generation is original and could link to control theory.
- Unified dynamics: a single equation set aims to produce saccades, fixations, and tracking, with saliency maps as a byproduct.
- Mathematical clarity: Euler–Lagrange derivation and link of brightness-invariance to optical flow are clearly presented in the appendix.
- Practicality: the ODEs are simple enough for real-time scanpath generation in videos.
- Affordances feel ad hoc: the curiosity term, oscillation form/frequency, and combination lack principled justification or ablation.
- Weak theoretical grounding: insufficient connection to optimal-control and motor-planning literature on eye movements (e.g., minimum-variance/jerk, signal-dependent noise).
- Physical interpretation issues: nonstandard kinetic-energy definition (K = (1/m)||ẋ||^2) and dimensional/stability concerns (λ < m/4) need justification and analysis.
- Limited evaluation: model performance is mixed and generally below top deep models; gains may reflect learned center-bias and blur tuning.
- Missing scanpath-level validation: no quantitative trajectory metrics (MultiMatch/ScanMatch, saccade stats, main sequence) or temporal video metrics are reported.
- Clarity gaps: lack of exemplar scanpath figures, insufficient hyperparameter justification (ω, k, annealing bounds), and unclear impact of preprocessing choices.
- V1 vs V2 brightness-invariance tradeoffs are presented but practical differences and computational costs are not well explained.
- Originality is moderate: variational derivation is new in this context, but components overlap with existing saliency and dynamical scanpath models.
- Limited significance as a saliency predictor: does not advance SOTA vs deep models; potential significance lies in providing a mechanistic dynamical model if validated.
- Suggest grounding the Lagrangian in eye-movement optimal-control literature and clarify how action encodes saccadic costs (precision, latency, energy).
- Suggest motivating/ablating affordances, justifying or learning the oscillation schedule, and reporting ablation results for key terms/parameters.
- Recommend providing sample trajectories compared to human scanpaths and reporting trajectory metrics, saccade distributions, fixation durations, and IOR effects.
- Ensure evaluation fairness: report sAUC and IG, control for center bias, and test robustness to different initial conditions.
- Clarify and correct kinetic-energy definition, units, stability constraints, and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Extend biological links by mapping model terms to SC/FEF/LIP or retinal/cortical sensitivities and discuss incorporating noise or signal-dependent variability.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually interesting, clear math, unifies fixation/tracking, lightweight and educational alternative to black-box models.
- Cons for acceptance: weak empirical scanpath support, hand-crafted affordances, weak ties to optimality principles, mixed performance, and clarity gaps.
- Overall: intriguing variational idea with potential, but currently under-motivated, under-evaluated at the scanpath level, and in need of stronger theoretical grounding and richer dynamic validations.
