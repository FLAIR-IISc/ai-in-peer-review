- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a novel visual-attention model grounded in the principle of least action.  
- Models scanpaths as a dynamical system driven by minimization of a generalized action.  
- Action decomposed into kinetic energy (motion), potential energy (saliency attraction), and a novel brightness invariance term.  
- Introduces two new affordances: curiosity and brightness invariance, to drive attention dynamics.  
- Derives model dynamics using Euler–Lagrange equations from classical mechanics.  
- Evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU datasets, showing improved fixation and scanpath prediction versus state-of-the-art.  
- Offers a unified framework capturing both bottom-up and top-down aspects of visual attention.  
- Provides a novel interpretation of brightness invariance’s role in visual attention.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the model and derivation.  
- Main limitation: lacks biological motivation and linkage to neural mechanisms.  
- Simplicity of the model raises doubts about capturing complex attention effects (object recognition, prior knowledge).  
- Evaluation is limited to benchmarks and needs testing on more complex tasks.  
- Recommendations: add biological grounding and literature links, evaluate on complex tasks, and consider increasing model complexity or advanced learning components.  
- Overall: valuable and novel contribution but would be strengthened by deeper biological justification and broader evaluation.
