- Proposes a novel paradigm modeling visual attention via foundational variational laws inspired by the Least Action Principle.  
- Combines three principles: trajectory boundedness, curiosity-driven attraction to detailed regions, and brightness invariance for fixation/tracking.  
- Formulates the model with a Lagrangian including kinetic, potential, and brightness invariance terms.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange equations to produce visual attention scanpaths.  
- Evaluated on datasets MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU and compared with popular saliency models.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough related-work discussion.  
- Successfully captures attention dynamics and simulates human-like scanpaths; strong results on dynamic scenes.  
- Variational approach and the curiosity+brightness invariance combination are novel contributions.  
- Model relies on several ad-hoc parameters (weights) tuned via simulated annealing.  
- Unclear how the model parameters and mechanisms map to underlying biological processes.  
- Does not address top-down/task-dependent modulation of visual attention.  
- Builds on existing work but acknowledges prior influences.  
- Potentially significant impact, especially for dynamic/video saliency applications.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, unified dynamic framework, promising dynamic-scene results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: ad-hoc parameter dependence, unclear biological grounding, no clear account of top-down influences.
