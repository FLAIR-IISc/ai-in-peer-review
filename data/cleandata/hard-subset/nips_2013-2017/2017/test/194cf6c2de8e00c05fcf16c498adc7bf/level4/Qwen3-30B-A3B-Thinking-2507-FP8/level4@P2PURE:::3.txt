

The paper introduces a novel approach to modeling eye movements. The authors accurately summarize the current state of the art (acknowledging my limited expertise in perception and eye movements, which I note from the outset). Existing methods typically rely on saliency maps defined over images, with fixation points selected via optimization or maximization. In contrast, the proposed EYMOL model operates under a distinct assumption, defining gaze trajectories directly through a "Least Action Principle." While some mathematical details in the extensive appendix were beyond my evaluation due to insufficient physics background, I understood the core methodology and its differentiation from prior models. The approach is applied to a real eye-movement dataset and compared against multiple alternative models. Although EYMOL does not consistently outperform all alternatives across every metric, the results are favorable and demonstrate the model's validity. I believe this work may interest researchers in vision science (e.g., VSS attendees). However, I am not fully convinced it constitutes a major contribution to NIPS; this assessment should be weighed against more specialized reviewers. Nevertheless, the paper advances a novel and compelling theoretical framework that could inspire further empirical research, establishing both novelty and merit for the perception sciences.
