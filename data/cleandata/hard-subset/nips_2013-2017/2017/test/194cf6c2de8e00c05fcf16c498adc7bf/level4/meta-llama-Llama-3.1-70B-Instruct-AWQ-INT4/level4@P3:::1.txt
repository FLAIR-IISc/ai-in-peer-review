This paper proposes a novel computational model of visual attention based on the principle that there are foundational laws that drive the emergence of visual attention. The model, called EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), is derived from a generalized view of the Least Action Principle in physics and consists of differential equations that describe the dynamics of scanpaths. The model is driven by three fundamental principles: boundedness of the trajectory, curiosity-driven principle, and brightness invariance. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in predicting human fixations on both image and video datasets.

The mathematical formulation of the model is sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the derivation of the Euler-Lagrange equations.
