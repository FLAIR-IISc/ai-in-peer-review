- Introduces EYMOL, a novel model for eye movements that departs from saliency-map-based approaches.  
- EYMOL is grounded in the Least Action Principle and models continuous gaze trajectories rather than discrete fixation points.  
- Mathematical/physics derivations are provided; reviewer not expert but finds the core concepts clearly presented.  
- Paper situates itself well with a competent summary of current state-of-the-art methods.  
- Empirical evaluation uses a real-world eye-movement dataset with comparisons to multiple alternative models.  
- Results are broadly favorable and demonstrate the viability of the new theoretical perspective, though EYMOL does not dominate all metrics.  
- Primary contribution is a novel theoretical framework that could inspire further empirical work in perception sciences.  
- Paper is well-argued and empirically validated, lending support to its central claims.  
- Main reservation concerns fit for the NIPS audience, as the work may be more relevant to vision science than core ML.
