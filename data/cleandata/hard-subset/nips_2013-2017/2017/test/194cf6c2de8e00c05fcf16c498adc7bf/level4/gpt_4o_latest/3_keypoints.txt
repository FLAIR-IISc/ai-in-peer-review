- Introduces EYMOL, a novel trajectory-based model of eye movements grounded in a Least Action Principle.  
- Authors give an accurate summary of the current state of the art (reviewer not an expert but agrees broadly).  
- Contrasts with existing methods that use saliency maps and fixation optimization; EYMOL is defined directly on gaze trajectories.  
- Some mathematical/physics details (appendix) were hard for the reviewer to fully follow, but core concepts are understood.  
- Applied to a real eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against several models; results are promising though not uniformly superior.  
- Likely of interest to the vision science/perception community (e.g., VSS), but reviewer questions its significance for NIPS and defers to other reviewers.  
- Presents a novel, intriguing theory with potential to inspire further empirical research in perception sciences.
