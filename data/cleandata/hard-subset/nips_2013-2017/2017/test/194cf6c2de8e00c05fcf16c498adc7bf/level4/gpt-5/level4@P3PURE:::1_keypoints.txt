- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model combined with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scan-path dynamics using the Least Action Principle.
- Defines “affordances” (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) as drivers of scan paths, but these affordances feel ad hoc and are not well motivated.
- Would benefit from connecting the Least Action formulation to established eye-movement literature (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab).
- Biological motivation is limited, which is a minor concern compared to other issues.
- Performance is mixed and notably worse than benchmark deep learning saliency models.
- Main paper lacks qualitative figures of generated scan paths — needs explicit examples showing dynamics and structure, not just aggregate statistics.
