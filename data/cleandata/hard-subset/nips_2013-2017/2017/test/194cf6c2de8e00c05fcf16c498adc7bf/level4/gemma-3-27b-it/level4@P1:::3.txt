The paper proposes a novel approach to modeling visual attention and eye movements. The authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing methods, which generally rely on constructing saliency maps and then selecting fixation points through optimization or maximization strategies. In contrast, their model (EYMOL) directly defines scanpaths based on a “Least Action Principle,” offering a fundamentally different perspective. While the detailed mathematical derivations, particularly in the appendix, were challenging for me to fully evaluate due to my limited physics background, I grasped the core concepts and how this approach diverges from previous work. The model is thoughtfully applied to real-world eye-movement datasets and benchmarked against several alternative methods. The results are encouraging, demonstrating competitive performance and validating the model’s potential.

I believe this paper may be of significant interest to researchers in the vision science community, particularly those attending conferences like VSS. Its suitability for NIPS is more debatable, and I recommend considering the opinions of reviewers with more specialized expertise in the field. However, the paper presents a potentially groundbreaking theory that could stimulate further empirical investigation, offering both novelty and value to perception sciences.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of formulating visual attention as a problem of variational principles and least action is a fresh perspective within the field.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The grounding of the model in established physical principles offers a compelling theoretical framework.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on both static images and dynamic scenes demonstrate promising performance and support the model’s validity.
*   **Potential Impact:** The proposed model could inspire new lines of research in understanding the underlying mechanisms of visual attention and eye movements.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Mathematical Complexity:** The extensive mathematical derivations may pose a barrier to understanding for readers without a strong physics background.
*   **NIPS Relevance:** The primary focus on perception and eye movements might be considered slightly outside the core areas typically addressed at NIPS.
*   **Limited Superiority:** While competitive, the model does not consistently outperform state-of-the-art methods across all metrics, suggesting room for improvement.
