- Integrates bottom-up visual saliency with dynamical systems to simulate scanpath dynamics; enjoyable and conceptually compelling.  
- Introduces "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies the Least Action Principle to drive scanpaths.  
- Justification for chosen affordances is ad-hoc and needs stronger motivation.  
- Derivation/discussion of the Least Action Principle should be connected to eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Model performance is poor compared to state-of-the-art static saliency methods.  
- Lack of deep biological motivation is more problematic here because the paper claims to capture biological dynamics.  
- Evaluation is insufficient: must include figures/metrics characterizing generated scanpaths and analyze dynamic properties beyond aggregate static measures.
