- Paper models locations attended in static/dynamic images by humans using an energy-minimization framework and derived differential equations for eye movements.
- Model is built from three principles: harmonic-oscillator constraint at image borders, curiosity-driven sensitivity to brightness changes at multiple scales, and brightness-invariance cost.
- Method predicts eye movements from initial position/velocity and is evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets.
- Empirical performance is comparable to state-of-the-art on several datasets.
- Novel and innovative formalization of saliency within a bottom-up framework that could advance understanding of attention and eye-movement modeling.
- Technical derivations and evaluation are sound and demonstrate good command of underlying principles.
- Writing is dense and organization is poor, making the paper hard to follow in places.
- Heavy use of abbreviations and unexplained technical terms reduces accessibility for some readers.
- Appendix should be moved to supplementary material and freed pages used to define every variable and function explicitly.
- Paper lacks an explicit connection to psychological literature on eye movements as optimal information-gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
- Recommend discussion of how this model compares to optimal-search models and whether it offers complementary information for integrated models.
- Overall, promising contribution contingent on clearer presentation, expanded definitions, and stronger linkage to existing psychological models.
