- Proposal: physics-inspired variational model where scanpaths extremize a Lagrangian with boundary potential, curiosity term, and brightness-invariance term, yielding Euler-Lagrange ODEs (EYMOL) for attention.
- Relation to prior work: builds on classic saliency and saccade models, optical flow constraints, and optimal-control ideas, recasting attention under a unified variational principle.
- Strength — conceptual unification: combines exploration (curiosity) and stabilization (brightness invariance) in one dynamical system that generates scanpaths directly.
- Strength — derivation/transparency: provides detailed Euler-Lagrange derivations and discusses well-posedness (e.g., bounds on λ).
- Strength — generality: framework handles static and dynamic scenes and produces scanpaths without hand-coded winner-take-all or explicit inhibition-of-return.
- Strength — empirical scope: evaluated on multiple image/video datasets with standard saliency metrics and approximate vs exact brightness-invariance comparison.
- Weakness — mixed empirical performance: generally weaker than modern CNN-based saliency models and needs up-to-date comparisons and human inter-observer baselines.
- Weakness — heuristics/confounds: center bias and output blur are tuned to benchmarks, and many runs are initialized near image center, potentially inflating metrics.
- Weakness — saliency focus over scanpaths: main text emphasizes saliency maps while lacking quantitative scanpath analyses (saccade amplitudes, durations, similarity to humans).
- Weakness — ad hoc modeling choices: alternation cos^2/sin^2, kinetic energy form/scaling, and parameter scheduling lack motivation and ablation.
- Weakness — incomplete simulated annealing details: acceptance probability, neighborhood definition, and parameter domain bounds are unspecified.
- Weakness — reproducibility issues: missing ODE solver settings, time step, runtimes, initial condition distributions, mass m, units, ω, and other implementation details.
- Weakness — presentation errors: typographical issues, notational inconsistencies, and a few stray lines reduce clarity.
- Weakness — limited biological grounding: loose connection to optimal/feedback control literature and sparse discussion of biological plausibility.
- Quality assessment: technically coherent variational setup but empirical support is weakened by tuned biases, small parameter search, and missing scanpath analyses.
- Clarity assessment: readable overall but hampered by typos and missing definitions; appendix helpful but could be clearer and more concise.
- Originality: novel explicit least-action formulation and brightness-invariance coupling, though related energy-based/dynamical ideas exist.
- Significance: conceptually promising for unifying saccades and tracking, but practical impact limited without stronger empirical and scanpath validation.
- Specific suggestion — bias control: report results with and without center bias/blur and include sAUC/shuffled-NSS plus human IO scores.
- Specific suggestion — scanpath analyses: add saccade amplitude distributions, fixation durations, inter-fixation distances, temporal metrics, and scanpath similarity (MultiMatch/ScanMatch).
- Specific suggestion — ablations/sensitivity: test removal of terms (no brightness invariance/curiosity), fixed vs alternating weighting, and sensitivity to ω, λ, m, k, and initial conditions.
- Specific suggestion — clarify modeling and SA: justify kinetic energy form/units, specify ω, detail simulated annealing (P(·), neighborhood, bounds), and give ODE solver/time-step/runtime.
- Specific suggestion — improve presentation: fix grammar/notation, define Iverson brackets, remove stray lines, and add a figure showing trajectory dynamics under varying weights.
- Pros for acceptance: elegant unified dynamical formulation, direct scanpath generation, and clear mathematical derivations useful for mechanistic modeling.
- Cons for acceptance: weaker empirical performance vs deep baselines, reliance on tuned biases, missing scanpath validation/ablations, ad hoc choices, and reproducibility/clarity issues.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger empirical validation, clearer justification of modeling choices, thorough scanpath analyses, and improved reproducibility and presentation.
