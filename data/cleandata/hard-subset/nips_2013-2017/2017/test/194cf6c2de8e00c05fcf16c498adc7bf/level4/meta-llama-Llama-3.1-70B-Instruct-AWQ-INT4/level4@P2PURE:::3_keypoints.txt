- Proposes a novel Eye Movement Optimization by Least Action (EYMOL) model that applies the Least Action Principle directly to gaze trajectories.
- Provides a thorough summary of current state-of-the-art eye movement models and accurate description of existing saliency-map–based methods.
- Contrasts with prior work that selects fixation points via optimization over image saliency maps.
- Some mathematical details (especially in the extensive appendix) were hard to evaluate due to the reviewer’s limited physics background.
- Despite those difficulties, the reviewer could grasp the main ideas and how the approach differs from previous models.
- Model evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared to several alternative models.
- Results do not consistently favor EYMOL but demonstrate its validity and potential benefits.
- Likely of interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees) but less clearly a fit for NeurIPS; recommends weighing with expert reviewer opinions.
- Overall presents a new, intriguing theory with novelty and merit that could inspire further empirical research in perception science.
