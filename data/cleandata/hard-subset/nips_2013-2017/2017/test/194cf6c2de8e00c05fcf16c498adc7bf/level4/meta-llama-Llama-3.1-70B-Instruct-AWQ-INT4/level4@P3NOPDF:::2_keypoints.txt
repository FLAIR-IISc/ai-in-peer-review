- Proposes a novel variational model predicting eye movements in static and dynamic scenes by minimizing an energy function derived from three principles.  
- Principle 1: imposes a harmonic-oscillator-like constraint at image borders within a limited-sized retina.  
- Principle 2: a curiosity-driven term that emphasizes regions with large brightness changes at fine and blurred scales.  
- Principle 3: brightness-invariance term that increases with brightness change.  
- Derives differential equations from the cost function to simulate eye movement trajectories given initial position and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Provides a formal definition of saliency and a novel bottom-up model for eye movements, contributing to computational vision and cognitive science.  
- Not universally state-of-the-art across all metrics/datasets, but offers a valuable, different perspective.  
- Paper is poorly written and hard to follow; recommends proofreading, expanded variable/abbreviation definitions, and moving the appendix to supplementary material.  
- Suggests connecting the model to psychological literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) on optimal eye-movement search strategies to clarify relations and potential integration.
