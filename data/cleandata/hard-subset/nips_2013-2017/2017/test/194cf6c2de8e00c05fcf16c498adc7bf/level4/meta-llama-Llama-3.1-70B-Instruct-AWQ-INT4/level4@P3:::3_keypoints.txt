- Proposes EYMOL, a novel trajectory-based visual attention model that departs from saliency maps.  
- Provides a thorough review of existing visual attention modeling and its limitations.  
- EYMOL is based on three principles: trajectory boundedness, curiosity-driven attention, and brightness invariance.  
- These principles are formalized into a Lagrangian and yield Euler–Lagrange equations of motion.  
- Model evaluated on real-world eye-movement datasets and compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Results show competitive performance, especially for dynamic-scene tasks.  
- Strengths: high originality, clear exposition, and detailed mathematical derivations.  
- Weakness: technical depth (notably the appendix) may be hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Unclear whether the model provides a significant empirical improvement over existing approaches.  
- The paper’s contribution to the broader machine-learning/NIPS community is not clearly articulated.  
- Recommendation: revise to better emphasize connections to machine learning and potential applications.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 8/10; Originality 9/10; Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid formulation, and effective experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: steep technical barrier, unclear ML significance, potentially limited broader impact.
