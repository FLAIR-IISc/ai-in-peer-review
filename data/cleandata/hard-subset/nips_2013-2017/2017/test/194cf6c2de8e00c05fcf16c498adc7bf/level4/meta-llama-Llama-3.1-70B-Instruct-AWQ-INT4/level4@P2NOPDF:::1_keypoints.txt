- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model extended with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces affordances (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and invokes a Least Action Principle to drive scanpath generation.  
- Affordances appear ad-hoc and lack sufficient motivation or justification.  
- Least Action Principle should be related to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab).  
- Biological motivation is weak but may be less critical given deep-learning saliency models; however, the model shows mixed performance compared to those models.  
- If claiming to capture biological dynamics, the paper needs main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths, including temporal dynamics, not just overall statistics.  
- Recommend more thorough explanation of affordances and their underlying motivations.  
- Recommend comparisons to existing eye-movement research and more detailed analyses of generated scanpaths to strengthen claims.
