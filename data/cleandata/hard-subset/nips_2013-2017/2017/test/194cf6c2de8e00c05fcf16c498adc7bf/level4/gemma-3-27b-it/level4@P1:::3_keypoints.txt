- Proposes a novel approach (EYMOL) that models visual attention and scanpaths via a “Least Action Principle” rather than saliency-map optimization.  
- Provides a comprehensive overview of existing saliency-based methods to contrast with the new formulation.  
- Mathematical derivations are extensive and grounded in physics, which the reviewer found hard to fully evaluate without a strong physics background.  
- Core concepts and the fundamental divergence from prior work were understood despite the technical depth.  
- Applied the model to real-world eye-movement datasets and dynamic scenes.  
- Benchmarked against several alternative methods, showing competitive empirical performance.  
- Results are encouraging but the model does not consistently outperform state-of-the-art across all metrics.  
- Theoretical grounding in established physical principles gives the model strong conceptual foundations.  
- The approach is potentially impactful for vision science and could stimulate further empirical research.  
- Paper likely of significant interest to vision/perception communities (e.g., VSS) but its fit for NIPS is debatable.  
- Recommends consulting reviewers with specialized expertise in perception/eye movements for evaluation.
