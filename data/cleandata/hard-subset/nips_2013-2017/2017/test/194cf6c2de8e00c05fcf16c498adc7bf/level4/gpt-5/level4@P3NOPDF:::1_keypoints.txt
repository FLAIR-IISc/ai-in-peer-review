- Summary: Proposes a dynamical scanpath model combining bottom-up saliency with affordance terms optimized via a Least Action Principle to generate biologically plausible gaze trajectories.

- Strength: Ambitious, timely attempt to model temporal scanpath dynamics rather than only static saliency.

- Strength: Clear mechanistic framing where affordances enter an energy functional and LAP yields motion.

- Strength: Interpretability—affordance contributions are, in principle, separable and testable.

- Strength: Potential to bridge optimization-based behavioral accounts with saliency-driven attention if validated.

- Concern 1 (biological grounding): Affordances feel ad hoc and lack connection to eye-movement and motor-control literature (e.g., minimum-variance/effort control, LQG, main-sequence constraints).

- Concern 1 (curiosity/brightness): “Curiosity” and “brightness invariance” are insufficiently tied to established constructs like Bayesian surprise, intrinsic motivation, or photoreceptor adaptation.

- Concern 2 (empirical validation): Reported performance is mixed/poor relative to modern deep saliency models and the paper needs to foreground temporal/sequence metrics if claiming dynamic plausibility.

- Concern 3 (scanpath dynamics): Paper lacks convincing analyses/visualizations of scanpaths—needs overlays, saccade amplitude/direction distributions, fixation durations, main-sequence, landing distributions, sequence-similarity metrics, and temporal sampling metrics.

- Concern 4 (ablations/sensitivity): Missing quantification of each affordance’s contribution and robustness checks—needs ablations, comparisons to simpler dynamical baselines, and sensitivity to hyperparameters/noise.

- Concern 5 (mathematical clarity): The Lagrangian/dynamical formulation needs detail on state variables, potential/kinetic/dissipation terms, stochasticity handling, constraints/boundary conditions, IOR modeling, and feature-level operationalization.

- Suggestion: Situate the model within motor-control and eye-movement frameworks (e.g., optimal feedback control, LATER, SceneWalk) and clarify how LAP differs or makes unique predictions.

- Suggestion: Relate curiosity to Bayesian surprise/information gain and tie brightness invariance to luminance adaptation mechanisms, showing empirical benefit.

- Suggestion: Expand evaluation to robust static metrics (AUC-Judd, sAUC, NSS, CC, KL) and dynamic metrics (MultiMatch, ScanMatch) with cross-dataset tests and statistical analysis.

- Suggestion: Provide qualitative scanpath figures comparing model, humans, and baselines, and report main-sequence and distribution statistics.

- Suggestion: Add comprehensive ablations including “no-LAP”/“no-affordance” variants and ensure fair comparisons to strong deep baselines (DeepGaze, SALICON, ML-Net).

- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost and stability—real-time integration feasibility and handling of oscillations or dead-ends.

- Minor: Clarify terminology—justify “affordances” or use alternatives like “priors”/“regularizers.”

- Minor: Improve notation consistency, define variables on first use, and fix typographical/grammatical errors.

- Overall assessment: Promising principled approach but currently under-motivated biologically and under-validated empirically; needs deeper literature connections, stronger dynamic evaluations, and thorough ablations before claims are supported.
