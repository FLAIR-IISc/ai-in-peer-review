This paper introduces a novel framework for modeling eye movements, diverging from traditional saliency map-based approaches that rely on optimization to determine fixation points. Instead, the authors propose a model (EYMOL) grounded in a “Least Action Principle” applied directly to gaze trajectories. While a full evaluation of the mathematical details, particularly within the extensive appendix, was limited by my background, the core concepts and distinctions from existing models were clear. The model is thoughtfully evaluated using a real-world eye-movement dataset, with comparisons to several alternative approaches. The results demonstrate promising performance, suggesting the validity of this new approach, even if it doesn’t consistently outperform all others across all metrics. 

I believe this work may be of significant interest to researchers in the vision science community, potentially at a conference like VSS. Its suitability for NIPS may benefit from consideration by reviewers with deeper expertise in the field. However, the paper presents a novel and potentially influential theoretical framework that could stimulate further empirical investigation, representing both novelty and merit within perception sciences.
