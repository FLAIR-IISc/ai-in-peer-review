This paper proposes a novel approach to the study of eye movements. The authors accurately summarize the current state-of-the-art, which, as I understand it, is primarily based on saliency maps over which fixation points are selected via an optimization or maximization process. The proposed model, EYMOL, operates under a different assumption, defining gaze trajectories directly according to a "Least Action Principle."

While a full evaluation of the mathematical details was beyond the scope of my expertise due to the necessary physics background (particularly in the extensive appendix), I was able to understand the key elements of the approach and how it diverges from previous models.

The model is appropriately applied to a real dataset of eye movements and benchmarked against a variety of alternative models. Although it does not outperform competitors on every metric, the results are favorable for the proposed approach and demonstrate its validity.

This paper will likely be of significant interest to the vision science community (e.g., attendees of VSS). I am less convinced of its contribution to the NIPS community; however, this assessment should be weighed against the opinions of reviewers with more direct expertise in this area. Ultimately, the paper advances a new and interesting theory that could inspire further empirical research, and as such, it possesses clear novelty and merit for the perception sciences.
