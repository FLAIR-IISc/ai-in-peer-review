This paper introduces a fresh perspective on modeling eye movements. The authors provide a competent overview of the existing literature (to the extent I can assess it; I note that I am not a specialist in perception or oculomotor research). Broadly, prior methods rely on saliency maps defined over images and select fixation points via optimization or maximization. In contrast, the proposed EYMOL framework operates directly on gaze trajectories using a Least Action Principle. Some of the mathematical derivations—especially in the lengthy appendix—go beyond my technical background in physics, so I cannot fully vet them. Nevertheless, the core ideas and the conceptual departures from prior saliency-map-based models are clear. The method is evaluated on real eye-movement datasets and compared against multiple baselines; while it is not superior across all metrics, the results are generally favorable and support the plausibility of the approach.

I can see this work appealing to segments of the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees). I am less certain it constitutes a strong fit for NIPS, though I defer to reviewers with deeper domain expertise. It appears to put forward a novel and thought-provoking theoretical framework that could motivate further empirical work, and thus offers both originality and potential value for perception research.
