This paper proposes a new approach for studying eye movements. The authors provide an accurate summary of the current state (as far as I understand, but I admit from the outset that I am not an expert in perception or eye movements). Essentially, these methods are based on a silence map, defined on an image, and the fixation point is chosen through optimization/maximization methods. This model (EYMOL) works on a different assumption and is instead defined directly on the trajectory of vision according to the "minimum action principle." The details of some mathematical concepts were beyond my assessment because I did not have the necessary physics background (especially the extensive appendix). However, I still understand the core components of the method and how it differs from previous models. The model has been experimentally applied to real-world data sets of eye movements and compared with various alternative models. Although it does not dominate every measurement, the results are favorable to the new approach and prove that it has some validity.

I believe this paper may be of interest to some people in the field of vision science (such as VSS participants). I am not entirely sure that it will make a very big contribution to NIPS, but I think it should be evaluated in contrast to the opinions of other reviewers who are more specialized. I think it probably advances a new and interesting theory that may inspire more empirical research and therefore has both novelty and merit for perception science.
