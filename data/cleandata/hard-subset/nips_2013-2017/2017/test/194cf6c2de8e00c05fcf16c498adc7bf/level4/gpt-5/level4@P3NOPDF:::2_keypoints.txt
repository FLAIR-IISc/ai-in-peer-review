- Paper proposes a variational formulation of bottom-up visual attention modeling gaze as trajectories minimizing an energy with harmonic-oscillator, curiosity-driven, and brightness-invariance terms, applicable to static and dynamic scenes, yielding competitive scanpath predictions.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty—clear variational principle and dynamical system unifying static and dynamic gaze modeling.
- Strength: Formalizes saliency as explicit energy terms grounded in image measurements and motion constraints.
- Strength: Empirical performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets.
- Strength: Potential to bridge computational vision and cognitive science with a principled eye-movement framework.
- Weakness: Paper is hard to follow; many symbols/abbreviations are undefined at first use and equations are not self-contained.
- Weakness: Some ideas are ambiguously described (e.g., sign and role of “brightness invariance” unclear).
- Weakness: Important derivation and implementation details are missing or relegated to a brief appendix.
- Clarification requested: Provide the explicit energy functional with all terms, signs, weighting coefficients, units, and definitions of every symbol/operator.
- Clarification requested: Specify harmonic-oscillator/border term details—boundary potential, retina size, damping, under/over-damped dynamics, and handling of saccades vs. smooth pursuit.
- Clarification requested: Detail curiosity-driven term’s multiscale construction—filters, blur kernel, pyramid design, scale normalization, and relation to local contrast.
- Clarification requested: Make brightness-invariance term explicit—whether it uses brightness-constancy (I_t + v·∇I), estimation under noise, and its exact formulation.
- Clarification requested: Include full variational derivation with Euler–Lagrange equations, boundary conditions, and treatment of saccade onset/offset.
- Clarification requested: Explain parameter selection—fixed, tuned, or learned—and provide sensitivity analyses and ablation studies for each energy term.
- Clarification requested: Describe initial condition choices for fixation and velocity and report sensitivity to these choices.
- Clarification requested: Provide numerical integration details—solver, step size, stability—and runtime information.
- Evaluation: Specify datasets and splits used for static/dynamic benchmarks and any train/val/test partitions.
- Evaluation: Ensure fair baselines with consistent handling of center bias and identical input modalities across models.
- Evaluation: Report standard metrics (AUC, sAUC, NSS, KL, CC, scanpath similarity) with confidence intervals, statistical tests, qualitative examples, and failure cases.
- Evaluation: Distinguish frame-wise saliency map evaluation from full scanpath prediction for dynamic scenes and evaluate both appropriately.
- Relation to prior work: Better situate relative to classic bottom-up saliency (Itti–Koch–Niebur), variational saliency, and dynamical/optimal-control eye-movement models.
- Relation to prior work: Connect to psychological/optimal-search literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler), clarify whether the energy approximates expected information gain or is a complementary surrogate, and consider empirical comparisons on search tasks.
- Scope: Clarify bottom-up limitations and how top-down/task demands could be incorporated (e.g., task prior term).
- Presentation: Thorough proofreading, expand abbreviations at first use, and make equations self-contained.
- Presentation: Move appendix to supplementary material; add a notation table, variable definitions, parameter values, and prediction pseudocode/algorithm.
- Presentation: Improve figure captions, add schematics of energy landscape, example trajectories, and release code for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: Promising, principled, and original approach with competitive results but requires major revisions for clarity, completeness, stronger empirical evaluation, and connection to optimal-search literature.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on substantial revisions addressing notation, derivations, experiments, and positioning relative to prior work.
