- Introduces a bottom-up saliency model combined with a dynamical-systems formulation to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Proposes novel “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and uses the Least Action Principle to guide scanpaths.  
- Reviewer found the ideas appealing and enjoyed reading the manuscript.  
- The proposed affordances feel ad hoc and lack further justification or substantiation.  
- The derivation should be related to established eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab) to strengthen biological grounding.  
- Biological grounding is limited, which is less critical given strong deep models for static saliency but still concerning for claims of biological plausibility.  
- Model performance compared to modern deep saliency methods is mixed to poor.  
- The paper lacks main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths (only overall statistics are shown); visual/qualitative scanpath examples are needed to support claims about eye-movement dynamics.
