- Paper proposes a principled model of human visual attention for static and dynamic scenes derived from three foundational principles and formulated as an energy function minimized by eye movements.
- Principle 1: a harmonic-potential constraint keeps gaze within image boundaries to simulate a finite retina.
- Principle 2: a "curiosity-driven" term prefers regions of high luminance contrast at both fine and coarse scales.
- Principle 3: invariance to global brightness makes the model respond to local luminance changes.
- From the cost function the authors derive differential equations that predict eye movement trajectories given initial gaze position and velocity.
- The model is quantitatively evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.
- Contribution: provides a formal, principled definition of saliency and a novel bottom-up attention model in a field lacking clear definitions.
- Limitation: writing is often unclear and technical details are difficult to follow, requiring thorough proofreading by a native English speaker.
- Methodological clarity issues: abbreviations, symbols, and variables (e.g., in Section 3) are not explicitly defined and should be clarified in the main text.
- Suggestion: move the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use the space to clearly define variables and functions for a multidisciplinary NIPS audience.
- Psychological grounding: the paper should connect its principles to literature on eye movements as optimal information gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and clarify whether it restates or complements information-theoretic models.
