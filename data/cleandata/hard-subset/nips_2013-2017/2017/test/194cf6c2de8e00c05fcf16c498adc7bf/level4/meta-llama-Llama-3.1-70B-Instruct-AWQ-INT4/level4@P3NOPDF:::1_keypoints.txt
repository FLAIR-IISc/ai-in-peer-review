- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model combined with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces novel "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) as drivers of scanpaths, which is an intriguing idea.  
- Affordances feel ad hoc and need stronger motivation, context, or theoretical justification.  
- Least Action Principle is central but lacks explicit connection to existing eye-movement literature.  
- Recommend discussing links to related work (e.g., Wolpert lab) to situate the Least Action Principle.  
- Biological motivation is limited; while minor given deep-learning salience models, the model’s mixed performance against SOTA is a larger concern.  
- Paper should include detailed visualizations of generated scanpaths (dynamics and examples) in the main text, not just summary statistics.  
- Provide clearer comparison of strengths and limitations relative to state-of-the-art approaches to help evaluate contributions.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-written, but some sections need more detailed explanations or examples.  
- Overall promising approach with limitations; revisions addressing motivation, connections to prior work, performance comparisons, and richer scanpath visuals would strengthen the paper.
