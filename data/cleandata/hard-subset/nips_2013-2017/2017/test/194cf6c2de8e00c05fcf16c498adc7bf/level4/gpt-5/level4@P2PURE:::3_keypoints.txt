- Introduces a new approach (EYMOL) to modeling eye movements, based on a Least Action Principle over gaze trajectories.
- Accurately summarizes the current state of the art in fixation modeling for context.
- Contrasts with existing methods that use saliency maps and select fixation points via optimization/maximization.
- Some mathematical details (especially in the appendix) are difficult for the reviewer due to limited physics background.
- Reviewer nonetheless grasps the key elements and how the model differs from prior work.
- Model is evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared to multiple alternative models.
- Results are favorable but the model does not dominate across every metric.
- Findings support the validity of the new trajectory-based approach.
- Likely of interest to the vision science/perception community (e.g., VSS attendees).
- Reviewer is less convinced it is a strong NIPS contribution but acknowledges novelty and potential to stimulate further empirical research.
