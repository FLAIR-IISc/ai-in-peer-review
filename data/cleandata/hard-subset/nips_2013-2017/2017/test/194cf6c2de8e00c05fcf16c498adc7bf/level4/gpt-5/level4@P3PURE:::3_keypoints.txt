- Proposes a novel model (EYMOL) for eye movements formulated directly over gaze trajectories and grounded in a Least Action Principle.
- Provides a clear and accurate summary of prior work; most existing methods use image saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization.
- Contrasts with prior approaches by modeling trajectories rather than saliency-map-based fixation selection.
- Core ideas and distinctions from prior models are understandable and well motivated.
- Some mathematical details (especially in the extensive appendix) exceed the reviewer’s ability to fully evaluate due to limited physics background.
- Evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared against multiple alternative models.
- Results do not dominate on every metric but are generally favorable and support the approach’s validity.
- Likely of interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees).
- Reviewer uncertain about fit for a major ML venue like NIPS but acknowledges clear novelty and potential to inspire further empirical research in perception sciences.
