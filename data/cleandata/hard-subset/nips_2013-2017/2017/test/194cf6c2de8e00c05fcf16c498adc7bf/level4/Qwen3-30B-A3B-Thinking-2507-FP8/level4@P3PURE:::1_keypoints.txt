- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model augmented with a dynamical-systems formulation to generate scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies the Least Action Principle—an interesting, compelling idea set.  
- The affordances are somewhat ad hoc and lack deeper theoretical or principled motivation.  
- The derivation/use of the Least Action Principle should be connected to established eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab and related work).  
- Absence of explicit biological motivation is a minor issue given current ML-dominated saliency research, but still worth noting.  
- Major limitation: model performance is inferior to state-of-the-art saliency approaches.  
- Critical omission: the main text lacks visualizations of generated scanpaths and relies only on static statistics.  
- Recommend including scanpath visualizations to rigorously evaluate temporal and spatial fidelity of the model.
