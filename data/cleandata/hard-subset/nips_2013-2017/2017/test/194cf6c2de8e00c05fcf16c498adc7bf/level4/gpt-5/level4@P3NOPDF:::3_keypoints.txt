- Introduces EYMOL, a novel framework modeling full gaze trajectories via a Least Action Principle rather than saliency-map maximization.
- Core idea and contrast with prior saliency approaches are clearly conveyed despite reviewer not being an eye-movement expert.
- Theoretical contribution: conceptually appealing trajectory-based, physics-grounded formulation; high-level logic clear though some appendix derivations exceed reviewer’s physics expertise.
- Empirical evaluation: tested on real eye-movement data against multiple baselines; generally competitive though not superior on every metric; evaluation appears fair and broad.
- Strength: Novel theoretical framing treating gaze as a dynamical process rather than isolated fixations.
- Strength: Clear articulation of differences from mainstream saliency-map methods.
- Strength: Empirical comparisons across baselines with favorable overall results.
- Strength: Potential to stimulate new research directions in vision science and computational perception.
- Weakness: Accessibility concerns due to heavy physics-based derivations in the appendix; needs a more intuitive, self-contained main-text explanation.
- Weakness: Validation depth lacking—insufficient analysis of conditions where EYMOL excels or struggles (sensitivity to parameters, noise, stimulus characteristics).
- Weakness: Limited interpretability—needs clearer mapping from least-action components to known visual/cognitive mechanisms (attention, task demands).
- Weakness: Missing computational analysis—report runtime and scalability relative to saliency-based models.
- Significance/venue fit: Likely of strong interest to vision science (e.g., VSS); fit for NIPS uncertain due to domain specificity and technical accessibility for broader ML audience.
- Suggestions: Add a concise, low-math overview of the Least Action Principle early in the paper.
- Suggestions: Include ablations/sensitivity analyses to identify which objective components drive performance.
- Suggestions: Clarify links to established perception/attention findings and discuss incorporation of task goals or context.
- Suggestions: Report runtime and scalability to inform practical adoption.
- Overall assessment: Fresh, well-motivated contribution with competitive empirical support; borderline recommendation for NIPS, with final judgment best left to area experts.
