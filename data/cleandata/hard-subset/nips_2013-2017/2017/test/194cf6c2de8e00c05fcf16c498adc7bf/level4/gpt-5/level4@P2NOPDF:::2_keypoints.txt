- Contribution: Proposes a variational energy-minimization framework for saliency and scanpath generation with three components (boundary harmonic oscillator, multi-scale brightness-driven curiosity, brightness-invariance), yielding differential equations for static and dynamic scenes and competitive performance.

- Strength: Provides a principled mathematical formalization of saliency/scanpath generation, addressing a gap in clear definitions in psychology and vision.

- Strength: The multi-scale, brightness-driven “curiosity” term is intuitive for dynamic scenes and the variational derivation yields explicit eye-motion equations.

- Strength: Competitive overall performance and offers a novel perspective likely to inspire integration with other models.

- Weakness: Paper is difficult to follow—many abbreviations aren’t expanded and numerous variables/functions are not explicitly defined.

- Weakness: Important definitions are pushed to the Appendix, disrupting the main-text flow and accessibility.

- Recommendation: Move the 2-page Appendix to supplementary material and use space to define every symbol, expand abbreviations on first use, and add a short table/summary of model components and roles.

- Recommendation: Substantially revise writing and organization—present a concise overview of the three principles, then derivation, then explicit differential equations and initialization.

- Recommendation: Situate the model within psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and clarify connections to optimal control/information-theoretic formulations and whether scanpath statistics align with optimal search findings.

- Recommendation: Add an analysis or qualitative discussion of sensitivity to initial conditions (starting location and velocity) to help interpret performance variability.

- Overall evaluation: Promising, innovative, and principled contribution that would be significantly stronger with clearer exposition, complete definitions, and stronger ties to psychological theory.
