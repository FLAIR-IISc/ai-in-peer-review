- Proposes a new approach (EYMOL) for studying eye movements.  
- Reviewer agrees authors correctly summarize current state of the art (though not an expert).  
- Existing methods use image saliency maps and select fixation points by optimizing saliency.  
- EYMOL instead operates directly on gaze trajectories and is derived from a Least Action Principle.  
- Mathematical details (especially appendix) were difficult for the reviewer to fully evaluate due to limited physics background.  
- Reviewer understood the main ideas and how the model departs from prior work.  
- Model evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared to multiple alternative models.  
- EYMOL does not dominate every metric but yields generally favorable results supporting its validity.  
- Likely of interest to vision science communities (e.g., VSS attendees).  
- Reviewer uncertain about its strength as a NIPS contribution and suggests deferring to more specialized reviewers.  
- Overall assessment: novel theoretical framework with potential to stimulate further empirical research and offers merit for perception sciences.
