This paper presents a novel approach to modeling eye movements, diverging from traditional methods based on saliency maps and optimization of fixation points. Instead, the authors propose EYMOL, a model grounded in the “Least Action Principle” and defined directly on gaze trajectories. While I readily admit to not being an expert in perception or eye movement research, the authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing work. 

The mathematical details, particularly those found in the extensive appendix, were challenging for me to fully evaluate due to my limited background in physics. However, the core concepts and the key distinctions between EYMOL and prior models were clear. The model’s application to a real-world eye-movement dataset, coupled with comparisons to several alternative models, strengthens the paper. The results, while not universally superior across all metrics, are encouraging and suggest the validity of the proposed approach. 

I believe this work will likely be of significant interest to researchers in vision science, potentially finding a receptive audience at conferences like VSS. Its suitability for NIPS is less clear to me, and I defer to the expertise of other reviewers to assess its alignment with the conference’s focus. Nevertheless, the paper introduces a new and potentially impactful theory that could stimulate further empirical investigation, representing a valuable contribution to the field of perceptual sciences with both novelty and merit.
