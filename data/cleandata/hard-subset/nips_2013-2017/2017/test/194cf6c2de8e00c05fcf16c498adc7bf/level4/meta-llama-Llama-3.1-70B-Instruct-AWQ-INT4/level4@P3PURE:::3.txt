The paper proposes a novel approach to the study of eye movements, providing a thorough summary of the current state of the art in this field. The authors accurately describe existing methods, which primarily rely on saliency maps defined over images, with fixation points selected through optimization or maximization techniques. In contrast, the proposed model, EYMOL, operates under a distinct assumption, employing a "Least Action Principle" defined directly on gaze trajectories. Although some mathematical details, particularly in the extensive appendix, were beyond my expertise due to my limited physics background, I was still able to grasp the key elements of the approach and its differentiation from previous models.

The authors demonstrate the applicability of their model by applying it to a real dataset of eye movements and comparing its performance to various alternative models. While the results do not overwhelmingly favor the new approach in every aspect, they are generally positive and validate the model's potential. I believe this paper may be of interest to certain members of the vision science community, such as attendees of VSS. However, I am less convinced that it makes a significant contribution to NIPS, and I think this should be evaluated in comparison to the opinions of other reviewers with more expertise in this area.

Despite my reservations, I think the paper likely advances a new and interesting theory that could inspire further empirical research, demonstrating both novelty and merit in the field of perception sciences.
