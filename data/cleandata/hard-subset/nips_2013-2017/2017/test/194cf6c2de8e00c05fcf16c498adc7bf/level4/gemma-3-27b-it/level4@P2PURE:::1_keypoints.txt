- Introduces a bottom-up visual saliency model enhanced with dynamical systems to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Proposes novel “affordances” (curiosity and brightness invariance) and applies the Least Action Principle.  
- The proposed affordances feel ad hoc and lack adequate justification.  
- The Least Action Principle derivation should be connected to existing eye-movement control literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Lack of strong biological motivation is a minor concern given current benchmark-focused deep-learning trends.  
- Model performance is weak relative to strong deep-learning baselines.  
- Claims of capturing biological eye-movement dynamics need supporting figures characterizing generated scanpaths beyond aggregate statistics.
