- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model augmented by dynamical-systems modelling to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) and a Least Action Principle as drivers of scanpaths—novel and interesting ideas.  
- Affordances appear somewhat ad hoc and lack deeper motivation or justification.  
- The derivation of the Least Action Principle should be related to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Biological motivation is limited; this is less critical given deep-learning baselines but still relevant given the biological claims.  
- Model shows mixed performance compared to benchmark deep models, which is concerning.  
- Paper lacks main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths and their temporal dynamics—only overall statistics are shown.
