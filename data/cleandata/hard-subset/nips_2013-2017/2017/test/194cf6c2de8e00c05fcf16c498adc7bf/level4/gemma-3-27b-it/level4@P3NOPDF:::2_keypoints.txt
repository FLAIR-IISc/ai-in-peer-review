- Proposes a novel model of visual attention for static and dynamic scenes based on three principles: constrained eye movements, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness changes, and brightness invariance.  
- Formalizes these principles into an energy function and derives differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation against human eye movement data shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods, though not uniformly superior across all metrics/datasets.  
- Offers a refreshing, more formal perspective on saliency that could advance principled bottom-up eye movement modeling.  
- Major clarity and presentation problems: dense writing, hard-to-follow exposition, and frequent unexplained abbreviations hinder assessment.  
- Strong recommendation to thoroughly revise text, expand abbreviations, and proofread carefully for accessibility.  
- Appendix is too short and should be moved to supplementary material; reclaimed space should provide comprehensive definitions of all variables/functions for reproducibility.  
- Needs an explicit glossary of notation to aid the diverse NeurIPS audience and improve reproducibility.  
- Should connect the model to cognitive/psychological literature on information-gathering eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and clarify relationships to optimal search strategies.  
- Overall: novel and potentially impactful work but requires substantial improvements in clarity, notation, and literature integration to realize its contribution.
