- Paper proposes an attention model minimizing an energy function from three principles: harmonic-oscillator eye movements, curiosity-driven multi-scale contrast, and brightness invariance.  
- The model yields differential equations predicting scanpaths from initial position and velocity.  
- Evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Presents a novel, formal, bottom-up perspective on saliency and attention that advances computational and cognitive understanding.  
- Does not consistently outperform all competitors across every metric and dataset.  
- Manuscript is poorly written and difficult to follow, requiring thorough proofreading and clearer exposition.  
- Authors should expand and define all abbreviations, variables, and functions explicitly for a multidisciplinary audience.  
- Recommend moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using freed space to clarify notation and definitions.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests connection to psychological literature on optimal eye-movement strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of how the variational approach relates to or integrates with existing models.
