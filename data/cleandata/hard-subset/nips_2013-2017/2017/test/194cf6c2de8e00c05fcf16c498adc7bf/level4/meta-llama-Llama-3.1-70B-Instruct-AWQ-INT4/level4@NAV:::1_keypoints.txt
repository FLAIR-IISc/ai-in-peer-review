- Presents a novel bottom-up visual saliency model using dynamical systems to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces "affordances" (curiosity and brightness invariance) and the Least Action Principle; affordances are interesting but somewhat ad hoc and under-justified.  
- Recommends relating the Least Action Principle derivation to prior eye-movement research (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Notes limited biological motivation is tolerable, but model performance is inconsistent compared to modern deep-learning static salience models.  
- Urges inclusion of main-paper figures characterizing generated scanpaths and their match to biological eye-movement patterns, not only aggregate statistics.
