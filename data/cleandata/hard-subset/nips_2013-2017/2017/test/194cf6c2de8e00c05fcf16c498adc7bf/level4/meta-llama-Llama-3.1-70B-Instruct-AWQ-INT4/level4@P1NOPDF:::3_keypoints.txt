- Proposes a novel approach (EYMOL) to study eye movements and provides a thorough summary of the current state of the art.
- Accurately describes existing approaches that use saliency maps and select fixation points via optimization/maximization.
- EYMOL departs from prior models by defining gaze trajectories directly according to the Least Action Principle.
- Some mathematical details (especially in the appendix) were hard for the reviewer to evaluate due to limited physics background, but the main ideas and distinctions were understood.
- Model is evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared against various alternative models.
- Results are generally favorable and demonstrate the model’s validity, though not overwhelmingly superior on every metric.
- Comparative analysis helps clarify EYMOL’s strengths and limitations.
- Reviewer considers the paper of interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS) but is less convinced of its fit for NIPS and suggests further expert opinions.
- Paper is likely to advance a new theory that could inspire further empirical research and has novelty and merit for perception sciences.
