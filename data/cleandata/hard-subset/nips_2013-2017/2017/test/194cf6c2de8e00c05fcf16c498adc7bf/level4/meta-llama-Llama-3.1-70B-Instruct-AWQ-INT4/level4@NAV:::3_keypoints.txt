- Introduces a novel approach (EYMOL) for studying eye movements, unlike prior saliency-map-based methods.  
- Provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of the art in eye-movement modeling.  
- Existing methods use saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization or maximization.  
- EYMOL instead defines gaze trajectories directly using the "Least Action Principle."  
- Some mathematical details (especially in the appendix) were hard for the reviewer to assess due to limited physics background.  
- Reviewer nevertheless grasped the core approach and how it differs from previous models.  
- Model was evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared to alternatives, showing favorable but not uniformly superior results.  
- Likely to appeal to vision science audiences (e.g., VSS) but its fit for NIPS is uncertain.  
- Recommends obtaining opinions from more expert reviewers; the paper offers a novel theory with potential to inspire further empirical work.
