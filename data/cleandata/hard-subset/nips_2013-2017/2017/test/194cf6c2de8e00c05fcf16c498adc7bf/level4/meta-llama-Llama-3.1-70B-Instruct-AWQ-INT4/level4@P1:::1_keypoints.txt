- Proposes a novel paradigm for visual attention based on variational laws and a generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Introduces three core principles: trajectory boundedness, curiosity-driven attention, and brightness invariance.  
- Formulates these principles mathematically via a variational approach, yielding differential equations for attention dynamics.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear mathematical derivations and explanations of assumptions.  
- Experimental validation on saliency detection tasks is presented and clearly reported.  
- Strength: unified framework that captures scanpath dynamics within a single model.  
- Strength: competitive saliency performance given the model’s relative simplicity.  
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion situating the contribution in the field.  
- Limitation: chosen "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) appear somewhat ad hoc and under-motivated.  
- Limitation: lacks biological grounding and connections to empirical eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab work).  
- Limitation: performance is not consistently superior to state-of-the-art models.  
- Limitation: requires more detailed analysis and temporal visualization of generated scanpaths.  
- Overall: novel and intriguing approach with potential, but needs stronger motivation and deeper empirical/bio-plausibility analysis.
