- Paper proposes a variational/energy-based model of visual attention predicting eye movements from three principles (retinal boundary oscillator, curiosity-driven brightness change, brightness invariance).  
- Model yields differential equations for eye trajectories dependent on start location and initial velocity.  
- Evaluated on static and dynamic scene datasets with human eye-tracking and shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art.  
- Novelty: applying a physics-inspired variational framework to saliency and eye-movement modeling is original and promising.  
- Theoretical strength: deriving dynamics from first principles gives a solid mathematical foundation.  
- Potential impact: could influence computer vision, robotics, and cognitive science if further developed.  
- Presentation problems: paper is hard to follow due to unclear writing, poor organization, and unexplained abbreviations and variables.  
- Recommendation to move the long appendix to supplementary material and clearly define all variables/functions for an interdisciplinary audience.  
- Psychological context is weak; the authors should compare and relate their model to literature on optimal eye-movement strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).  
- Unclear whether the model offers new insights beyond existing optimal-search models or could integrate with them for better performance.  
- Parameter sensitivity is not fully analyzed, and justification for parameter choices is lacking.  
- Missing ablation studies make it unclear which components drive the model’s performance.  
- Overall: promising and theoretically interesting but needs clearer exposition, stronger psychological grounding, and additional empirical analyses.
