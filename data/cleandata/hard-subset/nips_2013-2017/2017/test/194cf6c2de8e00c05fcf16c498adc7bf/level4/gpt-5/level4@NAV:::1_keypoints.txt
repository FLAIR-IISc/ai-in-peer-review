- Presents a bottom-up visual-feature saliency model augmented with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces appealing ideas including defined “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and use of the Least Action Principle.  
- The proposed affordances appear ad hoc and are not sufficiently justified.  
- The Least Action Principle formulation should be related to established eye‑movement research (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab).  
- Limited biological motivation is noted but would be a minor issue if performance were competitive with modern models.  
- The model shows mixed and overall weak performance compared to benchmark deep‑learning salience models.  
- Despite claiming to capture biological eye‑movement dynamics, the paper lacks main‑text figures showing generated scanpaths, presenting only aggregate statistics.
