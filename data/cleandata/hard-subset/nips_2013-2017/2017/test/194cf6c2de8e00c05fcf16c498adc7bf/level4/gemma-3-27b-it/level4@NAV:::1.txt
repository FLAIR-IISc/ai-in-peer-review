This paper presents a computational model of visual feature saliency, integrating bottom-up processing with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics. The work introduces appealing concepts – particularly in defining scanpath-driving “affordances” such as curiosity and brightness invariance, alongside the application of the Least Action Principle – and was a pleasure to read. However, the postulation of these affordances feels somewhat arbitrary and would benefit from further justification. 

Regarding the Least Action Principle, a connection to established eye movement research, including relevant work from the Wolpert lab, would strengthen the theoretical foundation. While a lack of strong biological motivation is increasingly common in high-performing deep learning models of static visual salience, this model’s comparatively weak performance is concerning, especially given its stated goal of capturing biological eye movement *dynamics*. To better assess the model, the main paper would be significantly improved by including visualizations of generated scanpaths, beyond simply reporting aggregate statistics related to eye movement matching.
