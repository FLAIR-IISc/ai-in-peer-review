- Introduces a novel variational framework (EYMOL) modeling scanpath dynamics via a generalized Least Action Principle and Euler-Lagrange equations.  
- Unifies curiosity-driven exploration (gradient magnitude) and brightness invariance (fixation/tracking) in a single mathematical formulation.  
- Diverges from prior saliency-map work by modeling scanpath dynamics directly rather than producing saliency maps as a byproduct.  
- Evaluated on static (MIT1003, CAT2000) and video (SFU) datasets with parameters tuned by simulated annealing to maximize NSS.  
- Originality: provides a fresh, mathematically rigorous unification of two attention principles and connects physics-based motion equations to visual attention.  
- Technical soundness: derivation of motion equations via Euler-Lagrange is clear and well-structured, with reproducible appendix details.  
- Clarity: model description and Lagrangian component exposition (Eq. 9–13) are well presented.  
- Significance: proposes a real-time, potentially biologically plausible scanpath model, advancing beyond static saliency prediction.  
- Performance shortfall: empirical results lag state-of-the-art saliency methods (e.g., 0.65 vs. 0.72 NSS on MIT300), weakening biological-dynamics claims.  
- Missing visualization: absence of explicit scanpath trajectory figures prevents assessment of trajectory realism against human fixations.  
- Biological grounding weak: proposed "affordances" lack deeper biological motivation and omit relevant optimal control literature (e.g., Körding & Wolpert, Triesch et al.).  
- Limited impact: marginal empirical improvements and no behavioral/temporal validation (e.g., temporal correlation with human scanpaths) reduce practical significance.  
- Pro acceptance argument: strong theoretical foundation and novel unification of attention principles with clear mathematics.  
- Con acceptance argument: limited empirical gains, insufficient visual/behavioral evidence, and inadequate engagement with related biological control work.  
- Suggested improvements: add scanpath visualizations comparing human/model trajectories, benchmark against optimal control models, and clarify biological justification for the principles.
