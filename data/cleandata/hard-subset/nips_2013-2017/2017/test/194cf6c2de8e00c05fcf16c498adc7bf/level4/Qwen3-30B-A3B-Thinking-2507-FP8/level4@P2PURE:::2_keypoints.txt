- Model derives an energy-function/differential-equation framework for eye movements from three principles: harmonic oscillator border constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness change, and brightness invariance.
- Framework predicts eye movements in static and dynamic scenes given initial location and velocity.
- Quantitative results are comparable to state-of-the-art on static/dynamic eye-tracking datasets, though not SOTA on every metric.
- Provides a novel, formal bottom-up model of saliency that advances understanding in computational vision and cognitive science.
- Paper’s poor writing and unclear notation hinder evaluation; manuscript needs careful proofreading and clearer exposition.
- Recommend moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using main text to define every variable, function, and expand all abbreviations (including in equations and Section 3).
- Reviewer (psychologist) asks for stronger connections to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to clarify similarities or complementary insights.
