- Proposes EYMOL, a novel trajectory-based eye movement model that departs from traditional saliency-map approaches and uses the principle of least action.  
- EYMOL is built on three principles—trajectory boundedness, curiosity-driven attention, and brightness invariance—encoded in a Lagrangian and yielding Euler-Lagrange motion equations.  
- Model parameters are estimated via simulated annealing and evaluated on multiple image and video datasets.  
- Shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods despite model simplicity.  
- Demonstrates the ability to track moving objects in dynamic scenes, indicating real-world applicability.  
- Strengths: novel paradigm, captures eye movement dynamics, competitive results, and potential impact on perception science.  
- Weaknesses: mathematical derivations and technical details are hard for non-experts to follow, reducing clarity.  
- Limitations: evaluation on dynamic scenes is limited and some model limitations are not fully addressed.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuitive explanations of math, address model limitations, expand dynamic-scene evaluations, and clarify appendix derivations.  
- Overall: a valuable and novel contribution that merits consideration for publication, though clarity and some evaluations should be strengthened.
