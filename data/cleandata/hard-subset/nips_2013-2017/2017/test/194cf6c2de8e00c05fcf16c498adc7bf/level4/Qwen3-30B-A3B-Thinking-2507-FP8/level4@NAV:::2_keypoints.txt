- Paper models human attentional priorities in static and dynamic scenes via an energy-minimization framework for eye movements.  
- Model is built on three principles: harmonic oscillator constraints near image borders/limited retina size; curiosity-driven sensitivity to brightness changes across fine and blurred scales; and brightness-invariance scaling.  
- These principles yield a cost function whose Euler–Lagrange/differential equations predict eye movement trajectories given initial position and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on static/dynamic datasets with human eye-tracking shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art, though not uniformly top across all metrics and datasets.  
- Formalizing saliency and eye-movement modeling is a significant contribution to computational vision and cognitive science, addressing ambiguities in psychological definitions.  
- The proposed model is novel and potentially advances understanding of bottom-up saliency and eye-movement prediction.  
- Paper clarity is insufficient: needs thorough proofreading, expanded definitions of abbreviations, and clearer variable/function definitions for a broad NeurIPS audience.  
- Suggest relocating the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using the freed space to explicitly define all variables and functions.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests deeper engagement with psychological literature on optimal eye-movement/search strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to situate and compare the model’s principles and implications.
