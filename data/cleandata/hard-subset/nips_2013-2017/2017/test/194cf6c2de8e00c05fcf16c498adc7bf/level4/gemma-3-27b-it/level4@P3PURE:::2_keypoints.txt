- Paper proposes a variational energy-based model of visual attention built on three principles: harmonic-oscillator boundary constraint, curiosity-driven sensitivity to brightness changes at multiple scales, and brightness-invariance scaling with change magnitude.
- From that cost function the authors derive differential equations to predict eye-movement trajectories given starting position and initial velocity.
- Method is evaluated on static and dynamic scene datasets with human eye-tracking and achieves competitive, though not uniformly state-of-the-art, performance.
- Contribution is novel and potentially valuable for formalizing saliency and bottom-up models of eye movements in vision and cognitive science.
- Paper’s writing is unclear and difficult to follow, hindering assessment of the work’s full potential.
- Reviewer recommends extensive proofreading and expansion of abbreviations and mathematical notation throughout, especially Section 3.
- Suggest moving the current two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using the freed pages to explicitly define every variable and function used.
- NIPS audience is multidisciplinary; the manuscript needs clearer explanations of formalism and conventions for non-specialists.
- Reviewer asks for connection to literature framing eye movements as optimal information-gathering strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and comparisons with Geisler’s lab work.
- Recommend discussion of whether the proposed model restates existing optimal-search models or provides independent/integrable information to improve combined models.
