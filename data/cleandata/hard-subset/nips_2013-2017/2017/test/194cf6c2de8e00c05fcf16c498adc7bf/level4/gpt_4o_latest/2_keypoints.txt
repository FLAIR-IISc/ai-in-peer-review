- Paper proposes a model of visual attention as an energy function minimized by eye movements, derived from three principles (harmonic oscillator boundary constraint, curiosity-driven brightness variation, and brightness invariance).  
- The model yields differential equations predicting eye movements for static and dynamic scenes from initial position and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on human eye-movement datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- The formalization of saliency and movement modeling is novel and could advance computational vision and cognitive science.  
- Results are strong and offer a refreshing bottom-up perspective, even if not superior on every metric or dataset.  
- The manuscript is poorly written and hard to follow; needs proofreading and clearer exposition.  
- Authors should expand and clarify abbreviations and explicitly define all variables/functions (especially in Section 3 and equations).  
- Recommend moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using the space to improve clarity and definitions.  
- As a psychologist, reviewer requests stronger linkage to psychological literature on optimal eye-movement strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of alignment/divergence with prior findings.
