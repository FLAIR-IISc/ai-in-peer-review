- Positive: Paper presents engaging, principled ideas—novel “affordances” and a Least Action Principle (LAP) formulation—to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Concern: Proposed affordances appear ad hoc and lack sufficient theoretical motivation or empirical justification.  
- Theory gap: LAP derivation should be better connected to eye-movement and motor-control literature (e.g., Wolpert lab, optimal saccade control).  
- Clarification needed: Explain how the proposed cost functions map onto established oculomotor-planning models.  
- Empirics: Model performance is mixed against modern deep-learning saliency baselines and may not clearly outperform static models.  
- Biological plausibility: Limited biological plausibility is problematic given claims of capturing biological dynamics.  
- Evaluation improvements: Must demonstrate added value over static saliency methods and recent scanpath predictors using standardized benchmarks.  
- Ablations/sensitivity: Include clear ablation studies for each affordance term and sensitivity analyses of key hyperparameters.  
- Dynamics-specific analyses: Provide explicit visualizations and quantitative characterizations of generated scanpaths (trajectory overlays, saccade amplitude/direction distributions, fixation durations, inter-saccadic intervals, temporal alignment to human sequences).  
- Overall verdict: Promising combination of concepts and dynamical formulation, but requires stronger theoretical grounding, tighter links to prior oculomotor work, and more compelling, dynamics-focused evaluation.
