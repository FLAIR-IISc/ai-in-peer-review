- Proposal of EYMOL: a visual-attention model that frames scanpath generation as a variational mechanics problem using a generalized Least Action Principle.
- Model formulation: Lagrangian with kinetic energy and a generalized potential combining boundary constraint, curiosity (high-gradient attraction), and brightness invariance.
- Dynamics: Euler–Lagrange equations produce differential equations that directly generate eye trajectories.
- Optimization: model parameters are tuned via simulated annealing.
- Output and evaluation: saliency maps are produced from simulated trajectories and evaluated on MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU datasets.
- Performance: achieves competitive results despite saliency being a byproduct rather than the primary output.
- Originality: novel and elegant departure from the two-stage saliency-map-plus-fixation paradigm.
- Theoretical contribution: provides a unified framework capturing both exploratory saccades and fixation/tracking behaviors.
- Empirical support: validated across multiple standard static and dynamic datasets.
- Clarity concern: mathematical derivations are dense and rely on classical mechanics, lacking sufficient intuition for a broad ML audience.
- Venue fit concern: contribution is more a theoretical model of perception than a general-purpose ML technique, raising questions about suitability for NIPS.
