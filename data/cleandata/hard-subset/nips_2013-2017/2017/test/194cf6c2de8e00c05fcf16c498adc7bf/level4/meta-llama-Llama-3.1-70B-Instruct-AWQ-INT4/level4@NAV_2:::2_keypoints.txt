- Paper proposes a novel variational model of visual attention that predicts eye movements by minimizing an energy function.  
- Model is built from three principles: harmonic-oscillator border constraint, curiosity-driven response to brightness changes at multiple scales, and brightness-invariance.  
- The authors derive differential equations from the cost function to predict eye trajectories in static and dynamic scenes.  
- Method is quantitatively evaluated on human eye-tracking datasets for static and dynamic images.  
- Performance is comparable to state-of-the-art on the evaluated datasets.  
- The approach offers a fresh, formally defined perspective on saliency and eye-movement modeling within bottom-up tradition.  
- Paper is poorly written and difficult to follow, with unclear notation and unexplained abbreviations.  
- Section 3 and equation variables need clearer definitions; recommend moving the 2-page appendix to supplementary material and using freed pages for definitions.  
- Recommend proofreading and broader audience-friendly exposition because NIPS readership spans multiple disciplines.  
- Reviewer requests connection to psychological literature on optimal eye-movement strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of integration or comparison.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, quantitative evaluation, competitive performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: clarity and presentation issues, missing links to relevant psychological work; revision required before publication.
