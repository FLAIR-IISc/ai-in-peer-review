- Proposes a novel eye-movement model (EYMOL) that defines gaze trajectories via a “Least Action Principle” rather than saliency maps.  
- Clearly contrasts the approach with traditional saliency-map and fixation-optimization methods.  
- Provides a sound summary of existing techniques and literature.  
- Mathematical details are extensive (appendix) and partly beyond the reviewer’s physics background, though the core idea is understandable.  
- Model is applied to a real-world eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against multiple alternatives.  
- Results are generally favorable and validate the approach, though it does not outperform competitors on every metric.  
- Likely of particular interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees).  
- Suitability for NIPS is uncertain; recommends evaluation by reviewers with specialized expertise.  
- Paper is novel and could inspire further empirical research in perception science.
