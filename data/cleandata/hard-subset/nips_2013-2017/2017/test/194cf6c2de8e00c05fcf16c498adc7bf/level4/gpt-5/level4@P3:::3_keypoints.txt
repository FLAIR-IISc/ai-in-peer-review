- Paper proposes EYMOL, a physics-inspired variational model that generates scanpaths by minimizing a generalized action functional.  
- Lagrangian terms: confining potential (image bounds), curiosity term (local + peripheral gradients alternated by cos^2/sin^2 modulation), and a brightness-invariance soft optical-flow constraint.  
- Stationary points yield Euler–Lagrange equations that define attention dynamics; parameters fit with simulated annealing on CAT2000; saliency maps obtained by trajectory visit counts.  
- Evaluations performed on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000 (images) and SFU (videos), compared to classic and learning-based saliency models.  
- Approximate brightness-invariance upper bound performs comparably to the exact term for static images.  
- Novelty: variational mechanics framing for attention dynamics is original and unifies saccades and tracking within one equation set.  

Strengths:
- Conceptually elegant and unified dynamical formulation where saliency emerges as a byproduct.  
- Single framework handles static and dynamic scenes with few parameters.  
- Competitive empirical performance on standard benchmarks given model simplicity and lack of high-level features.  
- Clear connection to optical-flow constraint offering a plausible fixation/tracking mechanism.  
- Theoretical attention to well-posedness and interpretable role for each term.  

Technical weaknesses / suggestions:
- Kinetic energy is written as K(ẋ) = (1/m)ẋ^2 versus the standard (m/2)∥ẋ∥^2 — either justify or correct and update derivations/parameters.  
- Time-varying curiosity modulation via cos^2(ωt)/sin^2(ωt) appears ad hoc — report how ω is chosen and provide sensitivity/ablation analyses (constant, no alternation, local vs peripheral).  
- Provide formal discussion of when the brightness-invariance bound is tight and its dynamical implications (acts like spatially varying velocity penalty).  
- Clarify stability bound λ < m/4 given b∈[0,1] after Gaussian weighting/blurring — confirm normalization across datasets and units to ensure D>0.  

Evaluation/design weaknesses:
- Center-bias and blur tuning (central Gaussian, grid search) may conflate model gains with generic priors — report center-bias–robust metrics (sAUC) and ablations without center bias/blur tuning.  
- Initialization sensitivity: runs initialized near center — show results for broader/different initializations, steps/saccades per second, and how multiple runs map to inter-subject variability.  
- Comparisons should include stronger contemporary deep saliency baselines and ensure comparable post-processing; report statistical significance and variance.  
- Scanpath claims need direct temporal evaluation (MultiMatch, ScanMatch, string-edit), not only heatmap metrics.  
- Video evaluation limited (SFU has 12 clips) — report dynamic-specific metrics (sAUC over time, tAUC) and quantitative tracking analyses.  

Clarity, reproducibility, and presentation:
- Manuscript needs grammar and consistency edits, consistent notation, and definition of all symbols at first use.  
- Several references incomplete; include full citations and situate work relative to dynamic/optimal-control/active-inference literature.  
- Add intuition paragraphs and example trajectories showing each term’s effect; include component ablation figures.  
- Specify all parameters (m, k, λ, ηb, ηp, ω), solver/integrator details, preprocessing, and release code and parameter files.  

Originality and significance:
- Variational mechanics framing is novel and promising but should be better situated vs prior dynamical/optimal-control eye-movement models and clarified in assumptions/predictions.  
- Significance is high as an interpretable alternative to black-box models, but broader impact requires bias-robust and scanpath-centric evaluations and clearer theoretical grounding.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Elegant, unified dynamical formulation with clear intuitions.  
- Generates scanpaths and saliency maps applicable to images and videos.  
- Competitive benchmark results despite simplicity and potential to inspire physics-based attention research.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Empirical evaluation confounded by center bias/blur tuning and lacks direct scanpath analysis.  
- Unclear technical details (kinetic term scaling, parameter normalization, ω choice).  
- Writing, references, and reproducibility details need significant improvement.  
- Limited comparison to strongest baselines and constrained video evaluation.  

Overall verdict:
- Creative and promising with appealing theoretical underpinnings and encouraging results, but currently borderline for acceptance pending clearer derivations, bias-robust and scanpath-centric evaluations, ablations, and polished presentation.
