- Proposes a model of visual attention based on three principled energy terms minimized by eye movements to predict salient regions in static and dynamic images.  
- Principle 1: eye motions constrained by a harmonic oscillator near image borders within a finite retinal extent.  
- Principle 2: curiosity-driven term that emphasizes regions with large luminance changes in both high-resolution and blurred versions.  
- Principle 3: brightness invariance term that scales with luminance changes.  
- Derives differential equations from the combined cost function to predict eye trajectories given initial position and velocity.  
- Evaluated quantitatively on static and moving scene datasets paired with human eye movements, achieving performance comparable to state of the art.  
- Offers a novel, potentially clarifying formal framework for saliency and bottom-up eye-movement modeling, though not universally SOTA across all metrics.  
- Manuscript is difficult to follow; needs thorough proofreading and expansion/clarification of abbreviations, variables, and functions.  
- Suggests moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using saved space to define each variable/function for a multidisciplinary audience.  
- Recommends stronger links to psychological literature on optimal information-gathering eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and a discussion comparing or integrating related models.
