- Proposes a model for visual attention in static and dynamic scenes by formalizing three principles as an energy function minimized by eye movements.  
- Principle 1: eye movements are constrained by a harmonic oscillator at image borders within a limited-size retina.  
- Principle 2: a curiosity-driven rule highlights regions with large brightness changes at both fine and blurred scales.  
- Principle 3: brightness-invariance term that increases with changes in brightness.  
- From the combined cost function the authors derive differential equations to predict eye-movement trajectories given start location and initial velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Offers a novel, formalized, bottom-up perspective on saliency and eye-movement modeling, potentially valuable despite not outperforming on every metric.  
- Paper’s writing is poor and hard to follow; reviewer recommends proofreading, expanding abbreviations, defining all variables/functions, and moving the two-page appendix to supplementary material.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests discussion of connections to psychological/optimal-search literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and how this approach compares or could integrate with those models.
