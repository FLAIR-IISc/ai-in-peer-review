- Proposes a novel variational/Least Action framework to model visual attention and derive attentional scanpaths via differential equations.  
- Defines an action functional with terms for boundedness, curiosity (image detail + peripheral context), and brightness invariance.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on static and dynamic scene benchmarks, comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Claims potential for real-time application due to efficient numerical integration.  
- Offers a unified, physically-motivated model that could integrate saccades and object tracking.  
- Core idea is interesting and potentially valuable at the intersection of cognitive science, neuroscience, and computer vision.  
- Writing is dense and unclear, with ambiguous presentation that hinders understanding and evaluation.  
- Mathematical derivations are insufficiently explained or motivated, often relegated to the appendix.  
- Limited contextualization and motivation for specific model/design choices (e.g., form of potential energy terms).  
- Insufficient literature review, notably missing relevant psychological/cognitive work (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).  
- Abbreviations and variables are poorly defined, reducing accessibility for non-specialists.  
- Parameter estimation relies on simulated annealing and is sensitive to choices; discussion of this is inadequate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, seemingly sound derivations, competitive results, promising unified model.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity/readability, limited motivation/context, weak related-work discussion, and low accessibility for broad NIPS audience.  
- Recommendation: reject but strongly encourage revision and resubmission with clearer writing, integrated core derivations, expanded literature review, and detailed parameter-sensitivity discussion.
