This paper introduces a novel framework for modeling eye movements, diverging from traditional saliency map-based approaches that rely on optimization for fixation point selection. Instead, the authors propose a model (EYMOL) grounded in a “Least Action Principle,” directly operating on gaze trajectories. While a comprehensive evaluation of the mathematical details, particularly within the extensive appendix, was limited by my background in physics, the core concepts and distinctions from existing models were clear. The model is thoughtfully evaluated using a real-world eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against several alternative approaches. The results demonstrate promising performance, supporting the validity of this new approach, though it doesn’t uniformly outperform all comparisons. 

I anticipate this work will be of significant interest to researchers in the vision science community, potentially at venues like VSS. Its suitability for NIPS may benefit from consideration alongside reviews from experts in the field. Nevertheless, the paper presents a novel and potentially impactful theoretical contribution that could stimulate further empirical investigation within perception science, demonstrating both novelty and merit.
