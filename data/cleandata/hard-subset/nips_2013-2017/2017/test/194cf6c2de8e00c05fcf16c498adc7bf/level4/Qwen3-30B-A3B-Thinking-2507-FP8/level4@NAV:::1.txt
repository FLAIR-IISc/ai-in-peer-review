

This computational neuroscience paper proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model enhanced by dynamical systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics. I found the paper enjoyable and appreciated its novel ideas in defining "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) that drive scanpaths, as well as the application of the Least Action Principle. However, these "affordances" are somewhat ad hoc and lack deeper theoretical motivation. Regarding the Least Action Principle derivation, it would have strengthened the work to connect it to established eye movement research, particularly the foundational and recent contributions from the Wolpert lab. While the absence of biological motivation is a minor concern in the current era of benchmark-driven deep learning models for static visual salience, the model's core limitation lies in its subpar performance relative to state-of-the-art approaches. Given the claim that the model captures biological eye movement dynamics, the main paper should include actual visualizations characterizing the generated scanpaths—not merely static statistics—to substantiate this assertion.
