- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model using dynamical systems to model human scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies the Principle of Least Action to drive scanpaths.  
- Affordances are introduced in an ad-hoc way and lack sufficient motivation or justification.  
- The use of the Principle of Least Action should be connected to established eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab work).  
- Model shows poor performance compared to state-of-the-art deep learning saliency methods, which is a major concern.  
- Biological motivation is weak, though this may be less critical given strong deep models for static saliency.  
- Main claim is modeling dynamics, yet the paper evaluates mainly static location metrics instead of characterizing scanpath dynamics.  
- The paper needs figures and dynamic metrics that explicitly analyze and validate the generated scanpaths.
