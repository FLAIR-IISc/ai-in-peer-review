- Proposes a novel approach to modeling visual attention that departs from traditional saliency map methods.  
- Thoroughly summarizes current state of the art and highlights limitations of existing models.  
- Introduces EYMOL, a model based on a generalized Least Action Principle modeling scanpaths as particle motion in a potential field.  
- Approach is distinct from prior methods that use optimization or maximization to select fixation points.  
- Mathematical formulation is rigorous, with a detailed Euler–Lagrange derivation provided in the appendix.  
- Some mathematical details may be challenging for non-experts, although core ideas are clearly explained.  
- Applied to real eye-movement datasets and shows favorable results compared to alternative models.  
- Strengths: originality, clarity, strong theoretical grounding, thorough evaluation, and clear writing.  
- Weakness: limited relevance to the NIPS community due to primary focus on perception science.  
- Evaluation could be more comprehensive and comparisons to alternative models more detailed.  
- Overall rating 7/10: technically sound but needs broader evaluation to increase impact.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to perception science but may not align with NIPS’s core interests.
