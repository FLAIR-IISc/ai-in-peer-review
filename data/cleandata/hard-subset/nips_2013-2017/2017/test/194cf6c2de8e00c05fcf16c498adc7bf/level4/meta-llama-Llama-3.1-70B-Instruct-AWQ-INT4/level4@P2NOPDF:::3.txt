The paper introduces a novel approach to studying eye movements, providing a clear summary of the current state of the art in this field. Although I am not an expert in perception or eye movements, the authors' overview appears to be accurate. Existing methods typically rely on a saliency map defined over an image, with fixation points selected through optimization or maximization techniques. In contrast, the proposed model, EYMOL, operates under a different assumption, using the "Least Action Principle" to define gaze trajectories directly. While some mathematical details, particularly in the appendix, were beyond my evaluation due to my limited physics background, I was still able to grasp the key elements of the approach and its distinctiveness from previous models.

The authors apply their model to a real dataset of eye movements and compare its performance to various alternative models. Although the results do not overwhelmingly favor the new approach in every aspect, they are generally positive and demonstrate the model's validity. The paper may be of interest to certain members of the vision science community, such as attendees of the Vision Sciences Society (VSS). However, its contribution to NIPS is less clear, and I believe this should be assessed in conjunction with the opinions of more expert reviewers. Nevertheless, the paper likely presents a new and interesting theory that could inspire further empirical research, exhibiting both novelty and merit in the field of perception sciences.
