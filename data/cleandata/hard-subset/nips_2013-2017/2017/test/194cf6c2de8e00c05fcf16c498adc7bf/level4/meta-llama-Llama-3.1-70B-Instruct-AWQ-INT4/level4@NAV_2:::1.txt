This paper proposes a novel model of visual attention based on the principle that there are foundational laws driving the emergence of visual attention. The authors devise variational laws of eye movement that rely on a generalized view of the Least Action Principle in physics. The model combines three fundamental principles: boundedness of the trajectory, curiosity-driven principle, and brightness invariance. These principles are given a mathematical expression by a variational approach based on a generalization of least action, whose stationary point leads to the correspondent Euler-Lagrange differential equations of the focus of attention.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a detailed description of the model and its underlying principles. The use of mathematical derivations and equations is appropriate and well-explained. The experimental setup and results are also clearly described.

The model is original and differs from previous contributions in the field. The use of the Least Action Principle and the combination of the three fundamental principles are novel and interesting approaches. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models and discuss the advantages and limitations of their approach.

The results are significant and demonstrate the potential of the model in capturing the dynamics of scanpaths. The authors report promising results on both image and video datasets of saliency. The model's ability to capture biological eye movement dynamics is also an important contribution to the field.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement. The model's performance is not consistently better than state-of-the-art models, and the authors could provide more detailed analysis and discussion of the results. Additionally, the authors could provide more biological motivation and relate their work to actual research in eye movement research.
