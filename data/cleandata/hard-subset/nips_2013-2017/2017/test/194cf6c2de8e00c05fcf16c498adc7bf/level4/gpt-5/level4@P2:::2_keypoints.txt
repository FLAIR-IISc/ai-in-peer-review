- Proposes a principled variational (least-action) model of visual attention producing scanpaths and saliency as a byproduct.  
- Encodes three terms in a Lagrangian: boundary harmonic potential, curiosity (local vs peripheral gradient energy), and brightness-invariance (optical-flow-like) constraint.  
- Euler–Lagrange equations from action minimization govern scanpath dynamics; saliency maps built from trajectory visit counts.  
- Evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000 (images) and SFU (videos), showing competitive performance with established baselines.  
- Contrasts with standard saliency-first pipelines by deriving scanpaths directly; brightness-invariance connects to classical optical-flow methods.  
- Needs clearer comparison to normative/optimal eye-movement models (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler) and interpretation of curiosity term as information gain.  
- Variational derivation is sound; approximate brightness-invariance is empirically justified for static images.  
- Numerical integration and stability discussion are adequate, but simulated annealing details (acceptance, neighborhood) are under-specified.  
- Experimental choices (center bias, blur tuning, center initialization) can materially affect scores; ablations and sensitivity analyses are needed.  
- Reproducibility is hindered by undefined symbols and missing preprocessing/integration details (bt, bx, px, NSS, blur construction, boundary conditions, step sizes).  
- Paper suffers from notation and editorial clarity issues; recommends proofreading, unified notation, and moving extended appendix to supplement.  
- The approach is original and elegant in unifying curiosity and brightness invariance via Euler–Lagrange dynamics.  
- Significant as a conceptually unified, real-time-capable model handling saccades and tracking, though not uniformly state-of-the-art.  
- Suggestion: explicitly relate formulation to optimal-search literature and check consistency with reported gaze statistics.  
- Suggestion: provide a symbol table, full preprocessing and integration parameters, and simulated annealing schedule and proposals.  
- Suggestion: include ablations for curiosity subterms, brightness weight, center bias, and initialization; report parameter sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: ensure fair baseline comparisons by matching center-bias/blur post-processing across methods.  
- Pros: novel variational formulation, unified saccade/tracking treatment, competitive results, interpretable with few parameters.  
- Cons: clarity and reproducibility issues, limited analysis of design choices, missing psychological/normative comparisons, not consistently state-of-the-art.
