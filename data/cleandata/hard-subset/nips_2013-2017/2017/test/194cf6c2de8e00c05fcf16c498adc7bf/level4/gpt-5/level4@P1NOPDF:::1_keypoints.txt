- Proposes a bottom-up visual-feature saliency model combined with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces novel “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and uses a Least Action Principle—ideas the reviewer found appealing.  
- The defined affordances feel ad hoc and are not sufficiently motivated.  
- The Least Action Principle derivation should be related to established eye-movement control literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Biological motivation is weak, though this may be less critical given powerful deep-learning saliency models.  
- Major concern: model performance is poor relative to state-of-the-art deep-learning static salience models.  
- Lacks visual examples of generated scanpaths in the main paper—only overall statistics are presented.
