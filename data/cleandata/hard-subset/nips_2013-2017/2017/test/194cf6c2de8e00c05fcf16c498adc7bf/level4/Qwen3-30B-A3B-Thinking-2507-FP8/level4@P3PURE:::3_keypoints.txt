- Introduces EYMOL, a novel framework modeling eye movements by operating on gaze trajectories via a "Least Action Principle" rather than using saliency maps or fixation optimization.  
- Reviewer acknowledges limited expertise in perception science and cannot fully assess the appendix’s physics-heavy mathematical details.  
- Core conceptual distinction and methodology are clearly articulated.  
- Authors show EYMOL provides a distinct theoretical foundation compared to existing models.  
- The approach is rigorously evaluated on real eye-movement datasets and benchmarked against multiple alternative models.  
- EYMOL does not consistently outperform all baselines across every metric, but results are generally favorable and support its validity.  
- The contribution is conceptually novel and theoretically grounded, likely of interest to vision-science venues (e.g., VSS).  
- Reviewer questions whether the work constitutes a substantial advancement for NIPS, as it aligns more with empirical perception science than central ML or computational neuroscience problems.  
- Recommends that the paper’s significance for NIPS be weighed by reviewers with deeper expertise in vision science.  
- Paper advances an interesting theoretical perspective with clear novelty that could inspire future empirical work.
