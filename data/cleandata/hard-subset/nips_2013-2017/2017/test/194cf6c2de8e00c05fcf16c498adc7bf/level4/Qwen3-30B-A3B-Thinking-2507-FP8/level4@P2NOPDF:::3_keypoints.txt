- Introduces EYMOL, a novel trajectory-centric framework for modeling eye movements that departs from saliency-based fixation approaches.  
- Models gaze as continuous paths governed by a "Least Action Principle" instead of discrete fixation points.  
- Authors provide a clear summary of prior work, though reviewer notes limited personal expertise in eye-movement science.  
- Mathematical appendix contains physics-heavy derivations that the reviewer found hard to follow, but core model concepts are clear.  
- Empirical validation on a real-world eye-tracking dataset with rigorous comparisons to multiple established baselines.  
- Results are generally competitive and often superior, but EYMOL does not uniformly outperform all competitors across every metric.  
- Offers a fresh theoretical perspective likely to interest vision science and the Vision Sciences Society community.  
- Main contribution appears more substantive for perception science than for core ML/NeurIPS audiences.  
- Paper lacks explicit connections to broader ML challenges (e.g., scalability, integration with deep learning, computational efficiency).  
- Recommendation: accept if authors better justify relevance to ML/NeurIPS or otherwise consider a vision/neuroscience venue.
