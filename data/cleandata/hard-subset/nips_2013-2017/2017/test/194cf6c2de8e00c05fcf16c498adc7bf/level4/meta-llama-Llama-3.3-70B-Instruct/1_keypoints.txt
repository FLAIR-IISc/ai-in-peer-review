- Proposes a novel bottom-up visual feature saliency model that uses dynamical systems to generate scanpath dynamics.  
- Paper is engaging and introduces interesting concepts like "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and application of the Least Action Principle.  
- The introduced "affordances" are presented intuitively but lack sufficient justification and grounding.  
- The paper needs a deeper discussion of the Least Action Principle's relevance to existing eye-movement research (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Lack of biological motivation may be acceptable, but the model shows inconsistent performance compared to deep-learning salience benchmarks.  
- Given the claim to capture biological eye-movement dynamics, the main paper should include detailed scanpath characterizations beyond static metrics.
