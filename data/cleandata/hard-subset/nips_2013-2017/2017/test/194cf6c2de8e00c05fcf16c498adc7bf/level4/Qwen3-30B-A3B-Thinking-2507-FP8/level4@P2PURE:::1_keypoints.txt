- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model augmented with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces a novel conceptualization of "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) driving scanpaths.  
- Employs the Least Action Principle in the model formulation.  
- Affordances appear ad hoc and lack sufficient theoretical motivation.  
- Least Action Principle derivation should be explicitly connected to established eye-movement literature, especially recent and foundational work from the Wolpert lab.  
- Lack of biological motivation is a minor concern given current benchmark-driven deep learning focus on static salience.  
- Model’s main weakness is poor performance relative to state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Given the claim of modeling biological eye-movement dynamics, the paper should include actual scanpath figures (not just scalar metrics) to demonstrate replication of empirical dynamics.
