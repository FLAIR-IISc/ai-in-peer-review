- Proposes a model of visual attention based on three principles: harmonic-oscillator-constrained eye movements with limited retina, a curiosity-driven focus on brightness changes at multiple scales, and increasing brightness invariance with brightness change.  
- Combines these principles into a cost function yielding differential equations to predict eye movements in static and dynamic scenes.  
- Evaluated quantitatively on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets, showing comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods (but not SOTA on all metrics).  
- Novel and innovative attempt to formalize saliency and model eye movements within a bottom-up framework, addressing vague saliency definitions in psychology.  
- Paper suffers from poor writing and organization; needs proofreading, clearer notation, expansion of abbreviations, and explicit definitions of variables/functions.  
- Recommend moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using main paper space to define mathematical terms for a multidisciplinary NIPS audience.  
- As a psychologist, reviewer requests connection to literature on eye movements as optimal information-gathering strategies (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).  
- Paper should discuss how the proposed approach compares to or integrates with existing models and whether it offers independent insights or restates known concepts.
