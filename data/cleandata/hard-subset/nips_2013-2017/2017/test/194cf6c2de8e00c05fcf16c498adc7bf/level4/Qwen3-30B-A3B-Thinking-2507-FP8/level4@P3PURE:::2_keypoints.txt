- Proposes a novel model of visual attention for static and dynamic scenes derived from three principles: border-constrained harmonic oscillator, curiosity-driven response to brightness changes across resolutions, and increasing brightness invariance.  
- Formalizes these principles into an energy/cost function and resulting differential equations that predict eye movement trajectories given initial position and velocity.  
- Evaluated quantitatively on static/dynamic datasets with human eye-tracking; performance is robust and comparable to state-of-the-art on several metrics/datasets.  
- Advances formal definitions of saliency and bottom-up eye-movement dynamics, offering a distinct theoretical perspective valuable to computational vision and cognitive science.  
- Paper clarity is poor: needs proofreading, expansion of all abbreviations (including in equations and Sec. 3), and clearer notation/definitions.  
- Strong recommendation to move the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use the freed pages to rigorously define every variable and function for a multidisciplinary audience.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests explicit connection to psychological literature on optimal information-gathering eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to clarify whether the model restates, complements, or extends optimal-search findings.
