This paper introduces a novel approach to modeling eye movements. The authors provide a clear and accurate summary of the current state of the art, to the best of my understanding; I note that I am not an expert in perception or oculomotor behavior. Existing models typically rely on a saliency map defined over the image and select fixation points via an optimization or maximization procedure. In contrast, the proposed model (EYMOL) operates directly on gaze trajectories and is grounded in a Least Action Principle.

Some of the mathematical derivations—especially those in the extensive appendix—are beyond my expertise due to their physics background requirements. Nevertheless, the core ideas are accessible, and the distinction from prior saliency-based approaches is clear.

The model is evaluated on real eye-movement datasets and compared against a range of alternative models. Although it is not superior on every metric, its performance is generally competitive and often favorable, supporting the plausibility of the approach.

I expect this work will be of interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees). I am less certain about its fit for NIPS, but I defer to reviewers with deeper expertise in perception and eye-movement modeling. Overall, the paper advances a novel and thought-provoking theoretical framework that could stimulate further empirical research and has clear merit for the perception sciences.
