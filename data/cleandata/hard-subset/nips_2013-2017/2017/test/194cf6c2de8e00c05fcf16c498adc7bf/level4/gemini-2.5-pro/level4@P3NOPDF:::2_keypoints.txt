- Paper proposes a variational model of visual attention based on three principles: spatial constraint (eye as harmonic oscillator near image boundaries), curiosity-driven multi-scale brightness-change saliency, and brightness invariance.  
- From a cost function integrating these principles, the authors derive differential equations that predict gaze trajectories from initial position and velocity.  
- Model is evaluated on static and dynamic scene benchmarks with human eye-tracking and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art on several metrics.  
- Strength: provides a novel, principled formalization of saliency and eye movements within a bottom-up variational framework.  
- Strength: offers a theoretically elegant and innovative contribution to computational vision and cognitive science despite not dominating all metrics.  
- Major weakness: manuscript is poorly written and extremely difficult to follow, with ambiguous exposition and derivations.  
- Major weakness: essential information is relegated to a two-page Appendix, obscuring understanding of the model.  
- Recommendation: perform a comprehensive proofreading and rewrite, preferably with outside readers, to improve clarity and accessibility.  
- Recommendation: clearly define every variable, function, operator, and abbreviation; include a dedicated notation table or section.  
- Recommendation: move essential appendix material into the main paper and relocate nonessential derivations to supplementary material to free space for clearer exposition.  
- Recommendation: connect the model to cognitive science and information-theoretic literature on eye movements (e.g., optimal search models) and discuss similarities, differences, or integrations.  
- Reviewer is willing to reconsider the rating if the authors undertake a major revision to substantially improve clarity.
