- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model paired with a dynamical-systems formulation to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces interesting concepts of “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies the Least Action Principle.  
- Affordances are somewhat ad hoc and not sufficiently motivated.  
- Derivation should be tied more explicitly to existing eye-movement and motor-control literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Biological grounding is limited; more concerningly, model shows mixed and sometimes poor performance versus benchmark saliency models.  
- Main paper lacks concrete figures of generated scanpaths—should show scanpath examples, not just overall statistics and human-match metrics.  
- Reviewer enjoyed reading the paper.
