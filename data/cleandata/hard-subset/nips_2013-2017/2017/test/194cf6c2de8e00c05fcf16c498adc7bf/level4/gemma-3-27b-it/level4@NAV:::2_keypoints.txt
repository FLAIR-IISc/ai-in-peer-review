- Proposes a novel computational model of visual attention for static and dynamic scenes framed as an energy minimization problem.  
- Model built on three principles: harmonic-oscillator constraints on eye movements, a multi-scale “curiosity” term for brightness changes, and a brightness-invariance luminance sensitivity.  
- Derives differential equations to predict eye movements given initial conditions.  
- Quantitative evaluation on human eye-tracking data yields competitive results but not uniformly state-of-the-art across all metrics and datasets.  
- Offers a distinctive, promising bottom-up formal framework for salience and eye-movement modeling.  
- Presentation clarity is lacking; paper is difficult to follow and needs thorough proofreading.  
- Abbreviations (in equations and Section 3) must be fully expanded and explained.  
- Appendix should be moved to supplementary material; reclaimed space used to explicitly define all variables and functions, even standard ones, for an interdisciplinary audience.  
- Psychological context is weak; authors should relate the model to literature on eye movements as efficient information gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).  
- Recommend comparing/contrasting the model with optimal-search findings from Geisler’s lab to assess compatibility or complementarity.
