This computational neuroscience paper proposes a bottom-up visual feature salience model, modified by dynamic system modeling, aimed at capturing the dynamics of scan paths. I was delighted to read this paper because it contains some beautiful ideas for defining "affordance" (curiosity, brightness constant) that drives scan paths, as well as the concept of the Lister Action Principle. This "affordance" is perhaps somewhat self-evident suggestion and not further encouraged.

It would have been interesting to relate the Lister Action Principle and its emergence in this context to real-world eye movement research, such as past and recent work by the Woolpert Lab. Although the lack of biological motivation (static) is a minor problem in the era of benchmark-breaking deep learning models of visual salience, the problem with this model is that its mixed performance with these models is weak. Since the model claims to capture the dynamics of biological eye movement, it would have been interesting to see the actual statistics generated by models that identify scan paths, rather than just their overall statistics.
