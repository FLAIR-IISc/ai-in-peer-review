- Proposes an energy-minimization model of gaze based on three principles: spatial harmonic-oscillator constraint, curiosity-driven multi-scale spatial-frequency preference, and brightness-invariance favoring temporal luminance change.  
- Derives differential equations from the cost function to predict gaze trajectories from initial position and velocity.  
- Evaluated on multiple static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and performs competitively with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: provides a formal, principled theoretical framework for saliency and saccadic scanpaths, addressing vague operational definitions in the field.  
- Strength: offers a novel, distinct perspective that could advance theoretical understanding even if it does not uniformly outperform all benchmarks.  
- Major weakness: paper is unclear and hard to follow, requiring conjecture to interpret the approach.  
- Suggestion: explicitly define every variable, function, symbol, and abbreviation in mathematical derivations and Section 3.  
- Suggestion: make the mathematical exposition self-contained and accessible to an interdisciplinary audience (NIPS readership).  
- Suggestion: move the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use main text space to expand explanations and intuition behind principles.  
- Literature connection: should relate and compare to psychological optimal-information models of eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to clarify novelty and integration with existing theories.
