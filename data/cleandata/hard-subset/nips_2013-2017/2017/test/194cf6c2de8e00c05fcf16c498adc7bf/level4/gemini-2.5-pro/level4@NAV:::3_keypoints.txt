- Introduces a novel framework (EYMOL) for modeling eye movements.  
- Clearly positions work relative to state-of-the-art saliency-map models that use optimization/maximization of fixation points.  
- EYMOL departs fundamentally by defining gaze trajectories directly via a "Least Action Principle."  
- Reviewer lacks expertise in perception; found the physics-heavy mathematical derivations in the appendix hard to fully evaluate.  
- Core concepts and the model's departure from prior approaches are presented clearly.  
- Model is validated on a real eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against alternatives; results are broadly favorable though not best on all metrics.  
- Likely of strong interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS); less clearly a fit for NIPS without input from domain experts.  
- Advances a new, potentially inspiring theory with novelty and merit for perception sciences.
