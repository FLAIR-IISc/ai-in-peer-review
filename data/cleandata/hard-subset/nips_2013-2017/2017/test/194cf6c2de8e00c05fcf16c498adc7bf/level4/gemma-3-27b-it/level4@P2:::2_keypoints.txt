- Proposes a novel variational/physics-inspired model of visual attention that frames scanpath prediction as an optimization problem.
- Derives differential equations for eye movements from a Lagrangian incorporating retinal boundedness, curiosity-driven attraction to details, and brightness invariance.
- Validates the model on static and dynamic scenes, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art saliency methods.
- Offers a unified framework capable of modeling both saccades and object tracking within a single system.
- Grounds the approach in principles analogous to the Least Action Principle, providing a strong theoretical foundation.
- Presents an intriguing and distinct perspective bridging computer vision, neuroscience, and cognitive science.
- Writing is dense and unclear, making the mathematical derivations and model details hard to follow.
- Excessive undefined abbreviations and notational shortcuts create barriers for readers outside the subfield.
- Important derivations and parameter choices lack sufficient explanation and justification.
- Paper insufficiently connects to psychological and eye-movement literature, limiting contextualization of contributions.
- Suggests moving some appendix material into the main text to better explain intuition and derivations.
- Results are promising but do not consistently outperform existing methods across benchmarks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical grounding, competitive results, and potential to inspire new research directions.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, inadequate contextualization, and insufficient detail on derivations and parameters.
- Overall recommendation: borderline submission; reject now but encourage resubmission after substantial rewrite and expanded explanations.
- Cites relevant prior work (Najemnik & Geisler 2008) as related literature to consider.
