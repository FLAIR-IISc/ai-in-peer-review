- Introduces a novel approach to studying eye movements with a clear summary of the state of the art.  
- Reviewer not an expert in perception/eye movements but finds the overview accurate.  
- Notes existing methods use image saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization/maximization.  
- Proposes EYMOL, which instead uses the Least Action Principle to define gaze trajectories directly.  
- Some mathematical details (appendix) were beyond the reviewer's physics background, but main ideas and distinctiveness were understandable.  
- Model evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared to alternatives; results are generally positive though not uniformly superior.  
- Work likely of interest to vision science audiences (e.g., VSS), but its fit/contribution to NIPS is uncertain and should be judged by experts.  
- Paper presents a new, interesting theory with novelty and potential to inspire further empirical research in perception sciences.
