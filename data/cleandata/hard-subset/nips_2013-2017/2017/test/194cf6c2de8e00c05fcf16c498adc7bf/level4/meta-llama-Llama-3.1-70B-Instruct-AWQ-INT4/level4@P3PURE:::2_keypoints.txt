- Paper models locations that capture human attention in static/dynamic images via an energy-minimization framework for eye movements derived from three principles.  
- Principle 1: Eye movements are constrained by a harmonic oscillator at image borders within a limited-size retina.  
- Principle 2: A "curiosity-driven" term highlights regions with large brightness changes in both fine and blurred image versions.  
- Principle 3: A brightness-invariance term that grows with changes in brightness.  
- Authors derive a cost function and differential equations to predict eye trajectories given starting position and initial velocity for static and dynamic scenes.  
- Quantitative evaluation on eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art, though not best on every metric.  
- Contribution: novel, formal definition of saliency and a fresh bottom-up model for eye movements with potential value for vision and cognitive science.  
- Major weakness: poor, hard-to-follow writing—needs proofreading, clearer variable/function definitions (move appendix to supplement and expand main text).  
- Suggested improvement: connect and compare to psychological/optimal search literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to clarify novelty and integration with existing models.
