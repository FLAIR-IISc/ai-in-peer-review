- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model augmented by dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.
- Introduces novel "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) as drivers of scanpaths.
- Applies the Least Action Principle to derive scanpath dynamics.
- The proposed affordances feel somewhat ad hoc and lack deeper theoretical justification.
- The Least Action derivation should be tied to established eye-movement literature, particularly work from the Wolpert lab.
- Lack of biological motivation is a minor concern given the prevalence of benchmark-driven deep learning in static salience.
- Main limitation is underperformance relative to state-of-the-art methods.
- Claim of capturing biological eye-movement dynamics is unsupported without actual scanpath visualizations in the main paper, not just summary statistics.
