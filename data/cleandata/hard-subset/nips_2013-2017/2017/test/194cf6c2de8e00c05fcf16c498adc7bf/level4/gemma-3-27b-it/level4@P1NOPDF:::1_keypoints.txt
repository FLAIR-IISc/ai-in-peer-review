- Proposes a bottom-up saliency model combined with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics, introducing affordances (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and using the Least Action Principle.  
- Affordances appear somewhat ad hoc and require stronger motivation.  
- Missing connections to prior eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab) that explore related concepts.  
- Model shows mixed performance versus strong deep-learning static saliency models; gains are modest.  
- Paper lacks visualizations of generated scanpaths—only overall statistics are shown—to support biological claims.  
- Quantitative improvements do not clearly justify increased model complexity; computational cost comparisons are needed.  
- Evaluation is limited to static saliency datasets; dynamic/scanpath-specific datasets are needed to validate claims.  
- A more detailed ablation and sensitivity analysis of each affordance and their weightings is required.  
- Writing is generally clear but could be more concise; clarify dynamical system components and the link between the Least Action Principle and the implemented cost function.  
- Overall judgment: interesting approach but limited impact due to modest gains, ad hoc affordances, and incomplete evaluation; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise.
