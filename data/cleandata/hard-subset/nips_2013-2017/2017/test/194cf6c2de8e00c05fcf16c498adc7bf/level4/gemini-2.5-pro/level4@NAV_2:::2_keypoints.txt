- Presents a generative model for attentional scanpaths in static and dynamic scenes derived from a variational principle (generalized least action).  
- Constructs a Lagrangian with kinetic energy plus a generalized potential comprising three parts: harmonic boundary constraint, curiosity (multi-scale image-gradient attraction), and brightness-invariance for fixation/tracking.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange differential equations that govern the focus-of-attention motion.  
- Validates by simulating scanpaths, aggregating them into saliency maps, and comparing to human fixations on standard datasets.  
- Demonstrates competitive saliency-prediction performance despite being a principled scanpath generator rather than an engineered saliency model.  
- Major strength: originality and theoretical elegance, offering a unified physics-based framework for saccades and tracking.  
- Potentially significant: provides a new theoretical lens for studying bottom-up attention and information-gathering dynamics.  
- Major weakness: paper presentation is unclear, making the mathematical contribution hard to follow for a broad audience.  
- Many variables and functions are not defined before use, forcing readers to infer meanings.  
- Key derivations (motion equations) are tucked into a dense two-page appendix instead of presented accessibly in the main text.  
- Recommendation: move derivations to supplementary material, expand and clarify the main exposition, and carefully proofread for polish and flow.  
- Suggests situating the work relative to psychological/optimal-search models (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) to clarify similarities, differences, and possible integrations.
