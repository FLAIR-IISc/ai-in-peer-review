- Presents a novel framework (EYMOL) for modeling eye movements using the Least Action Principle applied to gaze trajectories.  
- Contrasts with existing saliency-map approaches that select fixations via optimization/maximization.  
- Reviewer’s limited expertise in perception/eye movements but understands core principles and distinctions from prior models.  
- Some mathematical details (extensive appendix) were hard to follow due to limited physics background.  
- Model was evaluated on real eye movement datasets and compared to alternative models.  
- Results generally support the new approach, though it does not outperform others on every metric.  
- Likely of high interest to vision science communities (e.g., VSS).  
- Suitability for NIPS is unclear and should be judged by more specialized reviewers.  
- Offers a novel, intriguing theory that could stimulate further empirical research and has merit within perception sciences.
