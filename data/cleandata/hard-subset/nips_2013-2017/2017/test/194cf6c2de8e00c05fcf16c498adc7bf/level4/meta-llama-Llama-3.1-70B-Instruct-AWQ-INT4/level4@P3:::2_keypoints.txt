1. Proposes a novel model for predicting human eye movements in static and dynamic images.  
2. Model derived from three principles of visual attention formalized as an energy function.  
3. Principle 1: boundedness of trajectory modeled by a harmonic oscillator at image borders and limited retina size.  
4. Principle 2: curiosity-driven attention highlighting large brightness changes at fine and blurred scales.  
5. Principle 3: brightness invariance that increases with brightness changes.  
6. Authors derive differential equations from the cost function to predict eye movements.  
7. Evaluated quantitatively on static and dynamic scene datasets with human eye movements.  
8. Performance is comparable to state-of-the-art methods across datasets.  
9. Contribution is significant for formalizing saliency and modeling bottom-up eye movements.  
10. Strength: novel and innovative theoretical framework grounded in principled assumptions.  
11. Strength: method performs competitively with existing approaches.  
12. Weakness: paper is poorly written and hard to follow.  
13. Weakness: many abbreviations, variables, and functions are not clearly defined.  
14. Weakness: lacks comprehensive discussion and comparison with psychological literature on eye movements.  
15. Suggestion: revise for clarity and readability.  
16. Suggestion: expand definitions of abbreviations, variables, and functions.  
17. Suggestion: move 2-page Appendix to supplementary material to allow more literature discussion.  
18. Suggestion: add discussion comparing the approach to existing empirical findings.  
19. Acceptance arguments: novel model, competitive performance, and substantial formal contribution.  
20. Rejection arguments: poor writing, unclear notation, and insufficient engagement with psychological literature.  
21. Overall rating: 7/10 — substantial contribution but needs presentation improvements.
