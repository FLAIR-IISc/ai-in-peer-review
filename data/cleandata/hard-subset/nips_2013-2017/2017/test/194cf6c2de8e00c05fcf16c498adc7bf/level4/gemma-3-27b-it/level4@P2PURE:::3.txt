This paper introduces a novel approach to modeling eye movements, diverging from traditional saliency map-based methods. The authors provide a reasonable summary of existing work in the field (though I readily admit my limited expertise in perception and eye-tracking). Current approaches typically define a saliency map over an image and then select fixation points through optimization or maximization. In contrast, this model, EYMOL, operates under a different principle, directly modeling gaze trajectories using a "Least Action Principle." While a complete evaluation of the mathematical details – particularly those in the extensive appendix – was beyond my capabilities due to a lack of background in the relevant physics, I was able to grasp the core concepts and understand how this approach distinguishes itself from prior work. The model is thoughtfully applied to a real-world eye-movement dataset and compared against several alternative models. The results, while not uniformly superior across all metrics, are encouraging and support the validity of the proposed approach. 

I believe this work may be of significant interest to researchers in vision science, potentially those attending conferences such as VSS. Its suitability as a contribution to NIPS is less clear, and I suggest this be considered in light of opinions from reviewers with greater expertise in the area. Nevertheless, the paper presents a new and intriguing theoretical framework that has the potential to stimulate further empirical investigation, and therefore possesses both novelty and merit within the broader field of perception science.
