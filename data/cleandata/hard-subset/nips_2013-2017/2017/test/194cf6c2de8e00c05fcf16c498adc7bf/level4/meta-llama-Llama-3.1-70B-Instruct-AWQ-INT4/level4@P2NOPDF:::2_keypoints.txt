- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention based on three principles: a harmonic oscillator border constraint, a curiosity-driven term favoring large multi-scale brightness changes, and brightness invariance.  
- Combines these principles into a cost function and derives differential equations that predict eye movements for static and dynamic scenes, including start location and initial velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic datasets with human eye movements shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Offers a fresh, formalizable perspective on saliency and bottom-up eye-movement modeling that could aid understanding in computational vision and cognitive science.  
- Main weakness is poor writing and lack of clarity, making the paper hard to follow; needs proofreading and clearer notation.  
- Recommends moving the 2-page appendix to supplementary material and using the space to explicitly define every variable and function used.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests connection to psychological literature on optimal eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of how this model compares or integrates with those findings.  
- Overall: promising and innovative contribution but needs clearer exposition and stronger links to related cognitive work for the NIPS audience.
