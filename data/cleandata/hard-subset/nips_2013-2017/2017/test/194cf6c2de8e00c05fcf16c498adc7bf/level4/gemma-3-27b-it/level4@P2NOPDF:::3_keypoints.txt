- Proposes EYMOL, a novel eye-movement model grounded in the Least Action Principle that models gaze trajectories directly rather than relying on saliency maps.
- Explicitly diverges from traditional fixation-point optimization and saliency-based approaches.
- Authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing work, aiding readers unfamiliar with the subfield.
- Mathematical details (especially in the extensive appendix) were difficult for this reviewer to fully evaluate due to limited physics background.
- Core concepts and the distinctions between EYMOL and prior models are clearly communicated.
- Model evaluated on a real-world eye-movement dataset with comparisons to several alternative models.
- Results are encouraging but not uniformly superior across all evaluation metrics.
- Likely of strong interest to vision-science audiences (e.g., VSS); suitability for NIPS is less clear.
