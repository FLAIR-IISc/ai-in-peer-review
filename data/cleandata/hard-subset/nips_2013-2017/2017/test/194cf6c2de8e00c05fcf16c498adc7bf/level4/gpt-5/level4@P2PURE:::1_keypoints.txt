- Proposes a bottom-up visual-feature saliency model augmented with dynamical-systems modeling to capture scanpath dynamics.
- Introduces appealing ideas such as “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and the use of a Least Action Principle.
- Affordances seem ad hoc and are not sufficiently justified.
- The Least Action Principle derivation is not related to established eye-movement research (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab).
- Biological motivation is limited, which is more problematic given the claim to capture biological eye-movement dynamics.
- Model performance is mixed and overall poor compared to benchmark deep-learning static salience approaches.
- The main paper should show generated scanpaths themselves (figures) rather than only aggregate matching statistics.
