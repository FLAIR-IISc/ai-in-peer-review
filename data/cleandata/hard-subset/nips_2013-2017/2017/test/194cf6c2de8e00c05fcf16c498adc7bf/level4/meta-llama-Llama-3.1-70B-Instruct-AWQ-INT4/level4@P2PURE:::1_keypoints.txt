- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model combined with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces novel "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and the Least Action Principle to drive scanpaths.  
- Affordances appear somewhat ad hoc and lack deeper motivation, though they add appeal.  
- The derivation/role of the Least Action Principle should be related to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Limited biological motivation may be acceptable given deep-learning salience models, but the model’s mixed performance versus those models is concerning.  
- Claiming to capture biological eye-movement dynamics requires more detailed validation beyond matching summary statistics.  
- The main paper should include figures and a more detailed analysis characterizing generated scanpaths, not only overall statistics.
