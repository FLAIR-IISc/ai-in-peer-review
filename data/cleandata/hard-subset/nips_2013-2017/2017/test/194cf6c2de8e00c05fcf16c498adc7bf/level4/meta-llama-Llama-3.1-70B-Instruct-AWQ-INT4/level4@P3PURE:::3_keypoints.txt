- Proposes a novel model (EYMOL) for eye movements and provides a thorough survey of the field.
- Accurately describes existing methods that use image-based saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization/maximization.
- Key distinction: EYMOL formulates a "Least Action Principle" directly on gaze trajectories rather than on saliency maps.
- Mathematical details are extensive (in the appendix) and partly beyond the reviewer's physics expertise, though main ideas are clear.
- Model evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and compared against multiple alternative models.
- Results are generally positive and validate the model's potential, but do not uniformly outperform alternatives.
- Reviewer views the work as more relevant to vision science audiences (e.g., VSS) than to NIPS.
- Believes the paper advances an interesting, novel theory that could inspire further empirical research.
- Recommends considering other reviewers' opinions, especially those with greater domain expertise.
