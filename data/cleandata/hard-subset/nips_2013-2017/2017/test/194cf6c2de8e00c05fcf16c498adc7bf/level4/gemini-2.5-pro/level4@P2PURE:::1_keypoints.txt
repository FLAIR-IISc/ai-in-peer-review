- Proposes a bottom-up visual saliency model with a dynamical systems component to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Presents interesting ideas such as "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) driving scanpaths and applies the Least Action Principle.  
- Affordances are introduced in an ad hoc way without sufficient motivation or justification.  
- The derivation and discussion of the Least Action Principle lack connection to prior eye-movement work (e.g., Wolpert lab contributions).  
- Biological motivation is limited, though this is less critical given the prevalence of high-performing ML models for static saliency.  
- The proposed model performs poorly relative to state-of-the-art static saliency methods.  
- Evaluation is insufficient for a model claiming to capture biological eye-movement dynamics—needs dynamic characterization of generated scanpaths (figures/analyses) rather than only static correspondence metrics.
