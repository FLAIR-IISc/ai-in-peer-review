- Proposes a novel paradigm (EYe MOvement Laws, EYMOL) modeling visual attention via variational laws of eye movement using a generalized Least Action Principle.
- EYMOL is defined on gaze trajectories rather than saliency maps, offering a more dynamic, trajectory-based view of attention.
- Paper contrasts EYMOL with existing saliency/optimization approaches and highlights limitations of those methods.
- Technical derivation (Euler–Lagrange equations) is clear and concise, with sufficient context for understanding.
- Some mathematical content (especially in the appendix) may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Demonstrates applicability on real-world image and video datasets, showing competitive performance versus state-of-the-art models.
- Strengths: originality, theoretical grounding, and thorough empirical evaluation.
- Writing, organization, figures, and tables are good, though minor clarity/readability improvements are suggested.
- Concerns: direct relevance to core NIPS themes (machine learning/neural information processing) may be unclear.
- Concern: specialized technical focus could limit broader audience appeal.
- Overall recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions to improve clarity and readability.
