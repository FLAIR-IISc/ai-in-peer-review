- Proposes a variational computational model of visual attention using a generalized Least Action Principle to generate scanpaths in dynamic scenes.  
- Defines a potential energy landscape incorporating affordances like a “curiosity” (image detail) term and brightness invariance to drive eye movements.  
- Core idea of using affordances and Least Action is intriguing but the specific affordances feel ad hoc and need stronger justification.  
- Paper lacks connection to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab) and stronger biological grounding.  
- Weak biological motivation is noted but not necessarily fatal given current ML trends; nevertheless it should be strengthened given claims about biological dynamics.  
- Performance on standard benchmarks (MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU) is not consistently competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- The paper should include visualizations and quantitative characterization of generated scanpaths (not just saliency map statistics) to support biological dynamics claims.  
- Good literature overview situating the work between classic feature-based saliency and modern ML approaches.  
- Mathematical formulation (Lagrangian, Euler–Lagrange) and derivations are clearly presented, with detailed appendices.  
- Use of notation (Iverson) and relegation of lengthy derivations to the Appendix are appropriate.  
- Simulated annealing parameter estimation is described adequately, though justification for limiting the search domain could be expanded.  
- Experimental evaluation across static and dynamic datasets is a strength, enabling fair comparisons.  
- Results are promising but inconsistent; the paper lacks analysis of scene types or conditions where the model succeeds or fails.  
- Strengths summarized: novel variational approach, clear math, standard-dataset evaluation, emphasis on scanpath dynamics.  
- Weaknesses summarized: ad hoc affordances, inconsistent performance, missing detailed scanpath analysis, limited discussion of limitations/failure cases.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, well-defined mathematical framework, provides a baseline for future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical performance for a top-tier venue, weak biological justification, and inadequate analysis of scanpath characteristics.
