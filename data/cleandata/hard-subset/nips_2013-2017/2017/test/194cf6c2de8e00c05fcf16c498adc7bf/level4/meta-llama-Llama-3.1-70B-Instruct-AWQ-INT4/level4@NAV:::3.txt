This paper presents a novel approach to studying eye movements, providing a comprehensive overview of the current state of the art in this field. Existing methods rely on saliency maps defined over images, with fixation points selected through optimization or maximization techniques. In contrast, the proposed EYMOL model operates under a distinct assumption, utilizing the "Least Action Principle" to define gaze trajectories directly. Although certain mathematical aspects, particularly those in the appendix, were challenging for me to assess due to my limited physics background, I was able to grasp the core elements of this approach and its differentiation from previous models. The authors successfully apply their model to a real eye-movement dataset and compare it to alternative models, yielding favorable results that validate the new approach, even if it doesn't outperform others in every aspect.

The paper may appeal to specific segments of the vision science community, such as attendees of the Vision Sciences Society (VSS). However, its relevance to NIPS is less clear, and I recommend considering the opinions of more expert reviewers. The paper likely contributes a new and intriguing theory that could inspire further empirical research, demonstrating both novelty and merit in the field of perception sciences.
