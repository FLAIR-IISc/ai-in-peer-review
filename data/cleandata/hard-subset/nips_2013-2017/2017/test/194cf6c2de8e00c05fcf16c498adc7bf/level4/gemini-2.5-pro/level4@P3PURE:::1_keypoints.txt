- Presents a computational neuroscience model of bottom-up visual saliency using dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.
- Paper is interesting and enjoyable, introducing compelling ideas like "affordances" (curiosity, brightness invariance) and use of the Least Action Principle.
- Proposed "affordances" appear ad-hoc and lack sufficient motivation from existing literature or theory.
- Discussion of the Least Action Principle should be better connected to established eye-movement research (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab).
- Lack of deep biological motivation might be acceptable if performance were state-of-the-art, but model performance is mixed and appears poor compared to contemporary deep learning saliency models.
- Major concern: central claim of capturing eye-movement dynamics is not supported by the paper’s evaluation.
- Evaluation is limited to static aggregate fixation metrics and lacks detailed, dynamic characterization or analysis of generated scanpaths.
