- Paper proposes a variational energy-based model of visual attention for static and dynamic scenes, grounded in three principles.  
- Principle 1: eye movements constrained by a harmonic oscillator at image borders within a limited-sized retina.  
- Principle 2: curiosity-driven term highlights regions with large brightness changes at fine and blurred scales.  
- Principle 3: brightness-invariance term increases with changes in brightness.  
- From the combined cost, authors derive differential equations to predict eye-movement trajectories given start location and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Contribution and novelty: offers a formal, bottom-up variational framework for saliency and eye-movement dynamics that could advance understanding in vision and cognitive science.  
- Major clarity issues: paper is poorly written, needs proofreading, clearer notation, expanded abbreviations, and moved appendix material to supplementary to define all variables.  
- Literature connection: reviewer requests explicit comparison to optimal-search eye-movement literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and Geisler lab work to clarify novelty or complementarity.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful work but requires substantial revisions in writing, notation, and psychological-literature integration to be compelling and accessible.
