- Introduces a bottom-up visual feature saliency model extended via dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Praised for innovative ideas, notably defining "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) that influence scanpaths.  
- Applies the Least Action Principle to derive scanpath dynamics.  
- Criticism: the chosen "affordances" seem ad hoc and lack deeper justification or motivation.  
- Suggests connecting the derivation and theory to prior eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab) for better grounding.  
- Notes limited biological grounding is a minor concern given the dominance of deep learning salience models.  
- Major issue: mixed and underwhelming empirical performance compared to benchmark models.  
- Recommends including main-paper figures that explicitly characterize generated scanpaths and compare them to actual eye-movement patterns, not just summary statistics.
