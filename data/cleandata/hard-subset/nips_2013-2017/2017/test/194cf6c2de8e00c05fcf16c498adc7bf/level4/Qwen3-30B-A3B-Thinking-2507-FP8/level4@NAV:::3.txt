

The paper proposes a new approach to the study of eye movements. The authors accurately summarize the current state of the art (within the scope of my understanding, though I acknowledge upfront that I am not an expert in perception or eye movements). Fundamentally, existing methods rely on saliency maps defined over images, with fixations points selected via an optimization/maximization procedure. In contrast, the proposed EYMOL model operates under a distinct assumption, defining gaze trajectories directly through a "Least Action Principle." Some mathematical details exceeded my evaluative capacity due to insufficient physics background (particularly the extensive appendix), yet I grasped the core methodology and its differentiation from prior models. The model is effectively applied to a real eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against multiple alternative approaches. While it does not dominate all performance metrics, the results are favorable for the new approach, confirming its validity. I believe this work may interest vision science researchers (e.g., VSS attendees), though I am less convinced it constitutes a major contribution to NIPS; this assessment should be informed by reviewers with greater expertise. Overall, it advances a novel theoretical framework with potential to stimulate further empirical research, thereby demonstrating both novelty and merit for perception sciences.
