- Paper proposes a variational/dynamical model of visual attention that predicts eye movements by minimizing an energy function.  
- Model formalizes three principles: harmonic-oscillator border constraint with limited retina, curiosity-driven detection of large brightness changes at two scales, and a brightness-invariance term scaling with brightness change.  
- From the combined cost function the authors derive differential equations to generate scan paths for static and dynamic images given initial conditions.  
- Method is evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Contribution is novel and innovative, offering a principled bottom-up account that helps clarify saliency and eye-movement formation.  
- Presentation is poor: the paper is hard to follow and needs thorough proofreading and clearer exposition.  
- Notation and symbols are underdefined; all abbreviations, variables, and functions should be explicitly defined (especially in equations and Section 3).  
- Suggest moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using the space to provide clearer definitions and explanations of modeling choices.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests stronger connection to psychological literature on optimal information-gathering eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008 and related Geisler lab work).  
- Recommend a brief qualitative comparison that highlights similarities, differences, and potential synergies with optimal-search models to better position the work.  
- Overall assessment: creative, promising, and competitive, with the main weakness being clarity; improving writing, notation, and literature grounding will increase accessibility and impact.
