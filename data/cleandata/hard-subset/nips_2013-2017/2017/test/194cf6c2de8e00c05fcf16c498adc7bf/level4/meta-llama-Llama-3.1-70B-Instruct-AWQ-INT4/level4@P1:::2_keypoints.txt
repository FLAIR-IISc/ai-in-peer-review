- Paper models human visual attention in static and dynamic scenes using a variational/cost-function approach.
- Model is built from three principles: image-border harmonic oscillator constraint, a curiosity-driven term (large brightness changes at fine and blurred scales), and brightness-invariance.
- Authors derive differential equations from the cost function to predict eye movements given start location and velocity.
- Quantitative evaluation on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art.
- The approach is novel and offers a formal, bottom-up perspective on saliency and eye-movement modeling.
- The work could advance understanding of what makes regions salient and provide a formal model for eye movements.
- Paper is poorly written and difficult to follow, with unclear notation and unexplained abbreviations.
- Reviewer recommends extensive proofreading and expanding/defining all variables and functions for a multidisciplinary audience.
- Suggest moving the 2-page appendix to supplementary material and using main-paper space to define variables and functions.
- The manuscript lacks connection to relevant psychological literature on optimal eye-movement/search models (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
- Reviewer asks for discussion comparing this model to existing optimal-search findings and potential integration with those models.
- Strengths cited: novelty, solid performance, and quantitative evaluation on human-eye datasets.
- Weaknesses cited: clarity/writing, insufficient literature linkage, and inadequate explanation of mathematical components.
- Acceptance arguments: novel model and competitive results; rejection arguments: poor presentation and missing psychological context.
- Recommendations: proofread, expand abbreviations/definitions, move appendix, and add discussion linking to psychological optimal-search literature.
