- Paper proposes a model of visual attention based on minimizing an energy combining three principles to predict eye movements.
- Principle 1: eye movements constrained by a harmonic-oscillator-like potential near image borders within limited retinal extent.
- Principle 2: curiosity-driven term that emphasizes regions with large brightness changes in high-resolution and blurred image versions.
- Principle 3: brightness-invariance term that increases cost with changes in brightness.
- From the combined cost function the authors derive differential equations to predict scanpaths for static and dynamic scenes, conditional on start position and initial velocity.
- Method is evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and performs comparably to state-of-the-art.
- Contribution: offers a novel, formal, bottom-up account of saliency and eye movements that could clarify ambiguous notions of saliency in vision science.
- Major issue: paper is poorly written and difficult to follow; needs thorough proofreading and clearer notation.
- Recommendation: expand abbreviations/acronyms and explicitly define every variable and function used (especially in Section 3 and equations).
- Suggestion: move the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use freed space to define terms and notations.
- Scientific suggestion: situate and compare the work against literature on eye movements as optimal information-gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and other relevant Geisler-lab papers to clarify relations or complementarities.
