- Introduces EYMOL, a novel computational model of eye movements that models gaze trajectories directly.  
- Departures from saliency-map approaches by using a "Least Action Principle" from physics to govern trajectories.  
- Conceptual novelty is the paper's primary strength, offering a fundamentally different perspective on oculomotor control.  
- Core ideas and distinctions from prior work are presented clearly and accessibly.  
- Reviewer cannot fully assess the detailed physics-based mathematical derivations in the appendix.  
- Validated on a real-world eye-tracking dataset and compared against multiple alternative models.  
- Empirical results are promising and competitive, though EYMOL does not outperform all models on every metric.  
- Likely to stimulate further empirical research in perception and vision science and is a strong fit for venues like VSS.  
- Unclear fit for NeurIPS, since the contribution is primarily cognitive/perceptual modeling rather than machine-learning methodology.  
- Overall assessment: novel, theoretically interesting work with significant merit for the perception sciences.
