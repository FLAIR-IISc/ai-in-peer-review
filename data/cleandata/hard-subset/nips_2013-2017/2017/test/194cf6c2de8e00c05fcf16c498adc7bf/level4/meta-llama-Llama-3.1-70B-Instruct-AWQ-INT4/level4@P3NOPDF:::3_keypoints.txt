- Proposes a novel model (EYMOL) for eye movements and provides a thorough summary of the current state of the art.  
- Accurately describes existing approaches that use image saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization/maximization.  
- EYMOL departs from prior work by defining eye movements on gaze trajectories using the "Least Action Principle."  
- Some mathematical details (especially in the extensive appendix) were hard to follow due to the reviewer's limited physics background, though main ideas were understood.  
- Model evaluated on real eye-movement data and compared to alternative models.  
- EYMOL does not beat all existing models on every metric, but results are generally favorable and validate the approach.  
- Paper likely of interest to vision science audiences (e.g., VSS) but its contribution to the NIPS community is less clear.  
- Recommends consulting more expert reviewers; views the work as novel and potentially inspiring further empirical research in perception sciences.
