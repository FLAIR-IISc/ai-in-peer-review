- Introduces a novel trajectory-based eye-movement model (EYMOL) grounded in the Least Action Principle, differing from saliency-map approaches.
- Model defines gaze directly on trajectories instead of constructing pixel-wise saliency maps and selecting fixations.
- Mathematical formulation is detailed and in-depth; appendix requires physics background beyond the reviewer’s expertise.
- Core theory is clearly explained so the main ideas are understandable without specialized physics knowledge.
- Applied to a real-world eye movement dataset and rigorously compared against multiple baseline models.
- Results are encouraging and validate the approach, but EYMOL does not consistently outperform all baselines across every metric.
- Main strength is theoretical novelty and potential to stimulate further research beyond pixel-based saliency models.
- Questioned suitability for NIPS due to stronger relevance to vision and perception science venues; recommends deferment to specialized reviewers.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution that warrants consideration and could inspire future empirical work.
