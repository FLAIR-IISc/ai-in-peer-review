- Paper proposes a variational/energy-based model for eye movements in static and dynamic scenes, yielding differential equations that predict gaze trajectories from initial conditions.  
- Model is built on three principles: harmonic oscillator border constraint (limited retina), curiosity-driven sensitivity to brightness changes at multiple scales, and brightness invariance.  
- Authors derive a cost function from these principles and evaluate the method quantitatively on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets.  
- Performance is comparable to state-of-the-art methods, though not uniformly superior across all metrics and datasets.  
- Contribution: provides a formal definition of saliency and a bottom-up framework for modeling eye movements, addressing a long-standing vagueness in the field.  
- Paper is poorly written and hard to follow; needs proofreading, clearer exposition, and expanded explanations of abbreviations and equations.  
- Practical recommendation: move the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use the freed space to define all variables and functions for the NIPS audience.  
- Reviewer (psychologist) requests connections to psychological literature on optimal information-gathering eye movements (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of how this model compares, complements, or restates such work.
