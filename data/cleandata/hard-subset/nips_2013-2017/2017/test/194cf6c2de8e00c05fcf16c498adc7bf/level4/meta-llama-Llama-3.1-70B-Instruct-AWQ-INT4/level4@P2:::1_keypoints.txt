- Proposes a novel paradigm modeling visual attention via variational laws grounded in the Least Action Principle.
- Introduces affordances (boundedness, curiosity-driven, brightness invariance) to drive scanpath dynamics and estimates model parameters for saliency detection.
- Provides a clear derivation of Euler-Lagrange equations describing attention dynamics.
- Method is original in using the Least Action Principle for attention modeling.
- Affordances appear ad hoc and lack deeper motivation or biological grounding.
- Fails to sufficiently relate the model to existing eye-movement literature (e.g., Wolpert lab and other prior work).
- Empirical performance is mixed compared to benchmark deep-learning saliency models and lacks thorough comparisons.
- Paper lacks figures characterizing generated scanpaths, especially their temporal dynamics.
- Well written overall, though some mathematical derivations could be clarified (Clarity: 8/10).
- Technically sound but limited by weak biological motivation and mixed empirical results (Quality: 7/10).
- Original approach but some design choices reduce perceived originality (Originality: 6/10).
- Promising paradigm with potential impact but limited by performance and motivation issues (Significance: 7/10).
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address motivation, comparisons, and scanpath visualizations.
- For acceptance: novel paradigm, original Least Action approach, clear mathematical derivation.
- Against acceptance: ad hoc affordances, mixed benchmark performance, and missing scanpath/temporal analyses.
