- Well-written paper proposing a bottom-up visual saliency model extended with a dynamical-systems approach to simulate scanpaths.  
- Introduces novel ideas like "affordances" (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and uses the Principle of Least Action to drive simulated eye movements.  
- The proposed affordances appear ad hoc and lack sufficient motivation or rigorous justification for their selection.  
- The formulation does not adequately connect to prior eye-movement/decision-making literature (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab), weakening theoretical grounding.  
- Model performance is mixed and generally poorer than state-of-the-art deep-learning saliency models, undermining its practical impact.  
- Evaluation relies on static fixation-prediction metrics, which mismatch the paper’s central claim of modeling dynamics.  
- The paper omits quantitative scanpath analyses (saccade amplitude, orientation, fixation-duration distributions), a major omission for validating dynamic behavior.
