- Proposes a novel model (EYMOL) for eye movements.  
- Authors accurately summarize current state-of-the-art (saliency maps with fixation selection via optimization).  
- EYMOL departs from prior work by defining gaze trajectories via a "Least Action Principle."  
- Reviewer could not fully evaluate mathematical/physics details in the extensive appendix.  
- Despite that, reviewer understood key elements and how the approach differs from previous models.  
- Model is applied to a real eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against several alternatives.  
- Results are favorable and validate the approach, though it does not outperform competitors on every metric.  
- Likely of significant interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees).  
- Reviewer is less convinced of its contribution to the NIPS community and defers to more specialized reviewers.  
- Overall, the paper presents a novel, interesting theory with clear novelty and merit for perception sciences and potential to inspire empirical work.
