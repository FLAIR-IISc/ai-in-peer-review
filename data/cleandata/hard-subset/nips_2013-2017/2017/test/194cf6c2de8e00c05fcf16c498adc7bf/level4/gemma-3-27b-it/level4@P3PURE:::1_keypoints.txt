- Proposes a bottom-up visual feature saliency model combined with dynamical systems to capture scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces novel “affordances” (curiosity, brightness invariance) and applies the Least Action Principle.  
- Reviewer found the ideas interesting and enjoyed the paper.  
- The proposed affordances are somewhat ad hoc and lack sufficient motivation or justification.  
- The derivation should be related to prior eye-movement dynamics work (e.g., Wolpert lab).  
- Biological motivation is weak but considered a minor issue relative to focus on benchmark-driven salience models.  
- Model performance is poor compared to state-of-the-art deep learning salience models.  
- Claims of capturing biological eye-movement dynamics need stronger evidence—include scanpath figures in the main paper rather than only summary statistics.
