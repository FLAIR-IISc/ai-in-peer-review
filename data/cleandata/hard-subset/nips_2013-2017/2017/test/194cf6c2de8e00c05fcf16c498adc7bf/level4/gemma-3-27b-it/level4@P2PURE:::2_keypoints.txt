- Paper proposes a model of visual attention based on three principles: boundary-constrained eye movements (harmonic oscillator), multi-scale brightness-change “curiosity,” and brightness invariance under illumination changes.  
- These principles are formulated as an energy function whose minimization yields differential equations predicting eye movements given initial position and velocity.  
- Model is evaluated quantitatively on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- The approach is novel and offers a formal, bottom-up framework for salience and eye-movement modeling, potentially advancing theoretical rigor.  
- Method does not reach state-of-the-art on every metric/dataset, though results are favorable overall.  
- Paper’s clarity and writing are poor—difficult to follow—and require thorough proofreading and expanded definitions of abbreviations and variables.  
- Recommend moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using the freed space to explicitly define all variables/functions for broader interdisciplinary accessibility.  
- Suggest stronger connection to psychological literature on eye movements as optimal information gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discussion of how the model relates to or complements those optimal-search frameworks.
