- Introduces a novel framework (EYMOL) for modeling eye movements that departs from traditional saliency-map and optimization-based fixation models.  
- EYMOL is grounded in a “Least Action Principle” applied directly to gaze trajectories rather than optimizing saliency maps.  
- Reviewer could not fully assess the detailed mathematics in the extensive appendix due to background limitations, but found the core concepts and distinctions clear.  
- Model is evaluated on a real-world eye-movement dataset with comparisons to several alternative approaches.  
- Results are promising and support the validity of the approach, though it does not consistently outperform all baselines across every metric.  
- The work is potentially of significant interest to the vision science community (e.g., VSS).  
- Suitability for NeurIPS (NIPS) may require reviewers with deeper expertise in this theoretical area.  
- Presents a novel, potentially influential theoretical framework that could stimulate further empirical investigation and shows novelty and merit within perception sciences.
