- Paper proposes a new approach (EYMOL) to model eye-movement trajectories using a "Least Action Principle."
- Authors provide an accurate summary of the current state of the art (reviewer not an expert in perception/eye movements).
- Existing methods use image saliency maps with fixation points chosen by optimization/maximization; EYMOL differs by defining trajectories directly.
- Some mathematical details (appendix) were hard for the reviewer due to limited physics background.
- Reviewer understands the core methodology and how it differs from prior models.
- EYMOL is evaluated on a real eye-movement dataset and benchmarked against multiple alternatives.
- Results do not dominate all metrics but are favorable and validate the new approach.
- Likely of interest to vision science researchers (e.g., VSS attendees).
- Reviewer is unsure the work is a major contribution for NIPS and suggests judgment by reviewers with more domain expertise.
- Overall assessment: novel theoretical framework with potential to stimulate further empirical research and merit for perception sciences.
