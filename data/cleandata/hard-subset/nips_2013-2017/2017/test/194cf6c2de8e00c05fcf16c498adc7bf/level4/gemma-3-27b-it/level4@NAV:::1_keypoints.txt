- Paper proposes a computational model of visual feature saliency combining bottom-up processing with dynamical systems to model scanpath dynamics.  
- Introduces interesting concepts: scanpath-driving “affordances” (e.g., curiosity, brightness invariance) and use of the Least Action Principle.  
- Writing and presentation are pleasant and the ideas are appealing.  
- The chosen affordances appear somewhat arbitrary and need stronger justification or empirical grounding.  
- The use of the Least Action Principle lacks connection to established eye-movement literature (e.g., work from the Wolpert lab) and needs theoretical strengthening.  
- Model performance is comparatively weak, which is problematic given the goal of capturing biological eye-movement dynamics.  
- Main paper should include visualizations of generated scanpaths (not just aggregate statistics) to better assess model behavior.
