This paper proposes a novel approach to modeling eye movements. The authors provide a clear and accurate summary of the current state of the art (to the best of my understanding; I am not an expert in perception or eye movements). Most existing methods rely on saliency maps defined over images, with fixation points selected via an optimization/maximization procedure. In contrast, the proposed model (EYMOL) is formulated directly over gaze trajectories and is grounded in a Least Action Principle.

Some of the mathematical details—especially those in the extensive appendix—are beyond my ability to fully evaluate due to my limited physics background. Nevertheless, the core ideas and how the approach differs from prior models are understandable and well motivated.

The model is evaluated on a real dataset of eye movements and compared against a range of alternative models. Although it does not dominate on every measure, the results are generally favorable and support the validity of the approach.

I expect this work to be of interest to parts of the vision science community (e.g., VSS attendees). I am less certain about its fit as a strong NIPS contribution, and I would defer to other reviewers with deeper expertise in this area. That said, the paper advances a new and interesting theoretical perspective that could inspire further empirical research, and it offers clear novelty and potential merit for the perception sciences.
