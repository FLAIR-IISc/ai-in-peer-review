- Proposes a novel, principled model of visual attention based on three core principles: a biomechanical harmonic-oscillator constraint, a multiscale “curiosity” term favoring brightness changes, and a brightness-invariance term rewarding variation.  
- Formalizes these principles into a cost function and derives differential equations to predict eye movements (dependent on initial position and velocity) for static and dynamic scenes.  
- Quantitative evaluation vs. human eye-data shows overall strong performance comparable to state-of-the-art, though not uniformly best across all metrics and datasets.  
- Offers a clearer formalization of saliency and a distinct bottom-up perspective that could advance computational and cognitive understanding of eye-movement drivers.  
- Major weakness: clarity and dense writing—paper is difficult to follow and needs thorough proofreading and expansion of abbreviations and explanations (especially Section 3).  
- Structural recommendation: move the current two-page Appendix to supplementary material and use main-text space to define every variable and function for an interdisciplinary NIPS audience.  
- Psychological recommendation: better connect to literature framing eye movements as information-gathering (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008) and discuss how the approach replicates, extends, or complements those optimal-search findings.
