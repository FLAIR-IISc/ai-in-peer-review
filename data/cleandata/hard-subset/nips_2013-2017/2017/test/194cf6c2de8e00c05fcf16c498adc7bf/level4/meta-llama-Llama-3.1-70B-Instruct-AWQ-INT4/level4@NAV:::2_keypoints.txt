- Proposes a model of visual attention for static and dynamic scenes based on three principles: border harmonic oscillator, curiosity-driven brightness-change sensitivity, and brightness invariance.  
- Combines these principles into an energy/cost function and derives differential equations to predict eye movements from initial position and velocity.  
- Evaluated on static and dynamic eye-tracking datasets and shows performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Offers a novel, formalized bottom-up framework for saliency and eye-movement modeling that could advance understanding in computational vision and cognitive science.
