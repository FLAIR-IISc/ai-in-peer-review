- Proposes a novel visual-attention model based on variational laws and a generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Model integrates three principles: bounded trajectory, curiosity-driven exploration, and brightness invariance.  
- Principles are formalized via a variational approach whose stationary conditions yield Euler–Lagrange equations for attention focus.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with appropriate mathematical derivations.  
- Experimental setup and results are clearly described.  
- Model is original and differs from prior work through its physics-inspired formulation.  
- Provides detailed comparisons with state-of-the-art models and discusses advantages and limitations.  
- Results are significant and show the model can capture scanpath dynamics on image and video saliency datasets.  
- Demonstrates potential to capture biologically plausible eye-movement dynamics.  
- Performance is not consistently better than state-of-the-art methods.
