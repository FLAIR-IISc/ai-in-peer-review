- Proposes a model of gaze for static and dynamic scenes based on minimizing an energy functional encoding three principles.  
- Principle 1: enforces trajectory confinement within the retina via a harmonic potential at image boundaries.  
- Principle 2: a curiosity-driven term favors locations with large brightness gradients at fine and blurred scales.  
- Principle 3: a brightness constancy term penalizes brightness changes along the trajectory.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange differential equations that generate scanpaths given initial position and velocity.  
- Quantitative evaluation on image and video eye-tracking datasets shows performance on par with leading methods.  
- Paper is original and offers a principled, bottom-up dynamical model clarifying saliency and eye-movement mechanisms.  
- Manuscript is hard to follow; needs thorough proofreading and clearer definitions/expansion of abbreviations and symbols, especially in Section 3.  
- Reviewer recommends moving the two-page Appendix to supplementary material and using space to explicitly define variables and functions.  
- Suggests connecting and comparing the proposed variational laws to eye-movement-as-optimal-information-gathering literature (e.g., Najemnik & Geisler 2008).
