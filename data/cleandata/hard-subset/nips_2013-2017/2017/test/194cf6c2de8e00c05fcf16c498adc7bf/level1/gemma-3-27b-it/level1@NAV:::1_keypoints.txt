- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention inspired by the Least Action Principle.  
- Formulates scanpath generation by minimizing a Lagrangian with terms for trajectory boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange differential equations governing attention dynamics.  
- Evaluates model on static images and dynamic scenes (video).  
- Emphasizes shift from global saliency maps to physics-inspired trajectory generation, contrasting prior feature-integration and CNN-based methods.  
- Unique connection to optical flow constraints and the Least Action Principle.  
- Strength: conceptually novel and well-motivated theoretical framework.  
- Strength: rigorous mathematical formulation and clear derivations (appendix).  
- Strength: competitive performance on standard benchmarks (MIT300, CAT2000, SFU).  
- Strength: inclusion of dynamic scenes and brightness invariance constraint.  
- Weakness: model complexity and reliance on simulated annealing for parameter estimation.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to parameter choices not thoroughly investigated; parameters and search-space limitations unjustified.  
- Weakness: biological plausibility underexplored beyond physics inspiration.  
- Weakness: marginal improvement over state-of-the-art and lack of thorough ablation to quantify each Lagrangian term’s contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity, biological plausibility, and broader applications/future directions.
