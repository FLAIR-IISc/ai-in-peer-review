- Proposal: Introduces a novel variational, physics-inspired model where scanpaths emerge from principles like least action, combining curiosity and brightness invariance.  
- Mathematical formulation: Uses a Lagrangian to derive differential equations governing attention dynamics with clear math supporting reproducibility.  
- Experimental validation: Evaluated on static images and videos for saliency detection, showing competitive performance across datasets.  
- Novelty & theoretical grounding: Unique application of variational principles offering a potentially unifying theoretical perspective on attention.  
- Comprehensive factors: Model explicitly incorporates curiosity-driven exploration and brightness invariance in its formulation.  
- Complexity & parameter tuning: Requires multiple parameters (ηb, ηp, λ) tuned via simulated annealing, making it computationally expensive and potentially non-scalable.  
- Approximations concern: Brightness invariance term is approximated (Eq. 6), which may introduce inaccuracies, especially for dynamic scenes.  
- Limited biological validation: Lacks direct physiological or human eye-tracking comparisons of predicted scanpaths beyond saliency maps.  
- Appendix clarity: Detailed derivations in the appendix are hard to follow and need better exposition.  
- Discussion of limitations: Paper could provide a more thorough discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: The approach is novel, well-motivated theoretically, experimentally competitive, and offers a solid basis for future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical applicability is limited by complexity and tuning needs; biological/ecological validity and approximation impacts are insufficiently addressed.  
- Recommendation: Overall promising and recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to improve appendix clarity and discuss limitations.
