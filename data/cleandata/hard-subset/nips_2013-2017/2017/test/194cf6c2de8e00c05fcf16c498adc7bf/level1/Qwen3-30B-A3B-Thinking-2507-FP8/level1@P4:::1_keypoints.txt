- Proposes a variational/Lagrangian model of visual attention modeling scanpaths as particle trajectories with three Lagrangian terms, derives Euler–Lagrange dynamics, and estimates parameters via simulated annealing; evaluated on standard image/video saliency datasets with competitive results.

- Strength — Technical soundness: rigorous mathematical derivation and comprehensive experiments using appropriate metrics (NSS, AUC-Judd).

- Strength — Novelty: unified variational treatment of curiosity-driven attention and brightness invariance tied to the Least Action Principle offers a fresh conceptual perspective.

- Strength — Clarity: well-written, well-organized paper with clear explanations and a detailed appendix for reproducibility.

- Strength — Significance: real-time scanpath generation and simultaneous saliency map production provide theoretical and practical value.

- Weakness — Insufficient distinction from prior physics-inspired attention models; novelty claim not fully substantiated against related work.

- Weakness — Performance gains are marginal; results are competitive but not clearly superior to state-of-the-art.

- Weakness — Parameter estimation (simulated annealing) lacks sensitivity analysis and robustness evaluation.

- Weakness — Weak connection to neural/biological mechanisms; needs more discussion relating the framework to biological evidence.

- Argument for acceptance: conceptually novel, technically rigorous, comprehensively evaluated, and offers principled scanpath generation.

- Argument against acceptance: marginal empirical improvements, insufficient biological grounding, and novelty claim needs stronger comparison to prior physics-based approaches.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen biological relevance discussion and provide more thorough parameter sensitivity analysis.
