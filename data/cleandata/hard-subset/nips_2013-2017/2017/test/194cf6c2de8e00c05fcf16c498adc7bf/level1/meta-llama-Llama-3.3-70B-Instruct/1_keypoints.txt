- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a novel visual attention model grounded in the principle of least action.  
- Derives the model from a generalized Lagrangian that enforces boundedness, curiosity-driven movement, and brightness invariance.  
- Uses the resulting Euler–Lagrange equations to describe attention dynamics and predict human fixations.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical framework and mathematical derivations.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness on both image and video saliency detection tasks.  
- Model is simple and operates in real time, making it attractive for practical applications.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded variational approach that provides intuitive understanding.  
- Strength: empirical performance on various datasets supports the model’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art visual attention models.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of model parameters and their influence on results.  
- Weakness: requires further validation on more diverse datasets and tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add thorough SOTA comparisons, parameter analysis, and broader validation.
