- Introduces EYMOL: a variational, physics-inspired model that generates visual attention scanpaths by minimizing an action with a Lagrangian (kinetic + generalized potential).

- Potential energy composed of boundedness (frame confinement), curiosity (attraction to high-detail regions with oscillatory local/peripheral weighting), and brightness invariance (encouraging fixation on static edges and tracking moving objects).

- Derives Euler–Lagrange equations giving second-order ODEs for attention motion; numerically solves them from multiple initials to produce scanpaths and saliency density maps.

- Validated on image (MIT300, CAT2000) and video (SFU) benchmarks, showing competitive or superior performance versus state-of-the-art, including deep models.

- Conceptually novel: formulates attention via variational calculus and an analogy to classical mechanics, providing a principled unified framework.

- Unifies attentional behaviors: curiosity term drives saccadic exploration while brightness invariance yields both fixation and smooth pursuit/tracking.

- Provides a true generative temporal model of scanpaths, enabling potential dynamic validations beyond static saliency metrics.

- Strong empirical results despite not being explicitly optimized for saliency-map metrics.

- Biological plausibility is limited: the mapping from Lagrangian/Euler–Lagrange formulation to neural implementation is unclear.

- Some model components seem ad-hoc, notably the cos^2(ωt)/sin^2(ωt) oscillatory curiosity term and unspecified frequency ω.

- Lacks an explicit inhibition-of-return mechanism; dynamics may implicitly discourage revisits but no dedicated IOR component is provided.

- Parameter tuning (ηb, ηp, λ) uses simulated annealing to maximize NSS on a small batch of 100 images, raising robustness and principled-method concerns.

- Evaluation reduces rich temporal outputs to static density maps for benchmarking, which is computationally intensive (e.g., 199 runs for images) and discards the model’s core novelty.

- Paper is well-written, theoretically clear, and likely to inspire further physics-based dynamic cognitive models.

- Acceptance arguments: high originality, unified modeling of multiple attention behaviors, generative scanpaths, and strong benchmark performance.

- Rejection arguments: limited biological grounding, some ad-hoc modeling/tuning choices, and evaluation methodology that neglects temporal dynamics.

- Overall recommendation: Accept.
