- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a variational, least-action-based model of visual attention producing eye-movement scanpaths.  
- Derives the model via Euler-Lagrange equations, yielding two differential equations for scanpath dynamics.  
- Incorporates three principles: boundedness of trajectory, curiosity-driven movement, and brightness invariance.  
- Mathematical derivations are thorough and documented in the Appendix.  
- Experimental setup and results are clearly described and easy to follow.  
- Strength: provides a unified interpretation of curiosity-driven movements and brightness invariance for fixation and tracking.  
- Produces promising saliency performance on image and video datasets, treating saliency maps as a byproduct of an eye-movement model.  
- Limitation: parameter estimation uses simulated annealing, which may be inefficient for large training sets.  
- Limitation: weaker performance on dynamic scenes, suggesting the model needs extension to better capture temporal information.  
- Related work review is thorough but lacks detailed comparisons with other variational or similar-principle models.  
- Presentation is clear overall but has minor formatting and typography issues (spacing inconsistencies).  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10; arguments for and against acceptance summarized accordingly.
