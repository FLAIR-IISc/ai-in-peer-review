- Proposes EYMOL: a physics-inspired, variational Least Action Principle model that generates attention scanpaths for static and dynamic scenes.  
- Frames attention trajectory via a Lagrangian combining kinetic energy with a generalized potential energy.  
- Potential energy comprises three terms: boundary constraint, "curiosity" (image gradients), and "brightness invariance" (optical-flow-based) to encourage fixation/tracking.  
- Scanpaths obtained by solving Euler-Lagrange equations; saliency maps produced by aggregating fixations over time.  
- Conceptual departure from Itti & Koch and end-to-end deep models: a principled, generative model of attentional dynamics rather than a direct static predictor.  
- Main strength: originality and elegance—novel application of analytical mechanics to model attention.  
- Key contribution: unifies saccadic exploration (curiosity) and fixation/tracking (brightness invariance) within one coherent mathematical framework.  
- Empirical strength: competitive or superior performance on standard benchmarks (CAT2000, MIT300, SFU), showing a compact model can rival learned systems.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivations provided in the appendix.  
- Weakness: oscillatory weighting in the curiosity term is only briefly justified; the functional form and frequency parameter ω lack deeper explanation or analysis.  
- Weakness: model parameters are optimized on a small subset using simulated annealing; lacks thorough sensitivity and robustness analysis.  
- Limitation: as a bottom-up model, it cannot capture top-down, task-dependent attentional control and this scope limitation should be explicitly noted.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant contribution that makes a compelling case for acceptance.
