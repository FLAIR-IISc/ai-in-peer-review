- Proposes EYMOL: a variational formulation where scanpaths are stationary points of a generalized action combining retinal confinement, a “curiosity” term (brightness gradients + blurred image), and brightness-constancy.  
- Derives Euler–Lagrange equations yielding second-order dynamics for attention; parameters tuned by simulated annealing and saliency maps from aggregated runs.  
- Empirical results: competitive with several classical models and some learned baselines on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU videos; simplified brightness-invariance performs similarly on static images.  
- Relates to classic saliency (Itti et al.), information maximization, Bayesian surprise, and optical flow; least-action perspective is novel relative to NeurIPS attention work.  
- Strength: careful derivation and noted well-posedness condition on λ.  
- Modeling concerns: several choices seem ad hoc (cos/sin curiosity alternation), non-smooth boundary forces via indicators, and fixed m,k,ω without sensitivity analysis.  
- Evaluation concerns: center-bias/blur tuning can inflate AUC-Judd and NSS; sAUC/shuffled metrics should be emphasized.  
- Comparisons omit the strongest recent deep saliency models (e.g., DeepGaze II, SALICON) and lack key ablations isolating curiosity vs. brightness-constancy.  
- Reproducibility: code and full hyperparameter disclosure are not provided but needed.  
- Clarity: generally well written with detailed appendix, but some notation/typos, unclear “content rectangle,” and missing reporting of key constants/ranges.  
- Originality: moderate-to-high — unified least-action treatment producing scanpaths and saliency is novel and intellectually appealing.  
- Significance: moderate — interpretable and fast with potential impact, but overall influence depends on stronger empirical validation.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant unified framework, interpretable dynamics, competitive simplicity, clear derivations, potential real-time use.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited deep-baseline comparisons and sAUC emphasis, missing ablations and hyperparameter reporting, some heuristic/unjustified modeling choices, and exclusion of color/top-down influences.
