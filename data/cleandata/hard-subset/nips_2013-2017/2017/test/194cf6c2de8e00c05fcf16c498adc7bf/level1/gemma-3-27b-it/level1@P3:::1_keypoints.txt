- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention using the Least Action Principle to generate scanpaths by minimizing an action functional.  
- Models potential energy to capture image details and peripheral features, and kinetic energy to represent eye movement dynamics.  
- Incorporates a brightness invariance term to handle illumination changes.  
- Validated on standard saliency/dataset benchmarks (MIT, CAT2000, SFU) and evaluated on dynamic scenes.  
- Differentiates from prior centralized saliency and winner-take-all approaches by framing attention as emergent from physical principles.  
- Novel connection to the Least Action Principle distinct from information-maximization/surprise-based models.  
- Builds on prior variational methods in vision but applies them directly to scanpath prediction, a significant contribution.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear mathematical formulations and detailed appendix derivations.  
- Experimental results are competitive and the paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- Parameter estimation via simulated annealing is computationally expensive and sensitivity analysis is inadequate.  
- The brightness invariance approximation (V1 vs V2) lacks thorough analysis across image types and stronger statistical backing.  
- Biological plausibility of the specific potential/kinetic terms and their neural mapping is underexplained.  
- Comparisons omit recent deep-learning-based saliency models, limiting claims of superiority.  
- Claims of real-time performance need a more detailed computational cost and time-complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, theoretically sound contribution with promising results, but some methodological and evaluation gaps remain.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: analyze simulated annealing parameter sensitivity in detail.  
- Requested revisions: perform rigorous statistical comparison between V1 and V2 (e.g., confidence intervals).  
- Requested revisions: expand comparisons to include recent deep learning state-of-the-art models.  
- Requested revisions: discuss biological plausibility and potential neural correlates of model components.  
- Requested revisions: provide detailed analysis of computational complexity across image resolutions.
