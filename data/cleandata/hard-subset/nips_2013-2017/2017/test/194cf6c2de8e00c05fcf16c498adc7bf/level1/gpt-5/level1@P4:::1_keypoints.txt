- Proposes EYMOL, a dynamical visual-attention model derived from Euler–Lagrange equations with boundary, curiosity, and brightness-invariance terms producing scanpaths and saliency maps.  
- Parameters tuned by simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN and evaluated on MIT1003/MIT300/CAT2000/SFU vs several baselines.  
- Differs from saliency-map-plus-WTA paradigms by deriving attention dynamics from a variational principle rather than a centralized saliency map.  
- Relates to biologically motivated scanpath simulators but emphasizes a principled variational derivation; trades predictive power for interpretability versus CNN-based models.  
- Strength: Conceptual novelty in unifying curiosity-driven exploration and brightness constancy in a continuous-time dynamical system.  
- Strength: Nontrivial technical derivation of Euler–Lagrange equations with velocity-dependent potential and well-posedness analysis.  
- Strength: Computationally light, directly produces scanpaths, and yields saliency maps as a byproduct.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical performance against selected baselines and ablation support for brightness-invariance on static images.  
- Weakness: Some modeling choices appear ad hoc and under-justified (e.g., time-varying curiosity mixture ω, boundary potential acting only outside image).  
- Weakness: Kinetic energy definition likely incorrect (uses (1/m)ẋ^2 vs standard (1/2)m||ẋ||^2), affecting dimensions and parameter interpretations.  
- Weakness: Brightness-invariance term can be minimized by halting (ẋ=0); no stability or phase-behavior analysis for saccades vs fixations.  
- Weakness: Well-posedness derivation (determinant and λ bound) is hard to follow and contains apparent inconsistencies.  
- Weakness: Risk of overfitting to metrics due to multi-stage tuning (SA plus grid searches); center-bias tuning may inflate results.  
- Weakness: Baselines omit strong contemporary deep models (e.g., DeepGaze II, SALICON/ML-Net/DeepFix/SAM), weakening empirical claims.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility details missing (exact parameter values, SA domain, integration method, time discretization, tolerances).  
- Clarity: Paper readable with useful appendix but contains typos, missing parameter definitions (ω, α), and ambiguous equations that need correction.  
- Clarity: Center bias is implemented via input preprocessing, conflating dynamics and post-hoc bias—requires explicit ablation.  
- Clarity: Video tracking claims are qualitative; quantitative tracking/scanpath-similarity metrics are absent.  
- Suggestion: Report ω, m, k, ηb, ηp, λ, α, SA domain and sensitivity analyses, especially for ω and curiosity mixture.  
- Suggestion: Correct kinetic-energy expression and λ < m/4 derivation; normalize units/scales for b and gradients.  
- Suggestion: Add ablations (remove brightness-invariance, peripheral term, center bias), report sAUC/CC/KL/IG, and extend baselines to state-of-the-art deep models.  
- Suggestion: Provide scanpath-level metrics (MultiMatch, string-edit, time-to-first-fixation), analyze numerical stability, and report runtime.  
- For acceptance: Principled, interpretable variational model with clear math, simple implementation, and promising results.  
- Against acceptance: Under-specified equations/parameters, missing strongest baselines and metrics, potential metric overfitting, and limited behavioral/robustness analysis.
