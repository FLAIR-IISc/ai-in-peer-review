- Mathematical formulation is technically sound with clear Euler-Lagrange derivation.  
- Unified treatment of curiosity and brightness invariance is conceptually interesting; model generates scanpaths in real time and a simple V1 approximation suffices.  
- Experimental results are competitive (top scores on half the metrics on MIT300).  

- Major weakness — Lack of scanpath validation: evaluation uses only saliency-map metrics (NSS, AUC) with no trajectory-level comparisons (e.g., SSS, DSS, temporal metrics).  
- Major weakness — Insufficient novelty context: fails to clearly distinguish from Li et al. (NIPS 2015) and does not justify how brightness invariance/parameterization is fundamentally different.  
- Major weakness — Theoretical/empirical gaps: brightness invariance approximation (Eq.6) is unjustified; parameter constraint λ < m/4 (Eq.40) is not experimentally validated; simulated annealing hyperparameters/details are missing.  

- Minor issue — Curiosity term uses cos²(ωt)/sin²(ωt) without justification for ω or its role in avoiding local minima.  
- Minor issue — Paper does not explain how brightness invariance differs from optical flow–based fixation models.  
- Minor issue — Overstates significance: results are competitive but do not clearly advance the state of the art.  

- Recommendation: Weak Reject — elegant and practical model but central claim (scanpath modeling) is not validated and novelty is unclear.  

- Suggestion — Add scanpath-level validation (temporal alignment, saccade amplitude/direction distributions).  
- Suggestion — Explicitly contrast and differentiate from Li et al. (2015) and other variational attention work.  
- Suggestion — Justify brightness invariance with ablations on static images and show how λ behaves across content.  
- Suggestion — Provide full simulated annealing parameters and reframe claims to emphasize scanpath generation rather than saliency superiority.
