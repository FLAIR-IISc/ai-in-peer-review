- Central claim: EYMOL models visual attention scanpaths via a variational principle (Principle of Least Action) producing Euler-Lagrange equations that generate trajectories.  
- Model formulation: Lagrangian with standard kinetic energy and a potential combining a retinal bounding force, a curiosity term toward high-gradient details, and a brightness invariance term for fixation/tracking.  
- Emergent saliency: Saliency maps arise as an emergent property of the temporal scanpath rather than being computed first.  
- Originality/significance: Novel and compelling framing of attention dynamics using variational mechanics, unifying saccades and smooth pursuit in one differential-equation framework.  
- Theoretical contribution: Elegant, principled theory with potential to advance understanding of attention dynamics.  
- Technical quality: Sound mathematical derivation and solid experimental evaluation showing competitive or superior results on standard saliency benchmarks.  
- Evaluation mismatch: Core contribution is dynamic scanpath generation but quantitative evaluation relies on aggregate static saliency maps instead of direct scanpath comparisons.  
- Feature simplicity: Model uses only low-level brightness gradients and ignores color, orientation, and high-level semantics, limiting explanatory completeness.  
- Reproducibility concerns: Need more practical details on numerical integration and hyperparameter search (simulated annealing) to improve reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance given novelty and strong results, but urge authors to acknowledge the evaluation gap and add scanpath-based analyses in future revisions.
