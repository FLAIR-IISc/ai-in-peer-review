- Proposal: physics-inspired Lagrangian model where scanpaths are stationary trajectories of an action combining boundedness, curiosity (image gradients + blurred peripheral gradient alternated), and brightness-invariance terms.
- Mechanism: Euler–Lagrange yields a second-order ODE; saliency maps obtained by visit counts from near-center initializations.
- Experiments: parameters tuned via simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN (NSS) and evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, SFU video against baselines.
- Main novelty: variational least-action formulation unifying curiosity-driven saccades and brightness-invariance-driven fixation/tracking.
- Strength — conceptual novelty: compact variational formulation producing scanpaths without an explicit saliency map module.
- Strength — unification: same dynamics plausibly model saccades and fixation/tracking.
- Strength — efficiency/simplicity: real-time ODE integration using only image gradients and blurred peripheral channel.
- Strength — empirical scope: tested on images and videos with standard benchmarks and a parameter-tuning protocol.
- Key technical flaw: kinetic energy definition mismatch (K = (1/m)||ẋ||^2 vs. used d/dt ∂L/∂ẋ = m ẍ) undermines EL derivation and requires correction.
- Appendix errors: notational/typographical mistakes reduce confidence in derivations and results.
- Ad hoc components: cos^2/sin^2 alternation for curiosity lacks theoretical/psychophysical justification and ω is unspecified and untuned.
- Under-specified hyperparameters: retinal boundary, k, m, ω, Gaussian blur parameters and their selection are not fully reported.
- Center-bias confound: central Gaussian weighting and near-center initializations, plus grid-search of blur/center, likely inflate scores and contradict claim that saliency arises naturally.
- Metric weaknesses: robust-to-center-bias metrics (sAUC, shuffled NSS) are not emphasized, making performance claims ambiguous.
- Limited empirical support: no scanpath-level metrics or tracking evaluations, missing ablations for curiosity alternation, boundary term, and initialization effects.
- Reproducibility issues: missing implementation details (integrator, step size, ω, m, k, initialization noise, preprocessing, SA acceptance/neighborhood/bounds); code not provided.
- Positioning gap: related dynamical/saccadic models not directly compared on scanpath metrics; connections to control/optimal-control literature underexplored.
- Quality assessment: interesting idea but needs mathematical fixes and stronger, bias-controlled empirical support to substantiate central claims.
- Clarity: high-level exposition good, but derivation errors, typos, and undefined hyperparameters hinder understanding and replication.
- Originality: moderate — least-action framing is novel but components are classical; cos^2/sin^2 alternation is new but ad hoc.
- Significance: potentially useful if robust, but limited now by methodological and evaluation issues relative to modern deep saliency models.
- Questions requested: fix kinetic-energy inconsistency; report m,k,ω and sensitivities; evaluate without center bias (sAUC/shuffled NSS/other metrics); provide ablations; detail SA settings; report scanpath/tracking metrics.
- Suggested improvements: correct derivations, fully specify parameters, perform ablations and bias-controlled evaluations, report full quantitative results and stats, release code, and justify or replace cos^2/sin^2 switching with a principled mechanism.
- Recommendation: leaning to reject in current form pending mathematical fixes, bias-controlled experiments, and more thorough empirical validation.
