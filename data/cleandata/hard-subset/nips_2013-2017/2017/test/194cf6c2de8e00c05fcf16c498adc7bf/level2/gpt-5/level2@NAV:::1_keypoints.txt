- Paper proposes a physics-inspired visual attention model deriving scanpath dynamics from a least-action principle, producing saliency maps by aggregating simulated trajectories and relating to variational/dynamical prior work.

- Clear, elegant variational formulation with full derivations, stability condition, and interpretable parameters.

- Unifies saccadic exploration, fixation, and tracking in a single set of ODEs with biologically plausible behavior.

- Empirically competitive on MIT1003/300, CAT2000, and SFU videos despite model simplicity and real-time feasibility; V1 approximation validated for static images.

- Evaluation focuses on saliency-map metrics, lacking scanpath-level metrics (e.g., MultiMatch, string edit, saccade-amplitude KL) to validate dynamical claims.

- Center bias and blur tuned by grid search without emphasis on sAUC/shuffled metrics, risking inflated AUC/NSS scores.

- Limited ablation studies: unclear contributions of curiosity vs brightness-invariance, peripheral term and ω, and dependence on initialization.

- Parameter fitting via simulated annealing on 100 images risks overfitting; cross-dataset generalization is insufficiently analyzed.

- Reproducibility details are incomplete (code, integrator settings, m/k/ω, Gaussian σ) and some references are inconsistent/missing years.

- Suggest adding sAUC/shuffled metrics, toggling/removing center bias, and reporting ablations for ηb, ηp, λ, ω, and boundary potential.

- Suggest including scanpath comparisons to human data and WTA+IOR baselines, and analyzing temporal statistics.

- Suggest evaluating on additional modern datasets (e.g., SALICON, DHF1K) and comparing to current deep SOTA with matched post-processing.

- Suggest releasing code, publishing parameter settings, and reporting runtime and sensitivity analyses.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, unified, interpretable dynamical framework that is technically sound and potentially inspiring for physics-based attention models.

- Arguments against acceptance: current evaluation doesn't validate scanpath claims, reliance on center bias and limited ablations weaken significance, and reproducibility/literature positioning need strengthening.

- Overall: Interesting and potentially impactful work that would become a strong contribution after addressing evaluation, ablation, and reproducibility concerns.
