- Proposes EYe MOvement Laws (EYMOL), a novel visual attention model derived from the principle of least action.
- Model uses a generalized Lagrangian incorporating bounded trajectory, curiosity-driven movement, and brightness invariance.
- Euler–Lagrange equations from the variational framework describe attention dynamics and predict human fixations.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition and experimental setup.
- Evaluated on multiple image and video datasets, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art saliency models.
- Strength: theoretically grounded, novel integration of multiple principles for attention modeling.
- Strength: variational/physics-based derivation provides a consistent mathematical framework.
- Strength: robust evaluation across multiple datasets demonstrates generalizability.
- Weakness: brightness invariance term is simplified and may be inaccurate in some cases.
- Weakness: model parameters are estimated via simulated annealing, which may be inefficient or suboptimal.
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to alternative attention models based on information maximization or surprise.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and provide more comparisons and parameter-estimation alternatives.
- Suggestions: authors should compare with other attention principles, explore better parameter estimation methods, and discuss applications/implications more.
