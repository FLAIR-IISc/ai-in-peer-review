Proposes EYMOL, a novel visual-attention model deriving scanpath differential equations from a Lagrangian using the Least Action Principle (potential, kinetic, brightness-invariance terms).  
Evaluated on several image and video datasets and shown to compete with state-of-the-art attention models.  
Builds on feature-integration and saliency-map literature but differs by using a variational/physical approach; cites information-maximization and Bayesian surprise models.  
Strength: introduces a novel physics-based approach to modeling visual attention.  
Strength: provides a unified interpretation of curiosity-driven movements and brightness invariance.  
Strength: demonstrates good empirical performance across tested datasets.  
Strength: model is relatively simple and computationally efficient, suitable for real-time use.  
Weakness: evaluated on a limited set of datasets, leaving generalizability unproven.  
Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparisons to other state-of-the-art attention models.  
Weakness: does not present a detailed analysis of model parameters or their effect on performance.  
Weakness: omits analysis of temporal correlations between model predictions and human data.  
Originality: novel application of the Least Action Principle to visual attention, representing an original contribution.  
Significance: offers a new conceptual framework with potential impact for computer vision and robotics.  
Quality: paper is well-written with clear technical exposition and balanced theoretical/experimental content.  
Clarity: well-organized presentation with clear figures and tables.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel physics-based formulation, competitive empirical results, and unified theoretical interpretation.  
Arguments against acceptance: limited dataset evaluation and missing parameter/temporal analyses reduce confidence in conclusions.  
Recommendation: evaluate the model on a wider range of datasets to demonstrate generalizability.  
Recommendation: include more comprehensive comparisons to other state-of-the-art models.  
Recommendation: analyze model parameters and sensitivity to understand performance drivers.  
Recommendation: analyze temporal correlations with human gaze data to validate scanpath dynamics.  
Recommendation: provide more detailed performance breakdowns across different image/video types.
