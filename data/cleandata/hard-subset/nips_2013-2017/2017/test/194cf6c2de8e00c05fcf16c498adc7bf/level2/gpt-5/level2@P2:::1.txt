Summary
The paper proposes a physics-inspired model of visual attention, where scanpaths arise as stationary trajectories of a Lagrangian action. The Lagrangian includes: (i) a “boundedness” potential enforcing confinement within the retinal/image domain, (ii) a curiosity term that attracts the trajectory to regions with high local gradient magnitude and a blurred (peripheral) gradient, alternated over time via cos^2(ωt)/sin^2(ωt), and (iii) a brightness invariance term akin to the optical flow constraint (ḃ = b_t + ∇b · ẋ), encouraging fixation and motion tracking. The Euler-Lagrange equations yield a second-order ODE for the focus of attention. Saliency maps are produced as a byproduct by counting visits over many runs from near-center initializations. Parameters (η_b, η_p, λ) are tuned with simulated annealing on CAT2000-TRAIN (NSS), and the model is evaluated on MIT1003, MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU video, reporting competitive performance against several baselines.

Relation to prior work is made primarily to saliency models (e.g., Itti-Koch, information maximization, surprise, learning-based CNNs) and to brightness constancy/optical flow. The main novelty is the variational least-action formulation that unifies curiosity-driven exploration and brightness-invariance-driven fixation/tracking within one dynamical system.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: a compact variational formulation that yields a saliency-producing scanpath without an explicit saliency map module.
- Unification: the same dynamics plausibly model saccades (via curiosity) and fixation/tracking (via brightness constancy).
- Efficiency and simplicity: ODE-based simulator integrates in real time and uses only image gradients and a blurred peripheral channel.
- Empirical scope: experiments on both images and videos, with standard saliency benchmarks and metrics, and a parameter-tuning protocol.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Technical correctness: Eq. (8) defines kinetic energy as K(ẋ) = (1/m)‖ẋ‖^2, but later derivations use d/dt ∂L/∂ẋ = m ẍ − …, which would require K = (m/2)‖ẋ‖^2. This dimensional/derivation mismatch is non-trivial; it undermines the validity of the EL equations unless corrected. The Appendix also contains notational/typographical errors (e.g., repeated b_x1^2 terms), further reducing confidence.
- Ad hoc design choices: the alternation C(t,x) with cos^2/sin^2(ωt) lacks psychophysical or theoretical justification, and ω is not specified or tuned/ablation-tested. The “retina” boundary is a rectangular harmonic wall; choice of k, m, ω, and Gaussian blur parameters is under-specified.
- Evaluation confounds: center bias is explicitly injected by multiplying inputs with a central Gaussian and by initializing trajectories near the image center; blur and center parameters are grid-searched to optimize metrics (AUC-Judd, NSS). This compromises the claim that saliency is a byproduct and makes it difficult to separate model dynamics from priors known to inflate scores. Metrics robust to center bias (e.g., sAUC) are not emphasized.
- Limited empirical substantiation of key claims: despite promising qualitative statements (“best score in half the cases”), the paper does not provide full quantitative tables here. The scanpath-level claims (more realistic saccades, tracking) are not evaluated with scanpath metrics (e.g., string edit distance, multi-match, saccade amplitude distributions) or tracking metrics on videos. The brightness-invariance term’s role is supported only by a V1 vs V2 comparison on static images; no ablation for the curiosity alternation, boundary term, or initial condition distribution.
- Reproducibility: many crucial implementation details are missing (integrator, step size, ω, m, k, initialization noise distribution, exact preprocessing, acceptance probability in SA, parameter ranges). Without code or a complete specification, reproduction is difficult.
- Positioning vs prior work: related dynamical/saccadic models (e.g., Le Meur & Liu and other scanpath generators) are cited but not directly compared on scanpath metrics; connections to control/optimal control formulations in attention are not fully explored.

Quality
- The idea is interesting, but mathematical correctness needs tightening (kinetic energy, appendix typos). The empirical support is incomplete for the central narrative (unified saccade/track generation), and confounds (center bias) are not controlled.

Clarity
- The high-level exposition is clear; however, multiple derivation errors/typos, undefined hyperparameters, and missing details hinder understanding and replication. Figures/tables (not shown here) need to substantiate claims quantitatively.

Originality
- Moderate. The least-action formalism in attention is relatively novel in this context, but the components (gradient-based “curiosity,” brightness constancy) are classical. The cos^2/sin^2 alternation is new but ad hoc.

Significance
- Potentially useful if the dynamics can robustly produce competitive saliency and realistic scanpaths without heavy priors. As presented, significance is limited by methodological issues and evaluation gaps relative to strong contemporary deep saliency models (2016–2017).

Questions for the authors
- Please resolve the kinetic energy inconsistency and re-derive the EL equations accordingly. What are the exact values of m, k, ω, and their sensitivity?
- How much of the performance remains without explicit center bias and near-center initialization? Please report sAUC, shuffled NSS, and CC/SIM/EMD with and without priors.
- Can you provide ablations: remove cos^2/sin^2 alternation, remove blurred peripheral channel, and remove brightness-invariance term?
- How are acceptance probability P(·,·) and neighborhood defined in SA? What are parameter bounds?
- Can you report scanpath-level metrics and tracking performance on videos?

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix derivation errors (K, determinants) and provide a self-consistent, dimensionally correct Lagrangian with complete parameter specification.
- Provide comprehensive ablations and center-bias controls; emphasize metrics robust to priors.
- Report full quantitative results and statistical tests; release code.
- Evaluate scanpath realism and temporal dynamics; compare against saccadic models using established scanpath metrics.
- Justify or replace the cos^2/sin^2 alternation with a principled mechanism (e.g., stochastic switching, peripheral-to-foveal schedule) and analyze ω sensitivity.

Overall recommendation
- Leaning to reject in current form. The conceptual direction is appealing and could be impactful, but the paper needs mathematically consistent derivations, bias-controlled evaluations, and more thorough empirical support of its central claims to meet NeurIPS standards.
