- Proposes EYMOL: a variational, physics-inspired model that generates scanpaths by minimizing an action using a Lagrangian with kinetic energy and three attention potentials.
- Lagrangian potentials: boundary confinement, curiosity attraction to image gradients (sinusoidally modulated), and brightness-invariance for fixation/tracking; scanpaths solved via Euler–Lagrange.
- Produces saliency maps as visitation-frequency byproducts and reports competitive or state-of-the-art results on MIT300, CAT2000, and SFU benchmarks.
- Strength: highly original, elegant unification of attention principles within a principled variational framework.
- Strength: generative, dynamic model producing full temporal scanpaths x(t), enabling analysis of saccade/fixation dynamics.
- Strength: strong empirical validation across static images and video despite being low-parameter and not end-to-end trained.
- Weakness: curiosity term’s sinusoidal modulation choice appears arbitrary and lacks justification or links to cognitive mechanisms.
- Weakness: center bias implemented ad hoc by pre-multiplying images with a Gaussian rather than as a principled potential term.
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on time-aggregated saliency maps, missing direct validation of scanpath dynamics against human eye-tracking statistics.
- Weakness: reproducibility issues—key hyperparameters (m, k, ω) and simulated annealing details (search domain D, cooling α, neighborhood function) are not specified.
- Suggestion: add motivation/discussion for the sinusoidal curiosity term and consider principled incorporation of center bias into the Lagrangian.
- Suggestion: include comparisons of generated scanpath statistics (saccade amplitudes, fixation durations, velocities) with human data.
- Suggestion: provide a hyperparameter table and detailed simulated annealing setup in the appendix to aid reproducibility.
- Overall: significant, original contribution with elegant theory and strong empirical results, but needs clearer component justification, dynamic evaluation, and reproducibility details.
