This paper proposes a novel model of visual attention based on the principle that there are foundational laws that drive the emergence of visual attention. The authors devise variational laws of the eye-movement that rely on a generalized view of the Least Action Principle in physics. The model is derived from three fundamental principles: boundedness of the trajectory, curiosity-driven principle, and brightness invariance. The authors also propose an algorithm to estimate the model parameters using simulated annealing.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its underlying principles. The mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-presented. The experimental results are promising, and the authors provide a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art models.
