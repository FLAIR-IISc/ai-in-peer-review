- Proposes a novel variational model of visual attention inspired by the Least Action Principle, framing eye movements via a Lagrangian dynamical system.  
- Core drives are curiosity (maximizing detail) and brightness invariance, formalized within the action integral.  
- Derives differential equations governing attention and uses simulated annealing for parameter estimation.  
- Builds on classic saliency literature (Koch & Ullman, Itti et al.) but departs by using a global optimization principle instead of local feature combination.  
- The physics-based framing and inclusion of brightness invariance for dynamic scenes are original and noteworthy.  
- Mathematical derivations appear sound and the theoretical approach is elegant.  
- Experimental validation shows competitive performance on standard saliency datasets (MIT, CAT2000, SFU).  
- Authors acknowledge key limitations: need for behavioral scanpath validation and high computational cost.  
- Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility (e.g., simulated annealing parameter ranges not specified).  
- The theoretical framework is complex and presentation is dense, potentially hindering adoption.  
- Insufficient analysis of individual Lagrangian term effects (boundedness, curiosity, brightness invariance) on scanpaths.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve reproducibility, provide more intuitive explanations of Lagrangian terms, and discuss links to neural mechanisms.
