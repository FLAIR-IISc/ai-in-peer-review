- Proposal: Novel visual-attention model based on a generalized Least Action Principle producing variational eye-movement laws.  
- Foundations: Model derived from three principles — trajectory boundedness, curiosity-driven behavior, and brightness invariance.  
- Estimation: Parameter estimation algorithm based on simulated annealing.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Mathematical quality: Rigorous, well-presented mathematical derivations.  
- Experimental results: Promising results with thorough comparison to state-of-the-art, but experiments use only a few datasets.  
- Strengths: Solid mathematical framework, novel interpretation of curiosity and brightness-invariance terms, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: High mathematical sophistication may deter non-experts; unclear real-world application pathway; limited dataset evaluation and uncertain generalization.  
- Originality: Innovative application of the Least Action Principle to visual attention and unique modeling of fixation/tracking.  
- Significance: Offers a new perspective with potential applications in robotics, autonomous vehicles, and HCI.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong novelty, solid theory, and promising comparative experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: Accessibility and practical-application gaps plus limited experimental scope.  
- Verdict: Rating 8/10; recommended accept with minor revisions.
