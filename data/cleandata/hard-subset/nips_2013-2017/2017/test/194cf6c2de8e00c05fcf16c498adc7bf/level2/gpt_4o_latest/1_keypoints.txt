- Paper: Proposes a physics-inspired visual attention model based on the Least Action Principle that derives differential equations for eye movements from boundedness, curiosity-driven attention, and brightness invariance; saliency maps emerge as a byproduct and the model is validated on image and video datasets.

- Strength — Novelty & Rigor: Introduces an innovative variational, physics-based framework unifying curiosity-driven exploration and brightness invariance with strong theoretical grounding.

- Strength — Unified Framework: Integrates saccadic movements and tracking into a single mathematical model, differing from traditional saliency-focused approaches.

- Strength — Experimental Validation: Tested on multiple datasets (MIT1003, CAT2000, SFU) for static and dynamic scenes with competitive results despite model simplicity.

- Strength — Reproducibility: Provides sufficient mathematical and algorithmic detail, including parameter estimation via simulated annealing.

- Weakness — Practical Utility: Practical applicability to real-world, scalable, or real-time systems is unclear due to reliance on differential equations and iterative parameter estimation.

- Weakness — Evaluation Scope: Evaluation emphasizes saliency metrics; lacks direct comparison of generated scanpaths to human eye-tracking data.

- Weakness — Simplistic Assumptions: Assumes early vision is entirely data-driven, neglecting bottom-up/top-down interactions, limiting applicability to complex tasks.

- Weakness — Missing Limitations Discussion: Does not explicitly discuss dependencies on parameter tuning or challenges extending to 3D or multimodal inputs.

- Suggestion: Extend evaluation to compare temporal correlations between generated and human scanpaths to validate biological plausibility.

- Suggestion: Add discussion of limitations and future directions, including integrating top-down influences and adapting the model for real-time use.

- Suggestion: Provide qualitative comparisons of generated saliency maps against state-of-the-art deep models to highlight interpretability differences.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address evaluation scope and limitations discussion.

- Pro arguments: Innovative physics-based framework; competitive saliency performance; strong theoretical foundation and reproducibility.

- Con arguments: Limited practical scalability; narrow evaluation focus; oversimplified assumptions about vision.

- Final rating: 7/10 — Accept with Minor Revisions.
