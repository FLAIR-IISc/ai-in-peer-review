- Proposes a novel variational/least-action inspired model of visual attention producing scanpaths from a Lagrangian with boundedness, curiosity, and brightness invariance terms.  
- Derives differential equations for attention dynamics and validates them via saliency detection on images and videos.  
- Strength: ambitious physics-inspired grounding that is interesting and potentially insightful.  
- Strength: explicit, mathematically tractable Lagrangian formulation.  
- Strength: inclusion of brightness invariance as a mechanism for fixation is a notable contribution.  
- Strength: experimental validation shows the model can predict saliency in static and dynamic scenes.  
- Strength: simulated annealing for parameter estimation is a reasonable approach.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper justification for why physical principles should govern visual attention (cognitive/neural basis not developed).  
- Weakness: curiosity term C(t,x) appears ad-hoc with weak rationale beyond producing desired behavior.  
- Weakness: parameter estimation description lacks sensitivity analysis and robustness checks to initial values or SA settings.  
- Weakness: comparisons to state-of-the-art saliency models are not comprehensive, especially omitting recent deep-learning advances.  
- Weakness: claim that saliency maps are byproducts of scanpaths needs further exploration of their interrelationship.  
- Weakness: approximation used for static images (eq. 6, V1) lacks detailed impact analysis beyond asserting statistical equivalence.  
- Weakness: vague claims (e.g., “best score in half of the cases”) without specifying metrics and datasets.  
- Weakness: appendices contain derivations but are not clearly tied to the main text, hindering reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, clear math, experimental validation, and brightness invariance integration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification for chosen principles, ad-hoc components, limited robustness analysis, and incomplete SOTA comparisons.  
- Essential recommendations: provide stronger justification for the physics grounding and perform thorough parameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Important recommendations: expand comparisons to recent deep-learning saliency models and analyze the static-image approximation’s effect.  
- Nice-to-have recommendations: deepen analysis of scanpath–saliency map relationships and improve connection/clarity between main text and appendices.
