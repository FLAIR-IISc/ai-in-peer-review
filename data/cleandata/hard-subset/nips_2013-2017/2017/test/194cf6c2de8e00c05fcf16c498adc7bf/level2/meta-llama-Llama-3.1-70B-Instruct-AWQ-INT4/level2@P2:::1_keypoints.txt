- Proposes a novel visual-attention model grounded in a generalized Least Action Principle.  
- Uses a variational approach to derive differential equations of visual attention.  
- Introduces a brightness invariance term in the model.  
- Proposes simulated annealing to estimate model parameters.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and situates work clearly within prior literature.  
- Provides a thorough state-of-the-art review and clear explanations.  
- Experimental results are promising and competitive with state-of-the-art saliency models.  
- Model runs in real-time and can track moving objects.  
- Lacks sufficient detail on the simulated-annealing parameter estimation and implementation.  
- Needs more qualitative analysis (e.g., example saliency maps and comparisons to ground truth).  
- Should discuss model limitations and outline directions for future work.  
- Could better clarify how the model differs from and improves on prior work.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
