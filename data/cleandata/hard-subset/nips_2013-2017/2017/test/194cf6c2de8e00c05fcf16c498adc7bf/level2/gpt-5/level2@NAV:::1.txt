Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a physics-inspired model of visual attention that derives scanpath dynamics as Euler–Lagrange equations from a generalized least-action principle. The Lagrangian combines: (i) a boundary potential to confine motion, (ii) a “curiosity” drive alternating local and peripheral gradient energy, and (iii) a brightness-invariance term (akin to the optical flow constraint) to support fixation and motion tracking. Saliency maps are obtained as a byproduct by aggregating simulated trajectories. Experiments on MIT1003/300, CAT2000, and SFU videos report competitive performance with classical and CNN-based saliency models. This contrasts with earlier saliency-map-centric pipelines (e.g., Itti & Koch; Bruce & Tsotsos) and heuristic WTA scanpath simulators, offering a unified dynamical formulation. Related variational/dynamical approaches (e.g., Horn–Schunck, dynamic neural fields) are acknowledged in spirit, though a more explicit positioning versus contemporary deep models common at NeurIPS would help.

Strengths:
- Clear and elegant variational formulation with full derivations and a stability condition (λ < m/4); interpretable parameters.
- Unifies saccadic exploration, fixation, and tracking within one set of ODEs; conceptually appealing and biologically plausible.
- Empirically competitive on standard image and video saliency benchmarks despite model simplicity and real-time feasibility.
- Approximated brightness-invariance variant (V1) validated to match the exact formulation on static images.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation chiefly at the saliency-map level; lacks scanpath-level metrics (e.g., MultiMatch, string edit, KL on saccade amplitudes) that would substantiate the claimed dynamical advantages.
- Center bias and blur are tuned by grid search; sAUC and shuffled metrics controlling for center bias are not emphasized. This may inflate AUC/NSS.
- Limited ablations: need to quantify contributions of curiosity vs brightness-invariance, effect of peripheral term and ω, and dependence on initialization.
- Parameter fitting via simulated annealing on a small subset (100 images) risks overfitting; cross-dataset generalization analysis is minimal.
- Reproducibility details (code, integrator settings, m/k/ω values, Gaussian σ) are incomplete; references are partially inconsistent/missing years.

Suggestions:
- Add sAUC/shuffled metrics, remove/toggle center bias, and report ablations for ηb, ηp, λ, ω, and boundary potential.
- Include scanpath comparisons to human data and to WTA+IOR baselines; analyze temporal statistics.
- Evaluate on additional modern datasets (e.g., SALICON, DHF1K) and compare to current deep SOTA with matched post-processing.
- Release code and parameter settings; report runtime and sensitivity.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, unified, and interpretable dynamical framework with promising results.
- Technical soundness and potential to inspire further physics-based attention models.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Evaluation does not yet validate the core scanpath claims; reliance on center bias and limited ablations weaken significance.
- Reproducibility and literature positioning need strengthening.

Overall: Interesting and potentially impactful; with the suggested additions, this could be a strong contribution.
