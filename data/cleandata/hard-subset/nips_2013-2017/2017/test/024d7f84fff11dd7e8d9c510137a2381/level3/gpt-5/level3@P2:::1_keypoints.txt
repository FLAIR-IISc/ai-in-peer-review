- Studies consensus via Min-Sum; shows standard Min-Sum can fail on loopy graphs while Min-Sum Splitting converges.
- Main result: specific splitting/tuning yields accelerated (subdiffusive) rates matching shift-register (second-order) methods.
- Proves with δ=1 and Γ=γW (W symmetric, W1=1) an optimal γ gives contraction ρK = sqrt((1−√(1−ρW^2))/(1+√(1−ρW^2))) < ρW.
- Shows square-root improvement in convergence time (Θ(D) vs Θ(D^2) up to logs) approaching O(D log(1/ε)) on cycles/tori.
- Connects Min-Sum Splitting structurally to lifted non-reversible Markov chains and multi-step first-order/shift-register methods.
- Technical approach: derive linear edge-based message passing (Alg 2) and an auxiliary node-based two-step system (Alg 3) for spectral analysis.
- Novelty: first rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus and new bridges between BP, lifted chains, and accelerations.
- Technical contribution: auxiliary node-based process that makes spectral properties tractable and enables precise tuning (Theorem 1).
- Significance: integrates BP/Min-Sum into accelerated consensus literature without explicit graph lifting.
- Clarity and positioning: generally well written with comprehensive context, clean derivations, and good mapping between dynamics.
- Scope: convergence proven for arbitrary graph topologies with explicit asymptotics improving over diffusive methods.
- Ambiguity: “splitting into terms” wording suggests integer replication though δ and Γvw are real — needs rephrasing/clarification.
- Practicality concern: optimal γ depends on global spectral quantity ρW; need analysis of sensitivity to misspecification and decentralized estimation methods.
- Generality limitations: analysis restricted to quadratic objectives, synchronous updates, and symmetric W — ask about extensions to general convex, asynchronous/time-varying, and nonsymmetric W.
- Empirical gap: paper is theory-centric; small-scale experiments (cycles, grids, random graphs) would show constants, robustness, and comparisons to Metropolis, heavy-ball/Nesterov, ADMM.
- Initialization/stability: Proposition 1’s positivity condition needs discussion for nonzero initial messages and allowed initializations.
- Clarity fixes requested: emphasize assumptions on W, define diag(Γ1) at first use, and ensure consistent directed/undirected edge notation.
- Specific suggestions: clarify role of δ (is δ≠1 useful?), compare to ADMM-based accelerations, and discuss decentralized choice/estimation of W and ρW plus γ sensitivity.
- Evaluation summary: technically sound, clear, original, and significant, but leaves robustness/practicality and empirical validation open.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, bridges multiple literatures, provides general rates close to optimal on geometric graphs.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on global spectral tuning, restriction to quadratic/synchronous setting, lack of experiments.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
