- Proposes Min-Sum Splitting for consensus with claimed subdiffusive accelerated convergence matching shift-register methods.
- Claim of optimal convergence for graphs with good expansion is questionable because analysis focuses on complete graphs which are not representative.
- Authors should analyze a broader set of graph topologies; current results lack generality.
- Literature review is incomplete—missing key work such as Olshevsky on linear-time average consensus.
- Important consensus-optimization references omitted (e.g., Shi et al., Scaman et al., Jakovetic et al.).
- Related work on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM (Franca & Bento) is ignored.
- Last-paragraph claim is a long-known fact and should cite Rabbat et al. on generalized consensus computation.
- Proposition 3 appears trivial and offers limited technical impact.
- Claimed convergence improvement is not significant in worst-case; bound still appears O(n).
- The auxiliary message-passing scheme may be equivalent to Nesterov’s acceleration, reducing novelty.
- Any improvement likely stems from spectral-gap handling, but this does not resolve worst-case bounds.
- Use of directed edges on a simple graph is confusing; there is a typo referring to "Laplacian" instead of "Lagrangian".
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address the reviewer’s concerns; evaluation unchanged.
- Pros: novel modification to Min-Sum and some theoretical analysis plus simulations.
- Cons: incomplete literature, insufficiently supported claims, and technical contributions require significant revision.
