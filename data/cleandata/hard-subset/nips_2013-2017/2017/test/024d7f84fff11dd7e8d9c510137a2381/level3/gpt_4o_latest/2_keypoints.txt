- Novelty: Connects Min-Sum Splitting with lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods, offering a novel perspective.
- Theoretical rigor: Provides convergence proofs and an auxiliary-process analysis that are methodologically sound.
- Potential impact: Results are relevant to sensor networks, distributed ML, and multi-agent systems.
- Marginal improvement: Convergence rate improvement (O(D^2 log(1/ε)) → O(D log(D/ε))) is incremental and of limited significance.
- Poor worst-case: Worst-case bound remains O(n), which is unimpressive relative to existing methods.
- Misleading claim: Claim of achieving the "optimal rate" for complete graphs is misleading since complete graphs converge in one step.
- Ambiguity on graph classes: Fails to specify which graph categories get dimension-independent complexity or define what constitutes "good" graphs.
- Missing literature: Omits critical related work (e.g., "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs", Shi et al., and "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM").
- Recycled techniques: Auxiliary message-passing scheme appears to reapply Nesterov-like acceleration, questioning originality.
- Trivial result: Proposition 3 is trivial and contributes little to the paper.
- Notational issues: Use of directed edges in a simple graph is confusing; contains a typo ("Laplacian" vs. "Lagrangian").
- Overall recommendation: Reject unless authors address clarity, related work omissions, and better justify practical significance.
