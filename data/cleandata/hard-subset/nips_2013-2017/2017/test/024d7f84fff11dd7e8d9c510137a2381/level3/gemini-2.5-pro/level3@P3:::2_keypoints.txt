- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for average consensus and proves accelerated convergence O(D log(D/ε)) with a specific parameter tuning.  
- Analysis reduces Min-Sum Splitting on directed edges to an auxiliary multi-step linear system on nodes (Propositions 1–2).  
- Paper is generally well-written and technically coherent; derivations of the edge-to-node reduction are clear and useful.  
- Claim about fast convergence on "graphs with good expansion" is misleading; complete graphs converge trivially in one step and "good" graphs need a formal definition.  
- Proposition 3 is trivial consequence of reparametrization and adds limited insight.  
- Minor errors and unclear phrasing (typo "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian"; confusing use of directed edges on a simple graph).  
- Key prior work is omitted: Olshevsky (2014) achieves O(D log(1/ε)), making the claimed rate less compelling in worst cases.  
- Important consensus/optimization literature not cited: linear convergence of ADMM (Shi et al. 2014), Scaman et al. 2017, EXTRA (Shi et al. 2015), Jakovetic et al. 2014.  
- The connection between the method and momentum/acceleration is overstated; similar ideas (memory terms, Heavy Ball, Nesterov) are well-known and should be acknowledged.  
- Missing reference on lifting: Franca and Bento (2016) directly links Markov chain lifting with distributed ADMM and is relevant.  
- Novelty and significance are weakened by lack of engagement with existing literature and by reducing to spectral analysis of a linear system.  
- Required revisions: add and compare to key references (especially Olshevsky 2014 and Scaman et al. 2017), acknowledge established links to multi-step/momentum methods, and clarify claims about graph classes and technical presentation.
