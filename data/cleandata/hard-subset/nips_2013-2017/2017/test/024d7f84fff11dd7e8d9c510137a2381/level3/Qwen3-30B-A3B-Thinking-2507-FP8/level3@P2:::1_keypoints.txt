- Studies Min-Sum message-passing for distributed consensus and analyzes a modified version called Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Shows ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge on graphs with cycles, while Min-Sum Splitting converges with accelerated (subdiffusive) rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum algorithms, lifted Markov chain techniques, and multi-step first-order optimization methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and situates contributions clearly within existing literature.  
- Main contributions: (1) first link between Min-Sum and lifted Markov chains, (2) tuning directionality in Belief Propagation for acceleration, (3) first convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting, (4) Min-Sum protocol with improved rates for consensus.  
- Strong technical analysis using an auxiliary node-based process to relate convergence to the spectral gap of a simpler matrix.  
- Theorem 1: proper parameter choice yields a square-root improvement over diffusive methods, scaling like O(D log(D/ε)) for cycles and tori, approaching optimal O(D log(1/ε)).  
- Originality is high by connecting previously disparate areas and clarifying differences from prior Min-Sum work.  
- Clarity and organization are excellent; technical sections build logically from background to results.  
- Minor wording ambiguity on page 4 regarding "splitting" terminology (Γvw and δ introduced as reals).  
- Significance is high for distributed systems and suggests broader implications and future generalizations.  
- Main weakness: limited exploration of splitting parameter choices beyond the specific case in Theorem 1.  
- Recommendation: accept for presentation at NIPS due to strong theoretical and practical contributions.
