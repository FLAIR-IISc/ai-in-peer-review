- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for consensus and shows accelerated convergence O(D log(D/ε)) with connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex methods.
- Technical soundness: rigorous analysis and appropriate proof techniques linking Min-Sum Splitting to shift-registers.
- Clarity: well-written and well-organized, making core ideas accessible.
- Related-work connection: authors connect to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods.
- Limited improvement: claimed rate O(D log(D/ε)) is only a modest gain over prior work (e.g., Olshevsky’s O(D log(1/ε))) and can be worse when D ~ n.
- Incomplete literature review: misses key references including Olshevsky, Shi et al. (ADMM), Scaman et al. (accelerated methods), and França & Bento (Markov lifting/ADMM).
- Misleading claim about complete graphs: discussion irrelevant because complete graphs converge in one step; need to specify which graph classes benefit.
- Incremental contribution: Proposition 3 appears trivial and adds little new insight.
- Attribution oversight: “long-known fact” and links to acceleration (Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak) lack proper citations (e.g., Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew).
- Minor technical issues: typos and phrasing inconsistencies (e.g., “Laplacian” vs “Lagrangian”, directed edges on simple graphs).
- Auxiliary process insight limited: the message-passing auxiliary scheme’s relation to Nesterov-like acceleration is underexplored and reduces to a linear-system analysis.
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound analysis, interesting connections to accelerated methods, and contribution to message-passing understanding.
- Argument against acceptance: modest empirical/theoretical improvement, incomplete literature coverage, and overall incremental novelty.
- Overall assessment: solid but incremental contribution with sound analysis tempered by limited novelty and missing citations.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; fix literature gaps and clarify practical implications for future submission.
