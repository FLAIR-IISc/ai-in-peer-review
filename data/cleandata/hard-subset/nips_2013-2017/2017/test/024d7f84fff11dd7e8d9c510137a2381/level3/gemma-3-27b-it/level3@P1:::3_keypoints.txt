- Studies accelerated convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus, building on Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013) with analysis for quadratic consensus problems.  
- Introduces an auxiliary linear process to track Min-Sum Splitting dynamics, enabling a more tractable theoretical analysis.  
- Claims key contributions: novel proof technique using the auxiliary process, improved convergence rates, and links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly structured, with logical flow from background to contributions.  
- Manuscript contains numerous typographical and grammatical errors (e.g., "theirs neighbors", "double-stochastic", "casted") that reduce polish.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct on inspection but require more detailed verification.  
- Presentation of Algorithms 2 and 3 is incomplete: some variables (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}) are undefined and initializations (e.g., \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are missing.  
- Algorithm 3 should explicitly identify which variables correspond to components of the auxiliary linear process to improve clarity.  
- Related work section omits important references in (sub)gradient and decentralized optimization (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Srinivasan et al. 2009, Wei Shi 2015) and classical acceleration (Polyak, Nesterov).  
- Practical implications are under-discussed: computational overhead, scalability, conditions favoring Min-Sum Splitting, and limitations are not addressed.  
- The paper needs a more intuitive explanation of the auxiliary process and discussion on extending the framework beyond quadratic objectives to broader consensus problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical novelty, apparent technical soundness, effective auxiliary-process analytical tool, and insightful connections to existing methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical discussion, incomplete algorithmic specification, omitted related work, limited intuition for key choices, and typographical errors.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on completing algorithms, expanding related work, clarifying the auxiliary process and practical relevance, and thorough proofreading.
