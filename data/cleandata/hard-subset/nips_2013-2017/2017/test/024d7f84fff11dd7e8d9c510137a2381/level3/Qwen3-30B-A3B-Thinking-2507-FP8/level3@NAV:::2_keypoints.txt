- Paper studies Min-Sum message-passing with splitting for average consensus and claims accelerated convergence vs diffusive methods.
- Authors show Min-Sum Splitting yields linear message-passing dynamics on directed edges and propose an auxiliary algorithm on the original graph for analysis.
- Claim of convergence rate O(D log(D/ε)) for cycle graphs and tori, touted as a "square-root improvement" over O(D² log(1/ε)).
- Technical content is generally sound and the paper is well-written.
- Major misleading claim about complete graphs: paper states Θ(D log(1/ε)) despite complete graphs converging in one step.
- Paper fails to specify which graph classes yield dimension-independent complexity or to define what constitutes "good" graphs.
- Omits key related work, notably Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus.”
- Also fails to cite Shi et al.’s EXTRA, Scaman et al. on distributed ADMM, and Franca and Bento on Markov chain lifting.
- Neglects citations linking consensus acceleration to Heavy Ball/Nesterov methods (Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew).
- Claimed rate improvement is minimal—D log(D/ε) offers little over Olshevsky’s D log(1/ε) and is worse in worst-case D≈n.
- The auxiliary message-passing scheme appears to rederive Nesterov acceleration; main contribution is unclear as analysis reduces to spectral-gap arguments.
- Proposition 3 is trivial and the paper contains technical errors (e.g., "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian").
- Novelty is overstated, technical contribution is limited, and significance is reduced by poor contextualization within existing literature.
- Paper needs thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods and clearer definitions of the claimed improvements.
