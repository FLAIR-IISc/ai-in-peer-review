- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus optimization.  
- Uses reparameterization from Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013) and analyzes convergence on quadratic consensus problems.  
- Main technical tool is an auxiliary linear process that tracks the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough review of the literature on consensus problems and distributed optimization.  
- Main contribution 1: a new proof technique via an auxiliary process.  
- Main contribution 2: improved convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Main contribution 3: connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Offers clear and concise explanations of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm and its variants.  
- Delivers detailed convergence analysis supported by theoretical results and numerical simulations.  
- Manuscript contains several typos and grammatical errors that need fixing.  
- Algorithm descriptions are incomplete and some variables are not explained.  
- Omits relevant literature on (sub)gradient and decentralized optimization.  
- Originality: introduces a novel auxiliary-process approach not previously explored.  
- Significance: potential to improve efficiency and scalability of distributed optimization with better convergence rates.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear explanations, solid convergence analysis, and novel auxiliary-process technique.  
- Arguments for rejection: typographical/grammatical issues, incomplete algorithm descriptions, and missing relevant literature.  
- Minor comments: proofread to eliminate errors, clarify algorithm details and variables, and include broader literature on (sub)gradient and decentralized optimization.
