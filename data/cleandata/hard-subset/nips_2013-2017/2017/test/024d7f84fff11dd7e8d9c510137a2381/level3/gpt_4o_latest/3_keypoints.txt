- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, an accelerated Min-Sum message-passing protocol using reparametrization to handle cycles in graphs.  
- Establishes convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting, with detailed results for quadratic objectives.  
- Shows accelerated performance over classical diffusive methods, achieving subdiffusive convergence with a square-root improvement.  
- Uses an auxiliary linear process as a novel proof technique to track algorithm evolution.  
- Draws formal connections between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Technical soundness: proofs and propositions are rigorous and appear correct.  
- Novelty: first formal link between Min-Sum schemes, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step methods in convex optimization.  
- Clarity of theoretical contributions: paper is well-organized and clearly delineates main results.  
- Clarity issues: several algorithm descriptions are unclear and some variables are not properly initialized, hindering reproducibility.  
- Typos and grammatical errors throughout reduce readability.  
- Insufficient references to recent distributed optimization and machine learning literature limits contextualization.  
- Practicality concerns: limited discussion of practical limitations and applicability to general consensus problems or ML tasks.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, improved convergence rates, and novel analysis technique.  
- Argument against acceptance: weak justification of practical relevance to the NeurIPS ML audience and issues with clarity/polish.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on addressing clarity, applicability, and referencing issues.
