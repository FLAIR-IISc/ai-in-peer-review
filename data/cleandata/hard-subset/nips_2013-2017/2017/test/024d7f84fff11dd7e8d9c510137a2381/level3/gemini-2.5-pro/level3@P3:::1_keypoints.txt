- Paper shows Min-Sum Splitting converges on cyclic graphs and, with proper tuning, achieves accelerated (subdiffusive) consensus rates.
- Authors analyze the algorithm via a novel auxiliary multi-step linear node process that tracks the original edge-based message updates.
- Theoretical results prove Min-Sum Splitting matches shift-register accelerated rates (square-root improvement over diffusion) tied to the graph spectral properties.
- Conceptual contribution: draws a new connection between Min-Sum message-passing, lifted Markov chain acceleration, and momentum-based first-order methods.
- Originality/significance: elevates Min-Sum Splitting from a convergence heuristic to a principled, high-performance distributed algorithm design tool likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Technical quality: proofs are rigorous and the auxiliary scheme (Algorithm 3) is an elegant, effective analysis technique supporting Theorem 1.
- Clarity/presentation: paper is very well written, well organized, and situates contributions clearly within prior literature.
- Weakness: wording around "splitting" may mislead readers into thinking δ and Γ_vw are integer counts, whereas the algorithm uses real-valued parameters—clarification needed.
- Weakness: analysis focuses on a specific parameter choice (δ=1, Γ=γW), leaving sensitivity to other parameter settings (δ≠1, alternative Γ structures) underexplored.
- Conclusion: overall excellent, minor presentation issues only, strong accept recommendation.
