- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus that converges on loopy graphs with subdiffusive rates.
- Shows proper tuning of parameters (δ and Γ) lets Min-Sum Splitting match state-of-the-art shift-register convergence rates.
- Situates work within distributed optimization, building on prior Min-Sum consensus results and connecting to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods.
- Main theoretical contribution: a new proof technique using an auxiliary linear process on nodes (not directed edges) to analyze spectral properties and convergence.
- Establishes rigorous convergence-rate analysis and qualitative advance over previous Min-Sum results.
- Achieves convergence times scaling as O(D log(D/ε)) for cycle graphs and tori, matching best known accelerated rates.
- Novel connection between Min-Sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques, offering a new theoretical bridge between approaches.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and technically careful.
- Ambiguity in presentation: Γ_vw and δ introduced as real numbers but described as splitting into integer numbers of terms, causing confusion about algorithm interpretation.
- Does not fully explore sensitivity of convergence rates to parameter choices beyond the optimal theoretical setting.
- Practical implementation aspects are under-discussed, including how global information required for optimal tuning (e.g., ρ_W) would be obtained in a distributed system.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution, minor fixable presentation issue, overall meets acceptance criteria for NIPS.
