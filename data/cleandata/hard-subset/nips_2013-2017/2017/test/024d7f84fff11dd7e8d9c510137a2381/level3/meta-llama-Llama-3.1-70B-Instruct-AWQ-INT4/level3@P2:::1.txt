This paper applies the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm to solve the consensus problem in distributed optimization. They prove that a proper choice of the tuning parameters allows Min-Sum Splitting to achieve subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates, matching the rates obtained by shift-register methods.
