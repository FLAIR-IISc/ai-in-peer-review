- Proposal: Use Min-Sum Splitting for average consensus and show parameter tuning yields accelerated convergence.  
- Analysis: Introduces an auxiliary message-passing scheme to track node dynamics and links the method to lifted Markov chains and multi-step (Nesterov-like) acceleration.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and the auxiliary-process analysis is a noteworthy analytical technique.  
- Significance concern: Claimed O(D log(D/ε)) rate is not compelling compared to O(D log(1/ε)) (Olshevsky 2014) and can be worse in the worst case (D = O(n)).  
- Cause of gains: Improvements appear to stem from standard spectral-gap manipulation; worst-case bound remains O(n).  
- Missing prior work: Fails to cite/compare directly competitive results such as Olshevsky’s linear-time consensus.  
- Additional omitted references: Ignores EXTRA (Shi et al., 2015), fast distributed gradient methods (Jakovetic et al., 2015), Scaman et al. (2017) on optimal rates, and Franca & Bento on lifting/ADMM.  
- Established connections missed: The auxiliary/memory interpretation resembles known momentum methods (Polyak/Nesterov) and prior work on memory terms (e.g., Rabbat et al., 2005).  
- Novelty unclear: Acceleration may be a reformulation of existing momentum-based approaches applied to the consensus linear system.  
- Clarity issues: The phrase “dimension-independent complexity on ‘good’ graphs” is vague and requires a formal definition of such graphs.  
- Misleading claim: Statements about complete graphs are confusing since consensus on complete graphs can converge in one step.  
- Technical contribution: Proposition 2 seems to be a straightforward reparameterization and not a core/new contribution.  
- Overall verdict: Novelty and performance claims are not substantiated due to weak comparative analysis and lack of engagement with substantial related work.
