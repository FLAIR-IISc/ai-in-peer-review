- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus and claims accelerated convergence (O(D log(D/ε)) for cycles and tori), a purported square-root improvement over diffusive O(D² log(1/ε)).  
- Main technical contribution: analysis linking Min-Sum Splitting to accelerated methods and introducing an auxiliary message-passing scheme to track convergence.  
- Proof techniques and supplementary material are technically sound; presentation and organization are clear.  
- Claim of "dimension-independent complexity" is misleading and authors fail to specify graph classes for which their rates apply.  
- Authors omit critical related work, notably Olshevsky (2017), which achieves O(D log(1/ε)) and undermines the claimed novelty.  
- Several important distributed optimization and lifting references (EXTRA, ADMM results, Franca & Bento) are missing.  
- Proposition 3 is trivial and overstated as a main contribution.  
- The connection to Heavy Ball/Nesterov acceleration is stated without citing earlier works (e.g., Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew 2005).  
- Technical inaccuracies: misuse of "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian" (line 220) and misleading statements about worst-case convergence.  
- The auxiliary message-passing appears to mirror known Nesterov acceleration, leaving the true novelty unclear.  
- The claimed O(D log(D/ε)) improvement is marginal (log factor) and not meaningful in worst-case analysis where bounds remain O(n)=O(D).  
- Authors’ rebuttal addressed technical points, but the core issue—minimal or non-novel improvement compared to prior work—remains.  
- Overall assessment: technically correct but poorly contextualized, incremental contribution; recommendation: Weak Reject.
