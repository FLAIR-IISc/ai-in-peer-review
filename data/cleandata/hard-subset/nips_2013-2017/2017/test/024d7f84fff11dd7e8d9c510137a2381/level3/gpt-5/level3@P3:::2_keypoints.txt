- Studies average-consensus via Min-Sum with Splitting; specific parametrization yields a linear message-passing scheme tracked by an auxiliary node-based linear system with memory.  
- With δ = 1 and Γ = γW (γ tuned by spectral gap), achieves accelerated, subdiffusive convergence matching shift-register/Chebyshev consensus.  
- Positions the scheme between lifted Markov chains (edge directionality) and multi-step first-order acceleration (node momentum).  
- Strength: Clear derivation of a linear, quadratic-form Min-Sum Splitting and a tractable auxiliary node-based spectral analysis.  
- Strength: Theorem 1 recovers the known √-improvement over diffusive mixing, aligning with shift-register/Chebyshev rates.  
- Strength: Insightful pedagogical link between directionality on edges and momentum on nodes; generally clear writing and sound proofs.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty — main rate O(D log(D/ε)) reproduces known Chebyshev/shift-register acceleration and is mainly a reinterpretation.  
- Weakness: Claim about “good” graphs is misleading/incomplete; authors should define “good graphs,” distinguish complete graphs from expanders, and state algorithmic constraints (e.g., nonnegativity/reversibility).  
- Weakness: Missing comparison to Olshevsky (2014), which attains O(D log(1/ε)); need citation, rate/assumption comparison, and clarification whether O(D log(1/ε)) is achievable.  
- Weakness: Missing or underemphasized consensus-optimization references and quantitative comparisons (Shi et al. ADMM, Scaman et al. 2017, EXTRA, Jakovetić et al.).  
- Weakness: No substantive comparison to lifting-related ADMM work (França and Bento 2016); add detailed comparison of setup, overhead, and rates.  
- Weakness: Classic equivalences between consensus and momentum methods are stated without proper attribution; cite Rabbat/Nowak/Bucklew and classic acceleration literature (Polyak, Nesterov, Golub–Varga).  
- Weakness: Proposition 3 is a straightforward linear-algebra identity and should be framed as a technical lemma in the supplement.  
- Weakness: Spectral-gap tuning mirrors Nesterov/Chebyshev and isn’t novel; worst-case graphs (D = Θ(n), 1−ρ(W)=Θ(1/n^2)) can give worse scaling than prior work.  
- Weakness: Auxiliary process seems to restate known momentum/shift-register dynamics on the induced linear system; authors should clarify practical/conceptual benefits over Chebyshev.  
- Minor issues: Terminology/typos — clarify “directed edges on a ‘simple graph’” and correct “Laplacian”→“Lagrangian” at line 220.  
- Quality: Analysis is technically sound and consistent with spectral arguments, but evaluation against state-of-the-art is incomplete.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; auxiliary-system derivation improves readability but needs clearer graph-class statements and positioning.  
- Originality: Moderate — novelty mainly in framing Min-Sum Splitting as the mechanism; acceleration mechanism and rates are not new.  
- Significance: Limited-to-moderate — useful perspective for message-passing researchers but largely repackages known consensus accelerations for optimization/consensus experts.  
- Pros: Clean conceptual connection between Min-Sum Splitting, lifting, and multi-step acceleration; transparent linear-algebraic analysis; potential to extend Min-Sum to other objectives.  
- Cons: Modest novelty in rates, incomplete related-work coverage, and some misleading/inexact claims about graph classes.  
- Key questions for authors: Can tuning achieve O(D log(1/ε)) (match Olshevsky)? Are there graph classes where you provably beat diffusive methods and match linear-in-D? Any empirical comparisons vs Chebyshev, Nesterov, EXTRA, distributed ADMM?  
- After rebuttal: Clarifications and citations were added, but reviewer’s overall assessment remains that the contribution is incremental and SOTA improvement is limited.
