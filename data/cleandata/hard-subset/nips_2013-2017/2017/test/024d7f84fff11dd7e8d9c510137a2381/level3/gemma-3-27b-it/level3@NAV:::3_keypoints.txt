- Paper studies accelerated convergence of Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, using a reparameterization and an auxiliary linear process.  
- Main contribution: novel proof technique showing improved convergence rates and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step optimization.  
- Theoretical analysis and proof sketches appear sound; Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 are plausible.  
- Presentation of core ideas is clear and ties the work to known acceleration methods.  
- Practical implications are underexplored; the paper does not state when this method is preferable to existing consensus approaches.  
- Missing discussion on computational complexity, scalability, and implementation trade-offs.  
- The role and intuition behind the auxiliary process need clearer, more intuitive explanation.  
- Algorithm descriptions (especially Algorithm 3) are incomplete: undefined variables (Rvw, rvw) and missing initializations (R^0, r^0) hinder reproducibility.  
- Connection between Algorithm 3 variables and the auxiliary process is not explicitly clarified.  
- Numerous minor typos and grammatical errors (e.g., “theirs neighbors,” “double-stochastic,” “casted”); needs careful proofreading.  
- Possible misuse/confusion between “Laplacian” and “Lagrangian” around lines 220–221.  
- Relevant related work is omitted: (sub)gradient and decentralized optimization literature and classical acceleration (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Srinivasan et al. 2009, Wei Shi 2015, Polyak, Nesterov).  
- Paper should discuss limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and whether the framework extends beyond quadratic objectives to broader ML consensus problems.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision and resubmission after addressing practical, algorithmic, and literature shortcomings.
