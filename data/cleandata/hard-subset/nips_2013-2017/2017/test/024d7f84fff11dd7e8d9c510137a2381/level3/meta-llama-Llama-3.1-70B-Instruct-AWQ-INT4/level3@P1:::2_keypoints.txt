- Summary: Proposes Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum algorithm for distributed consensus, proving convergence and subdiffusive accelerated rates and linking it to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Thorough analysis of convergence properties and acceleration mechanisms.

- Strength — Originality: Introduces a new Min-Sum Splitting algorithm as a modification of ordinary Min-Sum.

- Strength — Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written, easy to follow.

- Strength — Significance: Addresses the important distributed consensus problem and reports improved convergence rates.

- Weakness — Limited improvement: Claimed convergence gains are modest and it’s unclear which graph classes achieve dimension-independent complexity.

- Weakness — Missing citations: Fails to cite and compare to relevant prior work (e.g., Olshevsky, Shi et al. on ADMM).

- Weakness — Omitted related work: Important references on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM (Franca and Bento) are missing.

- Weakness — Trivial contribution: Proposition 3 seems trivial and its significance is unclear.

- Weakness — Unclear contribution: The auxiliary message-passing algorithm may simply mirror Nesterov acceleration and seems redundant since consensus reduces to a linear system.

- For acceptance: Presents a new algorithm with improved rates, connects to lifted Markov chains and multi-step methods, and is well-written.

- Against acceptance: Improvements are unimpressive, lacks essential citations/comparisons, and omits key related work indicating incomplete scholarship.

- Rating: Overall score 6/10 — novel and well-presented but lacks thoroughness and has limited impact.
