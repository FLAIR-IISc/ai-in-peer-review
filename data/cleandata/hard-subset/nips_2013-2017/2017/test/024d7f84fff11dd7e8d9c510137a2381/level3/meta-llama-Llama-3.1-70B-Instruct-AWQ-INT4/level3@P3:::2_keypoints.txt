- Proposes accelerated consensus via Min-Sum Splitting, a modification of the traditional Min-Sum algorithm.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting can achieve subdiffusive convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Establishes connections between Min-Sum schemes, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and motivation.  
- Technical contributions are sound and include a thorough convergence analysis.  
- Claimed square-root improvement over diffusive methods exists but is not very impressive.  
- Fails to provide a clear comparison with other accelerated consensus algorithms (e.g., Olshevsky).  
- Missing important references (e.g., Shi et al. on ADMM linear convergence; Scaman et al. on consensus-optimization) and state-of-the-art linear rates.  
- Minor errors/confusing notation: uses directed edges on a simple graph and has a typo ("Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian" at Line 220).  
- Clarity rated 8/10 but some technical terms are not explained.  
- Originality rated 6/10; algorithm not shown to be substantially different from existing methods.  
- Quality and significance both rated 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: new algorithm, theoretical connections, readable presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: limitations, missing comparisons/references, and errors.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing limitations, adding comparisons/references, and correcting minor errors.
