- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus optimization and analyzes it on quadratic problems.  
- Introduces an auxiliary linear process as a main technical tool to track Min-Sum Splitting.  
- New proof technique via the auxiliary process is a significant contribution.  
- Establishes improved convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Connects the method to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization.  
- Manuscript is generally clear and well-written, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Paper contains several typos and grammatical errors that distract the reader.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 seem correct but need more detail and explanation for accessibility.  
- Algorithm descriptions are incomplete and some variables (including initialization and auxiliary process details) are not explained.  
- Omits relevant literature on (sub)gradient and decentralized optimization.  
- Lacks clear discussion of practicality and guidance on when to use this method versus other consensus methods.  
- Provides little intuition for using the auxiliary process and fails to state limitations of Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Originality is high due to the auxiliary-process proof technique and improved rates.  
- Presentation quality is mixed: generally clear but marred by errors and incomplete algorithmic details.  
- Relevant to NIPS and of interest to the ML community because of theoretical contributions and connections to related methods.  
- Recommended improvements: expand proof details, complete algorithm descriptions and initializations, discuss practicality and limitations, add intuition for the auxiliary process, include related (sub)gradient/decentralized literature, and better state ML relevance.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Presentation 7/10, Relevance to NIPS 8/10.
