- Contribution: Studies Min-Sum Splitting for average consensus, showing ordinary Min-Sum can diverge on loopy graphs while a split/reparametrized variant converges.  
- Technical result: Derives an auxiliary node-space linear system whose transition matrix matches shift-register form and proves accelerated “subdiffusive” rates.  
- Rates: For cycles and tori they obtain O(D log(D/ε)) convergence.  
- Conceptual links: Highlights relations between splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Quality: Analysis is careful and technically sound; auxiliary formulation clarifies the spectral argument.  
- Empirics/evaluation: Empirical and comparative evaluation is insufficient relative to the claims.  
- Claims vs improvement: Claimed improvement is modest and discussion of state-of-the-art rates is incomplete.  
- Clarity: Paper reads well overall but has notation/terminology issues (directed edges on a “simple graph”; “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian”).  
- Prior art citation: Last paragraph of p.5 repeats known facts—should cite Rabbat–Nowak–Bucklew and acknowledge links to Heavy Ball/Polyak and Nesterov.  
- Originality: Main novelty is framing min-sum splitting to match shift-register/Nesterov-like dynamics, but this largely mirrors standard acceleration and offers limited new algorithmic content.  
- Proposition placement: Proposition 3 appears trivial and should be moved to the supplement.  
- Significance: O(D log(D/ε)) is close to known accelerated rates and offers little over O(D log(1/ε)); can be worse in worst-case (D≈n).  
- Misleading claim: The “dimension-independent” complexity claim on complete graphs is misleading—complete graphs can reach consensus in one step with appropriate weights; clarify which graph classes benefit and define “good” graphs.  
- Source of gains: Any benefits mainly arise from spectral-gap handling; worst-case bound remains O(n) since O(n)=O(D).  
- Related work missing: Should cite/compare with Olshevsky (linear-time consensus), ADMM linear convergence (Shi et al.), Scaman et al., EXTRA (Shi et al.), Jakovetić et al., and França & Bento on lifting and ADMM.  
- Pros summary: Clear analysis, useful auxiliary formulation, and bridges between BP/splitting, acceleration, and lifting.  
- Cons summary: Overstated novelty, misleading claims about complete graphs, insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art, limited worst-case improvement, and some routine results.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer appreciated clarifications but keeps the same overall evaluation.
