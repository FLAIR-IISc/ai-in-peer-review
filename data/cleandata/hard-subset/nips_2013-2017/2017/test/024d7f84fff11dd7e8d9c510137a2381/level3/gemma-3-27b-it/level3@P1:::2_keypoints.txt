- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, proving convergence and targeting acceleration over diffusive methods.  
- Core idea: reparametrize (split) Min-Sum to force convergence on graphs where ordinary Min-Sum fails.  
- Main theoretical result: convergence rate O(D log(D/ε)) for cycles and tori, connected to shift-register methods.  
- Draws parallels between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated first-order methods.  
- Introduces an auxiliary process to track algorithm evolution, simplifying analysis.  
- Analysis is technically sound and the derivations are well-motivated.  
- Novelty: detailed convergence analysis and explicit link to shift-register/lifting, despite prior work on splitting.  
- Improvement in convergence rate is incremental and not clearly superior to existing results (e.g., Olshevsky 2007).  
- Claim about complete graphs is misleading; authors must clarify which graph classes benefit and when complexity is dimension-independent.  
- Important related work is omitted (e.g., Shi et al. on ADMM, Scaman et al., França & Bento on lifting/ADMM).  
- Discussion lacks sufficient contextualization and comparison to state-of-the-art linear-convergence methods.  
- Proposition 3 seems trivial and adds little to understanding.  
- Minor clarity issues and typos (e.g., “directed edges on a simple graph”; "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian").  
- Recommendation: weak accept — technically solid but limited impact; request to add missing references and clarify applicable graph classes.
