- Summary: Proposes Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, introducing a splitting parameter that yields accelerated convergence matching shift-register rates and links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step methods.

- Strength 1: Novel application of Min-Sum Splitting to the consensus problem.

- Strength 2: Thorough convergence analysis with proof of asymptotic convergence rate.

- Strength 3: Establishes connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.

- Weakness 1: Claimed convergence improvement is modest and lacks clear comparison to state-of-the-art.

- Weakness 2: Missing citation to Olshevsky's relevant work on linear-time average consensus.

- Weakness 3: Omits important references on consensus optimization (e.g., Shi et al. on ADMM linear convergence, Scaman et al. on fast distributed gradient methods).

- Weakness 4: Misleading claim about complete graphs, which actually converge in one step.

- Weakness 5: Proposition 3 seems trivial and of limited contribution.

- Quality: Technically sound with solid analysis but some clarity and writing issues and insufficient comparative evaluation.

- Originality: Presents a novel approach, but connections to existing accelerated methods are not fully contextualized or novel.

- Significance: Results are potentially important but impact would increase with more context, references, and detailed comparisons.

- Recommendation: Accept with revisions addressing comparisons to prior work, missing citations, clarification on complete-graph claims, and clearer exposition.
