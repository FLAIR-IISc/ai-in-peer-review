- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus (network averaging) and claims subdiffusive convergence matching shift-register methods.
- Authors link Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and multi-step accelerated first-order methods.
- Theoretical analysis is clear, with correct mathematical derivations and a connection to an auxiliary linear process.
- Identifies an interesting connection between Min-Sum message-passing and accelerated methods.
- Originality is questionable: connections appear known or trivial and key related works are missing (Olshevsky, Shi et al. EXTRA, Franca & Bento on ADMM).
- The Heavy Ball/Nesterov relationship is not properly acknowledged as established in prior work.
- Claimed improvement O(D log(D/ε)) over Olshevsky’s O(D log(1/ε)) is marginal and can be worse for some graphs (e.g., complete or cycle graphs).
- Misleading statements about complete graphs (they converge in one step) and overstated significance of the rate improvement.
- Insufficient empirical/theoretical comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms and to established optimal rates.
- Some clarity and writing errors: contradictory phrase "directed edges on a simple graph," typo "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian," and unclear novelty of the auxiliary message-passing reparameterization.
- Arguments for acceptance: sound theoretical analysis, interesting (if not novel) connection to acceleration, and a new proof technique using an auxiliary process.
- Arguments against acceptance: marginal improvement, missing key references, overstated significance, and lack of proper contextualization in the literature.
- Overall assessment: marginal theoretical contribution with proper math but inadequate contextualization, missing comparisons, and insufficient novelty to recommend acceptance without substantial revision.
