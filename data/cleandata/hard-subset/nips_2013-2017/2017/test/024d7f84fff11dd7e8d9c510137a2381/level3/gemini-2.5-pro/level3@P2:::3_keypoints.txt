- Paper proves first convergence-rate results for Min-Sum Splitting, showing Min-Sum Splitting can converge where ordinary Min-Sum fails on loopy graphs.  
- With appropriate splitting parameters, algorithm attains accelerated, subdiffusive convergence matching shift-register methods.  
- Novel connection established between Min-Sum Splitting acceleration and lifted Markov chains plus multi-step first-order optimization methods.  
- Key proof technique: introduce an auxiliary linear node-based process to track the edge-based Min-Sum dynamics.  

- Strength: Highly original theoretical contribution bridging belief-propagation-style algorithms and accelerated optimization literature.  
- Strength: Technical derivations appear sound; auxiliary scheme elegantly reduces analysis from 2m edge variables to 2n node variables.  
- Strength: Insight that splitting parameters introduce directionality/momentum explains the acceleration mechanism.  

- Weakness: Manuscript clarity suffers from numerous typos and grammatical errors that impede readability.  
- Weakness: Algorithm descriptions (Alg. 1 and 2) lack clear definitions and initialization of key variables, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Relationship between main and auxiliary algorithms is not made explicit enough for readers to follow the technical argument.  

- Weakness: Related work misses foundational references (Polyak, Nesterov) and key decentralized optimization works (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis; EXTRA by Shi et al.).  
- Weakness: Scope limited to quadratic network averaging; paper lacks discussion on extending to general convex consensus problems relevant to ML.  
- Weakness: Practical implications absent — no comparison of communication/computation cost or applicability versus ADMM, gradient tracking, or other state-of-the-art methods.  

- Request: Thorough proofreading to correct typos and grammar.  
- Request: Clarify notation in Alg.1 (functional minimization) and explicitly define initial conditions (e.g., R̂0 = r̂0 = 0) used in proofs.  
- Query: Confirm suspected typo “Laplace multipliers” vs. “Lagrange multipliers.”  
- Request: Provide more intuition and interpretation for the auxiliary process and variables Q and q.  
- Query: Explain how dependence on global quantity ρW fits the local message-passing paradigm and whether pre-computation is required.  
- Request: Discuss comparisons to ADMM/D-GD regarding convergence speed, communication cost, and implementation ease.  
- Query: Identify obstacles to extending analysis beyond quadratics and whether linearity of message updates breaks for general convex functions.
