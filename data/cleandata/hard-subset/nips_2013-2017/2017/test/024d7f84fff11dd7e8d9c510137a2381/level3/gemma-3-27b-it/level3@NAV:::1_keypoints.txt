- Applies Min-Sum Splitting to distributed consensus and shows it can converge where standard Min-Sum may not.  
- Demonstrates accelerated (subdiffusive) convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and situates the contribution clearly within existing literature.  
- Provides meticulous related-work discussion and precisely defines how the approach advances the state of the art.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and results are novel and convincing.  
- Derivation of the auxiliary message-passing scheme (Algorithm 3) is clever and simplifies analysis.  
- Minor ambiguity in the wording on page 4 about “splitting each term … into Γ_vw terms, and each φ_v into δ terms” which suggests a discrete split despite δ and Γ_vw being real.  
- Recommendation: accept with a minor revision to clarify the splitting description on page 4.
