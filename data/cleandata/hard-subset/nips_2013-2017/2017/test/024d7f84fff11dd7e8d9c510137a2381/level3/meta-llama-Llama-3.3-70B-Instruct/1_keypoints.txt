- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm applied to distributed consensus.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting converges with accelerated rates, achieving a square-root improvement over diffusive methods.  
- Establishes connections between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order convex optimization methods.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis of convergence rates supporting the claims.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and places contributions clearly within prior work.  
- Technical results are sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Strength: relates Min-Sum Splitting to other methods, offering insight into the acceleration mechanism and broader applicability.  
- Weakness: unclear explanation of the splitting terms (Γ_vw and δ) in the equivalent objective on page 4.  
- Suggestion: more experimental results would strengthen empirical validation.  
- Originality: novel application of Min-Sum to consensus and novel theoretical connections.  
- Significance: potential impact on distributed optimization and consensus methods.  
- Recommendation: accept.
