- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for quadratic consensus, showing ordinary Min-Sum can diverge on cycles but Min-Sum Splitting converges.  
- With δ=1 and Γ=γW (W symmetric, stochastic), authors get accelerated, subdiffusive rates matching shift-register/second-order methods.  
- Analysis introduces a novel auxiliary node-linear process to track edge-based Min-Sum dynamics and yields a 2n×2n spectral characterization (matrix K) coinciding with shift-register state matrix.  
- Positions Min-Sum Splitting conceptually relative to lifted nonreversible chains and multi-step methods, arguing it implicitly adds directionality and memory.  
- Improves prior Consensus Propagation results by providing rates for general graphs and recovering O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles/tori, approaching Nesterov-style optimal rates.  
- Complements Gaussian BP with splitting work by supplying explicit convergence rates for quadratic consensus.  
- Technical soundness: propositions and Theorem 1 appear correct; quadratic-message preservation and edge/node recursions are coherent.  
- Main result assumptions: requires δ=1 and Γ=γW with W symmetric, W1=1 and ρ(W−11^T/n)<1; optimal γ depends on global spectral gap.  
- Scope limitation: analysis restricted to quadratic objectives; general convex cases not treated.  
- Some algorithmic details missing or unclear: variables (e.g., R̂vw, r̂vw) not defined before use and initializations (R̂0, r̂0) not specified near Algorithm 2.  
- Mapping between Algorithms 2 and 3 needs explicit correspondence and explanation of Qs, qs in the final estimator.  
- Minor terminology error: likely misuse of “Laplace/Laplace multipliers” instead of “Lagrange multipliers.”  
- Typos and notation inconsistencies throughout; recommend consistent bold/hat/bar notation and symbol definitions at first use.  
- Originality: first convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus with a new auxiliary-process proof technique and novel conceptual links.  
- Significance: sharp theoretical understanding and a potentially broadly useful auxiliary-process technique, but limited practical guidance on parameter tuning.  
- Pros: clear theoretical contribution, unifies BP/lifted chain/multi-step views, and presents an elegant auxiliary-node analysis.  
- Cons: γ requires global spectral info with no adaptive/local tuning proposed; restricted to quadratics; missing robustness/asynchrony analysis; algorithm exposition needs tightening; limited discussion on when to prefer Min-Sum Splitting over ADMM/EXTRA/shift-register.  
- Question — parameter selection: how to choose γ in practice without global spectrum knowledge; can distributed power iteration or conservative bounds retain acceleration?  
- Question — beyond quadratics: can the auxiliary-process extend to strongly convex smooth or composite objectives (via linearization/proximal splitting)?  
- Question — robustness: how do asynchrony, delays, or time-varying graphs affect the analysis and results?  
- Question — practical trade-offs: compared to shift-register/Chebyshev or ADMM/EXTRA, when is Min-Sum Splitting preferable in communication, memory, computation, and constants?  
- Question — initialization: clarify admissible ranges ensuring positivity in Proposition 1 and why R̂0=r̂0=0 is valid.  
- Suggestions: define all algorithmic variables and default initializations in a self-contained pseudocode; state assumptions for xt being well-defined.  
- Suggestions: discuss decentralized γ estimation (distributed power method, bounds, adaptive momentum) and add intuition linking edge-directionality to node-memory and the K matrix.  
- Suggestions: include empirical comparisons on cycles, grids, and random graphs against Metropolis gossip, Chebyshev/shift-register, and ADMM/EXTRA to illustrate constants and robustness.  
- Suggested related work to add: Polyak (1964), Nesterov (1983), Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis (1989), Tsitsiklis et al. (1986), and classic decentralized optimization references.  
- Noted typos: “theirs neighbors”→“their neighbors”; “double-stochastic”→“doubly-stochastic”; “casted as”→“cast as”; replace “Laplace multipliers”→“Lagrange multipliers.”  
- Overall evaluation: strong theoretical development and originality but needs clearer presentation and practical guidance — recommendation: Weak accept (confidence: high).
