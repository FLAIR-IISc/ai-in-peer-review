- Proposes Min-Sum splitting for consensus with provable convergence and subdiffusive behavior, claiming O(D log(D/ε)) rates on cycles and tori.
- Mathematical analysis is rigorous (Theorem 1) and connects to shift-register methods.
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, making ideas accessible.
- Incremental novelty: useful unification with lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods, but not a major breakthrough.
- Reported O(D log(D/ε)) rate is a modest improvement and can be worse than existing methods (e.g., Olshevsky’s O(D log(1/ε))), especially when D ~ n.
- Misleading/incorrect claim about complete graphs converging in multiple steps; authors must clarify which graph classes have dimension-independent complexity and define “good” graphs.
- Crucial related work is missing: Olshevsky (2017), Shi et al. on ADMM linear convergence, Scaman et al. on EXTRA, Franca & Bento (2017) on lifted Markov chains/distributed ADMM.
- Omitted citation/context for diffusion random walks and connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak acceleration (e.g., Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew 1998).
- Proposition 3 appears trivial and adds little value.
- Novelty of auxiliary message-passing is questionable since analysis reduces to a linear system and mirrors Nesterov-like acceleration.
- Gains seem due to handling the spectral gap; worst-case bound remains O(n)=O(D) for n-node graphs.
- Minor technical errors and typos: confusing phrase “directed edges on a simple graph” (line 243) and “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian” (line 220).
- Pros for acceptance: technically sound analysis, useful connections to other methods, and relevance to distributed optimization.
- Cons for acceptance: modest improvement, significant missing related work, misleading claims, and minor technical errors weaken the paper.
- Overall: solid but not exceptional; would benefit from addressing missing citations, clarifying claims, and fixing errors.
- Recommendation/score: Reject (5/10) with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing concerns; post-rebuttal did not change this evaluation.
