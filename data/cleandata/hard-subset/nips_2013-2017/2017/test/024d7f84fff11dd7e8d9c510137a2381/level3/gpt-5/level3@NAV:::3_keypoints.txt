- Summary: Studies distributed consensus via Min-Sum Splitting, introduces an auxiliary node-level linear process, and proves accelerated subdiffusive rates with tuning δ=1, Γ=γW, linking to lifted chains and Polyak/Nesterov acceleration.

- Quality: Theoretical core is solid; Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 appear correct and the auxiliary-process analysis is technically neat.

- Practical weakness: Method requires global spectral information (ρW) to set γ and gives little guidance on when to prefer it over EXTRA, gradient tracking, or distributed ADMM.

- Robustness gap: No discussion or solutions for asynchrony, noise, or time-varying graphs.

- Algorithmic issues: Algorithm 2/3 incomplete—missing variable definitions (e.g., Rvw, rvw), initializations (R̂0, r̂0), inconsistent equation numbering, and unclear mapping of auxiliary to edge-level variables.

- Originality: Novel proof technique via an auxiliary node-level linear system that clarifies links between BP-style Splitting, lifted nonreversible chains, and multi-step acceleration.

- Significance: Advances understanding of accelerated consensus for quadratics and may enable extensions, but impact limited without clearer algorithms and broader applicability.

- Clarity: Generally well-written but marred by typos and missing definitions (e.g., “theirs neighbors”, “double-stochastic”, “casted”, likely “Lagrange/Lagrangian multipliers”).

- Missing related work: Should cite foundational decentralized optimization and acceleration literature (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Tsitsiklis et al. 1986; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei & Ozdaglar / Wei Shi et al.; Polyak 1964; Nesterov 1983/2004).

- Practicality question: How can γ be set/estimated without global eigen-information—any heuristics or adaptive tuning?

- Limitations question: When does Min-Sum Splitting fail or underperform, and intuition for the auxiliary process and its memory term?

- Generalization question: Can the framework extend beyond quadratics to general strongly convex, composite, or stochastic consensus problems?

- ML relevance question: Clarify scenarios (e.g., federated averaging under bandwidth constraints) where this approach is preferable.

- Pros: Solid theory, new proof technique, clear links to known accelerations.

- Cons: Incomplete algorithms, limited practical guidance, missing references, and typos.

- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying algorithms and strengthening practical discussion.
