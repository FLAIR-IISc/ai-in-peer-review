- Applies the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm to the distributed consensus optimization problem.  
- Shows ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge, while the Splitting modification converges to the solution.  
- Proves that appropriate tuning yields subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.  
- Results are novel and represent a qualitative advance in distributed optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates its contribution within the state of the art.  
- Provides a thorough introduction to Min-Sum and a comprehensive review of accelerated consensus methods.  
- Clarity of exposition is excellent with concise presentation of results.  
- Originality and significance of the contribution are high.  
- Minor presentation issue: ambiguous wording on page 4 about “splitting each term φ_vw into Γ_vw terms, and each φ_v into δ terms” since Γ_vw and δ were introduced as real numbers.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication despite the minor wording ambiguity.
