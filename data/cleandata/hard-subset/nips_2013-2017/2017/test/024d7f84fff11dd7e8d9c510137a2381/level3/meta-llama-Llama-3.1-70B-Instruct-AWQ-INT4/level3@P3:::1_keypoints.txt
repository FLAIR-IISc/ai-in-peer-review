- Presents Min-Sum Splitting as a novel approach to distributed consensus with clear situating in prior work.  
- Interprets Min-Sum Splitting as a process on the lifted space of directed edges, introducing directionality in message exchange.  
- Connects the directionality in splitting to non-reversible lifted Markov chain techniques.  
- Shows that splitting induces a memory term at nodes, linking the method to multi-step (accelerated) first-order optimization methods.  
- Proves that appropriate tuning yields subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Demonstrates technical soundness with theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides enough detail for expert reproduction of results.  
- Work is novel, addresses a difficult problem, and constitutes a qualitative advance in the field.  
- Results are important and likely to be useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- One ambiguity: wording on page 4 about "splitting each term φ_vw into Γ_vw terms, and each φ_v into δ terms" is unclear since Γ_vw and δ are real numbers.  
- Potential cons: technical density may limit accessibility for non-experts and may be less interesting outside distributed optimization.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on clarifying the ambiguous point on page 4.
