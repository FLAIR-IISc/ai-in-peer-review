- Paper applies Min-Sum splitting to consensus and claims accelerated convergence O(D log(D/ε)), but improvements feel incremental.
- Writing and problem presentation are clear; analysis is mathematically sound.
- Novelty is overstated and contribution unclear due to missing connections to existing accelerated methods.
- Claim that Min-Sum splitting outperforms on complete graphs is misleading—standard consensus converges in one step on complete graphs.
- Authors must define which graph classes have dimension-independent complexity and rigorously justify that claim.
- Missing crucial comparison to Olshevsky (2017) whose D log(1/ε) result is competitive or better, especially when D ~ n.
- Several important related works are omitted: Shi et al. (ADMM linear convergence, 2016), Scaman et al. (EXTRA, 2017), and Franca & Bento (Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM).
- Paper omits foundational citations (Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew) and fails to acknowledge connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak acceleration and other fast distributed gradient methods (Jakovetic et al.).
- Proposition 3 appears trivial and adds little to the main contribution.
- Auxiliary message-passing likely replicates Nesterov-type acceleration but this connection is not established.
- Gains seem to come from improved spectral gap handling, yet worst-case bound remains O(n)=O(D), limiting practical improvement.
- Minor issues: confusing use of directed edges on simple graph (Line 243) and typo ("Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian" on Line 220).
- Overall score 5/10: technically sound but limited contribution, significant omissions, and misleading claims reduce suitability for acceptance.
