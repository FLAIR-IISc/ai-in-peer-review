- Applies Min-Sum Splitting to distributed consensus; with properly tuned parameters it converges and attains accelerated, subdiffusive rates matching shift‑register methods.  
- Core contribution: proves a novel theoretical connection between Min‑Sum Splitting, directionality in lifted Markov chains, and momentum in multi‑step first‑order methods, unifying three acceleration paradigms.  
- Significance/originality: addresses a fundamental distributed optimization problem and offers a new, likely influential perspective that can spur further research.  
- Technical quality: theoretical results appear sound; the auxiliary node‑based process is a clever proof technique that simplifies analysis.  
- Theorem 1: provides a rigorous characterization showing a square‑root improvement over standard diffusive methods on certain graph classes.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well written and structured, with a clear introduction that situates the work in the literature.  
- Minor weakness: the term “Splitting” is justified using an integer‑parameter interpretation while the algorithm allows real parameters—this naming explanation should be clarified.  
- Overall verdict: high‑quality, significant work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
