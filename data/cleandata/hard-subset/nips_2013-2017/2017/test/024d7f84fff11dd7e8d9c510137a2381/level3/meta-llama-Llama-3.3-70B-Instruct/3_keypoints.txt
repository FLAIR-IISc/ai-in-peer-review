- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for solving consensus problems in distributed optimization.
- Establishes convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting and links the method to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Shows that appropriate tuning yields subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates matching shift-register methods.
- Provides a thorough analysis; proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.
- Paper is generally well-written but contains several typos and grammatical mistakes.
- Fails to justify the importance or relevance of the results to the Machine Learning community.
- Lacks intuitive explanation for the auxiliary process used in the Min-Sum Splitting method.
- Does not discuss extending the framework to broader consensus problems in Machine Learning.
- Minor terminology issues (e.g., use of “Laplacian” vs. “Laplace multipliers”) need clarification.
- Missing several relevant references, including works on (sub)gradient methods for consensus optimization.
- Overall a significant contribution but needs more context, clarification, and references to be accessible to a wider audience.
