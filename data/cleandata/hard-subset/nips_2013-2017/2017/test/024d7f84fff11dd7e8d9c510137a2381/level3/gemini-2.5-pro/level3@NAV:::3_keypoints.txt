- Proposal: apply Min‑Sum Splitting message‑passing to accelerate distributed consensus optimization, analyzed for quadratic consensus problems.  
- Core technical contribution: novel proof technique introducing an auxiliary linear process to track Min‑Sum Splitting dynamics.  
- Results: establish improved convergence rates and draw connections to lifted Markov chains and multi‑step first‑order methods.  
- Theoretical strength: elegant, novel auxiliary‑process approach and technically sound proofs providing previously absent convergence guarantees.  
- Conceptual strength: successfully bridges belief‑propagation/message‑passing with classical acceleration frameworks, with potential to inspire future work.  
- Clarity/reproducibility weakness: incomplete algorithmic descriptions (undefined variables R{vw}, r{vw} and missing initializations \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0).  
- Manuscript quality issues: numerous typos/grammar errors and possible misuse of terminology (e.g., "Laplacian" vs "Lagrangian").  
- Related‑work weakness: fails to adequately cite foundational decentralized optimization and acceleration literature (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov).  
- Significance/discussion weakness: lacks practical discussion, intuition for the auxiliary process, and clear guidance on when the method is preferable to alternatives.  
- Scope limitation: unclear whether the framework extends beyond quadratic objectives and how it applies to common machine‑learning consensus problems.  
- Recommendation: address clarity, complete algorithmic details, literature positioning, and practical implications/limitations to strengthen the paper.
