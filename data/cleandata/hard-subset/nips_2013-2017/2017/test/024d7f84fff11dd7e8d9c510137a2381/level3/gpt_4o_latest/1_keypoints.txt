- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, an accelerated variant of Min-Sum for distributed consensus.  
- Shows standard Min-Sum can fail to converge; Splitting ensures convergence with subdiffusive acceleration.  
- Establishes novel connections between Min-Sum algorithms, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Uses an auxiliary message-passing scheme to enable detailed spectral analysis of convergence.  
- Proves a square-root improvement in convergence time, scaling as O(D log(D/ε)) for graphs with diameter D.  
- Particularly effective for geometric graphs (e.g., cycles and tori) where diffusive methods are suboptimal.  
- Well-written with a thorough literature review and clear positioning as a unifying framework.  
- Strong analytical rigor and novel, rigorous derivation of convergence rates.  
- Clarity issue: interpretation and explanation of splitting parameters δ and Γ_vw are insufficient.  
- Practical limitation: acceleration relies on global graph properties (spectral gap), reducing fully decentralized applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept; minor clarity fixes recommended, strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses.
