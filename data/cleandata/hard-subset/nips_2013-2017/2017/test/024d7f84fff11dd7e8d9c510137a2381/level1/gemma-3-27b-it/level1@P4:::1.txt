This paper investigates the convergence rates of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm applied to the consensus problem. The authors demonstrate that, unlike the standard Min-Sum algorithm, the splitting version converges and achieves accelerated convergence rates, specifically matching those obtained by shift-register methods. The paper establishes a connection between Min-Sum Splitting and both lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.

The paper builds upon prior work on Min-Sum algorithms [34, 35, 27] and distributed consensus [42, 41, 20, 21, 9, 19, 29, 40, 17, 10, 6, 16, 39, 31, 11, 26, 3]. It correctly positions itself within the landscape of accelerated methods, citing relevant work on lifted Markov chains [37] and accelerated first-order methods [13, 24]. The introduction provides sufficient context and motivation for the study. The connection drawn to shift-register methods and the corresponding convergence rates is a significant contribution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The analysis of convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting is a novel contribution, as highlighted in the introduction. Establishing a square-root improvement over diffusive methods is a notable result.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous analysis, utilizing an auxiliary message-passing scheme to track the evolution of the algorithm and derive convergence rates. The use of spectral analysis is appropriate and well-executed. The proofs, while deferred to the supplementary material, are critical for validating the claims.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notations are reasonably clear, and the mathematical derivations are presented in a logical manner. The connections to existing techniques (lifted Markov chains, first-order methods) are explained well.
*   **Significance:** The accelerated convergence rates achieved by Min-Sum Splitting are potentially significant for large-scale distributed optimization problems. The insights gained regarding the role of splitting parameters could inspire new algorithm designs.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** While well-organized, the paper is dense and requires a strong background in distributed optimization and message-passing algorithms to fully understand. The numerous references add to this complexity.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The paper focuses solely on theoretical analysis and lacks any empirical validation. While theoretical results are valuable, demonstrating the performance of the algorithm on practical datasets or network topologies would strengthen the paper.
*   **Initialization Dependence:** The convergence analysis assumes specific initial conditions. A discussion on the sensitivity of the algorithm’s performance to different initialization strategies would be beneficial.
*   **Connection to ADMM:** The paper mentions a connection to ADMM [12], but this connection isn’t fully explored. Elaboration on this point could add value.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound analysis of Min-Sum Splitting, a relatively unexplored algorithm.
*   The achieved convergence rates are a significant improvement over existing methods.
*   The connections to other established techniques (lifted Markov chains, first-order methods) provide valuable insights.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of empirical validation limits the practical impact of the findings.
*   The paper's complexity may make it inaccessible to a broader audience.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper with a significant contribution to the field of distributed optimization. The analysis is rigorous, the results are novel, and the connections to related work are well-established. The lack of empirical validation is a drawback, but the theoretical findings are compelling enough to warrant acceptance.

**Recommendation:** Accept. A minor revision requesting some discussion on the sensitivity to initialization and a brief exploration of the connection to ADMM would be beneficial.
