- Studies Min-Sum Splitting for consensus and shows the splitting variant converges where standard Min-Sum can fail.  
- Proves the splitting method attains accelerated (subdiffusive) convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Highlights connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order (accelerated gradient) methods.  
- Builds on prior Min-Sum and distributed consensus literature and cites relevant accelerated-consensus work.  
- Comparison to existing work (especially [27]) is clear and improvements are well-stated.  
- Technical analysis is sound with clear mathematical derivations of convergence rates.  
- The connection between Min-Sum splitting, lifted chains, and accelerated methods is a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Proofs demonstrating subdiffusive scaling are strong and convincing.  
- Paper is logically organized from background to main results.  
- Presentation is dense and heavily notational, reducing accessibility.  
- Intuition behind choice of splitting parameters and their spectral interpretation is insufficiently explained.  
- Discussion of the connection to ADMM is brief and could be expanded.  
- Practical implications and computational cost comparisons to existing methods are under-discussed.  
- For acceptance: novel theory, demonstrable acceleration, and meaningful links to established acceleration techniques.  
- Against acceptance: mathematical density, limited practical discussion, and incomplete exploration of related-area connections.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with requests to improve clarity and expand practical/comparative discussion.
