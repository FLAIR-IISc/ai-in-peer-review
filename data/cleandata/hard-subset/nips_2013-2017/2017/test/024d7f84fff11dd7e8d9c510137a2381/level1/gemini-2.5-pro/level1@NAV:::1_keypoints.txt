- Paper shows Min-Sum Splitting converges for distributed consensus and, with proper tuning, achieves accelerated (subdiffusive) convergence matching shift-register methods.
- Novel conceptual bridge: links Min-Sum splitting parameters to acceleration mechanisms in lifted Markov chains and momentum-based multi-step optimization.
- Technical contribution: introduces an auxiliary node-based message-passing scheme to analyze edge dynamics, enabling a clear spectral analysis (Theorem 1).
- Proven result: spectral analysis yields a square-root improvement in convergence time over standard diffusive methods for important graph classes (e.g., tori).
- Originality/significance: provides a new lens for designing accelerated distributed algorithms by interpreting splitting as an acceleration mechanism.
- Quality: proofs and technical development appear sound and methodologically elegant.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and situates contributions within existing literature.
- Practicality concern: acceleration requires tuning parameter γ based on global spectral radius ρW, with no discussion of distributed estimation or implementation.
- Limited scope: theoretical analysis is restricted to least-squares network averaging and lacks discussion of extension to general convex consensus problems.
