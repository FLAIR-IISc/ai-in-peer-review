- Paper shows ordinary Min-Sum fails on loopy graphs but Min-Sum Splitting converges and, with tuned splitting parameters, achieves accelerated (subdiffusive) consensus rates.  
- Authors derive an equivalent linear edge-based iteration (Algorithm 2) for quadratic consensus.  
- They construct a node-based two-state linear auxiliary system (Algorithm 3) whose outputs match Min-Sum estimates under simple initialization (Proposition 2).  
- Choosing δ=1 and Γ=γW with γ=2/(1+√(1−ρW^2)) yields asymptotic rate ρK equal to the second-largest eigenvalue magnitude of a shift-register matrix K (Theorem 1), giving a square-root improvement over diffusive methods.  
- Results imply O(D log(D/ε)) time on cycles and tori, approaching best-known accelerated bounds O(D log(1/ε)).  
- Paper conceptually bridges Min-Sum Splitting with lifted non-reversible Markov chains (directionality) and multi-step first-order methods (momentum).  
- This is the first work to provide convergence-rate guarantees for Min-Sum Splitting and to show it can match shift-register asymptotics on consensus.  
- Analysis generalizes prior BP/Consensus Propagation work to arbitrary graphs via the auxiliary node-based system and explicit rates.  
- Technical core is sound; equivalence between Algorithms 2 and 3 is elegant and key to spectral analysis.  
- Strong assumptions: synchronous updates, static graph, symmetric W, quadratic objectives, and zero initialization; tuning requires global spectral information (ρW).  
- Positivity condition in Proposition 1 is mild but should be stated more explicitly for tuned parameters.  
- Proofs are deferred due to space; a brief proof sketch for Theorem 1 would aid readability.  
- No empirical validation is provided to assess constants, pre-asymptotic behavior, or robustness.  
- Clarity issues: heavy notation (K̂ vs K, R̂/R, r̂/r), some incomplete references and minor typos; a dependency figure would help.  
- Practical concerns: higher per-iteration communication cost (quadratic parameters per edge) and no guidance on choosing W or distributed/adaptive tuning of γ.  
- Suggested improvements: add simulations comparing to Metropolis, shift-register, Nesterov, and lifted gossip; quantify communication/computation overhead; propose adaptive spectral estimation; clarify positivity condition; discuss extensions beyond quadratic and asynchronous/time-varying graphs.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with clear novelty in BP literature and potential impact — reviewer leans toward acceptance despite limited scope and lack of experiments.
