- Introduces a novel application of the Min-Sum message-passing protocol to the distributed consensus problem.  
- Proposes a modified Min-Sum algorithm called Splitting that yields convergence to the solution.  
- Provides rigorous, well-presented mathematical derivations and proofs of convergence.  
- Thorough analysis of convergence rates showing accelerated performance.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting can match accelerated rates obtained by shift-register methods.  
- Connects Min-Sum Splitting to other accelerated methods (lifted Markov chains, multi-step first-order methods).  
- Interprets the algorithm as a process on a lifted space (directed edges of the graph), offering a new perspective.  
- Experimental results support the theoretical claims.  
- Assumes a quadratic objective, which limits applicability to non-quadratic problems.  
- Analysis is restricted to a specific class of graphs, raising generalization concerns to other topologies.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and concise in exposition.  
- Quality score: 9/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of application, rigorous convergence analysis, and connections to known accelerated methods.  
- Arguments for rejection: restrictive quadratic assumption and limited graph-class analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
