- Novel approach: applies Min-Sum Splitting to consensus in distributed optimization, achieving convergence where ordinary Min-Sum does not.  
- Convergence analysis: provides rigorous, thorough analysis of Min-Sum Splitting convergence rates.  
- Acceleration result: demonstrates subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Technical trick: introduces an auxiliary linear process to track and analyze Min-Sum Splitting dynamics.  
- Connections: links Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order accelerated methods.  
- Writing and organization: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Literature review: provides a thorough review and clearly positions its contributions relative to prior work.  
- Originality: presents novel insights connecting message-passing to accelerated distributed optimization.  
- Significance: results could impact distributed optimization, machine learning, and sensor networks.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes a quadratic objective, limiting generality to other objective classes.  
- Future work: authors acknowledge need to generalize results beyond quadratic functions.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, rigorous analysis, acceleration results, and broad potential impact.  
- Rejection arguments: restrictive quadratic assumption and need for further generalization.
