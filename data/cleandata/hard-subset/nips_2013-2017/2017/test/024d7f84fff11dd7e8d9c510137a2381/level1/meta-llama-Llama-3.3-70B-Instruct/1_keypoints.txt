- Proposes Min-Sum Splitting, a novel variant of Min-Sum that effectively solves the consensus problem.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis including convergence proof and characterization of convergence rate.
- Demonstrates accelerated convergence compared to traditional diffusive methods.
- Establishes connections between Min-Sum Splitting and other accelerated methods (lifted Markov chains, multi-step gradient).
- Paper is clear and well organized, making arguments easy to follow.
- Assumes substantial background in distributed optimization and graph theory, which may hinder non-experts.
- Analysis is restricted to the consensus problem; generalization to other optimization problems is unclear.
- Lacks a comprehensive empirical comparison with other state-of-the-art consensus methods.
- Novel algorithm and acceleration results argue for acceptance.
- Theoretical and numerical evidence supporting the claims strengthens the paper’s case.
- Connections to other accelerated methods provide additional insights motivating acceptance.
- Specialized assumptions and limited scope may restrict applicability, arguing against acceptance.
- Limited benchmark comparisons and technical complexity make it hard to assess practical impact and accessibility.
