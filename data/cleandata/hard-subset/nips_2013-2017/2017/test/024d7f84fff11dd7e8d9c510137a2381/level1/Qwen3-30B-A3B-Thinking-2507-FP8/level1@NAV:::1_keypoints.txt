- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting to apply Min-Sum message-passing for distributed consensus and achieve accelerated convergence.
- Shows ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge on graphs with cycles, while Min-Sum Splitting converges with subdiffusive rates matching shift-register methods.
- Establishes novel connections between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order convex optimization methods.
- Builds on prior results: [27] (Min-Sum failure on cyclic graphs) and [34] (splitting for Gaussian Belief Propagation).
- Provides the first rigorous convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting using a novel auxiliary-process proof technique.
- Theoretical development is technically sound with well-structured proofs.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Highly original by linking three distinct areas.
- Significant practical relevance by accelerating distributed consensus algorithms.
- Main weakness: limited empirical/numerical validation of the theoretical results.
- Additional weakness: insufficient exploration of sensitivity to parameter choices beyond the theoretical optimum.
- Review scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 4/5, Originality 5/5, Significance 4/5.
- Overall assessment: substantial theoretical contribution likely to inspire further work, but would be strengthened by experiments and deeper parameter analysis.
