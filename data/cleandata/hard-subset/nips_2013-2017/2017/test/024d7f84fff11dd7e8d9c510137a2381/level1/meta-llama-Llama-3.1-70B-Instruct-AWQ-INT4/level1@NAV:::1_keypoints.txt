- Paper proposes Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus that yields accelerated convergence.
- Main technical claim: Min-Sum Splitting achieves subdiffusive convergence rates matching shift-register methods, with rigorous asymptotic rate proof and convergence-time bound.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the algorithm and analysis easy to follow.
- Authors provide a thorough literature review of consensus algorithms and Min-Sum schemes, highlighting prior limitations.
- Analysis connects Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chain techniques and multi-step first-order methods, arguing a shared acceleration mechanism.
- Achieving subdiffusive rates requires choosing splitting parameters that depend on global graph information.
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of algorithm and analysis.
- Strength: rigorous convergence analysis including asymptotic rate and time bound.
- Strength: thoughtful exploration of links to other accelerated methods.
- Weakness: analysis restricted to quadratic objective functions; generalization is unclear.
- Weakness: no experimental results provided to validate theoretical findings.
- Weakness: connection to other accelerated methods is not fully explored and needs further investigation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should add experiments and deepen the exploration of method connections.
- Review rating: 8/10.
