- Applies Min-Sum to consensus and shows modified Min-Sum Splitting converges with accelerated, subdiffusive rates (O(D log(D/ε)) for cycles/tori), approaching optimal O(D log(1/ε)).  
- Four main contributions: links Min-Sum to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex methods; shows BP directionality tuning can accelerate; provides first convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting; designs a protocol improving prior Min-Sum consensus.  
- Novel proof technique: uses an auxiliary process to connect edge-based Min-Sum Splitting dynamics to node-based multi-step dynamics.  
- Theoretical strength: ties spectral graph properties to convergence; Theorem 1 shows parameter choice yields shift-register–level acceleration.  
- Major limitation: analysis is restricted to quadratic objectives (network averaging), unclear extension to non-quadratic consensus.  
- Practical drawback: parameter tuning (γ = 2/(1+√(1−ρ_W²))) requires global spectral knowledge, impractical in many distributed settings.  
- Empirical gap: lacks experimental comparisons with other accelerated methods and across graph topologies.  
- Implementation concerns: computational overhead and real-world deployment challenges are not addressed.  
- Underdeveloped connections: relation to distributed ADMM is mentioned but not fully explored, suggesting future work.  
- Overall assessment: strong and novel theoretical contribution offering a unified view of accelerated consensus; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to broaden scope and add empirical validation.
