- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, showing messages stay quadratic and reduce to a linear directed-edge update under proper parametrization.  
- Introduces an auxiliary node-based linear system with memory that exactly tracks the edge-based process.  
- Theorem 1 proves accelerated (square-root) improvement in spectral gap for symmetric W tuned with γ, yielding O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles/tori.  

- Connects Min-Sum Splitting conceptually to lifted nonreversible Markov chains (edge directionality) and to multi-step/shift-register accelerated methods (memory).  
- Provides the first explicit rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and equivalence to shift-register acceleration; improves on [27] by giving topology-agnostic rates.  
- Situates work relative to classical diffusive consensus, lifted-chain acceleration, and multi-step acceleration literature.  

- Strength: nontrivial theoretical contribution via the auxiliary node-based system exposing a 2n×2n K with analyzable spectral properties.  
- Strength: Theorem 1 rigorously establishes square-root speedup versus diffusion and provides exact spectral formula/constant estimates.  
- Strength: Conceptual unification linking BP-style message passing with known acceleration paradigms.  
- Strength: General-topology rates without relying on hard-to-analyze edge-space spectral gaps.  

- Weakness: Practicality — γ must be tuned using global ρW; no distributed estimation or robustness analysis for γ is provided.  
- Weakness: Scope limited to quadratic averaging on undirected graphs with symmetric W; extension to general φv or directed/time-varying graphs is unclear.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes specific initial conditions (R̂0=r̂0=0) and positivity conditions; initialization- and denominator-positivity issues need clarification.  
- Weakness: Relation to Chebyshev/second-order Richardson unclear; unclear if Min-Sum Splitting offers anything beyond re-deriving known polynomial acceleration in edge-space.  
- Weakness: No experiments are presented to validate finite-time behavior, constants, transients, or robustness to γ misspecification.  
- Weakness: Minor clarity and formatting issues; role of δ underplayed (main theorem sets δ=1 without justification).  

- Suggestion: Provide robustness analysis and plots for γ misspecification and discuss distributed estimation of ρW (e.g., power-method protocols).  
- Suggestion: Add experiments comparing Min-Sum Splitting to Metropolis, Chebyshev/shift-register, heavy-ball, and lifted-walk consensus on representative graphs.  
- Suggestion: Clarify assumptions ensuring equivalence between Algorithms 2 and 3 and the positivity of denominators; state initialization implications explicitly.  
- Suggestion: Discuss extensions to nonsymmetric/time-varying graphs and nonquadratic φv, noting which analysis parts break.  
- Suggestion: Make the Chebyshev/polynomial-acceleration connection explicit and relate to known optimality on paths/grids.  

- Evaluation: Solid theoretical development and a plausible spectral argument; missing empirical support and robustness analysis.  
- Clarity: Generally clear but needs polishing and more intuition; auxiliary-process insight is a highlight.  
- Originality: Novel rate analysis and auxiliary-node formulation for Min-Sum Splitting; accelerated rate itself known in other contexts.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — conceptually neat bridge between BP and accelerated consensus; would be stronger with empirical and robustness work.
