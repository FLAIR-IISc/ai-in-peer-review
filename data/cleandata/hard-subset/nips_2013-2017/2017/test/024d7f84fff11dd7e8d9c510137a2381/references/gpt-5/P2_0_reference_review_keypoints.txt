- Studies Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, specializing to quadratic averaging and showing edge-based linear message updates.  
- Constructs an exact node-based 2-step linear auxiliary system that tracks the edge dynamics.  
- With parameter choice δ = 1 and Γ = γW (W symmetric, W1 = 1, γ depends on spectral gap ρW), dynamics match shift-register/second-order methods.  
- Main theorem proves accelerated convergence ρK < ρW, giving O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles/tori (square-root improvement over diffusive O(D^2 log(1/ε))).  
- Draws conceptual links between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted non-reversible Markov chains, and multi-step first-order acceleration.  
- Builds on prior work that analyzed edge systems for regular graphs; novelty is general-graph guarantees via node-based auxiliary process.  
- Not proposing a new accelerated iteration per se, but showing Min-Sum Splitting recovers known accelerated behaviors and providing first rate theory for it.  
- Technical derivations reducing Min-Sum Splitting to linear quadratic updates and to a node-based 2-step system are sound and well motivated.  
- Main rate bound leverages known spectral analyses of shift-register/Chebyshev-like iterations and appears correctly mapped.  
- Key assumptions: δ = 1, Γ = γW with W symmetric and ρ(W − 11T/n) < 1, zero initialization of edge messages; γ depends on global spectral quantity ρW.  
- Analysis limited to quadratic consensus (averaging); these assumptions are reasonable but should be noted as limitations.  
- Theoretical contribution is complete for the considered setting, but proofs are deferred and some conditions (e.g., positivity) need clearer emphasis.  
- No experiments are provided; small-scale empirical validation would strengthen claims about constants and transients.  
- Paper organization is good but notation is dense; a small example (path/cycle) would aid intuition and presentation polish.  
- Originality: first convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus and a new node-based auxiliary proof technique.  
- Conceptual novelty: explicit parameter mapping linking BP/Min-Sum to lifted chains and momentum/shift-register methods.  
- Practical significance depends on ability to tune γ without global spectral knowledge, and robustness to asynchrony, noise, and non-quadratic objectives.  
- Pros: rigorous rate result with square-root improvement, simpler node-based spectral analysis, and unifying conceptual links.  
- Cons: restricted to quadratic consensus and synchronous updates, requires global spectral info to set γ, no empirical validation, and acceleration recovers known methods rather than a new algorithm.  
- Questions for authors: can γ be adapted/decentralized, sensitivity to γ misspecification, extensions to strongly convex objectives or ADMM-like variants, asynchronous/randomized schedules, and finite-time bounds with explicit constants?  
- Suggestions: add empirical comparisons (Metropolis, Chebyshev, ADMM) on cycles/grids/RGGs, clarify initialization/robustness, tighten presentation and references, and include a small illustrative example.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept—the paper is theoretically solid and novel in linking Min-Sum Splitting to accelerated consensus despite limited scope and practical considerations.
