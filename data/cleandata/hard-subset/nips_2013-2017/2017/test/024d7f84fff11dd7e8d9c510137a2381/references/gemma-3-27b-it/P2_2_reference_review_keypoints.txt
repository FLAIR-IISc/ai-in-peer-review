- Paper shows Min-Sum Splitting achieves accelerated consensus with O(D log(D/ε)) rates (square-root improvement) for cycle and toroidal graphs, approaching optimal O(D log(1/ε)).  
- Authors connect Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods, highlighting novelty.  
- Analysis uses an auxiliary linear process to simplify and rigorously justify convergence proofs.  
- Novel contribution: first rigorous accelerated convergence analysis for the relatively unexplored Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.  
- Theoretical rigor: proofs and mathematical formulation are sound and well-justified.  
- Significance: the square-root improvement is important for large-scale distributed optimization and multi-agent systems.  
- Paper is generally well-structured with a clear introduction and positioning relative to prior work.  
- Strong placement in literature and clear comparison to previous approaches (e.g., [27]).  
- Technical sections (Sections 5–6) are dense and hard to follow without deep message-passing/graph theory background.  
- No empirical validation; lack of simulations leaves practical performance and sensitivity questions open.  
- Parameter tuning (δ and Γ) is crucial but practical selection and sensitivity are not discussed.  
- Background material is somewhat repetitive and could be condensed to improve focus.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- For acceptance: novel theoretical result, rigorous analysis, and insightful connections justify publication.  
- Against acceptance: missing experiments, challenging technical exposition, and unclear practical parameter implications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify technical sections, discuss parameter tuning, and ideally add a small simulation.
