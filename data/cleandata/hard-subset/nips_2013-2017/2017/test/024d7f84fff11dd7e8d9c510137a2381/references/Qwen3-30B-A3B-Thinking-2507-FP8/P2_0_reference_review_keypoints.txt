- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus and proves accelerated convergence O(D log(D/ε)) vs classical diffusive O(D² log(1/ε)).
- Provides first rigorous convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and establishes Theorem 1 via an auxiliary linear process.
- Connects Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chain techniques (directional message exchange) and multi-step accelerated methods (memory terms).
- General theoretical analysis applicable to any graph topology, improving prior work that focused on cycles/tori.
- Shows proper tuning of splitting parameters yields a square-root improvement in convergence rate.
- Clearly distinguishes ordinary Min-Sum (which can fail on cycles) from Min-Sum Splitting and builds on prior literature.
- Mathematical presentation is precise with well-defined algorithms and consistent notation.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with strong literature context and explanations.
- Significance: results have important implications for large-scale distributed systems and bridge two lines of research.
- Weakness: optimal parameter tuning requires global information (ρW), challenging for fully distributed settings.
- Weakness: analysis restricted to quadratic (averaging) problems; generalization to non-quadratic cases remains open.
- Weakness: no empirical validation and limited discussion of communication or computational overhead.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with high recommendation for acceptance.
