- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a novel distributed algorithm for consensus (network averaging) on arbitrary graphs.  
- Extends Min-Sum with tunable splitting parameters to achieve accelerated convergence on loopy graphs.  
- Interprets the algorithm as a lifted process on directed edges and as a multi-step method with memory on nodes.  
- Proves a square-root improvement in convergence time for cycles and tori: from Θ(D² log(1/ε)) to Θ(D log(D/ε)).  
- Establishes the first connection between Min-Sum schemes, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step accelerated methods.  
- Provides the first convergence rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and a new proof technique using an auxiliary linear process.  
- Theoretical contribution is technically sound, rigorous, and mathematically thorough.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and positions itself appropriately within related literature.  
- Results are significant for distributed optimization, approaching optimal rates for certain graph topologies.  
- Lacks empirical validation or simulations to corroborate theoretical claims.  
- Requires global information (spectral gap) to tune splitting parameters, limiting practical distributed implementation.  
- Focuses only on the quadratic (network averaging) case, reducing generality to other objective functions.  
- Does not analyze or discuss communication complexity compared to baseline methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add experiments and discuss practical/global-information issues.
