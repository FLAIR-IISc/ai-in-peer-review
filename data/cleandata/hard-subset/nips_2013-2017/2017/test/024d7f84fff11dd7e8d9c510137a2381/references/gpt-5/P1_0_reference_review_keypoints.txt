- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for quadratic consensus and gives the first convergence-rate guarantees for averaging.

- Specializes Min-Sum Splitting to equality-constrained quadratic consensus, yielding a linear message-passing scheme on directed edges parameterized by (δ, Γ).

- Constructs an auxiliary node-based two-step linear process with memory that tracks Min-Sum Splitting, allowing spectral analysis via an n×n matrix instead of a 2m×2m edge system.

- Main theorem: with δ=1 and Γ=γW (W symmetric, W1=1, ρ(W−11^T/n)<1) and γ=2/(1+√(1−ρ_W^2)), the algorithm converges to the average with accelerated “subdiffusive” rate equal to shift-register/second-order Chebyshev.

- Achieves a square-root improvement over diffusive methods; on cycles and tori gives O(D log(D/ε)) iterations vs. Θ(D^2 log(1/ε)).

- Conceptual connections: operates on a lifted directed-edge space (like lifted non-reversible Markov chains) and manifests as a two-step memory method akin to shift-register/heavy-ball/Nesterov acceleration.

- Relates to prior work: addresses divergence of ordinary Min-Sum, extends Ruozzi & Tatikonda by providing rates, and connects to consensus propagation and known accelerated consensus literature.

- Strength: first rigorous convergence rate for Min-Sum Splitting matching best-known two-step accelerated rates.

- Strength: elegant reduction from edge dynamics to node dynamics with memory and an explicit spectral link to W and Γ.

- Strength: applies broadly to any symmetric W with a spectral gap and gives concrete iteration complexity for graphs with geometry.

- Strength: conceptually unifies lifted chains and multi-step optimization methods within the BP/min-sum literature.

- Weakness: scope limited to quadratic consensus; unclear if results extend to general strongly convex or constrained non-quadratic objectives.

- Weakness: requires global tuning (γ depends on ρ_W); no discussion of distributed estimation of ρ_W or robustness to misspecification.

- Weakness: implementation framed on directed edges (2m messages); no clear node-only implementation preserving equivalence and communication costs.

- Weakness: key assumptions/initialization (δ=1, Γ=γW, zero initial messages) and positivity condition in Proposition 1 are not proven to hold under the prescribed parameters.

- Weakness: lacks empirical validation and finite-time comparisons to Chebyshev-accelerated gossip, Nesterov/shift-register, ADMM/EXTRA/Exact Diffusion.

- Weakness: presentation issues—heavy notation, equation/numbering mistakes, distracting footnotes and typos.

- Quality: derivations are plausible and match classical second-order rates, but key proofs and the positivity condition are deferred and need strengthening.

- Completeness: asymptotic rates are complete for the quadratic consensus setting, but finite-time constant bounds and robustness to parameter errors are missing.

- Clarity: generally readable for experts but notation-heavy; recommends tightening Sections 5–6, fixing numbering, and adding a schematic linking Algorithms 1–3.

- Originality: novelty lies in demonstrating Min-Sum Splitting attains accelerated asymptotics and introducing the auxiliary node-process analytical tool, a meaningful contribution.

- Significance: clarifies when and how Splitting yields acceleration and could influence message-passing algorithm design if extended beyond quadratics.

- Questions/suggestions: guarantee the positivity condition under proposed parameters; provide a node-only implementation; analyze sensitivity to γ misspecification; add empirical comparisons; discuss distributed estimation/choice of W and ρ_W; consider extensions to strongly convex objectives and constraints.

- Minor comments: fix equation/algorithm cross-references, clean up footnotes/punctuation, and ensure reference formatting completeness.

- Decision: Weak accept — solid theoretical contribution that needs improved clarity and empirical illustration.
