- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting (MSS) for distributed consensus in the quadratic averaging case, reducing MSS to a linear message-passing scheme on directed edges.  
- Introduces an auxiliary node-based “multi-step” linear process of dimension 2n that exactly tracks MSS under suitable initialization.  
- Shows a specific parametrization (δ=1, Γ=γW with γ tuned to spectrum of W) yields accelerated (subdiffusive) convergence matching shift-register/Chebyshev rates.  
- Derives an improved asymptotic contraction factor ρK and O(D log(D/ε)) iterations on cycles and tori.  
- Draws conceptual links between MSS, lifted non-reversible Markov chains (edge messages), and multi-step first-order methods (node memory).  
- Proof technique uses the auxiliary node process to simplify spectral analysis versus edge-based approaches.  
- Ordinary Min-Sum convergence on loopy graphs is delicate; prior sufficient conditions don’t cover consensus constraints.  
- MSS generalizes Min-Sum and previously improved convergence domains, but prior work lacked convergence rates for MSS.  
- Classical symmetric linear iterations are diffusive with Θ(D^2 log(1/ε)) mixing; known accelerations (lifting, multi-step) achieve O(D log(1/ε)).  
- This paper generalizes prior Min-Sum consensus analyses beyond d-regular graphs and provides explicit accelerated rates for MSS.  
- Connections are noted to ADMM/lifting and accelerated first-order methods in existing literature.  
- Strength: first convergence rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting in the consensus setting.  
- Strength: technical novelty of the auxiliary node-based linear system reducing analysis from 2m to 2n dimensions.  
- Strength: sharp, elegant acceleration result recovering shift-register contraction factor and improved bounds on geometric graphs.  
- Strength: conceptual unification of message-passing, lifting, and multi-step methods; broad relevance to distributed optimization and ML.  
- Weakness: scope limited to quadratic averaging, synchronous updates, undirected fixed graphs; no extension to general convex objectives or directed/time-varying/asynchronous settings.  
- Weakness: acceleration requires global spectral tuning (γ depending on ρ(W−11^T/n)) with no distributed estimation or robustness analysis.  
- Weakness: no experiments to validate rates, constants, robustness, or comparisons to competing methods (Metropolis, Chebyshev, Nesterov, ADMM, lifted chains).  
- Weakness: dense exposition, minor notation/indexing issues, and unclear role of φvw and positivity/convexity assumptions under chosen initialization.  
- Weakness: novelty mainly in mapping/analysis within MSS rather than a fundamentally new accelerated consensus algorithm.  
- Weakness: results are asymptotic with limited explicit finite-time constants or comparisons to best polynomial/Chebyshev bounds.  
- Quality: technical claims appear sound; main gaps are missing experiments and limited discussion of robustness and implementability.  
- Clarity: generally clear but compact; needs notation fixes, equation numbering corrections, and a brief proof sketch for Theorem 1.  
- Originality: moderate-to-strong — new rates and auxiliary-process analysis, though acceleration matches known shift-register results.  
- Significance: good — advances understanding of BP-like splitting for near-optimal consensus rates and introduces extensible analysis tools.  
- Suggestions: add experiments across graph families and baselines, analyze robustness to γ mis-tuning, discuss distributed spectral estimation/adaptive tuning.  
- Suggestions: clarify assumptions ensuring convex beliefs/positivity, fix typos/equation numbers, and include a brief proof sketch of key results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first rate analysis for MSS, principled spectral acceleration, and conceptual bridge between BP, lifting, and multi-step methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, lack of experiments, reliance on global spectral tuning, and limited algorithmic novelty beyond MSS mapping.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: high in consensus/acceleration background; moderate on specific MSS literature pending supplement proofs.
