This paper investigates the convergence rates of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm applied to the consensus problem. The authors establish convergence and demonstrate a square-root improvement in convergence time compared to diffusive methods, approaching the optimal rate for certain graph topologies. The work builds upon prior research on Min-Sum algorithms [27, 34, 35] and relates the proposed method to both lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order optimization techniques [13, 37].

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a clear theoretical analysis establishing the convergence rates. The proofs, while not included in the main body, are mentioned to be available in the supplementary material. The authors carefully present the algorithm and its connection to existing work. The introduction of the auxiliary process is a clever technique for analyzing the convergence behavior. However, the reliance on the supplementary material for proofs hinders immediate verification of the core claims. The assumption that 0 < bv < 1 for each v ∈ V seems restrictive and its impact on generality should be discussed. The connection to shift-register methods is well-established.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, although dense. The notation is consistent, but the numerous references and algorithmic descriptions require careful reading. The motivation for the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm and its relation to existing methods is clearly explained. The introduction effectively summarizes the contributions and places the work in context. However, some sections, especially those detailing the theoretical analysis, could benefit from more intuitive explanations. Algorithm 2 and 3 feel somewhat abruptly introduced, without sufficient motivation for their construction before their presentation.

**Originality:** The main novelty lies in establishing the convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting specifically applied to the consensus problem. While Min-Sum Splitting itself is not new [34], the analysis and the demonstrated convergence speedup are original contributions. The connection to both lifted Markov chains and accelerated optimization methods is also insightful and provides a broader perspective on the algorithm's properties. The paper carefully differentiates its work from previous results [27], addressing open questions and improving upon existing convergence bounds.

**Significance:** The results are significant because they provide a theoretical understanding of the performance of Min-Sum Splitting and demonstrate its potential for faster convergence in distributed consensus problems. This has implications for various applications, including sensor networks, distributed optimization, and machine learning. The demonstrated convergence rate approaching the optimal bound suggests the algorithm is a valuable addition to the toolbox of distributed algorithms. The connection to different fields (lifted Markov chains, accelerated optimization) suggests broader applicability of the Min-Sum Splitting approach.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound analysis of Min-Sum Splitting convergence.
*   Demonstrated convergence speedup compared to diffusive methods.
*   Connection to related techniques (lifted Markov chains, accelerated optimization).
*   Clear presentation of the algorithm and its motivation.
*   Addresses open questions from previous work [27].

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Reliance on supplementary material for proofs.
*   The restrictive assumption of 0 < bv < 1 could limit generality.
*   Density of the writing style may hinder accessibility.
*   Algorithms 2 and 3 are introduced somewhat abruptly.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of distributed consensus algorithms. The technical analysis is rigorous, the results are meaningful, and the connections to related work are insightful. Despite minor issues with clarity and reliance on supplementary material, the paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend acceptance.
