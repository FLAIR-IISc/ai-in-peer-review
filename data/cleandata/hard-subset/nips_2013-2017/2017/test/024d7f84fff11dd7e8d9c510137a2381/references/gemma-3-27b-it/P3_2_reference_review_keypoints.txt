- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution proving convergence and near-optimal square-root acceleration for Min-Sum Splitting; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem & Contribution: Proves convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on any graph and links the method to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods.  
- Novelty: Identifies “splitting” as a mechanism introducing directionality that accelerates consensus, a previously unexplored connection.  
- Theoretical Rigor: Convergence proofs and rate derivations are solid and well-motivated, using a clever auxiliary process.  
- Generality: Results hold for any graph topology, improving over prior topology-restricted approaches.  
- Literature Connections: Effectively unifies perspectives from message passing, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated optimization.  
- Writing & Organization: Clear, logically structured, and well-motivated introduction and exposition.  
- Significance: Demonstrates a substantial square-root improvement over diffusive methods, approaching optimal performance.  
- Practicality Concern: Implementation complexity and overhead of determining optimal splitting parameters are not fully addressed.  
- Parameter Sensitivity: Performance depends on spectral gap and tuning γ; robustness to spectral gap estimation errors is unclear.  
- Empirical Evaluation: Limited or no simulations; simple experiments on representative graphs (cycle, grid) would strengthen the paper.  
- Repetitiveness: Introductory sections 1–4 contain overlapping descriptions of prior work and could be more concise.  
- Suggested Clarification: Discuss practical methods to estimate the spectral gap and implications for choosing γ.  
- Decision Rationale: Accept with minor revisions to address practical considerations, parameter sensitivity, repetition, and adding simple simulations.
