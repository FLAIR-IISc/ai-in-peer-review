- Summary: Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for quadratic consensus, showing it induces a linear edge-based message process and, via reparametrization, an auxiliary two-state node process with accelerated convergence.
- Main result: With δ = 1 and Γ = γW (γ tuned from spectral gap of W), Min-Sum Splitting achieves an accelerated rate ρK < ρW, matching shift-register (second-order consensus) methods.
- Convergence time: Achieves O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori, improving over diffusive O(D^2 log(1/ε)) and approaching O(D log(1/ε)) optima.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on classical Min-Sum/GBP convergence results and fills a gap by providing rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus.
- Connections: Links Min-Sum Splitting to lifted nonreversible Markov chains and multi-step acceleration (heavy-ball, Nesterov, Chebyshev/shift-register, ADMM).
- Prior art: Generalizes and extends prior "Consensus Propagation" analyses from regular graphs to arbitrary graphs with explicit node-space spectral bounds.
- Technical tool: Auxiliary node-space formulation (Algorithm 3) is the key to translating edge dynamics into analyzable multi-step node updates.
- Theorem precision: Main theorem precisely characterizes asymptotic rate ρK = sqrt((1 − √(1 − ρW^2))/(1 + √(1 − ρW^2))) and conditions for convergence with specific initialization.
- Proofs and soundness: Claims are well-supported by analysis, with proofs deferred to the supplement; edge→node translation appears technically sound.
- Limitations (scope): Focus limited to quadratic consensus; synchronous, undirected, fixed graphs only.
- Limitations (practical): Tuning requires global spectral information (ρ(W − 11T/n)); no distributed tuning/adaptation proposed.
- Missing evaluation: No experiments to evaluate constants, robustness, communication cost, or sensitivity to γ mis-specification.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-organized with clear algorithms, but has minor notation/typo issues and sparse intuition on positivity/initialization conditions.
- Suggestion on exposition: A compact proof sketch for Theorem 1 and more intuition for positivity conditions and R̂0 = r̂0 = 0 would help.
- Originality: Novelty lies in showing Min-Sum Splitting recovers shift-register rates, the auxiliary node analysis, and conceptual links across communities.
- Significance: Theoretical contribution strengthens ties between BP-style message passing and accelerated optimization; technique may generalize beyond consensus.
- Practical significance caveat: Practical impact limited by need for global spectral info and lack of empirical validation.
- Strengths: First rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting with accelerated bounds, conceptual unification, general graph applicability, and square-root improvement over diffusive methods.
- Weaknesses/questions: Restricted problem class, global tuning requirement, no asynchrony/time-variation/directed graph treatment, and unclear communication/state overhead vs. other methods.
- Suggested experiments: Compare to Metropolis, optimal symmetric W, Chebyshev/shift-register, Nesterov, and ADMM on cycles/grids/random graphs; test γ sensitivity and wall-clock metrics.
- Suggested extensions: Provide guidance or adaptive schemes for γ when ρW unknown, and discuss/aspect extensions to asynchronous, time-varying, or directed graphs.
- Acceptance arguments: Solid, novel theoretical result bridging BP and accelerated consensus with general-topology rates and potential to inspire broader work.
- Rejection arguments: Theory-only, depends on global spectral info, and narrow focus on quadratic consensus limits immediate applicability.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — interesting theoretical advance but would benefit from experiments and tuning/robustness discussion.
