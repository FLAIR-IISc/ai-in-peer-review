- Specialization: Adapts Min-Sum Splitting to quadratic consensus, yielding a linear message-passing protocol on directed edges.  
- Analytical tool: Introduces an auxiliary node-based two-step linear system that exactly tracks the algorithm and enables spectral analysis.  
- Accelerated rate: Proves accelerated “subdiffusive” convergence with specific tuning (δ=1, Γ=γW), giving O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles/tori versus Θ(D^2 log(1/ε)).  
- Conceptual links: Connects Min-Sum Splitting to lifted nonreversible Markov chains and multi-step/shift-register acceleration methods.  
- Novelty: First rate analysis of Min-Sum Splitting for consensus and first to expose concrete connections to lifted walks and shift-register acceleration.  
- Key technical step: Edge-to-node equivalence (2n-dimensional linear system) is central and makes spectral arguments tractable.  
- Rate expression: Main theorem gives ρK = sqrt((1−√(1−ρW^2))/(1+√(1−ρW^2))) matching known second-order acceleration behavior.  
- Required assumptions: Symmetric W with W1=1, ρ(W−11^T/n)<1, synchronous updates, quadratic objectives, and knowledge (or bound) of ρW to set γ.  
- Technical clarity issues: Algorithm 2’s output appears malformed (missing ratio); positivity/denominator conditions in Proposition 1 need explicit statements/proofs.  
- Notation and typesetting: Heavy notation and minor typos/missing fractions; a notational table and clearer separation of edge/node quantities recommended.  
- Practical limitations: Tuning requires global spectral information; no empirical validation provided to demonstrate robustness or practical gains.  
- Suggested extensions: Extend to general strongly convex objectives, directed/asynchronous settings, and discuss adaptive γ selection or robust parameter choices.  
- Empirics request: Recommend small-scale experiments on cycles/grids/random graphs comparing to Metropolis, Chebyshev, and ADMM/EXTRA.  
- Complexity discussion: Ask for per-iteration message/compute complexity, memory cost, and initialization overhead comparisons with other methods.  
- Questions for authors: Can γ be chosen adaptively without losing acceleration? Does the auxiliary-process extend beyond quadratics or equality constraints? How sensitive is convergence to model mismatch/asynchrony?  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid, novel, and significant for message-passing and distributed optimization; accept (borderline-to-strong) pending clarity fixes and, ideally, empirical validation.
