- Paper shows Min-Sum Splitting for quadratic consensus reduces to linear directed-edge message-passing and an equivalent node-level linear process with memory, achieving accelerated convergence with tuned splitting parameters.  
- Main theoretical result: with δ=1 and Γ=γW (γ chosen from ρ(W−11^T/n)), the method attains Chebyshev/shift-register-like accelerated rate O(ρ_K^t) and O(D log(D/ε)) iterations on cycles/tori.  
- Derivations appear careful: quadratic closure of messages (Prop.1), rigorous edge-to-node recursion connection (Prop.2), and spectral analysis leveraging known shift-register results (Thm.1).  
- Scope is limited to synchronous, static, undirected graphs and quadratic averaging objectives; γ requires global spectral information.  
- Missing: no empirical validation, no discussion of finite-time constants or robustness, and limited discussion on feasibility/positivity of Γ entries.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized but could better explain encoding of equality constraints in φ_vw and simplify heavy algorithm notation (role of K(δ,Γ)).  
- Noted that W may have negative entries; this assumption should be stated earlier and reconciled with message updates.  
- Originality: novelty lies in showing Min-Sum Splitting recovers known accelerated rates, introducing a tractable auxiliary node system, and linking to lifted non-reversible walks.  
- Practical significance is limited by global tuning requirements and lack of implementation guidance or experiments.  
- Strength: provides first rate guarantees for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus and insightful connections to lifted chains and multi-step methods.  
- Limitation: restricted to quadratic consensus; no treatment of general convex φ_v or hard-constraint variants.  
- Limitation: requires global knowledge (ρ_W) to set γ and offers no decentralized or robust tuning strategy.  
- Limitation: no empirical comparison to Chebyshev acceleration, Nesterov-like schemes, EXTRA/Exact Diffusion, or distributed ADMM to assess constants and pre-asymptotic behavior.  
- Concern: initialization and positivity condition in Prop.1 need proof sketch and practical guidance to ensure 1+ΣΓ R̂ stays positive for all t.  
- Concern: choice of W assumed symmetric with W1=1; joint design of W and γ and the use of potentially negative weights are not discussed.  
- Concern: communication/computation overhead from edge-based parameter maintenance lacks complexity comparison with node-based accelerated schemes.  
- Requests: clarify explicit φ_vw encoding of equality constraints used to obtain linear-quadratic updates.  
- Requests: under R̂0=r̂0=0, provide guarantees/lemma for positivity condition in Prop.1.  
- Requests: discuss whether conservative γ can be set from upper bounds on ρ_W (degrees, diameter) and provide robust γ ranges.  
- Requests: discuss extension of K(δ,Γ) approach to strongly convex smooth objectives beyond averaging (e.g., distributed least squares).  
- Requests: provide empirical comparison to Chebyshev, EXTRA/Exact Diffusion, and ADMM on grids/geometric graphs.  
- Minor: better cite foundational Chebyshev semi-iterative works (Golub & Varga 1961; Young 1971) and relate to SDP-optimized vs. Metropolis-Hastings W choices.  
- Suggestion: add a short numerical section validating rates and constants on cycles and grids.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution bridging BP/Splitting and accelerated consensus, but narrow in scope, needs clarification on constraints/positivity and ideally empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying constraint encoding and positivity, and preferably adding brief experiments.
