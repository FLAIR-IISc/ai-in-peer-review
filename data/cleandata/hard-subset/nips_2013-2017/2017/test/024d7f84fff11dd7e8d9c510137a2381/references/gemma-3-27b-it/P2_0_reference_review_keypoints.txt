- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, showing square-root improvement in convergence time with proper parametrization.
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence rates (e.g., O(D log(D/ε)) for cycles/tori) and links to shift-register methods.
- Novelly connects Min-Sum Splitting with lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods, unifying techniques.
- Well-motivated problem relevant to distributed optimization and machine learning.
- Generally clear and well-structured presentation, with a solid connection to prior work.
- Develops an auxiliary process that enables a rigorous and novel convergence analysis.
- Paper is technically dense, especially around the auxiliary process and Theorem 1 proof.
- Lacks empirical evaluation to demonstrate practical benefits on real or simulated graphs.
- Parameter tuning conditions (γ and W) are buried in theory and not user-friendly for practitioners.
- Background sections, particularly on accelerated algorithms, are somewhat repetitive and could be condensed.
- Quality rated 9/10 for technical soundness and analysis.
- Clarity rated 7/10 due to depth and technical density hindering accessibility.
- Originality rated 9/10 for the combination of techniques and analysis approach.
- Significance rated 8/10, though impact is limited by missing experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantees, novel technique connections, addresses a fundamental problem, high-quality analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of experiments, high technical density, unclear practical parameter guidance.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical contributions; recommend accept with encouragement to add empirical results and improve clarity.
