- Introduces theoretical analysis of Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, proving accelerated convergence with proper splitting parameters.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting operates on a lifted (directed-edge) space and exhibits acceleration analogous to lifted Markov chains and multi-step methods.  
- Proves square-root improvement: O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori vs Θ(D² log(1/ε)) for diffusive methods.  
- Builds on prior Min-Sum and network averaging work and extends [27] by establishing convergence rates.  
- Quality: rigorous proofs, novel auxiliary-node process technique, and well-structured presentation.  
- Clarity: well-written, consistent notation, clear contributions, with supplementary material for additional proofs.  
- Originality: first theoretical convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and a novel link between two acceleration paradigms.  
- Significance: meaningful improvement for consensus and potential implications for broader distributed optimization.  
- Limitation: analysis restricted to the quadratic (network averaging) case, limiting generality.  
- Lacks empirical validation; no experiments to support theoretical rates or practical impact.  
- Connection to lifted Markov chains is somewhat superficial and lacks detailed parameter mapping.  
- Does not discuss computational complexity or implementation overhead of Min-Sum Splitting.  
- For acceptance: first theoretical analysis, novel connections, square-root improvement, and rigorous presentation.  
- Against acceptance: narrow scope, no experiments, superficial lifting connection, missing complexity discussion, limited to consensus.  
- Suggestion: add empirical comparisons on cycles and tori against diffusive and accelerated methods.  
- Suggestion: discuss how the approach might extend to non-quadratic objectives and associated challenges.  
- Suggestion: include a brief computational complexity/implementation-overhead analysis.  
- Suggestion: strengthen the lifted Markov chain connection by explicitly comparing directionality/lifting parameters.  
- Suggestion: ensure supplementary material contains full detailed proofs and additional illustrative examples.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions addressing experiments and broader applicability.  
- Final recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
