- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, proving convergence on any graph and showing properly tuned parameters give accelerated (square-root) improvement over diffusive methods.
- Introduces an auxiliary linear/message-passing process to track algorithm evolution, simplifying analysis compared to prior work.
- Draws connections between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated first-order convex optimization methods.
- Extends prior Min-Sum work by providing analytical convergence-rate guarantees for all graph topologies, surpassing [27].
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence proofs and a novel accelerated convergence rate.
- Generality: results hold for any graph topology, increasing applicability.
- Novel analytical technique: the auxiliary message-passing scheme is a clever and effective tool.
- Good integration with related literature and clear presentation of core ideas despite dense notation.
- Weakness: paper is mathematically dense and may be hard to access without more intuitive explanations.
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation; simulations on representative graphs would strengthen practical impact.
- Weakness: optimal splitting parameter depends on knowing the graph spectral gap, which is costly to compute and not fully discussed.
- Weakness: background material in Sections 3–4 is somewhat repetitive and could be condensed.
- For acceptance: significant theoretical advance, clever technique, and clear placement within broader literature justify acceptance.
- Against acceptance: high mathematical density, missing experiments, and unaddressed practical parameter-selection issues temper impact.
- Overall: strong paper with important theoretical contributions; recommended Accept (Strongly).
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
