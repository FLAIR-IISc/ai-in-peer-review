- Overall assessment: Significant theoretical contribution showing a square-root speedup for Min-Sum Splitting on cycles/tori, insightful connections to lifted chains and accelerated methods, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

- Summary: Rigorous analysis of Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, proving subdiffusive O(D log(D/ε)) convergence on cycles/tori using tuned splitting and an auxiliary linear process, linking to shift-register methods.

- Strength — Strong theoretical results: Proves subdiffusive convergence rate, a notable improvement over diffusive methods for geometrical graphs.

- Strength — Novel analytical technique: Introduces an auxiliary linear process that simplifies analysis of message-passing on edges.

- Strength — Connections to existing work: Bridges Min-Sum Splitting with lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order optimization, providing broader context.

- Strength — Clear motivation and setup: Well-motivated introduction and clearly defined problem statement.

- Strength — Organization: Logical flow from Min-Sum basics to consensus results and related-work connections.

- Weakness — Technical density: Dense math and notation may limit accessibility to non-experts in distributed optimization and graph theory.

- Weakness — Limited empirical validation: Nearly all claims are theoretical; small-scale experiments could strengthen practical credibility.

- Weakness — Practical parameter selection: Lacks discussion on computing optimal splitting parameters (γW) and the cost of spectral radius estimation for large graphs.

- Weakness — Repetitive background: Sections 3 and 4 repeat existing work on diffusive and accelerated methods and could be streamlined.
