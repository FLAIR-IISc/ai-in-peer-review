- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for consensus, proving parameter-tuned acceleration with square-root improvement on cycles and tori and introducing an auxiliary linear process to simplify analysis.
- Novelty: first theoretical convergence-rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting.
- Strong theoretical results: rigorous proof of square-root speedup and links to shift-register and Nesterov-style acceleration.
- Technical ingenuity: auxiliary process reduces complex dynamics to a tractable linear system.
- Good connections to literature: relates to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order methods with a detailed review.
- Significance: faster consensus rates have practical impact for distributed systems and ML.
- Complexity: paper is dense and mathematically heavy, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Limited empirical validation: no experiments to demonstrate practical acceleration on simple graphs.
- Parameter tuning dependence: acceleration relies on specific δ and Γ choices; robustness to tuning deviations is not discussed.
- Overly detailed introduction: literature review is lengthy and could be streamlined.
- Quality: technically sound with supporting proofs in supplementary material.
- Clarity: generally well-written but notation density and complexity hinder intuition; more examples would help.
- Originality: original analysis and connections for Min-Sum Splitting.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel connections, clever analytical technique, and relevance to important problems.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical results, high technical complexity, and unaddressed tuning sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: add small-scale empirical evaluation, discuss robustness to parameter choice, and streamline the introduction.
