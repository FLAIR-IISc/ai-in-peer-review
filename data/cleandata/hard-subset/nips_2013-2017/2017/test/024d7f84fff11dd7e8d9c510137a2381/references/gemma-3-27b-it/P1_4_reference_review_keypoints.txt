- Overall: Significant theoretical contribution showing accelerated Min-Sum splitting for consensus; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main result: Proves convergence rates with a provable square-root improvement over classical diffusive methods via tuned splitting parameters.  
- Conceptual link: Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum splitting, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated first-order optimization.  
- Analytical tool: Introduces an auxiliary linear process that simplifies tracking the algorithm’s evolution and convergence analysis.  
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous, well-motivated mathematical analysis and complete theoretical treatment.  
- Presentation: Generally well-written and organized, though technically dense.  
- Literature: Thorough related-work coverage and situates the contribution within existing research.  
- Weakness — empirical: Lacks empirical validation; small-scale simulations would strengthen practical claims.  
- Weakness — accessibility: Heavy mathematical notation makes the paper challenging for non-specialist readers; more intuition needed.  
- Weakness — dependency: Results build substantially on prior work [27]; clearer discussion of differences/advancements is needed.  
- Weakness — generality: Analysis is restricted to a specific quadratic objective; unclear how results extend to other objectives.  
- For acceptance: Strong theoretical novelty, insightful cross-field connections, and solid presentation support publication.  
- Against acceptance: Limited experiments, dense exposition, and insufficient differentiation from [27] temper the contribution.  
- Questions for authors: Request practical implications/simulations, extensions beyond quadratics, and detailed comparison to [27].
