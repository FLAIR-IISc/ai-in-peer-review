This paper investigates the convergence rate of the min-sum splitting method for the average consensus problem. Overall, the paper is well-written, but the improvement in the result appears to be minimal. The following are detailed comments:

(1) The paper states that the convergence rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as the complete graph, where the convergence time is independent of the dimension of the problem. However, for complete graphs, the linear convergence rate is 0, as all nodes converge to the average in a single step. Furthermore, complete graphs are too specific to be representative. Therefore, the authors should clarify which general category of graphs exhibits a convergence complexity that does not depend on the dimension (number of nodes).

(2) The literature review in this paragraph should include the work "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control" by Olshevsky, and its convergence rate should be accurately reported (see comment 8). The reference in question has achieved a competitive, if not better, bound compared to the result presented in this submission.

(3) At the top of page 2, the authors should cite important references on consensus optimization, including "On the Linear Convergence of the ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization" by Shi, Ling, Kun, Wu, and Yin, and "Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks" by Scaman, Bach, Bubeck, Lee, and Massoulié. Additionally, the authors should report the state-of-the-art algorithms for consensus optimization and their corresponding (linear) convergence rates.

(4) When discussing lifted graphs and Markov chains, the authors have overlooked a related paper, "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM" by Franca and Bento.

(5) The content of the last paragraph on page 5 is a well-known fact and should be attributed to "Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links" by Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew. Furthermore, the connection between those variants and Heavy ball/Nesterov/Polyak is established in the field.

(6) The authors have ignored several important references on consensus optimization, including "Extra: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization" by Shi, Ling, Wu, and Yin, and "Fast distributed gradient methods" by Jakovetic, Xavier, and Moura.

(7) Proposition 3 appears to be trivial and is a supplementary contribution.

(8) The convergence rate achieved in this paper, D log(D/eps), does not seem to offer a significant improvement over the rate D log(1/eps) achieved in "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control" (see comment 2). In the worst-case scenario (applicable to all graphs), where D~n, the bound is even worse than that achieved in "Linear Time Average Consensus…".

(9) The paper "Linear Time Average Consensus…" improves the bound using Nesterov's acceleration. This reviewer suspects that the proposed "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" is essentially Nesterov's acceleration applied to the min-sum algorithm. While this is acceptable, the analysis is focused on consensus, which reduces to analyzing a linear system, and is not particularly challenging. As a result, the contribution of the paper is unclear in this context.

(10) The minor improvement may stem from a careful handling of the spectral gap of graphs. However, the worst-case bound remains O(n), as O(n) = O(D) for the set of all graphs with n nodes.

(11) Line 243 on page 6 features a simple graph, but the authors are using directed edges, which is confusing.

(12) There is a typo at line 220 on page 6: "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian".
