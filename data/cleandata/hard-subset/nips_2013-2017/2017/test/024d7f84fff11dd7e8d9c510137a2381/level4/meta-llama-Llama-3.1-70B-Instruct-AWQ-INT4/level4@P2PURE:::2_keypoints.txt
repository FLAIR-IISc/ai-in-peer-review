1. Claim that convergence time is dimension-independent and optimal for "good" graphs is unclear — complete graphs are trivial and authors must define what graphs qualify as "good" and when complexity is node-count independent.

2. Missing citation: Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus..." should be included and its convergence rate correctly reported, as it can match or outperform the paper's bound.

3. Missing important consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.); the paper should cite them and report state-of-the-art linear convergence rates for decentralized optimization.

4. Overlooked related work on lifted Markov chains: Franca and Bento's "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM" should be cited.

5. The last paragraph on page 5 states a well-known fact and should reference Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew; connections to Heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak are established and must be acknowledged.

6. Additional neglected references include "Extra" (Shi et al.) and "Fast distributed gradient methods" (Jakovetic et al.), which are important for consensus optimization context.

7. Proposition 3 is trivial and only provides a supplementary, not substantive, contribution.

8. The claimed rate D log(D/ε) is not a significant improvement over D log(1/ε) from Olshevsky; in worst-case graphs (D ~ n) the bound can be worse.

9. The proposed "auxiliary message-passing" appears to be essentially Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; since analysis reduces to a linear system for consensus, the novelty/contribution is unclear.

10. Any minor gain likely comes from tighter handling of the spectral gap, but the worst-case complexity remains O(n) because O(n)=O(D) across all n-node graphs.

11. Notational/graph model inconsistency: the graph is stated as simple but directed edges are used on line 243, which is confusing.
