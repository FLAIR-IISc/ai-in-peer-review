

This paper introduces an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, for solving the distributed consensus problem. The writing is clear and well-structured, with contributions effectively contextualized within the current literature. As a non-expert in this specific area, I find the results novel and represent a significant qualitative advance, particularly through the establishment of a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain methods.

A critical clarification is needed regarding the notation in the presentation. On page 4, the authors state: "splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms." However, $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are defined as real numbers on the preceding page. Since the splitting process requires integer counts of terms (e.g., splitting into 2.3 terms is impossible), this inconsistency renders the description ambiguous. The authors should explicitly clarify whether $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are intended as integer counts (and if so, how they relate to their earlier definitions as real numbers) or if a typographical error has occurred in the notation. This point must be resolved for the method to be properly understood and implemented.
