- Novel application: applies Min-Sum Splitting to consensus in distributed optimization.  
- Convergence result: modified Min-Sum (Splitting) converges where ordinary Min-Sum does not.  
- Rates: establishes convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Acceleration: achieves subdiffusive accelerated rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Improvement: shows a square-root (√) improvement in convergence time over diffusive methods.  
- Contextualization: paper is well-written and situates contributions within prior work.  
- Literature connection: links Min-Sum algorithms to lifted Markov chain techniques and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Technical soundness: detailed, rigorous analysis including tuning-parameter choices.  
- Comparisons: useful comparisons with other accelerated methods, supporting claims.  
- Minor issue: clarification needed on “splitting each term φ_vw into Γ_vw terms and φ_v into δ terms” since δ and Γ_vw were introduced as real numbers.  
- Recommendation: significant, novel contribution with no major objections; arguments strongly favor acceptance.
