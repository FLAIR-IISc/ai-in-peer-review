This work addresses the distributed consensus problem by applying min-sum splitting, an accelerated version of the min-sum algorithm. The manuscript is clearly written, and its contributions are well-contextualized within the current state of the art. Acknowledging that this is not my primary area of expertise, the results nonetheless appear to be novel and represent a substantial conceptual advancement. A particularly noteworthy contribution is the original connection established between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

One aspect of the presentation requires clarification. On page 4, the authors describe the equivalent objective function used to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, stating they are "...splitting each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,...". The intended meaning of this phrase is difficult to interpret, as the variables $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$ were previously defined as real numbers.
