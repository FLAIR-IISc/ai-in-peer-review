- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence rates for quadratic objectives.
- Main contributions: (i) novel analysis via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Paper is well written and readable, but contains several typos and grammatical errors throughout (author provided a list of specific line corrections).
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.
- Algorithms lack clarity: some quantities (e.g., R_vw, r_vw, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are not defined or initialized, and the variables corresponding to the auxiliary linear process in Algorithm 3 are not clearly identified.
- Missing or incomplete references on (sub)gradient methods for consensus and decentralized optimization; reviewer suggests adding classic and recent works (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Shi 2015).
- Reviewer suggests citing Polyak (heavy-ball) and Nesterov where relevant to multi-step/acceleration discussions.
- Requests more discussion on practicality: when to use this method vs. other distributed consensus optimization algorithms.
- Asks authors to state limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.
- Requests intuition for why and how the auxiliary linear process aids Min-Sum Splitting and the analysis.
- Asks whether the framework can be extended beyond quadratic consensus problems to more general objectives common in machine learning.
- Requests clearer justification of the relevance and importance of this approach for the Machine Learning community.
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting with novel proof technique and connections to established methods, but lacking practical discussion, intuition, and ML-oriented motivation.
