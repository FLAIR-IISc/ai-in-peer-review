- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting protocol for consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to derive convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) novel analysis via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly stated and the manuscript is generally readable.  
- Proofs for Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear sound upon inspection.  
- Paper contains numerous typographical and grammatical errors that need correction (many specific line-by-line suggestions provided).  
- Notation issues: several algorithm variables (e.g., R_{vw}, r_{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are undefined or uninitialized, and the auxiliary process is not explicitly mapped to Algorithm 3 variables.  
- Terminology query: lines 220–221 may have incorrect terms—likely "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" rather than "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers."  
- Missing key references on distributed (sub)gradient consensus methods (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015) and missing citations for original literature, Polyak, and Nesterov.  
- Recommend a discussion on practicality: when to prefer this method over other distributed consensus methods.  
- Ask authors to clarify inherent limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.  
- Request more intuition on the role and use of the auxiliary process in the analysis.  
- Question whether the framework can generalize beyond quadratic consensus problems to broader ML-relevant consensus tasks.  
- Authors should explicitly state the significance and potential impact of this approach for the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall: a novel theoretical contribution with promising links to known methods, but strengthened by clearer notation, proofreading, additional references, and discussions of practicality and ML relevance.
