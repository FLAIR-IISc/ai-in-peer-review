1) Overall: paper reads well but claimed improvement over existing results is not compelling.

2) Clarify complete-graph claim: identify which graph families make complexity independent of number of nodes and define what "good" graphs mean.

3) Include and accurately report Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus…” and compare its convergence rate to this work.

4) Cite Shi et al. and Scaman et al., and summarize state-of-the-art consensus optimization algorithms and their linear convergence rates.

5) Include Franca and Bento’s “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM” when discussing lifted graphs and Markov chains.

6) The last paragraph of page 5 states a long-known fact—cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew; the connection to Heavy-ball/Nesterov acceleration is already well known.

7) Add missing consensus-optimization references: EXTRA (Shi et al.) and Fast distributed gradient methods (Jakovetic et al.).

8) Proposition 3 is trivial and appears to be a supplementary, not substantive, contribution.

9) The achieved rate D log(D/ε) offers little improvement over prior D log(1/ε) bounds (Olshevsky); in worst-case graphs (D ≍ n) it is worse.

10) The auxiliary message-passing scheme may effectively be Nesterov acceleration on min-sum; since average consensus reduces to a linear system, the novelty is unclear.

11) Any improvement likely comes from a finer spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case complexity remains O(n)=O(D) across n-node graphs.

12) Clarify modeling: the graph is stated as simple but uses directed edges—explain this choice and implications (line 243, p.6).

13) Fix typo: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian” (line 220, p.6).

14) After rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with authors’ response but evaluation score remains unchanged.
