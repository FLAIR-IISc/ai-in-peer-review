- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to derive convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) a novel auxiliary-process-based proof technique, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence rates, (iii) links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Writing is clear and proofs for Propositions 1 & 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct, but the manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (enumerated by the reviewer).  
- Three algorithms are given but some quantities and initializations are unclear (roles of R_{vw}, r_{vw}, initialization of ĤR^0 and Ĥr^0), and the mapping to the auxiliary linear process in Algorithm 3 should be specified.  
- Several relevant references are missing; reviewer suggests adding classical distributed/subgradient and accelerated optimization works (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; incremental/stochastic subgradient literature; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov).  
- Reviewer requests clarification on practicality and suitability for real distributed optimization scenarios, and a discussion of the method’s limitations.  
- Suggests adding intuition for the auxiliary process, exploring extensions to more general consensus problems used in ML, and articulating the significance for the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with interesting ideas and convergence results, but needs clearer algorithmic exposition, additional references, and more discussion on practicality and ML relevance.
