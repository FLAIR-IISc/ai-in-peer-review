- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and proving convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) novel auxiliary-process proof technique, (ii) Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence rates, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex methods.  
- The auxiliary linear process is the key analytical tool used to track algorithm evolution.  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly stated; writing is generally clear but contains multiple typos and grammatical errors.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Reviewer lists several specific typos and grammar corrections (multiple line references provided).  
- Potential terminological confusion: lines 220–221 may intend “Lagrangian” and “Lagrange multipliers” rather than “Laplacian” and “Laplace multipliers.”  
- Algorithm clarity issues: quantities R{vw}, r{vw} undefined; \hat{R}^0 and \hat{r}^0 appear uninitialized; need to state which variables correspond to the auxiliary process in Algorithm 3.  
- Missing references on (sub)gradient and consensus optimization (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015).  
- Missing citations for acceleration literature (Polyak’s Heavy-ball and Nesterov).  
- Suggest authors comment on practicality, applications, and comparisons to other distributed consensus methods.  
- Recommend discussing limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and providing intuition for the auxiliary process.  
- Results are limited to quadratic objectives; reviewer asks to explore extending the framework to broader consensus problems in ML.  
- Authors should explicitly state the relevance and interest of this approach to the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with a novel proof technique and useful connections, but needs clearer algorithms, additional references, typo fixes, and more discussion of practicality and extensions.
