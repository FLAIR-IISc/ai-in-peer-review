- Paper presents an accelerated Min-Sum message-passing variant for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to analyze convergence for quadratic objectives.
- Main contribution (i): Introduces a novel proof technique via an auxiliary process to analyze Min-Sum Splitting.
- Main contribution (ii): Proposes a Min-Sum consensus protocol with improved convergence over existing results.
- Main contribution (iii): Demonstrates connections between the method and lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Overall writing is clear and accessible, but contains multiple typos and grammatical errors.
- The proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear to be correct.
- Typographical errors are numerous and specified at exact line numbers (e.g., “theirs neighbors” → “their neighbors”, “double-stochastic” → “doubly-stochastic”, etc.).
- Clarification requested on terminology: verify whether “Lagrangian” and “Lagrange multipliers” are more appropriate than “Laplacian” and “Laplace multipliers”.
- Algorithms need clarification: definitions of R_vw and r_vw in Algorithm 2 and initial values \hat{R}^0 and \hat{r}^0 are undefined.
- Crucial mapping missing: authors should explicitly map variables of the auxiliary linear process to those used in Algorithm 3.
- Missing citations: add foundational references on (sub)gradient methods for consensus (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Shi 2015).
- Vague phrase “The original literature…” should be replaced with specific citations.
- Missing references to optimization acceleration literature, specifically Polyak’s heavy-ball work and Nesterov’s accelerated methods.
- Suggestion: discuss practical implications and conditions under which this approach outperforms other distributed consensus algorithms.
- Suggestion: state inherent limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.
- Suggestion: provide intuition for using the auxiliary process to improve clarity and impact.
- Suggestion: discuss possible generalization beyond quadratic objectives to more complex ML consensus problems.
- Suggestion: clearly articulate the relevance and significance of the work for the Machine Learning community.
- Summary verdict: theoretically sound and interesting results, but needs more discussion of practical considerations, intuition, broader relevance, and fixes for clarity and missing references.
