This paper investigates the distributed consensus problem using min-sum splitting, an accelerated version of the min-sum algorithm. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clearly situating the work within the existing literature. While I am not a specialist in this area, the results appear novel and represent a significant improvement. Specifically, the paper establishes a new and insightful link between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain methods. 

However, a detail in the presentation requires clarification. On page 4, the authors describe the objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm leading to the min-sum splitting scheme, stating: “…splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms…” This statement is unclear, as both $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ were previously defined on the preceding page as real numbers, and it is not apparent what “splitting into” these quantities signifies.
