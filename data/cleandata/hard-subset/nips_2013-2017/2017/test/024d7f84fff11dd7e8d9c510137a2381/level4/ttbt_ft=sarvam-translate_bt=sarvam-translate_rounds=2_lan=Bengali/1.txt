This paper uses a fast type of Min-Max algorithm, called Min-Max Splitting, and applies it to distributed consensus problems. The paper is well-written, and the author's contributions are clearly placed in the context of the current state of the subject. As far as I know (although I am not an expert in this field), the results are new and represent a qualitative improvement. In particular, this paper presents a new connection between the Min-Max algorithm and lifted Markov chain techniques.

One point in the presentation is not elaborated upon, which is unclear. On page 4, while describing the equivalent objective function optimized by the Min-Max algorithm, the authors write: "...each term $\phi{vw}$ is divided into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms and each term $\phiiv$ is divided into $\delta$ terms,..." However, it is not clear what this means, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$, as mentioned on the previous page, are real numbers.
