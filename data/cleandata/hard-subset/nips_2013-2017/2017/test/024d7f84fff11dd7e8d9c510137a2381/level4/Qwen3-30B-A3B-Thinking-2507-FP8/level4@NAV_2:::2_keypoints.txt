1. Paper studies convergence rate of min-sum splitting for average consensus and claims subdiffusive rate O(D log(D/ε)), matching shift-register methods.  
2. Shows connections between min-sum splitting, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order optimization methods.  
3. Introduces an auxiliary message-passing scheme as a novel proof technique linking convergence to a spectral gap.  
4. Strength: first theoretical convergence-rate analysis for min-sum splitting (prior work only showed convergence).  
5. Strength: insightful, well-supported connections to two different literatures (Markov lifting and multi-step acceleration).  
6. Strength: auxiliary message-passing scheme is a novel technical contribution enabling analysis.  
7. Main weakness: improvement over existing work is unclear and appears incremental compared to known results.  
8. The claimed O(D log(D/ε)) rate is similar to Olshevsky’s O(D log(1/ε)) and the paper fails to properly contextualize this.  
9. Paper omits key references, notably Olshevsky’s "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs."  
10. Additional missing citations include Scaman et al. (optimal distributed optimization), Shi et al. (linear ADMM convergence), and Franca & Bento (Markov chain lifting and ADMM).  
11. Discussion of complete graphs is misleading — complete graphs converge in one step, so they are not representative.  
12. The paper does not clarify which graph classes (if any) have convergence times independent of node count.  
13. The auxiliary scheme’s relation to Nesterov/heavy-ball acceleration is not distinguished from existing methods and may be standard.  
14. The paper’s main novelty seems to be spectral-gap analysis for this consensus instance rather than a new acceleration mechanism.  
15. Claim that min-sum splitting is competitive with recent accelerated methods is not convincingly supported, especially since worst-case bounds remain O(n).  
16. Specific technical error: incorrectly states diffusive rate is optimal for complete graphs without noting single-step convergence.  
17. Specific omission: fails to cite several state-of-the-art consensus/optimization works (Scaman, Shi, Rabbat, Jakovetic, etc.).  
18. Proposition 3 is questioned as a trivial extension rather than a substantive contribution.  
19. The claimed rate improvement is not significant in the worst case where D ~ n, preserving O(n) complexity.  
20. Auxiliary message-passing may be just an application of Nesterov acceleration to min-sum and not novel.  
21. Presentation issues: use of directed edges for simple graphs is confusing.  
22. Minor error: typo on line 220 (Laplacian → Lagrangian).  
23. Overall assessment: technical analysis is sound but contribution is marginal due to lack of novelty and missing contextualization/references.
