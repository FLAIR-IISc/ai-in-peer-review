- Overall: Paper is reasonably well written but delivers only marginal improvement in results.

(1) Claim about optimality on complete graphs is misleading; complete graphs converge in one step and are not representative—need clarification which broader class of graphs yields complexity independent of n and what constitutes "good" graphs.

(2) Missing citation: Olshevsky’s "Linear Time Average Consensus..." should be included and its competitive (often superior) convergence bound reported.

(3) Missing key consensus-optimization references (Shi et al. ADMM paper; Scaman et al. optimal algorithms) and an overview of state-of-the-art algorithms with their linear rates is required.

(4) Overlooked related work on lifted graphs/Markov chains: Franca and Bento’s "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM."

(5) Last paragraph of page 5 states a well-established result; cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew and acknowledge known connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak methods.

(6) Several important consensus-optimization papers are omitted (e.g., EXTRA by Shi et al., and "Fast distributed gradient methods" by Jakovetic et al.).

(7) Proposition 3 is trivial and a minor/supplementary contribution.

(8) The achieved rate D log(D/ε) is not a meaningful improvement over D log(1/ε) from Olshevsky’s work and is worse in the worst case where D ~ n.

(9) The proposed auxiliary message-passing likely amounts to applying Nesterov acceleration to min-sum; since consensus reduces to a linear system, the analysis is not particularly challenging and the novel contribution is unclear.

(10) Any minor gains likely come from refined spectral-gap analysis, but the worst-case bound remains O(n)=O(D) over all n-node graphs.

(11) Line 243: inconsistency—graph called "simple" yet directed edges are used, which is confusing.

(12) Typo on line 220: "Laplacian" should read "Lagrangian."

After rebuttal: Reviewer satisfied with clarifications but retains the same evaluation score.
