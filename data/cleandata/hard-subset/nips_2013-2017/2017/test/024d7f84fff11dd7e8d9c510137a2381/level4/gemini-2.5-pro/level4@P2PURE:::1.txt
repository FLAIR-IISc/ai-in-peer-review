This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, known as min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is very well-written, and its contribution is clearly situated within the context of the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert on this topic), the results are novel and represent a qualitative advance. The novel connection the authors draw between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques is particularly noteworthy.

There is one detail in the presentation that requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function for the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state they are "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,...". The meaning of this is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are introduced on the preceding page as real numbers, not integers that can specify a number of terms.
