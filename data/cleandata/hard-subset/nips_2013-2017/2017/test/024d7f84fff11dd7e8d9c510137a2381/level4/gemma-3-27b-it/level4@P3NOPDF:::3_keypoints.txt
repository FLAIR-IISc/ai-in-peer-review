- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to establish convergence for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) new auxiliary-process-based proof technique, (ii) Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible; proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Several minor typos and grammatical errors throughout (e.g., "theirs neighbors"→"their neighbors", "double-stochastic"→"doubly-stochastic", "can be casted as"→"can be cast as").  
- Algorithm descriptions lack clarity: unspecified quantities R_vw and r_vw in Algorithm 2 and unclear initialization of ĤR^0 and Ĥr^0.  
- The correspondence between variables in Algorithm 3 and the auxiliary linear process is not explicitly indicated.  
- Reference list is incomplete; recommend adding Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis (1989), Srinivasan et al. (2009), Shi et al. (2015), and citing Polyak (Heavy-ball) and Nesterov.  
- Clarify whether "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" were intended instead of "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers" (Lines 220–221).  
- Request more discussion on practical applicability: scenarios where the method outperforms established distributed consensus methods.  
- Ask authors to state inherent limitations of the proposed Min-Sum Splitting method.  
- Request intuitive explanation for using the auxiliary process to analyze Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Ask whether the framework can be generalized beyond quadratic objectives to more general ML consensus problems.  
- Request explicit articulation of the work’s relevance and importance to the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that would benefit from clarifications on practicality, intuition, and ML relevance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
