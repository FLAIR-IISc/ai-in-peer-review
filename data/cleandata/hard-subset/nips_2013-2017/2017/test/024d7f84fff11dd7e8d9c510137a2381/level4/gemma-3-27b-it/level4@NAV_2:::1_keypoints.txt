- Applies min-sum splitting to distributed consensus and shows a novel connection to lifted Markov chains.  
- Paper is very well written and situates the contribution clearly in the literature.  
- Results are novel and constitute a qualitative advance for consensus algorithms.  
- Provides rigorous analysis and proofs establishing accelerated convergence rates.  
- Connects Min-Sum splitting to shift-register/multi-step methods, explaining acceleration mechanisms.  
- Convergence improvements are significant, especially for graphs with geometric structure.  
- Minor ambiguity: description of "splitting each term into Γ_vw and δ terms" is unclear since Γ_vw and δ are introduced as real numbers.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, clarity, originality, and potential high impact on distributed optimization.  
- Weakness: wording ambiguity about the splitting process that needs clarification.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance despite the minor presentation issue.
