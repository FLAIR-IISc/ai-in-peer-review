- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for distributed averaging and shows a reparameterized splitting version converges where ordinary Min-Sum can fail on loopy graphs.
- Core technical contribution: an auxiliary node-based linear dynamical system that tracks edge-based Min-Sum Splitting, enabling spectral analysis.
- With δ=1 and Γ=γW (γ tuned to W’s spectral gap), the method attains accelerated, subdiffusive rates matching shift-register/Chebyshev approaches.
- Achieves convergence time O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori, a square-root improvement over diffusive methods, and near O(D log(1/ε)) Nesterov-type rates.
- Conceptual connections drawn between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted nonreversible Markov chains, and multi-step first-order methods.
- Builds on prior Min-Sum/reparameterization work (Ruozzi & Tatikonda; Moallemi & Van Roy).
- Connects to lifted Markov chains and multi-step/shift-register accelerations (Chen–Lovász–Pak; Jung–Shah–Shin; Golub–Varga; Xiao & Boyd).
- Relates to distributed optimization literature including ADMM-based consensus and optimal first-order distributed methods (Boyd et al.; Wei & Ozdaglar; Scaman et al.).
- Strength: provides a clear, unified perspective linking BP/Min-Sum Splitting to lifting and multi-step methods.
- Strength: auxiliary node-based reduction simplifies analysis and is technically clean and reusable.
- Strength: gives concrete accelerated rates with explicit tuning and spectral characterization.
- Strength: improves diffusive consensus on geometric graphs with spectral-grounded analysis.
- Weakness: clarify which "graphs with good expansion" yield iteration counts independent of n (expanders, complete graphs, etc.).
- Weakness: must prominently compare to Olshevsky’s linear-time consensus (O(D log(1/ε))) and position assumptions/tuning/robustness.
- Weakness: cite and contrast decentralized ADMM (Shi et al.) and optimal distributed optimization (Scaman et al.), and state when linear convergence holds (strong convexity).
- Weakness: add citation to Franca & Bento on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM when discussing lifting.
- Weakness: acknowledge prior observations that consensus variants are second-order (heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak/Rabbat et al.) and clarify novelty in the mapping.
- Weakness: include EXTRA (Shi et al.) and fast distributed gradient methods (Jakovetić et al.) in related work.
- Weakness: delineate what mapping results are technically novel versus straightforward linear-algebra reformulations.
- Weakness: carefully compare O(D log(D/ε)) vs O(D log(1/ε)), discuss worst-case scaling (D ≍ n), assumptions, tuning knowledge, and constants.
- Weakness: state whether splitting-induced acceleration is equivalent to known momentum schemes and what is new from BP/splitting perspective.
- Weakness: discuss how global tuning parameter γ (depends on ρ(W)) can be estimated distributedly and robustness to misspecification.
- Weakness: clarify directed-edge implementation on an undirected graph early to avoid confusion.
- Weakness: fix typos (e.g., “Laplacian” vs “Lagrangian”) and proofread.
- Evaluation: technically sound and careful for linear/quadratic consensus; some contributions are structural more than deep.
- Evaluation: generally clear and organized but needs notational fixes and more citations/comparisons.
- Evaluation: originality moderate — fresh perspective but matches known shift-register rates and relies on linear-system analysis.
- Evaluation: significance limited-to-moderate — does not beat state-of-the-art rates but offers a new lens for BP-style acceleration.
- Arguments for acceptance: instructive bridge between BP/Min-Sum Splitting, lifted chains, and multi-step acceleration; general convergence guarantees; reusable auxiliary analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: does not improve best-known rates, omits related work, novelty mainly a recasting, and relies on global spectral info without practical estimation discussion.
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed several concerns but reviewer’s overall assessment remains unchanged — useful conceptual contribution but not surpassing state-of-the-art and requires better positioning.
