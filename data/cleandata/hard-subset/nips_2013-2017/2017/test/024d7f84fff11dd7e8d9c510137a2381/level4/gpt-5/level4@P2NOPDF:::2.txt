This paper analyzes the convergence rate of a min-sum splitting method for average consensus. The paper is generally well written, but the improvement over prior results appears limited. Detailed comments follow.

1) The paper states: “This rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as the complete graph. In this case the convergence time … does not depend on the dimension of the problem …” For complete graphs, consensus is achieved in one iteration, so calling the linear rate “optimal” is uninformative, and complete graphs are too special to be representative. Please specify a general class of graphs for which the complexity is dimension-free (independent of the number of nodes). What precisely constitutes “good” graphs here?

2) The literature review in that paragraph should include Olshevsky, “Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control.” Its convergence rate should be stated accurately (see also comment 8). That reference attains a bound that is competitive with, or in some regimes better than, the result in this submission.

3) At the top of page 2 (consensus optimization), please cite:
- Shi, Ling, Wu, and Yin, “On the Linear Convergence of ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization.”
- Scaman, Bach, Bubeck, Lee, Massoulié, “Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks.”
Also summarize state-of-the-art methods for consensus optimization and their (linear) rates.

4) In the discussion of lifted graphs and Markov chains, please include Franca and Bento, “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM.”

5) The content of the last paragraph on page 5 is well known; see Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew, “Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links.” The connections to Heavy-ball, Nesterov, and Polyak accelerations are also well established.

6) Additional important references on consensus optimization are missing, e.g.:
- Shi, Ling, Wu, and Yin, “EXTRA: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization.”
- Jakovetic, Xavier, and Moura, “Fast distributed gradient methods.”

7) Proposition 3 appears to be a straightforward, auxiliary result with limited standalone value.

8) The rate D log(D/ε) reported here does not constitute a clear improvement over the D log(1/ε) bound in Olshevsky (comment 2). In the worst case (over all graphs), D ≍ n, making your bound weaker than that of “Linear Time Average Consensus …”.

9) Olshevsky’s improvement uses Nesterov acceleration. The proposed “auxiliary message-passing scheme” appears to be another instance of Nesterov-type acceleration applied to min-sum. That is reasonable, but for consensus—where the analysis reduces to a linear system—the theoretical development should not be difficult. In this light, the incremental contribution is unclear.

10) The slight improvement likely comes from a sharper treatment of the spectral gap. However, the worst-case bound over all n-node graphs remains O(n) since D = Θ(n) in that class.

11) Line 243, page 6: The graph is described as simple, yet directed edges are used; this is confusing. Please clarify the assumptions.

12) Typo, line 220, page 6: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian.”

After rebuttal:
I appreciate the authors’ responses and clarifications. However, my overall evaluation remains unchanged.
