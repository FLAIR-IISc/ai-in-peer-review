This research paper implements a quick version of the minimum-sum algorithm for the distributed consensus problem, known as the minimum-sum partition. The paper is very well-written, with contributions clearly referencing the state of the art in the subject. To my knowledge (although I am not an expert in the subject), the results are novel and constitute a qualitative leap. In particular, this paper presents a new connection between the minimum-sum algorithm and lifted Markov chain techniques.

There is a detail in the presentation that is not clear. On page 4, while describing the equivalent objective function reduced by the minimum-sum algorithm to obtain the minimum-sum partition scheme, the author writes: "...dividing each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,..." However, it is not clear what this means, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$, as presented on the previous page, are real numbers.
