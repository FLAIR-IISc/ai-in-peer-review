- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting protocol for distributed consensus using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) new proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum consensus protocol with improved convergence, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is clear and well written; proofs of Propositions 1 & 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (many specific corrections are listed by the reviewer).  
- Algorithm descriptions lack clarity: key quantities (e.g., R_{vw}, r_{vw}) are not explained and some variables (\hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) appear uninitialized; mapping to the auxiliary process in Algorithm 3 is not explicitly stated.  
- Missing several relevant references on consensus and accelerated methods (suggested: Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov, etc.).  
- Reviewer asks for discussion of practicality, limitations, intuition for the auxiliary process, extension beyond quadratic objectives, and why the approach matters for the ML community.  
- Summary judgment: interesting theoretical contribution with new analysis and connections, but lacking practical guidance, intuition, and ML relevance.  
- Strengths: novel proof technique, clear links to established methods, readable presentation.  
- Weaknesses: typos, insufficient explanation of algorithmic quantities, missing references, inadequate discussion of practicality and limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix typos, add references, clarify algorithm variables/initializations, and discuss practicality/limitations.
