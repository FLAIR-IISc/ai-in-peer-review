This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is very well-written, with its contributions clearly placed in the context of the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (although I am not an expert on this topic), the results are novel and constitute a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper presents a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

However, one detail in the presentation is unclear. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function for the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state that it involves "...splitting each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,...". This statement is confusing because $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$ were introduced on the previous page as real numbers, and it is not obvious how a term can be split into a non-integer number of new terms.
