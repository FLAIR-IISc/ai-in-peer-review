

This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, known as min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The authors clearly position their work within the current literature, demonstrating how it relates to prior research on distributed optimization, min-sum message passing, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated first-order methods. The paper establishes a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques, which appears to be the first such connection in the literature. The technical content is sound, with well-supported claims and a clear demonstration of how the proposed method achieves accelerated convergence rates for the consensus problem.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, with a logical flow that makes the technical content accessible. The authors effectively situate their contribution within the broader context of distributed optimization, referencing relevant prior work and clearly articulating the novelty of their approach.

The main weakness identified is a lack of clarity regarding the equivalent objective function construction. On page 4, the authors describe splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms and each $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms. However, since $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are introduced as real numbers (not integers) on the previous page, this description is confusing and needs clarification. The authors should explicitly explain how the splitting interpretation extends to real-valued parameters, as the current presentation suggests integer values.
