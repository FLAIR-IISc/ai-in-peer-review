- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing algorithm for consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions claimed: (i) a novel proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) improved convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on quadratic consensus, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Presentation and motivation are clear and accessible; proofs of Propositions 1 & 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- The manuscript contains multiple typographical and grammatical errors (many specific line-by-line corrections are listed by the reviewer).  
- Minor technical/clarity issues: clarify "Laplacian"/"Laplace multipliers" vs Lagrangian/Lagrange multipliers; Algorithm 2 defines R{vw}, r{vw} without explanation and \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0 lack initialization; Algorithm 3 should explicitly identify the auxiliary linear-process variables.  
- Missing citations: add Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis (1989), Sundhar Ram et al. (2009), Wei Shi et al. (2015) for subgradient consensus methods; cite original literature referenced at line 170; add Polyak (Heavy-ball) and Nesterov references.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss practical applicability versus other distributed consensus methods, limitations of Min-Sum Splitting, intuition for the auxiliary process, extension to non-quadratic (ML-relevant) problems, and explicit relevance to the machine learning community.  
- Overall assessment: an interesting theoretical contribution with novel analysis and connections to existing literature, but lacking discussion of practical utility, intuition, and ML relevance.
