1) Claim about “optimal for graphs with good expansion” is problematic: complete graphs converge in one step, so authors must specify which graph classes avoid node-dependence and define “good expansion.”

2) Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus…” is omitted and its convergence bounds must be reported and compared, as they may be competitive or superior.

3) Missing key consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.); paper should discuss state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear rates.

4) Overlooks relevant work on lifted graphs/Markov chain lifting, specifically Franca and Bento’s “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM.”

5) Last paragraph of p5 states a known fact; cite earlier work (e.g., Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew) and acknowledge established connections to momentum methods.

6) Literature review incomplete: omit important works like EXTRA (Shi et al.) and “Fast distributed gradient methods” (Jakovetic et al.).

7) Proposition 3 appears trivial and only a minor, supplementary contribution.

8) Main result D log(D/ε) offers little improvement over prior D log(1/ε) bounds and can be worse when D = Θ(n).

9) The auxiliary message-passing scheme likely amounts to Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; novelty is unclear given linear-system analysis is straightforward.

10) Any improvement may stem from tighter spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case remains O(n) (D = O(n)), so no fundamental advance.

11) Notational inconsistency: paper claims the graph is simple but uses directed-edge notation—needs clarification.

12) Typo on p6 line 220: “Laplacian” should read “Lagrangian.”

13) After rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ response but keeps the original evaluation unchanged.
