This paper studies a faster variant of the min-sum algorithm—min-sum splitting—for the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is clearly written and situates the contribution well relative to prior work. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not a specialist in this exact area), the results are new and represent a qualitative advance. Notably, the paper establishes a novel link between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov-chain techniques.

One point in the presentation is unclear. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective optimized by min-sum to realize the splitting scheme, the text states: “…splitting each term phi_vw into Gamma_vw terms, and each term phi_v into delta terms…”. Since delta and Gamma_vw were introduced on the previous page as real-valued parameters, it is not obvious how to interpret “splitting into Gamma_vw or delta terms” under this generality. Clarification is needed on whether these are intended to be positive integers or whether “splitting” refers to a reparameterization that permits arbitrary real weights.
