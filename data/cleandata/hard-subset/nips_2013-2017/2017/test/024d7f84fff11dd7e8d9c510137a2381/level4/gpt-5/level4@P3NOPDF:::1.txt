This paper studies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm—min-sum splitting—for distributed consensus. The manuscript is clearly written, well organized, and situates the contribution convincingly within the existing literature on message passing, consensus, and acceleration techniques. To the best of my knowledge (I am not an expert on this specific subarea), the theoretical results and the conceptual bridge to lifted Markov chains are novel and represent a meaningful qualitative advance. The connection to lifted chains is particularly compelling, as it brings a principled perspective to understanding and tuning acceleration in min-sum–type procedures.

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation and motivation: accelerating consensus via structured message passing is timely and relevant.
- Conceptual novelty: the link between min-sum splitting and lifted Markov chains enriches both communities and suggests new design principles.
- Technical contribution: the proposed scheme and its analysis appear sound and provide concrete acceleration relative to standard min-sum/consensus dynamics.
- Exposition: the paper reads well overall and provides helpful context and intuition.

Main point needing clarification:
- On page 4, the description of the “equivalent objective function” minimized by min-sum to obtain the min-sum splitting scheme is ambiguous. The text states: “…splitting each term φ_vw into Γ_vw terms, and each term φ_v into δ terms…,” yet δ and Γ_vw were introduced as real-valued parameters. It is unclear how to interpret “splitting into Γ_vw (or δ) terms” when these quantities are not integers. 
  - If the intent is to construct an augmented factor graph with replicated factors, please clarify whether Γ_vw and δ are required to be integers in that construction, or whether one instead forms a weighted decomposition (e.g., φ_vw = ∑_t α_vw^(t) φ_vw with ∑_t α_vw^(t) = Γ_vw). 
  - If the splitting is purely conceptual and manifests only as weights in the message updates (rather than literal replication of factors), please rewrite this passage to avoid counting “terms” and instead state explicitly that the potentials are reweighted by Γ_vw and δ in the objective used by the min-sum updates.
  - In either case, it would help to give a precise definition of the augmented objective (including the indexing set of auxiliary terms/factors, their weights, and any normalization), and to show that the resulting min-sum updates are exactly those of the proposed splitting scheme. A small illustrative example on a 2- or 3-node graph would greatly improve clarity.

Minor suggestions:
- Provide a short paragraph with practical guidance on choosing the splitting parameters (δ and Γ_vw), ideally tied to interpretable graph properties or the lifted-chain analogy. Even a brief “rule of thumb” would be valuable.
- If available, include a discussion of computational overhead (per-iteration cost and memory) introduced by splitting compared to standard consensus/min-sum, to contextualize the acceleration gains.
- Consider adding a brief remark on robustness: how sensitive is performance to moderate misspecification of δ and Γ_vw?

Overall evaluation:
This is a strong and thoughtful contribution. The main results appear correct and relevant, and the manuscript is, for the most part, clearly written. Resolving the ambiguity around the “splitting into Γ_vw/δ terms” will remove a potential stumbling block for readers and strengthen the presentation. Subject to this clarification, I recommend acceptance.
