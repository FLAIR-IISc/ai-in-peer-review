1. Paper is generally well-written but offers only incremental improvement over existing results.  
2. Claim of optimality for “good expansion” graphs is unclear; complete graph is trivial and authors must define “good” graphs and specify classes where complexity doesn’t scale with node count.  
3. Missing key citation: Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus…” which achieves competitive or superior convergence bounds and must be discussed.  
4. Missing important consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.) and a thorough comparison of state-of-the-art linear convergence rates is needed.  
5. Omission of Franca and Bento’s “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM,” relevant to lifted graphs and Markov-chain connections.  
6. The connection in prior work (Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew) about consensus variants and links to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak is established and should be cited.  
7. Overlooks additional consensus-optimization contributions (Shi et al.’s EXTRA, Jakovetic et al.’s fast distributed gradient methods).  
8. Proposition 3 is a minor/trivial extension and adds little novelty.  
9. Achieved rate D log(D/ε) is not a substantial improvement over Olshevsky’s D log(1/ε) and can be worse in worst-case (D ~ n).  
10. The auxiliary message-passing scheme likely corresponds to applying Nesterov acceleration to min-sum; analysis for consensus reduces to a linear-system case, making contribution unclear.  
11. Any gains seem to hinge on careful spectral-gap handling, but worst-case complexity remains O(n) because O(n)=O(D) across all n-node graphs.  
12. Presentation issues: figure shows directed edges despite “simple” graph description (line 243), and typo “Laplacian” vs. “Lagrangian” (line 220).  
13. Post-rebuttal: authors’ response was satisfactory but reviewer’s overall score remains unchanged.
