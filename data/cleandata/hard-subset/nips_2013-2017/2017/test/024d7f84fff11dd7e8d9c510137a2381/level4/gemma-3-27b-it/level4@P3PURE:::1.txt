This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is very well written, with the contribution clearly positioned within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this area), the results are novel and represent a significant advance. In particular, the paper presents a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques. 

However, one detail lacks clarity in the presentation. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to yield the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state: “…splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms…” It is unclear what this splitting entails, as both $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$, as introduced on the previous page, are defined as real numbers.
