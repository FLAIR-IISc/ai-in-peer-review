- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus and proves convergence and accelerated rates for network averaging using a novel auxiliary linear process.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting can be tuned to achieve subdiffusive (square-root) acceleration matching shift-register methods.  
- Connects acceleration mechanism in Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and multi-step optimization methods (momentum/Nesterov).  

- Strength: First convergence rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and a novel auxiliary message-passing proof technique.  
- Strength: Demonstrates significant, competitive accelerated convergence results for consensus.  
- Strength: Provides valuable conceptual insight linking message directionality to momentum/non-reversibility.  

- Weakness: Analysis is limited to quadratic network averaging; unclear if results extend to general convex objectives used in ML.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks intuition behind the auxiliary process and a clear justification of relevance to the ML community.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues—algorithms not self-contained, key variables undefined/unanitialized, auxiliary-process variables not clearly labeled, and numerous typos.  
- Weakness: Incomplete literature review—missing foundational distributed optimization and acceleration references.  

- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution with sound proofs and insightful connections, but limited practical impact without broader generalization, clearer presentation, and discussion of ML relevance.  

- Requested revisions: clarify when Min-Sum Splitting is preferable to ADMM/accelerated gradient methods.  
- Requested revisions: explain obstacles to extending analysis beyond quadratics and whether linearity of message updates breaks down.  
- Requested revisions: address parameter tuning dependence on global spectral gap and suggest distributed estimation strategies.  

- Typo examples and wording fixes provided (e.g., "their neighbors", "doubly-stochastic", "can be cast as"); recommend citing Polyak and Nesterov.  
- Missing reference suggestions: Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis (1989), Shi et al. (EXTRA, 2015), plus original Polyak and Nesterov works.
