- Overall: Paper studies convergence of min-sum splitting for average consensus; well-written but claimed improvement is marginal.

1. The claim that the rate is "optimal for graphs with good expansion" is misleading—complete graphs converge in one step and are atypical; need to specify graph class for dimension-independent complexity and define "good expansion."

2. Missing citation: include Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus..." and contextualize its competitive or superior convergence bound.

3. Missing key consensus optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.); summarize state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear rates.

4. Omitted related work on lifted graphs/Markov chain lifting (Franca and Bento).

5. The well-known connection to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak and consensus with erasures should cite Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew and acknowledge established links.

6. Critical omissions: Shi et al.'s "Extra" and Jakovetic et al.'s "Fast distributed gradient methods."

7. Proposition 3 seems trivial and a minor contribution.

8. The claimed rate D·log(D/ε) offers little improvement over D·log(1/ε) from Olshevsky; for worst-case graphs (D ~ n) it is inferior.

9. Olshevsky achieves bounds via Nesterov acceleration; the authors' auxiliary message-passing likely rederives this, so contribution is unclear given consensus reduces to linear systems.

10. Any marginal gain likely comes from finer spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case complexity remains O(n)=O(D), matching known limits.

11. Notational/technical issue: line 243 uses directed edges for a simple (undirected) graph, causing confusion.

12. Typo: line 220 uses "Laplacian" but should read "Lagrangian."

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer satisfied with authors' response, but evaluation score unchanged.
