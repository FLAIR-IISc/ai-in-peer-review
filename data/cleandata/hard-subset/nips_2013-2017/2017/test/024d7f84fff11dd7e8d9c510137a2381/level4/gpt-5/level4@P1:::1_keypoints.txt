- Applies Min-Sum Splitting to distributed consensus; manuscript is clearly written.  
- Presents novel results and draws a new connection between Min-Sum methods and lifted Markov chains.  
- Work is technically sound and constitutes a qualitative advance.  
- Shows standard Min-Sum can fail to converge on graphs with cycles, while Splitting converges.  
- With appropriate tuning, Splitting achieves accelerated (subdiffusive) convergence rates comparable to shift-register methods.  
- Analysis via an auxiliary linear process on the nodes is insightful and broadly useful.  
- Links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods help unify related literature and could influence future algorithm design.  
- Exposition is generally very good but unclear when describing “splitting each term into Γ_vw/δ terms” because Γ_vw and δ are real, not integers.  
- Recommends clarifying that the “splitting” is an interpretive device (literal for positive integers) while the algorithm and analysis permit arbitrary real-valued parameters, or explaining reparameterization for non-integers.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with a minor presentation issue fixable by a short clarification.
