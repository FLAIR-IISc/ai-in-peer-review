

This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is exceptionally well written, with its contribution clearly situated within the current state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this specific area), the results are novel and represent a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper establishes a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

A key point remains unclear in the presentation. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,..." However, it is unclear what this refers to, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$, introduced on the previous page, are defined as real numbers.
