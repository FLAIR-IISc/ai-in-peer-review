**Accelerated Consensus via Min-Sum Splitting**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for solving the consensus problem in distributed optimization. The authors show that a modified version of the Min-Sum algorithm, known as Splitting, yields convergence to the problem solution, and they prove that a proper choice of the tuning parameters allows Min-Sum Splitting to achieve subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates, matching the rates obtained by shift-register methods.

**Strengths**

* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm and its convergence rates.
* The authors establish a connection between Min-Sum Splitting and lifted Markov chains techniques and multi-step first-order methods in convex optimization.
* The paper presents a new proof technique based on the introduction of an auxiliary process to track the evolution of the algorithm on the nodes.

**Weaknesses**

* The improvement of the result seems not impressive, and the convergence rate of D log(D/ε) does not seem to have a significant improvement on the rate D log(1/ε) achieved by other papers.
* The paper ignores some important references in the field of consensus optimization.
* The analysis is done for consensus, which boils down to analyzing a linear system, and the contribution of the paper becomes not clear given such a situation.

**Quality**

* The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.
* However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed literature review, including more recent papers in the field of consensus optimization.
* The writing is clear, but some sentences are long and convoluted, making them hard to follow.

**Clarity**
