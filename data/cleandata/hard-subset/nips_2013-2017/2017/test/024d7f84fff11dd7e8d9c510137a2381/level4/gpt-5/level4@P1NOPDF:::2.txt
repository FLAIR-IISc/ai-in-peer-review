This paper studies the convergence rate of a min-sum splitting method for the average consensus problem. Overall, the paper is readable, but the improvement over prior results appears limited. Detailed comments follow.

(1) The paper states: “This rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as the complete graph. In this case the convergence time … is independent of the dimension of the problem …”. For complete graphs, the method converges in one step, so the effective linear rate is 0; complete graphs are too special to be representative. Please clarify:
- For which general classes of graphs is the complexity independent of the dimension (number of nodes)?
- What precise notion of “good” graphs (e.g., expansion, spectral gap) are you using?

(2) The literature review should include “Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control” (Olshevsky). Its convergence rate should be reported accurately (see also comment 8). That paper achieves bounds that are competitive with, or better than, those obtained here.

(3) At the top of p.2, for consensus optimization, please cite:
- “On the Linear Convergence of the ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization” (Shi, Ling, Kun, Wu, Yin).
- “Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks” (Scaman, Bach, Bubeck, Lee, Massoulié).
Also summarize the state-of-the-art algorithms for consensus optimization and their (linear) convergence rates.

(4) In discussing lifted graphs and Markov chains, please cite “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM” (Franca and Bento), which is closely related.

(5) The content of the last paragraph on p.5 is well known; please cite “Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links” (Rabbat, Nowak, Bucklew). The connections between these variants and Heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak acceleration are also known in the field.

(6) Additional important references on consensus optimization are missing, e.g.:
- “EXTRA: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization” (Shi, Ling, Wu, Yin).
- “Fast distributed gradient methods” (Jakovetić, Xavier, Moura).

(7) Proposition 3 appears to be trivial and only a supplementary contribution.

(8) The rate D log(D/ε) reported here does not represent a significant improvement over the D log(1/ε) rate in Olshevsky (cited above). In the worst case over all graphs, where D ≈ n, your bound is even worse.

(9) Olshevsky improves the bound via Nesterov’s acceleration. The proposed “auxiliary message-passing scheme” appears to be Nesterov acceleration applied to the min-sum algorithm. This is reasonable, but for average consensus the analysis reduces to a linear system and should not be difficult. Given this, the paper’s contribution is unclear.

(10) Any small improvement seems to come from a careful treatment of the spectral gap. Nevertheless, the worst-case bound remains O(n) because, over all graphs with n nodes, D = Θ(n).

(11) Line 243, p.6: The graph is described as simple, yet directed edges are used. This is confusing; please clarify.

(12) Typo at line 220, p.6: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian”.
