- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process, and proves convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) novel analysis via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clear; the paper is generally well-written and the proofs of Propositions 1, 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- The manuscript contains numerous typos and grammar errors; the reviewer lists many precise line-by-line corrections to be made.  
- Terminology error: Lines 220–221 likely should read “Lagrangian” and “Lagrange multipliers” instead of “Laplacian” and “Laplace multipliers.”  
- Algorithms lack definitions/initializations: R{vw}, r{vw} and \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0 in Algorithm 2 are not defined/initialized.  
- The auxiliary linear process, central to the analysis, is not explicitly identified in Algorithm 3—authors should state which variables correspond to it.  
- Missing and weak citations: add (Sub)gradient/consensus refs (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015) and more specific references for Polyak (Heavy-ball) and Nesterov.  
- The paper should discuss practicality: when to use this method versus other distributed consensus algorithms, and what overheads it incurs.  
- Authors should elaborate limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and provide intuition for introducing the auxiliary process.  
- Results are limited to quadratic consensus problems; the reviewer urges exploring extensions to broader consensus objectives common in ML.  
- The authors need to clearly state and justify the relevance and interest of this approach to the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall: an interesting theoretical contribution with novel proof technique and useful connections, but lacking practical discussion, intuition, and clear ML relevance.
