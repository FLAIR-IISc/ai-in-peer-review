This paper presents an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, referred to as min-sum splitting, and applies it to the distributed consensus problem. The authors have written the paper in a clear and concise manner, effectively situating their contribution within the context of existing state-of-the-art research in the field. To the best of my knowledge, although I am not an expert in this area, the results appear to be novel and represent a significant qualitative advancement. A notable aspect of the paper is the establishment of a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

However, there is a detail in the presentation that requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to yield the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state: "...splitting each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,..." This explanation is unclear, as the symbols $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$, introduced on the previous page, represent real numbers. It would be beneficial for the authors to provide a more detailed explanation of this step to facilitate a deeper understanding of their approach.
