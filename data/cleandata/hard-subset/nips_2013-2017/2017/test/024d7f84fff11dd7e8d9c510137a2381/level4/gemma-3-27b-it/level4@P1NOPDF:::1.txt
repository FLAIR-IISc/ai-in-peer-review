This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly situating its contribution within the existing state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I admit I am not an expert in this area), the results appear novel and represent a significant advance. In particular, the paper establishes a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

One detail in the presentation requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms..." However, the meaning of this statement is unclear, as both $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$, as introduced on the preceding page, are defined as real numbers.
