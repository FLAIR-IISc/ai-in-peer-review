- Establishes first rigorous convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on arbitrary graph topologies, filling a key gap in prior work.  
- Shows square-root improvement over diffusive methods, achieving O(D log(D/ε)) rates on cycles and tori, matching best known accelerated methods.  
- Reveals a novel dual connection: Min-Sum Splitting acts like a lifted Markov chain on directed edges and like a multi-step first-order method via a memory term.  
- Uses an elegant auxiliary-process proof technique to track the algorithm's evolution and prove convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and properly contextualized within the literature.  
- Conceptual confusion: authors describe "splitting into Γvw/δ terms" while treating Γvw and δ as real numbers, which is misleading and needs clarification.  
- Needs clearer, more detailed comparison to recent optimal accelerated consensus results ([13, 24, 30, 36]) and precise relationship to the O(D log(1/ε)) rate.  
- Limited discussion of practical implementation issues and communication complexity.  
- Insufficient empirical comparison with state-of-the-art accelerated consensus methods.  
- Contribution is a significant theoretical advance likely to inspire further work in distributed optimization and message-passing algorithms.  
- Recommendation conditional: strong accept if the splitting-parameter interpretation is corrected and comparisons/empirical coverage are improved.
