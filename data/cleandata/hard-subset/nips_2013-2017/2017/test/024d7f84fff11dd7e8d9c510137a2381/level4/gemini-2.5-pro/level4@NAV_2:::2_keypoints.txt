- Study of Min-Sum Splitting for distributed average consensus: Splitting variant can converge on cyclic graphs and achieve accelerated "subdiffusive" rates.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 1): asymptotic convergence time O(D log(D/ε)), a square-root improvement over O(D^2) diffusive methods.  
- Key conceptual insight: links Min-Sum Splitting to lifted (non-reversible) Markov chains and momentum-based multi-step first-order methods.  
- Technical contribution: novel auxiliary node-based message-passing scheme that maps edge dynamics to nodes and simplifies spectral analysis.  
- Strength — originality: provides a new conceptual bridge between message-passing, lifted chains, and momentum acceleration.  
- Strength — analytical technique: Algorithm 3 (auxiliary process) is an elegant, effective reparametrization for analysis.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, logically structured, and explains the algorithm-to-theory progression clearly.  
- Weakness — literature positioning: overlooks relevant recent work (Scaman et al. ICML 2017, Olshevsky 2017, Shi et al. 2017, Jakovetić et al. 2014, Franca & Bento 2016) and needs direct comparisons.  
- Weakness — convergence significance: O(D log(D/ε)) has an extra log(D) factor versus O(D log(1/ε)), potentially making it asymptotically slower on large-diameter graphs; requires nuanced comparison and regime identification.  
- Weakness — originality of acceleration mechanism: may essentially re-derive known shift-register/Chebyshev acceleration for linear consensus, so authors should clarify novelty versus reinterpretation.  
- Minor clarity issues: imprecise claim about dimension-independent O(log 1/ε) on "good expansion" graphs; needs specification of graph classes and acknowledgment of prior links between multi-step updates and momentum.  
- Overall conclusion: interesting and valuable analysis connecting Min-Sum Splitting to other acceleration concepts, but needs stronger literature engagement and clearer comparison of convergence rates to strengthen its impact.
