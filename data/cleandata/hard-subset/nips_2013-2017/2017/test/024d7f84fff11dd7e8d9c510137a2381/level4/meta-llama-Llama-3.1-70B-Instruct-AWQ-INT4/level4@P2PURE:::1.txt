This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, called min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is very well written, with the contribution clearly placed in the context of the state of the art in the topic. To the best of my knowledge, although I am not an expert on the topic, the results appear to be novel and constitute a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper presents a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

There is a detail in the presentation that requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function that is minimized by the min-sum algorithm to yield the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state that each term $\phi_{vw}$ is split into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ is split into $\delta$ terms. However, the meaning of this statement is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are introduced on the previous page as real numbers, making their role in splitting the terms unclear.
