- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) novel analysis via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with faster convergence than prior methods, (iii) links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Exposition is generally clear and readable; proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- The manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (line-numbered corrections provided by the reviewer).  
- Algorithm presentation is unclear: several quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are not defined/initialized, and the correspondence between Algorithm 3 and the auxiliary process is not explicitly stated.  
- Missing or weak citations: additional references on (sub)gradient consensus methods and acceleration are suggested (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram et al. 2009; Wei Shi et al. 2015), plus citations to Polyak and Nesterov and other original literature.  
- Reviewer questions for authors: clarify practical implications and when to prefer this method; state limitations of Min-Sum Splitting; give more intuition on the auxiliary process; discuss extending beyond quadratic objectives to general convex problems; and articulate specific ML applications that would benefit.  
- Summary judgment: a strong theoretical paper with sound analysis and meaningful connections, but impact would increase by addressing practical relevance, providing more intuition, clarifying algorithms, fixing typos, and adding key references.
