1. Claim that convergence time is dimension-independent for “good” graphs is misleading—complete graphs are trivial and the authors must specify which broad graph families qualify and what “good” means.  
2. Missing citation of Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus…”; its convergence rate should be stated and compared since it may be competitive or superior.  
3. Important consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.) are missing; the paper should summarize state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear rates.  
4. Omission of related work on lifted graphs/Markov chain lifting (Franca and Bento).  
5. Last paragraph states a well-known fact and should cite Rabbat et al.; links to Heavy-ball/Nesterov acceleration are already known.  
6. Additional key consensus-optimization references are missing (EXTRA; Fast distributed gradient methods).  
7. Proposition 3 is trivial and only tangentially contributes to the main results.  
8. The D·log(D/ε) rate does not clearly improve upon Olshevsky’s D·log(1/ε); in worst-case graphs (D ≍ n) the new bound can be weaker.  
9. The auxiliary message-passing appears to be Nesterov-style acceleration applied to min-sum; for consensus the analysis reduces to a linear system, so the incremental contribution is unclear.  
10. Any gains likely come from finer spectral-gap handling; worst-case complexity remains O(n) since O(n)=O(D) for n-node graphs.  
11. Confusing description: graph called “simple” yet directed edges are used (line 243).  
12. Typo: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian” (line 220).
