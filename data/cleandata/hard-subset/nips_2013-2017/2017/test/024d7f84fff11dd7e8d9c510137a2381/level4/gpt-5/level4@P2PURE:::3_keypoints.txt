- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus and analyzes convergence for quadratic objectives via an auxiliary linear process.  
- Main contributions: a new proof technique using an auxiliary process, a faster Min-Sum-based consensus protocol, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is well motivated and generally clear, but contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (detailed corrections provided).  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Algorithms lack definitions/initializations for key quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) and do not clearly map the auxiliary linear process to variables in Algorithm 3.  
- Minor conceptual comment: possible confusion between "Laplacian/Laplace multipliers" and "Lagrangian/Lagrange multipliers" on lines 220–221.  
- Missing or weak citation coverage for related distributed/consensus optimization work (suggested additions: Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov).  
- Requests for clarification: practicality and when to prefer this method over others, limitations of Min-Sum Splitting, intuition for the auxiliary process, and extensibility beyond quadratic consensus problems.  
- Ask authors to better articulate why the approach is of interest to the machine learning community.  
- Summary judgment: interesting theoretical contribution with a new proof technique and useful connections, but needs more discussion of practical relevance, intuition, and stronger justification of impact for ML.
