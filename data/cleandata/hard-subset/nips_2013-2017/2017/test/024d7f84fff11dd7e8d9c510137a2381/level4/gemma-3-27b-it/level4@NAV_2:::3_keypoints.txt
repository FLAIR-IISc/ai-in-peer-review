- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to establish convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: novel auxiliary-process-based analysis, a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is clearly motivated, well-written overall, and proofs of Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 appear logically sound.  
- Contains multiple minor typos and grammatical errors (review lists specific line-by-line corrections).  
- Some algorithmic quantities are not fully defined (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, initial R̂0 and r̂0) and the mapping between Algorithm 2 and Algorithm 3 is unclear.  
- Possible misuse of terminology: authors may mean "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" rather than "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers."  
- Recommends adding references to (sub)gradient consensus methods (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015) and broader Min-Sum literature.  
- Suggests citing Polyak (Heavy-ball) and Nesterov in the context of acceleration.  
- Requests discussion of practical applicability and scenarios where the method outperforms other distributed consensus algorithms.  
- Asks for a clear articulation of the limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.  
- Recommends expanding intuition for the auxiliary process and how it yields accelerated convergence.  
- Encourages discussion of potential extensions beyond quadratic objectives to broader consensus problems in machine learning.  
- Advises explicitly stating the relevance and impact of the work for the machine learning community.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result, rigorous analysis, insightful connections to related methods, and a clever auxiliary-process technique.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overly theoretical with limited practical discussion, minor typos, weakly highlighted ML relevance, and insufficient exploration of limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address clarity, practicality, references, and proofreading.
