- Ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge on general graphs, but Min-Sum Splitting can be made to converge to the correct consensus solution.  
- Proper tuning of splitting parameters yields accelerated, subdiffusive convergence rates.  
- This is the first analysis of convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting, showing acceleration comparable to shift-register methods.  
- The paper links the reparametrization in Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chain techniques and multi-step (momentum) first-order methods.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound; Theorem 1 formally establishes the accelerated convergence rate.  
- Introducing an auxiliary node-based message-passing scheme is a clever analytical tool that simplifies edge-based analysis.  
- The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with a clear introduction and literature positioning.  
- Minor clarity issue: wording in Section 2 suggests integer-valued splitting counts (Γ_vw, δ) while the parameters are used as real numbers; clarify this intuition.
