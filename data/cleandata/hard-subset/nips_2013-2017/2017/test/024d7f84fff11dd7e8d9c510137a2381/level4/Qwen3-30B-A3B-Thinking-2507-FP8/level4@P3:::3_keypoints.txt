- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a Min-Sum variant that converges on cyclic graphs for consensus problems.  
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting achieves subdiffusive accelerated rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Key contribution: links Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Proves proper splitting parameter tuning yields accelerated convergence: O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori vs O(D² log(1/ε)).  
- Builds on Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013) splitting reparametrization and extends theory to consensus.  
- Relates approach to classical consensus algorithms (e.g., Metropolis-Hastings) and Nesterov-like acceleration.  
- Technical quality is strong: Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 are sound and rigorous.  
- Novel, elegant proof technique using an auxiliary linear process to track Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Limitations: currently restricted to quadratic objectives and requires global graph knowledge (through ρW) for optimal tuning.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains grammatical/typographical errors and some unclear algorithmic variable definitions (e.g., R̂vw, r̂vw).  
- Question raised about whether "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian" in lines 220–221.  
- Practical relevance to machine learning is underdeveloped; needs justification versus ADMM/Nesterov and discussion of communication/scalability costs.  
- Recommended improvements: expand practical implementation discussion, clarify ML relevance, add distributed optimization references, and fix grammar/algorithmic details.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality theoretical contribution, suitable for NIPS; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
