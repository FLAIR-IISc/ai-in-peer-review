- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm applied to the consensus problem in distributed optimization.
- Proves convergence of Min-Sum Splitting to the solution and establishes explicit rates of convergence.
- Shows a square-root (subdiffusive) acceleration over classical diffusive consensus methods.
- Develops a novel auxiliary message-passing scheme to analyze Min-Sum Splitting via a linear process with memory on nodes.
- Demonstrates similarities between Min-Sum Splitting and other accelerated methods (lifted Markov chains, multi-step first-order methods).
- Provides a comprehensive review of accelerated consensus algorithms to contextualize the contribution.
- Writing is clear and well-organized for readers familiar with message-passing and distributed optimization.
- Results are supported by rigorous mathematical analysis and simulations.
- Significant contribution with implications for distributed machine learning, sensor networks, and multi-agent systems.
- Novel application of Min-Sum to consensus and original proof techniques.
- Assumes high prior familiarity with message-passing protocols and distributed optimization, limiting accessibility.
- Introduction could give more context and motivation for the problem.
- Some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-expert readers.
- Acceptance arguments: novel algorithm, provable accelerated rates, thorough contextual review; rejection arguments: accessibility and insufficient introductory motivation.
