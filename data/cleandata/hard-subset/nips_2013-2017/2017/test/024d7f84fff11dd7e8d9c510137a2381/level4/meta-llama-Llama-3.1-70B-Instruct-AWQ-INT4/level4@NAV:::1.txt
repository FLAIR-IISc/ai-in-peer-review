This paper presents an innovative application of the min-sum splitting algorithm, an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, to address the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, effectively situating the contribution within the current state of knowledge in the field. To the best of my knowledge, although I am not a specialist in this area, the results appear to be novel and represent a significant qualitative advancement. Notably, the paper establishes a novel link between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

However, there is one aspect of the presentation that requires clarification. On page 4, the authors describe an equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, stating: "...splitting each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,..." This explanation is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$, introduced on the preceding page, are real numbers, making the intended meaning of this statement ambiguous.
