- Investigates Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus and establishes rigorous convergence rate guarantees.  
- Demonstrates subdiffusive, accelerated convergence matching shift-register methods, improving over diffusive methods.  
- Reveals a novel connection between Min-Sum algorithms, lifted Markov chains, and accelerated first-order methods.  
- Builds on prior work showing standard Min-Sum may not converge and that Splitting can ensure convergence.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly situates contributions within the literature.  
- Technical contribution is strong and appears novel and significant.  
- Minor clarity issue: unclear meaning of “splitting into” Γ_vw and δ terms when those are defined as real numbers.  
- Limited empirical evaluation; theoretical results would benefit from experimental validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental problem, rigorous analysis, clear presentation, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of empirical validation and a minor clarity issue in exposition.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with requested clarification on splitting.
