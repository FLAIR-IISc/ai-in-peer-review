1. Paper analyzes min-sum splitting for average consensus; technically sound but improvement over prior work is marginal and novelty is weak.

2. Claim that convergence time is dimension-independent for graphs with good expansion is misleading; complete graph trivializes the claim and no general graph classes are specified.

3. Omits Olshevsky (2015), which gives a superior O(D log(1/ε)) rate for all graphs—directly comparable and critical.

4. Missing foundational consensus-optimization references (Shi et al. 2015; Scaman et al. 2017); authors must compare to state-of-the-art rates like O(D log(1/ε)).

5. Discussion of lifted graphs/Markov chains omits Franca & Bento (2016) which addresses similar techniques.

6. Reiterates known equivalence between consensus variants and momentum methods without cite; should cite Rabbat et al. (2005).

7. Omits additional critical refs for consensus optimization (Shi et al. "Extra" 2015; Jakovetic et al. 2014) that show faster rates.

8. Proposition 3 is trivial, following directly from standard min-sum properties and adds little.

9. Claimed rate O(D log(D/ε)) gives no meaningful improvement over Olshevsky’s O(D log(1/ε)); for worst-case graphs (D = Θ(n)) it is strictly worse.

10. Auxiliary message-passing likely implements Nesterov acceleration, which Olshevsky already used to achieve O(D log(1/ε)), so the approach is not novel and the analysis is straightforward.

11. Any marginal gains may stem from spectral gap handling, but worst-case bounds remain O(n), undermining dimension-independence claims.

12. Presentation issues: line 243 uses directed edges while calling the graph "simple" (confusing), and line 220 has a typo ("Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian").

13. After rebuttal the authors clarified technicalities but did not resolve the central deficiency—the lack of meaningful improvement over Olshevsky—so the evaluation score is unchanged.
