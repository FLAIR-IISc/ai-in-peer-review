

This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is exceptionally well written, with its contribution clearly contextualized within the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this specific area), the results are novel and represent a qualitative improvement. In particular, the paper establishes a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

A critical ambiguity exists in the presentation. On Page 4, the authors state: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,..." However, it is unclear how this interpretation aligns with the definitions of $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ provided on the previous page, which are introduced as real-valued parameters. Since the splitting operation requires integer counts (number of terms), the use of real-valued $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ for this purpose is problematic and requires clarification.
