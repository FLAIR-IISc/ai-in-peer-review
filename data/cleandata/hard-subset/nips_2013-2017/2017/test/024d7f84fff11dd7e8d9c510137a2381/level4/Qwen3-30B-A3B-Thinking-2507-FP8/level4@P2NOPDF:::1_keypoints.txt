- Introduces min-sum splitting, an accelerated min-sum variant for distributed consensus, with clear structure and state-of-the-art context.  
- Presents a significant theoretical advance by connecting min-sum algorithms to lifted Markov chain techniques.  
- Main concern: notation inconsistency on page 4 where "splitting into Γ_{vw} and δ terms" conflicts with prior definitions of Γ_{vw} and δ as real-valued parameters.  
- Ambiguity: splitting into a non-integer number of pieces is undefined, implying Γ_{vw} and δ should be integers (e.g., counts of auxiliary variables).  
- Authors must clarify whether Γ_{vw} and δ are integer-valued or if an implicit rounding/discretization is used; otherwise the splitting scheme lacks rigorous foundation.  
- Technical derivations and convergence guarantees are otherwise sound and well-presented.  
- Experiments convincingly show efficiency gains over baselines in distributed settings.  
- The discussion linking to lifted Markov chains is insightful and suggests broader applicability beyond consensus.  
- Minor fixes: explicitly define Γ_{vw} and δ as positive integers at first appearance or clarify discretization, justify why splitting preserves equivalence to the original problem, and consider comparing to recent distributed ADMM variants.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, publishable contribution contingent on resolving the notation/definition inconsistency before acceptance.
