This paper investigates the application of min-sum splitting, an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, to the problem of distributed consensus. The presentation is exceptionally clear and effectively situates the work within the existing literature. While I am not a specialist in this area, the results appear novel and represent a significant improvement over current approaches. Specifically, the paper establishes a compelling and new connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques. 

However, a detail in the explanation requires further clarification. On page 4, the authors describe the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, stating: “…splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms…” The meaning of this splitting is unclear, as both $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are previously defined (on the preceding page) as real numbers. This suggests a division into a number of *sub*-terms, rather than simply scaling, but the text does not explicitly state this, and the implication requires elaboration for full understanding.
