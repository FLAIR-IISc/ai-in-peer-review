- Paper proposes Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm, and proves it converges for consensus with tunable parameters achieving subdiffusive accelerated rates.  
- Well-written exposition with a clear introduction to Min-Sum and its application to consensus.  
- Significant technical contributions with thorough convergence-rate analysis.  
- Main strengths: novelty and strong technical depth.  
- Paper is well-organized and presents results clearly and rigorously.  
- Main weakness: limited scope—focuses mainly on consensus without broader discussion of applications or implications.  
- Needs more detailed comparison with existing algorithms and techniques.  
- Suggest adding more numerical experiments to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Overall quality scores: Quality 8/10, Impact 7/10, Novelty 9/10, Technical depth 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Overall 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revision requests: expand comparisons to related work, discuss broader applications/implications, and add numerical experiments.  
- Reviewer acknowledges authors’ correction that convergence time for complete graphs is 0 and accepts their convergence-rate explanations for various topologies.  
- Reviewer notes missing key references that should be added (Olshevsky; Shi, Ling, Wu, Yin; Scaman et al.; Franca & Bento; Rabbat et al.; Jakovetic, Xavier, Moura; and others).  
- Reviewer concedes Proposition 3 is trivial and a supplementary contribution.  
- Reviewer observes the achieved rate D log(D/ε) does not substantially improve over prior D log(1/ε) results but finds the authors’ explanations acceptable.  
- Reviewer notes the proposed auxiliary message-passing is similar to Nesterov acceleration applied to Min-Sum.  
- Minor technical issues: possible tiny improvement due to spectral-gap handling; clarify use of directed edges on a simple graph; fix typo at line 220 (Laplacian → Lagrangian).  
- After rebuttal, reviewer is satisfied with responses and believes paper is ready for publication, though evaluation scores remain unchanged.
