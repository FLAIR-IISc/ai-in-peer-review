In this manuscript, the authors introduce an accelerated version of the Min-Sum message-passing scheme for consensus problems in distributed optimization. Building on the reparameterization techniques of [Ruozzi and Tatikonda, 2013], they derive convergence rates for the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm on consensus tasks with quadratic objectives. The central analytical device is an auxiliary linear process constructed to track the dynamics of Min-Sum Splitting.

The main contributions are as follows: (i) an analysis of Min-Sum Splitting via a new proof technique based on an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol for consensus that attains faster convergence than previously reported results, and (iii) an exposition of connections between the proposed method, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step methods in convex optimization.

The motivation and contributions are clearly presented. The paper is generally well written and easy to follow, though it contains several typographical and grammatical issues (listed below). The proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear to be correct.

It would be helpful if the authors could address the following in the paper:
- Although the paper is theoretical, please comment on the practicality of the method and when it should be preferred over other distributed methods for consensus optimization.
- What are the limitations of Min-Sum Splitting?
- What intuition motivates the use of the auxiliary process in Min-Sum Splitting?
- The results focus on consensus problems with quadratic objectives. Can this framework be extended to more general consensus problems commonly arising in Machine Learning?
- Please articulate why this approach is relevant to Machine Learning and of interest to the ML community.

In summary, this is a purely theoretical contribution that establishes convergence rates using a new proof technique and clarifies connections with established methods. The ideas are interesting; however, discussion of practical applicability and intuitive insights is limited, and further justification of the method’s relevance to the Machine Learning community would strengthen the paper.
