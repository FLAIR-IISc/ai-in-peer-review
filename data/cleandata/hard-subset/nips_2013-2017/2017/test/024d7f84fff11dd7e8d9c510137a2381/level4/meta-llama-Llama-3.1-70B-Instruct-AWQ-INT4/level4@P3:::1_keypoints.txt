- Applies an accelerated min-sum variant (min-sum splitting) to distributed consensus, showing practical algorithmic application.
- Presents a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.
- Thorough introduction and clear placement of contributions within the state of the art.
- Detailed convergence analysis with proofs showing the algorithm reaches the optimal solution under certain conditions.
- Frames min-sum splitting as a process on a lifted space; splitting parameters introduce directionality similar to non-reversible random walks, enabling faster convergence.
- Explores links to other accelerated methods, including multi-step first-order methods.
- Paper is very well written, clear, well-structured, and supported by helpful examples and illustrations.
- Results are novel and constitute a significant contribution to distributed consensus research.
- Technically sound analysis; arguments are well-supported by mathematical proofs.
- Main criticism: lacks a more detailed explanation of the min-sum splitting algorithm and its relation to the equivalent objective function.
- Needs more intuition and guidance on how splitting parameters are chosen and how they affect convergence.
- Minor comment: unclear wording on page 4 about splitting terms (Γ_vw and δ are introduced as real numbers but described as term counts).
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid proofs, thorough and organized presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient explanation/intuition regarding the algorithm and parameter selection.
