This paper studies the convergence rate of the min-sum splitting method on the average consensus problem. While the paper is generally well-written, the improvement of the result appears to be limited. The following detailed comments provide specific feedback on the paper.

Firstly, the paper claims that the convergence rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as the complete graph. However, for complete graphs, the linear rate is 0 because everyone converges to the average in 1 step. Moreover, complete graphs are too special to be representative. Therefore, it would be beneficial to specify which general category of graphs the complexity does not depend on the dimension (number of nodes) and what constitutes a "good" graph in this context.

In the same paragraph, the literature review should include "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control" by Olshevsky. The convergence rate of this reference should be properly reported, as it has reached a competitive or even better bound compared to the result of the submission.

The authors should also cite important references in consensus optimization, such as "On the Linear Convergence of the ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization" by Shi, Ling, Kun, Wu, and Yin, and "Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks" by Scaman, Bach, Bubeck, Lee, and Massoulié. These references should be included in the literature review at the top of page 2. Furthermore, the authors should report the state-of-the-art algorithms for consensus optimization and their corresponding (linear) convergence rates.

Additionally, the authors have ignored a very related paper "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM" by Franca and Bento when discussing lifted graphs and Markov chains.

The content of the last paragraph of page 5 is a well-known fact and should refer to "Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links" by Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew. Moreover, the connection between those variants and Heavy ball/Nesterov/Polyak is known to the field.

There are several important references regarding consensus optimization that the authors have ignored, including "Extra: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization" by Shi, Ling, Wu, and Yin, and "Fast distributed gradient methods" by Jakovetic, J Xavier, and Moura.

Proposition 3 appears to be trivial and is a supplementary contribution. The rate reached by this paper, D log(D/eps), does not seem to have a significant improvement on the rate D log(1/eps) that has been reached by "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control". Especially in the worst-case scenario (which holds for all graphs), D~n, the bound is even worse than that achieved in "Linear Time Average Consensus…".

The paper "Linear Time Average Consensus…" improves the bound through Nesterov's acceleration. The reviewer suspects that the so-called "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" proposed by the authors is again Nesterov's acceleration applied to the min-sum algorithm. While this is acceptable, the analysis is done for consensus, which boils down to analyzing a linear system and is not particularly challenging. Consequently, the contribution of the paper becomes unclear in this context.

The tiny improvement may come from a careful handling of the spectral gap of graphs. However, the worst-case bound is still O(n) because O(n)=O(D) for the set of all graphs with n nodes.

On a minor note, the graph in line 243 of page 6 is simple, but the author is using directed edges, which can be confusing. Additionally, there is a typo at line 220 of page 6, where "Laplacian" should be replaced with "Lagrangian".

After reviewing the authors' response, the reviewer is satisfied with their clarifications. However, the evaluation score remains the same.
