- Paper studies min-sum splitting for average consensus; well-written but improvement over existing results appears limited.

1. Claim of “optimal” rate for graphs with good expansion is misleading: complete graphs reach consensus in one step; authors should define “good” graphs and state which graph classes yield dimension-independent complexity.

2. Missing citation: Olshevsky “Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs…”, whose convergence rate competes with or outperforms the paper’s bound.

3. Missing consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.); authors should include and compare state-of-the-art linear convergence rates.

4. Missing reference on lifted graphs/Markov chains: Franca and Bento “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM”.

5. Last paragraph of p.5 is a known result and should be attributed to Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew; links to Heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak are well-known.

6. Additional overlooked references: “Extra” (Shi et al.) and “Fast Distributed Gradient Methods” (Jakovetic et al.).

7. Proposition 3 is trivial and could be moved to supplementary material.

8. The D log(D/ε) rate is not a substantial improvement over D log(1/ε) from Olshevsky; in worst-case D ≈ n the new bound is worse.

9. The auxiliary message-passing scheme likely reimplements Nesterov acceleration for min-sum; analysis reduces to a linear system, so the novelty is unclear.

10. Any improvement likely comes from refined spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case bound remains O(n) since O(n)=O(D).

11. Line 243 (p.6) uses directed edges on a graph described as simple, causing confusion.

12. Line 220 (p.6) has a typo: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian”.
