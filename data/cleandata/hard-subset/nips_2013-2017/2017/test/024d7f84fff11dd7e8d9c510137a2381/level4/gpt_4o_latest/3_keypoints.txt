- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting protocol for consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda reparametrization to derive convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Introduces a novel proof technique based on an auxiliary linear process to analyze Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Claims improved convergence rates for consensus problems compared to prior work and connects the method to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (many specific line-by-line corrections provided).  
- Algorithms are inconsistently specified: several variables (e.g., R_vw, r_vw, initializations of ĤR^0, Ĥr^0) are undefined and the auxiliary process variables are not explicitly identified in Algorithm 3.  
- Several relevant references are missing, including classic distributed (sub)gradient methods, Polyak (heavy-ball), Nesterov, and other original literature citations.  
- Requests clarification on practical applicability: when the method should be preferred over other distributed consensus methods.  
- Asks the authors to state limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and provide intuition for the auxiliary process’s role.  
- Questions extendibility beyond quadratic objectives to broader consensus problems (e.g., ML applications) and requests explicit relevance to the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution with novel analysis and interesting connections, but needs better exposition, missing references, and more discussion on practicality and significance.
