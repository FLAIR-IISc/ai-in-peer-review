This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly positioning its contribution within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this area), the results are novel and represent a significant advancement. Notably, the paper establishes a new connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques. 

One detail requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to derive the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors state: “…splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms…” However, the meaning of this statement is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$, as previously defined, are real numbers.
