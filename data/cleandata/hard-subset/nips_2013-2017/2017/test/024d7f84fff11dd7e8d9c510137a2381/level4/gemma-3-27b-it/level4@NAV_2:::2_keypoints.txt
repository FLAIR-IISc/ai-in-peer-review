- Overall: technically sound and clearly written, but practical improvement over existing methods is limited.

- (1) Clarify imprecise claim about optimal rates on complete graphs and define which graph classes yield convergence complexity independent of node count; define what makes a “good” graph.

- (2) Must acknowledge and compare to Olshevsky et al. “Linear Time Average Consensus…” and report its convergence rate.

- (3) Missing key consensus-optimization references (e.g., Shi et al., Scaman et al.); provide a more complete overview of state-of-the-art linear rates.

- (4) Cite França and Bento on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM when discussing lifted graphs/Markov chains.

- (5) Connection to heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak is well-known and should be contextualized with citations (e.g., Rabbat et al.).

- (6) Additional omitted references (e.g., Shi et al. “Extra”, Jakovetić et al. “Fast distributed gradient methods”); literature review needs expansion.

- (7) Proposition 3 appears trivial and contributes little.

- (8) The D·log(D/ε) rate is not a substantial improvement over D·log(1/ε) from prior work and can be worse when D ~ n.

- (9) The “auxiliary message-passing scheme” may be Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; analysis for consensus reduces to a linear system, weakening novelty.

- (10) Even with spectral-gap analysis, worst-case bounds remain O(n) since D = O(n) across all n-node graphs.

- (11) Terminology confusion: refers to a “simple graph” but discusses directed edges (line 243).

- (12) Typo: “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian” (line 220).

- Pro summary: technically sound analysis; clarifies relations to lifted Markov chains and accelerated methods; gives convergence guarantees for a specific parameter setting.

- Con summary: limited practical improvement, incomplete literature coverage, and some results/observations lack novelty.
