- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus and shows it accelerates convergence versus ordinary Min-Sum.
- Shows ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge on graphs with cycles, but Min-Sum Splitting converges with proper parameter tuning.
- Establishes subdiffusive convergence rates matching shift-register methods when parameters are chosen correctly.
- Novelly connects Min-Sum schemes to lifted Markov chain techniques and multi-step first-order optimization methods.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and well-constructed proofs linking an auxiliary process to spectral graph properties.
- Analyzes specific topologies (cycles, tori) and proves O(D log(D/ε)) convergence, a square-root improvement over diffusive O(D² log(1/ε)) rates.
- Acknowledges limitation that optimal parameter selection requires global graph information (e.g., ρ(W)).
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, with accessible technical exposition and helpful supplementary proofs.
- Original contributions: first link between Min-Sum and lifted chains, tuning directionality for acceleration, novel auxiliary-process proof technique, and improved rates over prior Min-Sum work ([27]).
- Results are significant for distributed optimization, offering near-optimal consensus rates and suggesting broader applicability.
- Strength: clear exposition and organization.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results with meaningful convergence guarantees.
- Strength: novel conceptual connections to two established acceleration techniques.
- Strength: comprehensive comparison to related work and practical implications for distributed systems.
- Weakness: unclear explanation of "splitting into Γvw terms" since parameters are real-valued, needs clarification.
- Weakness: focus is mainly on quadratic consensus; extension to general objectives is not addressed.
- Weakness: lack of discussion on practical approaches when global graph information for parameter tuning is unavailable.
