This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, known as min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is very well written and clearly situates its contributions within the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (although I am not an expert on the topic), the results are novel and represent a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper establishes a new connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

One aspect of the exposition is unclear. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective minimized by the min-sum algorithm to obtain the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors write: “... splitting each term phi_vw into Gamma_vw terms, and each term phi_v into delta terms, ...” It is not clear how to interpret this, since delta and Gamma_vw, as introduced on the previous page, are real numbers rather than counts.
