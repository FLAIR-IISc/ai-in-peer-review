- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for average consensus and shows tuning can yield accelerated, subdiffusive convergence.  
- Main theoretical result: convergence time O(D log(D/ε)), and conceptual links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Strength: novel conceptual connection interpreting splitting parameters as inducing memory/directionality, unifying belief-propagation, lifting, and momentum ideas.  
- Weakness: literature review is incomplete and omits several relevant works on accelerated consensus and distributed optimization.  
- Missing comparison to Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs..." which achieves O(D log(1/ε)).  
- Missing comparisons to consensus optimization literature with linear rates (e.g., Shi et al., Scaman et al.).  
- Missing citation/discussion of lifted Markov chain related work such as França and Bento ("Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM").  
- Other omitted fast distributed methods: Shi et al. ("Extra") and Jakovetić et al. ("Fast distributed gradient methods").  
- Significance concern: O(D log(D/ε)) offers limited improvement over existing O(D log(1/ε)) and may be worse in worst-case sparse graphs (D ~ n).  
- Originality concern: the auxiliary message-passing scheme is essentially equivalent to known multi-step momentum/shift-register methods.  
- Analysis specialized to average consensus reduces to standard spectral analysis, raising questions about novel analytical insights.  
- Known connections between consensus and momentum methods (e.g., Rabbat et al.) are not cited.  
- Clarity/minor issues: vague claim that diffusive rate is "optimal for graphs with good expansion" and misleading complete graph example.  
- Proposition 2 is presented as a minor technical bridge rather than a central contribution.  
- Presentation issues: clarify use of "directed edges" for simple graphs and fix a likely typo ("Laplacian" → "Lagrangian" on p.6, line 220).
