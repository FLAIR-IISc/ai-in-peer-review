Overall: Manuscript is clearly written but the claimed improvement over existing results is marginal.

(1) Example of complete graph is not illustrative; authors should specify broader graph classes for dimension-independent complexity and define "good" expansion precisely.

(2) Key omission of Olshevsky's work, which gives competitive or superior convergence guarantees.

(3) Missing foundational consensus-optimization references (e.g., Shi et al., Scaman et al.) and lacking thorough comparison with state-of-the-art linear rates.

(4) Overlooks relevant work on lifted graphs and Markov-chain lifting by Franca and Bento.

(5) Final-paragraph result is established and uncredited — should cite Rabbat et al. and acknowledge links to momentum-based acceleration methods.

(6) Further literature omissions include "Extra" (Shi et al.) and "Fast distributed gradient methods" (Jakovetic et al.).

(7) Proposition 3 is a straightforward, auxiliary result with limited contribution to the main thesis.

(8) Main rate D log(D/ε) is only marginally better than Olshevsky's D log(1/ε) and can be weaker in worst-case when D ∝ n.

(9) Novelty of the auxiliary message-passing scheme is questionable; it resembles applying Nesterov acceleration to a linear min-sum system.

(10) Any minor improvement likely stems from refined spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case complexity remains O(n) since D can be O(n).

(11) Use of directed edges is confusing given the graph is defined as simple (page 6, line 243).

(12) Typo: "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian" (page 6, line 220).

After rebuttal: Authors' rebuttal satisfactorily addressed points, but reviewer’s assessment and score remain unchanged.
