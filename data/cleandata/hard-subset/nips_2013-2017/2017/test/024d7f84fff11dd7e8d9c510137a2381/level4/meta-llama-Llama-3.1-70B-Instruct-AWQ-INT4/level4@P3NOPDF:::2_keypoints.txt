- Improvement of convergence rate is limited; overall contribution appears small.  
- Claim that rate is "optimal for graphs with good expansion (e.g., complete graph)" is misleading because complete graphs converge in one step and are not representative.  
- Authors should specify which graph families make complexity independent of node count and define "good" graphs precisely.  
- Missing citation and comparison: Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs…" and its competitive/better convergence bound must be included and reported.  
- Literature review should add consensus-optimization references: Shi et al. (ADMM linear convergence) and Scaman et al. (optimal distributed algorithms), and report state-of-the-art linear rates.  
- Related work on lifted graphs/Markov chains (Franca & Bento, "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM") is ignored and should be cited.  
- The statement in last paragraph of p.5 is well-known; cite Rabbat, Nowak & Bucklew on generalized consensus with erasures and note known links to Heavy Ball/Nesterov methods.  
- Additional ignored references: Shi et al. ("Extra") and Jakovetic et al. ("Fast distributed gradient methods") should be included.  
- Proposition 3 seems trivial/supplementary and not a major contribution.  
- Claimed rate D log(D/ε) offers little/no improvement over prior D log(1/ε) (Olshevsky), and in worst case (D~n) is worse.  
- The "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" may effectively be Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; analysis focuses on a linear system, so novelty is unclear.  
- Any small gains likely come from spectral-gap handling, but worst-case complexity remains O(n) for general graphs.
