- Paper analyzes min-sum splitting for average consensus; well-written but improvements over prior work are incremental.
- Claim of optimality for graphs with good expansion (e.g., complete graphs) is misleading; complete graphs reach consensus in one iteration, so authors should clarify broader graph classes and define "good" graphs.
- Missing key reference Olshevsky (2010); need thorough comparison including Olshevsky’s specific convergence rates.
- Must cite Shi et al. (2014) and Scaman et al. (2011) when discussing consensus optimization and provide a comprehensive overview of state-of-the-art linear rates.
- Omission of Franca & Bento (2016) on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM, which is relevant to the lifted graphs discussion.
- Assertion about generalized consensus with erasure links is well-known (Rabbat et al., 2005) and connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov methods should be acknowledged.
- Additional relevant citations needed: Shi et al. (2015) EXTRA and Jakovetic & Moura (2014) fast distributed gradient methods.
- Proposition 3 appears to be a relatively trivial extension and a minor contribution.
- The reported D·log(D/ε) rate is not a substantive improvement over Olshevsky’s D·log(1/ε); for worst-case D≈n the bound can be worse.
- The "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" may essentially be Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; novelty is unclear since consensus reduces to a linear-system analysis.
- Any improvement likely hinges on spectral-gap analysis, yet worst-case complexity remains O(n) because O(n)=O(D) for n-node graphs.
- Minor issues: confusing use of directed edges in a simple graph (line 243) and typo "Laplacian" vs. "Lagrangian" (line 220).
- After rebuttal the reviewer was satisfied with the authors' response but kept their evaluation score unchanged.
