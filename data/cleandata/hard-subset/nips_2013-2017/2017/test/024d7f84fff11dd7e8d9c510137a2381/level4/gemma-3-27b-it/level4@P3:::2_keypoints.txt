- Paper analyzes convergence of a min-sum splitting method for average consensus, claiming accelerated convergence over diffusive methods.  
- Core idea: modify min-sum with splitting and introduce an auxiliary message-passing scheme to enable convergence analysis.  
- Establishes convergence rates and links the method to lifted Markov chains and accelerated first-order convex methods.  
- Mathematical derivations and proofs appear rigorous and technically sound.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with consistent notation and accessible algorithm descriptions.  
- Novelty: applying min-sum splitting to consensus and connecting it to lifted chains and acceleration methods.  
- Completeness: presents a full analysis within the problem’s scope.  
- Limited significance: reported O(D log(D/ε)) rate is an incremental improvement and can be misleading in worst cases (D ~ n → O(n log(n/ε))).  
- Insufficient literature review: omits key related works (Olshevsky et al.; Shi et al.; Scaman et al.; Franca and Bento; Extra and fast distributed gradient methods).  
- Auxiliary message-passing may be essentially Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum, reducing perceived novelty since analysis is for a simple consensus (linear) problem.  
- Confusing terminology: use of “directed edges” in a “simple graph” requires clarification.  
- Minor error: typo—“Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian” (page 6, line 220).  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound analysis, useful connections to established techniques, and a clever analytical tool.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental/misleading improvement, incomplete literature positioning, and limited novelty.  
- Overall recommendation: technically solid but insufficiently novel/impactful for NIPS; reject with advice to broaden literature review and better position unique contributions.
