This paper investigates the distributed consensus problem using an accelerated min-sum algorithm, specifically min-sum splitting. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, effectively situating the work within the current state-of-the-art research. Based on my understanding (while acknowledging I am not a specialist in this area), the results appear novel and represent a significant improvement. A key contribution is the establishment of a new link between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain methodologies.

However, a specific detail requires clarification. On page 4, the description of the objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to achieve the splitting scheme is unclear. The authors state: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms...", but the meaning of this splitting is ambiguous given that $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$, as defined on the preceding page, are real numbers.
