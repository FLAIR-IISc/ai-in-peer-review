- Claims D·log(D/ε) convergence but this is only a marginal improvement over prior D·log(1/ε) results and may be worse in worst-case graphs (D ~ n).
- The paper's optimality claim for "good expansion" graphs is misleading (e.g., complete graph converges in one step); authors should precisely define the graph class benefiting from their rate.
- Improvement mainly comes from spectral-gap handling; overall worst-case complexity remains O(n), already achieved by prior work.
- Literature review is incomplete: missing key decentralized consensus/optimization works (e.g., Shi et al. 2015; Scaman et al. 2017).
- First-order methods and related algorithms are not discussed (e.g., EXTRA, Jakovetic et al. 2014).
- Relevant work on lifted Markov chains is ignored (e.g., Franca & Bento 2016).
- Connection to momentum methods is well-known and should be attributed to earlier sources (e.g., Rabbat et al. 2005).
- Novelty is questionable: the auxiliary message-passing appears to be Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum, which is unsurprising for the linear average-consensus setting.
- The paper does not clarify unique analytical challenges or new insights arising from this specific application.
- Proposition 3 is trivial and adds little to the main contribution.
- Notation is confusing (uses directed-edge notation while referring to simple graphs).
- Typo: "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian" on page 6, line 220.
- Overall conclusion: contribution is not substantial enough for publication due to marginal, non-universal improvement and poor engagement with relevant literature.
- Post-rebuttal: some questions were addressed, but core concerns about novelty and performance improvement remain unresolved.
