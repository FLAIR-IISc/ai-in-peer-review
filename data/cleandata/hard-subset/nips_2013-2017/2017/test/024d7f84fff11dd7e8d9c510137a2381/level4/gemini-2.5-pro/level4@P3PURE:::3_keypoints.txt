- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus optimization and analyzes it for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: new auxiliary-process-based proof technique, improved convergence rates for Min-Sum on consensus, and links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and proofs of Propositions 1, 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Major request: discuss practicality — when to prefer this algorithm over other distributed consensus methods (e.g., subgradient-based methods).  
- Major request: clearly state key limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.  
- Major request: provide intuition behind the auxiliary linear process to improve accessibility.  
- Major request: clarify whether and how the framework generalizes beyond quadratic consensus objectives to broader ML-relevant problems.  
- Major request: justify relevance to the Machine Learning / NeurIPS community.  
- Minor clarity: Algorithm descriptions omit definitions/initializations (e.g., R_{vw}, r_{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) and should map variables in Algorithm 3 to the auxiliary process.  
- Minor clarity: possible terminology error — likely "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" instead of "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers" (lines 220–221).  
- Missing citations: foundational distributed/subgradient consensus literature (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram et al. 2009; Wei Shi et al. 2015) should be added.
