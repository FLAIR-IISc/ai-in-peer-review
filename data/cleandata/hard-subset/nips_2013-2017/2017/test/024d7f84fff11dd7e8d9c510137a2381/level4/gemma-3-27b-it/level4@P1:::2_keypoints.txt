1. Claim that the rate is "optimal" on complete graphs is misleading; complete graphs converge in one step and the authors should clarify what graph classes yield node-count-independent convergence and define "good" graphs.  
2. Missing citation: Olshevsky et al.'s "Linear Time Average Consensus..." should be included and its convergence rate compared, as it may offer competitive or superior bounds.  
3. Important consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.) are omitted; state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear rates should be reported.  
4. Overlooks related work on lifted Markov chains, specifically França and Bento's "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM."  
5. The paragraph on page 5 covers well-known results; cite Rabbat et al. and acknowledge known connections to heavy-ball/Nesterov/Polyak methods.  
6. Additional key consensus optimization references (e.g., Shi et al.'s EXTRA and Jakovetić et al.'s fast distributed gradient methods) are missing.  
7. Proposition 3 is trivial and constitutes a minor contribution.  
8. The claimed O(D log(D/ε)) rate offers little improvement over O(D log(1/ε)) from Linear Time Average Consensus and can be worse in the worst case when D ~ n.  
9. The auxiliary message-passing scheme appears to be Nesterov acceleration applied to min-sum; since consensus reduces to linear systems, the novelty of the analysis is unclear.  
10. Any gains likely stem from spectral-gap handling, but worst-case complexity remains O(n) because D = O(n) for general graphs.  
11. Confusing terminology: the graph is called "simple" yet directed edges are discussed (line 243).  
12. Typo on line 220: "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian."
