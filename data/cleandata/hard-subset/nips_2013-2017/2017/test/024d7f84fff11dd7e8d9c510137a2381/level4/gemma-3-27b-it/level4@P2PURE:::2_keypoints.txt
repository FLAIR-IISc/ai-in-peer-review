1. Claim of optimality for “good” graphs (e.g., complete graph) is misleading — complete graphs converge in one step and the paper must clarify for which graph classes complexity is independent of node count and define “good” graphs.

2. Missing citation: Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs…” should be included and its competitive (potentially superior) convergence rate accurately reported.

3. Missing consensus-optimization references: cite Shi et al. (ADMM) and Scaman et al. and present current state-of-the-art algorithms with their linear convergence rates.

4. Missing related work on lifted graphs/Markov chains: cite Franca and Bento (“Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM”).

5. The final paragraph of page 5 states a known result; cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew and note the well-known connections to Heavy ball/Nesterov/Polyak methods.

6. Additional important consensus-optimization references omitted, e.g., EXTRA (Shi et al.) and “Fast distributed gradient methods” (Jakovetic et al.).

7. Proposition 3 offers a trivial contribution and would be more appropriate for the supplementary material.

8. The stated convergence D log(D/ε) offers little improvement over Olshevsky’s D log(1/ε) and can be worse in the worst case when D ≈ n.

9. The auxiliary message-passing scheme appears to be a re-application of Nesterov acceleration to min-sum; since consensus reduces to a linear system, the novelty of the analysis is unclear.

10. Any gains likely stem from spectral-gap analysis, but worst-case complexity over all n-node graphs remains O(n)=O(D).

11. Figure on line 243 (page 6) shows a simple graph with directed edges, which is confusing and inconsistent.

12. Typo at line 220 (page 6): “Laplacian” should read “Lagrangian.”

13. After rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ response, but the evaluation score remains unchanged.
