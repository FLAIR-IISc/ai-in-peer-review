- Core contribution: applies accelerated min-sum (min-sum splitting) to distributed consensus to improve convergence rate.
- Presentation: exceptionally clear and well-written, effectively situating the work within existing literature.
- Novelty: significant advance, notably the insightful connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.
- Math: derivations are generally well-structured and easy to follow.
- Ambiguity: unclear statement on page 4 about “splitting each term φ_vw into Γ_vw terms and φ_v into δ terms” since Γ_vw and δ are real numbers.
- Clarification request: authors should provide an explicit explanation, example, or mathematical consequence of the stated splitting.
- Technical soundness: method is principled and authors show good understanding of related work and limitations of prior approaches.
- Empirical evaluation: supportive of theoretical claims but not extensive.
- Suggested experiments: evaluate on larger-scale networks and under challenging conditions (e.g., asynchronous communication).
- Strengths summary: clear writing; novel connection to lifted Markov chains; well-motivated, technically sound approach; solid theoretical foundation.
- Weaknesses summary: ambiguity in splitting explanation on page 4; limited empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance pending clarification of the min-sum splitting description.
