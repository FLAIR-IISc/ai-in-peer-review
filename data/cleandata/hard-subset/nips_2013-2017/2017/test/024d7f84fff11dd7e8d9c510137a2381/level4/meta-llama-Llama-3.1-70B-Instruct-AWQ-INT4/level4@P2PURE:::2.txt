This paper studies the convergence rate of a min-sum splitting method on the average consensus problem. The paper is generally well-written, but the improvement in the result appears to be limited. The following are detailed comments:

(1) The paper states that the convergence rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as complete graphs, and that the convergence time does not depend on the dimension of the problem. However, for complete graphs, the linear convergence rate is 0, as everyone converges to the average in one step. Moreover, complete graphs are too special to be representative. Therefore, the authors should clarify which general category of graphs has a complexity that does not depend on the dimension (number of nodes) and what constitutes a "good" graph in this context.

(2) The literature review in the same paragraph should include the paper "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control" by Olshevsky. The authors should properly report the convergence rate achieved in this reference, which is further discussed in comment 8. Notably, the referenced paper has obtained a competitive or even better bound compared to the result presented in the submission.

(3) At the top of page 2, the authors should cite important references on consensus optimization, including "On the Linear Convergence of the ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization" by Shi, Ling, Kun, Wu, and Yin, and "Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks" by Scaman, Bach, Bubeck, Lee, and Massoulié. Additionally, the authors should report the state-of-the-art algorithms for consensus optimization and their corresponding linear convergence rates.

(4) When discussing lifted graphs and Markov chains, the authors have overlooked a related paper, "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM" by Franca and Bento.

(5) The content of the last paragraph on page 5 is a well-known fact and should be referenced to "Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links" by Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew. Furthermore, the connection between these variants and Heavy ball/Nesterov/Polyak is established knowledge in the field.

(6) There are several important references on consensus optimization that the authors have neglected, including "Extra: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization" by Shi, Ling, Wu, and Yin, and "Fast distributed gradient methods" by Jakovetic, Xavier, and Moura.

(7) Proposition 3 appears to be trivial and provides a supplementary contribution.

(8) The convergence rate achieved in this paper, D log(D/eps), does not seem to offer a significant improvement over the rate D log(1/eps) obtained in "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control" (see comment 2). In particular, in the worst-case scenario (which holds for all graphs), where D~n, the bound is even worse than that achieved in the aforementioned paper.

(9) The paper "Linear Time Average Consensus..." improves the bound through Nesterov's acceleration. The reviewer suspects that the "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" proposed by the authors is essentially Nesterov's acceleration applied to the min-sum algorithm. While this is acceptable, the analysis is done for consensus, which boils down to analyzing a linear system and is not particularly challenging. As a result, the contribution of the paper becomes unclear in this context.

(10) The minor improvement may stem from a careful handling of the spectral gap of graphs. However, the worst-case bound remains O(n), as O(n) = O(D) for the set of all graphs with n nodes.

(11) On line 243 of page 6, the graph is simple, but the authors use directed edges, which is confusing.

(12) There is a typo on line 220 of page 6, where "Laplacian" should be replaced with "Lagrangian."
