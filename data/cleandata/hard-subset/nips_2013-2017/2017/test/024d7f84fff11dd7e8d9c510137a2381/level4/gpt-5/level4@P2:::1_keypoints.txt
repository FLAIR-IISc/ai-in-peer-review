- Paper studies Min-Sum with splitting for distributed consensus, proving Splitting converges on loopy graphs where standard Min-Sum can fail.
- With appropriate tuning, Min-Sum Splitting attains accelerated, subdiffusive convergence rates matching shift-register methods.
- Highlights novel conceptual links between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted nonreversible Markov chains, and multi-step first-order optimization methods.
- Convergence analysis uses an auxiliary node-level linear process to track edge-based updates and ties rates to the spectrum of an associated matrix.
- Strength: clearly written and well-situated relative to prior work.
- Strength: results appear novel and constitute a qualitative advance.
- Strength: the connection to lifted Markov chains is particularly interesting and seemingly new.
- Clarification needed: explain the meaning of “splitting” when delta and Gamma_vw are non-integer (e.g., formal scaling vs. literal replication) and state assumptions ensuring Algorithm 1 is well-defined.
- Overall assessment: contribution is clear, technically sound, and makes a meaningful conceptual and theoretical advance.
