- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013) reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to establish convergence rates for quadratic objectives.
- Main contributions: (i) new proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence guarantees, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Motivation and contributions are clear; paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.
- Paper contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (specific line-by-line corrections are listed).
- Algorithms lack clarity: some quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}) are unexplained, \hat{R}^0 and \hat{r}^0 are not initialized in Algorithm 2, and the auxiliary process variables are not clearly identified in Algorithm 3.
- Several relevant references are missing, including classical distributed/subgradient literature and acceleration work (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, incremental subgradient papers, EXTRA 2015, Polyak, Nesterov).
- Requests for additional author discussion: practicality and use-cases versus other distributed methods, limitations of Min-Sum Splitting, intuition for the auxiliary process, extensibility beyond quadratic consensus, and relevance to the Machine Learning community.
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting with novel analysis, but lacks intuition, practical discussion, and clearer justification of importance for ML applications.
