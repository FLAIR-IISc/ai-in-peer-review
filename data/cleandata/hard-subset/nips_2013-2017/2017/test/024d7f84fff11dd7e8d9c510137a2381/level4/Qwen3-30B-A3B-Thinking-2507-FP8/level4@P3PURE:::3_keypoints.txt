- Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) a novel proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with faster convergence than prior results, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and accessible; proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Contains several typos and grammatical errors (reviewer provides a detailed list of corrections).  
- Minor comments: clarify "Laplacian"/"Laplace multipliers" vs Lagrangian/Lagrange multipliers; define R_vw, r_vw and initialize ĤR^0, ĥr^0 in Algorithm 2; explicitly map Algorithm 3 to the auxiliary process.  
- Missing references: include foundational consensus/subgradient works (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Sundhar Ram et al. 2009, Shi et al. 2015), cite the "original literature" at line 170, and reference Polyak (Heavy-ball) and Nesterov.  
- Key questions for authors: discuss practicality and when to prefer this method; state limitations of Min-Sum Splitting; provide intuition for the auxiliary process; address extension to non-quadratic consensus; clarify significance for the ML community.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution establishing convergence rates and connections, but needs more discussion of practicality, intuition, and ML relevance to strengthen impact.
