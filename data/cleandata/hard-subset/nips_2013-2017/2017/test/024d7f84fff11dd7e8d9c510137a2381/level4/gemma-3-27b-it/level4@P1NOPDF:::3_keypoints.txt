- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to establish convergence rates for quadratic objectives.
- Main contributions: (i) novel proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Paper is clear and well written overall, but contains numerous typos and grammatical errors that need fixing.
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear to be correct.
- Algorithms lack definitions/initializations for some quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) and should state which variables correspond to the auxiliary process in Algorithm 3.
- Several relevant references are missing for (sub)gradient consensus methods and momentum/acceleration work (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov) and "original literature" should be specified.
- Reviewer asks for discussion on practicality: when the method is preferable to other distributed consensus methods and what its limitations are.
- Reviewer requests intuition for the auxiliary process, potential extensions beyond quadratic objectives to general ML consensus problems, and clearer articulation of relevance to the Machine Learning community.
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting with novel analysis, but lacks exploration of practicality, intuition, and ML relevance.
