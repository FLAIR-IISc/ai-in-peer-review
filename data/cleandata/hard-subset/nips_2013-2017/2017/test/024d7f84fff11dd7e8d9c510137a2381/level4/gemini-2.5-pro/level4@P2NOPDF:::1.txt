This paper presents min-sum splitting, an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, and applies it to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is exceptionally well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of situating their contribution within the context of the relevant state of the art.

To the best of my knowledge, though this is not my primary area of expertise, the results are novel and constitute a significant technical advance. A key contribution is the novel connection the paper establishes between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques, which appears to be entirely new.

A minor point in the presentation, however, requires clarification. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function that yields the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors write that the objective is formed by "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,...". This description is confusing because, as introduced on the previous page, $\Gamma_{vw}$ and $\delta$ are real numbers. It is not conceptually clear how a term can be split into a non-integer number of new terms. The authors should clarify the precise meaning or interpretation of this splitting operation to strengthen the derivation.
