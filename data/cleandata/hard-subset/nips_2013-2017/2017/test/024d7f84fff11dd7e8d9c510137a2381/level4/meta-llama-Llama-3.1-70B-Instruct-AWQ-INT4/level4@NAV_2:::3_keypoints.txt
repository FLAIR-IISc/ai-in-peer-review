- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) new proof technique via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clear; the paper is well written and easy to follow overall.  
- Technical soundness: proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct and claims are theoretically supported.  
- The authors summarize ideas well and relate them to prior NIPS and other work, adequately distinguishing this work from previous contributions.  
- Results are important and likely useful to researchers/practitioners; the paper advances the state of the art.  
- Paper lacks discussion on practicality, limitations, and intuition behind the auxiliary process, and should state relevance to the Machine Learning community.  
- Reviewer requests explicit comments on: practicality, limitations of Min-Sum Splitting, intuition for the auxiliary process, extensions to more general ML consensus problems, and ML relevance.  
- Some references appear missing and should be added to better position the work.  
- Algorithmic clarity issues: several quantities in Algorithm 2 (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are not defined/initialized, and the mapping of variables to the auxiliary process in Algorithm 3 is not made explicit.  
- Numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout; reviewer lists multiple line-specific corrections (e.g., "their neighbors", "doubly-stochastic", "can be cast as", and several others).
