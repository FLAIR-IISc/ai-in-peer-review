- Paper analyzes Min-Sum Splitting for average consensus, showing ordinary Min-Sum may not converge, Splitting ensures convergence, and appropriate (δ, Γ) give accelerated, subdiffusive rates matching shift-register methods.  
- Uses an auxiliary node-based linear process to relate edge-based message passing to a simpler spectral analysis.  
- Highlights conceptual links between Min-Sum Splitting, lifted non-reversible Markov chains, and accelerated first-order methods.

Strengths:
- Clear formulation and careful specialization to quadratic consensus.  
- Useful auxiliary node-based linear system that makes analysis tractable and connects to multi-step methods.  
- Provable square-root speedup over diffusive consensus on graphs with geometry (e.g., O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori).  
- Interesting and timely conceptual connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step optimization.

Weaknesses / Requests for clarification:
- Clarify “good graphs” and dimension-independence: define the class (e.g., expanders) and give explicit bounds on 1−ρ(W).  
- Specify for which graph classes complexity provably does not depend on n.  
- Compare and cite Olshevsky (“Linear Time Average Consensus...”) and accurately report its guarantees, since it can outperform the presented rates.  
- Position relative to consensus-optimization literature (Shi et al., Scaman et al.) and summarize state-of-the-art rates including condition number and network-spectrum dependence.  
- Include and discuss “Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM” (França & Bento) in the lifting/ADMM discussion.  
- Cite prior work connecting consensus acceleration to heavy-ball/Nesterov (e.g., Rabatt, Nowak, Bucklew).  
- Add missing references: EXTRA (Shi et al.) and “Fast distributed gradient methods” (Jakovetić et al.).  
- Proposition 3 seems trivial and appears to be a supplementary observation rather than a core contribution.  
- Rate O(D log(D/ε)) is only a logarithmic improvement over diffusive rates and is weaker than O(D log(1/ε)) from Olshevsky; position the contribution relative to that prior result.  
- Novelty concern: the auxiliary two-step recursion resembles standard multi-step acceleration (Nesterov/shift-register) for linear dynamics; clarify conceptual novelty beyond rederiving known accelerated rates.  
- Explicitly discuss worst-case dependence: improvements rely on spectral information but worst-case remains O(D)=O(n).  
- Notational clarity: state that messages are indexed on directed edges despite undirected graph assumption and explain rationale.  
- Minor typo: page 6, line 220 “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian.”

Pros for acceptance:
- Clean analysis tying Min-Sum Splitting to a known accelerated linear system.  
- Constructive connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step optimization.  
- Generality across graph topologies and square-root improvement on geometric graphs.

Cons for acceptance:
- Incremental improvement compared to known O(D log(1/ε)) results and sometimes worse by a log factor.  
- Several important related works omitted or under-discussed, weakening positioning.  
- Main technique reduces to analyzing a linear two-step recursion; limited conceptual advance beyond known accelerations.  
- Some clarity and exposition issues.

Recommendation:
- Borderline: analysis is correct and perspective is interesting, but practical/significant improvement is limited and literature review needs strengthening.
