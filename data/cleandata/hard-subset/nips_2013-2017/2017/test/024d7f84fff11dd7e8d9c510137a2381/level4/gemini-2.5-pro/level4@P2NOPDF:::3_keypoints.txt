- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting for distributed consensus optimization and an auxiliary linear process to prove convergence for quadratic objectives.  
- Contributions: new auxiliary-process-based analysis, a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence rate, and links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step optimization methods.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, clearly written, and theoretically sound; ideas are interesting and connections insightful.  
- Main weakness: limited practical relevance discussion, analysis restricted to quadratics, and unclear importance for the ML community; manuscript needs proofreading.  
- Practicality/applicability: authors should explain when Min-Sum Splitting outperforms (sub)gradient methods and discuss communication, computation, and assumption limitations.  
- Auxiliary process intuition: request for clearer intuition and justification for the construction and effectiveness of the auxiliary linear process.  
- Generalization to ML: need discussion of extending beyond quadratic objectives (e.g., logistic regression, SVM) and explicit articulation of relevance to NeurIPS readership.  
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 2/3 missing definitions and initializations (R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0); clarify which variables correspond to the auxiliary process.  
- Potential terminology error: likely misuse of "Laplace multipliers" instead of "Lagrange multipliers."  
- Missing citations: add foundational consensus/Distributed optimization works (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Nedić & Özgür 2009; Wei Shi et al. 2015 EXTRA) and cite Polyak (heavy-ball) and Nesterov (acceleration) where appropriate.  
- Typos/errors: numerous minor grammatical and wording mistakes listed (e.g., "theirs neighbors"→"their neighbors", "double-stochastic"→"doubly-stochastic", "can be casted"→"can be cast", etc.).  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical paper whose impact would be substantially improved by addressing practicality, broadening scope, and strengthening ML connections.
