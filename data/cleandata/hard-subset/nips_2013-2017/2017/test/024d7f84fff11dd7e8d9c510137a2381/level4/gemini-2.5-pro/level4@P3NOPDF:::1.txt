This paper proposes an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, called min-sum splitting, and applies it to the distributed consensus problem.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, with a clear and logical presentation. The authors do an excellent job of positioning their contribution within the context of the relevant state of the art, making the novelty and potential impact of their work easy to appreciate. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this specific subfield), the results presented are novel and represent a qualitative advance. A particularly strong and insightful contribution is the novel connection established between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

I have one point of clarification regarding the presentation. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function that underpins the min-sum splitting scheme, the text states: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,..." This description is unclear, as $\Gamma_{vw}$ and $\delta$ are defined on the previous page as real-valued parameters. It is not obvious how a potential term can be split into a non-integer number of new terms. I would be grateful if the authors could clarify the precise meaning of this "splitting" operation, as it is central to understanding the proposed method. This may be a simple matter of rephrasing or clarifying the notation.
