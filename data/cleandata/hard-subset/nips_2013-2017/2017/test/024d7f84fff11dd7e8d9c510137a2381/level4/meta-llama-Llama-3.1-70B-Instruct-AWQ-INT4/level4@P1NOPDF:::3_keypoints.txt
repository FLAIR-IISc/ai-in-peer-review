- Paper presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using reparameterization from Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013).
- Establishes convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on quadratic consensus objectives via an auxiliary linear process analysis.
- Main contributions: new proof technique with an auxiliary process, an improved Min-Sum protocol with faster convergence, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Paper is well-written and proofs of Propositions 1, 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.
- Manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (many specific line-by-line corrections listed).
- Some notation and algorithm quantities are unclear or uninitialized (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0), and the auxiliary process variables should be explicitly linked to Algorithm 3.
- Possible terminology confusion: "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers" may be incorrect where "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" were intended.
- Several relevant references on (sub)gradient and distributed consensus methods are missing and should be cited (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Srinivasan et al. 2009, Wei Shi 2015).
- Missing historical references to optimization acceleration work (e.g., Polyak’s heavy-ball, Nesterov’s methods).
- Requests for authors to comment on practicality: when to use this method versus other distributed consensus algorithms.
- Requests to clarify limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and intuition behind employing the auxiliary process.
- Asks whether the framework can extend beyond quadratic objectives to more general consensus problems common in ML.
- Requests clearer motivation and explanation of the relevance and importance of the approach for the Machine Learning community.
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting with novel analysis and connections, but lacking practical discussion, intuition, and ML-oriented justification.
