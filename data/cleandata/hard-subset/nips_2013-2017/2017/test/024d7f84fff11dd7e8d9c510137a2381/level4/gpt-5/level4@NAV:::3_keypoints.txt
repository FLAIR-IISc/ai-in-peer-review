- Introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing scheme for distributed consensus and derives convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: (i) new auxiliary-process-based analysis of Min-Sum Splitting, (ii) a faster Min-Sum consensus protocol, (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly presented and the manuscript is generally easy to follow.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1, 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Manuscript contains numerous typographical and grammatical errors (many line-specific corrections listed by the reviewer).  
- Several algorithmic quantities are undefined or uninitialized (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0 in Algorithm 2) and the auxiliary linear process variables are not clearly identified in Algorithm 3.  
- Possible misuse of terminology: reviewer asks if "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers" should be "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers".  
- Missing citations to relevant prior work on (sub)gradient consensus methods and decentralized algorithms (examples listed: Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Srinivasan et al. 2009, Wei Shi 2015) and to momentum methods (Polyak, Nesterov).  
- Reviewer requests discussion of the method’s practicality and when it should be preferred over other distributed consensus methods.  
- Reviewer requests explicit discussion of the limitations of Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Reviewer asks for intuition motivating the use of the auxiliary process in the analysis.  
- Reviewer asks whether the framework can be extended beyond quadratic consensus objectives to more general ML-related consensus problems.  
- Overall: a solid theoretical contribution with interesting ideas, but needs more practical discussion, intuitive explanation, and clearer justification of relevance to the ML community.
