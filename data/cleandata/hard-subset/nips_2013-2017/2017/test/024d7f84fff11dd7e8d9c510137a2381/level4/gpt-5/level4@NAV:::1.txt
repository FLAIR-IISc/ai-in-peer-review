This work employs an accelerated form of the min-sum algorithm—min-sum splitting—to address the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is clearly written, with the contribution well positioned relative to the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not an expert in this area), the results are novel and constitute a qualitative advance. In particular, it establishes a new link between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

There is one aspect that is not entirely clear. On page 4, when presenting the equivalent objective minimized by the min-sum algorithm to obtain the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors write: "...splitting each term $\phi{vw}$ into $\Gamma{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phiv$ into $\delta$ terms,..." However, it is unclear how to interpret this, since $\delta$ and $\Gamma{vw}$, as defined on the previous page, are real numbers.
