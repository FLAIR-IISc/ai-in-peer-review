- Proposes a Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for distributed consensus and shows a modified Min-Sum yields convergence.  
- Proves that with proper tuning Min-Sum Splitting attains subdiffusive accelerated convergence matching shift-register rates.  
- Thorough technical analysis of the algorithm and its convergence behavior.  
- Establishes connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order convex optimization methods.  
- Introduces an auxiliary process to track node-wise algorithm evolution and uses it as a new proof technique.  
- Improvement in convergence is limited: rate D log(D/ε) is not a clear advance over prior D log(1/ε) results.  
- Important references in consensus optimization are omitted; literature review needs expansion.  
- Analysis is specialized to consensus (a linear system), making the novelty and broader contribution unclear.  
- Paper is technically sound but would benefit from a more comprehensive and up-to-date literature survey.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains some long, convoluted sentences that hinder readability.  
- Organization and introduction of the algorithm are clear, but the literature review and some sections need concision.  
- More figures and illustrations would help explain concepts and algorithms.  
- Originality: presents Min-Sum Splitting and some novel links, but auxiliary-process idea is not new and more innovation is desirable.  
- Significance is limited by modest rate improvement and narrow problem scope, reducing broad interest.  
- Acceptance pros: solid analysis and useful connections to related techniques; cons: limited practical improvement, missing citations, and restricted scope.
