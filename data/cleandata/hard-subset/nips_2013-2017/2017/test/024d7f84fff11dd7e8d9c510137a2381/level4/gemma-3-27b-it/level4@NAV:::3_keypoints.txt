- Paper introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting algorithm for distributed consensus with quadratic objectives using reparametrization from Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013).  
- Main technical novelty: a novel proof technique using an auxiliary linear process to analyze Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Claims improved convergence rates for Min-Sum Splitting on consensus compared to existing results.  
- Connects the method to convex optimization concepts (lifted Markov chains, multi-step methods).  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly stated; overall writing is accessible.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Paper contains multiple typographical and grammatical errors throughout (detailed line-by-line list provided).  
- Some algorithmic quantities are insufficiently explained (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, and uninitialized \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0 in Algorithm 2).  
- The correspondence between the auxiliary linear process variables and Algorithm 3 should be made explicit.  
- Bibliography is incomplete; missing key references for (sub)gradient methods and algorithmic connections (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989, Srinivasan et al. 2009, Shi 2015, Polyak, Nesterov).  
- Specific ambiguous phrasing: clarify "The original literature…" and confirm whether "Lagrangian/Lagrange multipliers" was intended instead of "Laplacian/Laplace multipliers."  
- Questions for authors: discuss practical implications and use cases compared to other distributed consensus methods.  
- Questions for authors: state inherent limitations of Min-Sum Splitting and intuition behind the auxiliary process.  
- Questions for authors: clarify whether the framework generalizes beyond quadratic objectives to more complex ML consensus problems.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically intriguing and connected to known methods, but needs clearer practical relevance, additional intuition, completeness in exposition, and bibliographic fixes.
