- Paper is well-written but claimed improvement is modest and needs stronger justification.
- Claim that graphs with “good expansion” yield dimension-independent complexity is unclear; complete graph example is trivial and unrepresentative—authors must specify which graph classes and what “good” expansion means.
- Missing citation and comparison to Olshevsky (2015); that work gives competitive or superior bounds undermining novelty.
- Key consensus-optimization references are omitted (Shi et al. 2015; Scaman et al. 2018); state-of-the-art algorithms and linear rates should be summarized and compared.
- Discussion of lifted graphs/Markov chains omits Franca and Bento (2018), which is highly relevant.
- Well-known consensus facts in the manuscript should cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew (2004); connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak are already in the literature.
- Additional missing consensus-optimization references: Shi et al. “EXTRA” (2015) and Jakovetic et al. (2014).
- Proposition 3 is trivial and reads as a supplementary rather than core contribution.
- Claimed rate D·log(D/ε) offers little improvement over Olshevsky’s D·log(1/ε) and is worse in worst-case graphs where D = Θ(n).
- Authors’ “auxiliary message-passing” likely reintroduces Nesterov acceleration; since consensus reduces to a linear system, the novelty of the contribution is unclear.
- Any apparent gains likely stem from refined spectral-gap handling, but worst-case complexity remains O(n), contradicting dimension-independence claims.
- Technical issues: use of directed edges for a simple graph is confusing (line 243), and a typo ("Laplacian" → "Lagrangian") on line 220.
- After rebuttal some concerns were addressed but overall evaluation unchanged; contribution still lacks sufficient novelty relative to existing accelerated methods.
