

This paper applies an accelerated min-sum variant, termed min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is well-written, with its contribution clearly contextualized within the current state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not a specialist in this area), the results represent a novel and qualitatively significant advancement, particularly through the introduction of a new connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques. However, a technical clarification is needed: on page 4, when describing the objective function reformulation for min-sum splitting, the authors state that "each term $\phi_{vw}$ is split into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms." This is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ (defined as real numbers on the preceding page) cannot represent counts of terms.
