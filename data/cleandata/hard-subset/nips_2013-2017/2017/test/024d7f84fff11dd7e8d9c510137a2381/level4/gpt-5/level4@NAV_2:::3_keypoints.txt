- Studies accelerated Min-Sum message-passing (Min-Sum Splitting) for consensus and proves convergence-rate guarantees on quadratic objectives using an auxiliary linear process.  
- Principal contributions: a new proof technique based on an auxiliary node-level process; a Min-Sum protocol with faster convergence than prior bounds; explicit links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly articulated; paper is generally well organized and readable.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear sound.  
- The manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (specific line-by-line corrections provided by reviewer).  
- Algorithms 2 and 3 lack definitions/initializations for several quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) and need clear indication of which variables correspond to the auxiliary node-level dynamics.  
- Possible terminology error: reviewer suggests "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers" may be intended instead of "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers."  
- Missing citations on (sub)gradient and consensus optimization literature (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Wei Shi et al. 2015) and foundational works, plus Polyak and Nesterov for acceleration.  
- Reviewer requests discussion of when this approach is practical and preferable relative to other distributed consensus methods.  
- Reviewer asks authors to state the main limitations of Min-Sum Splitting in this setting.  
- Reviewer requests intuition for why the auxiliary linear process captures and simplifies Min-Sum Splitting analysis.  
- Reviewer asks whether the framework can extend beyond quadratic objectives to more general consensus formulations common in machine learning.  
- Summary: interesting theoretical contribution situating Min-Sum Splitting among lifted chains and multi-step accelerations, but needs clearer exposition of algorithm variables/initializations, discussion of practical relevance/limitations, and additional references.
