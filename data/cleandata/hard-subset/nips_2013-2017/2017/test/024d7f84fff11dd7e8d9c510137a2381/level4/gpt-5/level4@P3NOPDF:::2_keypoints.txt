- Paper studies convergence of min-sum splitting for average consensus and proposes an auxiliary message-passing scheme; clearly written but improvement is modest and some related work is missing.  
- Claims of optimality for “graphs with good expansion” require clarification and precise definition.  
- Using the complete graph as an example is uninformative; complete graphs are too special to represent typical behavior.  
- Specify which graph families (e.g., expanders, bounded-diameter, Ramanujan) and spectral/diameter/ conductance conditions yield dimension-independent complexity.  
- Literature comparison must include Olshevsky’s result (D·log(1/ε)) and correctly report its competitive or superior performance to the claimed D·log(D/ε).  
- Provide a head-to-head comparison across canonical graphs (paths, cycles, grids, expanders) against existing bounds.  
- The auxiliary message-passing scheme appears closely related to Nesterov/heavy-ball acceleration on linear systems; clarify novelty and differences.  
- If the method is essentially an accelerated variant of min-sum, the contribution is mainly analysis of acceleration rather than a novel algorithm.  
- The claimed D·log(D/ε) improvement is not compelling in worst-case (D ≍ n) where it yields weaker O(n log(n/ε)) bounds than known linear-time results.  
- Discuss whether the bound can match O(n log(1/ε)) uniformly or identify structural advantages exploited by your method.  
- Several key references on accelerated consensus and decentralized optimization are missing and should be cited and discussed.  
- Report best-known linear convergence rates from prior works and position your results in terms of spectral gap, condition number, and diameter.  
- Clarify precise spectral conditions and parameter choices under which complexity becomes dimension-free.  
- If results rely on lifted Markov chains or refined spectral arguments, explain connections to known lifting/acceleration techniques and optimality within model classes.  
- Proposition 3 seems minor and could be moved to an appendix or reframed as supplementary.  
- Provide explicit constants in convergence bounds to enable practical comparisons with other accelerated schemes.  
- Explain whether auxiliary messages require extra memory, synchronization, or knowledge of global parameters and compare these requirements to prior methods.  
- Rewrite the optimality paragraph for precision, avoid using complete graphs as the principal example, and cite prior connections to heavy-ball/Nesterov.  
- Fix presentation issues: reconcile “simple” graph vs. directed-edge usage and correct the “Laplacian”/“Lagrangian” typo.  
- Strengths: clear writing, careful spectral analysis, and potentially practical simple acceleration; Weaknesses: incremental theory, insufficient benchmarking, and missing citations.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger related-work discussion, clearer positioning vs. Olshevsky and decentralized optimization, and clearer novelty/graph-class claims.
