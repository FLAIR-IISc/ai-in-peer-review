1. Presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus, deriving convergence rates for quadratic objectives using reparametrization from Ruozzi & Tatikonda (2013).  
2. Core analytical tool is an auxiliary linear process that tracks the Min-Sum Splitting evolution.  
3. Main contributions: (i) novel analysis via an auxiliary process, (ii) a Min-Sum protocol with improved convergence, and (iii) connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
4. Motivation and contributions are clearly articulated; paper is generally accessible and proofs of Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
5. Paper contains numerous typos and grammatical errors (many specific line corrections listed by the reviewer).  
6. Possible terminology errors: reviewer questions use of “Laplacian” and “Laplace multipliers” vs. likely “Lagrangian” and “Lagrange multipliers.”  
7. Algorithm descriptions lack clarity: R_{vw}, r_{vw} in Algorithm 2 are not explained, \hat{R}^0 and \hat{r}^0 not initialized, and the mapping from Algorithm 3 variables to the auxiliary process is not explicit.  
8. Missing citations on (sub)gradient consensus methods and acceleration literature (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Srinivasan et al. 2009; Shi 2015; Polyak; Nesterov).  
9. Reviewer requests discussion of practicality: scenarios where the method outperforms other distributed consensus techniques and identification of limitations.  
10. Reviewer asks for intuition behind using the auxiliary process within Min-Sum Splitting.  
11. Reviewer asks whether the framework can be generalized beyond quadratic objectives to more complex ML consensus problems.  
12. Reviewer requests clarification of why the approach is relevant and of interest to the Machine Learning community.  
13. Overall: interesting theoretical contribution with a novel proof technique and useful connections, but needs more practical discussion, intuition, references, and clarity.
