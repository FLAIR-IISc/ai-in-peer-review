- Misleading optimality claim: asserting O(D log(D/ε)) is "optimal for graphs with good expansion" is unsupported and incorrect for examples like the complete graph.  
- Unclear scope: the paper fails to specify which graph classes achieve dimension-independent complexity and does not define "good expansion" precisely.  
- Dependence on diameter: the bound O(D log(D/ε)) still depends on D (which can be Θ(n)), so it is not dimension-independent for worst-case graphs.  
- Worse than prior work in worst case: when D = Θ(n) the bound becomes O(n log(n/ε)), which is worse than Olshevsky’s O(n log(1/ε)).  
- Limited novelty: the "auxiliary message-passing scheme" appears to be a standard application of Nesterov acceleration to min-sum, offering little new insight beyond routine linear-system analysis.  
- Key literature omissions: fails to cite and compare to Olshevsky (2015), Shi et al. (2015), Scaman et al. (2017), Jakovetic et al. (2014), Franca & Bento (2018), among others.  
- Missing comparisons: the manuscript should explicitly compare its rate to Olshevsky’s O(n log(1/ε)) and situate itself relative to existing consensus methods.  
- Trivial or misplaced results: Proposition 3 is trivial given Lemma 2 and should be omitted or moved to supplementary material.  
- Missing/incorrect citations: the Heavy Ball/Nesterov connection is standard and needs citation (e.g., Rabbat et al., 2004).  
- Presentation errors: confusing use of directed edges for a "simple graph" (line 243) and a typo "Laplacian" vs. "Lagrangian" (line 220).  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: technically competent but lacking meaningful novelty or tighter bounds; reject until paper clarifies assumptions, addresses literature gaps, and demonstrates real improvement over Olshevsky.
