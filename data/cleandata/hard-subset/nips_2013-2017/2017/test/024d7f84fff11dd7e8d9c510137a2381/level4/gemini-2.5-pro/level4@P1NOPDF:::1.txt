This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, called min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is very well written, and its contribution is clearly placed within the context of the current state of the art. While I am not an expert on this specific topic, the results appear to be novel and constitute a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper presents a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

There is one detail in the presentation that was unclear. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function that the min-sum algorithm minimizes to yield the min-sum splitting scheme, the authors write: "...splitting each term $\phi_{vw}$ into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ into $\delta$ terms,...". However, as introduced on the previous page, $\Gamma_{vw}$ and $\delta$ are real numbers. It is not clear what it means to split a term into a non-integer number of new terms, so this description is confusing. This point would benefit from clarification.
