1. Improvement marginal: claimed rate D log(D/ε) only inserts log(D) inside the log compared to prior D log(1/ε), so worst-case complexity remains O(n) and can be worse when D = Θ(n).

2. Novelty limited: the auxiliary message-passing is essentially a Nesterov-style momentum applied to min-sum, a standard acceleration for linear consensus, making the conceptual contribution unclear.

3. Optimality claim weak: using the complete graph as an example is inappropriate (consensus in one step); the authors must precisely define the class of “good expansion” graphs for which dimension-independent complexity holds.

4. Literature review incomplete: missing key references in consensus optimization (Shi et al., Scaman et al., EXTRA, Jakovetic et al.), lifted graphs (Franca & Bento), and momentum/method connections (e.g., Rabbat et al.).

5. Proposition 3 marginal: the result appears direct and its significance to the paper’s main argument is not clearly justified.

6. Notation inconsistency: graph is described as simple but edge notation suggests directed edges—needs clarification.

7. Minor typo: page 6, line 220 — “Laplacian” should be “Lagrangian.”

8. After rebuttal: author clarifications noted but reviewer’s assessment and score remain unchanged.
