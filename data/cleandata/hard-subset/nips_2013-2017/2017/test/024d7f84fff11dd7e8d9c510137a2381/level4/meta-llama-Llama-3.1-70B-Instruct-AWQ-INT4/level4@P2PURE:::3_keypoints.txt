- Paper proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for consensus in distributed optimization and analyzes convergence for quadratic objectives.  
- Main analytical tool: construction of an auxiliary linear process that tracks the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.  
- Contributions claimed: new proof technique via auxiliary process, faster Min-Sum protocol for consensus, and links to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Writing is clear and proofs of Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 appear correct, but the manuscript contains several typos and grammatical errors (detailed list provided by reviewer).  
- Specific typographical corrections are provided for many lines (examples include “their neighbors”, “doubly-stochastic”, “can be cast as”, etc.).  
- Algorithms are under-specified: several quantities (e.g., R{vw}, r{vw}, \hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) are not explained or initialized.  
- The link between the auxiliary linear process and the variables in Algorithm 3 is not clearly stated and should be made explicit.  
- Several relevant references are missing and should be added (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Incremental stochastic subgradient work 2009; Wei Shi 2015; Polyak (Heavy-ball); Nesterov).  
- Reviewer asks for discussion of practicality: when to use this method versus other distributed consensus methods.  
- Reviewer requests clarification of limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method.  
- Reviewer asks for intuition behind using the auxiliary process in the analysis.  
- Reviewer asks whether the framework can be extended beyond quadratic consensus problems to more general ML settings.  
- Reviewer requests explicit motivation and justification of the approach’s relevance to the Machine Learning community.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting with correct proofs and useful connections, but lacks practical discussion, intuition, broader applicability, and some presentation/ citation fixes.
