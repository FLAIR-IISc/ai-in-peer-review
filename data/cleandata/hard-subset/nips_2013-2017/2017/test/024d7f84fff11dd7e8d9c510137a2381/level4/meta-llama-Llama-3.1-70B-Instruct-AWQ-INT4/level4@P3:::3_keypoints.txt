- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, analyzing quadratic objectives via an auxiliary linear process.  
- Contribution: introduces a new proof technique using an auxiliary process to analyze Min-Sum Splitting.  
- Contribution: designs a Min-Sum protocol that achieves improved convergence rates over prior results.  
- Contribution: establishes connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.  
- Strength: clear motivation and well-written; proofs of Propositions 1 & 2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains several typos and grammatical mistakes.  
- Missing references: (sub)gradient consensus methods and classical acceleration work (Polyak Heavy-ball, Nesterov).  
- Minor issue: probable misuse of "Laplacian"/"Laplace multipliers" instead of "Lagrangian"/"Lagrange multipliers".  
- Minor issue: algorithmic quantities are not always defined (e.g., R_{vw}, r_{vw}) and some variables (\hat{R}^0, \hat{r}^0) appear uninitialized.  
- Minor issue: unclear which variables represent the auxiliary linear process in Algorithm 3.  
- Suggestion: discuss practicality and scenarios where this method is preferable to other distributed consensus methods.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting approach.  
- Suggestion: provide intuition for using the auxiliary process and for the main results.  
- Suggestion: clarify relevance and importance of the approach for the Machine Learning community.  
- Suggestion: consider extending the framework to more general consensus problems encountered in ML.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting and novel but lacking practicality, intuition, and explicit ML relevance.
