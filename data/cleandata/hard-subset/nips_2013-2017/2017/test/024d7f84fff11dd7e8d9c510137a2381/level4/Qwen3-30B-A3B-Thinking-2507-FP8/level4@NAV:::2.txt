

This paper investigates the convergence rate of a min-sum splitting method for the average consensus problem. While the paper is generally well-written, the claimed improvement appears marginal. Detailed comments follow.

(1) The statement that "this rate is optimal for graphs with good expansion properties, such as the complete graph" is misleading. For complete graphs, convergence occurs in exactly one step (not a linear rate of zero), and complete graphs are an atypical special case. The paper must specify which general graph class exhibits dimension-independent complexity and clarify the definition of "good expansion properties."

(2) The literature review should include Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus on Fixed Graphs and Implications for Decentralized Optimization and Multi-Agent Control." The convergence rate in this reference should be properly contextualized (see Comment 8), as it achieves a competitive or superior bound compared to the submission.

(3) At the top of page 2, key references for consensus optimization must be cited, including Shi et al.'s "On the Linear Convergence of the ADMM in Decentralized Consensus Optimization" and Scaman et al.'s "Optimal algorithms for smooth and strongly convex distributed optimization in networks." The authors should also summarize state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear convergence rates.

(4) The discussion of lifted graphs and Markov chains omits Franca and Bento's "Markov Chain Lifting and Distributed ADMM," a highly relevant work.

(5) The last paragraph of page 5 states a well-known fact; it should reference Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew's "Generalized consensus computation in networked systems with erasure links." The connection to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak variants is established in the field and requires acknowledgment.

(6) Critical omissions include Shi et al.'s "Extra: An exact first-order algorithm for decentralized consensus optimization" and Jakovetic et al.'s "Fast distributed gradient methods."

(7) Proposition 3 appears trivial and constitutes a minor supplementary contribution.

(8) The claimed rate \(D \log(D/\varepsilon)\) does not significantly improve upon \(D \log(1/\varepsilon)\), achieved in Olshevsky's work (see Comment 2). For worst-case graphs (\(D \sim n\)), the bound is inferior to Olshevsky's result.

(9) Olshevsky's paper attains its bound via Nesterov acceleration. The authors' "Auxiliary message-passing scheme" likely rederives this acceleration for the min-sum algorithm. Since consensus analysis reduces to linear systems (a standard problem), the paper's contribution is unclear under this interpretation.

(10) The marginal improvement may stem from refined spectral gap handling, but the worst-case bound remains \(O(n)\) (as \(O(n) = O(D)\) for all graphs with \(n\) nodes), aligning with known limits.

(11) Line 243 (page 6) uses directed edges for a simple graph, causing confusion.

(12) Line 220 (page 6) contains a typo: "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian."

After rebuttal: The reviewer is satisfied with the authors' response, but the evaluation score remains unchanged.
