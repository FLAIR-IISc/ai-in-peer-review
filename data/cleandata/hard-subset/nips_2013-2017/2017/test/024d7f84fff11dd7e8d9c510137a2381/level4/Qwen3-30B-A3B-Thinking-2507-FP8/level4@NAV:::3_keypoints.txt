- Paper introduces an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting protocol for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to derive convergence rates for quadratic objectives.
- Main contributions: novel auxiliary-process-based analysis of Min-Sum Splitting, improved convergence rates for consensus, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex optimization methods.
- Writing is clear and motivation/contributions are well-defined, and proofs of Propositions 1, 2 and Theorem 1 are verified correct.
- Manuscript contains numerous typographical and grammatical errors that need correction (many line-specific suggestions provided).
- Algorithmic descriptions are inconsistent: key quantities (e.g., R_vw, r_vw) are unclear and initializations (e.g., ĤR^0, Ĥr^0) are missing.
- The correspondence between variables in the auxiliary linear process and Algorithm 3 is not explicitly stated and should be clarified.
- Possible misuse of terminology: "Laplacian" and "Laplace multipliers" may be intended as "Lagrangian" and "Lagrange multipliers."
- Several relevant references on (sub)gradient methods and accelerated methods are omitted and should be added (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram et al. 2009; Wei Shi et al. 2015; Polyak; Nesterov).
- The manuscript needs to contextualize "the original literature" and cite classical sources where appropriate.
- Authors should better explain the intuition behind the auxiliary process used in the analysis.
- The paper lacks discussion of practical applicability, comparative advantages over existing distributed consensus methods, and limitations of Min-Sum Splitting.
- It should investigate whether the framework extends beyond quadratic consensus problems common in machine learning.
- The relevance and significance of the work to the machine learning community should be explicitly justified.
- Overall assessment: theoretically sound and interesting, but needs improved exposition, practical discussion, missing references, and fixes to algorithmic/terminology clarity.
