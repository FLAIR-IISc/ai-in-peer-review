This paper presents an accelerated variant of the Min-Sum message-passing protocol for solving consensus problems in distributed optimization. The authors leverage reparametrization techniques from Ruozzi and Tatikonda [2013] and establish convergence rates for the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm applied to consensus problems with quadratic objective functions. A key analytical tool is the construction of an auxiliary linear process that tracks the evolution of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.

The main contributions of the paper are threefold: (i) a novel proof technique, based on the introduction of an auxiliary process, for analyzing the Min-Sum splitting algorithm; (ii) a Min-Sum protocol for consensus problems achieving improved convergence compared to previously known results; and (iii) a demonstration of the connection between the proposed method and lifted Markov chains and multi-step methods in convex optimization. 

The motivation and contributions are clearly articulated. The paper is generally well-written and accessible, although it contains several minor errors in grammar and typography (detailed below). The proofs of Propositions 1 and 2, and Theorem 1 appear correct.

**Questions for the Authors:**

While this is a strong theoretical contribution, addressing the following points would significantly enhance the paper's impact:

*   **Practicality:** Although this is a theoretical work, what commentary can the authors offer regarding the practical applicability of this method? In what scenarios would it be preferable to other established distributed consensus optimization methods?
*   **Limitations:** What are the inherent limitations of the Min-Sum Splitting method presented?
*   **Auxiliary Process Intuition:** What is the underlying intuition behind using the auxiliary process to analyze the Min-Sum Splitting method? A more intuitive explanation would be valuable.
*   **Generalization:** The current results focus on quadratic objective functions. Can this framework be extended to address more general consensus problems commonly encountered in Machine Learning?
*   **Relevance to Machine Learning:**  The authors should explicitly articulate the relevance of this work to the Machine Learning community and why this approach is of interest in that context.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper is a purely theoretical study that establishes convergence rates using a novel proof technique and highlights connections to existing methods. The ideas presented are interesting, but the paper would benefit from a discussion of the method’s practicality, a clearer explanation of the intuition behind the results, and a stronger justification of its importance for the Machine Learning community.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
