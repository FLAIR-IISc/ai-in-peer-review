- Proposes an accelerated Min-Sum message-passing variant for distributed consensus optimization using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process, analyzed for quadratic objectives.  
- Main contributions: a new auxiliary-process proof technique, a Min-Sum protocol with faster convergence than prior results, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step convex methods.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized; proofs of Propositions 1–2 and Theorem 1 appear correct.  
- Numerous typographical and grammatical errors throughout (examples: "theirs neighbors"→"their neighbors", "double-stochastic"→"doubly-stochastic", "can be casted"→"can be cast", etc.).  
- Technical ambiguities: likely misuse of "Laplacian"/"Laplace multipliers" vs "Lagrangian"/"Lagrange multipliers"; Algorithm 2 introduces R_{vw}, r_{vw} and inconsistent initialization of \hat{R}^0,\hat{r}^0 without explanation; Algorithm 3 should explicitly map variables to the auxiliary linear process.  
- Missing citations: (sub)gradient work (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram et al. 2009; Shi et al. 2015), Polyak’s Heavy-ball, Nesterov’s acceleration, and unspecified "original literature."  
- Key questions to address: when to prefer this method over EXTRA/D-ADMM, structural/topological limitations, intuitive explanation for auxiliary-process acceleration, extension beyond quadratic objectives (e.g., logistic or non-smooth losses), and explicit ML relevance.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons, concrete examples, and justification of utility in ML settings; theoretical novelty alone is insufficient for NeurIPS without practical context.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel proof framework, but practical applicability, unresolved technical issues, and insufficient ML relevance reduce the paper’s impact; addressing these points would substantially strengthen it.
