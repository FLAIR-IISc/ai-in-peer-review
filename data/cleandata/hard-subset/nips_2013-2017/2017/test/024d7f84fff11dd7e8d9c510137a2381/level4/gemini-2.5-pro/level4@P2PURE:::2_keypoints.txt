- Overall well-written but claimed improvements over existing results are marginal.
- Claim of optimality for "graphs with good expansion" is misleading; complete graph example is unrepresentative and authors should clarify which graph classes qualify and when complexity is node-count independent.
- Literature review is incomplete; must cite and discuss Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus..." which provides highly competitive bounds.
- Overlooks key consensus-optimization references (Shi et al., Scaman et al.); needs thorough comparison to state-of-the-art convergence rates.
- Missing citation on lifted graphs/Markov chain lifting: Franca and Bento's relevant work should be included.
- Last-paragraph result on page 5 is well-known; cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew and acknowledge existing links to acceleration methods (Heavy-ball, Nesterov, Polyak).
- Additional important consensus-optimization references omitted (EXTRA by Shi et al.; Fast distributed gradient methods by Jakovetic et al.).
- Proposition 3 is a straightforward, minor contribution.
- Main result O(D log(D/ε)) offers limited improvement over O(D log(1/ε)) from Olshevsky; the extra D inside the log is a drawback and can make the bound worse when D = Θ(n).
- Proposed auxiliary message-passing resembles Nesterov-style acceleration applied to min-sum; novelty is limited given consensus reduces to a linear system.
- Any marginal gains likely come from tighter spectral-gap handling but do not change worst-case O(n) bound for general graphs where D = Θ(n).
- Notational confusion on page 6 (line 243): text refers to a simple graph but notation uses directed edges.
- Typo on page 6 (line 220): "Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian".
- After rebuttal, clarifications appreciated but the reviewer's overall assessment and concerns remain unchanged.
