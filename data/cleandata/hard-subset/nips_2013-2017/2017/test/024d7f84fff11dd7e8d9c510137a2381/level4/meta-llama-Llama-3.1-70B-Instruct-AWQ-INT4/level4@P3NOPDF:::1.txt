This paper presents an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, called min-sum splitting, and applies it to the distributed consensus problem. The paper is well-written, with the contribution clearly placed in the context of the state of the art in the topic. To the best of my knowledge, although I am not an expert in this area, the results appear to be novel and constitute a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper establishes a novel connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov chain techniques.

One aspect of the presentation that requires clarification is on page 4, where the authors describe the equivalent objective function minimized by the min-sum algorithm to yield the min-sum splitting scheme. Specifically, the authors state that each term $\phi_{vw}$ is split into $\Gamma_{vw}$ terms, and each term $\phi_v$ is split into $\delta$ terms. However, this explanation is unclear, as $\delta$ and $\Gamma_{vw}$ are introduced on the previous page as real numbers, making it difficult to understand what the splitting of terms entails. Providing additional details or a clearer explanation of this step would strengthen the presentation.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field, and with some minor revisions to address the clarity issue mentioned above, it has the potential to be a strong addition to the conference program.
