- Claims marginal improvement: the D·log(D/ε) rate gives only a slight, logarithmic benefit over prior D·log(1/ε) bounds and can be no better (or worse) in worst-case D ≈ n.  
- Optimality assertion unclear: the claim of optimality for “graphs with good expansion” is unsupported; the complete-graph example is misleading and the class of “good” graphs must be precisely defined and justified.  
- Literature review incomplete: fails to compare adequately to Olshevsky’s “Linear Time Average Consensus…” which achieves nearly identical rates and uses similar acceleration.  
- Consensus-optimization literature omitted: key works (Shi et al. on ADMM and EXTRA, Scaman et al., Jakovetic et al.) and state-of-the-art rates are not cited or discussed.  
- Related techniques ignored: missing citation/discussion of Franca & Bento on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM; momentum–consensus connections (e.g., Rabbat et al.) are well-known and should be cited.  
- Limited technical novelty: the “auxiliary message-passing” appears to be a straightforward application of Nesterov-style acceleration to a linear consensus system, so novelty beyond prior accelerated approaches is not demonstrated.  
- Proposition 3 adds little: seems trivial and not materially valuable.  
- Graph notation ambiguous: paper calls the graph “simple” but notation suggests directed edges — clarify.
