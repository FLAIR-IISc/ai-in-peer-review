- Paper presents an accelerated Min-Sum Splitting variant for distributed consensus, using reparametrization and an auxiliary linear process to derive convergence rates for quadratic objectives.  
- Core contributions: novel auxiliary-process proof technique, a Min-Sum consensus protocol with improved convergence, and connections to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Motivation and contributions are clearly stated and the manuscript is generally well-structured.  
- Proofs of Propositions 1 and 2 and Theorem 1 appear technically sound.  
- Manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors that must be corrected (many specific line-level errors listed by reviewer).  
- Lines 220–221 likely misuse “Laplacian” and “Laplace multipliers”; should be “Lagrangian” and “Lagrange multipliers.”  
- Several important references on distributed consensus/optimization are missing (e.g., Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis 1989; Sundhar Ram et al. 2009; Shi et al. 2015).  
- Polyak’s heavy ball and Nesterov acceleration are mentioned without proper citations.  
- Authors should discuss practical applicability and scenarios where their method outperforms existing distributed consensus methods.  
- The limitations and scope of Min-Sum Splitting need to be clearly stated.  
- The intuition behind introducing and using the auxiliary process should be explained more thoroughly.  
- Clarify whether and how the framework can be extended to non-quadratic consensus problems common in machine learning.  
- Explicitly articulate the significance and impact of the results for the machine learning community.
