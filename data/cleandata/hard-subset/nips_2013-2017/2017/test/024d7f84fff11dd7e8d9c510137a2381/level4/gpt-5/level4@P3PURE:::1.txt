This paper applies an accelerated variant of the min-sum algorithm, min-sum splitting, to the distributed consensus problem. The manuscript is very well written and situates the contribution clearly within the state of the art. To the best of my knowledge (though I am not a specialist in this specific area), the results are novel and represent a qualitative advance. In particular, the paper establishes a new connection between min-sum algorithms and lifted Markov-chain techniques.

One point in the presentation is unclear. On page 4, when describing the equivalent objective function minimized by min-sum to obtain the min-sum splitting scheme, the paper states: “...splitting each term phi_vw into Gamma_vw terms, and each term phi_v into delta terms,...” It is not clear what this means, because delta and Gamma_vw, as defined on the preceding page, are real-valued parameters. The phrase “into X terms” suggests an integer multiplicity or replication of factors, which is at odds with delta, Gamma_vw ∈ R. If the intent is instead to reweight the factors (e.g., replace phi_v with delta · phi_v and phi_vw with Gamma_vw · phi_vw), please state this explicitly and provide the precise algebraic form of the modified objective. If the intent is truly to “split” into multiple subfactors, please clarify how non-integer multiplicities are handled (e.g., via a decomposition into scaled subpotentials) and specify any constraints on delta and Gamma_vw (positivity, normalization) required for the algorithm and analysis. As written, the construction that yields the min-sum splitting scheme is ambiguous.
