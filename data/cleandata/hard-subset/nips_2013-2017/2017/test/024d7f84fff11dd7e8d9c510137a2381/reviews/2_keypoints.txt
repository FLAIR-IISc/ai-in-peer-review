- Overall: Studies min-sum splitting for average consensus; paper reads fine but claimed improvement is not impressive.
- Claim about complete graphs is misleading—complete graphs converge in one step; authors must clarify which graph classes yield dimension-independent complexity and define "good" graphs.
- Missing citation and comparison to Olshevsky's "Linear Time Average Consensus…" whose convergence rate is competitive or better.
- Missing important consensus-optimization references (Shi et al. on ADMM linear convergence; Scaman et al.) and reporting of state-of-the-art algorithms and their linear rates.
- Omitted related work on Markov chain lifting and distributed ADMM by Franca and Bento.
- Last paragraph of p.5 states a long-known fact; should cite Rabbat, Nowak, and Bucklew and acknowledge known connections to Heavy Ball/Nesterov/Polyak.
- Additional ignored references on consensus optimization (EXTRA by Shi et al.; Fast distributed gradient methods by Jakovetic et al.).
- Proposition 3 appears trivial and only a supplementary contribution.
- Claimed rate D log(D/ε) offers little improvement over D log(1/ε) from Olshevsky; in worst case (D ~ n) it is even worse.
- The proposed "auxiliary message-passing" likely mirrors Nesterov acceleration on min-sum; contribution is unclear since consensus analysis reduces to a linear system.
- Any gains likely stem from spectral-gap handling; worst-case bound remains O(n) because O(n)=O(D) across all n-node graphs.
- Line 243 (p.6): using directed edges on a simple graph is confusing and should be corrected.
- Line 220 (p.6): typo—"Laplacian" should be "Lagrangian."
