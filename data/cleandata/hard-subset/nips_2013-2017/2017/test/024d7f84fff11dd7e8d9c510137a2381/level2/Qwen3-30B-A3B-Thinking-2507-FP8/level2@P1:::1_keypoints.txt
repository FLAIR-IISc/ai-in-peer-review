- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing method that achieves accelerated convergence for consensus.  
- Shows ordinary Min-Sum fails to converge on graphs with cycles, while Min-Sum Splitting converges with proper tuning and attains subdiffusive rates.  
- Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum schemes, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Provides the first proof that tuning directionality in Belief Propagation protocols can produce acceleration.  
- Introduces a novel auxiliary-process proof technique (Algorithm 3) to track and analyze convergence.  
- Well-motivated work addressing the fundamental consensus problem in distributed optimization.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous; Theorem 1 clearly characterizes convergence under parameter tuning.  
- Main strength is the interdisciplinary theoretical insight linking message-passing and acceleration techniques.  
- The auxiliary-process proof is elegant and clarifies the pathway to accelerated rates.  
- Demonstrates acceleration mechanisms paralleling dependence on graph diameter and eigenvalues.  
- Notation is dense in places, notably in definitions of K̂(δ,Γ) and K(δ,Γ).  
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why the specific parameter choice (δ = 1, Γ = γW) is optimal.  
- Practical concern: the γ that yields subdiffusive rates depends on global graph properties, with no discussion of implementation without global knowledge.  
- Would benefit from a brief discussion of practical implementation implications and how to choose parameters in distributed settings.  
- Well-written and organized overall, though some sections need more intuitive explanations of mathematical constructs.  
- Properly situates and distinguishes itself from prior work (e.g., [27]) with appropriate citations.  
- Significant contribution: achieves O(D log(D/ε)) on cycles and tori, a square-root improvement over diffusive methods.  
- Technically sound with rigorous supplementary proofs and appropriate venue suitability (NIPS).
