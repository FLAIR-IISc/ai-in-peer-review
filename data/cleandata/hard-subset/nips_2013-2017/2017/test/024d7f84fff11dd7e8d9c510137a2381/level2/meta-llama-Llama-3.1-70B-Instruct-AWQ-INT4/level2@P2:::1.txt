The paper "Accelerated consensus via Min-Sum Splitting" presents a novel approach to solving the consensus problem in distributed optimization using the Min-Sum message-passing protocol. The authors show that a modified version of the Min-Sum algorithm, known as Splitting, can achieve convergence to the problem solution with accelerated rates. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence behavior of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm and establishes a connection with lifted Markov chains techniques and multi-step first-order methods in convex optimization.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the Min-Sum algorithm and its application to the consensus problem. The presentation of the main results is well-organized, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence behavior of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1. The authors establish a connection between the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm and lifted Markov chains techniques, which allows for the acceleration of the convergence rate.
2. The authors show that the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm can achieve a subdiffusive rate of convergence, which is a significant improvement over the diffusive rate of classical consensus algorithms.
3. The authors provide a new proof technique based on the introduction of an auxiliary process to track the evolution of the algorithm on the nodes.
