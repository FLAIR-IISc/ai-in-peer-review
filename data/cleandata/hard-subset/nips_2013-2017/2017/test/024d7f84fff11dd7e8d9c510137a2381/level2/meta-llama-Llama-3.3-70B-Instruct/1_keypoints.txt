- Proposes Min-Sum Splitting, a novel variant of Min-Sum message-passing applied to consensus in distributed optimization.
- Shows Min-Sum Splitting converges to the consensus solution with accelerated asymptotic rates.
- Claims splitting parameters are essential for convergence and can be tuned for acceleration.
- Argues the acceleration mechanism parallels lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.
- Supports claims with theoretical analysis, convergence proof, and characterization of asymptotic rate.
- Provides numerical experiments validating theoretical findings.
- Derives a linear message-passing update and introduces an auxiliary process to track evolution.
- Compares thoroughly with existing methods (lifted Markov chains, multi-step methods).
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear algorithm descriptions.
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge and discusses limitations, extensions, and applications.
- High potential usefulness for distributed machine learning and signal processing.
- High novelty due to a new algorithm and connections to existing acceleration techniques.
- Completeness is good, though some proofs and derivations are deferred to supplementary material.
- Key limitations: restricted to quadratic objectives and requires global graph information.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, given novelty, analysis, and overall contribution.
