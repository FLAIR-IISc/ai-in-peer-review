- Paper shows Min-Sum Splitting converges for consensus and attains accelerated (subdiffusive) rates matching shift-register methods, linking it to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Well-motivated problem, rigorous theoretical analysis, and a strong convergence result (Theorem 1).  
- Clear, logical writing and an extensive, knowledgeable related work section.  
- Practical implications underexplored: sensitivity to parameter tuning (particularly γ) and computational complexity vs other accelerated methods are not discussed.  
- Algorithm 3 feels detached from Algorithms 1/2; needs an intuitive derivation explaining the edge-to-node variable transition.  
- Heavy reliance on existing shift-register results; paper should briefly summarize key ideas to be more self-contained.  
- Connection to ADMM is promising but currently underdeveloped and should be expanded.  
- The term “global type of information” is vague; authors should specify examples (e.g., spectral gap, graph diameter).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound contribution with significant convergence improvement and insightful unifying perspective.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of practical considerations and some presentation/clarity issues reduce immediate impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing minor weaknesses (practical discussion, clarify Algorithm 3, elaborate ADMM link).
