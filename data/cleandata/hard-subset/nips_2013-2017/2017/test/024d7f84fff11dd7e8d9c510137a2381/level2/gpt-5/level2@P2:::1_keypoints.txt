- Paper studies Min-Sum Splitting for distributed averaging and proves a reparameterized Splitting variant converges where ordinary Min-Sum fails on loopy graphs.  
- With δ=1 and Γ=γW (γ tuned from W’s spectral gap) the algorithm attains accelerated, subdiffusive rates matching shift-register/Chebyshev accelerations.  
- Core technique: track edge-based linear messages via an auxiliary node-based 2-step linear iteration, showing Min-Sum Splitting implements a second-order shift-register consensus.  
- Results give asymptotic rate ρK matching known accelerations and O(D log(D/ε)) iterations on cycles/tori (square-root improvement over diffusive mixing).  
- Paper connects Min-Sum Splitting to lifted nonreversible Markov chains (edge directionality) and to accelerated first-order methods (momentum).  

- Strength: principled derivation equating Min-Sum Splitting to node-space second-order acceleration, explaining acceleration via lifting and momentum.  
- Strength: solid theoretical result (Theorem 1) with explicit convergence factor and parameter choice, using a clean 2n-by-2n auxiliary system.  
- Strength: broad relevance—consensus is a core primitive in distributed learning/optimization and message-passing acceleration matters for ML/graphical models.  
- Strength: clarifies and improves on prior Min-Sum analyses by providing general-graph explicit rates and simpler node-based spectral objects.  

- Major weakness: analysis restricted to quadratic averaging with synchronous, noise-free updates; broader convex settings are not treated despite hints in the intro.  
- Major weakness: parameter γ selection requires global spectral info (ρW), raising decentralized practicality and robustness concerns; no distributed tuning or sensitivity analysis.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation — missing experiments on canonical graphs, comparison to Chebyshev/Nesterov/ADMM/EXTRA, and robustness/transient analysis.  
- Major weakness: positivity condition in Proposition 1 (1 + δ Σ Γwv R̂t_wv > 0) is nontrivial and not shown to hold for all t or under perturbations.  

- Minor weakness: should explicitly write node-space two-step recurrence (shift-register form) and map α to γ to link to prior work.  
- Minor weakness: need discussion of per-iteration computational/communication costs (edge vs node state) relative to classical and second-order methods.  
- Minor weakness: add a simple illustrative figure/example mapping edge directionality to lifted nonreversible chains for intuition.  

- Quality: technically sound derivations and spectral analysis; assumptions (global tuning, synchrony) limit applicability.  
- Quality: robustness to asynchrony, delays, time-varying graphs, and noise is not addressed.  

- Clarity: paper is generally well-organized but notation is dense; a succinct node update summary and running example would improve readability.  
- Clarity: include a brief proof sketch of Theorem 1 in the main text for accessibility.  

- Originality: novel—first convergence rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting and a clean equivalence to shift-register acceleration.  
- Significance: solid theoretical advance for consensus; practical impact depends on addressing tuning and robustness.  

- Reason to accept: first rigorous accelerated rates for Min-Sum Splitting matching shift-register performance.  
- Reason to accept: clear, well-motivated connections to lifted chains and multi-step methods.  
- Reason to accept: provides general-graph rates with simpler spectral characterization than prior work.  

- Reason to reject: lack of experiments and no treatment of practical tuning/robustness.  
- Reason to reject: scope limited to quadratic consensus and synchronous settings.  

- Questions for authors: please write explicit node-space recurrence for xt and map parameters to γ and W.  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to γ mis-specification and can you propose distributed or adaptive estimators for ρW?  
- Questions for authors: does the positivity condition in Proposition 1 hold for all t under your initialization; please provide a short argument.  
- Questions for authors: are there extensions to mildly asynchronous updates or stochastic communication?  
- Questions for authors: how does per-iteration cost compare to Chebyshev-accelerated consensus and ADMM-based methods?  

- Suggestions: add experiments on canonical graphs comparing to Metropolis, Chebyshev/shift-register, ADMM/EXTRA, and test γ sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: include a proof sketch of Theorem 1 and explicitly state the xt recurrence in the main text.  
- Suggestions: discuss practical parameter selection and robustness (distributed tuning, mis-specification effects).  

- Minor comments: fix formatting glitches in references and footnotes; standardize notation for norms and spectral radius.  
- Minor comments: clarify assumptions on Γ’s diagonal entries and consistency of symbols.
