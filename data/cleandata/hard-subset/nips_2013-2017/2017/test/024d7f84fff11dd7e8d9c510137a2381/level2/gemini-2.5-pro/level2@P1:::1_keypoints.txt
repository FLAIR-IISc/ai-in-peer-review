1. Applies Min-Sum Splitting to distributed averaging and shows standard Min-Sum fails on graphs with cycles while Min-Sum Splitting converges with proper splitting parameters and attains accelerated, subdiffusive rates.

2. Establishes a novel connection between Min-Sum schemes, lifted Markov chains, and multi-step first-order optimization methods.

3. Provides the first convergence rate analysis for Min-Sum Splitting, proving a square-root improvement in convergence time over diffusive methods for certain topologies (e.g., cycles, tori).

4. Matches the convergence rate of shift-register methods for the studied graph classes.

5. Introduces an auxiliary node-based message-passing scheme that simplifies spectral analysis by reducing a 2m×2m edge-dynamics problem to a structured 2n×2n nodal problem.

6. Theoretical analysis appears sound and Theorem 1 rigorously establishes accelerated convergence.

7. The paper is very well written, clearly organized, and provides comprehensive background and motivation.

8. Main conceptual contribution (unifying three distinct approaches) is original and likely to stimulate further research.

9. Practicality concern: the accelerated rate requires choosing γ based on ρ_W (a global spectral property), which is generally not locally known and costly to compute.

10. Scope limitation: analysis is restricted to quadratic network averaging; extension to general convex objectives is not addressed and may be nontrivial.

11. Comparison limitation: for some graphs the achieved rate (e.g., O(D log(D/ε)) for tori) is slightly suboptimal compared to Nesterov-style optimal rates (O(D log(1/ε))) and this context should be acknowledged.

12. Weaknesses are minor and concern practical implementation and scope rather than fundamental flaws.

13. Overall assessment: significant, original, and technical strong paper; clear recommendation for acceptance.
