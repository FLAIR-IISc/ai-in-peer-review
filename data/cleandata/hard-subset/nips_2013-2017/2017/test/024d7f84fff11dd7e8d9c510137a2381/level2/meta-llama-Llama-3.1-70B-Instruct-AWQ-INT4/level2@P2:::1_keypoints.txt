- Presents Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm for distributed consensus with accelerated convergence.  
- Thorough analysis of convergence behavior and connection to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods.  
- Main contribution 1: establishes link between Min-Sum Splitting and lifted Markov chains enabling acceleration.  
- Main contribution 2: proves Min-Sum Splitting can achieve subdiffusive convergence rates, improving on classical diffusive rates.  
- Main contribution 3: introduces a new proof technique using an auxiliary process to track algorithm evolution on nodes.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly introduces Min-Sum and its application to consensus.  
- Presentation of results is well-organized with detailed convergence analysis.  
- Strength: novel approach to consensus via Min-Sum message-passing.  
- Strength: rigorous connection to lifted Markov chains yielding acceleration insights.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: analysis restricted to quadratic objective functions, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical or theoretical comparison with other state-of-the-art consensus algorithms.  
- Suggestion: extend analysis to more general objective functions.  
- Suggestion: provide comprehensive comparisons with other consensus methods to demonstrate superiority.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution, well-structured, suitable for publication in a top-tier conference (Rating 9/10, Confidence 8/10, Recommendation: Accept).
