- Introduces Min-Sum Splitting, a modified Min-Sum message-passing algorithm that converges for consensus while ordinary Min-Sum does not.  
- Shows proper tuning yields subdiffusive accelerated convergence rates, matching shift-register methods.  
- Provides a new proof technique using an auxiliary process to track Min-Sum Splitting, enabling convergence rate bounds for any graph topology.  
- Proves a square-root improvement over diffusive methods in convergence speed.  
- Relates Min-Sum Splitting to lifted Markov chains and multi-step first-order methods, highlighting similarities and connections.  
- Claims practical advantage by avoiding construction of a lifted graph required by some accelerated methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow, with clear explanations of algorithm and related work.  
- Technical contributions are significant, original, and potentially impactful for distributed optimization.  
- Experiments and simulations are limited and insufficient to fully evaluate empirical performance.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity of the Min-Sum Splitting algorithm.  
- Could provide more insights and examples of practical applications and implementation considerations.
