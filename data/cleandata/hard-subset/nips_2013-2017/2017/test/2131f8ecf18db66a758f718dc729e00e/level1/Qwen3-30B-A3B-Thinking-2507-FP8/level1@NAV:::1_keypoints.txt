- Establishes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Shows "same cluster" (XOR) queries map to locally encodable source coding where each compressed bit depends on a constant number of inputs.  
- Derives new information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity from this coding perspective.  
- Proposes AND queries and proves they outperform XOR queries for approximate recovery.  
- Main contributions: (1) theoretical equivalence, (2) improved lower bounds, (3) AND-query proposal, (4) real crowdsourcing experiment validation.  
- Builds on recent crowdsourcing/clustering work but introduces a fundamentally different theoretical framework addressing query-complexity gaps.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundations with rigorous proofs for noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Strength: clear presentation of AND-query advantages and empirical validation showing reduced error rates.  
- Practical insight: crowdsourcing workers find AND queries easier than "same cluster" queries.  
- Weakness: technical depth may challenge readers unfamiliar with information theory.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of broader applications beyond the specific clustering problem and a less explicit connection to prior NIPS work.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-written theoretical contribution with practical significance; recommend acceptance.
