- Proposes a novel information-theoretic formulation of semisupervised clustering linking it to locally encodable source coding.  
- Establishes an equivalence that enables derivation of lower bounds on the number of queries needed to recover correct labels.  
- Introduces AND-query based querying strategy that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries in many settings.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including rigorous lower bounds.  
- Presents experimental results that validate the theoretical findings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and concise explanations of methods and results.  
- Technical contributions are significant and advance understanding of query-efficient clustering.  
- Focus is primarily theoretical, offering limited coverage of broader semisupervised clustering literature.  
- Assumes substantial background in information and coding theory, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Discussion of related work could be more comprehensive.  
- Results have broad significance for applications like data compression, clustering, and entity resolution.  
- Overall paper quality is high, with clear technical exposition and validated experiments.
