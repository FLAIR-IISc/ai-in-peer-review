- Novel connection between semi-supervised clustering with non-adaptive queries and locally encodable source coding.
- Problem setup: recover n labels from k classes using a minimal number of constant-size queries, motivated by crowdsourcing.
- New information-theoretic lower bounds derived for both exact and approximate recovery.
- Introduction of AND queries, shown to be provably more efficient than same-cluster (XOR) queries, especially for approximate recovery.
- Main algorithmic result: in the noisy oracle setting, an efficient scheme achieves (1−δ) accuracy with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.
- Technical contribution: rigorous analysis with matching upper and lower bounds across noiseless/noisy and exact/approximate settings.
- Empirical validation: real-world crowdsourcing experiment supports theoretical advantages of AND queries and the proposed algorithm.
- Clarity and presentation: well-written, well-organized paper with clear motivation and explicit contributions.
- Weakness: analysis assumes i.i.d. label priors and symmetric noise; robustness to structured or adversarial noise is not explored.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical framework, superior query type with theory and experiments, and significant query-complexity improvement.
