- Novel approach: links semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding, offering a fresh information-theoretic perspective.  
- Theoretical contribution: derives new lower bounds on the number of queries required for exact and approximate recovery, tighter in some regimes.  
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes an AND-based query scheme that outperforms traditional 'same cluster' queries for approximate recovery.  
- Noise robustness: shows the AND scheme can recover labels with high accuracy even with noisy oracle responses.  
- Empirical validation: includes a real-world crowdsourcing experiment that supports the theoretical findings.  
- Strength: introduces locally encodable source coding to clustering, an underexplored and original connection.  
- Strength: combines theoretical analysis and experiments to demonstrate improvements over existing querying strategies.  
- Weakness: assumes a simple noise model that may not reflect complex real-world crowdsourcing behavior.  
- Weakness: analysis depends on a Poisson approximation that may fail for small query degrees.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, raising generalization concerns.  
- Weakness: many proofs and technical details are deferred to the supplement, potentially hindering reader comprehension.  
- Significance: results impact applications like crowdsourcing, entity resolution, and clustering, and may spur further research in locally encodable coding.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, with clear explanations, figures, and accessible introduction.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical proofs and reasonable experimental validation, with good literature comparison.  
- Acceptance argument: novel theory and practical AND-query algorithm that improves efficiency and accuracy.  
- Rejection argument: limited noise model, narrow experimental scope, and supplementary-heavy technical details may weaken practical impact.
