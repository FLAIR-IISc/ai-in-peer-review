- Core idea: Models semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding (compression) problem to analyze query complexity and design queries.  
- Main contribution: Proves equivalence between clustering and locally encodable source coding and derives information-theoretic lower bounds on queries.  
- Main contribution: Proposes AND queries as an alternative to ‘same cluster’ queries and shows superior performance for approximate recovery.  
- Main contribution: Analyzes noisy oracle responses and presents a scheme achieving logarithmic query complexity under noise.  
- Main contribution: Provides experimental validation on real datasets supporting theoretical claims.  
- Strength: Novel and insightful connection between clustering and information theory, opening new research directions.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor with lower bounds and algorithmic analysis; proofs appear well-structured.  
- Strength: High practical relevance for crowdsourcing and entity resolution; AND queries offer potential real-world improvements.  
- Strength: Addresses realistic noisy-query scenarios and proposes algorithms to handle noise.  
- Strength: Experimental results, though limited, support the theoretical findings and the AND query benefit.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-organized and clearly written.  
- Weakness: Experiments are small-scale and lack diversity; crowdsourcing setup description is brief.  
- Weakness: Assumes independent oracle errors, which may not hold in real-world correlated-error settings.  
- Weakness: Some theorems (especially noisy-query ones) use heavy mathematical notation, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: Needs a deeper comparison to existing active learning strategies beyond non-adaptivity.  
- Weakness: Justification for disallowing repeated queries is weak and could be strengthened.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically significant, practical query strategy (AND) with supporting experiments; clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: Limited empirical validation, heavy math may hurt accessibility, and practical impact beyond theory is not fully demonstrated.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant theoretical contributions and a compelling novel connection; recommend accept with minor revisions (expand experiments).
