- Tackles non-adaptive semi-supervised clustering via minimum queries to recover k clusters of n items.  
- Main conceptual contribution: novel equivalence between non-adaptive querying and locally encodable source coding.  
- Derives new information-theoretic lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery using that connection.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to same-cluster (XOR) queries and analyzes them theoretically.  
- Shows AND queries can be significantly more efficient, especially for approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm that recovers a (1-δ) fraction with O(n log(k/δ)) queries in the noisy setting.  
- Theoretical claims are technically strong and supported by standard information-theoretic/probabilistic techniques.  
- Proof sketches are sometimes terse; some concentration arguments rely on cited general results without full detail.  
- Includes a small crowdsourcing experiment that supports practical relevance, though on a limited scale.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; problem setup and algorithm presentation are clear.  
- Novelty is high: the locally encodable coding viewpoint and systematic analysis of AND queries are original.  
- Significance is high: new framework, state-of-the-art noisy approximate recovery bounds, and practical algorithm.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, practical AND queries, strong theoretical results, potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental validation and some proofs need clearer exposition.
