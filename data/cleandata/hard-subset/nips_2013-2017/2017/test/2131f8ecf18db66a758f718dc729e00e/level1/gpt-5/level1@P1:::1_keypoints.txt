- Studies semisupervised clustering with limited Δ-local queries and links query answers to locally encodable source coding.  
- Introduces pairwise AND queries and shows they can greatly reduce query complexity for approximate recovery, especially under noise.  
- Proposes a simple nonadaptive algorithm achieving (1−δ)-accurate labeling with O(n log(k/δ)) pairwise queries under an independent noise model.  
- Presents small crowdsourcing evidence that AND queries are more sample-efficient and easier for workers than XOR/same-cluster queries.  
- Places work within clustering-with-queries and information-theory literature; the locally encodable source coding viewpoint is novel.  

- Provides upper and lower bounds for exact recovery (noiseless, k=2) with Δ-local queries using random sparse XOR encodings and ML decoding.  
- States a general Δ-local lower bound for approximate recovery (Thm 3) and gives an AND-query achievability analyzed via Poisson-degree approximation (Thm 4).  
- Gives a noisy k-ary AND scheme with a d-regular thresholding algorithm achieving O(n log(k/δ)) queries (Thm 5) and a variant not needing priors (Thm 6).  

- XOR achievability uses ML decoding (information-theoretically sound) but ML is computationally intractable and this practical limitation should be emphasized.  
- Notational confusions (e.g., “r(p)∆” vs “(1−2r(p))^Δ” and entropy function arguments) need clarification to avoid dimensional inconsistencies.  
- Main paper omits proof and intuition for the key Δ-local approximate lower bound (Thm 3); a sketch is necessary to assess tightness.  
- Separation arguments for XOR suboptimality rely on a Massey conjecture (proved for p=1/2); unconditional formal separations would strengthen the claim.  
- Repeated typo: query count written as “nd2” though construction uses nd/2 edges; this should be corrected everywhere.  
- Dependency counting in concentration bounds is incorrect: 2-hop neighborhoods are O(d^2), not “d + d”; constants in tail bounds are affected.  
- Assumes independence of query answers (standard but nontrivial); paper avoids repeat identical queries which is reasonable.  

- Paper is generally well written and well motivated but hampered by notation/typographical issues.  
- Needs consistent use of nd/2 vs nd^2, clear Δ notation, definition of α and r(p), and explanation of ML computational status.  
- Should include a proof sketch or intuition for Thm 3 in the main text.  

- Locally encodable source coding perspective is novel and conceptually clarifying.  
- Identification and analysis of AND queries as superior to XOR for approximate/noisy recovery is a fresh, practical insight.  
- New lower bounds under Δ-local constraints and an efficient O(n log(k/δ)) nonadaptive algorithm extend prior same-cluster/query work.  

- Theoretical bounds clarify limits when per-query locality is constrained.  
- AND-query idea is actionable for crowdsourcing and initial experiments suggest lower worker error.  
- Simple linear-time decoding algorithm makes the approach a practical baseline.  

- Conceptual novelty linking queries to local encoding and thorough theoretical treatment across noiseless/noisy and exact/approximate settings.  
- Clear identification of same-cluster limitations and principled AND alternative.  
- Simple, scalable algorithm with near-optimal query complexity and some empirical validation.  

- Notational and typographical inconsistencies (query counts, Δ usage) and likely error in dependency counting.  
- Reliance on a conjectured lower bound for linear/XOR separation rather than unconditional proof in all regimes.  
- ML-based achievability for XOR is computationally infeasible; no practical exact-recovery algorithms provided.  
- Experiments are small-scale, single-domain, few volunteers, and lack broader baselines.  

- Fix all instances of query-count typos and correct dependency neighborhood size calculations; clarify Δ’s role in formulas.  
- Add a proof sketch and intuition for Thm 3 in the main paper and discuss its tightness relative to achievability.  
- Provide an unconditional lower bound demonstrating XOR suboptimality in some regimes beyond p=1/2.  
- Expand empirical evaluation with larger synthetic experiments across p, q, k, compare to majority-vote/triangle/same-cluster baselines, and report runtimes.  
- Discuss worker interface design and per-query cost for k-ary AND queries.  

- Recommendation: Weak accept.
