- Connects non-adaptive, constant-∆ query-based clustering to locally encodable source coding (labels X compressed to query answers Y with local dependency constraints).  
- Problem setup: determine n item labels in k classes via non-adaptive ∆-item queries, minimizing number of queries for exact or approximate recovery.  
- Exact recovery (noiseless, k=2): upper bound on queries sometimes < n and a new lower bound improving on nH(p) in some regimes.  
- Approximate recovery (noiseless): shows standard same-cluster (XOR) queries are suboptimal; introduces AND queries with better distortion–rate tradeoff.  
- Approximate recovery (noisy): proposes generalized AND queries and simple decoder achieving m = O(n log(k/δ)) queries to recover (1−δ) labels under noise; validated with crowdsourcing.  
- Originality: novel and powerful theoretical lens bridging query-based clustering and locally encodable codes.  
- Practical query design: AND queries are well-motivated theoretically and argued to be easier for human annotators, supported by experimental error-rate data.  
- Theoretical strength: technically dense results (Theorems 2, 3, 5) and the O(n log(k/δ)) noisy recovery achievability.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, motivated, and formally states main results.  
- Weakness — asymmetry: main AND query is asymmetric (treats one class as default), raising unclear applicability to symmetric k-class clustering and potential k−1 factor in queries.  
- Weakness — assumptions: assumes known priors p and a simple BSC noise model; impact of prior estimation error and more realistic noise not analyzed.  
- Weakness — baselines: lacks direct empirical comparison to practical baselines (e.g., same-cluster queries + spectral/community detection) under equal query budgets.  
- Weakness — proofs: some proofs use approximations (Poisson, concentration inequalities) and need clarification that experimental graph constructions meet theoretical conditions.  
- Overall decision: strong, original, technically deep paper; recommend acceptance.
