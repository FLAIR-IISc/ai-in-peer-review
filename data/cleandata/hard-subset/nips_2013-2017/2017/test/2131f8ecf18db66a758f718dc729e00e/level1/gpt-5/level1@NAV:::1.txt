This paper studies nonadaptive, query-efficient semi-supervised clustering/label recovery through an information-theoretic lens, establishing an equivalence to locally encodable source coding. For k = 2, pairwise “same-cluster” queries are modeled as XORs; the authors show achievability below n queries for exact recovery by exploiting the prior p via sparse XOR measurements (Theorem 1) and prove improved converse bounds that account for local encodability (Theorem 2). For approximate recovery, they give a locality-aware lower bound (Theorem 3) and argue, using classical limits on linear rate–distortion codes (Ancheta–Massey), that XOR queries are suboptimal. They propose AND queries and analyze a simple decoder that achieves favorable error–query tradeoffs (Theorem 4). Extending to noisy answers and k ≥ 2, they design a k-ary “AND-like” query on d-regular graphs with a thresholding decoder achieving O(n log(k/δ)) queries for (1 − δ)-accurate recovery (Theorem 5), with a variant that only needs relative cluster sizes (Theorem 6). A small crowdsourcing experiment (movies) and synthetic results suggest AND queries are easier for workers and align with theory. The work situates within NIPS threads on clustering with queries and crowdsourcing (e.g., Ashtiani et al., Karger–Oh–Shah) and connects to locally decodable/smooth compression (Makhdoumi et al., Montanari–Mossel) and rate–distortion theory.

Quality: The technical contributions are largely sound and well-motivated. The new converse bounds that incorporate locality are insightful. Some proofs rely on typicality/expurgation and independence assumptions; details are deferred to the appendix. The exact-recovery achievability uses ML decoding, which is computationally infeasible; the paper is transparent about this but a polynomial-time counterpart would strengthen the result. The noisy algorithm is simple, analyzable, and near-linear time.

Clarity: Generally clear, though dense. A tighter separation of exact vs approximate/noisy settings and consistent notation would improve readability. Some typos and minor inconsistencies (binary vs k-ary outputs) should be fixed.

Originality: High. The equivalence to locally encodable source coding and the advocacy and analysis of AND queries are novel and differentiate from prior “same-cluster” (XOR) paradigms.

Significance: Potentially impactful for both theory (new locality-aware limits) and practice (better query primitives in crowdsourcing). The empirical validation is modest in scale; broader experiments would help.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually novel bridge to locally encodable source coding with new converse bounds.
- Clear demonstration that AND queries outperform XOR for approximate recovery.
- Simple, provably near-optimal algorithm under noise with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.
- Empirical evidence that AND queries are easier for annotators.

Cons for acceptance:
- Exact-recovery achievability uses intractable ML decoding.
- Practicality of k-ary outputs for k > 2 is under-discussed.
- Dependence on independence/known priors; robustness to misspecification not explored.
- Limited experimental scale and baselines.
