- Establishes a novel equivalence between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding, linking clustering queries to information theory.
- Studies an oracle model giving binary answers about labels and aims to minimize query complexity for label recovery.
- Shows pairwise "same cluster" (XOR) queries have fundamental limitations for approximate recovery.
- Proposes AND queries as a superior alternative that achieves better query complexity for approximate recovery.
- Extends prior NIPS work by introducing the locally encodable source coding perspective not previously applied to this problem.
- Provides rigorous theoretical lower bounds (Theorems 2,3) and matching upper bounds (Theorems 1,4,5) for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Proofs are well-structured using information-theoretic and probabilistic analysis.
- Presents practical algorithms (Algorithm 1) that are precise and implementable.
- Validates claims with a real crowdsourcing experiment showing AND queries have substantially lower error (105 vs. 234 errors).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; figures effectively illustrate trade-offs.
- Minor clarity issue: the connection to information theory (locally encodable source coding) is introduced somewhat abruptly.
- Original contribution: novel insight that AND queries outperform XOR for approximate recovery and the information-theoretic framing.
- Significance: theoretical advances in locally encodable source coding and practical impact for reducing crowdsourcing costs.
- Strengths: novel theoretical connection, rigorous bounds, practical AND query approach, experimental validation, clear exposition.
- Weaknesses: focus restricted to pairwise queries, requires knowledge of label priors, noisy-case theory tied to specific AND design, limited comparison to other information-theoretic clustering approaches.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contribution; results are important and distinct from prior work.
- Recommendation: Accept.
