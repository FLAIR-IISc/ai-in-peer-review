This paper presents a novel approach to semisupervised clustering by establishing an equivalence between this problem and locally encodable source coding. The authors leverage this connection to derive lower bounds on the number of queries required to recover correct labels and propose a new querying strategy based on AND operations that outperforms traditional 'same cluster' queries. The paper also explores the case of noisy queries and demonstrates that the proposed AND query scheme can recover labels with high accuracy even in the presence of errors.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a fresh perspective on semisupervised clustering by linking it to locally encodable source coding, a relatively unexplored area in information theory.
* The authors derive new lower bounds on the number of queries required for exact and approximate recovery, which are tighter than existing bounds in certain regimes.
* The proposed AND query scheme is shown to be more efficient than traditional 'same cluster' queries for approximate recovery, both theoretically and experimentally.
* The paper includes a real-world crowdsourcing experiment that validates the theoretical results and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a relatively simple noise model for the oracle's responses, which may not accurately capture the complexities of real-world crowdsourcing scenarios.
* The analysis of the AND query scheme relies on a Poisson approximation, which may not hold in all cases, particularly for small values of the query degree.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and may not generalize to other domains or scenarios.
* Some of the proofs and technical details are relegated to the supplementary material, which may make it difficult for readers to fully appreciate the contributions of the paper.

Originality:

* The connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding is novel and opens up new avenues for research in this area.
* The proposed AND query scheme is a new contribution that improves upon existing querying strategies for approximate recovery.
* The paper's focus on locally encodable source coding, a relatively underexplored area in information theory, adds to the originality of the work.
