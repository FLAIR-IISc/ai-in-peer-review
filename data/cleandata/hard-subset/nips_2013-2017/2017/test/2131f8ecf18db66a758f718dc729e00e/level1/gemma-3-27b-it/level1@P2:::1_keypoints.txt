- Novel connection: Frames semisupervised clustering with binary queries as locally encodable source coding, enabling information-theoretic tools.  
- Core idea: Treats querying as compression where each compressed bit depends on a few input bits, yielding lower bounds on query count.  
- AND queries: Proposes AND queries as a compelling, more efficient alternative to ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries, with theoretical support.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides clear information-theoretic derivations and lower bounds; proofs appear sound.  
- Noisy/noiseless settings: Analyzes both noiseless and noisy oracle models, increasing practical relevance.  
- Practical validation: Presents a small-scale crowdsourcing experiment that supports the theory.  
- Readability issues: Paper is dense, with heavy notation and abrupt transitions; more intuitive explanations needed.  
- Limited experiments: Empirical validation is small-scale and insufficient to convincingly demonstrate practical superiority.  
- Missing baselines: Lacks comparisons to standard baselines (e.g., spectral clustering) and broader dataset evaluations.  
- Underexplored background: The locally encodable source coding literature and broader applications are not discussed in depth.  
- Query selection details: Insufficient analysis of the impact of query graph structure (e.g., d-regular construction) on performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel connection, strong theory, potential practical impact, and relevance to crowdsourcing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor clarity, limited experimental evidence, and underdeveloped components of the proposed connection.  
- Overall recommendation: Worth accepting with revisions — improve clarity and expand experimental evaluation.
