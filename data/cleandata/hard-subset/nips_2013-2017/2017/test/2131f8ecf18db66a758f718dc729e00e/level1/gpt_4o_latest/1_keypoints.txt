- Introduces equivalence between locally encodable source coding (LESC) and semi-supervised clustering, proposing AND queries for crowdsourced label recovery.  
- Shows AND queries outperform traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries in query efficiency and robustness to noise.  
- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity and proposes near-optimal nonadaptive querying schemes.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and proofs grounded in information and rate-distortion theory.  
- Presents exact and approximate recovery algorithms and methods for handling noisy queries.  
- Validates methods empirically on synthetic and real-world crowdsourcing datasets (e.g., movie clustering).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scale (e.g., only ~200 movies) and lacks broader comparisons to state-of-the-art clustering methods.  
- Paper is technically dense; notation and derivations may be hard to follow for readers not versed in information theory—needs clearer exposition and visual aids.  
- Assumes independent noise for queries; does not address correlated or adversarial noise scenarios.  
- Theoretical focus is mainly on binary labels (k=2) with limited extension to multi-class (k>2) settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical connection, practical AND-query framework, and combined theoretical/empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, restricted experimental scope, and reduced generalizability due to binary-focus.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve accessibility and expand experimental evaluation.
