- Establishes an equivalence between nonadaptive, query-efficient semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.
- For k=2, models pairwise “same-cluster” queries as XORs and shows exact recovery below n queries via sparse XOR measurements (Theorem 1).
- Proves improved converse bounds that account for local encodability, tightening limits on query efficiency (Theorem 2).
- Gives a locality-aware lower bound for approximate recovery and argues XOR queries are suboptimal using rate–distortion limits (Ancheta–Massey).
- Proposes AND queries and analyzes a simple decoder that achieves favorable error–query tradeoffs for approximate recovery (Theorem 4).
- Extends to noisy answers and k ≥ 2 with a k-ary “AND-like” query on d-regular graphs and thresholding decoder achieving O(n log(k/δ)) queries for (1−δ)-accurate recovery (Theorem 5).
- Provides a variant requiring only relative cluster sizes rather than full priors (Theorem 6).
- Small crowdsourcing experiment and synthetic results suggest AND queries are easier for workers and align with theoretical predictions.
- Technical contributions are largely sound; new locality-aware converse bounds are insightful.
- Some proofs rely on typicality/expurgation and independence assumptions, with details deferred to the appendix.
- Exact-recovery achievability uses computationally infeasible ML decoding; a polynomial-time counterpart is lacking.
- The noisy algorithm is simple, analyzable, and near-linear time, which is practical.
- Presentation is generally clear but dense; needs tighter separation of exact vs approximate/noisy settings and consistent notation.
- Minor typos and inconsistencies (e.g., binary vs k-ary outputs) should be fixed.
- Originality is high, notably the locally encodable coding perspective and advocacy/analysis of AND queries.
- Potentially significant for theory and practice, though empirical validation is limited in scale.
- Pros: novel theoretical bridge, clear superiority of AND for approximate recovery, simple near-optimal noisy algorithm, empirical support for AND ease.
- Cons: reliance on intractable ML decoding for exact recovery, under-discussed practicality of k-ary outputs for k>2, dependence on independence/known priors, and limited experimental scale and baselines.
