- Novel connection between semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding enabling new information-theoretic lower bounds.
- Introduction and formal analysis of AND queries, shown to outperform XOR queries for both noiseless and noisy/approximate recovery.
- AND queries reduce query complexity for (1-δ)-good recovery to O(n log k/δ) vs. O(n log n) for XOR in relevant settings.
- Practical insight: AND queries impose less burden on crowd workers (one label sufficient for "no"), validated by a crowdsourcing experiment (105 vs. 234 errors).
- Proposed Algorithm 1 for noisy queries is simple, effective, and achieves near-optimal query complexity with experimental support.
- Clear exposition of the XOR vs. AND distinction and why AND queries are superior for approximate recovery.
- Weakness: exclusive focus on non-adaptive query schemes, which may be less practical than adaptive approaches shown effective in prior work.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis assumes knowledge of the prior distribution p; practical implementations need additional assumptions or estimators.
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited to a single small dataset (200 movies), reducing empirical generality.
- Weakness: paper could better distinguish and compare its results to recent adaptive query-based clustering work.
- Pro: significant theoretical contribution, elegant info-theoretic framing, AND model both theoretically and empirically superior.
- Con: limited practical applicability due to non-adaptive focus, prior assumption, and narrow experiments.
- Overall: strong, well-written theoretical contribution with empirical support; recommended for acceptance (NIPS 2017) despite noted limitations.
