- Paper establishes a surprising connection between semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding.
- Clustering is framed as a compression task where queries are compressed bits and labels are original data.
- This framing enables application of information-theoretic tools to derive lower bounds on query complexity.
- Authors propose AND queries and show they outperform traditional ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries in several scenarios, especially with noisy oracles.
- The paper is well-motivated and clearly positions itself relative to crowdsourcing and information-theory prior work.
- Theoretical contributions (lower bounds and analysis of AND queries) are strong and proofs appear rigorous.
- Experimental validation is present but limited; results support theoretical claims only partially.
- Connection to locally encodable source coding feels tenuous and not fully exploited beyond enabling lower bounds.
- Presentation is dense and could be streamlined for clarity; some sections (e.g., locally decodable codes) feel tangential.
- Experimental setup needs strengthening with more datasets and broader oracle error-rate evaluations.
- Analysis of why AND queries perform better lacks an intuitive explanation beyond tighter bounds.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental evaluation.
