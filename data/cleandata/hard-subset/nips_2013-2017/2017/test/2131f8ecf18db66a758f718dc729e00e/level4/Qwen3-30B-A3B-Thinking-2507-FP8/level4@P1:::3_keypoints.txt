- Novel connection established between semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding, yielding new theoretical insights on query complexity.
- Shows pairwise "same cluster" (XOR) queries have fundamental limitations for approximate recovery.
- Proposes AND queries as a more efficient alternative with tighter lower bounds for exact recovery and improved bounds for approximate recovery.
- Provides an algorithm and theoretical guarantees for clustering with noisy queries (Theorem 5).
- Builds on recent clustering-with-side-information work and connects to information-theory literature on locally encodable source coding.
- Theoretical contributions are significant, well-motivated, and include tight lower bounds (Theorems 1–4).
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanations and a well-articulated information-theoretic perspective.
- Empirical validation shows AND queries yield lower error rates than "same cluster" queries in a crowdsourcing experiment.
- Primary concern: practicality of AND queries — they may require workers to know label classes, contradicting crowdsourcing assumptions.
- Paper needs explicit discussion of how workers can answer AND queries without prior knowledge of label space to establish practical relevance.
- Suggests simplifying and providing intuitive interpretations or visualizations of the bounds in Theorems 1–4 to improve accessibility.
- Experimental results must clarify feasibility of AND queries for crowd workers to validate the comparative error rates.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing AND query practicality.
