This paper addresses the problem of crowdsourced clustering, where the goal is to recover the true labels of data points by asking same-cluster queries. The authors propose theoretical bounds on the number of queries required for label recovery in both noiseless and noisy settings. The primary contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations. The paper is well-written and accessible to a general machine learning audience. In particular, the information-theoretic approach used to derive the bounds is clearly explained.

I have the following concerns, which I hope the authors can address to improve the paper:

1.  The motivation for using "AND" queries, while theoretically interesting, appears to conflict with the practical premise of crowdsourcing. As I understand it, an AND query response effectively reveals the cluster label, rather than simply confirming if the queried items belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, it raises a fundamental question: why use these similarity queries instead of directly asking for an item's label? This point is my primary concern regarding the paper's contribution and practical relevance.

2.  Could the expressions for the theoretical bounds in Theorems 1-4 be simplified? A more streamlined presentation might improve readability without sacrificing the core theoretical message.

3.  The experiments appear to focus exclusively on AND queries. Consequently, the validity and interpretation of the experimental results are contingent on a satisfactory clarification of the first point regarding the motivation for such queries.

The authors have provided a satisfactory response to the questions above, resolving my initial concerns. I therefore recommend this paper for acceptance.
