1. Overall: Broad scope (theory, algorithm, experiments) but presentation, result clarity, and comparisons to prior work are weak; major revisions or new results likely needed.

2. Query-repeat constraint: Banning repeated queries is overly restrictive and unjustified; authors should compare to algorithms allowing repeats (possibly via different annotators) to show advantage.

3. Theorem 1: Claimed exact-recovery bounds conflict with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) for some settings (e.g., p=1/2), and the proof is hard to follow — requires clarification.

4. Theorem 3: The lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; authors should present m as a function of n and δ to quantify gains from relaxed recovery.

5. Theorem 4: Similar interpretability issues as Theorem 3 and unclear gap between this upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound (could the gap be unbounded?); proof is also difficult to follow.

6. Figure 2: Lacks comparison to an actual same-cluster query algorithm (only shows theoretical lower bound); the claim that AND-queries outperform same-cluster queries is unsubstantiated.

7. Algorithm 1: Appears to require prior knowledge of p or cluster size ratios without explaining how to obtain them in practice; pseudo-code is unclear and depends on hard-to-follow proofs.

8. Figures 3–5 (experiments): Experimental evaluation omits comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query algorithms), which is necessary to support performance claims.
