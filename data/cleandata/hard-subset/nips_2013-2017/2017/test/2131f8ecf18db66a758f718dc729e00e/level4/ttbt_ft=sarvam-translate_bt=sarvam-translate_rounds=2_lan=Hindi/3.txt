This is an interesting research paper. I enjoyed reading it. In this paper, the authors have considered the task of clustering data points through crowd sourcing, which involves the problem of retrieving the original labels of data points based on the answers to similar cluster questions. The authors have proposed a limit on the number of questions required to retrieve the labels, considering the noise and noise-free answers to the questions. The main contribution of this research paper is theoretical, along with some empirical simulations. The paper is well-written, making it easy for a general machine learning audience to understand the theoretical contributions. In particular, the relationship regarding information theory for deriving the limits is well explained.

My following concerns regarding improvements in the research paper are:

(1) While the idea of AND queries is theoretically interesting, it seems contrary to the original purpose of crowd sourcing. In the case of AND queries, effectively, the cluster label is also given as part of the answer, rather than just answering whether the data point of interest belongs to the same cluster or not. If that is indeed the case, as I understand from the paper, why would one need to ask those similarity questions? Why not just ask for the label for the data point? This is my primary confusion regarding the contribution of this research paper.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the limits in theorems 1, 2, 3, 4 while maintaining the essence?

(3) In the experiments, it seems only AND queries have been considered. If so, the experimental study is in question until the above point (1) becomes clear.

I am satisfied with the authors' response to my above questions. Therefore, I vote for the acceptance of this research paper.
