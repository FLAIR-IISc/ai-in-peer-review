- Paper derives information-theoretic bounds on the number of crowdsourced "same-cluster" (AND) queries needed to recover true labels in noiseless and noisy settings, supported by simulations.  
- Theoretical analysis is clear, well-written, and the connection to information theory is explained accessibly.  
- Main concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels directly, which seems to conflict with practical crowdsourcing motivations—why use these instead of asking item labels?  
- Request for clarification of the practical motivation and interpretation of AND queries given the potential label revelation.  
- Suggestion to simplify or provide asymptotic interpretations of Theorems 1–4 to improve intuition and interpretability.  
- Experimental validation appears to focus solely on AND queries, so its relevance depends on clarifying the motivation for that query type.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed concerns satisfactorily; reviewer recommends acceptance.
