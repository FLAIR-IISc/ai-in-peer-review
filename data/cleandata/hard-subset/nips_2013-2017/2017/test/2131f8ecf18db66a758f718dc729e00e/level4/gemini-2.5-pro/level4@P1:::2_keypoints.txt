1. Paper studies non-adaptive small-size noisy queries for semi-supervised clustering and gives lower bounds plus an AND-query algorithm, but requires major revisions.  
2. The restriction forbidding repeated queries is overly restrictive and unjustified; repeating queries (to independent annotators) is a natural noise-mitigation strategy and should be addressed or empirically justified.  
3. Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) appears inconsistent with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh 2016) — the stated query complexity differs and the proof is hard to follow.  
4. Theorem 3's lower bound on error rate δ is hard to interpret and not easily inverted to give m as a function of n and δ.  
5. Theorem 4's upper bound is also opaque; the paper does not analyze the gap between Theorems 3 and 4 or whether that gap is bounded, and its proof is difficult to follow.  
6. Figure 2 compares AND-queries only to a theoretical lower bound for same-cluster queries, not to any achievable same-cluster method, so the claimed superiority is unsubstantiated.  
7. Algorithm 1 assumes prior knowledge of label distribution p (cluster sizes) without explaining how to obtain it in practice; pseudocode and proof of Theorem 5 are unclear.  
8. Experiments (Figures 3–5) lack comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines, particularly same-cluster query algorithms, so empirical claims are not validated.  
9. Post-rebuttal issues remain: the authors have not clearly justified why repeating the same query is not useful, nor clarified whether cited literature supports that claim.  
10. The authors should incorporate the rebuttal’s detailed discussion comparing AND-queries to same-cluster methods into the main paper to improve clarity and strengthen claims.
