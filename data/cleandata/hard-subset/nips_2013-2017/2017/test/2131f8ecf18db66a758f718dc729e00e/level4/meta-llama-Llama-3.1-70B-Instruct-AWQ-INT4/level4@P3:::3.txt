This paper explores the problem of semisupervised clustering using crowdsourcing, where the goal is to recover the original labels of data points based on answers to similarity queries. The authors establish a connection between this problem and locally encodable source coding in information theory, leading to new bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels. These bounds are derived for both noiseless and noisy query answers.

The authors' main contribution is theoretical, and they provide a clear explanation of the connection to information theory. The paper is well-written, making it accessible to a general machine learning audience. The idea of using AND queries is particularly interesting, as it allows for more efficient recovery of labels.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the use of AND queries seems to conflict with the original motivation for crowdsourcing, as it effectively provides the label of the cluster as part of the answer. This raises questions about the necessity of asking similarity queries in the first place. The authors should clarify this point and explain why AND queries are still useful in this context.

Additionally, the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 are quite complex. While the authors have done a good job of explaining the underlying theory, it would be helpful to simplify these expressions while preserving their essence. This would make the results more accessible to a broader audience.

The experimental study is also limited, as it only considers AND queries. To strengthen the paper, the authors should consider comparing the performance of AND queries with other types of queries, such as "same cluster" queries. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the benefits and limitations of AND queries in this context.

Overall, the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of semisupervised clustering using crowdsourcing. With some clarifications and additional experimental results, it has the potential to be even stronger.
