- Paper connects semisupervised clustering via crowdsourcing to locally encodable source coding, yielding new theoretical bounds.
- Provides bounds for label recovery under both noiseless and noisy query answers.
- Main contribution is theoretical with a clear, accessible explanation of the information-theoretic connection.
- Idea of using AND queries is novel and enables more efficient label recovery.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to a general ML audience.
- Concern: AND queries may conflict with crowdsourcing motivation by effectively revealing cluster labels.
- Authors should clarify why AND queries are justified and still useful despite disclosing labels.
- Expressions in Theorems 1–4 are complex and should be simplified while preserving essence.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and only considers AND queries.
- Recommend comparing AND queries against other query types (e.g., "same cluster") in experiments.
- Overall contribution is valuable, but revisions are needed to address the above concerns.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying AND query role, simplifying theoretical expressions, and expanding experimental comparisons.
