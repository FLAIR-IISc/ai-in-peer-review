- Paper proposes crowdsourced clustering to recover original labels from similarity queries, derives query-complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless settings, and supports theory with simulations; writing is clear and ties to information theory.

- Main contribution is theoretical; empirical work is simulation-based.

- Concern 1: AND queries may implicitly reveal cluster labels (contradicting the similarity-only crowdsourcing motivation), so it's unclear why one wouldn't ask labels directly—needs clarification.

- Concern 2: The bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex and should be simplified for readability while retaining their core results.

- Concern 3: Experiments seem to only use AND queries, limiting the empirical study unless the AND-query interpretation issue is resolved.

- Reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and votes to accept.
