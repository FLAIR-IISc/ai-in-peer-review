- Paper studies clustering from noisy, non-adaptive subset queries, gives information-theoretic lower bounds, proposes an AND-queries algorithm for k clusters, and evaluates on real data.  
- Overall presentation, clarity of results, and comparisons to related work are weak; substantial revision needed.  
- Disallowing repeated queries seems overly restrictive and likely degrades performance; authors must justify this model or compare to the unrestricted/repeated-answers case (e.g., independent annotators).  
- Theorem 1 appears inconsistent with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (discrepancy for p=1/2 and constant Δ) and its proof is hard to follow.  
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is opaque; it’s unclear how to translate it into an m vs. n, δ tradeoff to understand gains.  
- Theorem 4 is similarly hard to interpret; the relationship and potential gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound are unclear and proofs are difficult to follow.  
- Figure 2 compares only to a same-cluster-query lower bound, not the actual same-cluster-query method; the claimed outperforming is not substantiated and potential crossing points are unexplored.  
- Algorithm 1 assumes prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes; the paper does not explain how this information is obtained in practice and the pseudo-code is hard to follow without opaque proofs.  
- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), weakening empirical claims of superiority.  
- Rebuttal did not resolve whether repeated questions are unhelpful; the cited Nature paper’s relevance should be clarified in a revision.  
- Authors should include a detailed comparison/discussion with the same-query method as promised in the rebuttal.
