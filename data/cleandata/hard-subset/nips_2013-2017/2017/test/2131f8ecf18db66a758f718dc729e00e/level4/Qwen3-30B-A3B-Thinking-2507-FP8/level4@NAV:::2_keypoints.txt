1. Paper studies noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries for clustering, gives lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm, and reports real-data experiments, but has presentation, clarity, and comparison weaknesses requiring major revision.  
2. Banning repeated queries seems unnecessarily restrictive; authors should justify this with real-data comparisons to an unrestricted repeated-query model or allow repeated queries with independent annotators.  
3. Theorem 1 conflicts with Ahn–Lee–Suh in the noiseless exact-recovery XOR setting (discrepancy in required m); the theorem and its proof need clarification.  
4. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is hard to interpret and does not clearly translate to m as a function of n and δ, obscuring its practical implications.  
5. Theorem 4 shares interpretability and proof difficulties; the gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound is unclear and could be large/unbounded.  
6. Figure 2 fails to compare AND-queries directly to the same-cluster-query method, so the claim that AND-queries outperform is unsupported and requires explanation of any crossing behavior.  
7. Algorithm 1 depends on prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios (per Theorem 6), raising practical concerns; the pseudocode is unclear without Theorem 5’s proof.  
8. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), which are necessary to validate performance claims.
