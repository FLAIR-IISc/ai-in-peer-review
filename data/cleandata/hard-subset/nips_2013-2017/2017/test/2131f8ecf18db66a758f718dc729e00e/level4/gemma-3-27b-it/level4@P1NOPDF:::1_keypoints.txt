- Paper studies query-based clustering where the learner queries an oracle to test if instances share a cluster, aiming to recover the target clustering efficiently.  
- Model generalizes to oracles that return a fixed binary-valued function of instance labels.  
- Main focus is the query complexity needed for perfect or approximate recovery.  
- Authors provide information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Analysis also covers noisy oracle responses.  
- Novel information-theoretic tools that could interest the ML community are introduced.  
- Limitation: analysis lacks structural assumptions (no predefined hypothesis class), so bounds depend mainly on label marginals and noise.  
- Consequently, the setting is somewhat removed from traditional learning-theory frameworks but serves as an important first step, especially for lower bounds.
