- Establishes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Considers a crowdsourcing clustering problem where an oracle answers relationship queries to recover true cluster labels with minimal queries.  
- Shows fundamental limitations of traditional "same cluster" queries.  
- Proposes AND queries as a superior alternative that yields better query complexity.  
- Demonstrates improved query complexity bounds for both noiseless and noisy settings using AND queries.  
- Provides theoretical upper and lower bounds for exact recovery, approximate recovery, and noisy scenarios.  
- Validates theoretical claims with a real-world crowdsourcing experiment.  
- Paper excels in clarity and presentation; complex concepts are made accessible.  
- Carefully situates the work within existing NIPS clustering literature and information-theory coding results.  
- Originality is strong: linking clustering to locally encodable source coding and introducing AND queries is a significant advance.  
- Shows practical significance via substantial query complexity improvements (e.g., O(n log 1/δ) vs O(n log n)).  
- Concern 1: Motivation for AND queries is unclear—why would workers answer AND queries instead of labels, and why are AND queries more feasible?  
- Concern 2: Theoretical bounds (Theorems 1–4) are complex and would benefit from asymptotic simplifications or intuitive explanations.  
- Concern 3: Experiments test only AND queries and lack a direct comparison with "same cluster" queries to validate superiority.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, minor revisions suggested, recommendation for acceptance.
