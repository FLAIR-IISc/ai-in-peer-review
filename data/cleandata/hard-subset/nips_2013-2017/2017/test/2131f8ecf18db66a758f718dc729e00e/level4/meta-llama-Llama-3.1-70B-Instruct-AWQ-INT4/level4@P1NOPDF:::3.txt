This is an interesting paper that I enjoyed reading. The authors tackle the task of clustering data points via crowdsourcing, where the goal is to recover the original labels of data points based on answers to similar cluster queries. They propose bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers for the queries. The paper's main contribution is theoretical, accompanied by some empirical simulations. The writing is clear, making it easy for a general machine learning audience to understand the theoretical contributions. In particular, the authors nicely explain the connection to information theory for deriving the bounds.

I have some concerns that I hope the authors can address to improve the paper:

(1) While the idea of AND queries is theoretically interesting, it seems to conflict with the original motivation for crowdsourcing. In the case of an AND query, the label of the cluster is effectively given as part of the answer, rather than just determining whether the data points of interest belong to the same cluster or not. If this interpretation is correct, I am left wondering why one would need to ask these similarity queries in the first place. Why not ask for the label of a data point directly? This is my primary point of confusion regarding the contributions in this paper.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 while preserving their essence?

(3) The experiments appear to consider only AND queries. If this is the case, the validity of the experimental study is questionable unless the above point (1) is clarified.

I am satisfied with the authors' response to these concerns, and based on this, I vote for the acceptance of this paper.
