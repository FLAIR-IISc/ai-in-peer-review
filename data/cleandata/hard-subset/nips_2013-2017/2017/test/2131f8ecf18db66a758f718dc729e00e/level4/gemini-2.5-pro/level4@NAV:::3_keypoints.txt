- Investigates crowdsourced clustering via same-cluster queries to recover ground-truth labels.
- Main contribution: theoretical bounds on query complexity for label recovery in noisy and noiseless settings, supported by simulations.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and accessible, with a clear, elegant information-theoretic derivation.
- Concern: AND queries may effectively reveal cluster labels, contradicting the crowdsourcing motivation—why not query labels directly?
- Suggestion: Theorem 1–4 expressions could be simplified or made more intuitive for accessibility.
- Concern: Experiments focus only on AND queries, so empirical validity depends on resolving the AND-query conceptual issue.
- Outcome: Reviewer’s concerns were addressed and they recommend the paper for acceptance.
