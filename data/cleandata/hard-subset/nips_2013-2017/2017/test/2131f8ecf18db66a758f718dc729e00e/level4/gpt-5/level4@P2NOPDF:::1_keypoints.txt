Paper studies clustering with interactive oracle queries to recover the target partition.

Allowed queries include same-cluster questions and evaluations of a fixed binary function of instance labels.

Main focus is the query complexity required for exact or approximate recovery.

Motivation is strong with applications in record deduplication and crowdsourcing.

Adopts an information-theoretic perspective, deriving upper and lower bounds and analyzing a noisy oracle setting.

The information-theoretic tools used appear novel to the ML community and are of independent interest.

Key limitation: absence of structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class or generative model), which limits scope and practical interpretability.

Derived bounds depend mainly on the marginal label distribution and noise level, placing the setting outside standard learning-theoretic frameworks.

Nonetheless, the work clarifies fundamental information-theoretic limits of query-based clustering—particularly lower bounds—and can guide future research incorporating richer structural assumptions.
