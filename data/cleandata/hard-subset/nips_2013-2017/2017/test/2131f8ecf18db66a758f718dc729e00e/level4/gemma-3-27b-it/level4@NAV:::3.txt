This paper presents an interesting and well-written contribution to the field of crowdsourced clustering. The authors tackle the problem of recovering data point labels from answers to cluster similarity queries, proposing theoretical bounds on the query complexity required for accurate label recovery, under both noisy and noiseless response models. The core of the work is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations. The exposition is clear and effectively communicates the theoretical results to a broad machine learning audience, with a particularly strong explanation of the connection to Information Theory in deriving the bounds.

However, I had a few points for the authors to address. First, the use of AND queries, while theoretically appealing, appears to deviate from the typical crowdsourcing paradigm. Specifically, an AND query seems to inherently reveal cluster labels alongside similarity information. If this interpretation is correct, it raises the question of why similarity queries are necessary when direct label elicitation would be more efficient. This was my primary point of confusion regarding the paper’s contributions. Second, I wondered if the expressions for the bounds presented in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 could be simplified without sacrificing their essential meaning. Finally, the experimental evaluation appears to focus solely on AND queries. Consequently, the validity of the empirical results hinges on a clarification of the issues raised in point (1).

I am pleased to report that the authors’ responses to my questions have adequately addressed my concerns. Therefore, I recommend accepting this paper for presentation at the conference.
