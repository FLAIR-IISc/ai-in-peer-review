- Paper studies reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries, derives query lower bounds, and proposes an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation.  
- Overall: solid theoretical effort but weak presentation, unclear proofs/results, and insufficient comparison to related work; needs major revision.  
1. No-repeats constraint is overly restrictive; allowing repeated queries could reduce noise and should be justified via experiments or by assuming independent annotators.  
2. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work ([Ahn-Lee-Suh]) for noiseless exact recovery (discrepant query complexities) and its proof is hard to follow.  
3. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is opaque; it's difficult to relate m, n, and δ to assess performance gains.  
4. Same interpretability issue for Theorem 4; unclear gap between upper and lower bounds and proofs are hard to follow.  
5. Figure 2 lacks comparison to standard “same-cluster” query methods (only compares to their lower bound); claimed superiority is unsupported.  
6. Algorithm 1 depends on knowing p or cluster size ratios—practical acquisition of this info is unexplained and pseudocode is hard to understand without Theorem 5’s proof.  
7. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster queries), undermining performance claims.  
- Rebuttal helped but more detail is needed on repeating queries (and Nature paper) and a thorough comparison to standard same-cluster methods.
