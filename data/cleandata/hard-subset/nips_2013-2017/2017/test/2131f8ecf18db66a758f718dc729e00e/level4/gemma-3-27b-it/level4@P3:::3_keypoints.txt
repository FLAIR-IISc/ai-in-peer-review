- Novel contribution: connects semi-supervised clustering via crowdsourcing to locally encodable source coding.
- Theoretical work: derives rigorous bounds on number of queries required for label recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written and explains information-theoretic aspects accessibly.
- Novel perspective: framing the problem via locally encodable codes is insightful and valuable.
- Core method concern: introduction of AND queries lacks sufficient motivation and practical justification.
- Crowdsourcing mismatch: AND queries seem to demand more label knowledge than standard pairwise “same cluster?” judgments, deviating from typical crowdsourcing tasks.
- Worker behavior unexplained: paper does not justify why workers would prefer or reliably answer AND queries over standard queries or individual labels.
- Fundamental question: paper must explain why asking for combined label (AND) is preferable to asking individual labels.
- Complexity of results: expressions for Theorems 1–4 are complex and hard to interpret.
- Need for intuition: bounds should be simplified or given more intuitive forms, with illustrative examples showing practical implications.
- Limited experiments: empirical validation focuses mainly on AND queries and omits direct comparison with standard same-cluster queries.
- Real-world linkage unclear: connection between crowdsourcing experiment results and theoretical scaling (e.g., dependence on δ) is not made explicit.
- Missing analysis: computational cost of the algorithm is not discussed.
- Strengths summarized: novelty, theoretical rigor, clarity, and some empirical validation.
- Weaknesses summarized: weak motivation for AND queries, complex theoretical expressions, limited experiments, and missing computational-cost discussion.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach that opens new research directions.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical relevance of AND queries is questionable, complexity may limit applicability, and experiments are insufficient.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on stronger justification for AND queries, clearer/simpler interpretation of bounds, and more comprehensive experimental comparisons.
