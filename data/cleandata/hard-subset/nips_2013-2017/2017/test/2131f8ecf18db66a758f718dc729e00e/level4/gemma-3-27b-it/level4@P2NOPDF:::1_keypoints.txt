- Studies query complexity of clustering where a learner queries an oracle to test whether two instances share a cluster.  
- Model generalizes beyond pairwise comparisons to any fixed binary function of instance labels.  
- Provides information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on queries needed for perfect and approximate recovery.  
- Extends results to settings with a noisy oracle.  
- The approach is novel and practically relevant (e.g., record deduplication, crowdsourcing).  
- Major limitation: no structural assumptions or hypothesis class are imposed, making the setting abstract.  
- Consequently, bounds depend mainly on label marginal distributions and oracle noise level.  
- Paper is an important initial step, especially in proving lower bounds and laying groundwork for future work adding realistic assumptions or data distributions.
