

This paper presents an interesting approach to clustering via crowdsourcing, where the goal is to recover original data point labels using answers to similarity cluster queries. The authors establish theoretical bounds on the number of queries required for label recovery under both noisy and noiseless settings, with empirical validation. The paper is well-written and accessible to a general machine learning audience, particularly for its clear exposition of the information-theoretic connections used to derive the bounds.  

I have the following concerns that should be addressed to strengthen the paper:  
(1) The use of AND queries appears conceptually problematic. In an AND query, the cluster label is effectively provided as part of the answer (rather than merely indicating whether data points belong to the same cluster), which conflicts with the core motivation of crowdsourcing. If the cluster label is already known (as implied by the AND query structure), it is unclear why similarity queries would be necessary instead of directly requesting the label for individual data points. This undermines the fundamental premise of the approach and requires clarification.  
(2) The expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 could be simplified while retaining their theoretical essence.  
(3) The experimental section solely considers AND queries. If this is the case, the experimental results are invalidated unless the conceptual issue raised in (1) is resolved and justified.  

I am satisfied with the authors' response to these concerns. I recommend acceptance.
