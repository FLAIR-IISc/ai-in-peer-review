- Studies query-based clustering where a learner uses same-cluster/binary queries to identify a target clustering.  
- Analyzes information-theoretic query complexity for exact and approximate recovery, including noisy-oracle scenarios.  
- Addresses well-motivated applications like record deduplication and crowdsourcing.  
- Provides novel, information-theoretic bounds that are valuable to the ML community.  
- Analysis is rigorous and yields tight upper and lower bounds.  
- Identifies label distribution (beyond noise level) as the primary parameter controlling query complexity.  
- Deliberately omits structural assumptions to isolate information-theoretic limits.  
- Lack of structural constraints limits applicability to standard learning-theoretic and practical clustering settings.  
- Offers a foundational theoretical baseline and useful lower-bound techniques for interactive clustering.  
- Represents an important first step and a pathway for future work that incorporates structural assumptions.
