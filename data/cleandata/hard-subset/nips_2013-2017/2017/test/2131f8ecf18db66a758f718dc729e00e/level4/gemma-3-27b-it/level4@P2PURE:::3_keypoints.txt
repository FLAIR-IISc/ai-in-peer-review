- Paper is interesting, well-written, and theoretically accessible to a general ML audience.  
- Tackles clustering via crowdsourcing, aiming to recover original labels from similarity queries.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on number of queries required for noisy and noiseless settings, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Connection to Information Theory for deriving bounds is clearly explained.  
- Major concern: use of AND queries deviates from typical crowdsourcing and may directly reveal cluster labels.  
- If AND queries reveal labels, it’s unclear why similarity queries are needed instead of direct label queries (primary confusion).  
- Ask whether expressions in Theorems 1–4 can be simplified without losing core meaning.  
- Experiments appear to focus solely on AND queries, making the experimental validity questionable until the AND-query concern is resolved.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
