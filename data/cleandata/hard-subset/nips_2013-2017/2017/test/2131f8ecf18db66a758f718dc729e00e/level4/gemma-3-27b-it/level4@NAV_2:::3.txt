This is an interesting paper that explores connections between semisupervised clustering via crowdsourcing and locally encodable source coding. The authors investigate the query complexity of learning labels (or performing clustering) by posing pairwise queries to an oracle, considering both noiseless and noisy query scenarios. A key contribution is proposing the use of AND queries as an alternative to traditional ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries, demonstrating theoretical advantages in certain settings. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the theoretical underpinnings, making it accessible to a broad machine learning audience. The connection to information theory for deriving bounds is particularly well-articulated.

I have several concerns that, if addressed, would strengthen the paper. 

(1) The core motivation for employing AND queries requires further clarification. The authors argue that AND queries offer advantages, but this seems to deviate from the typical crowdsourcing paradigm. An AND query effectively elicits information about the labels themselves, rather than simply a binary response regarding cluster membership. This raises the question: if the goal is to learn the labels, why not directly query for them? Addressing this apparent tension is crucial for fully appreciating the contribution.

(2) The expressions for the derived bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 are somewhat complex. While mathematical rigor is important, exploring potential simplifications—without sacrificing accuracy—could enhance readability and facilitate broader understanding of the key insights.

(3) The experimental evaluation primarily focuses on AND queries. Without a comparative analysis against ‘same cluster’ queries under equivalent conditions (specifically, addressing the point raised in (1) regarding the information elicited by each query type), the experimental validation feels incomplete. A more direct comparison would convincingly demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed AND query approach.

Overall, the paper presents a theoretically sound and potentially impactful contribution. Addressing the above concerns would significantly enhance its clarity, motivation, and empirical validation. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper establishes a novel and insightful connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.
*   The theoretical analysis provides rigorous bounds on query complexity, advancing our understanding of the fundamental limits of crowdsourced clustering.
*   The proposal of AND queries as an alternative to XOR queries offers a promising direction for improving query efficiency.
*   The writing is clear and the presentation is well-organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The motivation for AND queries is not fully convincing and requires further elaboration.
*   The complexity of the derived bounds may hinder accessibility.
*   The experimental evaluation lacks a direct comparison against existing methods (specifically, ‘same cluster’ queries with comparable information gain).
