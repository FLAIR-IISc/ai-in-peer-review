- Paper is engaging, studies clustering via crowdsourcing, and focuses on recovering item labels from same-cluster queries.  
- Authors derive query-complexity bounds for both noiseless and noisy settings; contributions are primarily theoretical with supporting experiments.  
- Exposition is clear and the information-theoretic framing for the bounds is well articulated.  
- Major concern: AND queries may contradict the crowdsourcing motivation since they can reveal a cluster label, so it’s unclear why not directly request item labels.  
- Request to simplify/streamline the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 while keeping essential content.  
- Experimental clarification needed: experiments appear to use only AND queries, which should be justified given the concern in point 1.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
