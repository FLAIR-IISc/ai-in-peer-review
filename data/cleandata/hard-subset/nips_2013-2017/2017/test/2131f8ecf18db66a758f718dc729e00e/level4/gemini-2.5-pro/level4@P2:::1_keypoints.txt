- Frames semi-supervised clustering with non-adaptive constant-arity queries as a locally encodable source coding problem.  
- Provides bounds for noiseless exact recovery (k=2), showing fewer than n queries can suffice with known label priors.  
- Gives a new lower bound for noiseless approximate recovery and shows XOR ("same-cluster") queries are limited in this regime.  
- Proposes AND queries for approximate/noisy settings and proves they can outperform XOR queries with a smaller query budget.  
- Presents an AND-based algorithm for noisy approximate recovery achieving (1-δ) accuracy with O(n log(k/δ)) queries, validated by a crowdsourcing experiment.  
- Novelty: the source-coding perspective is original and brings new theoretical tools and fundamental limits to the problem.  
- Theoretical rigor: comprehensive upper and lower bounds across noiseless/noisy and exact/approximate settings.  
- Practical query design: AND queries are theoretically justified and empirically shown to be easier/more accurate for humans.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explains the information-theoretic viewpoint accessibly.  
- Limitation: relies on i.i.d. labels from a known prior, neglecting feature/structure-based clustering common in practice.  
- Limitation: decoding complexity is under-discussed; some proposed decoders (e.g., ML) may be computationally intractable for large n.  
- Limitation: experimental validation is small-scale (200 items, 10 volunteers); larger benchmark evaluations are needed.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with original insights and practical query design; limitations are noted but do not outweigh the paper's value.
