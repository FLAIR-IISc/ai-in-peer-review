- Paper studies reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-size queries, gives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, proposes an AND-queries algorithm, and evaluates it on real data.  
- Overall critique: presentation, clarity of results, and comparisons to prior work are weak; significant revision and further development needed.  
- Restriction on repeating queries seems unjustified and possibly harmful; need strong justification or comparisons to settings allowing repeated queries (e.g., independent annotators).  
- Theorem 1 overlaps with prior work ([Ahn-Lee-Suh]) and shows apparent discrepancies in query-complexity claims (examples with p=1/2, constant Δ); the theorem and proof need clarification.  
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s difficult to translate into m as a function of n and δ to assess gains from relaxed constraints.  
- Theorem 4’s upper bound is likewise hard to interpret and its relation to Theorem 3’s lower bound (and possible unbounded gap) is unclear; the proof is also difficult to follow.  
- Figure 2 fails to compare the proposed method to the standard same-cluster query method (only compares to its lower bound); claims of outperforming same-cluster queries are not supported.  
- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes (Theorem 6); practicality and how to obtain this info in practice are not justified, and the pseudo-code is hard to follow without deep proofs.  
- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster queries), undermining claims of superiority of the AND-queries algorithm.  
- Rebuttal did not clarify whether repeating the same question yields no benefit; if the cited Nature paper supports this, it should be explicitly clarified.  
- The paper needs a more detailed discussion comparing to the same-cluster query method to substantiate relative performance claims.
