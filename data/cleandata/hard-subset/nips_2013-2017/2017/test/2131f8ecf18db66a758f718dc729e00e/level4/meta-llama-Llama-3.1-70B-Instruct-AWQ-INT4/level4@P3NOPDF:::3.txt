This paper presents an interesting approach to clustering data points via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering original labels based on answers to similar cluster queries. The authors derive bounds on the number of queries required to recover labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers. The main contribution of the paper is theoretical, supplemented by empirical simulations. The writing is clear, making the theoretical contributions accessible to a general machine learning audience. Notably, the authors effectively explain the connection to information theory in deriving the bounds.

I have several concerns that, if addressed, could further improve the paper:

1. The concept of AND queries, while theoretically intriguing, appears to contradict the original motivation for crowdsourcing. Specifically, AND queries seem to provide the label of the cluster as part of the answer, rather than simply indicating whether the data points belong to the same cluster. This raises questions about the necessity of similarity queries. Why not ask for the label of a data point directly? Clarifying this point is essential to understanding the contributions of the paper.

2. It would be beneficial to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, if possible, while maintaining their essence. This could enhance the readability and impact of the theoretical results.

3. The experimental section appears to only consider AND queries. If this is the case, the experimental study may be limited unless the concerns regarding AND queries (point 1) are addressed.

I am satisfied with the authors' responses to these concerns and believe that they have adequately addressed my questions. Therefore, I vote for the acceptance of this paper.
