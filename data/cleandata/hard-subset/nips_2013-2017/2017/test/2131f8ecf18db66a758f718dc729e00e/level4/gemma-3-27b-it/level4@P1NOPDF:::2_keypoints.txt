- Paper studies reconstructing true labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-element queries, gives lower bounds and an AND-query algorithm with real-data evaluation.
- Overall contributions are undermined by poor presentation, unclear claims, and weak comparison to prior work; substantial revision needed.
- Restricting query repetition is questionable and may hurt performance; authors need strong justification or to analyze the unrestricted setting (e.g., independent annotators).
- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (e.g., differing m scalings); the discrepancy and Theorem 1’s proof require detailed clarification.
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s unclear how m scales with n and δ and what improvement δ provides.
- Theorem 4 suffers the same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; clarity is needed on the gap between upper and lower bounds and whether the gap can be unbounded.
- Proofs overall are difficult to follow and need clearer exposition and justification.
- Figure 2 only compares to a same-cluster query lower bound, not the actual same-cluster query method; the claimed outperforming of same-cluster queries is unsupported.
- Algorithm 1 depends on prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes; authors must explain how these are obtained in practice and justify algorithm practicality.
- Algorithm pseudo-code and its connection to Theorem 5 are hard to follow without clearer proofs and exposition.
- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), which is necessary to support performance claims.
- Rebuttal did not fully resolve whether repeating questions is detrimental; clarify whether cited experiments (e.g., the Nature paper) support forbidding repetition.
- Rebuttal’s discussion of comparisons to same-query methods needs to be expanded and integrated into the main paper for clarity.
