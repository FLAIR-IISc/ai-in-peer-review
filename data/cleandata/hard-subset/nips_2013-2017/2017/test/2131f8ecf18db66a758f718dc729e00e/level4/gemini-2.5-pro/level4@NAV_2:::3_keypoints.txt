- Establishes a novel equivalence between semi-supervised clustering with non-adaptive queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed for exact and approximate label recovery.  
- Shows standard pairwise 'same cluster' (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery.  
- Introduces and analyzes 'AND' queries, proving their superiority in both noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Provides theoretical analysis supported by a real-world crowdsourcing experiment validating AND queries.  
- Strength: original theoretical framework linking clustering and locally encodable coding.  
- Strength: well-motivated proposal and rigorous analysis of AND queries for practical approximate recovery.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well organized, making complex information-theoretic concepts accessible.  
- Strength: comprehensive treatment covering exact/approximate recovery and noisy/noiseless models.  
- Weakness: practical motivation for AND queries in crowdsourcing is unclear since workers may need to know labels anyway.  
- Weakness: main theoretical bounds (Theorems 1–4) are complex and lack intuitive/asymptotic interpretation.  
- Weakness: experiments lack a direct empirical baseline comparison using 'same cluster' queries and corresponding algorithms on the same dataset.
