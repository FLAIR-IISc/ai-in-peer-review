- Presents clustering via crowdsourcing to recover original labels from similarity-query responses.
- Proposes bounds on the number of queries required for label recovery in noisy and noiseless settings.
- Primary contribution is theoretical, with empirical simulations supporting results.
- Theoretical findings are clearly and accessibly communicated for a general ML audience.
- The use of information-theoretic tools to derive the bounds is well-explained.
- Concern: AND queries may contradict the crowdsourcing motivation by effectively revealing cluster labels, undermining the need for similarity queries.
- Suggestion: simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 where possible.
- Concern: experiments focus solely on AND queries, which is problematic unless the AND-query issue is clarified.
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
