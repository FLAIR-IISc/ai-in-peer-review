- Studies non-adaptive query-based semi-supervised clustering with Δ-ary queries and links it to locally encodable source coding.  
- Develops information-theoretic lower bounds and matching/near-matching upper bounds for noiseless exact, noiseless approximate, and noisy approximate recovery (k classes).  
- Shows standard pairwise “same-cluster” (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery; AND-style queries can dramatically reduce query complexity.  
- Frames queries as a locally encodable compressor, yielding locality-aware lower bounds that refine m ≥ nH(p).  
- Achievability: O(n log(1/δ)) queries suffice to recover a (1−δ) fraction of labels under noise.  
- Noiseless exact recovery (k=2): Δ-ary XOR queries recover all labels with m ≈ n(h(p)+o(1))/log(1/α), improving over naive and random-query baselines.  
- Locality-aware lower bound shows necessity can exceed nH(p) for some p, sharpening classical limits.  
- Noiseless approximate (k=2): Locality imposes extra distortion; XOR queries are inefficient for approximation per linear rate–distortion results.  
- Proposes AND-query scheme (Poisson left-degree, threshold decoding) that achieves substantially better δ for given m with closed-form error bounds.  
- Noisy approximate (general k): k-ary AND queries plus counting/thresholding recover (1−δ) fraction with m = O(n log(k/δ)) under i.i.d. noise using dependency-graph concentration.  
- Methods can work with known priors or just cluster-size vectors via randomized query graphs.  
- Experiments: small crowdsourcing study (n=200) shows AND queries yield fewer worker errors than same-cluster queries and empirical error matches theory as d increases.  
- Includes synthetic comparison to spectral clustering on same-cluster answers.  
- Technical soundness: framing and bounds are solid; converses integrating locality are interesting; exact-recovery uses information-theoretic ML decoding (not computationally efficient).  
- Assumptions: i.i.d. labels and independent query errors are strong but standard; sensitivity to mis-specification of priors/noise needs discussion.  
- Completeness: some proofs deferred and XOR lower bound relies on a conjecture except at p=0.5; clarity needed on which results are unconditional.  
- Clarity: readable overall but needs clearer distinctions between labeling vs clustering, unified notation, explicit statement about ML decoding being infeasible, and discussion of k-ary practicality.  
- Originality: equivalence to locally encodable source coding and locality-aware lower bounds are novel; AND vs XOR insight is new and compelling.  
- Significance: advances theoretical limits of query-based clustering and suggests better query primitives for crowdsourcing, though practical impact limited by assumptions and small experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: provide unconditional XOR lower bound beyond p=0.5, analyze robustness to mis-specified p/q and propose adaptive calibration, discuss worker heterogeneity/correlated errors and majority-vote integration, expand experiments (larger datasets, stronger baselines, k>2 implementations), and comment on adaptivity benefits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical connection, locality-aware lower bounds, simple provable algorithms (O(n log(k/δ))), and practical insight favoring AND queries.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong modeling assumptions, partially conjectural XOR lower bound, ML decoding impractical for exact recovery, limited experimental validation, and under-discussed practicality of k-ary queries.
