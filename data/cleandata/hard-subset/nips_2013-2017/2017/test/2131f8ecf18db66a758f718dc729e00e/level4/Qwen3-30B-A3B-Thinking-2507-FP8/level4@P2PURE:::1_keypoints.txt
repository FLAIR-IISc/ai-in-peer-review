- Paper studies clustering via oracle queries (same-cluster checks or binary label functions) to identify a target clustering.  
- Focus is on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Adopts an information-theoretic viewpoint to derive results.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds on the number of queries required.  
- Extends the analysis to the case of a noisy oracle.  
- Employs information-theoretic techniques that are novel to the ML community.  
- The query-based model is well-motivated for applications like record deduplication and crowdsourcing.  
- Analysis omits typical structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class) common in learning theory.  
- Consequently, bounds rely mainly on the marginal label distribution and noise level.  
- The setting is therefore notably different from standard learning-theoretic frameworks but is a valuable initial contribution, especially for lower-bound methods.
