- Overall: Paper studies recovering cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries and proposes theoretical bounds + an AND-queries algorithm, but has major issues in presentation, clarity, and comparisons requiring significant revision.

1. Restriction forbidding repeated questions is overly limiting and unjustified; allowing repeats (with independent annotators) could improve noise robustness and should be compared via simulations or considered instead.

2. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (e.g., differing query complexity for p=1/2, constant Δ); this discrepancy and the proof need clarification.

3. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s unclear how m scales with n and δ and how the relaxed constraint improves performance.

4. Same issues apply to Theorem 4; unclear gap between upper and lower bounds in Theorem 3 and whether the gap can be unbounded, plus the proofs are difficult to follow.

5. Figure 2 lacks a comparison to an actual same-cluster query method (only shows a lower bound); claims that the proposed approach outperforms same-cluster queries are unsupported without method-level comparison and intuition about any crossing points.

6. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or relative cluster sizes (per Theorem 6); it’s unclear how this is obtained in practice and the pseudocode is hard to follow without complex proofs.

7. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query method), which is necessary to support performance claims for the AND-queries algorithm.

Additional comments:
- It remains unclear whether repeating the same query is unhelpful; the authors should clarify whether related work (e.g., the Nature paper) addresses this.
- The paper’s response about comparisons to same-query methods should be incorporated and clarified in the revision.
