- Strong, well-written theoretical contribution on recovering labels from cluster similarity queries with bounds for noisy and noiseless models, supported by simulations.
- Clear exposition, especially the connection to Information Theory used to derive the bounds.
- Major concern: use of AND queries may diverge from typical crowdsourcing because AND queries can implicitly reveal labels, calling into question the need for similarity queries versus direct label elicitation.
- Suggestion: simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 if possible.
- Empirical evaluation only uses AND queries, so experimental validity depends on resolving the AND-query/label-revelation issue.
- Reviewer’s concerns were addressed by authors’ responses; recommendation to accept.
