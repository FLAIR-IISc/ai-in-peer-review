- Proposes clustering via crowdsourcing to recover original labels from similarity cluster queries, derives theoretical query bounds for noisy and noiseless settings, and provides empirical validation.
- Paper is well-written and accessible, with a clear exposition of the information-theoretic connections used to derive bounds.
- Major concern: AND queries are conceptually problematic because they effectively reveal cluster labels, conflicting with the crowdsourcing motivation and questioning the need for similarity queries.
- Suggestion: The bound expressions in Theorems 1–4 could be simplified while retaining theoretical substance.
- Experimental concern: Experiments only use AND queries, which may invalidate results unless the conceptual issue about AND queries is addressed and justified.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ response and recommends acceptance.
