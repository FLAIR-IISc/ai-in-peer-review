- Paper studies query-based crowdsourced clustering and provides information-theoretic upper and lower bounds for exact label recovery in noiseless and noisy settings, supported by simulations.  
- Strength: Principled information-theoretic framing yielding sharp bounds and clear dependence on problem parameters.  
- Strength: Covers both noiseless and noisy regimes with explicit parameter dependence.  
- Strength: Clear exposition and notation that make results broadly accessible.  
- Strength: Simulations align with theory and illustrate scaling trends.  
- Concern: Initial confusion about AND queries seeming to reveal labels; authors clarified AND queries are composite, fit the noise model, and can be cheaper/more reliable than label queries in some settings.  
- Concern: Bound expressions in Theorems 1–4 are correct but hard to parse; recommend simplified corollaries and Big-O/Ω summaries in the main text.  
- Concern: Experiments focus on AND queries; while defensible after clarification, empirical comparisons to alternative query types or ablations on query cost/type are needed.  
- Technical quality: Theoretical results are sound, novel in this context, and connect well to information-theoretic limits.  
- Significance: Inclusion of matching upper and lower bounds demonstrates near-optimality in certain regimes and is of interest to interactive learning and crowdsourcing researchers.  
- Clarity: Writing is strong; suggest adding immediate intuition after each theorem, a concise table/bullet list of scaling takeaways, and upfront statement of noise models.  
- Clarity: Recommend concrete examples and interface considerations for AND queries, including potential cognitive load for workers.  
- Experiments: Recommend comparisons across query types under common cost/accuracy budgets, sensitivity analyses to noise and class imbalance, and discussion of latency and worker error modeling.  
- Suggestions: Add simplified corollaries/big-Θ statements, a practical guidance subsection on when to prefer AND queries, and clearer cost-model motivation or empirical justification for focusing on AND queries.  
- Overall: Well-executed theoretical paper with addressed concerns; minor revisions on presentation and empirical scope recommended — Accept.
