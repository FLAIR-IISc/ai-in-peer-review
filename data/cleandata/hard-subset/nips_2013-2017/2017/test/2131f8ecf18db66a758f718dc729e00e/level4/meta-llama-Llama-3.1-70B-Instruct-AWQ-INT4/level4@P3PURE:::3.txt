This paper presents an interesting approach to clustering data points via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering original labels from answers to similar cluster queries. The authors derive bounds on the number of queries required to recover labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers. The paper's primary contribution is theoretical, supplemented by empirical simulations. The writing is clear, making the theoretical contributions accessible to a general machine learning audience. The connection to information theory for deriving the bounds is particularly well-explained.

I have several concerns that I believe could improve the paper:

(1) The concept of AND queries, while theoretically intriguing, seems to contradict the original motivation for crowdsourcing. Specifically, an AND query effectively provides the label of the cluster as part of the answer, rather than simply indicating whether the data points of interest belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, it raises questions about the necessity of these similarity queries. Why not ask for the label directly? This is my primary point of confusion regarding the paper's contributions.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 while preserving their essence?

(3) The experiments appear to only consider AND queries. If this is the case, the experimental study may be limited unless the concerns raised in point (1) are addressed.

I am satisfied with the authors' responses to these questions and therefore support the acceptance of this paper.
