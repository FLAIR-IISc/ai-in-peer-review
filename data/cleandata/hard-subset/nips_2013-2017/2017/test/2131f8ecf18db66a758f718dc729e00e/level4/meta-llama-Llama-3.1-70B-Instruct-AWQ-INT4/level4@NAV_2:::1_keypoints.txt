- Paper studies query-based clustering where learner asks same-cluster (or binary label-function) queries to recover target clustering; analyzes query complexity.  
- Focuses on information-theoretic upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery, including noisy oracle settings.  
- Main contribution: shows equivalence between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.  
- Uses novel information-theoretic tools that are new to the ML community.  
- Derives lower bounds on number of queries from the coding equivalence.  
- Identifies limitations of pairwise same-cluster queries and proposes pairwise AND queries that can perform better.  
- Results are technically sound; proofs are well-presented.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and good related-work discussion.  
- Analysis is limited by lack of structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis classes).  
- Bounds primarily depend on label marginal distribution (and noise level), making setting farther from typical learning-theory scenarios.  
- Despite limitations, provides valuable first-step understanding of information-theoretic limits in query-based clustering.  
- Recommendation: accept (novel result and solid technical contribution), though scope is constrained.
