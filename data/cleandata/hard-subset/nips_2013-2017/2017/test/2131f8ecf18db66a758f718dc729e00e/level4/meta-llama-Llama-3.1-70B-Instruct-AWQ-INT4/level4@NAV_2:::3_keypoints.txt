- Interesting, well-written paper studying crowdsourced clustering via similarity (AND) queries to recover labels under noisy and noiseless answers.  
- Main contribution is theoretical: bounds on the number of queries required, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Information-theoretic connection for deriving bounds is explained clearly and accessibly.  
- Primary concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels directly, conflicting with crowdsourcing motivation—if labels are revealed, why not ask labels directly?  
- Experimental concern: experiments appear to use only AND queries, which is problematic unless the above motivation/interpretation is clarified.  
- Technical suggestion: simplify expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their essence.  
- Reviewer finds the paper technically sound with well-supported claims via analysis and simulations.  
- Paper is clear and easy to understand for a general ML audience.  
- Paper is original (novel AND-query scheme) and potentially significant for crowdsourcing and clustering.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and votes to accept.
