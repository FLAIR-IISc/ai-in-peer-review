- Paper studies reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, small-size, non-adaptive queries, gives lower bounds, proposes an AND-queries algorithm, and presents experiments; overall promising but needs major revisions for clarity and comparison.

- The ban on repeating the same query is insufficiently justified; repeated queries (e.g., via different annotators) are a natural noise-mitigation baseline and should be compared.

- Theorem 1's claimed query complexity for noiseless XOR/same-cluster queries conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) and its proof is hard to follow; this discrepancy needs clarification.

- Theorem 3’s lower bound is presented in a convoluted form that obscures how m scales with n and the target accuracy δ; it should be expressed more transparently.

- Theorem 4’s upper bound is similarly opaque and its gap relative to Theorem 3’s lower bound is unclear; proofs are also difficult to follow.

- Figure 2 compares to a lower bound for same-cluster queries but omits actual same-cluster algorithm performance, so claims that AND queries outperform same-cluster queries are unverified.

- Algorithm 1 depends on knowing p or relative cluster sizes (Theorem 6); the paper lacks practical methods to obtain these parameters and the pseudocode/proofs are hard to parse.

- Experimental figures (3–5) report the proposed algorithm’s results but do not compare against state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster queries), undermining empirical claims.
