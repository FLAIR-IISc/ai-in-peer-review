1. Studies clustering from noisy non-adaptive small-element queries, proving query lower bounds and proposing an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation.  
2. Presentation, clarity of claims, and related-work comparisons are weak; substantial revisions (and possibly new work) are needed.  
3. Forbidding repeated queries seems overly restrictive and may hurt performance; authors must justify this constraint or compare to the unrestricted model empirically.  
4. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work ([Ahn-Lee-Suh]) in scaling (e.g., m = n/log2 vs. m = n log n/Δ); the discrepancy and the proof need clear explanation.  
5. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is presented unclearly; it should give an explicit, interpretable bound on m as a function of n and δ.  
6. Theorem 4 has the same interpretability issue and lacks clear comparison to Theorem 3’s lower bound (possible unbounded gap); its proof is also hard to follow.  
7. Figure 2 compares only to a same-cluster lower bound, not to an actual same-cluster-query method, so claims of outperforming such schemes are unsupported.  
8. Algorithm 1 seems to require prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios; practical methods for obtaining these parameters are not explained and pseudocode is hard to parse.  
9. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster-query methods), which is essential to substantiate performance claims.  
10. Rebuttal addressed some points, but it remains unclear whether repeating the same query is truly unhelpful; authors should clarify if the cited Nature paper demonstrated this.  
11. The detailed rebuttal discussion—especially comparisons with same-query methods—should be incorporated into the paper for clarity.
