- Connects semisupervised clustering via crowdsourcing to locally encodable source coding.  
- Studies query complexity for label recovery under noisy and noiseless pairwise queries.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to “same cluster” queries, claiming theoretical advantages in some settings.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; the information-theoretic derivation of bounds is clearly presented.  
- Motivation for AND queries is unclear: authors should justify why soliciting label-like information fits crowdsourcing goals.  
- Must address whether AND queries deviate from typical crowdsourcing paradigms and why they are preferable to direct label queries.  
- Theorem expressions (1–4) are complex; simplifying or providing intuitive parameter interpretations would improve readability.  
- Experiments evaluate only AND queries, so empirical claims about superiority over same-cluster queries are unvalidated.  
- Include a baseline comparison with same-cluster queries to strengthen experimental validation and generalizability.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with potential impact but needs clearer practical motivation and more complete experiments.  
- For acceptance: novel theoretical connection, meaningful lower bounds, promising AND-query idea, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient practical justification for AND queries, inaccessible complexity of expressions, and incomplete experimental comparison.
