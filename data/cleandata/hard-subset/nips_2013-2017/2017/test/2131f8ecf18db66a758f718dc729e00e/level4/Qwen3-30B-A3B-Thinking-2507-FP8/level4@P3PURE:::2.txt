

This paper investigates a clustering problem where the goal is to reconstruct true labels from noisy, non-adaptive query-answers, each involving only a few elements. It derives lower bounds on query complexity for exact or partial recovery across various scenarios and proposes an "AND-queries" algorithm applicable to arbitrary k-cluster settings, evaluated on real data. While the work attempts theoretical analysis, algorithm development, and empirical validation, it suffers from limitations in presentation, clarity of results, and comparison to related work. Significant revisions—including potential new theoretical developments or empirical comparisons—may be required for publication.  

1. **Query Restriction Justification**: The constraint prohibiting repeated queries appears restrictive. Allowing repeated queries could mitigate noise more effectively, as it provides independent samples. The authors cite an independence-vs-dependence concern for multiple answers to the same query, but this can be resolved by assigning each query to distinct annotators (ensuring independent answers). If the authors intend to justify the restriction, they must provide extensive real-data simulations comparing their algorithm against one tailored for the unrestricted case, demonstrating superior performance. Otherwise, the unrestricted case is more plausible for investigation.  

2. **Theorem 1 vs. [Ahn-Lee-Suh]**: The noiseless exact-recovery setting with XOR-based "same-cluster" queries was recently studied in [Ahn-Lee-Suh] under non-adaptive measurement. The claimed query complexity in Theorem 1 (e.g., \(m = \Theta(n / \log n)\) when \(p = 1/2\) and \(\Delta\) is constant) conflicts with [Ahn-Lee-Suh]'s result (\(m = \Theta(n \log n / \Delta)\)). This discrepancy must be clarified, as the query models differ (XOR vs. AND). Additionally, Theorem 1's proof is difficult to follow.  

3. **Theorem 3 Interpretability**: The lower bound expression for \(\delta\) is opaque. Specifically, it is unclear how to derive a bound on \(m\) in terms of \(n\) and \(\delta\), which would directly reveal performance gains from the relaxed constraint (quantified by \(\delta\)).  

4. **Theorem 4 and Gap Analysis**: The same interpretability issues as in Theorem 3 apply to Theorem 4. Moreover, the gap between the upper bound (Theorem 4) and the lower bound (Theorem 3) is unaddressed—this gap may be unbounded, which requires clarification. The proofs for both theorems are also hard to follow.  

5. **Figure 2 Interpretation**: The figure compares the proposed method against the *lower bound* for same-cluster queries but omits comparison to the *same-cluster-query method* itself. Without this, the claim that "the AND-queries algorithm outperforms the same-cluster-query scheme" (e.g., in Section 4.3) is unsupported. If a crossing point exists with the method (not just the bound), the intuition behind it should be explained.  

6. **Algorithm 1 Practicality**: The algorithm requires prior knowledge of \(p\) (noise parameter) or relative cluster sizes (per Theorem 6). The paper lacks justification for how this information is obtained in practice, undermining the algorithm's practicality. The pseudo-code is also hard to follow without relying on the opaque proof of Theorem 5.  

7. **Lack of State-of-the-Art Comparison**: Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods like the same-cluster-query scheme. This is critical to support the paper’s central claim that AND-queries outperform existing approaches.  

**Additional Comments**:  
- The rebuttal addresses some points, but it remains unclear whether repeating queries is *unhelpful* (e.g., as suggested by the "Nature paper"). Clarification in revision is essential.  
- Regarding the same-cluster-query comparison: A detailed discussion of the results (as addressed in the rebuttal) should be included to strengthen claims.  

[Ahn-Lee-Suh]: K. Ahn, K. Lee, and C. Suh, "Community recovery in hypergraphs," *Proceedings of Allerton Conference on Communication, Control, and Computing*, 2016.
