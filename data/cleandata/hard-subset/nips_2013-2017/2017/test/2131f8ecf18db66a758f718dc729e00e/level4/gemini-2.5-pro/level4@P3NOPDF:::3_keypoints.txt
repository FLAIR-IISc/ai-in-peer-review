- Main contribution: derives information-theoretic lower bounds on number of "same-cluster" queries required for exact label recovery in both noiseless and noisy settings, supported by simulations.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and organized, making theory accessible to a general ML audience.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive explanation of the information-theoretic connection underlying the performance bounds.  
- Novelty: introduces novel query models that offer an interesting theoretical perspective on clustering via crowdsourcing.  
- Concern 1 (AND query motivation): practical justification for the AND query model is unclear—if workers return cluster labels when all points match, a direct label query seems simpler and more efficient.  
- Concern 2 (bound interpretability): Theorem expressions are complex; authors should provide simplified or asymptotic forms to clarify scaling with number of points, clusters, and noise.  
- Concern 3 (experiments scope): Empirical study only evaluates AND-query algorithms; include comparisons with standard pairwise similarity queries to provide meaningful baselines.  
- Recommendation: overall interesting and solid theory, but acceptance should depend on satisfactorily addressing the AND query motivation and improving interpretability and empirical comparisons.
