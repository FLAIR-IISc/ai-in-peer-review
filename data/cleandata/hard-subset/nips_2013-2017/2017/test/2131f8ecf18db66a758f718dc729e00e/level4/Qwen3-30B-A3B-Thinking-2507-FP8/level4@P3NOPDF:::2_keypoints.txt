- Paper studies non-adaptive clustering with noisy small-size pairwise queries, gives bounds and an AND-queries algorithm but presentation lacks rigor and justification.
- Restriction forbidding repeated queries is unjustified; repeated queries with independent annotators could reduce noise and the paper does not compare to an unrestricted baseline.
- Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh]; the discrepancy and differing assumptions/formulations are not clarified.
- Proofs (Theorem 1 and others) are opaque and require substantial elaboration for verification.
- Theorems 3–4 give bounds on δ that are hard to interpret; relationship between m and n and the gap between bounds are unclear and possibly large.
- Experimental claims that AND-queries outperform same-cluster-query methods are unsubstantiated because comparisons use only lower bounds, not actual algorithmic baselines.
- Figures 2–5 omit direct comparisons to state-of-the-art same-cluster-query algorithms, undermining empirical validation of claimed superiority.
- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of noise rate p or cluster-size ratios, but the paper does not explain how these are obtained or discuss this limitation.
- Pseudocode and algorithmic implementation heavily rely on hard-to-follow theoretical proofs, making reproducibility and practicality unclear.
- Rebuttal assertions (e.g., repetition being ineffective) lack empirical evidence; a small simulation would strengthen the claim.
- Required revisions before acceptance: resolve Theorem 1 vs. prior work, add comparative experiments to same-cluster-query methods, clarify interpretation of Theorems 3–4, and justify/handle Algorithm 1’s required prior knowledge.
