- Presents a theoretical analysis of clustering via crowdsourcing using cluster-similarity (AND) queries under noisy and noiseless settings, with novel query-complexity bounds and empirical validation.  
- Strong, well-structured exposition, especially the information-theoretic connections that make results accessible to ML audiences.  
- Concern 1: AND query model seems to conflict with crowdsourcing motivation—if queries reveal cluster labels, directly asking for point labels would be simpler and more practical.  
- Paper must explicitly justify the practical relevance of AND queries and explain why direct labeling is not used.  
- Concern 2: Theorems 1–4 produce complex bounds; presenting simpler, interpretable equivalents that highlight dependence on noise, cluster sizes, or dimensions would improve impact.  
- Concern 3: Experiments only evaluate the AND query model, leaving practical viability unclear; authors should clarify whether other query types were considered or justify focusing solely on AND queries.  
- Reviewers found the authors’ responses satisfactory in resolving the AND-query motivation issue.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, and valuable theoretical contribution; recommends acceptance contingent on clarifying practical motivation and simplifying presentation of bounds.
