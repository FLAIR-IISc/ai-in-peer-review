- Paper studies clustering from noisy non-adaptive small-set queries, proves lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, proposes an AND-queries algorithm, and gives real-data evaluation.

- Overall assessment: theoretical analyses and algorithm are interesting but the presentation, clarity, and comparison to prior work are insufficient; significant revision needed.

1. The "no repeated questions" constraint is unrealistic and may hurt performance; authors should justify it with extensive real-data comparisons or instead analyze the unrestricted case (e.g., repeated queries to independent annotators).

2. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] on noiseless exact recovery (XOR/same-cluster queries) — the stated query complexities disagree and the proof is hard to follow; this discrepancy requires clarification.

3. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret, preventing a clear relation of m to n and δ and obscuring the effect of relaxing constraints.

4. The same interpretability and proof-complexity issues apply to Theorem 4; additionally, the gap between upper and lower bounds in Theorem 3 can be unbounded.

5. Figure 2 omits a comparison to the same-cluster query method (only shows its lower bound), so the claim that the proposed approach outperforms same-cluster queries is unsupported.

6. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios (per Theorem 6); how to obtain these in practice is unclear, and the pseudocode and proof (Theorem 5) are difficult to follow, questioning practicality.

7. Figures 3–5 do not compare to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), which is necessary to substantiate claims that the AND-queries algorithm outperforms alternatives.

- Rebuttal did not fully resolve whether repeating the same question can help; this should be clarified with citations to relevant literature (e.g., a Nature paper).

- The paper should include the detailed comparison/discussion with the same-query method (as mentioned in the authors’ response) to make evaluation comprehensive.
