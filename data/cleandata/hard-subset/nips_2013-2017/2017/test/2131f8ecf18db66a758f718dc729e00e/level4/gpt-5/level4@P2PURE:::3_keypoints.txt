- Studies clustering via crowdsourcing, deriving query-complexity bounds for recovering true labels from same-cluster–type queries in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Contribution is primarily theoretical with supporting simulations; paper is clearly written and information-theoretic derivations are well explained.  
- Concern: AND queries appear to reveal cluster labels directly, which conflicts with the crowdsourcing motivation — if so, why not request labels instead of similarity queries?  
- Question: Can the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 be simplified while preserving their essential content?  
- Concern: Experiments seem to use only AND queries, making empirical evaluation hard to interpret unless the AND-query motivation/implications are clarified.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses to these issues and recommends acceptance.
