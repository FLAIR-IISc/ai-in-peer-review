- Paper studies reconstructing labels from noisy, non-adaptive few-element queries, gives lower bounds, proposes an AND-queries algorithm, and shows real-data evaluation, but overall suffers from presentation, clarity, and insufficient comparisons, needing major revision.

- Query restriction: forbidding repeated questions is unrealistic and may hurt performance; strong justification and extensive real-data comparisons to a repeat-allowed baseline are required (or assume independent annotators if repeats are used).

- Theorem 1: result conflicts with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) in key regimes (e.g., p=1/2, constant Δ); discrepancy and the proof need clear reconciliation and easier exposition.

- Theorem 3: lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s unclear how m scales with n and δ and what performance gain the relaxed constraint yields.

- Theorem 4: same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; unclear gap between upper and lower bounds and whether that gap can be unbounded; proofs are difficult to follow.

- Figure 2: compares only to the same-cluster-query lower bound, not the actual same-cluster-query method; need to show where/if the proposed method outperforms the method and provide intuition for any crossover.

- Algorithm 1: requires prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios—practicality of obtaining this information is unexplained and pseudo-code is hard to parse without opaque proofs.

- Figures 3–5: lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), so claims of outperforming existing schemes are unsupported.

- Additional/rebuttal comment: authors must clarify whether prohibiting repeats is beneficial (and whether cited works support that), and expand the comparison/discussion with the same-query method as promised in rebuttal.
