1. Overall: Paper studies label recovery from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries, gives lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm with real-data tests, but has presentation, clarity, and comparison issues needing major revision.

2. Restriction against repeating the same query seems unrealistic and may worsen performance; authors should justify it or compare to the no-restriction case (e.g., via independent annotators).

3. Claimed noiseless exact-recovery result conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh]; the discrepancy (e.g., differing query complexities) and the proof of Theorem 1 need detailed clarification.

4. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s not clear how m scales with n and δ to show benefits from relaxing exact recovery.

5. Theorem 4 has the same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; the gap between its upper and lower bounds is unclear and may be unbounded, and the proof is hard to follow.

6. Figure 2 compares only to a same-cluster query lower bound, not to the actual same-cluster-query method, so claims of outperforming that method are unsupported and potential crossing behavior is unexplored.

7. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster relative sizes; practical ways to obtain these parameters are not provided and the pseudocode is difficult to follow without opaque proofs.

8. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query schemes), undermining claims that AND-queries outperform existing approaches.

9. Rebuttal did not resolve whether repeating the same question can be helpful; the paper should clarify this (possibly citing Nature or using annotator-independence assumptions).

10. Authors should incorporate the detailed comparison/discussion with same-query methods (as touched on in their response) into a revision for clarity.
