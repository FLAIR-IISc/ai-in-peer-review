This is an interesting paper that explores the task of clustering data points via crowdsourcing, where the goal is to recover original labels of data points based on answers to similar cluster queries. The authors propose bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers for the queries. The paper's main contribution is theoretical, accompanied by some empirical simulations. The writing is clear, making the theoretical contributions accessible to a general machine learning audience. Notably, the connection to information theory for deriving the bounds is well-explained.

I have several concerns that could help improve the paper:

(1) The concept of AND queries, although theoretically interesting, seems to contradict the original motivation for crowdsourcing. Specifically, in the case of an AND query, the label of the cluster is effectively given as part of the answer, rather than just determining whether the data points of interest belong to the same cluster or not. If this interpretation is correct, it raises questions about the necessity of asking similarity queries. Why not ask for the label of a data point directly? This is my primary point of confusion regarding the paper's contributions.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, while preserving their essence?

(3) The experiments appear to only consider AND queries. If this is the case, the validity of the experimental study is questionable, unless the concerns raised in point (1) are addressed.

I am satisfied with the authors' response to these questions and therefore vote for the acceptance of this paper.
