- Proposes an interesting crowdsourcing-based clustering method framing recovery of data labels from cluster-similarity queries.  
- Derives theoretical bounds on the number of queries needed for accurate recovery in noisy and noiseless settings.  
- Primary contribution is rigorous theoretical analysis, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; the information-theoretic connection is clearly explained.  
- Main concern: use of AND queries may effectively reveal cluster labels, deviating from typical crowdsourcing motivations.  
- If AND queries reveal labels, it questions why not directly query labels, which impacts novelty and practical relevance.  
- Bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex; exploring simplifications would improve accessibility and impact.  
- Experimental evaluation focuses solely on AND queries without clear justification, raising concerns about validity and generalizability.  
- Authors’ responses were thorough and convincing; reviewer votes to accept.
