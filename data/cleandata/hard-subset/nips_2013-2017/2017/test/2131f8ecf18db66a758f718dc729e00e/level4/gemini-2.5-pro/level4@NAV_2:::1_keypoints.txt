- Connects semi-supervised clustering with oracle queries to the information-theoretic problem of locally encodable source coding.  
- Task: recover ground-truth labels of n items into k clusters using a minimum number of non-adaptive, small-arity binary queries.  
- Derives information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Analyzes both noiseless and noisy oracle models.  
- Novelty: framing the query-based clustering problem as a source-coding problem is original and brings new tools/insights.  
- Proposes AND-queries and shows they are provably superior to standard same-cluster/XOR queries for approximate recovery in noisy settings.  
- Theoretical results appear sound and the analysis covers multiple important settings comprehensively.  
- Includes a real-data crowdsourcing experiment that supports the theoretical advantages of AND-queries.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear in setup and arguments.  
- Main limitation: analysis relies almost exclusively on prior label distribution (relative cluster sizes) rather than structural or feature-based assumptions.  
- This abstraction may reduce direct applicability to typical ML scenarios with geometric separability or instance-feature structure.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with originality and significant findings; strengths outweigh the setting-related limitations.
