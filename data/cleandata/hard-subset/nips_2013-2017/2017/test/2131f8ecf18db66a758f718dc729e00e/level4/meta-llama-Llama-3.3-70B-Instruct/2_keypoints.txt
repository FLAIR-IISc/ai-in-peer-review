- Paper studies non-adaptive clustering with few-element queries, gives query lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm evaluated on real data, but has presentation and comparison issues requiring major revision.
- Disallowing repeated queries is restrictive and may harm performance; authors must justify the model or study the unrestricted case (e.g., repeated queries to independent annotators).
- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) for the noiseless/same-cluster setting; the discrepancy and the proof need clear clarification.
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; authors should restate bounds in terms of m, n, δ to show practical improvements.
- Theorem 4 suffers from similar clarity issues; need comparison between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound and discussion of possible unbounded gaps.
- Proofs (Theorems 1, 3, 4) and overall arguments are difficult to follow and need clearer exposition.
- Figure 2 does not compare to a same-cluster query method (only a lower bound); claims of outperforming same-cluster queries are unsupported and need empirical comparison and intuition for any crossing behavior.
- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster size ratios; practicality is unclear and requires justification or a method to estimate these parameters.
- Algorithm pseudo-code is hard to follow without digging into proofs (Theorem 5/6); present clearer algorithm description and rationale.
- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), which is essential to validate performance claims.
- Rebuttal left unresolved whether repeating the same question is unhelpful; the authors should clarify or cite evidence (e.g., the Nature paper) showing repetition’s (in)effectiveness.
- Rebuttal discussion about comparisons to the same-query method should be incorporated into the paper for clarity.
