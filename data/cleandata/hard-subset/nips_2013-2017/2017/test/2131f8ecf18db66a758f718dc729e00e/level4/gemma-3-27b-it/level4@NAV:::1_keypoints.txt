- Studies active clustering where the learner queries an oracle about whether pairs of instances share the same cluster (binary label function).
- Characterizes query complexity needed for exact and approximate clustering.
- Extends analysis to settings with noisy oracle responses.
- Uses information-theoretic methods to derive upper and lower bounds on query requirements.
- The information-theoretic approach is novel for this clustering/querying problem.
- No structural assumptions or hypothesis-class framework are imposed.
- Analysis is driven by the marginal distribution of labels and the noise level.
- Positions the work as an initial step toward fundamental information-theoretic limits, particularly achievable lower bounds.
- Highlights practical relevance to record linkage and crowdsourcing applications.
