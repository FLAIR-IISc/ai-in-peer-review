1. Studies clustering from noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries, gives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, and proposes an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation.  
2. Major issues in presentation, clarity of claimed results, and related-work comparison; substantial revisions likely needed.  
3. No-repeated-questions constraint is overly restrictive and unjustified—repeating queries is a natural noise-mitigation strategy and should be compared or justified (e.g., via independent annotators).  
4. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) for noiseless exact recovery (discrepant m scalings) and its proof is hard to follow; requires detailed clarification.  
5. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is hard to interpret and not easily inverted to express m as a function of n and δ.  
6. Theorem 4 has similar interpretability issues and it’s unclear how tight the upper bound is relative to Theorem 3’s lower bound; proofs are difficult to parse.  
7. Figure 2 omits empirical comparison to the actual same-cluster-query method (only its lower bound shown), so the claim of outperforming that method is unsupported and crossing-point intuition is missing.  
8. Algorithm 1 appears to require prior knowledge of p or relative cluster sizes—authors must explain how these are obtained in practice and clarify the pseudocode.  
9. Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster queries), weakening empirical claims of superiority.  
10. Rebuttal addressed some points, but it remains unclear whether repeating queries is unhelpful and the detailed same-query comparison from the response should be incorporated into the paper.
