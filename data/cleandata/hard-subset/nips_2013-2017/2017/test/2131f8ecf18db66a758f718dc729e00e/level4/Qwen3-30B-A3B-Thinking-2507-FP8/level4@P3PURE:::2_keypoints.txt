- Overall: Paper studies label reconstruction from noisy, non-adaptive small-element queries, provides lower bounds, an AND-queries algorithm, and experiments, but has presentation, clarity, and comparison shortcomings requiring major revision.

- Query restriction justification: Prohibiting repeated queries is inadequately motivated—repeats could reduce noise via independent annotators and need empirical justification versus the unrestricted case.

- Theorem 1 vs. prior work: Claimed query complexity in Theorem 1 contradicts Ahn–Lee–Suh results for XOR same-cluster queries; differences in query models and a hard-to-follow proof need clarification.

- Theorem 3 interpretability: The lower-bound expression for δ is opaque; it’s unclear how to translate it into m as a function of n and δ to quantify gains.

- Theorem 4 and gap analysis: Theorem 4 suffers the same interpretability issues, and the (potentially large) gap between Theorems 3 and 4 is unaddressed; proofs are difficult to follow.

- Figure 2 interpretation: Comparison is made to a lower bound rather than the actual same-cluster-query method, so claims that AND-queries outperform that method are unsupported without further explanation.

- Algorithm 1 practicality: Algorithm requires prior knowledge of noise parameter p or cluster-size ratios with no practical acquisition strategy provided; pseudo-code is hard to follow absent clearer proofs.

- Lack of state-of-the-art comparison: Empirical figures (3–5) omit comparisons to existing methods like same-cluster queries, weakening claims of superiority.

- Additional comments: Rebuttal partially addresses issues but leaves open whether query repetition is unhelpful and lacks a detailed inclusion of same-cluster comparison results.
