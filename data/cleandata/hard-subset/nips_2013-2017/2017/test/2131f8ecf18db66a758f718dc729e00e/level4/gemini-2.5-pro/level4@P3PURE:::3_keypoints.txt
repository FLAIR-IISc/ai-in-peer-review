- Problem and goal: Crowdsourced clustering to recover true labels using same-cluster queries, with theoretical bounds derived for noiseless and noisy settings.
- Contribution: Primary theoretical contribution supported by empirical simulations; paper is well-written and the information-theoretic derivations are clearly explained.
- Concern 1: Motivation for using AND queries is unclear and may conflict with crowdsourcing practice since AND responses can effectively reveal cluster labels, so it’s unclear why one wouldn’t ask for labels directly.
- Concern 2: The expressions in Theorems 1–4 are complex and could be simplified to improve readability.
- Concern 3: Experiments focus only on AND queries, so empirical validity depends on resolving the practical motivation for those queries.
- Outcome: Authors’ responses addressed the concerns satisfactorily; reviewer recommends acceptance.
