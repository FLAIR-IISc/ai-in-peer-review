- Paper studies non-adaptive, noisy clustering via small-set queries, derives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, and proposes an "AND-queries" algorithm with real-data evaluation.

- Restricting repeated queries is unrealistic and may hurt performance; authors should compare to a no-restriction algorithm or justify by simulations or an independent-annotator model.

- The noiseless exact-recovery result conflicts with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh); Theorem 1’s query complexity and its proof need detailed reconciliation and clarification.

- Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is hard to interpret; the relation between m, n, and δ and the practical implications are not clearly explained.

- Theorem 4 shares the same clarity issues as Theorem 3; the gap between upper and lower bounds is not quantified and may be large or unbounded.

- Figure 2 compares only to a same-cluster query lower bound, not the method; claims of outperforming same-cluster queries are unsupported and missing intuition about potential crossing points.

- Algorithm 1 requires knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios (per Theorem 6); how to obtain these in practice is not addressed and the pseudocode is hard to follow without opaque proofs.

- Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query algorithms), undermining performance claims.

- Overall conclusion: interesting theoretical work and a novel algorithm, but significant improvements in presentation, proof clarity, and empirical comparisons are required for publication.

Strengths:
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis across scenarios.
- Introduces a novel AND-queries algorithm applicable to k clusters.
- Includes real-data simulation for evaluation.

Weaknesses:
- Poor presentation and unclear statements/proofs for several theorems.
- Inadequate comparison to related work and prior methods.
- Practicality concerns due to required prior knowledge and restrictive query model.
- Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to relevant baselines.

Recommendations:
- Improve presentation, proof clarity, and intuitive explanations.
- Reconcile and compare results with prior work (e.g., Ahn-Lee-Suh).
- Add simulations comparing to no-restriction and same-cluster-query methods, including annotator-independence scenarios.
- Justify or remove the no-repetition constraint and address how to estimate required priors.
- Expand empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines to support claims.
