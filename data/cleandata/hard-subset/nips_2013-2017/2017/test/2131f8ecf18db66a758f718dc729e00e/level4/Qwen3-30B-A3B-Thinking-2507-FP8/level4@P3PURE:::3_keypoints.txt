- Proposes a crowdsourced clustering method that recovers original labels from similarity queries, with theoretical bounds for noisy and noiseless settings and empirical validation.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible, with clear exposition of the information-theoretic basis for the bounds.  
- Concern: AND queries may conflict with crowdsourcing motivation because they require workers to know cluster labels, potentially obviating the need for similarity queries—authors must justify this query model.  
- Suggestion: Simplify the expressions in Theorems 1–4 to improve readability without losing theoretical rigor.  
- Concern: Experiments only evaluate AND queries, which is problematic unless the conceptual issue in the first concern is resolved.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
