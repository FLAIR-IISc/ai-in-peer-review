- Main contribution: links semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding and frames clustering as a constrained source coding problem.  
- Problem setting: recovers n cluster labels with nonadaptive binary queries on ≤Δ elements per query.  
- Key insight: "same cluster" (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery; AND queries perform significantly better.  
- Theoretical results: new information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- AND query performance: achieves (1−δ)-recovery with O(n log k / δ) queries, markedly better than same-cluster queries (Theorem 4).  
- Intuition for AND: workers need only know one label to answer correctly, explaining practical advantage.  
- Experimental validation: crowdsourcing experiments show AND queries yield substantially fewer errors (105 vs. 234 out of 1000).  
- Novelty: original connection between clustering and locally encodable source coding and first theoretical justification for AND queries.  
- Quality and rigor: technically sound proofs and well-constructed theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity: well-written, accessible information-theoretic perspective and effective figures.  
- Weakness — practicality: unclear whether AND queries are always feasible for crowdsourcing or require workers to know labels.  
- Weakness — presentation: some theoretical expressions (e.g., α formula) could be simplified for readability.  
- Weakness — experiments: limited empirical comparison between AND and same-cluster queries under identical conditions.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant contribution bridging information theory and ML; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications.
