- Novel theoretical framework linking semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding, yielding nontrivial lower bounds.  
- AND queries proposed as an alternative to "same cluster" (XOR) queries, claimed to improve query complexity for approximate recovery.  
- Clear theoretical guarantees for noiseless and noisy settings, with Theorem 5 giving O(n log k / δ) query complexity for k clusters.  
- Real-world crowdsourcing experiment supports theory: AND queries show much lower error (10.5% vs. 23.4%) than same-cluster queries on the tested dataset.  
- Restrictive query model forbids repeating the same query, a practical limitation that the authors must justify or relax.  
- The no-repetition constraint may be unrealistic since independent repeated worker responses are possible and could change performance comparisons.  
- Inadequate empirical comparison with state-of-the-art same-cluster algorithms—Figure 2 compares only to a lower bound, not practical implementations.  
- Theoretical lower bounds (Theorems 3 and 4) are mathematically opaque and hard to interpret in terms of scaling with n and δ.  
- The expression for δ in Theorem 3 is especially unclear and needs simplified asymptotics or numerical examples.  
- Unclear how tight the gap is between Theorem 4’s upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound—need clarification on boundedness of the gap.  
- Algorithm 1 depends on precise cluster-size priors (p); practical methods for estimating p and robustness to estimation error need discussion and evaluation.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a single dataset, reducing confidence in generalizability.  
- Presentation issues: technically sophisticated but poor exposition of key results makes core contributions hard to grasp.  
- Originality is strong—the coding-clustering connection and AND queries are meaningful advances.  
- Recommendation: revise to justify or adopt repeated-query model, include direct comparisons to existing algorithms, simplify/clarify theoretical results, and address practical prior estimation.
