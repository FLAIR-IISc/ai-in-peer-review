- Paper studies recovering true labels from noisy, non-adaptive limited-size queries and derives lower bounds plus an AND-queries algorithm validated on real data.
- Novel framing: semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.
- Theoretical contribution: attempts to derive information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity.
- Practical proposal: AND queries presented as an alternative to same-cluster (XOR) queries, claimed to be advantageous in noise.
- Empirical validation: experiments on Amazon Mechanical Turk data demonstrate practical relevance.
- Major presentation issues: paper is dense, hard to follow, and lacks intuitive explanations for key claims.
- Proofs are opaque and often lack sufficient detail, making correctness hard to verify.
- Restricted model disallows query repetition, which is limiting and not convincingly justified.
- Lack of comparison to algorithms that allow repetition weakens claims about noise robustness.
- Insufficient positioning relative to related work (e.g., Ahn-Lee-Suh) and inadequate explanation of discrepancies.
- Lower bounds (Theorems 3 & 4) are hard to interpret in practical query-complexity terms; gap to achievable bounds unclear.
- Experimental baseline missing: no comparison to well-implemented same-cluster methods (e.g., spectral clustering) on the dataset.
- Figures (especially Figure 2 and 3–5) omit comparisons to same-cluster query methods, weakening empirical claims.
- Algorithm 1 depends on prior knowledge of cluster sizes/frequencies without discussing how to obtain it; pseudocode needs clarity.
- Claim about Nature paper on query repetition is vague and lacks proper citation or clarification.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel connection, theoretical effort, and some empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation, weak comparisons to prior work, missing experimental baselines, unjustified model restrictions, and impractical prior assumptions.
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage major revision and resubmission after addressing clarity, comparisons, theoretical explanations, and stronger experimental baselines.
