- Studies a query-based clustering model where queries are same-cluster or evaluations of a fixed binary function, aiming to recover a target clustering and analyze query complexity.
- Framework is well-motivated with applications in record deduplication, crowdsourcing, and related domains.
- Provides information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on queries needed for exact and approximate recovery.
- Also analyzes the setting with a noisy oracle.
- Uses information-theoretic techniques that are interesting and novel to the ML community.
- Analysis makes no structural assumptions (no geometry, parametric family, or explicit hypothesis class), limiting the scope.
- Resulting bounds depend mainly on the marginal label distribution and noise level.
- The setting departs from standard learning-theory formulations, so structure-based sharper or more interpretable guarantees are lacking.
- Represents a useful first step toward understanding information-theoretic limits, especially by establishing fundamental lower bounds.
