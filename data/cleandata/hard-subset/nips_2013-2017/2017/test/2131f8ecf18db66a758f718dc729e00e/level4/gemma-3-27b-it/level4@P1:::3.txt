This is an interesting paper. I enjoyed reading it. In the paper, the authors consider the task of clustering data points via crowdsourcing, specifically recovering the original labels of data points based on answers to ‘same cluster’ queries. The authors propose bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels, considering both noisy and noiseless query responses. The main contribution of the paper is theoretical, supported by some empirical simulations. The paper is well-written, making its theoretical contributions accessible to a general machine learning audience. The connection to information theory for deriving these bounds is particularly well explained.

I have the following concerns regarding potential improvements to the paper.

(1) While the idea of AND queries is theoretically interesting, it seems to diverge from the original motivation for crowdsourcing. An AND query effectively elicits information about the label of the cluster itself, rather than simply asking if two data points belong to the same cluster. If this is indeed the case, as I interpret it from the paper, why would one even need to ask these similarity queries? Why not directly ask for the label of a data point? This is my primary point of confusion regarding the paper’s contributions.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, while preserving their essential meaning?

(3) In the experiments, only AND queries appear to be considered. If so, the experimental study is questionable unless the point raised in (1) is clarified. 

I am satisfied with the authors’ responses to my questions. Therefore, I vote for the acceptance of this paper.
