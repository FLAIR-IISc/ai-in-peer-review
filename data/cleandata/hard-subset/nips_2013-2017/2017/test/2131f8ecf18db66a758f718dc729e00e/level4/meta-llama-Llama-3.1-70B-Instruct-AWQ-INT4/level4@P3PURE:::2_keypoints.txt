2. The ban on repeating the same question is restrictive and needs justification via real-data comparisons or by treating repeated queries as independent answers from distinct annotators.

3. Theorem 1 contradicts prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) in the noiseless exact-recovery case (discrepant query complexity formulas) and its proof is hard to follow.

4. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is hard to interpret; the implied relationship between m, n, and δ and the practical improvement from relaxing constraints is unclear.

5. Theorem 4 shares the same clarity issues as Theorem 3; the gap between its upper and lower bounds is unspecified and proofs are difficult to follow.

6. Figure 2 only compares to the same-cluster-query lower bound (not the method), so the claim that the proposed approach outperforms same-cluster queries is unsupported and any crossing behavior is unexplained.

7. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios, raising practicality concerns; the pseudo-code is hard to follow without the complex proofs of Theorem 5/6.

8. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), making performance claims unsubstantiated.
