- Study of a query-based clustering framework where a learner issues same-cluster or other fixed binary queries to an oracle to identify a target clustering.
- Primary focus on the query complexity required to perfectly or approximately recover the clustering.
- Applications and motivation include record deduplication and crowdsourcing; the area is well-motivated and relatively new.
- Information-theoretic approach: the paper derives novel upper and lower bounds on query complexity.
- The authors also analyze settings with a noisy oracle.
- The information-theoretic tools and techniques introduced are novel and of interest to the ML community.
- Analysis is limited by the lack of structural assumptions (no hypothesis class), so bounds rely mainly on the marginal label distribution and noise level.
- The setting departs from conventional learning-theory frameworks, which limits direct comparison to standard results.
- Despite limitations, the work is a valuable first step that yields important insights, particularly about lower bounds.
