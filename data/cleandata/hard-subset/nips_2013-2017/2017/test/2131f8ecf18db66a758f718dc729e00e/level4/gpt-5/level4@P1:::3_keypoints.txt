- Problem studied: semisupervised clustering via nonadaptive, low-arity queries to recover n labels from m (possibly noisy) binary/k-ary answers.  
- Key conceptual contribution: recasts crowdsourced clustering as locally encodable source coding (each answer depends on constant Δ labels).  
- Benefit of viewpoint: enables information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity and motivates new query designs beyond same-cluster (XOR) queries.  
- Noiseless exact recovery (k=2): achievability using Δ-ary XOR queries with m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) queries, improving on naive bounds for some p even with Δ=2; matching lower bounds when Δ constrained.  
- Noiseless approximate recovery: rate–distortion based lower bounds for Δ-local queries; XOR queries are inherently limited for approximation.  
- Alternative query design: proposes AND queries and analyzes a simple decoder on a random bipartite graph, showing strictly better query–error trade-offs than XOR in many regimes.  
- Noisy setting (k≥2): introduces k-ary AND generalization; with d-regular query graphs and thresholding, O(n log(k/δ)) nonadaptive queries suffice to recover (1−δ) fraction under independent per-query noise.  
- Why XOR fails in noisy k-ary case: symmetry removes the expectation gap their algorithm exploits, making XOR less suitable.  
- Experiments: small crowdsourcing study on movie genres showing AND-type queries yield lower error than same-cluster queries and error decreases with more queries.  
- Strength: novel, well-executed link to locally encodable source coding with clear theoretical development.  
- Strength: rigorous information-theoretic bounds in both noiseless and noisy regimes and well-motivated proofs.  
- Strength: insightful identification of XOR limitations and practical benefit of AND queries for approximation.  
- Strength: clear, accessible writing; empirical results align with theory.  
- Concern: practical meaning of AND queries—may leak label information, raising applicability questions compared to asking labels directly.  
- Concern: presentation—Theorems 1–4 have involved expressions and would benefit from simplification/refactoring to highlight dependencies and constants.  
- Concern: experiments focus on AND queries; rationale for evaluating only AND must be explicit, tied to practicality concerns.  
- Reviewer disposition: satisfied after authors’ clarifications about AND practicality; overall coherent and convincing.  
- Evaluation summary: technically sound and original work with meaningful significance and generally clear exposition.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical contributions, practical implications for crowdsourcing query design, and good connection/distinction from prior work.  
- Cons: heavy formulas that could be streamlined and modest-scale experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
