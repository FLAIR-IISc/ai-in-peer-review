- Paper studies crowdsourced clustering, giving query-complexity bounds for noiseless and noisy oracles with strong theoretical contributions and simulations.
- Writing is clear and the information-theoretic framework is well motivated and accessible.
- Major concern: practicality and interpretation of AND queries, which seem to reveal cluster labels and conflict with crowdsourcing motivations—clarify setting, cost model, and feasibility versus asking point labels.
- Theorem statements are complex; simplify by isolating dominant terms, providing big-O/Θ forms, and adding intuition on how parameters affect query complexity.
- Experiments focus on AND queries; either empirically justify the AND-query model (cost/reliability/availability) or include results for standard same-cluster or mixed-query regimes under comparable budgets and noise.
- Reviewer’s concerns were addressed by the authors and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
