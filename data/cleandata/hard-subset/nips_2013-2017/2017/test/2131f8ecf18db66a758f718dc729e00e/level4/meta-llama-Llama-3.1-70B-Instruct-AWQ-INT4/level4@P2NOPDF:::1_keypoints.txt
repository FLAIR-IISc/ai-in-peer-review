- Studies a clustering model where a learner queries an oracle (same-cluster or any fixed binary function of labels) to recover a target clustering.
- Primary focus is on analyzing query complexity for exact and approximate recovery.
- Addresses noisy-oracle scenarios as well as noiseless cases.
- Uses novel information-theoretic tools to derive upper and lower bounds on required queries.
- Motivated by practical applications like record deduplication and crowdsourcing.
- Makes no structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class), relying mainly on the label marginal and noise level for bounds.
- This lack of assumptions limits the analysis and differentiates the setting from typical learning-theory frameworks.
- Provides an important first step toward understanding information-theoretic limits and establishing lower bounds for query-based clustering.
