- Studies clustering where the learner can query an oracle with same-cluster queries or general binary functions of labels.  
- Main goal is to characterize the query complexity needed to identify the target clustering.  
- Provides applications motivation including record deduplication and crowdsourcing.  
- Derives information-theoretic upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Extends analysis to handle noisy oracle responses.  
- Employs novel information-theoretic tools that are of interest to the ML community.  
- Analysis does not rely on structural assumptions or a hypothesis class.  
- Bounds mainly depend on the marginal distribution of labels and the noise level.  
- The studied setting departs from standard learning-theory frameworks, limiting scope.  
- Represents a valuable first step, particularly for establishing lower bounds in query-based clustering.
