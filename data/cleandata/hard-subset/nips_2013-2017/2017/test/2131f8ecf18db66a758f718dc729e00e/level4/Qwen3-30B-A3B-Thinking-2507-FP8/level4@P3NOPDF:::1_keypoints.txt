- Studies query-based clustering and derives information-theoretic query complexity for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Establishes tight upper and lower bounds on queries for both noiseless and noisy oracles.  
- Analysis isolates the role of the label distribution (entropy/cardinality) rather than data geometry.  
- Provides matching bounds showing how query complexity scales with problem parameters and noise level.  
- Uses novel entropy-based information-theoretic techniques, contributing new tools for interactive learning.  
- No structural assumptions (e.g., cluster separability) — results are maximally general but highly abstract.  
- Limitation: bounds are insensitive to data geometry or representational structure, raising applicability questions.  
- Open question: whether mild structural priors could reduce query complexity in practical settings.  
- Noisy oracle analysis is timely and practically relevant for human-in-the-loop systems.  
- Well-written and rigorous; suitable for NeurIPS with minor revisions to clarify scope and limitations.
