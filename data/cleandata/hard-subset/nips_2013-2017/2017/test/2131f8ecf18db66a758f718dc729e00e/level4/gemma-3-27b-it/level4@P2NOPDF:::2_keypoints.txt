- Paper studies reconstruction of true labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries, gives lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm with empirical evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: solid theory and an algorithm but presentation, clarity, and related-work comparisons need major revision before publication.  
- Query restriction: forbidding repeated queries is restrictive and may hurt noise mitigation; strong justification or study of the unrestricted case (e.g., independent annotators) is required.  
- Theorem 1 & related work: conflict with Ahn-Lee-Suh’s prior results (different query complexities); Theorem 1’s proof is hard to follow and needs clarification.  
- Theorems 3 & 4: lower-bound expressions are hard to interpret and don’t yield clear m–n–δ relationships; proofs lack readability.  
- Gap between bounds: unclear relation between Theorem 3’s lower bounds and Theorem 4’s upper bounds and whether the gap can be unbounded; require clarification.  
- Figure 2: comparison only to the same-cluster lower bound, not to the actual same-cluster method; claim of outperforming that scheme is unsupported and crossover behavior needs explanation.  
- Algorithm 1 practicality: requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes—authors must explain how to obtain these in practice and simplify the algorithm/pseudo-code.  
- Figures 3–5: lack comparisons to the same-cluster (state-of-the-art) method, so performance claims are not substantiated.  
- Rebuttal point 1: still unclear whether repeating queries is unhelpful; need clarification on the Nature paper’s relevance.  
- Rebuttal point 2: authors should provide the more detailed same-query comparison promised in the response.
