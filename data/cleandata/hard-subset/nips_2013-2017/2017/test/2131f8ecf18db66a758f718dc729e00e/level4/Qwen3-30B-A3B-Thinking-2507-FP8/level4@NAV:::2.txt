

This paper investigates a clustering problem where the objective is to recover true element labels from noisy, non-adaptive query-answers, each involving only a small subset of elements. It establishes lower bounds on the query count needed for exact or partial ground-truth label reconstruction across diverse scenarios. Additionally, it introduces an "AND-queries" algorithm applicable to arbitrary k-cluster settings and evaluates its performance on real data. While the work provides theoretical analyses, algorithm development, and real-data simulation, it has limitations in presentation, clarity of results, and comparison to related work. Significant revisions may be required for publication. Detailed comments follow:  
1. The restriction against repeating queries appears unnecessarily limiting. Allowing repeated queries could mitigate noise more effectively than the restricted model. A justification should include real-data simulations comparing the proposed method against a no-restriction variant, demonstrating superior performance. If no such advantage exists, the no-restriction case is preferable. The authors' concern about independence in multiple answers per query could be resolved by assuming distinct annotators for repeated queries, ensuring independent responses.  
2. Theorem 1 conflicts with [Ahn-Lee-Suh] in the noiseless, exact-recovery setting (XOR-based same-cluster queries, non-adaptive). For instance, with $p=1/2$ and constant $\Delta$, Theorem 1 gives $m = n / \log 2$, while [Ahn-Lee-Suh] reports $m = n \log n / \Delta$. This discrepancy must be clarified. The proof of Theorem 1 is also unclear.  
3. Theorem 3's lower bound on $\delta$ is difficult to interpret, particularly for deriving $m$ in terms of $n$ and $\delta$ to assess performance gains under relaxed constraints.  
4. Theorem 4 shares the interpretability issues of Theorem 3. Moreover, the gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3's lower bound is unclear—could it be unbounded? The proof is similarly hard to follow.  
5. Figure 2 lacks comparison to the same-cluster-query method (only compares to its lower bound). The claim that AND-queries outperform same-cluster queries is unsupported by Figure 2; a crossing point in direct comparison requires explanation.  
6. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of $p$ or cluster size ratios (per Theorem 6), raising questions about practical obtainability of this information. The pseudo-code is opaque without the inaccessible proof of Theorem 5.  
7. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), which are essential to validate claims of superior performance.  
[Ahn-Lee-Suh] K. Ahn, K. Lee, and C. Suh, "Community recovery in hypergraphs," Proceedings of Allerton Conference on Communication, Control, and Computing, 2016.
