1. Summary: Studies non-adaptive small-size queries for clustering, gives lower bounds, proposes an AND-queries algorithm, and reports real-data experiments, but has major presentation, clarity, and comparison issues requiring substantial revision.

2. Model restriction on repeating queries: Banning repeated queries is overly restrictive since repetition aids denoising; the paper should justify this with experiments against repeat-allowing methods or analyze the unrestricted case (or clarify independence via multiple annotators).

3. Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) and prior work: Theorem 1 appears inconsistent with Ahn–Lee–Suh results (example discrepancy for p=1/2); the paper must precisely state assumptions/models/query types and reconcile or correct the proof.

4. Theorem 3 (partial-recovery lower bound): The δ-bound is hard to interpret—reparameterize to express m as a function of n and δ, and provide intuition and a compact user-facing statement.

5. Theorem 4 (upper bound) and gap to Theorem 3: Present the upper bound explicitly as m(n,δ), quantify the gap to the lower bound (sources of gaps: constants/log factors/regimes), and give a clearer proof sketch.

6. Figure 2 (comparison to same-cluster queries): Current comparison is to a lower bound, not an algorithm—add direct comparisons to concrete same-cluster-query methods and explain when/why each method wins.

7. Algorithm 1 (assumptions and clarity): Algorithm seems to require knowledge of p or cluster sizes—explain how to obtain these in practice and make the pseudocode self-contained and implementable without deep proof reading.

8. Figures 3–5 (empirical validation): Lacking head-to-head comparisons with state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster-query methods); add matched-budget/noise evaluations and clearly report regimes of superiority.

9. Post-rebuttal — repeating queries: Rebuttal did not clarify whether repetitions are useful; explicitly state whether the cited work shows repetitions are unhelpful and map that setting (noise/non-adaptivity) to the paper’s model.

10. Post-rebuttal — comparison to same-cluster method: Rebuttal discussion should be integrated into the paper with explicit baseline algorithms, experimental protocols, and reproducible results supporting claimed advantages.
