- Establishes a novel connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding, with information-theoretic lower bounds and proposed AND-queries.  
- Provides lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery and analyzes both noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Empirically validates the approach with a real crowdsourcing experiment.  

- Claim: AND-queries outperform "same cluster" (XOR) queries, giving substantial reduction in required queries for approximate recovery.  
- Weakness: No justification for forbidding repeated queries — ignores standard crowdsourcing practice of asking the same question to multiple workers for independent answers.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 appears to contradict Ahn et al. (Allerton 2016) for p=1/2, Δ constant; discrepancy needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Lower bounds in Theorems 3 and 4 are algebraically complex and hard to interpret; need more intuitive translation to query complexity.  
- Weakness: No direct empirical comparison to actual "same cluster" algorithms — figures compare only to a lower bound, not the method.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of cluster sizes (p); practicality and robustness to misestimated p are not addressed.  
- Weakness: Crowdsourcing experiment shows fewer errors for AND-queries but lacks direct comparison to the theoretical predictions.  
- Weakness: Insufficient positioning against related clustering-with-side-information work despite the novel coding connection.  

- Required revisions highlighted: justify or compare the no-repetition model to the standard repeated-queries approach; reconcile with Ahn et al.; simplify/explain lower bounds; add direct method-vs-method figures; address prior-knowledge practicality; compare experiment results to theory and better situate related work.
