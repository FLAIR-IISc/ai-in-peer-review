- Paper studies crowdsourced clustering via same-cluster queries and derives query-complexity bounds in noiseless and noisy settings, with theoretical results supported by simulations.  
- Writing is clear and accessible; the information-theoretic framing is well motivated and explained.  
- Major concern: AND queries may conflict with crowdsourcing motivation because they effectively reveal cluster labels, so it’s unclear why not directly ask for labels.  
- Request to clarify/justify the interpretational and practical role of AND queries versus direct labeling.  
- Suggestion to simplify the expressions in Theorems 1–4 to highlight key dependencies while preserving essence.  
- Empirical study appears to use only AND queries, making results hard to interpret unless the AND-query issue is resolved.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
