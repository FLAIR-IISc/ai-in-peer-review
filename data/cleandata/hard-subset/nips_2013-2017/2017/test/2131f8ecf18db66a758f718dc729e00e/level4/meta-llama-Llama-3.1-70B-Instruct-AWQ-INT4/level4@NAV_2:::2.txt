This paper explores a clustering problem of which the goal is to reconstruct the true labels of elements from possibly noisy and non-adaptive query-answers, each associating only a few elements. It derives lower bounds on the number of queries required to reconstruct the ground-truth labels exactly or partially in a variety of scenarios. It also proposes a so-called "AND-queries" algorithm that applies to arbitrary k-cluster case, and evaluates the performance of the algorithm under a real data set.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the clustering problem with non-adaptive queries, which is a common setting in crowdsourcing applications. The authors derive lower bounds on the number of queries required to reconstruct the ground-truth labels exactly or partially, and propose an AND-queries algorithm that outperforms the same-cluster-query scheme in certain scenarios. The paper also provides a real-data simulation to evaluate the performance of the algorithm.

However, the paper has some limitations in presentation, clarity of the claimed results, and comparison to related works. The authors should consider revising the paper to address these issues.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of lower bounds on the number of queries required to reconstruct the ground-truth labels exactly or partially. However, the authors should provide more details on how the algorithm works and how it compares to other state-of-the-art methods.
