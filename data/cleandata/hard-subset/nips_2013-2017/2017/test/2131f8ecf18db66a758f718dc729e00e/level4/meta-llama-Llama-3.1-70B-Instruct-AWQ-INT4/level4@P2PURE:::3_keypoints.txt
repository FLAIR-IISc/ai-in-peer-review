- Studies crowdsourced clustering via similarity/AND queries and provides query-complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless settings.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by simulations; writing is clear and the information-theoretic derivations are well-explained.  
- Concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels directly, contradicting the crowdsourcing motivation—why not ask for labels instead?  
- Concern: Can the bounds in Theorems 1–4 be simplified while preserving their core meaning?  
- Concern: Experiments seem to use only AND queries, calling experimental validity into question unless the AND-query issue is resolved.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
