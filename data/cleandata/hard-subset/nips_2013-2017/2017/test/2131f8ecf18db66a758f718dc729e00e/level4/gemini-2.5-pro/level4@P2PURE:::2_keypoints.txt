- Overall: Solid scope (theory, algorithm, experiments) but hampered by poor presentation, unclear theoretical claims, and weak comparisons — major revisions needed.

1. Model constraint (no repeated queries): Forbidding repeats is restrictive and unjustified; repeating queries (or using different annotators for independence) is a natural noise-mitigation strategy and should be empirically or theoretically justified.

2. Theorem 1: Claimed exact-recovery query complexity conflicts with Ahn-Lee-Suh’s results (example: p=1/2); the discrepancy must be explained and the proof clarified.

3. Theorem 3: The lower bound on δ is presented unclearly; authors should rephrase as an explicit m(n,δ) bound to show how query complexity scales with relaxed recovery requirements.

4. Theorem 4: The upper bound is similarly opaque; authors must discuss the gap between Theorems 3 and 4 (bounded or unbounded) and improve proof clarity.

5. Figure 2: Comparison against only a lower bound for same-cluster queries is insufficient — include comparisons to an actual same-cluster algorithm and analyze any performance crossover.

6. Algorithm 1: Appears to require prior knowledge of noise rate p or cluster sizes; explain practical estimation of these parameters and make the pseudocode clearer independent of theorem proofs.

7. Figures 3–5 (experiments): Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (including same-cluster query algorithms), weakening claims of superiority.

- Repeating queries: The justification for disallowing repeats remains unclear and should be explicitly motivated in the revised manuscript if based on prior work.
