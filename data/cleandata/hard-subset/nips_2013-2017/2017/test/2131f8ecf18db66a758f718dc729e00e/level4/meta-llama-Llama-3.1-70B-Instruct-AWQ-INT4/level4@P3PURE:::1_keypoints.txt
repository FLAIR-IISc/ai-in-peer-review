- Study of query-based clustering where the learner can query an oracle (same-cluster or any fixed binary function of labels) to identify a target clustering.  
- Main objective: analyze the query complexity required to perfectly or approximately recover the target clustering.  
- Focus on information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Also analyzes the setting with a noisy oracle.  
- Applications and motivation include record deduplication, crowdsourcing, and related domains.  
- Uses novel information-theoretic tools that are of interest to the ML community.  
- Main limitation: analysis omits structural assumptions (no hypothesis class or model structure).  
- Consequently, bounds rely primarily on the marginal label distribution and noise level.  
- The setting therefore diverges from typical learning-theory frameworks that exploit problem structure.  
- Despite limitations, the work is a valuable first step, especially for deriving informative lower bounds.
