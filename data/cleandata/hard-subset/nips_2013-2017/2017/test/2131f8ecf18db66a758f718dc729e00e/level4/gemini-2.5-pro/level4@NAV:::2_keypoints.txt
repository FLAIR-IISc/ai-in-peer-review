- Overall: Paper mixes theory, algorithms, and experiments but has major presentation, clarity, and literature-comparison issues requiring substantial revision.

1. Prohibition on repeating queries: Banning repeated queries is overly restrictive and unrealistic — repetitions naturally mitigate noise and should be compared empirically (or modeled via independent annotators).

2. Theorem 1: Claimed noiseless exact-recovery bounds conflict with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) for common parameter regimes (e.g., p=1/2), requiring detailed clarification and clearer proofs.

3. Theorem 3: Lower bound on δ is presented in an opaque form; the corresponding m(n,δ) relation is not made explicit, hindering interpretation of partial-recovery gains.

4. Theorem 4: Upper bound suffers the same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; the gap between Theorems 3 and 4 is unclear and proofs are hard to follow.

5. Figure 2: Comparison is inadequate because it pits the method against a lower bound for same-cluster queries rather than an actual same-cluster-query implementation, so superiority is unsubstantiated.

6. Algorithm 1: Requires prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios (practicality concern); pseudocode is unclear and tied to hard-to-follow proofs without justification for how required parameters are obtained.

7. Figures 3–5: Experimental evaluation omits direct comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (especially same-cluster queries), leaving central empirical claims unsupported.

Additional comments:
- It remains unclear why query repetition is not considered beneficial; if justified by cited work, that justification must be explicitly spelled out.
- The authors’ rebuttal analysis comparing to same-cluster queries should be integrated into the revised manuscript for clarity.
