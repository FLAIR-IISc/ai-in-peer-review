This is an interesting research paper. I enjoyed reading it very much. In this paper, the authors have considered the task of clustering data points through crowdsourcing, which involves the problem of retrieving the original labels of data points based on the answers to similar cluster questions. The authors have proposed limits on the number of questions required to retrieve the labels, considering noisy and noise-free answers to the questions. The main contribution of this paper is theoretical, along with some empirical simulations. The paper is well-written, making it easy for a general machine learning audience to understand the theoretical contributions. In particular, the relationship regarding information theory for deriving the limits is well explained.

Regarding improving the paper, I have the following concerns:
(1) While the idea of AND queries is theoretically interesting, it seems to contradict the core purpose of crowdsourcing. In the case of an AND query, effectively, the cluster label is also given as part of the answer, rather than simply providing an answer on whether the data point of interest belongs to the same cluster or not. If this is indeed the case, as I understand from the paper, why would anyone need to ask those similarity questions? Why not simply ask for the label for the data point? This is my primary confusion regarding the contribution of this paper.
(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the limits in theorems 1, 2, 3, 4 while maintaining the essence?
(3) In the experiments, it seems only AND queries have been considered. If this is the case, the experimental study is in question until the above point (1) becomes clear.

I am satisfied with the authors' response to my above questions. Therefore, I vote for the acceptance of this paper.
