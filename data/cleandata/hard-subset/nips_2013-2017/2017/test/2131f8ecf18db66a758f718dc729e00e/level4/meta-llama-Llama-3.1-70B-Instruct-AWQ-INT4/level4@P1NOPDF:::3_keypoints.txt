- Interesting and enjoyable paper addressing clustering via crowdsourcing using similarity (AND) queries.
- Provides theoretical bounds on the number of queries needed to recover labels for both noisy and noiseless answers.
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.
- Writing is clear and accessible to a general ML audience.
- Nicely explains the connection to information theory for deriving the bounds.
- Concern: AND queries may conflict with crowdsourcing motivation because they reveal cluster labels, raising the question why not ask for labels directly.
- Request to simplify expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their essence.
- Concern: Experiments appear to use only AND queries, which undermines validity unless the issue about AND queries is clarified.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ potential responses and votes for acceptance.
