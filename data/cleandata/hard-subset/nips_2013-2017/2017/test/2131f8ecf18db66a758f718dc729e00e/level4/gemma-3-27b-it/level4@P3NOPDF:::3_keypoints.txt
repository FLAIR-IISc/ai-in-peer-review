- Proposes crowdsourced clustering via recovering original labels from similarity queries, framing theory for label recovery.  
- Develops theoretical bounds on the number of queries needed for noisy and noiseless settings; main contribution is theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical simulations support the theoretical results.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible; connection to information theory is well explained.  
- Major concern: use of AND queries may reveal cluster labels in addition to similarity, calling into question the need for similarity queries versus directly soliciting labels.  
- The authors addressed the ABOVE concern satisfactorily.  
- Theorem expressions (Theorems 1–4) are complex; suggest simplifying for better accessibility and interpretation.  
- Experimental evaluation focuses only on AND queries, weakening empirical validation unless the rationale for this choice (and the AND-query concern) is convincingly justified.  
- Overall: strong paper with solid theory and clear exposition; recommend acceptance.
