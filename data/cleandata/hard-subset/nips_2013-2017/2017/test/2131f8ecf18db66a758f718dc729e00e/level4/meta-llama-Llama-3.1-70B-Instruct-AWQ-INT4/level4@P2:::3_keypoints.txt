- Paper studies clustering via crowdsourcing and derives query-complexity bounds for recovering labels from similar-cluster queries.  
- Main contribution is theoretical: bounds for number of queries under noiseless and noisy answers.  
- Uses Information Theory to derive fundamental limits and connects well to prior work.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to a general ML audience.  
- Empirical work consists of simulations supporting theoretical results.  
- Concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels directly, conflicting with crowdsourcing motivation and reducing need for similarity queries.  
- Concern: Expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex and hard to interpret; simplification is needed.  
- Concern: Experiments only use AND queries; should include other query types (e.g., same-cluster queries) for fuller validation.  
- Reviewer satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
