- Paper studies crowdsourced clustering via similarity queries and proposes bounds (noisy and noiseless) for label recovery, supported by simulations.
- Main contribution is theoretical; results are presented accessibly with a clear Information Theory connection.
- Well-written and explanations of the bounds are particularly clear.
- Major concern: use of AND queries seems to reveal cluster labels directly, which conflicts with the crowdsourcing motivation for similarity queries.
- If AND queries do reveal labels, it raises the question why not directly request individual labels instead of similarity queries.
- Suggestion: simplify the expressions in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their core meaning and implications.
- Experimental concern: experiments focus only on AND queries, making their validity questionable unless the AND-query issue is resolved.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and votes to accept.
