- Paper studies recovering cluster labels via crowdsourced similarity queries, giving query-complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless answers.
- Nice theoretical contribution connecting clustering-by-crowdsourcing to information theory, explained accessibly.
- Paper is well-written and readable for a general machine learning audience.
- Major concern: AND queries appear to reveal cluster labels (not just similarity), which conflicts with crowdsourcing motivation.
- If AND queries reveal labels, it’s unclear why one would use similarity queries instead of directly asking for labels.
- Experiments appear to only use AND queries, undermining empirical support unless the AND-query motivation is clarified.
- Bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex; simplify expressions if possible to improve readability and clarity.
- Authors’ responses to the AND-query motivation and bound simplification will be crucial for acceptance.
