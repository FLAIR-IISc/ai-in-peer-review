This paper addresses the problem of clustering via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering the true labels of data points from same-cluster queries. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels in both noiseless and noisy settings. The main contribution is theoretical and supported by empirical simulations. The paper is well-written, and its theoretical contributions are made accessible to a general machine learning audience. In particular, the connection to information theory used for deriving the bounds is explained clearly.

I have the following concerns that I hope the authors can address to improve the paper:

(1) My primary concern relates to the "AND query" model. While theoretically interesting, this model seems to conflict with the practical motivation for crowdsourcing. As I interpret the paper, an AND query effectively reveals the cluster label as part of the answer, rather than simply confirming if the queried data points belong to the same cluster. If this is correct, it is unclear why one would need to ask similarity queries at all, as it would be more direct to simply ask for the label of a single data point. I would appreciate clarification on this point, as it seems fundamental to the paper's contributions.

(2) The expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 are quite complex. Would it be possible to provide simplified versions of these bounds—for instance, by focusing on their asymptotic behavior or dominant terms—to offer clearer intuition about the key dependencies?

(3) The experiments appear to exclusively use AND queries. Consequently, the validity of the experimental study is contingent on a satisfactory resolution of the conceptual issue raised in point (1).

Provided the authors can satisfactorily address the questions above, I would vote for acceptance.
