- Engaging, clearly written paper with accessible theory and a well-explained information-theoretic connection.
- Main contribution: theoretical bounds on number of same-cluster queries needed to recover labels under noisy and noiseless responses, supported by simulations.
- Concern 1: AND queries seem to reveal cluster labels directly and are misaligned with crowdsourcing motivation—if so, why not ask for labels instead of similarity queries?
- Concern 2: The bounds in Theorems 1–4 may be simplifiable while preserving their essence.
- Concern 3: Experiments focus exclusively on AND queries, making empirical evaluation questionable unless concern (1) is clarified.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
