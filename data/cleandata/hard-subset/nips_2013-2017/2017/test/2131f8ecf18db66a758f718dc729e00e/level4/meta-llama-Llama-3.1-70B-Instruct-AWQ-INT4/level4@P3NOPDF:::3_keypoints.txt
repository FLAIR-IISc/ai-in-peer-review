- Paper proposes clustering via crowdsourcing using AND (similar-cluster) queries and derives query complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless settings; main contribution is theoretical with supporting simulations.
- Writing is clear and makes the theoretical contributions accessible to a general ML audience.
- Authors effectively explain the connection to information theory in deriving the bounds.
- Concern: AND queries may contradict crowdsourcing motivation by revealing cluster labels, raising the question why not ask labels directly.
- Suggestion: simplify expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 to improve readability and impact.
- Concern: experiments seem to only consider AND queries, which may limit the empirical study unless the AND-query issue is addressed.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
