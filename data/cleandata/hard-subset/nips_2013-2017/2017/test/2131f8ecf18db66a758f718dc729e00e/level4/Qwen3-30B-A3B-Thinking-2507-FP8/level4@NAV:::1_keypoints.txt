- Problem: studies query-based clustering where the learner uses same-cluster (or fixed binary label-function) queries to identify the target clustering and analyzes query complexity for exact and approximate recovery.
- Model motivation: the setting is well-motivated with practical applications in record deduplication and crowdsourcing.
- Approach/results: uses an information-theoretic framework to derive tight upper and lower bounds on required queries.
- Robustness: analysis covers scenarios with noisy oracles.
- Novelty: the information-theoretic techniques are novel for the machine learning community.
- Limitation: lack of structural assumptions (no hypothesis class) means results depend mainly on the marginal label distribution and deviate from standard learning-theory settings.
- Contribution: represents a valuable first step in establishing information-theoretic foundations for query-based clustering, especially for lower-bound methods.
