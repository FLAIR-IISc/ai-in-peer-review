This paper presents an interesting approach to data clustering via crowdsourcing, framing the problem as recovering original data point labels from responses to cluster similarity queries. The authors establish theoretical bounds on the number of queries needed for label recovery, accounting for both noisy and noiseless responses. The primary contribution lies in the theoretical analysis, supported by empirical simulations. The paper is clearly written and effectively communicates the theoretical results to a broad machine learning audience, particularly the connection to Information Theory used in deriving the bounds.

However, I have a few suggestions for improvement. First, the use of AND queries, while theoretically intriguing, appears to deviate from the typical crowdsourcing paradigm. An AND query seems to inherently reveal cluster labels alongside similarity information, raising the question of why separate similarity queries are necessary – why not directly solicit labels for individual data points? This is a central point of confusion regarding the paper’s contributions. Second, exploring potential simplifications of the expressions in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, without sacrificing their core meaning, could enhance readability. Finally, the experimental evaluation currently focuses solely on AND queries. The validity of this experimental study hinges on a clear justification for the choice of AND queries, particularly addressing the concern raised in point (1).

I am now satisfied with the authors’ responses to my previous questions and therefore recommend acceptance of this paper.
