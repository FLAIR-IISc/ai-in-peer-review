- Paper studies noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries for clustering, gives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, proposes an "AND-queries" algorithm, and presents empirical evaluation.

- Manuscript suffers from presentation, clarity, and related-work comparison issues; substantial revisions likely needed.

1. Forbidding repeated queries is an artificial restriction; allowing repeats (e.g., via independent annotators) is natural and should be justified or compared empirically.

2. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) for k=2 — the stated query complexity differs significantly and the proof is hard to follow; needs detailed clarification.

3. Theorem 3's lower bound on error δ is opaque; it’s unclear how to translate the bound into m as a function of n and δ or to assess gains from partial recovery.

4. Theorem 4's upper bound is similarly hard to interpret and its relation to Theorem 3 (possible gap or optimality) is not discussed; proofs are difficult to verify.

5. Figure 2 compares the proposed method to a theoretical lower bound for same-cluster queries rather than an actual same-cluster algorithm; a direct empirical comparison is required.

6. Algorithm 1 appears impractical because it requires prior knowledge of cluster proportions p; the paper lacks justification for how p would be known and the pseudocode is hard to read without proofs.

7. Experiments in Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (notably same-cluster-query methods), leaving key performance claims unsupported.

8. After rebuttal: authors should clarify whether forbidding repeated queries is beneficial or necessary and provide empirical/theoretical evidence if repeating is useless.

9. After rebuttal: the authors’ detailed rebuttal comparisons to same-cluster methods should be integrated into the main manuscript for clarity.
