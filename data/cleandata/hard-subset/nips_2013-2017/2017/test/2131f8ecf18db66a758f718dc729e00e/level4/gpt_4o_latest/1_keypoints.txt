- Studies query-driven clustering where the learner poses same-cluster or general binary queries to an oracle to recover a target clustering, focusing on query complexity.
- Well-motivated and timely topic with practical applications such as record deduplication and crowdsourcing.
- Uses an information-theoretic approach to derive upper and lower bounds for perfect and approximate recovery.
- Extends analysis to noisy oracle settings.
- Introduces novel information-theoretic techniques that are of interest to the ML community.
- Key limitation: no structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class), which restricts the scope of results.
- Resulting bounds mainly depend on the label marginal distribution and noise level, differing from conventional learning-theory frameworks.
- Overall contribution: an important initial step toward information-theoretic foundations for query-based clustering, especially in establishing lower bounds.
