- Studies query-based clustering where learner asks same-cluster or general binary queries to recover target clustering.  
- Focuses on query complexity: provides upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Considers noisy oracle models and derives corresponding query complexity results.  
- Uses novel information-theoretic tools by casting the problem as a locally encodable source coding problem.  
- Introduces AND queries as an alternative to same-cluster queries, showing improved performance in some settings.  
- Theoretical results are supported by experiments on real and synthetic datasets.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Main limitation: analysis assumes no structural hypothesis class or other problem structure.  
- Consequently, bounds depend primarily on the marginal label distribution (and noise level).  
- Despite limitations, the work is a significant and novel contribution to query-based clustering.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address analysis limitations.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10, confidence: 8/10.
