- Overall: Paper spans theory, algorithms, and experiments but has major exposition, formal-claim clarity, and related-work positioning issues requiring substantial revision.

- Modeling choice: Prohibiting repeated queries is a strong assumption that may hurt performance; need justification or analysis/empirical comparison to the repetition-allowed case.

- Theorem 1: Claimed scaling conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh); requires precise explanation of differences and clearer, stepwise proof with explicit log bases and typical-set sizes.

- Theorem 3: The lower bound on δ is hard to interpret operationally; present an explicit (even asymptotic) m ≥ m(n,δ,Δ,p) to show gains from δ>0.

- Theorem 4: Achievability’s closeness to the lower bound is unclear and the proof sketch is hard to parse; reorganize proof and isolate key probabilistic steps (Poisson approximation, two-hop-neighbor error).

- Figure 2: Comparison uses a lower bound for same-cluster queries rather than a concrete baseline; add direct comparisons to a standard same-cluster method and explain crossing behavior.

- Algorithm 1: Requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes; provide a practical estimation procedure (e.g., small pilot), robustness analysis to misspecification, and clearer pseudocode/explanations for thresholds, d, q, k.

- Figures 3–5: Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster querying with spectral or ML decoders); include such baselines to substantiate AND-query claims.

- Rebuttal follow-up 1: Clarify the role of repeated queries—whether repetition is unhelpful—and explicitly state what prior theory/experiments (e.g., the cited Nature paper) imply for this setting.

- Rebuttal follow-up 2: Incorporate the rebuttal’s detailed discussion into the paper, adding explicit experimental/theoretical comparisons that substantiate claimed advantages of AND-queries.
