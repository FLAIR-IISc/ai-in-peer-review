- Paper studies query complexity for clustering via oracle queries and links it to locally encodable source coding from information theory.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds for perfect and approximate recovery and extends analysis to noisy oracle responses.  
- Motivated by practical applications (record deduplication, crowdsourcing) and connects to recent querying-for-clustering work.  
- Novel connection: treating semisupervised clustering as locally encodable source coding opens new theoretical tools.  
- Theoretical rigor: derives rigorous lower bounds and analyzes performance of proposed querying schemes.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to traditional same-cluster/XOR queries and shows theoretical benefits.  
- Includes analysis of noisy oracle responses, increasing practical relevance.  
- Provides preliminary experimental validation with a real-world crowdsourcing experiment.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on prior label distribution limits applicability when priors are unknown or unreliable.  
- Weakness: practicality and empirical performance of AND queries need further investigation and larger experiments.  
- Weakness: experiment setup (d-regular graph) is insufficiently explained and justified.  
- Weakness: related work discussion could better integrate active learning and submodular optimization literature.  
- Weakness: paper is dense with heavy notation; clarity and more intuitive explanations are needed.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with significant information-theoretic contribution and new query concepts.  
- Against acceptance: strong dependency on prior knowledge, preliminary experiments, and presentation/clarity issues.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution; borderline but leaning toward accept if authors improve clarity and expand experimental validation.
