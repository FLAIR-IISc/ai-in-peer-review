- Problem studied: query complexity of semi-supervised clustering where a learner recovers a ground-truth clustering via limited oracle queries.
- Query model: non-adaptive, local queries on a small constant number of points returning simple functions of their labels (e.g., binary).
- Main contribution: novel framing of the problem as locally encodable source coding.
- Theoretical results: derived upper and lower bounds on queries needed for exact and approximate label recovery.
- Noise handling: bounds cover both noiseless and noisy oracle settings.
- Key finding: standard "same-cluster" queries have fundamental limitations; "AND-queries" can be provably more efficient in some regimes.
- Strengths: highly original approach, introduces new information-theoretic tools to ML, and provides rigorous, insightful lower bounds.
- Practical relevance: model and insights apply to crowdsourcing and entity resolution, suggesting better query designs.
- Primary limitation: analysis makes no structural assumptions about the data or cluster geometry.
- Consequence of limitation: bounds depend mainly on label marginals and oracle noise, so they do not reflect how data properties affect clustering difficulty.
