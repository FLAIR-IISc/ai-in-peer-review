- Paper studies crowdsourced clustering, deriving query-complexity bounds for recovering true labels under noiseless and noisy same-cluster queries.
- Main contribution is theoretical bounds supported by simulation; paper is well-written and accessible to ML readers.
- Information-theoretic connection used to derive bounds is clearly explained.
- Primary concern: the AND-query model may reveal cluster labels, conflicting with crowdsourcing motivation and potentially making similarity queries unnecessary.
- Request clarification on whether AND queries effectively disclose labels and why one would prefer them over directly asking for labels.
- Theoretical bounds (Theorems 1–4) are complex; ask for simplified/asymptotic versions to clarify dominant dependencies.
- Experiments use only AND queries, so empirical validity depends on resolving the conceptual issue about AND-query realism.
- Reviewer would accept if authors satisfactorily address the conceptual and presentation concerns.
