This paper presents an engaging discussion on clustering data points via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering original labels based on responses to similar cluster queries. The authors propose bounds on the number of queries required to recover labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers. The paper's primary contribution lies in its theoretical aspects, supplemented by empirical simulations. The authors successfully convey their theoretical findings in a clear and accessible manner, making it easy for a general machine learning audience to comprehend. The connection to information theory for deriving the bounds is particularly well-explained.

However, I have several concerns that the authors should address to further improve the paper.

(1) The concept of AND queries, although theoretically intriguing, appears to contradict the initial motivation for crowdsourcing. Specifically, AND queries seem to provide the cluster label as part of the answer, which raises questions about the necessity of similarity queries. If the label can be obtained through AND queries, why not directly ask for the label of a data point? This inconsistency is my primary concern regarding the paper's contributions.

(2) I suggest simplifying the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, if possible, without compromising their essence.

(3) The experimental study seems to focus solely on AND queries, which may be problematic unless the aforementioned concern (1) is clarified.

I am satisfied with the authors' response to these concerns and, therefore, recommend accepting this paper.
