- Addresses clustering via crowdsourcing, recovering original labels from similarity-based cluster queries.
- Derives bounds on the number of queries required for label recovery under noisy and noiseless responses.
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.
- Paper is well-written and accessible; the Information Theory connection is clearly explained.
- Concern: Use of AND queries may deviate from typical crowdsourcing motivation since they effectively reveal cluster labels.
- Implication: If AND queries reveal labels, it is unclear why one would use similarity queries instead of asking for labels directly.
- Request: Can the expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 be simplified without losing the core results?
- Experimental concern: Experiments focus exclusively on AND queries, making their validity questionable unless the AND-query issue is addressed.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and votes to accept.
