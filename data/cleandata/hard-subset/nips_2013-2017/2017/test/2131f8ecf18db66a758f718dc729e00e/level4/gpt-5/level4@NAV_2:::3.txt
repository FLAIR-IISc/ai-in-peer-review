This paper is engaging and enjoyable to read. It studies clustering via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering item labels from responses to same-cluster queries. The authors derive bounds on the number of queries needed for both noiseless and noisy settings, and the primary contributions are theoretical with supporting empirical evidence. The exposition is clear and accessible for a general machine learning audience; in particular, the information-theoretic framing used to obtain the bounds is clearly articulated.

I have the following suggestions and questions for improving the paper:
1) While AND queries are theoretically intriguing, they seem at odds with the original crowdsourcing motivation. An AND query effectively reveals a cluster label within the response rather than only indicating whether two items belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, why issue similarity-style queries at all instead of directly requesting an item’s label? This is my main source of confusion regarding the paper’s contribution.
2) Can the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 be simplified or streamlined while preserving their essential content?
3) In the experiments, it appears that only AND queries are used. If so, the experimental evaluation needs clarification in light of point (1).

I am satisfied with the authors’ responses to the above points and therefore recommend acceptance.
