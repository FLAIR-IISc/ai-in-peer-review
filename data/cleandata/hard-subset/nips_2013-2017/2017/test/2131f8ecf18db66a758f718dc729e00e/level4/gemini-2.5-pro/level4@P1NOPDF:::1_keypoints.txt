- Studies query-based clustering where the learner issues same-cluster (or general binary label-function) queries to recover a target clustering.
- Primary focus is on the query complexity required for exact and approximate recovery.
- Provides information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on the number of queries needed.
- Analysis extends to the setting of a noisy oracle.
- Employs novel information-theoretic tools; lower-bound techniques are particularly noteworthy.
- Major limitation: no structural assumptions (e.g., restricted hypothesis class) are made.
- As a result, bounds depend mainly on the label marginal distribution and noise level, making the setting somewhat removed from typical ML scenarios, but still an important foundational contribution.
