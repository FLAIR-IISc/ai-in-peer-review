This is an engaging paper, and I enjoyed reading it. The authors study the task of clustering data points via crowdsourcing, where the goal is to recover the true labels of data points from answers to same-cluster queries. They provide bounds on the number of queries needed to recover the labels under both noisy and noiseless responses. The primary contribution is theoretical, supported by some simulation-based experiments. The paper is clearly written, making the theory accessible to a broad machine learning audience. In particular, the information-theoretic connection used to derive the bounds is explained well.

I have the following concerns regarding improvements to the paper.
(1) While the notion of AND queries is theoretically interesting, it appears misaligned with the original motivation for crowdsourcing. In an AND query, the response effectively provides the cluster label rather than only indicating whether the queried points belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, why pose similarity queries at all? Why not directly ask for a data point’s label? This is my primary confusion regarding the paper’s contributions.
(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 while preserving their essence?
(3) The experiments seem to focus exclusively on AND queries. If so, the empirical evaluation is questionable unless point (1) is clarified.

I am satisfied with the authors’ responses to my questions above and therefore recommend acceptance of this paper.
