- Restrictive no-repeat-query model is questionable; allowing repeated queries (e.g., to independent annotators) may improve noise robustness and should be justified or replaced by the no-restriction case with comparisons.

- Lack of extensive real-data simulations comparing the proposed algorithm to a no-restriction-tailored method to justify the restricted model.

- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) in the noiseless exact-recovery regime (discrepant query complexities); this discrepancy needs detailed clarification.

- Proof of Theorem 1 is hard to follow and requires substantial improvement and clearer exposition.

- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for delta is difficult to interpret; it’s not clear how m scales with n and delta for direct comparison.

- Similar clarity issues for Theorem 4; it’s unclear how tight the upper and lower bounds are and whether the gap can be unbounded.

- Figure 2 only compares to a same-cluster-query lower bound, not the actual same-cluster-query method; the claim of outperforming that scheme is unsupported without method-level comparison and intuition about any crossing point.

- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster proportions; practicality and how to obtain these in real settings are not justified.

- Algorithm pseudocode and reliance on Theorem 5 are hard to follow; the presentation and explanation of the algorithm need to be clarified.

- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), so empirical claims of superiority are unsubstantiated.

- Rebuttal did not fully resolve whether repeating the same question can help; the paper must clarify this, and whether prior literature (e.g., the Nature paper) addressed it.

- Overall: presentation, clarity of claims, proofs, and comparisons to related work are insufficient; significant revisions and additional experiments/analysis are required for publication.
