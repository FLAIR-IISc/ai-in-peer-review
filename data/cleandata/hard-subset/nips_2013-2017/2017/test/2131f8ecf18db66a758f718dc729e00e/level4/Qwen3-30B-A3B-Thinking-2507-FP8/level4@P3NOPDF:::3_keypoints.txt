- Presents a theoretically grounded method for recovering true cluster labels from similarity queries with info-theoretic bounds for noisy and noiseless settings, supported by experiments.  
- Strong, well-motivated theoretical contribution with clear exposition of information-theoretic foundation.  
- Writing is accessible to a general ML audience without oversimplifying technical content.  
- Major practical concern: core reliance on "AND queries" (multi-point same-cluster checks) conflicts with standard crowdsourcing, which uses similarity judgments (pairwise/triplet), and AND queries effectively reveal cluster labels.  
- If workers can determine labels via AND queries, the problem reduces to direct labeling, undermining the clustering-query framework and practical motivation.  
- Authors must either justify why AND queries are more feasible/accurate in real crowdsourcing or revise the model to standard similarity queries and re-derive bounds.  
- Experiments only evaluate AND queries, making empirical results inconclusive unless practical justification for AND queries is established or limitations are explicitly stated.  
- Minor suggestion: simplify/approximate the complex bounds in Theorems 1–4 (e.g., asymptotic analysis) for readability and interpretability.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and well-written; recommend acceptance pending satisfactory revision that clarifies/justifies the role and necessity of AND queries in real-world crowdsourcing.
