1. Paper studies reconstructing true labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries, gives lower bounds, and proposes an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation.

2. Overall presentation, clarity, and comparison to prior work are weak; substantial revisions and further development are needed for publication.

3. The restriction forbidding repeated queries is artificial and likely hurts performance; authors must justify it with comprehensive simulations or consider the unrestricted case with independent annotators.

4. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (XOR same-cluster queries); the discrepancy (e.g., m scaling differences) needs detailed clarification and the proof is hard to follow.

5. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret, making it difficult to derive m as a function of n and δ and to assess the benefit of relaxing constraints.

6. Theorem 4 suffers from the same interpretability and proof difficulties as Theorem 3; the relation between its upper and lower bounds (and whether the gap can be unbounded) is unclear.

7. Proofs throughout are difficult to follow and need clearer exposition.

8. Figure 2 compares only to a same-cluster-query lower bound, not to the actual same-cluster-query method, so claims of outperforming that scheme are not supported.

9. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes (per Theorem 6); practical methods to obtain these parameters and justification of algorithm practicality are missing.

10. Algorithm pseudo-code is hard to understand without parsing the complex proofs of Theorem 5; the algorithm and its steps need clearer presentation.

11. Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), undermining empirical claims of superiority.

12. Rebuttal addressed some points, but the rationale for prohibiting repeated queries remains unclear—authors should explicitly state whether cited work (Nature paper) supports this and clarify the comparison to same-query methods in more detail.
