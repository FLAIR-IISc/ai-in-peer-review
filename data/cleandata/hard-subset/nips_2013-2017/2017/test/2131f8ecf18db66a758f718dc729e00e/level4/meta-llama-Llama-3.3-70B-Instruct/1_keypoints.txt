- Studies clustering with oracle queries (same-cluster or a fixed binary function of instance labels) to identify a target clustering.
- Main goal: determine query complexity required for exact or approximate recovery.
- Applies an information-theoretic approach to derive upper and lower bounds on query requirements.
- Analysis covers both noiseless and noisy oracle models.
- Information-theoretic methods used are novel and notable for the ML community.
- Results are largely driven by the marginal label distribution and noise level.
- Limitation: no structural assumptions or predefined hypothesis class are made, diverging from traditional learning-theory settings.
- Contribution: provides foundational insights and establishes lower bounds for query-based clustering.
