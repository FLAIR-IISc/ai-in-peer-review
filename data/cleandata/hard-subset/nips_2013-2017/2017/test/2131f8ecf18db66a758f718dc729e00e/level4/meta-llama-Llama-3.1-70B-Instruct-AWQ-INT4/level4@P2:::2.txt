This paper explores a clustering problem of which the goal is to reconstruct the true labels of elements from possibly noisy & non-adaptive query-answers, each associating only a few elements. It derives lower bounds on the number of queries required to reconstruct the ground-truth labels exactly or partially in a variety of scenarios. It also proposes a so-called "AND-queries" algorithm that applies to arbitrary k-cluster case, and evaluates the performance of the algorithm under a real data set.

While the paper attempts to provide theoretical analyses for various scenarios as well as develops an algorithm together with a real-data simulation, it comes with limitations in presentation, clarity of the claimed results, and comparison to related works. Perhaps significant changes and possibly new developments are to be made for publication.

The main idea of the paper is to view the clustering problem as a locally encodable source coding problem, which is a novel and interesting approach. The paper provides several theoretical results, including lower bounds on the number of queries required to reconstruct the ground-truth labels exactly or partially, and proposes a new querying strategy based on AND operation that performs substantially better than the traditional 'same cluster' queries.

However, the paper has several limitations. First, the constraint that repeating the same questions is not allowed seems a bit restricted. While the authors provide some justification for this constraint, it is not entirely clear whether this constraint is necessary or useful in practice. The authors should provide more evidence or justification for this constraint, or consider relaxing it to make the model more realistic.

Second, the paper lacks clarity in presenting the results. For example, the proof of Theorem 1 is hard to follow, and the expression of the lower bound on δ in Theorem 3 is difficult to interpret. The authors should provide more explanations and examples to illustrate the results and make them more accessible to readers.

Third, the paper does not provide sufficient comparison to related works. For example, the authors claim that the proposed approach outperforms the same-cluster query scheme, but this claim is not supported by sufficient evidence. The authors should provide more comparisons to other methods and explain why their approach is better.

Finally, the paper has some limitations in terms of practicality. For example, the proposed algorithm requires prior knowledge on p or the relative sizes of clusters, which may not be available in practice. The authors should provide more justification for the practicality of the algorithm and explain how to obtain the required information in practice.

In conclusion, while the paper provides some interesting and novel results, it has several limitations that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more evidence, justification, and comparisons to related works to make the paper more convincing and practical.
