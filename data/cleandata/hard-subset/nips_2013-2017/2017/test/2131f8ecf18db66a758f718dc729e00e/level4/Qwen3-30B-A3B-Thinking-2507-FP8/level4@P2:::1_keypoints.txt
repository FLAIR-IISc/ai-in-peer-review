- Establishes an equivalence between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding, giving a new theoretical framing.  
- Models an oracle that gives (possibly noisy) binary answers to bounded-arity queries; goal is recovering full labels with minimal queries.  
- Demonstrates fundamental limitations of pairwise "same-cluster"/XOR queries.  
- Proposes AND queries as a superior alternative in many scenarios.  
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Improves the naive nH(p) lower bound for the two-cluster noiseless case for certain label distributions.  
- Introduces an AND-based querying scheme that substantially reduces queries for approximate recovery.  
- Presents a practical Algorithm 1 for noisy k-cluster recovery achieving (1−δ)-good recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.  
- Validates methods on real crowdsourcing experiments (200 movies, 10 volunteers) showing AND queries yield far fewer errors (105 vs. 234).  
- Provides rigorous proofs and novel information-theoretic contributions on locally encodable source coding.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Experimental details are provided to aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: new information-theoretic perspective and novel demonstration that AND queries outperform same-cluster queries.  
- High practical significance for crowdsourcing by reducing query cost (e.g., O(n log(1/δ)) vs O(n log n)).  
- Limitation: theoretical focus on marginal label distribution may limit applicability to more structured clustering.  
- Some technical proof details could be clarified (e.g., expurgation argument in Theorem 1).  
- Could expand connection to broader machine learning applications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution recommended for acceptance.
