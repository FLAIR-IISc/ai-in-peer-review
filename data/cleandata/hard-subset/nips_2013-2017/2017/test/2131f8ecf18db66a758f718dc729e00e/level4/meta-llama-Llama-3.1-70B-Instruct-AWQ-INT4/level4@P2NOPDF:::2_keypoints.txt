- Paper studies non-adaptive, non-repeating noisy queries and proposes AND-queries with theoretical bounds and real-data evaluation, but presentation, clarity, and related-work comparison are weak.

- Disallowing repeated queries is restrictive and may harm performance; authors should justify this restriction or study/compare to the no-restriction case (e.g., independent annotators).

- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] on noiseless exact recovery (query complexity discrepancy) and its proof is hard to follow.

- Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is hard to interpret; the implied relationship between m, n, and δ is unclear, obscuring claimed improvements.

- Theorem 4 has similar clarity/proof issues, and the gap between upper and lower bounds in Theorem 3 is unclear and may be unbounded.

- Figure 2 omits empirical comparison to a same-cluster query method (shows only its lower bound), so claims of outperforming same-cluster schemes are unsupported.

- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes; authors must justify how such information is obtained in practice and make the pseudocode clearer.

- Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query), weakening empirical validation of claims.

- Rebuttal addressed some points, but it remains unclear whether repeating the same question is helpful; authors should clarify and cite relevant studies.

- Detailed comparison to the same-query method should be included as discussed in the authors’ response.
