- Novel framing: Views clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem, a novel and interesting perspective.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives lower bounds on the number of queries required for exact and partial label reconstruction in various scenarios.  
- Algorithm: Proposes an AND-queries strategy applicable to arbitrary k-cluster cases and evaluates it on real data.  
- Empirical claim: Authors claim AND-queries outperform traditional "same-cluster" queries, but evidence is limited.  
- Constraint concern: The prohibition on repeating the same questions is restrictive and insufficiently justified or motivated.  
- Clarity issues: Presentation is unclear in parts—proof of Theorem 1 is hard to follow and the bound in Theorem 3 is difficult to interpret.  
- Comparison gap: Insufficient comparison to related works and alternative query schemes to substantiate superiority claims.  
- Practicality concern: The algorithm requires prior knowledge of p or relative cluster sizes, which may be unavailable in practice.  
- Need for examples: Results lack intuitive explanations and illustrative examples that would make them more accessible.  
- Evidence needed: More empirical validation or justification is required to support model constraints and performance claims.  
- Organization: Paper is generally well-organized with adequate background and references despite clarity problems.  
- Originality: The AND-query strategy and the source-coding view are original contributions.  
- Significance: Theoretical bounds and query-efficient algorithms are potentially useful for limited-query practical settings.  
- Recommendation: Significant revisions are needed—address clarity, justification of assumptions, additional comparisons, and practical applicability.
