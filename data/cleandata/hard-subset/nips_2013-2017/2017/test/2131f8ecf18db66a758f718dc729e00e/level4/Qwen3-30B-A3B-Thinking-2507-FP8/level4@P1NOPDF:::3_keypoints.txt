- Presents a theoretical approach to crowdsourced clustering using similarity queries and derives bounds for label recovery in noisy and noiseless settings.  
- Experimental simulations support the theoretical bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured, accessible, with a clear information-theoretic justification.  
- Concern 1: Theoretical motivation for AND queries is unclear—AND queries may implicitly reveal labels and contradict the crowdsourcing premise.  
- Concern 2: Theorem bounds are complex; suggest simplifying expressions (asymptotic approximations or tighter characterizations) for readability and practical use.  
- Concern 3: Experiments only use AND queries, so empirical validation of similarity-based crowdsourcing is unjustified unless other query types or rationale are provided.  
- Reviewer found the authors’ responses satisfactory and recommends acceptance.
