- Studies query-based clustering where learner asks same-cluster (binary) queries to recover target clustering and analyzes query complexity.
- Focuses on information-theoretic upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.
- Also analyzes the noisy-oracle setting and incorporates noise level into bounds.
- Uses information-theoretic tools that are novel to the ML community.
- Paper is well-written, with clear introduction and proofs provided in the supplementary material.
- Experimental results validate and illustrate the theoretical findings.
- Main strength: rigorous, information-theoretic analysis yielding deep understanding and lower bounds.
- Results are applicable to real-world scenarios like record deduplication and crowdsourcing with noisy oracles.
- Limitation: analysis makes no structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class), relying mainly on label marginal distribution.
- Setting is somewhat removed from typical learning-theory frameworks, limiting direct comparability to standard models.
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid, original, and significant first step in the area despite limitations.
