- Proposes clustering via crowdsourcing to recover original labels from similarity/AND queries, deriving query-complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless settings; main contribution is theoretical with supporting simulations.  
- Writing is clear and the information-theoretic derivation of bounds is well explained.  
- Major concern: AND queries may implicitly reveal cluster labels, contradicting the crowdsourcing motivation and questioning why one wouldn’t just ask for labels directly.  
- Request to simplify the bound expressions in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their essence.  
- Experimental concern: experiments apparently only evaluate AND queries, which limits empirical validation unless the AND-query issue is addressed.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
