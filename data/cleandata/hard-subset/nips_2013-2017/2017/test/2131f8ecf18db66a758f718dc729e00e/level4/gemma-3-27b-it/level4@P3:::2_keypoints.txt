- Paper studies reconstructing true labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-element queries and derives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery.
- Establishes an equivalence between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding as a novel theoretical connection.
- Argues AND-queries outperform traditional “same-cluster” (XOR) queries, especially for approximate recovery.
- Proposes an AND-query based algorithm for arbitrary k-cluster problems and evaluates it on Amazon Mechanical Turk data.
- Provides theoretical lower bounds on the number of queries needed, aiming for rigorous analysis under noisy answers.
- Includes empirical validation but experiments are limited in scope and dataset variety.
- Strength: novel theoretical perspective connecting clustering and source coding.
- Strength: theoretical rigor in deriving query-complexity bounds.
- Strength: practical relevance by considering noisy crowdsourced answers and including real-data experiments.
- Weakness: model forbids repeated queries without sufficient justification; needs comparison to repeated-query approaches.
- Weakness: theorem statements (Theorems 1, 3, 4) are unclear about concrete implications for m relative to n and δ.
- Weakness: proofs are hard to follow and lack intermediate explanations for readability.
- Weakness: figures and experiments lack direct comparisons to existing “same-cluster” methods and other baselines.
- Weakness: requires prior knowledge of cluster sizes (p) which is practically limiting and needs discussion on estimation.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 is presented opaquely and needs a more intuitive explanation separate from proofs.
- Weakness: limited experimental validation and lack of comparisons to state-of-the-art algorithms.
- For acceptance: novelty of connection and AND-query proposal plus theoretical contributions could have significant impact.
- Against acceptance: poor clarity, incomplete evaluation, practical limitations, and complex proofs hinder assessment.
- Overall recommendation: lean reject but encourage revision to improve clarity, evaluations, and practical justification.
- Suggested revisions: justify non-repetition constraint, clarify theorem implications for query complexity, improve proof detail, add comparisons to same-cluster queries, discuss estimating priors, simplify algorithm description, and expand experiments across datasets.
