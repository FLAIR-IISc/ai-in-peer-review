- Paper studies clustering via crowdsourcing, recovering original labels from ‘same cluster’ queries.  
- Authors derive query-complexity bounds for both noisy and noiseless responses.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Paper is well-written and makes theoretical contributions accessible.  
- Connection to information theory for deriving bounds is clearly explained.  
- Major concern: AND queries may diverge from crowdsourcing motivation by revealing cluster labels rather than pairwise similarity.  
- Key confusion: if AND queries reveal label info, why not directly ask for data-point labels instead of similarity queries?  
- Request to simplify expressions in Theorems 1–4 while preserving essential meaning.  
- Experimental concern: only AND queries are evaluated, making experiments questionable unless the AND-vs-similarity issue is clarified.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and votes to accept the paper.
