1. Paper is ambitious (theory, algorithm, experiments) but suffers from poor presentation, unclear theoretical claims, weak literature engagement, and needs substantial revision.

2. The no-repeated-queries modeling constraint is overly restrictive and unjustified; authors must better motivate it or compare to models allowing repeated queries (e.g., independent annotators).

3. Theorem 1’s query-complexity claim conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) for key parameter regimes (e.g., p=1/2), and the proof is hard to follow.

4. Theorem 3’s lower bound on error rate δ is presented in an opaque form that doesn’t clearly show how m scales with n and δ for partial recovery.

5. Theorem 4’s upper bound is similarly hard to parse; the paper fails to clarify the gap between Theorems 3 and 4 or whether that gap is bounded.

6. Figure 2’s experiments compare the algorithm only to a theoretical lower bound (not an implemented same-cluster method), making the claimed superiority of AND-queries unsupported.

7. Algorithm 1 appears to require knowing the noise parameter p or cluster size ratios; its pseudocode and connection to Theorem 5 are opaque and impractical without parameter-estimation methods.

8. Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to baseline/state-of-the-art methods (including same-cluster queries); direct empirical baselines and discussion are needed.
