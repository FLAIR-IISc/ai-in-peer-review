- Studies crowdsourced clustering: recovers original labels from similarity cluster queries and derives query complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless responses.
- Primary contribution is theoretical, backed by empirical simulations; paper is well-written and accessible to a broad ML audience.
- Information-theoretic connections used to derive bounds are clearly and effectively explained.
- Concern 1: AND queries may implicitly reveal cluster labels, which conflicts with the crowdsourcing motivation—why not ask for labels directly?
- Concern 2: Expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 may be simplifiable to improve clarity and accessibility.
- Concern 3: Experiments focus exclusively on AND queries; the experimental validity depends on resolving the AND-query labeling concern.
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
