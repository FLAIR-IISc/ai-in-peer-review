1. Overall: Studies clustering from noisy, non-adaptive few-item queries and proposes lower bounds plus an AND-queries algorithm, but suffers from presentation, clarity, and weak comparisons to prior work.

2. The restriction forbidding repeated queries is unjustified and may hurt performance; need strong justification or experiments versus the unrestricted repeated-query setting (or model repeated answers as independent via distinct annotators).

3. Theorem 1 overlaps prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh); the stated query complexity conflicts with that prior result and the proof is hard to follow—needs detailed clarification.

4. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ is opaque; it’s difficult to express m in terms of n and δ or understand performance gains from relaxing constraints.

5. Theorem 4 has the same interpretability issues for δ; the gap between its upper and lower bounds (and whether it can be unbounded) is unclear and proofs are difficult.

6. Figure 2 only compares to the same-cluster lower bound, not the same-cluster method, so the claim of outperforming same-cluster queries is unsupported and any crossover behavior is unexplained.

7. Algorithm 1 depends on prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios—practical methods to obtain this are not provided, and the pseudocode is hard to follow without complex proofs.

8. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster queries), leaving empirical claims of superiority unsubstantiated.

9. Additional: It remains unclear if repeating questions is actually detrimental (did the cited Nature paper prove this conclusively?), and the authors’ response/comparison to same-query methods needs clearer expansion.
