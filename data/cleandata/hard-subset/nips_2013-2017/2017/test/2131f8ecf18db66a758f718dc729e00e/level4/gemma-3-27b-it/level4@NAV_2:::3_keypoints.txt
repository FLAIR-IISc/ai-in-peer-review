- Explores connection between semisupervised clustering via crowdsourcing and locally encodable source coding; analyzes query complexity for pairwise queries in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries and demonstrates theoretical advantages in some settings.
- Writing is clear and the information-theoretic derivation of bounds is well-articulated and accessible.
- Concern 1: Motivation for AND queries is unclear since AND queries reveal label information directly—why not query labels outright?
- Concern 2: Expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex; simplifying them would improve readability and insight.
- Concern 3: Experimental evaluation centers on AND queries without direct comparison to same-cluster (XOR) queries under equivalent conditions, making validation incomplete.
- For acceptance: novel theoretical connection, rigorous query-complexity bounds, promising AND-query approach, and strong presentation.
- Against acceptance: weak motivation for AND, high complexity of derived bounds, and lack of direct experimental baselines.
