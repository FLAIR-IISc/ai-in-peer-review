- Query restriction: Prohibiting repeated queries seems unnecessary and counterproductive for noise mitigation; requires justification via real-data simulations or reframing with independent annotators.

- Theorem 1 and related work: Theorem 1 appears to contradict Ahn–Lee–Suh (2016) for the noiseless XOR case (different query complexity claims); needs detailed clarification and a simpler, clearer proof.

- Theorems 3 & 4: Lower-bound expressions for δ are hard to interpret; the authors should present bounds on m in terms of n and δ and clarify the proofs.

- Gap in bounds (Theorem 4): The gap between upper and lower bounds is unexplained and might be unbounded; discuss implications and tighten/justify the proofs.

- Figure 2: Comparison is incomplete—missing direct comparison to the same-cluster query method and lacks analysis of any crossover point despite claims of superiority.

- Algorithm 1: Requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes; authors must justify how this information is obtained in practice and make the pseudocode more understandable.

- Figures 3–5: Empirical results lack comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster query), weakening claims that AND-queries outperform alternatives.

- Overall assessment: Potentially interesting work but needs major revisions—clearer theory, stronger relation to prior work, improved algorithm presentation, and thorough baseline comparisons.

- Post-rebuttal: Remaining concerns about the query restriction and same-query comparison persist; request evidence (e.g., from the cited Nature paper) and a more detailed discussion.
