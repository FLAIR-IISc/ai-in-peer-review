- Addresses semi-supervised clustering via queries and links it to locally encodable source coding.  
- Derives theoretical bounds on queries needed for exact and approximate recovery under noiseless and noisy oracles.  
- Shows standard "same-cluster" queries have limits for approximate recovery and proposes "AND" queries as a provably more efficient alternative in some regimes.  
- Presents small-scale crowdsourcing experiment supporting theoretical results.  
- Paper is well-written and makes the information-theoretic connection accessible to ML audience.  
- Concern: practical motivation for AND queries is unclear—if workers know absolute labels, asking pairwise ANDs vs single-item labels needs stronger justification and concrete examples.  
- Suggestion: simplify or give asymptotic forms of the complex bounds (Theorems 1–4) in the main text to clarify key parameter dependencies.  
- Experimental gap: lacking direct empirical comparison between AND and same-cluster queries on the same dataset, especially under noise.  
- Overall supportive of the theoretical contribution, conditional on clarifying AND motivation and strengthening theoretical/experimental presentation.
