1. Paper tackles noisy non-adaptive small-set queries for clustering and proposes an AND-query algorithm, but suffers from major presentation, clarity, and comparison issues requiring significant revision.

2. Prohibiting repeated queries is unjustified; allowing repeats is natural to mitigate noise and the authors must explain or empirically justify this restriction.

3. Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) appears to contradict prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) for key parameter regimes and its proof is unclear.

4. Theorem 3’s lower bound on δ lacks intuitive interpretation and does not yield a clean, practical bound on m in terms of n and δ.

5. Theorem 4 suffers the same interpretability problems, and the paper does not address the gap between Theorem 4’s upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound.

6. Figure 2’s claim that AND-queries outperform same-cluster queries is misleading because it compares to a lower bound, not to the actual same-cluster algorithm.

7. Algorithm 1 requires knowledge of noise rate p or cluster-size ratios without explaining how to obtain them, raising concerns about practicality; pseudocode and proofs are hard to follow.

8. Empirical comparisons (Figures 3–5) only use theoretical bounds and omit state-of-the-art algorithmic baselines like same-cluster-query methods.

9. Two critical rebuttal issues remain unresolved: the utility of banning repeated queries, and the need for explicit comparisons/discussion of same-cluster-query methods.

10. Overall: theoretical inconsistencies, weak empirical validation, and insufficient justification of assumptions undermine the paper’s contributions; major revisions are needed.
