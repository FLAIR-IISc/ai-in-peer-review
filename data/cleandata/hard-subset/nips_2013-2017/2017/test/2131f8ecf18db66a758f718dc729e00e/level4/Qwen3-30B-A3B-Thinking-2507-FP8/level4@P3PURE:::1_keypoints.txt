- Studies a query-based clustering model where a learner uses same-cluster (or general binary label-function) queries to recover the target clustering.  
- Provides an information-theoretic analysis of query complexity for exact and approximate clustering recovery.  
- Derives both upper and lower bounds on the number of queries required.  
- Extends the analysis to noisy-oracle settings.  
- Employs information-theoretic tools that are novel within the ML community.  
- Model and problem are well-motivated for applications like record deduplication and crowdsourcing.  
- Analysis lacks structural assumptions (no hypothesis class or data structure), so bounds depend only on label marginals and noise.  
- Consequently, applicability to standard learning-theoretic frameworks is limited despite being a valuable first step, especially for establishing lower bounds.
