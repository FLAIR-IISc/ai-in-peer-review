- Studies clustering via oracle queries, generalizing same-cluster queries to any fixed binary function of labels and analyzing query complexity.
- Motivated, contemporary problem with practical applications like record de-duplication and crowdsourcing.
- Provides information-theoretic upper and lower bounds for perfect and approximate recovery.
- Framework handles a noisy oracle.
- Uses novel information-theoretic techniques that are a noteworthy contribution.
- Analysis is limited by no structural assumptions (no hypothesis class); bounds depend mainly on label marginal distribution and noise level.
- Positions the setting apart from standard learning-theory frameworks but offers an important initial step toward fundamental lower bounds.
