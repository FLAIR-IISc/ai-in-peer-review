This paper presents an interesting take on clustering data points via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering original labels from answers to similar cluster queries. The authors propose bounds on the number of queries required to recover the labels, considering both noisy and noiseless answers. The paper's primary contribution is theoretical, supplemented by empirical simulations. The writing is clear and accessible to a general machine learning audience, effectively explaining the connection to information theory used to derive the bounds.

I have several concerns that could improve the paper:

1. The concept of AND queries, while theoretically intriguing, seems to contradict the original motivation for crowdsourcing. Specifically, AND queries appear to provide the label of the cluster as part of the answer, rather than just determining whether the data points belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, it raises questions about the necessity of asking similarity queries. Why not directly ask for the label of a data point? Clarifying this point is essential to understanding the paper's contributions.

2. It would be beneficial to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4, while preserving their essence. This could enhance the readability and accessibility of the theoretical results.

3. The experimental section appears to only consider AND queries. If this is the case, the experimental study may be limited unless the concerns raised in point 1 are addressed.

Despite these concerns, I am satisfied with the authors' responses to my questions, and I am voting for the acceptance of this paper.
