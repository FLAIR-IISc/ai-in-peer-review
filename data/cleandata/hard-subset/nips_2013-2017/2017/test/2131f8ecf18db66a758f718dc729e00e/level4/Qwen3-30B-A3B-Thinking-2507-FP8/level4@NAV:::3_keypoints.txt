- Paper studies recovering original labels via crowdsourced similarity queries, deriving theoretical bounds for noisy and noiseless answers; main contribution is theoretical with supporting simulations and clear exposition.  
- Concern: AND query mechanism seems to contradict crowdsourcing motivation because it implicitly reveals the cluster label, questioning the need for similarity queries.  
- Suggestion: Simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 if possible without losing key insights.  
- Concern: Experiments use only AND queries; the experimental evaluation needs clarification since its validity depends on resolving the conceptual issue about AND queries.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.
