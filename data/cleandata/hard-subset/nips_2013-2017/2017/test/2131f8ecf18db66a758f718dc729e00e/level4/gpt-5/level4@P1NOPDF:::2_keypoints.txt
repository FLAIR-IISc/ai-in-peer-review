- Overall: Studies clustering from noisy non-adaptive small-element queries with theory, an AND-queries algorithm, and experiments, but has presentation, clarity, and related-work comparison issues requiring substantial revision.

- Prohibition on repeats: Banning repeated queries is overly restrictive and may hurt performance; authors must justify this with real-data simulations or adopt an unrestricted model (or explain independent annotator assumption).

- Theorem 1: Claimed noiseless exact-recovery bound conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh]; needs detailed clarification of assumptions/models/constants and a clearer, more readable proof.

- Theorem 3: The lower-bound expression on δ is hard to interpret and not easily invertible to give m as a function of n and δ, obscuring the practical gain from relaxing constraints.

- Theorem 4: Upper bound suffers the same interpretability issues; unclear how it compares to Theorem 3’s lower bound and whether the gap can be unbounded; proofs are difficult to follow.

- Figure 2: Lacks empirical comparison to the actual same-cluster query method (only compares to its lower bound); need direct comparison and intuition about any crossing behavior.

- Algorithm 1: Appears to require prior knowledge of p or cluster size ratios—how to obtain these in practice is unclear; pseudocode is hard to follow without the involved proofs.

- Figures 3–5: Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster query method), which are essential to support claims of outperforming them.

- Post-rebuttal: Still unclear whether repeating the same question is unhelpful—paper must explicitly clarify this point (did the referenced Nature paper establish it?).
