- Studied a clustering model where the learner can query an oracle using same-cluster queries or any fixed binary function of instance labels, aiming to identify the target clustering.  
- Main focus is on the query complexity required to perfectly or approximately recover the clustering.  
- Approach is information-theoretic, providing novel upper and lower bounds on query complexity.  
- The analysis also handles the case of a noisy oracle.  
- Applications and motivation include record deduplication, crowdsourcing, and related settings.  
- The information-theoretic tools introduced are novel and of interest to the ML community.  
- A key limitation: no structural assumptions or hypothesis class are imposed, so bounds depend mainly on the label marginal and noise level.  
- Because of that limitation, the setting departs from typical learning-theory frameworks, but still offers important first-step insights, especially about lower bounds.
