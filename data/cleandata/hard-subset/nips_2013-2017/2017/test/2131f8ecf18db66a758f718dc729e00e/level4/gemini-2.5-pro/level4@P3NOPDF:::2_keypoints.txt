- Problem & contributions: studies clustering from noisy, non-adaptive small-subset queries, provides information-theoretic lower bounds, proposes an AND-queries algorithm for k clusters, and gives a real-data evaluation.

- Strength: combines theoretical lower bounds with a practical algorithm and experiments, addressing an interesting active clustering problem.

- Query-model concern: forbidding repeated queries is overly restrictive and needs stronger justification or an alternative model (e.g., repeated queries to different annotators).

- Theorem 1 issue: claimed noiseless exact-recovery complexity appears to contradict Ahn–Lee–Suh (2016) for same-cluster queries; the proof is hard to follow and requires clarification.

- Theorems 3 & 4 clarity: lower- and upper-bound expressions are difficult to interpret—dependence of m on n and δ is not made explicit and the gap between bounds is not discussed.

- Empirical shortcomings: experiments compare the proposed algorithm only to theoretical lower bounds rather than implemented competing methods, so claims of empirical superiority are unsubstantiated.

- Algorithm 1 practicality: appears to require prior knowledge of cluster parameters (e.g., p); authors should explain estimation, sensitivity to misspecification, and clarify the pseudocode.

- Presentation and verification: multiple proofs and theoretical results are hard to follow, hindering verification; overall the paper needs major revision before acceptance.
