- Paper studies non-adaptive noisy clustering with small-size queries, gives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, proposes AND-queries algorithm, and tests on real data.  
- Presentation, precision of claims, and comparisons to prior work are weak; major revisions likely needed.  
- Query repetition constraint is unjustified and unnecessarily restrictive; repeating queries (with independent annotators) could mitigate noise.  
- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (discrepant scaling for p=1/2, constant Δ) and its proof is hard to follow.  
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret and doesn’t clearly translate to m vs. n and δ.  
- Theorem 4 has similar interpretability and proof-clarity issues; unclear how its upper bound compares to Theorem 3’s lower bound or whether the gap can be unbounded.  
- Figure 2 compares only to a lower bound of the same-cluster method, not the method itself, so claims of outperforming same-cluster queries are unsupported.  
- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster ratios (per Theorem 6) with no practical guidance on obtaining them, reducing applicability.  
- The pseudocode and proofs (Theorem 5) are difficult to understand, hindering reproducibility.  
- Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster queries), failing to substantiate performance claims.  
- Authors’ rebuttal did not adequately justify the non-repetition constraint or provide sufficient comparison details to same-query methods.
