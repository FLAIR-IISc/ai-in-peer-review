- Paper studies reconstructing true labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-element queries, gives lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation, but overall presentation, clarity, and related-work comparisons are weak and require substantial revision.

1. The restriction forbidding repeated queries is unjustified; repeated queries are a natural noise-mitigation and the authors must either justify the restriction or show via extensive simulations that their restricted-algorithm outperforms an unrestricted variant.

2. Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) conflicts with prior work [Ahn-Lee-Suh] (claimed m = n/ log 2 vs. n log n / Δ), needs clarification and its proof is hard to follow.

3. Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s unclear how to derive m as a function of n and δ to assess gains from relaxing the constraint.

4. Theorem 4 suffers the same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; the gap between Theorems 3 and 4 isn’t quantified (could be unbounded) and the proof is difficult to follow.

5. Figure 2 compares the method only to the lower bound of the same-cluster-query scheme, not the actual method, so it does not support the claim that AND-queries outperform same-cluster queries.

6. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster-size ratios with no practical justification for obtaining them; the pseudo-code is hard to follow without the opaque proof of Theorem 5.

7. Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster-query scheme), undermining repeated claims that AND-queries outperform existing methods.

- The conflict with the referenced [Ahn-Lee-Suh] result must be resolved and discussed explicitly.

- The rationale for prohibiting repeated queries (and any evidence from the cited "Nature paper") should be clarified, and a detailed comparison to same-cluster-query methods should be added.
