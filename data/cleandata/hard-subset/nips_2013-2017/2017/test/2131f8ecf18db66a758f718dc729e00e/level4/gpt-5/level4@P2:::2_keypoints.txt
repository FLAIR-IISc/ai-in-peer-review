- Paper studies non-adaptive, few-element queries for noisy multiclass labeling, linking it to locally encodable source coding and deriving query-complexity bounds; proposes AND queries and gives a noisy k-class algorithm with O(n log(k/δ)) queries, plus a small crowdsourcing experiment.

- Relates to prior work on same-cluster queries, rate–distortion, locally decodable/encodable codes, and non-adaptive XOR-like measurements (e.g., Ahn–Lee–Suh).

- Strength: Novel equivalence to locally encodable source coding yielding new converse bounds.

- Strength: Identifies XOR/same-cluster limitations for approximate recovery and proposes AND queries with provable advantages.

- Strength: Provides a near-linear query algorithm for noisy k-class labeling with concentration-based correctness.

- Strength: Small real-data experiment suggests AND queries may be more reliable for crowd workers.

- Weakness: Disallowing repeated queries is restrictive; justification is weak and repetition could reduce noise—empirical/analytical comparison to repetition is needed.

- Weakness: Theorem 1 (noiseless exact recovery) seems inconsistent with Ahn–Lee–Suh; needs reconciliation of regimes, assumptions, and success definitions plus clearer proof and constants.

- Weakness: Theorem 3 (approximate lower bound) is hard to interpret; provide corollary expressing m/n vs p, Δ, δ and plots of δ vs m/n.

- Weakness: Theorem 4 (AND achievability) lacks interpretability and comparison to the lower bound; provide intuition, simplified proof, and asymptotic approximations.

- Weakness: Figure 2 compares to a same-cluster lower bound, not implementable baselines; add comparisons to concrete same-cluster algorithms and discuss regimes where XOR might win.

- Weakness: Algorithm 1 depends on known priors p or cluster sizes; clarify how these are estimated, robustness to misspecification, and improve pseudocode with thresholds and complexity notes.

- Weakness: Experiments lack comparisons to state-of-the-art same-cluster methods, sensitivity analyses, and clarity on worker assignment and quality controls.

- Quality: Promising technically but several proofs are hard to parse, some claims conflict with prior work, and experiments are limited.

- Clarity: Exposition needs tightening—clearer regimes/assumptions, intuitive proof sketches, interpretable corollaries, and better experiments.

- Originality: The locally encodable source-coding perspective and AND-query insight are original.

- Significance: Potentially significant if theoretical claims are clarified and empirical comparisons substantiate practical benefits.

- After rebuttal: Explicitly clarify whether repetition helps in this setting and under which models; incorporate detailed comparisons to same-cluster methods into the paper.

- Pros: Novel theoretical framing, simple scalable algorithm, and practical insight that AND queries may be easier for crowd workers.

- Cons: Unclear comparison with prior work, hard-to-interpret approximate-recovery bounds, limited experimental baselines, and reliance on known priors with little robustness guidance.

- Overall: Promising ideas but requires substantial theoretical clarification, stronger empirical comparisons, and improved presentation to meet conference standards.
