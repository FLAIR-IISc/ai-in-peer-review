This paper studies clustering via crowdsourcing, focusing on recovering the true labels of data points from answers to same-cluster queries. The authors derive bounds on the number of queries required in both noiseless and noisy settings, with the primary contribution being theoretical and supported by empirical simulations. The paper is clearly written and accessible to a broad machine learning audience. In particular, the information-theoretic perspective used to derive the bounds is well motivated and clearly explained.

I have the following concerns aimed at improving the paper:
(1) While AND queries are theoretically interesting, they appear to conflict with the original motivation for crowdsourcing. In an AND query, the cluster label is effectively provided as part of the answer, rather than only indicating whether the queried points belong to the same cluster. If this interpretation is correct, why ask similarity queries at all? Why not directly ask for the label of each data point? This is my primary source of confusion regarding the contribution of AND queries.
(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their essence and highlighting the key dependencies?
(3) In the experiments, it appears that only AND queries are considered. If so, the empirical study is difficult to interpret unless concern (1) is addressed.

I am satisfied with the authors’ responses to these points and recommend acceptance.
