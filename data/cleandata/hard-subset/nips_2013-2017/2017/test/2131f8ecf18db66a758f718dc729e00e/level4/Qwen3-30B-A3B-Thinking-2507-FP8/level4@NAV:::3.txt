

This paper presents an interesting study on clustering data points via crowdsourcing, specifically addressing the recovery of original labels from similarity queries. The authors establish theoretical bounds on the required number of queries under both noisy and noiseless answer scenarios, with the primary contribution being theoretical and supported by empirical simulations. The paper is well-written and accessible to a general machine learning audience, particularly excelling in its clear exposition of the information-theoretic connections used to derive the bounds.

I have the following concerns regarding improvements to the paper:  
(1) While the AND query mechanism is theoretically intriguing, it appears to contradict the core motivation of crowdsourcing. In an AND query, the cluster label is implicitly revealed through the answer (rather than merely confirming whether data points belong to the same cluster), raising the question: why employ similarity queries at all if the label is directly obtainable? This undermines the necessity of the proposed approach as interpreted from the paper.  
(2) Could the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1–4 be simplified without losing essential insights?  
(3) The experimental section exclusively uses AND queries. If this is the case, the experimental evaluation requires clarification of the above point (1), as its validity hinges on resolving this conceptual conflict.  

I am satisfied with the authors' responses to these points and recommend acceptance of the paper.
