- Studies a query-driven clustering framework where a learner asks an oracle same-cluster or other fixed binary label-function queries to recover the ground-truth partition.  
- Main focus is on the query complexity required for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Takes an information-theoretic approach, deriving both upper and lower bounds on query counts.  
- Also analyzes settings with noisy oracle responses.  
- Motivated and timely with applications to record deduplication, crowdsourcing, and related domains.  
- Techniques are information-theoretic and likely unfamiliar but appealing to much of the ML community.  
- Analysis omits structural assumptions (no hypothesis class), so bounds mainly depend on the marginal label distribution.  
- This abstraction limits connection to standard learning-theoretic formulations but provides a valuable first step and clarifies fundamental lower bounds.
