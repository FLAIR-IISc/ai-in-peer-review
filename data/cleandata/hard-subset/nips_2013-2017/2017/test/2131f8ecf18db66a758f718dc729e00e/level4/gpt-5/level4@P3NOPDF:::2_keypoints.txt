- Summary: Studies non-adaptive clustering from noisy small-subset queries, gives lower bounds for exact/partial recovery, proposes an AND-query algorithm for general k, and evaluates on a real dataset.

- Strength: Tackles an important, timely problem of label recovery from limited noisy non-adaptive queries.

- Strength: Provides a broad theoretical treatment across exact vs. partial recovery, noise levels, and query structures.

- Strength: Proposes a concrete algorithm for arbitrary k and supplies empirical results.

- Concern 1 (no repeated queries): The model forbids repeating identical queries, which seems restrictive and potentially suboptimal under noise; authors must justify this theoretically and empirically or analyze the repeat-allowed model.

- Concern 2 (Theorem 1 vs. Ahn–Lee–Suh): Theorem 1 appears to conflict with prior Ahn–Lee–Suh results in the noiseless case—clarify model differences (arity, operator, noise, balance, criteria), reconcile bounds, and provide a clearer proof roadmap and assumptions.

- Concern 3 (Theorem 3 interpretability): The δ-bound is hard to parse—restate as m(n,δ,...) with leading-order asymptotics in meaningful regimes and explain how allowing δ>0 lowers query complexity.

- Concern 4 (Theorem 4 gap & clarity): Present the upper bound as m(n,δ,...) with clear asymptotics, quantify the gap to Theorem 3’s lower bound (constant/log/unbounded), and improve proof structure and parameter dependencies.

- Concern 5 (Figure 2 baselines): Missing strong same-cluster algorithm baselines under matched budgets/noise; include implementations and explain any curve crossings with principled intuition.

- Concern 6 (Algorithm 1 practicality): Algorithm seems to require knowledge of p or cluster sizes—add an estimation/calibration phase, sensitivity analysis, and a high-level self-contained description (stages, complexity, stopping).

- Concern 7 (Figures 3–5 comparisons): Add quantitative comparisons to established baselines, report variance/error bars, and include ablations (repeats, query arity, imbalance).

- Presentation suggestion: Define all notation on first use, state main theorems informally before formal versions, and include simple special cases/back-of-envelope scaling.

- Post-rebuttal 1: The usefulness of query repetition remains unclear—either precisely cite prior work proving repeats unhelpful with matching assumptions or provide empirical/theoretical clarification.

- Post-rebuttal 2: Verbal clarifications on same-cluster comparisons are insufficient—incorporate concrete experiments and discussion in the revision.

- Overall assessment: Promising and important work but requires clearer theorem statements/reconciliations, tightened proofs, and stronger empirical validation to be a valuable contribution.
