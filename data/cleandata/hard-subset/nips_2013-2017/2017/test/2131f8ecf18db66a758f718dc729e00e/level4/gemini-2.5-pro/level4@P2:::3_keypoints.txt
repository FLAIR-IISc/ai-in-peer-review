- Problem & contribution: Establishes an equivalence between semi-supervised clustering with local queries and locally encodable source coding, enabling new analytical tools.  
- Theoretical results: Derives information-theoretic lower bounds and upper bounds for exact and approximate recovery under noiseless and noisy oracles.  
- Query analysis: Shows limitations of pairwise "same-cluster" (XOR) queries for approximate recovery and proposes AND-queries as a more efficient alternative in some regimes.  
- Noise robustness & algorithm: Extends analysis to noisy responses and provides an algorithm achieving approximate recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.  
- Experiments: Validates theoretical claims on a real crowdsourced dataset and shows empirical benefits of AND-queries.  
- Originality/significance: Novel conceptual link to locally encodable codes is an important and impactful contribution.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous, well-executed information-theoretic analysis using rate-distortion and coding-theory tools.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and makes technical content accessible to a broader ML audience.  
- Novel query type: AND-queries are a valuable idea with theoretical justification and empirical indication of lower worker error.  
- Practical concern: AND-queries require workers to know specific labels (e.g., "action"), which may undermine clustering’s label-discovery goal and needs stronger practical motivation.  
- Theoretical clarity request: Final bound expressions are complex; simplified/asymptotic forms or intuitive interpretations would improve accessibility.  
- Experimental suggestion: Strengthen empirical comparison by directly evaluating recovery algorithms for both query types on the real-world dataset and emphasize the observed error differences.  
- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical paper, leaning toward acceptance, contingent on better practical motivation for AND-queries and improved experimental comparison.
