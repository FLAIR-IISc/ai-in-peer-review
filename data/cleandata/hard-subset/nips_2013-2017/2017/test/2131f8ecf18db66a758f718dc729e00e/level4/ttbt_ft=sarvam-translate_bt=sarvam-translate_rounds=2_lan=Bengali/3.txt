This is an interesting paper. I enjoyed reading it. In this paper, the authors consider using crowdsourcing for data point clustering. The problem here is retrieving the original labels of the data points based on the answers to similar cluster searches. The authors have proposed a limit on the number of searches required to retrieve the labels, where the answers to the searches can be both noisy and non-noisy. The main contribution of this paper is theoretical, as well as some empirical modeling. The paper is well-written, which makes it easy for a general machine learning audience to understand the theoretical contributions. In particular, its connection to information theory for deriving the limits is beautifully explained.

I have the following concerns regarding the improvement of this paper:

(1) Although the concept of AND searches is theoretically interesting, it seems to deviate from the main purpose of crowdsourcing. In the case of AND searches, effectively, the cluster label is also given as part of the answer, instead of just answering whether the data points belong to the same cluster. If this is indeed the case, as I understood from the paper, then why would that similarity search be necessary? Why not simply ask the data point label itself? This is my main confusion regarding the contribution of this paper.

(2) Is it possible to simplify the expressions of the limits for theorems 1, 2, 3, and 4 while maintaining the core content?

(3) In the experiment, only AND searches seem to have been considered. If so, the experimental study is questionable until point number 1 above is clarified.

I am satisfied with the authors' responses to the above questions. Therefore, I vote to accept this paper.
