- Problem studied: clustering with same-cluster (or fixed binary-valued label-function) queries to an oracle aiming to recover the target clustering.  
- Goal: characterize query complexity needed to perfectly or approximately recover the clustering.  
- Results: provide upper and lower bounds on required queries, including analysis for noisy oracles.  
- Techniques: apply information-theoretic tools that are novel to the ML community.  
- Motivation: model is well-motivated with applications such as record deduplication and crowdsourcing.  
- Limitation: no structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class) are made, so analysis is limited in scope.  
- Dependence: bounds mainly rely on the marginal distribution of labels and the noise level.  
- Overall: a useful first step toward understanding information-theoretic limits and lower bounds for query-based clustering.
