- Paper tackles clustering via crowdsourced similarity queries and provides bounds for noisy and noiseless answers.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Paper is well written and accessible to a general ML audience.  
- Information-theoretic connection for deriving bounds is explained clearly.  
- Concern: AND queries may reveal the cluster label, conflicting with crowdsourcing motivation—if labels are revealed, why not ask labels directly?  
- Request to simplify expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 while preserving their essence.  
- Experiments appear to use only AND queries, raising validity concerns unless the AND-query motivation/implications are clarified.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
