- Paper studies reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries, gives lower bounds and an AND-queries algorithm with real-data evaluation, but has presentation, clarity, and comparison issues.

1. Disallowing repeated identical queries is a restrictive model that may hurt performance; authors should justify this choice or analyze the no-restriction case and/or independent annotator setting.

2. Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) in the noiseless exact-recovery regime (discrepancy in query complexity) and its proof is hard to follow.

3. Theorem 3's lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; it’s not clear how m scales with n and δ or how relaxation in δ improves performance.

4. Theorem 4 suffers from the same interpretability issues as Theorem 3; the gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound is unclear and proofs are difficult to follow.

5. Figure 2 lacks a comparison to the same-cluster-query algorithm (only compares to its lower bound), so the claimed superiority of the proposed method is unsupported and any crossing behavior is unexplained.

6. Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes (per Theorem 6); practical estimation of these parameters is not discussed and the pseudocode is hard to follow without complex proofs.

7. Figures 3–5 do not compare to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., same-cluster queries), so claims that AND-queries outperform existing schemes are not substantiated.

Additional comments:
- It remains unclear whether repeating the same question is unhelpful in practice; authors should clarify or cite evidence.
- The comparison to the same-query method needs to be expanded and made explicit in the paper.
