## Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

This paper establishes a surprising connection between semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding. The core idea – framing the clustering problem as a compression task where queries act as compressed bits – is novel and allows the authors to leverage information-theoretic tools to derive lower bounds on query complexity. The paper then proposes AND queries as an alternative to traditional ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries, demonstrating their superior performance, particularly in approximate recovery scenarios.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the recent interest in crowdsourced clustering and the associated cost of queries. The equivalence to locally encodable source coding is a significant contribution, opening up new avenues for analysis. The theoretical results, including the lower bounds and the analysis of AND queries, are mathematically sound and presented with sufficient detail. The experimental validation on real datasets, though limited in scope, provides supporting evidence for the proposed approach. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the assumption of independent query errors and the focus on non-adaptive querying.

A strength of the paper lies in its rigorous analysis and the clear connection drawn between seemingly disparate fields. The exploration of AND queries as a more efficient alternative to XOR queries is a valuable insight. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of the proposed AND queries. While the experiments show promise, a more extensive evaluation on diverse datasets and comparison with existing state-of-the-art clustering algorithms would strengthen the claims.  Additionally, the notation is dense and could be made more accessible.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and insightful connection between clustering and source coding.
*   Rigorous theoretical analysis with well-defined lower bounds.
*   Demonstrated improvement with AND queries over XOR queries.
*   Clear writing and motivation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited experimental evaluation.
*   Dense notation may hinder accessibility.
*   Practical implications of AND queries could be explored further.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution to the field. While the experimental evaluation could be more extensive, the theoretical results are compelling and the proposed approach has the potential to significantly impact the design of efficient clustering algorithms. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental validation.
