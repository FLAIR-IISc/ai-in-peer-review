- Novel connection established between semi-supervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Problem reframed: recovering cluster labels via queries is equivalent to source coding where each compressed bit depends on a constant number of input bits.  
- Main theoretical contributions: new information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Shows "same cluster" (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery.  
- Introduces AND queries that achieve substantially better performance than XOR, with rigorous analysis (Theorems 4 and 5).  
- Develops a practical algorithm using AND queries that handles noisy oracle responses.  
- Empirical validation includes real crowdsourcing experiments showing AND queries produce far fewer errors (105 vs 234 errors).  
- Theoretical novelty and rigorous analysis are the paper's main strengths, providing fresh perspective on crowdsourced clustering.  
- The noisy-query setting is addressed, making the work more realistic and practically relevant.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with sufficient technical detail.  
- Main weakness: most detailed theoretical analysis focuses on k=2; extensions to k>2 are limited.  
- Literature review could be strengthened by broader discussion of related concepts (e.g., locally decodable codes) and more comparisons to clustering-with-side-information work.  
- Proof of Theorem 3 is relegated to the supplement and not fully elaborated in the main text, which may hinder assessment by some readers.  
- Overall significance is high for information theory and machine learning; paper offers tight bounds, practical improvements, and is judged NIPS-worthy.
