- Maps query-efficient cluster label recovery to locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Provides theoretical lower bounds on queries for exact recovery, improving on the naive n bound for certain label distributions with pairwise XOR queries.  
- Shows pairwise 'same cluster' (XOR) queries are fundamentally limited for approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND queries as a superior alternative that achieves much better query complexity for recovering (1−δ) fraction of labels.  
- Develops a practical noisy-query algorithm achieving (1−δ)-good recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries and validates it empirically.  
- Real crowdsourcing experiment on 200 movie labels shows AND queries outperform 'same cluster' (105 errors vs 234 errors).  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, with careful proofs and tight lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- The k=2 case results are especially compelling, with AND queries improving the entire query-performance trade-off curve.  
- Connection to semisupervised clustering is overstated in the introduction; the setup is closer to active learning with query constraints.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a single small dataset; more datasets, error analysis, and comparisons to other query strategies would strengthen the paper.  
- The extension and applicability of the AND query approach to k>2 clusters are under-discussed and need clarification.  
- Paper is well-written, original (novel connection to source coding), significant for theory and crowdsourcing, and recommended for acceptance.
