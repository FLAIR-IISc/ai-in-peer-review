- Proposes a novel formulation of semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.  
- Derives new information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries required for correct labeling.  
- Introduces an AND-based querying strategy that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear, concise introduction.  
- Technical contributions are significant and supported by a thorough analysis.  
- Presents a unified framework applicable to noisy queries and approximate recovery.  
- Validates theoretical results with a real crowdsourcing experiment showing high accuracy with few queries.  
- Main weakness: assumes a simple oracle model (i.i.d. answers corrupted by a binary symmetric channel) that may be unrealistic.  
- Reviewer rates the paper highly (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance = 9/10).  
- Suggestions: add more details on experimental setup and results.  
- Suggestions: expand discussion of limitations and directions for future work.  
- Suggestions: include more related-work references and more details/proofs of theoretical analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong technical contributions, clear presentation, and supportive real experiment.  
- Arguments for rejection: unrealistic oracle model and insufficient discussion of limitations/future work.
