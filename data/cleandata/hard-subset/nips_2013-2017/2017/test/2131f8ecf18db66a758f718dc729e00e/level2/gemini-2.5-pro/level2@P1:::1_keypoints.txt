- Introduces a novel connection between non-adaptive, semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.  
- Problem setup: recover true labels of n items with a limited number of non-adaptive Δ-ary queries, relevant to crowdsourcing.  
- Derives new information-theoretic lower bounds for exact and approximate label recovery.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to common same-cluster (XOR) queries.  
- Theoretically and experimentally shows AND queries can be significantly more efficient, especially for noisy, approximate recovery.  
- Main algorithmic result: an efficient scheme that recovers a (1-δ) fraction of labels with O(n log(k/δ)) queries in the noisy setting.  
- Strength — originality/significance: framing querying as locally encodable source coding is a powerful, new theoretical lens.  
- Strength — AND queries novelty: challenges common assumptions and offers a practically motivated, better-performing query type, supported by a small real-world experiment.  
- Strength — technical quality: new non-trivial lower bounds (Theorems 2,3) and constructive achievability results (Theorems 1,4,5) across noiseless/noisy and exact/approximate settings.  
- Strength — completeness: combines theory, algorithms, and both synthetic and real experiments.  
- Weakness — clarity: some theoretical presentations could be clearer, e.g., comparisons to entropy bounds versus deterministic query baselines.  
- Weakness — experimental scale: real-world validation is limited (200 items, 10 volunteers), reducing empirical strength.  
- Weakness — applicability: limited discussion on how AND queries extend to symmetric multi-class problems without a natural default label.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical perspective, practical and provable superiority of AND queries, strong technical results, and balanced empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small-scale experiments and minor clarity issues in theoretical presentation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, impactful work with minor weaknesses.
