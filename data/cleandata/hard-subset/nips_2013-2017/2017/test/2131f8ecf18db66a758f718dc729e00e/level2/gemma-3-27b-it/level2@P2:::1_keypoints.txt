- Establishes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering with binary queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Frames queries as encoding operations and recovered labels as decoded data, allowing information-theoretic analysis.  
- Focuses on locally encodable codes (each query depends on few labels), a less-studied area compared to locally decodable codes.  
- Derives lower bounds on query complexity for clustering using information-theoretic tools.  
- Proposes AND queries as a new querying strategy that can outperform traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries.  
- Uses Massey’s result on limitations of linear codes to motivate non-linear (AND) queries.  
- Handles noisy query answers in the analysis and algorithm design.  
- Provides empirical validation via Amazon Mechanical Turk experiments showing practical benefits.  
- Theoretical derivations and proofs are rigorous and clearly presented overall.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible with generally clear notation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope; larger datasets and varied cluster types are needed.  
- Assumes independent query errors, which may not hold in real-world settings.  
- Some proofs for the noisy setting are complex and need clearer exposition or supplementary material.  
- Lacks in-depth empirical comparison to existing clustering algorithms under equal query budgets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor revisions suggested (expand experiments and clarify complex proofs).
