- Novel connection: Frames semi-supervised clustering with queries as locally encodable source coding, offering a new analytical lens.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives new lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate cluster recovery.  
- Practical contribution: Proposes pairwise AND queries as a superior alternative to standard XOR ("same-cluster") queries, especially in noisy, approximate recovery settings.  
- Originality & significance: Highly original idea that bridges theory and applications (crowdsourcing, entity resolution) and could influence practitioner choices.  
- Technical quality: Sound theoretical analysis with clear proof sketches and a simple recovery algorithm (Algorithm 1).  
- Empirical support: Includes a real-world crowdsourcing experiment validating theoretical predictions and showing fewer worker errors on AND queries.  
- Assumption clarity: Theorem 4 relies on a Poisson degree distribution approximation; this should be explicitly stated and justified.  
- Experimental scale: Crowdsourcing study is small (200 items, 10 volunteers); limitations and scalability should be acknowledged.  
- Overall verdict: Strong, complete contribution with minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
