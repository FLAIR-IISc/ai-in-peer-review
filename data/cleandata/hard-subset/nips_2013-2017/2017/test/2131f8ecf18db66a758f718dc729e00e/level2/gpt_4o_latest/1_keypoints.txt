- Introduces a novel theoretical framework connecting locally encodable source coding with semi-supervised clustering, bridging two distinct domains.  
- Proposes AND queries as a new querying strategy that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries in query efficiency and error reduction.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical bounds for exact and approximate label recovery in both noiseless and noisy query settings.  
- Presents Algorithm 1 that achieves (1−δ)-good recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries in noisy settings.  
- Validates theoretical claims with experiments on real crowdsourcing datasets and synthetic data.  
- Demonstrates the novelty and significance of locally encodable source coding within information theory.  
- Offers clear, detailed proofs and lower bounds grounded in information-theoretic analysis.  
- Paper is dense and may be difficult for readers without background in information theory or clustering.  
- Experimental validation is limited in scale (e.g., 200-movie crowdsourcing dataset and small synthetic sets).  
- Lacks extensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art clustering or crowdsourcing methods.  
- Relies on prior knowledge of cluster sizes or label distributions, which may limit applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental comparisons.
