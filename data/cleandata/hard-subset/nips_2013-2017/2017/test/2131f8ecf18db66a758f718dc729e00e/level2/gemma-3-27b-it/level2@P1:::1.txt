This paper establishes a surprising connection between semisupervised clustering with binary queries and locally encodable source coding. The authors leverage this connection to derive lower bounds on the number of queries required for clustering and propose a novel querying strategy based on AND queries that outperforms traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries in many scenarios, particularly when approximate recovery is sufficient. The work is well-motivated, mathematically rigorous, and includes experimental validation.

**Summary of the Paper:**

The paper frames the problem of clustering with pairwise queries as a locally encodable source coding problem. This allows the authors to apply information-theoretic tools to establish lower bounds on query complexity. The key insight is that the standard "same cluster" query (XOR) is suboptimal, and AND queries can achieve better performance. The paper provides theoretical analysis for both noiseless and noisy query scenarios, and validates the findings with a crowdsourcing experiment on movie classification.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Connection:** The connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding is a significant contribution. It provides a fresh perspective on a well-studied problem and opens up new avenues for research.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper is mathematically sound, with clear definitions, rigorous proofs, and well-motivated theorems. The lower bounds are particularly compelling.
* **Practical Relevance:** The proposed AND query strategy is not just theoretically interesting; it has practical implications for crowdsourcing applications, where query cost is a major concern. The experimental results support this claim.
* **Comprehensive Analysis:** The authors consider both noiseless and noisy query scenarios, providing a more complete picture of the problem. They also address the case when the prior distribution is unknown.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow the technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Locally Encodable Source Coding Background:** While the connection to locally encodable source coding is clever, the paper assumes some familiarity with this field. A slightly more detailed explanation of the concept and its relevance could be beneficial for readers who are not experts in information theory.
* **Experimental Setup:** The experimental setup, while validating, could be strengthened. The choice of movies and the limited number of participants (10) raise questions about the generalizability of the results. A more extensive evaluation on larger and more diverse datasets would be helpful. It’s not entirely clear why the d-regular graph construction improves results.
* **Complexity of Algorithm 1:** While theoretically sound, Algorithm 1 might be complex to implement in practice.  More discussion on the practical considerations of implementing and scaling this algorithm would be useful.
* **Relationship to Existing Work on Query Strategies:** The paper mentions existing work on adaptive querying algorithms. A more thorough comparison of the proposed non-adaptive AND query strategy with these adaptive approaches would strengthen the contribution.

**Originality:**

The connection to locally encodable source coding is novel and represents a significant contribution. The proposal of AND queries as an alternative to XOR queries is also a novel idea, and the analysis demonstrating its advantages is original. While the broader context of semisupervised clustering is well-established, this paper introduces a new and insightful perspective.

**Significance:**

The results have the potential to impact the design of more efficient crowdsourcing systems for clustering and entity resolution. The theoretical insights provided by the paper could also lead to new algorithms and techniques for data compression and coding theory. The work demonstrates the power of applying tools from information theory to problems in machine learning. The observation that AND queries are superior to XOR queries is a particularly impactful finding.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and insightful connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.
* Rigorous theoretical analysis with compelling lower bounds.
* Practical relevance with a proposed AND query strategy that outperforms XOR queries.
* Experimental validation on a real dataset.
* Well-written and organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited experimental evaluation.
* Complexity of the proposed algorithm.
* Could benefit from a more detailed explanation of locally encodable source coding.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field. The connection to locally encodable source coding is a clever insight, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous and compelling. The proposed AND query strategy has practical relevance, and the experimental results support the claims. While the experimental evaluation could be strengthened, the overall quality of the paper warrants acceptance.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept.** I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2017. The authors have presented a novel, theoretically sound, and practically relevant contribution. Addressing the minor weaknesses raised in the review during the discussion period would further strengthen the paper.
