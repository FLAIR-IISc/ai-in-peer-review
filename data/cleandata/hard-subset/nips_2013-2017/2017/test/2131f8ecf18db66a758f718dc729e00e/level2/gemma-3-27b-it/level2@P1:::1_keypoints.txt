- Establishes a novel connection between semisupervised clustering with binary queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Uses this connection to derive information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries required for clustering.  
- Shows the common "same cluster" (XOR) query is suboptimal and proposes AND queries that often perform better, especially for approximate recovery.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis for both noiseless and noisy query models, including cases with unknown priors.  
- Validates theoretical findings with a crowdsourcing experiment on movie classification.  
- Paper is mathematically rigorous, with clear definitions, theorems, and compelling lower bounds.  
- Practical relevance: AND queries could reduce query cost in crowdsourcing and entity resolution applications.  
- Writing and organization are generally clear and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with locally encodable source coding; could use more background exposition.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited (small participant count, single domain); generalizability is unclear.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 may be complex to implement and scale; more practical implementation discussion needed.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison to existing adaptive querying strategies.  
- Originality: connection to source coding and the AND-query proposal are novel and insightful.  
- Significance: findings can influence design of efficient crowdsourcing systems and inspire new coding/ML techniques.  
- Recommendation/overall: strong paper deserving acceptance, with minor issues to address.
