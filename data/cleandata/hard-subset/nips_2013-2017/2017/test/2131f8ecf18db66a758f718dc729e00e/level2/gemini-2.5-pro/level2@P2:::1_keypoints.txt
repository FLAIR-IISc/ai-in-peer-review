- Frames semi-supervised clustering with non-adaptive queries as locally encodable source coding, enabling information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- For noiseless exact recovery (k=2), provides upper/lower bounds showing fewer than n queries suffice by exploiting label priors.  
- For noiseless approximate recovery, derives a new lower bound, shows XOR ('same cluster') queries are suboptimal, and proposes AND queries that outperform XOR.  
- For noisy approximate recovery, presents Algorithm 1 using generalized AND queries achieving (1-δ)-recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries for k clusters.  
- Validates theoretical claims with a crowdsourcing experiment on movie-genre classification supporting AND queries and Algorithm 1.  
- Strength: Novel, significant connection between clustering queries and locally encodable source coding, offering principled limits and new directions.  
- Strength: Introduction and analysis of AND queries, with theoretical and experimental evidence they can outperform 'same cluster' queries and reduce human error.  
- Strength: Strong technical contributions including tight lower bounds and constructive non-adaptive schemes, notably the O(n log(k/δ)) result.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized exposition that logically progresses from noiseless/exact to noisy/approximate settings.  
- Weakness: Some proof sketches are dense and rely on coding-theoretic ideas (e.g., expurgation) without sufficient intuition for a general audience.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on i.i.d. label priors (known p); limited discussion of adversarial/structured settings and asymptotic arguments using random permutations.  
- Weakness: Insufficient direct comparison with 'same cluster' queries in noisy settings and limited experimental baselines for an apples-to-apples comparison.  
- Weakness: Crowdsourcing experiment is limited in scale and class balance (200 items, 10 volunteers), so scalability and robustness to imbalance are unclear.  
- Suggestion: Add higher-level intuition in proof sketches (e.g., explain expurgation) to improve accessibility.  
- Suggestion: Include a formal analysis showing why the decoding strategy fails for 'same cluster' queries in noisy settings to motivate AND queries.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion of the i.i.d. assumption, noting scenarios where it fails and implications for the results.
