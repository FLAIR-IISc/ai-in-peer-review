- Paper frames non-adaptive, low-arity query clustering as equivalent to locally encodable source coding (LESC) and derives bounds and algorithms from this viewpoint.
- Main message: pairwise XOR (same-cluster) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery; pairwise AND queries achieve better query–error trade-offs.
- Results cover noiseless exact recovery (k=2), noiseless approximate recovery (k=2), and noisy approximate recovery (general k).
- Theorem 1: parity (XOR) queries can achieve m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) queries for exact recovery in certain regimes (statement needs corrected/log-base consistency).
- Locality-aware lower bounds show limits below classical n h(p) and quantify penalties from Δ-locality constraints.
- New lower bound for approximate recovery under locality (Theorem 3) is interesting but hard to parse and needs a proof sketch and regimes of tightness.
- Achievability: AND queries with a simple local decoder match improved approximate-recovery trade-offs.
- For noisy queries and general k, a pairwise k-ary AND plus threshold decoder achieves (1−δ)-accurate recovery with m = O(n log(k/δ)).
- Crowdsourcing experiment (k=2) suggests workers make fewer errors on AND vs. same-cluster queries, supporting behavioral hypothesis.
- Technical arguments (typical set, expurgation, Chernoff, dependency bounds) are standard and appear correct at a high level.
- Several technical statements require clarification: Theorem 1’s formula, Theorem 2 parameter ranges, and a clearer exposition of Theorem 3.
- Conjecture 1 comparing XOR limits relies on an unproven lower bound; an unconditional (even weaker) bound would be preferable.
- Algorithmic note: exact-recovery decoder is ML over a typical set and computationally infeasible; discuss efficient alternatives or trade-offs.
- Noisy-recovery algorithm is linear-time and practical, a strong algorithmic contribution.
- Empirics are limited: only a small real experiment; recommend synthetic sweeps over p,q,d,δ and comparisons to strong same-cluster baselines (spectral/SDP).
- Clarity issues: notation inconsistencies (Δ vs d; m=nd^2), log bases, typos, and duplicated references need fixing.
- More intuition requested on why AND yields an exploitable expectation gap while XOR is symmetric under noise.
- For k>2, discuss practical phrasing and cognitive load of k-ary AND queries and whether they can be simulated by binary questions.
- Originality: LESC framing, locality-aware bounds, and the insight that AND outperforms XOR (with proofs) are novel contributions.
- Significance: refines limits for crowdsourced clustering under locality and offers a practical noisy k-class algorithm with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.
- Pros: clear conceptual bridge, new locality-aware lower bounds, scalable noisy algorithm, empirical support for AND, non-adaptive designs, linear-time decoding.
- Cons: some proofs/exposition need tightening, exact-recovery uses infeasible ML decoder, limited evaluation scope, and minor inconsistencies/typos.
- Concrete suggestions: fix Theorem 1, add proof sketch/plots for Theorem 3, provide unconditional XOR lower bounds, discuss k-ary query phrasing, expand experiments, and clean references/notation.
- Overall: compelling theoretical and practical case for AND queries via LESC; with clarifications and stronger experiments, the work would be a valuable contribution.
