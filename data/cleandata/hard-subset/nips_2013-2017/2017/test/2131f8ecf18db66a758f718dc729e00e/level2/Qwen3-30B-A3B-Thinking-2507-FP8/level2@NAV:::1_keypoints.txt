- Main claim: semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries is equivalent to locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Main claim: AND queries significantly outperform standard "same cluster" (XOR) queries for approximate recovery.  
- Main claim: the authors derive tight information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Theoretical strength: rigorous proofs for both lower and upper bounds, covering noiseless and noisy queries (Theorems 1–5).  
- Guarantees: provable exact and approximate recovery results under the proposed framework.  
- Experimental validation: crowdsourced movie-genre task (100 action, 100 romantic) shows AND queries yield 105 vs. 234 errors in 1,000 queries, matching theory.  
- Novelty: connects clustering and information theory, revealing fundamental limits of "same-cluster" queries and proposing a principled alternative.  
- Practical relevance: approach reduces query costs in crowdsourcing-style applications and is empirically effective.  
- Limitations: assumption of independent query errors may not reflect real-world correlated crowd behavior.  
- Limitations: experimental section lacks discussion of implementation challenges for AND queries at large scale.  
- Limitations: theoretical development for k>2 clusters is less complete than for k=2.  
- Recommendation: significant contribution with sound theory and appropriate experiments; paper should be accepted.
