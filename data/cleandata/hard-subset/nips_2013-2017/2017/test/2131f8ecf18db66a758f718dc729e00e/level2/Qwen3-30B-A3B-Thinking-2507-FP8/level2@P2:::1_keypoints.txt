- Establishes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Proposes a query model using pairwise "same cluster" and AND queries to cluster unlabeled elements with minimal queries.  
- Key contribution: shows AND queries significantly outperform "same cluster" queries for approximate recovery, with theoretical guarantees.  
- Proves equivalence between the clustering problem and locally encodable source coding, deriving novel information-theoretic lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Demonstrates suboptimality of "same cluster" (XOR-equivalent) queries for approximate recovery using Massey’s classical result.  
- Presents an AND query scheme with theoretical guarantees for approximate recovery (Theorem 4) and a main algorithmic result for noisy queries (Theorem 5).  
- Experimental validation on a crowdsourcing dataset (100 action + 100 romantic movies, 10 volunteers) shows AND queries produce far fewer errors (105 vs. 234).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates itself appropriately in prior work on query-based clustering.  
- Technical content and proofs appear sound, thorough, and convincing.  
- Main limitation: theoretical results focus primarily on the binary case (k=2), with only brief mentions of extensions to larger k.  
- Experimental limitation: small-scale dataset and limited number of volunteers may not generalize to more complex settings.  
- Practical limitation: assumes known prior distribution/cluster sizes and does not analyze the impact of estimation errors from subsampling.  
- Related-work concern: claim of novelty about locally encodable source coding may be overstated; [23] seems to address "smooth compression" and authors may have missed relevant information-theory literature.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and novel; accept with minor revisions to address k>2 extensions, related-work coverage, and broaden/strengthen experimental validation.
