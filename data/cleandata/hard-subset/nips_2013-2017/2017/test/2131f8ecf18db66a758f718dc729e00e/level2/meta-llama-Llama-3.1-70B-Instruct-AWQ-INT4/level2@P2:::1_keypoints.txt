- Reduces semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding, yielding new information-theoretic lower bounds on queries.
- Noiseless exact recovery: shows αn (α<1) nonadaptive pairwise 'same cluster' queries recover all labels w.h.p. for a range of priors.
- Noiseless approximate recovery: provides a new lower bound for recovering (1−δ) fraction and proposes an AND-based querying strategy that performs substantially better.
- Noisy approximate recovery: proves (1−δ) fraction can be recovered with O(n log(k/δ)) nonadaptive queries even with noisy answers.
- Strength: novel theoretical framework applying locally encodable source coding to semisupervised clustering.
- Strength: provides new information-theoretic lower bounds and superior querying strategies.
- Strength: empirical results on a real crowdsourced dataset demonstrate effectiveness.
- Weakness: relies on a specific oracle noise model that may not be realistic in all scenarios.
- Weakness: AND-scheme analysis depends on a Poisson approximation that may be inaccurate for some parameter values.
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited to a single dataset; broader experiments are needed.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address analytical assumptions and expand experiments.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
