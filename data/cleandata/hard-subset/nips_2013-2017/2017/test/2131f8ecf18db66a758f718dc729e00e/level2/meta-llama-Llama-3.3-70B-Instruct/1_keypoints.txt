- Paper proposes a novel semisupervised multiclass labeling approach by connecting it to locally encodable source coding from information theory.
- Authors derive new information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed to recover correct labels.
- They propose an AND-based querying scheme that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries.
- The paper is well-written with a clear introduction and related work discussion.
- Technical contributions are significant, with a thorough theoretical analysis of the querying scheme.
- Experimental results on a real crowdsourced dataset validate the theoretical findings and show algorithm effectiveness.
- Strength: novel connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding offering a new perspective.
- Strength: provision of new information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity.
- Strength: AND-based query scheme that empirically and theoretically outperforms same-cluster queries.
- Strength: thorough analysis including theoretical guarantees and experiments.
- Weakness: assumes the oracle returns binary answers, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: querying scheme is limited to pairwise queries and may be insufficient for complex clustering tasks.
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms for comprehensive evaluation.
- Overall assessment: significant and sound technical contribution that validates theory with experiments but could be improved by addressing practical assumptions and broader comparisons.
- Pro-accept: novel/significant contribution, sound technical results, and potential to inspire further research.
- Con-accept: reliance on binary-oracle assumption, restriction to pairwise queries, and missing SOTA comparisons limit evaluation.
