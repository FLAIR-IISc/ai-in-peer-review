- Novel connection established between semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding.
- Framing the querying problem as a compression task (each compressed bit depends on few inputs) enables use of information-theoretic tools.
- This framework yields well-motivated, rigorously derived lower bounds on the number of queries required.
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to traditional same-cluster (XOR) queries, showing better performance for approximate recovery.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and the cross-field insight is a major strength.
- Authors acknowledge and justify the non-adaptive query assumption in crowdsourcing contexts.
- Experimental validation is limited but provides encouraging support for theoretical claims.
- Presentation is dense and requires substantial background in information theory to follow.
- Practical implications beyond the specific pairwise-query setting are not clearly articulated.
- Experimental section is brief and lacks broader comparisons to existing crowdsourcing algorithms.
- The paper should discuss the potential impact of adaptive query strategies, which is currently missing.
- Connections to broader active learning and query-selection literature are insufficiently explored.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, clarify broader implications, and relate findings to active learning.
