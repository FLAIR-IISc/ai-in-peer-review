- Establishes an equivalence between semisupervised clustering with binary queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Uses that connection to derive information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed for clustering.  
- Proposes AND queries as a novel querying strategy that can outperform traditional ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries in several scenarios.  
- Well-motivated application to crowdsourcing-based clustering with the goal of minimizing query costs.  
- Technically sound framework; proofs appear rigorous though many details are deferred to supplementary material.  
- Brings information-theoretic tools to a practical clustering problem—a key conceptual contribution.  
- Considers noisy query environments, increasing real-world relevance.  
- Experimental validation on real datasets supports the approach but is brief and limited.  
- Comparison with existing crowdsourcing and information-theory literature is thorough, enhancing contextualization.  
- Extensions to k > 2 clusters and incorporation of prior knowledge improve generality and practical applicability.  
- Presentation issues: dense theoretical exposition with many theorems/equations that would benefit from more intuition and illustrative examples.  
- Empirical issues: experiments should be expanded and include comparisons to more baseline clustering and adaptive querying algorithms.  
- Notational clarity needs improvement (e.g., inconsistent use of 'm' and 'd').  
- Assumption of independent query errors should be discussed in greater depth, acknowledging real-world deviations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and technically solid paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on experiments and presentation clarity.
