- Formalizes nonadaptive, budgeted query-based labeling as “locally encodable” source coding where each query depends on ≤Δ labels.
- Noiseless exact recovery (k=2): Δ-wise XOR queries suffice with m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) and a new locality-exploiting converse improving on naive bounds for some p.
- Noiseless approximate recovery (k=2): Proves a new lower bound for any Δ-local scheme and argues XOR/same-cluster queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery.
- Proposes pairwise AND queries for approximate recovery and analyzes a simple decoder showing improved query–accuracy trade-offs.
- Noisy approximate recovery (general k): Introduces k-ary AND queries and a thresholding algorithm on d-regular graphs achieving recovery with m = O(n log(k/δ)) under independent per-query noise.
- Handles unknown priors by random permutation and analyzes cluster-size-known setting.
- Small real crowdsourcing experiment (200 movies, 10 volunteers, 1000 queries) shows AND queries produce fewer worker errors than same-cluster queries (105 vs 234).
- Theoretical analyses (Theorems 1–2) use standard random coding/expurgation and locality arguments and appear technically sound, though some converse expressions are terse.
- Theorem 3 (approximate/noiseless lower bound) is deferred and described as involved, lacking a sketch which makes assessment difficult.
- AND achievability (Theorem 4) is carefully analyzed using Poisson approximation and inclusion–exclusion; the decoder is simple and plausible.
- Noisy analysis (Theorem 5) uses Chernoff bounds and dependency-graph concentration; argument is clean but omits explicit constants and dependence on p,q,k.
- Claim that XOR is fundamentally inferior partly relies on a conjecture generalizing known results (proven only at p=1/2).
- Experiments are supportive but small-scale; synthetic comparisons are informative but limited in scope and baselines.
- Paper has clarity issues: typos, formatting glitches, misplaced footnotes, missing figures, and some theorem/variable definitions unclear.
- Algorithm 1 is clear; recommend proofreading and adding proof sketches for deferred results.
- Originality: novel framing as locally encodable source coding and new locality-aware converses; AND-based insight appears new.
- Significance: advances theoretical limits of crowdsourced clustering with practical constraints; k-ary AND may increase worker load for k>2 but binary AND (k=2) is practical.
- Pros: fresh conceptual connection, new locality-aware lower bounds, simple practical AND schemes with rigorous guarantees, empirical evidence for AND ease.
- Cons: deferred proofs, reliance on conjecture for part of XOR lower bound, limited experimental evaluation, and presentation/clarity issues.
- Suggested author actions: provide proof sketch for Theorem 3, clarify Theorem 2 (ρ ranges), give formal XOR lower bound under Δ-locality beyond p=1/2, report constants in Theorem 5, and compare to other small-arity/nonadaptive queries and adaptive schemes.
- Recommendation: Weak accept; novel theoretical contributions and practical AND primitive merit publication despite presentation and empirical limitations (confidence: medium).
