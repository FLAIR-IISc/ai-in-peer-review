- Summary: Proposes an information-theoretic framework modeling semisupervised clustering as locally encodable source coding and introduces AND queries as an alternative to "same cluster" queries, with theoretical and experimental support.

- Main claim: AND queries require significantly fewer queries for approximate recovery (O(n log(k/δ)) vs. O(n log n)) in noiseless and noisy settings.

- Theoretical strength: Provides non-trivial information-theoretic lower bounds and exact recovery results for certain priors (Theorems 1–3).

- Practical validation: Crowdsourcing experiment on 200 movies shows lower error for AND queries (10.5%) versus same-cluster queries (23.4%).

- Technical clarity: Carefully distinguishes exact vs. approximate recovery, which is relevant for practical use.

- Weakness — clustering vs labeling: Paper conflates clustering (partition equivalence) with labeling (specific label values), while algorithms seem to require label knowledge.

- Weakness — k > 2 generalization: Extensions to k > 2 are insufficiently detailed and the k-ary AND definition is confusing.

- Weakness — worker model justification: Claim that AND queries are easier for workers is underdeveloped and potentially incorrect given ambiguity in "no" answers.

- Weakness — comparison to prior work: Insufficiently thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art algorithms (e.g., [21,22]) to contextualize the claimed query complexity gains.

- Weakness — experiment scale: Real-world validation is small-scale (200 items) and may not generalize to larger, more complex datasets.

- Suggested fixes: Clarify clustering vs labeling, expand k > 2 analysis, better justify human-worker performance for AND queries, and provide a more comprehensive comparison to prior methods.

- Overall assessment: Valuable and novel contribution with sound theory and supportive experiments, but weakened by conceptual confusions and incomplete extensions; merits accept with minor revisions.

- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
