- Framing: Recasts semi-supervised clustering via querying as a locally encodable source coding problem, an insightful and novel perspective.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on recent crowdsourcing and query-complexity literature and positions itself against XOR-based querying approaches.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provides rigorous lower bounds on query complexity and provable guarantees for proposed schemes.  
- AND-query advantage: Demonstrates that AND-queries outperform traditional XOR/"same cluster" queries and challenges conventional wisdom.  
- Noise modeling: Explicitly addresses noisy oracles and proposes schemes with accompanying analysis.  
- Experimental validation: Includes a crowdsourcing experiment that gives some empirical support but is limited in scope and scale.  
- Experimental limitations: Dataset small (200 movies); lacks exploration of varying oracle noise levels and graph regularity d; spectral clustering comparison omitted from main text.  
- Presentation complexity: Paper is dense and requires strong background in information theory; some parts need more intuitive explanations and examples.  
- Assumption concerns: Assumes independent query errors, which may not hold in practice; lacks discussion of correlated errors.  
- Computational cost missing: Focuses on query complexity but omits discussion of decoding/reconstruction computational complexity and scalability.  
- Section-specific notes: Section 2 motivates non-adaptive querying well; Section 3.1’s exact recovery theorem has limited practical impact; Section 3.2’s lower-bound proof needs more intuition.  
- Graph design question: Use of d-regular graphs is sensible for parallel querying, but the impact of d on performance requires further analysis.  
- Experimental details needed: Clarify crowdworker instructions, training/examples provided, and how ground truth movie labels were established.  
- Acceptance recommendation: Recommend Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing experiments, clarity, computational discussion, and error-correlation issues.  
- Requested revisions: Expand experiments (larger datasets, noise levels, include spectral comparison), add intuitive explanations/examples, discuss decoding complexity and correlated errors.
