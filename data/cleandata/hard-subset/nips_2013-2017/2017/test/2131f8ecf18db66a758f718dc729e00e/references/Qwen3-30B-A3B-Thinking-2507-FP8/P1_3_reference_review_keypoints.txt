- Paper reframes label learning/clustering with minimal oracle queries as a locally encodable source coding problem and proposes AND queries over traditional XOR queries for improved approximate recovery.
- Strong theoretical contribution: derives rigorous information-theoretic bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Novel query strategy: introduces AND queries and proves (Theorems 3–5) they require fewer queries than XOR for comparable recovery performance.
- Practical validation: a crowdsourcing experiment on movies shows AND queries yield substantially lower error (105 vs. 234) than XOR queries.
- Clear organization: paper cleanly separates theory (Sections 3.1–3.3) and experiments (Section 4), with effective illustrative figures.
- Limited scope for k>2: main theoretical results focus on k=2 and extensions to general k are underdeveloped, especially for noisy queries.
- Practical implementation concerns: assumes known prior distribution p; Theorem 6 offers a relative-size workaround but practical application is not fully explored.
- Small-scale experiment: crowdsourcing test used only 100 movies and 10 volunteers, limiting empirical generality.
- Lack of comparison with recent related work: does not compare against newer clustering-with-queries papers (e.g., 2020 work), weakening positioning.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on significant theoretical novelty, empirical support, and practical implications.
- Final assessment: AND query approach is a meaningful, well-supported contribution despite minor issues (k>2 extension, experiment scale, prior knowledge), and merits NIPS publication.
