- Paper formulates label learning/clustering with limited noisy pairwise queries as a locally encodable source coding problem.  
- Leverages information-theoretic tools to derive lower bounds on query complexity and propose querying schemes.  
- Main technical contribution: AND queries can outperform traditional XOR ("same cluster") queries for approximate recovery.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 2–5) with bounds and achievability results.  
- Extends analysis to noisy oracle answers, increasing practical relevance for crowdsourcing.  
- Includes an Amazon Mechanical Turk experiment to validate theoretical claims.  
- Situates work well within crowdsourced entity resolution, adaptive querying, and information-theoretic crowdsourcing literature.  
- Novel connection to locally encodable source coding and links to prior work (e.g., Massey) are insightful.  
- Experimental details are sparse (participant pool, interface, quality control) and the claim that AND queries are "easier" lacks robust supporting data.  
- Experiments are limited to two clusters (k=2), leaving scalability to larger k untested.  
- Some theoretical derivations (Sections 3.2–3.3) are dense and could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Assumes known prior/cluster-size distribution without fully addressing practical estimation challenges.  
- The prohibition on query repetition is justified but its real-world impact and practicality need more discussion.  
- Figure 1 is hard to interpret due to unclear axes/curve explanations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and rigorous paper with minor clarity and experimental-scope weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to clarify theory and expand/clarify experimental details.
