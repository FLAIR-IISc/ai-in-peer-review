- Overall assessment: Novel, theoretically strong connection between crowdsourced clustering and locally encodable source coding; proposes AND queries and recommends acceptance with minor revisions.

- Summary: Frames label learning under query budgets as a locally encodable source coding problem, derives lower bounds, and shows AND queries can outperform XOR in noisy settings.

- Key theoretical contribution: Rigorous derivation of lower bounds on query complexity using information-theoretic tools.

- Query design insight: Demonstrates AND queries outperform traditional “same cluster” (XOR) queries in certain scenarios, especially with noise.

- Algorithmic result: Provides theoretical guarantees for an AND-query based algorithm that recovers a large fraction of labels with fewer queries.

- Noise handling: Analyzes noisy oracle responses and argues majority voting can be suboptimal, motivating more sophisticated methods.

- Experimental validation: Includes a small-scale crowdsourced experiment (100 movies) showing AND queries yield lower error than XOR, but scale is limited.

- Novel framing: The connection to locally encodable source coding is original and opens new analytical tools for the problem.

- Mathematical rigor: Analysis is solid and proofs appear sound, though notation is dense.

- Limited experiments: Dataset size and scope are small; comparisons to stronger baselines are lacking.

- Practical impact unclear: Real-world gains and implementation costs (e.g., worker cognitive load) of AND queries are not fully explored.

- Noisy-case complexity: The noisy-case analysis is complex and constant factors and practical implications are not easy to assess.

- Redundancy: Justification for disallowing repeated queries is reiterated multiple times and could be more concise.

- Clarity issues: Dense notation and some proof sketches require careful reading; Algorithm 1 is described informally and would benefit from pseudocode.

- Assumptions on noise: Independent error assumption is standard but its limitations and handling of correlated worker errors are not addressed.

- Sensitivity to priors: Dependence on prior cluster-size knowledge is noted, but sensitivity to inaccuracies is not evaluated.

- Local decodability link: The relation to locally decodable source coding is mentioned briefly and could be elaborated for potential synergies.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach; AND-query insight is significant; potential to influence crowdsourcing system design.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental validation, unclear practical implications, and complex analysis with uncertain constant factors.

- Recommendation and score: Accept with minor revisions (score 8.0/10); strengthen experiments, compare to baselines, clarify practical implications, and improve exposition.
