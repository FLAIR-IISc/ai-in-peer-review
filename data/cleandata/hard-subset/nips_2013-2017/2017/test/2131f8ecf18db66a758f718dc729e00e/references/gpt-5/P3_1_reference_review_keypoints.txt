- Summary: Frames non-adaptive, budgeted crowdsourced labeling as "locally encodable source coding" and studies XOR vs AND queries across three regimes: noiseless exact recovery (Δ-ary XOR, ML bounds), noiseless approximate recovery (AND shown better than XOR for lossy), and noisy approximate recovery (k-ary AND with O(n log(k/δ)) queries).  
- Novelty claim: Locally encodable source coding viewpoint and systematic XOR vs AND comparison are novel contributions.

- Strength: Conceptual contribution — insightful framing yields new locality-aware lower bounds.  
- Strength: Technical — achievability for exact recovery, improved converse in regimes, clean AND-query algorithm with O(n log(k/δ)) guarantees, simple analyses and robustness to unknown priors.  
- Strength: Practical — AND queries argued cognitively easier; small user study supports reduced worker error.

- Weakness: Query-count inconsistency — paper misstates m (nd^2 vs correct nd/2) across sections and experiments.  
- Weakness: Expurgation/achievability argument unclear and potentially invalid as written; needs formal probabilistic guarantees.  
- Weakness: Notation and presentation issues — inconsistent symbols, typos, missing figures, and key proofs deferred, harming verifiability.

- Weakness: Empirics limited — small-scale (n=200, 10 users), hand-picked categories, and uneven baselines (simple thresholding vs spectral), lacking stronger XOR baselines and larger datasets.  
- Weakness: Claim about XOR suboptimality rests partly on conjectured linear-code bounds and should be qualified in scope/generality.

- Weakness: Positioning — connections to LDGM lossy compression and group-testing literature are underdeveloped and should be strengthened.  
- Weakness: Noise model assumptions — symmetric confusion among incorrect outputs may be unrealistic; robustness discussion needed.

- Quality: High-level ideas and noisy-approximate analysis are sound, but technical slips and deferred proofs reduce confidence in some results.  
- Clarity: Manuscript needs substantial polishing: correct m, consistent notation, reorganized proofs, cleaned footnotes, and correct figure references.

- Originality: The locally encodable coding viewpoint and use of AND queries for non-adaptive crowdsourcing are original.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful if clarified and validated; practical influence depends on broader empirical support.

- Actionable suggestions: Fix m counting everywhere; formalize the expurgation-based achievability rigorously; state/sketch Theorem 3 in main text; add strong non-adaptive XOR baselines and larger crowdsourced experiments; discuss robustness to asymmetric noise and learning thresholds; relate to LDGM/group-testing literature.

- Pros: Novel theoretical framing, locality-aware lower bounds, and a simple AND-based practical scheme with provable noisy guarantees.  
- Cons: Notation/clarity errors, key proofs deferred, limited and uneven empirical validation, and some claims relying on conjectures.

- Recommendation: Score 5 (borderline reject); encourage resubmission after addressing clarity, proofs, and empirical/analytic strengthening.
