- Introduces a novel framework that frames clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.  
- Proposes AND-queries instead of traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries and shows they need far fewer queries for approximate recovery.  
- Establishes new information-theoretic lower bounds (Theorems 2, 3) and constructive upper bounds (Theorems 1, 4, 5), often asymptotically tight.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-structured, though some proofs (notably Theorem 3) are dense.  
- Empirical crowdsourced experiment (IMDB movies) validates claims: AND-queries yield 55% fewer errors than same-cluster queries.  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-defined problem statement, consistent notation, and effective figures.  
- Some technical sections would benefit from more intuitive explanations (e.g., error probability derivation in Theorem 4).  
- The connection between clustering and locally encodable source coding is original and novel for the community.  
- Extends analysis to noisy query settings, enhancing robustness and practical relevance.  
- Significant practical impact: reduces query complexity from O(n log n) to O(n log(1/δ)) for (1−δ)-approximate recovery.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical framework, strong guarantees, real-data validation, practical crowdsourcing insights, and analysis of exact/approximate recovery.  
- Weaknesses: limited focus on k>2 clusters, assumes prior knowledge of cluster sizes, single-dataset experiment, and some inaccessible proof details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance.
