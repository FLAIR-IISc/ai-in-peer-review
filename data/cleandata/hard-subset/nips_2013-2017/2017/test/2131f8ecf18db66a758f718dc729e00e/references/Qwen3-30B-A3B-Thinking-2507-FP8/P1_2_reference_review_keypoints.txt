- Introduces a novel information-theoretic framework framing semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.
- Proposes AND queries (answer 1 iff both labels are 1) as a more efficient alternative to "same cluster" (XOR) queries.
- Provides theoretical upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Establishes new information-theoretic lower bounds for locally encodable source codes, a novel contribution to information theory.
- Demonstrates rigorous, well-structured proofs and careful attention to conditions for the presented bounds.
- Validates theory with a real crowdsourcing experiment showing substantially fewer errors for AND vs. same-cluster queries (105 vs. 234 errors in 1000 queries).
- Connects clustering and locally encodable codes clearly, making the information-theoretic perspective accessible to experts.
- Some technical sections (notably Section 3.2) lack intuition explaining why AND queries outperform XOR queries.
- The paper could better justify why AND queries are easier for human workers beyond a brief statement.
- Much of the analysis focuses on binary labels (k=2), with only limited discussion of generalization to k>2.
- Original contributions include the new framework, the AND-query insight for approximate recovery, and first results on locally encodable codes.
- Results are significant: improved query complexity for approximate recovery (O(n log k/δ) vs. O(n log n)) and practical impact for crowdsourcing and entity resolution.
- Strengths: novel framework, AND queries, rigorous analysis, real-world validation, clear theory-practice link.
- Weaknesses: need more intuition in technical sections, limited human-worker explanation, focus on k=2, and more comparisons to state-of-the-art would help.
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, well-written, practically validated, and makes a clear advance over prior work.
