

# Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

This paper proposes a novel perspective on semisupervised clustering by framing it as a locally encodable source coding problem. The authors introduce AND queries as a more efficient alternative to traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries for crowdsourced clustering tasks. They provide theoretical bounds for exact and approximate recovery under noiseless and noisy settings, and validate their approach with a real crowdsourcing experiment. The paper makes a reasonable connection to information theory and source coding, with the main contribution being the AND query framework that achieves better query complexity than existing approaches.

**Major Weaknesses:**

1. **Critical Missing Citations:** The paper cites [4] in the Introduction ("Clustering with same-cluster queries, H. Ashtiani, S. Kushagra, S. Ben-David, Advances In Neural Information Processing Systems, pages 3216–3224") but [4] does not appear in the reference list. Similarly, the paper cites [21] in the Introduction ("Clustering with noisy queries, A. Mazumdar, B. Saha, Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS) 31") but [21] is not in the reference list. This is a serious omission that undermines the paper's credibility.

2. **Mathematical Errors in Theorems:**
   - Theorem 1: The expression for α should be "α = 1/2 (1 + (1 - 4p(1-p))^Δ)" but is written as "α = 12 (1 + (1 - 4p(1 - p)) ∆)" with a missing slash and incorrect exponent notation.
   - Theorem 3: The expression "δ ≥ δ̃ (m n ) + h(p)− h(δ̃(mn )) h′(δ̃(mn ))(1 + e ∆h′(δ̃(mn )))" contains multiple notational errors. It should be "δ ≥ δ̃(m/n) + [h(p) - h(δ̃(m/n))]/h'(δ̃(m/n)) * (1 + e^(Δh'(δ̃(m/n))))" but the formatting is incorrect and the exponent is unclear.
   - Theorem 5: The paper claims "O(n log 1δ)" queries but the actual result is "O(n log kδ)" (Theorem 5, Section 3.3). This is a significant discrepancy between the claim and the result.

3. **Inconsistent Notation and Definitions:**
   - The paper uses p to denote both the probability of label 1 (p1 = p) and label 0 (p0 = 1-p), but later uses p in theorems without clear definition. In Theorem 1, p is used for the probability of label 1, but in Theorem 4, it's used for the probability of label 0.
   - The noise model is described as "the oracle provides correct answer with probability 1− q, and any one of the remaining incorrect answers with probability qk−1" which is incorrect. It should be q/(k-1) for each incorrect answer, not qk-1.
   - In Algorithm 1, the threshold for Ci is written as "dq k−1 + dpi 2 ( 1− qkk−1 )" which is mathematically incoherent.

4. **Formatting and Typographical Issues:**
   - Inconsistent spacing around mathematical operators (e.g., "nH(p) ± o(n)" vs "nH(p)+o(1)").
   - Inconsistent use of "k" (number of clusters) vs "k" as an index in formulas.
   - Multiple missing parentheses and incorrect mathematical notation throughout the paper.

**Strengths:**

- The paper makes a novel connection between clustering and locally encodable source coding, which is a fresh perspective in the field.
- The AND query approach is theoretically sound and appears to offer better query complexity than existing methods.
- The real-world crowdsourcing experiment provides empirical validation of the theoretical claims.
- The paper's theoretical analysis for exact recovery (Theorem 1) is interesting and provides a better bound than the naive n queries for certain parameter ranges.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

**Pro:**
- Novel perspective on clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.
- AND queries provide a theoretically better approach than traditional "same cluster" queries.
- Real-world experiment validates the theoretical results.
- The paper makes a reasonable connection to information theory.

**Con:**
- Critical missing citations ([4] and [21]) undermine the paper's credibility.
- Multiple mathematical errors in theorems that affect the validity of the results.
- Inconsistent notation and definitions throughout the paper.
- Discrepancy between claimed complexity (O(n log 1/δ)) and actual result (O(n log k/δ)).
- The noise model description is incorrect, which could invalidate the theoretical results.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting theoretical framework that connects clustering with locally encodable source coding. The AND query approach is novel and appears promising, but the paper is marred by multiple mathematical errors and inconsistent notation. The most serious issue is the missing citations for [4] and [21], which are referenced multiple times in the Introduction but not in the reference list. This is a fundamental error in academic writing that cannot be overlooked.

The mathematical errors in the theorems (particularly the discrepancy between the claimed O(n log 1/δ) complexity and the actual O(n log k/δ) result) further undermine the paper's credibility. The inconsistent notation and formatting issues make the paper difficult to follow and raise concerns about the authors' attention to detail.

While the theoretical ideas are potentially valuable, the technical flaws and presentation issues are too severe to recommend acceptance. The paper would need to be substantially revised to correct the mathematical errors, fix the notational inconsistencies, and include all missing references before it could be considered for publication.

Given the stringent requirements of NIPS for technical precision and clarity, I recommend rejection of this paper due to the substantial number of technical and presentation flaws.
