- Novel framing: Recasts clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem, offering a new theoretical perspective.  
- Query innovation: Identifies AND queries as a superior alternative to XOR/"same cluster" queries for approximate recovery.  
- Theoretical results: Provides new information-theoretic bounds (Theorem 1 gives an improved upper bound for k=2 when p ≥ 0.0694).  
- Lower bounds: Theorem 2 offers a stronger lower bound than the standard entropy bound for small p.  
- AND vs XOR proof: Theorem 4 shows AND queries can significantly outperform XOR for approximate recovery.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Presents Algorithm 1 for noisy queries with k ≥ 2, running in O(n log k/δ) queries.  
- Rigorous analysis: Theorem 5 uses concentration bounds to tightly control error probability scaling.  
- Practical insight: Argues AND queries are more intuitive for crowd workers.  
- Empirical support: Small crowdsourcing experiment on IMDB movie labels shows fewer errors with AND queries (105 vs. 234).  
- Experimental limitations: Validation limited to one dataset and only 10 volunteers; larger/multiple datasets needed.  
- Accessibility and exposition: Proofs (Theorems 2–4) are dense; paper would benefit from more intuitive explanations for why AND outperforms XOR.  
- Related work and assumptions: Connection to "smooth compression" and contrast with locally decodable codes is underexplored; assumes known prior p without detailed discussion of estimation/subsampling.  
- Overall verdict: Substantial theoretical and practical contribution with sound analysis and experiments; recommendation to accept.
