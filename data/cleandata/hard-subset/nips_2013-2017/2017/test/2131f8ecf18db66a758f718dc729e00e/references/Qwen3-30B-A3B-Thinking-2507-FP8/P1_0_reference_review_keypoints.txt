- Novel connection: models clustering as locally encodable source coding and shows AND queries outperform XOR ("same cluster") queries for approximate recovery.
- New theoretical lower bounds for locally encodable source coding.
- Constructive scheme: AND-query algorithm achieving (1−δ)-good recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.
- Empirical validation: crowdsourcing experiment shows AND queries have fewer errors (105 vs. 234) than XOR queries.
- Rigorous analysis with matching upper and lower bounds demonstrating tightness.
- Addresses both noiseless and noisy query settings and discusses method limitations.
- Practical significance: reduces query complexity from O(n log n) to O(n log(1/δ)), important for cost-sensitive crowdsourcing.
- Clear exposition, well-organized paper, and effective illustrative figures.
- Limited experimental scope: real-world validation only for k=2 despite theoretical results for general k.
- Assumes prior distribution p is known (or estimable); lacks detailed discussion of effects when p is unknown or poorly estimated.
- Insufficient detailed comparison with recent state-of-the-art works [4,21,33].
- Suggestion: clarify relationship to literature on locally decodable source codes [19,16,26].
- Suggestion: provide more intuitive explanation for why AND queries outperform XOR.
- Suggestion: compare noisy-query strategy to common crowdsourcing error-correction methods (e.g., majority voting).
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution with practical relevance — Strong Accept.
