- Paper studies nonadaptive, budgeted querying for semi-supervised clustering under a probabilistic prior and noisy answers, framed as locally encodable source coding where each query depends on ≤ Δ labels.  
- Main technical results: exact recovery (noiseless) with Δ-ary XOR achieving m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) and a locality-aware converse improving on n h(p).  
- Approximate recovery (noiseless): locality-aware lower bound (Theorem 3), claim that XOR is suboptimal for rate–distortion, and AND pairwise queries with Poisson-degree analysis.  
- Approximate recovery (noisy, k classes): k-ary pairwise AND-type query with symmetric noise and a d-regular graph thresholding algorithm achieving (1−δ)-accuracy with m = O(n log(k/δ)).  
- Small crowdsourcing study suggests humans find AND-type queries easier than “same-cluster” queries.  
- Strength: conceptual novelty—locally encodable source-coding framing yields new converses beyond n h(p).  
- Strength: clear provable advantage of AND queries for approximate recovery, exploiting asymmetry and neighborhood structure.  
- Strength: scalable, nonadaptive noisy algorithm (d-regular graph + thresholding) with interpretable complexity m = Θ(n log(k/δ)).  
- Strength: practical motivation and initial human-subject evidence favoring AND queries.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3’s proof deferred and its unusual form needs more intuition, corollaries, and sanity checks against known rate–distortion limits.  
- Weakness: pervasive typesetting ambiguity (“nd2” should be n d / 2) causing confusion.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s expurgation argument needs a more self-contained sketch and explicit o(1) quantification.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s converse derivation is terse; needs more detailed steps, figures, and regimes where it beats n h(p).  
- Weakness: XOR-suboptimality claims rely on conjectured bounds; a rigorous XOR-only lower bound or fairer XOR baseline is needed.  
- Weakness: Experimental issues—small, nonindependent user study, weak baselines (no comparisons to known nonadaptive schemes or worker models), and no k>2 experiments.  
- Weakness: Related work coverage should better connect to nonadaptive group testing and LDGM/LDPC lossy compression literature.  
- Weakness: Practicality concern for k-ary queries—may be unintuitive for workers; UI decomposition or discussion needed.  
- Suggestions: fix notation (“nd2”), include a self-contained proof sketch/corollary for Theorem 3, add synthetic k>2 experiments, and compare against stronger XOR/noise baselines.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept (weak accept) — interesting and potentially influential but requires clarity fixes, stronger empirical validation, and broader literature comparisons.
