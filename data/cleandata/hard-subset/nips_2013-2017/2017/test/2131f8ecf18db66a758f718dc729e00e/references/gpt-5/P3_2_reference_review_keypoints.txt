- Problem framing: Studies nonadaptive, query-efficient labeling/clustering using Δ-local queries, cast as “locally encodable source coding.”
- Approach: Derives information-theoretic bounds and proposes concrete nonadaptive querying/decoding schemes.
- Contribution (noiseless exact, k=2): Δ-ary XOR (“same-cluster”) queries with random sparse linear encoding + ML decoding achieve exact recovery with m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) queries and a new locality-aware converse improving on naive bounds.
- Contribution (noiseless approximate, k=2): Shows XOR queries are inherently suboptimal for approximate recovery (via Massey’s argument) and proposes AND queries with a two-hop decoder that improve query–distortion trade-offs.
- Contribution (noisy approximate, general k): Introduces a pairwise k-ary AND-style query with symmetric noise and proves O(n log(k/δ)) nonadaptive queries suffice on random d-regular graphs; small crowdsourcing study supports AND over same-cluster.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty—“locally encodable source coding” provides a fresh lens linking query design to information limits under locality.
- Strength: Provable achievability—AND-based schemes are nonadaptive, linear-time, noise-robust, and admit clean Chernoff-style analyses.
- Strength: Practical insight—AND queries can outperform XOR/same-cluster for approximate recovery, relevant for crowdsourcing.
- Strength: Provides some improved locality-aware lower bounds extending classic source coding converses.
- Major weakness: Inconsistent query-count notation—paper repeatedly uses nd^2 despite experiments and graphs implying nd/2 edges; must be fixed throughout.
- Weakness: Proof completeness—Theorem 1’s probability approximations and hypergeometric-to-bound steps are unjustified and results are existential (ML decoding is NP-hard).
- Weakness: Theorem 2 converse is nontransparent, possibly contains typos, and lacks clear discussion or units.
- Weakness: Theorem 3 gives a complicated approximate-recovery converse without a self-contained proof or clear intuition for Δ dependence.
- Weakness: Concentration in Theorem 5 misbounds dependency degree (uses O(d) instead of true O(d^2) two-hop neighborhood), affecting tail bounds and citations.
- Weakness: XOR lower-bound claim depends on a conjecture from Massey’s linear rate–distortion limits; lacks firm lower bounds for XOR-only schemes.
- Weakness: Noise model assumptions are simplistic (uniform confusion among wrong labels) and not validated by the small crowdsourcing experiment; robustness not analyzed.
- Weakness: Asymmetric k-ary query design (label 0 special) and reliance on priors/class-size knowledge raise sensitivity and imbalance concerns.
- Weakness: Main noisy result uses k-ary outputs despite motivation for binary answers; binary-only alternatives for k>2 are not discussed.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—typos, formatting, unclear figures, collisions in Δ notation, and deferred proofs hinder readability.
- Relation to prior work: Correctly connects to locally decodable/smooth compression but should better acknowledge and contrast group testing, sparse pooling, and one-bit sensing literatures.
- Relation to crowdsourcing work: Relevant motivation, but should position nonadaptive constant-arity querying vs adaptive strategies and clarify regimes of advantage.
- Pros for acceptance: Novel perspective, analyzable algorithms with good scaling, and practical insight favoring AND queries.
- Cons for acceptance: Technical and presentation flaws, a key comparative claim resting on a conjecture, and limited/weak experimental validation.
- Recommendation: Reviewer leans toward rejection in current form due to inconsistencies and missing rigor, but views the ideas as promising.
- Suggested fixes: Correct nd/2 vs nd^2 inconsistencies; provide clearer/sketched proofs for Theorems 1–3; fix dependency analysis in Theorem 5; clarify exact achievable m for Δ=2.
- Suggested fixes (cont.): Either prove nontrivial XOR-only lower bounds or tone claims; analyze robustness to prior misspecification and nonuniform noise; present binary-only designs for k>2.
- Suggested fixes (cont.): Improve experiments (larger scale, error bars, more baselines), clean up presentation, and provide clearer intuition in main text.
