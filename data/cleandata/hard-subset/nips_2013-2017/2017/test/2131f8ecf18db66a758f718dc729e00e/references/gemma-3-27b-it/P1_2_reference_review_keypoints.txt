- Problem & approach: Frames clustering from pairwise oracle queries as a locally encodable source coding problem and derives information-theoretic lower bounds for exact and approximate label recovery in noisy and noiseless settings.

- Main contribution: Shows AND queries can outperform XOR/"same-cluster" queries for approximate recovery and proposes an AND-query-based algorithm with theoretical guarantees.

- Literature connection: Builds on adaptive querying, crowdsourcing, entity resolution, and links to locally decodable codes and smooth compression, offering a novel coding perspective.

- Strength — novel framing: The locally encodable source coding viewpoint enables new applications of information-theoretic tools.

- Strength — theoretical rigor: Provides rigorous lower bounds on query complexity and strong performance guarantees for proposed algorithms.

- Strength — AND-query insight: Demonstrates a non-trivial result that AND queries can be superior to traditional queries for approximate recovery.

- Strength — noise handling: Addresses realistic noisy oracle responses and designs a motivated algorithm for the noisy case.

- Strength — experiments: Includes a small crowdsourcing experiment that provides empirical support for the theoretical claims.

- Strength — presentation: Paper is generally well-written and organized.

- Weakness — experiment scale: Crowdsourcing validation is limited in scale and dataset diversity.

- Weakness — implementation complexity: Practical computational complexity, particularly for the noisy algorithm, is not sufficiently discussed.

- Weakness — prior knowledge assumption: Algorithm requires knowledge of the label prior p; sensitivity to estimation errors is not thoroughly explored.

- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks detailed empirical comparison to established clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering), especially under noise.

- Weakness — theorem exposition: Proof sketch for Theorem 5 is dense and needs a clearer, more intuitive explanation.

- Quality: Technically sound with correct, rigorous (though sometimes dense) proofs.

- Clarity: Generally clear but some sections need more intuition and exposition.

- Originality: Novel problem framing and the AND-query result constitute original contributions.

- Significance: Results offer potentially impactful theoretical limits and practical implications for cost-sensitive querying/crowdsourcing.

- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach; AND-query insight; fundamental bounds; some experimental support.

- Against acceptance: Small-scale experiments; potential practical complexity; reliance on prior knowledge.

- Overall assessment: Strong contribution worth acceptance with minor revisions.

- Recommendation & requested revisions: Accept with minor revisions — expand discussion of computational complexity, analyze sensitivity to prior estimation, provide an intuitive proof of Theorem 5, and add more detailed comparisons to existing clustering methods.
