- Frames clustering as a novel "locally encodable source coding" problem and introduces AND queries as a superior alternative to XOR ("same cluster") queries for approximate recovery.  
- Bridges information theory, clustering, and crowdsourcing, building on pairwise-query literature while making a conceptual leap.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with new information-theoretic lower bounds and proofs showing AND queries reduce query complexity in key parameter regimes.  
- Noisy-case analysis is rigorous, linking number of queries to error rate.  
- Practical validation via a crowdsourcing experiment (100 action vs 100 romantic movies) showing AND queries produce far fewer errors than same-cluster queries.  
- Clear problem framing and useful distinction between locally encodable and locally decodable source coding.  
- Comprehensive treatment across scenarios: noiseless exact, noiseless approximate, and noisy approximate recovery, with thorough AND vs XOR comparisons.  
- Exact recovery results are limited: meaningful improvements only for a narrow prior probability range (p ≈ 0.3–0.5).  
- Claim that AND queries are easier for crowd workers is under-supported and lacks behavioral/cognitive evidence or a pilot study.  
- Extension to k > 2 is algorithmic but lacks the same theoretical depth as the k = 2 case, limiting practical generality.  
- Novelty of "locally encodable source coding" is somewhat overstated given prior related discussion in [23]; insufficient differentiation from prior work.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-validated contribution with a strong core insight that warrants acceptance.  
- Recommendation: accept; paper meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
