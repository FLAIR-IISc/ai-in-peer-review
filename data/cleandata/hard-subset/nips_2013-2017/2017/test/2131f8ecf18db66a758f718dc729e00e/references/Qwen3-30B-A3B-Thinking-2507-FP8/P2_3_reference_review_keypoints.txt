- Strong technical quality with rigorous, tight lower and matching upper bounds across several clustering scenarios.
- For noiseless exact recovery (k=2), proves a lower bound better than nH(p) for certain p (Theorem 2) and provides a matching querying scheme (Theorem 1).
- For noiseless approximate recovery, shows "same cluster" (XOR) queries are fundamentally limited (Theorem 3) and that AND queries outperform them with explicit error-rate analysis (Theorem 4).
- For noisy queries with k≥2, presents Algorithm 1 with theoretical guarantees (Theorem 5) and validates it on real crowdsourced data.
- Theoretical claims are supported by detailed proofs and supplementary material; experiments on 200 movies corroborate the theory.
- Empirical result: AND queries produced 105 errors out of 1000 queries versus 234 errors for "same cluster" queries, demonstrating practical advantage.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written; problem setup, notation, and progression from noiseless to noisy cases are clear.
- Figures and supplementary proofs effectively illustrate and support results; minor omission: more detail on practical implementation of AND queries in crowdsourcing.
- Highly original framing of clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem and introduction of AND queries as a conceptual advance.
- Connects clustering query problems to information-theoretic limits, extending literature on locally encodable/decodable codes and smooth compression.
- Significant practical impact: shows potential reduction of queries from O(n log n) to O(n log(1/δ)) for (1−δ)-approximate recovery, lowering crowdsourcing cost.
- Limitations: primarily focused on k=2 clusters, requires prior knowledge of cluster sizes, and assumes independent query answers.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, practically relevant work that merits acceptance.
