- Introduces “locally encodable source coding”: non-adaptive, Δ-local queries for crowdsourced clustering/labeling.  
- Shows lower bounds and algorithms for noiseless and noisy settings, arguing XOR (same-cluster) queries can be suboptimal for approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND-type queries that improve query/accuracy trade-offs and simple local decoding schemes.  
- Noiseless exact recovery (k=2): achievability with Δ-ary XOR and ML decoding; converse improves on naive n h(p) in some regimes.  
- Noiseless approximate recovery (k=2): new Δ-limited lower bound and argument that XOR queries are intrinsically limited for approximation.  
- Provides an AND-query scheme with tractable error expression that outperforms XOR for certain parameters (provably at p=0.5).  
- Noisy approximate recovery (general k): proposes pairwise k-ary AND-like queries and a thresholding decoder achieving m = O(n log(k/δ)) queries on d-regular graphs.  
- Small experiment (200 movies) suggests AND queries are easier for workers and yield lower empirical error than same-cluster queries.  
- Connects to prior work on crowd-query clustering and locally decodable/smooth compression, and uses Massey’s limits to argue against XOR.  
- Claims AND reduces queries from O(n log n) to O(n log(1/δ)) for (1−δ)-approximate recovery compared to some prior noisy results.  
- Theoretical methods are sound; Theorem 1 uses standard random-ensemble expurgation but requires intractable ML decoding.  
- Theorem 2 converse leverages degree/locality constraints; Theorem 3 (approximate lower bound) is nontrivial and deferred to appendix.  
- Theorem 4 (AND achievability) is clean with explicit Poisson-model error expressions; Theorem 5 uses Chernoff bounds for noisy analysis.  
- Critical notation error: queries repeatedly written as nd^2 instead of nd/2 for d-regular graphs; this affects statements, proofs, and figures (must be fixed).  
- Some AND-vs-XOR superiority claims rely on a conjectured XOR lower bound (only proven at p=0.5); claims should be tempered and separated.  
- Computational limitation: exact-recovery ML decoding is intractable and should be acknowledged more explicitly.  
- Noise model is symmetric and specific; need clarification on extension to asymmetric noise.  
- Significant clarity and notation issues (log bases, qk−1 vs q/(k−1), ambiguous formulas, missing parentheses) that hinder readability.  
- Deferred proofs (Theorems 3 and 6) need summaries of core ideas in the main text.  
- Recommend more extensive experiments and comparisons to baselines (repeated same-cluster + majority/EM, spectral methods, entity-resolution heuristics) with confidence intervals.  
- Originality: framing as locally encodable source coding and highlighting AND-query advantages are novel and timely.  
- Significance: advances understanding of query/accuracy trade-offs under locality/non-adaptivity and provides practical AND-based primitives.  
- Pros: clear conceptual contribution, novel AND primitive, improved noisy recovery complexity, some user validation.  
- Cons: serious notation errors, reliance on conjecture for some claims, intractable exact-recovery scheme, limited experimental evaluation.  
- Requests: fix nd^2→nd/2 everywhere, state Theorem 5 with explicit constants/conditions, separate proven vs conjectured claims, discuss complexity, and expand experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: lean positive conditional on careful revision fixing exposition, notation, conjecture delineation, and strengthened empirical comparisons.
