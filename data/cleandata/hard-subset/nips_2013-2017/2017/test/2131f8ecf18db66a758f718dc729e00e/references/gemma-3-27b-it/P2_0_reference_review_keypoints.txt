- Frames crowdsourced pairwise-query clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem, deriving information-theoretic lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to "same cluster" (XOR) queries and shows they can outperform XOR for approximate recovery.  
- Provides rigorous information-theoretic lower bounds, with a notable lower bound for approximate recovery.  
- Develops schemes that work with noisy oracle responses and shows AND queries remain effective under noise.  
- Validates theoretical findings with Amazon Mechanical Turk experiments demonstrating practical effectiveness of AND queries.  
- Generally well-written and logically structured with clear explanation of problem setup and contributions.  
- Novel connection to information theory opens new avenues beyond typical algorithmic crowdsourcing approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is relatively small-scale and could be strengthened with larger, more diverse datasets and scenarios.  
- Theoretical derivations are complex and may be hard for a general audience to follow without additional simplification or supplements.  
- Introduction repeats the motivation for non-adaptive, low-cost queries multiple times, causing redundancy.  
- Lacks comparison to stronger or more sophisticated clustering baselines, especially methods for noisy clustering outside crowdsourcing.  
- Quality: technically sound with complete analysis, though experiments could be expanded to bolster claims.  
- Clarity: mostly clear but theoretical sections need simplification; notation is generally consistent.  
- Originality: novel framing and exploration of AND queries, though relation to crowdsourcing literature could be deeper.  
- Significance: insights and lower bounds are meaningful for crowdsourced clustering and entity resolution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to simplify theoretical presentation and expand comparisons to related work and stronger baselines.
