- Introduces AND-queries that outperform traditional "same-cluster" (XOR) queries for approximate recovery.  
- Frames clustering with queries as a locally encodable source coding problem and derives information-theoretic bounds and algorithms.  
- Main contributions: noiseless exact recovery with pairwise queries, noiseless approximate recovery with AND-queries, and noisy approximate recovery with AND-queries.  
- Proves new nontrivial lower bounds for locally encodable source coding and establishes theoretical advantages of AND-queries.  
- Theorems 1–5 are technically sound; key result shows AND-queries recover (1−δ) labels with O(n log(k/δ)) queries, improving over prior O(n log n).  
- Real crowdsourcing experiment on movie genres supports theory: AND-queries produced fewer errors (105 vs. 234) than same-cluster queries.  
- Paper is well-organized and figures effectively illustrate results (e.g., Figure 2).  
- Theoretical results focus mostly on k=2 despite claims for general k; more intuition/results for k>2 are needed.  
- Several key proofs and details are deferred to the supplementary material, making the main text harder to follow.  
- Crowdsourcing experiment is small-scale (200 movies, 10 volunteers), limiting generalizability of empirical claims.  
- Assumes known prior distribution p (cluster sizes) without addressing how to handle unknown priors in practice.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical contribution with practical implications; limitations are minor relative to the strengths.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical advance with practical validation.  
- Suggested improvements: expand main-text discussion of k>2, analyze unknown priors, run larger/diverse experiments, and explicitly compare to [27] on majority-voting errors.
