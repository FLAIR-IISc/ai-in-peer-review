- Novel theoretical framework: connects clustering with locally encodable source coding, a previously unexplored perspective with proper citations.  
- Strong theoretical results: rigorous lower/upper bounds showing AND queries outperform same-cluster (XOR) queries for approximate recovery; realistic noise model.  
- Compelling experimental validation: real crowdsourcing on movies shows AND queries produce far fewer errors (105 vs. 234) and aligns with theory.  
- Practical impact: applicable to crowdsourced entity resolution, reduces query costs, and focuses on non-adaptive queries relevant for real-time systems.  
- Limited comparison to prior work: paper does not explicitly compare AND query bounds (O(n log(1/δ))) to best-known results (e.g., O(n log n)) in similar settings.  
- Missing computational complexity analysis: lacks discussion of decoding and algorithmic efficiency (e.g., Algorithm 1) for large n.  
- Weak justification of cognitive advantage: no clear psychological explanation why AND queries are easier for workers than same-cluster queries.  
- Limited discussion of failure modes: insufficient exploration of performance under highly skewed priors and sensitivity to prior estimation errors.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical contribution with strong empirical support and significant practical relevance.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Suggestion 1: include direct comparisons of query complexity bounds with existing methods.  
- Suggestion 2: add a brief computational complexity analysis for proposed algorithms.  
- Suggestion 3: provide a clearer cognitive/psychological rationale for AND query ease.  
- Suggestion 4: expand discussion on practical limitations and sensitivity to prior estimation.
