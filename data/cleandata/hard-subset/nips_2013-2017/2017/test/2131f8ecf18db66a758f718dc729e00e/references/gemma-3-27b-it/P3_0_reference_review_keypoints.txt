- Paper studies semisupervised clustering via oracle queries, aiming to minimize queries for accurate label recovery.
- Novel framing: casts querying problem as a locally encodable source coding problem, linking clustering to information theory.
- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds and provides algorithms with provable performance guarantees.
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to XOR/same-cluster queries and shows they can outperform XOR for approximate recovery.
- Extends analysis to k clusters and develops algorithms for noisy oracle settings with theoretical guarantees.
- Connects to prior work in crowdsourcing for entity resolution, source coding, and locally decodable codes, and builds on query-complexity results.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and derivation of lower bounds and algorithmic analyses.
- Strength: practical insight that AND queries improve performance, especially under noise.
- Strength: includes real-world experimental validation on Amazon Mechanical Turk supporting theoretical claims.
- Strength: clear structure and generally well-written presentation despite technical depth.
- Weakness: dense notation and proofs that may be hard to follow for non–information-theory readers; needs more intuitive explanations.
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical scalability and computational cost for very large datasets (n >> 1000).
- Weakness: sensitivity to algorithm parameters (e.g., degree d, thresholds) not thoroughly explored or guided.
- Weakness: crowdsourcing experiment is small-scale (100 movies, 10 workers) and needs broader evaluation.
- Minor issues: some repetition across sections and repeated use of the term "locally encodable source coding" without frequent definition.
- Recommendation: overall strong paper, recommend accept with minor revisions to clarify notation, scalability, and expand experiments.
