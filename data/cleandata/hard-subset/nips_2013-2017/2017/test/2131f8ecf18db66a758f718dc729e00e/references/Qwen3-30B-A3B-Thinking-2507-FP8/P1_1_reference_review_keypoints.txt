- Novel perspective: frames clustering and label learning as locally encodable source coding and proposes AND queries that reduce query complexity.  
- Key theoretical contributions: new lower bounds for exact recovery (k=2), proof that XOR queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery, and an algorithmic result for noisy approximate recovery with k clusters.  
- Empirical validation: crowdsourcing experiment (200 movies, 10 volunteers) shows AND queries yield much lower error (10.5% vs 23.4%).  
- Clear presentation: well-structured paper with effective figures (e.g., Figure 2) illustrating AND vs XOR performance.  
- Weakness — limited k>2 development: theoretical focus is mainly on k=2 and the generalization to k>2 is under-explained.  
- Weakness — computational complexity missing: no discussion of decoding/runtime costs beyond query complexity.  
- Weakness — small-scale experiment: dataset and participant pool are small, leaving scalability questions.  
- Weakness — relation to recent work: needs a more detailed comparison with very recent clustering-with-queries papers (e.g., [21], [22]).  
- Quality assessment: technically rigorous and well-supported theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity assessment: clearly written but could use more intuitive explanation of locally encodable source coding for non-specialists.  
- Originality assessment: genuinely novel connection and new results; AND queries are a meaningful departure from XOR approaches.  
- Significance assessment: practical impact for reducing crowdsourcing costs and opens new research directions in source-coding perspectives.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with supporting empirical evidence; minor limitations do not outweigh the contribution.  
- Suggested improvements: expand k≥2 discussion, add computational complexity analysis, compare in detail with recent related work (e.g., [22]), and scale up crowdsourcing experiments.
