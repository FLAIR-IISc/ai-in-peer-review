- Studies non-adaptive pairwise querying for clustering, framing it as a locally encodable source coding problem and deriving information-theoretic lower bounds.
- Shows AND queries can outperform traditional same-cluster/XOR queries for approximate recovery and provides a practical algorithm leveraging this.
- Addresses noisy oracle responses and validates theoretical findings with a small crowdsourcing experiment.
- Builds on clustering-with-queries and crowdsourcing literature, connecting to source coding and locally encodable codes while distinguishing from locally decodable codes.
- Novel framing (locally encodable source coding) that enables application of information-theoretic tools.
- Strong theoretical results with lower bounds and performance guarantees; proofs appear sound though dense.
- Counterintuitive and significant insight that AND queries can be superior for approximate recovery.
- Practical validation through a real-world crowdsourcing experiment supports the approach, albeit preliminarily.
- Inclusion of noisy-query analysis increases practical relevance.
- Clear situating within existing literature with comprehensive citations.
- Proofs are dense and difficult to follow; a more detailed walkthrough is needed for full verification.
- The independent-error assumption for queries may not hold in practice and is not deeply explored.
- Crowdsourcing experiment is small-scale and may not generalize; dataset choice (movies) is limited.
- Algorithm 1 may be computationally costly for large datasets; scalability discussion is lacking.
- Paper mainly focuses on k=2; extensions and experiments for larger k are limited.
- Overall judged technically sound and original, with strong potential impact (quality/originality/significance scores high).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach; significant AND query insight; valuable information-theoretic perspective; encouraging empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: complex proofs hard to verify, scalability concerns, and limited experimental evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—improve proof clarity, address scalability, expand experiments, and clarify decoding in Algorithm 1.
