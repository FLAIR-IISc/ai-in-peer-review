

# Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

## Summary
This paper studies the problem of clustering elements using pairwise queries to an oracle, with a focus on non-adaptive querying schemes. The authors propose viewing the problem through the lens of locally encodable source coding, introducing AND queries as a more efficient alternative to traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries for approximate recovery. The paper provides theoretical results on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery, noiseless and noisy settings, and validates their approach with a crowdsourcing experiment. The work connects clustering with information theory, particularly source coding, and makes contributions to the theoretical understanding of locally encodable codes.

## Strengths
The paper identifies an interesting connection between clustering and information theory that hasn't been thoroughly explored. The AND query approach for approximate recovery is novel and theoretically sound. The experimental validation with real crowdsourcing data provides some practical support for the theoretical claims. The paper makes a clear distinction between exact and approximate recovery, which is important for practical applications.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Presentation Issues
The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility. The mathematical notation is frequently inconsistent and poorly formatted. For example:
- "α = 12 (1 + (1 − 4p(1 − p)) ∆)" should be "α = 1/2 (1 + (1 − 4p(1 − p))^Δ)"
- "log2 1 α" should be "log₂(1/α)"
- "∀i ∈ {1, . . . , n}" should have a space before "∀" as "∀ i ∈ {1, . . . , n}"
- "∑k i=0" should be "∑_{i=0}^k" or "∑_{i=0}^k"

These are not merely typographical errors but fundamental issues that make the paper difficult to read and understand, which is particularly problematic for a theoretical paper.

### Technical Flaws

1. **Incorrect claim about query complexity**: The paper states "the number of sufficient queries guaranteed by the above theorem is strictly less than n for all 0.0694 ≤ p < 0.5 even for ∆ = 2." However, for p = 0.5, h(p) = 1, α = 1/2, and log₂(1/α) = 1, so m = n(1+o(1)), which is not strictly less than n. This fundamental error undermines the paper's main claim.

2. **Misattribution of related work**: The paper states "the only place where it is mentioned to the best of our knowledge is in [23]" regarding locally encodable source coding. However, [23] is about "smooth compression," not locally encodable source coding. This misattribution suggests a lack of familiarity with the field.

3. **Confusing mathematical expressions**: Theorem 3 contains an ambiguous expression: "δ ≥ δ̃ (m n ) + h(p)− h(δ̃(mn )) h′(δ̃(mn ))(1 + e ∆h′(δ̃(mn )))". It's unclear whether this is [h(p) - h(δ̃(m/n))] / [h'(δ̃(m/n)) * (1 + e^(Δh'(δ̃(m/n))))] or something else. This ambiguity renders the theorem difficult to interpret.

4. **Inconsistent query definitions**: For k=2, the paper defines AND queries as Q(X,X') = X ∧ X', but in Section 3.3, the query for general k is defined as Q(X,X') = i if X=X'=i, 0 otherwise. The paper claims these are equivalent for k=2, but they're not exactly the same. The Section 3.3 query returns 1 for X=X'=1 and 0 otherwise, which is equivalent to the binary AND query, but the paper's presentation is confusing.

5. **Experimental flaws**: The experiment claims to have "105 erroneous answers" but doesn't explain how they determined these were erroneous (i.e., what the ground truth was). Without ground truth verification, the error rate claim is meaningless. The paper also states they instructed participants "not to check any resources" but doesn't verify this.

### Other Issues
- The paper claims "the reverse is not true" in the introduction but doesn't clarify what "the reverse" refers to.
- The paper uses "δ" for both the fraction of incorrect labels and as a variable in probability expressions, causing confusion.
- In the lower bound proof for Theorem 2, the derivation of the threshold p < 0.03475 is not explained, making it difficult to verify.
- The paper cites [21] for O(n log n) queries for two clusters, but [21] is actually about "Clustering with noisy queries" which may not make that claim.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper presents an interesting theoretical connection between clustering and information theory, and the AND query approach is potentially valuable, the numerous technical and presentation flaws are substantial. The paper contains fundamental errors in mathematical claims, misattributes key references, and has inconsistent notation throughout. The experimental validation is also significantly weakened by the lack of ground truth verification.

The paper's theoretical contributions are limited by these issues, and the confusion in presentation makes it difficult to assess the validity of the claims. As a NIPS reviewer, I must be meticulous about the technical accuracy of papers, and the paper fails to meet this standard in multiple places.

## Recommendation
Reject. The paper contains too many fundamental technical and presentation errors that undermine its core claims. The authors would need to thoroughly revise the mathematical presentation, correct the misattributed references, and properly verify the experimental results before this work could be considered for publication at NIPS.
