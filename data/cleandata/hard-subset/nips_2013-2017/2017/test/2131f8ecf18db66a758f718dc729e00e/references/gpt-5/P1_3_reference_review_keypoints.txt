- Problem: Non-adaptive, budgeted crowdsourced clustering framed as locally encodable source coding with queries over ≤Δ labels and m answers to reconstruct X.

- Noiseless exact (k=2): Existence result using Δ-sparse parity/XOR queries gives m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) and a stronger locality-based converse improving over n h(p) in some regimes.

- Noiseless approximate (k=2): Locality-aware lower bound on distortion and proposal of AND-based queries with a simple decoder and explicit error; empirically better m–δ trade-off than XOR.

- Noisy approximate (general k): k-ary AND-like pairwise queries plus thresholding decoder on d-regular graphs achieve (1−δ)-accuracy with m = Θ(n log(k/δ)) using d = Θ(log(k/δ)) under symmetric noise.

- Empirics: Small human study (200 movies, d=10, 1000 queries) and synthetic tests support AND-queries being easier for workers, but evaluations are limited and lack strong baselines.

- Prior work relation: Novel tie between crowdsourced clustering and locally encodable source coding, leveraging prior crowdsourcing/query-complexity and classic information-theory results (e.g., Massey).

- Theory quality: Parity achievability (existential/ML) and locality converse are sound; Theorem 3 is potentially novel but opaque; AND-analysis uses standard Poissonization/balls-in-bins.

- Noisy-theory quality: Theorem 5 is technically straightforward (Chernoff + dependency concentration) and yields an attractive O(n log(k/δ)) query complexity.

- Empirical quality: User study consistent with model but small-scale; missing larger-scale experiments and stronger baseline comparisons.

- Caveat: Exact-recovery result is non-constructive—ML decoding is computationally intractable and no efficient decoder is provided.

- Caveat: Notational/formula inconsistencies (notably m reported as n d^2 vs correct n d/2) occur and must be fixed.

- Caveat: Some theorem expressions lack clarity, valid argument ranges, and intuitive explanations (e.g., Theorems 2 and 3).

- Caveat: XOR suboptimality claim partially rests on a conjecture from linear rate–distortion limits; a rigorous locality-constrained XOR lower bound is missing.

- Caveat: k-ary query model assumes workers can reliably choose among k categorical outputs; practical guidance for k>2 is limited.

- Clarity: Overall well-motivated but marred by typos, inconsistent notation, heavy deferred proofs, and insufficiently described figures/axes.

- Originality: Framing as locally encodable coding, sublinear exact-recovery for parity queries, and the AND-query noisy scheme are novel and interesting.

- Significance: Advances theoretical bounds for query-limited clustering and provides a simple, practical algorithmic approach for noisy non-adaptive crowdsourcing.

- Pros: Timely non-adaptive focus, new information-theoretic perspective, simple scalable algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees, and user evidence favoring AND queries.

- Cons: Non-constructive exact-recovery, notation/formula errors, reliance on a conjecture for XOR comparison, and limited experimental validation.

- Requests: Correct and unify query count in Section 3.3/Algorithm 1 to m = n d/2.

- Requests: Provide a proof sketch or clearer intuition for Theorem 3 and ensure function arguments lie in valid ranges.

- Requests: State an unconditional lower bound for XOR-only queries under locality in some regimes to remove conjecture dependence.

- Requests: Describe computational properties or efficient relaxations of the ML decoder in Theorem 1.

- Requests: Provide practical implementation guidance for k>2 queries or consider using k separate binary AND questions.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending fixes to notation/clarity, stronger XOR comparison, and expanded empirical validation.
