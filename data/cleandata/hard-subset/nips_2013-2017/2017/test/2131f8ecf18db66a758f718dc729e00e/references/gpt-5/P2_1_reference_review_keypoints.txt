- Paper studies non-adaptive, budgeted crowdsourcing for clustering into k classes using Δ-local queries, framing it as “locally encodable source coding.”  
- Main contributions: noiseless exact recovery (k=2) via XOR/parity queries with ML decoding and improved converse; noiseless approximate recovery with AND queries and Δ-local lower bounds; noisy approximate recovery (k≥2) via k-ary AND on d-regular graphs achieving m = O(n log(k/δ)).  
- Novelty: locally encodable viewpoint and the AND vs XOR insight appear new and practically relevant.  
- Theoretical results are largely sound with clear intuition: locality worsens distortion; AND queries enable simple threshold decoders; sparse regular graphs give O(n log(k/δ)) scaling under noise.  
- Major inconsistency: Sec. 3.3 and Algorithm 1 state the total queries incorrectly as “nd^2/nd^2” instead of m = nd/2; fix throughout.  
- Proof slip: Chernoff tail in Theorem 5 is written as e^{-2 d^2}; correct exponent is e^{-Θ(d)} (linear in d).  
- Expurgation in Theorem 1 is under-explained; need to show fraction of removed typical sequences is o(1) so ML succeeds w.h.p.  
- Theorem 3 (Δ-local rate-distortion lower bound) is deferred; a sketch or key lemma is needed in the main text due to centrality.  
- XOR lower bound for approximate recovery is conjectural except at p=0.5; clarify which empirical comparisons rely on conjecture vs provable bounds.  
- Experiments: small real-user study (10 volunteers, 1000 pair queries) suggests AND easier than same-cluster but is underpowered and susceptible to confounds (no counterbalancing); report CIs and inter-worker variability.  
- Practical crowdsourcing methods (repeated queries, Dawid-Skene, minimax entropy) are not compared; discuss how the single-error-rate aggregation model relates to common aggregation methods.  
- Clarity issues: numerous typos/notation inconsistencies, footnote placement problems; define α cleanly in Theorem 1.  
- Some proofs (Theorems 1,2,4,5) are sketched with appendix references; ensure constants and dependence on Δ, p, q are correct in final version.  
- Originality: Δ-local rate-distortion bound and AND-query strategy are novel contributions bridging coding theory and crowdsourcing.  
- Significance: advances understanding of locality constraints on information-theoretic limits and offers a practical, simple query scheme with sample-efficiency benefits.  
- Pros: clear theoretical advances, novel AND-query strategy with provable advantages, simple non-adaptive scalable algorithm, useful link to information theory.  
- Cons: notational/proof slips (m=nd/2 inconsistency, Chernoff exponent, expurgation), limited empirical validation, some key results deferred.  
- Questions for authors: fix total query count and Chernoff exponent and state hidden constants in O(n log(k/δ)); provide a short proof sketch for Theorem 3; discuss Algorithm 1 sensitivity to mis-specified priors and robustness of thresholding; consider AND queries for Δ>2.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept (weak accept) conditional on fixing clarity/typos and tightening proofs; medium reviewer confidence.
