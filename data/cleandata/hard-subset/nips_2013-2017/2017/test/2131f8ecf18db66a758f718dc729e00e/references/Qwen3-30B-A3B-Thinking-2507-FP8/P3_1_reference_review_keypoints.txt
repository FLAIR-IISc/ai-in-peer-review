- Reframes clustering/label learning as a locally encodable source coding problem connecting clustering to information theory.  
- Key insight: AND queries outperform XOR ("same cluster") queries for approximate recovery, achieving better query complexity.  
- First nontrivial information-theoretic bounds for locally encodable source coding with elegant proofs (Theorems 1 & 2).  
- Empirical evidence: crowdsourcing experiment shows substantially fewer errors for AND vs same-cluster queries (105 vs 234).  
- Strong technical rigor, including noisy query analysis with Theorem 5 giving O(n log(k/δ)) query complexity.  
- Paper is well-written with clear definitions and logical flow.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete—misses recent non-adaptive query-based clustering papers (e.g., [22, 28]).  
- Practical implementation challenges for AND queries are underexplored; experiment shows lower errors but not why.  
- Claims that AND queries are "better" are somewhat overstated; advantage may not hold for skewed priors p.  
- k>2 theoretical results assume oracle returns k-ary answers, which departs from typical binary crowdsourcing and needs justification.  
- The work is significant and original, likely of interest to information theory and crowdsourcing communities.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—technically sound, original, and practically relevant.  
- Specific: Conjecture 1 is proven only for p=0.5; need clarity on general p.  
- Specific: Experiment should analyze cognitive reasons for lower AND error rates (e.g., task intuitiveness).  
- Specific: Paper should explicitly state conditions under which AND outperforms XOR beyond the provided bounds.  
- Specific: Connection to smooth compression [23] is mentioned but not fully developed; could strengthen theoretical ties.
