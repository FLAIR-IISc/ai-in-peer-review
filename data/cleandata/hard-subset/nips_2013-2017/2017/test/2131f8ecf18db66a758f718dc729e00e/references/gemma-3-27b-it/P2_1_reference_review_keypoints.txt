- Novel connection: frames querying for semi-supervised clustering as locally encodable source coding, bringing information-theoretic tools to the problem.  
- Provides rigorous information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND-queries as an alternative to XOR/same-cluster queries and argues they perform better, especially under noise.  
- Analysis convincingly explains why AND-queries reduce information needed for negative answers.  
- Extends analysis and algorithms to handle noisy oracle responses, addressing practical concerns.  
- Small-scale Amazon Mechanical Turk experiment supports theoretical claims but is limited in scope.  
- Writing and motivation are mostly clear, with a well-presented introduction and core ideas.  
- Experimental validation is limited (100 movies × 2 clusters); broader datasets and parameter sweeps are needed.  
- Proofs are dense and hard to follow, relying heavily on appendices; more intermediate steps/explanations would help.  
- Assumes known prior distribution p and independent query errors, which may be unrealistic in many real-world settings.  
- Related work discussion is scattered; stronger connections to crowdsourcing and active learning literature are needed.  
- Some notation is heavy or unnecessary (e.g., distortion function), which clutters presentation.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical framework, strong bounds, practical AND-query proposal, addresses relevant crowdsourcing problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, dense proofs, reliance on potentially unrealistic assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: strong and novel paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify proofs.
