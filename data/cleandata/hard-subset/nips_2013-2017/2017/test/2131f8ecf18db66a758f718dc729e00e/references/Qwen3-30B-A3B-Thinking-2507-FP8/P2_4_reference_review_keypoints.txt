- Addresses query complexity in clustering by modeling it as a locally encodable source coding problem and introduces AND queries for label recovery.
- Shows AND queries outperform traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries for approximate recovery, requiring far fewer queries.
- Provides rigorous, well-structured theoretical analysis with tight information-theoretic lower bounds and matching upper bounds for various query types.
- Distinguishes clearly between exact and approximate recovery and defines notions like “(1-δ)-good” querying schemes.
- Analyzes both noiseless and noisy settings, proving AND queries need O(n log(k/δ)) queries for (1-δ)-good recovery compared to O(n log n) for previous methods.
- Validates theory with a real crowdsourcing experiment (200 movies) where AND queries produced substantially fewer errors (105 vs. 234).
- Novel contribution: framing query-based clustering as locally encodable source coding linking information theory and crowdsourcing.
- Novel contribution: introduces and theoretically analyzes AND queries, and shows fundamental limits of XOR queries for approximate recovery.
- Well-written, organized, and self-contained paper with clear notation and helpful figures illustrating results.
- Thorough literature grounding and clear separation of contributions from prior work in clustering and information theory.
- Results are practically significant for crowdsourcing and query-costly applications and likely to interest multiple research communities.
- Strengths include strong theory, practical validation, and clear superiority demonstration of AND queries.
- Weaknesses: assumes known prior distribution p, focuses mainly on k=2 with k>2 secondary, some proofs (e.g., Theorem 3) are complex, and experiment size is modest.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical and practical contribution; recommend acceptance for publication.
