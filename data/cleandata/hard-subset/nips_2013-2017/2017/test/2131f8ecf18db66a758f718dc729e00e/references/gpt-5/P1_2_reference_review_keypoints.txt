- Summary: Models non-adaptive pairwise query answers as a locally encodable source code to derive new lower bounds and near-matching achievability for label recovery.
- Exact recovery (noiseless, k=2): Shows parity/XOR queries on Δ-tuples suffice with m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α) and gives a converse improving on n h(p) under locality constraints.
- Approximate recovery (noiseless, k=2): Derives a locality-aware rate-distortion lower bound and argues XOR is suboptimal for approximation; proposes AND queries with an explicit decoder that outperforms XOR in several regimes.
- Approximate recovery (noisy, k≥2): Introduces k-ary AND-per-class queries and a simple thresholding decoder on d-regular graphs achieving (1−δ) recovery with m = O(n log(k/δ)), with concentration guarantees.
- Experiments: Small crowdsourcing test suggests AND queries yield lower human error than same-cluster queries and match theoretical trends with more queries.
- Relation to prior work: Connects query-efficient clustering to locally encodable/source-channel coding and links AND-vs-XOR observations to classical linear rate-distortion limits.
- Theoretical quality: Provides substantial bounds and technically sound achievability proofs using standard tools; locality-aware converses are interesting and nontrivial.
- Needed tightening: Theorem 1’s expurgation argument is confusing and should be rephrased as existence of Q with E[#collisions]<1 or bounding ambiguous typical mass.
- Needed tightening: Theorem 2 locality converse is terse; expand the Hamming-ball image bound and degree counting steps.
- Caveat: Lower bound against XOR for approximate recovery depends on a conjecture except at p=0.5; comparisons should be labeled empirical/heuristic where conjectural.
- Notation issues: Fix typographical confusions (e.g., “nd2” should be nd/2) and clarify the threshold Ci expression and constants.
- Experimental limitations: Real-data test is small-scale; comparisons use spectral clustering which may not be optimal for noisy parity data and lack k>2 validation.
- Clarity: Generally readable but needs precise variable definitions, explicit assumptions (e.g., independent query noise), and error-criterion specification when using typical sets.
- Originality: Novel locally encodable source coding viewpoint and the AND-query insight for beating XOR on approximate recovery.
- Practical contribution: AND-query algorithms offer fewer queries for approximate recovery and likely lower annotator error.
- Significance: Advances fundamental limits under locality constraints and is relevant to crowdsourcing and simple-query settings.
- Pros: Fresh information-theoretic framing, simple effective AND algorithms with clean guarantees, and empirical evidence of cognitive ease for AND queries.
- Cons: Some proofs and notation need tightening; XOR lower bounds partly conjectural; experiments limited in scale and baselines; dependence on known priors.
- Suggestions: Clarify/correct notation, strengthen Theorem 1’s argument, provide tighter converses for noisy approximate recovery (Δ=2) if possible, and expand experiments (larger datasets, heterogeneity, stronger XOR decoders, k>2).
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — promising theoretical framework and practical insights that would benefit from improved clarity and stronger empirical comparisons.
