- Tackles clustering/label learning with limited, possibly noisy, pairwise queries to an oracle (crowd workers).  
- Frames the querying problem as a locally encodable source coding task, connecting it to information theory.  
- Derives theoretical lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate label recovery.  
- Shows traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND queries as a superior alternative, with better guarantees, especially under noise.  
- Models and explicitly handles noisy oracle responses in the analysis.  
- Provides empirical validation via an Amazon Mechanical Turk experiment demonstrating AND query effectiveness and issues with majority voting.  
- Builds on crowdsourced entity resolution and adaptive query literature while introducing a relatively unexplored information-theoretic lens.  
- Relates to and improves bounds from locally decodable codes and smooth compression literature.  
- Novel contribution: connecting locally encodable source coding to querying and introducing AND queries.  
- Strong theoretical results: rigorous bounds and performance guarantees for the proposed scheme.  
- Practical relevance: addresses cost-sensitive crowdsourcing and active learning settings.  
- Generally clear presentation and well-structured exposition.  
- Proofs (e.g., Theorem 5) are complex and may be hard for non-specialists; more intuition would help.  
- Experimental description is brief; lacks detailed task design, worker controls, and per-worker analysis.  
- Algorithm 1 may be impractical to implement directly (requires d-regular graph and nontrivial computations); complexity discussion missing.  
- Experiments limited to a movies dataset; broader datasets would improve generalizability.  
- Minor clarity issues in section transitions and motivation for certain technical choices.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution worth accepting with minor revisions for clearer proofs and expanded experimental detail.
