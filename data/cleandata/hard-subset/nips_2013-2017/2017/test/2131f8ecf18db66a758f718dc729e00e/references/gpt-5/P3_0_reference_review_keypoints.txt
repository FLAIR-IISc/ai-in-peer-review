- Paper frames non-adaptive crowdsourced clustering as locally encodable source coding and presents lower bounds and achievability results under Δ-locality.  
- Main achievability: exact recovery for k=2 below n queries using sparse Δ-ary XOR-type queries with ML decoding.  
- Main lower bounds: information-theoretic bounds for exact and approximate recovery under locality; Thm 2 (participation counts/Hamming packing) is nontrivial and improves naive bounds in some regimes.  
- Approximate-recovery lower bound (Thm 3) may be novel but its statement is opaque, proof deferred, and contains typographical ambiguities (e^{Δ h′(⋅)}?).  
- Conjectured XOR/XNOR rate–distortion lower bound is reasonable but proven only at p=0.5.  
- AND-type pairwise queries are proposed and analyzed (Thm 4) on random bipartite graphs, achieving better query–accuracy trade-offs than XOR/XNOR for many parameters.  
- Noisy k-class scheme uses a k-ary AND-like query Q(u,v)=i if both labels i else 0 and a threshold decoder, claiming (1−δ)-accuracy with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.  
- Serious technical inconsistency: edge/counting notation alternates between m=nd^2 and correct m=nd/2 for d-regular graphs, which breaks the O(n log(k/δ)) scaling claim unless fixed.  
- Additional typographical/notation errors (q/(k−1) vs “qk−1”, log(k/δ) vs “log kδ”, same-cluster vs XOR/XNOR) hinder verification and understanding.  
- Small crowdsourcing experiment suggests workers err less on AND queries than on same-cluster, but the comparison is confounded by differing noise levels and unequal sample sizes.  
- Experimental evaluation should match noise levels across query types or simulate equal-q noise and include stronger baselines to fairly compare methods.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor in places: many typos, ambiguous terminology (“two-hop neighbors”), insufficient figure explanations, and deferred proofs (notably Thm 3).  
- Originality: the locally encodable coding viewpoint and several bounds/achievability results appear novel and interesting.  
- Overlooked connections: ties to non-adaptive group testing (Δ=2) and parity-based compressive sensing/sparse-graph codes are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Practical significance: if corrected, the pairwise AND scheme with simple decoding and near-linear queries is attractive for large-scale crowdsourcing.  
- Pros: fresh information-theoretic framing, simple practical schemes with provable guarantees, and initial empirical validation.  
- Cons: critical m inconsistency undermines main scaling claim, clarity/typo issues, missing proofs, confounded experiments, limited baselines, and unaddressed computational hardness of ML decoding.  
- Required fixes/suggestions: resolve m=nd/2 vs nd^2 everywhere; clarify notation and logs; provide a proof sketch for Thm 3; strengthen and deconfound experiments (matched noise, more baselines, param sweeps); discuss efficient decoders and group-testing literature.  
- Recommendation: promising and potentially impactful but requires corrections to clarity, technical consistency, proofs, and experiments before acceptance.
