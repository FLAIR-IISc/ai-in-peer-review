- Paper studies nonadaptive, budgeted crowdsourced clustering/labeling via a locally encodable source coding viewpoint where each query depends on at most Δ labels.  
- Casting queries as locally encodable codes yields new locality-aware lower bounds and informs algorithm design.  
- Noiseless exact recovery (k=2): parity/XOR queries with a random Δ-sparse query matrix and ML decoding recover labels with m ≈ n h(p)/log(1/α); for some p and Δ=2 this can be strictly sublinear in n, and a new locality-aware lower bound can beat n h(p).  
- Noiseless approximate recovery (k=2): derived locality-aware rate–distortion lower bound; XOR (“same-cluster”) is suboptimal for approximation, and pairwise AND queries achieve much better query–distortion trade-offs.  
- Noisy approximate recovery (general k): k-ary label-AND queries on a d-regular query graph with thresholding recover a 1−δ fraction with m = O(n log(k/δ)) under an i.i.d. symmetric error model and also work with known cluster sizes; small crowdsourcing study suggests AND is easier for workers.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on same-cluster query literature, budget-optimal crowdsourcing, and local decoding—locally encodable coding lens and AND-based methods appear novel.  
- Theory quality: main theorems use standard information-theoretic techniques and are plausible; Theorem 3 is deferred but would be substantive if correct; Theorems 4–5 align with simulations and known concentration arguments.  
- Assumptions vs. claims: independence-of-errors and symmetric noise are standard but potentially unrealistic; small experiment likely violates independence though findings remain informative; Theorem 6 reduces reliance on priors.  
- Experiments: empirical validation is limited (200 movies, 1000 pairs, 10 workers), mainly qualitative, and synthetic comparisons are illustrative but not exhaustive.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized and readable for the target audience, but some notation inconsistencies, deferred proofs, and the k-ary noisy model needs stronger motivation; add intuitive sketches for key deferred results.  
- Originality: the locally encodable source coding perspective and demonstrating XOR suboptimality with AND-based recovery are original contributions bridging information theory and crowdsourcing.  
- Significance: theoretical refinements of limits for nonadaptive querying are meaningful and give actionable guidance (prefer AND, use sparse-graph threshold decoders), though practical constants and worker-model robustness remain open.  
- Pros: principled theoretical bridge, new locality-aware converses, sublinear improvements in regimes, simple AND-based algorithms with noise guarantees, and empirical hint that AND is easier.  
- Cons: small-scale and possibly nonrepresentative experiments, some key proofs deferred, k-ary noisy model and query primitives may be restrictive, and XOR exact-recovery gains are regime-limited.  
- Suggestions: include a concise proof sketch for Theorem 3, analyze robustness to nonuniform/correlated worker noise, compare quantitatively to adaptive schemes, and explore hybrid or triadic queries.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical novelty and potential impact but limited empirical support; reviewer confidence medium-high.
