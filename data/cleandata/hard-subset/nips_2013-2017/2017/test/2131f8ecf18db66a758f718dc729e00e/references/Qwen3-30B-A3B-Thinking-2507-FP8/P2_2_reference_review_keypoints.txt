- Novel connection between clustering with queries and locally encodable source coding, providing an information-theoretic framework.
- Introduction of AND queries as a superior alternative to "same cluster" (XOR) queries, with formal advantages for approximate recovery.
- New lower bounds for locally encodable source coding and matching upper bounds for the proposed schemes.
- Theoretical results cover both noiseless and noisy settings, including an algorithm requiring O(n log k / δ) queries for noisy queries.
- Strong technical rigor with well-constructed proofs and thorough theoretical analysis.
- Empirical validation via a crowdsourcing experiment showing AND queries yield lower error (105 vs. 234) than same-cluster queries.
- Main theoretical contributions and strongest results focus primarily on the k=2 (two-cluster) case.
- Practical justification for why AND queries are easier for workers is not clearly explained or experimentally validated.
- Experiment scale is small (10 volunteers, 200 movies), limiting empirical strength.
- Paper lacks direct comparison to some recent related works ([33, 30, 8, 20]).
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original theoretical contribution likely to generalize beyond k=2 and of interest to the community.
- Recommendation: accept.
