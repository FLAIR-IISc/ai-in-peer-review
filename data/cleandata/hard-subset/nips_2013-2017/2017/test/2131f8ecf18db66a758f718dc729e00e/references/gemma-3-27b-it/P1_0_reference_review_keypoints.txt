- Frames clustering-with-queries as a locally encodable source coding problem, enabling information-theoretic analysis.
- Derives rigorous information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed for exact and approximate label recovery.
- Shows AND queries can outperform traditional “same cluster”/XOR queries for approximate recovery, with clear intuition.
- Analyzes the noisy oracle scenario and proposes an AND-query–based scheme to mitigate errors.
- Validates theoretical findings with a small-scale crowdsourcing experiment, noting majority voting can increase errors.
- Connects the work to prior crowdsourcing and source coding literature, extending existing results and linking to rate-distortion ideas.
- Strong mathematical presentation and rigorous derivations, increasing confidence in results.
- Empirical validation is limited in scope (small experiment, movie genres), raising generalizability concerns.
- Noisy-case analysis relies on restrictive assumptions (k-ary answers, independent errors) and is complex to evaluate practically.
- Limited discussion of practical implementation details (e.g., choosing query graph structures, managing query costs).
- Weaker connection and comparison to existing clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering) beyond a brief experiment.
- Some notation and definitions (locally encodable/decodable, smooth compression) are introduced without sufficient intuition for non-experts.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with a novel framing and useful query strategy (AND), meriting acceptance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; concrete query improvements; rigorous analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, complex/noisy-case assumptions, and sparse practical guidance.
- Recommendation/rating: Strong Accept (7/10).
