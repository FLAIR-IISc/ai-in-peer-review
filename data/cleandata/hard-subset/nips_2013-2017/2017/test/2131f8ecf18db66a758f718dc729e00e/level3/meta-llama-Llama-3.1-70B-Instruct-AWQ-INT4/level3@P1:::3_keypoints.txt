- Proposes a novel semisupervised clustering approach by connecting it to locally encodable source coding.  
- Establishes an equivalence enabling information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed for clustering.  
- Presents a querying scheme that is near-optimal for exact recovery.  
- Derives a new lower bound on queries required for approximate recovery.  
- Provides an algorithm robust to noisy queries.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and explains the information-theoretic connection clearly.  
- Experimental results validate and support the theoretical analysis.  
- Concern: AND queries can reveal cluster labels, which may conflict with crowdsourcing privacy/motivation.  
- Authors counter that direct label queries would require at least n queries, while their approach uses fewer.  
- Reviewer requests simplifying the expressions in Theorems 1–4 for broader accessibility.  
- Concern: experiments only use AND queries, raising some validity questions (authors’ responses satisfactory).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance (rating 9/10).
