- Tackles semi-supervised clustering with crowdsourced similarity queries, providing bounds for noisy and noiseless answers.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Establishes a clear information-theoretic connection for deriving bounds.  
- Proposes a novel AND-querying scheme that can outperform traditional "same-cluster" queries in some scenarios.  
- Empirical simulations support the theoretical results, demonstrating AND queries' effectiveness.  
- Paper is well written, accessible, and provides a thorough introduction and related work.  
- Technical sections are well organized and easy to follow.  
- Concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels, which could conflict with crowdsourcing motivation.  
- Reviewer requests simplifying expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 to improve accessibility.  
- Experiments appear to use only AND queries, raising some validity concerns.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
