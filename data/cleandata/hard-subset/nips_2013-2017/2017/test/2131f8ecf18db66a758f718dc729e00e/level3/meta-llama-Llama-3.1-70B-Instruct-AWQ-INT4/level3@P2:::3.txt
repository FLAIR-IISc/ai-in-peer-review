This paper tackles the problem of semisupervised clustering via crowdsourced similarity queries, providing bounds for both noisy and noiseless answers. The main contribution of the paper is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations. The authors establish an information-theoretic connection for deriving bounds, which is explained clearly throughout the paper.

The paper is well written and accessible to a general machine learning audience. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, including relevant background information and related work. The technical sections of the paper are well organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the key concepts and results.

The authors' main contribution is the development of a novel querying scheme, based on AND queries, which is shown to outperform traditional 'same cluster' queries in certain scenarios. The theoretical results are supported by empirical simulations, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

One potential concern with the paper is that the use of AND queries may reveal the cluster label, conflicting with the motivation for using crowdsourcing in the first place. If the labels are revealed through the queries, it is not clear why the authors do not simply ask for the labels directly. However, this concern is mitigated by the fact that the authors provide a clear explanation of the benefits of using AND queries, including the fact that they can be more efficient and effective than traditional queries in certain scenarios.

The reviewer requests that the authors simplify the expressions for the bounds in Theorems 1-4, while preserving their essence. This would make the paper more accessible to a wider audience and improve the overall clarity of the presentation.

The experiments in the paper appear to use only AND queries, which raises some validity concerns. However, the authors provide a clear explanation of the motivation for using AND queries and demonstrate their effectiveness in certain scenarios. The reviewer is satisfied with the authors' responses and recommends acceptance of the paper.
