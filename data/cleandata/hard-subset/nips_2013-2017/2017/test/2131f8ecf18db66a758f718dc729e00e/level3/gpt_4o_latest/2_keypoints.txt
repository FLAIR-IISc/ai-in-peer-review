- Problem: Reconstructing true labels from noisy, non-adaptive query answers, cast as a clustering problem linked to locally encodable source coding.  
- Method: Introduces AND-queries as a novel querying mechanism for label reconstruction.  
- Theory: Derives information-theoretic lower bounds and query complexity results for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Advantage: Shows AND-queries outperform traditional "same cluster" queries, especially for approximate recovery.  
- Algorithm: Proposes Algorithm 1 that is well-motivated, near-optimal for approximate recovery, and works without full prior distributions (uses relative cluster sizes).  
- Experiments: Validated on synthetic and real crowdsourced datasets, showing fewer errors for AND-queries.  
- Strength: Novel equivalence between clustering with noisy queries and locally encodable source coding, offering original theoretical insight.  
- Strength: Significant theoretical contributions including new lower bounds and performance guarantees.  
- Weakness: Presentation is unclear; proofs (Theorems 1, 3, 4) are dense and hard to follow.  
- Weakness: Discrepancy with prior work ([Ahn-Lee-Suh]) is not adequately explained, raising concerns about validity/novelty.  
- Weakness: Lower bounds (Theorems 3 and 4) are hard to interpret in practical terms.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 assumes knowledge of parameters (cluster sizes, error rates) that may be unavailable in practice.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are insufficient—figures lack state-of-the-art baselines and Fig.2 omits "same cluster" results.  
- Weakness: Restriction on repeating queries is not well-justified; no comparisons to models allowing repetitions.  
- Recommendation: Substantial revisions needed—clarify discrepancies with prior work, improve proof clarity and interpretability of bounds, add stronger experimental comparisons, justify assumptions and repetition restriction.  
- Overall verdict: Weak Accept conditional on addressing presentation, experimental, and practical limitation concerns.
