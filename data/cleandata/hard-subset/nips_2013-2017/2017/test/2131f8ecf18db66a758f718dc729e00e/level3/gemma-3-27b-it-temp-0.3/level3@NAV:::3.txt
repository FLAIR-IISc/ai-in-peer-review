## Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

This paper presents a compelling connection between semisupervised clustering via crowdsourced similarity queries and locally encodable source coding, deriving theoretical bounds for both noisy and noiseless query scenarios. The core idea – framing the clustering problem as a source coding problem – is a novel and insightful approach, allowing the authors to leverage information-theoretic tools to establish fundamental limits on query complexity. The paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible, clearly explaining the information-theoretic concepts and their application to the clustering problem. The organization is logical, and the mathematical derivations appear sound. 

The main contribution lies in the theoretical analysis, particularly the demonstration that AND queries can outperform ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries in certain settings, and the derived bounds for approximate recovery in the presence of noise. The empirical simulations, while limited in scope, provide initial validation of the theoretical findings. The authors appropriately acknowledge the limitations of the experiments and suggest avenues for future work. 

However, a significant concern remains regarding the exclusive focus on AND queries. The motivation for using AND queries stems from their potential to reveal cluster labels, but this raises the question: why not directly solicit labels from the crowd? The authors briefly address this in the introduction, but a more thorough justification is needed, particularly given the potential for direct label acquisition to bypass the theoretical complexities explored in the paper. Furthermore, the experiments only utilize AND queries, which limits the ability to directly compare their performance against other query types in a practical setting. This raises concerns about the validity of the empirical results and their generalizability.

While the theoretical expressions in Theorems 1-4 are elegant, they could benefit from simplification to enhance clarity and impact. Preserving the core insights while reducing mathematical complexity would make the results more readily accessible to a broader audience.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and insightful connection between clustering and source coding.
*   Strong theoretical foundation with well-supported bounds.
*   Clear and accessible writing style.
*   Demonstrates the potential benefits of AND queries.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of justification for prioritizing AND queries over direct label acquisition.
*   Limited experimental evaluation focused solely on AND queries.
*   Mathematical expressions in theorems could be simplified.

**Overall:**

Despite the concerns outlined above, the paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the field of clustering and information theory. The authors have responded to initial concerns effectively, and the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with the request that the authors address the concerns regarding the justification for AND queries and consider simplifying the expressions in Theorems 1-4.
