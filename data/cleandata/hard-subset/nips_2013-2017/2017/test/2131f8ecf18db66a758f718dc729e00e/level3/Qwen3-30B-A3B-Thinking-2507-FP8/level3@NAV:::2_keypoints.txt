- Proposes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering and locally encodable source coding, introducing AND-queries as a potentially more efficient query type.  
- Derives theoretical lower bounds and presents an AND-query algorithm claimed to outperform same-cluster queries.  
- Provides real-world crowdsourcing experiments to validate the approach on real data.  
- Strength: insightful, well-motivated theoretical framework linking clustering and information theory.  
- Weakness: model disallows repeated identical queries without justification; practical case with repetition is not addressed.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 appears to conflict with prior work (Ahn-Lee-Suh) in the noiseless exact-recovery regime and its proof is hard to follow.  
- Weakness: Theorems 3 and 4 have unclear interpretation—unclear how δ scales with n and there is an unexplained gap between bounds.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 omits comparison to the same-cluster-query algorithm, so superiority claims are unsubstantiated.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes, lacks practical estimation methods, and its pseudocode is difficult to follow.  
- Weakness: Figures 3–5 do not compare to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., same-cluster queries), failing to substantiate claimed performance advantages.  
- Recommendation: strengthen paper by justifying/discussing repeated queries, resolving conflicts with prior work, clarifying theoretical results, improving algorithm presentation, and adding comprehensive baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: promising theoretical contribution but currently undermined by clarity, proof, and comparative-evaluation shortcomings.
