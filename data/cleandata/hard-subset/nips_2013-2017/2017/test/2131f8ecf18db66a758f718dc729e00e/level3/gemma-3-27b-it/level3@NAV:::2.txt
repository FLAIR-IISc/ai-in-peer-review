## Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

This paper investigates the problem of reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive pairwise queries, framing it as a locally encodable source coding problem. The authors establish lower bounds on query complexity and propose an algorithm utilizing AND-queries, demonstrating improved performance over ‘same cluster’ queries in several scenarios, supported by a real-data experiment. The connection to locally encodable source coding is a novel perspective on this clustering problem, offering a theoretical foundation for analyzing query efficiency.

The paper’s strengths lie in its attempt to provide theoretical guarantees for a practical problem. Establishing a connection to information theory and deriving lower bounds are valuable contributions. The proposal of AND-queries as an alternative to ‘same cluster’ queries, and the subsequent theoretical analysis suggesting their superiority, is a promising direction. The inclusion of a real-data experiment, albeit limited in scope, adds some empirical support to the proposed method.

However, the paper suffers from significant weaknesses in clarity, presentation, and comparative analysis. The restrictive assumption of disallowing repeated queries is not sufficiently justified; a discussion of its impact or analysis of an unrestricted case is crucial. Theorem 1’s result clashes with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) regarding exact recovery and the proof is difficult to follow. Theorems 3 and 4 present lower and upper bounds with expressions that lack intuitive interpretation—the scaling of ‘m’ with ‘n’ and ‘δ’ remains unclear. 

The experimental evaluation is particularly weak. Figure 2 lacks a comparison to a baseline implementation of the ‘same cluster’ query algorithm, rendering the claimed superiority of AND-queries unsubstantiated. Furthermore, Figures 3-5 lack comparison to state-of-the-art methods. The pseudocode in Algorithm 1 is hard to understand without a deep dive into the proofs and requires prior knowledge of ‘p’ or cluster sizes, for which a practical estimation method is not discussed. The additional comments regarding the justification for disallowing query repetition, and expanded comparison to the same-query method, highlight crucial missing pieces.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel framing of semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.
*   Derivation of theoretical lower bounds on query complexity.
*   Proposed AND-query approach showing potential for improved performance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity and interpretability of theoretical results (Theorems 3 & 4).
*   Insufficient justification for the restrictive query model.
*   Weak experimental evaluation lacking comparison to relevant baselines.
*   Difficult-to-follow pseudocode and assumptions.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **rejection** at this time. While the paper presents an interesting theoretical framework, the significant issues with clarity, completeness, and experimental validation outweigh its contributions. A major revision addressing the points mentioned above, particularly a stronger experimental evaluation and clearer presentation of theoretical results, is necessary for reconsideration.
