## Review of “Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding”

This paper establishes a compelling connection between semisupervised clustering with crowdsourced query oracles and the information-theoretic concept of locally encodable source coding. The authors leverage this connection to derive lower bounds on the number of queries required for accurate clustering, considering both perfect and noisy oracle responses. A key contribution is the introduction of AND-queries as a potentially superior alternative to standard ‘same cluster’ (XOR) queries, particularly in the noisy setting, with demonstrated improvements in experimental results.  The paper is well-written, clearly explains complex concepts, and presents a rigorous theoretical analysis.

The work builds on a growing body of research into crowdsourced clustering (e.g., [4, 8, 20, 30, 33]) and draws parallels to related areas like source coding [14, 23, 26] and locally decodable codes [16, 18, 19]. The motivation for minimizing query cost, stemming from the expense and latency of interacting with human labelers, is clearly articulated. The framing of the problem as locally encodable source coding provides a novel perspective and allows the authors to apply tools from information theory to establish fundamental limits. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding is a significant insight.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides mathematically sound lower bounds on query complexity, supported by detailed proofs.
* **Clarity:** The concepts are explained in an accessible manner, even for readers not intimately familiar with information theory.
* **Practical Relevance:** The introduction of AND-queries offers a potentially valuable practical improvement for crowdsourced clustering systems.
* **Experimental Validation:** The real-world experiments on Amazon Mechanical Turk provide evidence supporting the theoretical findings, even if limited in scope.

**Weaknesses:**

* **AND-Query Motivation:** A potential concern arises regarding the use of AND-queries. If these queries reveal information about cluster labels, it challenges the typical motivation for using a query oracle – avoiding direct access to labels when they are costly or unavailable. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of this trade-off and scenarios where AND-queries are particularly advantageous.  Is the reduction in queries worth the potential information leakage?
* **Expression Complexity:** The mathematical expressions for the bounds presented in Theorems 1-4 are somewhat complex and difficult to readily interpret.  While rigor is appreciated, simplifying these expressions, even if it means sacrificing some precision, could significantly improve readability and accessibility. Maintaining the core insight while streamlining the notation would be beneficial.
* **Experimental Scope:** The experiments primarily focus on validating the AND-query approach. A more comprehensive evaluation comparing the performance of AND-queries and ‘same cluster’ queries across a wider range of datasets and noise levels would strengthen the empirical validation.  The current experimental setup appears to solely utilize AND queries; clarifying the experimental design and rationale for this choice is crucial. It's unclear how the ‘same cluster’ queries perform in this setup.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the field of semisupervised clustering.
* The connection to locally encodable source coding offers a fresh perspective and valuable theoretical tools.
* The proposed AND-queries show promise for improving the efficiency of crowdsourced clustering.
* The writing is clear and the presentation is well-organized.
* The experimental results, while limited, provide supporting evidence for the theoretical findings.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The potential trade-off between query efficiency and information leakage with AND-queries needs further discussion.
* The complexity of the mathematical expressions could hinder accessibility.
* A more comprehensive experimental evaluation would strengthen the empirical validation.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, the paper represents a significant contribution to the field. The novel connection to locally encodable source coding, the rigorous theoretical analysis, and the potential for practical improvement with AND-queries make this work worthy of acceptance. Addressing the concerns regarding the AND-query motivation and simplifying the mathematical expressions would further strengthen the paper.  I recommend acceptance.
