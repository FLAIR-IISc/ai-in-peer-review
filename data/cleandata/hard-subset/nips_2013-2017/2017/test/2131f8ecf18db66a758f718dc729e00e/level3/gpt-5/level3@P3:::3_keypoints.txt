- Paper maps semisupervised clustering with bounded-arity queries to locally encodable source coding and derives matching lower/achievability results for noiseless and noisy settings.
- Main conceptual contribution: locally encodable source coding viewpoint distinguishing from classical/global source coding and locally decodable/smooth compression.
- Technical: solid, novel lower bounds and achievability; information-theoretic mapping yields tighter bounds than naive entropy limits.
- Key technical insight: XOR (“same-cluster”) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery; AND-style queries give better query–error tradeoffs.
- Noisy-case algorithm: simple, scalable pairwise degree-d graph scheme with guarantee m = O(n log(k/δ)).
- Experiments: real crowdsourcing shows AND yields lower empirical error than XOR on same pairs, but empirical evaluation is limited in scope.
- Clarity: well written and accessible; the IT connection is particularly clear though some theorem statements are lengthy.
- Originality: locally encodable viewpoint and locality-constrained lower bounds appear new and insightful.
- Significance: clarifies when sub-n queries suffice, shows AND can dramatically outperform same-cluster for approximate recovery, and gives a practical noisy-case guideline.
- Main concern: AND queries can reveal labels—need to clarify whether the target is labeling/semisupervised classification or unlabeled clustering.
- Request: compare theoretically and empirically to single-item label queries (noiseless and noisy regimes) and highlight sub-n advantage for skewed priors.
- Practical concern: discuss worker cognitive load and bias—AND may be easier to answer than XOR, which supports practical adoption.
- Experimental limitation: empirical validation focuses mainly on AND vs XOR; conclusions should not be generalized beyond those baselines without more datasets/benchmarks.
- Request: add practitioner-friendly corollaries alongside precise theorems to simplify bounds (short forms requested for Theorems 1–5).
- Theorem 1 (noiseless exact) corollary: m ≈ n h(p) / log2(2/(1+(1−2p)^{2Δ})); Δ=2: use (1−2p)^4.
- Theorem 2 (noiseless exact lower) rule of thumb: m ≥ n h(p) · max{1, max_ρ (1−ρ)/h(((1−ρ) Δ r(p))/ρ)}; can noticeably tighten nh(p) for small p, Δ=2.
- Theorem 3 (noiseless approximate converse) corollary: locality adds an additive penalty decaying as Θ(exp(−Δ·h′(δ̃))) to the rate–distortion optimum.
- Theorem 4 (noiseless approximate AND achievability) Poissonized corollary: with λ=2m/n, δ ≲ p·exp(−pλ), so m ≳ (n/(2p))·log(p/δ).
- Theorem 5 (noisy, k classes) corollary: pick d = Θ(log(k/δ)) (constants depend on q and min p_i), then m = n d/2 = Θ(n log(k/δ)) suffices.
- Pros summary: clear, original framing; new locality lower bounds; AND achievability and O(n log(k/δ)) noisy algorithm; readable proofs; supportive empirical indication of AND ease.
- Cons summary: AND can leak labels and needs application-scoping; experiments limited and focused on AND; some theorems are heavy—add simplified corollaries.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with practical implications; recommend acceptance provided authors clarify AND semantics, compare to label queries, and add streamlined corollaries.
