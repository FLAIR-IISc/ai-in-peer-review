- Presents a novel connection between semisupervised clustering via crowdsourced similarity queries and locally encodable source coding, deriving query complexity bounds for noisy and noiseless settings.  
- Framing the clustering problem as a source coding problem is interesting and allows leveraging information-theoretic tools.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible, with particularly clear explanations of the information-theoretic connections.  
- Main contribution is a solid theoretical analysis supported by empirical simulations.  
- Demonstrates limitations of pairwise ‘same cluster’ queries and proposes AND queries, which provably outperform pairwise queries in many cases.  
- Theorems are well-stated and proof sketches appear logically sound despite omitted full proofs for space reasons.  
- Major experimental concern: experiments use only AND queries, lacking thorough empirical comparison with other query types.  
- Motivation for exclusively using AND queries is not fully justified, raising questions about practical implications.  
- Paper asserts querying labels directly is suboptimal but does not fully explain why compared to AND queries.  
- Bounds in Theorems 1–4 are valuable but somewhat complex and could be simplified to improve readability and impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novel framing, accessibility, clear limitations identified, promising alternative, and thorough author responses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental bias toward AND queries, unclear motivation for exclusive focus on AND, and complex theorem expressions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggestions to clarify the rationale for exclusive AND queries and simplify the theorem expressions.
