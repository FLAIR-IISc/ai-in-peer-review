- Frames semi-supervised clustering as locally encodable source coding, enabling information-theoretic analysis and lower bounds on queries.
- Shows limitation of standard same-cluster (XOR) queries and introduces pairwise AND queries as an alternative.
- Proposes an algorithm using AND queries and claims improved performance in noisy, approximate recovery settings.
- Provides theoretical analysis across noiseless/noisy and exact/approximate recovery scenarios with new lower and upper bounds.
- Validates method with experiments on a real crowdsourced dataset.
- Novel and significant conceptual contribution by linking clustering to locally encodable source coding.
- Introducing AND queries is an interesting, non-obvious idea with potentially practical impact in noisy settings.
- Broad theoretical scope: attempts to cover multiple oracle noise models and recovery goals.
- Model disallows repeating the same query to reduce noise, a restrictive assumption that may not match practical crowdsourcing.
- Several theoretical results lack clarity or appear inconsistent with prior work (e.g., Theorem 1 vs. Ahn et al. 2016).
- Key bounds (Theorem 3) are presented in hard-to-interpret forms, obscuring how m scales with n and δ.
- Performance guarantee for AND queries (Theorem 4) is difficult to parse and its gap to lower bounds is not analyzed.
- Experiments are insufficient: comparisons use a lower bound for same-cluster queries rather than competitive algorithms, and baseline algorithmic comparisons are missing.
- Algorithm 1 relies on prior knowledge of cluster proportions p (or n_i), with subsampling estimation and pseudocode lacking clarity and reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: promising ideas but paper needs clearer theory, stronger experiments, and more realistic modeling; current form leans toward rejection.
