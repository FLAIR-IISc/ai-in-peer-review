Paper reframes semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem — novel theoretical perspective.  
Derives lower bounds on query complexity.  
Proposes AND-queries with theoretical analysis showing advantages over 'same cluster' queries in some scenarios.  
Includes a limited real-data experiment offering some empirical support.  
Strength: provides theoretical guarantees and an information-theoretic foundation.  
Weakness: overall clarity and presentation are poor.  
Weakness: restrictive no-repetition query model is unjustified and unanalyzed.  
Issue: Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) and its proof is hard to follow.  
Issue: Theorems 3 and 4 lack intuitive interpretation; scaling of m with n and δ is unclear.  
Experimental weaknesses: Figure 2 lacks a same-cluster baseline; Figures 3–5 lack comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
Algorithm 1 pseudocode is hard to understand and assumes knowledge of p/cluster sizes without practical estimation guidance.  
Recommendation: reject; major revision needed for clarity, stronger experiments, and clearer theoretical interpretation.
