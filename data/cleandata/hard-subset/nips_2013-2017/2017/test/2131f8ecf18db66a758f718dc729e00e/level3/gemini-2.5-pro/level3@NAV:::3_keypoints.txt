- Problem: Semi-supervised clustering with a limited budget of non-adaptive, crowdsourced queries.  
- Central contribution: Novel connection between this clustering problem and locally encodable source coding.  
- Method: Model queries as locally encodable compression where each bit depends on a small number of labels.  
- Query model: Proposes and analyzes an "AND-query" alternative to standard pairwise same-cluster queries.  
- Theoretical results: Derives lower bounds on number of queries for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings (Theorems 1–4).  
- Strengths: Original framing, technically sound theoretical results, and clear, accessible writing.  
- Main concern: Motivation for AND queries—yes-answers can reveal absolute labels, so it’s unclear why direct labeling wouldn’t be used instead.  
- Experimental concern: Empirical validity depends on practical justification for AND queries since experiments use only AND queries.  
- Suggestion: Simplify final bound expressions or provide asymptotic forms to improve reader intuition.  
- Resolution & recommendation: Authors’ response satisfactorily addressed the AND-query motivation; reviewer recommends acceptance.
