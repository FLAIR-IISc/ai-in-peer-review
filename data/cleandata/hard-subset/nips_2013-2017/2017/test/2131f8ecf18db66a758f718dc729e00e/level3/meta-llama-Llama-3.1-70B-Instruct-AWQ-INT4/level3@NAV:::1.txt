**Paper Review: Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding**

**Summary:**
This paper studies the problem of clustering with same-cluster queries to an oracle, aiming to recover the target clustering. The authors provide upper and lower bounds on the required queries, including analysis for noisy oracles. They apply information-theoretic tools to show that AND-queries can outperform traditional 'same cluster' queries in various scenarios.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel approach:** The authors introduce a new perspective on clustering by casting it as a locally encodable source coding problem. This approach leads to new insights and results.
2. **Theoretical contributions:** The paper provides several theoretical results, including upper and lower bounds on the required queries, and a new lower bound on the number of queries for approximate recovery.
3. **Practical implications:** The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on real datasets and a crowdsourcing experiment.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited scope:** The analysis is limited to the case of two clusters, and the extension to multiple clusters is not thoroughly explored.
2. **Assumptions:** The authors assume that the prior distribution of labels is known, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **No structural assumptions:** The analysis does not make any structural assumptions on the data, which may limit the applicability of the results.

**Originality:**

1. **New perspective:** The paper introduces a new perspective on clustering by casting it as a locally encodable source coding problem.
2. **AND-queries:** The authors propose AND-queries as a new type of query that can outperform traditional 'same cluster' queries.

**Significance:**

1. **Advances the state of the art:** The paper provides new insights and results on the query complexity of clustering, which can be useful for various applications.
2. **Practical implications:** The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on real datasets and a crowdsourcing experiment.

**Clarity:**

1. **Well-organized:** The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and their approach.
2. **Technical details:** The technical details are well-explained, and the authors provide a clear description of their methods and results.

**Quality:**

1. **Soundness:** The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach.
2. **Completeness:** The paper is a complete piece of work, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of their results and implications.

**Overall:**
This paper provides a novel approach to clustering by casting it as a locally encodable source coding problem. The authors provide several theoretical results and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on real datasets and a crowdsourcing experiment. While the analysis is limited to the case of two clusters, the paper advances the state of the art and provides new insights into the query complexity of clustering.

**Recommendation:**
I recommend accepting this paper for publication in NIPS. **Minor comments:**
