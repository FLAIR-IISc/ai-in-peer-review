- Presents a novel crowdsourcing-based clustering method using similarity queries to recover labels, framed as locally encodable source coding.
- Derives theoretical lower and upper bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery in noisy and noiseless settings.
- Establishes limitations of traditional "same cluster" (XOR) queries and introduces AND queries as a more efficient alternative in many scenarios.
- Validates theoretical results with simulations and a real-world crowdsourcing experiment on a real dataset.
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous and advance understanding of query complexity in crowdsourced clustering.
- The locally encodable source coding connection is novel, well-motivated, and broadly relevant to information-theoretic perspectives on clustering.
- Paper is clear and accessible, with detailed proofs and intuitive explanations suitable for a general ML audience.
- Practical relevance is strong: AND queries show both theoretical and empirical efficiency gains over XOR.
- Empirical validation is robust, aligning with theory and leveraging a real crowdsourcing platform.
- Weakness: Practical motivation for expecting workers to perform AND queries instead of intuitive "same cluster" queries is not fully justified.
- Weakness: Theorems 1–4 are mathematically dense and need more simplification or intuitive explanation for non-specialists.
- Weakness: Experiments focus mainly on AND queries, offering limited comparative analysis of XOR under similar conditions.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theoretical and practical contributions despite minor concerns.
