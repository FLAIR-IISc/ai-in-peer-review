- Problem: Studies semi-supervised clustering query complexity where an oracle answers binary pairwise cluster-membership queries.
- Novelty: Frames clustering queries via locally encodable source coding, applying information-theoretic tools.
- Main results: Derives upper and lower bounds on queries needed for perfect and approximate recovery.
- Noise handling: Extends analysis to noisy oracles and provides bounds in that setting.
- Query types: Shows advantages of AND queries over standard “same cluster” queries.
- Practical validation: Includes a limited real crowdsourcing experiment supporting theory.
- Strength: Technically sound and well-motivated with a novel theoretical framework.
- Strength: Provides meaningful information-theoretic upper and lower bounds.
- Strength: Insightful analysis of query types and noisy settings.
- Weakness: No structural assumptions on clustering restricts applicability to real datasets.
- Weakness: Practical scalability and algorithmic implementation for large datasets are not thoroughly evaluated.
- Weakness: Bounds depend on label marginals and noise levels that may be hard to estimate in practice.
- Weakness: Experimental validation is limited in scope and variety.
- Overall: Valuable theoretical contribution and first step toward information-theoretic limits of query-based clustering.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions asking for expanded discussion of limitations, extensions, and broader experimental evaluation.
