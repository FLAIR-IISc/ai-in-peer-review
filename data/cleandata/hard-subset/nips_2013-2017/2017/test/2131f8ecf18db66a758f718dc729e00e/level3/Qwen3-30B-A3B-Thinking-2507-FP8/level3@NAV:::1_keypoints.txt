- Studies clustering with same-cluster queries and provides information-theoretic query complexity bounds for exact and approximate recovery.  
- Novel connection between query-based clustering and locally encodable source coding used to derive tight upper and lower bounds.  
- Shows same-cluster (XOR) queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery.  
- Proposes AND queries that require significantly fewer queries to reach comparable accuracy.  
- Builds on recent query-based clustering literature while introducing information-theoretic tools new to ML.  
- Motivation grounded in crowdsourcing and record deduplication applications.  
- Empirical validation: real-world crowdsourcing experiment shows AND queries yield lower error rates than same-cluster queries.  
- Strengths: elegant theoretical framework, rigorous proofs, and practical validation.  
- Technical soundness and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Weaknesses: analysis lacks structural assumptions (e.g., hypothesis class), limiting generality to marginal label distributions and noise levels.  
- Additional limitation: focus on pairwise queries (Δ=2) restricts broader applicability.  
- Significance: provides fundamental insights and theoretical foundations for query-based clustering and future active learning/crowdsourcing work.
