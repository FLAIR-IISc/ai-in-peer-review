- Establishes a novel connection between semi-supervised clustering via oracle queries and locally encodable source coding.  
- Derives rigorous information-theoretic lower and upper bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery, in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Shows same-cluster (XOR) queries can be suboptimal and introduces AND queries as a potentially more efficient alternative in some regimes.  
- Provides theoretical justification (rate-distortion perspective) for why AND queries help with approximate recovery under noise.  
- Supports theoretical claims with simulations and a small crowdsourcing experiment showing lower error rates for AND queries.  
- Paper is original, significant, and could inspire cross-disciplinary work in ML, crowdsourcing, and information theory.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and covers important practical scenarios, including noisy oracles.  
- The manuscript is well-written and accessible despite theoretical depth.  
- Concern: practical motivation for AND queries is unclear when direct label queries appear to reveal the same information and may cost similarly.  
- Concern: mathematical expressions of bounds are complex and lack simplified asymptotic corollaries for intuition.  
- Concern: experiments compare AND to spectral clustering but lack direct empirical comparison between AND and XOR/coding schemes.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, recommend acceptance pending clarifications on motivation, clearer asymptotic statements, and broader experimental comparisons.
