- Paper frames non-adaptive semi-supervised clustering as locally encodable source coding and uses this to derive query-complexity lower bounds.  
- Introduces AND-queries as an alternative to same-cluster (XOR) queries and argues they are better for approximate recovery and noise robustness.  
- Provides theoretical results: lower bounds for exact/approximate recovery and achievability upper bounds for proposed schemes (Theorems 1–5).  

Strengths:
- Novel connection between query-based clustering and locally encodable source coding, offering a new theoretical lens.  
- Practical and intuitive proposal of AND-queries, potentially easier for annotators and more noise-resilient.  
- Substantial theoretical development with new information-theoretic bounds and achievability constructions.

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Main theoretical results (Theorems 3 & 4) are presented in complex forms that obscure scaling of queries m with n and distortion δ; need simplified asymptotics or plots.  
- Proofs are dense and hard to follow, making verification difficult.  
- Algorithm 1 pseudocode is unclear and depends on knowing cluster distribution p; costs and effects of estimating p are not analyzed.  
- Theorem 1's claim (m < n for some priors) appears to conflict with prior O(n log n) connectivity requirements; this discrepancy is not resolved.  
- The expurgation/random-ensemble-based proof does not clearly produce a constructive querying scheme.  
- Experimental validation lacks comparisons to same-cluster algorithmic baselines (e.g., spectral methods), so claims of AND-query superiority are unsubstantiated.  
- Figure 2 compares the proposed algorithm to a theoretical lower bound for same-cluster queries—an unfair comparison; must compare to practical same-cluster algorithms.  
- Real-world experiment mentions higher same-cluster error but the main paper lacks that direct comparison and the referenced Figure 6 is missing.  
- Modeling choice disallowing repeated queries is unrealistic for crowdsourcing and is not justified; implications of allowing repetitions (multiple annotators) are not analyzed.  

Overall:
- Core ideas are promising and original, but clarity, technical soundness, and fair experimental comparisons need substantial revision before claims are convincing.
