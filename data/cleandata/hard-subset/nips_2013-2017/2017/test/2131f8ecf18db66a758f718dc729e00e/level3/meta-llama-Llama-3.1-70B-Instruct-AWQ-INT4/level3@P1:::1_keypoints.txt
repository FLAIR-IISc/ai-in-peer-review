- Proposes a novel semisupervised clustering framework based on locally encodable source coding (equivalently, simple oracle queries).  
- Derives new information-theoretic lower bounds on the number of queries needed for exact and approximate label recovery.  
- Introduces an AND-based querying strategy that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries.  
- Extends theoretical results to handle noisy oracles.  
- Validates theory with a real crowdsourcing experiment.  
- Novel and well-motivated framework addressing query-based clustering.  
- Rigorous, clear, and well-structured technical presentation with solid proofs.  
- Empirical validation supports the practical relevance of the theoretical claims.  
- Analysis is limited in scope due to lack of structural assumptions on the hypothesis class.  
- Bounds primarily depend on label marginals and noise level, which may be insufficient in some settings.  
- Lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.  
- Overall significant contribution toward information-theoretic limits for query-based clustering, but with scope and comparative-evaluation limitations.
