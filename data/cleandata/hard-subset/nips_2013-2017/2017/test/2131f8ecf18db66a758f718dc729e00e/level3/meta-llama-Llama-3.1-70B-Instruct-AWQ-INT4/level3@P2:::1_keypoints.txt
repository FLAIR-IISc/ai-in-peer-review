- Novel formulation: Frames semisupervised clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem using binary oracle queries on element pairs.  
- Problem and goal: Oracle answers simple binary pairwise queries; aim is to recover clustering while minimizing query count.  
- Theoretical results: Establishes upper and lower bounds on queries needed for perfect and approximate recovery, including noisy oracle cases.  
- Technical novelty: Introduces information-theoretic tools to machine learning clustering problems.  
- New lower bound: Proves a lower bound on queries for perfect recovery that can be strictly better than the naive nH(p) bound for some priors.  
- AND-query scheme: Proposes a new querying scheme based on AND operations that outperforms traditional "same cluster" queries for approximate recovery.  
- Practical validation: Experimental results include a real crowdsourcing experiment that supports the theoretical findings.  
- Motivation and applications: Well-motivated by record deduplication and crowdsourcing scenarios.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Scope limitation: Analysis makes no structural assumptions about the hypothesis class, limiting applicability.  
- Dependence on marginals: Bounds mainly rely on label marginal distributions and noise level, potentially insufficient for real-world complexity.  
- Missing discussion: Lacks deeper discussion on implications for many clusters and for other clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, k-means).  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and significant as a first step toward information-theoretic limits for query-based clustering, with clear arguments both for acceptance and for further improvement.
