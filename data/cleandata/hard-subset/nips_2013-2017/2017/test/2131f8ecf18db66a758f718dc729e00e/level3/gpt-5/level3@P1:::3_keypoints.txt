- Paper maps non-adaptive, budgeted crowdsourced clustering with low-arity queries to locally encodable source coding, yielding new information-theoretic lower bounds and algorithms.
- For k=2, “same-cluster” queries correspond to XORs; sparse random XORs can achieve exact recovery with m approaching n h(p)/log(1/α) for constant arity Δ, beating trivial n queries in some regimes.
- Converse bounds are proved that improve on n h(p) in certain parameter regimes by accounting for Δ-locality.
- For approximate recovery, rate–distortion insights show XOR-based queries are provably suboptimal due to limitations of linear codes.
- The paper proposes pairwise AND queries and shows they achieve near-optimal approximate-recovery trade-offs with simple local decoding.
- In the noisy setting (symmetric error q) for general k≥2, a pairwise generalized-AND query with thresholding decoder on a d-regular graph achieves O(n log(k/δ)) queries to correctly label (1−δ) fraction with high probability.
- Empirical results (small crowdsourcing movie-genre study and synthetic data) align with theory and indicate AND queries incur fewer worker errors than XOR queries.
- Theoretical analysis is careful and largely sound; mapping to locally encodable coding is elegant and yields nontrivial Δ-locality lower bounds (e.g., the (1−ρ) coverage term).
- Assumptions are explicit: non-adaptive queries, independent label priors, independent query noise, no repeated queries, and known priors or relative cluster sizes (with a known-sizes extension).
- Proofs use standard information-theoretic techniques (typicality, expurgation, Chernoff bounds) and AND achievability is supported by explicit error calculations under a Poissonized degree model.
- Clarity is good overall, but Theorems 1–4 should include simplified corollaries, clean approximations, and plots to highlight regimes where m<n (e.g., Δ=2, p≈1/2).
- Notation should be unified (e.g., r(p) vs 2p(1−p)) and explicitly name XOR = “same-cluster” and AND = “conjunctive membership” to avoid confusion.
- Original contributions: novel locally encodable source-coding perspective, new Δ-locality lower bounds, AND identification/analysis as superior to XOR for approximate recovery, and a practical O(n log(k/δ)) non-adaptive noise-robust scheme.
- Significance: guides choice of query primitives (AND vs XOR), provides Δ-locality bounds likely to influence future query design, and offers empirical evidence that AND is easier for workers.
- Weakness: Practicality and acceptability of AND queries is ambiguous—may reveal individual membership and raises privacy/label-leakage concerns; trade-offs vs direct label queries need explicit discussion.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited (only AND baseline, small crowdsourcing study); add same-cluster baselines, sensitivity to prior mis-specification, degree/graph construction, and unknown-q thresholding.
- Presentation suggestions: move heavy algebra to appendix, add plots for achievable vs lower bounds (Δ=2, k=2), and provide a simplified corollary/table summarizing Theorems 1–4 for typical p.
- Questions for authors: clarify application settings where AND is feasible but direct labels are not and discuss privacy; provide simplified corollaries/tables for Δ=2; address robustness of Algorithm 1 to unknown q and whether thresholds can be learned; do results extend to higher-arity queries and limited adaptivity?
- Overall recommendation: accept after rebuttal and clarifications—contributions are solid, significant, and likely to inspire further research.
