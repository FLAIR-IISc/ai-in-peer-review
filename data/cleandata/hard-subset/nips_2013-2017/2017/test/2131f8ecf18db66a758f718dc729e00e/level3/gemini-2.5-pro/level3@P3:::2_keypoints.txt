- Paper studies non-adaptive semi-supervised clustering using limited-arity queries and frames it as locally encodable source coding to derive query lower bounds.
- Introduces AND-queries as a novel alternative to same-cluster queries and argues they are superior for approximate recovery, especially with noise.
- Presents an AND-query based algorithm and evaluates it on synthetic and real data.
- Strength: Novel connection between non-adaptive clustering queries and locally encodable source coding.
- Strength: AND-queries are a fresh, theoretically motivated alternative to same-cluster queries.
- Strength: Problem setup (non-adaptive, parallelizable, noisy queries) is relevant for practical large-scale systems like crowdsourcing.
- Weakness: Model forbids repeating the same query, an unrealistic and poorly justified restriction that ignores standard annotator-aggregation techniques.
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s noiseless exact recovery bound appears to conflict with prior Ω(nk) lower bounds for k=2 and is not reconciled or clearly proved.
- Weakness: Theorems 3 and 4 produce overly complex, opaque bounds without simplified asymptotic intuition about m’s scaling with n and δ.
- Weakness: Proofs are highly condensed and hard to follow, undermining confidence in theoretical soundness.
- Weakness: Figure 2 compares AND-algorithm to a theoretical lower bound for same-cluster queries rather than to an actual algorithm, making the superiority claim unfair.
- Weakness: Figures 3–5 evaluate the proposed algorithm in isolation without comparisons to baseline or state-of-the-art same-cluster methods.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of label distribution or exact cluster sizes, with no practical estimation strategy provided.
- Weakness: Pseudocode and threshold choices in Algorithm 1 are hard to follow and only become clear by unpacking complex proofs, harming reproducibility.
- Argument for acceptance: Theoretical linkage to source coding and the AND-query idea are potentially significant contributions.
- Argument for rejection: Central claims lack fair empirical support, theoretical results are opaque and possibly inconsistent with prior work, and modeling/clarity issues limit applicability.
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; authors should clarify theory, justify or relax the no-repeat query model, and run rigorous comparisons to strong baselines.
