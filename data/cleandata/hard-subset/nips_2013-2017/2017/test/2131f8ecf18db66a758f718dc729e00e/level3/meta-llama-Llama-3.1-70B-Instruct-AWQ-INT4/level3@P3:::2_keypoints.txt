- Novel approach: combines AND-queries with locally encodable source coding for semi-supervised clustering.
- The paper models multi-class labeling as equivalent to a locally encodable source coding problem.
- Theoretical contributions: lower bounds on query complexity and an algorithm to reconstruct labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries.
- Empirical contribution: real-data evaluation demonstrating practical relevance.
- Pairwise AND-queries are proposed and shown to outperform alternatives in many settings.
- Strength: thorough analysis combining information-theoretic bounds and algorithm design.
- Weakness: presentation and clarity need improvement; some proofs and expressions are hard to follow.
- Concern: prohibition of repeated identical queries is assumed without justification; no analysis of the unrestricted or independent-annotator cases.
- Technical clarity issue: proofs of Theorems 1 and 3 and the lower-bound expressions for δ are difficult to interpret.
- Unclear theoretical gap: the gap between the provided upper and lower bounds is not well explained.
- Experimental gaps: Figure 2 lacks comparison to same-cluster-query baseline; Figures 3–5 do not compare to state-of-the-art methods.
- Practicality concern: algorithm requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes, which may be unrealistic.
- Pseudocode and algorithm presentation are hard to follow without consulting complex proofs.
- Originality/significance: novel and potentially impactful approach for semi-supervised clustering and crowdsourcing, but comparisons to existing methods are insufficient.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10); authors should improve clarity, justify assumptions, and add stronger empirical comparisons.
