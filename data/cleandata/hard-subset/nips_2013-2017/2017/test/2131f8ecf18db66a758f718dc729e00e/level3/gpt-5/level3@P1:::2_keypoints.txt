- Paper reframes nonadaptive semisupervised clustering with small-set queries as locally encodable source coding and derives information-theoretic bounds plus algorithms, advocating AND over XOR queries for approximate recovery.

- Strength: Elegant conceptual unification using locally encodable source coding to get new lower bounds for query complexity.

- Strength: Identifies limitations of XOR/same-cluster queries for approximate recovery and proposes constructive AND-based alternatives with simple threshold decoding.

- Strength: Proposes simple nonadaptive schemes achieving near-linear queries m = O(n log(k/δ)) under noise, potentially practical.

- Strength: Provides new locality-aware lower bounds improving on naive entropy bounds.

- Strength: Small crowdsourcing experiment suggests AND questions may be easier and less noisy for workers.

- Weakness 1: Disallows repeated identical queries, a restrictive assumption not justified—need analysis of repetition as a resource and effect on bounds/algorithms.

- Weakness 2: Theorem 1 (sublinear-in-n claim for Δ = 2) conflicts with prior worst-case results; authors must reconcile model differences, clarify assumptions (i.i.d. labels/prior knowledge), and simplify proof intuition.

- Weakness 3: Theorem 3 (noiseless approximate lower bound) is hard to interpret—provide corollaries, asymptotics, and plots for common regimes (e.g., Δ=2, p=1/2).

- Weakness 4: Theorem 4 (AND achievability) is opaque due to Poissonization/combinatorial terms—need streamlined bounds with explicit constants and numeric examples (e.g., queries needed for δ=0.1 at p=0.5).

- Weakness 5: Empirical claim that AND outperforms XOR lacks fair algorithmic baselines—need budget-matched comparisons to strongest XOR decoders/designs.

- Weakness 6: Algorithm 1 depends on unknown p/cluster sizes without practical estimation guidance—need methods to estimate priors, sensitivity analysis, and clearer pseudocode/derivation of thresholds.

- Weakness 7: Experiments are limited and omit strong baselines and ablations—request larger synthetic studies, error bars, degree/noise ablations, and main-paper presentation of comparisons.

- Quality: Technical ideas are promising but many proofs are hard to parse and key statements lack quantitative interpretability.

- Clarity: Manuscript needs a sharper problem statement, a model comparison table, intuitive proof sketches, and clearer figures with baselines.

- Originality: Locally encodable source-coding view and AND-query advocacy appear novel; locality-aware lower bounds are original.

- Significance: With clearer proofs and fair comparisons, the work could influence crowdsourced query design and locally encodable coding theory.

- Pros for acceptance: Unifying perspective, new locality-aware lower bounds, simple AND-based algorithms with favorable scaling, and preliminary empirical support.

- Cons for acceptance: Restrictive no-repetition model, unclear reconciliation with prior work, hard-to-interpret bounds, missing algorithmic comparisons, and limited experiments.

- Suggested improvements: Justify/relax non-repetition; reconcile/simplify Theorem 1; add corollaries/plots for Theorems 3–4; include budget-matched XOR baselines; detail prior estimation and robustness for Algorithm 1; expand experiments with larger datasets and varied error models.

- Overall assessment: Promising and original paper requiring substantial revisions in theory presentation, interpretability, and experimental comparisons before making a strong contribution.
