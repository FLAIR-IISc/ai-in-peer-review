## Review of "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding"

This paper explores the connection between semisupervised clustering via pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding, aiming to establish lower bounds on query complexity and propose an efficient querying scheme based on AND queries. The authors demonstrate this connection by framing the problem as a source coding task, leveraging information-theoretic tools to derive bounds and algorithms. While the core idea of linking clustering to locally encodable codes is intriguing and potentially impactful, the presentation and supporting evidence require significant improvement.

The paper’s strength lies in its attempt to provide a theoretical foundation for query-based clustering, particularly in scenarios with noisy oracles. The derivation of lower bounds (Theorems 1 & 3) and the proposal of AND queries as an alternative to ‘same cluster’ queries are valuable contributions. The experimental validation on real datasets, though limited, provides some initial support for the proposed method.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. Firstly, the restriction of disallowing repeated queries (mentioned in the introduction) feels arbitrary and lacks sufficient justification. Analyzing the case with repetition or an independent annotator setting would strengthen the work. Secondly, Theorem 1’s claim conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) and its proof is difficult to follow, hindering confidence in its validity. Similarly, the interpretation of Theorem 3’s lower bound for δ is unclear, and the scaling of *m* with *n* and δ is not readily apparent. Theorem 4 suffers from the same interpretability issues, and the gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound remains unexplained.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation is insufficient. Figure 2 lacks a comparison to the straightforward ‘same-cluster-query’ algorithm, making the claimed superiority of AND queries unsubstantiated. Figures 3-5 lack comparison to state-of-the-art methods, failing to demonstrate the practical advantage of the proposed approach. The algorithm (Algorithm 1) requires prior knowledge of *p* or cluster sizes, and the paper doesn’t address the practical challenges of estimating these parameters. The pseudocode itself is also difficult to parse without a deep understanding of the proofs.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel connection between clustering and locally encodable source coding.
*   Derivation of theoretical lower bounds on query complexity.
*   Proposal of AND queries as a potentially more efficient alternative.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Presentation and clarity issues throughout the paper.
*   Conflicts with existing literature (Theorem 1).
*   Lack of interpretability of key theoretical results (Theorems 3 & 4).
*   Insufficient experimental evaluation and lack of comparison to relevant baselines.
*   Unjustified restriction on query repetition.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting idea but falls short of delivering a convincing contribution due to significant clarity, presentation, and validation issues. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and address the concerns outlined above. A clearer presentation, thorough comparison to existing work, and a more robust experimental evaluation are crucial for this paper to be considered for publication.
