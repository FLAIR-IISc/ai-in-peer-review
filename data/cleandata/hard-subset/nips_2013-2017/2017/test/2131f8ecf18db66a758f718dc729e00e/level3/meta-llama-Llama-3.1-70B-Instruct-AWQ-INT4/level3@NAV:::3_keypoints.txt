- Proposes a novel semisupervised clustering approach by leveraging locally encodable source coding from information theory.  
- Addresses clustering via crowdsourced similarity queries and derives bounds for both noisy and noiseless answers.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, supported by empirical simulations.  
- Shows equivalence between a recent semisupervised clustering model and locally encodable source coding, yielding new information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Introduces pairwise AND queries that provably outperform traditional "same cluster" queries in many settings.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; the information-theoretic connection and bounds are explained clearly.  
- Concern that AND queries may reveal cluster labels and conflict with crowdsourcing privacy/motivation; authors argue binary/simple query design mitigates this.  
- Reviewer recommends simplifying the expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 to improve clarity.  
- Experiments appear to use only AND queries, raising validity concerns unless the AND-query motivation and implications are clarified.  
- Quality scored 8/10: technically sound but some proofs/derivations could be simplified.  
- Clarity scored 9/10: generally clear but some technical details need clarification for non-experts.  
- Originality scored 9/10: presents a novel connection and insights into query limitations.  
- Significance scored 9/10: results potentially impact entity resolution, crowdsourcing, and data compression.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
