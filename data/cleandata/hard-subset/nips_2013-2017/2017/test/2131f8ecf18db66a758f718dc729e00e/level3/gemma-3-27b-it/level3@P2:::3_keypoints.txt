- Novel connection: Frames semisupervised clustering as locally encodable source coding, offering a fresh information-theoretic perspective.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives lower bounds on query complexity with rigorous theorems and proofs.  
- AND-query strategy: Proposes AND queries that outperform traditional "same-cluster" (XOR) queries, especially for approximate recovery.  
- Practical relevance: Addresses an important problem for crowdsourcing with noisy pairwise comparisons.  
- Empirical support: Experiments on real crowdsourced datasets provide validation for the proposed approach.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and accessible even to readers not expert in information theory.  
- Literature context: Builds on and situates itself among prior work on clustering with queries and side information.  
- Strength — novelty and rigor: Theoretical soundness and the new coding-theory connection are significant contributions.  
- Weakness — AND-query motivation: Insufficiently justified why AND queries are preferable to directly asking labels given they reveal partial label information.  
- Weakness — bound complexity: Theorem expressions are complex and hard to parse; would benefit from simplification or asymptotic interpretation.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Lacks direct experimental comparison against "same-cluster" query baselines in the same setup.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to better motivate AND queries, simplify bounds, and add baseline comparisons.
