- Paper frames clustering with noisy pairwise queries as a locally encodable source coding problem and derives information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Main contribution: proposing AND queries and arguing they can outperform same-cluster (XOR) queries for approximate recovery.  
- Novelty: connecting locally encodable source coding to semisupervised clustering is new but under-exploited.  
- Technical soundness is questionable; key theorems (especially Theorems 1 and 3) are hard to follow and insufficiently justified.  
- The restriction disallowing repeated queries is weakly motivated and undermines practical relevance.  
- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) and lacks a clear comparison or resolution.  
- Theorem 3’s parameter δ is opaque; scaling of m with n and δ is not clearly explained or interpreted.  
- Theorem 4 is similarly hard to interpret and its gap with Theorem 3’s lower bound is unexplained.  
- Algorithm 1 pseudocode is opaque, relies on prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes, and lacks practical parameter-estimation guidance.  
- Notation, organization, and proof presentation are poor, making the paper difficult to read and verify.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and do not include key baselines (same-cluster queries, spectral clustering), undermining claims of AND-query superiority.  
- Figure 2 lacks comparison to a same-cluster-query baseline and shows unexplained crossing behavior.  
- Figures 3–5 omit comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, weakening empirical claims.  
- Theoretical contributions do not clearly translate into meaningful practical improvements in query complexity.  
- Overall significance is limited by restrictive modeling choices, weak empirical support, and unclear advantages over existing methods.  
- Recommendation: reject unless major revisions address clarity, modeling justification, comparisons, theorem proofs, and expanded experiments.
