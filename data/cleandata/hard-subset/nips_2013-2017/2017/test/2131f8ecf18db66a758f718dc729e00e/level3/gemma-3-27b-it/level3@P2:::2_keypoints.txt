- Connects semisupervised clustering with locally encodable source coding to analyze query complexity.
- Derives lower bounds on queries needed for noisy-oracle clustering and proposes AND-queries as an alternative to ‘same cluster’ queries.
- Provides theoretical analysis and a small crowdsourcing experiment suggesting AND-queries can outperform traditional queries.
- Novel connection between clustering and source coding that could open new research directions.
- Theoretical contributions include fundamental lower bounds and a practical AND-query scheme.
- Includes real-world validation (limited-scale crowdsourcing) supporting theoretical claims.
- Explicitly models noisy oracle responses, increasing practical relevance.
- Presentation is unclear: dense notation, hard-to-follow proofs, and insufficient intuition.
- Insufficient comparison to prior work and baselines; key figures compare bounds only to themselves.
- Potential conflict between Theorem 1 and recent results (Ahn, Lee, Suh) is unaddressed.
- Restricting to non-repeated queries is arbitrary and weakly justified; relaxing this would strengthen the work.
- Theorems 3 and 4 give complex bounds that are hard to interpret regarding scaling with m, n, and δ.
- Algorithm 1 depends on prior knowledge of cluster-size distribution (p or ni), which is impractical and unexplored.
- Crowdsourcing experiment is small and lacks comparison to established methods (e.g., spectral clustering with same-cluster queries).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel perspective, valuable theoretical limits, and some empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, weak empirical and literature comparisons, restrictive modeling, and limited interpretability.
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially strong work but requires major revision—clarify exposition, add baseline comparisons, relax/justify no-repetition constraint, and address prior-estimation issues.
- Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
