- Establishes a novel connection between semisupervised clustering with pairwise queries and locally encodable source coding in information theory.  
- Shows pairwise "same cluster" queries map to a locally encodable source coding problem where each compressed bit depends on a constant number of input bits.  
- Derives tight information-theoretic bounds on the number of queries needed for exact and approximate cluster label recovery.  
- Proves XOR ("same cluster") queries are fundamentally limited for approximate recovery and that AND queries perform substantially better.  
- For noiseless two-cluster case, presents an AND-query scheme recovering (1−δ) fraction of labels with O(n log(1/δ)) queries, improving over XOR’s O(n log n).  
- For noisy k-cluster settings, provides an AND-based algorithm achieving (1−δ)-good recovery with O(n log(k/δ)) queries.  
- Validates the approach with a real crowdsourcing experiment showing AND queries yield fewer errors (105 vs. 234) than same-cluster queries.  
- Offers a persuasive analysis of why AND queries are easier for crowd workers (a correct "no" often requires knowing only one label).  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous, builds on established information theory, and advances locally encodable source coding literature.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible, with clear proofs and practical implications explained.  
- Major concern: insufficient motivation for AND queries relative to direct label queries — contradiction about whether AND queries reveal less information is not fully resolved.  
- Experimental use of only AND queries lacks justification for preferring them over direct label queries in crowdsourcing.  
- Suggestion: simplify and clarify expressions of bounds in Theorems 1–4 to improve accessibility for non-specialists.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution with strong theory and convincing experiments; recommends acceptance despite the motivation concern.
