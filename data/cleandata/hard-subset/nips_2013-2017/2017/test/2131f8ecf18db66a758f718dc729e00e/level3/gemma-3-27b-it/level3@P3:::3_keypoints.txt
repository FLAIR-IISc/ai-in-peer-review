- Establishes theoretical bounds for clustering using noisy and noiseless crowdsourced similarity queries.  
- Core contribution: proves equivalence between this querying model and locally encodable source coding, yielding new lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Proposes AND queries as an alternative to pairwise 'same cluster' queries, claiming provably better performance in many scenarios.  
- Strong theoretical rigor: proofs appear sound and derived bounds are insightful.  
- Exceptionally clear and accessible writing, with a thorough, intuitive explanation of the information-theoretic connection.  
- Novel perspective linking crowdsourced clustering to information theory and acknowledging related locally decodable/encodable coding work.  
- Key weakness: AND queries may inadvertently reveal cluster labels; the paper needs a stronger justification for using combination queries instead of directly asking labels.  
- Requires deeper discussion of how much information each query type reveals and the assumptions on oracle behavior.  
- Bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex and hard to interpret; providing asymptotic approximations or regime-specific simplifications would improve usability.  
- Experimental evaluation uses only AND queries and lacks a direct comparison against 'same cluster' queries under identical conditions.  
- Authors should explicitly state model assumptions that underlie theoretical results and discuss their impact on experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; significant insights into fundamental limits; excellent exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for AND queries, potentially limited practical impact due to complex bounds, and a limited experimental comparison.  
- Overall assessment/recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to simplify bound expressions and strengthen justification and empirical comparison for AND queries.
