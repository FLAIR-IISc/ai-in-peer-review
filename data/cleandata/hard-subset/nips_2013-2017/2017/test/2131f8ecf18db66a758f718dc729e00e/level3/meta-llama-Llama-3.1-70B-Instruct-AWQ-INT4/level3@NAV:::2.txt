The paper "Semisupervised Clustering, AND-Queries and Locally Encodable Source Coding" presents a novel approach to semisupervised clustering using AND-queries and locally encodable source coding. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the problem, including lower bounds and an efficient algorithm for approximate recovery.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and its significance. The technical contributions are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance. The experimental results on real crowdsourced data are also impressive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, there are some issues with the presentation and clarity of the paper. The writing is sometimes dense and hard to follow, and the authors could benefit from breaking up the text into smaller paragraphs and using more headings to improve the organization. Additionally, some of the proofs and technical details are relegated to the appendix, which can make it difficult to follow the main arguments.

The comparison to prior work is also somewhat limited, and the authors could benefit from a more thorough discussion of how their approach relates to other methods in the literature. Specifically, the authors should address the discrepancy between their results and those of Ahn-Lee-Suh, and provide a clearer explanation of how their approach differs from other querying schemes.

The algorithm proposed in the paper requires prior knowledge of the cluster sizes or the prior distribution, which may not always be available in practice. The authors should discuss the practical implications of this assumption and provide guidance on how to estimate these parameters in real-world scenarios.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a significant contribution to the field of semisupervised clustering. However, with some revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper could be even stronger.
