- Novel connection: Frames semi-supervised clustering as locally encodable source coding, offering a new information-theoretic analysis framework.  
- Main theoretical contribution: Derives improved lower bounds for exact recovery and a novel lower bound for approximate recovery.  
- Query analysis: Shows AND queries are theoretically more efficient than same-cluster (XOR) queries for noisy, approximate recovery and presents Algorithm 1 with guarantees.  
- Technical quality: Solid, complete analysis with rigorous proofs supporting the claims.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explains the information-theoretic viewpoint accessibly.  
- Practical motivation concern: AND queries may require workers to know labels (e.g., “action”), undermining the advantage of similarity queries and making real-world applicability unclear.  
- Experimental scope concern: Real-world experiment validates only AND queries; lack of direct comparison with same-cluster queries on the same dataset weakens empirical claims.  
- Presentation issue: Expressions for bounds (Theorems 1–4) are complex and hard to parse; simpler asymptotic forms or interpretations would improve readability.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, original theoretical work that would be stronger with clearer practical motivation and broader empirical validation.
