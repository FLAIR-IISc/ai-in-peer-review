- Summary: Studies clustering with same-cluster queries, gives upper/lower bounds (including noisy oracles), and shows AND-queries can outperform same-cluster queries.
- Strength: Novel perspective casting clustering as a locally encodable source coding problem.
- Strength: Provides several theoretical results, including upper/lower bounds and a new lower bound for approximate recovery.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical effectiveness via experiments on real datasets and a crowdsourcing study.
- Weakness: Analysis is limited to the two-cluster case; multicluster extension is not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness: Assumes the prior distribution of labels is known, which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Weakness: No structural assumptions on the data are made, potentially limiting applicability.
- Originality: Introduces a new encoding-based viewpoint for clustering.
- Originality: Proposes AND-queries as a new query type that can outperform traditional same-cluster queries.
- Significance: Advances understanding of query complexity for clustering and offers new theoretical insights.
- Significance: Shows practical implications through experiments, suggesting real-world utility.
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with a clear introduction to problem and approach.
- Clarity: Technical details and methods are clearly and thoroughly explained.
- Quality: Analysis appears technically sound with thorough treatment of results.
- Quality: Paper is complete and discusses implications of findings adequately.
- Overall: Novel, technically strong work that advances the state of the art despite scope limits.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance to NIPS.
- Minor: Extend analysis to multiple clusters.
- Minor: Provide more details on the crowdsourcing experiment (participants, time).
- Minor: Add more comparisons with other clustering algorithms.
