- Proposes a simple, closed-form screening rule that is a necessary and sufficient condition for exact blockwise recovery in L1-regularized Ising model estimation.  
- Screening rule is easily checkable and suitable for large-scale problems.  
- Empirical results on simulated and real datasets show significant speedups when combined with various optimization algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible to a general ML audience, and establishes a connection to information theory.  
- Experimental study is thorough and demonstrates the rule’s efficiency and effectiveness.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, with simulations and real-data experiments providing supporting evidence.  
- Particularly valuable for large-scale exploratory analysis focusing on relationships among a few variables within moderate-size clusters.  
- Potential concern: AND queries may conflict with the original crowdsourcing motivation, which is not fully explored.  
- The expressions for bounds in Theorems 1–4 are complex and could be simplified without losing essence.  
- Limited exploration of the screening rule’s applicability to other inexact optimization algorithms.  
- Strengths summarized: simple closed-form rule, strong empirical speedups, thorough experiments.  
- Weaknesses summarized: AND-query concern, complex bound expressions, limited applicability study.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel/efficient rule, strong empirical evidence, clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: unresolved AND-query issue, need for simpler bound expressions, limited evaluation with inexact solvers.
