- Problem studied: reconstructing cluster labels from noisy, non-adaptive small-set queries; provides lower bounds, an AND-queries algorithm, and links to locally encodable source coding.  
- Novelty: viewing querying as a locally encodable source coding problem and using AND-queries as a primitive.  
- Theoretical strength: clear lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery and well-organized theoretical presentation.  
- Restrictive modeling: forbids repeated identical queries — needs justification or analysis of the unrestricted/independent-annotator case.  
- Theorem 1 conflicts with prior work (Ahn–Lee–Suh) in the noiseless exact-recovery regime and its proof is hard to follow.  
- Theorem 3’s lower-bound expression for δ is hard to interpret; unclear how m scales with n and δ or how relaxing δ helps.  
- Theorem 4 has similar interpretability issues and the gap between its upper bound and Theorem 3’s lower bound is unclear.  
- Algorithm 1 requires prior knowledge of p or cluster sizes (per Theorem 6); practical parameter estimation is not discussed and pseudocode is hard to follow without proofs.  
- Empirical comparisons missing: no comparisons to state-of-the-art same-cluster-query algorithms; Figure 2 only shows a lower bound.  
- Unsupported claims: superiority of AND-queries over existing schemes is not substantiated and unexplained crossing behavior appears in results.  
- Limited experimental evaluation: Figures 3–5 omit existing schemes, so practical performance is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Originality caveat: provides a new perspective and primitive but builds on existing clustering/crowdsourcing work and should better highlight prior connections.  
- Significance: theoretical contributions are important, but practical impact is limited by the restrictive model and weak experimental validation.  
- Reviewer recommendation split: for acceptance — important theory and novel primitives; against — restrictive model, poor clarity, and inadequate comparisons/experiments.  
- Final rating and confidence: 6/10, confidence 8/10.
