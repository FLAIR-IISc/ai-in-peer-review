- Presents a novel crowdsourced clustering approach with theoretical bounds for noiseless and noisy query answers and supporting simulations.
- Main contribution is theoretical but with significant practical implications for crowdsourced clustering.
- Clearly explains the information-theoretic connection between clustering and source coding; notation is well-defined and accessible.
- Provides a unifying framework showing limitations of different querying strategies.
- Shows 'same cluster' queries are suboptimal for approximate recovery and proposes AND queries that often perform better.
- Empirical results are convincing on real-world datasets and show robustness to noise; comparison with 'same cluster' queries is useful.
- Concern: AND queries may reveal cluster labels, which conflicts with crowdsourcing motivation and requires justification.
- Concern: Theorem expressions (1–4) are complex and should be simplified for broader accessibility.
- Concern: Experiments appear to use only AND queries; authors should clarify motivation and add experiments to demonstrate robustness across query types.
- Reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
