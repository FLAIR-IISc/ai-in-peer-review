- Novel connection: Links semisupervised clustering with locally encodable source coding to apply information-theoretic tools.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives rigorous lower bounds on query complexity for perfect and noisy oracles.  
- AND-queries: Introduces AND-queries as an alternative to “same-cluster” queries and shows potential advantages, especially under noise.  
- Practical experiments: Real-world validation on Amazon Mechanical Turk supports theoretical claims, though scope is limited.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explains complex information-theoretic concepts accessibly.  
- Rigor: Detailed proofs and mathematically sound analysis underpin the results.  
- Relevance: Motivation to minimize costly crowdsourced queries is clearly articulated and practically important.  
- Novel perspective: Framing clustering as locally encodable source coding is a significant and fresh insight.  
- AND-query concern: Potential information leakage from AND-queries may undermine the oracle model and needs deeper discussion.  
- Trade-off question: Unclear whether query reduction from AND-queries justifies potential disclosure of label information.  
- Expression complexity: Theorems 1–4 present complex expressions that hinder interpretability and could be simplified.  
- Experimental limitations: Experiments focus mainly on AND-queries; lack of broader comparison with same-cluster queries and varied datasets/noise levels.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novelty, theoretical soundness, clear writing, and promising AND-query results support acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: Need more discussion on AND-query trade-offs, simpler presentation of bounds, and broader empirical validation.  
- Overall verdict: Strong contribution worthy of acceptance, with recommended revisions to discuss AND-query motivation and simplify mathematical presentation.
