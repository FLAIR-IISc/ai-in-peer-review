- Novel framing: reduces semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding, opening an information-theoretic approach.
- Theoretical results: derives rigorous upper and lower bounds on query complexity for exact and approximate recovery in noiseless and noisy settings.
- AND vs XOR: shows AND queries can outperform traditional “same cluster” (XOR) queries for approximate recovery, with theoretical and experimental support.
- Practical motivation: problem is relevant to crowdsourcing and entity resolution and validated on real datasets.
- Limited assumptions: analysis uses few structural assumptions, which simplifies theory but may limit applicability to specific data regimes.
- Experimental scope: real-data experiments are limited in scale and lack extensive comparisons to existing clustering methods under oracle constraints.
- Proof complexity: some proofs, especially lower bounds, are involved and could benefit from clearer, more detailed exposition.
- Related work coverage: cites relevant literature but needs deeper discussion on how it builds on or diverges from prior models (e.g., stochastic block model).
- Repetition: some redundancy in the introduction and related sections.
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, solid theoretical contributions, and practical query-design insight (AND queries) justify acceptance.
- Rejection arguments: limited assumptions, sparse experimental validation, and proof complexity could reduce practical impact and accessibility.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity of proofs and expand related work/experiments.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
