- Novel connection between semisupervised clustering and locally encodable source coding.
- Crowdsourcing model with queries of up to Δ elements shown equivalent to locally encodable source coding, enabling information-theoretic lower bounds.
- Introduces AND queries as a more informative and efficient alternative to "same cluster" (XOR) queries.
- Theoretical results are rigorous with matching upper and lower bounds for exact and approximate recovery in noisy and noiseless settings (Theorems 1–5).
- Empirical validation (Section 4) supports the theoretical advantages of AND queries.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and accessible to a general ML audience.
- Original contribution with clear practical implications for budget-constrained crowdsourcing.
- Main concern: AND queries can reveal labels directly, which may conflict with crowdsourcing motivations and needs stronger justification versus direct label queries.
- Experiments use only AND queries and lack direct comparison to "same cluster" queries in the same setting.
- Some bound expressions in Theorems 1–4 are complex and could be simplified for readability.
- Pro points: novel theoretical link, AND queries, strong guarantees, empirical support, well-suited for NIPS.
- Con points: unclear AND motivation vs direct labels, limited experimental comparisons, dense technical expressions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify AND query motivation and simplify technical expressions.
