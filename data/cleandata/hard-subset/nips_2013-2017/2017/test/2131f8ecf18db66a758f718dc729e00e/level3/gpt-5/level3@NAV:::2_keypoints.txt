- Summary: Connects non-adaptive small-set query semisupervised clustering to locally encodable source coding, gives information-theoretic lower bounds, and proposes AND-queries with analysis and a small real-data evaluation.

- Strength: Conceptual bridge to locally encodable source coding is interesting and potentially influential.

- Strength: Provides new lower bounds beyond nH(p), highlighting inherent rate–distortion limits for XOR/same-cluster queries.

- Strength: AND-query scheme achieves O(n log(1/δ)) scaling for approximate recovery with a simple decoder.

- Strength: Initial real-data study indicates lower error rates for AND vs. same-cluster responses.

- Weakness 1 (Model restriction): Banning repeated identical queries is a strong assumption; need empirical justification or analysis of repeated-query aggregation (Dawid–Skene/KOS) and its effect on bounds.

- Weakness 2 (Exact recovery conflict): Theorem 1 seems inconsistent with Ahn–Lee–Suh for noiseless exact recovery; must reconcile assumptions (graph/hypergraph, adaptivity, Δ, sparsity) and provide clearer step-by-step proof.

- Weakness 3 (Theorem 3 interpretability): The δ lower-bound is hard to parse; authors should give explicit asymptotic scaling m(n,δ), plots over p and Δ, and plain-language meaning of the eΔh′ term.

- Weakness 4 (Theorem 4 interpretability): Same issues as Theorem 3—quantify and plot gap to Theorem 3 and add proof sketches for key probabilistic steps (two-hop neighborhood, Poissonization).

- Weakness 5 (Experimental baselines): Figure 2 omits achievable same-cluster algorithms; include ML/SDP/spectral non-adaptive baselines and explain any crossing behavior.

- Weakness 6 (Algorithm 1 practicality): Assumes known p/cluster sizes; discuss practical estimation (sample complexity, plug-in estimators), robustness to misspecification, provide standalone pseudocode and computational complexity.

- Weakness 7 (Figures 3–5): Add comparisons to strong same-cluster schemes on synthetic and real data under identical query budgets to substantiate AND’s advantage.

- Originality/significance: Equivalence and some bounds are novel and relevant; clearer proofs and stronger comparisons could make the contribution significant.

- Pros for acceptance: Novel theoretical lens and bounds; simple, practical AND-query primitive with O(n log(1/δ)) scaling.

- Cons for acceptance: Restrictive model without repeated-query analysis, hard-to-parse proofs and unresolved conflict with prior exact-recovery results, and empirical evaluation lacks strong baselines and parameter-estimation discussion.

- Overall: Promising work that requires substantial clarifications, reconciliation with prior results, and stronger empirical comparisons.
