- Summary: Studies non-adaptive small-set queries for multiclass labeling under a probabilistic prior, links the problem to locally encodable source coding, gives info-theoretic lower bounds, and proposes AND-style queries and a non-adaptive Algorithm 1 with guarantees under noise; includes a small crowd experiment.

- Relation to prior work: Connects to crowdsourced same-cluster queries, hypergraph/community recovery, and local source-coding literature; the locally encodable viewpoint is novel.

- Strength: Conceptual novelty — locally encodable source-coding lens yields clean lower bounds and clarifies limits of small-set queries.

- Strength: Identifies limitations of XOR/same-cluster queries for approximate recovery and proposes AND queries with provable and empirical benefits.

- Strength: Provides a simple, non-adaptive, parallelizable algorithm with transparent decision rule and O(n log(k/δ)) query complexity under noise.

- Strength: Includes initial real-data and crowd experiments suggesting AND queries are easier for annotators.

- Weakness 1 (model choice): Disallowing repeated identical queries is restrictive; authors should justify or analyze models allowing repeats and independent annotators and clarify when repetition helps or hurts.

- Weakness 2 (Thm 1 vs prior work): Discrepancy with connectivity-style results (Θ(n log n)); clarify average- vs worst-case guarantees, decoder use of XOR answers, role of knowing p, and make the proof more transparent.

- Weakness 3 (Thm 3 interpretability): The lower-bound expression is opaque; provide simplified corollaries, plots for common regimes, and intuitive rate–distortion interpretation.

- Weakness 4 (Thm 4 and gap): The AND-achievability bound is hard to parse; give simplified asymptotics, quantify gap to Thm 3 across regimes, and clarify the Poissonization/inclusion–exclusion proof steps.

- Weakness 5 (Fig. 2 comparisons): Current figure compares AND only to same-cluster lower bound; add explicit same-cluster algorithmic curves (e.g., ML over XOR) and clarify axes/legends.

- Weakness 6 (Algorithm 1 practicality): Algorithm requires p or {n_i} and thresholds C_i; explain how to estimate parameters in practice, provide sensitivity/ablation studies, clearer pseudocode, and complexity/runtime details.

- Weakness 7 (Experiments): Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art same-cluster baselines (spectral, ML, adaptive); need systematic parameter sweeps, confidence intervals, and same-cluster results on real data.

- Additional comment: Soften claims about repetition being unhelpful unless supported by worker-model evidence, or provide such evidence.

- Quality: Technically interesting with nontrivial bounds and a simple algorithm, but key proofs are hard to follow and interpretability is limited.

- Clarity: Paper is dense; needs stronger theorem statements, intuition, simplified corollaries, improved figures, and clearer pseudocode.

- Originality: High — the locally encodable source-coding perspective and AND-query advocacy are novel.

- Significance: Potentially high but currently undermined by model restrictions, missing baselines, and unclear optimality gaps.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical perspective, clear identification of XOR limitations, and a simple non-adaptive scheme with promising scaling and some empirical support.

- Arguments against acceptance: Confusion around exact-recovery complexity vs prior work, hard-to-interpret bounds (Thms 3–4), unclear gap to lower bounds, and insufficient experimental baselines/parameter estimation discussion.

- Overall: Promising and original work that requires substantial revisions to clarify assumptions, reconcile with prior results, improve proof readability, interpret bounds, and strengthen empirical comparisons.
