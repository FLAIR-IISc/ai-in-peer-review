- Introduces pose-guided person image generation: synthesize a specific person in a target pose from a single source image and pose skeleton.
- Proposes PG², a two-stage network: Stage I (U-Net) generates a coarse pose-matched image; Stage II (conditional GAN) refines details via a learned difference map.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus reconstruction on the person foreground rather than the background.
- Discriminator in Stage II is conditioned on the source image to enforce identity preservation.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing qualitative and quantitative improvements over baselines.
- Thorough ablation studies validate design choices (pose embedding, pose mask, two-stage vs single-stage).
- Uses multiple metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) and a human perceptual AMT study for balanced evaluation.
- Acknowledges limitations and shows failure cases; notes SSIM can conflict with perceptual quality.
- Minor weakness: pose mask generation is heuristic and ad-hoc (morphological operations).
- Performance depends on the accuracy of an external off-the-shelf pose estimator, risking error propagation.
- Struggles with generating coherent backgrounds; addressed by focusing loss on foreground.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and supported by effective figures and experimental detail.
- Original contribution: explicit keypoint-based pose conditioning and the refinement difference-map design were novel and impactful.
- High significance and potential applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, content creation); reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
