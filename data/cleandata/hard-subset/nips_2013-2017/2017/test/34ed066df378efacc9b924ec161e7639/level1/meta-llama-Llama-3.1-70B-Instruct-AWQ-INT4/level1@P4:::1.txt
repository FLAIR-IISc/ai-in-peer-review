The paper "Pose Guided Person Image Generation" proposes a novel task of generating person images conditioned on a reference image and an intended pose. The authors introduce a two-stage approach, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), to address this task. The first stage focuses on capturing the global structure of the person, while the second stage refines the appearance details.

The authors provide a thorough review of related work in generative image modeling, including Variational Autoencoders (VAE), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Autoregressive models. They also discuss the limitations of existing methods, such as the lack of control over the generation process and the difficulty in generating high-quality images.

The proposed PG2 network consists of two stages: pose integration and image refinement. The first stage uses a U-Net-like architecture to integrate the condition image and target pose, while the second stage uses a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) to refine the appearance details. The authors also propose a novel pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes.

The paper presents extensive experimental results on two person datasets, Market-1501 and DeepFashion. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality person images with convincing details. The authors also conduct a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk, which shows that the generated images can confuse users into thinking they are real.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed method and experimental results. The use of visualizations and examples helps to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
