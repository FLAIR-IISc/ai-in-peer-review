- Introduces PG2, a novel two-stage method (U-Net coarse generation + DCGAN refinement) for pose-guided person image generation.  
- Proposes and evaluates different pose embedding methods and a pose mask loss to focus appearance transfer on the human body.  
- Builds on GAN and conditional generation literature and frames pose transfer as a less-explored conditional generation task.  
- Two-stage design conceptually separates global structure from fine appearance details, aligning with prior image-editing approaches.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear figures and network/motivation explanations.  
- Uses both low-resolution (Market-1501) and high-resolution (DeepFashion) datasets to validate the method.  
- Provides ablation studies (pose embeddings, loss functions, one vs. two-stage) that give insight into component contributions.  
- Quantitative and qualitative experiments support the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Comparison with closely related work (especially [36]) is limited and mostly qualitative.  
- Failure case discussion is brief and lacks detailed analysis of issues like leg generation or gender misrepresentation.  
- Relies on metrics (SSIM, IS) that have known limitations for generative model evaluation; user study partially compensates.  
- Some implementation details are missing (e.g., GAN latent space size).  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with valuable ideas (two-stage framework and pose mask loss) despite noted weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to expand related-work comparisons and provide deeper failure-case analysis in the final version.
