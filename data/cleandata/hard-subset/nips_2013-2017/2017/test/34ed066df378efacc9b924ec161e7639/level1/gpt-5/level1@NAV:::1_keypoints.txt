- Tackles pose-guided person image synthesis using PG2, a two-stage coarse-to-fine system conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Stage-I: U-Net variant integrates appearance with 18 heatmap-encoded keypoints and uses masked L1 to downweight background differences.  
- Stage-II: conditional DCGAN predicts a residual “difference map,” trained with a pairwise discriminator and masked L1.  
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include ablations (heatmaps vs coords, masked loss, one- vs two-stage), SSIM/IS variants, and an AMT user study.  
- Method is technically sound and ablations substantiate design choices (heatmap embedding, masked loss, two-stage residual, pairwise D).  
- Evaluation is weak: relies on SSIM/IS and a brief user study; missing task-relevant metrics like identity (re-ID), pose fidelity (PCK), FID, and LPIPS.  
- Background artifacts are acknowledged but not systematically analyzed or resolved.  
- Lacks comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., pix2pix-style keypoint-to-image, perceptual-loss cGANs) and provides no diversity/stochastic outputs.  
- Paper is generally clear with useful figures, but some details are unclear (chosen λ, mask construction robustness, training stability) and several references incomplete.  
- Originality is incremental: use of keypoint heatmaps and coarse-to-fine residual refinement echoes prior work (StackGAN, residual prediction); masked L1 and pairwise D are reasonable but not novel.  
- Significance: visually compelling results with applications (re-ID, fashion, graphics), but impact would increase with stronger quantitative identity/pose validation and comparisons.  
- Pros: well-motivated task and architecture, solid ablations, effective heatmap use and pairwise conditioning, two datasets and user study.  
- Cons: limited novelty, weak quantitative evaluation for identity/pose, heuristic background handling with artifacts, limited baselines, no diversity.  
- Recommendation/suggestions: weak accept; add re-ID and pose metrics, FID/LPIPS, compare to pix2pix/perceptual baselines, analyze mask sensitivity, consider explicit foreground segmentation or warping modules.
