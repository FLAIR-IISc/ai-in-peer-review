- Task: Pose-guided person image generation — synthesize a photorealistic person image conditioned on a target pose.
- Method: Two-stage PG2 network — Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like generator to produce a coarse pose-matched image with a masked L1 foreground loss.
- Method (cont.): Stage 2 refines the coarse output with a conditional GAN to add high-frequency detail and improve realism.
- Contribution: Applies established conditional-generation components to explicit keypoint-based pose manipulation; novelty mainly in their combination/application.
- Strength — Significance: Well-defined, impactful problem with applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on); pose heatmaps are an effective conditioning signal.
- Strength — Quality: Technically sound coarse-to-fine design, thorough ablations justifying pose embedding, masked loss, and two-stage pipeline; strong qualitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written and figures effectively illustrate architecture and progressive improvements.
- Weakness — Architectural novelty limited: Core components (U-Net, cGAN) are standard, making the contribution incremental.
- Weakness — Evaluation: Quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) do not fully correlate with perceptual quality; some metrics and user-study results (especially on Market-1501) are mixed and need deeper analysis.
