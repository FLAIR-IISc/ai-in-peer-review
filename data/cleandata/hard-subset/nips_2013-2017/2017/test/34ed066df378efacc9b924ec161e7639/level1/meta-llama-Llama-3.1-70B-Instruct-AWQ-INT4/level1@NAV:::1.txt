**Paper Review: Pose Guided Person Image Generation**

**Summary:**
This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that generates person images in arbitrary poses based on a reference image and a target pose. The framework consists of two stages: pose integration and image refinement. The authors evaluate their method on two person datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality person images with convincing details.

**Relation to Previous Work:**
The paper builds upon previous work on generative image modeling, including Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Autoregressive models. The authors also draw inspiration from recent works on pose estimation and image generation conditioned on images.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel task:** The paper proposes a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and a target pose, which is a challenging and interesting problem.
2. **Effective framework:** The two-stage approach, consisting of pose integration and image refinement, is effective in generating high-quality person images.
3. **Pose mask loss:** The proposed pose mask loss is a useful innovation that helps to alleviate the influence of background changes and focus the generation on the human body.
4. **Extensive evaluation:** The authors evaluate their method on two person datasets, providing both qualitative and quantitative results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited generalizability:** The method is specifically designed for person image generation and may not be easily generalizable to other domains.
2. **Dependence on pose estimation:** The method relies on accurate pose estimation, which can be a limiting factor in certain scenarios.
3. **Failure cases:** The authors acknowledge that their method can fail in certain cases, such as when the target pose is significantly different from the reference pose.

**Originality:**
The paper proposes a novel task and a effective framework to address it. The pose mask loss is also a new innovation that contributes to the field of image generation.

**Significance:**
The paper has significant implications for various applications, including movie making, image retrieval, and human pose estimation.

**Quality:**
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The evaluation is thorough, and the results are convincing.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting task.
* The framework is effective in generating high-quality person images.
* The pose mask loss is a useful innovation.

Con:

* The method may not be easily generalizable to other domains.
* The dependence on pose estimation can be a limiting factor.
* The paper could benefit from more analysis on the failure cases.

**Rating:** 8/10

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the limitations of their method and provide more analysis on the failure cases. Additionally, they should consider exploring ways to improve the generalizability of their method to other domains.
