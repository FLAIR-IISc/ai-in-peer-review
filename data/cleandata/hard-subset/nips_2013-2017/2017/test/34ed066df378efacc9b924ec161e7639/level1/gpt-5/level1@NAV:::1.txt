This paper tackles pose-guided person image synthesis by conditioning on a reference image and a target pose. The proposed PG2 is a two-stage coarse-to-fine system: Stage-I integrates pose with appearance via a U-Net variant, encoding 2D keypoints as 18 heatmaps and training with a masked L1 loss to downweight background differences; Stage-II refines details with a conditional DCGAN that predicts a residual “difference map,” trained with a pairwise discriminator on (generated, condition) vs (real, condition) and the same masked L1. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) include ablations on pose embedding (coordinates vs heatmaps), loss (L1 vs mask), and one- vs two-stage training, plus SSIM/IS variants and an AMT study. The approach relates to conditional generative modeling (GANs, CVAEs, PixelCNN), image-to-image translation (Isola et al.), stacked/coarse-to-fine GANs (e.g., StackGAN), and pose estimation for supervision without manual labels (Cao et al.).

Quality: The method is technically sound, and ablations substantiate design choices (heatmap embedding, masked loss, two-stage residual refinement, pairwise D). However, evaluation relies on SSIM/IS and a brief user study; stronger, task-relevant metrics are missing (e.g., identity preservation via person re-ID accuracy, pose fidelity via PCK, FID/LPIPS). Background artifacts are acknowledged but not systematically analyzed. Comparisons to strong baselines such as pix2pix-style keypoint-to-image or perceptual-loss cGANs would strengthen claims.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized with useful figures. Some details could be clearer (chosen λ, mask construction robustness, training stability) and several references are incomplete.

Originality: The overall task is timely; using keypoint heatmaps is standard, and the two-stage, coarse-to-fine refinement with residual learning echoes StackGAN and residual prediction paradigms. The masked L1 and pairwise discriminator are reasonable but incremental.

Significance: Results are visually compelling, and the framework could benefit data augmentation (re-ID), fashion, and graphics. Impact would increase with stronger quantitative identity/pose validation and comparisons.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, well-motivated task and architecture with solid ablations.
- Effective use of pose heatmaps and pairwise conditioning in the discriminator.
- Two datasets, qualitative gains, and user study evidence.

Cons against acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to existing conditional/stacked GAN literature.
- Weak quantitative evaluation for identity and pose fidelity; lack of FID/LPIPS.
- Background handling is heuristic; artifacts persist.
- Limited comparisons to strong baselines; no diversity/stochastic outputs.

Overall: Weak accept. Suggestions: add re-ID and pose accuracy metrics, FID/LPIPS, compare to pix2pix-style baselines with perceptual losses, analyze mask sensitivity, and consider explicit foreground segmentation or warping modules.
