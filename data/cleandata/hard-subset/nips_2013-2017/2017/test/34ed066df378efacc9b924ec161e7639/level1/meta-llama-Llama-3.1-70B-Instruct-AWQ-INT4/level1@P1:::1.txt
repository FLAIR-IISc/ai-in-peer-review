**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The generation framework PG consists of two key stages: pose integration and image refinement. In the first stage, a U-Net-like network integrates the target pose with the person image to generate an initial but coarse image of the person with the target pose. The second stage refines the initial result by training a U-Net-like generator in an adversarial way. The model uses a pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes and to focus on transferring the human body appearance.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose, which is a valuable contribution to the field of computer vision.
* The two-stage approach to address the task is well-designed, and the use of a pose mask loss is effective in alleviating the influence of background changes.
* The experimental results on both DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper provides a thorough comparison with related works and discusses the advantages and limitations of the proposed method.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the pose estimation is accurate, which may not always be the case in real-world scenarios.
* The method may not perform well when the background is complex or cluttered, as shown in the Market-1501 dataset.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity and memory requirements of the proposed method.
* Some of the generated images may still have artifacts or inconsistencies, as shown in the failure examples.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work. However, there are some minor issues with the writing, such as the use of abbreviations without proper explanation (e.g., "CE" and "HME" in Section 4.2).

**Clarity**

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel task and a new approach to address it, which is a significant contribution to the field of computer vision. The use of a pose mask loss and the two-stage approach are also original and effective.

**Significance**

The proposed method has the potential to be used in various applications, such as movie making, human pose estimation, and image retrieval.
