- Novel task: Pose-guided person image generation with explicit keypoint-based pose control offers greater flexibility than viewpoint-based approaches.
- Two-stage framework: U-Net-like network for global structure followed by DCGAN refinement for appearance details is well-motivated.
- Pose mask loss: Introducing a pose mask loss effectively focuses generation on the person's appearance.
- Experimental validation: Results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion and ablation studies demonstrate method effectiveness.
- Clear presentation: Paper is well-written with helpful diagrams and organization.
- User study: Human evaluation strengthens confidence in generated image quality.
- Limited comparisons: More thorough baselines (e.g., image warping/deformation-based methods) are needed.
- Weak failure analysis: Insufficient discussion and analysis of failure cases, especially for complex poses and gender imbalance.
- Metric limitations: SSIM and IS are insufficient; better perceptual or pose-accuracy metrics are needed.
- Pose estimator dependency: Reliance on an external pose estimator introduces error that constrains final performance.
- Reproducibility concerns: Missing implementation details and hyperparameters hinder exact replication.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel problem, solid technical approach, strong experiments, and useful ablations support acceptance.
- Rejection arguments: Limited related-work comparison, weak failure discussion, estimator dependency, and suboptimal quantitative evaluation argue against acceptance.
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance with revisions.
- Suggested revisions: Expand related work to include deformation/image-manipulation methods, analyze failure cases, explore alternative metrics, and provide implementation details.
