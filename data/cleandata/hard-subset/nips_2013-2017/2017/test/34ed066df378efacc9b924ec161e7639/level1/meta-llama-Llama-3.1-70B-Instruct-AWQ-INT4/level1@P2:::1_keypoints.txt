- Proposes PG2: a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation network (pose integration via U-Net-like net, refinement via DCGAN variant).
- Novel pose embedding using 18 heatmaps and a pose mask loss to reduce background influence.
- Clear, concise related work overview and explanation of method differences.
- Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion demonstrate effectiveness with qualitative and quantitative results.
- Includes an Amazon Mechanical Turk user study to evaluate photo-realism.
- Strength: Introduces a valuable novel task—conditioning image generation on a reference image and a target pose.
- Strength: Two-stage approach is effective and well-motivated for pose transfer.
- Strength: Pose embedding and pose mask loss are innovative and justified.
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-presented.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of limitations and failure cases (e.g., Figure 6).
- Weakness: User study is not comprehensive—needs more participants and deeper evaluation metrics.
- Weakness: Missing implementation details such as network architectures and hyperparameters.
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed discussion of potential applications.
- Originality: Novel task, two-stage pipeline, and pose embedding/mask loss are original contributions.
- Significance: Method could impact face editing, filmmaking, image retrieval, and synthetic data for pose estimation.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written; figures and tables effectively illustrate points.
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel task, innovative two-stage design, original pose methods, strong experimental results.
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper limitations analysis, a more comprehensive user study, and fuller implementation details.
