- Paper introduces PG2: a two-stage framework (U-Net-like coarse generator + conditional GAN refiner) for pose-guided person image synthesis, with a pose mask loss to focus on the human body.
- Novelty: tackles an underexplored pose-conditioned person generation problem and proposes a well-motivated two-stage design and pose mask loss.
- Technical soundness: method builds on GANs and U-Net, uses difference maps in stage two to speed convergence and improve detail refinement.
- Experimental validation: extensive qualitative and quantitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501, including mask-SSIM, mask-Inception Score, baseline comparisons, and a user study.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and supported by effective figures and examples.
- Limited baseline comparison: comparisons are mainly to VariGAN; more state-of-the-art GAN-based baselines are needed to contextualize improvements.
- Dataset bias: training data imbalances (gender, rare/complex poses) produce failure cases; mitigation strategies (augmentation, balanced sampling) are not explored.
- Generality concerns: method relies on pose keypoints and is tailored to single-person images; applicability to other object categories, occlusions, or multi-person scenes is unclear.
- Quantitative metrics: reliance on SSIM and IS is limited; additional perceptual metrics (e.g., FID) and hyperparameter ablations (e.g., λ) would provide deeper insight.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, introduces innovative components (two-stage framework and pose mask loss), and demonstrates strong empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to recent SOTA and failure cases on rare poses/imbalanced data raise concerns about robustness and generalizability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand baseline comparisons and address dataset bias issues.
