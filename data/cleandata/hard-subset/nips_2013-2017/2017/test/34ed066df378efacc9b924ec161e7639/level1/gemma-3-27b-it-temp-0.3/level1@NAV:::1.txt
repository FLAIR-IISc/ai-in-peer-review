## Review of Pose Guided Person Image Generation

This paper proposes a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for generating person images in arbitrary poses given a reference image and a target pose. The approach explicitly incorporates pose information through heatmap encoding and utilizes a U-Net-like architecture for initial pose integration followed by a DCGAN-based refinement stage. The authors address the challenge of pose transfer by dividing it into global structure and appearance detail learning, employing a masked L1 loss to focus on the human body and an adversarial loss for sharper results. 

The work builds upon the recent surge in GAN-based image generation (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Radford et al., 2015) and conditional image generation (Mirza & Osindero, 2014; Reed et al., 2016). It differentiates itself by explicitly guiding generation with pose keypoints, a more flexible representation than view-specific labels (Chen et al., 2017) or single viewpoints (Zhao et al., 2017). The two-stage approach echoes strategies used in other image manipulation tasks (e.g., Dosovitskiy et al., 2016) to decompose complex problems.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The task of pose-guided person image generation is a valuable contribution, with potential applications in areas like movie making and data augmentation for pose estimation.
* **Clear Methodology:** The two-stage approach is well-motivated and clearly explained. The use of pose heatmaps and the masked L1 loss are sensible design choices.
* **Strong Experimental Results:** The results on both datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion) demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, both qualitatively and quantitatively. The ablation studies effectively highlight the contributions of different components.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The paper includes a user study, providing further validation of the generated image quality.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison:** While the authors compare to a related work (Zhang et al., 2017), the field of image manipulation is rapidly evolving. A more comprehensive comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods would strengthen the paper.
* **Failure Cases:** The discussion of failure cases is limited. A more in-depth analysis of the limitations of the approach would be beneficial.
* **Dataset Bias:** The authors acknowledge potential dataset biases, but a more detailed discussion of how these biases might affect the results would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a novel and important problem.
* The proposed method is well-designed and effectively implemented.
* The experimental results are strong and support the claims made by the authors.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The comparison to related work is limited.
* The analysis of failure cases is superficial.




**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of image generation. The proposed method is novel, well-designed, and effectively implemented. While the comparison to related work could be more comprehensive, the overall quality of the paper is high. I recommend acceptance.
