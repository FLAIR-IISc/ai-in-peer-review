- Proposes a novel task: pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and explicit pose keypoints.  
- Introduces PG2, a two-stage framework: stage 1 generates a coarse pose-aligned image, stage 2 refines details with adversarial training and a difference map.  
- Represents pose as keypoint heatmaps and integrates them with a U-Net-like network for pose alignment.  
- Proposes a pose mask loss to focus learning on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Uses a difference map approach in refinement to speed up training.  
- Thorough experimental validation on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) and user studies.  
- Strong ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides reproducible implementation details and illustrative figures.  
- Original contributions include the task formulation, pose mask loss, and the two-stage separation of structure and detail generation.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over relevant prior work, producing more photo-realistic details.  
- Limitations in handling complex, cluttered backgrounds (notably on Market-1501) affecting user study results.  
- Does not analyze how pose estimation errors propagate through the pipeline despite relying on an external pose estimator.  
- Two-stage approach has similarities to prior image-to-image translation work, needing clearer differentiation.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear performance with multiple people in scenes and with rare/unseen poses; some failure cases shown.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, recommend acceptance — novel, well-validated, and practically significant despite minor limitations.
