- Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage framework: stage-I integrates pose (heatmaps) with the reference image (U-Net) to produce a coarse pose-aligned image; stage-II refines it with a conditional GAN producing a difference map.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Uses pose heatmaps instead of raw coordinates and explicitly conditions on pose keypoints, separating structure learning from appearance refinement.  
- Stage-II’s difference-map formulation enables faster convergence and sharper detail recovery.  
- Strong technical soundness with comprehensive ablation studies (pose embeddings, loss choices, one- vs two-stage).  
- Quantitative validation using SSIM and Inception Score plus AMT user studies supports the results.  
- Qualitative improvements are clearly illustrated in figures (notably Fig.4 and Fig.5), showing effectiveness of the pose mask loss.  
- Implementation and training details are sufficiently thorough for reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; Fig.2 provides a helpful overview of the method.  
- Minor clarity issue: the rationale for removing the fully-connected layer in stage-II is briefly explained and could be elaborated.  
- Novel contribution: formulating pose-guided person image generation with a new two-stage approach and pose-heatmap input.  
- Significant practical applications (film, pose estimation, re-identification) and a useful paradigm for complex image synthesis tasks.  
- Demonstrates clear improvements over one-stage methods and the most similar prior work [36]; user study shows 14.9% of refined results fooled humans.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation addressing a meaningful gap in pose-based image generation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation combining quantitative metrics and user studies.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of how pose-estimation errors (they use [2]) affect generation quality.  
- Weakness: failure cases (Fig.6) are acknowledged but not deeply analyzed to pinpoint limitations.  
- Weakness: could better connect to more recent pose-estimation literature beyond [2].  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, novel, well-validated contribution with only minor addressable weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task formulation, effective two-stage design and pose mask loss, strong experimental evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance (minor): need more detail on architecture choice (FC removal) and analysis of pose-estimation error propagation.
