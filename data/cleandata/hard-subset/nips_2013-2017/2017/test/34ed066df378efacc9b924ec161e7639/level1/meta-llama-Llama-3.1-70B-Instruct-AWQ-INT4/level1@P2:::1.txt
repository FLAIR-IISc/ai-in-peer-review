This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The authors tackle this challenging task by dividing the problem into two stages: pose integration and image refinement. The first stage uses a U-Net-like network to integrate the target pose with the person image, while the second stage refines the initial result using a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) model.

The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and existing methods. They also propose a novel pose embedding method, which uses 18 heatmaps to represent the pose information, and a pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes.

The experimental results on two person datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion) demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors provide both qualitative and quantitative results, including comparisons with alternative pose embedding methods and losses. They also perform a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk to evaluate the photo-realism of the generated images.

Strengths:

1. The authors propose a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose, which is of great value for many applications.
2. The two-stage approach is effective in addressing the challenging task of pose transfer, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the advantages of this approach.
3. The proposed pose embedding method and pose mask loss are innovative and well-motivated.
4. The experimental results are comprehensive and well-presented, including both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
