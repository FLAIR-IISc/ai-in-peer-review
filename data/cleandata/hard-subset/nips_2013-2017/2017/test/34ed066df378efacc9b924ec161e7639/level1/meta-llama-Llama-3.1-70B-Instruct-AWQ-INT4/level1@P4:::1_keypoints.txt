- Proposes a novel task: generate person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.
- Introduces PG2, a two-stage network: stage 1 integrates pose and reference (U-Net-like), stage 2 refines appearance (DCGAN variant).
- Proposes a pose mask loss to reduce background influence during training.
- Provides a thorough related-work review (VAE, GANs, autoregressive) and highlights limitations of prior methods.
- Extensive experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion show high-quality, detailed generated images.
- User study on Amazon Mechanical Turk indicates generated images can confuse humans into thinking they are real.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and supported with useful visualizations and examples.
- Strength: novel task and well-designed two-stage approach addressing pose transfer challenges.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating method effectiveness.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the pose mask loss and its specific impact.
- Weakness: lacks detail on hyperparameter choices and their effect on results.
- Weakness: user study is limited in size and depth; more comprehensive evaluation needed.
- Originality: novel combination of existing techniques and a new two-stage pipeline for pose-guided generation.
- Significance: addresses a challenging vision problem with practical applications (e.g., film, image retrieval).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address pose mask analysis, hyperparameter insights, and a more comprehensive user study.
- Rating: 8/10.
