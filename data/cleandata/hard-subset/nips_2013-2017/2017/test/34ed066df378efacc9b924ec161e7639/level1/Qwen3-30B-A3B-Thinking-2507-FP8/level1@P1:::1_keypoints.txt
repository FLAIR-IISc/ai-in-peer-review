Summary: Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage (pose integration + image refinement) framework with a pose mask loss to generate person images in arbitrary poses; evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501.

Quality: Strong technical quality with sound methodology (U-Net, adversarial training), thorough ablation studies, quantitative metrics, qualitative results, and a user study; limitations and failure cases are acknowledged.

Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear problem statement and reproducible architecture; minor ambiguity in how pose estimation integrates with the main framework.

Originality: Novel task formulation and pose mask loss; more expressive than prior multi-view approaches and well-positioned against related work.

Significance: Addresses an important application problem, produces high-quality images, advances state-of-the-art, and has practical value for areas like film, pose estimation, and retrieval.

Strength — Novel task formulation of pose-guided person image generation.

Strength — Effective two-stage approach that decomposes pose and appearance generation.

Strength — Pose mask loss effectively reduces background interference.

Strength — Comprehensive evaluation across datasets with multiple metrics.

Strength — Clear qualitative results demonstrating method effectiveness.

Strength — Strong user study validating generated image quality.

Weakness — Not fully evaluated on extreme pose changes (e.g., full rotations, unusual poses).

Weakness — Reliance on an external pose estimator creates a potential failure point.

Weakness — Computational efficiency of the two-stage pipeline vs. one-stage is not analyzed.

Weakness — Failure cases (rare poses, gender imbalance) need deeper discussion.

Weakness — Paper could better justify why two-stage is necessary beyond being effective.

Pro-acceptance — Novel, well-motivated task with strong technical contributions and a useful pose mask loss.

Pro-acceptance — Comprehensive experimental validation and clear differentiation from prior work.

Pro-acceptance — Demonstrated effectiveness in both quantitative and qualitative evaluations; well-written.

Contra-acceptance — Dependency on pose estimation and specific failure cases may limit robustness.

Contra-acceptance — Two-stage approach’s generalizability and computational cost are unclear.

Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution with convincing results and clear recommendation for acceptance despite some limitations.
