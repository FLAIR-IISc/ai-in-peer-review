- Introduces a novel task: single-image, pose-guided photorealistic person image generation using explicit keypoint control.

- Proposes PG², a two-stage pipeline: Stage I (G1) U-Net-like generator for coarse pose-accurate structure using 18-keypoint heatmaps; Stage II (G2 + conditional D) refines details in a conditional GAN.

- Introduces a pose mask loss (weighted L1) to focus reconstruction on the person foreground and mitigate background differences.

- Uses a conditional discriminator that compares (generated, source) vs (real, source) pairs to enforce identity preservation.

- Evaluates on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with qualitative results, SSIM/IS (and masked variants), and a user study to demonstrate effectiveness.

- Provides comprehensive ablation studies validating heatmap-based pose embedding, pose mask loss, and the two-stage coarse-to-fine design.

- Strength: novel, fine-grained skeletal control with clear application potential (data augmentation, virtual try-on, re-ID, etc.).

- Strength: methodologically sound coarse-to-fine decomposition with convincing empirical support.

- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and well-illustrated architecture and motivations.

- Weakness: core architectural components are derivative (U-Net, PatchGAN, coarse-to-fine GAN); main novelty is their combination and application; pose mask loss is a simple heuristic.

- Weakness: quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) are imperfect for GANs; masked metrics are nonstandard and the user study is relatively small.

- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases—handling complex textures, accessories, severe occlusion, and potential information bottleneck from G1’s fully connected layer.

- Question/suggestion: provide more detail on pose mask MB construction, sensitivity to morphological parameters, and consider using parsing/segmentation for better masks.

- Question/suggestion: explore alternatives to G1’s fully connected layer (dilated convolutions, attention) to preserve spatial detail while sharing information.

- Question/suggestion: clarify the specific role of feeding the source image IA into G2 alongside ÎB1—does it primarily reinforce identity?

- Question/suggestion: explain and investigate why the user study favored the one-stage model over the two-stage model (background artifacts from G2) and discuss person/background disentanglement.

- Conclusion: strong, well-executed paper whose contributions outweigh weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
