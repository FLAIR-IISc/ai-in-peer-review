- Introduces PG2, a novel two-stage framework (pose integration then image refinement) for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses.  
- Key innovation: pose mask loss that focuses generation on the human body, reducing background interference.  
- Uses explicit pose keypoints as conditioning, distinguishing it from prior methods that use abstract pose/view descriptors.  
- Strong technical quality with comprehensive ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and one- vs two-stage designs.  
- Two-stage refinement with adversarial training yields visually superior results compared to one-stage alternatives.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on high- and low-resolution datasets (DeepFashion, Market-1501) with quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) and user studies.  
- Acknowledges limitations of SSIM/IS for perceptual quality; addresses this via visual results and user study.  
- Demonstrates clear originality and practical significance for applications like filmmaking, pose estimation, and person re-identification.  
- Minor clarity improvements suggested: more detail on pose mask generation and deeper analysis of failure cases.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel contribution and recommend acceptance to NIPS.
