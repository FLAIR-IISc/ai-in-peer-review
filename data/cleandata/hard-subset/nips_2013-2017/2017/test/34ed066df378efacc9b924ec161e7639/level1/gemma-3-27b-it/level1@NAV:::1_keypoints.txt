- Introduces a novel task: generate person images in arbitrary poses from a reference image and target pose.  
- Proposes a two-stage model: pose integration via a U-Net-like network to produce a coarse image, then refinement with a conditional DCGAN for detail and realism.  
- Uses keypoint heatmaps to represent pose information, providing explicit and controllable pose conditioning.  
- Introduces a masked L1 loss to focus learning on the person’s body and reduce background influence.  
- Builds on prior GAN and image-to-image translation work, distinguishing itself by explicit pose conditioning.  
- Strength: clear motivation and a reasonable two-stage decomposition of the problem.  
- Strength: pose heatmaps and masked L1 are effective design choices.  
- Empirical results: quantitative and qualitative improvements over baselines (including VariGAN) on two datasets.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art generative models (e.g., StyleGAN, BigGAN).  
- Weakness: ablation studies helpful but could be more comprehensive.  
- Weakness: user study (AMT) has potential bias and limited controls.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and analysis of failure cases and incomplete implementation details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand SOTA comparisons and provide deeper failure-case analysis.
