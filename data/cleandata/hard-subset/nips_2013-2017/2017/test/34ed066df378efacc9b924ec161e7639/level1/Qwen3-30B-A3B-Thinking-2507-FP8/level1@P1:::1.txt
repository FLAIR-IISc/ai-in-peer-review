

# Peer Review: Pose Guided Person Image Generation

## Summary
This paper proposes a novel two-stage framework for generating person images in arbitrary poses based on a reference image and target pose. The core contribution is the Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), which divides the pose transfer problem into two stages: pose integration (stage-I) and image refinement (stage-II). The authors introduce a novel pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body rather than background, and they demonstrate their method on both high-resolution fashion images (DeepFashion) and low-resolution re-identification images (Market-1501). The work builds on recent NIPS advances in conditional image generation (e.g., Isola et al. 2017, Mirza & Osindero 2014, Zhao et al. 2017) but addresses a more specific and challenging problem of pose-guided person image generation, which has not been adequately explored in prior NIPS work.

## Quality
The paper demonstrates strong technical quality. The two-stage approach is well-motivated by the difficulty of simultaneously generating correct poses and detailed appearance in a single model. The pose mask loss is a clever solution to the background interference problem, and the authors provide thorough ablation studies showing its effectiveness. The methodology is sound, with appropriate use of U-Net architectures, adversarial training, and careful loss design. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score), qualitative results, and a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk. The authors also acknowledge limitations and show failure cases, which demonstrates scientific honesty.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem statement is clear from the outset, and the two-stage framework is logically motivated. Figures 2-5 effectively illustrate the methodology and results, with Figure 4 providing particularly compelling qualitative comparisons. The network architecture is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility. The only minor clarity issue is that the connection between the pose estimation step and the main framework could be slightly more explicit, but this is a minor point given the paper's overall clarity.

## Originality
The task of conditioning image generation on both a reference image and a target pose is novel in the context of person image generation, and the paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work. While Zhao et al. (2017) on multi-view person image synthesis is the closest related work, the authors demonstrate that their pose-guided approach is more expressive and flexible than conditioning on viewpoint labels alone. The pose mask loss is also a novel contribution that addresses a specific challenge in this domain. The paper thoroughly reviews related work from NIPS and other venues, ensuring the novelty claim is well-supported.

## Significance
This work addresses a significant application problem with potential impact in areas like movie making, human pose estimation, and image retrieval. The ability to generate realistic person images in arbitrary poses has clear practical value. The results demonstrate that the method produces high-quality, photo-realistic images with convincing details, particularly when compared to the state-of-the-art work of Zhao et al. The two-stage approach also provides a valuable framework for solving complex image synthesis problems that require both structural and appearance fidelity.

## Strengths
1. Novel task formulation of pose-guided person image generation
2. Effective two-stage approach that successfully decomposes a challenging problem
3. Pose mask loss is a clever and well-justified solution to background interference
4. Comprehensive evaluation across two different datasets with multiple metrics
5. Clear qualitative results demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach
6. Strong user study that validates the quality of generated images

## Weaknesses
1. The paper doesn't fully address how the method handles extreme pose changes (e.g., full body rotations or highly unusual poses)
2. The pose estimation step (using an external pose estimator) could be a limitation if the pose estimation fails
3. The computational efficiency of the two-stage approach compared to a one-stage approach is not thoroughly analyzed
4. The failure cases (Figure 6) show limitations for rare poses and gender imbalance, which could be more thoroughly discussed
5. The paper could better explain why the two-stage approach is necessary rather than just effective

## Arguments for Acceptance
- Novel task with clear motivation and well-justified methodology
- Strong technical contribution with the pose mask loss
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Clear differentiation from previous work, particularly Zhao et al.
- Demonstrated effectiveness in both quantitative and qualitative terms
- Well-written and well-organized paper

## Arguments against Acceptance
- The pose estimation dependency could be seen as a limitation
- Some failure cases reveal specific weaknesses that might be concerning
- The two-stage approach might not generalize to other image generation tasks as easily as a unified approach
- The computational cost of the two-stage approach isn't fully addressed

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of conditional image generation. The pose-guided person image generation task is well-motivated and the two-stage approach with the pose mask loss is a clever solution to a challenging problem. The experimental results are thorough and convincing, with clear advantages over previous work. The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a clear contribution to the field. While there are some limitations to address (particularly regarding extreme poses and computational efficiency), these do not detract from the paper's overall quality and significance. The work advances the state of the art in conditional image generation and has clear potential for practical applications.

I recommend acceptance. This paper represents a solid contribution that aligns well with NIPS' focus on high-quality, innovative machine learning research.
