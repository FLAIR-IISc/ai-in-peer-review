- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that generates person images from a reference image and a target pose using a two-stage pipeline: pose integration and image refinement.  
- Builds on prior generative modeling (VAEs, GANs, autoregressive) and pose-conditioned image generation literature.  
- Novel task: conditioning generation on a reference image plus target pose is challenging and interesting.  
- Effective framework: two-stage approach yields high-quality person images with convincing details.  
- Pose mask loss: reduces background influence and focuses generation on the human body.  
- Extensive evaluation: qualitative and quantitative results on two person datasets support effectiveness.  
- Limited generalizability: method is tailored to person images and may not transfer easily to other domains.  
- Dependence on pose estimation: requires accurate pose inputs, which can limit performance in real scenarios.  
- Failure cases: struggles when target pose differs greatly from the reference pose; more analysis needed.  
- Originality: introduces a novel task and the pose mask loss as a useful innovation.  
- Significance: relevant for applications like filmmaking, image retrieval, and human pose estimation.  
- Quality: well-written paper with clear explanations and thorough evaluations.  
- Recommendation: rating 8/10, accept with minor revisions to address limitations, analyze failures, and explore generalizability.
