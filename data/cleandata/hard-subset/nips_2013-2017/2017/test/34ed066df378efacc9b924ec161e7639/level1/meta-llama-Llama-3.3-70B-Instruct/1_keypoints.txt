- Proposes PG2: synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.
- Uses a two-stage pipeline: U-Net-like network for coarse generation, conditional DCGAN for refinement.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence on person synthesis.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains architecture and loss functions.
- Provides extensive experiments on two person datasets demonstrating high-quality, detailed results.
- Shows improvement over baseline approaches, including a one-stage model and a different pose embedding.
- Strength: defines a novel conditioning image-generation task with many potential applications.
- Strength: two-stage approach balances global structure and fine appearance details.
- Strength: pose mask loss effectively alleviates background change issues.
- Weakness: lacks direct comparisons with existing state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: second-stage GAN may suffer mode collapse, reducing output diversity.
- Weakness: requires large amounts of training data, limiting use in data-scarce settings.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
- Requested revisions: add direct comparisons, discuss limitations (mode collapse, data needs), and provide implementation/training details (hyperparameters and compute).
