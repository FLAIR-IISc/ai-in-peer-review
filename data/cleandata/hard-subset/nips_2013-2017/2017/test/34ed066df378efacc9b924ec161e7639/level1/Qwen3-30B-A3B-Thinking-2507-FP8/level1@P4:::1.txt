

# Review of "Pose Guided Person Image Generation"

This paper proposes a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses given a reference image and target pose. The authors address the challenging task of person pose transfer by dividing it into pose integration (stage-I) and image refinement (stage-II). Stage-I integrates pose information (represented as heatmaps of keypoints) with the reference image using a U-Net-like architecture to generate a coarse pose-aligned image. The authors introduce a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body rather than background. Stage-II refines the coarse result using a conditional GAN that generates a difference map between the coarse result and target image, enabling faster convergence and sharper details.

The paper relates to previous work on conditional image generation (e.g., [32, 18, 24, 22, 23, 36]) but makes a clear distinction by explicitly conditioning on pose keypoints rather than abstract attributes or viewpoints. It builds on GAN-based approaches like [6, 21] while introducing a novel two-stage strategy to handle the complexity of pose transfer.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates strong technical soundness. The two-stage framework effectively addresses the challenge of simultaneously learning pose structure and appearance details. The authors provide comprehensive ablation studies comparing different pose embeddings (coordinate vs. heatmap), loss functions (standard L1 vs. pose mask loss), and the two-stage vs. one-stage approaches. Quantitative results using SSIM and Inception Score, along with user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk, provide robust validation. The pose mask loss is well-motivated and effectively addresses background interference, as evidenced by the qualitative improvements in Figure 4. The authors carefully acknowledge limitations, including failure cases related to rare poses and gender imbalances (Figure 6). The implementation details are sufficiently thorough for reproducibility.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The method section provides adequate detail about the network architectures and loss functions, with Figure 2 providing a helpful visual overview. The experimental setup is clearly described, including dataset specifics, training parameters, and evaluation metrics. The figures (particularly Figures 4 and 5) effectively illustrate the strengths of the proposed approach. One minor weakness is the brief explanation for removing the fully-connected layer in stage-II (3.2), which could be elaborated to help readers understand the design choice.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution by formulating and solving the specific problem of pose-guided person image generation. The two-stage framework (pose integration followed by image refinement) represents a novel approach to this challenging task. The pose mask loss is a creative solution to the background interference problem, and the use of pose heatmaps as input (rather than raw coordinates) is a meaningful contribution. The authors properly contextualize their work against previous conditional image generation approaches, particularly highlighting the novelty of explicit pose conditioning compared to [36] and other related work. The task itself (person image generation conditioned on pose) appears to be novel for NIPS 2017.

**Significance:** The proposed method has clear practical applications in movie making, human pose estimation, and person re-identification. The results demonstrate significant improvements over one-stage approaches and the most similar prior work [36], particularly in generating photo-realistic details while maintaining correct pose structure. The two-stage framework provides a useful paradigm for complex image synthesis tasks that require both structural and appearance fidelity. The user study results (Table 2) confirm that the generated images are convincingly realistic, with 14.9% of refined results being misclassified as real by human subjects on DeepFashion.

**Strengths:**
- Clear problem formulation addressing a meaningful gap in pose-based image generation
- Novel two-stage framework that effectively separates pose structure learning from detail refinement
- Pose mask loss effectively addresses background interference
- Comprehensive evaluation with both quantitative metrics and user studies
- Strong comparison against the most relevant prior work [36]

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of how pose estimation errors might affect generation quality (they use [2] for pose estimation but don't analyze this pipeline)
- Brief explanation for removing the fully-connected layer in stage-II
- Failure cases in Figure 6 are acknowledged but not deeply analyzed to identify specific limitations
- The connection to recent pose estimation work beyond [2] could be strengthened

**Recommendation:** This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of conditional image generation with a novel approach to pose-guided person image synthesis. The technical soundness, thorough evaluation, and clear novelty make it a strong candidate for NIPS 2017. The minor weaknesses noted do not detract from the paper's overall quality and contribution. I recommend acceptance.

**Arguments for acceptance:** Novel task formulation, clear two-stage approach with effective pose mask loss, strong experimental results on two datasets, comprehensive ablation studies, practical applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:** Minor technical details could be expanded (e.g., fully-connected layer removal), limited analysis of pose estimation error propagation.
