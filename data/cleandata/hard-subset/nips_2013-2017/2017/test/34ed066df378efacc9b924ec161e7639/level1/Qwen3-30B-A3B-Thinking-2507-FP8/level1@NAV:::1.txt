

This paper introduces a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses based on a reference image and target pose. The approach divides the challenging pose transfer task into pose integration (Stage I) and image refinement (Stage II), with a key innovation being the pose mask loss that focuses generation on the human body rather than background. The work builds on recent advances in conditional GANs [10, 21, 32] and pose estimation [2], but significantly extends them by using explicit pose keypoints as conditioning information rather than abstract pose descriptions, which distinguishes it from related work like [36] that uses viewpoint descriptions.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with comprehensive ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and single vs. two-stage approaches. The pose mask loss effectively addresses background interference, and the two-stage refinement with adversarial training produces visually superior results compared to one-stage alternatives. The experimental evaluation on both high-resolution (DeepFashion) and low-resolution (Market-1501) datasets is thorough, with quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) and user studies providing convincing evidence of the method's effectiveness.

The work shows significant originality in its pose-guided approach and two-stage architecture, with clear differentiation from previous person image generation methods. While [36] also addresses multi-view synthesis, PG2's use of explicit pose information leads to substantially better quality results as shown in Figure 5. The significance is clear for applications in movie making, pose estimation, and person re-identification.

Some minor clarity improvements would strengthen the paper: a more detailed explanation of pose mask generation (Section 3.1) and deeper analysis of failure cases (Section 5). The quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) are well-chosen but the paper rightly notes their limitations in capturing perceptual quality, which is well addressed by the visual results and user study.

Overall, this is a high-quality contribution that advances the state-of-the-art in conditional image generation with clear technical innovation and practical significance. The two-stage approach with pose mask loss is both novel and effective, making this an excellent fit for NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
