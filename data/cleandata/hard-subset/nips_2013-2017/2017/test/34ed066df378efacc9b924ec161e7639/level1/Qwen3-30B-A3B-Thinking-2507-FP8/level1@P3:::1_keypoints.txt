- Proposes PG2: a two-stage pose-guided person image generation framework (Stage-I coarse structure with pose embedding, Stage-II GAN-based refinement).  
- Key innovations: pose heatmap embedding, pose mask loss to focus on body regions, and using difference maps in Stage-II for faster convergence.  
- Stage-I uses a U-Net to integrate reference image and target pose to produce a coarse output.  
- Stage-II refines Stage-I output via a GAN that learns residual details rather than generating from scratch.  
- Ablation studies show heatmap embedding, pose mask loss, and two-stage design each improve results over one-stage alternatives.  
- Evaluation is thorough: experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with SSIM, Inception Score, qualitative results, and an AMT user study.  
- Pose mask loss effectively reduces background interference and is well-justified methodologically.  
- Related work is well-covered and distinctions from prior pose-transfer and image-to-image methods are made.  
- Limited novelty: overall framework relies on existing components (U-Net, GAN) and could better clarify differences from Pix2Pix-like approaches.  
- Failure cases (inconsistent limbs, gender errors) indicate significant limitations not fully analyzed or mitigated.  
- Quantitative evaluation is incomplete: SSIM is insufficient and standard perceptual metrics like FID are missing.  
- Computational cost of the two-stage approach is not discussed, raising concerns about practical efficiency.  
- Should discuss dataset biases (e.g., gender imbalance) as potential causes of failure modes and remedies (more data or architectural changes).  
- Acceptance pros: clear problem, strong experiments and ablations, useful pose mask loss, and demonstrable human-perceived improvements.  
- Acceptance cons: incremental novelty, unaddressed failure cases, missing modern GAN comparisons and standard evaluation metrics.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed contribution with convincing results; recommend acceptance despite being somewhat incremental.
