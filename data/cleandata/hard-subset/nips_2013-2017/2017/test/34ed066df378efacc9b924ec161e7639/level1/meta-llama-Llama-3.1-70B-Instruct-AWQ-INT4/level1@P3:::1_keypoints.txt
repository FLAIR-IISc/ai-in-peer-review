- Summary: Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation (PG) with a two-stage pipeline—U-Net-like pose integration to produce a coarse image, then adversarial U-Net refinement—tested on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, yielding high-quality pose-guided person images.

- Related work: Provides thorough review of VAE/GAN/autoregressive and conditioned generation literature, highlighting novelty of conditioning on both a reference image and a target pose.

- Strength 1: Novel task definition of image generation conditioned on a reference image plus a target pose.

- Strength 2: Two-stage approach effectively captures global structure and refines convincing image details.

- Strength 3: Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence and focus pose transfer on the human body.

- Strength 4: Extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two person datasets demonstrating effectiveness.

- Weakness 1: Limited generalizability — evaluated only on two person datasets, unclear transfer to other datasets/tasks.

- Weakness 2: Depends on external pose estimation for keypoints, which may be inaccurate and affect performance.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods beyond a one-stage baseline and a related multi-view work.

- Originality: Two-stage pose-guided generation framework and the pose mask loss represent novel contributions.

- Significance: Potential applications in filmmaking, human pose estimation, and image retrieval; empirical results indicate practical promise.

- Pro acceptance: Novel task and effective two-stage method, useful pose mask loss, thorough related work and experiments.

- Con acceptance: Limited dataset evaluation, reliance on pose estimation, and insufficient comparisons to other SOTA methods.

- Rating: Reviewer score 8/10 — strong and well-written work but limited by generalizability and comparison gaps.
