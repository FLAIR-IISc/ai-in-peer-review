- Proposal: PG2 — a novel two-stage method using a U-Net for coarse pose transfer and a DCGAN for refinement to generate person images in arbitrary poses.
- Contributions: clear task formulation, exploration of pose integration methods, two-stage generation strategy, and a pose mask loss.
- Strength: novel and well-motivated task with practical applications (movies, data augmentation).
- Strength: two-stage approach effectively separates global pose structure from fine appearance details.
- Strength: pose mask loss focuses generation on the person and mitigates background influence.
- Strength: quantitative and qualitative results on re-identification and fashion datasets show plausible pose transfers.
- Strength: thorough ablation studies evaluating pose embeddings, loss functions, and model configurations.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and experimental setup.
- Weakness: limited comparison to related work (e.g., only a single example comparison to Zhao et al. 2017).
- Weakness: discussion of failure cases is brief; lacks analysis of causes (e.g., missing limbs, gender errors) and remedies.
- Weakness: method depends on an external pose estimator, and the impact of estimator errors is not well explored.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on SSIM as an evaluation metric; perceptual metrics like FID or more user studies are needed.
- Weakness: sensitivity of Stage II hyperparameter (lambda) is only briefly discussed, reducing reproducibility guidance.
- For acceptance: new task, effective method, strong results across datasets, and informative ablation studies.
- Against acceptance: incomplete related-work comparison, limited failure analysis, dependency on pose estimator, and questionable evaluation metrics.
- Overall: solid contribution with minor but addressable weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
