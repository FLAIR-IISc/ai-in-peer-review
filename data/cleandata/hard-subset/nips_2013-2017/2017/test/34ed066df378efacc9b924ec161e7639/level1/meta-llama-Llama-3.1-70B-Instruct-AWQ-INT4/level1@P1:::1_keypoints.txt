- Summary: Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation (PG), a two-stage pipeline (pose integration via U-Net-like network + adversarial image refinement) with a pose mask loss to transfer appearance to a target pose.

- Strength: Introduces a novel task of conditioning generation on a reference image and target pose, a valuable contribution.

- Strength: Well-designed two-stage approach and effective pose mask loss to mitigate background influence.

- Strength: Strong experimental results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 demonstrating effectiveness.

- Strength: Thorough comparison to related work and honest discussion of advantages and limitations.

- Weakness: Assumes accurate pose estimation, which may not hold in real-world settings.

- Weakness: Performance degrades with complex or cluttered backgrounds (e.g., Market-1501).

- Weakness: No detailed analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements.

- Weakness: Some generated images show artifacts or inconsistencies in failure cases.

- Quality: Technically sound with supported claims, but minor writing issues and unexplained abbreviations (e.g., "CE", "HME").

- Clarity: Paper is clearly organized and reproducible for experts, though some sentences are long and convoluted.

- Originality: Novel task and approach; pose mask loss and two-stage pipeline are original and effective.

- Significance: Results are important, applicable to filmmaking, pose estimation, and image retrieval, and open new research directions.

- Pro acceptance: Novelty, effective experimental validation, and comprehensive comparisons.

- Con acceptance: Reliance on pose accuracy, background sensitivity, and missing complexity/memory analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS 2017, with requests to improve writing and provide computational/memory details.
