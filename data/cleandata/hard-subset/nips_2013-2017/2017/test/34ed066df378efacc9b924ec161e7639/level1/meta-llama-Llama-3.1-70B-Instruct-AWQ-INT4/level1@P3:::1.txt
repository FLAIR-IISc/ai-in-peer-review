**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The generation framework PG consists of two key stages: pose integration and image refinement. In the first stage, a U-Net-like network integrates the target pose with the person image to generate an initial coarse image. The second stage refines the initial result using a U-Net-like generator in an adversarial way. The authors evaluate their method on two person datasets, Market-1501 and DeepFashion, and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in generating high-quality person images with convincing details.

**Related Work**

The authors provide a thorough review of related work on generative image modeling, including Variational Autoencoders (VAE), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Autoregressive models. They also discuss recent work on conditioned image generation, including attribute-conditioned image generation, category-conditioned image generation, and text-conditioned image generation. The authors highlight the novelty of their approach, which conditions image generation on both a reference image and a target pose.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel task definition**: The authors propose a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and a target pose, which is a challenging and interesting problem.
2. **Two-stage approach**: The proposed two-stage approach is effective in capturing the global structure of the person and generating high-quality images with convincing details.
3. **Pose mask loss**: The authors introduce a novel pose mask loss that helps to alleviate the influence of background changes and guides the generator to focus on the pose transfer of the human body.
4. **Extensive experimental evaluation**: The authors evaluate their method on two person datasets and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through qualitative and quantitative results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited generalizability**: The authors only evaluate their method on two person datasets, and it is unclear how well the approach generalizes to other datasets or tasks.
2. **Dependence on pose estimation**: The authors rely on a state-of-the-art pose estimation approach to obtain the desired human body keypoints, which may not always be accurate.
3. **Lack of comparison to other methods**: The authors only compare their method to a one-stage model and a related work on multi-view person image synthesis, but do not provide a comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Originality**

The authors propose a novel task definition and a two-stage approach that is different from previous work on conditioned image generation. The introduction of a pose mask loss is also a novel contribution.

**Significance**

The proposed method has the potential to be useful in various applications such as movie making, human pose estimation, and image retrieval based on synthesized images. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive experimental evaluation.
