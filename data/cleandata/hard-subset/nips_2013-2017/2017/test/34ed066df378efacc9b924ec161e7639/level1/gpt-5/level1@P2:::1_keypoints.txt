- Task: Proposes PG2, a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework to synthesize person images conditioned on an input image and target 2D pose keypoints.
- Stage-I: U-Net-like encoder-decoder integrates condition image and pose heatmaps, trained with a pose-weighted (masked) L1 loss to focus on the person.
- Stage-II: Adversarial U-Net predicts a residual (difference map) to refine Stage-I output; conditional pairwise discriminator receives (condition image, generated/real image) pairs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GANs/cGANs and coarse-to-fine generation (e.g., pix2pix, StackGAN) and uses pose heatmaps for finer control versus view-conditioned methods.
- Method soundness: Architectural choices (heatmaps, masked loss, residual refinement, pairwise discriminator) are reasonable and well-motivated.
- Ablations: Includes useful experiments on pose embedding variants, loss variants, and one- vs two-stage training supporting design choices.
- Metrics: Reports SSIM and IS (and mask variants) plus a user study, but acknowledges their limitations; no FID reported and IS is not ideal for conditional tasks.
- Missing identity evaluation: No identity-preservation metric (e.g., re-ID or face-ID similarity) which is critical for this task.
- Baselines: Includes one-stage adversarial baseline and comparison to [36], but lacks a strong pix2pix-style cGAN baseline with PatchGAN and perceptual (VGG) loss.
- Missing comparisons: Does not evaluate against perceptual/feature losses or appearance-flow/deformation baselines that are relevant.
- Robustness: Dependence on pose estimator quality is not systematically analyzed; robustness to pose noise, occlusions, and background variation is lacking.
- Failure modes: Background leakage and artifacts persist, especially on Market-1501; failure cases are only briefly discussed.
- Reproducibility: High-level architecture and training schedules provided, but some implementation details (mask construction, λ values) are underspecified or deferred to supplement.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-structured; figures and motivation are good though a few procedural details need clarification.
- Originality: Incremental but nontrivial—novelty lies in combining pose-masked loss, two-stage U-Nets, pairwise discriminator, and residual refinement for pose-driven person synthesis.
- Significance: Problem is important for controllable synthesis and applications like virtual try-on and re-id augmentation, but impact is limited by missing identity/robustness evaluations.
- Pros: Clear formulation, effective pose conditioning, well-motivated two-stage residual design, useful ablations, and compelling qualitative results.
- Cons: Lacks strong baselines (pix2pix+perceptual), no FID or identity metrics, limited robustness analysis, problematic artifacts on challenging datasets, and some missing implementation details.
- Suggested experiments: Add a pix2pix-style baseline with PatchGAN and VGG perceptual loss; report FID and identity-preservation metrics; test re-id augmentation benefit.
- Suggested analyses: Evaluate sensitivity to pose-estimation noise/occlusions (e.g., confidence-weighted heatmaps), and improve background handling via segmentation/attention.
- Suggested clarifications: Provide mask-generation procedure, λ ablations in main paper, exact residual combination/normalization, and runtime/compute details.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed contribution with moderate originality and clear results; recommend acceptance if stronger baselines, identity-preservation, and robustness experiments are added.
