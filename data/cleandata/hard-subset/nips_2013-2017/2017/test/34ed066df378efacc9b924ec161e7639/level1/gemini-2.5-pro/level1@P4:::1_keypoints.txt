- Novel task: generate an image of a specific person in an arbitrary target pose from a single source image and pose keypoints.  
- Proposed PG²: a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework with G1 (U-Net-like) producing a coarse image and G2 (U-Net-like) + conditional GAN refining it.  
- Pose representation: target pose encoded as a stack of keypoint heatmaps concatenated to the input, shown to be effective.  
- Pose mask loss: masked L1 loss that focuses training on the person foreground to mitigate background influence.  
- Conditional discriminator: discriminator conditioned on (generated/real, source) pairs to enforce realism and identity consistency.  
- Technical soundness: decomposition into global structure then detail refinement and the network/design choices are well-motivated.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and methodology is well illustrated.  
- Qualitative results: strong visual results on DeepFashion and convincing ablation studies demonstrating component benefits.  
- User study: perceptual evaluation supports the qualitative superiority of the method.  
- Quantitative metrics: standard metrics (SSIM/IS) are insufficient; introduced mask-SSIM/mask-IS but numerical improvements are inconsistent.  
- Background handling: model often ignores/produces inconsistent backgrounds and copies source patches, especially on cluttered datasets (Market-1501).  
- Robustness/failure analysis: limited exploration of failure modes and no detailed analysis of errors propagated from the off-the-shelf pose estimator.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant task; sound and effective two-stage approach; clear presentation and strong qualitative evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: quantitative evaluation not fully convincing, background artifacts, and insufficient investigation of robustness and pose-estimation impacts.  
- Overall conclusion: meaningful contribution that advances controllable image synthesis, with strengths outweighing weaknesses but notable limitations to address in future work.
