- Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage framework for pose-guided person image synthesis (Stage-I: pose-integrated coarse generation; Stage-II: adversarial refinement).
- Introduces pose mask loss to focus synthesis on the human body and a difference map in Stage-II to speed training.
- Shows strong qualitative and quantitative results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, with ablation studies and user evaluations.
- Strength: Novel task and well-motivated two-stage approach that handles global structure and fine details.
- Strength: Technical contributions (pose mask loss, difference map) improve generated image quality.
- Strength: Extensive experimental validation including baselines, ablations, and user studies.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful figures and method descriptions.
- Strength: Practical applications in movie production, pose estimation, and data augmentation.
- Weakness: Limited comparisons — missing benchmarks against recent GAN-based state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: Dataset bias (gender/pose imbalance) leads to failure cases and reduces robustness.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns — some hyperparameters and pose mask formulation need clearer specification.
- Weakness: Poor handling of differing backgrounds between reference and target images.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with suggestions to add state-of-the-art comparisons and address dataset biases.
- For acceptance: Novel methodology, strong experimental support, and clear presentation.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient benchmarking and unresolved background/dataset issues.
