- Proposes a novel two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that decouples pose integration and image refinement.  
- Uses U-Net-like architectures and GANs to generate person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Addresses a relevant problem with applications in movie making and data augmentation for pose estimation.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and presents a clear motivation and problem statement.  
- Related work coverage includes generative models and conditional image generation; method described in detail (pose embedding, generators, losses).  
- Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion are comprehensive and include ablation studies on pose embeddings, losses, and the two-stage approach.  
- Quantitative and qualitative results demonstrate effectiveness, supported by informative ablation studies.  
- Explicit incorporation of pose information provides better control than abstract cues.  
- Divide-and-conquer two-stage strategy and use of a difference map improve convergence and results.  
- Inclusion of a user study strengthens evaluation with subjective quality evidence.  
- Qualitative results on Market-1501 are limited and need more examples and deeper analysis.  
- Discussion of limitations (rare poses, data imbalance) is brief; needs more detail and future directions.  
- Connection to view synthesis literature [34,36] should be strengthened and compared in expressiveness.  
- Practical significance of quantitative metric improvements (e.g., SSIM, IS) to perceptual quality is not fully discussed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective approach with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
