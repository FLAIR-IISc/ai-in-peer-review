- Introduces PG2: a novel two-stage framework (Stage I pose integration with U-Net-like architecture; Stage II image refinement with GAN) to generate person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose.
- Uses pose keypoints as explicit conditioning, a clear extension over prior conditional generation and multi-view synthesis work.
- Proposes a pose mask loss to focus learning on transferring body appearance.
- Demonstrates results on low-resolution re-id and high-resolution fashion datasets with quantitative (SSIM, IS) and qualitative comparisons.
- Provides ablation studies (pose embeddings, loss functions, one vs. two-stage) that clarify component contributions.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes helpful diagrams explaining the two-stage process.
- Comparison to related work [36] is thorough and shows clear improvements.
- Novelty: explicit conditioning on reference image plus pose keypoints is a meaningful and original contribution.
- Technical soundness: combining U-Nets and GANs is well-motivated and appropriate for the task.
- Empirical validation is extensive and supports the method's effectiveness.
- Limited comparison: lacks broader comparisons to other image-to-image translation or pose-transfer methods.
- Qualitative limitations: visual comparisons can be hard to interpret; user study design and scoring could be improved.
- Failure case analysis is limited and should delve into causes (complex poses, clothing interactions).
- Dependency on a pre-trained pose estimator introduces a potential source of error that needs more discussion.
- Metric limitations: heavy reliance on SSIM and IS is problematic; alternative perceptual metrics would be useful.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with well-motivated approach and solid results despite minor shortcomings.
- Recommendation: Accept.
