- Problem & method: Proposes PG2, a two-stage U-Net framework that generates person images in a target pose using pose heatmaps and a coarse-to-fine residual GAN refinement.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on conditional VAEs/GANs, image-to-image translation, and view synthesis; uses explicit keypoint heatmaps for finer control versus view labels.

- Methodological soundness: Two-stage decomposition, pose heatmaps, masked L1, pairwise discriminator, and residual learning are sensible and align with standard practices.

- Empirical support: Includes qualitative results, ablations (pose embeddings, losses, stages), SSIM/IS metrics (and masked variants), and a user study on DeepFashion and Market-1501.

- Main evaluation gaps: No quantitative identity-preservation metrics, no pose-accuracy evaluation, no evidence for faster convergence claims, and background handling remains ad hoc.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized with architecture/training details; pose mask construction and discriminator distinction need clearer description for reproducibility.

- Originality: Integration of keypoint heatmap conditioning with a residual two-stage pipeline is a novel and timely contribution, though components are individually familiar.

- Significance: Important for controllable image synthesis and potential baseline for future work, but impact limited by missing identity/pose metrics.

- Strength: Clear, practical coarse-to-fine decomposition with strong qualitative results and convincing pose-embedding ablations.

- Strength: Foreground-weighted mask L1 and residual adversarial refinement improve robustness and detail.

- Strength: Comprehensive ablations, user study, and evaluation on two datasets with different resolutions.

- Weakness: Lacks identity preservation evaluation critical for person-centric tasks.

- Weakness: No quantitative pose-accuracy assessment of generated images.

- Weakness: Comparison to stronger single-stage baselines (e.g., pix2pix + perceptual loss) is missing.

- Weakness: Quantitative improvements are modest and masked metrics may obscure background artifacts.

- Essential suggestion: Add identity metrics using re-ID or face/body embeddings comparing reference and generated images.

- Essential suggestion: Report pose accuracy by running a pose estimator on generated images and measuring PCK/OKS versus target.

- Essential suggestion: Include a well-tuned single-stage conditional GAN baseline (concatenated input + pose, adversarial + L1 or perceptual loss).

- Essential suggestion: Provide training curves or time-to-target evidence to support faster convergence claims for residual refinement.

- Nice-to-have: Explore perceptual/feature-matching losses in stage II, clarify and release pose-mask generation, test robustness to pose noise, and consider learning foreground attention.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a clear, effective design, strong qualitative results, and useful ablations; likely to influence follow-up work.

- Arguments against acceptance: Missing key quantitative evaluations (identity, pose), limited baselines, and partly non-standard metrics limit scientific rigor.

- Overall assessment: Solid and timely contribution with convincing qualitative advances; adding identity/pose metrics and stronger baselines would substantially strengthen the paper.
