- Introduces pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.
- Proposes a two-stage network (PG): pose integration stage using a U-Net and a refinement stage using a conditional DCGAN.
- Introduces a novel pose mask loss to focus generation on the person and reduce background influence.
- Uses a difference map in the second stage as an efficient optimization strategy.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation and methodology.
- Demonstrates effectiveness with qualitative results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets.
- Ablation studies analyze different pose embeddings and the contribution of the pose mask loss.
- Comparison to prior work [36] highlights the advantage of explicit pose control.
- Quantitative evaluation relies on SSIM and IS, which have known limitations and weaken interpretation.
- User study measures realism but does not assess pose accuracy specifically.
- Failure case analysis is limited and should be expanded.
- Analysis of the L1 loss weight (λ) is relegated to supplementary material, limiting integration into the main discussion.
- The choice of 18 keypoints appears arbitrary and is not thoroughly justified.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with compelling qualitative results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand failure analysis and include more pose-specific evaluations.
