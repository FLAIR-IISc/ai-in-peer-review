

This paper introduces a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses given a reference image and target pose. The core innovation lies in explicitly conditioning generation on pose heatmaps (rather than keypoints or textual descriptions) and introducing a pose mask loss to suppress background interference, combined with a two-stage architecture that separates global pose structure learning from detail refinement. The work addresses a meaningful application gap in pose-transfer generation, with strong empirical validation on both low-resolution re-identification (Market-1501) and high-resolution fashion (DeepFashion) datasets.

**Strengths:**  
1. **Originality and Novelty:** The explicit use of pose heatmaps as conditioning input (Section 3.1) and the pose mask loss (Equation 1) are well-motivated and distinct from prior work like Zhao et al. [36], which relies on textual pose descriptions. The two-stage design (Section 3.2) effectively decomposes the challenging pose-transfer task into manageable subproblems (global structure vs. detail refinement), a strategy not previously applied to person image generation at this scale.  
2. **Technical Quality:** The ablation studies (Figure 4, Table 1) are thorough and convincing. They demonstrate that: (a) heatmap embedding outperforms coordinate/heatmap embedding alternatives; (b) the pose mask loss significantly improves sharpness over standard L1 loss; and (c) the two-stage refinement (G1+G2+D) consistently yields higher-quality results than one-stage approaches (G1+D) in both quantitative (SSIM/IS) and qualitative (user study, Table 2) metrics. The user study (Section 4.4) effectively shows that G1+G2+D generates more convincing images (14.9% real-rating vs. 9.3% for G1+D on DeepFashion).  
3. **Significance:** The method directly enables practical applications (e.g., movie character manipulation, pose estimation data augmentation) and advances the state of the art in conditional image generation. The comparison to Zhao et al. [36] (Figure 5) clearly demonstrates superior detail preservation and pose accuracy, especially for complex poses. The failure analysis (Figure 6) is also valuable for identifying dataset limitations (e.g., gender imbalance, rare poses).  

**Weaknesses and Suggestions:**  
1. **Clarity in Methodology:** The pose mask construction (Section 3.1) requires more detail. How is the mask *computed* from the pose keypoints? The description ("morphological operations") is vague. *Suggestion:* Add a brief pseudocode or reference to standard morphological operations (e.g., dilation/erosion) used to create the mask from the heatmap.  
2. **Discriminator Design Justification:** The discriminator’s pairwise input (IA, IB) vs. (IA, G2(IA, ÎB1)) (Section 3.2) is critical but under-explained. *Suggestion:* Clarify why this pairing is necessary beyond avoiding the trivial solution of outputting IA. A short argument (e.g., "This ensures the discriminator focuses on *refinement* rather than *synthesis*") would strengthen the design.  
3. **Dataset Limitations:** The Market-1501 results (Figure 4, Table 2) show degraded quality due to background clutter, which the authors note but don’t fully address. *Suggestion:* Briefly discuss how future work could incorporate background-aware conditioning (e.g., via segmentation masks) to improve robustness in challenging scenes.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
This is a high-quality, well-executed contribution that advances pose-guided image synthesis with clear technical innovations and strong empirical validation. The two-stage framework with pose mask loss is a principled solution to a challenging problem, and the results demonstrate tangible improvements over prior work. The paper is clearly written, logically structured, and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. While minor clarifications are needed (as noted), the core ideas are compelling and the methodology is sound.  

**Recommendation:** Accept. The paper makes a significant contribution to conditional image generation, with clear novelty (explicit pose heatmaps + two-stage refinement), strong technical execution, and meaningful applications. The weaknesses are minor and easily addressable in revision. The work aligns well with NIPS’ focus on rigorous, impactful advances in machine learning.
