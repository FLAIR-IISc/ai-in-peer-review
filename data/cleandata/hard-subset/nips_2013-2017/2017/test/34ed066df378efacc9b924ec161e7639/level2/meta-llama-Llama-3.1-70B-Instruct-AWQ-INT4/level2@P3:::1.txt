**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that generates person images in arbitrary poses based on an input image and a target pose. The network consists of two stages: pose integration and image refinement. The first stage uses a U-Net-like architecture to integrate the target pose with the person image, while the second stage uses a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) to refine the initial generation result. The network is trained on two person datasets, Market-1501 and DeepFashion, and achieves high-quality person image generation with convincing details.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel task**: The paper proposes a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose, which is a challenging and interesting problem.
2. **Two-stage approach**: The two-stage approach of the network is effective in generating high-quality person images, with the first stage capturing the global structure of the person and the second stage refining the appearance details.
3. **Pose mask loss**: The pose mask loss is a useful technique for alleviating the influence of background changes and guiding the generator to focus on the pose transfer of the human body.
4. **Extensive experiments**: The paper presents extensive experimental results on two person datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited comparison**: The paper only compares with one related work, [36], which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
2. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, which may make it difficult to understand the underlying principles of the network.
3. **Overemphasis on qualitative results**: While the paper presents many qualitative results, it may be helpful to provide more quantitative results to support the claims made in the paper.
