This paper proposes a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for generating person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. The core idea is to decouple the generation process into pose integration and image refinement, leveraging U-Net-like architectures and GANs. The work addresses a relevant problem with potential applications in areas like movie making and data augmentation for pose estimation.

The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the proposed approach. The motivation is clear and the problem statement is well-defined. The related work section covers relevant literature, including generative models (VAEs, GANs, autoregressive models) and conditional image generation techniques. The proposed method is described in detail, with clear explanations of the pose embedding, generator architectures, and loss functions. The experiments on both the Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets are comprehensive, with ablation studies exploring the influence of pose embeddings, loss functions, and the two-stage approach. The quantitative and qualitative results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. 

A key strength of this work lies in the explicit incorporation of pose information, which allows for greater control over the generation process compared to methods relying on abstract cues. The divide-and-conquer strategy seems reasonable and helps address the challenges of simultaneously generating correct poses and detailed appearances. The use of a difference map in the second stage also appears to be a clever approach, leading to faster convergence and improved results. The ablation studies are informative and provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed framework. The inclusion of a user study strengthens the evaluation and provides subjective evidence of the quality of the generated images.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. While the results on the DeepFashion dataset are convincing, the qualitative results on Market-1501 appear somewhat limited. It would be beneficial to show more examples and provide a more in-depth analysis of the performance on this more challenging dataset. The paper mentions limitations related to rare poses and data imbalance, but a more detailed discussion of these limitations and potential future directions would be helpful. The connection to the existing work on view synthesis [34, 36] could be strengthened. While the paper acknowledges the difference in the condition – keypoints versus viewpoint – a more direct comparison of the expressiveness of these conditions would be valuable. Finally, while the quantitative results are reported, the discussion could be more focused on the practical significance of the observed differences. For example, how does the improvement in SSIM or IS translate to a perceptual improvement in image quality?

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel task of pose-guided person image generation.
*   Well-designed two-stage framework that effectively addresses the challenges of the task.
*   Comprehensive experiments with ablation studies and user evaluation.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Potential for significant impact in applications like movie making and data augmentation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Qualitative results on Market-1501 could be improved.
*   Discussion of limitations and future work could be more detailed.
*   Connection to related work on view synthesis could be strengthened.
*   Discussion of the practical significance of quantitative results could be more insightful.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to pose-guided person image generation. While there are some minor areas for improvement, the strengths of the work outweigh the weaknesses. The paper addresses a challenging problem, proposes a well-motivated and well-designed solution, and provides compelling experimental evidence of its effectiveness. This reviewer recommends acceptance.
