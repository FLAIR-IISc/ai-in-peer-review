- Task: generate an image of a person in an arbitrary target pose from a single source image.  
- Method: two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) — G1 (U-Net) produces a coarse image; G2 (U-Net) refines via a residual and conditional adversarial loss.  
- Novelty: uses explicit keypoint-based pose heatmaps as conditioning, a flexible and novel control mechanism for person generation.  
- Loss innovation: introduces a masked L1 loss to focus reconstruction on the person rather than background.  
- Evaluation: tested on high-resolution DeepFashion and challenging, low-resolution Market-1501 datasets.  
- Strength — significance: well-motivated problem with clear applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, re-ID) likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Strength — methodology: coarse-to-fine decomposition is logical and empirically effective; Stage I captures pose/appearance, Stage II adds texture and realism.  
- Strength — ablations: comprehensive studies validate pose embedding choices and the benefit of masked L1; one-vs-two-stage comparisons are convincing.  
- Strength — visuals: compelling qualitative results preserving identity and clothing, handling complex textures and plausible faces.  
- Weakness — quantitative metrics: SSIM and Inception Score poorly reflect perceptual quality and sometimes favor blurrier outputs; masked metrics only partially mitigate this.  
- Weakness — user study: AMT results are ambiguous (one-stage sometimes fools more than two-stage), requiring deeper analysis of perceived artifacts.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: some implementation details are vague (exact pose mask construction, layer/filter/hyperparameter specs) and should be clarified.  
- Weakness — failure modes: sensitivity to noisy or incorrect pose estimates from external detectors is not analyzed and should be investigated.  
- Overall: strong, well-engineered contribution with high-quality visual results; minor issues in evaluation analysis and reproducibility but a solid candidate for acceptance.
