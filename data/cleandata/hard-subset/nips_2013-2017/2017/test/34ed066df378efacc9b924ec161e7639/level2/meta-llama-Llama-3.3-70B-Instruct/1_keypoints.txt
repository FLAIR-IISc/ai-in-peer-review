- Proposes PG2, a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network that synthesizes person images in arbitrary poses from a reference image and target pose.  
- Uses a two-stage pipeline: stage 1 generates a coarse pose-aligned image, stage 2 refines it for sharpness and details.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence on person synthesis.  
- Claims: PG2 generates high-quality, detailed person images; two-stage approach captures global structure and refines results; pose mask loss improves foreground focus.  
- Experimental support: extensive results on two datasets, comparisons with alternative pose embeddings, losses, and a one-stage model showing PG2 outperforms baselines.  
- User study indicates generated images are often indistinguishable from real images.  
- Paper is well-written with clear method description and comprehensive related work discussion.  
- Strength: novel and effective PG2 architecture for arbitrary-pose person generation.  
- Strength: well-designed two-stage approach and useful pose mask loss contribution.  
- Strength: extensive experiments demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of failure cases (e.g., Fig. 6).  
- Weakness: limited comparison with the most related work [36] and other existing methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add failure-case analysis and more comprehensive comparisons.  
- Rating: 8/10.
