- Proposes a novel two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) for pose-guided person image synthesis.
- Stage-I: U-Net-like architecture integrates pose (18 heatmaps) with reference image to produce a coarse output.
- Stage-II: adversarial training refines the coarse output to improve realism and detail.
- Introduces a pose mask loss that focuses generation on human body regions rather than background.
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on Market-1501 (128×64) and DeepFashion (256×256) datasets.
- Shows significant improvements over baselines and the most related prior work, including ablation variants.
- Qualitative results show better pose alignment, detail preservation, and more photo-realistic images.
- Quantitative validation via SSIM, Inception Score, and AMT user studies supports effectiveness.
- Well-situated in GAN-based image generation literature and offers meaningful innovations over prior pose conditioning methods.
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, comprehensive experiments, and an effective two-stage approach.
- Limitations: struggles with rare poses and background artifacts, which the paper acknowledges.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational efficiency, limited exploration of extreme pose variations, and user study could include more baselines.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written contribution with practical applications; recommends acceptance.
