- Paper proposes PG2, a two-stage network to generate person images in arbitrary target poses from a reference image.
- Strength: Introduces a novel and challenging task of pose-conditioned person image generation.
- Strength: Two-stage design (pose integration U-Net + DCGAN refinement) effectively captures global structure and appearance details.
- Strength: Pose mask loss helps ignore background changes and focuses generation on body pose transfer.
- Strength: Extensive experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion showing high-quality qualitative results.
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited, only evaluated against one related work, which weakens claims of superiority.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the architecture and losses work.
- Weakness: Overemphasis on qualitative results; more quantitative evaluation is needed.
- Originality: The two-stage pose-guided generation approach is original and interesting.
- Significance: Method has potential applications in filmmaking, pose estimation, and image retrieval.
- Quality: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Clarity: Good use of figures and tables to support explanations and results.
- For acceptance: Novel task and method, solid experimental demonstration, and practical significance argue in favor.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical grounding, limited baselines, and scarce quantitative metrics argue against.
- Recommendation: Add theoretical analysis to clarify underlying principles.
- Recommendation: Compare against more related works and baselines.
- Recommendation: Provide additional quantitative results to substantiate qualitative claims.
