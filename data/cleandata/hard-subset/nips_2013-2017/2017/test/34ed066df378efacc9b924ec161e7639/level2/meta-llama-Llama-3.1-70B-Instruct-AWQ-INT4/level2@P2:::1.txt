This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses, based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The authors present a two-stage approach to address this task, with each stage focusing on one aspect: the first stage focuses on global structure of the human body and the second stage on filling in appearance details based on the first stage result.

The paper is well-written and the proposed method is thoroughly evaluated on two person datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion). The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of their approach, including comparisons with different pose embeddings, losses, and network architectures.

The strengths of this paper are:

* The proposed method is novel and addresses a challenging task in the field of person image generation.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including both qualitative and quantitative results.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method. For example, the authors mention that the method may not work well for rare or complicated poses, but they do not provide a thorough analysis of this limitation.
* The authors could also provide more details about the computational complexity of the proposed method and its potential applications in real-world scenarios.
* Some of the figures and tables could be improved for better clarity and readability.

Overall, this paper presents a novel and well-evaluated method for person image generation. However, it could benefit from a more detailed analysis of its limitations and potential applications.
