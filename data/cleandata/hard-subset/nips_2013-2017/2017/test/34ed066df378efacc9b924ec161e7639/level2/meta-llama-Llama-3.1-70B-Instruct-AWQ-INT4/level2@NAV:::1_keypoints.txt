- Proposal: PG2 — a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (U-Net-like pose integration + DCGAN-based image refinement) with a novel pose mask loss.  
- Claim: Can generate high-quality, pose-correct, photo-realistic person images from a reference image and a target pose.  
- Evaluation: Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion show qualitative photo-realism and quantitative support (SSIM, IS).  
- Support: Extensive comparisons (pose embeddings, loss functions, one- vs two-stage) show the proposed components outperform alternatives.  
- Usefulness: Useful for applications like moviemaking, human pose estimation, and image retrieval through arbitrary-pose person generation.  
- Field knowledge: Authors demonstrate solid understanding and provide thorough related-work comparison.  
- Novelty: Significant improvement via the two-stage framework and novel pose mask loss; novel U-Net + DCGAN combination.  
- Completeness: Paper gives sufficient architectural, training, and experimental details for reproducibility.  
- Limitations: Struggles with complex backgrounds and rare/unusual poses.  
- Constructive criticism: Should explore more pose embedding and loss alternatives and provide deeper analysis of failure cases.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective method with convincing experiments and reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Performance issues on complex/rare poses and insufficient failure-case analysis.  
- Rating: 8.5 / 10.
