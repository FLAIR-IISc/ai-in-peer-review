The paper "Pose Guided Person Image Generation" proposes a novel approach for generating person images in arbitrary poses, conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. The authors employ a two-stage framework, where the first stage integrates the target pose with the person image using a U-Net-like network, and the second stage refines the initial result using a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN).

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain their proposed approach. The experimental results on two person datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The main contribution of this paper is the proposal of a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose. The authors also explore several ways to integrate pose information with a person image and propose a novel mask loss to encourage the model to focus on transferring the human body appearance instead of background information.

The paper has several strengths:

1. The proposed approach is novel and addresses a challenging task in the field of computer vision.
2. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain their proposed approach.
3. The experimental results on two person datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
4. The paper is well-organized and clearly written.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses:

1. The proposed approach is complex and requires a large amount of training data.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of their approach.
3. The paper does not provide a clear comparison with state-of-the-art methods in the field.

To improve the paper, the authors could:

1. Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of their approach.
2. Compare their approach with state-of-the-art methods in the field.
3. Provide more qualitative and quantitative results to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
