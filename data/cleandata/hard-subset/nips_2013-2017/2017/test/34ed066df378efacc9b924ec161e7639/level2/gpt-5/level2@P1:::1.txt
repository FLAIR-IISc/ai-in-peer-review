Summary
This paper proposes PG2, a two-stage conditional image generation framework for pose-guided person synthesis. Given a source image and a target pose (encoded as keypoint heatmaps), Stage-I uses a U-Net-like generator trained with a masked L1 loss to produce a coarse image aligned to the target pose. Stage-II refines details using a conditional GAN whose generator predicts a difference map relative to Stage-I’s output; a pairwise discriminator receives both the source image and the generated/real target image, and training combines adversarial and masked L1 losses. The authors conduct ablations on pose embeddings (coordinates vs. heatmaps), loss (L1 vs. masked L1), and one-stage vs. two-stage training, and evaluate on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64). They introduce mask-SSIM/mask-IS to de-emphasize background changes and include a small user study. The approach relates to conditional generation (CVAE/GAN), image-to-image translation (e.g., pix2pix), view synthesis (e.g., VariGAN), and human synthesis with segmentation/parts conditioning; the novelty lies in explicit pose conditioning via keypoint heatmaps and an effective two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline with a residual/difference-map refinement.

Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated task with compelling applications (content creation, data augmentation).
- Thoughtful decomposition into global pose alignment (Stage-I) and detail refinement (Stage-II).
- Practical modeling choices: pose heatmaps (vs. coordinates), masked L1 to mitigate background mismatch, pairwise conditional discriminator, and difference-map prediction to ease adversarial training.
- Solid ablations that isolate the effects of pose embedding, loss, and two-stage design; qualitative gains are convincing, especially on DeepFashion.
- Sensible evaluation adaptations (mask-SSIM/mask-IS) acknowledging background confounds; user study adds perceptual evidence.
- Comparison to the most related view-conditional work [36] favors PG2.

Weaknesses
- Baselines are limited. The one-stage adversarial baseline could be made stronger (e.g., a pix2pix-style generator/discriminator trained directly on concatenated source image + pose heatmaps).
- Quantitative metrics are not entirely decisive: SSIM/IS (even masked variants) may not correlate with perceived quality; no FID or perceptual distances (e.g., LPIPS), and the user study is small-scale without statistical analysis.
- Limited identity preservation analysis. Since identity fidelity is central, an evaluation with a re-id network (feature similarity or rank-1 match) would be informative.
- Mask construction and λ (the L1 weight) are under-specified in the main text; more detail is deferred to supplementary material.
- No joint end-to-end fine-tuning of both stages; it is unclear whether joint training further improves results.
- Generalization to complex occlusions and rare poses is challenging (authors show failures); the approach relies on pose estimates and foreground masks that can be noisy.

Quality
Overall technically sound. The two-stage architecture and loss design are reasonable and well-executed. The difference-map generator and pairwise discriminator are appropriate choices. Training details and ablations support the claims. However, stronger comparative baselines and more robust quantitative measures would strengthen the empirical case. Some implementation particulars (mask generation details, λ selection) should be clarified for full reproducibility.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. The motivation, architecture, and training procedure are described with sufficient detail to reimplement, though exact architectural hyperparameters and mask construction steps are partially in the supplement. Figures (especially qualitative comparisons) are helpful. Minor issues: explicitly state how the difference map is applied (added to Stage-I output), and report the chosen λ values and sensitivity in the main paper.

Originality
Moderately original. The task of explicit pose-conditioned person generation using keypoint heatmaps was not standard at the time, and the two-stage coarse-to-fine with a difference-map GAN is a nice, practical contribution. Several components (U-Net with skip connections, conditional GAN with concatenated conditions, masked regression losses) are known, but the combination tailored to pose-guided person generation is meaningful.

Significance
Significant for the subarea of conditional human synthesis. The results are visually strong on DeepFashion and establish a baseline approach that others can build on. Potential impact includes data augmentation for pose estimation/re-id and content creation. The lack of identity-preservation metrics and stronger baselines limits claims about advancing the state of the art quantitatively, but the qualitative advances and well-motivated pipeline are likely to be influential.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include a stronger one-stage baseline (e.g., pix2pix-style) using the same pose heatmaps and a comparable discriminator, and report FID/LPIPS (if feasible) alongside mask-SSIM/IS.
- Add identity preservation evaluation via a re-id network (e.g., cosine similarity in a pretrained embedding, or re-id rank-1 on synthesized-to-real retrieval).
- Report λ values and the sensitivity study in the main text; detail mask generation (algorithm and failure cases).
- Consider joint end-to-end fine-tuning of Stage-I and Stage-II, or a feature-level perceptual loss to improve texture fidelity while controlling artifacts.
- Analyze robustness to pose estimation noise and occlusions; report performance vs. pose detector confidence.
- If possible, quantify user study with statistical tests and longer exposure conditions.

Pros
- Clear problem setting and practical, effective architecture.
- Strong qualitative results, especially on high-resolution data.
- Careful ablations demonstrating design choices.
- Sensible masked losses and evaluation adaptations.

Cons
- Limited baselines and quantitative metrics; no identity fidelity measure.
- Some details deferred to the supplement.
- Performance degrades on cluttered surveillance data; occlusions/rare poses remain challenging.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper presents a well-motivated and practically impactful approach with convincing qualitative evidence and informative ablations. Strengthening baselines and quantitative evaluation would elevate it further.

Reviewer confidence: Medium.
