- Task: Pose-guided person image synthesis using a reference image and target keypoint pose to generate the person in the new pose.
- Method: Two-stage PG2 framework — stage I U-Net-like encoder–decoder with FC bottleneck produces a coarse image using masked L1 loss.
- Method (cont.): Stage II conditional adversarial U-Net refines via a residual “difference map” with a pairwise conditional discriminator.
- Experiments: Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with ablations (pose embeddings, masked L1, one- vs two-stage), user studies, and masked SSIM/IS metrics.
- Results: Strong qualitative results and interpretable failure cases; quantitative improvements are modest but consistent.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on VAEs/GANs/image-to-image translation; pose heatmaps and two-stage/residual designs are known but usefully integrated here.
- Novelty: Moderate — individual components are established; main contribution is their tailored integration and masked loss.
- Strength: Clear problem formulation with practical relevance (data augmentation, content creation).
- Strength: Sensible “structure then details” two-stage approach improves stability and quality.
- Strength: Convincing ablation showing keypoint heatmaps are effective pose embedding.
- Strength: Masked L1 loss effectively de-emphasizes background mismatch; supported by ablation.
- Strength: Residual refinement accelerates convergence; qualitative results and user study add perceptual evidence.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation limited—SSIM/IS (and masked variants) are weak proxies; FID/LPIPS or better human studies are needed.
- Weakness: Identity preservation is not quantitatively evaluated; no re-id feature distance or retrieval metrics.
- Weakness: Background handling is problematic (esp. Market-1501); skip connections may copy background despite masked loss.
- Weakness: FC bottleneck in stage I is not well justified and may hurt scalability versus alternatives (dilated convs/attention).
- Weakness: Missing/incomplete comparisons to tuned pix2pix baselines and recent pose-guided/deformable-GAN works.
- Weakness: Deterministic pipeline ignores diversity/uncertainty, causing averaged details in occluded regions.
- Quality: Technically sound with thoughtful ablations, but evaluation lacks stronger, task-relevant metrics and baselines.
- Clarity: Mostly clear and well-structured; some design and reference details deferred to supplementary material.
- Originality: Moderate — useful integration and incremental ideas (difference-map, masked L1).
- Significance: Promising for controllable person synthesis and data augmentation, but stronger identity/downstream validation needed.
- Questions for authors: Provide re-id identity metrics or augmentation gains, clarify masked-IS computation, test FC bottleneck alternatives, explore background stabilization, and assess robustness to pose-estimation errors.
- Recommendation: Weak accept; reviewer confidence medium.
