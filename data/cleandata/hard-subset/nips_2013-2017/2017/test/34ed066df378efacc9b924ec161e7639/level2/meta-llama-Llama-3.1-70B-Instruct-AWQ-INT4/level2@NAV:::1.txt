**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that generates person images in arbitrary poses based on a reference image and a target pose. The framework consists of two stages: pose integration and image refinement. The first stage uses a U-Net-like network to generate an initial coarse image, while the second stage refines the image using a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN). The authors also propose a novel pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes.

**Evaluation of Claims**

The authors claim that their method can generate high-quality person images with convincing details. The experimental results on two datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion) demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The qualitative results show that the generated images are photo-realistic and pose-wise correct. The quantitative results, including Structural Similarity (SSIM) and Inception Score (IS), also support the authors' claims.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide extensive experimental results to support their claims. They compare their method with alternative pose embedding methods, loss functions, and one-stage models. The results show that their proposed pose embedding method and pose mask loss outperform the alternatives. The two-stage model also generates better results than the one-stage model.

**Usefulness**

The proposed method has many potential applications, such as movie making, human pose estimation, and image retrieval. The ability to generate person images in arbitrary poses can be useful in various fields.

**Field Knowledge**

The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the field of generative image modeling and person image generation. They provide a thorough review of related work and compare their method with existing approaches.

**Novelty**

The proposed method presents a significant improvement over existing approaches by using a two-stage framework and a novel pose mask loss. The use of a U-Net-like network and a variant of DCGAN is also a novel combination.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility. The authors describe the network architecture, training process, and experimental setup in detail.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge that their method has limitations, such as the difficulty in generating images with complex backgrounds. They also note that the method may not perform well on images with rare or unusual poses.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism is that the authors could have explored more alternative pose embedding methods and loss functions. Additionally, the paper could have benefited from more detailed analysis of the failure cases.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The proposed method presents a significant improvement over existing approaches.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
* The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The method may not perform well on images with complex backgrounds or rare poses.
* The paper could have benefited from more detailed analysis of the failure cases.

**Rating**

I would rate this paper 8.5/10. The proposed method is novel and effective, and the experimental results are convincing. However, the paper could have benefited from more detailed analysis of the failure cases and exploration of alternative pose embedding methods and loss functions.
