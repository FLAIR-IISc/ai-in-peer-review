- Introduces a novel task: generate a photo-realistic image of a person in an arbitrary target pose from a single source image.  
- Proposes PG², a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (coarse-to-fine framework).  
- Stage I: U-Net-like generator uses source image + target pose heatmap to produce a coarse image capturing global structure.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss (masked L1) to focus learning on the foreground person and reduce background influence.  
- Stage II: conditional GAN with a second U-Net refines the coarse output, adding high-frequency details.  
- Discriminator is conditioned on the source image to enforce identity consistency.  
- Uses explicit pose keypoints as a detailed conditioning signal for fine-grained control, differing from abstract labels/attributes.  
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine design is sensible and effective for the complex generation task.  
- Originality/significance: defines a valuable problem with clear applications (data augmentation, re-identification, virtual try-on, media).  
- Technical quality: well-motivated decomposition, pose mask loss, thorough experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501, and ablation studies justify design choices.  
- Clarity/presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and supported by helpful diagrams and compelling qualitative results.  
- Quantitative evaluation weakness: SSIM/Inception scores poorly correlate with perceptual quality; L1 models get higher SSIM despite being blurrier.  
- Suggestion: consider perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to better align quantitative results with human judgment.  
- Related work contextualization: should more explicitly compare to contemporaneous image-to-image translation (e.g., Isola et al.) highlighting differences.  
- Limitations: lower performance and more artifacts on Market-1501; struggles with complex backgrounds/clothing textures and sensitivity to pose estimator errors.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with strong qualitative results; recommended for acceptance and likely to inspire follow-up work.
