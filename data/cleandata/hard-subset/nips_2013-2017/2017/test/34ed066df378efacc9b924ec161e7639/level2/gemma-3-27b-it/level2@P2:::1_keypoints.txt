- Proposes a Pose Guided Person Generation Network (two-stage: U-Net-like pose integration + GAN refinement) with a masked L1 loss; tested on low- and high-resolution datasets.  
- Tackles an interesting, challenging problem with potential applications; two-stage approach and mask loss are sensible contributions.  
- Technical motivations are weak: no clear explanation for simplifying the residual block in Stage-I.  
- Pose representation choice (18 keypoints) is arbitrary and lacks comparisons to other keypoint counts or encoding schemes.  
- Claim that Stage-II difference map yields faster convergence is unsupported by quantitative evidence (no training curves).  
- Ablation study is unclear about which loss functions were used with each pose embedding (ambiguity around “G1-CE-L1”).  
- Mask loss improvements are only qualitatively shown; no quantitative metrics (e.g., SSIM, IS) comparing with/without mask loss.  
- Comparison to related work (e.g., [36]) is superficial: single qualitative example and no analysis of dataset/training/architecture differences.  
- Missing clarity/details: hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size) not in main text; discriminator loss description is convoluted.  
- Explanation of why the generator learns a difference map is presented too late and should be stated earlier.  
- Failure case description uses vague language (“few man samples and complex texture”) and needs precise, informative diagnostics.  
- Originality limited: GANs and U-Nets are standard; contribution mainly in their combination and task application but not well differentiated from similar methods.  
- Significance limited by lack of downstream task demonstrations (e.g., using generated images to improve pose estimation or re-ID).  
- User study is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate real-world impact.  
- Overall recommendation: reject due to insufficient technical justification, limited quantitative evaluation/ablations, superficial related-work comparison, and weak real-world validation.
