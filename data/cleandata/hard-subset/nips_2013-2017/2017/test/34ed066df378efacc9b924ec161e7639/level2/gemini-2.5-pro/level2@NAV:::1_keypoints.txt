- Introduces a novel pose-guided person image generation task for synthesizing a person image from a source image given a target pose.  
- Proposes a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG²): Stage I (U-Net-like) generates a coarse pose-matched image; Stage II (conditional GAN) refines details.  
- Uses pose heatmaps for conditioning and a novel pose mask loss to handle background changes and guide structure.  
- The coarse-to-fine design effectively separates global structure transformation from local texture synthesis.  
- Thorough experimental validation on DeepFashion and Market-1501, including ablations, user study, and quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS).  
- Performance depends on upstream pose-estimator quality; robustness to pose inaccuracies or missing keypoints is not discussed.  
- Description of Stage-II as learning a "difference map" is ambiguous—unclear whether G2 outputs a residual or a full refined image.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses.
