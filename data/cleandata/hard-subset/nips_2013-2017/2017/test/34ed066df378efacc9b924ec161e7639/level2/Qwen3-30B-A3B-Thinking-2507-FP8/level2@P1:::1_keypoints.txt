- Novelty: Introduces pose heatmaps as conditioning and a pose mask loss, plus a novel two-stage design separating global pose structure from detail refinement.  
- Pose conditioning: Conditioning on heatmaps (vs. keypoints/text) is well-motivated and distinct from prior work.  
- Two-stage architecture: G1 for coarse pose transfer and G2 refinement effectively decomposes the task and improves results.  
- Ablations: Thorough ablation studies show heatmap embedding, pose mask loss, and two-stage (G1+G2+D) outperform alternatives.  
- Quantitative and user evaluation: Demonstrates improved SSIM/IS and higher user “real” ratings (14.9% vs. 9.3%) on DeepFashion.  
- Significance and applications: Advances conditional image generation with practical uses (e.g., character manipulation, data augmentation) and better detail/pose accuracy vs. prior work.  
- Failure analysis: Provides useful analysis identifying dataset limitations like gender imbalance and rare poses.  
- Clarity issue: Pose mask construction from keypoints is under-specified; needs explicit description or pseudocode for the morphological operations.  
- Discriminator rationale: Pairwise discriminator input (IA, IB) vs. (IA, G2(...)) is under-explained; require clearer justification for this design choice.  
- Dataset limitation: Market-1501 background clutter degrades quality; suggest future background-aware conditioning (e.g., segmentation masks).  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, well-executed contribution with minor clarifications needed.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
