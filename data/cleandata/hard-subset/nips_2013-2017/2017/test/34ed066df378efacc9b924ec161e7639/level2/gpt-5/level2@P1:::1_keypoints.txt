- Summary: Proposes PG2, a two-stage pose-guided person synthesis: Stage-I U-Net with masked L1 for coarse pose alignment, Stage-II conditional GAN predicting a residual/difference map for refinement; uses keypoint heatmaps as pose input.  
- Evaluation: Ablations on pose embedding, loss, and staging; tested on DeepFashion and Market-1501, introduces mask-SSIM/mask-IS and a small user study.  
- Strength: Well-motivated task with useful applications (content creation, data augmentation).  
- Strength: Thoughtful decomposition into coarse global pose alignment and fine detail refinement.  
- Strength: Practical modeling choices (pose heatmaps, masked L1, pairwise discriminator, difference-map prediction).  
- Strength: Solid ablations isolating effects of design choices; convincing qualitative gains on DeepFashion.  
- Strength: Sensible evaluation adaptations to de-emphasize background and inclusion of a user study.  
- Strength: Comparison to related view-conditional work favors PG2.  
- Weakness: Baselines are limited; one-stage adversarial baseline is weak and should include a pix2pix-style model.  
- Weakness: Quantitative metrics (SSIM/IS) may not reflect perceptual quality; missing FID and LPIPS and small, unanalysed user study.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of identity preservation; no re-id based evaluation.  
- Weakness: Mask construction and L1 weight λ are under-specified in main text (deferred to supplement).  
- Weakness: No joint end-to-end fine-tuning of both stages explored.  
- Weakness: Struggles with complex occlusions and rare poses; depends on potentially noisy pose estimates and masks.  
- Quality: Technically sound; two-stage design, difference-map generator, and pairwise discriminator are reasonable and well-executed.  
- Quality caveat: Stronger baselines and more robust quantitative measures needed to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and reproducible, but some architectural hyperparameters and mask details are only in the supplement; explicitly state difference-map application and λ in main text.  
- Originality: Moderately original—novel combination of explicit keypoint heatmap conditioning with a two-stage coarse-to-fine, though many components are standard.  
- Significance: Significant for conditional human synthesis subarea with strong qualitative results and potential impact, but limited quantitative claims due to missing identity metrics and baselines.  
- Suggestions: Add stronger one-stage baseline (pix2pix), report FID/LPIPS, include re-id identity evaluation, report λ and mask generation in main text, try joint fine-tuning and perceptual loss, analyze robustness to pose noise/occlusion, and statistically analyze user study.  
- Pros (summary): Clear problem, effective architecture, strong high-res qualitative results, and careful ablations.  
- Cons (summary): Limited baselines/metrics, some details in supplement, degraded performance on cluttered/occluded data.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable and well-executed but would benefit from stronger baselines and quantitative evaluation.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
