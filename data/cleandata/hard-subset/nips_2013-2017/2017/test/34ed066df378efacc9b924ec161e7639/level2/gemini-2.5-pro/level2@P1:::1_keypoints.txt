- Introduces a novel task: pose-guided person image generation — synthesize a photorealistic image of a target pose from a single source image.  
- Proposes a two-stage PG² framework: stage 1 generates a coarse image with a U-Net using pose heatmaps; stage 2 refines with a cGAN U-Net and discriminator.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss (weighted L1) to focus reconstruction on the person foreground and be robust to background changes.  
- Uses pose heatmaps instead of raw coordinates; ablations show heatmaps are more effective.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 (low-res) and DeepFashion (high-res) with strong qualitative and quantitative results and a user study.  
- Strength — originality and significance: new, well-defined problem with practical applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, media tools).  
- Strength — sound methodology: logical coarse-to-fine decomposition and thoughtful design choices justified by ablations.  
- Strength — thorough experiments: convincing qualitative progression, systematic ablations, and perceptual user study.  
- Weakness — limited architectural novelty: relies on standard components (U-Net, cGAN); novelty mainly in composition and mask loss.  
- Weakness — quantitative metrics: SSIM/IS can be misleading; mask-SSIM/mask-IS help but adversarial models don't always show numeric improvements despite perceptual gains.  
- Weakness — limited analysis of failure cases: needs deeper discussion on self-occlusion, unseen textures (e.g., back of clothing), and layered clothing ambiguities.  
- Questions for authors: why reintroduce the original image I_A into stage 2 (identity preservation?), and how does a single-stage pix2pix-style baseline compare to G1+D?  
- Overall: solid contribution with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
