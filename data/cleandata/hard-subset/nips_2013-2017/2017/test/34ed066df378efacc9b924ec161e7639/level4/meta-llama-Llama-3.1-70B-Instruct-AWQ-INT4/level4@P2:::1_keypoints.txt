- Proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation (PG) network that synthesizes person images in arbitrary poses from a source image and target pose.  
- Framework explicitly uses pose information and has two key stages: pose integration and image refinement.  
- Paper is well-written and the problem/approach are novel.  
- Extensive evaluation of design choices (pose embeddings, pose mask loss vs. L1, two-stage vs. one-stage).  
- Two-stage model produces more photo-realistic images than the one-stage model.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak—SSIM and Inception Score may not capture synthesis quality fully.  
- Authors do not clearly explain why residual blocks improve generation performance.  
- Paper does not explore using Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs trained adversarially, which could be a useful direction.  
- User study is preliminary; a more substantive human evaluation is recommended.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
