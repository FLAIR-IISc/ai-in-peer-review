- Paper introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine generator for pose-guided person image synthesis using keypoint heatmaps.  
- Novelty: tackles a challenging, novel task with significant application potential.  
- Architecture: progressive two-stage refinement is well-designed and effective for the problem.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Qualitative results are promising and generally high-quality, though some images show artifacts.  
- Evaluation: extensive ablation studies thoroughly analyze key design choices.  
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is limited by the lack of established objective metrics (minor issue).  
- Related work: should discuss links to iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Residual blocks: authors should clarify in what ways residual blocks improved performance (convergence, visual quality, user study scores, etc.).  
- Suggested experiment: train Stage 2 adversarially on Stage 1 inputs to test whether blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected bottleneck.  
- User study: current human evaluation is preliminary; a more rigorous, substantive user study is strongly recommended.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—solid technical contribution and promising results despite need for improved human evaluation.
