- Summary: Proposes a two-stage architecture to generate person images by combining appearance from an input image with a target pose represented as heat maps.
- Strength: Task is innovative and the two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is novel.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clear, and shows promising results despite some artifacts.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation of design choices.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is relatively weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics for the task.
- Comment: Recommend citing iterative refinement/cascaded network work (Carreira et al. CVPR 2016; Newell et al. ECCV 2016).
- Comment: Request clarification on exactly how residual blocks improve generation performance.
- Comment/Question: Ask how Stage 2 would perform if given Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially, and whether blurring in Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: Confidently recommend acceptance based on novelty and approach.
- Rebuttal note: User study is preliminary and unconvincing (discounted); authors should include a more substantive human evaluation, though the reviewer’s positive assessment remains.
