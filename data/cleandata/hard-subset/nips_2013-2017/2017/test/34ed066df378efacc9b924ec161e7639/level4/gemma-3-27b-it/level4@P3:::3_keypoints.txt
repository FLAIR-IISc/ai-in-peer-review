- Proposes a GAN-based system for novel-view person image synthesis guided by pose keypoints, using established techniques (DCGANs, U-Nets).  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- The core idea is interesting and addresses a challenging, potentially impactful application.  
- Uses a reasonable two-stage approach to separate pose and appearance modeling.  
- Explores different pose embedding methods and introduces a pose mask loss.  
- Lack of rigorous quantitative evaluation; a user study is strongly recommended.  
- Visual results are not sufficiently compelling: images are small and show noticeable artifacts.  
- Improvements from pose mask loss and two-stage design are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Related work coverage is adequate but misses some recent pose-conditioned image manipulation literature (e.g., Kanazawa et al.).  
- Contribution is incremental and builds heavily on existing techniques.  
- Empirical validation is limited and does not firmly establish high-quality or realistic synthesis.  
- Recommendation: weak reject due to insufficient quantitative evidence and visual result limitations.
