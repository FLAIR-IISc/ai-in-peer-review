- Introduces PG², a two-stage pose-guided person image generation network that synthesizes a person in a new target pose from a source image.
- Stage 1 (G1): U-Net-like generator combines source image and target-pose heatmaps to produce a coarse image capturing global structure and color.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus learning on the foreground person and handle unpredictable background changes.
- Stage 2 (G2): second U-Net-like generator predicts a residual "difference map" given the coarse output and source image to add fine details and correct artifacts.
- G2 is trained adversarially with a discriminator in a conditional GAN setup to improve high-frequency realism.
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with quantitative metrics, ablations, user study, and extensive qualitative examples showing robustness across datasets.
- Strength: novel and useful task (pose-conditioned synthesis) with explicit control via keypoint pose representation.
- Strength: well-motivated coarse-to-fine architecture that stabilizes training and separates global structure from detail refinement.
- Strength: technical choices (pose mask loss, residual prediction in G2) effectively address foreground/background and refinement challenges.
- Weakness: generated images still exhibit artifacts and fail to reproduce fine textures, limiting photorealism.
- Weakness: architectural novelty is incremental—builds on existing ideas (U-Net, StackGAN/pix2pix) with task-specific adaptations.
- Weakness: contribution is specialized to a narrow vision task and may have limited broader ML methodological impact.
- Overall: solid, well-evaluated baseline for pose-guided person generation that merits acceptance.
