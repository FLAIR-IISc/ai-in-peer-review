Summary:
This paper proposes a novel architecture for generating images of a person with a specified pose, combining the appearance of an input image with pose information encoded as heatmaps.

Strengths:
+ The proposed task is novel and addresses an interesting problem in image manipulation.
+ The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a novel contribution.
+ The paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed method.
+ Despite the presence of discernible artifacts in the generated images, the results are promising and demonstrate the potential of the approach.
+ The authors provide an extensive evaluation of key design decisions, indicating a thorough understanding of their system.

Weaknesses:
- The quantitative evaluation is generally weak; however, this is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics for this type of task and does not represent a major weakness. 

Comments:
Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be beneficial to discuss related work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks, such as:
[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
- Line 110 states: "we find that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance." Please elaborate on *how* the performance is improved – specifying which aspects of the generation benefit from the use of residual blocks would be helpful.
- While the evaluation is extensive, I am curious about the performance of using the Stage 2 network with the inputs designed for Stage 1, trained adversarially. Specifically, is the observed blurring with Stage 1 + GAN attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and the proposed approach, I confidently recommend that the paper be accepted.

Rebuttal:
While I appreciate the authors’ attempt at a user study, its preliminary nature renders it unconvincing and I therefore do not consider it in my review rating. The authors are strongly encouraged to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive assessment of this work remains unchanged.
