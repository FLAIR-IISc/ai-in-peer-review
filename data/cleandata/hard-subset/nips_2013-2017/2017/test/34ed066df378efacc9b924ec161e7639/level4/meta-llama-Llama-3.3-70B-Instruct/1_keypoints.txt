- Proposes a novel architecture to generate person images by combining input appearance with pose heat maps.
- Task is innovative and unprecedented.
- Introduces a unique two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Results are promising despite noticeable artifacts.
- Design-decision evaluation is thorough and extensive.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited, partly due to lack of established metrics.
- Recommend adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Clarify the claim that residual blocks improve generation—specify which performance aspects improve.
- Suggest testing Stage 2 using Stage 1 inputs trained adversarially and examine whether Stage1+GAN blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: strongly accept based on novelty of problem and approach.
- Rebuttal note: user study is preliminary and discounted; authors should include a more substantive human evaluation, though the positive assessment stands.
