- Introduces a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose maps.
- Uses a two-stage adversarial architecture: a U-shaped generator for coarse images and a second generator that predicts a residual map for refinement.
- High-resolution outputs are produced for discriminator input to improve fidelity.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Contribution: novel task framing by conditioning generation on both appearance and pose.
- Contribution: technical innovation via a stacked residual-predicting architecture and novel loss design.
- Strength: well-structured paper that clearly conveys the coarse-to-fine, appearance-and-pose conditioning approach.
- Strength: residual-based refinement meaningfully reduces blurriness compared to the first stage.
- Primary limitation: generated images exhibit persistent artifacts and fail to reconstruct high-frequency appearance details (e.g., black dots in patterned regions).
- Secondary limitation: perceived as incremental extension of existing conditional/staked GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs), limiting broader novelty.
- Domain limitation: suited to vision/graphics (dressing) but lacks clear applicability to other learning domains.
- Overall assessment: technically sound with sufficient novelty for acceptance but requires quality improvements for practical use.
