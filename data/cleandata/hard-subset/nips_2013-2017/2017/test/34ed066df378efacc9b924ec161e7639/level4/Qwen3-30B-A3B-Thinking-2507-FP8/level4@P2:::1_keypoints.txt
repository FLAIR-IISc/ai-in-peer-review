- Proposes a novel task: person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose, implemented via a two-stage PG2 architecture (Stage I: coarse pose-guided generation; Stage II: adversarial refinement).
- Introduces a pose mask loss to concentrate generation on human body regions and suppress background influence.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with extensive ablations on pose embedding, loss functions, and two-stage vs one-stage designs.
- Strength: Novel task with clear practical applications (movie making, pose estimation, image retrieval).
- Strength: Two-stage architecture effectively separates structural pose synthesis from detail refinement.
- Strength: Ablations show heatmap-based pose embeddings outperform coordinate-based methods.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and produces promising qualitative results.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is shallow, relying mainly on SSIM and Inception Score, which poorly correlate with perceptual quality.
- Weakness: Lacks stronger perceptual metrics (e.g., FID) and more robust human evaluation; current user study is small (30 subjects, 55 samples per condition).
- Weakness: Need deeper analysis of why G1+D (Stage I with adversarial training) attains comparable quantitative metrics to full G1+G2+D despite blurrier visuals.
- Clarification needed: Quantify the performance gain from using residual blocks—report specific metrics and amounts of improvement.
- Clarification needed: Explain the function and necessity of the fully connected layer in Stage I and why it is removed in Stage II.
- Suggestion: Situate the approach within iterative refinement literature (e.g., Carreira et al. CVPR 2016, Newell et al. ECCV 2016) in the related work.
- Overall: Technically sound and significant contribution with clear presentation; recommend acceptance pending stronger evaluation and technical clarifications.
