

The paper introduces Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), a two-stage framework for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses using a reference image and target pose. It addresses the pose transfer task by first integrating pose information via heatmap embeddings (Section 3.1) followed by adversarial refinement of appearance details (Section 3.2). The method builds on recent conditional GAN work like StackGAN [Zhang et al., NIPS 2016] and Zhao et al. [36] but introduces pose-specific innovations: a novel pose mask loss to suppress background influence, and a difference-map generation strategy for efficient refinement. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) datasets demonstrate its effectiveness over single-stage baselines and [36], with qualitative results showing improved pose consistency and detail (Figure 4).

Strengths:
- **Originality**: Proposes conditioning on explicit pose heatmaps (not view words [36]) for flexible pose transfer, with a pose mask loss that effectively focuses on human body structure (Section 3.1). This differs from prior work like [15, 32] which used abstract attributes or coarse segmentation.
- **Quality**: Rigorous ablation studies (Section 4.2) validate the pose embedding method (heatmap vs. coordinate embedding) and mask loss, showing consistent SSIM/IS improvements. The two-stage design (G1+G2+D) outperforms one-stage adversarial models (G1+D) in perceptual quality (user study: 14.9% vs. 9.3% real-image confusion, Table 2).
- **Clarity**: Well-structured with clear technical descriptions of the U-Net variants (Section 3.3) and loss functions (Eq. 1, 4). The figure-based results (Figure 4) effectively illustrate stage-wise contributions.

Weaknesses:
- **Quality**: Generated images still exhibit artifacts (e.g., "black dots" in ID346, Figure 4; inconsistent textures in Market-1501 samples), and high-frequency details (e.g., fabric patterns) remain imperfect despite the two-stage refinement. The mask-SSIM metric (Table 1) shows higher scores for blurry G1-poseMaskLoss than refined G1+G2+D, indicating a disconnect between quantitative metrics and perceptual quality.
- **Significance**: While technically sound, the application scope is narrow (person re-ID/fashion), with limited discussion of broader impact. The task resembles [36] (NIPS 2017) but lacks generalization beyond pose-conditioned image synthesis (e.g., to objects beyond humans).
- **Technical depth**: The λ hyperparameter sensitivity (Section 3.2) is noted but not thoroughly analyzed in the main text; supplementary materials would be needed for full reproducibility.

Pro/Con Acceptance:
- **Pro**: Novel pose embedding via heatmaps; effective pose mask loss; strong quantitative/qualitative validation on two datasets; clear two-stage design addressing pose-transfer challenges.
- **Con**: Persistent artifacts limit practical utility; significance constrained to narrow domain; minor novelty over conditional GANs (StackGAN [Zhang et al., NIPS 2016] used stacked generators for text-to-image).

The paper makes a solid technical contribution to pose-conditioned image generation, but its impact is limited by narrow applicability and residual quality issues. It meets NIPS standards for quality and clarity but falls short in significance for a broad audience. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to better contextualize novelty against [36] and address artifact limitations.
