- Paper proposes a two-stage GAN to generate person images in target poses conditioned on a source appearance.  
- Stage 1: U-Net-like generator produces a coarse image mapping global structure and color.  
- Stage 2: a refiner generator predicts a residual (difference) map using the coarse image and source to add high-frequency details.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  

- Strength: tackles a novel and practical pose-guided person image generation task with clear applications.  
- Strength: effective coarse-to-fine architecture where stage 1 handles pose transfer and stage 2 refines textures.  
- Strength: methodological contributions include learning a residual map for refinement and a pose mask loss to focus on foreground.  

- Weakness: generated images still contain visible artifacts and fail to preserve complex high-frequency clothing patterns.  
- Weakness: architectural novelty is incremental—relies on established components (U-Nets, conditional GANs, stacked refiners).  
- Weakness: limited discussion on generalizability beyond human images, narrowing broader impact.  

- Detailed notes: paper is well-written and ablation studies support claims, but final visual quality needs improvement.  
- Suggestion: better positioning relative to concurrent stacked GAN works (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs) would clarify novelty.  
- Conclusion: solid, well-executed contribution with room for visual-quality and novelty improvements; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
