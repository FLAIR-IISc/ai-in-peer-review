- Introduces an architecture to generate images of people with separate control of identity (from an image) and pose (via heatmaps).
- Novelty: explicit pose control while preserving identity is a significant and original contribution.
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a thoughtful and effective design choice.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
- Results are promising and realistic overall, though generated images show some artifacts.
- Authors provide a thorough analysis of key design choices, strengthening the paper’s claims.
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is limited due to lack of established metrics and should be improved in future work.
- Suggests citing related iterative/cascaded pose estimation work (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) for context.
- Requests more detail on how residual blocks improve generation performance.
- Recommends investigating training Stage 2 directly on Stage 1 inputs with adversarial loss to probe blurring and the role of Stage 1’s fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty and technical merit despite evaluation limitations.
- Rebuttal: user study is preliminary and inconclusive; a more rigorous human evaluation is strongly encouraged.
