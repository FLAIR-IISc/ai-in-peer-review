- Introduces PG2, a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network for synthesizing a specific person in arbitrary target poses.
- Stage I: U-Net-like generator (G1) uses source image + target-pose heatmaps to produce a coarse image; introduces a masked L1 loss focusing learning on the foreground person.
- Stage II: U-Net-like generator (G2) inside a conditional GAN predicts a residual/difference map to refine details and textures.
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) and a user study, showing strong qualitative and quantitative results.
- Strength: Tackles a novel and significant task with useful applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, media content).
- Strength: Well-motivated coarse-to-fine two-stage design that simplifies learning and stabilizes training.
- Strength: Technical contributions include effective heatmap concatenation for pose integration and the pose mask loss to handle changing backgrounds.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with ablations on pose embedding, mask loss, and one-stage vs two-stage models.
- Weakness: Generated images still contain artifacts and fail to reproduce some high-frequency texture details.
- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty—largely adapts U-Nets, skip connections, and stacked adversarial frameworks from prior work.
- Weakness: Methods (e.g., pose mask loss) are highly domain-specific and may have limited generalizability beyond articulated-person images.
- Overall: A solid, well-validated contribution to pose-conditioned person generation that is practically valuable despite incremental architecture and remaining image-quality issues.
