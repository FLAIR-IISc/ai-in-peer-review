This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that synthesizes person images in arbitrary poses, conditioned on an image of that person and a target pose. The proposed framework utilizes a two-stage approach, consisting of a pose integration stage and an image refinement stage. The first stage uses a U-Net-like network to generate an initial coarse image, while the second stage employs a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) to refine the initial result through adversarial training.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two datasets, Market-1501 and DeepFashion, and show that their method can generate high-quality person images with convincing details. The proposed pose embedding method, which uses 18 channels of pose heatmaps, is shown to be effective in guiding the generation process. The authors also propose a novel pose mask loss, which helps to alleviate the influence of background changes and focuses the generation on the human body.

The paper is well-organized and effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and provide sufficient details about the network architecture and training procedure. The experimental results are also well-presented, with both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.

One of the strengths of the paper is its novelty, as it introduces a new task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose. The proposed approach is also technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method.

However, there are some areas where the paper can be improved. One of the major concerns is the quality of the generated results, which still contain visible artifacts. While the second generator helps to mitigate the blurriness of the initial result, it seems that the model is still insufficient to recover high-frequency components in the target appearance.

Another possible concern is that some might view the proposed approach as an application of conditional GANs, which has been explored in previous work. While the authors do provide some application-specific challenges, this might not appeal to a large number of audiences.

Overall, the paper successfully proposes a solution to the pose-conditioned image problem and properly conducts evaluation. The proposed approach presents technical novelty, and the resulting images are visually consistent. However, the quality of the generated results still needs improvement.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose.
* The proposed approach is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method.
* The paper is well-organized and effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The quality of the generated results still contains visible artifacts.
* Some might view the proposed approach as an application of conditional GANs, which has been explored in previous work.

Rating: Accept, pending minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
