- Paper proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images guided by a target skeleton (keypoints).
- Uses established techniques (DCGANs, U-Nets); main novelty is applying them to this specific task.
- Manuscript is well-written and easy to follow.
- Empirical results show the system can synthesize somewhat realistic images.
- Major concern: lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user studies to measure fooling rate).
- Major concern: limited visual quality and noticeable artifacts in generated images; figures too small to assess.
- Similar visual-quality issues appear throughout the supplementary material.
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address the core quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Reviewer conclusion: work shows promise but is not yet ready for a top-tier venue like NeurIPS.
