- Proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images guided by target skeleton keypoints (uses DCGANs and U-Nets).  
- Contribution mainly lies in applying established GAN/U-Net techniques to a vision application rather than novel methodology.  
- Strength: concept is intriguing and the paper is clearly written.  
- Strength: empirical experiments show the system can synthesize somewhat realistic images.  
- Major weakness: lacks quantitative evaluation (e.g., user studies to measure how often generated images fool humans).  
- Major weakness: qualitative results are unimpressive—generated images are small, contain noticeable visual errors, and poorly match target images.  
- Quality issues persist across main and supplementary figures, casting doubt on realism and similarity to real images.  
- Rebuttal did not adequately address quantitative or qualitative concerns.  
- Reviewer conclusion: work is promising but not ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
