- Proposal: a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose using a two-stage adversarial architecture.
- Generator design: first U-shaped network produces a coarse image from appearance and pose; second network predicts a residual map to refine the output.
- Training/loss: adversarial training with a stacked architecture and carefully designed loss functions.
- Evaluation: method evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset with thorough experiments.
- Task novelty: conditioning on both appearance and pose is a valuable contribution.
- Technique strength: stacked architecture and predicting a difference/residual map instead of direct upsampling are positive innovations.
- Residual map benefit: residual prediction is a small but effective technique for improving image resolution.
- Presentation: paper is well-organized and clearly explains the architecture and coarse-to-fine strategy.
- Applicability: approach is well-suited to dressing and image manipulation tasks and shows sufficient technical novelty.
- Image quality concern: generated images exhibit visible artifacts and fail to fully recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in ID346).
- Impact concern: may be perceived as another instance of conditional GAN architectures, which could limit perceived novelty.
- Prior work overlap: related methods (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs) have explored conditioning and stacking generators.
- Generalizability concern: the approach may be difficult to generalize to other learning problems beyond human image generation.
- Recommendation: overall positive assessment with an initial rating of accept despite quality issues.
