

Summary:  
This paper introduces a novel architecture for generating images of people that synthesizes appearance from an input image with a specified pose (represented via heatmaps).  

Strengths:  
+ The proposed task is highly novel.  
+ The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture presents a meaningful technical contribution.  
+ The paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
+ While visible artifacts exist in the results, they are promising and demonstrate significant potential.  
+ The authors provide extensive evaluation of key design choices.  

Weaknesses:  
- Quantitative evaluation is somewhat limited, though this is understandable given the absence of established metrics for this task (not a major weakness).  

Comments:  
1. The paper would benefit from citing recent advances in iterative refinement via cascaded networks in the related work, such as [a, b]:  
  [a] J. Carreira et al., "Human Pose Estimation with Iterative Error Feedback," CVPR 2016.  
  [b] A. Newell et al., "Stacked Hourglass Networks for Human Pose Estimation," ECCV 2016.  

2. Line 110 states: "we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance." Please clarify *how* residual blocks specifically enhance performance (e.g., faster convergence, reduced artifacts, better pose alignment).  

3. While the evaluation is thorough, it leaves an important question unanswered: How would the Stage 2 network perform when given Stage 1 outputs (rather than raw inputs) and trained adversarially? Could the blurring artifacts observed with Stage 1 + GAN stem from the intermediate fully connected layer?  

Overall rating:  
The novelty of the problem and the proposed approach justify a strong recommendation for acceptance.  

Rebuttal:  
Although the authors include a preliminary user study, its limited scope makes it unconvincing. I recommend replacing it with a substantive human evaluation in the revision. However, this does not affect my positive assessment of the core technical contribution, and my recommendation for acceptance remains unchanged.
