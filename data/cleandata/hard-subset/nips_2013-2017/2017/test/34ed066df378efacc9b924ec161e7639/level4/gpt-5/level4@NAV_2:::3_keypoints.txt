- Presents a GAN-based system to synthesize person images in new poses using target skeletal keypoints, with the contribution mainly in application/formulation rather than new methods.
- Manuscript is clearly written and tackles a compelling problem with some empirically realistic-looking outputs.
- Major concern: lack of quantitative evaluation—no user study or metrics to show how often generated images fool humans.
- Visual results are unconvincing: figures are too small, contain many artifacts, and do not demonstrate high-quality or near-real outputs.
- The method relies on established components (DCGANs, U-Nets), so novelty is limited.
- Reviewers find the direction promising but current results premature and insufficient to claim success.
- After rebuttal, concerns remain unresolved; quantitative and qualitative issues are not satisfactorily addressed, making the work not ready for a top venue.
