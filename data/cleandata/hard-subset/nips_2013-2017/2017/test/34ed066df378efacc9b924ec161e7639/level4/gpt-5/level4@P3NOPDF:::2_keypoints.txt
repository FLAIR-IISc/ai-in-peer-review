- Proposes a two-stage adversarial framework for pose-conditioned human image synthesis: U-Net-like coarse generator followed by a residual refinement generator.
- Conditions generation on both clothing appearance and target pose to preserve appearance cues while changing pose.
- Uses residual (difference) prediction in the second stage to focus refinement on high-frequency corrections.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset with qualitative visual results showing pose alignment and appearance preservation.
- Clear problem setup and practical relevance for virtual try-on and pose-guided synthesis.
- Sensible coarse-to-fine design that stabilizes GAN training and improves detail compared to single-stage models.
- Residual prediction is a lightweight, effective technique to concentrate refinement on missing details.
- Paper is well organized and explains architecture and training setup clearly.
- Image quality remains limited with visible artifacts, especially on patterned clothing and fine textures.
- Second-stage refinement reduces blur but still struggles to reconstruct small logos, repetitive patterns, and stitching.
- Novelty is incremental; approach resembles prior stacked/conditional GANs and may be seen as an engineering refinement.
- Limited generality: method is task-specific and may not transfer broadly without adaptation.
- Needs clearer positioning and explicit comparison to prior multi-stage GANs and pose-guided/virtual try-on works.
- Suggested improvements: stronger texture handling (multi-scale discriminators, patch/style losses, attention), deeper failure-case analysis, and expanded quantitative evaluation (LPIPS, SSIM, user studies).
- Recommend ablation studies for residual vs direct prediction, loss components, and conditioning modalities.
- Ask for computational details (inference time, memory) and discussion of trade-offs from stacked design.
- Clarify terminology and add an annotated overview diagram showing conditioning signals and residual pathways.
- Improve reproducibility by providing dataset splits, preprocessing, pose estimation method, hyperparameters, and releasing code or pseudocode.
- Overall: coherent, practically useful approach with incremental novelty; borderline accept pending stronger evaluation and clearer novelty claims.
