

This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view person images conditioned on skeletal keypoint inputs. While the approach leverages established computer vision components (e.g., DCGANs, U-Nets), its primary contribution lies in the specific application domain.  

**Strengths:**  
The core idea is conceptually interesting, the presentation is clear and accessible, and the empirical results demonstrate that the method can produce visually plausible images under certain conditions.  

**Major Concerns:**  
As a practical application-focused work, the absence of rigorous quantitative evaluation is a significant drawback. The authors should have conducted user studies to measure how frequently generated images successfully fool human observers—this is essential for establishing credibility in such a visually driven task. Furthermore, the qualitative results lack visual appeal and fail to convincingly demonstrate high fidelity. For instance, Figure 1 presents generated images at insufficient resolution for meaningful assessment; even at this scale, clear visual discrepancies between generated outputs and target skeletons are evident (e.g., distorted limbs, inconsistent textures). Similar limitations persist across all figures, including supplementary materials. While the direction is promising, these shortcomings undermine confidence in the claimed quality and suggest the work is premature for claiming success.  

**Post-Rebuttal Assessment:**  
I remain unconvinced by the authors’ response. As noted by other reviewers, critical issues regarding both quantitative metrics and qualitative results persist. The rebuttal acknowledges these gaps but fails to provide adequate resolution—user study data remains absent, and visual quality concerns are not adequately addressed. For a venue like NIPS, where methodological rigor and empirical validation are paramount, the current submission does not meet the required standard. The work shows potential but requires substantially stronger evidence before it can be considered suitable for publication.
