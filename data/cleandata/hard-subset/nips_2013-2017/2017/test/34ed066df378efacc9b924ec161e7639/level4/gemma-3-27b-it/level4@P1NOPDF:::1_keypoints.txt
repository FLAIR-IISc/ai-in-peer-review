- Paper proposes a two-stage architecture that generates images of people by combining an input image's appearance with a target pose represented as heatmaps.
- The task addressed is novel.
- The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a novel contribution.
- The manuscript is well-written and clear.
- Results are promising overall but exhibit some discernible artifacts.
- The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their design choices.
- Quantitative evaluation is relatively weak, though this is understandable given the lack of suitable metrics for the task.
- Reviewer asks to include related iterative refinement work (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) in related work.
- Reviewer requests elaboration on how using residual blocks improves generation performance.
- Reviewer asks whether Stage 2 trained adversarially with Stage 1 inputs would perform differently and whether Stage 1+GAN blurring is related to the intermediate fully connected layer.
- The preliminary user study is unconvincing and should be strengthened; the reviewer discounted it in their rating.
- Overall recommendation: confidently accept the paper despite the noted concerns.
