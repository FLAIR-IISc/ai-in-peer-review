

This paper proposes a GAN-based framework for synthesizing novel-view person images conditioned on skeleton keypoints. While the underlying components (DCGANs, U-nets) are established, the contribution lies in the specific application to skeleton-guided image generation.  

Pros:  
* The core idea is compelling, the exposition is clear, and initial experiments demonstrate the system's capacity to produce reasonably realistic synthetic images.  

Cons:  
* As an application-focused work, robust evaluation is essential. The absence of quantitative metrics is a major drawback; for instance, human subject studies could objectively assess perceptual fidelity (e.g., "how often do generated images deceive human observers?").  
* The empirical results lack visual quality: Figure 1 and supplementary figures present generated images at insufficient resolution for meaningful assessment, with significant visual discrepancies between outputs and targets (e.g., distorted limbs, unnatural textures). These inconsistencies undermine claims of "realistic" synthesis, making it difficult to conclude the results are of sufficient quality or near-realistic.  
* While the direction is promising, the current results are premature to declare success.  

After Rebuttal:  
I remain unconvinced by the authors' response. As noted by other reviewers, critical gaps in quantitative evaluation and qualitative results persist—both vital for a practical application. The rebuttal attempts to address these but fails to resolve the core concerns adequately. This work does not meet the standards required for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
