This paper proposes a human image generator based on appearance and human physiology. The proposed generation is based on a adversarial training architecture, where a two-stage generative network creates a high-resolution image by feeding it to a discriminator. In the generator part, the first generator uses a U-shaped network to create a rough image using appearance and pose maps, then the second generator takes the rough input with the original appearance and estimates the remaining parts to refine the rough image. This paper uses a deep-learning dataset for evaluation.

This paper proposes several important concepts:

*   Novelty of work: The concept of conditioning based on appearance and pose maps is introduced for human image generation.
*   Technique: Stacked architecture that estimates difference maps instead of direct sampling and loss design.

This paper could be further improved in the following areas:

*   Still needs quality improvement.
*   Importance: This paper can be seen as another GAN architecture.
*   Problem domain: Good vision/graphics applications, but difficult to generalize to other learning problems.

This paper is well-organized to explain the main aspects of the proposed architecture. Based on appearance and pose information, the proposed generator stacks two networks to adopt a coarse-to-fine technique. This paper effectively uses the generation technique in the dressing problem. The proposed method seems suitable for the context of the relevant problem. Creating difference maps is a small but elegant technique for creating high-resolution images.

Perhaps the main complaint with this paper is that there are visible errors in the produced results and it needs a lot more improvement from an application perspective. For example, black dots appear in the final result due to the pattern of ID346 in Figure 4. Although the second generator reduces the blurry image of the first generator, it seems that the model is still insufficient to recover the high-frequency elements of the target appearance.

Another potential but serious concern is that one might say that the proposed method is an application of a conditional GAN. Generator conditioning or stacking has been proposed in the past for adversarial training; for example, below, although these are arXiv papers. This paper includes application-specific challenges, but it may not appeal to a large audience.

*   Han Zhang, Tao Ju, Honghong Li, Shaoting Zhang, Xiaolei Huang, Xiaogang Wang, Dimitri Metaxas, "Stackgan: Text to Photo-Realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks", arXiv:1612.03242.
*   Jun Huang, Yixuan Li, Omid Persaid, John Hopcroft, Serge Belongi, Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks, arXiv:1612.04357.

Overall, this paper proposes a successful solution to the pose-conditioned image problem and conducts a reasonable evaluation. The proposed method presents a sufficient amount of technical novelty. The result images still need quality improvement, but the proposed model at least produces some visually consistent images. My initial rating is acceptable.
