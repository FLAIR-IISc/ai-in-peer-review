- Proposes a GAN-based method for human image generation conditioned on a target pose and an appearance encoding.  
- Employs a two-stage generator: a U-Net-like coarse stage followed by a refinement stage that predicts a residual (difference) map for high-resolution outputs.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Main contributions: task novelty (pose+appearance conditioning), stacked coarse-to-fine residual architecture, and a well-designed loss function.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents the architecture; effectively applied to virtual try-on.  
- Predicting a residual map is an elegant technique that helps refine and reduce blurriness.  
- Major weakness: generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., clothing patterns like ID346 rendered as black dots).  
- The second-stage refinement mitigates blurriness but remains insufficient for restoring fine details.  
- Concern about incremental novelty: conditioning and stacked GANs have prior work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs), which may limit broader NIPS appeal.  
- Overall assessment: technically novel with proper evaluation and visually consistent results, but image quality needs improvement; reviewer recommends accept.
