This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view images of people conditioned on a target skeleton (represented as keypoints). The system relies largely on established components such as DCGANs and U-Nets, so the main contribution is in the specific computer vision application.

Pros:
- The idea is interesting, the paper is clearly written, and the experiments indicate the system can synthesize images that are realistic-looking to some extent.

Cons:
- Because this is an application-oriented paper, strong empirical evidence is essential. My main complaint is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, a user study could assess how often the generated images fool human observers.
- The visual results are not compelling. In Fig. 1, the generated images are too small to properly assess. Even so, there are many noticeable visual artifacts when comparing the results to the targets. It is therefore discouraging to conclude that the outputs are of high quality or close to real images. This issue persists across most results, including those in the supplementary material. I agree the direction is promising, but it seems premature to claim success at this stage.

After rebuttal:
I remain unconvinced after reading the authors’ feedback. As other reviewers also noted, there are still significant issues with both the quantitative and qualitative results, which are crucial for a practically oriented work. The rebuttal provides some attempts at addressing these points, but they do not resolve the concerns satisfactorily. Overall, I do not think the paper is ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
