- Proposes a novel architecture to generate person images by combining input appearance with pose heat maps.  
- Uses a unique two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Results are promising but contain discernible artifacts.  
- Authors conduct extensive evaluation of design decisions, yielding useful insights.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics (not considered a major flaw).  
- Recommend adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. CVPR 2016; Newell et al. ECCV 2016).  
- Ask for clarification on how residual blocks improve generation performance and their specific benefits.  
- Suggest experiment: train Stage 2 adversarially using Stage 1 inputs to test if blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Reviewer confidently recommends acceptance based on novelty and approach.  
- Reviewer discounted the preliminary user study in the rating and requests a more substantive human evaluation in revision.
