- Proposes a novel approach for human image generation conditioned on both appearance and pose using adversarial training.
- Uses a two-step generative network: a U-shaped coarse generator followed by a residual-predicting second generator to refine images.
- Introduces stacked architecture that predicts a difference (residual) map rather than direct upsampling.
- Proposes a new loss design that helps produce higher-quality images.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset, demonstrating the method’s effectiveness and visual consistency.
- Paper is well-structured, with clear motivation for conditioning on appearance and pose and for a coarse-to-fine strategy.
- Generated images still contain visible artifacts and fail to fully recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in ID346 of Fig.4).
- The second generator reduces blurriness but is insufficient for practical-quality image recovery of fine patterns.
- Risk of being perceived as an incremental application of conditional GANs (similar to StackGAN and Stacked GAN prior work).
- Concerns about the broader significance and generalizability of the problem beyond vision/graphics applications.
- Overall recommendation: technically novel and thoroughly evaluated; worthy of acceptance but needs further refinement to improve image quality.
