Paper proposes a two-stage GAN (PG2) using U-Nets and a novel pose mask loss to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose keypoints.  
Well-motivated divide-and-conquer design separates global structure learning (Stage-I) from detail refinement (Stage-II).  
Using heatmap pose embeddings instead of raw coordinates is a sensible technical choice.  
Pose mask loss helps prioritize foreground details and mitigate background interference.  
Writing is generally clear and the method shows promise on DeepFashion with plausible pose transfers.  
Ablation studies (pose embedding variants, mask loss vs L1) provide useful empirical insight.  
Evaluation is insufficient for a NIPS submission and lacks rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
User study is underpowered (only 30 users) and does not fully expose major visual failures.  
Quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS, mask-SSIM/mask-IS) are misleading because blurry images can score highly despite low perceptual quality.  
Visual results are inconsistent: good on DeepFashion but show severe artifacts on Market-1501 (background bleed, incorrect objects/gender, blobs).  
Pose mask loss and two-stage design fail on complex backgrounds, and Stage-II often inherits Stage-I artifacts.  
Failure cases are acknowledged but not quantified and no clear remedies are proposed.  
Novelty is limited—combines known components (U-Net, DCGAN, adversarial loss) with incremental contributions—insufficient without stronger validation.  
Rebuttal did not adequately address the evaluation and qualitative shortcomings; recommend rejection pending substantially stronger validation.
