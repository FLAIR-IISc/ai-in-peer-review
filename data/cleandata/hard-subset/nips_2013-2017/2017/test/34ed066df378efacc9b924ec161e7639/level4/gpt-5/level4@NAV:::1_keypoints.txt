- Paper proposes an architecture that combines an input subject’s appearance with a target pose heatmap to generate person images.  
- Addresses a novel problem with a new two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture.  
- Paper is clear and well-written.  
- Results are promising despite noticeable artifacts.  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation of architectural choices.  
- Quantitative evaluation is comparatively weak (due to lack of well-suited metrics).  
- Reviewer suggests adding related work on cascaded/iterative refinement (e.g., iterative error feedback and stacked hourglass networks).  
- Requests clarification on how residual blocks specifically improve generation performance.  
- Asks to evaluate a single Stage 2 network trained adversarially and whether Stage 1+GAN blurring is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Reviewer confidently recommends acceptance.  
- Rebuttal notes the user study is preliminary/unconvincing and urges a substantive human evaluation in revision, but maintains a positive assessment.
