- Presents a human image generator conditioned on explicit appearance and pose, using an adversarial two-stage coarse-to-fine generator; evaluated on DeepFashion.
- Key contribution: novelty in jointly conditioning generation on appearance and pose for human image synthesis.
- Key contribution: technical idea of a stacked generator predicting a residual (difference) map for refinement with a tailored loss.
- Strength: clear writing and well-explained architecture.
- Strength: coarse-to-fine residual refinement sensibly targets missing details and mitigates blur from the first stage.
- Strength: well aligned with virtual try-on/dressing applications where preserving appearance across poses is crucial.
- Strength: difference-map refinement is simple and effective for producing sharper, higher-resolution images.
- Strength: experimental setup on DeepFashion is appropriate and evaluation appears properly conducted.
- Weakness: output quality issues remain—visible artifacts and insufficient high-frequency detail (e.g., texture degeneration in Fig. 4 ID346).
- Weakness: second stage reduces blur but does not reliably recover fine textures, limiting practical applicability.
- Weakness: novelty/significance is limited given similarity to prior stacked GAN approaches (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).
- Weakness: method appears specialized to human pose and appearance and may be hard to generalize to broader tasks.
- Overall: coherent, well-motivated architecture with modest technical contribution and solid empirical validation, but visual quality and novelty concerns remain; preliminary recommendation: accept.
