- Paper develops a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images guided by target skeleton keypoints.
- Uses known techniques (DCGANs, U-nets); contribution is primarily specific application to vision.
- Pros: idea is interesting and writing is clear.
- Pros: experiments show the system can synthesize somewhat realistic-looking images.
- Major concern: lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user studies to test perceptual realism).
- Major concern: qualitative results are not visually convincing—figures are small and outputs exhibit noticeable visual errors compared to targets.
- The visual-quality issues persist across main and supplementary results, making claims of high-quality or near-real images premature.
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; authors’ responses do not sufficiently address quantitative/qualitative concerns.
- Reviewer opinion: work is promising but not ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
