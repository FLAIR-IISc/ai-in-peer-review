- Proposes a GAN-based system for synthesizing novel views of people conditioned on target skeleton keypoints, using DCGAN and U-net components.  
- Primary contribution is an application of established architectures rather than a novel method.  
- Strength: core idea is compelling and the paper is clearly written.  
- Strength: empirical results show some degree of visual realism in generated images.  
- Major weakness: complete absence of quantitative evaluation (no metrics or user studies) to assess fidelity.  
- Visual results are unconvincing—figures are too small, exhibit artifacts, and often mismatch the target skeletons.  
- Current results do not justify strong claims of success or photorealism; further refinement needed.  
- Overall assessment: interesting direction but insufficient evidence and evaluation for acceptance at NIPS.  
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; rebuttal did not satisfactorily address quantitative or qualitative evaluation deficiencies.
