- Paper proposes a GAN-based method to synthesize novel views of people conditioned on target skeleton keypoints.  
- Architecture mostly reuses established components (DCGANs, U-Nets), making the main contribution an application-specific adaptation.  
- Strength: concept is compelling, manuscript is clearly written, and experiments show some degree of realistic synthesis.  
- Weakness: no quantitative evaluation; user studies or perceptual metrics are needed to validate realism.  
- Weakness: visual results are unconvincing—figures are low-resolution and generated images show noticeable artifacts relative to target skeletons.  
- Weakness: quality issues persist across main and supplementary results, undermining claims of high-quality synthesis.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; authors’ response did not adequately address quantitative or qualitative concerns.  
- Recommendation: currently not suitable for presentation at a competitive venue like NeurIPS.
