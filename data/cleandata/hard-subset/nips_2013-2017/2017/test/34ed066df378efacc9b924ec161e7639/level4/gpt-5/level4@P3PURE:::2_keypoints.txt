- Proposed method: two-stage adversarial human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose (coarse U-net then residual-refinement), evaluated on DeepFashion.  
- Contribution: introduces conditioning on both appearance and pose maps for human image generation.  
- Contribution: stacked coarse-to-fine architecture that predicts a residual (difference) map and a thoughtful loss design.  
- Strength: paper is clearly organized and effectively communicates architecture and design choices.  
- Strength: coarse-to-fine residual strategy is appropriate and helps improve higher-resolution synthesis.  
- Strength: method is well aligned with the dressing problem and evaluated on a relevant dataset.  
- Weakness: output quality shows visible artifacts and fails to recover high-frequency details (e.g., patterned region producing black dots in ID346).  
- Weakness: perceived novelty is limited—conditional GANs and stacked generators have precedents and may be seen as incremental.  
- Weakness: potential limited generality—may be hard to generalize beyond the target vision/graphics application.  
- Relation to prior work: similar in spirit to StackGAN and Stacked GANs (Zhang et al., Huang et al.) on arXiv.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated solution that produces visually consistent but artifact-prone images; contribution acceptable — Rating: Accept.
