## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission

This paper presents a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)-based system for synthesizing novel views of people given a target skeleton, represented as keypoints. The architecture largely leverages established techniques – DCGANs and U-nets – positioning the primary contribution as a specific application of these methods within the domain of computer vision.

**Strengths:**

* The core idea is compelling and the paper is well-written, making it easy to understand the proposed approach.
* The empirical experiments demonstrate the system’s ability to generate images with a degree of visual realism.

**Weaknesses:**

* As an application-focused work, strong empirical validation is crucial. A significant shortcoming is the complete absence of quantitative evaluation. To assess the fidelity of the generated images, the authors should consider incorporating quantitative metrics or, ideally, conducting user studies to determine how often the synthesized images can convincingly deceive human observers.
* The visual results presented are not particularly compelling. Figure 1, for instance, contains images that are too small for detailed examination. Even at this resolution, noticeable visual artifacts and discrepancies between the generated images and their corresponding target skeletons are readily apparent. This limits the ability to confidently claim the results are of high quality or approach photorealism. This issue persists throughout the paper and supplementary materials. 
* While the approach appears promising and the authors are clearly moving in a valuable direction, the current results do not yet warrant a strong claim of success. Further refinement and more robust evaluation are needed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a potentially interesting application of GANs to a challenging problem. However, the lack of quantitative evaluation and the limited visual quality of the results currently prevent a positive recommendation. The work feels premature for presentation at a venue like NIPS, as the presented evidence is insufficient to convincingly demonstrate a significant advancement. 

**After Rebuttal:**

I remain unconvinced by the authors’ response. The concerns raised by myself and other reviewers regarding both quantitative and qualitative evaluation remain unaddressed to a satisfactory degree. The rebuttal demonstrates some effort to clarify aspects of the work, but does not fundamentally improve the evaluation or visual quality of the results. Given these ongoing deficiencies, I maintain my assessment that this work is not currently ready for presentation at NIPS. A more thorough and compelling evaluation, coupled with improved visual results, would significantly strengthen the paper and its potential impact.
