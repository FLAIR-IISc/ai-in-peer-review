- Paper introduces a novel architecture to generate images of people by combining source-image appearance with pose heatmaps.
- Novel task contribution to the field.
- Proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Generated results are promising despite noticeable artifacts.
- Design choices are supported by an extensive evaluation.
- Quantitative evaluation is relatively weak, attributed to lack of suitable metrics for this task.
- Recommend discussing related iterative refinement/cascaded network work (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Request elaboration on how using residual blocks improves generation performance.
- Ask whether training Stage 2 adversarially on Stage 1 inputs could help and whether blurring in Stage1+GAN is due to the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty of problem and approach.
- Rebuttal note: user study is preliminary and unconvincing; a more substantial human evaluation is encouraged, but overall positive assessment remains.
