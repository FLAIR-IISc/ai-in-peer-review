- Proposes a novel human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose using a two-step adversarial training architecture.  
- Generator design: first a U-shaped network produces a coarse image, then a second network refines it by predicting a residual/difference map.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Key technical contributions: conditioning on appearance+pose, stacked architecture predicting difference maps, and a well-designed loss function.  
- Coarse-to-fine strategy and difference-map refinement are appropriate and useful for the dressing/pose problem.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents the proposed architecture.  
- Main weakness: generated image quality still needs significant improvement, with visible artifacts and failure to recover high-frequency appearance details.  
- Example artifact: pattern in ID346 produces black dots in the final image, showing residual generator insufficiency.  
- Concern about novelty/significance: approach is similar to prior stacked/conditional GAN works and may be viewed as incremental.  
- Potential limited generalizability: problem domain may not extend easily to other learning tasks.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with sufficient novelty and thorough evaluation; images are visually consistent but require quality improvements; reviewer’s initial rating: accept.
