- Paper proposes PG2: pose-guided person image synthesis conditioned on a reference image and 18-keypoint heatmap pose, using a two-stage coarse-to-fine generator.
- Stage I: U-Net–like generator with residual blocks produces a coarse global-structure image; uses pose-aware masked L1 to emphasize person over background.
- Stage II: adversarially trained U-Net predicts a residual map to refine high-frequency details, with masked L1 and a pairwise discriminator conditioned on the reference image.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with extensive ablations on pose encodings, losses, and one- vs two-stage training; results show improved sharpness but some artifacts remain.
- Strength: Novel, well-scoped task combining identity image and explicit pose conditioning.
- Strength: Clear two-stage design (global structure then detail) and effective residual-map refinement simplifying learning.
- Strength: Effective pose encoding via keypoint heatmaps and practical pose mask to downweight background mismatch.
- Strength: Thorough ablation studies and good qualitative results; writing is clear and method is reproducible at a high level.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation relies on imperfect metrics (SSIM/IS and masked variants), weakening empirical claims.
- Weakness: User study is preliminary and not sufficiently convincing.
- Weakness: Some architectural choices (impact of residual blocks and FC layers) lack concrete justification or quantitative evidence.
- Quality: Method is technically sound and well-motivated; ablations help but stronger human evaluation and metrics needed.
- Clarity: Paper is readable but should clarify gains from residual blocks and the role of fully connected layers.
- Originality: Novel combination of pose heatmaps and two-stage residual refinement extends prior conditional GAN/image-to-image work.
- Significance: Addresses a practical problem with applications (content creation, data augmentation) and likely to influence follow-up work.
- Suggestion: Discuss iterative refinement/cascaded architectures (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass) more explicitly.
- Suggestion: Provide quantitative/qualitative evidence on how residual blocks help (e.g., SSIM or perceptual metrics).
- Suggestion: Test a one-stage adversarial model with Stage II architecture (fully convolutional U-Net) to isolate effects of FC bottleneck vs one-stage setup.
- Suggestion: Complement SSIM/IS with better evaluations (pairwise human A/B tests, longer exposure, LPIPS or learned perceptual metrics).
- Suggestion: Clarify and provide sensitivity analysis for λ in Stage II to show practical ranges avoiding artifacts vs blur.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel, well-motivated, carefully designed, promising results despite evaluation limitations.
- Rebuttal note: Reviewer remains unconvinced by the brief user study and urges a more substantive human evaluation, but keeps a positive recommendation.
