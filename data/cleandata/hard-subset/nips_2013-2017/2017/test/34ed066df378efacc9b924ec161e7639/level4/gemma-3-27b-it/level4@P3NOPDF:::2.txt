## Review for NIPS 2017 - Pose-Conditioned Human Image Generation

This paper presents a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and human pose. The proposed system employs an adversarial training framework with a two-stage generative network designed to produce high-resolution images. The generator architecture utilizes a U-shape network to initially generate a coarse image from appearance and pose maps. A second generator then refines this coarse output by predicting a residual image, incorporating the original appearance information to enhance detail. The approach is evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.

The paper introduces several noteworthy ideas. First, the task of conditioning image generation on both appearance *and* pose maps is a novel contribution. Second, the technical choices – specifically the stacked generator architecture that predicts residual maps instead of relying solely on direct upsampling, and the overall loss function design – demonstrate thoughtful engineering. 

However, several areas could be strengthened to elevate the paper’s impact. While the results are promising, the generated images currently exhibit visible artifacts, hindering their practical application. This represents a key limitation. Furthermore, the work, while technically sound, could be perceived as another iteration within the rapidly evolving landscape of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). While it tackles an application-specific challenge, its generalizability to other learning problems is limited. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly conveys the core concepts of the proposed architecture. The coarse-to-fine generation strategy, achieved through stacking two networks conditioned on appearance and pose, is effectively explained and logically applied to the problem of generating clothed human figures. The utilization of a difference map for refinement is a minor but valuable technical contribution, demonstrating a practical approach to generating higher-resolution images.

The most significant concern lies with the visual quality of the generated results. Despite the second generator mitigating blurriness from the initial stage, the model struggles to fully recover high-frequency details present in the target appearance. For instance, in Figure 4, the result for ID346 contains noticeable black dots, indicating artifacts in the generation process. Improving the fidelity and reducing such artifacts is critical.

Another potential, though less severe, concern is the novelty of the approach within the broader context of conditional GAN research. The core idea of conditioning GANs and stacking generators has been explored in previous work, such as:

* Han Zhang et al., "StackGAN: Text-to-Photo-realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks," arXiv:1612.03242.
* Xun Huang et al., "Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks," arXiv:1612.04357.

While this paper addresses application-specific challenges related to human pose and appearance, its broader appeal to the NIPS audience might be limited by the incremental nature of the contribution.

Overall, this paper successfully demonstrates a solution to the problem of pose-conditioned human image generation and presents a well-conducted evaluation. The proposed approach demonstrates sufficient technical novelty, and the resulting images, while still needing improvement, are visually coherent. Therefore, my initial rating is **accept**. Addressing the artifacting issues and potentially highlighting the unique aspects of the application domain could further strengthen this work.
