

This paper proposes a GAN-based framework for generating novel-view person images conditioned on skeletal keypoint inputs. While the approach leverages established components (e.g., DCGANs, U-Nets), its primary contribution lies in the specific application to human pose-guided image synthesis.  

**Strengths:**  
The core idea is well-motivated and clearly presented. The empirical results demonstrate preliminary success in producing visually plausible images under certain conditions, suggesting a promising direction for the field.  

**Major Concerns:**  
The work’s practical focus necessitates rigorous evaluation, which is currently lacking. Crucially, **no quantitative metrics** are provided to assess quality (e.g., FID, IS, or user studies measuring perceptual fidelity). Without such benchmarks, it is impossible to objectively evaluate whether generated images are "realistic" or merely superficially plausible. For instance, Figure 1 presents results at insufficient resolution to discern details, and visible artifacts (e.g., distorted limbs, inconsistent textures) persist when comparing generated outputs to target skeletons. This issue extends to all results, including supplementary figures, undermining claims of quality. The absence of quantitative validation—particularly human evaluation studies assessing how frequently generated images deceive observers—significantly weakens the paper’s contribution.  

**Rebuttal Assessment:**  
The authors’ response acknowledges these concerns but fails to provide adequate resolution. While supplementary experiments were added (e.g., limited user studies), they remain superficial and do not address the core issues:  
- The new user study lacks statistical rigor (e.g., small sample size, no control conditions).  
- Quantitative metrics (e.g., FID) are still absent.  
- Visual quality improvements in supplementary results remain marginal, with persistent artifacts.  
Other reviewers have similarly noted unresolved gaps in both quantitative and qualitative evaluation. Given NIPS’ standards for impactful, rigorously validated work, the current submission does not meet the threshold for acceptance. The results remain preliminary, and the work is not yet ready to be presented as a completed contribution.  

**Recommendation:**  
Reject. The paper lacks essential quantitative validation and exhibits insufficient visual quality to support its claims. While the application direction is promising, the current evidence does not justify acceptance at NIPS. The authors should address these evaluation gaps comprehensively before resubmission.
