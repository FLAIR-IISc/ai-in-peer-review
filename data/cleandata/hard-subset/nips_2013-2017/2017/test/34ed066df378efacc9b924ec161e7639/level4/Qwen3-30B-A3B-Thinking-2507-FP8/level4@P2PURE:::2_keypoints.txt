- Introduces a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose maps.
- Employs adversarial training with a two-step coarse-to-fine generative architecture.
- First generator (U-shaped) produces a coarse image; second predicts a residual map to refine it.
- Uses residual-based refinement and a novel loss design instead of direct upsampling.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Contribution: task novelty—conditioning on appearance and pose for human image generation.
- Contribution: technical innovation—stacked residual architecture for high-resolution synthesis.
- Strength: paper is well-structured; coarse-to-fine and residual refinement suit the dressing problem.
- Primary limitation: generated images exhibit visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in Fig.4).
- Secondary limitation: may be perceived as incremental relative to prior stacked GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).
- Limitation: limited generalizability beyond vision/graphics dressing applications.
- Overall: technically sound with sufficient novelty and visually consistent outputs despite quality issues; reviewer’s initial rating: accept.
