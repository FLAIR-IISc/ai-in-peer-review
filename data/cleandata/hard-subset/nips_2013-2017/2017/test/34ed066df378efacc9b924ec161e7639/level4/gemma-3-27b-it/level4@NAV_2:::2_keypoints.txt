- Proposes PG2: pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Uses a two-stage adversarial U-Net-like architecture: stage 1 produces a coarse image, stage 2 predicts a residual (difference) image to refine details.  
- Explicitly conditions on pose keypoints, offering more direct control than previous conditioning signals.  
- Introduces a novel pose mask loss and uses a difference map in stage 2 to speed convergence and refine details.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 (128×64) and DeepFashion (256×256) datasets.  
- Builds on existing GAN and conditional generation literature; the combination is seen as somewhat incremental.  
- Strength: novel task formulation with practical applications (film, data augmentation) and a well-motivated divide-and-conquer design.  
- Strength: thoughtful architecture and promising qualitative results across two datasets.  
- Weakness: generated images exhibit visible artifacts, especially at higher resolution, limiting practical use.  
- Weakness: quantitative analysis is insufficient to conclusively show the benefit of the two-stage approach.  
- Weakness: originality may be perceived as limited due to heavy reliance on prior GAN/conditioning techniques.  
- For acceptance: addresses a novel, useful task; technically sound method; pose mask loss; promising results; clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: image quality issues, incremental novelty, and need for stronger quantitative evidence.  
- Overall: solid contribution with potential; reviewer recommends acceptance.
