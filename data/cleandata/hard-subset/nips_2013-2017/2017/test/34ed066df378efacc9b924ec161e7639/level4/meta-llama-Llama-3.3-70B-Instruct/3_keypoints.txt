- Presents a GAN-based framework for novel-view person image synthesis guided by target skeleton keypoints.
- Relies on established components (DCGANs, U-nets); main novelty is its application to this task.
- Strength: concept is intriguing and writing is clear.
- Strength: experiments show some ability to synthesize somewhat realistic images.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative evaluation (no user studies or metrics to assess realism/deception).
- Weakness: qualitative results are unimpressive—figures are small and generated images contain noticeable visual errors compared to targets.
- Weakness: poor visual quality persists across main and supplementary results, undermining claims of success.
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; authors’ responses did not satisfactorily address quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Overall judgment: work shows promise but is premature for a top-tier venue like NIPS given current results.
