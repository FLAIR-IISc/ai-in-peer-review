- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that synthesizes person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose using a two-stage (pose integration + image refinement) pipeline.  
- Stage 1: U-Net-like network generates a coarse initial image; Stage 2: DCGAN variant refines the image via adversarial training.  
- Contributions: (i) novel task of pose-conditioned image generation, (ii) exploration of pose–image integration methods, (iii) two-stage decomposition for pose transfer.  
- Paper is well-written and idea is interesting.  
- Experiments conducted on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing promising results.  
- Visual quality concerns: generated images are not visually appealing and contain noticeable errors compared to targets.  
- Figure quality issues: some figures (e.g., Figure 4) are too small to evaluate properly.  
- Lacks sufficient quantitative evaluation—suggested user studies to measure human perceptual realism.  
- Needs more detailed analysis and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- NIPS scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 6/10.  
- Overall: promising direction but not yet ready for NIPS without stronger quantitative and visual results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task/approach, promising empirical results, evaluations on two datasets.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor visual appeal and artifacts, insufficient quantitative/user evaluations, mostly known techniques with limited new insights.
