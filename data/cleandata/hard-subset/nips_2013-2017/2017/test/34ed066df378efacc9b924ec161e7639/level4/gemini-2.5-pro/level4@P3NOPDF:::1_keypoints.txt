- Proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine generative architecture for pose-guided person image synthesis using source appearance and target pose heatmaps.  
- Stage 1 produces a blurred, low-resolution structural image; Stage 2 refines it to higher-fidelity output.  
- Addresses a novel, challenging task with useful applications in graphics, animation, and data augmentation.  
- The two-stage generator architecture is well-motivated and an effective contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.  
- Qualitative results are promising, though some artifacts remain.  
- Provides thorough ablation studies that support the key design decisions.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited by lack of established metrics, weakening objective assessment.  
- User study is preliminary and underpowered; a more substantive human evaluation is urged.  
- Suggest expanding related work to include cascaded/stacked iterative refinement methods (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass).  
- Ask authors to clarify how residual blocks improved generation (qualitative, quantitative, or training stability).  
- Recommend an ablation: train Stage 2 adversarially without Stage 1 input to probe causes of blurring in baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—novel problem formulation, sound approach, and compelling results.
