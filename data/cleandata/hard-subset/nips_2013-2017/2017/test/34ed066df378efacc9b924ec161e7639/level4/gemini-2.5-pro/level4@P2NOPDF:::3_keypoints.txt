- Paper proposes a GAN-based pose-guided person synthesis system using established components (DCGANs, U-Nets); contribution is mainly application, not new methodology.
- Addresses an interesting, relevant problem in computer vision.
- Manuscript is well-written and the approach is clearly described.
- Empirical results show the method can generate images that roughly match target poses and have some realism.
- Major weakness: no quantitative evaluation or metrics (no user studies or objective image-quality measures).
- Major weakness: qualitative results are unconvincing—low-resolution outputs, visible artifacts, and discrepancies from ground truth.
- Overall assessment: results appear premature and the method is not yet sufficient to claim success; lack of rigorous evaluation undermines the contribution.
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; authors did not satisfactorily address quantitative and qualitative evaluation concerns, so paper is not ready for NIPS.
