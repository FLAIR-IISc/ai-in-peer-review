- Proposes a GAN-based framework for pose-guided person image synthesis using target skeleton keypoints, mainly combining existing components (DCGANs, U-Nets).
- Main contribution is an application of known methods rather than a new methodology.
- Novel and interesting application area.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Experimental results show some ability to synthesize images with a degree of realism.
- Lacks any quantitative evaluation (e.g., user studies or metrics) crucial for generative tasks.
- Qualitative results are unconvincing: low-resolution images, visible artifacts, and discrepancies from ground truth across results and supplementary material.
- Author rebuttal did not adequately address core concerns; paper not mature enough for publication at a top-tier venue.
