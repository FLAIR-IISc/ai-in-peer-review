- Proposes a GAN-based framework for novel-view person image synthesis conditioned on skeletal keypoints, mainly applying established components (DCGANs, U-Nets).  
- Strength: Idea is well-motivated and clearly presented, with preliminary results showing some visually plausible outputs.  
- Major concern: Lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation—no FID, IS, or robust user studies—to objectively assess image quality.  
- Major concern: Presented figures are low-resolution and contain visible artifacts (distorted limbs, inconsistent textures), undermining realism claims.  
- Major concern: Artifacts and quality issues persist across main and supplementary results.  
- Major concern: Human evaluation is absent or insufficiently rigorous, preventing claims about how often outputs deceive observers.  
- Rebuttal assessment: Authors’ added experiments/user studies are superficial (small sample, no controls) and do not include key quantitative metrics like FID.  
- Rebuttal assessment: Supplementary visual improvements are marginal and do not resolve core quality issues; other reviewers noted similar gaps.  
- Recommendation: Reject—paper does not meet NIPS standards due to missing quantitative validation and insufficient visual quality; needs comprehensive evaluation before resubmission.
