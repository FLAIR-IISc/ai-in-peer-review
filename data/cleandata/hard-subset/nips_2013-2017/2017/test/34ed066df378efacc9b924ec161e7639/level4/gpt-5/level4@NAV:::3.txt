This paper proposes a GAN-based framework to generate novel-view person images conditioned on a target skeleton (pose keypoints). The architecture primarily assembles well-known components (e.g., DCGAN-style modules and U-Nets), so the main contribution lies in the specific computer vision application.

Pros:
- The idea is compelling, the presentation is clear, and the empirical demonstrations suggest the method can produce images that appear realistic to some degree.

Cons:
- Because this work is application-focused, strong empirical performance is crucial. My primary concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For example, user studies could assess how often the generated images fool human observers.
- The visual results are not particularly strong. In Fig. 1, the generated images are too small to assess properly, and even so, there are numerous visible artifacts when compared to the targets. This makes it difficult to conclude that the outputs are high quality or close to real images. Similar issues persist across most results, including the supplementary figures. I believe the direction is promising, but it seems premature to declare success at this stage.

After rebuttal:
I remain unconvinced after reading the authors’ response. As other reviewers have also noted, concerns about both quantitative and qualitative results persist—critical aspects for a practically oriented paper. The rebuttal makes some attempts but does not satisfactorily resolve these issues. I feel it may not yet be ready for a venue like NIPS.
