- Paper proposes an architecture to synthesize person images combining source appearance with a target-pose heatmap.
- Task is novel.
- Introduces a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.
- Manuscript is well written.
- Results show visible artifacts but are promising.
- Provides an extensive evaluation of design choices.
- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak due to lack of suitable metrics.
- Reviewer suggests adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Requests clarification on how residual blocks improve performance (e.g., sharper details, pose consistency, faster convergence).
- Suggests testing Stage 2 network with Stage 1 inputs trained adversarially to determine if Stage 1 blurring is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: confidently recommend acceptance based on novelty and approach.
- Reviewer discounts the preliminary user study and urges a substantive human evaluation in the revision.
