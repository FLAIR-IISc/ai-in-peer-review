Review- Summary:
The paper presents an architecture for generating person images that merges a subject’s appearance from an input image with a specified target pose represented via heatmaps.

Strengths:
+ the task addressed is novel
+ a new two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture
+ clear and well-written
+ despite noticeable artifacts, the results are promising
+ comprehensive evaluation of architectural choices

Weaknesses:
- the quantitative evaluation is comparatively weak; this is understandable (and not a major drawback) given the absence of well-suited metrics

Comments:
Given the iterative refinement nature of the approach, it would be helpful to include recent work on cascaded/iterative refinement in the related work, e.g., [a,b]:
[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. H man pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
- line 110: “we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance.” In what specific ways does it help? Please elaborate.
While the evaluation is generally thorough, I am curious how a single Stage 2 network, given the Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially, would perform. Is the blurring seen with Stage 1 + GAN attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Given the novelty of the problem and the proposed solution, I confidently recommend acceptance.

Rebuttal:
While I appreciate the attempt at a user study, its very preliminary nature makes it unconvincing, so I am not weighing it in my score. The authors are encouraged to add a substantive human evaluation in the revision. Nonetheless, my positive assessment of the work stands.
