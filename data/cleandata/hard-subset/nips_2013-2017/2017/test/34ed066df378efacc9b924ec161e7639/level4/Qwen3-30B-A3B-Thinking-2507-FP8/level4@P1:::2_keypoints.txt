- Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage framework for pose-guided person image synthesis from a reference image and target pose.  
- Stage I generates a coarse structural approximation by integrating pose information; Stage II refines appearance using adversarial training.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on human body regions and suppress background interference.  
- Uses a difference map in Stage II so the generator predicts appearance corrections, accelerating training and avoiding full-image synthesis.  
- Formulates a meaningful task of conditioning generation on both appearance and explicit pose, enabling direct pose control.  
- Explores multiple pose embedding strategies and shows the pose mask loss significantly improves quality.  
- Two-stage architecture separates structural pose generation from appearance refinement, outperforming single-stage baselines.  
- Strong experimental validation on DeepFashion and Market-1501 using SSIM, Inception Score, qualitative results, and ablation studies.  
- Proper comparisons to related work (e.g., Zhao et al. 2017) show more detailed and realistic results.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, reproducible, and supported by effective diagrams and visualizations.  
- Originality: novel combination of two-stage design, pose mask loss, and difference map for pose-guided generation, despite inspirations from StackGAN/stacked GANs.  
- Significance: valuable for fashion, film, and data augmentation, but impact is mainly within vision/graphics.  
- Main weaknesses: visible artifacts, difficulty recovering high-frequency details, and results still distinguishable from real images (14.9% fooling rate on DeepFashion).  
- Paper needs more thorough failure-case analysis and clearer quantification of the pose mask loss’s contribution.  
- Recommendation: solid technical contribution; recommend acceptance with recognition that image quality requires further improvement.
