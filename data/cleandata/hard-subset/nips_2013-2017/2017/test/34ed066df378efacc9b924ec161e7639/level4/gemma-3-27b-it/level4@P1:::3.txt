This paper develops a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images guided by a target pose, represented as keypoints. The system largely utilizes established techniques such as DCGANs and U-Nets, suggesting the primary contribution lies in its specific application to computer vision.

**Pros:**

* The proposed idea is interesting, the writing is clear and easy to follow, and the empirical experiments demonstrate a capacity to synthesize images that appear reasonably realistic to some extent.

**Cons:**

* As this work is application-oriented, achieving strong results is crucial. My main concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For example, conducting user studies to assess how often the generated images can deceive human observers would be valuable. 
* The empirical results are not particularly compelling visually. For instance, the images in Figure 1 are too small to clearly assess quality. Even at this size, noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the generated images to the targets. This makes it difficult to confidently conclude that the results are of high quality or closely resemble real images. This issue persists throughout the results, including those in the supplementary materials. I believe the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, but it may be premature to claim substantial success at this stage.
