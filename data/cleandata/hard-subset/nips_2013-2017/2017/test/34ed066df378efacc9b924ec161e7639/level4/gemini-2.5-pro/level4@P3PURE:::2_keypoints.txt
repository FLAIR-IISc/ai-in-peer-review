- Proposes a pose- and appearance-conditioned generative model for synthesizing human images.
- Uses a two-stage, coarse-to-fine GAN: U-Net-like first generator produces a coarse image; a second generator predicts a residual map to refine high-frequency details.
- Trained and evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Strength: novel and challenging task with clear vision/graphics applications.
- Strength: technical contributions include stacked coarse-to-fine architecture and residual-map refinement to improve high-resolution synthesis.
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly explains the method.
- Weakness: generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover complex high-frequency patterns (e.g., clothing patterns degrading to black dots).
- Weakness: contribution may be incremental—an application-specific extension of existing conditional/stacked GAN frameworks (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).
- Weakness: method is highly tailored to this vision task, limiting apparent generalizability to other domains.
- Conclusion: solid step forward with sufficient novelty; produces visually consistent results but image quality needs improvement; reviewer recommends acceptance.
