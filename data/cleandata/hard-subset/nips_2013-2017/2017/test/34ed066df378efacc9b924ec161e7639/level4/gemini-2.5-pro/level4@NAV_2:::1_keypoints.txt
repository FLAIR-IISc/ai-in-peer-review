- Introduces PG²: synthesizes a specific person in an arbitrary target pose from a source image plus target pose heatmaps.
- Core idea: two-stage coarse-to-fine generation — Stage I (U-Net-like) for global structure/colors, Stage II (conditional GAN) for high-frequency details.
- Novel pose mask loss that focuses learning on the foreground person to reduce background influence.
- Heatmap concatenation for pose embedding is shown to outperform alternatives.
- Comprehensive ablation studies validate key design choices and architectural components.
- Technical design is well-motivated; decomposing global structure and detail refinement is effective.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures and detailed methodology.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM and Inception Score poorly reflect human perceptual quality (Stage I scores higher despite blurrier outputs).
- User study on MTurk is preliminary (1s exposure), limiting strength of perceptual claims.
- Suggest adding related work on iterative/cascaded refinement (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass) to contextualize design.
- Recommend elaborating how residual blocks improve performance (stability, convergence, or quality).
- Ask for discussion on one-stage adversarial model bottleneck (fully-connected layer) and potential of a fully-convolutional one-stage alternative.
- Overall recommendation: accept — original, significant, well-executed contribution likely to inspire follow-up work.
