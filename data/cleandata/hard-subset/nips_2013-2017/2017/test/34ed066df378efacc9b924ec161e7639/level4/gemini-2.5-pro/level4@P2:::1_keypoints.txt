- Introduces novel task: generate an image of a specific person in an arbitrary target pose given a source image and pose keypoint heatmaps.  
- Proposes a two-stage, coarse-to-fine framework (G1 U-Net produces coarse structure; G2 U-Net with conditional GAN refines details).  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus learning on the foreground person rather than background changes.  
- Strength — originality/significance: tackles a challenging, well-motivated problem with clear applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, media).  
- Strength — technical quality: thorough experiments and ablations (pose embedding methods, pose mask loss) supporting design choices.  
- Strength — qualitative results: promising visual transfer of appearance to novel poses, particularly on DeepFashion.  
- Strength — clarity: well-written, clearly structured paper with informative diagrams and figures.  
- Weakness — quantitative evaluation: relies on SSIM and Inception Score which poorly correlate with perceptual quality; user study is preliminary.  
- Suggestion — stronger human evaluation: run more substantive user studies (A/B tests, Likert ratings) to validate perceptual quality.  
- Weakness — contextualization: needs stronger connection to prior cascaded/iterative error-correction architectures (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Weakness — clarification of design choices: unclear how residual blocks improved performance (quantitative, qualitative, or stability).  
- Weakness — ablation needed to isolate two-stage benefit: test G2 architecture in a one-stage adversarial setup to separate coarse-to-fine benefit from architectural differences.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, well-executed contribution despite quantitative evaluation limitations; suggestions given for strengthening the final version.
