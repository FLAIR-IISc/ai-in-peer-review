- Novel task: generate person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose.  
- Method: Pose Guided (PG) network using two-stage coarse-to-fine U-Net-like generators, with the second trained adversarially.  
- Technique: uses a difference-map generation approach to help produce higher-resolution details.  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized and clearly conveys the architecture and approach.  
- Experiments: extensive evaluation on two person datasets with both qualitative and quantitative results.  
- Results: generated images are visually consistent and show convincing details.  
- Limitations: outputs contain visible artifacts (black dots, blurry boundaries) and struggle to recover high-frequency appearance.  
- Community concern: approach may be viewed as an application of existing conditional GANs, which could reduce perceived novelty.  
- Generalizability: method has limited applicability to other learning problems.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans to accept for technical novelty and evaluation quality but asks for improved image quality for practical use.
