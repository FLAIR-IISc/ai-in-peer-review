- Paper proposes a GAN-based method to generate novel views of people from target skeleton keypoints; primary novelty is application rather than new architectures.
- Relies on established techniques (DCGANs, U-Nets) rather than introducing significant methodological innovations.
- Strength: interesting idea, well-written, easy to follow.
- Strength: qualitative experiments show the model can synthesize images that look reasonably realistic in places.
- Major concern: complete lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user study or metrics to assess realism).
- Major concern: visual results are not compelling—figures are small, artifacts are noticeable, and generated images do not closely match target skeletons.
- Supplementary material does not resolve visual quality issues; overall results feel premature to claim high quality or breakthrough.
- After rebuttal, authors did not satisfactorily address quantitative and qualitative evaluation concerns; reviewer believes paper is not ready for a top venue like NeurIPS.
