- Introduces pose-conditioned human image synthesis conditioned on source appearance and target pose.
- Proposes a two-stage GAN generator: a U-Net coarse renderer followed by a residual-refinement stage for high-resolution output.
- Validated experiments on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Main contributions: novel task formulation (appearance+pose conditioning) and a stacked generator that predicts residual maps.
- Loss function design is highlighted as a key methodological component.
- Coarse-to-fine generation strategy and residual refinement are clearly explained and well-suited to virtual try-on.
- Manuscript is well-structured and experimentally evaluated; approach produces visually coherent results.
- Generated image quality has noticeable artifacts and lacks high-frequency detail (e.g., pattern distortions like black dots).
- Second-stage refinement sharpens outputs but still struggles reconstructing fine details from appearance.
- Perceived novelty may be incremental given prior conditional/stacked GAN work (e.g., StackGAN).
- Limited apparent generalizability beyond this specific vision/graphics application.
- Overall assessment: technically novel and promising but image quality needs improvement; reviewer recommends acceptance.
