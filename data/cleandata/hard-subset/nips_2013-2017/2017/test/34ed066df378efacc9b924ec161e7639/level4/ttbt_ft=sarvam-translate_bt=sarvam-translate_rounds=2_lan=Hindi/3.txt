This paper develops a GAN-type system that generates novel-looking person images guided by a target skeleton (represented as a keypoint). The system incorporates most known technologies, such as DCGANs and U-nets, so its contribution focuses on specific computer vision applications.

**Pros:**
* The proposed idea is interesting, the writing is easy to understand, and empirical experiments show that it is capable of synthesizing somewhat realistic-looking images.

**Cons:**
* Since this work is application-oriented, it is important to achieve good results. My main complaint is that there is no quantitative evaluation. For example, it is possible to conduct user studies to evaluate how often the generated images can deceive the human eye. The empirical results are not very compelling. Take Figure 1, for example; the generated images are too small to see. However, there are several noticeable visual errors when comparing the results with the targets. Therefore, it is a bit disappointing to conclude that the results so far are of good quality or close to real images. This has been the problem with almost all the results, including the supporting figures. Personally, I think the authors are on the right track, and the work is promising, while at this stage, it remains to be stopped and declared a victory.

**After Rejection:**
Even after reading the authors' response, I am not convinced. In particular, it has also been pointed out by other reviewers that there are still issues regarding quantitative and qualitative results, which are all important for such a practically oriented work. The rejection shows some effort but still does not address these concerns to a satisfactory level. I think it may not be ready to be well-presented in a place like NIPS.
