- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) with a two-stage pipeline: Stage-I U-Net-like coarse generation and Stage-II adversarial U-Net-like refinement.  
- Explicitly leverages pose information via pose integration followed by image refinement.  
- Tackles the challenging problem of pose-controlled person image synthesis with a sensible divide-and-conquer strategy.  
- Paper is well-written and methodology is clearly explained.  
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies (pose embedding methods, loss functions) demonstrate thorough design investigation.  
- Qualitative results are strong, especially refined outputs showing convincing appearance details.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: reported SSIM and Inception Score do not fully capture perceptual quality.  
- Suggest exploring alternative/complementary metrics or more detailed perceptual/human studies.  
- Use of a pose mask to reduce background influence is clever but needs more detailed impact analysis.  
- Related work discussion is limited; should compare to iterative refinement methods (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016) and cited works [35,9,3,19].  
- Clarify how residual blocks improve performance (line 110) and investigate architectural variants (e.g., Stage-II applied directly to Stage-I outputs, effect of fully connected layer on blurring).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task/approach, well-designed two-stage architecture, thorough experiments, promising qualitative results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak quantitative evaluation, limited related-work discussion on iterative refinement, and insufficient exploration of alternative architectures.  
- Recommends acceptance despite weaknesses; advises larger-scale human evaluation in future work.
