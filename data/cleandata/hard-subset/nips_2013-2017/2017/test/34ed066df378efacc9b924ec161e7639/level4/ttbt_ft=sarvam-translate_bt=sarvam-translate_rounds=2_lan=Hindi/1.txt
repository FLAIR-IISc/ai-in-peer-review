Summary:

This paper proposes an architecture that generates images of a person from a combination of the person's presence in the input image and a specified input pose (using a heat map).

Strengths:

+ The proposed work is novel.
+ A novel two-step architecture from coarse to fine.
+ Well-written.
+ Although the results are with clear artifacts, they are promising.
+ Comprehensive evaluation of design decisions.

Weaknesses:

- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak, which is understandable (and not a major weakness) as there are no suitable evaluation metrics.

Notes:

Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it is suggested that some recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks be incorporated into the related work, for example, [a,b]:

[a] J. Carrera, P. Agarwal, K. Frgaki, and J. Malik. H-Man Pose Estimation with Iterative Error Feedback. At the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked Overglass Network for Human Pose Estimation. At the European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.

- Line 110: "We find that using residual blocks as a basic component improves generation performance." How? Please explain in detail.

While the evaluation is generally comprehensive, I was curious to know how stage 2 network using stage 1 input and training would perform adversititionally. Is the blur seen with stage 1 + gain entirely due to the connected layer?

Overall Rating:

Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I am confidently recommending that the paper be accepted.

Disagreement:

While this reviewer appreciates the effort of the user study, given its very preliminary nature, I do not feel convinced and therefore reduce it in my review rating. The authors are urged to include a concrete human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive review of the work still stands.
