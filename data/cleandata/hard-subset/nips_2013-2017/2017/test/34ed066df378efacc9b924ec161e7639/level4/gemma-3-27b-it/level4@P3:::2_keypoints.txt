- Proposes PG2: a two-stage GAN for pose-guided person image generation (stage1 coarse U-Net, stage2 refine via a difference-map U-Net).
- Conditions generation on both a reference image and a target pose, enabling controllable pose transfer.
- Two-stage architecture and difference map are well-motivated and help training and detail refinement.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus learning on the person and mitigate background changes.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 (re-ID) and DeepFashion, demonstrating applicability to person-image datasets.
- Paper is clearly written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Generated images contain visible artifacts and blurry textures; stage-two improvements are not always photorealistic.
- Novelty is limited since the method builds on established conditional and stacked GAN techniques.
- Evaluation is domain-limited to people; generalizability to other object categories is not shown.
- Quantitative metrics used (e.g., SSIM) may not capture perceived visual quality adequately.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-defined novel task, technically sound method, useful contributions (pose mask, difference map), and feasible results.
- Arguments against acceptance: noticeable image artifacts, limited novelty, and domain-restricted evaluation.
- Overall: solid and promising work that merits consideration—reviewer leans to a weak accept but urges addressing image-quality concerns.
