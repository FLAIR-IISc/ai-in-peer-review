- Paper proposes a GAN-based framework for pose-guided person image synthesis using established components (GAN + U-Net) to generate images conditioned on target keypoints.
- Clear writing and well-defined problem; the task is timely and practically relevant for augmentation and graphics.
- Method sometimes produces plausible images, indicating potential.
- Primary contribution is incremental—mainly integration of standard building blocks rather than novel methodology.
- Major lack of quantitative evaluation: no user studies or rigorous metrics for realism, identity preservation, or pose accuracy.
- Qualitative results are underwhelming with noticeable artifacts and deviations from target pose/appearance; figures are too small to assess quality.
- Insufficient comparison to strong baselines (pix2pix, conditional GANs, pose-transfer/video synthesis methods).
- Missing ablation studies to identify the contribution of individual components (loss terms, skip connections, multi-stage design).
- Suggested quantitative metrics: human perceptual studies, classifier fooling rates, re-identification/face recognition for identity, keypoint-based pose accuracy (PCK/OKS), and distributional metrics (IS, FID, SSIM/LPIPS).
- Suggested baselines and alternatives: pix2pix/conditional GANs and simpler warping or keypoint-driven deformation + refinement methods.
- Requested experimental details: image resolution, speed, memory, dataset splits, identity overlap, higher-resolution figures, zoomed crops, and failure mode discussion.
- Recommended analyses: architectural and loss ablations, generalization across identities/poses, performance vs. pose displacement, and cross-dataset tests.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced: additional results are limited, artifacts persist, and visual evidence remains insufficient; similar concerns echoed by other reviewers.
- Overall: promising direction with clear exposition but insufficient empirical strength and image quality; not ready for NIPS without stronger evaluation and improved results.
