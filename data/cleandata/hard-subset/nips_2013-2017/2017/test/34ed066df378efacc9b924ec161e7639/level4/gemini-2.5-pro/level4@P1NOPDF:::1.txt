### **Summary**

This paper proposes a two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture for person image generation. The model synthesizes an image of a person by combining the appearance from a source image with a target pose, which is specified using heat maps.

### **Strengths**

+ The paper addresses a novel and interesting task.
+ The proposed two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture is a novel and effective approach.
+ The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
+ While the qualitative results contain some discernible artifacts, they are promising.
+ The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their design choices through ablation studies.

### **Weaknesses**

- The quantitative evaluation is somewhat weak. However, this is an understandable limitation given the novelty of the task and the lack of established, suitable evaluation metrics. As such, this is not a major weakness.

### **Comments**

*   Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, the related work section would be strengthened by a discussion of recent work on cascaded networks, such as [a] and [b]:
    [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In *IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)*, 2016.
    [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In *European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV)*, 2016.
*   Line 110: The authors state that "using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance." Please elaborate on how performance is improved.
*   While the evaluation is generally extensive, it would be interesting to see how the Stage 2 network would perform if trained adversarially on the inputs for Stage 1. Could this ablation help determine if the blurring observed with the Stage 1 + GAN model is due to the intermediate fully connected layer?

### **Overall Rating**

Based on the novelty of the problem and the proposed approach, I confidently recommend this paper for acceptance.

### **Post-Rebuttal Comments**

While I appreciate the authors' attempt to include a user study, its preliminary nature makes it unconvincing. I have therefore discounted it in my rating. The authors are strongly urged to include a more substantive human evaluation in the camera-ready version. Nonetheless, my overall positive review of the work and my recommendation for acceptance still stand.
