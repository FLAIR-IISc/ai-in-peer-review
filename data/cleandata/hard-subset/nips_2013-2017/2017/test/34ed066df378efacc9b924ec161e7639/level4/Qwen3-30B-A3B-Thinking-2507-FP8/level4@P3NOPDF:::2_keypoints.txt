- Proposes a pose- and appearance-conditioned human image generation framework using a two-stage (coarse-to-fine) GAN with residual refinement.
- Strength: Novel task framing that jointly conditions on pose and appearance rather than on one modality alone.
- Strength: Stacked U-shaped generator plus residual prediction effectively reduces blurriness and improves high-frequency detail; loss design balances perceptual quality and fidelity.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation and a concise, application-appropriate residual refinement technique.
- Concern: Generated images show persistent artifacts and fail to recover complex textures/high-frequency details; causes (data, architecture) are not sufficiently analyzed.
- Concern: Technical novelty is incremental—closely related to prior stacked/residual GANs (e.g., StackGAN, Huang et al.), so contribution is largely application-specific.
- Concern: Evaluation is narrow (only DeepFashion) and lacks comparisons to recent pose-conditioned baselines and quantitative metrics.
- Overall: Technically sound and feasible but not transformative; artifact issues limit robustness and practical deployment.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requiring quantitative quality metrics (e.g., FID/user studies), clearer contrast with prior stacked GANs, and comparisons to pose-conditioned methods.
