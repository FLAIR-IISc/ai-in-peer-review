- Paper proposes a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to synthesize person images preserving source appearance and matching target pose via keypoint heatmaps.
- Strength: Tackles a novel and timely task of pose-conditioned person image synthesis that preserves identity and appearance.
- Strength: Introduces a well-motivated, novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture (rough generation followed by refinement).
- Strength: Clear and well-written presentation.
- Strength: Promising visual results despite some visible artifacts.
- Strength: Extensive evaluation of design choices and ablation studies.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is limited due to lack of established metrics for the task.
- Suggestion: Discuss connections to cascaded/iterative architectures (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass) and contrast how errors are corrected and supervised across stages.
- Question: Clarify how residual blocks improve generation—provide controlled ablations vs. plain conv blocks, quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, and training dynamics (stability/convergence).
- Experiment suggestion: Test Stage 2 refiner trained adversarially on Stage 1 inputs to see if stage division is essential versus a single expressive refiner.
- Experiment suggestion: Investigate whether Stage 1 blurring or GAN failure is due to the intermediate fully connected layer by replacing/removing it (e.g., fully convolutional features).
- Evaluation suggestion: Add proxy quantitative measures—pose consistency (PCK), perceptual similarity (LPIPS), and a more rigorous user study with adequate sample size and statistical analysis.
- Overall: Reviewer recommends acceptance—solid contribution, thoughtful design, meaningful ablations despite limited quantitative metrics; confidence: high.
- Rebuttal note: Reviewer found the preliminary user study unconvincing and encourages a more rigorous human evaluation, but the positive assessment stands.
