- Paper proposes a two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture to generate person images by combining source appearance with a target pose provided as heat maps.
- Task and two-stage architecture are novel and interesting.
- The proposed two-stage, coarse-to-fine design is a novel contribution.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Generated results are promising despite some discernible artifacts.
- Authors provide extensive evaluation and ablation studies of design choices.
- Quantitative evaluation is somewhat weak, but understandable given the lack of established metrics for the task.
- Reviewer suggests citing and discussing related iterative refinement work (Carreira et al. CVPR 2016; Newell et al. ECCV 2016).
- Reviewer asks authors to elaborate how residual blocks specifically improved generation performance.
- Reviewer asks about training Stage 2 adversarially on Stage 1 inputs to investigate whether blurring from Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but finds the authors' preliminary rebuttal user study unconvincing and urges a more substantive human evaluation for the final version.
