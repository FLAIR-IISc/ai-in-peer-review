- Introduces PG², a method to generate person images by combining source appearance with target keypoint poses.
- Uses a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: Stage 1 (U-Net-like) for global structure, Stage 2 (conditional GAN) for detail refinement.
- Proposes a masked L1 loss to focus learning on the foreground person and reduce background influence.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with extensive ablation studies.
- Novel and significant task with useful applications (data augmentation, content editing) and a strong baseline for pose-guided person generation.
- The coarse-to-fine decomposition is principled and effective for separating global structure from texture/detail synthesis.
- Masked L1 loss is a clever contribution to address changing backgrounds.
- Evaluation is thorough with qualitative comparisons across pose embeddings, losses, and architectures.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and motivations are well-argued.
- Qualitative results are promising: identity and clothing are often preserved though some artifacts remain.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: standard metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) do not align well with perceptual quality and can be misleading.
- User study is preliminary and coarse (binary real vs. fake); a more detailed perceptual evaluation or pairwise comparisons is needed.
- Suggest citing related cascaded/stacked network work (e.g., Carreira et al., Newell et al.) to contextualize the coarse-to-fine design.
- Ask for clarification/evidence on the claimed benefits of residual blocks (qualitative, quantitative, or training stability).
- Recommend an ablation testing a one-stage model using the Stage 2 architecture (G2) trained adversarially to assess whether two stages are necessary.
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid, novel contribution and strong foundation for future controllable human image synthesis.
