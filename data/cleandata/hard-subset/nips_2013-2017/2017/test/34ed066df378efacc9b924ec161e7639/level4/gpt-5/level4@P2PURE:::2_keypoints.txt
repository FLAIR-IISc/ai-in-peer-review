- Presents a human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose, trained adversarially with a two-stage generator producing high-resolution outputs for the discriminator.  
- First-stage U-shaped network creates a coarse image from appearance and pose; second stage predicts a residual (difference map) using the coarse output and original appearance to refine results.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Novelty: conditioning generation on both appearance and a pose map.  
- Technique: stacked architecture that predicts a difference map rather than directly upsampling, plus a specific loss design.  
- Strength: clear organization and effective coarse-to-fine (stacked) strategy appropriate for the dressing problem.  
- Strength: difference map is a small but effective technique for higher-resolution outputs.  
- Main weakness: generated images exhibit visible artifacts and lack high-frequency details, limiting practical applicability (e.g., black dots in ID346 of Fig. 4).  
- Secondary concern: may be perceived as another conditional GAN variant since conditioning and stacked generators have been explored previously (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).  
- Limitation: application-specific focus may limit generalization and broad appeal.  
- Overall: solid solution with sufficient technical novelty and visually consistent results despite quality issues; reviewer’s initial recommendation is accept.
