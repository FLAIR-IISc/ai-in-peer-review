- Paper proposes a GAN-based system for novel-view person image synthesis guided by target skeleton keypoints.  
- Methodology combines well-established components (DCGAN, U-Net); primary contribution is application to this problem, not a new generative method.  
- Strength: tackles an interesting and challenging problem of synthesizing realistic person images in arbitrary poses.  
- Strength: manuscript is well-written and the approach is clearly described.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate feasibility and can produce images that match target poses to some degree.  
- Weakness: No quantitative evaluation—no metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID) or user study to objectively assess image quality or diversity.  
- Weakness: Qualitative results are unconvincing—figures are too small, outputs show significant artifacts, blurriness and distortions in faces, hands, and clothing.  
- Weakness: Results appear preliminary and not close to realistic; presentation may be premature as conclusive success.  
- After rebuttal: Reviewer’s assessment is unchanged; additions in rebuttal are insufficient to address major concerns.  
- Consensus concern: Other reviewers raised similar issues, and the work is not ready for publication at a top-tier venue in its current state.
