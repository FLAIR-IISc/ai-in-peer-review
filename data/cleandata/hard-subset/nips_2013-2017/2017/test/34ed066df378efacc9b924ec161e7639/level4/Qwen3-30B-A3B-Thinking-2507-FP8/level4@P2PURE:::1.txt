

Summary:
The paper introduces an architecture for generating person images by combining the appearance from an input image with a specified input pose (represented via heatmaps).

Strengths:
+ The proposed task is novel.
+ The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is novel.
+ The paper is well written.
+ Despite discernible artifacts, the results remain promising.
+ Extensive ablation studies evaluate key design decisions.

Weaknesses:
- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak; this is understandable (and not a major weakness) given the absence of suitable evaluation metrics.

Comments:
Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, the related work should include recent advances on iterative refinement via cascaded networks, such as [a, b]:
[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In *IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)*, 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In *European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV)*, 2016.

- Line 110: "we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance." Please elaborate on how residual blocks specifically enhance performance.

While the evaluation is extensive, it leaves open the question of how the Stage 2 network would perform when applied to Stage 1 inputs with adversarial training. Additionally, is the blurring observed in Stage 1 + GAN results due to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend acceptance.

Rebuttal:
Although the authors include a preliminary user study, its limited scope renders it unconvincing. I therefore discount this evaluation in my rating. The authors should incorporate a substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive assessment of the work remains unchanged.
