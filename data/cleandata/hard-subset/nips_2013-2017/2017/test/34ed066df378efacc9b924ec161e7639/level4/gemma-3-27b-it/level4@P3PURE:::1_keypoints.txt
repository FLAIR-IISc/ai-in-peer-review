- Paper proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to generate person images in specified poses by combining an input appearance with pose heatmaps.  
- Task is novel and addresses an interesting image-manipulation problem.  
- The two-stage coarse-to-fine design is a novel technical contribution.  
- The paper is well-written and clearly presents the method.  
- Results are promising despite visible artifacts, demonstrating the approach’s potential.  
- Authors provide an extensive evaluation of key design decisions.  
- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak (limited by lack of suitable metrics), though not a major flaw.  
- Reviewer requests discussion of related iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Reviewer asks to elaborate how residual blocks improve generation and which aspects (e.g., fidelity, convergence) benefit.  
- Reviewer asks whether blurring seen with Stage1+GAN is due to the intermediate fully connected layer and suggests testing Stage2 with Stage1 inputs trained adversarially.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; reviewer urges a more substantive human evaluation in revision.  
- Overall recommendation: accept (positive assessment maintained despite concerns).
