- Proposes a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose maps.
- Employs a two-stage adversarial (stacked) architecture with collaborating generators and a discriminator.
- First generator is a U-shaped network producing a coarse image from appearance and pose.
- Second generator predicts residual/difference maps to refine the coarse output and enhance details.
- Uses a tailored loss function design to support the stacked, difference-map approach.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Stacked coarse-to-fine strategy is effective for the dressing problem and producing higher-resolution images.
- Generating difference maps is a noteworthy technique for improving output resolution.
- Generated images still contain visible artifacts and require quality improvements for real-world use.
- Model struggles to recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots/artifacts in ID346 of Fig.4).
- Some readers may view the work as incremental—another conditional GAN application rather than a broad breakthrough.
- Related prior work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs) raises questions about novelty.
- Limited generalizability beyond vision and graphics applications.
- Paper is well-organized, conducts a thorough evaluation, produces visually consistent images, and receives an initial "accept" rating.
