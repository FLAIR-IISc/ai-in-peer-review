- Summary: Proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to synthesize person appearance from an input image conditioned on target pose heatmaps.  
- Strength: Addresses a well-motivated, novel task with a distinctive solution.  
- Strength: Coarse-to-fine design (Stage 1 pose alignment, Stage 2 detail refinement) is technically effective and innovative.  
- Strength: Conducts extensive ablation studies that support architectural and loss choices.  
- Strength: Produces perceptually coherent, competitive results despite minor artifacts (blurring/texture inconsistencies).  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with logical flow and sufficient technical detail.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is overly qualitative with limited standardized metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID, pose consistency), weakening comparative claims.  
- Weakness: Claim that residual blocks improve performance lacks specific technical justification or supporting evidence (mechanism or measured benefits).  
- Weakness: Unexplored variant—adversarially combining Stage 1 outputs/features with Stage 2 might reduce Stage 1 blurring; the role of the intermediate fully connected layer is unclear.  
- Comment: Should cite iterative refinement works (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016 stacked hourglass) that relate conceptually to the coarse-to-fine strategy.  
- Comment: Preliminary user study is too small and not statistically rigorous; recommend a larger, controlled perceptual study.  
- Overall: Significant contribution with strong technical merit; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Rebuttal note: Acknowledges authors’ user study effort but deems it insufficient to change the positive recommendation.
