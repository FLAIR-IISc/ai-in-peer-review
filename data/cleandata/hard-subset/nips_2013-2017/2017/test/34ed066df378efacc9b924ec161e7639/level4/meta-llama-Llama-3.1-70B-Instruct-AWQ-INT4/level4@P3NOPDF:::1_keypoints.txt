- Novel task: generating person images conditioned on input appearance and pose is new and significant.  
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: novel, well-designed, enables iterative refinement for more realistic outputs.  
- Well-written: paper is clear and well-organized.  
- Promising results: outputs show potential despite some discernible artifacts.  
- Extensive evaluation: authors thoroughly evaluated design choices.  
- Weak quantitative evaluation: limited quantitative metrics, though partly understandable given metric gaps.  
- Missing related work: recommend citing iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Clarify residual blocks: need more explanation of how residual blocks improve generation performance.  
- Alternative evaluations suggested: try Stage 2 trained adversarially on Stage 1 inputs and investigate whether blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Overall recommendation: reviewer confidently recommends acceptance; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Rebuttal note: user study is preliminary/unconvincing and should be strengthened; reviewer discounted it but remains positive.
