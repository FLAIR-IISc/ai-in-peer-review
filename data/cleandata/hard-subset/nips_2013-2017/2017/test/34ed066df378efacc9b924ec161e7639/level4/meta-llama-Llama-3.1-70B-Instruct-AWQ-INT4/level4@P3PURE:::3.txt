This paper proposes a GAN-type system to generate novel-view person images guided by a target skeleton, represented as keypoints. The system primarily employs known techniques such as DCGANs and U-nets, so its contribution lies in the specific computer vision application.

Pros:

* The proposed idea is interesting, and the writing is easy to follow. The empirical experiments demonstrate the ability to synthesize images that are realistic-looking to some extent.

Cons:

* As this work is application-oriented, achieving good results is crucial. My main concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, it would be beneficial to conduct user studies to assess how often the generated images can deceive human perception.
* The empirical results are not visually appealing. Taking Figure 1 as an example, the generated images are too small to be clearly visible. Even so, noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the results to the targets. This makes it discouraging to conclude that the results are of good quality or close to real images. This issue persists in almost all the results, including the supplementary figures. While I believe the authors are heading in the right direction and the work is promising, it is premature to declare victory at this stage.

After rebuttal:

I remain unconvinced after reading the authors' feedback. Other reviewers have also pointed out that there are still issues regarding both quantitative and qualitative results, which are essential for a practically oriented work. Although the rebuttal shows some attempts to address these concerns, they do not adequately alleviate my concerns. I feel that the work may not be ready for presentation in a venue like NIPS.
