- Introduces PG²: a two-stage model to generate a person in a novel target pose from a single source image using pose heatmaps.  
- Stage 1 (G1): U-Net-like generator fuses source image with target pose heatmaps to produce a coarse, structurally correct output.  
- Pose mask loss: novel loss that focuses learning on the foreground person and reduces background copying.  
- Stage 2 (G2): U-Net-like conditional GAN that predicts a residual "difference map" to add high-frequency details and refine Stage 1 output.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  
- Task significance: novel and practically useful for data augmentation, virtual try-on, and controllable image synthesis.  
- Architecture motivation: coarse-to-fine decomposition logically separates global pose/structure from appearance refinement.  
- Technical merits: difference-map refinement and pose mask loss are effective, practical contributions.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Image quality issues: generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to fully preserve fine-grained textures.  
- Incremental novelty: builds on established components (U-Nets, conditional GANs, stacked generators) rather than introducing radical architectural innovations.  
- Scope/impact: valuable for vision/graphics but may have limited generalizability to broader ML problems.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, coherent approach that advances pose-conditioned person generation despite remaining artifacts.
