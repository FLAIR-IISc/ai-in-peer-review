- Proposes a novel task: generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose.  
- Introduces Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) using a two-stage pipeline: pose integration then image refinement.  
- Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like network to integrate condition image and target pose to produce a coarse global-structure result.  
- Stage 2 refines the coarse result with a DCGAN variant that generates a difference map to improve resolution.  
- Difference-map refinement is a useful addition for producing higher-resolution outputs.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, well-organized, and includes informative figures and tables.  
- Provides thorough analysis of architecture, loss functions, and training procedure.  
- Significance may be limited: approach could be viewed as applying existing conditional GAN techniques to a specific domain.  
- Generated images still show visible artifacts and need quality improvements for practical use.  
- Evaluation is incomplete: lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a deeper analysis of limitations.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with potential impact, recommended accept with minor revisions.
