- Presents a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose, evaluated on DeepFashion.  
- Uses an adversarial two-stage generator: a U-shaped network for a coarse image, then a second stage predicting a residual to refine output.  
- Key novelty: conditioning synthesis on appearance and pose maps for pose-conditioned human image generation.  
- Technique novelty: stacked architecture that predicts a difference/residual map instead of direct upsampling, plus a tailored loss design.  
- Manuscript is well structured and clearly explains the architecture and coarse-to-fine strategy.  
- Approach is well matched to virtual dressing and produces visually consistent images.  
- Generating a difference map is a modest yet effective method to improve high-resolution outputs.  
- Main concern: generated images still show visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., black speckles in examples).  
- Significance concern: method can be seen as another variant of conditional GANs and has prior related work (stacked GANs).  
- Limited generalizability: strong for vision/graphics use case but less applicable to broader learning problems.  
- Overall assessment: solid solution with sufficient technical novelty, but visual quality needs improvement; reviewer’s initial rating is accept.
