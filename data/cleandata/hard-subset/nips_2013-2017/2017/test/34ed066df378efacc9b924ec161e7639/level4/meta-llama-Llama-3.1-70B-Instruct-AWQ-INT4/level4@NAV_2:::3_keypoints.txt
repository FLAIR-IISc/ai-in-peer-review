- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images in arbitrary poses from a reference image and target pose.  
- Two-stage framework: pose integration to produce coarse global structure, then adversarial U-Net refinement for detail.  
- Introduces a novel pose mask loss to reduce background influence during training.  
- Novel task formulation and clear, easy-to-follow writing.  
- Explores multiple ways to integrate pose information with the input person image.  
- Empirical results show some realistic-looking synthesis but contain noticeable visual errors.  
- Lacks quantitative evaluation (e.g., user studies or metrics) to measure realism or success rates.  
- Figures and visual presentations could be improved to better illustrate results.  
- Technically sound method with clear explanations of the approach.  
- Originality is moderate: new application and task but built on existing GAN and U-Net techniques.  
- Potentially significant for applications (film, image retrieval), but impact limited by current qualitative shortcomings.  
- Pro-acceptance: novelty, clear exposition, application potential; Con-acceptance: missing quantitative evaluation and unimpressive visual quality.
