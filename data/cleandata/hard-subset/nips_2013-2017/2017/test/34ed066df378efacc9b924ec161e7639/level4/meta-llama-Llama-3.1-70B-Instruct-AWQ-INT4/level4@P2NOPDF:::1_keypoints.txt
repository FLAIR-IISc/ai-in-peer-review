- Introduces a novel architecture for pose-guided person image generation combining input appearance with pose heatmaps.  
- Proposes an innovative two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture tailored for the task.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Results are promising despite noticeable artifacts.  
- Provides an extensive evaluation of design decisions.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited due to lack of suitable metrics and should be acknowledged/addressed in future work.  
- Suggests adding related work on iterative refinement (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Requests clarification on how residual blocks improve generation and what specific benefits they provide.  
- Recommends testing Stage 2 trained adversarially with Stage 1 inputs and investigating whether Stage 1 + GAN blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty and thorough evaluation.  
- Rebuttal note: user study is too preliminary and was discounted; authors should include a more substantive human evaluation, though the reviewer remains positive.
