This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view person images guided by a target skeleton, represented as keypoints. The system primarily leverages existing techniques, such as DCGANs and U-nets, so its contribution lies in the specific application of these methods to a computer vision task.

Pros:
* The proposed idea is interesting, and the writing is clear and easy to follow. Empirical experiments demonstrate the system's ability to synthesize images that are realistic-looking to some extent.

Cons:
* Given the application-oriented nature of this work, achieving high-quality results is crucial. My primary concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, the authors could conduct user studies to assess how often the generated images deceive human observers.
* The empirical results are not visually appealing. Taking Figure 1 as an example, the generated images are too small to discern clearly. Moreover, upon closer inspection, numerous noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the results to the target images. This makes it challenging to conclude that the current results are of good quality or close to real images. Unfortunately, this issue persists across almost all the results, including the supplementary figures. While I believe the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, it is premature to declare success at this stage.

After rebuttal:
I remain unconvinced after reading the authors' response. As other reviewers have also pointed out, there are still concerns regarding both quantitative and qualitative results, which are essential for a practical, application-oriented work like this. Although the rebuttal shows some efforts to address these concerns, they are not adequately resolved. I feel that this work may not be ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
