This paper presents a GAN-based system for generating novel-view images of people, guided by a target skeleton represented as keypoints. The architecture relies primarily on established components (e.g., DCGANs and U-Nets), so the main contribution lies in applying these techniques to this specific computer vision task.

Pros:
- The idea is interesting, the writing is clear and easy to follow, and the experiments demonstrate that the method can synthesize images that look realistic to some extent.

Cons:
- Because this is an application-oriented paper, achieving strong results is crucial. My main complaint is the absence of quantitative evaluation. For example, a user study could assess how often the generated images fool human observers.
- The empirical results are not visually compelling. In Figure 1, the generated images are too small to inspect; even so, there are many noticeable visual errors compared to the targets. It is therefore difficult to conclude that the results are high quality or close to real images. Similar issues are present in most of the results, including the supplementary figures. While the direction is promising, it seems premature to declare success at this stage.

After rebuttal:
I remain unconvinced after reading the authors’ feedback. As other reviewers also noted, there are still unresolved issues regarding both quantitative and qualitative results, which are critical for a practice-oriented work. The rebuttal makes some attempts but does not satisfactorily address these concerns. I do not believe the paper is ready to be presented at a venue like NIPS.
