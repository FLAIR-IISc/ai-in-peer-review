- Introduces a novel two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) leveraging explicit pose keypoints, pose mask loss, and a divide-and-conquer strategy.  
- Builds on U-Nets and GANs but makes a specific contribution for pose-guided person image synthesis.  
- Well-motivated two-stage architecture and pose mask loss as key technical contributions.  
- Clear, well-organized writing with detailed methodology.  
- Thorough ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over baselines and visibly sharper outputs with the two-stage model.  
- Potentially useful for practical applications like movie-making and pose-estimation data generation.  
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation: reported SSIM and Inception Score are inadequate proxies for human-perceived image quality and no user studies were conducted.  
- Qualitative results exhibit visual artifacts (incorrect body parts, color mismatches, background inconsistencies) and are not convincingly photo-realistic.  
- Paper does not adequately analyze limitations or challenging failure cases (complex poses, gender inconsistencies).  
- Comparison with prior work ([36]) is limited and lacks sufficient context to demonstrate clear superiority.  
- Conclusion: interesting and promising approach but results and evaluations are not yet strong enough for acceptance without user studies and deeper failure analysis.
