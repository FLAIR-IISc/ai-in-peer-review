Summary:
The paper presents an architecture for synthesizing images of a person that combine the appearance of an individual from a source image with a specified target pose represented by heatmaps.

Strengths:
- The task is novel.
- The paper introduces a novel two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture.
- The manuscript is well written.
- While the results show some visible artifacts, they are promising.
- The paper provides an extensive evaluation of design choices.

Weaknesses:
- The quantitative evaluation is generally weak; this is understandable (and not a major weakness) given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics for this task.

Comments:
- Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be valuable to include related work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks, e.g., [a, b]:
  [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
  [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
- Line 110: “we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance.” In what way does it improve performance (e.g., sharper details, better pose consistency, faster convergence)? Please elaborate.
- While the evaluation is generally extensive, I am curious how a model using the Stage 2 network but with the inputs of Stage 1, trained adversarially, would perform. Is the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and the proposed approach, I confidently recommend acceptance.

Rebuttal:
I appreciate the attempt at a user study; however, given its very preliminary nature, I do not find it convincing and am discounting it in my rating. I urge the authors to include a substantive human evaluation in the revision. Nonetheless, my positive assessment of the work still stands.
