Summary:
The paper introduces an architecture for generating images of a person that combine the subject’s appearance from an input image with a specified target pose (encoded as heat maps).

Strengths:
+ Novel task
+ A new two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture
+ Well-written
+ Despite visible artifacts, the results are promising
+ Comprehensive evaluation of design choices

Weaknesses:
- The quantitative evaluation is generally weak. This is understandable (and not a major drawback) given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics.

Comments:
Given the architecture’s iterative refinement, it would be helpful to include related work on iterative/cascaded refinement, e.g., [a, b]:
[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In CVPR, 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In ECCV, 2016.
- Line 110: “we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance.” In what specific ways? Please elaborate.
- While the evaluation is generally extensive, I am curious how the Stage 2 network would perform if provided with the Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially. Is the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend acceptance.

Rebuttal:
I appreciate the attempt at a user study, but given its very preliminary nature, I do not find it convincing and am therefore discounting it in my rating. The authors are urged to include a substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nonetheless, my positive assessment of the work stands.
