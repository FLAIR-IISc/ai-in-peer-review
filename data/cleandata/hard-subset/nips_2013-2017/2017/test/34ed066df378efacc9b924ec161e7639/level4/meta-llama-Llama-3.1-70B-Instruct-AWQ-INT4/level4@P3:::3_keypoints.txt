- Proposes PG2, a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network for synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses.
- Two-stage framework: pose integration followed by image refinement.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence on generation.
- Employs a variant of DCGAN to refine initial generated images.
- Paper is well-written and the two-stage idea is interesting.
- Provides extensive experimental results on two person datasets.
- Quantitative evaluation uses SSIM and Inception Score, which may be insufficient.
- Qualitative results show noticeable visual errors and are not convincingly close to real images.
- Recommends user studies/human evaluation to assess how often generated images fool humans.
- Suggests improving visual quality and addressing small or error-prone outputs.
- Related work review is thorough but lacks a clear comparison to other pose-based image generation methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid experiments, pose mask loss and DCGAN variant are notable.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, visible visual artifacts, and subpar image quality.
- Reviewer rating: 6, Confidence: 4.
