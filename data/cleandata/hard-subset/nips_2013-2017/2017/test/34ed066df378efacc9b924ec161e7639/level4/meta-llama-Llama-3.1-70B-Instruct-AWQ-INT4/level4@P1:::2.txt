This paper proposes a novel task of generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose, and presents a Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to address this task. The proposed PG consists of two stages: the first stage generates a coarse image by integrating the condition image and the target pose using a U-Net-like network, and the second stage refines the initial result by training a U-Net-like generator in an adversarial way.

The paper is well-organized and effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture. The idea of conditioning on both appearance and pose information is novel and interesting. The proposed approach of stacking two networks to adopt a coarse-to-fine strategy is also well-suited for the problem scenario. The difference map generation technique is a nice touch in generating higher resolution images.

The paper provides extensive experimental results on two person datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The qualitative results show that the proposed PG can generate high-quality person images with convincing details. The quantitative results also validate the advantages of the proposed PG over other methods.

However, the paper still has some limitations. The generated results contain visible artifacts and require further improvement for practical applications. For example, some patterns in the results exhibit black dots or blurry boundaries. Although the second generator mitigates the blurry image from the first generator, it seems the model is still insufficient to recover high-frequency components in the target appearance.

Another possible concern is that some might view the proposed approach as an application of conditional GANs, which have been proposed in the past. While the paper includes application-specific challenges, this might not appeal to a large number of audiences.

In overall, the paper successfully proposes a solution to the pose-conditioned image problem and properly conducts evaluation. The proposed approach presents technical novelty, and the resulting images are visually consistent. However, further quality improvement is needed to make the method more practical.

Strengths:
- Novel task of generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose
- Well-organized and effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture
- Extensive experimental results on two person datasets
- Technical novelty in the proposed approach

Weaknesses:
- Generated results contain visible artifacts and require further improvement
- Some might view the proposed approach as an application of conditional GANs
- Limited generalizability to other learning problems

Acceptance:
I would initially rate this paper as accept, given its technical novelty, well-organized presentation, and extensive experimental results. However, I would like to see further quality improvement in the generated results to make the method more practical.
