- Proposes a Pose Guided Person Generation (PG) network that synthesizes person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Uses a two-stage pipeline: a U-Net-like pose integration stage to produce a coarse image and a DCGAN-based refinement stage for adversarial polishing.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets with visually convincing results.  
- Employs an 18-channel pose heatmap embedding shown effective for guiding generation.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence and focus generation on the human body.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear architecture, training details, and thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluations.  
- Claims novelty by framing the task of conditioning generation on both a reference image and an intended pose.  
- Method is technically sound and supported by comprehensive experiments.  
- Major weakness: generated images still exhibit visible artifacts and fail to recover some high-frequency appearance details.  
- Additional concern: may be viewed as an application of conditional GANs, limiting perceived novelty or audience appeal.  
- Recommendation: Accept, pending minor revisions to address the image quality issues.
