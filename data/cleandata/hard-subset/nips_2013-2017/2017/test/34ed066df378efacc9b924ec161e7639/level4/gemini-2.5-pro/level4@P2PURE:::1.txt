**Summary:**
This paper proposes an architecture to generate an image of a person that combines the appearance from a source image with a specified target pose, which is provided via heat maps.

**Strengths:**
+ The paper addresses a novel and interesting task.
+ The proposed two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture is a novel contribution.
+ The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
+ The generated results, while containing some discernible artifacts, are promising and demonstrate the potential of the approach.
+ The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their design choices, including ablation studies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The quantitative evaluation is somewhat weak. However, this is understandable given the lack of established evaluation metrics for this task, and therefore I do not consider it a major weakness.

**Comments:**
- The proposed architecture utilizes an iterative refinement process. The related work section could be strengthened by discussing recent work on iterative refinement with cascaded networks, such as Carreira et al. [a] and Newell et al. [b]:
[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.

- On line 110, the authors state: "we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance." The authors should elaborate on this point. In what specific ways was the performance improved?

- While the evaluation is extensive, I am curious about the performance of the Stage 2 network if it were trained adversarially on the inputs intended for Stage 1. This might also help clarify whether the blurring observed with the Stage 1 + GAN model is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.

**Overall Rating:**
Given the novelty of both the problem formulation and the proposed approach, I confidently recommend this paper for acceptance.

**Rebuttal:**
I appreciate the authors' attempt to include a user study in their rebuttal. However, due to its preliminary nature, I do not find the results convincing and have not factored them into my rating. I strongly encourage the authors to incorporate a more substantive human evaluation in the final version of the paper. Nevertheless, my positive assessment and recommendation for acceptance remain unchanged.
