- Proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation (PG) network to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Uses a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: pose integration for a coarse image and adversarial refinement for details.  
- Task is valuable and offers fine control over generated human figures.  
- Two-stage U-Net-like design is sensible and effectively decomposes the problem.  
- Experiments are extensive, including ablations on pose embedding methods, loss functions, and architectures.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Generated images are promising but exhibit artifacts and imperfections.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM and Inception Score provide an incomplete assessment; mask-SSIM helps but remains hard to interpret.  
- Related work section could include more recent cascaded/iterative refinement literature (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016).  
- Ablation gap: impact of the fully connected layer in Stage-I on blurring is noted but not directly tested.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; short exposure time (1s) may be insufficient.  
- Request clarification on why residual blocks improve generation (e.g., gradient flow, feature reuse).  
- Suggest evaluating a single-stage adversarial network on Stage-I output to validate the two-stage benefit.  
- Failure cases (complex poses, data imbalance) should be discussed more explicitly.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel important problem, promising results, thorough evaluation, clear writing, and constructive discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited quantitative metrics and weak preliminary user study evidence.  
- Overall assessment: minor weaknesses notwithstanding, reviewer confidently recommends acceptance as a strong contribution likely to inspire further work.
