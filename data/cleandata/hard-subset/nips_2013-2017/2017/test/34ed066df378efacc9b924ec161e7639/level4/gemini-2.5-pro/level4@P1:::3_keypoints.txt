- Proposes a two-stage generative model to synthesize person images in novel poses conditioned on a source image and target keypoint pose.
- Combines established components (U-Net, GANs); main contribution is the specific architecture/application rather than a novel methodology.
- Addresses an important, challenging task with clear practical applications (data augmentation, content creation).
- Manuscript is well-written and easy to follow.
- Results show the method can transfer overall structure and appearance of the target pose, yielding some degree of realism.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: relies on SSIM and lacks perceptual/user studies or fooling-rate measures that better assess realism.
- Qualitative results are unconvincing: low-resolution figures, visible artifacts, and blurry/distorted clothing, faces, and limbs.
- Current outputs do not reach photorealism; work appears preliminary for claims of “high-quality” or “convincing” generation.
- Author rebuttal did not sufficiently address core evaluation and result-quality concerns.
- Reviewer maintains the assessment that the paper is not ready for publication at a top-tier venue in its current form.
