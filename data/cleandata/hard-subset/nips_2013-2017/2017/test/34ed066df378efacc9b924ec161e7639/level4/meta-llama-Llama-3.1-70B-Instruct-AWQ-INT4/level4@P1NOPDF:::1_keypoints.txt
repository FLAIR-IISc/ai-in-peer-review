- Paper proposes a novel two-stage architecture that generates person images by combining input appearance with a target pose represented as heat maps.

- Task is novel and makes a meaningful contribution to image generation.

- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a unique and effective design choice.

- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.

- Results are promising despite some discernible artifacts.

- Authors provide an extensive evaluation of design decisions, yielding useful insights.

- Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics for the task.

- Reviewer suggests adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).

- Reviewer asks for clarification on how residual blocks improve generation and what specific benefits they provide.

- Reviewer asks whether adversarial training of Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs (and the role of the intermediate fully connected layer) could reduce Stage 1 blurring.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and approach.

- Rebuttal note: the user study is preliminary and unconvincing; reviewer discounted it and requests a more substantive human evaluation, but the acceptance recommendation remains.
