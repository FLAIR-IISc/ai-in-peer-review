The paper presents a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose. The approach adopts an adversarial training framework with a two-stage generator that produces a high-resolution output for a discriminator. In the generator, the first stage is a U-shaped network that generates a coarse image from the appearance and pose maps; the second stage takes this coarse output along with the original appearance to predict a residual that refines the coarse result. Evaluation is conducted on the DeepFashion dataset.

The paper introduces several notable ideas.
* Task novelty: conditioning human image synthesis on appearance and pose maps
* Techniques: a stacked architecture that predicts a difference/residual map rather than performing direct upsampling, along with a tailored loss design

The paper could be strengthened in the following respects.
* Image quality still needs improvement
* Significance: the method may be viewed as another variant of a GAN architecture
* Problem domain: strong vision/graphics use case, but limited generalizability to other learning problems

The manuscript is well structured and clearly explains the core aspects of the architecture. Given appearance and pose, the generator employs a two-stage, coarse-to-fine strategy. The approach is effectively applied to the virtual dressing scenario and appears well matched to the problem setting. Generating a difference map is a modest yet effective technique for producing higher-resolution outputs.

The main concern is that the generated images exhibit visible artifacts and require further refinement for practical use. For instance, in ID346 of Fig. 4, patterning leads to black speckles in the final image. Although the second generator reduces the blur from the first, the model still struggles to recover high-frequency details in the target appearance.

A secondary, less critical concern is that the method could be framed as an application of conditional GANs. Conditioning and stacking generators within adversarial training have prior art; for example, the following arXiv works. While the paper addresses application-specific challenges, this may limit its appeal to a broader audience.
* Han Zhang, Tao Xu, Hongsheng Li, Shaoting Zhang, Xiaolei Huang, Xiaogang Wang, Dimitris Metaxas, "StackGAN: Text to Photo-realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks", arXiv:1612.03242.
* Xun Huang, Yixuan Li, Omid Poursaeed, John Hopcroft, Serge Belongie, Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks, arXiv:1612.04357.

Overall, the paper offers a solid solution to pose-conditioned human image generation and provides appropriate evaluation. The approach presents sufficient technical novelty. While the visual quality still needs improvement, the model produces images that are at least visually consistent. My initial rating is accept.
