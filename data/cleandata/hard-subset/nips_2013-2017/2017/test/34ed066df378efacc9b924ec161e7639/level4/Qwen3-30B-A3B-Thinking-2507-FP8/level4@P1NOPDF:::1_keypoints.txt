- Summary: Proposes a novel two-stage architecture to generate images of people by synthesizing appearance from an input image conditioned on target pose heatmaps.  
- Strength: The task itself is highly novel.  
- Strength: Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a meaningful technical contribution.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
- Strength: Results are promising despite visible artifacts.  
- Strength: Provides extensive evaluation of key design choices.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is somewhat limited due to absence of established metrics.  
- Comment: Recommend citing recent iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Comment: Request clarification on how residual blocks specifically improve generation (e.g., faster convergence, fewer artifacts, better pose alignment).  
- Comment: Ask whether Stage 2 trained adversarially on Stage 1 outputs would improve results and whether blurring with Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Overall: Novelty and approach justify a strong recommendation for acceptance.  
- Rebuttal: Preliminary user study is unconvincing—recommend replacing with a substantive human evaluation, though this does not change the positive acceptance recommendation.
