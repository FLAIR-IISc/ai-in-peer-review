- Paper proposes a GAN-based system to synthesize novel-view person images conditioned on target skeleton keypoints.
- Method is mostly an assembly of existing components (DCGAN, U-Net), so novelty is application-focused rather than methodological.
- Core idea (synthesizing person images from poses) is compelling and the paper is clearly written.
- Empirical results show some degree of realism in generated images.
- Major weakness: complete lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user study or metrics assessing realism).
- Qualitative results are unconvincing—generated images are small, show noticeable artifacts and discrepancies across examples and supplementary material.
- Work appears immature and not close to photorealistic; significant improvements are needed for high-quality results.
- After rebuttal, authors did not sufficiently address the evaluation and quality concerns; reviewer maintains recommendation against publication.
