Summary:
This paper proposes a novel architecture for generating images of a person by combining the appearance of a person in an input image with a specified input pose represented as heat maps. The proposed task is innovative, and the authors present a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture that is well-suited for this problem.

Strengths:
+ The proposed task is novel and contributes to the field of image generation.
+ The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a unique approach to addressing this problem.
+ The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
+ Although the results exhibit discernible artifacts, they demonstrate promise and potential for future improvement.
+ The authors conduct an extensive evaluation of their design decisions, providing valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

Weaknesses:
- The quantitative evaluation is generally weak, which is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics for this task. However, this does not significantly detract from the overall quality of the paper.

Comments:
Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be beneficial to include a discussion of recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in the related work section. For example, the authors could cite [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016, and [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.

On line 110, the authors state that "we find that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance." However, it would be helpful to elaborate on how residual blocks contribute to improved performance.

While the evaluation is extensive, it would be interesting to see how the Stage 2 network performs when given the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially. Additionally, is the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN due to the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend accepting this paper.

Rebuttal:
Although I appreciate the authors' attempt at a user study, I find it too preliminary to be convincing and have discounted it in my review rating. I strongly encourage the authors to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive review of the work remains unchanged.
