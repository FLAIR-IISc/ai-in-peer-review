This paper introduces a GAN-based system for generating novel views of people from a target skeleton representation (defined by keypoints). The architecture relies largely on established techniques such as DCGANs and U-nets, positioning the contribution as a specific application within computer vision.

**Pros:**
* The core idea is compelling, the paper is well-written and easy to understand, and the empirical results demonstrate a capacity to synthesize images with a degree of realism.

**Cons:**
* Given the application-oriented nature of this work, strong results are crucial. My primary concern is the complete lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, user studies could assess the realism of the generated images by measuring their ability to convincingly fool human observers. 
* The presented empirical results are not visually compelling. For example, the images in Figure 1 are too small for detailed inspection, and even at this scale, noticeable visual artifacts are apparent when comparing the generated output to the target skeletons. Consequently, the claim of high-quality or near-photorealistic results feels premature. This issue persists throughout the results, including those in the supplementary material. While I believe the authors are pursuing a promising direction, further development is needed before claiming significant success.

**After Rebuttal:**
I remain unconvinced after reviewing the authors’ response. Several reviewers, including myself, have raised concerns regarding both quantitative and qualitative evaluation – both of which are essential for a practically-focused study. The rebuttal attempts to address these points, but does not do so to a satisfactory degree. In my assessment, this work is not yet ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
