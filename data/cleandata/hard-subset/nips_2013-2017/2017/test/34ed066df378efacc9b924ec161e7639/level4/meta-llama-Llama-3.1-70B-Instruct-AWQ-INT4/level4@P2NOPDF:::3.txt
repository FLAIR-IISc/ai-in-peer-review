This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view person images guided by a target skeleton represented as keypoints. The system primarily employs established techniques such as DCGANs and U-nets, making the contribution mainly focused on the specific computer vision application.

The paper has several positive aspects. The proposed idea is intriguing, the writing is clear and easy to follow, and the empirical experiments demonstrate the ability to synthesize realistic-looking images to some extent.

However, there are several drawbacks that detract from the overall quality of the paper. As this work is application-oriented, achieving good results is crucial. My primary concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, the authors could have conducted user studies to assess how often the generated images can deceive human eyes. Furthermore, the empirical results are not visually appealing. The generated images in Figure 1, for example, are too small to discern, and even at this scale, noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the results to the target images. This makes it difficult to conclude that the results are of good quality or close to real images. Unfortunately, this issue persists in almost all the results, including the supplementary figures. While I believe the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, it is premature to declare victory at this stage.

After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I remain unconvinced. Other reviewers have also pointed out issues with both quantitative and qualitative results, which are essential for a practical, application-oriented work like this. Although the rebuttal attempts to address these concerns, they are not adequately resolved. I feel that the paper may not be ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
