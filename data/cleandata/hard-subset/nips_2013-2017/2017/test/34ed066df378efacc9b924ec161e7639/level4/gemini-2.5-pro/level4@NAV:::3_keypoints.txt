- Paper proposes a GAN-based generative framework to synthesize novel views of a person conditioned on target pose represented by skeletal keypoints.
- Architecture mainly reuses established components (DCGANs, U-Nets); contribution is primarily an application rather than methodological novelty.
- Strength: core idea is compelling and well-motivated.
- Strength: manuscript is clearly written and easy to understand.
- Strength: empirical results show some capacity to generate visually plausible images.
- Weakness: no quantitative evaluation (e.g., perceptual studies) to measure realism or human indistinguishability.
- Weakness: qualitative results are unconvincing—low-resolution figures and visible artifacts limit fidelity and photorealism claims.
- Weakness: supplementary material suffers from the same quality issues; overall work feels premature and needs refinement.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer’s assessment unchanged; authors’ responses and additional results did not resolve key quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Conclusion: paper is not yet mature enough for publication at a premier venue like NeurIPS.
