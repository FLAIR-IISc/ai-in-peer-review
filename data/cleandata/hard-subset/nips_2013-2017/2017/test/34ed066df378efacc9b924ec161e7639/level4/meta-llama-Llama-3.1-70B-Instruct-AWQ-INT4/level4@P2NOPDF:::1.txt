**Summary**

The paper introduces a novel architecture for generating images of a person by combining the appearance of a person in an input image with a specified input pose represented as heat maps. This approach offers a unique solution to the problem of pose-guided person image generation.

**Strengths**

* The proposed task is novel and contributes to the field of computer vision.
* The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is innovative and well-suited for the task at hand.
* The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed approach.
* Although the results exhibit discernible artifacts, they are promising and demonstrate the potential of the proposed method.
* The authors provide an extensive evaluation of design decisions, which helps to justify their choices and provide insight into the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

* The quantitative evaluation is limited, which is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics for this task. However, this limitation should be acknowledged and addressed in future work.

**Comments**

* Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be beneficial to include recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in the related work section. Specifically, the authors may want to consider including [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016, and [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
* On line 110, the authors state that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance. However, it would be helpful to elaborate on how this improvement is achieved and what specific benefits residual blocks provide in this context.
* While the evaluation is extensive, it would be interesting to see how the Stage 2 network performs when trained adversarially with the inputs for Stage 1. Additionally, the authors may want to investigate whether the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN is due to the intermediate fully connected layer.

**Overall Rating**

Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend that the paper be accepted. The proposed method shows promise, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach.

**Rebuttal**

While I appreciate the authors' attempt at a user study, I find it too preliminary to be convincing. Therefore, I am discounting it in my review rating. I urge the authors to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nonetheless, my positive review of the work still stands.
