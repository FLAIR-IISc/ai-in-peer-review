Summary:

This paper proposes an architecture that generates images containing a person's face and a specific input pose (using a heat map) within the input image.

Strengths:

+ The proposed work is new.
+ The architecture ranges from broad to subtle across two stages.
+ Well-written.
+ Although there are some flaws in the results, they are promising.
+ Comprehensive evaluation of design decisions.

Weaknesses:

- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak; this is understandable (and not a major weakness) because there is no suitable evaluation metric.

Comments:

Due to the repetitive refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it is suggested to include some recent work related to iterative refinement using cascaded networks, such as, [a,b]:

[a] J. Careera, P. Agarwal, K. Fragkiani, and J. Malik. H. Man Pose Estimation with Iteration Error Feedback. IEEE Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) Conference, 2016.
[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked Wireglass Networks for Human Pose Estimation. European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV) Conference, 2016.
- Line 110: "We see that generation performance improves when residual blocks are used as basic elements". How? Please explain in detail.

Although the evaluation is generally comprehensive, I was curious how the stage 2 network would work with stage 1 input and trained adversities. Is the blurring seen with stage 1 + GAN due to the fully connected intermediate layers?

Overall Rating:

Based on the problems and novelty of the method, I confidently recommend that this paper should be accepted.

Disagreement:

Although this reviewer user has praised the study's efforts, due to its preliminary nature, I do not find it convincing and therefore reject it in my review rating. The authors are requested to include a significant human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive review of the work still stands.
