Summary:
This paper proposes a novel architecture for generating images of a person by combining the appearance of a person in an input image with a specified input pose represented using heat maps.

Strengths:
+ The proposed task is novel and contributes to the field of image generation.
+ The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is a unique approach to addressing this task.
+ The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
+ Although the results contain discernible artifacts, they are promising and demonstrate the potential of the proposed method.
+ The authors conduct an extensive evaluation of their design decisions, providing valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

Weaknesses:
- The quantitative evaluation is generally weak, which is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics for this task. However, this is not a major weakness.

Comments:
Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be beneficial to include a discussion of recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in the related work section. For example, [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016, and [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.

On line 110, the authors state that "we find that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance." However, it would be helpful to elaborate on how residual blocks improve performance. What specific benefits do they provide, and how do they contribute to the overall success of the architecture?

While the evaluation is extensive, it would be interesting to see how the Stage 2 network performs when given the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially. Is the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN due to the intermediate fully connected layer? Exploring this question could provide additional insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed architecture.

Overall rating:
Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend that the paper be accepted.

Rebuttal:
Although I appreciate the authors' attempt to conduct a user study, I find the results unconvincing due to their preliminary nature. I am discounting the user study in my review rating. However, I strongly encourage the authors to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Despite this, my overall positive assessment of the work remains unchanged.
