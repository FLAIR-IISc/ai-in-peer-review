- Introduces pose-guided person image generation: generate person images from a reference image and target pose using pose keypoint heatmaps.  
- Proposes PG2: a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture (Stage I: pose-aligned coarse image; Stage II: adversarial refinement) with a pose mask loss focusing on human regions.  
- Originality: novel task and well-motivated two-stage design that explicitly conditions on pose keypoints rather than attributes or labels.  
- Technical quality: carefully designed architecture, thorough implementation details, and convincing qualitative superiority of the two-stage approach.  
- Clarity: well-written paper with clear explanations, figures, and reproducibility details.  
- Significance: practical applications (content creation, data augmentation) and a framework potentially adaptable to other generation tasks.  
- Quantitative evaluation weakness: heavy reliance on qualitative results and a small user study (30 users); lacks established metrics and broader baselines.  
- Technical detail gap: claims about residual blocks improving performance lack quantitative evidence; unclear why G1+D (Stage I + GAN) yields blurry outputs.  
- Related work gap: should better compare and contextualize with iterative/coarse-to-fine refinement literature (e.g., iterative pose estimation works).  
- Recommendation: overall positive—novel, strong execution, recommend acceptance despite evaluation limitations.  
- Suggestion 1: expand related work to include iterative refinement approaches.  
- Suggestion 2: provide quantitative comparisons showing residual vs. non-residual block performance (e.g., SSIM/IS).  
- Suggestion 3: enlarge the user study and compare against additional baselines.  
- Suggestion 4: analyze and address causes of blurriness when combining Stage I with adversarial training (G1+D).  
- Pro/Con summary: pro—novel task, strong execution, clear writing, effective two-stage approach; con—insufficient quantitative validation and some unsupported technical claims.
