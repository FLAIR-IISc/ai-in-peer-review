- Paper proposes PG2: a two-stage network that synthesizes a person in a target pose from a reference image using 18-keypoint heatmap conditioning.  
- Stage I (G1) is a U-Net-like pose integrator producing a coarse image with masked L1 loss emphasizing foreground.  
- Stage II (G2) is a conditional GAN that predicts a difference map to refine G1 output, with a pairwise discriminator conditioned on the input.  
- Evaluations on DeepFashion and Market-1501 compare pose embeddings, masked L1 vs standard L1, and one-stage vs two-stage adversarial training.  
- Reports SSIM/IS and mask-SSIM/mask-IS metrics and a small AMT user study; qualitatively improves over prior multi-view person synthesis.  
- Strength: Novel formulation—conditioning on reference image plus explicit target pose enables controllable person synthesis.  
- Strength: Effective coarse-to-fine two-stage design separating global pose structure from high-frequency details.  
- Strength: Good architectural choices—keypoint heatmaps, masked L1, GAN difference map, and pairwise discriminator.  
- Strength: Extensive ablations on pose encoding, loss variants, and training strategies across resolutions.  
- Strength: Clear writing and illustrative figures showing qualitative gains.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation limited by SSIM/IS shortcomings for perceptual realism despite mask adaptations.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly relate PG2’s coarse-to-fine design to iterative/cascaded refinement works in pose estimation (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass).  
- Suggestion: Clarify the benefit of residual blocks with ablation reporting which metrics (SSIM/IS/AMT/sharpness) improve and by how much.  
- Suggestion: Provide ablation of one-stage adversarial training using the G2 architecture end-to-end to isolate effects of G1’s fully connected bottleneck vs two-stage optimization.  
- Suggestion: Quantify training speed-up from learning a difference map in G2 (e.g., convergence curves or epochs to target metrics).  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity of masked L1 to mask quality and λ trade-off.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen human evaluation (larger AMT study, multiple exposures, pairwise comparisons, inter-rater consistency).  
- Assessment: Technically sound, well-described, original in conditioning and decomposition, and significant for controllable human synthesis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept—novel and convincing qualitatively with solid ablations, but human evaluation should be strengthened for final version.  
- Rebuttal note: Preliminary user study not convincing; author encouraged to run a more substantive human evaluation while core technical contribution remains positively assessed.
