- Paper presents a human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose using a two-stage adversarial architecture for high-resolution images on DeepFashion.
- Generator has two networks: a U-shaped first stage producing a coarse image, and a second stage predicting a residual (difference) map for refinement.
- Novel task formulation: conditioning image generation on both appearance and pose maps.
- Effective technical choices: stacked/coarse-to-fine architecture, residual-map prediction, and a carefully designed loss function.
- Evaluation performed on the DeepFashion dataset with sound experimental setup.
- Strength: well-organized paper that clearly conveys the architecture and its application to clothing/dressing.
- Strength: difference-map refinement is a small but valuable technique for higher-resolution outputs.
- Primary concern: visible artifacts and failure to fully recover high-frequency appearance details (e.g., black dots in examples).
- Secondary concern: approach may be perceived as another conditional GAN variant given prior work on conditioning and stacking generators.
- Limitation: application focus on clothing/pose may reduce generalizability to broader learning problems.
- Overall assessment: technically novel with visually consistent results but requires improved image quality; reviewer’s initial rating: accept.
