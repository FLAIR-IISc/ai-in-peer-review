

Review Summary:
The paper introduces an architecture for generating person images by integrating the appearance from an input image with a specified pose (represented via heatmaps).

Strengths:
+ Novel task formulation
+ Innovative two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture
+ Well-structured presentation
+ Results, though exhibiting visible artifacts, demonstrate encouraging potential
+ Comprehensive assessment of architectural choices

Weaknesses:
- Quantitative evaluation is generally limited; this is understandable (and not a significant drawback) due to the absence of established evaluation metrics

Comments:
- Given the iterative refinement aspect of the proposed architecture, the related work should incorporate recent advances in iterative refinement via cascaded networks, such as [a,b]:
  [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In CVPR, 2016.
  [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In ECCV, 2016.
- Line 110: "we find that using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance" – Please specify the nature of the performance improvement.
- While the evaluation is thorough, it would be valuable to explore Stage 2 network performance when fed Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially. Could the blurring observed in Stage 1 + GAN stems from the intermediate fully connected layer?

Overall Rating:
The novelty of the problem and methodology warrants strong recommendation for acceptance.

Rebuttal:
Although the authors included a preliminary user study, its limited scope renders it insufficient for evaluation purposes. I therefore disregard this component in my assessment. The authors must incorporate a robust human evaluation in the revision. However, my endorsement of the core technical contribution remains unchanged.
