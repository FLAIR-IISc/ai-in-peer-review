- Proposed a human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose using an adversarial two-step (stacked) generator to produce high-resolution images.
- Generator design: first U-shaped network produces a coarse image from appearance and pose; second network predicts a residual (difference map) to refine the coarse output.
- Novelty: task-level novelty in conditioning on both appearance and pose maps for human image generation.
- Novelty: architectural novelty in predicting a difference map (residual) rather than direct upsampling, plus a specific loss design.
- Evaluation performed on the DeepFashion dataset with proper experimental setup.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the coarse-to-fine, conditioned generation strategy.
- Method is well-suited to the dressing/pose-transfer scenario and produces visually consistent results.
- Main weakness: generated images have visible artifacts and overall image quality needs substantial improvement.
- Model struggles to recover high-frequency details (e.g., pattern artifacts like black dots in Fig.4 ID346) despite the second-stage refinement.
- Perceived as incremental: can be viewed as another conditional/stacked GAN, related to prior work like StackGAN and Stacked GANs.
- Limited generalizability: problem domain may be hard to extend to other learning problems beyond the specific application.
- Overall assessment: technically novel enough with acceptable evaluation and visual consistency; reviewer’s initial recommendation: accept.
