**Revised Review**

**Summary:**
This paper introduces a novel architecture for generating images of a person by combining the appearance of a person from an input image with a specified pose, represented as heat maps. The proposed method is innovative and demonstrates potential in this area.

**Strengths:**

* The task addressed in the paper is original and contributes to the field.
* The authors propose a unique two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture that shows promise.
* The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.
* Although the results exhibit noticeable artifacts, they are encouraging and indicate the method's potential.
* The evaluation of design decisions is thorough and provides valuable insights.

**Weaknesses:**
The quantitative evaluation is somewhat limited, which is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics. However, this is not a major concern.

**Comments:**
Given the iterative refinement nature of the proposed architecture, it would be beneficial to include recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in the related work section, such as [a,b]:

[a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.

[b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.

* In line 110, the authors mention that using residual blocks as basic components improves generation performance. It would be helpful to elaborate on this point and explain how this improvement is achieved.
* Although the evaluation is extensive, it would be interesting to see how using the Stage 2 network with the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially would perform. Additionally, is the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN due to the intermediate fully connected layer?

**Overall Rating:**
Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I strongly recommend accepting this paper.

**Rebuttal:**
While I appreciate the authors' attempt at a user study, I find it too preliminary to be convincing and have discounted it in my review rating. I encourage the authors to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nevertheless, my positive review of the work remains unchanged.
