- Proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images using target skeleton keypoints; largely assembles established components (DCGANs, U-Nets), so contribution is application-oriented.
- Idea is interesting and the paper is clearly written.
- Empirical results show some degree of realism in generated images.
- Major weakness: lacks quantitative evaluation (no metrics or user studies to assess image quality or fooling rate).
- Qualitative results are not visually compelling—figures are small, show noticeable visual errors, and do not closely match real images or target skeletons.
- Claims of good-quality or realistic outputs are premature; further development is needed.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced—quantitative and qualitative concerns were not adequately addressed.
- Overall judgment: paper is not ready for presentation at a top venue like NIPS due to unresolved evaluation and result issues.
