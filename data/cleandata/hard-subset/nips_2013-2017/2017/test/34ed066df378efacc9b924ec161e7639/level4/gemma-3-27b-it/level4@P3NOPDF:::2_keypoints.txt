- Proposes pose-conditioned human image generation conditioned on both appearance and pose maps.  
- Uses a two-stage, stacked U-shaped generator: first produces a coarse image, second predicts a residual/difference map to refine details.  
- Employs adversarial training and a carefully designed loss function to enable high-resolution outputs.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset with generally coherent visual results.  
- Technical contributions include the coarse-to-fine stacking and residual refinement strategy.  
- Generated images still exhibit visible artifacts and fail to recover some high-frequency details, limiting practical use.  
- Example failure: noticeable black-dot artifacts in Figure 4 (ID346).  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental given prior stacked/conditional GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents the architecture and methodology.  
- Recommendation: accept, but should address artifact reduction and better emphasize application-specific novelty.
