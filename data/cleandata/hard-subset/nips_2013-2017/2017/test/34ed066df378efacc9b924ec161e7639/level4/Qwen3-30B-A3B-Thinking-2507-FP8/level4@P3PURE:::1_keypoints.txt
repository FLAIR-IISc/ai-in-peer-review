- Summary: proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to synthesize person images conditioned on an input appearance image and target pose heatmaps.  
- Novel task: addresses a meaningful and underexplored problem in person image synthesis.  
- Architecture: the two-stage coarse-to-fine design is innovative, well-motivated, and iterative-refinement–oriented.  
- Clarity and soundness: paper is clearly written and technically sound.  
- Visual quality: generated images show promising quality despite visible artifacts.  
- Evaluation: includes extensive ablation studies on design choices.  
- Quantitative evaluation: limited by lack of established metrics for this task, reducing quantitative rigor.  
- Related work: should cite iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Residual blocks: reviewer asks for clarification on how residual blocks improve performance (stability, artifact reduction, fidelity).  
- Stage 2/GAN question: ask how Stage 2 performs when initialized with Stage 1 outputs and trained adversarially, and whether Stage 1+GAN blurring stems from the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Overall recommendation: novelty, promising results, and thorough evaluation justify acceptance.  
- Rebuttal/user study: current preliminary user study is too limited; authors should replace it with a rigorous human evaluation in revision, though this does not change the acceptance recommendation.
