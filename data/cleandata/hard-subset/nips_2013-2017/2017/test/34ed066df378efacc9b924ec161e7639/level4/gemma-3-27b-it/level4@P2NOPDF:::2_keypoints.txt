- Paper proposes a human image generator conditioned jointly on appearance and pose, evaluated on DeepFashion.
- Uses adversarial training with a two-stage (coarse-to-fine) stacked generator architecture.
- First generator (U-Net) produces a coarse image from appearance and pose maps.
- Second generator refines the coarse output by predicting a residual/difference map, incorporating original appearance.
- Technical contributions: stacked generator predicting residual maps and a carefully designed loss function.
- Novelty: joint conditioning on appearance and pose for controllable human image synthesis.
- Strength: clear exposition and well-structured presentation of the architecture.
- Strength: coarse-to-fine strategy and use of difference maps are effective for higher-resolution output.
- Weakness: visual quality still needs improvement; persistent artifacts and loss of high-frequency detail (e.g., black dots in Fig. 4 ID346).
- Weakness: second-stage refinement reduces blurring but is insufficient to fully recover target appearance details.
- Concern: may be viewed as incremental application of existing conditional/stacked GAN techniques (StackGAN, Stacked GANs).
- Limitation: narrow application focus on human/dressing may limit broader generalizability and impact.
- Recommendation: accept, but address artifact issues as a priority for future work.
