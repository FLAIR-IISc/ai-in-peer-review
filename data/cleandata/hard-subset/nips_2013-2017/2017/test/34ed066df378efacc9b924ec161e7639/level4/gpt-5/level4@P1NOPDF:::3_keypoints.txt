- Paper proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel-view images of people conditioned on target skeleton keypoints; contribution mainly in the application rather than novel methods.
- Relies on established components (DCGANs, U-Nets); novelty is limited to the specific vision application.
- Idea is interesting, paper is clearly written.
- Experiments suggest the system can produce somewhat realistic-looking images.
- Major weakness: lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user study to measure how often generated images fool humans).
- Visual results are unconvincing: figures too small, many noticeable artifacts, outputs not close to real images.
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily resolve the quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Reviewer’s conclusion: significant issues remain and the paper is not ready for presentation at NIPS.
