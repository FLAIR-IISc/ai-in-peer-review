This paper presents a GAN-based system for generating novel-view images of a person, guided by a target skeleton represented as a set of keypoints. The methodology combines several well-established techniques, such as DCGAN and U-Net architectures. As such, the primary contribution of the work is its application to this specific computer vision problem rather than the introduction of a novel generative method.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper addresses an interesting and challenging problem: synthesizing realistic person images in arbitrary poses.
*   The manuscript is well-written and the proposed approach is described clearly, making it easy for the reader to follow.
*   The empirical experiments demonstrate the feasibility of the approach, successfully synthesizing images that, to a certain degree, appear realistic and match the target pose.

**Weaknesses:**
My main concerns with this paper relate to the evaluation and the quality of the results. For an application-oriented contribution, demonstrating high-quality output through rigorous evaluation is critical.

1.  **Lack of Quantitative Evaluation:** The paper does not include any quantitative metrics to assess the quality of the generated images. This makes it difficult to objectively gauge the method's performance or compare it with future approaches. A user study, for example, could be conducted to evaluate the perceptual realism of the results (e.g., how often the generated images can be mistaken for real ones). Standard quantitative metrics from the GAN literature, such as Inception Score or Frechet Inception Distance (FID), could also be employed to provide a more objective measure of image quality and diversity.

2.  **Unconvincing Qualitative Results:** The visual quality of the generated images is not compelling. In Figure 1, for instance, the output images are too small for detailed inspection. Even at this low resolution, numerous visual artifacts are noticeable when comparing the generated images to the ground truth targets. Details on the face, hands, and clothing are often blurry, distorted, or inconsistent. This issue persists across nearly all results, including those in the supplementary material. While the general direction of this work is promising, the current quality of the results is not yet at a level that could be considered close to realistic. The work appears to be in an early stage, and it may be premature to present these findings as a conclusive success.

**After Rebuttal:**
I have read the authors' feedback and appreciate their response. However, my assessment of the paper remains largely unchanged. The key issues regarding the lack of rigorous quantitative evaluation and the modest qualitative results still stand. These points were also raised by other reviewers, highlighting their significance for a practical, application-focused paper. While the rebuttal shows some attempt to address these concerns, the provided additions are not sufficient to demonstrate the level of quality and thorough evaluation expected at a top-tier venue like NIPS. I believe the work is promising, but in its current state, it is not yet ready for publication.
