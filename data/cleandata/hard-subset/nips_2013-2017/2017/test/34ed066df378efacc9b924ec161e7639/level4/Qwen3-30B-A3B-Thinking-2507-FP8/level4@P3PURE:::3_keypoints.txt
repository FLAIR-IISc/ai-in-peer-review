- Proposes a GAN-based framework for novel-view person synthesis conditioned on skeletal keypoints; main novelty is application-specific design rather than new architectures.  
- Integrates established components (DCGANs, U-Nets) rather than introducing core methodological advances.  
- Strength: core idea is compelling and well-motivated.  
- Strength: exposition is clear and accessible.  
- Strength: preliminary experiments show moderate realism in synthesized outputs.  
- Major concern: no quantitative evaluation of output quality (e.g., user studies or perceptual metrics).  
- Major concern: qualitative results are low-resolution and contain visible artifacts (inconsistent body parts, pose misalignment), undermining confidence.  
- The claim of “high-quality” synthesis is premature given the presented evidence.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not adequately address quantitative and qualitative evaluation gaps; issues persist across main and supplementary results.  
- Conclusion: promising direction but insufficiently validated for publication at this stage.
