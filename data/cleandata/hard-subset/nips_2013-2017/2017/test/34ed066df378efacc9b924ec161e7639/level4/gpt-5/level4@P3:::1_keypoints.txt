- Introduces PG2, a two-stage pose-guided person image generation: Stage I U-Net–like encoder-decoder for coarse synthesis, Stage II conditional DCGAN predicting a residual refinement map with a pairwise discriminator.  
- Conditions on reference appearance image and 18-keypoint heatmap pose; uses a pose-masked L1 loss to upweight human pixels and reduce background mismatch.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) with qualitative results and ablations on pose embedding, loss, and one- vs two-stage training.  
- Uses masked SSIM/IS metrics and reports a small user study.  
- Novel, useful task: explicit pose control while preserving identity with clear applications (content creation, data augmentation).  
- Well-motivated coarse-to-fine design; Stage II residual formulation and pairwise discriminator are elegant and practical.  
- Pose-masked L1 is simple and effective at mitigating background mismatch.  
- Thorough ablations; qualitative results are strong on DeepFashion and reasonable on Market-1501.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM/IS (even masked) are imperfect and the user study is small and inconclusive.  
- Identity preservation is central but not directly measured.  
- Some architectural choices lack justification (effect size of residual blocks; impact of Stage I fully connected bottleneck).  
- Background transfer artifacts occur in challenging cases; method depends on pose detector quality and robustness is unanalyzed.  
- Suggested identity evaluation: use a re-identification network (top-k retrieval or cosine similarity in re-id embedding).  
- Suggested pose accuracy evaluation: run a pose estimator on generated images and report PCK or similar metrics.  
- Recommend reporting effect of architecture choices: ablate residual blocks and remove FC bottleneck in Stage I to quantify impacts.  
- Recommend additional metrics such as FID on person crops to better align with human perception.  
- Clarify and quantify claims like “residual blocks improve performance” across datasets and metrics.  
- Explain rationale for Stage I FC layer vs its absence in Stage II and its effect on blur/background leakage.  
- Provide sensitivity analysis for λ (loss weighting) with main-text plots and cross-dataset guidance.  
- Suggest robustness experiments: perturb keypoints or use different pose estimators to test sensitivity.  
- Suggest background-mitigation strategies (segmentation conditioning or background masks) to reduce bleeding.  
- Recommend a stronger user study: A/B pairwise tests, longer viewing time, and separate identity/pose judgments.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel problem, strong qualitative results, thoughtful two-stage approach, reproducible architecture.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited quantitative metrics, missing identity/pose analyses, some unjustified architectural choices.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding stronger quantitative/human evaluations and minor clarifications.  
- Post-rebuttal: current user study is too preliminary to be persuasive; reviewer still recommends acceptance but urges more substantial human and quantitative evaluations.
