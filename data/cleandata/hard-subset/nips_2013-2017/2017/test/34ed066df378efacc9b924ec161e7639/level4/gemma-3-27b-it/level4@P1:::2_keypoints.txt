- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) for person image synthesis conditioned on both appearance and pose.  
- Employs a two-stage adversarial training architecture: a coarse U-Net-like first generator and a second generator that predicts a residual to refine outputs.  
- Refinement via predicting a difference/residual map instead of direct upsampling is a key technical contribution.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to better incorporate target pose information during training.  
- Evaluated quantitatively and qualitatively on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  
- Demonstrates improved results over a single-stage approach and visual consistency in generated images.  
- Generated images still show visible artifacts and fail to fully recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in ID 346 of Figure 4).  
- Second-stage reduces blurriness but is insufficient to eliminate artifacts and restore fine appearance details.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the architecture and method.  
- Work’s novelty may be limited by similarity to prior stacked/conditional GAN approaches (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs), potentially reducing broader impact.  
- Generalizability beyond the vision/graphics domain is limited and may not transfer to other ML problems.  
- Overall assessment: sufficient technical novelty and promising results but quality issues and perceived incrementalism; reviewer’s initial recommendation: accept.
