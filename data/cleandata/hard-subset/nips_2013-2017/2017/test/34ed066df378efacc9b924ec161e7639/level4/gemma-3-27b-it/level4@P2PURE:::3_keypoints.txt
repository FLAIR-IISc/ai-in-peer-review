- Proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel views of people from target skeleton keypoints.
- Uses established architectures (DCGANs, U-Nets), so contribution is mainly an application rather than novel model innovation.
- Paper is well-written and the core idea is compelling.
- Empirical results indicate the model can synthesize images with some degree of realism.
- No quantitative evaluation provided (e.g., user studies or metrics) which is critical for an application-focused paper.
- Visual results are not convincing: figures are too small, and generated images show noticeable artifacts against the target skeletons.
- Supplementary material suffers from the same qualitative shortcomings.
- Authors’ rebuttal failed to adequately address concerns about quantitative and qualitative evaluation.
- Reviewer concludes the work is not yet ready for a venue like NIPS.
