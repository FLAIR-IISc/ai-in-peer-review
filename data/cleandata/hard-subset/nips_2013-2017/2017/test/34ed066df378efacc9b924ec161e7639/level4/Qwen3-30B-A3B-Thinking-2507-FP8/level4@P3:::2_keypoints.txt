- Introduces a novel task: pose-conditioned person image generation and proposes a two-stage PG2 framework (pose integration then image refinement).  
- Uses 18-keypoint heatmap pose embedding and a novel pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body.  
- Stage-I employs a U-Net-like architecture to produce a coarse pose-integrated image; Stage-II refines via adversarial training by predicting a difference map.  
- Main contributions: task definition, heatmap pose embedding with pose mask loss, and a two-stage global-then-detail architecture.  
- Technical soundness supported by clear motivation and ablation studies demonstrating effectiveness of pose embedding and pose mask loss.  
- Difference-map strategy in stage-II is computationally clever and improves training/results.  
- Paper is well-organized with helpful architecture diagrams and detailed experimental/implementation descriptions.  
- Demonstrated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with consistent improvements over baselines, relevant for applications like movie making and fashion.  
- Generated image quality is limited: visible artifacts and only 14.9% of generated images rated as real in user study.  
- Technical novelty is somewhat incremental; many components adapt existing GAN and image-to-image translation techniques.  
- Broader ML significance is limited—primarily an application-focused contribution rather than a fundamental advance in generative modeling.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to more explicitly acknowledge image-quality limitations and clarify distinctions from related work; overall a solid, well-executed application contribution.
