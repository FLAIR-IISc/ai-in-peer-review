- Proposes a GAN-based system for novel-view person image synthesis guided by skeleton keypoints; main novelty is applying established components (DCGANs, U-Nets) to this task.
- Positive: idea is interesting, writing is clear, and experiments show the method can produce somewhat realistic images.
- Major weakness: no quantitative evaluation (e.g., user study) to measure realism or fooling rate.
- Visual results are not compelling: figures are too small and generated images show noticeable visual errors versus targets.
- Supplementary results show similar quality issues, so overall outputs do not convincingly match real images.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced that authors adequately addressed quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Conclusion: paper is not ready for a top venue like NIPS due to unresolved evaluation and result-quality problems.
