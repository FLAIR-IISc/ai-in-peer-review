- Proposes a novel GAN-based method for human image generation conditioned on target appearance and a pose map.
- Uses a two-stage, coarse-to-fine stacked generator: a U-Net-style coarse generator followed by a second network that predicts a residual (difference) map for refinement.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Technical novelty: stacked generator architecture and predicting a residual map instead of direct upsampling.
- Incorporates a well-designed loss function to train the model.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents the architecture.
- Coarse-to-fine strategy and residual-prediction are effective and suitable for the virtual try-on/dressing problem.
- Main weakness: generated images contain noticeable artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., clothing pattern artifacts like black dots in Fig. 4, ID346).
- Secondary concern: work may be seen as an incremental, application-focused extension of existing conditional/stacked GAN frameworks.
- Potential limited generalizability of the proposed techniques to other domains.
- Recommendation: accept (paper demonstrates sufficient technical novelty and produces plausible, consistent results despite quality issues).
