- Introduces PG2, a two-stage adversarial framework for pose-conditioned person image generation using a coarse U-Net and a residual-refinement U-Net.  
- Conditioned on a reference image and target pose; evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets.  
- Explicitly guiding generation with pose information is a valuable and well-motivated contribution.  
- Two-stage design (global structure then detail refinement) is reasonable and inspired by prior stacked-generation work.  
- Using a difference/residual map in stage two speeds training and improves stability.  
- Network architecture and experimental setup are clearly explained; pose embedding via heatmaps is well-justified.  
- Visual quality remains a major concern: visible artifacts and missing high-frequency detail limit practicality.  
- Contribution feels incremental—builds on established conditional GAN and stacked-GAN ideas without clearly differentiating advances.  
- Paper does not fully contextualize its novelty relative to prior stacked/conditional GAN works (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GAN).  
- More explanation needed for U-Net design choices (e.g., use of residual blocks).  
- Strengths: novel task focus, technically sound two-stage approach, clear presentation, feasible experimental validation.  
- Weaknesses: image quality, limited perceived significance, and potentially limited broader generalizability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/problematic task, sound methodology, demonstrated feasibility despite limitations.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient image quality for applications and incremental novelty may not meet venue standards.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve image quality and better articulate unique contributions relative to prior work.
