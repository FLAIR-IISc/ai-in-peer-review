- Paper introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to generate images of a person by combining appearance from an input image with a target pose encoded as heat maps.
- Task and approach are novel.
- New two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture is a key contribution.
- Paper is well-written.
- Results are promising despite visible artifacts.
- Evaluation of design choices is comprehensive.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics.
- Reviewer requests related work on iterative/cascaded refinement (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Reviewer asks the authors to specify how residual blocks improve generation performance (what specific ways).
- Reviewer asks whether Stage 2 trained adversarially with Stage 1 inputs would reduce blurring and whether blurring with Stage1+GAN is attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty of problem and approach.
- Rebuttal note: the preliminary user study is unconvincing and should be replaced by a substantive human evaluation, though the positive assessment remains.
