This research paper proposes a human image generator based on appearance and human pose. The proposed generation is based on an adversarial training architecture where two-stage generative networks generate high-resolution images to feed into a discriminator. In the generator part, the first generator creates a coarse image using a U-shaped network when appearance and pose maps are provided, then the second generator takes a coarse input with the original appearance to estimate the residual to refine the coarse image. The research paper uses a deepfashion dataset for evaluation.

This research paper proposes some key ideas:

*   **Work Novelty:** Introducing the idea of conditioning on appearance and pose maps for human image generation.
*   **Techniques:** Stacked architecture that predicts difference maps instead of direct upsampling, and loss design.

This research paper could be improved in the following contexts to make it stand out:

*   **Still requires quality improvement.**
*   **Significance:** This research paper can be seen as another GAN architecture.
*   **Problem Domain:** Good vision/graphics application, but difficult to generalize to other learning problems.

This research paper is well-organized to express the key aspects of the proposed architecture. Based on appearance and pose information, the proposed generator stacks two networks to adopt a coarse-to-fine strategy. This research paper effectively uses the generation strategy in the dressing problem. The proposed approach seems suitable for the relevant problem scenario. Difference map generation also seems like a small but good technique in generating high-resolution images.

Perhaps the main complaint with the research paper is that the generated results have visible artifacts and still require a lot of improvement for application perspective. For example, in ID346 of Figure 4, the pattern translates into black dots in the final result. Even though the second generator reduces the blurry image from the first generator, it seems that the model is still insufficient to recover high-frequency components in the target appearance.

Another potential but serious concern is that some people might say that the proposed approach is an application of conditional GANs. The conditioning or stacking of the generator for adversarial training has been proposed in the past; for example, below, although they are arXiv papers. The research paper includes application-specific challenges, but it may not attract a large audience.

*   Han Zhang, Tao Ju, Hongsheng Li, Shaoting Zhang, Xiaolei Huang, Xiaogang Wang, Dimitris Metaxas, "StackGAN: A Text for Photo-Realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks", arXiv:1612.03242.
*   Zun Huang, Yixuan Li, Omid Porbid, John Hopcroft, Serge Belongie, Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks, arXiv:1612.04357.

Overall, the research paper successfully proposed a solution to the pose-based image problem and was reasonably evaluated. The proposed approach presents sufficient technical novelty. The resulting images still require quality improvement, but the proposed model at least generates some visually consistent images. My initial rating is acceptable.
