- Paper proposes PG²: a two-stage network to generate a person image in a target pose from a single source image.  
- Stage I: U-Net-like generator produces a coarse image using source image + pose heatmap with a pose-mask L1 loss to focus on foreground.  
- Stage II: another U-Net within a conditional GAN refines coarse output to add details; discriminator enforces consistency via paired inputs.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501; task is explicit pose transfer for full-body person images.  
- Strength — originality/significance: addresses a novel, challenging problem with useful applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, content creation).  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written and the coarse-to-fine decomposition is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Strength — technical soundness: sensible two-stage design, masked L1 loss, and ablations justify architectural choices.  
- Weakness — generated image quality: outputs often contain artifacts, blurriness, and lack high-frequency/detail fidelity.  
- Weakness — identity/detail loss: fine clothing textures, facial features, and accessories are frequently blurred or lost.  
- Weakness — background and realism issues: background copying/distortion and overly smooth “airbrushed” appearance make images identifiable as synthetic.  
- Weakness — evaluation mismatch: SSIM/IS metrics don’t align with perceived realism and can be misleading; Stage II can be sharper but score lower.  
- Weakness — weak user study results: low fool rates (14.9% on DeepFashion, 8.2% on Market-1501) indicate limited photo-realism.  
- Overall: promising and well-designed proof-of-concept but current results lack the realism expected for a top-tier venue; leans toward rejection with encouragement to continue refinement.
