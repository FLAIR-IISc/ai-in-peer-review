- Summary: Proposes PG2, a two-stage pose-guided person image generation network that first captures global structure then refines appearance with adversarial training, evaluated on two datasets.

- Novel task: Pose-guided person image generation is new and has useful applications (movies, retrieval, pose estimation).

- Two-stage architecture: Coarse-to-fine design effectively captures global structure and appearance details.

- Writing: Paper is well-organized and clearly written.

- Evaluation: Extensive quantitative and qualitative results with comparisons on two datasets.

- Quantitative evaluation weakness: SSIM and Inception Score may be unsuitable for judging generated image quality.

- User study weakness: The conducted user study is preliminary and not fully convincing.

- Related work comment: Add recent iterative refinement/cascaded network work to contextualize the two-stage approach.

- Residual blocks comment: Authors should explain how residual blocks improve generation performance.

- Stage 2 experiment comment: Evaluate using Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs trained adversarially to assess its standalone contribution.

- Blurring comment: Investigate whether the intermediate fully connected layer causes blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN.

- Rating: Recommend acceptance based on novelty and approach, with encouragement to address weaknesses and comments.
