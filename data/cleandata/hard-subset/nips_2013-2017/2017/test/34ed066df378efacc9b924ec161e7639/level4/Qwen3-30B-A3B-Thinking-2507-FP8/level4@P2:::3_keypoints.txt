1. Proposes PG2: a two-stage pose-guided person generation network with Stage-I pose integration (U-Net + pose heatmaps + pose mask loss) and Stage-II adversarial image refinement on a difference map.  
2. Uses explicit keypoint-based pose conditioning rather than viewpoint labels, building on GAN/U-Net architectures and prior pose-synthesis work.  
3. Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing improvements over one-stage baselines and prior methods.  
4. Strength — Novelty/Significance: tackles a practical pose-guided synthesis problem with a useful two-stage decomposition and a pose mask loss to reduce background interference.  
5. Strength — Quality/Clarity: technically sound method, clear writing, thorough ablation studies, and sufficient implementation details.  
6. Strength — Evaluation: comprehensive metrics (SSIM, IS, mask-SSIM/mask-IS) plus AMT user study showing higher perceptual realism for the two-stage model.  
7. Weakness — Quantitative presentation: SSIM and perceptual quality conflict (e.g., blurrier models showing higher SSIM) and need clearer explanation.  
8. Weakness — Qualitative analysis: some visible artifacts and background inconsistencies; failure cases are shown but causes (data scarcity, bias) and mitigations should be expanded.  
9. Weakness — Comparison to Zhao et al. [36]: comparison is strong but should explicitly state why [36] fails (lack of explicit pose conditioning) to clarify the novelty gap.  
10. Balanced view: solid, validated contribution (pose mask loss + two-stage) that advances pose transfer despite imperfect results.  
11. Recommendation: Weak accept — technically rigorous with minor presentation revisions needed.  
12. Meta note: initial draft mistakenly claimed no quantitative evaluation; this was corrected to acknowledge included metrics and user studies.
