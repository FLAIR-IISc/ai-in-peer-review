- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that synthesizes person images in arbitrary poses from a reference image and target pose.
- Introduces a two-stage pipeline: stage 1 coarse U-Net-like generator, stage 2 adversarial refinement using a DCGAN variant.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background influence and focus generator on transferring human appearance.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing effectiveness and generation of convincing person details.
- Builds on generative modeling literature (VAE/GAN/autoregressive), framing a novel pose-conditioned generation task.
- Strength: novel task and two-stage design effectively address pose transfer challenges.
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate improved visual quality and detail preservation.
- Weakness: generated images still show visible artifacts and imperfect high-frequency detail recovery.
- Weakness: perceived incremental novelty since it can be viewed as an application of conditional GANs.
- Presentation: well-organized and clear methodology/experiments, with minor grammar issues.
- Overall judgment: technically novel and significant work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
