- Proposes a novel task and architecture for person image generation by combining input appearance with pose heat maps.  
- Uses a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture tailored to the problem.  
- Task novelty is a clear contribution to image generation.  
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine design is a unique and appropriate approach.  
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Results show noticeable artifacts but are promising and improvable.  
- Authors provide extensive evaluation of design decisions.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics.  
- Suggest adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Request elaboration on how residual blocks improve generation performance.  
- Recommend testing Stage 2 when given Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially.  
- Ask whether blurring in Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and approach.  
- Rebuttal note: user study is too preliminary and should be strengthened, but this does not change the positive recommendation.
