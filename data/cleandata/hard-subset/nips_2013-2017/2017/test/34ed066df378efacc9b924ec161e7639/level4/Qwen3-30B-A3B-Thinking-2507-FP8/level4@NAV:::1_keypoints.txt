- Paper proposes an architecture to generate person images by combining input appearance with a target pose represented as heatmaps (review summary).
- Strength: Novel task formulation for pose-guided person image generation.
- Strength: Innovative two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture enabling iterative refinement.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation of methods and results.
- Strength: Results show encouraging potential despite visible artifacts.
- Strength: Thorough exploration and assessment of architectural choices.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is limited due to lack of established metrics.
- Comment: Related work should include iterative refinement/cascaded network papers (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Comment: Line 110 — authors should specify what performance gains residual blocks provide (e.g., metrics or qualitative improvements).
- Comment: Suggest evaluating Stage 2 when fed Stage 1 outputs and trained adversarially; investigate whether blurring from Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Rebuttal note: Preliminary user study is too limited to be relied upon; a robust human evaluation must be added in revision.
- Overall: Novelty and methodology justify a strong recommendation for acceptance.
