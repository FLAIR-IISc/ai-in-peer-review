This paper creates a GAN-type system for generating images of a new-eyes person guided by a target skeleton (represented as keypoints). This system includes well-known techniques like DCGANs and U-nets, so its contribution is mainly about specific computer vision applications.

Pros:
* The proposed concept is interesting, the writing is easy to follow, and empirical tests show that it is capable of generating images that look realistic at some level.

Cons:
* Since this work is application-based, achieving good results is important. My main complaint is that there is no quantitative evaluation here. For example, it is possible to conduct user research to evaluate how often the generated images can deceive the human eye. The empirical results are not visually appealing. See Figure 1 for example, the generated images are too small to see. Even so, there are many noticeable visual defects when comparing the results with the target. Therefore, it is somewhat disappointing to conclude that the results are of good quality or close to real images so far. The same problem exists for almost all the results with complementary figures. Personally, I think the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, but it is not time to stop and declare victory at this stage.

After Rejection:
Even after reading the authors' response, I am still not sure. In particular, other reviewers have also noted that there are still some problems with quantitative and qualitative results, which is important for this type of application-based work. The rejection shows some effort but it still does not satisfactorily address these concerns. I don't think it might not be ready for a good presentation in a place like NIPS.
