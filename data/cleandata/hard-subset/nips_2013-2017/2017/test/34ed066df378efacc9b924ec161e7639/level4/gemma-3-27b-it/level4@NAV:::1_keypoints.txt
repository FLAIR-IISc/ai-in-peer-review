- Introduces a novel task: generate images of people in target poses by transferring appearance from a source image using pose heatmaps.  
- Proposes an innovative two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to address the task.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written.  
- Results are promising but suffer from artifacts.  
- Authors provide extensive, rigorous analysis of design choices.  
- Primary limitation: quantitative evaluation is difficult due to lack of established metrics for this task.  
- Missing related work on iterative refinement / cascaded networks (Carreira et al., 2016; Newell et al., 2016).  
- Statement that residual blocks improve generation (line 110) lacks detail on how performance is improved.  
- Suggestion to try applying the Stage 2 adversarial network to Stage 1 outputs and to investigate whether Stage1+GAN blurring is linked to the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- Recommends acceptance: novelty of problem and solution outweigh limitations.  
- Rebuttal’s user study is preliminary and unconvincing; a more substantive human evaluation is strongly encouraged.
