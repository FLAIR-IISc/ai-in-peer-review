

Summary:  
This paper introduces a novel architecture for generating person images by synthesizing appearance from an input image with a specified pose (represented via heatmaps). The approach employs a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework to progressively refine generated outputs.  

Strengths:  
- The task itself is novel, addressing a meaningful gap in person image synthesis.  
- The proposed coarse-to-fine architecture offers a clean and effective solution.  
- The paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
- Despite minor artifacts in results, the generated images demonstrate strong promise.  
- The authors provide extensive evaluation of key design choices, enhancing reproducibility.  

Weaknesses:  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited due to the absence of established metrics for this task; however, this is understandable and not a major drawback.  

Comments:  
1. **Related Work**: The iterative refinement nature of the architecture warrants inclusion of recent cascaded-network approaches. Specifically, consider adding:  
   - Carreira et al. (CVPR 2016) on iterative error feedback for pose estimation.  
   - Newell et al. (ECCV 2016) on stacked hourglass networks for pose.  
   These would strengthen the context for the proposed refinement strategy.  

2. **Line 110**: The claim that "residual blocks improve generation performance" requires elaboration. Please specify *how* (e.g., via quantitative metrics, qualitative comparisons, or ablation studies) residual blocks contribute to performance gains.  

3. **Stage 2 Exploration**: The evaluation focuses on Stage 1 followed by Stage 2. It would be insightful to investigate whether training Stage 2 *with Stage 1 inputs* (adversarially) could further reduce artifacts. Specifically, is the blurring observed in Stage 1 + GAN results attributable to the intermediate fully connected layer?  

Overall Rating:  
The novelty of the problem and the proposed solution justify a confident acceptance recommendation. The work makes a compelling contribution to person image synthesis, and the strengths outweigh the minor weaknesses.  

Rebuttal Note:  
While the authors included a preliminary user study in their rebuttal, its limited scope and lack of statistical rigor prevent it from being convincing. A substantive human evaluation (e.g., larger-scale, controlled study with clear metrics) is essential for revision. However, this does not affect the core assessment: the paper’s technical merits remain strong, and the recommendation for acceptance stands.
