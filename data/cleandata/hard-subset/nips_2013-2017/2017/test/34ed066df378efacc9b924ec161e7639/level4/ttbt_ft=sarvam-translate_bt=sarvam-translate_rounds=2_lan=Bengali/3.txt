This paper creates a GAN-type system to generate images of a person with a new eye, which is guided by a target skeleton (represented as a keypoint). This system incorporates well-known techniques like DCGAN and U-nets, so its contribution is mainly about specific computer vision applications.

Advantages:
* The proposed concept is interesting, the text is easy to follow, and empirical tests show that it is capable of generating images that look realistic at some level.

Disadvantages:
* Since this work is application-based, achieving good results is important. My main complaint is that there is no quantitative evaluation here. For example, it is possible to conduct user research to evaluate how often the generated images can deceive the human eye. The empirical results are not visually appealing. For example, see Figure 1; the generated images are too small to see. Nevertheless, there are many noticeable visual defects when comparing the results with the target. Therefore, it is somewhat disappointing to conclude that the results are of good quality or close to the original image so far. Almost all the results have the same problem with the complementary images. Personally, I think the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, but it is not time to stop and declare victory at this stage.

After Rejection:
Even after reading the authors' response, I am still not sure. In particular, other reviewers have also noted that there are still some problems with quantitative and qualitative results, which is important for this type of application-based work. The rejection shows some effort, but it still does not satisfactorily address these concerns. I don't think it might not be ready for a good presentation in a place like NIPS.
