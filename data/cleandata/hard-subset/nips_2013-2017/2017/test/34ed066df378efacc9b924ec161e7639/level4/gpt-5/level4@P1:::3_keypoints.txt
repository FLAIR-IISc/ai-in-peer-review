- Paper proposes a GAN-based method for pose-guided person image synthesis, with contribution mainly in application rather than novel methodology.
- Idea is interesting and practically relevant; conditioning generation on pose is natural and useful.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Empirical results show the method can produce images that look realistic to some extent.
- No substantive quantitative evaluation; a user study and objective metrics are missing.
- Visual quality is not compelling—generated images are small, contain noticeable artifacts, and are not convincingly close to real.
- Current evaluation is insufficient to support strong claims of success for this application-driven work.
- Suggested quantitative improvements: run a user study (e.g., 2AFC) and report mask-based SSIM/IS/FID to focus on person region.
- Suggested qualitative improvements: provide higher-resolution figures, zoomed-in crops (faces, hands, textures), side-by-side comparisons with targets and strong baselines, and include failure-case analysis.
- Recommend clarifying scope and limitations (pose complexity, occlusion, viewpoint, background shift) and quantifying performance across these factors.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced—additional evaluations were insufficient and the paper is not ready for a strong NIPS presentation.
