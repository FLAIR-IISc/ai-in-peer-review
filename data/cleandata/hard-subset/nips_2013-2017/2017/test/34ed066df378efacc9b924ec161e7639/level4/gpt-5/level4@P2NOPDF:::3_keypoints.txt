- Paper presents a GAN-based method for pose-guided person image synthesis using target skeleton keypoints; architecture largely reuses established components, so contribution is primarily application-driven.
- Problem is interesting, relevant, and the paper is clearly written.
- Experiments indicate the method can produce images that look realistic to some extent.
- Major lack of quantitative evaluation; no user study or metrics to assess how often generated images fool humans or to compare to baselines.
- Qualitative results are unconvincing: figures are too small to inspect and show numerous visible artifacts compared to targets.
- Visual-quality issues persist across figures and supplementary material, weakening claims of realism.
- Current results do not justify strong claims without improved visuals and rigorous evaluation.
- Recommended improvements: enhance visual quality and conduct perceptual evaluation (e.g., user study) for stronger empirical validation.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced; authors’ responses did not resolve quantitative and qualitative shortcomings.
- Conclusion: promising idea but, in current form, not suitable for a top venue like NIPS due to insufficient evidence.
