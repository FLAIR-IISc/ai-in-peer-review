- Novel task: conditioning image generation on a reference image and target pose is original and practically useful.
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is sensible for separating global structure learning from detailed appearance refinement.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method.
- Generated images show promising visual quality and accurate poses despite some artifacts.
- Authors provide extensive evaluation of design choices (pose embeddings, loss functions).
- Quantitative evaluation is limited by lack of established metrics; reliance on SSIM and Inception Score can be misleading.
- Related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks is not sufficiently connected to the proposed iterative architecture.
- Recommend citing and discussing iterative refinement papers (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Statement on residual blocks improving performance needs concrete elaboration (how they help).
- Single-stage adversarial baseline requires deeper analysis to explain observed blurring (e.g., role of intermediate fully connected layer).
- User study is preliminary and limited in scope, so its results should be treated cautiously.
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel contribution with significant potential despite noted shortcomings.
