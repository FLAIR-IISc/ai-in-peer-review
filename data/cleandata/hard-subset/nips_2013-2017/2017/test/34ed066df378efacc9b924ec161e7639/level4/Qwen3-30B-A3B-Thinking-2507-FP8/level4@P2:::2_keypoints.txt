1. Introduces PG2: a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network for pose transfer using a U-Net-like coarse generator and a conditional GAN refinement predicting a difference map.

2. Uses pose heatmaps as pose embedding and proposes a pose mask loss to focus generation on human body regions and reduce background interference.

3. Main contributions: (1) task formulation conditioning generation on reference image + target pose, (2) pose embedding techniques and pose mask loss, (3) two-stage framework separating structure and detail learning.

4. Strength: clear problem formulation and effective division into two stages for global structure and detail refinement.

5. Strength: difference map approach enables faster convergence and improved detail generation compared to directly generating final images.

6. Strength: pose mask loss effectively reduces background artifacts and improves focus on the person.

7. Strength: comprehensive evaluation on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with quantitative/qualitative results and detailed ablation studies.

8. Quality limitation: generated images still contain visible artifacts and lack high-frequency details (e.g., clothing textures, facial details).

9. Originality concern: two-stage conditional GAN approach is incremental and similar to prior stacked GANs (e.g., StackGAN), limiting perceived novelty.

10. Technical novelty limited: pose heatmaps and pose mask loss are sensible but not highly innovative relative to existing techniques.

11. Evaluation limitations: user study and metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) do not conclusively demonstrate superiority; comparisons to recent methods are insufficient.

12. Recommendations: provide stronger evidence of superiority, better articulate advances over prior GAN work, improve refinement/architecture to recover fine details, and compare to more recent pose-guided methods.

13. Overall: technically sound and well-executed for pose-guided person generation but somewhat incremental; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen novelty and image quality.
