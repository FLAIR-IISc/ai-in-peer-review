- Proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation (PG) network that synthesizes person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Architecture is two-stage (pose integration then image refinement), following a coarse-to-fine design.  
- Method explicitly uses pose information and integrates different pose embeddings.  
- Task formulation (conditioning on reference image + pose) is novel and distinct from prior work.  
- Two-stage architecture is well-designed and effective according to experiments.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and technically sound.  
- Results are promising but contain noticeable visual artifacts.  
- Authors provide extensive ablation studies on pose embeddings, loss choices, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Quantitative evaluation is generally weak due to lack of suitable metrics for the task.  
- Conducted an AMT user study for both datasets, offering some insight but considered preliminary and not fully convincing.  
- Related work on VAEs, GANs, autoregressive models, and conditional image generation is appropriately discussed.  
- Paper has clear significance and potential applications (face editing, filmmaking, image retrieval) and merits acceptance with stronger human evaluation recommended.
