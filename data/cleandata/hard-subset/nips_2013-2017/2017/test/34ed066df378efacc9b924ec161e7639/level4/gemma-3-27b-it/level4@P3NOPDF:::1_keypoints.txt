- Introduces a novel two-stage, pose-guided architecture for generating person images that preserves appearance while changing pose; results are visually compelling but show artifacts.  
- Novel task of pose-conditioned appearance transfer with clear applications (virtual try-on, avatars, image manipulation).  
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is well-motivated and effectively separates coarse pose generation from refinement.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Generated images convincingly portray target poses and preserve identity despite some artifacts.  
- Provides a thorough ablation study that systematically evaluates design choices.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited by lack of established metrics; proxy metrics or perceptual losses should be explored.  
- Missing discussion of related cascaded/iterative refinement work (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016).  
- Request more detail on how residual blocks improve generation (e.g., gradient flow vs. representational capacity).  
- Suggest trying direct Stage1->Stage2 input and end-to-end adversarial training; blurring might stem from the intermediate fully connected layer.  
- User study is preliminary and not rigorous; recommend a larger, standardized human evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—novel, elegant approach with promising results despite evaluation weaknesses.  
- Rebuttal note: a stronger human evaluation would strengthen the paper, but it does not change the positive assessment.
