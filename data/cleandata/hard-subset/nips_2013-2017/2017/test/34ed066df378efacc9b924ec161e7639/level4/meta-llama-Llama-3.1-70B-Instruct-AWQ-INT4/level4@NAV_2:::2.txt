The paper proposes a novel task of generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose, and presents a Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) to address this task. The proposed generation framework utilizes a two-stage approach, consisting of pose integration and image refinement stages. In the first stage, a U-Net-like network is employed to integrate the condition image and target pose, generating a coarse result that captures the global structure of the human body. The second stage then refines the initial result using a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) model, trained to generate a difference map that brings the initial result closer to the target image.

The paper effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture and demonstrates its application in generating person images with diverse poses. The proposed approach is well-suited to the concerned problem scenario, and the difference map generation technique is a nice addition in generating higher resolution images.

The paper's main strengths lie in its novelty, technical soundness, and clarity. The proposed task of conditioning image generation on both a reference image and a target pose is novel and has many interesting applications. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed approach, including its architecture, loss functions, and training process. The writing is clear, and the figures and tables are well-organized and informative.

However, the paper can be improved in terms of its significance and impact. While the proposed approach is technically sound, it may be seen as an application of existing techniques (e.g., conditional GANs) to a specific problem domain. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of its contributions to the broader field of computer vision and machine learning.

Additionally, the generated results, while visually consistent, still contain visible artifacts and require quality improvement for practical applications. The paper could also benefit from a more thorough evaluation of its approach, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and an analysis of its limitations.

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of computer vision, and with some revisions to address the above concerns, it has the potential to make a more significant impact.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel task and presents a technically sound approach to address it.
* The proposed approach is well-suited to the concerned problem scenario, and the difference map generation technique is a nice addition.
* The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may be seen as an application of existing techniques to a specific problem domain.

Rating: Accept with minor revisions.
