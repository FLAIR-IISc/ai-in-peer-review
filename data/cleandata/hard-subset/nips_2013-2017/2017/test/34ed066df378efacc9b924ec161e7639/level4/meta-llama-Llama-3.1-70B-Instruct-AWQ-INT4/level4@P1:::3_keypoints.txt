- Paper presents a GAN-based system for novel-view person image synthesis guided by target skeleton/keypoints.
- Contribution mainly lies in a practical application rather than novel network architectures (uses known techniques like DCGANs and U-nets).
- Proposed idea is novel and interesting.
- Writing is clear and easy to follow.
- Empirical images appear realistic to some extent but are not consistently convincing.
- Major weakness: lack of quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user studies or metrics to measure realism).
- Visual results are not visually appealing; figures are small and contain noticeable visual errors compared to targets.
- Supplementary results suffer from the same visual quality issues.
- Rebuttal did not satisfactorily address reviewers' quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Reviewer believes the work is promising but not ready for a top venue like NIPS in its current form.
- Suggested improvements: conduct user studies to assess how often generated images fool humans.
- Suggested improvements: improve the visual quality of generated images.
- Acceptance criteria: provide comprehensive quantitative evaluation, produce visually appealing empirical results, and address qualitative concerns.
