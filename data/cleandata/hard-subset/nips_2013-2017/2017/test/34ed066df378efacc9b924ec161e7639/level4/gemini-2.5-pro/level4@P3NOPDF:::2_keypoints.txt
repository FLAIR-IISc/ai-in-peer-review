- Tackles pose-conditioned human image generation with clear practical applications (virtual try-on, animation, editing).  
- Proposes a well-motivated two-stage coarse-to-fine generator: G1 (U-Net-like) for coarse layout and G2 predicting a residual for high-frequency refinement.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experiments are appropriate and reproducible.  
- Generated images exhibit noticeable artifacts and lack high-frequency detail, failing to faithfully transfer intricate clothing textures.  
- Core ideas are incremental in the broader GAN context, largely adapting established conditional and multi-stage generator concepts.  
- Method is specialized for human synthesis and may not generalize easily to other domains.  
- Overall a solid, well-executed contribution that provides a strong baseline; recommended for acceptance despite quality and novelty limitations.
