This paper develops a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images guided by a target skeleton, represented as keypoints. The system primarily utilizes established techniques such as DCGANs and U-nets; therefore, the contribution lies in its specific application to computer vision.

**Pros:**
* The proposed idea is interesting, the writing is clear and easy to follow, and the empirical experiments demonstrate the ability to synthesize images that exhibit a degree of realism.

**Cons:**
* Given the application-oriented nature of this work, achieving strong results is crucial. My primary concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. For instance, conducting user studies to assess the rate at which generated images can deceive human observers would be a valuable addition.
* The empirical results are not particularly visually compelling. For example, the generated images in Fig. 1 are too small for detailed inspection. Even at this size, noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the results to the target skeletons. Consequently, concluding that the current results are of good quality or closely resemble real images feels premature. This issue persists throughout the presented results, including those in the supplementary materials. I believe the authors are pursuing a promising direction, but further development is needed before claiming substantial success.

**After rebuttal:**
I remain unconvinced following the authors’ feedback. Specifically, concerns raised by multiple reviewers regarding both quantitative and qualitative results remain unaddressed. Both aspects are vital for a work focused on practical application. While the rebuttal demonstrates some effort, it does not sufficiently address these concerns. In my assessment, the paper may not be ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
