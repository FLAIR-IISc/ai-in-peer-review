- Proposes a GAN-based system to generate novel-view person images conditioned on skeletal keypoints.  
- Leverages established components (DCGANs, U-Nets); main novelty is the specific application domain.  
- Core idea is conceptually interesting and presentation is clear and accessible.  
- Empirical results show the method can produce visually plausible images in some cases.  
- Major shortcoming: lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation.  
- No user studies or human-evaluation metrics to assess how often outputs fool observers.  
- Qualitative results lack visual appeal and fail to demonstrate high fidelity.  
- Figures are low resolution and show clear discrepancies (distorted limbs, inconsistent textures) between outputs and target skeletons.  
- Similar visual limitations are present across all figures and supplementary materials.  
- These issues undermine confidence and make claims of success premature.  
- Post-rebuttal, the reviewer remains unconvinced as gaps persist and were not adequately addressed.  
- Verdict: potential exists but the work needs substantially stronger empirical evidence to meet NeurIPS standards.
