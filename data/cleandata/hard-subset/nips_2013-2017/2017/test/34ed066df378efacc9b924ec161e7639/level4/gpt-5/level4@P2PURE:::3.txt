This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view images of people conditioned on a target skeleton (represented as keypoints). The architecture relies largely on established components such as DCGANs and U-Nets, so the main contribution lies in applying these techniques to this particular computer vision task.

Pros:
- The idea is interesting, the writing is clear, and the experiments show the system can synthesize images that are realistic-looking to some extent.

Cons:
- Because this is an application-oriented paper, strong empirical results are crucial. My main concerns are:
  - There is no quantitative evaluation. For instance, a user study could measure how often the generated images fool human observers.
  - The qualitative results are not visually compelling. In Fig. 1, the generated images are too small to evaluate properly; even so, there are many noticeable visual errors relative to the targets. It is therefore difficult to conclude that the results are high quality or close to real images. The same issue holds for most results, including those in the supplementary material. I believe the authors are moving in the right direction and the work is promising, but it is premature to declare success at this stage.

After rebuttal:
I remain unconvinced after reading the authors’ response. As other reviewers also noted, there are still issues with both quantitative and qualitative results—both are essential for a practically oriented work. The rebuttal makes some attempts, but they do not address these concerns to a satisfactory degree. I do not think the paper is yet ready for a venue like NIPS.
