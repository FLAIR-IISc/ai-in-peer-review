This research paper develops a GAN-type system that generates novel-looking person images guided by a target skeleton (represented as a keypoint). The system incorporates most known technologies, such as DCGANs and U-nets, so its contribution is focused on specific computer vision applications.

**Pros:**
* The proposed idea is interesting, the writing is easy to understand, and empirical experiments show that it is capable of synthesizing images that look realistic to some extent.

**Cons:**
* Since this work is application-oriented, achieving good results is crucial. My main complaint is that there is no quantitative evaluation. For example, it is possible to conduct user studies to evaluate how often the generated images can deceive the human eye. The empirical results are not very appealing. Take Figure 1, for instance; the generated images are too small to see. Even so, there are several noticeable visual errors when comparing the results with the targets. Therefore, it is a bit disappointing to conclude that the results so far are of good quality or close to real images. The same problem persists for almost all the results, including the supporting figures. Personally, I think the authors are on the right track, and the work is promising, while at this stage, it remains to stop and declare victory.

**After Rejection:**
Even after reading the authors' response, I am not convinced. In particular, it has also been pointed out by other reviewers that there are still issues regarding quantitative and qualitative results, which is all important for such a practically oriented work. The rejection shows some effort but still does not address these concerns to a satisfactory degree. I think it may not be ready to be well-presented in a place like NIPS.
