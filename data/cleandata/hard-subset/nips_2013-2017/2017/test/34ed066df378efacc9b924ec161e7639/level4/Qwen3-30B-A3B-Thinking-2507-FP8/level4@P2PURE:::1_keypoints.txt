- Summary: Proposes a model that generates person images by combining an input appearance with a target pose represented as heatmaps.
- Strength: The task is novel.
- Strength: The two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is novel.
- Strength: The paper is well written.
- Strength: Results are promising despite some visible artifacts.
- Strength: Extensive ablation studies evaluate key design choices.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics.
- Comment: Related work should cite iterative refinement/cascaded networks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Comment: Explain how residual blocks specifically improve generation performance (Line 110).
- Comment: Evaluate how Stage 2 performs when applied to Stage 1 outputs trained with adversarial loss.
- Comment: Investigate whether blurring in Stage1+GAN results is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Overall: Reviewer confidently recommends acceptance based on novelty and approach.
- Rebuttal note: The preliminary user study is unconvincing; a substantive human evaluation should be added, though the reviewer’s positive assessment remains.
