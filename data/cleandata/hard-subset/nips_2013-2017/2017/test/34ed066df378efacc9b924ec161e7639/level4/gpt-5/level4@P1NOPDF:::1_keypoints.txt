- Summary: Proposes a two-stage architecture to generate person images combining appearance from an input image with a target pose encoded as heatmaps.

- Strength: The task is novel.

- Strength: The two-stage, coarse-to-fine architecture is novel.

- Strength: The paper is clearly written.

- Strength: Results are promising despite visible artifacts.

- Strength: Provides an extensive evaluation of design choices.

- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is weak (understandable due to lack of suitable metrics).

- Comment: Include recent iterative refinement related work (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).

- Comment: Clarify how residual blocks improve generation (e.g., textures, pose consistency, convergence, stability) and provide quantitative/qualitative evidence.

- Comment: Suggest ablation testing Stage 2 trained adversarially with the same inputs as Stage 1 (bypassing the intermediate representation) to determine if blurring is caused by the fully connected bottleneck or objective/architecture mismatch.

- Overall recommendation: Confidently recommend acceptance based on novelty and approach.

- Rebuttal note: Current user study is unconvincing—ask for a more substantive human evaluation, though the reviewer’s positive assessment remains.
