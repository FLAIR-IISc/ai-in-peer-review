This paper presents a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)-based approach for synthesizing novel views of people from a target skeleton representation (defined by keypoints). The architecture largely utilizes established techniques – DCGANs and U-nets – positioning the primary contribution within the specific context of this computer vision application.

**Strengths:**

* The core idea is compelling, the paper is well-written and accessible, and the experimental results demonstrate a capacity to generate images with a degree of visual realism.

**Weaknesses:**

* Given the application-focused nature of this work, robust results are crucial. A significant concern is the lack of quantitative evaluation. Exploring methods like user studies to assess perceptual realism – specifically, the ability of generated images to convincingly mimic real images – would be highly valuable.
* The visual quality of the empirical results is underwhelming. For instance, the images in Figure 1 are presented at a scale that hinders detailed inspection. Even at this size, noticeable visual artifacts are apparent when comparing generated outputs to the corresponding target skeletons. Consequently, claims of “good quality” or near-realistic image generation are difficult to substantiate. This issue persists across the presented results, including those in the supplementary materials. While the approach appears promising and the authors are pursuing a worthwhile direction, the work is not yet at a stage where strong conclusions regarding its success can be drawn.

**Post-Rebuttal:**

I remain unconvinced following review of the authors’ response. Concerns regarding both quantitative and qualitative evaluation, as highlighted by multiple reviewers, remain unaddressed. While the rebuttal demonstrates some effort towards improvement, it does not sufficiently resolve these issues for a practical-oriented work. I do not believe the paper is currently suitable for presentation at a competitive venue such as NeurIPS.
