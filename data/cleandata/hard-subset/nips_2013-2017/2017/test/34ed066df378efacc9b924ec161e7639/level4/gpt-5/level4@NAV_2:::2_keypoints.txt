- Method: Two-stage person image synthesis using a U-shaped coarse generator followed by a residual (difference) refiner conditioned on coarse output and reference appearance.
- Conditioning: Explicitly conditions generation on both a reference appearance and a pose map for pose-transfer/dressing.
- Architecture/technique: Coarse-to-fine, stacked design that predicts a difference map instead of directly synthesizing the final image, with a tailored loss design.
- Evaluation: Experiments conducted on datasets such as DeepFashion.
- Strength: Manuscript is well structured and clearly conveys architecture and components.
- Fit: The two-stage residual strategy is well matched to the dressing/pose-transfer task and modestly effective at sharpening higher-resolution details.
- Main weakness: Output fidelity is insufficient for application-level quality; visible artifacts and failure to recover fine-grained, high-frequency details (e.g., black speckling in Fig.4).
- Secondary concern: Method risks being viewed as another conditional GAN variant with stacking and limited novelty over prior multi-stage GAN work.
- Related work: Similar conditioning and multi-stage adversarial training noted (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GAN).
- Scope limitation: Approach may be hard to generalize beyond vision/graphics tasks.
- Overall verdict: Sound solution with sufficient technical novelty and visually coherent results despite quality issues; preliminary recommendation: accept.
