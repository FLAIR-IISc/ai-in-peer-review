- Novel task: human image generation conditioned on both appearance and pose using an adversarial two-stage framework.  
- Two-stage generative network: U-shaped coarse generator followed by a refinement generator that predicts residual/difference maps.  
- Stacked architecture and tailored loss design that predict difference maps rather than direct upsampling.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Manuscript is well-structured and clearly presents the proposed architecture.  
- Coarse-to-fine conditioning strategy is a notable approach for the dressing problem.  
- Generating difference maps is a valuable contribution for producing higher-resolution images.  
- Major quality concern: generated images exhibit visible artifacts (e.g., black-dot patterns in ID346).  
- Refinement stage reduces blur but is insufficient to recover high-frequency appearance details.  
- Significance concern: may be viewed as an incremental application of existing conditional GAN methods (e.g., works by Han Zhang, Xun Huang).  
- Generalizability beyond vision/graphics tasks is unclear.  
- Overall: technically novel with thorough evaluation and promise, but needs quality improvements; initial recommendation is accept.
