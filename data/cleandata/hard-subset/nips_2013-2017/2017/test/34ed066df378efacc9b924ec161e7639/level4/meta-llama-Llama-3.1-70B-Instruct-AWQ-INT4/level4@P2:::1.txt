This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses, based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The generation framework PG utilizes the pose information explicitly and consists of two key stages: pose integration and image refinement. The paper is well-written and the proposed task is novel.

The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their design decisions, including different pose embeddings, pose mask loss vs. standard L1 loss, and two-stage model vs. one-stage model. The results are promising, with the two-stage model generating more photo-realistic images than the one-stage model.

However, the quantitative evaluation is generally weak, which is understandable given that no suitable evaluation metrics exist. The authors use Structural Similarity (SSIM) and the Inception Score (IS) to measure the quality of synthesis, but these metrics may not fully capture the nuances of the generated images.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a clear explanation of how the residual blocks improve the generation performance. The authors state that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance, but they do not elaborate on this point.

Additionally, the paper does not explore the performance of using the Stage 2 network with the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially. This could be an interesting direction for future work, as it could potentially improve the performance of the model.

Overall, I am recommending that the paper be accepted based on the novelty of the problem and approach. However, I suggest that the authors include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision, as the current user study is preliminary and not convincing.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* Novelty of the problem and approach
* Well-written paper
* Extensive evaluation of design decisions
* Promising results

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* Weak quantitative evaluation
* Lack of clear explanation of how residual blocks improve generation performance
* Limited exploration of alternative architectures

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
