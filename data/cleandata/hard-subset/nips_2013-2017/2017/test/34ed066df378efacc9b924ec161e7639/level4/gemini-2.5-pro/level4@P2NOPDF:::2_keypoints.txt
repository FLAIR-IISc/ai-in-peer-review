- Introduces a clear, compelling task: pose-conditioned human image generation preserving appearance, with practical applications.  
- Proposes a two-stage coarse-to-fine GAN where the second stage predicts a residual map to refine high-frequency details.  
- Demonstrates method on the DeepFashion dataset with appropriate experiments and evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and likely reproducible given the detailed architecture and training descriptions.  
- Second-stage residual refinement improves results but generated images still exhibit noticeable artifacts and loss of fine texture (e.g., patterned clothing distortion).  
- Quality and realism remain insufficient for some practical applications due to persistent high-frequency reconstruction failures.  
- Architectural novelty is incremental: builds on existing conditional/stacked GAN ideas (e.g., StackGAN), so core contribution is more an adaptation than a fundamental advance.  
- Limited generality: techniques are highly tailored to pose-conditioned human images, and broader applicability to other ML problems is unclear.  
- Authors should better position the work relative to concurrent stacked/conditional GAN literature to clarify unique contributions.  
- Overall assessment: solid, promising approach that sets a strong baseline; recommended for acceptance despite quality and novelty caveats.
