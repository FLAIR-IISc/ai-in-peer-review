- Proposes a GAN-based method for pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target keypoints.  
- Uses established components (DCGANs, U-Nets) with the main novelty in the specific application.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Empirical results show the model can synthesize images with some degree of realism.  
- Lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation (no metrics) to assess image quality and fidelity.  
- Recommends a user study to evaluate realism and plausibility to human observers.  
- Visual examples are small and hard to assess; generated images display noticeable artifacts and discrepancies.  
- Strong claims feel premature given current visual quality and analysis.  
- Proposes a two-stage approach (pose integration then refinement) with sound motivation but lacking isolated analysis of each stage.  
- Uses a masked L1 loss to focus on the human body, but its specific contribution is not highlighted.  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, clear exposition, and a proof-of-concept demonstration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no quantitative evaluation, visible artifacts, limited novelty beyond established techniques, and weak experimental comparisons.  
- Overall: interesting and promising but not mature or sufficiently validated for a top venue; more refinement and comprehensive evaluation needed.
