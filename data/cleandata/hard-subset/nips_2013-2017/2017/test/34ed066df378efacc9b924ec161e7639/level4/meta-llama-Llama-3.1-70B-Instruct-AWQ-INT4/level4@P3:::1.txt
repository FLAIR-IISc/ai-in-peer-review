**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that synthesizes person images by conditioning on a reference image and a target pose. The network employs a two-stage approach, with the first stage focusing on capturing the global structure of the person and the second stage refining the appearance details through adversarial training. The authors evaluate their method on two person datasets and demonstrate its effectiveness in generating photo-realistic images with correct poses.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel task**: The proposed task of pose-guided person image generation is novel and has many potential applications in fields such as movie making, image retrieval, and human pose estimation.
2. **Two-stage architecture**: The coarse-to-fine architecture is well-designed and effective in capturing both the global structure and appearance details of the person.
3. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow and understand.
4. **Extensive evaluation**: The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their method, including quantitative and qualitative results on two datasets, as well as comparisons with other methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Quantitative evaluation**: While the authors provide quantitative results using metrics such as SSIM and Inception Score, these metrics may not be suitable for evaluating the quality of generated images.
2. **Limited user study**: The user study is preliminary and may not be convincing, as noted by the reviewer.

**Comments**

1. **Iterative refinement**: The reviewer suggests including recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in the related work section. This is a good suggestion, as it would provide more context for the proposed two-stage architecture.
2. **Residual blocks**: The authors should elaborate on how using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance.
3. **Stage 2 network**: The reviewer is curious about how using the Stage 2 network with the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially would perform. This is a good question, and the authors should consider providing more information on this.
4. **Blurring**: The reviewer notes that the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN may be due to the intermediate fully connected layer. The authors should investigate this and provide more information on the cause of the blurring.

**Rating**

Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I am confidently recommending that the paper be accepted. However, I encourage the authors to address the weaknesses and comments mentioned above to further improve their work.
