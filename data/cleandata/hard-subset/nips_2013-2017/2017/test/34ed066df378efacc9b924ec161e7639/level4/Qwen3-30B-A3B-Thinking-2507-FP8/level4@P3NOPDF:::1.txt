

Summary:  
This paper introduces a novel architecture for generating person images by synthesizing appearance from an input image with a specified pose (encoded as heatmaps). The approach employs a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework to progressively refine the generated output.  

Strengths:  
- **Novel task and solution**: The problem of pose-conditioned person image synthesis is well-motivated and addressed with a distinctive architecture.  
- **Effective architecture**: The coarse-to-fine design (Stage 1 for initial pose alignment, Stage 2 for detail refinement) demonstrates clear technical innovation.  
- **Thorough ablation studies**: Extensive evaluation of design choices (e.g., network components, loss functions) provides strong empirical support for the methodology.  
- **Promising results**: Despite minor visible artifacts (e.g., slight blurring or texture inconsistencies), the generated images are perceptually coherent and competitive with existing methods.  
- **Clear exposition**: The paper is well-written, with logical flow and adequate technical detail.  

Weaknesses:  
- **Limited quantitative evaluation**: While the authors appropriately acknowledge the lack of standardized metrics for this task, the evaluation relies heavily on qualitative analysis. The absence of metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID, or pose consistency scores) weakens the comparative claims. A brief discussion of metric limitations would strengthen the evaluation framework.  
- **Insufficient technical justification**: The claim in Line 110 ("using residual blocks improves generation performance") lacks specificity. Please elaborate on *how* residual blocks enhance performance (e.g., via faster convergence, reduced artifacts, or higher perceptual quality) with supporting evidence.  
- **Unexplored design variant**: The reviewer wonders whether combining Stage 1 outputs with Stage 2 *adversarially* (e.g., using Stage 1 features as input to Stage 2 during adversarial training) would mitigate the blurring observed in Stage 1 + GAN results. The authors should address whether the intermediate fully connected layer in Stage 1 contributes to this artifact.  

Comments:  
1. **Related work**: The paper would benefit from citing recent iterative refinement techniques, such as Carreira et al. (2016) on iterative error feedback for pose estimation and Newell et al. (2016) on stacked hourglass networks. These works share conceptual parallels with the proposed coarse-to-fine strategy.  
2. **User study**: While the authors include a preliminary user study, its small scale and lack of statistical rigor render it inconclusive. The reviewer urges the authors to replace this with a robust human evaluation (e.g., large-scale perceptual study with controlled variables) in revision. However, this does not affect the core recommendation.  

Overall Rating:  
The paper makes a significant contribution to person image synthesis through its novel architecture and thorough design analysis. The task novelty, technical soundness, and promising results outweigh the weaknesses. I strongly recommend acceptance.  

Rebuttal Note:  
The reviewer acknowledges the authors’ effort to include a user study but finds it insufficiently rigorous to influence the recommendation. The core contribution remains compelling, and the recommendation stands.
