Summary
The paper proposes a human image generator conditioned on appearance and human pose. The approach uses an adversarial training framework with a two-stage generator that produces a high-resolution image for a discriminator. In the first stage, a U-shaped network generates a coarse image given the appearance and a pose map. In the second stage, another generator refines the coarse output by predicting a residual (difference) map conditioned on the coarse image and the original appearance, thereby improving image quality. The method is evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.

Contributions
- Task novelty: introducing conditioning on both appearance and pose maps for human image generation.
- Techniques: a stacked, coarse-to-fine architecture that predicts a difference (residual) map rather than directly upsampling; thoughtful loss design.

Strengths
- The paper is clearly organized and effectively communicates the architecture and design choices.
- The coarse-to-fine strategy, with a residual-prediction second stage, is appropriate for the problem and is a small but useful technique for improving higher-resolution synthesis.
- The method is well aligned with the dressing problem, and the evaluation is properly conducted on a relevant dataset.

Weaknesses and Concerns
- Output quality: Generated images exhibit visible artifacts and require further improvement for practical use. For instance, in ID346 of Figure 4, the patterned region leads to black dots in the final result. Although the second generator reduces blur from the first stage, the model still appears insufficient to recover high-frequency components of the target appearance.
- Significance: Some readers may view this as yet another conditional GAN variant. The use of conditioning and stacked generators has precedents, and while the application-specific challenges are real, the perceived novelty may be limited for a broad audience.
- Generality: The work targets a strong vision/graphics application but may be difficult to generalize to other learning problems.

Relation to Prior Work
- The approach may be seen as an application of conditional GANs with stacked generators, which has been explored previously (albeit in arXiv works):
  - Han Zhang et al., “StackGAN: Text to Photo-realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks,” arXiv:1612.03242.
  - Xun Huang et al., “Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks,” arXiv:1612.04357.

Overall Assessment
The paper presents a solid solution to pose-conditioned human image generation with a well-motivated coarse-to-fine, residual-prediction architecture. It demonstrates technical novelty sufficient for the task and provides a reasonable evaluation on DeepFashion. However, the generated images still contain noticeable artifacts and lack high-frequency detail, which limits practical impact at this stage. Despite these issues, the method produces visually consistent images, and I find the contribution acceptable.

Rating: Accept.
