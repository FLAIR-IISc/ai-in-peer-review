- Proposes a human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose using a two-stage adversarial framework.  
- Generator uses a coarse U-Net to produce an initial image, then a second network predicts a residual map to refine it.  
- Evaluation is performed on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Novelty: conditions generation on both appearance and pose maps for human image synthesis.  
- Technical contribution: stacked architecture that predicts residuals and a carefully designed loss function.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the coarse-to-fine, two-network approach.  
- Residual map generation is a small but valuable technique for achieving higher-resolution outputs.  
- Main weakness: generated images exhibit visible artifacts and limited visual fidelity.  
- Specific failure: patterns (e.g., ID346) produce black-dot artifacts; the second stage reduces blurriness but cannot fully recover high-frequency details.  
- Concern: may be perceived as an incremental application of conditional/stacked GANs rather than a substantial departure from prior work.  
- Concern: unclear how readily the technique generalizes beyond clothing/appearance transfer and related vision tasks.  
- Related prior work includes stacked GAN approaches (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs) on arXiv.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with sufficient novelty; reviewer’s initial recommendation: accept.
