- Paper proposes synthesizing person images by combining a reference appearance and target pose via keypoint heatmaps using a two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline.
- Stage I: U-Net-like model with masked L1 loss produces structurally correct but blurry images by emphasizing the person over background.
- Stage II: adversarially trained U-Net-like generator predicts a residual map to refine high-frequency details and sharpen outputs.
- Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion show visually convincing results and thorough ablations over pose embeddings, loss design, and two-stage decomposition.
- Novel problem setting: conditioning person image synthesis on both a reference image and explicit pose representation.
- Clear and effective two-stage coarse-to-fine design with residual/difference-map refinement in Stage II.
- Well-written, easy to follow presentation.
- Results show strong qualitative improvements despite some remaining artifacts.
- Extensive ablations on pose encoding, loss functions (including pose mask), and one- vs two-stage training.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited, weakening empirical claims due to lack of robust metrics for this task.
- Reviewer requests citations to related cascaded/iterative refinement works (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Reviewer asks to clarify in what respects residual blocks improve performance (e.g., stability, sharpness, pose fidelity, convergence).
- Reviewer requests experiments using Stage II architecture directly with Stage I inputs and adversarial training to pinpoint causes of blurring (FC layer vs. loss balance vs. lack of residual prediction).
- Rebuttal note: included user study is preliminary and not persuasive; reviewer discounts it but still recommends acceptance and urges a more substantial human evaluation.
