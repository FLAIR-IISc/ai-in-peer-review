This paper introduces a GAN-based approach for generating novel views of individuals conditioned on a target skeleton (defined by keypoints). The architecture largely leverages established techniques – DCGANs and U-nets – positioning the core contribution within the context of this specific computer vision application.

**Strengths:**

* The proposed concept is compelling, the manuscript is clearly written, and the presented experiments demonstrate a capacity to synthesize images with a degree of realism.

**Weaknesses:**

* Given the application-focused nature of this work, strong empirical validation is crucial. A significant concern is the absence of quantitative evaluation. Conducting user studies to assess perceptual realism – for instance, by gauging the frequency with which generated images are mistaken for real images – would be a valuable addition. 
* The visual quality of the results is currently unconvincing. Figures, such as Figure 1, present images at a scale that hinders detailed assessment. Even at this resolution, noticeable visual artifacts are apparent when comparing generated outputs to the target skeletons. This limits the justification for claims of high-quality or near-realistic image synthesis. This issue persists throughout the results, including those in the supplementary materials. While the approach appears promising and the authors are pursuing a worthwhile direction, the results currently fall short of demonstrating conclusive success.

**Post-Rebuttal:**

I remain unconvinced following review of the authors’ response. Concerns regarding both quantitative and qualitative evaluations persist, as highlighted by multiple reviewers. These aspects are particularly important for a practical application-driven study. The rebuttal acknowledges these points but does not sufficiently address them to a satisfactory degree. Consequently, I do not believe the paper is currently suitable for presentation at a competitive conference such as NeurIPS.
