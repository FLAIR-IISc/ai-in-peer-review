- Paper proposes a GAN-based framework to generate novel-view person images conditioned on target skeletons, but mainly combines known components (DCGAN-style modules, U-Nets).
- Main contribution is the specific application rather than novel architecture or methods.
- Pros: idea is compelling, presentation is clear, and generated images can appear realistic to some degree.
- Major con: lacks strong quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user studies to measure how often generated images fool humans).
- Major con: visual results are weak—figures are too small to assess and produced images show numerous artifacts compared to targets.
- Supplementary results exhibit similar issues, casting doubt on output quality and realism.
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced; authors’ responses did not satisfactorily address quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Recommendation: not yet ready for a top venue like NIPS due to unresolved empirical shortcomings.
