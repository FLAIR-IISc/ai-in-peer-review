- Proposes human image generation conditioned on appearance and pose using a two-step adversarial (coarse-to-fine) generative pipeline.  
- First generator: U-shaped network produces a coarse image from appearance and pose maps.  
- Second generator: predicts a residual/difference map to refine the coarse output and recover high-resolution details.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Contribution: introduces appearance + pose conditioning for human image synthesis.  
- Contribution: technical innovation in stacked architecture that generates a residual map and a tailored loss function.  
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly communicates the coarse-to-fine strategy.  
- Strength: residual-based refinement is a notable idea for high-resolution synthesis.  
- Weakness: generated images show visible artifacts and fail to fully recover high-frequency details.  
- Weakness: quality limitations make the model currently inadequate for practical applications.  
- Weakness: perceived as a routine extension of conditional GANs with limited broader significance.  
- Weakness: method is domain-specific with unclear generalizability to other learning domains.  
- Weakness: substantial overlap with prior stacked-generator works (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs), limiting novelty.  
- Overall: technically sound and worthy of acceptance but requires significant quality improvements and stronger justification of broader impact.
