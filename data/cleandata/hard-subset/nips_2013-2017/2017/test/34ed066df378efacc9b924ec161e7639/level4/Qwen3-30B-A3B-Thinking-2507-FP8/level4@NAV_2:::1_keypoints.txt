- Novel framework (PG2) for person image synthesis that combines a reference appearance with target pose heatmaps.
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: Stage 1 generates a coarse pose-aligned image, Stage 2 refines it with adversarial training for higher-quality details.
- Task formulation (pose-conditioned person generation) is a significant novelty relative to prior NIPS work.
- Two-stage design effectively decomposes the pose-transfer problem into manageable subproblems.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, aiding understanding of methods and results.
- Generated images show promising quality but still contain occasional artifacts.
- Authors perform comprehensive ablation studies on pose embedding, loss functions, and one-stage vs two-stage architectures.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak due to lack of strong/established metrics or benchmarks for this task.
- Related work should cite iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Clarify how residual blocks concretely improve generation (quantitative/qualitative evidence requested).
- Question whether training Stage 2 adversarially using Stage 1 inputs, or removing the intermediate fully connected layer, would reduce Stage 1 blurring.
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing to the reviewer, but overall recommendation to accept remains unchanged.
