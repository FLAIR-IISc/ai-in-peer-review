- Introduces PG2, a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network that conditions on a reference image and target pose to synthesize person images.
- Key innovations: pose embedding via heatmaps, a pose mask loss focusing on human regions, and a coarse-to-fine (generation + adversarial refinement) architecture.
- Novel task with clear applications (movie making, data augmentation) and a well-motivated two-stage design addressing pose transfer challenges.
- Extensive ablation studies evaluating pose embeddings, pose mask loss, and two-stage vs one-stage architectures.
- Qualitative results show improved photo-realism and identity preservation compared to baselines.
- Writing is clear and the paper is well-organized.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM and Inception Score do not fully capture perceptual quality and can be misleading.
- Human evaluation via AMT is preliminary and lacks scale; a more rigorous user study is needed.
- Related work on iterative/cascaded refinement (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016) should be discussed.
- Need more detail on the performance gains from using residual blocks versus alternatives.
- Suggest investigating training Stage 2 adversarially with Stage 1 inputs and clarifying whether Stage 1 blurring is due to the fully connected layer or L1 loss.
- Overall recommendation: accept with confidence given novelty, strong architecture, thorough ablations, and promising qualitative results despite limited quantitative/human evaluation.
