- Proposed a two-stage adversarial human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose, with a U-shaped coarse generator followed by a residual-refinement generator.  
- Refinement stage predicts residual maps using original appearance instead of direct upsampling to improve detail.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Key contribution: novel conditioning paradigm combining appearance and pose maps for guided synthesis.  
- Key contribution: stacked generator architecture with residual refinement and tailored loss to tackle high-resolution outputs.  
- Major limitation: output quality issues—visible artifacts (e.g., black dots) and poor reconstruction of high-frequency textures.  
- Second limitation: limited novelty—approach resembles existing conditional and stacked GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).  
- Third limitation: narrow problem domain—solution is tightly coupled to human image synthesis and may not generalize broadly.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents the coarse-to-fine methodology and residual refinement idea.  
- Evaluation is appropriate for DeepFashion but lacks in-depth analysis on why high-frequency details fail.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and application-focused but quality problems and limited broader significance constrain impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with the expectation that quality limitations be addressed in future work.
