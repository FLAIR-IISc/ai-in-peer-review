- Proposes a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose using adversarial training.  
- Uses a two-step stacked generative network: a U-shaped first generator to produce a coarse image and a second generator to predict a residual that refines it.  
- Trains with a discriminator on high-resolution images and uses a difference-map (residual) prediction strategy and a unique loss design.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.  
- Novelty: task formulation of conditioning on appearance and pose maps for human image generation.  
- Architectural contribution: coarse-to-fine stacking and difference-map generation to improve high-resolution outputs.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the proposed architecture and generation strategy.  
- Results are visually consistent but still contain visible artifacts and insufficient high-frequency detail (e.g., black dots in ID346).  
- Quality improvements are still needed for practical applications; second generator reduces blur but fails to fully recover fine details.  
- Concern about broader novelty: approach may be seen as another application of conditional/stacked GANs, similar to prior works (e.g., StackGAN).  
- Domain limitation: problem is well-suited to vision/graphics but may be hard to generalize to other learning problems.  
- Evaluation is thorough and technical novelty is sufficient; reviewer’s initial recommendation: accept.
