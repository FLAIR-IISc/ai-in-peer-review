- Proposes a human image generator conditioned on both appearance and pose using a two-stage adversarial generative network.
- Generator Stage 1: U-shaped network produces a coarse image from appearance and pose map.
- Generator Stage 2: predicts a residual (difference) map to refine the coarse image using the original appearance.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Novelty: conditioning on both appearance and pose for human image generation.
- Novelty: stacked architecture that predicts a difference map instead of direct upsampling.
- Novelty: well-designed loss function.
- Strength: well-organized paper that clearly conveys the architecture and coarse-to-fine strategy.
- Strength: difference map technique shows promise for higher-resolution outputs.
- Major weakness: generated images still contain visible artifacts and require substantial quality improvements.
- Example artifact: pattern in ID346 (Fig.4) produces black dots in final images.
- Limitation: model struggles to recover high-frequency components of target appearance despite second-stage refinement.
- Concern: approach may be seen as another conditional GAN variant similar to prior Stacked GAN work, limiting broader significance.
- Limitation: problem domain (pose-conditioned dressing) may not generalize easily to other learning problems.
- Overall assessment: technically novel, produces visually consistent images, thorough evaluation, and initial recommendation to accept.
