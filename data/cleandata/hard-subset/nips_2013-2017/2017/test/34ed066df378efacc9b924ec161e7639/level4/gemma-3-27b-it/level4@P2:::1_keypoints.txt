- Introduces a novel task and method for person image generation: transferring appearance from a source to a target pose using heatmaps.
- Proposes a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2): pose integration followed by image refinement.
- Two-stage design (global structure then detailed appearance) is sensible and aligns with prior cascaded approaches.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.
- Results are promising but show some visual artifacts and blurring.
- Authors perform extensive analysis of design choices (pose embeddings, loss functions), demonstrating thorough evaluation.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM and Inception Score may be inappropriate for perceptual pose-based generation.
- User study is preliminary and does not strongly support claims.
- Missing related work discussion: should cite cascaded/iterative refinement papers to contextualize the architecture.
- Claim on benefits of residual blocks is under-explained; needs details on how they improve performance.
- Suggested experiment: train Stage 2 directly on Stage 1 inputs with adversarial loss to test necessity of two-stage training.
- Suggested analysis: evaluate if the Stage 1 intermediate fully connected layer causes blurring.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technically sound architecture, clarity, and thorough investigation.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak quantitative metrics and limited user study.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, but revise to address related work citations and elaborate on residual block benefits.
