This paper proposes a GAN-based system for generating novel-view person images, guided by a target skeleton represented as keypoints. While the system leverages established techniques such as DCGANs and U-nets, its contribution lies in the specific application of these techniques to a computer vision problem.

Strengths:
* The proposed concept is intriguing, the writing is clear, and the empirical experiments demonstrate the system's ability to synthesize somewhat realistic images.
Weaknesses:
* Given the application-oriented nature of this work, achieving high-quality results is crucial. However, several concerns arise:
    + The lack of quantitative evaluation is a significant drawback. For instance, user studies could be conducted to assess how often the generated images deceive human observers.
    + The empirical results, as seen in Fig. 1, are not visually impressive, with the generated images being too small to discern. Moreover, noticeable visual errors are apparent when comparing the results to the target images. This raises doubts about the quality of the results and their similarity to real images. Unfortunately, this issue persists throughout the results, including the supplementary figures. While the authors are on the right track, and the work holds promise, it is premature to declare success at this stage.

After Rebuttal:
Upon reviewing the authors' response, I remain unconvinced. As echoed by other reviewers, concerns regarding both quantitative and qualitative results persist, which are essential for a practical, application-oriented work like this. Although the rebuttal shows some effort to address these concerns, they are not adequately resolved. I believe the work may not be ready for presentation at a venue like NIPS.
