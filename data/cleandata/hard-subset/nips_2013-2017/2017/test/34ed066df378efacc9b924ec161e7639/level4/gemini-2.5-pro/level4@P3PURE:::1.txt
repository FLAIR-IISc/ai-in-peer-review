### Summary

This paper proposes a novel architecture for person image generation. The model synthesizes an image of a person by combining the appearance from a source image with a target pose, which is specified using keypoint heat maps.

### Strengths

*   The proposed task is novel and interesting.
*   The paper introduces a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
*   The qualitative results are promising, even though they contain some discernible artifacts.
*   The authors provide an extensive evaluation of their design decisions.

### Weaknesses

*   The quantitative evaluation is somewhat weak. However, the reviewer acknowledges that this is an inherent difficulty of the task due to the lack of suitable, established evaluation metrics, and thus does not consider it a major flaw.

### Comments for the Authors

*   Given the iterative, cascaded nature of the proposed architecture, the authors should consider discussing and citing recent work on iterative refinement in their related work section. Examples include:
    *   [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016.
    *   [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
*   On line 110, the authors state that "using residual blocks as basic component improves the generation performance." The authors should elaborate on this point: in what specific way was performance improved?
*   While the evaluation is extensive, a potentially insightful ablation is missing. How would the Stage 2 network perform if it were given the same inputs as Stage 1 and trained adversarially? This could help clarify whether the blurring observed with the "Stage 1 + GAN" model is primarily caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.

A note on the user study: While the reviewer appreciates the authors' attempt at a human evaluation, its preliminary nature makes it unconvincing. Consequently, this aspect of the evaluation was discounted when forming the final recommendation. The authors are strongly encouraged to include a more substantive user study in the final version of the paper. Nevertheless, the positive assessment of the work stands.

### Overall Rating

Overall, this is a strong paper that tackles a novel and challenging problem with an interesting approach. Based on the novelty of the problem and the proposed architecture, I confidently recommend this paper for acceptance.
