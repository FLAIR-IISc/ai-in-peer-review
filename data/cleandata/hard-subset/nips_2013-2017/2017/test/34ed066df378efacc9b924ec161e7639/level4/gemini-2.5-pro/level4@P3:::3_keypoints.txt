- Introduces PG², a two-stage framework that generates a person image in a target pose from a single source image using pose heatmaps and a coarse-to-fine pipeline.  
- Stage I: U-Net-like network fuses source image and target pose heatmap to produce a coarse image; introduces a pose mask loss to focus L1 error on the foreground person.  
- Stage II: Conditional GAN refines the coarse output to add details and improve realism.  
- Demonstrated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  
- Builds on prior conditional GAN and view-synthesis work, using explicit skeletal keypoints for direct pose control.  
- Coarse-to-fine two-stage design is sensible and aligns with prior successful architectures (e.g., StackGAN).  
- Strength: Tackles a novel, challenging problem with useful applications (data augmentation, re-identification, fashion) and offers explicit pose control.  
- Strength: Sound methodological design with a logical divide-and-conquer approach and a practical pose mask loss.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clear, and experiments are structured to validate model components.  
- Weakness: Generated images are often not photo-realistic—blurry, lacking fine textures, with artifacts and anatomical distortions (worse on Market-1501).  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is underwhelming—user study shows >85% of generated DeepFashion images identified as fake; SSIM metric is problematic for GAN outputs.  
- Weakness: Technical novelty is limited—main components are existing architectures (U-Net, cGAN); contributions are primarily application-focused (pipeline + pose mask loss).  
- Summary/Recommendation: Promising proof-of-concept but current image quality and evaluation are insufficient for a top-tier venue; reviewer leans toward rejection pending stronger generation quality and more rigorous evaluation.
