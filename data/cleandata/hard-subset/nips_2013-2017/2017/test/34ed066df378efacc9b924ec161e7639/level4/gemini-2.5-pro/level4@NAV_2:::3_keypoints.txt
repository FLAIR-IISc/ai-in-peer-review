- Introduces PG², a two-stage pose-guided person image generation method conditioning synthesis on a target pose and source image.  
- Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like network to produce a coarse image capturing global structure and colors; Stage 2 uses a conditional GAN to refine textures and details.  
- Builds on established generative models (U-Nets, GANs) in a coarse-to-fine pipeline tailored to pose-guided synthesis.  
- Proposes a pose mask loss to focus learning on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Addresses a novel and significant problem with useful applications (data augmentation, effects, e-commerce) and a compelling controllable generation direction.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clearly motivated, and illustrated; the divide-and-conquer two-stage design is logical and easy to follow.  
- Methodologically sensible decomposition that separates global structure estimation from appearance refinement.  
- Qualitative results are often blurry, lack fine textures, and contain visual artifacts; figures are small, hindering detailed assessment.  
- Quantitative evaluation relies on SSIM/IS and a user study, but pixel metrics poorly correlate with perceptual quality and user study shows low fooling rates (e.g., 14.9% on DeepFashion).  
- Overall the work is a promising first step but feels preliminary: visual quality and evaluation need substantial improvement to meet top-tier conference standards.
