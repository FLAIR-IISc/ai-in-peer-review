- Paper proposes a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to synthesize person images by combining source appearance with target pose heatmaps.  
- Novel and well-motivated task with an effective two-stage design.  
- Writing is clear and paper is well-structured.  
- Results are promising, though visible artifacts remain.  
- Extensive analysis and ablation studies of design choices are provided.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited due to lack of standard metrics, leaving a gap.  
- Related work should discuss recent cascaded/iterative refinement methods (e.g., iterative error feedback, stacked hourglass).  
- Clarify what improvements residual blocks provide (sharper textures, structural fidelity, fewer artifacts, faster convergence, or quantitative gains) with examples/metrics.  
- Investigate whether a single Stage-2 adversarially trained network (using Stage-1 inputs) could match the two-stage pipeline.  
- Ablation needed to test if Stage-1+GAN blurring is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer and whether a fully convolutional alternative improves sharpness.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; a more substantive human evaluation is encouraged.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel approach with sufficiently compelling results and analysis.
