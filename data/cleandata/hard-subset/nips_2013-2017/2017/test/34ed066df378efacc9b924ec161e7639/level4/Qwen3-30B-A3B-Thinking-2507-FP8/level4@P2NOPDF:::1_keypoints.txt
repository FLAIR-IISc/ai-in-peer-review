- Paper proposes a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture to synthesize person images conditioned on an input appearance and target pose heatmaps.  
- Strength: Tackles a new, meaningful task in person image synthesis.  
- Strength: Coarse-to-fine architecture is clean and effective.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
- Strength: Generated images show strong promise despite minor artifacts.  
- Strength: Extensive evaluation of design choices improves reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is limited by lack of established metrics for this task.  
- Comment: Add related work on cascaded/iterative refinement (e.g., Carreira et al. CVPR 2016; Newell et al. ECCV 2016) to contextualize the approach.  
- Comment: The claim that residual blocks improve performance needs specifics—quantitative metrics, qualitative comparisons, or ablation results.  
- Comment: Investigate training Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs adversarially and whether the intermediate fully connected layer causes blurring in Stage1+GAN outputs.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept—novel problem and compelling solution with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses.  
- Rebuttal note: Preliminary user study is too limited and lacks statistical rigor; a larger, controlled human evaluation with clear metrics is needed, though this doesn't change the positive technical assessment.
