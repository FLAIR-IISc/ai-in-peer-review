- Proposes a two-stage human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose maps: a U-shaped coarse generator followed by a residual-refining generator.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion with a coarse-to-fine strategy suited to dressing applications.  
- Key claimed contributions: conditioning on both appearance and pose, stacked architecture predicting residual maps, and tailored loss design.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the methodology.  
- Coarse-to-fine approach and residual-based refinement are sensible for high-resolution output.  
- Generated image quality is limited, with visible artifacts and poor recovery of fine texture details.  
- Second stage reduces blurriness but fails to resolve high-frequency pattern inconsistencies.  
- Novelty is questionable since stacking generators follows prior conditional/stacked GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).  
- Contribution appears incremental and may be viewed as a specialized application rather than a general methodological advance.  
- Evaluation lacks rigorous quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, LPIPS) and ablation studies isolating the residual refinement impact.  
- Recommendation: technically valid and relevant, but needs artifact reduction, stronger quantitative validation, and clearer positioning against prior stacked GAN literature; reviewer recommends acceptance with revisions.
