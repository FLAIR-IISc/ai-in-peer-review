This paper presents a GAN-based system for generating novel views of people from a target skeleton (represented as keypoints). The system largely relies on established techniques like DCGANs and U-nets, so the primary contribution lies in its application to this specific computer vision problem.

**Pros:**

* The proposed idea is interesting, the paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the empirical experiments demonstrate a capability to synthesize images that appear reasonably realistic.

**Cons:**

* Given the application-oriented nature of this work, strong results are crucial. My main concern is the complete lack of quantitative evaluation. For example, a user study could assess how often the generated images are mistaken for real images by human observers.
* The empirical results are not particularly compelling visually. For instance, the images in Figure 1 are too small for detailed inspection. Even at this size, noticeable visual artifacts are present when comparing the generated images to the target skeletons. Consequently, it feels premature to conclude that the results are of high quality or closely resemble real images. This issue persists throughout the results, including those in the supplementary material. I believe the authors are on the right track and the work is promising, but it is too early to claim a significant breakthrough. 

**After Rebuttal:**

I remain unconvinced after reviewing the authors’ response. Several reviewers, including myself, have raised concerns regarding both the quantitative and qualitative evaluation of the results—both of which are vital for a practically-focused work. The rebuttal offers some attempts to address these concerns, but they do not do so to a satisfactory degree. I do not believe the paper is currently ready for presentation at a competitive venue like NeurIPS.
