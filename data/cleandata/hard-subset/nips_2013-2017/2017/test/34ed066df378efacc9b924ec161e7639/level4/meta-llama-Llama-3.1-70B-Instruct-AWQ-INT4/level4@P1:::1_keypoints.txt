- Novel task addressing a challenging person image generation problem by combining appearance and pose heat maps.
- Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture is well-designed and produces high-quality results.
- Paper is well-written and clear with good explanations of method and components.
- Results are promising despite discernible artifacts.
- Extensive evaluation of design decisions increases confidence in the method.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak; more qualitative analysis or user studies are needed.
- Recommend adding related work on iterative refinement/cascaded networks (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Authors should explain how residual blocks improve generation performance.
- Investigate Stage 2 used with Stage 1 inputs and trained adversarially to determine cause of blurring (possible intermediate FC layer).
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty and contribution.
- Rebuttal: current user study is preliminary and not convincing; a more substantive human evaluation is urged, but the positive recommendation stands.
