- Proposes a pose- and appearance-conditioned human image generator trained adversarially with a two-stage coarse-to-fine generator and evaluated on DeepFashion.
- Key technical contributions: stacked U-Net–style first stage, second-stage residual (difference map) refinement, and tailored loss design.
- Task novelty: combines conditioning on both appearance and pose for human image synthesis.
- Strength: clear organization and explanation of architecture and training setup.
- Strength: coarse-to-fine stacked generator is an appropriate and effective design for virtual try-on/dressing scenarios.
- Strength: predicting a residual to refine the coarse image is a simple yet effective way to improve high-resolution outputs.
- Weakness: generated images show visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., patterned-clothing black-dot artifacts in Fig. 4).
- Weakness: perceived significance is limited—method may be seen as another variant of conditional/stacked GANs rather than a major advance.
- Weakness: generality is constrained; approach may be difficult to extend beyond this vision/graphics application.
- Relation to prior work: leverages conditioning and stacking ideas already explored in prior conditional/stacked GANs (e.g., StackGAN), making parts of the architecture appear incremental.
- Overall: solid, clearly written solution with sufficient application-level novelty (two-stage residual refinement and loss design) but uneven visual quality; reviewer recommends accept.
