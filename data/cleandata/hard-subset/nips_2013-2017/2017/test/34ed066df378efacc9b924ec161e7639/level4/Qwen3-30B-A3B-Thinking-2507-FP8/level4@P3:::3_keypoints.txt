- Proposes a two-stage pose-guided person image generation framework: Stage I U-Net generates coarse pose-aligned image; Stage II conditional GAN refines via a difference map.
- Introduces conditioning on explicit pose keypoints alongside a reference image and a novel pose mask loss that focuses learning on human body regions.
- Difference-map formulation in Stage II improves sharpness, speeds training, and targets residual errors.
- Builds on established tools (U-Net, GANs) but applies them to a novel, practical pose-transfer task.
- Technical approach is well-motivated, theoretically justified, and clearly described.
- Experimental evaluation uses two relevant datasets (Market-1501, DeepFashion) with reasonable baselines and ablation studies.
- Pose mask loss and difference-map refinement are useful, meaningful innovations for conditional image generation.
- Quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) do not adequately capture perceptual quality of results.
- Qualitative results are inconclusive: small/low-resolution figures, visible artifacts (incorrect body parts, color mismatches, blur) undermine photo-realism claims.
- User study is small (30 participants) and lacks statistical significance testing, weakening perceptual validation.
- Novelty is limited: core methods are incremental applications of U-Net and GANs; pose mask loss is not groundbreaking.
- Significance is moderate: problem is practical but current results do not demonstrate a transformative improvement over prior work.
- Overall assessment: technically sound but evidence insufficient to support strong quality claims.
- Recommendation: reject for NIPS; encourage higher-resolution results, larger/statistically rigorous user studies, and stronger perceptual evaluation for resubmission.
