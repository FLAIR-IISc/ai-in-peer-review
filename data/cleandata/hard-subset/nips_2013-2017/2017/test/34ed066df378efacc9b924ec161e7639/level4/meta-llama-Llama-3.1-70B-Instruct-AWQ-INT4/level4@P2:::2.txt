The paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that synthesizes person images in arbitrary poses based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The authors introduce a two-stage approach, where the first stage generates a coarse image using a U-Net-like network, and the second stage refines the initial result through adversarial training with a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN). The proposed method is evaluated on two person datasets, Market-1501 and DeepFashion, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach in generating high-quality person images with convincing details.

The main ideas of the paper relate to previous work on generative image modeling, particularly in the context of Variational Autoencoders (VAE), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Autoregressive models. The authors draw inspiration from recent advances in GANs, which have shown promise in generating sharp images through adversarial training. The proposed approach builds upon this work by introducing a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose, allowing for direct control over the generation process.

The strengths of the paper include the introduction of a novel task and a two-stage approach that effectively addresses the challenging problem of pose transfer. The authors also propose a pose mask loss that alleviates the influence of background changes and guides the generator to focus on transferring the human body appearance. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality person images with convincing details.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. The generated results still contain visible artifacts, and the model may not be sufficient to recover high-frequency components in the target appearance. Additionally, some might argue that the proposed approach is an application of conditional GANs, which has been explored in previous work. While the authors acknowledge this, they also highlight the novelty of their approach in introducing a pose-conditioned image generation task.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-organized and effectively conveys the key aspects of the proposed architecture. The writing is clear, and the authors provide sufficient details about the methodology and experimental setup. However, there are some minor issues with grammar and sentence structure that could be improved.

In conclusion, the paper proposes a novel and effective approach to pose-conditioned image generation. While there are some areas for improvement, the authors demonstrate the technical novelty and significance of their work. The paper is well-written and effectively conveys the key ideas and contributions.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel task and approach to pose-conditioned image generation.

* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality person images with convincing details.

Arguments against acceptance:
