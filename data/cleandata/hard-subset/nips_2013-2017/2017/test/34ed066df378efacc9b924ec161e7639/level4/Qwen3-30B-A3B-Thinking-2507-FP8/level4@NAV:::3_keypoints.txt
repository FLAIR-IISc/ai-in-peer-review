- Proposes a GAN-based framework for novel-view person image synthesis conditioned on skeleton keypoints; contribution is mainly application of established components.
- Core idea is compelling, exposition is clear, and initial experiments show the system can produce somewhat realistic synthetic images.
- Major methodological flaw: absence of quantitative evaluation metrics.
- Recommends human subject studies to objectively assess perceptual fidelity (e.g., how often generated images deceive humans).
- Qualitative results are weak: low-resolution figures and noticeable visual artifacts (distorted limbs, unnatural textures).
- These visual inconsistencies undermine claims of “realistic” synthesis and make results premature.
- Authors’ rebuttal failed to resolve the critical gaps in quantitative and qualitative evaluation.
- Reviewer concludes the work does not meet the standards required for presentation at NIPS.
