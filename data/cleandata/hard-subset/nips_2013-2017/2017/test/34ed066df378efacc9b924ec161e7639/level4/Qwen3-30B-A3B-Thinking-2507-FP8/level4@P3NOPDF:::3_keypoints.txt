- Contribution is application-focused and relies on established components (standard GANs, U-Net), offering limited methodological novelty.  
- Strengths: clear exposition, well-structured methodology, and a functional pipeline with some visually plausible outputs in specific cases.  
- Major deficiency: lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation—no comprehensive metrics (FID, LPIPS) or human subject studies to assess realism.  
- Qualitative results are unconvincing: low-resolution figures and persistent artifacts (distorted limbs, unnatural textures, misaligned body parts); examples cited in Fig.1b and Fig.4.  
- Supplementary material and rebuttal do not adequately address visual issues; the provided FID (e.g., 25.8) is high and unexplained relative to observed artifacts.  
- No user studies were conducted to validate perceptual realism, leaving a critical evaluation gap.  
- Overall: a promising foundation but not mature or robust enough for NeurIPS due to insufficient empirical validation and visual quality.  
- Recommendation: require substantial revision—add rigorous quantitative and human evaluations and improve visual quality via architectural or training refinements before reconsideration.
