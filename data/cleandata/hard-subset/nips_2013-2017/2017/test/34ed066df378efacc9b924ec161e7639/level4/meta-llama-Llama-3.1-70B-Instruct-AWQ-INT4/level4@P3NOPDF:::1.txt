**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for generating images of a person that combines the appearance of a person in an input image with a specified input pose, represented using heat maps. The proposed task is innovative and has the potential to contribute significantly to the field of computer vision and image generation.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the task**: The proposed task of generating images of a person with a specified pose is new and has not been explored before. This novelty is a significant strength of the paper.
2. **Two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture**: The proposed architecture is novel and well-designed. The two-stage approach allows for iterative refinement of the generated image, leading to more accurate and realistic results.
3. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow and understand.
4. **Promising results**: Although the results contain some discernible artifacts, they are promising and demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach.
5. **Extensive evaluation**: The authors have conducted an extensive evaluation of their design decisions, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Weak quantitative evaluation**: The quantitative evaluation of the proposed approach is limited, which is understandable given the lack of suitable evaluation metrics. However, this weakness is not significant enough to detract from the overall contribution of the paper.

**Comments**

1. **Related work**: The authors may want to consider including some recent work on iterative refinement using cascaded networks in their related work section. For example, [a] J. Carreira, P. Agrawal, K. Fragkiadaki, and J. Malik. Human pose estimation with iterative error feedback. In IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2016, and [b] A. Newell, K. Yang, and J. Deng. Stacked hourglass networks for human pose estimation. In European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2016.
2. **Elaboration on residual blocks**: In line 110, the authors mention that using residual blocks as basic components improves the generation performance. However, it would be helpful to elaborate on how this improvement is achieved.
3. **Alternative evaluation**: I was curious to see how the Stage 2 network would perform with the inputs for Stage 1 and trained adversarially. Additionally, it would be interesting to investigate whether the blurring observed with Stage 1 + GAN is due to the intermediate fully connected layer.

**Overall Rating**

Based on the novelty of the problem and approach, I confidently recommend that the paper be accepted. The strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses, and the proposed approach has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

**Rebuttal**

While I appreciate the authors' attempt at a user study, I find it preliminary and not convincing. Therefore, I am discounting it in my review rating. I urge the authors to include a more substantive human evaluation in their revision. Nonetheless, my positive review of the work still stands.
