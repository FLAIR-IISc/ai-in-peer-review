This paper proposes a human image generator conditioned on appearance and human pose. The model is trained adversarially with a two-stage generator that produces a high-resolution output for a discriminator. In the generator, the first stage uses a U-Net–style architecture to synthesize a coarse image from the appearance and pose maps; the second stage takes this coarse result together with the original appearance input and predicts a residual (difference map) to refine the output. Evaluation is conducted on the DeepFashion dataset.

Key ideas and contributions:
- Task novelty: conditioning human image generation on both appearance and pose maps.
- Techniques: a stacked, coarse-to-fine architecture that predicts a difference map rather than directly upsampling; and a tailored loss design.

Strengths:
- The paper is well organized and clearly conveys the proposed architecture and training setup.
- The coarse-to-fine stacking of generators is an appropriate design choice for this problem and is effectively applied to the dressing/virtual try-on scenario.
- Predicting a residual to refine the coarse image is a simple but effective technique for improving higher-resolution outputs.

Areas for improvement:
- Image quality still needs work. The generated results contain visible artifacts that limit practical applicability. For example, in Fig. 4, ID346 exhibits black dot artifacts introduced by the patterned clothing. While the second generator reduces the blur of the first stage, the model appears insufficient to recover high-frequency details in the target appearance.
- Significance may be perceived as limited, as the method could be viewed as another variant of conditional GANs with a stacked design.
- Generality is somewhat constrained. This is a strong vision/graphics application, but the approach may be difficult to generalize to other learning problems.

Relation to prior work:
- The paper leverages conditioning and stacking, which have been explored in prior adversarial frameworks. Although the application here introduces domain-specific challenges, some may view the core architecture as incremental relative to prior conditional/stacked GANs, such as:
  - Han Zhang, Tao Xu, Hongsheng Li, Shaoting Zhang, Xiaolei Huang, Xiaogang Wang, Dimitris Metaxas. StackGAN: Text to Photo-realistic Image Synthesis with Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks. arXiv:1612.03242.
  - Xun Huang, Yixuan Li, Omid Poursaeed, John Hopcroft, Serge Belongie. Stacked Generative Adversarial Networks. arXiv:1612.04357.

Overall assessment:
The paper presents a solid solution to pose-conditioned human image generation and evaluates it appropriately on DeepFashion. The approach introduces sufficient technical novelty for this application, especially the two-stage residual refinement strategy and the loss design. However, the visual quality remains uneven due to artifacts and limited recovery of high-frequency details. Despite these issues, the results are generally visually consistent, and the paper is clearly written. My initial rating is accept.
