- Proposes a GAN-based system to synthesize novel-view human images conditioned on target skeleton keypoints; main contribution is applying established components (DCGANs, U-Nets) to this task.
- Positive: idea is interesting, writing is clear, and experiments show some degree of realistic-looking synthesis.
- Major concern: no quantitative evaluation provided (e.g., missing user study to measure how often generated images fool humans).
- Qualitative results are not visually compelling: generated images are too small to evaluate and exhibit noticeable visual errors relative to targets.
- Supplementary results show the same shortcomings, making it difficult to claim high-quality or near-real images.
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address the quantitative and qualitative issues raised.
- Conclusion: reviewer remains unconvinced and considers the paper not ready for a venue like NIPS in its current form.
