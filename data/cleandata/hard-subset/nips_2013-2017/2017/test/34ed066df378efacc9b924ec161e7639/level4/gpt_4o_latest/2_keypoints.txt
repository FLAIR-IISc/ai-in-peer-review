- Introduces pose- and appearance-conditioned human image generation using an adversarial, two-step (coarse-to-fine) generator.
- Generator design: first U-shaped network produces a coarse image; second network refines via predicted residual (difference) map using original appearance.
- Uses a stacked architecture and carefully designed loss function to improve high-resolution outputs.
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset with visually consistent results.
- Small but notable contribution: predicting a residual map for refinement rather than direct upsampling.
- Main limitation: generated image quality still has visible artifacts and fails to recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in Fig. 4 ID346).
- Risk of low perceived novelty: can be seen as another straightforward application/variation of conditional GANs.
- Prior related work (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs) reduces novelty claim.
- Limited generalizability: approach may not extend easily beyond vision/graphics tasks.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly communicates the method.
- Suitable for virtual dressing applications but appeal may be limited to a narrower audience.
- Overall assessment: technically viable solution with sufficient novelty; initial reviewer recommendation: accept.
