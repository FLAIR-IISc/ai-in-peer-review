

# Peer Review: Pose Guided Person Image Generation

## Summary
This paper proposes a novel two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), for generating person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose (represented as keypoints). The authors address the challenging task of transferring a person from one pose to another by dividing the problem into two stages: Stage-I focuses on capturing global human body structure using a U-Net-like architecture with pose embedding (via 18 heatmaps) and a novel pose mask loss to suppress background influence, while Stage-II refines the coarse result using a conditional GAN that generates a difference map rather than the full image from scratch. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach on two datasets (DeepFashion and Market-1501), showing improvements over ablation studies and a comparison with the most similar prior work [36].

The work relates to recent advances in conditional image generation (e.g., [18, 24, 22, 23, 32]) and pose transfer tasks, but distinguishes itself by explicitly using pose keypoints as condition rather than abstract labels or viewpoints. It builds upon the divide-and-conquer strategy used in other image-to-image translation tasks [3, 9, 19, 35] but applies it specifically to the challenging problem of person pose transfer.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Task Definition**: The paper clearly defines and motivates a new task (pose-guided person image generation) with significant applications in movie making and pose estimation data generation. This is a meaningful contribution to the field of conditional image generation.

2. **Technical Soundness**: The two-stage approach is well-justified and addresses a genuine challenge in end-to-end pose transfer (simultaneously generating correct pose and detailed appearance). The pose mask loss is a clever solution to the background interference problem, and the difference map approach in Stage-II is a smart way to accelerate training.

3. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The paper provides thorough qualitative and quantitative results, including ablation studies on pose embedding methods, loss functions, and the two-stage vs. one-stage comparison. The user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk provides valuable human evaluation.

4. **Clear Methodology**: The paper explains the technical details well, with sufficient information for reproducibility (though some architectural details are in supplementary materials). The figures effectively illustrate the approach and results.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art**: While the paper compares with [36], it doesn't compare with other recent GAN-based image-to-image translation methods (e.g., [31, 34, 35]) that could be relevant to their task. The claim that their method is "the most related" to [36] is not sufficiently justified.

2. **Quantitative Evaluation Limitations**: The use of SSIM and Inception Score as primary metrics is problematic for this task. As the authors themselves note, blurry images often achieve higher SSIM but are less photo-realistic (a known issue in image generation evaluation). The paper doesn't provide more perceptually meaningful metrics (e.g., FID, human preference scores beyond the limited AMT study).

3. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: While the approach is well-motivated, there's no theoretical analysis explaining why the two-stage approach works better than a single-stage model. The paper states that the two-stage approach "converges faster" but doesn't provide evidence for this beyond qualitative observations.

4. **Inadequate Failure Analysis**: The paper mentions failure cases (Figure 6) but doesn't thoroughly analyze why they occur. For instance, the gender misclassification could be addressed by discussing how to improve the model's handling of gender-specific features.

5. **Comparison with [36] is Superficial**: The comparison with [36] in Figure 5 is qualitative but lacks quantitative metrics. The paper should provide numerical comparison to establish the magnitude of improvement.

## Overall Assessment

**Quality**: 7/10 - The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments, but lacks statistical significance testing for quantitative results and doesn't thoroughly address evaluation limitations.

**Clarity**: 8/10 - Well-organized and clearly written, though some technical details are relegated to supplementary materials.

**Originality**: 7/10 - The two-stage approach to pose-guided person generation is novel, but the core techniques (U-Net, GAN, pose embedding) are not particularly new. The pose mask loss is a valuable contribution but not groundbreaking.

**Significance**: 7/10 - The task is valuable with clear applications, but the paper doesn't strongly establish how this significantly advances the state-of-the-art beyond prior work. The improvement over [36] is demonstrated, but the paper doesn't convincingly show how much better it is than other potential approaches.

## Recommendation

The paper presents a well-executed approach to an interesting problem with clear applications. While the technical contributions are not revolutionary, they are sound and well-motivated. The method shows clear qualitative improvements over previous work, though the quantitative evaluation is somewhat limited.

I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions, particularly to strengthen the evaluation section by:
1. Including more perceptually meaningful metrics (e.g., FID)
2. Providing quantitative comparisons with [36] beyond the qualitative examples
3. Adding more analysis of the failure cases to better understand the method's limitations
4. Clarifying the comparison with other relevant image-to-image translation methods

The paper makes a solid contribution to the field of conditional image generation, particularly for the specific problem of pose-guided person image synthesis. While not groundbreaking, it's a valuable addition to the literature that provides a practical solution to a meaningful problem.

## Pro/Con Acceptance Summary

**Pros for Acceptance:**
- Novel task definition with clear applications
- Well-justified two-stage approach to a challenging problem
- Comprehensive evaluation with ablation studies
- Strong qualitative results demonstrating the method's effectiveness
- Practical implementation with clear technical details

**Cons for Acceptance:**
- Limited comparison to relevant state-of-the-art methods
- Quantitative evaluation relies on potentially misleading metrics
- Lacks theoretical justification for the two-stage approach
- Superficial comparison with the most similar prior work [36]
- Inadequate analysis of failure cases

Overall, the paper presents a well-executed approach to a meaningful problem that makes a solid contribution to the field. With minor revisions addressing the evaluation limitations, it would be an excellent addition to NIPS.
