## Review of "Pose Guided Person Image Generation"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and interesting task – generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a desired pose. The proposed two-stage approach, combining a U-Net for initial pose transfer and a conditional DCGAN for refinement, is a reasonable and well-motivated strategy. The experiments on two datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, and the ablation studies provide valuable insights. However, the paper suffers from a lack of strong quantitative results differentiating it from existing approaches, and the significance of the improvements, while visible qualitatively, feels somewhat limited. I lean towards **weak accept** with a request for stronger quantitative validation and a more thorough comparison to related work.

**1. Summary:**

The paper tackles the problem of pose-guided person image generation. The authors argue that explicitly controlling the generation process via pose is crucial for applications like movie making and data augmentation for pose estimation. Their approach leverages a two-stage pipeline: a U-Net (Stage-I) generates a coarse image based on the reference image and target pose, and a conditional DCGAN (Stage-II) refines this image to add details.  Key contributions include the novel task formulation, a pose mask loss to focus on body appearance, and the two-stage generation strategy.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Interesting Task:**  The combination of reference image and pose conditioning is a compelling direction. It moves beyond simply generating images from attributes or text, providing a more direct control mechanism.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** Dividing the problem into global structure and detail refinement is a sensible strategy, given the complexity of the task.
* **Pose Mask Loss:**  The proposed mask loss is a clever addition, effectively mitigating the impact of background differences between the reference and target images, a crucial consideration.
* **Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies systematically evaluate the different components of the proposed method (pose embeddings, loss functions, two-stage vs. one-stage), demonstrating their individual contributions.
* **Qualitative Results:**  The visual results, particularly the comparisons between different configurations, are convincing and show the benefits of the proposed approach. The comparisons with the related work [36] further highlight the advantage of the method.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making it easy to follow the authors' approach.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Quantitative Improvement:**  While the paper presents quantitative results (SSIM, IS), the improvements over existing methods are not overwhelming. The authors acknowledge the limitations of these metrics, but stronger quantitative evidence demonstrating the superiority of their approach is needed.  The mask-SSIM and mask-IS are good attempts to address background issues, but the numbers aren’t compelling enough.
* **Comparison to Related Work:** The comparison with [36] is helpful, but the field of image manipulation and generation is vast.  A more comprehensive comparison to recent GAN-based image editing techniques (e.g., StyleGAN-based methods) would be beneficial, even if they don’t directly address pose transfer. Could the refinement stage leverage techniques from image editing to improve quality?
* **Failure Cases & Limitations:** The authors briefly mention failure cases, but a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the approach would strengthen the paper.  For instance, what types of poses are particularly challenging? What happens with occlusions or complex clothing?
* **User Study Interpretation:** The user study results are somewhat ambiguous. While the G1+G2+D model confuses users slightly more often, the lower score on Market-1501 suggests it might be introducing artifacts that make the images less realistic, even if they are more pose-accurate.  Further analysis of the user study data is needed.
* **Dataset Bias:** The observation about dataset imbalance (male/female) influencing results is important, but the paper doesn’t fully address how this might affect the generalizability of the method.



**4. Evaluation of Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The technical approach is sound, and the results are reasonably well-supported by experiments. The two-stage pipeline is a logical design choice.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is very well-written and easy to understand.  The figures and diagrams are helpful.
* **Originality (7/10):** While the task is novel, the individual components (U-Net, DCGAN, pose estimation) are well-established. The originality lies in the combination and the specific application to pose-guided generation.
* **Significance (6/10):** The potential applications are relevant, but the improvements over existing methods, as demonstrated by the quantitative results, are not dramatic enough to consider this a major advance.  



**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Addresses a novel and interesting problem.
* Proposes a well-motivated and technically sound approach.
* Demonstrates effectiveness through ablation studies and qualitative results.
* Clear and well-written paper.

**Against:**

* Limited quantitative improvement over existing methods.
* Could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to related work.
* User study results are somewhat ambiguous.




**Recommendation:**

**Weak Accept**.  I believe the paper presents a worthwhile contribution, but its impact is somewhat limited by the lack of strong quantitative results.  I recommend acceptance with the following requests:

* **Strengthen Quantitative Evaluation:**  Explore alternative metrics that better capture the quality of pose transfer and overall image realism. Consider metrics beyond SSIM and IS.
* **Expand Related Work Comparison:**  Discuss how this work relates to recent advances in GAN-based image editing and manipulation.
* **Analyze Failure Cases:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the approach and the types of scenarios where it struggles.
* **Further Analyze User Study Data:**  Investigate the reasons for the lower score on Market-1501 in the user study and clarify the implications of the results.
