- Proposes a novel two-stage framework for pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Stage-I integrates pose information with the reference image to produce a coarse result using pose heatmap embedding and a pose mask loss to focus on the human body.  
- Stage-II refines the coarse result with a GAN that generates a difference map between the coarse result and the target image to accelerate training.  
- Introduces pose mask loss to reduce background interference; ablation studies show it outperforms standard L1 loss.  
- Uses pose heatmap embedding (instead of coordinates) as an effective pose representation.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with SSIM, IS, qualitative results, and a user study, outperforming one-stage baselines and Zhao et al. [36].  
- Technically sound with well-justified design choices and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Clear, well-organized writing and effective figures that illustrate contributions and comparisons.  
- Novel contributions: task formulation, two-stage pipeline, pose heatmap and pose mask loss, and difference maps in Stage-II.  
- Significant practical applications (film, pose estimation, fashion) and improved photo-realism and pose consistency.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations such as background transfer issues and struggles with rare poses.  
- Minor weakness: significance could be strengthened with more concrete real-world use cases and integration guidance.  
- Minor weakness: lacks detail on how the pose mask is generated and which morphological operations are used.  
- Minor weakness: should more explicitly differentiate this two-stage design from other two-stage generation works (e.g., [9, 35]).  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper that meets NIPS standards; recommendation to accept.
