Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes PG2, a two-stage conditional generative framework for pose-guided person image synthesis: given a reference image of a person and a target pose (encoded as keypoint heatmaps), the goal is to synthesize the same person in the target pose. Stage-I uses a U-Net-like encoder-decoder with skip connections and a fully-connected bottleneck to integrate the condition image with pose heatmaps, trained with a masked L1 loss to de-emphasize background differences. Stage-II refines the Stage-I output via a conditional GAN that predicts a residual “difference map” (i.e., residual learning) to sharpen details, regularized with the same masked L1. The discriminator takes paired inputs (condition image, generated/real image), encouraging pose-consistent realism. Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include ablations on pose encodings, loss functions, and one- vs two-stage training, plus a user study and a comparison to VariGAN.

This work sits within conditional generation and image-to-image translation (CVAE/CVGAN, pix2pix, StackGAN, Lassner et al. for people in clothing, Zhao et al. multi-view clothing). Using pose as explicit conditioning via keypoint heatmaps aligns with contemporaneous lines that add structural cues to drive generation. The two-stage, residual refinement is reminiscent of stacked/iterative refinement (e.g., StackGAN, SR methods), but specialized to pose-guided person synthesis.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation with practical value (content manipulation, data augmentation).
- Sensible architecture with a clean decomposition: Stage-I for global structure and Stage-II for detail, plus residual learning for faster convergence.
- Effective and simple pose encoding (stacked heatmaps) with empirical support over coordinate-based alternatives.
- Masked L1 addresses background mismatch and focuses learning on people pixels; ablations show benefit.
- Pairwise conditional discriminator incentivizes pose-consistent realism (consistent with best practices in conditional GANs).
- Comprehensive ablations (pose embeddings; loss; one- vs two-stage) on two datasets; user study; some failure analysis.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality
- Baselines are limited. The closest strong baseline is pix2pix-style conditional GAN with pose heatmaps and PatchGAN discriminators; the “G1+D” resembles this but architectural/discriminator details differ. A direct, well-tuned pix2pix comparison (e.g., PatchGAN, feature matching/VGG perceptual loss) would strengthen claims.
- Quantitative metrics: SSIM/IS (and masked variants) are imperfect for this task; no FID is reported, and no task-relevant metrics are used. For identity preservation, re-identification feature similarity (e.g., cosine in a re-ID embedding) should be reported; for pose accuracy, apply an off-the-shelf pose estimator on generated images and compare to target poses.
- The user study is very constrained (1s exposure, small sample). A pairwise A/B preference test with sufficient trials would be more informative.
- No analysis of robustness to pose detector noise/failures; since keypoints are estimated, this is important.
- The approach is deterministic; uncertainty in occluded garment regions is not modeled. Multi-modality (e.g., diverse plausible completions) is unaddressed.

Clarity
- Overall well written, but some architectural specifics are deferred to supplementary (e.g., exact filter counts, receptive fields, λ sweeps). A concise architectural summary in main text would aid reproducibility.
- The construction of the foreground mask via morphological ops is under-specified; reporting mask IoU or showing sensitivity to mask quality would help.

Originality
- Using heatmaps to inject pose and a two-stage residual refinement are reasonable but incremental; the pairwise conditional discriminator is standard in conditional GANs. The masked L1 is a minor but practical tweak. The novelty lies more in the specific task instantiation and the combination than in a fundamentally new learning principle.

Significance
- The task is useful and timely; the results are visually compelling on DeepFashion. However, the broader impact would be clearer with downstream utility (e.g., does augmentation improve pose estimation or re-ID?) and with stronger baselines/metrics. The Market-1501 results are weaker, highlighting challenges in cluttered settings.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add strong baselines: pix2pix with pose heatmaps and PatchGAN, perceptual/feature-matching losses; multi-scale discriminators; SPADE/attention-based variants if contemporaneous.
- Report FID (global and masked/cropped to person), re-ID similarity preservation, and pose accuracy on generated images. Include a more thorough user study (A/B preference without time limit).
- Analyze robustness to noisy/missing keypoints and to mask quality; possibly predict/refine masks jointly (e.g., with a segmentation branch or attention).
- Consider explicit warping (e.g., learned flow, deformable skip connections) from condition image to target pose before refinement to better preserve textures.
- Explore stochasticity for ambiguous completions (e.g., noise injection, conditional VAEs).
- Ablate the residual learning choice (difference map) vs direct generation under identical losses to isolate its contribution.
- Discuss ethics and potential misuse of person manipulation and consider safeguards or watermarking.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses a clear, relevant problem with a practical, well-motivated design.
- Solid qualitative results and ablations; two-stage residual GAN is effective.
- Simple, reproducible components (pose heatmaps; masked L1) with demonstrated benefit.

Cons for acceptance
- Novelty is moderate; many components align with established conditional GAN practices.
- Evaluation lacks strong baselines and task-relevant metrics (FID, identity/pose preservation).
- Limited robustness analysis; weaker results on the harder dataset.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The paper presents a useful and well-executed approach to a relevant conditional generation problem, with clear empirical gains and practical insights (pose heatmaps, masked losses, residual refinement). To clear the bar decisively, the authors should strengthen baselines and evaluation (FID, identity/pose metrics) and provide robustness analyses.
