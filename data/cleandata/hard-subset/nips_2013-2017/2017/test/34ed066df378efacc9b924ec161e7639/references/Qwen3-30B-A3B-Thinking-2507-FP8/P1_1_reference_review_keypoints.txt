- Introduces the novel task of pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Proposes a two-stage PG2 architecture: Stage-I for coarse structure, Stage-II for adversarial refinement and details.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing improvements over single-stage baselines and prior work [36].  
- Method is technically sound and well-motivated; two-stage design addresses simultaneous pose and appearance generation challenges.  
- Ablation studies support design choices (heatmap pose embedding vs. coordinates, pose mask loss vs. standard L1).  
- Comprehensive evaluation includes quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score), qualitative results, and an AMT user study.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, including failure cases for rare poses and gender imbalance in training data.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and supported by helpful diagrams and visualizations.  
- The core contribution and problem formulation are novel and distinct from related image-to-image or pose estimation work.  
- Pose mask loss and two-stage architecture are significant conceptual advances for complex image generation.  
- Work has practical significance for movie making, pose estimation training data, and image retrieval.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and novel task focus.  
- Strength: effective two-stage architecture that separates global pose from fine appearance.  
- Strength: pose mask loss that improves body-focused generation.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation across datasets and metrics.  
- Strength: well-written with clear explanations and visuals.  
- Weakness: limited quantitative comparison with Zhao et al. [36] (mostly qualitative).  
- Weakness: claim of manipulating “any object” is unsupported—only person results shown.  
- Weakness: insufficient details about the pose estimator for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: failure analysis is limited and could be expanded.  
- Specific: 18-heatmap pose embedding is superior to coordinate embedding and avoids mapping errors.  
- Specific: pose mask loss demonstrably reduces background artifacts compared to L1 loss.  
- Specific: AMT user study useful but lacks detail on rating criteria for realism.  
- Specific: need discussion of how pose estimator quality affects final generation results.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, technically sound, novel, and convincing—recommended for acceptance.  
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative comparison with Zhao et al. [36]; provide pose estimator details; clarify scope beyond persons; expand failure analysis.
