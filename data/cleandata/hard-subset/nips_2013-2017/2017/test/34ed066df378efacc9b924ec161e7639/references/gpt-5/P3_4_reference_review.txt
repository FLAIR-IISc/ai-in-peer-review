Summary
The paper addresses conditional person image synthesis: given a reference image IA and a target pose PB (18 keypoints from a pose estimator), generate an image IB in the target pose while preserving identity/appearance. The proposed PG2 framework is two-stage: (i) Stage-I integrates IA and PB via a U-Net-like encoder-decoder with skip connections and a fully connected bottleneck, trained with a masked L1 loss that upweights foreground (pose-derived mask), producing a coarse result ÎB1; (ii) Stage-II refines details using a conditional GAN that predicts a residual “difference map” G2(IA, ÎB1) to bridge ÎB1 to IB, trained with an adversarial loss and the same masked L1. The discriminator operates on pairs (I, IA), encouraging coherence with the reference. The paper explores different pose embeddings, compares L1 vs pose-mask loss, and one-stage vs two-stage training. Results on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) are shown, with SSIM/IS and masked variants, plus a user study. A qualitative comparison with VariGAN [36] is included.

Strengths
- Timely, well-motivated task: explicit control via pose, with clear applications (editing, data augmentation, re-ID training).
- Solid engineering: two-stage “coarse-to-fine” design is sensible; residual U-Nets with skip connections; pairwise conditional discriminator; predicting residuals to ease refinement.
- Useful ablations: comparison of pose embeddings (coordinate vs heatmaps), loss variants (L1 vs pose-masked L1), and training strategies (one-stage vs two-stage).
- Practical masked loss to mitigate background mismatch across cameras. The discriminator pairing with IA is a good design choice to avoid trivial copying.
- Qualitative results on two datasets are convincing; user study supports perceived realism on DeepFashion.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality
- Evaluation metrics: SSIM and Inception Score are known to correlate poorly with perceptual realism, especially for person images and low-resolution surveillance data. Masked SSIM/IS helps, but IS on masked person crops is difficult to interpret (classifier is not trained on person categories and masking alters statistics). More appropriate metrics (e.g., human studies with pairwise comparisons, FID on person crops, LPIPS perceptual distance, identity preservation scores using a re-ID or face/attribute network) are missing.
- Identity/appearance preservation is central to the task but not quantitatively assessed. A re-ID retrieval metric (rank-1/mAP) or feature distance between generated and true images using a re-ID backbone would substantiate claims.
- The “difference map” claim of faster convergence is plausible but not empirically substantiated (no training curves, time-to-quality plots).
- Stage-II does not explicitly condition on PB (only via ÎB1), and the discriminator also does not see PB. While ÎB1 likely anchors pose, showing that Stage-II does not drift from pose (e.g., angular/endpoint error of keypoints on outputs) would strengthen the argument.
- The masked L1 uses a pose-derived foreground mask constructed from target keypoints and morphology. This introduces supervision from target during training; that’s acceptable, but details (connectivity, dilation parameters) are sparse, and the effect of mask quality/noise is not analyzed.
- The fully connected bottleneck in G1 introduces many parameters and loses spatial equivariance; ablations without the FC layer (or with attention/long-range skip) would clarify its necessity, especially since it is removed in G2.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear and well organized, but some important hyperparameters are missing: the exact λ for masked L1 at Stage-II, mask construction details, and the relative weights if any between GAN and L1 at Stage-I (GAN is not used at Stage-I, but clarify).
- Figures are informative but some captions and cross-references are terse; reporting failure modes is appreciated.
- The user study protocol is described, but more detail on randomization, per-user variance, and inter-rater agreement would help.

Originality
- Conditioning on both a reference image and explicit pose heatmaps is a meaningful and more expressive control signal than view labels; related ideas exist in image-to-image translation and keypoint-conditioned generation, but the specific two-stage residual-GAN setup with pose-masked loss is a reasonable and practical combination. The comparison to one-stage pix2pix-style training is appropriate.
- Closely related contemporaneous works (e.g., image warping/flow-based pose transfer) are not discussed; while not required, a brief positioning versus warping-based or attention/flow models would clarify novelty.

Significance
- The task has clear downstream impact (editing, data synthesis for pose/re-ID). The qualitative gains over a strong one-stage baseline are meaningful, especially in high-resolution settings. However, the lack of identity-preservation metrics and stronger perceptual evaluation limits the ability to assess true utility for re-ID or editing pipelines.
- The method is deterministic and single-modal; under occlusion or ambiguity, diverse outputs may be preferred. Discussion of multimodality (CVAE/GAN, InfoGAN) could broaden impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add identity preservation evaluation using a pre-trained re-ID network (feature distance between generated and target; or retrieval performance when augmenting training with generated images).
- Replace or complement IS with FID on masked person crops and LPIPS perceptual distance; report keypoint error (pose accuracy) on generated images to ensure Stage-II maintains pose.
- Include training curves/time-to-reach-SSIM to support the “faster convergence” claim of residual refinement.
- Consider conditioning D (and possibly G2) on PB as well as IA to more directly enforce pose fidelity in refinement.
- Explore a perceptual (VGG) loss focused on clothing/body regions to improve texture and reduce blurring without overemphasizing background.
- Discuss/try feature warping from IA to PB (e.g., pose-guided deformable conv/flow) to better preserve high-frequency textures, especially on Market-1501.
- Provide precise details of the pose mask construction and the λ values; release code or pseudo-code to aid reproducibility.

Minor comments
- Clarify whether any post-processing or test-time augmentation is used.
- Some references are incomplete; please provide full bibliographic entries.
- Small typos and formatting glitches (equation spacing, footnote markers) should be fixed.

Overall assessment
Pros: clear problem and motivation; pragmatic two-stage design; sensible discriminator pairing; good ablations; strong qualitative results; useful masked loss.

Cons: weak quantitative evaluation for identity and perceptual quality; limited comparison with alternative architectures; some missing hyperparameters and training evidence; Stage-II/Discriminator not explicitly pose-conditioned.

I lean toward acceptance if identity-preserving evaluation and stronger metrics can be added; otherwise borderline, as the core ideas are solid and influential, but the current experimental evidence falls short of fully supporting the claimed benefits. Confidence: high.
