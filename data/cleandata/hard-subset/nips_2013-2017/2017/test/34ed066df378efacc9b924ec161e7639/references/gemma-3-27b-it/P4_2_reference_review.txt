This paper proposes a two-stage generative model for pose-guided person image generation. The core idea is to first generate a coarse image respecting the target pose, and then refine it with a GAN to add details. The approach addresses a challenging problem and leverages established techniques like U-Nets and GANs in a novel combination.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly defines the task of pose-guided person image generation and its potential applications.
*   **Well-Motivated Approach:** The divide-and-conquer strategy is logically sound, acknowledging the difficulty of end-to-end pose and appearance generation.
*   **Novelty:** Conditioning image generation on both a reference image *and* a specific pose is a relatively novel contribution, distinct from existing work on attribute-conditioned or single-view-to-view generation. The use of pose heatmaps is also a reasonable approach.
*   **Thorough Experiments:** The paper provides extensive experimental evaluation on two datasets, including ablation studies to justify design choices like the pose mask loss and the two-stage architecture. The inclusion of a user study adds further value.
*   **Good Baseline Comparison:** The authors compare against a recent related work [36].
*   **Detailed Explanation:** The paper provides a good explanation of the network architectures and training procedures.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Building Blocks:** The core components (U-Net, DCGAN) are well-established. The novelty lies in the combination and application to this specific task, rather than in fundamentally new architectural innovations.
*   **Quantitative Results Interpretation:** The quantitative results (SSIM, IS) are somewhat ambiguous. While the authors acknowledge limitations of these metrics, the correlation between numerical scores and visual quality isn't always clear, as noted in the paper itself.
*   **Failure Cases:** While failure cases are discussed, the analysis is somewhat limited. A more in-depth understanding of the failure modes could inform future improvements.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Pose Transfer Methods:** The paper focuses on generation from pose, but doesn't compare to existing pose *transfer* methods beyond image generation. A direct comparison to methods that manipulate pose in existing images (e.g., using image warping and inpainting) would strengthen the paper.
*   **Dataset Limitations:** The datasets used, while reasonable, have limitations (e.g. Market-1501’s surveillance-like images). The performance on more diverse and challenging datasets remains an open question.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a novel and challenging problem in image generation.
*   The proposed approach is well-motivated and demonstrates promising results through thorough experimentation.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
*   The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of different components.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The core technical contributions are incremental, relying on existing building blocks.
*   The quantitative evaluation is not fully convincing, and requires careful interpretation.
*   The comparison to related work is limited to one recent paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. While the technical innovations are not groundbreaking, the problem formulation, the proposed approach, and the thorough experimental evaluation make it a valuable addition to the NIPS workshop. The paper is well-written and clearly explained, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Recommendation:**

Accept. The paper’s strengths outweigh its weaknesses, and it represents a worthwhile contribution to the NIPS community. Minor revisions could be suggested to address the weaknesses identified above (e.g., providing more detailed analysis of failure cases, expanding the comparison to related work, and clarifying the interpretation of the quantitative results).
