This paper tackles the interesting and challenging problem of pose-guided person image generation. The core idea – conditioning generation on both a reference image *and* a target pose – is genuinely novel and holds significant potential for applications in areas like movie making and synthetic data generation. The proposed two-stage approach, dividing the problem into global structure and appearance refinement, is a reasonable and well-motivated strategy. The mask loss, aimed at focusing on the human body rather than background changes, is a clever addition. While the presentation isn’t flawless, and the validation isn’t exhaustive, the novelty and potential impact of the approach are strong enough to warrant acceptance, despite some shortcomings.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Conditioning image generation on both reference image and explicit pose information is a genuinely new contribution. This goes beyond existing work on attribute or text-conditioned generation, offering finer-grained control.
*   **Problem Formulation:** The task itself is well-defined and relevant to several practical applications.
*   **Divide-and-Conquer Strategy:** The two-stage approach makes the problem tractable and allows for targeted optimization at each stage.
*   **Mask Loss:** The pose mask loss is a practical and effective way to improve generation quality by mitigating background interference.
*   **Clear Structure:** The paper is reasonably well-organized and presents the method in a logical manner.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Ablation:** While the paper explores different pose embeddings and loss functions, the ablation studies could be more comprehensive. For example, a more thorough analysis of the λ parameter in Stage-II would be beneficial.
*   **Quantitative Evaluation:** While SSIM and IS are reported, they don't fully capture the perceptual quality of the generated images. The reliance on mask-SSIM highlights awareness of background issues but doesn't entirely resolve the limitations of the metric. The user study, while helpful, could be larger and more rigorously designed.
*   **Dataset Bias:** The datasets used are somewhat limited. While DeepFashion offers high-resolution images, the poses may be less diverse than those encountered in real-world scenarios. Market-1501 presents a challenging setting, but the images are lower resolution.
*   **Failure Cases:** The identified failure cases (incomplete body generation, inaccurate gender) suggest areas where the model struggles and warrant further investigation.
*   **Comparison:** While a comparison to Zhao et al. (2017) is included, it's somewhat limited and doesn’t fully highlight the advantages of the proposed approach. A more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art image manipulation techniques would strengthen the paper.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea is highly novel and addresses a significant challenge in image generation.
*   The two-stage approach and mask loss are well-motivated and contribute to improved results.
*   The qualitative results, particularly on DeepFashion, are promising and demonstrate the potential of the method.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it accessible to a broad audience.
*   The potential impact of this work on applications like movie making and synthetic data generation is substantial.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The quantitative evaluation is not entirely convincing, and the reliance on SSIM is problematic.
*   The ablation studies are somewhat limited.
*   The dataset selection could be more diverse.
*   The paper doesn’t fully address potential failure cases.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some limitations, I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of image generation. The novelty of the approach, combined with its potential impact, outweighs the weaknesses. The authors have identified a challenging problem and proposed a reasonable and effective solution. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest a more comprehensive ablation study, a more detailed discussion of the limitations, and exploration of more robust evaluation metrics. Addressing the identified failure cases would also strengthen the paper.
