

# Peer Review: Pose Guided Person Image Generation

## Summary

This paper proposes a novel two-stage approach for person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. The authors identify that generating realistic person images with arbitrary pose changes is challenging for end-to-end models, as they must simultaneously generate correct pose and detailed appearance. Their solution divides the problem into two stages: Stage-I generates a coarse pose-aligned image using a U-Net-like architecture with a novel pose mask loss to focus on the human body rather than background; Stage-II refines the coarse result using a conditional GAN that generates a difference map between the coarse result and target image. The approach addresses a practical need for applications like movie making and pose estimation data generation, and demonstrates significant improvements over baselines on two person datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion).

The work relates to conditional GANs [21, 32], pose estimation [2], and person re-identification [37], but distinguishes itself by using explicit pose information (keypoints) as conditioning rather than abstract attributes or viewpoints. The paper clearly positions itself against related work, particularly [36], which uses "side" as a word vector rather than explicit pose.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Task Definition**: The paper identifies and formalizes a meaningful problem (person image generation conditioned on reference image + target pose) with clear applications, filling an important gap in the literature.

2. **Effective Two-Stage Approach**: The divide-and-conquer strategy is well-motivated and leads to significant quality improvements. The Stage-I focus on global structure and Stage-II focus on appearance details is a clean and practical solution.

3. **Pose Mask Loss**: The novel mask loss for Stage-I effectively addresses the background interference problem, as clearly demonstrated in the ablation studies (Figure 4). This is a valuable contribution to the field.

4. **Difference-Map GAN**: The Stage-II approach of generating a difference map rather than the full image is clever, leads to faster convergence, and produces higher quality results.

5. **Comprehensive Experiments**: The paper provides thorough evaluation across multiple dimensions: different pose embeddings, loss functions, two-stage vs. one-stage models, and user studies. The qualitative results (Figure 4) clearly demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The paper only compares with [36], which is the most directly related work, but doesn't thoroughly compare with other relevant GAN-based image generation approaches that might be applicable to this task.

2. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The paper lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the two-stage approach works better than end-to-end. The ablation studies show empirical evidence, but a more principled explanation would strengthen the contribution.

3. **Implementation Details**: Some implementation details (e.g., exact pose mask computation, network architecture specifics) are sparse, making full reproducibility slightly challenging without supplementary materials.

4. **Failure Analysis**: The failure cases (Figure 6) are interesting but not thoroughly analyzed. It's unclear how often these failures occur or if they're specific to certain datasets.

## Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound with thorough experiments. Claims are well-supported by quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) and qualitative results. The authors are honest about limitations (failure cases, dataset challenges).

**Clarity**: Well-written and well-organized. The two-stage approach is clearly explained with good visualizations. The method section provides sufficient detail for reproduction, though some implementation specifics could be enhanced.

**Originality**: The core idea of using explicit pose as conditioning for person image generation is novel. The two-stage approach with pose mask loss and difference-map GAN are also novel contributions. The paper clearly differentiates itself from related work.

**Significance**: The work addresses an important problem with practical applications. The results show clear improvements over baselines, and the approach could inspire further work in conditional image generation with explicit structure guidance. It advances the state-of-the-art in person image generation.

## Recommendation

This is a strong paper that makes a clear contribution to the field of conditional image generation. The two-stage approach with pose mask loss and difference-map GAN is well-motivated, well-executed, and shows significant improvements over baselines. While there are some limitations in the comparison with state-of-the-art and theoretical justification, these don't detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Pro arguments for acceptance**:
- Novel task definition with clear applications
- Effective two-stage approach with strong empirical results
- Well-structured paper with clear contribution
- Valuable technical contributions (pose mask loss, difference-map GAN)
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple dimensions

**Con arguments against acceptance**:
- Limited comparison with broader state-of-the-art
- Lack of theoretical justification for two-stage approach
- Some implementation details could be more complete

I recommend acceptance to NeurIPS. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and makes a meaningful contribution to the field. The approach could inspire further work in conditional image generation with explicit structure guidance, and the results demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods. The authors have provided a complete piece of work with thorough evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.
