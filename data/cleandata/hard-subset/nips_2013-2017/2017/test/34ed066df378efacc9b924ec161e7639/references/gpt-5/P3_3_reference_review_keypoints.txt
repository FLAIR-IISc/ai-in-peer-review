- Paper tackles pose-guided person image synthesis: generate a target-pose image from a reference image and 2D keypoints using a two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline (G1 U-Net-like coarse stage, G2 adversarial refinement).
- Stage-I (G1) produces a coarse image with a pose-weighted L1 loss (pose mask loss); Stage-II (G2) is a conditional GAN that refines via a claimed “difference map” and a pairwise discriminator conditioned on the reference image.
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 use SSIM/IS (and masked variants), ablations (pose embeddings, loss, one vs two stage), and a small AMT user study; qualitative comparison to VariGAN [36].
- Builds on prior work in conditional image generation, image-to-image translation, and coarse-to-fine GANs; novelty is application to pose transfer and practical design choices (masked L1, pairwise D).
- Strength: clear, practical problem formulation with useful applications like data augmentation and content creation.
- Strength: sensible two-stage design separating structure from detail improves stability and output quality.
- Strength: empirical study of pose embeddings shows spatial pose-heatmap concatenation is effective.
- Strength: pose mask loss is well-motivated for re-id datasets with varying backgrounds and matches masked evaluation metrics.
- Strength: pairwise discriminator conditioned on the reference image encourages identity preservation and reduces trivial solutions.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across two datasets with ablations and a user study; qualitative improvement over a relevant baseline.
- Weakness: Ambiguity about whether G2 predicts a residual “difference map” or the final image; losses and equations are inconsistent with the textual claim.
- Weakness: Discriminator is not conditioned on target pose PB, limiting its ability to enforce pose-consistency beyond the L1 term.
- Weakness: Pose embedding ablation may be unfair—HME baseline injects pose only at the bottleneck, disadvantaging it relative to early fusion heatmaps.
- Weakness: Use of a fully connected bottleneck for 256×256 images is unconventional, expensive, and may hurt translation equivariance without adequate justification or ablation.
- Weakness: Omission of batch normalization/dropout is unusual for GANs; no justification or stability analysis provided.
- Weakness: Pose mask creation via morphology is heuristic and may be sensitive to occlusions/missing joints; robustness analysis is missing.
- Experimental critique: One-stage baseline must ensure D is conditioned on both IA and PB for fair comparison; warping baselines should be included.
- Experimental critique: Metrics (IS/SSIM) are weak for conditional human synthesis; recommend FID, LPIPS, and identity-preservation (re-id) metrics or retrieval evaluation.
- Experimental critique: User study results (e.g., 14.9% fool rate) need variance, significance, and breakdown by pose difficulty/background complexity.
- Experimental critique: Background leakage on Market-1501 suggests need for explicit human parsing or compositing with target backgrounds.
- Reproducibility: Hyperparameter (λ) analysis relegated to supplement; a brief main-text summary and practitioner guidance would help reproducibility.
- Originality: Approach is an incremental but meaningful combination of known components tailored to pose-guided person synthesis.
- Comparison limitation: Qualitative comparison to [36] is not strictly comparable due to different conditioning (views vs keypoints); stronger quantitative/user-study comparisons needed.
- Suggestions: Clarify/ablate difference-map vs direct refinement and enforce residual learning if claimed.
- Suggestions: Condition D on PB, consider PatchGAN/multi-scale D, and add perceptual/feature-matching losses for texture fidelity.
- Suggestions: Use stronger metrics (FID, re-id embeddings), fairer pose-ablation protocols, and analyze sensitivity to pose noise/missing joints.
- Suggestions: Ablate/remove the fully connected bottleneck and explore fully convolutional alternatives (dilated convs) and normalization choices (instance norm).
- Overall pros: Clear problem, convincing two-stage pipeline, useful engineering insights, and identity-preserving discriminator design.
- Overall cons: Methodological ambiguities, missing discriminator pose conditioning, baseline/metric weaknesses, and limited robustness/identity analysis.
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on clarifications, stronger baselines/metrics, and improved experimental rigor.
