- Proposal: PG2 — a two-stage pose-guided person image synthesis: Stage-I U-Net-like encoder-decoder with masked L1; Stage-II conditional DCGAN predicting a residual “difference map” with a pairwise discriminator.
- Inputs/goal: Generate a target-pose image of the same person given a reference image and 18-keypoint pose heatmaps.
- Experiments: Evaluated on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) with ablations (pose embeddings, loss, single- vs two-stage), SSIM/IS (and masked variants), and an AMT user study.
- Main empirical finding: Two-stage method yields sharper, more realistic images qualitatively; quantitative gains (SSIM/IS) are modest; user study favors PG2 on DeepFashion.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on conditional image generation and pose/view transfer literature; borrows coarse-to-fine GAN ideas but contributes residual refinement and pairwise discriminator tailored to pose transfer.
- Strength: Well-motivated architecture splitting global structure and detail, plus effective difference-map Stage-II and comprehensive ablations supporting design choices.
- Strength: Pairwise discriminator conditioned on the reference helps avoid trivial copying; experiments on two datasets and a user study add credibility.
- Weakness: Missing strong baselines — no fully convolutional pix2pix-style PatchGAN comparison, so one-stage baseline may be disadvantaged.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation relies on SSIM/IS which poorly capture perceptual quality; lacks FID, LPIPS, and identity-preservation (re-ID) metrics.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of pose mask robustness and failure modes, especially under pose estimator errors and occlusions.
- Weakness: No quantitative ablation for Stage-II design choices (residual vs direct output) or removal of FC in Stage-II.
- Weakness: Background artifacts and reference-copying acknowledged but no explicit foreground/background decomposition or mitigation implemented.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized, but key hyperparameters and some implementation details are left to the supplement, hindering reproducibility.
- Originality: Moderate — components are established but their combination, residual Stage-II, and pairwise discriminator are practical task-specific innovations.
- Significance: High practical interest for data augmentation and content creation, though empirical claims would be stronger with better baselines and metrics.
- Pros: Clear two-stage design, effective heuristics (pose mask, residual refinement), convincing qualitative results, and sensible pairwise discriminator.
- Cons: Missing strong baselines and perceptual/identity metrics, background leakage, limited robustness analysis, and incomplete Stage-II ablations.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to λ in loss; comparison to fully conv one-stage PatchGAN; identity-preservation (re-ID) and FID/LPIPS results; runtime and model size/training stability.
- Suggestions: Add pix2pix-style baseline and Stage-II residual vs direct ablation; incorporate foreground segmentation/attention; report LPIPS/FID and re-ID metrics; analyze pose-estimator noise robustness.
- Recommendation: Accept (weak) — solid, task-driven contribution with convincing qualitative results but evaluation and baselines need strengthening.
- Reviewer confidence: High-medium.
