Summary
This paper proposes the Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), which synthesizes a person image at a specified target pose PB given a reference image IA of the same person. The method uses 2D keypoint heatmaps (18 channels) as an explicit, controllable pose representation. The model is trained in two stages: Stage-I (a U-Net-like encoder-decoder with residual blocks) integrates IA and PB to produce a coarse image capturing the global human structure; a pose mask–weighted L1 loss emphasizes the human foreground to discount background mismatches. Stage-II refines appearance details via a conditional GAN that predicts a difference map between the Stage-I output and the target, paired with a discriminator that operates on (generated, condition) vs (real, condition) image pairs; a masked L1 term stabilizes training. Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include ablations on pose embedding, loss choices, and one- vs two-stage training, plus a user study and a comparison to VariGAN.

Relation to prior work: The paper sits at the intersection of conditional image generation and view/pose manipulation. It builds on cGANs (e.g., pix2pix), two-stage generation (LapGAN/StackGAN), and recent view-synthesis works for faces and clothing. Unlike prior view-conditioned approaches with coarse viewpoint labels or text, this work conditions on explicit keypoint heatmaps, providing fine-grained control. The two-stage refinement and difference-map prediction echo ideas from stacked/laplacian GANs and residual learning, but are adapted to the pose-conditioned person synthesis setting.

Strengths
- Clear, compelling problem formulation with explicit control via pose. This is useful for content creation and data augmentation and extends beyond category-/text-conditioned generation.
- Simple, well-motivated architecture that decomposes structure and detail, which empirically stabilizes training and improves perceptual quality.
- Practical innovations: pose mask–weighted L1 to downweight backgrounds; predicting a difference map in Stage-II; pairwise discriminator inputs to enforce consistency with IA.
- Thorough ablation on pose embeddings (coordinates vs heatmaps), loss design (L1 vs masked L1), and one- vs two-stage training; the ablations support the design choices.
- Results on both high- and low-resolution datasets, with qualitative improvements and AMT user study showing nontrivial fooling rates, and a comparison to the most related prior work.

Weaknesses
- Component-level novelty is incremental: U-Net, cGAN, two-stage refinement, and mask-weighted losses are well-known; the contribution lies mostly in their combination for this task.
- Evaluation metrics are limited: SSIM/IS (even with masking) do not align with perceptual quality; no identity preservation metric (e.g., re-id/face embedding similarity), no pose accuracy metric (e.g., running a pose estimator on generated images vs PB), and no diversity/stochasticity analysis.
- Baselines could be stronger: a pix2pix-style single-stage cGAN with IA+PB heatmaps and perceptual/feature matching loss would be a more competitive baseline; FID or human rater studies stratified by pose difficulty would strengthen claims.
- The model struggles on Market-1501; backgrounds leak or artifacts appear, and there is no mechanism to model uncertainty or multi-modality (only a single deterministic output).
- Clarity/organization: some writing issues (typos, broken references/figures) impede reproducibility; architectural details are described but could be more precise (exact channels, skip-connection specifics).

Quality
Technically sound overall, with sensible design and training. The ablation studies are appropriate, and the two-stage approach is validated qualitatively. However, the empirical evaluation would benefit from stronger, task-relevant metrics (identity, pose consistency) and baselines, and from reporting computational cost.

Clarity
Generally understandable, but the manuscript needs polishing (typos, incomplete citations, figure references). Include more precise architecture tables, loss weights (e.g., λ) sensitivity, and training/inference runtimes. Clarify mask construction details and whether any test-time priors are required beyond PB.

Originality
The explicit pose-conditioned person generation task is novel and valuable. The specific architectural choices are mostly known components; the novelty is in their integration and the difference-map refinement for this setting.

Significance
High practical significance: explicit pose control opens up new applications and datasets for controllable generation. The approach should be of interest to the community and can serve as a baseline for subsequent pose-guided generation work.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Report identity preservation using a re-id or face embedding distance and pose accuracy via a pose estimator on generated images.
- Compare against a strong pix2pix baseline with IA+PB inputs and perceptual/feature matching loss; include FID.
- Discuss and, if possible, enable stochastic outputs (e.g., noise injection, dropout) to capture multimodality for ambiguous regions.
- Analyze generalization to rare/extreme poses and occlusions; quantify failure modes by pose difficulty.
- Provide code/models and details to reproduce the exact architectures and training schedules.

Pros for acceptance
- Introduces a compelling, controllable generation task with explicit pose.
- Simple, well-executed two-stage pipeline with demonstrable qualitative gains.
- Solid ablations and user study; clear path for follow-up work.

Cons against acceptance
- Limited methodological novelty per component; evaluation misses key task-relevant metrics and stronger baselines.
- Not robust on challenging settings; some artifacts and background issues persist.
- Writing quality needs improvement.

Recommendation
Accept (7/10). Despite incremental components, the explicit pose-conditioned person generation task and the two-stage design constitute a meaningful and novel direction with clear impact. I am willing to overlook the evaluation gaps given the new setting and solid qualitative evidence.
