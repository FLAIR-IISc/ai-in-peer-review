- Paper proposes PG2: two-stage pose-guided person image generation (U-Net Stage-I coarse image with masked L1, Stage-II conditional DCGAN predicting residual difference map).
- Uses 18-channel keypoint heatmaps for pose conditioning and pairwise discriminator conditioning on (IA, ·).
- Strength: well-motivated task with intuitive keypoint control and clear applications.
- Strength: two-stage “structure-then-detail” decomposition is reasonable and empirically effective.
- Strength: residual Stage-II design stabilizes training and speeds convergence in practice.
- Strength: masked L1 loss appropriately reduces background confounds when backgrounds differ.
- Strength: useful ablations on pose encoding, mask loss, and one- vs two-stage training; clear implementation/dataset descriptions.
- Weakness: missing stronger baselines (e.g., pix2pix-style cGAN with U-Net + PatchGAN and perceptual loss) and single-stage alternatives.
- Weakness: evaluation relies on SSIM/IS which poorly correlate with perceptual realism; mask-SSIM/IS may be biased and insufficient.
- Weakness: AMT study is small and uses a strict 1s exposure; more robust human evaluations are needed.
- Weakness: no objective pose-accuracy metric (e.g., PCK/OKS) to quantify pose matching.
- Weakness: identity preservation is unmeasured; no re-ID embedding or downstream utility experiments.
- Weakness: Stage-II ignores target pose input; potential pose drift not analyzed.
- Weakness: background artifacts and copying are not addressed with segmentation or compositing approaches.
- Weakness: sensitivity to pose-estimation noise is unanalyzed; no experiments with noisy or ground-truth keypoints.
- Weakness: some design choices (removal of BN/dropout, masking weight (1+MB), convergence claims) lack justification or quantification.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—exact architectures, hyperparameter selection, runtime/memory, and code release not fully provided.
- Quality: method is technically sound and ablations support key claims, but evaluation lacks stronger baselines and task-relevant metrics.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; some notational/weight choices and discriminator details need clarification.
- Originality: components are known but their combination for explicit pose-conditioned person generation is novel.
- Significance: important problem with strong qualitative results, but empirical claims weakened by limited evaluations.
- Suggestions: add pix2pix/PatchGAN baseline, pose-accuracy (PCK) and identity metrics, re-ID augmentation tests, ablations for G2 input and convergence, keypoint-noise analysis, discriminator/λ details, background handling, and runtime/code release.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending stronger baselines and pose/identity metrics; confidence high.
