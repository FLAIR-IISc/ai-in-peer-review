- Introduces pose-guided person image generation using a two-stage framework conditioning on a reference image and target pose.  
- Stage-I: integrate reference image and keypoint heatmaps to generate a coarse global-structure image with a novel pose mask loss.  
- Stage-II: refine the coarse result with a conditional GAN that generates a difference map for sharper details.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with extensive qualitative and quantitative comparisons.  
- Strength: technically sound two-stage design effectively handles global structure and fine details.  
- Strength: comprehensive ablation studies and AMT user study support design choices; authors acknowledge limitations.  
- Strength: task novelty and meaningful contributions (pose mask loss, two-stage for pose transfer) with proper related-work distinctions.  
- Strength: results show clear improvements over existing methods and potential applicability to related tasks.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and provides figures and details supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: two-stage strategy is common in image generation and not sufficiently differentiated from similar prior approaches.  
- Weakness: comparison with most related work (Zhao et al. [36]) is primarily qualitative; lacks quantitative metrics.  
- Weakness: no analysis of how pose estimation errors affect final generation quality.  
- Weakness: failure cases are shown but not systematically analyzed for causes.  
- Weakness: paper omits discussion of computational requirements and training time.  
- Overall: solid contribution with strong empirical evidence; recommended for acceptance but should add quantitative comparison with [36] and discuss pose-estimation impacts.
