- Tackles pose-guided person image generation with a two-stage PG2: a U-Net-like encoder–decoder (Stage I) and a residual-refinement conditional DCGAN (Stage II).
- Conditions on input image plus 18-channel pose heatmaps and uses masked L1 loss focused on the foreground to emphasize the person over background.
- Stage II predicts a difference map with a pairwise discriminator to sharpen high-frequency details and mixes adversarial and masked L1 losses.
- Ablations show heatmap-based pose encoding outperforms coordinate embeddings, masked L1 helps, and two-stage training improves results over one-stage.
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include SSIM/IS (and masked variants), AMT user studies, and qualitative comparison to VariGAN.
- Architecture and losses are technically sound and align with established conditional generative modeling practice.
- Empirical support is convincing qualitatively and via ablations; AMT supports improved realism on DeepFashion.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: relies on SSIM/IS without perceptual (LPIPS) or distributional (FID) metrics.
- Identity preservation is not quantitatively measured (e.g., re-ID feature similarity), despite being central to the task.
- Baselines are limited; missing comparisons to standard image-to-image methods like pix2pix conditioned on pose heatmaps.
- Claim that difference maps speed convergence is not empirically demonstrated.
- Method depends on pretrained pose estimators and uses target-derived masks only during training; generalization to unconstrained images is unexplored.
- Model is deterministic and does not address diversity under pose ambiguity.
- Paper is generally clear and reproducible, but some implementation details (mask construction, FC layer rationale) need clarification and references are poorly formatted.
- Originality is incremental: components are established but their combination (pose heatmaps + foreground-weighted loss + staged residual GAN) is a useful, nontrivial synthesis.
- Significance is practical and likely to influence follow-up work, though methodological novelty is limited and evaluation gaps temper claims.
- Pros: well-motivated problem, effective staged design with informative ablations, sensible GAN refinement, and thorough experimental details.
- Cons: limited quantitative rigor and baselines, incremental novelty, background artifacts on Market-1501, and no demonstration of downstream utility.
- Overall: solid, well-executed application paper that advances controllable person synthesis; leaning toward acceptance with recommended stronger evaluations and clarifications.
