This paper proposes a two-stage generative model, PG2, for pose-guided person image generation. The core idea is to first generate a coarse image respecting the target pose and then refine it with a GAN to add details. The paper introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the person’s body and demonstrates results on both low- and high-resolution datasets.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, approach, and experimental setup. It relates to a growing body of work in image generation, specifically GANs and conditional generation. Existing work in conditional image generation (e.g., [18, 24, 32]) is properly discussed, and the authors distinguish their approach by explicitly using pose information rather than relying on abstract visual cues. The connection to related work in view synthesis [34, 36] is also appropriately made.  The two-stage approach is justified as a way to tackle the difficulty of generating both pose and appearance simultaneously, drawing parallels to existing divide-and-conquer strategies in other areas [35, 9, 3, 19].

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The task of explicit pose-guided person image generation is relatively novel, especially with the specific formulation of conditioning on both a reference image and a pose.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed architecture is logical and builds upon well-established techniques like U-Net and DCGAN. The inclusion of a pose mask loss is a sensible way to address background influence.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and organized. The figure illustrating the framework (Figure 2) is particularly helpful.
*   **Experimental Validation:** Experiments are performed on two datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in different settings. The ablation studies comparing different pose embeddings and loss functions are valuable. The user study provides additional support.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The paper provides detailed analysis of the components within the network architecture, explaining design choices and parameter settings.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Comparison:** While the authors compare with [36], this is a single related work.  There is scope for comparison with more recent and relevant methods, especially those focusing on controllable image generation or human pose manipulation. Specifically, techniques that directly manipulate latent spaces of GANs for pose control (even if not specifically person image generation) would be good comparisons.
*   **Qualitative Results:** The qualitative results (Figure 4) are somewhat difficult to interpret, and the improvement from the two-stage model isn’t always dramatically visible.  A more careful selection of examples that highlight the benefits of the full model would be beneficial.
*   **User Study Interpretation:**  The discussion of the user study results in Section 4.4 is somewhat contradictory. The authors mention lower G2R scores on Market-1501 due to more complex backgrounds, then attribute a *lower* R2G score to artifacts introduced by the refinement stage. This warrants further clarification.
*   **Failure Cases:** While failure cases are acknowledged, they are not deeply analyzed.  For example, the paper briefly mentions dataset imbalance for gender, but doesn't explore potential mitigation strategies.
*   **SSIM Limitation:** The paper acknowledges the limitations of SSIM for perceptual quality, and proposes mask-SSIM, but the analysis still heavily relies on this metric.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a novel and interesting problem in image generation.
*   The proposed approach is technically sound and well-motivated.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The comparison with related work is limited.
*   The qualitative results could be improved to better highlight the benefits of the approach.
*   The user study interpretation requires further clarification.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of image generation. While the comparison to related work could be strengthened and some aspects of the evaluation could be improved, the novelty, technical soundness, and clarity of the paper make it worthy of acceptance.
