- Overall: Novel pose-guided person image generation problem with a reasonable two-stage U-Net/DCGAN approach, mask loss and difference-map strategy; weak accept pending major revisions.  
- Problem & approach: Task framed as generating person images conditioned on reference image and target pose using a divide-and-conquer Stage-I (structure) and Stage-II (detail) pipeline.  
- Positioning: Paper situates itself among VAEs/GANs/autoregressive models but the related-work discussion is somewhat cursory, especially on pose vs. viewpoint expressiveness.  
- Strength: Novel, well-defined and impactful problem formulation.  
- Strength: Logical two-stage decomposition separating global structure from appearance refinement.  
- Strength: Masked loss effectively focuses learning on person regions and reduces background influence.  
- Strength: Difference-map training for Stage-II improves training stability and efficiency.  
- Strength: Qualitative results show plausible generated images; experiments run on two datasets.  
- Weakness: Insufficient ablation studies (e.g., effect of Stage-II lambda, network architecture variants).  
- Weakness: Superficial quantitative and conceptual comparison to related work (notably [36]).  
- Weakness: Overly optimistic language that overstates claims relative to empirical support.  
- Weakness: Limited and unsystematic failure-case analysis; lacks insight into causes of failures.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on imperfect metrics (SSIM, IS) and a noted mismatch between metrics and user study.  
- Weakness: Dependence on an external pose estimator, with no exploration of estimator uncertainty or mitigation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept with major revisions—required: more comprehensive ablations, deeper comparative experiments (quantitative where possible), toned-down claims, expanded failure analysis, and exploration of pose-estimation uncertainty.
