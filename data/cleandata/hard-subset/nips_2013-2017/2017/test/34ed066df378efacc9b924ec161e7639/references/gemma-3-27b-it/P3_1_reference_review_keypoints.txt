- Overall score: 7/10 (Strong Accept with Minor Revisions).
- Proposes pose-guided person image generation using a two-stage pipeline: Stage-I U-Net for coarse pose integration, Stage-II conditional DCGAN for refinement.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background influence.
- Uses keypoint-based pose conditioning for more precise control than viewpoint/attribute-based methods.
- Qualitative results (Fig.4) are compelling and quantitative results (Table 1) show improvements.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains methodology and experiments effectively.
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art image manipulation and latent-manipulation techniques.
- Dataset limitations: Market-1501 has noisy poses; DeepFashion may not represent full real-world pose diversity.
- Reported failure cases include inaccuracies on complex poses and gender imbalances; analysis is limited.
- SSIM and Inception Score have known limitations; more robust metrics and focused human evaluation are needed.
- Ablation studies exist but lack granular analysis of hyperparameters (e.g., Stage-II loss weight λ).
- Originality: novel application of keypoint conditioning and two-stage approach despite using established components.
- Significance: moderate-to-high with applications in virtual try-on, filmmaking, and data generation for pose estimation.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a novel problem, effective pipeline, strong results, clear writing, and useful pose mask loss.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons, dataset issues, insufficient failure-mode analysis, and metric concerns.
- Questions for authors: computational cost vs SOTA; sensitivity to external pose estimator and possibility of integrating pose estimation; remedies for complex-pose/gender failures; plan to release code/models.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add broader comparisons and deeper failure-case analysis.
