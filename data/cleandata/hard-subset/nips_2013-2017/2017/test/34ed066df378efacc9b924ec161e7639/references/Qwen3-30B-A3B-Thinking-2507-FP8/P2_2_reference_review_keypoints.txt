- Proposes a two-stage approach for pose-guided person image generation: Stage-I produces a coarse pose-aligned image, Stage-II refines via a conditional GAN that predicts a difference map.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background influence.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion using SSIM, Inception Score, and user studies.  
- Technically sound with thorough implementation details and reproducible setup.  
- Strong ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Acknowledges limitations: cannot generate new backgrounds and struggles with rare poses or gender imbalances.  
- Writing and organization are clear; method and figures are generally well explained.  
- Some figures (notably Figure 4) need more detailed captions for clarity.  
- Novel application of pose-conditioned generation; pose mask loss is a meaningful contribution.  
- Overall novelty is incremental, building on existing two-stage GAN and conditional generation techniques.  
- Results show clear improvements over one-stage baselines and related work [36], supported by user study.  
- Significance is practical for film, pose estimation, and fashion but limited to person image generation.  
- Strengths: clear problem, well-motivated two-stage design, pose mask loss, comprehensive evaluation, good ablations.  
- Weaknesses: incremental novelty, limited comparisons to recent conditional GAN advances, incomplete exploration of extreme poses.  
- Recommendation: accept, with a suggestion to better compare with recent GAN methods (e.g., attention mechanisms, improved GAN training).
