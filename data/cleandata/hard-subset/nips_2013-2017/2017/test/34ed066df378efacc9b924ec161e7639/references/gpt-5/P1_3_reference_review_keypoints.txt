- Proposal: PG2 — a two-stage pose-guided person image generator (Stage-I U-Net-like coarse output with pose-mask L1; Stage-II conditional DCGAN predicting a residual difference map with a pairwise discriminator); pose as 18 keypoint heatmaps.  
- Experiments: evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 (256×256 / 128×64), with ablations on pose encoding, loss, and one- vs two-stage; used SSIM/IS and masked variants plus a small AMT user study.  
- Strength: tackles a meaningful task (controllable pose-conditioned person synthesis) with practical downstream uses.  
- Strength: sensible coarse-to-fine two-stage design; residual refinement stabilizes training and sharpens details.  
- Strength: heatmap pose encoding and pose mask are effective and supported by ablations.  
- Strength: pairwise conditional discriminator helps enforce consistency with the conditioning image; qualitative results and user study show improvements.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics are limited—SSIM/IS (even masked) are weak proxies; missing FID, LPIPS, and task-driven metrics.  
- Weakness: identity preservation is central but unquantified; no re-identification or feature-similarity metrics reported.  
- Weakness: baselines are weak—missing a direct pix2pix-style baseline with pose heatmaps, PatchGAN, and perceptual/feature-matching losses.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation of Stage-II (residual vs full-image, pairwise vs standard conditional discriminator, FC layer effect, λ sensitivity).  
- Weakness: background handling is ad hoc; pose-mask from keypoints may be brittle and leads to background-transfer artifacts.  
- Weakness: no analysis of robustness to pose-estimation errors or missing/occluded keypoints.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps (exact filter counts, mask construction details, training/inference speed) and some incomplete references.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-motivated; experiments qualitatively support claims but would benefit from stronger baselines and identity-aware metrics; user study lacks statistical analysis.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, but needs precise pose-mask construction, explicit Stage-II residual description, and architecture hyperparameters.  
- Originality: moderate — novel pose-conditioned framing and practical combo of heatmaps + mask + residual + pairwise discriminator, but builds on known coarse-to-fine/residual GAN ideas.  
- Significance: high practical significance for controllable human synthesis and data augmentation, though claims limited by evaluation/identity-measure gaps.  
- Questions for authors: exact pose-mask construction/dilation/connectivity and robustness to missing joints; whether residual is constrained or compared to full-image prediction; experiments with perceptual/feature-matching losses; sensitivity to pose noise; runtime and training time.  
- Suggestions: add re-id/LPIPS/FID (masked) evaluations, stronger pix2pix-like baseline, ablate pairwise discriminator and residual prediction, and consider learned person segmentation masks.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (confidence 0.7).
