- Proposes PG2: a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network—Stage I pose-integrated U-Net with pose mask loss, Stage II conditional GAN producing a difference map for refinement.
- Key contributions claimed: (1) pose-guided person image generation task, (2) pose embedding via 18-channel heatmaps and pose mask loss, (3) two-stage framework separating structure and detail.
- Strength: clear problem formulation and well-motivated methodology.
- Strength: two-stage design justified for separating pose correctness from appearance detail.
- Strength: pose mask loss effectively addresses background mismatch between reference and target images.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments with ablation studies on Market-1501 and DeepFashion.
- Strength: qualitative results and Amazon Mechanical Turk user study show visual improvements over baselines.
- Strength: 18-channel heatmap embedding outperforms coordinate-based pose conditioning in ablations.
- Strength: Stage-II difference map technique speeds training and improves detail generation.
- Weakness: insufficient novelty differentiation from existing conditional image generation and pose-estimation literature.
- Weakness: limited quantitative comparisons to related work (e.g., only qualitative comparison to [36]) and missing comparisons to recent GAN/image-translation methods.
- Weakness: methodological details lacking for reproducibility (unspecified pose estimator, under-described pose mask construction and network hyperparameters).
- Weakness: significance is overstated; practical impact and integration into larger systems not convincingly demonstrated.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and handling of extreme poses, occlusions, and other challenging scenarios.
- Technical assessment: technically sound; pose mask loss and two-stage difference-map training are well-motivated and effective.
- Critique: claim of a "novel task" is overstated; novelty should be more precisely framed.
- Recommendation: recommend rejection for NIPS—contributions deemed incremental and insufficiently differentiated; better suited to application-focused venues like CVPR/ICCV.
- Pro points for acceptance summarized: clear statement/motivation, effective two-stage approach, strong experiments, useful pose mask loss, good qualitative outcomes.
- Con points for rejection summarized: inadequate differentiation from prior work, lacking quantitative comparisons, poor reproducibility details, overstated significance, limited discussion of limitations.
