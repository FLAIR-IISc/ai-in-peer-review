- Proposes a two-stage pose-guided person image generation framework: global U-Net with pose heatmaps then a GAN-based detail refinement.
- Uses pose heatmap embedding and a novel pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background interference.
- Evaluation on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with SSIM, Inception Score, user study, and ablation experiments.
- Two-stage design is well-motivated and addresses the trade-off between correct pose generation and appearance detail.
- Pose heatmaps are a practical pose representation that avoids expensive annotations.
- Pose mask loss is a notable technical contribution that mitigates background artifacts.
- Novelty claim is overstated relative to prior work (e.g., Zhao et al. [36]) and lacks clear differentiation and quantitative comparison.
- Relies on an external pose estimator but provides no analysis of how pose estimation errors affect final results.
- Market-1501 user study shows the two-stage model performing worse than a one-stage variant; the offered explanation (background transfer) is insufficient.
- Paper omits inference-time and computational cost analysis, limiting practical assessment and comparison with prior methods.
- Failure cases are shown but lack statistics and detailed attribution (pose estimation vs generation failure).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — clarify novelty vs related work, analyze Market-1501 discrepancy, report computational costs, and study pose-error impact.
