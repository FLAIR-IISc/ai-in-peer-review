- Proposes a novel two-stage pipeline for pose-guided person image generation: stage-I captures global structure with a U-Net variant and pose mask loss, stage-II refines details via a conditional GAN that predicts difference maps.
- Contributions: defining the pose-guided generation task, exploring pose integration methods with a novel pose mask loss, and introducing a two-stage framework for improved pose transfer.
- Uses difference maps in stage-II to stabilize training and speed convergence compared to generating full images.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with comparisons to prior work (e.g., Zhao et al. [36]).
- Quantitative evaluation includes SSIM, Inception Score, and an Amazon Mechanical Turk user study showing method effectiveness.
- Strength: technically sound method with thorough experiments and convincing quantitative and human-study results.
- Strength: well-written, clearly organized paper with detailed architecture/implementation and strong qualitative figures.
- Strength: pose mask loss is a simple, effective fix for background interference; two-stage design is well-motivated for separating pose and appearance.
- Originality: task and specific two-stage application are novel, though architecture borrows elements similar to prior work.
- Significance: meaningful applied contribution with applications in film, pose estimation, and data augmentation; preserves identity while changing pose.
- Weakness: inadequate analysis explaining why two-stage outperforms one-stage despite similar SSIM, and lacks theoretical justification for the design choice.
- Weakness: could better clarify why pose mask loss improves results and how it mitigates background interference.
- Honest evaluation notes failure cases like rare poses and gender imbalance in outputs.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed applied contribution that meets the conference quality bar but is not a fundamental theoretical advance.
- Recommendation: Accept.
