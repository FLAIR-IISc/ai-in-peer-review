- Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network for pose-conditioned person image synthesis.
- Stage I: coarse global structure generation using a U-Net variant with pose embedding and pose mask loss.
- Stage II: refinement via a conditional GAN that generates a difference map to add appearance details.
- Uses explicit pose conditioning with keypoint heatmaps rather than abstract attributes.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion and compared against baselines and prior work [36].
- Introduces pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and improve results.
- Two-stage design and difference map lead to faster convergence and better detail preservation.
- Comprehensive evaluation including SSIM, Inception Score, ablation studies, qualitative results, and a 30-user study.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow with helpful figures.
- Limited comparison to more recent image-to-image translation methods that may be stronger baselines.
- No analysis of computational cost or training time for the two-stage approach versus single-stage methods.
- Failure analysis is limited (rare poses, gender imbalance) and lacks deeper diagnosis or remedies.
- Reproducibility concerns: unspecified pose estimator and limited details on how pose estimation affects results.
- Quantitative metrics (SSIM) have limitations and the user study size may be insufficient for strong statistical claims.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Suggested improvements: compare to recent methods, report computational efficiency, detail pose estimator impact, and expand failure analysis.
