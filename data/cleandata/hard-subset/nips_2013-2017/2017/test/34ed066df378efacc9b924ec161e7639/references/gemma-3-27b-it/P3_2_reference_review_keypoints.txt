- Overall: Interesting, technically sound two-stage pose-guided person image generation paper; accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem & approach: Tackles pose-conditioned person image synthesis via a divide-and-conquer two-stage framework using U-Nets/DCGANs and a novel pose mask loss.  
- Positioning: Related work coverage is comprehensive; distinguishes itself by explicit keypoint-based pose guidance.  
- Strength — Motivation: The task is relevant and practically useful for applications like movie making and data augmentation.  
- Strength — Methodology: Two-stage design (global structure then detail refinement) is sensible and manageable.  
- Strength — Pose mask loss: Novel loss that focuses learning on the human body to reduce background artifacts.  
- Strength — Experiments: Strong qualitative results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, with supportive quantitative results and ablation studies.  
- Strength — Presentation: Clear writing, good organization, and helpful diagrams.  
- Weakness — Originality: Core components are incremental; main novelty is the pose mask loss and application.  
- Weakness — Quantitative evaluation: SSIM/IS have limitations; mask-SSIM/mask-IS help but more perceptual metrics (e.g., FID) are needed.  
- Weakness — Failure analysis: Some failure cases shown (missing limbs, gender errors) but lacks systematic analysis of failure modes.  
- Weakness — Comparisons: Limited comparisons to recent conditional generation methods beyond [36].  
- Suggestion — Pose embedding: Explain why heatmap embedding outperforms coordinate embedding.  
- Suggestion — Mask generation: Provide details and visuals of the morphological operations and parameters used to create pose masks.  
- Suggestion — Hyperparameters: Discuss sensitivity to key hyperparameters (e.g., λ in Stage-II loss).  
- Suggestion — User study: Analyze user-study responses to identify which artifacts reveal fakes and dataset-dependent differences.  
- Pro: Relevant problem, sound methodology, novel pose mask loss, multi-dataset validation, clear presentation.  
- Con: Incremental novelty, limited quantitative metrics and comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen quantitative evaluation and comparative/diagnostic analyses.
