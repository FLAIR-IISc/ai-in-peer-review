Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles pose-guided person image generation: given a reference image IA of a person and a target pose PB (18 keypoints), synthesize IB in the target pose while preserving appearance. The approach is two-stage (PG2). Stage-I (G1) is a U-Net-like encoder-decoder with skip connections and a fully connected (FC) bottleneck to encourage long-range interactions; it outputs a coarse image ÎB1 trained with an L1 loss reweighted by a pose mask MB (foreground gets double weight) to reduce background mismatch. Stage-II (G2) refines ÎB1 via a conditional GAN trained to predict a “difference map” added to ÎB1; the discriminator D operates on pairs (IA, I) to encourage identity-consistent realism. A masked L1 term regularizes adversarial training. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) show qualitative improvements over baselines; SSIM/IS and custom “mask-SSIM/IS” are reported; a user study is included. The paper relates to conditional VAEs/GANs, image-to-image translation, and view synthesis; it contrasts its explicit pose conditioning (keypoint heatmaps) with prior work using viewpoint labels or segmentation.

Strengths
- Clear, practical task with compelling applications in content creation and data augmentation.
- Simple and effective pose conditioning via per-keypoint heatmaps concatenated with the input image, which outperforms coordinate/bottleneck embeddings.
- Two-stage decomposition (coarse structure then detail) is reasonable; “difference map” refinement is a neat engineering idea that can stabilize training.
- Foreground-weighted (pose mask) L1 addresses background mismatch and improves perceptual quality on cluttered datasets.
- Solid qualitative results, especially on DeepFashion; user study suggests a perceptual gain of the two-stage model over single-stage adversarial training.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality
- Baselines are weak or not aligned. The comparisons of pose embeddings pit the proposed heatmap concatenation against bottleneck-only embeddings (CE/HME), which are strawman choices. A stronger baseline is a single-stage U-Net conditional GAN (pix2pix-like) conditioned on IA and pose maps (and possibly perceptual loss); you do include “G1+D,” but please clarify whether this is exactly pix2pix with your inputs and whether PB is fed at full resolution to the generator and discriminator. Also compare Stage-II “difference-map” vs direct refinement generation with identical capacity.
- Discriminator conditioning is odd: D sees (IA, I) but not PB or ÎB1. This does not enforce pose correctness; the generator could cheat by copying IA appearance without correcting pose. Consider conditioning D on PB (or on both IA and PB) and include an ablation.
- Stage-II inputs omit PB; thus refinement cannot correct pose errors from Stage-I. Please include PB in G2’s input or justify empirically why it is unnecessary.
- Identity preservation is not quantitatively evaluated. On person datasets, a reasonable metric is re-ID similarity (e.g., use a pretrained re-ID network to measure embedding similarity between generated and target vs. generated and condition). This directly tests whether appearance is preserved.
- Masked metrics are under-specified and potentially problematic. “Mask-SSIM” and “mask-IS” rely on masks derived from the target, which may bias evaluation and do not reflect realism. Applying a foreground mask before Inception Score is nonstandard; IS is not meaningful for person datasets with low intra-class variety. Report FID (or at least a classifier-based realism score) and provide details for mask construction and any preprocessing.
- Hyperparameters critical to reproduction are missing: the λ value for the masked L1 in Stage-II, the exact difference-map formulation (ÎB2 = ÎB1 + Δ? activation/clipping?), number of filters per block, weight init, D/G update ratio, normalization in D (you say no BN in G1/G2; does D use BN as in DCGAN?).
- The FC bottleneck in G1 breaks spatial equivariance and increases parameters; no quantitative justification is provided. An ablation without FC (or with dilated/residual blocks) is needed; you already remove FC in G2 due to detail loss—why is FC beneficial in G1?
- Pose estimation errors and missing keypoints are not analyzed. Show robustness curves vs. pose noise, or qualitative failure modes linked to pose errors.
- User study design lacks statistical analysis. Provide per-condition confidence intervals and significance tests; 1-second exposure Turing tests without A/B comparisons may be noisy. Also clarify whether images were normalized across methods and whether identities overlap with training.

Clarity
- Numerous formatting and referencing issues: broken headings (“st Conference on...”), orphan figure captions/text fragments, dangling footnote markers (2,3,6) without footnotes, typos, and inconsistent notation (ÎB1/ÎB2 sometimes unspecified). These hinder readability.
- Several references are incomplete/mismatched: e.g., U-Net is cited as [20] but [20] is DCGAN in your list; DCGAN is referred to as [21] earlier; many entries are arXiv-only without years/pages; titles truncated. Please correct and ensure all citations are accurate.
- The pose mask construction (“connecting body parts and morphological operations”) needs algorithmic detail and parameters to reproduce.

Originality
- The task and explicit keypoint conditioning were emerging circa 2017. While related to multi-view generation and pix2pix, the two-stage “difference map” refinement plus pose-mask loss is a reasonable combination. However, the novelty is moderate; several architectural choices are borrowed from standard cGAN/U-Net pipelines, and key ablations vs. known alternatives are missing.

Significance
- The problem is important and the qualitative gains on DeepFashion are appealing. However, the absence of strong baselines (pix2pix with pose maps, perceptual losses), identity metrics, and solid quantitative evidence limits the impact. The method’s failure to explicitly condition D and G2 on PB also weakens the claim of pose-faithful refinement.

Specific suggestions
- Compare against a pix2pix-style baseline conditioned on IA+pose heatmaps (both G and D), with and without VGG perceptual loss.
- Condition D and G2 on PB; ablate difference-map vs direct refinement.
- Report FID and re-ID similarity metrics; add LPIPS (or a perceptual distance) if possible.
- Provide complete implementation details (λ, filter counts, init, update schedules) and release code.
- Remove the FC in G1 or justify with ablations; consider dilated convolutions/residual bottlenecks instead.
- Clarify masked metric definitions; avoid masking for IS; provide statistical tests for user study.
- Fix all reference/formatting/notation issues.

Pros
- Clear problem; practical.
- Effective pose heatmap conditioning and foreground-weighted loss.
- Two-stage pipeline with difference-map refinement is efficient and intuitive.
- Good qualitative results on a high-res dataset.

Cons
- Missing strong baselines and key ablations.
- Discriminator/generator not conditioned on pose in Stage-II.
- Weak/controversial quantitative evaluation; identity preservation not measured.
- Many clarity and referencing issues; incomplete reproducibility details.

Recommendation
Vote: Weak reject. The paper is promising and likely to be impactful after revision, but the accumulation of methodological, evaluation, and presentation issues prevents me from recommending acceptance in its current form.

Confidence: High.
