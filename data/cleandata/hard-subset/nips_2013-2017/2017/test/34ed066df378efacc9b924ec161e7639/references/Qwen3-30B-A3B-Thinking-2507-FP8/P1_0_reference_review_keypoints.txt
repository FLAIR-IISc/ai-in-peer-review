- Proposes a novel two-stage method for person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Stage-I: U-Net-like network generates a coarse pose-aligned image using a novel pose mask loss to focus on the human body.  
- Stage-II: conditional GAN refines the coarse output by generating a difference map between coarse and target images.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with quantitative (SSIM, IS) and qualitative results.  
- Uses explicit pose keypoints as conditioning, distinguishing it from methods using abstract attributes or viewpoints.  
- Novel task definition with clear practical applications (e.g., movie making, pose-estimation data generation).  
- Effective divide-and-conquer strategy: Stage-I handles global structure, Stage-II handles appearance details.  
- Pose mask loss effectively reduces background interference, supported by ablation studies.  
- Difference-map GAN is a clever design that speeds convergence and improves final image quality.  
- Comprehensive experiments covering pose embeddings, loss functions, one-stage vs two-stage, and user studies.  
- Limited comparison with broader state-of-the-art beyond the most directly related work ([36]).  
- Lacks theoretical justification explaining why the two-stage approach outperforms end-to-end models.  
- Some implementation details (pose mask computation, network specifics) are insufficiently specified for full reproducibility.  
- Failure cases are shown but not analyzed in depth or quantified for frequency/causes.  
- Paper is technically sound and honest about limitations, with claims supported by experiments.  
- Well-written and organized; methods and visualizations are clear though some specifics could be expanded.  
- Original contributions: explicit pose conditioning for person generation, pose mask loss, and difference-map GAN.  
- Significant contribution with practical impact and potential to inspire further research in conditional image generation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NeurIPS based on strong empirical results and solid contributions.  
- Main counterpoints: limited baseline comparisons, theoretical gaps, and incomplete implementation detail.
