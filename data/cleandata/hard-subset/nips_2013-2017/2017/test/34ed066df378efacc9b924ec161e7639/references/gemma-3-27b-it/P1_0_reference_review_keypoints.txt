- Novelty: Explicit conditioning on both a reference image and a target pose for controllable person image generation is a key contribution.  
- Two-stage approach: Coarse-to-fine pipeline (global structure then appearance refinement) is well-motivated and effective.  
- Pose mask loss: Introduced to focus generation on the human body and reduce background influence.  
- Technical execution: Good use of U-Nets and conditional DCGANs with sound implementation.  
- Qualitative results: Visually appealing pose transfers that preserve identity.  
- Quantitative results: Reported metrics suggest competitiveness but may be misleading.  
- SSIM concern: Heavy reliance on SSIM is problematic since it may not correlate with perceptual quality.  
- Suggested metrics: Recommend adding perceptual metrics like FID or LPIPS.  
- Baseline comparisons: Comparison set is limited (mostly Zhao et al., 2017); need broader, stronger baselines including newer GAN architectures.  
- Relation to prior work: Connection to Zhao et al. (2017) is understated and needs deeper discussion.  
- Dataset bias: Experiments use mainly simple-background or low-res datasets (DeepFashion, Market-1501), limiting generalizability.  
- Ablation studies: Performed but not exhaustive; specific components (e.g., FC layer removal in Stage-II) need deeper analysis.  
- Failure analysis: Failure cases are mentioned but lack systematic investigation.  
- Originality assessment: Combination of reference-image conditioning and explicit pose control is novel despite using established building blocks.  
- Significance: Method is potentially impactful for content creation, VR, and data augmentation for pose estimation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking for expanded comparisons, improved quantitative evaluation, more diverse datasets, and more detailed failure analysis.
