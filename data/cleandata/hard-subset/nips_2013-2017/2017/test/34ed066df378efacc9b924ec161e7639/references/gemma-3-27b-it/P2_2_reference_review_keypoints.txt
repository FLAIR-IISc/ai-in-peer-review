- Overall: Two-stage pose-guided person image generation, technically sound, promising results on two datasets; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: Two-stage framework (coarse Stage-I, refinement Stage-II) using pose heatmaps, masked L1 loss, and difference map generation.  
- Datasets: Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with convincing qualitative results.  
- Strength: Novel task formulation with practical applications (movie making, data augmentation).  
- Strength: Technically well-motivated architecture (U-Nets, DCGANs) and clever use of difference maps in Stage-II.  
- Strength: Pose mask loss effectively focuses learning on the human body and reduces background influence.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written paper and organized presentation.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons to relevant baselines (insufficient against Zhao et al. [36] and other pose-transfer/warping methods).  
- Weakness: Ablation study is incomplete (notably lacking thorough analysis of Stage-II λ and other hyperparameters/architectures).  
- Weakness: Failure case analysis is brief; lacks depth on complex poses and challenging clothing textures.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation limited to SSIM and IS, which can be misleading; needs alternative/perceptual and pose-accuracy metrics.  
- Quality: Method is sound but weakened by incomplete baseline and ablation evaluation.  
- Clarity: Paper is clear and diagrams helpful.  
- Originality: Combination of pose heatmaps, two-stage processing, and difference maps is novel though individual components are standard.  
- Significance: Demonstrates practical advancement but could be better substantiated with stronger comparisons.  
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated approach; pose mask loss and difference map are solid contributions; good empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient baselines, incomplete ablations, and limited failure/metric analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add stronger baseline comparisons (e.g., warping, paired GANs), expand ablation (λ and architectures), deepen failure-case analysis, and use more robust/perceptual and pose-specific evaluation metrics.
