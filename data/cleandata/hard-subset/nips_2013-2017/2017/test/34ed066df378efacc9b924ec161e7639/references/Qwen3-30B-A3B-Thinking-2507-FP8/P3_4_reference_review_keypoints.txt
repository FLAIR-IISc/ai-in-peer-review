- Two-stage PG2 framework: Stage-I U-Net-like coarse pose-aligned image using pose heatmaps; Stage-II conditional GAN refines by predicting a difference map; pose mask loss focuses on the human body.  
- Technically sound approach: two-stage strategy, pose mask loss, and difference-map refinement are reasonable and likely improve convergence.  
- Experimental shortcomings: no quantitative comparison with the most similar work [36], only qualitative examples provided.  
- User study issue: fails to explain why G1+G2+D performs worse than G1+D on Market-1501 despite qualitative improvements.  
- Lack of ablations: key parameters (e.g., λ) and other design choices lack ablation studies in the main paper.  
- Clarity: well-organized and reproducible descriptions, but unclear why one-stage adversarial model underperforms despite similar metrics.  
- Originality concerns: contributions are incremental—two-stage setup and difference maps mirror existing techniques; pose mask idea not novel.  
- Insufficient differentiation from prior work: paper does not thoroughly distinguish itself from [36] and other conditional generation methods.  
- Significance: useful for fashion, film, and pose estimation but limited by requirement of a reference image and poor handling of occlusions/complex poses.  
- Strengths recap: clear framework, thoughtful pose mask loss, solid qualitative/quantitative results, good writing.  
- Weaknesses recap: limited novelty, insufficient comparisons and ablations, unaddressed failure cases, limited practical discussion.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (3) — technically solid but incremental; needs more comparisons, deeper failure analysis, and clearer novelty claims for top-tier acceptance.  
- Key revision requests: add quantitative comparison with [36]; include ablation studies for λ and other parameters; explain G1+G2+D behavior on Market-1501; expand discussion of differences from related methods; address limitations (reference requirement, occlusions).
