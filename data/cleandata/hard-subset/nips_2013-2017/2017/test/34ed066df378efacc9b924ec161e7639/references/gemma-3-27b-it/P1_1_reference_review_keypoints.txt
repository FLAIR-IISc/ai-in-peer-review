- Overall: Interesting, technically sound two-stage GAN for pose-guided person image generation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Two-stage pipeline — Stage I U-Net for coarse pose transfer, Stage II conditional DCGAN for detail refinement; contributions include pose embedding exploration, pose mask loss, and staged generation.  
- Related work: Good overview and accurate contextualization, especially relative to prior pose-conditioned generation (e.g., [36]).  
- Novelty: Conditioning on both a reference image and keypoint-specified pose enables direct, controllable human pose manipulation.  
- Technical soundness: U-Net + DCGAN design and the pose mask loss are well-justified and logically structured.  
- Experimental validation: Demonstrated effectiveness on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with informative ablation studies.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with helpful diagrams explaining architecture and pose mask concept.  
- Implementation details: Network architectures, optimizers, and training iterations are provided to aid reproducibility.  
- Significance weakness: Improvements over existing methods are not shown to be dramatic and quantitative comparisons are limited.  
- Failure cases weakness: Failure cases are only briefly mentioned; deeper analysis needed for complex poses and gender imbalance.  
- User study weakness: Interpretation of user study results (especially Market-1501) may be confounded by low-quality source images, not only artifacts.  
- Generalization weakness: Method is specific to person images and applicability to other object categories is unexplored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, well-motivated approach with solid methodology, strong experiments, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Modest demonstrated significance, limited failure analysis, user study interpretation issues, and lack of generalization discussion.  
- Question for authors: Provide more comprehensive quantitative comparisons with existing methods (even if for slightly different tasks).  
- Question for authors: Provide detailed analysis of failure cases and consistent failure patterns (poses, body types).  
- Question for authors: What modifications are needed to adapt the method to other object categories (e.g., cars, furniture)?  
- Question for authors: Clarify computational cost of the proposed method versus existing techniques.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
