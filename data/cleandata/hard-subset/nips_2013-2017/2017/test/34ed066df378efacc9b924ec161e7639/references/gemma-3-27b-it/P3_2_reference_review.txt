## Review of Pose Guided Person Image Generation

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents an interesting and technically sound approach to pose-guided person image generation. The problem is well-motivated and the proposed two-stage framework with a novel pose mask loss demonstrates a practical solution. While the originality is incremental, the combination of techniques and the demonstrated results are significant enough to warrant acceptance, with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper addresses the problem of generating a person image with a desired pose, conditioned on a reference image. This is a valuable task with potential applications in areas like movie making and data augmentation for pose estimation. The authors correctly identify the challenges of end-to-end pose and appearance generation and propose a divide-and-conquer strategy. They leverage U-Nets and DCGANs, along with a novel pose mask loss, to achieve this. 

The related work section is comprehensive, covering VAEs, GANs, and autoregressive models for image generation. It appropriately highlights relevant works on conditional image generation, including those based on attributes, text, and images, and specifically mentions related work in view and pose manipulation ([36], [4]). The authors effectively position their work by emphasizing the explicit and flexible nature of pose guidance using keypoints, distinguishing it from approaches relying on abstract cues or viewpoint estimation. 

**2. Strengths:**

* **Well-Motivated Problem:** The task is relevant and practically useful, addressing a gap in controllable image generation.
* **Sound Methodology:** The two-stage approach is a sensible strategy for tackling the complexity of the problem. Dividing it into global structure (Stage-I) and detail refinement (Stage-II) makes the learning process more manageable.
* **Novel Pose Mask Loss:** The proposed loss function is a clever addition that encourages the model to focus on the relevant parts of the image (the human body) and mitigate background artifacts. The formulation is clear and well-justified.
* **Strong Experimental Validation:** Experiments on two datasets (Market-1501 and DeepFashion) demonstrate the effectiveness of the method. Qualitative results are compelling, and the quantitative results, though with some caveats discussed below, support the claims.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the proposed method and experimental setup. The diagrams (especially Figure 2) are helpful.
* **Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contributions of the different components of the proposed method, such as the different pose embeddings and the pose mask loss.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Incremental Originality:** While the combination of techniques is effective, the core components (U-Nets, DCGANs, adversarial loss) are well-established. The novelty lies primarily in the specific application to pose-guided person generation and the pose mask loss.
* **Quantitative Evaluation Caveats:** The authors acknowledge the limitations of SSIM and IS, especially given the potential for background changes. The reliance on mask-SSIM and mask-IS is a good attempt to address this, but the observed discrepancy between quantitative and qualitative results warrants further investigation. Relying only on these metrics isn’t enough to fully assess the generation quality.  Consider including FID or other more perceptual metrics.
* **Failure Cases:**  While some failure cases are discussed (e.g., missing legs, gender inaccuracies), a more systematic analysis of failure modes would be beneficial. Understanding *why* the model fails in certain scenarios could inform future improvements.
* **Limited Comparison:** The comparison to [36] is a good start, but given the growing body of work in conditional image generation, a more comprehensive comparison to other relevant methods would strengthen the paper.

**4. Specific Comments & Suggestions:**

* **Pose Embedding:** The results showing the superiority of the heatmap embedding are convincing. A brief explanation of *why* the heatmap embedding outperforms the coordinate embedding would be helpful.
* **Mask Generation:** The description of the pose mask generation process could be more detailed.  What morphological operations are used, and what parameters are chosen? A visual example of the generated mask would be valuable.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** A brief discussion of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters (e.g., λ in the Stage-II loss function) would be helpful.
* **User Study:** The user study results are interesting.  The discrepancy between the datasets highlights the importance of background consistency.  Further analysis of the user responses (e.g., what types of artifacts led users to identify the images as fake) could provide valuable insights.



**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a relevant and challenging problem.
* Technically sound methodology with a novel contribution (pose mask loss).
* Strong experimental validation on multiple datasets.
* Clear presentation and well-organized paper.

**Con:**

* Incremental originality.
* Quantitative evaluation has limitations.
* Limited comparison to other relevant methods.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2017. The work is a solid contribution to the field of image generation, and the proposed method is both practical and effective. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by strengthening the quantitative evaluation and providing a more comprehensive comparison to related work, would further enhance the paper.
