Summary and relation to prior work
This paper tackles pose-guided person image generation: given a reference person image and a target pose, synthesize a realistic image of the same person in the new pose. The proposed PG2 framework is two-stage. Stage I uses a U-Net-like encoder–decoder with skip connections to integrate the reference image with a pose representation (18 keypoint heatmaps) and optimize a masked L1 loss that upweights foreground pixels derived from the pose mask, producing a coarse result that captures correct global structure. Stage II refines details via a conditional GAN that predicts a difference map relative to the Stage I output, coupled with a pairwise discriminator D(IA, I) that distinguishes (IA, IB) vs (IA, Î) and a masked L1 regularizer. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) include ablations on pose embeddings (coordinates vs heatmaps), losses (L1 vs masked L1), and one-stage vs two-stage. Quantitative metrics (SSIM/IS and mask-SSIM/mask-IS), user studies, and a comparison to VariGAN [36] are reported.

The work builds on conditional image generation (CVAE/C-GANs [18, 32]), image-to-image translation (pix2pix [9]), controllable generation with structure/attributes [22–24, 32], and view synthesis [4, 36]. Using explicit keypoint heatmaps to guide structure follows the “controllable structure” line (e.g., Reed et al.), while the two-stage “coarse-to-fine” and adversarial refinement resonate with stacked/conditional GANs [20, 33, 35]. The pairing of the discriminator with the condition image is standard in cGANs; here it is used to prevent trivial copying of IA.

Strengths
- Clear, practically useful problem formulation with explicit control via pose.
- Sensible two-stage decomposition that cleanly separates structure (Stage I) from appearance detail (Stage II).
- Effective engineering choices: pose heatmap concatenation; masked L1 loss that reduces background confounds; residual “difference-map” refinement that stabilizes training.
- Thorough ablations isolating contributions (pose embedding, loss choice, one- vs two-stage).
- Evaluations on two datasets with different characteristics; qualitative results often convincing, especially on DeepFashion.
- User study and an attempt to mitigate background effects via mask-SSIM/IS.

Weaknesses
- Limited quantitative evaluation of the central desiderata:
  • Identity preservation is not measured (e.g., via a re-ID network or face/body recognition features).
  • Pose correctness is not quantified (e.g., PCK/OKS by running a pose estimator on generated images and comparing to target pose).
- Baselines are narrow. A stronger single-stage baseline (pix2pix-style PatchGAN with perceptual loss, BN, spectral norm) and an appearance-warping baseline (e.g., learned flow/appearance transfer) would clarify gains from the two-stage design and the difference-map refinement.
- The adversarial discriminator is not conditioned on the target pose, so Stage II may trade pose fidelity for realism; no analysis of pose drift across stages is provided.
- Metrics (SSIM/IS) are known to be poorly correlated with perceptual quality; FID or a learned perceptual metric (e.g., LPIPS) would be more informative. The paper acknowledges SSIM limitations but still uses it as the main number.
- No analysis of robustness to pose estimator noise or missing keypoints, which is critical for deployment.
- Some clarity/formatting issues in references and minor typos; architectural details (e.g., λ settings, impact of “1+MB” weighting, training times) could be clearer.

Evaluation by criteria
- Quality: Method is technically sound and well-motivated. Experimental protocol is reasonable with ablations. However, the lack of identity and pose fidelity metrics weakens the empirical support for the main claims.
- Clarity: Generally well-organized with understandable figures and ablations. Mask construction and architectural choices are explained. Minor reference formatting issues.
- Originality: The specific combination—pose heatmaps + foreground-weighted loss + two-stage with residual refinement and pairwise discriminator—feels novel in aggregate for 2017, even if components are known.
- Significance: Addresses a relevant and challenging problem with promising results; likely to influence subsequent pose-guided generation work. Impact would be strengthened with stronger baselines and quantitative pose/identity measures.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, well-scoped contribution with practical value.
- Coarse-to-fine design and difference-map refinement are effective and likely reusable.
- Solid ablations; convincing qualitative gains over a one-stage approach and over [36].
- Public datasets; no heavy annotations (pose from off-the-shelf estimator).

Cons against acceptance
- Missing key quantitative evaluations (identity preservation, pose accuracy).
- Limited baselines; no strong pix2pix-style or warping-based comparisons.
- Potential pose drift in Stage II due to D not conditioned on pose; not analyzed.
- Reliance on SSIM/IS; more modern perceptual metrics absent.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add identity preservation evaluation using a re-ID model (e.g., feature cosine distance, retrieval mAP/Rank-1) between IA/IB and generated outputs.
- Quantify pose fidelity by running a pose estimator on generated images and computing PCK/OKS vs target pose.
- Report FID and a perceptual similarity metric (e.g., LPIPS). Include confidence intervals for user study.
- Strengthen baselines: a pix2pix/PatchGAN baseline conditioned on IA and pose heatmaps with perceptual loss; and an appearance-warping baseline (e.g., learned flow/appearance transfer or part-wise STN).
- Condition D on PB as well (D(IA, PB, I)) and/or add a pose-consistency loss to ensure Stage II preserves pose.
- Analyze robustness to noisy/missing keypoints; study λ and mask weighting; report runtime/memory.
- Explore multi-modal outputs (stochasticity) to reflect uncertainty in occluded regions.

Recommendation
Lean accept. The paper addresses an important problem with a well-designed, empirically validated two-stage approach that shows clear qualitative benefits and thoughtful ablations. With stronger quantitative evaluation of identity and pose fidelity and comparisons to stronger baselines, the case would be unequivocal.
