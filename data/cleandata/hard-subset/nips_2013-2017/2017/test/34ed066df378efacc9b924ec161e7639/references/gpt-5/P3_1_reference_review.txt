Summary and contributions
The paper tackles pose-guided person image synthesis: given a reference image IA and a target pose PB (as 2D keypoints), generate a new image of the same person in the desired pose. The method (PG2) is a two-stage pipeline: (i) Stage-I uses a U-Net-like encoder-decoder (with residual blocks and a fully connected bottleneck) conditioned on pose heatmaps concatenated to IA to produce a coarse image that captures global structure; training uses a masked L1 loss that upweights person pixels (derived from pose) to de-emphasize background differences. (ii) Stage-II refines the result with a conditional DCGAN that predicts a residual “difference map” added to the coarse image; the discriminator sees pairs (IA, Î) vs (IA, IB) to enforce consistency with the condition; a masked L1 term regularizes adversarial training. Ablations compare different pose embeddings, loss functions, and one- vs two-stage training. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) include SSIM/IS and masked variants, plus an AMT user study. A comparison to VariGAN shows clear improvement.

Relation to prior work
The paper situates itself among conditional generative modeling (CVAE, cGAN, autoregressive) and image-to-image translation. It is closest to view/pose-conditioned generation for faces and bodies, and to Pix2Pix-like keypoint-to-image models; compared to view labels (e.g., “side”), the use of explicit 2D keypoint heatmaps is more expressive. The two-stage design is in the spirit of stacked/graduate refinement (e.g., StackGAN, recent image translation works), with the novelty here in the specific decomposition (coarse pose transfer + residual cGAN) and foreground-focused masked losses. Related identity-preserving generation (e.g., face frontalization, perceptual losses) is mentioned but not directly leveraged. Overall, positioning and citations are reasonable, though missing a stronger baseline comparison to a Pose→Image Pix2Pix variant.

Strengths
- Well-motivated problem with clear practical relevance; explicit pose conditioning provides user-controllable generation.
- Simple, effective pose encoding via heatmap concatenation; careful ablation against coordinate and separate-encoder embeddings.
- Two-stage “coarse-to-fine with residual” design is intuitive, stabilizes training, and yields visibly sharper details than a one-stage GAN.
- Foreground-aware masked L1 is a pragmatic solution to nuisance background variation, and improves results especially on Market-1501.
- Solid experimental coverage: datasets, ablations, user study; attention to pitfalls of SSIM/IS and use of masked variants.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Identity preservation is not directly addressed or measured. The pipeline aims to preserve clothing/appearance, but no explicit perceptual or identity (re-ID) losses are used; no metrics quantify identity consistency (e.g., re-ID feature similarity/mAP, retrieval accuracy).
- Baselines: The “one-stage G1+D” is close to a Pix2Pix-style cGAN, but a strong baseline with a PatchGAN discriminator and perceptual loss would be informative. A pose-to-image Pix2Pix (using only keypoints-to-image) and an image+pose-to-image Pix2Pix with standard tricks would contextualize gains.
- Evaluation metrics: SSIM/IS (even masked) are weak proxies for realism; no FID/KID reported and no LPIPS for perceptual similarity. User study shows modest fooling rates (14.9% on DeepFashion), and on Market-1501 the refined model underperforms the one-stage in AMT, likely due to background artifacts.
- Foreground mask quality: The mask derived from keypoints plus morphology is coarse; using (1+MB) only doubles foreground weight rather than suppressing background loss, so the model is still penalized on unobservable backgrounds. A fully masked loss for backgrounds or a foreground-aware adversarial loss could further reduce artifacts.
- Architecture choices: The fully connected bottleneck in Stage-I breaks translation equivariance, is parameter-heavy, and may discard details; alternatives (dilated convs, non-local/self-attention) could provide long-range interactions without FC. Conversely, removing FC in Stage-II may limit global corrections.
- No analysis of robustness to pose estimation noise; the method depends on automatic keypoints but does not quantify sensitivity to keypoint errors or missing joints.
- Diversity: The mapping is inherently multimodal (occlusions, unseen regions), yet no stochasticity or diversity mechanisms are used; outputs are deterministic and may average plausible hypotheses in Stage-I.

Suggestions/questions for authors
- Report identity preservation metrics: cosine similarity or retrieval accuracy using a pretrained person re-ID network; color/texture consistency measures for clothing regions; user study specific to identity.
- Add stronger baselines: Pix2Pix with keypoint heatmaps + IA, PatchGAN, feature matching, and VGG/perceptual loss; possibly SPADE-like conditioning on pose maps.
- Use better realism metrics (FID/KID) on masked crops; report training stability and convergence curves to substantiate the “difference map speeds up convergence” claim.
- Consider a foreground-aware discriminator (mask-guided PatchGAN) or segmentation-guided adversarial to reduce background copying artifacts. Alternatively, inpaint background or condition D on the pose mask.
- Explore removing the FC layer in Stage-I or replacing it with dilations/self-attention; compare parameter counts and performance.
- Analyze robustness to pose noise: jitter keypoints, drop joints, or compare to ground-truth keypoints if available.
- Encourage diversity by injecting noise or employing multimodal cGANs (e.g., InfoGAN, latent style codes) to sample plausible completions of occluded regions.

Quality
Technically sound overall; the method is straightforward and the ablations support key design choices. Some claims (faster convergence via residual prediction) are qualitative. Evaluation lacks identity-aware metrics and stronger baselines.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized; architecture and losses are described with enough detail to reproduce. Some implementation details (mask construction, λ selection, discriminator specifics) are deferred to the supplement—consider surfacing key hyperparameters.

Originality
Moderate. The specific combination—pose heatmap conditioning, masked L1, two-stage with residual cGAN and pairwise discriminator—is a thoughtful assembly of known components for a new task definition.

Significance
Good. Pose-guided person synthesis is useful and likely to inspire follow-ups. The two-stage design is practical and shows clear qualitative gains, though quantitative evidence could be stronger and identity preservation remains under-explored.

Pros
- Clear problem formulation; controllable generation via pose.
- Effective two-stage design with residual refinement.
- Foreground-focused loss; informative ablations and user study.

Cons
- Weak baseline/metric choices; no identity preservation evaluation.
- Background artifacts; limited robustness/diversity analysis.
- FC bottleneck design choice questionable.

Recommendation: Weak accept (7/10). Confidence: 4/5.
