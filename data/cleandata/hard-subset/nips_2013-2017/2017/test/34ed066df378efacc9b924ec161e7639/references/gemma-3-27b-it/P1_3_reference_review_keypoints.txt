- Problem & contribution: Proposes PG2, a two-stage method to generate person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose, with a pose mask loss to focus on the person.  
- Method: Stage-I produces a coarse pose-aligned image; Stage-II refines it with a GAN for sharper details.  
- Datasets & evaluation: Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, with ablations and a user study.  
- Novelty: Explicit conditioning on both reference appearance and target pose provides finer control than prior work.  
- Divide-and-conquer design: Two-stage approach effectively separates global structure synthesis from detail refinement.  
- Pose mask loss: Helps mitigate background inconsistency by focusing learning on the human body.  
- Experimental thoroughness: Ablation studies and evaluations across two datasets strengthen the empirical claims.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear figures and explanation.  
- Quantitative gains: Improvements in SSIM and IS are modest and not always aligned with perceptual quality.  
- Blur in Stage-I: Initial-stage outputs remain blurry despite residual blocks, indicating room for architectural or loss improvements.  
- Dataset limitations: Market-1501’s low resolution/complex backgrounds limit convincing results; DeepFashion is more controlled and easier.  
- GAN stability: Training instability concerns aren’t sufficiently addressed or discussed.  
- Comparisons: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art (e.g., Zhao et al., 2017); more comprehensive baselines or justification needed.  
- Recommendation: Overall solid contribution with promising qualitative results; reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision suggestions: Expand related-work comparisons, discuss GAN stabilization, investigate Stage-I blurriness, and acknowledge dataset/generalization limits.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 7/10.
