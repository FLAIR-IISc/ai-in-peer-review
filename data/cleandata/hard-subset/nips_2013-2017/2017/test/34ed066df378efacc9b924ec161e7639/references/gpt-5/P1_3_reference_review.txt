Summary
The paper proposes PG2, a pose-guided person image generation method that conditions on a reference image of a person and a target pose to synthesize a new image of the same person in the desired pose. The method is structured as a two-stage, coarse-to-fine pipeline: (i) Stage-I integrates the input image and pose via a U-Net-like generator to produce a coarse image that captures global structure; it uses a pose mask loss (masked L1) to downweight background differences. (ii) Stage-II refines details using a conditional DCGAN: the generator predicts a residual “difference map” to add to the Stage-I output, and the discriminator operates on pairs (condition image, generated/real target) to enforce consistency with the conditioning image. Pose is encoded as 18 keypoint heatmaps. Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) include ablations on pose encoding (coordinates vs. heatmaps), loss (L1 vs. masked L1), and one-stage vs. two-stage training. Quantitative evaluation uses SSIM/IS and masked variants to reduce background effects, and a brief AMT user study. Qualitatively, the two-stage model yields sharper, more realistic results and better preserves pose and many appearance details. The paper compares to Zhao et al. (multi-view generation) and shows clear improvements.

Relation to prior work is appropriate: the approach builds on cGANs/image-to-image translation and stacked/coarse-to-fine GANs, distinguishes itself by explicit keypoint-based pose conditioning, and introduces practical training design choices (masked loss, pairwise discriminator, residual refinement).

Strengths
- Addresses an impactful, clearly-defined task: controllable human image synthesis conditioned on explicit pose, with plausible downstream uses (content creation, data augmentation).
- Sensible two-stage design that separates global structure from high-frequency details; the residual/difference-map refinement is practical and stabilizes training.
- Effective pose encoding via heatmaps and a simple pose mask to counter background mismatch; well-motivated and validated with ablations.
- Pairwise conditional discriminator aligns real/fake decisions with the conditioning image, a useful detail for this problem.
- Comprehensive qualitative results on two datasets with diverse poses; ablations demonstrate the importance of pose encoding and masked loss; user study supports perceptual gains of the adversarial refinement.
- Clear, honest discussion of metric limitations and failure cases.

Weaknesses
- Evaluation metrics are limited: SSIM/IS (even with masks) are weak proxies for perceptual quality and identity preservation in this domain; IS is especially questionable for masked, single-person images. No FID/LPIPS or task-driven metrics.
- Identity preservation is central but not quantified. A re-identification-based metric (e.g., retrieval accuracy or feature similarity from a pre-trained re-id network) would be informative.
- Baselines are not as strong as they could be. The “one-stage” G1+D baseline is close in spirit but differs architecturally; a direct pix2pix-style baseline with pose heatmaps + condition image, PatchGAN discriminator, and common tricks (feature matching/perceptual loss) would strengthen conclusions.
- Limited ablation on Stage-II: no comparison of (i) generating residual vs. generating a full image, (ii) pairwise discriminator vs. standard conditional discriminator without pairing, (iii) effect of removing the fully-connected layer, and (iv) sensitivity to λ (relegated to supplement).
- Background handling is ad hoc: the pose mask derived from keypoints may be brittle under occlusions or sparse detections; the method often transfers background from the condition image, which can add artifacts (as noted by authors).
- Robustness to pose estimation errors is not analyzed; success depends on estimated keypoints, but the effect of noisy or missing keypoints is not quantified.
- Reproducibility details are incomplete in the main text (exact filter counts per block, mask construction procedure, training/inference speed) and several references are incomplete.

Quality
Technically sound and well-motivated. The architectural and loss choices are reasonable; the pairwise conditioning and residual refinement are coherent. Experimental evidence supports the main claims qualitatively and via ablations, though stronger baselines and identity-aware metrics would make the case more convincing. User study is a plus, but lacks statistical analysis and comparison to external baselines.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. Figures convey the pipeline and results. The paper would benefit from more precise description of the pose mask construction, explicit architecture hyperparameters, and a clearer description of Stage-II residual addition. Some references are incomplete.

Originality
Moderate. The problem setting (explicit pose-guided generation from a reference image) is novel at the time, and the combination of (keypoint heatmaps + pose mask + residual refinement with pairwise discriminator) is a useful, pragmatic contribution. The two-stage/coarse-to-fine idea and residual learning are known, but applied effectively here.

Significance
High practical significance for controllable human synthesis; likely to influence subsequent work in pose-guided generation and data augmentation. The method improves over prior view-conditioned generation and provides a solid baseline. The lack of identity-aware evaluation and stronger baselines modestly limits the strength of claims, but overall the contribution is meaningful.

Questions for authors
- How exactly is the pose mask computed from keypoints (connectivity, dilation parameters), and how robust is it to missing/occluded joints?
- In Stage-II, is the residual constrained (e.g., via tanh scaling)? Did you compare predicting the full image vs. residual?
- Did you try perceptual or feature-matching losses in Stage-II to reduce artifacts while preserving details?
- How sensitive is performance to pose estimation noise? Any quantitative robustness study?
- Runtime per image and training time?

Suggestions
- Add identity-preservation evaluation via a re-id network and/or LPIPS/FID on masked regions.
- Include a stronger one-stage baseline (pix2pix-like with PatchGAN, perceptual/feature-matching loss).
- Ablate the pairwise discriminator and residual prediction in Stage-II.
- Consider a learned person mask (e.g., from a segmentation model) to improve background handling.
