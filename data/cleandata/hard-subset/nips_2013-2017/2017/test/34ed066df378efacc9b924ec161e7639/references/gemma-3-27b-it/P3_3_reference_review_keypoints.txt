- Proposes a novel two-stage method to generate person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose (Stage-I: global structure via U-Net, Stage-II: detail refinement via cGAN).  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the person and reduce background influence.  
- Evaluated on two datasets (low-res re-ID and high-res fashion) showing promising quantitative and qualitative results.  
- Situates work well within image/conditional generation and pose-guided manipulation literature, differentiating via explicit keypoint pose control.  
- Strength: novelty in combining reference-image conditioning with explicit pose guidance for controllable pose transfer.  
- Strength: divide-and-conquer two-stage architecture effectively separates structure and detail, improving image consistency.  
- Strength: pose mask loss is an effective, targeted loss to reduce background artifacts.  
- Strength: quantitative and qualitative comparisons (including one-stage baseline and [36]) support method effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear writing and organization aid reproducibility and understanding.  
- Weakness: evaluation scope is limited—datasets are small and qualitative examples are not extensive or systematically chosen.  
- Weakness: method depends on a pre-trained pose estimator; pose estimation errors could degrade generation and robustness is not explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation on Stage-I architecture choices (e.g., residual blocks, FC layer effects).  
- Weakness: limited and non-systematic discussion of failure cases and common failure modes.  
- Weakness: reliance on SSIM as a metric is problematic and can be misleading for generative models.  
- Originality: meaningful contribution by combining known components into a new controllable pose-conditioned generation framework.  
- Significance: potential impact in filmmaking, data augmentation for pose estimation, virtual try-on, and controllable image editing.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — solid contribution with some addressable weaknesses.  
- Suggested revisions: expand and diversify evaluation (including robustness to noisy poses), perform detailed Stage-I ablations, deepen failure-case analysis, downplay SSIM in favor of qualitative/user studies, and report computational cost.
