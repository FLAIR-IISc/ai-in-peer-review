- Summary: Proposes PG2, a two-stage pose-guided person image generation network (Stage-I coarse structure, Stage-II detail refinement) conditioned on pose heatmaps to transfer a person to a target pose while preserving appearance.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated problem with practical applications (fashion, entertainment, data augmentation).

- Strength: Effective two-stage approach empirically shown to outperform one-stage alternatives via ablations.

- Strength: Simple, effective pose representation using heatmaps (suits convolutional networks and avoids expensive annotation).

- Strength: Pose mask loss focuses generation on the human body and improves qualitative and quantitative results.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation on DeepFashion and Market-1501, including user study on MTurk.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to more recent related work (missing CVPR/ICCV 2017–2018 papers and similar two-stage methods).

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why two-stage training is superior (learning dynamics or complexity discussion absent).

- Weakness: Insufficient failure analysis for shown failure cases and unclear whether issues are data- or model-related.

- Weakness: Overreliance on SSIM/IS metrics that don't fully reflect perceptual quality; suggests adding perceptual metrics like FID.

- Weakness: Missing reproducibility details (exact G1/G2 architecture dimensions and pose estimator specifics).

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution with a useful two-stage design and pose mask loss; empirical gains outweigh the limitations.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Pro summary: Novel formulation, effective two-stage method, good pose representation and mask loss, comprehensive evaluation with human studies.

- Con summary: Limited recent comparisons, weak theoretical grounding, incomplete failure analysis, metric limitations, and some missing implementation details.
