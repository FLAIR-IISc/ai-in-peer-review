- Overall: Novel and interesting task with a reasonable two-stage (U-Net then conditional DCGAN) approach; reviewer leans weak accept but requests stronger quantitative validation.
- Contribution: Introduces pose-guided person image generation, a pose mask loss, and a two-stage generation pipeline (coarse structure then detail refinement).
- Strength — Novelty: Conditioning on both a reference image and target pose offers meaningful control beyond attribute/text-based generation.
- Strength — Approach: Splitting into global structure (U-Net) and detail refinement (GAN) is well-motivated for this complex task.
- Strength — Pose mask loss: The mask loss effectively focuses learning on body appearance and reduces background mismatch effects.
- Strength — Ablations: Ablation studies systematically show the contributions of pose embeddings, losses, and the two-stage design.
- Strength — Qualitative results: Visual comparisons convincingly demonstrate improvements and advantages over related work [36].
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and accompanied by helpful figures.
- Weakness — Quantitative gains: Improvements in SSIM and IS (and mask variants) are limited and not strongly convincing.
- Weakness — Related work: Comparisons are insufficiently comprehensive—missing recent GAN-based image editing/StyleGAN-related baselines or techniques for refinement.
- Weakness — Failure analysis: Limited discussion of failure modes (challenging poses, occlusions, complex clothing) and method limitations.
- Weakness — User study ambiguity: User study results are mixed/unclear, with lower Market-1501 scores suggesting possible realism artifacts despite better pose accuracy.
- Weakness — Dataset bias: Dataset imbalance (e.g., gender) may affect generalizability, but its impact is not thoroughly addressed.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 6/10.
- For acceptance: Novel problem, sound technical approach, good ablations/qualitative results, clear writing.
- Against acceptance: Limited quantitative improvement, incomplete comparisons, and ambiguous user study outcomes.
- Requested revisions: Strengthen quantitative evaluation with better metrics, expand related-work comparisons (GAN-based editing), analyze failure cases in depth, and further investigate user study anomalies.
