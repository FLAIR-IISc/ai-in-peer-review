- Proposes PG2, a novel two-stage method for pose-guided person image generation: stage-1 U-Net with pose heatmaps and pose-mask loss; stage-2 conditional GAN that predicts a difference map for refinement.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing qualitative and quantitative improvements over one-stage baselines and Zhao et al. (2017).
- Originality: introduces explicit pose-guided conditioning and a pose mask loss to focus learning on human regions.
- Significance: useful for film, pose estimation, and fashion; produces more realistic images balancing pose accuracy and appearance.
- Quality: technically sound methodology with ablations on pose embeddings, losses, and stage design; uses mask-SSIM and mask-IS metrics appropriately.
- Clarity: well-written, organized, with helpful figures and a user study complementing quantitative results.
- Weakness — theoretical: lacks deeper theoretical analysis explaining why the two-stage design outperforms one-stage models.
- Weakness — related work: insufficient comparison/distinction from broader image-to-image translation literature (e.g., Isola et al., 2017).
- Weakness — failure cases: limited discussion of failure modes (e.g., leg artifacts, gender misclassification) and their causes like data imbalance.
- Weakness — implementation: some choices (e.g., N=6 vs N=5, no batchnorm/dropout) are unexplained.
- Overall: strong technical contribution with clear empirical gains; method could generalize to other pose-guided domains despite being a narrow task.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Requested minor revisions: add theoretical discussion for two-stage design, strengthen comparisons to broader GAN/image-translation work, expand failure-case analysis, and justify implementation hyperparameters.
