- Proposes PG2: a novel two-stage framework (Stage-I U-Net-like pose embedding + pose mask loss; Stage-II conditional GAN producing a difference map) for pose-guided person image generation.

- Demonstrates effectiveness on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with qualitative results, ablations, and a limited AMT user study.

- Strength: Clearly defines a meaningful new task with useful applications (movie making, pose estimation data).

- Strength: Two-stage design and pose mask loss are technically sound and address background interference and fast refinement.

- Strength: Stage-II difference map idea speeds training and improves detail preservation.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments and ablation studies on pose embedding, losses, and stages.

- Strength: Clear method description with figures and reproducibility-oriented details (some in supplement).

- Weakness: Limited comparison to broader state-of-the-art GAN-based image-to-image methods beyond [36].

- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation relies on SSIM and Inception Score, which can be misleading for perceptual quality.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why two-stage outperforms single-stage or converges faster.

- Weakness: Failure cases are insufficiently analyzed (e.g., gender misclassification not deeply investigated).

- Weakness: Comparison with [36] is mainly qualitative and lacks numerical metrics.

- Overall: Technically solid, clear, and reasonably novel (scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10).

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen evaluation (add FID, quantitative comparison with [36], deeper failure analysis, clarify comparisons with related methods).

- Pros for acceptance: Novel task, well-justified two-stage method, thorough ablations, strong qualitative results, practical implementation.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited SOTA comparisons, potentially misleading metrics, no theoretical justification, superficial [36] comparison, inadequate failure analysis.
