Summary
This paper tackles pose-guided person image generation: given a reference image of a person and a desired target pose (as 2D keypoints), synthesize a new image of the same person in the target pose. The core idea is to make control explicit by conditioning on keypoint heatmaps and to decompose synthesis into two stages: (i) a U-Net-like generator (with skip connections and a bottleneck FC layer) integrates the reference image and target pose to produce a coarse result focused on correct global structure, trained with a masked L1 loss that emphasizes the human foreground; (ii) a conditional DCGAN refines details by predicting a difference map between the coarse output and the target image, trained with an adversarial pairwise discriminator and the same masked L1. The authors explore pose embedding choices, show that concatenating pose heatmaps to the image input performs best, and argue that difference-map refinement accelerates convergence and improves sharpness. Results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include ablations, SSIM/IS (and masked variants), and a small AMT study; they also compare qualitatively to a viewpoint-conditioned baseline (VariGAN).

Relation to prior work
The work sits at the intersection of conditional generation (CVAE/GANs, pix2pix, StackGAN) and controllable image synthesis. Compared to viewpoint-conditioned synthesis, using keypoints provides more precise and flexible control. The two-stage, coarse-to-fine strategy is in the spirit of stacked/RefineNet-style generation, but the difference-map refinement and the foreground-weighted loss tailored to pose transfer are the main architectural contributions.

Strengths
- Clear, impactful task definition with explicit control: conditioning on a reference identity and explicit 2D pose keypoints is a simple, powerful interface that could enable many applications (content creation, data augmentation).
- Coarse-to-fine design that isolates structure from detail, with a practical difference-map GAN refinement that is conceptually clean and empirically useful.
- Pose mask loss is a sensible, targeted tweak that addresses background mismatch and improves person-focused synthesis.
- Thorough ablations on pose embeddings and loss choices; the heatmap concatenation vs. coordinate/FC embedding comparison is informative.
- Results are qualitatively compelling on DeepFashion and reasonably robust on the more challenging Market-1501.
- Pairwise discriminator (condition, output) is appropriate for avoiding identity copying; conditioning choices are well-motivated.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Evaluation metrics: SSIM/IS (even masked) are weak proxies for perceptual quality. No FID or LPIPS, and the AMT study is limited (1 s exposure, modest confusion rates). The authors themselves note that sharper images can get lower SSIM.
- Identity preservation is not quantitatively measured. Given the application claims (e.g., re-ID augmentation), it would be natural to evaluate an off-the-shelf re-ID or face/identity recognizer on generated images or cross-pose consistency.
- Multimodality is not modeled. The mapping from (reference, pose) to image is deterministic; there is no stochasticity to capture clothing drape/hair/background uncertainty. Stage-II removes noise entirely.
- Background handling remains ad hoc. While the mask helps, background artifacts remain; there is no compositing/segmentation-based solution or explicit background branch.
- Architecture details: the FC bottleneck in G1 at 256×256 is heavy and may unnecessarily break spatial correspondence; indeed, G2 removes it, but an ablation on FC vs. fully-conv for G1 would help.
- Limited comparisons. The only related baseline is viewpoint-conditioned VariGAN; more contemporary pose-guided warping/deformation baselines (e.g., flow-based/appearance flow, TPS/part-based warping, perceptual loss variants) are missing.
- Some clarity/formatting issues: figures and references have typos/inconsistencies; λ effects are deferred to the supplement; details about the pose mask construction could be expanded.

Quality
Technically sound and well-executed for the proposed setting. The two-stage design, masked L1, and pairwise adversarial training are reasonable and backed by ablations. The main gaps are in evaluation rigor (perceptual and identity metrics) and breadth of baselines. Nonetheless, the empirical evidence supports the core claims.

Clarity
Generally clear and readable, with helpful figures and qualitative examples. Some reference formatting and minor typos detract. The method description could benefit from more specifics on mask generation and λ sensitivity (currently in the supplement).

Originality
High on problem framing and control interface; moderate on components. Using keypoint heatmaps as explicit pose control with a two-stage, difference-map refinement is a meaningful and novel combination at the time. The mask loss is a targeted but modest contribution.

Significance
High potential. Explicit pose-conditioned, identity-preserving generation is a compelling direction with practical impact. Even if some components are incremental, the overall system provides a clear step toward controllable human synthesis and is likely to inspire follow-on work.

Suggestions/questions for authors
- Report FID and LPIPS (masked and unmasked), and add a stronger user study (pairwise preference, unlimited viewing).
- Quantify identity preservation via re-ID/face-ID scores across poses; evaluate utility for data augmentation (e.g., train re-ID or pose estimators with generated data).
- Explore multi-modal outputs (inject noise at stage II, stochastic residuals, or diversity losses).
- Consider explicit foreground/background disentanglement (segmentation-guided compositing) to reduce artifacts.
- Provide ablations on the FC layer in G1 and on the difference-map vs. direct refinement.
- Analyze robustness to pose estimation errors and missing keypoints.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper proposes a bold, clearly defined task and a pragmatic, effective solution that enables explicit control via pose. Despite some evaluation and baseline gaps, the novelty and demonstrated potential outweigh the weaknesses. I expect this to stimulate further research on controllable person synthesis.

Confidence
High.
