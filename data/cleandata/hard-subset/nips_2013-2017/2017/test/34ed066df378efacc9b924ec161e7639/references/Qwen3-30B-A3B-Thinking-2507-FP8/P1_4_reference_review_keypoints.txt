- Introduces the novel task of pose-guided person image generation: synthesize a person in a target pose from a reference image and pose.
- Proposes a two-stage framework (PG2): Stage I generates a coarse pose-aligned image; Stage II refines appearance via a conditional GAN producing a difference map.
- Introduces a pose mask loss to mitigate background interference during pose transfer.
- Uses difference maps in Stage II to focus refinement and speed convergence instead of generating images from scratch.
- Demonstrates technical soundness with experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion using SSIM, Inception Score, qualitative results, and AMT user studies.
- Ablation studies effectively show contributions of pose embedding, mask loss, and the two-stage design.
- Paper is very well written, clear, and organized with reproducible method details and helpful figures.
- Novelty: explicit pose-controlled generation, pose mask loss, and the two-stage/difference map adaptation of GANs are meaningful contributions.
- Significance: practical applications (film, virtual try-on, data augmentation) and a principled approach for controlling pose vs appearance.
- Strengths: clear formulation, well-motivated design, strong quantitative and qualitative results, thorough ablations, and identified limitations.
- Weaknesses: no comparison to later GAN advances (e.g., StyleGAN) and limited discussion of pose-estimation robustness.
- Failure cases (rare poses, gender imbalance) are shown but not deeply analyzed or remedied.
- Recommendation: accept — strong, well-executed paper with meaningful contributions and potential practical impact.
