- Proposal: Two-stage framework for pose-guided person image generation (Stage-I U-Net with pose heatmaps for coarse structure; Stage-II conditional GAN for detail refinement), evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with improvements over baselines.

- Strength: Defines a novel, practical task with applications in fashion, entertainment, and data augmentation.

- Strength: Two-stage decomposition is well-motivated and empirically improves results over single-stage models.

- Strength: Pose heatmap embedding is an effective pose representation, outperforming coordinate-based alternatives in ablation.

- Strength: Pose mask loss focuses generation on the human body and materially improves output quality.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation (SSIM, Inception Score, user study) on two diverse datasets demonstrates robustness.

- Weakness: Paper lacks theoretical justification for why the two-stage decomposition is necessary or optimal.

- Weakness: Pose mask computation is described only vaguely ("connect parts" + morphological ops) without details or diagrams.

- Weakness: Failure analysis is limited; causes (e.g., rare poses) are asserted but not quantified.

- Weakness: Mechanism for preserving person identity across pose transfer is not clearly explained.

- Weakness: No comparison to recent conditional GAN approaches (e.g., StyleGAN) or other advanced conditioning methods.

- Critical assessment: Empirically strong contribution with a valuable pose mask technique, but needs deeper analysis of why the method works and how broadly the mask loss generalizes.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Pro-accept: Novel task, effective two-stage design, strong empirical gains, useful pose mask loss, thorough evaluation, clear presentation.

- Con-accept: Requires stronger theoretical rationale, clearer pose mask details, clarification of identity preservation, and comparisons to state-of-the-art conditional GAN methods.

- Suggested revisions: Provide pose mask computation details/diagram, clarify identity-preservation strategy, offer theoretical discussion of the two-stage benefit, and add comparisons to recent conditional generation models.
