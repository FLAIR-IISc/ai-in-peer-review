- Problem/method: conditional person image synthesis via PG2 — Stage-I U-Net+FC bottleneck with pose-masked L1 produces coarse result, Stage-II GAN predicts a residual difference map to refine details.

- Strength: well-motivated and timely task with clear applications (editing, data augmentation, re-ID).

- Strength: sensible coarse-to-fine two-stage design, residual prediction, skip connections, and pairwise conditional discriminator.

- Strength: informative ablations on pose embedding, loss variants, and one-stage vs two-stage training.

- Strength: practical pose-masked L1 to reduce background mismatch and discriminator pairing with IA to avoid trivial copying.

- Strength: convincing qualitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 and a supporting user study for DeepFashion.

- Weakness: quantitative evaluation relies on SSIM and Inception Score, which poorly reflect perceptual realism for person images and masked crops.

- Weakness: no quantitative identity/appearance preservation metrics (e.g., re-ID rank-1/mAP or feature distances) despite identity being central to the task.

- Weakness: claim that predicting a difference map yields faster convergence is unsubstantiated (no training curves or time-to-quality plots).

- Weakness: Stage-II (and the discriminator) are not explicitly conditioned on the target pose PB, risking pose drift; no keypoint/pose-error analysis on outputs is provided.

- Weakness: pose-derived foreground mask construction lacks detail (connectivity, dilation) and robustness to noisy keypoints is not analyzed.

- Weakness: fully connected bottleneck in G1 breaks spatial equivariance and adds parameters; no ablation comparing with/without FC or alternatives.

- Clarity issue: important hyperparameters missing (λ values for masked L1 at Stage-II, mask construction specifics, loss weighting), and some figure captions/cross-references are terse.

- Clarity issue: user study protocol lacks details on randomization, per-user variance, and inter-rater agreement.

- Originality: combination of conditioning on reference image and pose heatmaps plus two-stage residual GAN is a reasonable practical contribution, but relation to warping/flow-based pose transfer work is under-discussed.

- Significance: method shows qualitative gains and potential for downstream tasks, but limited perceptual and identity evaluations weaken claims about utility for re-ID/editing.

- Limitation: deterministic single-modal output; no treatment of multimodality for ambiguous or occluded cases.

- Suggested improvement: add identity preservation evaluation using a pre-trained re-ID network (feature distance or retrieval metrics).

- Suggested improvement: replace/augment IS with FID on masked person crops and LPIPS, and report keypoint error on generated images.

- Suggested improvement: provide training curves/time-to-quality to support convergence claims and ablate the FC bottleneck.

- Suggested improvement: consider conditioning D and G2 on PB and/or use perceptual (VGG) losses focused on clothing/body regions.

- Suggested improvement: explore feature warping/flow-based modules to better preserve high-frequency textures.

- Minor fixes: clarify post-processing/test-time augmentation, complete bibliographic entries, fix typos and formatting glitches.

- Overall assessment: promising and pragmatic work; lean toward acceptance if identity-preserving evaluations and stronger perceptual metrics are added, otherwise borderline.
