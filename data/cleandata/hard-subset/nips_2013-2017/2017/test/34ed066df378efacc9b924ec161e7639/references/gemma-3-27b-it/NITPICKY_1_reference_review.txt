## Review of Pose Guided Person Image Generation

This paper proposes a two-stage generative model for transferring a person's pose in an image, conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose defined by keypoints. Stage-I generates a coarse result capturing global structure, and Stage-II refines it with a GAN to produce sharper, more detailed images. The authors explore pose embedding methods, a novel mask loss for focusing on the human body, and compare one-stage and two-stage approaches.

The core idea of explicitly conditioning generation on pose is a reasonable and potentially useful contribution, given the applications outlined in the introduction (movie making, data augmentation for pose estimation). However, the execution and presentation are plagued by a multitude of issues, ranging from minor formatting inconsistencies to concerns about experimental rigor and clarity. While the idea holds promise, the current form falls short of NIPS standards.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty of Task:** The specific task of pose-guided person image generation, conditioning on both reference image *and* pose, is a distinct formulation.
* **Divide-and-Conquer Strategy:** The two-stage approach is a sensible way to tackle the complexity of the problem.
* **Pose Mask Loss:** The introduction of a mask loss to focus on the human body is a reasonable idea to mitigate background artifacts.
* **Quantitative and Qualitative Results:** The authors provide both quantitative (SSIM, IS) and qualitative results, including comparisons with a baseline.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Writing:** The paper suffers from numerous instances of unclear writing. Sentences are often convoluted and lack precision. For example, the phrase "Our generation sequence (refined results)" in the introduction is vague and uninformative. The flow between paragraphs feels disjointed.
* **Technical Details Lacking Rigor:** While the overall architecture is described, crucial details are missing. The specific number of filters in each convolutional layer, the activation functions used, and the exact network architectures are not fully specified, hindering reproducibility. “N residual blocks” is insufficient detail.
* **Experimental Setup Concerns:**  The experimental settings raise questions. The choice of datasets seems somewhat arbitrary. The ablation studies lack a systematic evaluation.  The user study, while attempted, is poorly described and the interpretation of results seems questionable given reported low G2R scores on Market-1501.  Why were different batch sizes used for different stages?
* **Mask SSIM:** The justification for introducing mask-SSIM is weak. If the goal is to evaluate *person* image generation, why not simply crop the images to the bounding box of the person before calculating SSIM?
* **Inconsistent Formatting and Style:** Several minor inconsistencies exist (e.g., using “Eq. 1” but not consistently applying this to all equations). The citation style appears inconsistent. “e.g.” is used in some places but not others.
* **Related Work Coverage:** While the related work section mentions relevant papers, it feels somewhat superficial. The connection to recent advances in image manipulation and pose transfer, beyond those cited, is not adequately discussed. The authors claim that the condition on pose is more expressive than previous works, but fail to convincingly demonstrate why.
* **Lack of Error Analysis:** The discussion of failure cases is limited. A more detailed analysis of common failure modes and their causes would be valuable. Why does the model struggle with complex poses or gender inconsistencies?
* **Overstated Claims:**  The claim of generating "photo-realistic" results is often not borne out by the qualitative examples. The generated images, while improved, still exhibit noticeable artifacts and inconsistencies.
* **Minor Issues:** A "low-resolution person re-identification dataset" is mentioned but its resolution isn’t specified. The notation “G2R” and “R2G” in the user study section are not defined until they appear in the results, disrupting reading flow.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

* The task is novel, and the proposed approach offers a potential solution.
* The two-stage architecture provides a reasonable framework for tackling the problem.
* The paper presents some experimental validation, although it is limited.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

* The paper suffers from significant clarity and technical detail issues, hindering reproducibility and understanding.
* The experimental evaluation is incomplete and lacks a thorough analysis.
* The writing quality and formatting are subpar.
* The claims of generating "photo-realistic" images are often overstated.
* The lack of robust error analysis limits the insights gained.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting idea but is currently not of sufficient quality for NIPS. The numerous shortcomings in clarity, technical detail, and experimental rigor outweigh the novelty of the proposed task. I recommend **rejection**. The authors should significantly revise the paper, addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, they need to provide more detailed technical specifications, conduct more thorough experiments, improve the writing quality, and tone down overly ambitious claims.  A more rigorous error analysis would also be beneficial. Addressing these issues would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential for acceptance in a future submission.
