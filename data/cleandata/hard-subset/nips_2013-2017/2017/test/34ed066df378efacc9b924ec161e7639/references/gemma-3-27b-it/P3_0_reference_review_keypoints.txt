- Overall: Novel two-stage pose-transfer (U-Net for structure + conditional GAN for refinement), good visuals, weak quantitative evaluation; recommend Accept with minor revisions.
- Task & approach: Generates person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose using a two-stage pipeline with explicit pose representation.
- Related work: Covers VAEs/GANs/autoregressive and conditional generation; positions work vs. Zhao et al. [36] well but could cite more pose-estimation advances.
- Pose representation: Uses keypoint heatmaps to embed pose; choice could be compared to coordinate-based embeddings.
- Strength — Novelty: Conditioning on both a reference image and explicit pose is a distinct and valuable contribution.
- Strength — Two-stage design: Separating global structure and detail refinement is sensible and effective.
- Strength — Pose mask loss: The pose mask loss focuses the generator on relevant regions and reduces background artifacts.
- Strength — Qualitative results: Generated images show strong realism and pose accuracy compared to baseline.
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is clear, well-structured, and accompanied by helpful figures.
- Weakness — Quantitative evaluation: Current metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) are unconvincing; recommend FID and pose-specific metrics.
- Weakness — Ablation: Limited analysis of Stage-II GAN architecture and loss weighting (λ); more thorough ablations needed.
- Weakness — Dataset/generalization: Experiments limited to Market-1501 and DeepFashion, raising concerns about generalization to diverse poses/clothing.
- Weakness — Failure analysis: Failure cases are shown but lack systematic investigation, especially for large pose differences.
- Weakness — Comparisons: Limited comparison to a single related work; needs benchmarking against more recent SOTA methods.
- Pro for acceptance: Novel problem, well-motivated framework, strong visuals, clear writing.
- Con for acceptance: Weak quantitative evaluation, limited ablation, dataset bias, and sparse SOTA comparisons.
- Questions for authors: Why heatmaps vs coordinates; computational cost of two-stage vs single-stage; degradation with increasing pose difference; extension to other object categories; plan to include metrics like FID.
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on metrics, ablations, and broader evaluation.
