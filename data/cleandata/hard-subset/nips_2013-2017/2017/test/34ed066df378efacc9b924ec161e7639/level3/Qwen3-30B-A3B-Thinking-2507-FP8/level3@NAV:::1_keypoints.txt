- Proposes a novel task: pose-guided person image generation from a reference image and target pose heatmaps.  
- Introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: Stage I (U-Net-like) integrates pose and appearance; Stage II refines via adversarial training on a difference map.  
- Novel pose mask loss focuses learning on human body regions and is well-motivated and experimentally validated.  
- Provides extensive ablation studies on pose embeddings and loss functions.  
- Shows strong qualitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  
- Two-stage design effectively separates global structure learning from detail refinement.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak, relying on SSIM and Inception Score that do not match perceptual quality.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; a more rigorous human evaluation is needed.  
- Fails to cite relevant iterative refinement works (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) that relate to the two-stage approach.  
- Claims that residual blocks improve performance but provides no specific metrics or qualitative evidence.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing evaluation weaknesses (stronger human study) and adding missing related work.
