- Introduces a novel task: pose-guided person image generation, with a two-stage PG2 network.  
- Core idea: transfer a person's appearance from a reference image to a target pose represented by heatmaps.  
- Architecture: U-Net-like stage for initial pose integration followed by a conditional DCGAN for refinement.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, solution, and experiments.  
- Builds on GANs and conditional generation, distinguishing itself by explicit pose heatmap conditioning.  
- Related work should additionally cite iterative refinement methods (e.g., Carreira, Newell) for coarse-to-fine context.  
- Strengths: task novelty, clear two-stage design, and extensive ablation studies on design choices.  
- Qualitative results are strong; pose mask loss sensibly focuses generation on the human body.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak; SSIM and Inception Score may not correlate with perceptual quality.  
- Lacks suitable metrics for pose-transfer quality; perceptual/human studies are needed for objective comparison.  
- Unclear how residual blocks concretely improve results—needs metrics or qualitative evidence (sharper images, fewer artifacts, faster convergence).  
- Stage 1 outputs are blurred; suggest training Stage 2 adversarially on Stage 1 outputs to reduce blur.  
- Fully connected layer in Stage 1 may cause information loss; recommend testing removal or replacement with convolutional layers.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and methodological soundness despite weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task/approach, clear architecture, extensive experiments, promising visuals.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak quantitative evaluation, limited baseline comparisons, unclear benefits of residual blocks.  
- Rebuttal note: preliminary user study is unconvincing; replace with substantive human evaluation rating realism and pose accuracy.
