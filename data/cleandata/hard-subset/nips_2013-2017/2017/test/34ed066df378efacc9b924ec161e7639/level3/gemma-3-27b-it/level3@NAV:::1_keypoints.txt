- Introduces a novel task: generate person images in arbitrary target poses from a reference image and pose heatmaps, and proposes a two-stage coarse-to-fine PG2 network.  
- Architecture: U-Net-like Stage 1 for initial pose integration and a conditional DCGAN Stage 2 for refinement, plus a mask loss to focus on person appearance.  
- Positions work within GAN and conditional generation literature, uniquely focusing on direct pose manipulation and controllable image synthesis.  
- Strength: clear motivation, well-defined task, and strong experimental analysis of design choices via ablation studies.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of pose embedding methods and the proposed mask loss; writing is clear and well-organized.  
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is weak—SSIM and Inception Score are insufficient for assessing pose-guided image quality.  
- Weakness: user study is preliminary and unconvincing; a more robust human evaluation is needed.  
- Weakness: observable blurring/artifacts, especially with Stage1+GAN; investigate whether the intermediate fully connected layer causes this.  
- Suggestion: consider adversarially training Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs to address blurring and improve refinement.  
- Suggestion: expand related work to include recent iterative refinement approaches (Carreira et al., 2016; Newell et al., 2016).  
- Acceptance argument: novelty of task and proposed approach plus solid analysis of design decisions make it a valuable contribution.  
- Rejection argument: weak quantitative metrics, preliminary user study, and visible artifacts could undermine impact.  
- Overall: recommendation to accept as a promising, novel contribution that would benefit from the suggested improvements.
