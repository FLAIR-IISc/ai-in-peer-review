- Proposes pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose to preserve identity while changing pose.  
- Introduces a novel two-stage architecture (Pose Integration followed by Image Refinement) explicitly conditioning on pose heatmaps.  
- Uses pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background mismatch.  
- Builds on GANs and U-Net but contributes a specific two-stage pipeline and pose-focused loss.  
- Strong experimental validation and comprehensive ablation studies examining pose embeddings and loss choices.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: relies on SSIM and Inception Score, which may not correlate with visual quality.  
- User study is preliminary and lacks statistical significance and methodological rigor.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and supported by helpful figures.  
- Insufficient explanation of how residual blocks improve generation (which metrics or qualitative effects).  
- Core contribution is original: two-stage pose-conditioned generation differs from prior coarse-pose or text-based methods.  
- Related work should better incorporate recent iterative refinement techniques (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).  
- Demonstrates convincing qualitative results and beats related work [36] in visuals, but significance limited by weak quantitative comparisons.  
- Clear application potential in filmmaking, pose estimation, and image retrieval.  
- Recommendation: accept, but strengthen quantitative metrics (e.g., FID) and perform a statistically valid human evaluation for the camera-ready version.
