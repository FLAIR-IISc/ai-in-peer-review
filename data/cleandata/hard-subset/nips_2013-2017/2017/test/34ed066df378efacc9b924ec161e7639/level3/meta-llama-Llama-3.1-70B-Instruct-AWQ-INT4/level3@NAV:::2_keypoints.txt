- Proposal: Introduces PG2, a two-stage pose-guided person image generation method conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Strength: Paper is well-organized and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Coarse-to-fine two-stage strategy effectively separates global structure capture from appearance refinement.  
- Strength: Difference-map refinement accelerates convergence and improves final image quality.  
- Weakness: Generated images contain visible artifacts (e.g., black dots).  
- Weakness: Image quality remains insufficient for practical application.  
- Weakness: Limited perceived significance—may be viewed as another conditional GAN variant.  
- Originality: Defines a novel task of reference-image-and-pose-conditioned generation.  
- Originality: Two-stage architecture and difference-map idea are novel contributions.  
- Related work: Thorough literature review covering VAEs, GANs, and autoregressive models and their limitations.  
- Experiments: Extensive qualitative and quantitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 showing promising, pose-correct outputs.  
- Conclusion: Promising approach but needs work on artifact removal and improving image fidelity.  
- Recommendation: Recommend accepting the paper despite image quality issues.
