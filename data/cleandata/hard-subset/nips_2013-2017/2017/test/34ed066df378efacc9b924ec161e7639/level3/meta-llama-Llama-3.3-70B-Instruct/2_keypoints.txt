- Proposes a novel two-step adversarial generative network that refines a coarse image via a stacked architecture.  
- Generator predicts a difference map to improve image details in a refinement stage.  
- Conditions generation on separate appearance and pose maps for controllable human image synthesis.  
- Evaluated on the DeepFashion dataset to demonstrate results.  
- Strength: technical novelty in stacked architecture and difference-map refinement.  
- Strength: effectively leverages generation strategy to produce higher-resolution outputs.  
- Weakness: generated image quality suffers from visible artifacts and needs further refinement.  
- Weakness: limited perceived significance—may be viewed as another GAN variant tied to vision/graphics.  
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments; paper is clear and well-organized.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques that materially differs from prior work.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art for human image generation but has a narrow application scope.  
- Recommendation: suitable for publication if authors address image-quality issues to improve visual consistency.
