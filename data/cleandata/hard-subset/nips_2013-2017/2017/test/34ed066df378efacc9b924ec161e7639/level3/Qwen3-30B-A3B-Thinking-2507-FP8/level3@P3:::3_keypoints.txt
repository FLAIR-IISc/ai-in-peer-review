- Paper proposes a two-stage GAN (U-Net stage-I for pose integration, DCGAN stage-II for refinement) for pose-guided person image generation.
- Builds on standard GAN techniques; application-focused rather than introducing new GAN methods.
- Clear writing and well-organized presentation with thorough ablation studies.
- Task is well-motivated with practical applications (movies, data generation, HCI).
- Some qualitative results (especially on DeepFashion) show reasonable pose transfer and appearance details.
- Lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation and adequate human studies; reported SSIM and IS are insufficient and potentially misleading.
- Visual quality is poor overall, with artifacts, incorrect body parts, color mismatches, and blurring, especially on Market-1501.
- Claims of "photo-realistic" or "high-quality" results are overstated and not supported by the figures.
- Comparison to related work (e.g., [36]) is superficial and not directly comparable due to different problem settings.
- Failure cases are shown but not analyzed to explain causes or possible fixes.
- Evaluation: technically sound but undermined by weak evaluation and poor outputs; clarity is high but results are unconvincing.
- Originality is limited to applying known architectures to a new task rather than novel methodology.
- Significance is limited by lack of demonstrated improvement or impact on the field.
- Recommendation: reject; paper needs significant additional work before a top-tier venue submission.
- Suggested Fixes: conduct comprehensive user studies measuring realism, provide higher-quality/larger visuals, analyze failure modes, and temper claims about result quality.
