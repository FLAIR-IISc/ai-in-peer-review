Proposes Pose-Guided Person Generation (PG2): a two-stage conditional framework to synthesize person images in new poses using a reference image and 18-keypoint heatmaps.  
Stage-I: U-Net encoder–decoder with skip connections and masked L1 loss produces a coarse global-structure output.  
Stage-II: adversarial U-Net predicts a residual/difference map conditioned on Stage-I output and reference; discriminator enforces identity consistency.  
Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with ablations on pose embeddings, loss masking, and one-stage vs two-stage training.  
Strength: Clear task setup with explicit pose control, relevant for editing and data augmentation.  
Strength: Sensible modular coarse-to-fine architecture; residual refinement is a practical choice that stabilizes training.  
Strength: Simple, effective explicit pose encoding via concatenated heatmaps.  
Strength: Masked loss appropriately de-emphasizes background changes for cross-camera settings.  
Weakness: Limited and unconvincing quantitative evaluation; SSIM/IS are unreliable for perceptual quality and no perceptual/task-based evaluations (e.g., re-id).  
Weakness: Visual quality uneven with artifacts (color bleeding, distorted limbs/hands, background leakage); figures too small to judge.  
Weakness: Contributions are largely incremental—combination of known components with only a minor novelty in residual refinement.  
Weakness: Narrow comparisons—no strong image-to-image baselines or identity-preserving methods evaluated.  
Weakness: Missing analyses of pose estimator failures, occlusion handling, identity preservation, and diversity/uncertainty.  
Quality: Technically sound architecture and training choices (masked L1, pairwise discriminator) but insufficient evidence for “high-quality” claims.  
Clarity: Writing is clear and reproducible, but figures are too small and failure cases/limitations under-discussed.  
Originality: Moderate—pose heatmaps and coarse-to-fine adversarial strategies are known; residual map is a pragmatic tweak.  
Significance: Potentially useful, but practical impact limited by weak empirical validation and lack of task-based proof.  
Pros for acceptance: clear problem definition, simple modular approach, some ablations, and a potentially useful baseline.  
Cons for acceptance: unconvincing visuals, weak quantitative evidence, missing identity evaluation, and incremental novelty.  
Suggestions: add rigorous human perceptual studies, re-id/task-based metrics (rank-1/mAP), FID/semantic/person-region metrics, keypoint error, stronger baselines, better figures, and identity-preserving losses.  
Post-rebuttal: Reviewer remains unconvinced; rebuttal did not address core concerns about evaluation and identity metrics.  
Recommendation: Reject—promising direction but insufficient empirical evidence and novelty for a top venue.
