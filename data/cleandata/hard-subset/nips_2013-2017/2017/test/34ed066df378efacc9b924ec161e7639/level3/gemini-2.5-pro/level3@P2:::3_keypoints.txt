- Paper introduces PG², a two-stage model (coarse U-Net with pose-heatmap + pose mask L1 loss, then conditional GAN refinement) for single-image person reposing on DeepFashion and Market-1501.
- Strength: Tackles a challenging, practically relevant problem (data augmentation, virtual try-on, content creation).
- Strength: Methodologically sound coarse-to-fine design decomposing global pose and local appearance, using U-Nets and conditional GANs.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-organized with motivations explained.
- Strength: Ablation studies validate design choices (pose heatmap embedding, pose mask loss).
- Weakness: Visual results fall short of claims—images show artifacts, blur, distorted faces, lost clothing patterns, and odd anatomies; figures are small and hard to inspect.
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is unconvincing—SSIM is misleading and user study shows low realism (14.9% on DeepFashion, 7.4% on Market-1501).
- Weakness: Technical novelty is limited—approach mainly composes existing components (U-Nets, conditional GANs, L1/adversarial loss).
- For acceptance: Addresses a novel, hard problem with an intuitive two-stage framework and informative ablations.
- Against acceptance: Low visual quality and user-study-supported lack of realism, plus limited technical novelty make it premature for a top-tier venue.
- Conclusion: Promising direction but overstated claims and insufficient results; needs significant improvement before publication at a premier conference.
