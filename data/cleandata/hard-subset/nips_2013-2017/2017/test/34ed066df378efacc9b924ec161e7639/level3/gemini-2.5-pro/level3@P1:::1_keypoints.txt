- Task introduced: synthesize an image of a specific person in a new target pose from a single source image.  
- Method (PG2): two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline — G1 (U-Net-like) produces a coarse pose-conditioned rendering using pose heatmaps and a masked L1 loss; G2 (U-Net-like) refines output with an adversarial loss for detail.  
- Datasets: experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501.  

Strengths:
- Originality/significance: novel, well-defined problem with clear applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, content creation).  
- Clarity/quality: paper is well-written; two-stage design and use of pose heatmaps and masked L1 loss are well-motivated.  
- Thorough experiments: extensive ablation studies (pose embeddings, pose mask loss, two-stage vs one-stage) provide insight.  
- Qualitative results: strong visual results on DeepFashion; Stage 2 clearly sharpens and adds detail over Stage 1.  

Weaknesses:
- Artifacts remain: generated images contain noticeable artifacts (blurry textures, distorted faces, inconsistent backgrounds), especially on Market-1501.  
- Weak quantitative evaluation: SSIM/IS are unreliable for perceptual quality; proposed mask-SSIM/mask-IS are non-standard and don't fully address metric shortcomings.  
- Limited user study: MTurk real-vs-fake with 1s exposure is insufficient to evaluate pose accuracy, identity preservation, or realism against baselines.  

Detailed comments / questions:
- Related work: connect the two-stage approach to coarse-to-fine/iterative refinement literature (e.g., stacked hourglass, iterative error feedback).  
- Role of residual blocks: clarify whether residual blocks improved results qualitatively or via specific metrics and provide evidence.  
- Blurring in one-stage model: consider ablation to test whether the FC layer in G1 causes a bottleneck (compare one-stage fully convolutional vs current designs) to disentangle architecture vs training-stage effects.
