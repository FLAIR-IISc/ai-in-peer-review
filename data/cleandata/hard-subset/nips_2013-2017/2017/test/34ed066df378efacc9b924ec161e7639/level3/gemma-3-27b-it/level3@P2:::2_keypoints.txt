- Proposes PG2: a two-stage pose-guided person image generation network (U-Net-like initial stage + DCGAN-based difference-map refinement).
- Task: synthesize a person image in a target pose given an input image and pose; evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion.
- Problem is relevant for controllable image generation and applications like virtual try-on and data augmentation.
- Positions work within generative modeling literature and differentiates via explicit pose conditioning.
- Strength: intuitive coarse-to-fine decomposition (pose integration then detail refinement).
- Strength: predicting a difference map is a clever refinement that focuses on corrections and may speed convergence.
- Strength: pose mask loss helps reduce background influence during training.
- Strength: clear writing and extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two datasets.
- Weakness: generated images show visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details, limiting practical applicability.
- Weakness: reported metrics (SSIM, IS) and user study do not fully capture perceptual shortcomings.
- Weakness: approach is somewhat incremental—similar ideas (stacked generators, difference maps) exist (e.g., StackGAN); novelty/significance limited.
- Weakness: impact of pose estimation errors is not analyzed; robustness to noisy poses is not evaluated.
- Acceptance arguments: relevant problem, technically sound two-stage method, pose mask loss, promising experimental results, well-presented paper.
- Rejection arguments: image quality issues, limited improvement over existing conditional GANs, insufficient analysis of pose-estimator sensitivity, incremental contribution.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions—authors should improve image quality, clarify novelty vs. stacked GANs, and analyze sensitivity to pose estimation errors.
