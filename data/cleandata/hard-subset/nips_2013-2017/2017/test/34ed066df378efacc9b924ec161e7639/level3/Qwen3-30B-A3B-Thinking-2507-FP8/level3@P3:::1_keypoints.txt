- Novel task: generate person images in arbitrary poses by combining input appearance with target pose heatmaps.  
- Two-stage architecture: Stage-I generates a coarse pose-aligned image; Stage-II refines it with adversarial training for sharper details.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to reduce background interference.  
- Distinct contribution from prior conditional generation work by explicitly conditioning on pose heatmaps.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear methodology and illustrative figures.  
- Related work connection is good but should include iterative refinement methods (e.g., Carreira et al., Newell et al.).  
- Comprehensive experiments and ablation studies on pose embeddings, losses, and architectures using DeepFashion and Market-1501.  
- Quantitative evaluation weak: SSIM and Inception Score do not correlate well with perceptual quality.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; needs a larger, better-designed human evaluation.  
- Clarity issues: need to clarify implementation details and explicitly show benefits of residual blocks.  
- Observed artifact: blurring in Stage-I+GAN results, possibly due to the intermediate fully connected layer, needs investigation.  
- Significance: practical applications in entertainment, fashion, and pose-estimation data augmentation; advances pose transfer.  
- Strengths: novel task, effective coarse-to-fine design, thorough ablations, clear presentation, and promising qualitative results.  
- Weaknesses: weak perceptual metrics, insufficient human evaluation, unclear residual-block impact, unexplained blurring artifacts.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with promising results despite evaluation shortcomings.  
- Suggested improvements: stronger human studies; quantify residual block benefits; investigate blurring causes; add iterative refinement citations; try adversarial training of Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs.
