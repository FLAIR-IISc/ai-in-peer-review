This paper proposes a novel task of Pose Guided Person Image Generation, where the goal is to generate images of a person in an arbitrary pose, conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. The authors introduce a two-stage coarse-to-fine network architecture, which first captures the global structure of the person and then refines the appearance details. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

The proposed method is novel and addresses a challenging task in computer vision. The use of a two-stage approach is effective in generating high-quality images that are both photo-realistic and pose-wise correct. The authors provide extensive experimental results on two person datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel task and a well-designed two-stage network architecture.
* The use of a pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes.
* The extensive experimental results on two person datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are some weaknesses and suggestions for improvement:

* The quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to the lack of suitable metrics. The authors should consider using more robust metrics to evaluate the quality of the generated images.
* The user study is preliminary and unconvincing. The authors should consider conducting a more comprehensive user study to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.
* The authors should clarify how the residual blocks improve the generation performance and provide more details on the effect of the fully connected layer on the generation results.
* The authors should consider adding recent iterative refinement related work (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) to the related work section.
* The authors should experiment with training Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs adversarially to see if that reduces blurring.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of this paper based on its novelty and approach. However, the authors should address the above-mentioned weaknesses and suggestions for improvement to strengthen their paper.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel task and a well-designed two-stage network architecture.
* The method is effective in generating high-quality images that are both photo-realistic and pose-wise correct.
* The authors provide extensive experimental results on two person datasets.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The quantitative evaluation is weak.
* The user study is preliminary and unconvincing.
* The authors should clarify how the residual blocks improve the generation performance.
* The authors should consider adding recent iterative refinement related work to the related work section.
