- Introduces a new task: generate a photorealistic image of a person in an arbitrary target pose from a single source appearance image and a target pose representation.  
- Proposes PG2, a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (coarse-to-fine) for the task.  
- Stage I: U-Net-like generator produces a coarse image capturing global structure and color; introduces a pose mask loss to focus on the foreground person.  
- Stage II: U-Net-like adversarial generator predicts a residual/difference map added to the coarse image to refine details; discriminator is conditioned on the source image.  
- Builds on conditional GANs and multi-stage generative models but uses an explicit pose+appearance conditioning scheme for more structured control.  
- Strength: Novel and significant task with valuable applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, content creation).  
- Strength: Well-motivated two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture; residual refinement is effective for focusing on corrections and stabilizing training.  
- Strength: Clear writing and thorough evaluation (DeepFashion, ablations, pose embeddings, pose mask loss, quantitative metrics SSIM/IS, user study).  
- Weakness: Final image quality has visible artifacts and lacks fine high-frequency detail, limiting photorealism.  
- Weakness: Core components are adaptations of existing techniques (stacked/multi-stage GANs, U-Nets), so architectural novelty is limited.  
- Weakness: Practical utility is constrained by image quality and the approach may be viewed as incremental application of known methods.  
- Overall: Solid, well-designed baseline for a novel task with promising results; recommended for acceptance despite realism limitations.
