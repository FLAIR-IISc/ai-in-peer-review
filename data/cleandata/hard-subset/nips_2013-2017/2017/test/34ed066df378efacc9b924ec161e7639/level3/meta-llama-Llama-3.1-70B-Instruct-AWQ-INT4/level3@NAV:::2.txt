**Paper Review: Pose Guided Person Image Generation**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach for generating person images in arbitrary poses, conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. The authors introduce a two-stage framework, Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), which first captures the global structure of the person and then refines the appearance details through adversarial training. The proposed method is evaluated on two person datasets, DeepFashion and Market-1501, and demonstrates promising results in generating photo-realistic and pose-wise correct images.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-organized paper**: The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their work, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. **Coarse-to-fine strategy**: The two-stage approach is effective in addressing the challenging task of pose transfer, allowing the model to focus on global structure and appearance details separately.
3. **Difference-map idea**: The use of a difference map to refine the initial generation result is a useful refinement, speeding up convergence and improving image quality.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Generated images contain visible artifacts**: The authors acknowledge that the generated images still contain visible artifacts, such as black dots, which need to be addressed.
2. **Image quality still needs improvement**: While the proposed method shows promising results, the image quality is not yet suitable for application use.
3. **Significance concern**: The approach may be viewed as another conditional GAN variant with limited broad appeal.

**Originality and Contribution**

1. **Task novelty**: The paper introduces a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and a target pose.
2. **Technique**: The proposed two-stage architecture and difference-map idea are novel contributions to the field.

**Related Work**

The authors provide a thorough review of related work in generative image modeling, including Variational Autoencoders, Generative Adversarial Networks, and Autoregressive models. They also discuss the limitations of existing methods and how their approach addresses these limitations.

**Experiments and Results**

The authors conduct extensive experiments on two person datasets, DeepFashion and Market-1501, and provide both qualitative and quantitative results. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating photo-realistic and pose-wise correct images.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, this paper proposes a novel approach for generating person images in arbitrary poses, conditioned on a reference image and a target pose. While the proposed method shows promising results, there are still areas for improvement, such as addressing visible artifacts and improving image quality. Overall, the paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work and extensive experimental results.

**Recommendation**

Based on the review, I recommend accepting this paper, despite the quality issues, as it presents a novel approach and demonstrates promising results in a challenging task.
