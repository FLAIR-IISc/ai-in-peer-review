- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) that synthesizes person images from a reference image and target pose using a two-stage generative model.  
- Stage-I: U-Net generates a coarse pose-conditioned image; Stage-II: conditional DCGAN predicts a residual/difference map to refine details.  
- Introduces a tailored pose mask loss to focus the model on the human body region and reduce background artifacts.  
- Situates work within conditional image generation literature and relates to stacked-GAN approaches like StackGAN.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and sensible divide-and-conquer two-stage design.  
- Strength: using a difference map for refinement is an effective training strategy to produce sharper details.  
- Strength: mask loss demonstrably encourages appearance transfer on the person while preserving background.  
- Strength: quantitative and qualitative results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion plus ablation studies strengthen the evaluation.  
- Weakness: generated images show noticeable artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (e.g., black dots, lack of textures).  
- Weakness: overall image quality lags state-of-the-art, limiting practical applicability despite pose consistency.  
- Weakness: core technical contributions are incremental (stacked generators and conditional GANs are evolutions, not fundamental innovations).  
- Weakness: user study highlights a gap between quantitative metrics and human perception of image quality.  
- Originality: novel in explicitly conditioning on both appearance and pose, but relies on established building blocks.  
- Significance: demonstrates a feasible approach with potential applications (virtual try-on, movie making, data augmentation), but image realism needs improvement for broad adoption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/interesting problem, technically sound method, convincing experiments and ablation studies, clear writing.  
- Arguments for rejection: visible artifacts and missing high-frequency details, perceived incremental contribution, substantial improvement needed for practical use.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising; recommend acceptance with minor revisions requesting strategies to improve image fidelity (e.g., higher resolution, better losses, alternative architectures).
