- Introduces PG2: a two-stage, coarse-to-fine network to synthesize a target-pose image of a person from one source image using keypoint heatmaps; G1 generates a coarse image with a pose-mask L1 loss, G2 predicts a residual and is trained adversarially with a source-conditioned discriminator.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501.  
- Clarity & organization: paper is very well-written with clear motivation, diagrams, and explanations.  
- Technical approach: sensible coarse-to-fine decomposition; residual prediction in G2 effectively focuses refinement; U-Net + GAN paradigm is appropriate.  
- Novelty: defines a new, practically relevant task (pose-guided person synthesis) and uses explicit pose conditioning as a strong signal.  
- Thorough evaluation: includes ablation studies, quantitative metrics (SSIM, IS) and a user study to validate design choices.  
- Weakness — image quality: generated images exhibit artifacts, blurred textures, and poorly rendered faces/hands, limiting photorealism.  
- Incremental components: core elements (U-Nets, GANs, stacked generators) are adaptations of existing techniques; pose-mask loss and residual map are useful but incremental.  
- Limited broader significance: impact may be mostly within human image synthesis rather than advancing general generative modeling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-defined problem; sound, well-motivated method; clear writing and solid experimental validation providing a strong baseline.  
- Arguments against acceptance: noticeable image-quality issues and methodological novelty perceived as incremental.  
- Overall: a strong first step and valuable baseline for pose-guided person generation despite quality limitations and incremental technical contributions.
