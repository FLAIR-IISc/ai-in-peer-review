- Task/approach: Two-stage pose-guided person image synthesis using U-Net-like coarse generator followed by adversarial U-Net refinement, with masked L1 losses and a difference-map formulation.
- Relevance: Timely and in-scope for NeurIPS, relating to conditional GANs and pose-conditioned image generation.
- Strength: Clear motivation and presentation; paper is easy to follow.
- Strength: Application (controllable single-image human pose manipulation) is interesting and useful for augmentation/content creation.
- Strength: Some generated results are plausible and can align appearance to target skeletons.
- Weakness: Claims of “high-quality” results are unsupported by rigorous quantitative evaluation (no FID, LPIPS, user studies, or fooling rates).
- Weakness: No assessment of pose accuracy, identity preservation, or downstream utility (e.g., PCK, re-ID embedding, retrieval gains).
- Weakness: Visual quality issues remain (artifacts, texture smearing, deformed hands/faces, background bleeding) and figures are too small to judge.
- Weakness: Originality is limited—uses well-known components (U-Nets, DCGAN, masked L1, coarse-to-fine) without clear conceptual advance.
- Weakness: Significance is weak due to lack of strong quantitative evidence and state-of-the-art comparisons (e.g., pix2pix, appearance-flow, pose-transfer).
- Suggestion: Add robust evaluations—FID/LPIPS, user studies/A-B tests, pose consistency (PCK), identity similarity, and failure-mode analysis by pose difficulty/occlusion.
- Suggestion: Include stronger baselines and ablations (single-stage vs two-stage, with/without difference map).
- Suggestion: Improve figure legibility and report runtime and training stability.
- Rebuttal outcome: Authors did not adequately address quantitative/qualitative shortcomings; reviewers remain unconvinced.
- Recommendation: Reject; future acceptance would need solid quantitative evaluation and clearer advances beyond standard components.
