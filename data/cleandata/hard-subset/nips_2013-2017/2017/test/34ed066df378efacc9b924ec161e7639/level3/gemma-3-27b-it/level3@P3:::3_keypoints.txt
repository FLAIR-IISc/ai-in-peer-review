- Proposes PG2: a GAN-based two-stage system (U-Net for coarse pose integration, adversarial refinement) to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose keypoints.
- Leverages standard components (DCGANs, U-Nets); contribution is application-focused rather than architecturally novel.
- Distinguishes itself by using keypoint-based pose guidance as the primary conditioning signal.
- Two-stage decomposition follows prior strategies to simplify complex generative tasks.
- Strength: Interesting, practically useful idea (e.g., film, data augmentation for pose estimation).
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well organized; two-stage method is clearly explained.
- Strength: Some generated images show plausible local details, indicating potential.
- Major weakness: Complete lack of quantitative evaluation (no user studies or metrics like FID/precision-recall).
- Major weakness: Generated images are often blurry, contain artifacts, and frequently do not match the target pose; figures are small.
- Weakness: Limited baselines—comparison only to VariGAN; broader comparisons needed.
- Weakness: Ablation study is incomplete and lacks robust quantitative backing.
- Claims of “high-quality” or “real-image-level” results are not supported by the evidence.
- Rebuttal failed to address core quantitative-evaluation concerns; added visuals did not resolve quality issues and reviewers remained unconvinced.
- Pro-acceptance points: novel application idea and clear exposition.
- Con-acceptance points: missing quantitative evaluation, poor visual quality, and lack of significant advance over existing methods.
- Overall: Promising proof-of-concept but premature for a top venue; requires substantial improvement and rigorous evaluation.
