The paper proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) that allows synthesizing person images in arbitrary poses, based on an image of that person and a novel pose. The generation framework PG utilizes the pose information explicitly and consists of two key stages: pose integration and image refinement. In the first stage, the condition image and the target pose are fed into a U-Net-like network to generate an initial but coarse image of the person with the target pose. The second stage then refines the initial and blurry result by training a U-Net-like generator in an adversarial way.

The proposed method is related to previous work on generative image modeling with deep learning techniques, including Variational Autoencoders (VAE), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Autoregressive models. However, the authors introduce a novel task of conditioning image generation on a reference image and an intended pose, which is different from previous work that focuses on unsupervised or category/attribute-conditioned image generation.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The proposed two-stage architecture is a good strategy for addressing the challenging task of pose transfer, and the use of a difference map to refine the initial result is a useful refinement. The authors also provide extensive experimental results on two person datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method.

However, the generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details, which is a significant weakness of the method. Additionally, the image quality still needs significant improvement for application use. Furthermore, the approach could be viewed as another conditional GAN variant with limited broad appeal, and the stacking/conditioning of generators has been proposed before.

Despite these weaknesses, the reviewer's initial recommendation is to accept the paper. The proposed method is technically novel, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, and the authors provide a good analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
