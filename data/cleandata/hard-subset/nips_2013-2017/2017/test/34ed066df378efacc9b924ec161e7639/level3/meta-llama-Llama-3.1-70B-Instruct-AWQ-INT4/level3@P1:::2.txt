This paper proposes a novel task of generating person images conditioned on both a reference image and a target pose, and introduces a Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) to address this task. The PG2 consists of two stages: the first stage uses a U-Net-like network to integrate the target pose with the person image and generate a coarse result, while the second stage uses a variant of Deep Convolutional GAN (DCGAN) to refine the initial result and generate sharper images. The authors also propose a novel pose mask loss to alleviate the influence of background changes and guide the generator to focus on transferring the human body appearance.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its components. The use of a two-stage approach, where the first stage focuses on capturing the global structure of the person and the second stage refines the appearance details, is a good strategy for addressing the challenging task of pose transfer. The idea of predicting a difference map to refine the initial result is also a useful refinement.

The experimental results on the DeepFashion dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating high-quality person images with convincing details. The authors also provide a comparison with a one-stage model and show that the two-stage model is able to generate more photo-realistic results.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details, which is a significant issue for application use. Additionally, the approach could be viewed as another conditional GAN variant with limited broad appeal, and the stacking/conditioning of generators has been proposed before in other works.
