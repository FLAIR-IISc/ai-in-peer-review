- Introduces a novel task: pose-guided person image generation and proposes the PG² two-stage generative model.  
- Stage 1: U-Net-like generator uses source image + target pose heatmap to produce a coarse image, trained with a pose-mask L1 loss to focus on the person.  
- Stage 2: U-Net-like generator predicts a residual (difference map) to refine the coarse output, trained adversarially with a discriminator.  
- Coarse-to-fine strategy adapts stacked generative model ideas to explicit pose conditioning; difference-map refinement is a novel design choice.  
- Strength: formalizes an important, controllable task with clear applications (virtual try-on, animation, data augmentation).  
- Strength: well-motivated two-stage decomposition—global rearrangement in stage 1, local detail correction in stage 2.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with detailed method description and helpful figures.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on DeepFashion and Market-1501, including ablations, quantitative metrics, qualitative results, and a user study.  
- Weakness: final image quality is limited—blurred textures and visual artifacts reduce realism and practical applicability.  
- Weakness: core framework is incremental, building on existing stacked GAN ideas rather than introducing a fundamentally new generative paradigm.  
- Weakness: failure cases on rare poses, complex clothing, and data imbalance; model struggles with non-standard poses and occlusions.  
- Suggestion: incorporate a perceptual (e.g., VGG) loss to preserve high-frequency details and improve sharpness.  
- Suggestion: explore multi-scale discriminators (e.g., pix2pixHD-style) to encourage finer detail at multiple resolutions.  
- Suggestion: analyze how errors from the off-the-shelf pose estimator propagate and affect generation quality.  
- Overall: a solid, well-evaluated baseline for a novel problem with useful contributions despite limitations; recommended for acceptance.
