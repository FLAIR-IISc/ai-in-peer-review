- Introduces a novel task: pose-guided person image generation (synthesize a specific person in a target pose from a source image and keypoints).  
- Proposes PG2: a two-stage approach—Stage I U-Net for coarse pose-aligned synthesis, Stage II conditional GAN (U-Net generator) for refinement.  
- Uses standard generative components (U-Nets, DCGANs) applied to a new conditional generation problem; tested on DeepFashion and Market-1501.  
- Strength: problem is interesting and well-motivated with useful applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, re-ID, VFX).  
- Strength: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient architectural and training detail.  
- Strength: Stage I demonstrates promising ability to capture global structure, pose, and color palette as a proof-of-concept.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is unconvincing—SSIM and Inception Score poorly correlate with perceptual quality, and blurrier outputs can score better.  
- Weakness: User study shows low fooling rates (14.9% on DeepFashion, 7.8% on Market-1501), indicating generated images are far from photo-realistic.  
- Weakness: Qualitative results contain major artifacts (distorted/blurry faces, inconsistent clothing textures, anatomically odd limbs, fuzzy person-background boundaries) and figures are small.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—largely a straightforward combination of existing techniques; main novelties are a pose mask loss and two-stage refinement (incremental).  
- Summary: Promising proof-of-concept but results are preliminary and do not support claims of high-quality photo-realism; not ready for a top-tier venue.  
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should improve generation quality and provide more robust, convincing evaluations before resubmission.  
- Update after rebuttal: Reviewer’s assessment unchanged—other reviewers echoed concerns and rebuttal lacked new experiments/evidence to address the main issues.
