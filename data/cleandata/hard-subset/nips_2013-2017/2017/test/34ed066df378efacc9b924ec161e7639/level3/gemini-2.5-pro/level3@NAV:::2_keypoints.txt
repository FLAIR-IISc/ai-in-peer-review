- Proposes pose-guided human image generation conditioned on a source appearance image and a target pose using a two-step adversarial network (coarse U-Net generator then residual/refinement generator).
- Coarse-to-fine design where the second stage predicts a difference map to focus on high-frequency details, inspired by stacked architectures like StackGAN.
- Method validated on the DeepFashion dataset.
- Strength: well-organized, clearly written; architecture is technically sound and appropriate for dressing and pose transfer.
- Strength: task novelty and tailored architecture/loss constitute a solid technical contribution.
- Weakness: final images show noticeable artifacts and fail to recover fine-grained textures, limiting practical applicability.
- Weakness: approach may be viewed as an incremental variation on existing stacked/conditional GAN frameworks, reducing broader significance.
- Overall assessment: demonstrates viability of the two-stage approach despite imperfections; recommendation: Accept.
- Suggestion: expand discussion on how this stacking approach differs from or improves upon prior stacked GAN models.
