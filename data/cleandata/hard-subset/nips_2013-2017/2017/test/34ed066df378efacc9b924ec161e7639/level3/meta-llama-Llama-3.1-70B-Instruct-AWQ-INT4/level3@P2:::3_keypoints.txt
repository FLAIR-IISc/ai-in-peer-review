- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images in arbitrary poses from an input image and target pose.  
- PG uses explicit pose information with two stages: pose integration and image refinement.  
- Pose integration implemented with a U-Net-like network; refinement uses a DCGAN variant.  
- Paper is well-written and the idea of pose-guided generation is interesting and novel.  
- Provides a thorough literature review covering VAE, GAN, and autoregressive generative models.  
- Adopts a divide-and-conquer strategy to address pose transfer challenges.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets with diverse poses.  
- Experiments study different pose embeddings, pose mask loss vs. L1 loss, and two-stage vs. one-stage models.  
- Compares against related prior work (reference [36]).  
- Visual quality of generated images is unconvincing; figures are too small to assess details.  
- Generated images contain many noticeable errors relative to target images.  
- Lacks quantitative human-evaluation or fooling-rate metrics to assess realism.  
- Authors’ rebuttal fails to satisfactorily address the quantitative and qualitative shortcomings.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to premature claims of high-quality results and insufficient evaluation, but encourages further work and more thorough evaluation.
