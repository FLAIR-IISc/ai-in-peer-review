Summary and relation to prior work
This paper introduces Pose Guided Person Generation (PG2), a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework for synthesizing images of a specific person under a new target pose, given a reference image and a pose specification. Stage-I integrates the target pose and the condition image via a U-Net-like encoder-decoder with skip connections (and residual blocks), trained with a masked L1 loss that emphasizes person regions over background. Stage-II refines the result with a conditional GAN that predicts a difference map to add high-frequency details, using a pairwise discriminator that receives (generated or real image, condition image) and the same masked L1 as a regularizer.

The paper is positioned within conditional generative modeling (CVAE, conditional GANs), and is related to work on text/attribute-conditioned generation, multi-view synthesis, and face rotation. Conceptually, PG2 follows the coarse-to-fine spirit of stacked/iterative refinement approaches (e.g., StackGAN; iterative error feedback and stacked hourglass for pose estimation: Carreira et al., 2016; Newell et al., 2016) and to image-to-image translation (Isola et al., 2017). Compared to prior multi-view person generation (e.g., Zhao et al., 2017), the explicit conditioning on 2D keypoint heatmaps yields finer control over pose and generally higher visual fidelity in the presented comparisons.

Strengths
- Novel and useful task formulation: person-specific generation conditioned jointly on appearance and explicit pose.
- Effective two-stage design: disentangles global structure (pose alignment) from detail synthesis; clever use of difference-map generation and pairwise discriminator.
- Clear architecture and training description with sensible design choices (pose heatmaps; masked L1).
- Thorough ablations: alternative pose embeddings, effect of mask loss, and one-stage vs two-stage GAN training.
- Qualitative results are compelling on both low-res (Market-1501) and higher-res (DeepFashion), with noticeable improvements in the refined stage.

Weaknesses
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: SSIM/IS (and masked variants) do not correlate well with perceptual realism; the user study is preliminary and not strongly diagnostic.
- Some synthesized images contain visible artifacts, background leakage, and identity/attribute inconsistencies; analysis of failure modes is brief.
- The contribution of residual blocks is claimed but not substantiated with metrics or a controlled ablation.
- The fully connected layer in Stage-I may contribute to blurring and information bottleneck; this is discussed but not ablated.
- Baselines could be stronger (e.g., pix2pix with pose heatmaps and condition image; segmentation/part-based conditioning).

Quality
- The methodology is technically sound and well motivated. The masked L1 is a reasonable and effective modification for this setting, and the difference-map GAN is a nice simplification that appears to stabilize training and focus refinement.
- Experimental evidence supports the main claims qualitatively and through ablations. However, stronger and more perceptually aligned metrics (and baselines) would make the case more convincing.
- Missing or underdeveloped ablations: (a) impact of residual blocks; (b) removing the Stage-I fully connected layer; (c) end-to-end joint training vs staged; (d) comparison to a pix2pix-style PatchGAN baseline taking [IA, PB] as input.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written and well organized. Figures communicate the pipeline effectively.
- Please report the λ used for masked L1 in Stage-II in the main text and provide sensitivity analysis (currently mentioned only as “supplementary”).
- Clarify the exact construction of the pose mask (morphological operations) and its robustness to pose estimation errors.

Originality
- While coarse-to-fine GANs and pose-conditioned generation have precursors, the specific combination—explicit pose heatmaps, masked L1, and difference-map refinement with a pairwise discriminator—constitutes a novel and coherent approach to pose-guided person-specific synthesis.

Significance
- The problem is relevant to vision and graphics (data augmentation for re-id/pose, content creation). The approach is likely to be adopted and extended.
- Results are promising but not yet artifact-free; impact would be strengthened by more rigorous evaluation of identity preservation and pose fidelity.

Requests for clarification and suggested experiments (for rebuttal/revision)
- Residual blocks: What quantitative or qualitative improvements do they bring? Please provide an ablation (with/without residual blocks) on mask-SSIM/mask-IS and a side-by-side visual comparison.
- Fully connected layer in Stage-I: Is it a major source of blur? Please report an ablation removing the FC (fully convolutional Stage-I) and compare.
- One-stage vs two-stage: You compare G1+D and G1+G2+D. Please also train a “G2-only” one-stage model that maps (IA, PB) to IB using the Stage-II fully convolutional U-Net and PatchGAN to decouple the effect of the FC layer from the effect of two-stage training.
- Stronger baselines: Include pix2pix (Isola et al.) with inputs [IA concatenated with pose heatmaps] and masked L1 + PatchGAN; optionally a part-segmentation-conditioned baseline (cf. Lassner et al.).
- Evaluation: Replace the brief AMT test with a 2AFC perceptual study comparing methods, and add (i) identity preservation via a re-id network similarity score and (ii) pose fidelity via PCK/OKS by running a pose estimator on generated images. Consider FID as an additional metric.
- Related work: Please explicitly discuss iterative/staged refinement connections (Carreira et al., 2016; Newell et al., 2016) in the context of your two-stage design.
- Robustness: Analyze sensitivity to pose estimation noise; e.g., add jitter to keypoints or use GT keypoints for a subset.

Pros
- Clear, well-motivated two-stage architecture with practical innovations (masked L1, difference-map GAN).
- Strong qualitative results; informative ablations.
- Addresses a relevant and timely problem with explicit control via pose.

Cons
- Quantitative evaluation and human study are weak; limited perceptual and identity/pose metrics.
- Some artifacts and background leakage remain; analysis could be deeper.
- Missing ablations on residual blocks and Stage-I FC layer; baselines could be stronger.

Overall recommendation
Accept. The paper proposes a novel, principled approach to an important problem with convincing qualitative results and solid analysis, despite weaknesses in quantitative evaluation and a few missing ablations.

Confidence
Moderate to high.
