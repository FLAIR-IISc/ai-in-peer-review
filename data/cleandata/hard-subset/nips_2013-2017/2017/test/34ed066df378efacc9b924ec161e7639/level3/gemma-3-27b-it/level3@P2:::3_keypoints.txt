- Proposes PG2, a two-stage GAN (U-Net coarse Stage-I, DCGAN refinement Stage-II) for pose-guided person image synthesis conditioned on a reference image and target pose.
- Explores different pose embedding techniques and introduces a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body.
- Evaluated qualitatively on Market-1501 (low-res) and DeepFashion (high-res) datasets.
- Problem is relevant with useful applications (e.g., filmmaking, data augmentation) and builds on existing image-generation literature.
- Two-stage design is logical and motivated by the difficulty of jointly learning pose and appearance.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making ideas accessible.
- Qualitative results are provided but figures are small and do not allow detailed inspection.
- Visual quality of generated images is often subpar, showing artifacts, blurriness, and pose inaccuracies, especially on Market-1501.
- Lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation beyond SSIM and Inception Score, which may not reflect perceptual quality.
- No user study or human evaluation to assess realism or success of pose transfer.
- Limited and insufficient comparison to related work (e.g., [36]) without shared-dataset quantitative benchmarks.
- Discussion of failure cases is present but superficial and lacks detailed analysis or mitigation strategies.
- Arguments for acceptance: interesting problem, logical approach, clear writing, exploration of pose/loss choices.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak evaluation, poor visual quality, and no clear significant advance over existing work.
- Overall assessment: not ready for a top-tier venue (NIPS); recommend rejection pending substantial improvements in evaluation and image quality.
