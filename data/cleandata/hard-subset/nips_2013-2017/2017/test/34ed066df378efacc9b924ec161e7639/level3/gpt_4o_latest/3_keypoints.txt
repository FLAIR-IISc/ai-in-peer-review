- Paper proposes PG2, a two-stage framework (U-Net-like Stage-I, DCGAN-style Stage-II) for pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Uses pose heatmaps as input and introduces a pose mask loss to focus synthesis on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Experiments conducted on Market-1501 and DeepFashion with qualitative and quantitative results reported.  
- Strength: Tackles an interesting, challenging problem with clear potential applications (movie production, pose estimation, data augmentation).  
- Strength: Two-stage divide-and-conquer design is logical and effective for the task.  
- Strength: Pose heatmaps and pose mask loss are novel contributions that improve body-focused generation; strong qualitative results on DeepFashion.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is weak—SSIM and Inception Score alone are insufficient; lacks user studies or perceptual metrics.  
- Weakness: Generated images suffer from blurry textures, unnatural boundaries, and low resolution, especially on Market-1501.  
- Weakness: Rebuttal failed to adequately address major concerns, leaving the work appearing immature for a top-tier venue.  
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should improve image quality (higher resolution), add user studies/perceptual evaluations, address failure cases, and broaden example diversity.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — novel task and approach, clear writing, pose mask loss; Con — poor visual quality, weak quantitative evaluation, insufficient rebuttal.
