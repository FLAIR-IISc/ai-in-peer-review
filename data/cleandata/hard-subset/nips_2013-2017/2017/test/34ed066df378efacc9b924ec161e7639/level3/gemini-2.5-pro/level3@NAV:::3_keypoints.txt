- Proposes a two-stage GAN (U-Net coarse generator followed by a conditional GAN refiner) for pose-guided person image synthesis conditioned on a source image and skeletal keypoints.  
- Builds on established architectures (U-Net, DCGAN) and situates within conditional image synthesis / pose-transfer prior work.  
- Strength: Novel application to human pose transfer with a clear, well-written, and methodologically straightforward two-stage design.  
- Strength: Potential practical uses (data augmentation, e-commerce, creative tools).  
- Weakness: Insufficient quantitative evaluation to support claims of "high-quality" and "photo-realistic" outputs.  
- Weakness: User study realism scores are weak; reliance on SSIM is inadequate for perceptual quality assessment.  
- Weakness: Qualitative results are unconvincing—figures are small and show artifacts, blurriness, distortion, and loss of identity/clothing detail.  
- Weakness: Limited technical novelty—mainly a recombination of known components, so the experimental evidence must be stronger.  
- Rebuttal did not adequately address these fundamental qualitative and quantitative concerns; other reviewers agree.  
- Overall: Promising proof-of-concept but premature for a top-tier venue; reviewer recommends rejection.
