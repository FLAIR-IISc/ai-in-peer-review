- Proposes PG2: a two-stage pose-guided person image generation pipeline (G1 coarse U-Net with masked L1, G2 adversarial residual U-Net with pairwise discriminator).
- Conditions on 18-keypoint pose heatmaps and a reference appearance image to synthesize target-pose person images.
- Evaluated on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) with SSIM/IS, masked variants, ablations, and an AMT user study.
- Two-stage design improves sharpness and detail preservation over a one-stage adversarial baseline in qualitative results.
- Novel components: foreground-weighted L1 mask, difference-map refinement at stage II, and a pairwise conditional discriminator.
- Builds on prior conditional/stacked GAN and keypoint-conditioned generation literature; novelty is mainly in the specific integration for pose transfer.
- Sound methodology: G1 handles global structure/color, G2 focuses on high-frequency detail; masked L1 and pairwise discriminator are well-motivated.
- Empirical support includes pose-embedding, mask loss, and one- vs two-stage ablations with consistent improvements and a user study.
- Limitations: visible artifacts (speckle/black dots), texture loss in challenging poses, and brittle background handling, especially on Market-1501.
- Missing quantitative evaluation of identity preservation (no re-ID or identity classifier metrics).
- Claims about faster convergence of the difference-map approach lack supporting training curves or timing data.
- Comparisons to strong single-stage image-to-image baselines (e.g., tuned pix2pix-style) are insufficient.
- Reproducibility: architecture/training described but some choices (no batchnorm/dropout), mask construction parameters, and λ sensitivity are underspecified.
- Clarity: paper is well written with clear figures; main text should better define mask pipeline, λ sensitivity, and failure case analysis.
- Originality: moderate—components individually known but integration for pose-guided person generation is practically useful.
- Significance: relevant to controllable generation and augmentation, with clear gains on DeepFashion but tempered by artifacts and missing identity metrics.
- Pros: coherent two-stage design, effective pose conditioning, practical difference-map refinement, thorough ablations, and clear presentation.
- Cons: residual artifacts and texture collapse, limited identity evaluation, incremental over prior stacked GANs, background failures, and missing runtime/convergence evidence.
- Key author requests: add identity-preservation metrics (e.g., re-ID retrieval), include a pix2pix-style baseline, provide training curves/runtime, detail mask construction and λ sensitivity, and test robustness to pose errors/occlusion.
- Suggested additional metrics: perceptual measures like LPIPS and FID to better match human judgments.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium-high.
