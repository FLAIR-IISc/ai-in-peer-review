- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2): two-stage pose integration then image refinement to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Novelty: generates persons conditioned on both reference image and explicit pose heatmaps, enabling flexible pose control.  
- Architecture: two-stage design (pose embedding + refinement) inspired by prior decomposition approaches; uses U-Nets/DCGAN components and masked L1 loss to focus on human body.  
- Technical soundness: architecture and design choices are well-motivated and supported by experiments.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear diagrams and explanations.  
- Extensive ablation studies: thoroughly evaluate pose embedding methods, mask loss, and two-stage effectiveness across two datasets.  
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets of differing resolutions, results are generally promising.  
- Image artifacts: generated images show noticeable artifacts (especially on Market-1501), indicating refinement stage limitations.  
- Quantitative metrics: reliance on SSIM and Inception Score is weak—SSIM may be misleading and Inception Score may not reflect visual fidelity.  
- Related work gap: misses some recent iterative refinement/pose estimation/generation works (e.g., Carreira et al., Newell et al.).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, strong technical approach, comprehensive ablations, clear presentation, and promising results.  
- Rejection arguments: visible artifacts in outputs, limited quantitative evaluation, and minor related-work omissions.  
- Suggestion: provide details showing how residual blocks improve generation (quantitative/qualitative evidence).  
- Suggestion: try adversarially training Stage 2 conditioned on Stage 1 outputs to reduce blurring.  
- Suggestion: investigate removing the fully connected layer in Stage 1 to assess its role in blurring.  
- Overall assessment: despite minor issues, strong paper with significant potential; reviewer recommends acceptance.
