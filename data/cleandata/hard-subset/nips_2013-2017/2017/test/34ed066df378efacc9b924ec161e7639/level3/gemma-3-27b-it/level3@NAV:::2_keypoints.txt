- Proposes a two-stage, pose- and appearance-conditioned person image generation pipeline: coarse U-Net followed by a residual-refining generator with adversarial training.
- Introduces a difference (residual) map for focused refinement and a pose mask loss to reduce background interference.
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets with SSIM and Inception Score metrics.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly presented, and shows sensible motivation for a coarse-to-fine approach.
- Builds on conditional GANs and stacked generator literature, combining existing ideas for this specific task.
- Generated images often suffer from artifacts, blurriness, and missing high-frequency details (example ID346).
- Quantitative metrics improve but do not fully capture perceptual quality deficiencies.
- Novelty is limited—approach may be seen as an incremental extension of existing conditioned and stacked GAN methods.
- Improvements, while statistically significant, might not be substantial enough for broad community impact.
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance but requests better image fidelity and stronger emphasis on the contribution beyond combining existing techniques.
