- Two-stage coarse-to-fine generative network for person image synthesis conditioned on appearance and pose.  
- Stage I (G1) uses a U-Net with pose heatmaps to produce a coarse image; Stage II (G2) refines via a residual/difference map.  
- Novel pose mask loss that focuses learning on the human body rather than background.  
- Difference-map training speeds convergence by having G2 predict the residual between coarse and target images.  
- Explicitly conditions on pose keypoints, extending conditional GAN and stacking ideas (e.g., StackGAN) to pose-guided generation.  
- Needs clearer comparison and differentiation from Zhao et al.'s multi-view person synthesis [36].  
- Strong technical execution with thorough ablation studies on pose embedding, loss choices, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Evaluation includes quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) and user studies that support the method.  
- Generated results show visible artifacts and poor recovery of high-frequency details (e.g., black dots in examples).  
- Training procedure is well-documented, but failure modes and their causes are not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; figures effectively illustrate architecture and results.  
- Difference-map refinement and pose mask loss are novel, practical contributions to the task.  
- Overall significance is limited by image quality and incremental relation to prior stacked GAN work.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound and valuable foundation despite quality issues; addressing artifacts would boost practical impact.
