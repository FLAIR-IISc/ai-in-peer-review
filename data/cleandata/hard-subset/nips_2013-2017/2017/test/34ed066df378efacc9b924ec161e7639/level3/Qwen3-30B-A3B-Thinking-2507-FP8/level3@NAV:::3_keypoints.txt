- Proposes a two-stage GAN framework (pose integration then image refinement) for pose-guided person synthesis using U-Net architectures.  
- Uses a pose mask loss to focus generation on the human body and reduce background interference.  
- Core components (DCGANs, U-Nets) are standard; novelty lies mainly in applying them to pose-guided person generation.  
- Writing is clear and methodology is well-structured.  
- Visual results are reasonable in some cases and the two-stage approach provides a logical decomposition of the task.  
- Pose mask loss effectively mitigates background transfer issues.  
- Comparison with prior multi-view person synthesis (Zhao et al., 2017) shows advantages.  
- Lacks robust quantitative evaluation beyond SSIM and Inception Score, which do not capture perceptual realism.  
- User study is insufficiently detailed, underpowered (30 users × 55 images) and lacks statistical significance testing.  
- Visual quality is inconsistent with noticeable artifacts (mismatched clothing textures, incorrect body parts).  
- Figures are too small to properly assess results and visual evidence is insufficient.  
- Fails on evaluation criteria: Quality (no robust metrics/ablations), Clarity (insufficient visuals), Originality (uses known techniques), and Significance (no convincing real-world utility).  
- Authors’ rebuttal did not address the quantitative evaluation gap or provide stronger visual evidence.  
- Recommends rejection; suggests substantial additional evaluation and refinement before submission to a top venue.
