- Introduces PG2: a two-stage, pose-guided person image generation framework conditioned on an appearance image and a target pose.
- Stage I: U-Net with residual blocks integrates appearance and 18-channel pose heatmaps; uses masked L1 loss to emphasize the person region.
- Stage II: a second U-Net predicts a difference map to refine the coarse output and is trained adversarially with a pairwise conditional discriminator.
- Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) demonstrate visually plausible pose transfers retaining many appearance cues.
- Ablations show benefits of pose heatmap embedding over coordinate-based alternatives, masked L1 over vanilla L1, and two-stage refinement over a single-stage adversarial baseline.
- Quantitative evaluation uses SSIM, IS, masked variants, and an AMT user study to assess perceptual quality while reducing background confounds.
- Relation to prior work: builds on conditional generative models and image-to-image translation; novelty lies in explicit pose heatmaps and staged difference-map refinement tailored to re-posing.
- Technical soundness: architecture choices are justified, sensible pairwise discriminator avoids trivial copying, and difference-map design aids optimization.
- Empirical support: convincing qualitative results on DeepFashion, mixed results on Market-1501; quantitative metrics and user study support claims but some analyses are missing.
- Missing analyses: no rigorous identity preservation metric (re-ID/face embedding), no comparison to stronger single-stage baselines with perceptual loss, and limited study of pose estimation errors/occlusions.
- Limitations: artifacts and insufficient high-frequency detail in challenging poses and textures; backgrounds can transfer spuriously or produce artifacts.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well organized, with explained staged design and mask generation details, though formal mask specification/ablation would help.
- Originality: keypoint heatmaps are becoming standard; two-stage and difference-map ideas are incremental but usefully applied to person re-posing with pairwise conditioning.
- Significance: task is practical and of interest; method advances prior multi-view clothing work but image quality and broader impact are limited.
- Suggestions: add identity preservation metrics, compare to perceptual-loss baselines, study pose-noise sensitivity (keypoint dropout), and incorporate perceptual/multi-scale losses or discriminators.
- Additional suggestions: improve background handling via segmentation/inpainting, report FID or perceptual metrics, and include runtime/memory/training stability details.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear problem formulation, modular two-stage design with useful refinements, thorough ablations, user study, and results on two datasets.
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate novelty, remaining visual artifacts, missing strong baselines and identity metrics, and limited analysis of pose estimator errors and downstream utility.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
