This paper tackles pose-guided person image generation by conditioning on both appearance (a reference image) and a target pose. The proposed PG2 framework is a two-stage, coarse-to-fine pipeline: Stage-I integrates pose via a U-Net to produce a structurally correct but blurry image under a masked L1 loss; Stage-II refines details adversarially by predicting a residual/difference map that is added to the stage-I output, with a discriminator conditioned on the pair (condition image, generated image). Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) include ablations over pose embeddings and losses, along with SSIM/IS and masked variants to discount background mismatch. The approach relates to conditional GANs and image-to-image translation (e.g., pix2pix), stacked/coarse-to-fine generators (StackGAN, Stacked GANs), and multi-view generation; the novelty lies in explicit conditioning on pose keypoint heatmaps and using a residual generator with a tailored pose mask loss.

Quality: The method is technically sound and the design choices are well motivated. The residual refinement and pairwise discriminator are appropriate, and ablations substantiate the importance of pose heatmaps and the mask loss. However, high-frequency fidelity remains limited: visible artifacts and texture dropout are common (e.g., black dots in ID346), and background transfer can introduce noise on Market-1501. Quantitative metrics do not fully reflect perceptual gains. Suggestions: incorporate perceptual/feature-matching losses, multi-scale or PatchGAN discriminators, and explicit frequency- or texture-aware objectives; analyze failure cases and sensitivity to pose estimation noise.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized, with architecture details, training hyperparameters, and informative figures. The pose mask construction is described; releasing code/masks would further aid reproducibility.

Originality: Conditioning on both appearance and explicit pose for human image generation is a useful task formulation. While coarse-to-fine stacking and cGAN conditioning have prior art (StackGAN, Stacked GANs), predicting a difference map with a pose-focused loss is a thoughtful and practical twist.

Significance: The approach delivers visually consistent pose transfer on DeepFashion, but artifact rates and missing fine detail limit immediate applicability. As a principled variant of conditional GANs, its broad appeal may be moderate, yet it is likely to inform subsequent work on controllable human synthesis.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, well-motivated two-stage design with solid ablations
- Useful difference-map refinement and pose mask loss
- Convincing qualitative gains on DeepFashion

Cons:
- Persistent artifacts and limited high-frequency detail
- Incremental relative to stacked cGAN literature
- Practical quality still below application needs

Overall recommendation: Accept (weak accept).
