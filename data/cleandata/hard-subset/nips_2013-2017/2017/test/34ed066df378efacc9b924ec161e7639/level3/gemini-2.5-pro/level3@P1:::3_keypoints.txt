- Paper proposes PG2: a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network that synthesizes a person in a target pose from a single source image and keypoint heatmaps.
- Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like architecture to create a coarse pose-matched image by integrating the source image and pose heatmaps.
- Stage 2 refines the coarse output with a conditional GAN to add finer details and improve realism.
- Addresses an interesting, practical task (pose-guided person generation) with applications in data augmentation, virtual try-on, and content creation.
- Problem formulation is clear and the coarse-to-fine two-stage strategy is intuitive and well-explained.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clearly described architecture and training procedure.
- Visual quality of generated images is poor: faces are blurry, identity lost, clothing textures smeared, and fine details consistently missing.
- Figures are small and supplementary material shows similar artifacts, making the "high-quality/photo-realistic" claims unconvincing.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak: SSIM and Inception Score are poor proxies for perceptual quality and can be misleading.
- User study results are inconclusive—14.9% fooling rate on DeepFashion indicates most images are easily recognized as fake.
- Technical originality is limited: method mainly combines established components (U-Net + conditional DCGAN) and resembles existing coarse-to-fine/image-to-image frameworks.
- Overall assessment: promising but preliminary work that overclaims results; requires substantial visual improvements and more rigorous perceptual evaluation.
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance in current form—improve image quality, evaluation methodology, and stronger demonstration of novelty.
