- Proposal: Introduces Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images in arbitrary poses from a source image and target pose.  
- Approach: Two-stage pipeline—first captures global structure, then refines appearance via adversarial training.  
- Strength: Interesting problem with potential applications; writing is clear and related work coverage is thorough.  
- Strength: The two-stage design is a novel contribution worth exploring.  
- Major weakness: No quantitative evaluation (no metrics or user studies) to assess realism or fooling rate.  
- Major weakness: Visual results are low quality, figures are too small to judge, and generated images contain noticeable errors versus targets.  
- Rebuttal: Authors’ rebuttal failed to satisfactorily address the quantitative and qualitative concerns.  
- Consensus: Other reviewers echoed the same shortcomings.  
- Scores: Quality 5/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 5/10.  
- Recommendation: Not ready for acceptance/presentation at a top venue like NIPS.
