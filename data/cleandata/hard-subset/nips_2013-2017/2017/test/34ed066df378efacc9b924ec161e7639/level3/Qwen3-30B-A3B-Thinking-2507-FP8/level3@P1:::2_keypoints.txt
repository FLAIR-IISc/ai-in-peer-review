- Introduces Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2), a two-stage framework to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose.  
- Stage-I uses a U-Net-like architecture with a novel pose mask loss to generate a coarse, structurally correct image.  
- Stage-II refines the Stage-I output by generating a difference map via adversarial training to add high-frequency details.  
- Novel task framing: conditions image generation on both appearance and explicit pose information.  
- Pose embedding with 18 heatmaps is effective and outperforms alternative embeddings in ablations.  
- Pose mask loss reduces background inconsistencies by focusing generation on the human body.  
- Two-stage divide-and-conquer design simplifies refinement and accelerates convergence versus one-stage approaches.  
- Difference map refinement is a strong technical contribution that improves photo-realism.  
- Experiments are thorough: ablations, quantitative metrics (SSIM, Inception Score), qualitative results, and user studies on DeepFashion and Market-1501.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and reproducible with clear explanations and contextualized related work.  
- Main weaknesses: core components (U-Net, GAN, difference maps) are not entirely novel and resemble stacked-generator prior work.  
- Limited broader significance beyond the person image generation domain.  
- Generated images still have visible artifacts and image quality is not yet sufficient for practical applications.  
- Overall originality and significance are moderate; technical quality and evaluation are strong, supporting acceptance despite limitations.
