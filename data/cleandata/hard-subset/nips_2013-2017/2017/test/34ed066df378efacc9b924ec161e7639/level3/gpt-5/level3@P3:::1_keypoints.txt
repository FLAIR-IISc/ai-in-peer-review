- Task: Novel conditional generation — synthesize an image of a specific person under a target pose from a single reference image.  
- Method: PG2 encodes pose via keypoint heatmaps and uses a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture (G1: U-Net-like with residuals + FC and masked L1; G2: difference-map refinement with pairwise conditional discriminator).  
- Results: Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion produce visually convincing pose transfer but with remaining artifacts.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions itself among conditional generation and pose-aware generation; two-stage design relates to iterative refinement literature.  
- Strength: Novel, useful task definition with clear applications.  
- Strength: Clean, well-motivated two-stage architecture and practical design choices (pose heatmaps, masked loss, difference-map, pairwise discriminator).  
- Strength: Thorough ablations on pose embedding, loss variants, and one- vs two-stage training.  
- Strength: Clear writing and effective figures; generally reproducible description.  
- Strength: Promising qualitative results, especially on higher-resolution DeepFashion.  
- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is limited; SSIM/IS poorly align with perceptual quality and identity/pose fidelity; user study is preliminary.  
- Weakness: Visible generation artifacts (ghost limbs, texture smearing, background leakage), worse on cluttered scenes.  
- Weakness: Some component claims (residual blocks, FC layer role) lack quantitative backing.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of sensitivity to pose-estimation noise and of identity preservation.  
- Quality: Technically sound and carefully engineered, but needs stronger task-aligned metrics and diagnostic ablations.  
- Clarity: Very clear and organized; request to add explicit comparisons to iterative refinement work and clarify ablation metrics.  
- Originality: Task and explicit identity+pose conditioning are novel; two-stage/difference-map combination is a novel application of existing GAN practices.  
- Significance: Likely to influence controllable human image synthesis, data augmentation for pose/re-ID, and media applications.  
- Q1: Quantify the benefit of residual blocks with metrics (e.g., SSIM/mask-SSIM/FID) and isolating visuals.  
- Q2: Ablate the FC layer in G1 (with/without) under L1 and adversarial training to test if the FC bottleneck causes blurring.  
- Q3: Compare G2 predicting a difference map versus directly predicting full images under equal training budgets.  
- Q4: Study robustness to noisy/missing keypoints and provide an upper bound using ground-truth annotations.  
- Q5: Provide quantitative identity-preservation proxies (re-ID matching scores, face-recognition similarity).  
- Suggested metric experiments: Report FID (person crops/masked), re-ID identity consistency (rank-1/mAP), and keypoint PCK on generated images.  
- Suggested human eval: Replace brief AMT test with pairwise A/B, Likert ratings for pose/identity/realism/background, and report inter-rater agreement.  
- Suggested training experiments: Explore end-to-end fine-tuning of G1+G2+D, iterative multi-step refinement, and intermediate supervision.  
- Suggested adversarial experiments: More aggressive Stage II training (lower λ, multi-scale discriminators, perceptual/feature-matching losses) and test FC-free G1 variants.  
- Suggested background handling: Evaluate segmentation-guided or inpainting variants to reduce background artifacts and leakage.  
- Related work request: Expand discussion of iterative refinement frameworks (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) and pix2pix-style conditioning with heatmaps.  
- Pros (summary): Novel task, effective/simple/extensible architecture, solid ablations, strong qualitative results, clear writing.  
- Cons (summary): Weak quantitative evaluation, visible artifacts, limited per-component quantification.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel and influential approach; address metrics, human eval, and related-work expansion as requested.  
- Rebuttal note: Replace the limited AMT study with substantive human evaluation and task-relevant metrics (e.g., FID on person crops, re-ID), but the positive recommendation stands.
