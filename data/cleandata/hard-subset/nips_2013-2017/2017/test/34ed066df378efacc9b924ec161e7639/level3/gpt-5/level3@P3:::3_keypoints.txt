- Paper proposes PG2: a two-stage pose-guided person image synthesis (U-Net coarse stage with masked L1 loss; residual refinement stage with a conditional adversarial discriminator).
- Experiments conducted on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64).
- Method largely assembles known components (pose heatmaps, U-Net skip connections, residual blocks, cGAN-style discriminator, coarse-to-fine refinement).
- Main novelty is application focus, mask-weighted reconstruction loss, and framing stage-II as residual prediction—overall incremental.
- Strength: tackles an interesting and timely problem with clear applications (data augmentation, content creation).
- Strength: clear exposition and sensible, likely reproducible architecture choices.
- Strength: pose heatmaps and masked reconstruction are practical for handling foreground/background mismatch.
- Strength: some generated images are reasonably consistent in pose and color; two-stage idea is intuitive.
- Major weakness: lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation of perceptual realism (no convincing user studies or statistically grounded human tests).
- Major weakness: no assessment of identity preservation or pose accuracy (no re-ID similarity or PCK/OKS measures).
- Major weakness: baselines are weak or insufficiently compared to strong conditional image-to-image methods and concurrent pose-conditioned work.
- Major weakness: claims of “high-quality” and “photo-realistic” are unsupported; many results show artifacts, geometry errors, texture smearing, and background bleeding.
- Qualitative concern: figures are too small to judge detail; supplementary material has similar issues and visible errors (hands/arms/legs/faces/garments).
- Originality concern: contribution is mainly an application-driven combination of established building blocks; novelty is limited.
- Clarity: writing and organization are clear, but figure sizing and missing implementation/ablation details hinder assessment.
- Significance: problem is important but current image quality and evaluation do not convincingly advance the state of the art.
- Suggested improvements: run rigorous human evaluations (A/B tests, fooling rates with CIs) and add perceptual metrics (e.g., FID).
- Suggested improvements: measure identity preservation (re-ID metrics) and pose accuracy (PCK/OKS from pose estimators).
- Suggested improvements: include stronger, fair baselines (pix2pix-style models, concurrent works) and more ablations (residual vs direct prediction).
- Suggested improvements: provide larger figures, zoomed crops, failure cases, and per-body-part analyses; test generalization to diverse backgrounds/occlusions.
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced due to lack of new quantitative evidence and persistent qualitative/quantitative shortcomings.
- Recommendation: Reject — promising idea and clear writing but insufficient evaluation, quality, and novelty for a top-tier venue.
