- Proposes pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose using a two-stage approach.
- Uses a U-Net-like architecture for pose integration and a conditional DCGAN variant for image refinement.
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion datasets, showing some effectiveness.
- Builds on existing GAN and U-Net methods, applying them to a practical, novel task.
- Writing is clear and some generated images look realistic in places.
- Major lack of quantitative evaluation; relies solely on qualitative results.
- No user study or metrics to measure how often generated images fool humans.
- Visual quality of generated images is often poor with noticeable errors versus targets.
- Figures are too small, hindering assessment of results; supplementary results share the same issues.
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address reviewers’ quantitative and qualitative concerns.
- Other reviewers echoed these shortcomings; reviewer remains unconvinced after rebuttal.
- Not ready for presentation at a top venue like NIPS without further development and evaluation.
- Recommendation: address quantitative/qualitative issues and resubmit to a more suitable venue.
