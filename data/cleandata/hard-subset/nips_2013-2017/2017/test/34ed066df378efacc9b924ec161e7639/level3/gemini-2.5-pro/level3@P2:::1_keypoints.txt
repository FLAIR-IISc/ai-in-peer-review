- Introduces a new task: generate an image of a specific person in an arbitrary target pose from one source image and pose representation.  
- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG²), a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework for the task.  
- Stage I: U-Net-like generator (G1) produces a coarse pose-matched image using source appearance and pose heatmaps, with a pose mask loss focusing on the foreground.  
- Stage II: U-Net-like generator (G2) in a conditional GAN refines G1 output to add details and improve sharpness.  
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 show qualitatively plausible results and effective appearance transfer.  
- Distinguishes from prior viewpoint synthesis by conditioning on explicit keypoint heatmaps for flexible body configuration control.  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel, challenging problem with useful applications and a sensible two-stage solution.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written and design choices are clearly motivated.  
- Strength — experiments: comprehensive ablation studies validating pose embeddings, pose mask loss, and two-stage benefits.  
- Strength — qualitative results: promising detailed transfers especially on DeepFashion.  
- Weakness — quantitative evaluation: common metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) don’t reflect perceptual quality, obscuring measured gains from refinement.  
- Weakness — result quality: artifacts remain (distorted faces, smeared textures, loss of complex patterns), especially on Market-1501.  
- Weakness — user study: AMT protocol (1s exposure) is preliminary and may produce noisy/counter-intuitive results.  
- Suggestions/questions: relate to iterative refinement literature (stacked hourglass, iterative error feedback); clarify residual block impact (quantitative vs qualitative); investigate whether removing G1’s fully-connected layer, rather than two-stage design, explains reduced blurring; compare against a single-stage G2-like adversarial model.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, well-motivated, and well-validated contribution despite evaluation and artifact limitations.
