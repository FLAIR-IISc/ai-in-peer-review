- Proposes PG2: a two-stage framework—stage I U-Net using 18 keypoint heatmaps + reference image to produce a coarse pose-aligned image; stage II adversarial (DCGAN) refinement for appearance; evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion.  
- Technical approach is well-structured, using standard components (U-Net, DCGAN) with reasonable modifications.  
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation: only SSIM and Inception Score reported; no user studies or human perceptual evaluation.  
- Qualitative results contain significant artifacts (incorrect body parts, poor texture synthesis, blurry clothing, background inconsistencies), contradicting “photo-realistic” claims.  
- Supplementary material does not resolve visual quality issues.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; methodology and pose mask loss are well-explained.  
- Figures are too small and lack strong visual comparisons to properly assess results.  
- Pose-guided person image generation is a novel and useful problem direction; two-stage separation of pose and appearance is a reasonable design.  
- Core technical components are not novel; pose mask loss is a minor but helpful contribution.  
- Insufficient distinction and comparison from related work (e.g., Zhao et al., Lassner et al.).  
- Potentially significant applications (fashion, film, data augmentation) but practical utility is limited by current visual quality.  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated problem, reasonable framework, clear writing, useful pose mask loss.  
- Cons for acceptance: inadequate evaluation, visible artifacts, small/unclear figures, weak differentiation from prior work.  
- Authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address evaluation and visual-quality concerns.  
- Recommendation: reject—interesting direction but premature for a top-tier venue until visual quality and evaluation are substantially improved.
