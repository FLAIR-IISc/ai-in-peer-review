- Proposes a novel task: Pose Guided Person Image Generation to synthesize person images in arbitrary target poses from an input image.  
- Introduces PG2, a two-stage coarse-to-fine network: stage 1 (U-Net-like) integrates pose to produce a coarse result; stage 2 (DCGAN variant) refines details via adversarial training.  
- Evaluated on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, showing high-quality images and advantages over VariGAN.  
- Paper is well-written and includes extensive ablation studies on pose embeddings, losses, and architectures.  
- Strength: novel task with practical applications.  
- Strength: well-designed and effective two-stage architecture.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation of design decisions.  
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is weak—SSIM and Inception Score may not adequately measure synthesis quality.  
- Weakness: user study is preliminary and poorly designed, producing inconclusive results.  
- Weakness: claim that residual blocks improve performance lacks supporting metrics or qualitative evidence.  
- Weakness: no discussion or examples of potential failure modes (e.g., poor input quality or ambiguous target poses).  
- Related work: compares to VariGAN but omits discussion of other relevant work such as iterative refinement methods.  
- Recommendations: provide stronger evidence (additional metrics and qualitative analysis), detail and improve the user study, show failure cases, and discuss real-world applications.  
- Decision: recommend acceptance based on task novelty and approach, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses.
