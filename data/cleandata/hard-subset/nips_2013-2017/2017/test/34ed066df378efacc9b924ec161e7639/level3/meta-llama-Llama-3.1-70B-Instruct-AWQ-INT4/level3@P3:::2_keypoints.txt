- Proposes a novel task: generating person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose for pose-guided person manipulation.  
- Introduces a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2): stage 1 for coarse pose integration, stage 2 for adversarial image refinement.  
- Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like network to capture global body structure in the target pose.  
- Stage 2 employs a conditional DCGAN variant to refine appearance details via adversarial training.  
- Proposes several pose-integration strategies and a novel mask loss to focus on transferring body appearance rather than background.  
- Uses a difference map to refine the initial result, speeding convergence and preserving input details.  
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 show qualitative and quantitative effectiveness, including comparisons and a user study.  
- Results demonstrate visually consistent, high-quality samples in many cases.  
- Generated images still show visible artifacts and sometimes fail to recover high-frequency details.  
- Example failure: black-dot artifacts in some results (e.g., Figure 4, person ID 346).  
- Practical applicability is limited by remaining quality issues for real-world use.  
- Approach is technically novel but can be seen as a variant of conditional GANs and stacking generators has prior art (e.g., StackGAN).  
- Paper is well-organized and clear; two-stage design and difference-map refinement are useful contributions.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance despite weaknesses, citing novelty and demonstrated effectiveness.
