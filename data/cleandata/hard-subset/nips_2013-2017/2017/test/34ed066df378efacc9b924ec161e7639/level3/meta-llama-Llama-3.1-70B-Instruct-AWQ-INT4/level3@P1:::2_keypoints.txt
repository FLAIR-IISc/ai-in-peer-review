- Proposes a novel task: generating person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Introduces PG2, a two-stage Pose Guided Person Generation Network.  
- Stage 1: U-Net-like network integrates target pose with person image to produce a coarse result.  
- Stage 2: DCGAN variant refines the coarse result to generate sharper images.  
- Proposes a pose mask loss to reduce background influence and focus on human appearance transfer.  
- Predicting a difference map is used as a useful refinement mechanism.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the method and components.  
- Two-stage strategy effectively captures global structure first, then refines appearance details.  
- Experimental results on DeepFashion show high-quality, convincing image generation and outperform a one-stage model.  
- Weakness: generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details.  
- Weakness: approach may be viewed as another conditional GAN variant with limited broad appeal.  
- Weakness: stacking/conditioning of generators has prior art, reducing novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions despite quality issues.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10, Overall 30/40.
