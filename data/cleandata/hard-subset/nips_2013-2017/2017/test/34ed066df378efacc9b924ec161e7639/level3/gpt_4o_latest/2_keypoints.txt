- Introduces PG2: a pose-guided person generation network that conditions on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Proposes a two-stage coarse-to-fine generative framework: a U-Net-like stage for coarse image and a conditional GAN for refinement.  
- Novel use of difference map prediction in the refinement stage instead of direct upsampling.  
- Introduces a pose mask loss to suppress background influence and focus on the person.  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets, showing pose-consistent and visually plausible outputs.  
- Qualitative results are visually compelling on DeepFashion.  
- Quantitative metrics (SSIM, user studies) support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Method builds on prior GAN work (e.g., StackGAN, DCGAN) while targeting a specific application.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains methodology and experiments.  
- Weakness: generated images show visible artifacts and lack high-frequency details, especially on Market-1501.  
- Weakness: perceived as an incremental conditional GAN variant with limited generalizability beyond vision/graphics.  
- Weakness: reliance on pose estimation introduces potential errors that are not deeply analyzed.  
- Weakness: comparisons to related work are limited; additional baselines would strengthen evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task formulation, technical contributions (two-stage + difference map), and strong experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: image quality issues limit practical utility, perceived incremental nature, and limited discussion of broader impact/generalizability.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising; reviewer recommends acceptance while urging the authors to address image quality, generalizability, and evaluation gaps in future work.
