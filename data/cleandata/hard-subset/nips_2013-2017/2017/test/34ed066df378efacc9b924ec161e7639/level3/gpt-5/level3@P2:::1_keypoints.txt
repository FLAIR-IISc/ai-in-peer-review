- Summary: Proposes PG2, a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework to synthesize a target-pose image of a person given a reference image and pose heatmaps (Stage-I U-Net with masked L1; Stage-II difference-map conditional GAN with pairwise discriminator).
- Relation to prior work: Builds on conditional generative models, image-to-image translation, and stacked refinement approaches; explicit pose heatmaps give finer control than prior multi-view methods.
- Novel task formulation: Person-specific generation conditioned jointly on appearance and explicit pose is useful and well-motivated.
- Two-stage design strength: Separates global pose alignment from high-frequency detail synthesis; difference-map and pairwise discriminator are effective choices.
- Clear methodology: Architecture and training are described clearly with sensible design choices (pose heatmaps, masked L1).
- Thorough ablations: Evaluates pose embeddings, mask loss, and one-stage vs two-stage training.
- Qualitative results: Compelling examples on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, with visible improvements after refinement.
- Limited quantitative evaluation: SSIM/IS (and masked variants) poorly correlate with perceptual realism; user study is preliminary and not diagnostic.
- Visual artifacts: Some outputs show artifacts, background leakage, and identity/attribute inconsistencies; failure analysis is brief.
- Unsubstantiated claims: Contribution of residual blocks is asserted but lacks controlled ablation and metrics.
- Stage-I FC concern: Fully connected layer may cause blurring/information bottleneck but no ablation provided.
- Weak baselines: Stronger comparisons needed (e.g., pix2pix with pose heatmaps, segmentation/part-based baselines).
- Methodological soundness: Masked L1 and difference-map GAN are reasonable and stabilize training.
- Missing experiments: Ablations needed for residual blocks, removing Stage-I FC, end-to-end vs staged training, and a pix2pix-style PatchGAN baseline with [IA, PB] input.
- Evaluation improvement requests: Conduct 2AFC perceptual studies, add identity preservation via re-id scores, pose fidelity via PCK/OKS, and consider FID.
- Clarifications requested: Report λ for masked L1 in Stage-II in main text and detail pose mask construction and robustness to pose-estimation errors.
- Related-work connection: Explicitly discuss links to iterative/staged refinement literature (Carreira et al., Newell et al.).
- Robustness analysis: Test sensitivity to pose estimation noise and use GT keypoints for comparison.
- Pros summary: Clear two-stage architecture with practical innovations, strong qualitative results, and informative ablations.
- Cons summary: Weak quantitative/human evaluation, remaining artifacts/background leakage, missing key ablations and stronger baselines.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel, principled approach with convincing qualitative results despite evaluation gaps.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate to high.
