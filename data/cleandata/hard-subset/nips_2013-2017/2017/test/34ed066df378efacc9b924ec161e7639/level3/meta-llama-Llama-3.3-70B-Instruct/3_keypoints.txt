- Proposes Tensorized LSTM (tLSTM) that represents hidden states as higher-dimensional tensors to increase capacity.
- Tensor representation enables flexible parameter sharing and efficient widening without extra parameters.
- Merges deep computations into temporal computations, allowing deeper networks with little additional runtime.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the approach.
- Demonstrates tLSTM effectiveness on language modeling, algorithmic tasks, and image classification.
- Reports tLSTM outperforming stacked LSTMs and convolutional LSTMs in performance and runtime efficiency.
- Strength: novel and innovative solution to increasing LSTM capacity via tensorized hidden states.
- Strength: promising empirical results across several challenging sequence-learning tasks.
- Weakness: lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation and comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: evaluation is mostly qualitative, making result significance difficult to assess.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis and visualization of tLSTM’s internal workings to explain strengths and limitations.
- Meets most conference criteria but requires more detailed evaluation and comparisons to be convincing.
- Overall: paper has strong potential but needs more rigorous experiments and analysis; with revisions it could be a strong candidate.
