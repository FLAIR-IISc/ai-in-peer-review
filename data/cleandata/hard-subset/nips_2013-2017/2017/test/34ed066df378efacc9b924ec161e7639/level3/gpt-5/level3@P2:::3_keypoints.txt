- Task: Pose-guided person image synthesis using a reference image and target pose keypoints.
- Method: Two-stage PG2 framework — Stage I U-Net encoder–decoder with masked L1 for coarse image, Stage II adversarial U-Net predicting a difference map with a pairwise discriminator.
- Pose and mask: Pose represented as 18 keypoint heatmaps; mask derived from connected body parts and morphology.
- Datasets: Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501.
- Relation to prior work: Combines known components (U-Nets, conditional GANs, pose heatmaps, coarse-to-fine refinement) into an application-focused pipeline.
- Strength — relevance and clarity: Task is important and paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- Strength — explicit conditioning: Pose heatmaps and mask-based L1 provide sensible, explicit control and foreground emphasis.
- Strength — decomposition idea: Two-stage coarse-to-fine design and difference-map refinement are logical and practically useful.
- Strength — qualitative successes: Method preserves coarse appearance and captures target poses in favorable cases.
- Weakness — insufficient quantitative evaluation: Missing human perceptual studies, identity preservation metrics, pose accuracy (PCK/PCKh), and distributional metrics (IS/FID).
- Weakness — lack of strong baselines: No comparisons to pix2pix-style baselines, appearance-flow/warping methods, or one-stage GANs with pose maps.
- Weakness — visual quality concerns: Many examples show artifacts (distorted limbs, texture smearing, wrong colors) and figures are too small to judge realism.
- Weakness — incremental novelty: Contributions (mask loss, difference-map GAN) are modest and mainly engineering tweaks.
- Weakness — limited analysis: Few ablations beyond pose embedding and mask; missing studies on difference-map impact, pairwise discriminator, loss weights, stability, and generalization.
- Weakness — presentation gaps: Small figures and limited discussion of mask robustness and sensitivity to pose-estimation errors.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound at high level but experimental validation is insufficient to support strong claims of high-quality synthesis.
- Clarity assessment: Clear exposition of task and architecture, but figures and robustness details hinder assessment.
- Originality assessment: Incremental — largely known components with a practical tweak in difference-map formulation.
- Significance assessment: Important problem but limited impact due to weak quantitative evidence and unclear advances over simpler baselines.
- Pros for acceptance: Important problem, reproducible two-stage design, explicit pose conditioning, some useful ablations.
- Cons for acceptance: Inadequate quantitative evaluation, visual artifacts, incremental novelty, and overstated quality claims.
- Suggestions for improvement: Add human studies, identity and pose metrics, IS/FID, stronger baselines, cross-dataset tests, sensitivity to pose errors, larger high-res figures, deeper ablations, and release code/models.
- After rebuttal: Reviewer remains unconvinced; rebuttal did not address missing evaluations or core qualitative issues.
- Overall assessment: Promising direction but insufficient evidence and incremental novelty — recommendation to reject with encouragement to strengthen evaluations and baselines.
