- Introduces PG2: a two-stage coarse-to-fine network for pose-guided person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose.
- Proposes a pose mask loss to focus learning on human body features and suppress background interference.
- Novel two-stage architecture decomposes the task into global structure generation then appearance refinement, addressing pose transfer challenges.
- Well-written and clearly organized paper with detailed methodology and thorough experimental setup.
- Extensive evaluations including ablation studies on pose embeddings, loss functions, and the two-stage design provide useful design insights.
- Qualitative results are visually compelling with photo-realistic, pose-consistent images and convincing appearance details.
- Good positioning within related work and comparisons (e.g., VariGAN), though iterative refinement literature could be discussed more.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited by lack of suitable metrics; SSIM and Inception Score may not capture perceptual quality adequately.
- Some generated images show artifacts, particularly for complex poses or textures, and this is not deeply analyzed.
- The user study on Mechanical Turk is preliminary and unconvincing due to limited scope and analysis.
- Clarifications needed on the impact of residual blocks and on training Stage 2 adversarially using Stage 1 inputs.
- Recommendation: confidently recommended for acceptance due to novelty, clear presentation, and promising results despite noted weaknesses.
