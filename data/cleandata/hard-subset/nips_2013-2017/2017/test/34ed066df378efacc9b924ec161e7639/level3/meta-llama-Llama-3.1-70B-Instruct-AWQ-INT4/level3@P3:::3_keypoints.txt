- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images conditioned on a reference image and a target pose using two stages: pose integration and image refinement.  
- Contributions: (i) new task of pose-conditioned generation, (ii) exploration of pose–image integration strategies, (iii) two-stage decomposition for pose transfer.  
- Paper is well-written and the related work is comprehensive.  
- Experiments on Market-1501 and DeepFashion indicate the method can produce pose-transferred images.  
- Quantitative evaluation is insufficient—SSIM and Inception Score alone do not convincingly measure image quality; user studies or human-fooling metrics are needed.  
- Visual quality is unimpressive: figures are small, generated images show noticeable errors vs. targets, and supplements exhibit the same issues.  
- Claims of high-quality or real-image-level results are premature—the outputs are not photorealistic and contain artifacts.  
- The method cannot plausibly infer new backgrounds and often produces artifacts in generated images.  
- The authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address the quantitative and qualitative shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: do not accept for NIPS due to the above limitations.  
- Scores summary: Quality 6/10 (technically sound but unsupported claims), Clarity 8/10 (clear but figures small), Originality 7/10 (novel but not substantially different), Significance 6/10 (interesting problem but weak results).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach and reasonable two-stage idea for pose transfer.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of comprehensive evaluation, poor visual results, premature quality claims, and method limitations.
