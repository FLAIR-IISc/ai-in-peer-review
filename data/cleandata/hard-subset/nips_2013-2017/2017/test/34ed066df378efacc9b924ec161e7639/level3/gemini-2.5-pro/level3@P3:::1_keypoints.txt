- Task/method: Introduces PG2, a two-stage coarse-to-fine framework that uses pose keypoint heatmaps, a pose-mask loss in a U-Net-like first stage and a conditional GAN second stage to synthesize target-pose person images from one source image.
- Novelty/significance: Addresses a novel, important problem with clear applications (data augmentation, virtual try-on, gaming, film).
- Architecture quality: Two-stage decomposition (coarse global structure then fine detail) is well-motivated and effective.
- Loss/pose handling: Uses explicit pose information and a pose-mask loss to focus generation on the person rather than background.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, figures are illustrative, and methods are clearly described.
- Evaluation thoroughness: Extensive qualitative and quantitative analyses and ablations (pose embeddings, pose-mask loss) support design choices.
- Result quality: Generated images still show artifacts, blurring, and inconsistent textures (clothing, faces), so not yet fully photorealistic.
- Quantitative metrics: Evaluation relies on metrics (SSIM, Inception Score) that are weakly correlated with perceived GAN image quality and lack established standards for this task.
- User study: AMT study is preliminary and insufficiently rigorous (brief, timed exposure); stronger human evaluation is needed.
- Related work: Related work section should discuss iterative refinement/staged methods (e.g., Stacked Hourglass, iterative error feedback) for contextual relevance.
- Residual blocks: Clarify how residual blocks improve generation—report qualitative vs. quantitative gains or include an ablation.
- One-stage blurring hypothesis: Suggest testing whether a fully connected layer bottleneck in one-stage models causes blurring by training a Stage-2-like adversarial generator from the start.
- Human evaluation suggestion: Recommend a more rigorous 2AFC or similar user study comparing model outputs to baselines or real images.
- Recommendation: Strong contribution despite limitations; recommend acceptance.
