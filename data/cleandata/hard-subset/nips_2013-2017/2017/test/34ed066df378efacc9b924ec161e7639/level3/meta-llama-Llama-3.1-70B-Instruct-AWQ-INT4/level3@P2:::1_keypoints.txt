- Proposes a novel Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images in arbitrary poses from a source image and target pose.  
- The approach is well-motivated and the paper is well-written.  
- Introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture: pose integration (U-Net-like) then image refinement (DCGAN variant).  
- Thorough discussion of related work but missing some recent iterative-refinement works (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016, Newell et al. 2016).  
- Extensive evaluation of design decisions (pose embeddings, losses, network architectures).  
- Demonstrates promising qualitative and quantitative results on Market-1501 and DeepFashion, but generated images contain discernible artifacts.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak; SSIM variant and Inception Score may not fully capture generation quality.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; needs a more substantive human evaluation.  
- Blurring in Stage 1 is a potential issue; authors blame L1 loss but the root cause is unclear.  
- Reviewer suggests experiments: train Stage 2 adversarially on Stage 1 outputs and investigate the intermediate fully connected layer as a blur source.  
- Despite limitations, reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and overall contribution.  
- Authors should address noted limitations and provide a more thorough evaluation.
