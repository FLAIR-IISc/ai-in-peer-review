- Proposes a novel task: pose-guided person image generation using a reference image and target pose heatmaps.  
- Introduces PG2, a two-stage architecture: Stage I produces a coarse pose-aligned image, Stage II refines high-frequency details.  
- Builds on U-Net and GAN but contributes explicit pose-heatmap conditioning and a divide-and-conquer two-stage design.  
- Stage I uses a novel pose mask loss to suppress background interference and focus on global structure.  
- Stage II employs adversarial refinement to add realistic details, effectively separating pose correctness from appearance quality.  
- Well-written paper with thorough ablation studies comparing pose embeddings, loss functions, and one-stage vs two-stage designs.  
- Qualitative results on DeepFashion and Market-1501 show convincing synthesis; the two-stage model outperforms the one-stage baseline.  
- Preliminary user study suggests refined outputs are more likely to be mistaken for real images, but the study is small and lacks statistical analysis.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak—relies on SSIM and Inception Score, which poorly correlate with perceived quality; recommends FID and larger user studies.  
- Mask-SSIM and mask-IS help address background issues but are insufficient as sole quantitative metrics.  
- Recommends citing and relating to iterative refinement work in pose estimation (Carreira 2016; Newell 2016) due to conceptual similarity.  
- Claim that residual blocks improve performance is under-supported; authors should provide metrics or qualitative examples.  
- Two technical experiments suggested: remove the intermediate fully connected layer to test its effect on blurring, and train Stage II adversarially on Stage I inputs to see if blurring is reduced.  
- High significance and clear applications (fashion, film, data augmentation); outperforms relevant prior work and merits acceptance if evaluation concerns are addressed.
