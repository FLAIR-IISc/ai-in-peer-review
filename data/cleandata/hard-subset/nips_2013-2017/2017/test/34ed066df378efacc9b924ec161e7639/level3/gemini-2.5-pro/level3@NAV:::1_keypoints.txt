- Task: Proposes generating photorealistic images by combining source appearance with an explicit target pose represented as keypoint heatmaps.
- Method: Introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline—Stage 1 U-Net for global structure and pose, Stage 2 conditional GAN for detail refinement.
- Originality/Significance: Novel, well-defined problem with valuable applications (e.g., data augmentation, creative tools).
- Architectural motivation: Two-stage approach is sensible and well-motivated.
- Technical quality: Sound methodology with extensive ablation studies evaluating pose embedding and masked loss choices.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Results weakness: Generated images still show noticeable artifacts and blurriness not fully fixed by refinement.
- Evaluation weakness: Quantitative evaluation is weak and the provided user study is preliminary and unconvincing.
- Related work gap: Needs stronger connections to iterative refinement methods in related fields (e.g., Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016).
- Request for clarification: Authors should quantify how residual blocks improve performance with specific metrics or qualitative effects.
- Suggested experiment: Try training Stage 2 adversarially using Stage 1 inputs (source image and pose map) to potentially reduce blurring.
- Architectural question: Investigate whether blurring in the one-stage "G1+D" model is due to an information bottleneck from the intermediate fully-connected layer.
- Overall recommendation: Despite image-quality limitations, novelty, soundness, and thorough analysis justify acceptance.
