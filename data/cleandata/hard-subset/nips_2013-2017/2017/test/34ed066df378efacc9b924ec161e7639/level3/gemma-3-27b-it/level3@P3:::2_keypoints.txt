- Proposes a two-stage adversarial network (PG2) for pose-guided person image generation using a coarse U-Net then a U-Net-like refiner predicting a difference map.  
- Uses pose heatmaps as input conditioning and trains on DeepFashion and Market-1501 datasets.  
- Builds on conditional GAN literature, extending conditioning to both appearance and pose for more direct control.  
- Coarse-to-fine architecture and difference-map refinement are well-suited to handle pose transfer and clothing deformation.  
- Architecture is technically sound, leveraging established components (U-Nets, DCGANs) in a thoughtful design.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with helpful diagrams.  
- Novel task contribution: conditioning on appearance and pose for human image generation with clear application potential (virtual try-on, animation, data augmentation).  
- Difference map idea and pose mask loss provide useful regularization and faster convergence.  
- Generated image quality is limited, with artifacts and lack of high-frequency detail, especially on Market-1501.  
- Current image quality undermines practicality for real-world applications like virtual try-on or realistic animation.  
- Contribution may be seen as incremental within stacked/conditional GAN research rather than a major leap.  
- Prior work on stacked generators (StackGAN, etc.) reduces perceived originality of the refinement strategy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task, sound approach, useful coarse-to-fine and difference-map techniques, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor image quality, incremental novelty, and limited broader impact for the NIPS community.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid and promising approach; recommended for acceptance with the caveat that future work must address image sharpness and artifact reduction.
