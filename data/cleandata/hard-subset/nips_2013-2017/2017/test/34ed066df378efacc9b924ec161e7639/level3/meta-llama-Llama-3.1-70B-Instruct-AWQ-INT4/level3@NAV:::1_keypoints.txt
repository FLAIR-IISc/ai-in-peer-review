- Proposes a novel task: Pose Guided Person Image Generation (generate person images conditioned on a reference image and target pose).  
- Introduces a two-stage coarse-to-fine network that first captures global structure then refines appearance details.  
- Produces high-quality, photo-realistic images that are pose-wise correct.  
- Uses a pose mask loss to mitigate background changes.  
- Provides extensive experimental results on two person datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak due to lack of suitable metrics; should use more robust metrics.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; needs a more comprehensive user study.  
- Authors should clarify how residual blocks improve generation performance and detail the effect of the fully connected layer.  
- Recommend adding recent iterative refinement related work (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) to related work.  
- Suggest experimenting with adversarial training of Stage 2 on Stage 1 inputs to reduce blurring.  
- Overall recommendation: accept based on novelty and approach, provided the above weaknesses are addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel task, well-designed two-stage architecture, effective high-quality outputs, extensive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak quantitative evaluation, preliminary user study, unclear component contributions, missing related work.
