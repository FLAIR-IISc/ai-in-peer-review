- Proposes a two-stage pose-guided person image generation: coarse U-Net then residual-based refinement, with a pose mask loss and difference map for faster convergence and detail recovery.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, validated on DeepFashion and Market-1501; coarse-to-fine and pose mask strategies effectively reduce background interference.
- Difference map refinement converges faster than standard GAN training and helps recover finer details.
- Generated images still show artifacts (e.g., black dots) and fail to recover high-frequency details, limiting practical applicability.
- Insufficient justification for superiority over advanced single-stage models; approach is conceptually similar to StackGAN and not clearly differentiated from prior stacked GAN work.
- Technical contribution is an incremental improvement rather than a fundamental advance; results improve over a one-stage baseline but lag behind state-of-the-art.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the remaining image quality limitations in future work.
