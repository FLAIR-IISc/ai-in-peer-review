- Proposes a novel task: person image generation conditioned on a reference image and a target pose.  
- Introduces a two-stage network: stage 1 captures global structure, stage 2 refines results adversarially.  
- Stage 1 uses a U-Net-like architecture with residual blocks to improve initial generation.  
- Stage 2 employs a variant of DCGAN for adversarial refinement of the initial output.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness with extensive experiments on two datasets.  
- Provides thorough evaluation of design decisions (pose embeddings, losses, network architectures).  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow with clear method explanations.  
- Related work is comprehensive but should include recent iterative-refinement papers.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak—SSIM and Inception Score may not capture person-image nuances.  
- User study is preliminary and unconvincing; needs a more substantive human evaluation.  
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to address metrics, user study, and related work.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
