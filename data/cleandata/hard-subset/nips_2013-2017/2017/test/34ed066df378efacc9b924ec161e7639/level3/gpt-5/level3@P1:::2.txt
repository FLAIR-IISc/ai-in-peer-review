The paper introduces PG2, a two-stage, pose-guided person image generation framework that conditions on both an appearance image and a target pose. Stage I integrates pose and appearance via a U-Net with residual blocks, using pose heatmaps (18 channels) concatenated to the input; a masked L1 loss emphasizes the person region over background. Stage II refines the coarse output by predicting a difference map with another U-Net and training it adversarially with a pairwise conditional discriminator, which distinguishes (IA, IB) versus (IA, ÎB2). Experiments on DeepFashion (256×256) and Market-1501 (128×64) show the benefits of (i) the pose heatmap embedding over coordinate-based alternatives, (ii) the pose mask loss over vanilla L1, and (iii) the two-stage refinement over a single-stage adversarial baseline. Quantitative evaluation includes SSIM and IS as well as masked variants to reduce background confounds, and an AMT user study. The method produces visually plausible pose transfers and retains many appearance cues.

Relation to prior work: The paper builds on conditional generation with VAEs, autoregressive models, and especially GANs (DCGAN; conditional GANs). It is related to image-to-image translation (e.g., pix2pix), stacked/conditional GANs (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs), and clothing/human synthesis work (e.g., Lassner et al., VariGAN). The novelty here is the explicit use of pose as fine-grained spatial conditioning via keypoint heatmaps and the staged coarse-to-fine refinement using a residual/difference prediction, tailored to the person re-posing task.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The architecture choices are justified and ablations are provided: pose embedding alternatives, loss choice (L1 vs masked L1), and one-stage vs two-stage. The pairwise conditional discriminator is a sensible modification to prevent trivial copying of IA. The difference-map refinement is a practical design that likely eases optimization.
- Empirical support: Qualitative results are convincing on DeepFashion; more mixed on Market-1501 due to clutter and low resolution. Quantitative metrics (SSIM/IS and masked variants) support the design choices, and a user study provides a human-perception assessment. However, identity preservation is not rigorously quantified (e.g., with a re-ID or face embedding), and comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., a pix2pix-style single-stage model with perceptual loss) are missing. There is limited analysis of sensitivity to pose estimation errors and occlusions.
- Limitations: Generated images exhibit artifacts and insufficient high-frequency detail (e.g., dots/texture issues as noted for ID346), especially in challenging poses and complex textures; backgrounds can be transferred spuriously or produce artifacts.

Clarity
- The paper is clearly written and well organized. The staged design and loss functions are explained, and the masked loss is motivated. Details of the mask generation (from keypoints and morphology) are described, but a more formal specification or ablation on mask quality would help. Training details are given; architectural diagrams are helpful.

Originality
- Using pose heatmaps as explicit spatial conditioning is a solid idea, though keypoint heatmaps are increasingly standard in 2017. The two-stage coarse-to-fine refinement and difference-map prediction are incremental but useful. Stacked refinement GANs have precedent (e.g., StackGAN), but the specific application to person re-posing with pairwise conditioning and masked loss is a reasonable contribution.

Significance
- The task is practical and of interest to the vision and generative modeling communities (data augmentation for pose estimation/re-ID; controllable content creation). The method advances the state of the art over the most related multi-view clothing work, and the staged conditioning clarifies an effective recipe. That said, image quality is not yet robust enough for downstream deployment, and the method may be perceived as another conditional GAN variant without broader methodological impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add an identity preservation metric (e.g., cosine similarity in a re-ID or face embedding) to quantify whether identity is maintained across poses.
- Compare against a strong single-stage baseline using perceptual/VGG loss and/or multi-scale discriminators, and report ablations on the necessity of the difference-map formulation.
- Include a sensitivity study to pose estimation noise and missing keypoints; consider simple keypoint dropout during training for robustness.
- Incorporate a perceptual loss to boost high-frequency detail and reduce artifacts; try multi-scale or patch discriminators, feature matching loss, or TTUR to stabilize adversarial training.
- Evaluate background handling more thoroughly: consider explicit background inpainting or segmentation-based compositing to reduce background artifacts and leakage from IA.
- Report FID or a contemporary perceptual metric if feasible, and include runtime/memory analysis and training stability details.

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear and well-motivated problem formulation with explicit, controllable conditioning by pose.
- Practical, modular two-stage design with a useful difference-map refinement that improves details.
- Thorough ablations on pose embedding and losses; user study supports perceptual gains.
- Demonstrated results at two resolutions and across datasets; visible improvement over a related prior method.

Arguments against acceptance
- Visual quality remains limited, with artifacts and weak high-frequency detail, especially on challenging, cluttered images.
- Novelty is moderate; stacked refinement GANs and keypoint heatmaps are known, and the contribution may be viewed as an application-specific combination.
- Missing strong baselines and key quantitative metrics (identity preservation), and limited analysis of pose estimator errors.
- Practical utility is not yet demonstrated in downstream tasks (e.g., data augmentation for re-ID or pose estimation).

Overall recommendation: Weak Accept. The paper offers a clear, well-executed approach to an important and challenging task, with sensible architectural and loss design and solid empirical evidence, despite remaining quality and analysis gaps.

Reviewer confidence: Medium.
