- Task: generate images of a specific person in a new pose by combining source appearance with target pose keypoint heatmaps using a two-stage coarse-to-fine architecture.  
- Method: Stage I integrates pose and appearance via a U-Net-like generator and masked L1 loss; Stage II refines via an adversarially trained U-Net predicting a difference map with a pairwise conditional discriminator.  
- Relation: Work relates to conditional image generation, pose-conditioned human synthesis, view synthesis, and iterative/refinement paradigms (should explicitly cite Carreira et al. 2016 and Newell et al. 2016).  
- Strength — novelty: Well-motivated, explicit pose conditioning and a novel two-stage design.  
- Strength — architecture: Clear choices (pose heatmaps, masked L1, difference-map GAN) with thorough ablations on pose embeddings, losses, and stages.  
- Strength — presentation: Well-written, easy to follow, and qualitatively convincing results showing meaningful control.  
- Weakness — metrics: Quantitative evaluation uses SSIM/IS which are ill-suited for perceptual fidelity; missing metrics that correlate with human judgment or identity preservation.  
- Weakness — artifacts: Results still show discernible artifacts and blurring, particularly from Stage I.  
- Weakness — related work: Omits discussion of iterative refinement literature (e.g., IEF, stacked hourglass).  
- Weakness — user study: AMT user study is preliminary (brief exposure, small scale) and not conclusive.  
- Question: How exactly do residual blocks improve generation — please quantify with metrics (SSIM, FID, LPIPS, re-id) and describe qualitative effects.  
- Question: Is blurring in Stage1+GAN due to the intermediate fully connected layer — request ablation removing the FC in Stage I.  
- Question: Why not backpropagate adversarial loss from Stage II through Stage I (end-to-end fine-tuning) to reduce Stage I blur?  
- Suggestion: Add Carreira et al. (IEF) and Newell et al. (stacked hourglass) to related work as conceptually aligned iterative refinement.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate with perceptual metrics (FID, LPIPS) and identity preservation (re-id matching/verification); run a larger, more substantive human study instead of the preliminary AMT test.  
- Suggestion: Try adversarially fine-tuning Stage I with Stage II and/or add feature/perceptual losses to mitigate blurring.  
- Overall verdict: Accept — novelty, clear design, and thorough analysis outweigh metric weaknesses and artifacts; recommend stronger human evaluation in rebuttal.
