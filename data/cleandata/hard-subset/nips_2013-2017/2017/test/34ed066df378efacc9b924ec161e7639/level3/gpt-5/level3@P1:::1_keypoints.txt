- Paper proposes PG2: synthesize a person in a target pose from a reference image using 18-keypoint heatmaps and a two-stage coarse-to-fine pipeline.  
- Stage-I: U-Net-like encoder-decoder with skip connections, residual blocks, and masked L1 loss produces a coarse global-structure image.  
- Stage-II: conditional DCGAN predicts a residual (difference) map, trained with adversarial loss plus masked L1 to refine details.  
- Experiments on DeepFashion and Market-1501 include ablations on pose embedding, loss choice, and one- vs two-stage architectures, plus qualitative comparison to VariGAN.  
- Metrics reported: SSIM/masked-SSIM and Inception/masked-IS, and a small AMT user study.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated task and controllable conditioning via explicit keypoint heatmaps.  
- Strength: two-stage design effectively captures global structure then sharpens details; empirically beneficial.  
- Strength: solid ablations (pose embeddings, masked L1, architecture variants) and thoughtful masked losses/metrics.  
- Strength: compelling qualitative results on DeepFashion and visible improvements over VariGAN.  
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation limited; SSIM/IS poorly correlate with perceptual quality and user study too small/brief.  
- Weakness: generated artifacts remain (background bleeding, texture distortions, gender/limb errors), especially on Market-1501.  
- Weakness: impact of residual blocks and fully connected bottleneck asserted but not rigorously quantified.  
- Weakness: unclear cause of blur in one-stage adversarial baseline (loss vs architecture).  
- Quality: methodologically sound, well-described training details and architectures, but needs stronger metrics (e.g., FID, identity preservation, pose accuracy).  
- Clarity: well written and organized; minor clarifications suggested (pose-mask construction, ablation tables, role of residual/FC layers).  
- Originality: meaningful combination of pose heatmaps, masked losses, and residual adversarial refinement though staged refinement and conditional GANs are established.  
- Significance: promising for controllable human synthesis and downstream uses (data augmentation, content creation); showing downstream benefits would boost impact.  
- Key question: quantify benefits of residual blocks (SSIM/IS/convergence/stability) via ablation.  
- Key question: effect of removing Stage-I FC layer on blur/artifacts—please ablate.  
- Key question: why G1+D remains blurry—FC bottleneck, masked L1 dominance, or discriminator conditioning?  
- Key question: sensitivity of Stage-II weight λ—provide qualitative sweeps showing artifact/blurriness trade-off.  
- Suggestion: relate work to iterative refinement/stacked architectures (Carreira et al., Newell et al.).  
- Suggestion: add FID/masked-FID, identity preservation (re-ID features), and pose accuracy (PCK) to evaluation.  
- Suggestion: perform a stronger user study (2AFC/Turing style, longer view time, more participants, statistical analysis).  
- Suggestion: include strong image-to-image baselines (e.g., pix2pix on heatmaps) and a joint end-to-end Stage-I+II variant.  
- Suggestion: provide failure analysis broken down by pose rarity, occlusion, and background complexity.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, practical problem, effective two-stage design, strong qualitative results, and informative ablations.  
- Cons against acceptance: weak quantitative/human evaluation, remaining artifacts on challenging data, and insufficient evidence for some architectural claims.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with revisions to strengthen evaluation and clarify architectural impacts.
