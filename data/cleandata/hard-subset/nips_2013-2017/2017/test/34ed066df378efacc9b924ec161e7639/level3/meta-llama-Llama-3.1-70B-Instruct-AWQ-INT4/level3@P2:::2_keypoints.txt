- Proposes Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG) to synthesize person images in arbitrary target poses from a reference image and pose.
- Uses a two-stage framework: pose integration (U-Net-like network to produce a coarse initial image) and image refinement (adversarial U-Net-like generator to sharpen results).
- Employs a difference map in the refinement stage to improve the initial blurry output.
- Related to VAEs/GANs/autoregressive models but introduces a novel conditioning task (reference image + target pose).
- Paper is well-organized with clear, concise explanations of the approach.
- Provides extensive experimental results on two person datasets demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.
- Generated images exhibit visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details.
- Image quality currently insufficient for practical application use.
- Approach can be seen as an incremental conditional GAN variant since stacking/conditioning of generators has been proposed before.
- Overall assessment: technically novel and convincing enough to accept despite quality issues.
