- Presents PG2, a two-stage GAN system (U-Net-like pose integration + DCGAN refinement) for person image generation conditioned on a reference image and target pose keypoints.
- Idea of explicitly guiding generation with pose is relevant to applications like virtual try-on and animation.
- Method largely reuses established components (U-Nets, DCGANs) and is reminiscent of prior conditional GAN and view synthesis work.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and logically organized with justified two-stage motivation.
- Includes useful ablation studies on pose embedding methods and a pose mask loss, showing analysis of design choices.
- Qualitative results show some plausible outputs, especially on DeepFashion, indicating potential of the approach.
- Major weakness: no quantitative evaluation — relies solely on visual inspection without metrics like FID or user studies.
- Visual examples are limited in scope and low resolution, with small figures that hinder assessment.
- Generated images often contain artifacts and inconsistencies with target poses, particularly on the Market-1501 dataset.
- Rebuttal did not adequately address fundamental concerns, offering superficial justifications for lack of quantitative metrics.
- Other reviewers echoed concerns about qualitative shortcomings and absence of rigorous evaluation.
- Conclusion: idea has merit but is premature for a top-tier venue due to missing quantitative evaluation and visual quality issues.
