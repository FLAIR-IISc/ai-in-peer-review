- Introduces PG2, a two-stage framework (pose integration + refinement) for pose-conditioned person image generation.  
- Stage-I: U-Net with pose heatmaps produces coarse structure; introduces pose mask loss to suppress background interference.  
- Stage-II: Conditional GAN predicts appearance difference maps to refine images without generating from scratch.  
- Formalizes pose-conditioned person generation as a distinct task conditioned on both appearance and target pose.  
- Proposes pose heatmaps and mask loss for effective pose integration and background suppression.  
- Employs a coarse-to-fine difference-map refinement strategy to improve training stability and quality.  
- Thorough ablation studies validate design choices (pose embeddings, mask loss, two-stage vs one-stage).  
- Evaluated on DeepFashion and Market-1501 with SSIM, Inception Score, and user studies showing improvements.  
- Visual results and user studies indicate perceptual gains but still limited realism (e.g., 14.9% “real” rating for refined results).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presented, with helpful figures and supplementary implementation details.  
- Novelty lies in combining pose heatmaps, mask loss, and difference-map refinement for person-specific pose transfer.  
- Limitations include visible artifacts, difficulty with complex poses and high-frequency textures, and degradation on cluttered backgrounds.  
- Method is conceptually similar to prior stacked GANs (e.g., StackGAN) and difference-map ideas have precedents, making some contributions incremental.  
- Impact may be limited to niche applications (fashion/pose synthesis) and not yet suitable for real-world deployment due to quality gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept — meaningful, well-validated contribution despite artifact issues and incremental aspects.
