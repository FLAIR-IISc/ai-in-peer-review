Summary and relation to prior work
This paper tackles pose-guided person image generation: given a source image of a person (appearance) and a target pose, synthesize a new image of the same person in the desired configuration. The proposed Pose Guided Person Generation Network (PG2) adopts a two-stage, coarse-to-fine design. Stage I is a U-Net-like generator that integrates a pose embedding (18 keypoint heatmaps) with the source image to produce a coarse image capturing global structure. A masked L1 loss emphasizes the person over background. Stage II refines details via adversarial training: a second U-Net predicts a residual (difference) map that, when added to the coarse output, improves high-frequency fidelity. The discriminator is conditioned on the pair (condition image, generated/real image) and operates on high-resolution outputs (up to 256×256). Experiments are conducted on DeepFashion and Market-1501, with SSIM/IS (and masked variants) and an AMT user study.

The work is well-situated within conditional generation. It goes beyond label/text conditioning (e.g., CVAE, conditional GANs, text-to-image) by conditioning explicitly on pose heatmaps, akin to works that use structure/keypoints for controllable generation. It differs from multi-view synthesis from single images (e.g., Zhao et al. 2017; Chen & Grauman 2016) by using explicit pose rather than a view token. The stacked/coarse-to-fine idea connects to StackGAN and Stacked GANs, and the adversarial refinement relates to pix2pix’s PatchGAN-style refinements, but here the refinement is explicitly formulated as predicting a difference map conditioned on the coarse image plus the source appearance.

Method
- Pose embedding: 18-channel heatmaps from a pre-trained pose estimator, concatenated with the source image.
- Stage I (G1): U-Net with residual blocks and a bottleneck FC layer; trained with masked L1 to downweight background mismatches.
- Stage II (G2 + D): U-Net without FC to preserve details, trained adversarially to predict a residual/difference image. The discriminator sees (IA, output) vs (IA, target), encouraging pose- and identity-consistent realism. A masked L1 is mixed with the adversarial loss.
- Training details are clearly specified; models are trained at 128×64 (Market-1501) and 256×256 (DeepFashion).

Empirical evaluation
- Ablations: pose embedding strategies (coordinates vs heatmaps vs direct concatenation), loss (L1 vs masked L1), and one-stage adversarial vs two-stage (coarse + residual GAN).
- Metrics: SSIM/IS and masked variants (mask-SSIM/mask-IS), plus a user study. Authors note SSIM’s well-known mismatch with perceptual quality.
- Qualitative results show improved pose correctness and sharper details in the two-stage residual setup. Nonetheless, artifacts persist and fine textures are often lost.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation and practical conditioning signal (pose heatmaps) that provides controllable generation.
- Sensible and well-motivated coarse-to-fine design; predicting a residual/difference map in Stage II is an effective refinement strategy that appears to stabilize training and speed convergence.
- Tailored masked L1 addresses background ambiguity and is ablated convincingly.
- Thorough experiments on two datasets with both qualitative and (masked) quantitative measures, plus an AMT study.
- The paper is well organized and written; architectural and training details are sufficient for reproduction.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Image quality: Despite adversarial refinement, results still exhibit visible artifacts and lack high-frequency details; e.g., black dot artifacts noted (ID346) and texture/edge blurring in challenging cases. This limits immediate practicality.
- Significance: The approach may be viewed as another conditional GAN variant; although the pose-conditioning and residual refinement are well executed, the conceptual novelty over stacked/conditional GAN literature (StackGAN; Stacked GANs; pix2pix-style refinements) is moderate.
- Prior art positioning: While the paper cites relevant works, the discussion could more explicitly contrast against stacked refinement pipelines and identity-preserving face/body synthesis that use structure or keypoints, clarifying what is fundamentally new (e.g., residual-as-target vs direct high-res synthesis; pairwise discriminator design).
- Evaluation metrics: IS/SSIM are limited; no FID or LPIPS (or identity preservation metrics) are reported. Identity consistency is central but not quantitatively assessed (e.g., via a re-ID network on Market-1501).
- Robustness to pose estimator errors and rare poses is a concern; failure cases suggest sensitivity to pose/keypoint quality and data imbalance.

Quality
Technically sound, with an appropriate loss design (masked L1 + adversarial), a principled pair-conditioned discriminator, and a refinement-by-residual formulation. Claims are supported by ablations and a user study, though stronger perceptual metrics and identity measures would help. Visible artifacts temper the quality claim.

Clarity
Clear and well-organized. Architectural choices and training schedules are specified. The rationale for mask-SSIM/IS is sensible and well explained.

Originality
Moderate. The specific combination—pose heatmap conditioning, two-stage U-Nets, adversarial residual prediction, and mask losses—is a thoughtful synthesis. However, stacked/coarse-to-fine GANs and structural conditioning have precedents.

Significance
The task—appearance- and pose-conditioned human image generation—is valuable and timely, with potential applications in content creation, data augmentation, and pose analysis. The work advances practicality but falls short of artifact-free, detail-rich synthesis required for deployment. Nevertheless, it is likely to be cited and built upon.

Suggestions for improvement
- Report perceptual metrics (FID, LPIPS) and identity preservation scores (e.g., re-ID accuracy between generated and target) to substantiate claims.
- Explore multi-scale or PatchGAN discriminators, perceptual/feature-matching losses, and attention/affinity modules to better preserve textures.
- Analyze sensitivity to pose estimation noise; consider training with pose uncertainty or part-based alignment warps before refinement.
- Provide quantitative comparisons to other stacked refinement approaches and more recent structure-conditioned baselines.
- Investigate artifact mitigation (e.g., spectral normalization, TTUR, or regularizers) to reduce dotting/ringing.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear formulation of a useful, controllable generation task.
- Solid, well-executed coarse-to-fine design with a novel residual refinement component and tailored mask loss.
- Comprehensive ablations and user study; produces visually consistent outputs.

Cons for acceptance
- Persistent artifacts and missing high-frequency details limit practical impact.
- Conceptual novelty over existing stacked/conditional GAN frameworks is incremental.
- Evaluation lacks strong perceptual and identity metrics.

Overall assessment
Technically novel in its combination and well presented. Despite quality issues and moderate conceptual novelty, the method produces compelling pose-consistent results and will interest the community. Initial recommendation: accept.
