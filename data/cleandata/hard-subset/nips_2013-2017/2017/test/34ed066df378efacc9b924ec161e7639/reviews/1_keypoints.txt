- Proposes a novel task and architecture to generate images of a person by combining appearance from an input image with a target pose (heat maps).
- Introduces a novel two-stage coarse-to-fine network architecture.
- Paper is well written.
- Results are promising but contain discernible artifacts.
- Extensive evaluation of design decisions is provided.
- Quantitative evaluation is weak, partly due to lack of suitable metrics.
- Suggest adding recent iterative refinement related work (Carreira et al. 2016; Newell et al. 2016) to related work.
- Request clarification: how do residual blocks improve generation performance (what metrics or qualitative effects)?
- Suggest experiment: train Stage 2 with Stage 1 inputs adversarially to see if that reduces blurring.
- Question whether the blurring with Stage1+GAN is caused by the intermediate fully connected layer.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and approach.
- Rebuttal note: the attempted user study is preliminary/unconvincing and should be replaced by a substantive human evaluation, though the overall positive recommendation stands.
