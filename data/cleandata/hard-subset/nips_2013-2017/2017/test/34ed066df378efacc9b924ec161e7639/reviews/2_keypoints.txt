Proposal: human image generator conditioned on appearance and pose using adversarial two-step generative networks.

Generator architecture: coarse U-Net produces a coarse image, then a second generator predicts a residual/difference map to refine it.

Training: adversarial training where the discriminator receives the high-resolution output.

Evaluation: experiments conducted on the DeepFashion dataset.

Contribution — task novelty: introduces conditioning on appearance and pose for human image generation.

Contribution — technique: stacked architecture predicting difference map and a tailored loss design.

Strength: well-organized paper and an appropriate coarse-to-fine strategy for the dressing/pose problem; difference-map idea is a useful refinement.

Main weakness: generated images contain visible artifacts and fail to recover high-frequency details (example: black dots in ID346).

Practicality issue: image quality still needs significant improvement for application use.

Significance concern: approach could be viewed as another conditional GAN variant with limited broad appeal.

Prior-art concern: stacking/conditioning of generators has been proposed before (e.g., StackGAN, Stacked GANs).

Overall assessment: technically novel and produces visually consistent results; reviewer’s initial recommendation is accept despite quality issues.
