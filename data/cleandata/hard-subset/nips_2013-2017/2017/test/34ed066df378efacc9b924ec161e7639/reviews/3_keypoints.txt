- Paper builds a GAN-based system to synthesize novel-view person images guided by target skeleton/keypoints, using mostly known components (DCGANs, U-Nets); contribution is application-focused.
- Pros: idea is interesting, writing is clear, and generated images look somewhat realistic in places.
- Major con: no quantitative evaluation (e.g., no user studies or metrics to measure how often generated images fool humans).
- Visual quality concerns: results are not visually appealing, figures are too small, and generated images show many noticeable errors versus targets; supplementary results suffer the same issues.
- Reviewer views the work as promising but premature to claim high-quality or real-image-level results.
- After rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced; other reviewers echo quantitative and qualitative shortcomings, and the authors’ rebuttal did not satisfactorily address them.
- Implication/recommendation: not ready for presentation at a top venue like NIPS.
