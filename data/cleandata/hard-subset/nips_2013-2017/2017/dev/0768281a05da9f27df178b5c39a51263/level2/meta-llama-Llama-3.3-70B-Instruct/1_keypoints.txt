- Main claim: DHA handles nonlinearity, high dimensionality, and many subjects for functional alignment in multi-subject fMRI.  
- Empirical support: Extensive experiments on five fMRI datasets show DHA outperforms other HA methods.  
- Methods compared: DHA outperforms HA, RHA, KHA, SVDHA, SRM, and SearchLight.  
- Metrics: DHA achieves higher classification accuracy and AUC percentages, indicating effective alignment.  
- Post-alignment use: DHA improves downstream classification performance after alignment.  
- Scalability: Use of rank-m SVD and stochastic gradient descent yields low runtime on large datasets.  
- Novelty: Uses a deep network as a kernel to learn nonlinear, subject-adaptive transformations.  
- Clarity: Paper provides a clear, detailed explanation of the method, optimization, and implementation.  
- Completeness: Thorough evaluation across multiple datasets and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Limitations noted: Computational complexity and need for careful hyperparameter selection.  
- Suggested improvements: More detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability to very large datasets.  
- Further comparisons needed: Sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters and comparisons with other deep-learning-based alignment methods.
