- Introduces Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), a deep learning-based kernel for flexible, nonlinear functional alignment across subjects.  
- Claims to address nonlinearity, high dimensionality, and scalability limitations of existing Hyperalignment methods.  
- Uses a multi-layer neural network, rank-m SVD, and SGD for optimization.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over state-of-the-art HA methods across multiple fMRI datasets.  
- Novel contribution: replaces fixed kernels with a learnable deep kernel for greater modeling flexibility.  
- Scalable: rank-m SVD and SGD improve computational efficiency for large datasets.  
- Strong empirical validation: better classification accuracy, runtime, and feature selection versus SVDHA, SRM, and CAE.  
- Practical advantage: does not require training data during testing, aiding real-world applications like brain tumor localization.  
- Presentation is dense; optimization details and algorithm exposition need clearer explanation.  
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, including potential sensitivity to hyperparameters and network architecture.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study to isolate contributions of components like rank-m SVD and the multi-layer kernel.  
- Pros: fills a critical gap with a scalable, flexible approach and promising empirical results.  
- Cons: accessibility hindered by dense technical presentation and limited analysis of robustness/generalizability.  
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to improved clarity and more thorough discussion/ablation of limitations.
