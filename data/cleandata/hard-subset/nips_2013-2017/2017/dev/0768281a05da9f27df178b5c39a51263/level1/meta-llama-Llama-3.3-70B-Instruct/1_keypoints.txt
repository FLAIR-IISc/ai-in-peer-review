- Proposes Deep Hyperalignment (DHA): a deep network used as a kernel to map multi-subject fMRI responses into a common nonlinear, high-dimensional space.  
- Claims DHA outperforms state-of-the-art hyperalignment methods (KHA, SRM, CAE) on classification accuracy and AUC.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a detailed method description and optimization algorithm.  
- Experimental setup and results are presented and show superior performance of DHA.  
- Strength: Novel combination of deep learning and hyperalignment techniques.  
- Strength: Using a deep network as a kernel enables modeling of nonlinear, high-dimensional fMRI data.  
- Strength: Empirical results indicate improved performance over competing methods.  
- Weakness: No clear comparison with other deep learning–based fMRI methods.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses a limited number of datasets, possibly non-representative.  
- Weakness: Results need further validation, including robustness checks across experimental conditions.  
- Pro-accept: Novel method with promising empirical gains and potential to improve fMRI analysis.  
- Con-accept: Requires additional comparisons to deep learning baselines, more datasets, and robustness validation.  
- Rating: 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons, dataset breadth, and validation.
