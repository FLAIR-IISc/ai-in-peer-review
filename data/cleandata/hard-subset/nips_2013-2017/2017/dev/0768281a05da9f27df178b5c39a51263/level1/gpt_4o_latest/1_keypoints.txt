- DHA introduces a parametric deep-kernel using multi-layer neural networks to handle nonlinearity and high dimensionality in hyperalignment.
- The method uses rank-m SVD and SGD for optimization, achieving computational efficiency and scalability to large datasets.
- DHA does not require training data during alignment of new subjects, improving practical usability.
- Empirical results show DHA outperforms state-of-the-art HA methods in classification accuracy and runtime across multiple fMRI datasets.
- Technical innovation addresses limitations of fixed-kernel methods like KHA by offering flexible, adaptable transformations.
- Presentation is dense with heavy mathematical formulations and extensive supplements, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.
- The paper underexplores potential limitations such as sensitivity to hyperparameter tuning and interpretability of learned representations.
- Comparative evaluation is limited; additional baselines from non-HA or other deep learning fMRI methods would strengthen claims.
- Generality beyond fMRI (e.g., applicability to EEG or other neuroimaging modalities) is not demonstrated.
- For acceptance: the paper tackles a significant problem with a technically sound, theoretically supported approach and strong empirical validation.
- Against acceptance: concerns remain about presentation clarity and limited discussion of robustness and generalizability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address identified weaknesses.
