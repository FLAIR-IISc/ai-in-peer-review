- Presents YASS: a multistage "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" pipeline for spike sorting on dense MEAs.  
- Uses neural network-based detection, DP-GMM clustering, and matching pursuit deconvolution.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and methodologically easy to follow.  
- Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real datasets showing strong accuracy, stability, and computational efficiency.  
- Claim of state-of-the-art performance supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Novelty is questionable since components resemble prior work and related work discussion is insufficient.  
- Alignment evaluation via a classification task is not convincing; could conflate alignment with representation quality.  
- The non-uniqueness of matrix G is not addressed and may substantially affect results.  
- Reported best performance over parameter ranges risks bias from overfitting without nested cross-validation.  
- Unclear whether functional hyperalignment gains persist without spatial normalization; spatial normalization’s role is not discussed.  
- Paper meets most conference criteria: technically sound, well-supported claims, and clear presentation.  
- Recommend stronger comparison to existing pipelines and more direct alignment quality metrics.  
- Recommend addressing identifiability of G and using more robust, nested evaluation to ensure generalizability.
