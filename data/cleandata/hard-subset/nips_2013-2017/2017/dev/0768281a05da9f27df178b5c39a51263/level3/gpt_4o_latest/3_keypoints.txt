- Proposes Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), using deep neural networks as flexible parametric kernels for functional alignment in multi-subject fMRI.  
- DHA handles nonlinearity, high dimensionality, and large cohorts better than traditional hyperalignment methods.  
- Uses rank-m SVD and stochastic gradient descent for scalable, efficient optimization.  
- Experimental results show DHA outperforms state-of-the-art HA methods across multiple open fMRI datasets.  
- Thorough experiments with diverse datasets, competitive baselines, and ablation studies enhance the paper’s validity.  
- Detailed parameter settings are provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Highlights scalability advantages over KHA and CAE.  
- Methods section is dense and would benefit from clearer summaries or illustrative figures.  
- Paper omits dataset preprocessing details, which can materially affect fMRI results.  
- Leave-one-subject-out cross-validation may not fully capture generalization performance.  
- Reliance on author-generated baselines without comparisons to literature-reported results weakens evaluation.  
- Minor English clarity and repetition issues that need proofreading.  
- Overall recommendation: accept if authors address clarity, preprocessing detail, and validation concerns.
