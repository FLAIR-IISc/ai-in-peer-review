- Introduces YASS, a novel multistage spike-sorting pipeline for dense multi-electrode arrays combining NN detection, outlier triaging, and DP-GMM clustering.
- Pipeline is modular, scalable, robust, and leverages prior information for large dense MEA settings.
- Methodology is thoroughly explained with detailed proofs but the methods section is dense and hard to digest.
- Lacks a clear summary/figure of the final design and explicit parameter choices.
- Experiments are thorough across multiple open datasets and competing methods, showing impressive performance on synthetic and real data.
- Needs more detail on preprocessing steps and clearer training/validation/test splits.
- Manuscript quality is generally good but has occasional English problems and repetitive sentences that need proofreading.
- Should include comparable results from the literature to validate and increase credibility of reported performance.
- Strengths: significant neuroscience problem, novel and well-motivated solution, sound methodology, strong experimental results.
- Weaknesses: dense methods section, insufficient preprocessing and validation detail, missing comparative baselines from prior work.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses (summary/parameters, preprocessing/validation, literature comparisons).
