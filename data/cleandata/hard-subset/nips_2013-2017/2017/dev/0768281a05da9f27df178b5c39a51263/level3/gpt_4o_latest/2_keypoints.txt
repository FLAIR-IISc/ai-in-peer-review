- Introduces Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), extending Hyperalignment with a deep network to handle nonlinearity and high-dimensional fMRI data.
- Uses stacked nonlinear transformations as a flexible kernel, overcoming fixed-kernel limits of methods like KHA.
- Employs rank-m SVD and stochastic gradient descent for efficient optimization and scalability.
- Eliminates need for training data when aligning new subjects, unlike prior approaches.
- Evaluated on five task-based and two movie-based fMRI datasets for robust empirical validation.
- DHA outperforms RHA, SVDHA, KHA, and CAE in classification accuracy and runtime efficiency.
- Feature selection experiments show DHA provides superior feature representations that boost classification.
- Runtime analysis indicates a favorable trade-off between computational cost and alignment accuracy.
- Paper is technically sound with convincing experimental setup and results.
- Section 3 (DHA formulation and optimization) is dense, highly mathematical, and hard to access for non-experts.
- Originality is strong: integrates deep learning into HA and reformulates the HA objective with a multi-layer kernel.
- Significant contribution due to improved scalability, flexibility, and potential impact on brain decoding and multi-subject analysis.
- Pros: innovative, strong empirical performance, scalable, thorough comparisons.
- Cons: Section 3 needs simplification; limited accessibility for readers without advanced math background.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify Section 3.
