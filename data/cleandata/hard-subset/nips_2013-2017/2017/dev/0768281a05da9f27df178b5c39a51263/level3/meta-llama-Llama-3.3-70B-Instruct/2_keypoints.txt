- Proposes YASS, a novel "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" spike-sorting pipeline improving robustness, scalability, and accuracy.  
- Pipeline comprises detection, feature extraction, collision screening, clustering, and template merging tailored for large MEA datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written but Section 3 is complex and needs clearer explanations or simplification.  
- Experimental results are convincing: YASS outperforms state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Demonstrates significant gains in accuracy, stability, and computational efficiency.  
- Thorough evaluation with multiple datasets and direct comparisons to other leading algorithms.  
- Provides detailed descriptions and implementation notes, aiding reproducibility.  
- Highlights scalability and robustness as critical for large-scale neuroscience applications.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of the pipeline’s limitations and potential future work.  
- Coreset construction algorithm description is unclear and could be simplified for accessibility.  
- Pro-acceptance points: novel/significant contribution, state-of-the-art performance, thorough evaluation, reproducibility.  
- Con-acceptance points: complexity in some sections, insufficient limitations/future-work discussion, unclear coreset description.
