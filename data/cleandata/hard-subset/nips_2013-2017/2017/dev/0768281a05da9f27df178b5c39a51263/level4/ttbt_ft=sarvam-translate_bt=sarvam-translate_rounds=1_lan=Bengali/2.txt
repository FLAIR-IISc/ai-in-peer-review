This work describes an extension of the Hyperalignment (HA) method, specifically functional alignment between different subjects. This extension directly addresses some of the limitations of previous HA methods in the literature: non-linearity, scalability, and dimensionality. The proposed method, known as Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), defines the objective function as minimizing the objective function by re-formulating it under the condition of multiple stacked non-linear transformations, including a (multi-layer) kernel function. The objective function is re-formulated under the conditions of templates and then eigendecomposition. The explicit derivative is determined. Thus, optimization can be performed even for datasets with a large number of subjects. The proposed method has been tested using standard SVM classification and compared with major HA algorithms in the literature. The experimental setup was based on 5 task-based datasets and 2 movie-based datasets. DHA shows improved performance in all cases compared to other algorithms.

The paper is well-written, although it is complex to read in section 3 ("Deep Hyperalignment"). I request the authors to make that part more accessible. I do not see any major problems in the paper, but I am not an expert on the mathematical derivation of the proposed method. The experimental evidence is convincing and significant.
