1. Paper proposes neural-network-based hyperalignment replacing basis functions, reporting higher fMRI classification accuracy and lower computation vs another deep model.  
2. Novelty is unclear—method seems to combine prior work, and replacing basis functions with networks may complicate optimization while borrowing heavily from earlier optimization procedures.  
3. Missing citation: the paper should reference Georg Lang’s work on functional alignment.  
4. Evaluation via classification confounds alignment quality with representational power; no visualizations of aligned activation patterns and unclear how to map aligned data back to anatomical space for interpretability.  
5. Non-uniqueness of the alignment transform G could materially affect results.  
6. Reported results risk overfitting/bias because they present best performance across parameter ranges rather than using proper nested cross-validation; choice of parameter ranges can strongly influence outcomes.  
7. It’s unclear whether the performance gains persist without spatial normalization, since hyperalignment is typically assumed to obviate pre-anatomical alignment.
