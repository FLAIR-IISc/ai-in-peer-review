Deep Hyperalignment

[Due to time constraints, I have not read the entire article in detail.]

The problem of effective alignment or functional alignment of multi-subject FMRI datasets is a fundamental and challenging problem in neuroimaging. Hyperalignment is a prominent class of non-anatomy functional alignment methods. The current manuscript presents an extension of this using a deep NN kernel. I think it is novel, although I do not know enough literature on this subject to be confident. Although it is not very surprising research, it is a natural and promising method to consider, and the experimental results are great.

The methods are explained in detail, and there is detailed evidence in the supporting materials. However, I found this section to be very complex and without a good summary of the final design and parameter choices. A diagram of the method would have been very helpful. Related works have been well surveyed. The new method can be described as a combination of several existing methods. However, the various components are thought of in a novel way and implemented carefully.

The experiments are impressively thorough, involving several open datasets and several competing methods, with detailed reports of the methods, including the parameters used for each alignment method. Some more details about pre-processing would have been helpful. The parameter values of the experimental data seem to have some validity ("the best performance is reported for each dataset" page 6), which is not ideal, but the number of parameter values tested is small, and the results are so good that I think they could stand up to better training/validity/test partitioning. My main concern about this section is that all reported results are computed by the current authors; it would also be helpful to identify comparable results in the literature.

The English language standard is good in most cases, but there are occasional problems. For example, "time complexity roughly scales with the size of the data" is ambiguous, and the sentence in which it appears is repeated three times in the manuscript (once in abstract). Some proof-reading is needed.
