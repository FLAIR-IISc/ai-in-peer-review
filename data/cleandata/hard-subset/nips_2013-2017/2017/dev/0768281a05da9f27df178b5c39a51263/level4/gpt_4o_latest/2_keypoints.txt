- Presents an enhancement to hyperalignment (HA) for functional alignment across subjects.  
- Addresses key limitations of existing HA methods: nonlinearity, scalability, and high dimensionality.  
- Introduces deep hyperalignment (DHA) using multiple stacked nonlinear transformations via a multi-layer kernel.  
- Reformulates the HA objective in terms of templates and eigendecomposition with an explicit gradient derivation.  
- Enables feasible optimization for datasets with large numbers of subjects.  
- Evaluates method with standard SVM classification and benchmarks against leading HA algorithms.  
- Experiments on five task-based and two movie-based datasets show DHA consistently outperforms alternatives.  
- Manuscript is well written but Section 3 ("Deep Hyperalignment") is dense and difficult to follow; recommend clarifying.  
- Reviewer not expert in the math but finds the experimental results compelling and showing significant improvements.
