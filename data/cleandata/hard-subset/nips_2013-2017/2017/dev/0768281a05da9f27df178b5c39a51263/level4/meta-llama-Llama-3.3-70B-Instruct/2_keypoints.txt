- Proposes deep hyperalignment (DHA), an enhanced hyperalignment technique for functional alignment across subjects.
- Claims DHA addresses key limitations of existing HA: nonlinearity, scalability, and high-dimensionality.
- Redefines the HA objective via multi-layer nonlinear transformations using a multi-layer kernel to enable large-scale optimization.
- Reformulates the objective with templates and eigendecomposition and derives an explicit derivative for optimization.
- Evaluates DHA with standard SVM classification against prominent HA algorithms.
- Experiments conducted on five task-based and two movie-based datasets.
- Reports that DHA outperforms other algorithms in all tested cases.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, though Section 3 could be simplified for readability.
- Reviewer not expert in the math but finds the experimental evidence compelling and notes no major issues.
