- Problem: functional alignment of multi-subject fMRI datasets is longstanding and challenging.
- Contribution: proposes a deep neural network kernel extension of hyperalignment that appears novel.
- Direction is logical and promising; experimental results are outstanding.
- Methodology is thoroughly explained with detailed proofs in the supplement but is overly dense and lacks a concise summary of final design and parameter choices.
- Missing a figure illustrating the method to aid clarity.
- Related work is well surveyed; the method is a thoughtful combination of existing approaches implemented in a novel way.
- Experiments are comprehensive across multiple open datasets and competing methods, with detailed parameter reporting.
- Missing preprocessing details; potential issue that parameter validation appears to have used test data, though the parameter grid was small and results likely robust.
- All reported results were computed by the authors; paper should reference comparable results from existing literature.
- English is generally good but contains occasional unclear phrasing and repeated sentences (e.g., “time complexity fairly scales with data size”); proofreading needed.
