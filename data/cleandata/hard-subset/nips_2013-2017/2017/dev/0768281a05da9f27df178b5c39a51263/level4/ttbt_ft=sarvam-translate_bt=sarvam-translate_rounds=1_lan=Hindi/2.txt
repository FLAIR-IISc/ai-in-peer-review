This work describes an extension of the Hyperalignment (HA) method, specifically functional alignment across subjects. This extension directly addresses some of the limitations of previous HA methods in the literature: non-linearity, scalability, and high dimensionality. The proposed method, called Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), defines the HA objective function by re-formulating it with a (multi-layer) kernel function in terms of multiple stacked layers of non-linear transformations. The objective function is then re-formulated in terms of templates and then eigendecomposition, resulting in clear derivations. Thus, it can be adapted even for datasets with a large number of subjects. The proposed method is tested using standard SVM classification and compared with the main HA algorithms in the literature. The experimental setup is based on 5 task-based datasets and 2 movie-based datasets. DHA shows better performance in all cases, compared to other algorithms.

This paper is well-written, even though reading section 3 ("Deep Hyperalignment") can be complex. I invite the authors to make that part more accessible. I don't see any major problems with the paper, but I am not an expert in the mathematical derivation of the proposed method. The experimental evidence is convincing and significant.
