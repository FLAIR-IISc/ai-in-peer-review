1. Originality is questionable — replacing basis functions with neural nets seems derivative and may introduce harder optimization; the optimization procedure appears inspired by prior work.  
2. Important related work (e.g., Georg Lang) is missing from the references.  
3. Using classification as the evaluation confounds alignment quality with representational power, and the paper does not show how to map aligned data back to anatomical space for interpretation.  
4. The non-uniqueness of the alignment matrix G could substantially affect results and requires further analysis.  
5. Reported results risk bias/overfitting because they report best performance over parameter ranges instead of using proper nested cross-validation; flexible models and parameter choices may unduly influence outcomes.  
6. It is essential to verify whether the performance improvement persists without spatial/anatomical normalization, since functional hyperalignment should not require pre-alignment.
