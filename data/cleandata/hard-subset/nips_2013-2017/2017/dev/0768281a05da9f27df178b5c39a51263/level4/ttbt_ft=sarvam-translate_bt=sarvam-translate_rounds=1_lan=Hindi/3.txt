Deep Hyperalignment

[Due to time constraints, I did not read the entire paper in detail.]

The problem of functional alignment of multi-subject fMRI datasets is a fundamental and difficult problem in neuroimaging. Hyperalignment is a major class of anatomy-free functional alignment methods. The presented manuscript presents an extension of this using a deep neural kernel. I believe this is new, although I don't have enough knowledge to be confident about it. While this is not a surprising research direction, it is a natural and promising approach to consider, and the experimental results are excellent.

The methods are well explained with detailed evidence in the supplementary material. However, I found this section very difficult and lacking a good summary of the final design and parameter options. A figure illustrating the method would have been very helpful. The related work is well surveyed. The new method can be described as a mixture of several existing approaches. Although various elements are thoughtfully combined in a new way, and carefully implemented.

The experiments are impressively thorough, including several open datasets and several competing methods, with detailed reporting of the methods, including the parameters used for each alignment method. Some more details on preprocessing would have been helpful. It seems that some parameter validation has been done on the test data ("best performance reported for each dataset" p6), which is not ideal, although the number of parameter values tested is small and the results are so good that I think they could withstand better training/validation/test partitions. My main concern about this section is that all the results reported were computed by the current authors; it would also be helpful to point out any comparable results in the literature.

The quality of the English is mostly good but sometimes with problems. For example, "time complexity increases significantly with data size" is unclear, and the sentence in which it appears is repeated three times in the manuscript (once in the abstract as well). Some proof-reading is needed.
