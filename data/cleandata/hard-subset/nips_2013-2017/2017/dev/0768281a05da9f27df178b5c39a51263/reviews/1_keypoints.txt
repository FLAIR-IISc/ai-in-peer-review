- Proposes neural-network-based hyperalignment (like kernel hyperalignment) and reports higher classification accuracy and lower computation time on fMRI datasets.  
- Novelty is unclear — approach seems to combine prior contributions and the resulting optimization may be harder without clear new objectives.  
- Missing citations: works on functional alignment by Georg Lang should be referenced.  
- Evaluation concern: classification results don't prove better subject correspondence; need visual alignment of activation patterns and a way to map aligned data back to anatomical space.  
- Non-uniqueness of the alignment matrix G raises questions about its impact on results.  
- Risk of overfitting/bias: reporting best performance over parameter ranges without nested cross-validation favors more flexible models and depends on chosen parameter grids.  
- Question whether performance gains persist without spatial normalization, since functional hyperalignment may not require pre-anatomical alignment.
