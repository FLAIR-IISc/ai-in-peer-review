- Paper proposes Deep Hyperalignment (DHA), an extension of hyperalignment for functional alignment across subjects.  
- DHA addresses prior HA limitations: nonlinearity, scalability, and high-dimensionality.  
- Method models HA with stacked nonlinear transformations and a multi-layer kernel.  
- Objective is reformulated using templates and eigendecomposition, with explicit derivative provided.  
- Derivation enables optimization on datasets with large numbers of subjects.  
- Evaluated using standard SVM classification against main HA algorithms.  
- Experiments use 5 task-based datasets and 2 movie-based datasets.  
- DHA outperforms competing algorithms in all reported cases.  
- Paper is well written overall, though Section 3 ("Deep Hyperalignment") is complex and should be made more accessible.  
- Reviewer is not an expert in the mathematical derivations but finds no major issues.  
- Experimental evidence is convincing and significant.
