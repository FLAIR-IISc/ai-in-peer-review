- Introduces YASS, a novel, well-engineered pipeline for spike sorting in dense MEA recordings with significant field contribution.  
- Combines neural network-based spike detection, DP-GMM clustering, and a modular "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" design.  
- Demonstrates superior accuracy, stability, and computational efficiency versus Kilosort, Spyking Circus, and MountainSort, especially at low SNR.  
- Modular and scalable architecture enables future updates and parallelization.  
- Particularly strong performance on retinal MEA data with fewer low-stability clusters.  
- Major reproducibility concern: code is not publicly available, preventing independent validation and adoption.  
- Insufficient description of the neural network spike detector, especially how training data are generated and validated.  
- Use of synthetic training data is inadequately justified; unclear whether it faithfully represents real recordings.  
- Limited generalizability to cortical data due to unaddressed waveform drift and lack of realistic cortical training data.  
- Minor clarity issues: unclear Y-axis labeling in Figure 3 and insufficient description of the spatial whitening procedure.  
- Recommendation: promising paper for retinal MEA sorting but requires major revisions—release code and clarify training/synthetic-data methods for acceptance.
