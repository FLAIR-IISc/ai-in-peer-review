- Presents YASS, a well-engineered pipeline for multi-electrode array spike sorting that combines state-of-the-art methods.
- Significant contribution: the pipeline outperforms other methods in accuracy and stability on synthetic and real datasets.
- Clear, well-organized structure makes the methodology easy to follow.
- Innovative "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" approach effectively handles collisions and noisy waveforms.
- Incorporates a neural-network detector and efficient outlier triaging to boost robustness and scalability.
- Major concern: code is not publicly available, limiting reproducibility and practical value.
- Major concern: poor description of the neural-network detection method, including how training data were generated and which architecture/training procedure was used.
- Applicability to cortical data is uncertain due to waveform drift and limited availability of suitable training data.
- Minor issues: odd y-axis label in Figure 3 and missing description of the spatial whitening procedure.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and significance; methodology is technically sound and claims are supported.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong performance, innovative handling of collisions/noise, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of code, inadequate NN description, and uncertain generalization to cortical datasets; revisions needed to address these.
