- Introduces a novel subgame-solving technique (Reach subgame solving) that outperforms prior methods in theory and practice.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks including heads-up no-limit Texas hold'em and shows potential to beat top human professionals.  
- Thorough analysis of limitations of existing subgame-solving techniques.  
- Reach subgame solving explicitly accounts for opponent potential actions and their impact on outcomes.  
- Provides detailed mathematical formulations and algorithmic details to aid understanding and replication.  
- Writing is sometimes opaque; presentation needs clearer exposition and more critical math/algorithmic details in the main paper.  
- Comparisons between the proposed method, YASS, and other approaches are unclear, especially regarding which methods require hand-labeled data.  
- Novelty of the YASS pipeline is questionable because many components appear derivative of prior work.  
- Authors should include a performance breakdown to isolate the impact of their novel contributions.  
- Design choice to discard signals that appear on multiple electrodes may be disadvantageous and complicates fair comparison with methods like Kilosort.  
- Results are impressive and the authors’ commitment to providing a performance breakdown is commendable.  
- The work is significant for advancing imperfect-information game solving and has potential applications in negotiation, auctions, and cybersecurity.
