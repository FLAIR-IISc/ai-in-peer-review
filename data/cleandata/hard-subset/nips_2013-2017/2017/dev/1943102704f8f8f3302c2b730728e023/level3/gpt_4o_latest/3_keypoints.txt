- Contribution: Proposes YASS, a modular "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" spike-sorting pipeline combining neural-network detection, outlier triage, DP-GMM clustering, and matching-pursuit deconvolution for dense MEAs.
- Evaluation: Demonstrates improved accuracy, stability, and runtime vs KiloSort, Spyking Circus, and MountainSort on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength — Relevance: Tackles an important, timely bottleneck in large-scale electrophysiology aligning with conference goals.
- Strength — Scalability & Modularity: Uses coreset summarization and parallelization; design supports iterative improvements and large datasets.
- Strength — Performance: Shows strong empirical gains, especially in low SNR regimes, across multiple dataset types.
- Strength — Innovation: Novel hybrid integration of NN detection, DP-GMM clustering, and matching pursuit tailored to dense MEA data.
- Weakness — Clarity: Overview section is dense and hard to follow, reducing accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness — Algorithm 1: Pseudocode is redundant and uninformative; needs a clearer or more illustrative presentation.
- Weakness — Transparency/Practicality: NN training depends on prior labeled data not available in many labs; key technical justifications are buried in supplements.
- Weakness — Complexity: Pipeline feels ad hoc and possibly overly complex, which may hinder practical adoption.
- Acceptance Pros: Addresses critical problem, shows SOTA empirical performance, and offers a scalable, modular design.
- Acceptance Cons: Presentation, transparency, and reliance on prior training data raise concerns about reproducibility and broad applicability.
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance pending clearer overview, improved pseudocode, and more detailed justifications moved into the main text.
