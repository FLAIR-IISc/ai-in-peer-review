- Technically sound: NN-based detection + coreset clustering + divide-and-conquer shows improved accuracy, stability, and runtime on synthetic and real data.  
- Highly scalable: processes a 512-electrode dataset faster than real-time on a single CPU core using divide-and-conquer and coreset strategies.  
- Clear and reproducible: well-organized paper with pseudocode and supplemental material; modular design facilitates reuse and improvement.  
- Novel: combines neural-network detection, coreset construction, and Bayesian clustering for dense MEA spike sorting and addresses collisions/noisy waveforms.  
- Significant impact: tackles a key bottleneck in neuroscience; open-source release increases community utility.  
- Limited generalization: reliance on prior training data may reduce applicability across different MEA setups or experimental conditions.  
- Narrow comparison scope: evaluated mainly against KiloSort, Spyking Circus, and MountainSort; more baselines would strengthen claims.  
- Nonstationarity not fully addressed: unclear how the method handles substantial waveform drift in long-term recordings.  
- Limited GPU utilization: most stages lack GPU acceleration, which could hinder real-time performance on larger datasets.  
- Suggest evaluating on more diverse datasets and setups to assess generalizability.  
- Suggest discussing NN detection limitations when training data is scarce.  
- Suggest exploring methods to handle significant waveform nonstationarity/drift.  
- Suggest further parallelization and GPU acceleration to improve runtime on larger datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept — innovative, scalable, and impactful with strong validation; address weaknesses in future work.
