- Proposes a novel multistage spike-sorting pipeline for dense MEAs (detection, triage, clustering, pursuit).
- Uses a mix of techniques: neural network detection, outlier triaging, nonparametric Bayesian clustering, and matching-pursuit deconvolution.
- Claims superior accuracy, stability, and computational efficiency versus existing methods.
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (thousands of electrodes, millions of spikes; high-dimensional feature spaces).
- Supports claims with extensive experiments on simulated and real datasets and detailed analysis.
- Paper is well-written; pseudocode and supplements clarify technical details.
- Pipeline is modular and flexible, facilitating integration of new methods.
- Limitation: may struggle with significant non-stationarity or template drift.
- Limitation: reliance on prior training data can limit applicability to some datasets.
- Further development is needed to address these limitations and broaden applicability.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential to enable large-scale neuroscience research.
