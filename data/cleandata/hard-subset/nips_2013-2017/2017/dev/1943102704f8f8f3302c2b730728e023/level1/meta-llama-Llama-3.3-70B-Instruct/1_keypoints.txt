- Paper presents YASS, a novel "triage-then-cluster-then-pursuit" pipeline for spike sorting on dense MEAs.  
- YASS combines neural-network-based detection, DP-GMM clustering, and deconvolution to improve accuracy, stability, and scalability.  
- Authors compare YASS to KiloSort, Spyking Circus, and MountainSort and report superior accuracy and stability on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Computational analysis claims several-fold speedup in template and cluster estimation versus the next fastest competitor (KiloSort).  
- Strength: highly scalable to large datasets with thousands of electrodes.  
- Strength: neural-network detection plus DP-GMM clustering yields efficient and accurate sorting.  
- Strength: detailed evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrating effectiveness across scenarios.  
- Strength: modular pipeline design that can be updated or adapted to different data/conditions.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison of computational resources required versus other methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient evaluation on datasets with high noise levels or non-stationarity.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential limitations or biases of the pipeline.  
- Pro-accept: presents a novel, effective, and scalable spike-sorting pipeline with thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Con-accept: missing resource comparisons, robustness tests under noise/non-stationarity, and detailed limitations discussion.  
- Overall quality score: 9/10.  
- Clarity score: 9/10.  
- Originality score: 8/10 (pipeline is novel though components individually are not entirely new).  
- Significance score: 9/10 (potential major impact and wide use in neuroscience research).
