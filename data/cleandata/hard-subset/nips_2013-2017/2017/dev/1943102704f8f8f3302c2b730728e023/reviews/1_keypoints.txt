- Reviewer maintains positive rating after author response and is optimistic authors will address concerns.
- Paper is valuable and well-engineered, combining state-of-the-art methods and advancing retinal MEA spike sorting.
- Major concern: code is not publicly available; reviewer strongly urges releasing implementation.
- Major concern: neural-network detection is poorly described and training-data generation is unclear (which method or combination was used).
- If pre-existing sorts were used for training, reviewer asks how labels were generated, how misalignments/label noise were handled, and why a NN was needed over the base algorithm.
- If synthetic training data were used, reviewer wants to know whether it was used for augmentation or primary training and for evidence that synthetic data is representative.
- Major concern: generalization to cortical data is questionable and should be explicitly discussed/acknowledged.
- Specific cortical issues: waveform drift is common and must be modeled for long recordings; current pipeline may not handle this.
- Specific cortical issues: obtaining good NN training data for cortex is difficult; synthetic overlay of templates may be unrealistic due to background spike-driven noise and correlated firing.
- Minor: Fig.3 y-axis notation (1^-x) is confusing and exaggerates tiny accuracy differences.
- Minor: spatial whitening procedure is referenced but not described in manuscript or supplement.
