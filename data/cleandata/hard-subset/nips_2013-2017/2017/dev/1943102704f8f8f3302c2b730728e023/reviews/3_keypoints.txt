- Addresses an important, contemporary problem: spike detection and sorting on large-scale MEAs.  
- Scaling to 10^4–10^6 electrodes is challenging and motivates the work.  
- Authors correctly emphasize robustness, scalability, modularity, and use of priors.  
- Authors demonstrate strong familiarity with methods and the problem domain.  
- Algorithm 1 is placed early, unreadable, and adds little beyond the text.  
- Overview section is important but poorly written and covers many topics too quickly.  
- The neural-net training stage is compelling but opaque and hard to evaluate in practice.  
- Reliance on potentially lab-specific training data raises concerns about generalizability.  
- Simulations provided are good, but it’s difficult to assess real-world veracity of the methods.  
- Method is complex, somewhat ad hoc, and presentation lacks transparency.  
- Technical justifications are missing from the main paper or relegated to extensive supplementary material.
