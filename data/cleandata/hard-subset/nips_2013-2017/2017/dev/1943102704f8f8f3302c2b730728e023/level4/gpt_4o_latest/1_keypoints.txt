- Overall positive: paper is valuable and well-written; reviewer optimistic authors will address concerns.  
- Code availability: authors did not provide public code — release is needed for reproducibility.  
- Neural-network detector under-described: manuscript fails to specify how detector training data were generated.  
- Pre-existing-sorts concern: unclear whether and how labeled snippets were obtained/cleaned, how misalignment/label noise were handled, and why an NN was needed.  
- Synthetic-data concern: unclear if synthetic waveforms were actually used, how they were generated, and whether they realistically reflect recordings.  
- Cortical generalization: pipeline’s applicability to cortical data is questionable and should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Waveform drift: cortical recordings face significant drift issues (especially non-chronic) that the paper does not address.  
- Cortical training-data challenges: high-quality ground truth is scarce and synthetic data may fail due to background spikes and correlated firing.  
- Minor — Figure 3: y-axis labeling ambiguous ("1^-x" vs "10^-x") and plot overstates trivial accuracy differences near perfection.  
- Minor — spatial whitening: manuscript/supplement lack a description of the spatial whitening procedure.
