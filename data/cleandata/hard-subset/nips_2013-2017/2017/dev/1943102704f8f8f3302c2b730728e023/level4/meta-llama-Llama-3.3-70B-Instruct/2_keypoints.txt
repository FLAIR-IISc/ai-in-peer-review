3. YASS shows superior performance but requires hand-labeled data for detection, making comparisons with unsupervised methods non-equivalent and adding user burden.

5. Method reportedly discards signals present on multiple electrodes, keeping only the largest-amplitude waveform—this differs from Kilosort and may bias comparisons; clarification on overlapping-signal handling and application setting is needed.  
6. Paper writing is opaque; key mathematical and algorithmic details are buried in supplements and should be moved into the main text for clarity.
