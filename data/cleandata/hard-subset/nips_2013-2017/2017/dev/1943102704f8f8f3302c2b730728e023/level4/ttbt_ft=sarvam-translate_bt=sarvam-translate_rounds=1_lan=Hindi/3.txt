This research paper addresses a significant contemporary problem of spike detection and sorting on multi-electrode arrays (MEA). Efficient algorithms are crucial for this problem, as the number of such experiments and neurons recorded simultaneously has increased. The ability to scale to electrode levels of 10^4 to 10^6 is challenging and requires sophisticated analysis methods.

The authors have correctly identified key qualities such as robustness, scalability, modularity, and the use of prior knowledge in the development of powerful algorithms. The paper utilizes a sufficient amount of methods and machinery with which the authors are well-versed and understand the problem area well.

Placing algorithm 1 at the beginning of the paper is not particularly helpful or readable, and it does not really provide anything that the text description does not offer. The observation section is important to contact first, but the writing here is not as clear as it could be, and it quickly moves on to a wide range of topics.

Determining the true nature of the proposed algorithm is very difficult. The neural net training phase is particularly attractive, but it is difficult to understand how it will work. Having training data, which could potentially be different for each laboratory to use, can be problematic, and it is difficult to assess how well it will actually work, although the authors provide some good simulations.
