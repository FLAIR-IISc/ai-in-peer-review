- Paper tackles finite-sum optimization in the SAGA framework, extending Leblond et al. (2017) to composite problems with non-smooth separable regularization.  
- Main contribution: a method to handle non-smooth regularization while preserving sparse updates; the splitting technique is interesting.  
- Analysis largely follows Leblond et al. (2017); asynchronous proof builds on Mania et al. (2015).  
- Major: "inconsistent read" is not clearly explained—what exactly is \hat{x}_k and how is index k determined; compare/contrast with Lian et al. (2016) and Liu & Wright (2015).  
- Major: derivation of the concise representation for the difference between \hat{x}_t and x_t is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Major: linear convergence rate and speedup claims depend on several implicit assumptions that must be stated explicitly.  
- Minor/typo: definition of Δ on line 217 is incorrect.  
- Missing citation: Asynchronous Parallel Greedy Coordinate Descent (NIPS 2016) is relevant and should be cited.
