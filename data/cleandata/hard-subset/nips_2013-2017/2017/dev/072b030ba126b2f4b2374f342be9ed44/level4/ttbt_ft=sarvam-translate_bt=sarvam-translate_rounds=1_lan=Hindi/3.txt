The main contribution of this paper is presenting a convergence proof for minimizing sum phi(x) + g(x), where phi(x) is smooth and g is non-smooth, in an asynchronous setting. The problem is well-motivated; to my knowledge, there is actually no known proof for it.

An important aspect of this work is the block-decomposability of the variable, which allows for some decomposition gain in the प्रॉक्स (prox) step (since the gradient of each phi can only affect certain indices of x). This is an important practical assumption, otherwise प्रॉक्स (prox) causes locks, which can be unavoidable. (It would also be interesting to see whether element-wise प्रॉक्स (prox), such as compression, can also be decomposed.)

It has two main theoretical results. Theorem 1 gives a convergence rate for प्रॉक्स (prox)SAGA, which is progressively better than the previous result. Theorem 2 gives a rate for an asynchronous setting, which is more unprecedented.

Overall, I think this is a good paper, with a very good theoretical contribution that is also very useful in practice. I think it would be even stronger without the rarity assumption, but the authors also say that investigating asynchronousity without rarity for non-smooth functions is an open problem, so it is accepted.
