This paper proposes an asynchronous variant of SAGA, a stochastic gradient method for finite sum convex optimization (Defazio et al, 2014). According to my understanding, this paper discusses two challenges: asynchronousity and proximity. The authors have achieved three improvements compared to the original SAGA. Firstly, they proposed a sparse update according to the block coordinate, where increased support for the gradient is used when the regulation is decomposable. Secondly, they designed an asynchronous variant of SAGA where the amount of delay can be up to sqrt(n)/10. Finally, they can deal with non-smooth regulators through proximal operators. In terms of convergence results, they still achieve a linear convergence rate under the strong convexity of the individual lipsheet gradients of the overall sum function f and fi. Although slightly smaller than step-size SAGA, the convergence factor is comparable, possibly due to different assumptions.

In fact, this paper has improved all these techniques by combining several advanced concepts from existing work like SAGA, such as variance reduction, sparse updates, hogwild, asynchronousity such as arock, etc. The proof is quite clean and well-organized.

In my opinion, such improvements are significant and important for practice for obvious reasons. The numerical results also strongly support their theoretical contributions. These experiments were conducted on three large-scale datasets and empirically show a linear speed increase of the new algorithm.

Overall, this paper has made significant contributions to both theory and experiment. It is worthy of acceptance by NIPS.
