This paper considers finite addition optimization solutions through the SAGA framework. The work considers composite optimization from LeBlond et al. 2017, where the additional component is non-smooth partition regularization. The main improvement is how to handle non-smooth regularization while adhering to the sparse update principle. The proposed technique for partitioning non-smooth regularization seems appealing to me. The analysis primarily follows the framework of LeBlond et al. 2017, and the concept of Asynchronous Variant from Manya et al. 2015.

Major Comments:
- The authors' "inconsistent reading" needs to be clarified more. In particular, what hat{x}_k is and how to determine the index of k. Is this similar to LeBlond et al. 2016 and Liu et al. 2015? What is the main difference?
- The difference between hat{x}t and xt is unclear to me as to how to get a neat representation.
- To obtain linear convergence rates and speedups, this paper makes several implicit assumptions. The assumption used by the authors in this paper should be explicitly stated.

Minor Comments / Typology:
- The definition of \Delta in line 217 was incorrect.
