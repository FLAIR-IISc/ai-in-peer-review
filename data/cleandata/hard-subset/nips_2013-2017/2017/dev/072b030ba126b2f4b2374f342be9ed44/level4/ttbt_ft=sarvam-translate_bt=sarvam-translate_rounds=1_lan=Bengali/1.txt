This paper considers finite sum optimization solutions through the SAGA framework. The work considers LeBlond et al. 2017's composite optimization, where the additional component is non-smooth separable regularization. The main improvement is how to handle non-smooth regularization while adhering to the sparse update principle. The proposed technique for splitting non-smooth regularization seems appealing to me. The analysis primarily follows LeBlond et al. 2017's framework, following the concept of Asynchronous Variant by Manya et al. 2015.

Major Comments:
- The authors' "inconsistent read" needs to be more clearly explained. In particular, what hat{x}_k is and how to determine the index of k. Is this like LeBlond et al. 2016 and Liu et al. 2015? What is the main difference?
- It is unclear to me how a neat presentation of the difference between hat{x}t and xt can be obtained.
- To obtain linear convergence rates and speedups, this paper makes several implicit assumptions. The assumption used by the authors in this paper should be explicitly stated.

Minor Comments / Typologies:
- The definition of \Delta in line 217 was incorrect.
