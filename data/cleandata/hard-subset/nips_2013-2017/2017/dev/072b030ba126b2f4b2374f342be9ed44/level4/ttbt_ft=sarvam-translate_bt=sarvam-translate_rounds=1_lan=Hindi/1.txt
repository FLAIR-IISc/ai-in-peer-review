This paper explores solving finite sum optimization through the SAGA framework. It moves beyond LeBlond et al. 2017 and considers aggregate optimization, where the additional part is non-smooth separable regularization. The main improvement lies in how to deal with non-smooth regularization while adhering to the theory of sparse updates. The proposed trick to divide non-smooth regularization seems interesting to me. The analysis essentially follows the framework given in LeBlond et al. 2017, and the proof for asynchronous variants follows the idea given in Menya et al. 2015.

Key Comments:
- The authors need to further clarify the "inconsistent read." Specifically, what hat{x}_k is and how to decide on the index of k. Is this the same as Lian et al. 2016 and Liu and Wright 2015? What is the main difference?
- It is not clear to me how to accurately represent the difference between hat{x}t and xt.
- To achieve linear convergence rate and speed, this paper makes several underlying assumptions. The authors should clearly indicate the assumption used in this paper.
