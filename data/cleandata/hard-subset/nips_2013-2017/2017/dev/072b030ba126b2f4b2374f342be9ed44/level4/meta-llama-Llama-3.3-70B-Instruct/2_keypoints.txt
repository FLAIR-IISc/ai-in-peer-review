- Introduces sparse block coordinate-wise updates using extended support for decomposable regularizers.  
- Develops an asynchronous variant that tolerates delays up to √n/10.  
- Incorporates proximal operators to handle nonsmooth regularizers.  
- Proves linear convergence under strong convexity of the sum and Lipschitz gradients of fi.  
- Uses a slightly smaller step-size than original SAGA, but achieves a comparable convergence factor (different assumptions).  
- Effectively integrates variance reduction, sparse updates, Hogwild-style asynchrony, and AROCK-like techniques.

- Empirical results on three large datasets show linear speedup, supporting theoretical claims.
