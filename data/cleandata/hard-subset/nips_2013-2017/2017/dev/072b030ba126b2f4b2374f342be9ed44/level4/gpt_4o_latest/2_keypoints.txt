- Introduces an asynchronous variant of SAGA addressing asynchronicity and proximity for finite-sum convex optimization.  
- Presents three main advances: sparse block-coordinate updates using extended support for decomposable regularizers.  
- Develops an asynchronous SAGA tolerating delays up to √n/10.  
- Extends SAGA to nonsmooth regularizers via proximal operators.  
- Retains linear convergence under strong convexity and Lipschitz gradients of individual fi.  
- Uses a slightly reduced step size versus original SAGA but achieves a comparable convergence factor.  
- Effectively integrates techniques from prior work (variance reduction, sparse updates, Hogwild, AROCK).  
- Proofs are well-structured and clearly presented.  
- Numerical experiments corroborate theory, showing linear speedup on three large-scale datasets.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical and practical contributions; recommends acceptance.  
- Minor issues: clarify notations (supp, Ω), ensure consistent phrasing and "step size" usage, and fix small typos/formatting (lines 46, 127, 152, 361).
