- Primary contribution: provides a convergence proof for minimizing ∑ fi(x) + g(x) with smooth fi and nonsmooth g in an asynchronous setting, addressing a gap in existing proofs.
- Central assumption: block separability of variables enables prox decomposition and avoids locking; practical but limits generality; question whether element-wise prox can fit the framework.
- Main results: Theorem 1 gives a proxSAGA convergence rate (slight improvement); Theorem 2 gives an asynchronous convergence rate (novel and impactful).
- Overall evaluation: strong paper with a valuable, practically relevant theoretical contribution; authors transparently acknowledge sparsity limitation.
- Major concern: Theorem 2 relies heavily on Leblond (2017), which assumes smooth g; unclear if those borrowed parts hold for nonsmooth g — needs clarification.
- Minor paper edits: remove unnecessary semicolon at Line 61; fix typo "a a" at Line 127.
- Proof issue (Eq. 14): sign appears reversed — should be x − z not z − x.
- Proof query (Line 425): clarify whether x^+ = x − γ v_i is intended.
- Lemma 7: triangle inequality used loosely — RHS could be halved.
- Reference/term errors: Line 435 mislabels the “third term” (should be “fourth term”); Line 444 unclear why E[Di] = I since Di seems problem-structure dependent.
- Equation/notation typos: Eq. (33) shows identical expressions (likely missing T_i); Line 447 unclear split/relationship among α_i, α_i^+, and ∇f_i; Line 448 mixes ∇f_i(x^*) and ∇f_i(x).
- Theorem 2 proof overall: skim appears reasonable but dependence on Leblond (2017) makes assumptions on g that must be explicitly stated.
