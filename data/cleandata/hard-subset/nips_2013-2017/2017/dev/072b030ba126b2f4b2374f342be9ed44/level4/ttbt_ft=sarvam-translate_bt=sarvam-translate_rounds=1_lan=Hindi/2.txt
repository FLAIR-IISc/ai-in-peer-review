This paper proposes an asynchronous type of SAGA, a stochastic gradient method for finite sum convex optimization (Defazio et al, 2014). According to my understanding, this paper addresses two challenges: asynchronousity and proximity. The authors have made three improvements compared to the original SAGA. First, they propose a sparse update according to the block coordinate using extended support for the gradient when regularization is decomposed. Second, they design an asynchronous type of SAGA where the delay amount can be up to sqrt(n)/10. Finally, they can deal with non-smooth regulators through proximal operators. In terms of convergence results, they still achieve a linear convergence rate under strong convexity of the individual lipschitz gradients of the overall sum function f and fi. Although the step-size is slightly smaller compared to step-size SAGA, the convergence factor remains comparable. This is probably due to different assumptions.

In fact, this paper combines several advanced ideas from existing works like SAGA, such as reduction in variance, sparse updates, hogwild, asynchronousization like arrow, etc., to improve all these techniques. The proof is quite clear and well-organized.

In my opinion, such improvements are important and significant in practice for obvious reasons. The numerical results also strongly support their theoretical contributions. These experiments are conducted on three large-scale data sets, and empirically show the linear speed of the new algorithm.

Overall, this paper has a significant contribution in terms of both theory and experiment. It is worthy of acceptance by NIPS.
