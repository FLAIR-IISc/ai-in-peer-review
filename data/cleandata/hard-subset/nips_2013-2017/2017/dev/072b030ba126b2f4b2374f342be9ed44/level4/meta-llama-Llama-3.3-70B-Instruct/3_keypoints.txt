- Paper gives a novel convergence proof for minimizing sum_i fi(x)+g(x) in an asynchronous setting, addressing a gap in existing theory.  
- Block separability of variables is assumed, enabling proximal decomposition and avoiding locks; exploring element-wise proximal operators (e.g., shrinkage) is suggested.  
- Theorem 1: improved convergence rate for proxSAGA compared to prior work.  
- Theorem 2: convergence rate in the asynchronous setting — the paper’s most significant and groundbreaking contribution.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contribution, but would be stronger without the sparsity assumption; authors note asynchrony without sparsity for nonsmooth g is an open problem.  
- Major concern: Theorem 2 relies heavily on Leblond (2017), which assumes smooth g; unclear if borrowed results require smoothness and g’s assumptions need clarification.  
- Minor paper typos: Line 61 needs a semicolon after “sparse”; Line 127 has “a a” to fix.  
- Proof issues (mainly Theorem 1): Eq. (14) sign mistake (should be x−z); Line 425 x+ should be defined as x−γv_i; Lemma 7 can be tightened (divide RHS by 2); Line 435 wrong term reference (third→fourth); Line 444 unclear justification for E D_i = I; Eq. (33) missing T_i; Line 447 unclear relation among α_i, α_i+, ∇f_i; Line 448 mistakenly uses ∇f_i(x) in place of ∇f_i(x*).  
- Despite these issues, problems are not fatal and Theorem 2’s proof looks reasonable aside from the Leblond dependency and g-assumption clarity.
