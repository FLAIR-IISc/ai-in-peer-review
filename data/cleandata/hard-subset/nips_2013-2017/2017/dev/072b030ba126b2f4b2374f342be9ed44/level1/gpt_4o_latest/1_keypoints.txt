- Introduces PROXASAGA, an asynchronous parallel algorithm extending SAGA to composite (nonsmooth) objectives with variance reduction and proximal support.
- Presents a sparse proximal variant of SAGA and its asynchronous extension with theoretical and empirical improvements over existing methods.
- Strength: Extends variance-reduced incremental gradient methods to the asynchronous, nonsmooth setting, filling a gap in the literature.
- Strength: Provides rigorous convergence proofs and conditions for linear speedup using the perturbed iterate framework and bounded overlap assumption.
- Strength: Empirical results show substantial runtime improvements over ASYSPCD and FISTA on large sparse problems, with thorough core-scaling analysis.
- Strength: Well-structured paper with clear algorithm exposition, theoretical analysis, experiments, and useful implementation details (e.g., memory compression).
- Weakness: Performance depends strongly on gradient sparsity and block-separable proximal terms, limiting generality to dense problems.
- Weakness: Maintains historical gradients and block reweighting, incurring memory overhead that may bottleneck very high-dimensional settings.
- Weakness: Lacks experimental comparisons on non-sparse datasets to assess limitations in less favorable scenarios.
- Weakness: Observed runtime speedup is sublinear due to memory access overhead; hardware-specific optimizations are not explored.
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important underexplored problem with a novel algorithm, strong theory, and significant empirical gains.
- Argument against acceptance: Dependence on sparsity and practical memory/throughput limits may reduce applicability and real-world benefits.
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial contribution likely to inspire further research despite noted limitations.
