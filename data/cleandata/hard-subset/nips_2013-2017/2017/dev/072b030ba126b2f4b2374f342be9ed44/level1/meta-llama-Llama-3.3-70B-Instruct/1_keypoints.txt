- Proposes PROXASAGA, an asynchronous parallel variant of SAGA for large-scale composite nonsmooth optimization.
- Introduces a sparse proximal SAGA that leverages gradient sparsity to reduce per-iteration cost.
- Extends the algorithm to an asynchronous parallel setting allowing inconsistent reads across cores.
- Provides convergence guarantees and proves a theoretical linear speedup under gradient sparsity and block-separable proximal terms.
- Empirical benchmarks show strong performance on large sparse datasets, with up to 12x speedup on a 20-core machine.
- Paper is well-written with a clear algorithmic description and comprehensive related-work comparison.
- Strength: addresses a key limitation of existing async SGD variants by handling nonsmooth objectives.
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis including convergence and speedup results.
- Strength: convincing empirical results demonstrating practical speedups.
- Weakness: relies on block-separable proximal term assumption, which may be restrictive in practice.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis of the proposed method.
- Weakness: does not explore extensions to other proximal incremental schemes (e.g., SGD, ProxSVRG).
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and recommended for acceptance, with suggested revisions to address weaknesses.
