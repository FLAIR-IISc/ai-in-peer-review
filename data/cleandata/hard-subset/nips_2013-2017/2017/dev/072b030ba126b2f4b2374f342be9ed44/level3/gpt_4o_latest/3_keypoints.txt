- Main contribution: Extends asynchronous stochastic optimization to nonsmooth composite objectives and proposes PROXASAGA, a sparse lock-free asynchronous SAGA variant with convergence and speedup guarantees.
- Theoretical contributions: Theorem 1 proves convergence for Sparse Proximal SAGA and Theorem 2 provides a novel convergence rate for the asynchronous nonsmooth setting.
- Practical relevance: Block separability enables efficient lock-free updates, yielding strong empirical speedups (up to 12x on 20 cores) and large improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- Clarity of contributions: Authors clearly position the work relative to ASAGA and ASYSPCD and highlight challenges in extending asynchronous methods to nonsmooth objectives.
- Open problems: Paper acknowledges reliance on sparsity and notes asynchrony without sparsity for nonsmooth functions as an important open direction.
- Weakness — proof dependence: Theorem 2 heavily borrows techniques from Leblond et al. (2017), raising questions about whether the smoothness assumption is fully avoided and about novelty.
- Weakness — minor errors: Typos, formatting issues, and equation/notation errors (e.g., eq. (14) reversed, unclear lines 425/447) reduce readability.
- Weakness — loose bounds: Some inequalities in proofs (e.g., Lemma 7) appear loose, potentially affecting the tightness of convergence guarantees.
- Weakness — limited proximal exploration: The paper does not investigate element-wise proximal decomposition, limiting broader applicability beyond block separability.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing proof-related concerns and improving manuscript clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel convergence proof for asynchronous nonsmooth objectives, strong empirical results, and clear identification of future research directions.
- Arguments against acceptance: Heavy reliance on prior work for key proofs, presentation errors and unclear notations, and limited exploration of alternative proximal decompositions.
- Overall verdict: Valuable theoretical and practical contribution that merits inclusion if reviewers' concerns are addressed.
