- Paper summary: PROXASAGA extends SAGA to asynchronous composite optimization with non-smooth separable regularizers via a novel sparse proximal SAGA variant, supported by theory and experiments.

Strengths:
- Novelty: Introduces a clever mechanism to handle non-smooth regularization while preserving sparse updates, enabling efficient Lasso/group Lasso optimization.
- Theoretical analysis: Provides rigorous convergence guarantees and shows linear speedup under sparsity and delay assumptions, building on prior asynchronous proof techniques.
- Empirical validation: Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming ASYSPCD and FISTA and achieving up to 12x speedup on 20 cores.
- Practical relevance: Easy to implement and well-suited for modern multi-core architectures.

Weaknesses:
- Clarity issues: The concept of "inconsistent read" and the indexing of ĥx_k need clearer explanation; derivation of differences between ĥx_t and x_t lacks detail.
- Implicit assumptions: Sparsity and bounded delay assumptions required for results are not explicitly stated upfront, harming reproducibility and generalizability.
- Missing reference: Omits citation to "Asynchronous parallel greedy coordinate descent, NIPS, 2016."
- Typographical error: Definition of Δ on line 217 contains a typo.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses an important problem with a novel, well-motivated solution.
- Strong theoretical and empirical results that clearly outperform existing methods.
- Highly relevant to the NIPS community working on optimization and large-scale ML.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Clarity and missing analytical details could limit accessibility to readers.
- Dependence on sparsity assumptions may limit applicability to certain problem classes.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance contingent on fixing clarity issues and explicitly stating convergence and speedup assumptions.
