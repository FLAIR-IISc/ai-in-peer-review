- Proposes augmenting VAEs with external memory and interpreting memory reads as a conditional mixture to enable variational inference for memory addressing.  
- Allows memory-module training guided by target information and is applied to generative few-shot learning.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough related-work positioning.  
- Technical approach is sound and experiments are well-designed.  
- Empirical results show the model finds relevant memory contents even with hundreds of unseen Omniglot characters.  
- Strength: novel combination of VAEs with external memory.  
- Strength: variational inference applied to memory addressing enables effective memory training.  
- Strength: demonstrates reliable approximate inference over memory locations.  
- Strength: outperforms or compares favorably to soft-attention baselines.  
- Weakness: uses a simple VAE architecture that may not scale to complex datasets.  
- Weakness: relies on a learned prior over memory addresses, which may be suboptimal in some settings.  
- Weakness: lacks exploration of alternative gradient estimators for discrete latent-variable models beyond VIMCO.  
- Acceptance argument: novel, significant contribution with solid experimental validation and comparisons.  
- Rejection argument: limited architecture complexity, dependence on learned address prior, and insufficient exploration of other gradient estimators.
