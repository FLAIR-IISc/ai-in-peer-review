- Paper extends SAGA to composite finite-sum optimization with non-smooth separable regularization and enforces sparse updates.  
- Key technical novelty: splitting the non-smooth regularizer to enable sparse updates; trick is interesting.  
- Analysis largely follows Leblond et al. (2017); asynchronous proof follows ideas from Mania et al. (2015).  
- Major: “inconsistent read” needs clearer explanation — define hat{x}_k, index k, and contrast with Lian et al. (2016) and Liu & Wright (2015).  
- Major: unclear derivation of the representation for the difference between hat{x}_t and x_t.  
- Major: several implicit assumptions underlie the linear convergence rate and speedup — authors should state them explicitly.  
- Minor/typo: definition of Δ (line 217) is incorrect.  
- Missing related work: Asynchronous parallel greedy coordinate descent (NIPS 2016) relevant to asynchronous greedy SDCA.
