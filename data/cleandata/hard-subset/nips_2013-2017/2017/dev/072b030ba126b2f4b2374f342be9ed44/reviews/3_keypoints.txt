- Main contribution: convergence proof for minimizing sum fi(x)+g(x) with smooth fi and nonsmooth g in an asynchronous setting.  
- Problem is well-motivated and addresses a gap (no known proof for this setting to the reviewer’s knowledge).  
- Key assumption: block separability of the variable enables prox decomposition and avoids locking; this is a practical and important assumption.  
- Suggestion: it would be interesting to see element-wise prox decompositions (e.g., shrinkage) handled as well.  
- Theoretical results: Theorem 1 gives a convergence rate for proxSAGA (incremental improvement over prior work).  
- Theoretical results: Theorem 2 provides a rate for the asynchronous setting (more novel/groundbreaking).  
- Overall assessment: good paper with a strong, practically useful theoretical contribution; would be stronger without the sparsity assumption but authors acknowledge that as an open problem.  
- Potential major concern: Theorem 2 relies heavily on Leblond 2017, which assumes smooth g; unclear whether borrowed results implicitly require g smoothness.  
- Minor paper edits: line 61 semicolon after “sparse”; line 127 has “a a”.  
- Proof comments (Theorem 1 and related): eq. (14) sign/order is reversed (x−z vs z−x).  
- Proof comments: line 425 likely typo—should x+ = x − γ v_i.  
- Proof comments: Lemma 7 can tighten RHS by dividing by 2 (triangle inequality used is loose).  
- Proof comments: line 435 refers to wrong term (says third term, should be fourth).
