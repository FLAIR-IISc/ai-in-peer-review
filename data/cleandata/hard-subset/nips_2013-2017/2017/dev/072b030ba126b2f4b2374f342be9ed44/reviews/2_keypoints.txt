- Proposes an asynchronous variant of SAGA addressing asynchronicity and proximal (nonsmooth) regularization.  
- Achieves three main improvements over SAGA: sparse block-coordinate updates using extended support for decomposable regularizers.  
- Designs an asynchronous SAGA that tolerates delays up to sqrt(n)/10.  
- Handles nonsmooth regularizers via proximal operators.  
- Proves linear convergence under strong convexity of the full objective and Lipschitz gradients of individual fi.  
- Uses a slightly smaller step-size than SAGA but attains a comparable convergence factor (likely due to different assumptions).  
- Combines ideas from prior work (SAGA, sparse updates, Hogwild, AROCK) with a clean, well-organized proof.  
- Empirical results on three large-scale datasets strongly support theory, showing near-linear speedup.  
- Reviewer judges the contributions significant in theory and practice and recommends acceptance.  
- Minor writing issues to fix: define notation (supp, Ω(·), "inconsistence"), make "step size" phrasing consistent, and correct several small typos/wording (specific line references).
