- Addresses LDP degradation in continuous data collection and proposes mechanisms for repeated counter data collection.  
- Introduces mean and histogram estimators that maintain formal privacy guarantees over arbitrarily long periods with comparable accuracy to single-round LDP.  
- Contributions: simplified 1-bit single-round response mechanisms; novel α-point rounding for memoization to prevent repeated-collection leakage; real-world deployment by Microsoft.  
- Theoretical rigor: clear LDP formulations, proofs, and accuracy/privacy bounds.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on real and synthetic datasets show robustness and competitive or superior accuracy versus single-round methods.  
- Strength: Technical novelty, especially the α-point rounding technique adapting approximation-algorithm ideas.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundations with formal guarantees.  
- Strength: Practical relevance demonstrated by large-scale industrial deployment.  
- Strength: Extensive empirical evaluation across distributions and parameter settings.  
- Weakness: Exposition is dense; α-point rounding and memoization sections are hard to follow.  
- Weakness: Evaluation focuses mainly on app-usage telemetry, limiting demonstrated generalizability to other domains.  
- Weakness: Privacy guarantees for repeated collection are weaker than single-round; implications under adversarial auxiliary information need deeper analysis.  
- For acceptance: tackles an important, underexplored problem with theoretical, empirical, and industrial impact.  
- Against acceptance: presentation density and limited evaluation scope may hinder broader accessibility and generality.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand discussion on privacy-utility trade-offs.
