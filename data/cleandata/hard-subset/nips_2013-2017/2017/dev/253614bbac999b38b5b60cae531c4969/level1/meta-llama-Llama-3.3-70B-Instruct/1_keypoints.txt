- Proposes a novel framework for repeated locally differentially private (LDP) collection of counter data, addressing a key telemetry problem.
- Combines 1-bit LDP mechanisms with memoization and output perturbation to maintain privacy over arbitrary long collections.
- Builds on prior single-round LDP work (Duchi et al., Bassily & Smith) and adapts mechanisms to repeated collection.
- Introduces α-point rounding to mitigate privacy leakage from small changes in user behavior.
- Provides formal privacy guarantees (Theorems 3 and 4) demonstrating the mechanisms' effectiveness.
- Empirical evaluation shows the proposed methods are comparable to or outperform state-of-the-art single-round mechanisms.
- Assumes user behavior is approximately consistent or slowly varying, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios.
- Mechanisms may be vulnerable to attacks using auxiliary information (Section 3.2.1).
- Requires careful tuning of parameters (discretization granularity s, perturbation probability γ) to balance privacy and accuracy.
- Significant contribution to LDP with practical relevance for telemetry applications.
- Theoretical soundness and empirical validation support acceptance.
- Clear and well-organized presentation aids reproducibility and understanding.
- Limitations (behavioral assumptions, auxiliary-info vulnerability, parameter tuning) could restrict applicability in some use cases.
