- Proposes a novel framework combining 1-bit LDP mechanisms, memoization, and output perturbation for repeated collection of counter data.  
- Main claims: new LDP mechanisms for mean and histogram estimation with accuracy comparable or superior to single-round LDP.  
- Introduces α-point rounding to enable memoization for private counter collection.  
- Provides rigorous privacy definitions and formal proofs that the mechanisms satisfy the stated guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation on real-world datasets shows competitive or better accuracy versus state-of-the-art.  
- Demonstrates practical applicability via deployment by Microsoft.  
- Theoretical analysis and empirical results strongly support the paper’s claims.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Contributions are significant and highly useful for telemetry and differential privacy applications.  
- Acknowledges and discusses potential vulnerability to attacks using auxiliary information.  
- Proposes output perturbation as mitigation, but notes it may not fully protect against all attack types.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh minor limitations and impact is likely substantial.
