- Addresses privacy in repeated telemetry counter collection by proposing novel locally differentially private (LDP) mechanisms.
- Targets mean and histogram estimation with formal privacy guarantees for prolonged data collection.
- Introduces a 1-bit response mechanism for efficient single-round data collection.
- Develops an α-point rounding technique combined with memoization to limit privacy leakage in repeated collection.
- Deployed at scale by Microsoft on millions of devices (Windows 10 Fall Creators Update), demonstrating practicality and scalability.
- Theoretical rigor: provides proofs of LDP preservation, error bounds, and introduces behavior patterns to quantify repeated-collection leakage.
- Empirical validation on real-world telemetry datasets shows comparable or better accuracy than state-of-the-art while enhancing privacy.
- Strength: novel, practically relevant solution that addresses a key limitation of existing LDP methods for repeated collection.
- Weakness: technical explanations (especially α-point rounding and its integration with memoization) are dense and hard to follow.
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and potential attacks using auxiliary information, and trade-offs from output perturbation.
- Weakness: comparative evaluation could include more diverse datasets and stress tests for highly dynamic user behavior.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand discussion of limitations and vulnerabilities.
