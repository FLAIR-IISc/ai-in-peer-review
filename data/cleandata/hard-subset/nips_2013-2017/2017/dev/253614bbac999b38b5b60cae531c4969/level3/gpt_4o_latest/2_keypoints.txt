- Paper: Proposes novel LDP mechanisms for repeated telemetry (1-bit mean, d-bit histogram) using α-point rounding and memoization, deployed at Microsoft.

- Strength — Practical relevance: Tackles privacy for continuous telemetry with real-world applicability to app usage statistics.

- Strength — Communication efficiency: 1-bit and d-bit mechanisms greatly reduce communication costs for large-scale deployment.

- Strength — Industrial deployment: Methods deployed across millions of devices at Microsoft, demonstrating practicality and robustness.

- Strength — Empirical validation: Convincing experiments on real-world datasets and synthetic data support accuracy and generalizability.

- Weakness — Clarity and organization: Significant overlap between main paper and supplement reduces readability and clarity.

- Weakness — α-point rounding guarantees: Theoretical guarantees and implications of α-point rounding are not fully elaborated.

- Weakness — Reproducibility concerns: Reliance on proprietary data limits independent verification; open-source experiments are needed.

- Weakness — Output perturbation trade-offs: Impact of output perturbation on accuracy and privacy is not explored in sufficient detail.

- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important, underexplored problem for continuous data collection with novel, practical, scalable solutions and real-world deployment.

- Argument against acceptance: Paper clarity and insufficient detail on key concepts plus limited reproducibility due to proprietary data.

- Conclusion: Significant contribution to differential privacy for continuous collection; worthy of acceptance pending clarifications and reproducibility improvements.
