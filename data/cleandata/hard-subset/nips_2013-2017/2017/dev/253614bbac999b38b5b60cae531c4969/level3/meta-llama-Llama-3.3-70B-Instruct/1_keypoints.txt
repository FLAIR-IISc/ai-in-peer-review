- Proposes augmenting generative models with external memory, treating memory outputs with stochastic addressing as a conditional mixture distribution.  
- Uses variational inference for memory addressing, enabling target-guided memory lookups during training.  
- Evaluated on generative few-shot learning and unconditional density modeling, showing promising and impressive results, especially in few-shot tasks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and strong relation to prior work.  
- Key technical contribution: stochastic addressing combined with variational inference allows precise memory lookups in training and inference.  
- Clarification needed on the 1-bit mean-estimation mechanism and how it differs from the basic randomizer in [3], including efficiency claims.  
- Theorem 2’s protocol should be compared to existing histogram protocols with analysis in terms of n and d.  
- Privacy guarantee after alpha-point rounding is unclear, particularly under repeated reports by a data collector—requires further explanation.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons to existing protocols and insufficient justification/details for certain design choices.  
- Overall: promising contribution that could be significant with revisions addressing the above clarifications.
