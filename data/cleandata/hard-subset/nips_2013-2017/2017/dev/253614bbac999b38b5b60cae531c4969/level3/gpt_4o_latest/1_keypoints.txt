- Problem: Addresses privacy for repeated telemetry (counter) collection under Local Differential Privacy (LDP) and continuous data collection.  
- Contributions: Proposes a 1-bit mechanism for mean and histogram estimation.  
- Contributions: Introduces randomized α-point rounding for memoization to handle changing values.  
- Contributions: Presents an output perturbation mechanism to hide behavior change points.  
- Deployment: Mechanisms were deployed at scale by Microsoft, demonstrating practical relevance.  
- Novelty: Mechanisms (especially α-point rounding and output perturbation) are innovative and tackle a real-world challenge.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides formal privacy guarantees in the repeated-collection setting.  
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on real and synthetic datasets show good accuracy/privacy trade-offs.  
- Scalability: Mechanisms are designed to scale to millions of users.  
- Weakness: Clarify whether the 1-bit mechanism is equivalent to the basic randomizer in [3] or how it differs.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 needs explicit utility comparisons to existing histogram protocols in terms of n (users) and d (bits).  
- Weakness: Privacy effects of α-point rounding under repeated composition are not fully explored; need analysis of privacy degradation over time.  
- Weakness: Some sections (e.g., d-bit memoization) are dense and need clearer exposition or examples.  
- Recommendation: Paper makes a valuable contribution and is recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to address the above clarifications.
