For local differential privacy, authors have proposed more effective methods. Specifically, they have suggested a 1-bit local differential privacy method that is capable of estimating averages in a distributed and efficient manner. Additionally, a D-bit method has been proposed for histogram estimation. It is argued that these two goals are partially relevant in the context of telemetry data collection. The main advancement is in communication efficiency, which is important for large-scale deployments. The authors also mention that this method is actually being used on a large scale in industry, and it is probably the largest use of differential privacy. Although this itself does not increase the value of scientific contributions, it strengthens the idea of an efficient, effective, and practical method.

The authors have submitted another version of their paper as a proof-concept. However, it contains some more figures and detailed original text. It is somewhat unclear. The proof-concept should only contain additional material, and there should be no repetition in the original paper. It is also quite strange that the reviewer has to look for the proof-concept in the given text. There should be a clear separation between the submission and the proof-concept.

Overall, the presentation is clear, partly due to some parts being submitted as proof-concept. However, the alpha-rounding part is partially unclear. Could the authors please explain in detail the guarantees they claim?

Although the experimental study is interesting, there are questions about the reproducibility of the results. Since this is real user and company data, how will the follow-up work here be compared? It would be beneficial to provide an alternative solution - potentially simulated data - so that the comparison remains open-source.

However, the experiments are considered credible and support the main claim.
