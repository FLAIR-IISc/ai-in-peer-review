The authors propose a more efficient mechanism for local differential privacy. Specifically, they propose a 1-bit local differential privacy method that enables mean estimation in a distributed and effective manner. Additionally, a D-bit method for histogram estimation is also proposed. It is argued that both of these approaches are partially relevant for the collection of telemetry data. The main advancement lies in communication efficiency – which is crucial for large-scale deployments. The authors also mention that this mechanism has actually been deployed on a large scale in the industry and is possibly the largest deployment of differential privacy to date. While this does not increase the value of the scientific contribution in itself, it reinforces the point of an efficient, effective, and practical approach.

The authors have presented another version of their paper as supplementary material that includes evidence. But it also includes more figures and expanded main text. This is the boundary line. Supplementary material should only contain additional content – while the main paper should not be self-contained and repetitive. It is also strange that the reviewer would have to look for evidence in the given text. There should be a clear division between the presentation and the supplementary material.

Overall, the presentation is clear – partially due to outsourcing some parts of the presentation for supplementary material. However, the alpha-rounding part is partially unclear. Could the authors please elaborate on the claimed guarantee?

While the experimental study is interesting – there is a question about the reproducibility of the results. Since it shows real user and company data – how will the follow-up work here be compared? It would be beneficial to provide an alternative solution – potentially simulated data – so that the comparison remains open source.

However, the experiments are considered convincing and support the main claim.
