The authors have proposed a more effective method for local differential privacy. Specifically, they propose a 1-bit local differential privacy method that is capable of estimating averages in a distributed and efficient manner. Additionally, a D-bit method has been proposed for histogram estimation. It is argued that these two goals are partially relevant to the field of telemetry data collection. The main advancement is in communication efficiency, which is crucial for large-scale deployments. The authors also mention that this method is actually being used on a large scale in industry, and it is probably the biggest use of differential privacy. Although this in itself does not increase the value of the scientific contribution, it reinforces the idea of an efficient, effective, and practical method.

The authors have submitted another version of their paper as a proof-of-concept. However, it contains some more figures and detailed original text. It is somewhat unclear. The proof-of-concept should only contain additional material, while the original paper should not contain any repetition. It is also quite strange that the reviewer has to look for the proof-of-concept in the given text. There should be a clear separation between the submission and the proof-of-concept.

Overall, the presentation is clear, partly due to the submission of some parts as proof-of-concept. However, the alpha-rounding part is partially unclear. Could the authors please explain their claimed guarantees in detail?

Although the experimental study is interesting, there is a question about the reproducibility of the results. Since this is real user and company data, how will the follow-up work be compared here? It would be beneficial to provide an alternative solution – potentially simulated data – so that the comparison remains open-source.

However, the experiments are considered credible and support the main claim.
