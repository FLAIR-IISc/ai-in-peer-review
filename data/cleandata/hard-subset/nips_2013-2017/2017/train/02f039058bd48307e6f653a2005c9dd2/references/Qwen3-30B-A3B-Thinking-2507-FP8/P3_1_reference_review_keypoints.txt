- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f_CI and training a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped samples.  
- Theoretical contributions include: Theorem 1 (NN bootstrap approximates f_CI in total variation), Theorem 2 (generalization bounds for near-independent samples); proofs in appendix.  
- Theorem 1 exhibits curse-of-dimensionality scaling O(1/n^(1/d_z)), but empirical results scale better than this bound, a tension that needs discussion.  
- Smoothness assumptions (Assumptions 1 and 2) are poorly motivated; the generalized curvature matrix definition is unclear and not well connected to standard density smoothness.  
- Empirical validation shows CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT as Z’s dimension increases, with meaningful real-world results on flow-cytometry data.  
- Unclear separation of effects: gains may be due to XGBoost classifier rather than the bootstrap method; ablation with other classifiers is needed.  
- Clarity issues: ambiguous use of f_CI, poorly motivated bias correction in Algorithm 3, underexplained definition of G(ε), and insufficient main-text justification for the NN bootstrap-to-f_CI connection.  
- Originality is moderate: classification reduction is not entirely new; the claimed novelty is the NN bootstrap but it is not sufficiently distinguished from other NN-based methods.  
- Calls for a more thorough comparison with related work, especially [10] and other nearest-neighbor approaches.  
- Significance: addresses an important CI testing challenge, notably better scalability with high-dimensional Z, relevant to causal inference and Bayesian networks.  
- Broader “model-powered” approach is promising but not fully explored beyond CI testing.  
- Strengths: novel classification-based CI test, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical scalability, practical application to protein signaling, and inspiring model-powered methodology.  
- Weaknesses: weak motivation for assumptions, limited theoretical-practical connection, insufficient ablation for classifier vs. bootstrap, poorly motivated bias correction, unexplained superior empirical scaling.  
- Recommendations: clarify and justify theoretical assumptions, explain empirical vs. theoretical scaling, justify bias correction, run classifier-agnostic experiments, and strengthen related-work comparisons.  
- Final recommendation: accept with minor revisions — technically sound, well-written, empirically strong, and practically significant.
