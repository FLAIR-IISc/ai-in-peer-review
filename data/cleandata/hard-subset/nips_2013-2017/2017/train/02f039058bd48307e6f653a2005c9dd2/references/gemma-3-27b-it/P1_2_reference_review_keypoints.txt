- Proposes a novel reduction of conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrap quality (total variation distance) and classifier generalization in near-independent settings (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Empirically demonstrates that CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic data and a flow cytometry dataset, and scales well with Z dimensionality.  
- Introduces Algorithm 3 to correct classifier bias, showing attention to practical implementation issues.  
- Positions the work well among related methods (kernel-based and permutation approaches) and cites classification-based two-sample testing literature.  
- Leverages modern classifier advances for a scalable, flexible CI testing approach.  
- Strong novelty by reframing CI testing and capitalizing on supervised learning progress.  
- Solid theoretical foundation differentiates the paper from many empirical-only CI tests.  
- Clear exposition and logical flow with accessible notation and algorithm descriptions.  
- Relies on strong smoothness/Lipschitz assumptions (Assumptions 1 and 2) that may not hold in practice and need sensitivity analysis.  
- Theoretical derivations are lengthy and could be inaccessible; more intuitive explanations are needed.  
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap parameter choices (e.g., number of neighbors) are not sufficiently explored or guided.  
- Bootstrap nearest-neighbor search may be computationally expensive in high-dimensional Z; runtime should be benchmarked against other tests.  
- Limited empirical comparison scope—needs evaluation against more recent/advanced CI methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theory, convincing empirical results, and potential impact on causal discovery.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, dense theoretical presentation, and unclear computational costs.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; benefits outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: analyze sensitivity to smoothness assumptions, provide more intuitive theory explanations, benchmark bootstrap computational complexity, study bootstrap parameter effects and offer guidelines, and compare to more recent CI methods.
