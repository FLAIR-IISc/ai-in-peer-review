- Presents a novel CI test by reducing CI testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on bootstrap sample quality (TV distance) and classifier generalization.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art CI tests on synthetic and real (flow cytometry) datasets.  
- Correctly situates itself relative to kernel-based CI tests and recent classification-based independence tests.  
- Leverages nearest-neighbor theory and non-i.i.d. generalization bounds from related literature.  
- Strength: Clever reduction to classification allowing use of modern supervised learning advances.  
- Strength: Theoretical contributions (Theorems 1 and 2) that increase credibility.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results, especially in high dimensions, and practical validation.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient nearest-neighbor bootstrap with potential fast implementations.  
- Strength: Modular design permitting different classifiers for flexibility.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: Theoretical guarantees depend on smoothness assumptions that may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Threshold selection in Algorithm 3 appears ad-hoc and needs a more principled method.  
- Weakness: Bias correction rationale (subtracting classifier loss on Y and Z) is under-justified and lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: Experimental details (XGBoost hyperparameters, synthetic data settings) are insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Scalability analysis as a function of dimensionality (especially d_z) is limited.  
- Weakness: VC dimension likely grows with classifier complexity and data dimensionality, potentially weakening generalization bounds.  
- Overall assessment: Promising, well-motivated, and strong contribution with minor issues to address.  
- For acceptance: Novel approach, solid theory, strong empirical validation, and practical adaptability.  
- Against acceptance: Reliance on restrictive assumptions, ad-hoc threshold/bias choices, and limited complexity/VC discussion.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, contingent on addressing assumptions, threshold selection, and bias-correction explanations.
