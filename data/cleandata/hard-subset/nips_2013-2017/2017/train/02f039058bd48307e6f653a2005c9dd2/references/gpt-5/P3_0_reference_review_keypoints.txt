- Proposes reducing nonparametric CI testing to binary classification using a 1-NN bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution and training a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant that subtracts the performance of a classifier trained only on (Y,Z) to mitigate finite-sample bootstrap artifacts.  
- Theoretical contributions: a TV bound showing the bootstrap distribution is O(n^{-1/dz})-close to fCI under smoothness, and generalization bounds for ERM under the near-i.i.d. dependence induced by the bootstrap.  
- Empirically outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear problems as dz increases and shows improved ROC-AUC on flow cytometry CI relations; a software package is provided.  
- Relates to prior work by contrasting with kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT, KCIPT), extending classifier two-sample tests to CI via a Z-conditional surrogate, and leveraging NN-based density estimation and dependence Rademacher theory.  
- Strength: conceptually clean reduction allowing off-the-shelf classifiers to be used for CI testing.  
- Strength: simple, efficient 1-NN bootstrap and three-way sample split that is easy to implement.  
- Strength: nontrivial theoretical analysis (TV proximity and near-independence generalization) of independent interest.  
- Strength: competitive empirical performance on synthetic and real data, plus an implementation.  
- Weakness: severe curse of dimensionality—O(n^{-1/dz}) rate and NN fragility mean the method may degrade quickly as dim(Z) grows.  
- Weakness: lacks calibrated Type I error control and p-values; test decision uses heuristic thresholds (e.g., τ = 1/√n) without formal level-α guarantees.  
- Weakness: generalization bounds may be vacuous for complex classifiers because of large VC dimensions and opaque constants (G(ε), cdz).  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (generalized curvature matrix) is nonstandard and lacks intuitive examples; dependence on unknown constants complicates interpretation.  
- Weakness: limited experimental breadth—mostly one PNL family, no discrete/mixed Z, and missing ablations for classifier choice, k in k-NN, number of bootstraps B, and hyperparameter tuning fairness.  
- Quality: technically competent with meaningful theoretical and empirical contributions, but the statistical testing aspect (false positive control) is underdeveloped.  
- Clarity: generally readable but has formatting issues, unclear assumptions, inconsistent algorithm descriptions, and lacks explicit pseudocode for bias correction and τ calibration.  
- Originality: novel reduction of CI testing to classification via an NN conditional bootstrap plus original TV and generalization analyses.  
- Significance: high practical potential to bridge ML classifiers and CI testing if calibration and high-dimensional Z issues are mitigated.  
- Suggestion: provide a level-α version (e.g., permutation of labels, studentization, or cross-fitting) to obtain p-values and control error rates.  
- Suggestion: analyze/use k-NN with k>1, local weighting, adaptive neighborhood selection or metric learning to mitigate high-dim Z issues.  
- Suggestion: broaden experiments to more DGPs (linear-Gaussian, heteroskedastic, non-smooth, discrete/mixed Z), include ablations (classifier type, k, B), and report runtime scaling.  
- Suggestion: give intuition and concrete examples for Assumption 1, and discuss constants/parameter regimes where bounds are informative.  
- Suggestion: consider cross-fitting to decouple bootstrap generation from training/testing and reduce dependence/bias.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, powerful, implementable idea with theoretical backing, empirical gains, and potential high impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no calibrated Type I control, fragile theoretical dependence on dim(Z), limited experiments and opaque assumptions.  
- Recommendation: weak accept.
