- Summary: Proposes CCIT — reduce CI testing to two-sample classification by NN-bootstrap (swap Y with Z 1-NN), label originals vs bootstraps, and classify; provides TV bound O(n^{-1/dz}) and generalization bounds for near-i.i.d. samples; shows strong empirical AUCs.

- Relation to prior work: Extends reduce-to-classification paradigm; contrasts with RKHS-based KCIT/RCIT and matching-based KCIPT; connects to k-NN entropy estimators and non-i.i.d. learning theory.

- Strength: Simple, practical reduction allowing modern classifiers and domain knowledge.

- Strength: Novel NN-based bootstrap with theoretical TV guarantee (Theorem 1).

- Strength: Generalization bounds for dependent-but-near-i.i.d. samples are theoretically interesting.

- Strength: Empirical gains over kernel baselines on synthetic high-dim Z and one real dataset; code provided.

- Weakness: No formal calibration or finite-sample Type I error control; τ=1/√n is heuristic and no p-values are provided.

- Weakness: Theory suffers curse of dimensionality via O(n^{-1/dz}) rate, offering limited guidance for large dz.

- Weakness: Generalization bounds use VC dimension and may be vacuous for modern classifiers like boosted trees or deep nets.

- Weakness: Strong smoothness and continuous-density assumptions exclude mixed discrete-continuous settings common in practice.

- Weakness: Missing empirical comparisons to closest baseline KCIPT and no ablations on classifier choice, k in k-NN, metric, or number of bootstraps B.

- Weakness: Sample-splitting uses 3n samples inefficiently; cross-fitting or repeated splits not analyzed.

- Weakness: Limited practical guidance on choosing τ, Z-metric, and handling high-dimensional Z (ANN considerations).

- Quality: Theoretical claims are careful under assumptions but do not yield a level-α test or power analysis, nor cover modern classifier complexity.

- Quality: Experiments are encouraging but narrow (1D X,Y, specific model family) and real-data ground truth is proxy-based.

- Clarity: Mostly clear and organized; notation and some assumptions could be better motivated and streamlined.

- Originality: NN bootstrap for emulating fCI and near-independence learning bounds appear novel.

- Significance: Valuable practical approach for CI testing with potential impact on causal discovery if calibration and broader validation are addressed.

- Suggestions: Provide calibrated testing (permutation/cross-fitting/wild bootstrap), explore k>1 NN, compare to KCIPT and CMI estimators, broaden experiments, and discuss computational/ANN strategies.

- Overall evaluation: Solid, well-motivated contribution with novel theory and empirical promise but needs calibration and stronger validation; recommendation: Weak accept (7/10).

- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
