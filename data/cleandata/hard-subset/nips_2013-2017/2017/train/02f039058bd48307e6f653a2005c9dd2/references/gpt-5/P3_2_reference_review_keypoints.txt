- Method: Reduces conditional independence testing to binary classification by nearest-neighbor bootstrap on Z to simulate fCI, labeling real vs bootstrapped samples and training a classifier; bias-corrected variant compares classifiers with and without X.  
- Theory: Provides a total-variation bound (O(n^{-1/dz}) under smoothness), generalization bounds for near-i.i.d. bootstrapped samples, and a connection between classifier risk and dTV(f, fCI).  
- Empirics: Method (with XGBoost) outperforms KCIT/RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear data and flow cytometry CI checks; code released.  
- Strength — simplicity/modularity: Conceptually simple reduction allows leveraging strong learners and domain knowledge.  
- Strength — NN bootstrap: Novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap with TV guarantees and reusable proof techniques under curvature assumptions.  
- Strength — generalization analysis: Nontrivial bounds for learning when one class is only near-i.i.d.  
- Strength — practical performance: Strong empirical performance in moderate-dimensional Z and a bias-corrected variant to address bootstrap artifacts.  
- Weakness — dimensionality: Theoretical rates degrade sharply with dz (curse of dimensionality), limiting guarantees in high-dimensional Z common in causal discovery.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Assumption 1 (generalized curvature) is nonstandard and hard to interpret or verify; G(ε) terms make bounds opaque.  
- Weakness — dependence in bootstrapped data: Only marginal closeness shown; U′ samples are dependent and generalization rates are loose/opaque—needs clearer finite-sample corollary.  
- Weakness — lack of calibration: No finite-sample type I error control or p-value procedure presented; permutation calibration under H0-exchangeability is suggested.  
- Weakness — NN sensitivity: 1-NN is fragile in high dimensions, sensitive to metric/scale and duplicate matches; more analysis (k-NN, matching without replacement) is needed.  
- Weakness — experimental gaps: Missing hyperparameter details for KCIT/RCIT, classifier ablations, runtime/memory comparisons, and type I error/power at fixed α.  
- Quality: Technically sound with careful assumptions and proofs, but main theoretical limitation is dimensional dependence and opacity of some terms.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but needs better intuition/sufficient conditions for Assumption 1, more proof steps in main text, explicit level-α testing procedure, and notation fixes.  
- Originality: Novel reduction of CI testing to classification and NN bootstrap for conditional decoupling; related to but distinct from KCIPT and classifier two-sample tests.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for causal discovery if testing is calibrated and robustness to high-dimensional Z is addressed.  
- Suggestions: Add permutation-based calibration, sensitivity studies to Z metric/scaling and k, classifier/hyperparameter ablations, strategies to reduce dependence in U′, and runtime comparisons.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear, scalable reduction; new NN bootstrap with TV guarantees; strong empirical results and released code.  
- Cons for acceptance: No calibrated hypothesis test, poor theoretical scaling with dz, and limited sensitivity/experimental analysis.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending addition of calibrated testing (e.g., permutation) and stronger clarity/analysis of assumptions and sensitivity.
