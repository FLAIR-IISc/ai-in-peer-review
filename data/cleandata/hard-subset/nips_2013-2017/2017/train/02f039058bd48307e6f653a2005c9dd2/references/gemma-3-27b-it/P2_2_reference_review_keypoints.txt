- Core idea: Reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification by generating conditionally independent samples via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap and training a classifier to distinguish real vs. generated data.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides bounds on bootstrap sample quality (Theorem 1) and classifier generalization in near-independent settings (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments and a flow cytometry dataset show CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT, scaling better with dimensionality.  
- Novelty: Framing CI testing as classification for the conditional case, combined with the bootstrap and theoretical analysis, is a novel contribution.  
- Bias correction: Algorithm 3 addresses classification bias, strengthening the method.  
- Presentation: Core idea and algorithm are mostly well explained and logically structured.  
- Complexity concern: Nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be computationally expensive in high dimensions; no detailed runtime/complexity analysis provided.  
- Strong assumptions: Theoretical results rely on smoothness assumptions whose real-world applicability and sensitivity to violations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Parameter tuning: Threshold parameter (τ) lacks detailed sensitivity analysis and practical tuning guidance beyond a heuristic.  
- Theory clarity: Theoretical sections, particularly Theorem 2, are dense with heavy notation and need more intuitive explanation.  
- Related work: Limited engagement with very recent advances in causal discovery and CI testing beyond kernel methods.  
- Significance: If computational costs are manageable, the method could substantially impact causal discovery by leveraging powerful classifiers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach with strong empirical performance and useful generalization analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Potential computational limits, restrictive assumptions, and dense theoretical exposition could hinder adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision suggestions: Add computational complexity discussion, sensitivity analysis for smoothness assumptions, practical guidance for τ, clearer exposition of theory, and broader related-work coverage.
