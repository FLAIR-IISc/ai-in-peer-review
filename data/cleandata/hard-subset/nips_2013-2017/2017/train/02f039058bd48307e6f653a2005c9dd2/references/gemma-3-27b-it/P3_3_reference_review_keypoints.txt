- Overall: Novel approach framing CI testing as binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap; promising theory and empirical results; recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Core idea: Use a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the fCI distribution and train a classifier to distinguish CI vs dependent data.  
- Positioning: Properly compared to kernel-based methods (KCIT, RCIT), permutation approaches (KCIPT), and classifier-based independence testing [19].  
- Strength: The nearest-neighbor bootstrap strategy for approximating fCI is a significant and clever contribution.  
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees on total variation distance between bootstrapped samples and fCI plus classifier generalization bounds.  
- Strength: Empirical results show competitive performance, especially as Z’s dimensionality increases.  
- Strength: Framework is modular and can leverage different classifiers and advances in classification.  
- Strength: Clear motivation to avoid computational bottlenecks of prior methods (e.g., KCIPT’s LP step).  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense with heavy notation; some sections (Alg. 3, bias correction) lack clear motivation and explanation.  
- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on strong smoothness assumptions; practical sensitivity to assumption violations is not explored.  
- Weakness: Improvements over existing methods appear incremental in some synthetic experiments.  
- Weakness: Empirical scope is limited—mostly synthetic plus one flow cytometry dataset; more diverse real-world tests needed.  
- Weakness: Use of VC dimension for bounds may be limiting; other complexity measures could be more relevant.  
- Weakness: Computational cost of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap, especially in high dimensions, is not clearly analyzed.  
- Detailed question: Why use 1-NN specifically—was sensitivity to k in k-NN explored?  
- Detailed question: Theorem 1’s 1/n^(1/dz) convergence rate suggests poor high-dim behavior—explain discrepancy with empirical scaling.  
- Detailed question: Motivation and practical determination of Alg. 3’s classifier-on-(Y,Z) subtraction and threshold τ are unclear.  
- Detailed question: Synthetic experiment parameter details (noise, Z dimension) are missing for reproducibility.  
- Detailed question: Rationale for selected flow cytometry "ground truth" graphs should be justified given observational causal uncertainty.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel, theoretically-grounded, modular, and empirically competitive approach with potential scalability.  
- Con acceptance: Clarity issues, strong theoretical assumptions, limited empirical breadth, and unclear computational claims.  
- Required revisions: Improve readability (rewrite Secs. 2–3), clearly explain Alg. 3, discuss sensitivity to smoothness assumptions and high-dimensional scaling, expand empirical evaluation, and provide computational-cost comparisons.
