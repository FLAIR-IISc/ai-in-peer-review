- Proposes CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using a 1-NN bootstrap over Z to create synthetic fCI-like samples, train a classifier to distinguish real vs synthetic, and reject H0 when accuracy > chance; includes a bias-corrected variant.

- Theoretical contributions: TV-distance guarantee between NN-bootstrap distribution and fCI with rate O(n^{-1/dz}), non-i.i.d. generalization bounds for ERM classifiers on bootstrap samples, and a risk sandwich establishing behavior under H0 vs H1.

- Empirical claims: CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic datasets (Z up to 70 dims) and on a flow cytometry dataset.

- Relation to prior work: connects to classifier-based two-sample tests, permutation/matching CI tests, and kernel CI methods, but is novel in using a simple 1-NN conditional resampling instead of costly matching/optimization.

- Technical strengths: well-motivated reduction, useful TV bound depending only on dz, and thoughtful treatment of non-i.i.d. issues in generalization analysis.

- Main theoretical caveat: TV rate O(n^{-1/dz}) implies a curse of dimensionality in Z; paper should clarify regimes (e.g., low intrinsic dimension or Lipschitz links) where practical performance is better.

- Statistical testing shortfall: lacks finite-sample Type I error control or calibrated p-values; threshold choice is heuristic and the bias term b(n) is unknown.

- Assumptions concern: smoothness/curvature and fZ conditions (bounded Hessian, integrability, Lipschitz, term G(ε)) are strong; need discussion of plausibility and failure modes (heavy tails, concentrated manifolds).

- Computational concerns: 1-NN degrades in high-dimensional Z; approximate NN mentioned but not empirically analyzed; no runtime or complexity comparisons to KCIT/RCIT.

- Clarity: paper is generally readable and well organized, but contains minor typos and some definitions/assumptions need more intuitive explanation.

- Experimental details lacking: missing sample sizes, classifier hyperparameters, number of trees/layers, sensitivity to classifier choice and k in kNN, and no reporting of standard errors.

- Originality: novel NN-based conditional resampling reduction and TV guarantee; adaptation of VC/Rademacher generalization tools to the geometric near-independence setting is original.

- Significance: practically useful framing that leverages ML classifiers and is flexible; theoretically clarifies when conditional resampling works; could be a go-to baseline if calibration and runtime issues are addressed.

- Pros: simple modular algorithm, clear theoretical link between NN bootstrap and fCI, near-independence generalization bounds, public code, real-data application.

- Cons: no formal level-α procedure, worst-case curse in dz, limited baselines and ablations, unclear sensitivity to NN and classifier choices, and strong assumptions with limited practical guidance.

- Questions for authors: can you provide a calibrated test with provable asymptotic level (e.g., null resampling or valid permutations)? How sensitive is performance to k and classifier G? What are runtimes vs KCIT/RCIT? How does CCIT handle mixed discrete/continuous Z or manifold-structured Z?

- Recommendation: promising and potentially NeurIPS-worthy pending clarifications and a basic calibration for Type I control and more experimental/runtime detail.
