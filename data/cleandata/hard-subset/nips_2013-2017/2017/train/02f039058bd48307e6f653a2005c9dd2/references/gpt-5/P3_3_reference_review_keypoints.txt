- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that swaps Y using Z-nearest neighbors and classifies original vs. synthetic samples.
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant (CCITv2) that subtracts a (Y,Z)-only classifier from an (X,Y,Z) classifier.
- Theoretical results: TV-distance bound between bootstrap distribution and product fX|Z fY|Z fZ (rate O(n−1/dz) plus low-density terms).
- Provides generalization error bounds for ERM under the bootstrap’s near-independent dependence structure.
- Empirical results show improved performance over KCIT/RCIT on synthetic and flow-cytometry data, especially as dim(Z) grows.
- Conceptually simple, easy to implement, and leverages modern classifiers and feature engineering.
- Theoretical analysis of the NN bootstrap in TV distance is valuable and the low-density trimming term is sensible.
- Non-iid generalization bounds are technically interesting and potentially broadly relevant.
- Lacks a procedure to control type I error or compute p-values; uses an ad hoc threshold τ ≈ 1/√n.
- Strong and unusual smoothness/curvature assumptions (Assumption 1) are hard to verify in practice.
- The O(n−1/dz) rate implies a severe curse of dimensionality, conflicting with empirical claims for high dz.
- Constants/rates in the dependence analysis may not be informative at practical sample sizes; effect of Y reuse on classifiers is unclear.
- Current risk bounds do not translate into explicit level or power guarantees for the test.
- Experimental evaluation reports ROC/AUC but not type I error vs. power at fixed α or sensitivity to τ, hindering fair comparison to KCIT/RCIT.
- Hyperparameter choices for XGBoost and kernel baselines are under-specified; kernel choices/approximations can materially affect results.
- Synthetic experiments are limited (1D X,Y, simple post-nonlinear); need more challenging settings (heteroskedasticity, non-smooth Y|Z, heavy tails).
- Method’s applicability to discrete or mixed data is not discussed.
- Some clarity issues: Assumption 1 is opaque; Algorithm descriptions confuse which split is held out vs. used for bootstrap.
- Role and computational cost of B (number of bootstraps) and the definition of τ are not fully specified.
- Suggest adding a principled calibration scheme for level-α control (e.g., cross-fitting, null resampling, permutation with bias correction).
- Recommend reporting type I error and power at fixed α across n and dz, and performing ablations over classifiers and hyperparameters.
- Suggest expanding experiments to tougher generative models and providing diagnostics (NN distance, G(·) estimates) to assess bootstrap reliability.
- Advise discussing approximate nearest-neighbor search and its effects on theory and practice.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, useful reduction with solid empirical showing and novel theoretical analysis of conditional resampling.
- Cons for acceptance: no calibrated level-α procedure, strong/opaque assumptions with pessimistic rates, and incomplete experimental fairness regarding type I control.
