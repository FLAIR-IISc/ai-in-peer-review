- Proposes CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f_CI-like samples.  
- Theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1): bootstrapped samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance.  
- Theoretical guarantee (Theorem 2): generalization bounds for classification under near-i.i.d. samples from the bootstrap.  
- Addresses technical challenges of non-i.i.d. samples, improving over permutation-based approaches.  
- Empirical results: CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and flow-cytometry datasets, especially with high-dimensional Z.  
- Demonstrates scalability of CCIT as conditioning dimension increases where other methods fail.  
- Paper is technically sound with substantial theoretical contributions and rigorous analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, well-designed, and includes a real-world biological application.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations of theoretical bounds (dependence on d_z) and note empirical performance exceeds them.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Could provide more intuition for why nearest-neighbor bootstrap works in high-dimensional Z beyond smoothness assumptions.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible with clear algorithms and figures.  
- Novelty: first to reduce CI testing to binary classification and introduce nearest-neighbor bootstrap with theoretical guarantees.  
- Situates contributions clearly relative to KCIT, RCIT, and KCIPT, highlighting simpler guarantees vs. complex previous methods.  
- Significant impact: important for causal inference and structure learning, and benefits from advances in classification methods.  
- Strong overall assessment and recommendation: high-quality contribution, recommend accept at NeurIPS.  
- Strengths summary: novel reduction, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical scalability, real-world application, careful analysis.  
- Weaknesses summary: limited complexity discussion, bound dependence on d_z, some theoretical details need more intuitive explanation.  
- Specific actionable suggestions: add intuition for high-d Z, provide complexity (e.g., O(n log n)) discussion, report XGBoost hyperparameters, and discuss extension to discrete variables.
