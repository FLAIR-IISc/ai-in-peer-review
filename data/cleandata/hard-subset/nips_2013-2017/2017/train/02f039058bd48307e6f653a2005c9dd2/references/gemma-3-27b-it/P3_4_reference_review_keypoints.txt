- Novel framing: Casts conditional independence testing as a binary classification problem using generated fCI samples.  
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap: Uses a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides bounds on total variation between bootstrapped samples and fCI and generalization bounds under near-independence.  
- Algorithm 3 (bias correction): Introduces a bias-correction algorithm with accompanying theoretical analysis for finite samples.  
- Strong empirical performance: Outperforms state-of-the-art CI tests on synthetic and real datasets and shows good scalability.  
- Clear presentation and reproducibility: Well-written paper with helpful figures and open-source code.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on ML-for-inference and classification-based testing, distinguishes itself from kernel-based CI tests and nearest-neighbor density estimation.  
- Assumptions concern: Theoretical results rely on smoothness assumptions (Assumptions 1 & 2) whose practical impact isn’t fully explored.  
- Bootstrap complexity: Nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be computationally expensive in high dimensions or large samples; complexity analysis needed.  
- Threshold selection issue: Algorithm 3’s threshold τ depends on a potentially conservative upper bound on variance, affecting robustness.  
- VC-dimension limitation: Generalization bounds use VC dimension V, which is hard to determine for complex classifiers (e.g., GBTs, deep nets).  
- Limited real-data analysis: Flow cytometry evaluation is limited; deeper comparisons with causal discovery methods are warranted.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach with rigorous theory, strong empirical gains, and open-source code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Strong distributional assumptions, potential bootstrap scalability issues, and practical challenges in applying theoretical results.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contribution; merits acceptance subject to minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: Add sensitivity analysis for smoothness assumptions, provide computational complexity details for the bootstrap, discuss alternative/adaptive τ selection, and acknowledge VC-dimension estimation difficulties.
