- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate approximate f_CI samples and a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped data.
- Provides theoretical guarantees that bootstrapped samples are close to f_CI in total variation (Theorem 1) and gives generalization bounds for classifiers under near-independent samples (Theorem 2).
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant (Algorithm 3) to handle residual bias under the null hypothesis.
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic data with increasing Z dimension and on real flow-cytometry data.
- Technically sound with rigorous derivations and careful treatment of the non-i.i.d. nature of bootstrapped samples.
- Thorough empirical validation and appropriate comparisons to relevant prior work.
- Acknowledges limitations such as dimension dependence in theoretical guarantees and offers algorithmic fixes.
- Main clarity issues: inconsistent notation for dimension (dz, d_z, dz) and insufficient intuition for technical terms like the “generalized curvature matrix.”
- Original contribution: novel extension of classification-based independence testing to the conditional setting with theoretical guarantees.
- Introduces a key technical innovation: nearest-neighbor bootstrap that overcomes Z-dependence issues of permutation approaches.
- Significant practical advantages, especially in high-dimensional settings, and modularity to leverage advances in classification methods.
- Demonstrates real-world relevance via protein signaling network application and superior results on flow-cytometry data.
- Pros summarized: novel approach, strong theory, superior empirical performance, bias correction, and clear relation to prior work.
- Cons summarized: some technical explanations lacking, theoretical dimension dependence may limit extreme high-d scenarios, and limited discussion of computational complexity.
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-quality contribution deserving acceptance (recommended by reviewer).
- Suggested improvements: add intuitive explanation of generalized curvature matrix, standardize d_z notation, discuss computational complexity of nearest-neighbor bootstrap, and address limitations of dimension dependence with future directions.
