- Summary: Reframes conditional independence testing as binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples and trains a classifier to detect dependence.

- Novelty: Creative reduction of CI testing to classification, leveraging modern ML and differing from kernel-based methods.

- Theoretical foundation: Provides guarantees on bootstrap sample quality (TV distance) and classifier generalization (Theorems 1 and 2), albeit under strong assumptions.

- Empirical results: Competitive performance on synthetic and real (flow cytometry) data, especially in higher dimensions.

- Clear presentation: Core idea and algorithmic steps are mostly well explained and modular, enabling use of existing classifiers.

- Addressing limitations: Authors acknowledge classifier bias and propose Algorithm 3 to mitigate it.

- Strong assumptions: Theoretical results depend on strong smoothness assumptions; unclear sensitivity to assumption violations.

- Computational cost: Nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be expensive in high dimensions; scalability analysis is limited.

- Parameter sensitivity: Performance likely depends on choice of classifier, hyperparameters, and threshold τ; selection strategies are not discussed.

- Clarity in Algorithm 3: Explanation of the bias correction (subtracting classifier loss on Y and Z) is convoluted and needs more intuition.

- Related work coverage: Related work is reasonable but could better cover recent non-parametric and information-theoretic CI methods.

- Quality score: 7.5/10 — technically sound with caveats tied to assumptions.

- Clarity score: 8/10 — generally well-written but needs clearer exposition for some parts (Algorithm 3).

- Originality score: 9/10 — novel reduction to classification.

- Significance score: 8/10 — promising for causal discovery and high-dimensional CI testing.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel promising approach, solid theory and empirical validation, potential high impact, competitive with state-of-the-art.

- Arguments against acceptance: Generalizability limited by assumptions, computational cost concerns, and unclear aspects needing clarification.

- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.

- Requested revision 1: Provide deeper discussion of the impact and robustness to the smoothness assumptions.

- Requested revision 2: Analyze computational complexity and scalability of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap.

- Requested revision 3: Clarify and give intuitive justification for Algorithm 3's bias correction.

- Requested revision 4: Expand related work to include recent advances in non-parametric CI testing (e.g., information-theoretic approaches).
