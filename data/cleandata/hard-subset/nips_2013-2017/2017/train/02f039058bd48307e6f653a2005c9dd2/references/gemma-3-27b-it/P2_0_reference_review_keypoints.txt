- Novel reduction of conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- The authors provide theoretical guarantees on bootstrap quality (TV distance) and classifier generalization.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real datasets, including flow cytometry.
- Practical approach using standard tools (nearest-neighbor search, off-the-shelf classifiers) with code released.
- Paper is generally well-written and figures clarify the method.
- Theoretical assumptions (smoothness) may be restrictive and the derivations/notation are dense.
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap can be computationally expensive and scalability to very large/high-dimensional datasets is unclear.
- Performance likely sensitive to classifier choice, hyperparameters, and threshold τ; sensitivity analysis is limited.
- Bias correction in Algorithm 3 adds complexity (extra classifier trained on Y and Z) and its practical benefit is not convincingly demonstrated.
- A more detailed ablation comparing Algorithm 2 vs Algorithm 3 would be valuable.
- Related work discussion could be expanded to cover recent nonparametric CI and causal inference advances.
- Overall, the paper is a significant and promising contribution that balances strong theory and empirical validation.
- Concerns: restrictive theoretical assumptions, computational cost, and added complexity from bias correction.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, solid theory, strong empirical evidence, clear presentation, and available implementation.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
