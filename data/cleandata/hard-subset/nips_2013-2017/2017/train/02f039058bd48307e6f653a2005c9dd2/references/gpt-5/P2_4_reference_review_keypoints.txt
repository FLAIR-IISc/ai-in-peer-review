- Proposes CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification by 1-NN bootstrap swapping Y among nearest Z neighbors and training a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Offers a bias-corrected variant that subtracts a classifier using only (Y,Z) to account for finite-sample bootstrap discrepancies.  
- Theoretically shows one bootstrapped sample is O(n^{-1/dz}) close in total variation to the conditionally independent distribution under smoothness assumptions.  
- Provides generalization bounds for ERM classifiers under the near-i.i.d. dependence induced by the NN bootstrap.  
- Empirical comparisons against kernel CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear models and flow-cytometry data show superior ROC AUC in several regimes.  
- Relates to classifier two-sample tests and kernel CI tests; closest prior work (KCIPT) uses an LP-based permutation, which this replaces with a simpler 1-NN bootstrap.  
- Strength: nontrivial technical development—TV closeness result and dependence-aware generalization analysis are valuable contributions.  
- Strength: sound reduction that matches (X,Z) marginals across classes to focus testing on conditional dependence.  
- Strength: practical and scalable nearest-neighbor bootstrap with available code.  
- Weakness: lacks principled calibration—no asymptotic or finite-sample Type I error control or valid p-value procedure presented.  
- Weakness: threshold choice is heuristic (τ = 1/√n) and emphasis on ROC AUC is insufficient for hypothesis testing needs.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (curvature bound) is nonstandard and assumptions (Lipschitz f(z), etc.) may be restrictive and hard to verify.  
- Weakness: slow TV rate O(n^{-1/dz}) indicates a curse of dimensionality; discussion of sample-size requirements for higher dz is missing.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 bounds excess risk but does not translate to explicit size/power guarantees for CCITv1 or CCITv2.  
- Weakness: bias-correction (CCITv2) lacks formal justification showing the difference concentrates at 0 under H0.  
- Weakness: experimental omissions—no comparison to KCIPT or conditional MMD variants and reliance on ROC AUC rather than fixed-α error/power curves.  
- Weakness: implementation details (classifier family, hyperparameter tuning, data splitting) are inadequately specified given classification dependence.  
- Weakness: flow-cytometry ground truth is proxy-based (learned graphs), limiting interpretability; runtime reporting is absent.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized with helpful pseudocode, but some heavy notation and a few typos; Assumption 1 needs intuitive examples.  
- Originality: novel NN-based bootstrap to simulate fCI and reduction to classification; dependence-aware generalization analysis is a distinct contribution.  
- Significance: method is potentially valuable for causal discovery and graphical modeling if calibration and evaluation concerns are addressed.  
- Pros: conceptually simple reduction, nontrivial theory, good empirical performance in several regimes, open-source implementation, and classifier flexibility.  
- Cons: no demonstrated p-values/Type I control, strong/opaque assumptions, missing crucial comparisons and runtime/ablation analyses, and CCITv2 lacks theoretical guarantees.  
- Suggestions: add a permutation/randomization scheme on labels to obtain valid p-values and demonstrate size control at target α.  
- Suggestions: study sensitivity to k-NN (k>1) and approximate NN, clarify hyperparameter tuning and data splits, include ablations over classifiers.  
- Suggestions: add fixed-α error/power curves varying n and dz, include KCIPT and conditional MMD baselines, and report runtimes.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—compelling, fixable idea with solid theory and promising results, contingent on addressing calibration and experimental gaps.
