- Proposal: Introduces CCIT, reducing conditional independence testing X ⟂ Y | Z to a binary classification problem via a nearest-neighbor (NN) bootstrap that swaps Y across matched Z neighbors.
- Bootstrap mechanism: Split data, for each (x,y,z) find 1-NN in Z in the other half and swap Y to synthesize samples approximating the CI factorization.
- Bias correction: Subtracts accuracy of a (Y,Z)-only classifier from an (X,Y,Z) classifier to account for finite-sample bootstrap deviations.
- Theory: Proves a total variation bound dTV(·, fCI) = O(n−1/dz) under smoothness and a generalization analysis for ERM on near-independent bootstrapped samples.
- Empirics: CCIT with XGBoost outperforms KCIT/RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear models (Z up to ~70) and on flow cytometry benchmarks; code released.
- Relation to prior work: Extends classifier two-sample and permutation CI ideas to the CI setting with a simpler NN bootstrap vs. prior LP-based KCIPT.
- Strength — simplicity: NN bootstrap is easy, fast with standard data structures, and compatible with off-the-shelf classifiers.
- Strength — theory: Provides novel TV closeness guarantees and generalization bounds under non-i.i.d. near-independence.
- Strength — empirical & modular: Scales to high-dimensional Z in practice, performs well empirically, and benefits from classifier advances and domain-specific classifiers.
- Weakness — calibration: No principled way to set threshold τ or obtain p-values, so Type I error control at a specified α is not guaranteed.
- Weakness — curse of dimensionality: Theoretical TV rate O(n−1/dz) is poor in high-dimensional Z and relies on strong smoothness/tail/Lipschitz assumptions.
- Weakness — dependence complexity: Bootstrapped samples are dependent; generalization bounds have many constants/log factors and unclear practical calibration implications.
- Weakness — experimental gaps: Missing exact n, tuning protocols, runtimes, sensitivity analyses (classifier choice, k, B), and Type I/power at fixed α.
- Weakness — scope/robustness: Assumes continuous variables; handling discrete/mixed Z and low-density regions is not addressed or evaluated.
- Clarity issues: Notational inconsistencies, typos, and formatting glitches hinder readability despite overall clear organization.
- Originality: Nearest-neighbor conditional bootstrap and near-independence generalization analysis are novel adaptations for CI testing.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if calibration is solved—useful for causal discovery and feature selection.
- Suggested improvements: Propose/practice a calibration procedure (resampling/permutation) for p-values, report Type I/power at fixed α, add runtime and sensitivity studies, explore k-NN and cross-fitting, evaluate discrete/mixed Z robustness, and fix notation.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — compelling idea with solid theory and results but limited by lack of statistical calibration.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
