- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that generates samples close to the conditionally independent distribution.

- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrap quality (Theorem 1) and classifier generalization (Theorem 2), addressing non-i.i.d. bootstrap samples and high-dimensional Z.

- Leverages advances in supervised learning (e.g., gradient boosted trees, deep nets) to make CI testing practical and scalable compared to kernel-based methods.

- Introduces a bias-corrected variant (CCITv2) and practical guidance (e.g., threshold choice τ) to mitigate classifier bias under H0.

- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic experiments (better scaling with dim(Z)) and a real-world protein signaling network, with convincing comparisons to KCIT and RCIT.

- Distinguishes clearly from prior work (e.g., KCIPT) by simplicity of bootstrap and stronger theoretical guarantees.

- Theorem 1’s bound is complex, with multiple terms and a hard-to-interpret G(ε) term, and lacks explanation for the gap between worst-case theory and empirical performance.

- No computational complexity analysis or runtime measurements provided; nearest-neighbor search may be costly in high-dimensional Z.

- Does not evaluate sensitivity to classifier choice; robustness across classifiers (or weaker classifiers) is unexplored.

- Theoretical guarantees do not explicitly handle the regime d_z > n, a common high-dimensional setting.

- Lacks ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (nearest-neighbor bootstrap vs alternatives, effect of bias correction).

- Paper is technically sound, rigorous, and honest about limitations, with careful empirical validation.

- Well-written and organized, though some implementation details (e.g., nearest-neighbor search specifics) require external code reference.

- The approach is novel and original, representing a significant paradigm shift for CI testing.

- Results are significant for causal inference and related fields, showing practical improvements over state-of-the-art.

- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions: add complexity discussion, classifier sensitivity analysis, clarify theory-vs-empirical discrepancy, and address d_z > n limitations.

- Overall recommendation: Accept; Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
