- Proposes CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using a 1-NN bootstrap to generate conditionally independent surrogates.  
- Procedure: split 3n samples into U1 (holdout), U2/U3 for 1-NN Y-swaps to make U’2, then train classifier to distinguish U1 vs U’2.  
- Theoretical contributions: TV-distance bound showing U’2 is O(n^{-1/dz})-close to fCI under smoothness, ERM generalization bounds under near-i.i.d. sampling, and a bias-corrected error subtraction.  
- Empirically, CCIT with XGBoost outperforms kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear data and shows reasonable results on flow cytometry.  
- Builds on classifier two-sample testing and kernel CI tests; conceptually closest to KCIPT’s conditional permutation but uses a simpler 1-NN swap.  
- Strength: simple, modular reduction allowing use of modern classifiers with provided code.  
- Strength: NN bootstrap is computationally lightweight and backed by a TV-distance guarantee.  
- Strength: nontrivial generalization analysis under near-independence and a practical bias-correction to address finite-sample bias.  
- Strength: strong empirical scaling with |Z| on synthetic data and reasonable real-data performance.  
- Major weakness: no formal level-α testing procedure or p-value calibration; threshold τ is ad hoc and type I error control is not established.  
- Major weakness: TV bound decays as n^{-1/dz}, implying severe curse of dimensionality for large dz.  
- Major weakness: strong smoothness and bounded-curvature assumptions limit applicability to many practical Z distributions (mixed, heavy-tailed, categorical).  
- Concern: bootstrap induces dependencies and paper lacks careful cross-fitting or disjoint surrogate generation details to avoid dependence leakage.  
- Concern: little guidance on classifier choice, hyperparameter tuning, and impact on size/power; VC-type bounds likely vacuous for boosted trees.  
- Scope limitation: only continuous variables considered; no treatment or experiments for discrete/mixed data.  
- Empirical gaps: missing additional baselines (kNN CMI, CRT-like tests, Gaussian partial correlation), ablations (k in kNN, metric choice), and runtime benchmarks.  
- Clarity issues: some notation, constants, and G(ε) terms are hard to parse; τ, B, classifier settings, and train/test splitting need clearer description for reproducibility.  
- Originality: NN bootstrap and accompanying TV/generalization analyses are novel contributions over prior conditional permutation tests.  
- Significance: promising, scalable approach relevant to causal discovery and feature selection but needs calibration to be a proper hypothesis test.  
- Suggestions: provide level-α calibration (permutation or null resampling), cross-fitting, explore k-NN and metric choices, extend to discrete/mixed Z, add baselines, report runtimes and hyperparameter details.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing testing calibration and clarifying experimental methodology; ideas are interesting despite pessimistic theory.
