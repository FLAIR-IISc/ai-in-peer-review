- Proposes reducing conditional independence (CI) testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f_CI-like samples.  
- Algorithm 1 (nearest-neighbor bootstrap) produces samples approximately from f_CI with theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1: d_TV = O(1/n^{1/d_z})).  
- Theorem 2 gives generalization bounds for classification under near-independent (non-iid) bootstrapped samples.  
- Introduces a classifier-based CI test and a bias-corrected variant (CCITv2) to handle classifier bias under H0.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and shows superior performance over KCIT and RCIT, especially for high-dimensional Z (up to 70).  
- Demonstrates practical utility on a flow-cytometry causal discovery application.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous and well-motivated; proofs are sound though some are deferred to the appendix.  
- Paper is clearly organized with consistent notation and clear algorithm pseudocode; minor drawback is deferred proofs.  
- Method is original: novel reduction and NN-bootstrap differ fundamentally from permutation-based methods and prior work.  
- Significant contribution: improves scalability of CI testing with higher-dimensional conditioning sets and leverages modern classifiers.  
- Strengths: novel approach, rigorous theoretical guarantees, strong empirical results, and real-world applicability.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on why NN-bootstrap outperforms others in high dimensions, some theory in appendix, and CCITv2 bias correction appears ad hoc.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — substantial, well-executed contribution to CI testing and causal inference.
