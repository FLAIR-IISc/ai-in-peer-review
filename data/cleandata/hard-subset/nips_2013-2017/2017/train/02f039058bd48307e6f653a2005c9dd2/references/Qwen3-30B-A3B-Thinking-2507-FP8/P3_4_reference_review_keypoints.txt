- Proposed method: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f_CI and a classifier to distinguish true vs. bootstrapped samples.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Theorem 1 bounds total variation between bootstrapped samples and f_CI; Theorem 2 gives generalization bounds for classifiers under near-independent samples.  
- Empirical performance: Outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic data up to dz=70 and on flow-cytometry data, showing practical utility.  
- Originality: Novel extension of classification-based independence testing to the conditional setting with a Z-dependent nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Computational claim: NN bootstrap is presented as a computationally efficient alternative to permutation/LP methods, but runtime advantages are not quantified.  
- Strong assumptions: Smoothness assumptions (bounded generalized curvature of f(y|z)) are strong and may not hold in practice; sensitivity to violations is not discussed.  
- Dimensional scaling concern: Theoretical rate d_TV = O(1/n^(1/dz)) suggests poor high-dimensional scaling, yet empirical results contradict this without a provided theoretical explanation.  
- Comparison with prior work [10]: Claim of superiority over [10] is superficial; lacks detailed computational complexity and empirical runtime comparison.  
- Bias correction issue: Algorithm 3’s bias correction appears ad hoc and lacks rigorous theoretical justification for its specific form.  
- Limited empirical diversity: Synthetic experiments use a single post-nonlinear noise model, so robustness across varied data-generating processes is unclear.  
- Critical gap: Key limitation is the unresolved disconnect between worst-case theoretical bounds and strong empirical performance in high dimensions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking for (1) detailed comparison with [10] including complexity analysis, (2) discussion/sensitivity analysis for smoothness assumptions, and (3) exploration of the theoretical basis for the empirical-theory gap.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, novel contribution — Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10; paper merits acceptance with minor revisions.
