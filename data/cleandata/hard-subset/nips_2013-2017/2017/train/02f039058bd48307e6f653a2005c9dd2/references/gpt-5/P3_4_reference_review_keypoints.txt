- Proposes CCIT: convert conditional independence testing into a two-sample classification problem using an NN bootstrap to approximate f_CI(x,y,z).
- Constructs approximate null by splitting data and replacing Y in U2 with Y from 1-NN in U3 (in Z-space); classifier discriminates between original and bootstrapped samples.
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant that trains a (Y,Z)-only classifier and subtracts its loss to reduce finite-sample bias.
- Theoretical contributions: TV bound showing NN-bootstrap distribution is O(n^{-1/dz})-close to f_CI under smoothness; generalization bounds for ERM on near-i.i.d. samples; link between classification error and dTV(f,f_CI).
- Empirical evaluation: synthetic post-nonlinear models (dz up to 70) and flow-cytometry data show CCIT often outperforms KCIT/RCIT in ROC-AUC and appears to scale well.
- Relates to kernel-based CI tests (KCIT/RCIT), permutation-based KCIPT, classifier two-sample tests, and NN estimators; differs by using NN bootstrap and supervised classifiers instead of LP permutations/MMD.
- Strength: conceptually simple and practical, enabling use of powerful off-the-shelf classifiers (GBMs, DNNs) for CI testing.
- Strength: technical novelty in a clean NN conditional resampling scheme with nontrivial TV guarantees.
- Strength: modular approach—improvements in classifiers directly translate to better CI tests.
- Strength: promising empirical scaling and publicly released code.
- Strength: bias-correction idea is intuitive and addresses bootstrap artifacts.
- Weakness: lacks calibrated p-values or finite-sample Type I error control; threshold choice is heuristic and depends on unknown bias.
- Weakness: strong and opaque assumptions (e.g., “generalized curvature” of f(y|z) and smoothness of f(z)); constants and practical regimes unclear.
- Weakness: core TV rate O(n^{-1/dz}) implies a curse of dimensionality in Z; no theory/guidance for k>1 NN or smoothing to improve bias-variance.
- Weakness: NN reuse induces complex dependencies; generalization bounds contain unusual rates (e.g., n^{-1/3}) and unspecified constants, making practical implications hard to judge.
- Weakness: experimental issues—unclear whether KCIT/RCIT were tuned, no runtime comparisons, and flow-cytometry ground truth is imperfect/ambiguous.
- Weakness: no discussion of mixed/categorical Z or choice of metric for NN search.
- Quality: main ideas are sound and theory is nontrivial, but strong assumptions and presentation weaken the contribution.
- Clarity: generally clear but needs better exposition of Assumption 1, interpretable constants in Theorem 1, disambiguation of overloaded notation, and practical guidance (metric, k-NN, classifier, cross-fitting).
- Originality: moderately high—NN-bootstrap CI construction with TV guarantees and near-independence generalization analysis is novel.
- Significance: potentially high practical impact, but lack of calibrated testing and heavy assumptions limit immediate applicability for structure learning requiring p-values.
- Suggested improvements: provide a calibrated level-α test (e.g., Monte Carlo NN bootstraps, cross-fitting, label-flip), explore k-NN/local regression theoretically and empirically, and clarify assumptions.
- Suggested improvements: perform thorough hyperparameter tuning for baselines, report runtimes, and include ablations for classifier choice, k, metric, dz/n, and low-density sensitivity.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — elegant and practical approach with novel analysis, but needs calibrated Type I control and stronger empirical baselines.
