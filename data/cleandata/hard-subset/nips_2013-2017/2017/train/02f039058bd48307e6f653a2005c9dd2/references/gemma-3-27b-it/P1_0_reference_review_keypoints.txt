- Presents a novel CI testing approach by reducing CI testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: total variation distance bounds for the bootstrap and generalization bounds for the classifier.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance, especially as the dimension of Z increases.  
- Positions method relative to nonparametric CI tests and contrasts advantages over kernel-based and entropy-based methods.  
- Highlights avoidance of computationally expensive linear programming required by KCIPT.  
- Connects to permutation-based independence testing and explains how the bootstrap handles Z-dependent permutations.  
- Novelty: conceptually appealing reduction of CI testing to classification.  
- Strong theoretical foundation offering insight into method behavior, albeit under potentially restrictive assumptions.  
- Empirically competitive, with apparent advantage in higher-dimensional Z settings.  
- Modular design separates bootstrap sampling from classifier choice, enabling flexibility and future improvements.  
- Clear and well-defined problem statement.  
- Presentation is dense with heavy notation and could be clearer with more intuitive explanations.  
- Relies on strong smoothness assumptions (Assumptions 1 & 2) that may not hold in practice and lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: synthetic experiments focus on a specific model and real-data ground truth is imperfect.  
- Bias correction algorithm (Algorithm 3) adds complexity and lacks clear empirical justification and intuition.  
- Missing detailed computational cost comparison (training and bootstrap times) with existing methods.  
- For acceptance: method is novel, theoretically grounded, empirically promising, and modular.  
- Against acceptance: dense presentation, strong assumptions, and insufficiently comprehensive experiments.  
- Requests clearer intuition for the nearest-neighbor bootstrap and its relation to the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Asks for sensitivity analysis to violations of smoothness assumptions.  
- Requests detailed computational cost comparison with existing CI tests.  
- Seeks clarification on threshold selection (τ) in Algorithms 2 and 3.  
- Asks about the impact of different classifier choices beyond XGBoost.  
- Questions why training a classifier only on Y and Z in Algorithm 3 suffices to estimate bias.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, test sensitivity to assumptions, expand experiments, and provide cost analysis.
