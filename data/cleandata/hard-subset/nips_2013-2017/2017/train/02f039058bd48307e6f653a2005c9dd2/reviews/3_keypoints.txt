- Proposes a general test for conditional independence among three continuous variables with weak parametric assumptions.  
- Constructs samples close (in total variation) to the conditional product distribution via neighbor-matching and uses them as one class.  
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural net) to distinguish joint vs. product samples and uses classifier predictive error to accept/reject conditional independence.  
- Empirically compares the method to existing CI tests on synthetic and real data.  
- Method is practically useful for causal-inference tasks (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and accessible for practitioners, though theoretical proofs can be hard to follow.  
- Theoretical guarantees are strong and impressive, extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting.  
- Authors acknowledge and connect to prior work ("Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests").  
- Question: Is there a discrete-variable analog, and does bootstrap become more powerful when Z is discrete?  
- Question: How sensitive is the test to choice of classifier and hyperparameters—could this change accept/reject outcomes?  
- Presentation issues: Section 1.1 is redundant and Algorithm 2 could be described more succinctly.  
- Minor typos noted: "classifier" (line 157) and "graphs" (line 280).
