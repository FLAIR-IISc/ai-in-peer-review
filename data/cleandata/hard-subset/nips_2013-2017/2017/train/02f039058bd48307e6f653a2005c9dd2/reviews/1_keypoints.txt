- Proposes a conditional independence test using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrapped samples; fail to reject CI if classifier performs like random.

- Provides bounds on total variation between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal classifiers and near-independent samples.

- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Major 1: Method decomposes into sample generation and classification—asks whether existing alternatives (e.g., permutation-based generation [7] or kernel two-sample tests) can replace one component and how their performance compares.

- Major 2: Questions how the method detects weak conditional dependence when the NN-bootstrapped distribution is already close to f^{CI} in finite samples.

- Major 3: Asks if the method is symmetric in x and y and how it handles causal asymmetry (x→y vs y→x) when deciding which variable to sample.

- Major 4: Serious concern about how to choose the parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3.

- Minor: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be divided by the sample size (normalization).
