- Problem and approach: Reduces non-parametric conditional independence testing to binary classification via a two-sample test between f and f_CI = f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Sample generation: Proposes a nearest-neighbor bootstrap (Algorithm 1) that swaps Y values using nearest Z neighbors to approximate f_CI.  
- Theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1): Bounds total variation distance between bootstrapped samples and the target f_CI, justifying the bootstrap.  
- Testing procedure: Trains a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped samples and rejects H0 if accuracy significantly exceeds chance.  
- Generalization theory (Theorem 2): Provides novel classifier generalization bounds for the near-independent, non-i.i.d. samples from the bootstrap.  
- Bias correction: Introduces a bias-correction step (Algorithm 3 / CCITv2) by training a second classifier on (Y,Z) to remove systematic bootstrap bias.  
- Empirical performance: Outperforms kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT), especially for high-dimensional conditioning Z, on synthetic and real flow cytometry data.  
- Technical quality: The work is technically sound, complete, and well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Theory–practice gap: Notes a pessimistic theoretical rate O(n^{-1/dz}) versus strong empirical high-d performance; calls for discussion of this gap.  
- Possible explanations for gap: Suggests addressing whether worst-case smoothness assumptions or classifier robustness explain practical success.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, organized, and aided by Figure 1 and clear pseudocode.  
- Threshold choice: Requests justification and sensitivity analysis for the test threshold τ (τ = 1/√n used in experiments).  
- Aggregation over runs: Asks to clarify how B repeated runs are aggregated into a single test statistic or p-value.  
- Classifier details: Recommends specifying the type and parameterization of the classifier ĝ0 used for bias estimation.  
- Originality: Novel and effective application of classifier-based two-sample testing to conditional independence via the nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Significance: Likely high impact for causal discovery, feature selection, and graphical models; modular approach leverages advances in classification.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel reduction, strong empirical results, solid theoretical contributions, and practical flexibility.  
- Rejection concerns: Main weaknesses are the theory–practice gap and incomplete implementation/hyperparameter details affecting reproducibility.
