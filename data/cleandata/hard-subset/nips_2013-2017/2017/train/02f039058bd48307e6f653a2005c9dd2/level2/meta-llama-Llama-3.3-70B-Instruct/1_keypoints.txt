- Proposal: convert non-parametric CI testing for continuous variables into a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- Method: introduce a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution, enabling classifiers like gradient-boosted trees and deep nets.
- Theory: provide bounds on total variation distance between bootstrapped and CI distributions and generalization bounds for classification under near-independent non-i.i.d. samples.
- Main claims: (1) CI testing reduces to binary classification, (2) NN bootstrap generates near-CI samples, (3) the approach outperforms existing methods empirically.
- Empirical evidence: thorough experiments on synthetic and real-world (flow-cytometry) datasets demonstrate effectiveness.
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations of approach and theoretical foundations.
- Strength: novel and innovative approach with potential to advance CI testing.
- Strength: thorough and rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the method.
- Strength: well-designed and convincing empirical evaluation.
- Weakness: relies on the assumption of continuous underlying distributions, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be ineffective in high-dimensional settings due to the curse of dimensionality.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
