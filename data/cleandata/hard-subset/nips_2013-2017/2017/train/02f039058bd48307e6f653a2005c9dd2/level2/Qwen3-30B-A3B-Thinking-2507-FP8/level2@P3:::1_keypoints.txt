- Proposes a model-powered approach that reduces conditional independence (CI) testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap (Algorithm 1) generates approximate samples from fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) using only joint samples.
- Theoretically proves generated samples are close to fCI in total variation distance (Theorem 1).
- Provides generalization bounds for classification under near-independent, non-i.i.d. samples (Theorem 2).
- Empirical results show substantial performance gains over KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real datasets.
- Bootstrap procedure is computationally efficient compared to KCIPT’s expensive linear-programming permutation sampling.
- Clear, well-motivated presentation linking CI testing to supervised classification.
- Method is flexible to different classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, neural networks).
- Demonstrated practical utility on protein signaling and flow cytometry applications.
- Theoretical TV convergence rate O(1/n^{1/dz}) can be slow in high-dimensional Z; needs more explanation for empirical robustness.
- Bias correction in Algorithm 3 appears ad-hoc and would benefit from a more rigorous justification.
- Empirical section lacks analysis of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings (uses XGBoost without alternatives).
- Overall: technically sound, significant contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support, deserving acceptance.
