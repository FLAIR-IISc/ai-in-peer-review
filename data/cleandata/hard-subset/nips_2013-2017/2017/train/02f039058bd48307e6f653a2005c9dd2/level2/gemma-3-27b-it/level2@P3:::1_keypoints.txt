- Tackles non-parametric conditional independence testing for continuous variables by reducing CI testing to a classification problem using classifiers like XGBoost and DNNs.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples approximating the conditional independence distribution f_CI.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: total variation distance bound for the bootstrap (Theorem 1) and generalization bounds for classification with non-i.i.d. bootstrapped samples (Theorem 2).  
- Connects generalization analysis to Rademacher complexity and carefully handles the non-i.i.d. nature of bootstrapped data.  
- Empirical validation on simulated and real datasets shows performance gains over existing CI tests (KCIT, RCIT).  
- Includes Algorithm 3 for bias correction, showing thoughtful refinement of the method.  
- Well-motivated problem with broad applications (Bayesian network discovery, causal inference); reduction to classification is natural and promising.  
- Assumptions required for theoretical results (smoothness of f(y|z) and f(z)) may be strong and could limit applicability.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis to violations of smoothness assumptions.  
- Insufficient analysis of scaling with dimensionality of Z and computational costs across dataset sizes/dimensions.  
- Choice of XGBoost is not justified; comparison with other classifiers is missing.  
- Related work section is extensive but could be more focused and more critically comparative.  
- Some notation inconsistencies (e.g., use of f vs q for distributions) affect clarity.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with rigorous theory and empirical gains; reviewer recommends acceptance despite the above concerns.
