- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that generates f_CI samples.  
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap enables use of powerful classifiers (gradient-boosted trees, deep nets) and avoids expensive linear programs used by permutation-based methods.  
- Theoretical guarantees: bootstrapped samples are close to f_CI in total variation (Theorem 1) and there are generalization bounds for classification under near-independent samples (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and flow-cytometry show significant performance gains over KCIT and RCIT, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: novel, model-powered approach that leverages advances in classification for CI testing.  
- Strength: nearest-neighbor bootstrap is an elegant, computationally practical alternative to prior methods.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and thorough empirical evaluation support the method.  
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed discussion of limitations and potential failure cases (e.g., highly non-uniform or clustered Z distributions).  
- Weakness: inadequate analysis connecting theoretical worst-case bounds to observed practical performance.  
- Significance: highly relevant for causal inference and structure learning; opens new ML-driven directions for statistical testing.  
- Presentation: well-written, clearly organized, and well-contextualized in the literature.  
- Overall: strong contribution with compelling theory and experiments; merits acceptance pending minor improvements to limitation discussion.
