- Frames non-parametric CI testing as a classification problem using powerful classifiers (boosted trees, neural nets), a promising model-driven approach.  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate fCI samples with theoretical guarantees on total variation distance.  
- Provides significant theoretical contributions, notably generalization bounds in a non-i.i.d. setting.  
- Empirical results show performance improvements over KCIT and RCIT, with stronger gains on synthetic data.  
- Includes Algorithm 3 with bias correction to address finite-sample issues.  
- Well-motivated paper that clearly outlines CI testing applications and limitations of prior methods.  
- Presentation is verbose and contains repetitive material, especially in related work.  
- Theoretical analysis is dense and may be inaccessible to readers without statistical learning theory background.  
- Real-data (flow cytometry) performance gains are moderate compared to synthetic results.  
- Biological interpretation of discovered CI relationships is limited and should be expanded.  
- Discussion of discrepancies in ground-truth graphs (edges present in all three) is insufficient and could be deepened.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve conciseness and accessibility.
