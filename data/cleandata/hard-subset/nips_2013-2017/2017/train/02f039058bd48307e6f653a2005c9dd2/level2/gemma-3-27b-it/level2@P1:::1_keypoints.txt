- Proposes framing conditional independence testing for continuous variables as a binary classification task using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate fCI samples.  
- Novel reduction allows leveraging modern classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: total variation bound for bootstrap samples (Theorem 1).  
- Provides generalization bounds for the classifier (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical results show improved performance on simulated and real-world datasets and good scalability with dimensionality.  
- Well-motivated problem with broad applications and a comprehensive related work section.  
- Algorithm 3 (CCITv2) with bias correction lacks clear rationale for subtracting the Y,Z-only classifier loss and the intuition behind this bias correction.  
- The threshold τ = 1/√n in Algorithm 3 appears arbitrary; a justification or sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Practical implications of theoretical assumptions (smoothness in Theorem 1, VC dimension in Theorem 2) are not fully discussed; sensitivity to assumption violations is unclear.  
- Guidance on hyperparameter choices (e.g., number of neighbors for bootstrap, classifier tuning) is missing.  
- Related work is comprehensive but somewhat dated; newer kernel-based CI tests and nonparametric methods (e.g., energy-based, neural ODE approaches) should be compared.  
- Computational cost of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be high in high dimensions; a detailed complexity analysis and comparison to methods like KCIT is needed.  
- Empirical evaluation could be deeper: test alternative classifiers beyond XGBoost and provide a more detailed biological analysis of the flow cytometry results.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Algorithm 3, discuss practical implications of assumptions, analyze computational complexity, and broaden empirical comparisons.
