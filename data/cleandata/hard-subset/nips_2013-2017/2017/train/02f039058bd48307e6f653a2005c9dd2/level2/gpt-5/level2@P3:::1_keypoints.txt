- Method: reduces nonparametric CI testing to binary classification using an NN-bootstrap that swaps y from 1-NN in z to approximate fCI(x,y,z).
- Variant: proposes a bias-corrected CCITv2 using the difference between classifiers trained on (x,y,z) and (y,z) to reduce finite-sample bias.
- Theory 1: proves bootstrapped sample distribution is close to fCI in total variation with rate O(n^{-1/d_z}) under smoothness assumptions.
- Theory 2: derives generalization error bounds for empirical risk minimization under near-independence, adapting VC/Rademacher tools to dependent samples.
- Empirics: outperforms KCIT/RCIT in synthetic post-nonlinear settings as d_z grows (to ~70) and shows gains on flow cytometry data; code released.
- Relation to prior work: extends classifier-based two-sample and independence tests to CI testing and avoids LP in KCIPT while connecting to non-i.i.d. generalization and NN entropy estimation literature.
- Strength — simplicity: conceptually simple and practical by leveraging modern supervised learners and domain knowledge.
- Strength — NN bootstrap: novel, efficient construction with explicit total variation guarantees.
- Strength — theory: original near-independence generalization analysis that adapts classical results.
- Strength — empirics & implementation: empirical gains in high-d_z settings, real-data evaluation, and open-source implementation.
- Weakness — calibration: lacks a principled level-α test with valid p-values and finite-sample Type I control; threshold τ=1/√n is heuristic.
- Weakness — assumptions and rates: smoothness assumptions are nonstandard and the O(n^{-1/d_z}) rate incurs a severe curse of dimensionality without clear guidance.
- Weakness — NN in high-d: nearest-neighbor search degrades in high dimensions; details on exact vs approximate NN, metrics, and impact on guarantees are missing.
- Weakness — baselines & runtime: missing comparisons to KCIPT at small scale, conditional mutual information estimators, CHSIC variants, and no runtime/scalability benchmarks.
- Weakness — sensitivity: insufficient ablations on classifier choice, hyperparameters, Type I behavior under H0, and sensitivity to non-smoothness or d_x/d_y.
- Quality: technical development and proofs are careful and nontrivial; experiments support claims but lack calibration and runtime analysis.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, but assumptions and practical guidance (choosing τ, G, NN implementation) need more intuition and detail.
- Originality: novel reduction of CI testing to classification via NN-bootstrap and original near-independence analysis.
- Significance: potentially impactful for structure learning and causal discovery by providing a simple, scalable, model-powered testing approach.
- Essential suggestions: provide calibrated p-values/Type I control (e.g., binomial test, permutation/split-conformal, repeated sample-splitting), clarify Assumption 1 with examples and robustness analysis, and document NN search details and effects of approximate NN.
- Important but optional: add runtime/memory benchmarks vs KCIT/RCIT/KCIPT, include CMI/CHSIC baselines, perform classifier/hyperparameter ablations, and explore k-NN or local resampling variants.
- Minor suggestions: specify tie-breaking and handling of rare z regions, and expand/ formalize intuition for the bias-correction in CCITv2.
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution with solid theory and promising empirical results; recommend acceptance after addressing essential calibration and empirical-analysis issues.
