This paper proposes a novel approach for Conditional Independence (CI) testing by converting it into a binary classification problem. The authors use a model-powered approach, which enables them to harness the power of supervised learning models like gradient-boosted trees and deep neural networks. The key idea is to generate samples from the conditional product distribution fCI(x, y, z) = f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure. The authors provide theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and also risk generalization bounds for their classification problem under non-i.i.d near-independent samples.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The problem setting is well-defined, and the authors have provided a thorough review of related work in the field. The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in CI testing.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors have provided a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure for generating samples from the conditional product distribution fCI(x, y, z). They have also provided theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples, showing that they are close to fCI in terms of total variational distance. Additionally, the authors have provided risk generalization bounds for their classification problem under non-i.i.d near-independent samples.

The empirical results presented in the paper are impressive. The authors have compared their proposed approach with other state-of-the-art algorithms, including KCIT and RCIT, and have shown that their approach outperforms these algorithms in various scenarios. The results on the flow-cytometry dataset are particularly interesting, as they demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach in real-world applications.

However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure and its theoretical guarantees. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the choice of the threshold parameter τ in Algorithm 3.
