- Proposes reducing nonparametric conditional independence testing to a supervised binary classification problem using an NN bootstrap to generate samples from the conditional product fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).
- NN bootstrap: split data into U1/U2, replace Y in U1 with Y of 1-NN in Z from U2 to form negative-class samples; label originals 1 and bootstraps 0, train classifier, reject H0 when accuracy exceeds chance.
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant that compares classifiers trained on (X,Y,Z) versus (Y,Z) to address finite-sample bias from non-i.i.d negatives.
- Theoretically proves the NN-bootstrapped distribution is close to fCI in TV distance under smoothness, with dTV = O(n^{-1/dz}) up to constants/small terms.
- Provides generalization (Rademacher-style) bounds for ERM classification when negative samples are dependent-but-near-i.i.d., linking Bayes risk to dTV(f,fCI).
- Empirically outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear models with high-dimensional Z and shows competitive results on flow cytometry-derived tasks.
- Leverages modern classifiers (e.g., XGBoost) for practical gains in challenging high-dim Z regimes and offers a simple, efficient data-generation alternative to KCIPT’s LP.
- Novel contributions include the NN bootstrap for approximating fCI and the learning-theoretic analysis under near-independence.
- Main weakness: lacks calibrated frequentist testing guarantees—no uniform asymptotic level-α control, principled thresholding, or p-value procedure (empirics use ROC/AUC instead).
- Theoretical rate suffers from the curse of dimensionality (n^{-1/dz}), with no adaptivity results (e.g., intrinsic manifold dimension or margin conditions) explaining practical success in high dz.
- Some regularity/smoothness assumptions (curvature on f(y|z), Hessian on f(z)) are strong or opaque and need clearer motivation or examples.
- Dependence induced by shared neighbors in bootstrapped samples is not fully quantified—more analysis on how this affects finite-sample error is needed.
- Algorithmic asymmetry: swapping only Y via Z-NN breaks X↔Y symmetry; the paper should discuss symmetrization or ensembling over both swaps.
- Missing direct empirical comparison to the closest prior KCIPT (despite computational cost) and other baselines like conditional MMD or partial distance correlation.
- Real-data evaluation (flow cytometry) has uncertain ground truth; recommended to include semi-synthetic or simulated causal-graph experiments for clearer validation.
- Quality: conceptually sound with nontrivial theory and reproducible experiments, but absence of calibrated testing procedures limits guarantees on Type I error.
- Clarity: generally well organized and clear on pipeline and bias correction, but some theoretical assumptions and which theorems apply to which algorithm need clearer exposition.
- Significance: scalable, modular alternative for CI testing that could be widely useful if coupled with valid calibration procedures.
- Suggested improvements: provide p-value/level-α calibration (null-label calibration, cross-fitting/conformal approaches), study k-NN/symmetrized variants, sensitivity analyses over dz, k, classifier class, and add KCIPT comparison.
- Recommendation: weak accept — promising, novel, and empirically strong work, but needs stronger calibration and clearer assumption/empirical comparisons to fully justify acceptance.
