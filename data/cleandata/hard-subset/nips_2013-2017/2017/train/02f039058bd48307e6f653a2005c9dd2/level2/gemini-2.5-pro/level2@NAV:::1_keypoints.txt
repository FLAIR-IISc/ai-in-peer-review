- Proposes reducing non-parametric conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem by distinguishing f(x,y,z) from f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating f_CI from joint samples, enabling the reduction.
- Positions the CI test to leverage modern high-dimensional classifiers (e.g., XGBoost), extending classifier-based two-sample testing to conditional independence.
- Competes with and provides an alternative to kernel-based CI tests like KCIT and RCIT.
- Originality/significance: the NN-bootstrap reduction is elegant and could significantly impact causal discovery and feature selection by using supervised learning advances.
- Technical quality: provides theoretical guarantees on bootstrap sample quality (Theorem 1) and novel generalization bounds for classifiers on non-i.i.d. data (Theorem 2).
- Empirical performance: thorough experiments showing substantial improvement over state-of-the-art, especially as dim(Z) grows; demonstrated on flow cytometry data.
- Theory-practice gap: theoretical bounds suffer from curse of dimensionality in dim(Z) while empirical results are much stronger; needs discussion on why modern classifiers mitigate this gap.
- Threshold selection: test performance depends on threshold τ with only a heuristic provided; calls for a principled selection method or sensitivity analysis.
- Bias correction clarity: CCITv2 is a useful practical fix but should be better connected to Theorem 1 to explain how small deviations from f_CI can produce false signals under the null.
