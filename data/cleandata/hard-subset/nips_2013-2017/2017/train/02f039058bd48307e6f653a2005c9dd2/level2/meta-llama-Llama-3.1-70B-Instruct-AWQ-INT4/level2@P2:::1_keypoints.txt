- Proposes converting Conditional Independence testing into a binary classification problem using supervised models (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets).  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples from the conditional product distribution fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that bootstrapped samples are close to fCI in total variation distance.  
- Derives risk generalization bounds for the classification problem under non-i.i.d. near-independent samples.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem setup and thorough related-work review.  
- Approach is novel and has potential to advance the state-of-the-art in CI testing.  
- Empirical results are strong, outperforming KCIT and RCIT across scenarios.  
- Real-world applicability demonstrated via promising results on the flow-cytometry dataset.  
- Minor clarity issue: nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure and its theoretical guarantees need more detailed explanation.  
- Minor methodological issue: insufficient insight into the choice and role of threshold parameter τ in Algorithm 3.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above clarifications.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
