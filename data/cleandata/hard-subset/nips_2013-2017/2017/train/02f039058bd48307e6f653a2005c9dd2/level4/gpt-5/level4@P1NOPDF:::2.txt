The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths
1) The paper correctly notes that, in order for the test to work, one needs an assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose such an assumption and, under it, bound the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the bootstrap density. This appears to be novel.
2) The paper then leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which combines the smoothness assumption, the assumption that hat R can be achieved with error eta, and the assumption on the gap between the errors of the optimal and trained classifiers, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses
The paper is difficult to read and provides little interpretation of the results, to the point that I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in 1.1 (iii). Assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1. Suppose G is a small class, say linear functions, and that it is not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI at all. Can you explain, intuitively, how a small error probability can still be achieved in this setting (i.e., how hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 can hold for large n)?
