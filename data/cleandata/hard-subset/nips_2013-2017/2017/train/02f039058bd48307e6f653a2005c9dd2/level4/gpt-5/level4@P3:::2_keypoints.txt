- Method: Reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to create fCI-like synthetic samples and train a classifier to distinguish original vs synthetic; reject H0 if classifier outperforms chance (with a bias-corrected variant CCITv2).
- Theory: Provide TV-distance bounds showing NN-bootstrap approximates fCI under smoothness, and generalization bounds for ERM under near-independence, yielding testing error bounds.
- Empirical: Outperforms kernel-based baselines (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic and flow cytometry data, especially as dim(Z) increases.
- Relation to prior work: Extends classifier-based two-sample/independence testing to CI, offers a simple alternative to permutation-with-LP (KCIPT) with explicit TV analysis.
- Strength — conceptual: Elegant, modular reduction that leverages off-the-shelf classifiers.
- Strength — bootstrap: Simple, efficient nearest-neighbor bootstrap with novel TV analysis.
- Strength — generalization: Valuable analysis of ERM when training on near-independent (NN-bootstrapped) data.
- Strength — practical: Good empirical performance and favorable scaling in Z; bias-correction helps finite-sample bias.
- Weakness — clarity: Paper is dense; assumptions (e.g., curvature) and terms in the TV bound need clearer intuition and a roadmap tying assumptions to results.
- Weakness — thresholding/calibration: Limited guidance on choosing threshold τ to control Type I error; needs calibration procedures for nominal level control.
- Weakness — classifier dependence: Guarantees depend on classifier class G; large approximation error η (weak G) can render the test powerless and should be discussed with diagnostics.
- Weakness — dimensional dependence: TV bound scales like O(n−1/dz), which is pessimistic; need to state regimes (manifold/low-effective-dim) where practice matches theory.
- Weakness — bootstrap details: Sensitivity to distance metric, ties, and using k-NN vs 1-NN is unexplored; empirical ablations/theory needed.
- Weakness — evaluation: Uses ROC–AUC but lacks size-vs-power curves and calibrated thresholds; additional baselines (CMI estimators, KCIPT) would strengthen comparisons.
- Quality: Technically sound with nontrivial analyses, though constants/finite-sample implications could be clarified.
- Clarity (overall): Main ideas clear but notation and exposition should be tightened, with plain-language assumption explanations and a worked example.
- Originality: High — novel NN-bootstrap for CI with TV and generalization analyses.
- Significance: Potentially high — modular test that can leverage advances in classification and useful theoretical tools.
- Questions for authors: Clarify role of η and when it is small; recommend τ selection for Type I control and if bias-correction can be combined with calibration; guidance on k-NN vs 1-NN; report runtime and complexity vs KCIT/RCIT.
- Pros for acceptance: Clean reduction, strong empirical results, novel and useful theory, available code.
- Cons for acceptance: Challenging exposition, lack of level-calibrated evaluation and τ guidance, dependence on classifier capacity, pessimistic dz scaling.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying η/G role, providing threshold calibration guidance, and improving exposition.
