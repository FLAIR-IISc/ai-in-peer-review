- Proposes a novel conditional independence (CI) test by reducing CI testing to a binary classification problem using a model-powered/bootstrap approach.  
- Introduces a smoothness assumption on the conditional density and provides a bound on total variation distance between null density and bootstrap-generated density.  
- Leverages the smoothness assumption to derive generalization bounds for the classifier trained on generated data, linking CI test performance to classifier error.  
- Presents Theorem 1 combining smoothness, classifier error, and distribution distance, offering a non-trivial theoretical foundation.  
- Major weakness: presentation lacks clarity, making technical arguments and result interpretation difficult.  
- Raises a concern about plausibility of small classifier error under limited function classes (e.g., linear) when alternative holds—needs intuitive explanation.  
- Intro is verbose and should be streamlined to better motivate CI testing and limitations of existing methods.  
- Notation is heavy and inconsistent; simplifying and standardizing subscripts would reduce ambiguity.  
- Algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1–3) are overly long; concise pseudocode with clear variable names recommended.  
- Theoretical results lack intuition and explanation of key terms (e.g., meaning and impact of d_z on bounds).  
- Empirical results are under-contextualized; need clearer experimental setup, parameter choices, metrics, and justification of claims about scaling.  
- Related work is comprehensive but should focus more on specific advantages of the proposed method over prior approaches.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framing of CI testing, theoretical guarantees for bootstrap and classifier, empirical performance gains, and formalization of smoothness assumptions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation, limited intuition for theory, insufficiently comprehensive empirical evaluation, and weak connection between theory and practical performance.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising but requires substantial revision for clarity and intuition; reviewer recommends weak reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
