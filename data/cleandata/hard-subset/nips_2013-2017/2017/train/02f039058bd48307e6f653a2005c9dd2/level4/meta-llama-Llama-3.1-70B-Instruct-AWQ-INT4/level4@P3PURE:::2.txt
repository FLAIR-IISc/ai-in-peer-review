The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths:

1. The authors identify a crucial assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density that must be made for a test to be effective. They propose such an assumption and derive a bound on the distance in total variation between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from the bootstrap under this assumption. This contribution is novel and noteworthy.

2. The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set, as shown in equation (1.1 (iii)).

3. Theorem 1 is a significant result, as it combines the assumption on smoothness, the assumption that the error rate can be achieved with error η, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers. This theorem is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses:

The paper is challenging to read, and the authors do not provide sufficient interpretation of the results. This lack of clarity makes it difficult to fully understand the implications of the findings. For instance, considering the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii), if we assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1, and suppose G is a small class of functions (e.g., linear functions) that are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI, it is unclear how a small probability of error can be achieved. Specifically, it is not immediately apparent how the condition hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 can be satisfied for large n. A more intuitive explanation of this result would be beneficial for readers.
