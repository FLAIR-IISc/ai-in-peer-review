- Paper proposes using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and a classifier to test conditional independence: low classification accuracy => accept null, high accuracy => reject.

- Authors prove bounds on total variation distance between the bootstrap distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal and near-independent samples.

- Overall assessment: tackles an important problem and is well-presented.

Major points:
- Could components be replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approaches or kernel two-sample tests); performance of such alternatives is not evaluated.

- Unclear how the method performs in finite samples when X and Y are weakly dependent given Z.

- Symmetry concern: method does not address causal asymmetry between X and Y and how to choose which variable to sample.

- Choice of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not justified or discussed.

Minor:
- Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to report empirical risk without dividing by the sample size.
