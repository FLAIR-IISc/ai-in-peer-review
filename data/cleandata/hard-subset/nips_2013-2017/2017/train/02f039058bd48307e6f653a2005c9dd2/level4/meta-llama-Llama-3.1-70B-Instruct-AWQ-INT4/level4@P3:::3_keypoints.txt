- Proposes a nonparametric conditional independence test using samples close to the conditional product distribution and a binary classifier to decide acceptance/rejection.
- Method adapts classifier two-sample testing (e.g., deep nets) to the conditional independence setting and is evaluated against existing methods on synthetic and real data.
- Important for causal inference applications where testing unconfoundedness is common.
- Paper is well-written and balances technical rigor with practitioner accessibility.
- Theoretical results are the most impressive aspect, providing strong guarantees for the method.
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness across synthetic and real datasets.
- Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the method’s limitations.
- Sensitivity to the choice of classifier and impact of hyperparameter tuning are not thoroughly explored.
- Some redundancy (e.g., Section 1.1) should be condensed to improve flow.
- Suggest adding a discussion of the discrete analog, where bootstrap may be more powerful for discrete Z.
- Recommend a more detailed sensitivity analysis to classification method and hyperparameters.
- Fix minor typos (e.g., "classifier" line 157, "graphs" line 280).
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to conditional independence testing and suitable for NIPS publication.
