- Proposes CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification using a 1-NN bootstrap that swaps Y between nearest Z pairs to approximate fCI = f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Method splits 3n samples, builds pseudo-samples via NN bootstrap, trains classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped; indistinguishable under H0, separable under H1.  
- Theoretical contributions: TV bound showing NN-bootstrap is O(n^{-1/dz}) close to fCI under smoothness on f(y|z) and f(z).  
- Provides generalization bounds for ERM classifiers trained on near-independent (non-i.i.d.) samples, with Rademacher/VC-style analysis.  
- Introduces CCITv2 bias correction that subtracts the accuracy of a classifier trained on (Y,Z) to mitigate finite-sample bias.  
- Empirically outperforms kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic high-dimensional Z settings and flow cytometry data.  
- Related to classifier two-sample tests and KCIPT, but uses simple NN bootstrap instead of expensive LP or permutations.  
- Strength: novel, elegant reduction leveraging modern classifiers and modular to use expressive models (trees, deep nets).  
- Strength: nontrivial theoretical guarantees (TV bound and near-i.i.d. generalization) that complement prior work.  
- Strength: practical scalability to larger/more-dimensional Z where kernel methods struggle.  
- Strength: thoughtful bias-correction comparing all-features vs. YZ-only classifiers for finite-sample issues.  
- Weakness: exposition is challenging; bounds and practical implications need clearer intuition and explanation.  
- Clarify why 1-NN in Z makes Y approximately independent given z, how rare-z regions are handled via G(·), and intuition for Theorem 1.  
- Explain curse-of-dimensionality dependence O(n^{-1/dz}), when it’s loose vs. observed performance, and role/calibration of threshold τ and finite-sample bias b(n).  
- State clearly whether Type I error is controlled at level α and consider permutation or cross-fitting for p-values to aid interpretability.  
- Provide computational complexity details (1-NN search, number of bootstraps B) and sensitivity to choice of Z metric (Euclidean vs. standardized/learned).  
- Discuss robustness to violations of smoothness (heavy tails, discontinuities) and practical consequences when classifier class G is misspecified or weak.  
- Empirical gaps: include ablations on classifier choice, k in k-NN (k>1), and sensitivity to Z scaling/normalization.  
- Technical question: clarify which term in bounds captures approximation error due to limited classifier class G and how the bound degrades when G is not expressive.  
- Quality: technically solid, substantive contributions in NN-bootstrap TV bound and non-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  
- Clarity: needs more intuition, clearer assumptions, practical guidance, streamlined notation, and proof roadmaps.  
- Originality: high — novel NN-bootstrap for approximating fCI plus adapted generalization bounds.  
- Significance: significant for causal discovery and feature selection; flexible, scalable approach with theoretical value.  
- Acceptance pros: novel practical reduction, strong empirical gains, and nontrivial theory.  
- Acceptance cons: presentation/interpretability gaps, strong smoothness and dimensionality dependence, limited guidance on classifier choice and calibration.
