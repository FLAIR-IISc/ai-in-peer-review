This paper proposes a novel conditional independence test, presenting several key strengths and weaknesses.

Strengths:

1. The authors identify a crucial assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density required for a test to be effective. They propose such an assumption and derive a bound on the distance in total variation between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from the bootstrap under this assumption. This contribution is novel and significant.

2. The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set, as stated in equation (1.1 iii). This result is a valuable addition to the field.

3. Theorem 1 is a notable achievement, combining the assumption on smoothness, the assumption that the estimated risk can be achieved with error η, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers. This theorem is novel, insightful, and non-trivial, demonstrating the authors' expertise in the subject matter.

Weaknesses:

The paper is challenging to read due to the lack of interpretation and explanation of the results. This lack of clarity raises concerns about the accuracy of the reviewer's understanding of the paper. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii). Assuming the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1, suppose G is a small class of functions, such as linear functions, which are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI. It is unclear how a small probability of error can be achieved, specifically how hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 can be guaranteed for large n. A more intuitive explanation of this result would be beneficial.

To improve the paper, the authors should focus on providing clearer explanations and interpretations of their results, making the content more accessible to readers. This would enhance the overall quality and impact of the paper.
