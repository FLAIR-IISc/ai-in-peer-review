- Proposes a novel CI testing method for continuous variables by reducing conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem.
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from the conditional product distribution f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).
- Uses classifier accuracy between original and bootstrap samples as the test statistic to decide whether to reject X ⊥ Y | Z.
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the bootstrap samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance (Theorem 1).
- Derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independent samples (Theorem 2), extending Rademacher complexity analysis to non-i.i.d. settings.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance over state-of-the-art methods on multiple synthetic datasets and real flow cytometry data.
- Evaluation is comprehensive across varying Z dimensions and the authors supply a GitHub implementation for reproducibility.
- Makes a significant, novel contribution to causal inference, Bayesian network learning, and feature selection by extending classifier-based testing to the conditional case.
- Positions itself relative to prior kernel-based nonparametric CI tests and handles the more challenging Z-dependent permutations.
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible, though some sections could be clearer or more concise.
- Notes redundancy in Section 1.1 and that Algorithm 2 is presented more verbosely than necessary.
- Lacks analysis of sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameter settings; experiments use XGBoost only.
- Theoretical results predict poor scaling with Z dimension, but empirical results show better-than-expected scaling.
- Does not address possible extensions to discrete variables, which the reviewer suggests could be effective.
- Meets NIPS criteria on quality, clarity, originality, and significance; reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
