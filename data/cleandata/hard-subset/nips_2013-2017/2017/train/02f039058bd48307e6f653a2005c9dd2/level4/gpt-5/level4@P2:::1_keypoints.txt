- Proposes reducing nonparametric conditional independence testing to binary classification using an NN-bootstrap to generate fCI-like samples.
- Implements the CI test as a two-sample classification between original data and NN-bootstrapped (Y-swapped) samples.
- Provides a TV-distance bound showing the NN-bootstrapped distribution approximates fCI under smoothness, with error ≈ O(n^{-1/dz}).
- Gives generalization bounds for empirical risk minimization trained on near-independent samples from the NN bootstrap.
- Empirical results show improved performance over kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) and a permutation-based approach (KCIPT), especially for higher-dimensional Z using strong classifiers (e.g., XGBoost).
- Strength: clear, modular reduction enabling use of modern learners.
- Strength: novel NN-bootstrap with total variation guarantees using only observed samples.
- Strength: technically interesting generalization analysis for dependent training samples.
- Strength: strong empirical performance and practical, efficient implementation with available code.
- Suggestion: study interchangeability of modules—replace NN-bootstrap with other generators or the classifier with kernel two-sample tests and include an ablation to quantify each module’s contribution.
- Concern: sensitivity to weak conditional dependence—request power curves versus effect size and n and analysis of how TV gap maps to expected excess classifier accuracy.
- Suggestion: practical calibration strategies near the decision boundary (repeated splits/bootstraps, ensembling).
- Concern: asymmetry introduced by fixing (X,Z) and swapping Y—suggest symmetric variant or averaging both swap directions and reporting effect.
- Critical: threshold τ choice is crucial for Type I control—recommend null calibration via repeated NN-bootstraps or conditional permutations, cross-fitting/sample-splitting, and sensitivity analysis.
- Additional: discuss when NN approximation is bottleneck for large dz and whether k-NN (k>1), local regression, or learned Z-representations could help.
- Additional: comment on computational cost of NN search in high dimensions (approximate NN) and overall runtime vs kernel methods including classifier tuning overhead.
- Additional: clarify strength and testability of smoothness assumptions on f(y|z) and f(z) and robustness to violations.
- Additional: ensure fair bandwidth/parameter tuning for competing methods and verify Type I error calibration.
- Quality: technical development is largely sound with novel proofs addressing non-i.i.d. challenges.
- Clarity: generally well written; request an algorithm schematic and summary of parameter choices for reproducibility.
- Originality: NN-bootstrap for fCI and generalization analysis are original contributions.
- Significance: practical impact for causal discovery and high-dimensional CI testing.
- Pros for acceptance: simple modular method, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical performance, and available code.
- Cons against acceptance: need clearer guidance on sensitivity/calibration near weak dependencies, X–Y asymmetry, and under-specified τ selection.
- Minor: normalize empirical risk notation, fix typos, and specify NN distance metric, tie-breaking, and feature standardization.
