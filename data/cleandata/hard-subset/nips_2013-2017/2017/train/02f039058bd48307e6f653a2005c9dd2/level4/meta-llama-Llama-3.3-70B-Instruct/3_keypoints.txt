- Proposes a nonparametric conditional independence test for three continuous variables by generating samples approximating the conditional product distribution (in total variation) and using a binary classifier's error to accept/reject the null.
- Method involves neighbor-matching on the conditioning variable Z to create fake samples, then training a classifier (e.g., deep net) to discriminate real vs. fake.
- Benchmarked against existing approaches with descriptive analysis and experiments on synthetic and real-world data.
- Highly relevant for causal inference where conditional independence (unconfoundedness) is critical.
- Paper is clearly written and makes complex theoretical results accessible, though some proofs are hard to follow in detail.
- Theoretical contributions are strong, extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional case with provable guarantees.
- Suggest exploring a discrete-variable analog, as bootstrap can be more powerful when Z is discrete.
- Recommend studying sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters, since these can affect test outcomes.
- Suggest streamlining repeated summaries in Section 1.1 and possibly removing the explicit Algorithm 2 to reduce redundancy.
- Minor typographical errors to fix (e.g., missing "classifier" at line 157 and "graphs" at line 280).
