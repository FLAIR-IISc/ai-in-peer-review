- Proposes a nonparametric CI test that uses a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z)-like samples and treats CI testing as a binary classification problem using classifier accuracy as the test statistic.  
- Method is significant and broadly applicable to causal inference and graphical model learning, leveraging modern classifiers to scale to high-dimensional Z.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: bounds on the bootstrap approximation in total variation distance and novel generalization bounds for classifiers on near-i.i.d. samples.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with good motivation and accessible presentation of methods and theory.  
- Concern about sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters; need analysis or experiments showing robustness and tuning impact on hypothesis decisions.  
- Unclear applicability to discrete or mixed-type variables; nearest-neighbor bootstrap may adapt well to discrete Z but authors should comment or extend.  
- Presentation redundancy: repeated summaries in the introduction/Section 1.1 and Algorithm 2 pseudocode could be condensed.  
- Minor typos to fix: "classier" → "classifier" (Line 157); "graph" → "graphs" (Line 280).  
- Overall: strong, well-supported paper with impressive empirical results and minor revision needs; recommend acceptance.
