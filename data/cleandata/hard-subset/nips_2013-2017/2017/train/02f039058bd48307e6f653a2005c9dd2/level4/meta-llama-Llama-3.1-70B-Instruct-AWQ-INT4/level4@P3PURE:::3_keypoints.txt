- Proposes a general nonparametric conditional independence test for continuous X,Y,Z by approximating the conditional product distribution in total variation distance.  
- Generates samples approximating the conditional product, trains a binary classifier on joint vs product samples, and uses classifier predictive error to test CI.  
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees, extending the classifier two-sample test to the conditional case via Z-nearest-neighbor matching.  
- Comprehensive empirical comparison to existing methods with experiments on synthetic and real data.  
- Method is straightforward, powerful, and relevant for causal inference (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; theoretical proofs are dense but overview is clear.  
- Question: Is there a discrete analog of the method (bootstrap may be more powerful for discrete Z)?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of classifier and hyperparameters?  
- Suggestion: Section 1.1 is redundant and overly repetitive.  
- Suggestion: Algorithm 2 can be described more succinctly or omitted.  
- Minor typos noted: "classifier" (line 157) and "graphs" (line 280).
