- Proposes a nonparametric conditional independence test by reframing CI as a two-sample test using a classifier to distinguish P(X,Y,Z) from samples approximating P(X|Z)P(Y|Z)P(Z).  
- Generates the second sample via nearest-neighbor matching on Z to approximate the conditional product distribution.  
- Uses classifier accuracy (e.g., deep neural network) as the test statistic to accept or reject conditional independence.  
- Provides both theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Addresses a fundamental CI problem with applications in causal inference and unconfoundedness testing.  
- Method is straightforward to implement and appears empirically powerful.  
- Paper is well-written and summarizes dense theoretical results accessibly for practitioners.  
- Main theoretical contribution: novel guarantees extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting via nearest-neighbor matching.  
- Query about discrete Z: nearest-neighbor becomes exact and bootstrap could be more effective—discussion of this special case is requested.  
- Request for analysis of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters to check robustness of test decisions.
