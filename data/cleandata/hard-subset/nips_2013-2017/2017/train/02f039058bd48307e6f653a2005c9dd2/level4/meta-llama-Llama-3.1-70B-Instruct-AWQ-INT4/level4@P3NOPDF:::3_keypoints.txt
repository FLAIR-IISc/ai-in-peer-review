- Proposes a nonparametric test for conditional independence among three continuous variables using samples approximating the conditional product distribution.  
- Generates synthetic samples close in total variation to the conditional product and trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep NN) to distinguish joint vs. product distributions.  
- Uses classifier predictive error as the test statistic to accept or reject conditional independence.  
- Provides comprehensive comparisons to existing methods with descriptive analysis and experiments on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Method is straightforward, powerful, and well-suited for causal inference tasks like testing unconfoundedness.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible, with clear overviews despite some proofs being challenging to follow.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong, adapting classifier two-sample tests to include a third variable Z via neighbor matching.  
- Neighbor-matching approach yields robust theoretical guarantees.  
- Suggestion: explore a discrete analog of the method, since bootstrap is especially powerful for discrete Z.  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to the choice of classifier and hyperparameters, as small changes may affect test decisions.  
- Suggestion: remove redundant Section 1.1 to streamline the paper.  
- Suggestion: avoid fully writing out Algorithm 2; describe it more succinctly.
