- Introduces a model-powered CI testing approach that reduces CI testing to binary classification by generating f_CI samples via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap (Algorithm 1).
- Proposes a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap and proves it approximates f_CI in total variation distance (Theorem 1), with error O(1/n^(1/d_z)) under smoothness assumptions.
- Provides generalization bounds for classification with near-independent samples (Theorem 2), linking classification error to the TV distance between true distribution and f_CI.
- Shows that under H0 the classifier error approaches 0.5 and is significantly lower under H1, using Rademacher-complexity-based analysis.
- Empirical results demonstrate clear advantages over kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) and strong scaling to higher-dimensional Z, including real flow-cytometry data.
- Paper is hard to follow due to limited intuition and interpretation of theoretical bounds and their practical implications for limited classifier classes.
- The smoothness assumptions in Theorem 1 are technical and insufficiently discussed; the paper lacks examples or discussion on how restrictive they are in practice.
- Connections to prior NIPS CI-testing work are superficial; a deeper comparison of theoretical and empirical advances is needed.
- Overall assessment: technically strong with rigorous theory and compelling empirical performance, but needs better exposition and contextualization of assumptions and prior work.
