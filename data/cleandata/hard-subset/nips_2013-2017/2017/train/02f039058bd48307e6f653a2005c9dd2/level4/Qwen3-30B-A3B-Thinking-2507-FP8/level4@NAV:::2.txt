

The paper introduces a novel conditional independence test.  
Strengths  
1) The paper establishes that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for the test to function, proposes such an assumption, and bounds the total variation distance between the null hypothesis density and the bootstrap-derived density under this assumption. This contribution is novel.  
2) The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1(iii)).  
3) Theorem 1, which integrates the smoothness assumption, the requirement that the empirical risk \(\hat{R}\) is achievable with error \(\eta\), and the bound on the error difference between the optimal and trained classifiers, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.  
Weaknesses  
The paper is challenging to follow and lacks sufficient interpretive discussion of results, leaving me uncertain about their correct understanding. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1(iii): assuming the alternative hypothesis holds and \(r_0 = 0.1\), let \(G\) be a small class (e.g., linear functions) that cannot distinguish \(f\) and \(f^{\text{CI}}\) at all due to insufficient expressiveness. How can the small error probability \(\hat{R} < 0.1 + o(1) < 0.2\) for large \(n\) be achieved intuitively?
