- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate fCI-like samples.  
- Leverages powerful classifiers (GBDT, deep nets) to handle complex, high-dimensional distributions.  
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap is novel and provably generates samples close to the conditional independence distribution.  
- Theorem 1 gives a TV-distance bound dTV(., fCI) = O(1/n^(1/dz)) under smoothness assumptions.  
- Provides new generalization/Rademacher complexity analysis for classification under near-independent samples (Theorem 2).  
- Shows that under H0 classifier error approaches 0.5 while under H1 error decreases, connecting classification performance to CI testing.  
- Empirical results show clear gains over KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real datasets, including flow-cytometry causal discovery.  
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant (CCITv2) to address finite-sample bias for practical use.  
- Paper is poorly organized with weak intuitive explanations linking classification error to CI conclusions.  
- Raises a concrete concern about expressivity: small classifier classes may fail to distinguish f from fCI, undermining the practical interpretation of error bounds.  
- Smoothness assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not well-motivated for real, high-dimensional applications.  
- Comparison with related work (e.g., KCIPT) is insufficient; computational complexity and clear distinctions are not fully quantified.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for strong technical contributions and empirical validation, but require improved exposition and discussion of assumptions and comparisons.
