- Proposes a classifier-based conditional independence test that approximates P(X|Z)P(Y|Z)P(Z) via resampling/matching over Z and uses a binary classifier to distinguish these from joint samples.  
- Provides nontrivial theoretical guarantees linking the resampling scheme and classifier performance to test power and error control.  
- Method is relevant to causal inference and modern ML, timely and practically valuable.  
- Conceptually clear: frames conditional testing as a classifier two-sample problem, leveraging advances in classification.  
- General and practical: accommodates flexible classifiers (including deep nets) with minimal parametric assumptions.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data strengthens the approach’s credibility.  
- Extends prior classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting, addressing nontrivial matching/weighting and approximation-error control.  
- Question about discrete or low-cardinality Z: request clarification on specialized/resampling variants and guidance for mixed discrete/continuous Z.  
- Concern about sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters; ask for variance reporting, ablations across classifier families, and a clear hyperparameter selection protocol under the null.  
- Calibration and Type I error control need explicit description: how thresholds/p-values are set (permutation, sample-splitting), and finite-sample calibration guidance.  
- Need more detail on constructing conditional product samples: neighbor selection (metric, kernel, bandwidth/k), effects on TV approximation, and sensitivity to Z dimensionality.  
- Request discussion of curse-of-dimensionality effects and empirical/theoretical behavior as dim(Z) grows.  
- Ask for computational complexity and scaling analysis for large n and high-dim Z.  
- Clarify data-splitting protocol for resampling, classifier training, and test-statistic computation; suggest cross-fitting or repeated splits to stabilize results.  
- Comparisons should ensure fair tuning of strong baselines (e.g., KCIT/RCIT), report power at matched Type I error with CIs, and give guidance on sample-size regimes.  
- Recommend reporting effect sizes (e.g., AUC gap, calibrated p-values) alongside binary decisions for interpretability.  
- Writing is generally clear and accessible, but Section 1.1 is repetitive and Algorithm 2 could be more concise.  
- Minor issues: typos at Line 157 and 280; suggest a brief glossary for theory notation.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper with meaningful theory and solid experiments; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications on calibration, robustness, and presentation.
