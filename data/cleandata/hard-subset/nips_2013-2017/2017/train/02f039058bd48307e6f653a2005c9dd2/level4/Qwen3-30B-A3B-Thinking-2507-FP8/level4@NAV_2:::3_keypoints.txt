- Reduces conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditional product samples.
- The bootstrap is theoretically shown to produce samples close to the true conditional product distribution in total variation distance.
- Enables use of powerful classifiers (gradient-boosted trees, deep nets), yielding strong empirical gains, especially in high-dimensional Z settings.
- Extends the Lopez-Paz & Oquab (2016) classifier two-sample framework to handle conditional independence with a third variable Z.
- Provides novel theory: Rademacher complexity analysis and generalization bounds for classification under near-independence.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, showing consistent improvements on synthetic datasets and real flow-cytometry data.
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and practically valuable for causal inference and Bayesian network structure learning.
- Weakness: Section 1.1 contains redundant repetition of core ideas.
- Weakness: Algorithm 2 pseudocode is overly detailed and adds little beyond the main text.
- Minor issues: typographical errors (missing "classifier" at line 157; "graphs" misspelled as "graps" at line 280).
- Suggestion: explore adapting the bootstrap method for discrete Z variables.
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters to assess robustness.
- Overall: significant, principled, and practical contribution, well-suited for NIPS and of interest to causal inference and statistical learning researchers.
