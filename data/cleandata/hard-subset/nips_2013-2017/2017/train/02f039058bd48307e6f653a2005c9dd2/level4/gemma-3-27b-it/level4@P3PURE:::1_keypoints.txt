- Proposes CI test using nearest-neighbor bootstrapping to approximate f^CI and a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrapped samples; near-random classification implies CI.  
- Provides finite-sample bounds on total variation distance between NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^CI and gives empirical risk bounds under ideal and nearly-independent sampling.  
- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.  
- Major: Method naturally splits into sample-generation and classification; reviewer asks whether existing approaches (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests) could replace components and how that affects performance.  
- Major: Unclear how the method differentiates weak conditional dependence from true conditional independence.  
- Major: Questions about symmetry in x and y — causal directions (x→y vs y→x) may require different sampling strategies but are not discussed.  
- Major: Serious concerns about selection and sensitivity of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3.  
- Minor: Empirical risk computation in Algorithms 2 and 3 appears to omit division by sample size (normalization).
