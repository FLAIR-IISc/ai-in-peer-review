The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths
1) The paper correctly recognizes that, in order for such a test to work, one must assume some smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose such an assumption and, under it, bound the total-variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained via the bootstrap. This is novel.
2) The paper uses this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1(iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which combines the smoothness assumption, the assumption that hat R can be achieved with error eta, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and the trained classifier, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses
The paper is hard to read and provides little interpretation of the results, to the extent that I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in Section 1.1(iii). Assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1. Suppose G is a small class (e.g., linear functions) that is not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI at all. Can you explain, intuitively, how a small error probability can be achieved in this case (i.e., hat R < 0.1 + o(1) < 0.2 for large n)?
