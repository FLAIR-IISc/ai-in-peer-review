- Tackles non-parametric conditional independence testing for continuous variables by reducing CI testing to binary classification using strong classifiers (GBTs, DNNs).  
- Main contribution is a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the CI distribution.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrap approximation quality under a stated smoothness assumption.  
- Introduces a well-defined smoothness assumption bounding total variation between true and bootstrapped distributions—novel and important.  
- Derives generalization bounds linking training classifier error to underlying distributions (section 1.1 (iii)).  
- Theorem 1 combines smoothness, generalization error, and classifier training/optimal error differences; technically insightful and non-trivial.  
- Paper is dense and lacks sufficient intuitive explanations, making theoretical implications hard to grasp.  
- Specific clarity issue: explanation of the inequality in section 1.1 (iii) is missing—how limited model classes can achieve low empirical error under the alternative.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and needs stronger connection to the theoretical claims, with more discussion of experimental setup and parameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of reduction and bootstrap, strong theoretical rigor, and potential impact via leveraging classification advances.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity/exposition and insufficient empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions focused on readability and intuitive exposition of key theoretical results.
