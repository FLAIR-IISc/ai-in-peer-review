- Introduces a novel classifier-based test for conditional independence of three continuous variables using samples approximating the conditional product distribution in total variation distance.  
- Generates synthetic samples from the joint to mimic the conditional product distribution, then trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural net) whose predictive error decides acceptance/rejection.  
- Benchmarked against existing CI tests with qualitative descriptions and quantitative experiments on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Method is straightforward, powerful, and applicable to causal inference tasks like assessing unconfoundedness.  
- Paper is well-written and practitioner-friendly despite complex theoretical details.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant, extending the classifier two-sample test to include a third variable Z with strong guarantees.  
- Suggest investigating a discrete-version/analog, where bootstrap methods might be especially effective for discrete Z.  
- Recommend studying sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters, since these may affect test outcomes.

- Writing out Algorithm 2 in full is unnecessary; a concise description would suffice.
