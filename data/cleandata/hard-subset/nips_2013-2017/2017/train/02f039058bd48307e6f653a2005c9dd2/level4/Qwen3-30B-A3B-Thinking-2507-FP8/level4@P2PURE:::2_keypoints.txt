- Identifies need for a smoothness assumption on the conditional density for test validity, proposes such an assumption, and bounds the total-variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density — novel contribution.  
- Leverages the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1(iii)).  
- Theorem 1 non-trivially integrates the smoothness assumption, the empirical-risk-achievability error (η), and the assumed error gap between optimal and trained classifiers — novel and insightful.  
- Paper is difficult to follow and lacks interpretation, making verification hard; e.g., unclear how a small empirical error \hat{R}<0.1+o(1)<0.2 is achievable when G is a small (e.g., linear) class that cannot distinguish f from f^{CI} under the alternative with r0=0.1.
