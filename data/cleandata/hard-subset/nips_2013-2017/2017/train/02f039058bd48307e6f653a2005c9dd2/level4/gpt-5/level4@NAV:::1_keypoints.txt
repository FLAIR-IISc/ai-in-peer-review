1. Introduces a model-based conditional independence test using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrapped samples; fail to reject if classifier performs near random.

2. Provides theoretical bounds on total variation distance between the nearest-neighbor bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, plus empirical risk bounds for idealized classification and near-independent samples.

3. Overall assessment: addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.

4. Major: Method decouples into sample-generation and classification—either step might be replaceable by existing methods (e.g., permutation tests or kernel two-sample tests); comparative performance of such hybrids is unclear.

5. Major: Nearest-neighbor bootstrap only approximates f^{CI} in finite samples—unclear how the test behaves when X and Y are weakly dependent given Z.

6. Major: Symmetry concern—paper does not discuss whether the method is symmetric in X and Y or whether the choice of which variable to resample matters in causal scenarios.

7. Major: Serious practical concern about how to choose the parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3.

8. Minor: In Algorithms 2 and 3, the empirical risk appears should be divided (normalized) by the sample size.
