- Novel contribution: recognizes the necessity of a smoothness assumption on the conditional density for CI testing and provides a novel bound on the total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density under that assumption.  
- Uses the proposed smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (section 1.1(iii)).  
- Theorem 1 is a novel, non-trivial integration of the smoothness assumption, an estimated-risk error bound (η), and an assumption on the difference between optimal and trained classifier errors.  
- Weakness: presentation is dense and under-interpreted — e.g., unclear how a small empirical error (ĤR < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n) can be achieved if G is a small, low-expressive class (like linear functions) when r0 = 0.1; requests intuitive clarification.
