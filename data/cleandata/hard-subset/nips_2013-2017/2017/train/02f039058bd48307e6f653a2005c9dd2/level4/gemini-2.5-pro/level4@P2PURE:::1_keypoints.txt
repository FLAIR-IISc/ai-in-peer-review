- Proposes a classifier-based conditional independence test using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate synthetic f_CI samples and a classifier to distinguish them from observed data.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation between the bootstrapped distribution and f_CI, and on empirical risk for ideal classifiers under perfect and near conditional independence.  
- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.  
- Major: Unclear whether the sampling or classification components could be replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based sampling or pairing the bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests); comparisons with such hybrids are needed.  
- Major: Unclear test power under weak conditional dependence because the bootstrap only approximates f_CI, so it may fail to distinguish small departures from the null.  
- Major: Potential asymmetry between X and Y—paper does not address whether bootstrapping X vs bootstrapping Y affects results or relates to causal direction.  
- Major: Practical selection of threshold parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not addressed, yet appears crucial for performance.  
- Minor: Algorithms 2 and 3 likely omit a normalization in the empirical risk definition (should be divided by sample size).
