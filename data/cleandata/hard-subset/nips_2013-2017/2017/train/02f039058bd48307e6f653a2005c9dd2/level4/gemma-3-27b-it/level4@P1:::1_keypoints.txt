- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification task using a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f^CI-like samples.  
- Trains a classifier to distinguish original joint samples from bootstrapped samples to decide conditional independence.  
- Provides theoretical finite-sample bounds on total variation closeness between the bootstrap distribution and f^CI, plus generalization bounds for the classifier under near-independence.  
- Addresses an important problem with clear presentation and solid literature positioning, relevant to causal inference, Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Major concern: performance dependence on choice of components—need comparisons replacing either bootstrap or classifier with existing methods (e.g., permutation-based bootstrap, kernel two-sample tests).  
- Major concern: sensitivity to weak dependencies—unclear how reliably the method distinguishes weak dependence from true conditional independence.  
- Major concern: causal directionality not addressed—method is symmetric in X and Y and does not investigate whether sampling one variable favors detecting a direction.  
- Major concern: threshold parameter τ selection lacks justification and sensitivity analysis; impact on Type I/II errors is not explored.  
- Minor fix: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, classification-based CI approach; provided theoretical guarantees; strong empirical performance in high dimensions; clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons with alternative component combinations; limited sensitivity analysis for weak dependencies; insufficient τ justification; no treatment of causal directionality.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing major concerns—especially parameter selection and robustness/sensitivity analyses.
