- Identifies that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for a theoretically sound classifier-based CI test, and proposes a formal such assumption.  
- Derives a novel bound on the total variation distance between the true null distribution and the bootstrapped sample using that smoothness assumption.  
- Uses the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (as in sec. 1.1 (iii)).  
- Presents Theorem 1, a novel result integrating the smoothness assumption, an optimization error term (eta) for empirical risk, and a generalization error bound.  
- Primary weakness: paper presentation is difficult to read and lacks interpretation of theoretical results, leaving the reviewer uncertain about the central claims.  
- Specific confusion/example: unclear intuition for sec. 1.1 (iii) in scenarios where classifier class G is too simple to distinguish dependency—how can empirical risk concentrate near the Bayes rate r0 if G cannot represent the dependency; requests clearer explanation of this point.
