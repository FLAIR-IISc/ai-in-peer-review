- Proposes using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating f^{CI}, then trains/tests a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrapped data for CI testing.  
- Provides bounds on total variation distance between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and derives empirical risk bounds under ideal and near-independent settings.  
- Paper tackles an important problem and is clearly written.  
- Major question whether either component can be replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based tests or kernel two-sample tests) and how those alternatives would perform.  
- Concern that in finite samples the method may not distinguish weak conditional dependence from true conditional independence.  
- Method is symmetric in x and y but does not address causal asymmetry—unclear if or how sampling should depend on causal direction.  
- The parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is poorly justified and its selection/impact is not discussed.  
- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.
