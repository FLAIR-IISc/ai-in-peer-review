- Introduces a smoothness assumption on the conditional density and proves a bound on the total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density.  
- Uses the smoothness assumption to derive a bound on the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Eq. 1.1 iii).  
- Presents Theorem 1 combining smoothness, achievability of estimated risk (with error η), and the gap between optimal and trained classifier errors—a nontrivial, novel result.  
- Writing is dense and difficult to follow, impairing comprehension of results.  
- Theorems and proofs are hard to interpret, making the practical implications unclear.  
- Unclear how a small error probability is achievable under the alternative when the function class G is not expressive enough (e.g., linear G cannot distinguish f and f^CI).  
- The paper needs a more intuitive explanation (and examples) clarifying conditions like hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n.
