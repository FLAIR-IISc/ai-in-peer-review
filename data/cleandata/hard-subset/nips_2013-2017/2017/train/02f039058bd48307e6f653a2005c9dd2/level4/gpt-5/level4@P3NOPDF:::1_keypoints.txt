- Summary: Proposes an NN-bootstrap to approximate the conditional-null fCI and uses a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped samples, with theoretical TV and empirical-risk bounds; modular and clearly presented.

- Strengths: Simple reduction to two-sample classification, NN bootstrap avoids density estimation, provides theoretical approximation/error bounds, and is flexible across losses and classifiers.

- Major 1 (modularity/alternatives): Discuss interchangeability of components—replace NN bootstrap with permutation/stratification or model-based generators, or replace classifier with MMD/HSIC—and include empirical comparisons (NN+MMD, permutation+classifier) analyzing power, runtime, and robustness.

- Major 2 (weak dependence): Provide a local-alternatives analysis and sensitivity experiments showing detection rates vs. dependence strength, NN parameters (k/radius), and classifier capacity; discuss finite-sample biases and their effect on type I/II errors.

- Major 3 (symmetry and causal asymmetries): Clarify which variable is resampled (X|Z vs Y|Z), consider symmetric or max-combination variants with multiplicity correction, give practical guidelines (resample lower-dimensional/easier-to-model variable), and briefly discuss interactions with causal directions.

- Major 4 (threshold τ calibration): Specify principled τ calibration (label-permutation null, sample-splitting/cross-fitting with binomial tails, or outer-loop CV for NN params), provide an algorithmic calibration procedure, and state resulting type I guarantees.

- Major 5 (data reuse/dependence): Warn that reusing observations for both NN bootstrap and classifier inflates type I error; recommend disjoint splits or cross-fitting with p-value aggregation to mitigate dependence.

- Major 6 (high-dimensional Z and metric): Discuss degradation in high-dimensional/heterogeneous Z, recommend metric learning or dimensionality reduction, report theory/practice sensitivity to Z dimension, and detail approximate NN search and computational costs.

- Minor comments: Correct empirical-risk normalization in Algorithms 2/3, use “reject/fail to reject” language, explicitly state i.i.d./support/smoothness assumptions used in proofs, and report variance/confidence intervals across splits/bootstraps.

- Suggested experiments: Type I control across Z dimension and sample sizes; power under varied dependence forms/strengths; component-swap comparisons; symmetry study (resample X|Z vs Y|Z and symmetrized tests); sensitivity to k/radius and classifier regularization.

- Overall assessment: Timely and promising method with useful theory, but needs clearer calibration, careful handling of data reuse, symmetry/high-dim discussion, and empirical validation of modularity to be a compelling practical alternative.
