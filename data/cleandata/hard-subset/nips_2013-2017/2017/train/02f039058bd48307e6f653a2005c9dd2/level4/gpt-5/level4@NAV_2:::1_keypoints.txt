- Proposes CI testing by turning it into a binary classification task: use NN bootstrap to synthesize fCI-like samples and train a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped; near-random accuracy implies failing to reject CI.

- Provides total variation bounds for how close the NN-bootstrapped distribution is to fCI and generalization bounds on empirical risk in i.i.d. and near-independence settings.

- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Method decomposes into sample-generation and classifier components; assess whether either component can be swapped with existing techniques (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests) and compare such hybrids.

- NN bootstrap is only approximate with finite samples; need analysis of sensitivity in near-null regimes with weak conditional dependence.

- Unclear if the method is symmetric in X and Y; need discussion on resampling choice when dependence arises via X→Y or Y→X.

- Choice of threshold parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is a practical concern; require clear guidance or a data-driven calibration procedure.

- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.
