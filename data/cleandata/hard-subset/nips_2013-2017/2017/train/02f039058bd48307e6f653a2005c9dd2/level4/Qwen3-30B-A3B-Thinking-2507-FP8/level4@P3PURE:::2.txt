

The paper introduces a novel conditional independence test.  

**Strengths**  
1) The paper identifies that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for the test to function, and proposes such an assumption. It then bounds the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from bootstrap under this assumption. This contribution is novel.  
2) The paper leverages this smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1(iii)).  
3) Theorem 1, which synthesizes the smoothness assumption, the assumption that the empirical risk \(\hat{R}\) can be achieved with error \(\eta\), and the assumption on the error difference between the optimal and trained classifiers, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.  

**Weaknesses**  
The paper lacks sufficient interpretive clarity, making it difficult to fully grasp the results. For instance, consider Section 1.1(iii): assuming the alternative hypothesis holds and \(r_0 = 0.1\), suppose \(\mathcal{G}\) is a small class (e.g., linear functions) that cannot distinguish between \(f\) and \(f^{\text{CI}}\) at all. How can the small error probability \(\hat{R} < 0.1 + o(1) < 0.2\) for large \(n\) be achieved intuitively? The argument does not provide sufficient explanation for this scenario.
