- Core idea: Reduce non-parametric conditional independence testing to binary classification by using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate approximate CI samples and training a classifier to distinguish real vs bootstrapped data.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provide a total variation bound between the bootstrap distribution and ideal f_CI and novel generalization bounds for classifiers on the near-i.i.d. dataset.  
- Empirical results: Strong performance on synthetic and real data, notably maintaining high AUC as conditioning dimension d_z scales up to 70, outperforming kernel-based methods.  
- Strength — Novelty: The model-powered reduction is elegant and leverages supervised learning advances for CI testing.  
- Strength — Empirical: Convincing state-of-the-art results, especially in high-dimensional Z settings.  
- Strength — Theory: Rigorous convergence guarantee for the bootstrap (Theorem 1) and classifier generalization bounds (Theorem 2).  
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible.  
- Weakness — Modularity/ablation: Lacks ablation to separate contributions of the nearest-neighbor sampler vs. the classifier (e.g., compare sampler with other sampling methods or classifier with other samplers/tests).  
- Weakness — Sensitivity to weak dependence: Concerns that bootstrap approximation error may mask weak conditional dependence when d_TV(f, f_CI) is small.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter τ: Choice τ = 1/√n is used without principled justification or data-driven selection guidance; sensitivity and effect on Type I/II errors are unclear.  
- Weakness — Asymmetry: Bootstrap procedure is asymmetric in X and Y (swapping y only); unclear whether swapping x would be equivalent or affect theory/results.  
- Minor issue: Algorithm pseudocode omits normalization factor 1/|D_r| in empirical risk minimization; text mentions it but formulas should be consistent.  
- Recommendation: Overall strong contribution with solid theory and empirical gains; recommend acceptance, with suggested clarifications and ablation in the final version.
