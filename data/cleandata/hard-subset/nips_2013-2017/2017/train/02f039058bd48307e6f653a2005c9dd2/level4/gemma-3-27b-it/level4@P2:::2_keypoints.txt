- Introduces a novel reduction of conditional independence (CI) testing to a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers.
- Proposes a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the CI distribution f_CI from joint samples.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: bounds on total variation distance between bootstrap samples and f_CI under smoothness assumptions (Theorem 1).
- Develops generalization bounds for the classification task, translating to CI testing error bounds in a near-i.i.d. setting (Theorem 2).
- Analyzes Rademacher complexity in the near-independent regime, strengthening theoretical rigor.
- Empirically validates the method on synthetic and real data, showing performance gains over existing approaches.
- Positions work relative to kernel-based and other non-parametric CI tests, noting computational and methodological differences.
- Highlights novelty in tackling conditional (Z-dependent) permutation via the proposed bootstrap, unlike prior classification-based independence tests.
- Paper is densely written and hard to follow; presentation and intuition for theoretical results need clearer exposition.
- Connection between theoretical assumptions (e.g., smoothness) and practical algorithm performance is under-explored.
- Bias correction in Algorithm 3 lacks sufficient justification; the subtraction term's validity is unclear.
- Experimental section omits important details (e.g., XGBoost hyperparameters, parameter ranges, sensitivity analyses).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with promising empirical results and significant contributions to CI testing.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, limited intuition linking theory to practice, and incomplete experimental details.
- Overall: significant contribution that likely warrants acceptance if authors improve clarity, justify bias correction, and expand experimental details.
