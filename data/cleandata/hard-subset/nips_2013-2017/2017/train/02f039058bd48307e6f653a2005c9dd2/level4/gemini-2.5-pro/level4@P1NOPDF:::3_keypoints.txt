- Proposes a general test for conditional independence between three continuous variables using samples to generate an approximate conditional product distribution.
- Method avoids strong parametric assumptions by synthesizing a second sample set and comparing it to the joint via classification.
- Uses a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural network) trained to distinguish real vs. generated samples; classifier accuracy is the test statistic.
- Compares the proposed approach to existing methods with qualitative discussion and empirical results on synthetic and real datasets.
- Relevant and practical for causal inference tasks where conditional independence (unconfoundedness) is critical.
- Paper is well-written and accessible despite intricate theoretical derivations.
- Strong theoretical results, extending the classifier two-sample test (Lopez-Paz & Oquab, 2016) via nearest-neighbor matching on Z.
- Question about a discrete analog and suggestion that the bootstrap may be powerful when Z is discrete.
- Requests sensitivity analysis to classifier choice and hyperparameters to assess robustness of test conclusions.
- Suggests condensing the redundant "Main Contributions" subsection for brevity.
- Recommends removing or shortening Algorithm 2 (bootstrap) since it is clearly described in text; notes minor typos ("classifier" at line 157, "graphs" at line 280).
