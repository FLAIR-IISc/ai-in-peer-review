- Introduces a novel model-powered CI testing approach by casting CI testing as a binary classification problem.  
- Uses powerful classifiers (gradient-boosted trees, deep nets), so classifier advances can directly improve the CI test.  
- Proposes a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) with theoretical total-variation guarantees.  
- Derives generalization bounds for the classifier-based test and error bounds for the CI test.  
- Provides strong empirical validation on simulated and real datasets, showing performance gains over state-of-the-art methods.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance with a real-world flow cytometry application.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and accessible despite dense technical details.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous, including accuracy guarantees for bootstrap samples and extended generalization bounds for near-independent samples.  
- Clearly positions and differentiates the work relative to prior art (e.g., classifier two-sample tests).  
- Novelty: the combined model-powered CI test, NN-bootstrap, and theoretical analysis are a substantive contribution.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous proofs and extensions of existing bounds strengthen the work.  
- Empirical performance: experiments convincingly show superior performance across datasets.  
- Practical relevance: applicable to causal inference, structure learning, and feature selection without strong parametric assumptions.  
- Algorithmic complexity: the full method (bootstrap + classifier training) may be computationally heavy, especially in high dimensions.  
- Sensitivity: performance may depend on classifier choice and hyperparameters; more sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Redundancy and flow: some repetitive summaries (e.g., Section 1.1) and verbose pseudocode (Algorithms 2 & 3) detract from conciseness.  
- Suggested improvements: investigate bootstrap for discrete Z, perform sensitivity/hyperparameter analyses, streamline repeated sections, condense or remove detailed pseudocode, and fix minor typos.  
- Overall: strong paper with novel theory and empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
