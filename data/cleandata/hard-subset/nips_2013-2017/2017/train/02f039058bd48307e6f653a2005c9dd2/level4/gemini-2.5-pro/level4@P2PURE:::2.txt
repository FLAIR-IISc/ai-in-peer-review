The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

### Strengths
1) The paper makes a novel contribution by identifying that for the test to work, a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is required. The authors propose such an assumption and use it to bound the total variation distance between the distribution under the null hypothesis and the one obtained via bootstrap.

2) This assumption is then leveraged to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1 (iii)).

3) Theorem 1 is a novel, insightful, and non-trivial result. It synthesizes three key components: the aforementioned smoothness assumption, an assumption that the empirical risk `hat R` can be achieved within an error of `eta`, and an assumption on the gap between the errors of the optimal and the trained classifiers.

### Weaknesses
The paper is difficult to read and lacks sufficient interpretation of its theoretical results. Consequently, I am uncertain whether I have understood them correctly.

For example, consider the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii). Assume the alternative hypothesis holds and `r0 = 0.1`. Suppose `G` is a simple function class (e.g., linear functions) that lacks the expressivity to distinguish between the true density `f` and the conditional independence density `f^CI`. In this scenario, could the authors provide an intuitive explanation for how a small classification error can still be achieved (i.e., `hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2` for large n)?
