The paper proposes a novel conditional independence test, which is a significant contribution to the field.

Strengths:

1. A key insight of the paper is the recognition that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for a conditional independence test to be effective. The authors propose such an assumption and derive a bound on the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from the bootstrap method under this assumption. This is a novel and valuable contribution.

2. The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set, as stated in equation (1.1 (iii)).

3. Theorem 1, which combines the smoothness assumption, the assumption that the optimal risk can be achieved with error η, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers, is a novel, insightful, and non-trivial result.

Weaknesses:

The paper is challenging to read, and the authors could provide more interpretation of the results to facilitate understanding. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii). If we assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1, and suppose we have a small class of functions, such as linear functions, that are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI, it is unclear how a small probability of error can be achieved (i.e., R̂ < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n). A more intuitive explanation of this result would be helpful to ensure that the reader fully comprehends the implications of the theorem.
