The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

**Strengths**

1) The paper identifies a crucial requirement for effective conditional independence tests: an assumption regarding the smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose such an assumption and provide a bound on the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from bootstrap under this assumption. This contribution is novel and significant.
2) The paper leverages this smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1(iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which integrates the smoothness assumption, an error bound (η) on the estimated risk (R̂), and a bound on the difference between the optimal and trained classifier errors, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

**Weaknesses**

The paper is challenging to read and lacks sufficient interpretation of the results. This lack of clarity hinders a complete understanding of the technical contributions. For example, consider the inequality in section 1.1(iii). Assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r<sub>0</sub> = 0.1. Suppose G is a small class, such as linear functions, and these functions are insufficiently expressive to distinguish between *f* and *f<sup>CI</sup>* at all. Can the authors provide an intuitive explanation of how a small error probability can be achieved (i.e., R̂ < 0.1 + o<sub>1</sub> < 0.2 for large *n*) in this scenario?
