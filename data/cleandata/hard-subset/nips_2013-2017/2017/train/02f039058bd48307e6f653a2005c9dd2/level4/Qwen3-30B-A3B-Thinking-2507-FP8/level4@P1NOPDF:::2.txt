

The paper introduces a novel conditional independence test.  

**Strengths**  
1) The paper correctly identifies that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for the test to function. The authors propose this assumption and establish a bound on the total variation distance between the null hypothesis density and the bootstrap density under this assumption. This contribution is novel.  
2) The paper leverages this smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1(iii)).  
3) Theorem 1—combining the smoothness assumption, the assumption that the empirical risk \(\hat{R}\) can be achieved with error \(\eta\), and the assumption on the gap between optimal and trained classifier errors—is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.  

**Weaknesses**  
The paper is challenging to follow and lacks sufficient interpretation of key results, leaving me uncertain about their correct understanding. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1(iii) under the alternative hypothesis with \(r_0 = 0.1\). Suppose \(\mathcal{G}\) is a restricted class (e.g., linear functions) that fails to distinguish between \(f\) and \(f^{\text{CI}}\) entirely. How can the bound \(\hat{R} < 0.1 + o(1) < 0.2\) (for sufficiently large \(n\)) hold intuitively? The paper does not clarify how such a small error probability is achieved in this scenario. Without this explanation, the practical significance of the bound remains unclear.
