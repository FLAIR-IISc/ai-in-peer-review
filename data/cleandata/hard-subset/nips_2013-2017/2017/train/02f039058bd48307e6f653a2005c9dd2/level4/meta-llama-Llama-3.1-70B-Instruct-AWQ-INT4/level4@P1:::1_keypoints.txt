- Method decouples into two parts: generating f_CI-like samples and classifier-based testing, and it's unclear if existing alternatives can replace either step (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests).  
- Nearest neighbor bootstrap is close to f_CI in finite samples, but behavior is unclear when x and y are weakly dependent given z.  
- Method is symmetric in x and y, raising issues for causal scenarios where sampling choice (which variable to resample) may matter.  
- The selection and role of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 are not explained and lack guidance.  
- Algorithms 2 and 3 report empirical risk but should divide by sample size to report average loss.  
- Strength: tackles an important problem in conditional independence testing.  
- Strength: presentation is clear and paper is well organized.  
- Strength: provides theoretical bounds on closeness to f_CI (total variation) and on empirical risks under ideal and near-independent settings.  
- Weakness: unclear whether components can be replaced by or combined with existing methods and how that affects performance.  
- Weakness: unclear sensitivity and power when dependence given z is weak.  
- Weakness: symmetry may obscure causal interpretation and sampling strategy.  
- Weakness: unclear hyperparameter selection for τ undermines practical use.  
- Originality: proposes a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap plus classifier approach with accompanying bounds.  
- Significance: potentially widely applicable across fields requiring conditional independence testing.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear theoretical analysis, but practical concerns remain about behavior and parameter choices.
