This paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths:

1) The paper identifies that an assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density should be made for a test to be effective. The authors propose such an assumption and a bound on the total change between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from the bootstrap under this assumption. This is novel.
2) The paper uses this assumption to limit the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which is the smoothness assumption, combines the assumption that the error between the optimal and trained classifier can be achieved with a hat R error eta, and the assumption related to the difference in error between the optimal and trained classifier, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses:

The paper is difficult to read and does not explain the results very well, to the point where I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in section 1.1 iii) and assume r0=0.1. Suppose G is a small class, such as a linear function, and assume that they are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI. Can you intuitively explain how a small probability of error can be achieved (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?
