- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem, enabling use of powerful supervised models (e.g., GBTs, deep nets).
- Introduces a novel smoothness assumption on the conditional density to bound total variation between the null and bootstrap densities.
- Uses that smoothness assumption to bound the optimal classifier’s training error, providing theoretical guarantees.
- Theorem 1 combines smoothness, achievable optimal risk η, and the gap between optimal and trained classifiers into a novel, non-trivial result.
- Paper is challenging to read and lacks intuitive interpretation of results (e.g., why small error is achievable when model class G is limited).
- Requires a clearer explanation of the proposed method and its underlying assumptions.
- Needs more details on the experimental setup and the datasets used.
- Should include more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a deeper discussion of empirical results.
- Argument for acceptance: proposes a novel, significant approach to conditional independence testing.
- Argument for acceptance: provides theoretical performance guarantees for the method.
- Argument for acceptance: empirical evaluation shows the method can be effective.
- Argument against acceptance: poor readability and insufficient interpretation of results.
- Argument against acceptance: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive enough.
- Argument against acceptance: exposition of the method and assumptions is unclear.
- Overall: promising contribution but needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical comparisons.
