- Introduces a model-based conditional independence test using nearest-neighbor bootstrapping to generate f^{CI} samples and a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped data.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on how well the nearest-neighbor bootstrapped distribution approximates f^{CI} (TV distance) and on empirical classification risks.  
- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.  
- Major: Unclear whether the two components (sample generation and classifier) are replaceable by existing methods or whether alternative combinations (e.g., permutation tests + classifier, NN bootstrap + kernel two-sample) would perform similarly—comparative analysis needed.  
- Major: Unclear sensitivity to weak conditional dependence; how reliably the method detects subtle dependence between x and y given z.  
- Major: Symmetry/asymmetry between x and y is not discussed—possible effects of causal direction and which variable is sampled are unaddressed.  
- Major: Choice and justification of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not provided and raises concerns.  
- Minor: Empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to omit normalization by sample size.
