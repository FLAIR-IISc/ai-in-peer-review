- Proposed a general conditional independence test for three continuous variables using generated samples approximating the conditional product distribution and a classifier two-sample test.
- Method avoids strong parametric assumptions; uses classifier predictive error to accept/reject the null of conditional independence.
- Empirical comparison to existing approaches on synthetic and real datasets is provided.
- Relevant and useful for causal inference (assessing unconfoundedness); conceptually straightforward and broadly applicable.
- Paper is overall well-written; theoretical details/proofs are dense but findings and methodology are clearly summarized for practitioners.
- Theoretical results are the strongest contribution, extending classifier two-sample tests to conditioning on Z with neighbor matching and providing strong guarantees.
- Query: does a discrete analogue of the method exist (bootstrap often works better for discrete Z)?
- Query: how sensitive are results to choice of classifier and hyperparameters; request analysis of hyperparameter impact on test decisions.
- Presentation critique: Section 1.1 is redundant and repetitive.
- Presentation critique: Algorithm 2 is unnecessary in full and could be more concise.
- Minor typographical corrections: “classifier” (line 157) and “graphs” (line 280).
