- Authors identify a necessary smoothness assumption on the conditional density and derive a novel bound on the total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density.

- They use this smoothness assumption to derive a bound on the error of the optimal classifier.

- Theorem 1 combines the smoothness assumption with bounds on the trained classifier’s error and the gap to the optimal error to yield a novel, non-trivial result.

- Primary weakness: the paper’s presentation is dense and difficult to follow, offering limited intuition or interpretation of key results.

- Reviewer requests clarification/intuition for the inequality in Section 1.1(iii), specifically how a trained classifier can achieve low error when the function class G is not expressive enough (e.g., linear G) despite the alternative holding (example: r0 = 0.1).
