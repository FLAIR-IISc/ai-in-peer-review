- Proposes a general conditional independence test for continuous X, Y, Z that avoids strong parametric assumptions.  
- Constructs synthetic samples approximating P(X|Z)P(Y|Z)P(Z) in total variation using samples from the joint distribution.  
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep net) to distinguish joint vs. synthetic samples and uses classifier error to accept/reject conditional independence.  
- Compares the method conceptually and empirically against existing CI tests on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Practical relevance emphasized for causal inference and unconfoundedness checks.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful summaries, though some proofs are dense.  
- Theoretical contributions are the most impressive aspect, providing strong guarantees.  
- Method is a natural extension of classifier two-sample tests, adapted to the conditional setting via Z-matching (e.g., nearest neighbors).  
- Reviewer asks about a discrete-Z analogue and suggests discussing or testing that setting.  
- Requests sensitivity analysis for classifier choice and hyperparameters to assess robustness of accept/reject decisions.  
- Notes redundancy in Section 1.1 (Main Contributions) and suggests consolidating repeated summaries.  
- Suggests Algorithm 2 could be shortened or merged with earlier descriptions.  
- Points out minor typos: “classifier” (line 157) and “graphs” (line 280).
