This paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths:

1) The paper identifies that an assumption about the smoothness of the conditional density should be made for a test to be effective. The authors propose a limit on the total change in density under such an assumption and the null hypothesis, and on the density obtained from the bootstrap under this assumption. This is novel.
2) The paper uses this assumption to limit the error of the best classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which is the smoothness assumption, combines this assumption with the assumption that the error between the best and trained classifier can be achieved with a hat and error eta, and the assumption related to the difference in error between the best and trained classifier is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses:

The paper is difficult to read and the results are not very well explained, to the extent that I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider inequality in section 1.1 iii) and assume r0=0.1. Suppose g is a small class, such as a linear function, and assume they are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI. Can you intuitively explain how a small probability of error can be achieved (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?
