- Novel contribution: introduces a classifier-based two-sample CI test using NN bootstrap to approximate the null and provides theoretical bounds.  
- Modularity/novelty unclear: need ablation comparing other sampling methods (e.g., permutation) and other two-sample tests (e.g., kernel tests) to isolate benefits of NN bootstrap vs classifier.  
- Power concerns: NN bootstrap may closely match the true distribution under weak dependencies, causing low power/false negatives; requires discussion and empirical study across dependency strengths.  
- Asymmetry issue: test resamples one variable (e.g., Y), so swapping X and Y may change results—unclear if the test is symmetric or how this affects validity and causal interpretation.  
- Practicality/hyperparameter selection: no guidance on choosing decision threshold τ or its sensitivity; need principled/data-driven selection (e.g., cross-validation) for reproducibility.  
- Minor bug: pseudocode for Algorithms 2 and 3 appears to omit normalization (division by sample size) in empirical risk calculation—clarify if typo or intentional.
