- Introduces a general non-parametric test for conditional independence among three continuous variables using constructed conditional product samples and a classifier-based two-sample approach.
- Constructs an approximate conditional product distribution via nearest-neighbor matching and proves closeness in total variation distance.
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural network) to distinguish joint vs constructed samples; classification error serves as the test statistic.
- Empirically and qualitatively validated against existing methods on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Method is simple, elegant, practically useful for causal inference, and the paper is clearly written despite dense proofs.
- Main theoretical contributions provide novel guarantees extending classifier-based two-sample tests to the conditional setting.
- Suggest extending the framework to handle discrete variables, potentially using bootstrap when Z is discrete.
- Recommend analyzing robustness to the choice of binary classifier and its hyperparameters (sensitivity analysis).
