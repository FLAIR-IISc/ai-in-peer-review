- Introduces a novel reduction of conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f_CI samples.  
- The nearest-neighbor bootstrap is shown to converge to f_CI in total variation distance under smoothness assumptions (Theorem 1).  
- Provides new generalization bounds for classifiers trained on non-i.i.d. bootstrapped samples (Theorem 2).  
- Leverages powerful classifiers (gradient-boosted trees, deep nets) to achieve state-of-the-art CI testing performance in high-dimensional settings.  
- Builds on classifier-based independence testing and contrasts with kernel-based methods by offering a simpler, model-driven approach.  
- Offers theoretical guarantees on the distance between generated samples and f_CI, unlike prior work that relied on kernel two-sample tests.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: meaningful TV distance bounds and novel non-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  
- Empirical validation shows clear performance gains on synthetic and flow-cytometry data, scaling to high-dimensional Z.  
- Originality: nearest-neighbor bootstrap + classification reduction is a novel, well-motivated idea that simplifies CI testing.  
- Significance: relevant to causal inference, Bayesian network learning, and feature selection; enables use of modern supervised learning tools.  
- Clarity issue: paper is technically dense and lacks intuitive explanation of how classifier error rates map to CI decisions.  
- Clarity issue: smoothness assumptions need more explanation and concrete examples of distributions that satisfy them.  
- Experimental gap: paper notes better empirical scaling than worst-case theory but does not analyze why this occurs.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel approach, strong theory, empirical SOTA, and valuable non-i.i.d. generalization results.  
- Cons / revisions needed: improve readability, justify smoothness assumptions, provide intuition for theoretical bounds, and explain theory–empirics discrepancy.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide illustrative examples and intuitive explanations.
