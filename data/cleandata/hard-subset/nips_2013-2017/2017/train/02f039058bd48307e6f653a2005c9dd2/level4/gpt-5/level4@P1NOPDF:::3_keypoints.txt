- Proposes a general conditional independence test for continuous X, Y, Z that avoids strong parametric assumptions.
- Constructs proxy samples approximating P(X|Z)P(Y|Z)P(Z) in total variation using joint samples and neighbor-matching.
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep net) to distinguish joint samples from proxy samples; classifier error is the test statistic for H0: X ⟂ Y | Z.
- Empirically and conceptually compares the method to existing CI tests on synthetic and real datasets.
- Method is practically useful for causal inference and unconfoundedness testing.
- Paper is generally well written and accessible to practitioners despite some dense theoretical proofs.
- Theoretical contributions are the strongest aspect, providing compelling guarantees and extending classifier two-sample tests to include Z.
- Suggest discussing a discrete-version of the method when Z is discrete, where resampling/bootstrap could be more powerful.
- Recommend reporting sensitivity analyses on classifier choice and hyperparameters to assess stability of accept/reject decisions.
- Minor editorial suggestions: tighten redundant text in Section 1.1, simplify or omit full presentation of Algorithm 2, and fix typos (“classifier” line 157, “graphs” line 280).
