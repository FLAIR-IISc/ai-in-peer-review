- Proposes a novel conditional independence test using nearest-neighbor bootstrap (NNB) to generate f_CI-like samples and a classifier to distinguish observed vs. generated data.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: total variation bounds between NNB distribution and f_CI, plus empirical risk bounds under ideal classification and near-independent sampling.  
- Major concern: No justification for the specific NNB+classifier combination versus alternatives (e.g., permutation-based references or kernel two-sample tests); needs empirical/theoretical comparisons on power, Type I error, and computational cost.  
- Major concern: Lacks analysis/simulations for weak (subtle) conditional dependence; NNB may poorly approximate f_CI in such cases, risking high Type II error.  
- Major concern: Test is symmetric in X and Y but paper discusses causal directions; authors must clarify whether the method is for statistical dependence or causal discovery and address directionality if claiming causal inference.  
- Major concern: Critical neighborhood parameter τ is unexplained and likely data-dependent; paper needs guidance on selecting τ (e.g., cross-validation/theory) and sensitivity analysis showing its impact on Type I/II errors.  
- Minor: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be defined as average loss (divide by n), not total loss.  
- Minor: Inconsistent notation—use f_{CI} (consistent subscripts) throughout.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject in current form; substantial revisions required to justify the method over alternatives, analyze weak dependence, clarify causal/statistical scope, provide τ selection guidance, and fix minor technical/notation issues.
