- Central contribution: proposes a CI test using a smoothness assumption on conditional densities plus a bootstrap scheme and gives finite-sample guarantees via a classification-based analysis.  
- Main technical novelty: proves a total variation (TV) bound between the null density and the bootstrap density under the smoothness condition.  
- Methodological insight: leverages the TV bound to relate empirical classifier performance to the ideal null-target classifier and derive Theorem 1.  
- Technical strength: Theorem 1 integrates smoothness, bootstrap validity, and learning-theoretic error terms in an original, non-trivial way.  
- Clarity concern: paper is hard to read and lacks plain-language explanations of key terms and how components fit together.  
- Ambiguity about r0/η: unclear whether r0 is unconstrained Bayes risk or Bayes risk within hypothesis class G, and how η accounts for approximation error.  
- Potential inconsistency: if G is too small (large approximation error), the test may have no power even when r0 (unconstrained) is small; this dependence must be explicit.  
- Assumption verifiability: the smoothness condition is abstract—authors should specify standard regularity classes (e.g., Hölder/Lipschitz) and give TV rates in terms of n, smoothness order, constant, and dimension.  
- Rates and scaling: need explicit statements for how η depends on n and G’s capacity (VC dimension, Rademacher complexity, etc.) and when smoothness rates dominate learning errors.  
- Theorem structure: current theorem conflates bootstrap validity, approximation bias, and estimation variance; recommend modular lemmas separating (i) TV control, (ii) approximation error within G, and (iii) generalization bounds.  
- Practical guidance lacking: no actionable advice on choosing G, calibrating the bootstrap, or diagnosing when assumptions hold.  
- Requests for clarification: precisely define r0, hat R, and η (population vs empirical, within G vs unconstrained) in Section 1.1(iii).  
- Small-G example: explicitly explain whether the bound permits hat R < 0.2 with high probability in that scenario and whether η must absorb approximation error.  
- Bootstrap intuition: provide a short sketch or canonical example showing which smoothness properties ensure bootstrap samples approximate the null in TV.  
- Presentation suggestions: add a high-level roadmap of the test steps and consolidate/rest assumptions before Theorem 1 with one-line intuitions.  
- Discuss G trade-offs: include discussion on how G’s capacity affects power, overfitting, η, and the test threshold.  
- Empirical illustration: add a simple synthetic experiment showing the effect of G’s expressiveness on power to validate theoretical claims.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel (smoothness-based TV control + classification reduction) but needs clearer statements, explicit rates, and better discussion of approximation capacity and practical guidance.
