- Introduces a general conditional independence test for three continuous variables that avoids strong parametric assumptions.  
- Generates samples approximating the conditional product distribution in total variation distance using joint-distribution samples.  
- Feeds these generated samples and original samples into a binary classifier (e.g., deep network); classifier error decides acceptance/rejection of conditional independence.  
- Method is compared rigorously to existing literature with theoretical analysis and empirical results on synthetic and real data.  
- Method is straightforward, powerful, and broadly applicable to causal inference problems relying on unconfoundedness.  
- Paper is well-written with technically dense proofs summarized clearly for practitioners.  
- Main theoretical contribution extends classifier two-sample tests to include a third variable Z via neighbor matching, with strong theoretical guarantees.
