- Proposes nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating f^{CI}, then trains a classifier to distinguish observed joint distribution from bootstrapped; test rejects if classifier performs better than random.

- Provides bounds on total variation distance between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risk in idealized and nearly-independent settings.

- Paper addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Can the two components be replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approaches or pairing NN-bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests like MMD)? Empirical comparisons of such hybrids on power and Type I error are requested.

- Sensitivity to near-conditional-independence: unclear how the test behaves for weak conditional dependence and whether bootstrap approximation error will mask small departures from CI; need power analysis in this regime.

- Symmetry concern: unclear if the method is invariant to swapping X and Y or if the choice of which variable to resample/condition on affects performance; paper should clarify and give guidance.

- Choice of τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not addressed: need practical/theoretical guidance or a data-driven selection rule since τ may strongly affect validity and power.

- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be reported as an average loss (divide by sample size), not left unnormalized.
