- Introduces a novel model-based CI test by reframing CI testing as a two-sample problem using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate null samples.  
- Uses a classifier to distinguish observed vs. bootstrapped samples; chance-level performance implies failure to reject the null.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation between the bootstrap and true CI distribution and on empirical classification risks.  
- Method is modular (sampling + two-sample test); needs discussion/comparison of interchangeability (e.g., permutation-based sampling or kernel two-sample tests).  
- Unclear sensitivity/power for weak but non-zero conditional dependence; requires analysis or empirical evaluation of detection ability.  
- Potential asymmetry in resampling X vs. Y: unclear if choice affects test outcome; authors should clarify and provide guidance.  
- Practical issue: selection of hyperparameter τ (classification risk threshold) is critical but lacks principled, data-driven selection guidance.  
- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 appears unnormalized and should likely be divided by sample size — please clarify.
