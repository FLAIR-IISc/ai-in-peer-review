This paper introduces a novel conditional independence test.

**Strengths:**

1) A key contribution of this work is the recognition that effective conditional independence tests require an assumption regarding the smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose a specific smoothness assumption and provide a bound, in total variation distance, between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained via bootstrap under this assumption. This is a significant and original contribution.
2) The proposed smoothness assumption is leveraged to establish a bound on the error of the optimal classifier when trained on the given dataset (specifically, result 1.1(iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which elegantly integrates the smoothness assumption, an error bound (eta) on the estimated risk (hat R), and the discrepancy between optimal and trained classifier errors, is a novel, insightful, and non-trivial result.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper’s presentation is dense and lacks sufficient interpretative discussion, hindering comprehension. I am uncertain if I fully grasp the implications of the results. For example, considering the inequality in section 1.1(iii), if the alternative hypothesis is true and r0 = 0.1, and G represents a limited class of functions (e.g., linear functions) incapable of distinguishing between f and f<sup>CI</sup>, it is unclear how a small error probability (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n) can be achieved. An intuitive explanation of this scenario would be beneficial.
