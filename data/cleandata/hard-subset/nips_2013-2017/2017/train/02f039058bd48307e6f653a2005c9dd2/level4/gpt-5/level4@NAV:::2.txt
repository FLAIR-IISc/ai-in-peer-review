Review- The paper introduces a new conditional independence test.
Strengths
1) The paper observes that, for a test to be valid, one must assume smoothness of the conditional density. Under this assumption, the authors bound the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the bootstrap density. This appears novel.
2) The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which combines the smoothness assumption, the assumption that hat R can be achieved with error eta, and the assumption on the gap between the optimal and trained classifiers’ errors, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.
Weaknesses
The paper is difficult to read and provides little interpretation of the results, to the point that I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in section 1.1 (iii): assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1. Suppose G is a small class, e.g., linear functions, and suppose it is not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI at all. Can you explain, intuitively, how a small error probability can be achieved (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?
