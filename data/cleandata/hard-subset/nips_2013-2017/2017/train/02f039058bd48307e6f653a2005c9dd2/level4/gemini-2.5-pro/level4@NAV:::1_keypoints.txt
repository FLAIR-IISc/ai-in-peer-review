- Proposes a model-based conditional independence test using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to synthesize f^{CI} and a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped data.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation distance between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risk under idealized assumptions.  
- Addresses an important problem and the manuscript is well written and clear.  
- Major point: the method is modular (bootstrap + classifier); authors should compare alternative combinations (e.g., permutation-based generation with their classifier, or NN bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests).  
- Major point: unclear statistical power for weak conditional dependence since the true distribution and f^{CI} may be very close and the bootstrap is only an approximation.  
- Major point: potential asymmetry between X and Y — paper does not discuss whether resampling choice affects outcomes or if the test is invariant to swapping X and Y.  
- Major point: no guidance on selecting the hyperparameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3; sensitivity analysis and practical selection strategy are needed.  
- Minor point: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 appears summed rather than averaged and should likely be normalized by sample size.
