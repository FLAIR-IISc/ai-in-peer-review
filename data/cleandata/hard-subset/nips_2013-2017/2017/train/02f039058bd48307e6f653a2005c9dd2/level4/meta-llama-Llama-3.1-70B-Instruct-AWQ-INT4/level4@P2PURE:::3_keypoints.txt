- Proposes a general nonparametric conditional independence test that generates samples approximating the conditional product distribution and uses a binary classifier's error to accept/reject the null.  
- Method feeds joint and constructed conditional-product samples into a classifier (e.g., deep neural net) and uses predictive error as the test statistic.  
- Empirically compared comprehensively to existing methods with experiments on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Useful and straightforward approach with clear applicability to causal inference and unconfoundedness testing.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible, though some theoretical proofs are challenging to follow.  
- Theoretical guarantees are impressive; the method extends classifier two-sample tests by incorporating a third variable Z and neighbor matching.  
- Query whether a discrete-variable analog exists and note bootstrap may be more powerful when Z is discrete.  
- Suggest evaluating sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters, as small changes might affect test outcomes.  
- Recommend removing redundant summaries (Section 1.1) and omitting the full verbatim listing of Algorithm 2 for brevity.  
- Minor typos to fix (e.g., "classifier" line 157 and "graphs" line 280).
