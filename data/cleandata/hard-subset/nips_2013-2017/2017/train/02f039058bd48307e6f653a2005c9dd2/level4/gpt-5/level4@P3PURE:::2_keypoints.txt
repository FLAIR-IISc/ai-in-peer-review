- Summary: Proposes a new conditional independence test using a smoothness assumption on conditional densities and provides theoretical guarantees via total variation bounds and a key combined theorem.

- Strength 1: Novel identification of a smoothness assumption and a bound on the total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density.

- Strength 2: Uses the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set.

- Strength 3: Theorem 1, which synthesizes the smoothness assumption, an achievable error η for hat R, and an assumed gap between optimal and trained classifier errors, is novel and non-trivial.

- Weakness 1: Paper is difficult to read and offers limited interpretation of results, leaving parts unclear to the reviewer.

- Weakness 2 / clarification request: Needs an intuitive explanation of the inequality in Section 1.1(iii), specifically how hat R can be < 0.1 + o(1) when G is a small hypothesis class that cannot separate f from f^CI.

- Overall assessment 1: The theoretical contributions (total variation bound and Theorem 1 synthesis) are novel and technically interesting.

- Overall assessment 2: Exposition must be substantially improved with more intuition, illustrative examples, and clearer explanations of the roles/interactions of assumptions, and the paper should address the scenario where G is too limited to separate f from f^CI.
