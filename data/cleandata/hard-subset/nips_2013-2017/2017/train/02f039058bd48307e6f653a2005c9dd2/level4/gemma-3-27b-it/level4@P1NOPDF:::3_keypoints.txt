- Proposes a general test for conditional independence of three continuous variables using generated samples approximating the conditional product distribution.  
- Generated samples are compared via a binary classifier (e.g., deep network); classifier error decides acceptance/rejection of conditional independence.  
- Avoids strong parametric assumptions and leverages joint-distribution samples.  
- Experimental comparison to existing techniques with results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Relevant and useful for causal inference tasks requiring conditional independence checks (e.g., unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and explains methodology clearly for practitioners despite some dense theoretical proofs.  
- Theoretical results and guarantees are particularly impressive and noteworthy.  
- Method is a natural extension of classifier two-sample tests, modified to incorporate a third variable Z via neighbor-matching.  
- Questions/suggestions: is there a discrete analog of the test (bootstrap may be more powerful when Z is discrete)?  
- Suggestion to analyze sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters (sensitivity analysis recommended).  
- Stylistic/organizational suggestions: Section 1.1 is redundant and too long; presenting Algorithm 2 in full seems unnecessary.  
- Minor typos: replace “classifier” at line 157 and “graphs” at line 280.
