The paper introduces a novel conditional independence test.

**Strengths:**

1) A key contribution of this work is the recognition that effective conditional independence tests require assumptions regarding the smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose a specific smoothness assumption and provide a bound, in total variation distance, between the density under the null hypothesis and the density estimated via bootstrap under this assumption. This is a genuinely new and valuable result.

2) The proposed smoothness assumption is leveraged to derive a bound on the error rate of the optimal classifier trained on the training data (as presented in 1.1(iii)).

3) Theorem 1, which integrates the smoothness assumption, an error bound (eta) for the estimator hat R, and a bound on the discrepancy between the optimal and trained classifier errors, represents a novel, insightful, and non-trivial theoretical development. 

**Weaknesses:**

The paper’s presentation is dense and lacks sufficient interpretive discussion, leading to potential difficulties in fully grasping the results. For instance, considering the inequality in section 1.1(iii), if the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1, and G represents a limited class of functions (e.g., linear functions) incapable of differentiating between f and f^CI, it is unclear how a small error probability can be achieved (i.e., how hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n). A more intuitive explanation of this scenario would be beneficial.
