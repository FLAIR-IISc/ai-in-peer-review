- Method: Frame CI testing as a two-sample test using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate null samples and a classifier to distinguish observed vs. bootstrapped data (random guess ⇒ do not reject).

- Theory: Provide finite-sample bounds on total variation between bootstrap and true null f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal and near-independent cases.

- Modularity concern: The approach has two interchangeable components (bootstrap sample generation and classifier test); paper should discuss/compare using other generators (e.g., permutation-based) or other two-sample tests (e.g., kernel methods).

- Power issue: Unclear how the test performs against weak conditional dependence when the true distribution is close to f^{CI}; needs analysis of statistical power in such regimes.

- Symmetry concern: Conditional independence is symmetric in X and Y, but the algorithm’s treatment may not be; authors should clarify whether the method is symmetric or sensitive to causal direction.

- Hyperparameter issue: No guidance on selecting τ in Algorithms 2 and 3, which is critical for performance and reproducibility.

- Minor bug: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 appears to lack normalization by sample size (missing division).
