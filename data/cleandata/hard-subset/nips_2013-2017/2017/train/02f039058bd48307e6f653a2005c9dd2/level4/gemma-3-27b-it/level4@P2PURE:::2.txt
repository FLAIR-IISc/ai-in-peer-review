The paper introduces a new conditional independence test.

**Strengths**

1) A key contribution of this work is the recognition that any effective conditional independence test requires an assumption regarding the smoothness of the conditional density. The authors propose such an assumption and provide a bound on the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from bootstrap under this assumption; this is a novel and valuable result.
2) The proposed assumption is leveraged to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set, as demonstrated in section 1.1(iii).
3) Theorem 1, which integrates the smoothness assumption, an error bound (η) on the estimated risk (hat R), and an assumption regarding the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

**Weaknesses**

The paper’s presentation is dense and lacks sufficient interpretation of the results, leading to some difficulty in fully understanding the technical details. For example, considering the inequality in section 1.1(iii), suppose the alternative hypothesis holds and r<sub>0</sub> = 0.1. If G represents a small, relatively unexpressive class of functions – such as linear functions – incapable of distinguishing between f and f<sup>CI</sup>, can the authors provide an intuitive explanation for how a small error probability (hat R < 0.1 + o<sub>1</sub> < 0.2 for large n) can be achieved?
