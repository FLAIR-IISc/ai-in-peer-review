- Overall: Addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Major 1: Method has two components (nearest-neighbor sample generation + classifier test); reviewer asks if each can be replaced by existing alternatives (e.g., permutation-based sample generation from [7] or using kernel two-sample tests) and wonders about hybrid performance.

- Major 2: Although bootstrap approximates f_CI in finite samples, when X and Y are only weakly conditionally dependent the classifier may not distinguish distributions, risking false negatives.

- Major 3: Question of symmetry: does bootstrapping X vs. Y matter? Different causal structures might make the test asymmetric, but the paper does not discuss this.

- Major 4: Practical selection of threshold parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is unclear and under-specified, posing a hurdle for practitioners.

- Minor: In Algorithms 2 and 3 the empirical risk calculation appears to be missing a division by the sample size.
