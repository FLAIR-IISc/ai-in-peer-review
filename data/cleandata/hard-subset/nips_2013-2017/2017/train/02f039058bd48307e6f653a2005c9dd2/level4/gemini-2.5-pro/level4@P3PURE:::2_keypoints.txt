- Identifies that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary and uses it to bound the total variation distance between the null and bootstrap distributions—novel theoretical contribution.  
- Leverages the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1 (iii)).  
- Theorem 1 is novel and non-trivial, combining the smoothness assumption with assumptions on empirical error achievability and the generalization gap.  
- Paper is difficult to read and lacks sufficient interpretation of the results.  
- Reviewer requests an intuitive explanation for a potential contradiction: if the alternative holds with optimal error r0=0.1 but classifier class G is too simple to distinguish f from f^CI, how can a small empirical error (hat R < 0.2) be achieved despite G’s limited expressiveness?
