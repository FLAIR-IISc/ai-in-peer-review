- Proposes a model-based conditional independence test using a nearest neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Trains/evaluates the classifier: near-random performance means do not reject the null of conditional independence; otherwise reject.  
- Provides finite-sample total variation bounds on how close the nearest neighbor bootstrap distribution is to f^{CI}.  
- Derives bounds on empirical risks for ideal classification and for nearly independent samples.  
- Addresses a significant problem and is clearly written.  
- Major concern: either step (sample generation or classification) might be replaceable by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests); performance of such alternatives is unclear.  
- Major concern: unclear behavior when x and y are weakly dependent given z — how sensitive is the method to weak dependence?  
- Major concern: potential asymmetry in x and y — causal direction may matter and the paper does not address whether one must choose which variable to sample.  
- Major concern: serious doubts about how parameter τ is chosen in Algorithms 2 and 3.  
- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be divided by the sample size.
