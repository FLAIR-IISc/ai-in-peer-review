The paper presents a new conditional independence test.

Strengths
1) The paper recognizes that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for the test to succeed. Under this assumption, the authors derive a total variation bound between the null distribution and the bootstrap-induced distribution, which appears novel.
2) The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training data (Section 1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1—combining the smoothness assumption, the assumption that ĥR can be realized within an error η, and the assumption controlling the gap between the optimal and learned classifiers—is original, insightful, and technically nontrivial.

Weaknesses
The paper is difficult to follow and provides limited interpretation of the results; to the point that I am uncertain I have understood them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii): assume the alternative holds and r0 = 0.1. Suppose G is a restricted hypothesis class (e.g., linear functions) that is not expressive enough to distinguish f from f^CI. Can you intuitively explain how a small error probability is still achieved (i.e., ĥR < 0.1 + o(1) < 0.2 for large n)?
