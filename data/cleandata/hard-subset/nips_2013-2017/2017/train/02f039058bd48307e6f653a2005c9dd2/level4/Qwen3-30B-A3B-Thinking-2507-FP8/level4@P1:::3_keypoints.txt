- Proposes converting conditional independence testing for continuous variables into a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Generates labeled data by combining bootstrap samples (approximate f_CI) with original joint samples and trains classifiers (e.g., GBTs, DNNs) to distinguish them.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the bootstrap samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance.  
- Establishes novel generalization bounds for classification under near-independent, non-i.i.d. samples.  
- Empirical results show significant performance gains over state-of-the-art CI tests on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Method scales well with the dimensionality of Z, maintaining accuracy where other methods degrade.  
- Demonstrates practical utility on protein signaling networks from flow cytometry data.  
- Clear connection and extension of prior classification-based independence testing literature.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners.  
- Some redundancy in Section 1.1 and Algorithm 2’s description could be trimmed.  
- Does not explore a discrete-variable analogue, which could be simpler and informative.  
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings.  
- Minor typographical errors noted (e.g., "classifer" and "graph"/"graphs").  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically grounded, empirically validated—recommend acceptance.
