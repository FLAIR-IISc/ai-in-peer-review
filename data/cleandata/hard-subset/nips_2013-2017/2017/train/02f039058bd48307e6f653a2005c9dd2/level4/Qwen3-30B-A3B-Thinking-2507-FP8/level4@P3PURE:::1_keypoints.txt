- Proposes CI test: nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} then train a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrap samples; reject null if classifier does better than chance.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation between the NN bootstrap distribution and f^{CI}, and on empirical risks for nearly independent samples.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly presented, addressing an important problem.  
- Decoupling/alternatives: method splits into (i) sample-generation and (ii) classifier; unclear if either can be replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approach [7] with classifier or NN bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests); needs empirical comparisons.  
- Weak dependence: no analysis or discussion of behavior when X and Y are only weakly dependent given Z (near-null cases).  
- Symmetry/causal direction: test is asymmetric in X and Y and gives no guidance on which variable to sample, problematic for causal inference where direction matters.  
- Parameter selection (τ): choice of τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is crucial but unjustified; no practical guidance or analysis of its effect.  
- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be divided by sample size n to match standard risk definitions.
