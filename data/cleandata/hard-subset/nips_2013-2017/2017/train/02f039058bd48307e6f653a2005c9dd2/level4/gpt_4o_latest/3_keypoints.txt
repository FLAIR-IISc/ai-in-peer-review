- Introduces a general, nonparametric framework for testing conditional independence among three continuous variables.  
- Generates samples approximating the conditional product distribution (in total variation) from joint samples, avoiding strong parametric assumptions.  
- Uses a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural network) trained on generated vs. joint samples to decide conditional independence via predictive error.  
- Benchmarks method against existing approaches with descriptive comparisons and experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Method is conceptually simple, versatile, and potentially valuable for causal inference (e.g., assessing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners, though some theoretical proofs are dense.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong, extending classifier two-sample tests to conditional settings by adding a third variable Z and neighbor-matching.  
- Provides impressive theoretical guarantees despite the simplicity of the extension.  
- Asks whether a discrete counterpart exists and suggests bootstrap might be more effective when Z is discrete.  
- Raises concern about robustness to choice of classification model and hyperparameters; recommends sensitivity analysis.  
- Notes redundancy: Section 1.1 over-summarizes and Algorithm 2 could be omitted or shortened.  
- Points out minor typos: "classifer" → "classifier" (line 157) and "graps" → "graphs" (line 280).
