- Proposes reducing nonparametric conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem using supervised models (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets).  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from the conditional product distribution and provides theoretical guarantees for the bootstrap.  
- Provides risk generalization bounds for classification under non-i.i.d near-independent samples.  
- Introduces a novel smoothness assumption on the conditional density and bounds total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density.  
- Uses the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set.  
- Theorem 1 combines smoothness, achievable optimal risk η, and the error gap between optimal and trained classifiers; deemed novel and non-trivial.  
- Paper is difficult to read and lacks intuitive interpretation of key inequalities (e.g., Section 1.1(iii) and the r0 = 0.1 scenario).  
- Lacks clarity and illustrative examples to help readers understand the results and their implications.  
- Suggests adding more explanations and concrete examples to illustrate key concepts and results.  
- Suggests improving writing clarity and defining technical terms more precisely.  
- Suggests discussing practical implications and how theoretical guarantees apply in real-world settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model-powered approach, theoretical guarantees for bootstrap and generalization, potential to advance CI testing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor readability, insufficient interpretation and examples, limited discussion of practical applicability.
