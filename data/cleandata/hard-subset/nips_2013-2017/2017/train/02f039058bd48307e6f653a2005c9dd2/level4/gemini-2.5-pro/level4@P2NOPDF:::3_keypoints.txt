- Proposes a general non-parametric conditional independence test for continuous X, Y, Z by reframing the problem as a two-sample test.  
- Constructs samples from the null conditional product P(X|Z)P(Y|Z)P(Z) using nearest-neighbor matching on Z with guarantees of closeness in total variation.  
- Uses a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural network) to distinguish joint vs constructed-null samples; classifier accuracy is the test statistic.  
- Validated through comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, compared to existing methods.  
- Emphasizes practical relevance for causal inference and unconfoundedness checks; method is simple and accessible.  
- Primary strength is theoretical contribution: extension of classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting with strong guarantees.  
- Asks about extending the framework to discrete or mixed-type variables; notes nearest-neighbor matching would be exact for discrete Z.
