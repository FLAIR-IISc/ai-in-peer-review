- Proposes CI test using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and a classifier to distinguish observed vs bootstrap samples; reject if classifier outperforms chance.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation between the NN-bootstrap distribution and f^{CI}, and on empirical risks for ideal classifiers and nearly independent samples.  
- Paper tackles an important problem and is clearly written.  
- Major: Method decomposes into bootstrap sample generation and classification—unclear if either component can be replaced by established alternatives (e.g., permutation methods or kernel two-sample tests) and what the performance trade-offs are.  
- Major: No analysis of performance when X and Y are weakly dependent given Z.  
- Major: Method is asymmetric in X and Y; no guidance on which variable to sample or how to handle causal asymmetry.  
- Major: Parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is unjustified and lacks practical selection guidance.  
- Minor: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be divided by the sample size (typo: "devided").
