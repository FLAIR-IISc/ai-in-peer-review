The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths:

1. The authors recognize that a key assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density is necessary for a test to be effective. They introduce such an assumption and establish a bound on the total variation distance between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from bootstrap under this assumption. This contribution is novel and noteworthy.

2. The paper leverages this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)), demonstrating the practical implications of their theoretical framework.

3. Theorem 1 is a significant achievement, as it combines the assumption on smoothness, the assumption that the error rate can be achieved with error η, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers. This result is novel, insightful, and non-trivial, showcasing the authors' technical expertise.

Weaknesses:

The paper is challenging to read and lacks interpretive guidance for the results, to the extent that I am uncertain whether I have fully grasped the implications. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii). Suppose the alternative hypothesis holds and r0 = 0.1. If G is a small class, such as linear functions, and these functions are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI at all, it is unclear how a small probability of error can be achieved (i.e., hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n). A more intuitive explanation of this result would be beneficial to facilitate understanding.
