- Proposes a CI test using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^CI and a classifier to distinguish real vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation closeness of the bootstrap to f^CI and bounds on empirical risks for ideal and near-independent samples.  
- Modularity concern: sampling and classification components may be replaceable by existing methods; no comparative analysis with alternatives (e.g., permutation-based tests or kernel two-sample tests).  
- Detection concern: unclear reliability for detecting weak conditional dependencies—risk of false negatives due to bootstrapping noise.  
- Symmetry/causal concern: method’s treatment of causal direction (x→y vs y→x) is unclear and may favor one direction without guidance.  
- Parameter sensitivity: selection and sensitivity of τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not justified; no practical guidance or principled selection method.  
- Minor issue: empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to omit normalization by sample size.  
- Overall: interesting, theoretically grounded approach but substantial weaknesses; reviewer leans to a weak reject and requests major revisions.
