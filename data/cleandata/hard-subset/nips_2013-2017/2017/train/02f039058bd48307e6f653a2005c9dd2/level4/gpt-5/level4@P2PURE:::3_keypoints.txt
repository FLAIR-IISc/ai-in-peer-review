- Proposes a general-purpose conditional independence (CI) test for three continuous variables using surrogate samples close in total variation and a classifier to distinguish joint vs proxy, with classifier error driving accept/reject decisions.  
- Method avoids strong parametric assumptions and is straightforward and potentially powerful for causal inference (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is generally well written; dense theoretical details are effectively summarized for practitioners.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong; method is a natural extension of classifier two-sample tests by incorporating Z via neighbor matching, as acknowledged.  
- Asks whether a discrete analogue exists and whether bootstrap would be more powerful when Z is discrete.  
- Requests assessment of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters, since modest changes might affect accept/reject outcomes.  
- Notes redundancy in Section 1.1 (Main Contributions) due to repeated summaries.  
- Suggests Listing Algorithm 2 is unnecessary and could be conveyed more succinctly.  
- Points out minor typos: “classifier” (line 157) and “graphs” (line 280).
