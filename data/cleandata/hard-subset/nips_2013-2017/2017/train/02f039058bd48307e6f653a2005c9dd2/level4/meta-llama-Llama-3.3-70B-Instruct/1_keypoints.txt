- Proposes a CI test that uses nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI} and trains a classifier to distinguish bootstrapped vs. observed samples, rejecting the null if classifier outperforms random guessing.

- Provides bounds on total variation distance between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal classifiers and near-independent samples.

- Tackles an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Major: Method splits into two components (sample generation and classification); authors should explore substituting either with existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approach [7] or kernel two-sample tests) for comparison.

- Major: Unclear how the method distinguishes weak conditional dependence in finite samples when x and y are only weakly dependent given z.

- Major: Symmetry between x and y is questionable—causal direction matters and the choice of which variable to sample is not addressed.

- Major: Choice and tuning of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is a significant concern.

- Minor: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by the sample size.
