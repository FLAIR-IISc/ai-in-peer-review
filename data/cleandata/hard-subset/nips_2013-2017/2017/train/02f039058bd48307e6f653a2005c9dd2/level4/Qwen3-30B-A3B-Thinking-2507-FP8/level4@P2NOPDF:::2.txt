

The paper introduces a novel conditional independence test grounded in a smoothness assumption for conditional densities.  

**Strengths**  
1. The paper correctly identifies that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is necessary for the test to function, proposing a specific such assumption and establishing a bound on the total variation distance between the null density and the bootstrap density under this assumption. This contribution is novel and addresses a critical gap.  
2. The authors leverage this smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1, Theorem 1(iii)), providing a concrete theoretical foundation for their approach.  
3. Theorem 1 is a significant contribution: it synthesizes three key assumptions (smoothness, achievability of empirical risk R̂ with error η, and bounded error difference between optimal and trained classifiers) into a non-trivial, insightful bound. This integration demonstrates clear theoretical depth.  

**Weaknesses**  
The paper is difficult to follow due to insufficient interpretation of results, making it challenging to verify the correctness or practical implications of the claims. For instance, consider the inequality in Section 1.1(iii): under the alternative hypothesis and with r₀ = 0.1, suppose the hypothesis class G (e.g., linear functions) is too restricted to distinguish between the true function f and the conditional independence estimate f^CI. How is the error bound R̂ < 0.1 + o(1) (for large n) achieved intuitively? The paper does not clarify how the small error probability arises in this scenario, leaving the reader uncertain about the feasibility of the result.  

Additional concerns:  
- The exposition lacks concrete examples or intuitive explanations for key steps (e.g., the role of the smoothness assumption beyond the theoretical bound).  
- The connection between the smoothness assumption and practical test performance is not elaborated, weakening the paper’s impact.  
- The notation (e.g., "r0", "f^CI") is ambiguous without clear definitions, further hindering comprehension.  

**Recommendation**  
The theoretical contributions are promising but require substantial clarification to be compelling. The paper should: (1) explicitly motivate the smoothness assumption with intuitive examples, (2) resolve the ambiguity in the error bound example (Section 1.1(iii)), and (3) provide a high-level interpretation of Theorem 1’s implications for test design. Without these revisions, the core novelty is obscured by poor exposition. As written, the paper’s technical merits are difficult to assess, and the lack of interpretive depth limits its contribution to the field. Revisions addressing readability and intuition are essential.
