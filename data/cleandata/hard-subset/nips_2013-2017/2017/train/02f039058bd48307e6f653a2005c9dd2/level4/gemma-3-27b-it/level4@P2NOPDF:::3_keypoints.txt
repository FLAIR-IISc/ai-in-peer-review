- Introduces a novel distribution-free test for conditional independence between three continuous variables using generated approximate-conditional samples and classifier error as the test statistic.
- Addresses an important problem for causal inference (assessing unconfoundedness) with practical significance.
- Method generates samples approximating the conditional distribution in total variation distance from joint samples, then trains a binary classifier (e.g., DNN) to form the test.
- Empirically evaluated with comparisons to existing methods, plus descriptive analysis and experiments on synthetic and real-world data.
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible; theoretical details require careful reading but key findings are well summarized.
- Theoretical guarantees are strong; approach builds on and extends “Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests” to the conditional setting after matching on the third variable.
- Suggest exploring extension to discrete conditioning variables, where bootstrap may boost power.
- Recommend investigating sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings, with simulations showing impact on test decisions.
- Section 1.1 is repetitive and should be streamlined.
- Algorithm 2 is overly detailed and could be summarized more concisely.
- Minor typos: replace “classifier” at line 157 and “graphs” at line 280.
