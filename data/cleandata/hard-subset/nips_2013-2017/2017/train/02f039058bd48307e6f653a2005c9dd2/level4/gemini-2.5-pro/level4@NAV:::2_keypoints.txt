- Novel recognition that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is crucial, formalized and used to bound the total variation between the null density and the bootstrap density—a novel insight.  
- The smoothness assumption is further used to derive a bound on the training error of the optimal classifier (reference 1.1 (iii)).  
- Theorem 1 is novel and non-trivial, successfully combining the smoothness assumption with (i) empirical risk approximability (hat R achievable up to error eta) and (ii) a condition on the error gap between the optimal and trained classifier.  
- Major weakness: paper readability and intuition are lacking, making the theoretical results hard to interpret.  
- Concrete concern/example: unclear how a small error probability arises when the alternative has r0=0.1 but the function class G is not expressive (e.g., linear), so explanation is needed for how hat R can be < 0.1 + o1 (<0.2) for large n.
