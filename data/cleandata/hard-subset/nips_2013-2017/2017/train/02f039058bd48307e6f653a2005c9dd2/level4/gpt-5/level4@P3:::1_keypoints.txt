- Proposes reducing nonparametric conditional independence testing to binary classification using an NN bootstrap to approximate fCI and labeling original vs. bootstrapped samples.  
- Provides TV-distance bounds showing the NN bootstrap distribution is close to fCI under smoothness assumptions, with rates depending on dz.  
- Proves generalization bounds for classifiers trained on near-independent (non-i.i.d.) samples from the bootstrap, adapting VC/Rademacher theory.  
- Empirically outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear problems and a flow cytometry dataset; code is publicly available.  
- Connects to prior kernel-based CI tests, classifier-based two-sample tests, NN entropy/MI estimation, and non-i.i.d. generalization literature.  
- Strength: clear, modular reduction enabling use of strong classifiers in high dimensions.  
- Strength: novel NN bootstrap with an intuitive, technically nontrivial TV bound to fCI.  
- Strength: generalization guarantees under near-independence that extend classical theory.  
- Strength: solid empirical validation including real data.  
- Strength: flexible pipeline—improvements in classifiers or NN search directly benefit the test.  
- Weakness: calibration and threshold selection (τ) are heuristic; need principled null calibration or asymptotic null for p-values/Type I control.  
- Weakness: potential low power against weak/local alternatives where dependence strength shrinks with n; needs power analyses and empirical power curves vs. effect size and dz.  
- Weakness: asymmetry in treating X and Y (only Y swapped via NN in Z) could affect power/Type I error; a symmetrized variant may be needed.  
- Weakness: lack of ablation studies exploring pairing NN bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests or permutation strategies with classifiers.  
- Weakness: theoretical rates degrade with dz (e.g., n−1/dz); need discussion of effectiveness in high-dimensional Z (manifolds, clustering, metric learning).  
- Weakness: experimental reporting focuses on accuracy/AUC; calibrated p-values and Type I error plots across n and dz are missing.  
- Weakness: computational complexity details (in n, dx, dy, dz) and wall-clock comparisons to KCIT/RCIT are not provided.  
- Quality assessment: methodology is technically sound and carefully argued, but practical calibration needs clarification.  
- Clarity assessment: paper is readable but should define constants, ensure consistent notation, separate bias term b(n) from τ, and include main-proof sketches.  
- Originality: novel combination of NN-based conditional product simulation and classification-based testing; distinct from kernel CI methods.  
- Significance: promising scalable CI testing approach that could influence causal discovery pipelines if calibration is provided.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple flexible reduction, new NN bootstrap with TV guarantees, novel generalization bounds, open-source code, real-data application.  
- Cons for acceptance: lack of principled calibration/p-values, asymmetry concerns, degraded theoretical rates with dz, limited ablations.  
- Suggested improvements: add resampling/asymptotic calibration for p-values, analyze power under local alternatives and dz sensitivity, evaluate a symmetrized procedure, run ablations (NN+MMD, permutation+classifier), and report computational complexity guidance.  
- Minor fixes: ensure empirical risks normalized in algorithms, fix typos, define G(ε) and constants on first use, and report confidence intervals for AUC/accuracy.
