- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers (e.g., GBTs, DNNs).
- Introduces a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution.
- Provides theoretical guarantees bounding closeness of the bootstrap samples to the true distribution in total variation distance.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with an effective introduction and related work placement.
- Main contributions: (1) classification-based CI testing, (2) nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure, (3) theoretical performance guarantees.
- Strengths: leverages modern classifiers, offers an efficient NN bootstrap, supplies theoretical guarantees, and includes extensive empirical evaluation.
- Question of modularity: can the generation and classification components be decoupled or replaced by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests)?
- Concern about detecting weak conditional dependence: will the method correctly detect weak x–y dependence given z or falsely accept independence?
- Symmetry concern: method treats x and y symmetrically, raising issues for causal interpretation where directionality matters.
- Unclear how to choose parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 and what different τ values imply for performance.
- Minor comment: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 appears should be normalized by sample size.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear behavior under weak dependence, ambiguous τ selection, and symmetry/causal limitations.
