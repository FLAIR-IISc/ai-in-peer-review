- Method: Converts CI testing into a binary classification task by generating f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) samples via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap and using classifier accuracy as the test statistic.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Proves bootstrapped samples are close to f_CI in total variation (Theorem 1) and derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independent (non-i.i.d.) samples (Theorem 2).  
- Contribution 1: Novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f_CI samples without expensive linear programming.  
- Contribution 2: Formal guarantees on closeness of bootstrap distribution to the true conditionally independent distribution.  
- Contribution 3: New Rademacher-complexity-style generalization analysis for near-independent samples.  
- Contribution 4: Empirical validation showing better scalability and performance than kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT).  
- Strength: Well-motivated, addresses an important problem with broad applications (Bayesian networks, causal inference, feature selection).  
- Strength: Substantial theoretical contributions extending beyond standard i.i.d. analyses.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation with pseudocode and method-theory-experiment linkage.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical results, especially in high-dimensional Z settings, and effective use of powerful classifiers.  
- Weakness: No analysis of symmetry between X and Y—method may be sensitive to which variable is used for nearest-neighbor bootstrap, important in causal contexts.  
- Weakness: Threshold choice τ (set to 1/√n) is not well-justified and lacks analysis of effects on Type I/II errors, especially for weak dependence.  
- Weakness: Limited investigation of weak conditional dependence regimes where classifier accuracy may be near random.  
- Weakness: Dependence on dimension of Z is not fully explained; empirical advantages exceed theoretical account.  
- Quality: Technically sound with well-supported theory and comprehensive experiments, but would benefit from deeper parameter and edge-case analyses.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and explains the bootstrap-classification connection effectively.  
- Originality: Novel combination of nearest-neighbor bootstrap and classifier-based CI testing, and novel non-i.i.d. Rademacher analysis.  
- Significance: Addresses key limitations of existing CI tests in high dimensions and is modularly compatible with future classifier advances.  
- Pro: Strong novel method, theoretical guarantees, and superior empirical performance.  
- Con: Missing symmetry analysis, insufficient justification for τ, limited weak-dependence study, and incomplete theoretical explanation of empirical gains.
