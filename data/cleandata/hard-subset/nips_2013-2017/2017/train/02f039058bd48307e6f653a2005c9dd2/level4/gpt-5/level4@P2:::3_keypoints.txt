- Proposes a nonparametric CI test that reduces CI testing to binary classification by creating Z-dependent pseudo-samples via nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- Synthetic samples approximate fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) by replacing y with the y of the nearest neighbor in Z from another split.
- Uses a classifier to distinguish original vs synthetic samples; rejects H0 if classifier performs well; offers a bias-corrected variant comparing classifiers with and without X.
- Builds on classifier two-sample tests for independence and complements kernel-based CI tests (KCIT/RCIT/KCIPT) with an efficient bootstrap construction.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: TV distance of bootstrap to fCI is O(n^{-1/dz}) under smoothness assumptions.
- Derives generalization bounds for empirical risk minimization with near-i.i.d. (near-independent) samples.
- Empirical results show strong performance on synthetic data (including increasing dim(Z)) and real flow cytometry data, outperforming KCIT/RCIT.
- Code is publicly available and the method is computationally practical compared to optimization-based alternatives.
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible, though some repetition and dense proofs exist.
- Novelty lies in the simple nearest-neighbor bootstrap for CI and analysis of classification under near-independence; the TV-distance guarantee is noteworthy.
- Main practical issue: type I error control and p-value calibration are not fully specified; threshold τ appears heuristic.
- Performance may depend on classifier choice and hyperparameters; robustness/sensitivity analysis is limited.
- TV bound degrades with dim(Z), indicating a potential curse of dimensionality in the bootstrap step.
- Assumptions (smoothness, bounded Hessian/Lipschitz density) may be restrictive in practice.
- Suggest discussing discrete/mixed Z: resampling within Z cells could simplify bootstrap and improve guarantees.
- Recommend reporting robustness across classifiers (XGBoost, RF, shallow/deep nets) and hyperparameter sensitivity plots.
- Ask for principled calibration/level-α testing: e.g., permutation tests, multiple splits/bootstrap replications, or cross-fitting to control type I error.
- Suggest exploring k-NN instead of 1-NN (random neighbor among k) to reduce variance or improve TV rates, and ablations with approximate-NN.
- Request runtime and scalability comparisons (wall-clock) vs KCIT/RCIT/KCIPT across n and dim(Z), and discussion of ANN indexing choices.
- Recommend adding baselines like conditional mutual information estimators (modern kNN) to broaden comparisons.
- Ask for practical guidance on preprocessing Z (scaling/whitening), distance metrics, train/test split strategy, and number of bootstrap replications.
- Minor edits: condense repetitive sections (Sec 1.1), shorten Algorithm 2, emphasize Algorithm 3, and fix typos (“classifier”, “graphs”).
- Overall assessment: strong, timely, theoretically grounded, simple to implement, and empirically effective; addressing calibration, classifier sensitivity, and mixed/discrete Z would improve practical impact.
