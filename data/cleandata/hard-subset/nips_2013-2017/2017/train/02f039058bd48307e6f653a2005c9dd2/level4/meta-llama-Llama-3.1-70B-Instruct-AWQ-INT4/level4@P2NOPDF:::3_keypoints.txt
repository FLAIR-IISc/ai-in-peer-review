Proposes a general nonparametric test for conditional independence of three continuous variables by constructing samples approximating the conditional product distribution (in TV distance) and using a classifier's predictive error to test the null.

Avoids strong parametric assumptions and is directly applicable to causal inference problems like testing unconfoundedness.

Provides comprehensive comparisons to existing methods with experiments on synthetic and real data.

Paper is well-written and accessible; theoretical proofs are dense but key findings are clearly summarized.

Theoretical contributions are strong: notable extension of classifier two-sample tests to the conditional case with Z and provable guarantees.

Recommendation to explore a discrete analog when Z is discrete, where a bootstrap approach might be more powerful.

Recommend analyzing sensitivity of the test to choice of classifier and hyperparameters, since small hyperparameter changes may alter test outcomes.

Section 1.1 (Main Contributions) appears redundant and could be condensed.

Algorithm 2 need not be written out in full; a succinct description would suffice.

Minor typographical corrections: "classifier" at line 157 and "graphs" at line 280.
