- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing for continuous variables to a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate CI-like samples.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on bootstrap approximation accuracy (total variation distance) and classifier generalization in a non-i.i.d. setting.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets, showing improvements over existing CI methods.  
- Addresses an important problem with applications in causal discovery, Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.  
- Well-written and clearly presented method and theoretical results.  
- Novel combination of classifier-based two-sample testing with a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Strong theoretical contributions, including Rademacher complexity analysis under near-independence.  
- Experimental results demonstrate performance gains compared to prior approaches.  
- Method decomposes into bootstrap and classification components; unclear why the specific combination outperforms pairing existing bootstrap or permutation schemes with classifiers or kernel two-sample tests.  
- Potential weakness when X–Y|Z dependency is weak; sensitivity to small effect sizes is not sufficiently studied.  
- Treats X and Y symmetrically and does not address causal directionality or asymmetry in dependencies.  
- Sensitivity to threshold parameter τ is critical but lacks guidance for selection and tuning.  
- Minor technical fix: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size n.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with promising empirical results and valuable non-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to natural baselines, concerns about weak-dependency performance and causal asymmetry, and unclear parameter selection.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution that warrants acceptance with minor revisions to address τ selection, limitations (weak dependencies, causal asymmetry), and additional baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
