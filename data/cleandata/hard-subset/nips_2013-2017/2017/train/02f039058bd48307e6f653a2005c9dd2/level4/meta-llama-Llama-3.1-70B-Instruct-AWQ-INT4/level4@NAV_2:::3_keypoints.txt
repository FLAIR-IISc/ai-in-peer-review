- Proposes a general test for conditional independence between three continuous variables using samples approximating the conditional product distribution.  
- Constructs surrogate samples close in total variation distance and uses a binary classifier (e.g., deep net) whose prediction error decides the CI test.  
- Compares the proposed method to existing approaches via description and experiments on synthetic and real data.  
- Method is straightforward, powerful, and well-suited for causal-inference applications (e.g., assessing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners with clear methodology presentation.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong, extending classifier two-sample test ideas to conditional independence with notable guarantees.  
- Provides generalization bounds for the classification-based test.  
- Strengths: clear writing, thorough comparisons, and impressive theoretical results.  
- Weaknesses: lacks analysis of sensitivity to the choice of classifier and hyperparameters.  
- Weaknesses: some redundancy in sections (e.g., Section 1.1 main contributions) that could be condensed.  
- Weaknesses: could include more experimental comparisons to existing methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, highly original and significant; recommended for acceptance (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance all rated 9/10).
