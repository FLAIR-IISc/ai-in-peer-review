- Introduces a novel conditional independence test by explicitly assuming smoothness of the conditional density and using it to bound the total variation distance between the null and bootstrap-obtained densities.  
- Applies the smoothness assumption to derive a bound on the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (see eq. 1.1 (iii)).  
- Theorem 1 coherently integrates the smoothness assumption, achievability of the estimator’s risk with error η, and the gap between optimal and trained classifier errors — a significant and nontrivial result.  
- Weakness: The text is dense and under-interprets results; it’s unclear how a limited function class G (e.g., linear) can achieve a small empirical error under the alternative (illustrated by the r0 = 0.1 example), so more intuitive explanation is needed.
