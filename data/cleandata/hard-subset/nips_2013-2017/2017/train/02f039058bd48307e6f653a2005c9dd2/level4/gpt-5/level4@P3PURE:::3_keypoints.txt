- Proposes a nonparametric conditional independence test for continuous X,Y,Z by approximating p(x|z)p(y|z)p(z) via neighbor matching and reducing to a classifier-based two-sample test.
- Constructs joint and approximate product samples and uses a binary classifier's predictive error to accept/reject conditional independence.
- Compares method conceptually and empirically to existing approaches on synthetic and real data.
- Strength: addresses a central problem for causal inference with a straightforward, broadly applicable method.
- Strength: paper is generally well written and makes dense theory accessible to practitioners.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting via neighbor matching.
- Question: clarify the discrete Z case (or discrete variables more broadly) and potential advantages of resampling/bootstrap there.
- Question: provide a robustness analysis on sensitivity to classifier choice, architecture, regularization, and hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: streamline Section 1.1 to avoid repetition of the main contributions.
- Suggestion: condense or move Algorithm 2 details to an appendix to avoid duplication.
- Minor issues: fix typos (“classifier” line 157, “graphs” line 280).
