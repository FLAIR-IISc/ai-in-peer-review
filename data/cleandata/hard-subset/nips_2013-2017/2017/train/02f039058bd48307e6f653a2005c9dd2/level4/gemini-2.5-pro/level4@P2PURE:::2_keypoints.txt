- Novel contribution: identifies and formalizes a smoothness assumption on the conditional density to enable bounding the total-variation distance between the null distribution and the bootstrap distribution.
- Leverages the smoothness assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1 (iii)).
- Theorem 1 is novel and non-trivial, synthesizing the smoothness assumption, an empirical-risk achievability error eta, and an assumption on the gap between optimal and trained classifier errors.
- Presentation weakness: paper is difficult to read and lacks sufficient interpretation of its theoretical results, leaving the reviewer uncertain of their understanding.
- Specific concern/question: under the alternative with r0 = 0.1 and a low-expressivity function class G (e.g., linear), how can a small classification error be achieved (i.e., hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?
