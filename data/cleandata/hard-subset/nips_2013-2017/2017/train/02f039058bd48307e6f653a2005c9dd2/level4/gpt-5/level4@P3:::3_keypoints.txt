- Proposes a nonparametric CI test that reduces CI testing to binary classification via an NN bootstrap that swaps Y between Z-nearest neighbors to approximate fCI.  
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant that subtracts baseline separability from (Y,Z) to improve calibration.  
- Provides finite-sample theoretical guarantees: TV closeness to fCI under smoothness and non-i.i.d. generalization bounds for classifiers on near-i.i.d. samples.  
- TV convergence rate given as dTV(·, fCI) = O(n^{-1/dz}) (up to constants and low-density region terms), suffering from the curse of dimensionality in Z.  
- Generalization analysis extends VC/Rademacher tools to dependent sampling and is of independent technical interest.  
- Method is simple, modular (works with any classifier), and computationally practical using fast NN search; bias correction helps finite-sample calibration.  
- Empirical results: strong performance on synthetic post-nonlinear models and flow cytometry benchmark, outperforming KCIT and RCIT, especially at larger dz.  
- Suggests reporting type I error control under H0 across sample sizes and sensitivity to classifier choice/hyperparameters.  
- Requests ablation comparing classifiers (XGBoost, RF, neural nets) and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Raises calibration/size-control concerns: asks about guarantees for nominal type I error and methods like cross-fitting or repeated NN bootstraps to estimate null distribution.  
- Notes extension to discrete or mixed Z is natural and requests brief discussion or experiments for broader applicability.  
- Implementation details need clarification: tie-breaking in NN, choice/whitening of distance metric for Z, and effects of approximate NN in high dimensions.  
- Questions sample-efficiency: 3-way sample split reduces power; suggests cross-fitting or data reuse strategies to improve efficiency.  
- Computational complexity concerns: kd-tree/ANN methods degrade in high dz; requests empirical timing vs. dz and n.  
- Writing/clarity: generally well written with clear pseudocode, but needs trimming of repetition, better intuition for Assumption 1, and minor typo fixes.  
- Originality: builds on classifier two-sample tests but novelty lies in the NN bootstrap with TV guarantees and near-independence generalization bounds.  
- Significance: practically important for causal discovery and scalable CI testing; theoretical contributions advance learning under dependent sampling.  
- Pros: simple/modular, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical performance, public code, practical bias correction.  
- Cons: curse of dimensionality in dz, insufficient exploration of classifier sensitivity and calibration, and some presentation issues.  
- Overall assessment: strong and timely work with careful theory and convincing experiments; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing sensitivity, calibration, and clarity.
