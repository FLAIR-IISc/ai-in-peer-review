This work introduces a novel method for conditional independence testing.

**Strengths**
1) A primary contribution is the recognition that a smoothness assumption on the conditional density is crucial for the test's validity. The authors introduce a formal assumption to this effect and leverage it to bound the total variation distance between the density under the null and the density derived from the bootstrap. This is a novel insight.
2) This assumption is further leveraged to establish a bound on the training error of the optimal classifier (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1 is a novel, insightful, and non-trivial result. It successfully integrates the smoothness assumption with two other key assumptions: that the empirical risk `hat R` can be achieved with an error of `eta`, and a condition on the error gap between the optimal and the trained classifier.

**Weaknesses**
The paper's readability is a major concern. It is difficult to follow and offers limited intuition behind the theoretical results. This lack of interpretation makes it challenging to be confident in one's understanding of the key findings. For example, regarding the inequality in Section 1.1 (iii), suppose the alternative hypothesis holds with r0=0.1. If the function class G is not sufficiently expressive (e.g., linear functions) and cannot distinguish between f and f^CI, it is not clear how a small error probability is achieved. An intuitive explanation is needed for how `hat R` can be less than `0.1 + o1 < 0.2` for large `n` under these conditions.
