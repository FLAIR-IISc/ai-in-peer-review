- Proposes nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^CI and a classifier to distinguish observed vs. bootstrapped data; failure to classify implies conditional independence.
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation closeness between the bootstrap distribution and f^CI, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal and nearly-independent samples.
- Major concern: The sampling and classification components may be replaceable by existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approaches or kernel two-sample tests); no comparative analysis is provided.
- Major concern: Unclear performance when x and y are only weakly dependent given z; potential difficulty detecting weak dependence and avoiding false positives.
- Major concern: Unclear symmetry w.r.t. x and y and whether variable ordering or causal structure affects results; sampling strategy may need to account for causality.
- Major concern: No clear guidance for choosing parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3; sensitivity analysis or robust selection method is missing.
- Minor comment: Empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to lack normalization by sample size.
