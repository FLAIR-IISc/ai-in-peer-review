- Proposes a nonparametric conditional independence test for continuous variables by generating samples approximating the conditional product distribution (via matching on Z) and using a binary classifier to distinguish joint vs product distributions.  
- After training the classifier, the test uses its predictive error to accept or reject the null of conditional independence.  
- Compares the method theoretically and empirically to existing approaches on synthetic and real data.  
- Method is straightforward, broadly applicable to causal inference, and the paper is well-written though theoretical proofs need careful reading.  
- Main contribution: strong, novel theoretical guarantees extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional case with neighbor matching.  
- Does not address the discrete-Z case; bootstrap-based procedures for discrete Z should be discussed.  
- Unclear sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters; recommend evaluating stability of decisions under minor hyperparameter changes.  
- Section 1.1 is repetitive and could be condensed.  
- Algorithm 2 is redundant given the textual description and can be removed.  
- Minor typos noted: "classifier" (line 157) and "graphs" (line 280).
