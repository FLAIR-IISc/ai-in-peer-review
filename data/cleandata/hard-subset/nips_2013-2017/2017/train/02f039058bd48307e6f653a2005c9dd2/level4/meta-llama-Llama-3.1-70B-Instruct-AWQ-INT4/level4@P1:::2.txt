The paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

Strengths
1) The paper identifies that in order for a test to work, an assumption on the smoothness of the conditional density should be made. The authors propose such an assumption and bound the distance in total variation between the density under the null hypothesis and the density obtained from the bootstrap under this assumption. This is a novel contribution.

2) The paper uses this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (Section 1.1, part (iii)).

3) Theorem 1, which combines the assumption on smoothness, the assumption that the optimal risk can be achieved with error η, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifiers, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

Weaknesses
The paper is hard to read and does not provide much interpretation of the results, to the extent that it is unclear if the results are correctly understood. For example, consider the inequality in Section 1.1, part (iii). Assume the alternative hypothesis holds and r0=0.1. Suppose G is a small class, say linear functions, and suppose they are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI at all. Can the authors explain, intuitively, how the small probability of the error can be achieved (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?

It would be helpful to have more explanations and interpretations of the results, especially in terms of the implications of the theorems and the performance of the proposed algorithm. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a clearer and more concise writing style to improve its readability.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel conditional independence test with theoretical guarantees.
* The test is based on a model-powered approach that reduces the problem to binary classification, allowing for the use of powerful supervised learning tools.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the test's performance, including bounds on the error of the optimal classifier.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper is hard to read and lacks clear interpretations of the results.
* The writing style is dense and could be improved for better readability.
* The paper could benefit from more explanations and discussions of the implications of the theorems and the performance of the proposed algorithm.

Overall, the paper has some strong contributions, but its readability and clarity need to be improved. With some revisions to address these issues, the paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field.
