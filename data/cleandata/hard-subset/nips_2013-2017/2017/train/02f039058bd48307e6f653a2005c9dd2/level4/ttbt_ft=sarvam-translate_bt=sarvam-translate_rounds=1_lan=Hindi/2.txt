This research paper proposes a new conditional independence test.

**Strengths**

1) The paper states that for a test to be successful, an assumption must be made on the ease of conditional density. The authors propose to bound the total difference between the density under such an assumption and the zero hypothesis, and the density obtained from a bootstrap under this assumption. This is novel.
2) The paper uses this assumption to bound the error of the optimal classifier on the training set (1.1 (iii)).
3) Theorem 1, which combines the assumption on ease, the assumption that the hat R can be obtained with error eta, and the assumption on the difference in errors between the optimal and trained classifier, is novel, insightful, and non-trivial.

**Weaknesses**

The paper is difficult to read and does not provide much interpretation of the results, to the extent that I am not sure I understand them correctly. For example, consider the inequality in section 1.1 iii) assume an alternative hypothesis and r0=0.1. Assume that G is a small class, assume linear functions, and assume that they are not expressive enough to distinguish between f and f^CI. Can you intuitively explain how a small probability of error can be obtained (hat R < 0.1 + o1 < 0.2 for large n)?
