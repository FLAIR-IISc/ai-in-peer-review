- Proposes reducing nonparametric CI testing to binary classification by NN-bootstraping Y across Z-nearest neighbors to approximate fCI and training a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Theoretically: proves TV bound O(n−1/dz) for NN-bootstrapped distribution under smoothness and provides generalization risk bounds for near-independent samples; empirically outperforms kernel CI tests on synthetic and flow cytometry data.  
- Connects to kernel-based CI tests, permutation-based CI samplers, classifier two-sample tests, and NN methods for density/entropy estimation; novelty is the NN bootstrap plus non-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  

Strengths:
- Modular reduction allowing use of modern classifiers for CI testing.  
- Theoretical guarantees: TV closeness of bootstrap to fCI and generalization bounds under near-independence.  
- Strong empirical performance, especially for higher-dimensional Z, with code released.  
- Bias-corrected variant addresses finite-sample bias from non-i.i.d. bootstrap samples.  

Major concerns:
1) Would benefit from experiments or discussion on hybridizations (e.g., pairing permutation-based fCI samples with classifiers or NN bootstrap with kernel two-sample tests) to isolate module contributions.  
2) Unclear power under weak conditional dependence when bootstrap approximation error is non-negligible; needs analysis/experiments on sensitivity vs n, dz, and classifier capacity.  
3) Symmetry between X and Y and potential causal interpretation not addressed; performance may depend on which variable is swapped.  
4) Practical selection and calibration of threshold τ is not well guided; need principled, data-driven methods to control Type I error.  

Minor:
- Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Tackles an important problem with a clear, implementable modular approach.  
- Provides novel theoretical analyses (TV bound and near-independence generalization).  
- Demonstrates strong, scalable empirical results.  

Cons against acceptance:
- Lacks guidance on setting τ and assessing power against weak alternatives.  
- Does not discuss X–Y symmetry, relevant for causal interpretations.  
- No experiments on hybrid variants to separate effects of sampling vs testing modules.  

Evaluation by criteria:
- Quality: Technically sound with solid bounds and empirical validation; needs practical calibration and power discussion.  
- Clarity: Well written and organized; algorithms clear aside from minor normalization issue.  
- Originality: Novel reduction using NN bootstrap plus non-i.i.d. generalization analysis distinct from kernel/permutation methods.  
- Significance: High potential impact by leveraging supervised learning advances and scaling to larger dz, contingent on addressing τ calibration and additional experiments.
