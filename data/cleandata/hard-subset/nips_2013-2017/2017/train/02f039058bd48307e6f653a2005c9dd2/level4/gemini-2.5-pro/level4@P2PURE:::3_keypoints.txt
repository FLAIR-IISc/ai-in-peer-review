- Proposes a general non-parametric conditional independence test for three continuous variables that avoids strong parametric assumptions.  
- Generates a second sample approximating the conditional product distribution from joint samples and uses both sets to perform a two-sample test.  
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural network) to distinguish the two distributions; classifier error is used as the test statistic.  
- Provides thorough comparisons to existing methods with both theoretical discussion and empirical evaluations on synthetic and real data.  
- Motivates importance for causal inference and unconfoundedness testing; method is straightforward and broadly applicable.  
- Paper is well-written; dense theoretical parts are effectively summarized for practitioners.  
- Main strength is the strong theoretical guarantees accompanying the classifier-based extension to the conditional setting.  
- Method is essentially an extension of classifier two-sample tests with a neighbor-matching step on the conditioning variable Z.  
- Questions/suggestions: potential extension to discrete Z (bootstrap may help) and need to analyze sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters.  
- Presentation issues: Section 1.1 is largely redundant, Algorithm 2 is unnecessary, and minor typos at lines 157 (“classifier”) and 280 (“graphs”).
