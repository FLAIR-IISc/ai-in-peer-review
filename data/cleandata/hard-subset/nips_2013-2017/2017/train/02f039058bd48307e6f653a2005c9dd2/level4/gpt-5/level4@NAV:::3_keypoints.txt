- Introduces a general-purpose conditional independence test for three continuous variables that generates samples approximating the conditional product distribution in total variation distance.  
- Uses generated samples as input to a binary classifier; the classifier’s predictive error is used to accept/reject the null of conditional independence.  
- Avoids strong parametric assumptions and relies on sample generation plus classification rather than model-based tests.  
- Method is a natural extension of classifier two-sample tests, adapting neighbor matching on Z to include a third variable.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that are impressive and constitute the paper’s most compelling contribution.  
- Experiments compare the proposed method to prior work on synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating practical performance.  
- Paper is well written and the methodology is clearly presented for practitioners, though some theoretical proofs are hard to parse.  
- The approach appears straightforward and sufficiently powerful for causal inference tasks (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Question: Is there a discrete-Z analog and would the bootstrap be more effective in that case?  
- Question: How sensitive is the test to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings; include robustness analyses.  
- Structural suggestion: Section 1.1 is redundant and could be tightened.  
- Presentation suggestion: Algorithm 2 can be described more concisely rather than written out in full.  
- Minor typos to correct: “classifier” (line 157) and “graphs” (line 280).
