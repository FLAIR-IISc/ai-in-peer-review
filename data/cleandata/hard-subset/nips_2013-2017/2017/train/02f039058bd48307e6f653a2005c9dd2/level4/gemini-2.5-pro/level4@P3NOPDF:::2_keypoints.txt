1. Introduces a novel CI testing method using a classification framework and a new smoothness assumption on the conditional density.  
2. Uses the smoothness assumption to provide theoretical guarantees, notably a bound on the total variation distance between the null and bootstrap distributions.  
3. Strength: formalizes the necessary smoothness condition and derives a significant TV-distance bound based on it.  
4. Strength: Theorem 1 non-trivially links the smoothness assumption, the classifier class’s ability to approximate the Bayes classifier, and the classifier’s generalization error.  
5. Weakness: Presentation is dense with limited intuition or interpretation, making the theoretical results hard to parse.  
6. Weakness/concern: The method implicitly requires the classifier class G to be rich enough to approximate the Bayes optimal classifier (else risk ≈0.5 and the test may fail); this assumption should be stated and discussed for practical use.
