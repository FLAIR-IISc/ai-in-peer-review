- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f_CI-like samples.  
- Uses classifier performance (near-random vs. better-than-random) to accept or reject X ⟂ Y | Z.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on closeness of nearest-neighbor bootstrap samples to f_CI in total variation distance.  
- Derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independent (bootstrapped) samples.  
- Builds on and extends prior CI methods (kernel tests, permutation methods) with a theoretically grounded bootstrap approach.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real datasets, especially with high-dimensional Z.  
- Major concern: unclear whether the method is symmetric in X and Y and how asymmetric causal directions affect results.  
- Major concern: threshold τ = 1/√n in Algorithms 2/3 is ad-hoc and lacks theoretical or data-driven justification.  
- Major concern: paper does not explore alternative ways to generate f_CI samples (e.g., permutation-based methods) or compare them to the NN bootstrap.  
- Major concern: behavior under very weak conditional dependence is not analyzed—unclear if classifiers can detect weak signals.  
- Minor concern: Algorithms 2/3 should explicitly state empirical risk is normalized by sample size.  
- Minor concern: inconsistent notation for f_CI / f^{CI}; needs consistent definition.  
- Minor concern: use of "weakly dependent" should be made precise (e.g., small conditional mutual information).  
- Strength: novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap with theoretical backing for generating f_CI samples.  
- Strength: novel theoretical analysis of classification generalization under near-independence.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains over baselines, notably in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured exposition.  
- Strength: modular design lets practitioners swap classifiers without changing core CI test.  
- Weakness: omission of symmetry discussion with respect to X and Y limits causal interpretation.  
- Weakness: lack of theoretical basis for τ may hinder practical use.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of weak-dependence detection and alternative f_CI generation methods.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated contribution with solid theory and empirical evidence, but needs revisions on symmetry, thresholding, and behavior under weak dependence.
