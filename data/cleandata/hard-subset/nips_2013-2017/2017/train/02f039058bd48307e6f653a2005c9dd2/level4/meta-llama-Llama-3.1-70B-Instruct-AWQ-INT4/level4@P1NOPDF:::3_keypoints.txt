- Proposes a general, nonparametric conditional independence test using samples approximating the conditional product distribution and a binary classifier to decide independence.  
- Method trains a classifier on joint vs. conditional-product samples and uses predictive error to accept/reject the null hypothesis.  
- Compares the proposed test to existing methods with descriptions and experiments on synthetic and real data.  
- Conditional independence testing is important for causal inference; the method is straightforward and broadly applicable.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners despite some theoretical complexity.  
- Theoretical guarantees are the most impressive aspect, extending classifier two-sample test results to include a conditioning variable Z.  
- The approach is similar to prior work after matching neighbors of Z, as acknowledged by the authors.  
- Questions/suggestions: is there a discrete-data analog, and does bootstrap become more powerful when Z is discrete?  
- Suggests investigating sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters, as they may affect test outcomes.  
- Recommends trimming redundancy in Section 1.1 and omitting Algorithm 2 for conciseness.  
- Notes minor typos (e.g., "classifier" at line 157 and "graphs" at line 280).  
- Overall positive: valuable contribution with minor revisions could increase impact.
