- Method summary: nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate f^{CI}, then train/evaluate a classifier to test conditional independence (random-guess performance ⇒ accept null).  
- Theoretical claims: derives total variation bounds between bootstrap distribution and f^{CI}, and provides empirical risk bounds for ideal and near-independent samples.  
- Overall impression: addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.  
- Component replaceability: questions whether the bootstrap/classifier components could be substituted with existing methods (e.g., permutation-based approach [7] or kernel two-sample tests) and how that would affect performance.  
- Finite-sample sensitivity: concerns about distinguishing weak conditional dependence from true conditional independence with finite samples.  
- Symmetry/causal interpretation: questions whether the method is symmetric in x and y and how causal directionality (x→y vs y→x) affects sampling and testing.  
- Hyperparameter choice: serious concerns about selecting the parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3.  
- Implementation detail: empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to miss division by the sample size.
