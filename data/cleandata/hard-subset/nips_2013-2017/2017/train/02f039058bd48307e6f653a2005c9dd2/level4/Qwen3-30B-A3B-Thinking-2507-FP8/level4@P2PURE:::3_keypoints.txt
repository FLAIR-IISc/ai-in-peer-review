- Proposes a conditional independence test for three continuous variables by generating samples approximating the conditional product distribution and training a binary classifier on joint vs product samples.  
- Sample generation targets total variation closeness to the conditional product distribution; classifier predictive error is used to accept/reject conditional independence.  
- Method avoids strong parametric assumptions and can use flexible classifiers (e.g., deep neural networks).  
- Includes theoretical analysis and empirical comparisons on synthetic and real datasets.  
- CI testing is crucial for ML and causal inference; the method is straightforward and broadly applicable to causal problems.  
- Paper is well-written and practitioner-friendly despite technically challenging proofs; key results are clearly summarized.  
- Main theoretical contribution: extends classifier two-sample tests to incorporate a third variable Z with impressive theoretical guarantees.  
- Acknowledged relation to prior work: becomes similar after matching Z-neighbors.  
- Open question: feasibility of a discrete analog and whether bootstrap gains power when Z is discrete.  
- Open question: sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters; a sensitivity analysis would strengthen results.  
- Presentation critique: Section 1.1 is repetitive and Algorithm 2 is unnecessary.  
- Minor typos: "classifer" (line 157) → "classifier"; "graph" (line 280) → "graphs."
