- Proposes a novel non-parametric CI test by reducing CI testing to a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers (GBTs, DNNs).  
- Core challenge: generating samples from the conditional product distribution fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Addresses sample generation via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap with theoretical guarantees on closeness in total variation distance.  
- Develops generalization bounds for classification under near-independence, yielding error bounds for CI testing.  
- Empirically validates the method on simulated and real datasets, showing gains over existing CI tests.  
- Method is well-motivated and leverages advances in classification to tackle CI testing.  
- Theoretical contributions (bootstrap analysis and generalization bounds) are significant and strengthen the work.  
- Paper is generally well-written, logically presented, and includes both synthetic and real experiments.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation combined with promising empirical performance, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion of the discrete Z analogue of the bootstrap, which may be practically beneficial.  
- Suggestion: Perform sensitivity analysis on classifier choice and hyperparameters to assess robustness.  
- Suggestion: Streamline repetitive parts (e.g., Section 1.1) and consider removing redundant Algorithm 2.  
- Minor issues: Typos (e.g., “classifier” line 157, “graphs” line 280) need fixing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theory, improved empirical results, broad applicability, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for clarity/conciseness improvements, missing sensitivity analyses, and some theoretical parts may be hard for some readers.
