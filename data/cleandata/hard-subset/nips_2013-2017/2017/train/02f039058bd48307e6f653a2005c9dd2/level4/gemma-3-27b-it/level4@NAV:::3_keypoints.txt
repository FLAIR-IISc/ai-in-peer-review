- Introduces a novel distribution-free test for conditional independence among three continuous variables.
- Constructs samples approximating the conditional product distribution via neighbor matching from joint samples, measured in total variation distance.
- Trains a binary classifier (deep neural network) to discriminate between these samples; classification error forms the basis of the hypothesis test.
- Evaluates performance against existing methods with experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Highlights relevance to causal inference and assessing unconfoundedness, noting conceptual simplicity and practical potential.
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible, though theoretical details and proofs require careful reading.
- Strong theoretical contributions, extending classifier two-sample test framework to a third variable with neighbor matching and providing substantial guarantees.
- Suggests exploring extension to discrete conditioning variables and leveraging bootstrapping in that setting.
- Recommends investigating sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings to assess robustness.
