- Proposed approach: use nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating f_CI, then train a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped data; near-random classification implies failure to reject conditional independence.

- Theoretical contributions: bounds on total variation between the nearest-neighbor bootstrap distribution and f_CI, and empirical risk bounds for ideal classifiers and nearly independent samples.

- Overall assessment: tackles an important problem and is clearly presented.

- Major concern: could existing alternatives replace either component (e.g., permutation-based method [7] or kernel two-sample tests), and how would those alternatives perform comparatively?

- Major concern: in finite samples, how does the method distinguish weak conditional dependence from true conditional independence?

- Major concern: symmetry/causal issue—method’s behavior with respect to x vs y is not discussed; unclear whether one must choose which variable to sample and implications for different causal models.

- Major concern: the tuning parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not justified and its effect on performance is unclear.

- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be divided by the sample size for a proper estimate.
