Novel formalization of a smoothness assumption on the conditional density and a bound on the total variation distance between the null and bootstrap distributions.

Leverages the smoothness assumption to derive a bound on the training error of the optimal classifier.

Theorem 1—combining smoothness, an achievable ĥR within error η, and an assumption on the gap between optimal and trained classifier errors—is novel, insightful, and nontrivial.

Paper is difficult to read and offers limited interpretation of the results.

Unclear how, under the alternative (e.g., r0 = 0.1) and with a small hypothesis class G, the small-error event ĥR < 0.1 + o(1) would be achieved for large n.

Needs clarification on whether the bounds depend on properties of the Bayes classifier in the full function class versus the restricted class G, and how classifier misspecification affects the inequality.
