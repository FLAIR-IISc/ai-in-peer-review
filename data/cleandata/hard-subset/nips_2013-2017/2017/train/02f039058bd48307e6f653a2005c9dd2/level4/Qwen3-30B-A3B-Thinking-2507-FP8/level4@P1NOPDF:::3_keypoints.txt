- Proposes a general conditional independence test for three continuous variables by generating samples approximating the conditional product distribution and applying a classifier-based two-sample test.  
- Avoids strong parametric assumptions; uses neighbor-matching on Z to produce samples close in total variation to the conditional product distribution.  
- Trains a binary classifier (e.g., deep neural net) on joint vs. conditional-product samples and uses its predictive error to accept/reject conditional independence.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees and a non-trivial extension of classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting.  
- Empirically compared to existing methods with experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Method is practical and powerful for causal inference tasks (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Paper is well-written and makes complex theoretical results accessible despite deep proofs.
