- Proposes nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate approximate f_CI samples, then trains a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped data for CI testing.  
- Provides finite-sample total variation bounds for the NN-bootstrap approximation to f_CI and empirical risk bounds for ideal and near-independent settings.  
- Addresses an important problem and is clearly presented.  
- Major: Method decomposes into sample generation + classification — could either step be replaced by existing techniques (e.g., permutation-based methods or kernel two-sample tests), and what are the performance trade-offs?  
- Major: Concern about power — how well does the approach detect weak conditional dependence between x and y given z?  
- Major: Symmetry/causal concern — is the test symmetric in x and y, and does one need to decide which variable to sample from under different causal directions?  
- Major: Significant concern about selecting the parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3; paper lacks guidance on choosing τ.  
- Minor: Empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to be missing division by the sample size.
