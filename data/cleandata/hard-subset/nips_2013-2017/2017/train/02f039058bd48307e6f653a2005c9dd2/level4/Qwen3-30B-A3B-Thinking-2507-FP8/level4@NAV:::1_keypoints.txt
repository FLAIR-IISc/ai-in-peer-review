- Proposes using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to synthesize samples approximating f^{CI}, then trains a classifier to distinguish real vs. bootstrapped data for conditional independence testing.  
- Provides bounds on total variation distance between the NN-bootstrapped distribution and f^{CI}, and bounds on empirical risks for ideal and near-independent samples.  
- Paper tackles an important problem and is clearly written.  
- Major: Method decouples into (i) sample generation and (ii) classifier inference — could either component be replaced by established alternatives (e.g., permutation tests or kernel two-sample tests); comparisons would be useful.  
- Major: Unclear behavior when conditional dependence is weak or only approximately holds — how sensitive/robust is the method in that regime?  
- Major: Potential asymmetry between X and Y (causal direction) not addressed; sampling strategy may need to differ for X→Y vs Y→X.  
- Major: Critical parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is unexplained — guidance for selecting τ in practice is missing.  
- Minor: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by the sample size.
