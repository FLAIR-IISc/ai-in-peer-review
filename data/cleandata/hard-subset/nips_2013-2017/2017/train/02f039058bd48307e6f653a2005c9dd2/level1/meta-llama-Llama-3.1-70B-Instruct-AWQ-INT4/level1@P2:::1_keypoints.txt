- Summary: Reframes conditional independence testing as a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples, with theoretical guarantees and strong empirical results.  
- Strength 1 (Novel approach): Introduces a new CI-testing paradigm by converting CI testing into classification, enabling use of powerful classifiers.  
- Strength 2 (Theoretical guarantees): Provides theoretical results on the bootstrap procedure and risk generalization under near-independent, non-i.i.d. samples.  
- Strength 3 (Empirical evaluation): Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data showing effectiveness.  
- Strength 4 (Improved performance): Demonstrates better performance than prior methods, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness 1 (Complexity): Paper assumes substantial ML/statistical background, potentially limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness 2 (Hyperparameters): Lacks detailed discussion on hyperparameter choices for classifiers and the bootstrap procedure.  
- Weakness 3 (Smoothness assumptions): Theoretical guarantees depend on smoothness assumptions that may not hold in practice.  
- Originality: Novel combination of classification-based CI testing and a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Significance: Tackles an important problem with potential applications in causal inference, feature selection, and Bayesian networks; empirically impactful.  
- Quality: Well-written and well-organized with appropriate mathematical notation and proofs.  
- Clarity: Clear and concise exposition and notation, though technical prerequisites are assumed.  
- Pro acceptance 1: Novel and significant methodological contribution.  
- Pro acceptance 2: Strong empirical validation on real-world datasets.  
- Pro acceptance 3: Solid theoretical support for the proposed method.  
- Con acceptance 1: Accessibility concerns for readers without strong ML/statistics background.  
- Con acceptance 2: Insufficient guidance on hyperparameter selection and practical implementation details.  
- Con acceptance 3: Reliance on smoothness assumptions that could limit applicability.  
- Overall: Reviewer recommends acceptance but suggests addressing accessibility, hyperparameter discussion, and robustness to assumption violations.
