- Proposes a model-powered non-parametric CI test that reduces CI testing to binary classification using supervised models (GBTs, DNNs).  
- Converts CI testing into classification by generating samples approximating fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on closeness of generated samples to fCI in total variation distance.  
- Shows bootstrap samples are near i.i.d. and derives novel generalization (Rademacher complexity) bounds under near-independence.  
- Empirical algorithm CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real data and scales better with Z dimensionality.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and presents thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Relies on smoothness assumptions on the joint distribution that may not hold in practice.  
- Nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be computationally inefficient for large datasets due to nearest-neighbor computations.  
- Lacks a clear comparison to other classification-based CI testing methods (e.g., the method in [19]).  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel, well-motivated approach; novel theoretical contributions; strong empirical results.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: restrictive assumptions, possible scalability issues, missing comparisons to related classification-based approaches.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
