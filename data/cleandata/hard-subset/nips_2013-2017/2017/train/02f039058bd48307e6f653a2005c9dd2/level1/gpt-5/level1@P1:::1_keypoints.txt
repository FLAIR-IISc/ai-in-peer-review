- Summary: Proposes CCIT, a model-powered nonparametric CI test that reduces CI testing to binary classification by creating negative samples via kNN-based Y-swapping and training a classifier to distinguish true vs. generated data.  
- Theoretical contributions: Proves TV closeness of the bootstrap negatives to f_CI at rate O(n^{-1/dz}), provides generalization bounds for ERM under “near-independence,” and offers a bias-corrected test variant.  
- Empirical findings: CCIT outperforms kernel CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear data, scales to larger dz, and shows favorable ROC/AUC on flow cytometry tasks.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends classifier two-sample testing and independence permutation ideas to CI, improves computationally on KCIPT, and connects to dependent-data generalization and kNN density estimation literature.  
- Strength: Simple, computationally efficient nearest-neighbor bootstrap with rigorous TV bound under clear assumptions.  
- Strength: Novel generalization analysis for ERM classifiers with near-independent samples linking test power to dTV(f,f_CI).  
- Strength: Intuitive bias-correction that empirically improves finite-sample behavior.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance in high-dimensional Z, modular design, and public code release.  
- Weakness: TV approximation rate O(n^{-1/dz}) is very slow in worst case and theory relies on strong smoothness assumptions (bounded Hessian, Lipschitz).  
- Weakness: Generated negatives are not i.i.d.; no formal finite-sample level-α control or calibration guarantee for the bias-corrected test and τ choice is heuristic.  
- Weakness: Dependence on classifier choice and hyperparameters not systematically characterized, harming practical guidance.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental ablations (noise, n, k in kNN), runtime comparisons, and controlled reporting of empirical type-I/II error.  
- Weakness: Flow cytometry evaluation uses proxy ground truth and ad-hoc negatives, limiting real-world conclusions.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and algorithms are clear, but Assumption 1 (generalized curvature) needs more intuition and notation can be heavy; a schematic would help.  
- Originality: kNN-based bootstrap for f_CI and near-independence ERM bounds are novel and extend C2ST ideas to conditional testing.  
- Significance: Provides a practical, scalable CI testing framework useful for causal discovery; theory establishes a foundation despite conservative dz dependence.  
- Suggested improvements: Add permutation/wild bootstrap or cross-fitting for calibration, use k>1 or local smoothing for swaps, relax Assumption 1, and include ablations on n, classifier, dz, noise, and runtime.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide guidance or data-dependent selection for τ and report test power/size rather than only AUC.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, effective method with new theoretical tools and likely community interest.  
- Cons for acceptance: Lack of formal α-level control, conservative high-dimensional theory, and incomplete experimental evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on stronger calibration and expanded experiments.
