- Proposes converting Conditional Independence testing into a binary classification problem to leverage modern classifiers.
- Introduces a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution.
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrap, including bounds on total variation distance to the target distribution.
- Derives risk generalization bounds for classification under non-i.i.d., near-independent samples.
- Presents empirical results showing effectiveness on synthetic and real datasets.
