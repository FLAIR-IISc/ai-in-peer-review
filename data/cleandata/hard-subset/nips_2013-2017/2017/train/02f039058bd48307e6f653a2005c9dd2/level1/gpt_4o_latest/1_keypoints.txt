- Proposes reducing non-parametric continuous CI testing to binary classification using powerful classifiers and a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditional-independence distribution with TV-distance guarantees.

- Strength: Novel reduction enabling use of modern supervised learners in CI testing, suited for high-dimensional settings.

- Strength: Rigorous theoretical contributions, including total variation bounds for the bootstrap and generalization/Rademacher complexity analysis for near-independent samples.

- Strength: Empirical superiority and scalability of CCIT over kernel-based tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic and flow-cytometry data.

- Strength: Modular design allowing interchangeable classifiers and domain-specific adaptations.

- Weakness: Computational complexity concerns from nearest-neighbor bootstrap and repeated classification; runtime trade-offs not fully detailed.

- Weakness: Bias-correction mechanism adds complexity and its empirical impact is not clearly quantified.

- Weakness: Limited real-world evaluation beyond the flow-cytometry benchmark, reducing evidence of broad generalizability.

- Weakness: Comparisons are mostly to kernel methods; lacks evaluation against mutual-information or entropy-based CI tests.

- For acceptance: Methodological innovation, solid theoretical guarantees, and strong empirical results demonstrate significance and robustness.

- Against acceptance: Potential practical limitations due to computational overhead and a narrower empirical/comparative evaluation.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational/runtime analysis and broaden empirical comparisons.
