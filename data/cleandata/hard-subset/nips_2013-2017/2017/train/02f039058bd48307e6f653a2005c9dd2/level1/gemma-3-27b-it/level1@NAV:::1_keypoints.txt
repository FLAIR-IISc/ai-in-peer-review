- Frames nonparametric conditional independence testing as a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers (GBDTs, DNNs).  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditional independence distribution (fCI).  
- Proves the bootstrap’s closeness to fCI in total variation distance under smoothness assumptions.  
- Derives non-i.i.d. generalization bounds (Rademacher-type) for classification and translates them into CI testing error bounds.  
- Extends model-powered classification-based independence testing (Lopez-Paz et al. 2016) to the more challenging CI setting.  
- Contrasts with kernel-based methods (KCIT, RCIT) by avoiding expensive linear programming and providing theoretical guarantees.  
- Theoretical contribution: analysis of Rademacher-style bounds for near-independent samples is novel and valuable.  
- Strength: technically sound, well-written, with clear methodology and detailed algorithms.  
- Strength: innovative bootstrap with rigorous theory and empirical performance gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Weakness: relies on smoothness assumptions for f(y|z) and f(z) that may be restrictive in practice.  
- Weakness: limited empirical exploration of performance when assumptions are violated and incomplete discussion of better-than-worst-case empirical scaling.  
- Weakness: could strengthen connections to broader causal discovery literature.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—paper is a strong contribution and recommended for acceptance, with suggested expansions on assumption analysis and empirical validation.
