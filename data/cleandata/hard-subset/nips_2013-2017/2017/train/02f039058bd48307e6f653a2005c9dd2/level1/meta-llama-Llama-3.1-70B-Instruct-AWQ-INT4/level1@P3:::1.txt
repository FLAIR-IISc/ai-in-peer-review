**Summary**

This paper proposes a model-powered approach to conditional independence (CI) testing by converting it into a binary classification problem. The authors utilize a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples that mimic the conditionally independent distribution, which can then be used for classification. Theoretical guarantees are provided on the bootstrapped samples and the generalization bounds for the classification problem. The authors also present empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach, outperforming state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper presents a fresh perspective on CI testing by reducing it to a binary classification problem, allowing for the use of powerful supervised learning tools.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors provide rigorous theoretical analysis, including bounds on the variational distance between the bootstrapped samples and the conditionally independent distribution.
3. **Efficient algorithm**: The nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure is computationally efficient, making it a viable approach for high-dimensional data.
4. **Empirical performance**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The theoretical guarantees rely on smoothness assumptions on the joint distribution, which may not always hold in practice.
2. **Bias correction**: The authors acknowledge the need for bias correction in the classification step, but the proposed solution (Algorithm 3) may not be optimal.
3. **Comparison to state-of-the-art**: While the authors demonstrate the superiority of their approach, a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods would strengthen the paper.
4. **Interpretability**: The classification-based approach may make it challenging to interpret the results in terms of the underlying CI relationships.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel approach to CI testing, combining ideas from classification and bootstrap sampling. The authors' use of a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples that mimic the conditionally independent distribution is a significant contribution.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a fundamental problem in statistics and machine learning, with applications in various fields, including causal inference, feature selection, and Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach has the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in CI testing, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the theoretical guarantees and empirical results. However, some sections, such as the proof of Theorem 1, may benefit from additional clarification or simplification.

**Qualitative Evaluation**
