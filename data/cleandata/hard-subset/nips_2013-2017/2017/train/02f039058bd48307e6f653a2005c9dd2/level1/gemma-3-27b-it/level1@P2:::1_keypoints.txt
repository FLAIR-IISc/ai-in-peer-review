- Novel framing: casts conditional independence testing as a binary classification problem using bootstrapped fCI samples.  
- Core method: nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution, then train a classifier to distinguish true vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Theoretical contributions: provides bounds on bootstrap accuracy and generalization guarantees in a non-i.i.d. setting.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates performance gains over KCIT and RCIT on simulated and real datasets.  
- Motivation: addresses an important problem with applications in causal discovery and feature selection.  
- Classifier advantage: leveraging powerful classifiers is sensible, especially vs. kernel methods in higher dimensions.  
- NN bootstrap merit: the nearest-neighbor bootstrap is a clever technique with supporting theory.  
- Dense theory & assumptions: theoretical analysis is dense and relies on strong smoothness assumptions (Assumptions 1 & 2).  
- Sensitivity unclear: practical impact of violating those assumptions is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Missing constants: theoretical bounds omit constant factors, making tightness and practical relevance hard to judge.  
- Ablation missing: empirical section lacks detailed ablation (bootstrap vs classifier choice contributions unclear).  
- Limited baselines: comparisons are limited to KCIT and RCIT; more recent/relevant methods should be included.  
- Complexity analysis lacking: more detailed computational complexity analysis is needed despite claimed scaling benefits.  
- Notational/implementation clarity: notation could be improved; ambiguity around threshold τ and bias correction in Algorithm 3.  
- Prior-work connection: connection to [19] (independence testing/permutation dependence) should be made more explicit.  
- Recommendation: overall a significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address assumptions, ablation, comparisons, complexity, and notation.
