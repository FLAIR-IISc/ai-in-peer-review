- Proposes a novel model-powered approach that converts conditional independence (CI) testing into a binary classification problem.  
- Uses a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples that mimic the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and risk generalization bounds for classification under non-i.i.d. near-independent samples.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the approach and results easy to follow.  
- Empirical results indicate the proposed algorithm outperforms other state-of-the-art CI testing methods.  
- Strength: novel framing of CI testing as classification.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees for bootstrap procedure and generalization under non-i.i.d. data.  
- Strength: convincing empirical performance against baselines.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with CI and existing methods; lacks a brief introductory overview.  
- Weakness: theoretical results could use more detailed, accessible explanations for non-experts.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to a few datasets and should be broadened.
