- Novel model-powered approach that reduces non-parametric conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem.  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) using only joint samples.  
- Theorem 1: generated samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance under smoothness assumptions.  
- Theorem 2: provides generalization bounds for classification with near-independent (bootstrap) samples.  
- Leverages powerful classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees) rather than kernel-based tests.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art kernel methods (KCIT, RCIT), especially in high-dimensional Z.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis combined with comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Clear presentation, well-structured algorithms, and explicit connection/distinction from prior work (e.g., KCIPT).  
- Significant and original contribution: novel reduction and theoretically grounded nearest-neighbor bootstrap alternative to permutation methods.  
- High practical significance for causal inference and Bayesian network learning; scalable and leverages advances in classification.  
- Real-world utility demonstrated on flow-cytometry dataset for causal discovery.  
- Minor weakness: theoretical analysis focuses on worst-case guarantees; tighter bounds may be possible.  
- Minor weakness: connection to recent non-i.i.d. generalization literature (e.g., [21]) could be strengthened.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-executed paper with strong recommendation for acceptance.
