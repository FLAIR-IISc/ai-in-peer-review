**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a model-powered approach for Conditional Independence (CI) testing by converting it into a binary classification problem. The authors develop a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples that mimic the conditionally independent distribution. They provide theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and risk generalization bounds for the classification problem under non-i.i.d near-independent samples. The approach is evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing performance gains over previous methods.
