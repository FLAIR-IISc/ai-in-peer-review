- Proposal: Convert non-parametric CI testing for continuous variables into a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent samples.
- Method: CCIT leverages nearest-neighbor bootstrap plus powerful classifiers (gradient-boosted trees, deep neural networks).
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides bounds on total variation between bootstrapped and true CI distributions and generalization bounds for non-i.i.d near-independent samples (Theorems 1 & 2).
- Empirical performance: CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real datasets, with strong results on flow-cytometry.
- Strength — originality: Novel, significant contribution to CI testing.
- Strength — theory: Rigorous theoretical analysis underpins the method.
- Strength — experiments: Thorough empirical evaluation across settings.
- Weakness — complexity: Nearest-neighbor bootstrap may be computationally expensive for large datasets.
- Weakness — classifier dependence: Performance may vary with the choice of classifier.
- Weakness — assumptions: Theoretical results rely on assumptions that may not hold in practice.
- Accept arguments: Novel approach, solid theoretical foundation, and strong empirical evidence.
- Reject arguments: Concerns about computational cost, sensitivity to classifier choice, and restrictive assumptions.
- Suggested improvements: Detail computational complexity, evaluate different classifiers, and relax/clarify theoretical assumptions.
