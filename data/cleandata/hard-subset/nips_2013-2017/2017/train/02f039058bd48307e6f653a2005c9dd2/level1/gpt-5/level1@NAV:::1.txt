This paper proposes a model-powered nonparametric conditional independence (CI) test for continuous variables by reducing CI testing to binary classification. The key idea is to generate a reference sample approximating fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) via a nearest-neighbor (NN) bootstrap that swaps Y across nearest Z-neighbors, then train a classifier to distinguish original samples from bootstrap samples. If indistinguishable, accept H0: X ⟂ Y | Z; otherwise reject. The authors provide (i) a total variation bound showing one bootstrap sample is close to fCI under smoothness assumptions, with rate O(n^{-1/dz}); (ii) generalization bounds for empirical risk minimization under near-independent samples extending VC/Rademacher-style results; and (iii) empirical comparisons showing improved power over kernel CI tests KCIT [32] and RCIT [28], plus a case study on flow cytometry. This relates to classifier two-sample tests for (unconditional) independence [19], kernel-based CI testing [32, 28], and permutation-based CI testing via optimal matching [10]; and to generalization bounds for non-i.i.d processes [21].

Quality: The method is technically sound overall, with a clear pipeline and reasonable use of sample splitting. The TV closeness and near-i.i.d generalization results are interesting, though the TV rate suffers from a curse of dimensionality in dz and requires strong smoothness/Lipschitz conditions. A notable gap is the lack of finite-sample level control or calibrated p-values; the heuristic threshold τ≈1/√n does not guarantee Type I error at a prescribed α.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized. Assumption 1 (curvature matrix Ia) could use more intuition and simpler sufficient conditions; constants in Theorem 1 are dense. A short discussion on practical choices (distance metric, k>1 NN, approximate NN) would help.

Originality: The NN bootstrap for approximating fCI and the generalization analysis under near-independence are novel and differentiate from prior kernel and permutation-based approaches.

Significance: A flexible, classifier-based CI tester is of broad interest (causal discovery, Markov blankets), and the empirical gains are compelling. The lack of level control limits immediate use in pipelines requiring valid p-values, but the approach is promising and likely to spur follow-up work.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, modular reduction enabling modern classifiers
- New NN bootstrap with TV guarantees
- Generalization bounds beyond i.i.d
- Strong empirical performance; code released

Cons for acceptance:
- No calibrated test or p-values; Type I control unclear
- Strong smoothness assumptions; dz-dependent rates
- Sensitivity to NN design not analyzed; high-dim NN issues
- Limited baselines (no KCIPT [10], no CMI estimators)

Overall: A strong and timely contribution; I recommend acceptance, encouraging the authors to add null calibration (e.g., permutation within Z-strata or cross-fitting) and broader baselines.
