- Converts conditional independence testing into a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditionally independent-like samples.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees including bounds on variational distance between bootstrapped and true CI distributions and generalization bounds for the classifier.  
- Introduces a computationally efficient nearest-neighbor bootstrap algorithm suitable for high-dimensional data.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Theoretical results rely on smoothness assumptions on the joint distribution that may not hold in practice.  
- Classification step requires bias correction; the proposed Algorithm 3 may be suboptimal.  
- Comparisons to other state-of-the-art CI methods are not comprehensive enough.  
- Framing CI testing as classification can reduce interpretability of the inferred CI relationships.  
- Originality: novel combination of classification and nearest-neighbor bootstrap for CI testing; significant methodological contribution.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental problem with broad applications (causal inference, feature selection, Bayesian networks), especially in high dimensions.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and explains methods and results clearly, though some proofs (e.g., Theorem 1) need clearer exposition.  
- Quality rating: 8/10—strong theory and experiments but concerns about assumptions and bias correction.
