- Proposes converting non-parametric conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem using supervised models (GBDTs, DNNs).
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution.
- Provides theoretical guarantees on total variation distance between generated and true conditionally independent distributions.
- Derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independence, yielding CI test error bounds.
- Extensive empirical evaluation comparing to state-of-the-art methods, showing effectiveness across settings.
- Key strength: well-written, clearly presented, technically sound contributions with supporting theory and experiments.
- Concern: unclear whether alternative sample-generation or test components (e.g., permutation-based generation, kernel two-sample tests) could replace parts and how performance compares.
- Concern: may fail to detect weak conditional dependence when the bootstrapped distribution is already close to conditional independence in finite samples.
- Concern: potential asymmetry in x and y — unclear handling of causal direction (x→y vs y→x) when choosing which variable to sample.
- Serious practical issue: how to choose parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not addressed.
- Originality is limited: builds on existing classification-based testing and kernel methods rather than presenting a major departure.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — authors should discuss limitations, clarify τ selection, and add empirical studies addressing the concerns.
