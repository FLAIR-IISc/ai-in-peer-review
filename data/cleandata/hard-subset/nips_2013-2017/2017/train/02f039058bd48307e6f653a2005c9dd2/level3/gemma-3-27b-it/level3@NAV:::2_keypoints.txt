- Proposes framing non-parametric conditional independence testing as a binary classification problem using modern classifiers.
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution.
- Provides a theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) bounding total variation distance between bootstrap and true distributions.
- Derives generalization bounds for the non-i.i.d. setting using Rademacher complexity.
- Demonstrates empirical performance gains on simulated and real datasets.
- Positions itself within “model-powered” inference and contrasts with kernel-based methods that use expensive linear programs.
- Theorem linking smoothness assumptions to bootstrap quality is a notable theoretical contribution.
- Strengths: novel combination of ideas, strong theoretical rigor, and empirical validation on a relevant problem.
- Weaknesses: writing is dense and clarity/organization are poor, making the paper hard to follow.
- Lacks intuitive explanations for key theoretical points (e.g., the r0 = 0.1 example).
- Many definitions and theorems are given without sufficient motivation or practical interpretation.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions focusing on clearer presentation, added intuition, and explicit practical implications.
