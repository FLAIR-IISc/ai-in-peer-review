- Proposes CCIT: reduce non-parametric conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem distinguishing real joint samples from f_CI = f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).
- Introduces a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from the synthetic null distribution f_CI.
- Leverages modern high-dimensional classifiers (e.g., boosted trees), extending classifier-based two-sample testing to the conditional setting.
- Theoretical contribution: a smoothness assumption on conditional densities used to derive a total-variation bound between the true null and bootstrap distributions.
- Theorem 2: provides a novel generalization bound for classifier error under the non-i.i.d. nature of bootstrapped samples.
- Analysis connects smoothness, classifier empirical risk, and TV-distance; this is non-trivial and of independent interest.
- Empirical results show strong performance, outperforming prior methods in high-dimensional regimes.
- Main weakness: paper is hard to read—dense theoretical sections with little intuition or interpretation.
- Unclear practical implications: gap between bounds for the optimal (Bayes) classifier r0 and achievable empirical risk for a given classifier class G is not well-explained.
- Needs a more thorough discussion bridging theoretical Bayes-error bounds and performance of realistic/simple classifier families.
