- Proposes MOSM kernel by designing a positive-definite matrix of complex-valued spectral densities and applying the inverse Fourier transform via Cramér's Theorem.  
- MOSM kernel parameters map directly to interpretable spectral quantities: frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for channel pairs.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners despite complex proofs.  
- Theoretical results are strong and provide formal guarantees for the proposed method.  
- Method is presented as an extension of the "Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests" framework.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world data demonstrate MOSM’s potential for causal inference tasks.  
- Strength: effectively models rich cross-output relationships with a clear spectral interpretation.  
- Strength: competitive empirical performance on real-world datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks a discrete-time analog.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the choice of classification methods in evaluations.  
- Weakness: paper structure and clarity need improvement; contains redundant sections and typos.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and claims supported, but experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning details are insufficient.  
- Originality: novel MOGP modeling approach with a distinct spectral interpretation.  
- Significance: promising and broadly applicable tool for MOGPs, useful for causal inference and regression, and a clear introductory resource.
