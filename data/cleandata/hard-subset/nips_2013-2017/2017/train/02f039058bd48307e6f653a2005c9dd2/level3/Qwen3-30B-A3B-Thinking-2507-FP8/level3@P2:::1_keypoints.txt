- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification by distinguishing true joint samples from samples of f^CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap (Algorithm 1) that generates f^CI-like samples by swapping Y-coordinates of nearest neighbors in Z-space.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: Theorem 1 bounds total variation distance between bootstrapped samples and f^CI; Theorem 2 gives generalization bounds for classification with near-independent samples.
- Carefully addresses the non-i.i.d. nature of the bootstrapped samples in the theoretical analysis.
- Builds on and differs from prior CI tests (KCIT, RCIT, KCIPT) and classifier-based two-sample testing, offering computational efficiency over KCIPT's linear program.
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real datasets and scales better with Z dimensionality; shows a compelling real-world example on flow cytometry data.
- Paper is clearly written, with well-defined algorithms and effective illustrations of the CI-to-classification connection.
- Highlights significance for causal inference, Bayesian network learning, and feature selection, and benefits from advances in supervised learning.
- Weakness: lacks sufficient comparison to alternative f^CI sample-generation methods (e.g., permutation approaches or nearest-neighbor CMI estimation) and runtime/complexity trade-offs.
- Weakness: unclear performance for detecting weak conditional dependence; needs discussion of sample size vs. detectable dependence strength.
- Weakness: method is asymmetric in X and Y, raising concerns for causal discovery where directionality matters.
- Weakness: critical threshold parameter τ lacks guidance and justification; empirical tuning or theory is not provided.
- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size (missing in pseudocode).
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and technically solid contribution with important empirical advantages; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing comparisons, weak-dependence behavior, τ selection, and symmetry concerns.
