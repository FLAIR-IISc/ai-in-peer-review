- Method: reduce conditional independence testing to supervised binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to create synthetic fCI samples; high classifier accuracy → reject H0, with a bias-corrected variant subtracting a (y,z)-only classifier.  
- Theory: TV-distance guarantee between NN-bootstrap distribution and fCI under smoothness, with rate O(n−1/dz); derived ERM generalization bounds for near-independent samples; bias-correction analyzed.  
- Empirics: Outperforms kernel CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear benchmarks and flow cytometry data; scales well with dim(Z); code released.  
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and flexibility—leverages standard classifiers (trees, neural nets).  
- Strength: Novel theoretical contributions (TV control, near-i.i.d. generalization bounds, bias-corrected test).  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance and clear positioning versus prior CI methods and classifier two-sample testing.  
- Weakness: No finite-sample level control or principled way to set threshold τ or produce p-values/α-controlled tests.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters not systematically studied; decisions may vary with model/tuning.  
- Weakness: Curse of dimensionality in Z — theoretical rate n−1/dz and limited guidance on constants and when assumptions hold.  
- Weakness: Dependence structure of non-i.i.d. bootstrap samples and constants in generalization bounds are not fully transparent for practice.  
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues—redundancy, verbose algorithm descriptions, and a few typos.  
- Quality: Technically sound and nontrivial; careful analysis of NN-bootstrap and near-independent classification; empirical comparisons are fair.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and accessible, but proofs dense and some sections/algorithms should be tightened; more hyperparameter/τ guidance needed.  
- Originality: High — NN-bootstrap for CI with TV guarantees and near-independence generalization bounds is novel.  
- Significance: High practical relevance for causal discovery, graphical models, and feature selection; likely to be adopted and extended.  
- Questions for authors: adaptation to discrete/mixed variables; robustness/defaults for classifiers and hyperparameters; calibration scheme for p-values/τ selection; k-NN vs 1-NN variants; guidance on Z distance metrics.  
- Suggestions: add a calibration procedure (permutation/cross-fitting) for p-values, perform classifier sensitivity analysis and give defaults, discuss k-NN and metric choices, streamline redundant text, and expand on practical implications of the bounds.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, extensible method with strong empirical gains and novel, solid theory relevant to NeurIPS.  
- Cons for acceptance: lack of level calibration, deteriorating theoretical rates with dim(Z) and unverifiable smoothness, limited classifier-sensitivity analysis.  
- Minor typos: fix “classifier” (line 157) and “graphs” (line 280).
