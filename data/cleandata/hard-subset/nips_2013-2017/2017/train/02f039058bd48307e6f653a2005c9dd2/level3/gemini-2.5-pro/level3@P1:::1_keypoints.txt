- Core idea: Reframes conditional independence test X ⟂ Y | Z as a binary classification problem using original vs. bootstrapped samples.  
- NN-bootstrap: Generates null samples by nearest-neighbor matching in Z and swapping Y to approximate f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Classification stage: Trains a powerful classifier (e.g., XGBoost) to distinguish true samples from bootstrapped ones; indistinguishability implies conditional independence.  
- Theoretical support: Provides a total variation bound (Theorem 1) between bootstrap distribution and true CI distribution and generalization bounds for the classifier (Theorem 2).  
- Relation to prior work: Positions method relative to kernel-based (KCIT/RCIT) and permutation-based tests, highlighting NN-bootstrap and classifier use as key differences.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, intuitive, and includes clear pseudocode and explanations.  
- Empirical performance: CCIT outperforms state-of-the-art kernel methods in synthetic and real flow cytometry experiments, especially with high-dimensional Z.  
- Major concern — threshold τ: No principled method for selecting τ to control Type I error; choice τ=1/√n lacks clear justification or calibration to significance level.  
- Major concern — sensitivity to weak dependence: Bootstrap approximation error d_TV(ψ,f_CI)=O(1/n^{1/d_z}) may mask weak dependencies, harming test power and risking false negatives.  
- Modularity question: Need comparison of NN-bootstrap to alternatives (e.g., permutation sampling) and assessment of whether gains come from sample generation or classifier choice (could use MMD instead).  
- Asymmetry in X/Y: Algorithm resamples Y based on Z; unclear whether results are invariant to swapping X and Y or how choice affects applications like causal discovery.  
- Minor point — pseudocode: Empirical risk definitions lack normalization by dataset size and should be corrected for clarity.  
- Acceptance pros: Novel, powerful reduction to classification, strong empirical results, solid theoretical analysis, and clear writing.  
- Acceptance cons: Unresolved threshold selection, unclear power for weak dependencies, and missing comparisons with alternative modular components.
