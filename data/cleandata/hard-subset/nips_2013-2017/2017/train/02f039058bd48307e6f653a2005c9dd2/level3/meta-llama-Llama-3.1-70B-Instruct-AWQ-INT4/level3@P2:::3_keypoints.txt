- Reduces conditional independence testing to a binary classification problem using supervised models (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets).  
- Main technical contribution: a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that generates samples approximating the conditional product distribution.  
- Theoretical guarantees: generated samples are close in total variation and the authors provide generalization bounds that yield CI test error bounds.  
- Empirical results: significant improvements over prior methods on simulated and real datasets.  
- Writing: overall clear and practitioner-friendly, though theoretical proofs can be hard to follow.  
- Properly situates and connects to prior work (e.g., classifier two-sample tests).  
- Strength: innovative leverage of powerful classifiers for a classical statistical problem.  
- Strength: strong and nontrivial theoretical analysis extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting.  
- Strength: convincing empirical validation demonstrating practical value.  
- Weakness: presentation could be tightened (Section 1.1 redundant; Algorithm 2 overly verbose).  
- Weakness: minor typographical errors throughout the manuscript.  
- Weakness: no discussion of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings.  
- Weakness: does not address discrete-variable analogues or bootstrap behavior when Z is discrete.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address presentation/typos and encourage analysis of sensitivity.  
- Overall rating: 8/10, reviewer confidence: 8/10.
