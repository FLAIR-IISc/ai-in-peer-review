- Proposed CCIT: reduce conditional independence testing to binary classification via a 1-NN bootstrap that swaps Y from nearest Z neighbors and trains a classifier to distinguish real vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Theoretical guarantee: NN-bootstrap distribution is close to the conditional-independence law in total variation under smoothness/tail conditions, with rate O(n^{-1/dz}).  
- Learning theory: novel generalization bounds for ERM under the “near-independent” (non-i.i.d.) samples produced by the bootstrap, adapting Rademacher/VC arguments.  
- Empirical results: CCIT outperforms kernel CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear data and flow cytometry protein signaling data, showing better power at higher dz.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and complete; TV bound and near-independence bounds are carefully stated and relevant; experiments demonstrate practicality and scalability.  
- Main weakness: calibration of Type I error and p-value computation (choice of τ) is heuristic and needs principled, data-driven calibration.  
- Main weakness: sensitivity to classifier choice and capacity not fully explored; need ablations comparing classifiers.  
- Main weakness: limited analysis and diagnostics for weak/near-null dependence and finite-sample closeness, especially in high-dz regimes.  
- Major comment (modularity/ablations): recommend holding generator fixed to compare classifiers, and holding discriminator fixed to compare generators (e.g., KCIPT generator with classifier) to isolate source of gains; report computational costs vs KCIPT LP.  
- Major comment (weak dependence/power): analyze power under local (Pitman-type) alternatives, sweep effect sizes, and consider k-NN, multiple splits/aggregation, and representation learning for Z to mitigate high-dz issues.  
- Major comment (symmetry): procedure may be asymmetric in X and Y; test invariance vs swapping should be checked and consider combining both directions to enforce symmetry and reduce bias.  
- Major comment (τ calibration): recommend null calibration via repeated pipeline under synthetic nulls or label permutations to form empirical nulls/p-values; document and analyze τ sensitivity used in experiments.  
- Minor comment (algorithms): clarify normalization of empirical risk/loss (1/|D|) and whether statistics are error rate or AUC.  
- Minor comment (complexity): discuss approximate NN search and its effect on TV bounds and power.  
- Minor comment (assumptions): provide intuition/examples where smoothness/tail assumptions hold and practical diagnostics for users.  
- Minor comment (baselines): consider adding conditional mutual information estimators (e.g., KSG) as baselines.  
- Pros: clear modular reduction to classification, practical and scalable implementation, strong theoretical guarantees (TV and learning bounds), and strong empirical performance with real-data validation.  
- Cons: lack of principled τ calibration and p-values, incomplete ablations isolating generator vs discriminator effects, insufficient study of power in weak-signal/local alternatives, and potential finite-sample asymmetry between X and Y.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying τ calibration and adding modular ablations; novel and promising with solid theory and encouraging experiments.
