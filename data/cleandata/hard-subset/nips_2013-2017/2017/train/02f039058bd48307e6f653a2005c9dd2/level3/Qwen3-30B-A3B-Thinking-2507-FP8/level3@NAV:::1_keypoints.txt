- Proposes reducing conditional independence (CI) testing to a binary classification problem using a model-powered approach.
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap (Algorithm 1) to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution f^{CI}.
- Uses classifiers to distinguish observed samples from bootstrapped samples; near-random classifier performance implies conditional independence.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: TV-distance bound between bootstrapped samples and f^{CI} (Theorem 1).
- Provides generalization bounds for classification under near-independent samples (Theorem 2).
- Strong empirical validation showing significant gains over KCIT and RCIT, especially in high-dimensional settings.
- Important problem with clear significance, excellent clarity, and a novel combination of nearest-neighbor techniques with classification.
- Lacks comparison with alternative sample-generation methods (e.g., permutation-based approaches), leaving necessity of NN-bootstrap unverified.
- Potential inability to detect weak conditional dependence because NN-bootstrapped distribution may be too close to f^{CI} in finite samples.
- Method is asymmetric in X and Y (choice of which variable to sample), raising concerns about causal asymmetry.
- Parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 lacks justification and practical guidance for selection across datasets.
- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size for fair comparison.
- Overall technically sound and impactful, but requires clarification on τ selection, symmetry, and comparisons with alternative methods before acceptance.
