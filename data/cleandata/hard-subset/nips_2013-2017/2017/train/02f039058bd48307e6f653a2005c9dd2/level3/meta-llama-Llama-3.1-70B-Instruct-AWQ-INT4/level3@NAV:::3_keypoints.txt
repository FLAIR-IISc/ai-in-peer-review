- Proposes a novel CI testing approach that converts conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers (e.g., GBTs, DNNs).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: bootstrapped sample validity and risk generalization bounds for the classification formulation under non-i.i.d near-independent samples.  
- Presents extensive empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data demonstrating strong performance that outperforms existing CI tests.  
- Connects to prior work (e.g., "Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests") and situates the method within the literature.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners, with clear explanations of method and guarantees.  
- Theoretical proofs are complex and can be challenging to follow, potentially limiting accessibility for some readers.  
- Limited discussion of hyperparameter tuning for the classifiers used, leaving practical guidance insufficient.  
- Does not investigate sensitivity to the choice of classifier, which may affect practical performance and robustness.  
- Recommendation to provide more details on hyperparameter selection and its impact on results.  
- Recommendation to study sensitivity across classifiers and offer guidance for classifier selection.  
- Recommendation to consider extensions to handle discrete variables for broader applicability.  
- Overall assessment: significant and valuable contribution combining a novel approach, solid theory, and strong empirical evidence.
