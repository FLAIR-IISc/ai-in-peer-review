- Method: uses NN-bootstrap to generate f_CI proxies and reduces CI testing to a binary classification problem (observed vs NN-bootstrapped).
- Theory: provides total-variation bounds between NN-bootstrapped distribution and f_CI under smoothness and near-i.i.d. generalization bounds extending VC/Rademacher analyses.
- Empirics: shows improved performance over KCIT/RCIT and favorable scaling in high-dimensional Z.
- Strengths: technically sound, clear exposition, novel combination of NN-based conditional shuffling with a learning-reduction and near-i.i.d. analysis, practically relevant for causal discovery.
- Main concern: statistical calibration and detectability for weak dependencies need deeper analysis.
- Major comment (modularity): request ablations comparing NN-bootstrap to other conditional permutation/matching strategies and classifiers to kernel two-sample tests (MMD).
- Major comment (power): ask for power analyses or lower bounds as functions of n, d_z, and effect size; clarify whether the n^(−1/d_z) rate is the practical bottleneck.
- Major comment (symmetry): clarify whether the test is symmetric in X and Y|Z, consider symmetrizing by swapping both Y|Z and X|Z, and discuss behavior under asymmetric causal mechanisms.
- Major comment (threshold τ): choosing τ is critical—provide data-driven calibration methods (conditional permutations/knn-resampling, cross-fitting, or conformal calibration) to control type I error beyond heuristic τ≈1/√n.
- Minor: explicitly normalize empirical risks by sample size in Algorithms 2–3 and ensure consistent notation.
- Minor: discuss sensitivity to the Z-metric, choice of k in kNN, and feature scaling; include ablation studies.
- Acceptance summary: pros—clear, novel reduction with solid theory and strong empirical results; cons—insufficient guidance on τ/calibration, unclear power in weak-dependence regimes, unaddressed asymmetry, and missing modular ablations.
