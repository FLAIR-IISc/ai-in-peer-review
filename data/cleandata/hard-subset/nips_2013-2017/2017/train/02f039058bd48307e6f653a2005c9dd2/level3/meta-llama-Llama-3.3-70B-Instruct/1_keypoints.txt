- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output spectral mixture kernel for MOGPs built from complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem to ensure positive-definiteness.  
- MOSM models delays and phase differences between channels, making it more expressive than prior MOGP kernels.  
- Kernel parameters have clear spectral interpretations (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) enabling interpretable cross-channel relationships.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanations and a comprehensive review of existing MOGP models and limitations.  
- Experimental results show MOSM effectively models complex multi-output relationships and is competitive in mean absolute error against existing methods.  
- Theoretical analysis supports the claims; the approach is technically sound.  
- The method is original and potentially impactful for Gaussian processes and machine learning.  
- Assumes spectral densities are complex-valued, which may not hold in all practical scenarios.  
- MOSM can be computationally expensive to evaluate, raising scalability concerns for large datasets.  
- Further research needed to develop sparse implementations and to test MOSM in additional application domains.  
- Paper meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, expressiveness, solid experiments, comprehensive motivation, and potential field impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on complex-valued spectra, computational cost, and required follow-up work on sparsity and broader applicability.
