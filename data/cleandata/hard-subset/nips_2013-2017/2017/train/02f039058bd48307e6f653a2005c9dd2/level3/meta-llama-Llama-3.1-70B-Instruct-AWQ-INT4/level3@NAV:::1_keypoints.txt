- Converts CI testing into binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution.
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the bootstrap samples and risk generalization bounds for non-i.i.d near-independent samples.
- Empirical results reportedly outperform state-of-the-art on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Relies on smoothness assumptions for the joint distribution that may not hold in practice.
- Choice of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is under-motivated and lacks practical selection guidance.
- Lacks clear comparison with other classification-based CI methods (e.g., permutation-based kernel CI tests).
- Experiments are not fully reproducible because the code was not released.
- Minor: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.
- Minor: related work discussion is insufficient and should include clearer comparisons to similar methods.
- Overall: significant and well-motivated contribution suitable for a top-tier venue, contingent on addressing minor issues and expanding related-work comparisons.
