- Proposes a model-powered approach to non-parametric conditional independence (CI) testing by reducing CI testing to binary classification.  
- Introduces a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples approximating the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the total variation distance between generated samples and the true conditionally independent distribution.  
- Derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independence that translate into CI testing error bounds.  
- Presents a novel Rademacher-type analysis for classification with non-i.i.d., near-independent samples.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Technically sound with thorough analysis; claims supported by theoretical results and experiments.  
- Authors are careful and honest in evaluating both strengths and weaknesses.  
- Main limitation: assumes smoothness of the conditional density, which may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks clear interpretation of results; unclear how small error probability arises when classifier class is too simple.  
- High significance and potential impact on causal inference, feature selection, and Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Recommendation: accept.
