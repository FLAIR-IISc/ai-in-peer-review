- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification two-sample test using powerful classifiers (e.g., XGBoost, DNNs).  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from the conditional product distribution f_CI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z).  
- Key insight: CI testing reduces to distinguishing original samples from bootstrapped samples via classification.  
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 1): NN-bootstrap samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance.  
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 2): Generalization bounds for classification under near-independent, non-i.i.d. samples.  
- Empirical contribution: Extensive validation showing strong gains over kernel-based methods (KCIT, RCIT), especially as dim(Z) increases.  
- Strength: Conceptually elegant, practically useful for causal inference and structure learning, and leverages modern supervised learning tools.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical guarantees extending classifier two-sample tests to the conditional setting.  
- Weakness: Theoretical proofs are dense and hard to follow for non-expert readers, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—redundant sections (e.g., repeated Section 1.1) and Algorithm 2 needs a more concise description.  
- Open question: Is there a discrete-variable analog, and would the bootstrap be more powerful when Z is discrete?  
- Open question: Sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters is insufficiently explored (only XGBoost used).  
- Minor issues: A few typos noted (e.g., "classifier" line 157, "graphs" line 280).  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, highly original, significant empirical gains—recommended for acceptance.
