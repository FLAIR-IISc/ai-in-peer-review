- Proposes a novel non-parametric CI test by framing CI testing as binary classification and generating fCI samples via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: bounds on total variation between bootstrapped distribution and fCI (Theorem 1) and empirical risk bounds for the classifier.  
- Addresses an important problem in causal inference and motivates moving beyond kernel-based methods for high-dimensional data.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well-organized, with appropriate citation of related work.  
- Major concern 1: The choice of nearest-neighbor bootstrap is not sufficiently justified or compared to alternatives like permutation-based or kernel two-sample methods.  
- Major concern 2: The method’s ability to detect weak conditional dependence is unclear, especially if the bootstrap closely approximates fCI.  
- Major concern 3: The bootstrap procedure may introduce asymmetry (choice of sampling X or Y) that isn’t justified, problematic for causal inference.  
- Major concern 4: Parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is critical but lacks justification and sensitivity analysis or selection guidance.  
- Minor issue: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size to avoid scaling with dataset size.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, presents a novel approach, and supports claims with theoretical guarantees and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks comparison to alternative sample-generation methods, unclear handling of weak dependence and symmetry issues, and unexplained τ; recommends rejection pending revisions.
