- Proposal: Reduce conditional independence testing for continuous variables to binary classification by NN-bootstraping Y across 1-NN matches in Z and training a classifier to distinguish original vs bootstrapped samples.  
- Test decision: Fail to reject H0 if classifier performs at chance; uses classifiers like XGBoost or DNN.  
- Theory: Proves NN bootstrap produces samples close in total variation to fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) under smoothness assumptions.  
- Rate: Theorem 1 gives a TV bound with rate essentially O(n^(-1/dz)) up to constants and tail/mass terms.  
- ERM analysis: Derives generalization bounds for empirical risk minimization under near-independence (non-i.i.d.) samples from the NN bootstrap.  
- Bayes/approximation decomposition: Bounds expose Bayes risk r0=0.5(1−dTV(f,fCI)), an approximation gap η (best-in-class vs Bayes), VC/Rademacher terms, and near-independence errors.  
- Bias correction: Proposes CCITv2 that subtracts a YZ-only classifier loss from XYZ classifier loss to mitigate finite-sample bootstrap bias.  
- Empirics: Strong performance on synthetic and flow-cytometry data, often outperforming kernel CI tests (KCIT, RCIT); implementation available.  
- Practicality: Method is modular and computationally light relative to Z-aware permutation LPs, scalable with fast NN search.  
- Readability: Paper is dense and hard to parse; limited intuition linking assumptions → TV bounds → test size/power in finite samples.  
- Classifier choice concern: Little guidance on choosing classifier class G or ensuring approximation gap η is small; weak G can mask strong signals.  
- Calibration concern: Type I error control relies on unknown bias threshold τ; experiments use τ=1/√n without principled calibration or p-value recipe.  
- Assumptions: Smoothness (curvature/Hessian/tail) may be restrictive; lacks examples and robustness discussion when assumptions fail.  
- Dimension dependence: Theory degrades with dz (O(n^(-1/dz))) but paper doesn’t explain empirical robustness to high dz (e.g., manifold structure or adaptivity).  
- Technical quality: Development appears sound; TV analysis and near-i.i.d. generalization bounds are novel and carefully executed.  
- Clarity needs: Key quantities (r0, η, G) and practical implications are not sufficiently highlighted; requests an end-to-end schematic and practical guidance.  
- Originality: High — novel reduction to classification with theoretical TV guarantees and non-i.i.d. ERM analysis beyond kernel/permutation approaches.  
- Significance: Potentially high if calibration and practitioner guidance are strengthened; could become a scalable alternative to kernel CI tests.  
- Pros for acceptance: Elegant, practical reduction; non-trivial, useful theory; faster and simpler than KCIPT with strong empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient guidance on classifier adaptivity and calibration, weak treatment of Type I control, and dense presentation hindering reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide actionable guidance on choosing G and τ, diagnostics for η, Monte Carlo/null calibration options, ablations varying G, examples validating assumptions, and computational comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — strong novelty and results but needs clearer calibration, guidance, and exposition to maximize impact.
