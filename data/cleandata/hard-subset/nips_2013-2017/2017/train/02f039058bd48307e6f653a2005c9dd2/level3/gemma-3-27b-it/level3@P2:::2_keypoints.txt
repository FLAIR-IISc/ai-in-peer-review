- Proposes reducing conditional independence (CI) testing to binary classification using powerful classifiers (GBDT, DNN).  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the CI distribution f_CI.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation distance between bootstrapped and true CI distributions (Theorem 1).  
- Derives generalization bounds for classification in the resulting near-i.i.d. setting (Theorem 2).  
- Connects to prior work on classification-based independence testing and Rademacher complexity for non-i.i.d. processes.  
- Empirical results on simulated and real datasets (including flow cytometry) show improvements over KCIT/RCIT.  
- Method is modular and flexible, allowing any underlying classifier to be used.  
- Novel approach with solid theoretical foundation is a main contribution.  
- Paper is dense with heavy notation and challenging to follow; flow between sections is disjointed.  
- Lacks sufficient intuition about smoothness assumptions and practical meaning of error bounds.  
- Practical aspects like parameter tuning, computational cost, and sensitivity to threshold τ are under-discussed.  
- Comparisons are limited (mainly KCIT/RCIT); lacks evaluation against other recent causal/information-theoretic methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, add intuition (including the classifier-error example r0=0.1), and expand practical discussion.
