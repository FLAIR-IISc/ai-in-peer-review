- Converts non-parametric CI testing into a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to simulate conditionally independent samples.
- Provides theoretical guarantees bounding total variation distance between bootstrap-generated and true null distributions.
- Develops generalization bounds for the classification problem under near-independent samples.
- Introduces a novel smoothness assumption on the conditional density that underpins the analysis.
- Theorem 1 (combining smoothness, achievability of ĤR with error η, and classifier error gap) is a key, non-trivial result.
- Paper is well-motivated with a thorough related-work review and clearly explained main ideas.
- Empirical results are strong, showing the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art across settings.
- Paper is hard to read; technical sections are dense and need clearer exposition and interpretation.
- Unclear how a small error probability in condition 1.1(iii) can arise when classifier class G is too simple to distinguish f and f^CI; needs intuition or examples.
- Authors should provide more discussion and interpretation of theoretical results.
- Experimental setup lacks detail on choices and hyperparameters; more transparency needed.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and novel with high impact potential, but clarity and experimental detail need improvement.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
