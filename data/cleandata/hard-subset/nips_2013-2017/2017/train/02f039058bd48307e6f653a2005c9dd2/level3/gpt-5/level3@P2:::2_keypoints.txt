- Proposal: Reduce continuous conditional-independence testing to supervised binary classification using an efficient nearest-neighbor (NN) bootstrap to generate synthetic samples approximating fCI(x,y,z).  
- Test procedure: Train a classifier to distinguish real versus NN-bootstrapped samples; high accuracy rejects H0: X ⫫ Y | Z; bias-corrected variant subtracts a (Y,Z)-only baseline.  
- Theoretical contribution 1: Proved a TV bound between the NN-bootstrap distribution and fCI of order O(n−1/dz) under smoothness assumptions (Theorem 1).  
- Theoretical contribution 2: Provided ERM generalization bounds for near-independent samples induced by the bootstrap, adapting VC/Rademacher analyses (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical claim: With XGBoost, the method outperforms kernel-based CI tests (KCIT, RCIT) on synthetic post-nonlinear data and shows strong ROC AUC on flow cytometry data.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on classifier two-sample testing and kernel CI tests, offering a simpler, efficient NN-bootstrap alternative to KCIPT’s costly permutation LP, with a TV guarantee.  
- Strength: Conceptually clean, practical reduction that leverages modern classifiers and is easy to implement and scale.  
- Strength: Theorem 1’s TV guarantee is non-trivial and the NN construction is intuitive and well-motivated.  
- Strength: Near-independence generalization results bridge a theoretical gap and have broader applicability.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance, scalability, and open-source code availability.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues — core ideas, non-standard Assumption 1, and the roles of constants/G(ε) are hard to follow and need better intuition/examples.  
- Weakness: Power depends critically on classifier capacity G and the gap η to Bayes risk; conditions on G, capacity, and achievable η are unclear.  
- Weakness: No formal statistical calibration or p-values; fixed thresholds don’t guarantee level-α control and calibration/permutation is recommended.  
- Weakness: Curse of dimensionality in Z — TV rate O(n−1/dz) is pessimistic; need discussion of practical mitigations (k-NN, weighting, metric learning) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: Dependence-term transparency — the o(n(1/ε))/n term and ε trade-offs lack explicit, usable constants/rates.  
- Weakness: Empirics need more ablations (classifier choice, k, n, dz) and controlled Type I error studies; flow cytometry ground truth is uncertain.  
- Quality: Technically sound with careful proofs, though some smoothness/Lipschitz assumptions may be restrictive; experimental methodology is reasonable but lacks formal calibration.  
- Clarity recommendations: Add a high-level sketch for Theorem 1, concrete examples for Assumption 1, explicit constants/rates in Theorem 2, and clearer definitions for hat R and η.  
- Originality: While classification reductions exist, the NN bootstrap for CI with a TV guarantee plus near-independence generalization analysis is novel and non-trivial.  
- Significance: Practically useful and potentially impactful; scalable and likely to influence follow-up work; theoretical analysis broadly useful.  
- Suggestions: Add calibrated testing (permutation/MC) or empirical Type I control, analyze classifier capacity/training effects, explore k>1 NN and distance-weighting, provide examples for Assumption 1, and include ablations and runtime comparisons.  
- Acceptance pro: Novel NN-bootstrap with TV bound, valuable generalization bounds, and strong empirical results justify acceptance.  
- Acceptance con: Clarity deficits, missing formal calibration/Type I analysis, pessimistic dz rates, and underexplained link between Bayes gap and realizable classifiers argue against acceptance as-is.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on improved exposition and adding a calibrated testing procedure or empirical Type I error study.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
