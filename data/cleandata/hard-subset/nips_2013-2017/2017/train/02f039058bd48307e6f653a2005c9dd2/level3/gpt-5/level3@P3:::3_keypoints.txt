- Reduces conditional independence testing to supervised binary classification via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that swaps Y to create fCI-like samples.  
- Bootstrap samples are provably close to f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) in total variation at rate O(n^{-1/dz}) under smoothness assumptions.  
- Label original joint samples as class 1 and bootstrapped samples as class 0; train a classifier and reject H0 if performance exceeds chance.  
- Bias-corrected variant subtracts performance of a (Y,Z)-only classifier from the (X,Y,Z) classifier.  
- Theoretical contributions: TV approximation for the bootstrap, generalization bounds for ERM with near-independent samples, and classifier error concentrating near 0.5 under H0.  
- Empirically, CCIT outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic data with increasing dim(Z) and on flow cytometry data.  
- Builds on classifier two-sample tests and improves on KCIPT by using simpler nearest-neighbor matching with new TV guarantees.  
- Technical soundness is strong; assumptions are explicit and the curse of dimensionality in Z is acknowledged.  
- Claims are supported by theory and experiments, but explicit Type I error control at fixed α is not fully developed.  
- The paper is complete with proofs and released code.  
- Writing is generally clear and the algorithm is easy to implement for practitioners.  
- Proofs are dense and may be hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Presentation issues: Section 1.1 is redundant, Algorithm 2 can be more concise, and there are minor typos.  
- Originality: novel nearest-neighbor conditional bootstrap and original near-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  
- Significance: practically useful for causal discovery and unconfoundedness checks; modular pipeline can leverage classifier/metric-learning advances.  
- Question: For discrete or small-support Z, would within-stratum permutation variants give exact finite-sample validity, and can the theory cover mixed discrete/continuous Z?  
- Question: How to calibrate τ for a level-α test and can repeated NN bootstraps approximate the null distribution to report p-values?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to classifier choice, hyperparameters, and random seeds; request robustness checks or recommended defaults.  
- Question: Impact of k-NN vs 1-NN, metric choice/normalization for Z, and approximate NN search on Type I/II error; guidance for high-dimensional Z.  
- Question: Given O(n^{-1/dz}), provide practical rules of thumb for required n vs dz to maintain power.  
- Pros: simple, modular algorithm; strong and original theory; competitive empirical performance; clear prior-work connections; high practical relevance.  
- Cons: strong dependence on dim(Z); test calibration and p-value reporting not fully addressed; likely sensitivity to classifier/hyperparameters; some redundancy and hard-to-parse proofs.  
- Suggestion: add a calibration section to obtain p-values/level-α tests (double bootstrap, label permutation, cross-fitting).  
- Suggestion: include robustness analyses over classifiers, hyperparameters, and random seeds; report variability.  
- Suggestion: discuss discrete/mixed Z variants (within-stratum permutations) for exact finite-sample validity.  
- Suggestion: tighten Section 1.1 and streamline Algorithm 2.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and practically impactful contribution; recommend acceptance.
