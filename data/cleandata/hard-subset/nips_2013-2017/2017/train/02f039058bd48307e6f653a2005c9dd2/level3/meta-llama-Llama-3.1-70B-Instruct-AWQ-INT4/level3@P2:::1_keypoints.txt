- Proposes a novel CI-testing approach by converting CI testing into binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Introduces theoretical guarantees: bounds on total variation between bootstrapped and CI distributions and on empirical classifier risks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via extensive numerical experiments.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem with clear presentation and thorough analysis.  
- Major concern: lacks thorough comparison to existing alternatives (e.g., permutation-based generation, kernel two-sample tests) and whether components can be replaced.  
- Major concern: unclear how weak conditional dependence is detected when the bootstrapped distribution is already close to the CI distribution.  
- Major concern: method is asymmetric in x and y; unclear handling of causal asymmetry and how to choose which variable to sample.  
- Major concern: choice of parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3 is not justified and needs discussion or alternatives.  
- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.  
- Overall: promising and potentially strong contribution but requires significant revisions to address the major concerns; rating 7/10.
