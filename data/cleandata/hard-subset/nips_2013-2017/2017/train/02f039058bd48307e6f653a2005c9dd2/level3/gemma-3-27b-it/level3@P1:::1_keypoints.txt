- Proposes recasting conditional independence (CI) testing as a binary classification problem using powerful classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets).
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples from the CI distribution fCI(x,y,z)=f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) from joint samples.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: bounds on total variation distance between bootstrapped and target fCI distributions.
- Derives generalization bounds for the classifier and translates these into CI test error bounds.
- Empirical results on simulated and real datasets show performance gains over existing methods, including robustness on flow cytometry data.
- Situates work within prior nonparametric CI research, contrasting with kernel-based methods (e.g., KCIPT) and classification-based independence testing.
- Strength: novel and potentially impactful reduction of CI testing to classification with nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor compared to largely empirical prior work.
- Strength: convincing empirical performance and clear presentation with helpful algorithm descriptions.
- Major weakness: lacks thorough comparison of individual components (e.g., nearest-neighbor bootstrap vs. permutation-based strategies, classifier vs. kernel two-sample tests).
- Major weakness: potential poor sensitivity when X and Y are weakly dependent given Z, making distributions hard to distinguish.
- Major weakness: does not discuss symmetry/asymmetry between X and Y or implications for causal directionality and bootstrap choices.
- Major weakness: threshold parameter τ choice (suggested τ=1/sqrt(n)) seems arbitrary and needs principled selection guidance.
- Minor issue: empirical risk calculations in Algorithms 2 and 3 appear to lack normalization by sample size n.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, important problem, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient component comparisons, unclear behavior under weak dependence and causal asymmetry, and unclear threshold selection.
- Overall recommendation: accept pending revisions addressing component comparisons, weak-dependence sensitivity, causal asymmetry, and threshold selection; suggested score 7/10.
