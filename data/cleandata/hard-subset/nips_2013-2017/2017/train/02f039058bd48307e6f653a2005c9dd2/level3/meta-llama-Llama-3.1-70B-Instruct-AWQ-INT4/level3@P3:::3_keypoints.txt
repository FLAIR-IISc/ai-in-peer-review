- Proposes a novel reduction of non-parametric conditional independence testing to binary classification.  
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples close to the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on the closeness of bootstrapped samples.  
- Establishes generalization bounds for classification under near-independence, extending to non-i.i.d. settings.  
- Well-written and accessible, with clear explanations aimed at practitioners.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets, showing performance gains over existing CI tests.  
- Connects to prior work (e.g., "Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests") and situates the contribution.  
- Strengths: novel/efficient approach, solid theoretical guarantees, strong empirical results, and clarity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of classifier choices and hyperparameter effects.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed analysis of the bootstrap procedure's computational complexity.  
- Weakness: assumes fixed Z dimension; unclear performance in high-dimensional Z.  
- Minor issues: typos and presentation problems (redundant Section 1.1, Algorithm 2 description).  
- Originality: interesting and distinct approach though reduction-to-classification idea is not entirely new (score 8/10).  
- Significance: high potential impact for causal inference tasks like testing unconfoundedness (score 9/10).  
- Recommendation: accept; paper is technically sound and practically useful (Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10).
