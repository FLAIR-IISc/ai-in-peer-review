- Converts conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem, leveraging supervised models.  
- Proposes a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate samples approximating the conditional product distribution f_CI(x,y,z).  
- Theoretically shows bootstrap samples are close to f_CI in total variation distance.  
- Provides generalization/error bounds for classification under near-independence, translating to CI test error bounds.  
- Develops novel Rademacher-type classification analysis for non-i.i.d. near-independent samples.  
- Empirical results demonstrate superior performance versus state-of-the-art on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Applies the method to protein signaling (flow cytometry) data, showing practical applicability.  
- Assumptions on conditional density smoothness and the TV-distance bound are novel but need clearer explanation and justification.  
- Paper is hard to read and would benefit from improved clarity and interpretation of results.  
- Lacks discussion of limitations and potential future work.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
