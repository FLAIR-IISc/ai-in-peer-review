- Method: Converts conditional independence testing into a binary classification task using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate f^CI samples and rejects when the classifier outperforms random guessing.
- Theoretical contributions: Presents a nearest-neighbor bootstrap with TV bound d_TV = O(1/n^{1/dz}) (Theorem 1) and generalization bounds for classifiers on near-independent bootstrap samples (Theorem 2).
- Empirical results: Shows substantial improvements over KCIT and RCIT in high-dimensional Z settings, with validation on synthetic experiments and a flow-cytometry dataset.
- Strength: Provides comprehensive, well-motivated theoretical guarantees for both bootstrap quality and classifier generalization.
- Strength: Modular design allows leveraging advances in classification algorithms for CI testing.
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation demonstrating superior performance as Z dimension increases.
- Major weakness — component replacement: Fails to investigate substituting alternative sample-generation or testing components (e.g., permutation-based generation, kernel two-sample tests) and compare them.
- Major weakness — weak dependence detection: Does not analyze sensitivity when the bootstrapped distribution is close to f^CI in finite samples, potentially missing weak conditional dependence.
- Major weakness — causal asymmetry: Approach appears asymmetric in X and Y; the paper does not clarify handling of causal direction (X→Y vs Y→X).
- Major weakness — parameter selection: No practical guidance for choosing τ (uses τ = 1/√n upper bound) across different problem settings.
- Minor concern: Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size rather than reported as absolute counts.
- Overall assessment/recommendation: Strong, original contribution with solid theory and empirical support; recommend acceptance if authors address major concerns, especially limits on detecting weak dependence and practical τ selection.
