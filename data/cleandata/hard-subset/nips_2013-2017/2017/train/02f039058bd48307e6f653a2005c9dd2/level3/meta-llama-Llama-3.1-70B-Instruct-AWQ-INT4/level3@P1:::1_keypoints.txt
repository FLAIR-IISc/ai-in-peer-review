- Proposes converting conditional independence testing into a binary classification problem using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate CI-distributed samples.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the bootstrap procedure and generalization bounds for the classification step.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and the problem setting and algorithm are thoroughly described.
- Method scales to high-dimensional CI testing and shows significant improvement over previous methods.
- Extensive numerical experiments (synthetic and real) demonstrate better performance than KCIT and RCIT.
- Unclear whether alternative sample-generation or two-sample methods (e.g., permutation-based or kernel tests) can replace components and how that affects performance.
- Concern about detecting weak conditional dependence when bootstrapped distribution is close to the CI distribution in finite samples, risking false negatives.
- Method is asymmetric in x and y; handling of causal asymmetry (x→y vs y→x) and choice of which variable to sample is unclear.
- Serious concern about selecting the parameter τ in Algorithms 2 and 3; current guidance may be insufficient and needs deeper study.
- Minor issue: empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size.
- Quality: technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and convincing empirical results.
- Originality: novel CI-to-classification framing and use of nearest-neighbor bootstrap.
- Significance: substantial contribution to non-parametric CI testing, especially in high dimensions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, effective in high dimensions.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on nearest-neighbor bootstrap may limit applicability, asymmetry concerns, and unclear τ selection.
