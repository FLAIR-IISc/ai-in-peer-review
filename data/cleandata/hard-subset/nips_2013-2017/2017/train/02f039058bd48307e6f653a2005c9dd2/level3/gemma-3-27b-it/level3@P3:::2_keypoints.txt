- Frames non-parametric CI testing as a classification problem using powerful classifiers and a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to approximate the conditionally independent distribution.  
- Core contribution: novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure with theoretical total variation distance guarantees (Theorem 1).  
- Builds on model-powered inference literature and avoids expensive linear programs used in prior work.  
- Novelty: reduction to classification plus specific bootstrap algorithm and accompanying non-trivial theoretical analysis.  
- Theoretical rigor: substantial theoretical foundation, including generalization bounds in a non-i.i.d. setting.  
- Empirical validation: shows performance gains over existing methods on synthetic and real data, including a flow cytometry example.  
- Potential significance: promising approach that leverages modern classifiers for CI testing, useful in high-dimensional settings.  
- Clarity/readability: paper is dense, often hard to follow, and lacks intuitive exposition.  
- Lack of intuition/discussion: insufficient explanation of implications, particularly how small classifier error arises when the classifier class is limited (e.g., r0=0.1 example).  
- Algorithm 3’s practical benefit is unclear; bias-correction appears tacked on and not convincingly shown to help empirically.  
- Limited empirical comparison: experiments mainly against KCIT and RCIT; should include more recent/relevant baselines.  
- Theorem 1 assumptions (smoothness) are strong; need discussion of sensitivity to assumption violations and intuition about their meaning.  
- Section 1.1(iii) is hard to parse; requires clearer linkage between generalization bounds, CI error, and classifier complexity (VC dimension).  
- Algorithm 2 & 3 presentation has inconsistent notation and needs clearer, stepwise explanation.  
- Empirical results need more detail: parameter sensitivity analysis and confidence intervals for ROC AUCs.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel with high potential impact, but accessibility is hindered by poor exposition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clarity, more intuitive explanations, demonstrating Algorithm 3’s benefit, and broader empirical comparisons.
