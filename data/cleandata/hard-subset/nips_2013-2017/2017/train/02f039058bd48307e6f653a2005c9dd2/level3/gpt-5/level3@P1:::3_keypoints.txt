- Summary: Proposes a nonparametric CI test by reducing CI testing to binary classification using an NN bootstrap that approximates fCI and a classifier to distinguish joint vs. bootstrapped samples; includes a bias-corrected CCITv2.

- Theoretical contributions: TV-distance bounds showing NN bootstrap closeness to fCI, generalization bounds for ERM under near-i.i.d. dependence, and CI testing error guarantees.

- Empirical contributions: Outperforms KCIT/RCIT on synthetic post-nonlinear models and flow-cytometry checks, especially as dim(Z) increases.

- Strength: Clear reduction enabling use of powerful classifiers (XGBoost, deep nets) in high-d settings.

- Strength: Simple, computationally attractive NN bootstrap that avoids expensive global matching/LPs.

- Strength: Strong theory (TV control and novel Rademacher/VC-style bounds) and a principled bias correction (CCITv2).

- Strength: Extensive experiments including scaling with dim(Z) and a real-world application.

- Strength: Good integration and positioning relative to prior classifier two-sample and kernel CI tests.

- Weakness: No α-calibrated testing procedure presented; unclear how to choose threshold τ or the finite-sample null distribution of the accuracy gap.

- Weakness: Dependence on Z dimension—rates degrade with dz (e.g., O(n−1/dz)); need discussion of failure regimes and mitigations (k-NN, trimming, feature selection).

- Weakness: Strong smoothness/curvature assumptions and opaque constants (G(·)); practical robustness to heavy tails/multimodality is unclear.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters could flip decisions; needs sensitivity analysis and guidance or ensembling.

- Weakness: Handling of discrete or mixed Z not fully addressed; stratified permutation or hybrid bootstrap could be more appropriate.

- Weakness: Data efficiency concerns—three disjoint splits reduce power at small n; cross-fitting to reuse data should be discussed.

- Quality: Technically solid and careful; proofs are involved and some steps (curvature bound, low-density handling) are dense.

- Clarity: Well-written overall but has redundancy and could benefit from concise algorithm descriptions and high-level proof sketches.

- Originality: High—novel extension of classifier two-sample testing to conditional testing via efficient NN bootstrap and near-independence analysis.

- Significance: High—practical, scalable CI testing is valuable for causal discovery and feature selection; method scales well with modern learners.

- Suggestions: Provide α-calibration (permutation/repeated splits) and report Type-I/II at fixed α in experiments.

- Suggestions: Add guidance and robustness results across classifiers/hyperparameters; consider ensembling.

- Suggestions: Explore k-NN (k>1), local-kernel bootstrap variants, and sensitivity to distance metric for Z.

- Suggestions: Include runtime comparisons with KCIT/RCIT/KCIPT and discuss discrete/mixed Z extensions.

- Suggestions: Improve presentation by merging redundant sections, condensing Algorithm 2, and adding proof roadmaps.

- Minor issues: Fix typos (e.g., “classifier” capitalization around line ~157, “graphs” agreement around line ~280).

- Acceptance pro: Strong, simple idea with solid theory and clear empirical gains; scales with off-the-shelf classifiers.

- Acceptance con: Missing α-calibrated procedure in experiments and theoretical rates degrade in high-dimensional Z.

- Recommendation: Leaning accept, contingent on addressing calibration and robustness concerns.
