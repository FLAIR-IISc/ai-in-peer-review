- Proposes converting conditional independence testing for continuous variables into a binary classification problem.
- Leverages supervised models (gradient boosted trees, deep neural networks) for the classification-based test.
- Introduces a nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples approximating the conditional product distribution.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the bootstrap procedure and the resulting classification problem.
- Presents an efficient algorithm called Classifier-based Conditional Independence Test (CCIT).
- Demonstrates effectiveness through extensive synthetic experiments and real-world applications.
- Capable of tackling high-dimensional conditional independence testing and reportedly outperforms existing tests.
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and includes a thorough review connecting to classifier two-sample tests.
- Some presentation issues: Section 1.1 is redundant, Algorithm 2 could be more concise, and minor typos exist.
- Open question: how to adapt the method and bootstrap when Z (conditioning variable) is discrete.
- Open question: sensitivity of test outcomes to the choice of classifier and hyperparameter settings.
- Open question: extensions to more complex conditional independence structures (multiple variables, non-linear relations).
- Quality assessment: technically sound with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Clarity assessment: generally clear but marred by minor presentation issues.
- Originality assessment: novel approach combining classifiers with nearest-neighbor bootstrap and theoretical analysis.
- Significance assessment: significant practical implications, especially for high-dimensional problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, strong guarantees, thorough empirical validation, practical value.
- Arguments for rejection: minor presentation flaws and missing sensitivity/limitations analysis.
