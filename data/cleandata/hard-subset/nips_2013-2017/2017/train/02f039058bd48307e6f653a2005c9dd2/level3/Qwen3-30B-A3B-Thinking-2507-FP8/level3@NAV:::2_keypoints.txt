- Proposes reducing conditional independence testing to binary classification using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap to simulate fCI samples.
- Converts CI testing into a two-sample test between original and bootstrap-generated samples.
- Theoretically contributes a novel smoothness assumption on conditional densities.
- Proves a bound on total-variation distance between bootstrap samples and fCI (Theorem 1).
- Provides generalization bounds for classifiers trained on the near-independent (bootstrap) samples.
- Empirical results are strong, outperforming state-of-the-art on synthetic and real datasets.
- Technical analysis is sound and Theorem 1 is novel and insightful.
- The classification–CI testing connection is well-motivated with appropriate theoretical guarantees.
- Presentation suffers from significant clarity issues and dense, technical exposition.
- Unclear how theoretical error bounds translate to practice, especially in examples like r0=0.1 where classifier class G may be too simple to distinguish distributions.
- Fails to explain how small error probabilities can realistically occur, raising concerns about practical applicability.
- Would benefit from more intuitive interpretations, clearer implementation guidance, and detailed linkage between theory and empirical performance.
