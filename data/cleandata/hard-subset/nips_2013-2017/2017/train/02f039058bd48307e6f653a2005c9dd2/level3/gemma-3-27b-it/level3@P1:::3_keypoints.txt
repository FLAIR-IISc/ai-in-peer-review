- Novel CI testing approach: cast conditional independence as a binary classification between joint samples and approximated conditional-product samples.  
- Generates null-hypothesis samples via a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure.  
- Theoretical guarantees: bounds on total variation distance of generated samples and generalization bounds in the induced non-i.i.d. setting.  
- Empirical validation: outperforms KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Shows scalability with increasing dimension of Z and practical utility on flow cytometry data.  
- Builds on and is well-connected to classifier-based two-sample test literature.  
- Strength: novel reduction and bootstrap procedure represent a significant contribution.  
- Strength: rigorous and comprehensive theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 2 is verbose and should be presented more concisely for readability.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameter settings (e.g., reliance on XGBoost).  
- Weakness: Section 1.1 is redundant with the introduction.  
- Weakness: minor typos and no discussion of extension to discrete variables or potential power changes when Z is discrete.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve Algorithm 2 clarity, fix typos, and discuss classifier sensitivity and discrete-variable extensions.
