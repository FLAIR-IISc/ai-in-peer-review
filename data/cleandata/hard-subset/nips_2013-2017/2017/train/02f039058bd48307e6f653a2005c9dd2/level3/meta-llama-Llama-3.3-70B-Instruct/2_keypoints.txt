- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel using a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's Theorem.  
- MOSM yields interpretable channel relationships in terms of frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Paper is well-structured with thorough background and related work on MOGPs.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets (climate signals, heavy metal concentrations).  
- MOSM performs competitively, especially when inter-channel relationships are complex and non-linear.  
- Strength: novel, expressive kernel capable of capturing complex cross-channel relationships.  
- Strength: principled kernel construction via Cramér's Theorem.  
- Strength: experimental validation across several datasets demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: paper is dense with heavy notation and lengthy derivations, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: interpretation of results and real-world implications needs improvement.  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison and discussion of trade-offs with existing MOGP models.  
- Overall: significant contribution with potential value pending clarity, interpretation, and comparative-analysis revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, principled design, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: readability concerns, interpretability gaps, and limited comparative analysis.
