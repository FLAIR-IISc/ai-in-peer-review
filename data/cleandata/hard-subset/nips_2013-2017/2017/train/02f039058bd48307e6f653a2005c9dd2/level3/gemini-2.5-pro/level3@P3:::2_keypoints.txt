- Proposed method: reduces non-parametric CI testing to binary classification by generating approximate f_CI samples via a nearest-neighbor bootstrap (swap Y across nearest Z neighbors) and training a classifier to detect dependence.

- Strength — Originality & significance: novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap reduction allows leveraging powerful supervised learners (e.g., XGBoost, deep nets) and scales better with high-dimensional Z than kernel methods.

- Strength — Theoretical quality: introduces a smoothness assumption on f(y|z) and proves a bound on the total-variation distance between bootstrapped samples and f_CI, justifying the generation procedure.

- Strength — Generalization bounds: Theorem 2 connects classifier generalization error to d_TV(f, f_CI) and analyzes Rademacher complexity for the near-independent bootstrap samples.

- Strength — Empirical performance: CCIT substantially outperforms KCIT and RCIT in experiments, especially as conditioning dimension increases.

- Weakness — Clarity & readability: paper is technically dense; theoretical sections (notably the smoothness assumption) lack accessible intuition and practical interpretation.

- Weakness — Dependence on classifier expressivity: theoretical guarantees assume the function class G can approximate the Bayes classifier; lack of discussion on approximation error η and consequences when G is underpowered.

- Overall: a significant, theoretically grounded, and practically effective contribution to CI testing, but needs clearer exposition and more explicit treatment of model expressivity limitations.
