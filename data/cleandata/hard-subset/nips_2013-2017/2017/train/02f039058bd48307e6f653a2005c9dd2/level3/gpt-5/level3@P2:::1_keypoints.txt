- Proposal: CCIT reduces nonparametric conditional independence testing to binary classification by creating a NN-bootstrap synthetic-null (approx. fCI) and running a two-sample classifier test, with a bias-corrected variant comparing XYZ vs YZ classifiers.  
- Theory: Provides a TV-distance bound between NN-bootstrap and fCI (rate ≈ O(n^{-1/dz})) and generalization bounds for ERM classifiers under near-independence.  
- Empirics: Shows improved performance over KCIT and RCIT on synthetic and flow cytometry datasets; code released.  
- Strength: Clear, modular reduction leveraging classifiers; intuitive and practical bias-correction via YZ vs XYZ comparison.  
- Strength: Novel NN-bootstrap with rigorous TV guarantee and technically interesting near-i.i.d. generalization analysis.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance, especially for higher-dimensional Z, and solid writing/motivation.  
- Major weakness 1: Lack of ablation isolating generator vs discriminator — suggest CCIT+MMD and CIPT+classifier comparisons.  
- Major weakness 2: Unclear power for weak dependence — needs power vs signal/sample-size analysis and possible variance-reduction methods (multiple bootstraps, k-NN, conditional generative models).  
- Major weakness 3: Asymmetry in resampling Y vs X — clarify test symmetry and whether running both directions improves robustness or affects causal applications.  
- Major weakness 4: Ad hoc τ calibration (τ=1/√n) — recommend principled null calibration (Monte Carlo), paired-tests (binomial/McNemar), reporting p-values, and note sign error in algorithm threshold.  
- Minor: Ensure empirical risk normalization consistency in pseudocode, report computational complexity and wall-clock comparisons, and give intuition/examples for Assumption 1 and G(·) constants.  
- Quality: Technically solid and novel; theoretical results are new but show curse of dimensionality in dz.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized, with a few notational glitches.  
- Originality: High — new NN-bootstrap for approximating fCI and adapted generalization results.  
- Significance: Significant for causal discovery and graphical modeling practitioners; practical and flexible tester.  
- Pro-accept arguments: Elegant reduction, new theory (TV + generalization), strong empirical results and available code.  
- Contra-accept arguments: Need better threshold calibration, ablations to attribute gains, questions about symmetry and weak-signal power, and discussion of dz-rate mitigation.  
- Overall: Strong, timely contribution; addressing calibration and ablation concerns would strengthen the paper — reviewer leans toward acceptance.
