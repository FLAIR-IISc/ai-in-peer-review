- Proposes a novel non-parametric CI test for continuous X, Y given Z by reducing CI testing to a binary classification problem.  
- Generates approximate samples from the conditional product distribution f(x|z)f(y|z)f(z) using a nearest-neighbor bootstrap that swaps y-values between nearest z neighbors.  
- Trains a classifier to distinguish original samples (class 1) from bootstrap samples (class 0) and uses held-out accuracy as the test statistic (accuracy ≈ 0.5 ⇒ fail to reject CI).  
- Provides a strong theoretical guarantee that the bootstrap samples are close in total variation distance to the target conditional product distribution.  
- Develops new generalization bounds for classification with the non-i.i.d., “near-independent” samples, extending Rademacher complexity analysis.  
- Empirically outperforms kernel-based baselines (KCIT, RCIT), especially as the dimension of Z increases, on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Introduces a bias-corrected variant (CCITv2) to address practical biases in the test.  
- Extends prior classifier-based two-sample testing work to the more challenging conditional independence setting — a non-trivial and original contribution.  
- Well-written and accessible exposition of main ideas, though theoretical sections and proofs can be dense for non-experts.  
- Some presentation could be tightened (Section 1.1 repeats intro; Algorithm 2 description is verbose).  
- Minor typos noted: “classier” → “classifier” and “graph” → “graphs.”  
- High potential impact for causal discovery, feature selection, and graphical model learning due to scalability and practicality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, strong theoretical guarantees, state-of-the-art empirical performance, and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to choice of classifier and hyperparameters not explored; theoretical sections could be clearer.  
- Question 1 for authors: how sensitive is test decision/p-value to classifier choice and hyperparameter tuning, and could powerful classifiers detect bootstrap artifacts?  
- Question 2 for authors: can the method and guarantees be simplified or strengthened for discrete Z where nearest-neighbor reduces to exact strata matching?
