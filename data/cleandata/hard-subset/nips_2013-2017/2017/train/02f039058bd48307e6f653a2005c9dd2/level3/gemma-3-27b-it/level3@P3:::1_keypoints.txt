- Novel reduction of CI testing to a classification problem using nearest-neighbor bootstrapping — a fresh perspective.  
- Provides theoretical bounds on total variation between bootstrapped and true CI distributions and classifier generalization bounds.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear algorithm descriptions and notation.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data shows gains over state-of-the-art; code is available.  
- Choice of parameter τ is sensitive and lacks concrete guidelines or tuning strategy.  
- Potential difficulty detecting weak conditional dependence when the NN-bootstrapped distribution closely approximates f_CI.  
- Does not address symmetry/causal asymmetry in choosing x vs y for sampling or provide a default strategy.  
- Empirical risk in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by sample size — a minor implementation oversight.  
- Claims modularity but lacks in-depth comparisons to alternatives (e.g., permutation-based generation, kernel two-sample tests) and trade-off analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong theoretical and empirical contributions, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: τ sensitivity, limited treatment of weak dependencies and asymmetry, and insufficient comparisons.  
- Overall: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address parameter tuning, weak-dependence analysis, asymmetry guidance, normalization, and comparative evaluation.
