This paper proposes a novel approach to non-parametric Conditional Independence (CI) testing for continuous random variables. The method, called Model-Powered Conditional Independence Test, converts the CI testing problem into a binary classification problem, allowing it to leverage powerful classifiers like gradient-boosted trees and deep neural networks. The authors provide theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and risk generalization bounds for the classification problem under non-i.i.d near-independent samples.

The paper is well-written and accessible to practitioners, although the theoretical proofs can be challenging to follow. The authors acknowledge and connect to prior work, including "Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests." The empirical evaluation of the method on synthetic and real data shows promising results, outperforming existing CI tests.

Strengths of the paper:

1. Novel approach: The paper proposes a new approach to CI testing by converting it into a binary classification problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. Theoretical guarantees: The authors provide strong theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and risk generalization bounds for the classification problem.
3. Empirical evaluation: The paper presents an extensive empirical evaluation of the method on synthetic and real data, demonstrating its effectiveness.
4. Connection to prior work: The authors acknowledge and connect to prior work, including "Revisiting Classifier Two-Sample Tests," which shows that they have done their due diligence in understanding the existing literature.

Weaknesses of the paper:

1. Complexity of theoretical proofs: The theoretical proofs can be challenging to follow, which may limit the paper's accessibility to some readers.
2. Limited discussion of hyperparameter tuning: The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning for the classifier, which is an essential aspect of any machine learning approach.
3. Sensitivity to choice of classifier: The paper does not investigate the sensitivity of the method to the choice of classifier, which could be an important consideration in practice.

Originality and significance:

1. Novel approach: The paper proposes a new approach to CI testing, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. Theoretical guarantees: The authors provide strong theoretical guarantees on the bootstrapped samples and risk generalization bounds for the classification problem, which is a notable achievement.
3. Empirical evaluation: The paper presents an extensive empirical evaluation of the method on synthetic and real data, demonstrating its effectiveness and significance.

Recommendations:

1. Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning: The authors should provide a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning for the classifier, including the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the method's performance.
2. Investigate sensitivity to choice of classifier: The authors should investigate the sensitivity of the method to the choice of classifier and provide recommendations for selecting the most suitable classifier for a given problem.
