This paper proposes a model-powered approach to non-parametric Conditional Independence (CI) testing, reducing the problem to binary classification. The authors develop a novel nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure to generate samples close to the conditionally independent distribution, and provide theoretical guarantees on the total variational distance between the generated samples and the true conditionally independent distribution. They also derive generalization bounds for classification under near-independence, which translate into error bounds for CI testing.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of CI testing is an important one in statistics and machine learning. The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the problem and their approach, and the paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

The main technical contributions of the paper are the development of the nearest-neighbor bootstrap procedure and the derivation of the generalization bounds for classification under near-independence. The authors also provide a novel analysis of Rademacher type classification bounds in the presence of non-i.i.d near-independent samples.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

However, the paper is not without its limitations. One of the main limitations is that the paper assumes a certain level of smoothness of the conditional density, which may not always be the case in practice. Additionally, the paper does not provide a clear interpretation of the results, and it is unclear how the small error probability in the main theorem can occur when the classifier class is too simple to distinguish between the true distribution and the conditionally independent distribution.

The paper also provides a novel analysis of Rademacher type classification bounds in the presence of non-i.i.d near-independent samples.
