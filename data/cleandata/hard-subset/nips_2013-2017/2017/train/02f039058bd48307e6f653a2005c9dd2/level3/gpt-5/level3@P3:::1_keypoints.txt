- Summary: Proposes NN-bootstrap to generate pseudo-samples approximating fCI and reduces CI testing to a two-sample classification problem with TV and generalization guarantees and strong empirical performance.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on kernel CI tests, KCIPT permutation methods, and classifier two-sample tests, offering a simpler NN-bootstrap with explicit TV bounds and the ability to use modern classifiers.

- Strength: Clear modular pipeline separating conditional resampling and two-sample discrimination.

- Strength: Novel NN-bootstrap with explicit O(n^{-1/dz}) TV bounds and careful learning-theoretic analysis under near-i.i.d. sampling.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance, especially for larger dz, using fast NN search and off-the-shelf classifiers.

- Strength: Well-written, timely work addressing an important problem.

- Weakness (Modularity): Lacks head-to-head ablations comparing NN-bootstrap to alternative resampling schemes (KCIPT, k-NN k>1, residual regressors, conditional GAN/VAEs).

- Weakness (Modularity): Needs comparisons between classifier-based discriminators and kernel two-sample tests (e.g., MMD) on the same resampled data to clarify trade-offs.

- Weakness (Power vs weak dependence): The O(n^{-1/dz}) closeness may mask weak dependence; paper should translate Theorem 2 into a detectable-separation rate and test weak-signal experiments.

- Weakness (Power tuning): Should discuss and evaluate strategies to boost power (k>1 NN, local weighting, repeated resampling/aggregation, cross-fitting).

- Weakness (Symmetry): Procedure may be asymmetric in X and Y; need to check empirical invariance under swapping and consider symmetrized statistics (average or max).

- Weakness (Causal misuse): Provide guidance for causal workflows where choice of resampled variable could bias decisions.

- Weakness (Threshold τ): Setting τ=1/√n lacks size control; propose calibration strategies (empirical null via multiple resamples, label-permutation, cross-fitting) and consider deriving conservative τ(α,n,V).

- Additional (Train/test dependencies): Clarify whether using same U2,U3 leaks dependence between train and test and whether independent resampling or cross-fitting is needed.

- Additional (Curse of dimensionality): Discuss when practice beats the O(n^{-1/dz}) worst-case (intrinsic/manifold dimension, metric learning, representation learning).

- Additional (Complexity): Report time/memory scaling and k-NN indexing details vs RCIT/KCIT for large n and dz.

- Additional (Multiple testing): Recommend FDR/FWER control procedures for graph discovery and multiple tests.

- Minor: Ensure empirical risks are normalized by sample size and fix inconsistent notation in Algorithms 2 and 3.

- Minor: Add intuitive explanation and examples for Assumption 1 (curvature matrix).

- Evaluation (Quality): Technically solid with careful TV and generalization analyses; main missing pieces are calibration and modular ablations.

- Evaluation (Clarity): Very clear and well-organized; minor clarifications would aid reproducibility.

- Evaluation (Originality): High; NN-bootstrap with TV guarantees and near-i.i.d. generalization analysis are novel.

- Evaluation (Significance): Significant practical impact likely given modularity and empirical gains.

- Pros for acceptance: Elegant modular reduction, strong theory and practice, simple efficient algorithm with empirical gains.

- Cons for acceptance: No calibrated p-values/Type I control, limited ablations, unresolved symmetry and weak-signal power issues.

- Questions for authors: How to set τ for level-α control and report calibrated p-values?

- Questions for authors: Is the test symmetric in X and Y and what are results if resampling X|Z instead?

- Questions for authors: How does power scale with weak dependence and large dz; can you provide separation-rate guidance?

- Questions for authors: Can you compare to KCIPT-style permutations and MMD-based discrimination within the same pipeline?
