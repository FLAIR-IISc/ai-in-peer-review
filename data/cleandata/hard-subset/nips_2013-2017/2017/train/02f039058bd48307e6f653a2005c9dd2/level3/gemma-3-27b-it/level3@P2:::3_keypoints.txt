- Proposes a novel reduction of non-parametric conditional independence testing to binary classification using nearest-neighbor bootstrap to generate conditional product samples.  
- Leverages powerful classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees, deep nets) to make CI testing effective in high-dimensional Z.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees on bootstrapped samples in total variation distance (Theorem 1).  
- Derives generalization bounds for classification under near-independence, extending classifier two-sample results to the conditional setting (Theorem 2).  
- Method is conceptually simple and benefits from advances in machine learning.  
- Strong theoretical analysis that underpins the method’s validity and error behavior.  
- Empirical results show improved performance over state-of-the-art CI tests on synthetic and real datasets, especially as Z’s dimensionality increases.  
- Practical relevance to causal inference tasks (e.g., testing unconfoundedness).  
- Clear and well-written presentation overall.  
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to classifier choice and hyperparameters; this could affect accept/reject consistency.  
- Some presentation issues: Section 1.1 is redundant and Algorithm 2 could be more concise.  
- Theoretical proofs are dense and may be hard to follow; more intuitive explanations would improve accessibility.  
- Recommendation: significant contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address classifier sensitivity and presentation clarity; reviewer rates as strong accept.
