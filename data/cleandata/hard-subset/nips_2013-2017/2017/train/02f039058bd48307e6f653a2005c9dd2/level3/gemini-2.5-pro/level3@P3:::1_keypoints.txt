- Method: Reframes non-parametric conditional independence testing as binary classification by generating NN-bootstrap samples approximating f^{CI} and training a classifier to distinguish real vs bootstrapped data.  
- Novelty/Importance: Model-powered, modular reduction leverages state-of-the-art classifiers and is a novel approach for CI testing relevant to causal discovery.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains methodology with helpful diagrams and algorithms.  
- Theory: Provides non-trivial theoretical results—Theorem 1 bounds TV distance between bootstrapped and target distributions; Theorem 2 gives generalization bounds for classifiers under non-i.i.d. sampling.  
- Empirical results: Strong experimental performance on synthetic and real flow cytometry data, outperforming existing methods particularly as dim(Z) increases.  
- Component evaluation weakness: Paper evaluates NN-bootstrap and classification as a unit, making it unclear which component drives gains; needs comparisons to alternative generators and tests (e.g., permutation-based generator, MMD).  
- Weak-dependence power concern: Approximation error of NN-bootstrap may hide weak conditional dependencies; lacks analysis of test power vs. sample size n and dim(Z).  
- Asymmetry issue: NN-bootstrap swaps Y only, making the test asymmetric in X and Y; unclear if swapping X yields equivalent results or how to choose which variable to resample.  
- Thresholding problem: No principled procedure to set decision threshold τ to control Type I error (τ=1/√n is heuristic); requires a method (e.g., permutation/null distribution) to map significance level α to τ.  
- Minor notation: Empirical risk definitions in Algorithms 2 and 3 should be normalized by dataset sizes (|D_r|, |D_e|).  
- Overall: Promising and powerful framework but needs clearer component ablation, weak-dependence analysis, symmetry clarification, and a principled thresholding procedure to be a robust formal test.
