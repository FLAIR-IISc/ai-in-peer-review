- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based, differentiable estimator for the Wasserstein metric with an SGD learning algorithm.  
- The paper proves theoretical consistency and a clear connection between PMD and the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD claims practical advantages over MMD: no bandwidth tuning, stronger gradients, and ability to use smaller mini-batches.  
- Empirical results show faster convergence in domain adaptation and competitive performance in generative modeling.  
- The idea is novel in applying matching directly as a differentiable loss, though matching itself is not entirely new.  
- Paper is well-written and definitions and algorithms are clearly presented.  
- Main computational concern: exact matching via the Hungarian algorithm is O(N^3); scalability beyond N≈2000 is unclear despite proposed approximations.  
- Generative modeling results are underwhelming (blurry images); L1 distance blamed but alternatives and architectures need exploration.  
- Sensitivity analysis is limited; impact of different distance metrics beyond L1 should be investigated.  
- Related work discussion is insufficient; needs deeper comparison with other sample-based Wasserstein estimation methods.  
- Overall assessment: solid, theoretically grounded, promising alternative to MMD with empirical gains but notable computational and empirical gaps.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, demonstrated practical benefits over MMD, clear presentation, tackles an important ML problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational bottlenecks, unconvincing generative results, limited comparisons to other Wasserstein estimators.  
- Rating given: Strong Accept (7/10).  
- Recommendations: address computational cost (GPU/alternative matchers), expand generative experiments and distance choices, and broaden related-work comparisons and discussion of metric choice.
