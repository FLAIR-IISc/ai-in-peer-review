- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based divergence using minimum weight bipartite matching, claiming strong consistency with the Wasserstein metric and practical deep-learning benefits.
- Theoretical connection to empirical Wasserstein/matching is correct but not novel—optimal transport literature already establishes this.
- Main contribution is practical application of matching-based estimation to deep learning rather than a new theoretical result.

Strengths:
- Provides a correct theoretical foundation showing PMD is a consistent estimator of Wasserstein via Varadarajan’s theorem and OT stability.
- Empirically shows practical advantages: smaller mini-batch requirements (≈100 vs. MMD’s 1000) and more uniformly distributed gradients.
- Clear experimental comparisons in domain adaptation where PMD outperforms MMD on specific tasks and converges faster.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical gap: no convergence rate is provided and empirical rate is slow (N^{-0.23}), weakening the theoretical claim.
- Unfair MMD comparison: generative experiments use a fixed MMD bandwidth (σ=1) without showing results for optimally tuned bandwidths.
- Generative modeling underwhelms: PMD-generated SVHN/LFW images are blurry and worse than MMD, contradicting performance claims and not explained.
- Computational complexity: O(N^3) matching (Hungarian) is expensive and comparable to SGD time; no comparison to Sinkhorn or more efficient Wasserstein approximations.
- Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art Wasserstein methods (e.g., WGAN-GP, Sinkhorn), limiting empirical relevance.

Significance and originality:
- Idea is largely incremental since minimum-weight matching as an estimator of Wasserstein is standard; practical gains are modest and task-limited.

Recommendation:
- Weak reject.

Suggested improvements:
- Compare against WGAN-GP and efficient Wasserstein approximations (e.g., Sinkhorn).
- Thoroughly evaluate MMD with optimal bandwidth tuning for fair comparison.
- Address blurry image generation by using better distance metrics or improved architectures.
- Provide or analyze a convergence rate for PMD.
