- Introduces PMD, a sample-based estimator for the Wasserstein metric with theoretical consistency and an SGD training procedure, applied to domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Novelty: frames Wasserstein as a computationally tractable population matching discrepancy for gradient-based learning, tied to optimal transport.
- Theoretical foundation: proves strong consistency of PMD as an estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Practical advantages: no kernel bandwidth tuning, potentially stronger gradients, and compatibility with standard mini-batch sizes.
- Empirical validation: outperforms MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks and shows faster convergence in experiments.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear notation and explanations.
- Limited experimental scope: only two application areas on standard datasets, unclear performance on more complex or high-dimensional data.
- L1 distance choice is weakly justified and may be suboptimal for natural images.
- Matching algorithm complexity discussion is brief and lacks runtime benchmarks across dataset sizes and method variants.
- GPU acceleration claims lack concrete experimental evidence of speedup.
- Key omission: no direct comparison to Wasserstein GAN (WGAN), limiting contextualization in generative modeling.
- Overall: valuable contribution with theoretical and practical merits, but needs expanded experiments and comparisons to strengthen the paper.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel estimator, strong theory, practical benefits, and positive empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation, questionable distance choice, and missing state-of-the-art comparisons.
- Rating: Accept (Borderline) conditional on addressing weaknesses (notably adding WGAN comparison and expanding experiments).
