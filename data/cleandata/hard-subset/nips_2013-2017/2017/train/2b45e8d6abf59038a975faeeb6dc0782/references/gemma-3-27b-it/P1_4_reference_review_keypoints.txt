- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based estimator for the first Wasserstein metric, minimizing matching cost between sample sets.  
- Claims PMD is a consistent estimator and offers an alternative to MMD with stronger gradients and no bandwidth tuning.  
- Demonstrates empirical benefits of PMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling, including faster convergence and potential for smaller mini-batches.  
- Positions PMD within existing literature on MMD and Wasserstein distances, noting connections to optimal transport and empirical distribution consistency results.  
- Novel framing of Wasserstein as a population matching problem and emphasis on gradient properties relative to MMD.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis establishing strong consistency of PMD.  
- Highlights practical advantages: no kernel bandwidth selection, stronger/more stable gradients, and improved training efficiency.  
- Empirical validation shows PMD can outperform MMD on the tested tasks.  
- Paper is clearly written with organized notation and explanations.  
- Experiments are limited to two tasks, reducing empirical generality.  
- Exclusive use of L1 distance feels arbitrary; effects of other distances (e.g., Euclidean) are not explored.  
- Computational complexity concerns (Hungarian algorithm O(N^3)) are noted but not fully analyzed empirically; GPU parallelization discussion is cursory.  
- Comparison to WGAN is brief; lacks detailed analysis of scalability and trade-offs versus critic-based approaches.  
- The term “strongly consistent estimator” lacks an intuitive, lay explanation for some readers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically promising, addresses MMD limitations, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, insufficient justification for L1 choice, and incomplete computational complexity discussion.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with strong theory and encouraging results; suitable for acceptance with expanded experiments/discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
