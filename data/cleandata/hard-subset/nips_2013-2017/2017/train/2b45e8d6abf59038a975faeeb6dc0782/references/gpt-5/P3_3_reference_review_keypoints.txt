- PMD is defined as the average cost of a minimum-weight bipartite matching between two i.i.d. samples — equivalent to the empirical 1-Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance with uniform weights.
- Authors propose using PMD as a training objective for deep models, claiming advantages over MMD (no bandwidth, stronger gradients, small minibatch use via frozen matching).
- The paper proves consistency of PMD for W1 (via Varadarajan’s theorem) and stability of optimal transport, gives an approximate matching algorithm, and presents empirical results on domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Related work on entropic regularization/Sinkhorn distances and their GPU-friendly differentiable approximations is not discussed.
- Connections to energy/Cramér distance and unbiased/linear-time MMD estimators are relevant but omitted.
- Strength: Clear presentation of empirical W1 as a parameter-free alternative to MMD with intuitive gradient properties.
- Strength: Theoretical consistency results align with known empirical rates (observed ~N^-0.23 in 5D consistent with N^-1/d behavior).
- Strength: Practical SGD procedure reusing a fixed matching for several steps allows smaller inner minibatches and reduced compute.
- Strength: Empirical comparisons show faster convergence and sometimes better target accuracy on Office, and qualitatively less noisy generative samples than MMD.
- Strength: Useful empirical observations about MMD sensitivity (σ, batch size) and PMD robustness to N and small batch benefits.
- Weakness: Novelty/positioning is limited — PMD is simply empirical W1/EMD and the main theoretical claim is classical, with no new sample-complexity or SGD convergence analysis.
- Weakness: Computing matchings plus O(N^2) distance matrices is expensive and scalability beyond N≈1–4k is unclear; no comparison to Sinkhorn methods.
- Weakness: Gradient treatment ignores dependence of the argmin matching on parameters and provides no formal justification or conditions; frozen-matching heuristic introduces bias with no convergence/ablation analysis.
- Weakness: Claims about MMD requiring large minibatches and bandwidth sensitivity are overstated given unbiased/linear-time MMD and multi-scale kernels; comparisons are incomplete.
- Weakness: Energy/Cramér distance, a more appropriate baseline for “stronger gradients,” is not evaluated.
- Weakness: Domain adaptation experiments compare only to MMD, use fixed VGG features instead of end-to-end training, and lack stronger baselines (DANN, JAN, CMD) and clarity on hyperparameter tuning.
- Weakness: Generative modeling evaluation is qualitative, lacks standard metrics (Inception, FID), uses unequal training schedules, and omits comparisons to GAN/WGAN and feature-space distances.
- Weakness: Efficiency claims are undermined by CPU matching implementation, unstandardized wall-clock comparisons, and no stress tests on larger batches or higher-dimensional data.
- Weakness: Related work coverage is incomplete (Sinkhorn, differentiability of OT, sliced Wasserstein, linear-time MMD/energy estimators); “parameter-free” claim is too strong because ground metric and feature choices matter.
- Questions for authors: compare to entropic-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) in speed/stability/accuracy and discuss epsilon vs “parameter-free” claim.
- Questions for authors: justify ignoring gradients through matching formally (e.g., via Danskin) and report sensitivity to number of frozen inner steps K.
- Questions for authors: provide results against stronger domain-adaptation and generative baselines (DANN/JAN/CMD, GAN/WGAN) with standard metrics and matched wall-clock budgets.
- Questions for authors: report behavior on higher-dimensional data, larger N, and GPU-based matching implementations.
- Minor: Explicitly state PMD = empirical W1/EMD with uniform weights and cite standard transportation LPs.
- Minor: Cite known rate results (e.g., Fournier–Guillin) to support observed convergence rates and fix typos/formatting issues.
- Assessment: Quality is mixed (correct but standard theory, promising but limited experiments); originality is limited; significance could be moderate if repositioned as a practical recipe with stronger baselines and analysis.
- Recommendation: Borderline reject; paper could be improved by reframing as “empirical W1 with frozen-matching SGD,” adding Sinkhorn/energy baselines, fairer experiments, and analysis of the optimization heuristic.
