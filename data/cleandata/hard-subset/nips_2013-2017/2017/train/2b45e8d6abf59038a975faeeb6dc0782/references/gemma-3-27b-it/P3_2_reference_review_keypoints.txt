- Overall: Novel sample-based estimator (PMD) for Wasserstein metric; conceptually simple, theoretically grounded, recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem: Estimates statistical divergence from samples; addresses MMD issues like bandwidth sensitivity and vanishing gradients.  
- Method: PMD matches samples via bipartite matching; proven strong consistency and favorable gradient profile.  
- Empirics: Improvements shown in domain adaptation and generative modeling; comparisons with MMD are convincing.  
- Theoretical strength: Clear connection to Wasserstein metric and formal consistency proof.  
- Practical advantages: Eliminates bandwidth tuning, yields stronger gradients, works with smaller mini-batches.  
- Presentation: Well-written paper with useful pseudocode for the algorithm.  
- Validation: Empirical results (figures/tables) support claimed benefits.  
- Limitations acknowledged: Authors discuss computational cost and potential GPU speedups; note L1 distance limitations for images.  
- Incremental novelty: Builds on optimal transport/Wasserstein ideas; novelty is application-focused rather than a major theoretical breakthrough.  
- Computational complexity: Exact matching (Hungarian) is O(N^3) and may not scale; impact of approximate matching on accuracy needs clarification.  
- Ablation gap: Limited ablation — should test different distance metrics within PMD and compare to other Wasserstein estimators.  
- Potentially misleading claim: Saying PMD avoids a critic network vs. WGAN is subtle; matching acts as an implicit critic and should be clarified.  
- Suggested revision (Sec 3.1): Add context on advantages of Wasserstein metric over other divergences (e.g., KL).  
- Suggested revision (Sec 3.2): Provide more intuition and illustrative example for why PMD gradients are stronger.  
- Suggested revision (Sec 5.3): Report runtimes for exact vs. approximate matching across population sizes and quantify accuracy–speed trade-offs.  
- Suggested revision (Sec 5.4): Clarify whether the 5-D Gaussian example matches dimensionality of application experiments.  
- Minor edit: Simplify "N-PMD" notation to just "PMD" with N defined contextually.
