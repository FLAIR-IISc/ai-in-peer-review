- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a matching-based divergence linked to the Wasserstein metric for training deep models.

- Theoretical claim: PMD is a strongly consistent estimator and connected to optimal transport (Theorem 3.3).

- Practical advantage: PMD requires no kernel bandwidth, yields stronger gradients, and works with smaller mini-batches.

- Empirical results: PMD outperforms MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling (faster convergence and better performance).

- Presentation: paper is well-written, logically organized, with helpful figures and pseudocode.

- Major omission: insufficient acknowledgment and discussion of Earth Mover’s Distance (EMD)/Wasserstein literature and prior uses in vision.

- Theoretical gap: lacks finite-sample error bounds for PMD (only asymptotic consistency shown).

- Generative limitation: PMD with L1 distance produces blurry natural images and the paper doesn’t explore remedies.

- Scalability concern: reliance on Hungarian O(N^3) matching and only limited discussion/proposal of approximations without large-scale evidence.

- Quality evaluation: technically sound with convincing experiments, but weakened by theoretical and scalability gaps.

- Clarity evaluation: clear exposition, but the relation to standard EMD/Wasserstein should be made more explicit.

- Originality evaluation: novel application in deep learning, but not sufficiently differentiated from existing EMD-based approaches.

- Significance evaluation: practically relevant improvements over MMD, but tempered by scalability and image-quality issues.

- Recommendation 1: expand literature review to properly cite and contrast EMD/Wasserstein work in computer vision.

- Recommendation 2: provide finite-sample error bounds to strengthen theoretical contributions.

- Recommendation 3: address image blurriness in generative experiments (e.g., perceptual losses or alternative metrics).

- Recommendation 4: include scalability experiments or analyses on larger datasets/population sizes.

- Final recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying relation to prior work and adding theoretical/scalability material.

- Pro-accept arguments: solid OT-theory link, practical advantages over MMD, thorough experiments, clear presentation.

- Con-accept arguments: omission of EMD context, missing finite-sample bounds, unclear scalability, and unresolved blurry image issue.
