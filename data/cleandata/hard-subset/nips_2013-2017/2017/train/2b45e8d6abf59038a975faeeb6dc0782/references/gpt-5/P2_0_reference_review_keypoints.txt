- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), the average pairwise distance in a minimum-weight bipartite matching, as a sample-based differentiable surrogate for 1-Wasserstein.  
- Formalizes PMD, shows it equals empirical OT for equal weights, and proves strong consistency to Wasserstein-1 via standard weak convergence + OT stability results.  
- Practical training recipe: alternate between computing a (approximate or exact) large-population matching and performing several SGD steps using fixed assignments (no backprop through discrete matching).  
- Argues PMD provides stronger gradients than MMD, removes kernel bandwidth tuning, and allows smaller effective minibatches.  
- Theoretical contribution is largely standard; no finite-sample rates and the gradient-ignore-through-assignment argument is heuristic and could be formalized with Danskin/Clarke.  
- Algorithmic complexity: Hungarian O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) matching plus O(N^2D) distance computation is feasible for N~1e3–1e4 and modest D; empirical timings are plausible.  
- Alternating scheme is a reasonable engineering compromise but introduces staleness; paper lacks analysis of how assignment changes affect convergence.  
- Empirically competitive with MMD on Office domain adaptation and often converges faster; generative modeling results are mostly qualitative (MNIST/SVHN/LFW).  
- Missing key baselines: no comparison to entropic OT/Sinkhorn, OT-based DA (e.g., Courty et al.), WGAN/WGAN-GP, or modern DA methods (DANN/GRL, JAN, CORAL, CMD).  
- Quantitative generative metrics (FID, Inception Score) and comparisons to WGAN/GP or Sinkhorn are absent.  
- Some claims about MMD needing large minibatches and bandwidth sensitivity are overstated; linear-time MMD estimators and multi-kernel approaches mitigate these issues.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, with helpful pseudocode and figures.  
- Needs to state precise conditions where gradients ignore matching (invoke Danskin) and discuss nondifferentiability/assignment non-uniqueness.  
- Should detail the specific approximate matching algorithm used and its approximation guarantees.  
- Should clarify schedule for recomputing matchings vs inner-loop updates and sensitivity to that choice.  
- Originality is limited: PMD is a natural empirical OT instantiation; main novelty is the practical outer-loop matching + inner-loop SGD recipe.  
- Significance is modest: PMD is a useful parameter-free alternative to MMD when intermittent O(N^2) cost is acceptable and ground metric is meaningful, but lacks strong empirical or theoretical advances over SotA.  
- Pros: clear OT connection, practical training recipe, stronger/more uniform gradients than MMD, faster convergence, reduced hyperparameter tuning, useful empirical analyses.  
- Cons: limited novelty, missing crucial baselines and quantitative metrics, overstated MMD claims, heuristic treatment of ignoring assignment derivatives, scalability and pixel-L1 issues leading to blurry outputs.  
- Suggestions: compare to Sinkhorn OT, OT-based DA, and WGAN-GP with FID/IS; include linear-time and multi-kernel MMD baselines; ablate matching recomputation frequency and matching quality; formalize gradient argument via Danskin/Clarke; explore learned feature-space metrics.  
- Overall evaluation: moderate quality, good clarity, limited originality, modest significance; reviewer leans toward weak reject pending stronger baselines, formalization, and additional analyses.
