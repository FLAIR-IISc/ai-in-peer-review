- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based minimum-weight matching estimator consistent with the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Introduces an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective.  
- Claims PMD outperforms MMD: fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and workable with smaller mini-batches.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on domain adaptation and generative modeling, showing better performance and faster convergence than MMD.  
- Novel contribution: a new estimator for distribution distance with theoretical grounding.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric and comparing it to MMD.  
- Presents comprehensive experiments supporting PMD’s effectiveness over MMD.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, enabling reproducibility for expert readers.  
- Main limitation: higher computational complexity than MMD, potentially problematic for large-scale use.  
- Sensitivity remains to choice of distance metric and mini-batch size despite fewer hyper-parameters.  
- Limited scope of applications tested (only two deep learning tasks), leaving generality uncertain.  
- Overall technically sound with well-supported claims and honest evaluation of pros and cons.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication due to novelty, solid theory, and strong experimental results.
