- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): sample-based, differentiable objective equal to minimum-cost perfect matching between two equal-size samples using a ground distance.  
- Shows PMD equals empirical W1 (Earth Mover’s Distance) and is a strongly consistent estimator of population W1 via standard OT results.  
- Presents a training algorithm that alternates outer-loop matching on a “population” with inner-loop SGD on smaller mini-batches reusing the fixed matching.  
- Argues PMD avoids kernel bandwidth tuning and provides stronger, less-saturating gradients than MMD, enabling smaller mini-batches.  
- Empirical results on Office (domain adaptation) and MNIST/SVHN/LFW (generation) show better or comparable accuracy to MMD and faster convergence.  
- Discusses practical assignment solvers: exact Hungarian (O(N^3)) and approximate Drake–Hougardy (O(N^2)).  
- Relation to prior work: PMD is equivalent to well-known empirical W1/EMD from OT and CV literature (Rubner et al. 2000).  
- Notes consistency of empirical OT is known and finite-sample rates (e.g., Fournier & Guillin 2015) are missing from citations.  
- Major omission: no comparison or discussion of entropic-regularized OT (Sinkhorn), a differentiable, GPU-friendly O(N^2) alternative used in ML by 2017.  
- Strength: clear formulation linking PMD to empirical W1 and standard consistency argument.  
- Strength: practical, simple-to-implement training recipe with minimal hyperparameters beyond ground metric choice.  
- Strength: convincing analysis and empirical support for why MMD gradients saturate with RBF kernels.  
- Strength: empirical ablations show benefits of smaller batches and faster convergence; useful timing breakdowns.  
- Strength: applies to domain adaptation and generative modeling with competitive Office results and visually less noisy generation vs MMD-GMMN.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—PMD is essentially empirical W1 and theoretical claims follow from standard OT stability.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to Sinkhorn OT undermines positioning and experimental baselines.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines are narrow—key DA methods (DANN, Deep CORAL, JAN, CMD, OTDA) and generative baselines (WGAN, Improved GANs) are not compared.  
- Weakness: generative experiments lack quantitative metrics (Inception Score, FID) and broader comparisons.  
- Weakness: optimization subtlety—gradients don’t propagate through discrete matching and stability when matchings flip is not rigorously treated.  
- Weakness: scope limited to equal-mass one-to-one matchings; unbalanced, partial, or class-aware OT not explored.  
- Weakness: using L1 ground metric in pixel space causes blur; feature/perceptual distances are only lightly explored via an autoencoder.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-explained, but empirical claims weakened by missing baselines and lack of assignment-stability theory.  
- Clarity: overall clear writing and helpful figures; should explicitly name PMD as empirical W1 and clarify notation and unequal-sample cases.  
- Originality: conceptually modest—the novelty is mainly the training recipe and empirical demonstration against MMD.  
- Significance: potentially useful in practice for stronger gradients and smaller batches, but needs broader baselines/metrics to be convincing.  
- Essential suggestions: add Sinkhorn comparisons (runtime, stability, performance), include standard DA baselines, and report generative metrics (Inception/FID) vs WGANs.  
- Optional suggestions: analyze assignment stability, explore unbalanced/partial/class-conditional OT, evaluate perceptual/feature ground costs, and discuss scaling to larger N with GPU strategies.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, well-motivated objective with practical advantages, clear algorithm and ablations, solid W1 connection.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty vs existing empirical OT, missing key related work (Sinkhorn) and strong baselines, and underexplored non-differentiability of matching.  
- Overall: a solid, practically useful contribution with incremental novelty that would be substantially strengthened by Sinkhorn/DA/generative baselines and assignment-stability analysis.
