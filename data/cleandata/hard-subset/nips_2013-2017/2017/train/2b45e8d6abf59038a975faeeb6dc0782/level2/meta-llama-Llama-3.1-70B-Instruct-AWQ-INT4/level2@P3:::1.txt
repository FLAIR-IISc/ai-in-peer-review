This paper proposes a novel approach to estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, called Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). The authors define PMD as the minimum weight matching of sample populations from each distribution and show that it is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed approach. The technical details are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the properties of PMD, including its relationship with the Wasserstein metric and maximum mean discrepancy (MMD).

The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of PMD in two deep learning tasks: domain adaptation and generative modeling. The results show that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both tasks.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel approach to estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, which is a significant contribution to the field.

* The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of PMD in two deep learning tasks.

The weaknesses of the paper include:
