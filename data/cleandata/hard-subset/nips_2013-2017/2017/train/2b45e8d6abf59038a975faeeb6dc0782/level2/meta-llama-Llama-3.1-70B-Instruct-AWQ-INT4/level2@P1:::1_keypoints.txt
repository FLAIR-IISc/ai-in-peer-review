- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based distance between distributions.
- Shows PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Defines PMD via minimum-weight matching between sample populations.
- Claims advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch requirement.
- Provides a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.
- Evaluates PMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.
- Empirical results report PMD outperforming MMD in performance and convergence speed.
- Includes an empirical study of PMD’s approximation error and sensitivity to population and mini-batch size.
- Strength: novel contribution to distribution distance estimation from samples.
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of method, theory, and applications.
- Strength: strong experimental demonstration of effectiveness in two deep learning tasks.
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with the Wasserstein metric; background could be expanded.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and resource comparison to MMD.
- Weakness: limited discussion of PMD’s limitations and potential applications beyond the evaluated tasks.
- Evaluation: scores high on quality, originality, and significance.
- Evaluation: lower score on clarity due to assumed background knowledge.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, empirical effectiveness, and clear exposition.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient background on Wasserstein concepts, missing computational complexity analysis, and limited discussion of limitations/applicability.
