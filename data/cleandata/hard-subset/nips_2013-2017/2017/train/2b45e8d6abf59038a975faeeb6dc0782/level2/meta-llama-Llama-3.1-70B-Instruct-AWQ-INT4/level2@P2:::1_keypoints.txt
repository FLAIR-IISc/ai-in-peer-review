- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a new estimator of distance between distributions based on samples.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- PMD avoids kernel bandwidth hyper-parameters required by MMD.  
- PMD provides stronger gradients than MMD, aiding optimization.  
- PMD can operate with smaller mini-batch sizes for gradient-based training.  
- Paper is well-written with clear method exposition and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Establishes connections between PMD, MMD, and the Wasserstein metric.  
- Experiments show PMD's effectiveness in domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Main weakness: PMD requires solving a matching problem, which can be computationally expensive.  
- Authors offer an approximate matching algorithm to reduce computational cost.  
- Limited empirical validation: only two deep learning tasks evaluated, broader applicability unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong theoretical results, and promising experimental evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational complexity and limited experimental scope.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall rating 8/10, confidence 8/10.  
- Minor revision requests: add detailed computational-complexity comparisons with MMD, discuss limitations and future work, and provide more experiments across applications.
