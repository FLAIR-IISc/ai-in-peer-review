- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance estimator using minimum weight matching between sample populations.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and presentation of the approach.
- Technical development is sound with thorough analysis of PMD’s properties and links to Wasserstein and MMD.
- Experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.
- Strength: Novel and significant sample-based approach to estimating distribution distances.
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis of PMD and its relationships to existing metrics.
- Strength: Well-designed experiments demonstrating practical effectiveness in deep learning tasks.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed discussion of PMD’s limitations, including computational complexity and potential biases.
- Weakness: Insufficient insight into how the choice of distance metric in PMD affects results.
- Weakness: Needs more detailed empirical comparison with alternatives like WGAN and other MMD variants.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and expand comparisons/metric-choice discussion.
