- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based estimator for the Wasserstein metric using minimum weight matching.
- Motivates PMD as addressing MMD’s sensitivity to kernel bandwidth and problematic gradients in deep learning.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.
- Shows faster convergence and favorable gradient magnitude behavior compared to MMD.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with solid theoretical analysis linking PMD to Wasserstein.
- Thorough comparison with MMD highlighting advantages in hyperparameter tuning and potential for smaller mini-batches.
- Empirical evaluation includes convergence studies and gradient analyses that support theoretical claims.
- Lacks in-depth discussion of computational cost and scalability of the matching algorithm for large sample sizes.
- Needs detailed comparison between the proposed approximate matching algorithm and the exact Hungarian algorithm (performance vs accuracy).
- Choice of L1 distance metric for PMD is insufficiently justified; alternative metrics and their effects aren’t explored.
- Generative modeling claims rely on visual comparisons; quantitative metrics like FID or IS are missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically promising, clearly presented, and relevant to deep learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited complexity discussion, weak justification for L1, and insufficient quantitative generative evaluation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational scalability and sensitivity to distance-metric choice.
