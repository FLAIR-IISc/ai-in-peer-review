- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based estimator using minimum-weight bipartite matching as a differentiable training objective.  
- Proves strong consistency of PMD to the 1-Wasserstein distance using classical OT stability results.  
- Argues PMD remedies MMD drawbacks (bandwidth tuning, weak gradients, large batch needs) and provides stronger gradients.  
- Introduces a practical training algorithm (freeze matching, update parameters, sub-sample with fixed matching).  
- Empirical results show improvements over MMD in domain adaptation (Office) and faster convergence in generative modeling.  
- Generative samples are qualitatively better than MMD in pixel space but remain blurry and lack quantitative metrics (FID/IS).  
- Theoretical contribution is sound but limited since consistency of empirical assignment OT is standard in OT literature.  
- Training algorithm is plausible and effective empirically, but non-smooth objective and lack of gradient-through-matching analysis are concerns.  
- Experimental comparisons omit key baselines: Sinkhorn/entropic OT, stochastic OT methods, WGAN/WGAN-GP, and domain-adaptation methods like JAN/DANN.  
- Runtime and scalability are concerning due to O(N^2)–O(N^3) matching complexity; no GPU-parallel matching experiments reported.  
- Paper is clearly written with described algorithms and complexities, but reproducibility would benefit from released code and fuller hyperparameter details.  
- Originality is moderate: useful engineering (matching-freeze trick) but core discrepancy is essentially empirical Wasserstein-1/EMD.  
- Potential significance as a drop-in replacement for MMD if competitiveness with contemporary OT and GAN methods is demonstrated.  
- Pros: simple parameter-free objective, clear theoretical grounding, practical algorithm, and solid empirical improvements over MMD.  
- Cons: missing crucial baselines and metrics, scalability and GPU concerns, and still-blurry generative outputs without quantitative evaluation.  
- Suggestions: add Sinkhorn/stochastic OT and WGAN(-GP) baselines with FID/IS, compare against DANN/JAN/CMD and end-to-end CNN adaptation, report wall-clock vs. accuracy with GPU matching, analyze sensitivity to ground cost and finite-sample behavior, and release code with hyperparameters.  
- Overall: promising and practical idea but empirically and experimentally under-positioned; revision encouraged with stronger baselines and metrics.
