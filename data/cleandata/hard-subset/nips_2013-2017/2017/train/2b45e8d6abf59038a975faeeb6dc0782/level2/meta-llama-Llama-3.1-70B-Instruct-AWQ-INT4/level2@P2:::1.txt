This paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel method for estimating the distance between two distributions based on samples. The authors demonstrate that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and has several advantages over the popular Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) method. Specifically, PMD does not require a kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, has stronger gradients, and can use smaller mini-batch sizes for gradient-based optimization.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the connections to other discrepancies, such as MMD and the Wasserstein metric, are well-established. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PMD in two deep learning tasks: domain adaptation and generative modeling.
