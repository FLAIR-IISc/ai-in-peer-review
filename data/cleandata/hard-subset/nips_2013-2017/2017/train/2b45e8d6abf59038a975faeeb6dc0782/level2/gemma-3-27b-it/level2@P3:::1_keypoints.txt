- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a new distance metric between probability distributions, alternative to MMD.  
- Casts PMD as a minimum weight matching problem and proves strong consistency with the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Introduces an SGD algorithm for learning with PMD, claiming reduced mini-batch needs and faster convergence.  
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling, reporting improvements over MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with a convincing motivation highlighting MMD drawbacks (bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, large mini-batches).  
- PMD’s definition and theoretical connection to Wasserstein are rigorous and well-justified.  
- Experimental setup is adequate and includes useful ablation studies on population size and matching algorithms.  
- Theoretical analysis and discussion of gradient behavior are informative strengths.  
- Practical runtime analysis is insufficient given PMD’s computational costs (exact O(N^3), approximate O(N^2)); scalability concerns remain.  
- Limited comparison to other Wasserstein estimators beyond MMD weakens broader contextualization.  
- Sensitivity analysis is limited to a toy dataset; needs extension to the more complex datasets used in applications.  
- GPU acceleration discussion is brief and lacks empirical timing/speedup results.  
- Claim of stronger gradients is visually supported but lacks quantitative assessment of how much they improve convergence.  
- Overall: high-quality, novel, and theoretically grounded contribution with a strong accept recommendation, contingent on addressing the noted minor concerns.
