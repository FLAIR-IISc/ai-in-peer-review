The paper "Population Matching Discrepancy and Applications in Deep Learning" proposes a novel method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, called Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). The authors demonstrate that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and has several advantages over the popular Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) method. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, its theoretical foundations, and its applications in deep learning tasks.

The main idea of PMD is to define a distance between two distributions based on the minimum weight matching of sample populations from each distribution. The authors show that PMD has no kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, stronger gradients, and can use a smaller mini-batch size for gradient-based optimization compared to MMD. They also provide a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD.

The authors evaluate PMD on two deep learning tasks: domain adaptation and generative modeling. The experimental results demonstrate that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both tasks. The paper also provides an empirical study of the approximation error of PMD and its sensitivity to the population size and mini-batch size.
