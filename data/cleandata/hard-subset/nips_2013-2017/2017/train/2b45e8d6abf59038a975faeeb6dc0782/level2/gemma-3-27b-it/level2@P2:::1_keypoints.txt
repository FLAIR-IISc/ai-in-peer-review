- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for estimating distribution distance.
- Defines PMD as minimum-weight matching between samples and proves strong consistency to the first Wasserstein metric.
- Empirically claims PMD overcomes MMD drawbacks (bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, large mini-batch requirements) and improves domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Strong theoretical contribution via a consistency proof grounding PMD in statistical theory.
- Directly addresses practical limitations of MMD and provides a more stable, efficient estimator.
- Shows stronger gradients for PMD with visual evidence (Fig. 2b).
- Paper is well written and accessible even to readers new to optimal transport.
- Empirical validation demonstrates consistent improvements and includes ablation studies (Fig. 5).
- Discusses implementation trade-offs between exact and approximate matching and mini-batch effects.
- Comparison to WGAN is limited and lacks discussion of how PMD handles Lipschitz constraint issues.
- Uses L1 distance within PMD without fully justifying the choice or testing other metrics.
- Generative modeling results are visually promising but lack quantitative metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID).
- Computational cost analysis is brief; more detailed profiling across population sizes is needed.
- Parameter sensitivity exploration is incomplete, especially for domain adaptation settings.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method that addresses MMD limitations with clear empirical gains and good presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to related work (WGAN), missing quantitative generative evaluation, and inadequate computational and sensitivity analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, quantitative metrics, and deeper cost/sensitivity analyses.
