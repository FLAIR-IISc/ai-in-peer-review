**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a new method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, called Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). PMD is defined as the minimum weight matching of sample populations from each distribution and is shown to be a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. The authors also propose an algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD as an objective. The paper compares PMD with the popular Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) method and shows that PMD has several advantages, including fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch sizes. The authors apply PMD to two deep learning tasks, domain adaptation and generative modeling, and demonstrate that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novelty**: The paper proposes a new method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning.
2. **Theoretical Analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of PMD, including its relationship with the Wasserstein metric and its advantages over MMD.
3. **Experimental Results**: The paper presents comprehensive experimental results on two deep learning tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of PMD over MMD.
4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its advantages.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Computational Complexity**: The paper notes that the computational complexity of PMD is higher than that of MMD, which may be a limitation for large-scale applications.
2. **Sensitivity to Hyper-parameters**: Although PMD has fewer hyper-parameters than MMD, the paper notes that the choice of the distance metric and the mini-batch size can affect the performance of PMD.
3. **Limited Applications**: The paper only applies PMD to two deep learning tasks, and it is unclear how well PMD will perform on other tasks.
