The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which is a significant advantage as it allows for training neural network-based models. 

A thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning, is provided by the authors. They also acknowledge the limitations of their method, noting that exact calculation has cubic complexity, and propose an approximation with quadratic complexity. The empirical results show that this approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well-structured and well-written, with proper citations to previous work where applicable. The theoretical results appear to be correct, and the experimental analysis is adequate, with comparisons of PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, as well as comparisons to MMD for generative modeling.

One potential avenue for future exploration is the application of PMD to generative modeling in domains with multiple modes. It is unclear whether PMD remains effective when the sample size is smaller than the number of modes. 

Overall, this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
