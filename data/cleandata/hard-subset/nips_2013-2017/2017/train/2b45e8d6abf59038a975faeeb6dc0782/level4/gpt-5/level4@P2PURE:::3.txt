The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and show that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, making it suitable for training neural network–based models. The authors thoroughly compare PMD to MMD, which is the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They also discuss a drawback of their method: exact computation has cubic complexity. To address this, they propose a quadratic-time approximation and show empirically that it does not substantially degrade statistical performance. The paper is well structured and well written, with appropriate references to prior work. The theoretical results appear correct. The experimental analysis is adequate: they compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modeling. I would have liked to see the method applied to generative modeling in domains with many modes, and I wonder whether PMD works when N is smaller than the number of modes. Overall, this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
