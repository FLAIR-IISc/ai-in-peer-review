- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sampled Wasserstein (earth mover’s) alternative to MMD.  
- Claims PMD gives stronger gradients, works with smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters.  
- Targets two MMD weaknesses: weak gradients and need for large mini-batches.  
- Empirical results convincingly show PMD enables training with small mini-batches.  
- The stronger-gradient claim is inconclusive because MMD should be compared using optimal or a range of scale (sigma) settings.  
- The hyperparameter reduction is unconvincing: PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Estimating reliable distance metrics in high dimensions is challenging, so PMD may face similar issues as MMD.  
- Suggests comparing PMD against MMD with established bandwidth heuristics to treat MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free.  
- Summary: PMD improves fast, small-batch training but its reliance on a distance metric may limit effectiveness on high-dimensional data.
