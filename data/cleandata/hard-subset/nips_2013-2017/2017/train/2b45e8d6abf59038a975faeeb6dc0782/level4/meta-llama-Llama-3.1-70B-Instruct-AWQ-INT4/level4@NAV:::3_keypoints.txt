- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and shows it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple, differentiable, and suitable for training neural-network models.  
- Provides a comprehensive empirical and conceptual comparison between PMD and MMD.  
- Notes exact PMD computation has cubic complexity but proposes a quadratic-cost approximation.  
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and properly cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear accurate and the experimental analysis is sufficient.  
- Evaluations include domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons against MMD and other methods.  
- Suggests further exploration of PMD for generative modeling in multimodal domains, especially when sample size is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall, PMD is presented as a valuable distribution-comparison method with solid theoretical and empirical support.
