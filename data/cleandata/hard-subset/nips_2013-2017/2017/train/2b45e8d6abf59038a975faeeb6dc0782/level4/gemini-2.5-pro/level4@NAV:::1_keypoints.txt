- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly in O(N^3) or approximately in O(N^2).
- Experimental study of this PMD-based Wasserstein computation is valuable, but generated image quality lags standard Wasserstein GANs.
- PMD likely requires large minibatch sizes (N) to accurately estimate Wasserstein distance for diverse or multimodal distributions.
- With insufficient N, the optimization can shift away from the true data distribution, producing lower-entropy / less diverse samples (e.g., SVHN shows overrepresentation of '8').
- Minor typographical issue: "usally" (Line 141) should be "usually".
- Post-rebuttal: authors added experiments but should more clearly discuss PMD limitations compared to MMD.
- MMD works well with very small batch sizes and has an unbiased estimator suitable for SGD, enabling convergence with small minibatches.
- PMD lacks a known unbiased estimator for its gradient, which is a significant drawback for training with small minibatches.
