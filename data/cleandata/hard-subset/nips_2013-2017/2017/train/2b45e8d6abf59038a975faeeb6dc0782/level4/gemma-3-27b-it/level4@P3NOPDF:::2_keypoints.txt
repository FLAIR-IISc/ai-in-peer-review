- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching in generative model training.
- Shows PMD is a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.
- Claims PMD offers improved gradient behavior and supports much smaller mini-batch sizes, reducing computational cost.
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate PMD’s benefit with smaller mini-batches.
- MMD is known to suffer vanishing gradients and large batch requirements, motivating PMD.
- The claim that PMD yields stronger gradients is not fully substantiated without thorough MMD bandwidth (σ) tuning.
- MMD performance is highly sensitive to bandwidth σ, so comparisons should include optimal σ or a σ sweep.
- The assertion that PMD has fewer hyperparameters is weak because PMD depends on a distance metric that may have its own hyperparameters.
- Reliable distance estimation in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, potentially limiting PMD’s applicability.
- PMD may therefore face similar distance-metric selection issues as MMD.
- Paper would be strengthened by comparisons to MMD using established bandwidth-selection heuristics (treating MMD as near hyperparameter-free).
- Suggested improvements: expand gradient comparisons with optimized MMD bandwidths, clarify hyperparameters of the chosen distance metric and their impact, and discuss/mitigate challenges for high-dimensional data.
