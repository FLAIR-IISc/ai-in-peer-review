- Defines Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed via exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.
- Experimental evaluation of the Wasserstein computation is interesting, but generated images do not match the quality of Wasserstein GANs.
- PMD likely requires large minibatch sizes (N) to accurately estimate distances for diverse, multimodal distributions; issues expected even for mixtures of Gaussians, especially when learning variances.
- Insufficient N can lead optimization to suboptimal, lower-entropy solutions; SVHN samples show limited diversity with digit '8' overrepresented.
- PMD lacks a known unbiased estimator for its gradient, which may hinder convergence with small minibatches.
- MMD performs well with very small batch sizes (e.g., batch_size=2) using the unbiased estimator and SGD, so the paper should more clearly contrast PMD's limitations versus MMD.
