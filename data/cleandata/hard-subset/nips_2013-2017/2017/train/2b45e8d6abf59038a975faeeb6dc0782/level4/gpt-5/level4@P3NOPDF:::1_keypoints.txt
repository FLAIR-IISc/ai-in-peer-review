- Proposal: PMD defines a distributional discrepancy as the Wasserstein distance computed on minibatches, using exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) solvers, with empirical evaluations.  
- Strength: Leveraging minibatch-level optimal transport as a training signal is interesting and timely given OT-based learning trends.  
- Strength: Experiments provide some practical assessment of minibatch Wasserstein training behavior.  
- Weakness: Minibatch Wasserstein likely requires large batch sizes; small N yields a biased objective that can encourage low-entropy solutions and mode dropping.  
- Weakness: PMD may struggle to match multimodal targets (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians, variances) unless N is large; a toy study could reveal failure modes.  
- Weakness: Exact O(N^3) OT per minibatch limits scalability; the paper should detail trade-offs, accuracy loss, and practical batch sizes for the O(N^2) approximation.  
- Weakness: Generated images are worse than WGAN; stronger quantitative comparisons (FID/Inception), ablations over N, and training curves are needed.  
- Request: Clearly articulate PMD’s limitations versus MMD in small-batch regimes, emphasizing MMD’s unbiased estimators and PMD’s apparent lack of unbiased gradient estimators.  
- Request: Add discussion and analysis of sample complexity for PMD to approximate population Wasserstein as data complexity (modes, variance, dimensionality) increases.  
- Request: Clarify practical compute/memory costs and report wall-clock times for exact and approximate solvers at experimental batch sizes.  
- Request/Experiment: Provide experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures to test recovery of means/variances as N varies.  
- Request/Experiment: Show sensitivity to batch size on real datasets, tracking diversity metrics and mode coverage.  
- Request/Experiment: Compare training stability and gradient variance versus MMD and WGAN (multiple seeds, variance measures).  
- Experiments quality note: Include quantitative metrics, multiple random seeds, and ablations over batch size and solver choice; ensure fair architecture/training budgets across baselines.  
- Clarity: Emphasize distinction between population Wasserstein and its minibatch approximation, state PMD’s bias, define the exact training objective, and report any variance-reduction techniques.  
- Minor: Typo at Line 141: “usally” → “usually.”  
- Overall: Promising direction but currently appears to require large batches and lacks unbiased gradients; empirical evidence shows reduced diversity, and PMD as presented is not yet competitive with MMD or WGAN.
