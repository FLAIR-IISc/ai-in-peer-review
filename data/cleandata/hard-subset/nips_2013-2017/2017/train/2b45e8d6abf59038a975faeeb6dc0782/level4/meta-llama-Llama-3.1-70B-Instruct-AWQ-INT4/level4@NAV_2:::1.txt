The paper "Population Matching Discrepancy and Applications in Deep Learning" proposes a novel method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples. The authors introduce Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), which is defined as the minimum weight matching between two random populations from the distributions. They show that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and propose a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD.

The authors relate their work to previous research in the field, including the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) and the Wasserstein metric. They highlight the advantages of PMD over MMD, including fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch sizes for gradient-based optimization.

The paper presents several applications of PMD, including domain adaptation and generative modeling. The experimental results demonstrate that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both tasks.
