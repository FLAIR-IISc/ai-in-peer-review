- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed via exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation of this minibatch-Wasserstein computation is interesting, though generated image quality lags state-of-the-art (e.g., WGANs).  
- Weakness: Accurate estimation of Wasserstein for diverse, multimodal distributions appears to require large minibatch size N, likely problematic even for simple mixture-of-Gaussians tasks.  
- Weakness: If N is too small, the optimization can have global minima that do not match the true distribution, producing lower-entropy learned distributions (e.g., SVHN samples with low diversity and overrepresented '8').  
- Minor: Typo on line 141: "usally" should be "usually".  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors should clarify PMD limitations versus MMD — MMD can work with very small batches due to an unbiased SGD estimator, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, a critical distinction.
