- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, suitable for neural-network training.  
- Thorough empirical and conceptual comparison to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Highlights MMD drawbacks: sensitive kernel bandwidth, weak gradients, and large mini-batch requirements.  
- PMD advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth, stronger gradients, and works with smaller mini-batches.  
- Paper is well-structured, properly referenced, with seemingly correct theoretical results.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate and demonstrates PMD’s effectiveness in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Limitation: experiments lack evaluation on multi-modal generative domains, especially when mode count exceeds sample size.  
- Computational drawback: exact PMD calculation has cubic complexity.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective method with thorough analysis and promising empirical results.  
- Rejection arguments: limited multi-modal generative experiments and potential scalability concerns due to cubic complexity.
