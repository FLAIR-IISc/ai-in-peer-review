The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and show it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which enables training neural network–based models.

They provide a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. The paper clearly discusses computational drawbacks—exact computation is cubic in the sample size—but proposes a quadratic-time approximation and empirically demonstrates that this approximation does not substantially degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well structured and well written, with appropriate references to prior work. The theoretical results appear sound. The experimental evaluation is adequate: PMD is compared to MMD and other approaches in domain adaptation, and to MMD in generative modeling.

One limitation is the lack of experiments on generative modeling in highly multimodal settings. It would be valuable to see how PMD performs when the number of samples N is smaller than the number of modes, and whether it maintains robustness and sample efficiency under such conditions.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
