- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Shows PMD is a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.  
- Claims advantages: stronger gradients, smaller mini-batch capability, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Addresses MMD’s problems (weak gradients, large mini-batch needs) and positions PMD as a solution for generative model training.  
- Empirical results convincingly support the small mini-batch claim.  
- The stronger-gradients claim is less conclusive; fair comparison requires tuning MMD’s scale parameter σ (optimal or range).  
- The fewer-hyperparameters claim is questionable because PMD relies on a distance metric that may introduce comparable hyperparameters.  
- Obtaining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face similar issues to MMD.  
- Established heuristics for MMD bandwidth selection should be compared against; MMD can be treated as relatively hyperparameter-free with those heuristics.  
- Overall: PMD is a worthwhile improvement enabling faster training with small batches, but its reliance on a distance metric is a potential limitation, especially in high dimensions.
