This paper introduces PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrates that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.

The proposed estimator is conceptually straightforward and differentiable, which enables its use in training neural network-based models. The authors conduct a thorough comparison of PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), one of the most established population-based divergences in machine learning.

The authors acknowledge the method's drawbacks, specifically the cubic complexity of an exact calculation. They propose a quadratic-time approximation to address this and show empirically that this approach does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately references prior work. The theoretical analysis appears to be sound. The experimental evaluation is adequate, comparing PMD against MMD and other baselines for domain adaptation and against MMD for generative modeling.

One suggestion for improvement would be to evaluate the method on generative modeling tasks with highly multi-modal distributions. It would be interesting to explore how PMD performs when the sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.

In conclusion, this is a strong paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
