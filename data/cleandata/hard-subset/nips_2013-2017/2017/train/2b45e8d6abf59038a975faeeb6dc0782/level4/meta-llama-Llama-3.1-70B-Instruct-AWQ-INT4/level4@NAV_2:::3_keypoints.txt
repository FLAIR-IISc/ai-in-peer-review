- Proposal of Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based estimator of distance between distributions.  
- PMD defined via minimum-weight matching between two random sample populations.  
- The authors prove PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein (W1) metric.  
- PMD advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth hyperparameter, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch requirements.  
- Demonstrated applications to domain adaptation and generative modeling with superior performance and faster convergence vs. MMD.  
- Thorough time-complexity analysis showing approximated matching for PMD can be significantly accelerated on GPU.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides adequate literature references.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and experimental analysis is thorough.  
- Concern: lack of experiments on generative tasks with many modes and unclear behavior when sample size N < number of modes.  
- Suggestion: include more analysis of PMD limitations (e.g., multi-modal domains).  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details on the approximated matching algorithm and its GPU acceleration.  
- Suggestion: include additional comparisons with other distribution-distance estimation methods beyond MMD.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant contribution with promising applicability.
