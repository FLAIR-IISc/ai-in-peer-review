- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, allowing integration with neural network models.  
- Thorough empirical and conceptual comparison between PMD and MMD, positioning PMD as a viable alternative.  
- Acknowledges cubic complexity of exact PMD computation and proposes a quadratic approximation.  
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation preserves statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, theoretically sound, and properly cites prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation includes domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons validating PMD’s effectiveness.  
- Suggests future work on PMD for generative modeling with multiple modes and small sample regimes.  
- Concludes PMD is a useful, compelling method likely to interest ML researchers.
