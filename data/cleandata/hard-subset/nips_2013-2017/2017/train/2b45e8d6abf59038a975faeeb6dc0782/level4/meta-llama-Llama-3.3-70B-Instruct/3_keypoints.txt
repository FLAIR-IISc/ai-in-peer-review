- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence measure and proves its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, making it suitable for neural network training.  
- Provides a comprehensive empirical and conceptual comparison between PMD and MMD.  
- Notes exact computation is cubic, but proposes a quadratic approximation that empirically preserves statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and properly situates itself with prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental evaluation is sufficient, covering domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons.  
- Limitations: needs testing on multimodal generative tasks and when sample size is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with a potentially very useful contribution.
