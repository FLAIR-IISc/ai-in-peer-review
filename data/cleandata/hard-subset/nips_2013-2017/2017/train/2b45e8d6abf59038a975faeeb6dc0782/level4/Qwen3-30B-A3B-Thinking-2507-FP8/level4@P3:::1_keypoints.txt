- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance estimator and learning objective.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric via optimal transport theory.  
- Proposes an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD and applies it to domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Claims practical advantages over MMD: less hyperparameter tuning, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch requirements.  
- Empirically shows faster convergence and better performance than MMD in presented tasks.  
- Situates work relative to MMD and WGAN, highlighting theoretical connection to Wasserstein and avoiding a separate critic network.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous and well-motivated, with clear ties to optimal transport.  
- Empirical results support PMD’s practical advantages and show stronger gradients than MMD.  
- Thorough implementation details and discussion of exact vs. approximate matching trade-offs.  
- Well-organized paper with clear motivation, theory, and experiments.  

Weaknesses:
- Dependence on large population sizes N not adequately addressed; concerns about capturing complex multimodal distributions (e.g., SVHN diversity issues).  
- Inaccurate characterization of MMD’s mini-batch capability—unbiased MMD estimators can use very small batches.  
- Insufficient analysis of computational trade-offs; O(N^3) matching complexity and overall training cost vs. MMD unclear.  
- Generative results (SVHN, LFW) are blurry and lack comparison to state-of-the-art generative models.  

Significance:
- Addresses real limitations of MMD and offers a promising alternative, but impact is limited by unproven effectiveness on complex multimodal data and lack of broader generative comparisons.  

Clarity:
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematics, logical presentation, and informative figures.  

Quality:
- Sound theoretical analysis and generally convincing experiments, but needs more rigorous testing on multimodal distributions and comparison to unbiased MMD.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions: clarify relation to unbiased MMD estimators, provide stronger experiments on multimodal distributions, and better quantify computational trade-offs.
