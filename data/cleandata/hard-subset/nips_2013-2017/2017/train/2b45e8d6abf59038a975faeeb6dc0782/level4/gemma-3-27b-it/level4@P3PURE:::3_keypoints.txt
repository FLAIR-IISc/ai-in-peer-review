- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and shows it consistently estimates the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling neural-network-based training.  
- Provides a thorough empirical and conceptual comparison of PMD to MMD.  
- Acknowledges exact PMD has cubic computational complexity.  
- Proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation and shows empirically it does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear sound.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate: domain adaptation comparisons and generative-modeling comparisons to MMD.  
- Reviewer requests additional evaluation on generative tasks with many modes, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper introducing a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
