- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance; estimator is simple and differentiable, enabling neural network training.
- Thoroughly compares PMD to MMD; acknowledges cubic complexity of exact computation but proposes a quadratic-time approximation that empirically retains statistical performance.
- Paper is well-structured, well-written, cites prior work properly, and the theoretical results appear correct.
- Experimental analysis is adequate: comparisons to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modelling.
- Reviewer would like to see generative-model experiments in domains with many modes and questions PMD performance when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.
- Overall assessment: a good paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
