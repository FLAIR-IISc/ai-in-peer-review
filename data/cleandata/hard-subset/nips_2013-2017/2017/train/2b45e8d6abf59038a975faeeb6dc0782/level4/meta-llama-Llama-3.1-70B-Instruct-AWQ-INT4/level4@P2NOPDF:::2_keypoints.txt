- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD is a sampled version of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD gives stronger gradients, supports smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly show PMD enables training with much smaller mini-batches.  
- Evidence for stronger gradients is inconclusive because comparisons are sensitive to the MMD kernel scale (sigma).  
- Recommendation: compare against optimal or a range of sigmas to fairly evaluate gradient strength.  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is weak: PMD relies on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Estimating reliable distances in high-dimensional spaces is hard, so PMD may face similar problems to MMD in high dimensions.  
- Suggest comparing PMD to MMD using standard bandwidth heuristics (treating MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free).  
- Overall: PMD is promising for fast, small-batch training but its reliance on distance metrics may limit performance on high-dimensional data.
