This paper introduces PMD, a population-based divergence measure between probability distributions, and demonstrates its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator is conceptually straightforward and differentiable, making it suitable for training neural network-based models.

The authors provide a comprehensive comparison between PMD and MMD, the most widely used population-based divergence in machine learning. They also acknowledge the limitations of their approach, noting that exact computation has cubic complexity. However, they propose an approximation with quadratic complexity, which their empirical results show does not significantly compromise statistical performance.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with proper citations to relevant prior work. The theoretical results appear to be accurate, and the experimental analysis is sufficient. The authors compare PMD to MMD and other methods in the context of domain adaptation and generative modeling.

One potential area for further exploration is the application of PMD to generative modeling in multimodal domains. It would be interesting to investigate whether PMD remains effective when the sample size is smaller than the number of modes.

Overall, this paper presents a potentially valuable method for comparing distributions, and its contributions are well-supported by theoretical and empirical evidence.
