This paper presents the population matching discrepancy (PMD) as a better alternative to MMD for distribution matching applications. It is shown that PMD is a sampled version of the Wasserstein metric or earth mover's distance, and it has several advantages over MMD, most notably stronger gradients and the applicability of smaller mini-batch sizes, and fewer hyperparameters.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of PMD, establishing its connection to the Wasserstein metric and demonstrating its strong consistency as an estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. They also propose a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn parameters of the distributions using PMD. The empirical results demonstrate that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do a good job of explaining the technical details of PMD and its advantages over MMD. The empirical results are also well-presented and provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of PMD.

One potential criticism of the paper is that the authors do not fully address the issue of choosing a distance metric for PMD. While they argue that PMD has fewer hyperparameters than MMD, they do not provide a clear justification for why the choice of distance metric is less important for PMD. In fact, the choice of distance metric can be critical in high-dimensional spaces, and the authors should provide more guidance on how to select a suitable distance metric for PMD.

Another potential criticism is that the authors do not compare PMD to other methods that also permit small mini-batch sizes, such as the Wasserstein GAN. While PMD has several advantages over MMD, it is not clear whether it is the best method for distribution matching applications.

Overall, I think the proposed method has the nice property of permitting small mini-batch sizes, which can lead to fast training. However, the paper could be improved by providing more guidance on how to select a suitable distance metric for PMD and by comparing PMD to other methods that also permit small mini-batch sizes.
