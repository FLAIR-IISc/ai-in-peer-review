- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- Establishes PMD as a sampled variant of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD offers advantages over MMD: stronger gradients, suitability for smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batches and faster convergence.  
- Evidence for PMD’s stronger gradients is unconvincing because comparisons fail to account for MMD kernel bandwidth (σ) selection or a range of σ values.  
- Claim that PMD has fewer hyperparameters is under-supported since PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Defining or selecting distance metrics in high-dimensional spaces is difficult, so PMD may inherit scalability issues similar to MMD.  
- MMD benefits from established bandwidth-selection heuristics; treating MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free via these heuristics would be a fairer comparison.  
- Conclusion: PMD is a meaningful improvement over large-batch MMD for small-batch training, but its reliance on distance metrics limits scalability to high-dimensional data.
