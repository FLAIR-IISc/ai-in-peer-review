- Proposes PMD as a sample-based estimator of the Wasserstein distance as an alternative to MMD, claiming stronger gradients, smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.
- PMD effectively addresses MMD’s need for large mini-batches; empirical evidence for small mini-batch training is compelling.
- Claim of “stronger gradients” is inconclusive; comparisons should optimize or sweep MMD’s bandwidth σ before concluding.
- “Fewer hyperparameters” is unconvincing because PMD requires choosing a distance function, which can introduce similar hyperparameters.
- Robust distance measurement in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may inherit sensitivity to distance choice like MMD.
- Overall: PMD is a practical improvement for faster, small-batch training, but its reliance on a distance metric may limit performance on high-dimensional data.
