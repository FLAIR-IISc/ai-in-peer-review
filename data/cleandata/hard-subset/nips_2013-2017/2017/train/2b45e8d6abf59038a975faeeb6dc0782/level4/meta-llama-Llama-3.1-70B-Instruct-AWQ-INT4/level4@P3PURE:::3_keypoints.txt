- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and shows it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.  
- Thorough empirical comparison to MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Authors acknowledge exact computation is cubic-time and propose a quadratic-time approximation.  
- Quadratic approximation empirically does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and experimental analysis is adequate.  
- Comparisons include domain adaptation methods and MMD for generative modeling.  
- Raises an open question about PMD’s effectiveness for generative modeling with many modes and small sample sizes.  
- Overall positive evaluation: useful method for comparing distributions.
