- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed exactly in O(N^3) or approximately in O(N^2).  
- Strength: Noteworthy experimentation on computing the Wasserstein distance, though generated images do not outperform Wasserstein GAN.  
- Weakness: PMD may require large minibatch size N to accurately estimate Wasserstein for diverse multimodal distributions (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians), complicating learning of variances.  
- Weakness: Small N can cause optimization to converge to an incorrect global minimum with lower-entropy learned distributions; SVHN samples show limited diversity (digit 8 overrepresented).  
- Minor error: Typo in Line 141 — "usally" should be "usually".  
- Rebuttal note: Additional experiments were provided but the paper should better clarify PMD and MMD limitations.  
- Key technical distinction: MMD can be used with minibatch size as small as 2 using the unbiased MMD estimator and SGD to converge correctly, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, limiting its small-batch training capability.
