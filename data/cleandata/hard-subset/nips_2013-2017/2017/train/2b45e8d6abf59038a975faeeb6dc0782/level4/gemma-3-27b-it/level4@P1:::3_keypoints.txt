- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling neural network training.  
- Thorough comparison to MMD, the prominent population-based divergence in ML.  
- Exact PMD computation has cubic complexity; authors propose a quadratic approximation.  
- Empirical results show the approximation does not significantly degrade performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites related work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear sound, proving strong consistency for PMD.  
- Experiments compare PMD to MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling and show competitive or superior performance.  
- Missing evaluation on generative tasks with multi-modal distributions; concern about PMD when population size N is smaller than number of modes.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong theory, practical advantages over MMD (e.g., less bandwidth sensitivity, smaller mini-batch needs), empirical validation, and clarity.  
- Rejection concerns: computational cost for very large datasets and limited generative modeling evaluation on challenging multi-modal data.
