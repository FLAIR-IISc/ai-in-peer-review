Introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence, and proves its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
PMD estimator is conceptually simple, differentiable, and amenable to neural network training.
Provides a comprehensive comparison between PMD and the widely used MMD.
Acknowledges exact PMD has cubic complexity and proposes a quadratic-time approximation that empirically retains acceptable statistical performance.
Paper is well-organized, clearly written, appropriately cites prior work, and has sound theoretical foundations.
Experimental evaluation is satisfactory, including domain adaptation and generative modelling comparisons versus MMD.
Suggests further evaluation on multimodal generative tasks, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.
Overall assessment: a strong, practical contribution for distribution comparison.
