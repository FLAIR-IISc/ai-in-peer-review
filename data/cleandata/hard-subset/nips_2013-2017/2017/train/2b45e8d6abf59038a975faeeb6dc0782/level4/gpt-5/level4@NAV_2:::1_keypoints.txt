- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) that measures distribution discrepancy by computing the Wasserstein distance between two sampled mini-batches using either an exact O(N^3) Hungarian solver or an O(N^2) approximate matching algorithm.
- Empirical evaluation of the Wasserstein-based objective is interesting and informative.
- Image generation results are informative but do not match the quality of Wasserstein GAN.
- Accurately estimating the Wasserstein distance for complex, multimodal distributions likely requires large batch size N; issues may appear on mixtures of Gaussians, especially when variances are learned.
- With small N, optimization can converge to a global minimum that deviates from the true distribution (e.g., overly low-entropy solutions); SVHN samples show limited diversity (digit 8 occurs frequently).
- Minor typo: Line 141: “usally” → “usually”.
- After rebuttal: paper should clearly state the limitations of both PMD and MMD.
- MMD can operate with batch_size=2 and be trained via SGD using the unbiased estimator from “A Kernel Two-Sample Test,” enabling convergence with small mini-batches, whereas PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator.
