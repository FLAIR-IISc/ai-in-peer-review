- Proposes PMD, a direct sample-based estimator of the primal Wasserstein distance using minimum-weight bipartite matching, avoiding a parameterized critic and adversarial instability.  
- Provides a proof that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator, giving a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Conceptually avoids kernel bandwidth tuning, offering an advantage over MMD.  

- PMD quality depends strongly on population size N; complex/high-dimensional/multimodal distributions likely require very large N, otherwise PMD may be a poor proxy and produce biased models (e.g., low entropy, mode collapse).  
- Experiments suggest reduced diversity (SVHN samples over-represent the digit '8'); controlled tests (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians) are needed to demonstrate this failure mode.  
- Claims about effective training with small mini-batches are misleading: PMD gradients computed w.r.t. a fixed matching M* are biased, unlike MMD’s unbiased U-statistic which supports SGD with small batches.  
- Generated image quality does not match contemporary methods like WGAN.
