- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD, shown to be a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein distance.
- Claims advantages over MMD: works with smaller mini-batches, provides stronger training gradients, and has fewer hyperparameters.
- Motivation is strong: MMD often yields weak gradients and requires large batches, which is computationally costly.
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batch sizes.
- Claim of stronger gradients is under-supported; needs rigorous comparisons against MMD tuned over a range of kernel bandwidths (sigma) or optimized values.
- Claim of fewer hyperparameters is questionable because PMD requires choosing a distance metric (e.g., Euclidean or cosine), which is itself a critical design decision.
- Reliance on standard distance metrics may suffer in high-dimensional spaces (curse of dimensionality), mirroring kernel-selection issues in MMD.
- Authors should compare PMD to MMD variants using automatic bandwidth selection heuristics (e.g., median heuristic) for a fairer evaluation.
- Overall strength: efficient small-batch training; main concern: dependence on an underlying distance metric limiting scalability to complex, high-dimensional data.
