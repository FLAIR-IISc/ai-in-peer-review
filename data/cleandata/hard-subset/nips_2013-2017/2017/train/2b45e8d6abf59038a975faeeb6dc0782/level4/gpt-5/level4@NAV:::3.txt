The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which clearly enables training of neural network–based models.
The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD with MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning.
They also discuss the method’s drawbacks: the exact computation has cubic complexity, but they propose a quadratic-complexity approximation and show in their empirical results that this does not substantially degrade statistical performance.
The paper is well structured and well written, and includes appropriate references to prior work.
The theoretical results appear correct.
The experimental analysis is adequate. They compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and compare to MMD for generative modelling.
I would have liked to see the method applied to generative modelling in domains with many modes. I wonder whether PMD works when N is smaller than the number of modes.
All things considered, I think this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
