- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.  
- Provides a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Notes computational limitation: exact PMD computation is cubic in complexity.  
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation and shows it does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, well-written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and experimental analysis is adequate with comparisons for domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Raises a concern for future work: effectiveness of PMD in multimodal generative modeling when sample size is smaller than number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
