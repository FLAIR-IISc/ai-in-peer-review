- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): compute Wasserstein distance directly between two minibatches and use it as the generator loss (exact O(N^3) or O(N^2) approx).

- Strength: Provides a primal, minibatch-based approach to Wasserstein distance as an alternative perspective to dual-formulation methods like WGANs.

- Major weakness: PMD is a biased estimator of the true population Wasserstein distance.

- Minibatch dependence: Accurate estimation requires extremely large minibatches; typical batch sizes likely yield poor approximations.

- Wrong optimization objective: For small N, the minibatch PMD’s global minimum may not correspond to matching the true distribution, biasing the model (e.g., toward lower entropy).

- Mode collapse evidence: Qualitative results (SVHN samples) show low diversity and overrepresentation of certain modes (digit "8").

- Gradient bias vs. MMD: Unlike MMD’s unbiased V-statistic that works with tiny batches, PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, so SGD gradients are biased and convergence is not guaranteed.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; minor typo ("usally" → "usually").

- Post-rebuttal stance: Reviewer remains concerned about PMD’s bias and requests explicit discussion of biased gradients and their impact on convergence in the paper.
