- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly (O(N^3)) or approximately (O(N^2)).  
- Experiments are intriguing and worth exploring, though generated image quality lags behind Wasserstein GAN.  
- Likely requires large minibatch size N to estimate Wasserstein reliably for diverse, multimodal distributions.  
- Small N may lead optimization to a global minimum that does not represent the true distribution (e.g., lower entropy, mode collapse).  
- Empirical symptom: limited diversity in SVHN samples (overrepresentation of digit 8).  
- Suggested test: matching mixtures of Gaussians (including variances) would likely reveal PMD’s limitations.  
- Minor correction: line 141 typo — use “usually” instead of “usally.”  
- After rebuttal: authors added useful experiments, but should more clearly state limitations of PMD vs. MMD; MMD can work with very small batches using the unbiased MMD estimator and SGD, while PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator.
