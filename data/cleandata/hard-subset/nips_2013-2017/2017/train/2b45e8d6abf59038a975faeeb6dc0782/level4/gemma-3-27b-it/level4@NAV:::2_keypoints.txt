- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD can be interpreted as a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD offers improved gradient behavior and requires smaller mini-batches with fewer hyperparameters than MMD.  
- Empirical results convincingly support the smaller mini-batch / faster-training advantage.  
- Evidence for stronger gradients under PMD is less conclusive and needs more substantiation.  
- MMD’s sensitivity to the kernel bandwidth σ means comparisons should consider optimal σ or a range of σ values.  
- The claim of fewer hyperparameters is questionable because PMD’s dependence on a distance metric can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Robustly defining a distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, potentially limiting PMD similarly to MMD.  
- Recommends comparing PMD to standard MMD bandwidth heuristics and treating MMD as a possible hyperparameter-free baseline for evaluation.
