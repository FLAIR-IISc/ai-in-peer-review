- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for sample-based distribution distance estimation.
- Shows PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Claims PMD offers advantages over MMD: stronger gradients, smaller mini-batch sizes, and fewer hyperparameters.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear explanation of MMD’s limitations in generative modeling.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of PMD and its relationship to the Wasserstein metric.
- Empirical results are strong, demonstrating PMD’s effectiveness in domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Thorough empirical comparison with MMD highlights faster convergence and improved performance.
- Weakness: the claim of fewer hyperparameters is unconvincing because the choice of distance metric may add hyperparameters.
- Weakness: limited comparison with standard heuristics for selecting MMD bandwidth, which could reduce MMD’s hyperparameter burden.
- Weakness: stronger-gradients claim would be better supported by experiments across a range of MMD sigma values.
- Overall recommendation: accept; paper is a valuable contribution with an overall rating of 8/10.
