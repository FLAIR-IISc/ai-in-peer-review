- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an improved alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD is a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD provides stronger gradients, supports smaller mini-batches, and reduces hyperparameter count compared to MMD.  
- Empirical results convincingly validate PMD’s ability to work with much smaller mini-batch sizes and speed up training.  
- Evidence for the claim of stronger gradients is less convincing and underexamined.  
- MMD’s sensitivity to the kernel bandwidth σ implies a fair comparison should evaluate optimal or a range of σ values.  
- The claim of fewer hyperparameters is weak because PMD depends on a chosen distance metric, which can introduce comparable hyperparameters.  
- Obtaining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, potentially limiting PMD similarly to MMD.  
- Established heuristics exist for selecting MMD bandwidths (treating it as effectively hyperparameter-free), so comparing PMD against those heuristics would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Overall, PMD is a valuable improvement for small-batch training, but its dependence on a distance metric raises concerns for high-dimensional data performance.
