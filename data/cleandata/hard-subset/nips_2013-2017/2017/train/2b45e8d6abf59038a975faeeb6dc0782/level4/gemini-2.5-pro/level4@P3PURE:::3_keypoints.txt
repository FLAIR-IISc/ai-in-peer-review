- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural network models.  
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison of PMD to MMD, the leading population-based divergence.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear sound.  
- Authors candidly note computational drawbacks: exact computation is cubic, but they propose a quadratic-complexity approximation.  
- Empirically, the quadratic approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate, covering domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons.  
- Reviewer requests additional evaluation on highly multimodal generative tasks and scenarios where sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper introducing a potentially very useful method.
