- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural network models.  
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison of PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Notes a major drawback: exact computation has cubic complexity.  
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation and shows empirically it preserves statistical performance.  
- Manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, including domain adaptation and generative modeling baselines.  
- Suggests additional evaluation on highly multi-modal generative tasks, especially when sample size N is smaller than number of modes.  
- Overall conclusion: strong paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
