- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly in O(N^3) or approximately in O(N^2).  
- Experimental exploration of a Wasserstein-based objective is interesting.  
- Generated images are less compelling than those from Wasserstein GANs.  
- Accurately estimating Wasserstein between diverse, multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch sizes N (issues expected even on mixtures of Gaussians, including variance learning).  
- With insufficient N, optimization can reach a global minimum that does not match the true distribution (e.g., learned distribution may have lower entropy); SVHN samples show limited diversity and over-representation of digit 8.  
- Minor typo: Line 141, "usally" → "usually".  
- After rebuttal: authors should clarify limitations of PMD vs MMD—MMD can work with batch_size=2 using the unbiased estimator and SGD, while PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator.
