- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly (O(N^3)) or approximately (O(N^2)).  
- Strength: experiments with Wasserstein computation produce interesting results.  
- Generated image quality is worse than Wasserstein GAN.  
- Limitation: accurately estimating Wasserstein for diverse, multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch size N.  
- Practical failure mode: small N can yield convergence to incorrect global minima (e.g., lower-entropy learned distributions).  
- Empirical sign of low diversity: SVHN samples show many repeated digits (notably digit 8).  
- Minor correction: typo on Line 141 ("usally" → "usually").  
- Rebuttal: reviewer appreciates additional experiments but requests clearer articulation of PMD and MMD limitations.  
- Comparison to MMD: MMD can train with batch size 2 and converge using an unbiased estimator, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, which may impair performance.
