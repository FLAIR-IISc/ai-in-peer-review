- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance computed between minibatches from two distributions, computed exactly (O(N^3)) or approximately (O(N^2)).  
- Experimental evaluation of the Wasserstein computation is noteworthy.  
- Generated image quality is inferior to that of Wasserstein GANs.  
- PMD likely requires very large minibatches to estimate Wasserstein distance for significantly different, multimodal distributions (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians), especially when learning variances.  
- Small minibatch size (insufficient N) can drive optimization to suboptimal, lower-entropy solutions that do not match the true distribution.  
- SVHN samples show lack of diversity and overrepresentation of digit 8, illustrating potential mode-collapse/low-entropy learning.  
- Minor typo: Line 141: "usally" → "usually".  
- Rebuttal experiments are appreciated.  
- PMD’s limitations vs MMD need clearer statement: MMD can work with very small batches (e.g., batch_size=2) using the unbiased estimator and SGD to converge correctly, whereas PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator.
