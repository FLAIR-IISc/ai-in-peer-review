- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a novel sample-based distance estimator between distributions.  
- Shows PMD is strongly consistent and relates it theoretically to the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Develops an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective.  
- Compares PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and highlights advantages over MMD.  
- Claims PMD avoids kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter tuning required by MMD.  
- Argues PMD provides stronger gradients and allows smaller mini-batch sizes for optimization.  
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks, with better performance and faster convergence.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides thorough technical explanations.  
- Theoretical analysis is comprehensive, including proof of PMD’s strong consistency.  
- The contribution is original, notably the PMD–Wasserstein connection and practical comparison to MMD.  
- Paper is significant for deep learning applications, especially large-scale tasks due to smaller mini-batch capability.  
- Concerns: PMD relies on a distance metric that may introduce hyperparameters and be hard to choose in high dimensions.  
- Concerns: Comparison to MMD is not exhaustive—best or a range of kernel bandwidths (sigma) were not explored.  
- Concerns: Computational complexity of PMD is not comprehensively evaluated, which could limit large-scale applicability.
