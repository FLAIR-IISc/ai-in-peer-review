- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance computed between two minibatches, with exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.
- Strength: Novel, direct non-adversarial use of Wasserstein distance for training generative models; experiments provide a valuable data point.
- Relies on large minibatch sizes: small N can distort the optimization landscape and fail for complex or multimodal target distributions.
- Leads to mode collapse / low sample diversity: qualitative results (e.g., SVHN overrepresenting digit "8") suggest reduced entropy in learned distributions.
- Biased gradient estimation: minibatch PMD gradients are biased, unlike MMD’s unbiased estimator, raising concerns about suitability for SGD with small batches.
- Weaker empirical performance: generated samples do not match state-of-the-art methods like Wasserstein GAN.
- Post-rebuttal concern: reviewer still worries about PMD bias and urges explicit discussion of limitations and contrast with MMD’s unbiased stochastic gradient and convergence guarantees.
