- Presents Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a compelling alternative to MMD for estimating distributional distance and as a training objective.  
- Core idea: frame PMD as a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein metric, which is well-motivated.  
- Key strengths: addresses MMD drawbacks (kernel bandwidth sensitivity, weak gradients, need for large mini-batches).  
- Theoretical contribution: shows PMD is a strongly consistent estimator with a clear connection to the Wasserstein metric.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains PMD definition, parameter learning algorithm, and theoretical underpinnings.  
- Empirical validation on domain adaptation and generative modeling shows faster convergence and comparable or superior performance to MMD.  
- Gradient analysis (Fig. 2(b)) demonstrates more consistent gradient magnitudes for PMD versus MMD.  
- Claim of “fewer hyper-parameters” is overstated: PMD still depends on a distance metric, which can be sensitive in high dimensions and require scaling choices.  
- Would benefit from comparison to well-tuned MMD (including standard bandwidth heuristics) to more robustly evaluate gradient and performance differences.  
- Experimental limitation: matching algorithms run single-threaded; scalability and need for GPU acceleration are not fully demonstrated.  
- Approximation error analysis (Fig. 2(a)) is useful but should be complemented by studying how distance-metric choice affects approximation quality.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend acceptance subject to addressing the above clarifications.
