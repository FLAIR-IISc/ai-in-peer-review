- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to MMD, showing it approximates the Wasserstein metric and claims stronger gradients, small mini-batch compatibility, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- PMD addresses MMD's weak gradients and large mini-batch needs for generative model training; the small mini-batch efficacy is well supported empirically.  
- Evidence for PMD's stronger gradients is inconclusive because MMD performance depends on the scale parameter σ; authors should evaluate against optimal or a comprehensive range of σ.  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is weak: PMD depends on a distance metric which can introduce hyperparameters akin to MMD’s σ.  
- Estimating distance metrics in high-dimensional spaces is difficult, so PMD may inherit MMD’s dependency on distance choices.  
- Authors should compare PMD to MMD using established bandwidth-selection heuristics rather than implying PMD is hyperparameter-free.  
- Overall, PMD enables faster training via small mini-batches, but reliance on distance metrics may limit applicability to high-dimensional data.
