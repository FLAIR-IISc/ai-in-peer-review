- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an improved alternative to MMD for distribution matching.
- Authors show PMD is a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.
- Claimed benefits over MMD: stronger gradients, effective use of smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.
- Empirical results convincingly show PMD works with much smaller mini-batch sizes, enabling faster training.
- Evidence for PMD providing stronger gradients is inconclusive and requires more rigorous comparison.
- MMD’s performance is sensitive to bandwidth σ; comparisons should test optimal σ or a range of σ values when assessing gradients.
- Claim of fewer hyperparameters is not fully substantiated since PMD depends on a distance metric that may introduce comparable hyperparameters.
- Defining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face similar difficulties to MMD.
- A fairer evaluation would compare PMD to MMD using established σ-selection heuristics to treat MMD as minimally tuned.
