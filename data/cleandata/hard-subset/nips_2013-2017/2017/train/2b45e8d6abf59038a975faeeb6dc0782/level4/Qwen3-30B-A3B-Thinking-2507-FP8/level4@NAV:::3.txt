

The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and establish it as a consistent estimator for the Wasserstein distance.  
The estimator is conceptually straightforward and differentiable, enabling seamless integration into neural network training pipelines.  
The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD against MMD, the predominant population-based divergence in machine learning.  
They acknowledge the computational drawback of exact PMD calculation (cubic complexity) but propose a quadratic-complexity approximation, demonstrating through empirical results that this approximation preserves statistical performance effectively.  
The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work.  
The theoretical claims appear sound.  
The experimental evaluation is sufficient, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and evaluating PMD against MMD in generative modeling.  
I would have liked to see PMD tested on generative modeling tasks involving high-mode-density distributions, particularly whether it remains effective when the sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
