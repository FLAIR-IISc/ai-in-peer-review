- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sampled Wasserstein alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- Claims: PMD gives stronger gradients, enables smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly support the small mini-batch / faster training claim.  
- Evidence for the “stronger gradients” claim is weak; MMD’s performance depends critically on kernel bandwidth σ and needs evaluation over an appropriate σ range.  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is not fully supported because PMD relies on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Constructing reliable distance metrics in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may inherit vulnerabilities similar to MMD.  
- MMD has practical heuristics (e.g., median heuristic for bandwidth) that can make it effectively hyperparameter-free in practice and should be used as a baseline.  
- Overall: PMD’s small-batch advantage is a practical strength, but dependence on distance metrics may limit its effectiveness on high-dimensional data.
