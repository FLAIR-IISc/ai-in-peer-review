- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an improved alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD is a sampled version of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD provides stronger gradients, works with smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters than MMD.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric and proving strong consistency as an estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.  
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Paper is well-written and explains technical details and advantages clearly.  
- Criticism: the paper does not adequately address how to choose the distance metric for PMD, which is important in high-dimensional spaces.  
- Criticism: lacks clear justification for why PMD’s choice of distance metric is less important than in MMD.  
- Criticism: does not compare PMD to other methods that support small mini-batches (e.g., Wasserstein GANs).  
- Suggestion: provide more guidance on selecting a suitable distance metric for PMD.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons to other small-mini-batch-capable methods to position PMD relative to alternatives.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theory, strong empirical results, broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved distance-metric choice, missing comparisons to similar small-mini-batch methods, needs more practical guidance.  
- Rating: 7/10; Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address metric selection and additional comparisons.
