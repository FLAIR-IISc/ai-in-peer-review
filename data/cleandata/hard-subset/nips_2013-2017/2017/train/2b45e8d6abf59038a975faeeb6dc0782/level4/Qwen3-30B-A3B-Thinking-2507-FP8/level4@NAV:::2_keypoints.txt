- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sampled Wasserstein alternative to MMD, claiming stronger gradients, smaller mini-batch compatibility, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- PMD addresses MMD's weak gradients and large-mini-batch requirement for generative training, and empirical results support the small mini-batch claim.  
- The stronger-gradient claim is unconvincing because MMD performance depends on the scale parameter σ; the paper fails to compare against optimally tuned or range-tuned σ.  
- The fewer-hyperparameters claim is weak: PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters, similar to MMD’s bandwidth.  
- Obtaining an effective distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may share MMD’s fundamental limitations in such settings.  
- Overall: small-mini-batch capability is a practical improvement, but PMD’s reliance on a distance metric may limit its effectiveness for high-dimensional data.
