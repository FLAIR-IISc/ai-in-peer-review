The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which is a significant advantage as it allows for training neural network-based models.

The authors conduct a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They also acknowledge the limitations of their method, noting that exact calculation has cubic complexity. However, they propose an approximation with quadratic complexity and show in their empirical results that this does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well-structured and written, and the authors provide references to previous work where necessary. The theoretical results appear to be correct, and the experimental analysis is adequate. The authors compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, as well as to MMD for generative modeling.

One potential avenue for future exploration is the application of PMD to generative modeling in domains with multiple modes. It is unclear whether PMD would be effective in such scenarios, particularly when the sample size is smaller than the number of modes.

Overall, this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
