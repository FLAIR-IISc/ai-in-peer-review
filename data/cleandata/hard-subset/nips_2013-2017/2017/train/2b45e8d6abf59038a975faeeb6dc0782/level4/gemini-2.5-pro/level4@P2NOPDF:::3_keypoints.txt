- Introduces Population-based Matched Divergence (PMD), a novel sample-based divergence for comparing probability distributions.  
- Proves PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD is conceptually simple and differentiable, making it suitable as a loss for training deep neural networks.  
- Thoroughly compares PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), situating PMD within existing literature and highlighting its strengths.  
- Notes exact PMD computation has cubic complexity.  
- Proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation and shows empirically it does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes a comprehensive related-work review and proper citations.  
- Theoretical claims are supported by provided proofs.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid, benchmarking PMD against MMD and other baselines on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Suggests further experiments on complex, multi-modal generative tasks, especially when sample size is smaller than the number of modes, to clarify PMD’s behavior.  
- Concludes PMD is a strong, well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated contribution likely of high interest to researchers.
