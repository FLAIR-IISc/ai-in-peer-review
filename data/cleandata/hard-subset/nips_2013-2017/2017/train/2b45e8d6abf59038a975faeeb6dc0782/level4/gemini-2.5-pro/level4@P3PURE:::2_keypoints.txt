- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD is a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein metric (Earth Mover's Distance).  
- Claims advantages over MMD: stronger training gradients, effectiveness with smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batches.  
- The “stronger gradients” claim is unconvincing because MMD’s performance depends on kernel scale (sigma); fair comparison requires tuned sigma or evaluation across sigma values.  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is not fully substantiated since PMD depends on a chosen distance metric that may introduce hyperparameters.  
- Defining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face similar difficulties to MMD.  
- Heuristics exist for setting MMD bandwidth, so MMD can be effectively hyperparameter-free; the paper should compare against such MMD baselines.  
- Overall, PMD’s main practical benefit is faster training via small batches, but reliance on a distance metric may limit its applicability on complex, high-dimensional data.
