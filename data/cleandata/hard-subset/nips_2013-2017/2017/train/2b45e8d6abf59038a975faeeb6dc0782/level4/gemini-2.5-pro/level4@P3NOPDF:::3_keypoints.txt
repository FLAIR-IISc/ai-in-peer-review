- Introduces Population-based Matched Divergence (PMD), a sample-based estimator for distributional divergence.  
- Shows PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD is conceptually simple and differentiable, suitable as a loss for neural-network training.  
- Authors acknowledge exact PMD has cubic complexity but provide a practical quadratic-time approximation.  
- Empirical results indicate the quadratic approximation maintains statistical performance.  
- Thorough comparison to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and other baselines, especially in domain adaptation.  
- Experiments include generative modeling as a proof of concept demonstrating PMD’s utility.  
- Missing experiments on highly multi-modal target distributions to fully test performance.  
- Unclear how PMD behaves when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes—risk of mode collapse vs. geometric fidelity needs investigation.  
- Overall: theoretically grounded, practical, empirically validated, and a valuable contribution, though additional challenging experiments would strengthen claims.
