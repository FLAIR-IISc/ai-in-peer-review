- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, aiding neural network training.  
- Thorough empirical comparison of PMD against MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Exact PMD computation has cubic complexity; authors propose a quadratic-Complexity approximation.  
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation causes minimal statistical performance loss.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate, covering domain adaptation (PMD vs. MMD and others) and generative modeling (PMD vs. MMD).  
- Missing evaluation: generative modeling on many-mode domains, especially when sample size n is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially valuable distribution-comparison method.
