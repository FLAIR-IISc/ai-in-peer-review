- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based estimator of the first Wasserstein metric via minimum weight matching between samples.  
- Proposes an algorithm that uses PMD as an objective for learning distribution parameters, applied to domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Highlights advantages over MMD: no bandwidth parameter, stronger gradients, and compatibility with standard mini-batch sizes.  
- Theoretical contribution: establishes a clear connection between PMD and the Wasserstein metric and proves strong consistency.  
- Empirical evaluation shows competitive performance on benchmark tasks and practical assessment in domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Observes that PMD can work effectively with smaller mini-batch sizes than MMD, potentially improving training efficiency.  
- Primary weakness: PMD may require large sample sizes (N) to accurately estimate Wasserstein distance for complex or multimodal distributions.  
- Experimental concern: low diversity in generated SVHN samples suggests possible mode collapse or limitations in modeling complex data.  
- Gradient claims are under-analyzed: only gradient magnitude distribution shown, lacking rigorous comparisons (variance, condition number).  
- Rebuttal note: authors correctly point out unbiased MMD estimators can converge with small mini-batches, but they do not explore an analogous unbiased PMD estimator.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, solid theoretical foundation, promising empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: sensitivity to sample size, insufficient analysis of limitations and unbiased alternatives, and limited evidence of substantial practical gains.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address sample-size sensitivity and the potential for unbiased gradient estimation in PMD.  
- Minor comment: fix typo "usally" → "usually" (Line 141).
