- Introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence between distributions, and shows it consistently estimates the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD’s key advantage is its differentiability, enabling integration into neural network models.  
- Conceptually simple measure with sound theoretical results and detailed proofs.  
- Comprehensive and well-executed comparison to MMD, highlighting relative strengths and weaknesses.  
- Exact PMD computation is cubic in complexity; authors propose a quadratic approximation to reduce cost.  
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation retains statistical performance comparable to exact PMD.  
- Experiments cover domain adaptation and generative modeling, with appropriate baselines and evaluations.  
- Reviewer requests additional experiments on highly multimodal distributions, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of true modes.  
- Paper is well-written, cites relevant work, and is recommended for acceptance.
