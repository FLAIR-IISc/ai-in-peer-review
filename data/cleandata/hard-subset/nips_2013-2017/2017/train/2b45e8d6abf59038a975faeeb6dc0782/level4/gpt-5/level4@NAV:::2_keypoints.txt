- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD, framed as a sampled version of the Wasserstein metric with claimed benefits (stronger gradients, small mini-batches, fewer hyperparameters).  
- The claim that PMD enables training with small mini-batches is convincingly demonstrated in experiments.  
- The claim of stronger gradients is not convincingly supported; comparisons should tune MMD bandwidth (sigma) or evaluate across sigma values.  
- The claim of fewer hyperparameters is weak because PMD depends on a chosen distance metric, which can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- PMD may inherit MMD’s vulnerability in high-dimensional settings since obtaining a reliable distance metric is challenging.  
- Fairer comparisons would include standard MMD bandwidth heuristics to treat MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free.  
- Overall: PMD’s small-batch advantage is a real, practical improvement, but dependence on a distance metric may limit performance on high-dimensional data.
