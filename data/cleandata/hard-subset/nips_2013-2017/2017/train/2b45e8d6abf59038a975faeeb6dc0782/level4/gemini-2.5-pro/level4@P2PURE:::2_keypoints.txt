- Paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- Authors show PMD is a sampled version of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.  
- Claimed advantages: stronger training gradients, applicability with smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batches.  
- The “stronger gradients” claim is not convincingly verified; comparisons should include MMD with optimal or varied kernel bandwidths (sigma).  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is questionable because PMD depends on a distance metric that may itself have hyperparameters.  
- Defining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face similar practical issues as MMD.  
- Standard heuristics exist for setting MMD bandwidth, which can make MMD effectively hyperparameter-free and should be used in comparisons.  
- Overall, PMD’s small-batch capability is a valuable improvement, but reliance on a user-defined distance metric could limit its practical success on high-dimensional data.
