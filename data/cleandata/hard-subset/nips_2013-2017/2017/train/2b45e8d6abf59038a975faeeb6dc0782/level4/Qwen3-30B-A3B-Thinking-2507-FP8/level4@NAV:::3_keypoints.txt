- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple, differentiable, and integrates easily into neural network training.  
- Provides a thorough empirical and conceptual comparison of PMD to MMD.  
- Notes exact PMD has cubic complexity but proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation.  
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation preserves statistical performance effectively.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical claims appear sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is sufficient, including domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons to MMD and other methods.  
- Reviewer requests additional tests on generative modeling with high-mode-density distributions, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper proposing a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
