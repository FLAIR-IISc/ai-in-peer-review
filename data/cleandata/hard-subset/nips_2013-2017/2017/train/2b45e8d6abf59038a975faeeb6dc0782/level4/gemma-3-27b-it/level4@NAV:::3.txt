This paper introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence measure, and demonstrates its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. 

The proposed estimator benefits from conceptual simplicity and differentiability, enabling its application within neural network training frameworks. 

A comprehensive comparison is provided between PMD and MMD, a widely used population-based divergence in machine learning. 

The authors acknowledge the cubic computational complexity of exact PMD calculation and offer a quadratic-complexity approximation, demonstrating through empirical results that this approximation maintains acceptable statistical performance. 

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work. The theoretical foundations appear sound.

The experimental evaluation is satisfactory, featuring comparisons of PMD to MMD and other techniques in domain adaptation, as well as a comparison to MMD in the context of generative modelling. 

It would be valuable to explore the method’s performance in generative modelling scenarios characterized by multi-modal distributions, particularly when the sample size (N) is smaller than the number of modes. 

Overall, this is a strong contribution that presents a potentially valuable and practical method for distribution comparison.
