- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.  
- Shows PMD is a sampled approximation of the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.  
- Claims PMD provides stronger gradients, supports smaller mini-batches, and reduces hyperparameters versus MMD.  
- Argues PMD addresses MMD’s weak gradients and large mini-batch requirements for training generative models.  
- Empirical results convincingly support the claim that PMD works with smaller mini-batch sizes.  
- The stronger-gradients claim is less conclusive because MMD’s performance depends on the scale parameter σ; comparisons should consider optimal σ or a σ range.  
- The fewer-hyperparameters claim is unpersuasive: PMD relies on a distance metric that may introduce comparable hyperparameters.  
- Reliable distance metrics are hard to obtain in high-dimensional spaces, so PMD may face similar difficulties as MMD.  
- A fairer comparison would tune MMD using existing bandwidth-selection heuristics to treat it as a properly hyperparameter-optimized baseline.  
- Overall: PMD enables small-batch, faster training and is a valid improvement over large-batch MMD, but its dependence on a distance metric may limit performance in high-dimensional settings.
