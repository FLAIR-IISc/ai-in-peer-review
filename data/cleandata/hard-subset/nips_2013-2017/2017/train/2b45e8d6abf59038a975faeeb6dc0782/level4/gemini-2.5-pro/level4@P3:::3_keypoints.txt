- Introduces PMD: a sample-based estimator using minimum-weight bipartite matching, proven strongly consistent for the Wasserstein-1 (W1) metric.  
- PMD is differentiable w.r.t. sample locations, enabling direct use as a loss for gradient-based training of deep networks.  
- Positions PMD as a principled alternative to MMD with no kernel bandwidth hyperparameter.  
- Claims PMD yields stronger, more stable gradients than MMD and can work with smaller mini-batches.  
- Provides a direct, parameter-free W1 estimate without needing an auxiliary critic network (contrast to Wasserstein GANs).  
- Computationally honest: exact Hungarian algorithm is O(N^3), authors evaluate an O(N^2) approximation that empirically performs well.  
- Solid empirical validation across domain adaptation (Office) and generative modeling (MNIST, SVHN, LFW), showing competitive or superior convergence and performance.  
- Theoretical contribution is sound: strong consistency result supported by optimal transport theory.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized with helpful pseudocode and illustrations.  
- Originality: novel use of bipartite matching as a direct, primal sample-based estimator for training deep models.  
- Significance: practical, likely impactful alternative to MMD for domain adaptation, generative modeling, and model criticism.  
- Main concern: unclear how PMD performs in highly multi-modal settings when the number of true modes ≫ sample size N; requests experiments or discussion.  
- Minor suggestion: discuss choice of ground distance d(·,·) (L1 vs L2 or learned metrics) and its effect on performance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
