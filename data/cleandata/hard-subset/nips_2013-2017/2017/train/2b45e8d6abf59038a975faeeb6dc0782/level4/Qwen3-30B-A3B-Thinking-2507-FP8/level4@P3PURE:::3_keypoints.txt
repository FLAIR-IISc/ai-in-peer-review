- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, allowing training of neural-network models.  
- Provides a thorough empirical and conceptual comparison to MMD, the main population-based divergence used in ML.  
- Exact PMD computation has cubic complexity, which the authors acknowledge as a limitation.  
- Proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation that empirically does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation and to MMD for generative modeling.  
- Missing experiments: application to generative modeling with many modes, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for comparing probability distributions.
