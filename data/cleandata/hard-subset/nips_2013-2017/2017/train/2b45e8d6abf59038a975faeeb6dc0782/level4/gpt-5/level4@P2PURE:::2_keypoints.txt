- Paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD, presented as an empirical approximation of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Authors claim PMD offers stronger gradients, ability to use smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters compared to MMD.  
- In generative model training, MMD often has weak gradients and needs large mini-batches; PMD addresses these issues.  
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s advantage of working with smaller mini-batches.  
- Evidence for PMD providing stronger gradients is unconvincing; comparisons should consider optimal or a range of MMD bandwidths (σ).  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is weak because PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- In high-dimensional spaces, reliable distance metrics are hard to obtain, so PMD may inherit the same challenges as MMD.  
- MMD has standard heuristics for choosing bandwidth; fairness requires comparing PMD to MMD with these heuristics, treating MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free.  
- Overall: PMD enables faster training via small-batch operation and is a meaningful improvement, but reliance on a distance metric may limit performance on high-dimensional data.
