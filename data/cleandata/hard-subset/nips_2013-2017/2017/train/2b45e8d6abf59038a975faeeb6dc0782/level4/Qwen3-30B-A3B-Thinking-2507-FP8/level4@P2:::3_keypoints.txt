- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator of distance between probability distributions.  
- Defines PMD as minimum weight matching between sample populations and connects it to the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the Wasserstein metric using Varadarajan’s theorem and OT stability.  
- Addresses key limitations of MMD (bandwidth sensitivity, weak gradients, large mini-batches) with a practical alternative.  
- PMD is parameter-free (no bandwidth), provides stronger gradients, and works with standard mini-batch sizes (e.g., 100).  
- Provides a differentiable, polynomial-time computable estimator unlike WGAN’s critic-based approach that doesn’t give absolute distances.  
- Proposes an approximate matching algorithm with O(N²) complexity and shows it doesn’t significantly hurt performance.  
- Experiments compare PMD vs MMD on domain adaptation (Office benchmark) and generative modeling (MNIST, SVHN, LFW), showing better performance and faster convergence.  
- Ablation studies investigate sensitivity to population size, mini-batch size, and matching algorithm choice, offering practical guidance.  
- Empirical gradient magnitude analysis supports theoretical claims of stronger gradients (Figure 2b).  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and connects thoroughly to prior work (MMD, Wasserstein, WGAN).  
- Minor weakness: generative outputs on SVHN and LFW are somewhat blurry, suggesting L1 distance may be suboptimal for natural images.  
- Minor weakness: lacks experiments on challenging multimodal distributions where sample size N is smaller than number of modes.  
- Overall significance: strong theoretical foundation, practical advantages over MMD, comprehensive validation, and recommended for acceptance.
