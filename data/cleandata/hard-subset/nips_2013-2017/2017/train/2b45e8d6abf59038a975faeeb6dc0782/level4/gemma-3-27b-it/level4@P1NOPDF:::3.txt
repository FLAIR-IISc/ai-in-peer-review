The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. 

The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, a key advantage that enables training neural network-based models. The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence used in machine learning. They also acknowledge the limitations of their method, noting that exact calculation has cubic complexity, but propose and validate an approximation with quadratic complexity that maintains strong statistical performance, as evidenced by their empirical results.

The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work. The theoretical results appear sound. 

The experimental analysis is adequate, with comparisons of PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD specifically for generative modelling. I would have appreciated seeing the method applied to generative modelling tasks in domains with multi-modal distributions. It raises a question of whether PMD performs well when the sample size *N* is smaller than the number of modes in the underlying distribution.

Overall, I believe this is a strong paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
