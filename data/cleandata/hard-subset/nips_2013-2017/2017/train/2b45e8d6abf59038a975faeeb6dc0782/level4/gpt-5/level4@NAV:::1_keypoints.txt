- Paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed exactly in O(N^3) or approximately in O(N^2).
- Experimental study of Wasserstein-based minibatch matching is interesting.
- Generated samples from PMD are not as good as those from Wasserstein GAN.
- Estimating Wasserstein between complex, multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch size N.
- PMD may fail on tasks like Gaussian mixtures (including learning variances) when N is insufficient.
- With small N optimization can reach a global minimum that differs from the true distribution (e.g., lower-entropy models).
- SVHN samples show limited diversity (digit 8 appears frequently), suggesting mode collapse or low diversity.
- Minor typo: Line 141 "usally" should be "usually".
- Rebuttal note: authors should clarify PMD vs MMD limitations—MMD can work with batch_size=2 using an unbiased estimator and converge with small minibatches.
- PMD currently has no known unbiased gradient estimator, unlike MMD.
