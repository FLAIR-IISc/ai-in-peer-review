The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence measure between probability distributions, and demonstrate its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
This estimator is conceptually straightforward and differentiable, a key property enabling its use in training neural network-based models.
A comprehensive comparison of PMD to MMD, the currently dominant population-based divergence in machine learning, is provided.
The authors acknowledge the cubic complexity of exact PMD calculation but propose and empirically validate a quadratic-complexity approximation that maintains strong statistical performance.
The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work.
The theoretical results appear sound.
The experimental evaluation is adequate, featuring comparisons of PMD to MMD and other techniques for domain adaptation, as well as comparisons to MMD for generative modeling. 
I would have appreciated seeing the method applied to generative modeling tasks involving multi-modal distributions, and I question its performance when the sample size *N* is smaller than the number of modes.
Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
