- Paper defines Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly in O(N^3) or approximately in O(N^2).
- Experiments using this minibatch-Wasserstein formulation are interesting.
- Generated images from PMD are not as good as those from the Wasserstein GAN.
- Matching diverse, multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch size N; small N may fail (e.g., mixture-of-Gaussians including learning variances).
- With insufficient N, optimization may converge to a different, lower-entropy distribution than the true one.
- SVHN samples show low diversity (e.g., frequent digit 8), illustrating the low-entropy issue.
- Minor typo: line 141 should read “usually” (s/usally/usually/).
- Update: paper should clarify PMD limitations versus MMD — MMD can work with batch_size=2 using an unbiased estimator and SGD to converge with small minibatches.
- PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, unlike MMD.
