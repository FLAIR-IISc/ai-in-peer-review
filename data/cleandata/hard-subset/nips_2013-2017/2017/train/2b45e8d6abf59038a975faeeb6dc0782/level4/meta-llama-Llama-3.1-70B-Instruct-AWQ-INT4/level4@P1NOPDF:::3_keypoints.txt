- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling easy integration with neural network models.
- Provides a thorough empirical and theoretical comparison of PMD with MMD, the leading population-based divergence in ML.
- Authors acknowledge exact PMD computation has cubic complexity and propose a quadratic-time approximation.
- Empirical results show the quadratic approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.
- Theoretical results appear correct and the experimental analysis is adequate.
- Comparisons include PMD vs. MMD for generative modeling and PMD vs. other methods for domain adaptation.
- Suggested improvement: evaluate PMD on multimodal generative tasks when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes to assess robustness.
- Overall assessment: a good paper presenting a potentially useful distribution-comparison method.
