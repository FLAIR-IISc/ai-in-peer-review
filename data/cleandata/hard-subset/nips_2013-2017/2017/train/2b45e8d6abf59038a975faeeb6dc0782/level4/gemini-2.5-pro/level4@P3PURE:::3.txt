The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and show that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which allows for the training of neural network-based models. The paper provides a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning.

The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately references prior work. The theoretical results appear sound. A notable strength is the authors' candid discussion of the method's drawbacks: while the exact calculation has cubic complexity, they propose a quadratic-complexity approximation and show empirically that it does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD against MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, as well as against MMD for generative modeling. However, I would have liked to see the method evaluated on generative modeling tasks in highly multi-modal domains. I am curious how PMD performs when the sample size N is smaller than the number of modes in the distribution.

Overall, this is a good paper that introduces a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
