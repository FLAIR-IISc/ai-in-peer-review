- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a differentiable estimator of distance between probability distributions.  
- Proves PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Differentiability enables using PMD as a training objective for neural-network models.  
- Thorough comparison to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) as a baseline population divergence.  
- Exact PMD computation is cubic in sample size, raising computational concerns.  
- Proposes and evaluates a quadratic-complexity approximation with minimal performance loss.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear sound and well-presented.  
- Experimental evaluation compares PMD to MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Suggests further study of PMD on multi-modal generative modeling and when population size N is smaller than number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with useful properties (differentiability, theoretical guarantees, competitive empirical performance).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, addresses MMD limitations, demonstrates empirical gains, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational cost of exact PMD and limited exploration in complex multi-modal generative scenarios.
