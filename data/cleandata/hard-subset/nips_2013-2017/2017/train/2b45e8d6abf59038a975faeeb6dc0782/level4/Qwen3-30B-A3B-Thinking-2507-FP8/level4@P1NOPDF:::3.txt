

The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, demonstrating it as a consistent estimator for the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually straightforward and differentiable, clearly enabling training of neural network-based models. The paper provides a thorough comparison of PMD against MMD, the dominant population-based divergence in machine learning. The authors acknowledge PMD's limitation: exact computation has cubic complexity, but they propose a quadratic-time approximation that, as shown in experiments, does not significantly compromise statistical efficacy. The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately cites prior work. The theoretical claims appear sound, and the experimental analysis is sufficient, comparing PMD against MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, as well as evaluating PMD against MMD in generative modeling. A notable omission is the application of PMD to generative modeling in high-mode settings (e.g., multi-modal distributions), where the method's behavior when the sample size $N$ is smaller than the number of modes remains unexplored. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially valuable tool for distribution comparison.
