- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling neural-network-based training.
- Provides thorough empirical comparison against MMD, the dominant population-based divergence.
- Notes computational limitation: exact PMD computation has cubic time complexity.
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation that experiments show does not substantially degrade statistical performance.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately cites prior work.
- Theoretical claims appear sound.
- Experimental analysis is sufficient, including domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons versus MMD and other methods.
- Significant omission: no evaluation in high-mode generative settings (e.g., when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes).
- Overall assessment: strong paper offering a potentially valuable tool for distribution comparison.
