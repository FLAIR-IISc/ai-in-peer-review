- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance via minimum-weight bipartite matching between samples.
- Theoretically proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Positioned as a direct alternative to MMD with clearer links to Wasserstein distance.
- Practical advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth hyperparameter, stronger gradient signals, and viable with smaller mini-batches.
- Authors transparently report computational cost (exact O(N³)) and introduce a justified O(N²) approximation.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, showing improved performance and faster convergence on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.
- Manuscript is clearly written, well-motivated, and methodologically well-explained.
- Primary weakness: computational scaling may limit applicability for very large population sizes compared to MMD.
- Suggested future analysis: behavior of PMD on highly multi-modal distributions, especially when N is smaller than the number of modes.
