- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and proves its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
- PMD estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network-based models.
- Provides a comprehensive comparison of PMD to MMD, the dominant population-based divergence in ML.
- Notes exact PMD has cubic complexity but proposes and empirically validates a quadratic-complexity approximation with strong statistical performance.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work.
- Theoretical results appear sound.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, including comparisons for domain adaptation and generative modeling against MMD and other methods.
- Lacks experiments on multi-modal generative tasks and raises concern about performance when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
