- Introduces PMD, a population-based discrepancy, and proves it is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural network–based models.  
- Provides a thorough comparison to MMD, the main population-based discrepancy used in ML.  
- Notes computational drawback of exact computation being cubic time, but proposes a quadratic-time approximation.  
- Empirically shows the quadratic approximation does not substantially harm statistical performance.  
- Manuscript is clearly organized, well written, and cites appropriate prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Empirical evaluation is adequate: PMD compared to MMD and other baselines for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modeling.  
- Lacks generative modeling experiments on many-mode datasets; unclear how PMD behaves when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
