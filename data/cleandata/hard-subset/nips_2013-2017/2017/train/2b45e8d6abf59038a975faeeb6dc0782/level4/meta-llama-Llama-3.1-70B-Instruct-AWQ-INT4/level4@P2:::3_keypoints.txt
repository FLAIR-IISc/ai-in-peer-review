- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), defined via minimum-weight matching of samples, and proves it is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as the training objective.  
- Thoroughly compares PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), claiming advantages such as fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller required mini-batch sizes.  
- Experimental results show PMD outperforms MMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks in both performance and convergence speed.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work appropriately.  
- Main computational drawback: exact PMD has cubic complexity; authors propose a quadratic-approximation that does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Suggested future work: evaluate PMD on generative modeling with many modes, particularly when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, potentially useful method with adequate experimental validation.
