- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to MMD, showing PMD is a sampled Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance with claimed advantages: stronger gradients, smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate PMD enables much smaller mini-batch sizes for training.  
- The stronger-gradients claim is not satisfactorily verified; MMD’s dependence on kernel bandwidth (sigma) requires comparing against the best sigma or a range of sigmas for fairness.  
- The claim of fewer hyperparameters is weak: PMD relies on a chosen distance metric, which can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Distance metrics can be unreliable in high-dimensional spaces, potentially limiting PMD’s success on high-dimensional data.  
- MMD has established bandwidth-selection heuristics; PMD should be compared to MMD using those heuristics.  
- Overall verdict: PMD’s ability to use small mini-batches and speed up training is valuable, but reliance on a distance metric is a notable limitation.
