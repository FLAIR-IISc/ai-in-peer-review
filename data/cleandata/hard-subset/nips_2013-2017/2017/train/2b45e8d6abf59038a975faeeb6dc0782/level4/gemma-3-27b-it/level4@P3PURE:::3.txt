The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, a key property that enables training of neural network-based models. The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence used in machine learning. They acknowledge limitations of their method – specifically, the cubic complexity of exact calculation – and propose an approximation with quadratic complexity, demonstrating through empirical results that this approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance. 

The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately cites relevant prior work. The theoretical results appear sound. The experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods in the context of domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modelling. 

I would have appreciated seeing the method evaluated on generative modelling tasks in domains with many modes. Specifically, I wonder about PMD’s performance when the sample size, *N*, is smaller than the number of modes in the underlying distribution. 

Overall, this is a strong paper that introduces a potentially very useful method for comparing probability distributions.
