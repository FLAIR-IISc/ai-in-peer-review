- Introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence that is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance and is conceptually simple and differentiable.  
- Differentiability makes PMD suitable for integration into neural network–based models, aiding practical implementation.  
- Comprehensive comparison to MMD highlights PMD’s benefits and trade-offs relative to the dominant population-based divergence.  
- Exact PMD has cubic computational complexity, but authors propose a quadratic-complexity approximation.  
- The quadratic approximation shows minimal empirical degradation in statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and engages thoroughly with prior work; theoretical and mathematical arguments appear sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, demonstrating promising results in domain adaptation and generative modeling compared to MMD.  
- Open question: performance on high-modality generative tasks when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes—potential limitation not yet explored.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with theoretical guarantees, differentiability, and competitive empirical performance, likely to be useful to the ML community.
