- Proposal: Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD, presented as a sampled approximation to the Wasserstein distance.  
- Claims: PMD yields stronger gradients, works with small mini-batches, and involves fewer hyperparameters than MMD.  
- Motivation: Addresses MMD’s weak gradients and large mini-batch requirements.  
- Empirical strength: Demonstrates PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batch sizes, suggesting faster and more stable training.  
- Theoretical appeal: Connection to Wasserstein distance gives principled grounding.  
- Gradient evidence concern: Claims of stronger gradients are unconvincing without comparisons to MMD with properly tuned kernel bandwidths.  
- Hyperparameter concern: “Fewer hyperparameters” is questionable because PMD depends on a chosen distance metric, which introduces its own sensitivities.  
- High-dimensional concern: Metric dependence may fail in high-dimensional settings due to distance concentration and feature-scaling issues.  
- Missing MMD baselines: Paper does not adequately compare to standard MMD heuristics (median heuristic) or multi-kernel approaches that reduce bandwidth sensitivity.  
- Suggested experiments: Compare against MMD with tuned sigmas, multi-kernel MMD, and report gradient norms across a range of bandwidths.  
- Sensitivity analysis request: Clarify PMD hyperparameters, provide sensitivity analyses for metric choices, and give practical guidelines analogous to MMD heuristics.  
- Wasserstein clarification: Detail PMD’s approximation quality to Wasserstein, including biases and sample complexity.  
- Overall assessment: Promising practical improvement for small-batch training, but needs stronger, well-tuned MMD comparisons and analyses of metric sensitivity and high-dimensional robustness.
