- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.
- Shows PMD can be viewed as a sampled version of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.
- Claims advantages of PMD: stronger gradients, support for smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.
- Argues PMD addresses known MMD issues in generative model training (weak gradients and large batch requirements).
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batches.
- Evidence for PMD providing stronger gradients is weak; comparison should account for MMD’s scale (sigma) selection.
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is unsubstantiated because PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce hyperparameters.
- Obtaining reliable distance metrics in high-dimensional spaces is hard, so PMD may share MMD’s limitations in high dimensions.
- Established heuristics exist for MMD bandwidth selection; the paper should compare PMD against MMD using those heuristics.
- Overall: PMD speeds training via small batches and is a meaningful improvement, but its reliance on distance metrics may limit applicability to high-dimensional data.
