1. Paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed via exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.  
2. Strength: solid empirical validation demonstrating PMD’s behavior through experiments.  
3. Weakness: scalability — PMD needs large minibatch sizes N to estimate Wasserstein distance for multimodal distributions.  
4. Weakness: optimization pitfalls — small N can lead to convergence to incorrect optima (e.g., reduced entropy).  
5. Weakness: empirical evidence (Fig. 4) shows limited diversity in SVHN samples, indicating practical failures at typical minibatch sizes.  
6. Minor issue: typo at Line 141 ("usally" → "usually").  
7. Rebuttal point: MMD has an unbiased estimator usable with batch size = 2, enabling SGD training and convergence guarantees with small minibatches.  
8. Rebuttal request: PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator; authors should explicitly state this limitation and contrast it with MMD.
