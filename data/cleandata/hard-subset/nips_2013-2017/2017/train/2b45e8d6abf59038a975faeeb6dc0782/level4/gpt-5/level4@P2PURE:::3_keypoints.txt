- Proposes PMD, a population-based divergence between distributions.  
- Shows PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, suitable for training neural networks.  
- Thorough empirical comparison to MMD, the prominent population-based divergence.  
- Exact computation has cubic complexity (drawback).  
- Introduces a quadratic-time approximation and empirically shows little statistical degradation.  
- Paper is well structured, well written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate: domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons.  
- Reviewer requests application to generative modeling with many modes and questions PMD performance when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper presenting a potentially very useful distribution-comparison method.
