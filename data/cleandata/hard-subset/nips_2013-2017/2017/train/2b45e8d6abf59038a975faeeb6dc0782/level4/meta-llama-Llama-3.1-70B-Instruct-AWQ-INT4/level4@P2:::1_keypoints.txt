- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based estimator of the Wasserstein metric and an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective.  
- PMD is defined via minimum weight matching of sample populations and is shown to be a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Paper gives a clear, concise related-work overview and contrasts PMD with Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Authors claim PMD has advantages over MMD: fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller required mini-batch sizes.  
- Experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed; generated images appear less noisy.  
- Major computational drawback: exact PMD requires O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms, which may be impractical for large N.  
- Potential statistical limitation: PMD may need large N to estimate Wasserstein distance between diverse multimodal distributions, risking biased/low-entropy learned distributions when N is small.  
- Paper does not provide a clear solution or analysis for the large-N requirement in multimodal settings.  
- Unclear whether PMD admits a known unbiased gradient estimator, raising concerns about convergence properties.  
- Rebuttal added experiments and clarifications but did not fully resolve questions about gradient bias and large-N behavior.  
- Minor typo noted: Line 141 "usally" should be "usually".  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel estimator, promising empirical results, and clear positioning versus prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational cost, unresolved large-N multimodal issues, and unclear gradient unbiasedness/convergence guarantees.
