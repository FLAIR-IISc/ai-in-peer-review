- Introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence estimator for the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD is a consistent estimator with solid theoretical grounding.  
- Method is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling integration into neural network training.  
- Thorough comparison to MMD, highlighting PMD’s distinct theoretical and practical advantages.  
- Exact PMD computation is cubic in sample size; authors propose a quadratic-time approximation.  
- Quadratic approximation empirically maintains competitive statistical performance across benchmarks.  
- Theoretical analysis includes proofs of consistency and convergence properties.  
- Comprehensive experiments on domain adaptation (Office-Home, VisDA) and generative modeling (CIFAR-10, CelebA) show consistent improvements.  
- Useful discussion of MMD’s limitations (kernel sensitivity, weaker Wasserstein approximation guarantees).  
- Presentation is clear and well-structured with appropriate references; experimental setup details could be slightly improved.  
- Notable gap: missing evaluation on high-mode generative scenarios where sample size N < number of modes K.  
- Overall assessment: technically rigorous, practically useful contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
