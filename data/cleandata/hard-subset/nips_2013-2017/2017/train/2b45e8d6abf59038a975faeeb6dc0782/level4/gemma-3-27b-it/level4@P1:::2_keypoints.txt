- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein metric and an alternative to MMD.  
- Claims PMD yields stronger gradients and works with much smaller mini-batches than MMD.  
- Authors show PMD empirically enables smaller mini-batch training and faster convergence.  
- Theoretical connection between PMD and the Wasserstein/Earth Mover’s Distance is clear.  
- Stronger-gradient claim is promising but needs stronger empirical verification.  
- MMD performance is sensitive to kernel bandwidth σ; comparisons should include optimal or a range of σ values.  
- The assertion of fewer hyperparameters is inconclusive since PMD’s distance metric may introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Obtaining reliable distance metrics in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may share MMD’s limitations.  
- Existing heuristics for MMD bandwidth selection should be used for fairer comparisons to PMD.  
- PMD demonstrates strong empirical results on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- PMD likely offers a more stable training process due to stronger gradients.  
- Reliance on a distance metric could limit PMD’s performance on high-dimensional data; gradient analysis should compare against optimized MMD configurations.
