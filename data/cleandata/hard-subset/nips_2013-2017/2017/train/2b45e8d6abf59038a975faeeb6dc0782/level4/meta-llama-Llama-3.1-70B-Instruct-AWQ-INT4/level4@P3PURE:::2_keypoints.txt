- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to MMD, showing PMD is a sampled Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance with claimed advantages: stronger gradients, smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.
- Addresses known MMD problems (weak gradients, large mini-batch need) and positions PMD as a solution for training generative models.
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s ability to work with much smaller mini-batch sizes.
- The stronger-gradients claim is less convincing because MMD’s performance depends on the kernel bandwidth sigma; comparisons should consider optimal or a range of sigmas.
- The claim that PMD has fewer hyperparameters is weak: PMD depends on a distance metric that may introduce its own hyperparameters.
- Obtaining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, which could limit PMD’s effectiveness on high-dimensional data.
- Established heuristics exist for choosing MMD bandwidths (potentially making MMD effectively hyperparameter-free); comparing to these would strengthen the paper’s claims.
- Overall, PMD’s small-batch advantage enables faster training and is a valid improvement, but reliance on a distance metric remains a key limitation.
