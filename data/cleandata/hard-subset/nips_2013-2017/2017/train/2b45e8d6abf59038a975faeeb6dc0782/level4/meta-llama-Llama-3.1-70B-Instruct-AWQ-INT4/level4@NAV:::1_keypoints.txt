- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed exactly (O(N^3)) or approximately (O(N^2)).  
- Strength: Experimental results using this Wasserstein computation are interesting, though generated image quality lags behind Wasserstein GAN.  
- Limitation: Accurately estimating Wasserstein between diverse multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch size N.  
- Risk: With insufficient N, optimization can converge to a global minimum that does not match the true distribution, often yielding lower-entropy models.  
- Empirical concern: SVHN samples (Fig. 4) show low diversity (many digit 8s), supporting the small-N failure mode.  
- Minor fix: Line 141 typo — replace "usally" with "usually."  
- Rebuttal note: Authors’ additional experiments are appreciated but should clarify limitations of PMD versus MMD.  
- Key comparative point: MMD has an unbiased estimator enabling SGD with tiny batches (even N=2) and can converge with small minibatches, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, a significant drawback.
