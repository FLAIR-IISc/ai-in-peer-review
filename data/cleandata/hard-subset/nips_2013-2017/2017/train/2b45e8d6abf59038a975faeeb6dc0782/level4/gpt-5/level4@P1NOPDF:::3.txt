The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and show that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which clearly enables training of neural network–based models.

The authors thoroughly compare PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They also discuss drawbacks: exact computation has cubic complexity, but they propose a quadratic-time approximation and show empirically that this does not substantially degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well structured and well written, and it cites prior work appropriately. The theoretical results appear correct. The experimental analysis is adequate: they compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modelling.

I would have liked to see the method applied to generative modelling in domains with many modes. I also wonder how PMD performs when N is smaller than the number of modes.

Overall, this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
