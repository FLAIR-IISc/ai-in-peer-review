- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural network models.  
- Provides a thorough comparison between PMD and Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Primary drawback: exact computation has cubic complexity.  
- Proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation and shows empirically minimal loss in statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear sound and the experimental analysis is adequate.  
- Experiments compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Suggests improving evaluation on generative modeling tasks with many modes.  
- Open question: how PMD performs when sample size N is smaller than the number of target distribution modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing probability distributions.
