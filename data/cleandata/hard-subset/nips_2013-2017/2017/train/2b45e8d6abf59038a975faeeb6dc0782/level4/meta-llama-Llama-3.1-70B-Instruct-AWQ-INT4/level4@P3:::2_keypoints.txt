- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD and shows PMD is a sampled version of the Wasserstein/earth mover’s distance.  
- Claims PMD provides stronger gradients, supports smaller mini-batches, and involves fewer hyperparameters.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear exposition of MMD’s limitations in generative-model training.  
- Empirical results indicate PMD’s practical benefits, notably effective training with much smaller mini-batches.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric.  
- Stronger-gradient claim is not fully validated because MMD’s behavior depends on the bandwidth sigma; comparisons should use best or swept sigmas.  
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is questionable since PMD depends on a distance metric that may introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Choosing a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is difficult, so PMD may face similar challenges to MMD.  
- Authors should compare PMD against standard MMD bandwidth-selection heuristics, which could mitigate MMD’s hyperparameter issue.  
- PMD’s support for small mini-batches yields faster training and appears to be a valid improvement over large-batch MMD methods.  
- Reliance on a chosen distance metric could limit PMD’s success on high-dimensional data.  
- Overall: promising and potentially valuable contribution, but needs stronger experimental/analytical support on bandwidth choice, distance-metric sensitivity, and high-dimensional performance.
