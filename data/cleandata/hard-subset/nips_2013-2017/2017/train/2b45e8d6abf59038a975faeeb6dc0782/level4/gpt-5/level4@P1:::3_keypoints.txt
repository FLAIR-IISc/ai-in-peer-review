- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based discrepancy using minimum-weight bipartite matching between two populations.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein distance.
- Proposes PMD as a differentiable training objective and describes not backpropagating through discrete matchings.
- Computes matchings exactly with Hungarian algorithm (O(N^3)) or approximately in O(N^2) and shows approximations perform well.
- Presents an SGD procedure that fixes matchings over several updates to enable efficient training.
- Positions PMD relative to optimal transport (empirical OT between equal-weight populations) and to MMD (kernel-based discrepancy).
- Argues PMD avoids kernel bandwidth tuning, yields stronger gradients than RBF-MMD, and allows smaller SGD mini-batches.
- Evaluates PMD on domain adaptation (as a feature-alignment regularizer) and on implicit generative modeling (pixel and code space) vs MMD and baselines.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and follows standard OT tools; learning algorithm and non-differentiation choices are clearly described.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate with analyses of convergence, hyperparameter sensitivity, and matching approximations; fair validation protocol in domain adaptation.
- Originality: PMD is a known OT construction but the paper’s contribution is exposing it as a practical deep-learning objective and thoroughly comparing to MMD.
- Significance: Simple, practical method that avoids MMD pitfalls and shows gains in convergence speed, accuracy, and sample quality in experiments.
- Weakness: Computational cost of matching can be high; scalability depends on approximations and implementation.
- Weakness/question: Generative results do not test highly multimodal datasets; unclear behavior when population size N is smaller than number of modes.
- Pros summarized: simple, consistent, practical training recipe, addresses MMD issues, empirically competitive or superior.
- Cons summarized: matching cost scalability concerns and limited multimodal generative evaluation.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with convincing theory and empirical validation; recommendation to accept.
