- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed with exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation of this Wasserstein computation is a valuable contribution.  
- Strength: Demonstrates method’s application with generated images, though image quality lags behind Wasserstein GANs.  
- Weakness: PMD requires large sample sizes (N) to accurately estimate Wasserstein, problematic for diverse/multimodal distributions (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians, variance estimation).  
- Weakness: Insufficiently large N can lead optimization to suboptimal solutions distinct from the true distribution (e.g., reduced entropy).  
- Weakness: SVHN samples show low diversity, with an over-representation of the digit ‘8’.  
- Minor: Typo at Line 141 — "usally" should be "usually."  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors should clarify PMD limitations vs MMD; MMD can work with very small batches using an unbiased estimator for SGD convergence, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator.
