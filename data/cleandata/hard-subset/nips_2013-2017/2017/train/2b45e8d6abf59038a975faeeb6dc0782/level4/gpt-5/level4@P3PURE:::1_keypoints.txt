- Paper introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches, computed via exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.  
- Positive: interesting to see direct computation of Wasserstein distance in experiments.  
- PMD likely requires large minibatch sizes N to accurately estimate Wasserstein between diverse, multimodal distributions.  
- Potential failures expected on mixture-of-Gaussians scenarios, including learning variances.  
- With small N, optimization may converge to a global minimum that differs from the true distribution (e.g., learned distribution with lower entropy).  
- SVHN samples show low diversity (digit 8 appears frequently).  
- Generated images are inferior to those produced by Wasserstein GAN.  
- Minor typo: Line 141 “usally” → “usually”.  
- Rebuttal note: paper should clearly state limitations of both PMD and MMD.  
- MMD works with batch_size = 2.  
- MMD can be trained with SGD using the unbiased estimator from “A Kernel Two-Sample Test,” so it can converge with small minibatches.  
- PMD does not have a known unbiased estimator of the gradient.
