Here, the authors present PMD as a population-based variation among probability distributions and demonstrate that it is a consistent measure of Wasserstein distance.

The presented measure is conceptually simple and differentiable, which creates a clear opportunity for training NN-based models.

The authors thoroughly compare PMD with MMD, which is the most prominent population-based variation in machine learning.

The authors comment on the shortcomings of their method: while exact computation requires dense complexity, they propose using an approximation that is of quadratic complexity, and their empirical results show that this does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well-structured and written, and includes references to previous work where necessary.

The theoretical results seem correct.

The experimental analysis is sufficient. They compare PMD with MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and with MMD for generative modeling.

I wanted to see if this method is being used for generative modeling in many modded domains. I wonder if PMD works when the number of modes is smaller than N.

Considering everything, I think this is a good paper, which presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
