- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to MMD, effectively a sampled Wasserstein (earth mover’s) metric.  
- Claims PMD provides more robust gradients, supports smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters.  
- Notes MMD is hindered in generative-model training by weak gradients and large mini-batch requirements.  
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s advantage in enabling much smaller mini-batch sizes and faster training.  
- Evidence for PMD producing stronger gradients is inconclusive because MMD’s performance depends on the kernel bandwidth (sigma); comparisons must consider optimal sigma or a sigma range.  
- The claim of fewer hyperparameters is weak: PMD relies on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Determining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face similar limitations to MMD.  
- Established heuristics for MMD bandwidth selection can make MMD effectively hyperparameter-free and should be included in comparisons.  
- Summary: PMD is a promising, practical improvement for small-batch training but its dependence on distance metrics may limit effectiveness on high-dimensional data.
