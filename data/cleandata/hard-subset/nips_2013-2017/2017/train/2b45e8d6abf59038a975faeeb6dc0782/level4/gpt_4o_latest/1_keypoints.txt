- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance computed between two minibatches, using either exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.
- Experiments using PMD are intriguing, but generated image quality lags behind Wasserstein GAN.
- Accurately estimating Wasserstein distance between diverse multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch sizes (N), posing practical challenges.
- PMD may struggle even on simple tasks like matching a mixture of Gaussians, notably in learning variances.
- With insufficient N, optimization can converge to a global minimum that does not match the true distribution (e.g., lower entropy).
- Empirical evidence: SVHN samples show low diversity and overrepresentation of digit 8.
- Minor typo: Line 141 — "usally" should be "usually."
- Reviewer appreciated additional rebuttal experiments.
- Authors should clarify limitations vs MMD: MMD can work with tiny batches (even N=2) using an unbiased estimator enabling SGD and correct convergence, whereas PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, limiting small-batch applicability.
