The authors introduce PMD, a novel population-based divergence measure, and demonstrate its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. 
This estimator benefits from conceptual simplicity and differentiability, facilitating its application within neural network-based models.
A comprehensive comparison to MMD, a widely used population-based divergence in machine learning, is provided.
The authors acknowledge the cubic computational complexity of exact PMD calculation and address this with a quadratic-complexity approximation, demonstrating through empirical results that this approximation maintains strong statistical performance.
The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and appropriately cites relevant prior work.
The theoretical foundations appear sound, and the experimental evaluation is satisfactory, encompassing comparisons to MMD and other techniques for domain adaptation, as well as a comparison to MMD in the context of generative modelling.
Further investigation into the method’s performance in generative modelling scenarios involving multi-modal distributions would be valuable, particularly considering cases where the sample size (N) is smaller than the number of modes.
Overall, this is a strong contribution that presents a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
