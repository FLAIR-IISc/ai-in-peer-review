- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.
- Provides a thorough comparison to MMD, the main population-based divergence used in ML.
- Acknowledges exact PMD has cubic complexity and proposes a validated quadratic approximation that retains strong performance.
- Theoretical results appear sound; paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.
- Experimental analysis is adequate, including domain adaptation and generative-model comparisons to MMD.
- Missing evaluation on generative modeling with multimodal distributions; raises concern about performance when sample size N < number of modes.
- Overall judgment: strong paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
