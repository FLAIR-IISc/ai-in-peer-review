- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for distribution matching.
- Shows PMD is a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein metric (Earth Mover’s Distance).
- Claims PMD offers stronger training gradients, works with small mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters.
- Correctly notes MMD’s known weaknesses: weak gradients and need for large mini-batches.
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate PMD’s effectiveness with small mini-batches.
- The stronger-gradients claim is less convincing because MMD performance depends on kernel bandwidth (sigma); fair comparison needs optimal or range of sigmas.
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is questionable since PMD requires choosing a distance metric, which can itself have hyperparameters.
- Defining meaningful distances in high-dimensional spaces is challenging, so PMD may face the same fundamental issues MMD faces with kernel choice.
- A proper comparison should benchmark MMD with standard bandwidth heuristics to treat both methods as effectively hyperparameter-free.
- Overall, PMD is a practical improvement for small-batch training, but reliance on a user-defined distance metric raises concerns for high-dimensional data.
