- Proposes PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.  
- Thorough comparison to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Exact PMD has cubic computational complexity (primary drawback).  
- Authors introduce a quadratic-time approximation to address complexity.  
- Empirical results show the approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear sound and experimental analysis is adequate.  
- Evaluated PMD for domain adaptation and generative modeling against MMD and other methods.  
- Reviewer requests additional experiments on generative modeling with many modes and when sample size < number of modes.  
- Overall: a good, promising, and potentially very useful method.
