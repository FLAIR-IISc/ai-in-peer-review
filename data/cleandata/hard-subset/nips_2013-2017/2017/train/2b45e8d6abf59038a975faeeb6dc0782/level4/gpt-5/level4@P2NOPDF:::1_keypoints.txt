- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as minibatch Wasserstein distance evaluated with exact (O(N^3)) or approximate (O(N^2)) solvers.
- Experimental exploration of minibatch-based Wasserstein computations is interesting and timely.
- Provides empirical comparisons, though image quality lags behind reported Wasserstein GAN results.
- Accurately approximating Wasserstein distance appears to require large minibatch sizes N, problematic for complex multimodal distributions.
- Small N can produce optimization minima that differ from the true distribution, biasing learned models toward lower-entropy solutions (e.g., limited diversity in SVHN with too many “8”s).
- Minor: typo on Line 141 (“usally” → “usually”).
- MMD can operate with very small batch sizes (even batch_size = 2).
- The unbiased MMD estimator from “A Kernel Two-Sample Test” allows straightforward SGD and can converge with small minibatches.
- PMD has no known unbiased gradient estimator, making it more sensitive to batch size and raising convergence concerns under practical minibatch regimes.
- Overall: conceptually simple and experimentally valuable, but reliance on large N, lack of unbiased gradients, and reduced sample diversity weaken practical impact versus MMD or WGAN variants.
- Recommendation: clarify trade-offs and limitations of PMD (and comparison to MMD) when batch sizes are constrained.
