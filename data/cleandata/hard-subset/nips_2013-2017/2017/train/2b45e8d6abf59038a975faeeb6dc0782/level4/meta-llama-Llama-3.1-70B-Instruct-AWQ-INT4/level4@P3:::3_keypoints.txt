- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a new sample-based estimator for distribution distance.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Thoroughly compares PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD).  
- Highlights PMD advantages: no kernel bandwidth hyperparameter, stronger gradients, and ability to use smaller mini-batches.  
- Comprehensive experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Empirically outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Provides empirical analysis of PMD’s approximation error, showing effective Wasserstein estimation.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and experimental analysis is adequate.  
- Main weakness: computational cost (exact cubic, approximate quadratic).  
- Approximation does not significantly degrade statistical performance and GPU can mitigate cost.  
- Suggested future work: apply PMD to generative modeling with many modes and limited samples.  
- Overall assessment: significant, practically useful contribution likely to impact machine learning applications.
