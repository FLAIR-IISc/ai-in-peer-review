

The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, demonstrating it as a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, providing a clear advantage for training neural network-based models. The authors conduct a thorough comparison of PMD against MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They acknowledge their method's drawbacks: exact calculation entails cubic complexity, but propose an approximation with quadratic complexity, showing empirically that this approximation incurs minimal degradation in statistical performance. The paper is well-structured and written, with appropriate references to prior work. The theoretical results appear correct. The experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modeling. I would have liked to see PMD applied to generative modeling in domains with many modes, particularly whether it remains effective when the sample size \(n\) is smaller than the number of modes. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for distribution comparison.
