The authors present Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel differentiable estimator for the distance between probability distributions, and demonstrate its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. This estimator is conceptually straightforward and, crucially, differentiable, enabling its application as a training objective for neural network-based models.

The paper offers a thorough comparison of PMD with Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), a widely used population-based divergence in machine learning. The authors acknowledge and address the computational complexity of exact PMD calculation (cubic in the sample size) by proposing and evaluating an approximation with quadratic complexity, demonstrating minimal performance degradation. 

The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately references relevant prior work. The theoretical results appear sound, and the experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks. 

One potential avenue for future investigation would be to assess PMD’s performance in generative modeling scenarios with multi-modal distributions. It would be valuable to understand how PMD behaves when the population size (N) is smaller than the number of modes in the target distribution.

Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially valuable method for comparing distributions. The advantages of PMD – differentiability, theoretical guarantees, and competitive empirical performance – suggest it could become a useful tool in various machine learning applications.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically grounded approach to distribution distance estimation.
*   Addresses limitations of existing methods (e.g., MMD’s bandwidth tuning and gradient issues).
*   Demonstrated empirical improvements over MMD in both domain adaptation and generative modeling.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of the method and results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Computational complexity of exact PMD calculation, although mitigated by the proposed approximation.
*   Limited exploration of PMD’s performance in complex generative modeling scenarios (specifically, multi-modal distributions).
