- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based distance estimator defined as the minimum weight matching between random populations from two distributions.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and proposes a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.
- Relates PMD to MMD and the Wasserstein metric, arguing PMD has advantages over MMD (fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, smaller mini-batches).
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling, with experiments showing better performance and faster convergence than MMD.
- Strength: Novel method with thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to Wasserstein and MMD.
- Strength: Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness across tasks.
- Quality/Clarity: Paper is technically sound, well-written, with clear notation and helpful figures.
- Originality/Significance: Significant contribution with potential impact on domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Weakness: Assumes distributions are continuous, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical comparison with other methods, especially Wasserstein GANs.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper analysis of PMD and MMD limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons and limitations.
