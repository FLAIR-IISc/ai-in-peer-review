- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a novel sample-based distance estimator addressing MMD drawbacks (kernel sensitivity, vanishing gradients, large minibatch needs).
- Defines PMD as the minimum weight matching between sample populations and proves strong consistency to the first Wasserstein metric.
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling, showing improved performance and faster convergence versus MMD.
- Strong theoretical grounding via connection to the Wasserstein metric.
- Empirical results are encouraging, demonstrating potential practical benefits.
- Clear articulation of MMD limitations and how PMD intends to address them (gradient behavior, minibatch size).
- Inclusion of pseudocode and discussion of computational complexity is helpful for reproducibility.
- Strength: observation of stronger, more consistent gradients compared to MMD.
- Strength: thoughtful analysis of trade-offs between exact and approximate matching algorithms.
- Weakness: insufficient theoretical analysis of PMD sensitivity to population size N, especially for diverse/multimodal distributions.
- Weakness: use of L1 distance may be suboptimal for natural images, evidenced by blurry generative outputs.
- Weakness: claim of “stronger gradients” lacks discussion on whether those gradients are more informative for optimization.
- Weakness: paper does not address existing unbiased MMD estimators that enable small-minibatch training, which could reduce PMD's advantages.
- Overall: valuable, theoretically sound contribution with promising empirical results but needing further analysis and clarification on limitations.
