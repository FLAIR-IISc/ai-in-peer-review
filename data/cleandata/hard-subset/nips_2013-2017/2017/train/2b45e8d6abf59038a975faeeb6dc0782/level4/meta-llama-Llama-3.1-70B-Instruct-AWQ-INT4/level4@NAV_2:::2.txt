This paper proposes the population matching discrepancy (PMD) as a novel approach for estimating the distance between two distributions based on samples, which is a crucial task in various deep learning applications. The authors establish a connection between PMD and the Wasserstein metric, demonstrating that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. Furthermore, they develop an algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD as an objective.

The paper provides a comprehensive comparison between PMD and the popular maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) method. The authors highlight several advantages of PMD, including the absence of a kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, stronger gradients, and the ability to use smaller mini-batch sizes for gradient-based optimization. These advantages are verified through empirical results on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks, where PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed.
