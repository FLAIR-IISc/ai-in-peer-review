- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance that is differentiable and suitable for neural network training.
- Conceptually simple estimator with theoretical correctness and clear proofs.
- Thorough empirical and theoretical comparison to MMD, the main competing population-based divergence.
- Notes computational drawback: exact PMD computation is cubic-time.
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation and shows empirically that it largely preserves statistical performance.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, including domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons to MMD.
- Requests additional generative-modeling experiments in high-mode settings, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially very useful method.
