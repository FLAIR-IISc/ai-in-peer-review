The authors present PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and demonstrate that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which allows for seamless integration with neural network-based models.

The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD with MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They also acknowledge the limitations of their method, noting that exact calculation has cubic complexity. To address this, they propose an approximation with quadratic complexity and show in their empirical results that this does not significantly degrade statistical performance.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with proper citations to previous work where relevant. The theoretical results appear to be correct, and the experimental analysis is adequate. The authors compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, as well as to MMD for generative modeling.

One potential area for improvement is the application of PMD to generative modeling in domains with multiple modes. It would be interesting to see how the method performs when the number of samples (N) is smaller than the number of modes. Exploring this scenario could provide valuable insights into the robustness of PMD.

Overall, I consider this to be a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
