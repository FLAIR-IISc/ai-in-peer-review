- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between two minibatches.  
- PMD computed via exact algorithm O(N^3) or approximate algorithm O(N^2).  
- Paper contains interesting experiments using minibatch Wasserstein computations.  
- Generated image quality is inferior to Wasserstein GAN.  
- PMD likely requires large minibatches to estimate Wasserstein between diverse, multimodal distributions.  
- Small N can fail on mixtures (e.g., Gaussians) and when learning variances, producing incorrect low-entropy solutions.  
- Empirical example: SVHN samples show low diversity (digit 8 overrepresented).  
- Minor correction: Line 141 should read "usually" instead of "usally".  
- After rebuttal, reviewer acknowledges attempts to address concerns but requests clearer statement of limitations for PMD and MMD.  
- MMD can be used with batch size as small as 2 and trained with SGD using the unbiased MMD estimator.  
- PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, which is a significant limitation.
