- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed with exact (O(N^3)) or approximate (O(N^2)) algorithms.
- The exploration is interesting and the empirical evaluation provides valuable insights; generated samples show potential but do not match WGAN quality.
- Method is sensitive to minibatch size and may struggle with complex or multimodal distributions, risking suboptimal convergence.
- With insufficient minibatch size, optimization can produce low-entropy learned distributions that satisfy PMD but do not represent the true distribution.
- SVHN results show limited diversity and over-representation of digit '8', supporting the minibatch-size/diversity concern.
- Paper does not adequately address practical computational costs of Wasserstein computation for very large N, even with approximations.
- PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, unlike MMD which can work with very small batches and has unbiased gradients, raising convergence and theoretical concerns.
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge PMD vs MMD differences and investigate strategies to mitigate minibatch sensitivity or develop an unbiased gradient estimator.
- Minor issue: typo "s/usally/usually/" at line 141.
- Overall recommendation: interesting idea but significant limitations warrant further work before recommending strong acceptance.
