- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD): Wasserstein distance between two minibatches from respective distributions.  
- Computes Wasserstein distance via exact O(N^3) algorithm or approximate O(N^2) algorithm.  
- Presents an interesting application to generative modeling with valuable experimental image results.  
- Estimating Wasserstein between diverse, multimodal distributions likely requires large minibatch size N.  
- Small N may cause optimization to converge to a global minimum far from the true distribution, yielding lower-entropy learned models.  
- Empirical concern: SVHN samples show low diversity and overrepresentation of digit 8, indicating mode-collapse risk.  
- Minor editorial issues (e.g., typo "usally" → "usually" at Line 141).  
- Rebuttal/clarification needed comparing PMD and MMD limitations explicitly.  
- MMD can be trained with small minibatches using the unbiased MMD estimator from "A Kernel Two-Sample Test" and can converge correctly.  
- PMD currently lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, which may limit its performance with small minibatches.
