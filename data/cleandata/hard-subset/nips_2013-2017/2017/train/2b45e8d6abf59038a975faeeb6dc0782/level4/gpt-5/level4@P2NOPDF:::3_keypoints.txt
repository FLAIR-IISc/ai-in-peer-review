- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.  
- Thorough comparison to MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Acknowledges computational cost: exact computation is cubic in sample size.  
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation and shows empirically that it does not substantially degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well structured, well written, and provides appropriate references; theoretical results appear sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate (domain adaptation and generative modeling comparisons).  
- Limitation: lacks experiments in highly multimodal generative settings, especially when sample size N is smaller than number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper presenting a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
