- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence that consistently estimates the Wasserstein distance.
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling integration into neural network training.
- Thorough comparison to MMD, highlighting key differences and positioning PMD relative to dominant methods.
- Exact PMD computation has cubic time complexity, which is a notable computational drawback.
- Authors propose a quadratic-time approximation to mitigate cubic cost.
- Empirically, the quadratic approximation maintains statistical performance close to the exact estimator.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates contributions within existing literature.
- Theoretical claims are sound and properly supported.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods on domain adaptation and validating MMD in generative modeling.
- Missing evaluation of PMD in high-mode generative modeling, especially when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.
- This omission limits confidence in PMD’s applicability to challenging multimodal generative tasks.
- Overall, a strong, well-executed contribution: a theoretically grounded, practical alternative to MMD that is simple, differentiable, and empirically robust.
