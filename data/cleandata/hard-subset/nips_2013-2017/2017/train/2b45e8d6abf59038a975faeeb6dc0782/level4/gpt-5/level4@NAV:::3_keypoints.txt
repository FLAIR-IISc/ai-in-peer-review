- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence, and shows it consistently estimates the Wasserstein distance.  
- The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling training of neural-network models.  
- Provides a thorough empirical and conceptual comparison with MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Notes a drawback: exact PMD computation has cubic complexity, but authors propose a quadratic approximation.  
- Empirical results indicate the quadratic approximation does not substantially degrade statistical performance.  
- Paper is well structured, well written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate, including comparisons for domain adaptation and generative modeling (against MMD).  
- Reviewer would like to see generative modeling experiments in highly multimodal domains and questions PMD’s performance when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper presenting a potentially useful method for comparing distributions.
