- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence that consistently estimates the Wasserstein distance.  
- PMD estimator is conceptually simple, differentiable, and suitable for training neural networks.  
- Provides a comprehensive comparison between PMD and the widely used MMD.  
- Notes exact PMD computation is cubic, proposes a quadratic approximation, and shows empirically that approximation rarely harms statistical performance.  
- Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Theoretical contributions appear sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is satisfactory, benchmarking PMD vs. MMD and other methods for domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Reviewer requests additional experiments on generative modeling with many modes and on settings where sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper introducing a promising, potentially impactful method.
