The authors propose population matching discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based estimation of the distance between two distributions, and demonstrate its advantages over the popular maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) in various deep learning tasks. The main idea of PMD is to find the minimum weight matching between two random populations from the distributions, and the authors prove that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.

The authors relate their work to previous research on estimating the distance between two distributions, including MMD and the Wasserstein metric. They highlight the drawbacks of MMD, such as its sensitive kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, weak gradients, and large mini-batch size requirement. In contrast, PMD has no kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, stronger gradients, and can use a smaller mini-batch size for gradient-based optimization.

The authors apply PMD to two deep learning tasks, domain adaptation and generative modeling, and demonstrate its superior performance over MMD in terms of both performance and convergence speed. They also provide a thorough analysis of the time complexity of PMD and MMD, and show that the approximated matching algorithm for PMD can be significantly accelerated with GPU.

The paper is well-structured and written, and the authors provide adequate references to previous work. The theoretical results seem correct, and the experimental analysis is thorough. However, I would have liked to see the method being used for generative modeling in domains with many modes, and I wonder if PMD works when the population size N is smaller than the number of modes.

In terms of the quality criteria, the paper scores well on technical soundness, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the PMD algorithm and its theoretical properties, and the paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The idea of using PMD as a sample-based estimation of the distance between two distributions is novel and has the potential to be widely applicable in various machine learning tasks. The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

Pros:

Cons:
