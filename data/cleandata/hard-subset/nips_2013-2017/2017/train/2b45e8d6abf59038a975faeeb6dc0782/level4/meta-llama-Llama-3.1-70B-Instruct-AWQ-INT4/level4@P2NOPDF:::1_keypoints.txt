- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between two minibatches.  
- PMD computed using either an exact O(N^3) algorithm or an approximate O(N^2) method.  
- Experimental results, especially Wasserstein computations, are noteworthy.  
- Generated image quality is inferior to Wasserstein GAN results.  
- PMD likely requires large batch size N to accurately estimate Wasserstein for diverse/multimodal distributions, causing potential failures (e.g., matching mixtures, learning variances).  
- With insufficient N, optimization can converge to a global minimum that misrepresents the true distribution and reduces entropy (SVHN example: low diversity, digit 8 overrepresented).  
- Minor correction: line 141 "usally" should be "usually."  
- Authors should clarify limitations of both PMD and MMD in the manuscript.  
- MMD can be trained with batch size 2 and converge using the unbiased estimator from "A Kernel Two-Sample Test"; PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator, which may hinder its performance.
