- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as the Wasserstein distance between minibatches from target and generated distributions.  
- Strength: Experimental results effectively demonstrate applying a Wasserstein-based discrepancy in training.  
- Limitation: PMD requires large minibatch sizes to estimate Wasserstein accurately for multimodal/complex distributions, failing for small N.  
- Limitation: Optimization can be unstable with small N, leading to low-entropy solutions and mode collapse (e.g., low diversity in SVHN samples).  
- Limitation: PMD lacks a known unbiased gradient estimator analogous to MMD’s (Gretton et al. 2012), making it unsuitable for small-batch SGD; this key distinction is understated.  
- Minor issue: Typo on Line 141 ("usally" → "usually").  
- Rebuttal update: Authors’ response and extra experiments do not resolve why PMD cannot adopt MMD’s unbiased gradient framework.  
- Summary: PMD is theoretically interesting but practically constrained by computational cost, dependence on large N, instability, and lack of unbiased gradients compared to MMD.
