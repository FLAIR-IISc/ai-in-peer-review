- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching.  
- PMD is presented as an empirical approximation to the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.  
- Claimed advantages: stronger gradients, effective training with smaller minibatches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Empirical evidence convincingly supports PMD’s ability to train with small minibatches.  
- Evidence for stronger gradients is weak because MMD gradient quality depends on kernel bandwidth σ; comparisons should consider best or a range of σ.  
- Claim of fewer hyperparameters is under-supported: PMD requires choosing a distance metric, which can introduce its own hyperparameters.  
- Defining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is difficult, so PMD may inherit metric-dependence issues similar to MMD.  
- MMD benefits from common bandwidth-selection heuristics, which can make it effectively hyperparameter-free in practice.  
- Overall: PMD is practically attractive for small-batch training and improves over large-batch MMD methods, but its reliance on a chosen distance metric may limit performance on high-dimensional data.
