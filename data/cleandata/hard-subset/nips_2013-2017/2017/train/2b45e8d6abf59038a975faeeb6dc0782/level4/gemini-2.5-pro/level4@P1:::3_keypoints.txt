- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based divergence using minimum-weight bipartite matching between sample populations.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance.  
- Estimator is differentiable, enabling use as a loss for training deep neural networks via gradient descent.  
- Compared thoroughly to MMD, highlighting practical advantages such as no kernel bandwidth tuning, stronger training gradients, and effective use of smaller mini-batches.  
- Acknowledges exact matching’s computational cost and proposes a faster quadratic-time approximation that performs well empirically.  
- Demonstrates utility on unsupervised domain adaptation and generative modeling with comprehensive experiments supporting claims.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, situates prior work, and candidly discusses limitations.  
- Main open question: behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes — risk of missing modes or inducing mode collapse in generative tasks.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, theoretically sound, practically useful contribution to distributional matching for deep models.
