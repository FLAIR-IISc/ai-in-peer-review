The authors present a novel population-based divergence, referred to as PMD, between probability distributions, demonstrating its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The proposed estimator boasts conceptual simplicity and differentiability, rendering it particularly suitable for training neural network-based models.

A thorough comparison of PMD with MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning, is provided. Notably, the authors acknowledge the limitations of their method, including the cubic complexity of exact calculations. To mitigate this, they propose an approximation with quadratic complexity and demonstrate in their empirical results that this approximation does not significantly compromise statistical performance.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with proper citations to relevant previous work. The theoretical results appear to be correct, and the experimental analysis is adequate. The authors provide a comprehensive comparison of PMD with MMD and other methods in the context of domain adaptation, as well as a comparison with MMD for generative modeling.

One potential avenue for future exploration is the application of PMD to generative modeling in domains with multiple modes. It would be intriguing to investigate the performance of PMD when the sample size is smaller than the number of modes. Nevertheless, considering the contributions and potential of the proposed method, this paper is well-written and presents a potentially very useful tool for comparing distributions.
