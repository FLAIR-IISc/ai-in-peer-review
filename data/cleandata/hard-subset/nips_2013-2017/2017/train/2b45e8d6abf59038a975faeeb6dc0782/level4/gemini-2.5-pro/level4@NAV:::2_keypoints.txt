- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for distribution matching.
- Shows PMD is a sample-based approximation of the Wasserstein metric (Earth Mover’s Distance).
- Claims PMD offers stronger gradients, works with smaller mini-batches, and has fewer hyperparameters than MMD.
- Notes MMD’s known issues in generative model training: weak gradients and reliance on large mini-batches.
- Empirical results convincingly support PMD’s superior performance with small mini-batches.
- Evidence for PMD providing stronger gradients is unconvincing; fair comparison requires tuning or sweeping MMD’s kernel bandwidth (sigma).
- The “fewer hyperparameters” claim is not fully supported because PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce hyperparameters.
- Highlight that defining a reliable distance metric in high-dimensional spaces is difficult, so PMD may share MMD’s fundamental limitations.
- Recommends comparing PMD to MMD variants using standard bandwidth-selection heuristics for a fairer baseline.
- Concludes PMD’s main practical benefit is enabling smaller batches and faster training, but dependence on a chosen distance metric could limit effectiveness in high dimensions.
