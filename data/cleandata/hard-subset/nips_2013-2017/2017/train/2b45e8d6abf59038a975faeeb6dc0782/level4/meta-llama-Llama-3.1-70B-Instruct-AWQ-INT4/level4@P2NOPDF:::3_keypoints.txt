- Introduces PMD, a novel population-based divergence that is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, making it well-suited for training neural networks.  
- Provides a thorough comparison between PMD and MMD, the main population-based divergence in ML.  
- Acknowledges exact PMD’s cubic computational complexity as a limitation.  
- Proposes a quadratic-complexity approximation and empirically shows it preserves statistical performance.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and are clearly presented with proper citations.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate and includes comparisons in domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Suggests future work applying PMD to generative modeling with multiple modes and small sample sizes.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper presenting a potentially useful tool for comparing distributions.
