

The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and establish its consistency as an estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, enabling the training of neural network-based models. The authors provide a thorough comparison of PMD against MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. They acknowledge the method's limitations: exact computation has cubic complexity, but propose a quadratic-complexity approximation that their empirical results demonstrate does not significantly degrade statistical performance. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with appropriate references to prior work. The theoretical results appear correct. The experimental analysis is adequate, comparing PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and to MMD for generative modeling. I would have liked to see PMD applied to generative modeling in domains with many modes, particularly to assess its behavior when the sample size $N$ is smaller than the number of modes. Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a potentially valuable method for comparing probability distributions.
