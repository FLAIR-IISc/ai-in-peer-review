The authors introduce PMD, a population-based divergence between probability distributions, and prove that it is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance. The estimator is conceptually simple and differentiable, which makes it suitable for training neural network–based models.

A major strength of the paper is its thorough comparison of PMD to MMD, the most prominent population-based divergence in machine learning. The authors also discuss the computational drawbacks of their method: the exact computation has cubic complexity, but they propose a quadratic-time approximation and provide empirical evidence that this does not substantially degrade statistical performance. The paper is well structured, clearly written, and appropriately references prior work. Theoretical results appear correct.

The experimental analysis is adequate. The authors compare PMD to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation, and they compare PMD to MMD for generative modeling. I would have liked to see additional experiments on generative modeling in domains with many modes. In particular, I wonder how PMD performs when N is smaller than the number of modes.

Overall, this is a good paper that presents a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
