- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator of distribution distance.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and explains their relationship clearly.
- Proposes an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective, enabling gradient-based training of neural models.
- Thoroughly compares PMD to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), the main population-based divergence in ML.
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling, with faster convergence.
- Visual generative results are high-quality and comparable to or better than MMD outputs.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and properly cites prior work.
- Balances theoretical rigor with practical considerations, providing clear explanations and analysis.
- Exact PMD computation has cubic complexity, which is a potential scalability limitation.
- Authors propose a quadratic approximation that empirically retains statistical performance, making PMD more viable for large-scale use.
- Reviewer requests additional experiments on generative modeling with many modes to evaluate PMD’s behavior.
- Concern about PMD’s behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes—potential practical limitation.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with significant potential impact across ML applications.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.
