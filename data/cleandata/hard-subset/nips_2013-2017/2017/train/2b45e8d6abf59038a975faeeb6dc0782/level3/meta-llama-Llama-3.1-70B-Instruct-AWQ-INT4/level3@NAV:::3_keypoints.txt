- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator of distance between distributions.  
- PMD is defined via minimum weight matching between random populations from the distributions.  
- The authors prove PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Thorough comparison to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) highlights PMD advantages.  
- PMD has fewer hyper-parameters, yields stronger gradients, and allows smaller mini-batch sizes than MMD.  
- Provides a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.  
- Experimental analysis (domain adaptation and generative modeling) is adequate and compares to relevant baselines.  
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Theoretical results appear correct with a detailed analysis of the PMD–Wasserstein connection.  
- Lacks experiments on generative modeling with many modes — an important missing evaluation.  
- Does not analyze PMD behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution; recommendation to accept.
