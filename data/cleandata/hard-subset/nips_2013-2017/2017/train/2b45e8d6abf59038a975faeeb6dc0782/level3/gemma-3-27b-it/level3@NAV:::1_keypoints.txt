- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distribution distance via minimum-weight matching, consistent with the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective, applied to domain adaptation and generative modeling.  
- Motivated as addressing MMD limitations (kernel bandwidth sensitivity and gradient issues) and shows more stable gradient behavior.  
- Theoretical contribution is sound and the PMD–Wasserstein connection is a primary strength.  
- Experimental results are promising but do not outperform state-of-the-art generative methods (e.g., WGAN); generated SVHN and LFW images are less impressive.  
- SVHN experiments show sample bias (overrepresentation of digit ‘8’), raising concerns about experimental validity and robustness.  
- Authors note PMD needs large mini-batch sizes for complex, multimodal distributions but do not analyze sensitivity to small mini-batches or risks of suboptimal convergence.  
- Lack of a known unbiased estimator for the PMD gradient is a limitation that may affect training behavior.  
- Reviewer suspects failure cases (e.g., matching mixtures of Gaussians with differing variances) that are highly sample-size sensitive and recommends stress tests.  
- Minor typo: “usally” → “usually” on line 141.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically well-motivated, improved gradient stability over MMD, and interesting empirical applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, non-competitive image quality, sensitivity to mini-batch size, and SVHN bias concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify PMD limitations vs MMD, run mixtures-of-Gaussians experiments, discuss computational cost for large N, and acknowledge/contrast unbiased MMD gradient estimators.
