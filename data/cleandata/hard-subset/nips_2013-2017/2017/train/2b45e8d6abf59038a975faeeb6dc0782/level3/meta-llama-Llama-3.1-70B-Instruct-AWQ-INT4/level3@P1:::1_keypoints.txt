- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a differentiable sample-based estimator of distribution distance via minimum weight matching.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as the objective.  
- Claims advantages over MMD: fewer hyperparameters, stronger gradients, and smaller required mini-batch size.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with a clear motivation and comprehensive related work.  
- Section 3 provides rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Experiments show PMD is effective in domain adaptation and generative modeling, outperforming MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Generated images from PMD are inferior to those from Wasserstein GAN (state-of-the-art).  
- PMD requires large minibatch size N to estimate Wasserstein distance for diverse, multimodal distributions.  
- Optimization can yield a global minimum different from the true distribution when minibatch size is small.  
- SVHN samples show low diversity, which may limit PMD’s effectiveness.  
- Authors should clarify limitations and potential failure cases (e.g., matching mixtures of Gaussians) and provide more optimization analysis.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original contribution with promising experiments; recommended improvements to address limitations.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel sample-based distance estimator, strong experimental results, useful contribution to deep learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unaddressed limitations of PMD/MMD, potential optimization pitfalls with small minibatches, and low diversity in some datasets.
