- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator for Wasserstein distance.  
- Core idea: minimize the cost of matching samples between distributions as an alternative to MMD.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Positions PMD against MMD, highlighting MMD’s bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, and large batch-size requirements.  
- Claims PMD addresses those MMD issues and often yields superior performance and faster convergence.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound and experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling provide initial evidence.  
- Discusses computational complexity and proposes a quadratic approximation for scalability.  
- Experimental evaluation should be strengthened with more complex, multi-modal datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10).  
- Raises concern about PMD’s behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes—potential instability or misrepresentation.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel and conceptually simple approach.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong theoretical justification (consistency proof).  
- Argument for acceptance: clear presentation and demonstrated improvements over MMD.  
- Argument for acceptance: practical consideration of computational complexity and approximation.  
- Argument against acceptance: experimental evaluation not comprehensive for multi-modal generative modeling.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient investigation of PMD when N < number of modes.  
- Overall recommendation: promising paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address suggested experiments.
