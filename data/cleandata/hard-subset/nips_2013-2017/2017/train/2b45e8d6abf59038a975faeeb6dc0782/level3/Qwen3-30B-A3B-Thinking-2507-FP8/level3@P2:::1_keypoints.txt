- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a differentiable sample-based estimator of the Wasserstein distance using minimum weight matching, with a proof of strong consistency.  
- Applies PMD as a training objective in domain adaptation and generative modeling, comparing it primarily to MMD.  
- Differs from WGAN by not requiring a critic network; situates work within MMD and optimal transport literature.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Empirical finding: PMD outperforms MMD in domain adaptation and generative modeling, showing faster convergence and better Office benchmark results.  
- Practical advantages claimed: no kernel bandwidth hyperparameter, stronger gradients, and ability to use smaller mini-batches (e.g., 100 vs 1000), improving training efficiency.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a logical flow from theory to experiments.  
- Key weakness: insufficient discussion of PMD’s limitations, particularly the need for large population size N for accurate estimation on diverse or multimodal distributions.  
- Potential failure modes (e.g., matching mixtures of Gaussians and learning variances) are not explored or clarified.  
- Comparison to MMD’s mini-batch requirements is incomplete/unfair; MMD can work with very small batches using unbiased estimators (Gretton et al.).  
- Important omission: no discussion of the lack of an unbiased gradient estimator for PMD and implications for training stability.  
- Generated images from PMD are inferior to WGAN’s; authors do not analyze causes or possible improvements.  
- SVHN results may be compromised by low dataset diversity (overrepresentation of certain digits), affecting generality.  
- Pros summarized: strong theory, demonstrated empirical advantages over MMD, practical implementation, clear presentation.  
- Cons summarized: inadequate limitations discussion, unfair MMD comparison, poorer image quality versus WGAN, and missing unbiased-gradient discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations and improve comparisons with MMD.
