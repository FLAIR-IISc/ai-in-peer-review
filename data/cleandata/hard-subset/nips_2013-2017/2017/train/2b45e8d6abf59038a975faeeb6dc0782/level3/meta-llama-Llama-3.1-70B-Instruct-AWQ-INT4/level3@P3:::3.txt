The authors propose PMD as a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and demonstrate its advantages over the popular Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) method.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed method. The introduction provides a thorough background on the importance of estimating statistical divergence in deep learning tasks and the limitations of existing methods. The authors then introduce PMD and provide a detailed explanation of its definition, properties, and advantages over MMD.

The authors establish the connection between PMD and the Wasserstein metric, and prove that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. They also compare PMD with MMD, highlighting its advantages in terms of fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch sizes.

The experimental results in Section 5 demonstrate the effectiveness of PMD in two deep learning tasks: domain adaptation and generative modeling.
