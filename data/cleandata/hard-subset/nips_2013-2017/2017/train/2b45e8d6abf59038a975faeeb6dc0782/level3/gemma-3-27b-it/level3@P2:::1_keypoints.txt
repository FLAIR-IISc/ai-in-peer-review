- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a matching-based estimator for the Wasserstein distance, alternative to MMD.  
- Provides theoretical justification that PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein metric.  
- Motivation: addresses MMD limitations (kernel bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, large mini-batch requirement).  
- PMD formulated as a minimum-weight matching problem between samples, avoiding kernel-based MMD.  
- Empirical results show faster convergence and improved performance on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Authors compare PMD to MMD and discuss computational complexity and implementations (exact and approximate matching).  
- Strength: potentially better gradient stability and suitability for smaller mini-batches than MMD.  
- Weakness: paper does not sufficiently explore PMD failure modes or scenarios where it may struggle.  
- Concern: performance on complex distributions (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians, learning variances) may be problematic and is not analyzed.  
- Concern: approximation quality may degrade with very small mini-batches, especially in high dimensions or complex structures.  
- Issue: blurry SVHN and LFW generations are noted but lack deeper analysis beyond L1 distance limitations.  
- Key technical gap: no explicit discussion of unbiased gradient estimators for PMD, raising practical scalability and training concerns.  
- Insufficient comparison to WGAN, particularly missing discussion of WGAN’s Lipschitz enforcement (weight clipping/gradient penalty).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel estimator, theoretical guarantees, promising empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited failure-mode analysis, missing gradient-unbiasedness analysis, weak WGAN comparison, mini-batch approximation concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject with encouragement to address limitations, gradient estimation, and comparisons before resubmission.
