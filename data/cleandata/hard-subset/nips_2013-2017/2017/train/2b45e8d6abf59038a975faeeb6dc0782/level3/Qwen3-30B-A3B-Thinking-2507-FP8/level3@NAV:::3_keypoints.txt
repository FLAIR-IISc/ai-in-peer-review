- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel population-based divergence estimating Wasserstein distance.
- PMD defined as minimum weight matching cost between two sample populations and proven strongly consistent for the first Wasserstein metric.
- Claims advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth tuning required.
- Claims advantages over MMD: provides stronger, non-saturating gradients.
- Claims advantages over MMD: enables smaller mini-batch sizes during training (≈100 vs. MMD’s ≈1000).
- Paper is clear and well-structured with well-motivated theoretical analysis linking PMD to optimal transport.
- Experimental evaluation shows PMD outperforms MMD in domain adaptation (Office) and generative modeling (MNIST, SVHN, LFW), especially in convergence speed and performance.
- Addresses computational cost by proposing a quadratic-time approximation to the cubic-time Hungarian algorithm, with maintained empirical performance.
- Main weaknesses: lacks experiments on challenging multimodal distributions to fully test PMD’s behavior.
- Main weaknesses: does not analyze PMD performance when sample size N is smaller than the number of distribution modes.
- Theoretical results are sound, writing and citations are appropriate, and the approach is original.
- Significance is high and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
