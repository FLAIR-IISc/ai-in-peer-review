- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance estimator between distributions defined via minimum weight matching.  
- Shows PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as the objective.  
- Compares PMD thoroughly to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and other population-based divergences.  
- Exact PMD computation is cubic time; authors propose a quadratic approximation that retains empirical performance.  
- Experimental results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed for domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and cites prior work appropriately.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and are clearly explained.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate and includes relevant baselines.  
- Strength: novel and potentially useful method for comparing distributions.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundations and thorough experimental validation.  
- Weakness: lacks experiments on generative models with many modes to demonstrate scalability/robustness.  
- Weakness: behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes is not fully explored.  
- Overall: technically solid, significant potential impact, but would benefit from additional experiments and analysis.
