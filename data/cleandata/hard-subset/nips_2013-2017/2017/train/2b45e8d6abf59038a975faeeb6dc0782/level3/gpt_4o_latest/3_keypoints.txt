- PMD is a novel, conceptually simple, differentiable population-based divergence that consistently estimates the first Wasserstein distance.  
- Theoretical results are sound and clearly connect PMD to MMD, highlighting PMD's advantages.  
- PMD avoids MMD's kernel bandwidth tuning, yields stronger gradients, and supports smaller mini-batches.  
- A quadratic-complexity approximation is proposed to mitigate PMD's cubic exact-computation cost with minimal performance loss.  
- Experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- The paper is well-written, well-structured, and situates PMD appropriately within prior work.  
- The method addresses an important problem and has potential for broad adoption in distribution comparison tasks.  
- Exact PMD computation is cubic, raising scalability concerns for very large datasets.  
- Evaluation is limited to standard tasks; more challenging settings (e.g., multi-modal generation, small-sample regimes) are not explored.  
- Generated images can be blurry, suggesting the L1 distance in PMD may be suboptimal for natural images and alternative metrics should be explored.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical and practical contributions warrant acceptance, with suggested future work on multi-modal generative modeling and constrained sample-size scenarios.
