- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance estimator between distributions, as an alternative to MMD.  
- Defines PMD as the minimum weight matching between two random populations and links it to the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the 1-Wasserstein distance.  
- Presents an algorithm that uses PMD as an objective to learn distribution parameters.  
- Empirically evaluates PMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Reports PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed, especially with small mini-batches.  
- Claims advantages over MMD: stronger gradients, works with smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.  
- Reviewer notes the stronger-gradients claim is unconvincing because MMD depends on kernel bandwidth and comparisons should use best/range of sigmas.  
- Reviewer notes the fewer-hyperparameters claim is weak since PMD relies on a distance metric that can introduce hyperparameters.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis relating PMD to Wasserstein and proving consistency.  
- Strength: empirical results convincingly show small mini-batch advantages.  
- Strength: well-written, clear, and easy-to-follow presentation.  
- Weakness: reliance on distance metrics may limit applicability in high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparisons to other distribution-matching methods and kernel choices.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and pitfalls of PMD, particularly in high dimensions.  
- Suggested experiments: compare PMD to MMD using standard bandwidth heuristics and treat MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free.  
- Suggested direction: explore different distance metrics or kernel functions to improve PMD in high-dimensional settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong theory, and convincing small-batch empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high-D limitations, incomplete comparative evaluation, and insufficient discussion of pitfalls.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
