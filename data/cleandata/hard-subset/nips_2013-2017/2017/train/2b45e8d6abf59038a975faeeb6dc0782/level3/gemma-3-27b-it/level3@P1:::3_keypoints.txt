- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel, theoretically grounded estimator that matches samples between distributions as an alternative to MMD.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and provides a differentiable objective for neural network training.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and contains a thorough literature review.  
- Effectively addresses MMD limitations: kernel bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, and large mini-batch requirements.  
- Learning algorithm using PMD is sound, leverages SGD, and empirical results show competitive or improved performance on domain adaptation and generative tasks.  
- Includes practical analysis of convergence speed and time consumption.  
- Uses a quadratic approximation to reduce matching cost but lacks detailed analysis of the accuracy vs. computational-efficiency trade-off.  
- Limited discussion on the impact of the chosen distance metric d (e.g., L1) and its suitability for complex data distributions.  
- Generative modeling experiments are limited to simpler datasets (MNIST, SVHN) and miss evaluations on more complex, multi-modal datasets (e.g., CelebA, ImageNet).  
- Generated SVHN/LFW images are blurry (attributed to L1); suggests exploring other distance metrics or more sophisticated generative architectures.  
- Important open question: PMD behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes needs theoretical or empirical investigation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical justification, addresses key MMD limitations, demonstrated empirical gains, clear writing, and a valuable new tool.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient complex-dataset experiments, limited metric discussion, need deeper analysis of matching approximation and low-sample regimes.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experimental evaluation and analyze low-sample/approximation behavior.
