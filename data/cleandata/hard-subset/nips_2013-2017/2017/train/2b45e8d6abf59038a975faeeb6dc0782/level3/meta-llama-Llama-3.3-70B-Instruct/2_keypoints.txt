- Introduces a novel framework for personalized screening that balances screening cost against detection-delay cost using a finite-state stochastic disease model with an absorbing state and optional external information triggering early visits.
- Well-written and clearly organized paper with a clear motivation and thoroughly described model and problem formulation.
- Derives an exactly optimal screening policy and an approximately optimal policy using point-based value iteration, with detailed methodological explanation.
- Strength: handles arbitrary finite-state stochastic processes, increasing generality and applicability.
- Strength: incorporates external information (patient-triggered arrivals), making the model more realistic.
- Provides thorough analysis of approximation error and robustness of the proposed approach.
- Weakness: relies on assumptions (e.g., current test result independent of past results and external info) that may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods and deeper result analyses.
- Originality: presents a distinct approach from prior work and clearly explains differences.
- Significance: has potential to reduce screening costs while maintaining detection delay, demonstrated on breast cancer screening with promising results.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but paper would benefit from minor revisions for clarity and expanded experiments.
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, rigorous error/robustness analysis, experimental validation, and practical impact; Con-accept arguments: strong assumptions, limited experiments, and minor clarity/organization issues.
