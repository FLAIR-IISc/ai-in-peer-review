- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) to estimate distribution distance via sample matching approximating the Wasserstein metric.  
- Proposes an algorithm that uses PMD as an objective for learning generative models and domain adaptation.  
- Claims PMD overcomes MMD drawbacks: kernel bandwidth sensitivity, vanishing gradients, and large mini-batch requirements.  
- The approach is grounded in optimal transport theory with a proof of strong consistency for the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Empirical results show PMD can outperform MMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling and exhibit faster convergence.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful pseudocode and explanations.  
- PMD provides a non-kernel alternative with potentially better gradient behavior.  
- Main computational concern: matching algorithm has O(N^2)–O(N^3) cost and scalability details are limited.  
- Mini-batch sensitivity: PMD still needs reasonably large batches and behavior with very small batches is unexplored.  
- Limited dataset diversity (e.g., SVHN biases) may affect generality of generative results.  
- Gradient comparison lacks rigorous quantitative/statistical analysis beyond visualizations.  
- No known unbiased estimator for PMD gradients; reliance on stable matching during SGD may cause instability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically justified, and empirically promising method addressing MMD limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability, insufficient mini-batch analysis, and potential optimization instability.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but with concerns — reviewer recommends weak accept.  
- Revision requests: add scalability experiments/analysis, study mini-batch/population size effects, discuss gradient estimator/stability, explicitly outline limitations and failure cases, and fix a typo.
