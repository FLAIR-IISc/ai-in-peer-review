- Proposal: Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based distance using first Wasserstein distance computed by minimum-weight matching between minibatches.  
- Positioning: Frames PMD as a parameter-free alternative to MMD and a direct optimization alternative to critic-based WGANs.  
- Novelty/strength: Novel use of explicit sample-based Wasserstein computation as a training objective for distribution matching.  
- Experiments: Minibatch experiments are interesting and provide a new perspective on distribution matching.  
- Theory: Shows PMD is a consistent estimator of the Wasserstein-1 metric.  
- Generative quality: Results are not competitive—generated images are blurrier than WGANs and SVHN samples lack diversity (mode collapse toward "8"s).  
- Minibatch dependence: PMD likely requires large minibatch size N to estimate distances for diverse, multimodal distributions reliably.  
- Computational cost: Matching computation is expensive (O(N^3) or O(N^2)), posing a serious practical bottleneck.  
- Small-batch issues: With small N, the optimization landscape may favor solutions that do not match the true data distribution (e.g., lower-entropy learned distributions).  
- Suggested failure test: Simple synthetic cases (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians) would likely expose PMD’s limitations.  
- MMD comparison: Comparison to MMD is unbalanced; MMD benefits from unbiased SGD-friendly estimators that work with very small minibatches (batch_size=2).  
- PMD gradient issue: PMD has no known unbiased gradient estimator, unlike MMD.  
- Recommendation: Authors should run further experiments on challenging synthetic distributions and discuss limitations more thoroughly.
