- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator of the Wasserstein distance via minimum weight matching.  
- PMD is proven to be a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- PMD is a primal, parameter-free, differentiable approach that is computationally tractable for deep learning.  
- PMD avoids MMD’s kernel bandwidth tuning, provides stronger gradients, and alleviates vanishing-gradient issues.  
- PMD enables training with smaller mini-batches (e.g., 100 vs. 1000), improving training efficiency.  
- Main contributions: theoretical grounding of PMD, a practical training algorithm for neural networks, and empirical validation.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with correct connections to optimal transport and appropriate citations (Villani, Varadarajan, stability results).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Proposes a quadratic-time approximation to the cubic-time Hungarian algorithm that maintains statistical performance.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over MMD in domain adaptation and generative tasks, with better convergence speed.  
- Generative modeling experiments are limited: issues with multimodality and blurry SVHN/LFW samples (attributed to L1 pixel loss) remain unaddressed.  
- Lacks comparison to other Wasserstein approximations such as WGAN and CramerGAN.  
- Does not explore theoretical limits or behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Paper is significant and practically useful; recommendation: Accept.
