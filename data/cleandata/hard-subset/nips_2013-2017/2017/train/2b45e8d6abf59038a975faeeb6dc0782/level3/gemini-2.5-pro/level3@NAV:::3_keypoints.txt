- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based divergence defined as the minimum weight matching cost between two sample populations.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein distance.
- Positions PMD as a compelling alternative to MMD and adversarial Wasserstein approximations.
- Advantages: parameter-free, no kernel hyperparameters, computed directly from samples, avoids vanishing gradients and kernel tuning.
- Addresses computational cost: acknowledges cubic complexity and proposes a quadratic approximation that preserves statistical performance in experiments.
- Experimental evaluation: thorough comparisons showing PMD outperforms MMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates prior work appropriately.
- Main weakness: generative modeling experiments do not sufficiently test highly multi-modal target distributions.
- Requested experiment: evaluate PMD on synthetic datasets with many distinct modes to assess diversity capture versus MMD/GANs.
- Requested discussion: analyze PMD behavior for small sample sizes (N), especially when N is less than the number of true modes.
- Overall judgment: high-quality work with minor addressable issues; strengths far outweigh weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
