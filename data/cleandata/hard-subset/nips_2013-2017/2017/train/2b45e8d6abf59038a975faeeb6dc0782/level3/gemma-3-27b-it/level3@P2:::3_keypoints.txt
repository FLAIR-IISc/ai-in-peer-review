- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel estimator for distance between distributions based on minimum weight matching between samples.
- Shows PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric with a proposed algorithm for learning distributions using PMD.
- Conceptually simple method that avoids MMD’s bandwidth hyperparameter and supports standard mini-batch sizes.
- Practical advantages over MMD: stronger gradients during training and reduced hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Theoretical analysis and literature positioning are rigorous and appropriate.
- Paper is technically sound and exceptionally well written, making it accessible to the community.
- Empirical validation includes domain adaptation and generative modeling, demonstrating improved performance and convergence speed vs. MMD.
- Authors propose an efficient approximation for the matching problem to address computational cost.
- Detailed analysis of gradient behavior provides insight into PMD’s training benefits (e.g., Fig. 2b).
- Main weakness: generative experiments are limited to simple datasets (MNIST, SVHN, LFW) and may not test complex multi-modal distributions.
- Concern that PMD might struggle when true data has far more modes than sample size N, risking suboptimal matchings or mode collapse.
- Lack of empirical results showing GPU acceleration or speedups for the matching algorithm limits assessment of scalability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; add experiments on more complex/multi-modal datasets and report GPU acceleration results to strengthen the paper.
