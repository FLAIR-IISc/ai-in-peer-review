- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) to estimate Wasserstein distance via minimum-weight matching of sample populations.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric (Theorem 3.3).  
- Builds on prior work (WGAN, MMD) but offers a different computational approach to Wasserstein estimation.  
- Claims practical advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch sizes.  
- Empirical results (domain adaptation, generative modeling) show faster convergence and better performance than MMD in reported experiments.  
- Claims about small mini-batch advantage are contradicted by MMD literature showing unbiased MMD estimators with batch_size=2.  
- PMD-generated images are visually inferior to WGAN outputs, undermining its practical generative modeling claim.  
- Paper fails to explain why PMD underperforms WGAN despite estimating Wasserstein distance.  
- Does not address a critical failure case: matching mixtures of Gaussians (likely to fail with discrete matching and small N).  
- Requires large N to estimate Wasserstein for diverse/multimodal distributions, contradicting claimed mini-batch advantages.  
- SVHN experiments show low dataset diversity, weakening claims that PMD handles complex/diverse distributions.  
- Empirical analysis has gaps: needs experiments on mixtures of Gaussians and better diversity analysis of datasets.  
- Minor typo noted ("usally" → "usually") and missing comparison to Cramer GAN (Bellemare et al., 2017).  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but experimental shortcomings; recommendation is rejection pending revisions and additional experiments.
