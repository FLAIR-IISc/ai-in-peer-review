- Introduces a novel framework for personalized screening policies that trade off screening cost versus delay, modeling disease as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state and external triggers.  
- Paper is well-structured, well-written, and cites prior work thoroughly with correct theoretical results.  
- Uses a POMDP framework, derives a recursive value function, and proposes a PBVI-based approximate solution.  
- Provides bounds on approximation error and discusses robustness to estimation errors.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large breast cancer dataset, showing significant screening reductions while maintaining detection delay.  
- Experimental comparisons with current clinical policies and other frameworks are thorough and well-presented.  
- Strength: clear, concise introduction and detailed methodological description.  
- Strength: robust, well-justified treatment of estimation errors.  
- Strength: substantial reduction in screenings without increasing detection delay.  
- Weakness: assumes the disease model is known, limiting practical applicability unless further explored.  
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on breast cancer, leaving generalizability to other diseases unclear.  
- Weakness: potential computational complexity and scalability concerns for large datasets.
