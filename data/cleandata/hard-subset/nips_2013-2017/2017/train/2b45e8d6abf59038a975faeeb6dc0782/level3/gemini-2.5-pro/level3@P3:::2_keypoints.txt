- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based estimator using average cost of minimum-weight bipartite matching to measure distance between distributions.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein (earth mover's) distance.  
- Positions PMD as an alternative to MMD, claiming fewer hyperparameters, stronger gradients, and effective optimization with small mini-batches.  
- PMD estimates the primal Wasserstein directly, avoiding a learned critic network required by WGANs.  
- Empirically outperforms MMD on unsupervised domain adaptation and generative image modeling in final performance and convergence speed.  
- Key practical advantage: can train effectively with small SGD batches, decoupling optimization batch size from population size (Fig. 5a).  
- Novel and practical formulation: differentiable distribution distance via minimum-weight matching is an elegant, non-adversarial approach.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in optimal transport theory.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes intuitive illustrations and pseudocode.  
- Claim that PMD provides stronger gradients than MMD is unconvincing due to lack of fair comparison and sensitivity of MMD to kernel bandwidth.  
- Claim of “fewer hyperparameters” is oversimplified because PMD depends on the choice of distance metric (e.g., L1, L2, learned or perceptual distances).  
- Missing discussion of high-dimensional challenges: distance metrics can degrade in meaning in high dimensions, possibly explaining blurred generated images.  
- Suggests stronger comparisons: evaluate MMD with standard data-driven bandwidth heuristics to ensure fair baseline.  
- Recommends nuanced discussion of hyperparameter equivalence (metric choice vs. kernel choice) and explicit limitations (curse of dimensionality, O(N^3) matching complexity).  
- Overall: solid, novel, and practically useful contribution with important advantages, but some superiority claims over MMD need stronger empirical and discussional support; recommends acceptance.
