- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a differentiable, sample-based distance estimator between distributions.  
- Defines PMD as the Wasserstein distance between minibatches, computed with exact O(N^3) or approximate O(N^2) algorithms.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric and MMD.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Claims advantages over MMD: fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller minibatch needs for gradient-based optimization.  
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Experimental results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Empirically studies PMD approximation error and validates stronger gradients versus MMD.  
- Limitation: PMD-generated images are not as good as those from Wasserstein GAN.  
- Limitation: PMD may require large minibatches for diverse, multimodal distributions, increasing computational cost.  
- Limitation: no known unbiased estimator of the PMD gradient, limiting some applications.  
- Minor issue: typo on Line 141 ("usally" → "usually").  
- Paper meets conference criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and is technically sound with supported claims.  
- Suggested revisions: expand discussion of PMD vs. MMD limitations and clarify scenarios where each is preferable; include more comprehensive comparisons with related methods (e.g., Wasserstein GAN).
