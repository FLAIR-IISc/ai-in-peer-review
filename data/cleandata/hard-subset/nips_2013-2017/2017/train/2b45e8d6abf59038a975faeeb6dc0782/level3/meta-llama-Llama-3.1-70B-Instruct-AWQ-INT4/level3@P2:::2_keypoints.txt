- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a differentiable sample-based distance estimator as an alternative to MMD.  
- PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and claims advantages over MMD (no kernel bandwidth, stronger gradients, smaller mini-batch size).  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric and comparing pros/cons with MMD.  
- Proposes a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.  
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Technically sound: claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; evaluations are careful and honest.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of techniques with clear relation to prior work.  
- Significant contribution: results likely useful to practitioners/researchers and advance the state of the art.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: new effective method, strong theory, convincing experiments, good presentation.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: stronger-gradients claim depends on MMD kernel bandwidth selection; PMD may introduce its own hyperparameters via the distance metric; high-dimensional distance estimation issues may persist.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept (rating 8/10) with request to address the listed concerns in the final version.
