- Introduces PMD, a sample-based distance estimator using minimum weight matching, proven consistent for the first Wasserstein metric.
- Proposes learning distribution parameters with PMD and applies it to domain adaptation and generative modeling.
- Empirical results show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed on the evaluated tasks.
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical support and well-designed experiments.
- Lacks detailed analysis of PMD’s limitations, notably computational complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Well-written and logically organized, though introduction and conclusion could be more concise.
- Would benefit from more visual aids (diagrams/flowcharts) to clarify concepts and algorithms.
- Original contribution: a new sample-based estimator and novel applications demonstrating PMD’s potential.
- Needs a deeper comparison and discussion of relations to existing methods (MMD, Wasserstein GAN).
- Significant potential impact by offering a new perspective on distribution-distance estimation in deep learning.
- Missing discussion of real-world applicability and practical challenges when deploying PMD.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical improvements, clear exposition, useful applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient limitation analysis, unclear computational cost, sparse comparisons and visuals.
- Minor comments: fix typo "usally"→"usually" and add experimental details (network architectures, hyperparameters).
