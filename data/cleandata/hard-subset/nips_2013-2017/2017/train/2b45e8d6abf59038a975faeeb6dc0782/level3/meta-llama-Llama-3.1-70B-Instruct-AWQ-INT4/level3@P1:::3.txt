The paper "Population Matching Discrepancy and Applications in Deep Learning" presents a novel approach to estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, referred to as Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). PMD is defined as the minimum weight matching of sample populations from each distribution and is shown to be a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. The authors propose an algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD as an objective and demonstrate its advantages over Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), a popular method for estimating the distance between two distributions.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with proper citations to prior work. The theoretical results appear correct, and the experimental analysis is adequate, including comparisons to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation and generative modeling.

The key contributions of the paper are:

1. Introduction of PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.
2. A thorough comparison to MMD, the primary population-based divergence used in machine learning.
3. Exact computation of PMD is cubic, but the authors propose a quadratic approximation that empirically maintains statistical performance.
4. Experimental analysis demonstrates the advantages of PMD over MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.

* The paper proposes a novel and potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.

Weaknesses of the paper:
