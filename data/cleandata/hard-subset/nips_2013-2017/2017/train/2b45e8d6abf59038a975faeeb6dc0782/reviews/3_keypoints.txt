- Introduces PMD, a population-based divergence and consistent estimator of the Wasserstein distance.  
- Estimator is conceptually simple, differentiable, and enables training of neural-network-based models.  
- Thorough comparison to MMD, the primary population-based divergence used in ML.  
- Exact computation is cubic; authors propose a quadratic approximation that empirically maintains statistical performance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and properly cites prior work.  
- Theoretical results appear correct.  
- Experimental analysis is adequate: comparisons to MMD and other methods for domain adaptation and to MMD for generative modeling.  
- Reviewer requests experiments on generative modeling with many modes and questions PMD’s behavior when sample size N is smaller than the number of modes.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper proposing a potentially very useful method for comparing distributions.
