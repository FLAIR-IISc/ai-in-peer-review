- Paper proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a superior alternative to MMD for distribution matching.
- PMD is shown to be a sampled version of the Wasserstein (earth mover’s) distance.
- Claimed advantages: stronger gradients, applicability with smaller mini-batches, and fewer hyperparameters.
- For generative model training, PMD addresses MMD’s weak gradients and large mini-batch requirements.
- Empirical results convincingly verify the small mini-batch advantage.
- Claim of stronger gradients is less convincing because MMD’s behavior depends on the kernel bandwidth (sigma); comparisons should use best or a range of sigmas.
- Claim of fewer hyperparameters is weak: PMD depends on a distance metric that can introduce its own hyperparameters.
- High-dimensional distance estimation remains problematic, so PMD may face similar issues to MMD in high dimensions.
- Reviewer suggests comparing PMD to MMD using standard bandwidth heuristics and treating MMD as effectively hyperparameter-free.
- Overall: PMD is a valid improvement for fast training with small batches but may be limited by reliance on distance metrics in high-dimensional settings.
