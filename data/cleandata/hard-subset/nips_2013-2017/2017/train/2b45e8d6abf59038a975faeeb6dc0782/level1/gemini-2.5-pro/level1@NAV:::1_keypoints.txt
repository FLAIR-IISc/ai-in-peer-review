- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a sample-based estimator defined via minimum-weight bipartite matching between two sample sets.
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the Wasserstein-1 distance, connecting it to optimal transport theory.
- Positions PMD as a practical, differentiable, parameter-free alternative to MMD and critic-based Wasserstein approximations.
- Claims PMD avoids MMD’s kernel hyperparameter sensitivity and GAN training instability by tackling the primal OT problem directly.
- Demonstrates empirical utility of PMD for training deep models in domain adaptation and generative modeling with convincing performance and faster convergence.
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation and ablations on population/mini-batch sizes and matching algorithms, supporting the paper’s claims.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates contributions relative to prior work.
- Main weakness is computational cost: exact matching O(N^3) (Hungarian) or approximate O(N^2), posing scalability concerns versus MMD.
- Experimental baselines are limited for generative modeling—missing comparisons to contemporaneous state-of-the-art (e.g., WGAN); generated images still somewhat blurry.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically grounded contribution offering a valuable new tool for deep learning despite computational and baseline limitations.
