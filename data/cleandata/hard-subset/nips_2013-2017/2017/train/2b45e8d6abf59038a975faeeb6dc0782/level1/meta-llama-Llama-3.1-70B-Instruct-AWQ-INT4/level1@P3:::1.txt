This paper introduces a novel method for estimating the distance between two distributions based on samples, called population matching discrepancy (PMD). The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of PMD, showing that it is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. They also propose an algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD as an objective. The paper compares PMD with the popular maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) method, highlighting the advantages of PMD, including fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch sizes.

The authors apply PMD to two deep learning tasks: domain adaptation and generative modeling. The empirical results demonstrate that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed in both tasks. The paper also provides an extensive list of references, showcasing the authors' thorough understanding of the related work.
