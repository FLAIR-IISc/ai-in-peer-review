- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a differentiable sample-based distribution distance estimator as an alternative to MMD.  
- Defines PMD as the minimum weight matching cost between two sample populations.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Claims advantages over MMD: no kernel bandwidth hyper-parameter, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch requirements.  
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling, showing superior performance and faster convergence versus MMD.  
- Provides strong theoretical grounding with clear proofs linking PMD to the Wasserstein metric.  
- Presents thorough empirical results with comprehensive comparisons across datasets and tasks.  
- Offers insightful analysis of gradient distributions and computational efficiency.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, with helpful pseudocode and illustrative figures.  
- Strengths: novel approach, solid theory, and compelling empirical evidence demonstrating practical advantages.  
- Situates work relative to prior NIPS contributions (MMD, WGAN) and addresses their limitations.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison with other Wasserstein-based methods beyond MMD.  
- Weaknesses: generative results on SVHN/LFW described as "blurry" despite using L1 distance.  
- Weaknesses: computational complexity discussion of the Hungarian algorithm could be expanded for larger N, though approximations are noted.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant contribution that addresses practical MMD limitations and benefits deep learning applications.
