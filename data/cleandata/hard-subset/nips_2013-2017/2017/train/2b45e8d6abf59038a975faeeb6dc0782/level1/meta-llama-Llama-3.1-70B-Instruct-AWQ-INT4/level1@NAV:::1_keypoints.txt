- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a minimum-weight matching estimator between sample populations.  
- PMD is proven to be a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein (W1) metric.  
- Authors present an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as the objective.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains PMD and its advantages over MMD.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis and relationships between PMD and other discrepancies.  
- Experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- Strength: introduces a new sample-based consistent estimator for W1.  
- Strength: solid theoretical characterization and comparisons.  
- Strength: empirical effectiveness demonstrated on two deep-learning tasks.  
- Weakness: assumes i.i.d. samples, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other relevant baselines such as WGAN for generative modeling.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add detailed computational-complexity analysis and comparisons (e.g., WGAN).
