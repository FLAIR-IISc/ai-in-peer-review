- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based distance between distributions defined via minimum-weight matching between random populations.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein (W1) metric and provides an SGD algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.  
- Experiments on domain adaptation and generative modeling show PMD outperforms MMD in performance and convergence speed.  
- PMD is flexible: works with different distance metrics (e.g., L1) and can be computed in polynomial time.  
- Main weakness: computationally expensive for large population sizes due to matching problem.  
- Sensitive to hyperparameters: choice of distance metric and population size affects performance.  
- Limited experimental evaluation: only two tasks tested, requiring broader assessment.  
- Originality: using minimum-weight matching to define a distributional distance is a novel approach in this context.  
- Significance: PMD offers a new practical tool for tasks requiring distribution-distance estimation (e.g., domain adaptation, generative modeling).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear figures/tables.  
- Quality: technically sound analysis, thorough discussion of limitations and future work, and well-designed experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, empirical improvement over MMD, and theoretical guarantees (strong consistency).  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially high computational cost and limited experimental scope.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
