- Introduces Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as a sample-based distance via minimum weight matching, proposed as an alternative to MMD.
- Proves PMD is a consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
- Applies PMD to domain adaptation and generative modeling, showing improved performance and faster convergence than MMD.
- Motivates PMD by addressing MMD’s bandwidth sensitivity, gradient vanishing, and large batch-size needs.
- Theoretical grounding links PMD to established Wasserstein theory.
- Builds on prior work including GANs and MMD-based generative models.
- Strength: technically sound and well-motivated alternative to MMD.
- Strength: strong theoretical analysis establishing consistency.
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate advantages in two relevant tasks.
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation and thorough comparison with MMD.
- Weakness: empirical improvements, especially in generative modeling, are modest.
- Weakness: insufficient ablation study on PMD components (e.g., choice of distance d).
- Weakness: computational complexity discussion is limited; GPU/acceleration considerations lacking.
- Weakness: experiments rely on L1 distance, limiting applicability and generalizability.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, empirical gains, addresses MMD limitations, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: modest gains, limited metric exploration, and missing detailed complexity analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand ablation studies, provide detailed complexity analysis, and acknowledge L1 limitations.
