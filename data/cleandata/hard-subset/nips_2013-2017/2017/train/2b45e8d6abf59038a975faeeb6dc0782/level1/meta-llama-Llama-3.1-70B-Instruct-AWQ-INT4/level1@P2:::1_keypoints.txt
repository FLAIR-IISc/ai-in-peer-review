- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a novel sample-based estimator of distance between distributions.  
- Defines PMD as minimum weight matching between two random populations and proves strong consistency to the first Wasserstein metric.  
- Introduces a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD.  
- Relates PMD to prior work, highlighting advantages over maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) and connections to the Wasserstein metric.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, with a detailed comparison to MMD.  
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate PMD’s effectiveness in domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.  
- Paper is well-organized and written clearly, making the method and advantages easy to follow.  
- Strength: novel method with theoretical backing and practical benefits over MMD.  
- Strength: convincing experiments showing practical significance in deep learning applications.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with MMD and Wasserstein, which may limit accessibility.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details for the matching algorithm, a crucial component of PMD.  
- Weakness: would benefit from more experiments across additional domains to broaden empirical validation.  
- Clarity could be improved with more figures and tables to illustrate results.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, given novelty, solid theory, and convincing experiments.
