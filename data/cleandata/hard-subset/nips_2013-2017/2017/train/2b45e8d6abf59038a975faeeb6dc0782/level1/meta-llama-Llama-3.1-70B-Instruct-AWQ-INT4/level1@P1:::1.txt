**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions based on samples, called Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). PMD is defined as the minimum weight matching between two random populations from the distributions. The authors prove that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric and propose a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn the parameters of the distributions using PMD. The paper also presents experimental results on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks, showing that PMD outperforms Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) in terms of performance and convergence speed.

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new method for estimating the distance between two probability distributions, which is different from existing methods such as MMD.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors provide theoretical guarantees for PMD, including its strong consistency as an estimator of the first Wasserstein metric.
3. **Improved performance**: The experimental results show that PMD outperforms MMD in terms of performance and convergence speed on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks.
4. **Flexibility**: PMD can be used with different distance metrics, such as L1 distance, and can be computed in polynomial time.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational cost**: The computational cost of PMD can be high, especially for large population sizes, due to the need to solve a matching problem.

3. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The experimental evaluation is limited to two tasks, and more experiments are needed to fully assess the performance of PMD.
