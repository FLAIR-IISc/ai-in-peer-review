- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD), a new sample-based distance estimator between distributions.  
- Defines PMD as minimum weight matching of sample populations and proves it is a strongly consistent estimator of the 1-Wasserstein metric.  
- Introduces an algorithm to learn distribution parameters using PMD as an objective.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise method explanations.  
- Relates PMD to prior work on MMD and Wasserstein metrics, and compares with WGAN's critic-based dual approximation.  
- Claims advantages over MMD: fewer hyper-parameters, stronger gradients, and smaller mini-batch requirements for optimization.  
- Demonstrates PMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling tasks, showing better performance and faster convergence than MMD.  
- Experimental results are convincing with detailed analysis.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of PMD and its relationship to the Wasserstein metric.  
- Method is original and novel, with comprehensive analysis of properties and applications.  
- Paper is significant and has potential for wide adoption in deep learning tasks.  
- For acceptance: novel estimator, solid analysis, convincing experiments, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: may be too specialized for general readers and might miss some relevant references.  
- Against acceptance: experimental validation limited to only two tasks; broader experiments suggested.  
- Suggestions: add more experiments across varied tasks to demonstrate generality.  
- Suggestions: discuss PMD limitations and potential applications in other fields.  
- Suggestions: provide more implementation details to help reproducibility and adoption.
