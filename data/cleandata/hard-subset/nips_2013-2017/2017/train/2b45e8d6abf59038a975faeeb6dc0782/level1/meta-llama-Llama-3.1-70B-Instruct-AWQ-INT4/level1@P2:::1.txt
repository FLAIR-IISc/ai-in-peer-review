The paper "Population Matching Discrepancy and Applications in Deep Learning" proposes a novel method for estimating the distance between two distributions based on samples, called Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD). The authors relate their work to previous research on maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) and the Wasserstein metric, highlighting the advantages of PMD over MMD.

The main idea of the paper is to define PMD as the minimum weight matching between two random populations from the distributions, and to show that PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric. The authors also propose a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to learn parameters of the distributions using PMD.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages over MMD. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with MMD.
