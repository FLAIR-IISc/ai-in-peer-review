- Proposes Population Matching Discrepancy (PMD) as an alternative to MMD for measuring distribution distance via minimum weight matching of sample populations.  
- Proves PMD is a strongly consistent estimator of the first Wasserstein metric, giving solid theoretical grounding.  
- Presents a learning algorithm using PMD and analyzes gradient properties, claiming stronger gradients and no bandwidth parameter.  
- Empirically demonstrates PMD outperforms MMD on domain adaptation and generative modeling, with better convergence and normal mini-batch compatibility.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides intuitive definitions and explanations.  
- Discusses computational complexity and offers both exact and approximate matching algorithms.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation linking PMD to Wasserstein metric.  
- Strength: directly addresses MMD limitations (bandwidth selection, vanishing gradients).  
- Strength: empirical validation across relevant tasks supporting claims.  
- Weakness: experiments use only L1 distance, limiting assessment of alternative metrics.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other Wasserstein estimators, weakening broader evaluation.  
- Weakness: generated images (SVHN, LFW) appear blurry, indicating potential issues with pixel-level distances.  
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for PMD beyond mini-batch size.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed runtime profiling across population sizes and matching algorithms.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with minor revisions to address metric choice, comparisons, and empirical analyses.
