- Proposes a novel trainable masking model for real-time image saliency detection that produces saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Method is model-agnostic and applicable to any differentiable image classifier (black-box setting).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization.  
- Introduces a new evaluation metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Shows improved performance over other weakly supervised techniques according to the new metric.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a detailed explanation of the method.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Claims are theoretically and experimentally well-supported and results are reproducible for expert readers.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thorough evaluation, and potential applications (video saliency, autonomous vehicles).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of learned salient regions to reveal biases or method properties.  
- Weakness: insufficient motivation for the need for real-time efficiency in some applications.  
- High originality and significance due to a new approach and a new evaluation metric.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with strong potential impact.
