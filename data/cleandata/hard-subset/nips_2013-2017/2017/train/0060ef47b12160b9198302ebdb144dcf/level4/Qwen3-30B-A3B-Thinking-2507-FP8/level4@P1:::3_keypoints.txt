- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency method that produces high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing salient regions for a target class using a U-Net with ResNet-50 backbone.  
- Introduces a novel saliency metric combining crop area and classification confidence that better isolates interpretability (SSR) from localization.  
- Demonstrates the metric outperforms existing methods and approaches ground-truth bounding-box quality.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50 as black-box classifiers) and CIFAR-10, showing superior weakly supervised localization versus baselines.  
- Achieves over 100 saliency maps per second on 224×224 images, enabling potential real-time applications like video saliency.  
- Replaces slow iterative optimization from prior work with a trainable model, directly addressing speed limitations of earlier methods.  
- Produces sharper, artifact-free masks compared to gradient-based saliency techniques while remaining model-agnostic.  
- The novel metric addresses a key evaluation gap by distinguishing saliency sufficiency from full-object localization.  
- Strengths: rigorous multi-dataset evaluation, careful ablations and adversarial handling, well-motivated metric and reproducible experimental protocol.  
- Strengths: significant novelty and practical impact via single-pass real-time capability; generalizes beyond ImageNet (CIFAR-10).  
- Strengths: clear organization, precise definitions (SSR/SDR), and transparent architecture description.  
- Weakness: real-time claim is measured only on 224×224 images and lacks latency analysis for higher-resolution inputs used in real applications.  
- Weakness: reliance on tight rectangular crops may fail for small objects, potentially including irrelevant background and reducing interpretability.  
- Weakness: Table 3 omits metric values for the iterative approach [2], weakening the comparative claim.  
- Pro: first single-pass real-time saliency method, novel SSR metric, strong empirical localization results (36.7% error on ImageNet) and CIFAR-10 generalization.  
- Con: missing larger-input speed validation, limited analysis of small-object cases, and incomplete metric comparison for prior iterative methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution to interpretable deep learning with addressable weaknesses.  
- Minor corrections: typos at LN 273 ("More over" → "Moreover") and LN 151 ("optimise" → "optimizes").
