- Proposes a masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, avoiding iterative methods and enabling real-time use.  
- Shows competitive results on ImageNet localization and CIFAR-10, with experiments indicating generalizability across datasets and classifiers.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric intended to improve evaluation rigor.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides a thorough related-work discussion.  
- Core idea of learning a saliency mask generator is sound, fast, and produces high visual quality.  
- Weakly supervised localization may not reflect true saliency when contextual information is important.  
- The proposed metric is susceptible to artifacts from resizing cropped regions (aspect ratio/scale changes) that can bias results.  
- The effect of the masking model architecture on saliency outputs is not sufficiently investigated; sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Claim of model-agnostic applicability is weakened by dependence on classifier scale and translation invariance.  
- Evaluation metrics used (weakly supervised localization and new metric) have inherent limitations for assessing saliency quality.  
- Results in Table 1 are for the correct class, but the LRP variant and parameter settings used for comparison must be specified for reproducibility.  
- Authors should discuss risks of visualizing masking-network biases versus classifier behavior and limitations for non-image tasks (e.g., spectrograms).  
- Verdict contingent: accept if the noted limitations, metric artifacts, architecture sensitivity, and reproducibility details are adequately addressed; otherwise reject.
