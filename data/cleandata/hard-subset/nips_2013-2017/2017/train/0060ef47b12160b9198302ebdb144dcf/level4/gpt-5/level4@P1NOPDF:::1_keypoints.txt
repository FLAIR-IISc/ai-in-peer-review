- Commendation: proposes a neural-network-based saliency mask method with high throughput (multiple images/sec).  
- Overall judgement: borderline paper—contains good ideas but has important unaddressed limitations.  
- Main concern: saliency is only evaluated indirectly (weakly supervised localization or the proposed metric), and both proxies have clear limitations.  
- Weakly supervised localization: may not correlate with saliency when contextual cues are essential—localization and saliency can diverge.  
- Proposed saliency metric: cropping then rescaling introduces artifacts—aspect-ratio changes and an implicit bias toward small regions.  
- Scale issue: heavy upscaling of small crops changes object scale, exploiting the classifier’s limited scale invariance and biasing the metric.  
- Architectural dependence: paper does not analyze sensitivity of the masking model to architecture—unclear if visualizations reflect the target network or masking-network artifacts.  
- Minor clarifications requested: whether Table 1 results are over all classes or only the ground-truth class.  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings (epsilon, alpha–beta, etc.) were used.  
- Post-rebuttal: metric effectiveness depends strongly on classifier quality and scale invariance, substantially limiting applicability.  
- Consequences: method cannot be used during training, is limited across domains (e.g., spectrogram CNNs), and is not generally applicable to black-box classifiers as claimed.  
- Recommendation: authors must explicitly and carefully discuss these limitations and dependencies for the paper to be acceptable.
