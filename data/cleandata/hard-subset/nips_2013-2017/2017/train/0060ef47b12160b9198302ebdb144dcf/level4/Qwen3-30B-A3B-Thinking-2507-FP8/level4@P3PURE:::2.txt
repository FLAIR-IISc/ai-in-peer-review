

This paper introduces a neural network designed to predict image regions critical for object categorization by another network. The presentation is clear, and the results are compelling, demonstrating strong performance relative to prior work on challenging datasets using state-of-the-art networks.  

I recommend strengthening the motivation for real-time efficiency applications, as the current justification lacks specificity regarding practical deployment scenarios. Additionally, I suggest including deeper analysis of the learned salient regions—specifically, whether certain object categories exploit background biases to facilitate recognition, which would provide valuable interpretability insights.
