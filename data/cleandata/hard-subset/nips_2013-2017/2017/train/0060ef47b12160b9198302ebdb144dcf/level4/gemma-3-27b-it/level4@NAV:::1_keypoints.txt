- Introduces a neural network method for learning saliency masks with promising speed (multiple images per second).  
- Paper is borderline acceptable; core concept of learning explanations is sound and merits consideration.  
- No critical errors identified, but several noticeable limitations exist.  
- Primary concern: saliency is measured indirectly, which is problematic and needs more discussion.  
- Weakly supervised localization is an imperfect proxy for saliency because object context important for classification may not align with localization accuracy.  
- Proposed saliency metric can introduce artifacts via cropping/rescaling (aspect ratio changes and upscaling of small regions) that may harm classification.  
- CNNs’ translation invariance does not guarantee scale invariance, so upscaling salient regions may produce non-ideal classifier inputs.  
- Paper lacks analysis of the masking model’s sensitivity to network architecture; testing different architectures would strengthen the work.  
- Minor clarification needed: whether Table 1 results are reported for all classes or only the correct class.  
- Minor clarification needed: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta).  
- Post-rebuttal: the saliency metric’s dependence on a classifier’s scale invariance severely limits applicability, excluding use during training or for models/domains without scale invariance.  
- Post-rebuttal: the claim of generalizability to black-box classifiers (as in the title) is unsupported.  
- Post-rebuttal: results may depend substantially on the masking network itself, raising ambiguity whether visualizations reflect the U-network’s saliency or the masking network’s behavior.  
- Recommendation: if the authors adequately discuss these limitations and concerns in revision, the paper could be acceptable; without such discussion, the reviewer would not support acceptance.
