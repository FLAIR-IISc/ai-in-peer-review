- Proposes an efficient saliency-mask generator that claims 100 images/sec (224×224) by training a mask model to find minimal bounding boxes for a target class using a black-box classifier.  
- Uses multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50), with a feature filter on final-scale features and upsampling blocks for mask refinement.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over existing weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization.  
- Related work coverage and references are adequate.  
- Technical approach appears sound, with clear presentation and insightful design discussions.  
- Clarify whether the 100 images/sec includes preprocessing (e.g., resizing) and provide runtimes for larger, real-world resolutions (e.g., 640×480).  
- Question performance on small objects and whether 224×224 inputs preserve necessary detail for small-instance localization.  
- Table 3 should include comparable metrics for other methods (e.g., Feed [2]) shown in Table 2 to strengthen comparisons.  
- Minor grammatical fixes: Line 273 “More over” → “Moreover”; Line 151 “optimise” → “optimizes”.  
- Overall: interesting and well-supported contributions but practical relevance depends on validating scalability to varied resolutions and small-object cases and substantiating the real-time claim.
