- Introduces training a separate U-Net masking model to produce saliency masks for any differentiable classifier in a single forward pass, avoiding iterative methods.  
- Uses a composite objective to produce smooth, small masks that identify the smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on classifier performance on a tight crop of the salient region.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on weakly-supervised object localization for CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.  
- Strength — Novelty/significance: promising direction toward practical, real-time explanations (e.g., video saliency).  
- Strength — Performance: significantly outperforms prior approaches on localization.  
- Strength — Speed: single feed-forward mask generation enables real-time use.  
- Weakness — Evaluation metrics have significant caveats that are insufficiently discussed.  
- Weakness — Localization accuracy can be misleading when classifiers rely on contextual cues outside object bounding boxes.  
- Weakness — Proposed saliency metric can be confounded by cropping/resizing (aspect-ratio distortion and upscaling), affecting classifier performance.  
- Weakness — Claims of black-box generalizability are questionable since metric relies on classifier invariances to scale/aspect ratio.  
- Weakness — Results may depend on the powerful U-Net + pretrained ResNet-50 masking architecture; unclear sensitivity to masking model choice.  
- Question for clarification: Do Table 1 localization error rates use the ground-truth class for every image or only for correctly classified images?
