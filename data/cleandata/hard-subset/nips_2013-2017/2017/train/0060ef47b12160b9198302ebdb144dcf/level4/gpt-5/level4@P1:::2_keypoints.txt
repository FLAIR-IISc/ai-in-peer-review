- Paper proposes a neural network that predicts, in a single forward pass, image regions most important to a separate black-box object classifier.  
- Method targets real-time saliency detection and is evaluated on challenging datasets with state-of-the-art architectures.  
- Experiments show strong empirical performance and favorable comparisons to prior work.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Training a separate masking network for arbitrary differentiable classifiers is an appealing and practical idea.  
- Empirical results are compelling on modern models and challenging datasets.  
- Need stronger motivation for why real-time saliency is essential; give concrete applications (interactive systems, streaming/video, on-device explanations, safety-critical uses).  
- Request deeper analysis of learned salient regions: which classes rely on background cues, whether masks reveal dataset/model biases, and consistency across classes and architectures.
