- Proposes a novel saliency mask generator that processes ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the smallest rectangular crop containing the class-salient region using a black-box classifier (AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet).  
- Architecture leverages multi-scale features (e.g., ResNet-50) with a low-res feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks for refinement.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- Literature review is thorough with appropriate citations and contextual understanding.  
- Technical approach appears sound, though reproduction is needed for full verification.  
- Authors provide clear intuition and rationale for design choices; paper is well organized.  
- Concern about the practical real-time claim and its applicability to real-world images.  
- Specific query: does the 100 fps include image resizing, and what is performance on larger images (e.g., 640x480)?  
- Specific query: how does the method perform for small objects within images and is 224x224 resolution sufficient?  
- Specific query: are saliency metric values available for other methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) for direct comparison in Table 3?  
- Minor edits: fix typos—Line 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; Line 151 "optimise" → "optimizes".  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel fast approach, state-of-the-art ImageNet localization performance, claimed real-time efficiency, and a well-defined saliency metric.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need clarification of real-time claim, unclear generalizability to small/complex objects, and lacking direct comparison to Fong & Vedaldi using the proposed metric.
