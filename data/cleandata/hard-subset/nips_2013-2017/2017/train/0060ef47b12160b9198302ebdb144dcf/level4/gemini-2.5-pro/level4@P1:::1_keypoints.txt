- Proposes amortizing saliency search by training a U-Net "masking model" to produce saliency masks in a single forward pass for any differentiable black-box classifier.  
- Masking model is trained with a composite objective to find Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR), Smallest Destroying Region (SDR), and to encourage mask smoothness.  
- Method is orders of magnitude faster than iterative per-image optimization, enabling real-time use (e.g., video).  
- Introduces a new saliency metric s(a,p) that evaluates classifier performance on cropped salient regions.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, including state-of-the-art weakly-supervised object localization on ImageNet and good performance on CIFAR-10.  
- Method is model-agnostic in treating the target classifier as a differentiable black box.  
- Localization as a primary saliency measure is limited because classifiers may rely on contextual cues, so high localization accuracy does not guarantee faithful saliency.  
- The proposed metric implementation crops and resizes salient regions, causing aspect-ratio distortion that can confound classifier performance.  
- The metric favors very small crops that must be heavily upsampled, relying on the classifier’s (imperfect) scale invariance and potentially producing misleading results.  
- Reliance on classifier scale invariance limits the claimed general applicability to arbitrary black-box classifiers and should be explicitly discussed.  
- Risk that masks reflect the inductive biases of the powerful masking model (pretrained ResNet-50 U-Net) rather than the target classifier’s reasoning.  
- No architectural ablation: paper lacks experiments showing sensitivity to masking model architecture or to training from scratch versus using a pretrained encoder.  
- Asks authors to clarify whether Table 1 localization errors are computed over all validation images or only those the black-box classifier correctly classifies.  
- Conclusion: novel and promising direction with strong results, but evaluation limitations and potential confounding by the explainer model make the paper borderline without further discussion/experiments.
