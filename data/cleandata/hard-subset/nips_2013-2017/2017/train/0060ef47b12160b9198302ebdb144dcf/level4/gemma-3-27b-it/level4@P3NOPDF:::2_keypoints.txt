- Proposes a novel neural method to predict image regions most salient for a separate object classifier; idea is compelling and well-executed.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over existing methods on benchmark datasets using state-of-the-art detectors.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance with quantitative gains and supporting visualizations; use of ImageNet/COCO and pre-trained models enhances robustness and practical relevance.  
- Weakness: lacks stronger motivation for real-time efficiency; should discuss application scenarios where speed is critical (e.g., robotics, autonomous driving, embedded systems).  
- Suggestion: include deeper analysis of learned salient regions to explain why the model attends to particular areas and reveal category-specific attention patterns or background biases.  
- Suggestion: validate attention maps against human attention or traditional saliency methods (or via human studies) for additional verification.  
- Minor: quantify computational overhead and efficiency gains (e.g., FLOPs or inference time) of the attention mechanism.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution to explainable AI; accept with minor revisions to address motivation and analysis suggestions.
