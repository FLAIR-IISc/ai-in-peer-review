- Saliency is evaluated only indirectly via weakly supervised localization and a new metric, both of which have significant limitations.
- Weakly supervised localization is not a faithful proxy for saliency because contextual cues can drive classification without correlating to true saliency.
- The proposed saliency metric may be biased by cropping/rescaling: aspect-ratio changes and heavy upscaling of small masks can distort classifier accuracy.
- The metric implicitly favors small masks and can reward degenerate solutions due to limited scale invariance of CNNs.
- The paper does not analyze architectural dependence—sensitivity of mask quality to masking-network or base-classifier choices is unaddressed.
- Clarify whether Table 1 reports results for all classes or only the ground-truth class.
- Specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used (e.g., epsilon rule, alpha–beta, and values).
- Post-rebuttal, the metric’s utility appears strongly dependent on classifier quality and scale invariance, substantially limiting applicability.
- The method cannot reliably analyze models during training or models lacking sufficient scale invariance.
- Applicability to other domains (e.g., CNNs on spectrograms) is likely constrained.
- The claim of general applicability to arbitrary black-box classifiers is overstated.
- Visualizations may reflect biases of the masking network rather than true saliency of the target model.
- Reviewer would lean to accept if limitations are explicitly discussed, claims qualified, and architectural sensitivity analyzed; otherwise recommends rejection.
