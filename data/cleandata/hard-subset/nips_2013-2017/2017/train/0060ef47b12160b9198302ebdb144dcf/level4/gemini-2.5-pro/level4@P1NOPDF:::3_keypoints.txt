- Presents a fast image saliency mask generation method claiming ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to find the tightest rectangular crop containing the class-salient region as identified by a black-box classifier (e.g., AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet).  
- Uses multi-scale feature maps (ResNet-50 backbone); final-scale features pass through a filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks refine masks.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms other weakly-supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.
