- Proposes a fast saliency-mask generator that processes ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU by training a masking model to find tight rectangular crops for a target class identified by a black‑box classifier.
- Architecture uses multi‑scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet‑50); final scale passed through a filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks refine the mask.
- Evaluated on ImageNet localization and shown to outperform other weakly supervised methods.
- Cites sufficient and relevant related work.
- Technical approach appears sound.
- Provides clear intuition and discussion for design choices.
- Paper is well‑written and clearly presented.
- Concerns about the real‑time speed claim: does the 100 img/sec include initial image resizing, and what is inference time for larger, non‑square images (e.g., 640x480)?
- Question about performance on small objects and whether 224x224 input resolution suffices to localize small targets.
