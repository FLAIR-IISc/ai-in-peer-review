- Introduces a high-throughput method for generating image saliency masks, claiming ~100 224×224 images/sec on a conventional GPU.
- Trains a masking model to produce the tightest rectangular bounding box around the salient region of a specified class using a black-box classifier.
- Architecture leverages multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50) with initial localization on the final-scale feature map and upsampling blocks to refine masks.
- Experimental results show the method outperforms other weakly supervised approaches on the ImageNet localization task.
- Paper is well-referenced and properly situated within related work.
- Technical contributions appear sound.
- Authors provide clear intuition and detailed discussion of their design choices.
- Overall presentation quality is high.
- Reviewer questions the real-time speed claim and its applicability to unconstrained, real-world images.
- Asks whether the 100 imgs/sec includes preprocessing (e.g., resizing) and requests benchmarks on larger resolutions like 640×480 (e.g., iPhone 6s).
- Requests evaluation of method performance when target objects are small and whether 224×224 resolution suffices.
- Suggests reporting Table 3 metrics for alternative methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) for fairer comparison.
- Minor corrections: fix “More over” → “Moreover” (line 273) and “optimise” → “optimises” (line 151) in phrasing.
