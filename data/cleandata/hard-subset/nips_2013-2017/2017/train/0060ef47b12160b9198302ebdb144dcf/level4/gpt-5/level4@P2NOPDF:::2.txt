This paper proposes a neural network that predicts which image regions are most influential for another neural network’s object categorization. The manuscript is clearly written, and the experiments are compelling: the method performs strongly relative to prior work, evaluated on challenging datasets and using state-of-the-art architectures.

I encourage the authors to better motivate the need for real-time efficiency by identifying concrete use cases and constraints (e.g., interactive systems, robotics, embedded or mobile deployment) where latency matters. Additionally, the paper would benefit from a deeper analysis of the learned saliency maps. For example, which object categories rely heavily on background context or dataset biases to achieve good recognition? How frequently do non-object regions drive predictions, and in what failure modes? Such insights could illuminate when and why the model succeeds, highlight potential biases, and guide future improvements.
