- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator (~100 images/sec on a standard GPU) that predicts the tightest rectangular crop covering a specified class for a black-box classifier.  
- Trains a masking model using multi-scale features (e.g., ResNet-50), with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks for mask refinement.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance among weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization.  
- Paper includes sufficient references and related work; technical correctness is not a major concern.  
- Provides clear intuition and discussion for design choices; presentation is well-organized.  
- Main concerns about the real-time speed claim and applicability to real-world images.  
- Question: Does the 100 images/sec include image resizing, and what is the runtime for larger images (e.g., 640×480 from an iPhone 6s)?  
- Question: How does the method handle small requested-class objects, and is 224×224 resolution sufficient?
