- Proposes a model-agnostic, trainable U-Net masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Achieves real-time performance (100+ FPS) and removes the computational bottleneck of iterative saliency methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric derived from the Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) objective for principled evaluation.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization (36.7% error with ResNet-50 black-box).  
- Empirically outperforms iterative baselines in speed and accuracy (Table 2).  
- Uses mask smoothness constraints and randomized evidence removal to mitigate adversarial artifacts.  
- Architecture choice (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) and objective function effectively balance smoothness, area minimization, and class confidence.  
- Results show masks align well with ground-truth bounding boxes (Table 3) and strong interpretability.  
- Paper is clearly written, reproducible, and includes informative figures and thorough ablation studies.  
- Key significance: enables real-time applications (e.g., video/autonomous driving) without pixel-level annotations.  
- Weakness: motivation for the necessity of real-time operation is underdeveloped and lacks concrete, safety-critical use cases.  
- Weakness: missing empirical analysis demonstrating that the method mitigates classifier biases (e.g., excluding snow in polar bear examples).  
- Recommendation: accept—high-quality, impactful work with minor revisions to expand real-time motivation and include bias analysis.
