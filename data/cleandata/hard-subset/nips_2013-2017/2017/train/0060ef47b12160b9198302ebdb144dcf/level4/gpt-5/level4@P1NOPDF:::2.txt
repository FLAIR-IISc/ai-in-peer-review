This paper introduces a neural network that predicts the image regions most informative to another neural network for object categorization. The manuscript is clearly written, and the experiments are compelling: the method performs remarkably well compared with prior work, including on challenging datasets and in combination with state-of-the-art architectures.

To strengthen the paper, I have two suggestions. First, better motivate the need for real-time efficiency by clarifying the target applications where latency is critical. Second, include analysis of the learned salient regions to provide insight into what the model has captured—for example, identifying which object categories rely on background context or other dataset biases to achieve recognition. Such analysis would illuminate how the approach achieves its gains and where it may be vulnerable.
