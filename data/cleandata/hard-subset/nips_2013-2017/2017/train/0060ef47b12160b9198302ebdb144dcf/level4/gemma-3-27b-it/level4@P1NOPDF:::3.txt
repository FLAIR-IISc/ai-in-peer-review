This paper introduces an image saliency mask generation approach capable of processing a hundred 224x224 images per second on a standard GPU. The authors train a masking model to identify the tightest rectangular crop containing the entire salient region of a specified class, leveraging a black-box classifier such as AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet. Their model architecture utilizes feature maps from networks like ResNet-50 at multiple scales. The final scale feature map undergoes initial localization via a feature filter, and subsequent upsampling blocks refine the resulting masks. Experiments demonstrate that their method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on the ImageNet localization task.

The paper includes a sufficient number of references and related works – a full check was not performed.

The authors present insightful rationale and discussion supporting their design choices. The paper’s presentation is clear and well-organized.

Overall, this paper presents interesting technical results. However, I have some reservations regarding the claimed real-time speed and its applicability to real-world images.
