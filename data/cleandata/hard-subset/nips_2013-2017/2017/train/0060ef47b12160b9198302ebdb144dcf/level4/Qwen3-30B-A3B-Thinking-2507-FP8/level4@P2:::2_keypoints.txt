- Introduces a novel model-agnostic masking model that generates saliency maps in a single forward pass, eliminating iterative optimization.
- Produces sharp, artifact-free saliency maps with real-time speed (100+ masks/sec).
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on weakly supervised object localization, e.g., 36.7% localization error on ImageNet without localization training data.
- Proposes a new saliency metric measuring the smallest crop that preserves classification confidence to directly assess interpretability.
- Shows that produced saliency maps have interpretability comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes under the new metric.
- Demonstrates strong empirical validation on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 using multiple classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).
- Shows the masking model can be trained from scratch (CIFAR-10) without pre-trained features, improving practical applicability.
- U-Net-based masking model architecture and training procedure are clearly described and well-designed.
- Paper clarity is excellent with effective figures illustrating maps, architecture, and comparisons.
- Original contribution is substantial as the first method to train a model for real-time saliency without iterative methods.
- Significance is high, enabling new applications such as video saliency analysis and other low-latency use cases.
- Strength: works with any differentiable classifier without requiring access to internal layers.
- Strength: introduces a more meaningful saliency evaluation metric than standard localization accuracy.
- Weakness: motivation for real-time applications is underdeveloped and needs concrete real-world examples (e.g., autonomous driving).
- Weakness: limited analysis of classifier biases revealed by saliency maps; lacks class-specific examples showing background reliance.
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion of real-time application scenarios and add a subsection analyzing class-specific saliency/bias patterns.
- Suggested improvement: provide more detail on some masking model architecture aspects and compare against more recent saliency methods.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, practically significant, and recommended for acceptance.
