- Strong empirical results: method significantly outperforms prior work on several large-scale datasets and is validated against state-of-the-art object recognition models.  
- Novelty and efficiency: provides an elegant solution that generates saliency maps in a single forward pass, faster than gradient- or perturbation-based methods.  
- Clarity of presentation: paper is well-structured and the methodology is easy to follow.  
- Motivation for real-time efficiency: paper should better justify why real-time saliency prediction is necessary and cite concrete downstream applications (e.g., autonomous driving, interactive robotics, high-throughput medical imaging).  
- Need for qualitative analysis: include deeper qualitative inspection of saliency maps to reveal model biases, counter-intuitive or sparse salient regions, and category-specific failure modes (e.g., background cues like water for boats).  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, novel, efficient, and effective contribution to model interpretability; recommend acceptance with suggested minor improvements.
