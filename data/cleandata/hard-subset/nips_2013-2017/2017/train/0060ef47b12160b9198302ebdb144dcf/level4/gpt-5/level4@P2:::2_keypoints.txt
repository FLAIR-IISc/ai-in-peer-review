- Proposes a trainable masking network that generates class-conditional saliency maps for any differentiable image classifier in a single forward pass.  
- Training objective balances smoothness (TV), sparsity (area), preserved-region recognition (SSR), and score suppression when salient regions are removed (SDR).  
- Reduces adversarial artifacts by enforcing mask smoothness and applying masks to randomized alternative images (blur or noise).  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50 as black boxes) and CIFAR-10, showing sharper, more interpretable maps than prior methods.  
- Demonstrates competitive weakly supervised localization without using bounding box annotations.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region principle, yielding favorable results.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured motivation and comparison to gradient-based and iterative baselines.  
- Strength: model-agnostic, fast (single pass), and produces visually sharp, artifact-resistant maps suitable for real-time use.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on challenging datasets and architectures.  
- Strength: proposed saliency metric is sensible and helps quantify interpretability beyond localization accuracy.  
- Area for improvement: better motivate real-time requirements with concrete scenarios and clarify latency/throughput targets.  
- Area for improvement: provide more analysis of learned saliency across classes to probe dataset/model biases, class-wise stats, and failure cases.  
- Quality: method is technically sound with strong empirical results; more failure/bias analysis would strengthen it.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but real-time motivation and bias analysis sections could be expanded.  
- Originality & significance: distinctive single-pass masking network and randomized evidence removal; practical for real-time explainability and may influence evaluation practices.  
- Overall: interesting, well-executed paper with strong performance; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and additional bias analysis.
