- Proposes a novel masking model for real-time image saliency that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for any differentiable classifier, trained with objectives for smoothness, small region size, and accurate classification.
- Paper is well-written and experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet demonstrate effectiveness.
- Method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization and yields better saliency metrics than popular detectors.
- Approach has potential for real-time applications (e.g., video saliency) and for interpreting black-box classifier decisions.
- Lacks detailed analysis of learned salient regions, including which categories exploit background biases.
- Insufficient motivation and context for the need for real-time efficiency in saliency detection.
- Potential biases in the masking model are not thoroughly investigated.
- Suggested improvements: provide application contexts for real-time needs, analyze learned regions in depth, and investigate model biases.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
