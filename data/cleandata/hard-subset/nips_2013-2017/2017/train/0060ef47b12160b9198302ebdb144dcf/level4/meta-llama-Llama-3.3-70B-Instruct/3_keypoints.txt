- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator claiming 100 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a specified class using black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet).  
- Model architecture leverages multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50) with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks for mask refinement.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance among weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- References and related work appear adequate, though not exhaustively checked.  
- Paper seems technically correct at a high level, but full verification was not performed.  
- Authors provide a clear design intuition and overall strong presentation.  
- Major concerns about the claimed real-time speed and whether it includes image resizing and scales to larger images (e.g., 640x480 from an iPhone 6s).  
- Question about performance when the target object is small in the image and whether 224x224 input is sufficient.  
- Requests clarification on Table 3 metrics for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]).  
- Minor typos to correct: LN 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; LN 151 "optimise" → "optimises" (British spelling) or "optimizes" (US).
