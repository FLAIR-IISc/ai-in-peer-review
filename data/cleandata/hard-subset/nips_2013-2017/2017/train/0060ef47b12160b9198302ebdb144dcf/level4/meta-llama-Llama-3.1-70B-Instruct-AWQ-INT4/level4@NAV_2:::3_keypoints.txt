- Proposes a novel trainable masking model to generate image saliency masks for a black-box classifier.  
- Claims real-time throughput (>100 224x224 images/s on a standard GPU).  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that measures classifier recognition from the preserved region.  
- Uses a U-Net-style architecture with multi-resolution feature maps and a ResNet-50 encoder (modified for the task).  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on ImageNet localisation (36.7% localisation error with ResNet-50 black box).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 with the masking model trained from scratch.  
- Paper is well-written with clear descriptions of architecture, training, and experiments.  
- Thorough related-work discussion and clear presentation of the new metric.  
- Concern: real-time speed claim may omit image resizing time and may not hold for larger images (e.g., 640x480).  
- Concern: performance on small objects is unclear since evaluated salient objects are relatively large and images are 224x224.  
- Concern: comparisons are incomplete; additional metrics (e.g., for Feed [2]) should be included in Table 3.  
- Recommendation: accept with requested revisions to clarify speed measurements, evaluate larger images and small-object cases, and include more comparative metrics.
