- Proposes a masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for black-box classifiers, avoiding slow per-image iterative optimization.  
- Trains the masker with a composite objective encouraging small, smooth masks that preserve classifier confidence on the Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) and destroy it when removed (Smallest Destroying Region, SDR).  
- Introduces a new information-concentration metric for evaluating saliency.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming other weakly-supervised methods on object localization.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among weakly-supervised approaches and approaches some fully supervised accuracies on ImageNet localization.  
- Novel and efficient approach enabling real-time saliency map generation, useful for applications like video analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, with a sound experimental setup.  
- Model-agnostic: treats the target classifier as a black box (only needs differentiability for training), making it broadly applicable.  
- Weakness: Motivation for real-time applications is underdeveloped and should be strengthened with concrete use-cases earlier in the paper.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper qualitative analysis of learned saliency maps and investigation of classifier biases (e.g., confirming whether classifiers rely on spurious cues like "snow" for polar bear).
