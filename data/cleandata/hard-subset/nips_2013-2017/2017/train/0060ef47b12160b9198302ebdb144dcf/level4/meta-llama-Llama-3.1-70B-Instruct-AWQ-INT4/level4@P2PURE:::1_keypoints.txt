- Proposes a neural-network method to learn saliency masks that runs at multiple images per second.
- Reviewer is borderline; paper has good ideas but clear limitations—could accept or reject.
- No obvious mistakes, but saliency measurement is indirect and depends on two imperfect metrics that need discussion.
- Weakly supervised localization is an imperfect proxy for saliency because localization may not reflect saliency when context determines class.
- The proposed saliency metric is flawed by its crop-and-rescale procedure, introducing aspect-ratio artifacts and preferring smaller regions.
- Scaling issues matter: CNNs are not inherently scale-invariant, so heavy rescaling can distort classifier performance and metric validity.
- The method’s reliance on classifier invariance limits applicability: not suitable during model training, for non-scale-invariant models, other domains, or black-box classifiers as claimed.
- Unclear how sensitive the masking model is to architecture—experiments with different architectures and their effects are requested.
- Minor: clarify whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the predicted/correct class.
- Minor: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta) were used for comparisons.
- Post-rebuttal concern: strong dependence on the masking network makes it unclear whether visualizations reflect the U-network or the masking network—must be discussed or paper should not be published.
