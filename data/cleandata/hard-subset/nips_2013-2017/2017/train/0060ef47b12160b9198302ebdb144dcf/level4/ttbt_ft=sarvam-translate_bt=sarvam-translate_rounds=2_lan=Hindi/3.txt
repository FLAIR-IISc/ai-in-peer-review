This paper presents an image-based saltenance mask generation approach that can process 100,224x224 images per second on a standard GPU. Their approach trains a masking model that finds the tightest rectangular crop containing the entire salten region of a particular class requested by a black box classifier, such as AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet. Their model architecture requires image feature maps at various scales, such as those by ResNet-50. The final scale feature will be passed through a feature filter that performs initial localization, while subsequent upsampling blocks correct the produced mask. Experiments show that their method performs better than other weakly supervised techniques in the ImageNet localization task.
