- Proposes a novel neural architecture that predicts salient image regions used by a separate classifier.  
- Tackles interpretability and efficiency by showing region identification can speed downstream tasks without losing accuracy.  
- Writing is clear and structure is sound.  
- Comprehensive experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet using state-of-the-art classifiers.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements in both speed and accuracy, especially under latency constraints.  
- High practical relevance for real-time applications (autonomous driving, AR, mobile vision).  
- Paper should more explicitly discuss concrete deployment scenarios requiring sub-second processing (e.g., drones, real-time medical imaging).  
- Lacks qualitative analysis of learned saliency patterns to explain why regions are deemed salient and to detect possible dataset biases.  
- Recommends visualizations/statistical analyses (e.g., t-SNE, background vs. foreground attention) to probe category-specific biases.  
- Technical execution is strong with thorough ablation studies and comparisons to Grad-CAM/CAM.  
- Needs expanded discussion of limitations and failure modes (occlusion, low-contrast scenes) and quantification of real-time claims (FPS on specific hardware).  
- Overall assessment: novel, rigorous, and impactful work with minor refinements suggested; recommendation to accept.
