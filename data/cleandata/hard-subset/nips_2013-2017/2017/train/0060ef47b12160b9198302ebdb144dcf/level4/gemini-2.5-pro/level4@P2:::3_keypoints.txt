- Proposes a masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that generates saliency masks in one forward pass for any differentiable black-box classifier.  
- Trains the masker with an objective balancing small, smooth masks that preserve classifier confidence on the kept region and destroy it on the removed region (SSR and SDR).  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on information concentration in a cropped region for evaluating explanations.  
- Achieves real-time inference (>100 images/sec on 224x224) and outperforms iterative per-instance methods in speed.  
- Shows strong experimental results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving SOTA weakly supervised object localization and competitive performance with some fully supervised methods.  
- Ablation on CIFAR-10 demonstrates general applicability, including training the masker from scratch without a pre-trained encoder.  
- Technical design is sound: thoughtful objective (TV, AV, sufficiency, destructiveness) and sensible U-Net architecture leveraging multi-scale features.  
- Novel contribution: amortizing mask optimization by learning a separate explanation network rather than per-instance optimization.  
- Clear writing and organization, with good intuition for SSR/SDR and introduced evidence issues.  
- Real-time performance claim lacks context: needs evaluation on larger, more realistic image sizes and clarity on whether preprocessing (resizing) is included.  
- Robustness concern: unclear performance for small, non-central objects due to 224x224 input downsampling; needs analysis vs. object scale/bounding box size.  
- Incomplete comparison: new saliency metric results (Table 3) omit some competing methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi), preventing full apples-to-apples evaluation.  
- Weaknesses are mainly in evaluation breadth and claim substantiation rather than fundamental methodological flaws.  
- Questions for authors: clarify whether 100 images/sec includes resizing and report inference times for larger resolutions (e.g., 640x480).  
- Request to evaluate other methods (especially Fong & Vedaldi) under the proposed saliency metric for direct comparison.  
- Ask whether downsampling to 224x224 impairs detection of small salient objects and for performance analysis by bounding box size.
