- Proposes a fast saliency mask generator processing ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the minimal bounding box for a class-salient region using a pretrained “black box” classifier.  
- Uses multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50) with a final-scale feature filter and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Claims to outperform other weakly supervised localization methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- Literature coverage is adequate and the paper shows good understanding of related work.  
- Technical approach appears sound and the authors provide clear intuition and design discussion.  
- Presentation is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Reviewer questions the claimed real-time speed and requests clarification whether the 100 imgs/sec includes image resizing and performance on larger images (e.g., 640x480).  
- Concerns about generalizability to real-world images beyond ImageNet.  
- Asks how the method handles small objects in images and whether a fixed 224x224 input is sufficient or if an adaptive approach is needed.  
- Requests that comparative metrics for methods in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) be added to Table 3 for direct comparison.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “More over, because our model” → “Moreover, because our model” (Line 273) and “only optimise the mask” → “only optimizes the mask” (Line 151).
