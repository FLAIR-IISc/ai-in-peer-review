- Proposes a novel trainable masking model that produces high-quality saliency masks in a single forward pass for black-box classifiers and achieves state-of-the-art on ImageNet localisation.  
- Model is trained to find the tightest rectangular crop that contains the entire salient region for a requested class.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a thorough discussion of related work and design intuition.  
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate effectiveness across multiple datasets.  
- Unclear whether the reported ~100 FPS for 224×224 images includes image resizing; request clarification and runtimes for larger images (e.g., 640×480 from iPhone 6s).  
- Concern about handling small objects—question whether 224×224 inputs are sufficient to capture small requested-class regions.  
- Table 3 compares methods using the proposed saliency metric, but comparable metrics for other methods in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) are missing.  
- Minor editorial corrections: LN273 "More over" → "Moreover"; LN151 "optimise" → "optimises".  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel real-time approach, thorough contextualization, and strong experimental validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential overclaim of real-time speed, possible ineffectiveness for small objects, and insufficient comparative evaluation on the proposed metric.
