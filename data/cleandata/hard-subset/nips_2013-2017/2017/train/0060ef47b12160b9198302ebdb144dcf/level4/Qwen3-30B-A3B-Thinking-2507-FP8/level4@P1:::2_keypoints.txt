- Novel masking model that predicts saliency maps for any differentiable classifier in a single forward pass, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Addresses the speed gap of iterative saliency methods, making real-time/video analysis feasible.  
- Key contributions: model-agnostic approach, a new Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) saliency metric, and evaluations on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Trains a U-Net-based masking network with a composite loss enforcing TV smoothness, minimal preserved region size, and high classification confidence on the masked region.  
- Produces sharp, artifact-free saliency maps that generalize to unseen images.  
- Achieves superior localization (36.7% error on ImageNet) and 100+ saliency maps/sec, outperforming iterative baselines in speed and quality.  
- Saliency maps have interpretability comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes despite no localization data used in training.  
- Strong experimental validation across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and datasets, with convincing ablation studies.  
- Originality: first single-model, one-pass, model-agnostic saliency predictor distinct from prior iterative methods.  
- Significance: real-time capability enables new applications (e.g., video/autonomous vehicles) and advances weakly supervised localization.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with clear figures, architecture, and reproducible training details.  
- Weakness: limited concrete motivation and latency context for real-time applications (e.g., sub-100ms requirements).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of whether saliency maps reveal classifier/background biases (e.g., “snow” cues for polar bears).  
- Weakness: SSR metric assumes classifier scale-invariance, an assumption not fully acknowledged or validated.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-impact contribution with robust evaluation — recommend acceptance.
