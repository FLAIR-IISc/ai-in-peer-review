- Proposes a trainable masking model that produces high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time use with black-box classifiers.
- Paper is well-written and presents an interesting, novel solution to image saliency detection.
- Provides a thorough overview of state of the art and limitations of existing saliency methods.
- Introduces a new evaluation metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).
- Demonstrates impressive experimental results on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, ImageNet).
- Shows improved localisation accuracy and saliency metric performance versus weakly supervised and existing methods.
- Strength: novel, effective approach suitable for real-time saliency detection of black-box models.
- Strength: comprehensive SOTA review and a new, principled saliency evaluation metric.
- Strength: strong empirical results supporting the method’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of learned salient regions and potential background-bias exploitation by object categories.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of real-time application contexts and concrete use cases for the method.
- Suggestion: provide more detail on real-time application scenarios and how the method would be deployed.
- Suggestion: include analysis of which object categories exploit background cues and other learned-region insights.
- Suggestion: add more visualizations of produced saliency maps to illustrate effectiveness.
- Acceptance arguments: novel method, strong experiments, and a new evaluation metric with thorough SOTA context.
- Rejection arguments: needs deeper analysis of learned regions and clearer articulation of application-level utility.
