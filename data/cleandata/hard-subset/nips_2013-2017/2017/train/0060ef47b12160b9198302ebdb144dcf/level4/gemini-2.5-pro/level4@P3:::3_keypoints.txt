- Proposes a masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that generates saliency maps for differentiable black-box image classifiers in a single feed-forward pass.  
- Trains the model with an objective combining smallest sufficient region (SSR), smallest destroying region (SDR), area penalty, and total-variation smoothness to avoid adversarial artifacts.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric measuring classifier confidence on a tight crop of the salient region normalized by crop area.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet with AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet-50, achieving state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization and real-time speeds (claimed >100 images/sec).  

- Novel and significant idea: amortizing saliency search via a generative masking model enables fast inference and potential real-time applications.  
- Strong empirical performance: sets new state-of-the-art on ImageNet weakly supervised localization, producing semantically meaningful masks.  
- Well-motivated objective: combines sufficiency, necessity, sparsity, and smoothness; new metric is a useful contribution.  
- Clear writing and organization with supportive qualitative figures.  

- Real-time claim needs more detail: clarify preprocessing inclusion and performance on larger, real-world resolutions (e.g., 640x480, megapixel).  
- Potential weakness on small objects: ResNet-50 encoder’s 32× downsampling may miss small-object features; results on datasets with small objects or discussion are needed.  
- Incomplete metric comparison: should include iterative optimization baseline (Fong & Vedaldi [2]) under the new saliency metric.  
- Practical overhead: training a separate complex masking model per black-box classifier incurs significant setup cost; discuss trade-off between inference speed and training overhead.  

- Two minor typos to fix: "optimise(s)" and "Moreover".  

- Overall: strong, well-executed paper that advances practical, high-quality saliency mapping, with evaluation/discussion gaps that could be strengthened.
