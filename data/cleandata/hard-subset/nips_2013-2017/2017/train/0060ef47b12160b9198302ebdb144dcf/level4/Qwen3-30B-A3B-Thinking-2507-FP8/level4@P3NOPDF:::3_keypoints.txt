- Introduces a fast saliency-mask method that processes 100 images/s at 224×224 using a pre-trained classifier, multi-scale feature maps, a feature filter, and upsampling blocks; outperforms prior weakly supervised localization on ImageNet.

- Paper is well-motivated with a clear technical contribution, thorough evaluation, comprehensive related work, sound approach, and clear presentation.

- Concern 1 — Real-time claim: clarify if the 100 images/s includes resizing to 224×224 and report runtimes for larger native resolutions (e.g., 640×480).

- Concern 2 — Small-object performance: unclear how method handles targets occupying <5% of the image; cropping to 224×224 may lose context—ablation by object size needed.

- Concern 3 — Table 3 comparability: Table 3 lacks direct comparisons to methods in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]); include competing metrics or justify omission.

- Minor corrections: Line 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; Line 151 "optimise" → "optimises".

- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance pending clarifications on input-resolution scalability, small-object evaluation, and table comparisons.
