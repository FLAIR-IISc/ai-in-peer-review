- Proposes a learned masking model that predicts saliency masks in one forward pass, avoiding iterative methods.  
- Method is very fast, enabling potential real-time applications.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on ImageNet weakly supervised localization and a newly proposed saliency metric.  
- Architecture uses a U-Net with a ResNet encoder; details are well-described and motivated.  
- Addresses an important problem of explaining image classifier decisions and reduces per-image optimization cost.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on weakly supervised localization, which may not align with human-perceived saliency when context matters.  
- The new saliency metric (cropping and resizing) can introduce artifacts and distort features, affecting classifier performance.  
- Paper does not sufficiently analyze how cropping/resizing pushes classifiers beyond learned invariances.  
- Unclear how dependent the masking model’s performance is on the underlying classifier architecture or its scale/translation invariance.  
- Reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder suggests possible dependency on ImageNet feature representations.  
- The “model-agnostic”/“black-box” claim is overstated given demonstrated dependencies on classifier properties.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient solution, strong quantitative results, clear presentation, and real-time/video potential.  
- Arguments for rejection: evaluation metrics have limitations, potential artifacts from metric steps, and unclear generalizability across classifiers.  
- Minor requests: clarify whether Table 1 uses all images or only correctly classified ones; specify exact LRP variants and parameter settings used.  
- Post-rebuttal concern: strong dependence on classifier invariances limits general applicability (e.g., spectrograms, non-translation-invariant models) and raises whether the masks reflect the classifier or the masking network itself; recommend thorough discussion of these limitations or consider rejection.
