- Proposes a neural masking model that predicts salient image regions relative to another network’s classification in a single forward pass, enabling fast saliency detection.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of method, motivation, and experiments.  
- Shows strong empirical performance versus existing techniques, notably on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 with state-of-the-art classifiers.  
- Situates work in interpretability literature, referencing adversarial perturbations and classifier failure modes; frames contribution as a fast, model-agnostic alternative to iterative saliency methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) and Smallest Destroying Region (SDR).  
- Architectural/training choices (U-Net with pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder, fake labels, randomized evidence removal) are well-justified and practical.  
- Produces sharp, interpretable saliency maps and achieves competitive localization accuracy.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of real-world, real-time application scenarios (e.g., autonomous driving, robotics, HCI).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the types of features emphasized by learned maps and whether they reveal classifier biases (e.g., background reliance).  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical novelty (speed improvement), strong empirical results, clear presentation, and a useful novel metric.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited application discussion and superficial feature/bias analysis.  
- Overall verdict: solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
