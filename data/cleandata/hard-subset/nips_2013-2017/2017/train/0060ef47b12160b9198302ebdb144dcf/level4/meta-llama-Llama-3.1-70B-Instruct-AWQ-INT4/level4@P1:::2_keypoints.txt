- Presents a neural network that predicts image regions important for another NN's object classification; paper is well-written and shows strong results.  
- Main idea: a fast, trainable masking model producing saliency maps in a single forward pass for any differentiable classifier.  
- Model trained with an objective encouraging masks that are smooth, small, and informative for the classifier.  
- Designed for real-time applications due to single-pass inference.  
- Extensive evaluation on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet using AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet-50.  
- Outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localisation and achieves SOTA on CIFAR-10.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical performance and a useful new metric.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of learned salient regions and potential dataset/category biases (e.g., background cues).  
- Weakness: insufficient motivation and concrete examples for the claimed need for real-time efficiency.  
- Weakness: additional experiments or comparisons with other methods would strengthen the claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, SOTA results, valuable new metric, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing deeper analysis, weak real-time motivation, need for more experiments/comparisons.  
- Overall score: 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
