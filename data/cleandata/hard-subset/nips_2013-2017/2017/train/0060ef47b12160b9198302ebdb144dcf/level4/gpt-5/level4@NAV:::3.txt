Review- This paper introduces an image saliency mask generation method capable of processing about 100 224×224 images per second on a standard GPU. The approach trains a masking model to find the tightest rectangular crop that fully covers the salient region of a requested class as determined by a black-box classifier, such as AlexNet, GoogLeNet, or ResNet. The architecture consumes multi-scale image feature maps, e.g., from ResNet-50. The final-scale features are passed through a feature filter for initial localization, and subsequent upsampling blocks refine the predicted masks. Experiments show the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on the ImageNet localization task.
This paper seems to include sufficient references and related work. Not fully verified.

Comments:
- Does the reported 100 images/second include the image resizing step? If so, what is the runtime for larger inputs, such as 640×480 images from an iPhone 6s?
- The salient objects in the paper’s examples are quite large; what happens when the target-class object is small? Is a 224×224 input sufficient in that scenario?
- In Table 3, are there corresponding metrics for other methods listed in Table 2, such as Feed [2]?
