- Proposes a fast image saliency detection method usable with any differentiable image classifier by training a masking model to find the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a requested class.  
- Model uses multi-scale image feature maps; final-scale features go through a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric to assess explanation quality from saliency detectors.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and intuitive design explanations.  
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- Paper appears technically correct and is well presented.  
- Concern: real-time speed claim unclear — does 100 images/sec include image resizing, and what is runtime on larger images (e.g., 640×480 from an iPhone 6s)?  
- Concern: handling of small salient objects is not discussed — is 224×224 input sufficient for small targets?  
- Request: include corresponding metrics for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) in Table 3 for a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Minor corrections: LN 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; LN 151 "optimise" → "optimises" (and phrasing fix).  
- Strength: fast, accurate, broadly applicable saliency method with solid presentation and new evaluation metric.  
- Weakness: insufficient evaluation of real-world speed and larger-image performance, inadequate discussion of small-object cases, and incomplete comparative metrics.  
- Acceptance argument: novel, technically sound approach with strong ImageNet localization results and good literature/metric contributions.  
- Rejection argument: unresolved concerns about runtime claims, small-object handling, and incomplete comparisons may limit practical impact.
