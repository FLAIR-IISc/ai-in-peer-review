- Paper proposes learning saliency masks with a neural network and shows efficient processing of multiple images per second.  
- Reviewer finds the paper borderline; would not object to acceptance or rejection.  
- Concept of learning to explain a model is valuable and presents interesting ideas.  
- No obvious errors, but clear limitations need addressing.  
- Saliency is only indirectly measured via weakly supervised localization or the proposed saliency metric, both of which have limitations.  
- Weakly supervised localization may not correlate with saliency when object context is essential for class determination.  
- The proposed saliency metric may introduce artifacts from cropping/rescaling: aspect-ratio changes can affect classification.  
- The metric’s preference for small salient regions and re-scaling may lead to suboptimal classifier performance because CNNs are not inherently scale-invariant.  
- The paper does not discuss dependence of the masking model on network architecture or whether different architectures were evaluated.  
- Minor: clarify whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the correct class.  
- Minor: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used in comparisons (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta).  
- Post-rebuttal: the saliency metric’s heavy reliance on classifier quality and scale invariance significantly limits applicability.  
- Post-rebuttal: method cannot be used during model training, for models lacking scale invariance, or broadly as a black-box explanation tool as claimed.  
- Post-rebuttal: strong dependence on the masking network raises uncertainty whether the method visualizes the U-network’s saliency or the masking network’s behavior.  
- Recommendation: if these effects are properly discussed in the paper it may be acceptable for publication; otherwise it should not be published.
