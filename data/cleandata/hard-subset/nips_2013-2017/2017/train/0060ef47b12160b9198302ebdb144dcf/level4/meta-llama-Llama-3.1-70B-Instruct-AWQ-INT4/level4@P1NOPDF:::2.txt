This paper introduces a neural network (NN) designed to predict the regions of an image that are important for another NN to perform object categorization. The paper is well-written and engaging, and the experimental results are particularly impressive, demonstrating the proposed method's effectiveness compared to previous works, even on challenging datasets with state-of-the-art NNs.

One potential area for improvement is in providing a clearer motivation for the need for real-time efficiency in specific applications. While the paper mentions the importance of efficiency, a more detailed explanation of the scenarios in which this is crucial would strengthen the argument.

Additionally, I would have liked to see a deeper analysis of the learnt salient regions, providing insights into which object categories tend to exploit biases in the background to recognize the object. For instance, do certain categories rely more heavily on contextual information, and if so, how does the proposed method handle these cases? Exploring these questions could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and limitations of the approach.
