This paper discusses a salient mask generation method capable of processing 100 images of size 224x224 per second on a standard GPU. Their method trains a masking model that finds the tightest rectangular crop containing the complete salient region of a specific class requested by a black box classifier, such as AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet. Their model architecture requires image feature maps at various scales similar to ResNet-50. The final scale feature will be passed through a feature filter that performs initial localization, while following up sampling blocks fine-tune the produced masks. Tests show that their method performs better than other weakly supervised techniques in image net localization tasks.

- The salient objects in this paper are quite large; what if the objects of the requested class are small in the image? Will 224x224 images be sufficient?
