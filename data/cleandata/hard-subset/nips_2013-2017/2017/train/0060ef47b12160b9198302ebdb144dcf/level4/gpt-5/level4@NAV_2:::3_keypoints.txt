- Introduces a real-time method for generating image saliency masks, reporting ~100 images/sec at 224×224 on a standard GPU.
- Trains a masking network that, given a target class for a black-box classifier, predicts a mask whose tightest enclosing rectangle captures salient evidence for that class.
- Architecture leverages multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50), applies a coarse feature filter for initial localization, then refines masks with upsampling blocks.
- Experiments claim the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.
- Related work and references appear adequate (reviewer did not check exhaustively).
- Technical claims seem reasonable though the reviewer did not verify every detail.
- Paper supplies several intuitions and design discussions motivating the approach.
- Presentation is clear and well organized.
- Reviewer expresses concern about the real-time speed claim and applicability to real-world imagery.
- Question: Does the 100 images/sec include image resizing, and what is throughput for larger inputs (e.g., 640×480 from an iPhone 6s)?
- Question: How does the method perform when the target object is small; is 224×224 sufficient for that regime?
- Request: In Table 3, report the corresponding metric for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., the approach in [2]).
