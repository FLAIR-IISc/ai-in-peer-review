- Introduces a neural-network method for learning saliency masks, running at multiple images per second; reviewer is borderline on acceptance.
- Core idea of learning to explain model behavior is valuable and the paper presents promising concepts without fundamental errors.
- Major concern: saliency is measured indirectly using weakly supervised localization or a proposed metric, both having inherent weaknesses that need fuller discussion.
- Weakly supervised localization is a problematic proxy for saliency because object localization may fail when contextual information is crucial for classification.
- The proposed saliency metric may introduce artifacts: cropping and rescaling (with preserved aspect ratio) can distort classification and favors smaller regions.
- Upscaling small salient regions can present unrealistic scales to the classifier, exceeding learned scale invariance and harming evaluation validity.
- Paper lacks analysis of how sensitive the masking model and resulting masks are to the underlying classifier architecture—this is critical for generalizability.
- Minor clarifications requested: Table 1 — are results reported over all classes or only correctly classified examples?
- Minor clarifications requested: specify exact LRP variant and parameter settings used for comparisons (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta).
- Post-rebuttal: method strongly depends on classifier quality and scale invariance, limiting when and where it can be applied.
- Practical consequences: method cannot reliably be used during model training or on models without inherent scale invariance.
- Applicability is limited outside standard image classification (e.g., spectrogram/CNN domains) due to scale and classifier-dependence.
- Title claim of general applicability to “black-box classifiers” is inaccurate given the method’s dependencies.
- Rebuttal suggests masking network architecture strongly influences results, raising doubt whether visualizations reflect the target model’s saliency or the masker’s characteristics.
- If authors address these limitations and provide a balanced discussion, publication could be warranted; without that, reviewer recommends rejection.
