- Introduces a learned U-Net masking model that generates saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass instead of slow per-instance optimization.  
- Trains masks with a custom objective promoting smoothness, smallest sufficient region (SSR), and destructive removal (SDR).  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on concentration of class-information in a cropped region.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving state-of-the-art weakly supervised localization (ResNet-50 localization error 36.7%).  
- Strength: Amortized approach enables real-time performance (100+ images/sec), enabling applications like video saliency.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results that substantially outperform other weakly supervised methods.  
- Strength: Well-motivated objective addressing “introduced evidence” and a thoughtful problem formulation.  
- Strength: Clear writing, well-explained architecture/training, and compelling qualitative results.  
- Weakness: Real-time speed claim needs clarification—does 100 images/sec include preprocessing/resizing and how does latency scale with larger resolutions (e.g., 640x480)?  
- Weakness: Unclear sensitivity to small objects; downsampling to 224x224 may hurt detection of small salient regions.  
- Weakness: New metric not evaluated for competing methods in Table 2—needs broader comparison to validate superiority.
