- Proposes a class-specific image saliency mask model that finds the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region, processing ~100 224×224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Model consumes multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50); uses a feature filter on the final-scale map for initial localization and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Demonstrates better performance than other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- References and related work appear sufficient (not exhaustively checked).  
- Paper appears technically correct and provides useful intuition and design discussion; overall presentation is good.  
- Reviewer finds the results interesting but has concerns about the real-time speed claim and real-world applicability.  
- Question: Does the reported ~100 imgs/sec include image resizing, and what is runtime for larger images (e.g., 640×480 iPhone photos)?  
- Question: How does the method perform when the requested-class object is small in the image— is a 224×224 input sufficient?  
- Question: In Table 3, is there a corresponding metric reported for other works listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2])?
