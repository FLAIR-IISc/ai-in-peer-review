- Paper proposes a neural saliency-mask method with real-time performance; conceptually promising but evaluation and scope are insufficient for NeurIPS.  
- Main flaw: saliency quality is measured indirectly and incorrectly via weakly supervised localization (WSL) and a new crop/rescale saliency metric.  
- WSL is inappropriate when classification relies on contextual cues, as it conflates localization accuracy with interpretability.  
- The proposed saliency metric creates artifacts by cropping and rescaling: aspect-ratio distortion and unnatural scale changes bias classifier behavior.  
- The metric’s validity entirely depends on the classifier’s (unexamined) scale/translation invariance, a dependency the paper fails to discuss.  
- This invariance dependence prevents use during model training, restricts domain applicability, and invalidates the claim of applicability to arbitrary black-box classifiers.  
- Method shows strong dependence on the masking network architecture (e.g., U-Net); authors do not test whether visualizations reflect the target model or the masker’s biases.  
- Required clarifications/revisions: Table 1 averaging policy, exact LRP variant/parameters for baselines, analysis of masking-architecture dependence, and explicit discussion of scale-invariance limitations.  
- Recommendation: promising idea but insufficiently rigorous—reject unless authors substantially analyze classifier/mask dependencies or acknowledge limited applicability.
