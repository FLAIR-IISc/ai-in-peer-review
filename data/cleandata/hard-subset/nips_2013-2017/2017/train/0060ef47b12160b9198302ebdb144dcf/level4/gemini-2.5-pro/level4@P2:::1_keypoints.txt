- Proposes a novel "masking model" (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that generates saliency maps in one forward pass, amortizing a costly optimization process.  
- Introduces a composite loss encouraging smooth, small masks that are both sufficient when kept (SSR) and critical when removed (SDR).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, achieving state-of-the-art weakly-supervised object localization on ImageNet and good results on CIFAR-10.  
- Proposes a new saliency evaluation metric based on classifier performance on cropped salient regions.  
- Claims real-time saliency generation enabling new applications (e.g., video), which is a significant practical advantage.  
- Strength: originality and significance of training a dedicated explainer network for fast, high-quality saliency.  
- Strength: well-designed objective combining TV, area, SSR and SDR terms to produce desirable mask properties.  
- Strength: effort to create a more interpretable evaluation beyond localization accuracy.  
- Weakness: equating saliency with object localization is a strong assumption—classifiers may use contextual cues outside bounding boxes.  
- Weakness: the proposed metric resizes cropped regions, assuming classifier invariance to scale/aspect-ratio changes, limiting generality and applicability to arbitrary differentiable classifiers.  
- Weakness: the "black-box" claim is overstated because success depends on unverified classifier invariances and may not transfer to other domains.  
- Weakness: masking model itself is powerful and may introduce its own priors/biases, confounding whether masks reflect the target classifier or the masker.  
- Weakness: no architectural ablation to assess how the masker’s design affects explanations and robustness.  
- Missing/unclear details: how Table 1 localization errors are computed (ground-truth class vs. averaged), which LRP variant/parameters used for baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: paper is borderline — promising and original but needs revision to temper claims, discuss limitations (classifier invariance, masker bias), and add analyses (ablations, clarifications) before acceptance.
