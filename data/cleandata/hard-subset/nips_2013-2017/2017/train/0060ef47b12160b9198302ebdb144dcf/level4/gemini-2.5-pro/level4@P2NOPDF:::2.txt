This paper presents a novel approach for generating saliency maps by training a neural network to predict which regions of an image are most influential for a separate object recognition model's classification decision. The paper is well-written, and the proposed method is presented clearly.

The primary strength of the work lies in its empirical results. The evaluation is conducted on challenging, large-scale datasets using state-of-the-art object recognition architectures, which convincingly validates the effectiveness of the approach. The proposed model demonstrates a significant performance improvement over existing methods for saliency prediction, which is an impressive achievement.

While the technical contribution and quantitative results are strong, the paper could be improved in two main areas:

1.  **Motivation for Real-Time Efficiency:** The manuscript highlights the real-time efficiency of the proposed model as a key advantage. However, the motivation for this requirement could be more thoroughly articulated. The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of specific applications or use cases where generating saliency maps at this speed is a critical necessity, thus distinguishing it from other, potentially slower, attribution methods.

2.  **Qualitative Analysis of Saliency Maps:** The paper currently lacks a deeper qualitative analysis of the learned saliency maps. While the quantitative results confirm *that* the method works well, a qualitative analysis could provide valuable insights into *why* it works and what it reveals about the underlying object recognition model's behavior. For instance, the authors could investigate systematic patterns in the saliency maps across different object categories. This could reveal interesting phenomena, such as which classes are more prone to relying on background context or spurious correlations for their predictions. Providing a few such case studies would significantly increase the paper's contribution to the field of model interpretability.

Overall, this is a solid paper with a compelling method and strong results. Addressing the points above would further enhance its impact.
