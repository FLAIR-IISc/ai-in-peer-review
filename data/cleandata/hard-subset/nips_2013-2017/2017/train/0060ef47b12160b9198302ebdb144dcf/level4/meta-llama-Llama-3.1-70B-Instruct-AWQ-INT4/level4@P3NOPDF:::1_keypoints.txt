- Proposes a fast neural-network method for learning saliency masks (multiple images/sec).
- Concept of learning to explain a model is compelling, but paper is borderline accept/reject.
- Saliency is only indirectly measured via weakly supervised localization and a proposed saliency metric, both needing caution.
- Weakly supervised localization may not correlate with true saliency, especially when context determines class.
- The proposed saliency metric involves cropping/rescaling which can introduce artifacts (aspect-ratio change and suboptimal scale) affecting classifier accuracy.
- CNNs are translation-invariant but not inherently scale-invariant, undermining the saliency metric’s assumptions.
- Paper does not discuss how the masking model’s architecture affects results; authors should report experiments with different architectures.
- Clarify whether Table 1 results are reported for all classes or only the correct class.
- Specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta).
- Post-rebuttal: heavy reliance on classifier quality and scale invariance is a major limitation restricting applicability.
- Consequences: method may not work during model training, on models without invariance, or broadly across domains (contradicts black-box applicability claim).
- Concern that results may reflect the masking network’s behavior (or U-network) rather than true model saliency.
- If limitations are properly discussed, the paper is balanced enough for publication; otherwise it should be rejected.
