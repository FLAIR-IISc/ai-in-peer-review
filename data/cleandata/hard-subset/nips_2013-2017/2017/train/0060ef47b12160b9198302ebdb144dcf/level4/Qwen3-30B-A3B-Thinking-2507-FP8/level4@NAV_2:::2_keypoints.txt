- Introduces a model-based, single-forward-pass saliency detector applicable to any differentiable classifier, achieving ~100 FPS on standard GPUs.  
- Replaces iterative masking methods (hundreds of evaluations) with a fast predictive masking model.  
- Proposes a new metric s(a,p) = log(ã) - log(p) that evaluates the smallest rectangular crop preserving classification confidence, argued to better measure interpretability than localization accuracy.  
- Shows saliency quality comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes (Table 3) and strong weakly supervised localization performance on ImageNet.  
- Reports competitive localization numbers (e.g., 36.7% localization error on ImageNet) compared to baselines (VGG fully supervised 34.3% cited).  
- Mitigates adversarial artifacts using smoothness constraints (TV penalty) and randomized evidence removal (Eqs. 2–6).  
- Extensive experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 demonstrate technical soundness and no task-specific fine-tuning required.  
- Masking model generalizes across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50) and datasets.  
- Novelty: shifts from iterative to model-based saliency and advances artifact mitigation beyond prior black-box methods.  
- Significance: enables real-time applications (e.g., autonomous vehicles, video analysis) previously infeasible with iterative methods.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with clear problem formulation, precise technical contributions, and effective visualizations.  
- Weakness: could strengthen impact by detailing concrete deployment/latency-sensitive scenarios (e.g., autonomous driving).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of whether saliency masks reveal or mitigate classifier biases (e.g., background cues like snow vs. polar bear).  
- Overall recommendation: technically rigorous, well-motivated, addresses NIPS scope, recommended for acceptance with minor expansions on application contexts and bias analysis.
