- Proposes a neural-network approach to learn saliency masks that is fast (multiple images/sec).
- Reviewer finds the paper borderline: promising core idea and good contributions, no obvious technical errors.
- Major concern: saliency is only measured indirectly via two methods that have significant, under-discussed limitations.
- Weakly supervised localization is a problematic proxy for saliency because context important for classification may not correspond to precise object localization.
- The proposed crop-and-rescale saliency metric can introduce aspect-ratio distortion and biases toward smaller regions due to assumed scale invariance.
- Strong dependence on classifier scale/deformation invariance limits applicability and contradicts the claimed generality for "black-box" classifiers.
- The paper omits discussion/ablation on how the masking network architecture affects saliency maps, leaving ambiguity whether explanations reflect the classifier or masker biases.
- Recommendation: accept only if authors explicitly address these caveats; otherwise reject.
- Minor: clarify whether Table 1 results are over all classes or only correctly classified instances.
- Minor: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used in comparisons.
