- Introduces a trainable masking model to predict salient image regions for any differentiable classifier, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance to existing weakly supervised saliency methods on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.  
- Core novelty: a fast, model-agnostic saliency approach using a U-Net with ResNet encoder trained for both SSR and SDR objectives.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on “smallest sufficient region” (SSR) and “smallest destroying region” (SDR) for finer evaluation.  
- Method appears technically sound with a clear objective function and architecture; ablation studies support robustness.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Original contribution: combining a trainable masking model with SSR/SDR optimization to bridge speed-quality gap.  
- Significant impact potential: real-time saliency enables applications like video analysis and improves localization over prior weakly supervised methods.  
- Weak motivation/examples for real-time efficiency—concrete application scenarios are underdeveloped.  
- Limited analysis of what the learned saliency captures and potential classifier biases; deeper qualitative/diagnostic analysis needed.  
- Objective function weightings (1,2,3,4) appear arbitrary and lack justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective, strong experiments, clear exposition, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient real-time use-case motivation, limited interpretive analysis, and unclear objective weight choices.  
- Overall: strong paper with minor improvements suggested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
