This paper presents a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers. The authors propose a trainable masking model that can produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications. The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.

The main idea of the paper is to train a masking model to predict saliency maps for any input image in a single feed-forward pass. The model is designed to be model-agnostic, meaning it can be applied to any differentiable image classifier. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the CIFAR-10 and ImageNet datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results in weakly supervised object localization.

The paper also introduces a new metric for evaluating the quality of saliency maps, which is based on the concept of smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR). The authors show that their method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques in terms of this new metric.

The strengths of the paper include its novelty, clarity, and thorough evaluation of the proposed method. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods. The paper also discusses potential applications of the proposed method, such as video saliency detection and autonomous vehicles.

However, there are a few weaknesses in the paper. One potential limitation is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the learnt salient regions, which could provide valuable insights into the biases and strengths of the proposed method. Additionally, the authors could have motivated the need for real-time efficiency in certain applications more clearly.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a new approach to real-time image saliency detection. The authors also introduce a new metric for evaluating the quality of saliency maps, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it has the potential to impact various applications, such as video saliency detection, autonomous vehicles, and image classification. The proposed method is efficient, accurate, and can be applied to any differentiable image classifier, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
