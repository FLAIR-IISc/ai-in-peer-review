- Overall assessment: borderline paper with interesting ideas; reviewer neither strongly for nor against acceptance.  
- Positive: supports the concept of learning to explain models and notes some good ideas and no obvious mistakes.  
- Main limitation: saliency is only indirectly measured (via weakly supervised localization or the proposed saliency metric), both of which have clear shortcomings.  
- Weakly supervised localization caveat: may not correlate with true saliency when contextual information is crucial for class decisions.  
- Saliency metric limitation: cropping and rescaling the estimated salient region can change aspect ratio and introduce artifacts that impact classifier performance.  
- Saliency metric bias: the metric’s preference for small salient regions can lead to suboptimal scaling for re-classification.  
- Invariance concern: CNNs are translation-invariant but have limited built-in scale invariance, so metric effectiveness depends on classifier scale invariance.  
- Unaddressed dependence: the paper does not discuss how the masking model’s architecture affects learned masks or report experiments across architectures.  
- Minor clarification 1: specify whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the predicted/correct class.  
- Minor clarification 2: state which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta).  
- Post-rebuttal limitation: method’s effectiveness depends on classifier quality and scale invariance, greatly limiting applicability.  
- Applicability consequences: cannot be used during model training, is limited in other domains (e.g., spectrograms), and may not apply to black-box classifiers as claimed.  
- Attribution ambiguity: strong dependence on the masking network raises uncertainty about whether visualizations reflect the base (U-)network or the masking network.  
- Recommendation condition: acceptable for publication only if these limitations and dependencies are properly discussed; otherwise, it should be rejected.
