- Introduces a neural network that predicts image regions most important to another network for object categorization.  
- Paper is well-written with compelling experimental results showing significant improvement over prior work.  
- Evaluation uses challenging datasets and state-of-the-art networks (ImageNet, Places), demonstrating strong generalization.  
- Core idea of identifying task-relevant regions is strong and consistently outperforms existing approaches across benchmarks.  
- Ablation studies are thorough and effectively isolate contributions of different components.  
- Lacks deeper qualitative analysis explaining why the method works; needs visualizations of identified salient regions.  
- Recommend analysis of whether learned regions focus on object parts, contextual cues, or spurious/background correlations.  
- Motivation should be expanded to better articulate real-time efficiency scenarios where the approach is critical.  
- Suggest naming concrete applications (robotics, autonomous driving, mobile vision) and quantifying runtime/inference benefits on resource-constrained platforms.  
- Minor: include a brief glossary of symbols to aid readers unfamiliar with the terminology.  
- Minor: related work should more directly discuss recent advances in attention mechanisms and their application to image recognition.  
- Overall: promising, well-executed work; addressing qualitative analysis and application motivation would increase impact.
