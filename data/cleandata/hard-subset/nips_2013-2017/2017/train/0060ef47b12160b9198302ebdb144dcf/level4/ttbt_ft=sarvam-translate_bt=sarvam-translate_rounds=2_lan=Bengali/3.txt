This paper discusses a significant mask generation method capable of processing 100 images of size 224x224 per second on a standard GPU. Their method trains a masking model that finds the most tight rectangular crop containing the entire significant region of a specific class requested by a black box classifier such as AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet. Their model architecture requires image feature maps at various scales such as ResNet-50. The final scale feature will be passed through a feature filter that performs initial localization, while follow-up sampling blocks fine-tune the produced masks. Experiments show that their method performs better than other weakly supervised techniques for image net localization tasks.
