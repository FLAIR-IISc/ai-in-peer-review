- Proposes a novel, fast neural method to learn saliency masks for image classification, suitable for real-time use.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, claiming state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization.  
- Paper is well-motivated and the idea of learning to explain a model is compelling.  
- Saliency is only measured indirectly (weakly supervised localization or the proposed saliency metric), and these measures have limitations that need discussion.  
- Weakly supervised localization may not correlate with saliency quality because object context can be essential for class decisions.  
- The proposed saliency metric is flawed: cropping and rescaling can introduce artifacts and change aspect ratio, affecting classifier recognition.  
- The method relies on classifier scale-invariance; this assumption’s limitations must be discussed.  
- Need to analyze dependence of the masking model on architecture: did different architectures affect results and robustness?  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the correct class.  
- Minor clarification requested: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparison.  
- Post-rebuttal concern: the saliency metric’s dependence on classifier quality and scale-invariance limits applicability to scale-invariant networks only.  
- Limitation: method cannot be applied to models during training, to non-scale-invariant models, or reliably to other domains (e.g., spectrogram analysis).  
- Claim of applicability to black-box classifiers is challenged; the method is not generally usable for black-box models.  
- Strong dependence on the masking network raises uncertainty about whether the visualization reflects the classifier’s saliency or the masking network/U-network; this should be clearly discussed.
