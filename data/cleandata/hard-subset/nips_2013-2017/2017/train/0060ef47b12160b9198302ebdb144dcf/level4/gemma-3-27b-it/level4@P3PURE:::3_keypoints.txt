- Paper presents a fast image saliency mask generator claiming ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing a class-specific salient region, guided by black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet).  
- Uses multi-scale feature maps from ResNet-50; highest-resolution map goes through a feature filter for initial localization with upsampling blocks refining masks.  
- Shows better performance than other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- References and related-work discussion are adequate; presentation is clear and well organized.  
- Technical approach appears sound and the authors provide insightful intuitions and justifications for design choices.  
- Reviewer questions the real-time speed claim and whether the 100 imgs/sec includes image resizing.  
- Reviewer asks for runtime on larger, real-world images (e.g., 640x480 from an iPhone 6s).  
- Concern about generalizability: method may struggle when the requested class object is small in the image; asks if 224x224 inputs suffice.  
- Requests corresponding metrics in Table 3 for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]).  
- Minor typographical corrections suggested: "More over" -> "Moreover" (Line 273) and "optimise" -> "optimizes" (Line 151).
