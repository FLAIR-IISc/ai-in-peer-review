- Introduces a U-Net-based masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time saliency detection.  
- Uses ResNet-50 encoder and multi-scale features to produce sharp, artifact-free masks without iterative optimization.  
- Trains masks to satisfy Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) and Smallest Destroying Region (SDR) criteria with a well-justified objective (Eq. 4).  
- Proposes a new saliency metric s(a,p) = log(ã) - log(p) measuring minimal crop size that preserves classification confidence.  
- Achieves 36.7% object localization error on ImageNet, outperforming weakly supervised baselines and approaching supervised VGG (34.3%).  
- Generalizes to CIFAR-10 without pretraining, showing robustness across datasets.  
- Strength: technically sound and innovative with clear theoretical grounding and effective training strategy.  
- Strength: Single-pass inference at 100+ fps (224x224) offers major speed advantage enabling real-time applications like video saliency.  
- Strength: New metric effectively captures interpretability and is validated by near-ground-truth performance; paper is clear and well-organized with reproducible code and ablations.  
- Strength: Thorough related-work discussion and first to train a model for saliency rather than rely on iterative optimization.  
- Weakness: Real-time speed claim limited to 224x224 images; no clarity on performance or overhead for larger resolutions (e.g., 640x480).  
- Weakness: Unclear effectiveness on small salient objects occupying <10% of the image; small-object performance not evaluated.  
- Weakness: Missing s(a,p) metric comparisons for key baselines (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi [2], "Feed"), making contextualization incomplete.  
- Recommendation: Accept — high-quality, impactful contribution with minor addressable gaps.  
- Minor corrections: fix "More over" → "Moreover" (LN 273) and "optimise" → "optimizes" (LN 151).
