- Proposes a novel neural-network method for learning saliency masks.
- Method processes multiple images per second, suitable for real-time applications.
- The idea of learning to explain a model is compelling and contains innovative elements.
- Overall verdict is borderline; reviewer would not object to either acceptance or rejection.
- No obvious mistakes but there are clear limitations that must be addressed.
- Saliency is only indirectly measured via weakly supervised localisation or the proposed saliency metric.
- Weakly supervised localisation may not correlate with saliency quality when object context determines class.
- The proposed saliency metric can introduce artefacts by cropping and rescaling, and may prefer small regions leading to suboptimal scaling.
- CNNs’ lack of scale invariance limits the metric’s applicability and may bias results.
- The paper does not discuss how mask learning depends on the masking-network architecture; experiments with different architectures are needed.
- Minor issues: specify which LRP variant/parameters were used and clarify whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the correct class.
- Rebuttal admits the metric depends on classifier quality and scale invariance, limiting use (e.g., not usable during training, not applicable to non-scale-invariant domains like spectrograms).
- Claim of general applicability to black-box classifiers is overstated given the method’s limitations.
- Rebuttal hints at strong dependence on the masking network, raising the concern that the method may visualize the masking network rather than the target model; this must be discussed or the paper should not be published.
