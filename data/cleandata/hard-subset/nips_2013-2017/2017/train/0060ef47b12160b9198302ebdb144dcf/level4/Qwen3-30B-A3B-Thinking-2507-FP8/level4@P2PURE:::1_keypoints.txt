- Proposes a neural-network approach for learning saliency masks with real-time performance; reviewer finds the idea promising but rates the paper borderline.  
- Strong support for learning-to-explain concept, but significant limitations need careful discussion.  
- Evaluation methods (weakly supervised localization and the proposed saliency metric) have clear limitations that are not adequately addressed.  
- Weakly supervised localization is an imperfect metric: object context can be essential for classification, so localization may not reflect true saliency quality.  
- The proposed saliency metric is flawed because cropping and rescaling the estimated region (while preserving aspect ratio) introduces artifacts that can hurt classifier performance.  
- The metric biases toward small salient regions which are heavily upscaled, exposing classifier scale-invariance limits and confounding the measure.  
- The paper omits analysis of how dependent the masking model is on the underlying classifier architecture; no experiments reported on varying architectures.  
- Minor clarification needed: were Table 1 results computed across all classes or only the correct class?  
- Minor clarification needed: specify which LRP variant (e.g., epsilon, alpha-beta) and parameter settings were used.  
- Post-rebuttal concern: the saliency metric’s dependence on classifier quality and scale invariance fundamentally limits applicability.  
- Consequence: method cannot be applied during model training or to classifiers lacking scale invariance, cannot generalize to other domains (e.g., spectrograms), and does not support black-box classifiers as claimed.  
- Ambiguity remains about whether the method visualizes the target classifier’s saliency or artifacts of the masking/U-network architecture.  
- Recommendation: accept only if these limitations are thoroughly discussed; otherwise, reject.
