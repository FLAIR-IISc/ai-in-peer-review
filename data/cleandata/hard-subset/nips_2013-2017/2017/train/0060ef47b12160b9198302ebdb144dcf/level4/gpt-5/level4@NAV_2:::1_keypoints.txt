- Paper proposes a learned, model-agnostic neural masking approach to produce saliency masks for black-box classifiers, running in real time.  
- Reviewer views the paper as borderline—supportive of the idea but notes clear limitations and no strong objection either way.  
- No obvious technical errors, but important shortcomings need addressing.  
- Saliency is assessed only indirectly; both localization results and the proposed metric have significant limitations.  
- Weakly supervised localization is an imperfect proxy for saliency, since contextual cues can decouple localization accuracy from explanation quality.  
- The proposed saliency metric is problematic because it crops and resizes estimated salient regions, which can change aspect ratio and favor small regions.  
- Cropping/resizing can alter classification confidence and present objects at unnatural scales, exploiting limited scale invariance in CNNs.  
- The paper does not discuss dependence on the masking model’s architecture or whether alternative architectures were explored.  
- Minor: clarify whether Table 1 results are for the ground-truth class only or across all classes.  
- Minor: specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparison (e.g., epsilon, alpha–beta).  
- Post-rebuttal concern: metric effectiveness depends heavily on the classifier’s robustness to scale and aspect-ratio variations, limiting applicability.  
- Implication: method is unsuitable for models during training or models lacking learned invariance.  
- Implication: applicability to other domains (e.g., spectrograms) is constrained.  
- Claim of general applicability to black-box classifiers is overstated given these dependencies.  
- Strong dependence on the masking network raises concern that visualizations may reflect masking-model biases rather than the black-box classifier’s saliency.  
- If these dependencies and limitations are thoroughly acknowledged and discussed, the paper could be balanced enough for publication; otherwise the reviewer would not recommend acceptance.
