- Proposes an image saliency mask generator that processes 100 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to produce the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a requested class using a black-box classifier (AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet).  
- Architecture uses multi-scale CNN feature maps with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks for mask refinement.  
- Empirically outperforms other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.  
- Provides sufficient references and related work; presentation is clear and well-structured.  
- Technical correctness appears sound and design choices are intuitively justified.  
- Reviewer doubts the real-time speed claim and overall applicability to real-world scenarios.  
- Requests clarification whether the 100 images/sec includes image resizing and asks for runtime on larger images (e.g., 640×480).  
- Questions how the method handles small objects in images and whether 224×224 input resolution is sufficient for such cases.
