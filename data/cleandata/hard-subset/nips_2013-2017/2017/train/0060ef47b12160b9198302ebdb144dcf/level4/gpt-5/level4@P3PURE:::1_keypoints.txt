- Strength: Clear motivation for learning saliency masks and a fast implementation capable of processing multiple images per second.
- Strength: No obvious methodological errors identified.
- Strength: Interesting and promising results on weakly supervised localization.

- Concern: Saliency is evaluated only indirectly (weakly supervised localization or the proposed metric), and both proxies have significant limitations that need discussion.
- Concern: Weakly supervised localization can be misleading because object localization need not correlate with true saliency when contextual cues drive classification.
- Concern: The proposed saliency metric may introduce artifacts due to cropping and rescaling, causing aspect-ratio changes and implicit preference for small regions that are heavily upscaled.
- Concern: Scale invariance limitations of convnets mean upscaling small regions can produce suboptimal classifier behavior, biasing the metric.
- Concern: Architectural dependence is not addressed—unclear how sensitive the masking model is to encoder/decoder or classifier architectures and how this affects masks and performance.

- Minor: Clarify whether Table 1 results are computed over all classes or only the ground-truth class.
- Minor: Specify the exact LRP variant and parameter settings used (e.g., epsilon, alpha–beta).

- Post-rebuttal: The saliency metric’s effectiveness strongly depends on classifier quality and scale invariance, substantially limiting applicability (not usable during training, problematic for models/domains lacking scale invariance).
- Post-rebuttal: The method may not be generally applicable to black-box classifiers as claimed, and applicability to other domains (e.g., spectrograms) is restricted.
- Post-rebuttal: Strong dependence on the masking network makes it unclear whether explanations reflect the underlying classifier or the masking network itself.

- Recommendation: Borderline—acceptable only if the paper explicitly and thoroughly discusses limitations of indirect evaluations, dependence on classifier scale invariance, and architectural sensitivity with ablation evidence; otherwise do not recommend publication.
