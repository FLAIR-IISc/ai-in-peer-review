- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency method: train a U-Net (ResNet-50 encoder) to predict class-conditional masks in one forward pass.  
- Training objective combines total variation, area penalty, SSR-like preservation of target confidence, and SDR-like suppression of the removed region.  
- Uses randomized alternative image contents (blur/noise/color) for removed regions to reduce masking artifacts.  
- Introduces a new metric: crop the tightest rect enclosing the salient region, resize, and score s(a,p)=log(max(a,0.05))−log(p).  
- Experiments on ImageNet/CIFAR-10 (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) show sharp masks, competitive weakly supervised localization, and real-time throughput.  
- Extends prior gradient/backprop and optimization-based perturbation saliency by training a feed-forward predictor and combining SSR/SDR with artifact mitigation.  
- Strength: well-motivated, carefully engineered objective addressing SSR, SDR, and adversarial artifacts.  
- Strength: practical single-pass method with throughput suitable for real-time applications.  
- Strength: clear localization improvements and informative experiments across multiple architectures.  
- Strength: proposed metric emphasizes interpretability beyond overlap-based localization.  
- Strength: sensible training tricks (fake labels, randomized alternative A) and multi-scale architecture design.  
- Weakness: localization is an imperfect proxy for saliency—contextual cues may not align with bounding boxes.  
- Weakness: proposed metric has confounds—aspect-ratio distortion and favoring very small crops that change classifier probabilities.  
- Weakness: “black-box”/general applicability claim is too strong because method is trained per classifier and depends on classifier invariances.  
- Weakness: unclear applicability to other domains or during model training when invariances aren’t established.  
- Weakness: learned masks may reflect masking-network inductive biases; lacking ablations across architectures and sanity checks (randomized classifier, cross-model transfer).  
- Weakness: experimental clarity issues—Table 1 label protocol, precise baselines/hyperparameters, sensitivity to alternative-image choices and objective weights, and missing failure cases.  
- Recommendation: add limitations section discussing metric dependence, scope of black-box claims, and masking-network bias; temper generality claims.  
- Suggested improvements: metric variants preserving aspect ratio, ablations on masking architectures, robustness checks (rescaling, A sensitivity, classifier randomization), and clearer baseline details.  
- Pros for acceptance: fast, high-quality saliency maps; novel SSR+SDR objective with artifact mitigation; competitive localization and a thoughtful metric.  
- Cons for acceptance: evaluation confounds (scale/aspect-ratio), overstated generality, and limited ablations disentangling explainer vs. target.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline—support if authors address metric limitations, temper generality, and add architectural ablations and evaluation clarifications; otherwise lean reject.
