- Proposes a novel masking network that generates saliency maps for any differentiable black-box classifier in a single forward pass, avoiding per-image iterative optimization.  
- Training objective enforces small, smooth masks that preserve classifier confidence on the salient region (Smallest Sufficient Region) and reduce confidence elsewhere (Smallest Destroying Region).  
- Introduces a new saliency evaluation metric based on information concentration within a cropped region.  
- Validated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, producing sharp, interpretable maps and achieving state-of-the-art weakly-supervised object localization on ImageNet.  
- Amortization to a single-pass model enables real-time performance and potential new applications like video saliency analysis.  
- Technically sound: well-motivated objective, reasonable architecture choices (U-Net with ResNet backbone), and extensive experiments on multiple classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).  
- New metric is a thoughtful addition to better assess saliency explanation quality.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and situates itself effectively within related work.  
- Motivation for real-time saliency is stated but not strongly justified with concrete application examples.  
- Lacks deeper qualitative analysis of what the saliency maps reveal about classifier behavior or biases (e.g., reliance on background context).  
- Could be strengthened by case studies showing classes where classifiers use background cues rather than the object.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective, model-agnostic, real-time advantage, SOTA localization results, new metric, thorough large-scale evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently compelling practical motivation for real-time use and missing deeper analysis of learned saliency maps.
