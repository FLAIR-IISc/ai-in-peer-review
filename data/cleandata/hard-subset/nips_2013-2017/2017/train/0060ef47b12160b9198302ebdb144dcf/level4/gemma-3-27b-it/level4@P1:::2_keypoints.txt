- Introduces a trainable, model-agnostic neural network that predicts saliency regions for another classifier’s decisions in a single forward pass.  
- Uses a U-Net with a ResNet-50 encoder to produce saliency masks, trading off between speed and accuracy.  
- Proposes a novel saliency metric based on “smallest sufficient region” (SSR) and “smallest destroying region” (SDR).  
- Demonstrates significant speed advantages over iterative saliency methods while maintaining strong explanation quality.  
- Shows competitive, state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet and strong weakly supervised localization results.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach and experimental setup.  
- Single-pass design enables real-time saliency detection and potential dynamic applications (e.g., video understanding).  
- Reviewer asks for clearer motivation and concrete real-time application examples to justify the emphasis on speed.  
- Requests deeper analysis of learned masks to reveal whether classifiers exploit background or spurious correlations.  
- Notes the objective function (Eq. 4) is complex with many terms/hyperparameters and recommends a thorough ablation study.  
- Finds the discussion on evidence removal dense and suggests more illustrative examples to clarify points.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective, fast method; strong experimental results; useful new metric; enables new applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation for real-time focus, lack of in-depth mask analysis, and unresolved objective-function complexity.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to explainable AI with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
