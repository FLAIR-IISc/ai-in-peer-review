- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator (~100 224×224 images/sec on a standard GPU) that finds the tightest rectangular crop containing a requested-class salient region, conditioned on a black-box classifier.
- Architecture uses multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50) with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks to refine masks.
- Trains a masking model to produce class-conditioned tight crops and reports superior performance vs. other weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization.
- Paper appears to have sufficient references, is plausibly technically correct, and provides useful design intuitions with good presentation.
- Major concern about the real-time speed claim and its applicability to real-world images.
- Asks whether the reported ~100 images/sec includes image resizing and requests runtime numbers for larger inputs (e.g., 640×480 from an iPhone 6s).
- Questions behavior when the requested-class object is small—whether a 224×224 input is sufficient for small objects.
- Requests clarification on Table 3: are there corresponding metrics for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2])?
