- Proposes a fast saliency method: a masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for any differentiable image classifier.  
- Uses features from a pre-trained ResNet-50 and trains with a novel loss combining smoothness, SSR, and SDR principles.  
- Demonstrates competitive ImageNet localization results and introduces a new saliency metric, outperforming other weakly-supervised methods.  
- Addresses speed limitations of iterative methods, claiming real-time performance (~100 images/second).  
- New information-theory/SSR-based saliency metric may provide a more nuanced evaluation than simple localization.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Real-time claim needs clarification regarding image resizing and performance on higher-resolution images (e.g., 640×480).  
- Unclear how the method handles small objects; 224×224 input resolution may limit small-instance performance.  
- Lacks direct comparison of the new saliency metric with existing metrics and does not report the metric for competing methods.  
- Ablation study is insufficient; the impact of each loss term (TV, SSR, SDR) should be quantified.  
- Needs deeper analysis of potential biases introduced by the masking model itself.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, competitive benchmark results, new metric, and real-time applicability.  
- Arguments for rejection: unclear runtime validation across resolutions, small-object and bias concerns, and weak metric comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept contingent on minor revisions to clarify runtime and expand evaluation.  
- Minor editorial fixes: LN 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; LN 151 "optimise" → "optimizes".
