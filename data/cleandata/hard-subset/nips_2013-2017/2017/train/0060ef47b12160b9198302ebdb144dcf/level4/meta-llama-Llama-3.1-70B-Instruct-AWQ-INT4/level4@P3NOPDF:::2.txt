This paper proposes a neural network (NN) approach to predict the regions of an image that are crucial for another NN to perform object categorization. The manuscript is well-written and engaging, and the experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to previous works. Notably, the results are achieved on challenging datasets using state-of-the-art NNs.

One potential suggestion for improvement is to provide a clearer motivation for the need for real-time efficiency in specific applications. While the paper highlights the importance of computational efficiency, it would be beneficial to elaborate on the practical implications of this requirement, such as in real-world object recognition systems or robotics applications.

Additionally, it would be insightful to include an analysis of the learnt salient regions, providing a deeper understanding of how the proposed method operates. For instance, exploring which object categories tend to exploit biases in the background to recognize the object could offer valuable insights into the strengths and limitations of the approach. This analysis could also shed light on potential avenues for future improvement.

Overall, the paper presents a compelling contribution to the field, and with some minor revisions to address these suggestions, it has the potential to be even more impactful.
