- Paper proposes a fast image saliency mask generator that processes 100 images/s on a standard GPU and finds the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a target class.  
- Model trains a masking network using a black-box classifier (e.g., AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet) to define saliency for a specified class.  
- Architecture leverages multi-scale image feature maps (e.g., from ResNet-50), with a feature filter at the final scale and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- References and related work coverage are sufficient.  
- Technical approach appears sound with intuitive design discussions and clear presentation.  
- Reviewer is concerned about the real-time speed claim and applicability to real-world images.  
- Question: Does the 100 images/s include image resizing, and what is the runtime for larger images (e.g., 640×480)?  
- Question: How does the method handle small objects in images, and is 224×224 resolution sufficient for such cases?  
- Question: In Table 3, are there corresponding metrics for other works cited in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2])?
