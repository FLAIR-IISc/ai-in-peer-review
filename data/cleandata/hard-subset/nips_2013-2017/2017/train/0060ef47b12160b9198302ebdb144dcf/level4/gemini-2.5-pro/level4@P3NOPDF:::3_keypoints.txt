- Proposes an efficient saliency mask generator capable of 100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Core contribution: masking model that predicts the tightest rectangular crop for a target class using weak supervision from a pre-trained black-box classifier.  
- Architecture: uses multi-scale features (e.g., ResNet-50) with a coarse feature filter and progressive upsampling blocks to produce high-resolution masks.  
- Empirical result: outperforms other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization benchmark.  
- Strength: novel and effective weakly supervised localization with superior performance.  
- Strength: high efficiency that could enable real-time applications.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and provides clear intuition and architectural discussion.  
- Strength: technically sound and well-situated within related work with sufficient references.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the 100 imgs/sec timing includes preprocessing (resizing to 224x224) and no reported performance on higher-resolution images (e.g., 640x480).  
- Weakness: potential limitation for small or off-center objects due to fixed 224x224 input—needs analysis/examples on small-object cases.  
- Weakness: ablation study (Table 3) should include key baselines from Table 2 (e.g., Feed-forward [2]) under the same metrics for direct comparison.
