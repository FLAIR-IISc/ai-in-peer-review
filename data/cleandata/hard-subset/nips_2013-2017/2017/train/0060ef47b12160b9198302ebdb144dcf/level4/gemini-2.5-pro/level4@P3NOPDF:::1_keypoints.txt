- Introduces a fast method using a separate network to generate saliency masks for image classifiers.  
- Overall assessment: promising idea but borderline due to important limitations in evaluation and generality.  

Major concerns:
- Indirect and potentially flawed evaluation: the saliency assessment methods have significant caveats that are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Weakly supervised localization is a questionable proxy for saliency because classifier-relevant regions may extend beyond object bounding boxes.  
- The proposed crop-and-rescale metric can distort aspect ratios and biases results toward small regions, confounding evaluation via classifier scale sensitivity.  
- Dependence on classifier scale/aspect-ratio invariance undermines the method’s claimed black-box generality.  
- Method may be unsuitable during training, for models lacking scale invariance, or for other domains (e.g., spectrograms).  
- Ambiguity over what is visualized: lack of ablations on the masking network leaves unclear whether masks reflect classifier saliency or the masker’s inductive biases.  

Minor comments:
- Clarify whether Table 1 metrics are averaged over all classes or computed for the ground-truth class.  
- Specify which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons.  

Recommendation:
- Claims of generality are unsupported without thorough discussion of evaluation caveats and conditions (e.g., requirement of scale-invariant classifiers); paper not ready for publication unless these limitations are addressed.
