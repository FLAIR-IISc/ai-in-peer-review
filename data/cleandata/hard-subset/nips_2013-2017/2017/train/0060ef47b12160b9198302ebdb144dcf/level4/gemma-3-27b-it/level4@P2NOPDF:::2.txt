This paper presents a neural network approach to predict salient image regions – those most influential for object categorization in another neural network. The paper is well-written and the experimental results are compelling, demonstrating performance superior to existing methods. The evaluation is performed on challenging datasets utilizing state-of-the-art object recognition networks, further strengthening the findings.

While the core idea and execution are strong, the motivation for prioritizing real-time efficiency could be more thoroughly explained. Expanding on the specific application areas where such speed is critical would enhance the paper’s impact. 

Furthermore, a deeper analysis of the learned salient regions would be valuable. For instance, investigating whether the model identifies and exploits biases within the background to aid object recognition – and if so, for which object categories – could provide significant insight into the network’s behavior and the underlying categorization process. Understanding *how* the model determines importance, beyond simply *that* it does so effectively, would be a beneficial addition.
