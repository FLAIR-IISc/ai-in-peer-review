- Proposes a fast class-specific saliency mask model achieving ~100 fps for 224x224 images on standard GPUs.  
- Trains a masking model using a differentiable “black box” classifier to highlight regions the classifier relies on.  
- Architecture uses multi-scale ResNet-50–inspired features with an initial localisation step and upsampling refinement blocks.  
- Reports superior weakly supervised object localisation performance on ImageNet.  
- Well-situated in literature, addressing limitations of gradient-based and iterative saliency methods.  

Strengths:
- Real-time speed potentially enables video analysis and interactive applications.  
- Model-agnostic: compatible with any differentiable classifier.  
- Novel objective combining smoothness, small-region preservation, and classification probability.  
- Introduces a “smallest sufficient region” (SSR) saliency metric for more nuanced evaluation.  
- Strong experimental results on a challenging benchmark (ImageNet).  

Weaknesses:
- 100 fps claim needs clarification (e.g., does it include resizing, and how does it scale to higher resolutions?).  
- Unclear performance on small objects; 224x224 input may limit detection of tiny instances.  
- New saliency metric lacks comparable values for baseline methods in Table 3.  
- Discussion of adversarial artifacts is insufficient; robustness analysis is needed.  
- Limited generalisation testing; CIFAR-10 experiment uses a different training paradigm and broader evaluation is required.  

Quality and clarity:
- Technically sound with a reasonably rigorous experimental setup; full implementation verification not performed.  
- Generally well-written and organised, with only minor grammatical errors.  

Originality and significance:
- Novel combination of a trainable masking model and objective for fast, model-agnostic saliency detection.  
- Addresses an important interpretability problem and offers practical, real-time potential.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Solves a significant interpretability problem with a novel, effective, and fast approach; strong benchmarks and a new metric; clear presentation.  

Arguments for rejection:
- Performance claims, scale sensitivity, adversarial robustness, and metric comparisons require further investigation.  

Overall assessment:
- Recommend accept conditional on addressing concerns about real-time performance claims, object scale sensitivity, and metric comparisons.
