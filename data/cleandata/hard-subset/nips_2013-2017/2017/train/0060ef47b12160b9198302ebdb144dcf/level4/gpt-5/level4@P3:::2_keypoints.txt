- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic feed-forward masking network that predicts class-conditional saliency masks in one pass, trained to satisfy SSR (retain) and SDR (remove) objectives with smooth, compact masks.  
- Uses a U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder and learned class gating, runs >100 images/s at 224×224, and is evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Introduces a crop-based saliency metric that trades off retained area vs class probability and reports strong weakly supervised localization results, competitive with some fully supervised methods.  
- Positions method relative to gradient-based saliency, CAM-style localization, and iterative perturbation approaches; key novelty is learning a saliency predictor across images/classes to avoid per-image optimization.  
- Clear problem formulation explicitly targeting SSR/SDR and addressing “introduced evidence” through randomized alternative images and TV regularization.  
- Practical and fast inference enabling real-time and potential video applications.  
- Strong empirical performance: improved ImageNet weakly supervised localization and high-quality interpretable masks under the new metric.  
- Sensible architecture and training tricks (U-Net skip connections, class gating, fake labels, randomized A) to enforce specificity and robustness.  
- Demonstrates generality across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and scales to CIFAR-10, including training the masker from scratch.  
- Notational ambiguities in the metric and loss (e.g., s(a,p)=log(ã) log(p)? λ4 vs fc(·)^4) need clarification.  
- “Black-box” claim is misleading: training requires backprop through the classifier, so clarify required access to gradients vs scores and applicability when gradients are unavailable.  
- The crop-based metric may be sensitive to resizing/aspect-ratio and classifier invariances; validate against deletion/insertion-style metrics and standard baselines.  
- Missing ablations on loss coefficients (λ1–λ4), TV strength, SSR vs SDR contributions, alternative image A choices, mask resolution, and threshold sensitivity.  
- Limited systematic bias analysis: paper notes background biases but lacks class-wise quantification or correlation analyses.  
- Comparisons omit important baselines (CAM/Grad-CAM, Excitation Backprop) that should be included for localization and the proposed metric.  
- Question of generalization: masking model is trained per classifier—discuss whether a single masker can generalize across architectures or unseen models.  
- Technically sound and well designed, but would benefit from clearer equations, more exhaustive ablations, and broader baseline comparisons.  
- Overall writing is clear, but fix notation, add implementation/training details (optimizer, LR schedule, batch size, GPU) and report failure cases for reproducibility.  
- Novelty lies in learning a feed-forward saliency predictor optimized for SSR/SDR across a dataset rather than per-image optimization.  
- High significance: makes perturbation-based saliency practical at scale and could be a reference method for real-time/video saliency with fuller validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: large speedup, solid architecture/training innovations, and strong localization/saliency results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear equations, limited ablations, incomplete baseline comparisons, and insufficient failure/bias analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify metric/loss notation, add sensitivity/ablation studies, include CAM/Grad-CAM/Excitation Backprop baselines, present failure/bias analyses, provide training/hardware details, and consider releasing code/models and video demos.
