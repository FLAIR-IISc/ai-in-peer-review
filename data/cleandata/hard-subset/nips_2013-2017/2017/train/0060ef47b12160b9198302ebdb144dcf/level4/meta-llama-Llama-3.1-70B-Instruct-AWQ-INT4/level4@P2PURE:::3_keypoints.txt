- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator reportedly processing 100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.
- Trains a masking model to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a specified class using a black-box classifier (AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet).
- Architecture uses multi-scale image feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50); final scale goes through a feature filter for localization and upsampling blocks refine masks.
- Experimental results claim to outperform other weakly supervised methods on the ImageNet localization task.
- References and related work appear sufficient (not exhaustively checked).
- Technical correctness seems sound though not comprehensively verified.
- Authors provide insightful design discussions and intuitions.
- Paper presentation is well-organized and clear.
- Concern about the real-time speed claim and its applicability to real-world images.
- Question whether the 100 images/sec timing includes image resizing and what runtime is for larger images (e.g., 640x480 from an iPhone 6s).
- Concern about performance on small objects and whether 224x224 input is sufficient for small-class instances.
- Request to include corresponding metrics in Table 3 for works listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]).
