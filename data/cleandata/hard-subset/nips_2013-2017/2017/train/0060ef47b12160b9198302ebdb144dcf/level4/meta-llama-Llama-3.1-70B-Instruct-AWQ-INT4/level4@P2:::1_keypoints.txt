- Proposes a trainable masking model that produces high-quality saliency masks in a single forward pass without needing the classifier at test time.  
- Achieves fast processing speeds (multiple images per second) suitable for practical use.  
- Training objective encourages smoothness, small mask size, and preservation of classifier accuracy.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric to evaluate mask quality.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on ImageNet for weakly supervised object localization.  
- Outperforms other saliency detection techniques and is comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes for interpretability.  
- Saliency metric can introduce artifacts via cropping/resizing, affecting classification accuracy and evaluation.  
- Metric limitations are problematic for classifiers that are not scale-invariant.  
- Dependence of results on the masking network architecture is not analyzed or reported.  
- Unclear whether the approach generalizes to black-box classifiers due to architectural and scale assumptions.  
- Missing experimental details: which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons.  
- Unclear reporting in Table 1—whether results are aggregated over all classes or only the correct class.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations in rebuttal, but these remain consequential for applicability.  
- Recommendation: paper should explicitly discuss metric and architecture limitations and provide analysis of architecture choices to be publication-ready.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel learnable saliency approach, fast and effective, plus a new evaluation metric.  
- Rejection arguments: imperfect metric with artifacts, unclear architectural dependence, insufficient detail on methodology, and limited applicability to black-box/non-scale-invariant models.
