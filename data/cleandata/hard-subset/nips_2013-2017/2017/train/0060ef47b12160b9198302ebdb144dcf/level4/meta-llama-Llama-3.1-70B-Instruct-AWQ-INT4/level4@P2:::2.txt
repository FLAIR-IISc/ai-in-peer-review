This paper proposes a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers. The authors develop a masking model that can be applied to any differentiable image classifier, which learns to predict saliency maps in a single forward pass. The model is trained to minimize an objective function that encourages smoothness, small region size, and accurate classification.

The paper is well-written, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on challenging datasets, including CIFAR-10 and ImageNet. The results show that the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques at the ImageNet localisation task and achieves a better saliency metric than other popular saliency detectors.

The proposed approach has the potential to be used in real-time applications, such as video saliency detection, and could provide valuable insights into the decision-making process of black box classifiers. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the learnt salient regions, such as which object categories exploit biases in the background to recognize the object.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of clear motivation for the need for real-time efficiency in saliency detection. The authors could provide more context on the applications where real-time efficiency is crucial and how their approach addresses this need.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of saliency detection and has the potential to be a valuable tool for understanding and interpreting the decisions of black box classifiers.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to real-time image saliency detection
* Effective on challenging datasets, including CIFAR-10 and ImageNet
* Outperforms other weakly supervised techniques at the ImageNet localisation task
* Achieves a better saliency metric than other popular saliency detectors

Weaknesses:

* Lack of clear motivation for the need for real-time efficiency
* Limited analysis of the learnt salient regions
* Potential biases in the masking model

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide more context on the applications where real-time efficiency is crucial
* Conduct a more detailed analysis of the learnt salient regions
* Investigate potential biases in the masking model
