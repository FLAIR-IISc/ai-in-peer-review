This paper proposes a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers. The authors introduce a trainable masking model that can produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications. The paper is well-written and presents an interesting solution to the problem of image saliency detection.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the current state of the art in image saliency detection, including the limitations of existing methods. They also propose a new metric for evaluating the quality of saliency maps, which is based on the concept of smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR). This metric is used to evaluate the performance of their proposed method.

The experimental results presented in the paper are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on challenging datasets such as CIFAR-10 and ImageNet. The authors also provide a comparison with other weakly supervised methods and existing saliency detection techniques, showing that their method outperforms these approaches in terms of localisation accuracy and saliency metric.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers.
* The authors provide a thorough overview of the current state of the art in image saliency detection and propose a new metric for evaluating the quality of saliency maps.
* The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on challenging datasets.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the learnt salient regions, such as which object categories exploit biases in the background to recognize the object.
* The authors could provide more insight into the applications where real-time efficiency is necessary and how their method can be used in these scenarios.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide more details on the applications where real-time efficiency is necessary and how the proposed method can be used in these scenarios.
* Include an analysis of the learnt salient regions, such as which object categories exploit biases in the background to recognize the object.
* Consider providing more visualizations of the saliency maps produced by the proposed method to help illustrate its effectiveness.

Overall, this paper presents a well-written and interesting solution to the problem of image saliency detection. With some additional analysis and insight, it has the potential to be a strong contribution to the field.
