- Introduces a novel, fast saliency method that trains a masking model to predict saliency maps in a single forward pass instead of iterative optimization.  
- Method is model-agnostic and designed to find the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a requested class.  
- Objective function (Eq. 4) is well-designed, balancing mask smoothness, region size, and classifier accuracy on preserved/removed regions.  
- Experiments on ImageNet show strong performance (ResNet-50 masking model localization error 36.7% vs VGG 34.3%) and outperform other weakly supervised methods.  
- New saliency metric (Eq. 3) is well-motivated and indicates masks are interpretable comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- U-Net masking-model architecture is clearly described and appropriate for the task.  
- Claim of "more than a hundred 224x224 images per second" needs clarification (does this include resizing?) and timing for larger images (e.g., 640x480) is requested.  
- Limited analysis and discussion on handling small objects; 224x224 resolution may be insufficient for small-object localization.  
- Incomplete comparison: Table 3 should include corresponding metrics for methods reported in Table 2 (e.g., [2]).  
- Originality: novel in training a single-pass predictor (distinct from per-image optimization approaches like Fong & Vedaldi), and introduces a new metric.  
- Significance: valuable for real-time applications (e.g., video saliency, autonomous vehicles) and shows meaningful improvements over weakly supervised baselines.  
- Strengths summarized: novel fast approach, strong ImageNet/CIFAR-10 results, solid metric, clear model description, applicability to any differentiable classifier.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unclear speed details for non-standard resolutions, limited treatment of small objects, missing comparative metrics in Table 3, and potential resolution limitations for small objects.  
- Minor edits requested: fix typos at LN 273 ("More over"→"Moreover") and LN 151 ("optimise"→"optimises").
