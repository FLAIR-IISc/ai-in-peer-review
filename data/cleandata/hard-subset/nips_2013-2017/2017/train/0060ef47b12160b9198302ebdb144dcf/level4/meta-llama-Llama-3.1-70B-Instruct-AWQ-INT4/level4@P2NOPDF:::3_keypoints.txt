- Proposes an efficient image saliency mask generator that processes ~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU.  
- Trains a masking model to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing the class-specific salient region using a black-box classifier (AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet).  
- Model uses multi-scale image feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50); final scale goes through a feature filter for localization and upsampling blocks refine masks.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- References and related work coverage appear sufficient given review time constraints.  
- Technical correctness seems satisfactory, though full verification was not possible within review time.  
- Presentation is well-organized and includes insightful design discussions and intuitions.  
- Concern: unclear whether the 100 images/sec speed includes image resizing; real-time claim needs clarification.  
- Concern: unknown runtime and performance on larger images (e.g., 640x480 from an iPhone 6s).  
- Concern: method’s effectiveness on small objects is uncertain—224x224 crops may be insufficient for small-class instances.
