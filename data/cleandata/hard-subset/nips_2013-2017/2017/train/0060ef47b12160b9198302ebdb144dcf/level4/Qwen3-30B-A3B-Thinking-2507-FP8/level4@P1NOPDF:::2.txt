

This paper proposes a neural network model for predicting salient image regions that are critical for object categorization by another neural network. The work is well-motivated and clearly presented, with strong empirical results demonstrating superior performance over prior methods on challenging benchmark datasets using state-of-the-art architectures. The experiments effectively validate the approach’s efficacy in identifying task-relevant visual features.

The authors could strengthen the practical motivation by elaborating on specific applications requiring real-time efficiency, beyond the general mention of computational constraints. For instance, discussing deployment scenarios like robotic vision or mobile applications would better contextualize the model’s utility.

Additionally, a deeper analysis of the learned saliency maps would provide valuable insights. The paper would benefit from examining whether certain object categories disproportionately rely on background biases (e.g., recognizing "zebras" through striped backgrounds rather than foreground features) or exhibit category-specific saliency patterns. Such analysis could illuminate potential model dependencies on dataset artifacts and inform robustness improvements.

Overall, the technical contribution is significant and well-executed, though the discussion of practical applications and model interpretability could be expanded to further enhance the paper’s impact.
