- Proposes a saliency mask generator that runs at 100 images/sec on a standard GPU for 224×224 inputs, training a model to predict the tightest bounding rectangle of the salient region for a target class using a black-box classifier.  
- Architecture uses multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50), with a feature filter on final-scale features and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms other weakly supervised approaches on ImageNet localization.  
- Paper contains sufficient references, related work discussion, and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Technical approach appears sound, though full verification was not performed.  
- Authors provide insightful discussions of design choices.  
- Major concern: unclear whether the 100 images/sec includes image resizing and how throughput scales to larger images (e.g., 640×480 typical smartphone captures).  
- Major concern: robustness to small objects—evaluations use relatively large salient objects, so performance when the target occupies a small region (and whether 224×224 suffices) is uncertain.  
- Clarification needed on Table 3: are metrics provided for methods listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) for direct comparison?  
- Minor language corrections: "More over" → "Moreover" (Line 273) and "optimise" → "optimizes" (Line 151).  
- Overall: promising technical results, but speed claim and robustness to small objects require further clarification before acceptance.
