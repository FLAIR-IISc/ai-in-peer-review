- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic masking network that produces class-conditioned saliency maps in a single forward pass instead of per-image optimization.
- Loss combines SSR-like preservation, SDR-like suppression, total variation smoothness, and area penalties to encourage compact, smooth masks.
- Trains with stochastic blending of masked regions to reduce introduced-evidence artifacts (strong blur or randomized color+noise).
- Architecture uses a U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder, a class-conditioned filter at the coarsest scale, and outputs masks at 1/4 resolution.
- Evaluated on ImageNet with AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet-50 and on CIFAR-10, showing sharper, more interpretable masks and improved weakly supervised localization.
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on area of the tight crop enclosing the mask and classifier confidence on that crop.
- Achieves high throughput (>100 images/s for 224×224) on a standard GPU (timing details need clarification).
- Strength: single-pass, practical method suitable for real-time saliency.
- Strength: well-balanced objective combining SSR and SDR with explicit regularization.
- Strength: careful mitigation of masking artifacts via stochastic alternative images and TV regularization.
- Strength: strong empirical localization results and clear qualitative improvements.
- Strength: simple, discriminative new saliency metric and demonstrated generality across classifiers and CIFAR-10.
- Weakness: “black box” claim ambiguous—training appears to require classifier differentiability/gradients; clarify if only scores/logits are used.
- Weakness: runtime and scalability details (hardware, batch size, preprocessing, higher-resolution inputs) are incomplete.
- Weakness: potential loss of fine detail and poor handling of small objects due to 224×224 resizing and 1/4 mask resolution.
- Weakness: sensitivity to choice of alternative image A (blur vs. noise) and loss weights; limited ablation studies.
- Weakness: new metric’s cropping/aspect-ratio distortions may interact with classifier scale/aspect invariance.
- Weakness: incomplete comparisons—some methods in one table are not evaluated under the new metric elsewhere.
- Weakness: requires per-classifier training; cross-classifier transfer not reported.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure modes and robustness to adversarial/test-time artifacts.
- Quality: method is well-motivated and technically sound but would benefit from additional ablations and runtime profiling.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written with helpful figures, but needs minor typos fixed and clearer implementation details (e.g., gradient flow, timing).
- Originality: meaningful contribution by amortizing per-image perturbation optimization with a trainable masking model and joint SSR/SDR objective.
- Significance: could be impactful for practical interpretability and weakly supervised localization if speed/quality generalize to higher resolutions and small objects.
- Pros for acceptance: single-pass high-quality saliency, artifact mitigation engineering, new evaluation metric, strong empirical evidence.
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete runtime/scalability details, limited ablations and small-object analysis, unclear black-box positioning.
- Key questions for authors: clarify throughput measurement details, small-object performance, report new metric for baseline methods, whether classifier backprop is used, provide ablations (λ1–λ4, A, mask resolution), and test transfer across classifiers.
- Minor typos: suggested fixes for LN 273 and LN 151 and request to state hardware/preprocessing/batch sizes and classifier differentiability dependence.
