- Proposes a fast neural-network method to learn saliency masks; concept is intriguing and contains good ideas.  
- Major limitation: saliency is measured only indirectly via weakly supervised localization or a proposed saliency metric, both of which have shortcomings.  
- Weakly supervised localization is imperfect since object context can determine class, so localization may not correlate with saliency quality.  
- The proposed saliency metric has artifacts from cropping/rescaling (aspect-ratio changes and extreme upscaling) that can hurt classifier performance and bias results toward tiny regions.  
- CNNs are not inherently scale-invariant, so the metric’s reliance on classifier scale invariance is problematic.  
- Paper lacks discussion/experiments on how mask-learning depends on the masking-network architecture and how architecture choices affect results.  
- Minor clarifications needed: whether Table 1 results are for all classes or only the correct class, and which LRP variant/parameters were used.  
- Rebuttal admits the metric depends on classifier quality and scale invariance, limiting applicability (not usable during model training, in other domains like spectrograms, or for true black-box classifiers).  
- Rebuttal suggests a strong dependence on the masking network, raising concern whether the method visualizes the base model’s saliency or artefacts of the masking network; this should be discussed for balance.
