- Introduces a fast, model-agnostic saliency method that produces high-quality maps in a single forward pass for real-time use.  
- Core idea: train a U-Net masking model (with ResNet-50 features) to predict the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region for a target class, avoiding iterative optimization.  
- Proposes a novel SSR-based saliency metric that evaluates interpretability and minimal region size.  
- Demonstrates strong ImageNet object-localization performance (36.7% error) competitive with fully supervised VGG (34.3% error) and superior to weakly supervised methods.  
- Method generalizes across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50) and outperforms baselines on saliency interpretability (Table 3).  
- Eliminates iterative computation used by prior methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi, Zhou et al.) while mitigating artifacts via TV smoothness penalties and randomized evidence removal.  
- Technical approach is sound with comprehensive ablations (including randomized evidence removal) and a well-motivated new metric.  
- U-Net with multi-scale features effectively balances precision and speed, achieving 100+ images/sec on 224×224 inputs.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear: well-organized, with precise definitions (SSR/SDR) and intuitive visualizations.  
- Original contributions include single-pass, model-agnostic framework and focus on smoothness to prevent adversarial artifacts.  
- Significance: real-time capability enables applications like video saliency for autonomous vehicles; weak supervision and CIFAR-10 results show generalizability.  
- Limitation: real-time claim is qualified—100 imgs/sec applies only to 224×224 inputs; larger resolutions may incur heavy resizing overhead and reduced throughput.  
- Limitation: performance on small salient objects (<10% of image) is untested and may degrade due to cropping/resizing.  
- Comparison gap: Table 3 lacks metrics from competing methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) to contextualize claims about interpretability.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to report timings for non-224×224 inputs, discuss small-object limitations and scaling solutions, and add baseline metrics (e.g., [2]) to Table 3.  
- Minor requested edits: fix typos ("More over" → "Moreover"; "optimise" → "optimizes").
