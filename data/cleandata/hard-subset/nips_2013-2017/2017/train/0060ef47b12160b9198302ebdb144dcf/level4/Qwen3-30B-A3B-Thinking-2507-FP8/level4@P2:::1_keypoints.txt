- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time use.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) concept.  
- Demonstrates the approach on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 and contrasts it with gradient-based, iterative patch removal, and optimization-based methods.  
- Achieves high speed (100+ saliency masks/sec), enabling real-time and video saliency applications.  
- New metric is argued to be more interpretable than standard localization accuracy.  
- Method outperforms existing weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet object localization (reported 36.7% error).  
- Architecture uses a U-Net with ResNet-50 features and multi-scale processing, and training with random evidence removal reduces adversarial artifacts.  
- Localization metric is limited because it assumes salient regions equal object locations, ignoring context reliance for classification.  
- SSR metric introduces artifacts via cropping/rescaling: aspect ratio changes and heavy upscaling of small regions can bias classifier performance.  
- The metric’s validity depends on the classifier’s scale-invariance properties, which are learned and limited.  
- Paper fails to analyze how masking-model performance depends on the classifier architecture.  
- Post-rebuttal admissions about scale-invariance contradict the claimed “black box” nature of the method.  
- It is unclear whether saliency maps reflect the original classifier’s behavior or biases of the masking network.  
- Pro-accept: novel, technically sound, well-validated approach with useful architecture and valuable real-time capability.  
- Con-accept: metric limitations and classifier-dependence reduce confidence in claimed advantages and generality.  
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper by thoroughly discussing metric limitations, clarifying dependence on classifier architecture, and stating scale-invariance limitations.
