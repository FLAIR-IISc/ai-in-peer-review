- Paper proposes a fast method to predict tight rectangular crops for class-specific saliency using multi-scale backbone features and upsampling, working with black-box classifiers.
- Reported throughput >100 images/s at 224×224 and improved ImageNet localization over prior weakly supervised methods.
- Strength: Clear problem setup and motivation for class-specific localization with only black-box classifier access.
- Strength: Elegant, modular architecture leveraging multi-scale features and sequential upsampling refinement.
- Strength: Strong empirical ImageNet localization results that surpass other weakly supervised techniques.
- Strength: Good presentation, writing, and literature coverage; practical emphasis on speed and black-box compatibility.
- Weakness: Runtime claim (>100 images/s) lacks clarity about what stages are included (preprocessing, feature extraction, postprocessing) and no throughput reported for higher resolutions.
- Weakness: Unclear robustness to small, thin, or small-fraction objects; fixed 224×224 input may limit small-object localization.
- Weakness: Reliance on a specific backbone without ablations on alternative feature extractors or scale configurations to support generality/efficiency claims.
- Weakness: Some methodological details are underreported (training dynamics, failure cases, sensitivity to classifier errors); proofs/derivations not fully verified by reviewer.
- Question: Does the >100 images/s include end-to-end processing from raw image to final boxes/masks, and how does runtime scale with input resolution (e.g., 640×480)?
- Question: How does performance vary with object size relative to image size; are results stratified by object scale or pixel fraction?
- Question: Can metrics in Table 3 be reported for all baselines in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) and are thresholds/criteria harmonized?
- Suggestion: Provide detailed runtime breakdown (preprocessing, backbone, filtering, refinement), throughput vs. resolution curve, and memory usage on standardized hardware.
- Suggestion: Add ablations on backbone choices (ResNet-18/50/101), number/placement of scales and filtering module, and input resolution effects on small-object performance.
- Suggestion: Include qualitative failure-case analysis (small/occluded/cluttered scenes) and discuss impact of black-box classifier misclassifications; evaluate on datasets with varied object scales.
- Minor: Fix typos and wording (e.g., “More over” → “Moreover”, “optimise” → “optimises”) and standardize capitalization/spelling for networks and venue style.
- Overall: Well-written and promising work with strong results, but reservations remain about end-to-end speed accounting and robustness to small/higher-resolution objects; clarifying runtime and adding scale-sensitive evaluations would strengthen the paper.
