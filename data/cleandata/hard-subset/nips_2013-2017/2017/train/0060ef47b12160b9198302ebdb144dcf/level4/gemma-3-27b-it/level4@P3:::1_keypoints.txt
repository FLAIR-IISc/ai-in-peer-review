- Proposes a fast, learned masking model for single-pass, real-time saliency maps for differentiable image classifiers.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, showing competitive weakly-supervised object localization.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the “smallest sufficient region.”  
- Novelty: learns a saliency detector (U-Net + custom objective) instead of iterative computation.  
- Clear writing and thorough methodological presentation.  

- Strength: significant speed improvement enabling applications like video saliency.  
- Strength: competitive or superior localization performance on benchmarks.  
- Strength: the new metric may offer a more nuanced saliency evaluation than simple localization.  

- Weakness: heavy reliance on the proposed metric and localization as primary evaluations, which are imperfect proxies for saliency.  
- Weakness: metric is sensitive to classifier scale/aspect ratio; cropping/resizing can introduce artifacts and bias.  
- Weakness: method depends on classifier scale/aspect invariance, reducing model-agnostic claims and general applicability.  
- Weakness: unclear whether maps reflect the classifier’s reasoning or the masking model’s learned biases — interpretability concerns.  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies (e.g., impact of pre-training and architectural choices).  
- Weakness: comparisons to LRP lack specifics about variant and parameter settings.  

- Argument for acceptance: addresses an important interpretability problem with a novel, fast approach and clear results.  
- Argument against acceptance: flawed evaluation metrics, limited generalizability, and unclear interpretability weaken the contribution.  

- Overall: interesting and potentially valuable but significant concerns about evaluation and classifier dependence; conditional acceptance if addressed, otherwise lean reject.  

- Recommendations: expand limitations discussion, analyze masking model interpretability, provide LRP comparison details, explore alternative evaluation metrics, and add ablation studies.
