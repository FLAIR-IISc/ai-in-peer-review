This paper presents a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers. The authors propose a trainable masking model that can produce high-quality saliency masks in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications. The model is trained to find the tightest rectangular crop that contains the entire salient region of a particular requested class, and it achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ImageNet localisation task.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and the intuition behind their design choices. The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various datasets.

However, I have a few concerns and questions that need to be addressed:

1. The authors claim that their approach can process a hundred 224x224 images per second on a standard GPU. However, it is unclear whether this processing time includes the image resizing operation. What is the running time for larger images, such as 640x480 images taken from iPhone 6s?
2. The salient objects in the paper are quite large. What if the requested class object is small in the images? Will a 224x224 image be sufficient to capture the salient region?
3. In Table 3, the authors provide a comparison of their approach with other methods using the proposed saliency metric. However, it is unclear whether there are corresponding metrics for other works in Table 2, such as Feed [2].

Minor comments:

* LN 273: "More over, because our model" should be changed to "Moreover, because our model".
* LN 151: "difference that [3] only optimise the mask" should be changed to "difference that [3] only optimises the mask".

Overall, this paper presents interesting technical results, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of image saliency detection.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers.
* The authors provide a thorough discussion of related work and the intuition behind their design choices.
* The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various datasets.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The authors' claim of real-time processing speed may not be entirely accurate, as it is unclear whether the processing time includes image resizing operations.
* The approach may not be effective for small objects in images.
* There is a lack of comparison with other methods using the proposed saliency metric.
