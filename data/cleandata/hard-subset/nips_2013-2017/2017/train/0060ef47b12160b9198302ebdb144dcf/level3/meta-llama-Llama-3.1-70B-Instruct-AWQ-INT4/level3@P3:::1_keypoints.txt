- Proposes a fast, neural-network-based, trainable masking model to learn saliency masks for image classifiers in a single forward pass.  
- Method is model-agnostic and applicable to any differentiable image classifier.  
- Training uses a novel objective that encourages masks to be smooth, small, and informative.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric to evaluate saliency map quality.  
- Extensive evaluation on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 shows improved object localization and saliency scores over other weakly supervised methods.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivated.  
- Strength: fast and efficient, suitable for real-time applications.  
- Strength: model-agnostic approach increases applicability.  
- Strength: new saliency metric provides an additional evaluation signal.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on two datasets.  
- Limitation: proposed saliency metric can be affected by object aspect ratio and scale.  
- Limitation: method assumes classifier invariance to scale/aspect ratio, which may not hold universally.  
- Limitation: dependence of the masking model on underlying architectures is not discussed or tested.  
- Limitation: lacks a clear discussion of method limitations and mitigation strategies for future work.  
- Assessment: novel and potentially significant contribution to explainable AI.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions requested: discuss limitations and future fixes, analyze architecture dependence, provide experimental/hyperparameter details, and add more visualizations.
