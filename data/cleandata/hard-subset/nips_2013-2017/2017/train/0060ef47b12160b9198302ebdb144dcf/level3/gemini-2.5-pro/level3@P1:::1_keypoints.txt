- Proposes a learning-based "masking model" (U-Net) that generates saliency masks in one forward pass for differentiable image classifiers.  
- Trains the mask with an objective encouraging smallest sufficient region (SSR), smallest destroying region (SDR), and smoothness (total variation).  
- Offers a fast method (~100 images/sec) trading per-image optimization cost for an upfront training cost, enabling real-time use.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ImageNet weakly supervised object localization with sharp, convincing qualitative masks.  
- Clever training strategy includes "fake" labels to ensure the mask attends to the class selector.  
- Relies heavily on weakly supervised localization, which is an imperfect proxy for saliency because classifiers may use contextual cues rather than the object.  
- Proposed saliency metric s(a,p) is flawed: cropping/resizing alters aspect ratio and scale, favors tiny regions blown up, and confounds classifier invariance with mask quality.  
- Claim of applicability to any "differentiable image classifier" is overstated; method requires differentiability and likely depends on classifier scale/aspect-ratio invariance.  
- Performance may depend strongly on the masking model's architecture (ResNet-50 U-Net) — unclear if masks reflect the classifier or the masker’s learned priors.  
- Important experimental details are unclear (e.g., whether localization error is computed only on correctly classified images; which LRP variant/parameters used).  
- Recommendation: Borderline — ideas are valuable and promising but evaluation and claims need stronger caveats and additional analysis.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, fast, SOTA localization, and high-quality qualitative results.  
- Rejection arguments: indirect/flawed evaluation metrics, overclaimed generality, and potential confounding from the masker’s biases.
