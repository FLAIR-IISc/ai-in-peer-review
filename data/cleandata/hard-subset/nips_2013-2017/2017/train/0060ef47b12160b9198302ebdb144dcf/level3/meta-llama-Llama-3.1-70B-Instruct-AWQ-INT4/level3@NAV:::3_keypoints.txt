- Proposes a fast, general saliency mask generator for differentiable image classifiers using multi-scale feature maps, a feature filter, and upsampling refinement.
- Claims to outperform other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains the method and training process clearly.
- Empirical results show high-quality saliency masks at over 100 images/sec on a standard GPU for 224x224 images.
- Introduces a new metric for evaluating saliency map quality and uses it for comparisons.
- Concern: the 100+ FPS real-time speed claim is based on small 224x224 images and may not generalize to larger, real-world image sizes.
- Concern: performance on small objects is not well-documented and may be a limitation.
- Suggestion: report results on larger images (e.g., 640x480) to validate real-world applicability.
- Suggestion: provide more details about the training procedure and hyperparameters used for the masking model.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with impressive results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
