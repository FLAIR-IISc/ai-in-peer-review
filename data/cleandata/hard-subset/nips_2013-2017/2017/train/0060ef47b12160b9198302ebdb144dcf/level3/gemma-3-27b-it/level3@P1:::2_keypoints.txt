- Proposes a masking model that predicts image saliency in a single forward pass for real-time use with differentiable classifiers.  
- Avoids computationally expensive iterative methods, improving speed for dynamic scenarios.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient and destroying regions for nuanced evaluation.  
- Builds on interpretable ML literature, addressing classifier decision explanations and dataset/adversarial concerns.  
- Uses a U-Net with a pre-trained ResNet encoder and training optimizations to prevent overfitting and artifacts.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming weakly supervised localization methods.  
- Ablation studies (including CIFAR-10 trained from scratch) show robustness of the approach.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, aiding understanding.  
- Authors acknowledge potential biases within the masking model, showing intellectual honesty.  
- Discussion of adversarial artifact mitigation is present but feels superficial and underexplored.  
- Introduction of alternative image 'A' appears ad-hoc; more systematic artifact-reduction exploration is needed.  
- Comparative analysis focuses mainly on localization accuracy, lacking broader qualitative comparisons of saliency map fidelity.  
- Future work suggestion on video saliency is brief; practical challenges for temporal adaptation are not detailed.  
- A deeper analysis of learned salient regions (e.g., background bias per category) is suggested but not performed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, fast, model-agnostic method; strong empirical results; clear presentation; significant real-time applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: superficial artifact mitigation, limited qualitative analysis, and unresolved potential biases.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with compelling results; recommended for acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
