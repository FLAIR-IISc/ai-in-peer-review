- Proposes a model-based U-Net masking network that predicts saliency maps for black-box classifiers in a single forward pass, amortizing expensive per-instance optimization.  
- Trains the masker with an objective balancing smallest sufficient region (SSR), smallest destroying region (SDR), and smoothness to produce high-quality maps.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization on ImageNet across multiple architectures (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that better captures interpretability goals than localization accuracy alone.  
- Novel idea: learning a generalizable saliency predictor for another model rather than per-example optimization.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-motivated, and experimentally rigorous.  
- Weakness: real-time motivation is underdeveloped—should motivate concrete applications (e.g., autonomous systems, interactive debugging, video saliency).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of learned saliency on categories with known dataset biases (e.g., snow/polar bear) to demonstrate utility for uncovering classifier shortcuts.  
- Overall: strong, minor-revision paper that represents a clear advance in model interpretability.
