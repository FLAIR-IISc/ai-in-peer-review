- Proposes a learned saliency predictor that outputs a per-image, per-class mask in a single forward pass.
- Optimizes a principled objective combining SSR (sufficiency) and SDR (necessity) terms.
- Uses TV regularization and randomized evidence-replacement (blur/noise) to discourage adversarial artifacts.
- Architecture: U-Net-style masking network with frozen ResNet-50 encoder trained against fixed classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50).
- Evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 with strong weakly supervised localization results.
- Introduces a new metric s(a,p) to quantify compactness and sufficiency of salient content.
- Situates work relative to gradient/backprop methods, perturbation-based explanations, and CAM-style localization, aiming for real-time speed.
- Technically sound with well-motivated objective and practical training tricks (fake labels, randomized A).
- Empirical results convincingly outperform prior weakly supervised baselines and produce sharp, artifact-resistant masks.
- Limitation: “black box” training still assumes differentiability and backprop access (not query-only).
- Clarity: well written with sufficient architectural and training details for reproduction; metric is clearly defined.
- Originality: novel combination of SSR and SDR in a trainable model that generalizes and runs in real time.
- Significance: advances state of the art in weakly supervised localization and offers a practical tool for practitioners.
- Suggestion: motivate concrete real-time applications and report end-to-end throughput/latency trade-offs.
- Suggestion: analyze learned saliency per category (outside-GT-box ratios), background reliance, and failure modes across classifiers.
- Suggestion: ablate evidence-replacement scheme and TV weight; report sensitivity to mask resolution and thresholds; evaluate targeted non-GT classes.
- Argument for acceptance: strong empirical gains, principled objective, thorough evaluation, and a useful new metric.
- Argument against acceptance: requires differentiable training access and limited analysis of learned-mask biases and application-oriented evaluation.
- Overall stance: reviewer leans positive; contributions are substantial and well supported.
