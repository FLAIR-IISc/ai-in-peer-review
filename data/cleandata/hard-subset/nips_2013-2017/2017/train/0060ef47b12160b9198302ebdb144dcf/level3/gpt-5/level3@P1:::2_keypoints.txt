- Proposal: Trainable masking network that produces class-conditional saliency maps in one forward pass by optimizing SSR (preserve) and SDR (suppress) with TV smoothness and randomized evidence removal.
- Architecture: U-Net with fixed ResNet-50 encoder, class-embedding "feature filter," and multi-scale upsampling to generate sharp masks.
- Training tricks: fake-label regime and randomized alternative images (blur or noisy constant) to discourage artifact overfitting and enforce class selector usage.
- New metric: SSR-inspired log-area vs. log-probability of the tight crop enclosing the mask, with a cropping protocol to reduce masking artifacts.
- Experiments: ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50 as black boxes) and CIFAR-10 show improved weakly supervised localization and better metric scores; runs >100 images/s.
- Relation to prior work: Amortizes optimization-based perturbation methods into a learned model; addresses limits of backprop-based saliency and differs from CAM by not needing architecture/localization supervision.
- Strength: Principled objective combining SSR/SDR, smoothness, and area regularization with clear exposition of introduced evidence issue.
- Strength: Real-time, model-agnostic inference yielding sharper, more precise masks than iterative alternatives.
- Strength: Practical, effective architecture and training heuristics that mitigate artifacts.
- Weakness: “Black-box” claim misleading—training requires differentiability/backprop through the classifier; clarify required information at train/test and whether only probabilities suffice.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to widely used baselines (Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, Integrated Gradients) to strengthen claims.
- Weakness: Lacks systematic ablations of objective weights (λ1–λ4), randomized alternative image choice, fake-label probability, mask resolution/upsampling, and TV weight.
- Weakness: Proposed metric may be sensitive to classifier calibration and to non-aspect-preserving crop/resizing choices—needs discussion and robustness checks.
- Weakness: Generalization/transfer unclear—evaluate cross-architecture transfer (e.g., train on ResNet-50, test on GoogLeNet) and cross-dataset robustness.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of learned class embeddings and context/background reliance; aggregate statistics and failure modes would strengthen interpretability claims.
- Weakness: Applications section should motivate specific real-time scenarios and report runtime/latency/memory on CPU/mobile.
- Quality: Technically sound and well-motivated; additional ablations and robustness tests would increase confidence.
- Empirical support: Compelling results on ImageNet/CIFAR-10 but should include more recent attribution baselines and ablations.
- Reproducibility: Most details provided; request training time, optimizer settings, exact ImageNet subset, and release of code/models.
- Clarity: Manuscript is clear and well-illustrated; clarify “black-box” terminology and the metric’s resizing/cropping choice.
- Originality: Novel and timely to train a real-time masking network jointly optimizing SSR/SDR with randomized evidence removal and a new SSR-based metric.
- Significance: Advances saliency quality and speed, enabling real-time explanations and improved weakly supervised localization with likely broader adoption.
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong, well-executed idea with clear gains, principled metric, and broad relevance to interpretability and real-time use.
- Arguments against acceptance: Incomplete baseline comparisons, missing ablations/robustness analyses, unclear gradient assumptions, and metric sensitivity concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding recommended baselines, ablations, and analyses of learned context biases.
