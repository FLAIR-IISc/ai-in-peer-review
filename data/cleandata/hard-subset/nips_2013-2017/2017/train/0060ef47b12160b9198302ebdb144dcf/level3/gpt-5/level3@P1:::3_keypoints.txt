- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency method that learns per-class masks for any differentiable image classifier in a single forward pass.
- Trains a masking network (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) to produce smooth, compact masks that preserve the target class when applied (SSR) and destroy it when inverted (SDR).
- Mitigates adversarial masking artifacts by applying masks against a stochastically chosen “alternative image” A (strong blur or random color+noise) and regularizing with TV and area penalties.
- Uses a class-embedding “feature filter” to gate high-level features for initialization and multi-scale upsampling to sharpen masks.
- Includes a “fake-label” training strategy (30% probability) to force reliance on the class selector.
- Evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet-50 black-boxes) and CIFAR-10 (FitNet black box) with differentiable access during training.
- Introduces a crop-based saliency metric s(a,p) to capture SSR, rewarding tight crops with high class probability (lower is better).
- Achieves competitive weakly supervised localization (e.g., 36.7% error with ResNet-50 black box) and favorable saliency metric values.
- Reports high throughput (~100 224×224 images/s on a single GPU).
- Positions method between gradient/backprop saliency and optimization-based perturbation approaches, amortizing iterative optimization into a learned predictor.
- Novelty: learned masking model for real-time, class-conditional saliency with randomized evidence-removal and fake-label training.
- Objective and architectural choices are well-motivated and empirically justified; training tricks are pragmatic and likely important.
- Experimental evaluation is reasonably broad and provides complementary views via localization and the new metric.
- Missing reproducibility/claim-substantiation details: throughput beyond 224×224, small-object behavior, ablations of loss terms and A choices, and clarification of the black-box assumption.
- Clarity: paper is well written with good intuition, figures, and explanation of the new metric; minor wording/typo issues noted.
- Significance: could enable real-time explanations in video/robotics and serve as a localization/segmentation prior if speed/robustness hold at higher resolutions and multi-object scenes.
- Pros: single-pass, class-conditional high-quality masks; orders-of-magnitude faster than iterative methods; balanced SSR/SDR objective with artifact mitigation; competitive results and clear exposition.
- Cons/questions: unclear end-to-end real-time claim and whether throughput includes preprocessing and black-box forward pass.
- Cons/questions: unclear performance on small objects given 224×224 input and 1/4 resolution masks; request for size-stratified metrics.
- Cons/questions: ambiguity about “black box” since gradients are required during training—clarify assumptions about access during training vs inference.
- Cons/questions: request ablations for all loss terms and alternative A choices, and inclusion of the new metric for other strong baselines (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi, CAM/Grad-CAM).
- Suggestions: report per-class/size breakdowns, visual failure cases, runtime/memory profiling across resolutions, consider segmentation variant and release code/pretrained models.
- Minor issues: specific wording/typo fixes and clearer definition/notation for s(a,p) and the area floor.
