- Proposes a novel U-Net-based "masking model" that generates saliency masks in a single forward pass, enabling real-time explanations for pre-trained classifiers.
- Main contribution is significant speed-up over iterative saliency methods, making real-time saliency generation possible.
- Original idea and promising research direction (training a network to explain another); technically sound with no obvious implementation errors.
- Evaluation of saliency quality is weak and indirect, relying on flawed metrics that undermine central claims of general applicability.
- Weakly supervised localization can fail as a saliency metric when classifiers use contextual cues (e.g., snow for a polar bear).
- The proposed saliency metric (crop-and-rescale) can introduce artifacts by changing object scale/aspect ratio, exploiting classifier scale sensitivity.
- The metric may favor small regions that look explanatory when blown up but do not faithfully reflect the classifier’s reasoning.
- Method’s reliance on the target classifier’s scale invariance limits applicability and is not adequately discussed.
- Unclear how the masking model’s architecture biases results; visualizations may reflect the masker’s learned biases rather than the target classifier’s reasoning.
- Authors should clarify whether Table 1 reports results over all classes or only the correct class and specify which LRP variant/parameters were used.
- Recommendation is borderline: accept if authors thoroughly address evaluation flaws and dependencies on both target and masking models; otherwise recommend rejection.
