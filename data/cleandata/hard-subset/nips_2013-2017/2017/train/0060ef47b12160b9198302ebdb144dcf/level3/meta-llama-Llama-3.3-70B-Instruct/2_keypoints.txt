- Introduces a novel PSDD-based approach to represent and learn route distributions on maps.
- Proposes a hierarchical approximation that factors route distributions into products of smaller regional distributions.
- Hierarchical representation enables control over component PSDD size and guarantees polynomial-size overall representation.
- Clear and well-written introduction to PSDDs and their application to routing.
- Technical contributions are sound and significant.
- Experimental results show the PSDD method outperforms a naive baseline and a Markov model, especially with destination knowledge.
- Strength: well-motivated problem statement and novel scalable modeling approach.
- Strength: strong empirical performance on the presented route prediction task.
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of limitations and potential biases.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset; generality is untested.
- Weakness: limited insight into interpretability and practical decision-making use of learned PSDDs.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10, reviewer confidence 0.8.
