- Proposes a fast saliency mask generator applicable to any differentiable image classifier via a single feed-forward pass for real-time use.  
- Uses a trainable masking model with a multi-scale U-Net-like architecture and a pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder.  
- Loss is designed to produce sharp, precise masks and avoid adversarial artifacts.  
- Claims to outperform weakly supervised techniques and achieve state-of-the-art on ImageNet localization.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the area of the rectangular crop containing the salient region and the classifier's class probability.  
- Experiments report visually appealing saliency maps and improved performance on the proposed metric versus baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with a comprehensive related work section and clear method explanation.  
- Strength: method is fast and accurate, suitable for real-time applications.  
- Concern: 100+ images/sec speed claim lacks detail (e.g., whether image resizing is included) and hardware/specs.  
- Concern: potential poor performance on small objects given 224x224 input size; need evaluation of input-size effects.  
- Minor issues: typos and grammatical errors to correct.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying computational requirements and providing further analysis on small-object/input-size performance.
