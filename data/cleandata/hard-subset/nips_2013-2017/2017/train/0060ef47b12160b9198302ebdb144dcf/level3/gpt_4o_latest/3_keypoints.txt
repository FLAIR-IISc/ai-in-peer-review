- Introduces a novel model-based saliency mask generator that identifies tight rectangular crops for a requested class without iterative optimization.  
- Achieves real-time speed (~100 224x224 images/sec on a standard GPU), making it suitable for video and autonomous applications.  
- Leverages multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50) and upsampling blocks to localize and refine saliency masks.  
- Works with black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet) and is model-agnostic.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on ImageNet localization, outperforming prior weakly supervised methods.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric that aligns with interpretability objectives and validates mask quality.  
- Generalizes to unseen datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10) without requiring pre-trained weights.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes detailed experiments and visual examples.  
- Scalability to larger or high-resolution images is not addressed and remains unclear.  
- Handling of small or sparse salient objects is not explicitly discussed and may be a limitation.  
- Table 3 is missing key metrics, weakening the completeness of the experimental evaluation.  
- Potential biases in the masking model are acknowledged but not thoroughly investigated.  
- Minor grammatical errors (lines 273 and 151) should be corrected.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions but notes concerns about scalability, small-object handling, missing metrics, and bias investigation.
