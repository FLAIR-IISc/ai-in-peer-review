- Proposes a novel trainable masking model that produces single-pass saliency maps for differentiable image classifiers.  
- Claims real-time performance (~100 images/sec on a standard GPU), enabling video-scale applications.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet for object localization.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that assesses map interpretability and shows strong performance.  
- Positions work between fast gradient-based methods and accurate but slow iterative refinement approaches.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive and situates the method well within explainable AI literature.  
- Uses a U-Net architecture with a ResNet backbone; objective includes mask smoothness and evidence removal.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and methodologically sound.  
- Authors address adversarial artifact concerns and include mitigation strategies.  
- CIFAR-10 experiments support the method’s generalizability.  
- Real-time speed claim requires clarification on whether image resizing is included and how runtime scales for larger images (e.g., 640x480).  
- Potential sensitivity to object scale due to fixed 224x224 input; small-object detection and multi-scale evaluation are needed.  
- New saliency metric comparison is incomplete; should report metric values for other methods (notably Fong & Vedaldi [2]) for direct comparison.  
- Minor issues: small typos and a phrasing ambiguity about [3] optimizing the mask.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, promising approach with strong benchmark results and practical real-time potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved runtime validation, object scale limitations, and incomplete metric comparisons.  
- Overall: strong paper with important contributions; acceptance recommended if authors address runtime on larger images, fixed-input/small-object effects, and complete metric comparisons.
