- Introduces a novel real-time saliency predictor that generates saliency maps in one forward pass for black-box classifiers, avoiding iterative methods.
- Well-motivated goal: improve interpretability and speed of saliency detection for deep models.
- Method uses a U-Net with a pre-trained ResNet encoder to produce high-resolution, precise masks.
- Objective function combines smoothness, small-region preservation, and classification-score maintenance terms and is thoughtfully designed.
- Demonstrates significant speed improvements over iterative and optimization-based approaches.
- Thorough experimental validation on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet across multiple black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region, and shows its validity through results.
- Strong performance on object localization, outperforming weakly supervised methods and competing with fully supervised approaches.
- Includes an ablation study showing effectiveness even without pre-training on CIFAR-10.
- Writing is clear and organized, making the paper accessible.
- Concern: artifact/evidence introduction mitigation (Eq.2, random A) seems ad-hoc and needs more rigorous analysis.
- Concern: analysis of learned class embeddings is relegated to the appendix and should be expanded in the main paper.
- Concern: potential biases in the masking model are acknowledged but not thoroughly investigated.
- Concern: lacks detailed computational-cost breakdown (e.g., inference time vs. image size) relevant to target applications.
- Concern: missing analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and how objective weights (λ1–λ4) affect performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical results, sound technical foundation, clear writing, and practical significance for real-time applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited artifact mitigation analysis, insufficient bias investigation, and lack of hyperparameter sensitivity discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
