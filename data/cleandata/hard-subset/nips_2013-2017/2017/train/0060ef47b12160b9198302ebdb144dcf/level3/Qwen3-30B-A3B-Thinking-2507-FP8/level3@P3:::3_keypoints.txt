- Proposes a model-based, single-pass saliency masker for black-box classifiers enabling real-time saliency.  
- Trains a U-Net-style masking model with ResNet-50 features to predict sharp saliency masks instead of per-image iterative optimization.  
- Claims high speed (~100+ 224x224 images/sec) while maintaining high-quality masks.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) for better interpretability evaluation.  
- Architecture leverages multi-scale feature maps, feature filters, and upsampling blocks—well-motivated and effective.  
- Empirical results outperform other weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization (36.7% error).  
- Shows generalization to CIFAR-10 without pre-training, indicating broader applicability.  
- Addresses adversarial artifacts via mask smoothness and randomized evidence removal—insightful implementation detail.  
- Weakness: Runtime claim needs clarification—100 imgs/sec is for 224x224 and may omit resizing/preprocessing; larger inputs (e.g., 640×480) likely much slower.  
- Weakness: Unclear performance on small objects—224x224 input may lack detail for small-object localization; authors should discuss and show examples.  
- Weakness: Table 3’s saliency metrics need clarification on whether they correspond to methods listed in Table 2.  
- Minor issues: a few typos and wording errors (e.g., "More over" on LN 273; LN 151 wording).  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, clear, original, and significant for practical interpretability and real-time applications.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—clarify runtime for larger inputs, discuss small-object limitations, align Table 3 with Table 2 methods, and fix typos.
