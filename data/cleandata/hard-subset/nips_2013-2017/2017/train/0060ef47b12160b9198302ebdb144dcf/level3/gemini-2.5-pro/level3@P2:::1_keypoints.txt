- Proposes a novel “masking model” (U-Net) that generates saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass, amortizing per-instance optimization.  
- Trains masks to be small and smooth while preserving classifier score on masked-in regions and destroying score on masked-out regions.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on weakly supervised object localization (CIFAR-10, ImageNet).  
- Introduces a new saliency-quality metric based on cropping the salient region and re-evaluating the classifier.  
- Strength: novel and significant "learning to explain" paradigm enabling orders-of-magnitude faster saliency generation and real-time applications.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance on the ImageNet localization benchmark supports mask quality for that proxy task.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Weakness: weakly supervised localization is an imperfect proxy for saliency because classifiers can rely on context (e.g., background) that localization metrics penalize.  
- Weakness: the proposed crop-and-resize saliency metric is flawed — it introduces scale/aspect-ratio artifacts and inherently favors tiny discriminative patches.  
- Weakness: evaluation assumes target classifiers are robust to scale and aspect changes, limiting applicability to models/tasks lacking such invariances (e.g., spectrograms or models during training).  
- Weakness: masking model (U-Net + ResNet-50 encoder) may impose strong architectural priors; no ablations to show masks reflect the target classifier rather than the masker’s biases.  
- Weakness: lack of experiments disentangling explanations from the explainer’s learned priors.  
- Minor clarification requested: Table 1 — are localization error rates conditioned on ground-truth class or averaged over all classes?  
- Overall recommendation: borderline — idea is promising and practical but evaluation disconnect undermines claims about reflecting classifier reasoning.  
- Arg for acceptance: introduces a promising, fast “learning to explain” approach and achieves SOTA on a standard benchmark.  
- Arg for rejection: relies on flawed/inappropriate proxy metrics, depends on classifier invariances, and fails to separate explainer bias from target-model explanations.  
- Condition for acceptance: authors should acknowledge and thoroughly discuss limitations and fix or mitigate evaluation issues before strong claims of general, high-quality explanations.
