- Proposes a fast, single-pass, neural “masking model” to generate saliency maps for black-box classifiers, contrasting with slow iterative methods.  
- Trains a U-Net-like architecture to predict salient image regions based on their effect on classifier outputs.  
- Demonstrates results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, and introduces a new saliency metric tied to minimal sufficient regions.  
- Achieves competitive performance on weakly supervised localization benchmarks.  
- Strength: major speed improvement enabling near real-time saliency generation compared to iterative approaches.  
- Strength: learning a direct mapping to saliency is a novel and sensible approach with practical potential.  
- Strength: competitive or superior weakly supervised localization results on ImageNet.  
- Strength: proposed metric attempts to move beyond localization by focusing on minimal sufficient regions.  
- Weakness: saliency evaluation is indirect, relying on weakly supervised localization and the new metric, both of which have limitations.  
- Weakness: the new metric uses cropping/rescaling that can introduce artifacts and exploit classifier scale/aspect-ratio sensitivities.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and analysis of masking-network architectural choices and their impact on results.  
- Weakness: strong dependence on the target classifier’s quality and scale invariance limits claimed general applicability.  
- Weakness: ambiguity whether saliency maps reflect the target classifier’s reasoning or biases learned by the masking network.  
- Specific request: clarify whether Table 1 results are averaged over all classes or only the correct class.  
- Specific request: specify the exact LRP variant and parameters used in comparisons for reproducibility.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, efficient approach with promising benchmark results and a thoughtful new metric.  
- Argument against acceptance: evaluation and metric flaws, classifier dependence, potential masking-network bias, and limited architectural analysis undermine claims.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline rejection unless major revisions address architectural analysis, classifier/invariance limitations, masking-network bias concerns, and metric artifacts.
