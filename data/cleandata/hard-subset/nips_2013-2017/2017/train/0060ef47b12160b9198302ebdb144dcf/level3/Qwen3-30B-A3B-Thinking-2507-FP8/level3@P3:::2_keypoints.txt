- Introduces a masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that predicts saliency maps in real time via a single forward pass.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) that better evaluates saliency quality than standard localisation accuracy.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 showing superior performance to existing weakly supervised methods (ImageNet localisation: 36.7% error vs. 34.3% fully supervised VGG).  
- Technically sound with clear theoretical justification addressing issues like introduced evidence during saliency computation.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with reproducible architecture and training details and helpful figures.  
- Novel paradigm shift from per-image iterative optimization to a trained predictor for saliency maps.  
- Real-time capability enables new applications (e.g., video saliency for autonomous vehicles, medical imaging, AR, interactive systems).  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of what the masking model actually learns and whether it exploits background biases (e.g., snow for “polar bear”).  
- Weakness: does not sufficiently explore the full range of applications enabled by the speed improvement.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential limitations when applying the method to non-CNN/non-differentiable classifiers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, orders-of-magnitude faster, meaningful new metric, strong benchmark results, solid theory and writing, addresses practical limitation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited bias analysis, underexplored application scope, insufficient discussion of non-CNN applicability.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
