- Proposes a fast neural-network masking model to learn saliency masks for any differentiable image classifier in a single feed-forward pass.  
- Claims model-agnostic applicability to black-box image classifiers.  
- Orders-of-magnitude faster than iterative saliency methods while producing higher-quality masks.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet and reported better localisation accuracy and saliency metrics than other weakly supervised methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach and advantages.  
- Strengths: clear method description, multi-dataset evaluation, and a new saliency metric.  
- Limitation: saliency is measured indirectly via weakly supervised localisation, which can fail when contextual cues determine class.  
- Limitation: the proposed SSR/SDR metric is flawed because cropping/rescaling salient regions can introduce scale/aspect-ratio artefacts.  
- Limitation: dependence of results on the masking model architecture is not discussed or evaluated across architectures.  
- Claim of general applicability is challenged due to reliance on classifier quality and scale invariance.  
- Method may not be usable during training, for models lacking scale invariance, or for domains like spectrograms.  
- Rebuttal indicates a strong dependence on the masking network, raising uncertainty whether visualisations reflect the target classifier or the masker.  
- Recommendation conditional: acceptable if limitations and dependencies are properly addressed in the paper, otherwise should be rejected.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
