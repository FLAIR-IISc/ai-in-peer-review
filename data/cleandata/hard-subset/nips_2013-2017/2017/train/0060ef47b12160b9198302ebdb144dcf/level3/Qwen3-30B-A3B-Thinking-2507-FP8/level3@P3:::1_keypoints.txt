- Proposes a trainable masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 backbone) for single-pass, real-time saliency detection instead of iterative optimization.
- Introduces a new saliency metric that crops the salient region and measures classifier confidence on the crop.
- Demonstrates large speed gains (≈100 saliency masks/sec) enabling real-time and video applications.
- Architecture is well-motivated and implemented; empirical results on ImageNet/CIFAR-10 show competitive localization accuracy.
- Reports a ResNet-trained model achieving 36.7% localization error versus 34.3% for a fully supervised VGG baseline.
- Saliency metric is flawed: cropping/rescaling changes aspect ratio and scale, creating classification artifacts and favoring tiny regions that get blown up.
- Weakly supervised localization is an insufficient proxy for saliency quality because contextual cues can mislead localization scores.
- The paper fails to analyze how the masking model depends on the underlying classifier architecture or whether it generalizes across architectures.
- Claim of general applicability to black-box classifiers is questionable because the method relies on classifier scale invariance and high classifier quality.
- Method may be unusable for models lacking scale invariance, during training, or for non-natural-image domains (e.g., spectrograms).
- Important experimental details are missing or unclear (e.g., scope of Table 1 results and the specific LRP variant/parameters used for comparisons).
- Critical limitation: dependence on classifier scale invariance fundamentally restricts applicability and undermines the general black-box claim.
- Critical limitation: insufficient discussion of whether visualizations reflect the target network or artifacts of the masking model itself.
- Recommendation: accept only if the paper thoroughly addresses metric flaws, classifier-dependence, and applicability scope; otherwise reject.
