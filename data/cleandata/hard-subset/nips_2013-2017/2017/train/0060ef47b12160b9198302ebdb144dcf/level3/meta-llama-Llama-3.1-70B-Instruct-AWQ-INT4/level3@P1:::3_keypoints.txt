- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator for differentiable classifiers to find the tightest rectangular crop for a requested class; claims superior ImageNet localization and real-time performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with good presentation quality.  
- Method uses multi-scale feature maps with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric for assessing explanation quality.  
- Experiments claim outperforming other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- Reviewer questions the 100 images/sec real-time claim and whether it includes image resizing.  
- Requests runtimes for larger images (e.g., 640×480 from an iPhone 6s) to assess real-world performance.  
- Concern about performance on small objects and whether 224×224 input suffices for small requested-class objects.  
- Asks whether Table 3 includes corresponding metrics for other works listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]) for fair comparison.  
- Overall technically correct and potentially significant, but real-time speed and real-world applicability need clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, clear explanation, thorough related work, potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: doubts about real-time claim, small-object performance, and incomplete comparative metrics.  
- Minor comments: fix "More over" → "Moreover" (LN 273); improve wording about [3] optimizing the mask (LN 151).
