- Proposes a novel masking model that generates saliency maps in a single pass for any differentiable classifier, trading off between speed and accuracy.  
- Trains the masker by manipulating classifier scores instead of using iterative optimization.  
- Architecture uses a U-Net decoder with a ResNet encoder and a loss combining smoothness, region size, and classification probability.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming other weakly supervised methods in object localization.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that correlates well with interpretability and ground truth.  
- Positions work clearly within existing literature, contrasting gradient-based (fast but low quality) and iterative (accurate but slow) methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and includes helpful figures and examples.  
- Related work coverage is thorough and cites relevant recent parallel efforts (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi).  
- Real-time claim (100 images/sec) lacks clarity on preprocessing/resizing overhead and runtimes for larger images.  
- No dedicated analysis of small-object performance or whether 224x224 inputs suffice for small/occluded classes.  
- Unclear whether the proposed saliency metric was computed for all baseline methods (inconsistency between Table 2 and Table 3).  
- Minor presentation issues and typos (e.g., “More over” → “Moreover”; unclear phrasing around reference [3]).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important interpretability problem, is novel and well-motivated, experimentally validated, and reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification of real-time claim, missing small-object analysis, and incomplete metric comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution deserving acceptance contingent on minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — clarify runtime across input sizes, analyze small-object performance, and provide complete metric comparisons for all methods.
