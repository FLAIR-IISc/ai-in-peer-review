- Proposes a trainable masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use.  
- Training objective enforces mask smoothness, minimal region preserving classification confidence, and removal of class evidence.  
- Well-written and well-organized with a clear explanation of architecture and training; uses ResNet-50 encoder in a U-Net.  
- Thorough evaluation on challenging datasets (ImageNet, CIFAR-10) using state-of-the-art networks.  
- Outperforms prior weakly supervised methods (Guided Backprop, Excitation Backprop, Fong & Vedaldi) on object localization and a new saliency metric.  
- Produces high-quality, interpretable, and sharp saliency maps without iterative optimization.  
- Provides a detailed analysis of desired saliency model properties (smoothness, small region size, evidence removal).  
- Novel contribution: trainable masking model that predicts saliency in one pass.  
- Potentially significant for real-time applications (video saliency, object detection, segmentation).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of learned salient regions and category-specific background biases.  
- Weakness: insufficient details on training procedures (learning rate schedule, batch size).  
- Weakness: limited evaluation on concrete real-time application scenarios and deployment details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough evaluation, superior results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing analysis of learned regions, missing training details, limited real-time evaluation.  
- Overall rating: 8/10; reviewer confidence: 8/10.
