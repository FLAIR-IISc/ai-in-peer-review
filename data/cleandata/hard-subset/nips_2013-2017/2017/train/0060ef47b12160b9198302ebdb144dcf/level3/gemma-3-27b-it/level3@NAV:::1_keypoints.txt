- Proposes a fast neural masking model to generate saliency maps for black-box classifiers by predicting masks directly instead of iterative optimization.  
- Demonstrates results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet and introduces a new saliency metric.  
- Practical focus with potential for real-time applications; U-Net with pre-trained ResNet features is an effective design.  
- Authors clearly discuss challenges of defining and measuring saliency and attempt to address them with a novel metric.  
- Major concern: the new saliency metric relies on cropping/rescaling which can introduce artifacts and exploit classifiers' limited scale invariance.  
- Metric flaw may favor small, spurious salient regions and thus produce misleading evaluations.  
- Insufficient exploration of dependence on the underlying classifier architecture and lack of evidence for generalizability across architectures.  
- Method’s applicability is heavily reliant on the target classifier’s quality and scale invariance, limiting utility during training or in domains where scale matters (e.g., spectrograms).  
- Unclear whether visualizations reflect the target classifier’s reasoning or the masking network’s learned associations.  
- Experimental details need clarification: whether Table 1 reports performance across all classes or only the correct class.  
- Request to specify the LRP variant and parameter settings used for comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, promising experimental results, and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: flawed metric, unaddressed dependence on classifier characteristics, and questionable generalizability.  
- Recommendation: lean toward rejection unless authors substantially expand discussion of limitations and convincingly demonstrate robustness and general applicability.
