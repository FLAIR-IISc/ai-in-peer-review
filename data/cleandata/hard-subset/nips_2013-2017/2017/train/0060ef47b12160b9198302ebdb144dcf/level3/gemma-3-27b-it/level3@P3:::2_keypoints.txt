- Introduces a learned feed-forward U-Net masking model for image saliency detection of black-box classifiers, replacing slower iterative methods.  
- Motivated by limitations of gradient-based and iterative patch-removal techniques; addresses artifact introduction with sensible solutions.  
- Proposes a novel, well-justified saliency metric linked to SSR/SDR and demonstrates strong experimental validation.  
- Technically sound with clear method description, reproducible details, and thorough experiments including an ablation study on CIFAR-10.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, well-organized presentation, informative figures, and consistent notation.  
- Original contribution: learning a saliency predictor (distinct from concurrent optimization-based work) and introducing a new metric.  
- Significant results: state-of-the-art weakly-supervised localization on ImageNet and real-time performance with practical applications.  
- Strengths: much faster than iterative approaches, high accuracy, novel approach, clear presentation, and a useful new evaluation metric.  
- Weaknesses: masking model must be trained per target black-box classifier, limited analysis of masking-model biases, and experiments focus mainly on ImageNet.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important interpretability problem with a novel, validated, and impactful method presented clearly.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on per-classifier training and insufficient depth on bias analysis could limit generality.  
- Overall: strong paper, significant contribution to interpretability; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Minor suggestions: expand bias analysis, evaluate on datasets beyond ImageNet, and mention more specific real-time applications (e.g., medical imaging, fraud detection).
