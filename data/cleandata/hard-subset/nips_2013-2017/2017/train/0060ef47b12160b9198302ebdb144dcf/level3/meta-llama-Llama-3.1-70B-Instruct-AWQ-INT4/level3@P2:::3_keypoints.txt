- Proposes a fast image saliency mask generator applicable to any differentiable image classifier with a single forward pass for real-time use.  
- Trains a masking model to manipulate classifier scores by masking salient image regions; generalizes to unseen images.  
- Claims greater efficiency and higher-quality masks than gradient-based and iterative optimization methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Uses multi-scale feature maps (e.g., ResNet-50), a feature filter for initial localization, and upsampling blocks to refine masks.  
- Reports outperforming other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- Paper is technically correct, with clear motivation, design discussion, and adequate related work.  
- Presentation quality is good but contains minor typos and wording issues to fix.  
- Reviewer questions the real-time speed claim (e.g., does 100 images/sec include resizing?) and requests runtimes for larger images (e.g., 640×480).  
- Concerns about performance on small objects and whether 224×224 input suffices for small target-class objects.  
- Originality: novel masking-model approach and new saliency metric constitute meaningful contributions.  
- Significance: high — fast, accurate saliency method applicable to real-time systems and offers a new evaluation perspective.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, speed/accuracy claims, new metric, thorough explanation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient evidence for speed, limited small-object evaluation, missing comparative metrics in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; overall rating 8/10.  
- Minor revisions requested: fix typos/wording, provide additional evidence for real-time speed, evaluate small-object/224×224 performance, and include corresponding metrics for other works in Table 2.
