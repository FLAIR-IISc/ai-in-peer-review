- Proposes a novel method to represent and learn distributions over map routes using Probabilistic Sentential Decision Diagrams (PSDDs).
- Introduces hierarchical route distributions to achieve more tractable and scalable route representations.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of technical details.
- Technical presentation is thorough and the approach is technically sound.
- Empirical evaluation shows the method outperforms Markov models and naive baselines on a route prediction task.
- Good discussion of related work and explicit acknowledgement of limitations.
- Main scalability concern: compilation of propositional logic to circuits with required properties can be a computational bottleneck.
- Assumes hierarchical regions are sufficiently small, which may not hold in real-world scenarios.
- Lacks clear comparison to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., graphical models), limiting evaluation completeness.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.
- Recommendation: paper needs revisions to address scalability and provide more comprehensive comparisons.
- With those revisions, the work has potential to be a strong contribution to the field.
