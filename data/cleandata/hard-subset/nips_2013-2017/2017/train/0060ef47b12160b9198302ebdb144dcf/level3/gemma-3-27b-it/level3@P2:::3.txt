## Review of “Real Time Image Saliency for Black Box Classifiers”

This paper presents a novel approach to generating image saliency maps for differentiable image classifiers. The core idea is to train a masking model to predict salient regions, enabling fast, single-pass saliency detection – a significant improvement over iterative methods. The authors demonstrate promising results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving competitive performance on object localization and introducing a new saliency metric.

The work builds upon a substantial body of research in image saliency and explainable AI. Early gradient-based approaches [11, 12, 16] are acknowledged as fast but limited in quality. Iterative refinement methods [17, 1, 2] are noted for producing interpretable maps but suffering from significant computational cost. This paper positions itself as bridging this gap, offering a fast *and* accurate model-based solution. The related work section is comprehensive, appropriately citing key contributions and contextualizing the authors' approach within the existing landscape. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed trainable masking model offers a genuinely novel approach to saliency detection, moving beyond iterative optimization towards a learned, feed-forward process.
* **Speed:** The claimed real-time performance (100 images/sec on a standard GPU) is a substantial advantage, opening doors to applications like video saliency analysis.
* **Performance:**  The results on the ImageNet localization task demonstrate competitive performance compared to other weakly supervised methods (Table 1 and 2). The introduced saliency metric (Table 3) provides a useful assessment of map interpretability and demonstrates a strong performance for the proposed model.
* **Technical Soundness:** The architecture (U-Net with ResNet backbone) is well-motivated and appears technically sound. The objective function incorporates important considerations like mask smoothness and evidence removal.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clear, and the method is explained in a logical manner.  The introduction provides sufficient context and the design choices are well-justified.
* **Thoroughness:** The authors address potential issues like the introduction of adversarial artifacts and provide strategies to mitigate them. The CIFAR-10 experiment strengthens the generalizability of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Real-time Claim:** The real-time speed claim needs further scrutiny. The review notes the crucial question of whether this performance includes image resizing, which can be a bottleneck.  Runtimes for larger images (e.g., 640x480) common in real-world applications would be helpful to assess practical applicability.
* **Object Scale Sensitivity:** The paper’s performance on small objects is unclear.  Using a fixed 224x224 input size could be limiting for detecting small instances of the requested class. The authors should address whether this impacts performance and potentially explore multi-scale inputs.
* **Metric Comparison:** The paper introduces a new saliency metric but the comparison to other saliency methods (Table 3) is incomplete. Including the proposed metric values for methods listed in Table 2 (specifically Fong & Vedaldi [2]) would facilitate a more direct comparison and provide a better understanding of the new metric’s value.
* **Minor Issues:**  A few minor typos were noted in the key points (e.g., "More over" should be "Moreover").  The phrasing in line 151 regarding [3] optimizing the mask could be clarified.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a genuinely novel and promising approach to image saliency detection.
* The results demonstrate competitive performance on established benchmarks.
* The paper is well-written and technically sound.
* The potential for real-time applications is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The real-time performance claim requires further validation, especially with respect to image resizing and larger input sizes.
* The limitations regarding object scale sensitivity and the incomplete comparison of the new saliency metric need to be addressed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. The proposed method addresses a critical need for fast and interpretable saliency detection. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, but addressing the concerns regarding real-time performance, object scale, and the completeness of the metric comparison would significantly strengthen the paper. 

**Recommendation:** 

I recommend **acceptance** contingent on the authors addressing the reviewer's concerns regarding the runtime for larger images, the impact of fixed input size on small objects, and completing the metric comparison with Fong & Vedaldi [2]. If these points are clarified, this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of explainable AI.
