

This paper introduces a novel, fast saliency mask generator for black-box classifiers that produces sharp, interpretable masks via a single forward pass (~100 images/sec on 224x224 inputs). The method trains a masking model using multi-scale ResNet features and a U-Net-like architecture, with a feature filter for initial localization and upsampling blocks for refinement. It proposes a new saliency metric (s(a,p)) based on the tightest rectangular crop preserving classification confidence, demonstrating superior performance over weakly supervised baselines on ImageNet localization (36.7% error vs. 34.3% for fully supervised VGG).

**Strengths:** The technical approach is sound, with clear motivation for addressing classifier biases (e.g., polar bear/snow misinterpretation). The new metric directly evaluates saliency quality beyond standard localization accuracy, and experiments convincingly show the method outperforms iterative techniques (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi [2]) and baselines. The architecture is well-motivated, and the paper is clearly written with sufficient related work. The real-time speed claim is compelling for applications like video analysis.

**Weaknesses:** Critical concerns remain unaddressed. The 100 images/sec claim lacks validation for real-world resolutions (e.g., 640×480 from iPhone 6s), which is essential for practical deployment. Performance on small objects is untested—224×224 inputs may discard critical details for small targets. Table 3’s metrics appear incomplete (e.g., missing Feed [2] results from Table 2), undermining comparative claims. Minor typos (e.g., "More over" LN 273) reduce professionalism.

**Overall:** This is a technically robust contribution with strong significance for real-time explainable AI. However, the unresolved concerns about scalability to practical resolutions and small objects weaken the real-world applicability argument. The authors should clarify the speed claim for larger images, address small-object limitations, and ensure Table 3 includes all referenced baselines. With these revisions, the paper would make a strong contribution. *Recommendation: Weak Accept (pending minor revisions).*
