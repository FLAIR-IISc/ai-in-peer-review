- Proposes a novel method using Probabilistic Sentential Decision Diagrams (PSDDs) and hierarchical route distributions to learn distributions over map routes for tractable, scalable representation.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a route prediction task with significant accuracy improvements over Markov models.  
- Strength: high originality and significance; addresses a difficult problem better than prior work.  
- Strength: well-written and clearly organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Technical quality: method is sound and supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Clarity: clear explanations of background, motivation, and technical details.  
- Originality: introduces a novel approach building on and understanding existing literature.  
- Significance: results have important implications for traffic prediction and route planning.  
- Limitation: relies on a hierarchical map representation that may not always be available or easy to obtain.  
- Limitation: assumes routes are simple and loop-free, which may not hold in real-world scenarios.  
- Limitation: lacks thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to fully evaluate effectiveness.  
- Suggestion: authors should better discuss potential applications and broader implications.  
- Recommendation: overall recommend accept, but advise addressing the noted limitations and comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, improved accuracy, and practical implications.  
- Arguments for rejection: dependency on hierarchical maps, loop-free route assumption, insufficient benchmarking, and limited discussion of applications.
