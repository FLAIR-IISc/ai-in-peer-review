- Proposes a fast, trainable masking model that generates saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that evaluates map quality by testing classifier recognition on the preserved region.  
- Masking model training objective encourages smoothness, small region size, and high class probability.  
- Strength: novel, fast, and accurate approach to saliency detection.  
- Strength: new metric offers a more comprehensive evaluation of produced maps.  
- Strength: single-pass mask generation makes the method practical for real-time applications.  
- Weakness: metric depends on classifier quality and is not scale-invariant.  
- Weakness: paper does not discuss dependence of the masking model on architecture or report experiments across architectures.  
- Weakness: lacks clear discussion of limitations, particularly the risk of adversarial artifacts.  
- Originality/significance: presents a novel, significant contribution for real-time saliency and evaluation.  
- Quality/clarity: paper is well-written and organized but needs minor clarifications (e.g., which LRP variant and parameter settings were used).  
- Recommendation: accept only if limitations and dependencies (adversarial risks, architecture effects) are properly addressed; otherwise reject.
