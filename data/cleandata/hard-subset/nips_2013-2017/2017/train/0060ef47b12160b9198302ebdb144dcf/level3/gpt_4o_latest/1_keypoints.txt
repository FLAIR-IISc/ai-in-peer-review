- Paper proposes a neural masking model that generates saliency maps in a single forward pass and is evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet with a novel saliency metric.
- Strength: Novelty — trainable masking model produces saliency maps in one pass, improving over iterative, costly methods.
- Strength: Real-time capability — generates >100 saliency masks/sec, enabling real-time and video/autonomous use cases.
- Strength: Empirical results — outperforms existing weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization and is competitive under the new metric.
- Strength: Generalizability — tested on multiple datasets and resolutions, adaptable to different architectures.
- Weakness: Saliency metric introduces cropping/rescaling artifacts that bias toward smaller regions and are not sufficiently discussed.
- Weakness: Using weakly supervised localization as a proxy for saliency is problematic because localization and saliency are not equivalent, especially with contextual cues.
- Weakness: Architecture dependence — reliance on U-Net isn’t explored; no experiments with alternative masking architectures to show robustness.
- Weakness: Classifier scale invariance assumption in the saliency metric limits applicability and contradicts claims of general black-box applicability.
- Weakness: Clarity/reproducibility issues — Table 1 lacks explanation and the LRP variant/parameter settings are unspecified.
- Suggestion: Discuss saliency metric limitations and potential biases in greater detail.
- Suggestion: Run experiments with alternative masking architectures to evaluate robustness.
- Suggestion: Clarify the relationship between saliency and weakly supervised localization and include additional evaluation metrics.
- Suggestion: Specify the exact LRP variant and parameter settings to improve reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Address potential biases of the masking model and how they influence produced saliency maps.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — publication only if the metric, architecture-dependence, and clarity/reproducibility concerns are adequately addressed.
