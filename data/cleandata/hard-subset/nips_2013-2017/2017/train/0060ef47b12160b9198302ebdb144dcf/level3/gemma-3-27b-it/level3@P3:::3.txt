This paper proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency detection method applicable to any differentiable image classifier. The core idea is to train a masking model to predict saliency maps directly, avoiding the iterative approaches common in previous work. The method leverages multi-scale feature maps from a pre-trained CNN (e.g., ResNet-50) and demonstrates strong performance on ImageNet localization, surpassing other weakly supervised techniques. The presentation is clear and well-organized, with good intuition provided for design choices.

**Quality:** The paper appears technically sound. The architecture description is detailed enough to understand the implementation, and the objective function seems well-motivated. The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on ImageNet localization. However, a deeper verification of the implementation details and mathematical formulations would be beneficial. The completeness of the work is reasonable, presenting a fully functional method and comprehensive experiments. The authors acknowledge potential limitations and discuss avenues for future research. Their evaluation includes a new saliency metric alongside standard localization accuracy, showing careful consideration of strengths and weaknesses.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and situates the work within the broader context of interpretability in deep learning. The related work section is comprehensive and provides appropriate citations. Figures are helpful in illustrating the architecture and example saliency maps. Minor typos are present (LN 273 “More over” → “Moreover”, LN 151 wording about [3] optimizing the mask), but do not significantly detract from the clarity.

**Originality:** The approach of learning a saliency mask generator directly is novel, as it moves away from iterative optimization techniques. Combining this with multi-scale feature maps and a carefully designed objective function represents a meaningful contribution. The new saliency metric is also a valuable addition, offering a more nuanced evaluation than simple localization accuracy. The paper appropriately references related work, demonstrating awareness of the current state-of-the-art.

**Significance:** The potential impact of this work is substantial. A real-time saliency detection method has applications in various domains, including video analysis, autonomous driving, and human-computer interaction. Outperforming existing weakly supervised localization techniques on ImageNet is a significant achievement. The method's model-agnostic nature increases its versatility and potential for widespread adoption. The presented work advances the state-of-the-art in weakly supervised localization and interpretability.

**Strengths:**

*   **Speed:** The claimed speed of ~100 images/sec on a standard GPU is a major advantage over iterative methods.
*   **Accuracy:** Competitive performance on ImageNet localization, surpassing other weakly supervised techniques.
*   **Model-Agnostic:** Applicable to any differentiable classifier.
*   **Clear Presentation:** Well-written and organized with good intuition.
*   **Novelty:** Direct learning of saliency masks is a unique approach.
*   **New Metric:** The proposed saliency metric provides a more insightful evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Real-time Claim:** The 100 images/sec claim needs further scrutiny. Does this include image resizing/preprocessing? Runtimes for larger images (e.g., 640x480 from an iPhone 6s) would be crucial to assess real-world applicability.
*   **Small Object Performance:** Performance on small objects is not explicitly discussed. The 224x224 input size might be insufficient for accurately localizing small objects within the requested class.
*   **Table 3 Comparison:** Table 3 would be more informative if it included corresponding metrics for other works listed in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]). This would allow for a direct comparison of saliency metric scores.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective approach to saliency detection.
*   Strong experimental results on a challenging dataset.
*   Potential for significant impact in various applications.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The real-time speed claim requires further validation.
*   Limited discussion of performance on small objects.
*   Incomplete comparison in Table 3.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of interpretability and computer vision. While some questions remain regarding the real-time performance and handling of small objects, the novelty, accuracy, and clear presentation of the work are highly commendable. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the concerns raised regarding the speed claim and providing a more complete comparison in Table 3.
