- Proposes a novel, model-agnostic masking model that predicts saliency masks directly and runs much faster than iterative methods.  
- Leverages multi-scale features from a pre-trained network (e.g., ResNet-50) in a U-Net architecture to produce real-time saliency masks.  
- Introduces a carefully designed objective to reduce adversarial artifacts in generated masks.  
- Presents a new saliency metric addressing both smallest sufficient and destroying regions.  
- Shows strong experimental results on ImageNet, outperforming existing weakly supervised localization methods.  
- Writing and paper organization are clear and technically sound.  
- Claimed real-time speed (~100 224x224 images/sec) requires further validation, including image resizing overhead.  
- Runtime performance on larger images (e.g., 640x480) is not reported and is crucial for real-world applicability.  
- Method’s effectiveness on small objects is not evaluated and may be limited by 224x224 input resolution.  
- Would benefit from experiments on datasets with varying object sizes to assess robustness.  
- Table 3 lacks corresponding saliency metric scores for methods in Table 2 (e.g., Feed [2]), hindering direct comparison.  
- Minor typographical errors and a clarification needed (LN 273 “More over” → “Moreover”; LN 151 about [3] optimizing the mask).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/practical fast saliency approach, improved performance, promising metric, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unvalidated speed claim, unclear performance on small objects, incomplete experimental comparisons.
