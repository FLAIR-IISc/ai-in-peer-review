- Novel feed-forward masking model for saliency that avoids iterative optimization.
- Addresses computational cost of backpropagation/iterative patch removal methods.
- Demonstrates strong ImageNet weakly-supervised localization performance, outperforming other methods.
- Introduces a new saliency metric.
- Uses a U-Net masking model with a ResNet backbone.
- Enables fast, single-pass saliency suitable for real-time and video applications.
- Visual results indicate the model captures meaningful regions relevant to classifier decisions.
- The new saliency metric is fundamentally flawed due to cropping/resizing artifacts and loss of aspect ratio.
- Metric biases toward small, scale-invariant regions and may reward exploiting classifier characteristics rather than true saliency.
- Masking model may overfit the specific classifier it is trained on; unclear whether masks reflect the target classifier or the masker.
- Limited exploration of alternative architectures and hyperparameters raises questions about generalizability.
- Evaluation is focused on localization, which may not directly correspond to true saliency because of contextual cues.
- Table 1 reporting is unclear (averaging over classes vs. only correctly classified images); LRP variant/parameter details are insufficient for reproducibility.
- Claim of applicability to “any” differentiable classifier is overstated due to dependence on classifier scale invariance and quality.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel fast approach, strong empirical results, new metric, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: flawed metric, classifier-dependence, limited architectural/hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall: valuable contribution but requires addressing metric flaws, classifier dependencies, and broader architectural evaluation to be acceptable.
