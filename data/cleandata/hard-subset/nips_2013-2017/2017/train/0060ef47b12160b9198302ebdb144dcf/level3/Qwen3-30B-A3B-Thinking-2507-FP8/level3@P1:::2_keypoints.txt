- Proposes a novel masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, removing need for iterative optimization.  
- Trains the mask with an objective balancing mask smoothness, small region size, preserved-region classification accuracy, and low post-removal class probability.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) to better evaluate interpretability than standard localization measures.  
- Method is applicable to any differentiable image classifier and demonstrated as a black-box approach.  
- Thorough experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet using state-of-the-art classifiers validate the approach.  
- Outperforms previous weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization and surpasses simple baselines (e.g., central rectangle).  
- Saliency maps achieve interpretability comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes per the new metric.  
- Real-time capability: generates ~100 saliency masks per second, enabling new application domains.  
- Addresses adversarial artifacts via evidence removal function and mask smoothness constraints.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and reproducible with careful methodological explanations.  
- Strengths: technically sound method, comprehensive experiments, originality, and high practical significance.  
- Minor weaknesses: could better motivate specific real-time applications (e.g., video for autonomous vehicles).  
- Minor weaknesses: needs deeper analysis of learned salient regions to detect background biases or failure modes.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—strengths substantially outweigh minor issues; straightforward to address suggested improvements.
