- Introduces a novel real-time saliency method for black-box classifiers using a masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass.
- Method is suitable for real-time applications and demonstrated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet with state-of-the-art networks.
- Experimental results show the approach outperforms iterative optimization and gradient-based saliency methods.
- Authors introduce a new saliency metric to evaluate saliency map quality.
- Achieves high speed (over 100 images/sec on a standard GPU), enabling video and other real-time uses.
- Model-agnostic: applicable to any differentiable image classifier.
- Thorough related-work analysis and discussion of prior methods’ limitations.
- Potential limitation: requires large amounts of training data; impact of training set size not analyzed.
- Lacks detailed analysis of the interpretability of the produced saliency maps and how the new metric relates to interpretability.
- Novelty stems from combining a U-Net architecture with a feature filter to produce saliency maps.
- Paper is well-written, clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Technically sound with supporting theoretical analysis and comprehensive experiments.
- Review recommends acceptance, citing significant contribution and potential broad applicability (computer vision, robotics, healthcare).
- Criticisms include need for more analysis on data requirements, interpretability, and further limitations discussion.
- Final rating: 9/10; reviewer confidence: 8/10.
