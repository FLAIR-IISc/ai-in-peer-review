- Proposes a novel NN-based saliency detection method that predicts regions critical for another NN’s classification in a single forward pass.  
- Produces saliency maps much faster than iterative methods (over 100 maps/sec), enabling real-time use.  
- Uses a U-Net architecture with a ResNet-50 encoder, which is well-justified and effective.  
- Validated on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, ImageNet) and multiple black-box classifiers, showing strong generalization.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over existing weakly supervised methods and achieves state-of-the-art localization and saliency results.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that offers a more nuanced evaluation than traditional localization accuracy.  
- Experimental rigor is high, with extensive comparisons and compelling quantitative results.  
- Practical real-time application scenarios and motivations (e.g., autonomous systems, video) are underexplored.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of learned saliency maps, including object-category biases and spurious correlation detection.  
- Acknowledges potential masking-model bias but does not thoroughly investigate or quantify it.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address motivation and analysis gaps.  
- Main pros: large speed/quality improvement, strong experiments, and a useful new metric; main cons: limited real-world motivation and insufficient bias/interpretability analysis.
