**Review of "Real Time Image Saliency for Black Box Classifiers"**

This paper proposes a fast neural-network-based method to learn saliency masks for black box image classifiers. The authors introduce a trainable masking model that can produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper begins by discussing the limitations of current image classifiers, which can exhibit unexpected behavior despite achieving high accuracy. The authors argue that saliency detection methods can help identify the most relevant parts of an image for a particular class. They propose a new saliency metric, which measures the quality of the produced map by evaluating the classifier's ability to recognize the object from the preserved region. The authors also introduce a masking model, which is trained to minimize an objective function that encourages smoothness, small region size, and high classification probability.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to saliency detection, which is both fast and accurate.
* The authors introduce a new saliency metric, which provides a more comprehensive evaluation of the produced maps.
* The masking model is able to produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications.

Weaknesses:

* The proposed saliency metric has limitations, such as the reliance on classifier quality and scale invariance.
* The masking model's dependence on architecture is not discussed, and it is unclear whether different architectures were tested and how they affect the results.
* The paper does not provide a clear discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach, particularly with regards to the potential for adversarial artifacts.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel approach to saliency detection, which is both fast and accurate. The introduction of a new saliency metric provides a more comprehensive evaluation of the produced maps. The masking model's ability to produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass makes it suitable for real-time applications, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Quality and Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and the proposed approach. However, there are some minor clarifications requested, such as specifying which LRP variant and parameter settings were used for comparisons.

**Recommendation**

If the limitations and dependencies of the proposed approach are properly discussed in the paper, it is acceptable for publication. However, if not, it should be rejected. The authors should provide a clear discussion of the potential limitations of the proposed approach, particularly with regards to the potential for adversarial artifacts. Additionally, the authors should provide more details on the masking model's dependence on architecture and how different architectures affect the results.
