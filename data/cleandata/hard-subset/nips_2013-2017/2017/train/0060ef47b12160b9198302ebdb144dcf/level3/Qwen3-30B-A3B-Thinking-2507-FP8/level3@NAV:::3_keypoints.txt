- Proposal: novel, fast saliency mask generator for black-box classifiers via a single forward pass (~100 images/sec on 224×224 inputs).  
- Architecture: trains a masking model using multi-scale ResNet features and a U-Net-like design with a feature filter and upsampling refinement blocks.  
- Metric: introduces s(a,p), a saliency metric based on the tightest rectangular crop that preserves classification confidence.  
- Results: shows superior performance to weakly supervised baselines on ImageNet localization (36.7% error vs. 34.3% for fully supervised VGG).  
- Strength: technically sound approach with clear motivation to address classifier biases (e.g., polar bear/snow misinterpretation).  
- Strength: new metric evaluates saliency quality beyond standard localization accuracy.  
- Strength: experiments convincingly outperform iterative techniques (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) and baselines.  
- Strength: architecture is well-motivated and the paper is clearly written with sufficient related work.  
- Strength: real-time speed claim is compelling for applications like video analysis.  
- Weakness: 100 images/sec claim lacks validation on real-world resolutions (e.g., 640×480 from iPhone 6s).  
- Weakness: performance on small objects is untested; 224×224 inputs may discard critical small-object details.  
- Weakness: Table 3 appears incomplete (missing Feed [2] results from Table 2), undermining comparative claims.  
- Weakness: minor typos (e.g., "More over") reduce professionalism.  
- Overall: technically robust and significant for real-time explainable AI, but scalability to larger images and small-object performance need clarification.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept pending minor revisions to clarify speed at larger resolutions, address small-object limitations, and include missing baselines.
