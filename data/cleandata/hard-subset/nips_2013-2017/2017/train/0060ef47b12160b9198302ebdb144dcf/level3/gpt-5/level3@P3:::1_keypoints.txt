- Proposes a fast U-Net–style masking network (ResNet-50 encoder) to predict saliency masks in one forward pass instead of per-image iterative perturbation.  
- Training objective combines total variation (smoothness), area penalty (small masks), SSR-like term (preserve class confidence when masked), and SDR-like term (reduce confidence when salient region removed).  
- Uses randomized evidence removal (blur or noisy/color fill) to mitigate adversarial masking artifacts.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric: log product of crop area and class probability on a resized crop.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, reporting weakly supervised localization and speedups over iterative methods.  
- Related to perturbation-based explanations (Fong & Vedaldi) and CAM/backprop localization, but novel in learning a reusable masking model and unifying SSR/SDR.  
- Strength: clear problem framing and a compelling learning-to-explain idea.  
- Strength: substantial speed advantage (real-time) with visually sharp masks, practical for video/interactive use.  
- Strength: thoughtful artifact mitigation (TV penalty, randomized A).  
- Strength: empirically competitive weakly supervised localization and sensible qualitative differences across backbones.  
- Strength: SSR/SDR-inspired objective is intuitive and end-to-end optimizable.  
- Weakness: evaluation proxies saliency indirectly (localization and proposed metric) with clear shortcomings.  
- Weakness: localization is not equivalent to saliency—contextual cues can confound localization as a proxy.  
- Weakness: proposed metric resizes crops without preserving aspect ratio, introducing scale/aspect distortions and potential artifacts.  
- Weakness: metric favors tiny masks blown up at test time, enabling gaming via limited scale invariance.  
- Weakness: strong dependence on classifier properties (differentiability and scale/aspect invariance) limits applicability and contradicts “black-box” claims.  
- Weakness: method unsuitable for use during classifier training and fragile in domains with rigid coordinates or lacking invariance.  
- Weakness: masking-network architecture dependence not systematically studied; results may reflect inductive bias of U-Net.  
- Weakness: limited ablations on objective weights, evidence removal schemes, resolution, and architecture choices.  
- Weakness: metric design choices (area floor, log formulation) are ad hoc and lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully designed, but evaluation is limited and metrics can be gamed.  
- Clarity: generally clear; requests clarification on Table 1 class computation and exact LRP variant/parameters used.  
- Originality: novel and useful combination—reusable saliency network plus SSR+SDR objective.  
- Significance: practically valuable for speed and conceptually interesting, but limited confidence due to evaluation and metric issues.  
- Requests: report masking-network backbone ablations; clarify gradient access/black-box requirements; test aspect-ratio-preserving cropping and original-image top-1 drop; provide area–confidence curves and A ablations; specify Table 1 computation details.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors’ rebuttal reinforced strong dependence on masking network and classifier invariances, further limiting generality.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical fast saliency, novel learning-to-explain approach, competitive localization.  
- Cons for acceptance: indirect/flawed evaluation, metric gaming potential, heavy dependence on classifier invariances and masking design, overstated black-box claim.  
- Recommendation: borderline—would accept if limitations and scope are clearly and prominently discussed; otherwise reject.  
- Reviewer confidence: moderate.
