- Proposes a novel trainable masking model for image saliency detection applicable to any differentiable classifier.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming other weakly supervised methods on ImageNet localization.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric to evaluate saliency map quality.  
- Uses a U-Net with a pre-trained ResNet encoder to produce high-quality saliency masks in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough related work discussion.  
- Method builds on the promising learning-to-explain direction but is not wholly novel conceptually.  
- Saliency metric is flawed: cropping/rescaling can introduce classification artifacts and favors small regions that can be overly blown up.  
- Dependence of the masking model on architecture is not analyzed or discussed.  
- Applicability is limited by reliance on classifier quality and scale invariance, challenging claims of generality to black-box classifiers.  
- Cannot be used during training or for models/domains lacking invariance (e.g., spectrograms), limiting scope.  
- Rebuttal leaves uncertainty whether visualizations reflect the target (U-)network or the masking network.  
- Overall scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, strong empirical results, clear writing and related work coverage.  
- Rejection arguments: flawed metric, missing analysis of architectural robustness, limited applicability, and visualization ambiguity.
