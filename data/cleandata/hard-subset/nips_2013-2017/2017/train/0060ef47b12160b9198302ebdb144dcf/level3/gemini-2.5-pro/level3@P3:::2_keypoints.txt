- Introduces a novel, model-based approach: train a U-Net "masking model" to predict saliency masks for any differentiable image classifier in a single forward pass.  
- Training objective enforces smallest sufficient region (SSR), destructive removal (SDR), and mask smoothness to produce meaningful saliency.  
- Amortizes per-instance optimization into network weights, achieving real-time inference (100+ images/sec) while retaining high-quality maps.  
- Contrasts with prior methods: faster than iterative optimization and less noisy than gradient-based approaches.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 using AlexNet, GoogLeNet, and ResNet-50, showing superior weakly-supervised localization and near supervised performance.  
- Well-written paper with a clear, well-justified objective and a new saliency metric for better interpretability assessment.  
- Demonstrates generalizability: works across datasets, architectures, and can be trained from scratch (CIFAR-10).  
- Weakness: real-time application motivation is underdeveloped—needs concrete use cases (e.g., video saliency, interactive debugging, safety-critical systems).  
- Weakness: missed opportunity for deeper qualitative analysis comparing how different classifiers use features or reveal dataset biases.  
- Weakness: "black box" terminology is imprecise since training requires backpropagation through the target; should clarify as "differentiable black box."  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and impactful work; recommend acceptance with suggested improvements.
