- Proposes a novel, efficient feed-forward masking network (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) to generate saliency masks for pre-trained black-box image classifiers in a single forward pass.  
- Contrasts with prior iterative optimization methods, offering a potentially much faster alternative.  
- Trains the masking model with a well-motivated objective balancing mask size, smoothness, and impact on classifier confidence.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on ImageNet weakly supervised localization and introduces a new saliency-quality metric.  
- Strength: original idea with significant potential to enable real-time saliency estimation.  
- Strength: paper is clear, provides good intuition for architecture and objective, and shows strong experimental performance.  
- Weakness: real-time speed claim (~100 images/sec) needs substantiation and clarity on whether preprocessing (e.g., resizing) is included.  
- Weakness: missing runtime measurements for larger, real-world image sizes (e.g., 640×480).  
- Weakness: reliance on 224×224 input may hurt detection of small salient objects and requires discussion.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could be improved—Table 3 should include the proposed saliency metric for other methods (e.g., [2]) for direct comparison.  
- Minor issues: manuscript needs proofreading and clearer phrasing (examples: LN 273 "More over" → "Moreover"; LN 151 wording about [3] optimizing the mask).
