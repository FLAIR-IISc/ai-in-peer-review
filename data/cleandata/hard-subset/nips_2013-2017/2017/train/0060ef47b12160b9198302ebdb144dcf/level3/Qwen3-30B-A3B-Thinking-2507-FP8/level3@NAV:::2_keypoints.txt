- Introduces a novel masking model for real-time saliency detection that predicts important image regions with a single forward pass.  
- Addresses computational inefficiency of iterative saliency methods, offering orders-of-magnitude speedup while maintaining or improving accuracy.  
- Builds on prior gradient-based and iterative saliency techniques but shifts to a model-based approach.  
- Proposes a well-motivated new saliency metric (Eq. 3) that provides a more nuanced evaluation than standard localization accuracy.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, outperforming state-of-the-art weakly supervised baselines.  
- Achieves 36.7% localization error on ResNet-50 and outperforms "center box" and iterative methods.  
- Strong technical execution with comprehensive experiments and clear problem formulation.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written with logical flow from motivation to evaluation.  
- Significant practical impact for real-time explainable AI systems.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of concrete real-time applications (e.g., autonomous vehicles, real-time video) to contextualize speed advantages.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of learned salient regions to assess exploitation of background/dataset biases (e.g., "snow" for "polar bear").  
- Overall recommendation: High-quality contribution; accept with minor revisions to strengthen application discussion and bias analysis.
