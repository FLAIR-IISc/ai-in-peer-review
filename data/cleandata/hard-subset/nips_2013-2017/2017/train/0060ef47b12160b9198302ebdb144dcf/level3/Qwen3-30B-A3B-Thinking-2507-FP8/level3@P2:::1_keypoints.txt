- Introduces a U-Net–based masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Proposes a new objective balancing mask smoothness, region size, classifier confidence on preserved regions, and confidence after salient region removal.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the tightest rectangular crop that preserves classification confidence (s(a,p)=log(ã)-log(p)).  
- Claims model-agnostic applicability to any differentiable image classifier and demonstrates generalization across ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Method is substantially faster than iterative approaches (100+ masks/sec), suitable for real-time/video applications.  
- U-Net architecture leverages multi-scale features for precise saliency maps and is well-designed for the task.  
- Weakly supervised object localization results are competitive with fully supervised baselines (e.g., ResNet vs. VGG).  
- The saliency metric is limited: cropping/rescaling can change aspect ratio/scale and introduce classification artifacts the metric ignores.  
- Weakly supervised localization can be misleading when classifiers rely on contextual cues (e.g., background) rather than object pixels.  
- Paper does not sufficiently explore dependence on masking network architecture or report experiments with alternative architectures.  
- Claim of applicability to “any differentiable classifier” is questionable because the method assumes classifier scale invariance.  
- Results in Table 1 lack clarity (unclear if averages are over all classes or only the correct class) and comparison details (which LRP variant) are missing.  
- Technical approach and experiments are sound, but evaluation methodology flaws weaken the claim of superior saliency maps.  
- Well-written and organized; U-Net and objective are clearly explained, though some training details are missing.  
- Novel contribution (learning-to-explain for real-time saliency) with practical significance, but broader impact is reduced by evaluation limitations.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on addressing metric limitations, contextual-cue issues, and clearly stating dependence on classifier properties; otherwise recommend rejection.
