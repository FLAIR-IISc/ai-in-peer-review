- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency detector that outputs interpretable saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time use.  
- Trains a masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 features) using an objective combining smoothness (TV), compactness (area), and classification fidelity (SSR/SDR).  
- Avoids iterative optimization used by prior work (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi, Zhou et al.) by using a trained model to generate masks.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the area/probability trade-off of the tightest rectangular crop containing the salient region.  
- Demonstrates competitive weakly supervised localization on ImageNet (36.7% error with ResNet-50) and superior interpretability metrics versus baselines and GT boxes.  
- Evaluates method across multiple black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and extends experiments to CIFAR-10.  
- Technical approach is sound: objective addresses adversarial artifacts, smoothness, and dual SSR/SDR goals.  
- Training strategy (30% fake labels, randomized evidence removal) effectively prevents overfitting to classifiers.  
- Experiments are comprehensive with ablation studies supporting the new metric’s validity.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear architecture and motivation; minor typos only.  
- Novelty: model-based saliency generation and the new metric are significant contributions distinguishing it from prior iterative or patch-removal methods.  
- Significance: real-time speed (~100 images/sec at 224×224) enables new applications (e.g., video/autonomous systems) and demonstrates practical impact.  
- Weakness: real-time claim lacks validation on larger resolutions (e.g., 640×480) needed for real-world use.  
- Weakness: performance on small objects (e.g., small birds) is unaddressed and may be problematic with 224×224 inputs.  
- Weakness: localization error (36.7%) still lags slightly behind fully supervised baselines (34.3% for VGG).  
- Weakness: Table 3 omits the proposed saliency metric for Fong & Vedaldi [2], preventing a complete comparison.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, original, well-validated work with minor revisions requested (larger-image runtimes, small-object analysis, add missing baseline metric).
