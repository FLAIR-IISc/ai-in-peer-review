- Proposes a novel, model-agnostic trainable masking model for image saliency that works with any differentiable classifier.  
- Predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications.  
- Trains the mask with an objective that enforces smoothness, smallness, and preservation of classification probability.  
- Thorough evaluation on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, ImageNet) showing improved localisation accuracy and saliency metrics over prior weakly supervised methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric for assessing explanation quality.  
- Strength: method is model-agnostic and broadly applicable to differentiable classifiers.  
- Strength: fast inference and suitable for real-time use.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of learned salient regions and category-specific/background biases.  
- Weakness: does not discuss potential applications (e.g., video saliency, autonomous vehicles).  
- Weakness: missing comparisons of computational complexity and memory requirements versus state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: makes a significant contribution and is worthy of acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses.
