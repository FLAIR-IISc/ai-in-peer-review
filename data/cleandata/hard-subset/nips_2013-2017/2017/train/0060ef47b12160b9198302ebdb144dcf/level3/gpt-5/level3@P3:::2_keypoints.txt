- Proposes a trainable masking U-Net that predicts class-specific salient image regions in a single forward pass.  
- Trains the mask end-to-end with a composite objective encouraging smooth, compact masks, high preserved-region confidence (SSR), and low removed-region confidence (SDR).  
- Mitigates introduced-evidence artifacts via randomized “alternative image” A and total variation regularization.  
- Architecture: class-conditional filter on a frozen ResNet-50 encoder, outputs soft mask at 1/4 resolution then upsamples.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (weakly supervised localization) and CIFAR-10; introduces a new saliency metric trading crop area vs. class probability.  
- Positions method between fast gradient-based saliency and slow optimization-based deletion/preservation methods by amortizing optimization into a learned predictor.  
- Reports stronger empirical performance than iterative baselines and competitive weakly supervised localization, running >100 fps on 224×224 inputs.  
- Technical formulation is clear and well-motivated; combining SSR and SDR is an elegant contribution.  
- New saliency metric is reasonable for “information concentration” but depends on classifier calibration and resizing choices could bias comparisons.  
- Recommended additional analyses: ablations on loss weights (λ1–λ4), form of A, mask resolution, and mask nonlinearity.  
- Suggested sensitivity studies on metric calibration (e.g., temperature scaling) and reporting calibrated probabilities.  
- Request cross-model generalization experiments (masking model trained on one backbone explaining others or multi-backbone model).  
- Clarify “black box” terminology and explicitly state classifier access assumptions, since training uses classifier gradients.  
- Ask for more rigorous runtime profiling across GPUs/CPUs and input resolutions (latency, throughput, memory).  
- Clarity improvements: add a concise threat model for introduced evidence, define metric intuition before formulas, and state that a separate mask model is trained per classifier.  
- Originality: amortizing deletion/preservation into a learned, class-conditional U-Net with SSR+SDR and randomized evidence-removal is novel and useful.  
- Significance: real-time, high-quality saliency maps are practically important and could enable video/interactive explainability and on-device use cases.  
- Recommend motivating concrete real-time applications and adding dataset-level analyses of learned masks to reveal contextual biases.  
- Pros: clear objective with defenses against introduced evidence, strong ImageNet results, real-time throughput, broadly applicable to differentiable classifiers.  
- Cons: requires per-classifier mask training, “black box” wording is misleading, limited ablations and calibration analysis, sparse runtime reporting, limited bias/failure analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper leaning toward acceptance pending additional ablations, clearer access assumptions, and richer bias and runtime analyses.
