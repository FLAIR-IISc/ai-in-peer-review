- Proposes a learned masking model that predicts image saliency in a single forward pass for any differentiable classifier.
- Provides a substantial speed advantage over iterative/optimization-based saliency methods.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results in weakly supervised object localization on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on Smallest Sufficient/Destroying Regions for evaluating explanations beyond localization accuracy.
- Positions the approach relative to gradient-based and optimization/iterative methods, notably addressing Fong & Vedaldi’s optimization bottleneck.
- Validates experimentally using strong baselines and established networks (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).
- Shows generalizability to low-resolution data via CIFAR-10 experiments.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with detailed descriptions of the objective function and architecture.
- Lacks stronger motivation for real-time applications; video-saliency mention should be expanded with concrete scenarios (e.g., autonomous driving, visual debugging).
- Needs deeper analysis of learned saliency maps to identify category-specific background biases (e.g., polar bear/snow).
- Should investigate adversarial artifacts in more detail despite applying mitigation strategies.
- Acceptance arguments: technically sound, experimentally validated, novel and efficient, outperforms existing methods, and clearly presented.
- Rejection concerns: insufficient articulation of real-time use-cases and limited analysis of saliency biases.
