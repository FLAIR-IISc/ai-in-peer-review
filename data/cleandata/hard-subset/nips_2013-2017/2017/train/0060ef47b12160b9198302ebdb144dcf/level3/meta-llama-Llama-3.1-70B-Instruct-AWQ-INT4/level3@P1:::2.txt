This paper introduces a novel approach to image saliency detection, which is a crucial aspect of understanding how image classifiers work. The authors propose a fast and accurate model-based saliency detection method that can be applied to any differentiable image classifier. The method involves training a masking model to manipulate the scores of the classifier by masking salient parts of the input image. The model generalizes well to unseen images and requires a single forward pass to perform saliency detection, making it suitable for real-time systems.

The authors provide a comprehensive review of existing saliency detection methods, highlighting their limitations and the need for a more efficient and effective approach. They also introduce a new metric for saliency, which assesses the quality of the produced saliency maps by evaluating the classifier's ability to recognize the object from the preserved region.

The paper presents extensive experimental results on the CIFAR-10 and ImageNet datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors also compare their approach to other weakly supervised methods and existing saliency detection techniques, showing that their method outperforms these approaches in terms of localisation accuracy and saliency metric.

The strengths of this paper include:

* A well-written and clear presentation of the proposed method and its underlying principles.
* Extensive experimental results on challenging datasets using state-of-the-art networks.
* The introduction of a new metric for saliency, which provides a more comprehensive evaluation of the produced saliency maps.
* The potential for real-time applications, such as video-saliency detection, which could be useful in various domains, including autonomous vehicles.

However, there are some potential weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the learned salient regions, such as which categories exploit background biases.
* The authors could provide more specific applications that require real-time efficiency, highlighting the practical implications of their method.
* The paper assumes that the classifier is differentiable, which might not always be the case. The authors could discuss the limitations of their approach in this regard.
* The proposed method relies on a pre-trained ResNet-50 model, which might introduce biases into the generated saliency maps. The authors could investigate the impact of using different pre-trained models or training the masking model from scratch.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to image saliency detection, which combines the strengths of existing methods with a new metric for saliency. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of existing methods, highlighting the need for a more efficient and effective approach.

The significance of this paper lies in its potential to advance the state of the art in image saliency detection, providing a more accurate and efficient method for understanding how image classifiers work. The paper's contributions could have a significant impact on various applications, including autonomous vehicles, medical imaging, and surveillance systems.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach to image saliency detection.
* The authors provide extensive experimental results on challenging datasets using state-of-the-art networks.
* The paper introduces a new metric for saliency, which provides a more comprehensive evaluation of the produced saliency maps.
* The potential for real-time applications makes the paper relevant to various domains.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes that the classifier is differentiable, which might not always be the case.
* The proposed method relies on a pre-trained ResNet-50 model, which might introduce biases into the generated saliency maps.
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the learned salient regions.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of image saliency detection, providing a novel and effective approach that has the potential to advance the state of the art. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, making it a strong candidate for acceptance.
