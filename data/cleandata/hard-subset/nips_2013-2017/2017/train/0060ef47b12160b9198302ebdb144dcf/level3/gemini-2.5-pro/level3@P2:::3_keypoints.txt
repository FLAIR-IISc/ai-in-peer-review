- Proposes training a U-Net–style "masking model" to generate saliency maps for any differentiable classifier in a single forward pass, replacing per-image iterative optimization.  
- Training objective combines mask smoothness (TV), area minimization (AV), and dual sufficiency/destructiveness terms (SSR and SDR).  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on classification confidence within the tightest rectangular crop around the salient region.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, producing sharp, interpretable maps and state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization.  
- Claims real-time inference speed of >100 images/sec, making the method suitable for interactive or streaming applications.  
- Motivation and technical design are compelling and well-justified; amortized inference shifts cost from test time to training.  
- Architectural choice (U-Net with pretrained ResNet-50 encoder) is appropriate for high-resolution mask generation from multi-scale features.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides insightful discussion on “introduced evidence” and saliency evaluation challenges.  
- Novelty lies in learning to predict saliency for a black-box model, significantly changing the speed-quality trade-off of perturbation methods.  
- Significance includes enabling real-time interpretability use cases (video, debugging) and contributing a new interpretable evaluation metric.  
- Strength: elegant, practical solution that achieves impressive empirical results on a challenging benchmark.  
- Strength: clear presentation and thoughtful experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and models.  
- Weakness: the >100 fps claim lacks context (e.g., preprocessing, larger input resolutions like 640×480).  
- Weakness: potential limitations on detecting small objects due to experiments at 224×224 and downsampling effects.  
- Weakness: incomplete validation of the new metric—comparisons omit key competing methods from Table 2.  
- Minor comment: wording about Fong & Vedaldi [2] could be more precise regarding their objective; minor typo noted.  
- Overall: reviewer recommends acceptance—core contribution is strong and addressable weaknesses do not outweigh merits.
