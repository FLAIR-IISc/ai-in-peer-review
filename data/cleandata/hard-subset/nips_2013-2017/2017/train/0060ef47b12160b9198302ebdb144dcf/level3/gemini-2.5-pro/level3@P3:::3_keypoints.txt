- Proposes a masking model that predicts saliency masks in a single forward pass for black-box image classifiers, avoiding slow iterative optimization.
- Uses a U-Net-like architecture with a pretrained ResNet-50 encoder and a composite loss promoting smooth, small masks that are sufficient and destroying (SSR and SDR).
- Introduces a new saliency metric measuring the trade-off between bounding-box area around the mask and classifier score on the cropped region.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, applicable to AlexNet, GoogLeNet, and ResNet-50.
- Strength: Novelty and efficiency—amortizes costly optimization and claims ~100 224x224 images/sec, making it relevant for real-time use.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—outperforms other weakly supervised methods (localization error 36.7% with ResNet-50).
- Strength: Generalizability—method is model-agnostic and works across multiple classifier architectures.
- Strength: Clarity and presentation—well-written, motivated, and supported by effective qualitative figures.
- Weakness: Real-time speed claim needs clarification (does reported speed include preprocessing/resizing?).
- Weakness: Unclear runtimes on larger, realistic image sizes (e.g., 640×480 or full HD) relevant for video saliency.
- Weakness: Potential poor performance on small objects due to fixed 224x224 input and downsampling; needs analysis versus bounding-box area.
- Weakness: Incomplete experimental comparison—new saliency metric should be reported for more baselines (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi, Zhou et al.) for fair comparison.
- Minor: Correct wording about Fong & Vedaldi’s contribution (they propose a general meaningful perturbation framework).
- Minor: Typo—“More over” should be “Moreover”.
- Overall: High-quality, technically sound, and influential work; concerns are clarifications/future work rather than fundamental flaws.
