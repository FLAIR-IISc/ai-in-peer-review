- Proposes an amortized approach: train a separate masking model to produce saliency maps in one forward pass, trading training cost for real-time inference.  
- Masking model: U-Net with ResNet encoder trained to produce small, smooth masks that act as both "smallest sufficient region" (SSR) and "smallest destroying region" (SDR).  
- Contrasts with prior work: faster than iterative optimization methods and argued to produce higher-quality maps than fast gradient-based methods.  
- Empirical evaluation: thorough experiments on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet using multiple pretrained classifiers, showing strong performance.  
- State-of-the-art: achieves clear improvement on weakly-supervised object localization, establishing a new benchmark.  
- Generalizability: experiments on a model trained from scratch (CIFAR-10) suggest method generalizes beyond ImageNet-pretrained encoders.  
- Well-motivated objective: combines sufficiency and destructiveness with smoothness regularizers (Eq. 4) and is clearly described.  
- Presentation: paper is exceptionally well written, with clear motivation, method description, and compelling qualitative figures.  
- Application motivation weak: real-time capability is emphasized but practical application scenarios (beyond a brief mention) are underdeveloped.  
- Lack of analysis: misses deeper interpretability analysis of classifiers (e.g., systematic biases or context reliance) that the maps could reveal.  
- Training overhead: requires training a new masking model per black-box classifier, a notable practical limitation compared to out-of-the-box methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and impactful formulation, strong empirical results, real-time performance, and solid technical rigor and presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation/examples for real-time need and missed opportunity to use the method to analyze classifier behavior.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, novel, and impactful work with strengths outweighing weaknesses; clear candidate for acceptance.
