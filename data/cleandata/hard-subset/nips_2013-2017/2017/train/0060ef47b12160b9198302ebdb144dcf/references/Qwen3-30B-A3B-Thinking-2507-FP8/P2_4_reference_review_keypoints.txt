- Proposes a trainable masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time saliency of black-box classifiers and introduces a new SSR-based saliency metric.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50) and CIFAR-10, showing improved SSR scores and localization accuracy versus existing methods.  
- Novelty: replaces iterative optimization with a learned masker, explicitly optimizing SSR and SDR objectives and distinguishing from parallel work by Fong & Vedaldi.  
- Introduces a useful saliency metric (Eq.3) that measures the smallest cropped region that preserves classifier confidence, reducing conflation with mere localization.  
- Technical soundness: U-Net masking architecture, random image manipulations to mitigate introduced evidence, and a 30% fake-class selector to enforce class-conditioned masks.  
- Experimental rigor: comprehensive baselines, multiple architectures, cross-dataset validation, and competitive localization performance (ResNet-50 model near supervised VGG).  
- Practical significance: real-time speed (~100 masks/sec), enabling video and real-world applications like autonomous driving; black-box applicability is valuable.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of potential biases introduced by the masking model itself and how they affect explanations.  
- Weakness: claim of being “model agnostic” is overstated given reliance on a ResNet-50 encoder pre-trained on ImageNet; CIFAR-10 results partially mitigate but don’t fully justify the claim.  
- Weakness: limited direct empirical comparison to Fong & Vedaldi on computational efficiency and saliency quality.  
- Weakness: new metric assumes classifier invariance to scale/aspect ratio; paper does not fully address metric limitations or sensitivity to such factors.  
- Overall: high-quality, original, and significant contribution with rigorous experiments; minor issues do not undermine the paper’s value.  
- Recommendation: accept.
