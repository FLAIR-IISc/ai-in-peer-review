- Summary: Proposes a model-based U-Net masking model to predict saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass, enabling real-time saliency and introducing a new SSR-based metric s(a,p).

- Strength: Addresses the computational bottleneck of iterative saliency methods with a practical, real-time solution.

- Strength: Technically strong masking model design (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) capturing multi-scale features for precise saliency.

- Strength: Introduces a useful saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)/log(p) linking salient region size to classifier confidence.

- Strength: Thorough experiments and ablations on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 showing better quality and much faster speed than baselines.

- Strength: Practical impact demonstrated (100+ masks/sec) enabling applications like video saliency for autonomous systems.

- Strength: Careful handling of adversarial artifacts with multiple techniques to prevent introduced evidence in saliency maps.

- Weakness: Limited discussion on limitations of training the masking model per black-box classifier and cross-classifier degradation.

- Weakness: Training complexity with many heuristics (fake labels, evidence removal schemes) lacks detailed ablation on each choice.

- Weakness: No direct empirical comparison of speed and quality with parallel work [2] developed concurrently.

- Weakness: Potential bias in masking model not analyzed, especially interactions with known classifier biases (e.g., snow/polar bear).

- Weakness: Vague implementation benchmarks—says “more than a hundred” masks/sec without specific comparative numbers.

- Quality: Technically sound with clear objective, architecture, and comprehensive validation across datasets and baselines.

- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized; figures effectively illustrate the approach and results.

- Clarity: Some explanations need more intuition—evidence removal function (Eq. 6) and mask smoothness/adversarial artifact discussion.

- Clarity: Training process would benefit from a visual pipeline and more implementation details (e.g., CIFAR-10 hyperparameters).

- Originality: Novel model-based real-time saliency approach and metric; differentiates from prior iterative and related model-based work.

- Significance: High practical and theoretical significance—enables real-time applications and advances saliency evaluation.

- Recommendation: Accept — strong contribution, well-validated, weaknesses not severe enough to reject.

- Suggestion: Add direct comparison with parallel work [2] for speed and quality.

- Suggestion: Detail limitations when masking model is trained on one classifier but applied to another.

- Suggestion: Discuss how the masking model could inherit or amplify classifier biases with examples.

- Suggestion: Provide exact benchmark numbers for speed on standard GPUs for clearer comparisons.

- Suggestion: Include more hyperparameter and training-detail information for CIFAR-10 and ablations on training heuristics.

- Overall Assessment: Well-executed, significant contribution to interpretability with real-time saliency and a valuable new metric; suitable for NeurIPS.
