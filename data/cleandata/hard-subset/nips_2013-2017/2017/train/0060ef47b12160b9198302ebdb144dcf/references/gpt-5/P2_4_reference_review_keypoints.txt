- Proposes a trainable, model-agnostic (per-differentiable-classifier) saliency U-Net that outputs per-class masks in a single forward pass.  
- Optimizes masks with a combined objective: smoothness/area penalty, Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR), and Smallest Destroying Region (SDR).  
- Mitigates adversarial masking artifacts via alternative-image masking (blur/noise) and total variation regularization.  
- Introduces a “saliency metric” based on classification confidence on the tightest crop containing the salient region.  
- Architecture/training heuristics (ResNet-50 encoder, class embeddings, fake labels, alternative-image randomization) are sensible and reproducible in principle.  
- Empirically produces sharp, localized masks, competitive weakly supervised ImageNet localization (36.7% error for ResNet-50 black box) and high throughput (~100 fps).  
- Claims of “black box/model-agnostic” are overstated: training requires differentiability and backprop through the classifier’s scores.  
- The saliency metric is underspecified and likely incorrect (formula appears to be a product instead of a ratio); numerical stability and the 0.05 area floor need justification.  
- Lacks critical ablations: contributions of SSR vs SDR vs TV, masking strategy (blur vs noise), and effect of training tricks (fake label frequency).  
- Comparison breadth is limited; needs direct, quantitative baselines (CAM/Grad-CAM/Excitation Backprop) under identical protocols.  
- Generalization claim weak: a separate mask model is trained per classifier and cross-architecture or unseen-class generalization is not evaluated.  
- Key reproducibility details are missing (optimizer, learning rate schedule, data augmentation, training split).  
- Clarity issues and typos: fix saliency equation, loss notation (Eq.4), and ambiguous hyperparameter formatting.  
- Need to clarify bounding-box derivation from masks (threshold selection/tuning) and behavior on multi-object/multi-instance images.  
- Contribution is moderately novel: amortizing perturbation-based saliency via a learned U-Net with SSR/SDR is a useful combination of known components.  
- Significance is promising if claims hold: practical, fast perturbation-grounded explanations useful for large-scale/video settings and closer-to-segmentation masks.  
- Suggested improvements: correct and analyze the metric, add loss/masking/training ablations, include direct comparisons to strong baselines, qualify black-box claims, and provide full training details and code.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—timely and useful idea that needs clarified metric and stronger empirical support.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
