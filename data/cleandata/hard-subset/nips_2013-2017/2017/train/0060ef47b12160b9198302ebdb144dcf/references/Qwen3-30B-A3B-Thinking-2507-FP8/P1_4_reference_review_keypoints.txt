- Proposes a trainable U-Net masking model for real-time saliency maps for black-box classifiers, replacing costly iterative methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric s(a,p) based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) to better evaluate interpretability.  
- Carefully addresses "introduced evidence" when manipulating images, using randomized removal and TV penalties to prevent artifacts.  
- Claims model-agnostic performance and achieves state-of-the-art weakly supervised localization on ImageNet while being orders of magnitude faster.  
- Significant practical impact: enables 100+ saliency maps per second, opening applications like video saliency and real-time debugging.  
- Novel metric aligns better with saliency theory and yields results comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Rigorous artifact treatment is a notable strength, addressing a common oversight in prior work.  
- Strong experimental results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, including competitive localization error rates without pretraining.  
- Insufficient differentiation from concurrent work (Fong & Vedaldi 2017); needs clearer methodological and metric-based comparisons.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why a trained masking model outperforms iterative optimization.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to ImageNet and CIFAR-10; more datasets and failure-case analysis are needed.  
- Missing implementation details (training procedure, hyperparameters, exact hardware) to substantiate speed claims.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to practical contribution and strong results.  
- Required revisions: clarify differences from Fong & Vedaldi, provide theoretical justification, discuss limitations/failure cases, and add implementation/training details.
