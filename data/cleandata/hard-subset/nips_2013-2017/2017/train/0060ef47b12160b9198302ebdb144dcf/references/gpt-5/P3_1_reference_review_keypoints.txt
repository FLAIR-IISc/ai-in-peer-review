- Proposes an amortized, feed-forward U-Net “masking model” that predicts class-conditional saliency maps to replace slow per-image perturbation optimization.
- Loss combines TV smoothness, area penalty, SSR (preserve salient region), and SDR (drop class when removing salient region) terms.
- Mitigates introduced-evidence artifacts by filling masked regions with random alternatives (blur/noise) and enforcing smooth masks.
- Proposes a cropping-based evaluation metric combining crop area and class log-probability.
- Empirical results show sharper masks, competitive weakly supervised localization on ImageNet, and fast real-time throughput.
- Clear and appealing idea: model-agnostic, SSR+SDR objectives, real-time inference.
- Thoughtful handling of introduced evidence and a cropping metric less sensitive to masking artifacts.
- Solid architecture (U-Net with class embedding/gating) and some evidence of cross-class/dataset generalization; CIFAR-10 trained from scratch reduces pretraining reliance.
- Empirically competitive localization, nearing fully supervised VGG performance, and improved saliency metric over baselines.
- Claims of “black-box”/model-agnosticism are misleading because training requires gradients through the target classifier; clarify access requirements.
- Equation (4) and loss formulation are ambiguous (coefficients/exponents/signs unclear) and must be corrected and justified.
- Limited quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (CAM/Grad-CAM, Guided Backprop, Excitation Backprop) and standard metrics (localization error, pointing game, deletion/insertion).
- Missing ablations for loss components (SSR vs SDR), alternative A choices, TV weight, class-embedding gating, and mask resolution.
- Runtime comparisons vs iterative methods are asserted but lack wall-clock numbers on comparable hardware.
- Proposed metric s(a,p) is underspecified (product vs difference of logs) and not analyzed for sensitivity to resizing, aspect ratio, or classifier differences.
- Portability is unclear: model must be trained per classifier and transfer to other target nets is not demonstrated.
- “Real-time” needs clarification whether timing includes only mask prediction or also target classifier inference.
- Presentation issues: typos, notational inconsistencies, missing operators, and incomplete training details hinder reproducibility.
- Evaluation bias: most tests condition on ground-truth class rather than the model’s predicted class; no human studies or failure analyses.
- Technically sound at a high level but loss under-specified and empirical mitigation of artifacts lacks theoretical grounding.
- Readable overall but key equations, training details, and experimental setups need fixing and full disclosure.
- Originality moderate: novelty lies in amortizing perturbation-based saliency with combined SSR/SDR and random alternatives, but relation to prior work and black-box claims need clearer positioning.
- Significance promising if validated with stronger baselines and ablations; could be useful for practitioners and video explanations.
- Suggestions: fix Eq. 4 and justify terms, state classifier access assumptions, report wall-clock runtimes and deletion/insertion curves, compare to CAM/Grad-CAM/etc., ablate components, test predicted-class explanations and transfer, analyze metric robustness, and release code/models.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (borderline) pending objective fixes, clearer black-box claims, and stronger empirical comparisons.
