- Proposal: trainable masking model that predicts class-conditional saliency maps in a single forward pass for a fixed classifier.  
- Objective: encourages both smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR) with smoothness and small-area penalties.  
- Artifact mitigation: mix inputs with heavy blur or random color+noise and enforce total-variation regularization.  
- Architecture: class-gated U-Net with frozen ResNet-50 encoder and learned class embedding at coarsest level.  
- Training target: trained against the classifier’s softmax outputs (requires differentiability).  
- New metric: saliency score s(a,p)=log(ã)−log(p) using area of tightest bounding box and classifier probability on the crop.  
- Empirics: improved weakly supervised localization on ImageNet, better SSR-like metric vs baselines, CIFAR-10 training-from-scratch experiment, and real-time inference.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on meaningful perturbations, occlusion/patch-removal, and backprop saliency; amortizes optimization into a learned predictor (meta-learning flavor).  
- Missing comparison: no direct comparisons to CAM/Grad-CAM despite relevance.  
- Strength — novelty: amortizing saliency optimization into a fast predictor is conceptually novel and useful for real-time/video.  
- Strength — objective design: combines SSR and SDR, randomized evidence-removal, and fake-class training to encourage class conditioning.  
- Strength — architecture/results: simple class-gated U-Net yields sharp high-resolution masks and competitive localization.  
- Strength — metric: crop-based SSR metric is intuitive and complementary to localization evaluation.  
- Weakness — "black-box" claim: overstated — training needs backprop through classifier and a separate masker per classifier.  
- Weakness — missing baselines/ablations: no CAM/Grad-CAM, no loss-term or class-embedding ablations, no study of mask thresholding or A choices.  
- Weakness — missing fidelity metrics: no deletion/insertion curves or pointing-game evaluations to assess faithfulness.  
- Weakness — unclear details: ambiguous Equation 4 formatting/coefficients, unclear definition of “tightest” crop from soft masks and threshold tuning.  
- Weakness — generalization claim unclear: model’s post-training independence holds only for that specific classifier; cross-architecture/dataset transfer untested.  
- Weakness — robustness/failure modes: limited analysis of adversarial/artifact robustness and whether the model regularizes against adversarial masks.  
- Weakness — training cost: favorable test-time speed but nontrivial training cost and no train-vs-test trade-off discussion.  
- Quality: technically sound with thoughtful objective and artifact mitigations, but empirical evaluation incomplete and some implementation details under-specified.  
- Clarity: generally readable with good intuition, but mathematical/experimental ambiguities and inconsistent reference formatting hinder reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel combination — amortized meaningful perturbation predictor and SSR-inspired metric — though components are known.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for fast, high-resolution explanations, especially in video, but significance depends on adding baselines and fidelity tests.  
- Questions for authors: clarify Equation 4 (λs and exponent), define crop extraction from soft masks and threshold sensitivity, compare to CAM/Grad-CAM and occlusion, report deletion/insertion, and provide multiple ablations and transfer experiments.  
- Recommendation summary: weak accept conditional on added comparisons/clarifications; otherwise borderline.
