- Proposal: amortized feed-forward U-Net “masking model” that outputs perturbation-based saliency masks in one pass instead of per-image optimization.  
- Training objective: combines TV smoothness, area penalty (AV), recognizability from preserved region (SSR), and suppressibility when removed (SDR), with randomized replacement images to reduce artifacts.  
- New metric: crop tightest rectangle covering the mask and compute s(a,p)=log(ã)/log(p) to reward small crops with high classifier confidence.  
- Experiments: evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet-50 as fixed classifiers) and CIFAR-10, showing competitive weakly supervised localization, sharp maps, and >100 FPS.  
- Positioning: bridges gradient/backprop attribution and optimization-based perturbation methods; novelty is amortizing perturbation-based saliency with a learned predictor.  
- Missing baselines: omits comparisons to widely used Grad-CAM and Integrated Gradients, making significance hard to assess.  
- Strength: conceptually novel and practical (orders-of-magnitude speedup), with thoughtful artifact mitigation (randomized replacements, TV, down/up masks) and strong qualitative results.  
- Strength: clear articulation of SSR vs SDR and combined training objective targeting both.  
- Claim issue: “black-box”/model-agnostic claim is overstated—method requires backprop through a differentiable fixed classifier.  
- Objective bug: Eq. (4) appears to have a sign error (should encourage recognizability but equation as written pushes classifier output toward zero).  
- Metric flaw: s(a,p)=log(ã)/log(p) is ill-posed and promotes low p; the ratio’s behavior contradicts the stated SSR goal and risks perverse incentives.  
- Metric bias: reducing masks to tight rectangles disadvantages multi-part objects and introduces background/scale/aspect-ratio biases.  
- Evaluation issues: localization derived from threshold=0.5 without sensitivity analysis; metric depends on classifier invariance to crop/aspect ratio not examined.  
- Potential overfitting/bias: masking model may learn ImageNet dataset priors (fixed ResNet encoder) rather than the target classifier’s idiosyncrasies; limited cross-dataset testing.  
- Missing ablations: no studies on λ weights, TV strength, fake-class probability, replacement A choices, or quantitative artifact-reduction analysis.  
- Clarity issues: conflicting equations (3) and (4), informal descriptions of training tricks, and ad hoc mask nonlinearity lacking justification and hyperparameter details.  
- Quality assessment: broadly technically sound but requires correction of objective/metric errors and stronger baselines/ablations to validate claims.  
- Originality assessment: amortized perturbation approach is novel and timely; SSR/SDR combo sensible; crop-based metric idea is interesting but currently flawed.  
- Significance: could be a practical advance for fast class-conditional saliency and localization if validated with corrections and stronger evaluation.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical amortization, strong qualitative/localization results, artifact mitigation, real-time potential.  
- Cons for acceptance: incorrect signs in objective/metric, overstated black-box claims, missing key baselines/ablations, and unjustified metric/evaluation choices.  
- Suggestions: correct Eq.(3)/(4) and metric, add Grad-CAM/Integrated Gradients baselines, perform λ/TV/replacement/threshold ablations, test multi-object and non-present-class specificity, report single-image latency.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept if objective/metric are fixed and stronger baselines/ablations added; otherwise methodological and evaluation gaps limit confidence.
