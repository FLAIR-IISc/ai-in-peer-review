- Proposes a learned model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time explanation of black-box classifiers.
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient/destroying regions (SSR/SDR).
- Shows competitive performance on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 for object localization and saliency quality.
- Properly positions itself relative to gradient-based and iterative patch-removal methods, emphasizing end-to-end learning.
- Builds on prior SDR/SSR ideas and improves handling of adversarial artifacts.
- Major strength: substantial speedup (single forward pass) suitable for real-time use.
- Novelty: first learned saliency detector moving away from iterative optimization.
- The SSR/SDR-based metric is a thoughtful attempt to formalize “good” saliency.
- Experimental results are promising and demonstrate generalizability (including CIFAR-10).
- Addresses adversarial artifacts using blurring, random noise, and a TV penalty to improve map quality.
- Paper is well-written and uses helpful figures to illustrate concepts.
- Weakness: limited ablation study—insufficient analysis of objective components and choices like alternative image A.
- Weakness: reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder raises questions about encoder vs. U-Net contributions.
- Weakness: proposed saliency metric depends on the underlying classifier, limiting classifier-independent interpretability.
- Weakness: multiple hyperparameters in the objective may require extensive tuning; tuning process not discussed.
- Weakness: presentation of results is somewhat crowded, particularly tables 1–3.
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound, promising experiments, and addresses an important interpretability problem.
- Against acceptance: encoder dependence and limited ablation weaken analysis and generality of the metric.
- Overall: reviewer recommends strong accept; rates the paper 7/10.
