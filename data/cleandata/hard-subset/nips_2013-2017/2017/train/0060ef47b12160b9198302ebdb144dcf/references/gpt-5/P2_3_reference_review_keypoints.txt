- Proposes a fast, model-driven saliency method: train a U-Net masking network with ResNet-50 encoder and class-embedding feature filter to predict saliency in one forward pass.  
- Training objective jointly enforces small/smooth masks, sufficiency (preserved region yields high class score), and destructiveness (removing region suppresses class).  
- Mitigates "introduced evidence" via randomized alternative image A (blur vs color+noise), TV regularization, and up/downsampling to encourage low-frequency masks.  
- Introduces a saliency metric based on log-area of the tightest crop containing the salient region and classifier confidence on that crop.  
- Reports sharp, interpretable maps, real-time inference (~100 images/s), improved weakly-supervised localization and saliency scores, and CIFAR-10 generalization.  
- Strength: practical single-pass saliency generator useful for video and interactive analysis.  
- Strength: well-motivated objective balancing sufficiency and destructiveness with defenses against introduced evidence and adversarial artifacts.  
- Strength: sensible architecture and training tricks (class-conditioned filtering, fake-labels) with reported empirical gains.  
- Weakness: "model-agnostic/black-box" claim is overstated—training requires differentiating through the classifier, so gradients must be available.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies on thresholding and bounding-box proxies; the new metric is bespoke and needs validation against human judgments and invariance issues.  
- Weakness: missing important baselines (Grad-CAM, CAM, Meaningful Perturbations, RISE-like methods) and quantitative ablations of loss terms and regularization.  
- Weakness: equation typos/sign ambiguities (Eqs. 3–4) and truncated references hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness: training cost, backbone sensitivity, cross-model transferability, and robustness to adversarial/texture artifacts are not studied.  
- Quality: technically plausible and coherent but needs corrected equations and more comprehensive ablations/baselines.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept (leaning weak accept) pending fixes to clarity, stronger baselines, ablations, and metric validation.
