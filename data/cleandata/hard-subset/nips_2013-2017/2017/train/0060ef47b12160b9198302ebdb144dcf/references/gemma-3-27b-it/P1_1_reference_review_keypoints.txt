- Summary: Proposes a learned feed-forward U-Net to produce real-time saliency maps for black-box classifiers, introduces an SSR-based metric, and shows improved localization and metric performance on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 while addressing masking artifacts.

- Relation to previous work: Builds on gradient, iterative masking, and optimization-based saliency methods; closest to Fong & Vedaldi (2017) but differs by learning a direct predictor and combining SSR/SDR ideas.

- Novelty: Learning saliency maps directly (non-iterative) is a significant departure enabling real-time use.

- Performance: Demonstrates strong empirical gains in localization accuracy and the proposed metric versus existing techniques.

- Practicality: Real-time capability enables applications like video analysis and explainable AI for autonomous systems.

- Artifact mitigation: Authors identify and propose strategies to reduce introduced evidence when masking inputs.

- New metric: SSR-based saliency metric is a valuable, information-theory-motivated addition to evaluation beyond localization.

- Thorough experimentation: Tests on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 indicate generalizability.

- Objective complexity: Loss (Eq. 4) has multiple components and hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) without sufficient ablation or sensitivity analysis.

- Architecture choice: U-Net choice is justified but alternatives and reasons for rejection are not discussed in depth.

- Pre-training dependence: Reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder may limit discovery of novel saliency patterns beyond ImageNet features.

- Failure modes: Paper lacks detailed analysis and examples of failure cases and explanations for poor saliency maps.

- Metric interpretation: The SSR metric’s relationship to human perception and when lower scores may not be preferable is not fully explored.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, practical real-time impact, artifact handling, and a useful new metric.

- Arguments against acceptance: Requires careful tuning of a complex objective, may over-rely on pre-training, and needs deeper failure-case analysis.

- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution to explainable AI with convincing results; some areas need clarification.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add objective hyperparameter ablation and a more detailed discussion of failure cases.
