- Proposes a fast, trainable feed-forward saliency detector that predicts per-pixel masks for a target class in one forward pass.
- Trains the mask generator with a combined objective for smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).
- Mitigates adversarial artifacts by randomizing the masking baseline (blur or random color+noise), adding a TV smoothness penalty, and down/up-sampling masks.
- Uses a U-Net-like architecture with a ResNet-50 encoder and class-conditional feature gating.
- Introduces a saliency metric s(a,p)=log(max(a,0.05))·log(p) to trade off preserved area and classifier confidence.
- Empirically achieves competitive weakly supervised localization on ImageNet and produces visually sharp masks.
- Shows applicability without ImageNet pretraining via CIFAR-10 experiments.
- Main novelty: learning a feed-forward approximation to iterative perturbation-based saliency and jointly training SSR/SDR.
- Methodology choices (randomized A, TV, class gating) are sensible and align with saliency desiderata.
- Concern: training appears to require gradient access to the classifier—contradicts “black box” claim; needs clarification.
- Concern: loss (Equation 4) is ambiguously typeset—unclear whether terms are powers, weights, or both.
- Concern: the proposed metric is ad hoc (rectangular crop, aspect-ratio distortion, 0.05 floor) and may favor compact high-confidence crops; sensitivity analysis needed.
- Missing comparisons to CAM/Grad-CAM (and Guided Grad-CAM) and need to ensure comparable thresholding/post-processing across methods.
- Missing ablations on random-A variants, fake-label probability, SSR vs SDR contributions, and TV weight to quantify each component’s effect.
- Runtime claims (100 fps) lack hardware, batch-size, preprocessing, and upsampling details; iterative baseline runtime also needed for fair comparison.
- Practicality concern: a separate saliency model per classifier limits plug-and-play use; transferability should be discussed or tested.
- Clarity issues: tighten notation for Φ(X,M), unambiguously state gradient access during training, and provide full post-processing details used for box derivation.
- Originality: moderate—novel feed-forward approximation and joint SSR/SDR, but uses standard architecture components.
- Significance: potentially impactful for real-time saliency and weakly supervised localization if stronger comparisons and ablations are added.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—promising approach but needs clearer exposition, stronger baselines, ablations, and analysis for full validation.
