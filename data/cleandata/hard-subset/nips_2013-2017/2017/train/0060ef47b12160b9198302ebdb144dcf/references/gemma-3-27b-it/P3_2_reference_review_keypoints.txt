- Proposes a feed-forward “masking model” (U-Net with ResNet backbone) to produce saliency maps in a single pass for black-box image classifiers, addressing iterative method slowness.
- Trains the model with a novel objective balancing smoothness, small sufficient regions, and destructive region performance (SSR and SDR).
- Demonstrates fast inference and strong saliency quality on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, introducing a new saliency metric alongside localization evaluations.
- Positions work well within XAI literature, bridging fast gradient methods and accurate iterative approaches by learning saliency directly.
- Builds on Fong & Vedaldi’s model-agnostic approach by replacing iterative optimization with a learned feed-forward model.
- Connects to adversarial robustness concepts and discusses “introduced evidence” in masking.
- Novelty: learning a real-time saliency detector enables new applications like video saliency.
- Strong experimental validation across datasets and multiple black-box classifiers with competitive localization and metric performance.
- Well-defined loss that encourages smooth, minimal, and truly informative saliency regions while minimizing introduced evidence.
- Introduces an SSR-inspired saliency metric that better captures explanation quality than simple localization alone.
- Clear, well-organized writing with thorough method descriptions and presented results.
- Provides ablation studies and justifies architectural and objective design choices (U-Net, ResNet, masking loss).
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters not thoroughly explored; robustness to different settings is unclear.
- Weakness: training is computationally intensive; no detailed breakdown of training time/resources provided.
- Weakness: saliency quality depends on the black-box classifier; effects of flawed or biased classifiers are not deeply analyzed.
- Weakness: limited qualitative error analysis and failure-case discussion — unclear which images/classes cause issues.
- Weakness: the random choice of replacement image A in evidence removal is underexplored; learned or alternative choices might help.
- For acceptance: novel, effective contribution with state-of-the-art results, clear presentation, and a useful new metric.
- Against acceptance: training cost and hyperparameter sensitivity could hinder adoption and robustness.
- Overall assessment: strong paper that addresses a key limitation of existing methods with compelling results; minor weaknesses do not detract significantly.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggested revisions addressing hyperparameter sensitivity and training cost details.
