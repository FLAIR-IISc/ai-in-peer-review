- Proposes a trainable U-Net “masking model” that outputs class-conditional saliency masks in a single forward pass, combining SSR and SDR objectives.  
- Trains the model to preserve target-class prediction on the masked region and destroy it on the complement while encouraging smoothness and small area.  
- Mitigates introduced-evidence artifacts by replacing masked pixels with randomized alternatives (strong blur or noisy constant color) and adding TV regularization.  
- Introduces a new SSR-aligned saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)·log(p) (lower is better) based on area of a tight rectangular crop and classifier probability on the crop.  
- Uses a frozen ResNet-50 encoder and U-Net decoder with a class-embedding feature filter; trained per black-box classifier on ImageNet.  
- Produces qualitatively sharper, class-specific maps than gradient-based and iterative methods and is orders of magnitude faster at inference.  
- Empirically improves weakly-supervised localization error on ImageNet and performs well under the proposed SSR metric; CIFAR-10 results suggest pretrained encoder not required.  
- Novelty: amortizes per-image perturbation optimization into a learned predictor and combines SSR/SDR training with randomized replacements and class-conditioning.  
- Strength: clear motivation and thoughtful mitigation of introduced-evidence; practical architecture and sensible training tricks.  
- Strength: real-time saliency enables new applications (e.g., video) and reports practical details (hyperparams, speed).  
- Weakness: Not truly black-box—training requires backprop through the classifier; paper should clarify assumption of differentiable frozen classifier.  
- Weakness: Eq. (4) has sign/notation inconsistencies that could hinder reproducibility; needs correction.  
- Weakness: Evaluation details (protocols, splits, variances) are incomplete and some comparisons (supervised vs weakly supervised) may be unfair.  
- Weakness: New SSR metric may be sensitive to scale/aspect, rectangular crops can include spurious context; sensitivity analyses are missing.  
- Weakness: Missing quantitative ablations (SSR vs SDR vs TV vs area losses, fake-label prob, random A choices, encoder freezing, mask resolution).  
- Weakness: Per-classifier training only; no study of cross-classifier transfer or multi-classifier generalization.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to iterative methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) are largely qualitative; need controlled quantitative comparisons with matched settings.  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-motivated overall, but requires corrected loss notation and more rigorous evaluations.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; needs clarification on black-box claim, corrected equation, and precise evaluation protocols.  
- Originality & Significance: Novel and practically significant—real-time class-conditional saliency with likely broad applicability.  
- Requests for authors: clarify gradient requirements, provide ablations, test cross-classifier transfer, analyze SSR metric sensitivity, and report training time and scaling.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending clarifications and additional experiments/ablations.
