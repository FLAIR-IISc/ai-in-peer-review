- Paper proposes a trainable, amortized saliency masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) that outputs per-class saliency masks in a single forward pass.  
- Training objective combines SSR (preserve classifier confidence on salient region) and SDR (reduce confidence when salient region removed).  
- Masking blends the image with heavy blur or random color+noise to reduce artifact-driven explanations; loss includes TV and mask-area penalties.  
- Model is trained against fixed classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and can run without classifier access at inference time.  
- Authors introduce a saliency metric based on area of tight crop containing the mask and classifier probability on that crop, aiming to reward small, faithful regions.  
- Empirically reports strong weakly-supervised localization on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 variant, with real-time throughput.  
- Builds on prior work: gradient-based saliency, iterative deletion/preservation, and Meaningful Perturbations; main contribution is amortizing per-image mask optimization into a learned predictor.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation combining SSR and SDR, with explicit discussion of introduced evidence and artifact countermeasures.  
- Strength: solid engineering choices — class-conditional gating, U-Net skip connections, and a “fake class” trick to enforce class conditioning.  
- Strength: competitive empirical results and sensible evaluation via cropping to mitigate artifact confounds.  
- Weakness: likely sign error in Eq. (4) — term written as +λ3 log(fc(Φ(X,M))) contradicts intent to maximize classifier confidence on preserved region.  
- Weakness: “model-agnostic/black-box” claim overstated — training requires backprop through the classifier, so only inference is truly black-box.  
- Weakness: saliency metric definition s(a,p)=log(ã)·log(p) appears inconsistent with desiderata (small area, high probability) and may perversely favor larger areas; needs correction/clarification.  
- Weakness: missing experimental details — tables/figures absent, unclear baseline configurations, and omitted hyperparameters, hardware, thresholds that hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness: lack of ablations for key design choices (randomized A vs fixed blur, TV term, SDR term, mask resolution, fake-label prob).  
- Weakness: limited faithfulness evaluation — should include deletion/insertion curves or analogous tests to confirm masks capture evidence rather than context.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of generalization/conditioning fidelity — transfer across classifiers and behavior for non-ground-truth class conditioning not reported.  
- Clarity issues: fix Eq. (4) sign, clarify black-box wording, correct metric description, provide numeric sanity checks, and resolve notational/typo errors (repeated equation numbers, missing operators).  
- Originality: amortizing meaningful-perturbation optimization into a conditional feed-forward saliency predictor and combining SSR+SDR with class-embedding gating is a novel and useful twist.  
- Significance: real-time, faithful saliency generation is valuable for interactive/video applications and the approach could be widely adopted if validated.  
- Requests for authors: correct Eq. (4), clarify training-time gradient requirements, fix/justify saliency metric (with sanity plots), provide ablations, report deletion/insertion curves, test mask threshold sensitivity, and evaluate conditioning on non-ground-truth classes.  
- Pros summarized: fast amortized saliency, thoughtful objective and anti-artifact measures, strong gains over baselines, and reproducible simple architecture.  
- Cons summarized: objective/metric sign errors, overstated black-box claim, missing experimental details/ablations, and sensitivity to thresholding choices.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on fixing loss/metric issues and adding key experimental details and ablations.
