## Review of "Real Time Image Saliency for Black Box Classifiers"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of generating saliency maps for image classifiers in a fast and efficient manner. Existing iterative methods for saliency detection are computationally expensive. The authors propose a learned approach: training a U-Net to directly predict saliency maps for any input image, given a class label. They introduce a new saliency metric based on the "smallest sufficient region" (SSR) concept and demonstrate through experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 that their method achieves higher quality saliency maps and significantly faster performance compared to existing techniques.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a practical problem:**  Generating interpretable explanations for deep learning models is crucial for trust and debugging. The speed bottleneck of existing methods is a significant hurdle, and this paper offers a compelling solution.
* **Novel approach:**  Learning a saliency detector rather than computing it iteratively is a novel and effective idea. It shifts the computational burden from inference time to training time.
* **Strong experimental validation:** The authors demonstrate strong results on ImageNet using multiple black-box classifiers.  The CIFAR-10 experiments further demonstrate the generalization capability of their approach.
* **New Saliency Metric:** The SSR-based saliency metric appears well-motivated and provides a useful lens for evaluating the quality of saliency maps, focusing on both localization and information concentration.  The comparison to ground truth bounding boxes is insightful.
* **Clear writing and organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured. The motivation is clear, and the methodology is explained in detail.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a good overview of existing saliency detection methods.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Objective Function Complexity:**  The objective function (Equation 4) contains several terms with hand-tuned hyperparameters (λ1, λ2, λ3, λ4).  The sensitivity of the results to these parameters is not thoroughly explored, and the justification for the specific values chosen is limited. 
* **Adversarial Artifact Mitigation:** While the authors acknowledge the potential for adversarial artifacts and propose techniques to mitigate them (e.g., using a blurred alternative image, TV penalty), the effectiveness of these strategies isn’t rigorously quantified.  A more systematic evaluation of artifact robustness would be valuable.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** While the authors mention the benefit of the feature filter, a more extensive ablation study to quantify the contribution of each component of the U-Net architecture (e.g., ResNet encoder, upsampling blocks, class embedding) would strengthen the results.
* **U-Net Architecture Specifics:** The choice of the U-Net architecture and its modifications for CIFAR-10 are described briefly. A more detailed justification of these architectural decisions would be helpful.
* **Comparison to state-of-the-art:** While the authors compare against prior work, a comparison against more recent and sophisticated saliency methods (if any exist) would be useful.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with a novel and effective solution. The experimental results are convincing, and the introduction of a new saliency metric is a valuable contribution. While there are some minor weaknesses regarding the objective function’s sensitivity and limited ablation studies, these do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.  

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  Strong. The technical approach is sound, and the results are well-supported by experiments. The paper demonstrates a complete piece of work.
* **Clarity:** Very Good. The paper is clearly written and well-organized.
* **Originality:** Good. The learned saliency detector approach is novel and distinct from previous work.
* **Significance:**  Good.  The potential for real-time saliency detection has significant implications for model interpretability and debugging, as well as applications like video saliency.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Novel and effective approach to a significant problem.
* Strong experimental results on standard datasets.
* Introduction of a useful new saliency metric.
* Clear writing and organization.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Sensitivity analysis of the objective function hyperparameters is lacking.
* Limited ablation studies.
* Further comparison to more recent SOTA saliency methods would be beneficial.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept**.  I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field and is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.  The authors should be encouraged to address the minor weaknesses identified in the review in a future version of the paper.
