- Proposes a U-Net that predicts saliency maps in real time for black-box classifiers and introduces an SSR-based saliency metric, showing speed and localization gains on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.
- Positions the work between fast gradient-based methods and accurate iterative methods, distinguishing itself by learning a predictive saliency function rather than doing iterative optimization.
- Novelty: learning a saliency function instead of computing maps iteratively is a significant conceptual shift.
- Achieves real-time performance, enabling practical applications like video saliency analysis.
- Strong empirical performance: competitive localization accuracy and interpretability despite weak supervision.
- SSR metric appears well-motivated and correlates with interpretability.
- Paper is generally well-written with a clear motivation and sufficiently detailed model architecture.
- Experiments are thorough, using multiple black-box classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and datasets (ImageNet, CIFAR-10).
- Addresses the problem of introduced evidence when applying masks, indicating thoughtful design.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) is not thoroughly analyzed; robustness is unclear.
- Weakness: limited justification for specifically using a U-Net; rationale beyond multi-resolution features is thin.
- Weakness: learned class embedding visualization is too small/hard to interpret and needs clearer presentation.
- Weakness: ablation study is limited; contributions of individual loss terms (e.g., TV penalty) are not isolated.
- Some mathematical parts (objective formulation) could be simplified or better explained for clarity.
- Significance: method has substantial potential impact for explainable AI and efficient saliency applications.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity and architecture justification.
