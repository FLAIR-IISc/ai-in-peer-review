- Proposes a learned, feed-forward masking network (U-Net + ResNet-50 encoder) to generate class-conditional saliency masks in one forward pass, amortizing iterative perturbation methods.
- Training objective enforces smoothness (TV), compactness (area), sufficiency (SSR-like), and destructiveness (SDR-like) of masks.
- Mitigates “introduced evidence” by randomizing the masked-region replacement (blur or random color+noise) during training.
- Applied to ImageNet classifiers (AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet-50) and CIFAR-10; proposes a new saliency metric comparing classifier probability on tight crop vs crop area.
- Positioning: intersects backprop-based saliency, CAM methods, and perturbation-based explanations; main novelty is amortized mask generation and explicit SSR/SDR objective combination.
- Soundness: objective aligns with goals and U-Net is appropriate for sharp masks; random A and smoothing reduce artifact exploitation.
- Empirical: produces sharper, localized masks; weakly supervised localization competitive with fully supervised VGG; runs >100 images/s at 224×224 supporting real-time claim.
- Loss clarity: Equation (4) is ambiguous (signs/coefficient effects unclear); exact loss formulation must be fixed.
- “Model-agnostic” claim is misleading: training requires classifier differentiability to backpropagate through fc(φ(X,M)), so only test-time is classifier-free.
- New saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)/log(p) is unclear and possibly inconsistent with intended behavior; rationale for ratio vs alternatives is missing.
- Crop-resize evaluation may conflate sufficiency with scale/aspect distortions; metric sensitivity and monotonicity should be analyzed.
- Missing ablations: sensitivity to λ1–λ4, threshold choice for boxes, alternative A choices, fake-class strategy impact, and speed/accuracy trade-offs vs iterative methods.
- Reproducibility: training hyperparameters (optimizer, LR, batch size, schedule) are missing or relegated to appendix and should be included.
- Pretrained encoder confound: using fixed ResNet-50 encoder may inject object biases making localization easier; ablation with/without pretraining on ImageNet needed.
- Clarity: generally well written but has typographical/formatting issues (broken equations, ambiguous operators) that hinder reproducibility.
- Originality: amortizing perturbation-based saliency via a learned generator and randomized A to reduce introduced evidence is novel and practically useful.
- Significance: method is potentially impactful (real-time, sharper maps) and likely to inspire follow-up work if evaluations are strengthened.
- Pros: clear SSR/SDR objective, artifact mitigation, real-time performance, sensible architecture/tricks, competitive weakly supervised localization.
- Cons: ambiguous equations and metric, misleading “black box” wording, missing ablations/sensitivity analyses, potential pretraining confound.
- Suggested clarifications: provide exact loss and training hyperparameters, correct black-box wording, and fix typographical issues.
- Suggested analyses: justify and analyze the saliency metric (monotonicity, sensitivity to area cap), compare to deletion/insertion metrics, and show metric robustness.
- Suggested ablations/experiments: vary λ’s and threshold, test alternatives for A, measure fake-class probability effects, report timing vs iterative methods at matched budgets with hardware details.
- Suggested extensions: test transferability of masking model across classifiers, evaluate multi-object/multi-label images and non-GT class masks.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel, practical, and promising but weakened by clarity issues, overbroad claims, and missing evaluations.
