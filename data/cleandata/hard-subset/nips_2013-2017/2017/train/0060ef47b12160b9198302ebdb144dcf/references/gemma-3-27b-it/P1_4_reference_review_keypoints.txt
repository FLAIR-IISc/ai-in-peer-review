Introduces a novel real-time saliency method that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass instead of costly iterative optimization.  
Uses a U-Net decoder with a pre-trained ResNet encoder to produce saliency maps for black-box classifiers.  
Proposes a new "Smallest Sufficient Region" (SSR) saliency metric grounded in information theory to better evaluate map quality.  
Demonstrates strong empirical performance on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, outperforming existing methods in localization and the SSR metric.  
Significantly faster than iterative methods, enabling real-time and video saliency applications.  
Generated saliency maps appear visually more refined and precise compared to baselines.  
Addresses adversarial artifacts from masking via mitigation techniques (equation 2, random A).  
Experimental validation is thorough, comparing across datasets and black-box classifiers.  
Paper is well written, clearly motivated, and logically structured.  
Architecture choices (U-Net details, two-channel output, non-standard nonlinearity) lack thorough justification or ablation.  
Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially weights in the objective (equation 4).  
Limited ablation: simplified CIFAR-10 model and lack of comparison with/without pre-trained ResNet encoder.  
SSR metric depends on classifier robustness to cropping and scale/aspect changes, which limits applicability.  
Brief mention but insufficient discussion of potential biases in the learned masking model and mitigation strategies.  
Arguments for acceptance: practical speedup, state-of-the-art results, novel metric, strong understanding of limitations, high potential impact.  
Arguments against acceptance: need more investigation of architectural/hyperparameter choices and reliance on classifier robustness and masking biases.  
Overall assessment: strong accept; merits outweigh weaknesses, but authors should address the noted minor issues.  
Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
