- Proposes a trainable masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Introduces a new objective combining smoothness, small region size, classifier confidence on preserved regions, and low confidence after salient region removal.  
- Proposes a novel saliency metric based on the Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) to separate saliency from localisation.  
- Uses a U-Net-based masking model trained to produce high-quality saliency maps.  
- Positions the work relative to prior iterative methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) and aims to address their speed limitations.  
- Strong technical contribution by shifting from iterative optimization to a model-based approach.  
- Thorough experiments on ImageNet with three black-box classifiers and additional CIFAR-10 validation.  
- Evaluation includes standard localisation accuracy and the new SSR-based saliency metric.  
- Demonstrates real-time capability (~100 saliency masks/sec), enabling practical applications like video saliency.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes effective illustrative figures.  
- Limited discussion of limitations, e.g., handling non-visual classifier cues or spurious correlations learned by classifiers.  
- Claim of "model agnostic" is weakened by using ResNet components, which may introduce biases.  
- Comparison to parallel work (Fong & Vedaldi) is limited; more detailed comparison of complexity and quality is needed.  
- CIFAR-10 experiment underdeveloped: uses a randomly initialized masking model without sufficient justification or comparisons.  
- Training data requirements unclear: trained on 250k ImageNet images but unclear generalization to out-of-distribution images.  
- Lacks a thorough ablation study of objective components (e.g., TV penalty, area penalty).  
- Figure 5 visuals are strong but would benefit from side-by-side comparison with iterative methods to show speed-accuracy tradeoff.  
- Objective function hyperparameters lack intuitive explanation and sensitivity analysis.  
- The "fake class" training trick is interesting but needs more explanation of why it outperforms other regularization techniques.  
- Results would benefit from statistical significance testing (e.g., for Table 3).  
- Future work suggestion to detect biases in the masking model is promising but needs concrete investigation strategies.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, novel and significant contribution to interpretability.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses despite some areas needing more analysis and comparison.  
- Pros: orders-of-magnitude speedup, well-motivated metric, strong experimental results, real-time demonstration, clear presentation.  
- Cons: limited limitations discussion, underdeveloped CIFAR-10 evaluation, incomplete comparison to parallel work, lacking comprehensive ablation study.
