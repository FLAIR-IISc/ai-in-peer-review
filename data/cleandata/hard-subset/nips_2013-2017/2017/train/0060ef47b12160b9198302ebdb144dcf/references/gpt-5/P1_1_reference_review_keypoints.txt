- Proposes a trainable, model-agnostic saliency detector that predicts a class-conditioned mask in one forward pass.  
- Objective combines smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR) with TV/sparsity regularization and defenses against introduced evidence.  
- Mask produced by a U-Net-style network with ResNet-50 encoder and class-embedding feature filter; classifier is fixed during mask training.  
- Introduces a crop-based saliency metric to measure how much of an image suffices to retain classifier confidence.  
- Novelty: amortized mask inference approximating per-image perturbation objectives for real-time saliency, combining SSR and SDR.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) for weakly supervised localization and on CIFAR-10.  
- Strength: clear, principled objective and thoughtful defenses (randomized alternative image A, TV regularization, mask smoothing).  
- Strength: amortized inference yields orders-of-magnitude speedup and produces sharper, more precise masks.  
- Strength: architecture choice (U-Net + class embedding gating) is appropriate for multiscale precision.  
- Strength: competitive weakly supervised localization (36.7% error with ResNet-50 black box), close to supervised baselines.  
- Strength: new metric encourages small, sufficient crops and correlates better with interpretability than IOU.  
- Strength: method generalizes to CIFAR-10 and pretraining of the mask model not strictly required.  
- Weakness: “black box”/“model-agnostic” claims are overstated — training requires backprop through the classifier (white-box access).  
- Weakness: saliency metric s(a,p) is ambiguously defined (likely s = log(ã) − log(p)); need justification for the 5% floor and sensitivity to resizing/aspect ratio.  
- Weakness: evaluation details incomplete (localization protocol specifics, top-1 vs top-5, threshold sensitivity, missing baseline numbers for CAM/Grad-CAM/etc.).  
- Weakness: no ablation studies (SSR vs SDR weights, TV weight, area penalty, A randomization, class-embedding, mask resolution/upsampling).  
- Weakness: portability concerns — separate masking model per classifier, unclear transferability and compute/data trade-offs vs iterative methods.  
- Weakness: robustness to adversarial/peculiar artifacts argued qualitatively without quantitative analysis of defenses.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps (optimizer, LR schedule, batch size, epochs, loss weights, alternative-image distributions); code release recommended.  
- Quality: method is technically sound and well-motivated but needs clearer math (eq.3 sign) and more quantitative evidence and ablations.  
- Clarity: generally well written, but notation errors and missing implementation details reduce reproducibility.  
- Originality: amortized saliency prediction and combining SSR+SDR appears novel relative to per-image optimization and gradient methods.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for real-time, class-specific saliency if backed by stronger experiments and ablations.  
- Key author questions: clarify s(a,p); state whether classifier gradients are required for training; provide ablations on λ1–λ4 and A; compare quantitatively to CAM/Grad-CAM/Fong & Vedaldi; report robustness/failure modes; test transfer across classifiers.  
- Minor comments: fix eq.(3) ambiguity, correct eq.(4) formatting, add Grad-CAM and Integrated Gradients references.  
- Reasons to accept: novel amortized approach with principled objective, strong qualitative results, competitive localization, and a useful new metric.  
- Reasons to reject: misleading “black box” claim, incomplete quantitative evaluation and ablations, ambiguities and reproducibility issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (7/10), reviewer confidence 0.7.
