- Proposes a learned, feed-forward masking model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder + class embedding) to produce class-conditional saliency maps in one pass, optimizing both SSR (preserve) and SDR (destroy).
- Objective combines total variation for smoothness, area penalty, high-probability on preserved region, low-probability on removed region, and randomized fill to mitigate introduced evidence.
- Trains separate maskers for different classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and demonstrates real-time speed (100+ images/s).
- Evaluates on ImageNet via weakly supervised localization (thresholding masks → tightest box) and introduces a crop-based saliency metric combining area and predicted probability.
- Claims novelty in distilling iterative perturbation methods into a trainable feed-forward masker that jointly optimizes SSR/SDR and handles introduced evidence.
- Strength: Treating saliency as a conditioned prediction task is conceptually compelling and enables real-time explanations.
- Strength: Well-motivated loss design and artifact-mitigation via randomized masking fills.
- Strength: Practical architecture and training tricks (e.g., fake class selectors) to prevent selector ignorance.
- Strength: Strong empirical results—competitive ImageNet localization and substantial speed advantage over iterative baselines; CIFAR-10 shows method can be trained from scratch.
- Strength: New metric attempts to quantify interpretability and avoids adversarial artifacts by using cropping rather than masking.
- Weakness: “Black-box” claim is overstated—training requires gradient access to the classifier and a separate masker per model.
- Weakness: Saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã) log(p) appears problematic (product of logs may underweight area when p→1); formula and trade-off need correction or alternative (sum/Pareto/multi-threshold) analysis.
- Weakness: Evaluation details unclear—fixed 0.5 threshold usage, sensitivity to threshold, fairness/tuning of iterative baselines, and baseline runtimes need reporting; localization with predicted labels should be reported.
- Weakness: Missing ablations—no studies removing SSR/SDR terms, varying TV/area weights, fixing fill A, removing fake labels, or varying mask resolution to identify drivers of performance.
- Weakness: Generalization/portability unclear—discussion needed on data/time to train new masker, single-masker for multiple classifiers, and robustness to classifier changes.
- Weakness: Potential encoder-induced bias—using ResNet-50 encoder to explain ResNet-50 may bias masks; cross-architecture studies suggested.
- Weakness: CIFAR-10 results are only qualitative; quantitative metrics (saliency score, localization) are needed.
- Weakness: Some notation/equations are terse or ambiguous (Hadamard products, thresholds, metric/removal operator definitions) and should be clarified.
- Overall: Technically solid and practically significant, but needs metric fix, more ablations/sensitivity analyses, and clearer claims about black-box assumptions.
- Recommendation: Lean accept contingent on addressing metric formulation, adding ablations/sensitivity studies, and clarifying training/test access assumptions to the classifier.
