This paper tackles the important problem of explaining the decisions of black-box classifiers via saliency detection. The core idea – learning a saliency *model* instead of iteratively computing saliency maps – is genuinely novel and, crucially, addresses a major bottleneck in existing approaches. The authors correctly identify the limitations of gradient-based and iterative methods and propose a compelling alternative. The introduced saliency metric, based on the smallest sufficient/destroying region concepts, is insightful and well-motivated. While the experimental validation isn't exhaustive, the results on ImageNet are promising, and the CIFAR-10 experiment further strengthens the claim of generalizability.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The learned saliency model is a significant departure from existing iterative methods, representing a genuinely fresh perspective. This is the paper's strongest asset.
*   **Speed:** The potential for real-time saliency detection opens up exciting new avenues for application (video saliency is a particularly attractive direction).
*   **Strong Conceptual Foundation:** The SSR/SDR definitions and the proposed saliency metric provide a solid theoretical grounding for the work. The attention to adversarial artifacts within the mask creation process is a thoughtful detail.
*   **Solid Experimental Results:** The ImageNet experiments demonstrate competitive performance, and the CIFAR-10 results demonstrate adaptability to different datasets. Outperforming existing weakly supervised localization methods is a notable achievement.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it relatively easy to follow the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Ablation Studies:** While the authors discuss several design choices (e.g., the evidence removal function, the nonlinearity in the mask prediction), a more systematic ablation study would be valuable. For instance, how sensitive is the performance to the choice of the alternative image *A* in Eq. 2?
*   **Objective Function Sensitivity:** The various regularization terms (TV penalty, area penalty) in the objective function have hyperparameters (λ values). A more thorough discussion of how these parameters were tuned and their influence on the results would be beneficial.
*   **Dataset Bias Concerns:** The fact that the masking model benefits from a pre-trained ResNet encoder, and the potentially inherent biases within that encoder, isn’t fully explored. While the CIFAR-10 experiment mitigates this somewhat, further investigation is warranted.
*   **Saliency Metric Limitations:** The saliency metric, while conceptually sound, relies on resizing the cropped region to a fixed size. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but it's a potential source of inconsistency.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While comparisons to previous saliency methods and the “Center” baseline are provided, a comparison to more recent and sophisticated interpretability techniques (e.g., SHAP, LIME) would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea of learning a saliency model is genuinely novel and has the potential to significantly impact the field of explainable AI.
*   The paper demonstrates a clear understanding of the limitations of existing approaches and offers a compelling solution.
*   The experimental results are promising and support the claim that the proposed method achieves competitive performance.
*   The authors exhibit a strong theoretical grounding and attention to detail, particularly in addressing the challenges of adversarial artifacts.
*   The potential for real-time applications is exciting.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental validation isn’t completely comprehensive, and some ablation studies are missing.
*   The reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder introduces potential bias.
*   A more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art interpretability techniques is needed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and impactful idea. While not without its limitations, the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I believe this work represents a valuable contribution to the field and has the potential to inspire further research. I lean heavily towards acceptance, recognizing that the authors have presented a bold approach that prioritizes innovation over absolute methodological rigor. The potential impact of enabling real-time saliency detection is significant enough to justify accepting the paper despite the minor shortcomings.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
