Summary
This paper proposes training a feed-forward masking network (U-Net with a ResNet-50 encoder and class embeddings) to predict per-image, per-class saliency masks in one pass for a given image classifier (“black box”). The loss combines: (i) TV regularization for smoothness, (ii) an area penalty (favoring small masks), (iii) a “preserve” term to keep the class probability high on the masked-in region (SSR), and (iv) a “destroy” term to reduce the class probability after removing the salient region (SDR), with masking applied against a randomly sampled alternative image (strong blur or colored noise) to mitigate adversarial artifacts. The authors also propose a new saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)·log(p) using the area of the tightest rectangle covering the saliency and the classifier probability on that crop. They report improved weakly supervised localization on ImageNet (vs. several baselines, including iterative optimizations) and provide a CIFAR-10 proof of concept without a pre-trained encoder.

Strengths
- Clear motivation to amortize iterative saliency optimization into a fast, single-pass predictor.
- Combining SSR and SDR in the training objective is sensible and addresses both sufficiency and necessity of evidence.
- Practical measures to reduce spurious artifacts (randomized alternative image A, TV) are well motivated.
- Runtime claims (≈100 images/s) suggest potential for video/real-time applications.
- Empirical gains on weakly supervised localization over several baselines and a cross-dataset demonstration on CIFAR-10.

Weaknesses (technical/methodological)
- “Black-box/model-agnostic” claims are inconsistent with the method: the masking model must backpropagate through the classifier during training. If the classifier is truly a black-box oracle without gradients, training is unclear. Please clarify assumptions and gradients used.
- Loss formulation (Eq. 4) is ambiguous and likely incorrect as written (missing signs, parentheses, and the role of “4” as coefficient vs. exponent). A rigorous, unambiguous specification is required. Softmax probabilities are used in the loss; logits are typically more stable and calibration-free.
- New saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)·log(p) is ill-posed: s=0 for p=1 (perfect preservation) and also s=0 for a=1 (whole-image crop), contradicting “lower is better.” The metric can favor trivial solutions. No analysis of its properties, sensitivity, or failure modes; no error bars. The “information-theoretic” interpretation is hand-wavy.
- Evaluation lacks ablations: effect of each loss term, the exponent on the SDR term, the mix/choice of A, TV weight, mask resolution, fake-class probability, threshold for binarization, and using logits vs probabilities.
- Generalization and scope: A separate masking model is trained per classifier; there is no demonstration that a learned model transfers across classifiers or architectures, limiting practical “black-box” utility.
- Mask resolution is 1/4 of input with bilinear upsampling; yet the paper claims “sharp” masks. Quantitative boundary/edge quality measures are not provided.
- The class embedding and “feature filter” (Eq. 7) are under-specified: training details, dimensionality, initialization, and ablations missing. The suggested trick to maximize max(X^T_ij C_s) is not formalized in the objective.
- Thresholding at 0.5 to derive boxes is ad hoc; no sweep/analysis. Multi-object cases are not discussed.
- Comparisons: important contemporaneous baselines are missing or under-discussed (e.g., CAM/Grad-CAM, LRP, DeepLIFT, SmoothGrad). It’s unclear how iterative baselines were implemented/tuned; runtime/quality trade-offs not rigorously compared.
- No statistical analysis (variance, confidence intervals) or significance testing for localization and metric results.
- Using randomized A during training but evaluating with cropping introduces a train-test mismatch; the impact is not quantified.
- Claims about real-time performance lack hardware details, batch size, and precise timings vs. iterative methods.
- CIFAR-10 experiment is minimal (20 epochs) and qualitative; no quantitative metrics (e.g., pointing game, localization error) are provided.

Clarity and presentation issues
- Numerous notation/typo/formatting problems: Eq. (4) unclear; Eqs. (1–2) and masking operator lack precise definitions (Hadamard product, dimensions); undefined symbols (Φ), misuse of subscripts/superscripts.
- References are inconsistent, incomplete, and in several cases malformed (missing years, venues, titles). Several key related works are missing.
- Figures and tables are referenced (e.g., Fig. 1–6, Table 1–3, Appendix) but not provided here; ensure they contain quantitative comparisons and clear captions.

Questions for authors
1. Please precisely state the loss (with correct signs, coefficients, and whether logits or probabilities are used) and justify the exponent on the SDR term.
2. Do you backpropagate through the classifier? If so, in what sense is this black-box/model-agnostic? Can your training work with a non-differentiable oracle (scores only)?
3. Provide ablations for each component (SSR term, SDR term, TV, A choices, thresholding), and include variance over multiple runs.
4. Revisit the proposed metric; address the zero-at-trivial-solution issue, and compare against established insertion/deletion-style curves or pointing-game metrics.
5. Add comparisons to CAM/Grad-CAM, LRP, DeepLIFT, and SmoothGrad with standard evaluation protocols.
6. Report hardware, batch size, and precise throughput; include iterative baseline runtimes.
7. Clarify whether the mask TV is computed before or after upsampling; report mask boundary sharpness quantitatively.

Minor/formatting issues
- “st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)” footer appears; check anonymity and formatting.
- Many grammatical errors and inconsistent notation (e.g., missing multiplications, mismatched parentheses, spacing).
- Reference list contains multiple malformed entries; please fix.

Assessment
- Quality: 5/10 (promising idea, but loss/metric clarity and evaluation rigor are insufficient)
- Clarity: 5/10 (notation/citations need significant cleanup; missing ablations)
- Originality: 6/10 (amortizing optimization via a learned mask is reasonable; SSR+SDR joint objective is a nice consolidation)
- Significance: 6/10 (fast saliency is useful, but current evidence and comparisons are not yet convincing)

Recommendation: Reject
While the approach is promising and potentially impactful, the current submission has substantial methodological ambiguities (loss, metric), over-claims about black-box agnosticism, missing ablations and key baselines, and presentation issues. With clarified objectives, stronger and fairer evaluations, and corrected metric, this could become a solid contribution.
