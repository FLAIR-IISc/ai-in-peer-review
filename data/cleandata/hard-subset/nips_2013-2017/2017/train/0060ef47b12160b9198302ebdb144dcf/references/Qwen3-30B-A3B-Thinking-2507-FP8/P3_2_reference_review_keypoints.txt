- Summary: Proposes a U-Net masking model to produce real-time saliency maps in one forward pass and introduces an SSR-based saliency metric; evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 showing faster and better localization than iterative methods.

- Strength 1 (Novel Approach): Trains a model to predict saliency maps instead of using iterative optimization, achieving ~100 masks/second.

- Strength 2 (Metric): Introduces a well-motivated saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)-log(p) tied to the smallest sufficient region concept.

- Strength 3 (Experiments): Provides comprehensive experiments across ImageNet, CIFAR-10, and three black-box classifiers with multiple baselines.

- Strength 4 (Clarity): Paper is well-written with a clear description of the masking architecture and training procedure.

- Strength 5 (Practical Impact): Real-time capability enables practical applications like video saliency for autonomous systems.

- Weakness 1 (Model-agnostic Claim): Claim of being "model agnostic" contradicts reliance on a pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder for the masking model.

- Weakness 2 (Incomplete SOTA Comparison): Lacks direct, detailed comparison with concurrent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) on the same metrics.

- Weakness 3 (Mask Quality Analysis): Insufficient quantitative analysis of mask quality (e.g., comparison to ground-truth segmentation, multi-object images).

- Weakness 4 (Limitations/Bias): Does not sufficiently analyze or discuss potential biases introduced by the masking model.

- Weakness 5 (Evidence Removal Ambiguity): Effectiveness of artifact-avoidance strategies (blur vs. noise) is not rigorously validated.

- Weakness 6 (Generalization Limits): Limited tests on more challenging datasets or different classifier types; claim of working with any differentiable classifier is under-validated.

- Weakness 7 (No Ablations): Missing ablation studies to measure contributions of objective components and hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., TV penalty).

- Tech Comment 1 (Backbone Choice): Need justification for choosing ResNet-50 encoder and evaluation of alternative backbones.

- Tech Comment 2 (Fake Labels): Training trick using "fake labels" lacks empirical evidence showing its effectiveness.

- Tech Comment 3 (Metric Edge Cases): Metric handling of classifier uncertainty and the 0.05 area floor (ã) is arbitrary and needs justification.

- Tech Comment 4 (CIFAR-10 Context): CIFAR-10 results lack comparisons to existing weakly supervised methods and contextual baselines.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, novel contribution with practical value but requires addressing comparative, evaluative, and limitation analyses.

- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.

- Revision 1: Clarify or temper the "model agnostic" claim and/or show masking model can be trained without ResNet dependency.

- Revision 2: Add direct comparisons with Fong & Vedaldi and other recent methods (e.g., gradient-based work by Bach et al.).

- Revision 3: Include ablation studies isolating each objective component and penalty term.

- Revision 4: Expand a dedicated discussion on potential biases and their impact on interpretability.

- Revision 5: Provide comparative baselines and context for CIFAR-10 results against existing weakly supervised methods.

- Revision 6: Justify the 0.05 area threshold in the saliency metric and analyze metric behavior for uncertain predictions.

- Revision 7: Add more quantitative analyses of mask quality (e.g., segmentation overlap, multi-object evaluations, artifact impact studies).
