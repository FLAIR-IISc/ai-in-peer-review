- Proposes a learned, single-forward-pass U-Net (ResNet backbone) for real-time saliency detection of black-box classifiers.  
- Introduces a new SSR/SDR-based saliency metric that evaluates informativeness, not just spatial localization.  
- Claims significant speed improvement over iterative methods, enabling real-time and video applications.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior localization accuracy on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Visual results are compelling, showing high-quality saliency maps (Figure 5).  
- Addresses adversarial artifacts during evidence removal and proposes mitigation strategies.  
- Uses a sensible U-Net/ResNet architecture and leverages prior work in saliency and explainability.  
- Shows generalization by training from scratch on CIFAR-10.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and impacts on results.  
- Objective function is complex with multiple weighted terms and insufficient justification for chosen weights.  
- Ablation study is limited; need more analysis (e.g., pretrained ResNet encoder vs. training from scratch on ImageNet).  
- Training computational cost and resource requirements are not reported.  
- New metric has limitations (resizing cropped regions may distort aspect ratio) and could be improved.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to explainable AI with significant speed and quality gains; reviewer recommends Strong Accept.
