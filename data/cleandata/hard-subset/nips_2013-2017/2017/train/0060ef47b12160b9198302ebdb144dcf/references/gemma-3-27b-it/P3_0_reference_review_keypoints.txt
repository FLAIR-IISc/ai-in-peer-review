- Overall: Compelling, well-motivated paper on real-time image saliency for black-box classifiers; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Trains a U-Net to predict saliency maps directly (no iterative optimization), introduces SSR/SDR metric, and shows speedups and competitive results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Practicality and speed: Significant real-time performance improvement enabling applications like video saliency.  
- Novel approach: Learning a saliency predictor shifts cost to training and avoids slow iterative methods.  
- Empirical results: Strong, competitive localization and new-metric performance on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Saliency metric: SSR/SDR-based metric provides a more nuanced evaluation of explanation quality.  
- Clarity and presentation: Generally well-written, organized, and motivated.  
- Adversarial robustness: Authors address introduced-evidence/adversarial artifacts with mitigation techniques.  
- Theoretical depth: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis or guarantees explaining why the approach works.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No comprehensive sensitivity analysis or guidelines for the multiple loss weights (λ1–λ4).  
- Architecture justification: Limited justification and ablation for choosing U-Net/ResNet-50 encoder and channel configuration.  
- Dependency on classifier: Insufficient exploration of how saliency quality varies with different black-box classifiers or classifier accuracy.  
- "Fake label" training: Description and motivation are unclear and need more detail and empirical support.  
- Figure 4: Diagram could be clearer about information flow through upsampling blocks.  
- Equation 8: Nonlinear activation choice for mask generation needs stronger rationale (why sigmoid/tanh failed).  
- Table 3: Should report standard deviations to show metric variability.  
- Future work: Discussion is vague; needs specific challenges and directions for video saliency extension.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Practical, effective solution with strong empirical evidence, useful metric, clear writing, and application potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing theoretical analysis, limited hyperparameter and architectural justification.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on clarity, hyperparameter choices, architecture justification, and theoretical limitations.
