- Overall: Presents a practical, real-time saliency detector for black-box classifiers with strong writing and a recommendation to accept.  
- Core idea: Learn a saliency detector (U-Net) to predict masks directly instead of iterative optimization.  
- Objective: Novel loss balancing smoothness, small-region preservation (SSR), and disruption of classification (SDR).  
- Metric: Introduces a new saliency metric based on minimal cropped region preserving classifier confidence.  
- Results: Evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, showing superior speed, localization accuracy, and interpretability.  
- Practicality & Speed: Real-time saliency enables video and interactive debugging applications.  
- Improved quality: Learned masks are sharper and more accurate than iterative or patch-removal methods.  
- Loss function novelty: Effectively balances competing goals and addresses spurious evidence from masking.  
- New metric value: SSR-inspired metric provides a nuanced evaluation beyond standard localization accuracy.  
- Thorough experiments: Multiple black-box classifiers and datasets support generalizability.  
- Writing: Clear, well-structured presentation of motivation, method, and results.  
- Hyperparameter concern: Sensitivity to λ1–λ4 is insufficiently analyzed; robustness unclear.  
- Architecture choice: U-Net justification is limited; alternative architectures not thoroughly explored.  
- Pre-training bias: Reliance on pre-trained ResNet may introduce biases; implications not fully discussed.  
- Metric limitation: New metric depends on classifier robustness to scale/aspect changes and may not align with human perception.  
- Adversarial artifacts: Masking mitigates but does not eliminate adversarial artifacts; examples and mitigations lacking.  
- Acceptance arguments: Tackles important interpretability problem and improves speed/quality with novel loss and metric.  
- Rejection arguments: Unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity, limited architecture justification, and pre-training reliance.  
- Suggestion: Include visual examples of adversarial masks and their classification effects.  
- Suggestion: Explain why sigmoid nonlinearity failed and justify chosen nonlinearity (Eq. 8).  
- Suggestion: Provide confidence intervals for localization accuracy in Tables 1 & 2.  
- Future work: Explore biases in the masking model itself.
