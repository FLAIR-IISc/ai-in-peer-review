Proposes a U-Net masking model that predicts high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time black-box classifier explanations, plus a new saliency metric and validation on ImageNet/CIFAR-10.

Technically sound objective (TV, small mask, preserved/destroyed confidence) with thorough experiments and ablation studies showing improved localisation and metric performance.

Novel idea: train a model to predict saliency maps rather than per-image optimization; jointly optimizes SSR and SDR (unlike concurrent work).

Real-time capability (100+ masks/sec), weakly supervised training, and results comparable to ground-truth boxes make it practically significant and suitable for video applications.

Paper is well-written with effective figures and a replicable U-Net description, though some objective details need clarification.

Lacks a detailed quantitative comparison with concurrent work by Fong & Vedaldi [2] to fully establish relative advantages.

The rationale and interpretability link for the new saliency metric (equation 3) are not sufficiently explained.

Insufficient technical details on training, especially class embedding and the fake class mechanism, and unclear comparison of pre-training vs training from scratch.

Real-time performance claim is not supported by a timing comparison table versus iterative and other baseline methods.

Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important explainability problem, offers a novel and much faster approach, introduces a useful metric, and is validated across multiple models/datasets.

Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparisons with concurrent work, unclear metric explanation, missing training/runtime details, and limited discussion of real-world impact and limitations.

Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparisons with [2], clarify the metric, and provide more training/runtime details.

Suggested improvements: add a per-image processing-time comparison table; include quantitative comparison with Fong & Vedaldi [2]; expand explanation and justification of the saliency metric; detail class embedding and fake-class training; discuss method limitations and potential biases.
