- Proposes a trainable mask generator (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder and class-embedding gating) that produces class-specific saliency maps in one forward pass.  
- Learns masks by optimizing combined losses: TV smoothness, area penalty, smallest sufficient region (SSR), and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Mitigates introduced evidence/adversarial artifacts by mixing masked regions with randomized alternative images (blur or noise) and using low-res masks + smoothing.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric s(a,p)=log(max(a,0.05)) log(p) based on tightest crop area and class probability (paper claims lower is better).  
- Experiments on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50 as black boxes) and CIFAR-10 show sharper masks, >100 fps runtime, competitive weakly-supervised localization, and improved scores on the proposed metric.  
- Builds on iterative perturbation-based explanations and backprop saliency; novelty is amortizing perturbation via an end-to-end learned mask with SSR+SDR and randomized evidence.  
- Strength: large practical impact — orders-of-magnitude speedup enabling real-time/video applications while producing crisper maps.  
- Strength: sound formulation combining SSR and SDR with explicit defenses against adversarial introduced evidence.  
- Strength: plausible and effective architecture (class-gated U-Net) and sensible training tricks (fake labels, randomized alternatives).  
- Strength: strong empirical results including near VGG-level weakly-supervised localization and good CIFAR-10 performance from scratch.  
- Weakness: “black-box” claim is overstated — training requires backprop through the classifier, so classifier must be differentiable and accessible; only inference is black-box.  
- Weakness: the proposed metric is likely flawed/misstated (sign and interpretation of log product confusing); needs correction and sensitivity analysis (0.05 clamp, aspect-ratio).  
- Weakness: missing important baselines, notably Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++, and more comparisons to CAM where applicable.  
- Weakness: per-classifier trained masking model limits generality; transferability across classifiers is not tested.  
- Weakness: no ablation studies on loss term weights, alternative-image choices, TV strength, mask resolution, fake-label probability, or gating.  
- Weakness: reproducibility details (optimizer, LR schedule, batch size, training time/hardware) are missing.  
- Weakness: evaluation protocol needs clarity (training data vs. val split, use of ground-truth labels at test time vs predicted class).  
- Quality: technically sound and well-motivated, but experimental/methodological gaps (metric and baselines) need addressing.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized with good figures, but notational/interpretation issues (metric sign, information-theoretic claim).  
- Originality: moderately novel — meaningful extension by amortizing perturbation-based saliency with SSR/SDR and randomized evidence removal.  
- Significance: high practical significance due to real-time saliency and broad applicability if evaluation and metric are strengthened.  
- Key author requests: clarify gradient/backprop requirements, correct/justify metric and run sensitivity analyses, compare to Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++, provide ablations, and test transfer across classifiers.  
- Pros: real-time, interpretable saliency; well-motivated objective; thoughtful treatment of introduced evidence; competitive localization without bbox supervision.  
- Cons: flawed metric, limited baselines and ablations, classifier-specific training, overstated “black-box” claim, and missing reproducibility details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on fixing the metric, clarifying black-box claims, adding Grad-CAM comparison, and brief ablations.
