- Proposes a trainable masking model that predicts saliency maps in one forward pass for black-box classifiers, enabling real-time saliency.  
- Introduces a new objective combining mask smoothness, small region size, and preserved classification confidence.  
- Presents a new saliency metric based on the tightest rectangular crop that retains classifier confidence.  
- Uses a U-Net–style architecture to achieve 100+ masks/second inference speed.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to weakly supervised baselines on ImageNet object localization and the new metric.  
- Addresses adversarial artifacts via careful evidence removal techniques (Section 3.1).  
- Provides thorough theoretical justification for the objective (Eq. 4).  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation: multiple baselines, ImageNet localization, new metric, CIFAR-10 generalization, and ablations.  
- Paper is very well written and organized with clear figures and reproducible methodological details (some training details missing).  
- Novel contribution: model-based real-time saliency departs from iterative optimization approaches and complements related concurrent work.  
- Significant practical impact: enables new applications (e.g., video saliency) and improves interpretability tooling.  
- Strengths: real-time capability, strong evaluation, novel metric, clear theory, and thorough comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison with the newest saliency methods (e.g., Grad-CAM/Score-CAM), no discussion of training cost, limited bias analysis, and unclear applicability beyond classification.  
- Minor concern: lack of sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (λ₁–λ₄).  
- Final recommendation: Accept — technically sound, novel, well-evaluated, and practically significant.
