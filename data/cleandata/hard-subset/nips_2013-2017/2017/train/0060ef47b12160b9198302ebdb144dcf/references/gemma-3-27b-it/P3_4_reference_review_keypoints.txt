- Overall: Presents a promising learned, model-based approach for real-time saliency that is fast and achieves competitive/superior quality; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Problem: Addresses interpreting image classifiers by producing saliency maps that explain predictions.
- Gap: Bridges gradient-based (fast/low-quality) and iterative (slow/high-quality) methods by predicting saliency with a trained U-Net.
- Prior work: Correctly situates work relative to Simonyan et al., Zhou et al., Cao et al., and Fong & Vedaldi.
- Key difference: Uses both SSR and SDR objectives in the loss to improve map quality compared to prior black-box methods.
- Strength — Speed: Core strength is real-time performance enabling dynamic applications like video saliency.
- Strength — Quality: Produces sharper, more focused saliency maps with strong visual and quantitative results.
- Strength — New metric: Introduces the 's' metric (based on smallest sufficient region) that balances map size and classification probability.
- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 with multiple black-box classifiers and an ablation study.
- Strength — Adversarial handling: Addresses spurious-evidence artifacts from masking and proposes mitigation strategies.
- Strength — Writing: Paper is well-written and organized, making methods and results easy to follow.
- Weakness — Failure cases: Lacks detailed discussion and analysis of scenarios where the method fails.
- Weakness — Hyperparameters: Performance appears sensitive to objective hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) without a sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — Architecture choice: U-Net choice is reasonable but not thoroughly justified or compared to alternatives.
- Weakness — Embedding viz: Class embedding mentioned is intriguing but visualization and analysis are limited.
- Weakness — Comparative baselines: Needs broader comparison to other non-iterative and classical saliency methods.
- Pro-accept: Solves a practical problem, introduces a useful metric, shows strong results, and handles artifacts well.
- Con-accept: Shortcomings include missing failure analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity, architecture justification, and broader comparisons.
- Suggestion: Add a section discussing failure cases and method limitations.
- Suggestion: Perform sensitivity analysis over key objective hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: Provide more justification for choosing U-Net and consider alternative architectures.
- Suggestion: Expand comparisons to additional saliency detection methods, including non-iterative approaches.
- Suggestion: Show and analyze more examples of the learned class embeddings.
- Suggestion: Include a human qualitative study to assess interpretability of the saliency maps.
