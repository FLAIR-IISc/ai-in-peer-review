- Proposes a learned, real-time saliency masker (U-Net with frozen ResNet-50 encoder) that predicts class-conditional masks in one forward pass, amortizing iterative optimization.  
- Training objective combines TV smoothness, area sparsity, SSR (preserve region keeps class score), and SDR (removing region reduces class score).  
- Mitigates introduced-evidence artifacts by randomizing replacement background A (blur or noise/color) and penalizing mask roughness.  
- Introduces a crop-based saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)·log(p) that rewards tight, high-confidence crops.  
- Empirical evaluation: models trained per classifier (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) on ImageNet and shown on CIFAR-10; reports improved weakly-supervised localization and ~100 images/s throughput.  
- Novelty: amortizing saliency via a learned predictor with joint SSR+SDR objective and randomized evidence removal is a distinct and practical contribution.  
- Strength: achieves real-time inference with sharper masks and improved mask quality vs iterative methods.  
- Strength: SSR+SDR objective and TV+randomized-A countermeasures are well-motivated to reduce artifact-driven explanations.  
- Strength: clever training tricks (fake class selector, class embedding with feature filtering) stabilize and enforce class-conditionality.  
- Strength: new operational saliency metric aligns with SSR notion and is less confounded by masking artifacts.  
- Strength: demonstrates generality across multiple classifiers and datasets with strong localization performance.  
- Weakness: “black-box/model-agnostic” claim is misleading—training requires backprop through the classifier, so the classifier must be differentiable and accessible during training.  
- Weakness: quantitative gains versus strong baselines (CAM/Grad-CAM/Excitation Backprop and iterative perturbation) are not clearly presented here; tables and exact numbers missing.  
- Weakness: no ablation studies on loss weights, SSR vs SDR, choice/mixing of A, TV weight, or architecture choices, limiting insight into what matters.  
- Weakness: the crop-based metric can penalize fragmented masks and be sensitive to aspect-ratio/rescaling decisions; needs comparison to deletion/insertion metrics.  
- Weakness: requires training a separate mask network per target classifier and lacks analysis of cross-model transferability or robustness.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written but should explicitly state training-time gradient access requirement, provide optimizer/learning details, and expand metric behavior.  
- Quality: method is technically coherent and appropriate, but empirical claims would be strengthened by ablations and fuller quantitative comparisons.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for real-time interpretability (e.g., video), though per-classifier training and training-time requirements may limit deployment.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on clarifying black-box claims, providing full quantitative tables, and including minimal ablations and transfer/metric analyses.
