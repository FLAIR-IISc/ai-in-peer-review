- Summary: Proposes a trainable U-Net masking network that predicts class-conditional soft saliency masks in one forward pass, trained with SSR and SDR objectives and evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 variants.

- Key technical idea: Combine total variation, area minimization, SSR (maximize class on preserved region), and SDR (minimize class on removed region) into a single loss.

- Artifact mitigation: Apply masks by blending with a randomized alternative image (blur or random noise/color) and encourage smooth, low-res masks to reduce adversarial artifacts.

- Architectural tweak: Use a class-conditional “feature filter” gating encoder features via learned class embeddings.

- Training nuance: Use “fake label” instances to force dependence on the class selector and train with fixed classifier weights.

- Empirical claim: Reports superior weakly supervised localization and saliency metric scores versus backprop-based and iterative perturbation baselines, with >100 images/s throughput.

- Strength: Clear motivation combining SSR and SDR to address limits of gradient-only or iterative methods.

- Strength: Practical contributions include the U-Net with class gating and training tricks (random A, fake labels) to reduce artifacts and enforce class-conditional behavior.

- Strength: Fast single-pass saliency suitable for real-time applications and convincing qualitative masks.

- Weakness: “Black-box/model-agnostic” claim is overstated — training requires classifier differentiability and access to gradients.

- Weakness: New saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)−log(p) is inconsistently presented, needs sign correction, justification for 0.05 area floor, and sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Missing important baselines (Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++, SmoothGrad, Integrated Gradients) and related discussion/comparisons.

- Weakness: Limited ablation/sensitivity analysis on loss weights, TV vs low-res upsampling, random A choices, thresholding, and the feature filter.

- Weakness: Reproducibility details are sparse (optimizer, LR schedule, batch size, training time/hardware, augmentations, evaluation splits, precise speed measurements).

- Weakness: Use of a pretrained ResNet-50 encoder may inject dataset priors, complicating the agnostic claim despite CIFAR-10-from-scratch experiment.

- Weakness: Evaluation scope limited — single IoU threshold, no per-class or multi-instance analysis, and dataset bias (center baseline) not sufficiently addressed.

- Quality: Technically coherent and plausible but would benefit from clearer definitions, stronger baselines, and thorough ablations.

- Clarity issues: Fix metric equation, clarify “black-box” meaning, provide full training/evaluation details, and correct confusing notation in Equation (4).

- Originality: Novel synthesis of feed-forward saliency trained with perturbation-inspired objectives and the feature filter, but should better relate to CAM/Grad-CAM literature.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for interactive/video settings if reproducible and shown to outperform strong baselines.

- Main requests for authors: Clarify gradient access requirement, fix and justify the metric, provide ablations on λ1–λ4 and other design choices, add comparisons with Grad-CAM family and attribution methods, report precise speed with hardware, and release full training details/code.

- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying the metric and strengthening baselines/ablations; otherwise borderline (high reviewer confidence).
