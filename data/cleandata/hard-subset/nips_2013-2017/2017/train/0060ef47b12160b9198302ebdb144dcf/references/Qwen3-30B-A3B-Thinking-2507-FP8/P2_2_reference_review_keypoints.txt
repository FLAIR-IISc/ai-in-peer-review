- Proposes a model-based saliency method that trains a U-Net masking model to predict saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass, enabling real-time operation (~100 masks/sec).
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR) to better measure interpretability than standard localization accuracy.
- Evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, showing state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization and improved saliency quality under the new metric.
- Technically sound objective formulation motivated by smallest sufficient/destroying region concepts and careful handling of introduced evidence when masking.
- Novel idea of learning a masking model instead of per-image iterative optimization, addressing speed while maintaining quality.
- Significant practical contribution via real-time capability enabling applications like video saliency.
- Clear, well-written paper with effective figures, mathematical formulation, and architecture details aiding reproducibility.
- Thorough experimental design with multiple datasets and baseline comparisons demonstrating both speed and quality gains.
- Lacks a direct quantitative comparison with closely related parallel work (Fong and Vedaldi [2]), which is a notable omission.
- Relies on a pre-trained ResNet encoder for main experiments; impact and cost of training the masking model from scratch are not thoroughly discussed.
- Claim of being "model agnostic" is ambiguous—architecture is tailored to high-resolution inputs and uses a ResNet encoder, limiting true agnosticism.
- Brief mention of potential model biases in the conclusion but insufficient exploration of how the masking model may inherit biases.
- Missing some implementation details (learning rate schedule, batch size, optimizer) needed for full reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution to explainable AI with novel, fast, and high-quality saliency maps; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Recommendation: accept—paper is technically solid, impactful, and well-presented despite minor issues in comparisons and claims of agnosticism.
