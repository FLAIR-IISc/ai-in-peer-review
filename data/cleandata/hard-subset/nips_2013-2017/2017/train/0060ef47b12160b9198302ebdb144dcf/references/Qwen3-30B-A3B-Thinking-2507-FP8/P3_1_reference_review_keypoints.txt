- Proposal: Train a U-Net masking model to predict saliency maps in a single forward pass with a new multi-term objective and an SSR-based saliency metric; claims real-time, high-quality results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.
- Quality concern: No theoretical justification or ablation studies for the four-term objective and chosen weights.
- Quality concern: Claims of being "model agnostic" but no experiments testing generalization of a masking model across different classifiers.
- Quality concern: No analysis or quantification of potential biases introduced by the masking model.
- Reproducibility issue: Missing training details (optimizer, learning rate, batch size, training duration) and lack of quantified impact of the random evidence removal scheme.
- Clarity issue: New saliency metric lacks example values or scale interpretation to help readers judge good vs. bad scores.
- Clarity issue: Description of using "fake classes" in training is vague and the choice of 30% probability is unexplained.
- Clarity issue: Speed claims lack concrete timing benchmarks (e.g., ms per image) for compared iterative methods.
- Originality: Novel model-based, real-time saliency approach and SSR metric, but potential overlap with recent related work [2] is not clearly distinguished.
- Metric comparison: SSR-based metric is well-motivated but not sufficiently contrasted with existing evaluation metrics.
- Significance: Addresses an important, practical problem (e.g., video/autonomous vehicles) but would benefit from concrete application examples and limitations discussion.
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution with solid experiments but significant limitations requiring major revisions.
- Recommended revisions: Add ablation studies, cross-classifier generalization experiments, bias analysis, full reproducibility details, clarify differences from [2], and provide metric example values.
- Strengths: Novel real-time approach, well-motivated new metric, and strong experimental improvements over baselines.
- Weaknesses: Lack of theoretical grounding for key components, insufficient generalization/bias/reproducibility analysis, and unclear relation to recent parallel work.
- Recommendation: Reject with resubmission pending major revisions.
