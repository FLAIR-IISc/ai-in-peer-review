- Proposes a U-Net masking model to predict saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time (100+ maps/sec) explanations for black-box classifiers.  
- Introduces an objective that balances smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on the tightest crop that maintains classification confidence.  
- Builds on prior iterative and black-box optimization methods (e.g., Zhou et al., Fong & Vedaldi) but moves to a trained, feed-forward approach.  
- Technically sound with a clear theoretical foundation for the objective and a well-motivated new metric.  
- Comprehensive experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 show improvements, including a reported 36.7% error rate for ResNet-50 localization.  
- Implementation details are mostly sufficient for reproduction but key training hyperparameters are missing (learning rate, batch size, epochs).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; SSR/SDR concepts and the new metric are well explained and illustrated.  
- Does not sufficiently differentiate from or compare to the concurrent work of Fong & Vedaldi.  
- Novel contribution: training a model for real-time saliency is a significant departure from iterative/gradient-based methods.  
- Significant practical impact: enables applications like video saliency for autonomous systems and achieves faster, higher-quality maps than iterative methods.  
- Strengths: real-time approach, new metric, clear benchmark improvements, thorough validation, and practical feasibility.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete comparison to all relevant prior work, lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and limited exploration of failure modes or category-specific performance.  
- Recommendation: strong, high-quality paper recommended for acceptance with only minor issues to address.
