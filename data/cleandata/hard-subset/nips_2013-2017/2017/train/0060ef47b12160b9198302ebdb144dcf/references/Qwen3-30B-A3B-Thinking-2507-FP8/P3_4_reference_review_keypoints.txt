- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency method that produces saliency maps in a single feed-forward pass for real-time use.  
- Introduces a masking model (U-Net based) trained to predict saliency maps for black-box classifiers.  
- Presents three main contributions: a new SSR-based saliency metric, a U-Net masking architecture, and a training method to prevent ignoring the class selector.  
- Objective function balances mask smoothness, minimal area, high confidence on preserved regions, and low confidence on removed regions.  
- Uses randomized evidence removal and TV regularization to mitigate adversarial artifacts in saliency maps.  
- Proposes a novel saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)-log(p) aligned with the smallest sufficient region concept.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, including state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization (36.7% error for ResNet-50).  
- Demonstrates generalizability on CIFAR-10 without relying on pre-trained models.  
- Real-time capability (100 masks/sec) enables practical applications like video saliency.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees explaining why the masking architecture and objective work.  
- Incomplete empirical comparison—missing standard baselines like Grad-CAM and Grad-CAM++.  
- Does not thoroughly analyze the trade-off between mask smoothness (TV) and localization precision.  
- Insufficient investigation of potential biases introduced by the masking model itself.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, clearly written, significant, and meets NIPS criteria.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Suggested improvements: add theoretical discussion of the objective, compare with Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++, analyze TV penalty effects, and study masking model biases.
