- Proposes real-time saliency for black-box image classifiers by training a U-Net to predict saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Introduces an SSR-based saliency metric (s(a, p)) to evaluate the smallest sufficient region, with an information-theoretic connection.  
- Extends Fong & Vedaldi by combining SSR and SDR considerations and addressing introduced-evidence issues in masking.  
- Demonstrates competitive localization accuracy and high-quality maps on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, with much faster runtime than iterative methods.  
- Strength: substantial speedup enabling real-time saliency generation.  
- Strength: well-motivated combined objective (smoothness, area penalty, classification confidence, destruction performance).  
- Strength: SSR metric provides a sensible, insightful evaluation beyond simple localization.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation and clear comparisons to prior methods.  
- Strength: clear writing, organized presentation, and sufficient architectural detail.  
- Strength: thoroughness in addressing adversarial artifacts and exploring evidence-removal parameters.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly analyzed (TV, area, and other weights).  
- Weakness: limited justification for U-Net choice and ResNet-50 encoder; unclear if other encoders perform similarly.  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies, particularly on the impact of the fake-label training scheme.  
- Weakness: scalability to very high-resolution images is not addressed.  
- Weakness: qualitative saliency results are subjective; more rigorous quantitative quality comparisons needed.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, effective approach solving an important problem, with speed gains, strong results, and a new metric.  
- Argument against acceptance: some methodological choices lack deeper investigation; scalability and reliance on pre-trained encoder raise concerns.  
- Overall: strong contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions (add hyperparameter sensitivity, U-Net justification, and a small scalability experiment).
