- Proposes a learned U-Net saliency detector that predicts class-specific saliency maps in real time for black-box classifiers.  
- Introduces a new "smallest sufficient region" (SSR) metric to evaluate saliency quality focusing on localization and information concentration.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, showing higher-quality maps and much faster performance than iterative methods.  
- Addresses a practical need by removing the inference-time computational bottleneck of existing saliency methods.  
- Shifts computational cost to training time, enabling fast runtime explanations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes thorough related-work coverage.  
- Novel approach distinct from prior iterative saliency techniques.  
- Method shows generalization across datasets and multiple black-box classifiers.  
- Objective function has multiple hand-tuned hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) with limited sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- Adversarial artifact mitigation (blurred alternatives, TV penalty) is proposed but not rigorously or systematically evaluated.  
- Ablation studies are limited; contributions of U-Net components (ResNet encoder, upsampling, class embedding, feature filter) are not fully quantified.  
- Choices and modifications of the U-Net architecture, especially for CIFAR-10, lack detailed justification.  
- Comparison to more recent or sophisticated state-of-the-art saliency methods is limited.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution with convincing experiments and a useful new metric.  
- Qualitative ratings: quality — strong; clarity — very good; originality — good; significance — good.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective solution, strong results, useful metric, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, limited ablations, and incomplete SOTA comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to address the minor weaknesses in a future revision.
