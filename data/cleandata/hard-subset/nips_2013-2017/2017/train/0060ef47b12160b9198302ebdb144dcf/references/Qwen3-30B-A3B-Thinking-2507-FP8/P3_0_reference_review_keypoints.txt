- Proposes a real-time saliency method that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass for black-box classifiers.  
- Introduces a new objective balancing smoothness, area minimization, and classification confidence for saliency masks.  
- Uses a U-Net-based masking model trained to produce high-quality saliency maps.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR).  
- Achieves ~100 saliency masks/sec on a GPU and outperforms existing methods on benchmarks.

- Clear problem motivation and practical relevance (real-time needs like video analysis).  
- Novel and well-motivated SSR metric addressing limitations of localization metrics.  
- Strong experimental results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 with competitive localization accuracy.  
- Demonstrates real-world applicability via real-time performance.  
- Well-designed masking architecture and clever training strategy (fake labels to force class conditioning).

- Insufficient differentiation and comparison with parallel work by Fong & Vedaldi, undermining novelty claims.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why the learned model outperforms iterative methods; "model as regularizer" is vague.  
- Incomplete evaluation of limitations: handling of spurious correlations, incorrect black-box predictions, and adversarial artifacts is unclear.  
- Hyperparameter choices (e.g., λ values) are not justified and lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Overstated claims about mask sharpness without sufficient quantitative comparisons.  
- Insufficient comparison to recent/parallel related work overall.

- Section 3.1: no quantitative evidence that randomized evidence removal prevents adversarial artifacts.  
- Section 4.1: the 30% fake label probability is unexplained and lacks ablation.  
- Figure 5: visual comparisons are good but should highlight specific cases where the method clearly outperforms others.  
- Section 5.3: no discussion of performance when the black-box classifier has lower accuracy or is trained on less data.  
- Conclusion: brief mention of masking-model biases but lacks deeper analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Requested revisions: add detailed comparison with Fong & Vedaldi, provide theoretical justification or discussion, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and more thoroughly address limitations regarding biases and spurious correlations.
