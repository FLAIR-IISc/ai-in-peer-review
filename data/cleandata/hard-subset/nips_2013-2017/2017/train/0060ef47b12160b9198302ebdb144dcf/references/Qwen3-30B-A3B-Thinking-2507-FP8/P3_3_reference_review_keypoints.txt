- Introduces a U-Net masking model that produces saliency maps in one forward pass with a new objective and SSR-based metric, achieving real-time speed and competitive quality on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.
- Clearly motivates the need for real-time saliency (e.g., polar bear in snow) and limitations of iterative methods.
- Novel single-pass approach yields 100+ saliency maps/sec and competitive localization (36.7% on ImageNet).
- Proposes a thoughtful SSR metric that better captures interpretability than traditional localization accuracy.
- Thorough evaluation across datasets, multiple black-box classifiers, baseline comparisons, and objective ablations.
- Limited analysis of biases introduced by the masking model and interactions with black-box classifier biases.
- Evidence removal scheme (50% blur / 50% random color+noise) is ad-hoc and lacks theoretical justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Does not compare against recent popular saliency methods (e.g., Grad-CAM, Integrated Gradients, attention-based approaches).
- Architecture explanations are incomplete—class embedding and feature filter lack clarity, visualization, or analysis.
- Does not address temporal coherence for real-world video applications.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant practical advance with a valuable metric; weaknesses are important but do not undermine the core contribution.
- Recommendation: accept.
