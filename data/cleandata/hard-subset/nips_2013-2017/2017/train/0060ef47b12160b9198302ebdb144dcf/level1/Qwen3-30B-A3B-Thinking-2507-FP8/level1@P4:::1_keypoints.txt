- Proposes a masking model that produces saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling model-agnostic, real-time saliency detection.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR).  
- Achieves strong empirical results: state-of-the-art ImageNet localization performance and ~100 masks/sec, applicable to AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet-50 and datasets like ImageNet/CIFAR-10.  
- Tackles adversarial artifacts using TV penalty, randomized evidence removal, and a U-Net architecture.  
- Defines a well-justified objective (Eq. 4) that balances smoothness, mask size, and preserved/removed class probabilities.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments, ablation studies, and comparisons (including concurrent work).  
- Novelty: meaningful advance over iterative and gradient-based methods; SSR metric argued to better capture saliency quality.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with effective figures and a reproducible architecture diagram.  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison/discussion of how their approach outperforms concurrent work (Fong & Vedaldi) specifically regarding SSR vs SDR optimization.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/evidence about limitations (e.g., very small objects or classifiers relying on subtle features).  
- Weakness: lacks strong justification for choosing U-Net and ResNet-50 encoder over alternative architectures.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, significant contribution to model interpretability with practical real-time applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
