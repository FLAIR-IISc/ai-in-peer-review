- Proposes a learned, feed-forward saliency generator: a U-Net–style masking model (ResNet-50 encoder) that outputs class-conditional masks in one pass.  
- Replaces per-image optimization (meaningful perturbations) by amortizing mask generation into a single trained network.  
- Uses a composite objective balancing smoothness (TV), area, sufficiency (retain-class SSR), and destruction (remove-class SDR).  
- Mitigates adversarial/artifact evidence by applying masks to randomly sampled “alternative images” (heavy blur or noisy constant) and regularizing for smoothness.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric s = log(ã) − log(p) that trades off tight crop area and retained class probability.  
- Experiments on ImageNet (AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet-50 black boxes) and CIFAR-10 show sharper, artifact-resistant masks and improved weakly supervised localization.  
- Achieves real-time inference rates due to amortized mask generation.  
- Positions method between perturbation-based explanations and backprop/activation methods; novelty in amortization and joint SSR/SDR enforcement.  
- Technically sound with practical tricks (fake-label training, randomized alternatives) that address known pitfalls.  
- Needs more ablations: individual loss-term contributions, sensitivity to λi, and choices of alternative images.  
- “Black-box” claim is misleading because training requires classifier gradients, so it’s closer to a differentiable gray-box approach.  
- The proposed metric is sensible but depends on classifier calibration and resizing distortions; insertion/deletion curves would strengthen evaluation.  
- Clarity: generally well written with useful training/architecture details; would benefit from a hyperparameter sensitivity table and explicit comparisons to Grad-CAM/Integrated Gradients.  
- Originality: high for amortizing saliency optimization and combining SSR/SDR with stochastic evidence removal; metric is thoughtful.  
- Significance: valuable real-time, sharper masks and improved localization with potential video applicability, but per-classifier training limits immediate adoption.  
- Pros: amortized real-time saliency, principled SSR+SDR objective with effective regularization, new interpretable metric, thorough ImageNet/CIFAR-10 experiments.  
- Cons: limited ablations/sensitivity analyses, unclear “black-box” scope, metric dependence on calibration and missing insertion/deletion and direct comparisons to Grad-CAM/Integrated Gradients.
