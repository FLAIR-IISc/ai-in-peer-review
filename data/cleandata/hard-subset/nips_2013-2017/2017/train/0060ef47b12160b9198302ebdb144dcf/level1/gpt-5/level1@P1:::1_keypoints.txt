- Proposal: Learn a U-Net masking model with a ResNet-50 encoder and class-conditional gating to produce per-class saliency maps for a fixed classifier in one forward pass.  
- Training objective: Composite loss combining TV smoothness, area penalty, score-keeping (SSR), and score-drop (SDR), with randomized alternative images and fake labels to reduce artifacts and collapse.  
- Saliency metric: New SSR-inspired metric computing s(a,p)=log(max(a,0.05))/log(p) on the tightest crop enclosing the mask (lower is better).  
- Evaluation: Experiments on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and CIFAR-10 show sharp masks, competitive weakly supervised localization, and applicability without pretrained encoders.  
- Relation to prior work: Replaces per-image iterative perturbation methods with a learned generator; compared to gradient-based, CAM-style, and perturbation-based approaches.  
- Strength: Clear unified SSR+SDR objective with practical regularization to mitigate introduced evidence and artifacts.  
- Strength: Effective training tricks (randomized alternatives, fake labels, class gating) that enable reproducible, real-time inference.  
- Strength: Produces sharp, high-resolution masks suitable for video and shows strong weakly supervised localization results.  
- Strength: Demonstrated across multiple classifiers and datasets, including training from scratch on CIFAR-10.  
- Weakness: “Black box” claim is misleading—training requires gradients through the fixed classifier, so it’s a fixed white-box explainer.  
- Weakness: Proposed metric depends on the same classifier used in training, calibration and resizing artifacts, ad hoc clipping, and ignores aspect ratio—potentially biased.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations—no quantitative breakdown of SSR vs SDR, TV strength, alternative image choices, class gating, or mask-threshold sensitivity.  
- Weakness: Incomplete comparisons—missing fair, standardized baselines for CAM/Grad-CAM, Guided Backprop, Excitation Backprop, and full details/variances.  
- Weakness: Requires training a separate masking model per classifier; cross-classifier generalization and unseen-class transfer not evaluated.  
- Weakness: No systematic analysis of failure cases (multi-object scenes, clutter, small objects, non-top-1 conditioning).  
- Quality recommendation: Method is sound and well-motivated but needs rigorous ablations, stronger baselines, cross-classifier tests, additional metrics, and ideally a user study.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is generally clear but omits optimizer/learning-rate/schedule, data splits, mask-threshold rationale, and must clarify gradient access through the “black-box.”  
- Originality: Moderate-to-high—novel learned conditional mask generator approximating perturbation objectives and unified SSR/SDR loss.  
- Significance: High practical impact if claims hold (real-time, sharp per-class saliency), though per-classifier training and metric dependence limit generality.  
- Suggested improvements: Add comprehensive ablations, standardized baseline comparisons, cross-classifier transfer tests, metric robustness analyses, clarify black-box claim, and release code/models.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, practical, and timely approach with promising results and potential for impactful follow-up work.  
- Rejection arguments: Empirical analysis is incomplete, metric may favor the method, and framing around “black box” is inaccurate.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on stronger experimental validation and clearer limitations.
