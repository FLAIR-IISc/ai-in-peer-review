- Proposes a novel trainable masking model for real-time saliency detection of black-box classifiers, producing maps in a single forward pass.  
- Training objective encourages mask smoothness, small region size, and preservation of classification probability.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that offers a more nuanced evaluation than traditional metrics.  
- Paper is well-written with a thorough related work section and clear method description.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms other weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization.  
- Demonstrates real-time performance (over 100 images/sec on a standard GPU), enabling video saliency applications.  
- Strength: combines model-based and optimization-based advantages into a fast, accurate approach.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of learned class embeddings and their relation to produced saliency maps.  
- Weakness: robustness to different types of noise and perturbations is not explored.  
- Significance is high due to addressing a challenging computer vision problem with broad application potential.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; rated 9/10 with confidence 8/10.
