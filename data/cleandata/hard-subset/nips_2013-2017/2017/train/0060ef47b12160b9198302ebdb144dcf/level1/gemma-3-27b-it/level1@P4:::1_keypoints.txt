- Introduces a fast saliency detection method that trains a masking model to predict saliency maps for any differentiable image classifier, avoiding iterative methods.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric tied to the smallest sufficient region concept (Eq. 3).  
- Well-motivated paper that situates itself clearly within saliency literature and highlights limitations of existing approaches.  
- Novel approach: learning a direct mapping to saliency maps offers speed advantages over iterative techniques.  
- Technically sound design using a U-Net with ResNet encoder; objective function (Eq. 4) and loss terms are carefully considered to mitigate adversarial artifacts.  
- Demonstrates clear experimental improvements on ImageNet object localization and shows generalizability with CIFAR-10 experiments.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; explanations of SSR/SDR and the masking model are comprehensible.  
- The proposed saliency metric is a useful and valuable evaluation tool.  
- Objective function is complex with multiple hyperparameters (λ1–λ4); chosen values lack full justification and risk brittleness.  
- Lacks comprehensive ablation studies to quantify contributions of each objective component, adversarial mitigation, and total variation penalty.  
- Comparisons are limited to weakly supervised baselines; no evaluation against stronger fully supervised saliency methods.  
- Assumes the classifier is differentiable, which is not always true and should be acknowledged as a limitation.  
- Minor clarity issue: explanation of the non-standard non-linearity in Eq. (8) is abrupt and needs elaboration.  
- Overall: novel, technically solid, and experimentally validated work deserving acceptance if authors address objective complexity and provide more analysis; reviewer confidence 7/10.
