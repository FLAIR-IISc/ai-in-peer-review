- Proposes a novel model-based method: train a U-Net masking network to produce saliency maps in one forward pass, amortizing per-image optimization.
- Enables real-time, high-quality perturbation-based saliency (e.g., video) previously impractical with iterative methods.
- Contrasts with prior slow iterative optimization (Fong & Vedaldi 2017) and faster but lower-quality gradient methods.
- Uses a comprehensive objective combining mask smoothness, area, SSR- and SDR-like criteria.
- Introduces a new information-theoretic saliency metric for evaluation.
- Strong empirical results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet across multiple architectures, including state-of-the-art weakly-supervised ImageNet localization.
- Well-written and reproducible: clear motivation, objective, and training details.
- Conceptual concern: explaining a black-box with another complex network may introduce masking-model biases that undermine true interpretability.
- Needs stronger technical justification for some design choices (e.g., the nonstandard final-mask nonlinearity).
- Lacks analysis of failure cases and scenarios where the method produces poor or misleading saliency maps.
- Overall assessment: novel and practically significant with compelling results, but conceptual and analysis gaps remain.
