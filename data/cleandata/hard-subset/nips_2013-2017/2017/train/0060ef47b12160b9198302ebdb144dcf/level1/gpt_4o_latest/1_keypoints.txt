- Proposes a trainable masking model that produces saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use and model-agnostic application.
- Demonstrates superior performance over existing weakly supervised methods on ImageNet object localization; produces sharper, more interpretable maps on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR) to better evaluate map quality and interpretability.
- Evaluates method comprehensively on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 with comparisons to baselines and state-of-the-art approaches.
- Achieves high speed (>100 saliency masks/sec), making it practical for real-world tasks like autonomous driving and video analysis.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful visual examples of saliency outputs.
- Method is model-agnostic and does not require access to classifier internals (black-box compatibility).
- Weakness: potential biases in the masking model are acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed, raising concerns for sensitive domains.
- Weakness: suggested extensions (video saliency, weakly supervised segmentation) are not experimentally validated.
- Weakness: performance still slightly behind fully supervised methods, with limited discussion on narrowing that gap.
- Weakness: dependence on pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder for ImageNet experiments may limit generalizability to high-resolution datasets without pretraining.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient, and effective approach that advances weakly supervised saliency detection and contributes a new evaluation metric.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of masking biases and limited experimental scope for proposed extensions.
- Recommendation: accept — substantial contributions and practical impact despite areas needing further exploration.
