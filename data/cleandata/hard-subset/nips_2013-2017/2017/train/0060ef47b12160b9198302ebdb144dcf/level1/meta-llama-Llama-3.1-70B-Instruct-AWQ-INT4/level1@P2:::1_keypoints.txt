- Introduces a novel saliency detection method using a trainable masking model applicable to any differentiable image classifier, producing saliency maps in a single forward pass for real-time use.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving state-of-the-art results in weakly supervised object localization and outperforming other saliency methods.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a thorough introduction on saliency importance and limitations of existing methods.
- Related work section is comprehensive, covering various saliency approaches and their pros/cons.
- Method is technically sound with a clear explanation of the masking model architecture and training process.
- Use of a U-Net architecture with a pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder is well-justified and described in detail.
- Feature filter and upsampling blocks are clearly detailed.
- Objective function is well-defined and the four composing terms are clearly explained.
- Experiments are well-designed with thorough ImageNet evaluation and informative comparisons to baselines and other methods.
- Introduces a new saliency metric and demonstrates its effectiveness for evaluating saliency map quality.
- Originality: novel combination of existing strengths via a trainable masking model and U-Net architecture.
- Significance: potential impact on video saliency detection, object localization, image segmentation, and real-time explanations for black-box classifiers.
- For acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, strong experiments, and a new evaluation metric support publication.
- Against acceptance: reliance on a pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder may limit applicability to other domains or datasets.
- Against acceptance: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis for real-time application concerns and evaluation beyond ImageNet, and unclear extension to other applications like video or segmentation.
- Overall rating 8/10 with confidence 9/10.
