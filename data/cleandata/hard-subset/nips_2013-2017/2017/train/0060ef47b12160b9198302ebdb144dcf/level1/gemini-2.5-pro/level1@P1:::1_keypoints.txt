- Paper summary: Proposes a U-Net "masking model" that amortizes iterative saliency optimization to produce high-quality, real-time saliency masks by optimizing an objective enforcing SSR (preserve) and SDR (destroy) criteria.  
- Strength — Originality/significance: Reframes saliency as learned, amortized inference, bridging speed-quality gap and enabling real-time, high-quality saliency useful for video and other applications.  
- Strength — Technical quality/performance: Well-motivated objective combining SSR/SDR and regularization; achieves state-of-the-art weakly-supervised localization on ImageNet with compelling qualitative results.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear motivation, related work, method description, and helpful figures.  
- Strength — Generality/impact: Model-agnostic for differentiable classifiers, demonstrates versatility (e.g., CIFAR-10) and enables new applications in real-time explainability.  
- Weakness — "Black box" terminology: Claim is misleading because training requires gradient access through the target classifier; only at inference is the target not needed.  
- Weakness — Training cost/complexity: Paper omits discussion of potentially heavy training cost (backprop through large classifiers) versus cheap inference.  
- Weakness — Masking-model bias risk: The masking model may introduce its own objectness priors that could mask or obscure target-classifier biases.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Objective uses four weighting hyperparameters with no analysis of selection or sensitivity.  
- Pro (accept): Novel, elegant, and effective solution to saliency generation with strong empirical validation.  
- Pro (accept): Achieves SOTA on a challenging benchmark, supporting quantitative superiority.  
- Pro (accept): Enables real-time, high-quality saliency maps, opening new application and research directions.  
- Pro (accept): Clear writing and well-supported claims/experiments.  
- Con (reject): Needs stronger discussion of training costs and potential masking-model biases.  
- Con (reject): "Black box" claim should be refined for accuracy.  
- Conclusion: Strong, impactful paper deserving acceptance; weaknesses are clarifications or areas for future work rather than fundamental flaws.
