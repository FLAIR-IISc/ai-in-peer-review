- Proposes a novel real-time saliency masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass instead of iterative optimization.  
- Builds on prior work (Zhou et al., Fong & Vedaldi) and targets their computational inefficiency.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region to better measure interpretability than localization accuracy.  
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with comprehensive experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Outperforms existing weakly supervised approaches on object localization while achieving real-time performance (~100 saliency maps/sec).  
- Uses a U-Net architecture with ResNet-50 encoder, which is well-suited for the task.  
- Provides thoughtful analysis of artifacts and evidence removal introduced by masking.  
- New saliency metric is well-motivated and yields explanations comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Practical contribution enables new applications such as video saliency due to real-time capability.  
- Thorough evaluation includes multiple baselines, iterative methods, and central/crop baselines.  
- Shows generalizability across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50).  
- Weakness: limited exploration of generalizability across radically different classifier architectures beyond the three tested.  
- Weakness: new metric evaluation would be strengthened by human evaluation of saliency map quality.  
- Overall: advances interpretability with a practical, efficient, technically sound method; paper is well-written and recommended for acceptance.
