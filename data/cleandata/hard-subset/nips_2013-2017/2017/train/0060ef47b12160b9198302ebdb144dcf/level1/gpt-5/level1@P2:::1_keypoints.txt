- Proposes a fast, model-conditioned saliency method training a masking U-Net to produce per-class saliency maps for a fixed differentiable image classifier.
- Objective unifies SSR and SDR using TV smoothness, area sparsity, sufficiency, and destruction terms.
- Mitigates masking artifacts by blending with randomized alternatives (blur/noise) and enforcing smooth, low-frequency masks.
- Class conditioning via a learned class embedding and multiplicative feature filter; training injects fake labels 30% of the time to force attention to the class selector.
- Introduces a saliency metric s(a,p)=log(max(a,0.05))−log(p) measuring crop area vs retained class confidence.
- Empirical evaluation on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) and CIFAR-10 shows sharp masks, competitive WSOL, and strong metric scores.
- Claims orders-of-magnitude speed advantage over iterative perturbation methods by using a single forward pass.
- Strength: clear SSR/SDR framing and objective with thoughtful artifact-handling design (TV, down/up-sampling, random A).
- Strength: practical, real-time, feed-forward saliency predictor that generalizes across images/classes.
- Strength: sensible, interpretable architecture and an effective fake-label training trick.
- Strength: empirical breadth across multiple classifiers and datasets; WSOL results strong for a weakly supervised method.
- Strength: proposed metric aligns with SSR intuition and reduces adversarial masking by validating on crops.
- Weakness: “black box” term is misleading since training requires backprop through a differentiable classifier; clarify scope.
- Weakness: limited ablations (TV weight, mask resolution, random A schedule, fake-label probability, objective weight trade-offs).
- Weakness: metric depends on aspect-ratio-ignoring resizing of crops, which may bias results given classifier invariances.
- Weakness: quantitative comparisons to iterative and CAM-style baselines lack depth (compute budgets, threshold sensitivity, tuning details).
- Weakness: generalization under domain shift and to unseen classes/datasets is not evaluated.
- Weakness: CIFAR-10 evaluation is mainly qualitative; lacks quantitative localization or metric results.
- Quality: generally strong but weakened by missing ablations and fairness checks.
- Clarity: good overall; needs minor clarifications on optimizer/training details and “black box” phrasing.
- Originality: moderate-to-strong—novelty in feed-forward class-conditioned predictor and combined SSR/SDR objective.
- Significance: high practical impact due to speed and interpretability; scientific impact would increase with broader analysis.
- Questions for authors: do you require gradient access during training, and can you clarify applicability when gradients are unavailable?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to random A schedule, TV weight, and fake-label probability—please provide ablations?
- Questions for authors: does using predicted class vs ground-truth as the class selector change results?
- Questions for authors: have you tested aspect-ratio-preserving crops or multiple crop shapes, and how does that affect the metric/rankings?
- Overall assessment: solid, timely contribution with demonstrated effectiveness and speed; lean accept pending clearer classifier-access scope and additional ablations.
