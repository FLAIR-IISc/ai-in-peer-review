This paper presents a novel approach to real-time image saliency detection for black box classifiers. The authors propose a trainable masking model that can produce high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications. The model is trained to minimize an objective function that encourages smoothness, small region size, and high classification probability. The authors also introduce a new saliency metric that evaluates the quality of explanations produced by saliency detectors.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The related work section is thorough, and the authors provide a good overview of existing saliency detection methods. The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed method outperforming other weakly supervised techniques on the ImageNet localisation task.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide high-quality saliency maps in real-time. The authors demonstrate that their method can process over 100 images per second on a standard GPU, making it suitable for applications such as video saliency detection. The proposed saliency metric is also a valuable contribution, as it provides a more nuanced evaluation of saliency detectors than traditional metrics.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide more analysis of the learned class embeddings and how they relate to the saliency maps produced by the model. Additionally, the authors could explore the robustness of their method to different types of noise and perturbations.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to saliency detection that combines the strengths of model-based and optimization-based methods. The use of a trainable masking model and the introduction of a new saliency metric are both original contributions.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a difficult problem in computer vision and provides a new approach to saliency detection that is both fast and accurate. The results have the potential to impact a wide range of applications, from image classification to video analysis.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of computer vision, and their work has the potential to impact a wide range of applications.
