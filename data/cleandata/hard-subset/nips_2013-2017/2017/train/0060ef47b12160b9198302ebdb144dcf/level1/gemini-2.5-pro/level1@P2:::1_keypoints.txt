- Introduces a novel masking model that predicts saliency masks in a single forward pass for real-time explanation.
- Uses a U-Net architecture with a ResNet-50 encoder trained to produce small, smooth masks that preserve sufficient and remove critical evidence (SSR and SDR).
- Objective combines TV smoothness, area minimization, and SSR/SDR losses to encourage high-quality masks.
- Positions method between fast gradient-based approaches and slow perturbation-based methods, aiming for high quality at inference speed.
- Proposes a new saliency metric s(a,p)=log(ã)/log(p) to quantify information concentration of cropped regions.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on ImageNet weakly supervised object localization.
- Shows qualitative results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 and an ablation where the masking model is trained from scratch on CIFAR-10.
- Evaluates across three classifiers (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50), highlighting applicability and classifier-dependent differences.
- Clear, well-organized writing with helpful figures explaining SSR/SDR and introduced evidence issues.
- Novel contribution: amortizing per-image optimization by training a predictive model for mask generation.
- Distinct from, but related to, contemporaneous optimization-based work (Fong & Vedaldi), with the predictive model as the key advance.
- Practical impact: achieves high-fidelity saliency at over 100 FPS, enabling real-time XAI applications.
- Advances weakly supervised localization and suggests potential for reducing annotation needs in object detection.
- Opens future directions: weakly supervised segmentation, video saliency, exploring model biases, and applying the explanation-network paradigm to other modalities.
- Strengths: strong novelty, compelling empirical results, real-time performance, and thorough presentation.
- Weakness: method requires classifier gradients during training, so not fully a black-box approach in practice.
- Weakness: a separate masking model must be trained per classifier, representing non-trivial computational overhead.
- Weakness: loss uses four hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) with no sensitivity analysis or guidance on selection.
- Overall conclusion: excellent, high-impact paper with minor issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
