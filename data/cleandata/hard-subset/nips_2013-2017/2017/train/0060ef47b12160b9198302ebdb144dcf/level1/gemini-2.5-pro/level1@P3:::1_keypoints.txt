- Proposes a learnable feed-forward U-Net masking model that generates saliency maps in one pass, amortizing iterative optimization.  
- Training objective combines smallest sufficient region (SSR), smallest destroying region (SDR), sparsity, and smoothness, with randomized masking to avoid introduced evidence.  
- Achieves real-time speed (100+ images/sec), enabling video and interactive model debugging.  
- Strong empirical results: new state-of-the-art on ImageNet weakly supervised localization (36.7% error) and sharper qualitative masks.  
- Introduces a new information-concentration saliency metric to evaluate compact, sufficient explanations.  
- Missing baselines: should compare to single-pass methods like Grad-CAM for speed and localization context.  
- The term "black box" is misleading because training requires classifier gradients (the classifier must be differentiable during training).  
- Training cost is high and a separate masking model must be trained per classifier, which is a practical limitation.  
- Metric design choices (tight rectangular crop, resizing) may penalize non-rectangular objects and distort classifier confidence and need justification.  
- Overall: novel, technically strong, and empirically validated work with minor clarifications needed; recommendation to accept.
