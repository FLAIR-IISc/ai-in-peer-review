- Introduces a real-time single-pass saliency model (U-Net with ResNet-50 backbone) for black-box classifiers.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR).  
- Prevents adversarial mask artifacts via mask smoothing and randomized evidence removal.  
- Outperforms existing weakly supervised methods on ImageNet/CIFAR-10 (36.7% localization error vs. VGG 34.3%).  
- Objective balances mask smoothness, region size, and classification performance; training uses 30% fake labels and randomized removal to avoid overfitting.  
- Acknowledges limitations: assumes classifier invariance to scale/aspect ratio and requires differentiable classifiers (so not truly "black box").  
- Metric instability concern when predicted probability p → 0; authors threshold area but do not fully address p-related instability.  
- Unclear generalization to unseen classes — model trained only on ImageNet classes.  
- Comparison to parallel work (Fong & Vedaldi) is present but lacks deeper, explicit contrast of objective differences.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and reproducible with clear figures; some implementation details (e.g., hardware for 100 masks/sec) are missing.  
- Novelty: learning to predict saliency maps in one forward pass for real-time use is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: enables 100+ masks/sec real-time applications (e.g., video saliency, autonomous systems) and advances interpretability metrics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, strong empirical results, and a valuable new metric.  
- Arguments against acceptance: misleading "black box" claim, metric instability, limited generalization, and incremental novelty relative to parallel work.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor issues to address in the camera-ready version.
