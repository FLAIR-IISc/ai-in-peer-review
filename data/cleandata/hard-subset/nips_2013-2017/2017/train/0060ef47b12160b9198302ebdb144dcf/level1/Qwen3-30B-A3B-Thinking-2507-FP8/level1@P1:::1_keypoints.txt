- Summary: Proposes a U-Net masking model with ResNet-50 encoder to predict saliency maps in one forward pass, introduces a combined objective (smoothness, small mask, classification) and a new SSR saliency metric, and outperforms weakly supervised methods.  
- Quality: Technically sound with robust handling of introduced evidence, comprehensive ImageNet/CIFAR-10 experiments, competitive localization (36.7% error), and thorough ablation studies; some hyperparameter/implementation details are under-specified.  
- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with helpful figures and math; relationship between the new metric and the objective and some exact training details could be clearer.  
- Originality: Novel single-pass saliency prediction and SSR metric, distinct from iterative or gradient-based prior work, with a new class-embedding masking architecture.  
- Significance: Enables real-time, high-quality saliency (100+ masks/s) for practical applications (e.g., video/autonomous vehicles) and provides a better evaluation tool for interpretability.  
- Strengths: Real-time speed, high-quality interpretable maps, new metric, thorough evaluation, clear differentiation from related work, and careful handling of introduced evidence.  
- Weaknesses: Reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder, limited analysis of masking-model biases, SSR metric evaluated only on ImageNet, and sparse discussion of memory/scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, practical approach with strong empirical results, valuable new metric, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on pre-trained encoder, incomplete bias analysis of the masker, and some reproducibility details missing.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, and significant contribution with minor weaknesses; strong recommendation to accept.
