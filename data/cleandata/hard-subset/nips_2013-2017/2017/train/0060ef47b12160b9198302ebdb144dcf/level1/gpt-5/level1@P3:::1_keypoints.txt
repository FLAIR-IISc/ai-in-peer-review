- Proposes a trainable U-Net-style masking model that outputs class-conditional saliency maps in one forward pass, optimized for smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).
- Training objective combines TV smoothness, area regularization, a preserve term (high class score on masked image), and a destroy term (low class score on inverse mask), using a randomized alternative image A to reduce artifacts.
- Introduces a new saliency metric s(a,p) based on log-area of tight crop and log-probability on the crop to measure SSR while discouraging adversarial artifacts.
- Amortizing perturbation optimization via a learned masker yields real-time inference and sharper, more faithful masks than iterative perturbation methods.
- Practical architecture/training details include class-conditional gating at coarse scale, two-channel output nonlinearity, and fake-class sampling to enforce conditioning.
- Demonstrates transfer to CIFAR-10 with a scratch-trained masking network, showing the method can work without a pre-trained encoder.
- Clear framing of SSR/SDR and thoughtful discussion of introduced-evidence risks with practical countermeasures (random-A, TV, low-res mask upsampling).
- Metric aims to reward faithfulness to SSR and reduce adversarial confounds by using cropping rather than masking.
- Claim of “black-box” is overstated: training requires a differentiable classifier and end-to-end gradients, so it is not strictly black-box.
- Equation/notation issues: Eq. (4) has sign inconsistencies with the text; the preserve term sign and λ4’s role need formal clarification.
- Metric definition ambiguity: s(a,p) = log(a˜) log(p) is unclear (possibly product of negative logs), needs precise formula, normalization, and sensitivity analysis.
- Missing comparisons to strong contemporaneous baselines (CAM/Grad-CAM, SmoothGrad, Integrated Gradients) and omission of common localization metrics (pointing-game, pixel overlap).
- Evaluation may be biased by metric–training alignment since the loss and metric both encourage SSR; needs metrics less aligned to training (e.g., deletion/insertion curves).
- Limited analysis of generalization: separate masker per classifier restricts practicality; cross-classifier transfer experiments are needed to support model-agnostic claims.
- Robustness and failure modes insufficiently explored: need qualitative failure cases, tests for adversarial/color-shift artifacts, and ablations (choices of A, TV strength, mask resolution).
- Reproducibility lacking: missing full optimizer/hyperparameter, schedule, batch size, augmentation, and dataset split details; public code recommended.
- Methodologically sound and well-motivated, with careful artifact discussion and sensible training/architecture choices.
- Paper is generally clear and well written, but key definitions (objective signs, metric formula) and evaluation protocols require correction and clarification.
- Amortized perturbation-based saliency is a novel and practical contribution with potential impact for real-time interactive use and video.
- Acceptance contingent on clarifying core equations/metric, adding stronger baselines, ablations, robustness analyses, and cross-classifier experiments.
- Suggestions: fix equation signs, define s(a,p) precisely, report deletion/insertion AUC and pointing-game, compare to Grad-CAM/SmoothGrad/Integrated Gradients, run ablations, analyze failure modes, explore cross-classifier transfer, and release code/models.
- Arguments for acceptance: compelling amortization idea, promising qualitative/quantitative results, and careful artifact mitigation.
- Arguments against acceptance: ambiguous equations/metric, incomplete comparisons, limited robustness/ablation analysis, and misleading “black-box” claim.
