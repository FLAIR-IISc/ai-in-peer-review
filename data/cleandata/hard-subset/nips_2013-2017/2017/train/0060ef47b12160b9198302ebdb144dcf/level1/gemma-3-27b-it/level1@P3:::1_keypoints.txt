- Proposes a novel trainable masking model for single-pass, real-time saliency detection for black-box classifiers.  
- Moves away from computationally expensive iterative saliency methods, offering substantial speed improvements.  
- Well-motivated work addressing interpretable explanations of classifier behavior.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (SSR), theoretically grounded.  
- Demonstrates competitive localization performance on ImageNet against weakly supervised and iterative methods.  
- Shows generalizability by applying the method successfully to CIFAR-10.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful diagrams and insightful discussion (e.g., fighting introduced evidence).  
- Objective function (Eq. 4) is complex with multiple hyperparameters; sensitivity and impact not thoroughly studied.  
- Choice of U-Net architecture is sensible but not sufficiently justified or compared to alternatives.  
- Proposed defenses against adversarial artifacts (Eq. 2, random A) are not rigorously or quantitatively evaluated.  
- Limited direct comparison to gradient-based saliency methods in runtime and quality using the new metric.  
- Dependence on a pre-trained ResNet encoder may introduce bias; training from scratch on ImageNet not explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical approach with promising results, good writing, and a valuable new metric.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablation of objective, limited adversarial mitigation analysis, potential pre-training bias.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that balances speed and accuracy; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: conduct thorough ablation of hyperparameters, evaluate adversarial mitigation methods quantitatively, compare to a representative gradient-based method, and explore training masking model from scratch on ImageNet.
