- Proposes a fast, accurate model-based saliency detection method applicable to any differentiable image classifier.  
- Trains a masking model to manipulate classifier scores by masking salient input regions.  
- Requires a single forward pass for saliency detection, enabling real-time use.  
- Generalises well to unseen images and is suitable for real-time systems.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet with sharp, interpretable, artifact-free saliency maps.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on smallest sufficient region (SSR) and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Evaluated on ImageNet object localisation, outperforming other weakly supervised methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough experimental results and analysis.  
- Strength: model-agnostic approach that works with any differentiable image classifier.  
- Strength: novel metric (SSR/SDR) offering a more nuanced saliency evaluation.  
- Limitation: reliance on a pre-trained ResNet encoder; unclear performance with other architectures or training protocols.  
- Limitation: sensitivity to hyperparameters requiring grid search, which may be impractical for some users.  
- Limitation: insufficient detailed analysis of computational complexity and unclear comparison to other methods despite reporting >100 images/sec.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to saliency detection with strong scores (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance = 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel metric, versatile model-agnostic method, thorough experiments and analyses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependency on ResNet pretraining, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited computational complexity analysis.
