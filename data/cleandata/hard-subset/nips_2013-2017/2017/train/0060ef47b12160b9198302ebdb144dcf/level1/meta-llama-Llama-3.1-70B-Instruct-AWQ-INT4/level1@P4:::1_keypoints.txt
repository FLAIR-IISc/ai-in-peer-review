- Proposes a fast, model-based saliency detection method for differentiable classifiers using a masking model that requires a single forward pass.  
- Trained and tested on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming other weakly supervised methods.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and concise with a thorough related-work review and effective motivation.  
- Methodology and training process are clearly explained, using a U-Net decoder and a pre-trained ResNet encoder.  
- Technical soundness is strong with well-justified design choices and a clear objective function.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric and demonstrates superior performance, comparable to ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Potential weakness: heavy reliance on the quality of the pre-trained ResNet encoder; performance with different or unpretrained encoders is unclear.  
- Evaluation could be more comprehensive—needs tests on additional datasets and more analysis of failure cases.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to saliency detection and explainability with strong potential impact.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, thorough evaluation, strong writing, and technical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependency on encoder pre-training and limited evaluation scope.
