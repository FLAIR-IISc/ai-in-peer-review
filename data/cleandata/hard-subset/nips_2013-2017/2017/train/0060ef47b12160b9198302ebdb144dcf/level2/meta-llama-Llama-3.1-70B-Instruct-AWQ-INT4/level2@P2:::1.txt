This paper introduces a novel approach for real-time image saliency detection using a trainable masking model. The proposed method can be applied to any differentiable image classifier and requires only a single forward pass, making it suitable for real-time applications. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the CIFAR-10 and ImageNet datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results in weakly supervised object localization.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The introduction effectively sets the stage for the problem of image saliency detection and the need for a fast and accurate method. The related work section is thorough, and the authors provide a good overview of existing methods, highlighting their limitations and the advantages of their proposed approach.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors propose a new saliency metric that measures the quality of explanations produced by saliency detectors. They also introduce a novel objective function that encourages the masking model to produce saliency maps that are both informative and smooth. The use of a U-Net architecture with a pre-trained ResNet encoder is a good choice, and the authors demonstrate that their method can be trained from scratch without relying on pre-trained models.

The experimental results are impressive, with the authors achieving state-of-the-art results in weakly supervised object localization on the ImageNet dataset. The comparison with other saliency detection methods is thorough, and the authors provide a good analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

One potential limitation of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their method. While they mention that their method can process over 100 images per second on a standard GPU, it would be useful to provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational requirements.

Another potential area for improvement is the evaluation of the saliency metric. While the authors provide a good explanation of the metric and demonstrate its effectiveness, it would be useful to provide a more detailed analysis of its properties and limitations.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes significant contributions to the field of image saliency detection. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, and the experimental results are impressive. With some minor revisions to address the potential limitations mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
