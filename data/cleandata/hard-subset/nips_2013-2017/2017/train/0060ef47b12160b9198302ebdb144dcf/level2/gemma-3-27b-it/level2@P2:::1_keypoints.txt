- Proposes training a dedicated masking model to produce real-time saliency maps for black-box classifiers, claiming much faster results with comparable or better quality on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet and introducing a new saliency metric.

Strengths:
- Novelty: Trains a direct saliency-predicting model instead of using iterative methods, addressing speed-accuracy trade-offs.
- Speed: Achieves real-time performance enabling potential video and large-scale applications.
- Empirical results: Strong ImageNet performance and object localisation, outperforming some weakly supervised methods and comparing well to Fong & Vedaldi (2017).
- New metric: Introduces an SSR/SDR-inspired saliency metric (s(a,p)) with an information-theoretic justification.
- Thoroughness: Addresses adversarial/artifact issues and validates across datasets (CIFAR-10 and ImageNet).
- Clarity: Well-written presentation and helpful figures.

Weaknesses:
- Objective complexity: Loss (Eq.4) has multiple terms and hyperparameters (λ1–λ4) with insufficient justification and limited ablation.
- Architecture choices: U-Net and design details (two-channel output, non-standard nonlinearity) lack explanation; unclear why standard activations failed.
- Generalization: Unclear how well the masking model transfers to very different classifier architectures beyond AlexNet/GoogleNet/ResNet-50.
- Evidence removal ablation: No explicit experiments comparing different evidence removal methods (blurring, noise) to show which are most effective.
- Metric validation: New saliency metric’s correlation with human-perceived saliency is not established.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, technically sound approach addressing a practical need for fast saliency detection.
- Promising empirical results and a useful new evaluation metric.
- Well-presented and carefully considered methodology.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Multiple hyperparameters in the objective lack strong justification.
- Limited exploration of generalization across diverse classifiers.
- Ablation studies (including evidence removal comparisons) are incomplete.

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions: provide more justification and ablations for hyperparameter choices, compare evidence-removal techniques, and expand ablation studies to clarify design decisions and contributions.
