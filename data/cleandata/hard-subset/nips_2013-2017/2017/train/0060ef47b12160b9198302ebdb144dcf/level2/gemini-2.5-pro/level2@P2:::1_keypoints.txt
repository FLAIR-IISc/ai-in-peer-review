- Proposes training a U-Net “masking model” to predict saliency masks in one forward pass, replacing slow per-image iterative optimization.
- Trains masks to be small and smooth while preserving classifier score for the selected region (SSR) and destroying score for the removed region (SDR).
- Treats the target classifier as a black box at inference but requires differentiability (white-box access) during training.
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on information concentration in a cropped region.
- Achieves state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet and produces sharp, real-time saliency maps.
- Novel reframing from per-instance optimization to a learned predictive model enables dramatic speedups and real-time applications.
- Strong empirical results that approach some fully supervised methods, validating the approach.
- Qualitative outputs are high-quality—sharp, well-localized, and less noisy than gradient-based or iterative baselines.
- The “black box” claim is misleading since training requires gradients; clarify as white-box training with black-box inference.
- Lacks discussion of offline training cost and storage overhead for training a masking model per classifier.
- Missing analysis on hyperparameter sensitivity, deeper justification for the new metric (log-log form, resizing confounder, 0.05 area threshold), and direct quantitative comparison to concurrent iterative methods.
