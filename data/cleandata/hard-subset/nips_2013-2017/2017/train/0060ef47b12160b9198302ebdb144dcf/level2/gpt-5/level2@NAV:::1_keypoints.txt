- Proposes a trainable masking network that outputs per-class saliency maps in one forward pass, amortizing optimization into a U-Net conditioned on class embeddings.
- Claims: fast, model-agnostic saliency generation; unified SSR+SDR objective with TV/area penalties; new confidence-vs-crop-area saliency metric; improved weakly supervised localization results.
- Method is technically sound and well-motivated; training tricks (randomized evidence removal, TV/area regularization, fake-label conditioning) effectively address artifacts and class-agnostic behavior.
- Empirical support includes qualitative masks across AlexNet/GoogLeNet/ResNet, competitive ImageNet localization, and CIFAR-10 experiments from scratch.
- Quantitative evaluation lacks error bars, statistical tests, and ablations for loss weights, removal schemes, and TV strength.
- “Real-time” claim needs precise hardware/runtime profiling to substantiate throughput/latency.
- Clarity is good with implementation details, but the “black-box” descriptor is misleading because training requires backprop through the classifier.
- Novel contribution: amortizing SDR/SSR into a learned saliency predictor is original and practically impactful.
- New metric s(a,p) is interesting but ad hoc; dependence on resize/aspect-ratio handling and classifier scale invariance needs deeper discussion.
- Significance: useful for interactive/real-time saliency and weakly supervised localization; potential for video saliency.
- Adoption would be improved by releasing code and including broader baselines (e.g., Grad-CAM) for comparison.
- Recommended experiments: ablations (loss components, A choices, mask resolution, TV strength), variance across seeds, metric calibration with human judgments, deletion/insertion comparisons, runtime profiling, and cross-class/model generalization tests.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, fast, accurate approach with clear architectural/training insights and strong qualitative results.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited quantitative rigor (no ablations/statistics) and overstated “black-box” claim.
