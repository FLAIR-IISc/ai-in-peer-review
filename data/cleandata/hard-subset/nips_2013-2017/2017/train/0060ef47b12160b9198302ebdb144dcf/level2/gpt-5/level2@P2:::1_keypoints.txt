- Proposes a trainable, model-agnostic U-Net masking network that produces class-conditional saliency maps in a single forward pass, replacing iterative perturbation optimization.  
- Objective combines mask smoothness (TV), area penalty, smallest sufficient region (SSR), and smallest destroying region (SDR).  
- Reduces artifact-driven explanations via randomized evidence-removal strategies (strong blur vs. random color+noise) and low-res masks upsampled bilinearly.  
- Introduces a saliency metric s(a,p)=log(area)·log(probability) intended to reflect SSR trade-offs.  
- Experiments on ImageNet (AlexNet, GoogLeNet, ResNet-50) show improved weakly supervised localization and favorable saliency metric values; CIFAR-10 qualitative results suggest encoder-free applicability.  
- Positions itself relative to gradient-based saliency, iterative erasure methods (Fong & Vedaldi), and activation-sparsification; closest to Fong & Vedaldi but amortized and with SSR+SDR and artifact countermeasures.  
- Missing discussion/comparison to contemporaneous methods like Grad-CAM and SmoothGrad.  
- Strength: very fast (single forward pass, >100 fps) enabling real-time use.  
- Strength: well-reasoned objective addressing artifacts and occlusion heuristics.  
- Strength: appropriate U-Net architecture with class-embedding filtering and effective fake-label trick for selectivity.  
- Strength: empirical gains—sharper masks and competitive/better localization vs. several baselines.  
- Strength: generality across external classifiers and datasets.  
- Weakness: “black-box” claim misleading since training requires backprop through the classifier’s softmax.  
- Weakness: proposed metric is ad hoc, calibration-sensitive, not normalized, and could be gamed by overconfident models.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparisons—no CAM/Grad-CAM/Excitation Backprop or matched Fong & Vedaldi baselines.  
- Weakness: missing ablations (removal choices, TV weight, mask resolution, mask nonlinearity) and limited iterative-baseline budgeted comparisons.  
- Weakness: reproducibility underspecified (optimizer, LR schedule, batch size, data splits, formalization of the fake-label loss trick).  
- Weakness: localization/evaluation protocol unclear (label availability at test, split, crop regime, classifier-accuracy confounds).  
- Weakness: CIFAR-10 results qualitative only; no deletion/insertion curves or human interpretability study.  
- Quality: technically sound with coherent objective; evidence supports claims but needs stronger baselines and metric analysis.  
- Clarity: generally clear but some notation/objective and training/eval details ambiguous.  
- Originality: novel amortization of perturbation-based saliency and SSR+SDR combination; metric is new but heuristic.  
- Significance: promising practical impact if validated by stronger comparisons; likely adoptable.  
- Suggestions: clarify black-box assumption and training details; add CAM/Grad-CAM and matched Fong & Vedaldi baselines; analyze metric calibration/robustness; run ablations; report failure cases and class-conditional results; release code/models.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, fast, well-motivated with promising empirical results and practical relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete strongest-baseline comparisons, heuristic metric, missing ablations and reproducibility gaps.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on strengthening baselines, clarifying evaluation, and adding ablations.
