- Introduces a novel, model-based saliency detection method producing saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use.  
- Uses a U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder to generate sharp, precise, and artifact-free saliency maps.  
- Evaluated comprehensively on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming weakly supervised baselines in interpretability and sharpness.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric that aligns with the method’s results and aims to better assess saliency quality.  
- Achieves high practical utility by running at over 100 images per second, suitable for video and autonomous systems.  
- Paper is well-organized with sufficient technical details, visuals, and quantitative results to support reproducibility.  
- Lacks deep discussion of potential biases in the masking model and the impact of relying on pre-trained networks like ResNet-50.  
- Does not address generalization beyond image domains (e.g., text or tabular data), limiting broader applicability.  
- Comparison to fully supervised saliency/localization methods is brief and could be more thorough.  
- The proposed metric is well-motivated but its validation, adoption, and alignment with human interpretability remain unclear.  
- Strong empirical results and a novel efficient solution address an important explainability problem, supporting acceptance.  
- Real-time capability and practical relevance further justify the paper’s contribution.  
- Concerns about limited bias discussion and the need for broader validation of the new metric argue against acceptance by some.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to expand bias analysis and validate the new metric in future work.
