- Proposes a novel masking model that predicts saliency maps directly for black-box classifiers, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Trains a masking model instead of using iterative methods, substantially improving speed for real-time applications.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric (SSR/SDR-based) that complements localisation accuracy with more nuanced evaluation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on ImageNet object localisation and CIFAR-10.  
- Well-motivated problem framing and clear positioning within interpretability and adversarial example literature.  
- Related work review is comprehensive, covering gradient, iterative removal, and optimization-based methods.  
- Uses a sensible masking architecture (U-Net with ResNet encoder) leveraging established feature extraction.  
- Operates as a true black-box method, not requiring classifier internals, which is a practical strength.  
- Thorough comparisons to baselines and state-of-the-art methods, including a useful ablation on CIFAR-10 without pretraining.  
- Carefully considers and mitigates “introduced evidence” when applying masks, showing methodological thoughtfulness.  
- Objective function (Eq. 4) is well-defined, incorporating smoothness (TV) and preservation of classification score.  
- Choice of alternative image A (blurred vs. random noise) during training feels arbitrary and needs stronger justification and systematic exploration.  
- Potential biases of the masking model are acknowledged but not analyzed; impacts and mitigation strategies are missing.  
- The new saliency metric’s reliance on resizing crops may introduce distortions; the metric’s robustness should be investigated.  
- The learned class embedding is visualized in the appendix but its significance and relation to saliency maps are not discussed in the main text.  
- Connection between the total variation penalty and reduction of adversarial artifacts is not made explicit; visual examples varying TV strength would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, fast black-box approach with strong results, solid objective, comprehensive experiments, and a novel metric.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for design choices (A), unexamined masking biases, unclear metric resizing effects, and limited embedding discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; revision to address these points is recommended.
