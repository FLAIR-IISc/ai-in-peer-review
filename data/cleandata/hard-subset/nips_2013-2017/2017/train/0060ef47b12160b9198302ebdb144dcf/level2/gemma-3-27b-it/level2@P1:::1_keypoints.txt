- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, avoiding iterative methods.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the "smallest sufficient region" (SSR) concept.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, outperforming other weakly supervised methods on object localization.  
- Well-motivated work addressing interpretability and the need for real-time saliency detection.  
- Single-pass approach enables potential applications in video and dynamic scenarios.  
- Extensive related work coverage and useful ablation studies (e.g., pre-training effects).  
- Limited analysis of artifacts introduced by mask application; mitigation techniques (blurring, noise) discussed but not deeply analyzed.  
- Evaluation focuses primarily on localization accuracy; lacks human-centric validation such as eye-fixation correlation.  
- Some notation and equations (notably equation (8)) are dense or appear ad-hoc and need clearer justification.  
- The term “evidence” is used frequently but not clearly defined relative to features.  
- Insufficient discussion of regularization strategies for the masking model and their effects on overfitting.  
- Supplementary material referenced but not included, limiting assessment of additional results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective, and fast method with compelling experimental results and practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need deeper artifact/bias analysis, broader evaluation (human-centric), and clearer notation/definitions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand artifact analysis, clarify notation, and include supplementary material.
