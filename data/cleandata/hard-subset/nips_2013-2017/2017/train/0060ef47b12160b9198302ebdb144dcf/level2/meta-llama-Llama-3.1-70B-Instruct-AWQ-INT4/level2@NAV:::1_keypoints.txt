- Proposes a fast, trainable masking model for saliency detection that manipulates classifier scores and generalizes to unseen images.  
- Requires only a single forward pass, enabling real-time saliency detection.  
- Model-agnostic: applicable to any differentiable image classifier.  
- Introduces a new, more informative saliency metric than traditional metrics.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ImageNet object localization among weakly supervised methods.  
- Technically sound with clear, detailed method description and experimental support.  
- Well-written and easy to follow.  
- Method is original and different from existing saliency approaches.  
- Addresses an important problem with potential for wide adoption.  
- Requires large amounts of training data to learn the masking model.  
- May struggle on images with complex or ambiguous content.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of performance across different image types and classifier architectures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical results, and sound methodology.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited analysis on failure modes and generalization across datasets/classifiers.  
- Recommendations: provide per-image/type and per-classifier performance analysis.  
- Recommendations: evaluate on additional datasets and tasks.
