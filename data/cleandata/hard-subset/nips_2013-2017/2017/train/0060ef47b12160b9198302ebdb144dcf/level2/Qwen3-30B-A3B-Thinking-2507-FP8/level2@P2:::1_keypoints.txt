- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency method: a masking model predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric based on the smallest sufficient region (s(a,p) = log(ã) - log(p)).  
- Demonstrated on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving real-time speed (~100 masks/second).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art weakly supervised localization on ImageNet (36.7% error).  
- Generalizes across classifiers (AlexNet, GoogleNet, ResNet-50) and works on CIFAR-10 without pretraining.  

- Fails to adequately differentiate from parallel work by Fong & Vedaldi (2017), harming originality claims.  
- New metric lacks strong theoretical justification and no human-evaluation correlation is provided.  
- Limited evidence for generalization beyond the presented ImageNet and CIFAR-10 results.  
- Training details (e.g., 30% fake labels, class embedding training) are under-described, hurting reproducibility.  
- Missing comparisons with important baselines like Grad-CAM and Guided Backprop limits significance.  

- Provide a clear methodological and empirical comparison to Fong & Vedaldi, including efficiency and quality.  
- Validate the new metric with human studies to show correlation with perceived saliency.  
- Add ablation studies on masking model architecture and objective function components.  
- Analyze failure modes and limitations (multiple objects, ambiguous classes).  
- Include comparisons with Grad-CAM and other popular fast saliency methods.  

- Overall: technically sound with significant speed gains and a potentially useful metric, but originality/significance weakened by insufficient differentiation and metric justification.  
- Recommendation: reject unless authors clearly compare to prior work and strengthen metric validation and reproducibility.
