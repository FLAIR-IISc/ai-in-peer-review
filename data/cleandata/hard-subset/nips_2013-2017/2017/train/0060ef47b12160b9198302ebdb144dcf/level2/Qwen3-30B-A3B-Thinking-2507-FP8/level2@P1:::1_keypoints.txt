- Paper proposes a novel, fast saliency detection model for differentiable classifiers that produces high-quality masks in a single forward pass.  
- Addresses slowdown of iterative saliency methods (e.g., Zhou et al., Fong & Vedaldi) by enabling real-time use.  
- Masking model uses a U-Net on ResNet-50 features to produce sharp, artifact-free saliency maps.  
- Introduces a new interpretability metric s(a,p) that better matches SSR/SDR notions than standard localization accuracy.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10; ResNet-50 model achieves 36.7% localization error and strong performance on the new metric.  
- Technical strengths: well-motivated masking framework, artifact-resistant masking (Eq. 2) and TV penalty, and a thoughtful new metric with convincing baseline comparisons.  
- Experimental strengths: CIFAR-10 ablation shows generalization without pretraining; ImageNet experiments include classifier ablations and weakly supervised comparisons.  
- Practical significance: 100+ FPS speed enables real-time applications like video saliency for autonomous systems.  
- Weakness: Claim of being a “black box” method is overstated—training requires classifier gradients / access to differentiable models.  
- Weakness: s(a,p) assumes classifier scale/aspect-ratio invariance and needs validation on diverse classifiers (e.g., MobileNet).  
- Weakness: Missing direct comparison to prominent gradient-based methods like Grad-CAM.  
- Weakness: Section 3.1 (adversarial artifacts) is slightly redundant and could be streamlined for clarity.  
- Recommendation: Strong contribution with minor clarifications and additional comparisons requested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
