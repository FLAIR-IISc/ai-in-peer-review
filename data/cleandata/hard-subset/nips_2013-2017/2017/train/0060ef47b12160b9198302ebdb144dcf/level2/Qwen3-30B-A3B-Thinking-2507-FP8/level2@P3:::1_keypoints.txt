- Proposes a fast, model-agnostic saliency detection method producing high-quality saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Core innovation: a trainable masking model optimizing an objective balancing smoothness, region size, preserved-region classification confidence, and confidence drop after removal.  
- Introduces a novel saliency metric s(a,p) that evaluates the smallest sufficient region (SSR) and better captures interpretability than standard localization measures.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, outperforming iterative baselines in localization accuracy and on s(a,p).  
- Reports 36.7% localization error on ImageNet, competitive with fully supervised methods, and generalization across AlexNet, GoogleNet, and ResNet.  
- CIFAR-10 experiments show robustness without pre-training, indicating adaptability to different datasets and architectures.  
- Technically sound approach that mitigates adversarial artifacts via dynamic evidence removal (randomized blur/noise) and a total variation penalty.  
- Practical advance: real-time saliency detection (100+ images/sec on standard GPU) with minimal computational overhead.  
- Clearly positioned relative to prior work by eliminating iterative optimization used in methods like Fong & Vedaldi and distinguishing from Zhou et al.  
- Weakness: claim of working “for any differentiable image classifier” is not fully substantiated—experiments only use ImageNet-trained convolutional models.  
- Suggests adding an ablation study across diverse classifier types (including non-convolutional architectures) to strengthen generalization claims.  
- Evidence removal strategy (50% blur, 50% random color) lacks theoretical justification and may introduce artifacts in high-frequency or natural scenes.  
- CIFAR-10 results need deeper analysis explaining how a randomly initialized masking model matches pre-trained variants and addresses bias.  
- Clarity issues: better connect the masking model architecture (U-Net with ResNet-50 backbone) to the objective, provide an example for s(a,p), and justify the 0.05 threshold.  
- Minor requests: clarify relationship between the feature filter (Eq.7) and class embedding learning, and provide GPU memory requirements to contextualize the “real-time” claim.  
- Overall significance: meaningful efficiency leap to single-pass saliency with a useful new metric; meets NIPS standards pending minor clarifications on generalization and evidence removal.
