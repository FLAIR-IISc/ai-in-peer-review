- Proposes a novel masking model that predicts saliency maps in a single forward pass, enabling real-time use with any differentiable image classifier.  
- Trains the model with an objective encouraging smoothness, small saliency regions, and preservation of classification.  
- Provides a thorough literature review and highlights limitations of iterative saliency methods.  
- Introduces a new metric measuring relative information between an indicator variable and the cropped region for evaluating saliency.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, outperforming weakly supervised methods in localization and achieving state-of-the-art ImageNet saliency performance.  
- Shows produced saliency maps are interpretable and generalize to unseen images.  
- Strength: novel, efficient approach applicable to any differentiable classifier.  
- Strength: comprehensive comparison to existing methods and clear motivation against iterative approaches.  
- Weakness: relies on classifier differentiability, limiting applicability to some black-box models.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity important for real-time claims.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and future work directions.  
- Originality: combines a masking model with a new objective and introduces a novel saliency evaluation metric.  
- Significance: impactful for real-time saliency applications (video, autonomous vehicles) and for improving classifier interpretability.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but needs clearer mathematical exposition and complexity details.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address limitations, add computational complexity analysis, and expand mathematical explanations.
