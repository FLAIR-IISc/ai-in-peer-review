- Introduces a novel trainable masking model for real-time image saliency detection requiring a single forward pass.  
- Applicable to any differentiable image classifier and suitable for real-time use.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, achieving state-of-the-art weakly supervised object localization.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise method description and effective introduction.  
- Related work section is thorough and highlights limitations of existing methods.  
- Technical contributions include a new saliency metric for explanation quality.  
- Proposes a novel objective that encourages saliency maps to be informative and smooth.  
- Uses a U-Net with a pre-trained ResNet encoder and shows training-from-scratch is possible.  
- Experimental comparisons are thorough with good analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis despite reporting >100 images/sec on a GPU.  
- Evaluation of the proposed saliency metric could use deeper analysis of properties and limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions and potential high impact.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, technical soundness, impressive experimental results.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient computational complexity analysis and limited evaluation of the saliency metric.
