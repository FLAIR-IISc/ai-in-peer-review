- Proposes a fast saliency detection method using a trainable masking model that manipulates classifier scores and generalizes to unseen images with a single forward pass for real-time use.
- Introduces a new saliency metric and reports superior weakly supervised localization results on ImageNet.
- Builds on gradient-, iterative-, and optimization-based saliency work, claiming improved efficiency and fewer artifacts.
- Strength — Efficiency: single-pass, real-time capable, suitable for video saliency.
- Strength — Effectiveness: produces high-quality, interpretable saliency maps that outperform weakly supervised methods.
- Strength — Generality: applicable to any differentiable image classifier.
- Strength — New metric: proposes a more informative saliency evaluation metric.
- Weakness — Assumes classifier differentiability, limiting applicability to some models.
- Weakness — Requires large datasets to train the masking model.
- Weakness — Requires careful hyperparameter tuning (objective weights), which is time-consuming.
- Originality: Novel combination of a trainable masking model and a new metric that advances saliency detection.
- Significance: Impacts image classification, object detection, and video analysis through real-time, improved saliency/localization.
- For acceptance: Novel, effective, real-time approach with a more informative metric.
- Against acceptance: Differentiability and data/training demands plus limited experimental breadth.
- Revision — Provide more extensive experiments and comparisons to other methods.
- Revision — Discuss method limitations and potential future work.
- Revision — Add detailed training procedures and hyperparameter settings to aid reproducibility.
