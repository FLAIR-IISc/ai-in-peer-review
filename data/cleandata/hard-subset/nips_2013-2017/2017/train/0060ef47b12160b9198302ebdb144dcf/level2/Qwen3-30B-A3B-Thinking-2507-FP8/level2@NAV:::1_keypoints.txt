- Introduces a novel single-pass masking model for real-time saliency detection of black-box classifiers.  
- Trains a model to predict saliency masks directly, avoiding slow iterative methods.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric (Eq. 3) that evaluates the "smallest sufficient region" for interpretability.  
- Uses a U-Net with ResNet backbone architecture, justified and optimized for speed.  
- Achieves real-time performance (100+ images/sec on GPU), enabling practical applications.  
- Experiments on ImageNet and CIFAR-10 show strong results; ResNet-trained model reports 36.7% localization error vs 34.3% for supervised VGG, notable for weak supervision.  
- Addresses adversarial artifacts via careful evidence-removal techniques in the architecture.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior methods (e.g., Fong & Vedaldi) and discusses limitations.  
- Claim of generalization to unseen images needs further validation on datasets beyond ImageNet/CIFAR-10.  
- The new metric’s behavior on edge cases or specific object classes is not fully explored.  
- Would benefit from clearer justification for choosing U-Net+ResNet over simpler architectures.  
- High significance for safety-critical real-time applications; paper is clear, reproducible, and recommended for acceptance.
