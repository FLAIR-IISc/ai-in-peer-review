- Introduces a generative masking model (U-Net with ResNet encoder) that produces saliency masks for an image-class pair in a single forward pass.  
- Trains the model with a composite objective enforcing smoothness, smallness, and both Smallest Sufficient Region (SSR) and Smallest Destroying Region (SDR).  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on weakly supervised object localization and evaluates on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric for evaluating explanation quality.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and experimentally convincing, including a from-scratch CIFAR-10 experiment.  
- Lacks ablation/sensitivity analysis for the four hyperparameters (λ₁–λ₄) and the contribution of each loss term.  
- The claim of a “black box” classifier is misleading: training requires white-box (gradient) access and a new masker must be trained per target classifier.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, with effective figures and good explanations of SSR and SDR.  
- The new metric’s specific form and the hard threshold at 0.05 need clearer justification.  
- Equation 8’s non-standard nonlinearity outperforms sigmoid/tanh, but lacks explanation for why.  
- Conceptually original: reframes saliency as a learned, amortized prediction rather than per-instance optimization.  
- Potentially high impact by enabling real-time saliency (100+ FPS) and broader interactive or video applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and impactful approach, SOTA results, general applicability, strong experiments and writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise “black box” terminology and missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with minor, addressable weaknesses mainly around clarity and additional analysis.
