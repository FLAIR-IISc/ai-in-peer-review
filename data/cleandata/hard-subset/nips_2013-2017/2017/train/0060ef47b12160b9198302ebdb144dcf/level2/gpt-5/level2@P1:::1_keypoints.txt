- Proposes a trainable U-Net masking network with class-conditional gating that produces class-specific saliency maps in a single forward pass.  
- Loss encourages smooth, compact masks, preserves classifier confidence when keeping salient region (SSR), and suppresses it when removing salient region (SDR).  
- Mitigates introduced-evidence artifacts by blending with blurred or randomized backgrounds and using total variation regularization for smoothness.  
- Introduces a saliency metric based on class probability from the tightest crop containing the salient region normalized by crop area (SSR-inspired).  
- Evaluated on ImageNet (weakly supervised localization) and CIFAR-10; claims sharper maps, competitive localization, and much faster inference than iterative methods.  
- Positioned relative to gradient/backprop explanations and iterative perturbation methods; closest to Fong & Vedaldi (2017) but novel in learning a reusable predictor and joint SSR/SDR loss.  
- Should more directly compare to CAM/Grad-CAM variants (e.g., Grad-CAM, CAM) as relevant baselines for localization.  
- Strength: novel, practical design enabling real-time, high-quality saliency maps and a reusable predictor.  
- Strength: thoughtful artifact analysis and use of randomized alternatives plus TV regularization to reduce introduced evidence.  
- Strength: simple effective architecture (U-Net with class embedding gating) and clever “fake class” training trick.  
- Strength: proposed SSR-inspired crop-probability/area metric offers a complementary evaluation viewpoint.  
- Strength: qualitative maps look sharp; CIFAR-10 shows approach can work without a pre-trained encoder.  
- Weakness: possible sign/notation errors—Eq. (4) likely has sign mistake for the preservation term; Eq. (3) metric definition is ambiguous (product vs ratio).  
- Weakness: method is not strictly “black box” since training requires backprop through the classifier; clarify gradient access assumptions.  
- Weakness: missing evaluation specifics—binarization thresholds, threshold sensitivity, metric stability across crop shapes/resizing, and variance/confidence intervals.  
- Weakness: missing key ablations (λ1–λ4, exponent choice, alternative image A, TV weight, fake-class trick) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: generalization not studied—no cross-classifier transfer or few-shot adaptation results.  
- Weakness: robustness and artifact suppression lack quantitative analysis and failure-case reporting (small objects, contextual dependence).  
- Weakness: incomplete baselines—need Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++ and CAM-style comparisons.  
- Weakness: real-time/video claims lack video experiments or latency/streaming analysis.  
- Quality: technically sound overall, but needs equation fixes and stronger statistical/ablation rigor.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized; request precise notation, normalization/threshold details, training hyperparameters, and an algorithm box.  
- Originality: high—training a reusable saliency predictor with joint SSR/SDR and artifact-aware design is a substantive contribution.  
- Significance: high practical impact for large-scale and potential video use, though per-classifier training is a deployment limitation.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, practical, artifact-aware, competitive localization, useful metric, strong qualitative results.  
- Cons for acceptance: equation ambiguities, missing ablations/statistics, black-box terminology mismatch, incomplete baselines, limited robustness/generalization analysis.  
- Questions for authors: clarify Eq. (4) sign and Eq. (3) metric definition; specify binarization thresholds and sensitivity; provide ablations on λ’s, A choices, and exponent; report per-classifier training time/data and cross-classifier transfer; include Grad-CAM results or discussion; provide quantitative artifact-suppression analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept conditional on fixing equation/notation issues and strengthening evaluation with requested ablations, baselines, and statistical reporting.  
- Confidence: high for novelty/positioning assessment; medium for quantitative claims due to missing details.
