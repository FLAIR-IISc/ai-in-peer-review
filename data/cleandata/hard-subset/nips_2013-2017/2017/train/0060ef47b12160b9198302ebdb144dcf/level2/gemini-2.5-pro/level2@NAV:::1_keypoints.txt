- Proposes training a separate generative model (U-Net with ResNet-50 encoder) to produce saliency masks for any differentiable classifier in a single forward pass.  
- Amortizes the cost of perturbation-based explanations, aiming for both high speed and high-quality saliency maps.  
- Contrasts with prior methods: gradient-based (fast but lower-quality) and iterative optimization (high-quality but slow).  
- Introduces a comprehensive objective function for training the masking model.  
- Proposes a new saliency metric to evaluate interpretability of maps.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on ImageNet and CIFAR-10.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on weakly supervised object localization.  
- Extensive experiments across multiple datasets and target models support the claims.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and discusses pitfalls like “introduced evidence” from masking.  
- Main practical weakness: requires training and storing a separate complex masking model per target classifier, which can be prohibitive.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential for a single masking model to generalize across similar black-box architectures.  
- New saliency metric is biased toward classifiers robust to aspect ratio distortion and this limitation should be noted.  
- Arguments for acceptance: highly novel, impactful, SOTA empirical results, thorough validation, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: significant computational/storage overhead and unaddressed metric biases.  
- Overall judgment: novelty and strong empirical evidence outweigh weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
