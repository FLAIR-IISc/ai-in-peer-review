- Proposal: learned, feed-forward masking model to generate image saliency maps for differentiable classifiers, avoiding iterative methods.  
- Motivation: well-motivated by computational expense of existing techniques and need for speed (e.g., video saliency).  
- Model design: masking model built on a U-Net architecture; some architectural choices (two-channel output, specific nonlinearity) lack full justification.  
- New metric: introduces SSR/SDR-based saliency metric providing a more nuanced evaluation than standard localization accuracy.  
- Experiments: promising results on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, showing competitive weakly supervised object localization.  
- Ablation and robustness: includes ablation study and discussion of adversarial artifacts with mitigation strategies.  
- Regularization: uses TV penalty and averaging, but the link between these terms and preventing adversarial artifacts is not clearly articulated.  
- Classifier dependence: evaluates impact of different black-box classifiers but lacks deeper analysis of why some classifiers produce better masks.  
- Assumptions/limitations: assumes classifier differentiability; this limitation should be stated and discussed in real-world contexts.  
- For acceptance: novel and efficient approach, strong experiments, new theoretically grounded metric, clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient justification for some architectural choices, limited discussion of differentiability assumption, and incomplete analysis of classifier-driven performance differences.  
- Recommendation: overall solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted issues.
