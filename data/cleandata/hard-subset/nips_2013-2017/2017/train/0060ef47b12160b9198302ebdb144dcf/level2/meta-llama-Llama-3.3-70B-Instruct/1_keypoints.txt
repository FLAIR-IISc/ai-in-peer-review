- Novel fast and accurate model-based saliency detection method applicable to any differentiable image classifier.  
- Main claim: outperforms weakly supervised techniques on ImageNet localization and produces high-quality saliency maps.  
- Method is well-supported by theoretical analysis, experiments, and comparisons with existing methods.  
- Clear explanation of objective function, architecture, and training process.  
- Experimental results achieve state-of-the-art performance on ImageNet localisation and beat popular saliency detectors.  
- Introduces a new saliency metric that better assesses interpretability and nuances strengths/weaknesses of approaches.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, clarity, originality, thorough related-work analysis, and well-organized writing.  
- Limitation: the approach may have biases that influence generated saliency masks.  
- Limitation: paper focuses mainly on image classification—generalization to other tasks/domains is unclear.  
- Paper would benefit from additional analysis and discussion of limitations and potential biases.  
- Overall: significant contribution with potential impact on applications like autonomous vehicles and medical imaging.  
- Pro acceptance: novelty, strong experiments, SOTA results, and a useful new metric; Con acceptance: limited generalization and acknowledged biases.
