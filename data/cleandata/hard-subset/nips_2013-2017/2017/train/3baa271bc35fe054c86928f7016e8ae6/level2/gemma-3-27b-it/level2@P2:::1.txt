This paper tackles the problem of robust streaming submodular maximization, a relevant and challenging area with applications in data summarization, influence maximization, and recommendation systems. The authors present STAR-T, an algorithm that efficiently computes a robust summary of a data stream, and provides theoretical guarantees on the approximation ratio even after removing a specified number of elements from the summary. The contributions are well-defined and the problem is clearly motivated. The paper builds upon existing work in streaming submodular maximization (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and robust submodular maximization, and proposes a novel approach using partitioned thresholds.

**Strengths:**

* **Problem Relevance:** The problem addressed is important and practically motivated. Robustness against removals is a crucial consideration in many real-world applications where data is unreliable or dynamic.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, appears to be well-designed, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous, providing a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  The partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule are innovative. The proof sketch in Section 4 demonstrates a good understanding of the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The problem statement is clearly defined, and the algorithm is presented in a concise and understandable manner.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both dominating set and movie recommendation tasks are well-executed and demonstrate the effectiveness of STAR-T, particularly its robustness compared to baseline methods like SIEVE-STREAMING. The comparison to RANDOM provides a useful baseline.
* **Interactive Setting:** Recognizing the applicability in interactive scenarios (e.g., user-specified filters in movie recommendation) is a strong point. The experiments showcase this benefit, demonstrating the value of a precomputed robust summary.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Constant Factor:** While a constant-factor approximation guarantee is provided, the constant (0.149) is quite small. This might limit the practical applicability of the algorithm in scenarios where a higher approximation ratio is desired. The dependence on `dlog k` in the approximation ratio is also concerning as it diminishes the constant as k grows.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The algorithm has several parameters (k, m, τ, w). The paper provides guidance on setting w, but the choice of τ, which relies on estimating `f(OPT(k, V \ E))`, might be challenging in practice. The extension in Theorem 4.5 addresses this but introduces additional complexity.
* **Space Complexity:** The space complexity of O((k + m log k) log k) can be significant when dealing with very large datasets and large values of m. Although the authors claim logarithmic dependence on *m* is an advantage over some other methods, the overall space requirement may still be a bottleneck.
* **Related Work Coverage:** While the related work section is reasonably comprehensive, it could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the trade-offs between different approaches to robust submodular maximization, particularly in the context of streaming algorithms. The work of [18] is mentioned but the key difference - their linear dependence on *k* in memory - could be highlighted more prominently.
* **Experimental Details:**  More details on the experimental setup would be helpful. For example, what specific low-rank matrix completion technique was used for the movie recommendation task? What are the parameter settings for SIEVE-STREAMING? Reporting confidence intervals or standard deviations in the experimental results would also strengthen the analysis.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem with strong theoretical foundations.
* The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, is novel and provides a provable approximation guarantee.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm and its robustness.
* The interactive setting highlighted is a valuable contribution and expands the applicability of the work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The constant factor in the approximation guarantee is relatively small.
* The space complexity may be prohibitive for very large datasets.
*  The dependency on estimating `f(OPT(k, V \ E))` could be a practical hurdle.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of robust streaming submodular maximization. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the algorithm is well-designed, and the experimental results are encouraging. While the small constant factor and space complexity are concerns, the overall quality of the work and its potential impact justify acceptance. I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, specifically providing more details on the experimental setup and further discussing the practical implications of the constant factor and space complexity. The authors should also consider clarifying how sensitive the performance is to the choice of parameter τ.
