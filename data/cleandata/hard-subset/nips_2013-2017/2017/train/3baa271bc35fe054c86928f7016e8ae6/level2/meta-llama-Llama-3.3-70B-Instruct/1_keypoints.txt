- Proposes STAR-T, a novel robust submodular streaming algorithm for monotone submodular maximization with cardinality and robustness constraints.  
- STAR-T makes one pass over the data and retains O((k + m log k) log k) elements.  
- Introduces STAR-T-GREEDY, which runs on the retained set and achieves a constant-factor approximation after removal of any m elements.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and well-organized technical sections; proofs are thorough and easy to follow.  
- Experimental results on two data summarization tasks show STAR-T outperforms existing greedy and streaming methods.  
- Strength: novel algorithm addressing an important problem in submodular optimization.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis including a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: algorithm requires a parameter τ that depends on the unknown value f(OPT(k, V \ E)).  
- Weakness: the proposed parallel variant to approximate τ increases space complexity.  
- Weakness: overall high space complexity O((k + m log k) log k), which may limit practicality.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective method, solid theory, and compelling experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on unknown τ and high space complexity.  
- Suggestions for improvement: explore reducing space complexity, add more experimental scenarios, and consider extending to more general submodular functions.
