- Addresses robust streaming submodular maximization; introduces STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY with theory and experiments.  
- Core technical idea: partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds to maintain a robust stream summary.  
- Provides theoretical approximation guarantees and a rigorous analysis.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on graph-based (dominating set) and recommendation tasks.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and convincingly motivated with real-world applications.  
- Related work is well-covered and correctly cites recent results (e.g., [18]).  
- Algorithm maintains a relatively small summary and can be reused for different removal sets, aiding interactive scenarios.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated algorithmic contribution with solid theoretical backing.  
- Weakness: small constant factors in the approximation guarantee (≈0.149) that may limit practical impact.  
- Weakness: theoretical bound on parameter w (w ≥ ⌈4d log k e m / k⌉) conflicts with experiments showing good performance at w=1; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: experimental results lack detailed statistical analysis (confidence intervals or p-values) to strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: baselines are appropriate but comparisons to more recent or specialized algorithms could further validate benefits.  
- Overall: high-quality paper making a significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
