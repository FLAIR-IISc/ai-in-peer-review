- Presents STAR-T, a novel streaming algorithm using partitioned thresholding for robust monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint.  
- STAR-T is one-pass, keeps a small memory footprint, and is designed to withstand removal of up to m elements.  
- Authors prove constant-factor approximation guarantees for STAR-T.  
- The partitioned thresholding approach is a novel methodological contribution to submodular optimization.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of problem, algorithm, and theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation on two data summarization tasks shows STAR-T performs comparably to or better than SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- STAR-T outperforms or matches SIEVE-STREAMING even when the latter knows removals in advance.  
- Theoretical results are thorough and support the algorithm’s performance.  
- Experimental results are convincing but could include more baselines for stronger validation.  
- Related work discussion is brief and would benefit from a more in-depth treatment of robust submodular maximization literature.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating given by reviewer: 8/10.
