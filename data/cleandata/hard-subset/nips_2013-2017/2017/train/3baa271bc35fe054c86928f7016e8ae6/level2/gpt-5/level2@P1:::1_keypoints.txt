- Paper studies one-pass streaming robust monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k with up to m deletions; proposes STAR-T summary and STAR-T-GREEDY query giving a constant-factor guarantee (≈0.149 as k→∞).
- STAR-T builds exponentially-sized buckets across partitions with exponentially decreasing thresholds; summary size O(k log k + m log^2 k).
- A τ-free parallel variant uses multiple τ values (like SIEVE-STREAMING), adding a log1+ε k space factor and a 1/(1+ε) loss.
- Experiments (dominating set on Twitter/Amazon, movie recommendation) show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING and supports multiple removals from one summary.
- Strength: problem is practically important (deletions, privacy) and fits the conference’s interests.
- Strength: algorithmic novelty—clean streaming extension combining partitioned robustness and thresholding.
- Strength: nontrivial theory—single pass, memory O(k log k + m log^2 k), constant-factor post-deletion guarantee with careful analysis.
- Strength: practical appeal—reusing one summary for different deletions and competitive empirical performance.
- Strength: clear positioning relative to greedy, streaming, robust non-streaming, and concurrent deletion-robust streaming work.
- Weakness: modest approximation constant (0.149) and further degradation in τ-free variant; need discussion on tightness and potential improvements.
- Weakness: practical details of maintaining multiple τ’s (choice of ε, number of instances, per-element overhead) are not reported in experiments.
- Weakness: theoretical requirement w ≥ ⌈4 m log k / k⌉ conflicts with experiments using w = 1 even for large m; need plots showing m vs. w trade-offs and guidance for practitioners.
- Weakness: missing empirical comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming algorithm (1/2 − ε, higher memory); at least a quantitative discussion of regime trade-offs is needed.
- Clarity issues: inconsistent threshold description (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}); unify and clarify to ensure full-bucket value claim.
- Clarity issues: inconsistent space bound expressions in abstract/conclusion vs theorem; standardize and highlight different scalings for k and m terms.
- Clarity issues: time complexity per-element update not clearly stated; theoretical scan is O(w k) with given w, report empirical throughput.
- Experimental details missing: streaming order, τ instantiation, whether lazy evaluations used in GREEDY, variance/error bars, and code release for reproducibility.
- Quality: theoretical contributions are sound, handling edge cases and standard assumptions; experiments are thoughtful but not exhaustive.
- Originality: combination of partitioned buckets with exponential thresholds for streaming robustness appears novel and meaningful.
- Significance: practical, theoretically grounded approach with attractive space bounds; relevant to many submodular applications with deletions.
- Pros for acceptance: simple single-pass algorithm, novel analysis with favorable space dependence, practical reuse of summaries, convincing experiments.
- Cons against acceptance: weak approximation constant, missing empirical comparison to stronger-memory baseline, limited guidance on τ and w and computational overhead.
- Key questions for authors: (1) how many τ-instances used, per-element time and memory, and sensitivity to ε? (2) can post-processing replace GREEDY (lazy/stochastic) without breaking theory? (3) provide experiments varying m and w to validate scaling? (4) can τ be adaptively tuned online to reduce parallel instances?
- Overall recommendation: solid and timely contribution; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity and experimental-detail concerns.
