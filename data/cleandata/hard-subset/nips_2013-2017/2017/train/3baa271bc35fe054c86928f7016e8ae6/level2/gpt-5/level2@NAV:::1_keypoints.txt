- Problem & contribution: Proposes STAR-T (streaming summary with partitioned, exponentially decreasing thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY for robust submodular maximization under post-stream deletions.
- Theoretical guarantee: Proves a constant-factor approximation (~0.149 as k grows) using memory O((k + m log k) log^2 k) and gives a parallel variant that removes the need to know OPT.
- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING thresholding to a robust setting and connects to partitioned robust structures from PRO (ICML’17) while differing in bucket population.
- Trade-off vs concurrent work: Trades a weaker approximation for much better k-dependence compared to [18], which achieves ~1/2−ε but uses O(mk log k/ε) memory.
- Strength — analysis quality: Nontrivial, careful analysis yielding a constant-factor guarantee and handling unknown OPT via parallel instances.
- Strength — originality: Partitioned thresholds in a streaming robust context appear novel and distinct from PRO and SIEVE-STREAMING.
- Strength — significance: Memory scaling nearly linearly in m and logarithmically in k is practically attractive for influence and recommendation tasks.
- Strength — empirical results: On dominating set and personalized recommendation tasks, STAR-T variants match or beat strong baselines, even when baselines know deletions.
- Weakness — approximation constant: The ~0.149 factor is low and the practical gap to 0.5 is not discussed.
- Weakness — clarity/inconsistencies: Discrepancies between text and Algorithm 1 (thresholds τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}) and inconsistent memory bound notation (log k vs log^2 k).
- Weakness — assumptions/sensitivity: Performance depends on τ and w; experiments use w=1 and often |E|>m, which exceed theoretical guarantees and lack systematic sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — empirical diagnostics: Missing wall-clock time, oracle-call counts, memory measurements, ablation studies, and reproducibility details (code, protocol, oracle costs).
- Pro-acceptance rationale: Novel, theoretically sound approach with improved memory scaling and convincing application-driven experiments.
- Con-acceptance rationale: Weak constant, limited empirical diagnostics, presentation inconsistencies, and incomplete parameter-sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested fixes — clarity: Unify and clearly state thresholds and memory bounds; fix Algorithm 1 vs. text discrepancies.
- Suggested fixes — empirical reporting: Report wall-clock/runtime, oracle-call counts, and actual memory; add ablations over w, τ, and number of partitions.
- Suggested fixes — comparisons & reproducibility: Compare memory/accuracy trade-offs to [18], discuss regimes of preference, and provide code plus detailed experimental protocols.
- Suggested improvements — theory: Explore tightening constants via improved lemma mixing or adaptive threshold schedules.
