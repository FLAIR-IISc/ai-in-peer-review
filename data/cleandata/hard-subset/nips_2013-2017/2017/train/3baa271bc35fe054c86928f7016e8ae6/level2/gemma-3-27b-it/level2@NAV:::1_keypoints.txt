- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where up to m elements may be removed after processing.  
- Proposes STAR-T: a partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholding for streaming robustness.  
- Positions work relative to SIEVE-STREAMING and prior robust submodular maximization research.  
- Well-motivated problem with applications in data summarization, influence maximization, and recommendations.  
- Novel combination of partitioning and dynamic thresholding to achieve robustness in streaming.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and a formal constant-factor approximation guarantee.  
- Proof sketch connects partitioning, thresholding, and resulting robustness clearly.  
- Requires knowing or approximating the optimal solution value; offers a parallel extension to address this.  
- Presentation clarity could be improved; partitioning and thresholding descriptions feel dense and would benefit from examples/diagrams.  
- Achieved constant factor (0.149) is relatively small, raising practical utility concerns versus simpler methods.  
- Experimental results show comparable performance to SIEVE-STREAMING but no clear advantage for STAR-T.  
- Memory parameter w and its impact are not thoroughly explored or analyzed experimentally.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, rigorous guarantees, and demonstrated comparable practical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small approximation constant, limited clarity, and weak experimental superiority.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and analyze sensitivity to parameter w.
