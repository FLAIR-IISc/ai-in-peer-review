- Summary: Proposes STAR-T (streaming) + STAR-T-GREEDY (query-time) for deletion-robust monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k, giving a constant-factor approximation after up to m deletions with space O((k + m log k) polylog k).  
- Summary: Parallel-guess variant removes need to know OPT with only logarithmic overhead; empirical results on dominating set and movie recommendation match or beat strong baselines.

- Prior work: SIEVE-STREAMING achieves ~0.5 one-pass non-robust approximation with O(k log k) space.  
- Prior work: Offline robust methods (Orlin et al., PRO) use partitioned constructions but not streaming constraints.  
- Prior work: Concurrent Mirzasoleiman et al. (ICML’17) gives 1/2 − ε approximation using O(m k log k/ε) memory; this paper trades approximation for much better memory scaling.

- Strength: Novel partitioned bucket design with exponentially decreasing thresholds extending greedy-thresholding to robust streaming.  
- Strength: Provable constant-factor guarantee (~0.149) for arbitrary deletions using one pass and near-linear space in m.  
- Strength: Two-stage summary supports multiple post-hoc queries (suits “right to be forgotten” and personalization).  
- Strength: Empirical validation shows competitive or superior performance even when competing methods know deletions.

- Weakness: Inconsistency in threshold specification (text τ/2^i vs Algorithm τ/min{2^i, k}); needs reconciliation for analysis and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Space bound ambiguity: paper alternately reports O((k + m log k) log k) and O((k + m log k) log^2 k); clarify source of extra log factor (e.g., parallel guesses).  
- Weakness: Theorem 4.1 τ expression has missing parentheses and is dimensionally unclear; provide exact formula and derivation.  
- Weakness: Experiments omit explicit measurements of memory footprint |S|, running time, and sensitivity to m, k, w, which are central to the paper’s claims.  
- Weakness: Lacks empirical comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming (Mirzasoleiman et al.); would contextualize memory–approximation trade-off.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup differences (removing from S vs running baselines on V\E) should be documented clearly (stream order, one-pass constraints, space limits).

- Quality: Algorithm is well-motivated; analysis carefully handles two complementary cases and uses standard greedy bounds.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written, but needs tightened formulae and parameter descriptions (w, τ) and pseudocode for parallel-guess variant.  
- Originality: Partitioned thresholding for robust streaming with constant-factor robustness and near-linear m scaling appears novel.  
- Significance: Timely and relevant; advances practical robust summarization under single-pass constraints.

- Pros: New provable robust streaming algorithm; favorable memory scaling vs concurrent work; supports multiple deletion queries; solid empirical results under conservative evaluation.  
- Cons: Algorithmic/space inconsistencies need correction; limited empirical reporting and comparison to concurrent methods; modest approximation constant (~0.149).

- Essential suggestions: Reconcile and correct threshold rule and precise τ in Theorem 4.1.  
- Essential suggestions: Clarify asymptotic space bound and separate core STAR-T space from parallel-guess overhead.  
- Essential suggestions: Report empirical memory usage |S|, running time, objective values, and sensitivity analyses over m, k, w.

- Optional suggestions: Empirically compare to Mirzasoleiman et al. (2017) and include STAR-T-GREEDY vs STAR-T-SIEVE ablations.  
- Optional suggestions: Describe practical instantiation of τ (parallel guesses, step sizes, stopping) and explore adaptive thresholding without parallel copies.
