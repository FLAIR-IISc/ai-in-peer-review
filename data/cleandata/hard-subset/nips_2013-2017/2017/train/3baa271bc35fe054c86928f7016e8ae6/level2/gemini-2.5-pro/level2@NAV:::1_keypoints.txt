- Problem: maximizing a monotone submodular function under a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting robust to m adversarial deletions.  
- Proposed method: two-stage procedure—STAR-T single-pass streaming summary, then STAR-T-GREEDY selects final k elements after m removals.  
- Core technical contribution: STAR-T builds a summary via partitions with exponentially sized buckets and exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds.  
- Conceptual synthesis: combines streaming thresholding ideas (SIEVE-STREAMING) with partitioning structure from Bogunovic et al. (2017).  
- Theoretical guarantee: Theorem 4.1 provides a constant-factor approximation (0.149) for this challenging setting.  
- Memory complexity: O((k + m log k) log k) space, notably only logarithmic dependence on k.  
- Empirical validation: convincing experiments on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Baseline comparison: strongly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when that baseline is given removed elements in advance.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and the algorithm is clearly presented.  
- Weakness: the 0.149 approximation constant is modest; discussion on gap between theory and strong empirical results would help.  
- Weakness: algorithm requires an estimate of f(OPT); the parallel-instance fix (Theorem 4.5) is standard but not used in main experiments.  
- Weakness: experiments should address practical overhead of parallelization and its effect on the threshold parameter τ.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, novel contribution with strong theory and experiments—clear candidate for acceptance.
