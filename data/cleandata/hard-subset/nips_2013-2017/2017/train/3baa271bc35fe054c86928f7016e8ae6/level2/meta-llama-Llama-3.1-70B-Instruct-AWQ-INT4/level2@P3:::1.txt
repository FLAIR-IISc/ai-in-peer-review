**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization in the context of streaming data. The first stage involves a novel streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which makes one pass over the data and constructs a robust summary of the elements. The second stage uses a simple greedy algorithm, STAR-T-GREEDY, to produce a solution of size at most k from the remaining elements after any set of size m is removed from the summary.

The paper relates its ideas to previous work on submodular maximization, streaming algorithms, and robust optimization. The authors highlight the novelty of their approach, which combines a partitioned thresholding structure with an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust streaming problem.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel algorithmic approach**: The paper proposes a new streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which is specifically designed for robust submodular maximization. The algorithm's partitioned thresholding structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule are innovative and effective in achieving a constant-factor approximation guarantee.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The paper provides strong theoretical guarantees for the performance of the proposed algorithm. The authors prove that the algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust streaming problem, which is a significant improvement over previous results.
3. **Experimental validation**: The paper includes experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in practice. The authors show that their algorithm outperforms existing methods in two different data summarization applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the algorithm**: The paper's algorithm, STAR-T, has a complex structure, which may make it difficult to implement and understand. The authors could provide more intuitive explanations and visualizations to help readers understand the algorithm's behavior.
2. **Limited robustness**: While the paper's algorithm is designed for robust submodular maximization, its robustness is limited to a specific number of removals (m). The authors could explore ways to extend the algorithm's robustness to more general settings.
3. **Comparison to existing methods**: While the paper includes experimental comparisons to existing methods, the authors could provide more detailed comparisons and analyses to help readers understand the strengths and weaknesses of their algorithm relative to other approaches.

**Originality**

The paper's algorithmic approach is novel and original, combining a partitioned thresholding structure with an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust streaming problem. The authors' use of a two-stage procedure, with a streaming algorithm followed by a simple greedy algorithm, is also innovative and effective.

**Significance**

The paper's contributions are significant, as they provide a new and effective approach to robust submodular maximization in the context of streaming data. The authors' algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable in various machine learning and data science applications, such as data summarization, influence maximization, and budget allocation.
