- Addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings: find small representative subsets that remain effective after m removals.  
- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T streaming summary and STAR-T-GREEDY query-stage greedy selection after removals.  
- Main theoretical result: constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149 for the robust streaming problem.  
- Novel partitioned thresholding structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds across buckets enables the robustness guarantee.  
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting with analysis showing memory scales as O(m log^2 k) for robustness.  
- Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING, even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removals.  
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithms, sound theory, and good connection to prior work.  
- Strengths: well-motivated practical problem, elegant two-stage approach reusable for multiple removal scenarios, and thorough case-based theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical strength: robust summary handles more removals than theoretically guaranteed in some cases (e.g., |E| > k).  
- Weakness: approximation (0.149) is weaker than (1-1/e) for non-robust greedy; tradeoff between robustness and approximation not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete memory vs. approximation comparison with related work (e.g., [18] giving 1/2-ε with O(mk log k/ε) memory).  
- Presentation issue: discrepancy in stated memory complexity — abstract/conclusion claim O((k + m log k) log^2 k) while Theorem 4.1 shows O((k + m log k) log k) — needs clarification.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for large-scale streaming where re-optimization is infeasible; single precomputation for many removal scenarios is valuable.  
- Overall: solid theoretical and practical contribution, minor weaknesses fixable, suitable for acceptance.
