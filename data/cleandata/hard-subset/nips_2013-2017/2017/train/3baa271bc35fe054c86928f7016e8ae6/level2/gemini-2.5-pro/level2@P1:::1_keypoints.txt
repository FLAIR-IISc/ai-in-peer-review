- Problem: Maximizes a monotone submodular function with cardinality k in a single-pass streaming setting robust to removal of any m elements.
- Method: Proposes a two-stage framework—STAR-T streaming summary construction and STAR-T-GREEDY post-removal query stage.
- Summary structure: Uses partitions with exponentially decreasing thresholds and buckets placing elements by marginal gain.
- Novelty: Synthesizes ideas from prior robust and streaming work into a single-pass robust algorithm with new analysis.
- Theoretical guarantee: Proves a constant-factor approximation (~0.149) for the robust streaming problem.
- Memory complexity: Constructs a summary of size O((k + m log k) log k), with only logarithmic dependence on k.
- Unknown OPT handling: Uses parallel instances of the algorithm to remove dependence on knowing the optimal value.
- Experiments: Evaluated on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation, comparing to SIEVE-STREAMING given oracle removal info.
- Empirical results: Performs comparably to or better than the strong baseline despite the baseline’s advantage.
- Quality: High technical quality with sound analysis and a helpful proof sketch balancing two bucket-based bounds.
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, clear pseudocode and visualizations; theoretical results and experimental setup are clearly presented.
- Minor clarification needed: Inconsistency between text (τ/2^i) and algorithm (τ/min{2^i,k}) should be explicitly explained.
- Originality and positioning: Correctly situates contribution relative to related and concurrent work, highlighting improved memory dependence.
