- Tackles robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where selected elements may be removed.
- Proposes STAR-T, a partitioned thresholding algorithm with exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee (approx. 0.149) and proof sketch; full proofs in supplementary material.
- Builds on streaming and robust submodular literature and highlights different memory dependence on k versus prior work.
- Motivations and applications (data summarization, influence maximization, recommendations) are clearly articulated.
- Novel combination of partitioned thresholding and exponential thresholds; design choices are well-motivated.
- Theoretical guarantees are clearly stated and supported by the analysis.
- Empirical evaluation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks shows competitive performance, including under random and greedy removals.
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and a clear problem statement.
- Requires knowledge of f(OPT(k, V\E)) for analysis; authors propose a parallel approximation approach (Theorem 4.5).
- Parameter sensitivity: memory-controlling parameter w significantly impacts performance and its practical tuning is unclear.
- Approximation constant is relatively small (0.149); potential to explore techniques to improve it.
- Experiments are on relatively small datasets; scalability to larger, realistic datasets is not demonstrated.
- Connection to related work could be strengthened, especially relating the partitioned structure to approaches in [10,11] and broader trade-offs.
- Key proofs are deferred to supplementary material; main text could include more critical proof details.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add guidance on selecting w, discuss ways to improve the approximation constant, and elaborate connections to related work.
