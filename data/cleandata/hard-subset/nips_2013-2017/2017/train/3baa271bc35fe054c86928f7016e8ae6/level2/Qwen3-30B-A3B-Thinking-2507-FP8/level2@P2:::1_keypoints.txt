- Presents a novel streaming approach for robust submodular maximization that handles up to m arbitrary removals.  
- Proposes a two-stage algorithm: STAR-T (streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query).  
- Key technical idea: partitioned thresholding structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Guarantees a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log^2 k) and a 0.149 constant-factor approximation after m removals.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with thorough related-work discussion.  
- Effectively connects to SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular work (PRO), highlighting structural differences.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, but the 0.149 approximation is much lower than SIEVE-STREAMING’s ~0.5, raising practical concerns.  
- Experiments (dominating set, personalized movie recommendation) convincingly show matching or outperforming SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removals.  
- The interactive reuse of the same precomputed summary for different removals is a strong practical benefit.  
- Major concern: memory bound O((k + m log k) log^2 k) is substantially larger than SIEVE-STREAMING’s O(k log k) and is not sufficiently justified.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with concurrent work [18], especially on memory (O(mk log k/ε) vs O((k + m log k) log^2 k) ) and approximation (1/2-ε vs 0.149).  
- Needs stronger motivation for why robustness to m removals is necessary in practice and discussion of realistic values of m.  
- Recommendations: provide detailed comparison with [18], explain/justify the log^2 k memory factor or improve it, and better contextualize practical relevance of the robustness trade-offs.
