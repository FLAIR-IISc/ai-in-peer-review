- Introduces robust submodular maximization in streaming settings, aiming to withstand removal of up to m elements under cardinality k.  
- Proposes a two-stage algorithm: STAR-T (streaming summary construction) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query-phase solution after removals).  
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee approaching 0.149 as k → ∞, with summary size O((k + m log k) log² k).  
- Key technical novelty: partitioned thresholding structure with an exponentially decreasing threshold rule enabling robustness to any m with logarithmic k-dependence in memory.  
- Situates work relative to prior art (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular methods), noting improvement over concurrent work that has linear k-dependence.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, handling two main cases (many full buckets vs. none) with detailed, well-structured proofs in the supplement.  
- Experimental validation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation shows matching or outperforming baselines (even when baselines know removals), averaged over 100 iterations.  
- Clarity and presentation are excellent: well-organized, clear notation, helpful illustration of STAR-T, and comprehensive supplementary proofs.  
- Originality is significant: novel partitioned thresholding approach and exponentially decreasing thresholds represent a meaningful advance.  
- High practical significance: useful for robust data summarization in realistic failure or preference-change scenarios with reasonable memory guarantees.  
- Minor concerns: lower approximation (0.149) than non-robust methods (≈0.5), which is expected given robustness.  
- Practicality issues: algorithm assumes knowledge of f(OPT) for threshold τ; the parallel variant remedies this but increases space by a log₁₊ε k factor.  
- Would benefit from more detailed runtime comparisons with baselines.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, experimentally convincing, and a valuable contribution suitable for NIPS.
