Summary and relation to prior work: The paper tackles robust streaming submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k when up to m items may be removed after the stream. It proposes STAR-T, a one-pass algorithm that builds a partitioned summary with exponentially decreasing thresholds, and a query-time STAR-T-GREEDY that runs greedy on S\E. The authors prove a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149 as k grows) with memory O((k + m log k) log2 k), and provide a parallel variant that removes the need to know OPT. This extends SIEVE-STREAMING’s thresholding to a robust setting and connects to partitioned robust structures from PRO (ICML’17) while differing in how buckets are populated. The concurrent deletion-robust streaming work [18] attains 1/2−ε but uses O(mk log k/ε) memory; this paper trades a weaker factor for much better k-dependence.

Strengths:
- Quality: Nontrivial analysis yielding a constant-factor guarantee in a challenging setting; careful handling of unknown OPT via parallel instances.
- Originality: Partitioned thresholds in a streaming robust context appear novel and distinct from both PRO and SIEVE-STREAMING.
- Significance: Robust streaming summarization is practically important (influence, recommendation). Memory that scales near-linearly in m (up to logs) and only logarithmically in k is attractive.
- Empirics: On two applications (dominating set, personalized recommendation), STAR-T(-GREEDY/-SIEVE) matches or exceeds strong baselines, even when baselines know deletions.

Weaknesses:
- Approximation constant (≈0.149) is low; the practical gap to 0.5 is not discussed.
- Clarity issues/inconsistencies: thresholds are described as τ/2^i but Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i,k}; memory is variously stated as O((k+m log k) log k) and O((k+m log k) log2 k). Please reconcile.
- Assumptions/sensitivity: Performance depends on τ and w; experiments use w=1 and often |E|>m, beyond guarantees—more systematic sensitivity analysis is needed.
- Empirics lack space/time measurements and ablations (e.g., effect of partitioning, thresholds, and τ estimation). Reproducibility details (code, oracle costs) are missing.

Pro acceptance:
- Novel, theoretically sound approach with improved memory scaling.
- Addresses a timely, impactful problem; convincing application-driven experiments.

Con acceptance:
- Weak constant and limited empirical diagnostics.
- Presentation inconsistencies and incomplete analysis of parameter sensitivity.

Constructive suggestions:
- Unify and clearly state thresholds and memory bounds; fix Algorithm 1 vs. text discrepancies.
- Report wall-clock, oracle-call counts, and actual memory; add ablations on w, τ grids, and number of partitions.
- Compare against [18] on memory/accuracy trade-offs; discuss when each method is preferable.
- Provide code and detailed experimental protocols to aid reproducibility.
- Explore tightening constants (e.g., improved mixing of lemmas) or adaptive threshold schedules.
