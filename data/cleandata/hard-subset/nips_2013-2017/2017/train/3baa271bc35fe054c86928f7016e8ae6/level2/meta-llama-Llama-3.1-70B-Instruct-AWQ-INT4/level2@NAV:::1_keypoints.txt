- Introduces STAR-T, a novel one-pass streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.  
- Retains O((k + m log k) log k) elements in memory, where k is cardinality and m robustness.  
- Provably constant-factor approximation after removal of any m elements, followed by a greedy post-processing.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with concise explanation of algorithm and theoretical guarantees.  
- Experiments on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation show practical effectiveness.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments jointly support the main claims.  
- Useful for large-scale ML data summarization, especially with noisy or incomplete data.  
- Novelty stems from a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- New analysis enables robustness to arbitrary m removals using O(m log k) memory.  
- Paper is largely complete and reproducible, though several proofs are in the supplementary material.  
- Limitation: performance may degrade when robustness parameter m is very large.  
- Mitigation: authors propose a parallel variant to handle larger m values.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel robust algorithm, suitable for large-scale applications, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: degraded performance for large m and reliance on supplementary proofs for full understanding.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution (rating 8/10, confidence 8/10).
