## Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

This paper addresses the important problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting, where elements arrive sequentially and a subset may be removed after processing. The authors propose STAR-T, an algorithm based on a partitioned structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding, which guarantees a constant-factor approximation even after the removal of *m* elements. 

The paper builds upon existing work in streaming submodular maximization (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and robust submodular maximization, clearly positioning itself within the field. The problem formulation is well-motivated, outlining relevant applications in data summarization, influence maximization, and recommendation systems. The proposed approach appears novel in its combination of partitioning and dynamic thresholding to achieve robustness in a streaming context. 

The theoretical analysis is a significant strength, providing a formal approximation guarantee. The proof sketch in the paper outlines the key ideas and connections between the partitioning structure, thresholding rule, and resulting robustness. The authors acknowledge the dependency on knowing or approximating the optimal solution value, and provide a parallel algorithm extension to address this practical concern.

However, the clarity could be improved. The description of the partitioning and thresholding mechanism, while conceptually explained, felt somewhat dense. More intuitive examples or diagrams could aid understanding. While the theoretical bounds are rigorous, the constant factor achieved (0.149) is relatively small, raising questions about the practical utility compared to simpler alternatives. Further, the experimental section, while demonstrating comparable performance to SIEVE-STREAMING, doesn't showcase a clear advantage for STAR-T. The settings for *w*, the memory parameter, and the impact of different choices for *w* are not thoroughly explored.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to robust streaming submodular maximization.
*   Rigorous theoretical analysis with a provable constant-factor approximation guarantee.
*   Demonstrated performance comparable to existing algorithms in practical applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Small constant factor in the approximation guarantee.
*   Limited clarity in the description of the algorithm's core mechanisms.
*   Experimental results do not strongly demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.

**Overall:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of submodular optimization. While the constant factor is a concern, the theoretical novelty and potential for robust data summarization warrant consideration. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on improving clarity and potentially expanding the experimental evaluation to showcase a more compelling advantage of STAR-T. The authors should also discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm’s performance to the choice of parameter *w*.
