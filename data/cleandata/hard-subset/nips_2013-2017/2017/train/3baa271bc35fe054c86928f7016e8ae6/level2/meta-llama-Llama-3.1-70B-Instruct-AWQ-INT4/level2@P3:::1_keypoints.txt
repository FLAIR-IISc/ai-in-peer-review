- Proposes a two-stage approach (STAR-T streaming summary, then STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization under streaming and up to m removals.  
- Novel algorithmic design: STAR-T uses partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary in one pass.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: proves a constant-factor approximation for the robust streaming problem.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show the method outperforms existing approaches on two data summarization tasks.  
- Algorithm complexity: STAR-T has a complex structure that may hinder implementation and comprehension.  
- Limited robustness scope: robustness is currently limited to a fixed number m of removals and not generalized.  
- Comparative evaluation could be deeper: more detailed comparisons/analyses with existing methods are needed.  
- Originality: the combination of partitioned thresholding and exponential thresholds in a two-stage streaming+greedy pipeline is novel.  
- Significance: offers a potentially widely applicable solution for streaming robust submodular tasks (summarization, influence, budgeting).  
- Writing quality: generally well-written with clear proofs and presentation, but could use more intuitive explanations and visualizations.  
- Clarity: logical and well-organized overall, though some sections would benefit from more concise language and clearer explanations.  
- Overall assessment: strong and significant contribution with impressive theory and experiments, despite some implementation, robustness, and comparison shortcomings.
