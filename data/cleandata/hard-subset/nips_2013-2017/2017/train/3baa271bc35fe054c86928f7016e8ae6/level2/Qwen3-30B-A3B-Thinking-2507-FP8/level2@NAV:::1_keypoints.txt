- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting with cardinality k where up to m elements may be removed after processing.  
- Method: Proposes STAR-T, a one-pass streaming algorithm that builds a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Postprocessing: Introduces STAR-T-GREEDY, a greedy algorithm on remaining elements achieving a 0.149 constant-factor approximation.  
- Technique: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to robustness via a novel partitioned thresholding scheme.  
- Theory: Rigorous analysis with Theorem 4.1; reduces memory dependence on k from linear to logarithmic compared to prior work.  
- Experiments: On dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removed elements.  
- Technical quality: Strong proofs and comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Clarity: Excellent presentation with clear algorithms and intuitive explanation of partitioning.  
- Novelty: Partitioned thresholding is distinct from prior robust submodular approaches [9,10,11].  
- Significance: High practical importance for large-scale ML and precomputed robust summaries for multiple queries.  
- Limitation: Low approximation factor (0.149) relative to non-robust streaming methods (~0.5); needs more discussion on practical impact.  
- Suggestion: Provide more intuition on why partitioning yields better robustness with lower memory.  
- Recommendation: Strong contribution; should be accepted.
