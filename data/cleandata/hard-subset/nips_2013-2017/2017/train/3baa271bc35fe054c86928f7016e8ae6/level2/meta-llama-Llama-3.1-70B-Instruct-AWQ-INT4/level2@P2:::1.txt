The paper "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach" presents a novel algorithm, STAR-T, for the problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting with robustness to element removals. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through theoretical guarantees and experimental results.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the algorithm, and the theoretical results. The use of a partitioned thresholding approach is a novel contribution to the field of submodular optimization, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of the STAR-T algorithm, which is a streaming algorithm that makes one pass over the data and retains a small set of elements in memory. The algorithm is designed to be robust against the removal of up to m elements, and the authors provide a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the algorithm's performance.

The paper also presents experimental results on two different data summarization tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the STAR-T algorithm in practice. The authors compare the performance of their algorithm to that of the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm, which is a state-of-the-art algorithm for submodular maximization in the streaming setting. The results show that the STAR-T algorithm performs comparably to or even outperforms the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm, even when the latter is allowed to know in advance which elements will be removed.

The paper has several strengths. First, the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the problem and the algorithm, making it easy to follow and understand. Second, the theoretical results are well-presented and provide a strong foundation for the algorithm's performance. Third, the experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in practice.

However, there are a few minor issues that could be improved. First, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work, particularly in the context of robust submodular maximization. While the authors provide a brief overview of the related work, a more in-depth discussion would help to better situate their contributions within the broader literature. Second, the experimental results could be strengthened by including more baselines or comparisons to other algorithms. While the comparison to SIEVE-STREAMING is useful, additional comparisons would help to further demonstrate the effectiveness of the STAR-T algorithm.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the algorithm, and the theoretical and experimental results are convincing. With a few minor improvements, the paper could be even stronger.
