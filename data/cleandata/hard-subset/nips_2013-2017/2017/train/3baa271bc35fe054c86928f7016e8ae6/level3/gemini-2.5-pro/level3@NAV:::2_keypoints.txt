- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting with cardinality constraint k and resilience to removal of up to m elements.  
- Method: Two-stage procedure—STAR-T single-pass streaming algorithm (partitioned structure, decreasing thresholds) to build a summary, then greedy on the summary after removals.  
- Novelty: First formal treatment of robust submodular maximization in the streaming model, extending Sieve-Streaming concepts to a harder setting.  
- Theoretical strength: Original and theoretically deep; proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee (Theorem 4.1).  
- Practical usability: Provides an approximation scheme for setting the threshold τ (Theorem 4.5).  
- Experimental weakness: Empirical evaluation is not sufficiently expressive to demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Baseline performance: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline across experiments.  
- Influence maximization issue: Uses simplified objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| instead of standard diffusion models (IC/LT), limiting relevance to canonical influence maximization.  
- Overall summary: Strong theoretical contribution but weak and potentially unconvincing empirical validation; experiments need standard formulations and clearer performance gains over baselines.
