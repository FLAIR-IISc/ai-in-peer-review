- Introduces STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm for robust streaming submodular maximization under cardinality constraint with up to m removals.
- Uses a streaming phase to build a robust summary and a query phase to select a near-optimal subset after removals.
- Extends prior work (greedy, SIEVE-STREAMING, robust submodular maximization) via partitioned thresholding and exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Technical analysis appears correct and proofs are rigorous.
- The partitioning/thresholding approach is a clever, non-trivial solution that achieves a constant-factor approximation.
- Presentation is mostly clear with consistent notation.
- Empirical results on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks show strong practical performance, often matching or beating SIEVE-STREAMING.
- Provides a parallel algorithm variant (Theorem 4.5) to approximate the unknown optimal value.
- Motivation and application examples (movies, influence maximization) are weak and feel contrived.
- Problem formulation is somewhat restrictive by modeling removals only from the selected set S rather than the entire ground set V.
- Relevance to the main NIPS audience is questionable given the conference's trend toward deep learning.
- Space complexity O((k + m log k) log k) may be limiting for very large datasets and could use mitigation strategies.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, solid theory, and empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation, limited applicability, questionable conference fit, and space concerns.
- Overall: strong technical contribution but limited impact and fit; reviewer leans toward reject.
