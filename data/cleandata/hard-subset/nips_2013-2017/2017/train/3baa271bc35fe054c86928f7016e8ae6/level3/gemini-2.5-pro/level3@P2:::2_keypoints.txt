- Problem: Streaming maximization of a monotone submodular function under cardinality k, robust to removal of up to m elements.
- Method: Two-stage algorithm—STAR-T (single-pass streaming summary with partitioned/exponential-threshold buckets) then STAR-T-GREEDY (greedy on summary after up to m removals).
- Novelty: New partitioned summary structure and assignment rule adapting thresholding to the robust streaming setting.
- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING threshold idea and resembles non-streaming PRO structure; achieves logarithmic memory in k vs. linear in concurrent Mirzasoleiman et al.
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a constant-factor approximation (Theorem 4.1) with a sound case-based analysis (Lemmas 4.2–4.4).
- Practical issue addressed: Removes dependence on knowing f(OPT) using a standard parallelization technique (Theorem 4.5).
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, algorithms and analysis are clearly explained and illustrated.
- Experiments: Empirical results show viability but do not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING; matching a clairvoyant baseline is notable but should be framed carefully.
- Objective limitation: Use of dominating set objective as an influence maximization proxy is a simplification and may not reflect performance on stochastic diffusion models.
- Hyperparameter w: Theory requires w scaling with m and log k, but experiments find w=1 suffices—needs more intuition or discussion for this gap.
- Suggested improvements: Emphasize that STAR-T does not need knowledge of the removed set while the baseline does; acknowledge the dominating-set limitation and propose testing on standard influence models; discuss practical trade-offs with Mirzasoleiman et al. and consider empirical comparisons.
- Overall verdict: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with solid analysis; experimental evidence is positive but not decisive—recommend acceptance.
