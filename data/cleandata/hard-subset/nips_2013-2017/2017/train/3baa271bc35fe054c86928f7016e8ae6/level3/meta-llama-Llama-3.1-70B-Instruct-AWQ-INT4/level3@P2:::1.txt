**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach" studies the problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting, with the additional requirement of robustness against the removal of up to m elements. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that constructs a robust summary of the data, and a query stage algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that runs on the remaining elements after the removal of m elements. The authors prove that their algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee and demonstrate its effectiveness in two data summarization tasks.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The problem of submodular maximization has been extensively studied in the literature, and the authors build upon previous work on streaming submodular maximization and robust submodular maximization. The authors' approach extends the algorithmic ideas of SIEVE-STREAMING, a popular submodular streaming algorithm, to the robust setting. The authors also mention related work on robust versions of submodular maximization, such as influence maximization and budget allocation.

The paper has several strengths:
