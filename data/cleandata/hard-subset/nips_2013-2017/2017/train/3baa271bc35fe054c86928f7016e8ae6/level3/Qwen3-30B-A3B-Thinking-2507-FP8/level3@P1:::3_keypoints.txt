- Novel problem setting: first to combine robust submodular maximization with the streaming model.  
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes STAR-T, a streaming algorithm that builds a robust summary using partitioned thresholding.  
- Structural innovation: partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds assign elements by marginal gain.  
- Memory guarantee: summary size O((m log k + k) log² k).  
- Approximation guarantee: greedy query after up to m removals achieves 0.149(1 − 1/log k) approximation.  
- Theoretical significance: non-trivial guarantee improving over prior robust or streaming-only methods.  
- Empirical strength: experiments show STAR-T matches/exceeds SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines know removals.  
- Practical relevance: setting and two-stage (precompute + fast query) approach are useful for large-scale, failure-prone applications.  
- Weakness — analysis clarity: theoretical analysis and proof sketch are poorly explained and hard to follow.  
- Weakness — missing lower bounds: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee.  
- Weakness — influence experiments: uses simplified neighborhood-size objective instead of standard network-dependent spread model.  
- Presentation issues: algorithm/analysis description is dense; key parameters (e.g., τ vs f(OPT)) and critical proof details are omitted from the main paper.  
- Quality assessment: results appear sound and experiments convincing, though presentation needs improvement.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to revisions clarifying the analysis and addressing experimental modeling concerns.
