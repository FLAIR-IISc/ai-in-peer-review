This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Streaming Robust submodular algorithm with Partitioned Thresholding (STAR-T), for the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization. The algorithm makes one pass over the data and constructs a set S of elements that is robust against the removal of any m elements. The paper also presents a simple greedy algorithm, STAR-T-GREEDY, that runs on the remaining elements after removal and achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee.

The problem formulation is interesting and relevant to many real-world applications, such as data summarization, influence maximization, and personalized movie recommendation. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and its importance.

The technical quality of the paper is high. The algorithm and analysis are non-trivial and technically challenging. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance, including a proof of the constant-factor approximation guarantee. The paper also presents experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in practice.

However, the relevance of the paper to the conference is questionable. While the paper presents a novel algorithm for a specific problem, it is not clear how this problem relates to the broader themes of the conference. The authors could have done a better job of explaining the relevance of their work to the conference and its potential impact on the field.

One concern is that the problem formulation is not general enough. The authors assume that the submodular function is monotone and normalized, which may not be the case in all applications. It would be interesting to see how the algorithm and analysis can be extended to more general settings.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization. However, the authors could have done a better job of explaining the relevance of their work to the conference and its potential impact on the field.
