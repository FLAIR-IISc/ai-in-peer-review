- Problem: monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k in a streaming setting robust to adversarial removal of up to m elements; two-stage approach (STAR-T streaming summary, STAR-T-GREEDY query).

- Algorithmic contribution: STAR-T is a single-pass streaming algorithm that builds a compact summary S using partitioned buckets and exponentially decreasing thresholds.

- Theoretical guarantee: Theorem 4.1 gives a constant-factor approximation with memory O((k + m log k) log k), achieving logarithmic dependence on k.

- Novelty: Builds on Sieve-Streaming and prior robust partitioning work and is among the first provable streaming algorithms for this robust problem.

- Significance: Addresses a practically relevant, understudied problem with potential applications (resilient sensor placement, robust influence maximization, recommender systems).

- Technical quality: Rigorous analysis, clever partition/threshold design, and favorable memory scaling compared to concurrent work.

- Clarity: Well-written, precise problem formulation, and clearly described algorithms and intuition.

- Empirical weakness: Experimental results are underwhelming—STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming (which has the unrealistic advantage of knowing the removed set).

- Modeling concern: Influence-maximization experiment uses a dominating-set proxy instead of standard stochastic diffusion models (IC/LT), undermining claims about performance on true influence tasks.

- Experimental scope: Limited to two applications and two datasets; broader evaluation on more submodular functions and larger datasets is needed.

- Practical note: Authors handle unknown f(OPT) via parallelization and report w=1 works well in practice, simplifying tuning.

- Theoretical caveat: Approximation constant c ≈ 0.149 is relatively low; discussion on tightness or routes to improvement would be beneficial.

- Recommendation: Novel, theoretically solid work worthy of acceptance, contingent on addressing or justifying the experimental and modeling concerns.
