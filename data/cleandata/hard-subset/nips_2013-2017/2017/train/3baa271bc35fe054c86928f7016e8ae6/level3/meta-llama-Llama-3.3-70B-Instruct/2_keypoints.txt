- Proposes a two-stage robust submodular maximization: STAR-T streaming (one pass, small memory) followed by STAR-T-GREEDY, with a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, detailed algorithm description, and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show the algorithm’s practical effectiveness.  
- Strength: Novel STAR-T streaming algorithm achieving constant-factor approximation for robust submodular maximization.  
- Strength: Simple STAR-T-GREEDY second stage yields efficiency and scalability.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical performance bounds guaranteeing effectiveness.  
- Strength: Demonstrates applicability across two data summarization tasks.  
- Weakness: Experimental results are not very expressive; STAR-T-GREEDY does not always beat Sieve-Streaming.  
- Weakness: Influence maximization objective differs from a referenced work, casting doubt on performance under the standard spread model.  
- Weakness: Two hyperparameters (τ and w) need tuning; τ is approximated but choice of w is not well-motivated.  
- Overall: Significant contribution with solid theory and practical results, but needs more expressive experiments and clearer hyperparameter justification.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel, effective, scalable algorithm with theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.  
- Con acceptance: Limited experimental superiority, objective mismatch for influence maximization, and unclear hyperparameter choice.  
- Scores & recommendation: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; recommend accept with minor revisions.
