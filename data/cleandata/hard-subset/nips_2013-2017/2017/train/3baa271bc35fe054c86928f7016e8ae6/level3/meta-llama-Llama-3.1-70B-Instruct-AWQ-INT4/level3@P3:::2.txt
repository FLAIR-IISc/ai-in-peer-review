This paper proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k, which is a fundamental problem in machine learning and data summarization. The authors introduce a streaming algorithm called STAR-T, which uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule to construct a robust summary of the data. The summary is then used as input to a simple greedy algorithm, called STAR-T-GREEDY, which produces a solution that is guaranteed to be within a constant factor of the optimal solution, even after the removal of up to m elements.

The paper makes several important contributions. First, it introduces a new streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which is a significant improvement over existing algorithms that require multiple passes over the data or have limited robustness guarantees. Second, it provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the algorithm's performance, including a constant-factor approximation guarantee and bounds on the size of the summary. Finally, it demonstrates the effectiveness of the algorithm through experiments on two real-world datasets, including a social network and a movie recommendation system.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its analysis. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain how their algorithm differs from existing approaches. The experimental results are also well-presented and provide a clear comparison of the algorithm's performance with existing baselines.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the experiments are not very extensive, and the performance of the algorithm is not consistently better than the baselines.
