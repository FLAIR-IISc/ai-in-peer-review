- Summary: Proposes STAR-T, a streaming algorithm for deletion-robust monotone submodular maximization that stores a compact partitioned summary and yields a constant-factor approximation after up to m deletions.  
- Contribution vs prior work: Adapts partitioned summary ideas to streaming with thresholded placement; contrasts memory–approximation trade-off with concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18].

- Strength — novel design: Partitioned structure with exponentially growing buckets and decreasing thresholds is elegant and well-justified.  
- Strength — provable robustness: Constant-factor guarantee with one pass and sublinear space, plus clear memory–accuracy trade-offs and a two-case analysis.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Two-stage workflow fits applications needing post-hoc deletions (privacy, personalization).  
- Strength — empirical validation: On dominating set and movie recommendation, STAR-T-GREEDY matches or exceeds SIEVE-STREAMING even when handicapped.

- Weakness — limited constraint scope: Focuses only on monotone cardinality constraints; extension to matroid/knapsack would increase generality.  
- Weakness — conservative constant: Theoretical constant (~0.149) is weaker than 1/2−ε in [18]; recommend plotting empirical memory–quality trade-offs vs [18] or showing higher empirical constants.  
- Weakness — clarity on thresholds: Inconsistency between τ/2^i and τ/min{2^i,k}; need to reconcile and give practical guidance on setting w.  
- Weakness — space bound discrepancy: Abstract/conclusion claim O((k+m log k) log^2 k) while Theorem shows O((k+m log k) log k); clarify that extra log k comes from parallel instances to estimate f(OPT).  
- Weakness — experiments need more depth: Include sensitivity to m and adversarial vs random removals, show performance vs |E| (including beyond m), add ablations (no partitioning/uniform thresholds), compare to [18] or provide resource-adjusted comparison, and report wall-clock time and oracle call counts.  
- Weakness — motivate ML relevance more: Emphasize continual learning, privacy deletions, interactive recommendation to better align with NIPS audience.

- Evaluation — quality: Technical development is sound and nontrivial; case analysis leverages submodularity and greedy properties appropriately.  
- Evaluation — clarity: Generally well-written but fix threshold/space inconsistencies and minor typos; add a figure illustrating buckets if not present.  
- Evaluation — originality: Novel combination of partitioned summaries with thresholded streaming and robust analysis, distinct from [8,11,18].  
- Evaluation — significance: Advances deletion-robust streaming with a favorable space–approximation trade-off; likely impactful for robust ML systems.

- Questions for authors: Can tighter constants be provided for practical regimes (random order/deletions)?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to τ mis-specification in the parallel-instance scheme and what ε was used in experiments?  
- Questions for authors: Can the analysis extend to knapsack/matroid constraints or non-monotone objectives with post-processing?  
- Questions for authors: In movie recommendation, how is α chosen per user and is STAR-T robust across α values?

- Pros for acceptance: Solid novel algorithm with provable guarantees; strong space efficiency; two-stage reusable summary with promising empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Conservative approximation constant and limited empirical exploration of sensitivity and competing baselines; narrow constraint class and presentation issues.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound and practically relevant, but needs clearer exposition and stronger experiments to fully convince.
