- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in streaming settings; STAR-T constructs a robust summary of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).
- Guarantee: Ensures a k-size subset post-removal of up to m elements achieves a constant-factor approximation of optimal.
- Strength — Technical: Introduces a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding with rigorous proofs and constant-factor guarantees.
- Strength — Practical relevance: Model is relevant for dynamic/incomplete large-scale ML tasks (e.g., recommendations, influence maximization).
- Strength — Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments showing STAR-T matches or outperforms strong baselines (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING), even against advantaged baselines.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with separated theoretical and experimental sections.
- Weakness — Motivation/scope: Practical motivation is somewhat narrow (examples like movie recommendations) and could be broadened.
- Weakness — Related work comparison: Novelty vs. existing robust/streaming methods (e.g., PRO) and trade-offs (memory vs. approximation) not deeply contrasted.
- Weakness — Significance: Achieved constant-factor approximation (0.149) is relatively low and may limit practical impact.
- Argument for acceptance: Tackles a challenging problem with a novel algorithm and rigorous theoretical guarantees.
- Argument for acceptance: Method is applicable to real-world scenarios and empirically strong.
- Argument for acceptance: Clear presentation makes the work accessible to a broad audience.
- Argument against acceptance: Practical motivation and broader impact could be stronger.
- Argument against acceptance: Low approximation factor may limit utility in some applications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, emphasizing stronger motivation and deeper discussion of trade-offs.
