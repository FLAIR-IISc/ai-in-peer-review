- Introduces STAR-T, a novel two-stage streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraints that tolerates up to m removals.  
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING with a partitioned thresholding structure where buckets across partitions have exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Main theoretical result: STAR-T produces a summary of size O((k + m log k) log² k) and, with STAR-T-GREEDY, achieves a constant-factor approximation approaching ~0.149 as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- Proof relies on analysis of the partitioned thresholding scheme, with Lemmas 4.2–4.4 handling full versus partially filled bucket cases.  
- Provides a practical extension (Theorem 4.5) using parallel instances to handle unknown f(OPT(k, V\E)).  
- Empirical evaluation on dominating set (Twitter, Amazon) and MovieLens recommendation tasks shows STAR-T-GREEDY is competitive and sometimes slightly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING, despite SIEVE-STREAMING knowing removals.  
- Demonstrates that a single robust summary can serve multiple different removal scenarios, a practical advantage.  
- Strength: First streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with a constant-factor guarantee.  
- Strength: Partitioned thresholding is an elegant, novel mechanism for achieving robustness in streaming.  
- Strength: Experiments show competitive performance and a useful practical note that w=1 works well.  
- Weakness: Experiments limited—dominating set objective differs from standard diffusion/influence models, so generalization is unclear.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains over SIEVE-STREAMING are marginal and sometimes negative.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of parameter sensitivity (τ and w) beyond w=1.  
- Weakness: Theoretical constant (~0.149) is relatively low compared to classical greedy (1−1/e ≈ 0.632).  
- Originality: Novel combination of robustness and streaming for submodular maximization not addressed by prior work.  
- Significance: Addresses an important large-scale problem where reoptimization after removals is infeasible; two-stage approach enables multiple queries on one summary.  
- Quality: Theoretical analysis is sound and well-motivated; proofs are detailed in supplementary material.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and provides precise algorithm descriptions and proofs.  
- Overall recommendation: Technically solid and novel contribution; recommend acceptance despite marginal experimental advantages.
