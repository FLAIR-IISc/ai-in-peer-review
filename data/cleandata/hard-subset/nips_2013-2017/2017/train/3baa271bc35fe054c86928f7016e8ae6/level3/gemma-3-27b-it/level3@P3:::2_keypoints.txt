- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm using partitioned thresholding to build a robust summary and a greedy stage after removals for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint.

- Tackles the novel problem of streaming robust submodular maximization, relevant to applications like influence maximization and recommender systems.

- Positions the work within prior streaming and robustness literature, extending Sieve-Streaming–style methods to the robust setting.

- Theorem 4.1 provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee that holds even as k → ∞.

- Analysis (Lemmas 4.2–4.3) supports the algorithm’s tolerance to element loss; Thm 4.5 gives a practical method to approximate the threshold τ.

- Strength — Novelty: First streaming algorithm explicitly designed for robustness in submodular maximization.

- Strength — Theoretical guarantees: Strong constant-factor approximation with formal proofs.

- Strength — Practicality: Simple greedy second stage, claim that w=1 suffices, and ability to reuse the summary for multiple removal scenarios.

- Strength — Clear problem definition and well-motivated two-stage setting.

- Strength — Good connection and comparison to existing literature on submodular maximization and robustness.

- Weakness — Experiments: Empirical results are not convincingly better than Sieve-Streaming; STAR-T-Greedy often only comparable.

- Weakness — Influence maximization setup uses a non-standard objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| rather than the Kempe et al. spread model, limiting generalizability.

- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Limited analysis of sensitivity to w and τ; more thorough study needed.

- Weakness — Space complexity: Theoretical space bounds given, but practical memory comparisons to Sieve-Streaming are lacking.

- Quality: Technically sound with clear problem statement and logical theoretical analysis, but experimental weaknesses reduce impact.

- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized; some proof sketches could use more detail.

- Originality: Novel streaming approach for robust submodular maximization; partitioned thresholding is a specific contribution.

- Significance: Addresses an important scalable robustness problem, but limited empirical validation weakens claimed impact.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an unstudied problem, strong theoretical guarantees, novel algorithm, and reasonable experimental performance.

- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient empirical improvement over existing methods, non-standard influence experiment, and lack of comprehensive sensitivity analysis.

- Overall assessment: Solid theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical evidence; recommendation: weak accept conditional on stronger experiments (standard influence model and parameter sensitivity) to demonstrate clear advantages.
