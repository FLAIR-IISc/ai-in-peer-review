- Introduces a novel two-stage procedure (STAR-T + STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in streaming settings.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule, yielding a constant-factor approximation as k → ∞.
- Theoretical proofs (Lemmas 4.2–4.3) show STAR-T’s robustness to element loss.
- Experiments validate effectiveness on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Claimed as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with broad application potential.
- Originality: addresses a practical, challenging problem and extends prior streaming techniques with a novel robust design.
- Theoretical contributions: rigorous analysis and clear connections to prior work supporting approximation and robustness claims.
- Practical relevance: evaluated on real-world tasks, demonstrating applicability across domains.
- Ease of use: hyperparameters (τ and w) are simple to approximate; w=1 works well in experiments.
- Efficiency: memory-efficient (O((k + m log k) log^2 k) space) and single-pass operation suitable for large-scale data.
- Experimental limitation: does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING, especially under adversarial removals.
- Objective discrepancy: influence maximization experiment uses a nonstandard objective, potentially limiting generalizability.
- Low approximation factor: constant c = 0.149 is relatively small and may limit practical accuracy.
- Presentation clarity: needs clearer intuition for partitioning and thresholding for readers unfamiliar with submodular optimization.
- Pro: significant theoretical and practical contribution as the first robust streaming algorithm for this problem.
- Con: experimental results and low approximation factor weaken the case for clear practical advantage over existing methods.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve experimental comparisons and clarify implications of the low approximation factor.
