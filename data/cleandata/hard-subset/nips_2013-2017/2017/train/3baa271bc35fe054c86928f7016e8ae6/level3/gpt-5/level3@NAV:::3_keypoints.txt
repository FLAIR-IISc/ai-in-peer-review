- Tackles streaming robust submodular maximization: select k items resilient to up to m adversarial deletions using STAR-T summary + GREEDY post-deletion.  
- STAR-T stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements via partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149 as k grows) under m deletions.  
- Empirical results strong on coverage-style influence and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Positioned against non-streaming robust methods, streaming non-robust methods (SIEVE-STREAMING), and concurrent deletion-robust streaming, improving memory dependence on k from linear to logarithmic.  
- Technical direction is sound and addresses a challenging combined constraint.  
- Partitioned thresholding design is plausible and the 0.149 guarantee under deletions is nontrivial.  
- Analysis presentation is hard to follow; “innovative analysis” insufficiently explained and key invariants are obscured.  
- Role and data-dependent tuning of τ are unclear and need better exposition.  
- Paper lacks nontrivial lower bounds (e.g., memory vs. approximation tradeoffs) to contextualize the 0.149 factor and log^2 k memory.  
- Minor inconsistency between sections about exact log factors in the space bound.  
- Experiments are promising but use neighborhood-size (dominating set) objective instead of IC/LT diffusion, so applicability to standard network diffusion is uncertain.  
- Clarity could be improved with more intuition (why exponential partitioning), a small running example, explicit per-element update complexity, and moving τ-approximation details into main text.  
- Joint treatment of streaming and robustness with a constant-factor guarantee appears novel; bucket-filling rule meaningfully differs from prior work.  
- Addresses a relevant, practical problem with compelling empirical gains versus baselines that know deletions.  
- Pros: first constant-factor streaming-robust algorithm with near-logarithmic k-dependence; simple two-stage design and strong empirical performance.  
- Cons: unclear analysis/exposition, no lower bounds, simplified experimental objective, minor space-guarantee inconsistencies, and limited discussion of computational costs.
