- Studies maximizing a monotone submodular function under cardinality k in a streaming setting robust to removal of up to m elements.  
- Proposes a two-stage approach: single-pass streaming STAR-T to build a robust summary S, then STAR-T-GREEDY (greedy on S \ E) after up to m removals.  
- STAR-T uses partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds to maintain the summary in one pass.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the two-stage procedure.  
- Shows summary size is O((k + m log k) log k), sublinear in the ground set size.  
- Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular literature, extending streaming techniques to robustness.  
- Compares favorably to concurrent work with better memory dependency on k.  
- Empirical validation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks supports theoretical claims.  

Strengths:
- Addresses a significant, practical problem at the intersection of streaming, submodular, and robust optimization.  
- Presents a novel, well-motivated two-stage model suitable for interactive applications.  
- Technical contribution is strong: elegant algorithm design and non-trivial, apparently correct analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear explanations, useful figures, and intuitive proof sketch.  
- Experiments are compelling; STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms a strong baseline even when the baseline knows the removals.  

Weaknesses:
- Motivation and comparison to alternative robustness formulations (e.g., classical max_Z min_E f(Z \ E)) need clearer discussion.  
- Limited discussion of generalizability: removals during the stream or adaptive adversaries are not addressed.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important practical problem with a novel algorithm and constant-factor guarantee.  
- Strong technical analysis and improved memory complexity versus concurrent work.  
- Convincing empirical results against a powerful baseline.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Problem formulation motivation and its advantages over other robustness models could be better articulated.  
- Connections to machine learning applications could be strengthened to clarify relevance to the conference audience.
