- Paper proposes STAR-T + STAR-T-GREEDY for streaming robust submodular maximization: single pass summary S, handle up to m deletions, then greedy on S\E to produce size-k solution.
- Summary construction uses partitions of exponentially sized buckets with exponentially decreasing marginal-gain thresholds; each element placed in first non-full bucket meeting threshold.
- Theoretical claim: constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ 0.149·f(OPT(k, V\E)) with summary size O((k + m log k) log k)–O((k + m log k) log^2 k) (discrepancy noted).
- Relation to prior work: combines streaming (SIEVE-STREAMING) and deletion robustness (PRO/others); trades better memory scaling (logarithmic in k) for a smaller constant vs. concurrent work giving 1/2−ε with O(mk log k/ε) memory.
- Strength: first single-pass streaming method with deletion robustness and provable constant-factor guarantee using compact summary.
- Strength: simple, implementable thresholded partitioning and plausible theoretical analysis.
- Strength: empirical results show STAR-T matches or outperforms strong baselines (even those that know deletions) on two tasks.
- Major weakness: inconsistency in stated space bounds (log k vs log^2 k) — needs reconciliation and tightest bound with constants.
- Major weakness: mismatch between described thresholds (τ/2^i) and Algorithm 1 (τ/min{2^i,k}); analysis/notation must be unified.
- Major weakness: approximation constant 0.149 is low compared to 1/2−ε; paper should discuss the approximation–memory trade-off and regimes where STAR-T is preferable.
- Major weakness: no lower bounds or impossibility results to contextualize the achieved constant and memory trade-offs.
- Major weakness: proof sketch is hard to follow; needs clearer high-level roadmap and intuition for key lemmas, especially the balancing argument yielding 0.149.
- Experimental concerns: influence objective uses neighborhood coverage (easier than IC/LT); applicability to realistic influence models is unclear.
- Experimental omissions: missing wall-clock time, number of oracle evaluations, final summary size, and sensitivity to w, m, and stream order.
- Practical guidance missing: how to choose τ/ε and trade-offs for the parallel τ variant; empirical sensitivity to these parameters not provided.
- Quality: technical structure and lemmas look plausible, but inconsistencies and deferred proofs hinder verification; more proof intuition in main text needed.
- Clarity: algorithm description mostly clear but thresholds, space expressions, and parameters (w, τ) must be defined consistently; illustrative bucket-filling example recommended.
- Originality: partitioned thresholded streaming design for robust deletions is novel relative to prior centralized robust and streaming non-robust work.
- Significance: potential practical approach for deletion-robust summarization with modest memory, valuable for multiple post hoc queries if tightened and clarified.
- Suggestions: resolve space/threshold discrepancies, discuss trade-off with concurrent work explicitly, report runtime/memory/evaluations, add IC/LT or RR-set experiments, and include sensitivity analyses and guidance for τ selection.
- Arguments for acceptance: first constant-factor streaming robust method with polylog(k) memory, simple implementable algorithm, competitive empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: exposition and theorem issues, low approximation constant, limited experimental scope, missing resource/sensitivity analyses, and lack of lower bounds.
