- Studies robust streaming submodular maximization and proposes STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm that builds a robust summary in one pass and uses greedy selection after removals.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee and analyzes space complexity.  
- Introduces a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Experimental results on two summarization tasks show STAR-T compares favorably to existing algorithms, even when competitors know removals.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with thorough theoretical proofs.  
- Strong technical contribution and novel algorithmic ideas.  
- Motivation and real-world applications for robustness to element removals are not clearly explained.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs more datasets and comparisons to other algorithms.  
- Possible limited broad appeal to the NIPS community due to topic specialization.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on technical novelty and empirical performance.
