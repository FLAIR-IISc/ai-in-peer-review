- Problem: robust submodular maximization in streaming setting — build small summary T so after any m removals from T one can still pick k elements with high f(S).  
- Method: two-stage algorithm — STAR-T (single-pass streaming summary) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query stage after removals).  
- Memory/size guarantee: summary T size O((m log k + k) log² k) with logarithmic dependence on k.  
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 for the robust streaming objective.  
- Technical novelty: partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds to handle arbitrary m removals.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING for non-robust streaming and relates to robust non-streaming works (Orlin et al., Bogunovic et al.) and concurrent work (Mirzasoleiman et al.).  
- Analysis quality: strong, careful proofs addressing both streaming constraints and robustness.  
- Empirical results: experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show performance matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when latter knows removals.  
- Strengths: complete theoretical analysis, simple implementable algorithm, convincing experiments, and a novel technical idea.  
- Weaknesses: weak motivation and application examples; movie recommendation case not compellingly justified.  
- Scope limitation: problem formulation only allows removals from the summary T, not from the whole ground set, reducing practical applicability.  
- Relevance concern: questionable fit for NIPS—limited connections to ML, neural nets, or broader ML applications.  
- Recommendation: technically solid but narrow and weakly motivated; reviewer recommends rejection as marginal fit for the conference.
