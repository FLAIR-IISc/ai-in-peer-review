- Paper analyzes Bitcoin P2P anonymity, comparing pre-2015 trickle spreading to post-2015 diffusion spreading.
- Models the Bitcoin network as a graph and quantifies deanonymization probability for an eavesdropper.
- Develops and analyzes estimators including first-timestamp and maximum-likelihood approaches.
- Provides theoretical analysis and simulations to support estimator performance claims.
- Finds both trickle and diffusion spreading exhibit poor anonymity; the switch to diffusion did not materially improve anonymity.
- Shows graph structure matters: centrality-based estimators can outperform first-timestamp estimators.
- Strength: thorough, technically sound analysis with new models and estimators, and significant original contributions.
- Weakness: writing and exposition need improvement and clearer explanations of novel analysis and applications (e.g., influence maximization).
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement presented upper bounds.
- Weakness: simulations are limited to regular trees and a single 2015 Bitcoin snapshot, limiting generalizability.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, originality, and significance; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Recommendation: accept but request improved clarity, broader simulations across topologies/time, and additional lower-bound results.
