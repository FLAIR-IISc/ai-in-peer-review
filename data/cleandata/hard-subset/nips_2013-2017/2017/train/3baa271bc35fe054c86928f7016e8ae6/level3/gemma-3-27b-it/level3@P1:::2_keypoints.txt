- Introduces STAR-T, a novel two-stage algorithm for robust streaming submodular maximization addressing sequential data and element removals.  
- STAR-T builds a robust summary in-stream using partitioning with exponentially decreasing thresholds, then applies STAR-T-GREEDY after removals.  
- Main contribution: first streaming algorithm with a constant-factor approximation guarantee for robust submodular maximization (Theorem 4.1).  
- Theoretical analysis is detailed, with supporting lemmas (4.2–4.4) clarifying robustness properties.  
- Leverages and adapts prior work (greedy, SIEVE-STREAMING, partitioning/thresholding from robust algorithms) to the streaming setting.  
- Practical design supports single-pass operation and limited memory; authors discuss parameter τ and suggest w=1 works well in practice.  
- Demonstrated applicability to influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation, showing versatility.  
- Recognizes interactive use cases where summaries must withstand multiple removal scenarios.  
- Experimental evaluation shows competitive but often marginal improvements over SIEVE-STREAMING; STAR-T-GREEDY is not consistently superior.  
- Influence maximization experiment uses an unconventional objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, raising concerns about generalizability to standard propagation models.  
- Parameter guidance is limited: τ choice and Theorem 4.5 approximation need clearer explanation for practical tuning.  
- Lacks an ablation study isolating effects of partitioning and thresholding to better understand component contributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, solid theoretical foundation, clear presentation, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental convincingness, nonstandard objective in influence experiment, and insufficient practical parameter guidance.  
- Overall: solid contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions focused on stronger experiments (including standard influence objective) and clearer parameter/sensitivity analysis.
