- Problem: Jointly address single-pass streaming and deletion-robustness for monotone submodular maximization, building a compact summary S that remains useful after up to m adversarial deletions.  
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T, a partitioned-bucket streaming scheme with exponentially growing bucket capacities and exponentially decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY extracts the final k-set.  
- Guarantees: Stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements if f(OPT) known, O((k + m log k) log^2 k) otherwise, and achieves a constant ~0.149 approximation as k → ∞ after m deletions.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and PRO-style partitioning but uses first-above-threshold placement; compares favorably to concurrent deletion-robust streaming (better k-dependence).  
- Theory quality: Novel and nontrivial guarantees with interesting case analysis, but proofs are terse and lack intuition; tightness of constants and lower bounds unclear.  
- Algorithms/complexity: Per-element processing may require scanning O(wk) buckets; practical guidance for parameter w is missing, and τ management via parallel instances is standard.  
- Experiments: Two tasks (influence via neighborhood-size and movie recommendation); STAR-T matches or beats baselines (even ones knowing deletions), but influence setup uses a simplified objective unlike standard IC/LT models.  
- Clarity: High-level exposition is OK, but analysis/proofs, parameter roles (w, τ), and the memory blowup explanation need clearer organization and intuition.  
- Originality: High — first constant-factor result for streaming + deletion-robust monotone submodular maximization with sublinear memory overhead.  
- Significance: High — practical and timely result; logarithmic k-dependence for robustness is attractive despite modest constant.  
- Strength: First constant-factor algorithm for robust streaming submodular maximization.  
- Strength: Clear, implementable partitioned-threshold algorithm distinct from prior robust methods.  
- Strength: Near-tight practical memory bounds: O((k + m log k) log^2 k) without f(OPT); O((k + m log k) log k) with τ.  
- Strength: Empirical performance competitive with strong baselines, including those with advance deletion knowledge.  
- Weakness: No non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize approximation/memory trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Analysis and writing often unclear; proof sketch lacks intuition and relies on appendix.  
- Weakness: Influence experiments use a simplified neighborhood objective, limiting claims for classic influence models.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of per-element runtime and practical parameter selection/sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: Add intuitive explanation and figure for the two main analysis cases, clarify when memory becomes O((k + m log k) log^2 k), and give practical recipes/sensitivity for w and τ.  
- Suggestions: Discuss per-element processing time and data structures to avoid scanning many buckets; extend or justify influence experiments (RR-set or IC/LT) or explain barriers.  
- Suggestions: Provide any partial lower bounds or comparisons to known streaming/robust lower bounds.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, significant setting with the first constant-factor streaming+robust guarantee and strong empirical results.  
- Rejection arguments: Clarity gaps in analysis, lack of lower bounds, and simplified influence experiments hinder full assessment.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — important core contribution but needs improved exposition and stronger empirical justification for influence tasks.
