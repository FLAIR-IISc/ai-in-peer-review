This paper presents a novel algorithm, STAR-T, for the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization. The algorithm addresses the challenge of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting, where elements arrive in a streaming fashion and a portion of the retained set may be removed. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, where the first stage involves a streaming algorithm that constructs a robust summary, and the second stage uses a simple greedy algorithm to produce a solution of size at most k.

The paper relates to previous work on submodular maximization, including the simple greedy algorithm and its limitations in the streaming setting. The authors also discuss related work on robust submodular maximization and submodular streaming algorithms. The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, extends the ideas of SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting, using a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.

The strengths of the paper include the achievement of a constant-factor approximation guarantee in the challenging combined setting of streaming and robust submodular maximization. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of STAR-T in two different data summarization applications, including influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. The algorithm performs well even when compared to algorithms that know the removals beforehand.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. Firstly, the analysis and writing are unclear, making it difficult to follow the proof sketch. The authors claim to have developed an innovative analysis, but it is not well-explained. Additionally, the paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee, which would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the problem. Furthermore, the influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, which raises questions about the applicability of the algorithm to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The algorithm is carefully designed, and the authors have made a significant effort to evaluate its performance in different scenarios.

Regarding clarity, the paper could be improved by providing a clearer explanation of the innovative analysis and the proof sketch. The writing is sometimes dense and hard to follow, which makes it challenging to understand the technical details.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel algorithm and a new approach to the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization. The combination of partitioning and thresholding with buckets is a unique aspect of the algorithm.

Finally, regarding significance, the paper addresses an important problem in machine learning and data summarization. The achievement of a constant-factor approximation guarantee in the combined setting of streaming and robust submodular maximization is a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel algorithm for a challenging problem in machine learning and data summarization.
* The algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee in the combined setting of streaming and robust submodular maximization.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in different scenarios.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The analysis and writing are unclear, making it difficult to follow the proof sketch.
* The paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee.
* The influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, which raises questions about the applicability of the algorithm to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, but with revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
