- Novel problem: first formal study of monotone submodular maximization combining streaming single-pass setting with robustness to adversarial removal of up to m elements.  
- Algorithm: two-stage method — STAR-T builds a compact summary S in one pass using partitioned buckets and exponential thresholding; STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on S \ E after removals.  
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ 0.149 · f(OPT(k, V \ E)) with summary size O((m log k + k) log k).  
- Empirical evaluation: tested on dominating-set (proxy for influence maximization) and personalized movie recommendation; competitive with or better than SIEVE-STREAMING even when that baseline knows the removed set E.  
- Strength — originality/significance: tackles a timely, well-motivated combination of streaming and robustness with practical applications.  
- Strength — theory quality: achieving any constant approximation in this setting is a significant technical contribution; memory bound has only log dependence on k.  
- Strength — experiments: well-designed comparisons and strong empirical evidence that the summary S is robust and useful in practice.  
- Weakness — clarity of analysis: proof sketch and high-level intuition are hard to follow; the case-based lemmas need better narrative and justification that they cover all possibilities.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: using neighborhood-size as a proxy for influence maximization oversimplifies real influence objectives that require costly Monte Carlo evaluations; no discussion of performance when function evaluations are expensive.  
- Weakness — missing lower bounds: paper lacks hardness results or lower bounds to contextualize how close the 0.149 approximation might be to optimal.  
- Overall: strong and valuable contribution with correctable presentation and scope limitations; theoretical advance and empirical validation make it a noteworthy addition to submodular optimization literature.
