- Proposes STAR-T, a novel two-stage algorithm for streaming robust submodular maximization (streaming summary then greedy selection).
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING ideas with a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.
- Achieves a constant-factor approximation in the combined streaming and robust removal setting.
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show strong empirical performance, even versus algorithms that know removals ahead of time.
- Algorithm is technically sound and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Analysis and writing are unclear; the proof sketch and novel analysis are hard to follow.
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper-bound guarantees.
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about applicability to traditional network-dependent influence measures in streaming.
- Originality: novel combination of partitioning, thresholding, and bucket structure for robustness in streaming.
- Significance: addresses an important problem and provides a meaningful constant-factor result.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, strengthen proofs, add lower bounds, and validate on more realistic influence objectives.
