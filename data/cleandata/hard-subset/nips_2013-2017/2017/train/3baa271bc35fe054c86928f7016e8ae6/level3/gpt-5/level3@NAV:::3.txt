This paper tackles robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting: select k high-value elements while remaining robust to adversarial removal of up to m items after the stream. The proposed STAR-T algorithm maintains a compact summary using partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds and, after deletions, applies GREEDY on the retained set. The authors prove a constant-factor approximation (0.149 as k grows) while storing O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements, and report strong empirical results on influence-style coverage and movie recommendation. The work is positioned against non-streaming robust methods (e.g., Orlin et al. 2016; PRO by Bogunovic et al. 2017) and streaming non-robust approaches (SIEVE-STREAMING), as well as concurrent deletion-robust streaming (Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017), improving the memory dependence on k from linear to logarithmic.

Quality: The technical direction is sound and addresses a difficult combined constraint. The partitioned thresholding design is plausible, and the 0.149 approximation under m deletions is nontrivial. However, the analysis presentation is hard to follow. The claimed “innovative analysis” is not sufficiently explained; the proof sketch obscures key invariants behind the balancing of lemmas, and the role of τ (and its data-dependent tuning) should be clearer. The paper also lacks nontrivial lower bounds (e.g., memory vs. approximation tradeoffs) to contextualize the 0.149 factor and the log^2 k memory. There is a minor inconsistency between sections on the precise log factors in the space bound. Experiments are promising, but the “influence maximization” task uses the neighborhood-size (dominating set) objective rather than IC/LT models, so applicability to traditional network diffusion in streaming remains uncertain.

Clarity: Writing is generally organized but technical sections need more intuition (why partition sizes and thresholds decrease exponentially; a small running example), an explicit per-element update complexity, and moving details of the τ-approximation (parallel instances and space blow-up) into the main text.

Originality: The joint treatment of streaming and robustness with a constant-factor guarantee appears novel; the bucket-filling rule differs meaningfully from prior robust work.

Significance: Addresses a relevant problem with clear potential for practitioners (summarization, recommendations, right-to-be-forgotten); the empirical gains against baselines that know deletions are compelling.

Pros for acceptance:
- First constant-factor algorithm for streaming robust submodular maximization with near-logarithmic dependence on k.
- Simple, practical two-stage design; strong empirical performance.
- Clear advancement over concurrent/deletion-robust streaming memory bounds.

Cons against acceptance:
- Analysis and exposition lack clarity; no lower bounds.
- Experimental influence objective is simplified; limited evidence for IC/LT settings.
- Minor inconsistencies in space guarantees and limited discussion of computational costs.
