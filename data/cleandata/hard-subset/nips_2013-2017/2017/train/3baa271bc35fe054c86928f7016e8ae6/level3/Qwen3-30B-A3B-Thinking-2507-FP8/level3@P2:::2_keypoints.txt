- Paper proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraints, building a robust summary in one pass and applying greedy after up to m removals.
- Extends partitioning structure from centralized robust algorithms and uses exponentially decreasing thresholds to handle robustness in streaming.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and provides constant-factor approximation guarantees and memory bounds O((k + m log k) log k).
- Empirically, STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows the removed elements in advance.
- Main strength: clear theoretical contribution and novel algorithmic approach for robust streaming submodular maximization.
- Strength: Guarantees hold for any number of removals m within memory constraints.
- Strength: Practical relevance demonstrated on two data summarization tasks (dominating set and personalized movie recommendation).
- Strength: Analysis balances memory-approximation trade-offs carefully.
- Strength: Practical implementation details (e.g., w=1) shown to work well in experiments.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited and does not thoroughly validate robustness claims.
- Weakness: Influence maximization experiments use a non-standard objective instead of the classical diffusion model, unclear real-world performance.
- Weakness: STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING in experiments, weakening practical significance.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to other robust submodular maximization methods in the streaming setting.
- Weakness: Theoretical reliance on knowing f(OPT(k, V\E)) is only addressed by a parallel algorithm; practical implementation of that extension is under-discussed.
- Quality: Theoretical results are sound and well-motivated; experiments are adequate but limited and acknowledged.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, logically structured, with helpful figures; some experimental presentation improvements suggested.
- Originality: Novel extension of partitioning with exponential thresholds to the robust streaming setting, well-situated in related work.
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem with a meaningful constant-factor guarantee; practical advantage needs stronger empirical support.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, practical relevance, demonstrated application performance, and clear relation to prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation of robustness, inconsistent empirical advantage over SIEVE-STREAMING, non-standard influence objective, and limited comparisons to other robust approaches.
- Overall recommendation: Technically solid and novel work deserving acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen experimental validation and robustness discussion.
