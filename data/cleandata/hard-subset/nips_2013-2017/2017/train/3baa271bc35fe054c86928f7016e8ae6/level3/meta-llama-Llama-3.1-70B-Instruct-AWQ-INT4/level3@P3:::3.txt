This paper addresses the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization, which is a novel combination of two challenging settings: streaming and robust submodular maximization. The problem requires selecting k high-value elements from a small retained set while being robust to the removal of m items. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm called STAR-T and a simple greedy algorithm called STAR-T-GREEDY.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by providing the first algorithm that jointly considers both streaming and robust submodular maximization. The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, uses a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule. The authors show that STAR-T maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation under m removals.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithm, and the theoretical results. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on two different data summarization tasks: influence maximization and movie recommendation. The results show that STAR-T performs well even when compared to algorithms that know the removals beforehand.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The paper addresses a novel and challenging problem that combines streaming and robust submodular maximization.
* The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, is based on a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithm, and the theoretical results.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on two different data summarization tasks.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper:

* The paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee. This is an important aspect of any approximation algorithm, as it provides a benchmark for evaluating the performance of the algorithm.
* The analysis and writing of the paper could be improved. The claimed "innovative analysis" is not explained clearly, and the proof sketch is hard to follow.
* The influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, which raises questions about the applicability of the algorithm to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization and provides a novel solution to the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization. However, there are some areas that need improvement, such as providing non-trivial lower bounds and improving the analysis and writing of the paper.
