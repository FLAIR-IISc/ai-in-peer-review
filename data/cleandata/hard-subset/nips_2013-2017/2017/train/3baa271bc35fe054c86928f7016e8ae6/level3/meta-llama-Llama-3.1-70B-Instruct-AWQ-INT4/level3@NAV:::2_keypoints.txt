Proposes STAR-T, a novel two-stage streaming method using partitioning and a decreasing-threshold rule to build a compact, robust summary for submodular maximization under cardinality k.  
Applies a simple greedy algorithm to the STAR-T summary to obtain a constant-factor approximation.  
Theoretical analysis is thorough and sound, including proofs of the approximation guarantee and time/space complexity.  
Original contribution: claimed to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, robust to any number of removals m with O(m log^2 k) memory.  
Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and accessible to readers not expert in submodular optimization.  
Results are significant and likely to be adopted or built upon by practitioners and researchers.  
Experimental weaknesses: tests are not very expressive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming.  
Influence-max experiment uses a non-standard setup (differs from Kempe et al.), so performance on the true diffusion model is unclear.  
Overall assessment: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
