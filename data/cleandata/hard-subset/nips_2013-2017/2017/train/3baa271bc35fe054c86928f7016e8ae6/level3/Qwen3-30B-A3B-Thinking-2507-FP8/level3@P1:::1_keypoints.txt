- Problem: Studies robust streaming submodular maximization — build summary T so that after removing any m elements one can still pick a k-sized set with high value.  
- Method: Proposes STAR-T, a streaming algorithm using partitioned thresholding to construct T with size O((k + m log k) log^2 k).  
- Guarantee: Running greedy on the remainder yields a constant-factor approximation (Theorem 4.1: 0.149(1−1/log k)).  
- Novelty: Extends prior robust submodular work to the streaming setting via an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule across partitions.  
- Technical quality: Strong, non-trivial algorithm design with comprehensive analysis and full proofs in supplementary material.  
- Practicality: Provides a parallel variant (Theorem 4.5) to handle unknown f(OPT) with slightly increased memory.  
- Empirics: Shows competitive performance against SIEVE-STREAMING, even when that baseline knows the removed elements.  
- Strengths: Technically sound algorithm, clean analysis, and promising empirical results for the targeted setting.  
- Weakness — motivation: Application examples (movies, dominating sets) do not convincingly justify the need for robustness in practice.  
- Weakness — formulation: Problem is narrowly defined (only m removals from summary) and ignores other robustness models (adversarial/probabilistic removals).  
- Weakness — significance: Limited broader impact; too specialized for NIPS and does not clearly advance the field beyond prior robust submodular work.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear algorithms and accessible proofs for experts.  
- Recommendation: Recommend rejection for NIPS due to weak motivation, narrow formulation, and limited significance; suggest stronger motivation and generalization to other robustness models.
