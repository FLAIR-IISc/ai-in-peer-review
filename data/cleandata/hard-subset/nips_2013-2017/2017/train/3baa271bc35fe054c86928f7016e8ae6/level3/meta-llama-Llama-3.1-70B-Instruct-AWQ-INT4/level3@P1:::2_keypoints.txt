- Proposes a novel two-stage method: STAR-T streaming summary followed by greedy selection for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and decreasing-threshold rule and is claimed to have a constant approximation even as k → ∞.  
- Provides theoretical bounds and lemmas (including Lemmas 4.2–4.3 and Theorem 4.5) showing robustness to element loss.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show competitive performance with existing methods.  
- Novel contribution: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with potential large-scale applications.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees supporting the approach and approximation claims.  
- Experimental validation across two application domains demonstrates practical potential.  
- Experiments lack expressiveness and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming.  
- Influence-max experiments use an objective differing from the classical Kempe et al. diffusion model, leaving real diffusion performance unclear.  
- Hyperparameter choices (τ and w), particularly w=1, are not thoroughly justified despite an approach to approximate τ.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with concise problem setup and algorithm description.  
- Overall high quality with clear writing, strong theory, and competitive experiments but notable experimental and hyperparameter limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to expand experiments, justify w=1 and hyperparameter tuning, and evaluate with a more realistic influence-spread model.
