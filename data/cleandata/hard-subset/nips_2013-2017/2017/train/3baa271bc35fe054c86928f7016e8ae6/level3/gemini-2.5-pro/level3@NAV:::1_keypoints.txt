- Problem: single-pass streaming robust submodular maximization to build a small summary T that remains useful after any m adversarial deletions.
- Prior work: builds on streaming submodular maximization and non-streaming robust optimization literature.
- Approach: novel two-stage algorithm—STAR-T constructs a partitioned summary with exponentially decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY selects the final k-set.
- Theoretical contribution: non-trivial constant-factor approximation for a challenging problem, merging streaming and robustness ideas.
- Summary size: O((m log k + k) log^2 k), which has favorable logarithmic dependence on k and compares well to concurrent work.
- Originality and technical quality: algorithm design and analysis are novel and technically strong.
- Weak motivation: practical examples (e.g., movie recommendations) feel contrived and do not convincingly justify the complex formulation.
- Problem formulation concern: robustness considers m deletions from the summary T rather than from the ground set E, limiting practical applicability.
- Impact limitation: the restrictive formulation may reduce real-world relevance and the paper’s influence on practitioners.
- Relevance to NeurIPS: contribution is largely theoretical/algorithmic with limited ties to mainstream ML applications; may be better suited for a theoretical CS venue.
- Overall verdict: high-quality, original theoretical result but significance is hampered by narrow formulation and weak motivation.
