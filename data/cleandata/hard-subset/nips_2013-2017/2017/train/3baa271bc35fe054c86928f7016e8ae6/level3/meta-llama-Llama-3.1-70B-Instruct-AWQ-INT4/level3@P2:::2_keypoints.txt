- Proposes a novel two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization: STAR-T (streaming summary) followed by a greedy selection on the summary.
- STAR-T is a one-pass streaming algorithm that retains a small memory summary and uses a novel partitioning structure with a decreasing-threshold rule.
- Authors claim STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞.
- Distinct from prior work (greedy, SIEVE-STREAMING, other robust methods) due to its partitioning structure and thresholding strategy.
- Technical presentation and analysis are clear; main result (Theorem 4.1) is supported by detailed proofs and lemmas showing tolerance to element loss.
- Experiments are well-designed and show competitive performance on two data summarization tasks.
- Empirical weaknesses: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING, so practical gains are not definitive.
- Experimental objective (influence-max used) differs from the classical Kempe et al. diffusion model, leaving performance on true diffusion unclear.
- Originality is high: claims to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.
- Significance is high due to potential wide applications in data summarization and related areas.
- Clarity and writing are good, with well-defined notation and organized exposition.
- Main strengths: novel algorithmic approach, new partitioning/thresholding ideas, rigorous theoretical guarantees, and competitive empirical results.
- Main weaknesses: limited experimental expressiveness, mismatch of experimental objective with classical diffusion model, and lack of explicit discussion on limitations and future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, and potentially impactful approach with supportive experiments.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficiently expressive experiments and unclear behavior on the true diffusion/influence model.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, but encourage authors to address limitations and expand discussion of future directions.
