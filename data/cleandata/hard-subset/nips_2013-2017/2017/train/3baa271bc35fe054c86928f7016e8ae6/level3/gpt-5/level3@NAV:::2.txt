This paper proposes a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in the streaming model. Stage 1 (STAR-T) maintains a partitioned summary with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds; Stage 2 runs standard GREEDY on the surviving summary after up to m elements are removed. Theoretically, Theorem 4.1 establishes a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149 as k→∞) with space O((k + m log k) log k), and Lemmas 4.2–4.4 analyze the two structural regimes (many full buckets vs. none) to argue robustness to deletions. A parallel instantiation (Thm. 4.5) removes dependence on OPT via a geometric grid over τ with an extra log factor. Empirically, the method is tested on dominating set/influence-style selection and a personalized movie recommendation objective, showing competitive performance with Sieve-Streaming and GREEDY variants.

Relation to prior work: The method extends streaming thresholding (Sieve-Streaming; Badanidiyuru et al., 2014) to the robust setting using a non-uniform partitioning idea akin to offline robust methods (e.g., PRO; Bogunovic et al., 2017). It is concurrent with deletion-robust streaming work (Mirzasoleiman et al., 2017) but improves memory from O(mk log k/ε) to logarithmic in k, at the cost of a smaller approximation constant.

Quality: The technical core is solid and carefully argued, with clear case analyses and a principled use of GREEDY’s guarantees. A few issues merit clarification: (i) minor inconsistencies in space bounds (abstract/conclusion state O((k + m log k) log^2 k) vs. Thm. 4.1’s O((k + m log k) log k), and Thm. 4.5 adds another logarithmic factor); (ii) the threshold condition in pseudocode uses τ/min{2^i,k}, whereas the text describes τ/2^i—please reconcile; (iii) report per-element processing time (expected O(w log k)?) and constant factors; (iv) more details on estimating τ and sensitivity to τ and w (beyond w=1).

Clarity: Generally well-written and organized; the partition/threshold mechanism is understandable. The above notational and complexity clarifications would improve readability and reproducibility.

Originality: High. To my knowledge, this is the first streaming algorithm with provable robustness to deletions with memory only logarithmic in k.

Significance: Potentially broad impact for large-scale, deletion-robust summarization. The 0.149 constant is modest but acceptable in streaming; practical competitiveness is important.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel robust streaming formulation and algorithm with clean analysis and memory-log(k).
- Clear, modular two-stage design; easy to deploy.
- Handles unknown OPT via parallel thresholds (Thm. 4.5).

Cons against acceptance:
- Experimental setup is limited: the “influence” objective f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| differs from classical IC/LT diffusion; performance on true diffusion models remains unclear.
- STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming; ablations on τ,w and robustness vs. m are sparse.
- Minor inconsistencies in bounds and threshold specification; limited runtime/memory reporting.

Suggestions: Evaluate on IC/LT with RIS/CELF++ on smaller graphs; add ablations for w and m, report throughput/memory; compare against the concurrent deletion-robust streaming baseline; release code and parameter grids. Overall, a strong theoretical contribution with room to strengthen the empirical evidence.
