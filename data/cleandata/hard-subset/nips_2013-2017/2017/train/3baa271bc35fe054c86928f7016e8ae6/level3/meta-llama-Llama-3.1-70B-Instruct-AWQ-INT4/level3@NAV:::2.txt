This paper proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k, which is a crucial problem in many large-scale machine learning applications. The proposed method, called STAR-T, uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule to make one pass over the data and retain a short but robust summary. The authors then apply a simple greedy algorithm to the summary produced by STAR-T to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their algorithm, including a proof of the constant-factor approximation guarantee and a discussion of the algorithm's time and space complexity. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in two different data summarization applications: influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.

The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors provide sufficient background information and explanations of the technical concepts, making the paper accessible to readers who are not experts in submodular optimization.

The paper is original and makes a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization. The proposed method is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, and it has potentially wide applications in large-scale machine learning. The authors also provide a novel analysis that allows them to be robust to any number of removals m as long as they are allowed to use O(m log2 k) memory.

The results are significant, and other people (practitioners or researchers) are likely to use these ideas or build on them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way. The authors provide unique data, unique conclusions on existing data, or a unique theoretical or pragmatic approach.

However, there are some minor concerns. The experiments are not very expressive, and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming. Additionally, the experiment setup for the influence-max objective used differs from the classical Kempe et al. spread model, so STAR-T's performance on the true diffusion model is unclear.

In conclusion, this paper is a strong contribution to the field of submodular optimization, and it has the potential to make a significant impact in large-scale machine learning applications.
