- Problem: robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting—build small summary S so after an adversary removes m elements one can still find a k-element solution with good value.  
- Algorithm: two-stage procedure—STAR-T builds summary in one pass using partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY extracts final k-set after removals.  
- Main theoretical result: constant-factor approximation (~0.149) with summary size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Novelty: combines streaming thresholding ideas with a partitioned structure in a non-trivial way; analysis balances multiple lemmas across scenarios.  
- Comparison to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and Bogunovic et al.; concurrent work [18] achieves a different memory-approximation trade-off (linear vs logarithmic dependence on k).  
- Strength: technically strong, intricate and apparently correct analysis, and a solid complete theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: significant theoretical advance—first constant-factor streaming-robust result with logarithmic dependence on k in memory bound.  
- Weakness: practical motivation and application examples are weak and do not convincingly justify the specific robust streaming formulation.  
- Weakness: crucial ambiguity about adversary model—paper/theory says removals E ⊂ V but experiments remove elements from S; this needs clarification.  
- Implication of ambiguity: if adversary can remove from V the guarantee is much harder and possibly infeasible; if restricted to S the model is weaker and should be stated.  
- Weakness: tenuous connection to ML practice and conference relevance unless a concrete application requiring this exact formulation is shown.  
- Weakness: gap between theoretical memory requirements (w scaling with m log k / k) and practical experiments using w=1; lacks intuition or discussion on this discrepancy.  
- Recommendation: clarify adversarial model throughout, strengthen motivation/use-cases where pre-computed summaries face unforeseen removals, and discuss the theory-vs-experiment parameter gap.
