- Problem: novel combination of streaming and robust submodular maximization—select k high-value elements from a small retained set robust to removal of m items.  
- Approach: two-stage procedure—streaming algorithm STAR-T followed by greedy STAR-T-GREEDY.  
- Novelty: first algorithm to jointly handle streaming and robust submodular maximization.  
- Algorithmic ideas: STAR-T uses a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theoretical guarantees: stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation under m removals.  
- Writing/clarity: paper is generally well written, but the claimed “innovative analysis” and proof sketches are hard to follow.  
- Experiments: evaluated on influence maximization and movie recommendation; performs well, even compared to algorithms that know the removals.  
- Strengths: tackles a challenging novel problem; introduces significant algorithmic ideas; clear exposition of methods and theoretical results; strong empirical validation.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper-bound guarantee.  
- Weakness: analysis and writing need improvement—proofs and key arguments are not clearly explained.  
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about applicability to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—authors should provide non-trivial lower bounds and improve the analysis/writing.  
- Rating: 8/10.
