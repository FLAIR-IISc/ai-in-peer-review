- Problem/setting: robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming with up to m arbitrary deletions from memory.  
- Algorithm: two-stage method — STAR-T streaming summary + STAR-T-GREEDY offline greedy on the summary after deletions.  
- STAR-T structure: partitioned summary with exponentially many buckets per partition, exponentially increasing capacities and decreasing thresholds; insert into first non-full bucket exceeding that partition’s threshold.  
- Main theoretical guarantee: builds a summary of size O((k + m log k) log k) and after ≤m deletions returns a k-set with a constant-factor approximation (→0.149 as k→∞); parameter w controls memory–robustness tradeoff.  
- τ-unknown handling: run parallel instances over a geometric grid of τ values, incurring an extra log factor and a (1+ε) loss (space → O((k + m log k) log^2 k)).  
- Proof approach: two-case analysis (many full buckets vs. last partition not-full) supported by Lemmas 4.2–4.4 to obtain lower/upper bounds and robust guarantees.  
- Novelty/originality: first streaming algorithm explicitly designed for deletion robustness, extending Sieve-Streaming ideas to a partitioned, decreasing-threshold design.  
- Comparison to related work: acknowledges concurrent deletion-robust streaming achieving 1/2−ε but with memory O(mk log k/ε); STAR-T’s memory is logarithmic in k and linear in m (up to logs).  
- Quality of theory: careful and technically sound; constant is small but typical in robust/sublinear settings and mitigated by experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation: competitive on two tasks (dominating-set surrogate and movie recommendation) but limited — sometimes matches rather than beats Sieve-Streaming and uses a nonstandard influence surrogate.  
- Experimental weaknesses: unclear performance under true diffusion models (IC/LT); insufficient details on τ selection, sensitivity to w, wall-clock/memory, stream/adversarial orders, and function evaluation counts.  
- Clarity: well written with clear pseudocode; should emphasize earlier the distinction between the base O((k + m log k) log k) bound and the τ-grid O((k + m log k) log^2 k) bound.  
- Significance: important and timely problem; one-pass, small-memory summary enabling multiple queries is practically valuable if backed by broader empirical evidence.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate under IC/LT or justify surrogate; add ablations on w and ε, provide τ selection guidance and stream-order sensitivity; report memory/time/evaluations; compare to concurrent/offline baselines; clarify deletion protocol fairness.  
- Pros: principled partitioned thresholding, one-pass algorithm, logarithmic-in-k memory for base algorithm, simple reusable greedy query stage, empirical competitiveness.  
- Cons: limited and nonstandard experiments, inconsistent advantage over strong baselines, sparse practical guidance on hyperparameters and runtime, and a small theoretical constant requiring broader validation.
