- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting—select k elements robust to up to m removals.  
- Algorithm/Contribution: Proposes STAR-T, the first algorithm to jointly handle streaming and robustness via partitioned thresholding.  
- Key idea: Exponentially increasing bucket sizes with exponentially decreasing thresholds to maintain robustness.  
- Memory bound: Summary size claimed O((m log k + k) log² k).  
- Theoretical guarantee: Rigorous constant-factor approximation of 0.149 under stated assumptions.  
- Proof quality: Technical proofs appear sound but the “innovative analysis” is poorly explained and key steps are deferred/omitted.  
- Missing theory: No non-trivial lower bounds provided, so optimality of the 0.149 factor is unclear.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on influence maximization and movie recommendation show STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removed elements).  
- Experimental limitation: Influence experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, not standard diffusion models (IC/LT), limiting real-world applicability.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and algorithm descriptions are clear, but the theoretical analysis and proof sketch lack intuition and detail.  
- Originality: Strong—first to combine streaming and robustness; partitioned thresholding departs from prior greedy bucket methods.  
- Significance: Relevant for large-scale ML applications needing streaming summaries robust to removals.  
- Overall assessment: Novel and technically significant but weakened by clarity issues and missing lower bounds.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept.
