**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage uses a streaming algorithm called STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule to produce a robust summary of the data. The second stage applies the standard greedy algorithm to the summary produced by STAR-T. The authors claim that STAR-T has a constant approximation guarantee even as k approaches infinity. They provide theoretical bounds and lemmas to support their claims, including Lemmas 4.2-4.3, which show that STAR-T combined with the greedy algorithm can tolerate the loss of elements. The paper also presents experimental results on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation, demonstrating competitive performance with existing methods.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novelty**: The paper proposes the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which has potentially wide applications in large-scale machine learning tasks.
2. **Theoretical Guarantees**: The authors provide theoretical bounds and lemmas to support their claims, including a constant approximation guarantee for STAR-T.
3. **Experimental Results**: The paper presents experimental results on two different applications, demonstrating competitive performance with existing methods.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited Expressiveness of Experiments**: The experiments are not very expressive, and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming.
2. **Questionable Experiment Setup**: The influence-max objective used in the experiments differs from the classical Kempe et al. spread model, so STAR-T's performance on the true diffusion model is unclear.
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning**: The paper has two hyperparameters (τ and w), and while Theorem 4.5 provides a way to approximate τ, the choice of w=1 is not thoroughly justified.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. The use of a streaming algorithm and a partitioning structure is innovative, and the theoretical guarantees provided are impressive. The experimental results demonstrate the potential of the method in real-world applications.

**Clarity and Organization**

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a concise introduction to the problem and the proposed method, followed by a detailed description of the algorithm and its theoretical guarantees. The experimental results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

**Quality of the Paper**

Overall, the paper is of high quality, with a clear and concise writing style, impressive theoretical guarantees, and competitive experimental results. However, the limitations of the experiments and the choice of hyperparameters are notable weaknesses.

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I would recommend accepting the paper for publication, but with some revisions to address the limitations of the experiments and the choice of hyperparameters. Specifically, the authors should:

1. Provide more expressive experiments that demonstrate the performance of STAR-T-Greedy in a wider range of scenarios.
2. Justify the choice of w=1 and provide a more thorough analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process.
3. Consider using a more realistic influence-max objective in the experiments, such as the classical Kempe et al. spread model.
