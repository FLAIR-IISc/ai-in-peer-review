- Problem: Studies deletion-robust streaming submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint with a two-stage (streaming summary S, query after m deletions) setting.
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T, a partitioned-bucket streaming algorithm with exponentially increasing bucket capacities and decreasing thresholds; query uses GREEDY on S\E.
- Theoretical guarantees: With memory parameter w ≳ (m log k)/k, stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements with known τ and achieves a constant-factor approximation; guessing τ adds an extra O(log k) factor.
- Approximation factor: Proven constant-factor that tends to ≈0.149 as k grows.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and centralized robust methods (Orlin et al., PRO); concurrent with [18] which achieves 1/2−ε but uses O(mk log k/ε) memory.
- Tradeoff vs concurrent work: Trades a smaller approximation for substantially better memory dependence in k (logarithmic vs linear).
- Strength — motivation: Tackles a timely, practical problem (deletion-robust summarization in one pass).
- Strength — simplicity: Uses a simple, clean two-stage algorithmic template easy to implement conceptually.
- Strength — theory: Provides provable constant-factor robustness with memory sublinear in mk and favorable scaling for large k.
- Strength — experiments: Empirical results competitive with strong baselines, often matching or beating baselines even when they know deletions.
- Weakness — unclear analysis: Some parts of the analysis are hard to follow and contain inconsistencies (threshold formula vs text, storage bounds).
- Weakness — no lower bounds: Lacks nontrivial lower bounds to contextualize the memory–approximation tradeoff and constants.
- Weakness — influence experiments: Influence-maximization evaluation uses dominating-set proxy rather than standard IC/LT models, limiting applicability claims.
- Weakness — runtime concerns: Per-element processing could be high in worst-case due to scanning many buckets; runtime/throughput not fully addressed.
- Quality — proofs: High-level proof strategy is sensible but presentation leaves gaps and inconsistent statements (thresholds, storage bounds); needs reconciliation and unified intuition for key lemmas.
- Quality — experiments: Tasks are reasonable proxies and results strong, but should add IC/LT experiments and report wall-clock time and memory usage.
- Quality — practicality: Need explicit per-element complexity discussion and ideas (data structures, heuristics) to avoid Θ(wk) scans.
- Clarity issues: Clarify τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}, exact storage bounds (with/without τ guessing), log base and ⌈log2 k⌉ usage, and provide pseudocode for parallel-τ instances.
- Originality: Novel combination of streaming and deletion-robustness with constant-factor guarantee and sublinear memory; adaptation of non-uniform partitioning to streaming is principled despite concurrency with [18].
- Significance: Advances deletion-robust streaming summarization and is relevant for robust sketches and “right-to-be-forgotten” scenarios despite modest constant.
- Questions for authors: quantify per-element time and mitigation techniques; sensitivity of performance to w; include IC/LT results; detail τ discretization and practical overhead; empirical comparison to [18] under matched memory.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses important problem, solid theoretical guarantees with favorable memory scaling, simple design with competitive empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: Clarity/analysis inconsistencies, limited classical influence experiments, lack of runtime/memory plots, and no lower bounds on tradeoffs.
- Minor nits: Unify threshold expression, clarify when each storage bound applies, specify log base and ⌈log k⌉ usage, discuss constants and small-k behavior, expand intuition for balancing lemmas and choice of w.
