- Tackles the novel problem of robust streaming submodular maximization (streaming + resilience to removals).  
- Proposes STAR-T with a 0.149-approximation after m removals and memory O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Problem statement is clear and motivations (e.g., influence maximization, recommendations) are well-grounded.  
- Prior work connections are reasonably established, positioning this work as an extension of streaming and robust methods.  
- Novelty/significance: first to combine streaming and robustness with a constant-factor approximation.  
- Practical performance: experiments show STAR-T is competitive and sometimes outperforms baselines on multiple tasks.  
- Well-motivated application scenarios make the problem relevant to real-world large-scale summarization.  
- Lacks lower bounds to contextualize how close the achieved approximation is to optimal.  
- Analysis and writing are often unclear; the proof sketch is hard to follow and key steps need more explanation.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, limiting applicability to realistic cascade models.  
- The choice of simplified influence objective requires clearer justification and acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Parameter sensitivity (dependence on 'w') is underexplored; empirical reliance on w=1 needs systematic study.  
- Overall: valuable and promising contribution but weakened by missing lower bounds and unclear analysis.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions—clarify analysis, attempt (even preliminary) lower bounds, and address experimental limitations.
