- Introduces STAR-T, a novel algorithm for streaming robust submodular maximization combining streaming and robustness.  
- Proposes a two-stage procedure: one-pass streaming algorithm that retains a small set, then a greedy second stage to produce a solution of size ≤ k.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding; stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation under m removals.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two applications: influence maximization (with a simplified objective) and personalized movie recommendation.  
- Strength: achieves a constant-factor approximation in the challenging combined streaming+robust setting.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper-bound guarantee.  
- Weakness: analysis and writing are unclear; the claimed “innovative analysis” and proof sketch are hard to follow.  
- Weakness: influence-max experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about applicability to traditional network-dependent influence objectives.  
- Originality: introduces a new partitioning and thresholding (bucket) approach distinct from prior robust submodular work.  
- Significance: addresses a hard problem with important implications for large-scale machine learning and provides a valuable constant-factor result.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity and add complementary lower bounds.
