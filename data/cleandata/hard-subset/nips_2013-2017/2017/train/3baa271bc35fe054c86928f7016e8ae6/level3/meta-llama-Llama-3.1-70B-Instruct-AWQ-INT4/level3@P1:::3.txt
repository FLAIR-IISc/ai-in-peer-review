This paper introduces a novel algorithm, Streaming Robust submodular algorithm with Partitioned Thresholding (STAR-T), to tackle the challenging problem of streaming robust submodular maximization. The authors successfully address the joint consideration of streaming and robustness, which is a significant contribution to the field. 

The problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint is well-studied, but the addition of streaming and robustness requirements makes it particularly challenging. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, where the first stage involves a streaming algorithm that makes one pass over the data and retains a small set of elements in memory, while the second stage uses a simple greedy algorithm to produce a solution of size at most k.

The STAR-T algorithm is based on a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule. The algorithm maintains a set of elements of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) and achieves a 0.149 approximation under m removals. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in two different data summarization applications: influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.

One of the strengths of the paper is that it achieves a constant-factor approximation in this challenging combined setting, which is a strong result. However, there are a few weaknesses that need to be addressed. Firstly, the paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee. Secondly, the analysis and writing are unclear, and the claimed "innovative analysis" is not explained well. The proof sketch is hard to follow, making it difficult for readers to understand the underlying intuition. Finally, the influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about the applicability of the algorithm to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.

In terms of originality, the paper introduces a new approach to robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting. The use of partitioning and thresholding with buckets is different from prior robust submodular work. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in real-world applications.

The significance of the paper lies in its ability to tackle a challenging problem that has significant implications for large-scale machine learning applications. The algorithm's ability to achieve a constant-factor approximation in this setting makes it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above. The authors should provide more clarity in their analysis and writing, and consider adding non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee.
