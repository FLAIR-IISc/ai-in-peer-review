- Problem: first to study monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a setting that is both streaming and robust to up to m removals.
- Two-stage formulation: single-pass streaming builds a small robust summary S; after adversarial removal of m items, a query algorithm selects k items from the remaining summary.
- Algorithm (STAR-T): uses partitioning and thresholding with partitions having decreasing thresholds and buckets of exponentially increasing size; items are placed into the first qualifying bucket.
- Theoretical guarantees: constructs summary of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) and achieves a constant-factor approximation of 0.149 via greedy on the summary.
- Technical contribution: merges streaming thresholding ideas with partitioning from robust algorithms to give the first constant-factor guarantee for the combined problem.
- Empirical evaluation: strong performance on influence-maximization (using a neighborhood proxy) and movie recommendation tasks, often outperforming baselines even with baselines given unrealistic oracle access to removals.
- Strength — originality/significance: addresses a practical, novel problem and opens a new research direction.
- Strength — technical quality: solid analysis and reasonable memory complexity with logarithmic dependence on k.
- Strength — experiments: empirical results convincingly show practical utility and robustness of the summary approach.
- Weakness — clarity: theoretical presentation and proof sketch are hard to follow and lack intuition about the novel analysis steps and lemma interactions.
- Weakness — completeness: no discussion of lower bounds or hardness, so the optimality of the 0.149 constant is unclear.
- Weakness — experiment realism: influence-maximization uses a simplified dominating-set proxy instead of simulation-based IC/LT objectives, potentially overstating practicality in true streaming settings.
- Acceptance arguments: novel and important problem, first constant-factor guarantee, elegant algorithm with strong empirical results.
- Rejection arguments: unclear technical exposition, missing hardness/lower-bound discussion, and an oversimplified key experiment.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on substantially improving clarity (provide intuitive proof explanation) and addressing theoretical/experimental limitations.
