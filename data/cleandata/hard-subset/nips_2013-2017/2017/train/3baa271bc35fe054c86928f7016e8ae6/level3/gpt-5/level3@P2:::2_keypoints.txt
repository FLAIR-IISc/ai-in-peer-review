- Problem: Robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a single-pass streaming setting where up to m items may be deleted after the stream.

- Method: Two-stage approach—STAR-T builds a compact partitioned-bucket summary with decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs a greedy query on S \ E to return k items.

- Main theoretical result: Constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 as k → ∞ with memory O((k + m log k) log^2 k) in one pass; multi-guess adds a log_{1+ε} k factor and (1+ε) loss.

- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING and offline robust methods (PRO); concurrent to Mirzasoleiman et al. (ICML’17) which gets 1/2−ε but uses O(mk log k/ε) memory—STAR-T trades approximation for much better k-dependence.

Strengths
- Novelty: First streaming algorithm with provable robustness to m adversarial deletions and constant-factor guarantee with memory scaling only logarithmically in k (up to logs).

- Sound theory: Partitioned, decreasing-threshold design is well-motivated and analyzed via complementary lemmas covering key cases.

- Practicality: Query-time decoupling allows reuse of the summary S for many deletion sets (useful for “right to be forgotten” scenarios).

- Empirics: On two summarization tasks STAR-T-GREEDY matches strong baselines, and experiments suggest w=1 can work well in practice.

Weaknesses
- Approximation constant: Proven constant (~0.149) is modest and no discussion on whether this is near the analytical limit for the approach.

- Parameter dependence and clarity: Relies on τ ≈ f(OPT) with multi-guess increasing space; inconsistency between prose (“τ/2^i”) and Alg.1 (“τ/min{2^i,k}”) needs clarification.

- Experimental scope limited: Uses a coverage proxy for influence instead of IC/LT diffusion models; lacks runtime/oracle-call/memory profiles and ablations over w and τ guesses; no comparison to Mirzasoleiman et al.

- Assumption on m: Requires m (or an upper bound) to set w and offers no strategies for unknown or varying m.

Quality
- Theory quality: Careful and technically sound development with explicit constants and memory bounds, but thresholding rule and per-element oracle complexity should be stated clearly.

- Experimental quality: Reasonable initial validation but lacks breadth (true influence models), ablations, and efficiency metrics.

Clarity
- Organization: Paper is readable and intuitively explains the partitioned-threshold idea.

- Fixes needed: Resolve the thresholding inconsistency and justify τ/min{2^i,k} vs τ/2^i; provide implementation details (τ guesses, ε, memory, avg. buckets per element, wall-clock times).

Originality
- Contribution: Novel combination of partitioned buckets and decreasing thresholds in a single-pass robust streaming setting, distinct from prior non-robust streaming work.

Significance
- Impact: Robust deletions and improved k-scaling are practically relevant for large-scale summarization and personalization; stronger empirical validation would increase impact.

Pros for acceptance
- First constant-factor, single-pass robust streaming method with near-optimal dependence on k.

- Clean, reusable two-stage design with solid theory and competitive empirical performance.

- Broad applicability to summarization and recommendation under deletions.

Cons for acceptance
- Small proven approximation constant and empirical parity rather than clear wins.

- Experimental limitations (no diffusion models, no efficiency metrics, missing ablations).

- Minor clarity issues and dependence on τ guesses and known m.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify and make consistent the thresholding rule across text, algorithm, and proofs.

- Report runtime, oracle calls per element, actual memory usage, and add ablations over w and ε.

- Evaluate on IC/LT influence models and compare to Mirzasoleiman et al. (2017).

- Discuss handling unknown m (e.g., multiple STAR-T instances for geometric m or adaptive w).

- Add analysis showing when STAR-T’s memory beats O(mk log k) baselines as a function of k and m.

Overall evaluation
- Solid theoretical contribution with attractive memory scaling; empirical evaluation and some clarity issues need strengthening—reviewer leans toward acceptance pending those fixes.
