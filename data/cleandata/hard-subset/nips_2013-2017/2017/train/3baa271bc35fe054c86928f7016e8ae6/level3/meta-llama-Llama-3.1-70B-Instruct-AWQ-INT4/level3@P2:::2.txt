This paper proposes a novel two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage involves a streaming algorithm called STAR-T, which makes one pass over the data and retains a small set of elements in memory. The second stage applies a simple greedy algorithm to the summary produced by STAR-T. The authors claim that STAR-T has a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞.

The paper relates to previous work on submodular maximization, including the simple greedy algorithm [7] and the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm [8]. The authors also discuss related work on robust submodular maximization, including [9] and [10]. However, the proposed approach is distinct from these previous methods, as it uses a novel partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule.

The technical soundness of the paper is good, with a clear and well-organized presentation of the algorithm and its analysis. The authors provide a detailed proof of the main result, Theorem 4.1, which shows that STAR-T has a constant approximation guarantee. The proof is based on a combination of lemmas, including Lemmas 4.2-4.4, which show that STAR-T can tolerate the loss of elements.

The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the competitive performance of STAR-T on two different data summarization tasks. However, the experiments are not very expressive, and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming. Additionally, the influence-max objective used in the experiments differs from the classical Kempe et al. spread model, so it is unclear how STAR-T would perform on the true diffusion model.
