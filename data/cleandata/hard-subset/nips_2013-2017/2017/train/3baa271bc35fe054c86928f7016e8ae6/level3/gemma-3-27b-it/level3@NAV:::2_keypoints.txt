- Proposes STAR-T, a novel two-stage streaming algorithm (partitioned thresholding + greedy) for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraints.  
- Provides constant approximation guarantees that hold even as the cardinality k grows large.  
- Includes practical parameter estimation analysis (Theorem 4.5) improving usability.  
- Problem setting is well-motivated, clearly defined, and presented logically.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous with supporting lemmas for the main theorem.  
- Experimental results show competitive performance but do not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Improvements observed in experiments are modest, weakening claims of significant empirical advance.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a non-standard objective (|N(Z) ∪ Z|), which limits generalizability to the standard diffusion model.  
- Comparisons against random selection are useful but insufficient to justify algorithmic complexity.  
- Some proof sections lack clarity and would benefit from more detailed explanations.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong novelty and theoretical contribution that open a new research direction.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited empirical advantage and use of a non-standard objective question practical impact; overall recommendation is weak accept with stronger experiments urged.
