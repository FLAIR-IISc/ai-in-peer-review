- Problem: streaming submodular maximization robust to removal of up to m elements after the stream.
- Algorithm: STAR-T builds a summary T of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) enabling a greedy post-removal constant-factor approximation.
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and extends it to handle robustness requirements.
- Technique: introduces partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Analysis: non-trivial proof combining partition structure and decreasing thresholds to obtain guarantees.
- Guarantee: proves a 0.149(1 − 1/log k)-approximation that approaches 0.149 as k grows.
- Empirical result: STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines know removed elements in advance.
- Technical quality: strong, with well-structured proofs and careful handling of robustness.
- Literature context: correctly relates to recent robust and streaming submodular work and notes differences with concurrent work.
- Weak motivation: paper fails to convincingly justify why robustness is essential in the presented applications.
- Applications: movie recommendation and dominating set examples are not compellingly argued as needing robustness.
- Narrow formulation: focuses only on cardinality constraints rather than more general constraint types.
- Practical gap: does not explain why recomputing the summary after removals is infeasible or undesirable in practice.
- Significance concerns: problem seen as a specialized variant with limited broader impact for NIPS.
- Presentation: well-written and organized with clear algorithm descriptions and helpful figures.
- Suggested improvements: strengthen motivation, broaden problem formulation, and better demonstrate practical utility of robustness.
- Pros for acceptance: technically sound, non-trivial analysis, constant-factor guarantee, strong empirical performance, better memory complexity vs concurrent work.
- Cons for acceptance: narrow scope, weak practical motivation, questionable relevance to the broader machine learning community.
