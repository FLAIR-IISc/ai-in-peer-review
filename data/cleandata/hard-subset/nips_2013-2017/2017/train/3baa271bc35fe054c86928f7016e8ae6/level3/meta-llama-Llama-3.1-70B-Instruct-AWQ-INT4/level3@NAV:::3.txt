This paper addresses the problem of streaming robust submodular maximization, which is a novel combination of two challenging settings: streaming and robust submodular maximization. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) and a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY), to solve this problem. The STAR-T algorithm uses a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to construct a robust summary of the data. The STAR-T-GREEDY algorithm then uses this summary to produce a solution that is robust to the removal of up to m elements.

The paper makes several contributions. First, it provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust streaming submodular maximization problem, which is a significant improvement over previous results. Second, it introduces a novel analysis that allows for the use of O(m log^2 k) memory, which is a significant reduction in memory requirements compared to previous algorithms. Finally, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm through experiments on two real-world datasets.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a novel and challenging problem, and provides a significant improvement over previous results.
* The proposed algorithm is efficient and scalable, and can be used in a variety of applications.
* The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a constant-factor approximation guarantee.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in practice.

Weaknesses:

* The paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee, which would provide a more complete understanding of the problem.
* The analysis and writing are unclear in some places, and the claimed "innovative analysis" is not fully explained.
* The proof sketch is hard to follow, and some of the lemmas and theorems are not fully proven in the paper.
* The influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, which may not be representative of real-world influence objectives in streaming.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization, and provides a novel and efficient algorithm for solving the robust streaming submodular maximization problem. However, there are some areas for improvement, including providing non-trivial lower bounds and clarifying the analysis and writing.
