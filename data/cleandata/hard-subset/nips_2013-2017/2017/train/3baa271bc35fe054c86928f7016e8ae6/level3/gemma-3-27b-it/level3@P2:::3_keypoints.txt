- Introduces STAR-T for robust streaming submodular maximization, motivated by influence maximization and recommendations.  
- Maintains a partitioned summary with bucket assignment using a decreasing threshold rule to balance approximation and robustness.  
- Uses STAR-T-GREEDY to efficiently extract solutions after element removals.  
- Provides a constant-factor theoretical approximation guarantee (≈0.149).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets shows competitive performance and reusability of precomputed summaries for multiple removals.  
- Novel contribution: directly addresses the intersection of streaming and robustness in submodular maximization.  
- Builds on prior streaming and robust submodular work but uniquely handles combined constraints.  
- Weakness: Presentation and analysis lack clarity; proof sketch and numerous lemmas are insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness: No lower bounds provided for the combined problem to contextualize the approximation quality.  
- Weakness: Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, limiting applicability to realistic influence models.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity (e.g., w and τ) is not thoroughly investigated and lacks practical guidance.  
- For acceptance: Novel problem, theoretical foundation, strong empirical results, and extension of existing techniques.  
- Against acceptance: Unclear analysis, missing lower bounds, simplified experiments, and insufficient parameter discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve analysis clarity, add lower-bound discussion, expand experimental details, and study parameter sensitivity.
