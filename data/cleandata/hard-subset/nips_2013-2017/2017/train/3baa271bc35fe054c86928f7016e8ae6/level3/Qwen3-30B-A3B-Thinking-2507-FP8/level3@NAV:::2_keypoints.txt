- Proposes STAR-T, a novel two-stage streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraints (handles up to m removals).
- Uses a partitioning structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary, followed by a greedy stage.
- Theoretical results strong: Theorem 4.1 gives a constant approximation as k→∞; Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show robustness to removals.
- First streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, extending Sieve-Streaming to the robust setting.
- Practical implementation addressed: Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate parameter τ and experiments indicate w=1 works well.
- Major experimental weakness: influence maximization tests use a non-standard objective f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| rather than the classical Kempe diffusion model.
- Empirical results do not show STAR-T-Greedy consistently outperforming Sieve-Streaming, undermining practical advantages.
- Experiments lack depth and broader comparisons, failing to convincingly demonstrate superiority over existing methods.
- Paper is well-written and organized with comprehensive proofs.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but limited experimental evidence; additional standard-model experiments and comparisons needed.
