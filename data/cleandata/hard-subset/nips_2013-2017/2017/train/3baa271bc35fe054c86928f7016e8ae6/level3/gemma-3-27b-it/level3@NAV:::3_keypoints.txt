- Tackles the novel and important problem of streaming monotone submodular maximization with robustness to element removal.  
- Proposes STAR-T, a partitioned-thresholding algorithm with a theoretical 0.149-approximation under removal of m elements.  
- Builds on prior streaming (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and robust submodular work, introducing a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Empirical results (influence maximization, movie recommendation) show competitive performance, even versus algorithms that know removed elements.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds; absence of such bounds weakens theoretical completeness.  
- Analysis and writing are unclear; the “innovative analysis” is vaguely described and the proof sketch is hard to follow.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising concerns about applicability to realistic network-dependent objectives.  
- Recommendation: rejection with request for revision — improve clarity of analysis, add (even preliminary) discussion of lower bounds, and address experimental limitations.
