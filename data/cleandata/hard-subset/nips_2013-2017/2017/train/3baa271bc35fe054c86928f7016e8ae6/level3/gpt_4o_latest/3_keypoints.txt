- Addresses maximizing a monotone submodular function under a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting robust to post-stream element removal.  
- Novel contribution: first work to jointly handle streaming and robustness against adversarial/random removals.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm using partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholding.  
- Achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149.  
- Maintains a compact summary of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Significant theoretical contribution: constant-factor approximation in this challenging setting.  
- Empirical validation on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Performs comparably to or better than SIEVE-STREAMING, even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removed elements.  
- Summary reuse across different removal scenarios demonstrates practical utility.  
- Strength: strong novelty filling a gap in literature.  
- Strength: innovative algorithm design and solid theoretical result.  
- Strength: practical relevance shown by convincing experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement upper bounds.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is dense and poorly explained.  
- Weakness: influence maximization experiment uses an oversimplified objective.  
- Weakness: unclear how traditional influence objectives fit the streaming framework.  
- For acceptance: novel, significant theoretical advance with practical performance and well-designed algorithm.  
- Against acceptance: weak theoretical rigor due to missing lower bounds and unclear analysis; limited practical impact from simplified IM objective.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve analysis clarity and strengthen theoretical guarantees.
