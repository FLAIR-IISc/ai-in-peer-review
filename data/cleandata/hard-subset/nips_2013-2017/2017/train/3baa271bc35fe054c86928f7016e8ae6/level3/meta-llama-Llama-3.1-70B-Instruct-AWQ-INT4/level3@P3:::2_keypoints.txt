- Proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k.
- Introduces STAR-T, a streaming algorithm using partitioning and a decreasing-threshold rule to build a robust summary.
- Uses STAR-T-GREEDY (greedy on the summary) to obtain solutions with constant-factor guarantees after up to m removals.
- Advances over prior work by providing a single-pass streaming algorithm with stronger robustness than multi-pass or weaker-guarantee methods.
- Offers rigorous theoretical analysis including constant-factor approximation and bounds on summary size.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on two real-world datasets (social network and movie recommendation).
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and includes a thorough related-work discussion.
- Experimental results are well-presented and compare against existing baselines.
- Weakness: experiments are limited in scope and algorithm performance is not consistently superior to baselines.
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a different objective than the classic Kempe et al. spread model, limiting comparability.
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theory and experiments.
- High originality and significance for submodular optimization and data summarization.
