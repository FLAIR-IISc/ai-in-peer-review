- Introduces the novel problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting, combining two challenging constraints for the first time.  
- Problem formulation is well-motivated: single-pass summary that remains useful after adversarial removal of m items to produce a k-element solution.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that extends streaming (e.g., Sieve-Streaming) and robust optimization ideas via a partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Theoretical guarantees: summary size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) and a constant-factor approximation of 0.149.  
- Main strength is originality and theoretical significance—nontrivial constant-factor result for a new, important problem.  
- Empirical results are promising: STAR-T performs competitively even against methods with oracle access to removed items.  
- Major weakness: clarity of writing and analysis is poor; the “innovative analysis” and proof sketch are hard to follow and verify.  
- Lacks discussion of non-trivial lower bounds, so the optimality gap of the 0.149 guarantee is unclear.  
- Experimental limitation: influence maximization evaluation uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than standard network-dependent objectives.  
- Applicability concerns: simplified experimental objective raises questions about performance on traditional, computationally intensive influence maximization tasks.
