- Proposes STAR-T, a streaming algorithm using partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds for robust submodular maximization under element removal, with constant-factor approximation guarantees.  
- Addresses sequential arrival and post-selection removal, aiming to maintain a robust summary without reprocessing the full stream.  
- Positions work within existing literature, building on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust selection methods and citing relevant prior work.  
- Technical strengths include a thoughtful partitioning structure and non-trivial analysis to achieve robustness in streaming.  
- Theorem 4.1 gives solid theoretical bounds; Theorem 4.5 extends results to approximate f(OPT(k, V\E)) without prior knowledge.  
- Empirical evaluation (two applications) supports theoretical claims and shows comparable or improved performance versus SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- Practical advantage: precomputed summary can be reused for multiple removal scenarios (Section 5.2).  
- Primary weakness: limited motivation and narrow problem scope—robustness to arbitrary post-selection removal feels contrived.  
- Example applications (dominating set, movie recommendation) appear forced to fit the formulation and do not convincingly justify the setting.  
- Algorithm descriptions lack intuitive explanation—partition sizes and thresholding rationale are not clearly motivated in the text.  
- The role and link of parameter 'w' to theoretical bounds should be made more explicit.  
- Originality is solid in combining techniques but is incremental rather than a breakthrough.  
- Significance is meaningful within the defined setting but likely limited in broader impact due to narrow applicability.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, novel integration, empirically validated, and well-connected to prior work.  
- Against acceptance: questionable relevance to NIPS, limited motivation/scope, incremental novelty, and clarity/writing issues.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept — rigorous technical contribution but needs stronger justification and broader applicability to increase impact.
