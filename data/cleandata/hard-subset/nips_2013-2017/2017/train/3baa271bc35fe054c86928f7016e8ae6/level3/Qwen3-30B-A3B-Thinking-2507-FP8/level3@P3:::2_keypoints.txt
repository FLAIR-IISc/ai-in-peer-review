- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm with partitioned thresholding and STAR-T-GREEDY to handle m removals under cardinality constraints.  
- Constructs a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log² k) using exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee (c ≥ 0.149 as k → ∞) with rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorem 4.1 and Lemmas 4.2–4.4).  
- Novel partitioning structure and analysis extend streaming submodular maximization to the robust setting.  
- Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and prior robust work but differs by using partitioned thresholding instead of greedy buckets.  
- Claims superior memory complexity compared to concurrent work for large k (O((k + m log k) log k) vs O(mk log k/ε)).  
- Offers a practical implementation and a method to approximate the unknown parameter τ without losing guarantees (Theorem 4.5).  
- Empirically finds hyperparameter w = 1 works well in practice.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (only two applications) and lacks sufficient statistical depth and broader comparisons.  
- Results are inconsistent: SIEVE-STREAMING sometimes slightly outperforms STAR-T-GREEDY in experiments.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a non-standard objective f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, making applicability to classic spread models unclear.  
- Limited practical validation and no clear demonstration of cases where STAR-T-GREEDY clearly outperforms existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, and theoretically sound despite weaker experimental support.  
- Original and significant theoretical contribution as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.  
- Recommendation for acceptance based on strong theory and novelty, with a request to strengthen experimental validation in revisions.  
- Arguments against acceptance focus on inadequate experiments, questionable influence-max setup, and limited demonstration of real-world advantages.
