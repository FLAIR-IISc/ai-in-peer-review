- Tackles streaming robust submodular maximization, a novel combination of streaming and robustness challenges.
- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T (streaming summary via partitioning and exponential thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY (robust query).
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust streaming problem.
- Achieves reduced memory usage of O(m log^2 k), a significant improvement over prior algorithms.
- Empirically validated on two real-world datasets, showing practical effectiveness.
- Strength: addresses a novel, challenging problem and significantly improves prior results.
- Strength: algorithm is efficient, scalable, and broadly applicable.
- Strength: includes substantial theoretical analysis supporting the approach.
- Strength: experimental results corroborate theoretical claims.
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee.
- Weakness: analysis and writing are unclear in places; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not fully explained.
- Weakness: proof sketch is hard to follow and some lemmas/theorems are not fully proven in the paper.
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that may not reflect real-world influence goals.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, efficiency, and empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing lower bounds, unclear exposition, and incomplete proofs.
