- Problem: streaming monotone submodular maximization with cardinality k robust to removal of up to m elements — a novel, challenging setting.  
- Method: two-stage approach — STAR-T builds a robust summary via partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on S \ E after removals.  
- Theory: proves a constant-factor approximation of 0.149 while storing O((k + m log k) log k) elements.  
- Experiments: validated on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation; competes with baselines (including SIEVE-STREAMING given advance knowledge of removals).  
- Strength — originality/significance: first formal theoretical treatment of streaming + robustness for submodular maximization.  
- Strength — technical quality: elegant synthesis of robust partitioning and streaming thresholding, non-trivial constant approximation, and favorable log(k) memory scaling.  
- Strength — empirical: convincing results where STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms baselines despite operating only on the precomputed summary.  
- Weakness — clarity: theoretical analysis and proof sketch are hard to parse; key innovative steps (Lemmas 4.2–4.4) need clearer exposition.  
- Weakness — missing theoretical context: no discussion of lower bounds or hardness to contextualize the 0.149 guarantee.  
- Weakness — limited evaluation scope: dominating set marginals are cheap; applicability to expensive influence models (IC/LT) with costly oracle evaluations is unclear and not discussed.  
- Conclusion: strong, well-motivated contribution with presentation and evaluation scope issues; recommendation to accept.
