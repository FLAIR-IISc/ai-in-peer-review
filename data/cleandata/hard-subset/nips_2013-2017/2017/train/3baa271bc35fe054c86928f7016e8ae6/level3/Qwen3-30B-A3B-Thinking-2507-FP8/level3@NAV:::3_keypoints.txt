- Proposes STAR-T, a novel algorithm for robust streaming submodular maximization that jointly handles streaming and robustness constraints.  
- Targets selecting k elements from a stream while remaining robust to the removal of up to m elements.  
- Uses exponentially sized buckets with decreasing thresholds to build the summary.  
- Claims summary size O((m log k + k) log² k) and a 0.149 approximation guarantee.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Empirically outperforms baselines, including some that know removed elements in advance.  
- Significant contribution: combines streaming and robustness settings previously studied separately.  
- The constant-factor approximation for the combined problem is a meaningful theoretical advance over prior work (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING, PRO).  
- Analysis is poorly explained; the proof sketch is hard to follow and the 0.149 factor lacks a clear derivation.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds, making it hard to assess optimality of the approximation ratio.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than a network-dependent influence model, raising applicability concerns.  
- Writing is unclear in technical sections (bucket assignment and thresholding); paper needs clearer exposition and more rigorous theoretical justification.
