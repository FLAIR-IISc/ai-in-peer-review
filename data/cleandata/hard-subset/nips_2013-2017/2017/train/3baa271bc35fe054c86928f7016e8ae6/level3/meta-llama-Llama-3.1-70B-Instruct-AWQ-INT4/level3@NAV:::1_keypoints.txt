- Proposes STAR-T, a novel one-pass streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization that constructs a set S robust to removal of any m elements.
- Introduces STAR-T-GREEDY, a simple greedy post-processing algorithm with a constant-factor approximation guarantee on the remaining elements.
- Problem formulation is relevant to real-world applications (data summarization, influence maximization, personalized recommendation).
- Paper provides a clear and concise introduction explaining the problem and importance.
- Technical quality is high: non-trivial algorithm, challenging analysis, and proof of the approximation guarantee.
- Experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- Conference relevance is questionable: authors do not clearly connect the problem to broader conference themes or impact on the field.
- Formulation is limited: assumes monotone, normalized submodular functions, which may not hold in all applications.
- Authors should clarify relevance and potential impact to the conference audience.
- Authors should consider extending or discussing extensions to more general (non-monotone or unnormalized) settings.
- Overall well-written and a significant contribution to submodular optimization.
- Reviewer rating: 7/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to better explain relevance and broaden/justify the problem formulation.
