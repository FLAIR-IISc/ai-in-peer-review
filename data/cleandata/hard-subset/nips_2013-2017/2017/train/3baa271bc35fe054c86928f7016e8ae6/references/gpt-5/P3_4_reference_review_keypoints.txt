- Problem and approach: Proposes a two-stage streaming-robust submodular maximization method (STAR-T summary via partitioned thresholding, then STAR-T-GREEDY after deletions).

- Main theoretical result: With a good guess τ, stores O((k + m log k) log k) items and achieves ≈0.149(1−1/⌈log k⌉) approximation; unknown OPT adds an extra log factor for guessing.

- Relation to prior work: Extends sieve-style streaming and partitioned robust methods; trades worse constant for drastically lower memory vs concurrent robust streaming work.

- Strength — novel design: Partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds cleanly extend sieve methods to robust one-pass streaming.

- Strength — memory–approximation trade-off: Achieves O((k + m log k) log^2 k) memory (unknown OPT) vs O(mk) in concurrent work, improving scaling when k is large.

- Strength — two-stage model: Clear reusable summary S enabling many deletion queries via GREEDY, useful in practice.

- Strength — careful analysis: Proofs combine fullness guarantees, complementary bounds, and balancing on τ to obtain constant-factor robustness.

- Strength — empirical evidence: Experiments on dominating set and recommendation tasks show competitive performance with SIEVE-STREAMING and demonstrate reuse across deletions.

- Weakness — small constant: Approximation ≈0.149 is far below 0.5−ε; unclear if analysis is loose or constants can be improved.

- Weakness — τ specification: Theorem 4.1 contains a malformed τ expression; need a corrected, explicit τ and intuition for its choice.

- Weakness — threshold inconsistency: Text says τ/2^i but Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i,k}; reconcile and explain which is essential for analysis.

- Weakness — use of w in practice: Theory requires w ≥ ⌈4⌈log k⌉ m/k⌉ but experiments use w=1; paper should discuss practical choice, effects on memory/runtime, and sensitivity to m.

- Weakness — missing comparison to concurrent work: No experimental or discussion comparison to the independent concurrent deletion-robust streaming method [18].

- Weakness — missing space/time reporting: Experiments don’t report |S| or query-time (oracle calls) vs baselines; space/runtime plots are needed given the main contribution.

- Weakness — deletion sampling in experiments: Some experiments sample E ⊆ S rather than E ⊆ V; should include both random and adversarial deletions on V.

- Weakness — limited empirical scope: More datasets, larger k, and wall-clock runtimes would strengthen scalability claims.

- Weakness — proof sketch gaps: The origin of the 1−e^{-1/3} factor and the dependence on γ = 4m/(wk) need clearer intuition and a more transparent balancing argument.

- Minor — notation: Clarify whether E ⊆ V or E ⊆ S at query time and state that guarantees compare to OPT(k, V\E).

- Minor — derivations: Provide small derivation for inequalities in Equation (3) in the main text.

- Minor — introduction vs theorem: Clarify O((m log k + k) log^2 k) vs Theorem 4.1’s O((k + m log k) log k), noting extra log from guessing τ (Theorem 4.5).

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a principled, simple algorithm; provable constant-factor robustness with low memory and reusable summaries; advances memory scaling.

- Cons for acceptance: Low approximation constant, clarity issues in parameters/algorithm, limited experiments, and theory–practice gap on w.

- Recommendation: Weak accept.

- Reviewer confidence: High.
