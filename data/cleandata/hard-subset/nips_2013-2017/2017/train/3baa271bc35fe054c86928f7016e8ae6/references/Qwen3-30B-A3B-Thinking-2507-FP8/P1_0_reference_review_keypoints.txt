- Summary: Proposes a two-stage streaming approach (STAR-T + STAR-T-GREEDY) using partitioned thresholding to build a small robust summary that yields constant-factor approximations after removal of up to m elements.

- Strength 1 (Novelty): Introduces a partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and decreasing thresholds, extending SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting.

- Strength 2 (Theory): Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149) and well-structured lemmas addressing multiple cases.

- Strength 3 (Experiments): Empirical results show the method matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows removals) on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks.

- Strength 4 (Related work): Clearly differentiates from prior work, notably improving memory dependence over [18].

- Strength 5 (Practicality): Shows practical resilience beyond theoretical bounds, performing well even when |E| » k.

- Weakness 1 (Approximation): Approximation factor (0.149) is much lower than non-robust SIEVE-STREAMING’s (0.5−ε), and the trade-off needs more discussion.

- Weakness 2 (Memory): Memory O((m log k + k) log² k) is higher than SIEVE-STREAMING’s O(k log k), needing better practitioner context.

- Weakness 3 (Unknown τ): Handling unknown f(OPT) via parallelization increases memory by log^(1+ε) k; more practical guidance is needed.

- Weakness 4 (Comparisons): Experimental comparisons are limited, primarily against SIEVE-STREAMING, lacking evaluation versus other robust methods (e.g., [11]).

- Quality: Technically sound with thorough experiments and transparent limitation discussion.

- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with precise algorithms and reproducible supplementary material.

- Originality: Presents a novel, non-trivial extension of SIEVE-STREAMING for robustness via partitioned thresholding.

- Significance: Addresses a practical and relevant robustness problem; the two-stage summary-and-query design is valuable for applications.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance; solid theoretical and empirical contribution despite the lower approximation factor, which is an acceptable robustness trade-off.
