

# Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

This paper proposes a two-stage algorithm (STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization in streaming settings. The main contribution is a novel partitioned thresholding approach that aims to provide a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log² k) which can be used to quickly recover a good solution after any m elements have been removed.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a detailed description of the algorithm and its theoretical properties.
- The theoretical analysis provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149), which is a meaningful theoretical contribution.
- The experimental results demonstrate reasonable performance against SIEVE-STREAMING in several applications.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Theoretical guarantee is too weak:** The claimed approximation ratio (0.149) is substantially lower than the standard greedy algorithm's (1-1/e)≈0.632. This is a major drawback, as it suggests the algorithm would produce significantly worse solutions in practice compared to standard approaches. The paper fails to adequately justify why such a weak approximation ratio is acceptable for practical applications.

2. **Unrealistic assumption:** The algorithm assumes knowledge of f(OPT(k, V\E)), which is not available in practice. While the authors provide a workaround using multiple parallel instances, this increases space complexity by a factor of log₁₊ᵋ k, which may be prohibitive for large-scale applications. The paper does not discuss how this impacts practical deployment.

3. **Misleading experimental comparison:** The comparison against SIEVE-STREAMING is unrealistic because SIEVE-STREAMING is allowed to know in advance which elements will be removed. This is an artificial advantage that doesn't reflect real-world scenarios. In practice, no algorithm would have this information, making the comparison misleading. The authors should compare against a baseline that doesn't know which elements will be removed.

4. **Inadequate comparison with related work:** The paper cites [18] as concurrent work that provides a 1/2 - ε approximation guarantee with O(mk log k/ε) memory, which is superior to their approach in both approximation ratio and memory complexity. The paper fails to adequately discuss how their method compares to this work, which undermines the claimed novelty.

5. **Limited practical significance:** The marginal improvement over SIEVE-STREAMING (e.g., "STAR-T-GREEDY slightly outperforms the other two" in Figure 2(a,c)) doesn't justify the substantially weaker theoretical guarantee. The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate why the robust approach is worth the additional complexity and lower approximation ratio.

**Theoretical analysis:** The analysis is technically sound but unnecessarily complex. The authors should have focused on providing a stronger theoretical guarantee rather than a weak one with complex analysis. The connection to PRO [11] and other partitioned approaches is not clearly differentiated.

**Experimental validation:** The experiments are well-designed but suffer from the unrealistic comparison scenario. The authors should have included a more realistic baseline that doesn't know which elements will be removed. Additionally, the paper doesn't compare against other robust submodular maximization methods like PRO [11].

**Originality:** While the partitioned thresholding approach is novel, its practical contribution is limited by the weak approximation guarantee. The approach extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting, but the novelty is diminished by the fact that similar partitioned structures have been used in other robust submodular maximization algorithms.

**Significance:** The problem of robust submodular maximization is important, but the proposed solution doesn't advance the state of the art in a meaningful way. The paper fails to demonstrate that the robust approach provides significant practical benefits over simply running a standard submodular maximization algorithm on the reduced dataset after removals.

**Recommendation:** I recommend rejection. While the paper makes a theoretical contribution to the problem of robust submodular maximization, the practical significance is limited due to the weak approximation guarantee and the unrealistic experimental comparison. The authors should reconsider their approach to provide a stronger theoretical guarantee or better practical validation against realistic baselines before resubmission. The current work does not demonstrate sufficient advancement over existing methods to warrant publication at NIPS.
