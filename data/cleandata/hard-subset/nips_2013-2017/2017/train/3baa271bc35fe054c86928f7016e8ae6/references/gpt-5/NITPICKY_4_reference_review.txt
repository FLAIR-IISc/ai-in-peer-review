Summary
This paper studies robust streaming submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint. The authors propose a two-stage method: (i) a one-pass streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that stores a summary S organized into exponentially sized partitions and buckets guarded by exponentially decreasing thresholds; (ii) a query-stage algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that runs GREEDY on S\E after an arbitrary set E of at most m elements is removed. The theoretical result asserts a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 (as k→∞), using memory roughly O((k + m log k) log k), and a parallel-guessing variant to avoid requiring τ ≈ f(OPT(k, V\E)). Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation suggest performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING (even when giving the latter knowledge of E).

Strengths
- Tackles an important and timely problem: robust streaming submodular maximization with deletion constraints, relevant to data summarization, recommendation, and influence maximization.
- Algorithmic structure (partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds) is natural and connects to non-streaming robust constructions and to SIEVE-STREAMING, yet adapted to robustness in a single pass.
- Theoretical constant-factor robustness guarantee with space that depends logarithmically on k (improving over competing O(mk)-type memory bounds) is attractive.
- Clear separation of streaming and query stages; reusable summary supports multiple deletions E.
- Empirical results on two distinct applications, with attempts to benchmark against strong baselines.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity:
- τ dependence on f(OPT(k, V\E)): The algorithm and Theorem 4.1 assume access to a threshold τ that is an α-approximation to f(OPT(k, V\E)), which is unknown during streaming and depends on E (also unknown at stream time). While Section 4.1 proposes a parallel-guessing fix, details are insufficient: the range of guesses, how they relate to f(OPT(k, V)), and why the overhead is only a factor of log_{1+ε} k (rather than depending on a value range) are unclear. The statement “increases the space by a factor of log1+ k” is dimensionally odd and imprecise.
- Inconsistency in thresholds: Early text states partition i uses threshold τ/2^i; Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}. The latter seems required to ensure f(Bi,j) ≥ τ when full, but the discrepancy must be resolved and justified.
- Theorem 4.1 τ expression appears malformed: “τ = 1 + (1−e−1)(1−e−1/3)(1−1/dlog k) f(OPT(·))” mixes a dimensionless constant with an objective value; likely missing a division factor or parentheses. As written, units and magnitude are inconsistent.
- Proof sketches rely on vague averaging arguments: e.g., Lemma 4.2 uses “on average |B∩E| is relatively small,” yet E is adversarial. The rigorous argument should use a pigeonhole/counting bound leveraging the number of buckets per partition and the choice of w; the current wording undermines confidence.
- The factor (1 − e−1/3) in Lemma 4.3 is nonstandard; the conditions under which GREEDY yields that factor should be explicitly derived. As written, it is unclear what effective curvature/ratio or partial budget leads to 1/3.
- Space complexity inconsistencies: The intro and conclusion state O((k + m log k) log^2 k), while Theorem 4.1 and Eq. (3) give O((k + m log k) log k). Theorem 4.5 adds another factor log_{1+ε} k. These must be reconciled; the headline claim should match the strongest proved bound.
- The parameter requirement w ≥ ceil(4 dlog k e m/k) is awkwardly typeset and referenced. Later text claims “w=1 works well in practice” and “theoretically allows for up to k removals,” which contradicts the bound (w=1 implies m ≤ k/(4 log k)). This inconsistency appears multiple times.
- Several definitions and notational choices are sloppy (e.g., “dlog ke” base and ceiling/rounding not specified; undefined constants; inconsistent use of min{2^i,k} vs 2^i).

Experimental methodology:
- For dominating set, E is sampled from S (the summary) rather than from V, which disproportionately harms the proposed method. This is unconventional and confounds the interpretation. If the baseline SIEVE-STREAMING is “clairvoyant” about E, then E should be sampled from V, and both methods should be evaluated on S\E vs V\E fairly.
- No reporting of memory usage (|S|), number of function evaluations, or wall-clock time. The main claim is about space-efficiency and one-pass streaming; these metrics are essential.
- Statistical rigor is limited (100 iterations for some settings but no error bars). Parameter choices (τ guesses, ε, w, α for the recommendation task) are not documented in reproducible detail.
- The recommendation objective uses inner products of non-normalized feature vectors; this can be negative, violating monotonicity. The paper asserts monotone submodularity but does not justify why 〈vu, vz〉 ≥ 0 holds. If negative entries exist, guarantees do not apply.
- For graph experiments, directed vs undirected neighbors are not clarified; f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| is defined with “adjacent” but the datasets are directed; which direction is used?
- Claims of resilience to |E|≫k (e.g., removing 500 movies) contradict the theoretical robustness bound unless w is scaled. This should be framed as empirical robustness beyond theory, not as implied by guarantees.

Presentation and formatting issues (numerous minor but pervasive):
- Typos and inconsistent capitalization (STreAming Robust - Thresholding, “st Conference on NIPS 2017”), awkward math punctuation, missing parentheses and subscripts, and dangling references (“10, ,”).
- Algorithm 1 uses “break: proceed to the next element” but runs inner loops in an order that can be O(log k · w k/2^i) per element; no complexity commentary.
- Figures are referenced but not described with axes/units; no mention of k, m ranges.
- Several claims use vague phrases (“close to τ,” “relatively small,” “slightly outperforms”) without quantification.

Originality and relation to prior work:
- The partitioned robust structure echoes PRO [11], and the thresholding idea extends SIEVE-STREAMING [8]. The combination in the robust streaming context appears novel. However, the comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18] should be more precise: problem settings, adversary model, and approximation/space trade-offs side-by-side.
- Related work coverage is adequate in scope but lacks nuanced technical differentiation and misses some implementation details important for streaming.

Pros
- Addresses a relevant and difficult problem with a clean, scalable structure.
- Provides a constant-factor guarantee with memory nearly logarithmic in k for the robust case.
- Potentially impactful in practice due to reusable summaries and one-pass operation.

Cons
- Multiple technical inconsistencies (τ, thresholds, space, w vs m) and unclear or incomplete proofs lower confidence.
- Experimental setup lacks fairness and key resource metrics; potential violations of monotonicity in the recommendation objective.
- Numerous presentation/formatting issues hamper readability and reproducibility.

Questions for authors
1. Please provide a consistent and correct statement of the thresholding rule (τ/min{2^i,k} vs τ/2^i) and fix the definition of τ in Theorem 4.1.
2. Clarify how the parallel-guessing scheme avoids dependence on f(OPT(k, V\E)) and justify the claimed log_{1+ε} k overhead.
3. Reconcile the space bounds across sections and specify the exact constants hidden in O(·).
4. In experiments, sample E from V and report memory, function evaluations, and runtime. Explain the handling of negative inner products to preserve monotonicity.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The idea is promising and potentially significant, but the accumulation of technical and presentation issues, inconsistencies in statements/bounds, and weaknesses in the experimental methodology prevent acceptance in its current form. With a thorough revision addressing the above points, this work could be strong. My confidence is high.
