## Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the important problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The authors propose a two-stage algorithm, STAR-T, which first constructs a robust summary of the data in a single pass and then uses a greedy algorithm on this summary to respond to queries involving the removal of elements. The core innovation lies in a partitioned thresholding scheme designed to balance memory usage and robustness guarantees.  They prove a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robustness and demonstrate competitive performance against existing methods like SIEVE-STREAMING on several practical datasets.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Robustness is a crucial consideration in real-world deployments of submodular maximization, and the streaming setting adds significant practical challenges. Addressing both simultaneously is a valuable contribution.
* **Novel Approach:** The partitioned thresholding approach and the resulting data structure are novel and well-motivated. The idea of exponentially decreasing thresholds is clever and effectively balances the trade-off between summarization quality and robustness.
* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a formal approximation guarantee for the algorithm, which is a significant achievement. While the constant factor is small, the theoretical analysis is thorough and establishes a solid foundation for the work.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both the dominating set and movie recommendation tasks demonstrate that STAR-T performs well in practice, often matching or outperforming SIEVE-STREAMING, even when SIEVE-STREAMING has access to foreknowledge of removals.
* **Interactive Component:** The ability to generate multiple sub-summaries efficiently from a single precomputed robust summary is a valuable feature for interactive applications.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the technical details accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Small Constant Factor:** The constant factor in the approximation guarantee (0.149) is rather small. While theoretically sound, it raises questions about the practical effectiveness of the algorithm, especially when compared to algorithms with larger (though potentially requiring more information).
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The performance of the algorithm relies on the careful tuning of parameters like 'w' and 'τ'.  The paper acknowledges this, but more guidance on parameter selection would be beneficial. The mention that w=1 works well in practice is a little hand-wavy; a more detailed exploration of parameter sensitivity in the experimental section would strengthen the work.
* **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While the comparison to SIEVE-STREAMING is relevant, it would be helpful to compare against stronger centralized algorithms (e.g., PRO [11]) even if it requires simplifying the experimental setup.  This would give a clearer picture of the trade-offs between streaming and centralized approaches.
* **Complexity of Proofs:**  The theoretical proofs appear complex and are relegated to the supplementary material.  While this is acceptable, it makes it difficult to fully assess the tightness of the bounds and the intricacies of the analysis without a significant time investment.
* **Memory Usage:** Although the memory usage is stated as O((k + m log k) log k), this still feels substantial for very large values of k and m.  A more detailed discussion of the practical memory footprint and its scalability would be helpful.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of submodular optimization. The problem is important, the proposed approach is novel and theoretically sound, and the empirical results are promising. While the small constant factor in the approximation guarantee and the potential parameter sensitivity are weaknesses, the overall strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a relevant and challenging problem.
* Introduces a novel and well-motivated algorithm.
* Provides theoretical guarantees.
* Demonstrates competitive performance on real-world datasets.
* Offers a practical solution for robust data summarization.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Small constant factor in the approximation guarantee.
* Potential parameter sensitivity.
* Limited comparison to stronger baselines.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the NIPS community and warrants acceptance. I recommend the authors consider addressing the weaknesses mentioned above (particularly providing more guidance on parameter selection and comparing to stronger baselines) in a revised version of the paper.  The potential for this work to impact applications requiring robust data summarization is significant.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** Good. The technical aspects are sound, and the analysis is thorough.
* **Clarity:** Good. The paper is generally well-written and understandable.
* **Originality:** Good. The partitioned thresholding approach and analysis are novel.
* **Significance:** Good. The work advances the state-of-the-art in robust submodular maximization and has the potential to be widely used.
