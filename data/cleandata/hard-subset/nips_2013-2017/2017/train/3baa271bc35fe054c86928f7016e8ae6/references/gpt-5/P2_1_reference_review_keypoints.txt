- Problem and goal: robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming under cardinality constraint, precompute compact summary S robust to up to m deletions and answer size-k queries without revisiting the stream.  
- Proposed method: STAR-T (one-pass partitioned threshold streaming) + STAR-T-GREEDY (GREEDY on S \ E at query time).  
- Main theoretical result: constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) with space O((k + m log k) log k) (or larger with τ-guessing).  
- Proof approach: two-case analysis (many full buckets vs. many non-full buckets) using exponentially increasing bucket sizes and decreasing thresholds; memory parameter w trades space for robustness.  
- Space bound clarity issue: inconsistent statements O((k + m log k) log k) vs O((k + m log k) log^2 k); inferred |S| = O(k log k + m log^2 k) from Eq. (3).  
- Threshold inconsistency: text uses τ/2^i while Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}; needs a single correct rule (likely τ/min{2^i,k}).  
- τ expression/statement issue: Theorem 4.1’s τ appears garbled/missing ε and parentheses; needs a clean restatement.  
- Per-element time concern: STAR-T may require O(w k log k) marginal evaluations per element in the worst case; discuss expected/practical cost and optimizations (early stopping, indexing).  
- Experimental results: STAR-T matches or slightly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when SIEVE knows deletions) on influence, dominating-set, and recommendation tasks.  
- Experimental parameter issues: w fixed to 1 in experiments despite theoretical prescription; recommend plots of performance vs. w and m to show memory–robustness trade-off.  
- Missing empirical measures: running times and number of function evaluations are not reported; including them would clarify streaming practicality.  
- Comparison to concurrent work: acknowledges concurrent deletion-robust streaming [Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017] which trades better approximation for worse memory scaling; discussion of when each is preferable is needed.  
- Clarity and presentation: generally well organized and readable but needs fixes for threshold rule, space bound, τ expression, consistent use of log k/⌈log k⌉, define ε, and minor typos.  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial partitioned thresholding combining ideas from PRO and SIEVE-STREAMING, yielding O(m log^2 k + k log k) memory dependence.  
- Significance: important setting with potentially large practical impact due to improved memory scaling when k is large, despite modest worst-case constant.  
- Pros: addresses a timely problem, elegant one-pass algorithm, improved space scaling in k, solid empirical validation, clear relation to prior work.  
- Cons: small approximation factor (~0.149), presentation inconsistencies, potential per-element processing cost, limited exploration of w–m–memory trade-off in experiments.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound and original contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance provided the noted inconsistencies are fixed and brief runtime/parameter trade-off discussion is added.
