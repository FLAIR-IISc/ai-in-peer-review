- Problem: Studies robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming with adversarial removal of up to m items, aiming to produce a small summary S to extract a near-optimal k-set after deletions.

- Algorithms: Proposes STAR-T (partitioned thresholding streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (run GREEDY on S\E at query time).

- Structure: STAR-T uses partitions with exponentially increasing bucket capacities and exponentially decreasing thresholds; elements inserted into first non-full bucket exceeding its threshold.

- Main theoretical result: With w ≥ ⌈4 m log k / k⌉ buckets per partition, STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) items if τ=OPT; with multi-guessing τ memory becomes O((k + m log k) log^2 k).

- Approximation guarantee: STAR-T-GREEDY achieves a constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) f(OPT(k, V\E)).

- Proof approach: Analysis splits into two cases—many full buckets in a partition or the best non-full bucket in the last partition controls value—and balances lemmas to obtain the bound.

- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING, PRO, and robust/streaming literature; offers better logarithmic dependence on k at the cost of a smaller constant compared to concurrent work [Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017].

- Theoretical critique: Proof is sensible and uses standard submodular/greedy tools innovatively, but threshold specification and τ definition have clarity/typos needing reconciliation.

- Threshold inconsistency: Text uses τ/2^i while Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}; this must be reconciled and justified (min needed when 2^i > k).

- τ notation issues: Theorem 4.1’s τ formula appears misprinted and should be restated clearly with parentheses and constants; log bases and ceiling notation should be explicit.

- Constant concern: The 0.149 factor is conservative; authors should discuss whether it is an artifact of analysis or can be tightened.

- Experiments praised: Real-data tests (Twitter/Amazon dominating set, MovieLens) show STAR-T-GREEDY comparable to baselines and support reusable summaries across multiple deletions.

- Experimental gaps: Missing comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18], systematic variation of m/w/k, runtime/memory measurements, and tests against stronger/worst-case adversaries.

- Presentation issues: Inconsistent memory claims across Intro/Theorems/Conclusion, minor typos, and reference/formatting errors to fix.

- Originality: Partitioned-threshold streaming structure and robust analysis are novel extensions of prior partitioning ideas to the streaming setting.

- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem (deletions/right-to-be-forgotten, personalization) with a one-pass, memory-efficient, and practical algorithm.

- Pros summarized: One-pass, memory-efficient algorithm; constant-factor guarantee independent of n; simple reusable query; empirical competitiveness; novel extension of PRO to streaming.

- Cons summarized: Small approximation constant; limited empirical ablations and comparisons; presentation inconsistencies; limited runtime/memory sensitivity analysis and no lower bounds.

- Suggested improvements: Fix threshold/τ specs and justification; add experiments varying m/w/k, measure runtime/memory; compare or discuss [18]; test stronger adversaries; provide ablations (threshold schemes, query algorithms, bucket multiplicity).

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — important problem with novel, well-analyzed algorithm and practical promise, but needs clarity fixes and stronger empirical evaluation.
