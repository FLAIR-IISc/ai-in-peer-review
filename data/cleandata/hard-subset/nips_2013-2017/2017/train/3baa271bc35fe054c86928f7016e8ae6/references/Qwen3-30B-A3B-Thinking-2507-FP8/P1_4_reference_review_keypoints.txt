- Problem & approach: Proposes a two-stage one-pass streaming algorithm (STAR-T) plus query procedure (STAR-T-GREEDY) using partitioned thresholding with exponentially-sized buckets and decreasing thresholds for robust submodular maximization under up to m removals.  
- Theoretical claim: Achieves a constant-factor approximation (~0.149 for large k) with memory O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Builds on prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular methods to the single-pass robust streaming setting.  
- Strength — theory: Offers a novel partitioned thresholding method with rigorous analysis covering different partition-fill cases.  
- Strength — practicality: Two-stage framework is practical for high-rate, storage-limited settings and demonstrated on real tasks.  
- Strength — empirical validation: Experiments show competitive or better performance than SIEVE-STREAMING even when that baseline knows removals in advance.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-organized paper with clear pseudocode, thorough proofs in supplementary material, and reproducible experiments.  
- Strength — novelty: Meaningful innovation adapting exponentially decreasing thresholds to robust streaming.  
- Weakness — approximation: Approximation (~0.149) is much lower than SIEVE-STREAMING’s ~0.5, and the trade-off to robustness could be better quantified.  
- Weakness — parameter dependency: Requires knowledge of f(OPT(k, V\E)) for threshold τ, addressed by parallel instances but feels ad hoc.  
- Weakness — memory-claim clarity: Comparison with prior memory bounds (e.g., O(mk log k/ε)) needs clearer explanation across m and k regimes.  
- Weakness — theory vs practice: Theoretical bound on w is much larger than empirical w=1; the gap lacks sufficient explanation.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-written contribution with rigorous theory and convincing experiments; main concerns are lower approximation and parameter dependence but are acceptable trade-offs for robustness.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pro for acceptance: Novel method, rigorous guarantees, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Con for acceptance: Lower approximation ratio than non-robust methods and dependence on f(OPT(k, V\E)) requiring extra complexity.
