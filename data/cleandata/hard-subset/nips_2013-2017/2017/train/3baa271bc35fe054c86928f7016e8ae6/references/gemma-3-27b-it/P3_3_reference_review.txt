## Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the area of streaming submodular maximization, specifically addressing the crucial, yet often overlooked, aspect of robustness. The proposed algorithm, STAR-T, is well-motivated, technically sound, and offers a compelling theoretical guarantee. While the performance improvements over existing methods (like SIEVE-STREAMING) are sometimes modest in the experimental section, the ability to provide robustness without significant overhead is a strong selling point. The paper is generally well-written and organized, though some sections could benefit from further clarification. I recommend **acceptance**, with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. This means finding a representative subset of data that not only maximizes a submodular function but also remains effective even if a certain number of elements are removed.  The authors propose a two-stage algorithm: 1) a streaming phase (STAR-T) that builds a robust summary of the data using a partitioned thresholding scheme and 2) a query phase (STAR-T-GREEDY) that efficiently selects a new subset from the summary after elements have been removed. 

The work builds upon existing literature in several areas: submodular maximization (Nemhauser et al., 1978), streaming algorithms (Badanidiyuru et al., 2014), robust submodular maximization (Krause et al., 2008; Orlin et al., 2016; Bogunovic et al., 2017), and specifically, the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm (Badanidiyuru et al., 2014).  The key novelty lies in adapting the greedy thresholding approach of SIEVE-STREAMING to achieve robustness through a clever partitioning strategy and a decreasing threshold scheme, coupled with a rigorous analysis to prove constant-factor approximation guarantees.  The authors correctly position their work relative to PRO (Bogunovic et al., 2017), highlighting the advantages of their method in terms of memory usage, particularly with respect to *k*.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Robustness is a critical consideration for real-world applications of submodular maximization, and the paper explicitly addresses this often-neglected aspect.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, demonstrating a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the proposed algorithm. The proofs appear well-structured and the arguments are convincing (though a deeper dive into the supplementary material would be beneficial).
* **Novel Algorithm:** The partitioned thresholding approach with decreasing thresholds is a clever and well-designed solution for achieving robustness in the streaming setting.
* **Practical Considerations:** The authors acknowledge and address the challenge of not knowing the optimal objective value *a priori* and provide a solution for approximating it.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both the dominating set problem and personalized movie recommendation demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, showing competitive performance against existing methods like SIEVE-STREAMING.
* **Interactive Potential:** The ability to quickly re-compute solutions after removals highlights the algorithm's suitability for interactive applications.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Constant Factor:** While the theoretical guarantee of a constant-factor approximation is positive, the actual constant (0.149) is quite small and might limit the practical utility of the algorithm in some cases.  This could be discussed more frankly.
* **Experimental Results:** While the experimental results are encouraging, the improvements over SIEVE-STREAMING are not always dramatic.  More extensive experiments with varying parameters (m, k, dataset sizes) and a wider range of applications would strengthen the claims. A clearer discussion of the trade-offs between memory usage and performance is also needed.
* **Clarity in Algorithm Description:**  The description of the partitioning and thresholding process in Algorithm 1 could be more intuitive. Visual aids (e.g., diagrams illustrating the bucket structure) would be helpful.
* **Complexity of Proofs:** The theoretical analysis is quite complex. While the authors provide detailed proofs in the supplementary material, a more intuitive, high-level explanation of the key ideas behind the analysis would improve the accessibility of the paper.
* **Memory Parameter 'w':** The choice of *w* seems somewhat arbitrary.  A more systematic investigation of how *w* impacts performance and memory usage would be valuable.



**4. Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Addresses an important and under-explored problem (robustness in streaming submodular maximization).
* Provides a technically sound and novel algorithm with a theoretical guarantee.
* Demonstrates competitive performance in practical experiments.
* Contributes to the growing body of literature on streaming and robust machine learning.

**Against:**

* The constant factor in the approximation guarantee is relatively small.
* The experimental improvements over existing methods are not always substantial.
* Some aspects of the algorithm description and analysis could be made more clear.


**5. Recommendations for Improvement:**

* **Discussion of Constant Factor:**  Address the implications of the small constant factor in the approximation guarantee. Is it possible to improve it? How does it impact the practical applicability of the algorithm?
* **Extended Experiments:** Conduct more comprehensive experiments with varying parameters and datasets to further validate the performance of the algorithm.
* **Enhanced Clarity:** Improve the clarity of the algorithm description and the theoretical analysis. Consider adding visual aids and providing more intuitive explanations.
* **Memory Parameter Analysis:** Investigate the impact of the memory parameter *w* on performance and memory usage.  Provide guidance on how to choose an appropriate value for *w* based on the application requirements.
* **Comparison to PRO:** Provide a more direct comparison of memory usage and runtime to PRO, given that they address similar problems.



**Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, technically sound, and offers a promising solution for robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting. With minor revisions to address the points outlined above, this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.**
