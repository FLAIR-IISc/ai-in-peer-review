- Paper proposes a two-stage streaming algorithm (STAR-T) with partitioned thresholding and a greedy query stage (STAR-T-GREEDY) achieving a constant-factor approximation c = 0.149(1−1/log k).
- Key novelty: partitioned thresholding with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary in one pass.
- Strength: addresses a relevant problem of robust submodular maximization under removals with practical application domains (influence, recommendations).
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs and careful justification of bucket properties and marginal gains.
- Strength: experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show competitive performance versus SIEVE-STREAMING that knows removals in advance.
- Weakness: low theoretical approximation (≈0.149) is far below standard greedy (≈0.632) and other streaming algorithms (≈0.5−ε), limiting practical appeal.
- Weakness: algorithm requires knowing f(OPT(k, V\E)), impractical in practice; workaround using parallel instances increases space by log(1+ε)k, worsening practical complexity.
- Weakness: inadequate comparison with concurrent work (Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017) that achieves 1/2−ε with memory O(mk log k/ε), lacking clear trade-off analysis.
- Weakness: experimental baselines are unrealistic—compare only to SIEVE-STREAMING with prior removal knowledge instead of a removal-agnostic baseline.
- Technical concern: the 0.149 constant seems derived from a specific balancing choice and the paper doesn't explore whether different thresholds or partitions could improve it.
- Technical concern: analysis assumes normalized monotone submodular functions and does not address performance on non-normalized functions common in practice.
- Significance: conceptual contribution is interesting, but low approximation and impractical parameter requirements substantially limit impact and differentiation from concurrent work.
- Recommendation: clarify trade-offs with Mirzasoleiman et al., including memory vs. approximation comparisons.
- Recommendation: develop better practical estimation for the threshold τ or eliminate the need for it to reduce space overhead.
- Recommendation: strengthen experiments by comparing to more realistic baselines (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING without prior removal knowledge).
- Recommendation: investigate theoretical improvements to the approximation factor or provide intuition for the 0.149 constant.
- Conclusion: recommend rejection due to insufficient advancement over existing methods and practical limitations, though the approach could be valuable if the above issues are addressed.
