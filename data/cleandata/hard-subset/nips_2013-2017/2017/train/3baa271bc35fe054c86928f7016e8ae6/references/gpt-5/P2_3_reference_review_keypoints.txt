- Problem and approach: Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm that stores partitioned buckets with exponentially growing sizes and decreasing thresholds, and runs GREEDY on the summary after up to m deletions.

- Main theoretical claim: Achieves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 to OPT(k, V\E) with memory O((k + m log k) log k) when OPT is known and O((k + m log k) log^2 k) when OPT is approximated.

- Empirical claim: Performs comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING and a STAR-T-SIEVE variant on dominating set and recommendation tasks.

- Novelty: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING-style thresholding to the robust streaming setting and adapts PRO’s partitioned-bucket ideas to a one-pass algorithm with new robustness analysis.

- Relation to concurrent work: Contrasts with [18], which achieves 1/2−ε approximation with O(mk log k/ε) memory — this work trades approximation constant for memory that scales logarithmically in k.

- Theory quality: Proofs and case analyses are plausible and technically careful; memory–approximation tradeoff is clearly presented and standard multi-instance trick used for unknown OPT.

- Theoretical issues: Minor inconsistencies/typos — ambiguous threshold rule (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}), garbled expression for τ in Theorem 4.1, and inconsistent statements about log factors across abstract/theorems.

- Experimental scope: Tasks and baselines are appropriate and results show competitiveness, but experiments are limited in scope.

- Missing empirical comparisons: No experimental comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming baseline [18] and no discussion of expected empirical tradeoffs.

- Missing resource metrics: Memory and runtime usage are not reported.

- Sensitivity/ablation gaps: No sensitivity analysis or ablations for parameters (w, τ, number of OPT instances).

- Empirical regime mismatch: Experiments use w=1 despite guarantees requiring larger w, and sometimes remove more than k elements — evaluations often lie outside the proven regime.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written with helpful pseudocode and intuition, but needs to fix the noted inconsistencies and explicitly state dependencies (w on m,k; known vs unknown OPT).

- Significance: Addresses an important problem (robust streaming submodular maximization) with appealing memory scaling for large k, though the approximation constant is modest.

- Pros summary: Clear one-pass algorithm with small memory, new robustness analysis, competitive empirical results, and good related-work positioning.

- Cons summary: Reproducibility-hindering typos/inconsistencies, limited experiments lacking resource and ablation metrics, no empirical/theoretical comparison to [18], and relatively small approximation constant without practical guidance for tuning w.

- Suggested fixes: Reconcile threshold rule and τ expression, report memory/runtime and sensitivity within theoretical regimes, discuss/compare to [18], clarify parallel-instance overhead, and release code.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid theoretical and algorithmic contribution but needs clearer presentation and stronger experiments.
