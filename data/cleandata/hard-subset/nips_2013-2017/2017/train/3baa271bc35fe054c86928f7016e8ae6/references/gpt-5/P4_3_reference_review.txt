Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses robust submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting under a cardinality constraint. It proposes a two-stage procedure: (i) a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that stores a compact summary S of elements arranged into partitions of buckets with exponentially increasing capacities and exponentially decreasing thresholds; (ii) a query-time algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that, after the removal of any set E with |E| ≤ m, runs GREEDY on S \ E to return a size-k solution. The main theoretical result shows that, with a suitable memory parameter w = Θ((m log k)/k), STAR-T uses O((k + m log k) log k) space (or O((k + m log k) log k log1+ε k) when OPT is unknown and approximated via parallel instances) and guarantees a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉). Experiments on dominating set (Twitter, Amazon co-purchasing) and MovieLens-based recommendation show performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING [8] even when the latter is given the removed set in advance.

The work builds on: (a) classical greedy approx. for submodular maximization [7]; (b) streaming threshold methods (SIEVE-STREAMING) [8]; (c) robust non-streaming algorithms using partitioned structures (e.g., PRO) [11]; and (d) concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18], which achieves a 1/2 − ε approximation but with O(m k log k/ε) space. The contribution here trades a smaller approximation factor for polylogarithmic dependence on k in memory, which is novel among robust streaming methods.

Strengths
- Novel algorithmic structure: Partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds adapt thresholding to robustness and enable a compact robust summary. This extends SIEVE-STREAMING-style ideas to the deletion-robust setting in a principled way.
- Theoretical guarantees: One-pass streaming, space O((k + m log k) log k) (or O((k + m log k) log k log1+ε k) without knowing OPT), and a constant-factor approximation at query time after adversarial deletion of up to m elements. The memory dependence on k is logarithmic (vs. linear in [18]).
- Clear tradeoff vs. prior work: Compared to [18], substantially lower space with worse approximation constant; compared to non-streaming robust methods [10,11], single-pass streaming with robustness.
- Practical viability: Empirical results on large graphs and a recommendation task demonstrate that a single precomputed robust summary can answer multiple deletion scenarios, with performance close to SIEVE-STREAMING (and GREEDY in the small MovieLens setup).

Weaknesses and suggestions for improvement
- Approximation constant: The worst-case constant 0.149 is modest, especially relative to 1/2 − ε in [18]. While the space-approximation tradeoff is a core contribution, a discussion quantifying regimes where the smaller space is decisive (e.g., m, k, function evaluation cost) would strengthen the significance.
- Assumption on τ and clarity of its setting: Theorem 4.1 states a formula for τ in terms of f(OPT(k, V \ E)), but the expression in the text appears malformed/ambiguous. Please present τ explicitly and consistently (with parentheses) and summarize how the “parallel thresholds” variant chooses the grid and bounds the extra space and approximation loss.
- Threshold inconsistency: The prose uses τ/2^i per partition, while Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}. The last partition analysis (τ/k) aligns with the latter; please unify the description early to avoid confusion.
- Memory bound consistency: The introduction and conclusion state O((m log k + k) log^2 k), while Theorem 4.1 yields O((k + m log k) log k) for known OPT, and Theorem 4.5 gives O((k + m log k) log k log1+ε k) for unknown OPT. Please reconcile these statements, explain the extra log factor, and specify ε’s impact.
- Update/compute complexity: STAR-T checks partitions and buckets until it places or discards an item. The worst-case per-element time can be O(∑i w⌈k/2^i⌉) = O(w k log k). Please state and discuss update time, function evaluation counts, and query-time costs (GREEDY on S \ E), and include runtime/memory plots in experiments.
- Experimental fairness and breadth:
  - Some figures sample E from S, which is adversarial to the proposed method and favorable to baselines accessing V \ E. While conservative, also include settings where E is sampled from V (without conditioning on S) to present a balanced view.
  - Report actual space used (|S|) and w in all experiments, and compare memory usage to SIEVE-STREAMING and to the parallel variant when OPT is unknown.
  - Include sensitivity to m and w, and to different k values, and a case where m is close to the theoretical bound permitted by w.
  - If feasible, compare to the concurrent deletion-robust streaming approach [18] (even if approximate or on smaller instances), or at least provide a more quantitative discussion of the space-approximation tradeoff.
- Notational/typo issues: Multiple minor inconsistencies (e.g., dlog ke vs log k; “⌈4 dlog k e m / k⌉”; missing parentheses; line breaks within equations). A careful pass will improve readability.

Quality
- The theoretical development appears sound at a high level, with lemmas covering both “many full buckets” and “few full buckets” cases and a balancing argument. However, clarity issues around τ and thresholds should be corrected. The memory and approximation statements are plausible and well-motivated. Experiments are reasonable but could better document resource usage and sensitivity.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well organized, but has notation/typo inconsistencies and ambiguous parameter definitions. Clarifying τ, thresholds, memory bounds, and computational costs would materially improve clarity.

Originality
- The partitioned-threshold streaming design for deletion-robustness is, to my knowledge, novel relative to [8,10,11,18], and the space-approximation tradeoff is new.

Significance
- Robust streaming submodular maximization is relevant to large-scale summarization. The method’s low memory footprint and single-pass property are valuable. The constant factor is modest; nonetheless, the structural ideas and analysis are likely to influence follow-up work.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel structural approach achieving robust guarantees with polylogarithmic dependence on k in memory.
- Solid theoretical results with single-pass streaming and a clear tradeoff vs. prior work.
- Practical demonstrations across two application domains, showcasing interactive reuse of a single summary.

Arguments against acceptance
- Low approximation constant relative to concurrent alternatives; limited empirical analysis of memory/runtime tradeoffs and sensitivity.
- Clarity issues (τ definition, thresholds, memory bounds) and minor inconsistencies.
- No direct empirical comparison to [18].

Questions for authors
- Please provide a precise, unambiguous formula and rationale for τ, and reconcile the thresholding rule (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i, k}) across the paper.
- What is the worst-case per-element update time and number of oracle calls? Can thresholds be implemented to reduce checks?
- How large is |S| in practice vs. the bound, for typical m, k, and w = 1? How sensitive is performance to w?

Overall assessment
The paper presents a technically interesting and practically relevant advance with a distinctive space-approximation tradeoff. With improved clarity and expanded empirical characterization, it would make a strong contribution. I lean toward acceptance.
