- Summary: Proposes STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY, a two-stage streaming algorithm using partitioned thresholding to build a robust summary and deliver solutions after up to m removals; proves a 0.149(1−1/log k) approximation and memory O((k + m log k) log k), improving over concurrent O(mk log k).

- Strength 1 (Novelty): Introduces an elegant partitioned thresholding structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds to achieve robustness with sublinear memory.

- Strength 2 (Theory): Provides careful analysis and a non-trivial constant-factor (0.149) approximation for the robust streaming setting.

- Strength 3 (Experiments): Empirical results on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks show competitive performance, nearly matching SIEVE-STREAMING even though it knows removals in advance.

- Strength 4 (Presentation): Well-written, organized paper with clear pseudocode and reproducible details.

- Weakness 1 (Approximation): The 0.149 factor is much lower than non-robust greedy (≈0.63); the paper should better discuss whether this gap is inherent or improvable.

- Weakness 2 (Memory discrepancy): Title claims O((k + m log k) log² k) but Theorem 4.1 states O((k + m log k) log k); this inconsistency needs clarification.

- Weakness 3 (Theory vs practice): Theoretical analysis requires w ≥ ⌈4dlogkem/k⌉, yet experiments use w=1 with strong results; the theory/practice gap is unexplained.

- Weakness 4 (Baselines): Lacks comparison to a robust variant of SIEVE-STREAMING, which would be a more direct robust-streaming baseline.

- Originality/Significance: Novel combination of robustness and streaming with a significant methodological contribution (partitioned thresholding) and practical relevance for removal-prone applications.

- Recommendation: Strong accept to NIPS with minor revisions to clarify the memory bound discrepancy and to explain the practical use of w=1 despite theoretical requirements.

- Final assessment: Significant advance in robust streaming submodular optimization—sound theory, convincing experiments, and justified trade-offs despite lower approximation factor.
