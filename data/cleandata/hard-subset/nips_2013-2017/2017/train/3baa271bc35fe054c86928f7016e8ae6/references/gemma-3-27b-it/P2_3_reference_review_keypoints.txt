- Addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings and proposes STAR-T: a two-stage streaming summary plus greedy query response.
- Introduces a partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build robust summaries.
- Provides a theoretical constant-factor approximation guarantee (approx. 0.149) for the algorithm.
- Empirical results on synthetic and movie-recommendation tasks show competitive or superior performance to SIEVE-STREAMING, even when that baseline knows removals.
- Highlights the benefit of a single precomputed robust summary usable for multiple removal scenarios.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear algorithm descriptions and consistent notation.

- The proved constant-factor approximation is small (≈0.149) and depends on log k, raising concerns about practical impact.
- Memory parameter w is sensitive; theoretical lower bound exists but experiments suggest w=1 suffices, needing more sensitivity analysis.
- Limited empirical comparisons beyond SIEVE-STREAMING weaken validation against other streaming submodular algorithms.
- Space complexity includes a log k factor (O((k + m log k) log k)), which may be significant for large k and isn’t further mitigated.
- Theory assumes normalized monotone submodular functions; implications of relaxing this assumption aren’t discussed.

- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and theoretically sound contribution with encouraging empirical results.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel approach, solid theory, strong empirical performance, and good fit for the venue.
- Arguments against acceptance: small approximation constant, parameter sensitivity concerns, and limited baselines.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestion to address constant factor, parameter sensitivity, and broader baseline comparisons in revision.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
