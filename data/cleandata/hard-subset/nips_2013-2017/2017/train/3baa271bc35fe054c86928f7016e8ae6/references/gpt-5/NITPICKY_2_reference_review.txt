Summary
The paper studies robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting, where one must (i) build, in a single pass and with limited memory, a summary S robust to the removal of up to m elements, and (ii) at query time, after an arbitrary removal E (|E| ≤ m), extract a size-k solution from S\E with a provable approximation. The proposed algorithm STAR-T arranges stored elements into exponentially sized partitions with exponentially decreasing marginal-gain thresholds; elements are placed into the first non-full bucket whose threshold they meet. At query time, STAR-T-GREEDY runs GREEDY on S\E. The main theorem proves that with w ≳ (m log k)/k, the memory is O((k + m log k) log k) and the final solution achieves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉). For unknown OPT, parallel instances yield an extra log factor. Experiments on dominating-set and recommendation show competitive performance vs. SIEVE-STREAMING (sometimes allowing the baseline to know deletions in advance) and GREEDY on smaller data.

Strengths
- Tackle an important and timely problem: deletion robustness in streaming submodular maximization with post-hoc querying, relevant to “right to be forgotten” and robustness needs.
- Novel algorithmic structure combining partitioning with decreasing thresholds; analysis is nontrivial and leverages the robust structure.
- One-pass streaming with memory that depends only logarithmically on k (and linearly on m up to logs), contrasting with concurrent deletion-robust streaming that uses O(mk) memory.
- Clean query-time mechanism (GREEDY on S\E), enabling multiple queries E with a single precomputed summary.
- Empirical evaluation on two application domains; demonstrates practical robustness beyond the proven regime and closeness to strong baselines.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical clarity and consistency:
- There are multiple inconsistencies between text, algorithms, theorem statements, and the conclusion that impede verification:
  - Threshold rule: In the description, partition i uses threshold τ/2^i; Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}; Lemma 4.4 relies on the latter (giving τ/k in the last partition). Please unify and explain the choice; otherwise proofs are hard to follow.
  - Memory bounds: Theorem 4.1 yields |S| = O((k + m log k) log k), while the abstract and conclusion claim O((k + m log k) log^2 k). Section 4.5 attributes an extra log factor to parallel guesses (O(log_{1+ε} k)), which, for constant ε, is Θ(log k). The paper must consistently separate the “known OPT” and “unknown OPT” regimes and state the bounds accordingly in all places.
  - Parameter τ: Theorem 4.1 defines τ with a malformed expression (“τ = 1 + (1−e−1)(1−e−1/3)(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) f(OPT(…))”), which appears syntactically incorrect. Earlier, τ is described as an α-approximation to f(OPT). Precise scaling of τ with OPT and how it balances Lemmas 4.2–4.4 needs to be clearly and correctly written.
  - Partitions: Sometimes there are ⌈log k⌉+1 partitions; Algorithm loops i ← 0 to ⌈log k⌉ (inclusive). Please reconcile “+1” vs inclusive bounds.
  - The approximation factor c depends on (1 − 1/⌈log k⌉), which is undefined at k = 1 and negative at k = 2 if misinterpreted; the domain assumptions (e.g., k ≥ 2) should be explicitly stated.

Proof sketch adequacy:
- Several critical steps are elided to the supplement and some arguments are informal in the main text (e.g., “on average, |B∩E| is relatively small”). Given the novelty of the analysis, more precise main-text statements (even if brief) would help assess correctness without the supplement.
- The balancing step producing Equation (4) to (5) is terse; provide the explicit τ choice and constraints (e.g., γ < 1) and how they are guaranteed by w.

Algorithmic efficiency:
- Update-time complexity per streamed element is not analyzed. STAR-T checks up to ∑_i w·(k/2^i) = O(wk) buckets; with w ≈ (m log k)/k, this is O(m log k) per element in the worst case, which could be large for big m. A discussion (or heuristics) to reduce per-element processing (e.g., early-stopping, hashed routing, lazy thresholds) is needed for a streaming context.
- Oracle evaluation costs are not discussed; for facility-location-like objectives, marginal gains can be expensive unless carefully maintained.

Experimental rigor:
- The graph experiments choose E as a subset of S, which is adversarial to STAR-T (as acknowledged), but atypical for a deletion-robust guarantee that assumes E ⊆ V and |E| ≤ m. Please include experiments where E ⊆ V is chosen independent of S to reflect the theoretical model.
- Several experiments remove more than k elements (e.g., 2k) while using w = 1, violating w ≥ ⌈4⌈log k⌉ m/k⌉. While it’s useful to show robustness beyond theory, please also include runs within the provable regime (vary m, set w accordingly) to validate the trade-off between w and robustness.
- Baselines: It would be informative to compare against the concurrent deletion-robust streaming algorithm [18] in at least one setting, to empirically demonstrate the memory–approximation trade-off.
- Report actual memory sizes (|S|) and per-element processing time for all methods.

Positioning and related work:
- The comparison to [18] emphasizes memory, but the approximation factor here (≈0.149) is substantially lower than 1/2−ε; a more thorough discussion of practical impact of the constant and when one would choose STAR-T over [18] would strengthen significance claims.
- Some references and formatting are inconsistent or incomplete.

Presentation and formatting issues:
- Numerous typographical issues hinder readability: “st Conference on NIPS …” in the header; inconsistent capitalization (STreAming); spacing around equations; “dlog ke” notational oddities; missing or malformed parentheses; min{2i,k} vs min{2^i,k}; figures and captions not included; references have inconsistent styles and stray commas.
- Algorithm 1 line 5 uses τ/min{2^i, k} but the text repeatedly says τ/2^i; please standardize.
- The statement of Theorem 4.1 (τ expression) needs correction.

Pros
- Novel and interesting algorithmic idea with a careful robust structure.
- One-pass streaming with memory sublinear in n and only logarithmic in k (for known OPT).
- Query-time flexibility to answer multiple E without revisiting the stream.
- Empirical results suggest strong practical performance.

Cons
- Multiple inconsistencies and typos in core definitions and theorems.
- Missing discussion of update-time complexity; unclear practicality for large m.
- Experiments do not convincingly validate the claimed theoretical regime; no comparison to the concurrent 1/2−ε method.
- Approximation constant is quite small; significance vs prior work is under-discussed.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The core idea is promising and potentially impactful, but the paper, as written, has too many inconsistencies in definitions, bounds, and notation, limiting verifiability. Strengthening the exposition (fixing τ and threshold inconsistencies, unifying memory bounds, clarifying proofs), adding update-time analysis, and bolstering experiments (including within-regime tests and comparison to [18]) would substantially improve the submission.
