- Paper studies streaming deletion-robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint with a two-stage approach: STAR-T (streaming summary) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query-time greedy).
- STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements (or an extra log factor when τ is unknown) and STAR-T-GREEDY achieves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 as k grows.
- Main novelty: combines PRO-style exponentially sized partitions with streaming thresholding to get logarithmic-in-k memory dependence at the cost of a smaller approximation than concurrent work.
- Experiments on dominating set and recommender tasks show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or slightly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows deletions) and beats random baselines, even for adversarial or larger-than-theoretical deletions.
- Problem is practically important (right-to-be-forgotten, node failures, user-specific filtering) and challenging in streaming settings.
- Algorithmic idea of partitioned thresholding with decreasing thresholds and growing bucket sizes is novel, intuitive, and implementable.
- Theoretical contribution: single-pass algorithm with explicit memory bound and constant-factor guarantee after arbitrary deletions up to m.
- Two-stage design supports interactive re-querying without rescanning the stream, improving practical relevance.
- Presentation is generally clear with well-motivated two-case analysis and balancing lemmas.
- Approximation factor (~0.149) is modest; discussion on whether better constants are possible with same memory would help.
- Worst-case per-element running time can be high (up to O(k log k) when m ≈ k); the paper should discuss per-element time, marginal-oracle calls, and practical pruning/amortization.
- Parameter τ selection is required and the parallel guessing increases memory; the schedule, overheads, and practical guidance for τ and ε are insufficiently detailed.
- Notational and algorithmic clarity issues: inconsistent use of 2^i vs 2i, mismatch in threshold expressions (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}), and a mangled τ formula in Theorem 4.1.
- Space bound statements are inconsistent between O((k + m log k) log k) and O((k + m log k) log^2 k); separate known-τ and unknown-τ regimes should be stated clearly.
- Empirical evaluation lacks reporting of actual summary sizes |S| and wall-clock times; fair memory caps and throughput comparisons are needed for context.
- Missing or insufficient baselines: include or discuss concurrent deletion-robust streaming methods and provide sensitivity analyses over w and m (and performance vs. number of deletions).
- Experiments sometimes use settings (e.g., removals > k or m with w=1) beyond theory; quantifying performance degradation curves would be valuable.
- Scope limited to cardinality constraints; a brief discussion on extending to matroid or knapsack constraints would broaden impact.
- Proofs are plausible and the analysis is coherent, but some details are deferred and notational fixes are needed for rigor.
- Questions for authors requested: precise per-element time and throughput, sensitivity to τ-guessing and w, possibilities to tighten the approximation constant or matching lower bounds, and extensions to matroid constraints.
- Minor editorial fixes: use 2^i consistently, fix τ in Theorem 4.1, and correct stray capitalization in Algorithm 1.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid, novel contribution with good experiments but needs clarity fixes and stronger runtime/memory/sensitivity evaluations.
