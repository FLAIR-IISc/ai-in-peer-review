- Problem and approach: Proposes STAR-T (one-pass streaming) + STAR-T-GREEDY (query-time GREEDY) for deletion-robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint.
- Main novelty: Partitioned, exponentially decreasing thresholding (τ/2^i across partitions) with exponentially growing bucket sizes to build a compact deletion-robust summary.
- Theoretical guarantee: Constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 as k grows, with rigorous analysis covering complementary cases.
- Space complexity: O((k + m log k) log k) when OPT is known; O((k + m log k) log^2 k) with standard parallel-guessing when OPT is unknown.
- Trade-off vs prior work: Trades a smaller approximation constant for much better dependence on k (logarithmic vs linear), compared to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18] (1/2−ε but space O(mk log k/ε)).
- Relation to prior methods: Blends SIEVE-STREAMING-style thresholding with PRO-style partition/bucket structure; differentiates clearly from non-robust and non-streaming robust methods.
- Theory quality: Main theorem and lemmas are plausible and technically careful; parallel-guessing extension is standard and sound.
- Minor theoretical/clarity issues: Inconsistency in threshold notation (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}) and need to spell out known-OPT vs unknown-OPT big-O distinctions earlier.
- Empirical strengths: Experiments on dominating-set and recommendation tasks show competitive performance, often close to SIEVE-STREAMING that knows deletions in advance.
- Empirical weaknesses: No direct comparison to concurrent method [18]; limited exploration of robustness m and memory parameter w; no runtime/memory reporting; parallel-guessing variant not evaluated.
- Reproducibility: Uses standard datasets/objectives but needs more experimental details or code to reproduce deletion protocols and exact settings.
- Presentation issues: Generally well written with helpful proof sketch, but contains minor typos/formatting glitches that should be fixed.
- Suggestions: Clarify algorithm thresholds and big-O claims, report stream/query runtime and memory, perform sensitivity sweeps over m/k and w, and compare (theoretically or empirically) to [18]; describe parallel-guessing used in experiments.
- Recommendation: Lean accept — technically solid, novel streaming-robust approach with meaningful space improvements, but empirical evaluation and minor clarifications should be strengthened.
