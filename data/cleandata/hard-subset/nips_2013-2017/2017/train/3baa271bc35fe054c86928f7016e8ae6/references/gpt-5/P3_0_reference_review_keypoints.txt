- Paper addresses robust submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting, building a compact summary S that supports near-optimal size-k solutions after up to m deletions.  
- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T (streaming partitioned buckets with exponential sizes and thresholds) and query-time GREEDY recovery on S \ E.  
- Main theoretical claim: constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) with space O((k + m log k) log k) for known τ (extra log factor when τ is guessed), trading approximation for much lower memory than [18].  
- Analysis is novel and structurally sound, combining thresholded partitions, GREEDY guarantees, and occupancy arguments (Lemmas 4.2–4.4).  
- Inconsistent space bounds across the paper: abstract/intro/conclusion state O((k + m log k) log^2 k), Theorem 4.1 states O((k + m log k) log k), and Theorem 4.5 adds another log — reconcile per-setting bounds (known vs. guessed τ).  
- τ specification in Theorem 4.1 is typeset/malformed and unclear (is τ proportional to OPT or its reciprocal?) — needs correction and clarity.  
- Approximation constant degenerates for small k (c vacuous for k ≤ 2); state valid k range and any alternative small-k bounds.  
- Missing time/oracle complexity: STAR-T may check O(w k log k) buckets per element; provide per-element and total oracle query counts and practical speedups.  
- Lemma 4.3 uses 1 − e^{-1/3} (nonstandard vs. 1 − 1/e); provide intuition for the 1/3 factor and its origin.  
- Experiments use relevant tasks and a strong baseline (SIEVE-STREAMING given deletions), showing practical competitiveness.  
- Missing empirical baselines: no comparisons to deletion-robust streaming [18] or centralized robust methods (e.g., PRO [11]); include at least [18] on small instances.  
- Resource reporting is incomplete: report memory footprint |S|, wall-clock throughput, sensitivity to w and m, and ablations for w values.  
- Evaluation fairness concern: graph experiments sample E from S (adversarial for STAR-T); also report results for E sampled from V and include uncertainty measures (standard errors).  
- Unknown-τ experiment unclear: clarify whether experiments used single τ or multi-instance guessing and report extra memory cost and impact.  
- Notation/consistency issues: threshold text says τ/2^i while Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i,k}; unify presentation and explain last partition choice.  
- Additional notation fixes: consistent log bases and ceiling notation, avoid overloading “e” (Euler’s constant vs. element).  
- Spell out dependence of w on m and k and propagate into final space bounds.  
- Suggest adding a succinct theorem table summarizing approximation vs. memory vs. passes for STAR-T (known/unknown τ), SIEVE-STREAMING, PRO [11], and [18].  
- Originality: introduces partitioned, thresholded streaming summary with exponentially varying capacities and thresholds; analysis and streaming thresholding rule are novel relative to PRO.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to robust streaming summarization with attractive memory-approximation trade-offs and reusable summaries for multiple deletion queries.  
- Pros: single-pass robust algorithm, compact memory, reusable two-stage design, first constant-factor guarantee with O((k + m log k) log k) space (known τ), empirical competitiveness.  
- Cons: low approximation constant (~0.149) and degeneracy for small k, clarity issues (τ, space bounds, thresholds), missing runtime/oracle complexity and sensitivity analyses, no empirical comparison to [18], limited resource reporting.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on fixing inconsistencies, adding resource/sensitivity reporting, and, if possible, including comparison to [18] on a small instance.
