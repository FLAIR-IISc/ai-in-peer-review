- Proposal: STAR-T — a two-stage streaming algorithm that builds a robust summary in one pass and runs greedy on that summary after removals.  
- Core idea: partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds enabling a constant-factor approximation.  
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation with O((k + m log k) log k) memory dependence (proofs in supplementary material).  
- Empirical results: competitive on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, often handling more removals than theory predicts.  
- Related work: correctly positioned against streaming and robust submodular literature; contrasts with concurrent work that has linear dependence on k.  
- Strength — Novelty: clever partitioned thresholding balances robustness and streaming efficiency.  
- Strength — Theory: rigorous constant-factor approximation analysis.  
- Strength — Practicality: two-stage design useful for frequent updates and limited dataset access.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: thorough comparisons (including SIEVE-STREAMING) showing strong performance.  
- Strength — Presentation: generally clear writing and helpful figures.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: performance depends on w and τ; τ approximation and guidance feel hand-wavy.  
- Weakness — Space constants: hidden constant factors in O((k + m log k) log k) may be large; need more precise space/memory analysis.  
- Weakness — Experimental assumptions: baselines have varying levels of knowledge (e.g., removals known), which should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Weakness — Scalability: limited discussion/experiments for truly massive datasets and potential partitioning bottlenecks.  
- Weakness — Clarity: dense notation and unclear symbols (e.g., α1, α2 in Theorem 4.1) reduce readability.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and broadly impactful work.  
- Against acceptance: issues remain on parameter sensitivity, possible large space constants, scalability, and need for more intuition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing parameter sensitivity, space complexity analysis, scalability discussion, and clearer high-level exposition.
