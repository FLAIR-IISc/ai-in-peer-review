

# Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

This paper addresses a significant challenge in large-scale machine learning: efficiently computing robust data summaries for submodular maximization problems in a streaming setting. The authors propose a two-stage approach (STAR-T for the streaming phase and STAR-T-GREEDY for the query phase) that provides theoretical guarantees for robustness against up to m element removals while maintaining a small memory footprint.

## Summary and Relation to Prior Work

The paper builds upon the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm [8] for submodular maximization, extending it to handle robustness requirements. Unlike previous robust approaches [9, 10, 11] that require multiple passes or centralized computation, this work provides a one-pass streaming algorithm that constructs a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log k). The key innovation is their partitioned thresholding approach, where the summary is structured into partitions with exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds. This differs from prior robust approaches like PRO [11] that fill buckets greedily one after another.

## Quality

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with a clear proof sketch and multiple supporting lemmas that lead to the main result (Theorem 4.1) showing a 0.149 approximation guarantee. The authors properly address the practical challenge of not knowing f(OPT(k, V\E)) in advance by introducing a parallel variant (Theorem 4.5) that provides a (1+ε)-approximation. The experimental validation on two applications (dominating set and movie recommendation) demonstrates that their method matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING, even though SIEVE-STREAMING knows in advance which elements will be removed. The experiments are well-designed, with appropriate baselines (RANDOM, STAR-T-SIEVE) and meaningful metrics.

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The problem statement, algorithm descriptions (with pseudocode), theoretical results, and experimental validation follow a logical flow. The proof sketch provides sufficient intuition without overwhelming detail, and the experimental section clearly explains the datasets, evaluation metrics, and key findings. The authors effectively use figures to illustrate their approach and results.

## Originality

This work makes a meaningful contribution to the literature on submodular optimization. The partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds is novel and directly addresses the robustness requirement in a streaming setting. The authors clearly distinguish their work from related contributions (PRO [11], SIEVE-STREAMING [8], and the concurrent work [18]), demonstrating how their approach improves upon previous methods in terms of memory efficiency (logarithmic dependence on k versus linear in [18]) and practical performance. The two-stage approach is particularly elegant and practical for real-world applications.

## Significance

The problem addressed is highly significant for large-scale machine learning applications where data cannot fit in memory and robustness is required. The paper demonstrates practical relevance through two compelling applications: dominating set in social networks and personalized movie recommendations. The fact that their method matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (which has knowledge of future removals) in practice is particularly impressive. The theoretical guarantees (constant-factor approximation) provide a solid foundation for the practical utility of their approach.

## Weaknesses

The main limitation is the lower approximation ratio (0.149) compared to non-robust streaming algorithms (which achieve 0.5-ε). However, this trade-off is expected given the added robustness constraint. The authors briefly mention the concurrent work [18] but could have provided a more thorough comparison of their memory efficiency (O((k + m log k) log k) vs. O(mk log k/ε)). The experimental section could have included more ablation studies to better understand the impact of the parameter w.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of submodular optimization in streaming settings. It successfully addresses an important practical challenge (robustness in streaming settings) with a theoretically sound and practically effective solution. The quality of the theoretical analysis, clarity of presentation, and significance of the problem make this a strong candidate for NIPS.

The paper meets all the reviewing criteria well:
- **Quality**: Strong theoretical guarantees, well-supported experimental results
- **Clarity**: Excellent organization and writing
- **Originality**: Novel partitioned thresholding approach with clear distinction from prior work
- **Significance**: Addresses an important practical problem with demonstrable improvements

The only minor weakness is the limited comparison with the concurrent work [18], but this does not detract from the paper's overall strength. The paper represents a significant advance in streaming robust submodular maximization and is well-suited for publication at NIPS.

**Recommendation: Accept**
