- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm using partitioned buckets with exponential sizes and decreasing thresholds to robustly maximize submodular functions under removals.  
- Uses a query-stage greedy algorithm on the retained elements after a removal set is specified.  
- Provides constant-factor approximation guarantees and empirical validation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Builds on classical submodular and streaming work and extends SIEVE-STREAMING/PRO ideas to incorporate robustness with logarithmic memory dependence.  
- Novel partitioned thresholding scheme balances robustness and efficiency through bucket sizing and threshold scheduling.  
- Theoretical analysis yields a constant-factor approximation (0.149), with guarantees decreasing as k grows.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance with state-of-the-art (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING), even when competitors know removals.  
- Addresses unknown OPT via parallel-instance technique to make the approach practical.  
- Clear and mostly well-organized presentation with consistent notation and clearly described algorithms.  
- Constant approximation factor is relatively small and may limit practical performance versus algorithms with better constants.  
- Performance appears sensitive to parameters (w, τ), and guidance/tuning analysis is limited.  
- Proofs are dense and some are deferred to supplementary material, reducing readability.  
- Limited empirical comparison to other robust streaming algorithms; broader baselines would strengthen evaluation.  
- Practical implications (e.g., cost of maintaining summaries vs. re-optimization) need deeper discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically promising, and addresses unknown OPT.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small constant factor, complex proofs, and insufficient comparisons to related work.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept—valuable contribution despite limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clarifying proofs, expanding experiments, and discussing practical implications and parameter tuning.
