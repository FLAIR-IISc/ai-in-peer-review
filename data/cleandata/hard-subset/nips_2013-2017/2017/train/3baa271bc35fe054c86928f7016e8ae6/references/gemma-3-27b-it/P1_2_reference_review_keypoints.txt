- Summary: Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm using partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds to produce robust submodular summaries resilient to element removals.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust streaming setting, situates against non-streaming robust methods and concurrent work, and highlights logarithmic memory dependence on k versus linear in a concurrent paper.  
- Strength — Important problem: Tackles practical and challenging robust submodular maximization in streaming.  
- Strength — Novel approach: Partitioned thresholding balances exploration and exploitation and promotes robustness.  
- Strength — Theoretical guarantees: Provides constant-factor approximation and memory complexity bounds.  
- Strength — Experiments: Competitive empirical performance on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, often effective even with w=1.  
- Strength — Interactive component: Precomputed summaries can be reused for different removal scenarios enabling quick adaptation.  
- Strength — Presentation: Clear writing and organized algorithm/theory exposition.  
- Weakness — Small approximation constant: The 0.149 factor (and its log k dependence) is relatively weak for large k.  
- Weakness — Parameter tuning: Requires several parameters (k, m, τ, w); τ depends on unknown f(OPT) and needs parallel instances to approximate.  
- Weakness — Proof complexity: Key arguments are complex and relegated to the supplement; main text could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Empirical validation is restricted to two datasets and could be broadened.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated contribution with rigorous analysis and convincing results, meriting acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, introduces a novel algorithm, gives strong theory, shows competitive experiments, and offers practical interactivity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Small constant approximation, challenging parameter tuning, and limited experimental scope.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
