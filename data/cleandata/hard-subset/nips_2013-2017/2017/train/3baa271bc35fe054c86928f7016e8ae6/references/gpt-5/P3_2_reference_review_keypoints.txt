- Tackles single-pass streaming robust submodular maximization with up to m adversarial deletions, using a compact summary S and GREEDY at query time.  
- Proposes STAR-T: partitioned buckets with exponentially increasing capacities and decreasing thresholds; each element placed in first qualifying bucket.  
- Guarantees a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 as k grows, space O((k + m log k) log k) with known OPT and O((k + m log k) log^2 k) when guessing OPT.  
- Empirically competitive with SIEVE-STREAMING on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, even when SIEVE is given deletions.  
- Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING thresholding and PRO’s non-uniform partitions to obtain streaming robustness.  
- Trades a weaker approximation (~0.149) for much better space dependence on k versus prior work (logarithmic vs linear factors).  
- Extends parallel OPT-guessing to the robust streaming setting with quantified complexity overhead.  
- Strength: simple, single-pass algorithm with an elegant partitioned-threshold design and sound two-case analysis.  
- Strength: space-bound derivation is transparent for known-OPT, and query guarantee compares to the correct benchmark OPT(k, V \ E).  
- Weakness: modest approximation constant; unclear if this is due to proof looseness or inherent to the approach and whether it can be tightened.  
- Weakness: inconsistency between text and Algorithm 1 on threshold definition (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}); needs reconciliation and explanation.  
- Weakness: malformed τ expression in Theorem 4.1 — likely a typesetting/notation error that must be fixed.  
- Weakness: inconsistent space-bound statements across the paper; must state both known-OPT and unknown-OPT bounds clearly and consistently.  
- Weakness: requires prior knowledge of m to set width w; needs guidance or adaptive schemes for unknown/varying m and the space trade-off for multi-w approaches.  
- Empirical weaknesses: missing comparison to concurrent method [18], no runtime/throughput or sensitivity-to-w studies, and deletion sampling in one experiment conditions E on S which is nonstandard.  
- Minor: several proofs deferred and the main text would benefit from more intuition for key lemmas (e.g., balancing step).  
- Significance: addresses a practical precompute-and-answer model (privacy/deletions) and offers a compelling memory-approximation tradeoff for large k moderate m.  
- Clarity requests: fix threshold and τ definitions, harmonize space statements, explicitly state final space for known/unknown OPT, and add an “algorithmic intuition” subsection.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, simple structure with nontrivial robust streaming guarantee and clear memory advantage; empirical support on real data.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak approximation constant, formal inconsistencies/typos, and limited empirical comparisons and runtime analysis.  
- Key questions for authors: can the constant be improved or is it inherent; how to handle unknown/varying m at stream time; can you provide objective vs memory and runtime comparisons (including [18]); please clarify threshold rule and τ.  
- Overall: solid, thoughtful contribution with promise; leans toward acceptance contingent on fixing clarity issues and strengthening empirical comparisons.
