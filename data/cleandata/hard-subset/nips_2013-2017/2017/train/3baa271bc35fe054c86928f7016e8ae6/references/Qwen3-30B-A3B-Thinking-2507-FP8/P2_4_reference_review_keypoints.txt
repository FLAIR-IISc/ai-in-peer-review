- Tackles robust submodular maximization in streaming settings with a two-stage method (STAR-T for streaming, STAR-T-GREEDY for queries).
- One-pass algorithm builds a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log k) using a partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to handle robustness and differs from prior robust methods (which are multi-pass or centralized) like PRO.
- Rigorous theoretical analysis yielding Theorem 4.1 with a 0.149 approximation guarantee.
- Provides a parallel variant (Theorem 4.5) to handle unknown f(OPT) with a (1+ε)-approximation strategy.
- Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even though SIEVE-STREAMING knows future removals.
- Experimental setup and baselines (RANDOM, STAR-T-SIEVE) are appropriate and evaluation metrics are meaningful.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized; pseudocode, proof sketch, and figures aid understanding.
- Novel contribution: partitioned thresholding and two-stage approach yielding better memory efficiency (logarithmic in k) and practical performance compared to prior/concurrent work.
- High practical significance for large-scale ML where streaming and robustness are critical.
- Main weaknesses: lower approximation ratio (0.149) versus non-robust streaming algorithms and limited comparison/ablation (notably parameter w and more detailed comparison to concurrent work [18]).
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept for NIPS given the theoretical quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
