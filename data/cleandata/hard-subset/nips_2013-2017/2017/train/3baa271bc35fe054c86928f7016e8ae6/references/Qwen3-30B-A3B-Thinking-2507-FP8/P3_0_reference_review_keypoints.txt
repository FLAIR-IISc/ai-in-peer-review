- Addresses robust streaming submodular maximization: construct summaries that remain good after up to m removals using STAR-T (streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query).
- Key idea: partitioned thresholding with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds to balance memory and robustness.
- Novel algorithmic structure that efficiently trades off memory usage and robustness through its bucket/threshold design.
- Theoretical analysis provides approximation guarantee (≈0.149(1−1/log k)) and memory bound O((k + m log k) log²k) with careful case-based proofs.
- Practical variant handles unknown f(OPT(k, V\E)) with only logarithmic space overhead, making the method implementable.
- Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show matching or outperforming SIEVE-STREAMING, despite SIEVE-STREAMING knowing removals.
- Approximation factor (0.149) is significantly lower than SIEVE-STREAMING’s 0.5−ε, and the paper doesn’t fully justify this trade-off for robustness.
- Memory bound includes an extra log²k factor over SIEVE-STREAMING, and the necessity of this overhead versus alternatives is not convincingly argued.
- Parameter sensitivity: theoretical requirement for w is large, but empirically w=1 works, with insufficient explanation for this gap.
- Experimental comparison lacks a robust baseline that does not know removals in advance, possibly overstating advantages.
- Overall strong theoretical and algorithmic contribution with elegant design and convincing empirical results.
- Concerns remain about the practical value given the lower approximation and unresolved memory/parameter questions.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on the trade-off discussion, explaining w in practice, and adding comparisons to non-omniscient robust baselines.
