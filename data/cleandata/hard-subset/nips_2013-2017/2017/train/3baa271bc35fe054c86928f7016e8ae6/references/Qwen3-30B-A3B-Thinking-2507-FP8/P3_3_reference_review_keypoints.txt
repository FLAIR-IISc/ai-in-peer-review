- Summary: tackles robust submodular maximization in streaming setting via a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming summary + STAR-T-GREEDY query) using partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Theoretical contribution: proves summary size O((k + m log k) log k) and a constant-factor (0.149) approximation for the robust problem.
- Novel algorithmic design: introduces an elegant partitioned thresholding scheme that combines robustness structure with streaming thresholding.
- Practical validation: experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING that knows removals in advance.
- Space complexity advantage: achieves logarithmic dependence on k versus linear dependence in concurrent work [18].
- Practical implementation challenge: main analysis assumes known τ = f(OPT(k, V\E)), and the parallel workaround increases space by a log₁₊ᵋ k factor, which may be large for big k.
- Constant factor comparison concern: 0.149 approximation is much lower than non-robust SIEVE-STREAMING (0.5-ε), and claimed comparisons are fair only when competitors know removals in advance.
- Comparison with concurrent work lacking: paper mentions [18] but needs deeper discussion of tradeoffs (constant factor vs. practical memory) and contextualization of memory claims.
- Theoretical assumptions limitation: analysis assumes normalized monotone submodular functions and should discuss extensions to other function classes.
- Experimental design critique: comparisons are not against a truly robust version of SIEVE-STREAMING; stronger baselines (robust variants) would improve evaluation.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and technically sound contribution to robust streaming submodular maximization with practical implications, despite some implementation and comparison gaps.
- Recommendation: Accept.
