- Problem: One-pass streaming robust submodular maximization that builds a summary S enabling near-optimal size-k solutions after up to m deletions without revisiting the stream.  
- Algorithm: Two-stage method—STAR-T streaming builds exponential partitions of buckets with decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs GREEDY on S\E at query time.  
- Memory: Summary size claimed O((k + m log k) log k) (or extra log_{1+ε} k factor when τ unknown); paper text inconsistently also states O((k + m log k) log^2 k).  
- Guarantee: Constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ c·f(OPT(k, V\E)) with c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉), using w = ⌈4·⌈log k⌉·m/k⌉.  
- Analysis: Careful two-case proof (many full buckets vs. none) leveraging exponentional bucket sizes and decreasing thresholds; parallels SIEVE-STREAMING and PRO ideas.  
- Novelty: New combination of partitioned bucket structure and streaming thresholding for deletion-robust streaming with improved k-dependence.  
- Comparison to concurrent work: Better memory scaling in k (logarithmic vs linear) but lower approximation constant than concurrent deletion-robust streaming (1/2 − ε).  
- Experiments: Tests on dominating set and MovieLens show performance close to SIEVE-STREAMING with known E and near GREEDY in small cases.  
- Empirics limitations: Missing wall-clock time and memory reporting, limited exploration of varying m and w, sensitivity to α in recommendation, and some tests remove more than theoretical m.  
- Complexity concern: No explicit per-element/time complexity; worst-case per-element oracle calls appear O(wk log k), which may be costly.  
- Clarity issues: Inconsistent space bound statements (log k vs log^2 k) and threshold notation mismatch (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}); minor typos present.  
- Practical suggestions: Provide per-element amortized oracle counts, data-structure optimizations, and empirical studies of w and m trade-offs.  
- Significance: Addresses important practical setting (large-scale, deletions), gives attractive memory bounds and simple GREEDY query mechanism despite modest constant.  
- Pros: One-pass sublinear-memory algorithm, principled design with rigorous analysis, improved k-scaling, practical two-stage workflow.  
- Cons: Low approximation factor (~0.149), missing computational complexity details, and incomplete experimental evaluation.  
- Suggested comparisons: Include or discuss comparisons to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18] to clarify trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable contribution with novel design and memory advantage but needs clarity and stronger empirical/complexity evidence.
