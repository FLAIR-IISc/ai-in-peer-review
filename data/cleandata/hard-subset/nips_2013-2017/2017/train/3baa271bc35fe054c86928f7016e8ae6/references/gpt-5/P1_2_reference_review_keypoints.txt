- Paper addresses one-pass streaming robust submodular maximization: build compact summary S so after adversarial deletion of ≤m elements you can extract a size-k solution approximating OPT on V\E.
- Two-stage approach: STAR-T streaming maintains partitioned buckets with exponentially growing capacities and decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs GREEDY on S\E at query time.
- When OPT known and w ≥ ⌈4⌈log k⌉m/k⌉, stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements and guarantees constant-factor approximation c→0.149 as k→∞.
- When OPT unknown, uses multi-τ parallel instances (SIEVE-STREAMING style), incurring extra O(log k) space factor and (1+ε) loss, giving space O((k + m log k) log^2 k).
- Analysis splits into two cases: many full buckets (value near τ survives deletions) and all buckets sparse (trade-off between minimally hit bucket and residual coverage) and balances them to get the constant.
- Empirically matches or slightly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when that baseline knows E) on dominating set and facility-location-style recommendation tasks, and supports multiple interactive queries on S.
- Approach blends ideas from centralized robust methods (PRO) and streaming thresholding (SIEVE-STREAMING) and is distinct from concurrent deletion-robust streaming work [18] which uses much more memory.
- Theorems are technically plausible and well-structured; memory/approximation trade-off is clearly articulated and the OPT-removal via multi-τ follows established practice.
- Constants (≈0.149) are modest but constant; the m-dependence is near-linear up to log factors, which is attractive.
- Minor correctness/precision issues: inconsistent space claims across sections (Theorem 4.1 vs intro/conclusion) — align upfront; text vs pseudocode mismatch on threshold expression (use min{2^i,k} consistently).
- Major practical concern: per-element processing time may be high — Θ(wk) buckets total implies up to O(wk) marginal evaluations per element, far worse than SIEVE-STREAMING’s O(log k) checks.
- Runtime issue needs discussion, empirical timing, or heuristics (skip full buckets, random bucket choice, cap checks, lazy evaluation).
- Experimental methodology concern: deleting elements from S instead of V disproportionately hurts the proposed method; should also test E sampled/selected from V to complement results.
- Experiments should report sensitivity to w and m, memory usage (|S|), per-element processing time, and comparison to [18] or explain infeasibility.
- Paper is well organized and readable; proofs and figures help; supplement contains full proofs.
- Clarify in paper: how τ was chosen experimentally (grid/ε), provide pseudocode/footprint for multi-τ parallelization, and justify parameter choices (growth factor 2, threshold schedule).
- Novelty: partitioned-thresholding with exponentially growing capacities and decreasing thresholds is novel in streaming robust setting; analysis combining full-bucket robustness and sparse-bucket coverage is original.
- Significance: addresses an important problem (deletions/right-to-be-forgotten, failures, interactive recommendation), offers strong space-approximation trade-off and reusable summary for many queries.
- Pros: timely problem, elegant one-pass design, solid theoretical guarantees with near-optimal m-scaling, competitive empirical validation.
- Cons: potentially high per-element computational cost, missing sensitivity/runtime analyses and comparison to [18], minor clarity/notation issues, and relatively small approximation constant.
- Suggested improvements: include time-per-element and memory plots; analyze impact of w and m; add practical heuristics to reduce bucket checks; clarify τ selection and multi-τ implementation; consider comparing or simulating trade-off with [18]; explore alternative partition/threshold schedules.
- Recommendation: Lean accept — novel, relevant, solid theory and experiments; addressing runtime and clarity concerns would strengthen the paper.
