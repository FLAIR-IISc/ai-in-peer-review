- Tackles robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting and proposes STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm (partitioned thresholding summary + greedy on summary).  
- Provides a novel partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially decreasing thresholds to balance space, robustness, and approximation.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee (approximately 0.149) with a sophisticated analysis.  
- Empirically validated on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks, performing competitively even against algorithms that know removals.  
- Highlights practical utility in interactive settings where user preferences change.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Main theoretical weakness: the approximation constant is small and depends on 1/dlog k, which may hurt performance for large k.  
- Space complexity O((k + m log k) log k) could be limiting; impact of parameter w on constants is unclear and theoretical guarantees need larger w.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited—more baselines for robust submodular maximization (especially non-streaming ones without removal knowledge) should be included.  
- Some experimental assumptions (e.g., greedy removals) may not reflect all real-world scenarios.  
- Lacks scalability experiments on much larger datasets to assess practical limits for large k and m.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically supported work with convincing empirical results; recommended for acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel approach, strong theory, competitive experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small approximation constant, potential space limitations, and limited experimental breadth.  
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
