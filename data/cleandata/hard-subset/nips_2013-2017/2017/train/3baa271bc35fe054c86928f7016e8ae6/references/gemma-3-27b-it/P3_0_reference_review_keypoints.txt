- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm using partitioned buckets and decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary and answer removal queries with a greedy stage.
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee for robustness (~0.149) and analyzes memory complexity (logarithmic in k).
- Empirically competitive with SIEVE-STREAMING on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removals a priori.
- Positions work relative to classic submodular and streaming literature, bridging theoretical robustness (Orlin/Bogunovic) and practical streaming methods (Badanidiyuru et al.).
- Connects to interactive/adaptive submodular optimization through the query stage.
- Novel partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially decreasing thresholds is well-motivated and central to the approach.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and addresses both streaming construction and query response stages.
- Two-stage framework is practically relevant for robust summarization and adaptation to removals.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Performance depends on careful choice/estimation of threshold τ; practical estimation challenges and sensitivity are not deeply explored.
- The approximation constant (0.149) is relatively small and may limit applicability; discussion on tightness or improvement is lacking.
- The analytical arguments are complex and could benefit from more intuitive explanations.
- Limited direct experimental or analytical comparison to PRO (Bogunovic et al.); memory-usage claims would be stronger with empirical validation.
- Experimental scope is narrow (two applications); broader datasets and functions would increase confidence in generalizability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, promising empirical results, clear presentation, and relevant problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: small constant factor, complex proofs, and need for further study of parameter sensitivity.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: discuss practical τ estimation and robust strategies, add intuitive explanation of key proofs, include a PRO comparison, and expand experiments.
