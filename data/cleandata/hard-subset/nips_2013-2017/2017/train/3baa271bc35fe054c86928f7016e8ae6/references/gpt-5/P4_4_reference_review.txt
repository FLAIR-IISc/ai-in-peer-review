Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in the streaming setting. The goal is to make a single pass over a sequence of elements, store a small summary S that is robust to the removal of up to m elements, and at query time, after an arbitrary E with |E| ≤ m is revealed, produce a size-k set Z ⊆ S \ E whose value approximates OPT(k, V \ E). The proposed two-stage method consists of STAR-T (streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query). STAR-T partitions memory into exponentially increasing bucket sizes across partitions and associates each partition with an exponentially decreasing marginal-gain threshold. Each incoming element is placed in the first non-full bucket whose marginal gain exceeds the partition’s threshold. At query time, GREEDY is run on S \ E to output Z.

The main theoretical result shows that with w ≥ ⌈4 m log k / k⌉ buckets per partition and a threshold τ chosen as a constant-factor proxy for f(OPT(k, V \ E)), STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements (Theorem 4.1) and STAR-T-GREEDY achieves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 · (1 − 1/⌈log k⌉). When τ is unknown, a standard “guessing” scheme analogous to SIEVE-STREAMING [8] runs multiple instances in parallel, increasing space by a factor O(log_{1+ε} k) and losing a (1+ε) factor in the guarantee (Theorem 4.5).

This work builds on: (i) classical GREEDY guarantees for monotone submodular maximization [7]; (ii) streaming submodular maximization via thresholding (SIEVE-STREAMING) [8]; and (iii) robust submodular maximization in the offline setting, including PRO’s non-uniform partitioning structure [11] and prior robust formulations [9,10]. The paper also discusses concurrent deletion-robust streaming work [18], which attains a (1/2 − ε) approximation but uses O(m k log k / ε) memory; the present method’s memory overhead due to robustness scales as O(m log^2 k) (when accounting for τ-guessing), rather than O(m k).

Quality (technical soundness):
- The analysis is structured around two cases: (a) a partition with at least half full buckets exists; then a full bucket with limited overlap with E yields a good value after deletions, and GREEDY’s (1 − 1/e) factor applies; (b) all partitions are mostly non-full; then either the last partition’s selected bucket already carries large value (Lemma 4.3) or most of OPT must lie in earlier buckets, whose marginal gains are upper-bounded by τ, leading to a complementary bound (Lemma 4.4). Balancing the two yields the 0.149 constant. This is a reasonable and standard style of submodular analysis, leveraging diminishing returns and greedy guarantees.
- The memory bound |S| = O((k + m log k) log k) for known τ follows from counting buckets and their capacities across partitions; the additional log factor for unknown τ aligns with standard threshold-guessing techniques.
- Several clarity/consistency issues in the technical presentation should be corrected:
  - The threshold rule in text states τ/2^i, while Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}. The latter is plausible (and necessary when 2^i > k), but the paper should explicitly reconcile these definitions.
  - The expression for τ in Theorem 4.1 appears malformed (“τ = 1 / + (1−e−1)(1−e−1/3)(1 − 1^{...}) f(OPT(·))”). Please provide the correct formula and its derivation (and define all constants α1, α2 where used).
  - The abstract/conclusion cite O((k + m log k) log^2 k) storage, while Theorem 4.1 shows O((k + m log k) log k) for known τ, and Theorem 4.5 indicates an extra log_{1+ε} k factor for τ-guessing. Unify these statements, indicate when each bound applies, and include ε-dependence.
- Time complexity is not discussed. Each arriving element may scan O(log k) partitions times O(w k/2^i) buckets per partition, implying potentially O(w log k) marginal evaluations per element in practice, but a worst-case bound and practical cost should be reported.

Clarity:
The overall structure is understandable and the intuition behind partitioned thresholds is conveyed. However, the noted inconsistencies (threshold definition, τ formula, storage bounds) impede reproducibility. Symbols (e.g., dlog ke, ceilings/floors) are used inconsistently, and some constants appear without clear definitions. Including a self-contained description of the τ-guessing scheme in the main text (not only appendix) would improve clarity.

Originality:
The combination of a partitioned, exponentially increasing bucket structure with a streaming, exponentially decreasing thresholding scheme tailored to robustness is novel relative to SIEVE-STREAMING and to offline PRO. The work addresses the robust streaming setting with provable guarantees and improved robustness-related space dependence compared to concurrent work [18], albeit with a smaller approximation constant.

Significance:
Robust streaming submodular maximization is relevant to large-scale summarization and recommendation scenarios where post-hoc deletions are common (e.g., privacy, failures, user constraints). The constant-factor guarantee with reduced robustness overhead is a useful theoretical contribution. Empirically, the method performs comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING even when the latter knows E in advance, and to GREEDY on smaller data, illustrating practical viability. The experimental section would be stronger with runtime/space measurements and ablations (e.g., sensitivity to w and m), but the scenarios are representative.

Pros:
- Addresses an important robust streaming variant with a principled algorithm and analysis.
- Memory overhead due to robustness scales as O(m log^2 k) (with τ-guessing), improving over O(m k log k) in [18].
- Clear two-stage design; reusable summary supports multiple queries with different E.
- Empirical results on graphs and recommendation show competitive performance against strong baselines.

Cons:
- Approximation factor is modest (~0.149), substantially lower than (1/2 − ε) in [18].
- Several inconsistencies/typos (threshold rule, τ specification, storage bounds) reduce clarity and reproducibility.
- Lack of time complexity analysis and empirical efficiency (runtime, memory) reporting.
- Reliance on knowing m and selecting w; practical guidance for setting w is limited beyond w=1 in experiments.
- Baseline comparisons could be expanded (e.g., to [18]) and fairness clarified; SIEVE-STREAMING is granted oracle knowledge of E whereas STAR-T operates from S only.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Unify and correct all threshold-related definitions and the τ expression; provide a concise, correct statement of all constants and parameters in the main text.
- Clarify the exact storage bounds in each regime (known τ vs. τ-guessing), including ε dependence, and reconcile with abstract/conclusion.
- Provide per-element time complexity and total runtime/memory measurements in experiments; include sensitivity to w and m, and guidance on choosing w when m is unknown or variable.
- Compare empirically and theoretically against [18] where possible, discussing the trade-off between approximation quality and robustness-related memory.
- Expand on how τ is guessed in practice (range, grid), and how the algorithm behaves if m is mis-specified.

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to robust submodular maximization in the streaming model, with meaningful theoretical and empirical contributions. Addressing the noted clarity and evaluation gaps would significantly strengthen the work.
