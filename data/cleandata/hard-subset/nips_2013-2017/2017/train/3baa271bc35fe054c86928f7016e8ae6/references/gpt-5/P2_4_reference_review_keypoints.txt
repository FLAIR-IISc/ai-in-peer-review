- Problem: robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming under cardinality constraint, summary S resilient to up to m deletions.  
- Goal: produce S so that for any E (|E| ≤ m) a size-k set Z ⊆ S\E achieves constant-factor approximation to OPT(k, V\E).  
- Algorithm: STAR-T organizes partitions i=0..⌊log k⌋ with exponentially many buckets and decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs GREEDY on S\E at query time.  
- Main theoretical result: with w ≥ ⌈(4 m log k)/k⌉, space |S| = O(k log k + m log^2 k) and approximation c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉).  
- τ dependence: τ ≈ f(OPT) is handled via parallel-guessing (as in SIEVE-STREAMING), adding O(log_{1+ε} k) space and (1+ε) loss.  
- Novelty: combines partitioned buckets (PRO) and streaming thresholding (SIEVE-STREAMING) to get logarithmic dependence on k versus linear in prior [18].  
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING, PRO, and concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18] which achieves better constant but much larger memory.  
- Analysis soundness: careful two-case proof with explicit constants; w controls deletion robustness; GREEDY’s (1−1/e) property used appropriately.  
- Practicality: robustness guarantee holds for any deletion set E; memory claims stated clearly (with extra log for τ guessing).  
- Minor fixes needed: harmonize inconsistent space bounds across sections; clarify τ selection in experiments; fix typographical errors.  
- Clarity: algorithm and proof sketch are intuitive and readable, with pseudocode provided.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing experimental details on τ, per-dataset |S|, and runtime reduce reproducibility.  
- Originality assessment: approach is novel in streaming-robust setting and meaningfully trades memory for approximation.  
- Significance: useful for large-scale and “right to be forgotten” scenarios; advances memory regime when k large and m nontrivial.  
- Experimental gaps: no empirical comparison to [18]; lack of systematic study of m–w–memory–accuracy trade-offs; w set to 1 in practice without ablation.  
- Strengths: one-pass algorithm, sublinear memory O(k log k + m log^2 k), constant-factor approximation independent of m and n, elegant partitioned-threshold design, practical demos.  
- Weaknesses: modest approximation constant (~0.149); inconsistent space statements; missing experimental ablations and resource reporting; presentation rough edges.  
- Questions for authors: how τ is chosen in practice (ε, number of guesses, |S| and runtime overhead); can the constant be improved; sensitivity to w; why [18] not included in experiments.  
- Recommendation: Leaning accept (weak accept), contingent on clarifying space/τ details and strengthening experiments.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
