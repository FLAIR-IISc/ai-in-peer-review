

# Review: Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach

This paper proposes a novel two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization in streaming settings. The authors design a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that constructs a summary set S of size O((k + m log k) log²k), followed by a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that, after any removal of up to m elements, returns a solution of size at most k with a constant-factor approximation guarantee (c ≈ 0.149).

## Quality
The paper presents technically sound theoretical analysis with careful proofs for the approximation guarantees. The authors provide a detailed proof sketch and reference supplementary material for complete proofs, which is appropriate for NIPS. The theoretical bounds are well-justified, and the paper properly addresses the limitation of needing to approximate f(OPT(k, V \ E)) through a parallel variant that increases memory complexity by a factor of log₁₊ᵋ k while providing a (1+ε)-approximation.

The experimental validation across two distinct applications (dominating set and personalized movie recommendation) demonstrates strong empirical performance. The authors compare against relevant baselines including SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows in advance which elements will be removed), RANDOM, and a variant of their method using SIEVE-STREAMING in the query stage. The results show that STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING while requiring no advance knowledge of removals, which is a significant practical advantage.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The problem statement is precise, with careful definitions of robustness and the two-stage framework. The algorithm is well-described with pseudocode and illustrative figures (Figure 1). The proof sketch provides sufficient intuition for the main results, though some parts could be slightly clearer (e.g., the exact relationship between τ and f(OPT(k, V \ E))). The experimental results are presented with clear figures and appropriate statistical validation (averaged over 100 iterations).

## Originality
The partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds is a significant novelty for robust submodular maximization. The two-stage framework (streaming followed by query) represents a meaningful departure from previous robust submodular maximization work. The memory complexity of O((k + m log k) log²k) improves upon the O(mk log k/ε) complexity of concurrent work [18], making this a meaningful theoretical contribution. The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work, particularly [11] (which uses a different construction) and [18] (which has higher memory complexity).

## Significance
The problem of robust submodular maximization in streaming settings is highly relevant to real-world applications like data summarization, influence maximization, and personalized recommendations. The constant-factor approximation guarantee, while lower than some non-robust streaming algorithms (e.g., 0.5 for SIEVE-STREAMING), is valuable for settings where robustness is essential. The empirical results demonstrate practical viability, with the method performing comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING even without knowing removals in advance. The two-stage approach enables interactive applications where the same summary can be reused for multiple different removal scenarios, which is a significant practical advantage.

## Strengths
- Novel partitioned thresholding approach with exponential thresholding
- Strong theoretical guarantees with clear approximation bounds
- Comprehensive empirical validation across multiple domains
- Practical two-stage framework enabling interactive applications
- Clear comparison with relevant baselines

## Weaknesses
- The approximation constant (0.149) is lower than some previous results (e.g., 0.5 for SIEVE-STREAMING), though this is expected due to the added robustness constraint
- The requirement to approximate f(OPT(k, V \ E)) is addressed through a parallel variant that increases memory complexity by a factor of log₁₊ᵋ k
- The theoretical bound has a log²k factor, though the empirical results suggest the method is effective in practice

## Conclusion
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of streaming submodular optimization. The two-stage framework provides a practical solution to the challenging problem of robust submodular maximization in streaming settings. The theoretical guarantees are solid, the empirical validation is comprehensive, and the approach has clear practical value for real-world applications. The work advances the state of the art in robust submodular maximization and demonstrates that robustness can be achieved with manageable memory overhead.

I recommend acceptance. This paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound (quality), clearly written (clarity), makes a novel contribution (originality), and addresses an important problem with practical significance (significance). The work is well-positioned to influence future research in streaming submodular optimization and robust data summarization.
