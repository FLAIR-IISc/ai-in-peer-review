- Paper addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings and proposes STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm using partitioned thresholding and greedy selection on a robust summary, with theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.

- Strength: tackles an important, practical problem (robustness + streaming).

- Strength: novel partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds balances summarization quality and robustness.

- Strength: provides a formal constant-factor approximation guarantee (though small).

- Strength: empirical results on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks show competitive or superior performance to SIEVE-STREAMING.

- Strength: supports efficient generation of multiple sub-summaries from a single precomputed summary for interactive use.

- Strength: paper is generally well-written and organized.

- Weakness: the approximation constant (0.149) is small and may limit practical effectiveness.

- Weakness: algorithm performance depends on parameter tuning (e.g., w, τ) and the paper gives limited guidance beyond noting w=1 often works.

- Weakness: lacks comparisons to stronger centralized baselines (e.g., PRO [11]), which would clarify trade-offs.

- Weakness: theoretical proofs are complex and mostly in the supplement, making tightness and details hard to verify quickly.

- Weakness: stated memory bound O((k + m log k) log k) could be substantial for large k and m; more practical memory analysis is needed.

- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and theoretically sound contribution with promising empirical results; weaknesses do not outweigh merits.

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a challenging problem, proposes a novel algorithm with guarantees, and shows competitive empirical performance and practical benefits.

- Arguments against acceptance: small approximation constant, parameter sensitivity, and limited baseline comparisons.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Qualitative evaluation: Quality—Good; Clarity—Good; Originality—Good; Significance—Good.
