## Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The authors propose a two-stage algorithm, STAR-T, which first builds a robust summary of the data in a single pass using a partitioned thresholding scheme, and then uses a greedy algorithm on this summary to provide solutions even after a certain number of elements are removed.  The core contribution lies in a novel data structure (partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds) and a corresponding analysis that provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  The paper also includes empirical validation demonstrating competitive performance with existing streaming algorithms.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Robustness is a critical consideration in many real-world applications of submodular maximization, and the streaming setting is crucial for scalability.
* **Novel approach:** The partitioned thresholding scheme is a clever way to balance memory usage, robustness, and approximation quality. The decreasing threshold idea allows the algorithm to capture elements with varying marginal contributions.
* **Theoretical guarantees:** The paper provides a theoretical guarantee of a constant-factor approximation, which is a significant achievement for this problem. The analysis appears rigorous and combines several key insights.
* **Empirical validation:** The experiments in both dominating set and personalized movie recommendation demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the algorithm and its competitiveness against strong baselines (especially SIEVE-STREAMING).
* **Interactive Component:** The paper notes that this approach is also useful for interactive learning and can provide different sub-summaries given multiple removals.
* **Clear writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the algorithm and its analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Constant factor is small:** While a constant-factor approximation is achieved, the constant (0.149) is relatively small. This might limit the practical utility of the algorithm in scenarios where a higher approximation ratio is required.  The authors do acknowledge this and discuss potential avenues for improvement.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The theoretical guarantee relies on setting the threshold parameter τ appropriately. The paper briefly mentions approximation strategies in Section 4.1, but a more detailed discussion of practical parameter tuning would be helpful. The dependency on `w` is also not immediately intuitive.
* **Space Complexity:** While the space complexity of O((k + m log k) log k) is reasonable, the dependency on `m` can become significant when `m` is large. The assumption of w ≥ ⌈ 4dlog kem/k ⌉ seems conservative and is a key element in ensuring performance and theoretical guarantees, but may impact practicality.
* **Comparison to PRO:** The related work mentions the PRO algorithm, which achieves the same approximation result with a potentially greater robustness `m = o(k)`.  The comparison is brief. A more detailed discussion of the trade-offs between the two algorithms (e.g., memory usage, computational complexity) would strengthen the paper.



**Evaluation based on NIPS criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results. The algorithm seems complete and the authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The algorithm and analysis are explained in sufficient detail.
* **Originality:** The partitioned thresholding approach and the corresponding analysis are novel contributions. The paper adequately references related work.
* **Significance:** The problem is important, and the proposed solution provides a valuable contribution to the field of robust submodular maximization.  The results are likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners working in data summarization, influence maximization, and related areas.




**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant and challenging problem.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm.
* Provides strong theoretical guarantees.
* Demonstrates competitive performance through empirical validation.
* The work aligns well with the NIPS focus on machine learning and optimization.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The constant factor of the approximation guarantee is relatively small.
* Parameter tuning may be challenging in practice.
* Space complexity can be a concern for large `m`.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of robust submodular maximization. The proposed algorithm is novel, well-motivated, and supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results. While the constant factor of the approximation guarantee could be improved, the overall quality of the work warrants acceptance.

**Recommendation:** **Accept** (with minor revisions). I suggest the authors to:

*  Elaborate on practical parameter tuning strategies for τ and w.
*  Provide a more detailed comparison with the PRO algorithm, highlighting the trade-offs between the two approaches.
*  Discuss how the parameter `w` impacts the algorithm's performance in practice.
