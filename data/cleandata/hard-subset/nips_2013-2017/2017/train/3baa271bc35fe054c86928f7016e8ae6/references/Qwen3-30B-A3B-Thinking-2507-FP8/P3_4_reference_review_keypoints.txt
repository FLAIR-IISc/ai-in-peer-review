- Proposes STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY: a two-stage streaming method that builds a summary S robust to up to m removals and then extracts a size-k solution, claiming a 0.149 approximation and summary size O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Uses a novel partitioned thresholding design with exponentially decreasing thresholds and exponentially growing buckets to handle robustness.  
- Provides a detailed, rigorous theoretical analysis with multiple lemmas covering different partition cases.  
- Empirical results (dominating set, movie recommendation) show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE-STREAMING is given the removed elements.  
- Clearly formulates the two-stage robust streaming problem and its practical motivation.  
- The 0.149 approximation is weak compared to standard greedy (0.632) and SIEVE-STREAMING (0.5-ε) and lacks adequate justification for practicality.  
- The comparison to SIEVE-STREAMING is misleading; it should compare to a robust variant rather than the standard algorithm that ignores removals.  
- Main algorithm assumes knowledge of f(OPT(k, V\E)), which is unrealistic; the parallel variant resolving this inflates space to O((k + m log k) log k log_{1+ε} k) without clear trade-off justification.  
- Conflicting memory claims: paper states O(m log^2 k) but the theorem gives O((k + m log k) log k), a significant and unexplained discrepancy.  
- Limited comparison with concurrent robust work [18]; a direct comparison would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: novel and technically sound contribution to robust streaming submodular optimization but hampered by a weak approximation guarantee and unclear/practical concerns.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing the weak approximation factor, clarifying comparisons with SIEVE-STREAMING, and providing a realistic discussion of the f(OPT) assumption.
