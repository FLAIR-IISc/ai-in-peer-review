This paper tackles the important and practically relevant problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The core idea – a partitioned thresholding approach – is novel and represents a significant departure from existing streaming algorithms. The authors correctly identify the challenges of robustness, especially when data arrives sequentially, and propose a structured approach to maintain a representative, resilient summary. The theoretical analysis, while dense, appears sound and establishes a constant-factor approximation guarantee. I particularly appreciate the attempt to address the practical issue of unknown optimality gap through the parallel instance approach outlined in Section 4.1. The experimental validation on both synthetic and real datasets further strengthens the paper and demonstrates competitive performance against established baselines.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The partitioned thresholding scheme is a genuinely new approach to robust streaming submodular maximization. This isn't a minor tweak; it’s a fundamentally different structure for maintaining the summary.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, offering guarantees on the approximation ratio. The interplay between the partition structure and the decreasing thresholds is cleverly exploited.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The problem addressed is highly relevant to many large-scale machine learning applications, and the two-stage framework is well-motivated.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation are well-designed and demonstrate competitive performance. The ability to handle multiple removal scenarios with a single precomputed summary is a valuable practical feature.
*   **Addressing Limitations:** The authors acknowledge the need to estimate the unknown optimality gap and propose a practical solution with parallel instances.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance seems sensitive to the choice of parameters, particularly *w*. While the paper provides guidance on setting *w*, a more in-depth analysis of its impact on performance would be beneficial.
*   **Constant Factor:** The constant factor in the approximation guarantee (0.149) is quite small. While theoretically interesting, it may limit the practical applicability of the algorithm in some scenarios. However, given the complexity of the problem and the robustness constraint, achieving a significantly higher constant is likely extremely challenging.
*   **Experimental Scope:** While the experiments are solid, expanding them to other applications beyond dominating set and movie recommendation would further strengthen the evaluation. 
*   **Clarity of Proofs:** The theoretical proofs, particularly in Section 4, are quite dense and could benefit from more detailed explanations and intermediate steps. While mathematically correct, they are difficult to follow without substantial effort.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a genuinely novel approach to a challenging problem.
*   The theoretical analysis is rigorous and establishes meaningful guarantees.
*   The empirical results demonstrate competitive performance and highlight the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The authors address potential limitations and propose practical solutions.
*   The two-stage framework is well-motivated and provides a flexible solution for robust data summarization.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The constant factor in the approximation guarantee is relatively small.
*   The paper’s readability could be improved through more detailed explanations of the theoretical proofs.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of submodular optimization. While the constant factor in the approximation guarantee is a concern, the novelty of the approach, the rigor of the analysis, and the practical relevance of the problem outweigh this drawback. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance, even though it isn’t a perfectly polished piece of work. The introduction of the partitioned thresholding scheme is a valuable contribution that will likely stimulate further research in this area. I recommend acceptance.
