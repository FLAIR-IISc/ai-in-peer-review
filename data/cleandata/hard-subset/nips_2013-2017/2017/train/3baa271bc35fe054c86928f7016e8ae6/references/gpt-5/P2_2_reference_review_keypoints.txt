- Problem and setup: One-pass streaming algorithm for deletion-robust submodular maximization that builds a compact summary S to handle up to m deletions and extract a k-size solution from S\E at query time.  
- Algorithm: STAR-T arranges exponentially growing buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds; elements are placed into the first non-full bucket exceeding the bucket threshold.  
- Query procedure: STAR-T-GREEDY runs standard GREEDY on S\E to produce the final solution.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Constant-factor approximation c→0.149 as k grows, with space O(k log k + m log^2 k) (or O((k + m log k) log^2 k) with parallel τ estimation).  
- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to robust deletions and resembles PRO’s partitioning but uses online thresholded placement instead of offline filling.  
- Memory trade-off: Achieves logarithmic dependence on k (vs. linear in prior robust methods), at the cost of a smaller approximation factor.  
- Analysis quality: Careful, nontrivial proof balancing two structural regimes (many full vs. many non-full buckets) and clear space bound derivation.  
- Unknown-OPT handling: Addresses need for τ≈f(OPT) via parallel-thresholding at an extra log factor in space.  
- Approximation critique: 0.149 is modest compared to 0.5 non-robust or concurrent 1/2−ε robust results, but the memory savings are meaningful.  
- Per-element cost concern: Worst-case per-item processing can be O(m log k) (or O(k) in practice with w=1), which may be large and is not analyzed or measured.  
- Experimental results: STAR-T(-GREEDY) matches or outperforms strong baselines (including a baseline that knows E), and supports multiple deletions from one summary.  
- Experimental gaps: Missing wall-clock time and memory measurements, sensitivity to w and τ-parallelization overhead, and systematic sweeps over m including mis-specified m.  
- Clarity issues: Inconsistent threshold notation (τ/2^i vs. τ/min{2^i,k}) and inconsistent space bound statements across sections/theorems.  
- Originality: Novel streaming partitioned-thresholding design and a new analysis balancing two regimes; meaningful advance for one-pass deletion-robust streaming.  
- Significance: Addresses important practical issues (deletions, streaming) and provides the first one-pass constant-factor algorithm with only logarithmic dependence on k.  
- Pros: Simple one-pass algorithm, strong memory bounds, clear theoretical analysis, practical competitiveness in experiments.  
- Cons: Low approximation factor, missing runtime analysis and empirical efficiency metrics, minor inconsistencies, and dependence on w tied to m with limited guidance.  
- Suggestions: Add per-element time complexity and empirical runtime/memory; unify threshold/space statements; include sensitivity analyses for w and m; compare to concurrent robust streaming method if feasible.  
- Overall: Technically sound, original, and significant with solid empirical support despite modest approximation and missing efficiency analyses; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
