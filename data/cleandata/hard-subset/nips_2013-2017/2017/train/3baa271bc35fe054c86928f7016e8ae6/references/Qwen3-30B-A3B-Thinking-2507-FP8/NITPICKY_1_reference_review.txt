

# Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

This paper proposes a two-stage streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The authors introduce STAR-T for the streaming stage (constructing a robust summary S) and STAR-T-GREEDY for the query stage (retrieving a solution after element removals). The main theoretical claim is that STAR-T constructs a summary of size O((k + m log k) log k) and STAR-T-GREEDY provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee of c ≈ 0.149.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a meaningful problem in large-scale submodular optimization with robustness requirements. The two-stage approach is well-motivated by practical applications like social network analysis and recommendation systems. The theoretical analysis attempts to combine partitioned thresholding with robustness guarantees, which is non-trivial. The experimental results demonstrate reasonable performance compared to SIEVE-STREAMING, even though the comparison is somewhat misleading (see below).

## Weaknesses

1. **Critical Theoretical Error**: The claimed approximation constant c = 0.149 in Theorem 4.1 is incorrect. The derivation in the proof shows:
   c = (1-e^(-1/3))(1-e^(-1)) / [(1-e^(-1/3)) + (1-e^(-1))] ≈ 0.1957, not 0.149. This is a significant error that undermines the main theoretical contribution.

2. **Inconsistent Logarithmic Terms**: The paper claims O((m log k + k) log² k) elements in the abstract but states O((k + m log k) log k) in Theorem 4.1. The discrepancy between log² k and log k is not explained, which is a serious inconsistency.

3. **Misleading Comparison to SIEVE-STREAMING**: The paper states "we allow the latter to know in advance which elements will be removed from the dataset." However, SIEVE-STREAMING is a streaming algorithm that processes data without knowledge of future elements, so it cannot "know" which elements will be removed in the query stage. This comparison is fundamentally unfair and misleading.

4. **Inconsistent Use of Parameter w**: The theoretical analysis requires w ≥ ⌈4dlog kem/k⌉, but the paper states "w = 1 works well in practice" in Section 5. This contradicts the theoretical guarantee, as w=1 would not satisfy the requirement for the approximation ratio.

5. **Inaccurate Claim About Space Complexity**: The paper claims "memory requirement of their method increases linearly with k, in the case of our algorithm this dependence is logarithmic." However, both STAR-T (O((k + m log k) log k)) and [18]'s method (O(mk log k/ε)) have linear dependence on k, not logarithmic. This misrepresentation is misleading.

6. **Technical Inconsistencies**: 
   - The notation "dlog ke" is used throughout without clarification (should be floor or ceiling of log k)
   - In Algorithm 1, line 5: "f(e | Bi,j) ≥ τ/min{2i, k}" - the denominator should be consistent with the threshold definition
   - The proof of Lemma 4.2 incorrectly claims f(Bi?,j) ≥ τ, when it should be ≥ |Bi?,j| × τ/min{2i, k}
   - The paper states E ⊆ V but should specify E ⊆ S (removals happen from the summary S)

7. **Experimental Flaws**: The experiments report averages over 100 iterations without providing error bars or statistical significance tests, making it difficult to assess the reliability of the claimed performance improvements.

## Significance and Originality
The problem of robust submodular maximization is meaningful, but the paper's contribution is marginal compared to prior work. The partitioned thresholding approach is similar to PRO [11], and the extension to streaming is not particularly novel. The constant factor of 0.149 is substantially worse than the (1-1/e) ≈ 0.632 approximation of standard greedy algorithms, which significantly limits practical applicability.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper addresses an important problem, the theoretical claims contain critical errors (particularly the constant factor), and the comparison to SIEVE-STREAMING is misleading. The inconsistencies in notation, terminology, and theoretical guarantees undermine the paper's credibility. The experimental results are not sufficiently rigorous to support the claimed advantages over SIEVE-STREAMING.

Despite the interesting problem setting, the paper's technical shortcomings and misleading claims make it difficult to recommend acceptance. The theoretical result is fundamentally flawed, and the experimental comparison is not properly executed. These issues are severe enough to warrant rejection, even if the problem is of interest.

**Recommendation: Reject**
