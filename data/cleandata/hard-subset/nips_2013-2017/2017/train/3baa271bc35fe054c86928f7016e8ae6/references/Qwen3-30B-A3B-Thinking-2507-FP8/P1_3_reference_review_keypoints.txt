- Addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings, proposing a two-stage method (STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY) with a partitioned thresholding structure.  
- Guarantees a constant-factor approximation (c = 0.149) using O((k + m log k) log k) memory.  
- Novel theoretical analysis combining partitioned thresholding with decreasing greedy thresholding and parallel instances to handle unknown OPT.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, matching SIEVE-STREAMING despite not seeing removals.  
- Clear comparison to prior and concurrent work, showing superior memory complexity to [18].  
- Experiments cover random and greedy removals and multiple query scenarios, supporting practical utility.  
- Approximation factor (0.149) is lower than non-robust methods (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING’s ~0.5), and trade-offs could be discussed more.  
- Proofs are intricate (Lemmas 4.2–4.4) with full details in supplementary material, which may reduce accessibility.  
- Experimental scope is limited to two applications; more diverse submodular functions would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough experimental validation and careful acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with helpful pseudocode and proof sketches, though an earlier intuitive explanation of partitioned thresholding would help.  
- Original contribution: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with constant-factor guarantee and memory-efficient partitioned thresholding.  
- Significant practical impact for large-scale, robustness-critical applications and for reuse of a single precomputed summary across multiple queries.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
