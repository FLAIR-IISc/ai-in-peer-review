- Proposes a novel two-stage streaming approach (STAR-T summary construction and STAR-T-GREEDY query) for robust submodular maximization under up to m deletions.  
- STAR-T builds a summary S of size O((k + m log k) log² k); STAR-T-GREEDY returns a size-k solution after deletions with constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149.  
- Provides technically sound theoretical analysis with careful proofs and a proof sketch; supplementary material contains full proofs.  
- Offers a parallel variant to approximate f(OPT(k, V \ E)) up to (1+ε) at the cost of an extra log₁₊ᵋ k memory factor.  
- Empirical validation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks shows STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING despite not knowing removals in advance.  
- Experimental comparisons include relevant baselines: SIEVE-STREAMING, RANDOM, and a SIEVE-STREAMING query variant; results averaged over 100 iterations.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with precise problem formulation, pseudocode, illustrative figures, and an intuitive proof sketch.  
- Main novelty: partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds and the two-stage streaming+query framework.  
- Memory complexity O((k + m log k) log² k) improves upon related work [18] (O(mk log k/ε)), marking a meaningful theoretical advance.  
- Significance: addresses a practical and important problem (data summarization, influence maximization, recommendations) and enables interactive reuse of summaries under multiple deletion scenarios.  
- Strengths: novel method, strong theoretical guarantees, comprehensive empirical validation, practical two-stage design, clear baseline comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: lower approximation constant (≈0.149) compared to non-robust algorithms (e.g., 0.5 for SIEVE-STREAMING); parallel approximation increases memory; theoretical bound includes a log² k factor.  
- Conclusion and recommendation: solid contribution with practical value and manageable memory overhead; reviewer recommends acceptance.
