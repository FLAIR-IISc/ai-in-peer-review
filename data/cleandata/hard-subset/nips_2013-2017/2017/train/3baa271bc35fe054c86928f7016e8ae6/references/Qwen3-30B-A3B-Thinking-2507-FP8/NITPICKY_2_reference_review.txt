

# Review of "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

## Summary
This paper proposes a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization in streaming settings. The first stage (STAR-T) constructs a robust summary S of size O((k + m log k) log k) using a partitioned thresholding structure. The second stage (STAR-T-GREEDY) runs a greedy algorithm on S \ E to produce a solution of size at most k after any m elements are removed. The authors claim a constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149(1-1/⌊log k⌋)) and demonstrate competitive performance against SIEVE-STREAMING in experiments.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important problem in large-scale data summarization with robustness requirements. The partitioned thresholding approach represents a novel algorithmic structure that differs from previous robust submodular maximization methods. The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, with detailed lemmas and proofs for the approximation guarantee. The experimental results demonstrate practical utility in two applications (dominating set and movie recommendation), showing competitive performance against baselines.

## Weaknesses

1. **Critical Notation Inconsistency**: The paper uses "dlog ke" throughout, which appears to be a typo for "⌊log k⌋" or "⌈log k⌉". This is not a minor detail - it's a fundamental notation error that appears in the abstract, theorem statements, and proof sketches. This undermines the paper's technical precision.

2. **Discrepancy in Theoretical Bounds**: The abstract claims the summary size is O((m log k + k) log² k), but Theorem 4.1 states O((k + m log k) log k). This is a significant inconsistency that needs to be resolved. The proof shows O((log k + 5)wk) with w = O(m log k/k), which would give O((m log k + k) log k), not O((m log k + k) log² k). The abstract must be corrected.

3. **Problematic Definition of τ**: In Theorem 4.1, τ is defined as "1 + (1−e⁻¹)(1−e⁻¹/³)(1−1/dlog ke)f(OPT(k, V \ E))". This "1 +" term makes τ not proportional to f(OPT(k, V \ E)), which contradicts the paper's description of τ as "an α-approximation to f(OPT(k, V \ E))". This is a critical technical error.

4. **Misunderstanding of SIEVE-STREAMING**: The paper claims to compare against "SIEVE-STREAMING [8] (see Section 5) – even though we allow the latter to know in advance which elements will be removed from the dataset." However, SIEVE-STREAMING is a streaming algorithm, not a query algorithm. The paper should be comparing against a greedy algorithm that knows E in advance, not SIEVE-STREAMING. The paper's claim about "STAR-T-SIEVE" (running SIEVE-STREAMING on S \ E) is nonsensical since SIEVE-STREAMING is designed for streaming data, not for processing a fixed set.

5. **Formatting and Typographical Errors**: 
   - "st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)" should be "The Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)"
   - "peformance" in Section 5.2 should be "performance"
   - In references, "10, ," is an obvious typo that needs removal
   - "dlog ke" appears 12 times incorrectly (should be "⌊log k⌋" or "⌈log k⌉")

6. **Inconsistent Threshold Definition**: In Algorithm 1, the condition is written as "f(e | Bi,j) ≥ τ/min{2i, k}", but the text states "τ/2^i". This discrepancy must be resolved.

7. **Overstated Memory Claim**: The paper claims "the memory requirement of their method increases linearly with k, in the case of our algorithm this dependence is logarithmic." However, their bound is O((k + m log k) log k), which is linear in k, not logarithmic.

8. **Experimental Figure Reference Issue**: The paper refers to "Figures 2(a,c)" in Section 5.1, but these figures are not included in the provided text, making it impossible to verify the experimental claims.

9. **Misleading Statement**: In Section 5.2, the paper states "we are able to afford running GREEDY, which may be infeasible when working with larger datasets." However, the MovieLens 1M dataset used in experiments is not exceptionally large, and running GREEDY is feasible. This statement misrepresents the practical context.

## Recommendation
The paper presents an interesting algorithmic approach with a novel partitioned thresholding structure. However, the critical technical errors in the definition of τ, the notation inconsistencies, the discrepancy between the abstract and theorem, and the fundamental misunderstanding of SIEVE-STREAMING are too severe to ignore. These are not minor issues but rather undermine the paper's technical credibility.

While the problem is important and the algorithmic ideas show promise, the paper's current form contains multiple serious technical flaws that need to be addressed before it can be considered for publication. The authors should correct the definition of τ, resolve the bound discrepancy, fix all notation issues, and clarify the comparison with SIEVE-STREAMING.

Given the paper's current state, I recommend rejection. The technical flaws are substantial enough to outweigh the paper's contributions, especially since they affect core claims about the algorithm's performance and theoretical guarantees.
