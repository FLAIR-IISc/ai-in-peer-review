- Tackles robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting and proposes a two-stage algorithm (STAR-T).  
- STAR-T builds a single-pass robust summary via partitioned thresholding and then applies greedy selection on the summary.  
- Partitioned thresholding organizes elements into buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds to enable robustness to removals.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: a constant-factor approximation (reported constant ≈ 0.149).  
- Empirical validation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks shows competitive performance and resilience to removals beyond k.  
- Strength: addresses an important, practical problem of robustness in streaming submodular maximization.  
- Strength: introduces a novel partitioned thresholding scheme balancing memory and robustness.  
- Strength: offers rigorous theoretical analysis supporting constant-factor guarantees.  
- Strength: experiments demonstrate practical effectiveness against strong streaming baselines.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, well-organized, with comprehensive related work.  
- Weakness: approximation constant is small (≈0.149) and degrades with dependence on d log k as k grows.  
- Weakness: performance sensitive to memory parameter w; theoretical requirement (w scaling with m/k) may be conservative.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis assumes knowledge of f(OPT(k, V \ E)); the parallel workaround adds overhead and is limiting.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited—more baselines, including non-streaming robust methods, would strengthen evaluation.  
- Quality: technically sound and significant theoretical contribution, but small constant and assumptions temper impact.  
- Clarity: clear exposition, consistent notation, and easy-to-follow presentation.  
- Originality: partitioned thresholding with robustness guarantees is novel and meaningful.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with useful practical demonstrations and reusable summaries for different removals.  
- Pro: novel, theoretically grounded solution with competitive empirical results and clear writing.  
- Con: small approximation constant, limiting theoretical assumptions, and limited baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: solid contribution meriting acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: analyze sensitivity to w, expand comparisons to other robust methods, and acknowledge/outline remedies for the small constant factor.
