Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting with a cardinality constraint. The goal is to compute, in one pass and sublinear memory, a small summary S that is robust to the removal of up to m elements; later, given any removal set E with |E| ≤ m, a solution of size ≤ k must be returned using only S \ E.

The proposed two-stage method consists of:
- STAR-T (streaming stage): maintains a partitioned data structure of buckets whose capacities grow geometrically with the partition index. Partitions are associated with thresholds that (essentially) decrease exponentially with the index. Each arriving element is inserted into the first non-full bucket where its marginal gain exceeds the corresponding threshold.
- STAR-T-GREEDY (query stage): after a removal E, runs GREEDY on S \ E to return a size-k subset.

The analysis shows that for w ≥ ceil(4 log k · m/k) and an appropriate threshold level τ (a constant-factor estimate of OPT), STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements, and STAR-T-GREEDY achieves a constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) · f(OPT(k, V \ E)). When OPT is unknown, a parallel multi-guess variant (as in SIEVE-STREAMING) incurs an additional log_{1+ε} factor in space with a (1+ε)-loss.

The approach extends two strands of prior work: (i) partition-based robust constructions from centralized robust submodular maximization (e.g., PRO [Bogunovic et al., ICML’17]), and (ii) threshold-based streaming submodular maximization (SIEVE-STREAMING [Badanidiyuru et al., KDD’14]). It compares to concurrent deletion-robust streaming work [Mirzasoleiman et al., ICML’17], which attains a 1/2 − ε approximation using O(mk log k/ε) memory; the present paper trades a smaller constant-factor approximation for substantially reduced memory, O(m log^2 k + k log k), and a simple query procedure.

Strengths
- Novel algorithmic structure: the partitioned, geometrically growing buckets with decreasing thresholds is a clean adaptation of partitioned robust summaries to the streaming/thresholding regime.
- Clear memory–approximation trade-off: achieves a constant-factor approximation with memory independent of n and only logarithmic in k for the m-dependent term.
- One-pass algorithm with a simple query stage: GREEDY over S \ E is standard and efficient given the bounded size of S.
- Theoretical guarantees: a rigorous approximation proof via case analysis, balancing lemmas, and careful accounting of removals across partitions/buckets.
- Empirical validation on real datasets (dominating set and personalized recommendation), showing performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING that is advantaged by knowing E in advance.

Weaknesses
- Constant factor is modest (≈0.149 asymptotically), notably below 1/2 − ε from concurrent work [18]; the gain here is memory, but the practical impact of the smaller constant is not explored in depth.
- Dependence on τ (an estimate of OPT) requires multiple parallel instances; the algorithmic and memory overhead of the guessing scheme is mentioned but not evaluated experimentally. Details of the guess range and instance management are deferred to the appendix.
- Time complexity per element: inserting an element may require scanning O(log k) partitions and up to Θ(wk/2^i) buckets per partition; in the worst case this is O(wk) marginal evaluations per element. This is potentially much higher than SIEVE-STREAMING’s O(log k), and empirical runtime is not reported.
- Some notational/clarity issues: “log2 k” seems to mean (log k)^2 in some places but the base-2 logarithm elsewhere; the definition of τ in Theorem 4.1 appears misformatted; thresholds are described as τ/2^i but Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}, which should be clarified.
- Experimental scope: comparisons are limited to SIEVE-STREAMING and random; no comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18]. Sensitivity to w, m, and accuracy of τ-guessing, as well as memory/runtime measurements, are missing. Some experiments operate outside the guaranteed regime (e.g., w=1 with removals > k), which is interesting but should be clearly labeled as beyond-guarantee.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The proof strategy is standard and appears correct at a high level: (i) if many buckets are full in some partition, at least one retains value after deletions; (ii) otherwise, either the last partition’s not-full bucket already captures large value or the remaining OPT must lie in earlier buckets and can be approximated by GREEDY. The balancing of bounds yields the stated constant. Minor issues (τ formatting; threshold notation) should be fixed, but do not seem to undermine the main claims.
- Completeness: The streaming and query stages are fully specified; the unknown-OPT issue is addressed with a parallelization scheme; memory bounds are derived. Runtime analysis and empirical resource usage are not provided.

Clarity
- Generally well-organized with clear algorithms and intuitive explanation. However, clarify:
  - The precise meaning of “log2 k” and use consistent notation.
  - The threshold schedule (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i, k}) and why the plateau in the last partition is intended.
  - The exact definition of τ in Theorem 4.1 (fix typesetting).
  - The choice and impact of w; highlight the regime where w=1 ensures guarantees.

Originality
- The combination of partitioned robust summaries with per-partition thresholding in a streaming setting, and the corresponding analysis for deletion robustness with memory O(m log^2 k + k log k), appears novel relative to prior NIPS/ICML work.

Significance
- Addresses a relevant and timely problem (robustness under deletions in streaming). Improves the space complexity over prior robust streaming work at the expense of approximation constant, offering a distinct point on the trade-off curve. Practical viability would be strengthened by runtime/memory measurements and broader comparisons.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide explicit per-element time complexity and empirical runtime/memory usage versus SIEVE-STREAMING and [18].
- Clarify τ-guessing: range, number of instances, merging logic; include an ablation analyzing accuracy vs space.
- Unify and disambiguate logarithm notation; fix τ typesetting in Theorem 4.1.
- Report sensitivity to m and w; include cases where theoretical guarantees hold and where they do not.
- If feasible, add comparison to the concurrent deletion-robust streaming method [18].

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and principled algorithm with provable guarantees in a challenging setting.
- Strong memory efficiency: O(m log^2 k + k log k) vs O(mk log k) in concurrent work.
- One-pass algorithm and simple query stage; relevant applications demonstrated.
- Sound analysis and clear presentation overall.

Arguments against acceptance
- Approximation constant is small; no empirical exploration of the trade-off vs 1/2 − ε approaches.
- Lack of runtime and memory measurements; potential per-element cost may limit scalability.
- Some clarity issues and reliance on appendix for key practical details (τ-guessing).
- Experimental comparisons limited; no evaluation against concurrent robust streaming methods.
