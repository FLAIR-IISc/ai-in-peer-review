- Addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings with a two-stage approach: STAR-T (streaming summary) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query after removals).  
- Key innovation: partitioned thresholding structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary using sublinear memory.  
- Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and centralized robust work [11], extending them to streaming with a new bucket-filling strategy.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis and proof of the approximation guarantee (Theorem 4.1), handling cases of full vs. mostly empty partitions.  
- Offers a parallel variant (Theorem 4.5) to handle unknown f(OPT) achieving a (1+ε)-approximation with modest memory overhead.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, comparing against SIEVE-STREAMING and other baselines.  
- Empirical results show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms baselines, even against adversarial removals and compared to methods that know removed elements.  
- The proved approximation ratio (~0.149 as k→∞) is substantially lower than the non-robust greedy guarantee (≈0.632), and the paper lacks sufficient intuition for this gap.  
- The dependence on 1/dlog k in the theoretical bound and its practical implications are not clearly explained.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and provides helpful proof sketches and illustrative figures.  
- The streaming adaptation and partitioned thresholding are novel and constitute a significant contribution relative to prior centralized and concurrent work.  
- Memory complexity O((k + m log k) log² k) improves on concurrent work [18]’s dependence on k.  
- The two-stage framework is practically significant for interactive, large-scale applications where reoptimization is costly.  
- Recommendation: accept, with the main revision suggestion to better explain the low approximation guarantee and the inherent limits imposed by robustness requirements.
