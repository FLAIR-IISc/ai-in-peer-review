- Summary: Proposes STAR-T (streaming summary S of size O((k + m log k) log k)) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query after up to m removals) using partitioned thresholding with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and decreasing thresholds, extending SIEVE-STREAMING to robust streaming.

- Theoretical quality: Strong technical analysis with well-proven main result (Theorem 4.1) and lemmas covering partition cases; parallel variant handles unknown τ at cost of extra log factor in memory.

- Empirical quality: Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows removed elements), supporting theoretical claims.

- Main weakness: Low approximation factor (0.149) compared to non-robust streaming algorithms (≈0.5), and limited comparison to other robust methods ([11], [18]).

- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with precise pseudocode and detailed proofs, but Lemmas 4.3–4.4 need more intuitive discussion.

- Suggested clarifications: Provide more detail on τ approximation, practical guidance for setting parameters (e.g., w), and clarify relation to greedy bucket filling in [11].

- Originality: Novel partitioned thresholding scheme that differs from prior bucket-filling approaches and achieves memory O((k + m log k) log k) versus linear-in-k memory of [18].

- Significance: Addresses an important real-world gap—robustness in streaming submodular maximization—with practical two-stage solution and modest memory overhead.

- Overall assessment: Solid, novel contribution with convincing theory and experiments despite lower approximation factor; worthwhile trade-off for robustness in many applications.

- Recommendation: Accept — first streaming algorithm offering constant-factor robust guarantee with logarithmic dependence on k; practical and of interest to the community.
