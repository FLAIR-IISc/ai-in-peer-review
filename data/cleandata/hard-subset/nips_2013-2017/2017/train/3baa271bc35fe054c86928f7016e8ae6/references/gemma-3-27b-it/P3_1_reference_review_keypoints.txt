- Proposes STAR-T: a two-stage streaming/query algorithm that builds a compact, robust summary via partitioned thresholding.
- Uses exponentially decreasing thresholds in the streaming phase to balance summary size and robustness.
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee (≈0.149 in the limit as k increases).
- Validates approach experimentally on graph dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Demonstrates competitive performance against SIEVE-STREAMING and other baselines and reuse of the summary for multiple removals.
- Situates work within streaming and robust submodular optimization literature and notes related areas like influence maximization.
- Acknowledges concurrent work [18] and discusses memory trade-offs relative to prior approaches.
- Strength: two-stage streaming/query formulation is realistic and practical for dynamic data.
- Strength: partitioned thresholding is an innovative way to create a compact, robust summary.
- Strength: theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a solid foundation.
- Strength: experiments are diverse and well-conducted, supporting practical effectiveness.
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and organized.
- Weakness: approximation constant (0.149) is low, raising practical utility concerns.
- Weakness: performance sensitive to parameters (w and τ), with τ requiring hard-to-obtain estimates.
- Weakness: parallel τ-estimation adds significant computational and space overhead (log(1+k) factor).
- Weakness: limited empirical exploration of w’s impact on performance and memory trade-offs.
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison to PRO despite structural similarities.
- Recommendation: solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity and provide a more comprehensive comparison to PRO.
