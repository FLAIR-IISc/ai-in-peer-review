- Addresses robust submodular maximization in streaming settings: build a small summary S that remains effective after up to m removals.  
- Proposes a two-stage approach: streaming algorithm STAR-T to build S and query algorithm STAR-T-GREEDY to return a solution from S \ E.  
- Key innovation: partitioned thresholding with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and decreasing greedy thresholds.  
- Achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149 and space O((k + m log k) log k) (paper abstract incorrectly states log² k).  
- Builds on prior work in robust and streaming submodular optimization and relates to recent concurrent deletion-robust work.  

- Strength: clear theoretical contribution extending streaming submodular optimization to the robust setting with novel analysis.  
- Strength: practical relevance for large, dynamic, and deletion-prone applications (e.g., influence maximization, recommendations).  
- Strength: experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show competitive or better performance vs SIEVE-STREAMING, even when that baseline knows deletions.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, organized, and provides clear algorithms and proofs.  

- Weakness: approximation factor 0.149 is much lower than standard SIEVE-STREAMING’s ~0.5, and the practical acceptability of this trade-off needs more discussion.  
- Weakness: discrepancy in reported memory complexity (abstract/conclusion vs Theorem 4.1) needs clarification.  
- Weakness: insufficient contrast with concurrent work [18] that claims 1/2-ε with O(mk log k/ε) memory; trade-offs are not fully explained.  
- Weakness: theory requires w ≥ ⌈4 d log k e m / k⌉ while experiments use w = 1; this gap should be addressed.  
- Weakness: experimental description lacks sufficient detail on how τ was approximated in practice.  

- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution, sound analysis, strong experiments, and clear writing despite lower approximation factor.  

- Specific request: correct memory complexity to O((k + m log k) log k) throughout the paper.  
- Specific request: provide a more detailed memory vs approximation comparison with [18], especially for large k.  
- Specific request: explain why w = 1 works in practice despite theoretical requirements.  
- Specific request: detail how τ was estimated in experiments (number of parallel instances and achieved approximation for f(OPT(k, V \ E))).  
- Specific request: give concrete approximation-factor examples for typical k values (e.g., k = 100) for practitioners.  

- Final thought: paper is a valuable, well-supported contribution to streaming submodular optimization with robustness focus and should be accepted after the suggested clarifications.
