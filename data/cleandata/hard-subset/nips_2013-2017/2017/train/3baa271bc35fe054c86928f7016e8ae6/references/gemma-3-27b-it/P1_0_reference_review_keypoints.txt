- Tackles robust submodular maximization in streaming via a two-stage algorithm (STAR-T): streaming summary construction then greedy on summary for removals.  
- Introduces a novel partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially decreasing thresholds for the streaming phase.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149) and bounds on summary memory size.  
- Empirical evaluation on social network and MovieLens 1M datasets shows competitive performance versus SIEVE-STREAMING, RANDOM, and STAR-T-SIEVE.  
- Positions the work within prior streaming and robust submodular literature, building on SIEVE-STREAMING and partitioning ideas like PRO but with a different thresholding/analysis.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant and challenging problem (streaming robustness) with practical importance.  
- Strength: novel algorithmic idea (partitioned thresholding) balancing robustness and space.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundations and explicit memory bounds.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and effective figures; generally clear presentation.  
- Strength: supports efficient interactive handling of multiple removal queries from a single summary.  
- Weakness: approximation constant is small (0.149) and degrades with log k, raising practical-performance concerns.  
- Weakness: algorithm depends on parameters (w, τ); accurately estimating τ without knowing OPT is challenging despite partial theoretical remedies.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons may be limited/unfair (SIEVE-STREAMING sometimes knows removals) and MovieLens 1M is small for scalability claims.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is complex and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.  
- Weakness: inadequate discussion of the trade-offs and impact of choosing different w values.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, experimentally competitive, and valuable for large-scale summarization/robust ML.  
- Against acceptance: small constant guarantee, parameter tuning demands, and analytical complexity could hinder adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions, asking for better practical τ estimation, discussion of w trade-offs, larger-scale experiments, and more intuition in the proofs.
