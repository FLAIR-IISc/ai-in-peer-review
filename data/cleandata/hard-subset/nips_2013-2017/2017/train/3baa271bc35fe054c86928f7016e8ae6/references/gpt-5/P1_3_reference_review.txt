Summary and relation to prior work
This paper studies robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting under a cardinality constraint. The goal is to construct, in one pass and sublinear memory, a summary S from which, after the adversarial removal of up to m elements (unknown at stream time), one can extract in the query stage a size-k set whose value approximates OPT(k, V\E). The authors propose STAR-T (Streaming Robust submodular algorithm with Partitioned Thresholding) for the streaming stage and STAR-T-GREEDY for the query stage. STAR-T organizes retained elements into partitions of exponentially increasing bucket capacities, with exponentially decreasing thresholds per partition; each arriving element is inserted into the first non-full bucket where its marginal gain exceeds the bucket’s threshold. STAR-T-GREEDY then runs GREEDY on S\E to output the final k-set.

The main theoretical result shows that, with w ≥ ceil(4 m log k / k) parallel buckets per partition, STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements when τ is set from OPT; with a standard multi-guessing scheme for τ, total memory becomes O((k + m log k) log^2 k), and STAR-T-GREEDY yields a constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) f(OPT(k, V\E)). The analysis hinges on a dichotomy: either some partition has many full buckets (yielding a near-τ value after deletions), or the last partition’s best non-full bucket controls the value; balancing two lemmas yields the bound.

This builds on and contrasts with: (i) Greedy’s (1−1/e) approximation but multi-pass memory usage [Nemhauser et al.], (ii) SIEVE-STREAMING’s 0.5−ε non-robust, single-pass solution [Badanidiyuru et al.], (iii) robust non-streaming work (Krause et al., Orlin et al., PRO [Bogunovic et al. 2017]) that uses non-uniform partitions but is not streaming, and (iv) concurrent deletion-robust streaming [Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017], which achieves 1/2−ε but needs O(m k log k/ε) memory. This paper’s memory depends only logarithmically on k (at the expense of a smaller constant factor), and linearly in m up to log factors.

Quality
– Theory: The proof structure is sensible and uses standard submodular/greedy tools in a novel way with the partitioned thresholding. The main trade-offs (approximation vs memory) are clearly articulated. However, there are clarity issues in the statement of thresholds: the text states thresholds τ/2^i whereas Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}; this should be reconciled and justified in the analysis (the latter seems necessary when 2^i > k). Also, the formula for τ in Theorem 4.1 appears misprinted; please restate τ cleanly and explain the dependence. The factor 0.149 is conservative; it would help to discuss whether it is an artifact of analysis (e.g., balancing lemmas) and whether tighter constants are plausible.

– Experiments: The two applications (dominating set on Twitter/Amazon graphs and MovieLens recommendations) are relevant and show that STAR-T-GREEDY performs comparably to baselines, even giving SIEVE-STREAMING the advantage of knowing deletions E. The interactive reuse of S across multiple E is compelling. Missing, however, are: (a) comparisons to the concurrent deletion-robust streaming algorithm [18], at least in memory/quality trade-offs; (b) systematic variation of m, w, and k to demonstrate the predicted scaling; (c) runtime/memory measurements; and (d) stronger adversaries (beyond removing 2k) and worst-case removals targeted at the partition structure.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized and readable, with clear algorithms and a high-level proof roadmap. Specific clarity issues to fix:
– Threshold inconsistency: text (τ/2^i) vs Algorithm 1 (τ/min{2^i, k}); use one and justify.
– The definition of τ in Theorem 4.1 is garbled; rewrite with parentheses and constants.
– Be explicit about log bases and the ceiling notation dlog ke, and the factor (1 − 1/dlog ke).
– Reconcile memory claims consistently across Intro, Theorem 4.1, Theorem 4.5, and Conclusion (known τ gives O((k + m log k) log k); unknown τ adds another O(log k)).
– Minor typos (e.g., references formatting, spacing, “st Conference” in the header).

Originality
The partitioned-threshold streaming structure and the robust analysis are novel relative to SIEVE-STREAMING and PRO. The work tackles a timely robust variant in streaming, with a different point in the memory–approximation trade-off space than [18].

Significance
Robust streaming summarization is important in large-scale ML systems with deletions/right-to-be-forgotten or interactive personalization. The algorithm is practical (one pass, small memory, fast query via GREEDY on S\E), and the experimental sections suggest it works well in practice. Although the constant 0.149 is modest, the method’s memory efficiency and simplicity make it likely to be adopted or built upon.

Pros
– One-pass, memory-efficient robust streaming algorithm with principled partitioned thresholding.
– Constant-factor guarantee independent of n, with memory O((k + m log k) log^2 k) without OPT.
– Simple and practical query stage; reusable summary supports multiple deletions E.
– Empirical evidence on real datasets showing competitive performance versus strong baselines.
– Clear connection to and extension of prior partitioning ideas (PRO) to the streaming regime.

Cons
– Approximation constant is small; no empirical exploration of quality vs w or thresholds.
– No empirical comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18]; limited ablations.
– Some presentation inconsistencies (threshold definition, τ formula, memory factors).
– Limited analysis of runtime/memory and sensitivity to m, k; no lower bounds.

Suggestions for improvement
– Fix threshold and τ specification; provide a short intuition for τ selection and its estimation via parallel guesses, including the overhead constant.
– Add experiments varying m and w to show robustness–memory trade-offs; report memory and time.
– Include an empirical comparison or a detailed discussion with [18] to position contributions.
– Explore stronger adversarial deletions tailored to the partition structure; report robustness beyond the theoretical regime.
– Provide ablations: e.g., uniform thresholds vs partitioned thresholds; GREEDY vs SIEVE in the query stage; effect of bucket multiplicity w.

Overall recommendation: Weak accept. The paper addresses an important problem with a novel and well-analyzed algorithmic design, offers good memory–quality trade-offs, and demonstrates practical utility. Addressing the clarity issues and strengthening the empirical evaluation would further solidify the contribution.
