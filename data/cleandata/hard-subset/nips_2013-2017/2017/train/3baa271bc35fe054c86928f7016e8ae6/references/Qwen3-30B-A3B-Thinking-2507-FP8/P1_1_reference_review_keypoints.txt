- Paper proposes a streaming two-stage algorithm (STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization with single-pass summary construction and post-removal greedy query, achieving a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 and summary size O((k + m log k) log² k).

- Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular literature, introducing partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds to obtain robustness in the streaming model.

- Theoretical contribution: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with a provable constant-factor approximation and novel partitioned thresholding structure.

- Practical validation: experiments on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation show the approach matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removals).

- Clear organization and thorough theoretical analysis, with multiple lemmas covering different proof cases.

- Addresses an important gap between offline robust submodular optimization and non-robust streaming approaches.

- Weakness: low approximation factor (≈0.149) compared to standard and some streaming results (e.g., ~0.5).

- Weakness: practical implementation complexity requires multiple parallel instances to approximate f(OPT(k, V\E)), increasing space by a log₁₊ᵋ k factor.

- Weakness: limited comparison with concurrent work [18]; deeper analysis of memory vs. approximation trade-offs is lacking.

- Weakness: no discussion of tightness or lower bounds for the O((k + m log k) log² k) memory bound.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, significant contribution bridging streaming and robustness with convincing experiments.

- Recommendation: accept.

- Pro: solves a practical problem, offers a novel theoretically-grounded approach, and demonstrates strong experimental utility.

- Con: relatively low approximation factor, requires multiple parallel instances, and memory complexity may be high for very large datasets.
