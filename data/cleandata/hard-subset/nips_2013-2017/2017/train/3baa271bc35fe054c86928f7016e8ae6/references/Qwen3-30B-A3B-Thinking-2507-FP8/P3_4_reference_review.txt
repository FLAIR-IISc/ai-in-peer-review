

# Peer Review: "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach"

## Summary
This paper introduces a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization in streaming settings. The authors propose a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that constructs a robust summary set S that can withstand removal of up to m elements, followed by a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that produces a size-k solution from S after any such removal. The theoretical contribution is a constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149) with a summary size of O((k + m log k) log k). The work builds on SIEVE-STREAMING [8] and extends it to the robust setting, while also drawing inspiration from PRO [11] for the partitioned structure.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Algorithmic Design**: The partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds across partitions is cleverly designed to handle robustness constraints. The bucket structure with size increasing exponentially with partition index is particularly elegant.

2. **Comprehensive Theoretical Analysis**: The paper provides a detailed and rigorous analysis with multiple lemmas addressing different cases in the partition structure. The proof sketch demonstrates careful handling of the robustness requirement.

3. **Practical Validation**: The experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks convincingly show that STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows in advance which elements will be removed), demonstrating the practical viability of the approach.

4. **Clear Problem Formulation**: The paper clearly articulates the two-stage robust streaming problem and its practical motivation, distinguishing it from standard submodular maximization.

## Weaknesses
1. **Weak Theoretical Guarantee**: The approximation factor of 0.149 is significantly weaker than the standard greedy (0.632) and even weaker than SIEVE-STREAMING (0.5-ε). The paper doesn't adequately address why such a weak guarantee is acceptable for practical applications. This is a major limitation that needs more justification.

2. **Misleading Comparison**: The claim that "our procedure matches (and in some cases outperforms) the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm [8] even though we allow the latter to know in advance which elements will be removed" is misleading. The comparison should be against a robust version of SIEVE-STREAMING, not the standard version that doesn't account for robustness.

3. **Practical Assumption of Known f(OPT)**: The main algorithm assumes knowledge of f(OPT(k, V\E)), which is unrealistic. While the paper addresses this with a parallel variant, the space complexity becomes O((k + m log k) log k log_{1+ε} k), which is significantly larger than the main algorithm and not well-justified in terms of practical trade-offs.

4. **Inconsistent Memory Claim**: The paper claims "our algorithm uses O(m log^2 k) memory" but the main theorem states O((k + m log k) log k). The difference between log^2 k and (k + m log k) log k is significant, especially for large k.

5. **Limited Comparison with Robust Algorithms**: The paper mentions [18] as a concurrent work but doesn't compare against it directly. A comparison with this alternative robust streaming algorithm would strengthen the paper.

## Overall Assessment
This paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field of streaming submodular optimization by addressing the robustness requirement in a streaming setting. The algorithmic design is novel and the theoretical analysis is sound. However, the weak approximation guarantee (0.149) is a significant drawback that needs more justification. The comparison with SIEVE-STREAMING is misleading, and the practical assumption about knowing f(OPT) needs better justification.

The paper is technically sound and well-written, but the theoretical guarantee is too weak to be practically compelling. For robustness to be useful, the approximation guarantee should be closer to the standard 0.632. The paper would be much stronger if it could demonstrate a better approximation factor while maintaining the same robustness properties.

## Recommendation
**Weak Accept**

The paper presents a novel approach to robust submodular maximization with a well-constructed theoretical analysis and practical validation. However, the weak approximation guarantee (0.149) is a major limitation that needs to be addressed. With stronger theoretical guarantees or better justification for the weak guarantee, this could be a strong paper. As is, it makes a solid contribution but doesn't advance the state-of-the-art sufficiently to warrant a strong accept.

The authors should address the weak approximation factor, clarify the comparison with SIEVE-STREAMING, and provide a more realistic discussion of the f(OPT) assumption. These improvements would significantly strengthen the paper.
