### Summary and Relation to Prior Work

This paper addresses the problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The goal is to select a small, robust summary `S` from a large dataset in a single pass, such that even after an adversary removes up to `m` elements from `S`, a near-optimal solution of size `k` can still be extracted from the remainder. The authors propose a two-stage procedure: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) to build the summary, and a simple greedy query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) to extract the final solution.

The proposed STAR-T algorithm is the core contribution. It processes the stream and populates a structured summary `S` consisting of multiple partitions. Each partition contains a number of "buckets" and is associated with an exponentially decreasing threshold. An incoming element is placed into the first available bucket for which its marginal gain exceeds the corresponding partition's threshold. This design elegantly combines ideas from prior work: the thresholding principle is reminiscent of SIEVE-STREAMING [8] for non-robust streaming maximization, while the partitioned structure for robustness builds upon the approach of PRO [11] for the non-streaming robust setting. This paper's novelty lies in synthesizing these concepts into a new algorithm for the combined streaming-robust setting and providing a novel analysis for it.

The work is also compared to a concurrent paper by Mirzasoleiman et al. [18]. A key differentiator highlighted is the memory complexity: this paper achieves a memory size of `O((k + m log k) log k)`, which has a more favorable logarithmic dependence on `k` compared to the linear dependence in the concurrent work.

### Strengths

1.  **Problem Significance:** The paper tackles a highly relevant and practical problem. In many large-scale applications, data arrives as a stream, and robustness against removals (due to system failures, user feedback, or adversarial attacks) is a critical requirement. This work is among the first to provide a provably-good algorithm for this challenging intersection of streaming processing and robust optimization.

2.  **Novel Algorithm and Analysis:** The STAR-T algorithm is an intelligent combination and extension of existing ideas. The partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds is a clever way to ensure that elements with varying marginal gains are captured, providing the necessary diversity for robustness. The theoretical analysis, sketched in the paper, appears sound and provides the first constant-factor approximation guarantee for this problem setting. The technique of balancing two different lower bounds on the solution quality (derived from cases where partitions are mostly full vs. mostly not-full) is a non-trivial analytical contribution.

3.  **Strong Memory Scalability:** The memory complexity of `O((k + m log k) log k)` is a significant strength, particularly its `log k` dependence on the solution size `k`. In many applications, `k` can be large, and avoiding a linear dependence is crucial for scalability. This gives the proposed method a distinct advantage over the concurrent work [18].

4.  **Convincing Experimental Validation:** The empirical evaluation is well-designed and compelling.
    *   The choice of baseline (SIEVE-STREAMING) is strong. Crucially, the authors give the baseline an unrealistic advantage by allowing it to know the removed set `E` in advance. The fact that STAR-T performs comparably or even better under these conditions strongly supports its effectiveness.
    *   The experiments cover two distinct and relevant applications (dominating set and personalized recommendation), demonstrating the method's versatility.
    *   The inclusion of both random and adversarial (greedy) removals in the dominating set experiment effectively tests the algorithm's robustness under different failure models.
    *   The interactive recommendation scenario highlights a key practical advantage: a single precomputed summary `S` can be used to respond to multiple, diverse queries (e.g., filtering by genre, removing seen movies), which is highly efficient.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Modest Approximation Constant:** The theoretical approximation guarantee of `c ≈ 0.149` is quite low. While obtaining any constant factor in this difficult setting is a significant achievement, this value may be too small for applications that require solutions very close to optimal. The authors are transparent about this, but it is a limitation of the current analysis.

2.  **Dependence on Unknown OPT Value:** The algorithm, in its basic form, requires an estimate of `f(OPT(k, V \ E))` to set the threshold `τ`. The proposed solution—running multiple instances in parallel with geometrically increasing guesses for `τ`—is a standard technique adapted from [8]. While effective, it increases the memory and complexity by a logarithmic factor (`log_{1+\epsilon} k`) and feels more like a wrapper than an integrated solution.

3.  **Clarity of Pseudocode:** There is a minor inconsistency between the description of the threshold in the text and in Algorithm 1. The text states the threshold decreases as `τ/2^i`, while the pseudocode uses `τ/min{2^i, k}`. While these are nearly identical for `2^i < k`, this could be clarified to ensure precise reproducibility. This is a very minor point that can be easily fixed.

### Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of large-scale submodular optimization. It introduces a novel and practical problem (robust submodular maximization in a single pass) and provides the first algorithm with a constant-factor approximation guarantee and excellent memory scalability. The combination of a novel algorithm, solid theoretical analysis, and strong empirical results makes this a complete and compelling piece of work. The weaknesses identified, such as the low constant and reliance on a standard technique for estimating OPT, are relatively minor when weighed against the importance of the problem being solved and the novelty of the proposed solution. The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way. It is very likely that other researchers will build upon these ideas. I strongly recommend acceptance.
