

# Peer Review

## Summary

This paper addresses the problem of robust submodular maximization in streaming settings, where the goal is to find a small representative subset of elements that remains effective even after up to m elements are removed. The authors propose a two-stage approach: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that constructs a robust summary set S in a single pass, and a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that, given any removed set E, returns a solution from S \ E.

The key innovation is a partitioned thresholding approach where the summary is arranged into partitions with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds. This enables the authors to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149) for the robust setting with O((k + m log k) log k) space complexity (not log² k as stated in the abstract). The paper builds upon related work on robust submodular maximization [9-11] and streaming submodular optimization [8, 12-13], and relates to recent concurrent work on deletion-robust submodular maximization [18].

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Contribution**: The paper makes a clear and significant theoretical contribution by extending streaming submodular optimization to the robust setting with a constant-factor approximation guarantee. The partitioned thresholding approach is novel and the analysis carefully combines properties of the robust structure with decreasing greedy thresholding.

2. **Practical Relevance**: The two-stage framework is highly relevant to real-world applications where data is large and dynamic, and robustness against element removal is critical (e.g., social network influence maximization, personalized recommendations).

3. **Experimental Validation**: The experiments on two distinct applications (dominating set and movie recommendation) demonstrate that the proposed approach matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING, even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows in advance which elements will be removed. The results are well-presented and convincingly support the theoretical claims.

4. **Clarity and Organization**: The paper is exceptionally well-written with a logical flow from problem statement to theoretical results to experiments. Algorithm descriptions are clear and the proofs are well-structured.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Bound**: The approximation factor of 0.149 is significantly lower than the 0.5-ε approximation achieved by SIEVE-STREAMING for the standard problem. While the authors justify this by the added robustness requirement, the paper could better explain why this trade-off is acceptable in practical scenarios.

2. **Memory Complexity Discrepancy**: The paper states the memory complexity as O((k + m log k) log² k) in the abstract and conclusion, but Theorem 4.1 shows |S| ≤ (log k + 5)wk, which is O((k + m log k) log k). This discrepancy should be clarified.

3. **Comparison with Concurrent Work**: The paper mentions [18] as a concurrent work achieving 1/2-ε approximation with O(mk log k/ε) memory, but doesn't fully contrast the trade-offs between the two approaches. The paper should more thoroughly explain why O(m log² k) memory dependence is more favorable than O(mk log k) when k is large.

4. **Parameter Selection**: The paper states that w = 1 works well in practice, but the theoretical analysis requires w ≥ ⌈4dlog kem/k⌉. This gap between theory and practice should be better addressed.

5. **Experimental Details**: The paper doesn't provide sufficient detail on how τ was approximated in practice. The description in Section 4.1 of using parallel instances is vague in the experimental context.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The work makes a significant contribution to the field of streaming submodular optimization by addressing the important robustness requirement. The theoretical analysis is sound, the experimental validation is strong, and the paper is clearly written. While the approximation factor is lower than standard streaming results, this is justified by the added robustness requirement, and the memory complexity advantage over related work is compelling.

## Specific Comments for Authors

1. **Clarify Memory Complexity**: Please correct the stated memory complexity to O((k + m log k) log k) throughout the paper to match the proof in Theorem 4.1.

2. **Enhance Comparison with [18]**: Provide a more detailed comparison of the memory-approximation trade-off with the concurrent work [18], especially for large k values.

3. **Address w Parameter Discrepancy**: Explain why w = 1 works well in practice despite the theoretical requirement. This would strengthen the practical relevance of your results.

4. **Detail τ Estimation in Experiments**: Specify how τ was approximated in the experiments. How many parallel instances were used? What was the actual approximation ratio achieved for f(OPT(k, V \ E))?

5. **Approximation Factor Clarification**: Consider stating the exact approximation factor for typical values of k (e.g., k=100) to provide more concrete guidance for practitioners.

## Final Thoughts

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of streaming algorithms for submodular maximization. The robustness requirement addressed is highly relevant to real-world applications, and the proposed partitioned thresholding approach is both novel and effective. The theoretical results are well-supported by experiments, and the paper is exceptionally well-written. With the suggested clarifications, this would be an excellent contribution to NIPS.

I believe this paper would be of significant interest to the NIPS community and should be accepted for publication.
