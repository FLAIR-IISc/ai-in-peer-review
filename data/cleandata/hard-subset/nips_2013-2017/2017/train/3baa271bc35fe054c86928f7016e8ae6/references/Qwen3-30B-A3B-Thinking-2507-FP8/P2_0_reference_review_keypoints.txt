- Addresses robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting with a practical two-stage procedure (STAR-T summary + STAR-T-GREEDY query).
- Introduces a partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust summary in one pass.
- Theoretical guarantees: summary size O((k + m log k) log² k) and query-stage constant-factor approximation 0.149(1 − 1/log k).
- Builds on and extends prior streaming and robust submodular work (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING), handling robustness in the streaming context for the first time.
- Provides a practical extension to approximate f(OPT(k, V\E)) via parallel instances to obtain a (1+ε)-approximation.
- Proofs are rigorous and carefully handled via case analysis; full proofs in supplementary material.
- Experimental validation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks compares against SIEVE-STREAMING, RANDOM, and STAR-T-SIEVE.
- Empirical results show competitive or better performance than baselines, even when baselines know removed elements in advance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with precise algorithms, helpful figures, and self-contained exposition.
- Novelty lies in combining robustness with streaming and in the partitioned thresholding analysis; memory dependence improves over concurrent work.
- Significance: important for large-scale ML applications requiring memory-efficient, robust summaries (summarization, influence maximization, recommendations).
- Main limitation is the small constant-factor approximation (0.149), acknowledged as inherent to robustness, but practical results mitigate concern.
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-motivated, technically sound, and significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
