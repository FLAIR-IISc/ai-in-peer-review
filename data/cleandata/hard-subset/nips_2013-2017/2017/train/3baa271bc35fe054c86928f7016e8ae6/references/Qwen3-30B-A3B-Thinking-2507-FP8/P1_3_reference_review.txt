

# Peer Review: Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach

## Summary
This paper addresses the important problem of robust submodular maximization in streaming settings, where the goal is to create a small representative subset of elements that remains effective even after the removal of up to m elements. The authors propose a two-stage approach: (1) a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that constructs a robust summary S in one pass over the data, and (2) a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that, given any removed set E of size m, returns a high-quality subset from S\E. The key innovation is a partitioned thresholding structure where the summary is arranged into partitions with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds. The authors prove that their method achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee (c = 0.149) while using O((k + m log k) log k) memory. This work extends prior streaming submodular maximization approaches like SIEVE-STREAMING [8] to the robust setting, while outperforming concurrent work [18] in terms of memory requirements.

## Strengths

1. **Strong Theoretical Contribution**: The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with a clear approximation guarantee (c = 0.149). The proof strategy is novel, combining properties of the partitioned thresholding structure with the decreasing greedy thresholding rule. The authors carefully handle the challenge of unknown f(OPT(k, V\E)) by extending the approach of [8] with multiple parallel instances.

2. **Practical Relevance**: The problem of robust data summarization is highly relevant to real-world applications like social network influence maximization and personalized recommendation systems. The paper demonstrates the approach's effectiveness on two concrete applications (dominating set and movie recommendation), showing performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows removals in advance).

3. **Clear Differentiation from Related Work**: The paper thoroughly compares with prior work on robust submodular maximization ([9, 10, 11]) and streaming submodular maximization ([8, 12, 13]), clearly explaining how their approach advances the state of the art. The memory complexity (O((k + m log k) log k)) is superior to concurrent work [18] which requires O(mk log k/ε) memory.

4. **Well-Structured Experiments**: The experimental validation is comprehensive, covering both random and greedy removal scenarios, and demonstrating the practical utility of a single robust summary for multiple query scenarios (e.g., removing movies by genre or already-seen movies in the recommendation application).

## Weaknesses

1. **Approximation Factor**: The approximation factor (0.149) is lower than some prior work (SIEVE-STREAMING achieves 0.5-ε), which is expected due to the robustness requirement but should be more explicitly discussed. While the paper acknowledges this, a more detailed comparison of the trade-offs between robustness and approximation quality would strengthen the contribution.

2. **Theoretical Complexity**: The proof is quite intricate (Lemma 4.2-4.4), and while the paper provides proof sketches, the full details are relegated to supplementary material. This may make the paper less accessible to some readers, particularly those without deep expertise in submodular optimization.

3. **Limited Experimental Scope**: While the two applications are well-chosen, the paper doesn't explore how the method performs with different types of submodular functions beyond the specific examples. More diverse experiments would strengthen the practical claim.

## Evaluation

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound with a well-supported theoretical analysis. The authors carefully address the challenges of the robust streaming setting and provide a complete solution with both theoretical guarantees and empirical validation. The experimental design is sound, and the results consistently support the theoretical claims. The paper is careful to acknowledge limitations, particularly regarding the approximation factor.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The algorithms are presented in pseudocode with sufficient detail. The theoretical results are explained with proof sketches, though the full proofs are in the supplementary material. The experimental section is thorough and well-illustrated. A minor suggestion would be to provide a more intuitive explanation of the partitioned thresholding structure early in the paper.

**Originality**: The paper makes a clear and significant contribution by extending submodular streaming to the robust setting. The partitioned thresholding approach with exponentially decreasing thresholds is novel and well-motivated. The two-stage framework for robust streaming submodular maximization represents a meaningful advance over prior work, particularly in terms of memory efficiency.

**Significance**: The problem addressed has significant practical importance in large-scale machine learning applications where robustness to element removal is critical. The streaming aspect makes the approach applicable to massive datasets. The constant-factor approximation guarantee is valuable for practitioners, and the experimental results demonstrate clear practical utility. The method's ability to use a single precomputed summary for multiple query scenarios (e.g., different removal sets) is particularly valuable for interactive applications.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of streaming submodular optimization with a novel approach to robustness. The theoretical guarantees are solid, the experimental validation is thorough, and the practical significance is clear. While the approximation factor is lower than some non-robust methods, this is expected given the added robustness constraint, and the authors properly contextualize this trade-off.

The paper advances the state of the art in robust submodular maximization by providing the first streaming algorithm with a constant-factor approximation guarantee. It clearly differentiates itself from prior work, including concurrent contributions, and demonstrates practical utility through well-designed experiments.

**Recommendation**: Accept

**Key Strengths**: Novel partitioned thresholding approach, strong theoretical guarantees for robust streaming submodular maximization, practical validation on two important applications, clear comparison with prior work.

**Key Weaknesses**: Approximation factor lower than non-robust methods (expected), proof complexity could be simplified for broader accessibility.
