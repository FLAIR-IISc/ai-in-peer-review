- Tackles robust streaming submodular maximization with practical applications (summarization, recommendations, influence maximization).
- Proposes STAR-T: a two-stage algorithm using partitioned structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding to build robust summaries.
- STAR-T-GREEDY query stage finds solutions efficiently even after removals from the summary.
- Builds on prior streaming methods (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and extends robust submodular maximization to streaming settings.
- Novel combination of partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds for robustness in streaming contexts.
- Provides a constant-factor theoretical approximation guarantee for the algorithm.
- Empirical results on dominating set and movie recommendation show competitive or superior performance, sometimes outperforming methods with removal knowledge.
- Algorithm is practically relevant due to ability to handle removals from precomputed summaries.
- Parameter w (memory allocation) is sensitive and requires careful, instance-dependent tuning; guidance on choosing w is insufficient.
- Some proof sketches (notably Section 4) are under-explained and need more elaboration for full accessibility.
- Experimental comparisons could be deeper; lacks detailed ablation studying partitioning and thresholding effects.
- Stated space complexity O((k + m log k) log k) can be high and problematic for large m.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees, and supportive empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter sensitivity limits practicality, proofs are dense, and space complexity may be too large for big m.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution, theoretically sound and empirically validated; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, tuning, and complexity concerns.
