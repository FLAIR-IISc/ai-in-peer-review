- Problem: Tackles robust streaming submodular maximization—summarizing large datasets efficiently while tolerating arbitrary removals.  
- Proposed method: STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm using novel partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a constant-factor approximation after arbitrary removal of m elements (approximation constant c = 0.149).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on greedy and streaming algorithms (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and robust submodular maximization literature, adapting these to the streaming+robust setting.  
- Technical quality: Analysis appears rigorous and components (partitioning, thresholding) are well-defined; proofs seem plausible though supplementary verification is needed.  
- Experimental validation: Empirical results support theoretical claims and show competitive performance vs. baselines, including SIEVE-STREAMING given removal info.  
- Practical parameter concern: Experiments use w = 1 despite theoretical requirement w ≥ ⌈4d log k e m / k⌉; this discrepancy needs clearer justification and may indicate sensitivity to tuning.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear problem statement and algorithm description, though variable-heavy notation can be overwhelming.  
- Intuition: Would benefit from more intuitive explanations (e.g., rationale behind exponentially decreasing thresholds).  
- Originality: Novel integration of streaming, robustness, and submodular maximization with new partitioning/thresholding tailored to the setting.  
- Significance: High practical relevance—robust streaming summaries with constant-factor guarantees are valuable for real-world applications (e.g., recommendations, influence maximization).  
- Strengths for acceptance: Well-motivated, technically sound, good experiments, clear presentation, and significant contribution to submodular optimization.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: Need justification for experimental parameter choice, potential need for deeper proof scrutiny, and relatively small approximation constant.  
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance based on solid contributions outweighing the minor weaknesses.
