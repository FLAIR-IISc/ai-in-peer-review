- Problem and goal: Maximizing a monotone submodular function under a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting with robustness to removal of up to m selected elements.  
- Main contribution: Proposes STAR-T, a novel two-stage, single-pass streaming algorithm using a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Second stage: Uses STAR-T-GREEDY, a simple greedy post-removal selection, to produce the final summary.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Achieves a constant-factor approximation with memory O((k + m log k) log^2 k).  
- Novelty: Extends prior streaming methods (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) to handle robustness via new partitioning and thresholding ideas.  
- Empirical performance: Experiments show STAR-T matches or outperforms strong baselines, including SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines know removed elements.  
- Practical relevance: Demonstrated on applications like influence maximization and personalized recommendation.  
- Strength of analysis: Rigorous proofs support approximation and memory claims.  
- Clarity concern: Presentation and proofs are dense and may be hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Related-work comparison: Distinctions from other robust methods (e.g., PRO) and practical trade-offs need clearer elaboration.  
- Empirical limitations: Experiments are limited to specific datasets; larger/diverse benchmarks would strengthen claims.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Impact of parameters (τ, w) on practical performance is not fully explored.  
- Overall recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant theoretical and empirical contributions despite clarity and empirical-breadth weaknesses.
