- Summary: Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm that builds a partitioned summary allowing a greedy post-removal step (STAR-T-GREEDY) to achieve ~0.149 approximation for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k with memory O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Strength 1 (Significance & Originality): Tackles the novel and practical combination of streaming and robustness with an original partitioned, thresholding design and non-trivial analysis.  
- Strength 2 (Technical Quality): Provides a rigorous proof of a constant-factor guarantee and a practical parallel-instances fix for unknown f(OPT), with favorable logarithmic dependence on k.  
- Strength 3 (Empirical Validation): Experiments (dominating set, movie recommendations) show STAR-T-GREEDY is competitive with or better than SIEVE-STREAMING (even given an oracle), and supports versatile query use-cases.  
- Weakness 1 (Clarity of Theory): The proof sketch is dense and transitions between lemmas and final bounds lack intuition in the main text.  
- Weakness 2 (Theory-Practice Gap): The theoretical requirement w >= ceil(4 log(k) m / k) is large, yet experiments use w=1 and perform well; the paper lacks discussion of this gap.  
- Weakness 3 (Typo): Notation "dlog ke" appears incorrect and should be clarified (likely ceil(log_2 k)).  
- For acceptance: Addresses a novel, important problem; introduces a creative algorithmic synthesis; gives the first constant-factor guarantee with logarithmic memory in k; and presents strong, convincing empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Approximation constant is modest and the theoretical exposition could be clearer.  
- Conclusion: Strong, well-motivated contribution with minor clarity and theory-vs-practice gaps; recommendation to accept.
