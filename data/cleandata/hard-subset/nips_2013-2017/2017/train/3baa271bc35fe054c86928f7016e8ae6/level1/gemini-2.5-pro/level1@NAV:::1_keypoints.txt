- Addresses robust monotone submodular maximization with streaming access and adversarial removal of m items under cardinality k.
- Proposes a two-stage method: single-pass streaming algorithm STAR-T to build a robust summary, and STAR-T-GREEDY to select k items after m removals.
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds, combining ideas from SIEVE-STREAMING and centralized robust methods.
- Main theoretical result: first constant-factor approximation guarantee for this robust streaming problem using summary size O((k + m log k) log k).
- Key strengths: original and significant problem formulation relevant to large-scale, failure-resilient summaries.
- Technically sound with complete analysis culminating in a constant-factor approximation.
- Strong experimental validation showing competitiveness with a non-robust baseline that even knows the removed set in advance.
- Clear, well-organized writing that makes the algorithm design intuitive.
- Weakness: modest approximation constant (≈0.149), which is low though expected in robust settings.
- Weakness: overall space O((k + m log k) log k) may be large in practice when m is large despite logarithmic dependence on k.
- Weakness: use of multiple parallel instances to estimate the unknown optimum adds an extra logarithmic memory factor.
- Recommendation: strengths and validation outweigh minor weaknesses; solid and timely contribution worthy of acceptance.
