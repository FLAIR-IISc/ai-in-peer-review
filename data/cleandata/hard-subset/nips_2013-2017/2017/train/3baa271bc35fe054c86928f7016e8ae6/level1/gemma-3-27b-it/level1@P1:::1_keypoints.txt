- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where elements arrive sequentially and up to m chosen elements may be removed.  
- Contribution: Proposes STAR-T, a partitioned-bucket algorithm with exponentially decreasing thresholds and theoretical guarantees.  
- Motivation and positioning: Well-motivated by data summarization needs and correctly placed within submodular, streaming, and robust optimization literature.  
- Novelty: Partitioned thresholding is a clever approach balancing space efficiency and robustness; exponential thresholds enable the guarantees.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides a constant-factor approximation despite removals, with rigorous analysis (proofs in supplement).  
- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments show STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING in practical tasks like dominating set and movie recommendation.  
- Practical utility: Allows reusing the same summary for multiple removal scenarios, increasing real-world applicability.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Algorithm depends on multiple parameters (k, m, τ, w); choosing τ and approximating f(OPT) in practice is underaddressed.  
- Constant factor concern: The approximation constant (~0.149) is fairly small and may limit practical utility.  
- Space complexity: Stated O((k + m log k) log k) hides potentially large constants; theoretical w requirement seems at odds with experimental w=1.  
- Limited comparisons: Evaluation mainly contrasts with SIEVE-STREAMING; comparisons with other robust methods ([10], [11]) and more diverse datasets are lacking.  
- Proof accessibility: Key proof details are in the supplementary material, making independent validation harder without deeper review.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem with novel, theoretically sound methods, strong empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Low approximation constant, space/parameter concerns, and somewhat limited experimental comparison.  
- Overall assessment: Reviewer recommends strong accept, suggesting authors address parameter tuning, expand comparisons, and reconcile theory/empirical inconsistencies.
