- Problem & setting: Studies monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a streaming model with up to m adversarial deletions after the stream.  
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T, a one-pass streaming algorithm with partitioned summary (exponentially growing buckets, decreasing thresholds) plus STAR-T-GREEDY at query time.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Proves a constant-factor approximation that approaches c → 0.149 as k grows.  
- Space (known-optimum): Uses O((k + m log k) log k) memory when the optimal value is known.  
- Unknown-optimum extension: Runs parallel instances to remove the need for knowing OPT, incurring O((k + m log k) log^2 k) memory and a (1+ε)-loss.  
- Empirical results: On dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, STAR-T matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING, even when SIEVE-STREAMING is given the removed set.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING, robust (PRO) and concurrent deletion-robust streaming work (Mirzasoleiman et al.); trades a smaller approximation constant for improved (logarithmic in k) memory vs O(mk log k) of concurrent work.  
- Algorithmic novelty: The partitioned thresholding scheme for robust streaming is novel and distinct from PRO’s bucket greedy and SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- Quality of analysis: Design and analysis are sound, leveraging partitioned structure and thresholding with lemmas for full vs non-full buckets.  
- Clarity issues (algorithms): Discrepancy in Algorithm 1 between τ/min{2^i,k} and text saying τ/2^i—need reconciliation and explanation of the last partition.  
- Clarity issues (claims): Abstract/conclusion emphasize O((k+m log k) log^2 k) while Theorem 4.1 states O((k+m log k) log k); role of parallel instances should be made explicit earlier.  
- Presentation gaps: Provide full proofs and conditions (e.g., requirement γ < 1) in the main text or a clear roadmap to the appendix.  
- Reproducibility: Pseudocode and parameter choices (w, τ) need clearer guidance and a practical recipe for choosing τ.  
- Significance: Addresses a timely “right to be forgotten” use case; improved space bound is meaningful for large k and summaries are reusable across queries.  
- Experimental limitations: Missing empirical comparison to the concurrent deletion-robust streaming method and lacking sensitivity analyses (w, m/k) and runtime/scalability metrics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel robust streaming framework with principled analysis, favorable space–approximation tradeoff, and promising practical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Weaker approximation constant than concurrent work, missing comparison to that method, inconsistencies/clarity gaps, and limited evaluation breadth.
