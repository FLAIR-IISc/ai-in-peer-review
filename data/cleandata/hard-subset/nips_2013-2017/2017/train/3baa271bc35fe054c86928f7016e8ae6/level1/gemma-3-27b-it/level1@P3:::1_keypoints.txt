- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where selected elements may be removed.  
- Method: Proposes STAR-T using partitioned buckets and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a constant-factor approximation after removal of m elements (stated constant ≈ 0.149).  
- Algorithmic design: Two-stage approach—streaming selection followed by a greedy query stage.  
- Novelty: Partitioning structure and decreasing threshold rule are novel and tailored for robustness.  
- Analysis: Rigorous theoretical analysis combining partitioning and thresholding; extends to unknown OPT (Theorem 4.5).  
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-world (dominating set, movie recommendation) results show STAR-T is competitive or superior to baselines, including Sieve-Streaming.  
- Practical demonstration: Interactive movie-recommendation experiment convincingly demonstrates applicability.  
- Motivation & related work: Well-motivated problem with clear, comprehensive placement among prior streaming and robust submodular work.  
- Constant concern: The approximation constant (0.149) is small; paper should discuss practical significance and sensitivity to this constant and the dlog ke term.  
- Parameter tuning: Insufficient guidance on choosing parameters (w, τ); mentioning w=1 is not enough for general practice.  
- Memory/scalability: Theoretical memory O((k + m log k) log k) may be large in practice; needs more detailed analysis and optimization strategies.  
- Missing comparison: No direct empirical comparison to PRO [11], which has a similar guarantee—such a comparison is needed.  
- Proof presentation: Proofs in supplementary material are fine, but the main text should include a high-level summary of key proof ideas.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel approach, strong theoretical guarantees, solid empirical validation, relevance, and clear writing support acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: Small approximation constant and lack of PRO comparison are the main concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address constant impact and include comparison to PRO.
