- Addresses streaming monotone submodular maximization with cardinality k plus robustness to removal of up to m elements.  
- Proposes a two-stage procedure: STAR-T (streaming summary) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query after removals).  
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting using a novel partitioned thresholding approach.  
- Summary structure uses partitions with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Achieves memory complexity O((k + m log k) log k), claimed better than concurrent work [18].  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149.  
- Introduces a parallel variant that attains a (1 + ε)-approximation with only logarithmic space overhead.  
- Proofs are well-structured with careful case handling via multiple lemmas.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes detailed pseudocode.  
- Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation validate theory and show competitive/outperforming results.  
- Method outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines know removed elements in advance.  
- Problem and solution are significant for large-scale ML where storage and robustness matter.  
- Main weakness: approximation factor (0.149) is notably lower than non-robust 0.5 − ε results.  
- Limited empirical comparison with other robust submodular methods beyond the cited related work.  
- Experimental section lacks some parameter and implementation detail (e.g., why w=1).  
- Overall recommendation: accept—novel, technically sound, practically validated, and memory-efficient.
