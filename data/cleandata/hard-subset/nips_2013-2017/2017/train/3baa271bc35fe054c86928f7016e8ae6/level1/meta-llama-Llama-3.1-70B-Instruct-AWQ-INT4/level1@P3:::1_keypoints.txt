- Proposes STAR-T, a one-pass streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization that keeps O((k + m log k) log k) elements and achieves a constant-factor approximation.  
- Introduces a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule enabling robustness; analysis is thorough with detailed proofs.  
- Provides comprehensive literature comparison and clearly positions the work as novel in combining streaming and robustness.  
- Empirical evaluation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation is convincing; STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms baselines, even those aware of removals.  
- Strength: novel algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees and convincing experiments.  
- Weakness: assumes knowledge of f(OPT(k, V \ E)) (partially addressed in Sec. 4.1).  
- Weakness: requires memory parameter w that depends on m and may be hard to choose in practice.  
- Pro-accept: innovative solution, solid analysis, and strong empirical results.  
- Con-accept: realism of the OPT-value assumption and practical tuning of w are concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept.
