- Problem & contribution: Proposes STAR-T (streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (post-removal greedy) for robust monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in one-pass streaming with small memory.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING, introducing a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding to handle robustness.

- Analysis: Provides theoretical analysis exploiting robust structure and thresholding, proving a constant-factor approximation and bounding retained set size.

- Strength: Novel and efficient algorithm tailored to robust streaming submodular maximization.

- Strength: Achieves a constant-factor approximation, improving over prior results.

- Strength: Thorough theoretical proofs and bounds for the algorithm.

- Strength: Empirical validation on two data summarization tasks shows practical effectiveness.

- Weakness: Requires careful tuning of threshold parameter τ, which may be hard in practice.

- Weakness: Analysis relies on technical assumptions (monotonicity and submodularity).

- Weakness: Lacks clear empirical or theoretical comparison with other state-of-the-art robust submodular algorithms.
