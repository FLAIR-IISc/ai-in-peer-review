- Problem: Addresses streaming submodular maximization where m elements may be removed after the stream; proposes STAR-T (stream) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query).  
- Key innovation: Partitioned summary with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Memory complexity: Achieves O((k + m log k) log k) memory, improving over concurrent O(mk log k/ε) work.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Constant-factor approximation (Theorem 4.1) supported by lemmas handling partition cases.  
- Handling f(OPT): Requires an approximation of f(OPT(k, V\E)), addressed in Theorem 4.5 with only logarithmic memory overhead.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on dominating set and movie recommendation show matching or outperforming SIEVE-STREAMING even when that baseline knows deletions.  
- Quality of analysis: Technically sound, rigorous proofs, and honest discussion of limitations.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; algorithms and experimental setup are clearly presented and reproducible.  
- Originality: Novel combination of partitioned structures and thresholding distinct from prior streaming or robust methods.  
- Significance: Highly relevant for large-scale, deletion-prone applications; two-stage approach is practically useful.  
- Strengths: Novel method, rigorous theory, strong experiments, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: Theoretical approximation (≈0.149) is weaker than greedy and SIEVE-STREAMING, needs f(OPT) approximation (extra memory), and parameter w may need tuning.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — advances state of the art with theoretical and practical contributions.  
- Pro arguments: Novel approach, solid theoretical backing, convincing experiments, clear writing, memory improvement over concurrent work.  
- Con arguments: Lower theoretical factor, extra memory for f(OPT) approximation, possible attribution of gains to two-stage design rather than robustness alone.
