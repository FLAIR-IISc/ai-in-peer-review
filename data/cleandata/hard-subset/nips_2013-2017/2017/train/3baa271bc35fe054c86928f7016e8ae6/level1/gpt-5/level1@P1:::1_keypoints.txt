- Problem: Robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a single-pass streaming setting with up to m adversarial deletions.  
- Algorithm: Two-stage method — STAR-T (one-pass streaming summary using partitioned buckets and decreasing thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY (greedy on S \ E after deletions).  
- Theoretical guarantee: Constant-factor approximation approaching ~0.149 as k grows, with summary space roughly O(k log k + m log^2 k) and a parallel-thresholding variant to remove OPT dependence.  
- Novelty: Adapts nonuniform partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds to a one-pass streaming algorithm for deletion robustness.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on streaming submodular maximization (SIEVE-STREAMING), robust submodular optimization (PRO and others), and differs from concurrent work [18] which gets 1/2 − ε but requires linear memory in k and m.  
- Proof strategy: Balances two complementary bounds (many full buckets vs. many non-full buckets) and uses replication and decreasing thresholds to ensure deletion resilience.  
- Empirical results: Experiments on dominating set and recommendation tasks show STAR-T often matches or improves SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE is given E, and handles |E| > k.  
- Concern — threshold inconsistency: Text uses τ/2^i, Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i,k}, and proofs rely on τ/k for the last partition; needs a single consistent definition (Alg.1 likely correct).  
- Concern — space complexity inconsistencies: Intro/conclusion, Theorem 4.1, and other places report different forms (O((k + m log k) log^2 k), O(k log k + m log^2 k), O((k + m log k) log k)); unify and clarify role of w = max{1, ⌈4 m log k / k⌉}.  
- Concern — τ in Theorem 4.1: τ expression appears dimensionally wrong (starts with “1 + … f(OPT)”); τ should be proportional to f(OPT) — correct and relate to parallel-thresholding.  
- Concern — per-element oracle complexity: Worst-case bucket checks give O(wk) oracle calls per arriving element; discuss/measure this and runtime vs. SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- Clarity — parallel-thresholding: Include pseudo-code or brief description and state its memory multiplicative factor (log1+ε k).  
- Clarity — replication intuition: Add short intuition for how replication across partitions prevents deletions from concentrating on a single bucket.  
- Clarity — experiments: Report memory, wall-clock runtime, number of function evaluations, and an ablation over w.  
- Originality: The single-pass combination of nonuniform partitioning with exponential thresholds and the balanced-analysis yielding deletion robustness is novel.  
- Significance: Offers practical trade-off — much smaller memory (logarithmic vs linear in k) for a modest constant approximation, useful for privacy/failure scenarios.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled new algorithm and analysis, single-pass small-space summary, solid theoretical guarantee, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Presentation bugs (threshold/memory/τ), missing runtime/memory measurements, no empirical comparison to concurrent work [18], and only modest approximation constant.  
- Suggested fixes: Reconcile threshold/memory/τ inconsistencies, add per-element oracle-call complexity and runtime/memory experiments (including w ablation), discuss extensions/non-monotone or matroid settings, and compare/discuss [18] practical trade-offs.  
- Overall: Well-motivated, technically sound, and practically relevant work; lean toward acceptance pending the presentation and empirical clarifications above.
