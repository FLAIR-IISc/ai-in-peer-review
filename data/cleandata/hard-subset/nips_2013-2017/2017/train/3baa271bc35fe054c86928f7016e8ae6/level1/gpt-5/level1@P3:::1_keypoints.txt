- Problem & contribution: Proposes STAR-T (streaming summary) + STAR-T-GREEDY (post-removal query) for deletion-robust streaming maximization of a monotone submodular function under cardinality k.
- Main theoretical claim: Constant-factor approximation f(Z) ≥ c·f(OPT(k, V\E)) with c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/log k) while storing O((k + m log k) log k) elements (extra log_{1+ε}k factor when OPT unknown).
- Algorithmic design: Uses partitioned buckets with exponentially increasing capacities and exponentially decreasing thresholds, accepting items into first non-full bucket meeting a marginal threshold.
- Relation to prior work: Extends streaming submodular maximization (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) to deletions and is a streaming analogue of PRO and other robust selection works; contemporaneous with Mirzasoleiman et al. which trades memory for better approximation.
- Theoretical quality: Analysis is careful and novel; partitioned bucket design and case analysis are sound; dependence on m is near-linear (m log k).
- Assumptions/parameters: Requires knowing m and an estimate τ ≈ f(OPT) (parallel-thresholds variant removes τ need at log-factor memory cost); knowing m limits adaptivity if mis-specified.
- Completeness issues: Proof sketch is clear but there are inconsistencies in reported space bounds and no per-element runtime complexity analysis.
- Clarity strengths: High-level idea and acceptance rule are clearly explained and motivated.
- Clarity issues: Inconsistent space bounds (abstract/intro vs theorem), garbled τ formula in Theorem 4.1, and discrepancy between τ/2^i and τ/min{2^i,k} descriptions need fixing.
- Originality: Novel combination of non-uniform partitioning and thresholding in a one-pass streaming robust context; meaningful memory–approximation trade-off relative to concurrent work.
- Significance: Practically important for “right to be forgotten” and fault tolerance; achieves constant robustness with one pass and space sublinear in n and logarithmic in k (aside from m log k).
- Empirical strengths: Tests on dominating set and MovieLens; STAR-T matches/outperforms strong baselines even though baselines are given V\E while STAR-T only sees S\E.
- Empirical gaps: Lacks runtime/per-element cost measurements, sensitivity sweeps over m and w, experiments when m is mis-specified, and a post-hoc comparison to concurrent work [18].
- Implementation detail missing: Need clarification on efficient marginal gain computation/updates for facility-location objectives in streaming.
- Pros summary: Simple two-stage framework, solid theoretical guarantees, memory advantage vs prior concurrent work, competitive empirical performance.
- Cons summary: Modest approximation constant (~0.149) and degradation for small k, need to know m and τ (or pay memory for parallel thresholds), presentation inconsistencies, and limited experimental coverage.
- Suggested improvements: Reconcile and clearly state space bounds, fix τ expression, add runtime analysis and wall-clock/per-element timings, sweep m and w and test mis-specification, compare memory–quality trade-offs with [18], and formalize query-time GREEDY vs SIEVE-STREAMING trade-offs.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, well-motivated contribution with novel analysis and practical promise; accept if bounds, clarity, and empirical/runtime evaluations are strengthened.
