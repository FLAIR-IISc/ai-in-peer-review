- Problem & contribution: Addresses streaming robust submodular maximization and proposes STAR-T and STAR-T-GREEDY with a constant-factor approximation and O((k + m log k) log²k) memory.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust methods; contrasts with centralized O(m² log k) approaches and concurrent work [18].

- Key insight: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING via a partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds to tolerate m arbitrary removals.

- Theoretical contribution: Provides a clean, technically sound analysis achieving a 0.149(1−1/log k) approximation with careful case handling.

- Practical algorithm: STAR-T is one-pass, simple to implement, and uses an elegant partitioning scheme motivated by robustness needs.

- Experimental validation: STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when SIEVE-STREAMING knows removals) on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks.

- Memory efficiency: Achieves better memory dependence on k than concurrent work [18], with logarithmic dependence on k emphasized as a key advantage.

- Weakness — comparison to [18]: Paper briefly mentions [18] but lacks a detailed theoretical and empirical comparison of approximation vs memory trade-offs.

- Weakness — practical memory impact: The log²k factor may be significant in practice and the paper lacks experiments showing scaling of memory with k; claim w=1 may not generalize.

- Weakness — τ approximation: Algorithm requires an approximation τ of f(OPT), and the parallel remedy increases space by log_{1+ε} k without empirical assessment of its impact.

- Weakness — approximation quality: The achieved approximation (≈0.149) is much lower than greedy (0.632) or SIEVE-STREAMING (0.5−ε), and the paper does not clarify if this is inherent or improvable.

- Recommendation: Strong paper with sound theory, practical algorithm, and convincing experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to better compare with [18] and discuss the log²k practical impact.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a meaningful problem, offers novel theory, improved memory complexity, convincing empirical results, and a well-motivated two-stage approach.

- Arguments against acceptance: Low approximation factor, potential practical significance of the log²k memory term, and limited discussion of robustness versus approximation trade-offs.
