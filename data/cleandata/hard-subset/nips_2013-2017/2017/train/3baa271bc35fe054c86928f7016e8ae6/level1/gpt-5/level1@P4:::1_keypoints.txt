- Problem: deletion-robust monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality k in one-pass streaming with compact summary S that tolerates up to m deletions.
- Algorithm: STAR-T builds partitioned buckets with exponentially increasing sizes and decreasing thresholds; GREEDY on S \ E at query time (STAR-T-GREEDY).
- Storage guarantee: with w ≥ ceil(4 m log k / k), stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements (or O((k + m log k) log^2 k) when τ unknown via parallel thresholds).
- Approximation guarantee: after up to m deletions, GREEDY on S \ E achieves constant c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) times OPT(k, V \ E).
- Analysis technique: two-case analysis—many full buckets imply a robust high-value bucket; otherwise value captured in final/fewer buckets—balanced to get the guarantee.
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust non-streaming methods; trades off lower memory dependence on k (logarithmic) for a smaller constant compared to concurrent 1/2−ε work.
- Soundness: algorithm design and proofs are consistent with standard submodular analyses and carefully track constants.
- τ assumption: base algorithm requires τ≈f(OPT); authors handle this by parallel geometric thresholds at extra log factor and (1+ε) loss—standard approach.
- Time complexity: worst-case per-element oracle calls unclear (potentially O(w k) across O(log k) partitions); need clarification on short-circuiting/early stopping heuristics.
- Minor inconsistencies: threshold spec (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}) is inconsistent; Theorem 4.1 τ expression misformatted; space complexity statements vary—clarify which corresponds to parallel-threshold variant.
- Clarity: high-level intuition and proof sketch are good but some derivations are terse; a compact figure and consistent notation (log base, ⌈log k⌉ vs dlog ke) would help.
- Practical parameter w: theory requires w ≥ ceil(4 m log k / k) but experiments use w=1—need quantitative discussion of this gap and practical guidance.
- Originality: novel combination of partitioned storage with streaming thresholding to achieve deletion robustness with logarithmic k dependence in memory.
- Significance: addresses practical "right to be forgotten" requirement; attractive memory–approximation trade-off when k large and m moderate.
- Empirical results: performance comparable to GREEDY/SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines know E, supporting practical utility, but evaluation needs broader/systematic metrics.
- Pros: one-pass provably robust streaming algorithm, compact summaries, clear theoretical guarantees, simple GREEDY query stage, experiments on real datasets showing robustness.
- Cons: modest approximation constant (~0.149) vs 1/2−ε; time complexity per element not fully analyzed; experiments omit runtime/memory, w ablations, and statistical averages; minor algorithm description inconsistencies.
- Key questions for authors: worst-case and typical number of marginal evaluations per element and pruning heuristics; sensitivity of performance to w; potential to tighten analysis or improve constant c; empirical comparison to concurrent method at matched memory.
- Suggested improvements: unify/correct threshold definition and Theorem 4.1 τ; report wall-clock time, oracle calls, and memory vs baselines; include sensitivity plots for w and m; average/variance reporting in recommendation experiments; add pseudo-code or schematic and describe early-stopping for implementation.
