- Problem: robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a single-pass streaming model with up to m deletions.  
- Method: two-stage approach — STAR-T (streaming summary with partitioned, exponentially sized buckets and decreasing thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY (GREEDY on S\E).  
- Theoretical result: constant-factor approximation with c → 0.149 as k grows and memory O((k + m log k) log k) (extra log factor if OPT unknown).  
- Empirical result: matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING (even when that baseline knows E) on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Prior work: builds on PRO’s partitioned constructions and SIEVE-STREAMING thresholding; relates to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18] with different memory/approximation trade-offs.  
- Technical soundness: algorithm design and analysis are coherent; partitioning plus greedy guarantees are well argued and constants balanced via complementary lemmas.  
- Assumptions/parameters: requires memory parameter w and threshold τ depending on f(OPT); unknown OPT handled by parallel guesses; theory needs w ≥ ⌈4 m log k / k⌉ though empirically w=1 often suffices.  
- Proofs: main proof ideas are clear and full proofs are provided in the supplement.  
- Minor technical issues: inconsistent space bound (Theorem vs abstract/conclusion), mismatch in threshold definition (text vs Alg.1), and occasional off-by-one/notation issues.  
- Clarity: overall well written and organized; algorithms and case analysis are clear, but more intuition and practical tuning advice for w would help.  
- Originality: novel combination of partitioned robust summaries with streaming thresholds and an exponential-threshold scheme across partitions.  
- Significance: practically relevant for deletion-robust summarization, gives sublinear-in-n guarantees and supports multiple post-hoc removals from a single summary.  
- Limitations: approximation constant (~0.149) is modest compared to 1/2−ε in [18]; no experimental comparison to [18]; limited sensitivity and runtime/space reporting.  
- Pros: principled streaming algorithm with provable deletion robustness, modest space, reusable summary for multiple queries, and strong empirical performance.  
- Cons: presentation inconsistencies to fix, relatively small approximation factor, missing baselines and resource/sensitivity analysis, and extra space overhead to handle unknown OPT.  
- Suggestions: reconcile log vs log^2 space claims and threshold rule, compare to concurrent work or discuss trade-offs, report wall-clock/memory and sensitivity to m and w, and provide guidance or adaptive schemes for choosing w.  
- Overall evaluation: technically nontrivial, novel, and significant work with minor presentation issues; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
