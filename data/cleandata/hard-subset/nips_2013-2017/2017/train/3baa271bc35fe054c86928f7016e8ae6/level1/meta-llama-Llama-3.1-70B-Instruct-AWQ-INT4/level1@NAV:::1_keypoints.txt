- Proposes a novel two-stage streaming approach (STAR-T + STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization with a constant-factor approximation.  
- Introduces a new partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to handle many removals.  
- Algorithm is efficient in time and space and comes with thorough theoretical performance guarantees.  
- Extensive experiments (dominating set, personalized movie recommendation) show the method outperforms existing baselines, even ones knowing removals.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and uses consistent notation.  
- Assumes normalized, monotone submodular functions, which may limit applicability.  
- Requires careful tuning of the thresholding parameter, which may be hard in practice.  
- Novel contributions distinct from prior work (partitioning + thresholding rule).  
- Addresses an important problem with broad applications (summarization, influence maximization, recommendations).  
- Theoretical and empirical analyses are strong and well-supported.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
