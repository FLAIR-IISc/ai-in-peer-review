- Problem: robust streaming submodular maximization where up to m elements may be removed after the stream.  
- Method: STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm that builds a one-pass robust summary then runs greedy after removals.  
- Key innovation: partitioned thresholding with buckets having exponentially decreasing thresholds to enable robustness.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with a constant-factor approximation.  
- Theoretical result: rigorous proof of a 0.149 constant-factor approximation, with analysis for full vs not-full buckets.  
- Relation to prior work: bridges centralized robust methods (e.g., PRO) and streaming methods (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING).  
- Comparison to concurrent work: lower memory O((k + m log k) log k) vs O(mk log k/ε) in [18] when m is small relative to k.  
- Experiments: validated on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation, matching/outperforming SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE knows removals.  
- Memory efficiency: matches SIEVE-STREAMING for non-robust case (O(k log k)) and is memory-efficient for small m.  
- Weakness: approximation factor (0.149) is much lower than non-robust SIEVE-STREAMING’s ~0.5-ε.  
- Weakness: requires knowing τ = f(OPT(k, V\E)); using parallel instances to avoid this increases memory by another log factor.  
- Weakness: proof sketch in the main paper is dense and full proofs are in the appendix, making technical details harder to follow.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution with sound theory and convincing experiments; recommended for acceptance.  
- NIPS criteria: meets quality, clarity, originality, and significance, with the main caveat being the inherent trade-off in approximation versus robustness.
