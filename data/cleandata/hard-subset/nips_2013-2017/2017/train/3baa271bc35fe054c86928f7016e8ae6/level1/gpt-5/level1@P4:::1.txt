Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k in a streaming setting with robustness to post-hoc deletions: after one pass and storing a compact summary S, up to m elements may be removed from S, and one must return a size-k solution using only S \ E. The proposed STAR-T constructs S using a partitioned structure with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds. Each arriving element is inserted into the first non-full bucket whose marginal gain exceeds the partition’s threshold; the union of all buckets forms S. At query time, STAR-T-GREEDY runs GREEDY on S \ E.

The analysis proves that with w ≥ ceil(4 m log k / k), STAR-T stores at most O((k + m log k) log k) elements (or O((k + m log k) log^2 k) when τ is unknown and approximated via parallel thresholds in the style of SIEVE-STREAMING). After removing any |E| ≤ m, GREEDY on S \ E achieves a constant-factor guarantee c ≈ 0.149(1 − 1/⌈log k⌉) relative to OPT(k, V \ E). The proofs combine two regimes: (i) many full buckets in some partition imply existence of a high-value bucket robust to deletions; (ii) otherwise, value is captured either in the final partition’s not-full bucket or in elements placed earlier, yielding a bound that balances with the first regime.

This builds on submodular streaming (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING, KDD’14) and robust non-streaming methods (e.g., Orlin et al. IPCO’16; PRO, ICML’17). Concurrent work (Mirzasoleiman et al., ICML’17) gives a 1/2−ε deletion-robust streaming algorithm but with memory O(mk log k/ε); STAR-T trades a smaller constant (~0.149) for substantially lower memory dependence on k (logarithmic).

Quality (technical soundness)
- The algorithmic design and proof skeleton are sound and consistent with standard submodular maximization analyses (use of GREEDY’s 1−1/e factor, case splits, and submodularity arguments).
- The memory bound follows from summing partition sizes with w chosen to dilute adversarial deletions; the dependence O(m log^2 k) (when τ is unknown) is favorable.
- Query-time guarantee is derived by balancing two lemmas; constants are carefully tracked to c ≈ 0.149.
- Assumption on τ: the base algorithm requires τ ≈ f(OPT). The paper addresses this by running parallel instances over a geometric grid of thresholds, incurring an extra log factor and a (1+ε) loss—standard and reasonable.
- Time complexity is not fully characterized. Per-element processing may scan up to sum_i w ceil(k/2^i) buckets, i.e., O(w k), across O(log k) partitions. Clarifying worst-case oracle calls per element and the role of short-circuiting on thresholds would strengthen the practicality argument.
- Minor inconsistencies/typos suggest clarification is needed for reproducibility:
  - Text states thresholds τ/2^i, Algorithm 1 uses τ/min{2^i, k}. The analysis (e.g., f(e | B_last) < τ/k) aligns with the latter; please make the specification consistent throughout.
  - Theorem 4.1’s displayed expression for τ appears misformatted (“τ = 1 + … f(OPT(·))”); τ should be a constant multiple of f(OPT(·)).
  - Space complexity alternates between O((k + m log k) log k) (Thm 4.1) and O((k + m log k) log^2 k) (abstract/conclusion); clarify that the latter corresponds to the parallel-threshold variant.

Clarity
- The high-level intuition (partitioned buckets with decreasing thresholds) is well-motivated. The proof sketch is helpful, but some derivations are terse; a compact figure connecting the two-case analysis and the balancing step would aid readers.
- Notation is mostly standard; defining log’s base and consistently using ⌈log k⌉ vs dlog ke would avoid confusion.
- The role and practical setting of w deserve more exposition; the gap between w ≥ ceil(4 m log k / k) in theory and w = 1 in experiments should be discussed quantitatively.

Originality
- Novel combination of partitioned storage (inspired by PRO) with streaming thresholding and a new analysis for deletion robustness with logarithmic dependence on k in memory.
- The approach extends SIEVE-STREAMING’s thresholding to a robust, two-stage setting in a principled way.

Significance
- Addresses a relevant practical requirement (“right to be forgotten”/post-hoc deletions) in streaming summarization.
- Memory–approximation trade-off is attractive when k is large and m is moderate.
- Experiments on influence/dominating set and recommendation show performance comparable to GREEDY/SIEVE-STREAMING even when baselines are advantaged by knowing E, supporting practical utility. However, empirical evaluation would benefit from broader and more systematic metrics.

Pros
- One-pass, provably robust streaming algorithm with compact summaries.
- Clear theoretical guarantees with explicit constants and memory bounds.
- Simple and practical query stage (standard GREEDY).
- Empirical results on real datasets; robustness demonstrated under random and adaptive deletions.

Cons
- Approximation constant (~0.149) is modest; comparison to the 1/2−ε concurrent result focuses on memory, not quality.
- Time complexity per element not analyzed in detail; potential overhead from scanning many buckets.
- Experimental evaluation lacks runtime/memory reporting, ablations over w, and averages over multiple users/seeds in recommendation.
- Minor inconsistencies in the algorithm description (threshold definition) and parameter τ specification.

Questions for the authors
- What is the worst-case and typical number of marginal evaluations per element as a function of k, m, and w? Any heuristics to prune bucket scans?
- How sensitive is performance to w? Provide plots of objective vs memory (via varying w) and vs m for fixed k.
- Can the analysis be tightened to improve the constant c, or to replace the 1 − 1/⌈log k⌉ term by a smaller loss?
- How does STAR-T compare empirically to the concurrent deletion-robust streaming method [18], at matched memory?

Suggestions for improvement
- Unify and correct the threshold definition (τ/min{2^i, k} vs τ/2^i) and fix the τ expression in Theorem 4.1.
- Report wall-clock time, number of oracle calls, and memory used by STAR-T vs baselines; include sensitivity to w and m.
- In recommendation, report averages over multiple users and α settings; add variance bars. Include scenarios with varying |E| beyond 500 and genre filters.
- Add a brief pseudo-code or schematic illustrating bucket insertion and early stopping to clarify implementation.

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to a timely problem with promising empirical results. Addressing the clarity issues and expanding the experimental evaluation would strengthen the submission.
