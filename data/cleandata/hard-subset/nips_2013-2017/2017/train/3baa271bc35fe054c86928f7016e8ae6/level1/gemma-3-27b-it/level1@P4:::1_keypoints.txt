- Introduces STAR-T, a streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization addressing element removals from summaries.  
- Novel problem formulation combining streaming data arrival with robustness to removals, motivated by practical summarization scenarios.  
- Algorithm uses a partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds to maintain a constant-factor approximation after up to m removals.  
- Provides a theoretical constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149) for the robust streaming setting.  
- Builds on and correctly cites prior work in greedy submodular maximization, streaming algorithms, and robustness.  
- Algorithm design and partitioning/thresholding scheme are clear and well-defined.  
- Empirical validation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks shows strong performance versus existing methods.  
- Evaluation includes multiple removal scenarios (random and greedy), strengthening empirical claims.  
- Practical method to approximate the required threshold parameter without knowing OPT (Theorem 4.5) improves usability.  
- Space complexity can be large: O((k + m log k) log k) and may be problematic when m is large.  
- The proven approximation constant (0.149) is relatively small, leaving room for improvement.  
- Performance likely sensitive to parameters (w and initial threshold τ); more sensitivity analysis would help.  
- Some proofs are relegated to supplementary material, making quick verification of technical details difficult.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and relevant problem, strong theoretical guarantees, convincing experiments, practical algorithm, solid literature connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small approximation constant, potentially high space usage, and limited ability to verify proofs without supplement.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with well-designed algorithm and supportive theory/experiments despite some limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
