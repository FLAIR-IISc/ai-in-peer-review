- Proposes a novel two-stage approach for robust streaming submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k with up to m deletions.  
- Introduces STAR-T (streaming stage) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query stage).  
- Key innovation: partitioning structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds enabling one-pass streaming and compact summaries.  
- Summary memory size bound: O((k + m log k) log² k).  
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation c = 0.149(1 − 1/log k) after any m removals.  
- Builds on prior submodular streaming and robust maximization work but novelly combines both settings.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough with clear proof sketches and detailed supplementary material.  
- Experiments (dominating set and movie recommendation) show the method matches or outperforms baselines, even when baselines know removals in advance.  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, solid theoretical foundation, elegant algorithmic design, high technical quality and originality.  
- Weaknesses: relatively low approximation factor (~0.149 vs ~0.63 for standard algorithms) and limited discussion of performance scaling with larger m.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for large-scale ML with limited memory and robustness needs; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address approximation-factor discussion.
