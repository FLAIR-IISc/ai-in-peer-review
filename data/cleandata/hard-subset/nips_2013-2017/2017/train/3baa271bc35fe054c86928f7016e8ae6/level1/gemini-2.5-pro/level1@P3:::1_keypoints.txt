- Problem & approach: Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T summary built in one streaming pass, then STAR-T-GREEDY after up to m removals) for robust monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality k in the streaming setting.

- Algorithmic innovation: STAR-T combines partitioned summaries (from PRO) with greedy thresholding (from Sieve-Streaming), using partitions with exponentially decreasing thresholds and buckets of exponentially increasing size.

- Theoretical guarantee: Proves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 for the robust objective with summary size O((k + m log k) log k), giving polylogarithmic dependence on k.

- Memory advantage: Memory complexity O((k + m log k) log k) substantially improves over concurrent work (Mirzasoleiman et al. 2017) that scales linearly in k (O(mk log k)).

- Empirical validation: Experiments on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms strong baselines, including Sieve-Streaming that knows removals in advance.

- Strength — practical significance: Addresses a realistic and important problem (streaming + robustness) with a well-motivated two-stage formulation.

- Strength — novelty: Elegant, non-trivial synthesis of prior techniques with the key innovation of exponentially decreasing thresholds enabling single-pass robust summaries.

- Strength — technical quality: First constant-factor approximation with favorable memory scaling; theoretical analysis appears sound and highlights key advantages over related work.

- Strength — experiments: Well-designed comparisons; matching/exceeding a full-information baseline is a strong empirical endorsement.

- Weakness — approximation constant: The achieved constant c ≈ 0.149 is quite low and the paper lacks discussion on whether this is inherent or an artifact of the analysis.

- Weakness — theory/practice gap for w: Theory requires w ≥ ⌈4 d log(k) e m / k⌉ for robustness, but experiments use w=1 and still perform well, suggesting a gap that needs discussion (loose analysis vs. benign datasets).

- Weakness — minor clarity issues: Small inconsistency in threshold definition (τ/2^i vs τ/min{2^i,k}) and a concise proof sketch that would benefit from slightly more intuition (e.g., bounding f(Y) in Lemma 4.4).

- Overall assessment & recommendation: Strong, well-executed contribution with minor issues; recommend accept.
