- Proposes a novel two-stage algorithm (STAR-T streaming summary + STAR-T-GREEDY query) for robust submodular maximization in streaming.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a thorough related-work review.  
- Significant technical contributions with detailed algorithmic analysis.  
- Main strength: rigorous, well-organized proof of a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  
- Performance is sensitive to parameter choices, especially the threshold τ, with limited guidance on tuning.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets) and lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- No information provided on computational resources or runtime requirements for the streaming setting.  
- Novel contributions include a new partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Analysis provides a new theoretical treatment of robust submodular maximization.  
- Algorithm is potentially significant for applications like data summarization, influence maximization, and personalized recommendation in streaming/real-time settings.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; technical merits outweigh minor weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong technical contributions, rigorous analysis, practical applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter sensitivity, limited experiments, missing computational-resource details.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
