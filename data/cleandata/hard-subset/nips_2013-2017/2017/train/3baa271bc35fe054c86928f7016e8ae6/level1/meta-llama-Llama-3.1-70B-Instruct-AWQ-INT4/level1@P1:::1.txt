The paper "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach" presents a novel algorithm for the problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting with robustness requirements. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) that makes one pass over the data and retains a small set of elements in memory, and a simple greedy algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) that runs on the remaining elements after any set of size at most m is removed.

The main ideas of the paper relate to previous work on submodular optimization, particularly in the context of streaming algorithms and robust optimization. The authors build upon the ideas of SIEVE-STREAMING [8] and extend them to the robust setting, introducing a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule. The analysis of the algorithm exploits the properties of the robust structure and the decreasing greedy thresholding rule.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a novel and efficient algorithm for the robust submodular maximization problem in a streaming setting.
* The algorithm is shown to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee, which is a significant improvement over previous results.
* The paper includes a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a proof of the approximation guarantee and a bound on the size of the retained set.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm through numerical experiments on two different data summarization tasks.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The algorithm requires a careful tuning of the thresholding parameter τ, which may be difficult to determine in practice.
* The analysis of the algorithm relies on a number of technical assumptions, including the monotonicity and submodularity of the objective function.
* The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art algorithms for robust submodular maximization.
