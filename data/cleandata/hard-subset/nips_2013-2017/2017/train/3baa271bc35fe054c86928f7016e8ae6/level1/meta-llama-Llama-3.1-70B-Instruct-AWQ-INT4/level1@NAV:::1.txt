This paper presents a novel approach to robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The authors propose a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) and a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY), which achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for robust submodular function maximization. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a robust submodular streaming algorithm that can handle a large number of removals. The authors achieve this by introducing a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule. The algorithm is shown to be efficient, both in terms of time and space complexity, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its performance.

The paper also presents an extensive set of experiments, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in various applications, including dominating set and personalized movie recommendation. The results show that the algorithm compares favorably to existing methods, even when the latter are allowed to know in advance which elements will be removed.
