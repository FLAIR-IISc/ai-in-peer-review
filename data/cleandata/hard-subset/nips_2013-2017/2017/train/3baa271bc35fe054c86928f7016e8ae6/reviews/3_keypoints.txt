- Paper addresses streaming + robust submodular maximization, the first to consider both jointly.
- Problem: select k high-value elements from a small retained set while being robust to removal of m items.
- Proposed STAR-T algorithm maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation under m removals.
- STAR-T uses partitioning and thresholding with buckets, filling buckets differently from prior robust submodular work.
- Experiments show STAR-T performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even vs. algorithms that know removals beforehand.
- Strength: achieving a constant-factor approximation in this challenging combined setting is a strong result.
- Weakness: paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound guarantee.
- Weakness: analysis/writing is unclear; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not explained and the proof sketch is hard to follow.
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about applicability to traditional network-dependent influence objectives in streaming.
