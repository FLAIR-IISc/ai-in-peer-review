- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T streaming algorithm followed by a simple greedy stage.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule.  
- The second stage applies the standard greedy algorithm to the summary produced by STAR-T.  
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T has a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show STAR-T combined with greedy can tolerate loss of elements.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show competitive performance.  
- Novelty: the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potentially wide applications.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Thm 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments suggest w=1 works well (convenient).  
- Major concern: experiments are not very expressive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming.  
- Questionable experiment setup: the influence-max objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the classical Kempe et al. spread model, so STAR-T’s performance on the true diffusion model is unclear.
