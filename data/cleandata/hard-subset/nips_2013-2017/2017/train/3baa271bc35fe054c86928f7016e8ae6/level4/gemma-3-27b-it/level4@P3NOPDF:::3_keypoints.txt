- Tackles joint problem of streaming submodular maximization with robustness to removal of m elements.  
- Novel contribution: first work to address streaming and robustness constraints together.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 constant-factor approximation.  
- Uses partitioning, thresholding, and bucket-based candidate management, modifying bucket filling for the streaming setting.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show competitive performance, sometimes outperforming streaming methods that know removals in advance.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this challenging setting is a significant theoretical accomplishment.  
- Lacks lower bounds, weakening understanding of the optimal achievable approximation and theoretical limits.  
- Presentation and analysis are unclear; the proof sketch is dense and the novel analytical steps are not well articulated.  
- Influence-maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that does not capture full network-dependent diffusion dynamics.  
- Paper should discuss limitations of the simplified objective and how conventional, network-dependent influence objectives might be adapted to streaming.  
- Overall valuable contribution, but addressing lower bounds, analytical clarity, and more realistic experiments would strengthen impact.
