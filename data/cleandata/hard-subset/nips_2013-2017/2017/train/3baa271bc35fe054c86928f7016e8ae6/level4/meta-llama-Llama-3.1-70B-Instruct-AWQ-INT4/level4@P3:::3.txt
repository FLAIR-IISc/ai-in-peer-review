This paper studies the problem of submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting. In this setting, the goal is to find k elements of high value among a small number of elements retained during the stream, where m items might be removed after the stream. The authors develop the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant (0.149) approximation guarantee while being robust to the removal of m elements.

The paper relates to previous work on submodular maximization, particularly in the context of streaming and robust optimization. The STAR-T algorithm builds upon ideas from previous work, such as the use of buckets for submodular optimization. However, the authors introduce a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule, which allows them to obtain a constant-factor approximation for the robust streaming problem.

The paper's main contribution is the development of the STAR-T algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The authors prove that the algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee while being robust to the removal of m elements. The paper also presents experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the algorithm in influence maximization and movie recommendation applications.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a challenging problem setting, combining streaming and robustness aspects.
