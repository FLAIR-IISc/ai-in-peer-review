- Tackles a novel problem: streaming monotone submodular maximization with robustness to up to m adversarial removals.  
- Proposes STAR-T, a one-pass streaming algorithm that builds a summary of size O((m log k + k) log² k) using partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholding.  
- After streaming, runs a greedy algorithm on the remaining summary to produce the final k-element solution.  
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149.  
- Empirical validation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks shows STAR-T performs comparably or better than SIEVE-STREAMING, despite the baseline knowing removals a priori.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated problem and theoretical advance of achieving a constant-factor approximation under streaming+robustness.  
- Weakness: theoretical completeness lacking — no non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize optimality.  
- Weakness: clarity of analysis — main-paper proof sketch is hard to follow and does not clearly highlight the core innovations.  
- Weakness: influence-maximization experiment uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective, leaving unclear applicability to standard diffusion models (IC/LT) that require costly simulations.
