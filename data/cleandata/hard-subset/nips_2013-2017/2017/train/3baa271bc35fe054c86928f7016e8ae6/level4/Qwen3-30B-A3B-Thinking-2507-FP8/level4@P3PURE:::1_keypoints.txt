- Problem: Finds T ⊆ E in a streaming setting such that after any m deletions from T, the remaining set still contains a subset S of size k achieving maximum value of a submodular function f.  
- Applications: Motivation for the robust variant is underdeveloped; the cited movie-selection example is vague and not compelling for real-world necessity or scalability.  
- Result: Presents a constant-factor approximation algorithm returning T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.  
- Technical merit: The algorithm and analysis are non-trivial, rigorous, and technically sophisticated.  
- Correctness: The theoretical results are correct.  
- Relevance/impact: Relevance to NeurIPS is questionable—problem formulation is narrow, omits common streaming constraints (limited memory, adaptive queries), and doesn’t clearly advance streaming submodular maximization or fill a significant literature gap.
