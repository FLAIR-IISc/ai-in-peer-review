- Proposes a two-stage procedure: STAR-T (robust streaming) followed by a simple greedy second stage.  
- STAR-T leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theoretically, STAR-T attains a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞ (Thm 4.1).  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show that combining STAR-T with greedy effectively mitigates element loss.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization; streaming setting has broad potential applications.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives guidance for τ and experiments suggest w = 1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Main concern: experimental results are limited—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-St.  
- Concern: Influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard diffusion model (Kempe et al. 2003), so performance under the standard spread model is unclear.
