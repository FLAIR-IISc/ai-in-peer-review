- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T streaming summary (partitioned buckets, exponentially decreasing thresholds) followed by GREEDY on the summary after up to m deletions.  
- STAR-T builds a compact, deletion-robust summary in a single pass.  
- Theorem 4.1 gives a constant-factor approximation that approaches a fixed constant as k grows.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show partitioning, thresholds, and GREEDY mitigate the impact of removed items.  
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation shows good performance.  
- Claimed novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.  
- Practicality: two hyperparameters τ and w; τ can be approximated with parallel instances (Theorem 4.5) and w=1 works well in experiments.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is not very revealing; STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING and margins are small.  
- Concern about influence experiments: objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| differs from standard diffusion-based spread models (Kempe et al.), so behavior under standard propagation models is unclear and needs clarification or additional experiments.
