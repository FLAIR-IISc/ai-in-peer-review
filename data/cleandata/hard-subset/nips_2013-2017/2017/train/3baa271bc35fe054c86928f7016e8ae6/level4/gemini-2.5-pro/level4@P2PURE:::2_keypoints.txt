- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (robust streaming) followed by a simple greedy post-processing.  
- STAR-T employs a partitioning structure with a decreasing thresholding rule as its core streaming mechanism.  
- Claims to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, highlighting broad applicability of the streaming setting.  
- Theoretical guarantees: STAR-T attains a constant approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1); Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show robustness to element loss when combined with greedy.  
- Algorithm has two hyperparameters (τ, w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments suggest w = 1 works well, reducing tuning burden.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show competitive performance, but STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- The influence maximization objective used, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, deviates from the standard diffusion-based spread model (Kempe et al.), raising concerns about performance on conventional IM formulations.
