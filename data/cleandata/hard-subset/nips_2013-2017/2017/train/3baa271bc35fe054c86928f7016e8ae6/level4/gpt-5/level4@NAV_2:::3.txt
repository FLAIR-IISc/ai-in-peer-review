This paper investigates submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a setting that is both streaming and deletion-robust. The objective is to identify k high-value elements from a compact summary constructed in a single pass, while tolerating the removal of up to m items after the stream concludes. Streaming submodular maximization has been widely explored due to large-scale scenarios permitting few passes over data, and robustness has received recent attention to handle post hoc deletions. To my knowledge, this is the first work that jointly tackles streaming and robustness.

The authors introduce STAR-T, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor approximation (0.149) even after the removal of any m elements. The method combines partitioning with thresholded bucketing. While bucketed constructions have appeared in prior robust submodular work, this paper fills buckets via a different threshold-based rule. Empirically, the approach performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation, and remains competitive with streaming baselines that are granted foreknowledge of the deleted set.

Given the dual challenges of streaming and robustness, obtaining a constant-factor guarantee is a noteworthy contribution. That said, several aspects could be strengthened. Some nontrivial lower bounds would nicely complement the approximation guarantees. The exposition of the analysis could be clearer: the paper alludes to an “innovative analysis” but does not sufficiently articulate what is novel, and the proof sketch makes it hard to distill the main ideas. Finally, the influence maximization evaluation is less compelling, as it relies on a simplified neighborhood-based objective; it remains unclear how more standard diffusion-based influence functions—whose value depends on the entire network for any seed set—fit naturally into this streaming framework.
