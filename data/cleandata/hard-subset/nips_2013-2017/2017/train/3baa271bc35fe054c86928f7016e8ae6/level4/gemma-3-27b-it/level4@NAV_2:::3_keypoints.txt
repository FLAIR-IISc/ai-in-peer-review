- Tackles robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where up to m elements may be removed after summary construction.  
- Introduces STAR-T, using a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Achieves a constant-factor approximation with memory O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- First constant-factor approximation for the combined streaming-plus-robustness objective.  
- Partitioning and thresholding build on prior robust submodular work but use a distinct bucket-filling approach and bespoke analysis for arbitrary removals.  
- Experimental evaluation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation shows competitive performance versus baselines, including methods with knowledge of removals.  
- Paper is technically sound; theoretical claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Authors acknowledge analytical limitations and potential improvements.  
- Writing is generally clear but the description of the “innovative analysis” lacks a high-level, accessible outline.  
- No lower bounds are provided, leaving optimality of the approximation ratio unclear.  
- Use of a simplified influence maximization objective may reduce generalizability of experimental findings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and important problem, strong theoretical result, and validated practical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: analysis clarity needs strengthening and absence of lower bounds limits assessment of optimality.
