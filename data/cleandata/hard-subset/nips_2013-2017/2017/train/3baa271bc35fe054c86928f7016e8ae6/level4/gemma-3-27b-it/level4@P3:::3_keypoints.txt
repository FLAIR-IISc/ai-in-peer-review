- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting, combining efficiency and resilience to data removal.
- Proposes STAR-T, which maintains a compact stream summary and guarantees a constant-factor approximation after removing m elements.
- Core algorithmic idea: partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds to build the summary.
- Theoretical guarantee: achieves a 0.149-approximation as k grows large.
- Experiments on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation show STAR-T is competitive and sometimes outperforms baselines.
- Practical advantage: the robust summary can be re-used for different removal scenarios (demonstrated in movie recommendations).
- Proof sketch (Section 4) lacks sufficient detail to convey the novelty and core analytical insights.
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity beyond space usage.
- Missing lower bounds on achievable approximation ratios, which would contextualize the theoretical results.
- Dominating set experiment uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective, limiting relevance to realistic influence maximization tasks.
- Parameter sensitivity (e.g., effect of w) is not thoroughly explored or discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: first to tackle combined robust+streaming problem, strong constant-factor guarantee, empirical validation, and practical relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear analysis, missing lower bounds, and simplified experimental setup.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution worth accepting, with a recommendation to improve analysis clarity and address theoretical/experimental gaps in revision.
