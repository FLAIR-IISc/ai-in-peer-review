- Proposes a novel two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint: a robust streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy refinement.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule in the streaming stage.  
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show the combined STAR-T + greedy pipeline is robust to element loss.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Contribution: introduces the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with potential broad applicability.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 provides a way to approximate τ.  
- Empirically, setting w = 1 performs well, reducing the need for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Major concern: experiments are limited because STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Methodological concern: the influence-maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the classical spread model (Kempe et al.), leaving unclear performance under the traditional diffusion model.
