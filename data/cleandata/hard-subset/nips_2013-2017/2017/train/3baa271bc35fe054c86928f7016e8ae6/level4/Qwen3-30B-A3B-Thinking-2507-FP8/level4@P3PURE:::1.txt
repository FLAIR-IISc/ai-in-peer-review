

Problem studied: The paper investigates the problem of finding a set \( T \subseteq E \) from a ground set \( E \) in a streaming setting, such that for any deletion of \( m \) elements from \( T \), the remaining set still contains a subset \( S \) of size \( k \) achieving the maximum value of a submodular function \( f \).  

Applications: While submodular maximization has broad applications (e.g., in recommendation systems, feature selection), the paper’s motivation for the robust variant is underdeveloped. The cited example—selecting movies for a user who later discards some—lacks specificity and fails to demonstrate compelling real-world necessity or scalability challenges.  

Results: The authors present a constant-factor approximation algorithm that returns a set \( T \) of size \( (m \log k + k) \log^2 k \). The technical approach and analysis are non-trivial and rigorously developed.  

Comments: The theoretical results are correct and the algorithmic solution is technically sophisticated. However, the paper’s relevance to NIPS is questionable. The problem formulation is narrow (e.g., it does not address common streaming constraints like limited memory or adaptive queries), and the application motivation is weak and unconvincing. The work does not sufficiently advance the state of the art in streaming submodular maximization or address a significant gap in the literature.
