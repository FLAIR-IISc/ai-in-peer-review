- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy post-processing step.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule to operate in streaming settings.
- The authors prove STAR-T attains a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show that STAR-T combined with the greedy step effectively handles element loss (robustness).
- Experimental evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrates empirical performance and claims this is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives guidance for approximating τ, and experiments suggest w = 1 performs well, reducing tuning needs.
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the baseline Sieve-Streaming algorithm.
- Major methodological concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard diffusion-based spread model in Kempe et al. (2003), leaving STAR-T’s performance under standard influence models unclear.
