- Proposes a two-stage robust submodular maximization method: STAR-T (robust streaming) followed by a greedy completion stage.  
- STAR-T leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding strategy.  
- Theorem 4.1 proves a constant-factor approximation for STAR-T as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show the combined STAR-T + greedy approach is resilient to element loss.  
- Demonstrated empirically on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with broad application potential.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives guidance for τ and experiments suggest w = 1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Experimental results are limited and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline.  
- Influence maximization experiment uses f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, which departs from the standard Kempe et al. spread model, leaving performance under the standard model unclear.
