- Paper introduces STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm for robust monotone submodular maximization under cardinality constraints with up to m removals.  
- STAR-T builds a one-pass robust summary using a partitioned structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY yields a constant-factor approximation after removals.  
- Novelty: the partitioned thresholding structure is a new combination achieving robustness in the streaming setting, unlike prior centralized robust or streaming-only methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis (Theorem 4.1) showing storage O((k + m log k) log² k) and a 0.149(1 − 1/log k)-approximation.  
- Practical claim: w=1 suffices in real-world settings (Theorem 4.5), reducing hyperparameter tuning.  
- Technical quality and clarity: proof strategy, algorithm design, and supplementary material are strong and reproducible; figures and problem framing are clear.  
- Weakness: experiments do not consistently show superiority over SIEVE-STREAMING—some plots (Figures 2b,d) show SIEVE-STREAMING slightly outperforming STAR-T-GREEDY.  
- Weakness: the influence maximization experiment uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which deviates from standard diffusion-based objectives (Kempe et al.), weakening that validation.  
- Requested fixes: clarify/justify the influence objective or re-run experiments with standard diffusion models and provide more experiments (larger datasets, varied m) to explain/resolve performance inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions conditional on (i) clarifying the influence objective and (ii) providing additional experiments to demonstrate consistent improvements.
