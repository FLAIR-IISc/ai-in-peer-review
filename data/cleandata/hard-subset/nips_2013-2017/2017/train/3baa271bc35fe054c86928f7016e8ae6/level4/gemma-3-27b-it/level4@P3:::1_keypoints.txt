- Addresses robust streaming submodular maximization: streaming summary must remain near-optimal after up to m removals.  
- Formalizes problem as two-stage procedure: streaming summary construction and query-stage recovery after removals.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm using a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for STAR-T.  
- Extends approach to unknown optimal values via multiple parallel instances (Theorem 4.5).  
- Motivation and applications (data summarization, influence maximization, recommendations) are clearly presented.  
- Builds appropriately on prior streaming and robust submodular work and cites relevant literature.  
- Presentation could be clearer: proofs and notation are dense and may be hard for non-experts.  
- Experimental evaluation shows effectiveness but is not comprehensive and relies mainly on SIEVE-STREAMING as baseline.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of practical choices (e.g., why w=1 works despite theoretical requirements).  
- Application scenarios are discussed but may not fully justify the problem’s complexity or constraints.  
- Strengths: novelty, sound technical approach, theoretical guarantees, and clear motivation.  
- Weaknesses: clarity issues, limited experiments, and potentially narrow applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: challenging problem, novel algorithm, solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity, limited broader relevance, and insufficient experimental breadth.  
- Overall: solid contribution with a recommendation to accept conditional on addressing clarity and expanding experiments.
