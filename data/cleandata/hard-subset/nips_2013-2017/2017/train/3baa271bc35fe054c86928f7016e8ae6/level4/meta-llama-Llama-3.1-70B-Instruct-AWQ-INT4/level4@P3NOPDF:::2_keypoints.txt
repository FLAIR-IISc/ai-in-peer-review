- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (robust streaming with partitioning and decreasing thresholds) followed by greedy.  
- Proves STAR-T has a constant approximation guarantee as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show STAR-T + greedy can effectively handle element loss.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrate the algorithm and claim it is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization.  
- STAR-T uses two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ, and w = 1 worked well in experiments, reducing tuning needs.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming, questioning robustness.  
- Important omission: influence-maximization objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| differs from Kempe et al.’s standard spread model, so performance on the standard model is unclear.  
- Recommendations: provide more comprehensive experiments (additional baselines, standard spread model), analyze hyperparameter robustness, and offer deeper theoretical insights.
