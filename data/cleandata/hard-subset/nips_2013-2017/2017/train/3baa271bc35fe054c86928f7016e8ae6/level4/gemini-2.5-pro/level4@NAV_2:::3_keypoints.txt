- Studies monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a two-stage streaming+robust model where a single-pass summary must withstand up to m deletions before a final k-size solution is extracted.
- First work to jointly address streaming access and adversarial robustness for submodular maximization.
- Proposes STAR-T: uses a partitioning structure with exponentially-sized buckets and an exponentially-decreasing thresholding rule to build the summary.
- Summary is built in one pass using O((m log k + k) log^2 k) memory.
- Query stage applies a simple greedy algorithm to the summary after up to m elements are removed.
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation of 0.149 for the combined procedure.
- Empirical validation on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks shows strong performance, sometimes outperforming streaming baselines that know removals in advance.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated problem formulation relevant for large-scale, failure-resilient summaries.
- Strength: elegant, non-trivial algorithmic design combining partitioning and thresholding.
- Strength: first algorithm with a constant-factor approximation for this problem and compelling experiments.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis presentation is hard to follow and lacks clear articulation of key technical novelties and intuitions.
- Weakness: missing discussion of lower bounds or hardness results to contextualize the tightness of the 0.149 bound.
- Weakness: experimental setup—dominating set objective is a simplification of influence maximization, leaving unclear how the approach handles standard IC/LT models that require simulations or full-graph access.
- Overall: solid and significant contribution with room for clearer proofs and stronger experimental realism.
