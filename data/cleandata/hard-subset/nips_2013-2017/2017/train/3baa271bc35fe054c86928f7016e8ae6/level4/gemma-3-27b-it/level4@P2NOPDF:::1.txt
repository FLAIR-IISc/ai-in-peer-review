This paper investigates a robust version of the streaming submodular maximization problem. Specifically, the authors aim to maintain a set *T* of elements, selected from a ground set *E* in a streaming manner, such that even after removing any *m* elements from *T*, a near-optimal set *S* of *k* elements can still be found to maximize a submodular function *f*.

The motivation stems from the practical utility of submodular maximization in various applications and the existence of established streaming algorithms for the standard problem. This robust variant addresses scenarios where a previously computed solution *T* needs to be adapted due to unforeseen changes – for instance, removing movies from a user’s recommended list if the user expresses disinterest. While the application domain is well-defined, the presented justification feels somewhat narrow and doesn't fully convey broad impact.

The core contribution of the paper is a constant-factor approximation algorithm for this problem, which returns a set *T* of size *O( (m log k + k) log² k )*. The authors demonstrate the correctness of their approach through a technically challenging analysis, and to the best of my understanding, the results are sound. The algorithm itself is non-trivial, and its analysis represents a significant technical achievement.

However, my primary concern lies with the paper’s relevance to NeurIPS. While the problem is interesting from a theoretical perspective, its generality and the strength of its motivating applications are questionable. The provided application example, while illustrative, doesn’t convincingly establish the widespread need for a solution to this specific robustness constraint. The problem setting feels somewhat specialized, and it’s unclear how broadly applicable the proposed techniques might be beyond this specific framework. A stronger connection to more prevalent areas within the conference, or a clearer articulation of the problem’s broader implications, would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution.
