- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in streaming—maintain a summary T so after any adversarial deletion of m elements a size-k subset with high f-value can still be extracted.
- Setting: Ground set E arrives as a stream and f is a nonnegative submodular function under a cardinality constraint.
- Motivation/applications: Useful when selected items may later become unavailable (e.g., recommendations where items are later removed), enabling post-hoc deletions without rescanning the stream.
- Main contribution: A streaming algorithm achieving a constant-factor approximation for the robust objective.
- Output size guarantee: Returns T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k, from which a k-element solution survives any m deletions.
- Analysis: Guarantees established under standard submodularity assumptions; analysis is nontrivial and appears correct.
- Strengths: Thoughtful summary construction, careful handling of deletions, and clearly stated theoretical guarantees.
- Weaknesses/concern: Motivation and practical use cases are lightly developed, limiting perceived relevance to the broader ML conference audience; needs stronger empirical/real-world integration.
