- Proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (streaming; partitioning + decreasing threshold) followed by a simple greedy stage.  
- Theoretically, STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation for arbitrary k (Thm. 4.1); Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show STAR-T+greedy copes with element loss.  
- Experiments show good performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.  
- Novel contribution: likely the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization; streaming setting has broad applicability.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (tau, w); Thm. 4.5 gives an approach to approximate tau and experiments suggest w=1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Additional concern: influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which differs from the canonical Kempe et al. spread model, so performance under the standard model is unclear.
