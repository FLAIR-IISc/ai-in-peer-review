- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in streaming — build T so that after deleting any m elements one can still extract a k-element set S that approximately maximizes a submodular objective f.  
- Applications: Submodular maximization has many uses; robustness is useful when selected items may later be removed (e.g., recommendations excluding disliked items).  
- Result: Presents a constant-factor approximation algorithm returning T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.  
- Correctness: Reviewer believes the results are correct.  
- Technical novelty: Solution is non-trivial; both the algorithm and its analysis are technically challenging.  
- Concern: Main issue is conference relevance — weak motivation/applications and a formulation that may not be general enough for broad impact.
