- Tackles submodular maximization with combined streaming and robustness constraints (select k from a stream, robust to up to m removals).  
- Claims the first framework to jointly address streaming and robustness.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm maintaining O((m log k + k) log² k) elements after the stream.  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149 and robustness to m element removals.  
- Uses partitioning and thresholding with a novel bucket-filling strategy adapting prior robust techniques.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show strong performance, sometimes outperforming baselines that know removed elements.  
- The joint streaming+robustness result and constant-factor guarantee are a significant theoretical advance.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds, leaving approximation optimality unclear.  
- Analysis is under-explained: the paper fails to clarify how its "innovative analysis" differs from prior work or present core proof ideas.  
- Influence maximization experiments use an overly simplified neighborhood-size objective, not standard network-dependent formulations.  
- Unclear how network-dependent influence objectives would be handled in the proposed streaming framework.  
- Recommendations: add lower bounds, elaborate analytic innovations, and use more realistic influence-maximization objectives to strengthen impact and credibility.
