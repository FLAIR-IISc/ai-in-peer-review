- Problem: Robust streaming submodular maximization—select a set T from a stream so that after any m removals, a greedy algorithm can still find a near-optimal size-k solution for a monotone submodular f.  
- Motivation: Aimed at summaries resilient to component loss (e.g., changing user preferences, “right to be forgotten”), but motivating applications feel limited.  
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T using a partitioned structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds to achieve robustness.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a constant-factor approximation with space O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Relation to prior work: Extends SIEVE-STREAMING and connects to robust optimization, robust influence maximization, and interactive submodular work.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Analysis and proofs appear sound and non-trivial (supplementary material referenced).  
- Strength — Novelty: Combines streaming, robustness, and submodularity with a creative partitioned thresholding approach.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Experiments (dominating set, movie recommendation) show competitive performance, even versus methods that see removed elements.  
- Weakness — Application impact: Examples are illustrative but not compelling; could better explore real-world connections like “right to be forgotten.”  
- Weakness — Generality: Focuses on fixed m removals and doesn’t handle unbounded or adaptive removal scenarios.  
- Weakness — Space complexity: The O((m log k + k) log^2 k) space may be prohibitive for very large datasets or large m, k.  
- For acceptance: Technically solid, novel, strong theory and experiments, advances robust submodular optimization.  
- Against acceptance: Limited motivation/generalizability and uncertain broader ML community impact for NIPS.  
- Overall: Solid technical contribution; reviewer leans weak accept contingent on stronger application discussion and acknowledgment of generality/space limitations.
