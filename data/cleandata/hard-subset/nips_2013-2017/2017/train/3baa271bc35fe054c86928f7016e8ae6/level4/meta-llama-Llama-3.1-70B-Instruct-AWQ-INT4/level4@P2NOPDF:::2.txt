This paper presents a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage involves the design of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage employs a simple greedy method. Notably, the authors establish that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate the effectiveness of combining STAR-T with the simple greedy method in handling element losses.

The experimental results indicate that the proposed algorithm performs well on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is particularly interesting as it introduces the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which may have a wide range of applications. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w, and the authors provide a method to approximate τ in Theorem 4.5. Moreover, the experiments show that setting w=1 yields satisfactory results, making it convenient to use STAR-T without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

However, my primary concern is that the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive. Specifically, the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Additionally, the objective function used for influence maximization, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function presented in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T would perform on the spread model described in the original article, and this discrepancy warrants further investigation.
