- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a novel streaming algorithm STAR-T followed by a simple greedy post-processing.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing threshold rule to build a robust summary in streaming settings.  
- Theoretical guarantee: STAR-T attains a constant approximation even as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show that combining STAR-T with the greedy stage effectively mitigates element loss.  
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation shows the algorithm can be effective in practice.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potential for broad streaming applications.  
- Algorithm has two hyperparameters (τ and w); the paper gives a way to approximate τ (Theorem 4.5) and finds w = 1 works well empirically, reducing tuning.  
- Concern: experimental evaluation is limited and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) deviates from the standard diffusion model in Kempe et al. (2003), leaving STAR-T’s performance under the usual spread model unclear.
