- Paper studies robust single-pass streaming submodular maximization with a small retained summary and up to m adversarial deletions before query-time selection.
- Proposes STAR-T: partitioned buckets with exponentially growing sizes and exponentially decreasing thresholds, plus STAR-T-GREEDY for query-time selection.
- Theoretically proves a constant-factor approximation (≈0.149 asymptotically) after any m deletions while storing O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements.
- Extends streaming thresholding ideas to robustness by combining bucketed storage with decreasing thresholds; relates to non-streaming robust bucket methods.
- Empirical evaluation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation shows STAR-T matches or outperforms strong baselines, even when baselines know deletions.
- Novelty: first constant-factor result for robust streaming setting with near-linear memory in k plus O(m log^2 k) overhead.
- Algorithmic design is natural and efficient; the reusable summary for multiple deletions is practically compelling for interactive scenarios.
- Strong theoretical contribution: one-pass algorithm with provable constant-factor robustness and bounded memory.
- Empirics are credible and competitive against oracle-advantaged streaming baselines.
- Exposition of the analysis is unclear about what is technically new beyond prior bucket structures.
- Paper should isolate lemmas relying on exponential thresholds and clarify the balancing argument that yields 0.149.
- Two-case analysis (many full buckets vs. mostly non-full) needs a self-contained high-level outline before technical details.
- Missing complementary lower bounds to contextualize the 0.149 constant and the space complexity trade-offs.
- Use of dominating set as a surrogate for influence maximization is a significant simplification; applicability to IC/LT diffusion objectives is not discussed.
- Practical details about multi-guess threshold τ are sketched but lack explicit space/time overhead and sensitivity analysis.
- Presentation would benefit from a clearer roadmap of proof ideas and explicit highlighting of novel analytical components.
- Originality: the combination of partitioned buckets and thresholding for robust streaming is novel even if components have precedents.
- Significance: results are relevant for scalable robust summarization and useful for answering multiple post-deletion queries from one summary.
- Pros summarized: first constant-factor robust streaming algorithm, clean design, reusable summary, solid bounds and empirical performance.
- Cons summarized: limited discussion of diffusion models, lack of lower bounds and tightness analysis for the constant, and unclear exposition of core innovations.
- Suggested improvements: add a high-level proof outline emphasizing novelty, discuss streaming handling of diffusion-based influence functions, and include sensitivity studies for τ-guessing and memory parameters.
- Suggested improvements (cont.): provide, if possible, an impossibility/trade-off or lower-bound result to contextualize approximation vs. memory.
