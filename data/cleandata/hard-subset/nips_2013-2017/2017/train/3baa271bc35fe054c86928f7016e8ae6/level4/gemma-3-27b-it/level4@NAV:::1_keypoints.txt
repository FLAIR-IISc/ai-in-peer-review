- Problem: maintain a robust set T in a stream so that after deletion of any m elements a k-sized subset S maximizing a submodular function f can still be found.
- Objective: select T to guarantee a near-optimal k-element solution remains attainable post-deletions.
- Motivation: leverages broad applicability of submodular maximization; robustness motivated by practical needs (e.g., recommendation systems with user feedback).
- Contribution: a constant-factor approximation algorithm for the robust streaming selection problem.
- Guarantee: constructs T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.
- Technical assessment: the algorithm and analysis are sound, correct, and technically deep.
- Concern: paper’s fit to the conference is questionable—presented applications seem tenuous and the formulation lacks broad generalizability.
