- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting with compact summaries resilient to removal of up to m elements.  
- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm using partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds.  
- Proves a constant-factor 0.149 approximation guarantee after any m-element removal.  
- Summary size/memory bound is O((k + m log k) log^2 k).  
- Extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting and reduces memory dependence on k from linear to logarithmic compared to concurrent work [18].  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and self-contained, generalizing prior robust approaches and enabling robustness for any m.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show STAR-T-GREEDY matches or exceeds baselines, even when baselines know the removed elements.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear algorithms and supplementary proofs aiding reproducibility.  
- Missing lower bounds for the approximation constant, leaving open whether 0.149 is tight.  
- Proof sketch lacks explicit contrast with prior work [11], making the key analytical innovation (exponential threshold decay) less clear.  
- Influence maximization experiment uses a simplified deterministic objective |N(Z) ∪ Z| and does not discuss extending STAR-T to standard probabilistic influence models.  
- Overall: significant and novel contribution to streaming + robust submodular optimization; recommend accept with minor revisions to address lower bounds, analysis clarity, and influence-model extensions.
