- Tackles submodular maximization under both streaming and post-stream adversarial removal (robustness) constraints — first work to combine these two challenges.  
- Well-motivated setting: single-pass streaming for large-scale data plus robustness to element unavailability.  
- Proposes STAR-T: single-pass algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements.  
- Achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation guarantee robust to removal of up to m elements.  
- Technical approach: novel bucket-filling strategy combining partitioning and thresholding, extending prior robust submodular ideas.  
- Empirical results (influence maximization and movie recommendation) show strong performance, even beating non-robust streaming baselines with oracle removal knowledge.  
- Major theoretical strength: obtaining a constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is significant.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize the upper-bound result.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis presentation is unclear; the claimed "innovative" analysis and provided proof sketch are hard to parse and insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness: influence-maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than standard influence spread; paper lacks discussion on adapting global influence metrics to the streaming model.
