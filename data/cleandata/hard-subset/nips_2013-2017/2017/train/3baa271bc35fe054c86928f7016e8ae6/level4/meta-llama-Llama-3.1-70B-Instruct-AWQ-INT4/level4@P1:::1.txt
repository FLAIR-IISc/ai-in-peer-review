The paper "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach" studies the problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting, with the additional requirement of robustness against the removal of a certain number of elements. The goal is to find a set of elements that is not only optimal for the submodular function but also remains nearly optimal even after the removal of up to m elements.

The problem has various applications in large-scale machine learning tasks, such as data summarization, influence maximization, facility location, and exemplar-based clustering. The authors argue that the robustness requirement is especially important in scenarios where the extracted representative set may be subject to removals due to failures, user preferences, or other factors.

The paper proposes a two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) and a query algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY). STAR-T makes one pass over the data and constructs a set of elements that is robust against the removal of up to m elements. STAR-T-GREEDY then takes this set and returns a subset of size at most k that maximizes the submodular function.

The authors prove that their algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the robust submodular function maximization problem. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two data summarization tasks, showing that it matches or outperforms existing greedy and streaming methods.
