- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (robust streaming) followed by a simple greedy selection.  
- STAR-T is built on a partitioning structure with a decreasing-threshold rule.  
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T attains a constant-factor approximation for arbitrarily large k.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: STAR-T combined with greedy remains effective under element deletions.  
- Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with potentially broad applicability.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate τ and experiments indicate w=1 works well in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation includes influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Main concern: experimental results are not very compelling—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Influence maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) deviates from standard diffusion-based models (Kempe et al.), leaving STAR-T’s performance on standard spread models unclear.  
- Suggestion (implied): clarify the objective discrepancy and evaluate STAR-T on common influence propagation models to strengthen empirical claims.
