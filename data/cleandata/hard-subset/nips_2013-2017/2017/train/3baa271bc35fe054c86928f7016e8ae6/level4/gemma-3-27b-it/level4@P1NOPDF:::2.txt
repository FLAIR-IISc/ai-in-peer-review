This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint *k*. The first stage introduces a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. A simple greedy method is then employed in the second stage. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as *k* approaches infinity (Thm 4.1). Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that STAR-T, combined with the simple greedy method, effectively handles element loss.

Experimental results demonstrate the algorithm's performance on both influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is significant as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting has the potential for a wide range of applications, and this work provides valuable insights in that direction. The STAR-T algorithm incorporates two hyperparameters, τ and *w*. The authors provide guidance for approximating τ in Theorem 4.5 and show through experiments that setting *w* = 1 yields strong performance. This simplifies the use of STAR-T, as it reduces the need for extensive hyperparameter tuning.

However, a primary concern is the limited expressiveness of the experimental evaluation. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. 

Furthermore, the objective function used for Influence Maximization – f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219) – differs from the function presented in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). Consequently, it is unclear how STAR-T performs within the standard influence spread model.
