- Studies streaming robust monotone submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint and resilience to removal of any m elements.
- Problem motivated by large-scale ML tasks (data summarization, influence maximization, personalized recommendation).
- Thorough related-work review covering submodular maximization, streaming algorithms, and robust optimization.
- Proposes a two-stage approach: STAR-T streaming algorithm (partitioned thresholding) and STAR-T-GREEDY query-stage algorithm.
- STAR-T builds a robust data summary; STAR-T-GREEDY returns a constant-factor approximation after removals.
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis: approximation guarantee, memory usage, and computational complexity.
- Empirical validation on two real-world datasets with comparisons to existing methods (including SIEVE-STREAMING).
- Results are technically sound and demonstrate strong algorithm performance.
- Concern that relevance to the NIPS conference is unclear and applications/motivation could be strengthened.
- Paper might be more suitable for an optimization/algorithms venue despite its ML relevance.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen application motivation and clarify NIPS relevance.
