- Studies robust submodular maximization in streaming: maintain summary T so after any m deletions a size-k S ⊆ T\D has constant-factor value vs optimal.
- Algorithm achieves constant-factor approximation with summary size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).
- Addresses a timely combination of streaming constraints and robustness to adversarial deletions.
- Provides polylogarithmic dependence on k and linear dependence on m in memory, appealing for streaming.
- Algorithmic ideas and analysis appear nontrivial and technically correct.
- Offers a principled method to build a robust coreset T for post-deletion recovery.
- Motivation and real-world applications are underdeveloped and not convincingly argued.
- Empirical evaluation is missing or minimal; synthetic and baseline comparisons are needed.
- Key modeling/assumption details are unclear (monotonicity, oracle access, whether k and m must be known, deletion timing/adversary model).
- Practicality of the space bound and hidden constants, plus per-element update time, is unclear.
- Positioning relative to prior streaming and robust submodular work needs strengthening and clearer comparisons.
- Presentation is generally good but definitions and assumptions should be centralized and stated precisely early.
- Main theorem should be stated clearly with all quantifiers, parameter dependencies, and the approximation factor highlighted.
- Include pseudocode, a high-level roadmap of invariants, and an intuitive example showing recovery after deletions.
- Questions: is f monotone/normalized; extend to non-monotone or matroids; does algorithm need k/m; are deletions adversarial/only after stream; what are hidden constants and update time; are lower bounds known?
- Suggestions: strengthen motivation with concrete use cases, add empirical results, expand related work to delineate novelty, discuss extensions and limitations, and tighten notation/grammar.
- Overall: technically solid and novel at the intersection of streaming and robustness but needs better motivation, clearer modeling, stronger positioning, and empirical validation to be compelling.
