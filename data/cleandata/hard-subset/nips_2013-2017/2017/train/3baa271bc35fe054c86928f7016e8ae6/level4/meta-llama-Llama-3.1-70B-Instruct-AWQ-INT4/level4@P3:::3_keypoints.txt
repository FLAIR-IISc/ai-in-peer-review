- Problem studied: robust submodular maximization in streaming setting where k items are selected while up to m items may be removed post-stream.  
- Proposed method: STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and is robust to removal of m items.  
- Theoretical guarantee: STAR-T achieves a constant 0.149 approximation factor.  
- Technical novelty: introduces a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule built on prior bucket ideas.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on streaming and robust submodular maximization literature, extending bucket-based approaches.  
- Main contribution: design and analysis of STAR-T with provable constant-factor robustness in streaming.  
- Experiments: empirical validation on influence maximization and movie recommendation showing effectiveness.  
- Strength: addresses a challenging combined streaming+robust problem and attains a strong constant-factor result.  
- Strength: experimental results are convincing for the presented applications.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound analysis.  
- Weakness: analysis writing is unclear; needs clearer exposition of innovations and main proof steps.  
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments could be more convincing using standard objectives.  
- Originality: combination of streaming and robustness and the new partitioning/thresholding ideas are novel.  
- Significance: relevant to large-scale ML and data summarization with implications for robust streaming algorithms.  
- Clarity & quality: generally well-written with strong theoretical analysis, but analysis exposition should be improved.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address lower bounds, clearer analysis, and stronger influence-maximization evaluation.
