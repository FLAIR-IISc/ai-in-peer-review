- Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming with partitioned decreasing-threshold selection, then greedy post-processing) for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k.
- Proves STAR-T gives a constant-factor approximation independent of k (Thm. 4.1) and shows robustness to element loss with Lemmas 4.2–4.3.
- Empirically evaluates on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation, reporting τ approximation (Thm. 4.5) and that w=1 works well.
- Novel contribution: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with potential broad applicability.
- Strong theory: solid analysis and robustness guarantees that do not deteriorate with k.
- Practical design: few hyperparameters and guidance for τ reduces tuning burden.
- Demonstrated scope: experiments on two representative tasks suggest generalizability.
- Experimental weakness: STAR-T+Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming in non-robust settings, weakening practical superiority claims.
- Influence maximization objective uses coverage proxy f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| rather than IC/LT expected spread, raising concerns about empirical relevance.
- Comparative baselines are limited; need stronger/diverse and robust/deletion-aware methods for fair comparison across settings.
- Reporting gaps: lacks detailed runtime, memory, and throughput metrics for the streaming stage.
- Question on deletion model: clarify adversarial vs. random deletions, max m, and how guarantees scale with m, plus experiments varying m.
- Question on threshold τ: sensitivity beyond Thm. 4.5 approximation and possibility of adaptive/data-driven estimation in the stream.
- Question on w: request ablation over w values and discussion of robustness vs. utility vs. memory trade-offs.
- Question on influence objective: justify coverage proxy or provide correlations/experiments with IC/LT spread on smaller graphs.
- Suggest expanding robustness experiments by varying deletion number/strategy to show where STAR-T+Greedy outperforms non-robust baselines.
- Suggest including IC/LT diffusion experiments or strong empirical evidence that coverage closely tracks expected spread.
- Suggest ablations/sensitivity analyses for τ and w and reporting time/memory overheads to validate streaming practicality.
- Suggest presentation fixes: rephrase “k up to infinity,” ensure consistent theorem numbering, and give more intuition on partitioning and threshold schedule.
- Overall: theoretically strong and novel but empirical validation—especially under deletions and canonical IM models—and efficiency/sensitivity reporting need strengthening for a compelling practical case.
