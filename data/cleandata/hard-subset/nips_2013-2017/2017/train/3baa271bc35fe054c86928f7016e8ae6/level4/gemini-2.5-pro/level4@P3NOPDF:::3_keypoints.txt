- Addresses joint problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting, a novel and practical combination of constraints.  
- Proposes STAR-T: a single-pass streaming algorithm using O((m log k + k) log^2 k) memory.  
- Key theoretical result: constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149 for the robust objective.  
- Algorithmic approach adapts partitioning/thresholding and "bucket" techniques from prior robust submodular work to the streaming context.  
- Empirical results show STAR-T is competitive on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus an oracle-based streaming baseline.  
- Strength: Tackles a novel, well-motivated problem at the intersection of streaming and robust optimization.  
- Strength: Achieves a non-trivial constant-factor approximation in a highly constrained model — a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: Demonstrates promising practical performance, with compelling comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Weakness: No discussed lower bounds to contextualize the 0.149 approximation or show near-optimality.  
- Weakness: Theoretical analysis and proof sketch are dense and lack a clear, high-level exposition of novel ideas.  
- Weakness: Influence maximization experiment uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective that may not reflect standard IC/LT models; needs better justification or broader evaluation.
