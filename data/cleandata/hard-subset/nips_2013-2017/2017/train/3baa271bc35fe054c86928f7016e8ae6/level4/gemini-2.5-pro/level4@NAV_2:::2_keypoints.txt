- Addresses robust monotone submodular maximization under a streaming model with adversarial removal of m elements.  
- Proposes a two-stage method: single-pass STAR-T builds a robust summary, then STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on the summary after removals.  
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioned bucket structure with exponentially decreasing thresholds for element assignment.  
- Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee with theoretical analysis (Theorem 4.1).  
- Offers a practical scheme to handle unknown optimal value for threshold parameter τ (Theorem 4.5).  
- Extends thresholding ideas from SIEVE-STREAMING and adapts partitioning from PRO to the robust streaming setting.  
- Claims a memory complexity with logarithmic dependence on k, improving over concurrent work [18] which is linear in k.  
- Paper is generally well-written and the algorithm/proofs are clearly presented.  
- Experiments show STAR-T-GREEDY performs comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING, but baselines were given the advantage of knowing the removed set, weakening the robustness claim.  
- Experimental choice: uses deterministic dominating-set objective but frames it as influence maximization, which is inconsistent with stochastic IC/LT models—needs clarification or experiments with standard stochastic models.
