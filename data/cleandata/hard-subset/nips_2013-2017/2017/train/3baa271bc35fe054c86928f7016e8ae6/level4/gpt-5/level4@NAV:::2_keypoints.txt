- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k.  
- Stage 1: STAR-T, a streaming algorithm using a partitioning scheme with a decreasing threshold rule.  
- Stage 2: apply a simple greedy selection on STAR-T’s output.  
- Theorem 4.1: constant-factor approximation guarantee for arbitrarily large k.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: STAR-T combined with greedy can tolerate element loss/deletions.  
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Novelty: likely the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization; streaming setting has broad applicability.  
- Hyperparameters: STAR-T uses τ and w; Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate τ and experiments indicate w=1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is weak—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming.  
- Modeling concern: influence objective used is f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which differs from standard diffusion models (Kempe et al.), so performance under standard spread models is unclear.
