- Tackles robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting with practical relevance.
- Proposes STAR-T: two-stage algorithm with novel partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds in streaming, plus a greedy query stage.
- Provides constant-factor approximation guarantee as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Evaluates empirically on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- First streaming algorithm specifically designed for robust submodular maximization (novel contribution).
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and gives a solid foundation for performance claims.
- Practical considerations addressed: estimating threshold τ and showing good performance with w = 1.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.
- Experimental comparisons to SIEVE-STREAMING are mixed and need deeper analysis of when each method wins.
- Justification for chosen removal strategies (random vs. greedy) is insufficient.
- Discrepancy between experimental dominating set objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| and standard influence maximization needs clarification and discussion of validity as a proxy.
- Limited sensitivity analysis for parameter w; more exploration of trade-offs is needed.
- Space complexity O((k + m log k) log k) stated but lacks intuitive scaling examples for large m and k.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel important problem, sound theory, promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently comprehensive experiments, objective-function discrepancy, and limited parameter analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to compare STAR-T vs SIEVE-STREAMING more thoroughly, clarify the dominating set objective, add w sensitivity analysis, and give a concrete space-complexity example.
