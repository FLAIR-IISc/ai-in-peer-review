- Novel setting: first paper to jointly study streaming and robust submodular maximization (select k items while retaining few items and being robust to m removals).  
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes STAR-T, which maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 constant approximation while tolerating removal of m items.  
- Techniques: combines partitioning, exponentially decreasing thresholding, and bucket-based filling (similar in spirit to prior robust and streaming algorithms but with different bucket filling).  
- Theoretical analysis: provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee with a thorough (but dense) proof.  
- Empirical evaluation: shows strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even against streaming baselines that know which items are removed.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on centralized robust submodular maximization and streaming algorithms like SIEVE-STREAMING, adapting ideas to the robust-streaming setting.  
- Strength: novel algorithmic structure and solid experimental validation across applications.  
- Strength: technically sound analysis and clear overall presentation.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper bound.  
- Weakness: analysis writing is hard to parse—the claimed “innovative analysis” is not clearly highlighted or explained in the proof sketch.  
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a very simplified neighborhood-size objective, which may not reflect traditional network-wide influence objectives.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to submodular streaming and robustness, but would be improved by clearer analysis exposition, realistic IM objectives, and lower bounds.
