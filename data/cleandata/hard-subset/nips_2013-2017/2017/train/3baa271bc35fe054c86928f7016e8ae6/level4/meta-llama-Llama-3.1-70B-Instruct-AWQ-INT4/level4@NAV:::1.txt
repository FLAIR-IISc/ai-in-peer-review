**Problem Studied**: This paper addresses the problem of identifying a subset of elements T from a ground set E in a streaming context, such that even after removing any m elements from T, a subset S of k elements can be found that maximizes a submodular function f.

**Applications**: While maximizing submodular functions has numerous applications and existing streaming algorithms, the robust version of this problem is useful in scenarios where elements may need to be removed after an initial solution is found, such as in personalized movie recommendations.

**Results**: The authors present a constant-factor approximation algorithm for this problem, yielding a set T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.

**Comments**: To the best of my understanding, the paper's results are correct, and the proposed algorithm and its analysis are technically sound and non-trivial. However, my primary concern lies in the paper's relevance to the conference. The applications and motivations presented are somewhat weak, and the problem scope may not be sufficiently general to warrant inclusion in the conference program.
