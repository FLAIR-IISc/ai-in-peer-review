The paper "Streaming Robust Submodular Maximization: A Partitioned Thresholding Approach" studies the problem of maximizing a monotone submodular function subject to a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting with an additional robustness requirement. Specifically, the goal is to find a set of elements in a streaming fashion such that, if any m elements are removed from the set, a constant-factor approximation to the optimal solution can still be obtained.

The problem is well-motivated by various applications in large-scale machine learning tasks, such as data summarization, influence maximization, and personalized recommendation. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including previous studies on submodular maximization, streaming algorithms, and robust optimization.

The paper proposes a novel two-stage procedure, consisting of a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) and a query-stage algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY). The STAR-T algorithm uses a partitioned thresholding approach to construct a robust summary of the data, which is then used by STAR-T-GREEDY to obtain a constant-factor approximation to the optimal solution. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's performance, including its approximation guarantee, memory requirements, and computational complexity.

The paper's results are technically sound, and the algorithm's performance is demonstrated through experiments on two real-world datasets. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with existing algorithms, including the SIEVE-STREAMING algorithm, which is allowed to know in advance which elements will be removed.

However, the relevance of this paper to the NIPS conference is not entirely clear. While the problem is interesting and the solution is non-trivial, the applications and their motivation could be stronger. The paper's contribution to the field of machine learning is significant, but it may be more suitable for a conference focused on optimization or algorithms.
