- Addresses streaming + robust submodular maximization: select k high-value elements from a retained set while m items may be removed post-stream.  
- Novelty: first paper to combine streaming and robustness in submodular maximization.  
- Algorithm: proposes STAR-T using partitioning and thresholding with buckets.  
- Theoretical guarantees: retains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149 approximation, robust to removal of m elements.  
- Builds on prior robust submodular optimization techniques.  
- Experiments: evaluated on influence maximization and movie recommendation, outperforming streaming baselines even when they know removed items.  
- Strength: achieving a constant-factor approximation in this challenging setting is significant.  
- Weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.  
- Weakness: analysis presentation is unclear; “innovative analysis” is asserted but not elucidated.  
- Weakness: influence maximization experiment needs stronger justification/adaptation for traditional network-wide objectives in a streaming setting.
