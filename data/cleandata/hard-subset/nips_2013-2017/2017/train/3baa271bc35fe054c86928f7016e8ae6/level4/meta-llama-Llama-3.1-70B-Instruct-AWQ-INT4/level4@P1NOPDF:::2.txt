This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint of k. The procedure consists of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, in the first stage, which relies on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule, followed by the simple greedy method in the second stage. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that STAR-T, combined with the simple greedy method, can effectively handle the loss of elements.

The experimental results show that the proposed algorithm performs well on the problems of influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. This work is interesting, as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have a wide range of applications, and this work provides valuable insights. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w. The authors provide a method to approximate τ in Theorem 4.5 and show that setting w = 1 yields good results in the experiments, making it convenient to use STAR-T without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

However, my major concern is that the experiments are not very comprehensive. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Additionally, I find it unusual that the objective function for influence maximization is defined as f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219 in this paper), which differs from the function presented in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T performs on the standard spread model, which is a significant limitation of the current evaluation.
