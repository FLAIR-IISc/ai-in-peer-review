- Problem studied: robust streaming submodular maximization—compute T of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) in one pass so any m deletions still leave a k-set with constant-factor approximation to optimum.

- Main contribution: novel algorithm and rigorous analysis achieving a constant-factor approximation under the stated size bound.

- Technical merit: sophisticated techniques to handle adversarial deletions in streaming and a size bound that improves naive approaches.

- Motivation weakness: relevance to NeurIPS is questionable due to weak and narrow motivation.

- Application example critique: the movie-recommendation example is superficial and does not justify the need for post-hoc robustness over incremental feedback methods.

- Model limitations: assumes arbitrary deletions and non-monotone f, reducing applicability to common monotone submodular problems like coverage/diversity.

- Comparison gap: authors do not explain why existing streaming algorithms are insufficient for their use case.

- Empirical gap: no experiments or empirical comparisons to show practical necessity or baseline performance.

- Scope narrowness: focuses on exact deletions without addressing noise, partial feedback, or dynamic updates, limiting impact.

- Overclaiming: title/abstract overstate broad utility while motivation remains speculative.

- Summary critique: technically sound but limited impact due to poor application justification, narrow problem scope, and lack of experiments.

- Suggested improvements: identify concrete applications requiring robustness (e.g., sensor failures), generalize to monotone functions, and provide empirical comparisons.

- Recommendation: reject for NeurIPS.
