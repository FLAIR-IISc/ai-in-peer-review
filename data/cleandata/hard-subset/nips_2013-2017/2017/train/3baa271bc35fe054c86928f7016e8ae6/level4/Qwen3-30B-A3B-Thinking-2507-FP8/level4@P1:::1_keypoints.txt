- Problem: two-stage robust streaming submodular maximization—build compact summary S in one pass, then after any m removals produce k-sized solution Z with approximation guarantee.  
- Algorithms: propose STAR-T (streaming) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query-stage) using partitioned thresholding.  
- Key innovation: partitioned thresholding with exponentially increasing partitions and decreasing thresholds (τ/2^i) yields robustness without prior knowledge of removed set E.  
- Theoretical guarantee: 0.149(1 − 1/log k)-approximation (approaches 0.149 as k → ∞).  
- Memory bound: O((k + m log k) log k) space, better practical scaling than concurrent work [18] with linear k-dependence.  
- Analysis approach: balances two cases (many full buckets vs. sparse partitions) and leverages submodularity and greedy properties for tight bounds.  
- Relation to prior work: extends SIEVE-STREAMING to robust setting and adapts Bogunovic et al.’s partitioning to a one-pass, sublinear-space streaming algorithm.  
- Empirical results: strong performance on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks; STAR-T-GREEDY matches/exceeds SIEVE-STREAMING even when SIEVE knows E.  
- Practical benefit: two-stage design supports interactive use cases (e.g., regenerating recommendations after preference changes).  
- Strengths: technically novel partitioning/thresholding, rigorous analysis, tight constant-factor guarantee, and empirical validation.  
- Weakness: application motivation is weakly connected to NIPS-relevant ML challenges and needs stronger framing for modern streaming/adaptive ML scenarios.  
- Suggested improvement: better contextualize robustness in contemporary ML settings (adaptive recommendations, sensor networks, influence maximization) and cite adaptive submodular work.  
- Overall verdict: strong technical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen motivation and ML relevance.
