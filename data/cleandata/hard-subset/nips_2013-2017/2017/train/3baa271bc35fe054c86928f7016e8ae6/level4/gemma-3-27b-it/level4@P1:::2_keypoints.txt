- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T (robust streaming with partitioning and decreasing thresholds) followed by a simple greedy selection.
- Proves STAR-T gives a constant-factor approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1) and uses Lemmas 4.2–4.4 to show robustness to element loss when combined with greedy.
- Empirically evaluated on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, filling a gap in the literature.
- STAR-T uses two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate τ and experiments indicate w = 1 works well.
- Main concern: experimental evaluation lacks expressiveness—STAR-T-GREEDY is comparable to but does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING.
- Specific discrepancy: influence maximization experiment uses f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, which differs from the standard influence spread model in the cited Kempe et al. reference.
- Argument for acceptance: novelty of approach, theoretical guarantees, practical relevance to large-scale ML, and clear presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: limited and not overwhelmingly convincing experimental validation, non-standard influence setup raising validity concerns, and a complex analysis that may be hard for some readers.
