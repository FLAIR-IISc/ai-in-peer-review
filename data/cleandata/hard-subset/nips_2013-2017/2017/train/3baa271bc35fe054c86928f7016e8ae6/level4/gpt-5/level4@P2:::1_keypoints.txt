- Paper proposes STAR-T: a two-stage single-pass streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k with up to m deletions, using partitioned exponentially-sized buckets and decreasing marginal-gain thresholds.
- After deletions, STAR-T runs GREEDY on the compact summary S\E to produce a k-element solution.
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 (approaching this as k grows).
- Memory claim: O((k + m log k) log k) if OPT known; with parallel-threshold guessing memory grows by a log_{1+ε} factor (paper text inconsistently mentions an extra log k factor).
- Connects to prior work: relates to classical GREEDY, SIEVE-STREAMING, PRO-like partitioned robust methods, and concurrent deletion-robust streaming (Mirzasoleiman et al. ICML’17).
- Novelty: combines PRO-style partitions with streaming threshold placement for deletion robustness, yielding logarithmic dependence on k in memory.
- Quality: algorithm and analysis appear technically sound; case analysis and use of submodularity are plausible and nontrivial.
- Proof clarifications needed: formalize the “on average” argument in Lemma 4.2 (use counting/averaging) for rigor.
- Pseudocode inconsistency: reconcile threshold formula τ/min{2^i, k} versus τ/2^i in text.
- Memory inconsistency: unify whether memory is O((k + m log k) log k) or O((k + m log k) log^2 k) and state assumptions (OPT-known vs guessed).
- Complexity reporting missing: provide per-item time (number of threshold checks) and query-time complexity for STAR-T-GREEDY.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear but should concretely describe OPT-guessing parameters (ε, number of instances, overhead).
- Significance: first constant-factor guarantee with polylog memory in k and near-linear dependence on m; relevant to forgetting/failure scenarios.
- Empirics: STAR-T matches or slightly outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING on tested tasks, but experiments are narrow and omit comparison to deletion-robust streaming baseline.
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled streaming structure, memory-efficient one-pass summary, deletion-agnostic and reusable, strong theoretical connection to prior techniques.
- Cons for acceptance: low approximation constant compared to 1/2−ε bounds, limited empirical evaluation, missing baseline comparisons, and clarity/inconsistency issues.
- Suggestions: reconcile memory/threshold statements, compare or discuss Mirzasoleiman et al., run sensitivity analyses over m/k and deletion types, report runtime/throughput and overhead of multiple guesses, and expand datasets/ablations.
- Overall assessment: technically solid and novel with an appealing memory–approximation trade-off; borderline accept pending clarity fixes and stronger empirical evaluation.
