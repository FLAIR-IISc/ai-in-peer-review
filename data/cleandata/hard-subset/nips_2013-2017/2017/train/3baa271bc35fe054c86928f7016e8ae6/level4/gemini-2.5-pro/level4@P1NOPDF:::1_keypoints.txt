- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in the streaming model—select T in one pass that remains useful after up to m adversarial deletions.
- Objective: From T, after any m deletions, still be able to extract a k-element subset S that achieves high submodular value f.
- Motivation: Practical scenarios (e.g., recommender systems) where elements of a computed solution may be removed post-computation.
- Main contribution: A streaming algorithm with a constant-factor approximation that outputs a robust set T of size O((m log k + k) log² k).
- Technical assessment: The results appear correct; the algorithm and analysis are non-trivial and technically sophisticated.
- Concern: Relevance and impact to the ML community are unclear—motivation and applications are not sufficiently compelling.
- Limitation: The robustness model feels narrow; the paper needs a stronger case that this model is broadly applicable and addresses pressing practical needs.
