- Problem: Studies robust streaming submodular maximization—select T in a stream so a constant-factor approximation still holds after any m removals.
- Contribution: Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage algorithm using a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds.
- Output size: STAR-T maintains a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log^2 k).
- Query stage: Uses STAR-T-GREEDY to extract a near-optimal solution from the remaining elements after removals.
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a constant-factor approximation with a technically sound analysis.
- Novelty: The partitioning and thresholding ideas are new and address the robustness constraint under streaming.
- Empirical validation: Experiments on dominating set and MovieLens 1M show STAR-T matches or outperforms baselines (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING).
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear definitions, consistent notation, and informative figures.
- Motivation weakness: Application examples (influence maximization, recommender systems) are superficial and lack compelling real-world cases.
- Specificity concern: Problem formulation is relatively specific and dependence on m may limit broader applicability.
- Space complexity concern: Summary size’s logarithmic factors could hurt scalability for very large k and m.
- Related work: Comparison to related papers (notably [18]) is insufficient; trade-offs in memory, approximation, and runtime need clearer discussion.
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses a relevant, challenging problem with significant technical contribution and supporting experiments.
- Rejection arguments: Weak application motivation, limited broader impact, and potential scalability issues.
- Overall: Solid technical work, borderline but leaning toward accept if authors better motivate applications and discuss space limitations.
