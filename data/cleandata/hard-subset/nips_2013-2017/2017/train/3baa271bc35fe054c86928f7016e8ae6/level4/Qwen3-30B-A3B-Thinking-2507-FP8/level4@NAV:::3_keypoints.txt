- Problem: first work to jointly address streaming submodular maximization with post-stream robustness to removal of m elements.  
- Motivation: streaming submodular maximization is important for large-scale limited-pass applications; robustness is increasingly relevant for element removals.  
- Contribution: proposes STAR-T algorithm that maintains O((m log k + k) log² k) elements during streaming.  
- Guarantee: STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation (0.149) while tolerating m removals.  
- Method: combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket-filling strategies adapted from prior robust submodular work.  
- Experiments: shows strong empirical performance on influence maximization (simplified objective) and movie recommendation, outperforming some baselines.  
- Theoretical weakness: no non-trivial lower bounds are provided to complement the upper bound.  
- Presentation weakness: analysis/proof sketch is inadequate and does not clearly explain the novel analytical steps.  
- Experimental weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that does not reflect standard network-based influence functions and raises questions about streaming applicability.
