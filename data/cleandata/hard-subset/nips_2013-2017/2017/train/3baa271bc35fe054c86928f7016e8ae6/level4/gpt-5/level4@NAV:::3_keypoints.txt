- Novelty: First work to jointly address streaming and robust submodular maximization.  
- Problem setup: Select k high-value elements in a stream while storing few items and remaining robust to up to m deletions.  
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T, storing O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieving a 0.149 constant-factor approximation under m removals.  
- Technique: Combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket structures; bucket-filling differs from prior robust submodular work.  
- Empirical results: Performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus streaming baselines that know removed elements in advance.  
- Significance: Achieving a constant-factor guarantee in the simultaneous streaming+robust setting is notable given the problem’s difficulty.  
- Missing theory: Paper would benefit from nontrivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.  
- Clarity: Presentation and analysis are unclear; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not well articulated and the proof sketch is hard to follow.  
- Application concern: Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising doubts about applicability to standard diffusion-based objectives in a streaming context.
