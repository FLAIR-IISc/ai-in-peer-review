- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization: STAR-T streaming algorithm (partitioning + exponentially decreasing thresholds) followed by a greedy stage.  
- STAR-T is proven to have a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞ (Thm 4.1).  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.4 show STAR-T combined with greedy can handle loss of elements (robustness to deletions).  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Thm 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments suggest w=1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization; streaming setting may have broad applications.  
- Experiments: evaluated on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation and show promising performance on those tasks.  
- Experimental weakness: evaluation is not very expressive and STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- Modeling concern: Influence Maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the classical Kempe et al. spread model, raising questions about real IM performance.  
- Quality and clarity: technically sound proofs, thorough analysis, well-written with clear notation and helpful figures.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for submodular maximization due to the streaming formulation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, new streaming setting, and supportive experimental results.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited/insufficient experiments and questionable choice of IM objective compared to prior work.
