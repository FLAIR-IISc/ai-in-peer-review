- Problem and setting: Studies robust streaming maximization of a monotone submodular function under a cardinality constraint with up to m deletions after a one-pass stream.  
- Algorithm and pipeline: Proposes STAR-T (one-pass partitioned-threshold summary) and STAR-T-GREEDY (post-deletion greedy on S \ E) to extract k items.  
- Key design idea: Partitions with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing marginal-gain thresholds; place each item into first qualifying non-full bucket.  
- Theoretical guarantees: With known f(OPT) stores O((m log k + k) log k) items and achieves a constant-factor approx ≈0.149 · f(OPT(k, V \ E)).  
- Unknown-OPT handling: Standard parallel guesses add another log factor to memory, yielding O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Analysis approach: Proof uses case distinctions based on fullness of buckets and balances contributions across partitions.  
- Novelty and relation to prior work: Novel robust streaming partitioned-threshold scheme; improves memory dependence on k for robustness (O(m log^2 k) vs. O(m k log k/ε) in concurrent work).  
- Relation to concurrent work: Concurrent to Mirzasoleiman et al. (ICML’17) which achieves 1/2 − ε but with larger memory scaling.  
- Theory quality: Analysis appears technically sound; bounds and constant approximation are clearly stated with proof sketch.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on dominating set (Twitter/Amazon graphs) and MovieLens recommendation; STAR-T-GREEDY matches or slightly outperforms baselines that know deletions.  
- Experimental scenarios: Includes random, adversarial deletions and interactive genre-filtering demo.  
- Reproducibility: Algorithms are simple given an f oracle, but practical choices (τ, w, number of parallel instances) need clearer documentation.  
- Clarity issues to fix: Inconsistency between threshold formula τ/2^i and τ/min{2^i, k} in Algorithm 1; reconcile.  
- More clarity: Inconsistent memory bounds in abstract vs. theorems—summarize known-OPT and unknown-OPT cases together.  
- Practical guidance lacking: Specify how τ and w were chosen, number of parallel instances, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Significance: Relevant for large-scale summarization with removals; one-pass robust algorithm with compact memory and constant guarantee is a meaningful advance.  
- Strengths: Simple implementable one-pass method, improved memory scaling for robustness, constant-factor guarantee, conservative empirical comparisons where STAR-T remains competitive.  
- Main weaknesses: Low approximation constant (~0.149), extra log factor for unknown-OPT increases memory, and limited empirical scale and scope of objectives.  
- Specific suggestions: Discuss tightening the constant or w-dependence, quantify practical overhead of parallel instances, vary w empirically, and test on expensive-to-evaluate f or larger streams.  
- Presentation issues: Fix minor typos and ensure consistent notation/thresholds across text and pseudocode.  
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses an important robust streaming variant with novel algorithm and solid theory paired with practical evidence.  
- Rejection arguments: Modest approx constant, light practical guidance on parameter choices, and limited empirical scale/realism when m is large relative to k.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: Technically solid, novel contribution with room for improved constants and experiments — borderline accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
