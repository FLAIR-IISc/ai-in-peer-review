This paper tackles submodular maximization in a joint streaming and robust setting. The goal is to output k high-value elements while retaining only a small subset during the stream, and to remain robust to the post-stream removal of up to m items. Streaming submodular maximization has been widely studied because large-scale applications permit only a few passes over the data, and robustness has recently gained attention for scenarios where elements may be removed and solutions must withstand such deletions. To my knowledge, this is the first work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.

The authors propose the STAR-T algorithm, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation while being robust to the removal of m elements. The method combines partitioning and thresholding via bucket-based structures. While bucketed approaches have appeared in prior work on robust submodular optimization, here the buckets are populated differently. Experiments indicate strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, including against streaming baselines that know in advance which elements will be removed.

Given the simultaneous streaming and robustness requirements, the setting is challenging, and obtaining a constant-factor guarantee is a strong result. However, several aspects could be improved. It would be valuable to include nontrivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound. The exposition of the analysis could be clearer: the paper claims an “innovative analysis” but does not explain what is novel about it, and the proof sketch makes it hard to discern the main ideas and steps. Finally, the influence maximization application is not fully convincing. The experiments use a highly simplified objective—namely, the size of the neighborhood of the seed nodes. It remains unclear how standard influence maximization objectives, where the value depends on the entire network for any seed set, can be accommodated in a streaming setting.
