- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T (robust streaming) followed by a simple greedy selection.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k → ∞.
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3: STAR-T combined with greedy can effectively handle loss of elements.
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with broad potential applications.
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives an approximation for τ and experiments show w=1 works well.
- Major concern 1: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Stream.
- Major concern 2: the influence-max objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z|) differs from the standard spread model; unclear how STAR-T performs on true spread models and needs clarification.
