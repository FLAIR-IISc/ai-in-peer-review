This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint of k. The procedure consists of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule, followed by the simple greedy method. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that the combination of STAR-T and the simple greedy method can effectively handle the loss of elements.

The experimental results presented in the paper showcase the proposed algorithm's performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is notable for being the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, and its potential applications in the streaming setting are vast and promising. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w, and the authors provide a method to approximate τ in Theorem 4.5. Additionally, they demonstrate that setting w = 1 yields satisfactory results in the experiments, making it convenient to use the STAR-T algorithm without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

However, my primary concern is that the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm, which raises questions about its robustness. Moreover, the objective function used for influence maximization, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function used in the referenced article by Kempe et al. (2003). It is unclear how the STAR-T algorithm performs on the standard spread model, which is a significant omission.

To strengthen the paper, I recommend that the authors provide a more detailed experimental evaluation, including a comparison with additional baselines and an analysis of the algorithm's performance on the standard spread model. Additionally, they may want to consider exploring the robustness of the STAR-T algorithm to different hyperparameter settings and providing more insights into the theoretical guarantees of the proposed method.
