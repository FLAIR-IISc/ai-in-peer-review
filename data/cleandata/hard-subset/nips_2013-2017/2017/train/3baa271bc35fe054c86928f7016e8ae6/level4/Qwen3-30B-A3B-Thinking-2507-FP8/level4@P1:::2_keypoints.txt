- Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming summary + STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k.
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholds to build a robust streaming summary.
- STAR-T-GREEDY applies a simple greedy selection on the summary to produce a solution after up to m removals.
- Proves a constant-factor approximation c ≈ 0.149 as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1) with summary size O((k + m log k) log² k).
- Lemmas 4.2–4.4 establish the algorithm’s robustness to element removals supporting the main theorem.
- Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, filling a gap in prior work.
- Effectively connects and extends SIEVE-STREAMING and prior robust submodular maximization literature.
- Provides a method to approximate the threshold τ without prior knowledge of OPT(k, V\E) (Theorem 4.5).
- Empirical validation on dominating-set tasks (ego-Twitter, Amazon) and MovieLens recommender shows competitive performance.
- Demonstrates practical benefits: the same summary can be reused for multiple removal patterns.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is low; w = 1 works well in practice.
- Experimental shortcoming: SIEVE-STREAMING slightly outperforms STAR-T-GREEDY in some settings (Fig. 2b,d) without sufficient explanation.
- Methodological concern: the influence-maximization objective used (|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard expected spread definition and needs clarification.
- Overall: strong theoretical framework and practical promise, but experiments require deeper analysis and clarification of the influence objective.
