This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint *k*. In the first stage, a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, is designed, based on a novel partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. In the second stage, a simple greedy method is applied. The authors prove that STAR-T provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee as *k* approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1), and Lemmas 4.2-4.4 establish how STAR-T, combined with a greedy approach, handles element loss.

Experimental results demonstrate the algorithm’s performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is valuable as a first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, potentially opening avenues for a wide range of applications. STAR-T has two hyperparameters, τ and *w*. Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate τ, and the authors demonstrate that *w* = 1 performs well in experiments, making STAR-T convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My primary concern lies with the expressiveness of the experimental evaluation. While STAR-T-GREEDY performs comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING, it doesn’t consistently outperform it. 

Furthermore, there is a discrepancy in the objective function used for the influence maximization experiment (line 219): *f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|*, which differs from the function defined in the cited reference (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). This difference makes it unclear how STAR-T performs on the standard influence spread model.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** This paper introduces the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, addressing a critical gap in the literature.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The authors provide a constant-factor approximation guarantee for their algorithm, demonstrating its theoretical soundness.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The problem addressed – robust data summarization – is relevant to many large-scale machine learning applications.
*   **Well-structured Presentation:** The paper is generally well-organized and presents the algorithm and analysis clearly.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimental Validation:** The experimental results, while positive, are not overwhelmingly convincing. STAR-T does not consistently outperform existing methods.
*   **Discrepancy in Influence Maximization Setup:** The use of a non-standard objective function for the influence maximization experiment raises concerns about the validity of the results in that context.
*   **Complexity of Analysis:** While theoretically sound, the analysis is intricate and might be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the field to fully grasp.
