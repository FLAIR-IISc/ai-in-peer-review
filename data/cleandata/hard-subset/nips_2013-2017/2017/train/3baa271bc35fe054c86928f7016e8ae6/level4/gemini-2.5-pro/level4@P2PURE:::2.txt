This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint `k`. The first stage introduces STAR-T, a robust streaming algorithm that utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. In the second stage, a simple greedy method is applied. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as `k` approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that the combined STAR-T with the greedy method is robust to the loss of elements. Experimental results on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show that the proposed algorithm performs well.

A key contribution of this work is that it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. This is a significant step, as the streaming setting has a wide range of potential applications, making this work particularly enlightening. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w. The authors show how to approximate τ in Theorem 4.5 and demonstrate empirically that setting w=1 yields good performance. This is a practical advantage, as it simplifies the use of STAR-T by reducing the need for hyperparameter tuning.

My primary concern is that the experimental evaluation is not entirely convincing, as the proposed STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline.

Additionally, the objective function for Influence Maximization is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), which differs from the standard influence spread model in the cited work by Kempe et al. (2003). This choice makes it unclear how STAR-T would perform on the more conventional formulation of the problem.
