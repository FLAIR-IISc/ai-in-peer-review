- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a streaming, single-pass setting: choose T so that after any adversarial deletion of m elements, a k-subset S of the remainder gives a constant-factor approximation for monotone submodular f.  
- Motivated by applications like recommendation systems where users may reject some recommended items.  
- Main contribution is a streaming algorithm that achieves a constant-factor approximation and returns a robust set T of size O((m log k + k) log² k).  
- Technical results appear sound; the solution is non-trivial and the algorithm and analysis are technically challenging.  
- Primary concern is weak relevance and impact for the NIPS community: the motivation is not compelling and application scenarios feel contrived.  
- Due to the narrow problem formulation and limited practical motivation, the paper’s overall significance for the conference is questionable.
