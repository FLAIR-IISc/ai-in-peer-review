- Novel contribution: first work to jointly address streaming submodular maximization with robustness to up to m element removals.  
- Main algorithm: STAR-T uses partitioning, thresholding, and buckets with a novel bucket-filling strategy.  
- Memory/performance guarantee: maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 constant-factor approximation under m removals.  
- Empirical evaluation: competitive results on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus streaming methods that know removals in advance.  
- Significance: obtaining a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming+robust setting is a noteworthy theoretical advance.  
- Missing theory: paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds, leaving the tightness of the 0.149 approximation unclear.  
- Presentation issues: the claimed "innovative analysis" is insufficiently elaborated and the proof sketch is dense and hard to follow.  
- Requested improvement: provide a clearer, more detailed explanation highlighting the novel analysis steps.  
- Influence-maximization limitation: experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that may not reflect full network-wide influence dynamics.  
- Practical concern: unclear how standard influence-maximization objectives could be adapted to a streaming setting tractably; paper should discuss these limitations and potential solutions.
