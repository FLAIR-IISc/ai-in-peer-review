- Addresses maximizing a submodular function in a streaming setting with robustness to up to m element removals, combining two previously separate problems.
- Claims the first constant-factor approximation algorithm for the combined streaming + robust submodular maximization problem.
- Proposes STAR-T, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and guarantees a 0.149-approximation after m removals.
- STAR-T uses partitioning, thresholding, and a novel bucket-filling strategy to manage candidate elements.
- Empirically evaluated on influence maximization (using a simplified neighborhood-based objective) and movie recommendation, showing competitive performance even versus streaming baselines that know removed elements a priori.
- Technical analysis appears sound but is presented unclearly; the paper claims an “innovative analysis” without sufficiently detailing novel analytical steps.
- Lacks lower bounds or theoretical context to compare the presented upper bound against fundamental limits.
- The influence maximization experiments use a simplified objective and do not demonstrate scalability or applicability to standard network-wide influence objectives in streaming contexts.
- Prior work used bucket-based approaches, so the main algorithmic novelty centers on the specific bucket-filling technique rather than the high-level method class.
