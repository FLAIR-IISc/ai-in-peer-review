

This paper introduces a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraint $k$. The first stage employs a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage applies the simple greedy method. Theoretical analysis establishes a constant approximation guarantee for STAR-T when $k \to \infty$ (Theorem 4.1), while Lemmas 4.2–4.3 demonstrate that STAR-T combined with the greedy method effectively handles element loss.  

Experiments indicate strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is significant as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potential broad applications and high insightfulness. STAR-T features two hyperparameters, $\tau$ and $w$; Theorem 4.5 provides a method to approximate $\tau$, and the experiments show $w=1$ suffices, simplifying practical deployment.  

The primary concern lies in the experimental evaluation's limited expressiveness: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-St. Additionally, the influence maximization objective function $f(Z)=|N(Z) \cup Z|$ (line 219) differs from the standard model in Kempe et al. (SIGKDD 2003), raising uncertainty about STAR-T's performance under the conventional spread model.
