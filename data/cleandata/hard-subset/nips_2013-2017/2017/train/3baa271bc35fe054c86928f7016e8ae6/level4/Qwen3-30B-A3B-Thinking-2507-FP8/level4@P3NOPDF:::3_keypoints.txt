- Introduces STAR-T, the first algorithm for robust streaming submodular maximization, selecting k items robust to up to m removals after the stream.  
- STAR-T stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements during streaming and achieves a constant-factor approximation of 0.149.  
- Algorithmically novel: adapts bucket-based partitioning and thresholding with a distinct bucket-filling strategy for the streaming+robustness setting.  
- Experiments show strong empirical performance on influence maximization (with a simplified objective) and movie recommendation, even against informed adversaries.  
- Well-motivated problem combining streaming and robust submodular optimization, with clear practical relevance.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant—first constant approximation in this joint setting—representing notable progress.  
- Major gap: no non-trivial lower bounds, leaving unclear whether the 0.149 approximation is improvable or near-optimal.  
- Analysis lacks clarity and exposition: the paper does not clearly explain what is innovative in the proofs or distill key technical insights.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that deviates from standard diffusion-based spread, weakening applicability claims.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent algorithm naming ("STAR-T"), unclear integration of streaming/robustness in the movie recommendation setup, and occasional passive/vague writing.
