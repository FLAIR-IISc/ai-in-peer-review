- Paper studies robust streaming submodular maximization: find T so any m deletions leave a k-subset maximizing a monotone submodular f.  
- Authors give a constant-factor approximation with |T| = O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- The algorithmic techniques and analysis are novel, technically sophisticated, and appear correct.  
- The application motivation is weak and uses an imprecise example (movie recommendations) that doesn’t justify worst-case deletions.  
- The robustness model (survive any m deletions) is a highly constrained worst-case formulation with limited practical justification.  
- The work does not generalize to more realistic settings: adversarial deletions after seeing T, probabilistic failures, dynamic/incremental deletions, or non-uniform deletion costs.  
- Existing robust submodular literature covers broader and more relevant frameworks (budgeted/adversarial/noisy settings), making this contribution narrow.  
- The paper lacks connection to common ML failure modes (adaptive user feedback, aggregate utility measures).  
- To improve relevance, authors should provide a concrete ML application requiring worst-case robustness (e.g., critical infrastructure), compare to established robustness frameworks, or show empirical gains on real data.  
- Overall: technically solid and non-trivial, but insufficiently motivated or general for a top-tier ML venue like NeurIPS.
