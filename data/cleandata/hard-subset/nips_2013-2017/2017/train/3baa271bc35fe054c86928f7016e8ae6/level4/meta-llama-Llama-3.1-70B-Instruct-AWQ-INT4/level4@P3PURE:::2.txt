This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint k. The procedure consists of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule, followed by the simple greedy method. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Additionally, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that the combination of STAR-T and the simple greedy method can effectively handle the loss of elements.

The experimental results show that the proposed algorithm performs well on the problems of influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. This work is interesting, as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have a wide range of applications, and this work provides valuable insights. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w. The authors provide an approximation method for τ in Theorem 4.5 and demonstrate that setting w=1 yields good results in the experiments, making it convenient to use STAR-T without tuning different hyperparameters.

However, I have two major concerns. Firstly, the experimental results are not very comprehensive, as the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Secondly, the objective function used for influence maximization, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function used in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T would perform on a spread model, and further clarification is needed.
