- Problem: Finds T in a stream so that after removing any m elements, the remainder still contains a size-k subset S that maximizes a submodular function f (deletion robustness).
- Result: Gives a constant-factor approximation algorithm maintaining |T| = (m log k + k) log^2 k; technically sophisticated with sound analysis.
- Relevance concern: Motivation for this specific robustness model is weak and not clearly compelling for NIPS.
- Example critique: The provided movie-recommendation example is superficial and fails to show a concrete high-impact use case for this constraint.
- Narrow formulation: Focuses only on deletion resilience for fixed m and k, ignoring broader streaming issues (adversarial arrivals, dynamic updates, budgets, privacy).
- Impact assessment: Paper does not justify why this variant is more significant than existing formulations, making the contribution feel incremental rather than transformative.
