- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting, robust to removal of up to m elements.  
- Contribution: Proposes STAR-T, a streaming algorithm producing a summary of size O((m log k + k) log² k) with a constant-factor (0.149) approximation after m removals.  
- Novelty: Introduces partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds, combining streaming and robustness techniques.  
- Theoretical result: Careful analysis showing that greedy on the remaining summary yields the constant-factor guarantee.  
- Empirical evaluation: Competitive results on dominating set (Twitter, Amazon) and personalized movie recommendation (MovieLens 1M).  
- Practical advantage: Single robust summary handles multiple different removals without re-optimizing.  
- Comparison baseline: Performs well even against SIEVE-STREAMING that knows removals in advance.  
- Strength — motivation: Problem is well-motivated by real-world robustness needs (network failures, user preferences).  
- Strength — theory: Strong theoretical contribution with clear approximation guarantee under reasonable memory.  
- Strength — writing: Generally clear presentation and good connection to prior work.  
- Weakness — analysis clarity: The claimed “innovative analysis” is not clearly distinguished from prior robust submodular work.  
- Weakness — proof sketch: Section 4’s proof sketch is opaque; key insights about partitioning and thresholds need clearer exposition.  
- Weakness — experiments: Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood objective rather than realistic diffusion models (e.g., independent cascade).  
- Weakness — lower bounds: Paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize the upper bound and approximation optimality.  
- Overall: Solid theoretical and empirical advance that merits consideration, but would benefit from clearer exposition of analytical innovations and more realistic influence experiments.
