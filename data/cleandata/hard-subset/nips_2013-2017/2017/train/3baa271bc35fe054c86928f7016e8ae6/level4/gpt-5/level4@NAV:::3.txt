This paper investigates the problem of submodular maximization in a setting that is both streaming and robust. Here, the objective is to select k high-value elements while retaining only a small number of items during the stream, with up to m items potentially removed after the stream concludes. Streaming submodular maximization has been extensively explored due to large-scale scenarios where only a few passes over the data are practical. Robustness has also recently drawn attention, motivated by cases where some elements may be deleted and one seeks solutions resilient to such deletions. This work is, to my knowledge, the first to jointly address streaming and robust submodular maximization.

The authors introduce the STAR-T algorithm, which keeps O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation while remaining robust to the removal of m elements. The approach combines partitioning and thresholding techniques for submodular optimization using bucket structures. While bucket-based ideas have appeared in prior work on robust submodular optimization, the bucket-filling strategy here differs. Experiments show that the algorithm performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, even when compared against streaming algorithms that are informed in advance about which elements will be removed.

Given the simultaneous streaming and robustness requirements, the problem is challenging, and obtaining a constant-factor guarantee is a significant achievement. However, several aspects could be improved. Providing nontrivial lower bounds would complement the upper bound. The presentation of the analysis could be clearer: the paper claims an “innovative analysis” without articulating what is innovative, and the proof sketch does not make the main ideas and steps easy to follow. Finally, the influence maximization application is less convincing. The experiments use a very simplified objective that counts only the size of the seed set’s neighborhood, and it remains unclear how standard influence maximization objectives, which depend on the entire network for any seed set, would fit within a streaming framework.
