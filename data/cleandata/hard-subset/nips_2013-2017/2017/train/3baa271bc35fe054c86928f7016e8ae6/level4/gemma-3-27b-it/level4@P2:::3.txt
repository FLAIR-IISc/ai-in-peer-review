This paper addresses the challenging problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. This combines two important considerations: efficiently processing large datasets via streaming algorithms and ensuring the solution remains effective even after the removal of a subset of elements. While submodular maximization has been widely studied, and both streaming and robustness have received individual attention, this work presents the first algorithm designed to handle both simultaneously.

The authors propose STAR-T, an algorithm that achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation guarantee while maintaining a set of size O((m log k + k) log<sup>2</sup> k). STAR-T cleverly utilizes a partitioning and thresholding scheme, building upon existing techniques for robust submodular optimization but employing a novel bucket-filling strategy. The empirical evaluation demonstrates strong performance in both a dominating set (influence maximization) and personalized movie recommendation task, often matching or exceeding the performance of algorithms with the advantage of knowing which elements will be removed in advance.

The significance of this work lies in its ability to provide provable guarantees for a problem relevant to numerous large-scale machine learning applications. Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is a valuable contribution. However, several aspects could benefit from further development. The absence of lower bounds leaves the question of optimality open. While the authors highlight an “innovative analysis,” the proof sketch lacks sufficient detail to clearly convey the core analytical insights. Expanding on the novelty of the analysis would significantly enhance the paper's impact. 

The experimental setup for the influence maximization task is somewhat simplistic. Using only the size of the neighborhood as the objective function does not fully capture the complexities of traditional influence maximization models, where the value depends on the network structure and propagation of influence. Demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness with a more representative objective function would strengthen the results.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** This is the first work to address the combined problem of streaming and robust submodular maximization.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The algorithm achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee, a significant result for this challenging problem.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Experiments demonstrate competitive performance against strong baselines in relevant application areas.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The problem setting and proposed solution are highly relevant to large-scale machine learning tasks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Lack of Lower Bounds:** The absence of lower bounds leaves the optimality of the solution unclear.
*   **Analytical Clarity:** The analysis is difficult to follow due to a lack of detailed explanation and insufficient highlighting of its novel aspects.
*   **Simplified Influence Maximization Setup:** The experimental evaluation in the influence maximization setting could be strengthened by using a more realistic objective function.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of submodular optimization. While some aspects of the presentation and analysis could be improved, the novelty of the problem setting, the theoretical guarantees, and the promising empirical results make this a strong submission. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the clarity of the analysis and potentially expanding the evaluation of the influence maximization application.
