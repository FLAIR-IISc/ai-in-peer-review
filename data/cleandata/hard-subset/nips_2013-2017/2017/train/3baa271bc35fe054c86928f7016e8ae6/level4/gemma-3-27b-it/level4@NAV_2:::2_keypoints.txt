- Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming algorithm + greedy second stage) for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraint k.  
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding in the streaming stage.  
- Authors prove STAR-T attains a constant-factor approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1) with supporting Lemmas 4.2–4.4 for robustness under element loss.  
- Provides a parallel variant to estimate unknown parameter τ, addressing practical parameter estimation.  
- Empirically, STAR-T-GREEDY is comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING but does not consistently outperform it.  
- Practical use is simplified by the observation that STAR-T performs well with w = 1.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks breadth and fails to clearly identify scenarios where STAR-T outperforms baselines.  
- Discrepancy in the dominating set experiment: the paper uses f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, differing from Kempe et al.’s expected influence spread objective.  
- Unclear how STAR-T performs under the standard influence spread model, weakening claims about relevance to influence maximization.  
- The constant approximation factor is relatively small and diminishes with growing k, limiting practical guarantees.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, representing a meaningful conceptual contribution.  
- Theoretical contributions: clear approximation bounds and proofs for robustness properties.  
- Practical considerations (τ estimation, w choice) are addressed, enhancing usability.  
- Overall accept arguments: novel approach, theoretical guarantees, motivated application; reject arguments: inconsistent empirical superiority, objective-function discrepancy, and weakening approximation factor.
