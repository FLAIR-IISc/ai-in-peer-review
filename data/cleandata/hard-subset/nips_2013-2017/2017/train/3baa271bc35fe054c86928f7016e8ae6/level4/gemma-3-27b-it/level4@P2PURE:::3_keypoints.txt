- Paper tackles the novel joint problem of streaming submodular maximization with robustness to up to m element removals.  
- First work to consider streaming and robustness together.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm using partitioning, thresholding, and buckets with a novel bucket-filling strategy.  
- STAR-T stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor 0.149 approximation despite up to m removals.  
- Empirical results on influence maximization and movie recommendation show STAR-T outperforms streaming baselines that know removals in advance.  
- Theoretical result (constant-factor approximation) is strong and notable.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds to strengthen theoretical grounding.  
- Technical analysis is sound but presented unclearly; the “innovative analysis” claims are not well highlighted or easy to follow.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective; applicability to standard global influence objectives in streaming is unclear.
