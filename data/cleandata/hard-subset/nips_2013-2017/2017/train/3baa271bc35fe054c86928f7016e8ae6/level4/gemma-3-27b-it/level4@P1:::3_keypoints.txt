- Studies streaming robust submodular maximization: find k elements from a stream that remain effective after removal of m elements.  
- First work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that maintains a set of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) after streaming.  
- STAR-T achieves a constant 0.149 approximation guarantee while robust to removal of m elements.  
- Algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket storage and introduces a novel bucket-filling method.  
- Experimental evaluation on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Empirical results outperform or match existing streaming algorithms even when baselines know which elements will be removed.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: constant-factor approximation in a challenging combined setting.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds to strengthen theoretical results.  
- Analysis/presentation is unclear; core ideas and key proof steps are not clearly articulated in the proof sketch.  
- Influence maximization experiment uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about relevance to standard probabilistic contagion models.  
- Unclear how traditional influence models depending on the entire network would adapt to the proposed streaming setting.
