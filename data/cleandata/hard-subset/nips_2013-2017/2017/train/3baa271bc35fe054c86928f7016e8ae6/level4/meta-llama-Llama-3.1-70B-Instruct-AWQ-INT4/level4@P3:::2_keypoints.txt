- Proposes a two-stage procedure (streaming STAR-T and greedy STAR-T-GREEDY) for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint.  
- Claims to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with broad applications.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with clear algorithm descriptions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for the algorithms.  
- STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation even as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- STAR-T-GREEDY has provable performance bounds when elements are removed from the summary (Lemmas 4.2–4.4).  
- Experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show the algorithm performs effectively.  
- Algorithm often competes well with Sieve-Streaming, despite Sieve knowing removals in advance.  
- Limitation: STAR-T-GREEDY does not always outperform Sieve-Streaming.  
- Limitation: Influence-maximization objective differs from the standard spread model (Kempe et al.), so performance on the true spread model is unclear.  
- Experimental evaluation is not comprehensive and comparisons with other methods could be more detailed.  
- Overall, the work is a significant, novel contribution with strong theoretical support and practical relevance for large-scale ML.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, wide applicability, rigorous theory, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental breadth, objective mismatch for influence experiments, and need for further evaluation.
