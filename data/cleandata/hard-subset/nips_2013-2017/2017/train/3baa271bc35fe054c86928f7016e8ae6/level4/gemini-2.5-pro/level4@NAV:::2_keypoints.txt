- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k.
- Stage 1 introduces STAR-T, a novel robust streaming algorithm using a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.
- Stage 2 applies a simple greedy method after STAR-T.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: STAR-T attains a constant approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Proves the combined approach handles element loss (Lemmas 4.2–4.3).
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation shows effectiveness.
- Novelty claim: appears to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, relevant for many applications.
- Practicality: STAR-T has hyperparameters τ and w; offers a τ approximation method (Theorem 4.5) and shows w=1 works well empirically, reducing tuning.
- Experimental concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline across all experiments.
- Experimental concern: Influence maximization experiment uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which deviates from the standard influence spread model (Kempe et al., 2003), complicating assessment.
