- Motivation: resilience to element removal (example: adjusting movie recommendations when a user loses interest).
- Contribution: a constant-factor approximation algorithm that returns T of size (m·log k + k)·log² k.
- Validity: results appear correct and the solution is non-trivial.
- Technical merit: the algorithm and its analysis are technically challenging.
- Concern: relevance to NIPS is questionable.
- Criticism: applications provided are weak and the problem’s generality seems limited.
- Conclusion: the motivation does not strongly justify the method’s complexity in a broader context.
