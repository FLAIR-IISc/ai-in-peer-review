This paper introduces a two-stage approach to robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint *k*. The first stage employs a novel robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, built upon a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding strategy. A simple greedy method is then applied in the second stage. The authors demonstrate a constant-factor approximation guarantee for STAR-T when *k* approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1), and Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 establish the algorithm’s resilience to element loss when combined with the greedy approach.

Empirical evaluation showcases the algorithm’s effectiveness on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is a significant contribution, representing the first streaming algorithm designed for robust submodular maximization. The potential for broad applicability of a streaming setting makes this research particularly insightful. The STAR-T algorithm utilizes two hyperparameters, τ and *w*; the authors provide guidance for approximating τ (Theorem 4.5) and demonstrate through experimentation that setting *w*=1 yields strong performance, simplifying the algorithm’s practical implementation.

However, a key concern is the limited expressiveness of the experimental results. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. 

Furthermore, the objective function used for Influence Maximization, *f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|* (line 219), deviates from the standard formulation presented in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). Consequently, the performance of STAR-T within this specific spread model is unclear.
