- Introduces the first joint study of streaming and robust submodular maximization.  
- Problem: single-pass selection of k elements while retaining a small set robust to up to m post-stream removals.  
- Proposes STAR-T, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and guarantees a constant-factor (0.149) approximation.  
- STAR-T combines partitioning and thresholding with a novel bucket-filling strategy building on prior bucket-based robust methods.  
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus streaming baselines that know the removals.  
- Theoretical significance: achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming+robust setting is a notable advance.  
- Major weakness: lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement and contextualize the upper bound.  
- Major weakness: analysis is unclear and does not clearly highlight the novel components of the proof, making the argument hard to follow.  
- Major weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that does not reflect standard diffusion models, raising doubts about practical relevance and streaming compatibility.
