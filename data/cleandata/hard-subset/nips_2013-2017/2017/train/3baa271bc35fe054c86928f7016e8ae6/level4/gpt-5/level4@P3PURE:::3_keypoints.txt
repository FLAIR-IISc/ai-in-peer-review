- Problem: streaming, robust submodular maximization—select k high-value elements while retaining few during the stream and tolerating m removals.
- Novelty: claimed to be the first work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.
- Algorithmic result: STAR-T maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149-approx robust to removal of m items.
- Techniques: combines partitioning and thresholding with a bucket-based design; buckets are populated differently than prior robust methods.
- Empirical performance: STAR-T performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, outperforming streaming baselines even when they know removed elements in advance.
- Significance: obtaining a constant-factor approximation under combined streaming and robustness constraints is a notable theoretical contribution.
- Suggestion: add non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper bound.
- Suggestion: analysis presentation needs clarity—explicitly state what is innovative and outline main proof ideas and key steps.
- Critique of experiments: the influence maximization application uses a very simplified neighborhood-size objective, making the application less convincing.
- Concern: unclear how standard influence maximization objectives (which depend on the entire network for any seed set) would be handled in the streaming setting.
