This paper addresses the challenging problem of robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting. The authors consider a scenario where elements arrive sequentially and a subset of size *m* may be removed after a summary is constructed, requiring a solution that remains effective despite these removals. This combines the practical need for streaming algorithms with the robustness concerns arising in many real-world applications. The paper introduces STAR-T, an algorithm leveraging a novel partitioning structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule, achieving a constant-factor approximation guarantee with a memory footprint of O((m log k + k)log<sup>2</sup> k).

The core contribution lies in the algorithm’s design and analysis, providing the first constant-factor approximation for this combined streaming and robustness objective. The partitioning and thresholding scheme builds upon ideas from previous work in robust submodular maximization (e.g., [10, 11]) but introduces a distinct approach to bucket filling and a dedicated analytical technique to handle arbitrary removals. The experimental evaluation on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks demonstrates competitive performance against existing streaming algorithms, even those with prior knowledge of the removed elements.

The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors acknowledge the limitations of the analysis and mention the potential for improvement. While the paper doesn’t present lower bounds, the achieved constant-factor approximation is a strong result given the problem’s inherent difficulty. The writing is generally clear, although the description of the “innovative analysis” could be more explicit; providing a higher-level overview of the key analytical steps would improve readability. The choice of a simplified objective function in the influence maximization experiment is a reasonable pragmatic decision, but acknowledging its limitations and potential impact on generalizability would strengthen the discussion.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of streaming and robustness constraints is a novel and relevant problem setting.
*   **Theoretical Guarantee:** The constant-factor approximation guarantee is a significant theoretical contribution.
*   **Practical Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance against established baselines.
*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The problem statement and motivation are clearly articulated.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Analysis Clarity:** The description of the analytical innovation could be more detailed and accessible.
*   **Missing Lower Bounds:** Absence of lower bounds limits the understanding of the achieved approximation ratio’s optimality.
*   **Simplified Influence Maximization:** The use of a simplified influence maximization objective limits the generalizability of the results.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a novel and important problem with a strong theoretical result.
*   The experimental results validate the practical performance of the proposed algorithm.
*   The contribution is significant and likely to be of interest to the NIPS community.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The analysis could benefit from greater clarity and a more explicit explanation of the key innovations.
*   The lack of lower bounds leaves open the question of whether the approximation ratio is optimal.
