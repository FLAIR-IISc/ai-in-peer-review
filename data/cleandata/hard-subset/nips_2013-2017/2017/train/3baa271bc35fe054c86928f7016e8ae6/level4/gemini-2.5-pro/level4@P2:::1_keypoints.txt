- Problem: maximize a monotone submodular function under cardinality k in a streaming setting robust to removal of up to m elements.  
- Approach: two-stage method — STAR-T (single-pass streaming summary S with partitioned buckets and exponentially decreasing thresholds) then STAR-T-GREEDY on S \ E to produce final k-set.  
- Novelty: combines streaming submodular maximization with robustness, adapting partitioned-structure ideas to the streaming context.  
- Technical contribution: non-trivial algorithmic design (partitioned buckets, decreasing thresholds) and sophisticated analysis.  
- Theoretical guarantee: constant-factor approximation (Theorem 4.1) for the robust streaming problem.  
- Memory complexity: summary size O((k + m log k) log k), showing logarithmic dependence on k and favorable comparison to concurrent work with linear k-dependence.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides helpful intuition and proof sketches.  
- Empirical weakness: experiments are not decisively compelling; results are comparable to a strong baseline (SIEVE-STREAMING) that is given the removed set E in advance.  
- Experimental setup concern: removals E are chosen as subsets of the summary S (worst-case) rather than more realistic removals from the whole ground set V, limiting applicability of empirical claims.  
- Suggested improvement: include experiments or discussion on removals from V \ S to better match motivating scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and novel problem formulation; experimental validation is modest but acceptable.  
- Recommendation: accept — theory and novelty outweigh experimental limitations.
