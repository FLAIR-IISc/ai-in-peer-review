- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm for robust monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k with up to m removals.
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.
- The problem motivation and related work are well-presented and the contribution is clearly defined.
- Provides a sound theoretical analysis and a proof of a constant-factor approximation guarantee.
- Experimental results on two data summarization tasks demonstrate the algorithm’s effectiveness.
- Experiments are promising but not comprehensive and lack sufficient baseline comparisons.
- Influence maximization experiment uses a different objective than the referenced work, which may affect result validity.
- Some paragraphs are long and the paper could improve readability by breaking them up.
- Certain notation and terminology need clearer explanation for non-expert readers.
- The algorithm is novel and combines existing techniques in a new way to achieve robustness to m removals.
- The method has potential for wide applicability in submodular optimization tasks.
- Overall quality is high, the paper is well-written, and the reviewer rates it 8/10.
