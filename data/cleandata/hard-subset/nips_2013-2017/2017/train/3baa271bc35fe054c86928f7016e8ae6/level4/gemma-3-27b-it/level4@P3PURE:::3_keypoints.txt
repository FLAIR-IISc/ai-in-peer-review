- Paper addresses the novel joint problem of streaming and robust submodular maximization (first work to do both).
- Proposes STAR-T, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and guarantees a constant 0.149 approximation robust to removal of m elements.
- Algorithmic approach combines partitioning and thresholding with a novel bucket-filling strategy building on prior bucket-based robust methods.
- Experiments show strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, sometimes outperforming streaming baselines that know removed elements.
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in the combined streaming+robust setting is a noteworthy theoretical result.
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize the provided upper bound.
- The analysis is not clearly presented; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not well articulated and the proof sketch is hard to follow.
- Influence-maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than standard network-dependent objectives, leaving applicability to real influence models and streaming adaptations unclear.
