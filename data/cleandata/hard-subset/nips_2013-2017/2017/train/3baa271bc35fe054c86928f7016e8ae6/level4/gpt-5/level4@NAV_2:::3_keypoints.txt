- Problem/setup: first work to jointly address streaming and deletion-robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint (pick k from a one-pass compact summary tolerant to up to m deletions).  
- Goal: construct a compact summary in one pass that still yields k high-value elements after any m deletions.  
- Method: introduces STAR-T, which combines partitioning with a thresholded bucketing scheme (fills buckets via a novel threshold-based rule versus prior bucketed constructions).  
- Theoretical guarantee: stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor approximation (0.149) after removal of any m elements.  
- Empirical results: competitive performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, remaining competitive with streaming baselines even when those baselines know the deleted set.  
- Contribution significance: obtaining a constant-factor guarantee under the combined streaming and robustness constraints is a notable result.  
- Missing elements: the paper would benefit from nontrivial lower bounds to complement the approximation guarantees.  
- Clarity issue: the exposition and analysis are unclear—claims of “innovative analysis” are not well articulated and the proof sketch is hard to distill.  
- Evaluation concern: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-based objective, leaving unclear how standard diffusion-based influence functions fit into this streaming framework.
