This paper presents a novel two-stage approach to robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage involves the design of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage employs a simple greedy method. The authors establish that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2-4.3 demonstrate the algorithm's robustness to element loss when combined with the greedy method.

The experimental results indicate the proposed algorithm's effectiveness in tackling influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation problems.

This work is notable for introducing the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which may have far-reaching applications. The STAR-T algorithm features two hyperparameters, τ and w, with Theorem 4.5 providing a method for approximating τ. The experiments show that setting w=1 yields satisfactory performance, making the algorithm more convenient to use without requiring hyperparameter tuning.

However, a major concern is the limited expressiveness of the experiments, as the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm.

Additionally, the objective function used for Influence Maximization, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function presented in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is unclear how the STAR-T algorithm would perform under the traditional spread model.
