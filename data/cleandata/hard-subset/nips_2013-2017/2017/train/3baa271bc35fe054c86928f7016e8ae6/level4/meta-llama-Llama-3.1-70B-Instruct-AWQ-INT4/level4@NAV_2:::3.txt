This paper studies the problem of submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting. In this setting, the goal is to find k elements of high value among a small number of elements retained during the stream, where m items might be removed after the stream. Streaming submodular maximization has been extensively studied due to the large scale applications where only a small number of passes over the data is reasonable. Robustness has also attracted some attention recently and is motivated by scenarios where some elements might get removed and the goal is to obtain solutions that are robust to such removals. This paper is the first to consider jointly the problem of streaming and robust submodular maximization.

The authors develop the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k )log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant (0.149) approximation guarantee while being robust to the removal of m elements. This algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding techniques for submodular optimization which uses buckets. Similar ideas using buckets were used in previous work for robust submodular optimization, but here the buckets are filled in a different fashion. It is then shown experimentally that this algorithm performs well in influence maximization and movie recommendation applications, even against streaming algorithms which know beforehand which elements are removed.

The main ideas of the paper relate to previous work in submodular optimization, particularly in the context of robust submodular maximization and submodular streaming algorithms. The paper builds upon the work of [10] and [11] which introduced robust submodular maximization and proposed algorithms for the centralized setting. The authors also draw inspiration from submodular streaming algorithms such as SIEVE-STREAMING [8] and extend these ideas to the robust setting. The paper's contribution is the development of a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule, which allows the algorithm to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee in the streaming and robust setting.

The strengths of the paper include its novel algorithmic contribution and its experimental evaluation. The STAR-T algorithm is well-motivated and its performance is demonstrated in various applications. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance and provide a constant-factor approximation guarantee. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

However, there are several aspects of the paper that could be improved. It would have been nice if there were some non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound. The writing of the analysis could also be improved, the authors mention an "innovative analysis" but do not expand on what is innovative about it and it is difficult to extract the main ideas and steps of the analysis from the proof sketch. Finally, the influence maximization application could also be more convincing. For the experiments, the authors use a very simplified objective for influence maximization which only consists of the size of the neighborhood of the seed nodes. It is not clear how traditional influence maximization objectives where the value of the function depends on the entire network for any set of seed nodes could fit in a streaming setting.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the algorithm is technically sound and the analysis is thorough. However, the writing could be improved to make the main ideas and steps of the analysis more clear. The experimental evaluation is convincing, but more realistic influence maximization objectives could strengthen the results. Overall, the paper is a strong contribution to the field of submodular optimization and streaming algorithms.

Arguments pro acceptance:
- The paper studies a novel problem setting that combines streaming and robust submodular maximization.
- The authors develop a novel algorithm that achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee in this setting.
- The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance and demonstrates its effectiveness in various applications.

Arguments con acceptance:
- The paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.
- The writing of the analysis could be improved to make the main ideas and steps more clear.
- The influence maximization application could be more convincing, using more realistic objectives.
