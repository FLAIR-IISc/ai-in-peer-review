- Proposes a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint: streaming algorithm STAR-T followed by simple greedy selection.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k → ∞.
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3: STAR-T combined with greedy can effectively handle loss of elements.
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with practical streaming applications.
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a method to approximate τ and experiments show w = 1 works well.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive.
- STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.
- Influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) deviates from the standard spread model in Kempe et al., raising doubts.
- It is unclear how STAR-T performs on the standard influence spread model, a significant limitation of the evaluation.
