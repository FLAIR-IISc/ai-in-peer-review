This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with cardinality constraint k. The procedure consists of a streaming algorithm, STAR-T, and a simple greedy method, STAR-T-GREEDY. The authors claim that their algorithm is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization and that it has a wide range of applications.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of their algorithm and its theoretical guarantees. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a robust streaming algorithm for submodular maximization. The authors show that their algorithm, STAR-T, achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee when k is up to infinity (Theorem 4.1). They also provide theoretical guarantees for the performance of STAR-T-GREEDY when elements are removed from the summary (Lemmas 4.2-4.4).

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on two different applications: influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. The results show that the proposed algorithm can perform well on these applications, even when compared to the Sieve-Streaming algorithm, which knows in advance which elements will be removed.

However, there are some limitations to the experimental results. For example, the STAR-T-GREEDY algorithm does not always outperform the Sieve-Streaming algorithm. Additionally, the objective function used for influence maximization is not the same as the one used in the referred article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is not clear how STAR-T performs on the spread model.
