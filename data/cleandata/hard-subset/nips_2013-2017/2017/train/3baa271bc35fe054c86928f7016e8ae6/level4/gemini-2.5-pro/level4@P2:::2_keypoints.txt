- Problem: streaming monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k, robust to removal of up to m elements.  
- Method: two-stage approach — STAR-T single-pass streaming builds summary S using partitioned buckets and exponentially decreasing thresholds; STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on S \ E after removals.  
- Theoretical contribution: first streaming algorithm with a provable constant-factor approximation for robust submodular maximization (Theorem 4.1).  
- Relation to prior work: builds on thresholding streaming ideas (SIEVE-STREAMING) and partitioned robust maximization techniques.  
- Experiments: evaluated on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks; practical fixes include parallel runs to handle unknown τ and evidence that memory parameter w can be 1.  
- Strength — originality/significance: addresses a timely, practical problem combining streaming constraints with robustness to deletions.  
- Strength — technical quality: elegant partitioned thresholding design with sound theoretical analysis.  
- Strength — practical considerations: provides a practical scheme for unknown thresholds and simple hyperparameter behavior in experiments.  
- Weakness — empirical strength: performance is sometimes only comparable to or slightly worse than SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows E), so experiments could be more compelling.  
- Weakness — influence maximization clarity: the dominating set objective f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| is not the standard diffusion model; authors should justify this choice and discuss expected performance on IC/LT models.  
- Weakness — adversarial model in experiments: removals appear restricted to the summary S; experiments should also consider adversaries removing any m elements from the full ground set V.  
- Overall: solid, novel, and theoretically rigorous contribution; main improvements needed in experimental evaluation and clarity on the influence objective.
