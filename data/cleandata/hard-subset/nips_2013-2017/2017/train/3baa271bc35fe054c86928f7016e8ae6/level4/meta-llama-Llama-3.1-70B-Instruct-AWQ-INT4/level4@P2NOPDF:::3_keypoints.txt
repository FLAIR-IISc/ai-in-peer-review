- Paper tackles streaming + robust submodular maximization: select k items from a small retained subset with up to m removals after the stream.  
- Claims to be the first work to jointly consider streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation robust to m removals.  
- STAR-T combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket-based structure, similar to prior robust methods but with a different bucket filling strategy.  
- Experiments show STAR-T performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus streaming baselines that know removed items.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this joint setting is a significant and challenging result.  
- Paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement its upper-bound guarantees.  
- The analysis is poorly explained: the claimed “innovative analysis” is not clearly elaborated in the proof sketch.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising concerns about realism versus traditional diffusion objectives.  
- It is unclear how standard influence-maximization objectives, which depend on the whole network, adapt to the streaming setting used.  
- Experimental evaluation would benefit from more comparisons to other robust streaming algorithms and deeper insight into STAR-T’s behavior.
