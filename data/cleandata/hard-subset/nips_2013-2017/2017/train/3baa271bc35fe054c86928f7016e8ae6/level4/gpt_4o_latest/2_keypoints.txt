- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy second stage.  
- STAR-T is built on a partitioning framework with a decreasing thresholding mechanism.  
- The final selection is obtained by applying the simple greedy algorithm to STAR-T's output.  
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T achieves a constant approximation as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show STAR-T combined with greedy is robust to the loss of elements.  
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Novelty: this is the first streaming algorithm proposed for robust submodular maximization and the streaming setting has wide application potential.  
- STAR-T uses two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments indicate w = 1 works well, minimizing tuning.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is not sufficiently expressive; STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Stream.  
- Major concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard diffusion/spread model of Kempe et al., so performance under the standard model is unclear.
