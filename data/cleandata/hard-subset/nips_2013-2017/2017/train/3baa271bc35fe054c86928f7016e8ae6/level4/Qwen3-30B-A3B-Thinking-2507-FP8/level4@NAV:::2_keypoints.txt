- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by greedy selection.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule in the streaming stage.  
- Theoretical results: constant approximation guarantee for STAR-T as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- Additional theory (Lemmas 4.2–4.3) shows STAR-T combined with greedy handles element loss robustly.  
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Claimed significance: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with broad applications and high insightfulness.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments suggest w=1 suffices.  
- Primary experimental concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.  
- Modeling concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard Kempe et al. spread model, leaving uncertainty about performance under the conventional model.
