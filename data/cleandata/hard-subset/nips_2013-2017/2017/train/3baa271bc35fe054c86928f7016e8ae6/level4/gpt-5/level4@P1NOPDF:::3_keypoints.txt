- Paper addresses streaming submodular maximization with robustness to adversarial removal of up to m items, claimed as the first joint treatment of streaming + robustness.

- Proposes STAR-T algorithm using partitioning, thresholding, and bucketed data structures to store a small subset in one pass.

- STAR-T stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149-approximation after up to m deletions.

- Empirical results show strong performance on influence maximization (using a simplified neighborhood-size surrogate) and movie recommendation, and compete with baselines even when baselines know deletions.

- Obtaining a constant-factor approximation under both streaming and robustness constraints is a significant theoretical contribution.

- Suggests need for nontrivial lower bounds to clarify trade-offs between robustness, memory, and approximation in the streaming model.

- Requests clearer exposition of the analysis: explicitly state what is innovative, provide a high-level roadmap of key lemmas, how partitioning/thresholding yield robustness, origin of the 0.149 constant, and tightness of log factors.

- Criticizes the influence maximization experiments as unconvincing because the objective was simplified; asks discussion of handling realistic diffusion-model objectives in streaming settings.

- Recommends either a more compelling empirical evaluation for influence maximization or justification for using the simplified surrogate.

- Overall: interesting and notable result, but would be strengthened by clearer analysis exposition, stronger empirical justification for IM, and additional theoretical lower bounds.
