- Tackles joint streaming and robust submodular maximization, claiming to be the first work to address both simultaneously (novel problem).
- Problem: output k high-value elements while storing a small subset during a stream and remaining robust to post-stream removal of up to m items.
- Proposes the STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation under m removals.
- Method combines partitioning and thresholding via bucket-based structures, with buckets populated differently from prior robust-submodular work.
- Experiments show strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus streaming baselines that know removals in advance.
- Positive note: obtaining a constant-factor guarantee in this simultaneous streaming+robust setting is a significant result.
- Critique: paper should include nontrivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper bound.
- Critique: exposition and analysis are unclear—“innovative analysis” is not explained and the proof sketch obscures main ideas and steps.
- Critique: influence maximization experiments are not fully convincing, using a simplified neighborhood-size objective and leaving unclear how standard IM objectives fit the streaming model.
