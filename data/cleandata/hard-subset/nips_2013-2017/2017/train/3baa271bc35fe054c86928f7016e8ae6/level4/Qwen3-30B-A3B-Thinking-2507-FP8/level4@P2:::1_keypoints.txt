- Problem: Robust streaming submodular maximization—build a small summary S that remains useful after any m elements are removed.  
- Algorithms: Two-stage approach—STAR-T constructs a robust summary in one pass; STAR-T-GREEDY finds a good solution from remaining elements after removals.  
- Theoretical result: Constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149 with summary size O((k + m log k) log² k).  
- Technical novelty: Introduces a partitioned structure and exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to handle robustness in the streaming setting.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SIEVE-STREAMING (0.5-approx, O(k log k) memory) and prior robust submodular work, extending robustness to streaming.  
- Strength: Thorough, rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs and precise algorithm descriptions.  
- Strength: Technically sophisticated algorithm design that empirically matches or outperforms baselines even when baselines know removed elements.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is well-designed with appropriate baselines and multiple metrics across two applications.  
- Weakness: Applications/motivation underdeveloped—examples don’t strongly justify robustness as more critical than other streaming challenges.  
- Weakness: Practical significance limited and problem may be too specialized for a general ML audience (NIPS).  
- Quality: Technically sound and competitive approximation; treatment of threshold parameter τ is superficial in main text (supplement addresses it).  
- Clarity: Well-structured, clearly written, precise algorithms and helpful figures; supplement contains complete proofs.  
- Originality: Novel intersection of streaming and robust submodular maximization; partitioned thresholding approach is distinct.  
- Significance concern: Relevant for niche applications but insufficiently argued for broader impact; needs stronger connections to wider ML challenges or more diverse applications.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong theoretical novelty, sophisticated algorithm, and convincing empirical results.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited broader significance, weak motivating examples, and perceived narrow scope for a general conference.  
- Overall: Technically strong contribution whose acceptance hinges on better motivating the robustness requirement or demonstrating wider applicability.
