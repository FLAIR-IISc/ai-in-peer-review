This paper studies submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting. The goal is to select k elements of high value while retaining only a small number of elements during the stream, under the possibility that m items may be removed after the stream. Streaming submodular maximization is well motivated by large-scale applications where only a few passes over data are feasible, and robustness is increasingly important when selected elements may later be removed. To the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to jointly address streaming and robust submodular maximization.

The authors propose the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant 0.149-approximation while being robust to the removal of m elements. The algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding with a bucket-based design. While bucketed approaches have appeared in prior work on robust submodular optimization, here the buckets are populated differently. Empirically, STAR-T performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, including against streaming baselines that are given the set of removed elements in advance.

Given the combined streaming and robustness requirements, the problem setting is challenging, and obtaining a constant-factor approximation is a significant result.

That said, several aspects of the paper could be improved:
- It would be valuable to include non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.
- The presentation of the analysis could be clearer. The paper refers to an “innovative analysis” but does not articulate what is innovative about it, and the proof sketch makes it difficult to extract the main ideas and key steps.
- The influence maximization application is less convincing. The experiments use a very simplified objective—namely, the size of the neighborhood of the seed nodes. It is unclear how traditional influence maximization objectives, where the function value depends on the entire network for any set of seed nodes, would fit into a streaming setting.
