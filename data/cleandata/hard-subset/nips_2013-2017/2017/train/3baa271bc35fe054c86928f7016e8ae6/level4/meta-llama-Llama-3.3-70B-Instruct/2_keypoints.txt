- Proposes a novel two-stage solution for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality k constraint.  
- Stage 1: introduces STAR-T, a robust streaming algorithm using partitioning and a decreasing threshold rule.  
- Stage 2: applies the simple greedy algorithm after STAR-T to finalize the solution.  
- Theoretical guarantees: STAR-T attains a constant approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1), and Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show the STAR-T + greedy combination handles element loss.  
- Empirical results show strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Significance: first streaming algorithm targeted at robust submodular maximization, offering broad application potential and new insights.  
- Hyperparameters: STAR-T uses τ and w; Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ and experiments suggest w = 1 works well, reducing tuning.  
- Major concerns: experimental evaluation is limited—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming—and the influence maximization objective f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| deviates from the classic Kempe et al. (2003) spread model, casting doubt on results under the conventional model.
