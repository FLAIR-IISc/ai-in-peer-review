- Proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (streaming) followed by a greedy selection stage.  
- STAR-T is a novel robust streaming algorithm using a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee for sufficiently large k.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: the combined STAR-T + greedy procedure is robust to the loss of elements.  
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrates the algorithm’s effectiveness.  
- Main contribution: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, opening the streaming setting for this problem.  
- Practicality: STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ, and experiments indicate w=1 works well, reducing tuning needs.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is not fully convincing because STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline.  
- Methodological concern: the influence maximization experiment uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| rather than the standard influence-spread model (Kempe et al. 2003) without justification, leaving unclear how STAR-T performs on the standard formulation.
