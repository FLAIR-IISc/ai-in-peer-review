This paper studies submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting with post hoc robustness to deletions. The goal is to select k high-value elements while storing only a small subset during the stream, and to remain competitive even if up to m elements are removed afterward. Streaming submodular maximization is motivated by large-scale applications that permit few passes over the data, and robustness addresses settings where selected or stored items may later be invalidated. To my knowledge, this is the first work to address streaming and robustness jointly.

The authors propose STAR-T, which stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements over the stream and achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation that remains valid after the adversarial removal of any m elements. The algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding via bucket structures. While bucket-based ideas have appeared in prior work on robust submodular optimization, STAR-T differs in how buckets are populated and used. Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation suggest strong empirical performance, even compared to streaming baselines that are given advance knowledge of which elements will be removed.

Overall, the problem is challenging because it couples streaming constraints with robustness, and obtaining a constant-factor guarantee in this regime is a significant contribution. The paper is timely and technically interesting.

That said, several aspects could be improved:

- Lower bounds or impossibility results: The paper would be stronger with nontrivial lower bounds that quantify the inherent trade-offs among space, robustness (m), and approximation. Even discussion of known hardness or sketching barriers tailored to this robust streaming setting would help contextualize the 0.149 approximation and the storage O((m log k + k) log^2 k).

- Clarity of the analysis: The authors refer to an “innovative analysis,” but the paper does not clearly articulate what is new relative to prior thresholding/bucketing analyses. The proof sketch makes it difficult to extract the central lemmas and the intuition behind them (e.g., how the partitioning interacts with thresholds to ensure coverage after m deletions, and where the 0.149 constant comes from). Highlighting the key structural properties, outlining the main cases, and locating precisely where robustness drives the analysis would improve readability and impact.

- Influence maximization evaluation: The influence maximization objective used in the experiments is a simplified proxy (size of the immediate neighborhood of the seed set). This choice avoids the global dependency inherent in standard diffusion models (e.g., Independent Cascade, Linear Threshold), but it weakens the empirical claim. It remains unclear how the traditional influence objectives—whose values depend on reachability over the entire network for each seed set—can be accommodated in a streaming framework, both algorithmically and computationally (e.g., what access model or oracles are assumed during streaming, and the cost of function evaluation). Either a stronger justification for the proxy (with discussion of when it correlates with true spread) or experiments under more realistic objectives (perhaps via sketch-based estimators or influence oracles) would make the application more convincing.

Additional suggestions:

- Explicitly compare to prior robust submodular algorithms that use buckets, isolating what change in bucket filling or thresholds is essential for robustness under streaming constraints.

- Provide more implementation details (e.g., memory overhead per bucket, update time per element) and sensitivity analyses with respect to m, k, and the threshold schedule.

- If full lower bounds are out of scope, include a discussion section outlining plausible barriers and open problems (e.g., whether improving the constant factor necessarily increases space beyond O((m log k + k) log^2 k)).

In summary, this work tackles a novel and challenging problem and presents a practical algorithm with provable guarantees and encouraging empirical results. Clarifying the analytical innovations, strengthening the influence maximization evaluation, and adding complementary lower-bound insights would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and significance.
