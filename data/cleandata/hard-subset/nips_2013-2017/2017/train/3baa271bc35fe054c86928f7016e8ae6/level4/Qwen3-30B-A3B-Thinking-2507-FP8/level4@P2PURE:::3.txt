

This paper addresses the problem of submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting, where the objective is to identify k high-value elements from a small retained subset following stream processing, even after m elements are removed post-stream. Streaming submodular maximization has been widely studied due to scalability demands requiring only a few data passes, while robustness has recently gained attention for handling potential element removals. This work is the first to jointly consider both streaming and robustness aspects.  

The authors propose the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k) log² k) elements post-stream, achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee (0.149), and remains robust to the removal of m elements. STAR-T integrates partitioning and thresholding via bucketing, adapting bucket-filling strategies from prior robust submodular work. Experimental results demonstrate strong performance in influence maximization and movie recommendation, even against streaming algorithms that know the removed elements in advance.  

The joint consideration of streaming and robustness presents a challenging problem, and achieving a constant-factor approximation constitutes a significant theoretical contribution. However, the paper would benefit from three improvements: (1) non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound; (2) a clearer exposition of the analysis, particularly regarding the claimed "innovative analysis," which lacks specific elaboration on its novel components and obscures key proof steps; and (3) a more convincing influence maximization application. The experimental objective for influence maximization—based solely on neighborhood size of seed nodes—is oversimplified; it remains unclear how standard influence maximization objectives (which depend on the full network structure for any seed set) can be accommodated within a streaming framework.
