- Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming algorithm with partitioning and decreasing thresholds, followed by a simple greedy step) for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality k constraint.
- Theoretical result: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show the STAR-T + greedy combination can effectively handle the loss of elements.
- Experimental results show strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Claimed novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potential wide applicability.
- STAR-T uses two hyperparameters (τ and w); authors provide an approximation for τ (Theorem 4.5) and report w = 1 works well in practice.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive.
- Empirically, STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming baseline.
- Methodology issue: the influence-maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard spread model in Kempe et al., leaving performance on the true spread model unclear and requiring further investigation.
