This paper introduces a two-stage approach to robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint, *k*. The first stage implements a novel robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, leveraging a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding strategy. A subsequent greedy method completes the procedure in the second stage. The authors demonstrate a constant-factor approximation guarantee for STAR-T when *k* approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1), and Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 establish the algorithm’s resilience to element loss when combined with the greedy approach.

The proposed algorithm's efficacy is demonstrated through experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work represents a significant contribution as the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, potentially opening avenues for diverse applications and providing valuable insights into this area. The STAR-T algorithm utilizes two hyperparameters, τ and *w*; the authors provide guidance on approximating τ (Theorem 4.5) and show empirically that *w*=1 performs well, simplifying the algorithm’s practical application by minimizing hyperparameter tuning. 

However, a key concern is the limited expressiveness of the experimental results. The performance of the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently surpass that of the Sieve-St algorithm. 

Furthermore, the objective function used for Influence Maximization, *f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|* (line 219), deviates from the standard formulation presented in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). Consequently, the algorithm’s performance within this specific spread model is unclear.
