- Problem: Robust streaming submodular maximization—build compact summary S in one pass so that after any m removals, greedy on remaining elements gives a constant-factor approximation to the optimal for the reduced set.  
- Proposed method: STAR-T, a streaming algorithm using partitioned thresholding with exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Theoretical guarantees: summary size O((k + m log k) log^2 k) and guarantee f(Z) ≥ 0.149(1 − 1/log k) · f(OPT(k, V \ E)).  
- Technique novelty: partitioned buckets with thresholds τ/2^i enable robustness and are adapted to streaming for the first time.  
- Relation to prior work: extends SIEVE-STREAMING to the robust setting and differs from centralized partition approaches like [11].  
- Memory advantage: achieves O(m log k) dependence on m versus O(mk log k) in concurrent work [18], beneficial for large k.  
- Experimental results: STAR-T-GREEDY matches or exceeds SIEVE-STREAMING (even though SIEVE-STREAMING knows E) on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Practical demo: interactive reuse of S for multiple E sets is a compelling use case shown in experiments.  
- Strength — technical: rigorous analysis (Theorems 4.1, 4.5) handling adversarial m-element removals and both partition cases.  
- Strength — clarity: well-organized paper with clear algorithms and figures; proofs are logically structured.  
- Weakness — significance: paper does not convincingly justify why robustness after streaming (vs. re-streaming) is critical in real-world applications.  
- Weakness — motivating examples: dominating set example doesn’t explain why removals would happen after summary creation; movie recommendation experiment tests m = k while theory assumes m = o(k).  
- Weakness — approximation factor: 0.149 is much lower than standard greedy (≈0.632) and the trade-off for robustness is not well discussed.  
- Originality: distinct adaptation of partitioned thresholding to streaming with exponential thresholds and improved memory scaling vs prior robust methods.  
- Clarity — minor issues: “robust summary” not explicitly defined in Section 3 and the practical role of memory parameter w could be emphasized earlier.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (7/10); technically strong but needs better practical motivation and clarification of theoretical vs experimental settings.  
- Suggested revisions: better justify need for post-stream robustness, discuss the 0.149 trade-off versus robustness (especially for small m), and reconcile m = o(k) theory with m = k experiments.
