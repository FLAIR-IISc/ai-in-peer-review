- Proposes STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm with partitioned thresholding to build a robust summary of size O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Uses STAR-T-GREEDY in the query stage: simple greedy on summary after up to m removals.  
- Theoretical contribution: constant-factor approximation guarantee ≈0.149 as k → ∞, improving over prior non-streaming robust methods and streaming baselines.  
- Novelty: first work addressing robust submodular maximization specifically in the streaming setting, bridging two previously separate lines of work.  
- Technical approach: partitioned buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds; theoretical analysis is careful and sound.  
- Empirical evaluation on real datasets (Twitter, Amazon, MovieLens) for dominating set and recommendation tasks demonstrates practical relevance.  
- Experimental result: STAR-T-GREEDY performs comparably to SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows removals), despite not requiring prior removal knowledge.  
- Clarity and presentation are generally good; algorithms and proofs are precise and Figure 1 effectively shows partitioning.  
- Implementation detail: authors handle unknown τ via parallel instances and report w=1 works well in practice.  
- Weakness — experiments: evaluation is limited, lacks comprehensive comparisons across m, k, removal patterns, and statistical significance tests.  
- Weakness — influence maximization: uses f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| instead of standard expected influence spread, requiring clarification and comparison to standard metrics.  
- Weakness — memory discrepancy: practical finding w=1 contradicts theoretical requirement w ≥ ⌈4dlog kem/k⌉; needs explanation.  
- Weakness — approximation constant: 0.149 is non-trivial but could potentially be improved.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — strengthen experimental section, clarify influence objective, and reconcile theoretical vs practical memory settings.
