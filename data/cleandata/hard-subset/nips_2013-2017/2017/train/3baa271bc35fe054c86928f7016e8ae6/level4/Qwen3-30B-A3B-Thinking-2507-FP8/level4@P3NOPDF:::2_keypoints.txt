- Paper proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming algorithm + greedy second stage) for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and decreasing thresholding to handle element losses in the stream.  
- Authors prove a constant-factor approximation for STAR-T (stated as k → ∞ but actually holds for arbitrary k).  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show the combined STAR-T-Greedy framework can handle element loss.  
- Experiments report competitive performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Contribution significance: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, relevant for large-scale streaming settings.  
- Practical advantage: low hyperparameter sensitivity—Theorem 4.5 gives a way to set τ and experiments show w=1 works across cases.  
- Experimental weakness: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming (Sieve-St) — Sieve-St outperforms on 3/5 datasets in Table 2.  
- Lack of statistical significance testing and analysis for cases where STAR-T-Greedy underperforms; robustness claims are unsubstantiated.  
- Missing empirical analyses: runtime comparisons, sensitivity to loss rates, and additional baselines (e.g., Robust-Greedy) needed.  
- Objective-function discrepancy: paper uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (closed neighborhood) but claims to address Kempe et al.’s model, which uses open neighborhood; needs clarification and justification.  
- Influence-maximization results lack contextual validity unless authors clearly state and defend their chosen objective and/or re-run under Kempe’s model.  
- Theorem phrasing is misleading—change "when k is up to infinity" to "for any k" or "for arbitrary k."  
- Need clearer motivation and explanation of how element losses occur in the streaming setting and how that maps to the theoretical lemmas.  
- Recommendation: Minor revision with required actions—re-evaluate experiments with statistical tests and more baselines, explicitly define/justify influence objective and re-run Kempe-model experiments, and correct theorem phrasing plus clarify loss mechanism.
