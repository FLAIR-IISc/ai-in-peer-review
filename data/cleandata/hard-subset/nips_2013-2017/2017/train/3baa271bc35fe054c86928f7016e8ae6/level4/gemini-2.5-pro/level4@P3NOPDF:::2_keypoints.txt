- Problem: robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a streaming setting.  
- Method: a two-stage procedure—STAR-T (streaming candidate selection with partitioning and decreasing thresholds) followed by a greedy algorithm on the candidate set.  
- Theory: STAR-T has a constant approximation factor (Theorem 4.1) and the two-stage method is provably robust to element loss (Lemmas 4.2–4.3).  
- Experiments: evaluated on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Strength: Novelty — appears to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with solid theoretical foundations and potential impact.  
- Strength: Practicality — STAR-T uses two hyperparameters (τ, w); τ can be approximated (Theorem 4.5) and w=1 works well empirically, reducing tuning burden.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous empirical performance — STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Stream baseline and needs more thorough comparison or analysis of regimes where it excels.  
- Weakness: Influence experiment objective — uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (neighbors plus seeds) instead of standard stochastic spread models (e.g., Independent Cascade), raising questions about motivation and generalizability to typical influence maximization.
