- Proposes a two-stage method: STAR-T (robust streaming algorithm using a partitioning structure and decreasing-threshold rule) followed by a simple greedy second stage.  
- Theoretical guarantee: STAR-T attains a constant-factor approximation for arbitrarily large k (Thm. 4.1).  
- Robustness to deletions: Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show STAR-T combined with greedy can handle loss/removal of elements.  
- Hyperparameters: STAR-T has τ and w; τ can be approximated (Thm. 4.5) and experiments indicate w=1 works well, reducing tuning burden.  
- Novelty/impact: Likely the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with wide potential applications.  
- Empirical results: Method performs well on tested tasks (influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation).  
- Experimental weakness: Evaluation is not very expressive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline.  
- Modeling concern: Influence objective is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which differs from the standard expected spread objective, leaving unclear how STAR-T performs under standard influence models.
