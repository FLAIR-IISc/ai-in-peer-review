Introduces STAR-T, a two-stage streaming algorithm with greedy post-processing for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k.  
STAR-T leverages a partitioning structure and decreasing thresholding rule to operate on data streams.  
Provides a constant-factor approximation guarantee for STAR-T in the limit k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show resilience to element loss when STAR-T is paired with greedy refinement.  
Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm specifically for robust submodular maximization and notes simple parameter tuning.  
Gives guidance for approximating the threshold τ (Theorem 4.5) and empirically finds w = 1 works well.  
Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrates effectiveness but is limited in scale and scope.  
Results do not consistently show clear advantage over Sieve-St and lack statistical significance testing.  
Influence maximization experiment uses non-standard objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, deviating from classical spread models without justification.  
Unclear how the algorithm performs under standard independent cascade or linear threshold models; needs clarification or additional experiments.  
Hyperparameter sensitivity (τ and w) and computational scalability with stream size and k are not thoroughly investigated.  
Suggestions: expand experiments/datasets, include significance tests, justify or switch to standard influence objective, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity and computational complexity.  
Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable contribution but experimental shortcomings and objective mismatch lead to a weak reject recommendation.
