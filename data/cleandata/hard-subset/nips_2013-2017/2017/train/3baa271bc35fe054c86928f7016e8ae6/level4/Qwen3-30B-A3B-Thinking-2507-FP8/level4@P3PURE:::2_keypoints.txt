- Paper proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization: STAR-T (robust streaming with partitioning and decreasing thresholds) followed by greedy selection.  
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T achieves a constant approximation as k grows large.  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show the STAR-T + greedy combination effectively handles element loss.  
- Theorem 4.5 provides guidance for setting the threshold hyperparameter τ.  
- Experiments report competitive results on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.  
- The algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w; experiments suggest w = 1 is sufficient, simplifying use.  
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potentially broad applications.  
- Primary experimental concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Stream baseline.  
- Methodological concern: the influence-maximization objective used, f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, differs from the standard Kempe et al. spread model f(Z)=|⋃_{v∈Z} N(v)|; performance under the intended spread model is unclear and needs clarification.
