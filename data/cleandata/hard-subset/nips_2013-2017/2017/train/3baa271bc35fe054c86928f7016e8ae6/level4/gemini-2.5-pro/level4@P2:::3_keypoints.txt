- Novel problem: single-pass streaming + robustness to adversarial removal (up to m) for monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k.  
- Method: two-stage approach — STAR-T builds a robust summary via partitioned buckets with exponential thresholding; STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on the summary after removals.  
- Theory: provable constant-factor approximation (0.149) with memory O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Algorithmic insight: effectively combines robust partitioning schemes with streaming thresholding techniques.  
- Experiments: competitive results on dominating set and personalized movie recommendation tasks; compares favorably even to a SIEVE-STREAMING variant given advance knowledge of removals.  
- Strength — originality/significance: first constant-factor single-pass algorithm for this robust setting; practical and well-motivated.  
- Strength — technical quality: strong theoretical guarantee and elegant synthesis of prior ideas; solid empirical validation.  
- Strength — clarity/presentation: well-written, clear problem setup and helpful illustrations (notably the movie recommendation example).  
- Weakness — clarity of analysis: main-text proof sketch is dense and lacks high-level intuition about handling partitioning, thresholds, and adversarial removals.  
- Weakness — lack of lower bounds: no discussion or hardness results to contextualize whether the approximation or memory tradeoff is near-optimal.  
- Weakness — influence-maximization experiment: uses a dominating-set objective (local neighborhood) rather than standard global diffusion models, raising questions about streaming feasibility or misframing the experiment.  
- Overall: significant and solid contribution with minor, addressable issues around exposition and experimental framing.
