- Paper studies robust submodular maximization under cardinality k in streaming with up to m deletions; proposes two-stage STAR-T (streaming summary) + STAR-T-GREEDY (query) approach.  
- STAR-T builds a compact partitioned summary with exponentially decreasing thresholds; items go to the first bucket meeting threshold.  
- After removals, STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on S \ E to produce a size-k solution.  
- Theoretically proves a constant-factor approximation; ratio approaches c → 0.149 as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show STAR-T remains effective despite deletions; analysis tailored to robustness.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show good practical performance in some settings.  
- Originality: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization; novel and timely contribution.  
- Algorithmic design: simple, intuitive partitioned thresholding yields provable guarantees when combined with greedy.  
- Practical usability: only two hyperparameters (τ, w); τ can be approximated via parallel instances (Thm 4.5) and w=1 works in experiments.  
- Empirical weakness: experimental section is limited and STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently outperform strong baselines like SIEVE-STREAMING.  
- Modeling weakness: influence maximization uses f(Z)=|N(Z)∪Z| instead of the standard probabilistic diffusion model, leaving applicability to the KKT spread model unclear.  
- Quality/clarity: theoretical results are clear and sound; high-level presentation and hyperparameter discussion are helpful.  
- Significance: robust streaming summarization is important and the theoretical contribution is nontrivial, but stronger experiments are needed to demonstrate practical gains.  
- Pros for acceptance: first streaming robust submodular algorithm with constant guarantee, simple two-stage design, and easy hyperparameter handling.  
- Cons for acceptance: mixed empirical results vs. baselines and nonstandard influence objective raise concerns about practical impact.
