- Problem: Robust streaming submodular maximization formalized as a two-stage task—streaming summary S, then query selects k elements after any m removals.  
- Proposed methods: STAR-T (streaming algorithm with partitioned thresholding and exponentially decreasing thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY (query-stage greedy).  
- Theoretical guarantees: summary size O((m log k + k) log^2 k) and constant-factor approximation 0.149(1−1/log k) after any m removals.  
- Technical novelty: partitioned thresholding structure and exponential threshold rule distinguish it from prior work.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and robust submodular maximization literature, extending them to the robust streaming setting.  
- Quality: theoretical results are technically sound with detailed analysis and proofs.  
- Empirics: matches or outperforms SIEVE-STREAMING on two tasks (dominating set, personalized movie recommendation), even when baselines know removed elements.  
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, algorithms with pseudocode and comprehensive supplementary proofs.  
- Originality: novel single-framework treatment of both streaming and robustness, distinct from previous isolated approaches.  
- Significance: provides a valuable theoretical framework for robust streaming summarization, but demonstrated applications are limited.  
- Strengths: strong technical contribution, non-trivial analysis, convincing experiments, clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Weaknesses: motivation and broader applications could be stronger; problem formulation is somewhat narrow and may be incremental.  
- For acceptance: significant technical novelty, solid guarantees, and practical empirical performance.  
- Against acceptance: limited application scope and unclear broader impact compared to other recent work.  
- Overall: technically strong and valuable contribution, though its broader significance needs better justification.
