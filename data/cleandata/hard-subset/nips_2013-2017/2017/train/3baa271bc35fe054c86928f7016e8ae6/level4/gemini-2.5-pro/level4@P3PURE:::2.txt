This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage introduces STAR-T, a novel robust streaming algorithm based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage applies the simple greedy method. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee for sufficiently large k (Theorem 4.1). Furthermore, Lemmas 4.2-4.3 show that the full procedure, combining STAR-T with the greedy method, is robust to the loss of elements. Experimental results on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness.

A key contribution of this work is that it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting has a potentially wide range of applications, and this work is an enlightening first step in this direction. On a practical note, the STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, \(\tau\) and w. In Theorem 4.5, the authors show how to approximate \(\tau\), and their experiments suggest that w=1 performs well. This makes STAR-T convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My major concern is that the experimental evaluation is not entirely convincing, as the proposed STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline.

In addition, the choice of objective function for the Influence Maximization experiment is questionable. The paper defines the objective as f(Z) = |N(Z) U Z| (line 219), which is different from the standard influence spread model in the cited work by Kempe, Kleinberg, and Tardos (2003). The paper does not justify this deviation, which makes it unclear how STAR-T would perform on the standard formulation of the influence maximization problem.
