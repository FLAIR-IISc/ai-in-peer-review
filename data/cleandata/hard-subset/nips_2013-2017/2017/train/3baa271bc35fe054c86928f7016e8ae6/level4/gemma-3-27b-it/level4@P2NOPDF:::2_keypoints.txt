- Introduces a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T streaming algorithm followed by a greedy refinement.  
- STAR-T is built on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding strategy.  
- The second stage applies a simple greedy method to improve the streaming output.  
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T attains a constant-factor approximation even as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 establish robustness to element loss when combined with the greedy step.  
- Empirical validation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation reports promising results.  
- Significance: claimed as the first streaming algorithm specifically for robust submodular maximization, with broad potential impact.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 offers guidance for τ and experiments indicate w = 1 is sufficient.  
- Experimental concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat Sieve-Streaming, weakening empirical support.  
- Methodological concern: Influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|, which deviates from the standard Kempe et al. spread model and requires clarification.
