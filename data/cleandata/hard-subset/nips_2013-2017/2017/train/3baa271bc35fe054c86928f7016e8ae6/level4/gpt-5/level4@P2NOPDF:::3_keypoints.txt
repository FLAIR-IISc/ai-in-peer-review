- Problem and goal: single-pass streaming submodular maximization with post hoc robustness to up to m deletions, selecting k items while storing a small subset.  
- Novelty: first work (to reviewer’s knowledge) to jointly address streaming and deletion-robustness.  
- Algorithm: STAR-T uses partitioning and thresholded bucket structures to store O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements.  
- Guarantee: provable constant-factor approximation 0.149 that holds after any adversarial removal of up to m elements.  
- Empirics: strong performance on influence maximization (using a proxy objective) and movie recommendation, outperforming some streaming baselines even given advance deletion info.  
- Technical significance: obtaining a constant-factor guarantee under streaming+robustness is a meaningful and timely contribution.  
- Major critique — lower bounds: paper lacks nontrivial lower bounds or impossibility results quantifying trade-offs among space, m, and approximation.  
- Major critique — clarity of analysis: the “innovative analysis” is not clearly distinguished from prior thresholding/bucketing work; key lemmas, intuition, case structure, and source of the 0.149 constant are hard to extract.  
- Critique — influence-maximization evaluation: uses a simplified neighborhood-size proxy that avoids global diffusion dynamics, weakening empirical claims and leaving unclear the streaming access/oracle model and evaluation cost for true diffusion objectives.  
- Suggestion: justify the proxy’s correlation with true spread or run experiments using realistic diffusion objectives (e.g., via sketches or influence oracles).  
- Suggestion: explicitly compare to prior robust submodular bucket algorithms to isolate what bucket-filling/threshold changes enable streaming robustness.  
- Suggestion: provide implementation details (memory per bucket, per-element update time) and sensitivity analyses over m, k, and threshold schedules.  
- Suggestion: if lower bounds are out of scope, add a discussion of plausible barriers and open problems (e.g., whether improving the constant requires more space).  
- Overall summary: promising and practical algorithm with provable guarantees and good empirical results, but would benefit from clearer analytical exposition, stronger evaluation for influence maximization, and complementary lower-bound or discussion material.
