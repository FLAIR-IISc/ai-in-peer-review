- Paper studies single-pass streaming robust maximization of a monotone submodular function under cardinality k with up to m adversarial removals from the summary S.  
- Proposes a two-stage approach: STAR-T (streaming summary built via thresholded partitioned buckets) and STAR-T-GREEDY (greedy on S \ E to produce final set Z).  
- Proves a constant-factor approximation (~0.149) and builds a summary of size O((k + m log k) log k), improving memory dependence on k.  
- Extends thresholding ideas from SIEVE-STREAMING and partitioning from centralized PRO while introducing a streaming-appropriate filling mechanism.  
- Claims advantage over concurrent work (Mirzasoleiman et al.) in memory complexity (logarithmic vs. linear in k).  
- Strength: tackles a well-motivated, challenging intersection of submodular optimization, streaming, and robustness with a single-pass low-memory algorithm.  
- Strength: technically strong and non-trivial analysis, using a clever case-based proof balancing full vs. not-full partitions.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and places contributions relative to prior work effectively.  
- Weakness: adversarial model assumes removals E ⊆ S (adversary knows algorithm’s output), which may be weakly motivated in practical settings.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited, primarily comparing to a non-robust baseline (SIEVE-STREAMING) and not to other robust methods or concurrent work.  
- Weakness: proven approximation constant (0.149) is modest and may limit practical appeal; unclear if this is analysis looseness or inherent.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a novel problem, offers a solid technical contribution, provides superior memory complexity, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical motivation for E ⊆ S is questionable, experimental comparisons are incomplete, and the approximation constant is small.  
- Conclusion: overall high-quality, technically valuable paper with some concerns about model motivation and experiments; reviewer recommends acceptance.
