- Problem: Maximizes a monotone submodular function under cardinality k in a streaming setting robust to removal of m elements via a two-stage summary-and-greedy process.
- Method: Proposes STAR-T to build a compact robust summary using partitioning with exponentially decreasing thresholds and bucket sizes, then STAR-T-GREEDY on the remaining summary.
- Theory: Proves a constant-factor approximation ≈0.149 and summary size O((k + m log k) log k).
- Novelty: One of the first works to jointly handle streaming and robustness, building on SIEVE-STREAMING and recent robust partitioning ideas; acknowledges concurrent work.
- Experiments: Evaluated on dominating set and movie recommendation tasks, often matching or outperforming strong baselines that know removed elements.
- Strength — Originality/Significance: Tackles a novel, practically important problem at the intersection of streaming and robust submodular optimization with a well-motivated framework.
- Strength — Technical Quality: Provides a non-trivial, technically sound analysis and constant-factor guarantee; STAR-T is an elegant algorithmic combination.
- Strength — Experimental Quality: Thoughtful experiments emphasizing robustness, including a strong baseline comparison where the baseline has extra information.
- Weakness — Clarity of Analysis: Main theorem proof sketch is hard to follow and lacks intuitive explanation of the case analysis and how the final constant arises.
- Weakness — Theoretical Completeness: No discussion of lower bounds or whether the provided constant is close to optimal, leaving theoretical context incomplete.
- Weakness — Experimental Scope: Dominating set is a simplified proxy for influence maximization; the limitation and justification for this choice (vs. standard diffusion models) should be discussed.
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated contribution with significant theoretical and empirical results; weaknesses are addressable and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
