- Novel problem: first paper to jointly address streaming and robust submodular maximization.
- Main contribution: STAR-T algorithm retains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) items and achieves a 0.149 approximation robust to removal of m elements.
- Technical approach: combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket-based maintenance, filling buckets differently from prior work.
- Experimental results: thorough evaluation showing strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, outperforming streaming baselines even those that know removals.
- Strength: achieves a constant-factor approximation in a challenging combined streaming+robust setting; results are technically sound.
- Weakness: no non-trivial lower bounds provided to complement the upper bound.
- Weakness: analysis is hard to follow; claims of an “innovative analysis” are not clearly explained or distilled.
- Weakness: influence maximization evaluation uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective, questioning realism and applicability to standard influence models.
- Presentation: paper quality is high but would benefit from clearer exposition to reach a broader audience.
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions encouraged to improve analysis presentation and provide more convincing applications.
