- Studies streaming robust submodular maximization: find T so that after any m deletions, a k-element S maximizing f can still be found.  
- Frames this as a robust version of the standard submodular maximization problem.  
- Notes prior work on streaming submodular algorithms but argues robustness is useful when elements may be removed after computation.  
- Gives a concrete motivation/example: recommendation systems where some recommended items may be removed.  
- Presents a constant-factor approximation algorithm that returns T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.  
- Claims the results are correct; the algorithm and analysis are non-trivial and technically challenging.  
- Main concern: relevance to the conference is unclear—applications/motivation are not convincingly strong and the problem formulation may lack sufficient generality.
