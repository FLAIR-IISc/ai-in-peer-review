- Paper addresses streaming + robust submodular maximization jointly — first work to tackle both challenges together.  
- Problem: pick k high-value elements from a retained subset after a stream while being robust to up to m removals.  
- Proposed algorithm (STAR-T) retains O((m log k + k) log² k) elements and guarantees a 0.149 approximation.  
- Algorithmic approach: partitioning and thresholding using buckets; bucket-filling differs from prior robust-submodular work.  
- Experiments show strong performance on influence maximization and movie recommendation, even versus algorithms that know removed elements.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is a notable contribution.  
- Theoretical gap: paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper bound.  
- Presentation/analysis issues: claims of innovation are not well explained and the proof sketch does not clearly convey key ideas or steps.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective; unclear how standard network diffusion objectives would work in the streaming setting.  
- Overall: solid empirical and algorithmic results but needs stronger theory and clearer exposition, especially for the analysis and influence-maximization modeling.
