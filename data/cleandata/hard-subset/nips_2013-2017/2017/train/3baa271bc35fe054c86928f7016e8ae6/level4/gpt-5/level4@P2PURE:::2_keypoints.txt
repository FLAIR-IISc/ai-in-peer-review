- Proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy stage.  
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for arbitrary k.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: STAR-T combined with the greedy step can handle the loss of elements.  
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with broad potential applications.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 shows how to approximate τ, and experiments suggest w = 1 works well.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is limited—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-St.  
- Concern: influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|, differing from the standard Kempe et al. spread model, so performance under the standard model is unclear.
