- Paper tackles streaming submodular maximization with robustness to removal of up to m elements, presenting the first unified approach for streaming + robustness and motivating real-world relevance.

- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log² k) elements and achieves a 0.149-approximation while tolerating any m removals.

- STAR-T uses partitioning, thresholding, and a novel bucket-filling strategy adapting prior robust submodular ideas.

- Experiments show competitive results on influence maximization and movie recommendation, sometimes outperforming baselines that know removals in advance.

- Theoretical contribution is strong: obtaining a constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is non-trivial.

- Major weakness: no non-trivial lower bounds are provided—lack of matching impossibility results weakens the theoretical narrative.

- Major weakness: analysis presentation is unclear; the paper claims innovation but buries core insights and key lemmas in technical detail, needing a more structured exposition.

- Major weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than standard diffusion-based spread, raising concerns about realism and streamability.

- Minor issues: unclear if removals are adversarial or stochastic; space complexity notation could be more precise; connection between the 0.149 approximation/space bounds and empirical results is not well explained.

- Overall: technically solid and important contribution, but needs clearer analysis exposition, stronger theoretical lower bounds or justification, and more realistic experimental validation to be fully convincing.
