This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, the authors design a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing threshold rule. In the second stage, they apply a simple greedy method. STAR-T is proven to achieve a constant-factor approximation for arbitrary k (Thm. 4.1). Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show that STAR-T, combined with the simple greedy method, can handle the loss of elements.

Experimentally, the proposed approach performs well on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. This work is interesting as, to my knowledge, it is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have broad applicability, and the paper is therefore quite enlightening. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, tau and w. Thm. 4.5 shows how to approximate tau, and the experiments indicate that setting w=1 works well. This makes STAR-T convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My main concern is that the experimental evaluation is not very informative or comprehensive. In particular, the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming (Sieve-St) baseline.

In addition, it is unusual that the influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), which is not the same as the objective in the canonical reference (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, “Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network,” KDD 2003). It is unclear to me how STAR-T performs under that standard spread model.
