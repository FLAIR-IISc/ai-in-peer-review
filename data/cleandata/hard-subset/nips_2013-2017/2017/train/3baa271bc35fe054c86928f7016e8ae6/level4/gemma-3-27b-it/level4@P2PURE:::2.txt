This paper introduces a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization subject to a cardinality constraint *k*. The first stage employs a novel robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which leverages a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. A simple greedy method is then applied in the second stage. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as *k* approaches infinity (Thm 4.1). Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 establish that combining STAR-T with the subsequent greedy method effectively handles element loss. 

Experimental results demonstrate the algorithm’s effectiveness on both influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is notable as a first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, and the potential for wide-ranging applications of a streaming setting makes it a valuable contribution. The STAR-T algorithm features two hyperparameters, τ and *w*. The authors provide guidance on approximating τ (Thm 4.5) and demonstrate empirically that *w*=1 performs well, simplifying the algorithm’s usage by reducing the need for hyperparameter tuning. 

However, a primary concern is the limited expressiveness of the experimental evaluation. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Furthermore, the objective function used for Influence Maximization, f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the standard formulation presented in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). Consequently, the performance of STAR-T within this specific spread model is unclear.
