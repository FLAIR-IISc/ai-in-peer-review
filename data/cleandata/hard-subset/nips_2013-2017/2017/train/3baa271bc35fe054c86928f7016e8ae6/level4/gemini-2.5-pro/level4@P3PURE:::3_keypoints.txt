- Tackles the novel joint problem of streaming submodular maximization with robustness to adversarial removal of up to m elements.  
- Proposes STAR-T with space O((m log k + k) log^2 k) and a constant-factor approximation guarantee of 0.149.  
- Achieves robustness via a combination of partitioning, thresholding, and a bucket-based scheme.  
- Introduces a new method for populating buckets specifically for the streaming setting, building on prior bucket ideas.  
- Provides empirical results on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks.  
- Shows strong empirical performance, even against non-robust streaming baselines that have oracle knowledge of removals.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: first constant-factor approximation for the combined streaming-plus-robust model.  
- Missing non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper-bound approximation guarantee.  
- Analysis is unclear: “innovative” techniques are not well-explained and the proof sketch is hard to follow.  
- Influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, leaving uncertainty about applicability to standard, graph-dependent influence functions in the streaming model.
