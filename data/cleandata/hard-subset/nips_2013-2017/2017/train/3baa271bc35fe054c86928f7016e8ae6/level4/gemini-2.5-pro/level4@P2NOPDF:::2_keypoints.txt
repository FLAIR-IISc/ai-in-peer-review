- Paper proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint: STAR-T streaming selection followed by a greedy refinement.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing threshold rule to construct a candidate set in streaming fashion.
- Final solution is produced by running a simple greedy algorithm on the candidate set.
- Theoretical results include a constant approximation guarantee for STAR-T as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show the combined STAR-T-Greedy procedure is robust to loss/removal of selected elements.
- The authors give a practical way to approximate the threshold τ (Theorem 4.5) and empirically find w = 1 works well, reducing hyperparameter tuning.
- Novelty: first work (to reviewer’s knowledge) addressing robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting.
- Empirical evaluation covers influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.
- Experiments are a weakness: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently beat the Sieve-St baseline across all settings and needs deeper analysis on when it should outperform.
- Major concern: influence maximization experiment uses a simplified objective f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| rather than standard stochastic spread models (IC/LT), making it unclear how the method performs on the conventional problem.
- Recommendation: clarify/justify the simplified influence objective or evaluate the algorithm under standard influence spread models to better assess performance.
