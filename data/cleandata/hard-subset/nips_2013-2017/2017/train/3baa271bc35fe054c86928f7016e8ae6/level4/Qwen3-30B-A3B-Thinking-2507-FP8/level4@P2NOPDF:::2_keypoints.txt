- Proposes a two-stage method (STAR-T streaming algorithm with decreasing thresholds + greedy second stage) for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k.  
- Theoretical guarantees: STAR-T attains a constant-factor approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1); Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show robustness to element loss when combined with greedy.  
- Practicality: STAR-T uses only two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a procedure to approximate τ.  
- Empirical claims: experiments on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation show promising performance.  
- Novelty claim: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, filling a gap with potential broad applicability.  
- Experimental concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-St across datasets/settings (Table 2), undermining claimed superiority.  
- Missing efficiency comparison: paper lacks thorough runtime/compute-cost comparisons to competitors, limiting practical assessment.  
- Modeling concern: influence objective uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| rather than standard f(Z)=|N(Z)|, which may invalidate applicability to standard influence maximization.  
- Unclear robustness: paper does not evaluate or discuss STAR-T under the conventional influence spread model, leaving experimental validity in question.  
- Related work gap: insufficient explicit contrast with existing offline robust methods and limited discussion of streaming advantages in dynamic settings.  
- Hyperparameter justification: empirical claim w=1 needs stronger justification; ablation/sensitivity studies on τ and w are missing.  
- Writing issues: generally clear but contains minor grammatical problems and wording improvements are needed.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with solid theory, but experimental shortcomings (model choice, inconsistent results, lack of baselines/efficiency and hyperparameter analysis) prevent acceptance in current form.
