- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a robust streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy selection.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure combined with a decreasing thresholding rule to build the stream summary.
- Theorem 4.1 claims STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k → ∞.
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 show that combining STAR-T with the greedy post-processing effectively handles element losses.
- Experiments report strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Novelty: introduces the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potential broad applicability.
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ, and experiments indicate w = 1 performs well, reducing tuning burden.
- Concern: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive and STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming.
- Concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the classical diffusion/spread model in Kempe et al., so STAR-T’s performance on the original spread model remains unclear and needs further study.
