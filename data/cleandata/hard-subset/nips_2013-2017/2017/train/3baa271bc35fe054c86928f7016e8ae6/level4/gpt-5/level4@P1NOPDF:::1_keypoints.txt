- Problem: Studies robust streaming submodular maximization—construct a summary T from a stream so that after deletion of any m elements from T, one can still select a k-element set S that maximizes a submodular function f.

- Applications: Useful when chosen items may later need removal (e.g., recommender systems), but the paper’s stated applications and motivation are weak.

- Prior context: Builds on known streaming algorithms for the non-robust case by addressing the less-studied robust variant.

- Results: Provides a constant-factor approximation while returning a summary T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.

- Technical assessment: Results appear correct; the algorithm and analysis are nontrivial and technically challenging.

- Main concern: Relevance to the conference is questionable—the problem may not be general enough and lacks strong motivation.
