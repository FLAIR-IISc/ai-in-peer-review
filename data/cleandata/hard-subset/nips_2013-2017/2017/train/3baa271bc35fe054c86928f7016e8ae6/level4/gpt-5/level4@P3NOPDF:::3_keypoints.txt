- Problem and contribution: Proposes STAR-T for single-pass streaming robust submodular maximization, storing O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and giving a 0.149 approximation after any adversarial removal of up to m items.

- Empirical claim: Shows competitive performance on simplified influence (one-hop coverage) and movie recommendation tasks, including against a baseline that knows removed items.

- Strength: Tackles the combined streaming+robustness setting, which is practically relevant and underexplored.

- Strength: Provides a constant-factor approximation with explicit near-linear space bounds (up to polylog factors).

- Strength: Algorithmic design is simple and implementable, using partitioned thresholded buckets tied to recent robust submodular ideas.

- Strength: Empirical results are encouraging even under strong testing conditions.

- Weakness: No lower bounds or hardness discussion, limiting judgment of optimality of space and approximation guarantees.

- Weakness: Analysis is unclear; the paper does not clearly articulate what is fundamentally new and the proof sketch is hard to follow.

- Weakness: The origin and potential tunability of the 0.149 constant and necessity of the log^2 k storage factor are opaque.

- Weakness: Unclear whether algorithm requires prior knowledge of m, k, or value ranges and how it handles stream order or mis-specified parameters.

- Weakness: Practical per-item update time, constants in memory, and post-deletion reconstruction cost are not reported.

- Weakness: Influence experiments use a simplified one-hop coverage objective, raising concerns about realism and scalability to global diffusion models; the “knows deletions” baseline may be unfair.

- Suggestion: Add a high-level roadmap of the analysis, intuitions for partitioning/thresholds, and a small illustrative example showing bucket behavior under deletions.

- Suggestion: Discuss or derive lower bounds/barriers or relate to known streaming impossibility results to contextualize guarantees.

- Suggestion: Clarify required parameters, propose/adopt adaptive schemes (doubling/guessing), and evaluate sensitivity to mis-specification of m and k.

- Suggestion: Report per-item update time, memory footprints with constants, post-deletion reconstruction cost, and throughput/memory on datasets.

- Suggestion: Broaden experiments to standard submodular objectives (facility location, weighted coverage, determinantal diversity), use sketch-based or multi-hop influence proxies, compare to non-robust streaming and offline robust baselines, vary k and m, test stream order, and include ablations on bucket/threshold choices.

- Suggestion: Ensure baseline fairness by clearly stating what information each method uses during streaming.

- Presentation: Improve notation clarity, summarize space and approximation bounds in main text, and include a compact table of theoretical guarantees.

- Minor clarifications: State whether deletions occur post-stream or online, assumptions on monotonicity/normalization/curvature, and provide code or full pseudocode for reproducibility.

- Overall evaluation: Novel and nontrivial first constant-factor streaming-robust result with promising experiments, but needs clearer analysis, lower-bound context, and stronger/more realistic experiments; reviewer leans weak accept if addressed, otherwise borderline.
