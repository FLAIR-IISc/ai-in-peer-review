- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: a streaming algorithm (STAR-T) followed by a simple greedy selection.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule to build its streaming summary.
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T admits a constant-factor approximation guarantee asymptotically as k grows large.
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: combining STAR-T with the greedy stage effectively copes with element loss (robustness).
- Experimental results claim strong empirical performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with potential broad applicability in streaming settings.
- Method has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives guidance for τ and experiments suggest w=1 suffices, reducing tuning needs.
- Empirical concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Stream baseline across all scenarios.
- Major validity concern: the influence-maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard cascade model (Kempe et al.), so reported performance may not reflect behavior under the conventional spread objective.
