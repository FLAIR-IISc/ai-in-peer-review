- Problem: Robust streaming submodular maximization — choose T from ground set E so that after any removal of up to m elements, one can still pick a k-size S maximizing submodular f(S).
- Context: Extends well-established streaming submodular maximization to a robust variant where chosen items may later be removed.
- Applications: Motivated by real-world cases (e.g., recommendations where some items get rejected), but reviewer finds the application motivation relatively weak.
- Main result: A constant-factor approximation that outputs T of size (m · log k + k) · log^2 k.
- Correctness: Reviewer believes the results and proofs are correct.
- Technical contribution: Non-trivial algorithm and significant technical depth in the algorithm and analysis.
- Concern: Relevance to the conference is questionable due to limited generality and weak practical motivation.
