- Problem: Studies streaming robust submodular maximization where k high-value elements are selected while retaining few items and being robust to removal of m elements.  
- Motivation: Relevant for large-scale ML with limited passes over data and need for robustness to deletions.  
- Novelty: First work to jointly consider streaming and robust submodular maximization.  
- Related work: Builds on greedy and SIEVE-STREAMING and prior robust submodular optimization but combines ideas in a new way.  
- Algorithm: Proposes STAR-T that maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements in the stream.  
- Approximation: STAR-T achieves a constant 0.149 approximation guarantee while tolerating removal of m items.  
- Technique: Combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket-based filling done differently for streaming efficiency.  
- Theory: The theoretical analysis and proof sketch are sound, but the novelty of the analysis vs prior work is not clearly highlighted.  
- Missing theory: Paper would benefit from non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on influence maximization and movie recommendation are convincing overall.  
- Influence-max caveat: Influence experiment uses a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising questions about applicability to standard network-wide influence objectives in streaming.  
- Writing/clarity: Paper is well-written but the analysis section is sometimes hard to follow and needs clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Impact: Strong, significant contribution with potential wide use in large-scale ML.  
- Review summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; arguments for acceptance include novelty and guarantees, arguments for rejection include clarity, experimental realism, and lack of lower bounds.
