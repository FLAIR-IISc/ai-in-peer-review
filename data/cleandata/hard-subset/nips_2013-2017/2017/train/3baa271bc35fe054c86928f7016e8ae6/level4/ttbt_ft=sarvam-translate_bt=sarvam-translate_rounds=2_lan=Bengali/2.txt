This paper proposes a two-stage approach for robust submodular maximization with cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, is designed, which is based on a partitioning framework and a reducing thresholding rule. In the second stage, a simple greedy approach is used. Proof is provided to ensure the constant approximation of STAR-T when k is up to infinity (Thm4.1). lem4.2~4.3 proves that STAR-T can handle element loss with a simple greedy approach.

Experimental results show that the proposed algorithm can perform well in problems of influence maximization and personalized movie recommendations.

This work is interesting because it is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. It is likely to have wide-ranging applications in streaming, and the work is very instructive. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, \tao and w. In Thm 4.5, the authors show how to find the approximation of \tao. The authors show that "w=1" can perform well in the test. It is convenient to use STAR-T without trying different hyperparameters.

My main concern is that the test is not very understandable. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm cannot always outperform the Sieve-St algorithm.

Also, it is strange that the objective function of influence maximization is f(Z)=|N(Z) \cup Z| (line 219 of this paper), which does not match the function mentioned in the article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is not clear to me how STAR-T works in such a spread model.
