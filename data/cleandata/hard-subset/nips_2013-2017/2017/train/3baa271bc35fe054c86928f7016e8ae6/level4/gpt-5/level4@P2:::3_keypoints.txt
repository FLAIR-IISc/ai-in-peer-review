- Problem: Monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a single-pass streaming model with up to m adversarial deletions and a small stored summary used at query time.

- Proposed method: STAR-T organizes stored elements into exponentially sized buckets with decreasing thresholds and uses STAR-T-GREEDY (GREEDY on surviving summary) at query time.

- Guarantees: With an estimate of OPT, stores O((k + m log k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor approximation asymptotically ~0.149; unknown OPT handled via parallel thresholds with extra log factor.

- Relation to prior work: Bridges streaming submodular maximization (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) and offline robust methods (e.g., PRO); contrasts with concurrent deletion-robust streaming that gets 1/2−ε but uses O(mk log k/ε) memory.

- Theoretical quality: One-pass algorithm with constant-factor guarantee after deletions and near-linear memory in k; proof relies on many full buckets or properties of the last underfull bucket plus GREEDY bounds, but key invariants are under-explained.

- Empirics: Sensible summarization experiments showing STAR-T matches/exceeds baselines (even when baselines know deletions); would benefit from runtime/memory measures and sensitivity analyses.

- Clarity: High-level description is clear, but analysis does not crisply isolate novel technical ingredients, roles of lemmas, or necessity of the threshold schedule; space bound statements are inconsistent.

- Originality: Novel combination of deletion-robust streaming summary via partitioned buckets and decreasing thresholds; interesting memory–approximation trade-off.

- Significance: First deletion-robust one-pass summary with provable constant-factor guarantee; practical performance suggests real-world value despite modest constant.

- Strengths: First principled robust streaming treatment, careful bucket/threshold design, competitive empirical results, and favorable memory–approximation trade-offs versus concurrent work.

- Weaknesses/suggestions: Improve analysis exposition and intuition, discuss practical constants and parameter choices, clarify parallel-threshold/OPT dependence and space bounds, strengthen experiments (diffusion models, wall-clock, memory, larger deletions), and expand related-work comparison.

- Pro-accept reasons: Novel and well-motivated setting, clean one-pass algorithmic design, and promising empirical performance.

- Con-accept reasons: Analytical novelty not well distilled, small constant guarantees, and potentially weak experimental proxy for influence maximization.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept pending clearer analysis exposition and stronger empirical validation.
