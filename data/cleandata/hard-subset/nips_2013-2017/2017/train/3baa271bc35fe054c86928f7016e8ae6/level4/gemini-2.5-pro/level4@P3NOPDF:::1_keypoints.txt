- Problem: Robust submodular maximization in a single-pass stream—select summary T robust to any m adversarial deletions so a k-subset remains a good approximation; algorithm returns T of size O((m log k + k) log² k).
- Strength: Technically deep—non-trivial streaming algorithm with rigorous, sound proofs and analysis.
- Strength: Novel—appears to be the first work achieving a constant-factor approximation for this formulation in the streaming setting.
- Weakness: Motivation weak—application examples (e.g., recommender systems) are underdeveloped and the adversarial deletion model feels unrealistic for those scenarios.
- Weakness: Adversarial deletions unlikely to model real user behavior; stochastic feedback or online learning models may be more appropriate.
- Weakness: Relevance to NIPS is questionable—the paper reads like a theoretical CS contribution (SODA/FOCS) rather than addressing core ML problems.
- Suggestion: Strengthen impact by connecting techniques to ML-relevant uncertainty models (stochastic noise, corrupted data) or demonstrating practical applicability.
- Conclusion: Technically impressive and novel, but practical relevance and conference fit are limited; better suited for a theory venue unless motivation and ML connections are substantially improved.
