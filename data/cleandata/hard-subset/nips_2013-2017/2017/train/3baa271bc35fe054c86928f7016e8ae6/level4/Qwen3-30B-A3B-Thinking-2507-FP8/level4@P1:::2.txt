

This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. The first stage employs a streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which leverages a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule to construct a robust summary. The second stage uses a simple greedy algorithm (STAR-T-GREEDY) to generate a solution from the summary after any m elements have been removed. The authors prove that STAR-T-GREEDY achieves a constant-factor approximation guarantee (c ≈ 0.149) as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1), with the summary size bounded by O((k + m log k) log² k). Theoretical analysis is supported by Lemmas 4.2-4.4, which establish the algorithm's robustness to element removals.

The work is significant as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, addressing a gap in the literature. The authors effectively connect their approach to prior work on robust submodular maximization (e.g., [9, 10, 11]) and streaming algorithms (e.g., [8]). Their partitioning structure extends the SIEVE-STREAMING framework to the robust setting, and they demonstrate how to approximate the threshold parameter τ without prior knowledge of OPT(k, V\E) (Theorem 4.5).

The paper's strengths include a clean theoretical framework, well-motivated problem formulation, and practical validation across two distinct applications: dominating set problems (on ego-Twitter and Amazon co-purchasing networks) and personalized movie recommendation (using MovieLens 1M). The experiments demonstrate that STAR-T-GREEDY achieves performance comparable to SIEVE-STREAMING (which knows the removed elements in advance) across various removal scenarios. The authors further show that the same robust summary can be reused for multiple removal patterns, highlighting the method's practical utility.

The authors also address hyperparameter sensitivity effectively. They show that w=1 performs well in practice (as demonstrated in Section 5) and provide a method to approximate τ without prior knowledge of OPT(k, V\E) (Theorem 4.5), making the algorithm more accessible to practitioners.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. While STAR-T-GREEDY generally matches SIEVE-STREAMING's performance, the results in Figure 2(b,d) show SIEVE-STREAMING slightly outperforming STAR-T-GREEDY for certain parameter settings. The paper does not sufficiently explain these cases or provide a clear comparison of when STAR-T-GREEDY's advantages become apparent. Additionally, the objective function for influence maximization (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from the standard definition used in Kempe et al. (2003), which is defined as the expected number of influenced nodes. This discrepancy requires clarification, as it affects how the algorithm's performance should be interpreted in the context of influence maximization.

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the intersection of streaming algorithms and robust optimization, with clear potential for applications in large-scale machine learning. While the theoretical results are strong and the experimental validation is generally compelling, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the specific scenarios where STAR-T-GREEDY outperforms competing methods, as well as clarification regarding the influence maximization objective function.

In summary, this work presents a novel and well-motivated approach to robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting. The theoretical guarantees are sound, the algorithm is practical with minimal hyperparameter tuning (w=1 works well in practice), and the experimental results demonstrate competitive performance against strong baselines. The paper addresses an important problem with significant practical relevance, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Strengths: Novel problem formulation, strong theoretical guarantees, practical implementation with minimal hyperparameters, compelling experimental results across two applications.

Weaknesses: Experimental comparison with SIEVE-STREAMING could be more detailed, particularly regarding scenarios where STAR-T-GREEDY outperforms the baseline; unclear explanation of the influence maximization objective function compared to standard definitions.
