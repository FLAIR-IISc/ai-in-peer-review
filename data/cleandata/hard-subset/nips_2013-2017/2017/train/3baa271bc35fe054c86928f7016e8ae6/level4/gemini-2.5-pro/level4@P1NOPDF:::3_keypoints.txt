- Tackles first combination of streaming and robust submodular maximization (select k from a stream, resilient to adversarial removal of up to m elements).  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm with single-pass streaming and novel bucket-population method.  
- Space complexity O((m log k + k) log² k) and constant-factor approximation 0.149.  
- Robustness guarantee: solution remains high-value after any adversarial removal of m elements.  
- Builds on partitioning and thresholding (buckets) but claims novel streaming-specific population strategy.  
- Empirical validation on influence maximization and movie recommendation shows strong performance.  
- STAR-T remains competitive even against non-robust streaming baselines with oracle access to removals.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: constant-factor approximation in this challenging combined setting.  
- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize tightness of the upper-bound guarantee.  
- Theoretical presentation is unclear: novelty of analysis not well explained and proof sketch is dense.  
- Influence-maximization experiments use a simplified local-neighborhood objective, raising concerns about applicability to standard global propagation models (IC/LT).
