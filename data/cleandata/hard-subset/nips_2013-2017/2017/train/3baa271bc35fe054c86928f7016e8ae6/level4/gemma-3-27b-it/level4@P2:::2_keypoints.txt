- Proposes STAR-T: a two-stage robust submodular maximization method using a streaming partition with decreasing thresholds followed by greedy selection.
- Proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for STAR-T as cardinality k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1) with supporting lemmas on resilience to element removal (Lemmas 4.2–4.3).
- Provides a method to approximate the threshold parameter τ (Theorem 4.5) and shows practical simplicity with w = 1 performing well.
- Claims novelty as the first streaming algorithm specifically for robust submodular maximization.
- Positions the work well within related literature and correctly motivates the need for robustness in dynamic/large-scale settings.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and gives a solid foundation for the algorithm’s guarantees.
- Experimental results show competitive but not consistently superior performance compared to SIEVE-STREAMING.
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of when and why STAR-T outperforms or underperforms competing methods.
- Discrepancy in the dominating set objective (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) versus standard influence maximization (Kempe et al.) needs clarification and justification.
- Unclear whether reported performance reflects standard influence maximization tasks due to the objective mismatch.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experimental analysis and clarify the objective function choice and implications.
- Main risks against acceptance: insufficiently compelling experimental evidence and the objective-definition inconsistency impacting applicability.
