- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting where up to m elements may be removed, combining two previously separate problem settings.
- Proposes STAR-T, a novel single-pass streaming algorithm that maintains a summary of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).
- Achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation guarantee even after removal of any m elements via STAR-T-GREEDY.
- Key innovation is a partitioned thresholding scheme with exponentially increasing bucket sizes and exponentially decreasing greedy thresholds.
- Differs from prior robust methods (e.g., PRO) and streaming methods (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) by its partitioned thresholds and structure.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and shows robustness and streaming efficiency simultaneously.
- Empirical validation on dominating set (ego-Twitter, Amazon) and MovieLens 1M shows STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms baselines, even when baselines know removals in advance.
- Problem has clear practical relevance to large-scale ML systems requiring robustness under streaming arrivals.
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified objective f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| that does not reflect standard, network-dependent influence objectives.
- Weakness: paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to contextualize the 0.149 approximation and theoretical optimality.
- Weakness: description of the analytical innovations and proof sketch is insufficiently clear and somewhat opaque.
- Suggestion: provide a clearer, more explicit explanation of the novel analytical techniques combining the robust structure and decreasing thresholds.
- Weakness: limited connection to recent NIPS work on related topics; literature linkage could be strengthened.
- Overall recommendation: accept — meaningful contribution with strong theory and promising experiments, despite the noted clarifications and extensions needed.
