

This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint $k$. The first stage employs a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage applies the simple greedy method. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as $k$ grows large (Theorem 4.1). Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 establish that the combination of STAR-T with the greedy method effectively handles element loss. Experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  

This work is interesting as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which may have broad applications and offers valuable insights. STAR-T involves two hyperparameters, $\tau$ and $w$. Theorem 4.5 details how to set $\tau$, while the experiments show that $w=1$ suffices for good performance, simplifying implementation.  

The primary concern is the experimental evaluation's lack of expressiveness: STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Additionally, the objective function for influence maximization is defined as $f(Z)=|N(Z) \cup Z|$ (line 219), which differs from the standard spread model in Kempe et al. (SIGKDD 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T performs under the intended influence spread model, as the cited reference uses $f(Z)=\left|\bigcup_{v \in Z} N(v)\right|$. This discrepancy requires clarification.
