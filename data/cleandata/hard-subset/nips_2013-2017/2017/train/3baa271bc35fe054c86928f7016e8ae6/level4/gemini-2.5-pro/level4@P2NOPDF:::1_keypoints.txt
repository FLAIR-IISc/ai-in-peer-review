- Addresses robust submodular maximization in a single-pass streaming setting where a summary T must remain useful after adversarial deletion of m elements.  
- Goal: after any m deletions from T, extract a k-element subset S from remaining T that attains high value for a monotone submodular function f.  
- Motivation: models scenarios like recommender systems where users may reject part of an initially proposed set.  
- Main contribution: a novel single-pass streaming algorithm achieving a constant-factor approximation for the robust objective.  
- Algorithm returns a summary T of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Technical work is sound, non-trivial, and the analysis appears correct.  
- Primary concern: weakly developed motivation and generic application examples, so relevance to the NIPS/ML community is questionable.  
- Suggestion: need stronger justification of this adversarial-deletion model and clearer connections to other robustness notions to improve impact.
