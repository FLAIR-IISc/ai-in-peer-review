- Paper studies robust streaming submodular maximization: select k high-value items from a stream while remaining robust to removal of up to m retained items.
- Novelty claim: first work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.
- Proposal: STAR-T algorithm that retains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements after the stream.
- Guarantee: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation factor of 0.149 under removal of up to m elements.
- Technique: combines partitioning and thresholding with a bucket-based (but distinct) bucket-filling process for optimizing submodular functions.
- Empirical results: shows effectiveness on influence maximization (with a simplified neighborhood-size objective) and movie recommendation, outperforming some streaming baselines even those with prior removal knowledge.
- Positive assessment: obtaining a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming+robust setting is a significant achievement.
- Critique — theory: paper would benefit from non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper-bound results.
- Critique — clarity: analysis is not presented clearly; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not sufficiently explained in the proof sketch.
- Critique — experiments: influence maximization evaluation is unconvincing because it uses a simplified objective and does not clarify how standard network-dependent influence objectives would be handled in streaming.
