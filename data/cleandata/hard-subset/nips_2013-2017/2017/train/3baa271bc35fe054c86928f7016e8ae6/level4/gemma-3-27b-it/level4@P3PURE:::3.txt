This paper studies the problem of submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting. The goal is to identify a set of *k* high-value elements from a stream of data, while ensuring the solution remains effective even after *m* elements are subsequently removed. Streaming submodular maximization has been widely studied due to its relevance to large-scale applications where only limited data passes are feasible. Robustness has also recently gained attention, motivated by scenarios where elements may be lost and a resilient solution is desired. This work is the first to jointly address both streaming and robustness in submodular maximization.

The authors develop the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log *k* + *k*)log<sup>2</sup> *k*) elements after processing the stream and achieves a constant (0.149) approximation guarantee while being robust to the removal of *m* elements. The algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding techniques for submodular optimization, utilizing buckets to manage the candidate elements. While bucket-based approaches have appeared in prior work on robust submodular optimization, STAR-T employs a novel bucket-filling strategy. Experimental results demonstrate that the algorithm performs well in both influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, even outperforming streaming algorithms with prior knowledge of the removed elements. 

The combination of streaming and robustness presents a challenging problem, and obtaining a constant-factor approximation is a significant achievement. However, several aspects of the paper could be strengthened. The inclusion of non-trivial lower bounds would help contextualize the upper bound. Furthermore, the presentation of the analysis could be improved; the authors claim an “innovative analysis” but do not clearly articulate its novel aspects, making it difficult to follow the key ideas and steps within the proof sketch. Finally, the influence maximization application could be more compelling. The experiments utilize a simplified objective function – solely the size of the seed nodes’ neighborhood – which does not fully reflect traditional influence maximization objectives where the function value depends on the entire network for a given seed set. It is unclear how these more standard objectives could be adapted to a streaming setting.
