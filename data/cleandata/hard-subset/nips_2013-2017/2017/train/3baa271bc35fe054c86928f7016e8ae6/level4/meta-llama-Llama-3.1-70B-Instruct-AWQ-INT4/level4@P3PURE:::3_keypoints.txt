- Paper tackles streaming + robust submodular maximization: select k high-value elements from a small retained subset with up to m removals after the stream.
- Claimed novelty: first work to jointly consider streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.
- Algorithm: STAR-T maintains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149 approximation while tolerating m removals.
- Methodology: combines partitioning and thresholding using buckets; bucket-filling differs from prior robust submodular work.
- Empirical results: strong performance on influence maximization (simplified) and movie recommendation, outperforming some streaming baselines even those knowing removals.
- Strength: obtaining a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming+robust setting is a significant contribution.
- Weakness: no non-trivial lower bounds provided to complement the upper bound.
- Weakness: analysis and writing are unclear—“innovative analysis” not explained and proof sketch lacks main ideas/steps.
- Weakness: influence maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective, raising concerns about realism.
- Open concern: unclear how standard influence maximization objectives that depend on the entire network can be adapted to the streaming setting.
