This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, is designed, which is based on a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule. In the second stage, the simple greedy method is used. STAR-T is proven to have a constant approximation guarantee when k is up to infinity (Theorem 4.1). Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 prove that STAR-T with the simple greedy method can deal with the loss of elements.

Experimental results show that the proposed algorithm can perform well on the problem of influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.

This work is interesting as it is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have a wide range of applications, and this work is very enlightening. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, τ and w. In Theorem 4.5, the authors showed how to approximate τ. The authors also showed that "w=1" can perform well in experiments, making it convenient to use STAR-T without trying different hyperparameters.

However, there are some concerns. The experiments are not very expressive, as the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm cannot always outperform the Sieve-Streaming algorithm. Additionally, the objective function of influence maximization is f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219 in this paper), which differs from the function in the referred article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is not clear how STAR-T performs on such a spread model.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the experiments could be more comprehensive and expressive.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a good effort to explain the technical details. However, some parts of the paper, such as the proof of Theorem 4.1, are not easy to follow and may require multiple readings.

Originality: The paper proposes a novel algorithm for robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.

Significance: The paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of machine learning and data summarization, as it provides a new approach to robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting.
