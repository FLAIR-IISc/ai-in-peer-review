- Summary: Proposes STAR-T (streaming summary with partitioned exponentially decreasing thresholds) and STAR-T-GREEDY (GREEDY on summary after up to m deletions) for deletion-robust monotone submodular maximization under cardinality k in one pass.

- Summary (cont.): Stores O((k + m log k) log k) items for known τ≈f(OPT) and achieves a constant-factor guarantee after removals; parallel thresholds give O((k + m log k) log^2 k) and factor → ~0.149 as k→∞.

- Strength: Problem relevance — addresses practical need for deletion-robust streaming summarization/recommendation without re-scanning data.

- Strength: Algorithmic novelty — combines SIEVE-STREAMING thresholding with non-uniform partitioning (PRO-style) in a new streaming robust summary.

- Strength: Theoretical guarantees — single-pass, small-space summary with constant-factor approximation; careful case analysis and handling of unknown OPT via parallel instances.

- Strength: Practicality — few hyperparameters (w=1 works well empirically) and empirical robustness beyond theoretical regime.

- Weakness: Low worst-case approximation (~0.149) compared to concurrent work achieving ~1/2 − ε, raising practical-impact questions.

- Weakness: Experimental scope — “influence maximization” experiment actually measures dominating set/coverage, not diffusion (IC/LT), weakening claims about influence maximization.

- Weakness: Baseline positioning — STAR-T-GREEDY is not consistently superior to SIEVE-STREAMING (even when SIEVE knows removals); more comprehensive empirical comparisons needed.

- Weakness: Parameter realism — experiments use w=1 and sometimes remove >k items, outside theoretical guarantees; sensitivity studies aligning with theory are missing.

- Quality: Technical soundness — proofs and case decomposition (full vs non-full buckets) appear plausible and handling of unknown OPT mirrors standard practice.

- Quality: Empirical rigor — tasks are reasonable but lack comparisons to concurrent deletion-robust streaming [18], offline robust methods (PRO), and metrics like function-evaluation counts and memory usage.

- Clarity: Writing is generally clear but should explicitly call the graph experiment dominating set/coverage (not influence diffusion) early.

- Clarity: Need guidance on setting w in practice relative to m and k, and explicit reporting of memory footprint.

- Clarity: Should summarize sources of log factors in space bounds and reconcile O((k + m log k) log k) vs log^2 k statements.

- Originality: Novel combination of partitioned thresholding in deletion-robust streaming with memory-logarithmic dependence on k for the robust component; concurrent but explores a different approximation–memory trade-off than [18].

- Significance: Important and practical problem; small memory footprint and competitive empirical results suggest adoption potential despite conservative worst-case constant.

- Pros: Single-pass, low-memory robust summary; reusable at query time for multiple deletions.

- Pros: Simple second stage (GREEDY) and solid theoretical treatment including unknown OPT handling.

- Pros: Empirically competitive even against advantaged baselines.

- Cons: Approximation constant smaller than competing methods; experimental claims need tightening.

- Cons: Missing experiments on diffusion models and limited sensitivity analysis for m, w, τ-grid.

- Cons: No reporting of runtime/memory and no empirical comparison to concurrent deletion-robust streaming method.

- Suggestions: Clarify task naming or add IC/LT experiments; vary m and w including theory-aligned w ≥ ⌈4 log k · m/k⌉ and report memory/performance trade-offs.

- Suggestions: Report function-evaluation counts, wall-clock runtime, and memory usage; compare (or synthetically contrast) with method [18] and offline PRO.

- Suggestions: Provide practitioner guidance on τ-grid and w heuristics that worked well empirically.

- Overall: Well-motivated, novel, and carefully analyzed approach that needs clearer experimental positioning and broader evaluations to strengthen practical claims.
