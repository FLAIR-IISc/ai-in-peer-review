This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization with a cardinality constraint *k*. In the first stage, a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, is designed, leveraging a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. The second stage employs a simple greedy method. The authors prove that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as *k* approaches infinity (Thm 4.1). Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that combining STAR-T with the simple greedy method effectively addresses the loss of elements. 

Experimental results demonstrate the proposed algorithm's effectiveness on both influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks. This work is interesting as it represents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting has the potential for a wide range of applications, and this work offers valuable insights. The STAR-T algorithm includes two hyperparameters, τ and *w*. The authors provide guidance on approximating τ in Theorem 4.5 and show through experiments that setting *w*=1 yields good performance, simplifying the use of STAR-T by eliminating the need for extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My primary concern lies with the limited expressiveness of the experimental results. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. 

Furthermore, the objective function used for Influence Maximization, f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function presented in the referenced work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). Consequently, it is unclear how STAR-T performs within the context of the standard influence spread model.
