

This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint $k$. The first stage employs a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing threshold rule. The second stage applies the simple greedy method. STAR-T is proven to achieve a constant approximation guarantee as $k$ approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Lemmas 4.2–4.3 establish that STAR-T combined with the simple greedy method effectively handles element loss. Experimental results demonstrate strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.  

This work is interesting as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting holds broad applicability, and the paper is highly enlightening. STAR-T has two hyperparameters, $\tau$ and $w$. Theorem 4.5 details how to approximate $\tau$, and the experiments show that $w=1$ performs well, making STAR-T convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.  

The primary concern is that the experimental evaluation lacks sufficient expressiveness: the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Streaming (Sieve-St) algorithm. Additionally, the influence maximization objective function is defined as $f(Z) = |N(Z) \cup Z|$ (line 219), which differs from the standard spread model in Kempe et al. (SIGKDD 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T performs under this non-standard objective.
