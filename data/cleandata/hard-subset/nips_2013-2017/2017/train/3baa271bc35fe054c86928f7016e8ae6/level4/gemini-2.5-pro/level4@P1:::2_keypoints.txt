- Problem: tackles robust submodular maximization with a single-pass streaming algorithm that must withstand removal of up to m elements.  
- Method: proposes a two-stage procedure (STAR-T streaming summary using partitioning + exponential thresholding, then GREEDY on remaining items after removals).  
- Theory: proves a constant-factor approximation guarantee for the two-stage procedure (Theorem 4.1) via non-trivial partitioning and threshold analysis.  
- Practicality: provides methods to set hyperparameters (Theorem 4.5 for τ approximation) and reports w=1 works well in practice.  
- Experiments: evaluates on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation, showing competitive practical performance.  
- Weakness — empirical strength vs baseline: STAR-T-GREEDY does not consistently beat SIEVE-STREAMING and is sometimes slightly worse for certain k values.  
- Weakness — experimental clarity: the dominating set experiment uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (standard dominating set), not standard influence spread models, leaving applicability to IC/LT models unclear.
