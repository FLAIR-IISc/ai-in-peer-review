- Addresses robust submodular maximization in the streaming setting: pick k high-value items resilient to removal of m items.  
- Claims to be the first work to jointly handle streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.  
- Introduces STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) items and gives a 0.149 constant-factor approximation robust to m removals.  
- Uses partitioning, thresholding, and a novel bucket-filling strategy building on prior bucket-based approaches.  
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization (simplified) and movie recommendation shows competitive performance, even vs algorithms that know removals.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation under both streaming and robustness constraints is a significant technical contribution.  
- Lacks a companion lower bound to contextualize the upper-bound guarantee.  
- The analysis is hard to follow; the claimed “innovative analysis” is not clearly explained or highlighted.  
- Influence-maximization experiments use a simplified neighborhood-size objective that may not reflect standard network-based influence functions and leaves unclear how to extend to full objectives in streaming.
