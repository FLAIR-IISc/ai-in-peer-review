This paper presents a two-stage approach to robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, the authors design a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, built on a partitioning scheme with a decreasing threshold rule. In the second stage, they apply a simple greedy method. Theorem 4.1 proves a constant-factor approximation for arbitrarily large k. Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show that STAR-T combined with the simple greedy procedure can handle the loss (deletion) of elements. Experiments indicate good performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.

The work is noteworthy as, to my knowledge, the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting could find broad application, and the paper is illuminating. STAR-T has two hyperparameters, τ and w. Theorem 4.5 explains how to approximate τ, and the experiments show that setting w=1 works well. This makes STAR-T convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My main concern is that the experimental evaluation is not very compelling. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform Sieve-St.

Additionally, it is unusual that the influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), which differs from the function used in the referenced work by Kempe, Kleinberg, and Tardos (KDD 2003). It is therefore unclear how STAR-T performs under the standard diffusion-based spread model.
