- Studies robust submodular maximization in streaming: find T so that after any m removals a k-element S in T still approximately maximizes f.  
- Motivation: robustness to post-selection removals (e.g., user disinterest), but motivation and application breadth are limited.  
- Main technical contribution: a constant-factor approximation algorithm.  
- Algorithm returns T of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Analysis is technically sound, non-trivial, and uses sophisticated proofs.  
- Primary concern: problem is specialized and of questionable relevance to the core NIPS audience.  
- Lacks presentation as a generalization or clear connection to broader research areas.  
- Concept of removals occurring after selection is less compelling than handling noisy/adversarial inputs during streaming.  
- Practical worry: solution size depends on m, undermining utility as m grows; needs discussion of acceptable m regimes.  
- Recommendation: expand scope, clarify applications, or justify regimes where the robust approach offers significant advantage; overall leans toward reject.
