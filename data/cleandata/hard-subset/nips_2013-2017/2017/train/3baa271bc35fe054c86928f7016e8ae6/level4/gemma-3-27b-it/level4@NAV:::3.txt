This paper addresses the challenging problem of maximizing a submodular function in a streaming environment while ensuring robustness to element removals. Existing work has focused on streaming submodular maximization for large-scale applications with limited data passes, and separately on robust submodular maximization to handle potential element loss. This work uniquely combines these two aspects, providing the first constant-factor approximation algorithm for this joint problem.

The authors present STAR-T, an algorithm that maintains O((m log k + k)log^2 k) elements after processing the stream and achieves a 0.149-approximation guarantee, even when m elements are subsequently removed.  STAR-T leverages a combination of partitioning and thresholding techniques, employing buckets to manage candidate elements. While bucket-based approaches have appeared in prior robust submodular optimization research, this paper introduces a novel bucket-filling strategy. The algorithm’s effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, showcasing competitive performance against streaming algorithms with prior knowledge of removed elements.

Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming and robust setting is a significant accomplishment. However, the paper could benefit from further development in several areas.  The inclusion of lower bounds would provide valuable context for the presented upper bound. The analysis, while technically sound, lacks clarity; the authors allude to an “innovative analysis” without sufficiently detailing its novelty, hindering a clear understanding of the key analytical steps.  Furthermore, the application to influence maximization could be strengthened. The experimental evaluation utilizes a simplified objective function focused solely on seed node neighborhoods, and it remains unclear how this approach scales to more traditional influence maximization objectives that consider network-wide effects in a streaming context.
