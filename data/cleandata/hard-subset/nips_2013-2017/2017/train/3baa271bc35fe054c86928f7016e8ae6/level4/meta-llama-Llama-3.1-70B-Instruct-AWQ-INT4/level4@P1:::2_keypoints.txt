- Proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under cardinality constraint k: streaming algorithm STAR-T followed by greedy selection.  
- STAR-T uses a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee even as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3: STAR-T combined with greedy handles loss of elements (robustness).  
- Experiments show competitive performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.  
- Claimed novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization with potentially wide applications.  
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives an approach to approximate τ.  
- Empirically w = 1 works well, reducing hyperparameter tuning.  
- Concern: experiments are not very comprehensive or expressive.  
- Concern: STAR-T-Greedy does not always outperform Sieve-Streaming in experiments.  
- Concern: influence maximization objective used (f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z|) differs from classical spread model, raising validity questions.  
- Unclear how STAR-T performs on the true diffusion/spread model from prior work.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and claims supported, but experiments could be broader.  
- Clarity assessment: paper is well-written overall but some proofs (e.g., Theorem 4.1) are hard to follow.  
- Originality: novel and significant contribution to streaming robust submodular maximization.  
- Significance: potential high impact for ML and data summarization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, constant approximation guarantee, some positive real-world experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited/weak experiments, objective mismatch for influence task, and difficult-to-follow proofs.
