- Problem studied: Robust submodular maximization in a streaming setting—select T so that after deleting any m elements, a size-k subset S from the remainder still has high submodular value.  
- Goal: Ensure resilience to deletions while operating under streaming constraints.  
- Applications: Motivated by scenarios needing post-hoc removals (e.g., recommending movies and later removing disliked options), but practical motivations feel weak.  
- Main result: A constant-factor approximation algorithm returning T of size O((m log k + k) log^2 k).  
- Technical assessment: Results and analysis appear correct and non-trivial, reflecting significant technical effort.  
- Primary concern: Relevance to NIPS is uncertain due to limited motivating applications and lack of broad generality in the problem setting.
