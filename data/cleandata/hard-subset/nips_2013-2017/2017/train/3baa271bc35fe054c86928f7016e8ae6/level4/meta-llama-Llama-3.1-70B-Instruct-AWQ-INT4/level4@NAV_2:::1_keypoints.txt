- Problem: streaming maximization of a monotone submodular function under a cardinality constraint with robustness to removal of up to m elements; relevant to summarization, influence maximization, recommendations.  
- Proposed method: two-stage approach — STAR-T streaming algorithm to build a robust summary and STAR-T-GREEDY query algorithm to produce a solution.  
- Theoretical guarantee: STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation for the robust problem using memory O((k + m log k) log k).  
- Technical quality: algorithm and analysis are non-trivial and technically sound.  
- Presentation quality: paper is well-written, clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient background.  
- Originality: introduces a novel partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule enabling the constant-factor guarantee.  
- Empirical claim: reported to match or outperform existing greedy and streaming methods on several data summarization tasks.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging submodular optimization problem with applications across multiple domains.  
- Main concern: motivation and relevance to the conference are weak; applications and practical motivation for robustness need strengthening.  
- Requested improvement: more discussion on practical implications and real-world scenarios where robustness arises.  
- Requested improvement: more comparisons with existing algorithms and more detailed performance analysis across scenarios.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a challenging problem, technically sound, and has applicable use cases.  
- Arguments against acceptance: motivation/applications could be stronger, problem scope may be narrow, and comparative/empirical analysis is insufficient.
