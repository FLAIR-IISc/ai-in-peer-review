- Problem and setting: Studies monotone submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint in a two-stage robust streaming model where up to m items may be removed after a single-pass summary is retained.  
- Algorithm and idea: Proposes STAR-T, which uses partitioned buckets of exponentially increasing size and exponentially decreasing thresholds, followed by GREEDY on the summarized set after deletions.  
- Guarantees: With an OPT estimate, retains O((k + m log k) log k) items and achieves a constant-factor approximation (~0.149 as k grows) robust to any m removals.  
- Unknown OPT: A multi-instance SIEVE-STREAMING style variant yields a (1+ε)-approximate threshold estimate at an extra log factor in memory.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends streaming thresholding (e.g., SIEVE-STREAMING) to robust two-stage setting and builds on bucketed robust methods (Orlin–Schulz–Udwani, PRO) while differing in streaming fill rules.  
- Comparison to concurrent work: Trades a smaller approximation constant for much better dependence on k (logarithmic vs linear) and overall memory O((k + m log k) log k) vs O(mk log k/ε).  
- Theory quality: Analysis is nontrivial and appears sound with outlined case analysis, but there are clarity issues in notation that affect interpretation.  
- Notation/clarity issues: Ambiguity between τ/2^i and τ/min{2^i,k} in the pseudocode, and unclear meaning of "log2 k" (base-2 log vs (log k)^2).  
- Experimental results: Encouraging results on dominating set (ego-Twitter, Amazon) and a movie recommendation dataset; STAR-T-GREEDY matches or outperforms baselines that know deletions.  
- Influence-maximization caveat: The “influence maximization” experiments use a dominating-set proxy (neighborhood size) instead of standard diffusion models, weakening influence-maximization claims.  
- Empirical reporting gaps: Recommend reporting wall-clock time, number of function evaluations, and actual memory used vs theoretical bounds for stronger empirical validation.  
- Reproducibility concern: Need details on how τ (or τ-grid and ε) and parameters w, ε are chosen in practice and sensitivity plots for w and m.  
- Presentation recommendations: State per-partition threshold clearly, summarize the main analytic invariant early, unify log notation, and define all symbols on first use.  
- Originality: Likely the first to achieve constant-factor robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming with memory polylogarithmic in k; partitioned exponential thresholding is novel in this context.  
- Significance: Problem is important; theoretical guarantees and small memory footprint are meaningful, and empirical results suggest practicality, though broader tasks and realistic diffusion models would increase impact.  
- Pros: Addresses a timely problem, provides one-pass algorithm with explicit small memory bound, constant-factor robustness, reusable summary, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Cons: Modest approximation constant (~0.149), simplified influence objective and limited task diversity, presentation inconsistencies, and missing experimental detail on τ, runtime, and sensitivity.  
- Suggested fixes: Reconcile and justify threshold rule in proofs, add a high-level analytic roadmap, clarify log/memory notation, provide runtime/memory/evaluation stats, and include sensitivity analyses or diffusion-model experiments.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance contingent on addressing presentation issues and clarifying the experimental setup.
