- Paper studies streaming + robust submodular maximization: selecting k high-value items from a small retained subset with up to m removals after the stream.

- Authors claim this is the first work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.

- They propose STAR-T, which retains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149 approximation while tolerating m removals.

- STAR-T combines partitioning and thresholding with bucket-based selection, similar to prior robust submodular work but with a different bucket-filling strategy.

- Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show STAR-T performs well, even versus streaming methods that know the removed elements.

- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is a notable theoretical contribution.

- Paper lacks complementary non-trivial lower bounds to contrast the provided upper bound.

- The analysis is under-explained: the claimed “innovative analysis” is not sufficiently detailed, making the proof sketch hard to follow.

- Influence maximization experiments are unconvincing: they use a simplified neighborhood-size objective and do not clarify how full-network influence objectives would work in a streaming setting.
