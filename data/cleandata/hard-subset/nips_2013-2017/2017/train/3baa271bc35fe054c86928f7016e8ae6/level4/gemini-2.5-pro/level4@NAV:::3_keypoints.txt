- Addresses streaming + robust submodular maximization: select k items from a small buffer in a stream while tolerating up to m deletions post-stream.
- Novelty claim: first paper to jointly consider streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.
- Algorithm: STAR-T stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and gives a constant 0.149 approximation while tolerating m deletions.
- Technique: uses partitioning, thresholding, and a bucket-based approach, with a novel bucket-filling method distinct from prior robust work.
- Empirical results: performs well on influence maximization and movie recommendation tasks, even versus streaming baselines with oracle knowledge of deletions.
- Strong theoretical contribution: obtaining any constant-factor approximation in this combined setting is nontrivial.
- Missing theoretical context: paper would be stronger with non-trivial lower bounds to place the upper bound in context.
- Presentation issues: authors claim the analysis is "innovative" but do not clearly explain or make the main ideas and proof sketch easy to follow.
- Experimental weakness: influence maximization experiment uses a highly simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than traditional network-dependent influence functions.
- Practical concern: unclear how standard influence maximization objectives, which depend on whole-network diffusion, can be handled in a streaming setting.
