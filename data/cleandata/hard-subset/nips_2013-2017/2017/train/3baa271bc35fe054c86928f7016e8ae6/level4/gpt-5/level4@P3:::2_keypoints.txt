- Studies robust submodular maximization in one-pass streaming with deletions: build summary S so greedy on S\E yields a near-optimal k-set.
- Proposes STAR-T: organizes elements into exponentially sized partitions/buckets with exponentially decreasing thresholds; query algorithm STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on S\E.
- Theorem 4.1: with τ ≈ OPT, STAR-T stores O((k + m log k) log k) elements and STAR-T-GREEDY achieves approximation c → 0.149 as k → ∞.
- Unknown OPT handled by parallel τ-guessing (à la SIEVE-STREAMING) adds a log factor, giving O((k + m log k) log^2 k) memory and (1+ε) loss.
- Builds on robust centralized partitioning methods and submodular streaming; contrasts concurrent work achieving 1/2−ε but with memory linear in k.
- Novel algorithmic design: exponential buckets + thresholds is elegant and well-motivated by analysis.
- Provable one-pass robustness is nontrivial and practically useful for deletion-robust summaries.
- Memory–approximation tradeoff favorable: milder dependence on k (logarithmic) compared to concurrent methods.
- Practical evaluation on dominating set and movie recommendation shows competitive performance against strong baselines.
- Experimental scope limited: STAR-T-GREEDY not consistently better than SIEVE-STREAMING; lacks systematic exploration across m, k, and memory.
- Experiments omit reporting summary sizes and runtimes, hindering assessment of practical costs.
- Terminology issue: calling the graph task “influence maximization” is misleading—dominating set is coverage, not IC/LT diffusion.
- Inconsistency in threshold rule between text (τ/2^i) and pseudocode (τ/min{2^i,k}) needs reconciliation.
- Theoretical condition w ≥ ⌈4 m log k / k⌉ conflicts with experiments using w=1 and removals beyond the guaranteed regime; report and analyze w in experiments.
- Practicality concern: online marginal gain evaluations may be expensive for some objectives (e.g., IC/LT); discuss evaluation complexity or approximations.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and the two-case lemmas lead cleanly to the constant approximation; assumptions and bounds are explicit.
- Recommend adding more baselines (e.g., centralized PRO when feasible), ablations (effect of w and τ guesses), and runtime/memory reporting.
- Clarity is generally good but needs fixes for threshold rule, consistent asymptotic statements, and practical guidance on setting w.
- Originality: partitioned thresholding for robust streaming appears novel and distinct from prior streaming and robust algorithms.
- Significance: method addresses important practical needs for deletion-robust streaming summaries in large-scale settings.
- Pros for acceptance: clear novel contribution, solid theory, simple deployable algorithm, and competitive empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: experiments need broadening and alignment with theory, terminology confusion, and minor presentation inconsistencies.
- Suggested improvements: standardize threshold schedule, report S size and runtime, study sensitivity to w and τ, avoid/mend influence-max terminology, and include ablations across m within/outside guarantees.
