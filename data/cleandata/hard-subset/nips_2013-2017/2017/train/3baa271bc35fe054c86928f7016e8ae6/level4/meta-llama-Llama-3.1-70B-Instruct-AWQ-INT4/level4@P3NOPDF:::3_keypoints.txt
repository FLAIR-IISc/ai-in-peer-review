- Paper studies streaming + robust submodular maximization: select k items from a small retained subset with up to m removals post-stream.  
- Claims novelty as the first work to jointly address streaming and robustness for submodular maximization.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm that stores O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant 0.149 approximation under m deletions.  
- Algorithm uses partitioning and thresholding with buckets; bucket filling differs from prior robust-submodular work.  
- Empirical results on influence maximization and movie recommendation show strong performance, even versus streaming baselines that know which items will be removed.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this combined streaming+robust setting is a significant contribution.  
- Paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the provided upper bound.  
- The analysis is poorly written: the claimed "innovative analysis" is not clearly explained and main proof ideas are hard to extract.  
- Influence maximization experiments are unconvincing because they use a simplified neighborhood-size objective rather than full-network influence models, and streaming adaptation is unclear.  
- Overall: interesting and notable result, but needs stronger theoretical lower bounds and clearer analysis and experiments to increase impact.
