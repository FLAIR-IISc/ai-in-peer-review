This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, the authors introduce a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing-threshold rule. In the second stage, they apply a simple greedy method. The paper proves that STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation even as k grows arbitrarily large (Thm. 4.1). Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show that STAR-T combined with the simple greedy method can handle the loss (removal) of elements. Empirically, the approach performs well on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation.

The work is interesting and appears to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have a wide range of applications, and the paper is enlightening in this regard. STAR-T has two hyperparameters, τ and w. The authors show how to approximate τ (Thm. 4.5), and the experiments indicate that setting w=1 works well, making the method convenient to use without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My main concern is that the experimental evaluation is not very expressive. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St baseline. 

Additionally, it is unusual that the influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219). This differs from the expected spread objective used in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, “Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network,” SIGKDD 2003). It is therefore unclear how STAR-T performs under the standard influence spread models.
