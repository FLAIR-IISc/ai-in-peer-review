This paper proposes a two-step process for robust submodular maximization with cardinality constraint k. In the first step, a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, is designed, which is based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing threshold rule. In the second step, a simple greedy method is used. It is proven that when k is infinite, STAR-T has a continuous approximation guarantee (Thm 4.1). lem4.2~4.3 proves that STAR-T can handle the loss of elements with a simple greedy method.

Experimental results show that the proposed algorithm can work well on the problem of influence maximization and personalized film recommendation.

This work is interesting because it is the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. It will probably have a wide range of applications in the streaming setting, and this work is very informative. The STAR-T algorithm has two hyperparameters, \tao and w. In Thm 4.5, the authors showed how to estimate \tao. The authors showed that "w=1" can perform well in experiments. It is convenient to use STAR-T without trying different hyperparameters.

The main problem I am concerned about is that the experiment is not very expressive. The STAR-T-Greedy algorithm may not always outperform the Sieve-St algorithm.

Furthermore, it is strange that the objective function of influence maximization is f(Z)=|N(Z) \cup Z| (line 219 in this paper), which is not the same as the function in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and E. Tardos, "Maximizing the Spread of Influence Through a Social Network," Int. Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is not clear to me how STAR-T performs on such a spread model.
