- Paper studies streaming submodular maximization with post-stream robustness (select k from a small retained set even after m removals).  
- Claims novelty as the first work jointly addressing streaming and robustness.  
- Proposes STAR-T algorithm maintaining O((m log k + k) log² k) elements and guaranteeing a 0.149 constant-factor approximation.  
- STAR-T is robust to removal of m elements and uses partitioning/thresholding with bucket-filling adapted from prior robust submodular methods.  
- Experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show strong empirical performance, outperforming streaming baselines that know removed elements.  
- Achieving a constant-factor approximation in this joint setting is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Major suggested improvement: provide non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound.  
- Major suggested improvement: clarify the analysis and explicitly state what parts of the analysis are novel, as key proof steps are currently obscured.  
- Major suggested improvement: strengthen the influence maximization experiment—current objective (neighborhood size of seeds) is oversimplified and it’s unclear how standard influence objectives fit the streaming model.
