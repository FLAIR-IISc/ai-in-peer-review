- Problem: maximization of a monotone submodular function under cardinality k in a single-pass streaming setting robust to adversarial removal of m elements.
- Method: two-stage approach—STAR-T constructs a compact streaming summary, then STAR-T-GREEDY runs greedy on the summary after removals.
- STAR-T design: uses a partitioned structure with exponentially sized buckets and an exponentially decreasing thresholding rule.
- Theoretical result: first constant-factor approximation guarantee for this robust streaming problem with summary size O((k + m log k) log k).
- Novelty/technical quality: non-trivial synthesis of partitioning and multi-level thresholding, adapting streaming techniques to robustness.
- Relation to prior work: builds on SIEVE-STREAMING and centralized partitioning [11], and improves memory complexity (logarithmic in k) over concurrent work [18].
- Significance: relevant for large-scale ML where single-pass summaries and quick adaptation to removals are practical and memory-efficient.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, algorithms precisely described, and proof sketch and experiments are clearly presented.
- Motivation weakness: needs stronger justification why a pre-computed summary is required versus re-streaming or querying original data subsets in applications.
- Experimental weakness: lacks comparison to concurrent work [18] and to running non-streaming PRO [11] on a sampled subset; more comprehensive baselines would help.
- Approximation constant: achieved constant (~0.149) is modest and the paper should discuss whether this is a proof artifact or an inherent algorithmic limit.
