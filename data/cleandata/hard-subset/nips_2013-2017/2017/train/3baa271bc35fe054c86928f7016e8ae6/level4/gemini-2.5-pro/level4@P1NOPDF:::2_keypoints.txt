- Proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T streaming algorithm followed by a greedy stage.  
- STAR-T is based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule.  
- Theorem 4.1: STAR-T achieves a constant-factor approximation in the limit as k → ∞.  
- Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3: the full two-stage procedure is robust to loss of elements from the solution set.  
- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation shows strong performance.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, opening a valuable research direction.  
- Practical strength: handles hyperparameters well—provides a method to approximate τ and finds w=1 works empirically.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is not entirely convincing; STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform the Sieve-Stream baseline.  
- Concern about evaluation choice: influence objective uses f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (neighborhood coverage) rather than standard IC/LT spread models, so applicability to standard influence maximization is unclear.
