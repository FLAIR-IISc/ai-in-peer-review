Problem studied: The paper tackles the following task. It seeks to construct, in a streaming setting, a set T drawn from a ground set such that, after removing any subset of m elements from T, one can still extract a subset S of size k with f(S) maximized, where f is a submodular function.

Applications: Submodular maximization underlies many applications, and streaming algorithms are known for the non-robust case. A robust variant is valuable when a computed solution may later require discarding some items (e.g., assembling a set of recommended movies only to learn the user is uninterested in some of them).

Results: The paper provides a constant-factor approximation for this robust streaming problem while outputting a summary T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.

Comments: To the best of my understanding, the results are correct. The approach is non-trivial, and both the algorithm and its analysis are technically demanding. While the problem is interesting, my primary reservation concerns the fit for the conference: the application scenarios and their motivation feel relatively weak, and the problem formulation does not seem broad enough.
