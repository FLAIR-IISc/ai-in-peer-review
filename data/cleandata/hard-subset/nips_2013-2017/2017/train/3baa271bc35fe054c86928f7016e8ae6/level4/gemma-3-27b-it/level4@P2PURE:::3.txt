This paper addresses the challenging problem of maximizing a submodular function in a streaming setting while ensuring robustness to element removals. Existing work has explored streaming submodular maximization, motivated by large-scale applications with limited data access, and robust submodular maximization, relevant when elements may be lost after the initial selection. This paper makes a significant contribution by being the first to jointly consider both streaming and robustness.

The authors introduce the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k)log^2 k) elements and achieves a constant-factor (0.149) approximation guarantee, even with the potential removal of *m* elements. This algorithm leverages partitioning and thresholding techniques, employing buckets to manage the selection process. While bucket-based approaches have appeared in previous work on robust submodular optimization, this work distinguishes itself through a novel bucket-filling strategy. The efficacy of STAR-T is demonstrated through experiments in influence maximization and movie recommendation, where it outperforms streaming algorithms with prior knowledge of element removals.

Achieving a constant-factor approximation for this combined problem is a strong result. However, the paper could benefit from several improvements. The inclusion of non-trivial lower bounds would strengthen the theoretical grounding. The analysis, while technically sound, could be presented more clearly; the authors claim an “innovative analysis” but do not adequately highlight its novel aspects, making it difficult to follow the key ideas and steps within the proof sketch. Furthermore, the application to influence maximization could be made more compelling. The experimental evaluation relies on a simplified objective function focusing solely on the neighborhood size of seed nodes. It is unclear how more standard influence maximization objectives – which consider the value of the function across the entire network for a given seed set – could be adapted to a streaming context.
