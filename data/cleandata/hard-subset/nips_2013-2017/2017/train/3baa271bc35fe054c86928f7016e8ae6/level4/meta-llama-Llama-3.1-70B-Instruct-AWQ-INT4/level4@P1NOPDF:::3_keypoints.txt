- Problem: first work to study joint streaming and robust submodular maximization (identify k items from a stream while robust to removal of up to m items).  
- Significance: setting is challenging and obtaining a constant-factor approximation is an important result.  
- Algorithmic result: STAR-T retains O((m log k + k) log^2 k) elements and achieves a 0.149 approximation while tolerating m removals.  
- Techniques: combines partitioning and thresholding with a bucket-based approach; bucket population differs from prior robust submodular work.  
- Empirical performance: experiments on influence maximization and movie recommendation show strong performance, even versus streaming methods that know which items will be removed.  
- Missing theory: paper lacks non-trivial lower bounds to complement the presented upper bound.  
- Presentation/analysis: the claimed “innovative analysis” is not clearly explained; proof sketch fails to highlight main ideas and steps.  
- Experiment realism: influence maximization tests use a highly simplified neighborhood-size objective, making applicability to standard network-based IM unclear.  
- Applicability concern: unclear how traditional influence-maximization objectives that depend on the whole network can be accommodated in the streaming setting.
