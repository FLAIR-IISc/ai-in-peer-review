- Tackles submodular maximization under both streaming constraints and adversarial robustness (select k from a stream resilient to up to m removals), reportedly the first to jointly consider both.

- Proposes the STAR-T algorithm with a constant-factor approximation of 0.149 and memory O((m log k + k) log^2 k).

- Algorithmic approach combines partitioning and thresholding via a bucket-based scheme, with a novel bucket-population method adapted to streaming.

- Empirical evaluation on influence maximization and movie recommendation shows STAR-T performs competitively, even versus non-robust streaming algorithms given an oracle of removed items.

- Main strength: achieving a constant-factor approximation under the challenging combined single-pass and robustness constraints is a significant theoretical contribution.

- Lacks non-trivial lower bounds; providing them would contextualize STAR-T’s upper bound and clarify problem difficulty.

- Theoretical exposition is unclear: the “innovative analysis” is not contrasted with prior work, and the proof sketch is dense and hard to follow.

- Influence-maximization experiments are unconvincing because they use a simplified proxy (immediate neighborhood size) rather than standard, simulation-based influence metrics.

- Paper should either address how to compute/approximate standard influence objectives in the streaming model or focus experiments on objectives naturally amenable to single-pass computation.
