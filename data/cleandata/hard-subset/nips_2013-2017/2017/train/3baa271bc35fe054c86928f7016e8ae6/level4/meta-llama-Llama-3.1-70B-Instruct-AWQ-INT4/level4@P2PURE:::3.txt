This paper explores the problem of submodular maximization in a streaming and robust setting, where the objective is to identify k high-value elements from a small subset of retained elements in the stream, with the possibility of m items being removed after the stream. The study of streaming submodular maximization has been extensive due to its large-scale applications, where only a limited number of passes over the data is feasible. Recently, robustness has gained attention, motivated by scenarios where elements may be removed, and the goal is to obtain solutions resilient to such removals. This work is the first to jointly address the problem of streaming and robust submodular maximization.

The authors propose the STAR-T algorithm, which maintains O((m log k + k )log^2 k) elements after the stream and achieves a constant (0.149) approximation guarantee while being robust to the removal of m elements. This algorithm combines partitioning and thresholding techniques for submodular optimization using buckets, building upon similar ideas employed in previous work for robust submodular optimization, but with a distinct approach to filling the buckets. Experimental results demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in influence maximization and movie recommendation applications, even when compared to streaming algorithms with prior knowledge of removed elements.

The problem setting, incorporating both streaming and robustness aspects, is challenging, and achieving a constant factor approximation is a notable result. However, several aspects of the paper can be improved. The inclusion of non-trivial lower bounds to complement the upper bound would have been beneficial. The analysis writing could be enhanced, as the authors mention an "innovative analysis" without elaborating on its innovative aspects, making it difficult to extract the main ideas and steps from the proof sketch. Furthermore, the influence maximization application could be more convincing. The experiments employ a simplified objective for influence maximization, focusing solely on the size of the neighborhood of seed nodes. It is unclear how traditional influence maximization objectives, which depend on the entire network for any set of seed nodes, could be adapted to a streaming setting.
