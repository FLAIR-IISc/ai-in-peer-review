- Proposes a two-stage method for robust submodular maximization under cardinality k: STAR-T (robust streaming with partitioning and decreasing thresholds) followed by simple greedy.
- Theoretical guarantee: STAR-T achieves a constant approximation as k → ∞ (Theorem 4.1).
- Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show STAR-T combined with greedy handles element loss effectively.
- Experimental results claim strong performance on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.
- Novelty: first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, with broad applicability of the streaming setting.
- STAR-T has two hyperparameters (τ and w); Theorem 4.5 gives a way to approximate τ, and experiments indicate w = 1 works well, reducing tuning burden.
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is not fully convincing—STAR-T-Greedy does not consistently outperform Sieve-Streaming.
- Methodological concern: the influence maximization objective used (f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z|) deviates from the standard Kempe et al. spread model, making it unclear how STAR-T performs on the canonical objective.
