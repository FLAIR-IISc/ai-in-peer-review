- Problem: Streaming robust submodular maximization—build T from ground set E so that after deleting any m elements from T one can still extract a k-element set S with high f(S) (f submodular).
- Applications: Submodular maximization has many uses; robust streaming variant is useful when solutions may later lose elements (e.g., recommending movies then learning some are unwanted).
- Results: Presents a constant-factor approximation returning T of size (m log k + k) log^2 k.
- Correctness/technical merit: Results appear correct; the algorithm and analysis are nontrivial and technically challenging.
- Concern: Relevance to the conference is questionable—motivation and applications feel weak and the problem may not be sufficiently general.
