This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint of k. The approach consists of a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, which utilizes a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule, followed by the simple greedy method. The authors establish that STAR-T achieves a constant approximation guarantee as k approaches infinity (Theorem 4.1). Additionally, Lemmas 4.2 and 4.3 demonstrate that the combination of STAR-T with the simple greedy method can effectively handle the loss of elements.

The experimental results indicate that the proposed algorithm performs well on the problems of influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation. This work is notable as it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization, which may have a wide range of applications and provides valuable insights. The STAR-T algorithm involves two hyperparameters, τ and w, for which the authors provide an approximation method for τ in Theorem 4.5. Furthermore, the experimental results show that setting w=1 yields satisfactory performance, making it convenient to use the STAR-T algorithm without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

However, a major concern is that the experimental evaluation is not comprehensive. Specifically, the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. Moreover, the objective function used for influence maximization, f(Z)=|N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), differs from the function presented in the referenced article (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network," in Int. Conf. on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (SIGKDD), 2003). It is unclear how the STAR-T algorithm would perform on the spread model, and further investigation is needed to address this discrepancy.
