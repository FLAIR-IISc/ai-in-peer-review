This paper proposes a two-stage procedure for robust submodular maximization under a cardinality constraint k. In the first stage, the authors design a robust streaming algorithm, STAR-T, based on a partitioning structure and a decreasing thresholding rule. In the second stage, they apply a simple greedy method. STAR-T is proven to achieve a constant-factor approximation guarantee for arbitrary k (Theorem 4.1). Lemmas 4.2–4.3 show that STAR-T combined with the simple greedy method can handle the loss of elements. Experimental results indicate that the proposed algorithm performs well on influence maximization and personalized movie recommendation tasks.

This work is interesting as, to my knowledge, it presents the first streaming algorithm for robust submodular maximization. The streaming setting may have a wide range of applications, and the paper is illuminating in this regard. STAR-T has two hyperparameters, τ and w. In Theorem 4.5, the authors show how to approximate τ. The experiments suggest that setting w = 1 works well, making it convenient to use STAR-T without extensive hyperparameter tuning.

My main concern is that the experimental evaluation is not very expressive: the STAR-T-Greedy algorithm does not consistently outperform the Sieve-St algorithm. In addition, it is unusual that the influence maximization objective is defined as f(Z) = |N(Z) ∪ Z| (line 219), which differs from the objective in the cited work (D. Kempe, J. Kleinberg, and É. Tardos, “Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network,” KDD 2003). It is unclear how STAR-T performs under that standard spread model.
