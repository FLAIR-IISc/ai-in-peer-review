This paper proposes a novel approach to identifying the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression. The authors rigorously define the intrinsic group additive structure and develop a structure-penalized kernel method for simultaneous identification of the intrinsic group additive structure and nonparametric function estimation.

**Summary of the main ideas**

The paper starts by discussing the limitations of the traditional additive model for nonparametric regression, which neglects possible interactions between predictor variables. The authors then introduce the concept of group additive structure, which allows for interactions between variables within groups. They define the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group additive structure among all correct group additive structures.

The authors then propose a kernel method with a complexity penalty term to identify the intrinsic group additive structure. The complexity penalty term is based on the covering number of the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) corresponding to the group additive structure. They show that the proposed method is consistent in identifying the intrinsic group additive structure.

**Strengths**

1. **Rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structure**: The authors provide a rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure, which is a key contribution of the paper.
2. **Novel complexity penalty term**: The authors propose a novel complexity penalty term based on the covering number of the RKHS, which is a new and interesting idea.
3. **Consistency result**: The authors provide a consistency result for the proposed method, which is an important theoretical contribution.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational cost**: The authors mention that the exhaustive search algorithm has high computational cost, especially for large models. This is a limitation of the proposed method.
2. **Sensitivity to tuning parameters**: The authors mention that the performance of the method is sensitive to the choice of tuning parameters, which can be a limitation in practice.
3. **Limited empirical evaluation**: The authors only evaluate the proposed method on a few simulated datasets and one real dataset, which is a limited empirical evaluation.

**Originality and significance**

The paper proposes a novel approach to identifying the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression, which is an original contribution. The paper also provides a consistency result for the proposed method, which is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Clarity and organization**

The paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed method, and the paper is easy to follow.
