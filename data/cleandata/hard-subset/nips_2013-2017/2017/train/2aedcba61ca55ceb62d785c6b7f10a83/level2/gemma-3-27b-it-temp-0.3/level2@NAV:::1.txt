## Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper addresses the important problem of identifying underlying group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, a common limitation of standard additive models. The authors propose a structure-penalized kernel method, introducing a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers of RKHSs. The core idea of balancing model fit with structural complexity via a penalty term is sound and builds upon existing work in additive modeling and kernel methods. The paper clearly articulates the motivation, defines the intrinsic group additive structure rigorously, and provides theoretical guarantees for consistency.

The strengths of the paper lie in its theoretical contributions. The derivation of the complexity measure and the subsequent consistency proof are significant. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing approaches (like functional ANOVA) that can suffer from exponential growth in the number of parameters and offer a compelling middle ground between fully additive and fully interactive models. The inclusion of both an exhaustive search and a stepwise algorithm for identifying the structure demonstrates practical consideration. The simulation studies provide initial evidence of the method’s effectiveness, and the real data application offers a glimpse into its potential utility.

However, the paper also has weaknesses. The theoretical analysis, while rigorous, relies on assumptions about kernel properties and may not translate directly to all practical scenarios. The simulation studies, while demonstrating the method's ability to *recover* the true structure when known, lack a more comprehensive comparison against existing state-of-the-art methods for variable selection and interaction detection in nonparametric regression. The real data application is somewhat limited in scope; a more detailed analysis of the chosen structure and its implications would be beneficial. The discussion of computational cost, particularly for the exhaustive search, is brief and could be expanded. The connection to graphical models, while mentioned, feels underdeveloped and could be explored further.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem.
*   Rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure.
*   Consistency guarantees for the proposed method.
*   Potential for practical applications in high-dimensional data analysis.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited empirical comparison to existing methods.
*   Real data application could be more in-depth.
*   Computational considerations not fully addressed.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of nonparametric regression. While some aspects could be strengthened through more extensive experimentation and analysis, the theoretical novelty and potential impact warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough empirical evaluation and a more detailed discussion of computational complexity. The authors should also consider expanding the discussion of the connection to graphical models.
