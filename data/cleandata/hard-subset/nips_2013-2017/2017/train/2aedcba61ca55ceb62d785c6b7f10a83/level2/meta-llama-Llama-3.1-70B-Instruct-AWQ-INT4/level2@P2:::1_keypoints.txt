- Proposes a novel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression.  
- Provides a rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structure and a structure-penalized kernel method for simultaneous structure identification and function estimation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of method and theoretical foundation.  
- Uses RKHS and covering number as complexity measure, with thorough theoretical discussion.  
- Simulation study and real-data application demonstrate effectiveness in structure identification and function estimation.  
- Results are promising and implications are well discussed.  
- Minor issue: lacks details on computational complexity, especially for large datasets.  
- Minor issue: does not discuss robustness to outliers and noisy data.  
- Minor issue: insufficient comparisons with existing methods (e.g., functional ANOVA, graphical models).
