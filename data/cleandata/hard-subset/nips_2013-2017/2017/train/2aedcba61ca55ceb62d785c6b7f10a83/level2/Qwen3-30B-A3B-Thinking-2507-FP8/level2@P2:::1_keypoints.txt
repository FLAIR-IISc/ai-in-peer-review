- Addresses identification of intrinsic group additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, resolving non-uniqueness in prior models.
- Introduces the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the minimal group structure representing the target function (Theorem 1).
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method that jointly identifies structure and estimates nonparametric components.
- Develops a novel complexity penalty based on RKHS covering numbers, enabling tractable structure selection (Section 2.3.2).
- Proves theoretical consistency for the proposed approach (Theorem 4).
- Provides two practical algorithms for structure identification: exhaustive search and backward stepwise (Algorithms 1 and 2).
- Situates the work relative to functional ANOVA and graphical models, clearly differentiating the approach.
- Empirical validation via simulations and a real-data application demonstrates effectiveness in recovering true group structures.
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear definitions, propositions, and rigorous proofs (Definitions 1-2, Propositions 1-3).
- The complexity measure C(G)=∑j α|uj|+1 is well-motivated and implementable, filling a literature gap.
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison with alternative structure-learning methods for nonparametric regression (e.g., methods in [6], [10]).
- Weakness: scalability to very high-dimensional settings (p >> n) is not adequately addressed theoretically or empirically.
- Weakness: tuning parameter α is not well-justified or guided; selection procedure and its impact need clarification.
- Weakness: authors do not investigate causes or remedies for performance deterioration seen in Model 1 under validation-based selection.
- Recommendation: acceptance — paper is original, well-written, theoretically sound, and empirically convincing, with suggested improvements on comparisons, scalability, and tuning guidance.
