- Core contribution: rigorously defines "intrinsic group additive structure" and proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to identify structure and estimate functions simultaneously.
- Novel complexity measure: introduces a new complexity metric for group additive structures used in the method.
- Theoretical guarantees: proves consistency for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure.
- Builds on prior work: situates group additive models as a middle ground between simple additive models and full interaction models.
- Motivation: convincingly argues the need to identify optimal variable grouping to balance flexibility and complexity.
- Strength of theory: rigorous definitions and derivations are a major strength, with detailed proofs in the supplement.
- Algorithmic description lacking: practical implementation details for the exhaustive and stepwise search algorithms are brief.
- Computational concerns: computational complexity and scalability are not thoroughly discussed.
- Simulations insufficient: simulation studies show promise but lack breadth, especially across noise levels and higher dimensionality.
- Kernel choice unexplained: the rationale for selecting specific kernel functions is not well justified.
- Real-data evaluation limited: the single plausible example is not comprehensive enough to demonstrate general applicability.
- Recommendation and fixes: recommends acceptance with minor revisions, asking for expanded implementation details, more comprehensive simulations, and deeper real-data analysis.
