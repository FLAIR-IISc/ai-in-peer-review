- Tackles identification of group additive structure in nonparametric regression, generalizing additive models to allow within-group interactions.  
- Addresses non-uniqueness of group structures and lack of systematic identification methods.  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to recover intrinsic group additive structure.  
- Derives a novel complexity measure for group structures based on RKHS covering numbers.  
- Proves consistency for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure under appropriate conditions.  
- Builds on and connects to relevant prior work (e.g., [11], [4], [6]) and graphical model literature ([2], [7], [9]).  
- Presents comprehensive evaluation: simulations across models, real-data application, exhaustive search and backward stepwise algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, making technical details accessible.  
- Computational cost is a concern; scalability and efficiency of the backward stepwise algorithm are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Method requires tuning multiple parameters (λ, µ, α); parameter selection strategies and links to theory are under-discussed.  
- Performance is sensitive to kernel choice; guidance and exploration of different kernels are limited.  
- Real-data analysis is limited in scope and lacks comparisons to other relevant methods (e.g., graphical models).  
- The intuition behind the covering-number–based complexity measure could be better explained.  
- Backward stepwise algorithm performs poorly on a specific simulation (Model 4); causes and remedies are not analyzed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound contribution with promising empirical results; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with theoretical guarantees and comprehensive experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential scalability issues, challenging parameter tuning, limited kernel and real-data exploration.  
- Recommendation: provide complexity/efficiency analysis for stepwise algorithm and explore scalability improvements.  
- Recommendation: elaborate parameter selection strategies and sensitivity analyses.  
- Recommendation: investigate and report effects of different kernel choices.  
- Recommendation: test on more complex real datasets and compare with alternative methods.  
- Recommendation: give more intuition for the complexity measure and analyze why stepwise fails on Model 4.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (7/10).
