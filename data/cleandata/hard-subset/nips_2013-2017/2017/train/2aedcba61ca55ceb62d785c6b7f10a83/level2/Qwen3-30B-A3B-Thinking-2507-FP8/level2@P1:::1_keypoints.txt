- Proposes a novel method to identify group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
- Defines the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group representation of the true function, resolving non-uniqueness.
- Introduces a structure-penalized kernel estimator that jointly identifies the group structure and estimates nonparametric functions.
- Develops a novel complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers to penalize group structures.
- Proves consistency of intrinsic structure identification (Theorem 4) under the proposed complexity measure.
- Provides two practical algorithms: exhaustive search and backward stepwise.
- Empirically validates the method with simulations and a Boston Housing data application, showing successful structure recovery and interpretable results.
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and connects to related work (functional ANOVA, graphical models).
- Needs a more thorough comparison and explicit distinction from prior work (especially reference [11]) in the introduction.
- Lacks concrete computational complexity analysis for the exhaustive search and trade-off discussion between the two algorithms.
- Provides limited guidance on tuning parameter selection (µ, α) and why certain values work better across models.
- Real data results would benefit from deeper interpretation of identified groups in terms of actual variables.
- Strengths: clear problem definition, rigorous theory, well-motivated complexity measure, strong experiments, practical algorithms.
- Weaknesses: minor gaps in prior-work connection, tuning parameter discussion, computational-cost analysis, and real-data interpretation.
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical and practical contribution to high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
