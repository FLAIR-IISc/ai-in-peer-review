- Proposes a method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression, addressing limitations of traditional additive models.  
- Formally defines the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group partition representing the regression function, resolving nonidentifiability.  
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify group structure and estimate nonparametric functions.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure based on covering numbers of RKHSs to balance fit and structural complexity.  
- Provides theoretical consistency guarantees showing estimated group structure converges to the intrinsic structure as sample size grows.  
- Contributions: formal definition, practical kernel-based estimation method, and consistency theory.  
- Empirical evaluation includes simulations and real data (Boston Housing) demonstrating effectiveness in recovering true group structures.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with sound mathematical foundations and logical flow.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior literature, notably acknowledging and distinguishing from OKGT [11].  
- Theoretical contributions are strong; consistency proofs and RKHS-based complexity derivation are well-motivated.  
- Thoughtful discussion of how group additive structure differs from graphical model conditional dependence.  
- Stepwise algorithm offers a computationally feasible alternative to exhaustive search for larger problems.  
- Visualizations (e.g., Boston Housing estimated functions) enhance interpretability of results.  
- Main limitation: exhaustive search is computationally infeasible as predictor count increases; scalability is a concern.  
- Does not address extremely high-dimensional settings (p >> n) or compare to LASSO-style interaction detection methods.  
- Would benefit from deeper analysis of stepwise algorithm failure modes and computational complexity.  
- Lacks principled guidance on selecting α in the complexity measure; currently treated as a tunable hyperparameter.  
- Could strengthen impact by discussing extensions to other kernel-based or nonparametric frameworks beyond the specific kernels used.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically implementable, and a meaningful advancement in interpretable nonparametric interaction modeling.
