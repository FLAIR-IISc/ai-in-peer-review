- Proposes GASI, a novel method for nonparametric regression that jointly identifies group additive structure and estimates nonparametric functions.  
- Rigorously defines the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal amiable group structure, resolving prior non-uniqueness issues.  
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel estimator using a new complexity measure derived from RKHS covering numbers.  
- Proves consistency for the identified structure and provides clear theoretical statements (definitions, propositions, theorems).  
- The RKHS-based complexity measure is well-motivated and practically implementable.  
- Simulation studies largely demonstrate accurate recovery of true group structures across multiple settings.  
- Real-data application (Boston Housing) yields meaningful and interpretable results.  
- Clearly distinguishes the approach from graphical models and conditional dependence structures.  
- High technical quality and novelty, addressing the flexibility–interpretability trade-off in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Concern: computational scalability is under-addressed; backward stepwise (Algorithm 2) is greedy and may miss optimal structures.  
- Suggests more detailed discussion of computational complexity and potential algorithmic improvements.  
- Empirical performance shows some deterioration in Model 1 that needs deeper investigation despite theoretical consistency.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, well-written and technically sound; recommends acceptance (NIPS).
