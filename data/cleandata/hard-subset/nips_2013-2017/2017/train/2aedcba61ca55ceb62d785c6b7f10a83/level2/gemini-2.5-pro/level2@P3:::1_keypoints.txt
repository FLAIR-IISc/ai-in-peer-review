- Paper addresses identifying interaction structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression via group additive models.  
- Introduces the "intrinsic group additive structure" as a unique, minimal grouping resolving prior non-uniqueness.  
- Proposes GASI: a method to jointly estimate the regression function and identify the intrinsic grouping.  
- Formulates a structure-penalized optimization in RKHS to perform structure identification.  
- Derives a novel structural complexity penalty from covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Proves consistency of the proposed method for identifying the intrinsic group structure (Theorem 4).  
- Provides algorithms for both small-p (exhaustive) and large-p (greedy stepwise) settings.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data (including Boston Housing) support the method’s effectiveness and interpretability.  
- Technical quality is high; formalization using a partial order is elegant and theoretically solid.  
- The RKHS-derived structural penalty is principled and a major novel contribution.  
- Clarity and organization are good; the exposition from definition to method to penalty is logical.  
- Description of the backward stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2) is high-level and needs more detail on candidate moves.  
- Role and selection of regularization parameter λ (and interaction with µ and α) are under-discussed and should be elaborated.  
- Originality: first formal treatment of intrinsic structure and novel RKHS-based complexity measure; consistency proof is new and important.  
- Significance: important problem with useful, interpretable results; framework likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Main practical limitation is scalability: exhaustive search is infeasible for moderate p and greedy search can be suboptimal.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous solution, principled penalty, strong theoretical results, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability of search algorithms and insufficient implementation/hyperparameter details.  
- Essential suggestion: clarify the backward stepwise procedure and the candidate set of moves for each variable.  
- Recommended suggestion: expand discussion and sensitivity analysis of hyperparameter selection and interactions (λ, µ, α).  
- Optional suggestion: compare more deeply with two-stage CD-based approaches and discuss potential pitfalls of those methods.
