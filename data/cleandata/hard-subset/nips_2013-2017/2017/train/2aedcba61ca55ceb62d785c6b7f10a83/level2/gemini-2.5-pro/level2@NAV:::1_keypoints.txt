- Proposes GASI, a novel method to identify group additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, striking a balance between simple additive and full ANOVA models.  
- Formalizes the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the most parsimonious grouping explaining the data.  
- Introduces a structure-penalized kernel method that simultaneously estimates nonparametric functions and identifies group structure.  
- Major technical contribution: a principled complexity penalty for groupings derived from RKHS covering numbers, enabling rigorous comparison of group structures.  
- Theoretical support: provides a consistency proof for structure identification (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical validation: demonstrates practical utility on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-motivated, and logically organized.  
- Weakness — computational scalability: exhaustive search is infeasible for moderate p; backward stepwise is greedy, can fail, and advanced search strategies are not explored.  
- Weakness — tuning sensitivity: method depends on three tuning parameters (λ, µ, α), with limited practical guidance and an assumption that λ is given.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution with excellent theoretical grounding, but computational and tuning aspects need further development.
