- Problem & model: Proposes a group additive model f(X)=Σ f_j(X_{u_j}) to balance interpretability and interaction modeling in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

- Main contributions: (1) formal definition of the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the unique minimal grouping, (2) a structure-penalized kernel estimator to identify structure and estimate components, (3) a novel RKHS-based complexity penalty and a consistency proof.

- Theoretical novelty: Formalization of intrinsic structure (Definition 1, Theorem 1) clarifies what it means to recover the “correct” grouping.

- Penalty design & theory: The group complexity penalty C(G) is principled (based on RKHS covering numbers) and Theorem 4 proves consistency of structure identification.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes helpful examples for intuition.

- Computational feasibility: Exhaustive search over all partitions is infeasible for moderate p (Bell numbers), so practical use is limited to very small p.

- Greedy search concerns: Backward stepwise Algorithm 2 is a necessary heuristic but can get stuck in poor local optima (e.g., failed on Model 4); authors should explain failure modes and consider alternative search strategies.

- Algorithm 2 ambiguity: The algorithm description (step 4) is unclear—appears to mix splitting and moving operations—so the exact set of moves should be clarified.

- Experimental comparison gap: Experiments lack comparisons to competing methods (e.g., COSSO, SpAM, Random Forests, MARS), making practical advantages hard to assess.

- Tuning parameter sensitivity: Method needs tuning of λ, μ, and α; simultaneous grid search is costly and α’s theoretical justification vs. practical tuning is insufficiently discussed.

- Recommended revisions: Address computational limitations, clarify Algorithm 2, analyze sensitivity to tuning parameters (especially α), and add comparisons to alternative methods.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contributions and principled penalty outweigh practical weaknesses; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing the computational/experimental clarifications.
