- Summary: Introduces Group Additive Structures (GAS), defines intrinsic group G* via a partition partial order, and proposes a structure-penalized kernel estimator in a direct-sum RKHS with a covering-number-based complexity penalty and selection consistency results; uses stepwise heuristic for large p and shows synthetic and Boston Housing experiments.

- Relation to prior work: Positions GAS between additive models and functional ANOVA/interaction models, relates to OKGT by addressing identifiability/structure learning, and leverages RKHS capacity literature; notes but differs from graphical-model objectives.

- Theoretical strengths: Partial-order framework and direct-sum RKHS are sensible; covering-number penalty (Proposition 3) and deviation bounds support the consistency claims.

- Uniqueness concerns: Theorem 1 claiming uniqueness of intrinsic G* lacks clear additional assumptions; minimal partitions exist but uniqueness generally requires extra conditions (e.g., distributional non-degeneracy).

- Independence assumption ambiguity: Proposition 1 needs independence for strict L2 inclusions; it's unclear whether later results (uniqueness/consistency) implicitly rely on independence, so this must be stated.

- Penalty derivation gap: The move from a covering-number bound involving ε to the implemented penalty ∑u α|u| "absorbing" factors into μ elides dependence on ε and kernel constants; derivation and sensitivity of α must be detailed.

- Tuning & asymptotics missing: The paper lacks guidance on how α, μ, and λ are chosen/tuned, and how they should scale with n for the theoretical results to hold.

- Stepwise algorithm limitations: Consistency is proved for the global minimizer, but the used backward stepwise heuristic for large p has no guarantees and experiments show it can fail; this gap needs emphasis.

- Experimental shortcomings: No comparisons to strong baselines (GAM, SS-ANOVA/ESP kernels, SALSA, OKGT, lasso-based interaction selection), making empirical significance unclear.

- Predictive evaluation lacking: Real-data analysis focuses on interpretability without reporting predictive performance (CV MSE/RMSE) against baselines.

- CV/hyperparameter protocol unclear: It's not specified whether nested CV was used; reuse of validation could bias results and should be clarified or nested CV adopted.

- Scalability and runtime: Limited experiments in modest p and no systematic scaling/runtime studies; stepwise scalability story lacks theoretical or empirical guarantees.

- Clarity issues: Notation and roles of r, ε, α are mixed; assumptions for main theorems are not all stated; Proposition 3 needs a proof sketch; several references and minor slips need fixing.

- Originality: Formalizing intrinsic GAS and applying covering-number-based RKHS penalties to partition selection is novel and conceptually interesting.

- Significance conditional: GAS could be a useful middle ground between additive and full-interaction models, but impact depends on stronger empirical validation and clarified assumptions.

- Concrete theory suggestions: State assumptions for uniqueness, provide full derivation of C(G) including kernel-constant dependence, and give asymptotic scaling guidance for μ and λ.

- Algorithmic suggestions: Propose stochastic/beam-search variants with restarts, or surrogate/submodularity-based relaxations to obtain approximation guarantees for structure search.

- Experimental suggestions: Add baseline comparisons for structure recovery and prediction, perform p/n scaling and runtime studies, and run sensitivity/ablation analyses for μ and α.

- Pros for acceptance: Clear motivation, novel formalization of intrinsic group structure, principled capacity-based penalty, and selection consistency result.

- Cons for acceptance: Unclear uniqueness/identifiability assumptions, informal theoretical steps (penalty derivation), limited empirical validation, and heuristic search without guarantees.

- Overall recommendation: Promising and original work that needs stronger theoretical clarity (assumptions and derivations) and substantially expanded, baseline-driven empirical evaluation before acceptance.
