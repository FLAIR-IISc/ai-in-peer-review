- Proposes a novel structure-penalized kernel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression, with a complexity measure from RKHS covering numbers and empirical validation.
- Positions group additive models as a middle ground between additive models and functional ANOVA, and distinguishes its focus from graphical models by directly targeting group additive structure.
- Technically rigorous: clear problem formulation and well-supported theoretical results, including consistency proofs in the supplement.
- Empirically effective: simulation studies and real-data applications demonstrate the method’s performance.
- Well-written and organized with clear notation, derivations, algorithm descriptions, and discussion of computational complexity.
- Original contributions include the structure-penalized kernel approach and a novel RKHS-based complexity measure, plus a new viewpoint connecting group additive and graphical models.
- Significant potential to improve efficiency and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression across multiple application areas.
- Strengths: novel method, rigorous theory, consistency guarantees, and empirical validation.
- Weaknesses: assumes existence and uniqueness of intrinsic group additive structure, may be computationally expensive for large datasets, and lacks comprehensive comparisons to existing methods.
- Overall rating by reviewer: 8/10.
