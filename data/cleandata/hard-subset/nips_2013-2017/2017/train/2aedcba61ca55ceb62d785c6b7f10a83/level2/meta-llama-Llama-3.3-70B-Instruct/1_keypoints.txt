- Proposes a novel structure-penalized kernel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression.  
- Introduces a rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure.  
- Develops a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on RKHS capacity.  
- Method jointly identifies the group structure and estimates the nonparametric component functions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction and well-explained technical details.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of consistency and performance.  
- Simulation studies and real-data applications validate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structure is a significant contribution.  
- Strength: method is novel and effective for structure identification and function estimation.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical and empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: computational cost can be high for large datasets; backward stepwise helps but is insufficient.  
- Weakness: tuning parameters (µ, α) are critical and current cross-validation approach may be inadequate.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address computational efficiency and tuning selection, and clarify explanations for broader accessibility.
