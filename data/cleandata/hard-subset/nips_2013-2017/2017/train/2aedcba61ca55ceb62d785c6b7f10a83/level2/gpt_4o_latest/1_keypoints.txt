- Main contribution: Proposes a novel structure-penalized kernel method to identify intrinsic group additive structures and estimate nonparametric functions in high-dimensional regression.

Strengths:
- Novelty & theory: Rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structures and theoretical consistency guarantees.
- Practical relevance: Balances interpretability and flexibility for high-dimensional regression problems.
- Empirical validation: Extensive simulations and two real-world applications showing effectiveness in finding true group structures.
- Algorithms: Provides exhaustive search and a computationally cheaper backward stepwise algorithm for different settings.
- Literature connection: Situates work relative to graphical models and kernel methods, addressing gaps in prior approaches.

Weaknesses:
- Computational cost: Exhaustive search is expensive; stepwise greedy algorithm can miss true structures in some cases.
- Limited applications: Only two real-world examples, limiting evidence of broad robustness.
- Tuning complexity: Multiple hyperparameters (λ, µ, α) with insufficient exploration of their impact and selection strategies.
- Complexity measure interpretability: Covering-number–based complexity measure needs clearer practical interpretation for model selection.

Suggestions:
- Develop more efficient algorithms and improve robustness of stepwise approaches.
- Add more diverse real-world datasets to demonstrate generalizability.
- Provide deeper sensitivity analysis and automated tuning strategies for hyperparameters.
- Explore alternative complexity measures or penalty functions to potentially improve performance.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions emphasizing improved computational efficiency and expanded real-world validation.
