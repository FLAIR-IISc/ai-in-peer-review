- Paper studies nonparametric regression with interactions via a minimal “intrinsic” group additive structure between univariate additive models and high-order ANOVA kernels.
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel estimator using RKHS direct-sum capacity control, jointly fitting f and selecting predictor groups.
- Proves existence/uniqueness of the intrinsic structure, risk concentration for amiable vs non-amiable structures, and structure selection consistency under tuning.
- Algorithms: exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise greedy heuristic for larger p; simulations and Boston Housing case study show feasibility.
- Strength: clear formalization of a minimal intrinsic group structure under a natural partial order—useful conceptual advance.
- Strength: principled structural penalty derived from covering-number based RKHS capacity control rather than ad hoc regularization.
- Strength: nonasymptotic theoretical bounds distinguishing amiable/non-amiable structures and a consistency theorem for selection.
- Strength: sensible algorithms and interpretable empirical visualization on real data.
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—small datasets, no statistical tests, and missing comparisons to strong baselines (SpAM, kernel ANOVA/ESP, SALSA, OKGT).
- Weakness: scalability concerns—greedy backward stepwise can miss true partitions, with no guarantees or runtime analysis.
- Weakness: penalty derivation simplifies constants (e.g., to µ∑α|u|) without sensitivity analysis or clear interpretation of α and kernel/scale dependence.
- Weakness: some theoretical assumptions (e.g., independence for strict inclusion, kernel universality) may not hold; robustness to correlated covariates/non-universal kernels is unstudied.
- Weakness: sparse reproducibility details (kernel choices, tuning of λ/µ/α, and code).
- Suggestion: add comprehensive comparisons on larger synthetic/real benchmarks (p ≥ 50–100) including SALSA, SpAM, kernel ANOVA, and OKGT, plus penalty/search ablations.
- Suggestion: provide stronger search strategies (beam search, MCMC over partitions, submodularity analysis or relaxations) and complexity/runtime results.
- Suggestion: clarify penalty mapping from covering numbers, analyze sensitivity to α/µ/λ, and give practitioner guidance.
- Suggestion: discuss and empirically/theoretically address covariate dependence; release code.
- For acceptance: solid, elegant theory, well-motivated penalty, and interpretable modeling framework.
- Against acceptance: empirical validation, scalability, and practical applicability are not yet convincing.
