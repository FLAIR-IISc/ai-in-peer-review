## Review of “Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression”

This paper addresses the challenge of high-dimensional nonparametric regression by revisiting the group additive model. The core contribution lies in rigorously defining the “intrinsic group additive structure” and proposing a structure-penalized kernel method for simultaneously identifying this structure and estimating the nonparametric functions. The method incorporates a novel complexity measure for group additive structures, and the authors prove consistency in identifying the intrinsic structure. 

The paper builds upon the existing literature on additive models and functional ANOVA, correctly identifying their limitations in capturing interactions without introducing excessive complexity. The introduction of the group additive model as a middle ground is well-motivated, and the authors convincingly argue for the need to identify the optimal grouping structure. The theoretical development, including the definition of intrinsic structure and the derivation of the complexity measure, is a significant strength. The consistency proofs, though detailed and relegated to the supplementary material, provide a strong foundation for the proposed method.

However, the paper also has several weaknesses. While the theoretical framework is sound, the practical implementation details are somewhat lacking. The description of the algorithms (exhaustive search and stepwise approach) is brief, and computational considerations are not thoroughly discussed. The simulation studies, while demonstrating the method's effectiveness in some scenarios, could be more extensive, particularly in exploring the impact of noise levels and dimensionality. The choice of kernel functions is not extensively justified.  The real data application is limited; while providing a plausible example, a more comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets would bolster the claims of general applicability.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically grounded approach to group additive structure identification.
*   Rigorous definition of the “intrinsic group additive structure”.
*   Consistency guarantees for the proposed method.
*   Potentially useful tool for high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of practical implementation and computational complexity.
*   Simulation studies could be more comprehensive.
*   Real data application lacks depth.
*   Kernel selection not adequately justified.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of nonparametric regression. The theoretical foundations are strong, and the proposed method has the potential to be a useful tool for practitioners. However, the limited discussion of practical considerations and the somewhat superficial evaluation raise concerns.  I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**.  The authors should expand the discussion of implementation details, conduct more comprehensive simulation studies, and provide a more in-depth analysis of the real data application. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
