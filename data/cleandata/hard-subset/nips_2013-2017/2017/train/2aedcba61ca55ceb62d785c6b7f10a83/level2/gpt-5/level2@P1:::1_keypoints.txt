- Paper studies nonparametric regression with group-additive structure and defines a partial order over groupings to identify a unique minimal intrinsic grouping G*.
- Proposes simultaneous identification of G* and estimation of f via a structure-penalized kernel method on direct-sum RKHSs, with a penalty C(G) based on covering-number bounds.
- Provides generalization bounds, shows non-amiable structures incur a positive risk gap, and proves selection consistency under appropriate tuning.
- Implements exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise greedy algorithm for larger p; presents simulations and a Boston Housing case study.
- Novelty: explicit lattice-theoretic intrinsic grouping notion and linking structural penalties to RKHS covering numbers for direct sums.
- Strength: clear formalization of group-additive structures and the intrinsic minimal structure as a conceptual contribution.
- Strength: principled structure-penalized objective combining empirical loss, RKHS norms, and a theoretically grounded structural complexity term.
- Strength: thoughtful consistency analysis partitioning amiable vs. non-amiable structures and deriving uniform bounds and risk gaps.
- Strength: practical algorithms and empirical examples showing frequent recovery of true structure in simulations.
- Weakness: penalty construction unclear — dependence on arbitrary ε and α, unjustified simplification of exponents, and potential bias toward small groups.
- Weakness: insufficient guidance for choosing/tuning α, µ, and ε in theory and practice; no data-driven calibration provided.
- Weakness: key propositions assume independent covariates and universal kernels; extension to correlated X and explicit kernel assumptions are not stated.
- Weakness: capacity bound applicability is not fully specified for common kernels (Gaussian/Laplace) and the aggregation across groups lacks precise conditions.
- Weakness: backward stepwise algorithm is greedy with no approximation guarantee or consistency results; can miss true structure.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to strong baselines (functional ANOVA, hierarchical kernel learning, SALSA, sparse additive models), predictive accuracy and runtime metrics, and sensitivity analyses.
- Weakness: notational overload and informal transitions from covering-number bounds to α-based penalties reduce clarity; minor typos and ambiguities exist.
- Suggestions: provide data-driven selection procedures for µ and α with theoretical scaling in n and p, and clarify the role of ε.
- Suggestions: explicitly state assumptions on covariate dependence and kernel universality/bandwidths for theorems, and, if possible, extend results to correlated X.
- Suggestions: strengthen Proposition 3 with full proofs/conditions and consider data-dependent complexity measures (Rademacher/localized bounds).
- Suggestions: improve large-p search (multi-start, beam search, swap moves), provide complexity analysis, and report ablation studies.
- Suggestions: expand empirical comparisons to relevant baselines, report structure recovery, predictive performance, runtime, and sensitivity to λ, µ, α, and kernel hyperparameters.
- Overall assessment: promising and original conceptual/theoretical contribution but needs tighter penalty derivation, clearer assumptions, stronger empirical comparisons, and better large-p algorithmic support; reviewer leans borderline/weak reject pending revisions.
