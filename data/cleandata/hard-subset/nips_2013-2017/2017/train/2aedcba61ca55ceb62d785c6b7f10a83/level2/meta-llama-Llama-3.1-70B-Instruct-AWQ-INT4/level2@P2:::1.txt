This paper proposes a novel method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression, which is a crucial step in high-dimensional data analysis. The authors provide a rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure and develop a structure-penalized kernel method for simultaneous identification of the intrinsic group additive structure and nonparametric function estimation.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical foundation. The use of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and the covering number as a complexity measure is a nice touch, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the theoretical properties of the proposed method.

The simulation study and real data application demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in identifying the intrinsic group additive structure and estimating the nonparametric functions. The results are promising, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of their findings.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. First, the authors could provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially for large datasets. Second, the authors could discuss the robustness of the proposed method to outliers and noisy data. Finally, the authors could provide more comparisons with existing methods, such as functional ANOVA models and graphical models.
