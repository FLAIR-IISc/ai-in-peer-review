- Paper studies nonparametric regression with group-wise additive structure; defines a partial order over partitions, amiable structures, and proves existence/uniqueness of a minimal intrinsic partition G*; proposes penalized kernel regression in a direct-sum RKHS and gives two algorithms plus experiments.

- Builds on additive/ANOVA and SS-ANOVA literature and OKGT; uses covering-number based RKHS capacity control; but omits and insufficiently contrasts COSSO, sparse additive models, and hierarchical kernel learning / MKL work.

- Strength: clear formalization of intrinsic group structure and a natural partial order over partitions.

- Strength: principled RKHS tools — direct-sum RKHS formulation and product-covering argument linking covering numbers to group RKHSs.

- Strength: theoretical results include uniform risk bounds for amiable structures, separation for non-amiable ones, and selection consistency under appropriate penalties.

- Strength: practical motivation and interpretable examples (Boston Housing) showing the tradeoff between interpretability and flexibility.

- Major concern: structural penalty is ambiguous and inconsistently notated (exponent vs product), with an ad hoc switch from ε-dependence to hyperparameter α that is not justified.

- Major concern: selection consistency (Theorem 4) requires tuning parameters (µ, λ, α) with specified scaling, but no explicit rates or practical guidance are given for choosing them.

- Major concern: backward stepwise algorithm is underspecified (how splits/placements are tried, objective at each step, tuning procedure, stopping rule) and lacks guarantees.

- Major concern: scalability is not addressed — exhaustive search is intractable (Bell numbers) and the heuristic may still be expensive due to repeated kernel ridge solves.

- Major concern: identifiability/uniqueness of G* relies on independence assumptions; paper needs clearer assumptions and discussion of how covariate dependence could lead to non-unique minimal partitions.

- Major concern: kernel choices, bandwidth selection, and regularization tuning are under-specified and their impact on theory and practice is unexplored.

- Clarity issues: notational inconsistencies around the penalty, missing reference years, typos, and lack of a worked example connecting the covering-number bound to the implemented penalty.

- Originality: intrinsic group structure and consistency results are interesting, but novelty is weakened by prior MKL/HKL and COSSO-related methods that perform component selection in RKHSs.

- Empirical weaknesses: experiments lack comparisons to strong baselines (COSSO, SS-ANOVA, SALSA, OKGT, HKL/MKL, sparse additive methods), are small-scale, and do not vary n, p, SNR, dependence or report runtimes.

- Suggestions: precisely define and reconcile the structural penalty with covering-number theory and provide explicit conditions on (λn, µn, αn) for consistency.

- Suggestions: add and compare to relevant baselines (COSSO, HKL/MKL, SALSA, OKGT, sparse additive/interactions), and expand experiments to study sensitivity and runtime.

- Suggestions: provide detailed pseudocode, complexity analysis, and consider convex or more scalable alternatives (e.g., MKL-style formulations with structured sparsity).

- Suggestions: clarify assumptions and provide a proof sketch for uniqueness of G*; discuss effects of covariate dependence.

- Suggestions: fix notation and references, and include a worked example linking covering-number bounds to the final optimization penalty.

- Recommendation: borderline reject — promising theory and framing but currently undermined by penalty ambiguity, missing related work, limited experiments, and underdeveloped algorithms.
