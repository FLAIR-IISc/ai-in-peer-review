- Paper addresses identification of group additive structures in nonparametric regression, defining the intrinsic group additive structure to resolve non-uniqueness.

- Proposes a structural-penalty approach using RKHS covering numbers as a complexity measure and proves consistency.

- Develops two practical algorithms (exhaustive search and backward stepwise) with implementation details.

- Connects to prior work (additive models, functional ANOVA, graphical models) while focusing on group additive structure rather than conditional dependence.

- Theoretical contribution: rigorous definition (Theorem 1) and consistency results (Theorem 4) with a novel complexity derivation.

- Practical algorithms: designed for both small and large p, and described clearly (Algorithms 1 and 2).

- Clear problem formulation framing trade-off between model fit and structural complexity.

- Strong empirical validation: simulation studies and a Boston Housing real-data application showing effective structure identification and interpretability.

- Weakness: backward stepwise algorithm is greedy and can miss the true structure if optimal path is not followed.

- Weakness: computational burden of exhaustive search for large p and limited theoretical justification for stepwise scalability.

- Weakness: tuning parameter selection (µ and α) appears ad hoc and sensitivity is not thoroughly explored.

- Weakness: limited direct comparisons with other interaction-structure identification methods (e.g., [6], [11]).

- Quality assessment: technically sound with rigorous theory and well-designed experiments; limitations acknowledged.

- Clarity assessment: clearly written, well-organized, consistent notation, and reproducible algorithmic detail; real-data presentation is informative.

- Originality: novel concept of intrinsic group additive structure and innovative use of RKHS covering numbers.

- Significance: addresses an important practical gap between additive and high-interaction models, providing an interpretable, theoretically grounded solution.

- Recommendation: accept—substantial contribution despite some algorithmic and tuning limitations.

- Pro: first rigorous intrinsic group additive structure definition, novel RKHS-based complexity measure, theoretical guarantees, practical algorithms, clear distinction from graphical models.

- Con: greedy stepwise method risk, limited large-p complexity analysis, and non-principled parameter selection approach.
