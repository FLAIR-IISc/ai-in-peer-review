

# Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper introduces a novel concept of "intrinsic group additive structure" for nonparametric regression, addressing two critical gaps in previous work on group additive models (specifically the OKGT method). The authors define the minimal group additive structure among all correct group additive structures for a given function, which represents the most concise representation that maintains estimation efficiency while improving interpretability. They propose a method to identify this intrinsic structure through a penalized kernel approach using a novel complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers.

The key novelty lies in the formalization of the intrinsic group additive structure and the development of a principled approach to identify it. While previous work on additive models (e.g., [5, 13]) and group additive models (e.g., [11]) has focused on estimation under a known structure, this paper tackles the more fundamental problem of structure identification. The authors derive a tractable complexity measure C(G) = ∑j α^{|uj|} for group structures G and incorporate it into a penalized risk minimization framework. This leads to a theoretically grounded method with consistency guarantees for identifying the intrinsic structure.

The paper makes several important contributions: (1) the rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structure (Theorem 1), (2) the novel complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers (Section 2.3.2), (3) the theoretical consistency results (Theorems 2-4), and (4) practical algorithms for structure identification (Algorithms 1-2). The connection to graphical models is acknowledged but properly contextualized as a different problem (Section 1), with the authors correctly emphasizing that they target the group additive structure directly rather than the more complex conditional dependence structure.

Strengths:
- The core concept of intrinsic group additive structure is genuinely novel and well-motivated. It provides a principled way to address the nonidentifiability issue in group additive models.
- The theoretical foundation is strong, with clear consistency proofs that address the key challenge of structure identification.
- The complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers is creative and appropriately designed to reflect the structural complexity of group additive models.
- The paper successfully bridges theoretical concepts with practical algorithms, making the method implementable.
- The experimental results demonstrate the method's ability to recover true group structures across various synthetic models.

Weaknesses:
- The connection to related work on sparse graphical models and group sparsity could be more thoroughly contrasted (e.g., [9] on sparse block structures). While the paper acknowledges this, a more detailed discussion of how their approach differs from these related methods would strengthen the contribution.
- The experimental validation is somewhat limited (only 5 synthetic models and one real dataset). More comprehensive experiments on larger-scale problems would better demonstrate practical utility.
- The computational efficiency of the exhaustive search approach (Algorithm 1) is not thoroughly addressed for large p, though the stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2) helps mitigate this issue.

The paper makes a significant contribution to nonparametric regression by addressing the important problem of structure identification rather than assuming it. While the methodological details are sound, the true novelty lies in the conceptual framework of intrinsic group additive structure and the principled approach to identify it. This represents a meaningful step beyond incremental improvements in additive modeling.

The authors have clearly established how their work differs from previous contributions, particularly by distinguishing their focus on group additive structures from the more complex conditional dependence structures considered in graphical models. The theoretical results provide a solid foundation for the method, and the practical algorithms make it implementable.

In terms of significance, this work opens up a new direction for nonparametric regression that could influence future research on high-dimensional function estimation. The intrinsic group additive structure concept provides a clear theoretical foundation for model selection in additive nonparametric regression, which is a fundamental problem in the field.

I believe this paper makes a strong contribution to the field of nonparametric regression, with a novel conceptual framework that addresses a significant gap in the literature. While the experimental validation could be expanded, the theoretical novelty and soundness of the approach justify acceptance. The paper successfully advances the state of the art by introducing a new concept (intrinsic group additive structure) and a method to identify it, rather than offering only incremental improvements to existing approaches.

Pro for acceptance: The intrinsic group additive structure concept is genuinely novel and addresses a fundamental gap in group additive modeling literature.

Con for acceptance: More comprehensive experimental validation on larger-scale problems would strengthen the practical contribution.

This paper meets NIPS standards for novelty, significance, and technical soundness. The authors have made a clear and meaningful contribution to the field, and I recommend acceptance.
