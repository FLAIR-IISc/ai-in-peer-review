- Summary: Proposes a kernel-RKHS-based method to identify group additive structure in nonparametric regression with a complexity penalty, theoretical consistency, and empirical validation.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem of balancing flexibility and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

- Strength: Introduces a novel RKHS-based complexity measure (covering numbers) to penalize complex group structures.

- Strength: Provides theoretical consistency guarantees for identifying the true group structure.

- Strength: Offers practical algorithms: exhaustive search for small p and a stepwise greedy algorithm for larger p.

- Strength: Empirical validation via simulations shows recovery of true structures; applied to Boston Housing data.

- Strength: Clearly situates the contribution relative to existing work (functional ANOVA, OKGT, etc.).

- Weakness: Exhaustive search is computationally expensive; the stepwise algorithm’s greedy nature may miss optimal structures.

- Weakness: Method performance depends on tuning hyperparameters (λ, µ, α) with limited guidance beyond validation.

- Weakness: Paper lacks analysis of the impact of different kernel choices on performance.

- Weakness: Real-data analysis is superficial and needs more domain-specific interpretation and comparison.

- Weakness: Theoretical guarantees rely on assumptions (smoothness, predictor independence) with limited discussion of robustness to violations.

- Qualitative — Quality (8/10): Technically sound and well-motivated with strong theoretical analysis.

- Qualitative — Clarity (7/10): Generally well-written but theoretical sections are dense; RKHS complexity measure needs more intuitive explanation.

- Qualitative — Originality (8/10): Novel complexity measure and penalized framework represent a significant contribution.

- Qualitative — Significance (7/10): Promising results with potential impact contingent on scalability and robustness.

- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a relevant problem, presents a novel and well-motivated approach, offers strong theoretical guarantees and supportive simulations, and is clearly written.

- Arguments against acceptance: Computational scalability concerns, sensitivity to hyperparameters, limited real-data insight, and insufficient discussion of theoretical assumptions.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address scalability, hyperparameter guidance, kernel choice analysis, deeper real-data interpretation, and clearer explanation of the RKHS complexity measure.
