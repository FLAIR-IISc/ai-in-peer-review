- Paper studies nonparametric regression with group additive structure and aims to recover the minimal (intrinsic) group partition.
- Contributions: formalize intrinsic group partition, propose RKHS-based estimation with a complexity penalty for group selection, and give high-level selection consistency results.
- Positions work relative to additive models, SS-ANOVA, ESP/SALSA, OKGT, and block-structured graphical models, focusing on discovering interacting variable groups without enumerating all interactions.
- Strength: formal lattice/partial-order view and uniqueness/existence of minimal intrinsic group are conceptually clean and useful.
- Strength: penalized RKHS formulation is reasonable and reduces to kernel ridge regression via the representer theorem.
- Strength: theoretical distinction of “amiable” structures and use of covering-number bounds to obtain selection consistency statements.
- Major weakness: complexity penalty derivation-to-practice gap — covering-number-based penalty collapsed into a tuning scalar α and treated ad hoc.
- Major weakness: inconsistent/ambiguous penalty notation (different forms of C(G) and Eq. (6) inconsistency) requiring correction and justification.
- Major weakness: key theoretical assumptions (kernel classes, entropy, independence) are not reconciled with the practical algorithm (Gaussian kernels, finite-n KRR, CV for λ, µ, α).
- Major weakness: final consistency conditions are vague (“choose proper µ”, “λn * n → 0”) with no concrete rates or alignment with implemented tuning.
- Algorithmic weakness: direct-sum RKHS norm and group kernels are not precisely defined; combined kernel K_G should be explicitly stated.
- Algorithmic weakness: greedy backward splitting (Algorithm 2) is ambiguously specified, likely brittle, lacks complexity analysis and guarantees, and may get stuck in local minima.
- Empirical weakness: experiments report recovery frequencies but lack detailed data-generation descriptions and comparisons to strong baselines (SALSA/ESP-KRR, OKGT, HKL, SpAM, HSIC-based grouping).
- Empirical weakness: real-data example is descriptive with no held-out predictive comparisons or scalability analysis; practical advantage over existing methods is not demonstrated.
- Clarity issues: notational inconsistencies, typos, missing definitions, deferred proofs without sufficient intuition, and incomplete algorithm specification.
- Originality: idea of intrinsic group additive structure and RKHS-capacity-based structural penalty is novel, but practical step from theory to a usable penalty is not fully justified.
- Significance: approach could be useful for identifying low-order interactions with interpretability, but current theoretical-practical gaps and weak experiments limit immediate impact.
- Suggestions: fix and justify penalty form or propose a principled surrogate linking α and µ to kernel/ε/n; clarify RKHS construction and combined kernel.
- Suggestions: strengthen experiments with competitive baselines, report prediction and structure recovery, and analyze scalability and algorithmic robustness.
- Suggestions: fully specify Algorithm 2 (candidate moves, stopping criteria, multi-pass/restart strategies), and state precise assumptions and rates in theorems consistent with implementation.
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject — interesting and promising framework but requires theoretical/practical fixes, clarity improvements, and stronger empirical validation.
