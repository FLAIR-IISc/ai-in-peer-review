- Proposes an RKHS-based method to identify the minimal “group additive” structure in nonparametric regression.  
- Introduces a novel complexity penalty using covering numbers to quantify group-structure complexity.  
- Proves consistency for identifying the intrinsic group structure under specified assumptions.  
- Presents practical algorithms for structure identification: exhaustive search and a stepwise approach.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on simulations and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, and OKGT while addressing non-identifiability and lack of systematic identification.  
- Grounds complexity control in RKHS theory and covering-number-based learning theory.  
- Strength: tackles an important trade-off between flexibility and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees and a principled complexity measure.  
- Strength: clear presentation and connection to graphical models.  
- Weakness: exhaustive search is computationally expensive and impractical for high dimensions.  
- Weakness: stepwise algorithm performance is not consistently superior and may need efficiency improvements.  
- Weakness: method is sensitive to tuning parameters μ and α and lacks robust automatic selection.  
- Weakness: limited real-world validation (single dataset) reduces empirical generality.  
- Weakness: reliance on RKHS and kernel choice; impact of different kernels is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Weakness: theoretical results depend on specific assumptions; robustness to assumption violations is not discussed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: analyze computational complexity and improve stepwise efficiency, develop automatic tuning for μ and α, test on more datasets, discuss kernel choice impact, and address robustness of theoretical assumptions.
