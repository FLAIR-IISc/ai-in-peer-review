- Paper defines the "intrinsic group additive structure" and proposes GASI, a penalized kernel method to identify it.  
- Theorem 1 proves uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure, addressing prior non-identifiability issues.  
- Theoretical results (including Theorem 4 and supporting propositions) establish consistency of the proposed method.  
- Complexity measure C(G) is derived from RKHS covering numbers, linking group complexity to kernel theory.  
- Empirical results (simulations and Boston Housing) show GASI can recover true group structures and that a backward stepwise algorithm is practical.  
- The paper clearly distinguishes targeting group additive structure from graphical-model-based conditional dependence methods.  
- The proposed complexity measure C(G)=Σ α^|uj| appears somewhat ad hoc and lacks justification or comparison to alternatives.  
- Exhaustive search (Algorithm 1) is computationally infeasible for moderate p, and the greedy backward stepwise (Algorithm 2) may miss optimal structures.  
- The paper lacks detailed comparisons with other structure-identification approaches (e.g., sparse graphical models or methods in [2], [9]).  
- Theoretical analysis assumes fixed p and does not provide guarantees for p growing with n, limiting high-dimensional applicability.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and conceptual contribution with practical utility; recommend acceptance despite computational and comparative limitations.
