- Paper studies nonparametric regression with group-additive structure f(x)=∑u fu(xu), formalizes intrinsic (minimal) group structure, and proposes RKHS-based penalized KRR to estimate f and recover groups.  
- Authors define a partial order over groupings, propose a capacity-based structural penalty from covering-number bounds, and give exhaustive and greedy search algorithms with selection consistency results.  
- Connects to additive models, functional ANOVA/ESP kernels, OKGT, kernel covering-number theory, and graphical-model ideas; needs stronger positioning against COSSO/SpAM, MKL, SALSA/ESP methods.  
- The intrinsic group definition and partial-order formalism are clean and conceptually useful.  
- Proposition 3 on covering numbers and the capacity-motivated penalty are plausible, and Theorems 2–4 give consistency under universal kernels and tuning, for fixed p.  
- Penalty form is ambiguous in notation (is α exponentiated or multiplied?), undermining implementation and theoretical interpretation.  
- Dependence of α on ε and r is non-operational; no constructive or data-driven choice or rates for µ and α are provided.  
- Proposition 1’s strict inclusion/identifiability needs predictor independence; behavior under correlated covariates is unclear.  
- Assumptions for covering-number bounds and universality (kernel types, decay conditions) are scattered and insufficiently consolidated.  
- Exhaustive search is intractable (Bell numbers); greedy backward splitting is heuristic, lacks guarantees, and can fail empirically.  
- Experiments are limited: synthetic details (kernels, bandwidths, λ tuning) are missing and evaluation focuses on recovery frequency without prediction or estimation error.  
- No comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., SALSA, pairwise KRR/ESP, COSSO/SpAM, hierarchical interaction selection).  
- Real-data validation is minimal (only Boston Housing) and anecdotal without robustness checks.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent notation for the penalty, typos/subscript/superscript inconsistencies, and unclear use of r vs. ε; needs a dedicated assumptions section.  
- Originality: novel intrinsic-group concept and capacity-based direct-sum RKHS penalty offer a fresh angle versus sparsity-based approaches.  
- Needs clearer positioning to explain advantages over existing sparse/additive RKHS and interaction-selection methods.  
- Practical significance limited by scalability concerns, hyperparameter dependence, and sparse empirical validation for larger p or correlated X.  
- Pros: clear problem setup, novel intrinsic structure, principled penalty, and selection consistency theory.  
- Cons: ambiguous penalty definition, scattered theoretical assumptions, no constructive µ/α selection, limited experiments and baselines, greedy algorithm lacks guarantees, unclear behavior with correlated predictors.  
- Key questions for authors: clarify exact C(G) form and α, specify kernels/bandwidth/λ tuning, address correlated predictors and Proposition 1, compare to strong baselines, and provide guidance/theory for µ/α scaling with n, p, group sizes.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject — promising conceptual/theoretical work but needs penalty clarification, stronger baselines, scalability and robustness experiments, and improved exposition to be a strong contribution.
