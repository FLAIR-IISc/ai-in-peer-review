- Addresses identification of "intrinsic group additive structure" in nonparametric regression, proposing a kernel-based method with an RKHS covering-number penalty.  
- Defines and proves uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure (Theorem 1).  
- Develops a complexity measure from RKHS covering numbers (Equation 6) and uses it to penalize model complexity.  
- Establishes consistency of the proposed method (Theorems 2–4).  
- Provides two algorithms (exhaustive and stepwise) with clear descriptions and trade-offs.  
- Empirically validates the method with simulations and a Boston Housing data application, showing accurate recovery of group structure.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically rigorous; amiable vs non-amiable structures are well explained.  
- Novel contribution: introduces intrinsic group additive structure and distinguishes from ANOVA, OKGT, and graphical models.  
- Significant practical impact: offers dimension reduction and interpretability between additive and high-order interaction models.  
- Theoretical scope limited by the universal-kernel assumption and lack of discussion on non-universal kernels and sensitivity to the complexity measure C(G).  
- Computational scalability concerns: exhaustive search is infeasible for many predictors and the stepwise algorithm is greedy with limitations.  
- Could better situate work relative to recent interaction-structure literature (e.g., Kandasamy & Yu 2016).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and empirical contribution; recommendation: Accept.
