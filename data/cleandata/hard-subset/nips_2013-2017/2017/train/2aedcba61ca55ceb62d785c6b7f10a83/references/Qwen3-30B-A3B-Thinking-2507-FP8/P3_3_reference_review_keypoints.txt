- Paper proposes identifying a minimal “intrinsic group additive structure” for kernel nonparametric regression to allow interactions while avoiding full functional ANOVA complexity.  
- Defines the intrinsic group additive structure and uses an RKHS-based structural penalty to identify it.  
- Provides theoretical consistency results (Theorems 2–4) linking the penalty to RKHS capacity measures.  
- Offers two algorithms for structure identification: exhaustive search and a scalable backward stepwise procedure.  
- Empirical validation on simulations and the Boston Housing dataset shows successful recovery of group structures in most tested cases.  
- Theoretical results depend on universal kernels and specific covering-number assumptions; practical sensitivity to kernel choice (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth) is not explored.  
- Scalability concerns: backward stepwise helps but computational complexity for very high-dimensional settings (p > 100) is not analyzed.  
- Tuning-parameter sensitivity: grid search used for (µ, α) with observed instability in some simulations, and no principled selection strategy is provided.  
- Insufficient comparison to related interaction-structure methods (e.g., sparse additive models, interaction screening, sparse group lasso) and only brief mention of graphical models.  
- Use of covering numbers as the complexity measure is not justified relative to alternatives (Rademacher complexity, VC dimension).  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, novel, and empirically promising work that warrants acceptance, contingent on addressing practical sensitivity, scalability, and comparative analysis.
