- Problem & contribution: Proposes a kernel-based method to identify group additive structure in nonparametric regression with a novel complexity penalty based on RKHS covering numbers, plus consistency guarantees and empirical results.
- Strength: Addresses an important problem balancing flexibility and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
- Strength: Introduces a creative, theoretically grounded complexity penalty using covering numbers to penalize overly complex groupings.
- Strength: Provides theoretical consistency results supporting the method.
- Strength: Clear motivation and good positioning relative to fully nonparametric, additive, and ANOVA approaches.
- Strength: Empirical validation via simulations and a real-data application shows promising performance.
- Strength: Offers both exhaustive-search and a scalable stepwise algorithm for practical use.
- Weakness: Computational complexity remains a major concern, especially for covering number calculations and very high-dimensional data.
- Weakness: Method appears sensitive to tuning parameters (λ, μ, α) and parameter-selection strategy is not thoroughly detailed.
- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on kernel and smoothness assumptions with limited discussion of robustness to violations.
- Weakness: Real-data analysis is limited in scope and could use more comparisons to other methods.
- Weakness: Implementation and computational burden of covering number calculation are not clearly explained.
- Weakness: Brief connection to graphical models is superficial and does not explore deeper synergies.
- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10—technically strong but some dense/mathematical exposition.
- Pro arguments: Significant problem, novel/theoretically sound approach, well-motivated positioning, and promising empirical results.
- Con arguments: Computation and scalability concerns, parameter sensitivity, and limited real-data validation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—ask authors to clarify covering number computation, detail parameter selection, expand real-data analysis, and discuss scalability/approximations.
