- Tackles high-dimensional nonparametric regression by learning a group additive model that groups interacting variables to avoid the curse of dimensionality.  
- Proposes a method to identify intrinsic group additive structure from data and estimate nonparametric functions per group.  
- Formulates the task as a penalized kernel ridge regression with a novel RKHS covering-number-based complexity measure for group structures.  
- Provides theoretical consistency guarantees for structure identification and estimation.  
- Empirically validates the method on simulations and the Boston Housing dataset, showing ability to recover true structure.  
- Positions the work as a generalization of additive models and a parsimonious alternative to functional ANOVA and higher-order interactions.  
- Distinguishes GASI from graphical models by focusing on group structure rather than conditional dependencies.  
- Extends OKGT by addressing non-identifiability and structure identification left open in prior work.  
- Novelty: first to identify group structure directly from data rather than requiring pre-specified groups.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: tractable complexity measure and consistency proof bolster the penalty choice.  
- Practical algorithmic options: offers both exhaustive search and a stepwise (greedy) approach for different problem sizes.  
- Empirical strength: simulations show structure recovery across scenarios; real-data example is compelling.  
- Well-written and clearly connected to existing literature.  
- Major weakness: exhaustive search is computationally intractable in moderate/high dimensions; stepwise is greedy and may miss global optima.  
- Practical computation of the covering-number complexity and sensitivity to α is unclear; heuristics for α are not provided.  
- Parameter tuning details (especially for µ and α) are insufficient beyond grid search mention.  
- Limited empirical comparison to alternative variable selection methods for functional ANOVA (e.g., sparse ANOVA) is a gap.  
- Real-data results lack deeper interpretation of the identified groups in domain context.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing computational complexity analysis, heuristics for α, fuller comparisons, and expanded real-data interpretation.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
