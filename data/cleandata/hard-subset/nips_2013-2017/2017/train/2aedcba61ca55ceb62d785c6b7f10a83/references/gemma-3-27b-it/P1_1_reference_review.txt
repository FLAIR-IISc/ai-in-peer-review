## Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of identifying an appropriate group additive structure in nonparametric regression, aiming to bridge the gap between fully nonparametric methods (prone to the curse of dimensionality) and restrictive additive models (lacking interaction modeling). The authors propose a kernel method-based approach with a novel complexity penalty for group structures, based on covering numbers of RKHSs. They derive theoretical consistency guarantees for their method and demonstrate its effectiveness through simulations and a real-data application. The core idea is to balance model flexibility with structural simplicity, allowing for interactions within groups while avoiding the exponential increase in parameters seen in higher-order ANOVA models.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Identifying an appropriate structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression is crucial for both statistical efficiency and interpretability. This paper directly addresses this challenge.
* **Novel Complexity Penalty:** The use of covering numbers to quantify the complexity of group structures is a creative and theoretically grounded approach.  This provides a principled way to penalize overly complex groupings.
* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The consistency results, while perhaps complex to fully digest without access to the supplementary material, offer strong theoretical support for the proposed method.
* **Clear Motivation and Connections to Existing Work:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing approaches (fully nonparametric, standard additive models, ANOVA models) and positions their contribution effectively within the existing literature.
* **Empirical Validation:** The simulations and real-data application provide initial evidence of the method's performance and practical utility.
* **Algorithm Development:** Providing both exhaustive search and a more scalable stepwise algorithm is a good approach for broad applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the stepwise algorithm is proposed for large-p settings, the overall computational cost, particularly with the covering number calculation, remains a concern. The paper doesn’t fully address the scalability for very high-dimensional data.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance seems sensitive to the choice of the tuning parameters (λ, μ, α), and the method for selecting them isn’t as detailed as the main algorithmic proposal. The reliance on a validation set is standard but can be data-dependent.
* **Assumptions:** The theory relies on certain assumptions about the kernels and the smoothness of the underlying function. It would be beneficial to discuss the robustness of the method to violations of these assumptions.
* **Limited Real-Data Analysis:** The real-data application is relatively limited in scope.  More detailed analysis and comparison with other relevant methods would strengthen the empirical validation.
* **Clarity of Covering Number Calculation:** While the concept is introduced, the precise implementation and computational burden of calculating the covering numbers isn’t fully explained, leaving some ambiguity about practical feasibility.
* **Connection to Graphical Models:** The brief discussion connecting this work to graphical models feels somewhat superficial and doesn’t explore potential synergies in depth.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis and reasonable experimental validation.  The proposed approach is well-motivated and addresses a relevant problem.
* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally well-written, but the mathematical details (especially surrounding the complexity measure and theoretical proofs) can be dense.  Improving the exposition of the covering number calculation would be helpful.
* **Originality (8/10):** The combination of kernel methods, group additive structures, and a complexity penalty based on covering numbers is novel and represents a significant contribution.
* **Significance (7/10):** The proposed method has the potential to be useful for researchers and practitioners dealing with high-dimensional nonparametric regression problems.  The ability to identify underlying group structures could lead to more interpretable and efficient models.



**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*  Addresses a significant problem in nonparametric regression.
*  Novel and theoretically sound approach with consistency guarantees.
*  Well-motivated and clearly positioned within the existing literature.
*  Demonstrates promising results in simulations and a real-data application.

**Con:**

*  Computational complexity concerns, particularly regarding covering number calculation.
*  Parameter sensitivity and limited detail on parameter selection.
*  Real-data analysis could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The authors have addressed an important problem with a novel and theoretically grounded approach. While there are some concerns regarding computational complexity and parameter sensitivity, these issues do not fundamentally undermine the paper’s significance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity of the covering number calculation, providing a more detailed discussion of parameter selection, and expanding the real-data analysis.  Addressing the computational scalability concerns, even with a discussion of potential approximations or limitations, would further strengthen the paper.
