- Proposes identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression using a novel complexity measure based on covering numbers and an optimization balancing fit and complexity.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem of structure discovery in high-dimensional nonparametric regression for efficiency and interpretability.  
- Strength: Introduces the novel concept of an "intrinsic" group additive structure and a clever covering-number-based complexity measure.  
- Strength: Provides strong theoretical guarantees for consistency with clearly stated, well-motivated theorems.  
- Strength: Offers practical algorithms (exhaustive search and stepwise) acknowledging computational challenges.  
- Strength: Empirical validation via simulations and Boston Housing data with informative visualizations.  
- Strength: Connects appropriately to related work (functional ANOVA, graphical models, kernel group transformation).  
- Weakness: Theoretical results are dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with covering-number theory; more intuition is needed.  
- Weakness: Exhaustive search is computationally expensive for high dimensions and the stepwise approach may be insufficiently optimized.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited to a standard, relatively low-dimensional dataset; more complex, high-dimensional real data would strengthen the paper.  
- Weakness: Method performance appears sensitive to the regularization parameter µ; parameter tuning strategies need more analysis.  
- Weakness: Greedy nature of Algorithm 2 risks local optima; alternative or improved stepwise methods should be explored.  
- Quality: Technically sound and rigorous with strong theoretical and experimental support (Strong).  
- Clarity: Generally clear writing but theoretical sections are dense and need more intuitive explanation (Good).  
- Originality: Novel intrinsic structure concept and complexity measure represent a significant advance (Excellent).  
- Significance: Likely impactful for nonparametric regression, dimensionality reduction, and statistical learning (Good).  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded method with practical algorithms and promising empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Dense theory may limit accessibility, computational cost in high dimensions, and limited real-data breadth.  
- Overall assessment: Strong accept with minor revisions recommended for clarity and expanded empirical evaluation.
