- Overall: Tackles high-dimensional nonparametric regression by identifying group additive structures; strong theory and promising simulations, recommend accept with major revisions.  
- Problem/Goal: Aim is to find a minimal "intrinsic group additive structure" and estimate group-wise functions to balance interpretability and interaction modeling.  
- Method: Proposes a penalized kernel (RKHS) method with a complexity penalty C(G) to jointly identify group structure and estimate functions.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides consistency guarantees and novel use of covering numbers/capacity measures for group structures.  
- Positioning: Clearly distinguishes from fully additive and ANOVA models and notes connections to graphical models.  
- Strength: The intrinsic group additive definition and consistency proof are strong theoretical assets.  
- Strength: The penalized kernel approach is principled and includes a complexity penalty to avoid overfitting.  
- Strength: Problem is well-motivated as a middle ground between simple additive and combinatorial interaction models.  
- Strength: Simulations show initial evidence the method can recover true group structures.  
- Strength: Good integration with RKHS theory adds depth.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and notation-heavy, needing more intuitive exposition and examples, e.g., transition from C(G) to optimization.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited — simulations focus on simple structures and real-data example (Boston Housing, p=13) is small.  
- Weakness: Scalability is unclear; stepwise algorithm description is brief and not demonstrated for high-dimensional p > 100.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity (λ, µ, α) is not well explored beyond grid search.  
- Weakness: Real-data analysis is superficial with limited interpretation and comparisons to baseline methods.  
- For acceptance: Strong theory, novel structure-identification approach, clear motivation, and promising simulations.  
- Against acceptance: Issues with clarity, limited empirical/scalability evidence, superficial real-data application, and parameter sensitivity.  
- Suggested clarity fixes: Add intuitive explanations, examples, and visualizations (e.g., diagrams for group structures and C(G)).  
- Suggested empirical work: Run broader simulations with varied noise, distributions, complex interactions, and larger p.  
- Suggested scalability work: Analyze computational complexity, expand stepwise algorithm details, and test on higher-dimensional datasets.  
- Suggested tuning work: Explore robust parameter selection (e.g., more extensive cross-validation) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggested real-data improvements: Provide deeper interpretation, compare to additive and penalized baselines, and relate discovered groups to domain knowledge.  
- Suggested extensions: Expand discussion on connections to graphical models and potential for estimating sparse block structures.
