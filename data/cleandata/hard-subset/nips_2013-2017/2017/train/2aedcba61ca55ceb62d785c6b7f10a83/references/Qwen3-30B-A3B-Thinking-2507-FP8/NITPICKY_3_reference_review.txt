

# Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper proposes a method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression, addressing two limitations of prior work: non-uniqueness of group structures and lack of systematic identification approach. The authors define the intrinsic structure as the minimal amiable group additive structure and propose a structure-penalized kernel method using a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers.

**Strengths:** The paper makes a clear distinction between group additive models and functional ANOVA models, and provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for the intrinsic structure. The connection to graphical models is well-articulated, and the proposed two-step estimation procedure (with exhaustive search and backward stepwise algorithms) is well-motivated. The simulation study demonstrates reasonable performance on synthetic data.

**Weaknesses (minor but significant):**

1. **Technical inconsistencies:** The complexity measure derivation contains multiple notational errors. In section 2.3.2, they define C(Hk) = (ln(r/))^p+1 = α( )^p+1, which is confusing - the second equality should be α^p+1 where α = ln(r/). In equation (6), the penalty term should be µ∑_{j=1}^d α^{|u_j|}, not µ∑_{j=1}^d α|uj|.

2. **Theoretical gaps:** The proof of Proposition 3 (relating covering numbers of direct sums) is missing, which is critical to their complexity measure. The paper states "the growth rate of N(, IK) or its logarithm can be viewed as a capacity measure," but fails to justify why (ln(r/))^p+1 specifically is appropriate, especially since the paper later uses α^{|u_j|} rather than α^{|u_j|+1}.

3. **Algorithmic details:** The backward stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2) lacks critical implementation details. The condition "if R̂penG' < R̂penG then Keep G' as the new group structure" is ambiguous - does it mean strictly less, or less than or equal? The algorithm doesn't specify how to handle ties.

4. **Experimental shortcomings:** The paper claims Model 1 performance "deteriorated" without explaining why, which is a significant omission. In the real data application, they don't compare with competing methods or provide error metrics beyond the group structure identification.

5. **Notational inconsistencies:** The paper uses both "G" and "G*" to refer to the intrinsic structure, creating confusion. The notation "G ≤ G'" in Definition 1 is unclear without specifying whether it's set inclusion or subset relation.

6. **Missing technical details:** The paper claims "the number of nonparametric functions that need to be estimated quickly increases" for functional ANOVA models but doesn't quantify this growth rate. The paper also doesn't explain how the choice of α (which appears in the complexity measure) affects the method's performance.

7. **Formatting issues:** Inconsistent spacing around mathematical operators (e.g., "O ( n−d/(2d+p) )" vs "O(n^{-d/(2d+p)})"), missing publication years in references, and undefined symbols (d∞ for sup-norm).

**Overall assessment:** While the paper presents an interesting concept and provides a theoretically grounded approach, the numerous minor technical inconsistencies, missing proofs, and ambiguous algorithmic details significantly undermine the paper's technical rigor. The theoretical results are promising, but the presentation contains enough errors to make replication difficult. The methodological contribution is novel, but the execution lacks the precision expected at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** Reject. While the paper has a good idea and reasonable experimental results, the technical inconsistencies and lack of rigorous presentation prevent it from meeting NIPS's high standards. The errors in the mathematical formulation, particularly in the complexity measure derivation, are too significant to be overlooked. A revised version addressing these technical issues would be needed for acceptance.
