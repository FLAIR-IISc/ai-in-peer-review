

# Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper proposes a method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression using kernel methods and Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The authors define a group additive structure where the regression function can be expressed as a sum of functions each depending on a subset of predictors, and they aim to identify the minimal (intrinsic) such structure. The method involves a structural penalty based on a complexity measure derived from RKHS covering numbers.

**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**
The paper builds upon additive models and functional ANOVA approaches to address the curse of dimensionality in nonparametric regression. The authors correctly identify the gap in the literature regarding the non-uniqueness of group additive structures and the lack of a systematic identification method. Their approach extends the Optimal Kernel Group Transformation (OKGT) method from [11] by introducing a structural penalty based on RKHS covering numbers. The work relates to graphical models [2,7,9] but emphasizes that their method directly targets group additive structures rather than conditional dependence structures.

**Strengths**
1. The paper clearly defines the intrinsic group additive structure and proves its uniqueness, addressing a key theoretical gap.
2. The proposed complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers is theoretically grounded.
3. The authors provide two algorithms (exhaustive search and stepwise) for practical implementation.
4. The simulation studies demonstrate the method's ability to identify the true group structure in several models.
5. The real data application on the Boston Housing dataset provides a concrete example of the method's utility.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical Inconsistencies and Ambiguities:**
   - The paper uses inconsistent notation for the intrinsic group structure (G* vs G*), causing confusion in the proofs and algorithms.
   - The definition of the complexity measure C(G) = ∑α^|uj| is problematic because α is not defined in the main text (only mentioned in Section 2.3.2). This makes the penalty term mathematically ambiguous.
   - Theorem 4 claims consistency when λ_n * n → 0, but fails to specify how λ_n should be chosen to satisfy this condition in practice.
   - The paper states that "the intrinsic group structure can be considered lying in the middle between the original additive model and the functional ANOVA models" but fails to provide a precise theoretical basis for this claim.

2. **Theoretical Gaps:**
   - The paper claims uniqueness of the intrinsic group structure (Theorem 1) but doesn't address cases where the function f is not square-integrable, which is common in practical nonparametric regression settings.
   - The derivation of the complexity measure (Equation 6) lacks justification for why the covering number bound (Equation 4) is appropriate for their problem. The relationship between the covering number bound and the actual model complexity is not clearly established.
   - The proof of consistency (Theorem 4) relies on an assumption that λ_n * n → 0, but the paper doesn't provide guidance on how to choose λ_n to satisfy this condition in practice.

3. **Methodological and Experimental Issues:**
   - The simulation study (Section 4) lacks comparison with baseline methods (e.g., standard additive models, functional ANOVA). Without such comparisons, it's difficult to assess whether the proposed method offers meaningful improvements.
   - In Table 2, the performance for Model 1 deteriorates when using Algorithm 1 (with validation), but the authors don't provide an explanation for this unexpected result.
   - The stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2) is described as "greedy" but lacks a clear explanation of how to handle ties or when multiple group splits yield similar performance.
   - The real data application (Section 5) fails to provide any comparison with alternative methods to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach.

4. **Formatting and Presentation Issues:**
   - Inconsistent spacing in mathematical expressions (e.g., "O ( n−d/(2d+p) )" vs "O(n^(-d/(2d+p)))").
   - Inconsistent use of "group additive structure" vs "group additive model" throughout the paper.
   - The reference list has formatting inconsistencies (e.g., some entries include "pages" while others don't).
   - The paper incorrectly cites "st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)" in the header, which is an error for a current review.

**Recommendation**

While the paper addresses an important problem in nonparametric regression and makes a theoretically interesting contribution, the numerous technical inconsistencies, theoretical gaps, and methodological shortcomings significantly undermine its scientific contribution. The paper would benefit from a more rigorous treatment of the complexity measure, clear guidance on parameter selection, and meaningful comparisons with baseline methods in the experimental section.

The most concerning issue is the ambiguous penalty term (Equation 6) due to the undefined α parameter, which makes the method practically unusable without additional clarification. The theoretical results also lack sufficient detail for reproducibility.

Given these substantial issues, I recommend rejection. The paper's core idea has potential, but the current version does not present a sufficiently rigorous or complete solution to the problem it aims to solve. The authors would need to significantly revise the paper to address these technical and methodological concerns before it would be suitable for publication at NIPS.

**Pro/Con Summary**

*Pro:*
- Clear theoretical foundation for the intrinsic group structure
- Novel approach to group additive structure identification
- Well-motivated problem statement

*Con:*
- Ambiguous complexity measure with undefined parameters
- Theoretical results lack practical guidance for implementation
- Experimental results lack meaningful comparisons
- Numerous technical inconsistencies throughout the paper
- Incomplete treatment of the theoretical foundations
