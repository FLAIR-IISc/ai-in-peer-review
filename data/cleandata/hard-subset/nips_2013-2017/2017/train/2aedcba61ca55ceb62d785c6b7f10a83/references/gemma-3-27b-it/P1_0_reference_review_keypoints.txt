- Tackles identification of group additive structure in nonparametric regression and defines the “intrinsic group additive structure.”
- Proposes a penalty-based kernel method combining empirical risk, RKHS norm, and a novel covering-number-based complexity penalty for group structures.
- Provides consistency proofs and theoretical guarantees for recovering the true structure under stated assumptions.
- Empirically validated with simulations and a Boston Housing application; visualizations of estimated functions are informative.
- Builds on OKGT [11], connects to graphical model literature, and uses standard covering-number RKHS complexity results.
- Strength: Addresses an important problem for interpretability and efficiency in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
- Strength: Formal definition of intrinsic structure gives a clear identification target.
- Strength: Introduces a well-motivated novel complexity measure for penalizing group structures.
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees (consistency) are a major positive.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation making ideas accessible.
- Weakness: Exhaustive search (Algorithm 1) is computationally infeasible for high-dimensional problems.
- Weakness: Greedy stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2) can be unreliable and may miss the true structure.
- Weakness: Method is sensitive to tuning parameters (λ, µ, α) with limited practical guidance beyond cross-validation.
- Weakness: Performance depends on kernel/RKHS choice, but the paper lacks comparative analysis of kernels.
- Weakness: Consistency relies on several assumptions; robustness to violations is not thoroughly discussed.
- Argument for acceptance: Significant theoretical contributions, practical effectiveness, and clear presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: Scalability and robustness concerns (exhaustive search impracticality, stepwise limitations, tuning sensitivity).
- Overall: Strong paper worthy of acceptance but would benefit from algorithmic refinements and more discussion of practical considerations.
- Recommendation: Accept; Confidence: 8/10.
