- Addresses identification of intrinsic group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, resolving non-uniqueness of groupings.
- Defines "intrinsic group additive structure" as the minimal amiable group structure and provides a principled target for identification.
- Proposes an RKHS-complexity-based structural penalty to select the intrinsic group structure.
- Provides rigorous theoretical development with clear definitions and proofs (Theorems 2–4), including consistency of the estimated structure.
- Complexity measure derived from covering numbers of RKHSs to penalize overly complex groupings.
- Empirical validation is thorough: comprehensive simulations across models and a Boston Housing real-data example showing successful recovery.
- Presents a two-step estimation procedure with Algorithms 1 and 2; algorithmic details are sufficiently described.
- Stepwise (greedy) Algorithm 2 has limitations (e.g., poor performance in some models) that are acknowledged but not fully addressed.
- Writing and organization are clear overall, though Section 2.3.2 and derivation of C(G) may be hard for readers unfamiliar with RKHS.
- Original contribution: novel intrinsic structure concept and use of RKHS complexity as a structural penalty, extending prior group additive work.
- Clearly distinguishes the approach from graphical-model/conditional-dependence methods.
- Significant practical importance for mitigating the curse of dimensionality while preserving interpretability and allowing interactions.
- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel penalty, consistency results, comprehensive experiments, and clear relation to prior literature.
- Weaknesses: algorithmic limitations, need for more intuitive explanation of C(G), missing computational complexity discussion, and potential tuning sensitivity.
- Overall recommendation: strong, significant paper recommended for acceptance despite some algorithmic and exposition weaknesses.
