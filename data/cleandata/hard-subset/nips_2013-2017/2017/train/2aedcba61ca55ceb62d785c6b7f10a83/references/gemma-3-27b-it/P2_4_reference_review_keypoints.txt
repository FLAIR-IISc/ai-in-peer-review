- Problem: Identifies group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression to improve interpretability and mitigate the curse of dimensionality.  
- Approach: Proposes a penalty-based RKHS method to recover the intrinsic (most concise) group additive representation.  
- Algorithms: Develops exhaustive-search and stepwise algorithms for structure identification.  
- Theory: Provides a rigorous theoretical framework, definition of intrinsic structure, and a consistency proof using covering-number-based complexity penalties.  
- Empirical validation: Simulations and a real-data application show the method can recover true group structures and is practically useful.  
- Connection to related work: Clarifies relationship to graphical models and highlights advantage of directly targeting group additive structures.  
- Strength — importance: Tackles an important and practical problem for high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Strength — novelty: Combines RKHS theory and complexity penalization to target intrinsic group additive structure, a novel contribution.  
- Strength — methodology: Penalized kernel regression is well-formulated and builds on established techniques.  
- Weakness — implementation complexity: Practical implementation may be complex due to kernel choices, multiple tuning parameters (λ, µ, α), and potentially costly exhaustive search.  
- Weakness — scalability: Stepwise algorithm’s scalability to very high dimensions is not convincingly demonstrated; performance limited in some simulations.  
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: Method shows significant sensitivity to µ and α, needing more robust or adaptive tuning strategies.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Consistency relies on specific kernel and smoothness assumptions; robustness to violations is not discussed.  
- Weakness — comparisons: Experimental section lacks direct comparisons to existing nonparametric regression or structure-identification methods.  
- Review criteria — quality/climate: Technically sound and rigorous, but implementation complexity and tuning sensitivity raise practical concerns.  
- Review criteria — clarity: Generally well-written but theoretical sections are dense and need more intuitive explanations of the complexity measure.  
- Review criteria — significance: Significant potential impact due to improved interpretability and statistical efficiency in high-dimensional settings.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically strong, and empirically promising work deserving acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns about implementation complexity, tuning sensitivity, limited scalability evidence, and restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions including guidance on parameter tuning, robustness discussion, experimental comparisons, and addressing scalability concerns.
