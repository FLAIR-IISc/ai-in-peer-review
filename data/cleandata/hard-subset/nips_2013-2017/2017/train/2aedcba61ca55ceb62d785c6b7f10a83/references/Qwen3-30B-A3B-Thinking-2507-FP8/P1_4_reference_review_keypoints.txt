- Summary: Proposes identifying an intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression using a penalized kernel method with a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers, offering theoretical consistency and empirical validation.  
- Builds on OKGT and fills gaps in non-uniqueness and systematic identification of group structures, positioning group additive models between additive models and functional ANOVA.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical contribution: defines "intrinsic group additive structure" and proves consistency with amiable structures and a partial order framework.  
- Novel complexity measure: uses RKHS covering numbers to quantify group structure complexity and justifies this choice over other capacity measures.  
- Practical algorithms: provides exhaustive search and backward stepwise algorithms, with the stepwise method suitable for higher dimensions.  
- Empirical validation: simulations and a real-data application demonstrate successful recovery of group structures across settings.  
- Clear problem formulation: effectively motivates the middle-ground model balancing interpretability and flexibility.

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparison: lacks thorough empirical and methodological comparisons to other structure-learning and sparsity-based nonparametric approaches.  
- Implementation detail gaps: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability, especially for the stepwise algorithm.  
- Theoretical assumptions: key conditions (e.g., λ_n * n → 0) and kernel/true-function assumptions are not fully justified or explained.  
- Model selection sensitivity: insufficient exploration of sensitivity to validation-set size and hyperparameter ranges.  
- Real-data interpretation: the Boston Housing results lack deeper domain-specific explanation for the identified groups.

Specific comments for authors:
- Provide more detailed justification and practical guidance for choosing λ_n to satisfy theoretical conditions.  
- Expand comparisons to related methods (e.g., Salsa, sparse additive models) and discuss differences in performance and assumptions.  
- Include complexity analysis of the backward stepwise algorithm and practical limits of exhaustive search.  
- Report sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (α, μ) and validation-set choices.  
- Offer richer domain-specific interpretation of the real-data groupings (Boston Housing) to strengthen applied relevance.

- Originality: 5/5.  
- Significance: 4/5.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance: technically sound, novel, empirically validated, and of interest to the ML community despite some areas needing clarification.
