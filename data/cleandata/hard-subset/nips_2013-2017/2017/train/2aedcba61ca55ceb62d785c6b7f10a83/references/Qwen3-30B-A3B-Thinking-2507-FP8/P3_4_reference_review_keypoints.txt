- Tackles high-dimensional nonparametric regression by identifying an "intrinsic group additive structure" that allows within-group interactions but blocks across-group interactions.  
- Main contributions: (1) formal definition and proof of existence/uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure, (2) a structural penalty based on RKHS covering numbers to identify that structure.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: clear definitions (Theorem 1), existence/uniqueness, and consistency results (Theorem 4).  
- Insightful complexity measure derivation C(G)=∑α|uj|+1 linking RKHS capacity to group structure complexity.  
- Technical gaps: no practical guidance on kernel selection or its effect on learned groups.  
- Technical gaps: analysis assumes the true function has group additive structure; performance under misspecification is not studied.  
- Technical gaps: lacks empirical comparison to state-of-the-art high-dimensional nonparametric methods (e.g., ESP kernel, related prior work).  
- Well-organized and clearly written; algorithms (Algorithm 1 and 2) are described clearly.  
- Confusing presentation around graphical models—need clearer distinction and motivation for targeting group additive structure over conditional dependence.  
- Novelty: the intrinsic group additive concept and RKHS-based structural penalty are original and address limitations of OKGT, but relation to similar block-structure methods is insufficiently distinguished.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical gap between additive models and full ANOVA, offering automatic group identification for improved interpretability and feasibility.  
- Empirics are promising (simulations and Boston Housing), but the paper should better demonstrate interpretability and predictive gains versus standard approaches.  
- Major weaknesses: limited comparisons with related methods, lack of kernel/tuning/categorical-variable guidance, unclear scalability to p >> n and unquantified complexity of the stepwise algorithm, and no discussion of misspecification robustness.  
- Strengths summary: solid theoretical foundation, proven consistency, practical estimation algorithms, and useful empirical validation that addresses a real shortcoming in prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; requested changes include broader method comparisons, clearer relation to graphical/block models, practical kernel/tuning guidance, and discussion of limitations under model misspecification.
