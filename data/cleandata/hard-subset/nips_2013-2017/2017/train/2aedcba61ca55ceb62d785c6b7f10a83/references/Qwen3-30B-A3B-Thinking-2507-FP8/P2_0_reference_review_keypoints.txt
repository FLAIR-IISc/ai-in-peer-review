- Addresses identification of group additive structures between standard additive models and full interaction (functional ANOVA) models.  
- Introduces the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the minimal partition representing the true function and proves its uniqueness.  
- Proposes a penalized kernel method using RKHS covering-number–based complexity to identify group structure.  
- Provides rigorous consistency results (Theorems 2–4) supporting the method.  
- Presents two algorithms for structure search: exhaustive search and backward stepwise.  
- Empirically validates the method via five simulation models and a Boston Housing real-data application.  
- Novel theoretical contribution: formal definition and uniqueness proof of intrinsic group additive structure.  
- Innovative complexity measure: uses RKHS covering numbers to quantify group structure capacity.  
- Strong theoretical grounding: well-justified and rigorous proofs.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation that documents successes and limitations (e.g., Model 1).  
- Real-data example yields interpretable groupings consistent with domain knowledge.  
- Clear positioning relative to prior work, distinguishing from graphical models and functional ANOVA.  
- Weakness: No concrete computational complexity analysis for exhaustive search as p grows.  
- Weakness: Underdeveloped tuning-parameter selection strategy; relies on grid search without guidance for high dimensions.  
- Weakness: Performance degradation in Model 1 is noted but not thoroughly explained.  
- Weakness: Connection to graphical models is mentioned but not fully explored or leveraged.  
- Weakness: Little discussion of scenarios with many small groups and potential bias from the C(G)=∑α^{|u_j|} complexity measure.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound with substantial theoretical and empirical support.  
- Significance: Meaningful advance offering a practical middle ground for high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Recommendation: Accept—meets NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance).  
- Suggested improvements: Add discussion/analysis of computational complexity and more guidance on tuning parameter selection in the camera-ready.
