- Proposes a penalized kernel ridge regression method to identify group additive structure and estimate nonparametric functions simultaneously.
- Introduces a complexity penalty based on covering numbers of RKHSs and proves consistency for identified group structure.
- Offers both exhaustive search and a scalable stepwise algorithm for group identification.
- Validated via simulations and a real-data application (Boston Housing) showing recoverability and interpretability of groups.
- Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, and higher-order interaction work, addressing gaps in OKGT regarding identifiability and systematic grouping.
- Connects to graphical models but focuses directly on group additive structure rather than conditional dependence.
- Theoretical foundation is strong, with novel use of covering numbers for complexity control.
- Clear problem formulation and mostly clear mathematical notation, though RKHS concepts may challenge some readers.
- Exhaustive search is computationally expensive and may not scale to high-dimensional settings.
- The stepwise algorithm’s performance and justification relative to exhaustive search need further study.
- Method depends on hyperparameters (λ, µ, α) with limited practical guidance and unknown sensitivity.
- Theoretical results assume universal kernels; the effect of different kernel choices is not thoroughly explored.
- Real-data evaluation is limited to one dataset, reducing evidence of generalizability.
- Detailed theoretical derivations (covering numbers) are largely in supplementary material, making assessment harder.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers theoretical novelty, practical algorithms, and positive empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, hyperparameter tuning issues, and limited real-data analysis.
- Overall assessment: solid, theoretically justified contribution that outweighs weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: provide hyperparameter selection guidance/sensitivity analysis, further evaluate stepwise scalability vs exhaustive search, test on more real datasets, and summarize key theoretical results more accessibly in the main text.
