- Problem addressed: Identifies the "intrinsic group additive structure" for high-dimensional nonparametric regression, between additive models and full ANOVA.  
- Main contributions: (1) formal definition of intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group representation; (2) complexity-penalized identification method using RKHS/kernel theory.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides careful mathematical foundation, partial order on group structures, and proof of existence/uniqueness of the intrinsic structure.  
- Consistency result: Theorem 4 shows the proposed method consistently recovers the intrinsic group structure as sample size grows.  
- Complexity measure: Introduces C(G)=∑α^{|u_j|+1} based on RKHS covering numbers and justifies choosing covering numbers over other capacity measures.  
- Methodological innovation: Novel application of learning-theoretic capacity measures to structure identification in nonparametric regression.  
- Empirical validation: Simulations and Boston Housing application demonstrate successful recovery of group structures and interpretable results aligned with domain knowledge.  
- Computational limitation: Exhaustive search (Algorithm 1) is infeasible for p>20; backward stepwise (Algorithm 2) is practical but greedy and can fail (e.g., Model 4).  
- Need for practical guidance: Paper should offer clearer guidance on algorithm choice or propose hybrid/scaleable approaches.  
- Interpretation shortfall: Real-data results show patterns but lack deeper analysis of their implications for housing markets.  
- Relation to prior work: Could better compare and position method relative to other high-dimensional nonparametric approaches (e.g., Salsa, sparse additive models) and graphical models.  
- Overall evaluation: Technically excellent, well-written, original, and significant; advances structured nonparametric regression with practical interpretability benefits.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
