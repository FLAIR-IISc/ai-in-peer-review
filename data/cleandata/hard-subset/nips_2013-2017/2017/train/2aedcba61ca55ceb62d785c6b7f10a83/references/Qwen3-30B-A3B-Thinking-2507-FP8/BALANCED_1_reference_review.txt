

Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper addresses the problem of identifying group additive structures in nonparametric regression, proposing a method to find the "intrinsic" group additive structure that is minimal among all possible correct structures. The authors define the intrinsic structure rigorously, develop a structural penalty based on RKHS covering numbers, and prove consistency of the proposed method. The work builds on previous group additive models like OKGT [11] but addresses key gaps in structure identification and non-uniqueness.

Strengths:
1. Theoretical contribution: The paper's most significant strength is the rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure and proof of its uniqueness. This solves a fundamental limitation in previous group additive models where structures were not unique, leading to nonidentifiability.

2. Methodological innovation: The authors propose a novel complexity measure using RKHS covering numbers that provides a principled way to balance model complexity and fit. The penalty term C(G) = ∑α^|u_j|+1 is well-justified through theoretical connections to RKHS capacity.

3. Empirical validation: The simulation studies demonstrate successful identification of true group structures across multiple model settings. The Boston Housing application shows practical utility with interpretable visualizations of estimated nonparametric functions.

4. Clear distinction from related work: The paper carefully differentiates its approach from graphical models, noting that it targets group additive structure directly rather than the more complex conditional dependence structure.

Weaknesses:
1. Computational scalability: While Algorithm 2 (backward stepwise) addresses computational concerns, the exhaustive search approach (Algorithm 1) becomes infeasible for large p. The paper doesn't adequately address the practical limitations for very high-dimensional problems.

2. Limited comparison with alternatives: The simulation studies don't compare the proposed method with related approaches like functional ANOVA models or graphical models. A comparative analysis would strengthen empirical claims.

3. Kernel choice implications: The theoretical analysis assumes universal kernels, but the paper doesn't discuss how kernel choice affects performance or how to select appropriate kernels for different problems.

4. Tuning parameter guidance: The paper mentions using validation sets for parameter selection but provides limited practical guidance on choosing α and μ, which are crucial for implementation.

Recommendations for improvement:
1. Include comparisons with alternative methods (functional ANOVA, graphical models) in the simulation studies to better demonstrate relative advantages.

2. Expand the discussion of computational complexity and scalability, potentially suggesting additional algorithmic improvements for very high-dimensional settings.

3. Add a section discussing how kernel choice affects results and providing recommendations for kernel selection in different scenarios.

4. Provide more specific guidance on tuning parameter selection, including practical strategies for choosing α and μ.

Overall evaluation:
The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to high-dimensional nonparametric regression by rigorously defining and identifying the intrinsic group additive structure. The method is technically sound with strong theoretical foundations, and the empirical results demonstrate practical utility. The paper clearly addresses a meaningful gap in the literature.

While the computational limitations and limited comparisons with alternative methods are notable weaknesses, they don't undermine the paper's core theoretical contribution. The paper meets NIPS' high standards for technical quality and originality.

The authors should address the suggested improvements to strengthen the empirical claims and practical relevance. With these revisions, the paper would make a strong contribution to the field.

Recommendation: Accept
