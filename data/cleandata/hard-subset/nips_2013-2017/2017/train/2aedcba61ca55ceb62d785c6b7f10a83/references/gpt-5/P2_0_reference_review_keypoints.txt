- Paper studies nonparametric regression with group-additive structure, defines an intrinsic minimal grouping, proposes a structure-penalized kernel estimator using covering-number-based penalties, proves selection consistency, and gives algorithms plus experiments.

- Positioned between classical additive models and functional ANOVA; builds on OKGT and RKHS capacity results; novelty is applying covering-number bounds to select additive groupings in direct-sum RKHSs.

- Theoretical idea is sensible: penalize a capacity proxy growing with group dimensionality and show the minimal (intrinsic) grouping is selected asymptotically.

- Theorem 1 (existence/uniqueness of intrinsic structure) is under-specified: it omits conditions ensuring the meet of amiable partitions is amiable (e.g., independence/product-measure assumptions).

- Penalty derivation is unclear and possibly incorrect: an exponent drops from |u|+1 to |u| without justification, and reparameterization of α severs the link to the covering-number bound.

- Key assumptions are not fully enumerated (kernel type, domain boundedness, noise distribution, input measure, and scaling/choice of λn, µ, α), yet these affect consistency claims.

- Computational formulation is incomplete: a representer-theorem-based finite-dimensional form for the direct-sum RKHS problem is needed, exhaustive search is exponential, and the backward stepwise heuristic has no guarantees.

- Empirical evaluation is limited: shows recovery frequencies but lacks predictive metrics (MSE), comparisons to relevant baselines (SALSA, sparse additive models, spline-ANOVA, BART, HSIC), stability analysis, and larger-p scalability experiments.

- Presentation issues: missing/partial references, typos, inconsistent notation, and unclear exposition of the penalty; proofs or proof sketches for key claims should be provided.

- Originality: formalizing intrinsic group additive structure and penalizing RKHS capacity in this setting are nice contributions, but connections to related hierarchical/interaction-selection work need clearer differentiation.

- Significance: promising for interpretable interaction modeling if theoretical gaps and computational scalability are fixed; current tuning sensitivity and limited empirical support reduce immediate impact.

- Pros: clear motivation, appealing intrinsic-structure concept, RKHS-based structural penalty with some theoretical backing, simple algorithms and encouraging preliminary empirical results.

- Cons: theoretical gaps (intrinsic structure proof, penalty exponent change), unclear guidance on tuning (µ, α), computational intractability at scale, and weak empirical validation.

- Suggested improvements: state and prove Theorem 1 under explicit conditions, fix/justify the penalty derivation and specify µ/α scaling with n, provide a representer-theorem formulation and complexity analysis, add competitive baselines and predictive metrics, and develop principled scalable search strategies.

- Overall recommendation: Weak reject — direction is timely and promising but requires stronger theoretical clarity, correct penalty justification, and more thorough empirical validation.
