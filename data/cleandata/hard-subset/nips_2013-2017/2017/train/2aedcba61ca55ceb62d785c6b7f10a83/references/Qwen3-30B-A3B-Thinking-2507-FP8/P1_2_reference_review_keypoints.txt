- Addresses identification of group additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, balancing flexibility and interpretability.
- Defines "intrinsic group additive structure" and proves its uniqueness.
- Proposes a structural penalty method based on RKHS covering numbers to identify group structure.
- Establishes theoretical consistency for the proposed method (Theorems 1 and 4).
- Provides two algorithms for implementation: exhaustive search and backward stepwise.
- Builds on OKGT and fills gaps: resolves non-uniqueness and provides systematic identification.
- Situates work relative to additive models and functional ANOVA, offering a middle ground between simple additive and high-order interaction models.
- Simulation studies (five models) show accurate recovery of true group structure.
- Real-data application (Boston Housing) demonstrates interpretability and practical utility.
- Backward stepwise algorithm improves practicality for larger p.
- Exhaustive search is computationally prohibitive for moderate p (e.g., p > 20) and limits scalability.
- Parameter tuning (µ, α) relies on grid search and validation set without clear practical guidance or sensitivity analysis.
- Lacks empirical comparison to alternative interaction/structure identification methods (e.g., graphical models).
- Theoretical results depend on specific kernel and structural assumptions that need clearer discussion for real-world applicability.
- Discussion of relationship to graphical models is brief and underdeveloped.
- Overall assessment: theoretically sound, practically implementable, and empirically validated contribution.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Pro arguments: novel concept with uniqueness proof, consistency results, practical algorithms, strong experiments, clear motivation and literature connection.
- Con arguments: scalability of exhaustive search, limited method comparisons, insufficient parameter selection guidance, and underdeveloped link to graphical models.
