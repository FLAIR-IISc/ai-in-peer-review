- Addresses identification of group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression to improve interpretability and reduce dimensionality.  
- Proposes a penalty-based kernel ridge regression approach using covering numbers to quantify group-structure complexity.  
- Defines an "intrinsic group additive structure" and provides a consistency proof for the method.  
- Provides two algorithms for structure identification: exhaustive search and backward stepwise.  
- Empirical validation via simulations and the Boston Housing dataset shows effective recovery of group structures.  
- Connects the approach to graphical models and highlights differences/advantages.  
- Covering number computation may be computationally expensive, potentially limiting scalability.  
- Method performance appears sensitive to regularization/tuning parameters (λ, µ, α); more tuning strategy and sensitivity analysis needed.  
- Backward stepwise algorithm is greedy and can miss optimal structures; alternative optimization approaches should be explored.  
- Real-data validation is limited (only Boston Housing); more complex, higher-dimensional datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Some theoretical sections (e.g., Proposition 3, Theorem 2) are dense and need clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Quality: 8/10 — technically sound with rigorous theory, but computational and tuning issues temper the score.  
- Clarity: 7/10 — generally well-written but theoretical derivations can be dense.  
- Originality: 8/10 — novel integration of kernels, group additive modeling, and covering-number regularization.  
- Significance: 7/10 — promising and relevant, though practical adoption may be limited by computational/tuning challenges.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem, offers rigorous theory, practical algorithms, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational complexity, tuning sensitivity, stepwise algorithm limitations, and limited real-data scope.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting more discussion of computational costs and stepwise algorithm limitations.
