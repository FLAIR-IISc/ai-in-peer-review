- Tackles identification of group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression to balance flexibility and interpretability.  
- Proposes penalized kernel ridge regression with a novel covering-number-based complexity penalty to identify groups and estimate group-wise functions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, including consistency proofs.  
- Demonstrates empirical success: simulations recover true group structures and a real-data application shows practical utility.  
- Develops both exhaustive-search and stepwise algorithms for different problem sizes.  
- Strength: addresses an important practical problem in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Strength: sound and novel theoretical foundation using covering numbers as a complexity measure.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and thoughtful connections to additive and graphical models.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments that validate the method.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is dense and could use more intuition about the complexity measure.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of sensitivity to tuning parameters (µ, α).  
- Weakness: stepwise algorithm underperforms in some scenarios (e.g., Model 4), raising robustness concerns.  
- Weakness: exhaustive search is computationally expensive; stepwise alternative is not consistently reliable.  
- Weakness: unclear how well the complexity penalty translates to practical real-world performance.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add intuitive explanations for the complexity measure, analyze parameter sensitivity, and further investigate stepwise algorithm performance.
