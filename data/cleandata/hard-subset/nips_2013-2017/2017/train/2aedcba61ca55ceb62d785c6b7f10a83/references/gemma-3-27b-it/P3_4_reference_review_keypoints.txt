- Problem: Identifies group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression to balance flexibility and interpretability.
- Contribution: Defines an “intrinsic” group additive structure to resolve non-uniqueness and proposes a penalty-based kernel method to identify structure and estimate functions.
- Theory: Provides theoretical guarantees (consistency, Theorem 4) grounded in RKHS theory.
- Complexity measure: Introduces a novel complexity penalty based on covering numbers of RKHSs.
- Algorithms: Offers exhaustive-search and a more scalable stepwise algorithm for structure identification.
- Empirics: Validated via simulations and a Boston Housing real-data example with informative visualizations.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, and kernel/RKHS literature while distinguishing itself from graphical-model approaches.
- Strength — motivation: Addresses a practical high-dimensional tradeoff between interpretability and model flexibility.
- Strength — rigor: Presents a principled definition and theoretical analysis of the problem.
- Strength — novelty: Combines covering-number-based complexity control with kernel methods for structure identification.
- Strength — practical: Provides both exhaustive and heuristic search methods and empirical evidence of effectiveness.
- Strength — presentation: Generally well-written with clear notation and logical flow.
- Weakness — accessibility: Theory is dense and requires substantial RKHS background; more intuition is needed.
- Weakness — computation: Exhaustive search is infeasible in high dimensions; stepwise has limited performance on complex cases.
- Weakness — tuning: Insufficient exploration of kernel choice and sensitivity to tuning parameter λ.
- Weakness — comparisons: Limited empirical comparison to alternative group- or variable-selection methods (e.g., sparse graphical models).
- Weakness — real-data analysis: Interpretation of the Boston Housing results is somewhat superficial.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound approach with empirical support and relevance to the NIPS audience.
- Against acceptance: Practical applicability limited by computational cost and incomplete empirical sensitivity/comparison analyses.
- Overall: Strong paper with valuable contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
