

# Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

## Summary
This paper addresses a critical limitation of group additive models in high-dimensional nonparametric regression: the non-uniqueness of group structures and the lack of a systematic method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure. The authors define the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group structure that can represent the target function, establish theoretical properties of this structure, and propose a structure-penalized kernel method for its identification using RKHS covering numbers as a complexity measure. They provide theoretical consistency results and two algorithms (exhaustive search and backward stepwise) for implementation. The method is evaluated through simulations and a real data application on the Boston Housing dataset.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with rigorous mathematical foundations. The authors clearly define the problem, establish the existence and uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure (Theorem 1), and prove the consistency of their method (Theorem 4). The theoretical results are well-supported, with proofs provided in the supplementary material. The empirical evaluation is thorough, including five synthetic models with different structures and a real data application. The paper acknowledges limitations, such as the greedy nature of the stepwise algorithm that might miss the optimal structure.

The only minor quality concern is the somewhat abrupt transition from the theoretical complexity measure to the practical implementation. While the authors justify using covering numbers as a capacity measure, a more detailed explanation of why this particular measure is optimal for their specific problem would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem statement to theoretical results to empirical evaluation. The mathematical definitions are precise and well-motivated. The authors effectively use examples to illustrate key concepts (e.g., the example in Section 2.1 showing different amiable structures).

The paper could be slightly improved by:
1. Providing more intuition about why the specific complexity measure (based on covering numbers) is appropriate for group additive structures
2. Including a brief discussion of computational complexity for the proposed algorithms
3. Adding a figure to visually illustrate the relationship between group structures and the intrinsic structure

## Originality
This paper makes a significant original contribution by addressing the non-uniqueness problem in group additive models, which has been overlooked in previous work (including the OKGT method by Pan et al.). The concept of "intrinsic group additive structure" is novel, and the authors establish it rigorously through the partial order of group structures. The structure-penalized kernel method that incorporates the RKHS-based complexity measure is also original.

The paper adequately references related work, including functional ANOVA models, graphical models, and the OKGT method. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from these alternatives, particularly noting that they directly target the group additive structure rather than the more complex conditional dependence structure considered in graphical models.

## Significance
The problem addressed is highly significant for high-dimensional nonparametric regression, where dimensionality reduction is crucial. The proposed method provides a theoretically grounded solution to a key limitation of group additive models, which has practical implications for improving both estimation efficiency and interpretability in high-dimensional settings.

The empirical results demonstrate the method's effectiveness across diverse model settings, with successful identification of true group structures in simulations and meaningful visualization of estimated functions in the Boston Housing application. The real-world application shows how the method can reveal interpretable patterns in the data (e.g., the relationship between house value and crime rate).

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Addresses a clear and important limitation in group additive models
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundation with consistency proofs
- Develops practical algorithms for implementation
- Demonstrates effectiveness through comprehensive empirical evaluation
- Clearly distinguishes contributions from related work

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of computational complexity for the proposed algorithms
- The choice of complexity measure could be better motivated
- Simulation studies limited to 5 models (more extensive evaluation would strengthen empirical claims)
- No direct comparison with alternative methods for group structure identification

## Recommendation
This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of high-dimensional nonparametric regression. It successfully addresses a significant limitation in group additive models by providing a theoretically sound method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure. The paper is technically rigorous, well-written, and demonstrates practical utility through simulations and a real data application.

The main weaknesses are the limited empirical evaluation and the somewhat abrupt justification for the complexity measure. However, these do not detract from the overall significance of the contribution. The paper advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way and provides a method that could be widely applicable in high-dimensional nonparametric regression problems.

I recommend acceptance at NIPS, as this work meets all the key criteria for a high-quality contribution to the field. The paper's theoretical depth, practical utility, and clear presentation make it a strong candidate for publication.

## Final Assessment

Originality: High (Novel concept of intrinsic group structure, new method)

The authors have identified and solved an important problem that has been overlooked in previous work, providing a theoretically grounded method with practical utility.
