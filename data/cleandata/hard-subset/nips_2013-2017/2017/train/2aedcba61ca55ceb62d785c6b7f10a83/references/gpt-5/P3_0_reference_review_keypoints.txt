- Paper studies nonparametric regression with additive structure over variable groups (partitions G), defines amiable structures and an intrinsic minimal group structure G*, and estimates via RKHS direct-sum with a structural penalty C(G).  
- Relates to additive/GAM, functional ANOVA, SALSA/ESP kernels, RKHS model selection, and prior group-structured kernel/SpAM works (some relevant citations missing).  
- Strength: targets interactions via grouping to avoid combinatorial explosion of high-order terms.  
- Strength: principled attempt to link partition model selection to RKHS capacity via covering-number-derived penalties.  
- Strength: presents selection consistency results for G* (population) and provides exhaustive and greedy search algorithms with empirical illustrations.  
- Major issue: Theorem 1 claiming unique minimal G* is nontrivial and currently lacks required assumptions (e.g., independence or full-support PX); multiple incomparable minimal partitions can exist.  
- Major issue: Structural penalty C(G)=∑u α|u| is weakly justified — constants are absorbed ad hoc, α is treated as a tuning integer, and partitions with same size profile are indistinguishable by C(G).  
- Major issue: Statistical identifiability of partitions is not addressed under correlated designs; correlation can create multiple amiable partitions and jeopardize consistency claims.  
- Major issue: Algorithmic scalability is poor (exhaustive search infeasible for moderate p), backward stepwise is under-specified and lacks guarantees, and empirical instability is observed.  
- Major issue: Experiments are limited — tuning optimistically by maximizing recovery frequency, no comparisons to strong baselines (SALSA, OKGT, SpAM, hierarchical kernel learning), limited predictive/runtime/stability analysis.  
- Concern: Theory assumes specific kernel capacity bounds (e.g., Gaussian-like), but empirical kernels used are not precisely matched or verified against theory.  
- Concern: Presentation and notation are imprecise (least vs. minimal element, density assumptions for universal kernels, informal statements), and some references are incomplete or missing.  
- Pro: Addresses a relevant trade-off between interaction modeling and complexity control and bridges kernel methods with partition selection.  
- Con: Key identifiability/uniqueness claims lack sufficient assumptions, the practical penalty is weakly tied to theory, and algorithmic/empirical evaluation is insufficient.  
- Suggestion: Clarify/strengthen Theorem 1 with precise PX conditions or allow multiple minima and adapt consistency claims to sets of minimal partitions.  
- Suggestion: Tighten link between covering-number theory and practical penalty — consider data-dependent complexities (localized Rademacher) or convex structured penalties that distinguish partitions beyond group sizes.  
- Suggestion: Develop more scalable/robust search methods (beam search, branch-and-bound, convex relaxations) and provide approximation/consistency guarantees.  
- Suggestion: Expand experiments with proper validation (no oracle tuning), include strong baselines, report predictive performance, runtime, and stability across folds.
