- Paper tackles identifying group additive structure in nonparametric regression, balancing interpretability and interaction complexity.
- Proposes a penalized kernel regression that jointly identifies predictor groupings and estimates functions.
- Introduces a novel covering-number-based complexity measure for group structures to penalize model complexity.
- Proves consistency of the proposed group-structure identification method.
- Provides both exhaustive-search and stepwise algorithms for practical implementation.
- Validated via simulation studies and a real-data application showing structure recovery and reasonable prediction.
- Positions work well relative to additive models, functional ANOVA, ESP kernels, OKGT, and graphical models, highlighting unique focus on group additivity.
- Strength: well-motivated problem addressing high-dimensional nonparametric regression challenges.
- Strength: theoretically grounded complexity measure that promotes parsimony.
- Strength: rigorous consistency proof for structure identification.
- Strength: practical algorithmic options and generally clear presentation.
- Weakness: exhaustive search is computationally prohibitive for large p; limited exploration of alternative scalable search strategies.
- Weakness: sensitivity to kernel and bandwidth choices with little practical guidance on selection or tuning.
- Weakness: the covering-number complexity penalty is hard to interpret intuitively for practitioners.
- Weakness: limited real-data analysis and lack of comparisons to other relevant methods.
- Weakness: theory assumes independent predictors and does not address robustness to correlated predictors.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and theoretically solid contribution with practical caveats.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theory, practical relevance, clear presentation, empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, interpretability of complexity measure, limited kernel/bandwidth guidance, independence assumption limits applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: analyze computational complexity and explore scalable search strategies; provide kernel/bandwidth selection guidance or adaptive methods; give an intuitive explanation of the complexity measure; expand and compare real-data analyses; discuss limitations with correlated predictors.
