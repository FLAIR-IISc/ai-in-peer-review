- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution with strong theory; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: Proposes a penalized method to identify group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Main idea: Introduces a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers to penalize group structures and prevent overfitting.  
- Main idea: Formulates simultaneous estimation of group structure and component functions via a penalized regression problem.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, higher-order interaction models, and OKGT, addressing OKGT's lack of structure identification.  
- Relation to prior work: Leverages statistical learning theory tools like covering numbers and Rademacher complexity for theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation including definition of intrinsic group additive structure and consistency results.  
- Strength: Novel, principled complexity measure using RKHS covering numbers.  
- Strength: Tackles an important problem—identifying interactions in nonparametric regression—using a data-driven approach.  
- Strength: Algorithms (exhaustive and stepwise) are clearly presented for different scenarios.  
- Strength: Empirical validation via simulations and a Boston Housing application shows promising recovery of true group structures.  
- Weakness: Exhaustive search is computationally expensive and may not scale to high dimensions.  
- Weakness: Stepwise algorithm may perform suboptimally in complex scenarios; more efficient search strategies needed.  
- Weakness: Method is sensitive to tuning parameters (λ, μ, α); parameter selection lacks systematic treatment and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Limited real-data evaluation—more diverse datasets would strengthen conclusions.  
- Weakness: Reliance on universal kernels for consistency; practical impact of kernel choice not thoroughly discussed.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 unclear about jointly optimizing (µ, α) and the dependent estimated group Ĝ.  
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated, theoretically sound method with clear exposition and supportive empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Practical concerns about computational cost, tuning sensitivity, and limited real-data analysis.  
- Revision requests: Improve discussion/solutions for computational efficiency, provide detailed parameter tuning strategy, expand real-data experiments, clarify Algorithm 1’s optimization, and discuss kernel choice impact.
