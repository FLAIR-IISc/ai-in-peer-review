- Problem: Identifies group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression to improve efficiency and interpretability in high dimensions.
- Contribution: Proposes an “intrinsic” minimal group structure and a penalized kernel ridge regression with a novel RKHS covering-number complexity penalty.
- Algorithms: Develops both exhaustive search (for small p) and a stepwise algorithm (for larger p) for structure identification.
- Theory: Provides consistency guarantees for the proposed method.
- Empirics: Demonstrates feasibility via simulations and a Boston Housing data application.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem of variable grouping for interpretability and efficiency.
- Strength: Well-motivated middle ground between fully additive models and functional ANOVA; intrinsic structure concept is useful.
- Strength: Solid theoretical foundation with consistency results and a principled complexity measure.
- Strength: Algorithmic contributions (exhaustive and stepwise) increase applicability.
- Strength: Empirical validation shows method works in simulations and real data.

- Weakness: Complexity measure based on covering numbers is computationally demanding and hard to interpret; practical impact and sensitivity to tuning parameter α are unclear.
- Weakness: Exhaustive search is only feasible for small p; stepwise algorithm may get stuck in local optima and its performance is not rigorously evaluated.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative complexity measures (e.g., model-size or information-criterion based).
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to existing variable selection/grouping methods in nonparametric settings.
- Weakness: Real-data results (Boston Housing) are described briefly and lack detailed interpretation.

- Overall: Technically sound and novel with potential impact, but computational scalability and empirical comparisons need strengthening.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, strong theoretical guarantees, and potential practical impact for interpretability.
- Arguments against acceptance: Scalability concerns, limited empirical evaluation against baselines, and unclear practical utility of the complexity measure.
- Rating: Weak Accept.
