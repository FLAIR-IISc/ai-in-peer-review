- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression where predictor variables are partitioned into groups and the group structure must be learned from data.

- Method: novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group structure and within-group estimates.

- Two algorithms are proposed to approximately solve the combined optimization over groups and functions.

- Theory: rates for empirical risk approaching true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure (probability → 1 as n → ∞).

- Experiments: empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method.

- Key innovation: quantifying complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.

- Overall assessment: comprehensive, well-motivated method with solid theory and experiments; potentially significant if literature context is complete.

- Main limitation: optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large.

- Writing: generally clear but marred by numerous typos.
