- Studies high-dimensional kernel regression via a "group additive model" that groups features to model correlations ignored by standard additive models.  
- Provides a formal treatment and proposes ad hoc algorithms for finding optimal feature groupings.  
- Theoretical contribution: defines a complexity measure (favoring many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The complexity measure is not easily optimizable; authors resort to exhaustive search (poor scaling) or a greedy forward-selection approach.  
- Reviewer disappointed the measure couldn’t be optimized more efficiently and that alternative measures are only mentioned without substantive insight.  
- Related work coverage is poor: missing links to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.  
- Experiments are thin—small synthetic cases and Boston Housing—there are no baselines or comparisons to other methods.  
- Post-rebuttal: some clarifications addressed concerns, but experiments and literature discussion remain insufficient; reviewer urges stronger related-work treatment.
