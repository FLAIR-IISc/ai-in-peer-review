- Tackles high-dimensional nonparametric regression by extending additive models to a “group additive model” to capture correlated features.  
- Core idea of grouping features based on relationships is intuitive and potentially valuable.  
- Provides formalization of “intrinsic group additive structure” and a penalized kernel method for joint structure identification and estimation.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure (upper bound on covering number) to guide grouping.  
- Builds on additive modeling and functional ANOVA but poorly contextualizes connections to graphical models, structured sparsity, and feature selection.  
- Missing citations and deeper discussion of related work in block-sparse estimation and conditional independence testing.  
- Optimization of the complexity measure is weak: relies on exhaustive search or greedy forward-selection, which are intractable or suboptimal for large problems.  
- Theoretical analysis establishes consistency but doesn’t address practical issues like regularization parameter selection or kernel choice impact.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small synthetic data and Boston Housing, lacking comparisons to established baselines and benchmarks.  
- Post-rebuttal clarifies some theory but does not remedy weak experiments or literature coverage.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant problem and provides a theoretical foundation for group additive structure.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty due to weak connections to related fields, inefficient complexity optimization, weak experimental validation, and poor related-work coverage.  
- Overall recommendation: rejection; needs substantially more experiments, thorough literature review, and discussion of efficient optimization strategies for reconsideration.
