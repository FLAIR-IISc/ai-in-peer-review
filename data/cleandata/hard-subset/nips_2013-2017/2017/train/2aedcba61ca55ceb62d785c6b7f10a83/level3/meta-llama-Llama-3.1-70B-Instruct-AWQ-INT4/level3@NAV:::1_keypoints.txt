- Proposes a novel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression using a RKHS covering-number-based complexity measure and a structure-penalized kernel estimator.
- Rigorously defines intrinsic group additive structure and a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers.
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel method and proves consistency for identifying the true group additive structure.
- Presents two algorithms to find group structure: exhaustive search and greedy forward-selection.
- Complexity measure is not easily optimizable; exhaustive or greedy search may not scale to large datasets.
- Lacks a comprehensive review of related work, especially on structured sparsity, graphical models, and feature selection.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small synthetic examples and the Boston Housing dataset, with no baselines or method comparisons.
- Original contribution is the novel use of RKHS covering-number complexity for group additive structure identification.
- Addresses a significant problem—detecting interactions in high-dimensional nonparametric regression—with potential for improved accuracy and interpretability.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clear in notation, but empirical validation is weak.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve optimizability discussion, expand experiments, and broaden related-work coverage.
