- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs within a kernel ridge regression framework.
- Well-motivated solution addressing additive models’ neglect of variable interactions.
- Core innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers.
- Strong theoretical results proving consistency for estimated group structure and empirical risk approximation.
- Provides two algorithms for practical implementation, acknowledging the combinatorial search challenge.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets supports method effectiveness.
- Major weakness: optimization and computational cost become problematic for large numbers of variables (large p).
