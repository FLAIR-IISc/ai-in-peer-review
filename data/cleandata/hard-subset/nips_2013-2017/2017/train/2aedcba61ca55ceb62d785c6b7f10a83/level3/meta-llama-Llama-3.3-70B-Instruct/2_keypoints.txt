- Proposes group-additive nonparametric regression: additive between groups of variables, filling gap between additive and full nonparametric models.  
- Key innovation: quantifies group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum space.  
- Formulates joint optimization over group structure and regression estimate.  
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the compound optimization problem.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: rates of convergence and consistency of the group-structure estimate.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data shows the method performs effectively on complex datasets.  
- Strengths: original idea, strong technical development, clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Main weakness: optimizing group structure is computationally challenging for large numbers of variables.  
- Additional concerns: writing needs minor clarity fixes and typo corrections.  
- Lacks more detailed comparisons to existing methods and broader exploration of applicability to other domains.  
- Overall: significant contribution whose strengths outweigh weaknesses, recommended for acceptance after revisions.
