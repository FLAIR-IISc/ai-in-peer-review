- Proposes a novel method for group additive nonparametric regression that learns predictor group structure from data.
- Introduces a penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize groups and within-group functions.
- Proposes two algorithms to approximately solve the combined optimization over group structures and functions.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: rates for empirical risk approaching true risk and consistency of estimated group structure (probability → 1 as sample size increases).
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show the method can identify intrinsic group additive structure and estimate nonparametric functions.
- Key innovation: quantifies complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS for principled group learning.
- Provides a clear, well-motivated discussion and situates the work relative to existing nonparametric regression and kernel methods.
- Main limitation: optimization over group structures becomes difficult as the number of variables grows.
- The proposed algorithms partially address scalability, but further research is needed for more efficient, scalable methods.
- Writing is generally clear but contains numerous typos and minor errors that detract from overall quality.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, comprehensive theory and experiments, and has potential to make a significant contribution.

- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns for large variable counts, and minor errors/typos throughout the paper.
