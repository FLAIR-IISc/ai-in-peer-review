- Proposes a group additive model that partitions predictors into groups to model within-group interactions while avoiding full high-dimensional nonparametric complexity.  
- Introduces a formal notion of a unique, minimal "intrinsic group additive structure" and a structure-penalized kernel estimator to identify it.  
- Derives a complexity penalty based on an upper bound on the RKHS covering number and proves consistency for recovering the intrinsic structure.  
- Proposes exhaustive search for very small p and a greedy backward stepwise algorithm for larger p; evaluates on synthetic data and Boston Housing.  
- Strength: tackles a significant problem, offering an intuitive compromise between additive and fully nonparametric models.  
- Strength: strong theoretical contributions—clear definition of target structure and consistency guarantees (Definition 2, Theorem 1, Theorem 4).  
- Strength: original idea of penalizing group structure via RKHS-capacity-derived complexity measure.  
- Weakness: Related-work discussion is limited—misses connections to structured sparsity (e.g., group LASSO), graphical models/Bayesian networks, and interaction-detection literature.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is weak—no baseline comparisons, experiments limited to low-dimensional settings (p ≤ 6), and poor performance on at least one simulated model.  
- Weakness: Optimization is problematic—the proposed penalty is hard to optimize over partitions; exhaustive search is infeasible for moderate p and the greedy algorithm’s details are vague.  
- Weakness: Clarity lacking on why covering numbers were chosen over other capacity measures (Rademacher complexity, VC dimension) and implications of that choice.  
- Overall: Theoretical contributions are solid and novel, but practical significance is questionable without stronger experiments, comparisons, clearer algorithms, and better contextualization; major revision needed.
