**Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"**

This paper proposes a novel approach to identify the intrinsic group additive structure for high-dimensional nonparametric regression analysis. The authors define a complexity measure for group additive structures based on the covering number of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and develop a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper starts by discussing the limitations of traditional additive models in high-dimensional nonparametric regression analysis, which neglects possible interactions between predictor variables. The authors then introduce the concept of group additive models, which can accommodate interactions between variables by grouping them. They rigorously define the intrinsic group additive structure and propose a complexity measure based on the covering number of RKHSs.

The authors develop a structure-penalized kernel method to identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions. They also propose two algorithms, one using exhaustive search and the other employing a greedy forward-selection approach, to find the optimal group structure.

**Strengths**

* The paper provides a novel approach to identify the intrinsic group additive structure for high-dimensional nonparametric regression analysis.
* The authors rigorously define the complexity measure for group additive structures based on the covering number of RKHSs.
* The proposed structure-penalized kernel method is shown to be consistent in identifying the intrinsic group additive structure.

**Weaknesses**

* The complexity measure is not easily optimizable, and the authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection approach, which may not be efficient for large datasets.
* The paper lacks a comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the areas of structured sparsity, graphical models, and feature selection.
* The experimental results are limited to small synthetic cases and the Boston Housing dataset, and there are no baselines or comparisons to other methods.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to identify the intrinsic group additive structure for high-dimensional nonparametric regression analysis. The use of a complexity measure based on the covering number of RKHSs is a new contribution to the field.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant problem in high-dimensional nonparametric regression analysis, which is the identification of interactions between predictor variables. The proposed approach has the potential to improve the accuracy and interpretability of nonparametric regression models.

**Quality**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. However, the experimental results are limited, and the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The notation is well-defined, and the paper is easy to follow.
