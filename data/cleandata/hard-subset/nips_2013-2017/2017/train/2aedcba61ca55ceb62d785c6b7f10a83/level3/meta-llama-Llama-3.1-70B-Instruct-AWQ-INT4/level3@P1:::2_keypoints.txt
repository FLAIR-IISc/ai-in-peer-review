- Proposes a novel method for group additive nonparametric regression that learns predictor groupings from data using RKHS covering-number penalties added to kernel ridge regression.  
- Introduces two approximate algorithms to jointly optimize group structure and within-group functions.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees including convergence rates of empirical to true risk and consistency of recovered group structure.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: novel methodological contribution for group additive nonparametric regression.  
- Strength: solid and comprehensive theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation supports practical effectiveness.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem statement and clear explanation of the approach.  
- Weakness: combinatorial optimization over group structures scales poorly with large numbers of variables.  
- Weakness: paper contains numerous typos that hinder readability.  
- Weakness: minor technical errors (e.g., missing summation in Equation (3)).  
- Relation to prior work: builds on nonparametric regression, kernel methods, and RKHS literature and clarifies distinctions from prior approaches.  
- For acceptance: novel method, strong theory, and empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: scalability of group optimization, typographical issues, and minor errors need correction.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos and minor errors.
