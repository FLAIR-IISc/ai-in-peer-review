- Introduces a method to identify the intrinsic group additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Defines intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal group partition, resolving prior non-uniqueness.  
- Proposes a novel complexity measure based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs, used in a penalized kernel regression framework.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 2–4) establish consistency: estimated group structure converges to the intrinsic structure as sample size grows.  
- Clearly differentiates from functional ANOVA and graphical models by focusing on group additive structure rather than conditional dependence.  
- Experiments show high recovery rates in simulations and produce interpretable findings on the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Provides two algorithms (exhaustive search and backward stepwise), with the stepwise method offering scalability for larger feature sets.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical foundation, clear problem formulation, and comprehensive empirical validation linking theory and practice.  
- Writing contains numerous typos and grammatical errors that require careful proofreading.  
- Technical issues: ambiguous use of "almost surely" (likely asymptotic, needs clarification) and a missing summation in Equation (3).  
- Paper needs a more intuitive explanation for why covering numbers are an appropriate complexity measure for group structures.  
- Main limitation is computational complexity for very high-dimensional problems; more discussion and extensions for scalability are needed.  
- Overall: valuable, publishable contribution with fixable minor technical and presentation issues.
