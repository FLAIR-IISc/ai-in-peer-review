- Tackles group-additive nonparametric regression and the problem of identifying underlying group structure from data.
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method using a novel complexity measure based on covering numbers of RKHSs.
- Provides theoretical guarantees of consistency (Theorems 2, 3, 4).
- Offers algorithms including exhaustive search and a backward stepwise approach for larger datasets.
- Demonstrates method via simulations and a real-data application, showing group structure recovery.
- Motivation is clear: group-additive models balance additive models and higher-order interactions and address identifiability gaps from prior work.
- Briefly discusses connection to graphical models while noting different focus (group structure vs conditional dependence).
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded complexity measure for penalizing group structures.
- Strength: strong theoretical analysis supporting consistency.
- Strength: practical algorithmic proposals and clear mathematical formulation.
- Weakness: exhaustive search algorithm is computationally infeasible for larger datasets.
- Weakness: greedy stepwise algorithm may miss the globally optimal group structure.
- Weakness: paper contains numerous typos and grammatical errors that reduce professionalism.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited and lacks quantitative performance metrics (e.g., prediction error).
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions for consistency may be restrictive in practice.
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of the impact of kernel choice and the tuning parameter λ.
- Overall assessment: valuable, theoretically sound contribution whose merits outweigh weaknesses if revised.
- Request: clarify meaning of “almost surely” (Line 169) — whether it refers to the n→∞ limit.
- Correction: Equation (3) should include a summation over i in the loss term.
- Suggestion: use the term “translation invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel” (Line 205).
