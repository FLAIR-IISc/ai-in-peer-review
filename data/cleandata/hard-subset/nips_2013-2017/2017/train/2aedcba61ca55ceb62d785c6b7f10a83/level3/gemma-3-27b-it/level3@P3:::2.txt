This paper addresses the important problem of group additive structure identification for nonparametric regression, a natural extension of the standard additive model that allows for interactions between variables without the combinatorial explosion of functional ANOVA. The authors propose a novel penalty term based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs, added to kernel ridge regression, to simultaneously learn the group structure and estimate the functions within each group. The theoretical analysis provides consistency guarantees for both group structure identification and function estimation, and empirical results on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.

The paper is well-motivated and tackles a relevant problem. The connection to graphical models is appropriately discussed, highlighting the difference in focus – directly targeting group structure versus conditional dependencies. The proposed penalty term, quantifying the complexity of a group structure using covering numbers, is a key innovation. The theoretical results are comprehensive, covering both statistical rates and consistency of the estimated group structure. The experimental evaluation provides support for the method’s efficacy. Overall, this is a strong submission with potentially significant contributions to the field. However, its ultimate impact hinges on how it compares to existing methods in the broader literature, which isn't entirely clear from the presentation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The covering number-based penalty for group structure complexity is a genuinely new idea.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides strong theoretical guarantees, including consistency results for both group structure and function estimation.
*   **Comprehensive Approach:** The method jointly optimizes group structure and function estimates, offering a complete solution.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data support the method’s performance.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly explains the limitations of standard additive models and the benefits of the group additive approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability:** The optimization over group structures becomes computationally challenging with a large number of variables, as the authors acknowledge. Exhaustive search is impractical beyond small datasets.
*   **Writing Quality:** The paper contains numerous typos and grammatical errors, which detract from clarity.
*   **Limited Comparative Analysis:** The paper doesn't present a thorough comparison against existing methods for group structure identification, making it difficult to assess its advantages.
*   **Parameter Tuning:** The role and sensitivity of the tuning parameters (µ and α) are not discussed in sufficient detail.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem.
*   The consistency guarantees are a significant contribution.
*   The experimental results are promising and support the method's effectiveness.
*   The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the key ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability limitations restrict the applicability of the method to relatively small datasets.
*   The numerous typos and grammatical errors detract from the overall quality of the paper.
*   The lack of a comprehensive comparative analysis makes it difficult to assess the method’s relative performance.
