- Proposes a novel “group additive structure” for high-dimensional nonparametric regression and methods to identify intrinsic groups.
- Defines a minimal group additive structure that captures necessary interactions while retaining estimation efficiency.
- Introduces a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers used as a penalty in a structure-penalized kernel estimator.
- Proves consistency for the proposed estimator and establishes uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure.
- Develops two structure-identification algorithms: exhaustive search and a backward stepwise greedy algorithm.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and clearly distinguishes the approach from graphical models.
- Related work coverage is inadequate—misses connections to structured sparsity (e.g., group Lasso), sparse additive models, functional ANOVA, and existing complexity measures.
- Experimental evaluation is thin: only five synthetic models and Boston Housing, with no baselines or comparisons to alternatives for group identification.
- Lacks computational complexity or scalability analysis despite proposing a stepwise algorithm for large problems.
- Optimization is problematic: the complexity measure is hard to optimize, reliance on exponential exhaustive search or greedy heuristics without evidence of reliability, and no exploration of better alternatives.
- Contribution appears incremental relative to OKGT [11]; authors do not convincingly demonstrate substantial practical improvement.
- Overall quality: strong theory but insufficient empirical validation and weak connection to practical implementation.
- Significance limited by narrow evaluation and missing comparisons; authors should strengthen related work, experiments, and optimization methods.
- Reviewer recommendation: reject.
