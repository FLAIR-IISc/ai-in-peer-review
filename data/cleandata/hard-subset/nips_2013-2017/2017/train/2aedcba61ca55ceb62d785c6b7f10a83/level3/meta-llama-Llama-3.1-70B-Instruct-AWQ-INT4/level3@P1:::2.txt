**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel method for group additive nonparametric regression, where the predictor variables are partitioned into groups, and the group structure is learned from the data. The method uses a penalty based on the covering numbers of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) balls, added to kernel ridge regression, to jointly optimize the group structure and within-group estimates. Two algorithms are proposed to approximately solve the combined optimization over groups and functions. The paper also provides theoretical results, including rates for the empirical risk approaching the true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure. The method is evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel method**: The paper proposes a new method for group additive nonparametric regression, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Solid theory**: The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including rates for the empirical risk approaching the true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure.
3. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper evaluates the method on synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
4. **Well-motivated**: The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Optimization over group structures**: The optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large, which is a limitation of the method.
2. **Writing**: The paper is generally clear, but there are numerous typos, which can make it difficult to read.
