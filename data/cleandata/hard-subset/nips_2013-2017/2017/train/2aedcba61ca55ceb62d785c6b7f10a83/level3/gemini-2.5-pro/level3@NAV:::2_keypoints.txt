- Problem: jointly learn group structure of predictors and estimate a group-additive nonparametric regression function.
- Method: propose structure-penalized kernel ridge regression with a penalty derived from covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.
- Algorithms: provide two optimization procedures — an exhaustive search and a backward stepwise heuristic.
- Theory: prove consistency of the estimated group structure.
- Experiments: empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength — novelty/significance: first to quantify group-partition complexity via direct-sum RKHS capacity, giving a principled trade-off between additive and fully nonparametric models.
- Strength — quality: comprehensive treatment with strong theoretical results and supportive experiments; practical consideration via two algorithms.
- Weakness — scalability: combinatorial optimization over groupings is computationally challenging; stepwise heuristic is greedy and lacks guarantees, raising concerns for large p.
- Weakness — clarity: many typos and grammatical errors reduce readability and require thorough proofreading.
- Question: is there a simpler diagnostic to assess pairwise interaction (X_i, X_j) without fitting the full group-additive model?
- Question: clarify the meaning of "almost surely" — with respect to the data-generating distribution or asymptotically as n → ∞?
- Issue: Equation (3) likely misses a summation over data points i = 1…n in the first term.
- Suggestion: use the standard term "translation invariant kernel" instead of "convolutional kernel."
