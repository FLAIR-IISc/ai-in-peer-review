- Paper formulates a group-additive model f(X)=∑u∈G fu(Xu) and defines the intrinsic (finest) partition G*.
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel estimator over direct-sum RKHS HG with a capacity-derived complexity penalty C(G).
- Shows selection consistency for the estimated partition Ĝ under suitable tuning of penalties.
- Theoretical contributions: definition of amiable structures and uniqueness/existence of intrinsic partition are clean and useful.
- Capacity-based penalty derived from covering-number bounds leading roughly to C(G) ≈ ∑u α|u|+1.
- Theorem 4 (selection consistency) is valuable but depends on several not-fully-specified assumptions and tuning choices.
- Assumptions to clarify: kernel boundedness/universality, conditions on PX, noise model, covariate independence beyond Proposition 1, and behavior of λn, µn.
- The role and practical meaning of α (linked to approximation scale ε) and the simplification to Eq. (6) are opaque and need clarification.
- Algorithms: exhaustive search is exponential in p and the backward stepwise procedure is heuristic with no approximation guarantees.
- No discussion of time complexity, pruning strategies, or certificates for the search procedures.
- Empirics are limited: simulations are small-scale and lack comparisons to meaningful baselines.
- Missing empirical baselines: SS-ANOVA, SALSA, OKGT, hierarchical/interaction lasso, additive MKL, and tree-based interaction methods.
- Boston Housing case study is illustrative but does not demonstrate recovery or significant predictive gains.
- Sensitivity analysis to λ, µ, α is limited and stepwise failures (e.g., Model 4) indicate fragility of the search.
- Related work coverage is incomplete: insufficient engagement with structured sparsity, MKL, sparse additive models, hierarchical interaction selection, and structure learning literatures.
- Clarity: Section 2.3.2 needs a concrete, example-driven exposition of the penalty, units, and α selection tied to ε.
- Notation issues: unify G ≤ G′, HG vs L2G, and state all assumptions up-front for propositions/theorems.
- Provide pseudocode with computational complexities and clear hyperparameter selection protocols.
- Originality: intrinsic group additive structure and capacity-based identification are novel, but the overall penalized partition-search idea overlaps existing structured sparsity/MKL work.
- Significance: method could be impactful for interpretability if made scalable and robust, but current optimization and empirical shortcomings limit practical impact.
- Suggested optimizations: convex/continuous relaxations (MKL with ℓ1/ℓp), overlapping-group penalties, submodular/cardinality-based penalties, agglomerative/beam search, MCMC, mixed-integer formulations, or differentiable relaxations (Gumbel-Softmax).
- Penalty calibration: recommend a data-driven mapping of α to sample size, kernel bandwidth, and noise level and justification for simplifications.
- Experimental suggestions: add listed baselines, test correlated covariates and higher p (≥50–200), include misspecification scenarios, recovery metrics (Rand index/VI), and report runtimes.
- Pros: clear formalization of intrinsic group-additive structure, capacity-based penalty, and a middle ground between additive and full ANOVA models.
- Cons: non-scalable optimization, thin empirical evaluation, insufficient related-work discussion, and unclear practical tuning guidance.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage revision addressing scalability, stronger baselines, and fuller contextualization within structured sparsity/MKL/interaction-selection literatures.
