- Proposes a novel group additive model for high-dimensional nonparametric regression to capture feature correlations ignored by standard additive models.
- Provides a formal problem treatment and defines a complexity measure for group additive structure derived as an upper bound on the covering number.
- Complexity measure favors many small groups but is not easily optimizable in practice.
- Authors resort to exhaustive search or greedy forward-selection, limiting scalability.
- Theoretical development is the paper's main strength and technical quality is high.
- Related work coverage is weak—missing links to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.
- Experimental evaluation is thin: only small synthetic cases and the Boston Housing dataset.
- No baselines or comparisons to existing methods, making empirical performance unclear.
- Authors provided clarifications but did not sufficiently improve experiments or literature discussion.
- Significance is potentially high for the problem area but reduced by practical scalability and evaluation limitations.
- Paper is well-written and clear technically, but needs stronger related-work and more comprehensive experiments to increase impact.
