- Problem: learns unknown group-additive structure in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, a middle ground between additive and fully nonparametric models.
- Main contribution: proposes a principled method to simultaneously identify variable groups and estimate within-group functions.
- Novel complexity penalty: introduces a penalty based on covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS to quantify partition complexity.
- Algorithms: offers exhaustive search for small p and a greedy backward stepwise algorithm for larger problems.
- Theoretical guarantees: proves empirical risk consistency and that the method consistently recovers the true intrinsic group structure as n → ∞.
- Empirical validation: successfully recovers group structures on synthetic data and yields interpretable results on the Boston Housing dataset.
- Strength — novelty/significance: provides a theoretically grounded trade-off between fit and structural simplicity for structured nonparametric regression.
- Strength — theoretical soundness: formalizes an "intrinsic group additive structure" and provides strong asymptotic consistency results (Theorem 4).
- Strength — completeness: paper includes problem definition, methodology, algorithms, theory, and experiments.
- Weakness — computational scalability: partition search is combinatorially explosive (Bell numbers); exhaustive search only for tiny p and greedy heuristic lacks optimality guarantees.
- Weakness — presentation quality: numerous typos and grammatical errors reduce readability and require thorough proofreading.
- Question — interaction definition: asks whether a model-free characterization of interaction / intrinsic structure can be given directly from f, independent of RKHS.
- Clarification request: asks for clarification of "almost surely (in population)" and whether it refers to P_X; recommends careful probabilistic language.
- Technical corrections: points out missing summation in Equation (3), suggests using "translation-invariant" or "stationary" instead of "convolution kernel," and lists several specific typos/phrasing fixes.
