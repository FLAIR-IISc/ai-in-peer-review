## Review of “Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression”

This paper addresses the important problem of group additive structure identification in nonparametric regression, a challenging area when dealing with high-dimensional data. The authors propose a novel penalty term based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs, integrated into a kernel ridge regression framework, to simultaneously learn the group structure and estimate the within-group functions. This is a comprehensive approach with strong theoretical foundations and empirical validation.

The key innovation lies in quantifying the complexity of a group structure using covering numbers, providing a principled way to penalize overly complex groupings. The theoretical results – rates for empirical risk and consistency of the estimated group structure – are significant and rigorously presented (though Equation (3) requires a summation over *i*). The experiments on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, supporting the theoretical findings. The inclusion of both exhaustive search and a stepwise algorithm caters to different data scales, enhancing the practical applicability of the work.

However, the method’s scalability is a limitation. As acknowledged by the authors, optimization over group structures becomes computationally expensive with a large number of variables. While the stepwise algorithm attempts to mitigate this, it’s a greedy approach and may not always find the optimal structure, as evidenced by the results on Model 4 in the simulations. The writing, while generally clear, suffers from numerous typos (e.g., Line 145, 152, 160, 247, Algorithm 2 Line 1, Line 251) which detract from the overall polish. 

A minor clarification is needed regarding the interaction between two variables – a simpler characterization than the full model in Equation (1) would be helpful. Additionally, the meaning of “almost surely” on Line 169 should be explicitly defined (presumably as *n* → ∞). Finally, using “translation invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel” on Line 205 would be more standard terminology.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Solid theoretical foundation with proven consistency.
*   Novel and principled approach to quantifying group structure complexity.
*   Comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.
*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Scalability limitations with a large number of variables.
*   Numerous typos and minor clarity issues in the writing.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with significant potential. Addressing the identified limitations and polishing the writing would further strengthen the contribution. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the noted issues.
