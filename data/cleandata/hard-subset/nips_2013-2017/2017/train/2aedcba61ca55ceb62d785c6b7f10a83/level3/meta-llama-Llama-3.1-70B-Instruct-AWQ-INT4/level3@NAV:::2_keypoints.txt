- Proposes a novel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression.  
- Uses a penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls combined with kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group structure and within-group estimates.  
- Provides a rigorous problem formulation and a structural-penalty-based solution.  
- Introduces a new complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers.  
- Develops two algorithms to approximately solve the joint optimization over groups and functions.  
- Presents theoretical analysis showing consistency of the proposed method.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates effectiveness at identifying group additive structure.  
- Strength: comprehensive treatment linking theory, algorithms, and experiments.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis and clear relation to prior work.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-organized and clearly written.  
- Limitation: optimization over group structures scales poorly as the number of variables grows.  
- Proposed backward stepwise algorithm helps but more efficient algorithms are needed.  
- Limitation: lacks a detailed comparison to alternative approaches such as functional ANOVA/higher-order interaction models.  
- Minor issues: numerous typos/grammatical errors and headings could be more descriptive.  
- Recommendation: significant contribution with accept-with-revisions suggested to address scalability, comparisons, and presentation issues.
