- Proposes a group additive model for high-dimensional nonparametric regression to capture feature interactions while avoiding full ANOVA complexity.  
- Introduces an "intrinsic group additive structure" and a structure-penalized kernel method to identify groups and estimate functions simultaneously.  
- Presents a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers to penalize overly complex groupings.  
- Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, and connects to OKGT and sparse graphical models, claiming differences in scope and identifiability.  
- Strength: formalizes group additive structure and derives a theoretically grounded complexity measure.  
- Strength: provides consistency results that offer theoretical guarantees for the method.  
- Strength: uses a penalty term based on grouping complexity to control overfitting.  
- Weakness: computational complexity is high—exhaustive search scales poorly and the greedy stepwise algorithm is not guaranteed optimal.  
- Weakness: related work section is limited, missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical models, block-sparse methods, and feature selection literature.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is weak—synthetic experiments are small and the Boston Housing study lacks baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: the proposed complexity measure is hard to interpret and may not balance complexity and fit effectively.  
- Weakness: discussion of alternative complexity measures is cursory and lacks depth on pros/cons.  
- Acceptance argument: addresses a relevant problem in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Acceptance argument: makes solid theoretical contributions in formalization and complexity derivation.  
- Acceptance argument: consistency results lend theoretical credibility.  
- Rejection argument: computational limitations significantly weaken practical applicability.  
- Rejection argument: incomplete related work undermines novelty and positioning.  
- Rejection argument: insufficient empirical validation and missing baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: reject with advice for major revision—expand related work, strengthen empirical comparisons, develop more efficient optimization, and analyze complexity measure alternatives.
