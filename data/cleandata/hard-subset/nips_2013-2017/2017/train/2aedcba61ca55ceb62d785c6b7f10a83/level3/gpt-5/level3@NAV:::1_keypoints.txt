- Paper proposes GAM-G: group additive nonparametric regression with a partial order on partitions and a structure-penalized kernel method, plus selection-consistency proof for the population objective.  
- Authors derive a complexity penalty from covering-number bounds of direct-sum RKHSs and give exhaustive and greedy search methods to identify groups.  
- Related-work discussion is incomplete, missing sparse additive models/SALSA, hierarchical kernel learning/MKL, structured sparsity/group-lasso, additive GPs, and graphical-model structure learning threads.  
- The intrinsic group formalization and RKHS capacity-based penalty are interesting and technically solid at the population level.  
- Major gap between theory and practice: complexity measure is hard to optimize and methods rely on exhaustive search or an ungaranteed greedy heuristic.  
- Experiments are limited (small synthetics and Boston Housing) and lack comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., SALSA, additive GP, group-lasso, OKGT).  
- Paper is generally clear in definitions/derivations but offers limited intuition for the penalty’s tightness and omits important implementation details (kernel choice, hyperparameter tuning, runtime).  
- Originality is modest: ideas are novel refinements but closely related to existing structured-sparsity and hierarchical kernel literature.  
- Potential significance if made scalable and empirically competitive, but current validation is insufficient to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Pros: crisp formalization of intrinsic group additive structure; RKHS-based complexity penalty and consistency analysis.  
- Cons: optimization unscalable/unguaranteed; sparse experiments with no strong baselines; incomplete related-work coverage.  
- Post-rebuttal: some clarifications but experiments and literature discussion still inadequate; reviewer recommends rejection pending stronger empirical evaluation, scalability analysis, and deeper connections to related work.
