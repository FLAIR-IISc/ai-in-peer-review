- Paper studies nonparametric regression with unknown group-additive structure and proposes jointly estimating partitions and within-group functions.
- Introduces a structure-penalized kernel ridge regression with a penalty based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS balls.
- Presents two solvers: exhaustive search with validation and a backward stepwise heuristic.
- Theoretical guarantees: empirical-to-population risk convergence and consistency of estimated group structure.
- Empirical results on synthetic data and Boston Housing show the method can recover true partitions.
- Situates work relative to additive models, spline ANOVA, interaction kernels (ESP/SALSA), OKGT, and graphical-model concepts.
- Modeling and penalty are well-motivated; covering-number bounds for direct-sum RKHSs are elegant.
- Structure-consistency result is a meaningful theoretical contribution; proofs use standard capacity-control techniques.
- Experiments support claims but lack comparisons to functional ANOVA/ESP kernels and hierarchical kernel learning.
- Clarity is generally good but hampered by numerous typos and ambiguity about tuning parameters (μ, α) and interpretation of α.
- Request to clarify computational complexity of the backward stepwise method.
- Novelty: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the induced direct-sum RKHS appears new.
- Potential significance if positioned against broader literature on multiple/hierarchical kernel learning for interactions.
- Pros: novel principled structural penalty, clear formalization of intrinsic group structure, solid theory with supportive experiments.
- Cons: partition optimization scales poorly and heuristics may fail for large p; limited empirical comparisons; sensitivity to tuning not fully analyzed.
- Request: provide a simple pairwise interaction diagnostic (e.g., HSIC-based test) not requiring full model fit.
- Clarification needed: whether “almost surely” refers to convergence in probability or an a.s. statement as n → ∞.
- Technical error: Equation (3) appears to miss the summation over i in the empirical risk.
- Terminology suggestion: use “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”
- Typos/corrections: Line 145 “on RHS”→“on the RHS”; Line 247 “turning”→“tuning”; Algorithm 2 start “State with”→“Start with”; Line 251 “by compare”→“by comparing”; fix grammar at Lines 152,160; other minor fixes.
