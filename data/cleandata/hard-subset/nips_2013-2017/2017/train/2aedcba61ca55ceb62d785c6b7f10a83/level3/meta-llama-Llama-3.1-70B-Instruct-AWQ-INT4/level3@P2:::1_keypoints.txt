- Proposes a group additive model that generalizes additive models by allowing interactions between predictors.
- Rigorously defines an intrinsic group additive structure and develops a structure-penalized kernel method to identify it and estimate functions.
- Introduces a novel complexity measure derived from RKHS covering numbers that favors many small groups.
- Main idea: group features to capture correlations ignored by standard additive models, with ad hoc algorithms to find groupings.
- Theoretical contribution: complexity measure provided as an upper bound on the covering number.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the method and theoretical foundation.
- Major concern: the complexity measure is not easily optimizable; authors rely on exhaustive search or greedy forward selection.
- Scalability worry: current optimization approaches may not scale to high-dimensional problems.
- Reviewer expected more efficient optimization methods or alternative, more tractable complexity measures.
- Poor coverage of related work: missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.
- Experimental evaluation is thin, limited to small synthetic cases and the Boston Housing dataset.
- Reviewer requested more extensive experiments and comparisons to other methods and baselines.
- Authors’ rebuttal addressed some issues, but reviewer still urges stronger related-work treatment and broader experiments.
- Ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing related work and experimental evaluation concerns.
- Pro acceptance arguments: novel method for high-dimensional nonparametric regression, formal treatment, and a theoretical complexity measure favoring small groups.
- Con acceptance arguments: non-optimizable complexity measure, inadequate related-work coverage, and limited empirical validation.
