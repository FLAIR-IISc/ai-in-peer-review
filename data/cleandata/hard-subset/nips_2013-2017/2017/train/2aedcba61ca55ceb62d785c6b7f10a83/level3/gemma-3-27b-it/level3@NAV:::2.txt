## Review of "Group Additive Structure Identification for Kernel Nonparametric Regression"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of nonparametric regression in high dimensions by focusing on group additive models, where the goal is to simultaneously learn both the group structure and the nonparametric functions within each group. The authors propose a novel penalty term, based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs, added to a kernel ridge regression framework to achieve this. The paper is well-motivated, addressing a known limitation of standard additive models—the neglect of variable interactions—and offering a principled approach to learn suitable groupings. 

The core innovation – quantifying the complexity of a group structure via covering numbers – is particularly noteworthy. The theoretical results establishing consistency for both the estimated group structure and the empirical risk approximation are strong and demonstrate a solid understanding of the underlying mathematical principles. The authors provide two algorithms for practical implementation, recognizing the computational challenges associated with exploring the group structure space. Empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets further supports the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, the paper isn't without weaknesses. The authors correctly identify the difficulty of optimization when dealing with a large number of variables, which is a significant practical limitation. While two algorithms are proposed, the computational cost for large *p* remains a concern and could benefit from further discussion. The writing, while generally clear, suffers from numerous typos and minor grammatical errors that detract from the polish of the presentation. 

Specifically, the request for a simple characterization of interaction between two variables (mentioned in the submission details) is a valid point - it would be beneficial to see a more intuitive explanation of how the model captures interaction effects. Further clarification is needed on Line 169 regarding the use of "almost surely," ideally specifying the limiting behavior (n → ∞). The technical error in Equation (3) – the missing summation – needs to be addressed.  Adopting "translation invariant kernel" instead of "convolutional kernel" (Line 205) would align with standard terminology. The enumerated typos (Lines 145, 152, 160, 247, Algorithm 2 Line 1, Line 251) should be carefully corrected.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation with consistency guarantees.
*   Novel and well-motivated approach to group structure identification.
*   Solid empirical results on both synthetic and real data.
*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in nonparametric regression.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Computational limitations with large numbers of variables.
*   Numerous typos and grammatical errors impact readability.
*   Limited discussion of practical considerations for large-scale datasets.



**Overall:** This is a comprehensive and technically sound contribution with the potential to significantly advance the field. Despite the identified weaknesses, the strengths of the paper – particularly the theoretical guarantees and novel approach – outweigh the drawbacks. Addressing the typos and clarifying the points raised would further strengthen the manuscript. I recommend acceptance contingent on minor revisions.
