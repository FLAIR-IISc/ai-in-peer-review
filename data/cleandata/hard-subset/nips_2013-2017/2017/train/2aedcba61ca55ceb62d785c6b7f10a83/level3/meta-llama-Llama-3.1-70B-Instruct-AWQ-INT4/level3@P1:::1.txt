This paper proposes a novel approach to high-dimensional nonparametric regression by introducing a group additive model that groups features to capture correlations ignored by standard additive models. The authors provide a formal treatment of the problem, define a complexity measure for the group additive structure, and propose algorithms for finding the optimal feature groupings.

The paper's main contribution lies in its theoretical development, particularly in defining a complexity measure that favors many small groups. This measure is derived as an upper bound on the covering number, which is a common approach in learning theory. However, the reviewer notes that this measure is not easily optimizable, leading the authors to resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection approach. This limitation is a significant drawback, as it restricts the scalability of the method.

The paper's related work coverage is also lacking, with missing links to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature. The authors could have strengthened their contribution by discussing how their approach relates to these areas and how it addresses the limitations of existing methods.

The experimental evaluation is thin, with only small synthetic cases and the Boston Housing dataset used to demonstrate the method's effectiveness. The reviewer notes that there are no baselines or comparisons to other methods, making it difficult to assess the method's performance in a broader context.

In response to the reviewer's comments, the authors provided some clarifications, but the experiments and literature discussion remain insufficient. The reviewer urges the authors to provide a stronger related-work treatment and more comprehensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate the method's effectiveness and scalability.

**Strengths:**

* Novel approach to high-dimensional nonparametric regression using a group additive model
* Formal treatment of the problem and definition of a complexity measure for the group additive structure
* Theoretical contribution in deriving an upper bound on the covering number

**Weaknesses:**

* Complexity measure is not easily optimizable, leading to restrictive algorithms
* Limited scalability due to exhaustive search or greedy forward-selection approach
* Poor related work coverage, missing links to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature
* Thin experimental evaluation, lacking baselines and comparisons to other methods

**Originality:**

* The paper's approach to high-dimensional nonparametric regression using a group additive model is novel and contributes to the existing literature.
* The definition of a complexity measure for the group additive structure is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Quality:**

* The paper's technical quality is high, with a clear and formal treatment of the problem.
* However, the experimental evaluation and related work coverage are limited, which reduces the overall quality of the paper.

**Significance:**

* The paper's contribution to high-dimensional nonparametric regression is significant, as it addresses a challenging problem in machine learning.
* However, the limitations of the method, particularly its scalability, reduce its significance in practice.

**Clarity:**

* The paper is well-written, and the technical treatment is clear and concise.
* However, the experimental evaluation and related work coverage could be improved for better clarity.

**Overall:**

* The paper is a good contribution to the literature on high-dimensional nonparametric regression, but its limitations, particularly its scalability, reduce its overall impact.
* The authors should address the reviewer's comments by providing a stronger related-work treatment and more comprehensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate the method's effectiveness and scalability.
