- Problem: nonparametric regression with unknown partitioned predictors, additive across groups and nonparametric within each group; learn partition and group functions in RKHS with a new structural penalty.
- Key concept: formalizes intrinsic (minimal) group-additive structure via a partial order on partitions.
- Method: structural penalty based on covering-number bounds of direct-sum RKHSs, leading to KRR-like joint optimization with group-structure penalty.
- Algorithms: exhaustive search with validation for small p and a backward stepwise heuristic for larger p.
- Theory: uniform convergence of empirical to population risk under amiable partitions and consistency of estimated group structure (P(Ĝ = G∗) → 1).
- Experiments: synthetic and Boston Housing data show meaningful discovered groups and functions.
- Relation to prior work: connects to additive models, smoothing spline ANOVA, SALSA, MKL/structured sparsity, and OKGT but adds identifiability and principled selection via capacity control.
- Strength: clear formalization of intrinsic group structure resolving identifiability ambiguities.
- Strength: novel, interpretable structural penalty tied to RKHS covering numbers that increases with group size/number.
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees linking capacity control to structure selection consistency.
- Strength: practical solvers and empirical evidence supporting the approach.
- Weakness: partition search scales super-exponentially (Bell numbers); greedy stepwise can miss true structure.
- Weakness: tuning of penalty parameters (µ, α) is opaque and selection is non-intuitive; needs guidance and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: theory depends on strong kernel assumptions (translation-invariant kernels with rapidly decaying Fourier transforms) and predictor independence; robustness not fully discussed.
- Weakness: empirical comparisons lack competitive baselines (GAM/sparse additive, ANOVA kernels, SALSA, MKL) and runtime comparisons.
- Quality: technically careful and well-structured, but empirical study could be broader; minor technical issues and typos reduce polish.
- Clarity: generally clear but contains numerous typos and some points needing clarification; algorithmic complexity and convergence of the stepwise method should be detailed.
- Originality: quantifying partition complexity via covering numbers is original; intrinsic structure identification and consistency results are meaningful advances.
- Significance: potentially impactful for interpretable nonparametrics if scalability is addressed; theory gives confidence in model selection.
- Requests: provide an operational test/definition for pairwise interaction that doesn’t require fitting the full model (e.g., projection or ANOVA/Sobol criterion).
- Requests: clarify the meaning of “almost surely” at Line 169 (population equality vs. asymptotic a.s. convergence).
- Requests: give Big-O complexity for exhaustive and stepwise procedures and discuss practical scaling and possible pruning/beam/warm-start strategies.
- Suggestions: use “translation-invariant kernel”; relate penalty to MDL or group-sparsity/MKL frameworks; add ablations for µ and α and guidance beyond grid search.
- Technical/typos: Eq.(3) missing summation over i; multiple wording/typo fixes (Lines 145, 152, 160, 205, 247, 251, Algorithm 2 start); notational inconsistency in C(G) exponent needs clarification.
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled penalty with strong theory, clear identifiability, practical algorithms, and relevant topic for NeurIPS.
- Cons for acceptance: scalability concerns, limited baseline comparisons, and some clarity/typo issues.
- Overall: comprehensive, well-motivated contribution with novel complexity-driven penalty and consistency theory; leans toward acceptance pending stronger experiments and clearer discussion of scalability and assumptions.
