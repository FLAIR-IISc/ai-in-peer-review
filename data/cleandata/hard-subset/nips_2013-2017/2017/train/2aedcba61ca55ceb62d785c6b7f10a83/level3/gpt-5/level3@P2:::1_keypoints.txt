- Paper studies nonparametric regression with a group additive model where the regression decomposes as sums over disjoint covariate groups.
- Contributions: formalize “intrinsic group additive structure” (unique minimal partition), propose a structure-penalized kernel method to jointly estimate function and partition, derive a complexity penalty C(G) via covering-number bounds, prove selection consistency, and give exhaustive and greedy search strategies.
- Relates to additive models, functional ANOVA, kernel ridge regression with ANOVA/ESP kernels, OKGT, and sparse/structured additive modeling (SpAM, SALSA) and structure discovery in graphical models.
- Strength: clear formalization and uniqueness/existence result for intrinsic group additive structure.
- Strength: theoretical selection consistency under suitable conditions, distinguishing amiable vs non-amiable structures.
- Strength: principled derivation of a structure penalty from covering-number bounds that favors many small groups.
- Strength: provides practical algorithms (exhaustive and greedy backward splitting) and interpretable real-data illustration.
- Weakness: theoretical penalty C(G) relies on strong assumptions (convolution kernels with exponentially decaying Fourier transform, compact domain, sup-norm covering numbers) that are not all stated explicitly.
- Weakness: C(G) uses a reparameterized α tied to ln(r/ε); since α is tuned in practice, the practical link to covering numbers is ad hoc and needs sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: combinatorial optimization over partitions is intractable for large p; greedy heuristic is heuristic and can miss true structure; no runtime/complexity analysis or comparison to alternative search methods.
- Weakness: experiments are limited (small synthetic cases and one UCI dataset) with no quantitative comparisons to relevant baselines, making empirical gains unclear.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but needs tighter notation (empirical vs penalized risks) and explicit statements of when independence assumptions are used.
- Clarity: the role and interpretation of α require more concrete discussion and practical guidance.
- Clarity: pseudocode should clarify how μ and α are selected during search and how kernel hyperparameters (e.g., bandwidth) are tuned.
- Originality: definition of intrinsic group additive structure and covering-number–based penalty over direct-sum RKHSs is original, though related to structured sparsity and hierarchical kernel learning ideas.
- Significance: approach is potentially useful for interpretable interaction discovery but current algorithms do not scale and empirical comparisons to strong baselines are missing.
- Missing related work: structured sparsity/grouped interactions (SpAM, group lasso, overlapping group lasso), hierarchical interaction selection (Bien et al.), hierarchical kernel learning (Bach), and scalable partition/graph search heuristics.
- Missing link: clearer connection needed between group additivity and conditional independence/graphical model structure learning literature.
- Suggestions: add quantitative baselines (SALSA, OKGT, KRR with ANOVA/ESP, SpAM), report predictive error, partition recovery metrics (e.g., ARI), and runtime.
- Suggestions: provide scalability analysis in p and n, consider relaxations or alternative search strategies (merge-split, stochastic search, MIP), and report improvements.
- Suggestions: clarify theorem assumptions, analyze sensitivity to kernel choice/bandwidth, and give systematic tuning protocols for (μ, α, λ).
- Suggestions: study robustness to correlated covariates (beyond independence assumptions) and consider Bayesian partition models as alternatives.
- Post-rebuttal: rebuttal clarified theory and implementation but experiments and related-work coverage remain insufficient for NIPS standards.
- Argument for acceptance: conceptually clean formalization, theoretically grounded penalty, and selection consistency — potentially impactful for interpretable interaction discovery.
- Argument against acceptance: limited empirical validation, unresolved scalability and optimization, and insufficient coverage/contrast with related structured sparsity and hierarchical kernel work.
- Overall recommendation: interesting theoretical contribution but empirical and algorithmic components need substantial strengthening before acceptance.
