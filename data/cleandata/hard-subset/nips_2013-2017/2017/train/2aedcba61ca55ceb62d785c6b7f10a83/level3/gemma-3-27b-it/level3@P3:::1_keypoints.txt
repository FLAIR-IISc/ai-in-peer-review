- Investigates high-dimensional kernel regression via a "group additive model" to capture feature correlations missed by standard additive models.  
- Main contribution: formalizes an "intrinsic group additive structure" and a penalized kernel method to jointly identify groups and estimate the nonparametric function.  
- Introduces a covering-number–based complexity measure designed to favor many small groups as a regularizer for grouping.  
- Practical usability of the complexity measure is limited because optimization is hard; authors use exhaustive search or greedy forward selection, which scale poorly.  
- The paper notes alternative measures but does not substantively explore or evaluate them.  
- Theoretical contribution: a consistency proof for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure, which is valuable but relies on strong conditions.  
- Practical implications of the theory are unclear due to unspecified constant factors and potentially unrealistic assumptions.  
- Related work coverage is inadequate: insufficient discussion and comparison to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, lasso-type group penalties, causal inference approaches, and functional ANOVA literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: small synthetic datasets and Boston Housing only, with no baselines or comparisons to standard additive models, functional ANOVA, or other methods.  
- Experiments also lack detail on hyperparameter tuning, model validation, and broader synthetic scenarios.  
- Post-rebuttal clarifications did not resolve the core issues of insufficient experiments and shallow related-work discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, provides a coherent formalization of group additive structure, and supplies a nontrivial consistency result.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complexity measure not efficiently optimizable, poor related-work positioning, weak experimental validation, and theoretical analysis lacking practical detail.  
- Overall assessment: interesting idea but not a strong contribution in current form; recommend rejection with encouragement to improve optimization, expand literature review, and run comprehensive comparative experiments.
