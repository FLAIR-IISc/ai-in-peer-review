- Addresses learning group-additive structure in nonparametric regression where predictors are partitioned into unknown groups.  
- Rigorously defines the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the minimal, identifiable group representation of the true function.  
- Proposes a novel estimator combining kernel ridge regression with a structural penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls.  
- Introduces a theoretically grounded complexity measure for group structures via covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS.  
- Develops two optimization algorithms: exhaustive search and a backward stepwise procedure.  
- Proves consistency: the estimated group structure converges to the intrinsic structure with high probability as sample size grows.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical proofs and solid empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Key innovation bridges standard additive models and full functional ANOVA by allowing controlled interaction patterns.  
- Strong originality and significance, filling a meaningful gap in nonparametric regression methodology.  
- Writing has numerous typos and grammatical errors that reduce clarity (e.g., "on RHS," missing summation in Eq. 3).  
- Computational complexity is prohibitive for large numbers of variables, limiting scalability to high-dimensional problems.  
- Connection to recent related NIPS work (e.g., refs [6], [9]) is incomplete and should be improved.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos/technical errors and address related-work and scalability discussion.
