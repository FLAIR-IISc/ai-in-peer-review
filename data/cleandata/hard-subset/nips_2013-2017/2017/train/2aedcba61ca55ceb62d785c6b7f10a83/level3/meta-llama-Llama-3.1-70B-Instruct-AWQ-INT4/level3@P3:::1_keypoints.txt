- Proposes a group additive model for high-dimensional kernel regression that groups features to capture ignored correlations.  
- Main contribution is a complexity measure (upper bound on covering number) used as a penalty in the kernel method.  
- Provides a formal treatment of the problem and ad hoc algorithms to find feature groupings (exhaustive and greedy).  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and supports the claims.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via simulation studies and one real-data application (Boston Housing).  
- Writing and organization are clear and easy to follow.  
- Strength: rigorously addresses non-uniqueness by defining an intrinsic group additive structure and a method to identify it.  
- Limitation: the complexity measure is not easily optimizable, relying on exhaustive search or greedy selection, which may not scale.  
- Limitation: related work coverage is poor, missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.  
- Limitation: experimental evaluation is limited—small synthetic cases, only one real dataset, and no baselines or method comparisons.  
- Originality: moderate—group additive modeling itself is not new, but the formal treatment and complexity penalty are novel.  
- Significance: moderate—addresses a common problem but impact is limited by weak empirical comparisons and coverage.  
- Recommendation: improve experiments (more datasets and baselines) and expand related-work discussion before acceptance.  
- Overall rating: 7/10.
