- Proposes a group additive model for high-dimensional nonparametric regression to capture feature interactions.  
- Defines an intrinsic group additive structure and a structure-penalized kernel method for joint structure identification and function estimation.  
- Introduces a complexity measure from covering number bounds that favors many small groups.  
- Proves consistency for the proposed estimator.  
- Develops two structure-identification algorithms: exhaustive search and greedy forward selection.  
- Strength: rigorous formal treatment with clear theoretical consistency results.  
- Strength: intrinsic group additive structure gives a useful conceptual framework for minimal interaction structure.  
- Weakness: complexity measure is not efficiently optimizable, forcing costly exhaustive search or possibly suboptimal greedy search.  
- Weakness: alternative complexity measures are only briefly mentioned without comparison or development.  
- Weakness: related work coverage is inadequate—omits connections to structured sparsity (group lasso), graphical models, Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to small synthetic data and Boston Housing, lacking proper baselines and comparisons to competing methods.  
- Overall assessment: sound theory but insufficient empirical validation and literature comparison; reviewer recommends rejection pending stronger experiments and more comprehensive related work.
