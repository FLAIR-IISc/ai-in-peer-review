- Addresses identifying group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression, extending OKGT to find minimal (intrinsic) group partitions.
- Formally defines the intrinsic group additive structure, resolving previous non-uniqueness issues.
- Proposes a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHSs.
- Incorporates that complexity measure into a structure-penalized kernel estimation method.
- Proves consistency of the proposed method for recovering the intrinsic group structure as sample size grows.
- Implements two algorithms: exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise (greedy) approach for larger p.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset shows ability to recover true group structures and yield interpretable models.
- Technically sound with well-constructed consistency proofs and solid theoretical foundations.
- Clearly distinguishes its focus on group additive structure from graphical-model conditional-dependence approaches.
- Strengths: rigorous definition, novel complexity measure, comprehensive theoretical guarantees, practical algorithms, and convincing empirical validation.
- Weakness: manuscript contains numerous typos and grammatical errors that distract from the technical content.
- Weakness: Equation (3) is incorrect/missing the summation over i in the empirical risk term.
- Weakness: The "almost surely" claim is unclear and needs clarification about the limiting behavior.
- Weakness: The backward stepwise Algorithm 2 is greedy and may fail to find the optimal structure.
- Weakness: Computational complexity becomes prohibitive for very large p; scalability is acknowledged but not deeply addressed.
- Recommendations: carefully proofread, fix technical errors (especially Eq. 3), clarify "almost surely," and expand discussion of computational scalability and extensions for larger problems.
- Overall judgment: strong, well-motivated contribution with solid theory and empirical support; recommended for acceptance pending fixes to presentation and scalability discussion.
