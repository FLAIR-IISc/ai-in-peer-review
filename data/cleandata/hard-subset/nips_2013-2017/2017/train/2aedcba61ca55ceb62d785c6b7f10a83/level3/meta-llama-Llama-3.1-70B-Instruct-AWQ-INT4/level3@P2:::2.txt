This paper proposes a novel method for group additive nonparametric regression, where the predictor variables are partitioned into groups and the group structure must be learned from data. The authors introduce a penalty based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is added to kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize the group structure and within-group estimates. Two algorithms are proposed to approximately solve the combined optimization over groups and functions.

The paper provides a comprehensive study of the method, including theory and experiments. The authors prove rates for the empirical risk approaching the true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure, with the probability of correct estimation approaching 1 as the sample size increases. The empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method, demonstrating its ability to identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions.

The key innovation of the paper is the quantification of the complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS. This allows for a principled approach to learning the group structure, rather than relying on ad-hoc methods. The authors also provide a clear and well-motivated discussion of the method, including its relationship to existing work on nonparametric regression and kernel methods.

However, the paper has some limitations. One of the main limitations is that the optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large. The authors propose two algorithms to address this issue, but further research is needed to develop more efficient and scalable methods.
