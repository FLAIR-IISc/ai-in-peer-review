This paper proposes a novel approach to identifying the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression. The method utilizes a penalty based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize the group structure and within-group estimates. The authors provide a comprehensive treatment of the problem, including a rigorous formulation of the problem, a structural penalty method to solve it, and a theoretical analysis of the method's consistency.

The paper's main contributions are the introduction of a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers and the development of two algorithms to approximately solve the combined optimization over groups and functions. The authors also provide an empirical evaluation of the method on synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying the intrinsic group additive structure.

The strengths of the paper include its comprehensive treatment of the problem, solid theoretical analysis, and empirical evaluation. The authors have done a good job of relating their work to previous research in the field, and the paper is well-organized and clearly written.

However, there are a few limitations and areas for improvement. One limitation is that the optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large. The authors propose a backward stepwise algorithm to address this issue, but further research is needed to develop more efficient algorithms.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the proposed method and other approaches to nonparametric regression, such as functional ANOVA models. The authors mention that their method can be seen as lying between the original additive model and functional ANOVA or higher-order interaction models, but a more detailed comparison would be helpful.

Minor issues with the paper include numerous typos and grammatical errors. The authors should also consider using more descriptive headings and subheadings to improve the paper's organization and readability.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of nonparametric regression and has the potential to be a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners. With some revisions to address the limitations and areas for improvement mentioned above, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication.
