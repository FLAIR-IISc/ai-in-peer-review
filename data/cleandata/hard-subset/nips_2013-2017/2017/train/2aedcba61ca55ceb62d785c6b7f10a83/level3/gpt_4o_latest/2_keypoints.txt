- Introduces a novel RKHS covering-number–based penalty to quantify group complexity and jointly estimate group structure and regression functions.  
- Balances flexibility and interpretability by extending additive/functional ANOVA models to unknown group-additive structures.  
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees, including consistency in group structure estimation and convergence of empirical to true risk.  
- Derivations are mathematically clear and support the proposed theoretical results.  
- Proposes two practical optimization algorithms: exhaustive search and a backward stepwise (greedy) approach for different problem scales.  
- Validated on synthetic and real datasets, recovering true group structures and yielding interpretable results; Boston Housing application is persuasive.  
- Scalability is limited: optimization is computationally challenging in high dimensions and may not scale to many variables.  
- The backward stepwise algorithm’s greedy nature risks missing global solutions and full exploration of the solution space.  
- Writing quality suffers from typos, unclear phrasing, and ambiguous equations, hindering readability and reproducibility.  
- Minor methodological gaps: insufficient characterization of variable interactions beyond group membership and imprecise use of terms like “almost surely” and kernel terminology.  
- Strong case for acceptance: addresses a significant problem with a novel, theoretically grounded method that advances interpretability and flexibility.  
- Counterarguments for rejection: computational limitations and poor manuscript clarity may restrict applicability and reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvements: develop more efficient algorithms/approximations for scalability, fix typos and clarify equations/algorithms, and expand discussion on interactions and kernel terminology.  
- Overall conclusion: meaningful contribution to nonparametric regression deserving acceptance if scalability and writing issues are adequately addressed.
