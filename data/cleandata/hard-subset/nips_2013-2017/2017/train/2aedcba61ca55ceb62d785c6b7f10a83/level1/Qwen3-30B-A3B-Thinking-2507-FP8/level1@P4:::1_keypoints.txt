- Problem addressed: identification of group additive structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression and resolution of non-uniqueness.  
- Key definition: rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure as the minimal correct structure.  
- Method: structure-penalized kernel method that simultaneously identifies group structure and estimates nonparametric functions.  
- Complexity measure: novel RKHS covering-number-based complexity measure for group additive structures.  
- Theoretical results: Propositions 1–4 and Theorems 2–4 provide rigorous consistency proofs showing convergence to the intrinsic structure.  
- Algorithms: two implementations provided—exhaustive search and backward stepwise—with clear pseudocode.  
- Simulations: simulation studies (Table 2) demonstrate successful recovery of true group structures across multiple models.  
- Real-data application: Boston Housing analysis shows practical utility and interpretable insights.  
- Clarity and presentation: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and links theory to implementation effectively.  
- Originality: first formalization of intrinsic group additive structure and a novel RKHS-based complexity contribution.  
- Significance: offers a middle ground between additive and full interaction models, improving interpretability in high dimensions.  
- Strength: resolves a known gap, provides rigorous theory, introduces a novel complexity measure, and shows empirical effectiveness.  
- Limitation: exhaustive search is computationally intensive and may not scale to extremely high dimensions.  
- Limitation: limited scope of simulation scenarios (five models) and limited comparisons with alternative structure-identification methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, significant, and meets conference criteria for quality and impact.
