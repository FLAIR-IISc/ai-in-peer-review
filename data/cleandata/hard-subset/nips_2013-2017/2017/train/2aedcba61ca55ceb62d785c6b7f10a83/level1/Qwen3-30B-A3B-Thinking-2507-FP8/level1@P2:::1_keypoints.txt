- Addresses the important problem that standard additive models miss interactions in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
- Proposes identifying the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the minimal group partition that represents a target function.
- Resolves non-uniqueness by rigorously defining the intrinsic structure and proving its uniqueness (Theorem 1).
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel method to perform simultaneous structure identification and function estimation.
- Introduces a novel complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers (C(G) = ∑α|uj|).
- Provides strong theoretical results, including a careful consistency proof (Theorem 4) built on Propositions 1–4.
- Presents a clear two-step estimation procedure (Algorithm 1) and a backward stepwise algorithm (Algorithm 2).
- Experimental results (simulations and Boston Housing) demonstrate effective recovery of true group structures and practical performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Clearly distinguishes the approach from related work on group additive models, functional ANOVA, and graphical models.
- Strengths: rigorous mathematical foundation, novel complexity measure, strong consistency results, comprehensive experiments, clarity.
- Weaknesses: stepwise algorithm has limitations in some models; sensitivity to kernel and complexity choices is not fully explored; exhaustive search scalability not fully addressed.
- Pro acceptance points: fills a clear gap, novel and well-motivated concept, rigorous theory, empirical effectiveness, high practical impact.
- Con acceptance points: potential robustness issues of the stepwise algorithm, incomplete exploration of implementation choices, computational limits for large p.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
