- Summary: Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression, with theoretical consistency and empirical validation.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated approach to identifying intrinsic group additive structure.

- Strength: Rigorous definition of intrinsic group additive structure and a method to identify it.

- Strength: Uses a well-established complexity measure (RKHS covering number) with clear theoretical justification.

- Strength: Extensive simulations and real-data applications demonstrate effectiveness.

- Strength: Clear and well-organized presentation.

- Weakness: Assumes existence and uniqueness of the intrinsic group additive structure, which may not hold in practice.

- Weakness: Method depends on kernel and complexity measure choices, which can be problem-sensitive.

- Weakness: No clear practical guidance for choosing tuning parameters (λ, µ, α).

- Weakness: Exhaustive search algorithm can be computationally expensive for large models.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison with other existing methods for identifying group additive structures.

- Originality: Specific structure-penalized RKHS approach is original and well-motivated despite related prior ideas.

- Significance: Addresses an important problem and could improve accuracy and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

- Clarity: Well-written and easy to follow, with clear theoretical and algorithmic explanations.

- Quality: High-quality work with rigorous definitions, methods, theory, and empirical validation.

- Pro (accept): Novel and well-motivated methodological contribution.

- Pro (accept): Rigorous formulation and development of the identification method.

- Pro (accept): Strong empirical results across simulations and real-data examples.

- Con (reject/concern): Reliance on existence/uniqueness assumption for intrinsic structure.

- Con (reject/concern): Sensitivity to kernel and complexity-measure choices.

- Con (reject/concern): No practical guidance for tuning parameter selection.
