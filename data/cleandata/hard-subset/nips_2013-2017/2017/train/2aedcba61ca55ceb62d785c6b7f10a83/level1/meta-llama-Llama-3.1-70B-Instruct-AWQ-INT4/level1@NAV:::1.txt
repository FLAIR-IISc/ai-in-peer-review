**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach to identifying the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression. The authors rigorously define the intrinsic group additive structure and develop a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions. The method uses a complexity measure based on the covering number of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) to penalize the complexity of the group structure. The authors prove the consistency of the estimated group additive structure and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method through extensive simulation studies and real data applications.

1. The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to identifying the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression.
2. The authors provide a rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure and develop a structure-penalized kernel method to identify it.
3. The method is based on a complexity measure that is well-established in the literature, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the theoretical underpinnings of the approach.
4. The paper includes extensive simulation studies and real data applications that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
5. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of the material, making it easy to follow their arguments and understand the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the intrinsic group additive structure exists and is unique, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The method relies on the choice of a kernel and a complexity measure, which can be sensitive to the specific problem at hand.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the tuning parameters (λ, µ, and α) in practice.
4. The computational cost of the exhaustive search algorithm can be high, especially for large models.
5. The paper does not provide a comparison with other existing methods for identifying group additive structures.
