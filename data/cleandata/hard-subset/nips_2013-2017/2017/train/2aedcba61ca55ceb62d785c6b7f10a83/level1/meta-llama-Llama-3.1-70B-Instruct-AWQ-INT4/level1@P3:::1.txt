**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression. The authors define a group additive structure as a partition of the predictor variables into groups, where each group has a nonparametric function associated with it. They then develop a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions. The method uses a novel complexity measure based on the covering number of the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) to penalize the complexity of the group structure.

**Related Work**

The authors mention several related works, including the Optimal Kernel Group Transformation (OKGT) method, which is a special case of their proposed method. They also discuss the connection between their method and graphical models, highlighting the key difference that their method directly targets the group additive structure instead of the more complex conditional dependence structure.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel complexity measure**: The authors propose a novel complexity measure based on the covering number of the RKHS, which is a more nuanced and effective way to penalize the complexity of the group structure.
2. **Consistency result**: The authors provide a consistency result for their method, showing that the estimated group structure converges to the intrinsic group additive structure as the sample size increases.
3. **Extensive simulation study**: The authors conduct an extensive simulation study to evaluate the performance of their method, demonstrating its ability to identify the intrinsic group additive structure in various model settings.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational cost**: The authors mention that the exhaustive search algorithm can be computationally expensive, especially for large models. However, they propose a backward stepwise algorithm as a more efficient alternative.
2. **Limited interpretability**: While the authors provide some visualizations of the estimated nonparametric functions, the interpretability of the results is limited, especially for large models.
3. **Lack of comparison to other methods**: The authors do not compare their method to other existing methods for nonparametric regression, making it difficult to assess its relative performance.

**Originality**

The authors' proposal of a novel complexity measure based on the covering number of the RKHS is an original contribution. Additionally, their development of a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify the intrinsic group additive structure and estimate the nonparametric functions is a new approach to nonparametric regression.

**Significance**

The authors' method has the potential to improve the accuracy and interpretability of nonparametric regression models, especially in high-dimensional settings. The ability to identify the intrinsic group additive structure can help mitigate the curse of dimensionality and improve the efficiency of the model.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and results. However, some of the technical details, such as the derivation of the complexity measure, may be challenging for non-experts to follow.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their method. However, the lack of comparison to other methods and the limited interpretability of the results are areas for improvement.
