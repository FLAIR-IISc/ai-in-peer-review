- Proposes a novel method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression.  
- Defines intrinsic structure as the minimal amiable group partition, resolving prior non-uniqueness issues.  
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel estimator using a new RKHS-based complexity measure from covering numbers.  
- Proves consistency of the proposed approach (Theorems 2–4).  
- Bridges simple additive models and full interaction models by allowing structured interactions while preserving estimation efficiency.  
- Provides two practical algorithms (exhaustive search and stepwise) with implementation details.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance via comprehensive simulations and a Boston Housing data application.  
- Makes a significant technical contribution by formalizing intrinsic structure and introducing the RKHS complexity metric.  
- Exposition is clear and mathematically precise.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation coupled with convincing empirical validation.  
- Weakness: exhaustive search scales poorly to large p, though stepwise mitigates this partially.  
- Weakness: theoretical results rely on independent predictors, limiting applicability to correlated-data settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, rigorous proofs, strong experiments, clear exposition, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of large-scale computational constraints and the independent-predictor assumption.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality contribution that advances nonparametric regression; recommended for acceptance.
