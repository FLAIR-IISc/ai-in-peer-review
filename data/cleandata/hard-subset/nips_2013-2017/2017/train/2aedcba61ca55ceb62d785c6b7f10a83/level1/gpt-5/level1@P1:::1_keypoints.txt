- Paper studies high-dimensional nonparametric regression with group additive structure, defines a partial order over partitions, amiable structures, and the unique minimal intrinsic partition G*; proposes a structure-penalized RKHS estimator with selection consistency and offers exhaustive and backward-stepwise algorithms plus experiments.

- Novelty: proving existence and uniqueness of an intrinsic group additive structure under the partition partial order is a useful theoretical contribution.

- Novelty: using RKHS covering-number-based penalties for scoring partitions and proving structure-selection consistency is a fresh approach.

- Theorems: provide uniform convergence for amiable G, separation for non-amiable G, and consistency of the selected partition Ĝ.

- Key technical concern: many assumptions are implicit (kernel boundedness/universality, compact domain, sub-Gaussian noise, tuning schedules), and they are not centralized.

- Key technical concern: Proposition 1 requires independence to get strict inclusion of function spaces, but dependence among covariates is common and used in experiments — identifiability under dependence is unclear.

- Penalty derivation unclear: the move from covering-number ln N(ε,·) bounds to the penalty C(G)=Σ α|u| is not cleanly justified, and α is introduced inconsistently.

- Role of ε and α: α is defined via α=(ln(r/ε)) but then treated as a discrete hyperparameter independent of n, r, or ε; the coupling with µ and finite-sample behavior is not explained.

- Rates/choices unclear: consistency requires λn·n→0 and a “proper” µ, but the paper gives no explicit guidance on scaling µ or how to set α from data; constants in bounds are large and implications unaddressed.

- Algorithmic scalability: exhaustive search is exponential; the backward stepwise heuristic has no approximation or consistency guarantees and may get stuck in local minima.

- Algorithmic details missing: no complexity analysis and Algorithm 2’s split/merge rules are insufficiently specified.

- Experiments: synthetic results report recovery frequencies for chosen (µ,α) and CV-tuned variants, but stepwise fails on one model and behavior across settings is limited.

- Baselines missing: no comparisons to strong alternatives such as SALSA, ESP-kernel KRR (order-controlled), sparse additive models, smoothing spline ANOVA, or OKGT.

- Real-data analysis: qualitative only, lacks predictive metrics or runtime comparisons versus baselines.

- Reproducibility gaps: kernel choices, bandwidths, and λ selection are not fully specified; code and implementation details are not provided.

- Clarity issues: the term “amiable” is unconventional (suggest “compatible/admissible”), and notation/formatting has occasional typos.

- Clarity issues: the complexity penalty section is confusing — ε and r disappear, α becomes free, and exponents change — reconcile and clearly state the final penalty and defaults.

- Clarity issues: assumptions (compact X, kernel properties, noise) should be stated upfront and which results require independence indicated.

- Practical significance: the method is potentially impactful for interpretable discovery of low-order interacting groups if validated, but current empirical and scalability limitations limit practical utility.

- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated problem, unique intrinsic partition formalization, RKHS capacity–based penalty with selection theory, and interpretable results with illustrative examples.

- Cons for acceptance: ambiguous penalty derivation, misaligned assumptions (independence), heuristic search without guarantees, limited experiments and baselines, and reproducibility gaps.

- Suggested improvement 1: clarify and tighten the penalty — explicitly relate α and µ to ε and n, provide principled defaults, and reconcile exponent inconsistencies.

- Suggested improvement 2: state all assumptions upfront, discuss dependent covariates, and indicate which theorems need independence.

- Suggested improvement 3: provide algorithmic refinements and analysis (beam search, stochastic restarts, relaxations/submodular surrogates) and report computational complexity empirically.

- Suggested improvement 4: expand experiments to compare recovery, prediction, and runtime against SALSA, ESP-kernel KRR, sparse additive models, smoothing spline ANOVA, and OKGT.

- Suggested improvement 5: detail implementation choices (kernels, bandwidths, λ tuning, CV protocol) and release code for reproducibility.

- Suggested improvement 6: improve terminology/notation consistency and add examples showing G* vs amiable superstructures under correlated X.

- Overall assessment: promising theoretical contribution but empirical validation, penalty clarity, assumption consolidation, and algorithmic scalability need strengthening; reviewer leans toward borderline reject with encouragement to revise.
