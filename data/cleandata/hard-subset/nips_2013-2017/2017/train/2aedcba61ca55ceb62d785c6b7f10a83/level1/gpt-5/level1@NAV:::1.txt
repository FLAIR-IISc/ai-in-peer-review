This paper revisits additive modeling for high-dimensional nonparametric regression by introducing and formalizing an intrinsic group additive structure: the minimal partition of predictors into groups such that f(X) = sum_u f_u(X_u). Building on earlier “group additive” ideas (e.g., OKGT; Pan et al., KDD’15) and classical additive models (Hastie & Tibshirani), the authors define a partial order over partitions and prove existence and uniqueness of the intrinsic structure. They then propose a structure-penalized kernel method that jointly estimates f and the group structure via an RKHS complexity penalty derived from covering number bounds (Zhou, 2002; Kühn, 2011). Theoretical results establish that, under universal kernels and suitable tuning, the selected partition is consistent. Algorithms include exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise heuristic for larger p. Simulations and a Boston Housing case study illustrate feasibility. The work relates to functional ANOVA and bounded-order interaction models (Gu, 2013; Kandasamy & Yu, ICML’16) but imposes sparsity at the group level rather than enumerating interactions; and to graphical models (Koller & Friedman) but targets functional grouping rather than conditional independence.

Quality: The mathematical formalization (partial order, intrinsic structure) is clean; the use of covering numbers to upper-bound the capacity of direct-sum RKHSs and derive a tractable penalty is sound in spirit, and the selection consistency theorems are carefully stated. However, the complexity penalty ultimately depends on a parameter α introduced via reparameterization of ε; the operational choice and interpretation of α are under-specified, and the resulting penalty depends only on group sizes, not on kernel specifics or data geometry, which may be suboptimal. Algorithmic scalability and guarantees for the stepwise procedure are not provided, and empirical comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., GAM + pairwise interactions, ANOVA kernels, SALSA, SpAM/hierarchical sparsity) are limited.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-organized, but Eq. (5)→(6) and the α reparameterization could be clarified; some notation is heavy and references lack complete details.

Originality: Defining and consistently identifying the intrinsic group additive structure with an RKHS capacity-based penalty is novel relative to OKGT and ANOVA-kernel approaches.

Significance: If scalable, the approach offers a middle ground between additive models and high-order interaction expansions, potentially improving interpretability. Current empirical evidence is promising but modest.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear formalization of intrinsic group additive structure and consistency proof.
- Conceptually appealing RKHS capacity-based structural penalty.
- Addresses a practically relevant modeling regime (small group interactions).

Cons for acceptance:
- Penalty design (α) is ad hoc in practice and size-only; limited guidance and sensitivity analysis.
- Scalability and lack of guarantees for stepwise search; exhaustive search impractical.
- Limited empirical comparisons to established interaction-detection baselines; mixed results on some models.
- Some clarity/notation issues and incomplete citations.
