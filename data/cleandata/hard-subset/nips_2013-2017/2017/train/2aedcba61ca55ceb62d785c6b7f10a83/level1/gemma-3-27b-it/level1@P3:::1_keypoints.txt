- Tackles identification of group additive structures in nonparametric regression, bridging fully nonparametric and restrictive additive models.  
- Correctly highlights non-uniqueness of group structures and lack of systematic identification methods in prior work.  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method using a novel complexity measure for group additive structures.  
- Introduces a principled complexity measure C(G) based on RKHS covering numbers to penalize group complexity.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees, including a consistency proof (Theorem 4) supported by supplementary lemmas.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and logical presentation of ideas.  
- Offers both exhaustive search and a stepwise algorithm to handle different data scales.  
- Empirical validation via simulations and real-data applications demonstrates practical effectiveness.  
- Situates the work appropriately within literature on additive models, ANOVA, graphical models, and kernel methods.  
- Implementation may be complex due to computing covering numbers and exhaustive searches in high dimensions.  
- Computational cost and scalability are not thoroughly analyzed or discussed.  
- Performance is sensitive to tuning parameters (µ, α), with limited guidance on tuning procedures and robustness to λ.  
- The greedy stepwise algorithm can be suboptimal; alternative optimization or initialization strategies are not explored.  
- Impact of different kernel choices on performance and computational trade-offs is not sufficiently examined.  
- Connection to graphical model estimation is brief; deeper comparisons and potential synergies are lacking.  
- Key theoretical and experimental details are primarily in the supplementary material, making full evaluation harder.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with a principled complexity measure and consistency result.  
- Against acceptance: practical implementation complexity, tuning sensitivity, and insufficient exploration of related methods may limit applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision: provide detailed guidance on tuning µ and α, possibly rules of thumb or cross-validation strategies.  
- Revision: analyze and discuss computational complexity and strategies for scaling to higher dimensions.  
- Revision: expand discussion and experiments on kernel choice and its effects.  
- Revision: elaborate the relationship to graphical model estimation techniques and potential advantages.  
- Revision: consider including key steps of the consistency proof in the main paper for accessibility.
