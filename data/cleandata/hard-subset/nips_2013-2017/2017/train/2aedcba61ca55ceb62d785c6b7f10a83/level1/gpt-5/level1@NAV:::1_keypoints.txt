- Revisits additive modeling by defining the intrinsic group additive structure: the minimal partition of predictors with f(X)=sum_u f_u(X_u).  
- Defines a partial order over partitions and proves existence and uniqueness of the intrinsic structure.  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method that jointly estimates f and the group structure using an RKHS complexity penalty derived from covering number bounds.  
- Establishes selection consistency of the selected partition under universal kernels and suitable tuning.  
- Provides algorithms: exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise heuristic for larger p.  
- Demonstrates feasibility via simulations and a Boston Housing case study, but empirical evidence is modest.  
- Positions work relative to OKGT, functional ANOVA, bounded-order interaction models, and graphical models; novelty is group-level sparsity via RKHS capacity-based penalty.  
- Strength: clean mathematical formalization (partial order, intrinsic structure) and carefully stated consistency theorems.  
- Strength: sensible use of covering numbers to upper-bound RKHS capacity and derive a tractable penalty in spirit.  
- Weakness: complexity penalty depends on an under-specified reparameterization parameter α with unclear operational choice and interpretation.  
- Weakness: resulting penalty depends only on group sizes, not kernel specifics or data geometry, which may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns and no theoretical guarantees for the stepwise search; exhaustive search is impractical for large p.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons to strong baselines (GAM+pairwise, ANOVA kernels, SALSA, SpAM/hierarchical sparsity) and mixed performance on some models.  
- Clarity issues: Eq. (5)→(6), the α reparameterization, heavy notation, and incomplete references need clarification.  
- Significance: potentially offers a useful middle ground between additive models and high-order interaction expansions, improving interpretability if made scalable.
