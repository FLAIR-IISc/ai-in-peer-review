- Problem & model: Identifying interaction effects in high-dimensional nonparametric regression using group additive models.

- Contribution 1: Formalizes the "intrinsic group additive structure" G* as the unique, minimal grouping that explains the regression function.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method with a complexity penalty C(G) derived from RKHS covering numbers, and proves consistency for identifying G*.

- Experiments: Synthetic simulations recover true structures; Boston Housing example demonstrates interpretable interaction visualizations.

- Relation to prior work: Extends OKGT by providing a formal objective and systematic structure identification.

- Theoretical quality: Strong, rigorous theoretical contributions (Definition 1, Theorem 1, Theorem 4) and principled penalty derivation.

- Scalability concern: Exhaustive search is only feasible for very small p; backward stepwise is greedy and can fail in moderate dimensions.

- Hyperparameter complexity: Method introduces µ and α in addition to λ and kernel parameters, making tuning computationally intensive and potentially unstable.

- Clarity & organization: Well-written, well-motivated, with logical flow and provided algorithms.

- Clarity issues: Insufficient discussion of kernel choice/bandwidth effects; Algorithm 2’s neighborhood-generation step is ambiguously described.

- Originality: Novel formalization of intrinsic structure and an RKHS-derived complexity penalty that is more principled than ad-hoc penalties.

- Significance: Addresses an important interpretability problem; useful for hypothesis generation in many applied fields and likely to inspire further work.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory, novel methodology, important problem, clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited scalability and the complexity of hyperparameter tuning.

- Recommendation: Overall positive assessment; recommend acceptance.
