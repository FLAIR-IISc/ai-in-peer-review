- Proposes group additive model f(X)=Σ f_uj(X_uj) to identify interaction structures in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
- Defines a partial order on group structures and proves existence and uniqueness of an intrinsic group additive structure G* (most parsimonious).
- Introduces GASI, a structure-penalized kernel method combining empirical risk, RKHS norm, and a novel group-structure complexity penalty.
- Derives the structural penalty from RKHS covering-number bounds, linking theoretical capacity to model selection.
- Provides a consistency proof for identification of G* and validates the approach on simulations and the Boston Housing dataset.
- Strength: original and significant formalization that makes structure identification well-posed and advances prior work.
- Strength: technically sound derivation of the penalty grounded in learning theory (RKHS covering numbers).
- Strength: consistency proof for structure selection lends theoretical credibility.
- Strength: clear, well-written exposition with precise definitions and logical flow.
- Weakness: exhaustive search over partitions is computationally infeasible as p grows; backward greedy search is heuristic and can fail.
- Weakness: paper insufficiently discusses or experiments with more sophisticated search strategies (e.g., stochastic or evolutionary search).
- Weakness: introduces additional hyperparameters (μ, α) beyond λ and kernel params, complicating practical tuning.
- Weakness: interpretation and tuning of α is unclear and treated arbitrarily in experiments; lacks sensitivity analysis or tuning guidance.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks predictive comparisons to methods like GBMs, Random Forests, or SALSA and is limited to low-dimensional settings (p ≤ 6).
- Weakness: needs experiments with larger p to assess practical performance of the backward stepwise algorithm.
- Pro: strong theoretical contributions, practical relevance, and clarity argue for acceptance.
- Con: scalability, hyperparameter tuning, and limited empirical comparisons argue for caution and further work.
- Overall: high-quality, novel paper with strong theory but practical limitations; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing scalability, tuning guidance, and expanded experiments.
