- Tackles group additive structure identification for kernel nonparametric regression by defining an “intrinsic” structure and proposing a structure-penalized kernel method.  
- Novelty: defines a complexity measure for group structures using covering numbers and integrates it into a penalized regression framework.  
- Well-organized presentation: clear problem setup, thorough related work review, and logical method development from known to unknown structure.  
- Theoretical strength: provides a consistency proof establishing theoretical guarantees for the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical support: comprehensive simulations and a real-data application demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Strong situating of work within literature on additive models, functional ANOVA, and OKGT.  
- Major weakness: exhaustive search algorithm (Algorithm 1) is computationally expensive and may not scale to very high dimensions.  
- Stepwise algorithm underperforms in some settings (notably Model 4); more analysis and improvements are needed.  
- Method is sensitive to tuning parameters (λ, μ, α); current guidance relies on cross-validation without deeper selection strategies.  
- Lack of guidance on kernel selection and limited exploration of how different kernels affect performance.  
- Proposition 1 is correct but lacks intuitive exposition or a concrete example (e.g., illustrating L2G1 ⊂ L2G2).  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem, novel and theoretically sound approach, strong empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, tuning sensitivity, and inconsistent stepwise performance could limit practical use.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper suitable for acceptance with minor revisions focusing on parameter selection guidance, computational complexity discussion, and stepwise algorithm improvements.
