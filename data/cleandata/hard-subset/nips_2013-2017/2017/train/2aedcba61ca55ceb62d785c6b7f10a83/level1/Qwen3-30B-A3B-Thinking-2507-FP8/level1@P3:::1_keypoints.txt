- Rigorous definition of the intrinsic group additive structure (Theorem 1) and consistency proof for the identification method (Theorem 4).  
- Novel complexity measure C(G)=∑α^{|u_j|} derived from RKHS covering numbers, capturing dimensionality vs. complexity trade-offs.  
- Two practical algorithms provided (exhaustive search and backward stepwise) with theoretical justification for implementation at different p.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: simulations showing correct group recovery and a real-data Boston Housing application demonstrating utility.  
- Addresses limitation of additive models by identifying minimal variable groups where interactions occur, improving interpretability and estimation.  
- Main scalability concern: exhaustive search is intractable for large p and stepwise algorithm scalability/theory for p > 100 is not fully established.  
- Sensitivity to tuning parameters (μ and α); cross-validation used but parameter choice can substantially affect performance.  
- Limited real-world validation — only one dataset (Boston Housing) analyzed, more diverse applications would strengthen claims.  
- Insufficient exploration of connections to related work (e.g., sparse graphical models and conditional dependence methods).  
- Consistency results rely on strong technical assumptions (RKHS dense in L2, specific covering number bounds) with limited discussion of practical implications.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and significant contribution that advances high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Recommendation: Accept — valuable theoretical advance and practical method despite scalability and tuning limitations.  
- Pro: principled intrinsic structure definition, consistency guarantees, RKHS-based complexity penalty, implementable algorithms, and clear motivation addressing additive model limits.  
- Con: concerns about computational scalability in very high dimensions, tuning sensitivity, limited empirical validation, and assumptions needing better justification.
