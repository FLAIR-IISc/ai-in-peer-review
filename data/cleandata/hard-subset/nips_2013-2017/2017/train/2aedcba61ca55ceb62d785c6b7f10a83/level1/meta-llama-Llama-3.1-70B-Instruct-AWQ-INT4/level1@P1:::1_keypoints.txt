- Proposes a method to identify intrinsic group additive structure in kernel nonparametric regression and simultaneously estimate component functions.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on RKHS covering numbers to penalize structure complexity.  
- Develops a structure-penalized kernel estimation procedure and associated algorithm.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees, including consistency of the estimated group additive structure.  
- Demonstrates method effectiveness via simulation studies and real-data applications.  
- Builds on additive models, functional ANOVA, and OKGT, distinguishing itself by directly targeting group additive structure.  
- Relates to graphical models but focuses on group additive structure rather than graph recovery.  
- Strength: the new RKHS-based complexity measure is tractable and principled for penalization.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results support the method’s validity.  
- Strength: algorithm appears effective in experiments.  
- Weakness: exhaustive search algorithm can be computationally expensive for large models.  
- Weakness: performance may be sensitive to tuning parameters (λ, µ, α).  
- Weakness: interpretability may be limited for complex interactions among predictors.  
- Pro-accept: novel method, theoretical support, and empirical evidence of effectiveness.  
- Con-accept: scalability, tuning sensitivity, and limited interpretability raise practical concerns.  
- Paper quality: technically sound, well-organized, and supported by experiments.  
- Clarity: well-written with consistent notation and clear figures/tables.  
- Originality: presents a significant novel contribution via the RKHS covering-number complexity measure.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential impact in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.
