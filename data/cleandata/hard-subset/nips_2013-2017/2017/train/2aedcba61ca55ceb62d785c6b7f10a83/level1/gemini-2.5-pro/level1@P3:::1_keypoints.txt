- Problem: balances model flexibility and interpretability via group additive models as a middle ground between additive and fully nonparametric models.  
- Contribution 1: defines a partial order on group structures and proves existence and uniqueness of a minimal "intrinsic group additive structure" (identifiability result).  
- Contribution 2: proposes a structure-penalized kernel estimator with complexity penalty C(G) derived from RKHS covering numbers.  
- Theoretical guarantee: proves consistency of the structure selection procedure (Theorem 4).  
- Strength — originality: formalization of intrinsic group additive structure addresses a largely overlooked identifiability problem.  
- Strength — principled methodology: structural penalty is derived from function-space capacity rather than ad-hoc heuristics.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: consistency proof lends credibility beyond algorithmic heuristics.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written with clear notation and illustrative examples.  
- Weakness — scalability: exhaustive search is infeasible for moderate p; proposed backward stepwise heuristic is greedy and can fail.  
- Weakness — algorithm description: Algorithm 2 (backward search) is imprecisely described—steps read more like forward moves than true splits.  
- Weakness — empirical validation: no comparisons to competing methods (e.g., COSSO, sparse group Lasso, tree/boosting methods).  
- Weakness — limited experimental scale: simulations use very small p (5–6); need experiments with p in the dozens to justify high-dimensional claims.  
- Weakness — tuning sensitivity: method relies on three tuning parameters and results appear sensitive to their selection; robustness study needed.  
- Weakness — assumptions clarity: technical assumptions (e.g., kernel properties for covering number bounds) should be stated more explicitly in the main text.  
- Overall summary: solid and novel theoretical contribution likely merits acceptance, but practical aspects (optimization, broader experiments, baseline comparisons, and clearer assumptions) should be strengthened in the final version.
