**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper proposes a method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression. The authors extend the additive model by considering interactions between predictor variables and introduce a novel complexity measure for group additive structures. The method is based on kernel methods and Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs). The authors also establish a connection between their proposed group additive model and graphical models.

The paper relates to previous work on nonparametric regression, additive models, and functional ANOVA models. The authors mention the limitations of the additive model in neglecting interactions between predictor variables and propose a solution to this problem. They also discuss the connection between their method and graphical models, highlighting the key difference between the two approaches.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel complexity measure**: The authors propose a novel complexity measure for group additive structures, which is based on the covering number of RKHSs. This measure is used to penalize the complexity of the group structure in the optimization problem.
2. **Consistency proof**: The authors provide a consistency proof for their method, showing that the estimated group additive structure converges to the intrinsic group additive structure as the sample size increases.
3. **Effective algorithm**: The authors propose two algorithms for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure: an exhaustive search algorithm and a backward stepwise algorithm. The algorithms are shown to be effective in identifying the true group structure in simulation studies.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational cost**: The exhaustive search algorithm has a high computational cost, which makes it impractical for large models. The backward stepwise algorithm is more efficient but may not always find the optimal solution.
2. **Choice of tuning parameters**: The method requires the choice of tuning parameters (λ, µ, and α), which can be challenging in practice. The authors propose using a validation set to select the tuning parameters, but this may not always be feasible.
3. **Assumptions on the data**: The method assumes that the data are i.i.d., which may not always be the case in practice. The authors do not discuss the robustness of their method to non-i.i.d. data.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel method for identifying the intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression. The method is based on a new complexity measure for group additive structures, which is used to penalize the complexity of the group structure in the optimization problem. The consistency proof and the effective algorithms proposed in the paper are also original contributions.
