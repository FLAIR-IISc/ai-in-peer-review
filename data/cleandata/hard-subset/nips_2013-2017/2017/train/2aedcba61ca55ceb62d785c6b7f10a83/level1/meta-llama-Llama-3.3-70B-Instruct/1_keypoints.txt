- Proposes a novel approach to identify the intrinsic group additive structure for nonparametric regression.  
- Rigorously defines the intrinsic group additive structure and formulates a structure-penalized kernel method to jointly identify structure and estimate functions.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on RKHS capacity.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees, including consistency of the estimated group additive structure.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanation of the method.  
- Simulation studies and real data applications demonstrate effectiveness in identifying the intrinsic structure.  
- Strength: mitigates the curse of dimensionality and improves efficiency and interpretability in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Strength: method simultaneously selects group structure and estimates nonparametric components.  
- Strength: derivation of an RKHS-based complexity measure for group additive models.  
- Strength: strong theoretical support (consistency proofs).  
- Weakness: computationally inefficient for large datasets due to exhaustive search over possible group structures.  
- Weakness: no clear guideline for selecting tuning parameters (λ, µ).  
- Weakness: potential lack of robustness to outliers/noisy data and no discussion of robustness.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational efficiency, robustness, and tuning-parameter guidance.  
- Rating: 8/10.  
- Suggestion: include more discussion on potential applications and comparisons with existing methods.
