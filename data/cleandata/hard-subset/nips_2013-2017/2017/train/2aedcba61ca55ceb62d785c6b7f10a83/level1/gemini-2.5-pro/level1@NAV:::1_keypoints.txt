- Defines a unique "intrinsic group additive structure" to resolve non-identifiability in prior work.  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to simultaneously identify group structure and estimate the regression function.  
- Derives a novel complexity penalty for group structures based on the RKHS covering number from learning-theory principles.  
- Formalization and RKHS-based penalty are original and provide a principled alternative to ad-hoc approaches.  
- Provides theoretical proofs of selection consistency showing estimated structure can converge to the true intrinsic structure under conditions.  
- Method aids exploratory analysis by uncovering interaction patterns, improving interpretability and predictive accuracy in high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  
- Exhaustive search algorithm is computationally intractable for even moderate numbers of predictors.  
- Backward stepwise algorithm is greedy and not guaranteed to find the optimal structure in practice.  
- Method introduces three tuning parameters (λ, μ, α) whose grid-search selection can be computationally expensive and potentially unstable.  
- Description of the backward stepwise "split" and "add to an existing group" operations is brief; more detail or pseudocode would improve reproducibility.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and foundational contribution with an elegant complexity penalty, but practical scalability, tuning, and clarity issues remain and warrant future work.
