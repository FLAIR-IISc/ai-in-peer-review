- Problem: Identify intrinsic group additive structure for kernel nonparametric regression to capture interactions without exponential complexity.  
- Contribution: Formal definition of intrinsic group additive structure and a structure-penalized kernel method to identify structure and estimate functions.  
- Penalty: Novel RKHS-based complexity measure using covering numbers to construct the penalty term.  
- Theory: Consistency proof showing the method can recover the true structure asymptotically.  
- Algorithms: Practical algorithms proposed (exhaustive search for small p, stepwise for larger p).  
- Empirics: Simulation studies and a real-data example demonstrate effectiveness in structure identification.  
- Positioning: Bridges additive and fully interactive models; relates to but differs from functional ANOVA and graphical models.  
- Novelty: Formalization of structure and RKHS complexity penalty are novel and theoretically grounded.  
- Clarity: Clear problem formulation and generally well-written paper, though theory sections are dense.  
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees (consistency) strengthen confidence in the method.  
- Strength: Practical algorithmic solutions make the approach implementable.  
- Weakness: Theoretical analysis relies on covering numbers and may be inaccessible; needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: Exhaustive search is computationally expensive and stepwise greedy search may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: Performance sensitive to tuning parameters (µ and α); more guidance needed beyond cross-validation.  
- Weakness: Empirical comparisons are limited; should compare to sparse interaction screening and other baselines on benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Real-data interpretation is limited; deeper linkage to domain knowledge and function interpretation needed.  
- For acceptance: Addresses a relevant problem with a novel, theoretically sound approach and supportive empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Highly technical theory, scalability issues, tuning sensitivity, and limited empirical comparisons are concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Revision requests: (1) Add intuitive explanation of the covering-number complexity measure; (2) discuss scalability improvements beyond stepwise search; (3) provide guidance for selecting µ and α; (4) include more comprehensive comparisons on benchmark datasets; (5) expand interpretation of real-data results and relate group structure to domain knowledge.
