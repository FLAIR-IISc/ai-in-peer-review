- Proposes identifying intrinsic group additive structure in nonparametric regression by extending additive models to include interactions and linking to graphical models.  
- Uses kernel methods and RKHSs and introduces a novel complexity measure for group additive structures based on covering numbers.  
- Establishes consistency: the estimated group additive structure converges to the true structure as sample size grows.  
- Presents two algorithms (exhaustive search and backward stepwise) shown effective in simulation studies.  
- Novel complexity penalty and theoretical consistency proofs are original contributions.  
- Method could improve accuracy and interpretability and has connections to graphical models for broader applicability.  
- Exhaustive search has high computational cost, making it impractical for large models.  
- Backward stepwise is more efficient but may not find the optimal grouping.  
- Requires selection of tuning parameters (λ, µ, α), which is challenging and may be infeasible with limited validation data.  
- Assumes i.i.d. data; robustness to non-i.i.d. settings is not discussed.  
- Recommendation: analyze and report robustness to non-i.i.d. data.  
- Recommendation: provide practical guidance or methods for tuning-parameter selection.  
- Recommendation: explore alternative, more efficient optimization algorithms to reduce computational cost.  
- Pro-acceptance summary: novel method, new complexity measure, and original consistency proof and algorithms.  
- Con-acceptance summary: tuning difficulties, computational scalability issues, and restrictive i.i.d. assumptions.
