This paper addresses the important problem of identifying group additive structures in nonparametric regression, a topic bridging the gap between fully nonparametric methods and restrictive additive models. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing approaches – namely the non-uniqueness of group structures and the lack of systematic identification methods. Their proposed solution, a structure-penalized kernel method leveraging a novel complexity measure for group additive structures, is a significant contribution. The theoretical analysis, proving consistency, is a strong point. The paper is well-written and presents a clear and logical progression of ideas.

**Strengths:**

* **Problem Formulation:** The authors clearly articulate the problem of group additive structure identification and its relevance to high-dimensional nonparametric regression.  The motivation, detailing the drawbacks of purely additive models and the limitations of ANOVA-type expansions, is well-presented.
* **Novel Complexity Measure:** The derivation and justification of the complexity measure C(G) based on covering numbers of RKHSs is a key contribution. This provides a principled way to penalize more complex group structures.
* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The consistency proof (Theorem 4) is a substantial achievement, demonstrating the theoretical soundness of the proposed method.  The supporting lemmas (mentioned as being in the supplementary material) are crucial and their rigor is implied by the main result.
* **Algorithm Development:** Providing both an exhaustive search and a stepwise algorithm offers practical considerations for application to different data scales.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The simulation studies and real-data applications demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, although more details on the parameter tuning would be beneficial (see weaknesses).
* **Connections to Related Work:** The paper appropriately positions itself within the existing literature, referencing relevant work on additive models, ANOVA models, graphical models, and kernel methods.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the algorithms are presented, the practical implementation might be challenging due to the need to calculate covering numbers and potentially perform an exhaustive search over many possible group structures, especially in higher dimensions. The computational cost is not thoroughly discussed.
* **Parameter Tuning Sensitivity:** The simulation results (Table 2) show that performance is sensitive to the choice of µ and α.  The paper briefly mentions using a validation set, but doesn’t provide detailed guidelines for parameter tuning or sensitivity analyses, which is crucial for practical usability.  How robust are the results to different choices of λ as well?
* **Stepwise Algorithm Limitations:** The authors acknowledge the potential limitations of the stepwise algorithm (greedy nature) and its suboptimal performance in some cases.  Exploring alternative optimization strategies or initialization schemes could be valuable.
* **Kernel Choice:** The paper states universal kernels allow unbiasedness, but doesn’t discuss the impact of different kernel choices on performance. A more thorough exploration of different kernel options, including their computational trade-offs, would strengthen the work.
* **Connection to Graphical Models - Depth:** The connection to graphical models is briefly mentioned. A more detailed discussion of how the proposed method relates to existing graphical model estimation techniques (e.g., sparse Cholesky factorization) and potential synergies could be insightful. This could also highlight the advantages of the proposed approach.
* **Supplementary Material Reliance:** Crucial details like the proofs of theorems and detailed simulation results are relegated to the supplementary material. While this is acceptable, it makes it harder for reviewers to fully assess the rigor and validity of the work without significant effort.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded approach to a challenging problem in nonparametric regression.
* The proposed complexity measure is a significant contribution and provides a principled way to balance model complexity and goodness of fit.
* The consistency result is a strong indicator of the method’s reliability.
* The empirical validation, although limited, demonstrates the practical effectiveness of the method.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The implementation complexity and sensitivity to parameter tuning could limit the practical applicability of the method.
* The connection to related work (especially graphical models) could be explored in more detail.
* The reliance on supplementary material for key details makes thorough evaluation more difficult.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of nonparametric regression. The theoretical and methodological contributions are significant. While there are some weaknesses regarding implementation complexity and parameter tuning, these do not outweigh the strengths of the work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  

**Specific Revision Suggestions:**

* **Expand the discussion of parameter tuning:** Provide more specific guidance on how to choose µ and α, potentially including rules of thumb or cross-validation strategies.
* **Address computational cost:**  Discuss the computational complexity of the algorithms and potential strategies for scaling to higher dimensions.
* **Elaborate on the kernel choice:**  Provide a brief discussion of the impact of different kernel choices on performance.
* **Detail the connection to graphical models:** Expand the discussion of the relationship between the proposed method and graphical model estimation techniques.
* **Consider including key proofs in the main paper:** If space allows, incorporating at least the key steps of the consistency proof into the main paper would improve accessibility.
