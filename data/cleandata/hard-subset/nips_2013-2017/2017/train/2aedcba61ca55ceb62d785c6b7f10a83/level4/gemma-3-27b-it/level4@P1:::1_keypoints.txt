- Proposes a "group additive model" for kernel regression to capture within-group predictor correlations missed by standard additive models.
- Introduces a novel complexity measure for groupings based on covering-number upper bounds that favors many small groups.
- Provides a formal mathematical treatment and definition of intrinsic group additive structure.
- Claims potential interpretability benefits, balancing additive models and higher-order interaction models.
- Related-work discussion is insufficient, omitting structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature-selection literature.
- Heavy reliance on a single prior (KDD-2015 [11]) makes it hard to judge true novelty and positioning.
- The proposed complexity measure does not lend itself to efficient optimization; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–like method.
- No clear rationale given for choosing the specific complexity measure despite mentioning alternatives.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small synthetic experiments and the Boston Housing dataset.
- No baseline methods or comparative experiments are provided, preventing assessment of empirical benefit.
- Practical applicability is questioned due to computational challenges and weak empirical validation.
- Reviewer recommendation: reject for now, with advice to expand related work and provide comprehensive experiments and baselines for resubmission.
