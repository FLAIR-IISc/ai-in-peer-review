Summary:  
This paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models, where the regression function is additive across predefined groups of predictor variables. This framework bridges the gap between fully nonparametric models (difficult to fit) and restrictive additive models. Specifically, the authors address the problem of learning the unknown group structure from data. They propose a novel penalty function based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This yields an objective function optimized jointly over group structure (which defines a function space via direct sums of RKHSs over groups) and regression estimates within each group. Two algorithms approximate the compound optimization, and theoretical results establish (a) the rate at which empirical risk converges to true risk, and (b) consistency of the estimated group structure (probability of matching the true structure approaches 1 as n → ∞). Empirical validation is provided on synthetic and real datasets.

Main Comments:  
The paper’s key innovation lies in quantifying the complexity of a group structure using covering numbers of the corresponding direct sum space. The method is well-motivated, novel, and comprehensive, supported by solid theoretical and empirical results. While I am not deeply familiar with approaches fitting models between additive and fully nonparametric ones, the discussion in Lines 31–50 appears thorough. Assuming this context is complete, the work represents a potentially significant advance. As noted in the Discussion, the primary limitation is computational difficulty in optimizing over group structure when the number of variables is large. The paper is otherwise clearly written, though it contains numerous typos.
