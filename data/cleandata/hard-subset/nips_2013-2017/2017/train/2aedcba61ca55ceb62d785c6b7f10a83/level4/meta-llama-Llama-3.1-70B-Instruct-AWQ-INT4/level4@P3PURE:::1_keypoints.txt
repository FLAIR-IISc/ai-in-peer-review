- Studies kernel regression in high-dimensional settings, extending the additive model to a "group additive model" to capture correlations between dimensions.  
- Provides a formal treatment of the group additive setting and proposes algorithms to find groupings, but the algorithms are somewhat ad hoc.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure for groupings (upper bound on covering number) that favors many small groups.  
- The proposed complexity measure is difficult to optimize; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–style method.  
- Authors did not develop or present a measure that can be optimized more efficiently, nor give insight into alternative measures despite claiming to investigate them.  
- Related work coverage is inadequate: misses connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.  
- Related work primarily cites a single KDD-2015 paper [8], making it hard for non-experts to judge novelty.  
- Experiments are limited (small synthetic cases and Boston Housing) and lack baselines or comparisons to other methods.  
- After rebuttal the reviewer raised the score modestly, acknowledging valid contributions but maintaining concerns about experiments and related work.  
- Strong recommendation that the authors substantially improve the related work discussion before publication.
