- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the regression function is additive across unknown variable groups.  
- Introduces a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls to quantify group-structure complexity.  
- Proposes jointly optimizing over group structure and group-wise RKHS functions via a modified kernel ridge regression objective.  
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the combined optimization over groups and functions.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rate of empirical risk to population optimum and consistency of recovered group structure as n → ∞.  
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Key innovation: using covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS to quantify complexity of group structures.  
- Paper is comprehensive, combining method, theory, and experiments; potentially significant advance if related work discussion is complete.  
- Main practical concern: optimizing over group structures is difficult when the number of variables is large.
