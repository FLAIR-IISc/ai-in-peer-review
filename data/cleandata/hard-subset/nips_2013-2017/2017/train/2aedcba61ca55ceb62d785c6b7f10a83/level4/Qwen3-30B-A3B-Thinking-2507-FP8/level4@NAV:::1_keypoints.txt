- Proposes a group additive model that partitions features into groups to capture cross-dimensional correlations, extending standard additive models.  
- Provides a formal framework and ad hoc algorithms for finding optimal groupings.  
- Empirical validation is limited to small synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Major concern: related work coverage is inadequate; key areas like structured sparsity, graphical model learning, and feature selection are not cited.  
- Paper cites only one prior KDD-2015 work, making it difficult to assess novelty.  
- Theoretical contribution: a complexity measure favoring small groups as an upper bound on the covering number is novel and well-motivated.  
- Practical issue: the proposed complexity measure does not yield efficient optimization; authors rely on exhaustive search or a greedy method similar to forward feature selection.  
- Lack of discussion on more tractable alternative measures (beyond brief mention) is disappointing.  
- Experiments lack baselines and comparative methods, so results are inconclusive and efficacy is unproven.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors partially addressed concerns, but related work and experiments remain underdeveloped; reviewer modestly raised score but urges substantial enhancement of related work in the final version.
