- Extends additive kernel regression by proposing a "group additive model" to capture correlations between feature dimensions.  
- Provides a formal framework and introduces somewhat ad hoc algorithms for determining optimal groupings; evaluated on small synthetic and real datasets.  
- Major concern: inadequate related work discussion, missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.  
- Related work section is too narrow (mainly cites one KDD-2015 paper), making it hard to judge novelty.  
- Theoretical contribution: novel complexity measure for groupings that favors many small groups, derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- Optimization of the proposed measure is nontrivial; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–style method that scales poorly.  
- Disappointment that no more efficiently optimizable measure was developed; authors note other measures but give no substantive analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to simple synthetic data and Boston Housing and lack baseline comparisons, hindering assessment of empirical quality.  
- After rebuttal some concerns were mitigated, but experimental evaluation and links to broader ML concepts remain underdeveloped; improve related work in final version.
