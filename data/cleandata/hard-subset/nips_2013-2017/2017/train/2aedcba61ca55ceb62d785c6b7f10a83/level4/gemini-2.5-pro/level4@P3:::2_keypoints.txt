1. Addresses high-dimensional nonparametric regression by learning unknown group-additive interaction structure.  
2. Formalizes "intrinsic group additive structure" as the unique minimal partition explaining the data.  
3. Proposes a structure-penalized kernel ridge regression objective with a penalty based on RKHS covering numbers.  
4. Optimizes jointly over regression function and group partitions.  
5. Provides two optimization algorithms: exhaustive search for small p and backward stepwise heuristic for larger p.  
6. Proves consistency of the group structure estimator under appropriate conditions.  
7. Evaluates method on synthetic and real-world data, showing practical potential.  
8. Strength: novel, principled complexity penalty linking group structure to RKHS covering numbers.  
9. Strength: technically sound with comprehensive theoretical backing and consistency proof.  
10. Strength: well-written and clearly structured presentation.  
11. Main weakness: computational scalability—search over partitions grows rapidly with p and limits practicality.  
12. Backward stepwise heuristic is greedy, not guaranteed to find the global optimum and can fail (e.g., Model 4).  
13. Request clarification: provide a simpler/intuitive characterization of "interaction" (e.g., relation to functional ANOVA).  
14. Request clarification: interpret "almost surely (in population)"—equal a.e. under P_X or asymptotic n→∞?  
15. Suggest proofreading and fixes: missing summation in Eq. (3), prefer "translation-invariant kernel," and several minor typos/phrasing corrections.  
16. Overall conclusion: high-quality, significant contribution; primary limitation is computational scalability.
