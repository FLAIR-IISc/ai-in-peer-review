Summary:
This paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models, which offer a balance between fully nonparametric models (difficult to fit) and traditional additive models (sometimes overly restrictive). Specifically, the work addresses the scenario where the group structure is unknown and must be learned from the data. The authors propose a novel penalty function, based on the covering numbers of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) balls, which is added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an optimization problem that can be solved for both the group structure (which, along with the kernel, defines a function space via a direct sum of RKHSs over each group of variables) and the regression estimate within each group. Two algorithms are presented for approximately solving this compound optimization problem, accompanied by theoretical results demonstrating (a) the rate at which the empirical risk of the estimate converges to the true risk of the optimal estimator, and (b) the consistency of the group structure estimate—that is, the probability that the estimated group structure matches the true structure approaches 1 as n approaches infinity. The paper concludes with experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets.

Main Comments:
The key innovation of this paper appears to be the recognition that the complexity of a given group structure can be quantified using covering numbers of the corresponding direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, presenting a well-motivated and novel method, supported by reasonably strong theoretical and empirical results. While I am not intimately familiar with all alternative approaches for fitting models between fully additive and fully nonparametric models, assuming the discussion in Lines 31-50 is a fair and complete overview, this work appears to represent a potentially significant advance. As the authors note in the Discussion section, the primary challenge with their method is the computational difficulty of solving the optimization problem over the group structure when the number of variables is large. Aside from a number of typos, the paper is clearly written.
