- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, starting from an additive model f = ∑ fp(Xp) that ignores inter-dimension correlations.  
- Introduces a "group additive model" to capture correlations by grouping dimensions.  
- Provides a formal treatment and algorithms to find optimal groupings, but the proposed methods appear somewhat ad hoc.  
- Empirical results are limited to small synthetic examples and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Related work discussion is inadequate, making novelty hard to assess for non-experts.  
- Important connections (structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, feature selection) are not discussed.  
- Related work section largely cites a single KDD-2015 paper [8], which is insufficient.  
- Theoretical contribution seems novel: a complexity measure for groupings (upper bound on covering number) that favors many small groups.  
- The complexity measure is sensible but not amenable to efficient optimization.  
- Authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like method, which scale poorly.  
- Authors mention exploring alternative measures but provide no details or comparative insights.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, score improved, but experiments remain limited and related-work coverage should be strengthened in the final version.
