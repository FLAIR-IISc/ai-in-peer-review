- Addresses high-dimensional nonparametric regression by proposing a "group additive model" that allows nonparametric interactions within variable groups while keeping additivity across groups.
- Contribution: defines the "intrinsic group additive structure" as the unique, minimal grouping representation for a given function.
- Contribution: proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to jointly identify the intrinsic group structure and estimate nonparametric components.
- Contribution: derives a novel complexity penalty C(G) based on an upper bound on the covering number of the associated RKHS.
- Contribution: proves consistency (Theorem 4) showing the method identifies the intrinsic group structure with probability → 1 as sample size increases.
- Contribution: provides two optimization algorithms — exhaustive search for small problems and a backward stepwise greedy algorithm for larger problems.
- Evaluated on multiple synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing dataset.
- Strength: provides a rigorous formalization of group additive models, including partial ordering and uniqueness/minimality (Theorem 1).
- Strength: grounds the structural penalty C(G) in learning theory via RKHS covering numbers, making it principled.
- Strength: supplies theoretical consistency guarantees for structure identification.
- Weakness: related-work discussion is narrow, omitting extensive literature on structured sparsity (e.g., group LASSO), graphical model/Bayesian network structure learning, and automated feature construction.
- Weakness: the derived penalty leads to a difficult combinatorial optimization; exhaustive search is infeasible and the greedy backward stepwise algorithm has no optimality guarantees and can fail.
- Weakness: optimization choices are not well-justified and the claim of investigating alternatives is not substantiated.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks baselines (e.g., Kernel Ridge Regression, functional ANOVA, gradient boosting) and is limited to small-scale problems, making practical significance unclear.
- Recommendation: expand related work to contextualize contributions within structured sparsity and graphical model learning, and strengthen empirical evaluation with relevant baselines and scalable optimization methods.
