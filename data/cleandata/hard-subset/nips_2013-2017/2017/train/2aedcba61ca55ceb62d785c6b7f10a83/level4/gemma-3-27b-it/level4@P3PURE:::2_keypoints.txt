- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression with unknown group partition learned from data.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, incorporated into kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group structure and group-wise functions.  
- Provides two algorithms for approximately solving the joint optimization over group structure and functions.  
- Gives theoretical guarantees: empirical risk convergence rates and consistency of group-structure recovery (probability of correct partition → 1 as n → ∞).  
- Presents experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing the method interpolates between fully nonparametric and additive models.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Main limitation: computational difficulty of optimizing group structure when the number of variables is large.  
- Paper is well-motivated and comprehensive but contains several typographical errors.
