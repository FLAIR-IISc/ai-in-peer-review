- Introduces an additive kernel regression model assuming f(x)=sum_p f_p(x_p), then generalizes to a group additive model to capture correlations by grouping dimensions.

- Formally develops the group-additive framework and provides algorithms for identifying optimal groupings.

- Evaluations are limited to small-scale synthetic datasets and a basic real-world example (Boston Housing).

- Related work discussion is inadequate, making it hard for non-experts to judge novelty.

- Fails to connect to relevant literature on structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.

- Related work mainly cites a single KDD-2015 paper [8], which is insufficient coverage.

- Theoretical contribution: defines a novel complexity measure for groupings based on an upper bound on the covering number that favors many small groups.

- The complexity measure is sensible but not amenable to efficient optimization; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy method.

- Disappointing lack of more advanced optimization techniques beyond cross-validation for tuning/group selection.

- Experimental section is underdeveloped and lacks baselines or comparisons to alternative methods, making results hard to assess.

- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments and related work remain insufficient; reviewer recommends improving related work in the final version.
