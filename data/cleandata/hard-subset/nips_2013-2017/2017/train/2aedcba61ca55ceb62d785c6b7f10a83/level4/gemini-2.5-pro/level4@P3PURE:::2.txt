### Summary

This paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models. In these models, the regression function is additive across groups of predictor variables, based on a partition of those variables. This model class interpolates between the fully nonparametric model, which is difficult to fit, and the additive model, which can be overly restrictive. The paper specifically addresses the problem of learning this group structure from data when it is not known a priori.

To achieve this, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is incorporated into the kernel ridge regression objective. The resulting objective function can be optimized over both the group structure and the regression estimate. The group structure, in conjunction with the kernel, defines the function space as a direct sum of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) over the variable groups. The authors present two algorithms for approximately solving this challenging optimization problem. They also provide theoretical results establishing (a) the convergence rate of the estimator's risk to the optimal risk and (b) the consistency of the group structure estimate, showing that the probability of recovering the true group structure approaches 1 as the sample size increases. Finally, the paper validates the proposed method with experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

### Main Comments

The key innovation of this paper is the idea of quantifying the complexity of a group structure using the covering numbers of the corresponding direct sum of RKHSs. The paper is comprehensive, presenting a well-motivated and novel method supported by solid theoretical analysis and empirical results.

I am not an expert on models that interpolate between additive and fully nonparametric approaches; however, assuming the literature review in Lines 31-50 is accurate and reasonably complete, this work appears to be a potentially significant advance. As the authors acknowledge in the discussion, a key limitation of the proposed method is the difficulty of optimizing over the group structure when the number of variables is large. The paper is generally well-written and clear, although it contains numerous typographical errors.
