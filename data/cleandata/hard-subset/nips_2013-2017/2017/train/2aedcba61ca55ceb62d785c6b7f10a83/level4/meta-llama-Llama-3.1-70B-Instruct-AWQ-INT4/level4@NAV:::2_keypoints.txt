- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the predictor partition is unknown and must be inferred from data.  
- Method bridges fully nonparametric and additive models by allowing group-wise additive structure.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into kernel ridge regression.  
- Objective jointly optimizes over group structure and within-group regression estimates.  
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the combined optimization problem.  
- Provides theoretical results: empirical risk converges to true risk and group-structure estimator is consistent.  
- Empirical evaluation includes both synthetic and real-data experiments supporting the approach.  
- Main innovation: complexity of a group structure quantified via covering numbers of the direct-sum space.  
- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, and potentially a significant advance assuming completeness of the referenced discussion.  
- Primary limitation: optimization over group structures becomes computationally difficult as the number of variables grows.  
- Writing is generally good but contains several typos.  
- Minor questions: need a simpler characterization of variable interaction than full model (Eq.1); clarify use of “almost surely” in Line 169; Equation (3) lacks a summation over i; consider using “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”  
- Typos/corrections: Line 145 “on RHS” → “on the RHS”; Line 152 “reduces” → “reduce”; Line 160 “G^ exists and unique” → “G^ exists and is unique”; Line 247 “turning parameters” → “tuning parameters”; Algorithm 2 Line 1 “State with” → “start with”; Line 251 “by compare” → “by comparing.”
