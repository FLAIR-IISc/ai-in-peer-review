Paper proposes a group additive model for kernel regression to capture correlations within predefined feature groups, with formal analysis and algorithms for group identification.

Theoretical contribution: introduces a complexity measure (upper bound on the covering number) that favors many small groups; derivation appears sound.

Optimization issue: the complexity measure is difficult to optimize, requiring exhaustive search or a greedy method similar to forward feature selection.

Authors note alternative measures (line 174) but give no details, preventing assessment of better optimizable alternatives.

Major weakness: inadequate placement in broader ML literature—fails to discuss connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.

Related work is narrowly reviewed (mainly one KDD-2015 paper [8]), making it hard to judge novelty.

Experimental evaluation is limited to simple synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing case study.

Crucially, experiments lack baseline comparisons, so reported performance gains cannot be meaningfully assessed.

Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments and related-work treatment remain insufficient; reviewer still recognizes valid contributions and raised the score.
