- Proposes a group additive model that partitions dimensions into groups to capture intra-group dependencies while keeping additivity across groups.  
- Provides a formal theoretical treatment and introduces a complexity measure favoring many small groups, derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The proposed complexity measure is not amenable to efficient optimization; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection heuristic.  
- Related work discussion is insufficient and overly focused on a single KDD 2015 paper.  
- Fails to situate the work relative to structured sparsity (e.g., group LASSO), graphical model learning, and broader feature selection literature.  
- The rationale for choosing the specific complexity measure is under-explained; brief mention of alternative measures is inadequate.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a simple synthetic example and the Boston Housing dataset, which is insufficient to demonstrate practical utility.  
- No baselines or comparisons to relevant methods from related fields, making it impossible to judge competitiveness.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns addressed but major weaknesses remain—experiments still insufficient and literature positioning still lacking.  
- Positive: the theoretical contribution appears novel and valid; reviewer requests substantial expansion of related work and stronger empirical validation in the final version.
