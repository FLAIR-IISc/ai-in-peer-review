- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression models that interpolate between fully nonparametric and additive models.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls to learn group structure from data.  
- Key innovation: quantifies group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum space.  
- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, and generally well-written (few typos).  
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the compound optimization problem.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of group-structure estimation.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Main scalability issue: optimizing over group structures is difficult for large numbers of variables.  
- Authors propose a backward stepwise algorithm to mitigate scalability but note the need for more efficient methods.  
- Minor technical/comment questions: need a simpler characterization of variable interaction and clarification of “almost surely” (n → ∞).  
- Technical fixes: Equation (3) missing summation; consider “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”  
- Several typos and phrasing errors throughout (examples provided by reviewer).  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated method for learning group structure in group-additive models.  
- Strength: solid theoretical results supporting the estimator and group recovery.  
- Strength: convincing experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: optimization over group structure remains computationally challenging at scale.  
- Weakness: paper needs a more detailed discussion comparing the proposed method to other approaches between additive and nonparametric models.  
- Originality: introduces a new penalty and perspective for learning group structure via RKHS covering numbers.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem in nonparametric regression with practical applicability.  
- Clarity/quality: technically sound and organized, but would benefit from more discussion of limitations, comparisons, and typo fixes.
