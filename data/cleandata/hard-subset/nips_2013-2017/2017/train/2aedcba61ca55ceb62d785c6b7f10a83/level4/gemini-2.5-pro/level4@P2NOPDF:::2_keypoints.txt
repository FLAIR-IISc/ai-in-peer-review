- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression that learns variable grouping to interpolate between additive and fully nonparametric models.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, incorporated into kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group structure and function.  
- Defines overall function space as a direct sum of per-group RKHSs determined by the chosen grouping.  
- Presents two algorithms for approximate optimization over group structures.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rate for empirical risk and consistency of estimated group structure.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Key insight: complexity of a grouping can be quantified via covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum function space.  
- Literature review appears thorough and the work is potentially significant if prior-art discussion (Lines 31–50) is accurate.  
- Major limitation: computational difficulty of optimizing group structure when the number of predictors is large.  
- Paper is generally well-written but needs careful proofreading to fix numerous typographical errors.  
- Minor methodological/questions: request clearer, intuitive definition of “interaction” beyond group membership; clarify “almost surely” in Line 169 (asymptotic?); Equation (3) seems to miss a summation over i; suggest using “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”  
- Noted typos/corrections: Line 145 “on RHS” → “on the RHS”; Line 152 grammar on bias reduction; Line 160 “G^ exists and unique” → “G^ exists and is unique”; Line 247 “turning parameters” → “tuning parameters”; Line 251 “by compare” → “by comparing”; Algorithm 2, Line 1 “State with” → “Start with.”
