- Problem: studies group-additive nonparametric regression where group structure is unknown and must be learned from data.  
- Method: proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression.  
- Optimization: objective optimized over both group structure and per-group regression estimates; two approximate algorithms are presented.  
- Theory: proves empirical risk convergence rates and consistency of group-structure recovery (probability of correct grouping → 1 as n → ∞).  
- Experiments: validates method on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Key innovation: quantifies complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Strengths: comprehensive, well-motivated approach with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Main issue: optimization over group structures is computationally difficult when the number of variables is large.  
- Reviewer limitation: not familiar with other intermediate approaches; assumes literature discussion (Lines 31–50) is complete.  
- Open question from reviewer: is there a simple characterization of interaction between two variables that avoids full-model specification?
