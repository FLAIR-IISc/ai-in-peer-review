- Proposes a kernel regression "group additive model" that groups correlated dimensions to extend traditional additive models.  
- Provides a formal treatment and algorithms for finding optimal groupings.  
- Demonstrates results on small synthetic datasets and one real-world (Boston Housing) case.  
- Related work discussion is limited, making novelty hard to assess.  
- Fails to cite connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Only cites a single KDD-2015 paper as related work, which is insufficient.  
- The theoretical contribution (a complexity measure on groupings favoring many small groups via a covering-number bound) appears novel.  
- The complexity measure is sensible but difficult to optimize in practice.  
- Proposed optimization relies on exhaustive search or a greedy method; no efficient alternative developed.  
- Authors mention comparing different measures but provide no further insights or results.  
- Experimental validation is limited and simplistic, lacking diverse real-world tests.  
- No baseline comparisons or evaluations against other methods, making results hard to judge.  
- Overall judgement: valid contributions but weakened by poor related-work discussion and limited experiments.  
- Review scores: Quality 6, Clarity 7, Originality 7, Impact 5, Overall Score 6.2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model extension and novel theoretical treatment.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited related work, weak experimental validation, and inefficient optimization.
