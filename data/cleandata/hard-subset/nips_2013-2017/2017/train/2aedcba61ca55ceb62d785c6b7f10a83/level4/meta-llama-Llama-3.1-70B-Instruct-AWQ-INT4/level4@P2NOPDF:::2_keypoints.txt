- Investigates group-additive nonparametric regression models that interpolate between fully nonparametric and additive models, learning unknown group structure from data.

- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to the kernel ridge regression objective to jointly optimize group structure and per-group regression.

- Defines the function space as the direct sum of RKHSs determined by group structure and kernel.

- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the combined optimization over group structure and regression functions.

- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of the group-structure estimator (probability of recovering true grouping → 1 as n → ∞).

- Includes experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets.

- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.

- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, novel, and potentially a significant advance with solid theory and experiments.

- Main limitation: optimization over group structures becomes difficult as the number of variables grows.

- Writing is generally clear but contains several typos.

- Minor points/questions: request for a simpler characterization of variable interaction; clarify "almost surely" in Line 169 (as n → ∞?); Equation (3) missing summation; suggest using "translation invariant kernel" instead of "convolutional kernel."
