Summary:
This study investigates group-additive nonparametric regression models, which interpolate between fully nonparametric models, often difficult to fit, and additive models, sometimes too restrictive. Specifically, the paper focuses on the problem where the group structure is unknown and must be learned from the data. To address this, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an objective that can be optimized over both the group structure and the regression estimate within each group. Two algorithms are presented to approximately solve this compound optimization problem, followed by theoretical results demonstrating (a) the rate at which the empirical risk of the estimate approaches the true risk of the true optimum, and (b) consistency of the group structure estimate, where the probability of matching the true group structure approaches 1 as n approaches infinity. Experimental results are also presented on both synthetic and real data.

Main Comments:
The key innovation of this paper appears to be the recognition that the complexity of a particular group structure can be quantified in terms of covering numbers of the direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, featuring a well-motivated and novel method, as well as reasonably solid theoretical and empirical results. Assuming the discussion in Lines 31-50 is fairly complete, this paper seems to represent a potentially significant advance in the field, given the dearth of approaches to fitting models between additive and nonparametric models. The main issue with the method, as noted in the Discussion section, is the difficulty in solving the optimization problem over the group structure when the number of variables is large. The paper is generally well-written, aside from a number of typos.
