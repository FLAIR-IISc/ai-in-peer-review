- Problem: studies group-additive nonparametric regression with unknown group structure to be learned from data.  
- Method: introduces a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, incorporated into kernel ridge regression.  
- Optimization: formulates joint optimization over group structure (direct-sum RKHS) and within-group regression, and proposes two approximate algorithms.  
- Theory: proves empirical risk convergence rates and consistency of the estimated group structure (probability →1 as n→∞).  
- Experiments: provides synthetic and real-data validation supporting the approach.  
- Key innovation: quantifies group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum function space.  
- Limitation: computationally challenging to optimize over group structures for large numbers of variables.  
- Presentation: paper is generally clear but contains numerous typos.  
- Minor queries: ask for a simpler characterization of pairwise “interaction” avoiding Eq.(1); confirm “almost surely” refers to n→∞ (Line 169); fix missing summation in Eq.(3); prefer term “translation-invariant kernel” (Line 205).
