- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression that interpolates between fully nonparametric and additive models and aims to learn the variable partition/group structure from data.
- Authors propose a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into a kernel ridge regression objective.
- The objective is optimized over both group structure and regression estimate, with the function space defined as a direct sum of RKHSs over groups.
- Two algorithms are presented to approximately solve the optimization, along with theoretical guarantees.
- Theoretical results include estimator risk convergence rates and consistency of group-structure recovery (probability of recovering true groups → 1 as n increases).
- Empirical validation is provided on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum RKHS.
- Reviewer views the work as potentially significant if the literature review (Lines 31–50) is accurate, though not an expert on intermediate models.
- Main limitation: optimizing over group structures is difficult when the number of variables is large.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear but contains numerous typographical errors.
- Minor question: request for a simpler characterization of "interaction" between two variables that does not rely on the full model definition.
- Minor clarification needed: Line 169’s use of "almost surely" — does this refer to the limit as n → ∞?
- Minor error: Equation (3) appears to be missing a summation over index i.
- Terminology suggestion: prefer "translation-invariant kernel" to "convolutional kernel" (Line 205).
