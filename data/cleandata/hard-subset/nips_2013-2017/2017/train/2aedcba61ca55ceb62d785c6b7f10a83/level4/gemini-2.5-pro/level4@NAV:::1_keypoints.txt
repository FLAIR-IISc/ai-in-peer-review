- Studies kernel regression in high dimensions by extending additive models (f = Σ f_p(X_p)) which cannot capture inter-dimensional correlations.  
- Proposes a "group additive model" that partitions dimensions into groups to model within-group correlations.  
- Provides a formal theoretical analysis and defines a complexity measure favoring many small groups (derived from an upper bound on the covering number).  
- Complexity measure appears reasonable but is not amenable to efficient optimization.  
- Authors propose exhaustive search (not scalable) or a greedy forward-selection–style algorithm for grouping.  
- Disappointing that the measure does not permit a more efficient optimization strategy than cross-validation.  
- Authors mention investigating alternative measures but give no discussion or justification for the final choice.  
- Introduces several heuristic algorithms to identify groupings.  
- Demonstrates the approach on small-scale synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Experimental validation is limited and lacks baselines or comparisons to alternative methods, so competitiveness is unclear.  
- Major related-work omission: fails to discuss structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection literature.  
- Related work section is overly focused on a single KDD 2015 paper and insufficient to situate the contribution.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments remain preliminary and related-work coverage is still inadequate.  
- Reviewer acknowledges valid contributions, raised score, and strongly recommends substantially expanding related work for the camera-ready version.
