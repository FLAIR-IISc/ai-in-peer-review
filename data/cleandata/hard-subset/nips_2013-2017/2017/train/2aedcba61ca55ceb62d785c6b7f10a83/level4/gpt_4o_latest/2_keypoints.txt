- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the function is additive across unknown groups of predictors.  
- Authors propose a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into kernel ridge regression.  
- The method jointly optimizes over group structure and per-group regression functions defined by direct-sum RKHSs.  
- Two approximate algorithms are provided to solve the joint optimization.  
- Theoretical results show convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk.  
- Theoretical results also prove consistency of group-structure estimation (probability of correct grouping → 1 as n → ∞).  
- Experiments are reported on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrating the approach.  
- Main conceptual contribution: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum space.  
- Paper is well-motivated and appears to advance methods between fully nonparametric and additive models.  
- Primary limitation: computational difficulty scaling the group-structure optimization to high-dimensional problems.  
- Writing is generally clear but the manuscript contains numerous typos that should be fixed.  
- Minor question: request for a simpler characterization of pairwise interaction not requiring full model expression.  
- Minor technical/questions: unclear use of “almost surely” (Line 169), missing summation in Equation (3), and suggestion to use “translation-invariant kernel” (Line 205).  
- Listed typographical corrections for specific lines and Algorithm 2 wording.
