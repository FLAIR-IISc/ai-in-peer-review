- Proposes a group additive model for kernel regression that models correlations within groups of dimensions.  
- Provides formal treatment and algorithms for learning optimal groupings.  
- Demonstrates approach on small synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion and engagement with related literature.  
- Fails to connect to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Related work section overly focused on a single KDD-2015 paper and is inadequate.  
- Theoretical contribution: defines a complexity measure favoring many small groups, derived from a covering number bound.  
- The complexity measure is intuitively sensible but difficult to optimize directly.  
- Optimization relies on exhaustive search or a greedy method akin to forward feature selection.  
- Lacks a more efficiently optimizable measure or detailed alternatives (only brief mention of ongoing work).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks baselines or comparisons to existing methods.  
- Post-rebuttal: some clarifications were provided, but concerns about limited experiments and related work remain; recommends substantially expanding related work coverage.
