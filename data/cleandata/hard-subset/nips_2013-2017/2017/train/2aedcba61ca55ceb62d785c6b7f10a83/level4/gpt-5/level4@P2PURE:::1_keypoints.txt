- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, moving from additive models f = ∑_p f_p(X_p) to group additive models that model correlations within groups.  
- Authors present a formal framework for group additive models and propose algorithms for identifying groupings.  
- Proposed grouping algorithms are somewhat ad hoc, relying on exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–style approach.  
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure over groupings (favoring many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The complexity measure is not easy to optimize, and the paper offers no efficient alternative to cross-validation.  
- Authors mention exploring other measures but give no details, making it hard to assess measure suitability.  
- Experiments are limited to simple synthetic datasets and a small Boston Housing example.  
- No baselines or comparisons to other methods are provided, leaving experimental effectiveness unassessed.  
- Major concern: inadequate discussion of related work; key connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection are omitted.  
- Related work is effectively reduced to a single KDD-2015 paper, insufficient to judge novelty.  
- After rebuttal, some concerns were addressed, but experiments remain thin and the paper still poorly situates itself within broader ML literature.  
- Reviewer acknowledges valid contributions and raised the score somewhat but strongly urges improved related-work discussion in the final version.
