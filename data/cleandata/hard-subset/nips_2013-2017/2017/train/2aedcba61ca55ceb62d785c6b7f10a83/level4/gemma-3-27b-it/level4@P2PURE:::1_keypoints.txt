- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, moving from additive models to a new “group additive model” that allows within-group correlations.
- Authors provide a formal treatment of the group additive setting and propose algorithms for finding groupings, described as somewhat ad hoc.
- Experiments are limited to small synthetic datasets and real-world cases (Boston Housing).
- Major concern: related work discussion is inadequate, making novelty hard to assess for non-specialists.
- Missing citations/connections to structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection literature.
- Related work section over-focuses on one KDD-2015 paper [8] and lacks broader contextualization.
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure for groupings derived as an upper bound on the covering number that favors many small groups.
- Practical issue: the complexity measure is hard to optimize, leading to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like method.
- Authors mention alternative measures (line 174) but give no details, preventing assessment of measure suitability.
- Experimental shortcomings: no baselines or comparisons to existing methods, so significance of results is unclear.
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments remain limited and contextualization in ML literature is still insufficient.
- Strong recommendation: substantially expand the related work discussion before publication; reviewer adjusted score but remains unconvinced on some points.
