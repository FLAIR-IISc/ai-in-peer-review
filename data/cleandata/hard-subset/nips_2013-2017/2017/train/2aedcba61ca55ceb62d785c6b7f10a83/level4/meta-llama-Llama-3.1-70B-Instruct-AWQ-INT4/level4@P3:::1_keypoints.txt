- Proposes a novel group additive model for high-dimensional nonparametric regression that models interactions while avoiding the curse of dimensionality.  
- Formally defines intrinsic group additive structure and introduces a complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers.  
- Complexity measure theoretically favors many small groups but is hard to optimize in practice.  
- Proposes two structure-identification algorithms: exhaustive search and backward stepwise.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant, especially the derivation linking complexity to RKHS covering numbers.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic data and a single real dataset (Boston Housing).  
- Results are not compared to baseline methods or other relevant approaches, hindering assessment of novelty and impact.  
- Major omission: insufficient discussion of related work (e.g., structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks).  
- Rebuttal addressed some concerns, but experimental evaluation and related-work discussion remain inadequate.  
- Strengths: novel approach, rigorous model treatment, and a derived RKHS-based complexity measure.  
- Weaknesses: limited experiments, lack of related-work discussion, and no baseline comparisons.  
- Suggestions: expand related-work discussion, include thorough comparisons with existing methods, explore alternative optimization for the complexity measure, and provide more experimental results.  
- Overall assessment: valid theoretical contributions but needs stronger experiments and literature integration.
