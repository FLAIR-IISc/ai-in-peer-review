- Addresses high-dimensional kernel regression by proposing a "group additive model" to capture within-group feature correlations instead of standard additive models.  
- Provides a formal treatment, several algorithms for finding optimal groupings, and results on small synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Related work discussion is inadequate, omitting connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Related work section relies almost entirely on a single reference, insufficiently situating the contributions.  
- Theoretical contribution: defines a complexity measure for partitions derived as an upper bound on the covering number, favoring many small groups.  
- The complexity measure is not amenable to efficient optimization; authors use exhaustive search and a greedy forward-selection-like heuristic.  
- Disappointing that the framework does not yield a measure more efficiently optimizable than standard cross-validation; no details on other measures investigated.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a simple synthetic example and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- No comparisons to baselines or alternative approaches, making it impossible to assess practical performance.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns clarified, theoretical contributions acknowledged and score raised, but experiments and literature positioning remain weak.  
- Strong recommendation to substantially expand the related work discussion in the final paper.
