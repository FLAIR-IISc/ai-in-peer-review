Summary:
The paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models in which, for some partition of the predictor variables, the regression function is additive across groups. This framework interpolates between fully nonparametric models (often difficult to fit) and classical additive models (sometimes too restrictive). The group structure is assumed unknown and must be learned from data. To address this, the paper introduces a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This yields an objective that can be optimized jointly over the group structure (which, together with the kernel, defines a function space via the direct sum of RKHSs over each group) and the group-wise regression functions. Two algorithms are proposed to approximately solve this compound optimization. Theoretical results establish (a) a rate at which the empirical risk of the estimator approaches the true risk of the population optimum, and (b) consistency of the estimated group structure, in the sense that the probability of recovering the true group structure converges to 1 as n -> infinity. The paper also presents experiments on synthetic and real data.

Main Comments:
- The key innovation is to quantify the complexity of a given group structure via covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum RKHS. This is a well-motivated and novel idea.
- The paper is comprehensive, combining a new method with reasonably solid theoretical guarantees and empirical results. I am not deeply familiar with other approaches that interpolate between additive and fully nonparametric models, but, assuming the discussion in Lines 31–50 is fairly complete, this appears to be a potentially significant advance.
- As noted in the Discussion, the main practical challenge is the difficulty of optimizing over group structures when the number of variables is large.
- The paper is generally clearly written, aside from numerous typos.
