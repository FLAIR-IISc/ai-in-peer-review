- Tackles kernel regression in high dimensions by revisiting group additive models that allow interactions within groups but not across them.  
- Provides a formal definition of an “intrinsic group additive structure.”  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method to jointly identify group structure and estimate nonparametric functions.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure for group structures derived from covering number bounds.  
- Theoretical analysis shows consistency in identifying the intrinsic group structure.  
- Empirical validation includes simulations and an application to the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Core contribution: formal treatment of group additive models offering a middle ground between additive models and full interaction models.  
- Novelty: using covering numbers to define a complexity penalty for group structures.  
- Major weakness: insufficient engagement with related work (structured sparsity, graphical models, feature selection) limits assessment of novelty.  
- Optimization concern: reliance on exhaustive search or greedy approaches raises scalability and practicality issues; alternative measures mentioned but not developed.  
- Experimental weakness: evaluations lack baseline comparisons and benchmarks, making practical benefits unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: formalization of group additive models, interesting complexity measure, and theoretical consistency guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor related-work coverage, limited optimization scalability, and weak experimental evaluation.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising, but needs stronger related-work connections and more comprehensive empirical validation.
