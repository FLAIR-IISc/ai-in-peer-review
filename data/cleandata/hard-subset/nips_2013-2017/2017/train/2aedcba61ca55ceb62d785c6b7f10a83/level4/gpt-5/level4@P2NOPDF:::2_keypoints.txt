The paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression where predictors are partitioned into unknown groups and the regression function is additive across groups.  
The authors introduce a new penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls and add it to the kernel ridge regression objective.  
The optimization is over both the group partition (defining a direct-sum RKHS) and the within-group regression functions; two approximate algorithms are proposed.  
Theoretical guarantees include rates for empirical risk convergence to the population risk and consistency of the estimated group structure (recovery probability → 1 as n → ∞).  
Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the method’s empirical performance.  
Central contribution: quantifying complexity of a group partition via covering numbers of the associated direct-sum RKHS.  
Strengths: well-motivated, novel approach with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
Main practical limitation: optimizing over group structures is difficult when the number of variables is large.  
Clarity: paper is generally clear but marred by numerous typos.  
Minor question: request a simple definition of “interaction” between two variables that does not require expressing the full model as in Eq. (1).  
Clarification needed: use of “almost surely” at Line 169—does this refer to the n → ∞ limit?  
Error: Equation (3) appears to be missing a summation over i.  
Terminology suggestion: use “translation-invariant kernel” rather than “convolutional kernel” at Line 205.  
Typos noted (examples): “on RHS” → “on the RHS” (L145); grammatical and phrasing fixes at L152, L160, L247, Algorithm 2 L1, and L251.
