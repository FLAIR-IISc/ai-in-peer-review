- Proposes a novel method to identify group additive structures in nonparametric regression, filling a gap between additive and fully nonparametric models.  
- Defines the group additive model where predictors are partitioned into groups additive across groups but allowing interactions within groups.  
- Introduces a new complexity measure for group additive structures based on RKHS covering numbers.  
- Incorporates this complexity measure into a penalized kernel ridge regression objective to jointly estimate the function and group structure.  
- Provides rigorous consistency proofs for the proposed estimator.  
- Proposes two practical algorithms for implementation: exhaustive search and a stepwise approach.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data show effective recovery of true group structures and practical utility (e.g., Boston Housing).  
- Strong technical quality and comprehensive theoretical analysis; well-motivated problem with clear ties to existing work.  
- Clearly distinguishes contribution from related work (functional ANOVA, graphical models) and addresses limitations of Pan et al. (2015).  
- Significance: offers a middle ground between interpretability and flexibility with applicability in high-dimensional regression.  
- Weakness: several typos and grammatical errors that detract from readability.  
- Weakness: discussion of "interaction" is vague and could be aligned more clearly with standard statistical definitions.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability for very large p, especially regarding the stepwise algorithm.  
- Weakness: could better explain scenarios where this method is preferable to alternatives.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality contribution, empirically validated and theoretically sound; recommend acceptance.
