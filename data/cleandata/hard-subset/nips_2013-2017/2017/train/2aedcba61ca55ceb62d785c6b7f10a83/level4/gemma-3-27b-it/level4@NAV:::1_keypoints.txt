- Introduces a group additive model for kernel regression in high dimensions that allows intra-group correlations, extending beyond standard additive models.  
- Provides a formal analysis of the grouped structure and proposes heuristic algorithms to identify effective groupings.  
- Empirical evaluation uses synthetic data and a small real dataset (Boston Housing).  
- Fails to adequately situate the work within existing literature, missing links to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Related work section over-relies on a single KDD-2015 paper, limiting assessment of novelty.  
- Theoretical contribution is original: a complexity measure (upper bound on covering number) that favors many small groups.  
- The complexity measure is difficult to optimize; proposed exhaustive search and greedy approaches are computationally weak and rely on cross-validation.  
- Authors mention alternative measures but provide no details or justification for the chosen measure.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks baseline comparisons, hindering interpretation of results.  
- After rebuttal, some concerns were addressed but empirical evaluation and literature connections remain insufficient; reviewer urges expanded related work before publication.
