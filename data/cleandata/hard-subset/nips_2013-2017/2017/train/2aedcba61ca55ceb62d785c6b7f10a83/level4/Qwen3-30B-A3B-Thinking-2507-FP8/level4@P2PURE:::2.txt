Summary:  
This work investigates group-additive nonparametric regression models, where the regression function is additive across disjoint groups of predictor variables. The model interpolates between the fully nonparametric approach (difficult to fit) and the restrictive additive model. Specifically, the paper addresses the scenario where the group structure is unknown and must be learned from data. To this end, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, incorporated into the kernel ridge regression objective. This yields an objective that can be optimized jointly over the group structure (defining a function space via direct sums of RKHSs over groups) and the regression estimate within each group. Two algorithms are developed to approximately solve this compound optimization problem. Theoretical results establish (a) the rate at which the empirical risk of the estimate converges to the true risk of the optimal solution, and (b) consistency of the group structure estimate (probability of matching the true structure approaches 1 as $n \to \infty$). Experimental validation is provided on synthetic and real data.  

Main Comments:  
The paper’s key innovation lies in recognizing that the complexity of a group structure can be quantified via covering numbers of the direct sum space. The work is comprehensive, presenting a well-motivated, novel method with reasonably solid theoretical and empirical results. I am not deeply familiar with other approaches for models between additive and fully nonparametric forms, but assuming the discussion in Lines 31–50 is sufficiently complete, this represents a potentially significant advance. As noted in the Discussion, the primary limitation is computational difficulty in optimizing over group structure when the number of variables is large. The paper is otherwise clearly written, aside from numerous typos.
