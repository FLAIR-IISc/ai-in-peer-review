Summary:  
This paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models, where the regression function is additive across groups of predictor variables for an unknown partition. The model bridges the gap between fully nonparametric regression (computationally challenging) and restrictive additive models. The authors propose a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, integrated into kernel ridge regression. The resulting objective optimizes jointly over the group structure (defining a function space via direct sum of RKHSs per group) and the regression estimate within groups. Two algorithms approximate the compound optimization, and theoretical results establish (a) convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk, and (b) consistency of the estimated group structure (probability of matching the true structure approaches 1 as n → ∞). Experimental validation is provided on synthetic and real data.  
Main Comments:  
The key innovation lies in quantifying the complexity of a group structure using covering numbers of the direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, featuring a well-motivated novel method with solid theoretical and empirical results. Assuming the discussion in Lines 31–50 is sufficiently complete, this represents a potentially significant advance for models between additive and fully nonparametric forms. As noted in the Discussion, the primary limitation is computational difficulty in optimizing over group structure for large variable sets. The paper is clearly written except for numerous typos.
