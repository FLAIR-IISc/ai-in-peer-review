This study examines group-additive nonparametric regression models, where the regression function is additive across groups of variables for a specific partitioning of the predictor variables. This model bridges the gap between the fully nonparametric model, which is challenging to fit, and the additive model, which can be overly restrictive. The paper focuses on the scenario where the group structure is unknown a priori and must be inferred from the data. To address this, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is incorporated into the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an objective that can be optimized over both the group structure and the regression estimate within each group. The authors present two algorithms for approximately solving this compound optimization problem and provide theoretical results demonstrating the convergence rate of the empirical risk of the estimate to the true risk of the true optimum and the consistency of the group structure estimate. Experimental results are presented for both synthetic and real data.

The paper's primary innovation appears to be the recognition that the complexity of a particular group structure can be quantified using covering numbers of the direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, featuring a well-motivated and novel method, as well as solid theoretical and empirical results. Assuming the discussion in Lines 31-50 is complete, this paper seems to represent a potentially significant advance in the field. The main limitation of the method, as noted in the Discussion section, is the difficulty in solving the optimization problem over group structure when the number of variables is large.
