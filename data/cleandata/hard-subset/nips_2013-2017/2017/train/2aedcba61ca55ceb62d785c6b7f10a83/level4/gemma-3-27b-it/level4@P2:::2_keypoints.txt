- Proposes group-additive nonparametric regression that learns variable group structure from data to balance flexibility and curse of dimensionality.  
- Introduces a novel penalty based on RKHS covering numbers integrated into kernel ridge regression to jointly estimate groups and functions.  
- Presents two algorithms for approximate optimization and provides theoretical guarantees for empirical risk convergence and group consistency.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing effectiveness for moderate-sized problems.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Paper is comprehensive, addressing both statistical and algorithmic aspects.  
- Strong theoretical rigor with guarantees for risk and structure consistency.  
- Clear motivation contrasting fully nonparametric and standard additive models.  
- Empirical validation supports the method’s utility.  
- Major weakness: computational scalability — exhaustive search is impractical for many predictors and the stepwise alternative is inconsistently effective.  
- Manuscript contains numerous typographical errors and occasional awkward phrasing that hurt readability.  
- Comparative evaluation with existing scalable methods is limited and could be expanded.  
- Conceptual motivation for using covering numbers needs more intuitive explanation for non-experts.  
- Specific comment: request for a simpler, more intuitive characterization of interactions than specifying the full model.  
- Specific comment: clarify "almost surely" usage (convergence as n→∞).  
- Specific correction: Equation (3) should include summation over i=1..n.  
- Terminology suggestions and typos listed (e.g., "convolutional kernel" → "translation invariant kernel"; multiple line-specific wording fixes).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; covering-number complexity measure; empirical success on moderate datasets.  
- Arguments for rejection: scalability concerns, presentation typos, and insufficient comparison with other methods.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance provided authors fix typos, clarify concepts, and discuss scalability improvements.
