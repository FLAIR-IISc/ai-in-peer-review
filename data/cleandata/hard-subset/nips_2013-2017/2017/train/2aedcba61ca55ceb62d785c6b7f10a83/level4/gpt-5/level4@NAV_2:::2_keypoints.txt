- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression and proposes learning the unknown group structure from data using a penalty based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS balls.  
- Method adds the new structural penalty to kernel ridge regression and jointly optimizes group structure and within-group functions.  
- Authors provide two algorithms to approximately solve the joint problem and theoretical guarantees: empirical risk converges to population risk and group selection is consistent as n → ∞.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data support the method; approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Main practical limitation: combinatorial search over group structures is expensive when p is large.  
- Exposition is mostly clear but marred by numerous typographical errors.  
- Conceptual question: request for a simpler definition of “interaction” between two variables that doesn’t require the full model expression.  
- Clarification needed on Line 169: meaning of “almost surely” and whether it is intended as an asymptotic statement in n.  
- Technical correction: Equation (3) appears to be missing the summation over i in the empirical risk term.  
- Terminology suggestion: use “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel” (Line 205).  
- Typos to fix: Line 145 “on RHS” → “on the RHS”; Line 152 “reduces” → “reduce”; Line 160 “G^ exists and unique” → “G^ exists and is unique”; Line 247 “turning parameters” → “tuning parameters”; Algorithm 2 Line 1 “State with” → “Start with”; Line 251 “by compare” → “by comparing.”
