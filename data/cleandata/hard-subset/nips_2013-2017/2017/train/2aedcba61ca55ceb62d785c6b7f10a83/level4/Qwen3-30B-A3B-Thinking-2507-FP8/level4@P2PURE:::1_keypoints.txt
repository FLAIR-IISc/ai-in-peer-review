- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, starts from additive model f = sum_p f_p(X_p) which ignores inter-dimension correlations.  
- Authors propose a "group additive model" to capture correlations by grouping dimensions.  
- Provide a formal framework and introduce ad hoc algorithms for finding groupings; evaluated on small synthetic and real datasets.  
- Related work coverage is inadequate, omitting links to structured sparsity, graphical model learning, Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Related work discussion reduces to a single KDD-2015 citation [8], insufficient to assess novelty.  
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure for groupings (favoring many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The complexity measure is not efficiently optimizable, forcing exhaustive search or a greedy method akin to forward feature selection.  
- Authors acknowledge discrete optimization challenges but did not propose or compare more tractable alternative measures.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only synthetic validation and one Boston Housing example, with no baselines or comparisons to alternative methods.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments and related work remain insufficient; reviewer raised score but urges substantial improvement to related work.
