- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions using an additive model f = sum_p f_p(X_p).  
- Authors extend to a group additive model to capture within-group correlations.  
- Provides formal analysis of the group additive framework.  
- Proposes ad hoc algorithms to identify optimal groupings.  
- Experiments on small synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Related work discussion is weak, making novelty hard to assess for non-experts.  
- Authors omit connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection.  
- Theoretical contributions appear novel, including a complexity measure favoring many small groups.  
- The complexity measure is reasonable but hard to optimize in practice.  
- Optimization strategies suggested (exhaustive search or greedy forward selection) are computationally expensive or limited.  
- No baselines or comparisons provided, so empirical results lack context.  
- After rebuttal, some concerns were addressed but experiments and related-work discussion remain insufficient.  
- Strong recommendation to substantially enhance the related-work section before publication.
