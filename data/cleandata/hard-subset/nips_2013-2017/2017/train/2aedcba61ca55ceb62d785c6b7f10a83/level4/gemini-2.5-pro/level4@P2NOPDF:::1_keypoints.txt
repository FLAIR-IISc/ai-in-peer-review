- Proposes a group additive model that partitions features and models interactions within each group to extend additive models.  
- Provides a formal theoretical treatment and derives a complexity measure based on an upper bound on the model's covering number.  
- The complexity measure favors many small groups and appears to be a novel theoretical contribution.  
- Introduces heuristic algorithms for finding groupings, including exhaustive search and a greedy forward-selection-style method.  
- The complexity measure does not admit efficient optimization, and the proposed search strategies are elementary and impractical for moderate feature counts.  
- Authors mention investigating other measures (line 174) but give no details, leaving the choice unexplained.  
- Related work engagement is very limited (mostly a single KDD 2015 citation), making novelty hard to assess.  
- Fails to connect to relevant literatures such as structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection/grouping.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only a small synthetic dataset and Boston Housing are used.  
- No baselines or comparisons to alternative methods are provided, so empirical effectiveness is unproven.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns clarified and score slightly raised, but major issues with experiments and related work remain; authors urged to revise before publication.
