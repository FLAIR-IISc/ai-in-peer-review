- Extends additive models to a “group additive model” allowing correlations within variable groups and provides a formal treatment.  
- Core contribution: formal definition of the “intrinsic group additive structure.”  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure based on covering numbers to penalize group structures.  
- Proposes algorithms to find groupings: exhaustive search (expensive) and a greedy stepwise method (potentially suboptimal).  
- The complexity measure favors smaller groups and gives a principled penalty term for the optimization.  
- Limited discussion of related work; overlooks structured sparsity, graphical model learning, and feature selection literature.  
- Lack of connection to broader literature weakens the novelty claim; citing KDD 2015 is insufficient.  
- Optimization is challenging in practice: exhaustive search is not scalable and the greedy approach may be suboptimal.  
- Authors mention alternative complexity measures but provide little justification or trade-off analysis for their choice.  
- Experiments are limited: synthetic results show structure recovery but lack comparisons to established baselines.  
- Real-data (Boston Housing) evaluation is unconvincing without benchmark comparisons.  
- Practical effectiveness and applicability are unclear due to scalability and evaluation gaps.  
- Strengths: rigorous formalization, novel complexity measure, and clear algorithm presentation.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient related work, scalability/suboptimality concerns, limited empirical comparisons, and limited insight into measure choice.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically valuable but practical impact unclear; rated “weak accept” pending expanded related work and stronger experimental comparisons.
