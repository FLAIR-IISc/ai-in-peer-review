- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, starting from additive models that ignore cross-dimension correlations.
- Authors propose a group additive model to capture within-group correlations and provide a formal framework.
- Algorithms for finding groupings are presented but are somewhat ad hoc.
- Empirical validation is limited to small synthetic datasets and a Boston Housing example.
- Major concern: related work discussion is insufficient, hindering assessment of novelty.
- Optimal grouping relates to structured sparsity, graphical model learning, and feature selection, which the authors fail to address beyond a single citation.
- Theoretical contribution: a complexity measure for groupings (favoring many small groups) derived from an upper bound on the covering number.
- The measure is theoretically sound but not amenable to efficient optimization; authors use exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like method.
- Criticism: authors did not propose an alternative measure that permits efficient optimization and gave only weak justification for their chosen measure.
- Experimental shortcomings: no baselines or comparisons to alternative methods, making empirical performance unevaluable.
- Post-rebuttal: authors partially addressed concerns; reviewer still finds experiments and related work inadequate but acknowledges the theoretical contribution and urges a stronger related work section.
