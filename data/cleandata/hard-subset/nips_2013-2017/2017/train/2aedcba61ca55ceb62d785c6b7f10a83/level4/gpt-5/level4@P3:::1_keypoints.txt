- Proposes group additive models (GAM) allowing interactions within groups but not across, positioned between additive and full-interaction models.  
- Formalizes group structures with a partial order and defines a unique intrinsic (minimal) group additive structure.  
- Uses RKHS-based estimation for fixed group structures and a structure-penalized estimator when the partition is unknown.  
- Derives a complexity penalty for partitions from covering-number capacity bounds of direct-sum RKHSs (penalty ∝ sum α|u| over groups).  
- Proves selection consistency for the structure-penalized estimator under specified conditions and uniform bounds over amiable structures.  
- Provides two search algorithms for partitions: exhaustive search for small p and a greedy backward stepwise heuristic for larger p.  
- Demonstrates the approach on simulations and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Strength: clear formalization of partitions, partial order, and existence/uniqueness of intrinsic structure.  
- Strength: conceptually appealing penalization linking structural complexity to RKHS capacity via covering numbers.  
- Strength: nontrivial consistency results and uniform bounds for structure recovery.  
- Strength: practical algorithms and illustrative experiments.  
- Weakness: related-work coverage is too narrow; underplays connections to structured sparsity and interaction-selection literature.  
- Weakness: the penalty depends on α (from covering precision ε) with unclear choice, interpretation, and finite-sample impact.  
- Weakness: search procedures are either combinatorial or heuristic with no approximation guarantees or stopping-rule discussion.  
- Weakness: experiments lack baselines, ablations, and systematic sensitivity analyses for λ, μ, and α.  
- Weakness: some theoretical assumptions (e.g., independence in Prop.1) and tuning conditions (λn, μn) need clearer practical guidance.  
- Technical soundness: theory appears careful; covering-number approach is standard but mapping to a single α is opaque.  
- Empirical support: simulations are promising but insufficient without competitive baselines (e.g., SALSA, functional ANOVA, structured sparsity).  
- Clarity issues: need expanded related work, clearer explanation and tuning guidance for α, and more detailed description of the stepwise algorithm.  
- Originality: the covering-number–based penalty on direct-sum RKHSs and intrinsic structure notion are novel contributions.  
- Significance: could enable interpretable nonparametric interaction discovery if validated with stronger empirical evidence.  
- Suggestions: expand related work, add baselines (SALSA, KRR/ANOVA, group/overlap lasso), provide tuning heuristics and sensitivity analyses, and detail/strengthen stepwise search and runtime reporting.  
- Acceptance arguments: principled RKHS-capacity penalty, novel intrinsic structure formalism, and consistency guarantees.  
- Rejection arguments: weak related-work positioning, limited empirical evaluation, and heuristic search without guarantees.  
- Post-rebuttal: theory/positioning clarified somewhat, but concerns about experimental depth and related work remain.
