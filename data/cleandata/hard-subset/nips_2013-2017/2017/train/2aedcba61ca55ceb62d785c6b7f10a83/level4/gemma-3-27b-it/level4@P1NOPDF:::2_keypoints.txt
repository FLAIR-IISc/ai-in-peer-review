- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the group structure is unknown and must be learned from data.
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to a kernel ridge regression objective.
- Joint optimization estimates both the group structure and regression functions via direct-sum RKHSs over groups.
- Presents two algorithms for approximately solving the compound optimization problem.
- Theoretical results: convergence rate of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of the group-structure estimate (probability → 1 as n → ∞).
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method.
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity using covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum RKHS.
- Method is well-motivated, novel, and supported by reasonably strong theory and empirical results.
- Primary limitation: computational difficulty of optimizing the group structure when the number of variables is large.
- Paper is clearly written overall, aside from a number of typographical errors.
- Minor question: is there a simpler characterization of interaction between two variables that avoids expressing the full model in Eq. (1)?
- Clarification needed: meaning of “almost surely” in Line 169—does it refer to the limit as n → ∞?
- Technical correction: Equation (3) is missing a summation over i.
- Terminology suggestion: use “translation invariant kernel” rather than “convolutional kernel.”
- Typos and suggested corrections: Line 145 (“on RHS” → “on the RHS”); Line 152 (“reduces” → “reduce”); Line 160 (“G^ exists and unique” → “G^ exists and is unique”); Line 247 (“turning parameters” → “tuning parameters”); Algorithm 2 Line 1 (“State with” → “start with”); Line 251 (“by compare” → “by comparing”).
