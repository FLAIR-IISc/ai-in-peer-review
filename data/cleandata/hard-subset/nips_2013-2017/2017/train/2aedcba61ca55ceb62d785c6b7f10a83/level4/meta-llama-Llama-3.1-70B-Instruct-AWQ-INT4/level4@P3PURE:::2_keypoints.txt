- Study addresses group-additive nonparametric regression where group structure is unknown and must be learned from data.
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to control complexity of group structures.
- Optimization jointly over group structure (defining direct-sum RKHS) and within-group regression estimate.
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the compound optimization problem.
- Theoretical results: empirical risk converges to true risk at a provable rate.
- Theoretical results: consistency of group-structure estimation (probability of recovering true partition → 1 as n → ∞).
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets supports practical performance.
- Key innovation: quantifying complexity of groupings via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.
- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, and potentially a significant advance between additive and fully nonparametric models.
- Main practical limitation: optimization over group structures is difficult when the number of variables is large.
- Writing is fairly clear overall, but there are a few unclear phrasings and typos.
