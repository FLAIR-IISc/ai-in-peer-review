This paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models, which interpolate between the fully nonparametric model and the additive model. Specifically, the paper focuses on the problem where the group structure is not known in advance and must be learned from the data. To address this issue, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an objective that can be optimized over both the group structure and the regression estimate within each group.

The authors present a comprehensive paper that includes a well-motivated and novel method, along with solid theoretical and empirical results. The key innovation of the paper is recognizing that the complexity of a particular group structure can be quantified in terms of covering numbers of the direct sum space. This approach has the potential to be a significant advance in the field, especially considering the discussion in the paper about the limitations of existing methods.

The authors also provide experimental results on both synthetic and real data, which demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
