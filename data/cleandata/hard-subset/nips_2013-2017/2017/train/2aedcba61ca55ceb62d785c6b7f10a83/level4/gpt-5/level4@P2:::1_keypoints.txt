- Paper proposes group additive models that partition predictors into groups to allow within-group interactions while avoiding full interaction combinatorics.  
- Defines an intrinsic (minimal) group additive structure, proves existence and uniqueness, and links group structures to direct-sum RKHSs.  
- Introduces a structure-penalized kernel regression objective with a penalty derived from covering-number bounds for the induced RKHS.  
- Proves selection consistency for recovering the intrinsic group structure, distinguishing amiable vs. non-amiable cases.  
- Offers two search strategies: exhaustive search for small p and a backward stepwise greedy heuristic for larger p.  
- Experiments are limited to small synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing dataset, demonstrating interpretability but lacking broader validation.  
- Related-work discussion is too narrow; strong connections to structured sparsity, sparse additive models, hierarchical interaction modeling, and graphical models are missing.  
- Novelty is unclear relative to existing literatures (group/overlapping group penalties, SALSA, functional ANOVA, OKGT, etc.) due to insufficient comparison.  
- Theoretical framework and intrinsic-structure formalization are clear and valuable contributions.  
- The covering-number–based structural penalty is principled and yields a tractable surrogate that grows with group size.  
- Algorithmic approach is limited: the discrete optimization is challenging, exhaustive/greedy searches do not scale and lack approximation or recovery guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks baselines and ablations (no comparisons to ANOVA/RKHS kernels, SALSA, sparse additive models, group-lasso interaction methods, or graphical-model approaches).  
- Practical aspects are underexplored: sensitivity to kernel choice and hyperparameters (λ, μ, α), robustness to noise/misspecification, runtime scaling with p, and behavior under approximate group additivity.  
- Some theoretical results rely on capacity upper bounds and universal kernels; finite-sample implications and non-universal kernel behavior need clearer discussion.  
- Suggested improvements: expand related work, add competitive baselines and ablations, provide computational complexity analysis and pruning heuristics, analyze sensitivity to kernels/hyperparameters, and consider alternative data-dependent penalties.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear formalization of intrinsic group structures, principled penalty with consistency guarantees, and potentially interpretable middle-ground models.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate related-work coverage, limited small-scale experiments with no baselines, non-scalable search procedures without guarantees, and minimal practical tuning guidance.  
- Post-rebuttal: some clarity issues were addressed but empirical evaluation and related-work remain insufficient; reviewer raised score but urges stronger related-work and baselines for camera-ready.
