- Introduces a group additive model for high-dimensional kernel regression that clusters dimensions to model within-group interactions.  
- Provides a formal theoretical treatment and a novel complexity measure favoring many small groups, derived from an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The proposed complexity measure is not efficiently optimizable; authors rely on exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection heuristic.  
- Inadequate survey of related work: misses connections to structured sparsity, graphical models, and feature selection; discussion largely limited to one KDD 2015 paper.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a small synthetic study and the Boston Housing dataset, with no baselines or comparisons to alternatives.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer remains concerned about experiments and related-work coverage but increased score and urges significant expansion of related work in the final version.
