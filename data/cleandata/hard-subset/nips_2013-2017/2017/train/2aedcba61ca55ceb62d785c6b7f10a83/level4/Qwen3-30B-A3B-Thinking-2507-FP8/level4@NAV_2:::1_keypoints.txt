- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method that defines the intrinsic group additive structure and uses an RKHS covering-number-based complexity penalty for simultaneous group discovery and function estimation, with consistency guarantees and two algorithms (exhaustive and backward stepwise); evaluated on synthetic data and Boston Housing.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with well-motivated complexity measure and consistency proofs.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to established baselines (e.g., functional ANOVA, group sparsity) and has limited scalability analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; connection to graphical models is insightful but the distinction from conditional-dependence structures could be emphasized more.  
- Algorithm descriptions are detailed enough for reproduction, but tuning parameter selection needs clearer guidance.  
- The intrinsic group formulation and RKHS covering-number penalty are novel contributions that advance prior work.  
- Related work is underdeveloped and should more directly contrast with structured sparsity (e.g., group Lasso) and graphical-model-based group discovery.  
- The problem tackled is significant and the method offers a middle ground between simple additive models and full functional ANOVA.  
- Practical impact is weakened by limited experimental scope (one real dataset) and uncertain applicability to large-scale problems.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation for intrinsic group structures and consistency.  
- Strength: novel complexity measure grounded in RKHS capacity theory.  
- Strength: clear exposition and useful connection to graphical models.  
- Strength: provides practical algorithms with some empirical validation.  
- Weakness: related work fails to position contributions against key ML literature.  
- Weakness: experiments lack baselines and scalability assessment.  
- Weakness: greedy algorithm’s potential suboptimality is noted but not mitigated.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees assume known kernel properties, which may be unrealistic without tuning.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and of moderate interest but not sufficiently impactful for acceptance as submitted; strengthen related work and experimental comparisons (including methods like [9,14]) to justify acceptance.
