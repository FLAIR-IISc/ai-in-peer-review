This work investigates group-additive nonparametric regression, a modeling framework that bridges the gap between fully nonparametric approaches – which can be computationally challenging – and traditional additive models – which may be overly restrictive. The paper addresses the scenario where the grouping of predictor variables is unknown and must be learned directly from the data. The authors introduce a novel penalty term, grounded in the covering numbers of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) balls, integrated into the kernel ridge regression objective. This yields a combined optimization problem concerning both the identification of the group structure (defining a function space through the direct sum of RKHSs) and the estimation of the regression function within each group. Two algorithms are proposed for approximate solution, accompanied by theoretical guarantees regarding (a) the convergence rate of the empirical risk to the true risk of the optimal estimator, and (b) the consistency of the estimated group structure – demonstrating its probabilistic convergence to the true grouping as the sample size grows. The methodology is evaluated through experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.

The core contribution lies in the insightful connection between the complexity of a group structure and the covering numbers of the corresponding direct sum space. The paper is thorough, presenting a well-justified and original method supported by robust theoretical and empirical findings. While I lack comprehensive familiarity with alternative approaches in this model space, the discussion presented in Lines 31-50 appears reasonably complete, suggesting this work represents a potentially significant advancement. As highlighted in the Discussion, a primary limitation of the method seems to be the computational difficulty of optimizing over the group structure, particularly with a large number of variables. The writing is generally clear, although numerous typographical errors are present.
