- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where predictors are partitioned into groups and the group structure is unknown and must be learned from data.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to the kernel ridge regression objective.  
- The objective is optimized over both the group structure (which defines a direct-sum RKHS) and the within-group regression estimates.  
- Presents two algorithms for approximately solving this joint optimization.  
- Theoretical results: (a) rates at which empirical risk approaches true risk; (b) consistency of the estimated group structure (probability → 1 as n → ∞).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum function space.  
- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, and potentially a significant advance assuming the related-work discussion (Lines 31–50) is complete.  
- Main practical drawback: difficulty solving the optimization over group structures when the number of variables is large.  
- Writing is fairly clear but contains multiple typos.
