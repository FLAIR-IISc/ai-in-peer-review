- Introduces the “group additive model” for kernel regression, allowing correlations within groups while simplifying inter-group relationships.
- Provides a formal treatment and proposes several somewhat ad hoc algorithms for finding optimal groupings.
- Theoretical contribution: defines a novel complexity measure (favoring many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.
- The complexity measure is difficult to optimize; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like method.
- Authors mention ongoing work on alternative measures but offer no concrete insights or efficient optimization strategies.
- Related work discussion is very limited, omitting connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection; focuses mainly on one KDD-2015 paper.
- Experiments limited to small synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset, with no baselines or comparisons to existing methods.
- After rebuttal some concerns were partially addressed, but experiments and related work remain weak; reviewer urges significant expansion of related work before publication.
