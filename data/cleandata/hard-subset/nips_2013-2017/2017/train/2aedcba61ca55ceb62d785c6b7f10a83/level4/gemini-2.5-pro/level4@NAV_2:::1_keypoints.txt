- Problem: Generalizes additive models to "group additive models" that allow arbitrary interactions within variable groups while assuming additivity across groups for high-dimensional nonparametric regression.

- Contribution 1: Defines the "intrinsic group additive structure" as a unique, minimal, and concise representation of the target function.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a structure-penalized kernel regression method to jointly identify the intrinsic group structure and estimate nonparametric functions.

- Contribution 3: Derives a novel complexity penalty for group structures based on an upper bound on the RKHS covering number.

- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Provides a rigorous formalization using a partial ordering of partitions to address non-uniqueness of group additive representations.

- Strength — Novel penalty derivation: Grounds the complexity penalty in RKHS capacity (covering numbers), linking partition complexity to function-space richness.

- Strength — Problem significance: Tackles an important trade-off between interpretability and predictive accuracy, offering a middle ground between additive and fully nonparametric models.

- Weakness — Related work positioning: Limited discussion of related areas (structured sparsity, graphical models, interaction detection) leaves originality and context unclear.

- Weakness — Optimization and scalability: Leads to a non-convex problem; exhaustive search is infeasible and the proposed greedy heuristic lacks guarantees, limiting applicability to high dimensions.

- Weakness — Empirical evaluation: Experiments are limited to low-dimensional synthetic cases and a single real dataset with no comparisons to relevant baselines, making practical benefits unconvincing.

- Post-rebuttal: Authors clarified some concerns, but weaknesses in empirical evaluation and literature positioning remain; reviewer raised score for theoretical novelty but urges expanded related work discussion and stronger empirical comparisons.
