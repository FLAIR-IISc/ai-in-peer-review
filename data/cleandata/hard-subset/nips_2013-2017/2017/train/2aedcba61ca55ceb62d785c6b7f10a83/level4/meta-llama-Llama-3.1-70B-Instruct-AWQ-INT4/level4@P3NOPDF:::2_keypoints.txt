- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression and the problem of learning unknown group structure from data.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group structure and within-group estimates.  
- Introduces two algorithms to approximately solve the compound optimization over groups and functions.  
- Provides theoretical results: convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets, showing reasonable performance.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Main limitation: optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large.  
- Paper is generally well written and comprehensive, with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Suggestion: provide a simpler characterization of variable interactions not requiring the full model in Equation (1).  
- Clarify the use of "almost surely" (Line 169)—likely intended as n → ∞.  
- Minor errors: Equation (3) missing a summation over i.  
- Terminology: consider "translation invariant kernel" instead of "convolutional kernel" (Line 205).  
- Typos/corrections noted: "on RHS" → "on the RHS" (Line 145); grammar and phrasing fixes at Lines 152, 160, 247, 251; Algorithm 2 "State with" → "start with".  
- Overall assessment: novel, significant advance in nonparametric regression despite optimization scalability concerns.
