- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the regression function is additive across a partition of predictor variables and the group structure is learned from data.

- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into kernel ridge regression to quantify complexity of a group structure.

- Optimizes jointly over group partitions (which define a direct-sum RKHS) and regression estimates.

- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve the resulting combinatorial/functional optimization problem.

- Provides theoretical results: convergence rates for the estimator and consistency of group-structure selection (probability of recovering true partition → 1 as n increases).

- Validates method empirically on synthetic and real-world datasets.

- Key innovation: measuring group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum RKHS.

- Main limitation: computational challenge of optimizing over group structures when the number of variables is large.
