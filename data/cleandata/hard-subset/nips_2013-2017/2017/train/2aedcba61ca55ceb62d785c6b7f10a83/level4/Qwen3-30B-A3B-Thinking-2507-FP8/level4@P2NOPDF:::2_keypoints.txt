- Proposes a group-additive nonparametric regression model that learns unknown group structure from data, between fully nonparametric and additive models.  
- Core innovation: a novel penalty based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS balls to quantify group-structure complexity.  
- Integrates this penalty into kernel ridge regression to jointly optimize group partitions and within-group estimates.  
- Provides two algorithms to approximate the resulting compound optimization problem.  
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence rates of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of group-structure estimation (probability → 1 as n→∞).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method’s effectiveness.  
- Main limitation: computational scalability issues when the number of variables is large.
