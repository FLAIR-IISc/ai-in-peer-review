- Paper introduces a group additive kernel regression model that partitions features into groups to capture interdimensional correlations and proposes heuristic group-selection algorithms with results on small synthetic and real datasets.

- Related work is inadequately treated: the authors fail to situate their approach within structured sparsity, graphical model learning, or feature selection literature and cite only one KDD-2015 paper, leaving novelty unclear.

- Theoretical contribution is novel: a complexity measure favoring finer groupings that upper-bounds the covering number and is well-motivated.

- The proposed complexity measure lacks efficient optimization: authors rely on exhaustive search or a greedy heuristic, with no efficient alternative or justification over cross-validation.

- Experimental validation is weak: experiments are limited to synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset, with no comparative baselines or benchmarks against existing methods.

- Post-rebuttal, theoretical concerns were partly addressed but experimental and related-work deficiencies remain unresolved.

- Reviewer modestly raised the score for theoretical novelty but recommends major revisions: expand related work (structured sparsity/feature selection) and add rigorous comparative experiments.
