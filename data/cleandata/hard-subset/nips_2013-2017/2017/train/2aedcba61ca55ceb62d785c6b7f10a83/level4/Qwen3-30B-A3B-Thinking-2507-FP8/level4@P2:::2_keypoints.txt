- Addresses gap in high-dimensional nonparametric regression by identifying group additive structures to avoid the curse of dimensionality.  
- Rigorously defines an intrinsic group additive structure for any square-integrable function (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method that simultaneously identifies group structure and estimates nonparametric functions.  
- Introduces a novel complexity measure based on RKHS covering numbers to penalize group structures.  
- Proves consistency for recovering the minimal (true) group additive structure as sample size increases.  
- Bridges standard additive models and higher-order functional ANOVA models, offering a middle ground between flexibility and tractability.  
- Provides theoretical novelty and a systematic method distinct from prior group-additive work (e.g., [11]).  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on synthetic data and Boston Housing; real-data interpretation could be deeper.  
- Offers two algorithms (exhaustive search and stepwise) to address computational scalability.  
- Paper is technically sound with well-justified theoretical results and clear organization overall.  
- Some technical explanations and distinctions (e.g., between group additive and conditional dependence/graphical models) need clarification.  
- Would benefit from more explicit comparisons to related methods (sparse additive models, functional ANOVA, higher-order interaction models).  
- Minor technical issues and typos need fixing (e.g., Eq.(3) summation, "almost surely" clarification, terminology and algorithm text).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify graphical-model connections, improve real-data interpretation, and correct minor technical/typo issues.
