- Paper tackles high-dimensional nonparametric regression by proposing a "group additive model" that allows interactions within variable groups while keeping additivity across groups.  
- Main contributions: formal framework, definition of a unique "intrinsic group additive structure," and a method to identify that structure.  
- Novel complexity penalty derived from an upper bound on the covering number of the associated RKHS.  
- Two search procedures proposed for grouping: exhaustive search and a greedy backward stepwise algorithm.  
- Empirical demonstration on synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing dataset.  
- Major concern: insufficient engagement with related work, making novelty and significance unclear.  
- Missing connections to related areas such as structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network structure learning, and feature grouping.  
- Literature review appears narrowly focused on a single prior work ([11]) rather than the broader field.  
- Practical weakness: the proposed covering-number-based penalty is hard to optimize in practice.  
- Reliance on exhaustive search or greedy heuristics is computationally infeasible/scalable for moderate to large variable counts.  
- Authors mention comparing other measures but give no details, leaving the choice of a difficult-to-optimize penalty unjustified.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: few datasets and no comparisons against baselines or alternative methods.  
- Experiments show functionality in idealized settings but do not establish practical utility or superiority.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns partially addressed, but related-work discussion and experimental depth remain insufficient.  
- Reviewer acknowledges valid theoretical contributions but urges substantial revision of the introduction and related work if accepted.
