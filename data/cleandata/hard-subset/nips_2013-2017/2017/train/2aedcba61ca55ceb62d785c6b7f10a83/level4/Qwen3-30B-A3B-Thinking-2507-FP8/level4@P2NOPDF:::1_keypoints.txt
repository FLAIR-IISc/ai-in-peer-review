- Proposes a "group additive model" extending additive models to capture correlations by grouping dimensions.  
- Related-work section is insufficient and relies almost exclusively on a single KDD-2015 reference.  
- Fails to acknowledge significant overlap with structured sparsity, graphical model learning, and feature selection.  
- Omitting comparisons to methods that explicitly handle grouped features (e.g., structured sparsity) undermines novelty claims.  
- Theoretical contribution is a complexity measure for groupings (upper bound on covering number) that favors many small groups.  
- The measure is theoretically sound but its practical utility and superiority over alternatives are unverified.  
- Optimization approaches proposed (exhaustive search or greedy heuristic) are computationally intractable or simplistic and lack scalable alternatives.  
- Authors did not investigate measures amenable to efficient optimization (e.g., convex relaxations).  
- Experiments consist of synthetic validation and a limited Boston Housing case study, lacking baselines.  
- Absence of comparisons to standard kernel regression and structured sparsity methods makes experimental significance unclear.  
- Synthetic experiments do not address scalability or robustness to noise, key concerns in high-dimensional settings.  
- Post-rebuttal: related-work clarification was partial but still superficial; empirical shortcomings and lack of baselines remain.  
- Recommendation: reject—technically plausible but insufficient novelty, weak literature engagement, poor empirical validation, and limited scalability; revision with stronger related work and baselines could merit reconsideration.
