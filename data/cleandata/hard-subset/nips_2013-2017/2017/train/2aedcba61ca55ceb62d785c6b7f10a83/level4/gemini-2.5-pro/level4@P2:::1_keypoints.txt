- Addresses limitation of additive models by introducing a "group additive model" that allows interactions within predictor groups.  
- Proposes three main contributions: unique minimal "intrinsic group additive structure", a structure-penalized kernel method to identify/estimate it, and a novel RKHS-derived complexity penalty for group structures.  
- Provides theoretical analysis establishing consistency of the proposed method.  
- Evaluated empirically on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: formalization of the "intrinsic group additive structure" offers a clear, novel theoretical framework for interaction detection.  
- Strength: principled complexity penalty derived from RKHS covering numbers gives a theoretically motivated trade-off between fit and structural complexity.  
- Weakness: related work discussion is insufficient, failing to connect to structured sparsity, graphical-model structure learning, and interaction detection literature.  
- Weakness: gap between theoretical penalty and practical optimization — leads to a hard combinatorial problem and reliance on naive heuristics (exhaustive search or backward stepwise) without strong guarantees.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is thin and lacks comparisons to baseline/competing methods, making practical benefits unclear.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raised score acknowledging theoretical value but urges much stronger related-work positioning and expanded experiments for the final version.
