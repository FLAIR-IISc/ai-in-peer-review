This study explores group-additive nonparametric regression models, which interpolate between fully nonparametric models, often difficult to fit, and additive models, sometimes too restrictive. Specifically, the paper investigates the problem of learning the group structure from data when it is not known in advance. To achieve this, the authors propose a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is then added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an objective that can be optimized over both the group structure and the regression estimate within each group.

The authors present two algorithms for approximately solving this compound optimization problem and provide theoretical results showing the rate at which the empirical risk of the estimate approaches the true risk of the true optimum and the consistency of the group structure estimate. Experimental results on both synthetic and real data are also presented.

The key innovation of the paper appears to be the recognition that the complexity of a particular group structure can be quantified in terms of covering numbers of the direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, including a well-motivated and novel method, and reasonably solid theoretical and empirical results. Assuming the discussion in Lines 31-50 is fairly complete, this paper seems like a potentially significant advance in the field of nonparametric regression. The main issue with the method, as noted in the Discussion section, appears to be the difficulty in solving the optimization problem over group structure when the number of variables is large.

The paper is fairly clearly written, aside from several typos. Some minor comments and questions are noted below.

One potential area of improvement is in providing a simple characterization of "interaction" between two variables that does not rely on writing the whole model in Equation (1). Additionally, the use of "almost surely" in Line 169 is unclear, and it would be helpful to clarify whether this is meant as n -> infinity.

Several minor errors were also noted. Equation (3) is missing a summation (over i). The term "translation invariant kernel" may be more commonly used than "convolutional kernel" (Line 205). Furthermore, several typos were found, including "on RHS" instead of "on the RHS" (Line 145), "not only can the bias of \hat f{\lambda,G} reduces" instead of "not only can the bias of \hat f{\lambda,G} reduce" (Line 152), "G^ exists and unique" instead of "G^ exists and is unique" (Line 160), "turning parameters" instead of "tuning parameters" (Line 247), and "by compare" instead of "by comparing" (Line 251). In Algorithm 2, "State with" should be corrected to "start with" (Line 1).

Overall, the paper presents a novel and comprehensive approach to group-additive nonparametric regression and provides a significant contribution to the field.
