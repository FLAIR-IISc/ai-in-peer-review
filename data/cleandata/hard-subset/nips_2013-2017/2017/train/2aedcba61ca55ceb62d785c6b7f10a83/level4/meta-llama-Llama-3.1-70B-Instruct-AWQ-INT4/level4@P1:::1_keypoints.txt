- Paper studies kernel regression under a "group additive model" that groups input dimensions to capture within-group correlations.  
- Authors provide a formal treatment and propose algorithms to find optimal groupings.  
- They introduce a novel complexity measure for groupings derived as an upper bound on covering numbers.  
- The complexity measure favors many small groups but is hard to optimize directly.  
- Proposed optimization approaches are exhaustive search (scales poorly) and a greedy method similar to forward feature selection.  
- Authors mention exploring alternative measures but give no substantive insights or comparisons.  
- Experiments include simple synthetic tests and a Boston Housing real-world example.  
- Experimental evaluation is thin: no baselines, no comparisons to related methods, and limited datasets.  
- Related work coverage is poor; key areas like structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection are not discussed.  
- Theoretical contribution is novel and interesting, but practical optimization and empirical validation are weak.  
- Post-rebuttal, some concerns were clarified but experiments and related-work treatment remain inadequate.  
- Strengths: formal group-additive treatment, novel complexity measure, some empirical demonstration.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate related work, optimization difficulties, limited experiments and no baselines.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
