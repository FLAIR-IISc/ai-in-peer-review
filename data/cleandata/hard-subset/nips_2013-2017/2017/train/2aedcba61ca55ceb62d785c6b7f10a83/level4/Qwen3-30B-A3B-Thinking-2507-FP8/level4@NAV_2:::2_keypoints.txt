- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where additivity is across groups rather than individual variables, bridging fully nonparametric and standard additive models.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, incorporated into kernel ridge regression to learn unknown group structure.  
- Formulates a joint optimization that simultaneously determines group structure (direct-sum RKHS) and estimates group-wise regression functions.  
- Develops two algorithms to solve the compound optimization problem.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rate of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure as sample size grows.  
- Empirical validation via synthetic experiments and the Boston Housing dataset supports the method.  
- Novelty: using covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS spaces to quantify group complexity is a fresh contribution.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, technically sound theory, solid empirical results, and clear linkage to prior work.  
- Significance: offers a more interpretable approach for high-dimensional nonparametric regression that can capture meaningful interactions.  
- Clarity is generally good, but numerous typos detract from readability.  
- Main practical limitation: computational complexity for large numbers of predictors; stepwise algorithm helps but scalability remains an issue.  
- Suggestion: discuss more concrete application domains where the method yields clear benefits beyond the Boston Housing example.  
- Minor request: provide a more intuitive description of variable interactions without relying on the full formal model in Eq. (1).
