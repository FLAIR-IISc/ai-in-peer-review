- Proposes learning group-additive nonparametric regression when groupings are unknown by adding a covering-number-based penalty to kernel ridge regression and jointly optimizing group partition and per-group functions.  
- Introduces two algorithms to approximately solve the joint problem and proves empirical-risk convergence rates and consistency of the recovered grouping; validates with synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Main conceptual contribution: quantifying grouping complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS to trade off between fully nonparametric and purely additive models.  
- Method is well-motivated, novel, and supported by reasonably strong theoretical and empirical results.  
- Major practical concern: optimization over groupings is difficult when the number of variables is large.  
- Exposition is generally clear but contains numerous typos.
