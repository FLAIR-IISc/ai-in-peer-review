- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the regression function is additive across unknown variable groups, interpolating between fully nonparametric and additive models.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls to quantify group-structure complexity, incorporated into a kernel ridge regression objective.  
- Jointly optimizes over group structure (which defines a direct-sum RKHS) and group-specific regression estimates.  
- Provides two approximate algorithms for the joint optimization.  
- Theoretical results: (a) rates for empirical risk approaching the population optimum, and (b) consistency of the estimated partition (probability of recovering true partition → 1 as n → ∞).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the approach.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the associated direct-sum space.  
- Overall assessment: method is well motivated, novel, and the theoretical and empirical results are reasonably solid.  
- Reviewer unfamiliar with alternative interpolating approaches but believes the work is a meaningful advance if the related-work overview is complete.  
- Primary limitation: optimization over group structures is challenging when the number of variables is large.  
- Writing is generally clear but the manuscript contains numerous typos.  
- Minor question: is there a simple characterization of interaction between two variables that avoids expressing the full model in Equation (1)?  
- Clarification requested: use of “almost surely” at Line 169 — is this meant as n → ∞?  
- Error noted: Equation (3) appears to be missing a summation over i.  
- Terminology suggestion: use “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”
