- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression models that assume additivity across a partition of predictors to balance flexibility and tractability.
- Central problem: learning the unknown group structure from data.
- Novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls to quantify group-structure complexity.
- Penalty incorporated into kernel ridge regression, optimizing jointly over group structure and regression function.
- Group structure + kernel defines the function space as a direct sum of RKHSs for each variable group.
- Two algorithms proposed to approximately solve the joint optimization over groups and functions.
- Theoretical results: convergence rate for empirical risk and consistency of group-structure recovery (probability → 1 as n increases).
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets supports the method.
- Key innovation: measuring complexity of group structures via covering numbers of the corresponding direct-sum space.
- Main limitation: computational difficulty of optimizing over group structures when the number of variables is large.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear but needs careful proofreading to fix typographical errors.
- Minor question: request for a simple characterization of interaction between two variables without full model specification.
- Clarification requested on use of "almost surely" in Line 169 (is it meant in the limit n → ∞?).
- Technical issues noted: Equation (3) missing a summation; suggest using "translation-invariant kernel" instead of "convolutional kernel."
- List of specific typos provided (e.g., "on RHS" → "on the RHS", several grammatical fixes, Algorithm 2 Line 1 "State with" → "Start with", etc.).
