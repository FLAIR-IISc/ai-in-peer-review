- Studies group-additive nonparametric regression where the predictor partition (group structure) is unknown and must be learned from data.
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to the kernel ridge regression objective.
- Resulting objective is optimized jointly over group structure and per-group regression functions via direct-sum RKHSs.
- Presents two algorithms to approximately solve this compound optimization problem.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: (a) rate at which empirical risk approaches true risk, and (b) consistency of the group-structure estimate (probability of correct structure → 1 as n→∞).
- Includes experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets.
- Key innovation: quantifying complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum function space.
- Paper is comprehensive, well-motivated, and potentially a significant advance in models between additive and fully nonparametric.
- Main practical issue: optimization over group structures becomes difficult when the number of variables is large.
- Writing is fairly clear but the manuscript contains many typos.
- Minor queries/suggestions: ask for a simple characterization of interaction between two variables; clarify use of "almost surely" (Line 169); Equation (3) misses a summation; prefer "translation-invariant kernel" over "convolutional kernel" (Line 205).
- Specific typo corrections noted (Lines 145, 152, 160, 247, Algorithm 2 Line 1, Line 251, etc.).
