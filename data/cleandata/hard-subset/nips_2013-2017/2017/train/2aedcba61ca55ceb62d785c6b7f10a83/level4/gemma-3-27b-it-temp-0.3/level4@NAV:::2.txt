This work investigates group-additive nonparametric regression models, offering a flexible framework that bridges the gap between fully nonparametric approaches – which can be computationally challenging – and traditional additive models, which may be overly restrictive. The central problem addressed is learning the group structure directly from the data. The authors propose a novel penalty term, grounded in the covering numbers of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) balls, integrated into the kernel ridge regression objective. This leads to a combined optimization problem targeting both the group structure (defining a function space through the direct sum of RKHSs) and the regression estimate within each group. Two algorithms are presented for approximate solutions, accompanied by theoretical guarantees regarding (a) the convergence rate of the empirical risk to the true risk of the optimal estimator, and (b) the consistency of the estimated group structure, demonstrating its convergence to the true structure as the sample size grows. The method’s performance is evaluated through experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.

The core contribution lies in the insightful connection between group structure complexity and the covering numbers of the corresponding direct sum space. The paper is thorough, presenting a well-justified and original method supported by robust theoretical and empirical findings. While I lack extensive familiarity with alternative methods for modeling between purely additive and nonparametric structures, the discussion in Lines 31-50 suggests this work represents a potentially significant advancement in the field. As the authors acknowledge, a key limitation is the computational difficulty of optimizing over the group structure when dealing with a large number of variables. The writing is generally clear, though it contains numerous typographical errors.
