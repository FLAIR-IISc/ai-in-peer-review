- Extends additive kernel regression to a group additive model to capture within-group feature correlations, and proposes ad hoc algorithms for selecting groupings with results on small datasets.

- Related work is inadequately discussed, missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection; relies mainly on a single KDD 2015 citation.

- Theoretical contribution: derives a complexity measure over groupings (favoring many small groups) based on an upper bound on the covering number.

- The complexity measure is difficult to optimize in practice; proposed solutions are exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–like scheme that scale poorly.

- No efficient, well-motivated optimization criterion is provided, and the mention of evaluating other measures lacks substantive detail or guidance.

- Experiments are thin: only simple synthetic examples and the Boston Housing dataset, with no baselines or comparisons to alternative methods, hindering empirical evaluation.

- Post-rebuttal: some clarifications addressed concerns, but experiments and related-work discussion remain weak; reviewer increased score but urges major improvement to the related work section.
