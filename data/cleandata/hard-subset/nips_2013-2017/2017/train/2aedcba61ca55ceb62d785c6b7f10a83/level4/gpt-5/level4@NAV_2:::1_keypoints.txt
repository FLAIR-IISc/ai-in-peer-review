- Proposes a group additive model for kernel regression to capture within-group feature interactions in high dimensions.  
- Provides a formal framework and ad hoc grouping-selection procedures, with experiments on small synthetic data and a simple real-world example.  
- Related work is inadequately discussed, omitting connections to structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection, and relying mainly on one KDD paper.  
- Theoretical contribution (a structural complexity measure from covering-number bounds that favors many small groups) appears novel and reasonable.  
- The optimization objective is difficult to optimize in practice; authors use exhaustive search (not scalable) or a greedy forward-selection–like heuristic and give little insight into alternative measures.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (basic synthetic and Boston Housing only) with no baselines or direct comparisons, so results are hard to contextualize.  
- After rebuttal some issues were partially addressed, but experimental evaluation and related-work connections remain insufficient; reviewer requests substantial improvement to related work for the camera-ready version.
