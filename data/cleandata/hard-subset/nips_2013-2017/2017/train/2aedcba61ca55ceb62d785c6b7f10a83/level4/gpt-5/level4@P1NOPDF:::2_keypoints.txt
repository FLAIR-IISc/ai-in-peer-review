- Problem and method: Studies group-additive nonparametric regression with unknown predictor partition; proposes a penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression, jointly optimizing group structure and within-group functions.

- Algorithms and results: Provides two algorithms to approximately solve the coupled optimization; proves empirical-risk convergence rates and consistency of the estimated grouping (P(match) → 1), and shows experiments on synthetic and real data.

- Key innovation: Quantifies group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS corresponding to a grouping.

- Overall assessment: Comprehensive, well-motivated, and novel; potentially significant advance if related-work discussion is complete.

- Main practical limitation: Optimizing over group structures is challenging when the number of variables is large.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but contains numerous typos.

- Minor question 1: Ask for a simple characterization of interaction between two variables that avoids writing the full model (Eq. 1).
