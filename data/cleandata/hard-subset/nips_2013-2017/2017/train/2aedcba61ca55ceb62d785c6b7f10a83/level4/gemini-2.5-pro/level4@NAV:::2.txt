### Summary
This paper investigates group-additive nonparametric regression models, where the regression function is additive across pre-defined groups of predictor variables. This model class provides a flexible intermediate between fully nonparametric models, which are often difficult to estimate, and purely additive models, which can be overly restrictive. The central problem addressed is the scenario where this group structure is unknown and must be inferred from the data.

To this end, the authors propose a novel penalty function derived from the covering numbers of RKHS balls, which is then incorporated into the kernel ridge regression objective. The resulting objective function is optimized over both the group partition and the regression function within that structure; here, the function space is defined by a direct sum of RKHSs corresponding to the variable groups. The paper introduces two algorithms for the approximate optimization of this compound problem. Theoretical contributions include establishing the convergence rate of the empirical risk to the true risk of the optimal estimator and proving the consistency of the group structure estimation, showing that the probability of recovering the true group structure converges to 1 as the sample size increases. Finally, the methodology is validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

### Main Comments
The primary innovation of this work is the insight that the complexity of a given group structure can be quantified using the covering numbers of the associated direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, presenting a well-motivated and novel methodology supported by solid theoretical analysis and empirical validation. Assuming the literature review in Lines 31-50 is a fair representation of the field, this work appears to be a potentially significant advance in fitting models that bridge the gap between additive and fully nonparametric approaches. As acknowledged in the discussion, a principal limitation of the proposed method is the computational challenge of optimizing over the space of group structures, especially when the number of variables is large. The paper is generally well-written and clear, though it would benefit from careful proofreading to correct numerous typographical errors.
