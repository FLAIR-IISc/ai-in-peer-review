- Problem addressed: learning group-additive nonparametric regression where the additive group partition is unknown and must be inferred from data.  
- Motivation: provides a middle ground between hard-to-fit fully nonparametric models and overly restrictive additive models.  
- Novel penalty: introduces a penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into the kernel ridge regression objective.  
- Joint optimization formulation: objective optimized jointly over group structure (direct-sum RKHS over groups) and within-group regression estimates.  
- Algorithms: two algorithms proposed to approximate the complex optimization over partitions and functions.  
- Theoretical guarantees: shows convergence rate of empirical risk to true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure (probability of correct recovery → 1).  
- Main contribution: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS space.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and real datasets support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, novel methodology with solid theoretical and empirical results.  
- Major limitation: optimization over group structures is computationally difficult for large numbers of variables.  
- Writing quality: generally well-written but contains numerous typos.  
- Minor conceptual questions: requests a simpler characterization of pairwise “interaction” without specifying the full model.  
- Technical/notation issues: unclear use of "almost surely" (Line 169), Eq. (3) missing summation over i, and prefer "translation invariant kernel" to "convolutional kernel" (Line 205).
