- Study of group-additive nonparametric regression where predictor variables are partitioned into unknown groups, interpolating between fully nonparametric and additive models.
- Proposed a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls, added to kernel ridge regression to penalize group-complexity.
- Optimization jointly over group structure (which defines a direct-sum RKHS) and within-group regression functions.
- Two approximation algorithms are presented for solving the compound optimization problem.
- Theoretical results: rates at which empirical risk approaches true risk, and consistency of the group-structure estimator (probability of correct recovery → 1 as n → ∞).
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets supports the method.
- Key innovation: quantifying complexity of a group structure via covering numbers of the direct-sum function space.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, comprehensive, and potentially significant with reasonably solid theory and experiments; writing is clear aside from typos.
- Main limitation: optimization over group structures becomes difficult/scales poorly when the number of variables is large.
- Minor question: request for a simple characterization of pairwise “interaction” that does not require the full model formulation.
