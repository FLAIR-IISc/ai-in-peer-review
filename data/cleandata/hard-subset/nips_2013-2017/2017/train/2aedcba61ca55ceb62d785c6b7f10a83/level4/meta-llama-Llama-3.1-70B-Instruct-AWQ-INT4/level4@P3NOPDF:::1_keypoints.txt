- Paper studies kernel regression in high-dimensions, extending the additive model to a "group additive model" to capture correlations between grouped dimensions.  
- Authors provide a formal treatment and propose algorithms to find groupings, but the grouping methods are somewhat ad hoc.  
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure on groupings derived as an upper bound on the covering number that favors many small groups.  
- The proposed complexity measure is hard to optimize; authors use exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like algorithm.  
- Reviewer disappointed the authors did not identify a measure that permits more efficient optimization; claims of exploring other measures are not substantiated.  
- Related work discussion is poor and incomplete—fails to cite structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.  
- Paper relies mainly on a single KDD-2015 citation for related work, making it hard to judge novelty.  
- Experiments are thin: only small synthetic tests and a Boston Housing example.  
- No baselines or comparisons to other approaches are provided, so empirical quality is unclear.  
- Authors’ rebuttal clarified some points; reviewer raised score but still flags weak experiments and inadequate literature grounding.  
- Strong recommendation that authors substantially improve the related work discussion before publication.
