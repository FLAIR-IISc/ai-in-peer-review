- Introduces a method for identifying group-additive structures in nonparametric regression, offering a middle ground between additive models and fully nonparametric models.  
- Core contribution: simultaneously identifies optimal group partitioning and estimates the regression function without prior group knowledge.  
- Proposes a structure-penalized kernel method adding a covering-number-based complexity penalty to kernel ridge regression.  
- Novelty: uses covering numbers of RKHS balls to quantify complexity of group structures for principled structure identification.  
- Formulates a joint optimization over group structure and regression estimates with a rigorously defined penalty.  
- Theoretical results: proves consistency of the estimated group structure as sample size increases.  
- Algorithms: provides exhaustive-search and backward stepwise algorithms to solve the optimization problem.  
- Empirical validation: extensive simulations and real-data experiments showing recovery of true group structures across settings.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical contribution, comprehensive theoretical results, thorough empirical validation, clear presentation, and practical algorithms.  
- Main weakness: computational complexity for high-dimensional predictor sets; scalability needs further work despite the stepwise alternative.  
- Additional weakness: the connection to graphical models is under-explored.  
- Manuscript issues: several typographical errors throughout that need correction.  
- Minor methodological/comments: clarify simple characterization of interactions (Eq.1), meaning of "almost surely" on line 169, and add missing summation over observations in Eq.(3).  
- Terminology suggestion: "convolutional kernel" may be better referred to as "translation-invariant kernel."  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated contribution suitable for NIPS after typographical fixes.
