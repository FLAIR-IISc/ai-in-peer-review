- Problem: Introduces group-additive nonparametric regression that learns both the regression function and unknown predictor groupings.  
- Method: Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression for joint estimation of groups and within-group functions.  
- Optimization: Presents two algorithms for approximate optimization of the joint problem.  
- Theory: Provides convergence rates for empirical risk and consistency results for the estimated group structure.  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets supports the proposed method.  
- Strength: Strong, principled connection between group-structure complexity and covering numbers for model regularization.  
- Contribution: Well-motivated, novel approach with solid theoretical and empirical backing.  
- Limitation: Computationally challenging for large numbers of variables; acknowledged by the authors.  
- Writing: Generally well-written but contains several minor typographical errors.  
- Interaction question: Requests a more concise indicator/characterization of interaction between variables rather than the full model representation.  
- Clarification needed: “Almost surely” (Line 169) usage is unclear—ask if it refers to the n→∞ limit.  
- Correction needed: Equation (3) appears to be missing a summation over i.  
- Terminology suggestion: “Convolutional kernel” (Line 205) is likely better termed “translation invariant kernel.”
