- Proposes a novel structure-penalized kernel method to identify variable grouping and estimate group-additive nonparametric regression functions.  
- Introduces a new complexity penalty for group structures derived from RKHS covering numbers.  
- Provides two optimization algorithms for the proposed objective (exhaustive search and backward stepwise heuristic).  
- Establishes theoretical consistency results for identifying the intrinsic group structure.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Contribution is original, theoretically sound, and well-motivated for model selection in flexible interpretable regression.  
- Paper is comprehensive, covering formulation, algorithms, consistency proofs, and experiments.  
- Main weakness: computational scalability—exhaustive search is infeasible for many predictors.  
- Backward stepwise algorithm is greedy, not guaranteed optimal, and can fail to recover true structure (e.g., Model 4).  
- Scalability and heuristic limitations may reduce practical applicability in high-dimensional settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS-based penalty, complete theoretical analysis, addresses flexibility vs interpretability, solid experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: optimization scalability issues and limitations of the greedy stepwise algorithm.  
- Suggestion: provide an intuitive, informal characterization of variable "interaction" separate from formal Eq. (1).  
- Clarify meaning of "almost surely" in the amiable group structures discussion (population vs. asymptotic vs. Px).  
- Fix missing summation over data points in the empirical risk term of Equation (3).  
- Prefer "translation-invariant kernel" over "convolution kernel" for ML readership.  
- Correct typos and grammatical errors (examples: "on RHS" → "on the RHS"; "bias of f̂ reduce" → "bias of f̂ be reduced"; "G∗ exists and unique" → "G∗ exists and is unique"; "turning parameters" → "tuning parameters"; Algorithm 2: "State with" → "Start with"; "by compare" → "by comparing").
