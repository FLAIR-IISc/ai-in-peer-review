- Paper studies high-dimensional kernel regression and extends additive models to a group additive model to capture within-group correlations.
- Authors provide a formal treatment of the group additive setting and propose ad hoc algorithms for finding feature groupings.
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure on groupings derived as an upper bound on the covering number, which favors many small groups.
- The proposed complexity criterion is difficult to optimize in practice.
- Optimization approaches offered are exhaustive search (not scalable) and a greedy method similar to forward feature selection.
- Authors mention investigating alternative measures but give no details or guidance on better optimization-friendly criteria.
- Related work is poorly developed: missing connections to structured sparsity, graphical models, Bayesian networks, and feature selection literature.
- Related work section relies heavily on a single KDD 2015 paper [8], which is insufficient to establish novelty.
- Experiments are limited to small synthetic datasets and a single small real-world example (Boston Housing).
- Experimental evaluation lacks baselines and comparisons to alternative methods, making practical value unclear.
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were clarified, but experiments remain thin and related-work connections still underdeveloped; reviewer acknowledges valid contributions and urges substantially improved related work in the final version.
