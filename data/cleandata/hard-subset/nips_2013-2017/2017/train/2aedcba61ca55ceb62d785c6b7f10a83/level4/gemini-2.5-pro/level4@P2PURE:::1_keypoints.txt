- Paper extends additive kernel regression to a "group additive model" that partitions features into groups to model within-group correlations.
- Authors provide a formal treatment and propose heuristic algorithms for finding groupings.
- Evaluation is limited to small synthetic data and the Boston Housing dataset.
- Major weakness: inadequate discussion of related work; omits connections to structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.
- Related-work discussion relies largely on a single KDD 2015 paper, providing insufficient context to assess novelty.
- Theoretical contribution: a novel complexity measure (upper bound on covering number) that favors many small groups.
- Practical downside: the complexity measure does not admit efficient optimization; authors rely on exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection–style method.
- Authors claim to have explored alternative measures but provide no details or guidance on suitability.
- Experimental shortcomings: no baselines or comparisons, making practical competitiveness unclear.
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns clarified but experiments remain insufficient and related-work coverage is still inadequate; reviewer recommends expanding related work if accepted.
