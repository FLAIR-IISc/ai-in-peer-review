- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, moving from univariate additive models to group additive models that allow correlations within groups.  
- Authors formalize the group additive setting and propose algorithms to identify optimal groupings.  
- Theoretical contribution: define a complexity measure for groupings (prefers many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The complexity measure appears reasonable but is not amenable to efficient optimization.  
- Proposed optimization strategies are exhaustive search (not scalable) and a greedy heuristic akin to forward feature selection.  
- Reviewer disappointed no alternative, more optimizable complexity measures were developed despite mentioning other measures.  
- Related work discussion is limited and omits relevant areas like structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.  
- Cited related work is sparse (mainly one KDD-2015 paper), making novelty hard to assess.  
- Experiments are limited to small synthetic tests and the Boston Housing dataset with no baselines or comparisons.  
- Summary: novel theoretical idea for group complexity, but weak literature context and lack of comparative experiments undermine overall impact.
