- Paper studies group-additive nonparametric regression and proposes identifying unknown group structure via a structure-penalized kernel method.  
- Key contribution: a novel complexity penalty for group structure derived from RKHS covering numbers, integrated into kernel ridge regression for joint optimization of partition and function.  
- Two algorithms proposed to optimize the combinatorial problem: exhaustive search and a backward stepwise greedy heuristic.  
- Theoretical result: consistency of the group structure estimator (probability of recovering the true structure → 1 as sample size grows).  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the method's effectiveness.  
- Strength: original, principled derivation of penalty using function-space capacity, filling a gap between additive and fully nonparametric models.  
- Strength: strong technical quality with clear definitions (e.g., intrinsic group additive structure) and rigorous proofs.  
- Strength: complete contribution—method, theory, algorithms, and experiments—presented coherently.  
- Strength: generally well-written and organized, making complex ideas accessible.  
- Weakness: computational scalability—optimizing over group partitions is combinatorial and the greedy backward stepwise method may not find the global optimum in high dimensions.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains numerous typographical and grammatical errors that need correction.  
- Detailed comment: consider a simpler or pairwise definition of “interaction” to improve intuition without full group-additive structure.  
- Detailed comment: clarify “almost surely (in population)”—whether it refers to asymptotics as n → ∞ or a statement for fixed n under the data-generating distribution.  
- Detailed comment: Equation (3) seems to be missing a summation over sample index i in the empirical risk term.  
- Detailed comment: prefer the term “translation-invariant kernel” over “convolution kernel” for clarity in ML literature.  
- Suggested edits/typos: multiple specific corrections (e.g., “on RHS” → “on the RHS”; fix grammar in Lines 152, 160; “turning” → “tuning”; Algorithm 2 Line 1 “State with” → “Start with”; “by compare” → “by comparing”).
