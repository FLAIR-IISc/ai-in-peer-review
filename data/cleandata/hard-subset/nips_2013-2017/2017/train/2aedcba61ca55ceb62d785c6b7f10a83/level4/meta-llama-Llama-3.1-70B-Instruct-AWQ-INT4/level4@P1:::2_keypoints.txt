- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls added to kernel ridge regression to learn group structure in group-additive nonparametric models.  
- Method interpolates between fully nonparametric and additive models by optimizing group structure and within-group regressors.  
- Key innovation: quantifying group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the direct-sum RKHS.  
- Paper includes two algorithms for approximately optimizing the compound problem over groups and functions.  
- Theoretical results: rates showing empirical risk approaches true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure.  
- Empirical results presented on both synthetic and real datasets supporting the method.  
- Overall assessment: comprehensive, well-motivated, and a potentially significant advance for fitting models between additive and nonparametric extremes.  
- Main weakness: optimization over group structures may be computationally challenging when the number of variables is large.  
- Presentation issues: some minor clarity questions (e.g., definition of "interaction", use of "almost surely") and a few typos.  
- Noted errors: missing summation in Equation (3) and several wording/typo mistakes that should be corrected.  
- Strengths summarized: novel penalty, solid theoretical and empirical backing, and high originality/significance.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
