- Paper studies high-dimensional kernel regression, moving from additive models to a new “group additive model” that allows within-group correlations.  
- Authors provide a formal treatment and propose several (somewhat) ad hoc algorithms to find optimal groupings.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a complexity measure for groupings (favoring many small groups) derived as an upper bound on the covering number.  
- The complexity measure is not easily optimizable; proposed solutions are exhaustive search (not scalable) or a greedy forward-selection-style method.  
- Reviewer disappointed no alternative, more efficiently optimizable measures were developed or detailed despite mention of investigating others.  
- Major concern: inadequate discussion of related work—fields like structured sparsity, graphical models/Bayesian networks, and feature selection are not cited or connected.  
- Related-work section relies largely on a single KDD-2015 paper [8], making it hard to assess novelty for a non-expert.  
- Experiments are limited: only small synthetic tests and one Boston Housing example, with no baseline comparisons to other methods.  
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, but experiments remain limited and connections to ML literature are still insufficient.  
- Reviewer acknowledges valid contributions, adjusted score, and strongly urges a substantially improved related-work discussion before publication.
