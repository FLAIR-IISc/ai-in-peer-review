- Addresses group-additive nonparametric regression, learning additive functions over unknown partitions of predictors.  
- Proposes a novel penalty based on covering numbers of direct-sum RKHS balls, integrated into kernel ridge regression to jointly learn group structure and estimate the function.  
- Bridges the gap between fully nonparametric (computationally intractable) and additive (potentially restrictive) models and is well-positioned relative to sparse additive models.  
- Presents two practical algorithms approximating the compound optimization and provides strong theoretical guarantees: convergence rates for empirical to true risk and consistency of the estimated group structure as n → ∞.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets supports the proposed methodology.  
- Primary limitation: computational scalability when the number of predictors p is large; acknowledged as future work.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains minor grammatical issues and typos that slightly hinder readability.  
- Minor technical clarifications requested: a simpler characterization of "interaction" (e.g., pairwise/conditional-independence), explicit use of asymptotic wording for "almost surely," and the missing summation in Equation (3).  
- Terminology note: prefer "translation-invariant kernel" over the non-standard "convolutional kernel."  
- Specific typos to fix (examples): "on RHS" → "on the RHS"; "bias... reduces" → "bias... reduce"; "G^ exists and unique" → "G^ exists and is unique"; "turning parameters" → "tuning parameters"; Algorithm 2 "State with" → "Start with"; "by compare" → "by comparing."  
- Overall assessment: substantial, theoretically rigorous contribution with solid experiments; recommendation: Accept.
