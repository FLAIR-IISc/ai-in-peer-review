- Study of group-additive nonparametric regression where the regression function is additive across unknown groups of predictors, positioned between fully nonparametric and purely additive models.
- Proposal of a novel penalty based on covering numbers of RKHS balls incorporated into kernel ridge regression to regularize over group structures.
- Formulation optimizes jointly over group partitions and regression functions defined on the direct-sum RKHS of groups.
- Two algorithms proposed for approximate optimization of the combined group-structure and function-estimation problem.
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence rate of empirical risk to true risk for the estimator.
- Theoretical guarantees: consistency of group-structure recovery (probability of recovering the true grouping → 1 as n increases).
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets supports the methodology.
- Key innovation: measuring group-structure complexity via covering numbers of the associated direct-sum space.
- Strengths: well-motivated, novel approach with solid theoretical analysis and empirical results.
- Main limitation: computationally challenging to optimize over group structures for large numbers of variables.
- Writing is generally clear but the paper needs careful proofreading to correct numerous typographical errors.
- Question: is there a simpler characterization of pairwise variable interaction than the full model in Equation (1)?
- Clarification needed: the phrase “almost surely” (Line 169) is ambiguous—does it refer to the limit as n → ∞?
- Minor technical issues: Equation (3) appears to miss a summation over i; suggest using “translation-invariant kernel” instead of “convolutional kernel.”
- Noted typos to fix (examples): “on RHS” → “on the RHS”; grammar fixes at Lines 152 and 160; “turning parameters” → “tuning parameters”; Algorithm 2 “State with” → “Start with”; “by compare” → “by comparing.”
