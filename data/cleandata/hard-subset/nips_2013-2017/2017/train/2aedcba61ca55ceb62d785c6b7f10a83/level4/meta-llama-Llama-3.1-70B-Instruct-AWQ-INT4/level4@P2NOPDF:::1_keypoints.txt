- Introduces additive model f = Σ fp(Xp) (ignores inter-dimension correlations) and proposes group additive model to capture correlations by grouping dimensions.
- Provides a formal treatment of group additive setting and algorithms for finding optimal groupings, though algorithms appear somewhat ad hoc.
- Demonstrates approach on small synthetic datasets and the Boston Housing dataset.
- Related work discussion is inadequate, making novelty hard to assess for non-experts.
- Fails to cite or connect to relevant areas like structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.
- Related work section largely relies on a single KDD-2015 paper [8], which is insufficient.
- Theoretical contribution (complexity measure for groupings) appears novel and favors many small groups.
- Complexity measure is derived as an upper bound on the covering number and seems reasonable but is not easy to optimize.
- Proposed optimization methods are exhaustive search (poor scalability) or a greedy forward-selection–style algorithm.
- Reviewer expected a more efficient or principled optimization method and more comparison of alternative measures.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks baselines or comparisons to other methods, making result quality unclear.
- After rebuttal, some concerns were addressed; reviewer raised score but still urges stronger experiments and much improved related work discussion.
