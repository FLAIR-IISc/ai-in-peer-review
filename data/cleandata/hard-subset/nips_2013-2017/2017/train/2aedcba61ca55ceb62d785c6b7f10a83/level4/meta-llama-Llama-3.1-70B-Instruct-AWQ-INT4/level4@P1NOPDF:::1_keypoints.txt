- Paper studies kernel regression in high dimensions, starting from the additive model f = Σ f_p(X_p).
- Additive model ignores cross-dimension correlations, which is problematic.
- Authors propose a "group additive model" that groups dimensions to capture correlations.
- The paper gives a formal treatment and proposes algorithms to find groupings, but these are somewhat ad hoc.
- Empirical results are limited to small synthetic experiments and a basic Boston Housing example.
- Related work is inadequately described, making novelty hard to assess for non-experts.
- Authors omit connections to structured sparsity, graphical model/Bayesian network learning, and feature selection.
- Related work cited is essentially limited to a single KDD-2015 paper, which is insufficient.
- The theoretical contribution is novel: a complexity measure on groupings favoring many small groups, derived via an upper bound on the covering number.
- The complexity measure is hard to optimize; authors resort to exhaustive search or a greedy forward-selection-like method.
- Reviewer disappointed authors did not find a more efficiently optimizable measure or provide insights despite mentioning ongoing investigations.
- Post-rebuttal: some concerns were addressed, score improved, but experiments remain limited and related-work discussion is still poor; reviewer urges stronger literature discussion on publication.
