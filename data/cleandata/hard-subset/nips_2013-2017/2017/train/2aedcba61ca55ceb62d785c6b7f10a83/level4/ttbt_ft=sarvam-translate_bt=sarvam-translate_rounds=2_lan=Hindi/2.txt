Summary:

This paper studies group-forcing non-parametric regression models, where the regression function is a forcing between groups of variables for certain partitions of the predictor variable; this model interpolates between completely non-parametric models, which are difficult to fit, and forcing models, which are sometimes too restrictive. In particular, this paper studies the problem where the group structure is not known beforehand and must be learned from the data. To do this, the paper proposes a novel penalty function based on the covering numbers of the RKHs balls, which is later added to the kernel ridge regression objective. This results in an objective that can be optimized on both the group structure (which, together with the kernel, determines a function space through the direct sum of the RKHs over each group of variables) and the regression estimate within each group. Two algorithms are presented to approximately solve this combined optimization problem, and then theoretical results are presented showing (a) the rate at which the empirical risk of the estimate approaches the actual risk of the true optimum, and (b) the stability of the group structure estimate, given that it is likely to match the correct group structure as n -> infinity. Finally, experimental results are presented on both synthetic and real data.

Key Comments:

The main novelty of the paper seems to lie in recognizing that the complexity of a particular group structure can be measured in terms of the covering numbers of the direct sum space. The paper is comprehensive, including a well-motivated and novel method, and has reasonably solid theoretical and empirical results. I am not very familiar with other approaches to model fitting between forcing and non-parametric models, but, assuming that the discussion in lines 31-50 is quite complete, this paper seems like a potentially significant advance. As mentioned in the discussion section, the main issue with the method seems to be the difficulty in solving the optimization problem on the group structure when the number of variables is large. The paper is quite clearly written, except for a lot of typos.
