- Paper addresses interesting GAN questions: link between GAN solution and parameter convergence, what generator fits at convergence, and choosing discriminator output activation for compositeness.
- Uses deformed exponential distributions framework to derive theoretical results about GANs.
- Presents a variational generalization connecting f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters.
- Shows that under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Suggests careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation may improve GANs.
- Briefly offers an alternative GAN interpretation via expected utility theory.
- Reviewer finds the theory interesting but questions practical relevance and usefulness in current form.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model lie in the deformed exponential family—need justification and whether results extend beyond this assumption.
- Major concern: the parameter-space f-GAN identity (Eq.7)—need clarification of practical benefits, implementability, and experimental comparison to distribution-based optimization.
- Criticism that authors underuse the RHS of Eq.7 and must explain why convergence in divergence might not imply parameter convergence with a realistic example.
- Practical implication about output activation gf is interesting but needs experimental comparison to prior heuristics (Ref. [34]).
- Experiments are inconclusive: μ-ReLU (theoretically preferred) performs worse than ReLU; authors should explain this and discuss reversibility in DCGAN and comparison to WGAN.
- Results in Table 2 appear within error bars, providing insufficient empirical support for theoretical claims.
- Minor: request more intuition on deformations, signatures, and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.
- Minor: ask for comparison among the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8, implications for activation choice, and explanation of why decreasing bound with escort normalization Z is beneficial.
- Overall conclusion: theoretical insights are interesting but currently not substantial or convincing enough for GAN practitioners.
