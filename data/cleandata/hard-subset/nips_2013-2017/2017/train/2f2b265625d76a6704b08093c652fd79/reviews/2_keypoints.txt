- Paper proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, shows parameter-space convergence via f-/chi-divergence mapping and Bregman divergence.
- Provides implementation guidance for f-GAN and a factorization result of deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions.
- Reviewer checked most proofs (except Theorem 8) and finds them likely correct.
- Theorem 4 (IG exposition for f-GAN) is a standout contribution and impressive.
- Recommendation: clear accept, but paper is very dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with information theory/geometry.
- Suggest moving Fig.1 (IT–IG connections) from appendix to main text for clarity.
- Recommend stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analysing optimisation behavior) instead of introducing it late.
- Unclear how Section 5 connects to f-GAN — it explains representational power of deformed exponential families but doesn't show how to choose f or guide f-GAN design.
- Question the claim in Theorem 6 that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics”; eq. (13) may not match a deformed exponential family form.
- Suggest relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257–269) to appendix to focus the main text.
- Experiments seem disconnected from main claims: activation-function results don’t require IG view; WGAN results distract from f-GAN focus.
- Overall: many useful results but the paper needs reorganization and clearer, more coherent presentation.
