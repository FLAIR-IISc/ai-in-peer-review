

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

## Summary
This paper attempts to establish a connection between f-GANs and information geometry by claiming an identity that relates f-divergences to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families. The authors propose that f-GAN(P, escort(Q)) = D(θ‖ϑ) + Penalty(Q), where D is a Bregman divergence, θ and ϑ are natural parameters, and escort(Q) is an escort distribution. They claim this identity holds for deformed exponential families (χ-exponential families) and provide theoretical results supporting this claim, along with some experimental validation.

## Major Weaknesses

### Technical Inaccuracies and Notation Issues
1. **Inconsistent and Contradictory Notation**: The paper defines R++ = R\R++ (Section 3, Theorem 7), which is a logical contradiction. This is a fundamental error that undermines the mathematical rigor of the paper.

2. **Misrepresentation of Prior Work**: The introduction claims Nowozin et al. [30] "showed that the GAN principle can be extended to the variational formulation of all f-divergences." This misrepresents [30], which introduced f-GAN as a way to use any f-divergence, not as an extension to "all" f-divergences. This inaccuracy suggests the authors don't fully understand the prior work they're building upon.

3. **Misuse of References**: The paper cites [5] for "the only generalizations known fall short of the f-divergence formulation," but [5] is "Geometry of deformed exponential families," which doesn't discuss f-divergences in the context of GANs. This citation is completely misapplied.

4. **Theoretical Claims Without Proof**: The paper states "the difference vanish only for exponential families" without defining what "the difference" refers to. This is an important claim but lacks sufficient definition or proof.

5. **Incorrect Mathematical Formulation**: The paper claims "T*(x) = −(1/Z) · (χ(Q(x))/χ(P(x)))" (Theorem 7), but χ is defined as a function from R+ to R+, while Q(x) and P(x) are densities (real numbers, not necessarily in R+). This formulation is mathematically nonsensical.

### Formatting and Clarity Issues
1. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses multiple notations for the escort distribution (P̃, P̃χ,C, P̃) without clear definition, creating confusion.

2. **Undefined Terms**: The paper uses "IP,Q" (the range of density ratios) without formal definition, relying on the reader to infer its meaning from context.

3. **Typos and Inconsistencies**: Multiple typos throughout (e.g., "st Conference" instead of "at Conference"), inconsistent use of "deformed exponential families" vs. "deformed exponential family," and missing punctuation.

4. **Misleading Experimental Claims**: The paper claims "Matsushita entropy [18, 28] for which ΨMAT(z) = (1/2) · (1 + z/√(1 + z^2))" but this formula does not match the standard definition of Matsushita entropy. The cited references [18, 28] don't appear to contain this formula.

### Weaknesses in Argumentation
1. **Overstated Claims**: The paper claims "the f-GAN problem can be equivalent to a geometric optimization problem," but only provides theoretical results for specific cases, not general f-divergences.

2. **Lack of Experimental Validation**: The experiments (Section 6) claim to validate the theoretical contributions but fail to provide sufficient evidence. For example, the claim that "µ-ReLU performs overall the best" is not supported by clear statistical significance tests or comprehensive comparisons.

3. **Unsubstantiated Claims**: The paper claims "the standard deep generator architecture is powerful at modelling complex escorts of any deformed exponential family" but provides no empirical evidence beyond the theoretical result in Theorem 9.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Insight**: The connection between deformed exponential families and f-divergences is potentially interesting, though the paper doesn't fully develop this insight.

2. **Novel Notation**: The use of χ-exponential families is an interesting extension of regular exponential families.

3. **Potential Practical Value**: The paper's suggestions about activation functions and link functions could be useful for practitioners, though the experimental validation is weak.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses an interesting connection between f-GANs and information geometry, it is undermined by multiple technical inaccuracies, inconsistent notation, and misrepresentation of prior work. The mathematical claims are often poorly defined or incorrect, and the experimental validation is insufficient to support the paper's main claims. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to fix these fundamental issues.

The paper makes several claims that are either incorrect (R++ = R\R++), misrepresent prior work (Nowozin et al. [30]), or are unsupported by the evidence provided. These issues are too substantial to be overlooked, even if the core idea has potential.

## Recommendation

Reject. The paper contains too many fundamental technical errors and inconsistencies that undermine its main claims. The authors would need to substantially revise the paper to address these issues before it could be considered for publication. The quality of the paper does not meet the standards expected for NIPS.
