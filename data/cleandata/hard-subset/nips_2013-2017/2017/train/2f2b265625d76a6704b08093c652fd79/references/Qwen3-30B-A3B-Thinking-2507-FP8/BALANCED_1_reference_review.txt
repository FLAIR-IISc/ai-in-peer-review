

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper addresses a significant theoretical gap in the understanding of f-GANs by connecting them to information geometry through deformed exponential families. The authors build upon Nowozin et al.'s [30] work on f-GANs, which established that f-GANs provide a variational approximation to f-divergences. The key contribution is a variational information geometric identity (Corollary 8) showing that the f-GAN game can be interpreted as a Bregman divergence optimization problem for distributions in deformed exponential families, with the generator learning escorts of these distributions. The paper also provides theoretical results on how deep architectures can model these escorts, with implications for activation function design.

**Strengths:**

1. **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by bridging f-GANs with information geometry. The variational information geometric identity (Corollary 8) elegantly connects the f-GAN game to Bregman divergences, providing a more complete theoretical foundation than previous work. This connection is novel and well-motivated.

2. **Technical Rigor:** The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs (in the supplement) for all key results. The authors carefully define the necessary mathematical concepts (deformed exponential families, χ-logarithms, etc.) and build their argument systematically.

3. **Practical Insights:** The paper provides concrete theoretical insights about activation functions (Lemma 11) that have direct implications for practitioners. The authors demonstrate that common activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfy the theoretical conditions, and they provide experimental validation of the impact of activation choice on GAN performance.

4. **Clear Organization:** The paper is well-structured, with a logical flow from theoretical foundations to practical implications. The definitions and theorems are clearly presented and referenced.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Experimental Validation:** While the experiments (Section 6) validate the theoretical insights to some extent, they are relatively limited. The paper compares only a few activation functions (µ-ReLU variants, Softplus, LSU) and one alternative link function (Matsushita entropy). The results show marginal improvements over baselines, but it's unclear how these improvements compare to state-of-the-art GAN methods. The paper would benefit from more comprehensive experiments showing the practical impact of the theoretical insights on standard GAN benchmarks.

2. **Accessibility:** The paper assumes significant familiarity with information geometry and statistical manifolds. While this is appropriate for a NIPS audience, the paper could be made more accessible by providing more intuitive explanations of the geometric interpretations, especially for readers less familiar with these concepts.

3. **Connection to Practical GAN Applications:** The paper demonstrates that the theoretical insights can lead to marginal improvements in GAN training, but it doesn't clearly articulate how these insights could be broadly applied to improve existing GAN architectures or solve specific practical challenges in generative modeling. The significance of the theoretical results for the broader GAN community could be more strongly emphasized.

4. **Comparison to Related Work:** While the paper cites relevant work (Amari, Nowozin et al.), it could better contextualize its contribution relative to recent advances in understanding GANs (e.g., the connections to optimal transport in Wasserstein GANs [6], or the theoretical analyses of GAN convergence [12, 21]).

**Recommendation:**

This paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution to the understanding of f-GANs by connecting them to information geometry. The theoretical results are sound and novel, and the practical insights about activation functions have potential value for practitioners. However, the limited experimental validation and lack of clear articulation of broader practical significance weaken the paper's impact.

I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper's theoretical contribution is strong and fits well within NIPS' focus on foundational advances in machine learning. To strengthen the paper for publication, the authors should:

1. Expand the experimental section to include more comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art GAN methods, demonstrating the practical value of their theoretical insights.
2. Provide clearer explanations of the geometric interpretations for readers less familiar with information geometry.
3. Better articulate how the theoretical insights could be broadly applied to improve existing GAN architectures and solve practical challenges in generative modeling.
4. More thoroughly contextualize their contribution relative to recent advances in GAN theory.

The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs, and with these improvements, it would be a strong candidate for publication at NIPS.
