- Establishes a connection between f-GANs and information geometry: f-GAN(P, escort(Q)) = Bregman divergence between natural parameters + a penalty that vanishes for exponential families.  
- Extends prior KL–Bregman results to general f-divergences for deformed exponential families, addressing a long-standing open problem.  
- Provides rigorous theorems with proofs (in supplement); technically sound and comprehensive theoretical framework.  
- Empirically validates theory: specially designed activations (e.g., μ-ReLU) and link functions improve GAN performance across architectures (DCGAN, MLP) and datasets (MNIST, LSUN).  
- Presents a novel link (Theorem 9) between deep neural networks and deformed exponential families, offering a theoretical reason for deep architectures’ effectiveness in GANs.  
- Clarifies the supervised GAN game by relating it to proper composite losses, resolving ambiguities in Nowozin et al.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with consistent notation and sufficient experimental detail for experts.  
- Practical impact may be limited primarily to the GAN research community; broader applicability remains unclear.  
- Theorem 9 is interesting but gives limited practical guidance on selecting optimal activation functions for specific problems.  
- Experiments are somewhat limited (only MNIST and LSUN tower) and lack comparisons to more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) and state-of-the-art GAN variants.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, rigorous proofs, experimental support, and resolution of prior ambiguities.  
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain broader impact, limited experimental scope, and lack of a clear methodology for choosing activations/link functions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — expand discussion on broader implications, add experiments on harder datasets, and provide clearer guidance for selecting activations/link functions.
