This paper attempts to connect f-GANs to information geometry, specifically by establishing an identity relating f-divergences to Bregman divergences within the context of deformed exponential families. While the core idea of bridging these two perspectives is potentially valuable, the paper suffers from a multitude of issues, ranging from notational inconsistencies and imprecise definitions to a lack of clarity in the derivation of key results and an overestimation of the practical impact of the theoretical findings.

**Summary of Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:** The authors correctly identify the gap in understanding the parameters and geometric interpretation of f-GANs, building upon Nowozin et al. [30]. They aim to provide an information-geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective function, linking it to Bregman divergences and demonstrating how deep neural networks can effectively model escorts of deformed exponential families. This work attempts to extend the KL divergence connection in exponential families [2, 4] to the broader f-divergence framework. 

**Strengths:**
*   The attempt to connect f-GANs with information geometry is a worthwhile goal, potentially providing a deeper understanding of the method.
*   The theoretical exploration of deformed exponential families and their relationship to GANs is novel.
*   The identification of the link function as a crucial, often overlooked, parameter in the supervised game is a valid point.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistencies & Imprecision:**  Numerous notational inconsistencies abound. For example, the introduction uses θ and ϑ for parameters, but later definitions seem to interchange their roles. The use of  "escort(Q)" is ambiguous without consistently defining its relationship to the χ-escort.  The definition of `If (P‖Q)` uses an integral, while the equation following it is a summation, without clear justification.
*   **Lack of Clarity in Derivations:** Many derivations are presented without sufficient detail. The leap from Theorem 6 to Corollary 8 feels abrupt, lacking crucial steps to justify the connection. The proof sketches mentioned ("in SM") are inadequate; a robust NIPS submission requires complete, self-contained proofs, or exceptionally well-justified omissions.
*   **Overstated Claims & Limited Practical Impact:** The claim of unveiling a "key parameter left arbitrary in [30]" is overstated. The link function was already known to be important, albeit not explicitly formalized in that manner. The experimental results are insufficient to support the claims regarding the benefits of carefully designed activation functions or tuned link functions. The improvements observed are modest and lack statistical significance.
*   **Redundancy & Verbosity:** The paper suffers from redundancy. Concepts are repeated multiple times without adding substantial value. The writing style is unnecessarily complex, hindering clarity.
*   **Formatting and Typographical Errors:** Numerous typos and minor formatting inconsistencies exist throughout the paper, detracting from its professionalism.  For instance, repeated use of "e.g." without subsequent examples is poor style.
*   **Incomplete treatment of the "Penalty(Q)" term:** The nature and interpretation of `Penalty(Q)` in eq. (3) are glossed over, diminishing the significance of the purported identity.
* **Missing citations:** Proper composite loss functions are well known and have a rich literature [32, 33]. These should be cited.

**Detailed Concerns:**

*   **Definition 1:** The notation `If (P‖Q) . = ...` is unconventional and creates visual clutter.  Standard notation (e.g., D<sub>f</sub>(P||Q)) would be preferable.
*   **Theorem 5:** The condition “sup ξ(IP,Q) < ∞” is critical but not adequately motivated. Its implications for GAN training are not fully discussed.
*   **Section 4:** The connection between the deep network architecture and the factorization of escorts (Theorem 9) is presented as a significant result but lacks rigorous justification. The assumption of network invertibility is strong and unrealistic for most practical GAN architectures. The proof sketch promises a constructive way to build `v` from `χ`, yet the details are relegated to the supplement.
*   **Theorem 12:**  The connection to proper composite losses is a valuable observation, but it’s presented late in the paper and lacks sufficient elaboration.
*   **Experiments:** The experimental setup is poorly described. The choice of hyperparameters is not justified.  The results lack statistical analysis, making it difficult to assess the significance of the observed improvements. The LSUN visual examples are unconvincing.

**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   Significant lack of clarity and rigor in derivations.
*   Numerous notational inconsistencies and typographical errors.
*   Overstated claims with limited experimental support.
*   Redundant and verbose writing style.
*   The paper feels incomplete and hastily written.

**For:**

*   The core idea of connecting f-GANs to information geometry is potentially interesting.
*   The identification of the link function is a valid point.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially valuable core idea, this paper falls short of NIPS standards. The lack of clarity, rigor, and sufficient experimental validation outweigh the strengths. The numerous minor flaws accumulate to create a significant barrier to understanding and appreciating the contributions. I recommend **rejection**. The authors should address the concerns outlined above through a substantial revision, focusing on clarifying the derivations, providing a more thorough experimental evaluation, and improving the overall presentation. Specifically, the supplement needs to be drastically expanded to contain complete proofs and detailed experimental methodology.
