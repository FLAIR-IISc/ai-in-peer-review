

# Peer Review: "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

## Summary

This paper establishes a theoretical connection between f-GANs (a generalization of GANs using f-divergences) and information geometry. The authors derive a key identity (Corollary 8) showing that the f-GAN objective can be expressed as a Bregman divergence between natural parameters of distributions in deformed exponential families, plus a penalty term. This bridges the gap between the variational f-GAN formulation [30] and geometric optimization. The paper also shows that common activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) naturally model the required escort distributions in deep generators, and provides a principled characterization of the discriminator's link function using proper composite losses. The authors validate their theory with experiments showing improved performance when using theoretically motivated activation and link functions.

This work builds on Nowozin et al.'s f-GAN framework [30] and connects it to the well-established information geometry literature (Amari, Nagaoka [4]) and deformed exponential families (Amari et al. [5, 13]). It addresses a critical gap in the f-GAN literature regarding the connection between the variational formulation and geometric optimization.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Novelty**: The core identity (Corollary 8) is a significant theoretical contribution that provides a geometric interpretation of f-GAN training. This is the first known generalization of the KL divergence/Bregman divergence connection to the full f-divergence framework.

2. **Comprehensive Analysis**: The paper thoroughly analyzes both information-theoretic and geometric aspects of f-GANs, filling a major gap in [30] regarding the link between distributions and the generator.

3. **Practical Implications**: The theoretical results directly inform how to design better GAN architectures (activation functions) and discriminators (link functions). The experiments demonstrate tangible improvements with these theoretically motivated choices.

4. **Rigorous Proofs**: The proofs are well-constructed and appear technically sound, with detailed supplement supporting the theoretical claims.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Experimental Scope**: The experiments are restricted to MNIST and LSUN tower, and only compare a few activation functions and link functions. More extensive experiments on diverse datasets would strengthen the practical claims.

2. **Mathematical Density**: The paper is quite dense with mathematical notation (χ-logarithms, deformed exponential families, etc.). While necessary for the theory, this may limit accessibility for readers without strong background in information geometry.

3. **Connection to Recent GAN Advances**: The paper doesn't clearly position itself relative to recent state-of-the-art GAN techniques (e.g., WGAN-GP, StyleGAN). It would be helpful to discuss how these techniques might benefit from the information geometric perspective.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims. The proofs are rigorous, and the authors are careful to acknowledge limitations (e.g., the need for complex applications to comply with geometric convergence). The experiments provide reasonable validation of the theoretical claims, though they could be more comprehensive.

## Clarity

The paper is well-organized but suffers from the complexity of the mathematical concepts. The introduction provides a good overview, but technical sections assume significant prior knowledge of information geometry. Some concepts (like χ-logarithms) are introduced without sufficient intuitive explanations, which may challenge readers without this background.

## Originality

The core identity connecting f-GANs to Bregman divergences is novel. While building on [30] and information geometry literature, the paper provides a significant extension that wasn't previously known. The connection between activation functions and escort distribution modeling is also novel.

## Significance

This work has substantial significance for both theory and practice. The geometric interpretation provides deeper understanding of GAN training dynamics, which could guide future research. The practical implications for activation function design and link function selection could lead to more stable and efficient GAN training. While the paper doesn't claim to directly improve state-of-the-art performance, the theoretical insights could inform future work in the field.

## Arguments for Acceptance

- Provides a deep theoretical connection between f-GANs and information geometry
- Establishes a novel identity (Corollary 8) explaining the geometric underpinnings of GAN training
- Shows practical implications for activation function design and link functions
- Well-supported theoretically with rigorous proofs
- Addresses a critical gap in the f-GAN literature

## Arguments Against Acceptance

- Experiments are somewhat limited in scope
- Paper is mathematically dense, potentially limiting accessibility
- Doesn't clearly connect to recent state-of-the-art GAN techniques

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong theoretical contribution that significantly advances our understanding of GANs. The paper makes a substantial novel contribution by connecting f-GANs to information geometry, providing both theoretical depth and practical implications. While the mathematical complexity may limit accessibility for some readers, the theoretical contribution is substantial and the practical implications are promising. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to NeurIPS, providing deeper theoretical insights that could guide future work in GAN design and training.

I recommend acceptance.
