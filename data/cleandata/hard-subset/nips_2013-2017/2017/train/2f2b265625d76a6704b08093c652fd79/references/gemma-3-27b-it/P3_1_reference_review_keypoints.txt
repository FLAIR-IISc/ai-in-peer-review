- Presents a significant theoretical contribution linking f-GANs with information geometry and information theory.  
- Proves an identity connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families (eq. 3).  
- Reinterprets the f-GAN optimization as a geometric optimization problem analogous to Wasserstein GANs.  
- Clarifies unspecified parameters in the f-GAN framework, notably the discriminator’s link function.  
- Analyzes deep networks’ capacity to model escorts of deformed exponential families and gives sufficient conditions based on activation functions.  
- Provides experimental validation (MNIST, LSUN) that modestly supports theoretical claims, including effects of activation and link function choices.  
- Strong mathematical rigor with proofs in the supplement and thorough literature referencing.  
- Paper is dense and mathematically demanding, with many definitions/theorems introduced without enough intuition.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (few datasets and configurations) and lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art GANs.  
- Practical implications and actionable guidance for improving GAN training are under-discussed.  
- Main results rely on the assumption that data follow deformed exponential families, which may limit applicability.  
- Theorem 9 assumes invertible network layers—a strong assumption that should be relaxed or analyzed further.  
- Section 3 needs clearer motivation for introducing deformed exponential families and their relation to generalizing beyond KL divergence.  
- Section 6 should present experimental results more clearly, with richer metrics and sample visualizations.  
- The connection to proper composite losses is valuable; a brief discussion of benefits of proper scoring rules would enrich the paper.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experiments.  
- Overall confidence in the review: 8/10.
