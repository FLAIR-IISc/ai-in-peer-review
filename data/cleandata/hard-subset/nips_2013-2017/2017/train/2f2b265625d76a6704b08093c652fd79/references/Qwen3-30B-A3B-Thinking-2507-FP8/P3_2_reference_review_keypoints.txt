1. The paper establishes a formal variational connection between f-GANs and information geometry, linking f-divergences, KLχ divergences, and Bregman divergences via Theorems 5–8.  
2. It shows neural networks can model escort distributions in deformed exponential families, explaining why activations like ReLU/ELU/Softplus work well (Theorem 9).  
3. Theorem 12 completes the "proper loss" framework for GANs by specifying the discriminator link function for proper composite losses.  
4. Experiments provide empirical support that tuning activation and link functions can modestly improve GAN performance (µ-ReLU variations, Matsushita entropy).  
5. The paper is well-organized and mathematically rigorous, with clear definitions, theorems, and proofs.  
6. Practical impact is limited: empirical gains are modest and major GAN issues (mode collapse, instability) are not addressed.  
7. The focus on deformed exponential families may be too restrictive for many real-world GAN applications.  
8. The claimed "information geometric" advantage is not clearly justified or linked to concrete practical benefits like geometric optimization.  
9. Experimental comparisons are narrow (few activations/link functions) and lack evaluation against state-of-the-art GAN architectures and training techniques.  
10. Builds appropriately on Nowozin et al. and Amari and cites relevant deformed-exponential-family and loss literature, but insufficiently engages broader GAN theory (e.g., Wasserstein GANs, convergence analyses).  
11. Overall assessment: a strong, technically sound theoretical contribution that advances mathematical understanding of GANs but with limited demonstrated practical utility.  
12. Recommendation: Accept—novelty and rigor justify publication despite practical gaps.  
13. Pro: rigorous theoretical foundation, completes supervised GAN theory, ties activations to info-geometry, excellent exposition.  
14. Con: limited practical demonstrations, narrow theoretical scope, unclear advantage over original f-divergence formulation.
