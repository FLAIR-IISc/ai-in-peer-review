## Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of f-GANs, bridging the gap between information theory and information geometry. It provides a compelling and mathematically rigorous connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences within the context of GANs, unveiling a deeper geometric interpretation of the training process. The theoretical results are substantial, and the experimental validation, while limited in scope, offers promising support. Despite some minor clarity issues and a somewhat dense presentation, I believe this work is a strong contribution and recommend acceptance.

**Summary:**

The paper builds upon the recent generalization of GANs to f-divergences (Nowozin et al., 2017). It identifies a key limitation in that framework – a lack of connection to information-geometric optimization. To address this, the authors establish a crucial identity (eq. 3) linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences for a class of distributions called deformed exponential families.  This connection allows interpreting the f-GAN problem as a geometric optimization problem akin to Wasserstein GANs.  Further, they drill down into the information-theoretic and geometric aspects, elucidating parameters within the supervised game (discriminator's link function) and demonstrating the suitability of deep networks for modeling escorts of deformed exponential families. Finally, they refine the understanding of the supervised learning problem by framing it in terms of proper composite losses.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The core mathematical contribution – the identity connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences – is well-motivated, rigorously proven (with proofs in the supplement), and provides a valuable new lens through which to view f-GANs.
* **Bridging Information Theory and Geometry:** The paper successfully connects two important fields, offering a more comprehensive understanding of the underlying mechanisms of GAN training. This is particularly important given the increasing interest in geometric perspectives on generative modeling (e.g., Wasserstein GANs).
* **Completing the f-GAN Picture:** The work clarifies previously unspecified parameters within the f-GAN framework, particularly the discriminator’s link function, grounding the theoretical framework in practical considerations.
* **Deep Network Analysis:**  The analysis of deep networks’ ability to model escorts is insightful. Demonstrating a sufficient condition for this modelling based on activation functions is a strong result.
* **Experimental Validation:**  While modest, the experiments provide initial evidence supporting the theoretical claims, demonstrating the impact of activation function choice and link function tuning.  The exploration of different activations (ReLU variants) and link functions (Matsushita entropy) is a valuable step.
* **Well-Referenced:**  The paper demonstrates a strong awareness of related work in information theory, information geometry, and GANs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Density & Presentation:** The paper is quite dense and mathematically demanding.  The introduction of numerous definitions and theorems without sufficient motivation can be overwhelming.  While rigor is appreciated, some sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations.  The writing style occasionally hinders accessibility.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are relatively limited in scope, focusing on MNIST and LSUN with a somewhat restricted set of configurations. A more extensive empirical evaluation across diverse datasets and architectures would further bolster the claims. It would have been beneficial to see comparison with state-of-the-art GAN implementations.
* **Practical Implications:** While the theoretical advances are substantial, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of these findings. How can these insights be directly translated into improved GAN training strategies or architectures?
* **Reliance on Deformed Exponential Families:** The key results rely on the assumption that data lies within deformed exponential families. While this is a reasonable assumption for many scenarios, it's important to acknowledge the limitations of this assumption and explore potential extensions to more general distributions.



**Detailed Comments & Questions:**

* **Section 3:** The motivation for introducing deformed exponential families could be clarified. Connecting it more explicitly to the broader goal of generalizing beyond KL divergence would be helpful.
* **Theorem 9:** The assumption of invertibility of network layers is strong. While the authors acknowledge this, exploring methods to relax this assumption or analyzing the impact of non-invertibility would be valuable.
* **Section 6:** The experimental results could be presented more clearly, with more detailed analysis of the performance metrics and visualizations of the generated samples.
* **Proper Composite Losses:** The connection to proper composite losses is excellent. A brief discussion of the benefits of using proper scoring rules in general would enrich this section.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a fundamentally new and valuable theoretical insight into the workings of f-GANs.
* The mathematical results are rigorous and well-supported.
* The connection between information theory and information geometry offers a deeper understanding of GAN training.
* The experimental results provide initial support for the theoretical claims.
* The work has the potential to inspire further research in the area of generative modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper is dense and may be difficult for some readers to follow.
* The experimental validation is limited in scope.
* The reliance on deformed exponential families may restrict the applicability of the results.




**Overall, I believe the strengths of this paper significantly outweigh its weaknesses. The theoretical contribution is substantial, the work is well-motivated, and the potential impact is considerable. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental evaluation.**
