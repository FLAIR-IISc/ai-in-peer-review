

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper attempts to connect f-GANs to information geometry through a variational identity, claiming to bridge a gap in the theoretical understanding of f-GANs. The main contribution is a proposed identity (equation 3) that relates f-GANs to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors also claim to provide a complete theoretical picture of the supervised GAN game and demonstrate that standard deep architectures can model escorts of deformed exponential families.

## Strengths
The paper identifies an interesting theoretical gap in f-GANs literature and attempts to fill it with a connection to information geometry. The authors demonstrate some theoretical depth in relating f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families, and their treatment of proper composite losses provides a more complete picture of the supervised game.

## Weaknesses

**1. Fundamental definition missing**: The paper states "f-GAN(P, Q) ≤ If(P‖Q)" (equation 1) but never defines what f-GAN(P, Q) actually is in the main text. This is a critical omission for a paper claiming to characterize the f-GAN game. The authors reference [30, Eq. 4] but do not provide the definition themselves, which is unacceptable for a main contribution of the paper.

**2. Technical inconsistencies**: The paper claims "the difference vanish only for exponential families" (singular "difference" with plural "families"), which is grammatically incorrect. More importantly, the paper states "IfKL(P‖Q) = D(θ‖ϑ)" in the introduction but in Definition 1 claims "IKL(P‖Q) = DC(ϑ‖θ)", creating a significant contradiction that undermines the paper's credibility.

**3. Missing figures**: The paper states "Results are shown in Figure 1" (section 6) but no figures are included in the submission. This is a serious formatting error that prevents evaluation of the experimental claims.

**4. Incomplete proofs**: Lemma 11 claims "ReLU is weakly admissible" but states the proof is in the supplement (Section V) without providing the proof in the main text. For a paper claiming to have theoretical contributions, this is an unacceptable omission.

**5. Citation errors**: The paper cites "[5, Theorem 9]" but [5] is "Geometry of deformed exponential families" by Amari et al., which is a paper about deformed exponential families, not the theorem referenced. This suggests a fundamental misunderstanding of the cited work.

**6. Theoretical gaps**: The paper claims "the standard deep generator architecture is powerful at modelling complex escorts of any deformed exponential family" but provides no theoretical justification beyond the proof of Theorem 9, which is limited to a specific construction. The connection between deep architectures and the modeling of escorts is not rigorously established.

**7. Experimental deficiencies**: The paper claims to have "experiments that display the uplift" but provides no quantitative results to support this claim. The description of experiments is vague ("Results are shown in Figure 1"), and no comparison with mainstream approaches is provided, making the experimental claims unsubstantiated.

**8. Formatting inconsistencies**: The references contain numerous formatting errors, including missing publication years, extra commas, and inconsistent citation styles. For example, "A general class of coefficients of divergence... Journal of the Royal Statistical Society B, 28:131–142,, ," has extra commas.

**9. Misattribution**: The paper claims "[30] is also silent regarding a key part of the game" but [30] is the paper that introduced f-GANs, making this claim factually incorrect.

**10. Technical errors**: In Theorem 7, the authors define "R++ . = R\R++" which is confusing and mathematically nonsensical (R\R++ would be empty set). This is a serious technical error that should not appear in a NIPS paper.

## Recommendation

While the paper identifies an interesting theoretical gap in f-GANs literature and makes some theoretical connections to information geometry, it contains numerous critical technical, formatting, and presentation flaws that undermine its credibility. The fundamental definition of f-GAN(P, Q) is missing, there are significant technical contradictions, and the experimental claims are unsubstantiated due to missing figures and results. The paper would need substantial revisions to address these issues, including providing the missing definition, resolving the technical contradictions, adding the experimental figures, and providing complete proofs for all theoretical claims.

Given the number and severity of these minor flaws, which collectively prevent proper evaluation of the paper's contributions, I recommend rejection. A paper of this caliber should not contain such basic technical errors and omissions. The authors have not demonstrated sufficient technical rigor to warrant publication in NIPS.
