- Summary: Connects f-GANs and information geometry by linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families, gives variational identity (eq.3), and argues deep generators can model escorts with tuned activations/link functions.

- Strength: Genuine theoretical contribution linking f-divergences and Bregman divergences through a well-motivated identity.

- Strength: Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with coherent definitions and exposition.

- Strength: Experimental validation shows certain activations/link functions (μ-ReLU, Matsushita entropy) yield small practical gains.

- Weakness: Main identity (eq.3) only holds for deformed exponential families, a restrictive assumption that is not sufficiently emphasized.

- Weakness: Key technical result (Theorem 5) is deferred to the supplement, leaving the main paper lacking sufficient proof details.

- Weakness: Practical relevance is limited — reported improvements are marginal and don’t justify the added theoretical complexity.

- Weakness: Theorem 9 requires reversible networks (invertible weights/activations), a highly restrictive condition not met by typical deep networks.

- Comparison: Properly cites prior f-GAN and information-geometry work but fails to distinguish itself from other geometric GAN approaches and does not engage with the more applicable Wasserstein/optimal-transport perspective.

- Recommendation: Interesting theoretical note but limited significance; recommend reject unless authors clearly state limitations, include Theorem 5 proof, show stronger empirical gains, and better contextualize against Wasserstein GANs.

- Final assessment scores: Quality 6/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 7/10; Significance 5/10 — overall not compelling for NeurIPS.
