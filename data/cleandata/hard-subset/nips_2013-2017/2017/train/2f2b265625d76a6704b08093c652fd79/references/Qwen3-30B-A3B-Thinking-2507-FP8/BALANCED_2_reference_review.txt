

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper presents a theoretical connection between f-GANs and information geometry through a novel variational identity that relates f-divergences to Bregman divergences. The authors show that for distributions in deformed exponential families, the f-GAN objective can be expressed as a Bregman divergence between parameters plus a penalty term (equation 3). They further analyze how deep neural networks can model the "escort" distributions required by this framework, providing conditions on activation functions that enable this modeling.

**Strengths:**

1. **Theoretical Novelty:** The paper makes a significant contribution by bridging the gap between the f-GAN framework [30] and information geometry. The variational information geometric identity (Corollary 8) is a substantial theoretical advance that clarifies the mathematical foundations of f-GANs beyond previous work.

2. **Rigorous Analysis:** The paper provides formal theorems and proofs establishing the connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families (Theorems 9, 11), which is a valuable theoretical contribution to understanding deep learning architectures.

3. **Clear Positioning:** The paper effectively situates itself within the literature, distinguishing its contributions from both the f-GAN literature [30] and information geometry [4, 7, 9]. The comparison with related work is thorough and well-referenced.

4. **Practical Insights:** The theoretical analysis yields concrete practical recommendations about activation functions (Lemma 11) and link functions (Theorem 12), with experiments demonstrating the value of these insights.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Theoretical Framework:** The paper's focus on deformed exponential families creates a potential disconnect from practical GAN applications, where data distributions are rarely from such structured families. The authors acknowledge this limitation but don't sufficiently address how their framework can be applied when this assumption fails.

2. **Experimental Limitations:** The experiments are limited to MNIST and LSUN tower category with DCGAN and MLP architectures. More diverse experiments across different datasets and architectures would better demonstrate the generalizability of the theoretical claims. The paper also doesn't compare with state-of-the-art GAN variants beyond basic GAN and WGAN.

3. **Underdeveloped Connection to Practical GAN Training:** While the paper provides theoretical insights about activation functions, it doesn't clearly articulate how these insights translate to practical GAN training improvements beyond the limited experiments presented. The connection between the theoretical framework and actual GAN performance gains needs to be more explicit.

4. **Limited Discussion of Related Geometric Approaches:** The paper doesn't sufficiently compare its information-geometric framework with other geometric approaches to GANs (e.g., Wasserstein GANs [6] or the Fisher-Rao metric [14]), which would help contextualize the significance of their contribution.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution by establishing a deep connection between f-GANs and information geometry. The main identity (Corollary 8) is elegant and well-supported by theorems. However, the limited scope of the theoretical framework (deformed exponential families) and the modest experimental validation reduce the paper's significance for the broader GAN community.

**Recommendation:** The paper has strong theoretical merits but would benefit from addressing the limitations noted above. I recommend **weak acceptance** with the following suggestions for improvement:

1. Expand the experimental section to include more diverse datasets and architectures, and compare with recent state-of-the-art GAN variants.
2. Provide a more detailed discussion of how the framework applies to practical GAN training scenarios where the deformed exponential family assumption doesn't strictly hold.
3. Include a more comprehensive comparison with other geometric approaches to GANs (e.g., Wasserstein GANs) to better contextualize the contribution.
4. Clarify the practical implications of the theoretical results for GAN practitioners, beyond the limited experiments presented.

The paper makes an important theoretical contribution to understanding f-GANs, but to strengthen its significance for NIPS, the authors should better bridge the gap between their elegant theoretical framework and practical GAN applications. With these improvements, the paper would be a strong contribution to the field.
