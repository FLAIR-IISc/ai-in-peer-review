- Overall recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution linking f-GANs to information geometry; recommend acceptance with minor revisions for clarity and experiments.  
- Main contribution: Establishes a novel connection between f-divergences, Bregman divergences, and deformed exponential families in the GAN setting.  
- Theoretical value: Rigorous, detailed mathematical development including a noteworthy variational information-geometric f-GAN identity (Corollary 8).  
- Generator analysis: Shows deep generator architectures relate to escorts of deformed exponential families and that common activations naturally support this factorization (Theorem 9).  
- Supervised-game clarification: Resolves ambiguities in Nowozin et al. (2017) by linking supervised game parameters to proper composite losses (Section 5, Theorem 12).  
- Experimental support: Modest experiments (MNIST, LSUN tower, DCGAN/MLP) provide preliminary evidence for activation/link function claims but are limited in scope.  
- Novelty & originality: The conceptual bridge between information geometry and GANs is highly original and insightful.  
- Mathematical depth: Theorems and proofs are thorough and convincing, though dense.  
- Clarity/readability issue: Paper is very dense with many definitions and abrupt transitions, making it hard to follow without more intuition.  
- Limited experiments: Evaluation on simple datasets and few architectures; needs broader comparisons with state-of-the-art GANs.  
- Practical impact unclear: Paper does not fully elaborate actionable guidance for practitioners beyond activation choice.  
- Accessibility of proofs: Lengthy proofs relegated to supplement reduce accessibility; key proof steps should be summarized in the main text.  
- Broader applicability: Paper should briefly discuss potential extensions to other generative models or ML areas.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, rigorous foundations, potential to inform principled GAN design.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High mathematical complexity limiting accessibility, limited empirical validation, and underdeveloped practical guidance.  
- Suggested revisions — clarity: Add intuitive explanations, examples, and visual diagrams to improve flow.  
- Suggested revisions — experiments: Expand experiments to harder datasets and more architectures; systematically test activation/link functions.  
- Suggested revisions — practical guidance: Offer concrete advice on using the framework for hyperparameter and architecture choices.  
- Suggested revisions — proofs: Summarize key proof steps in the main paper to improve accessibility.  
- Related work: Citations are comprehensive and demonstrate good awareness of prior work in information geometry and GAN literature.
