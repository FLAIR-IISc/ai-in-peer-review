- Connects f-GANs to information geometry, expressing the f-GAN objective as a geometric optimization with Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families.  
- Proves a key variational information-geometric identity (Corollary 8) with rigorous supplementary proofs.  
- Shows deep networks can efficiently model escorts of deformed exponential families, providing theoretical justification for certain activation functions.  
- Builds on Nowozin et al.’s f-GAN framework and links it to Amari’s information geometry, filling a theoretical gap.  
- Strong technical quality; assumptions are carefully addressed and limitations acknowledged.  
- Experiments validate theoretical claims and show tuning activation/link functions can improve GAN performance.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with good definitions, though practical guidance for practitioners could be clearer.  
- Original contribution: novel connection (Corollary 8) and results on modeling escorts (Theorem 9, Lemma 11).  
- Significant: deepens theoretical understanding of GAN objectives, offers practical activation/link insights, and opens new research directions.  
- Strengths: missing theoretical connection provided, rigorous proofs, activation-function justification, relevant experiments, careful scope acknowledgment.  
- Weaknesses: limited articulation of practical implications for complex applications; sparse comparisons to other theoretical GAN work (e.g., WGAN); information-geometry concepts may be hard for some readers; experiments limited to MNIST/LSUN and two architectures.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, novel, and significant contribution to the community.
