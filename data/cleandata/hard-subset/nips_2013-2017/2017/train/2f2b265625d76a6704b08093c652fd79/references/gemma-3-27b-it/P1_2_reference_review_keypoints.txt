- Main contribution: Establishes a theoretical link between f-divergences and Bregman divergences for f-GANs using information geometry and deformed exponential families.
- Addresses open questions from Nowozin et al. (2017) about discriminator loss parameters and generator modeling capacity.
- Offers practical insights on generator architecture, discriminator loss design, and desirable activation function properties.
- Theoretical development is strong and provides a geometrically grounded understanding of GAN optimization.
- Paper is generally well-structured with clear definitions and logically presented theorems.
- Experimental results support theoretical claims about activation and link function choices, though experiments are limited.
- High mathematical density may overwhelm readers not versed in information geometry; more intuition alongside formalism is needed.
- Experimental scope is narrow: results are mainly tabular, lack detailed trend analysis, and ablations are insufficiently granular.
- Paper could better situate its contributions within recent GAN advances (e.g., spectral normalization, self-attention).
- Practical applicability is limited for typical practitioners; a cookbook-style section with concrete design recommendations is missing.
- Specialized terminology is sometimes used without adequate explanation, hindering accessibility.
- Theorem 5’s assumption on bounded subdifferentials needs clarification regarding practical validity during GAN training.
- Clarification requested on Section 4’s factorization claim, specifically its practical memory and computational implications.
- Section 5 needs a more intuitive explanation of why proper composite losses are advantageous in the GAN framework.
- Experiments limited activation comparison to ReLU, Softplus, and LSU; broader activation exploration is recommended.
- Questions about link-function experiments: were other link functions considered and what are the computational trade-offs of using Matsushita entropy?
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contribution, resolves open f-GAN questions, supports claims experimentally, and is well written.
- Arguments against acceptance: highly dense mathematics, limited experiments, weaker connection to broader GAN literature, and underexplored practical impact.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper worth acceptance with minor revisions to improve intuition, experiment breadth, and contextualization.
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions contingent on adding intuition, expanding experiments, and strengthening links to existing GAN work.
