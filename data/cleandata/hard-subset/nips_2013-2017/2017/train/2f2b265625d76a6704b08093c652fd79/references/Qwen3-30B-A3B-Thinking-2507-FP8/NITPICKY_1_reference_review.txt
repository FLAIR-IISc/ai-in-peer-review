

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

## Summary
This paper aims to establish an information-geometric interpretation of f-GANs by connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors claim to provide a variational information-geometric identity for f-GANs (eq. 3), complete the supervised game picture of Nowozin et al. [30], and demonstrate how standard deep architectures can model escorts of deformed exponential families. The work builds on information geometry and extends the theoretical foundation of GANs.

## Quality
The paper contains multiple technical inconsistencies that undermine its main claims. The most serious issue is the definition of KLχ divergence (Definition 4), which is defined as KLχ(P‖Q) = EX∼P[−logχ(Q(X)/P(X))]. This is the negative of the standard KL divergence, which would be EX∼P[log(P(X)/Q(X))]. This sign error fundamentally breaks the connection between KLχ divergences and Bregman divergences that the paper claims to establish.

The paper also contains multiple notational inconsistencies. The title uses "f -GAN" with a space, while the main text uses "f-GAN" without a space. The paper inconsistently uses "P" to denote both the distribution and its density (e.g., "P(x)" vs "P"). The notation "R++" for positive reals is non-standard (should be R+ or R>0).

Theorem 5 claims to provide a constructive proof for the equivalence between f-divergences and KLχ divergences, but the proof is relegated to the supplement. The paper states "the proof of Theorem 5 (in SM, Section I) is constructive" without providing sufficient details in the main text to verify the claim.

The paper states "for the escort to exist, we require that Z < ∞ and therefore χ(P) is finite almost everywhere." This is backwards: Z < ∞ implies χ(P) is finite almost everywhere, not the other way around.

## Clarity
The paper suffers from inconsistent and ambiguous notation throughout. The paper uses "χ" as both the signature of the deformed exponential family and as a function in the escort definition, without clear distinction. The term "selection of the subdifferential" (Theorem 5) is not defined or explained, leaving readers confused about what this means.

The connection between the f-GAN game and the information-geometric formulation (eq. 3) is obscured by inconsistent notation and mathematical errors. The paper claims "the difference vanish only for exponential families (escort(Q) = Q, Penalty(Q) = 0 and f = KL)" but doesn't provide a clear explanation of how this follows from the main identity.

The definition of the escort density (eq. 6) is problematic: "P̃χ,C = Z · χ(Pχ,C)" with Z = ∫X χ(Pχ,C(x|θ,φ))dµ(x). The standard definition of an escort distribution is P̃(x) = P(x)^α / Z for some α, but the paper uses χ(P(x)) for an arbitrary function χ, without explaining how this generalizes the standard definition.

## Originality
The paper claims to be the first to connect f-divergences to Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families, but this connection has been explored in previous work. The paper cites [5, Theorem 9] and [13, Theorem 3] as limitations, but doesn't properly contextualize these prior results or explain how the current work differs.

The paper's contribution regarding the "link function of proper composite losses" (Section 5) appears to be a minor extension of existing work on proper composite losses [32], rather than a significant new contribution.

## Significance
The paper's main claim—that f-GANs can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem—remains unconvincing due to the mathematical inconsistencies. The experimental results (Section 6) are not compelling, with results that are "globally equivalent to ReLU" or "almost consistently better" without clear evidence of significant improvement over state-of-the-art methods.

The paper's claim that "the standard deep generator architecture is powerful at modelling complex escorts of any deformed exponential family" is not sufficiently supported. The proof of Theorem 9 is constructive but relies on the assumption that χnet is continuous, which isn't explicitly stated as a condition.

## Minor Flaws Requiring Rejection
1. The sign error in KLχ definition (Definition 4) fundamentally undermines the main identity (eq. 3)
2. Inconsistent notation (f -GAN vs f-GAN, P vs P(x), R++ vs R+)
3. Ambiguous terms ("selection of the subdifferential" without definition)
4. Incorrect causal relationship in escort definition (Z < ∞ implies χ(P) finite, not vice versa)
5. Over-reliance on supplement for critical proofs (Theorem 5, Theorem 9)
6. Inconsistent use of "χ" as both signature and function
7. Non-standard notation "R++" for positive reals
8. Incomplete explanation of how the proposed framework differs from prior work

## Conclusion
While the paper addresses an interesting theoretical question about GANs, the numerous technical inconsistencies, notational errors, and fundamental mathematical issues (particularly the sign error in KLχ definition) significantly undermine its main claims. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to correct these errors, but as presented, it lacks the technical soundness required for publication at NIPS. The authors should thoroughly revise the paper to address these fundamental issues before resubmission.

The paper's claim of a "variational information-geometric identity for the f-GAN game" cannot be verified due to the mathematical inconsistencies. The experimental results do not demonstrate sufficient improvement to justify the theoretical claims. I recommend rejection.
