## Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to understanding the connection between f-GANs and information geometry. It successfully bridges a gap between the variational GAN formulation and geometric optimization, offering a deeper insight into the underlying mechanisms of GAN training. While the mathematical exposition is dense and requires a strong background, the results are compelling and have the potential to influence future GAN research. I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS, despite some clarity issues which could be addressed with minor revisions.

**Summary of Main Ideas:**

The paper builds upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2017) which showed the connection between f-GANs and f-divergences. The authors demonstrate an identity connecting the f-divergence formulation of f-GANs to Bregman divergences within the framework of deformed exponential families. This establishes a geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective, similar to Wasserstein GANs. They further delve into the information-theoretic and geometric aspects, specifying parameters of the supervised game, analyzing the generator’s ability to model escorts, and providing theoretical support for the role of activation functions. Finally, they revisit the supervised learning problem within f-GANs, clarifying the role of the discriminator's link function.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The core result – the identity connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences (Eq. 3) – is a significant advance. It provides a valuable theoretical foundation for understanding the geometric properties of f-GANs.
* **Bridging Information Theory and Geometry:**  The paper effectively links information-theoretic concepts (f-divergences) with geometric optimization (Bregman divergences), offering a more holistic view of GAN training.
* **Insights into Generator Architecture:**  The analysis of the generator’s ability to model escorts of deformed exponential families is insightful and provides a theoretical justification for the effectiveness of deep neural networks in this context.  The discussion regarding activation functions is particularly compelling.
* **Completing the Supervised Game Picture:**  The clarification of the discriminator's link function and its connection to proper composite losses is a valuable contribution, addressing a previously open question in Nowozin et al.’s work.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments, while not extensive, provide support for the theoretical findings, demonstrating the potential benefits of tuning activation functions and link functions.
* **Comprehensive Related Work:** The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature, providing appropriate citations to previous work in information theory, information geometry, and GANs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Accessibility:** The paper is mathematically dense and the notation is complex.  This makes it challenging for readers without a strong background in information geometry to fully grasp the arguments.  More intuitive explanations and examples would greatly improve clarity.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental results are somewhat limited and primarily serve to validate the theoretical findings rather than demonstrate a significant practical improvement over existing GAN techniques.  More extensive experimentation with a wider range of datasets and architectures would strengthen the paper.
* **Potential for Overselling:** The paper occasionally hints at the potential for transformative improvements in GAN training without fully demonstrating it in the experiments.  A more cautious tone would be appropriate.
* **Length:** The paper is quite long and could benefit from more concise writing and potentially moving some of the more technical details to the supplementary material.



**Addressing the NIPS Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. It represents a complete piece of work, although further experimental validation would be beneficial. The authors are careful to point out the limitations of their work and the assumptions they make.
* **Clarity:** As mentioned above, clarity is a significant weakness. The paper would benefit from more intuitive explanations, clearer notation, and a more gradual introduction of concepts.
* **Originality:** The paper presents a novel and significant combination of ideas from information theory and information geometry, offering a new perspective on GAN training. The work is clearly differentiated from previous contributions.
* **Significance:** The results have the potential to be highly significant, as they provide a deeper theoretical understanding of GANs and could lead to the development of more effective training techniques. The connection to Bregman divergences, analogous to Wasserstein GANs, is particularly promising.



**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Novel and theoretically significant results.
* Bridges a gap between f-GANs and information geometry.
* Provides valuable insights into generator architecture and discriminator design.
* Offers a complete picture of the supervised learning aspect of f-GANs.
* Backed by initial experimental validation.

**Con:**

* Mathematically dense and potentially inaccessible to some readers.
* Limited scope of experiments.
* Potential for overselling the practical implications.




**Final Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept**.  Despite the clarity issues and limited experimental validation, the theoretical contribution of this paper is substantial and warrants acceptance to NIPS. I recommend that the authors focus on improving the clarity and accessibility of the paper, and consider expanding the experimental results to further demonstrate the practical impact of their findings.  Minor revisions addressing these points would further strengthen this already excellent work.
