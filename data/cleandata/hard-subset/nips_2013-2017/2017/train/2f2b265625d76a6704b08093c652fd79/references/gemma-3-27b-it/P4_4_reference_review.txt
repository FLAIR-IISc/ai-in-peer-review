This paper aims to provide an information geometric interpretation of f-GANs, extending the work of Nowozin et al. (2017). It establishes a connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences within the framework of deformed exponential families, and delves into the implications for generator and discriminator design in GANs, specifically relating to activation functions and link functions. The authors provide a theoretical analysis and supporting experiments.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound and mathematically rigorous treatment of the topic. The derivations appear correct, and the connection between f-divergences, Bregman divergences, and deformed exponential families is well-established. The results build logically from definitions and theorems, culminating in the “vig-f-GAN” identity (Corollary 8). However, the paper sometimes feels overly dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the mathematical concepts. The experimental section is relatively limited, but it does offer some support for the theoretical claims, though the effect sizes seem modest. The completeness of the work is moderate; while the theoretical framework is strong, the practical implications and exploration of the design space remain somewhat unexplored.  The authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., assumptions about invertibility of network layers), demonstrating carefulness.

**Clarity:** The writing is generally clear, but the technical depth makes it challenging for readers unfamiliar with information geometry. The paper is well-organized, following a logical progression from definitions to the main results and experimental validation. However, the numerous definitions and theorems without sufficient motivation can make it difficult to grasp the big picture. Providing more examples illustrating the concepts could greatly improve readability. The notation is consistent, but often complex.  The supplemental material is essential for fully understanding the proofs, indicating a potential for integrating some key steps into the main text for clarity.

**Originality:** The work is novel in establishing the connection between f-GANs and Bregman divergences using the framework of deformed exponential families. While the individual concepts (f-divergences, Bregman divergences, GANs) are well-established, their synthesis in this way represents a significant contribution. The theoretical results, particularly the vig-f-GAN identity, offer a fresh perspective on the underlying principles of GAN training. The exploration of activation functions and link functions through this lens also contributes to the novelty. The related work section is comprehensive, accurately situating the work within the broader literature.

**Significance:** The results have the potential to be significant for the GAN research community. By providing a geometric interpretation of f-GANs, the paper offers insights into the convergence properties and design principles of these models. The identification of key parameters and the exploration of activation functions could lead to improved GAN architectures and training strategies. The connection to proper composite losses is also a valuable contribution, offering a more principled approach to discriminator design. However, the practical impact depends on the extent to which these theoretical insights can be translated into substantial performance gains in real-world applications. The experimental results suggest potential improvements but are not conclusive.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound connection between f-GANs and information geometry.
*   Rigorous mathematical framework and derivations.
*   Potential for informing GAN architecture and training design.
*   Comprehensive related work section.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper is highly technical and may be inaccessible to a broad audience.
*   Experimental validation is limited and effect sizes are modest.
*   Practical implications and exploration of the design space require further investigation.
*   The density of information makes it difficult to quickly grasp the core contributions.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the understanding of GANs. While the technical depth and limited experimental validation may be drawbacks, the novelty and potential significance of the work warrant acceptance. I recommend accepting the paper with the suggestion that the authors consider adding more intuitive explanations and expanding the experimental section to provide more compelling evidence of the practical benefits of their approach.
