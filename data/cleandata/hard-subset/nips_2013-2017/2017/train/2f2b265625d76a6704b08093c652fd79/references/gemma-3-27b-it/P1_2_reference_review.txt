## Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

**Summary:**

This paper delves into the theoretical foundations of f-GANs, building upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2017). The authors bridge the gap between f-divergence-based GANs and information geometry by establishing a connection between f-GANs and Bregman divergences, specifically within the framework of deformed exponential families.  They demonstrate how this connection elucidates previously undefined parameters in the f-GAN objective and offers insights into the generator’s capacity to model complex distributions. Furthermore, they explore the implications of this geometric interpretation for generator design and discriminator loss functions, culminating in experimental results validating their theoretical findings.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The core contribution – establishing the link between f-divergences and Bregman divergences within the context of f-GANs – is significant. This provides a more complete and geometrically grounded understanding of the optimization process in GANs.  The authors successfully leverage information geometry to shed light on the underlying mechanisms.
* **Completeness:** The paper addresses several open questions raised in the original f-GAN work, particularly concerning the specification of parameters in the discriminator’s loss and the modeling capacity of the generator.  
* **Practical Implications:**  The analysis of generator architectures and the discussion of proper composite losses offer concrete guidance for improving GAN training and performance. The identification of desirable properties of activation functions is particularly valuable.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):** The paper is generally well-structured, with definitions clearly laid out and theorems presented logically. The organization allows readers to follow the progression of ideas.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments, though not extensive, provide evidence supporting the theoretical findings regarding activation function selection and link function tuning.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Mathematical Density:** The paper is *highly* mathematically dense. While rigor is appreciated, the constant stream of definitions, theorems, and equations can be overwhelming for readers not deeply familiar with information geometry and related fields. Increased intuition and explanation alongside the formalism would be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section feels somewhat limited. The results are presented primarily as tables, lacking detailed analysis of the observed trends.  More extensive experiments on diverse datasets and architectures would strengthen the claims. The ablation studies lack enough granularity.
* **Connection to Existing GAN Literature:** While the paper references key GAN works, it sometimes feels disconnected from the broader GAN research landscape.  A more thorough discussion of how these findings relate to, and potentially improve upon, recent advancements in GAN training (e.g., spectral normalization, self-attention GANs) would be valuable.
* **Not immediately actionable for practitioners:** While the theoretical insights are fascinating, directly translating them into practical improvements for a typical GAN practitioner might require significant expertise. A "cookbook" style section outlining specific recommendations for model design would be helpful.
* **Terminology:** The use of specialized terminology without sufficient explanation occasionally hinders understanding.



**Detailed Comments & Questions:**

* **Theorem 5:** The assumption regarding the subdifferential being upper-bounded requires further clarification.  How often does this assumption hold in practice within the context of GAN training?
* **Section 4 (Deep Architectures):** The statement about the compactness of the factorization seems significant. Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of this factorization in terms of memory usage and computational efficiency?
* **Section 5 (Proper Composite Losses):** The connection between proper composite losses and the GAN framework is valuable. However, a more intuitive explanation of why using a proper composite loss is advantageous would be helpful.
* **Experiments - Activation Functions:** Why were only ReLU, Softplus, and LSU compared? Exploring a wider range of activation functions, including more recent developments, could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
* **Experiments - Link Functions:**  The comparison of Matsushita entropy versus the standard link function is interesting.  Were any other link functions considered? What are the computational trade-offs associated with using Matsushita entropy?



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of f-GANs.
* The connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences provides a novel and insightful perspective on GAN training.
* The authors address previously open questions in the f-GAN literature.
* The experimental results, while limited, support the theoretical claims.
* The paper is well-written and logically organized (despite its density).

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's high mathematical density may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
* The experimental validation is somewhat limited.
* The connection to the broader GAN research landscape could be stronger.
* The practical implications of the theoretical findings are not fully explored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of f-GANs. While the mathematical density and limited scope of the experiments are drawbacks, the novelty and significance of the theoretical results outweigh these concerns. I believe this paper is a worthwhile addition to the NIPS proceedings, albeit with the caveat that it might be best suited for readers with a strong background in information geometry and related fields.

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, I suggest adding more intuitive explanations alongside the mathematical formalism, expanding the experimental evaluation, and providing a more comprehensive discussion of the connection to existing GAN literature.  Addressing the questions raised in the detailed comments would also be beneficial.
