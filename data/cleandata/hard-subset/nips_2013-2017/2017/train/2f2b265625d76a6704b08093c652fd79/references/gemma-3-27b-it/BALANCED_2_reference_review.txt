This paper proposes a novel connection between f-GANs and information geometry, establishing an identity relating the f-divergence formulation to Bregman divergences within the context of deformed exponential families. The authors build upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2016) to provide a more complete theoretical understanding of f-GANs, specifying parameters previously left arbitrary and analyzing the capabilities of deep generator architectures. The paper is well-motivated, attempting to bridge a gap between information-theoretic and geometric approaches to GANs.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The central identity (eq. 3) connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences is a significant theoretical result, offering a new perspective on the f-GAN framework. This provides a geometric interpretation of the learning process, linking it to existing work in information geometry.
* **Completeness:** The paper addresses limitations identified in the work of Nowozin et al. (2016), clarifying the parameters of the supervised game and offering a more comprehensive analysis.
* **Deep Generator Analysis:** The analysis of deep generator architectures and their ability to model escorts of deformed exponential families is insightful. The factorization property and the discussion of activation functions (Lemma 11) are valuable contributions.
* **Proper Composite Losses:** Connecting the framework to proper composite losses (Theorem 12) provides a practical and theoretically sound foundation for understanding the discriminator's role.
* **Experiments:** The experiments, while not extensive, offer preliminary evidence supporting the theoretical findings, exploring the impact of activation function choice and link function tuning.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Notational Density & Complexity:** The paper is densely written with significant mathematical notation. This can make it challenging for readers not already well-versed in information geometry and f-divergences to follow the arguments. A more gradual introduction of concepts and notation would improve readability.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental validation is somewhat limited. The results on MNIST and LSUN are promising but lack a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art GAN architectures and training techniques. The improvements demonstrated are not substantial.
* **Practical Impact is Unclear:** While theoretically elegant, the practical implications of the proposed framework remain somewhat unclear. The paper doesn't fully articulate how these insights can be readily applied to improve GAN training in real-world scenarios beyond the specific parameter tuning explored in the experiments.
* **Connection to Existing Geometric GANs:** The paper aims to connect f-GANs to geometric approaches like Wasserstein GANs, but this connection could be more thoroughly explored. A more detailed discussion of the similarities and differences between these approaches would be beneficial.
* **Overly Ambitious Scope:** The paper attempts to cover a large amount of theoretical ground, potentially sacrificing depth in certain areas. Some sections feel rushed and lack sufficient explanation.



**Detailed Comments:**

* **Introduction:** The introduction is good at motivating the problem but could benefit from a more concise summary of the main contributions.
* **Definitions (Section 2):**  The definitions are mathematically precise but could be more accessible to a broader audience. Consider adding intuitive explanations alongside the formal definitions.
* **Theorem 5:** The assumption of a bounded subdifferential selection is crucial, but its implications and practical relevance aren’t fully discussed.
* **Section 4 (Deep Architectures):** The discussion of the factorization property is interesting but somewhat abstract. Providing concrete examples of how this factorization manifests in specific network architectures would enhance understanding.
* **Section 5 (Proper Losses):** This section is a valuable addition, but the connection between the theoretical framework and practical loss function design could be further emphasized.
* **Section 6 (Experiments):**  The experiments are a weak point of the paper. More extensive evaluations with different datasets, architectures, and evaluation metrics are needed. Including a comparison to baseline GANs trained without the proposed insights is essential. The visual results in the supplementary material are not particularly compelling.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound connection between f-GANs and information geometry.
* The identity established in the paper is a significant contribution to the understanding of f-GANs.
* The analysis of deep generator architectures provides valuable insights into their capabilities.
* The paper addresses limitations of previous work in the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's mathematical complexity and density can hinder understanding.
* The experimental validation is limited and lacks a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
* The practical impact of the proposed framework is not fully clear.
* The connection to related work in geometric GANs could be strengthened.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable theoretical contribution to the field of GANs. However, the mathematical complexity, limited experimental validation, and somewhat unclear practical implications raise concerns. I believe the paper, in its current form, is **borderline reject**. 

**Recommendations for Improvement:**

* **Improve Clarity:** Revise the paper to improve clarity and readability, providing more intuitive explanations of complex concepts and notation.
* **Expand Experimental Evaluation:** Conduct a more thorough experimental evaluation, comparing the proposed framework to state-of-the-art GAN architectures and training techniques on a wider range of datasets.
* **Focus on Practical Implications:**  Elaborate on the practical implications of the theoretical findings, providing concrete guidance on how they can be used to improve GAN training in real-world applications.
* **Strengthen Connection to Related Work:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the relationship between f-GANs and other geometric approaches to GANs.
* **Consider Reducing Scope:**  Focus on a smaller subset of the theoretical results, exploring them in greater depth.
