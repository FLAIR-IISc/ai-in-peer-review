- Major contribution: establishes a novel identity linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences within deformed exponential families, giving a geometric interpretation of f-GANs.  
- Completes prior work by specifying the previously unspecified link function parameter in Nowozin et al.’s f-GAN formulation.  
- Analyzes deep generator capacity, showing generators can model escorts of deformed exponential families and proving a useful factorization property.  
- Frames the problem via proper composite losses, offering a practical tuning pathway.  
- Preliminary experiments indicate potential gains from activation and link-function design, but results are limited.  
- Paper is mathematically rigorous and novel but is dense and could be made more accessible with intuitive explanations and examples.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (MNIST, one LSUN class; DCGAN, MLP) and needs expansion to larger datasets and state-of-the-art architectures.  
- Link-function tuning (Matsushita entropy) shows modest, inconclusive improvements and requires broader validation.  
- Discussion of theoretical limitations (e.g., geometric convergence on complex real-world distributions) is brief and should be expanded.  
- Suggestion: provide a concrete example of a deformed exponential family to aid understanding.  
- Suggestion: make the practical implications of Theorem 3 and Corollary 8 explicit with a “big picture” explanation.  
- Suggestion: improve experimental reporting with hyperparameters, procedures, error bars/statistics, and comparisons to recent GAN variants.  
- Suggestion: further explore practical implications of the activation–escort connection for generator architecture design.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical advance, fills a literature gap, offers compelling geometric view, and shows promising experiments.  
- Against acceptance: steep mathematical prerequisites, insufficient experimental validation, inconclusive link-function benefits, and under-discussed limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on improving clarity and expanding experimental validation, especially demonstrating practical benefits of link-function tuning.
