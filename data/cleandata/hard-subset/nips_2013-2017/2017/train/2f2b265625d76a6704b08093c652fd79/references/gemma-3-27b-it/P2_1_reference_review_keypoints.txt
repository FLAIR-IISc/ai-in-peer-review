- Connects f-divergences and Bregman divergences in deformed exponential families; central identity (eq.3) is a key theoretical contribution.  
- Provides a detailed information-geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective.  
- Analyzes the supervised game, clarifying parameters and showing deep generators can model complex escorts.  
- Links discriminator behavior to proper composite losses, enriching understanding of discriminator roles.  
- Yields practical design insights (activation and link function choices) that can improve GAN performance.  
- Presentation is mostly clear and logically organized but densely mathematical.  
- Empirical results offer preliminary support but are limited in scope and show only modest uplift.  
- High mathematical density makes the paper hard to access for readers not versed in information geometry; needs more intuition and examples.  
- Experimental validation is narrow (few datasets/architectures); practical impact and implementation effort remain unclear.  
- Some definitions/theorems feel repetitive and could be more concise.  
- Connections to very recent GAN advances (e.g., spectral normalization, architectural improvements) are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Technically sound work, but gap remains between theoretical insights and demonstrable real-world gains.  
- Originality lies in the synthesis of known concepts into a novel connection between f-GANs and information geometry.  
- Potentially significant if insights translate to performance improvements, but currently of moderate significance as a theoretical contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical framework, novel connections, and actionable generator/discriminator insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, limited experiments, and incomplete linkage to recent GAN developments.  
- Overall recommendation: strong borderline accept contingent on improving clarity, expanding experiments, and discussing limitations/future work.
