

# Peer Review: "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

## Summary and Context

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by bridging the gap between f-GANs (a generalization of GANs to all f-divergences) and information geometry. The authors establish a variational identity (eq. 3) showing that the f-GAN objective can be expressed as a Bregman divergence plus a penalty term when distributions lie in deformed exponential families (χ-exponential families), a natural generalization of regular exponential families. This connects the supervised adversarial learning framework to the geometric perspective of information geometry, where Bregman divergences play a central role. The paper also completes the theoretical picture of the supervised game by showing that the discriminator's link function must be a proper composite loss, and provides insights into how deep neural networks can effectively model these deformed exponential families.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Novelty and Depth**: The core identity (eq. 3) is a substantial theoretical contribution that connects two important areas in machine learning: the f-divergence framework of GANs and information geometry. This connection has been missing in previous work on GANs, with the authors correctly noting that prior work (e.g., Nowozin et al.) "falls short of justifying or elucidating all parameters of the supervised game."

2. **Rigorous Treatment of Deformed Exponential Families**: The paper provides a careful treatment of χ-exponential families, which generalizes the exponential family framework. The authors correctly note that the KL divergence between distributions in the same exponential family equals a Bregman divergence (eq. 2), and they successfully extend this to the more general f-divergence setting.

3. **Insightful Analysis of Deep Networks**: The paper's analysis of how deep neural networks can factorize escorts (eq. 14) is particularly strong. The authors demonstrate that common activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfy conditions to model these escorts, providing a theoretical justification for why these activations work well in practice.

4. **Completion of the Supervised Game Picture**: The paper resolves an important gap in the f-GAN framework by showing that the link function in the discriminator is not arbitrary but must be a proper composite loss (Theorem 12). This is a significant theoretical contribution that clarifies the design of the discriminator.

## Weaknesses

1. **Modest Experimental Impact**: While the theoretical contributions are strong, the experimental results (Section 6) show only modest improvements over baselines. The best results with µ-ReLU activations are "essentially beaten" by ReLU for some settings, and the Matsushita link function only provides marginal gains over sigmoid. The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that these theoretical insights translate to significant practical improvements.

2. **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art**: The paper only compares to basic GAN and WGAN implementations. It lacks comparisons to more recent GAN variants (e.g., StyleGAN, BigGAN, or improved WGAN variants) that have achieved state-of-the-art results on standard benchmarks. Without these comparisons, it's difficult to assess the practical significance of the theoretical insights.

3. **Lack of Computational Cost Analysis**: The paper doesn't discuss the computational cost of implementing the theoretical insights (e.g., using the Matsushita link function or specialized activation functions). This is important for assessing practical applicability.

4. **Overstatement of Practical Impact**: The paper states that "experiments display that the tools we develop may help to improve further the state of the art," but the results don't strongly support this claim. The improvements are marginal and not consistently observed across different architectures and datasets.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of the f-GAN framework. The paper provides a rigorous connection between f-GANs and information geometry, which is a valuable theoretical insight for the field. The analysis of how deep networks can model deformed exponential families is particularly insightful and well-motivated.

However, the practical impact of these theoretical insights is limited by the modest experimental improvements and lack of comparison to more recent GAN approaches. While the theoretical contributions are significant, the paper would benefit from stronger empirical validation of the practical benefits of the proposed insights.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper makes a clear theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of GANs from an information geometric perspective. The theoretical results are sound and well-motivated, and the paper fills an important gap in the literature.

To strengthen the paper, I would recommend:
1. Adding comparisons to more recent GAN variants (e.g., StyleGAN, BigGAN) to better demonstrate practical impact.
2. Providing a more thorough discussion of the computational cost implications of the proposed approaches.
3. Clarifying the practical significance of the theoretical insights in the conclusion.

The paper addresses an important theoretical gap in the GAN literature and provides a valuable connection between two important areas in machine learning. While the experimental results don't demonstrate dramatic practical improvements, the theoretical contribution is substantial and would be valuable to the NIPS community.

The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs. It is clearly within the scope of NIPS as a theoretical contribution to machine learning.
