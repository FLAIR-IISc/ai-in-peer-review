This paper investigates the connection between f-GANs and information geometry, providing a theoretical framework to understand the geometric interpretation of the GAN objective. The authors establish an identity relating the f-divergence formulation of f-GANs to Bregman divergences in deformed exponential families, offering insights into the parameters of the supervised game and the modeling capacity of deep generators. The work is mathematically rigorous and attempts to bridge a gap between information-theoretic and geometric perspectives on GANs.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Depth:** The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution by establishing a link between f-divergences and Bregman divergences in the context of f-GANs. This connection provides a deeper understanding of the underlying geometric principles governing the GAN training process.
* **Completeness of the Supervised Game:**  The authors address a previously identified gap in the f-GAN framework by fully specifying the parameters of the supervised game, including the link function.
* **Generator Capacity Analysis:**  The analysis of deep generator architectures and their ability to model escorts of deformed exponential families is a valuable contribution, providing insights into the expressive power of common neural network architectures.
* **Proper Composite Loss Connection:** The connection drawn between the work and proper composite losses provides a robust foundation and suggests avenues for further analysis and improvement.
* **Experimental Validation:**  The experiments, while not extensive, provide some evidence supporting the theoretical claims, particularly regarding the impact of activation function choices and link function tuning.
* **Clear Mathematical Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written mathematically, with clear definitions and derivations of key results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section feels somewhat limited in scope. The results are primarily focused on MNIST and LSUN, and the exploration of different activation functions and link functions is not exhaustive.  A more comprehensive experimental evaluation, potentially including more complex datasets and architectures, would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Complexity & Accessibility:** The paper is very dense and relies on a substantial background in information geometry and statistical inference. This may limit its accessibility to a broader audience within the NIPS community. While theoretical depth is appreciated, greater effort could be made to explain the core ideas in a more intuitive manner.
* **Practical Implications Not Fully Explored:** While the theoretical framework is well-developed, the paper doesn’t fully articulate the practical implications of these findings for GAN practitioners. More discussion on how these insights can be leveraged to improve GAN training or architecture design would be beneficial.
* **Dependency on Reversible Networks:**  The analysis of generator capacity relies on the assumption of reversible networks, which is a strong constraint and not universally applicable to all deep generator architectures. The authors acknowledge this, but the implications of relaxing this assumption are not fully explored.
* **Potential for Redundancy:** Some of the mathematical derivations and proofs might be overly detailed for a conference paper and could be streamlined or moved to the supplementary material to improve readability.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically significant contribution to the understanding of f-GANs.
* The connection to information geometry provides a valuable new perspective on the GAN training process.
* The analysis of generator capacity and the identification of key parameters are insightful.
* The experimental results, while limited, provide some support for the theoretical claims.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The paper is highly theoretical and may not be accessible to all NIPS attendees.
* The experimental evaluation is limited in scope and doesn’t fully demonstrate the practical impact of the theoretical findings.
* The reliance on reversible networks may limit the generalizability of the results.
* The density of the mathematical presentation could hinder comprehension.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper with the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of GAN research.  However, the limited experimental validation and the high degree of mathematical complexity raise some concerns. I believe the paper is borderline, leaning slightly towards rejection.

**Recommendations:**

* **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include more diverse datasets, architectures, and hyperparameter settings to provide more compelling evidence for the theoretical claims.
* **Improve accessibility:** Add more intuitive explanations and examples to make the core ideas more accessible to a wider audience.
* **Discuss practical implications:** Clearly articulate how the theoretical insights can be leveraged to improve GAN training or architecture design.
* **Address the limitations of the reversible network assumption:** Explore the implications of relaxing this assumption and investigate alternative approaches.
* **Streamline the mathematical presentation:**  Consider moving some of the detailed derivations to the supplementary material to improve readability.
