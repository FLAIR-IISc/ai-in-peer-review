This paper provides a comprehensive and rigorous theoretical understanding of the f-GAN framework, building upon the work of Nowozin et al. The authors introduce a novel information-geometric identity that connects the f-GAN formulation to a geometric optimization problem, which sheds light on the convergence of the generator and discriminator in the parameter space. They also provide a complete proper loss picture of the supervised GAN game, which includes a characterization of the generators' convergence and a principled design of the activation functions in the generator.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to provide a clear and concise presentation of their results. The introduction is thorough, and the authors have done an excellent job of relating their work to previous research in the field. The technical content is sound, and the proofs are well-organized and easy to follow.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to bridge the gap between the information-theoretic and information-geometric aspects of the f-GAN framework. The authors have successfully shown how the f-GAN formulation can be captured in an information-geometric framework, which provides a more complete understanding of the supervised game.

The experiments are also well-designed and provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach. The authors have tested their theoretical contributions on two different architectures (DCGAN and MLP) and two different datasets (MNIST and LSUN), which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.

Overall, this paper is a significant contribution to the field of generative adversarial networks, and it provides a rigorous theoretical understanding of the f-GAN framework. The authors have successfully addressed the limitations of previous work and have provided a more complete picture of the supervised game.
