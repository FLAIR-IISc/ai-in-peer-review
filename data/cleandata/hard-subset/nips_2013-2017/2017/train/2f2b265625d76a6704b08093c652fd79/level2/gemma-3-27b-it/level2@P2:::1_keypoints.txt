- Extends Nowozin et al. (2017) with a geometric interpretation of f-GANs tied to deformed exponential families.  
- Shows that under certain conditions the f-GAN problem can be reformulated as a Bregman divergence optimization, giving convergence insights.  
- Provides mathematically rigorous derivations, notably the core identity (eq. 3) linking f-divergences and Bregman divergences.  
- Identifies the role of deformed exponential families and links this to the factorization capabilities of deep architectures.  
- Presents a sufficient condition on activation functions that has practical relevance for generator design.  
- Suggests concrete GAN design guidelines (activation and link function choices) with preliminary experimental support.  
- Addresses an unaddressed parameter in Nowozin et al., completing the supervised game picture.  
- Overall presentation is clear and accessible despite complex information-geometry material.  

- Experimental evaluation is limited to simple datasets (MNIST, LSUN tower) and basic architectures (DCGAN, MLP).  
- Activation function analysis is narrow (ReLU, Softplus, ELU) and the claim of ReLU being "weakly admissible" needs stronger justification.  
- Link function experiments are restricted to Matsushita entropy and lack broader exploration.  
- The reversibility assumption for the generator is a significant limitation and its applicability to common non-reversible architectures is unclear.  
- Connections to broader GAN literature (training instabilities, spectral normalization, self-attention, etc.) are underdeveloped.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, insightful info-geometry connection, practical design implications, fills a gap from Nowozin et al.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited experimental scope, restrictive assumptions (reversibility), and insufficient ties to broader GAN work.  

- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand experiments to complex datasets/architectures, discuss and mitigate reversibility limitations, strengthen literature connections, and better justify ReLU admissibility.
