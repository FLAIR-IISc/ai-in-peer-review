The paper "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell" provides a novel framework for understanding the f-GAN principle in the context of information geometry. The authors extend the f-GAN principle to all f-divergences and show that it can be equivalent to a geometric optimization problem. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their contributions.

The main contribution of the paper is the variational information-geometric f-GAN identity, which connects the f-GAN problem to a geometric optimization problem. The authors show that this identity holds for a broad class of distributions, namely deformed exponential families. They also provide a constructive proof of this result, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The paper also explores the implications of this result for deep learning. The authors show that standard deep generator architectures are powerful at modeling complex escorts of deformed exponential families. They also provide a sufficient condition on the activation function to guarantee the escort modeling, and show that this condition is satisfied by most popular activation functions.

The authors also complete the supervised game picture of f-GANs by providing a proper composite loss view of the f-GAN game. They show that the use of proper composite losses sheds further light on the connection between the value of the optimal discriminator and the density ratio between the generator and discriminator distributions.

The experimental results provided in the paper are also impressive. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several datasets, including MNIST and LSUN. They also show that their approach can be used to improve the state of the art in generative modeling.
