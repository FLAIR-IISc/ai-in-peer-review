- Introduces a novel information-geometric framework connecting the f-GAN principle to geometric optimization.  
- Extends the f-GAN principle to all f-divergences and frames it as an equivalent geometric problem.  
- Main contribution: a variational information-geometric f-GAN identity linking f-GANs to geometric optimization.  
- Shows the identity holds for deformed exponential families and provides a constructive proof.  
- Demonstrates standard deep generator architectures can model complex escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Provides a sufficient condition on activation functions to guarantee escort modeling, satisfied by most popular activations.  
- Completes the supervised game picture by giving a proper composite loss view of the f-GAN game.  
- Clarifies the relation between the optimal discriminator value and the density ratio via proper composite losses.  
- Experimental results on MNIST and LSUN are strong and claim improvements toward state of the art.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and concisely explains contributions.  
- Requires substantial background in information geometry and deep learning, which may limit accessibility.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and avenues for future research.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for NIPS 2017 due to significance and demonstrated effectiveness.
