- Main contribution: derives an identity linking the variational f-GAN objective to a Bregman divergence between true and generated distribution parameters (via KLχ divergences).
- Main contribution: shows deep invertible networks with "strongly admissible" activations can compactly represent escort densities of deformed exponential families, explaining generator expressiveness.
- Main contribution: frames the discriminator output activation through proper composite losses, clarifying its design as composition of divergence derivative and a link function.
- Strength: novel and significant theoretical advance unifying information-theoretic and information-geometric views of f-GANs, extending KL-Bregman duality.
- Strength: provides theoretical grounding for deep generator architectures and identifies admissible activation functions as design guidance.
- Strength: completes and extends Nowozin et al.’s f-GAN picture by giving geometric interpretation and formal discriminator treatment.
- Weakness: relies on strong invertibility assumptions for network layers (Theorem 9), limiting applicability to common non-invertible architectures like strided convs/pooling.
- Weakness: dense, theory-heavy presentation; needs more intuitive explanations of concepts like deformed exponential families for broader accessibility.
- Weakness: experiments are limitedly connected to theory (e.g., ReLU often matches "strongly admissible" activations); more analysis needed to reconcile theory and empirical findings.
