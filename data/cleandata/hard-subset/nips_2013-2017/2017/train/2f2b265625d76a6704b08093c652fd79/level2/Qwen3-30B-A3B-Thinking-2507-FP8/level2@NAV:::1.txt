

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing a variational information geometric identity that connects f-GANs to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families. The main claim is that f-GAN(P, escort(Q)) = D(θ‖ϑ) + Penalty(Q), where P and Q are distributions in deformed exponential families (a superset of exponential families). The authors demonstrate that standard deep architectures can effectively factorize these densities, providing theoretical grounding for the success of deep GANs.

The paper's strength lies in its elegant theoretical framework that unifies f-divergences with information geometry. The proofs of the key theorems (Theorems 3, 5, 6, 7, 9) are rigorous and well-constructed, building on existing work in information geometry. The connection to generator design through activation functions is particularly insightful, with Lemma 11 showing that popular activations (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfy the necessary conditions for factorization. The experimental results (Section 6) support these theoretical claims, demonstrating that principled activation function design and link function tuning can improve GAN performance on MNIST and LSUN.

The paper demonstrates strong field knowledge, correctly referencing foundational work on f-divergences (Nowozin et al.), information geometry (Amari), and GANs (Goodfellow et al.). The novelty is clear: extending the geometric interpretation from KL divergence to general f-divergences and connecting it to deep architecture design. The significance is high, as it provides a theoretical foundation for understanding why certain GAN architectures work well.

The main limitation is that the theoretical results assume P can be modeled as a deformed exponential family, which may not hold for complex real-world data distributions. While the authors acknowledge this limitation, more discussion on practical implications for complex applications would strengthen the paper. The experimental section could also provide more detailed analysis of the activation function variations.

Overall, this is a high-quality theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of GANs from an information geometric perspective. The paper meets all four NIPS criteria: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written, original, and significant. The theoretical framework provides valuable insights for both practitioners and researchers. I recommend acceptance.
