

This paper extends Nowozin et al.'s f-GAN framework [30] by establishing a variational information geometric identity that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences for a broad class of distributions. The authors introduce a novel identity (Corollary 8) showing that the f-GAN objective can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem, specifically relating to deformed exponential families that generalize regular exponential families. This theoretical foundation addresses a key gap in the original f-GAN work [30], which did not fully characterize the generator's behavior or provide a geometric interpretation of the training process.

The paper makes two main contributions. First, it proves a variational information geometric identity (Theorem 7 and Corollary 8) that shows how the f-GAN objective corresponds to a Bregman divergence between natural parameters of deformed exponential families. This identity reveals that solving the f-GAN game guarantees convergence in parameter space (for suitable families), addressing a key theoretical question about generator convergence. Second, the authors establish a sufficient condition on activation functions (Lemma 11) that ensures deep generator architectures can factorize escorts of deformed exponential families. They demonstrate that common activation functions like ReLU, ELU, and Softplus satisfy this condition, providing theoretical grounding for their widespread use in practice.

The paper's strengths are significant. The theoretical connection between information geometry and GANs is novel and well-motivated, filling a crucial gap in the literature. The identification of a sufficient condition for activation functions provides valuable theoretical insights that could guide future architectural designs. The experiments (Section 6) effectively validate the theoretical claims, showing that principled activation function design and link function tuning can improve GAN performance across different architectures (DCGAN and MLP) and datasets (MNIST and LSUN).

However, the paper has some weaknesses in presentation and clarity. The theoretical results are presented in a highly abstract form that makes them difficult to follow for readers unfamiliar with the technical details. While the mathematics is sound, the paper would benefit from more concrete examples or intuitive explanations of the key theorems (particularly Theorems 5-7). The connection between the experimental results in Section 6 and the theoretical claims is not sufficiently clear. For instance, it's not immediately evident how the observed performance improvements directly stem from the theoretical guarantees provided in Sections 3-5.

Specifically, on page 7, line 258, the phrase "For eq.(7), is means when C..." contains a grammatical error that disrupts the flow of the argument. Additionally, while the paper mentions that "the right-hand side of eq. (3) is the information geometric part," the precise relationship between the information-theoretic and information-geometric components could be clarified with more explicit examples.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs. The results provide a deeper mathematical foundation for the f-GAN framework and offer practical insights for generator design. The connection to information geometry is particularly significant as it bridges a gap between two important areas of machine learning research. While the presentation could be improved for clarity, the core theoretical contributions are strong and well-supported.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims. The experiments provide adequate validation of the theoretical insights. The originality is high, as the connection between f-divergences and information geometry for the GAN framework appears to be novel. The significance is substantial, as it provides a theoretical foundation for a widely used framework that could guide future research in GAN design and training.

Overall, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and strengthen the connection between theory and experiments. The paper addresses an important gap in the theoretical understanding of GANs and provides valuable insights that will benefit both researchers and practitioners.
