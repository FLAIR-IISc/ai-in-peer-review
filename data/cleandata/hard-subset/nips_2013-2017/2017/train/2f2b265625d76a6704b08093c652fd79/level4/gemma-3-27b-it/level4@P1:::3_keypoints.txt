- Paper studies whether GAN convergence implies parameter-space convergence, what the generator fits at convergence, and how discriminator output activations affect loss compositeness.
- Analysis is framed using deformed exponential distributions and information-theoretic tools.
- Main theoretical result: a variational generalization (f-GAN identity) linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between their parameters.
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential families.
- The theory suggests specific choices for generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation gf to improve GAN behavior.
- Authors briefly propose an alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.
- Major concern: many theorems assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family—need justification whether this holds in practice or can be relaxed.
- Questioned practical value of the parameter-based viewpoint: unclear gains, lack of implementation guidance, and no experimental comparison to distributional optimization.
- The implication that solving the GAN game yields parameter convergence (modulo small residual J(Q)) needs clearer motivation and realistic counterexamples where it fails.
- Important practical claim that gf (composition of f′ and link) matters for proper compositeness is not validated experimentally against prior heuristics.
- Experiments: μ-ReLU (theoretically favored) performs worse than baseline ReLU, contradicting expectations and needing explanation.
- Unclear whether common architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy the reversibility assumption; results for WGAN (outside f-divergence framework) are not compared.
- Reported experimental differences are within error bars, providing weak empirical support for theoretical claims.
- Minor request: provide more intuitive explanations of deformation, signature, and escort for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.
- In Theorem 8, need comparison between derived upper bounds for J(Q), implications for activation choices, and clarification why decreasing J(Q) with escort normalization Z is beneficial.
- Overall assessment: solid and novel theoretical contributions but limited current practical relevance due to strong assumptions and weak experimental validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel link between f-GANs, Bregman divergences, and deformed exponential families; sound theory; potential architectural design implications.
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on deformed exponential assumption, weak/ambiguous experiments, and lack of concrete practical use of the parameter-based identity.
