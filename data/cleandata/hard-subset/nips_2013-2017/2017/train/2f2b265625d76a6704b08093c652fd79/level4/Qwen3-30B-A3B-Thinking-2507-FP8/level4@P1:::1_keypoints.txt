- Extends f-GAN by deriving a variational information geometric identity linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families.  
- Theorem 7 & Corollary 8: f-GAN objective equals a Bregman divergence between natural parameters, implying generator convergence for suitable families.  
- Lemma 11: Provides a sufficient condition on activations for deep generators to factorize escorts; ReLU, ELU, and Softplus satisfy it.  
- Novel theoretical connection between information geometry and GANs fills an important gap in the literature.  
- Activation-function condition offers practical guidance for generator architecture design.  
- Experiments (Section 6) validate claims: tuning activations/link functions improves GAN performance on DCGAN/MLP and MNIST/LSUN.  
- Presentation is overly abstract and hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with the technical details.  
- Needs more concrete examples/intuitive explanations for key theorems (especially Theorems 5–7).  
- The link between experimental improvements and the theoretical guarantees is not made sufficiently clear.  
- Minor errors: grammatical mistake on page 7 line 258 ("is means when C...") disrupts flow.  
- Clarify the precise relationship between the information-theoretic and information-geometric parts (rhs of eq. (3)) with explicit examples.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and strengthen theory–experiment linkage.
