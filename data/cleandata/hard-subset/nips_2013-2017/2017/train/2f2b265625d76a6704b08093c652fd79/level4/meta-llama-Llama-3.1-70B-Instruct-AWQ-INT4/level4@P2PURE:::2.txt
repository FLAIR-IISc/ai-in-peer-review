I appreciate the opportunity to review this interesting paper, which proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm. The authors first demonstrate that f-GAN converges in parameter space by establishing a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and utilizing a Bregman divergence result. They also discuss a proper implementation of f-GAN and provide a factorization result of the deep neural network representation, highlighting the 1-1 mapping between the choice of activation functions and the chi (or f) function.

Although I did not verify every detail in the appendix, the proofs appear to be correct, with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to thoroughly review. I believe this paper is dense and contains numerous new results that could be of significant interest to the machine learning and information theory communities. The exposition of Theorem 4, which reveals the IG component of f-GAN, is particularly impressive. Therefore, I recommend accepting this paper.

However, I also think that this paper may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), such as the mapping of KL divergence to Bregman divergence for an exponential family and Fenchel duality. As deep learning engineers are likely to be the primary audience for a GAN paper, I suggest the following revisions to enhance clarity:

1. Consider relocating Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text, as it effectively illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the context of f-GAN.
2. Provide a clear statement at the beginning of the paper explaining the benefits of adopting an IG view. My understanding is that this perspective enables the discussion of optimization behavior using the geometry in parameter space, which is briefly mentioned on page 7 but could be introduced earlier.
3. I am unclear about how Section 5 relates to f-GAN. It appears that the deformed exponential family is used to explain the distribution represented by a deep neural network, rather than being a consequence of f-GAN optimization results. While I acknowledge the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi, I would appreciate further discussion on how this result can inform the design of the f-GAN game, such as selecting the optimal f-divergence or determining the most suitable f-GAN objective for a given activation function.
4. Could you clarify why φ_l in Theorem 6 can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics"? Equation (13) does not seem to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider relocating lines 257-269 to a more relevant section. Additionally, the utility theory part appears to be unrelated to the IG view, so it might be beneficial to remove this paragraph (or relegate it to the appendix) and allocate the space to explaining the main results.
6. I feel that the experiments are not directly related to the primary claims made in the paper. For example, the results from Section 5 could be discussed independently of the IG view of f-GAN, and the experiments in Section 4 are not closely related to the IG view. The inclusion of WGAN results is particularly distracting, as this paper primarily focuses on f-GANs.

In summary, while this paper presents many valuable results and derivations, I believe that the material could be organized more clearly. The current structure appears to be a condensed version of multiple papers, which may obscure the main claims and coherence of the paper. Although I support acceptance, I recommend editing the paper to enhance clarity and coherence.
