- Proposes an information geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, offering a novel perspective on convergence and implementation.  
- Shows f-GAN convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and using a Bregman divergence result.  
- Provides implementation guidance and a factorization result linking deep network activations to chi (or f) functions.  
- Appendix proofs largely appear correct, though Theorem 8 was not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Paper is dense with many new results of potential interest to machine learning and information theory communities.  
- Theorem 4’s exposition (explaining the IG aspect of f-GAN) is particularly strong.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance despite density.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers lacking a strong IT/IG background (e.g., KL–Bregman and Fenchel duality familiarity).  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from appendix to main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.  
- Recommend an early, clear statement of the benefits of the IG perspective (e.g., how geometry informs optimization).  
- Request clarification of the link between Section 5 and f-GAN: whether the deformed exponential family result follows from f-GAN optimization and how it informs choosing f/GAN objectives.  
- Ask for justification why phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6, since Eq. (13) doesn’t look like a deformed exponential family form.  
- Propose relocating or deleting the utility theory lines (257–269) as they seem unrelated and could free space for main results.  
- Point out experiments are not clearly tied to main claims; Section 5 results can be used independently of the IG view and WGAN discussion distracts from the f-GAN focus.  
- Overall recommendation: accept but revise for clearer organization, coherence, and accessibility.
