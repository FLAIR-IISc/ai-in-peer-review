- Paper studies GAN convergence vs parameter-space convergence, generator objective fit, and optimal discriminator output activation using deformed exponential distributions.
- Provides a variational generalization linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences, connecting information-theoretic and information-geometric views.
- Proves that, under a reversibility assumption, deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Argues that careful selection of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation (gf) may improve GAN performance and briefly interprets the GAN game via expected utility theory.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical contributions interesting but questions their practical applicability.
- Major concern: authors assume data and model distributions belong to the deformed exponential family and must justify this assumption or show whether key theorems hold without it.
- Requests clarification of practical advantages of the parameter-based perspective in Eq.(7), whether GANs can be implemented via this approach, and empirical comparisons between the two optimization views.
- Asks for a concrete scenario where f-divergence convergence does not imply parameter-space convergence, since the implication is nontrivial.
- Requests experimental comparison of the theoretically derived discriminator activation gf against the heuristic choice from Ref. [34].
- Notes a discrepancy: μ-ReLU (invertible) performs worse than baseline ReLU in Table 2 despite reversibility assumptions—needs explanation.
- Asks about reversibility of DCGAN architecture and comparison of the paper’s results to Wasserstein GANs, which use different divergence principles.
- Points out that performance differences in Tables 2 (center/right) are within margin of error, giving insufficient evidence for practical relevance.
- Minor: recommends adding intuitive explanations of deformed exponential densities, deformation/signature, and escorts for readers unfamiliar with these concepts.
- Minor/theoretical: requests comparison of the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8, discussion of implications for activation selection, and explanation why the bound decreases with escort normalization parameter Z.
- Overall conclusion: solid theoretical analysis but currently insufficient to significantly influence GAN practice.
