- Paper links the f‑GAN variational objective to an information‑geometric Bregman divergence for distributions in a deformed exponential family, deriving parameter‑space interpretations and architectural implications.
- Main theoretical contribution: the "variational information‑geometric f‑GAN identity" (Corollary 8) that bridges f‑divergences and geometry on parameter manifolds.
- Clarifies the discriminator output activation choice by connecting Nowozin et al.'s gf to proper composite losses and link functions.
- Theorem 9 and Lemma 11 give architectural insights: under a reversibility assumption, common activations (ELU, Softplus, µ‑ReLU variants) enable factorization of escort distributions.
- Central assumption is strong: results require both P and Q to lie in the same deformed exponential family, raising concerns about applicability to complex real‑world data like images.
- Practical utility of the geometric view is not demonstrated — no proposed optimization algorithm using the parameter‑space identity or experimental comparison to show improved stability or performance.
- Question about parameter vs distribution convergence: authors should clarify scenarios where Qθ → P does not imply θ → θ* and why their result is necessary.
- Experimental support is weak/inconclusive: activation comparisons show no clear winner, µ‑ReLU underperforms ReLU in some cases, and many differences lie within error bars.
- Unclear whether experimental architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy the reversibility assumption, and how the theory applies to WGAN since Wasserstein is not a Csiszár f‑divergence.
- Suggestion to provide more intuition and accessibility for information‑geometry concepts (deformed exponential families, escorts, signatures) for a broader audience.
- Request for better interpretation of the penalty term J(Q) and guidance on how its bounds inform activation/link function choices.
- Conclusion: elegant and original theoretical work that offers new perspectives, but it lacks sufficient justification of assumptions and empirical evidence to demonstrate practical benefits.
