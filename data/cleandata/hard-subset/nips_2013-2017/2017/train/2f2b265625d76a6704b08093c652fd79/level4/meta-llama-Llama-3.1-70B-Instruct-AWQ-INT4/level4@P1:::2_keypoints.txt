- Proposes an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, analyzing convergence in parameter space.  
- Establishes a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and leverages a Bregman divergence result for convergence proofs.  
- Presents recommendations for a proper implementation of f-GAN and a factorization result for deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions.  
- Paper is dense with many new results relevant to ML and information theory; Theorem 4 (IG view of f-GAN) is particularly strong.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept.  
- Major clarity concern: paper may be hard to follow for readers without strong IT/IG background.  
- Suggestion: move Figure 1 from appendix to main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.  
- Suggestion: add an early, explicit statement of benefits of the IG view (e.g., using geometry to discuss optimization behavior).  
- Suggestion: reorganize Section 5 to better connect deformed exponential family discussion to f-GAN optimization and the v–f–chi correspondence.  
- Request clarification: explain why φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6, since eq. (13) isn’t clearly a deformed exponential family form.  
- Suggestion: remove or move the utility theory section to the appendix, as it seems unrelated to the IG narrative.  
- Suggestion: reorganize experiments to align with main claims—use Section 5 results to motivate new activations, and separate or remove WGAN discussion to avoid confusion.
