- Develops an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, proving parameter-space convergence via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman divergence argument.  
- Presents practical implementation choices for f-GANs and a factorization of deep network representations, analyzing activation functions that map bijectively to the chi (or f) function.  
- Reviewer checked proofs broadly and found them correct except Theorem 8, which was not verified before the deadline.  
- Overall assessment: dense but novel work of interest to ML and information theory; strong exposition around Theorem 4; recommends acceptance.  
- Main readability concern: paper may be hard to follow for readers lacking IT/IG background (e.g., KL-to-Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality).  
- Suggestion: move Appendix Figure 1 (summary of IT–IG connections) into the main text to aid comprehension.  
- Suggestion: state early why the IG viewpoint is useful—e.g., for reasoning about optimization via parameter-space geometry—rather than delaying to page 7.  
- Suggestion: clarify how Section 5 connects to f-GAN training and how the deformed exponential family characterization guides choosing f-divergences or activations and affects convergence.  
- Suggestion: for Theorem 6, justify interpreting φ_l as “deep sufficient statistics” and show how equation (13) matches the deformed exponential family canonical form.  
- Suggestion: move the utility-theoretic discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix, since it appears tangential to the IG perspective.  
- Concern about experiments: empirical results feel loosely tied to theoretical claims; WGAN results are distracting and the brief WGAN note is easy to miss.  
- Overall recommendation: paper contains valuable results but needs streamlining—foreground IG motivation, tighten narrative, and better connect theoretical claims to empirical support.
