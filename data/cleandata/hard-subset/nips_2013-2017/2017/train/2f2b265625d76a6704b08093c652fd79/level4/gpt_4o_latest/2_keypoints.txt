- Paper presents an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, showing convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 correspondence between f-divergence and chi-divergence and a Bregman-divergence result.  
- Provides implementation guidance for f-GAN and a factorization result for deep network representations, linking activations to chi/f functions.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not reviewed in detail.  
- The paper is dense, introduces many novel results, and is likely of high interest to ML and information theory communities; reviewer strongly supports acceptance.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG foundations (e.g., KL↔Bregman, Fenchel duality); clarity and accessibility need improvement.  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 (IT–IG connections) from appendix to main text for better reader guidance.  
- Recommend stating early why the IG perspective is useful (e.g., leveraging parameter-space geometry to analyze optimization).  
- Connection between Section 5 and f-GAN is unclear; need to clarify how the deformed exponential family results inform f-GAN design or objective choice.  
- Question Theorem 6’s claim that φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq. (13) does not resemble a deformed exponential family form.  
- Propose relocating lines 257–269 and moving the utility theory discussion to the appendix, as it seems peripheral to the IG perspective.  
- Experiments feel tangential to core claims; Section 5 results could stand without IG framing, Section 4 isn’t tightly tied to IG, and inclusion of WGAN results is distracting or out of place.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve organization, exposition, and focus to avoid feeling like multiple papers compressed into one submission.
