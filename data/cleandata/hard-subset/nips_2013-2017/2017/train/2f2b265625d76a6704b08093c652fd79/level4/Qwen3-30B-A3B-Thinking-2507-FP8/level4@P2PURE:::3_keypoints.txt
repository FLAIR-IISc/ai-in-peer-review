- Paper poses important GAN questions: whether solving GAN objective implies parameter convergence, what generators fit upon convergence, and how discriminator output activations should be chosen for compositeness.  
- Develops an information-theoretic framework using deformed exponential families and derives a variational generalization linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences over parameters.  
- Shows a connection between distributional (information-theoretic) and parameter-space (information-geometric) notions of dissimilarity via the f-GAN identity (Eq. 7).  
- Proves that under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions and suggests choosing hidden/discriminator activations accordingly.  
- Offers an alternative expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.  
- Major concern: practical relevance and utility of the theoretical results are unclear in current form.  
- Key concern 1: strong assumption that data/model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family — authors must justify practicality or show results without this assumption.  
- Key concern 2: the parameter-space formulation (Eq. 7) lacks demonstrated practical advantages, implementations, or experiments comparing distributional vs parameter optimization; need examples where divergence convergence does not imply parameter convergence.  
- Key concern 3: the theoretically derived discriminator output activation g_f is valuable but lacks direct empirical comparison to prior heuristic choices.  
- Experimental issues: under reversibility the invertible μ-ReLU should outperform ReLU but does not in experiments — authors must explain the discrepancy.  
- Clarify whether common architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy reversibility and how analysis relates to methods like WGAN that do not use f-divergences.  
- Results in Table 2 are within error bars and provide insufficient evidence for practical impact; stronger experimental validation required.  
- Minor: deformed exponential concepts (deformation, signature, escort) need intuitive explanations for ML audience.  
- Minor: Theorem 8’s upper bounds for J(Q) need comparative discussion and concrete implications for activation selection; explain why smaller escort normalization Z helps and how to use it.  
- Overall conclusion: theoretical contributions are interesting but need stronger assumptions justification, experimental validation of claims, and clearer practical utility for GAN practitioners.
