- Proposes an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, analyzing convergence in parameter space.
- Establishes a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence using a Bregman divergence result.
- Describes a proper implementation of f-GAN and gives a factorization result for deep neural network representations.
- Studies the relationship between activation functions and chi (or f) functions.
- Theorem 4 (IG part for f-GAN) is a particularly strong and impressive result.
- Paper is dense and could be difficult for readers unfamiliar with information theory or information geometry.
- Recommend moving Figure 1 into the main text because it clarifies IT–IG connections for f-GANs.
- Suggest stating upfront the practical benefits of the IG view, e.g., how parameter-space geometry informs optimization behavior.
- Ask for clearer connection between Section 5 and f-GANs, explaining how deformed exponential family results guide f-GAN design or divergence choice.
- Request justification for viewing phi_l in Theorem 6 as “deep sufficient statistics,” since (13) does not obviously match a deformed exponential family form.
- Propose relocating or removing the utility-theory lines (257–269) as they are not central to the IG perspective.
- Experiments appear only tangentially related to the paper’s main claims; discuss Section 5 results without depending on the IG framing.
- Inclusion of WGAN results is potentially distracting since the paper focuses on f-GANs.
- Strengths: novel IG view of f-GANs, the f↔chi mapping, implementation/factorization results, and activation–chi analyses.
- Weaknesses: high density/low clarity, unclear Section 5 link to f-GAN, experiments not tightly connected to main claims, and distracting WGAN content.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to improve clarity, coherence, and stronger linkage between theoretical results and experiments.
