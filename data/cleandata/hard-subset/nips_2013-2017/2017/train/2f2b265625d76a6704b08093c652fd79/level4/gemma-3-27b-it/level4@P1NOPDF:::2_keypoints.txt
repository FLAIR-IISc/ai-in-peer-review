- Presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN and shows convergence in parameter space via the f-divergence ↔ chi-divergence mapping and Bregman divergence results.
- Gives a principled implementation and a factorization result linking deep network representations to activation choices through a one-to-one mapping to the chi (or f) function.
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not fully checked by the reviewer.
- Theorem 4’s exposition strongly clarifies the IG foundation of f-GAN; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Paper may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with information theory/geometry, KL-to-Bregman mapping, and Fenchel duality.
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to better illustrate IG–information theory relationships.
- Recommend explicitly stating the benefits of the IG view earlier (e.g., using parameter-space geometry to analyze optimization).
- Connection between Section 5 and f-GAN is unclear; need to explain how the one-to-one mapping guides f-GAN design or choice of f-divergence given an activation.
- Question the designation of phi_l in Theorem 6 as “deep sufficient statistics,” since Equation (13) doesn’t follow deformed exponential family form.
- Propose relocating the utility theory discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it feels tangential.
- Experiments feel disconnected from the main theoretical claims; some results depend only on individual sections and the WGAN experiments may distract from the f-GAN focus.
- Overall the paper is insightful but disjointed—supports acceptance but needs editing for clarity and coherence.
