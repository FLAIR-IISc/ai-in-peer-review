This paper explores the f-GAN principle through the lens of f-divergences, providing a generalized framework for understanding the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families. By leveraging the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which offers a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Furthermore, they derive a sufficient condition for activation functions to yield factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical contribution, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and shows promise for practical applications. However, the presentation of the main theorems in overly general forms hinders their clarity and accessibility.

A notable area for improvement lies in Section 6, where the experimental observations and their connections to the theoretical results are not clearly explained. Elucidating these relationships would significantly enhance the paper's overall coherence and impact.

Additionally, there is a grammatical error on page 7, line 258, in the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C …". This sentence requires rephrasing to convey its intended meaning effectively.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting and potentially impactful contribution to the field, addressing these issues would greatly improve its readability and overall quality.
