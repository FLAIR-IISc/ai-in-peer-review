Thank you for an engaging read.
This paper advances an information-geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN algorithm. It first establishes convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one correspondence between f-divergence and chi-divergence together with a Bregman divergence argument. It then discusses a proper implementation of f-GAN. Finally, the main text provides a factorization result for deep neural network representations and analyzes activation functions that are in one-to-one correspondence with the chi (or f) function. I did not verify every detail in the appendix, but the proofs appear sound (except for Thm. 8, which I did not have time to read before the deadline).
I find the paper dense and containing many novel results of potential interest to both machine learning and information theory. I am particularly impressed by the exposition of Thm. 4, which articulates the IG aspect of f-GAN. Overall, I view this as a clear accept.
That said, the paper may be difficult to follow for readers less familiar with the links between IT and IG (e.g., the basic mapping of KL for exponential families to Bregman divergence, and the Fenchel duality background). Many deep learning practitioners—likely the primary audience for a GAN paper—fit this profile. The following suggestions might improve clarity:
1. I like Fig. 1 in the appendix summarizing IT–IG connections in the f-GAN context; consider moving it to the main text.
2. It would help to state early why the IG viewpoint is beneficial. My understanding is that parameter-space geometry informs optimization behavior. You mention this on page 7, which felt a bit late.
3. I am unsure how Section 5 ties back to f-GAN. It seems to use the deformed exponential family to characterize distributions representable by deep nets, rather than deriving from f-GAN optimization. I accept that v, f, and chi are in one-to-one correspondence, but the paper does not explain how this informs the f-GAN game—for instance, which f-divergence to choose, or, given an activation, which f-GAN objective is preferable in terms of convergence.
4. For Thm. 6, why can phi_l be regarded as “deep sufficient statistics”? Equation (13) does not seem to be in deformed exponential family form.
5. As noted, consider relocating lines 257–269. The utility theory paragraph appears tangential to the IG perspective; removing it from the main text (and keeping it in the appendix) could free space to explain the main results.
6. The experiments do not seem tightly connected to the central claims. For (A), Section 5 alone suffices—you do not need the IG view to propose the new activations. For (B), Section 4 is adequate and is not closely tied to the IG view either. The WGAN results are distracting and potentially confusing in a paper focused on f-GANs; I had to search for the brief mention on lines 227–228.
In summary, while the paper delivers many useful results and dense derivations, the organization is not crystal clear; it reads like multiple papers compressed into an 8-page NIPS submission. I support acceptance, but recommend edits to make the claims sharper and more coherent.
