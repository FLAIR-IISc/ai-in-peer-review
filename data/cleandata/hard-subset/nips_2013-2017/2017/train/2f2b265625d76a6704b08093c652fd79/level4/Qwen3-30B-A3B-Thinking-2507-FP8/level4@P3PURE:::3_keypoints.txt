- Paper studies GAN questions in the deformed exponential framework, linking variational f-divergences to parameter-space Bregman divergences, showing factorization under a reversibility assumption and suggesting activation-function choices matter.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on the assumption that data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family; authors must justify this or generalize results.  
- Major concern: unclear practical utility of the information-geometric f-GAN identity (Eq. 7); authors do not show how to implement parameter-space optimization or give scenarios where f-divergence convergence does not imply parameter convergence.  
- Major concern: lack of direct experimental comparison between the theoretically derived discriminator output activation g_f and the heuristic used in prior work (Ref. [34]).  
- Major concern: contradiction in Table 2 where theoretically superior invertible µ-ReLU performs worse than baseline ReLU; authors must explain, state whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility, and compare to WGAN; reported differences are within statistical error.  
- Minor concern: deformed exponential concepts ("deformation," "signature," "escort") are unfamiliar and need intuitive explanations for ML readers.  
- Minor concern: Theorem 8 gives multiple upper bounds for J(Q) without comparing them or clarifying implications for activation selection and the role/benefit of the escort normalization parameter Z.  
- Conclusion: mathematically interesting but presently limited practical value due to restrictive assumptions, lack of empirical validation, unresolved experimental contradictions, and unclear applicability to practitioners.
