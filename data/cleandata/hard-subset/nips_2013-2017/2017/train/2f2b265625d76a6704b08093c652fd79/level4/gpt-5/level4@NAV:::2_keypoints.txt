- Advances an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, proving parameter-space convergence via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence plus a Bregman divergence argument.  
- Proposes a proper implementation of f-GAN.  
- Derives a factorization result for deep network representations and analyzes activation functions that correspond one-to-one with the chi (or f) function.  
- Appendix proofs largely appear sound, though Theorem 8 was not checked by the reviewer.  
- Paper is dense, contains many novel results of interest to ML and information theory; Theorem 4 exposition is particularly strong; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with IT–IG links (e.g., KL–Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality).  
- Suggest moving Fig. 1 (IT–IG summary) from appendix to main text for clarity.  
- Recommend stating early why the IG viewpoint is beneficial (e.g., how parameter-space geometry informs optimization), since this appears late in the text.  
- Section 5’s connection to f-GAN is unclear: it characterizes representable distributions via deformed exponential families but doesn’t explain implications for f-GAN objective or divergence choice.  
- For Theorem 6, question the claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Equation (13) doesn’t appear in deformed exponential family form.  
- Recommend removing or relocating the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it seems tangential.  
- Experiments are not tightly tied to the central claims: new activations follow from Section 5 without the IG view, Section 4 suffices for other claims, and WGAN results distract from an f-GAN-focused paper.  
- Overall organization feels like multiple papers compressed into one; supports acceptance but advises edits to sharpen and unify claims.
