This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, offering a novel perspective on the subject. The authors first demonstrate the convergence of f-GAN in parameter space by leveraging the 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, as well as a Bregman divergence result. They also discuss a proper implementation of f-GAN. Furthermore, the main text presents a factorization result for deep neural network representations and explores the choice of activation functions, which have a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function. Although I did not thoroughly verify every detail in the appendix, the proofs (with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to review prior to submission) appear to be correct.

I believe this paper is dense and contains numerous new results that could be of significant interest to the machine learning and information theory communities. The exposition of Theorem 4, in particular, effectively conveys the IG aspect of the story for f-GAN. As a result, I think this paper is a clear accept.

However, I also believe that this paper may be challenging to understand for those without a strong background in the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), such as the mapping of KL divergence to Bregman divergence for an exponential family and the Fenchel duality concept. Many deep learning engineers may fall into this category, and as the primary audience for a GAN paper, they may struggle to follow the authors' arguments. To address this concern, I offer the following suggestions to improve the paper's clarity:

1. I appreciate Figure 1 in the appendix, which illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the context of f-GAN. Consider relocating it to the main text to provide a clearer introduction to these concepts.
2. It would be helpful to include a clear statement at the beginning of the paper explaining why considering the IG view is beneficial. My understanding is that the geometry in the parameter space can be used to discuss the behavior of optimization, which is briefly mentioned on page 7, but could be introduced earlier.
3. I am unsure how Section 5 connects to f-GAN. It appears that the authors use the deformed exponential family to explain the distribution that a deep neural network can represent, but this does not seem to be a direct consequence of the f-GAN optimization results. While I acknowledge the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi, I would like to see a more explicit explanation of how this result can inform the design of the f-GAN game, such as which f-divergence to choose or how to select an f-GAN objective given an activation function.
4. I do not understand why Theorem 6's phi_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics." Equation (13) does not seem to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider relocating lines 257-269 to a different section or removing them altogether. The utility theory part appears unrelated to the IG view, so it may be beneficial to delete the paragraph (or relegate it to the appendix) to free up space for explaining the main results.
6. I feel that the experiments are not directly related to the main points claimed in the paper. For example, the results from Section 5 could be discussed independently of the IG view of f-GAN, and the experiments in Section 4 seem only tangentially related to the IG view. The inclusion of WGAN results may be distracting and confusing, as this paper primarily focuses on f-GANs. I spent some time searching for the sentence (lines 227-228) mentioning WGAN.

In summary, while this paper presents many useful results and dense derivations, I believe the material could be better organized to convey the authors' claims more clearly and coherently. The paper appears to be a condensed version of multiple papers, which may have contributed to the lack of clarity. While I support the paper's acceptance, I think it would benefit from editing to improve the presentation and flow of the main ideas.
