

This paper generalizes the f-GAN framework to f-divergences using the chi-logarithm transformation, establishing a variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity that interprets the f-GAN objective. The authors further derive a sufficient condition on activation functions to achieve factorized generative distributions. The main theoretical contribution—the vig-f-GAN identity—is mathematically novel and holds significant potential for advancing f-GAN theory and applications.  

However, the presentation of key results impedes their accessibility. Theorems are stated in highly abstract terms without sufficient motivation or concrete examples, making their practical significance difficult to grasp. For instance, Theorem 1 (Section 4) introduces the vig-f-GAN identity through general functional-analytic constructs but fails to connect it to familiar concepts in GAN optimization or information geometry. A brief discussion of how this identity simplifies or extends existing results (e.g., the standard f-GAN duality) would greatly enhance clarity. Similarly, the sufficient condition for factorized generative distributions (Theorem 2) is technically sound but lacks intuitive justification—readers are left to infer why this condition *matters* for model design.  

Section 6’s experimental analysis compounds these interpretability issues. The authors report performance gains on MNIST and CIFAR-10 but do not explicitly link these results to the theoretical claims. For example:  
- The observed improvements in FID scores (Table 1) are attributed to "better divergence approximation," yet the paper does not demonstrate how the vig-f-GAN identity *directly* enables this.  
- The ablation study (Figure 2) tests activation functions but omits discussion of whether they satisfy the sufficient condition from Theorem 2. Without this connection, the experiment fails to validate the core theoretical contribution.  
The experiments thus feel disconnected from the main theorems, weakening the paper’s narrative.  

Several minor issues also affect readability:  
- On p.7, line 258: "For eq.(7), is means when C …" contains a grammatical error. It should read: "For Eq. (7), it means when C …"  
- The term "chi-logarithm" (p.4) requires a brief definition or reference to avoid confusion with standard logarithmic functions.  
- Section 5’s discussion of "information geometric perspective" is vague; clarifying how this perspective informs the choice of activation functions would strengthen the argument.  

Despite these concerns, the paper addresses a meaningful gap in f-GAN theory. The vig-f-GAN identity is a valuable theoretical tool, and the factorization condition could guide future architectures. To maximize impact, the authors should:  
1. **Reframe theorems** with concrete examples (e.g., "For the KL divergence, the identity reduces to X, which aligns with existing work...").  
2. **Explicitly bridge theory and experiments** by showing how the identity or factorization condition *causes* observed improvements (e.g., "The activation function in Figure 2 satisfies Theorem 2, leading to faster convergence in Table 1").  
3. **Trim excessive generality**—the proofs are rigorous but overly abstract. A middle ground between generality and accessibility would serve the community better.  

In summary, this work offers a promising theoretical extension to f-GANs but requires clearer exposition to convey its value. The core ideas are sound and potentially influential, yet the current presentation obscures their significance. With revisions focusing on interpretability and experimental-theoretical alignment, this paper could make a strong contribution. I recommend *major revision* to address these points.
