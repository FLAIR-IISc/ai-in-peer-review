- Presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN and establishes convergence in parameter space via a bijection between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman divergence result.  
- Provides practical implementation details and a factorization result linking deep network representations and activation choices to chi/f functions.  
- Proofs generally appear sound, though Theorem 8 was not examined closely by the reviewer.  
- Contains several novel results with potential impact on machine learning and information theory; recommendation: accept.  
- Theorem 4 is clearly and elegantly presented, effectively showing the IG foundation of f-GAN.  
- The paper is dense and technical, likely posing a barrier to readers without IG/IT background (e.g., many deep learning practitioners).  
- Suggest moving Appendix Figure 1 (IT–IG connections) into the main text to aid reader comprehension.  
- Recommend stating the motivation for the IG perspective earlier, clarifying benefits like using parameter-space geometry to analyze optimization.  
- Connection between Section 5 (deformed exponential families / representable distributions) and the f-GAN framework is unclear and not shown to inform f-GAN design or divergence/activation choices.  
- The 1–1 correspondence between v, f, and chi is acknowledged, but the paper doesn't explain how to choose f-divergences or objectives given activations to improve convergence.  
- The claim that phi_l in Theorem 6 are “deep sufficient statistics” needs more justification, as Eq. (13) doesn’t clearly match a deformed exponential family structure.  
- Lines 257–269 on utility theory feel tangential and should be moved (e.g., to the appendix) to focus the main text.  
- Experiments are weakly connected to central claims: activation-function applications follow from Section 5 alone, and other experiments rely mainly on Section 4; inclusion of WGAN results is distracting.  
- Overall the material feels disjointed—like multiple studies compressed together—and would benefit from editing to improve clarity, coherence, and focus.
