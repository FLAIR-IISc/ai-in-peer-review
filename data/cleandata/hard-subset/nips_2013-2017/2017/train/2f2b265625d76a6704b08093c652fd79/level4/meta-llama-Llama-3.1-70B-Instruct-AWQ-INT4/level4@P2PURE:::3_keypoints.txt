- Paper studies whether solving GAN objectives implies parameter convergence, what the generator fits at convergence, and how discriminator output activation affects loss compositeness.
- Analysis is framed using deformed exponential distributions and derives theoretical results within that family.
- Main theoretical contribution: a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between distribution parameters (information-theoretic ↔ information-geometric connection).
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generators can be represented as escorts of deformed exponential distributions, suggesting activation choices matter.
- Authors propose that choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation carefully could improve GAN performance; they also mention an expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical results interesting but questions their practical relevance and usefulness in current form.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model lie in the deformed exponential family—request for justification and whether analogous results hold without this assumption.
- Question on the value of the parameter-space f-GAN identity: what practical gains, can GANs be optimized directly in parameter space, and empirical comparison between approaches are missing.
- Reviewer asks for examples where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply parameter-space convergence, since implication is asserted but not shown to be non-obvious.
- Practical implication about proper compositeness of discriminator output activation gf is interesting; reviewer requests experimental comparison with the heuristic gf from Ref.[34].
- Empirical results puzzling: μ-ReLU (theoretically favored) performs worse than ReLU in GAN MLP experiments—request explanation and whether reversibility holds for DCGAN.
- Reviewer asks how results extend or compare to Wasserstein GANs, which are not f-divergence based, and notes many experimental differences lie within error bars.
- Minor request: provide more intuition for deformations/signatures and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.
- Theorem 8: reviewer requests comparisons between the derived upper bounds for J(Q), implications for activation choice, and clarification of the role of the escort normalization parameter Z.
- Overall judgement: theoretical insights are interesting but currently insufficiently convincing or useful for GAN practitioners.
