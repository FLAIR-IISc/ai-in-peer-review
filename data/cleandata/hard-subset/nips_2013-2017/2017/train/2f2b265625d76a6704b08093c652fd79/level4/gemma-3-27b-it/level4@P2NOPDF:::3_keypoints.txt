- Paper tackles important questions about GAN convergence, generator representation, and discriminator activation functions using deformed exponential distributions.
- Presents theoretical results: variational generalization linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences, connecting information theory and geometry.
- Shows deep generative networks can be factored as “escorts” of deformed exponential distributions under a reversibility assumption.
- Argues careful choice of generator and discriminator activations could improve GAN performance and offers an expected utility interpretation of GANs.
- Major concern: strong assumption that data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family needs justification and prevalence in practice.
- Request to show whether similar results hold without the deformed exponential assumption, especially for Theorems 4 and 6.
- Key contribution Eq. 7 (information-geometric f-GAN identity) is interesting but the practical benefits and applicability of the parameter-based perspective are unclear.
- Wants experiments comparing optimization via f-divergence minimization versus parameter-based optimization to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Critiques overlooking the right-hand side of Eq. 7 and asks for scenarios where minimizing f-divergence does not imply parameter-space convergence.
- Praises theoretical result on discriminator output activation compositeness but requests direct experimental comparison with the original GAN activation heuristic.
- Questions the reversibility assumption for deep networks and whether common architectures like DCGAN satisfy it.
- Experimental inconsistency: μ-ReLU (theoretically superior invertible activation) performs worse than ReLU in Table 2 (left), requiring explanation.
- Notes no statistically significant differences in Tables 2 (center/right) between activation and link functions, undermining practical relevance.
- Requests clearer, more intuitive explanations of deformed-exponential concepts like “deformation/signature” and “escort” for broader accessibility.
- Asks for contextualization of Theorem 8’s upper bounds on J(Q), comparisons between bounds, and implications for activation selection.
- Seeks clarification on why decreasing the upper bound on J(Q) with escort normalization Z is beneficial and how to leverage this in practice.
- Concludes that while the theory is interesting, practical impact is limited without stronger experimental validation and clarification of key assumptions.
