This paper investigates the theoretical underpinnings of f-GANs, extending the GAN principle to f-divergences as originally proposed by Nowozin et al. (2017). The authors address a key gap in that earlier work – a lack of clarity regarding the generator’s convergence properties and a connection to information geometry. They achieve this by introducing a variational generalization of a relationship between KL divergence and Bregman divergence, applicable to a broad class of distributions termed “deformed exponential families.” This leads to the “vig-f-GAN” identity, providing a geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective. Furthermore, the paper demonstrates that standard deep architectures can efficiently model these deformed exponential families under a reasonable condition on activation functions, highlighting a potential justification for their empirical success.

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work in information geometry (Amari, 2016) and GANs (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Arjovsky et al., 2017). The connection made between f-divergences and Bregman divergences is a significant contribution, potentially bridging the gap between purely information-theoretic GAN analyses and those focused on geometric distortions (e.g., Wasserstein GANs). The analysis of deep architectures in the context of deformed exponential families is also a valuable insight, potentially explaining their representational power.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Novelty:** The vig-f-GAN identity is a novel and potentially insightful result, providing a more complete theoretical understanding of f-GANs.
*   **Bridging Information Geometry and GANs:** The paper successfully connects concepts from information geometry with the practical aspects of GAN training, offering a potentially unifying framework.
*   **Justification for Deep Architectures:** The analysis provides a theoretical justification for why deep neural networks are well-suited for modeling distributions within the f-GAN framework.
*   **Completeness:** The authors address a specific shortcoming of Nowozin et al. (2017) and offer a more comprehensive picture of the supervised game.
*   **Experimental Validation:** While not extensive, the experiments provide initial evidence supporting the theoretical findings, particularly regarding activation functions and link function tuning.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity and Accessibility:** The paper is densely written and relies on significant mathematical notation. The core ideas, while conceptually strong, are often obscured by the generality of the presentation. While mathematical rigor is appreciated, increased intuition and concrete examples would significantly enhance understanding.
*   **Experimental Connection:** The connection between the theoretical results and the experimental setup is not always clear. It's difficult to directly see how the theoretical findings translate into the specific experimental choices and observations. More detailed explanations of how the experiments validate the theory would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental evaluation is relatively limited in scope. Expanding the experiments to a wider range of datasets and architectures would strengthen the empirical support for the theoretical claims.
*   **Minor Grammatical Issues:** As noted in the draft review, a few minor grammatical errors detract from the overall readability (e.g., “is means” on p.7).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the understanding of f-GANs.
*   The connection between information geometry and GANs is a significant advancement in the field.
*   The analysis offers a potential explanation for the empirical success of deep architectures in generative modeling.
*   The experimental results, while limited, provide some initial validation of the theoretical findings.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper’s density and mathematical complexity may make it inaccessible to a broad audience.
*   The connection between the theoretical results and the experiments is not always clear.
*   The limited scope of the experiments leaves some questions unanswered.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of GANs. While the density and complexity of the presentation pose a challenge, the core ideas are novel and potentially impactful. The connection between information geometry and GANs is a significant advancement, and the analysis provides a plausible explanation for the effectiveness of deep architectures. I believe that, with revisions addressing the clarity and experimental validation concerns, this paper would be a strong addition to NIPS. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, focusing on improving clarity and expanding the connection between the theory and experiments. Specifically, the authors should consider adding more intuitive explanations of the key concepts and providing more detailed explanations of how the experiments validate the theoretical findings.
