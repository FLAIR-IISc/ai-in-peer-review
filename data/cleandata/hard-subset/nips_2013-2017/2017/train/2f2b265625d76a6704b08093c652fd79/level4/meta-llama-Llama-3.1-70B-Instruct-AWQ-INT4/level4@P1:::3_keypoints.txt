- Paper asks important GAN questions: parameter-space convergence, what generator fits at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice for compositeness.
- Authors frame the questions using deformed exponential distributions and derive several theoretical results.
- Present a variational generalization linking f-divergence between distributions to a Bregman divergence between their parameters.
- This theorem connects information-theoretic distribution dissimilarity to information-geometric parameter dissimilarity.
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generative networks factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Theory suggests careful hidden activations in the generator and proper discriminator output activations could improve GANs.
- Also offers a brief alternative interpretation of the GAN game using expected utility theory.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family; this assumption needs justification and discussion of practicality.
- Authors should clarify whether similar results can be derived without the deformed exponential-family assumption.
- Concern about Eq.(11): need to explain the practical gains of the parameter-based f-GAN identity and whether GANs can be implemented via parameter-based optimization.
- Request for experiments comparing distribution-based vs parameter-based optimization and discussion of benefits.
- Experimental results are underwhelming; activation/link-function comparisons mostly fall within error bars.
- Authors must explain why theoretical predictions do not appear in practice and provide stronger empirical support.
- Minor concern: paper lacks intuition and examples for deformed exponential densities, deformations, and escorts for a general ML audience.
- Need clearer explanation of implications and comparisons of the provided upper bounds for J(Q).
- Overall assessment: theoretical insights are interesting but currently lack practical relevance for GAN practitioners.
- Recommendation: revise to justify assumptions, improve empirical validation, add intuition/examples, and clarify practical implications.
