- Paper addresses interesting GAN questions: parameter convergence, what generator fits at convergence, and choosing discriminator output activation for proper compositeness.  
- Main theoretical contribution: variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters (information-theoretic ↔ information-geometric).  
- Shows under a reversibility assumption deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Derives that choosing the discriminator output activation gf influences proper compositeness of the GAN loss.  
- Mentions an alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.  
- Major concern: many results rely on assuming data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family—practical validity of this assumption is unclear.  
- Question whether key theorems (e.g., Theorems 4 and 6) can be derived without the deformed-exponential-family assumption.  
- Requests clarification and justification of benefits of the parameter-based f-GAN identity and whether GANs can be practically optimized in parameter space.  
- Asks for experimental scenarios showing distributional convergence does not imply parameter convergence and empirical comparisons of the two optimization viewpoints.  
- Experimental concern: μ-ReLU (theoretically preferred) performs worse than baseline ReLU in GAN MLP, contradicting expectations from the reversibility assumption.  
- Wants clarification on whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility and comparisons against Wasserstein GAN (theory may not apply to WGAN).  
- Notes reported differences in activation/link function experiments are within error bars and insufficient to support practical relevance.  
- Requests more intuition/explanation of deformations, signatures, and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.  
- Asks for clarification on Theorem 8’s upper bounds for J(Q), how they compare, and how the dependence on escort normalization Z can be leveraged.  
- Overall assessment: theoretical results are interesting but currently lack sufficient practical relevance and experimental support for GAN practitioners.
