- Presents a novel information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, establishing convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences and a key Bregman-divergence result.

- Provides a principled implementation of f-GANs and a factorization theorem linking activation choices to the chi (and thus f) function for deep networks.

- Theorem 4 is a core, impressive theoretical contribution formalizing IG dynamics of f-GAN training.

- Proofs checked by the reviewer appear correct.

- Paper is theoretically rich and of high interest to machine learning and information-theory communities; recommendation: accept.

- Major presentation issue: motivate the IG perspective and its value (analysis in parameter space) earlier in the introduction.

- Move Figure 1 (appx.) to the main text to improve reader comprehension.

- Narrative cohesion is weak; results (IG theory, implementation, architecture analysis, experiments) feel like loosely connected pieces rather than a unified story.

- Section 5’s network factorization and the f-GAN optimization results need clearer links showing how activation/divergence choices guide practical training decisions.

- Experiments validate parts of the paper independently but don’t demonstrate the unified IG framework’s holistic benefits; should test hypotheses derived from IG theory (e.g., geometry’s effect on optimization); WGAN results are somewhat distracting.

- Streamline content by moving tangential material (e.g., brief utility-theory mention) to the appendix.

- Minor clarification requested: explain “deep sufficient statistics” for features phi_l and how Eq.(13) fits a deformed exponential-family structure.
