- Paper addresses key GAN questions: parameter-space convergence, what generator models at convergence, and choosing discriminator output activation for proper composite loss.  
- The authors derive a variational generalization (f-GAN compatible) linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters.  
- They show that under a reversibility assumption deep generative networks factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.  
- Analysis suggests principled choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation can potentially improve GAN performance.  
- They briefly reinterpret the GAN game via expected utility theory.  
- Reviewer finds the paper contains interesting theoretical insights but doubts its current practical relevance and utility.  
- Major concern: many results assume data and model belong to a deformed exponential family—authors should justify how often this holds in practice and whether results extend without that assumption.  
- Question about value of the parameter-space f-GAN identity: what do we gain, is direct parameter optimization feasible, and what concrete benefits/implementations exist?  
- Manuscript does not empirically exploit the parameter-space identity; reviewer requests examples where distribution-level convergence does not imply parameter convergence.  
- Practically relevant claim that discriminator output activation gf matters—reviewer requests experiments comparing the theoretically motivated gf to the heuristic in prior work.  
- Experimental concern: μ-ReLU (theoretically preferable under reversibility) underperforms baseline ReLU in GAN MLPs—authors should explain this mismatch and whether DCGAN meets reversibility.  
- Relation to non-f-divergence methods (e.g., Wasserstein GANs) is unclear and appears outside the theory’s scope.  
- Results in Table 2 show differences within error bars, so empirical support for theoretical impact is unconvincing.  
- Minor request: provide more intuition on deformed exponentials, signatures, and escorts for ML readers.  
- Theorem 8: compare upper bounds for J(Q), explain guidance for activation selection, and clarify why larger escort normalization constant Z reduces the upper bound and how to exploit this in practice.
