This paper develops the f-GAN principle in the setting of f-divergences. By extending the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families via the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information-geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which provides an interpretation of the f-GAN objective. They also present a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical contribution—the vig-f-GAN identity—is interesting, and its potential applicability is promising. However, the central theorems are presented in highly general form, which makes their meaning and implications difficult to follow.

Section 6: The relationship between the experimental observations and the theoretical results is unclear. It is hard to see how the experiments connect to, illustrate, or validate the theory.
