This paper studies the f-GAN framework generalized via f-divergences. By extending the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families through the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information-geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which offers an interpretation of the f-GAN objective. The authors also present a sufficient condition on activation functions to achieve factored generative distributions. 
The primary theoretical contribution, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and its potential applicability is promising. However, the meanings of the main theorems are difficult to follow because they are stated in highly general terms.
