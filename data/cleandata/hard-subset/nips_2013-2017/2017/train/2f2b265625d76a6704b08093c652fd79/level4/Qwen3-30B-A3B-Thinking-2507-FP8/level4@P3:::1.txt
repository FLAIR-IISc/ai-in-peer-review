

This paper extends the theoretical foundations of f-GANs by establishing a variational information geometric identity (vig-f-GAN) that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors build upon Nowozin et al.'s [30] work by showing that when the generator's output distribution lies within a deformed exponential family (a superset of regular exponential families), the f-GAN objective can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem. This provides a novel perspective on the generator's convergence properties and offers theoretical justification for common deep learning practices in GAN architectures.

The main theoretical contribution is Theorem 8, which establishes the vig-f-GAN identity (equation 11). This identity reveals that the f-GAN game can be viewed as optimizing a Bregman divergence between natural parameters, with the generator's output needing to factorize escort distributions. The authors further prove Theorem 9, showing that standard deep architectures can factorize these escorts when using appropriate activation functions, and Lemma 11, which identifies conditions for activation functions (such as ReLU, ELU, and Softplus) to satisfy this requirement.

The paper makes significant progress in connecting information theory with information geometry in the context of GANs. It addresses a gap left by Nowozin et al. [30] regarding the generator's role in the f-GAN game, providing a theoretical framework that explains why certain deep architectures work well for GANs. The experimental section (Section 6) validates these theoretical claims by demonstrating that principled activation function design and link function tuning can improve GAN performance across multiple architectures and datasets.

Strengths: 
- **Quality**: The theoretical results are well-supported by rigorous proofs (provided in the supplement). The paper properly addresses the limitations of the f-GAN framework and establishes clear conditions under which their results hold. The experiments appropriately test the theoretical claims.
- **Clarity**: The paper is well-organized and the key contributions are clearly articulated. The theoretical development is logically structured, though some readers might find the notation challenging. The authors provide a helpful supplement with additional proofs and experimental details.
- **Originality**: The connection between f-GANs and information geometry via deformed exponential families is novel. While Nowozin et al. [30] extended GANs to f-divergences, this paper provides the missing information geometric interpretation that was previously absent from the literature. This represents a significant advancement beyond the NIPS 2016-2017 GAN literature.
- **Significance**: The results provide a theoretical foundation for understanding why certain deep architectures work well for GANs, which is a major open question in the field. The paper demonstrates how these theoretical insights can lead to practical improvements in GAN performance, making it valuable for both theoretical and applied researchers.

Weaknesses:
- **Clarity**: The most significant issue is the presentation of the main theoretical results. Theorems 5-8 are stated in highly general forms without sufficient intuition or examples to help readers grasp their meaning. For instance, the connection between Theorem 5 (f-divergence = KLχ) and the practical implications for GAN training is not sufficiently explained. The relationship between the escort distribution and the generator's architecture could be made clearer.
- **Clarity**: The experimental section (Section 6) lacks sufficient explanation of how the results directly support the theoretical claims. While the paper reports performance improvements, it doesn't clearly demonstrate how these improvements are attributable to the specific theoretical insights (e.g., the activation function conditions in Lemma 11). The comparison between different activation functions in Figure 1 lacks statistical significance testing, making it difficult to assess whether observed differences are meaningful.
- **Originality**: While the theoretical results are novel, the paper could better contextualize them against related work. For example, it doesn't sufficiently discuss how their information geometric perspective relates to the Wasserstein GAN's geometric interpretation [6] or the recent work on information-geometric GANs [14].

Pro/Con acceptance list:
- PRO: The paper establishes a theoretically sound connection between f-GANs and information geometry that addresses a significant gap in the literature. The results provide a principled foundation for understanding why certain deep architectures work well for GANs.
- PRO: The paper demonstrates practical relevance through experiments that validate the theoretical insights, showing how activation function design and link function tuning can improve GAN performance.
- PRO: The work is technically rigorous with well-supported theoretical claims, addressing the quality criterion thoroughly.
- CON: The presentation of the main theoretical results is overly abstract and lacks sufficient intuitive explanation, making it difficult for readers to grasp the practical significance.
- CON: The experimental section doesn't clearly link results to theoretical claims, weakening the evidence for the practical value of the proposed framework.

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs, particularly in connecting information theory with information geometry. While the presentation of the main theoretical results could be improved, the results themselves are significant and well-supported. The work has the potential to influence both theoretical research and practical GAN implementation, making it a strong candidate for NIPS. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the authors should improve the exposition of the main theoretical results in the camera-ready version.
