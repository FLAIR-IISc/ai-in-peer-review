This paper explores the extension of the f-GAN principle to f-divergences, building upon the connection between KL divergence and regular exponential families through the chi-logarithm. The authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which provides a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Additionally, they derive a sufficient condition on activation functions to achieve factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and holds promise for potential applications. However, the presentation of the primary theorems in overly general forms makes their meanings difficult to grasp.

Section 6 lacks clarity in connecting experimental observations to theoretical results, making it challenging to understand the relevance of the findings.

On page 7, line 258, the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C …" is grammatically incorrect and requires rephrasing for better comprehension.
