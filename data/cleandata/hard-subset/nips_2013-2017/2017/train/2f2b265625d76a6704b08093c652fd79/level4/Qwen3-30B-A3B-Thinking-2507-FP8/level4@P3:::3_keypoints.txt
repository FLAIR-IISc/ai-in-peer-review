- Novel theoretical contribution: generalizes the KL/Bregman identity to f-divergences for deformed exponential families, linking f-GANs to information geometry.  
- Technical soundness: proofs for the main identity (Corollary 8) and network factorization (Theorem 9) are careful and valid.  
- Insight on losses: connects proper composite losses to the f-GAN framework, filling a literature gap (Section 5).  
- Activation design principle: provides theoretical justification for common activations (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) and guidance for generator/discriminator link functions (Theorem 12).  
- Restrictive assumptions: main results depend on the strong assumption that data and model distributions are deformed exponential families, with little justification for real data.  
- Limited practical relevance: authors do not show that the deformed exponential family assumption holds for datasets like MNIST or LSUN, weakening applicability.  
- Missing implementation guidance: paper claims parameter-space convergence but fails to show how to implement parameter-based optimization or compare it to variational f-GAN training.  
- Weak experimental support: experiments do not consistently validate theory (e.g., μ-ReLU underperforms ReLU) and discrepancies aren’t adequately explained.  
- Insufficient evidence: results across activations/link functions lie within error bars, offering limited practical validation of theoretical claims.  
- Specific request — theory: strengthen justification that real-world GAN data fits deformed exponential family assumptions.  
- Specific request — implementation: provide concrete methods and experiments applying the parameter-based optimization perspective to improve GANs.  
- Specific request — comparisons: directly compare parameter-based optimization (Eq. 11 RHS) with standard variational f-GAN training (LHS).  
- Clarity needed: better explanations of deformed exponential families, escorts, and the reversibility assumption’s relevance to architectures like DCGAN.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting and original but practically limited due to restrictive assumptions, lack of implementation detail, and weak experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — needs stronger empirical validation, clearer practical guidance, and justification of core assumptions before acceptance.
