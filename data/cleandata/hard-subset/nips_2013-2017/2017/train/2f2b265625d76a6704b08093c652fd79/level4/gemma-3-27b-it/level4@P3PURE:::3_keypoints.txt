- Paper addresses GAN questions: parameter-space convergence, what the generator fits at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice, using deformed exponential distributions.
- Authors derive a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters, connecting information-theoretic and information-geometric views.
- They show that under a reversibility assumption deep generative networks factor as escorts of deformed exponential families.
- Theory suggests choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation carefully could improve GAN performance; an expected-utility interpretation is also noted.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical results interesting but questions their practical relevance and usefulness.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family — authors must justify this assumption and its practicality.
- Reviewer asks what is gained by the parameter-based f-GAN identity and whether GANs can be implemented/benefit from parameter-based optimization; empirical comparisons are missing.
- Reviewer requests a realistic example where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply convergence in parameter space, since the implication is asserted but not clearly motivated.
- Practical implication about discriminator output activation gf is interesting — reviewer wants experiments comparing the theoretically derived gf to the heuristic from Ref. [34].
- Reversibility assumption vs experiments: μ-ReLU (invertible) performs worse than ReLU in MLP GANs, contrary to theory; authors should explain.
- Reviewer asks whether DCGAN satisfies the reversibility assumption and how results compare to Wasserstein GAN, which lies outside the f-divergence framework.
- Many experimental differences are within error bars, so results do not convincingly support practical relevance of the theory.
- Minor: request more intuition/explanation of deformations/signatures and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.
- Minor: in Theorem 8, reviewer asks how the different upper bounds for J(Q) compare, what they imply for activation choice, and why a bound decreasing with escort normalization Z is advantageous and how to exploit it.
- Overall conclusion: insights are interesting but not yet substantial enough to convince GAN practitioners of practical impact.
