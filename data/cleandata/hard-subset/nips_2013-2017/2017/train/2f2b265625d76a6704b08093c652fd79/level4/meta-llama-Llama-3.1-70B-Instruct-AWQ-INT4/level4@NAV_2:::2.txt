This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, providing a comprehensive framework that connects f-divergence and chi-divergence with a Bregman divergence result. The authors demonstrate that f-GAN converges in parameter space using a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, and they discuss a proper way to implement f-GAN. Additionally, they present a factorization result of the deep neural network representation and discuss the choice of activation functions, which has a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function.

The paper is very dense and contains many new results that could be of interest for machine learning and information theory. The exposition of Theorem 4, which tells the IG part of the story for f-GAN, is particularly impressive. The paper's contributions, including the variational information geometric identity for the f-GAN game, the connection between KLχ divergences and f-divergences, and the factorization result of the deep neural network representation, are significant and well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

However, the paper could benefit from some reorganization and clarification to make the material more accessible to a broader audience. The connections between information theory and information geometry in the f-GAN context are not immediately clear, and the paper could benefit from a clearer statement of why considering the IG view is helpful. Additionally, some sections, such as Section 5, could be more clearly connected to the main results of the paper.

The experimental results, while interesting, could also be more directly related to the main points claimed in the paper. The comparison of varying activations in the generator and the comparison of varying link functions in the discriminator could be more clearly connected to the IG view of f-GAN.

In terms of specific suggestions, the reviewer recommends:

* Moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to help illustrate the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
* Making a clear statement at the beginning of the paper about why considering the IG view is helpful.
* Clarifying the connection between Section 5 and the main results of the paper.
* Providing more context for Theorem 6 and explaining why the phi_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics."
* Reorganizing the paper to make the material more coherent and easier to follow.
* Considering moving some sections, such as the utility theory part, to the appendix or deleting them to free up space to explain the main results.

Overall, while the paper provides many useful results and dense derivations, it could benefit from some reorganization and clarification to make the material more accessible to a broader audience.
