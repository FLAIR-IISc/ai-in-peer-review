This paper generalizes the f-GAN principle to encompass a broader class of f-divergences. By extending the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families via the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which offers a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Furthermore, they prove a sufficient condition on activation functions that guarantees factored generative distributions. 

The core theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and demonstrates promising applicability. However, the abstract nature and generality of the main theorems make their precise implications difficult to grasp. 

Section 6 lacks clarity in connecting the experimental observations to the theoretical findings presented in the paper. 

Specifically, on p.7, l.258, the phrase “For eq.(7), is means when C…” contains a grammatical error and awkward phrasing.
