- Paper asks whether solving the GAN problem implies convergence in parameter space, what generator fits when convergence occurs, and how discriminator output activation affects loss compositeness.  
- Analysis is developed within deformed exponential distributions, producing theoretical results linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences between parameters.  
- The variational generalization connects information-theoretic and information-geometric views of distribution dissimilarity.  
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generative networks can be factored into escorts of deformed exponential distributions, suggesting activation-function design matters.  
- The authors propose an alternative expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.  
- Reviewer doubts the practical relevance and usefulness of the theoretical results in their current form.  
- Major concern: many theorems assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family; authors should justify this and indicate whether results generalize without it.  
- Question about the benefit and implementability of the parameter-based (information-geometric) f-GAN identity and request for experimental comparisons with the standard variational approach.  
- Reviewer asks why converging in variational f-divergence does not obviously imply parameter-space convergence and requests a counterexample or clarification.  
- Theoretical implication that discriminator output activation gf matters for proper compositeness is interesting but needs experimental comparison with existing heuristics (Ref.[34]).  
- Empirical discrepancy: theoretically motivated μ-ReLU performs worse than baseline ReLU; authors should explain and discuss whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility and how results compare to WGAN.  
- Experimental results for activations and link functions lie within error bars, offering limited support for practical impact.  
- Minor concern: deformed exponential densities, deformations/signatures, and escorts need more intuition for ML readers unfamiliar with them.  
- In Theorem 8, upper bounds for J(Q) are presented; reviewer asks how these bounds compare and how decreasing the escort normalization Z helps in practice.  
- Overall judgment: theoretical insights are interesting but not sufficiently compelling or actionable for GAN practitioners.
