- Paper studies GAN convergence, generator behavior at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice for proper compositeness.  
- Uses deformed exponential distributions to derive theoretical results and a variational generalization linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences over parameters.  
- Shows under a reversibility assumption that deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential families, implying activation choices may matter.  
- Reviewer finds the theory interesting but questions its practical relevance and usefulness.  
- Major concern: many results assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family—authors must justify this assumption and whether results extend beyond it.  
- Question about the benefit and implementability of the parameter-space f-GAN identity (Eq.7); requests experimental comparison to distribution-space optimization.  
- Asks why distributional convergence doesn’t obviously imply parameter convergence and requests realistic counterexamples where it fails.  
- Highlights the practical implication that the discriminator’s output activation gf is crucial for proper compositeness and asks for experimental comparison to prior heuristics.  
- Notes a counterintuitive experimental result: invertible μ-ReLU performs worse than baseline ReLU despite theory predicting the opposite; requests explanation.  
- Requests clarification whether common architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy the reversibility assumption and how results relate to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN.  
- Observes reported differences in activation/link function performance are within error margins, weakening empirical support for the theory.  
- Minor: recommends more intuition/exposition on deformed exponential concepts such as “deformation/signature” and “escort” for broader ML readership.  
- Minor: asks for comparison among the upper bounds on J(Q) in Theorem 8, implications for activation selection, and why larger escort normalization Z is beneficial.  
- Overall conclusion: theoretical insights are interesting but not yet sufficiently actionable for GAN practitioners.
