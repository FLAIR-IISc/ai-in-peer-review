- Novel theoretical contribution: introduces a variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity generalizing f-GANs via the chi-logarithm—mathematically novel and promising.  
- Main theorems are stated very abstractly, impairing accessibility and practical understanding.  
- Theorem 1 (vig-f-GAN identity) lacks connection to familiar GAN optimization or information geometry and needs clarification on how it extends/simplifies existing f-GAN duality.  
- Theorem 2’s sufficient condition for factorized generative distributions is technically sound but lacks intuitive justification and explanation of its design relevance.  
- Experiments report FID improvements on MNIST/CIFAR-10 but do not explicitly link these gains to the theoretical claims.  
- Claim that improvements arise from “better divergence approximation” is unsupported—no demonstration that the vig-f-GAN identity enables this.  
- Ablation study on activation functions omits whether tested activations satisfy Theorem 2, failing to validate the core theoretical contribution.  
- Overall experimental-theoretical disconnect weakens the paper’s narrative and evidence.  
- Suggestion: reframe theorems with concrete examples (e.g., show reduction to KL divergence cases and links to existing results).  
- Suggestion: explicitly bridge theory and experiments by demonstrating causality (e.g., show activations meeting Theorem 2 produce measured improvements).  
- Suggestion: trim excessive generality—balance rigor with accessibility and intuitive exposition.  
- Minor issue: grammatical error on p.7, line 258 ("is means" → "it means").  
- Minor issue: define or reference the term “chi-logarithm” to avoid confusion.  
- Minor issue: Section 5’s “information geometric perspective” is vague; clarify how it informs activation function choices.  
- Overall verdict: promising and potentially influential work but requires major revision for clarity, intuition, and stronger theory–experiment alignment.
