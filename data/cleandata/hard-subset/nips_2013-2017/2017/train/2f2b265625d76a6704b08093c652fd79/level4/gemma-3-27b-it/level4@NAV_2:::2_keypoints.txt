- Proposes an information geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, showing convergence via a bijection between f-divergence and chi-divergence using a Bregman divergence result.
- Provides a principled implementation approach for f-GAN and a factorization result for deep network representations, linking activation functions to the chi/f function.
- Proofs mostly appear correct, though Theorem 8 was not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is dense with numerous novel results valuable to machine learning and information theory.
- Theorem 6’s exposition effectively illuminates the IG aspects of f-GAN and is particularly impressive.
- Recommends acceptance despite complexity.
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with connections between information theory, information geometry, KL/Bregman divergences, and Fenchel duality.
- Suggests moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to better visualize IT–IG connections.
- Recommends a clearer early statement of the benefits of the IG perspective, especially regarding leveraging parameter-space geometry for optimization.
- Requests clarification of Section 5’s connection to f-GAN and more exploration of implications for activation/function choice and f-divergence design.
- Asks to justify interpreting phi_l in Theorem 6 as “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq.(13) doesn’t explicitly show a deformed exponential family.
- Suggests moving lines 257–269 (utility theory) to the appendix, and streamlining experiments—activation results could stand alone, while WGAN material is distracting and should be de-emphasized.
