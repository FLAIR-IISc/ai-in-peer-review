- Paper links f-divergence minimization to information-geometric optimization over deformed exponential families, providing a variational generalization connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences.
- Shows deep generative networks, under a reversibility assumption, can be interpreted as factorizations of escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Derives a theoretically optimal discriminator output activation gf as the composition of f' and the link function, suggesting improved loss composition.
- Main concern: central assumption that data and model distributions belong to the deformed exponential family needs rigorous justification and discussion of practical prevalence.
- Question whether key results (e.g., Theorems 4 & 6) can be extended or derived under weaker, more realistic assumptions.
- The information-geometric f-GAN identity (Eq. 11) is interesting, but the paper lacks clear tangible benefits, implementation strategies, or empirical comparisons to standard variational optimization.
- Authors should provide a concrete example where variational f-divergence convergence does not imply parameter-space convergence (due to residual J(Q)) to illustrate practical implications.
- Theoretical gf should be empirically compared to the heuristic choice from Nowozin et al. to demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Experimental results are unconvincing: μ-ReLU (invertible) performs worse than baseline ReLU in GAN MLP, contradicting theoretical expectations under reversibility.
- Need clarification whether DCGAN satisfies the reversibility assumption and comparisons to Wasserstein GANs, since the framework may not apply to non-f-divergence GANs.
- Reported differences across activation and link functions (Table 2) appear within margin of error, limiting support for practical relevance.
- Recommend adding intuitive explanations for "deformation"/"signature" and "escort" to improve accessibility for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential families.
- Theorem 8 lists upper bounds for J(Q); paper should compare these bounds, discuss implications for activation selection, and explain why decreasing the escort normalization Z is desirable and how to exploit it.
- Overall: intriguing theoretical contributions but limited experimental validation and strong distributional assumptions constrain immediate impact for GAN practitioners.
