This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm. First, it shows that f-GAN optimization converges in parameter space by leveraging the one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and χ-divergence, along with a related result on Bregman divergence. The paper then discusses a principled method for implementing f-GAN. Finally, it provides a factorization of the learned deep neural network representation and discusses the choice of activation functions that have a one-to-one mapping to the χ (or f) function. While I did not check every detail in the appendix, the proofs I examined appear to be correct (with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to review before the deadline).

The paper is dense and presents several novel results that should be of interest to both the machine learning and information theory communities. I was particularly impressed by the exposition of Theorem 4, which elucidates the information-geometric interpretation of f-GAN. For these reasons, I recommend acceptance.

However, the paper may be very difficult for readers who are not already familiar with the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), such as the relationship between KL divergence and Bregman divergence for exponential families or the concept of Fenchel duality. This group likely includes many deep learning practitioners, who are a primary audience for papers on GANs. To improve the paper's clarity and accessibility, I offer the following suggestions:

1.  Figure 1 in the appendix, which clearly illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the context of f-GAN, is very helpful. The authors should consider moving it into the main body of the paper.

2.  It would be beneficial to state upfront why the IG perspective is useful. My understanding is that it allows for analyzing the optimization behavior in the parameter space. This point is mentioned briefly on page 7, but it would have a much greater impact if introduced earlier.

3.  The connection between Section 5 and the f-GAN framework is unclear. It appears that the deformed exponential family is used to characterize the distributions representable by a deep neural network, rather than being a direct consequence of the f-GAN optimization results. While I accept the one-to-one correspondence between v, f, and χ, the paper does not explain how this insight can be used to guide the design of the f-GAN framework. For instance, it is not clear how this result helps in selecting an appropriate f-divergence or in determining which f-GAN objective would lead to the best convergence for a given activation function.

4.  In Theorem 6, why are the functions φ_l described as "deep sufficient statistics"? Equation (13) does not appear to conform to the structure of a deformed exponential family.

5.  As mentioned above, the motivation for the IG view (lines 257-269) would be better placed earlier in the paper. Additionally, the discussion on utility theory seems tangential to the core information-geometric arguments. Moving this paragraph to the appendix would free up valuable space to elaborate on the main contributions.

6.  The connection between the experiments and the paper's central theoretical claims feels tenuous. For example, the new activation functions (Experiment A) seem to follow directly from the results in Section 5, without needing the full IG framework of f-GAN. Similarly, Experiment B appears to rely only on the implementation details from Section 4, which are not tightly coupled with the IG perspective. Furthermore, including results for WGAN is potentially confusing, as the paper's focus is on f-GANs. I had to search for the brief mention (lines 227-228) that justifies its inclusion.

In summary, while the paper presents numerous valuable results and detailed derivations, the material is not organized with sufficient clarity. This gives the impression that results from multiple lines of inquiry have been condensed into a single submission, at the expense of a cohesive narrative. Therefore, while I support the paper's acceptance, I strongly urge the authors to revise it for clarity and coherence, ensuring the connections between the different theoretical and empirical contributions are made explicit.
