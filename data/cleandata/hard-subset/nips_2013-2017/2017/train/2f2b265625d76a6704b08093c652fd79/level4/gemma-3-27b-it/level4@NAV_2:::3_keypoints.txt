- Paper poses interesting GAN questions: parameter-space convergence, what generator fits at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice for proper compositeness.
- Authors work within deformed exponential distributions and derive theoretical results linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences on parameters.
- They present a variational generalization (f-GAN amenable) of a known theorem connecting distributional and parameter-space dissimilarities.
- Under a reversibility assumption, authors show deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential families.
- Theoretical analysis suggests careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output/link functions could improve GANs.
- Authors mention an alternative expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Major concern: many results require P and Q to be deformed exponential family members — unclear if this holds in practice or can be relaxed.
- Question about the practical benefit of the information-geometric f-GAN identity: what is gained, can GANs be optimized directly in parameter space, and what are empirical benefits?
- Reviewer notes the paper does not empirically exploit the parameter-space identity beyond inferring parameter convergence when residual J(Q) is small.
- Reviewer asks for a realistic scenario where distributional convergence does not imply parameter convergence.
- Important practical implication: output activation gf matters for proper compositeness — reviewer wants experiments comparing the theoretically derived gf to existing heuristics.
- Empirical concern: μ-ReLU (theoretically motivated invertible activation) performed worse than baseline ReLU in experiments, contradicting expectations under reversibility.
- Reviewer requests clarification on whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility and comparisons to non-f-divergence frameworks like Wasserstein GAN.
- Experimental results (Table 1) appear within error bars and insufficient to support theoretical claims in practice.
- Minor: the paper should provide more intuition for deformations/signatures and escorts for ML readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential families.
- Minor: clarify comparisons among the multiple upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8 and practical implications for activation choice.
- Minor: explain why J(Q) decreasing with escort normalization Z is beneficial and how to leverage it.
- Overall judgment: interesting and mathematically sound theoretical contributions but lacking practical relevance and strong experimental validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical connection between f-GANs and information geometry, correct derivations, highlights role of activation/link functions.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental support, reliance on possibly unrealistic assumptions (deformed exponentials, reversibility), limited practical impact, and accessibility concerns due to unfamiliar framework.
