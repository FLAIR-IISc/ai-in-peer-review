

This paper generalizes the f-GAN framework to f-divergences, leveraging the chi-logarithm to extend the connection between KL divergence and exponential families. The authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which offers a new interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Additionally, they derive sufficient conditions on activation functions to yield factored generative distributions.  

The main theoretical contribution—the vig-f-GAN identity—is intriguing and holds significant potential. However, the core theorems remain difficult to interpret due to their highly abstract formulations, obscuring their practical relevance. The presentation lacks concrete examples or step-by-step explanations that would clarify how these general results translate to specific applications.  

Section 6 presents a critical disconnect: the experimental observations do not clearly align with the theoretical claims. For instance, the reported performance improvements in Table 1 lack explicit linkage to the vig-f-GAN identity or the activation function conditions established earlier. Without this connection, the empirical results fail to validate the paper’s theoretical contributions.  

On page 7, line 258, the phrase "For eq.(7), is means when C …" contains a grammatical error and is confusing. It should read: "For Equation (7), it means when C …" to correctly convey the intended meaning.  

The paper’s technical depth is commendable, but its current presentation impedes accessibility. The authors should:  
1) Simplify the theorem statements by introducing intermediate lemmas or concrete examples,  
2) Explicitly map experimental results to theoretical guarantees in Section 6, and  
3) Correct grammatical errors to improve readability.  

While the core idea is promising, these revisions are essential for the work to resonate with the broader NIPS community. Without them, the theoretical novelty remains underutilized, and the paper’s impact is diminished.
