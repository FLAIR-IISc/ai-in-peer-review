- Contributions: Provides theoretical links between f-divergences and Bregman divergences, a reversibility-based factorization of generators as escorts, and architectural implications for generator/discriminator activations plus an expected-utility view of GANs.

- Positive: Several connections are insightful and potentially impactful.

- Major concern — scope/assumptions: Many results require P and Q to lie in a deformed exponential family; need justification for when this holds in practice and extension/approximation guarantees beyond this family.

- Major concern — Eq. 7 identity: Unclear algorithmic/statistical benefits of the parameter-space Bregman formulation; request experiments implementing/minimizing the parameter objective and examples where small f-divergence does not imply parameter convergence (illustrating the role of J(Q)).

- Major concern — discriminator activation/proper compositeness: Theoretically prescribed discriminator output gf should be empirically compared against heuristic choices across datasets, architectures, and f-divergences with calibration and stability metrics.

- Major concern — reversibility/escorts vs empirical results: μ-ReLU (invertible) underperforms ReLU in experiments, contradicting theory; need explanations (optimization, mismatch, stability, implicit regularization), clarity on DCGAN reversibility in practice, and relation/comparison to Wasserstein GANs.

- Major concern — statistical significance: Report more runs, confidence intervals, and effect-size tests since current differences appear within error bars.

- Major concern — Theorem 8 and J(Q) bounds: Need assessment of bound tightness in realistic settings, concrete guidance for choosing activations/links, and explanation of the role and practical leverage of escort normalization Z.

- Minor request — exposition: Add intuitive explanations and simple examples of deformed exponential signatures and escorts, and relate them to familiar families and neural activations.

- Minor request — empirical diagnostics: Track parameter-space Bregman distances, estimate/bound J(Q) approximately, perform reversibility ablations, and test whether generator layers empirically behave like escorts.

- Overall evaluation: Elegant and theoretically intriguing work but currently lacks convincing empirical support and generality; strengthening experiments, clarifying assumptions, and improving exposition are needed for practical impact.
