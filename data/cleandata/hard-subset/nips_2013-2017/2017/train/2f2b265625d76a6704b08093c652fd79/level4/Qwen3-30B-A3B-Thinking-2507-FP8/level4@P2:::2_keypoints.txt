- Presents an information-geometric perspective linking f-divergences and Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families.  
- Interprets f-GAN optimization as a geometric optimization on parameter space, showing generator convergence to the true distribution under certain conditions.  
- Establishes a one-to-one correspondence between activation functions and parameters of deformed exponential families; ReLU, ELU, Softplus meet the conditions.  
- Provides a complete characterization of the supervised game by incorporating proper composite losses into the f-GAN framework.  
- Theorem 8 linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences is a significant theoretical advance.  
- Characterization of generator convergence fills a critical gap in the original f-GAN formulation.  
- The activation–deformed-exponential-family connection offers a principled approach to generator design, supported by experiments.  
- Overall technically sound with rigorous proofs, though Theorem 8 could use clearer exposition.  
- Presentation is dense and may be inaccessible to practitioners unfamiliar with information geometry.  
- Practical implications for GAN implementation are not clearly articulated early enough in the paper.  
- Section 5 needs stronger justification of how proper composite losses relate to the f-GAN framework and practical choices (which f-divergences/activations to use).  
- The claim that φ_l in eq. (13) are "deep sufficient statistics" requires clearer explanation and justification.  
- The discussion of utility theory (lines 257–269) appears tangential and would better belong in an appendix.  
- Experimental section should be more directly tied to main claims; some experiments (e.g., WGAN) distract from the f-GAN focus.  
- Organizational issues: multiple theoretical threads feel squeezed into the page limit rather than structured around a central narrative.  
- Recommended fixes: move Figure 1 to main text, state practical value of the geometric perspective early, restructure Section 5, clarify network ↔ deformed exponential family links, and streamline experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel theoretical contribution that warrants acceptance provided organizational and explanatory issues are addressed.
