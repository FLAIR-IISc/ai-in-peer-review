This paper presents an insightful information-geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN algorithm. The authors demonstrate f-GAN convergence in parameter space by leveraging the bijective mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences, alongside a relevant Bregman divergence result. Furthermore, they address practical implementation details and provide a factorization result for deep neural network representations, linking activation function choices to the underlying chi (or f) function. While I did not exhaustively verify every detail in the appendix, the proofs appear sound, with the exception of Theorem 8, which I was unable to fully assess within the review timeframe.

Overall, I find this paper to be a strong contribution with the potential to significantly impact both machine learning and information theory. I am particularly impressed by the exposition of Theorem 4, which elegantly clarifies the IG foundations of f-GAN. Therefore, I recommend acceptance. 

However, the paper’s density and technicality may present a barrier to readers unfamiliar with the interplay between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG). Many practitioners in the deep learning community, who constitute a primary audience for GAN papers, may lack a strong background in these areas. To enhance clarity and broaden the paper’s accessibility, I suggest the following:

1. **Relocate Figure 1:** The diagram in the appendix illustrating the connections between IT and IG within the f-GAN context is highly valuable. Moving this figure to the main text would provide readers with an early and concrete visualization of the core concepts.

2. **Motivate the IG View Earlier:** The paper would benefit from a more prominent statement early on explaining the utility of an IG perspective. My understanding is that this approach allows the use of geometric properties of the parameter space to analyze optimization behavior. While this is mentioned briefly on page 7, it feels somewhat delayed and lacks sufficient emphasis.

3. **Clarify the Connection of Section 5:** The link between Section 5 and the f-GAN optimization process is currently unclear. While the application of deformed exponential families to characterize the distribution representable by a deep neural network is interesting, it appears independent of the f-GAN results. Establishing how the bijective relationship between *v*, *f*, and *chi* informs f-GAN design choices—such as selecting an appropriate f-divergence, or matching an activation function to an optimal f-GAN objective based on convergence properties—would strengthen this section.

4. **Justify “Deep Sufficient Statistics”:** The claim that φ<sub>l</sub> in Theorem 6 represents “deep sufficient statistics” requires further justification. Equation (13) does not immediately appear to conform to the structure of a deformed exponential family. Providing a clearer explanation of this connection would be helpful.

5. **Streamline Content:** The discussion surrounding utility theory (lines 257-269) feels tangential to the core IG view of f-GAN. Removing this paragraph (and potentially relocating it to the appendix) could create space to elaborate on the paper's primary contributions.

6. **Refocus Experiments:** The experimental results seem somewhat disconnected from the main theoretical arguments. The benefits of the results from Section 5 (activation function design) can be demonstrated independently of the IG framework. Similarly, the insights from Section 4 can be presented without explicitly relying on the IG view of f-GAN. The inclusion of WGAN results, while potentially interesting, is distracting given the paper's focus on f-GANs and required considerable effort to locate the relevant discussion (lines 227-228).

In summary, this paper presents numerous valuable results and intricate derivations, but its organization currently feels somewhat fragmented. It reads as a consolidation of findings from multiple investigations into a single 8-page NIPS submission. While I support acceptance, I believe a thorough editorial revision to improve clarity and coherence is necessary to maximize the impact of this work.
