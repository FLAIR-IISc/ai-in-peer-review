- Paper studies GAN questions about parameter convergence, what generator fits on convergence, and discriminator output activation choice, using deformed exponential distributions.
- Presents a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters.
- Shows that under a reversibility assumption deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential families.
- Argues activation choices for generator hidden units and discriminator output may improve GAN performance; briefly mentions an expected-utility interpretation of GANs.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical results interesting but questions their practical relevance and usefulness as presented.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family—ask for justification and whether results extend beyond this family.
- Requests clarification on the practical benefit of the parameter-based f-GAN identity and whether GANs can be implemented/optimized directly in parameter space.
- Asks for experiments comparing distribution-based vs parameter-based optimization, since the RHS of the identity is not exploited beyond an implication claim.
- Challenges the claim that distributional convergence implies parameter convergence; asks for a realistic counterexample where it does not hold.
- Finds the theoretical result on the discriminator output activation gf (for proper compositeness) interesting and requests experimental comparison to prior heuristics.
- Notes a discrepancy: μ-ReLU (theoretically favorable under reversibility) performs worse than baseline ReLU in experiments and asks for explanation.
- Requests clarification whether common architectures like DCGAN satisfy the reversibility assumption and how results compare to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN.
- Points out experimental differences in Table 2 are within error bars, so empirical support for theoretical claims seems weak.
- Minor request: provide more intuition for deformations/signatures and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.
- Asks how the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8 compare and what they imply for activation selection.
- Requests elaboration on why a decreasing upper bound with escort normalization Z is beneficial and how to leverage it.
- Overall conclusion: theoretical insights are interesting but currently insufficiently substantiated for practical GAN practitioners.
