- Contribution: Presents a variational generalization of an f-divergence/Bregman-divergence relationship and links information-theoretic and information-geometric views.  
- Contribution: Derives conditions under which deep generative networks factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions and suggests activation design (e.g., g_f = f' ∘ link).  
- Major concern: Core assumption that data/model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family is very strong and lacks justification for real-world datasets.  
- Major concern: Unclear practical usefulness of parameter-space optimization (Eq.7) — benefits over distribution-space optimization, implementability, and realistic counterexamples for parameter vs. distribution convergence are not provided.  
- Major concern: Empirical results contradict theory: reversible generators (μ-ReLU) perform worse than ReLU in Table 2, undermining the main claim.  
- Major concern: Unclear whether common architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy reversibility assumptions and how results compare to alternatives like Wasserstein GANs.  
- Major concern: Performance differences across activation/link choices are within error bars, providing weak empirical support for theoretical claims.  
- Minor concern: Specialized terms (“deformation,” “escort”) need brief intuitive explanations for a general ML audience.  
- Minor concern: Theorem 8 bounds need clarification — which bound is tightest for practical activations and why a decreasing bound with escort normalization Z is desirable.  
- Overall assessment: Theoretical results are novel and elegant but lack empirical grounding and actionable guidance for GAN practitioners.  
- Recommendation: Reject — paper requires substantial empirical validation and concrete implementation examples to demonstrate practical significance.
