Thank you for this interesting and insightful work.

This paper presents an information geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN algorithm, establishing a connection between f-divergence and chi-divergence via a 1-1 mapping and leveraging a Bregman divergence result to demonstrate f-GAN convergence in parameter space. It also offers a refined implementation strategy for f-GANs and provides a factorization result for deep neural network representations, linking activation function choices to the chi (or f) function. While I did not exhaustively verify all details in the appendix, the proofs appear sound, with the exception of Theorem 8, which I was unable to fully examine within the review timeframe.

I find this paper to be highly substantial, packed with novel results potentially impactful for both machine learning and information theory. I am particularly impressed by the presentation of Theorem 4, which effectively articulates the IG foundation of f-GAN. Therefore, I recommend acceptance.

However, the paper’s density and reliance on background knowledge in information theory and information geometry may present a significant barrier to comprehension for some readers, particularly those from deep learning backgrounds—a primary audience for GAN research. To enhance clarity, I suggest the following revisions:

1. Consider relocating Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text, as it effectively visualizes the crucial connections between information theory and information geometry within the f-GAN framework.
2. Explicitly state the motivation for adopting an IG view early in the paper. The potential to leverage parameter space geometry for optimization analysis—briefly mentioned on page 7—would be a strong introductory point.
3. Clarify the connection between Section 5 and the core f-GAN algorithm. While the application of the deformed exponential family to describe deep neural network outputs is interesting, its relationship to the optimization results of f-GAN isn’t immediately apparent. Further elaboration on how these results inform f-GAN design—specifically, guiding the selection of f-divergences or matching activation functions to objectives—would be beneficial.
4. Provide justification for viewing phi_l as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6. Equation (13) doesn't appear to align with the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Reconsider the placement of lines 257-269. The discussion of utility theory seems somewhat tangential to the IG perspective and could potentially be moved to the appendix to create space for clarifying the central findings.
6. Evaluate the relevance of the experimental results to the paper’s core contributions. The observations related to (A) seem independent of the IG view of f-GAN, relying primarily on results from Section 5. Similarly, (B) appears to stem from Section 4 and lacks a strong connection to the IG analysis. The inclusion of WGAN results feels potentially distracting, given the paper’s focus on f-GANs, and required a deliberate search to locate the relevant context (lines 227-228).

In summary, this paper offers numerous valuable insights and rigorous derivations. However, the organization feels somewhat disjointed, resembling a collection of related results compressed into a single submission. While I support acceptance, I believe that focused editing to streamline the narrative and enhance coherence would significantly improve the paper’s impact and accessibility.
