This paper offers an information-geometric perspective on f-GANs. It establishes convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences, together with a Bregman divergence argument; discusses implementation considerations for f-GAN; and presents a factorization result for deep neural network representations that ties activation choices to the chi (or f) function. While I did not verify every appendix detail, the proofs I checked appear correct (with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to read).

Overall assessment: The paper is dense, technically ambitious, and contains several results that are likely to interest both the machine learning and information theory communities. I was particularly impressed by the exposition around Theorem 4, which makes the information-geometric story behind f-GAN explicit. I support acceptance.

Strengths
- Novel perspective: The IG framing of f-GAN is original and illuminating, especially the link from f-divergences to Bregman divergences and the resulting convergence story in parameter space.
- Breadth of results: The combination of theoretical results, implementation guidance, and representation/activation analysis is valuable and, to my knowledge, new in this unified form.
- Technical correctness: Aside from Theorem 8 (not evaluated), the arguments and derivations appear sound, and the paper demonstrates strong command of both IG and IT tools.

Main concerns and suggestions for clarity
- Accessibility and audience: The paper assumes substantial familiarity with IT, IG, Fenchel duality, and the KL–Bregman equivalence for exponential families. Many deep learning practitioners—the main audience for GAN work—may struggle. I recommend:
  1) Moving the figure in the appendix that summarizes the IG–IT connections into the main text.
  2) Adding, at the outset, a compact motivation for why the IG view helps with optimization (e.g., geometry of parameter space guides convergence properties). This is currently mentioned late (around page 7), which makes the early sections harder to contextualize.
- Connection of Section 5 to f-GAN: As written, Section 5 reads primarily as a characterization of the distributions representable by deep networks via deformed exponential families, with an activation–chi–f correspondence. This is interesting, but the connection back to the f-GAN optimization game is not made explicit. Concretely:
  - How should this correspondence inform the choice of f-divergence in practice?
  - Given a chosen activation, which f-GAN objective is recommended for better convergence or stability, and why?
  - Are there actionable design rules that follow from the theory (e.g., “activation X suggests divergence Y under assumptions Z”)?
- Theorem 6 and “deep sufficient statistics”: Please clarify in what precise sense the φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics.” Equation (13) does not obviously take the standard form of a deformed exponential family; either make this mapping explicit or refine the terminology to avoid confusion.
- Structure and placement of material:
  - Consider moving lines 257–269 to a more contextually appropriate location, closer to the first appearance of the relevant concepts.
  - The brief excursion into utility theory in the main text feels tangential to the IG narrative; cutting or moving it to the appendix could free space to clarify the central contributions.
- Experiments and alignment with claims:
  - The empirical sections do not tightly validate the central IG claims. The activation-function results (A) seem to follow from Section 5 alone, independent of the IG perspective. The convergence/optimization insights from Section 4 are also somewhat orthogonal to the empirical demonstrations.
  - The WGAN results are potentially distracting, as the paper is primarily about f-GANs; I had to search for the brief WGAN remark (lines 227–228). If WGAN is included, explain clearly how it fits the presented framework or move it to the appendix.
- Exposition density: The paper reads like multiple contributions compressed into the page limit. While the results are substantial, the narrative would benefit from streamlining:
  - Lead with the main IG thesis and its implications for optimization and convergence.
  - Then present the implementation guidance that directly follows from the IG analysis.
  - Finally, discuss the representation/activation factorization and its practical consequences for selecting divergences and network architectures.

Minor comments and requests
- Where feasible, provide short “intuitions” following key theorems (e.g., after Theorem 4) to help readers unfamiliar with IG build geometric intuition.
- Include a brief glossary or boxed summary that maps the paper’s symbols (f, chi, v, φ, etc.) to their roles and theorems where they appear.
- State upfront the assumptions under which the Bregman convergence argument applies (e.g., smoothness, convexity/strict convexity, parameterization constraints), and discuss how violations (common in deep nets) affect conclusions.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper presents a compelling IG account of f-GANs with several technically solid and potentially impactful results. However, to maximize its impact for the NeurIPS audience, the authors should revise for clarity and cohesion: bring the key IG–IT map to the main text, motivate early why the IG viewpoint matters for optimization, make the Section 5 implications actionable for f-divergence and activation choices, clarify the “deep sufficient statistics” claim in Theorem 6, and tighten the empirical section to better support the central theoretical claims.
