- Paper presents theoretical results linking f-GAN objectives to information-geometric views using deformed exponential families.
- Main contributions: variational generalization connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences, factorization of deep generators as escorts under reversibility, and guidance on activation/link function choices.
- Reviewer questions the practicality of assuming data and model distributions belong to deformed exponential families and asks if results can be derived without this assumption.
- Unclear practical benefit of the variational information-geometric f-GAN identity; reviewer asks if GANs could be optimized directly in parameter space and for experimental comparisons.
- Reviewer finds the implication "f-GAN convergence implies parameter convergence" insufficiently motivated and requests realistic scenarios where distributional convergence does not imply parameter convergence.
- Theoretical claim about discriminator output activation gf being critical for proper composite loss is compelling but lacks experimental comparison to heuristic choices from prior work.
- Experiments intended to validate activation/link function choices are not fully convincing and show minor differences likely within experimental noise.
- Empirical inconsistency: µ-ReLU (invertible) often underperforms standard ReLU, contradicting theoretical motivation; authors need to explain.
- Reviewer asks whether DCGAN architectures used in experiments satisfy the reversibility assumption required by theory.
- Theory is developed for f-divergences, but some experiments use WGAN; reviewer questions applicability of theoretical results to WGAN.
- Request for clearer intuition and exposition on deformed exponential families, deformations (signatures), and escorts for broader ML audience.
- Reviewer asks for a comparative analysis of bounds on the penalty term J(Q) and clearer explanation of its role in choosing activation functions.
- Specific request to elaborate why a bound on J(Q) decreasing with the escort normalization constant Z is beneficial and how to leverage this in practice.
- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical direction but current practical insights and experimental support are insufficient for GAN practitioners.
