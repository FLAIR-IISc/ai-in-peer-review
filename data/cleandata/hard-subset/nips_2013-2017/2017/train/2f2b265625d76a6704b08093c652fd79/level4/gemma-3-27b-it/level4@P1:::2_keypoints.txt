- Proposes an information geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, linking f-divergence, chi-divergence, and Bregman divergence via a one-to-one mapping.  
- Provides a principled implementation approach for f-GAN and a factorization result for deep neural network representations.  
- Discusses activation function choices connected to the chi (or f) function for network design.  
- Proofs in the appendix appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Paper is very dense and technical but contains numerous novel results of potential interest to ML and information theory.  
- Theorem 6 is especially well-explained and illuminates the IG aspects of f-GAN.  
- Recommends acceptance but warns the paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with information theory/geometry.  
- Suggests moving Figure 1 to the main text because it effectively illustrates key IG–information theory connections.  
- Advise stating the practical benefits of the IG view earlier (currently only briefly on page 7).  
- Finds the connection between Section 5 and core f-GAN optimization unclear; representational results don’t clearly inform f-GAN design choices.  
- Questions why φ_l is called “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6; Equation (13) doesn’t match standard deformed exponential family form.  
- Recommends relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it feels tangential.  
- Finds experiments somewhat disconnected from the central claims; activation-function results could stand alone without the IG framing.  
- Notes section 4 and WGAN results feel loosely tied to the IG narrative and may distract from the f-GAN focus; WGAN clarification is hard to find (lines 227–228).  
- Overall: valuable but better organization, clearer links between sections, and editing for clarity/coherence are needed.
