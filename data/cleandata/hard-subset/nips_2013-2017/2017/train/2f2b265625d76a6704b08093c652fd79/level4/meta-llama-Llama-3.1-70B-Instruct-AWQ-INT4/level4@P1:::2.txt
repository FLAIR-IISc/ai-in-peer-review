This paper proposed an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, providing a novel perspective on the convergence of f-GANs in parameter space. The authors establish a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, leveraging a Bregman divergence result to demonstrate this convergence. Additionally, they discuss a proper implementation of f-GAN and present a factorization result for the deep neural network representation, highlighting the connection between activation functions and chi (or f) functions.

The paper is dense and contains numerous new results that could be of interest to both machine learning and information theory communities. The exposition of Theorem 4, which reveals the IG part of the story for f-GAN, is particularly impressive. Overall, this paper is a clear accept.

However, it may be challenging for readers without a strong background in the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG) to fully understand the paper. To improve clarity, the authors could consider the following suggestions:

1. Moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text could help illustrate the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
2. A clear statement on the benefits of considering the IG view, such as utilizing geometry in the parameter space to discuss optimization behavior, could be added at the beginning of the paper.
3. Section 5 could be reorganized to better connect to f-GAN. The current explanation of the deformed exponential family seems unrelated to the f-GAN optimization results. The authors could elaborate on how the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi can aid in designing the f-GAN game.
4. The authors could provide further clarification on why φ_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics" in Theorem 6, as equation (13) does not appear to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. The utility theory part, which seems unrelated to the IG view, could be removed or relocated to the appendix to free up space for explaining the main results.
6. The experiments could be reorganized to better align with the main points claimed in the paper. For example, the results from section 5 could be used to discuss the new activation functions without relying on the IG view of f-GAN. Similarly, the discussion of WGAN could be removed or separated from the main f-GAN results to avoid confusion.

In summary, while the paper provides valuable results and dense derivations, the material could be better organized to present a clearer and more coherent narrative. With some editing to address these concerns, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact on the machine learning and information theory communities.
