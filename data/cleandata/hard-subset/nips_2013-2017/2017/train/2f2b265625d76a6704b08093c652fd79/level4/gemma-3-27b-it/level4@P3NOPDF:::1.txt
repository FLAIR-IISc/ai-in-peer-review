## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission: [Paper Title - Replace with actual title]

This paper investigates a generalization of the f-GAN framework to encompass a broader class of f-divergences. Building upon the established connection between the Kullback-Leibler divergence and regular exponential families via the chi-logarithm, the authors derive a novel “variational information geometric f-GAN” (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity offers a valuable interpretation of the f-GAN objective function, and the authors further present a sufficient condition on activation functions that guarantees the generation of factored distributions.

The central theoretical contribution – the vig-f-GAN identity – is genuinely interesting and holds considerable potential. A more complete understanding of f-GANs through this information geometric lens is a worthwhile pursuit. However, the paper’s presentation hinders full appreciation of these contributions. The theorems are stated in a highly general and abstract form, which significantly complicates their comprehension and makes it difficult to grasp their practical implications. While generality is often desirable, in this instance, providing concrete examples or simplified instantiations alongside the general results would substantially improve clarity. 

Specifically, the notation and mathematical constructs, while technically correct, often lack intuitive explanation. Readers unfamiliar with the specific areas of information geometry may find the arguments difficult to follow, even with effort. A more deliberate effort to bridge the gap between the mathematical formalism and the underlying intuition would be beneficial.

The experimental section (Section 6) exacerbates this issue. The connection between the empirical observations and the theoretical results is not clearly articulated. The authors present experimental results, but fail to convincingly demonstrate *how* these results validate or illustrate the vig-f-GAN identity or the derived sufficient conditions.  It remains unclear which aspects of the experimental setup are specifically designed to test the theoretical findings, and what conclusions can be drawn from the observed performance in light of those findings. A more focused experimental evaluation, directly linked to the theoretical propositions, is needed. Perhaps demonstrating the advantage of using activations satisfying the derived sufficient condition, or illustrating the behavior of the f-GAN with different f-divergences and relating it back to the vig-f-GAN identity, would strengthen the paper considerably.

Finally, there are minor issues with the writing. For instance, on page 7, line 258, the phrase “For eq.(7), is means when C …” is grammatically incorrect and unclear. It should be rephrased for clarity (e.g., “Regarding equation (7), this implies that when C…”). While these are relatively minor, addressing such instances would contribute to the overall polish of the manuscript.

**Strengths:**

*   Novel and potentially impactful theoretical contribution (vig-f-GAN identity).
*   Interesting connection between information geometry and generative modeling.
*   Identification of a sufficient condition for factored generative distributions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   High level of abstraction and generality makes the theoretical results difficult to understand.
*   Lack of clear connection between experimental results and theoretical contributions.
*   Minor grammatical errors and unclear phrasing in places.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a potentially valuable theoretical advance in the area of f-GANs. However, the lack of clarity in presentation and the weak link between theory and experiment significantly detract from its impact. While the ideas are promising, substantial revisions are needed to make the paper accessible and convincing to a broader audience. I recommend rejection in its current form, but encourage the authors to resubmit a revised version addressing the concerns outlined above.  Specifically, focusing on concrete examples, improving the clarity of the theoretical exposition, and providing a more targeted and insightful experimental evaluation would greatly enhance the paper’s quality and potential contribution.
