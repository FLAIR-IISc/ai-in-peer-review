This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, providing a novel perspective on the convergence of f-GAN in parameter space. The authors establish a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, leveraging a Bregman divergence result. They also discuss a proper way to implement f-GAN and present a factorization result for deep neural network representations, highlighting the choice of activation functions with a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function.

The paper is dense and contains many new results that could be of interest to machine learning and information theory. The exposition of Theorem 4, which tells the IG part of the story for f-GAN, is particularly impressive. Overall, the paper is well-written and the proofs appear to be correct.

However, the paper may be challenging for readers without a strong background in the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG). To improve clarity, the authors could consider the following suggestions:

1. Move Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to illustrate the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
2. Clearly state the benefits of considering the IG view at the beginning of the paper. This could include explaining how the geometry in the parameter space can be used to discuss the behavior of optimization.
3. Clarify the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN. It appears that the section uses the deformed exponential family to explain the distribution that a deep neural net can represent, but does not directly follow from the f-GAN optimization results.
4. Explain why the phi_l in Theorem 6 can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics." The equation (13) does not seem to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider moving lines 257-269 to a different location or deleting the utility theory part, which seems unrelated to the IG view.
6. Revise the experiments to more directly relate to the main points claimed in the paper. For example, the results in Section 5 could be used to discuss the new activation functions without relying on the IG view of f-GAN.

In summary, while the paper provides many useful results and dense derivations, it could benefit from editing to make the claims clearer and more coherent. With some revisions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
