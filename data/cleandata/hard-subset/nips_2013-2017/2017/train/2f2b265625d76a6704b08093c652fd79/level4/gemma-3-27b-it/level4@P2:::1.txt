This paper investigates the theoretical foundations of f-GANs, extending the GAN principle to encompass f-divergences. Building upon the generalization of the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families via the chi-logarithm, the authors establish a key result – the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity offers a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function, linking it to information geometry. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate a sufficient condition on activation functions that enables the factorization of generative distributions, highlighting the expressive power of deep architectures in this context.

The core theoretical contribution, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and holds considerable potential for practical applications. However, the abstractness of the main theorems and their presentation in highly general terms make it challenging to fully grasp their implications. Clarity could be significantly improved by providing more concrete examples or intuitive explanations alongside the formal derivations. 

The experimental section (Section 6) suffers from a similar lack of connection to the theoretical framework. While the experiments explore the impact of different activation functions and link functions, it’s not immediately apparent how these choices directly relate to, or validate, the theoretical results presented earlier in the paper. A more explicit discussion bridging the theory and experiments is crucial. For instance, clarifying which aspects of the vig-f-GAN identity are being tested and how the experimental results support the theoretical claims would greatly enhance the impact of this section.

Specifically, on page 7, line 258, the phrasing “For eq.(7), is means when C…” is grammatically incorrect and requires revision. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Theoretical Framework:** The vig-f-GAN identity offers a fresh perspective on understanding f-GANs through the lens of information geometry.
*   **Connection to Deep Architectures:** The work establishes a link between deep neural network architectures and the theoretical framework, providing insights into why these networks are effective in generating complex distributions.
*   **Potential for Architectural Improvements:** The sufficient condition on activation functions suggests a principled approach to designing better GAN architectures.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Clarity:** The highly abstract nature of the theoretical results and their presentation hinders understanding.
*   **Weak Connection Between Theory and Experiments:** The experiments do not clearly demonstrate the validity or utility of the theoretical framework.
*   **Grammatical Errors:** Minor grammatical errors detract from the paper’s polish.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a fundamental theoretical question in the field of GANs.
*   The vig-f-GAN identity provides a potentially valuable new tool for analyzing and designing GANs.
*   The work highlights the geometric properties of deep generative models.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The lack of clarity makes it difficult to assess the significance of the theoretical contributions.
*   The limited connection between theory and experiments weakens the empirical support for the claims.
*   The paper might be too theoretical for NIPS, without concrete algorithmic or practical implications that are clearly demonstrated.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a theoretically interesting contribution to the understanding of f-GANs. However, its impact is diminished by a lack of clarity and a weak connection to experimental validation. While the theoretical framework is promising, significant revisions are needed to improve its accessibility and demonstrate its practical relevance. I would recommend rejection in its current form, but encourage the authors to resubmit a revised version addressing the concerns outlined above. A stronger emphasis on clarifying the theoretical results and providing more compelling experimental evidence would significantly strengthen the paper.
