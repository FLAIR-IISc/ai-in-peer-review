Thank you for an engaging and thought-provoking submission.

This paper develops an information-geometric perspective on the f-GAN framework. It first argues that f-GAN can yield convergence in parameter space by leveraging a one-to-one correspondence between f-divergences and χ-divergences together with a Bregman divergence identity. It then clarifies a principled way to instantiate the f-GAN objective. The main text also presents a factorization result for deep-network generators and analyzes activation choices, highlighting a one-to-one correspondence between activations and the χ (or f) function. I did not verify every detail in the supplement, but the arguments I checked appear sound; I did not have time to fully parse Theorem 8 before the deadline.

Overall, this work is dense and packed with novel results that should interest both machine learning and information theory communities. I was particularly impressed by the presentation around Theorem 4, which, in my view, captures the core IG story for f-GAN. I recommend acceptance.

That said, the paper may be hard to digest for readers without a strong background in IT/IG (e.g., the classical KL/Bregman equivalence for exponential families and Fenchel duality). Many deep learning practitioners—likely a primary audience for a GAN paper—may fall into this category. I have several suggestions to improve clarity:

1) I found Figure 1 in the appendix, which maps IT-IG connections within the f-GAN setting, very useful. Consider moving it into the main text.

2) It would help to clearly motivate early on why the IG viewpoint is beneficial. My understanding is that it enables reasoning about optimization via geometry in parameter space; this point is mentioned on page 7 but would be better introduced earlier.

3) I am not entirely clear on how Section 5 ties back to the f-GAN objective. It seems to use deformed exponential families to characterize distributions expressible by deep nets, which is not obviously a consequence of f-GAN optimization. While I appreciate the one-to-one correspondence among v, f, and χ, the paper does not spell out how this informs the design of the f-GAN game—for example, guidance on selecting an f-divergence or, given an activation, which f-GAN objective would yield better convergence.

4) Could you clarify why, in Theorem 6, the φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics”? Equation (13) does not appear to be in deformed exponential-family form.

5) Consider relocating lines 257–269; the utility-theoretic discussion seems orthogonal to the IG narrative. Moving it to the appendix could free space to better explain the main contributions.

6) The experiments feel somewhat decoupled from the central IG claims. Point (A) could be supported purely from Section 5 without invoking the IG perspective. Similarly, point (B) largely relies on Section 4, which is only loosely tied to the IG view. Including WGAN results is potentially distracting for a paper focused on f-GANs; I had to hunt for the brief justification (lines 227–228).

In summary, while the paper offers valuable results and careful derivations, the organization could be tighter. It currently reads like multiple contributions compressed into an 8-page submission. I support acceptance, but recommend revisions to make the claims crisper and the narrative more coherent.
