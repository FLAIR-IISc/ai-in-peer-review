- Proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, showing convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and χ-divergence and related Bregman-divergence results.  
- Presents a principled method for implementing f-GAN.  
- Provides a factorization of learned deep network representations and discusses activation functions that map one-to-one to χ (or f) functions.  
- Reviewer checked many proofs and found them correct, though Theorem 8 was not examined due to time.  
- Paper is dense, contains several novel results of interest to ML and information-theory communities; recommendation: accept.  
- Theorem 4’s exposition is especially clear and illuminating for the IG interpretation of f-GAN.  
- Paper may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG concepts (e.g., KL–Bregman links, Fenchel duality), including many deep-learning practitioners.  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 (currently in appendix) into the main paper because it clarifies IT–IG connections.  
- Recommend stating early why the IG perspective is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior in parameter space).  
- Connection between Section 5 and the f-GAN framework is unclear; deformed exponential family seems to describe representable distributions rather than follow from f-GAN results, so practical guidance (choosing f-divergence or objective) is lacking.  
- Question why functions φ_l in Theorem 6 are labeled “deep sufficient statistics,” as Eq. (13) doesn’t appear to match deformed exponential-family structure.  
- The motivation/utility-theory discussion is tangential and should be moved to the appendix to free space for core IG arguments.  
- Experimental links to theory feel tenuous: Experiment A’s activations follow from Section 5 without needing full IG; Experiment B uses Section 4 implementation details not tightly tied to IG; including WGAN results is potentially confusing.  
- Overall organization and narrative need improvement—results from multiple lines of inquiry feel condensed together; urge revision for clarity and explicit connections among theoretical and empirical contributions.
