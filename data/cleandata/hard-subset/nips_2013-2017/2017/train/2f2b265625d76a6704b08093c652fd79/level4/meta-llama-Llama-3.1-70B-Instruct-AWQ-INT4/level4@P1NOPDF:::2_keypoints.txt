- Paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, connecting information theory and information geometry.  
- Shows f-GAN convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and a Bregman divergence result.  
- Presents a recommended practical implementation for f-GAN.  
- Provides a factorisation result for deep neural network representations and links activation functions 1-1 to the chi (or f) function.  
- Appendix proofs appear correct overall (Thm. 8 not checked by reviewer).  
- Thm. 4 exposition effectively conveys the IG interpretation and is a highlight of the paper.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but finds the paper very dense and potentially hard for readers without IT/IG background.  
- Suggest moving Fig. 1 (illustrating IT–IG connections) into the main text for clarity.  
- Recommend stating early why the IG view is beneficial, especially regarding optimization behavior.  
- Unclear how Section 5 ties to f-GAN; it seems to describe representable distributions rather than consequences of f-GAN optimization.  
- Authors should explain how the 1-1 correspondence (v, f, chi) informs design choices for f-GAN objectives or activation functions.  
- Request clarification on why phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” in Thm. 6, since Eq. (13) doesn’t resemble a deformed exponential family.  
- Suggest relocating or removing the utility theory paragraph (lines 257–269) as it seems unrelated to the IG view.  
- Experiments seem only loosely connected to the main theoretical claims; inclusion of WGAN results is distracting and confusing.  
- Overall organization feels like multiple results squeezed into one paper; paper needs editing to clarify claims and improve coherence.
