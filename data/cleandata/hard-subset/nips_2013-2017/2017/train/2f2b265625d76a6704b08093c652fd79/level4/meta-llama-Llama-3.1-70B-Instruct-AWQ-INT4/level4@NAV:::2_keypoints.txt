- Paper presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs and is appreciated by the reviewer.  
- Main theoretical contribution: shows f-GAN convergence in parameter space using a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence plus a Bregman divergence result.  
- Provides implementation guidance and a factorization result linking neural network representation to a 1-1 mapping between activation functions and chi/f functions.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not reviewed by the reviewer.  
- Theorem 4 is well-exposed and effectively communicates the IG viewpoint for f-GAN.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on novelty and interest to ML and information theory communities.  
- Concern: paper is dense and may be hard to follow for readers without IT/IG background; intended audience (deep learning engineers) may struggle.  
- Suggestion: move Figure 1 from appendix to main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.  
- Suggestion: add a clear early statement of the benefits of the IG view (e.g., geometry-informed optimization behavior).  
- Clarify how Section 5 (deformed exponential family) connects to f-GAN optimization and how the v–f–chi correspondence informs GAN design choices.  
- Explain why phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6, since equation (13) doesn’t resemble a deformed exponential family.  
- Recommend relocating or removing the utility theory discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it seems unrelated to the IG focus.  
- Concern: experiments are tangential to main claims; Section 5 results can stand alone and WGAN results may distract from the f-GAN focus.  
- Overall: work is valuable but reads like multiple condensed papers; reorganize and clarify claims/coherence before final version.
