- Paper offers novel theoretical links between GANs, information geometry, and deformed exponential families, including a variational f-divergence/Bregman generalization.  
- Shows that under reversibility, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions and suggests activation/link choice matters.  
- Provides an alternative expected-utility-theory interpretation of some results.  
- Core theorems assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family; the practical validity and necessity of this restrictive assumption are not justified.  
- The benefit of the parameter-space (information-geometric f-GAN) reformulation is unclear—authors must explain concrete gains and possible novel implementations.  
- No experimental comparison between distribution-based and parameter-based optimization; need evidence that parameter convergence follows from distribution convergence (or a counterexample).  
- Empirical results contradict theory: reversible activations (μ-ReLU) perform worse than ReLU in Table 2 (left), requiring explanation and verification of the reversibility assumption for tested architectures.  
- Need discussion of DCGAN compatibility with reversibility and comparison to WGAN, which lies outside the f-divergence framework.  
- Many reported differences in Table 2 are within error bars and not statistically significant, undermining practical claims about activation/link advantages.  
- Minor: paper assumes familiarity with deformed exponential families—add intuitive explanations of "deformation," "signature," and "escort."  
- Minor: clarify tightness and practical implications of Theorem 8 bounds on J(Q), and explain why decreasing J(Q) with escort normalization Z is beneficial and actionable.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but limited practical impact due to restrictive assumptions, lack of implementation pathways/experiments, and contradictory empirical findings; substantial revisions required.
