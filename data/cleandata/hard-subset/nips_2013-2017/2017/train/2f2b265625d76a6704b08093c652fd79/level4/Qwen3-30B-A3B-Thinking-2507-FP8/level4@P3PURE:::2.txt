

Thank you for the opportunity to review this paper. The work presents an information geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN algorithm, establishing several novel theoretical contributions. The paper first demonstrates convergence of the f-GAN algorithm in parameter space through a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, combined with a Bregman divergence result. It then provides practical implementation guidance and derives a factorization of deep neural network representations in the main text, linking activation functions to the chi-divergence (or f-divergence) function via a one-to-one correspondence. I have not verified all appendix details, but the proofs (excluding Theorem 8, which I have not reviewed) appear correct.  

This paper is dense with significant new results of interest to machine learning and information theory. The exposition of Theorem 4, which elucidates the IG foundation for f-GAN, is particularly compelling. Consequently, I support a strong accept.  

However, the paper's accessibility for its primary audience—deep learning practitioners—requires substantial improvement. The assumed background in information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), including basic connections like the mapping between KL divergence for exponential families and Bregman divergence, and Fenchel duality, will hinder comprehension. To enhance clarity, I recommend:  

1. Moving Figure 1 (from the appendix) to the main text to explicitly illustrate IT-IG connections within the f-GAN framework.  
2. Adding a clear early statement (e.g., in the introduction) explaining *why* the IG perspective is useful—specifically, to analyze optimization behavior in parameter space. The current mention on page 7 is too late.  
3. Clarifying Section 5’s relevance to f-GAN. The section uses the deformed exponential family to characterize deep network representations, but does not demonstrate how this enables f-GAN design (e.g., selecting optimal f-divergences or activation functions for convergence). The one-to-one correspondence between v, f, and χ is noted, but its practical implications for f-GAN are unexplored.  
4. Justifying why the functions φₗ in Theorem 6 can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics." Equation (13) does not conform to the standard deformed exponential family form, requiring explicit clarification.  
5. Relocating lines 257–269 to a more appropriate section (e.g., the appendix) and removing the utility theory paragraph (retaining it in the appendix). This would free space to elaborate on core contributions.  
6. Realigning the experiments with the paper’s central claims. For instance:  
   - (A) The activation function results (Section 5) do not require the IG framework to be applied.  
   - (B) Section 4’s content (e.g., activation functions) is only tangentially related to the IG view of f-GAN.  
   - The WGAN results (noted only on lines 227–228) are distracting, as the paper focuses on f-GANs, and I spent unnecessary time locating this brief mention.  

In summary, while the paper delivers valuable theoretical insights and rigorous derivations, the material is currently organized in a manner that obscures the core narrative. It appears to consolidate results from multiple works into an 8-page NIPS submission without sufficient narrative cohesion. I strongly support acceptance but emphasize that the paper requires significant editing to clarify claims, strengthen the IG narrative, and ensure experiments directly support the stated contributions.
