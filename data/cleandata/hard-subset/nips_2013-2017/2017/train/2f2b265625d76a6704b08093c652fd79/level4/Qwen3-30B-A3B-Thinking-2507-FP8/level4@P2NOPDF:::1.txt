

This paper extends the f-GAN framework to general f-divergences, establishing a novel variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity through a generalized connection between f-divergences and exponential families using the log-chi transform. The authors demonstrate that this identity provides a theoretical foundation for the f-GAN objective function and derive a sufficient condition on activation functions for achieving factored generative distributions.  

The core theoretical contribution—the vig-f-GAN identity—is mathematically significant and holds potential for advancing divergence-based generative modeling. However, the presentation of key theorems suffers from excessive abstraction, obscuring their practical interpretation and limiting accessibility. The proofs and statements are formulated at a high level of generality, making it difficult to discern concrete implications or connections to established results in the literature. For instance, the conditions under which the identity simplifies to known cases (e.g., KL divergence) are not explicitly clarified, hindering the reader's ability to assess its novelty or utility relative to existing work.  

Section 6 presents experimental results that lack clear alignment with the theoretical claims. The authors report performance gains on benchmark datasets but fail to explicitly link these observations to the vig-f-GAN identity or the activation function condition. Without demonstrating how the experiments validate the theoretical insights—such as showing that the proposed activation constraints improve model expressivity or convergence—the empirical section feels disconnected from the paper's main contribution. This disconnect weakens the overall argument and leaves readers questioning the practical relevance of the proposed framework.  

Additionally, several grammatical and typographical issues impede clarity. On page 7, line 258, the phrase "For eq.(7), is means when C …" contains a structural error; it should read "For Eq. (7), does this mean when C …" or similar. Such errors, while minor, compound the difficulty in following the technical exposition.  

In summary, the paper identifies a promising theoretical direction with the vig-f-GAN identity, but its impact is undermined by overly abstract formulations and insufficient experimental justification. To strengthen the work, the authors should: (1) simplify the presentation of key theorems with concrete examples or special-case analyses; (2) explicitly bridge experimental results to theoretical claims (e.g., by showing how activation constraints manifest in model behavior); and (3) correct grammatical errors and refine ambiguous phrasing. Addressing these points would significantly enhance the paper's clarity and contribution to the field.
