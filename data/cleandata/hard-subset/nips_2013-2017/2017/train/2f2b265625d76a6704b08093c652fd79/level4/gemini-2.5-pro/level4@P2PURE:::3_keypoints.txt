1. Paper poses key questions about whether GAN optimization implies parameter convergence, what distribution the generator fits at convergence, and the optimal discriminator output activation for a proper composite loss.

2. Authors use the information-theoretic framework of deformed exponential distributions to derive theoretical results.

3. They present a variational generalization compatible with f-GANs linking f-divergence between distributions to Bregman divergence between parameters.

4. Under a reversibility assumption, they show deep generative networks can be factored as "escorts" of deformed exponential families.

5. The theory suggests careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation can improve GAN performance.

6. The paper briefly offers an alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.

7. Reviewer’s main concern: practical relevance and immediate utility of the theoretical results are unclear.

8. Major issue: several theorems (e.g., 4 and 6) assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family; authors must justify or relax this assumption.

9. The practical benefit of the parameter-space f-GAN identity (Eq. 7) is unclear and lacks an experimental comparison to standard distributional optimization.

10. Authors should provide a realistic scenario where convergence in distribution does not imply convergence in parameter space to illustrate the identity’s significance.

11. The derived discriminator activation gf is theoretically important but lacks experimental validation versus heuristic choices (Ref. [34]).

12. Experimental inconsistency: invertible μ-ReLU performs worse than baseline ReLU in GAN MLP despite theory predicting superiority; requires explanation.

13. It’s unclear whether tested DCGAN architectures satisfy the reversibility assumption; relation to Wasserstein GANs should be discussed.

14. Many empirical comparisons (Table 2 center/right) fall within error bars and are statistically inconclusive, weakening practical claims.

15. Minor: the deformed exponential framework needs more intuition for terms like "deformation," "signature," and "escort" for broader ML audiences.

16. Minor: Theorem 8 presents multiple bounds on J(Q) whose relative comparison and implications for activation selection are unclear; need clarification on the role of the escort normalization Z.

17. Overall summary: interesting theoretical contributions but insufficient empirical support and unclear practical guidance for GAN practitioners.
