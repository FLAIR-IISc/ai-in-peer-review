- Paper asks whether solving the GAN objective implies parameter convergence, what generators fit at convergence, and how discriminator output activations should be chosen for compositeness.
- The authors derive a variational generalization connecting f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters, bridging information-theoretic and information-geometric views.
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generative networks factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Analysis suggests choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations carefully could improve GANs.
- They briefly propose an alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical results interesting but questions their practical relevance and usefulness in current form.
- Major concern: many results assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family—authors must justify when this holds in practice and whether results generalize beyond it.
- The information-geometric f-GAN identity (Eq. 7) needs justification of concrete advantages, feasibility of parameter-based GAN optimization, and experimental comparison to distribution-based optimization.
- Reviewer asks why the implication from variational f-divergence convergence to parameter convergence is nontrivial and requests a realistic counterexample where distributional convergence fails to imply parameter convergence.
- Practical recommendation about discriminator output activation g_f lacks experimental validation comparing the theoretically derived g_f to existing heuristics.
- Empirical inconsistency: theoretically motivated μ-ReLU underperforms standard ReLU in GAN MLPs, contrary to expectations from the reversibility assumption.
- Reviewer requests clarification whether common architectures like DCGAN satisfy the reversibility assumption and how findings relate to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN.
- Reported performance differences across activations and link functions appear within error bars, weakening practical support for the theory.
- Minor request: provide more intuition on deformations/signatures and escorts for broader ML readership.
- In Theorem 8, reviewer asks for comparison of the upper bounds on J(Q), implications for activation choice, and practical exploitation of the bound’s dependence on escort normalization Z.
- Overall conclusion: reviewer does not find the practical impact of the theoretical analysis substantial for GAN practitioners.
