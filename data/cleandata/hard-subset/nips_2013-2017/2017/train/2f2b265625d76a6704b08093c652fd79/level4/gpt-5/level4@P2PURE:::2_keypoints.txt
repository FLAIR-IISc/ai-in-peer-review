1. Presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, establishing convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman divergence argument.  
2. Proposes a recommended practical implementation of f-GAN.  
3. Provides a factorization result for deep neural network representations and analyzes activation functions, showing a one-to-one correspondence with the chi (or f) function.  
4. Most proofs appear correct; reviewer did not check every appendix detail and did not read Theorem 8.  
5. Theorem 4’s exposition clearly articulates the IG aspect and is a highlight of the paper.  
6. Paper is dense with many new results likely of interest to machine learning and information theory communities; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
7. Paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with information theory/geometry (KL-to-Bregman reduction, Fenchel duality role).  
8. Suggest moving Figure 1 (IT–IG summary) from appendix to main text for clarity.  
9. Recommend stating earlier why the IG view is useful (e.g., how parameter-space geometry elucidates optimization), since this motivation appears too late.  
10. Section 5’s connection to f-GAN is unclear: it characterizes representable distributions via deformed exponential families but doesn’t explain how this informs f-GAN design or choice of f-divergence/activation.  
11. Question Theorem 6’s claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Equation (13) does not look like a deformed exponential family form.  
12. Suggest relocating/removing the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257–269) from the main text to free space for main results.  
13. Experiments are not tightly linked to central claims: activation-function results (A) need only Section 5, IG view not required; convergence results (B) rely mainly on Section 4; WGAN results are distracting/confusing.  
14. Overall presentation is not optimally organized—reads like compressed material from multiple papers—and needs significant editing for coherence.
