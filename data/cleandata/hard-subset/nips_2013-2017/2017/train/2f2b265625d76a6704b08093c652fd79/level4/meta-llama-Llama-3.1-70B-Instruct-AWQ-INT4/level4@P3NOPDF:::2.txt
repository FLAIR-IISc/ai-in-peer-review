I appreciate the opportunity to review this paper, which proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm. The authors demonstrate that f-GAN converges in parameter space using a 1-1 mapping of f-divergence and chi-divergence, and a Bregman divergence result. They also discuss a proper way to implement f-GAN and provide a factorization result of the deep neural network representation, highlighting the choice of activation functions that have a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function. Although I did not verify every detail in the appendix, the proofs appear to be correct, with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to thoroughly review.

I believe this paper is dense and contains numerous new results that could be of interest to the machine learning and information theory communities. The exposition of Theorem 4, in particular, effectively conveys the IG component of the f-GAN story. Therefore, I think this paper is a clear accept.

However, I also think that this paper may be challenging for readers without a strong background in the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), particularly the simplest mappings, such as the KL divergence for an exponential family to Bregman divergence, and the Fenchel duality concept. Many deep learning engineers may fall into this category, and as they are a primary audience for GAN papers, I offer the following suggestions to improve the paper's clarity:

1. Consider moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text, as it effectively illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
2. Provide a clear statement at the beginning of the paper explaining why considering the IG view is beneficial. My understanding is that the geometry in the parameter space can be used to discuss the behavior of optimization. This point is mentioned briefly on page 7, but it may be more effective to introduce it earlier.
3. I am unclear about how Section 5 connects to f-GAN. It appears that the deformed exponential family is used to explain the distribution that a deep neural net can represent, rather than being a consequence of f-GAN optimization results. While I understand that v, f, and chi have a 1-1 correspondence, it is not clear how this result can help design the f-GAN game, such as choosing the f-divergence or selecting the f-GAN objective that works best in terms of convergence.
4. I am unsure why φ_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics" in Theorem 6. Equation (13) does not appear to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider relocating lines 257-269 to a different section. Additionally, the utility theory component does not seem directly related to the IG view, so it may be beneficial to remove that paragraph (and potentially include it in the appendix) to free up space to explain the main results.
6. I feel that the experiments are not directly related to the main points claimed in the paper. For example, the results from Section 5 can be discussed independently of the IG view of f-GAN, and the experiments in Section 4 are not closely related to the IG view of f-GAN. The inclusion of WGAN results may be distracting and confusing, as this paper primarily focuses on f-GANs.

In summary, while this paper provides numerous useful results and dense derivations, I believe that the material could be organized more clearly. The paper appears to be a compilation of results from multiple papers, condensed into an 8-page NIPS submission. Therefore, while I support the acceptance of this paper, I think it requires editing to clarify and cohere the claims.
