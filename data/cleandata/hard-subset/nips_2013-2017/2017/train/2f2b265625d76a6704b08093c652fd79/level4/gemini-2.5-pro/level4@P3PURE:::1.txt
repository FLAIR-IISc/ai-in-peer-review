This paper presents a generalization of the f-GAN framework using f-divergences. By employing the chi-logarithm to extend the known connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families, the authors derive the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity offers a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. The authors also prove a sufficient condition on activation functions for obtaining factored generative distributions.

The central theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and its potential applications are promising. However, the paper's clarity could be significantly improved. The main theorems are stated in a highly general form, which makes their meaning and implications difficult to parse.

This lack of clarity extends to the experimental evaluation in Section 6. The connection between the empirical observations and the theoretical results is not clearly explained, making it difficult for the reader to understand how the experiments support or illustrate the paper's theoretical contributions.
