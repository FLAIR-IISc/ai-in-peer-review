Summary and relation to prior work
This paper develops an information-geometric perspective on f-GANs. Building on Nowozin et al. (2016), the authors show that, under mild conditions, f-divergences can be represented as KLχ-divergences, and connect these to Bregman divergences between the natural parameters of deformed (χ-)exponential families (Amari; Naudts). The central result (Corollary 8) provides a variational identity equating the f-GAN objective to a Bregman divergence DC(θ‖ϑ) between parameters plus a penalty term J(Q) that depends only on the generator distribution via its escort. This yields an interpretation of f-GAN training as parameter-space optimization akin to geometric optimization perspectives used for Wasserstein GANs.

The paper also completes the supervised part of the f-GAN game by recasting the discriminator objective as a proper composite loss (Reid & Williamson), thereby clarifying the role of the “output activation” as a composition of the generator f and the link Ψ. On the generator side, the authors prove that standard deep architectures factorize escorts of deformed exponential families: inner layers correspond to sufficient statistics, rows of weight matrices to coordinates, and activation choices determine the family’s signature χ. A sufficient condition on activations (strong admissibility) is given and shown to be met by popular choices (e.g., ELU, Softplus; ReLU in a limiting sense). Experiments illustrate that tuning activations and discriminator links can provide performance gains.

Strengths
- Theoretical contribution: The variational information-geometric identity for f-GANs over deformed exponential families is novel and insightful. It generalizes the classical KL/Bregman identity for exponential families to a much broader setting and ties the variational f-GAN objective to parameter-space geometry.
- Clarification of the discriminator: Framing the discriminator as a proper composite loss clarifies the previously “somewhat arbitrary” choice of output activation in f-GAN and provides actionable guidance on link functions.
- Generator expressivity: The factorization result for deep networks establishes a principled connection between network architecture, activation functions, and the χ-signature of deformed exponential families, offering a compact expressivity argument for deep generators.
- Breadth: The paper weaves information theory, information geometry, and modern GAN practice in a coherent framework that could influence how future GAN objectives and architectures are designed.

Weaknesses and suggestions for improvement
- Clarity and accessibility: The paper is very dense and assumes familiarity with both information geometry (e.g., KL/Bregman identities) and convex duality. This will be challenging for many readers in the GAN community.
  • Suggestion: Move the summary figure from the appendix (illustrating the IT–IG connections) to the main text, and include an early high-level roadmap explaining why an IG view helps (e.g., it yields parameter-space convergence and principled design knobs for generator/discriminator).
- Linking sections and claims: The connection between the deep factorization (Section 5 in the draft) and the f-GAN optimization is not always explicit.
  • Suggestion: Clearly state how the factorization result and the activation–χ mapping feed back into the choice of f-divergence and training dynamics. For instance, offer guidance on selecting f given a chosen activation (or vice versa), and discuss whether the IG identity suggests specific combinations with better convergence properties.
- “Deep sufficient statistics” interpretation: The claim that φl are “deep sufficient statistics” deserves more intuition and a short proof sketch in the main text.
  • Suggestion: Explicitly show how rows of Wl define coordinates and φl define statistics in the χ-exponential family, and explain how the escort factorization in Theorem 9 implies sufficiency in the deformed family sense.
- Assumptions and scope: The invertibility assumption for all layers and the escort penalty J(Q) are strong and may limit practical applicability.
  • Suggestion: Discuss to what extent approximate invertibility (e.g., typical DCGAN components) suffices, and whether the penalty J(Q) can be estimated/controlled in practice. Provide practical checks or diagnostics indicating when the IG interpretation meaningfully applies.
- Organization and focus: Some content (e.g., the brief utility theory paragraph) feels tangential and could be moved to the supplement.
  • Suggestion: Reallocate space to expand the main identity (Corollary 8), include a concise proof sketch, and provide worked examples (e.g., for the original GAN f and a χ induced by a standard activation).
- Experiments: The empirical section, while suggestive, does not tightly validate the theoretical story. In particular, results on WGAN are tangential, and the experiments do not directly test implications of the IG identity (e.g., parameter-space convergence or the role of J(Q)).
  • Suggestion: (i) Demonstrate a case where the deformed exponential family modeling assumption is plausible and monitor DC(θ‖ϑ) during training; (ii) compare f choices matched/mismatched to activations as predicted by the χ mapping; (iii) either remove or clearly motivate the WGAN results to avoid confusing the scope.

Evaluation against review criteria
- Quality: The technical development appears sound and carefully grounded in prior work. While I did not verify every proof in the supplement, the main arguments are convincing. Please consider adding small proof sketches (especially for the central identity) and more discussion of assumptions.
- Clarity: The writing is precise but dense; onboarding readers earlier with intuition, a roadmap figure, and simplified special cases would greatly improve accessibility.
- Originality: The variational IG identity for f-GANs over deformed families, the proper-composite reinterpretation of the discriminator, and the deep escort factorization are, to my knowledge, new and nontrivial.
- Significance: The work offers a unifying lens on GAN training with concrete design implications for activations and discriminator links. If clarified and accompanied by tighter empirical validation, it could have substantial impact.

Arguments for acceptance
- Strong and novel theoretical bridge between f-divergences, KLχ, and Bregman geometry for deformed exponential families.
- Clarifies and systematizes the discriminator design via proper composite losses.
- Offers principled guidance for generator architecture and activation choices, with some empirical support.

Arguments against acceptance
- Presentation is challenging for a broad NIPS audience; key intuitions and connections are sometimes deferred to the supplement.
- Practical implications are not fully validated; experiments are somewhat decoupled from the main theoretical claims and mix in WGAN results.

Recommendation
Accept, contingent on a revision focused on clarity and tighter linkage between theory and practice. The contributions are substantive and timely, and with improved exposition and targeted experiments, the paper could be highly influential.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide explicit guidance (table or recipe) mapping common activations to χ and corresponding f choices expected to work best?
- How sensitive is the theory to non-invertible layers in practice? Are there approximate conditions under which the factorization and IG interpretation remain meaningful?
- Is there a way to estimate or control J(Q) during training, or to detect when it dominates?
- Can you include a simple synthetic experiment demonstrating parameter-space convergence (via DC) predicted by Corollary 8?
