- Presents an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, proving convergence in parameter space using the f-divergence ↔ chi-divergence mapping and a Bregman-divergence result.
- Provides a principled implementation and a factorization result linking deep network activation choices to corresponding chi (or f) functions.
- Appendix proofs generally appear correct; Theorem 8 was not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is dense with numerous novel results valuable to machine learning and information theory; Theorem 4’s IG exposition is particularly strong.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance despite concerns about readability and accessibility.
- Main concern: heavy reliance on specialized IT/IG knowledge may impede understanding for typical deep learning practitioners.
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.
- Recommend stating the benefits of the IG viewpoint earlier (e.g., its ability to analyze optimization behavior in parameter space).
- Ask to clarify how Section 5 connects to f-GAN: how the v–f–chi correspondence should guide f-GAN design and choice of objective for given activations.
- Request explanation for treating phi_l as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6, since Eq. (13) seems inconsistent with a deformed exponential family form.
- Advise re-evaluating placement of lines 257–269 and consider moving the utility-theory discussion to the appendix as it feels tangential.
- Critique experiments: they do not clearly support the central IG claims; Section 5 (activations) and Section 4 (convergence) results appear independent of the IG framing, and WGAN results are potentially distracting.
- Overall organization feels disjointed—reads like multiple investigations condensed into one paper—suggest substantial editing to improve clarity and coherence.
