This paper explores the generalization of the f-GAN principle to f-divergences. By leveraging the chi-logarithm to extend the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families, the authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity provides a meaningful interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Additionally, the authors derive a sufficient condition on activation functions that enables the construction of factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and shows promise for potential applications. However, the presentation of the main theorems in quite general forms makes their meanings challenging to grasp.

Section 6 is particularly difficult to follow, as it is unclear how the experimental observations relate to the theoretical results. A clearer connection between the empirical findings and the underlying theory would strengthen the paper.

On page 7, line 258, the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C ..." is grammatically incorrect and should be revised for clarity.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting contribution to the field, but could benefit from a more accessible presentation of its theoretical results and a clearer explanation of the experimental findings.
