- Proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, proving convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman divergence result.  
- Discusses proper implementation details for f-GAN and provides a factorization result for deep network representations with a one-to-one mapping between activations and the chi (or f) function.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not checked by the reviewer.  
- Paper is dense with many new results of interest to ML and information theory communities; Theorem 4 exposition is particularly strong.  
- Reviewer gives a clear accept recommendation.  
- Paper may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG concepts (e.g., KL-to-Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality), potentially limiting accessibility to deep learning practitioners.  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to summarize IT–IG connections.  
- Recommend an upfront statement explaining why the IG view is helpful (e.g., analyzing optimization behavior via parameter-space geometry).  
- Clarify how Section 5 connects to f-GAN—whether it characterizes representable distributions or provides actionable guidance for choosing f-divergences/activations in the f-GAN game.  
- Explain why the functions φ_l in Theorem 6 are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Equation (13) does not obviously match a deformed exponential family form.  
- Move or revise lines 257–269 and remove or relocate the utility-theory paragraph from the main text to improve focus.  
- Experiments are not tightly aligned with main claims: activation-function results relate to Section 5, Section 4 suffices for other claims, and the WGAN results feel distracting/confusing.  
- Overall: valuable and well-developed results but presentation reads like multiple papers compressed into one; recommend revision for clearer focus and coherence.
