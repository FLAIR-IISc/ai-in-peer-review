This paper studies the f-GAN framework generalized to f-divergences. By extending the classical connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families using the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information-geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which offers a principled interpretation of the f-GAN objective. They also provide a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical result—the vig-f-GAN identity—is interesting, and its potential applicability is promising. However, the presentation is difficult to follow because the theorems are stated in very general forms with abstract assumptions and notation. This makes it hard to see the concrete implications, the precise scope of validity, and how one would apply the results in practical settings.

Specific comments:

- Clarity of theorems: Please restate the central results in more accessible forms, ideally with:
  - Concrete special cases (e.g., recovering KL and JS as f-divergences, or standard exponential-family instances).
  - Explicit assumptions and minimal conditions (e.g., smoothness, convexity, regularity) required for the identity to hold.
  - A short interpretation of each term in the identity and how it relates to the original f-GAN objective.

- Role of the chi-logarithm: The bridge from KL/exponential families to general f-divergences via the chi-logarithm is a compelling idea, but the narrative could better motivate why the chi-logarithm is the right tool and how it compares to other possible generalizations. A brief intuitive explanation and a simple worked example would help.

- Sufficient condition for factored generative distributions: The result on activation functions leading to factored generative distributions is potentially useful, but the statement would benefit from:
  - Clarifying the exact factorization claimed (e.g., product structure across dimensions or components).
  - A simple example of an activation function that satisfies the condition and one that does not, with a short explanation.
  - Discussion of implications: does this restrict expressivity, or does it facilitate optimization or identifiability?

- Section 6 (Experiments): It is difficult to see how the empirical observations connect to the theoretical results.
  - Please make explicit which theorem or corollary each experiment is intended to validate or illustrate.
  - Indicate which quantities in the plots correspond to terms in the vig-f-GAN identity.
  - If the experiments are exploratory or illustrative rather than validating, state this clearly and explain what qualitative behavior they are meant to demonstrate.

- Presentation and readability:
  - Reduce the level of abstraction where possible; provide concrete instances and intuitive explanations alongside formal statements.
  - Summarize key takeaways at the end of sections that introduce major technical machinery (e.g., after presenting the vig-f-GAN identity).
  - Consider adding a “recipe” that shows how to use the identity to derive or analyze a practical f-GAN objective.

- Minor issue:
  - Page 7, line 258: “For eq.(7), is means when C …” has an incorrect clause structure. Suggested fix: “For Eq. (7), this means that when C …” (adjust the rest of the sentence accordingly). Also ensure consistent capitalization and spacing around equation references (e.g., “Eq. (7)”).

Overall assessment: The paper contains a promising theoretical contribution that could clarify the geometry and variational structure underlying f-GANs. However, the presentation in its current form is too abstract, and the connection between theory and experiments is insufficiently clear. Making the results more concrete, tightening the link to empirical sections, and improving exposition would significantly strengthen the paper.
