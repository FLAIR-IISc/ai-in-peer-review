

Thank you for this interesting contribution.  
This paper introduces an information geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN algorithm. It establishes convergence in parameter space for f-GAN using a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, alongside a Bregman divergence result. The work also proposes a practical implementation approach for f-GAN. In the main text, it presents a factorization result for deep neural network representations and discusses activation functions with a one-to-one correspondence to the chi (or f) function. While I did not verify every detail in the appendix, the proofs (excluding Theorem 8, which I did not review due to time constraints) appear correct.  

This paper is dense with novel results that could significantly interest researchers in machine learning and information theory. I am particularly impressed by Theorem 4, which elegantly articulates the IG perspective for f-GAN. Thus, I strongly support acceptance.  

However, the paper may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), especially foundational concepts like the mapping of KL divergence for exponential families to Bregman divergence and Fenchel duality. Given that deep learning engineers—often the primary audience for GAN papers—may lack this background, I offer the following suggestions to enhance clarity:  

1.  Figure 1 in the appendix effectively illustrates IT-IG connections in the f-GAN context; consider moving it to the main text.  
2.  Clearly state the utility of the IG perspective early on (e.g., leveraging parameter space geometry to analyze optimization behavior). The brief mention on page 7 is too late.  
3.  Section 5 appears to be a general discussion of deep neural network representations via deformed exponential families, not a direct consequence of f-GAN optimization. While the one-to-one correspondence between *v*, *f*, and chi is valid, the section does not explain how this informs f-GAN design—e.g., which f-divergence to choose or how activation functions affect convergence.  
4.  The justification for viewing $\phi_l$ as "deep sufficient statistics" in Theorem 6 is unclear. Equation (13) does not appear to conform to a deformed exponential family form.  
5.  Move lines 257–269 to another section (or delete them). The utility theory discussion is tangential to the IG perspective and should be relocated to the appendix to free space for core contributions.  
6.  The experiments lack clear alignment with the paper’s central claims. For instance:  
    -   Result (A) could be discussed using only Section 5’s findings (the IG view of f-GAN is not required to apply the new activation functions).  
    -   Result (B) relies primarily on Section 4, which is only loosely connected to the IG perspective of f-GAN.  
    -   The WGAN results (lines 227–228) are distracting, as the paper focuses on f-GANs; their mention required additional searching.  

In summary, while the paper delivers valuable results and rigorous derivations, its organization lacks clarity. The material appears to be a compressed collection of findings from multiple works, rather than a cohesive narrative. Although I support acceptance, the paper requires substantial editing to sharpen its argument flow and ensure claims are consistently aligned with the IG perspective.
