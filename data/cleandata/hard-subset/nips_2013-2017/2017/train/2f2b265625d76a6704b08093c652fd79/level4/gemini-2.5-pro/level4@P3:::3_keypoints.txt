- Connects f-GAN variational objective to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families via a "variational information-geometric f-GAN identity," implying parameter-space convergence plus a penalty term.  
- Analyzes deep generators under a reversibility assumption, showing they factorize as "escorts" and motivating "strongly admissible" activations (e.g., Softplus, ELU).  
- Formalizes the discriminator's output activation through proper composite losses: determined by the f-divergence derivative composed with a link function.  
- Technical derivations are solid and present a novel, original information-geometric interpretation of GANs and generator architectures.  
- Core assumption that data and model distributions belong to the same deformed exponential family is strong and not justified for complex, high-dimensional data.  
- Experimental results are largely inconclusive, with many differences within error bars and some results contradicting theoretical predictions.  
- Specific empirical contradiction: µ-ReLU (theoretically aligned invertible activation) performs worse than non-invertible ReLU in an MLP GAN experiment without explanation.  
- The practical implications of Corollary 8 (parameter-space convergence) are not made clear nor leveraged for optimization or algorithm design.  
- The formalization of discriminator outputs via proper composite losses is an elegant and valuable contribution.  
- Paper is written for readers familiar with information geometry; key concepts (deformed exponential families, signatures, escorts) lack intuitive explanations.  
- Experiments include WGAN despite the theory being f-divergence based, leaving the experimental-theory connection unclear.  
- The reversibility assumption for generators is unrealistic for common architectures (e.g., DCGAN with strided convolutions) and the impact of violating it is not discussed.  
- The work is significant theoretically and could inspire future research, but its immediate practical relevance is limited by weak experimental validation.  
- Suggested improvements include justifying the deformed exponential family assumption, providing intuition for core concepts, clarifying practical gains, and comparing theory-derived output functions directly to heuristics.  
- Reviewer questions: request examples where variational divergence convergence does not imply parameter convergence, clarification on WGAN applicability, and discussion of the reversibility assumption's realism.  
- Overall conclusion: fascinating, original theoretical advance but falls short in experimental support and practical applicability — gap between elegant theory and demonstrable benefits.
