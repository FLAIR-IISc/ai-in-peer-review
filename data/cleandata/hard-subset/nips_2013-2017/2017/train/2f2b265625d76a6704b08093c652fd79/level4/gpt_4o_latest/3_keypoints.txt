- Paper explores GAN convergence in parameter space, what generators approximate at convergence, and how discriminator output activations affect proper compositeness of the loss.  
- Uses an information-theoretic framework based on deformed exponential distributions to derive theoretical insights.  
- Proposes a variational generalization (within the f-GAN framework) linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between their parameters.  
- Shows that, under a reversibility assumption, deep generators decompose into escorts of deformed exponential distributions.  
- Suggests that choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations carefully could improve GAN performance and offers an expected utility interpretation of GANs.  
- Major concern: many results critically rely on assuming data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family—practical validity and possible extensions without this assumption are not justified.  
- Key identity (Eq. 7) linking variational f-divergence to a parameter optimization problem lacks discussion of practical advantages, implementability, and experimental comparison to distribution-level optimization.  
- It is unclear why solving the variational GAN game does not straightforwardly imply parameter-space convergence; authors should present realistic counterexamples or clarify the implication.  
- Theoretical prescription for discriminator output activation (gf) is interesting but lacks experimental comparison against heuristic activations from prior work (Ref. [34]).  
- Reversibility assumption issues: empirically mu-ReLU (invertible) underperforms ReLU; authors must explain this discrepancy and clarify whether common architectures like DCGAN meet the reversibility assumption.  
- Comparison to non-f-divergence methods (e.g., WGAN) is missing, raising questions about the scope and practical relevance of the theory.  
- Several reported empirical differences fall within error bars, casting doubt on practical significance of the theoretical findings.  
- Minor: the paper needs more intuition/exposition on deformed exponential densities, signatures, and escorts for ML readers.  
- Minor: bounds on J(Q) need clearer comparison and practical guidance; explain why decreasing the escort normalization Z reduces the bound and how to exploit this.  
- Conclusion: theoretically interesting contributions but insufficient practical impact without stronger experimental validation and clearer applicability.
