This paper develops an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs. Building on Nowozin et al., the authors show that many f-divergences can be rewritten as KLχ divergences, and they connect these to Bregman divergences between natural parameters within deformed (χ-)exponential families. This yields a variational identity (their Corollary 8) that links the f-GAN objective to a geometric optimization problem in parameter space: under appropriate modeling via escorts, the variational f-GAN objective equals a Bregman divergence between parameters plus a penalty term. They further articulate a “proper composite loss” view that removes an arbitrariness in the discriminator’s output activation (link) and ties it to the chosen f-divergence. Finally, they prove a factorization result showing that standard deep generators can represent a large class of χ-escorts with compact architectures, derive sufficient conditions on activations (strong/weak admissibility), and validate design choices for generator activations and discriminator links empirically.

Relation to prior work: The paper extends classical identities connecting KL divergence within exponential families to Bregman divergences to the broader f-GAN setting via deformed exponential families and escorts, bringing the f-GAN variational formulation closer to geometric approaches (e.g., Wasserstein GANs). To my knowledge, existing generalizations do not yield a variational GAN-compatible formulation, so the proposed identity is new and significant.

Quality: The results are mathematically substantial and, as far as I could verify, technically sound. I did not check every detail in the supplement, but the proofs I read appear correct (I did not have time to go through their Thm./Cor. 8 before the deadline). The mapping from f-divergence to KLχ and on to Bregman divergences is carefully developed, and the proper composite loss treatment convincingly clarifies the discriminator’s link choice.

Clarity: The paper is dense and assumes significant background in both information theory and IG (e.g., KL/Bregman duality, Fenchel conjugates). This will be challenging for many GAN practitioners. The narrative could be reorganized to make the motivation and consequences of the IG view more accessible (see suggestions below).

Originality: High. The variational identity for f-GANs in terms of parameter-space Bregman divergences, the escort-based generative factorization for deep nets, and the resolution of the “arbitrary” discriminator link via proper composite losses appear novel and valuable.

Significance: High. The identity provides a principled interpretation of generator convergence in parameter space (via DC(θ‖ϑ)) for a broad class of models, and it offers concrete design levers: activation functions in the generator (through χ) and link functions in the discriminator (through proper composite losses). The experiments, while limited, suggest these design choices can matter in practice.

Strengths:
- A clear, principled variational IG identity for f-GANs that connects distributional and parameter-space views.
- A constructive factorization theorem showing deep generators can compactly model escorts of deformed exponential families, with explicit activation conditions.
- A complete proper composite loss picture that removes arbitrariness in the discriminator link and clarifies the optimal discriminator’s form.
- Helpful empirical probes on activation/link choices.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
1) Improve accessibility and flow:
- Move the motivation for the IG view earlier. A brief paragraph up front explaining how geometric structure in parameter space informs optimization and convergence would help. The current mention around page 7 feels late.
- Consider moving Appendix Figure 1 (the IT–IG connections in the f-GAN context) into the main text.

2) Clarify the connection between the deep factorization and the f-GAN optimization:
- As written, the deep factorization via deformed exponential families reads more as a capacity/representation theorem than as a consequence of the f-GAN optimization. I accept the 1–1 correspondence among v, f, and χ, but it would strengthen the paper to discuss how this correspondence guides the choice of f-divergence for a given activation (or vice versa), and what this implies for convergence or stability in practice.

3) Deep sufficient statistics:
- Please clarify why φl can be viewed as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6. Equation (13) does not appear to be in deformed exponential family form. Spell out the mapping from network layers to χ-exponential family components to make this interpretation airtight.

4) Editorial organization:
- Consider moving the discussion on page 7 (lines 257–269) earlier. The “utility theory” paragraph does not seem central to the IG narrative; moving it to the supplement could free space for clearer exposition of the main identity and its consequences.

5) Experiments:
- The experiments are informative but not tightly coupled to the main theoretical claims. For (A), one could motivate activation choices from the deep factorization alone, without the IG identity; for (B), the proper composite loss view suffices. The inclusion of WGAN results is potentially distracting in a paper about f-GANs; I only found the rationale in lines 227–228. Consider either tightening the focus on f-GANs or more explicitly motivating why WGAN comparisons are relevant to the IG story.

Pros for acceptance:
- Substantial and novel theoretical contribution bridging f-divergences and IG in a variational GAN-compatible way.
- Concrete design implications for generator activations and discriminator links.
- Broad relevance to both the ML and IG communities.

Cons for acceptance:
- Very dense presentation; difficult for non-IG readers.
- Some gaps in exposition linking representation results to f-GAN optimization choices.
- Experiments, while useful, are not tightly integrated with the central theoretical narrative.

Recommendation: Accept. This is a dense but impactful paper that advances the theory of GANs and offers actionable insights. With improved organization and clearer guidance for practitioners, it could have strong influence.
