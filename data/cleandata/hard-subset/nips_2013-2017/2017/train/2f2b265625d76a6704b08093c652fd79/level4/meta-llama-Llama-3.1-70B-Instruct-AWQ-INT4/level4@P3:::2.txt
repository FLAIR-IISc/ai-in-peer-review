This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, providing a novel perspective on the convergence of f-GANs in parameter space. The authors establish a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, leveraging a Bregman divergence result. They also discuss a proper implementation of f-GAN and present a factorization result for deep neural network representations. Furthermore, they investigate the choice of activation functions and their relationship with chi (or f) functions.

The paper is dense and contains multiple new results that could be of interest to machine learning and information theory. The exposition of Theorem 4, which tells the IG part of the story for f-GAN, is particularly impressive. However, the paper's clarity and coherence could be improved.

To enhance the paper's accessibility, especially for deep learning engineers who may not be familiar with the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), the authors could consider the following suggestions:

1. Move Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text, as it effectively illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
2. Clearly state the benefits of considering the IG view at the beginning of the paper. This could include explaining how the geometry in the parameter space can be used to discuss the behavior of optimization.
3. Clarify the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN. It seems that the section primarily explains how deformed exponential families can represent the distribution of a deep neural net, rather than being a direct consequence of f-GAN optimization results. The authors could elaborate on how this result can help design the f-GAN game, such as choosing the f-divergence or selecting the f-GAN objective that works best for convergence.
4. Explain why the phi_l in Theorem 6 can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics." The equation (13) does not appear to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider moving lines 257-269 to another section or deleting the utility theory part, as it is not directly related to the IG view. This could free up space to explain the main results more clearly.
6. The experiments seem to be only tangentially related to the main points claimed in the paper. The authors could discuss the results from Section 5 without relying on the IG view of f-GAN. Additionally, the inclusion of WGAN results may be distracting and confusing, as the paper primarily focuses on f-GANs.

In summary, while the paper provides valuable results and dense derivations, its organization and clarity could be improved. The authors could consider editing the paper to make the claims clearer and more coherent, ensuring that the material is presented in a crystal-clear manner.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel IG view of the f-GAN algorithm.
* It establishes a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, leveraging a Bregman divergence result.
* The authors discuss a proper implementation of f-GAN and present a factorization result for deep neural network representations.
* They investigate the choice of activation functions and their relationship with chi (or f) functions.

Weaknesses:

* The paper is dense and may be challenging for some readers to follow.
* The connection between Section 5 and f-GAN is not clearly explained.
* The experiments seem to be only tangentially related to the main points claimed in the paper.
* The inclusion of WGAN results may be distracting and confusing.

Acceptance: Yes, but with revisions to improve clarity and coherence.
