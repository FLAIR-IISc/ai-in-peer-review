- Paper offers an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, claiming convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence plus a Bregman divergence result.  
- Authors present an implementation and a factorization result for deep networks, asserting a 1-1 mapping between activation functions and the chi/f function.  
- Proofs mostly appear correct; Theorem 8 was not reviewed and Theorem 4’s exposition effectively conveys the IG aspect.  
- Paper is dense with numerous novel results that will interest ML and information theory communities; reviewer recommends accept.  
- Paper may be difficult for readers without a solid IT/IG background (e.g., many deep learning engineers).  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix into the main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.  
- Recommend an early clear statement explaining why the IG view is useful (e.g., how geometry informs optimization behavior).  
- Unclear how Section 5 relates to f-GAN: deformed exponential family discussion seems descriptive of network representational capacity rather than a consequence of f-GAN optimization, and it’s unclear how to use it to choose f-divergences or objectives.  
- Question the claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6, since equation (13) doesn’t appear to match a deformed exponential family form.  
- Propose relocating lines 257–269 and removing the utility theory paragraph as it seems unrelated to the IG perspective to free space for core results.  
- Experiments do not clearly support the main claims: Section 5 results could address point (A) without IG, point (B) only needs Section 4, and inclusion of WGAN results is distracting.  
- Overall the paper reads like a compilation of multiple works condensed into 8 pages and needs reorganization and editing to make claims clearer and more concise.
