- Paper studies GAN questions: whether solving the GAN problem implies parameter convergence, what the generator fits at convergence, and how discriminator output activation affects loss compositeness.
- Uses deformed exponential distributions and derives a variational generalization linking f-divergence between distributions to Bregman divergence between parameters.
- Establishes a connection between information-theoretic and information-geometric views on distribution and parameter dissimilarity.
- Shows that under a reversibility assumption, deep generative networks can be factorized into escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Argues that choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation carefully could improve GAN performance; offers an expected utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Reviewer finds the theoretical results interesting but questions their practical relevance and usefulness in current form.
- Major concern: many results assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family; needs justification and discussion of generalization beyond this assumption.
- Requests explanation of benefits and implementability of the parameter-based (information-geometric) perspective and experimental comparison to distribution-based optimization.
- Requests a concrete scenario where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply parameter convergence.
- Finds the compositeness/activation-function result interesting and asks for empirical comparison with the heuristic activation from Ref. [34].
- Experimental concern: μ-ReLU, claimed theoretically superior, performs worse than ReLU in Table 2 (left); asks for explanation and whether DCGAN meets the reversibility assumption.
- Asks for comparison to Wasserstein GAN and notes Table 2 (center/right) provide insufficient evidence for practical relevance.
- Minor concern: deformed exponential framework may be unfamiliar—paper should provide more intuition about deformations, signatures, and escorts.
- Theorem 8: asks for comparison of derived upper bounds for J(Q), discussion of implications for activation choice, and elaboration on benefits of the bound decreasing with escort normalization Z.
- Overall conclusion: theoretical insights are interesting but not yet substantial or convincing for GAN practitioners.
