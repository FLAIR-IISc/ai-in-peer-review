- Introduces an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, linking f-divergence and χ-divergence via a one-to-one correspondence.  
- Establishes convergence in parameter space supported by a Bregman divergence framework.  
- Provides a principled implementation of f-GAN and a factorization result for deep neural representations.  
- Links activation function choices to the χ (or f) function through a one-to-one correspondence.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not reviewed in detail.  
- Theorem 4’s exposition of the IG foundation for f-GAN is particularly compelling.  
- Paper is highly dense with numerous novel results of interest to ML and information theory.  
- Recommends acceptance given the theoretical contributions.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG foundations (KL-to-Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality).  
- Suggests moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to clarify IT–IG connections.  
- Advise stating the utility of the IG perspective earlier (e.g., parameter-space geometry for optimization analysis).  
- Request clearer explanation of how Section 5 (deformed exponential families) informs f-GAN design and choice of f-divergences/activations.  
- Ask for justification that φₗ in Theorem 6 are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq. (13) doesn’t match the deformed exponential family form.  
- Recommend relocating or omitting the utility theory paragraph (lines 257–269) from the main text.  
- Propose refocusing experiments since results in (A) and (B) follow from Sections 4–5 without IG, and remove the distracting WGAN discussion.  
- Overall organization feels like multiple results compressed into 8 pages; narrative coherence should be improved while retaining core contributions.
