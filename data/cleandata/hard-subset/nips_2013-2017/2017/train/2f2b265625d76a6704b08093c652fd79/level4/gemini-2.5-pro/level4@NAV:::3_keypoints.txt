- Paper asks how solving the GAN objective maps to parameter-space convergence, what distribution the generator models at convergence, and how discriminator output activation affects loss compositeness.

- Authors adopt a deformed exponential/information-theoretic framework to analyze GANs.

- Theoretical contribution: a variational generalization (compatible with f-GAN) linking f-divergence between distributions to Bregman divergence between their parameters.

- Theoretical contribution: under a reversibility assumption, deep generators can be factored as "escorts" of deformed exponential distributions.

- Theoretical claim: careful choice of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation can improve GAN performance.

- Paper briefly reframes the GAN objective via expected utility theory.

- Major concern: core theorems assume data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family—authors must justify this assumption and whether results hold if relaxed.

- Major concern: unclear practical advantage of the parameter-space (information-geometric) formulation—no experimental comparison or clear benefit shown for parameter-based optimization.

- Major concern: authors claim distributional convergence implies parameter convergence (given small residual J(Q)), but do not justify why this is nontrivial or provide counterexamples where distributional convergence fails to imply parameter convergence.

- Major concern: theoretical prescription for discriminator activation gf is interesting, but lacks experimental comparison against the heuristic gf from prior work [34].

- Major concern: experimental results contradict theory (μ-ReLU underperforms ReLU despite satisfying reversibility); authors must explain this and clarify whether architectures like DCGAN satisfy reversibility.

- Major concern: applicability to architectures outside f-divergence (e.g., WGAN) is unclear.

- Major concern: reported activation/link function comparisons fall within error margins and do not convincingly support theoretical claims.

- Minor concern: deformed exponential concepts (deformation, signature, escort) need more intuitive explanation for ML readers.

- Minor concern: Theorem 8's multiple upper bounds on residual J(Q) need comparison and discussion; authors should explain why decreasing the bound with escort normalization Z is desirable and how to exploit it.

- Overall: intriguing theoretical results but current practical applicability and experimental support are insufficient for GAN practitioners.
