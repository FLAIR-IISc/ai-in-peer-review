- Paper addresses interesting GAN questions: parameter convergence, fitted distribution at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice.
- Uses deformed exponential distributions framework to derive theoretical results connecting distribution and parameter spaces.
- Theorem: variational f-divergence relates to a Bregman divergence between distribution parameters, linking information-theoretic and information-geometric views.
- Shows (under a reversibility assumption) that deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Derives practical suggestions for choosing generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations to improve GAN behavior.
- Briefly offers an alternative expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Major concern: many results rely on the strong assumption that P and Q belong to the deformed exponential family, which needs justification and assessment of practical validity.
- Questionable whether similar results can be obtained without the deformed exponential family assumption.
- Key contribution (Eq. 7) links variational f-divergence to parameter-space optimization, but practical gains and implementability of this parameter-based approach are unclear.
- Paper lacks experimental comparison between distributional optimization and the proposed parameter-based optimization to demonstrate benefits.
- The implication that solving the GAN game implies parameter-space convergence (when residual J(Q) is small) is not convincingly motivated or exemplified.
- Theoretically derived discriminator output activation g_f is important, but the paper lacks experiments comparing g_f to prior heuristic activations (Ref. [34]).
- Empirical contradiction: theoretically justified invertible μ-ReLU performs worse than baseline ReLU in GAN MLP experiments—needs explanation.
- Unclear whether common architectures like DCGAN satisfy the reversibility assumption; comparison to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN is missing.
- Experimental differences across activation/link functions are within error bars, providing weak evidence for practical relevance.
- Clarity concern: deformed exponential concepts ("deformation," "signature," "escort") need more intuition for ML readers.
- Interpretation of Theorem 8 is unclear; need clearer comparison of its upper bounds and practical implications for activation choice.
- Claim that J(Q) upper bound decreases with escort normalization Z needs explanation of why this is beneficial and how to leverage it in practice.
- Overall judgment: interesting theoretical insights but currently insufficient practical relevance or evidence to be compelling for GAN practitioners.
