- Paper presents a strong information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, connecting divergence minimization, neural network representation, and optimization dynamics.  
- Main theoretical contributions: one-to-one correspondence between f-divergences and chi-divergences, factorization result for deep network representations, activation↔f/chi characterization, and IG analysis of f-GAN optimization.  
- Core proofs appear sound and novel; Theorem 4 gives an elegant IG interpretation; Theorem 8 was not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Technical depth and density of results make the paper valuable for information-theory/machine-learning researchers.  
- Major concern: accessibility — exposition assumes heavy IG background and is hard for typical deep learning practitioners to follow.  
- Recommendation to state the IG perspective’s practical utility and convergence implications clearly and early in the introduction.  
- Move Appendix Figure 1 into the main text (early in Section 2) to provide a visual anchor for the IG narrative.  
- Section 5 (deformed exponential families and activations) is underdeveloped w.r.t. f-GAN optimization; explicitly link f/chi/activation correspondence to practical divergence/architecture choices.  
- Clarify or revise the “deep sufficient statistics” claim (Theorem 6), since Eq. (13) does not match standard deformed exponential family form.  
- Remove or relegate tangential material (utility theory, WGAN experiments) to the appendix unless their relevance to the IG story is made explicit.  
- Experiments do not test IG-derived predictions; revise empirical section to directly evaluate IG-based claims (e.g., compare convergence across f-divergences as predicted).  
- Overall: accept pending revisions to improve narrative flow, emphasize optimization implications, streamline Section 5, relocate Figure 1, tighten experiments, and justify specific theoretical claims.
