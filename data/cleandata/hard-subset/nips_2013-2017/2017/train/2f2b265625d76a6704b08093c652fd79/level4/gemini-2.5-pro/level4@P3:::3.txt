### **Summary**

This paper aims to provide a deeper theoretical understanding of the f-GAN framework by connecting it to the field of information geometry. The authors extend the work of Nowozin et al. by establishing a link between the information-theoretic view of f-divergences between distributions and an information-geometric view of Bregman divergences between their corresponding parameters.

The main contributions are threefold:
1.  A novel "variational information-geometric f-GAN identity" is presented. This identity shows that, for a broad class of distributions called deformed exponential families, the variational objective of the f-GAN game is equivalent to a Bregman divergence between the distributions' parameters, plus a penalty term. This result suggests that solving the f-GAN game can imply convergence in the parameter space.
2.  The paper analyzes deep generator architectures under this framework. It shows that, under a reversibility assumption, standard deep networks can be seen as factorizing "escorts" of these deformed exponential families. This analysis provides a theoretical basis for choosing generator activation functions, identifying a class of "strongly admissible" functions (e.g., Softplus, ELU) that fit the theory.
3.  The authors formalize the choice of the discriminator's output activation function. They re-interpret this component within the theory of proper composite losses, showing that it is not an arbitrary choice but is determined by the composition of the f-divergence's derivative and a chosen link function.

Experiments are conducted to test the practical implications of these theoretical insights by varying generator activation functions and discriminator link functions on MNIST and LSUN datasets.

### **Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is technically dense and presents a series of theoretical results that appear sound. The derivation of the central information-geometric identity and the connection to deep network architectures is a solid piece of theoretical work. However, the connection between this theory and its practical application is not as strong.

*   **Weakness:** The core theoretical results rely on the assumption that the data distribution (P) and the model distribution (Q) are members of the same deformed exponential family. This is a strong assumption, and its validity for complex, high-dimensional datasets like natural images is not discussed or justified. The paper would be significantly strengthened by an argument for why this is a reasonable modeling assumption in the context of modern GAN applications.
*   **Weakness:** The experimental results provide inconclusive support for the theory. Many of the performance differences reported between different activation functions and link functions appear to be within the error bars, making it difficult to draw firm conclusions about their practical benefit. In some cases, the results seem to contradict the theory's predictions. For example, in the GAN MLP experiment, the µ-ReLU activation (which aligns with the theory's reversibility assumption) performs worse than the standard, non-invertible ReLU. These discrepancies are not adequately explained.
*   **Strength:** The formalization of the discriminator's output activation via proper composite losses is an elegant and valuable contribution. It provides a principled way to think about a component of the f-GAN objective that was previously described as "somewhat arbitrary."

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written for an audience already familiar with information geometry. However, it could be challenging for the broader machine learning community.

*   **Weakness/Suggestion for Improvement:** Key concepts central to the paper's theory, such as "deformed exponential families," "signatures," and "escorts," are introduced with formal definitions but little intuition. A few sentences explaining what these concepts represent and why they are the right tool for this analysis would greatly improve the paper's accessibility and impact.
*   **Weakness:** The practical implications of the main theoretical identity (Corollary 8) are not fully elucidated. The authors use the right-hand side of the identity (the Bregman divergence) to argue for convergence in parameter space but do not leverage it for optimization. It is unclear what a practitioner gains from this alternative perspective. Could this lead to a new, parameter-based optimization algorithm for GANs? A comparison between the two optimization viewpoints would have been very compelling.

**Originality:** The paper is highly original. Connecting the variational formulation of f-GANs to the geometric framework of Bregman divergences and deformed exponential families is a novel approach. To my knowledge, this is the first work to provide such a detailed geometric interpretation of the generator's role and architecture within the f-GAN framework.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a significant goal: building a more rigorous theoretical foundation for GANs. A deeper understanding of what the generator is learning and how network architecture choices impact the underlying distributions is of great interest to the community.

*   **Strength:** The paper introduces a new and powerful theoretical lens through which to analyze GANs. This could inspire future theoretical work and potentially lead to more principled architectural design.
*   **Weakness:** The immediate practical significance is limited by the inconclusive experimental results. As it stands, the paper provides interesting theoretical insights but does not yet offer a clear, experimentally-validated recipe for improving GAN performance. For example, while the analysis of proper composite losses is interesting, the experiments do not directly compare the theoretically-derived output function against the heuristic one from the original f-GAN paper, which would have been a direct test of the theory's utility.

### **Detailed Comments and Questions**

1.  **Parameter-Space Convergence:** The paper argues that Corollary 8 guarantees convergence in parameter space. Could you provide a realistic scenario where convergence in the variational f-divergence formulation (the left-hand side) would *not* imply convergence in parameter space, thereby highlighting the novelty of this result?
2.  **Applicability to WGAN:** The experiments test different activation and link functions on WGAN. However, the paper's entire theoretical framework is built upon f-divergences. Since WGAN is based on the Wasserstein distance, which is not an f-divergence, the theoretical motivation for these experiments is unclear. Please clarify why the insights derived from the f-GAN analysis are expected to hold for WGAN.
3.  **Reversibility Assumption:** The analysis in Section 4 relies on the generator network being reversible. How reasonable is this assumption for modern architectures like DCGAN, which use strided convolutions that are not trivially invertible? Can you comment on how violations of this assumption might affect the theoretical conclusions?

### **Summary of Arguments**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Presents a novel and original theoretical framework connecting f-GANs with information geometry.
*   Provides a principled theoretical account of the generator's architecture, linking activation functions to properties of deformed exponential families.
*   Offers an elegant formalization of the discriminator's output activation function through the theory of proper composite losses.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   The practical relevance and utility of the main theoretical results are not convincingly demonstrated.
*   The core assumption that data and model distributions belong to a deformed exponential family is strong and not sufficiently justified for typical GAN use cases.
*   The experimental validation is weak; results are often inconclusive (within error bars) and sometimes appear to contradict the theory without adequate explanation.

In conclusion, the paper presents a fascinating and original theoretical analysis but falls short of connecting its insights to demonstrable practical improvements. The gap between the elegant theory and the inconclusive experimental results is a significant weakness.
