This paper considers the f-GAN principle generalized to f-divergences. By leveraging a variational generalization of an old theorem that relates the KL divergence between regular exponential families and divergences between their natural parameters, the authors establish a connection between the general f-divergence formulation and a geometric optimization problem. Specifically, they prove the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which provides an interpretation of the objective function of the f-GAN. The authors also investigate the information-theoretic and information-geometric parts of the vig-f-GAN identity, shedding light on the supervised game and the design of the activation functions in the generator.

The main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and its applicability is promising. However, the presentation of the main theorems could be improved for better clarity. The authors may consider providing more intuitive explanations or illustrations to facilitate the understanding of their results.

The paper also explores the implications of the vig-f-GAN identity for deep learning. The authors show that standard deep generator architectures can model complex escorts of deformed exponential families, and they provide a sufficient condition on the activation functions to guarantee this modeling. They also demonstrate that popular activation functions, such as ELU, ReLU, and Softplus, satisfy this condition.

The experimental results presented in the paper are somewhat disconnected from the theoretical contributions. While the authors provide some comparisons of different activation functions and link functions, it is not entirely clear how these observations relate to the theoretical results. The authors may consider adding more discussions or analyses to bridge the gap between their theoretical and experimental contributions.

There are some minor issues with the writing, such as the clause "For eq.(7), is means when C …" on page 7, line 258, which has a wrong structure.

Strengths:

* The paper establishes a connection between the general f-divergence formulation and a geometric optimization problem.

* The paper explores the implications of the vig-f-GAN identity for deep learning.

Weaknesses:

* There are some minor issues with the writing.
