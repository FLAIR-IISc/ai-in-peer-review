This paper considers the f-GAN principle generalized to f-divergences. Generalizing the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families by using the chi-logarithm, the authors prove the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, which provides an interpretation of the objective function of the f-GAN. The authors also prove a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical result, vig-f-GAN identity is interesting and its applicability is promising. However, the meanings of main theorems are hard to follow since they are written in quite general forms. For example, Theorem 3, 5, 6, and 7 are all defined with abstract variables and functions, making it difficult to understand their implications. It would be beneficial to include more explanations and examples to illustrate the theorems and their applications.

The paper also lacks clear connections between the theoretical results and the experimental evaluations. Section 6 presents the experimental results, but it is hard to understand how the observations are related to the theoretical results. For instance, the experiments compare different activation functions and link functions, but it is not clear how these comparisons relate to the vig-f-GAN identity or the sufficient condition on activation functions.

There are also some minor issues with the writing. For example, on page 7, line 258, the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C …" has a wrong structure and is unclear.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear, although some parts could be improved for better readability. The paper adequately informs the reader, and the experimental results provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce them.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, namely the generalization of the GAN principle to f-divergences and the use of chi-logarithm to prove the vig-f-GAN identity. The paper also provides a new characterization of the generators' convergence and a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, namely providing a theoretical understanding of the f-GAN principle and its applications. The paper also provides a new framework for understanding the GAN game and its components, which could be useful for other researchers in the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity.
* The paper provides a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research.
* The paper provides a new framework for understanding the GAN game and its components.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's main theorems are written in quite general forms, making it difficult to understand their implications.
* The paper lacks clear connections between the theoretical results and the experimental evaluations.
* Some minor issues with the writing, such as unclear sentences and wrong structures.
