This paper explores the f-GAN principle, extending it to f-divergences. By generalizing the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families through the chi-logarithm, the authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity provides an interpretation of the objective function of the f-GAN, offering a promising theoretical foundation. Additionally, the authors derive a sufficient condition on activation functions to achieve factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and demonstrates potential applicability. However, the theorems are presented in a general form, making it challenging to grasp their meanings. To enhance clarity, it would be beneficial to provide more specific examples or illustrations of the theorems' implications.

In Section 6, the experimental results are presented, but it is difficult to understand how these observations relate to the theoretical findings. To strengthen the connection between theory and practice, the authors could provide more detailed explanations of how the experimental results validate or illustrate the theoretical concepts.

On page 7, line 258, the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C …" appears to have a grammatical error. A rephrased version of this sentence would improve the overall clarity of the text.
