- Proposes an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, proves parameter-space convergence via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman result, discusses implementation, and presents a factorization result and activation↔chi mapping; proofs mostly appear correct (Thm.8 unchecked).

- Paper is dense with many novel results of interest to ML and information theory; Thm.4 exposition is particularly clear.

- Primary critique: accessibility—readers without background in information theory/geometry will struggle; key IG facts (KL↔Bregman, Fenchel duality) are not made sufficiently accessible.

- Suggest moving Fig.1 from the appendix to the main text to better illustrate IT↔IG connections.

- Recommend stating earlier why the IG view is useful (e.g., how geometry informs optimization); the paper only mentions this late (page 7).

- Section 5’s connection to f-GAN is unclear: it describes representable distributions via deformed exponential families but does not explain how the v–f–chi mapping guides f-GAN design or divergence/objective choice.

- For Thm.6, clarify why the φ_l can be considered “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq.(13) does not resemble a deformed exponential family.

- Lines 257–269 and the utility-theory paragraph seem tangential to the IG perspective and should be moved to the appendix to free space for main results.

- Experiments are loosely tied to the paper’s core claims: activation choices can be motivated by Section 5 alone, Section 4 suffices for point (B), and inclusion of WGAN results is distracting/confusing.

- Overall recommendation: accept, but reorganize and edit for clearer signposting, stronger integration between IG perspective and practical f-GAN guidance, and improved readability for non-IT/IG specialists.
