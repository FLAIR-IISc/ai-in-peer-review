- Presents an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, connecting information theory and optimization with novel theoretical links.  
- Proves f-GAN convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, supported by Bregman divergence results.  
- Provides a practical implementation framework and a factorization of deep representations linking activation functions to chi/f-functions.  
- Theorem 4 offers a particularly insightful IG interpretation of f-GAN dynamics; most proofs appear sound.  
- Theorem 8 requires deeper verification and is not yet fully convincing.  
- Paper is dense and heavily relies on IG/IT concepts, risking alienation of deep-learning practitioners unfamiliar with exponential-family/Bregman duality.  
- Recommend stating the utility of the IG viewpoint early (before page 7) to contextualize the approach for non-specialists.  
- Move Appendix Figure 1 into the main text as an early visual anchor that maps IT/IG concepts to f-GAN.  
- Strengthen Section 5 by explicitly connecting deformed exponential family representations and the claimed 1-1 correspondence to practical f-GAN optimization and activation/divergence selection.  
- Clarify the interpretation of φ_l as “deep sufficient statistics” (Theorem 6) with a precise theoretical justification beyond Equation (13).  
- Remove or relocate tangential material (e.g., utility theory paragraph lines 257–269) to the appendix to free space for core contributions.  
- Realign experiments to support the IG-f-GAN narrative: either integrate WGAN results into the f-GAN focus or omit them, and ensure activation/function claims are tied to optimization implications.  
- Overall, strong technical contributions but presented as disconnected theorems; needs a unifying narrative demonstrating practical impact.  
- Support acceptance contingent on targeted revisions to improve clarity, accessibility, experiment alignment, and theoretical justification.
