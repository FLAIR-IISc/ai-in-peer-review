- Paper studies theoretical GAN questions: meaning of parameter-space convergence, what generator models at convergence, and choice of discriminator output activation for compositeness.  
- Theoretical contributions use deformed exponential distributions to generalize an f-divergence ↔ parameter-space Bregman divergence identity (variational/f-GAN form).  
- Shows under a reversibility assumption deep generators can be represented as escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Argues generator and discriminator activation choices could improve GAN performance.  
- Major concern: practical relevance is limited in current form.  
- Heavy reliance on the assumption that data and model distributions lie in the deformed exponential family—needs justification for real-world applicability.  
- Requests clarification of practical benefits of the parameter-based (information-geometric) perspective and whether GANs can be implemented via parameter-space optimization.  
- Criticizes lack of experimental comparisons between variational f-divergence optimization and the parameter-based approach.  
- Notes the authors do not fully exploit the right-hand side of Equation 7 beyond a convergence implication—asks for scenarios where variational convergence does not imply parameter convergence.  
- Finds the theoretical result on discriminator output activation gf compelling, but asks for experiments comparing the derived gf to heuristic choices from prior work.  
- Points out experimental contradiction: μ-ReLU (invertible) performs worse than ReLU in MLP GANs despite theory predicting the opposite—requests explanation.  
- Asks whether DCGAN satisfies the reversibility assumption and requests comparisons to Wasserstein GAN (not f-divergence-based).  
- Notes experimental differences between activations/link functions are within error bars, providing weak empirical support for theoretical claims.  
- Requests more intuitive explanations of deformed exponential concepts (deformation/signature, escort) for a broader audience.  
- Asks how the various upper bounds on J(Q) in Theorem 8 compare, implications for activation choice, and why larger escort normalization Z reducing the bound is beneficial/practical.  
- Overall judgment: interesting theoretical work but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer assumption justification, and concrete practitioner guidance.
