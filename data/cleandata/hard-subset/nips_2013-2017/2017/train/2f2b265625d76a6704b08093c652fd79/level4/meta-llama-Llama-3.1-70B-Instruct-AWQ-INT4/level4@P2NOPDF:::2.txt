Thank you for an interesting read. This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, presenting a novel perspective on the convergence and implementation of f-GAN. The authors demonstrate that f-GAN converges in parameter space by establishing a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, and leveraging a Bregman divergence result. Additionally, they discuss a proper way to implement f-GAN and provide a factorization result of the deep neural network representation, highlighting the relationship between activation functions and chi (or f) functions.

I have reviewed the proofs in the appendix, and while I did not verify every detail, they appear to be correct, with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to thoroughly examine. Overall, I believe this paper is very dense and contains many new results that could be of interest to the machine learning and information theory communities. The exposition of Theorem 4, which explains the IG aspect of f-GAN, is particularly impressive. Therefore, I think this paper is a clear accept.

However, I do think that this paper could be challenging for readers without a strong background in the connections between information theory (IT) and information geometry (IG), particularly those familiar with the simplest mappings, such as the relationship between KL divergence and Bregman divergence for an exponential family, as well as Fenchel duality. As the primary audience for a GAN paper consists of deep learning engineers, I suggest the following revisions to improve clarity:

1. Consider moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text, as it effectively illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context.
2. Provide a clear statement at the beginning of the paper explaining the benefits of considering the IG view. My understanding is that this perspective allows for the use of geometry in the parameter space to discuss optimization behavior, which is briefly mentioned on page 7, but could be introduced earlier.
3. Clarify the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN. It appears that the deformed exponential family is used to explain the distribution that a deep neural network can represent, but this does not seem to be a direct consequence of the f-GAN optimization results. While the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi is established, it is not clear how this result can be used to design the f-GAN game, such as selecting the optimal f divergence or determining the best f-GAN objective for convergence.
4. Explain why phi_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics" in Theorem 6. Equation (13) does not appear to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider relocating lines 257-269 to a more relevant section or deleting the utility theory part, as it does not seem to be directly related to the IG view. This could free up space to elaborate on the main results.
6. The experiments do not appear to be directly related to the main claims of the paper. For example, the results from Section 5 can be applied without understanding the IG view of f-GAN, and the discussion of WGAN in the experiments seems distracting and confusing, as this paper primarily focuses on f-GAN.

In summary, while this paper presents many useful results and dense derivations, the material could be better organized for clarity and coherence. The paper appears to be a condensed version of multiple papers, which may make it difficult for readers to follow. Therefore, while I support acceptance, I recommend editing the paper to make the claims clearer and more coherent.
