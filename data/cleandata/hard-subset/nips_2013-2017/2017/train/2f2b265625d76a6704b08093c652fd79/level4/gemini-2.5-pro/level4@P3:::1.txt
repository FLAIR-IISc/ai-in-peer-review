### **Summary**

This paper presents a novel and deep theoretical connection between the `f-GAN` framework and information geometry. Building upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2016), which generalized the GAN principle to arbitrary `f`-divergences, this work aims to provide a more complete picture, particularly concerning the generator's role and its convergence properties. The authors' main contribution is the "variational information-geometric `f`-GAN identity" (vig-`f`-GAN). This identity shows that for a broad class of distributions known as deformed exponential families, minimizing the `f`-GAN objective is equivalent to minimizing a Bregman divergence between the natural parameters of the true and generated distributions, plus a penalty term. This elegantly connects the information-theoretic perspective of `f`-divergences with a geometric optimization problem in a parameter space.

Further contributions include:
1.  An analysis of deep generator architectures, demonstrating that standard deep networks with common activation functions (like ReLU, ELU, Softplus) are naturally suited to model and compactly factorize densities from these deformed exponential families.
2.  A clarification of the discriminator's objective function by framing it within the theory of proper composite losses. This provides a principled basis for choosing the output activation of the discriminator, a point left ambiguous in the original `f`-GAN paper.

The authors support their theoretical claims with experiments on MNIST and LSUN, investigating the practical effects of varying generator activation functions and discriminator link functions.

### **Strengths and Weaknesses**

#### **Quality**
The paper appears to be technically sound. The theoretical development, which generalizes the known relationship between KL divergence and Bregman divergence for exponential families, is built upon established concepts in information geometry (e.g., deformed exponential families, χ-logarithms). The proofs provided in the supplementary material seem rigorous. The main identity (Corollary 8) is a significant result that provides a new and valuable lens through which to understand the `f`-GAN training process.

#### **Clarity**
While the paper's contributions are significant, its primary weakness is clarity. The paper is mathematically dense and assumes a high level of familiarity with information geometry, making it challenging for a general NIPS audience to fully grasp the implications of the main theorems.
*   The main results, particularly Theorems 5, 6, and Corollary 8, are presented in a very abstract form. More intuition or a running illustrative example (perhaps with a simple 1D distribution) would greatly help in demystifying concepts like χ-logarithms, escorts, and their role in connecting the variational objective to the Bregman divergence.
*   The connection between the theoretical results and the experiments in Section 6 is not clearly articulated. The theory suggests a constructive link between an `f`-divergence, its corresponding signature χ, and a suitable generator activation function `v`. However, the experiments compare a fixed set of standard activations without explaining how this comparison directly validates or is guided by the theory. It reads more like an empirical exploration than a verification of the theoretical framework.

#### **Originality**
The work is highly original. To my knowledge, this is the first paper to establish such a direct and general link between the variational formulation of `f`-divergences used in `f`-GANs and Bregman divergences on the parameter space of deformed exponential families. While connections between information theory and information geometry are known, this specific identity in the context of GANs is novel and insightful. The analysis of how deep architectures naturally implement these families is also a new and important contribution.

#### **Significance**
The paper has the potential for high impact. By providing a geometric interpretation of the generator's learning process, it bridges the gap between the `f`-GAN family and other geometrically-motivated GANs like the Wasserstein GAN. This unified view can deepen our understanding of GAN dynamics and could inspire new, more principled architectural designs and training algorithms. The results concerning activation functions and proper composite losses provide a theoretical grounding for what have largely been empirical design choices. If the ideas are made more accessible, other researchers are likely to build upon this foundational work.

### **Detailed Comments and Suggestions for Improvement**

*   **Connecting Theory and Experiments:** The authors should make a more explicit effort to link the experimental setup to the theory. For instance, does the theory predict which activation functions should perform better for a given `f`-divergence (e.g., the original GAN divergence)? The current experiments show that different activations perform differently, but it's unclear if this is a direct confirmation of the paper's core claims or just a general empirical finding.
*   **Improving Readability:** I strongly encourage the authors to add a section or a running example that illustrates the main concepts with a concrete case. For example, starting with a specific `f` (like the one for the original GAN), derive the corresponding χ, show what the deformed exponential family looks like, and explain what activation function this would imply for the generator. This would make the abstract theory much more tangible.
*   **Minor Correction:** On page 7, line 258, the sentence starting "For eq.(7), is means when C …" appears to be grammatically incorrect or incomplete. Please revise for clarity.

### **Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The paper establishes a novel, significant, and elegant connection between `f`-GANs and information geometry.
2.  **New Insights:** It provides a new theoretical understanding of the generator's role in GANs and offers principled justifications for architectural choices like activation functions.
3.  **Unification:** The work helps unify different views on GANs (information-theoretic vs. geometric), which is valuable for the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
1.  **Poor Clarity:** The paper is very difficult to read and its key takeaways are obscured by dense mathematical formalism. The potential impact is limited if the community cannot easily understand and build upon the results.
2.  **Weak Link to Practice:** The experimental validation feels disconnected from the main theoretical claims, weakening the overall message of the paper.

### **Conclusion**

This paper presents a theoretically deep and important contribution to our understanding of GANs. The core results are original and significant. However, the paper's potential impact is currently hampered by a lack of clarity and a tenuous connection between the theory and the empirical results. I lean towards acceptance because the theoretical ideas are strong and valuable to the community. However, I strongly urge the authors to substantially revise the manuscript to improve its accessibility and to better motivate the experiments in the context of their theory. A clear and intuitive explanation of their results would transform this from a good paper into an excellent one.
