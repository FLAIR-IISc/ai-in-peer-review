- Presents an information-geometric perspective on f-GANs, using a one-to-one correspondence between f- and χ-divergences plus a Bregman identity to argue parameter-space convergence.  
- Clarifies a principled way to instantiate the f-GAN objective and analyzes generator factorization and activation choices, showing a one-to-one correspondence between activations and χ/f functions.  
- Reviewer found the arguments they checked sound but did not fully verify the supplement (specifically Theorem 8).  
- Theorem 4 is highlighted as a strong, core contribution capturing the IG story for f-GANs.  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty and relevance to ML and information-theory communities.  
- Paper is dense and may be hard to digest for readers without a strong IT/IG background (e.g., many deep-learning practitioners).  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 (IT-IG connections) from the appendix into the main text for accessibility.  
- Recommend motivating early why the IG viewpoint is beneficial (e.g., enabling geometric reasoning about optimization) and introducing that point sooner.  
- Unclear how Section 5 ties back to the f-GAN objective; needs explicit connection and practical guidance for choosing f-divergences or matching activations to f-GAN objectives.  
- Request clarification on Theorem 6’s claim that φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq. (13) doesn’t appear in deformed exponential-family form.  
- Proposes relocating the utility-theoretic discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it feels orthogonal to the IG narrative.  
- Experiments feel decoupled from the central IG claims; some empirical points can be supported by Section 5 alone and Section 4 is only loosely tied to IG.  
- Inclusion of WGAN results is potentially distracting for a paper focused on f-GANs and needs clearer justification.  
- Overall organization should be tightened—current submission reads like multiple contributions compressed into eight pages; revise to make claims crisper and narrative more coherent.
