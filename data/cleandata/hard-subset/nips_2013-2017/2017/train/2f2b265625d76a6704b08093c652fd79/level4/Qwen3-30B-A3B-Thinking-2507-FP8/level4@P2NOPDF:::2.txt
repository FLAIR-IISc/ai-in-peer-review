

This paper presents a compelling information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, establishing novel connections between information theory and optimization. The authors first prove f-GAN convergence in parameter space using a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence, supported by Bregman divergence results. They then propose a practical implementation framework and derive a factorization of deep neural network representations, linking activation functions to chi/f-functions via 1-1 correspondences. The proofs (excluding Theorem 8, which requires deeper verification) appear sound, and Theorem 4 provides a particularly insightful IG interpretation of f-GAN dynamics. The paper delivers dense, valuable contributions for machine learning and information theory, warranting a clear acceptance.  

However, the paper’s high density and reliance on specialized IG/IT concepts risk alienating its primary audience: deep learning practitioners unfamiliar with the exponential family/Bregman duality foundations. To maximize accessibility and impact, the following revisions are recommended:  

1.  **Early justification for the IG perspective**: The utility of the IG viewpoint (e.g., enabling geometric analysis of optimization behavior in parameter space) should be explicitly stated upfront, not deferred to page 7. This contextualization is critical for readers lacking IT/IG background.  
2.  **Figure 1 integration**: The appendix’s Figure 1, which clearly maps IT/IG concepts to f-GAN, should be moved to the main text as an early visual anchor. This would immediately clarify the core framework for all readers.  
3.  **Strengthening Section 5’s relevance**: Section 5 discusses neural network representations via deformed exponential families but fails to explicitly connect this to *f-GAN optimization design*. The paper claims a 1-1 correspondence between activation functions, f-divergences, and chi-divergences, yet neglects to explain *how this informs practical f-GAN choices* (e.g., selecting optimal f-divergences or activation functions for convergence). This gap weakens the central thesis.  
4.  **Clarify "deep sufficient statistics" (Theorem 6)**: The interpretation of $\phi_l$ as "deep sufficient statistics" requires justification beyond Equation (13), which does not manifest as a standard deformed exponential family. A precise theoretical link must be provided.  
5.  **Streamline non-essential content**: The utility theory paragraph (lines 257–269) is tangential to the IG framework and should be relocated to the appendix to free space for clarifying core contributions. Similarly, the discussion of line 257–269 should be integrated earlier where relevant.  
6.  **Align experiments with core claims**: The experiments (e.g., WGAN results on lines 227–228) distract from the IG-f-GAN focus. Section 5’s activation function results (e.g., "new activations") could be presented without the IG framework, while Section 4’s insights (e.g., on divergence choices) should be explicitly tied to f-GAN optimization. The WGAN material is confusingly placed and should be either integrated into the f-GAN narrative or omitted to maintain focus.  

The paper’s strength lies in its theoretical depth, but its current structure presents results as a collection of disconnected theorems rather than a cohesive argument. The integration of concepts from information geometry, optimization, and deep learning lacks a unifying narrative that demonstrates *why* the IG perspective directly improves f-GAN practice. This is especially critical for the practitioner audience, who need to see clear design implications.  

In summary, while the paper’s technical contributions are significant and well-motivated for specialists, its presentation risks obscuring these insights for the intended readership. With targeted revisions to clarify the IG perspective’s practical value, strengthen Section 5’s relevance, streamline non-essential content, and better align experiments with core claims, the paper would be exceptionally strong. As currently written, it requires substantial editing to achieve coherence. I support acceptance but emphasize that the suggested revisions are essential to maximize the paper’s impact. The current draft reads as though results from multiple domains have been tightly packed into an 8-page limit without sufficient narrative cohesion. Addressing these points would transform it from a dense technical contribution into a clear, influential work for both theory and practice.
