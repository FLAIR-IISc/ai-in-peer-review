- Paper proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN, showing convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and a Bregman divergence result.
- Authors provide implementation guidance for f-GAN and a factorization result for deep nets, highlighting activations that have a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function.
- Proofs in the appendix generally appear correct, though Theorem 8 was not fully checked by the reviewer.
- The paper is dense, contains numerous new results, and Theorem 4 effectively conveys the IG component; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- The exposition may be hard to follow for readers without a strong information theory/geometry background (e.g., deep learning engineers).
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix into the main text because it clarifies IT–IG connections in f-GAN.
- Recommend stating up front why the IG view is beneficial, especially how geometry informs optimization behavior.
- Unclear how Section 5 connects to f-GAN: deformed exponential family seems to describe representable distributions rather than follow from f-GAN optimization.
- Questioning the usefulness of the v, f, chi 1-1 correspondence for designing the f-GAN game or choosing divergences/objectives for convergence.
- Doubt about treating φ_l as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6; Eq. (13) doesn’t look like a deformed exponential family.
- Suggest relocating lines 257–269 and removing or moving the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix since it’s not central to the IG view.
- Experiments are not well aligned with the main IG claims; Section 5 and Section 4 results seem tangential and WGAN results may distract from the f-GAN focus.
- Overall recommendation: accept but requires reorganization and editing to clarify, cohere, and better target the primary audience.
