- Introduces a novel information geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN framework.  
- Proves convergence of f-GAN in parameter space using the f-divergence ↔ chi-divergence correspondence and a Bregman-divergence result; proofs appear sound though not exhaustively checked.  
- Offers practical implementation guidance for f-GANs.  
- Presents a factorization theorem linking deep network activation functions to chi (and f) functions via a one-to-one mapping.  
- Theoretical contributions are substantial and of interest to ML and information theory communities; Theorem 4 is highlighted as especially strong.  
- Recommends acceptance based on strong theoretical merit.  
- Accessibility is a major concern: exposition assumes deep IT/IG background, making it hard for many deep-learning practitioners.  
- Suggests moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main paper to aid comprehension.  
- Advise stating the IG motivation earlier (analysis of optimization behavior in parameter space is introduced too late).  
- Requests clearer explanation of how Section 5’s activation↔f↔chi correspondence should guide f-GAN training and objective/activation choices.  
- Calls for clarification of Theorem 6’s claim that ϕ_l are “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq. (13) doesn’t match a standard deformed exponential family form.  
- Recommends moving the motivation (lines 257–269) to the introduction and removing or relegating the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix.  
- Criticizes experiments as disconnected from theory: novel activations seem justified only by Section 5, other results rely on Section 4, and inclusion of WGANs is potentially confusing.  
- Overall structure and narrative feel fragmented; requests significant revision to improve clarity, organization, and coherence despite strong technical results.
