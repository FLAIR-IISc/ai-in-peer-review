- Paper poses compelling GAN questions: does solving GAN objective imply parameter convergence, what does generator fit at convergence, and how to choose discriminator output activation for loss compositeness.  
- Derives a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters (f-GAN–friendly), connecting information-theoretic and information-geometric views.  
- Shows that under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.  
- Argues generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations can be chosen (via deformed-exponential theory) to potentially improve GAN performance.  
- Offers an alternative brief interpretation of the GAN game using expected utility theory.  
- Reviewer’s overall view: interesting theoretical contributions but significant concerns about practical relevance in current form.  
- Major concern 1: results depend on assuming data and model lie in the deformed exponential family—authors must justify this assumption or show which results hold without it.  
- Major concern 2: Eq. 7’s parameter-space f-GAN identity is unexplained practically—no experimental parameter-vs-distribution optimization comparison, unclear benefits, and no concrete example where distribution convergence fails to imply parameter convergence.  
- Major concern 3: The theoretically derived discriminator activation g_f (f' ∘ link) is intriguing, but no experiments compare it to the heuristic from Ref. [34].  
- Major concern 4: Under reversibility, µ-ReLU should help, yet Table 2 shows µ-ReLU worse than ReLU and no significant differences across activations/links—authors must explain why reversibility fails or why DCGAN/WGAN behave differently.  
- Minor concern: deformed exponential concepts (“deformation/signature,” “escort”) need intuitive explanations for a general ML audience.  
- Minor concern: Theorem 8’s upper bounds on J(Q) need comparison and interpretation—why decreasing bound with escort normalization Z is beneficial and how to exploit it.  
- Conclusion: theoretical insights are interesting but currently lack empirical evidence and practical guidance for GAN practitioners.
