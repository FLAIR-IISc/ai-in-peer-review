

This paper presents an information geometric perspective on f-GANs, establishing connections between f-divergences and Bregman divergences through the lens of deformed exponential families. The authors demonstrate that the f-GAN optimization problem can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem on parameter space, revealing that the generator converges to the true distribution in parameter space under certain conditions. They further establish a one-to-one correspondence between activation functions in deep neural networks and the parameters of deformed exponential families, showing that standard activation functions like ReLU, ELU, and Softplus satisfy the necessary conditions for this correspondence. The paper also provides a complete characterization of the supervised game by incorporating proper composite losses into the f-GAN framework.

The paper's strengths are significant. The theoretical contribution linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences (Theorem 8) represents a substantial advance in understanding the mathematical foundations of f-GANs. The authors demonstrate that the generator's convergence can be characterized in parameter space, addressing a critical gap in the original f-GAN formulation. The connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families provides a principled approach to generator design, and the experimental results validate these theoretical insights. The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and rigorous proofs (except for Theorem 8, which appears to be correct based on the available evidence).

However, the paper's dense presentation may hinder accessibility for many readers, particularly practitioners who are less familiar with information geometry. The connection between the theoretical contributions and practical implications for GAN implementation needs clearer articulation. Specifically:

1. The paper would benefit from a more explicit statement at the beginning explaining why the information geometric perspective is valuable for GAN practitioners. The current discussion of optimization behavior (mentioned on page 7) appears too late in the paper.

2. Section 5, which discusses proper composite losses, needs stronger justification for how it relates to the f-GAN framework. The connection to practical implementation (e.g., which f-divergences to select or how activation functions affect convergence) is not sufficiently developed.

3. The explanation of why φ_l in equation (13) can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics" requires clarification. The current presentation doesn't clearly demonstrate how these components satisfy the requirements of deformed exponential families.

4. The discussion of utility theory (lines 257-269) appears tangential to the main information geometric narrative and would be better placed in an appendix to free space for core contributions.

5. The experimental section could be more directly connected to the paper's main claims. For instance, the results on activation functions (point A) could be presented without requiring the full information geometric framework, and the WGAN experiments (point B) seem unnecessarily distracting given the paper's focus on f-GANs.

The paper's main weakness is organizational. While the content is rich and technically sound, the results from multiple theoretical threads are presented in a way that feels like they've been "squeezed" into an 8-page limit rather than being coherently structured around a central narrative. This makes it challenging to see how each component contributes to the overall understanding of f-GANs.

To improve the paper, I recommend:
- Moving the helpful Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to immediately illustrate the information theory-geometry connections
- Adding a clear statement about the practical value of the information geometric perspective early in the paper
- Restructuring Section 5 to explicitly connect proper composite losses to the f-GAN optimization process
- Clarifying the relationship between the network components and deformed exponential families
- Streamlining the experimental section to focus exclusively on results that directly support the paper's main claims

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of f-GANs. The paper contains novel results with significant implications for both theory and practice. While the current presentation requires some refinement to maximize accessibility and clarity, the core contributions are valuable and warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the authors should address the organizational and explanatory issues raised above. The paper represents an important step toward a more rigorous theoretical foundation for GANs, which is increasingly crucial as these models become more widely adopted.
