- Paper extends f-GANs to general f-divergences via a chi-logarithm connection, proposing the vig-f-GAN identity and a sufficient condition on activations for factored generative distributions.

- Main theoretical result (vig-f-GAN identity) is interesting and potentially useful but presented in very general, abstract form that obscures concrete implications and applicability.

- Theorems need restatement in more accessible forms with concrete special cases (e.g., KL, JS, standard exponential families), explicit minimal assumptions (smoothness, convexity, regularity), and short interpretations of each term relative to the f-GAN objective.

- The chi-logarithm bridge is compelling but needs better motivation, comparison to other generalizations, and a simple worked example to build intuition.

- The sufficient-condition claim for factored generative distributions should clarify the exact factorization (e.g., per-dimension product), give example activation functions that do and do not satisfy it, and discuss implications for expressivity, optimization, and identifiability.

- Experiments (Section 6) currently do not clearly connect to theory; each experiment should state which theorem/corollary it targets, map plotted quantities to terms in the vig-f-GAN identity, and explicitly label exploratory vs. validating aims.

- Presentation/readability should be improved by reducing abstraction, adding concrete instances and intuitive explanations alongside formal statements, summarizing key takeaways after technical sections, and providing a practical “recipe” for using the identity.

- Minor editorial fix: rephrase the sentence on page 7, line 258 (e.g., “For Eq. (7), this means that when C …”) and ensure consistent equation capitalization/spacing.
