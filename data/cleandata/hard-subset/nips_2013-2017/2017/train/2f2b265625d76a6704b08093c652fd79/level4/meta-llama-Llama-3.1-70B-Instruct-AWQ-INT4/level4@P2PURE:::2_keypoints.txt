1. Proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN and proves convergence in parameter space via a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence using Bregman divergence results.  
2. Provides a practical f-GAN implementation and a factorization of deep network representations, showing a 1-1 mapping between activation functions and the chi/f function.  
3. Appendix proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not fully checked.  
4. Paper is dense with many novel results valuable to ML and information theory; Theorem 4’s IG exposition is particularly strong—recommend accept.  
5. Paper may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG connections (e.g., KL-to-Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality).  
6. Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix into the main text to illustrate IT–IG connections.  
7. Recommend an early, clear statement of the benefits of the IG view, especially its implications for optimization behavior in parameter space.  
8. Unclear how Section 5 relates to f-GAN—needs explanation of how the deformed exponential family result informs f-GAN design or choice of f-divergence/objective.  
9. Request clarification why φ_l in Theorem 6 is called “deep sufficient statistics,” since Equation (13) doesn’t look like a deformed exponential family form.  
10. Lines 257–269 and the utility theory paragraph seem misplaced or unrelated to the IG view—move to appendix or remove to free space for main results.  
11. Experiments do not clearly support the main IG claims; Section 5 and Section 4 experiments are weakly related and inclusion of WGAN results is distracting.  
12. Overall recommendation: accept but reorganize and edit for clarity and coherence—current structure feels like several papers condensed.
