- Proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GAN tying f-divergence and chi-divergence via a Bregman divergence result.  
- Shows a 1–1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and proves f-GAN convergence in parameter space.  
- Provides guidance on a proper way to implement f-GAN.  
- Presents a factorization result for deep neural network representations and links activation choices to the chi/f function.  
- Paper is dense with many new results of interest to ML and information theory.  
- Theorem 4 (IG interpretation of f-GAN) is particularly strong and well-explained.  
- Key contributions: variational IG identity for the f-GAN game, connection between KLχ and f-divergences, and the DNN factorization result.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments generally support the claimed contributions.  
- Paper needs reorganization and clarification to reach a broader audience.  
- The practical benefit of adopting the IG view is not stated clearly enough upfront.  
- Connections between information theory and information geometry in the f-GAN context are not made sufficiently explicit.  
- Section 5 is not clearly tied to the main results and needs clearer linkage.  
- Experiments (varying activations and discriminator link functions) are interesting but not clearly connected to the IG claims.  
- Suggested fixes: move Figure 1 to the main text, state why IG view helps early, clarify Section 5, give context for Theorem 6 and justify phi_l as “deep sufficient statistics.”  
- Recommend reorganizing the paper for coherence and consider moving or removing the utility theory section to free space for main results.
