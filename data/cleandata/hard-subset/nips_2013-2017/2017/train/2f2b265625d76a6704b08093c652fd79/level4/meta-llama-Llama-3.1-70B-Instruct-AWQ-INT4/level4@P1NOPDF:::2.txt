Thank you for an interesting read. This paper proposed an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm, providing novel insights and connections between information theory (IT) and IG. The authors first demonstrated that f-GAN converges in parameter space using the 1-1 mapping of f-divergence and chi-divergence, along with a Bregman divergence result. They also discussed a proper way to implement f-GAN. Furthermore, the main text presented a factorisation result of the deep neural network representation and explored the choice of activation functions, which has a 1-1 mapping to the chi (or f) function. Although I did not thoroughly check every detail in the appendix, the proofs (except for Thm. 8, which I did not have time to read) appear to be correct.

I believe this paper is very dense and contains many new results that could be of interest to the machine learning and information theory communities. I am particularly impressed by the exposition of Thm. 4, which effectively conveys the IG part of the story for f-GAN. Therefore, I think this paper is a clear accept.

However, I also think that this paper could be challenging to understand for those without a strong background in the connections between IT and IG (at least the simplest ones, such as the mapping of KL divergence for an exponential family to Bregman divergence, and the Fenchel duality). Many deep learning engineers may fall into this category, and they are likely the primary audience for a GAN paper. To address this, I suggest the following potential improvements to clarify the paper:

1. I appreciate Fig. 1 in the appendix, which illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context. Consider moving it to the main text to provide a clear visual representation of these connections.
2. It would be helpful to make a clear statement at the beginning of the paper explaining why considering the IG view is beneficial. My understanding is that the geometry in the parameter space can be used to discuss the behavior of optimisation, which was briefly mentioned on page 7, but could be introduced earlier.
3. I am not entirely sure how section 5 connects to f-GAN. It seems to me that the authors used the deformed exponential family to explain the distribution that a deep neural net can represent, rather than as a consequence of f-GAN optimisation results. While I understand the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi, the authors did not discuss how this result could help design the f-GAN game, such as which f divergence to choose or how to select an f-GAN objective given an activation function.
4. Could the authors clarify why phi_l can be viewed as "deep sufficient statistics" in Thm. 6? Equation (13) does not appear to be in the form of a deformed exponential family.
5. Consider moving lines 257-269 to another section. Additionally, the utility theory part seems unrelated to the IG view, so it might be beneficial to remove that paragraph (and potentially include it in the appendix) to free up space to explain the main results.
6. I feel that the experiments are not directly related to the main points claimed in the paper. For example, the results from section 5 could be used to discuss point (A) without requiring the IG view of f-GAN. Similarly, point (B) only requires an understanding of section 4, which is not closely related to the IG view of f-GAN. The inclusion of WGAN results may be distracting and confusing, as this paper primarily focuses on f-GANs. I spent some time locating the sentence about WGAN (lines 227-228).

In summary, while this paper provides many useful results and dense derivations, I believe the material is not organised in a crystal-clear manner. The presentation appears to be a collection of results from multiple papers squeezed into an 8-page NIPS submission. Although I support the acceptance of this paper, I think it requires editing to clarify the claims and make them more coherent.
