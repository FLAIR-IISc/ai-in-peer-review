- Presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, proving parameter-space convergence via a bijection between f-divergences and chi-divergences plus a Bregman divergence result.  
- Addresses practical implementation details and gives a factorization result linking deep network activation choices to the underlying chi (or f) function.  
- Proofs appear sound overall, though Theorem 8 was not fully verified within the review timeframe.  
- Reviewer views the paper as a strong contribution with potential impact on ML and information theory and recommends acceptance.  
- The paper is dense and highly technical, which may hinder accessibility for practitioners lacking IG/IT background.  
- Move Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to provide an early, concrete visualization of the core concepts.  
- Motivate and explain the utility of the IG viewpoint earlier in the paper, emphasizing how geometric parameter-space properties inform optimization behavior.  
- Clarify the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN optimization, showing how the bijection between v, f, and chi informs design choices (e.g., selecting f-divergences or activation functions).  
- Provide stronger justification that φ_l in Theorem 6 are “deep sufficient statistics,” and clarify how Eq. (13) fits a deformed exponential-family structure.  
- Remove or move the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257–269) to the appendix to streamline focus on the IG view.  
- Refocus experiments so they align more directly with the theoretical claims (Section 5 activation-design and Section 4 IG results); deemphasize or better integrate WGAN results.  
- Overall organization feels fragmented (appears as multiple investigations consolidated); recommend a substantial editorial revision to improve clarity and coherence.
