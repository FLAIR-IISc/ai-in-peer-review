This paper explores the extension of the f-GAN principle to f-divergences. By leveraging the chi-logarithm to generalize the relationship between KL divergence and regular exponential families, the authors establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity offers a novel interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Furthermore, the authors derive a sufficient condition for activation functions that yield factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical contribution, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and shows promise for practical applications. However, the presentation of the main theorems in overly general forms makes them challenging to comprehend.
