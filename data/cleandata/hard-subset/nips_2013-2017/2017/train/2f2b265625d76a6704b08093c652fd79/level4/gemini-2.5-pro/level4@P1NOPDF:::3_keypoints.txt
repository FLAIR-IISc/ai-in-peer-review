1. Variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between their parameters (applicable to f-GANs).  
2. Proof that, under a reversibility assumption, deep generative networks can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.  
3. Theoretical suggestion that careful choice of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation can improve GAN performance.  
4. Brief alternative interpretation of the GAN game using expected utility theory.  
5. Major concern: key results rely on the assumption that P and Q belong to the deformed exponential family without justification or evidence this holds in practice (questions about Theorems 4 & 6).  
6. Major concern: practical benefit of the information-geometric identity (Eq.7) is unclear—no implementation or experiments showing parameter-based optimization advantages or realistic cases where distributional convergence fails to imply parameter convergence.  
7. Major concern: lack of experiments validating the theoretically derived discriminator activation g_f versus the heuristic f-GAN choice.  
8. Major concern: weak/contradictory experimental support (μ-ReLU underperforms ReLU despite theory; results within error bars; no discussion of DCGAN reversibility or comparison to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN).  
9. Minor concern: paper needs more intuition for deformed exponential concepts (“deformation,” “signature,” “escort”) for broader accessibility.  
10. Minor concern: Theorem 8’s bounds on the residual J(Q) need comparison and discussion, including why decreasing J(Q) with escort normalization Z is desirable and how to exploit it.  
11. Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but insufficient practical relevance and actionable guidance for GAN practitioners in current form.
