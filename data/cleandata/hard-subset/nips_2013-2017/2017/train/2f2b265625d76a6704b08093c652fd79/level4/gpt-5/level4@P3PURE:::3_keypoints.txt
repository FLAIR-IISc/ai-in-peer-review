- Paper asks three GAN questions: does solving GANs imply parameter convergence, what generator fits at convergence, and how discriminator output activation affects loss compositeness.
- Authors use deformed exponential distributions to derive theoretical results and a variational generalization linking f-divergences over distributions to Bregman divergences over parameters.
- They show (under a reversibility assumption) deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential families and suggest generator/discriminator activation choices could improve GANs; they also note an expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Major practical concern: many results assume data and model lie in the deformed exponential family—no justification that this holds in practice or that results extend beyond it.
- The information-geometric f-GAN identity (Eq. 7) is interesting but the paper lacks clarity on concrete benefits, feasibility of parameter-space optimization, and empirical comparison to distribution-space training.
- Reviewer asks for a realistic scenario where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply parameter-space convergence, since the implication is asserted but not thoroughly motivated.
- Theoretical recipe for discriminator output activation (gf) should be empirically compared to the heuristic from Ref. [34] on standard benchmarks.
- Experimental mismatch: invertible μ-ReLU (satisfying reversibility) underperforms ReLU in MLP GANs—authors should explain; also question whether DCGANs satisfy reversibility and how WGANs fit the analysis.
- Reported performance differences for activations and link functions are within error bars, weakening empirical support for the theoretical claims.
- Minor: framework accessibility needs clearer intuition and definitions for “deformation/signature” and “escort” for broader ML readers.
- Bounds on J(Q) need comparison and practical guidance: how they inform activation choices, why decreasing with escort normalization Z is desirable, and how to exploit this in practice.
- Overall conclusion: theoretically appealing connections (f-divergence ↔ Bregman divergence, escort factorization, discriminator guidance) but limited empirical support and unjustified assumptions reduce practical impact for GAN practitioners.
