- Paper develops an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs, showing f-divergences can be represented as KLχ and tied to Bregman divergences between natural parameters of deformed (χ-)exponential families.  
- Central result (Corollary 8) gives a variational identity equating the f-GAN objective to a parameter-space Bregman divergence DC(θ‖ϑ) plus a generator-dependent escort penalty J(Q).  
- Frames f-GAN training as parameter-space/geometric optimization, analogous to geometric perspectives for Wasserstein GANs.  
- Recasts the discriminator objective as a proper composite loss, clarifying the role of output activation as composition of generator f and link Ψ.  
- Shows deep generator architectures factorize escorts of deformed exponential families: inner layers → sufficient statistics, weight matrix rows → coordinates, activations → χ-signature.  
- Introduces a sufficient condition on activations ("strong admissibility") satisfied by ELU/Softplus and ReLU in a limiting sense.  
- Experiments suggest tuning activations and discriminator links can improve performance, but empirical validation is limited.  
- Strength: Novel variational IG identity generalizing KL/Bregman identities to deformed exponential families.  
- Strength: Clarifies discriminator design via proper-composite loss framework with actionable link-function guidance.  
- Strength: Provides a principled expressivity argument linking network architecture and χ-signature for generators.  
- Strength: Broadly integrates information theory, geometry, and GAN practice in a coherent framework.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and assumes strong background in information geometry and convex duality, hurting accessibility.  
- Weakness/Suggestion: Improve exposition—move key figure to main text, include a high-level roadmap and early intuition.  
- Weakness: Connection between deep factorization and f-GAN optimization is not always explicit; recommend clearer guidance on matching activations to f and implications for training.  
- Weakness: "Deep sufficient statistics" claim needs more intuition and a short proof sketch in the main text.  
- Weakness: Invertibility assumptions and the escort penalty J(Q) may limit practical applicability; discuss approximate invertibility and how to estimate/control J(Q).  
- Weakness: Some content feels tangential; suggest reallocating to supplement and expanding Corollary 8 discussion and worked examples.  
- Weakness: Experiments do not tightly validate theoretical claims (e.g., parameter-space convergence or role of J(Q)); suggest targeted experiments and clearer motivation for WGAN results.  
- Evaluation: Technical development appears sound and convincing, but add small proof sketches and discuss assumptions more.  
- Evaluation: Writing is precise but dense; onboarding readers earlier would help.  
- Evaluation: Contributions are original (variational IG identity, discriminator reinterpretation, deep escort factorization) and potentially significant if clarified and empirically tightened.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical bridge between f-divergences, KLχ, and Bregman geometry; systematic discriminator design; principled guidance for generator architecture with some empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Challenging presentation for broad audience and incomplete/practical experimental validation; WGAN results may confuse scope.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on revision focused on clarity and tighter theory–practice linkage.  
- Questions for authors: Provide a mapping (table/recipe) from common activations to χ and recommended f choices.  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is the theory to non-invertible layers and are there approximate conditions that suffice?  
- Questions for authors: Can J(Q) be estimated or controlled during training, or diagnosed when dominant?  
- Questions for authors: Include a simple synthetic experiment demonstrating parameter-space convergence (tracking DC) predicted by Corollary 8.
