This paper explores the extension of the f-GAN principle to f-divergences, offering a novel perspective on the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families through the chi-logarithm. The authors successfully establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, providing a meaningful interpretation of the f-GAN objective function. Furthermore, they derive a sufficient condition for activation functions that enables the derivation of factored generative distributions.

The main theoretical contribution, the vig-f-GAN identity, is intriguing and holds promise for practical applications. However, the presentation of the main theorems in a highly general form makes it challenging to grasp their significance. It would be beneficial for the authors to provide more concrete examples or illustrations to facilitate a deeper understanding of these results.

The experimental section (Section 6) could be improved by explicitly connecting the observations to the theoretical findings. As it stands, the relationship between the empirical results and the proposed framework is not entirely clear. The authors should consider adding more discussion or analysis to bridge this gap and demonstrate the practical relevance of their theoretical contributions.

Additionally, there are some minor errors in the text that need attention. For instance, on page 7, line 258, the sentence "For eq.(7), is means when C …" is grammatically incorrect and should be revised for clarity.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and original idea, but could benefit from more detailed explanations and concrete examples to make the theoretical results more accessible. With some revisions to address these issues, the paper has the potential to make a more significant impact in the field.
