- Paper introduces an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, proving parameter-space convergence via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and a Bregman divergence result.  
- Proposes a practical implementation approach for f-GAN and presents a factorization result for deep neural representations with activation–chi/f correspondences.  
- Theorem 4 is a particularly strong and elegant articulation of the IG perspective; proofs appear correct overall (Theorem 8 not checked).  
- The work is dense and novel, likely of high interest to researchers in machine learning and information theory; reviewer strongly supports acceptance.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers without IT/IG background (e.g., KL-to-Bregman mapping, Fenchel duality); requires clearer exposition for deep-learning engineers.  
- Move Figure 1 (currently in appendix) to the main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections early.  
- State the practical utility of the IG perspective early (e.g., how parameter-space geometry informs optimization), not only on page 7.  
- Section 5 reads as a general discussion of deformed exponential-family representations rather than a direct outcome of f-GAN optimization; it doesn’t clarify design choices like which f-divergence or activations to pick.  
- The justification for treating φ_l as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6 is unclear; Equation (13) does not evidently match a deformed exponential-family form.  
- Move or remove tangential utility-theory discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix to prioritize core contributions.  
- Experiments are not clearly aligned with central claims: Result (A) could rely solely on Section 5, Result (B) depends mainly on Section 4, and WGAN mentions distract from the f-GAN focus.  
- Overall organization lacks clarity and feels like compressed findings from multiple works; substantial editing is needed to sharpen narrative and align claims with the IG perspective.
