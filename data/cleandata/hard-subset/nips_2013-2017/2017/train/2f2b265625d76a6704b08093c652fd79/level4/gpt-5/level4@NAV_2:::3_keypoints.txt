- Paper addresses: whether solving the GAN game implies parameter convergence; what generator learns at convergence; and how discriminator output activation should be chosen for a proper composite loss.

- Contribution: variational generalization linking f-divergences over densities to Bregman divergences over natural parameters, connecting information-theoretic and information-geometric views.

- Contribution: under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factorize as “escorts” of deformed exponential (χ-exponential) families.

- Contribution: design guidance suggesting specific generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations can improve GAN training by ensuring proper compositeness.

- Contribution: brief alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.

- Overall judgment: elegant theoretical insights but practical impact is currently insufficiently substantiated.

- Concern: scope of the χ-exponential assumption — many key results require P and Q to lie in a deformed exponential family; authors must justify realism and whether results extend beyond this class.

- Concern: practical value of the parameter-space (Bregman) viewpoint — unclear concrete advantages, lack of experiments implementing/compared to parameter-space optimization, and missing examples where distributional convergence does not imply parameter convergence.

- Concern: discriminator output activation & proper compositeness — important theoretical point, but experiments comparing the prescribed gf against heuristics (Ref. [34]) are missing.

- Concern: experimental results vs theory — invertible μ-ReLU (satisfying reversibility) underperforms ReLU in MLP GANs; authors should explain this, verify whether DCGAN experiments meet reversibility, and clarify applicability to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN.

- Concern: empirical evidence weak — many observed differences (activations, link functions) lie within error bars, reducing support for practical claims.

- Minor: need more intuition for deformed exponentials, the deformation/signature χ, and escorts for a broader ML audience.

- Minor: bounds on J(Q) in Theorem 8 — need discussion on comparative tightness, implications for selecting activations, and why decreasing the escort normalization Z is desirable in practice.
