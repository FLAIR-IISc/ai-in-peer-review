- Paper frames GAN analysis in deformed exponential-family information-theoretic terms and derives several theoretical results.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters (information-theoretic ↔ information-geometric connection).  
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generative nets factor into "escorts" of deformed exponential distributions, suggesting design constraints for activations.  
- Authors derive a theoretically motivated discriminator output activation g_f for proper compositeness and propose an expected-utility view of the GAN game.  
- Reviewer questions practical relevance: many results assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family and this assumption is not justified empirically.  
- Request to show whether key theorems (e.g., Theorems 4 and 6) can be derived without the deformed-exponential assumption or to provide empirical support for the assumption.  
- The parameter-centric f-GAN identity (Eq. 7) is interesting, but the reviewer asks what concrete advantages it gives over standard variational f-GAN optimization and whether it can be used to implement GANs in practice.  
- Reviewer asks for experimental comparisons between standard f-GAN optimization and the proposed parameter-based approach to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- The claim that variational f-divergence convergence implies parameter-space convergence (when residual J(Q) is small) is insufficiently justified; reviewer asks for a concrete counterexample where variational convergence does not imply parameter convergence.  
- Theoretically derived activation g_f should be empirically compared to the heuristic used in Ref. [34]; no such comparison is provided.  
- Empirical concern: mu-ReLU (theoretically motivated, reversible) performs worse than baseline ReLU in MLP experiments, contradicting expectations and lacking explanation.  
- Reviewer asks whether common architectures (e.g., DCGAN) satisfy the reversibility assumption and requests comparisons to approaches outside the f-divergence framework (e.g., WGAN).  
- Reported performance differences are within error margins in Table 2, providing weak empirical evidence for the theoretical claims.  
- Minor requests: provide intuitive explanations for "deformation"/"signature" and "escort," compare the multiple upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8, and explain practical implications of the escort normalization parameter Z and how to leverage it.  
- Overall verdict: mathematically sound but currently of limited practical value due to restrictive assumptions, lack of empirical validation, and unclear practical benefits; authors should supply justification, broader experiments, and clearer practical guidance.
