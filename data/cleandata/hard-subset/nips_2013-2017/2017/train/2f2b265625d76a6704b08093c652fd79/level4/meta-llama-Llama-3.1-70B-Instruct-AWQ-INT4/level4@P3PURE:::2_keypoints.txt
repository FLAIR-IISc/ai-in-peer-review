- Paper proposes an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, offering a novel viewpoint.  
- Authors prove convergence of f-GAN in parameter space using a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and Bregman divergence results.  
- Paper discusses proper implementation details for f-GAN.  
- Presents a factorization result for deep neural network representations and analyzes activation functions with a 1-1 mapping to chi/f functions.  
- Appendix proofs generally appear correct, though Theorem 8 was not reviewed by the reviewer.  
- Theorem 4 effectively communicates the IG aspect and is a highlight of the paper.  
- Paper is dense with many new results of interest to ML and information theory communities; overall recommendation: clear accept.  
- Paper may be hard to follow for readers lacking background in information theory/geometry and Fenchel duality.  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 (currently in appendix) to the main text to introduce IT–IG connections earlier.  
- Recommend an early, explicit statement of why the IG view is beneficial, especially for understanding optimization behavior.  
- Unclear how Section 5 connects to f-GANs; need explicit explanation of how the deformed exponential family result informs f-GAN design or choice of f-divergence.  
- Theorem 6’s claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” is not convincing; Equation (13) does not resemble a deformed exponential family.  
- Recommend removing or relocating the utility theory paragraph (lines 257–269) as it seems unrelated to the IG narrative.  
- Experiments seem weakly connected to the main IG claims; inclusion of WGAN results is distracting since the focus is f-GANs.  
- Paper appears like a condensed collection of multiple papers and would benefit from reorganization and clearer exposition to improve flow and accessibility.
