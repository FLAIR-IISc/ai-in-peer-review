This paper examines the f-GAN framework extended to general f-divergences. By broadening the classical link between KL divergence and regular exponential families through the χ-logarithm, the authors derive a variational, information-geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity that explains the f-GAN objective. They also establish a sufficient condition on activation functions that yields factored generative distributions.

The principal theoretical contribution—the vig-f-GAN identity—is appealing and appears broadly applicable. However, the statements of the main theorems are difficult to interpret because they are presented in highly general form.
