- Paper presents theoretical contributions linking f-divergences to parameter-space Bregman divergences via a variational generalization compatible with f-GANs.  
- Authors show (under a reversibility assumption) deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.  
- Paper proposes specific choices for generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation to improve GAN training and loss compositeness.  
- Also offers an alternative interpretation of the GAN objective through expected utility theory.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on the deformed exponential family assumption without justification or evidence that real-world GAN data satisfy it.  
- Question whether key theorems (e.g., Theorems 4 and 6) can be generalized beyond the deformed exponential family.  
- Practical utility of the information-geometric identity (Eq. 7) is unclear—no experiments compare training directly in parameter space versus the standard variational approach.  
- Need for a concrete scenario showing when convergence in f-divergence would not imply convergence in parameter space to demonstrate non-triviality.  
- Lack of empirical validation that the theoretically derived discriminator output activation (gf) outperforms common heuristic choices.  
- Experimental results are ambiguous: μ-ReLU (theoretically motivated) performs worse than standard ReLU, contradicting theory.  
- Unclear whether the DCGAN architecture used satisfies the reversibility assumption, complicating interpretation of experiments and relevance to non-f-divergence methods like WGAN.  
- Reported performance differences appear within error bars, so statistical significance and practical impact of activation/link choices are not established.  
- Clarity issue: paper uses deformed exponential terminology (deformation, signature, escort) without intuitive explanations for a broad ML audience.  
- Ambiguity about the implications of Theorem 8 bounds: unclear relations between bounds, guidance for activation selection, and the practical role of the escort normalization parameter Z.  
- Overall summary: interesting theoretical lens on GANs, but practical implications and empirical support are insufficiently developed.
