- Paper studies several GAN questions and contributes: (i) a variational/generalized f-GAN identity linking f-divergences to parameter-space Bregman divergences.  
- (ii) A factorization showing reversible deep generators can be expressed as escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- (iii) Recommendations that careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation could improve GAN training.  
- (iv) An alternative interpretation of the GAN game via expected utility theory.  
- Major concern: many results require P and Q to lie in the deformed exponential family — the reviewer asks how often this holds in practice and whether results can be generalized.  
- Questioning practical benefit of the parameter-space f-GAN identity: what concrete gains, can one train directly in parameter space, and empirical comparisons are missing.  
- Reviewer asks for a realistic scenario where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply parameter convergence (to justify the implication).  
- Practical implication on discriminator output activation gf is interesting, but experiments comparing the theoretically derived gf versus the heuristic choice are missing.  
- Experimental concern: under reversibility, μ-ReLU should help but performs worse than ReLU in GAN MLP — authors should explain the underperformance.  
- Clarification requested on whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility and how results relate to architectures like WGAN that do not use f-divergences.  
- Empirical differences reported (Table 2 center/right) are within error bars, so results do not convincingly support the theoretical recommendations.  
- Minor exposition: provide more intuition on deformed exponential densities, deformations/signatures, and escort distributions for ML readers.  
- Minor technical: compare tightness of Theorem 8 bounds on J(Q), explain practical implications for activation choice, and why decreasing J(Q) with escort normalization Z is desirable and actionable.  
- Overall verdict: interesting theoretical insights but, as presented and evaluated, lacking sufficient practical relevance and empirical support for GAN practitioners.
