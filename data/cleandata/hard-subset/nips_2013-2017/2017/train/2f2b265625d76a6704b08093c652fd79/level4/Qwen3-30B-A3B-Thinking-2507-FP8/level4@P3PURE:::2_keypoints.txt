- Presents an information-geometric perspective on f-GAN with several novel theoretical contributions.  
- Proves convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence combined with a Bregman divergence result.  
- Provides practical implementation guidance and derives a factorization of deep network representations linking activation functions to chi-/f-divergences.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not reviewed.  
- Theorem 4 (IG foundation for f-GAN) is especially compelling and a highlight of the paper.  
- Reviewer recommends strong acceptance based on significance and rigor.  
- Paper is currently inaccessible to deep learning practitioners due to assumed IT/IG background and missing basic explanations (e.g., KL↔Bregman, Fenchel duality).  
- Suggestion: move Figure 1 from appendix to main text to illustrate IT–IG connections explicitly.  
- Suggestion: add an early clear statement explaining why the IG perspective is useful for analyzing optimization in parameter space.  
- Clarify Section 5’s relevance to f-GAN by showing how the deformed exponential family characterization informs f-GAN design (e.g., choosing f-divergences or activations).  
- Justify viewing φₗ in Theorem 6 as “deep sufficient statistics,” since Eq. (13) does not match the standard deformed exponential family form.  
- Editorial changes: move lines 257–269 (and the utility theory paragraph) to the appendix to free space for core contributions.  
- Realign experiments to support central claims: activation-function results don’t require the IG framework, Section 4 is only tangentially related to IG, and the brief WGAN mention is distracting.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical insights but needs substantial reorganization and clarification to strengthen the IG narrative and ensure experiments directly support contributions.
