- Paper studies when solving GAN objectives implies parameter-space convergence, what generators fit at convergence, and how discriminator output activations affect loss compositeness.  
- Analysis is framed within deformed exponential distributions and yields several theoretical results.  
- Key theoretical contribution: a variational generalization connecting f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between their parameters.  
- Shows an information-theoretic ↔ information-geometric link between distributional and parameter dissimilarities.  
- Under a reversibility assumption, deep generators factor as escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Suggests that careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activations could improve GANs.  
- Major concern: many results require P and Q to be in the deformed exponential family—authors must justify this assumption and its practical validity.  
- Unclear practical benefit of the parameter-space (information-geometric) formulation and whether GANs can be implemented/optimized in that form.  
- The RHS of the f-GAN identity is underused; claim that solving the GAN implies parameter convergence needs clearer motivation and counterexamples.  
- Experimental comparisons between distributional and parameter-based optimization are missing but would be useful.  
- Need experiments comparing the theoretically derived gf activation to the heuristic from Ref. [34].  
- μ-ReLU (theoretically motivated) performs worse than baseline ReLU in experiments; authors should explain this and whether DCGAN meets the reversibility assumption.  
- Several experimental results (activation/link comparisons) are within error bars and do not strongly support theoretical claims.  
- The paper lacks intuitive explanations of “deformation/signature” and “escort,” limiting accessibility to the broader ML community.  
- Authors should clarify implications and comparisons of the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8.  
- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical insights but insufficient practical relevance and experimental support; current form is not ready for acceptance.
