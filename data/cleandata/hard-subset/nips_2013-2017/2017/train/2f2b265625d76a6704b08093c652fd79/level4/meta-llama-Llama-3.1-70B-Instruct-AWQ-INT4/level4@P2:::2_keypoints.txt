- Proposes an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN, analyzing convergence in parameter space.  
- Establishes a 1-1 mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence using a Bregman divergence result.  
- Presents guidance for proper f-GAN implementation and a factorization result for deep network representations.  
- Highlights activation function choices that map 1-1 to the chi (or f) function.  
- Theorem 4 (IG characterization for f-GAN) is a standout result with impressive exposition.  
- Paper is dense, contains many novel results, and proofs appear correct.  
- Likely difficult for readers lacking strong information theory or information geometry background.  
- Suggestion: move Figure 1 to main text to illustrate IT–IG connections in f-GAN.  
- Suggestion: state early the benefits of the IG view, e.g., how parameter-space geometry informs optimization behavior.

- Suggestion: justify why phi_l in Theorem 6 qualifies as "deep sufficient statistics" since eq. (13) doesn’t look like a deformed exponential family.
