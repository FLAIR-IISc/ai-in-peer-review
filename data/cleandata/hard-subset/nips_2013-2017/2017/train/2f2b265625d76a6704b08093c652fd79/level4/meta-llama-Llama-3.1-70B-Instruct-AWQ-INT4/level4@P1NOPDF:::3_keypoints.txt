- Authors pose interesting GAN questions: parameter-space convergence, generator fit at convergence, and discriminator output activation choice for proper compositeness.
- Work uses deformed exponential distributions to derive several theoretical results.
- Present a variational generalization linking f-divergences between distributions to Bregman divergences between parameters, connecting information-theoretic and information-geometric views.
- Show that, under a reversibility assumption, deep generators can be factored as escorts of deformed exponential distributions.
- Theoretical results suggest careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation could improve GANs; they also note an expected-utility interpretation of the GAN game.
- Major concern: many theorems assume P and Q lie in the deformed exponential family—need justification if this holds in practice and whether results extend beyond this family.
- The information-geometric f-GAN identity (Eq.7) is interesting, but authors must clarify practical benefits of the parameter-based viewpoint and whether GANs can be implemented/optimized this way.
- Lack of experiments comparing distribution-based vs parameter-based optimization; authors largely do not exploit the RHS of Eq.7 beyond claiming parameter convergence when J(Q) is small.
- Request for example where convergence in variational f-divergence does not imply parameter-space convergence—the implication is not obviously true.
- Practical implication about discriminator output activation gf (composition of f′ and link) is interesting; authors should experimentally compare their theoretically derived gf to the heuristic from Ref.[34].
- Reversibility-based result predicts invertible μ-ReLU should help, but experiments (Table 2 left) show μ-ReLU worse than ReLU—authors should explain this and discuss whether DCGAN satisfies reversibility and comparisons to Wasserstein GAN.
- Reported activation/link-function comparisons (Table 2 center/right) are within error bars and thus provide weak empirical support for theoretical claims.
- Minor: community may need more intuition/examples for deformations (signatures) and escorts.
- In Theorem 8, authors derive multiple upper bounds for J(Q)—they should compare these bounds, clarify implications for activation choice, and explain why decreasing J(Q) with escort normalization Z is beneficial and how to leverage it.
- Overall judgment: interesting theoretical contributions, but current practical relevance and usefulness to GAN practitioners are limited.
