This paper proposes an information geometric (IG) view of the f-GAN algorithm. The authors demonstrate that f-GAN convergence can be understood through a mapping between f-divergences, chi-divergences, and Bregman divergences, establishing a connection to parameter space geometry. They further discuss a principled implementation of f-GAN, and provide a factorization result for deep neural network representations, linking activation function choice to the chi (or f) function. The proofs appear correct, though a detailed check of all appendix material wasn’t possible within the review timeframe.

This is a dense and theoretically rich paper with many potentially valuable results for both machine learning and information theory. The exposition of Theorem 6, which elucidates the IG aspects of f-GAN, is particularly impressive. Overall, this paper represents a strong contribution and I recommend acceptance. 

However, the paper’s complexity and reliance on specialized background knowledge (information theory and information geometry) may hinder accessibility for a broad audience, particularly deep learning practitioners who are the primary target for GAN-related work. The following suggestions aim to improve clarity and coherence:

**1. Enhanced Visual Introduction to IG Concepts:** I agree with the authors’ internal assessment that Figure 1 in the supplementary material, illustrating the connection between IT and IG within the f-GAN context, would significantly benefit the main text. Placing this figure earlier would provide crucial context for readers less familiar with these concepts.

**2. Motivation for the IG View:** The paper would benefit from a clearer upfront statement of *why* considering an IG perspective is beneficial. While implicitly present, explicitly stating that the geometry of the parameter space enables analysis of optimization behavior (as briefly mentioned on page 7, but perhaps too late) would strengthen the motivation.

**3. Clarifying the Connection Between Section 5 and f-GAN Optimization:** The link between Section 5, which details the representation power of deep networks in terms of deformed exponential families, and the core f-GAN optimization results isn't immediately clear. While the 1-1 correspondence between v, f, and chi is established, it remains unclear how this factorization informs f-GAN design—for example, how to choose an appropriate f-divergence given an activation function, or vice versa, and what convergence benefits might arise. More explicit discussion of these practical implications would be valuable.

**4. Justification for "Deep Sufficient Statistics":** The assertion that phi_l in Theorem 6 represents "deep sufficient statistics" requires further justification. Given that equation (13) doesn’t appear to conform to a standard deformed exponential family form, elaborating on this connection would strengthen the argument.

**5. Streamlining the Discussion of Proper Composite Losses:** The material on utility theory (lines 257-269) feels somewhat tangential to the central IG view of f-GAN. While interesting, removing this section (perhaps relegating it to the appendix) would free space for more detailed explanations of the core results.

**6. Re-focusing Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental results, while valid, feel somewhat disconnected from the paper's central claims. The utility of the theoretical developments (particularly the factorization result in Section 5) could be better highlighted by demonstrating how they directly inform activation function design. The WGAN results, while potentially insightful, risk distracting from the primary focus on f-GANs. A more targeted experimental evaluation would strengthen the narrative. For instance, demonstrating improved performance on f-GAN tasks specifically by leveraging the insights gained from the activation function analysis would be compelling.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper presents a novel and insightful information geometric perspective on f-GANs, bridging a gap between information theory and generative modeling.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The core theoretical results appear well-founded, with rigorous proofs (as far as could be assessed).
*   **Potential Significance:** The insights gained could lead to principled approaches for designing GAN architectures, activation functions, and loss functions.
*   **Completeness:** The paper provides a comprehensive treatment of the topic, covering both theoretical foundations and experimental validation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance (Mitigated by Improvements):**

*   **Clarity:** The paper is dense and challenging to follow for those without a strong background in information theory and information geometry.
*   **Coherence:** The organization of material sometimes feels disjointed, with connections between different sections not always clearly articulated.
*   **Experimental Focus:** The experimental evaluation doesn’t fully leverage the theoretical contributions, potentially diminishing their impact.



In summary, while this paper could benefit from further refinement to enhance clarity and coherence, its significant theoretical contributions and potential impact warrant acceptance. Addressing the suggested improvements would undoubtedly elevate the work and broaden its accessibility.
