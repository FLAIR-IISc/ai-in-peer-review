- Proposes a variational generalization linking f-GANs (f-divergences) to information geometry, culminating in the vig-f-GAN identity that interprets the f-GAN objective.  
- Leverages a theorem relating KL between exponential families to divergences between natural parameters to derive a geometric optimization view.  
- Main theoretical result (vig-f-GAN identity) is novel and potentially broadly applicable.  
- Presentation of the main theorems lacks clarity and would benefit from more intuitive explanations or illustrations.  
- Analyzes information-theoretic and information-geometric components, illuminating the supervised game perspective and generator activation design.  
- Shows that standard deep generator architectures can model complex escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Provides a sufficient condition on activation functions to guarantee this modeling capability.  
- Demonstrates that common activations (ELU, ReLU, Softplus) satisfy the sufficient condition.  
- Experimental results compare activations and link functions but feel disconnected from the theoretical contributions.  
- Paper needs more discussion/analysis to bridge theory and experiments and clarify how empirical observations follow from theory.  
- Minor writing issues (e.g., grammatically incorrect clause on page 7, line 258) should be fixed.  
- Overall: interesting and promising theoretical contributions, but requires clearer presentation and tighter experimental-theoretical linkage before acceptance.
