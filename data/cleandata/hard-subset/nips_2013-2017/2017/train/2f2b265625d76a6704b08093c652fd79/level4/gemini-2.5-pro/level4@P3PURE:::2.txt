This paper presents a novel and interesting information geometric (IG) perspective on the f-GAN framework. The authors first establish that f-GAN converges in parameter space by leveraging the one-to-one mapping between f-divergences and chi-divergences, along with a key result on Bregman divergence. The paper also discusses a principled approach to implementing f-GANs. A significant contribution is a factorization result for deep neural network representations, which connects the choice of activation functions to the chi (and therefore f) function. The proofs I was able to check appear correct (with the exception of Theorem 8, which I did not have time to review before the deadline).

The paper is dense and contains numerous results that will be of interest to both the machine learning and information theory communities. I was particularly impressed with the exposition of Theorem 4, which elegantly articulates the information geometric story underpinning f-GANs. For these reasons, I believe the paper is a clear accept.

However, the paper's density and reliance on specialized knowledge present a major barrier to accessibility. Readers unfamiliar with the connections between information theory and information geometry (e.g., the relationship between KL divergence in exponential families and Bregman divergence, or Fenchel duality) will likely find the paper very difficult to follow. Since many deep learning practitioners—the primary audience for a GAN paper—may fall into this category, I believe the paper would benefit significantly from revisions aimed at improving clarity.

The following are specific suggestions to make the paper clearer and more coherent:

1.  **Improve visual aids:** Figure 1 in the appendix, which illustrates the connections between IT and IG in the f-GAN context, is extremely helpful. I strongly recommend moving it into the main text to provide readers with a conceptual map early on.

2.  **Motivate the IG view earlier:** The benefits of adopting an IG perspective should be stated clearly at the beginning of the paper. My understanding is that its primary utility is in analyzing the optimization dynamics in the parameter space. This is a powerful motivation, but it is mentioned only briefly on page 7, which is too late to frame the reader's understanding of the paper's goals.

3.  **Clarify the contribution of Section 5:** The connection between the DNN representation analysis in Section 5 and the core f-GAN framework is not well-articulated. While the one-to-one correspondence between the activation function `v`, the convex function `f`, and the `chi` function is an elegant result, the paper does not explain how this insight can be used to better design the f-GAN objective. For instance, does this theory help us select an optimal f-divergence for a given network architecture, or choose an activation function to improve convergence?

4.  **Justify terminology in Theorem 6:** The paper describes the feature vectors `phi_l` as "deep sufficient statistics." However, it is not clear how Equation (13) conforms to the standard form of a deformed exponential family, making this terminology potentially confusing. A more detailed justification is needed.

5.  **Reorganize for better flow:** The motivational text on lines 257-269 would be more effective in the introduction. Additionally, the paragraph on utility theory seems tangential to the main IG narrative. Removing it from the main text (perhaps moving it to the appendix) would free up valuable space to expand upon the core results.

6.  **Strengthen the link between theory and experiments:** The experiments feel disconnected from the central IG thesis of the paper.
    *   (A) The results on new activation functions seem to follow directly from the representation theory in Section 5 and do not require the IG framework.
    *   (B) The implementation improvements are based on Section 4 and are not explicitly tied to the IG convergence analysis.
    *   The inclusion of WGAN results is distracting in a paper focused on f-GANs. The connection is mentioned only briefly (lines 227-228) and could easily be missed by the reader.

In summary, this paper provides a collection of dense and valuable theoretical results. However, the material is not organized in a crystal-clear manner, giving the impression of multiple distinct research threads being condensed into a single submission. While I am supportive of acceptance due to the significance of the contributions, I strongly urge the authors to revise the paper to make the claims clearer, the narrative more coherent, and the arguments more accessible.
