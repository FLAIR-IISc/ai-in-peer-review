- Paper generalizes f-GANs via information geometry by introducing a chi-logarithm and the vig-f-GAN identity linking f-divergences to exponential-family structure.
- Main theoretical contribution is interesting and potentially impactful but presented too abstractly, hindering accessibility.

- Strength: Ambitious, original extension of f-GANs grounded in information geometry.
- Strength: vig-f-GAN identity appears to unify variational representations across divergences and offers a principled interpretation beyond KL/JS.
- Strength: Activation-function condition for factored generative models could be useful if it yields practical design guidance.

- Weakness: Theorems are overly general with limited intuition, obscuring core message and applicability.
- Weakness: Unclear which class of f-divergences the chi-logarithm and identity apply to (convexity, differentiability, smoothness, domain restrictions).
- Weakness: Experiments do not clearly validate the theory; unclear which hypotheses or theorems are tested and how to interpret results.
- Weakness: Activation-function condition is stated only as sufficient without discussion of necessity, scope, or which common activations satisfy it.
- Weakness: Notation is dense and introduced abruptly; many objects lack quick intuitive explanations before formal statements.

- Suggestion: Provide intuition and special cases—show KL reduces to standard log and recover classical variational forms, and work out reverse KL and JS examples.
- Suggestion: Include a simple toy example (e.g., Gaussians) to demonstrate how the identity guides discriminator choice.
- Suggestion: State precise conditions on f (convexity, lower semicontinuity, differentiability, Legendre type, domain restrictions) required for chi-logarithm and identity.
- Suggestion: Make explicit the discriminator function classes used in variational suprema and discuss approximation error from restricting them.
- Suggestion: Precede theorems with short intuition paragraphs and follow with sanity-check corollaries in familiar cases; move heavy measure-theoretic statements to the appendix.
- Suggestion: Clarify the activation-function condition with interpretable statement and examples (ReLU, leaky ReLU, ELU, GELU, tanh, softplus) and discuss interaction with architecture.
- Suggestion: Empirically validate factorization (pairwise correlations, mutual information, total correlation) and ablate activation choices.
- Suggestion: Connect each experiment to a specific theoretical claim, run ablations over divergences and activations, and report stability, gradient variance, and sample quality metrics.
- Suggestion: Provide implementation details, a roadmap of mathematical objects, a notation table, and pseudocode/algorithmic description for training under vig-f-GAN.

- Questions: For which precise class of f-divergences does the chi-logarithm exist and yield the identity, and are common divergences excluded?
- Questions: How sensitive are results to restricting the discriminator class and can approximation bias be bounded?
- Questions: Does the activation-function condition imply independence only at output or throughout latent representation, and how do BN, residuals, or attention affect it?
- Questions: Do certain f choices yield optimization or stability benefits predicted by the identity, and is there empirical evidence?

- Minor comment: Fix typos and phrasing (e.g., p.7 l.258), be consistent in equation/reference capitalization and spacing, and define symbols at first use.

- Overall assessment: Compelling theoretical generalization with promise, but needs clearer assumptions, concrete special cases, and experiments directly validating theoretical claims to be a strong contribution.
