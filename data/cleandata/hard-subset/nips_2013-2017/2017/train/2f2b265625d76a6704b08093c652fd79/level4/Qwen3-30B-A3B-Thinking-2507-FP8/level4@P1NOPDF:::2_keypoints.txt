- Introduces an information geometric (IG) perspective on f-GAN and shows convergence in parameter space via a one-to-one correspondence between f-divergence and chi-squared divergence.  
- Supports the convergence claim with a Bregman divergence result and provides a practical implementation framework for f-GAN.  
- Provides a factorization result linking deep network activation functions to the chi (or f) function via a one-to-one correspondence.  
- Proofs appear correct overall, though Theorem 8 was not verified by the reviewer due to time constraints.  
- Theorem 4 is well-exposed and effectively frames the IG perspective for f-GAN.  
- Paper is dense with novel theoretical results that will interest ML and information theory researchers.  
- Technical depth and reliance on IT/IG background reduce accessibility for deep learning practitioners.  
- Suggest moving Appendix Figure 1 to the main text to summarize IT-IG connections early.  
- Recommend stating the motivation for the IG view earlier (e.g., to analyze optimization behavior in parameter space).  
- Section 5’s relevance to f-GAN is unclear; it links activations to f-divergences but doesn’t explain implications for f-GAN design or convergence.  
- Theorem 6’s claim that φₗ are “deep sufficient statistics” needs clarification; Eq. (13) doesn’t clearly match deformed exponential family form.  
- The utility theory paragraph seems tangential and should be moved to the appendix to free space for core clarifications.  
- Experiments do not directly support the central theoretical claims; activation results could be separated from the IG framework.  
- WGAN results are peripheral and distracting from the f-GAN focus, and are hard to locate.  
- Overall organization is crowded and needs substantial editing to sharpen narrative, explicitly link sections to f-GAN, and improve accessibility; recommend acceptance with these revisions.
