This paper proposes a novel framework for understanding the f-GAN principle, which generalizes the connection between the KL divergence and regular exponential families. The authors introduce the concept of deformed exponential families and utilize the χ-logarithm to establish the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity provides a new interpretation of the objective function of the f-GAN and offers insights into the geometric optimization problem.

The main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and has promising applications. However, the presentation of the main theorems is quite general, making it challenging to follow their meanings. For instance, Theorem 5 and Theorem 6 are crucial to the development of the vig-f-GAN identity, but their implications and relationships to the rest of the paper are not immediately clear.

The authors also investigate the properties of deep architectures in the vig-f-GAN game and prove a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions. This result is significant, as it provides a theoretical understanding of the power of deep architectures in the f-GAN framework.

The experimental section is somewhat disconnected from the theoretical results. While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several datasets, it is unclear how the observations in the experiments relate to the theoretical findings. For example, the comparison of varying activations in the generator and the comparison of varying link functions in the discriminator are interesting, but their connection to the vig-f-GAN identity and the theoretical results is not explicitly stated.

There are also some minor issues with the presentation. For instance, the sentence structure is sometimes incorrect, as noted in the comment on page 7, line 258. Additionally, some of the references are not properly formatted.

Overall, this paper presents an innovative framework for understanding the f-GAN principle and provides valuable insights into the geometric optimization problem. However, the presentation could be improved to make the main results more accessible and the connections between the theoretical and experimental sections clearer.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting framework for understanding the f-GAN principle.
* The vig-f-GAN identity is a significant contribution to the field, providing a new interpretation of the objective function of the f-GAN.
* The authors investigate the properties of deep architectures in the vig-f-GAN game and prove a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions.

Arguments for rejection:

* The presentation of the main theorems is quite general, making it challenging to follow their meanings.
* The experimental section is somewhat disconnected from the theoretical results.
* There are minor issues with the presentation, such as incorrect sentence structures and improperly formatted references.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting this paper, but with minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above. The authors should strive to make the main results more accessible and clarify the connections between the theoretical and experimental sections. Additionally, they should correct the minor errors in presentation and formatting.
