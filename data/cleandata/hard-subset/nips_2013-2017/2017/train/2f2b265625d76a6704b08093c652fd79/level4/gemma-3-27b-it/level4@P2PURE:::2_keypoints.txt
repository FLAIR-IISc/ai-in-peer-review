- Presents an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, showing convergence in parameter space via a mapping between f-divergence and chi-divergence and Bregman divergence results.  
- Proposes a refined f-GAN implementation and a factorization result linking deep network representations and activation choices to the chi/f function.  
- Proofs generally appear sound on inspection, though Theorem 8 was not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Theorem 4 is a standout, clearly elucidating the IG foundation of f-GAN.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on substantive and valuable results for ML and information theory.  
- Paper is dense and technically deep, likely creating a barrier for readers unfamiliar with IT/IG and Fenchel duality.  
- Suggest moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to better illustrate IT–IG connections.  
- Recommend earlier and clearer articulation of the concrete benefits of the IG viewpoint (e.g., how geometry informs optimization behavior).  
- Connection between Section 5 and f-GAN is unclear; paper does not explain how the deformed exponential-family characterization informs f-GAN design or divergence/activation choices.  
- Rationale for calling phi_l “deep sufficient statistics” (Theorem 6) is unclear and Equation (13) does not appear to match a deformed exponential-family form.  
- Suggest relocating the utility-theory discussion (lines 257–269) to the appendix as it seems tangential to the IG core.  
- Experiments weakly support central claims: activation-function results rely on Section 5 independently of the IG view, WGAN results are distracting and not well integrated with the f-GAN focus.  
- Paper reads like multiple investigations compressed into one submission and would benefit from editing to improve clarity and coherence.
