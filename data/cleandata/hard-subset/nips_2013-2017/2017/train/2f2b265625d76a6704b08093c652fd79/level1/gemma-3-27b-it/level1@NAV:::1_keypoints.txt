- Addresses gap in understanding generator convergence and its relation to network architecture for f-GANs.  
- Shows convergence occurs for a broad class of distributions (deformed exponential families).  
- Proves an identity linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences, providing an information-geometric interpretation (eq. 3).  
- Argues deep neural networks are naturally well-suited to represent deformed exponential families.  
- Discusses proper composite losses and their implications for the discriminator’s role.  
- Experimental results provide initial validation and show benefits of tuning activation/link functions.  
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous mathematical derivations.  
- Dense mathematical notation sometimes obscures conceptual flow and clarity.  
- Limited experimental scope: lacks rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art GANs and extensive ablation studies.  
- Practical implications for complex, high-dimensional settings are under-discussed.  
- The sufficient condition for activation functions is asserted but not fully explored for common architectures.  
- The “penalty” term in eq. (3) needs more detailed contextualization.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, due to significant theoretical contribution and potential impact despite clarity and experimental limitations.
