## Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper delves into the theoretical underpinnings of f-GANs, building upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2017) which extended the GAN principle to f-divergences. The authors identify a key gap in that work – a lack of understanding of generator convergence and its implications for design. They bridge this gap by demonstrating that convergence occurs within a broad class of distributions, namely deformed exponential families, and importantly, show that deep architectures are well-suited to model these distributions. The core contribution lies in establishing a variational information-geometric identity connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences, providing a geometric interpretation of the GAN game.

The paper is technically sound and presents a compelling argument. The mathematical derivations appear rigorous, and the connection to information geometry is clearly articulated. The identification of a sufficient condition for activation functions, satisfied by popular choices like ReLU and ELU, is a valuable insight. The experimental validation, while limited in scope, provides initial support for the theoretical findings, demonstrating potential improvements through principled activation function design and link function tuning. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations and point towards future research directions.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. While the theoretical contributions are significant, the practical implications remain somewhat unclear. The experiments are relatively small-scale and focus primarily on MNIST and LSUN, limiting the generalizability of the findings. The discussion of the experimental results could be more in-depth, providing a more nuanced analysis of the observed improvements. Furthermore, the paper assumes a level of familiarity with information geometry that may be challenging for some readers. While the authors attempt to provide definitions, the concepts are complex and could benefit from more intuitive explanations.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically grounded contribution to the understanding of f-GANs.
*   Establishes a clear connection between f-divergences, Bregman divergences, and deep architectures.
*   Provides actionable insights for GAN design, particularly regarding activation functions and link functions.
*   Rigorous mathematical derivations and careful analysis.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited scope of experimental validation.
*   Potential accessibility issues for readers unfamiliar with information geometry.
*   Practical implications of the theoretical results could be more clearly articulated.



**Overall:**

Despite some weaknesses, this paper represents a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs. The novel connection to information geometry and the insights into generator convergence are valuable. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the authors should consider adding a more detailed discussion of the experimental results and providing more intuitive explanations of the key concepts for a broader audience.
