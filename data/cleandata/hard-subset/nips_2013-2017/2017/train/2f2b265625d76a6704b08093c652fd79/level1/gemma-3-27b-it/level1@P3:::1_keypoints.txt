- Core contribution: connects f-divergences and Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families, giving a geometric interpretation of the GAN game.  
- Theoretical soundness: key theorems (notably Corollary 8 and Theorem 9) are clear and proofs appear solid (in supplement).  
- Generator capacity: shows deep architectures can model deformed exponential families, linking activation choices to modeling escorts.  
- Experimental support: limited but consistent with theory, especially regarding activation functions and link tuning.  
- Overclaim: the paper’s statement of “completing the supervised understanding” is overstated.  
- Loss analysis: treatment of proper composite losses and the link function’s role is valuable and well explained.  
- Assumption concern: reliance on network reversibility is potentially restrictive and not always realistic.  
- Clarity issues: well-written overall but dense mathematical notation and some inconsistent divergence notation hinder accessibility.  
- Suggested improvements: add more intuitive explanations, examples, and ensure supplement is accessible and well-formatted.  
- Originality: offers a novel information-geometric perspective and extends prior work; activation–escort link is particularly original.  
- Related work gap: should discuss implicit generative modeling and normalizing flows more thoroughly.  
- Significance: theoretical framework could improve GAN design, stability, and unify variants (including Wasserstein GANs).  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with minor revisions to clarity and related-work coverage.
