The paper "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell" presents a novel approach to understanding the f-GAN principle in an information geometric framework. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the f-GAN game, extending the original formulation to a broader class of distributions, namely deformed exponential families. They establish a variational information geometric identity that connects the f-GAN game to a geometric optimization problem, providing a new characterization of the generators' convergence.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of summarizing the main ideas and relating them to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere. The introduction provides a clear overview of the f-GAN principle and its limitations, while the rest of the paper presents the authors' contributions in a logical and well-structured manner.

The strengths of the paper lie in its technical soundness, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the f-GAN game, and their results are well-supported by theoretical derivations and experimental evidence. The paper is also well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the significance of their contributions.

One of the key contributions of the paper is the establishment of a variational information geometric identity that connects the f-GAN game to a geometric optimization problem. This result provides a new characterization of the generators' convergence and sheds light on the information-theoretic and information-geometric parts of the f-GAN game.

The authors also provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. They compare different activation functions and link functions in the generator and discriminator, respectively, and show that their approach can lead to improved performance in certain cases.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. One of the limitations of the paper is that it focuses primarily on the theoretical aspects of the f-GAN game, without providing a comprehensive evaluation of the approach in practice. While the experimental results are encouraging, they are limited to a few specific cases, and more extensive experiments are needed to fully assess the effectiveness of the approach.

Another limitation of the paper is that it assumes a certain level of familiarity with information geometry and f-GANs, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow. While the authors provide a clear introduction to the f-GAN principle, they could have done more to explain the underlying concepts and terminology.

Overall, I believe that the paper meets the reviewing criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The authors provide a rigorous and well-structured analysis of the f-GAN game, and their results are well-supported by theoretical derivations and experimental evidence. While there are some limitations to the paper, they do not detract from the overall significance of the contributions.
