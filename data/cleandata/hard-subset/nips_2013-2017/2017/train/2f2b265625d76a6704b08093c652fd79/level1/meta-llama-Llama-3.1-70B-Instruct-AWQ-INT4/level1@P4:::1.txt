This paper presents a novel information geometric interpretation of the f-GAN game, providing a variational formulation that connects the general f-divergence formulation to the Bregman divergence formulation. The authors derive a new identity that relates the f-GAN game to a geometric optimization problem, which is equivalent to a variational formulation of the KLχ divergences. This identity, named vig-f-GAN, provides a more detailed understanding of the generator's convergence and its connection to the discriminator.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the related work. The technical contributions are well-presented, and the proofs are provided in the supplementary material. The experiments are thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a novel and insightful interpretation of the f-GAN game, which connects the information-theoretic and information-geometric aspects of the problem.
* The derived identity, vig-f-GAN, provides a more detailed understanding of the generator's convergence and its connection to the discriminator.
* The experiments are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes a strong background in information geometry and f-divergences, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more intuition and explanations for the technical contributions, especially for the derivation of the vig-f-GAN identity.
* The experiments could be more comprehensive, including more datasets and architectures.
