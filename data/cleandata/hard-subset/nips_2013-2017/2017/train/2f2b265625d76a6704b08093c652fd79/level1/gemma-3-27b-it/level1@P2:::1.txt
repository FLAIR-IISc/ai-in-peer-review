This paper tackles a fascinating and important problem: understanding the theoretical underpinnings of GANs, specifically in the context of f-GANs as generalized by Nowozin et al. (2017). The authors aim to connect the information-geometric and information-theoretic perspectives, revealing convergence properties and offering guidance for generator design. The core contribution is establishing an identity (eq. 3) linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences within the framework of deformed exponential families, and then leveraging this to provide insights into deep generator architectures.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly situating itself within the growing body of work on GAN theory (Goodfellow et al. 2014, Arjovsky et al. 2017, Gulrajani et al. 2017). The authors do a good job of summarizing prior work, particularly Nowozin et al. (2017), and highlighting the gaps that their research addresses.  The connection to information geometry (Amari 2000) and Bregman divergences (Bregman 1967) is nicely articulated, and the link to deformed exponential families (Naudts 2003) provides a solid mathematical foundation. The attempt to bridge the gap between purely distributional divergence measures and parameter-space optimization is a significant undertaking.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Depth:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, building upon established results in information geometry and statistical physics. The main identity (eq. 3) is a significant result, offering a novel perspective on f-GANs.
* **Clear Mathematical Formulation:** The mathematical notation and derivations are generally clear and well-presented, making the core ideas accessible.  The definitions are precise and the proofs (in the supplementary material) seem sound.
* **Practical Implications:** The paper doesn’t stop at theory; it translates the results into practical implications for generator design, specifically regarding activation functions and discriminator link functions. The experimental results, although limited, support these claims.
* **Completeness:** The paper attempts to provide a complete picture, addressing both the information-theoretic and information-geometric aspects of the f-GAN framework and completing the "supervised game picture" of Nowozin et al. (2017).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity & Density:** The paper is quite dense and mathematically demanding. While this is not necessarily a flaw for NIPS, it could limit its accessibility to a broader audience. The large number of equations and definitions can be overwhelming.
* **Experimental Scope:** The experimental section feels somewhat limited. While the experiments provide some validation of the theoretical findings, they are relatively small in scale and scope.  The comparison of activation functions and link functions is somewhat superficial, and a more comprehensive evaluation on a wider range of datasets and architectures would be beneficial. The ablation studies could be more rigorous.
* **Practical Impact – Specificity:** While the paper suggests the importance of activation function choice and link function tuning, it doesn't provide specific guidelines or recommendations for practitioners. The conditions for admissibility of activations (Lemma 11) are stated, but the practical guidance for selecting an appropriate function is minimal.
* **Redundancy/Verbose:** Some sections, particularly the introduction, feel verbose and could benefit from tighter writing. Some concepts are reiterated multiple times, adding to the overall length.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the understanding of GANs.
* The connection between f-divergences, Bregman divergences, and deformed exponential families is a significant insight.
* The work has the potential to inform the design of more effective GAN architectures.
* The paper is well-written (despite its density) and clearly articulates its contributions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental validation is limited and doesn't fully demonstrate the practical impact of the theoretical findings.
* The paper is highly mathematical and may be inaccessible to some readers.
* The practical guidance offered is somewhat limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field. While the experimental evaluation could be strengthened, the theoretical insights and the rigorous mathematical analysis are compelling. The paper meets the NIPS criteria of quality, clarity (though demanding), originality and significance.  I would lean towards **accepting** the paper, with a request for the authors to consider expanding the experimental section and streamlining the presentation to improve accessibility. A minor revision addressing the experimental scope and potentially providing more concrete guidelines for practitioners would significantly strengthen the paper.
