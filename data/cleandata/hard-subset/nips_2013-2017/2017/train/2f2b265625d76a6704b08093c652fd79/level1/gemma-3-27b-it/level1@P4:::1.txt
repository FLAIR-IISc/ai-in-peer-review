This paper aims to provide a theoretical underpinning for f-GANs, extending the work of Nowozin et al. (2017). It establishes a connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences within the context of deformed exponential families, demonstrating that solving the f-GAN game can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem. A key contribution is identifying sufficient conditions for deep neural networks to effectively model densities within this framework, relating network architecture to the factorization of escorts of deformed exponential families. The paper also delves into a complete picture of the supervised game, including a principled design for activation functions and integration of link functions in the discriminator.

**Quality:** The paper presents a mathematically rigorous treatment of f-GANs, building upon established information-geometric principles. The main theorem connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences (Theorem 3) is a significant contribution, as is the analysis of deep network architectures (Theorem 9). The proofs, while likely dense, appear logically sound, though a deeper dive would be needed to fully assess them. The experimental results, though limited in scope, offer some validation of the theoretical findings regarding activation function design. However, the connection between theoretical results and practical GAN performance isn’t always clear. The claim of a “complete picture” of the supervised game feels slightly overstated; while valuable additions are made, the field continues to evolve. There’s a reliance on citing the authors’ own preprints ([27]) to supplement details, which isn’t ideal for independent assessment.

**Clarity:** The paper is densely written and requires a strong background in information theory, differential geometry, and statistical inference. While the authors attempt to provide definitions, the rapid introduction of notation and concepts can be overwhelming. The flow of arguments sometimes feels abrupt. The connection between the theoretical results and the practical implications for GAN design could be more clearly articulated. More intuitive explanations and illustrative examples would significantly improve accessibility. While well-organized into sections, some subsections feel disjointed. The experimental section is also concise and could benefit from more detailed descriptions of the setup and results.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel perspective on f-GANs, bridging the gap between information theory and information geometry. While the underlying concepts (f-divergences, Bregman divergences, exponential families) are well-established, their integration within the GAN framework and the subsequent analysis of deep networks represent a significant contribution.  The analysis of activation functions in the generator (Theorem 9 and Lemma 11) and the discussion of proper composite losses are novel and provide useful insights.  However, the paper builds heavily upon the foundation laid by Nowozin et al. (2017), so the originality lies more in extending and elaborating upon that work than in presenting a completely new paradigm.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a fundamental question in the field of GANs: *why* do they work? By providing a theoretical justification for the effectiveness of deep networks in the context of f-GANs, the authors offer a path towards more principled GAN design. The identification of sufficient conditions for activation functions and the discussion of link functions could lead to improved GAN architectures and training procedures. The connection to Bregman divergences provides a potential link to other geometric approaches to GANs (e.g., Wasserstein GANs).  While the immediate impact may be limited by the mathematical complexity, the long-term potential for influencing GAN research is substantial. The experimental validation, though preliminary, suggests that the theoretical insights have practical relevance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Provides a rigorous theoretical framework for understanding f-GANs.
*   Establishes a novel connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences.
*   Offers insights into the design of GAN architectures, particularly activation functions.
*   Potentially links f-GANs to other geometric approaches.
*   Presents mathematically sound results and offers preliminary experimental validation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper is extremely dense and mathematically challenging, limiting accessibility.
*   The connection between theory and practice isn't always clear.
*   Reliance on supplementary materials and preprints for complete details.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited in scope.
*   Claims of a "complete picture" may be overstated.



Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of f-GANs. While the complexity and density of the material may pose a barrier to some readers, the originality and potential significance of the work warrant acceptance. The authors should consider expanding the experimental section and providing more intuitive explanations of the key concepts to enhance the paper’s impact.
