- Tackles theoretical understanding of GANs, focusing on f-GANs and connecting information-theoretic and geometric perspectives.  
- Core contribution: establishes identity (eq. 3) linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences within deformed exponential families.  
- Leverages this identity to derive insights for deep generator architecture design (activation and discriminator link functions).  
- Well-motivated and clearly situated within prior GAN theory literature, especially Nowozin et al. (2017).  
- Strong connection to information geometry (Amari) and deformed exponential families (Naudts) providing solid mathematical foundation.  
- Theoretical depth: rigorous analysis with a significant novel perspective on f-GANs.  
- Clear mathematical formulation and precise definitions; proofs in supplementary material appear sound.  
- Translates theory to practical implications and presents supporting experiments, though limited.  
- Attempts completeness by addressing both information-theoretic and geometric aspects and completing Nowozin et al.’s supervised game picture.  
- Complexity and density: paper is mathematically demanding and may limit accessibility to broader audience.  
- Experimental scope limited: small-scale experiments, superficial activation/link comparisons, and insufficient ablation rigor.  
- Practical impact limited by lack of specific, actionable guidelines for practitioners despite Lemma 11 conditions.  
- Redundancy/verbosity: some sections (notably the introduction) are repetitive and could be tightened.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance for theoretical novelty and significance, with requests to expand experiments and improve accessibility.
