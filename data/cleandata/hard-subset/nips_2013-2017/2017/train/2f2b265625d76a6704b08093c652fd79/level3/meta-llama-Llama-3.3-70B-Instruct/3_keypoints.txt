- Proposes FS-RNN, a novel RNN architecture combining multiscale and deep-transition RNN ideas.  
- Architecture: lower layer with multiple RNN cells updating multiple times per time step; higher layer with a single RNN cell updating once.  
- Evaluated on Penn Treebank and Hutter Prize Wikipedia, achieving state-of-the-art results and surpassing a top text compression algorithm on Hutter.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and organized with a thorough related-work section.  
- Provides analysis of network dynamics showing ability to learn long-term dependencies and adapt to unexpected inputs.  
- Strength: clear presentation and strong empirical performance.  
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to hyperparameters (number of lower-layer cells, update frequency) not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to character-level language modeling; unclear generalization to other sequential data (speech, image sequences).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, well-motivated architecture; impressive experimental results; clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: hyperparameter sensitivity concerns, limited evaluation scope, and need for deeper sensitivity analysis and broader experiments.
