This paper proposes an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, establishing parameter-space convergence via f-/chi-divergence mapping and Bregman divergence. It further offers implementation guidance for f-GANs and a factorization result linking deep network activations to chi/f functions. The authors demonstrate a strong theoretical understanding of the subject, with most proofs (excluding Theorem 8) appearing correct upon review. Theorem 4, providing the IG exposition for f-GAN, is a particularly impressive contribution. Overall, I recommend a clear accept, albeit with the caveat that the paper’s density and reliance on information theory/geometry may pose a challenge for readers unfamiliar with these areas.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Novel IG Perspective:** The paper successfully frames f-GANs within an information-geometric framework, offering a fresh and potentially insightful viewpoint.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The mathematical treatment is strong, and the core results (especially Theorem 4) appear well-justified.
* **Factorization Result:** The connection established between deep network representations and chi/f functions is a valuable finding, hinting at architectural principles for GANs.
* **Practical Guidance:** The implementation guidance provided is useful for researchers attempting to apply f-GANs.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement:**

* **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is extremely dense and requires significant effort to follow. The motivation for adopting an IG view is initially unclear; explaining its benefits (e.g., for analysing optimisation behavior) *before* introducing the technical details would improve readability.
* **Connection to f-GAN:** Section 5, while interesting in its own right regarding the representational power of deformed exponential families, feels disconnected from the central f-GAN theme. The paper doesn't demonstrate how to *choose* f or guide f-GAN design based on these results.
* **Theorem 6 & “Deep Sufficient Statistics”:** The claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” requires further justification. Equation (13) doesn’t immediately appear to match the standard form of a deformed exponential family. More elaboration on this connection is necessary.
* **Utility-Theory Paragraph:** The paragraph on utility theory (lines 257–269) seems tangential to the main contribution and could be moved to the appendix to streamline the presentation.
* **Experiments – Disconnected & Confounding:** The experimental section is weak. The activation function results don’t necessarily *require* an IG view to interpret, and the inclusion of WGAN results distracts from the f-GAN focus. The experiments feel more like independent explorations than a validation of the core theoretical claims.
* **Fig. 1 Placement:** Moving Figure 1 (IT–IG connections) from the appendix to the main text would significantly improve comprehension. It provides a crucial visual overview of the relationship between information theory and information geometry.



**Detailed Comments:**

* **Introduction:**  As mentioned, the motivation for an IG view should be stated earlier. The current introduction spends too long setting up background before articulating the paper’s contribution.
* **Section 3:** The notation is heavy and sometimes inconsistent (e.g., the permutation of arguments in Bregman divergences). Careful attention to consistency would improve clarity.
* **Theorem 9:** The assumption of network reversibility is a strong one. The authors should acknowledge the limitations imposed by this assumption and discuss the potential impact of non-reversibility.
* **Section 6 (Experiments):** A more focused experimental design is needed. Experiments should directly test the predictions of the theory, such as the impact of specific activation functions on the convergence properties of f-GANs. Reporting hyperparameter tuning details would also be beneficial. The LSUN results in the supplement should be discussed in the main text, if space permits.
* **Overall:** The paper feels like a collection of interesting results that haven't been fully synthesized into a cohesive narrative.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical contribution providing a novel and rigorous perspective on f-GANs.
* Impressive mathematical results, particularly Theorem 4.
* Potential for significant impact on the design and analysis of GAN architectures.
* Provides valuable implementation guidance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  The paper is very dense and challenging to read, even for experts in the field.
*  The connection between theory and experiments is weak.
*  Some aspects of the argument (e.g., Theorem 6) require further clarification.
*  The organization could be significantly improved.



**Final Recommendation:**

Accept, with the strong encouragement that the authors address the clarity and organizational issues identified in this review.  Specifically, clarifying the motivation, streamlining the presentation, focusing the experimental validation, and providing more intuitive explanations of key concepts will significantly enhance the impact of this valuable work.
