Summary and relation to prior work: The paper develops an information-geometric (IG) account of f-GANs by showing that, for deformed exponential families (via χ-escorts), the variational f-GAN objective coincides with a Bregman divergence in parameter space, up to a penalty term. This yields a parameter-space convergence interpretation of GAN training. It further provides (i) a proper-composite-loss formulation clarifying the discriminator “output activation/link,” and (ii) a factorization result showing that deep generators can represent escorts of χ-exponential families under mild activation assumptions, linking common activations (ELU/ReLU/Softplus) to χ/f choices. This work builds on Nowozin et al. (f-GAN), classical KL–Bregman equivalences for exponential families (Amari et al.), and recent treatments of deformed exponential families, advancing them to a general f-divergence/IG setting.

Quality: I checked most proofs (except the variational IG identity labeled Theorem 8 in the SM) and they appear technically careful; Theorem 4 (IG exposition and mapping from f-/χ-divergences to Bregman geometry) is particularly strong and, to my knowledge, new. However, Section 5’s connection between the proper-loss view and concrete guidance for choosing f (or χ) for f-GAN design remains opaque; the experiments, while clean, seem only loosely tied to the central IG claims.

Clarity: The paper is dense and assumes substantial background in information theory/geometry. I recommend moving the IT–IG connections figure (Fig. 1) from the appendix to the main text and stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., to analyze optimization behavior and parameter convergence), rather than introducing it late. The “utility-theory” paragraph (lines 257–269) distracts from the main narrative and could go to the appendix. The claim in Theorem 6 that the φl are “deep sufficient statistics” seems overstated; Eq. (13) may not match a canonical deformed exponential-family form—please clarify assumptions (e.g., reversibility, normalization learned via bl) and the precise sense of sufficiency.

Originality: High. The variational IG identity for general f-divergences and the activation–χ linkage are novel and insightful.

Significance: Potentially high. A principled parameter-space view of f-GANs can influence analysis, stability, and architecture design. Empirical evidence is preliminary; WGAN results feel off-focus for an f-GAN paper.

Pros for acceptance:
- Elegant, general IG identity; clear theoretical advance.
- Useful implementation guidance via proper composite losses.
- Nontrivial link between activations and χ/f functions.

Cons:
- Presentation is dense; organization could be improved.
- Section 5’s practical guidance remains under-specified.
- Experiments insufficiently connected to the main theoretical claims; WGAN ablations distract.

Recommendation: Clear accept, contingent on reorganization and clarifications noted above.
