## Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper delves into the theoretical underpinnings of f-GANs, building upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2017) to provide a more complete picture of the generator’s behavior and convergence properties. The core contribution lies in establishing a variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity, linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences within the framework of deformed exponential families. This identity offers a geometric interpretation of the GAN objective and provides insights into generator design, specifically regarding activation functions. The authors also demonstrate a sufficient condition for achieving factored generative distributions using deep architectures.

The work is highly relevant to the NIPS audience, as it tackles a fundamental theoretical question in the rapidly evolving field of GANs. The vig-f-GAN identity is a significant result, potentially offering a new lens through which to analyze and improve GAN training. The connection to information geometry is well-motivated and the exploration of deformed exponential families is a valuable extension of previous work. The claim that deep architectures are well-suited to model these families is also promising.

However, the paper suffers from a significant drawback: the presentation of the theoretical results is overly general and abstract. While mathematical rigor is appreciated, the numerous definitions and theorems, presented without sufficient intuitive explanation, make it difficult to grasp the core ideas. The notation is dense, and following the logical flow requires considerable effort. This lack of clarity hinders the reader’s ability to assess the practical implications of the theoretical findings.

Furthermore, the experimental section (Section 6) feels disconnected from the theoretical contributions. While the experiments explore the impact of activation functions and link functions, the connection to the vig-f-GAN identity and the theoretical insights derived from it is not clearly articulated. It's difficult to see how the experimental observations validate or inform the theoretical results. The typographical error on page 7, line 258 ("For eq.(7), is means when C…") is a minor issue but contributes to the overall impression of a rushed presentation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The vig-f-GAN identity is a novel and potentially impactful theoretical result.
*   The work provides a valuable connection between f-GANs and information geometry.
*   The analysis of deformed exponential families and their relationship to deep architectures is insightful.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical presentation is overly abstract and difficult to follow.
*   The experimental section lacks a clear connection to the theoretical results.
*   The paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations and concrete examples.



**Overall:** I believe this paper presents an interesting and potentially significant contribution, but its impact is diminished by its lack of clarity and the weak link between theory and experiment. I recommend **acceptance with major revisions**, specifically focusing on simplifying the presentation, providing more intuitive explanations, and strengthening the connection between the theoretical results and the experimental findings.
