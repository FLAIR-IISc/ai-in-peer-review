- Central contribution:Provides a variational identity linking the f-GAN objective to a Bregman divergence between generator parameters for deformed exponential families (Corollary 8), giving an information-geometric view of f-GAN training.  
- Deep networks result:Shows standard deep generator architectures can factorize deformed exponential families and links activation choices to the family signature (Theorem 9).  
- Supervised loss framing:Completes the supervised part of the f-GAN game by casting it in proper composite loss language, clarifying the link function’s role.  
- Technical quality:Technically strong with mostly verified proofs, though the proof of Theorem 8 was not checked by the reviewer.  
- Experiments weakness:Empirical evaluation is weak and does not directly validate the central information-geometric claims.  
- Clarity issues:Paper is dense, disjointed, and likely inaccessible to non-specialists; motivation for the IG perspective appears too late.  
- Originality:Highly original—first formal bridge between f-divergence minimization in f-GANs and parameter-space Bregman geometry.  
- Significance:Highly significant theoretical contribution that can reshape analysis and design of generative models despite limited immediate practical takeaways.  
- Suggestion — motivate IG early:State upfront why the information-geometric view is useful for convergence, optimization, and architecture design.  
- Suggestion — improve intuition/figures:Move Figure 1 into the main text to build intuition and aid reader navigation.  
- Suggestion — clarify practical implications:Explain how the theory guides choice of f-divergence, link functions, activations, or architectures in practice.  
- Suggestion — temper Theorem 9 claims:Provide more detail or careful phrasing for the “deep sufficient statistics” claim and its adherence to deformed exponential family form.  
- Suggestion — refocus narrative:Move the utility-theory detour to the appendix to tighten the main argument.  
- Suggestion — align experiments with theory:Design experiments that explicitly test IG predictions (e.g., which activations suit which f-divergences) and remove distracting WGAN results.  
- Recommendation:Clear accept — major, novel theoretical contribution worth presentation despite presentation/clarity issues.
