- Proposes the Fast-Slow RNN (FS-RNN), a novel RNN architecture combining multiscale and deep-transition ideas.  
- FS-RNN design: lower “fast” layer with multiple per-step updates and an upper “slow” layer with one update per time step.  
- Evaluated on Penn Treebank and Hutter Prize Wikipedia, achieving state-of-the-art character-level language modeling results.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with an accessible introduction and thorough related work.  
- Technical contribution includes both the new architecture and a thorough analysis of FS-RNN network dynamics.  
- Experimental results show strong ability to learn long-term dependencies and adapt to unexpected inputs.  
- Strength: novel architecture with potential broad applicability.  
- Strength: detailed analysis that helps explain how FS-RNN operates.  
- Strength: impressive empirical performance on benchmark datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks more extensive comparisons to other RNN variants (e.g., Clockwork RNN, Recurrent Highway Networks).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and efficiency claims.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—novel, technically sound, and empirically strong despite needing additional comparisons and complexity analysis.
