- Presents a novel theoretical link between f-GAN variational divergence minimization and information geometry via a variational identity relating f-divergences to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families, with implications for parameter convergence and architecture/activation design.  
- Main theoretical strength is an elegant, original information-geometric grounding of heuristic GAN design choices.  
- Major concern: analysis heavily relies on the strong assumption that data and model distributions lie in a deformed exponential family and needs better justification and discussion of robustness when this fails.  
- Practical significance is unclear: Eq. 11’s parameter-space optimization is not shown how to implement and lacks comparison to traditional distribution-based optimization.  
- Experimental validation is weak and inconclusive, failing to substantiate theoretical claims.  
- Contradiction: the theoretically-preferred μ-ReLU activation performs worse than standard ReLU in several settings and is not adequately addressed.  
- Results (e.g., Table 2) appear within error bars, providing insufficient empirical evidence for proposed methods.  
- Missed opportunity: discriminator output activation design is not compared to established heuristics from prior work [34].  
- Overall: thought-provoking theory but not yet practically substantial due to strong, under-justified assumptions and unsupported experiments.
