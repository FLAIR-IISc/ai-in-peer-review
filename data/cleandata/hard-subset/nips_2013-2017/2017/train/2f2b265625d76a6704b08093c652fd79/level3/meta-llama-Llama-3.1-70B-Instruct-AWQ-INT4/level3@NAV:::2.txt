This paper proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs, providing a novel perspective on the variational formulation of f-divergences. The authors show that the f-GAN problem can be equivalent to a geometric optimization problem, leveraging the concept of deformed exponential families and Bregman divergences. The paper's main contributions include:

1. A variational information-geometric identity for the f-GAN game, which connects the general f-divergence formulation to the geometric optimization problem.
2. A proof that standard deep generator architectures can model complex escorts of deformed exponential families, factorizing a large number of such densities using a compact design.
3. A characterization of the generators' convergence in the parameter space, which is guaranteed by the Bregman divergence.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solution. The technical sections are dense but well-written, and the proofs are thorough and convincing.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a novel and interesting perspective on the f-GAN problem, leveraging information geometry and deformed exponential families.
* The authors provide a thorough and convincing proof of their main results, including the variational information-geometric identity and the characterization of the generators' convergence.
* The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solution.

Weaknesses:

* The paper is very dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with information theory and geometry to follow.
* Some sections, such as Section 5, seem disconnected from the main claims of the paper and could be removed or relocated to an appendix.
* The experiments seem disconnected from the main claims of the paper and could be improved to provide more direct evidence for the proposed approach.

Recommendations:

* Consider moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to provide a clearer illustration of the IT-IG connections.
* State early in the paper why the IG view is useful, e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior, instead of introducing it late.
* Consider relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph (lines 257-269) to focus the main text on the key contributions.
* Improve the experiments to provide more direct evidence for the proposed approach and to demonstrate the practical benefits of the IG view.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a novel and interesting perspective on the f-GAN problem. With some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Acceptance: Clear accept, but with revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
