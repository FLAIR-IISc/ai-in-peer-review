- Extends f-GANs to an information-geometric view, connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families.  
- Introduces the "vig-f-GAN" identity showing f-GAN solutions can guarantee parameter-space convergence (θ vs ϑ) for deformed exponential family distributions.  
- Addresses the gap in prior work by providing generator convergence guarantees beyond distribution-level results.  
- Theoretical contribution is technically sound, with main results in Theorem 9 and Lemma 11 and rigorous proofs.  
- Shows a sufficient condition on activation functions enabling deep nets to model complex escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Experimental validation provides practical evidence but does not clearly tie results to the main theorems.  
- Presentation is highly general and abstract, making core results hard to follow and in need of concrete examples.  
- Minor grammatical and typographical errors reduce readability.  
- Novel connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families explains why common activations (ReLU, Softplus) work well.  
- Results are significant for understanding GANs, potentially guiding principled generator and architecture design.  
- Limitations include unclear scalability to complex real-world applications and reliance on reversible networks.  
- Strengths: novel theory filling a key gap, deeper understanding of f-GANs, insightful activation-function link, careful evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: overly abstract theorems, weak experimental-theory linkage, readability issues, unclear practical scaling.  
- Recommendation: Accept, given the important theoretical advance, but revise to add concrete examples and strengthen experiment-theory connections.
