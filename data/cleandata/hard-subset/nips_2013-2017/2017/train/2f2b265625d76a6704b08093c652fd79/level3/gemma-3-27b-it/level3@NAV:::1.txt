## Review of “f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell”

This paper delves into the theoretical underpinnings of f-GANs, building upon the work of Nowozin et al. (2017) to provide a more complete picture of the generator’s convergence properties and to connect f-GANs to information geometry. The core contribution lies in establishing the “vig-f-GAN identity,” relating the f-GAN objective to a Bregman divergence, and demonstrating that deep architectures naturally support the factorization of densities within deformed exponential families. This work attempts to move beyond simply stating that f-GANs minimize f-divergences, and begins to address *how* and *where* convergence occurs in parameter space, offering insights into generator design.

The paper’s strength lies in its ambitious attempt to provide a rigorous, information-geometric interpretation of f-GANs. The vig-f-GAN identity is a genuinely interesting result with potential for informing future GAN architectures and loss function design. The analysis demonstrating the compatibility of deep networks with deformed exponential families is also a valuable contribution, suggesting that the success of GANs may be rooted in their ability to model these distributions effectively. The experimental section, though limited, attempts to validate the theoretical claims by exploring activation functions and link functions.

However, the paper suffers from a significant weakness: the extreme generality of the theorems makes them difficult to grasp. While mathematically sound, the abstract nature of the derivations obscures the practical implications and hinders intuitive understanding. The notation is dense, and following the logical flow requires significant effort. Furthermore, the connection between the theoretical results and the experimental observations in Section 6 is tenuous. The experiments, while well-executed, don't strongly demonstrate the benefits predicted by the theory, and it’s difficult to discern a clear link between specific theoretical findings and observed performance improvements. The isolated typographical error on page 7, line 258, is a minor issue, but indicative of a need for careful proofreading.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The theoretical contribution (vig-f-GAN identity) is novel and promising, potentially advancing our understanding of GANs. It tackles a fundamental question regarding convergence and generator design.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The abstract and general presentation of the theoretical results significantly hinders accessibility and impact. The experimental validation is weak and lacks a clear connection to the theory.

**Overall:** While the paper presents a valuable theoretical result, its lack of clarity and weak experimental validation make it a borderline submission. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent upon the authors significantly improving the clarity of the presentation, providing more concrete examples to illustrate the theoretical concepts, and strengthening the connection between theory and experiments. Specifically, more detailed explanations of the implications of the vig-f-GAN identity for practical GAN design would be beneficial.
