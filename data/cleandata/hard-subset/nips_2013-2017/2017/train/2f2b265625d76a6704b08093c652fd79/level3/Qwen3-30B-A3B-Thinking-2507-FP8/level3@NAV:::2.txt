

This paper introduces an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, establishing a novel connection between f-divergences and Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors demonstrate that the f-GAN optimization problem can be equivalently formulated as a geometric optimization problem in parameter space, with Theorem 4 (the variational information-geometric identity) representing a significant theoretical advance that extends the classical KL-divergence to Bregman divergence connection. The paper also provides a factorization result showing how deep network architectures can efficiently represent complex escorts of deformed exponential families, with popular activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfying the necessary conditions.

The paper's strengths lie in its theoretically rich contribution, particularly the IG framework that provides a principled understanding of generator convergence in parameter space. The proofs appear technically sound (except for Theorem 8), and the factorization result offers valuable insights for network design. The experimental validation of activation function choices and link function tuning provides practical guidance for f-GAN implementation.

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues. The IG perspective is introduced late, and the paper would benefit from explicitly stating its utility early (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior). The connection between Section 5 (on representational power) and f-GAN is unclear, and Theorem 6's claim about "deep sufficient statistics" requires stronger justification. The experiments on activation functions and link functions appear disconnected from the main theoretical claims, with WGAN results distracting from the f-GAN focus. The utility-theory paragraph (lines 257-269) should be moved to the appendix to streamline the main narrative.

The paper is highly original in its theoretical approach, significantly advancing our understanding of the f-GAN framework. While the core ideas are compelling, the dense presentation and organizational issues significantly hinder accessibility. The paper would benefit greatly from moving Figure 1 to the main text and reorganizing to better connect theory with practical implications.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The theoretical contributions are strong and novel, but the paper requires substantial reorganization to clarify its main contributions and improve readability for a broader audience. The authors should explicitly state the utility of the IG framework early, strengthen the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN, and better align experiments with the main theoretical claims.
