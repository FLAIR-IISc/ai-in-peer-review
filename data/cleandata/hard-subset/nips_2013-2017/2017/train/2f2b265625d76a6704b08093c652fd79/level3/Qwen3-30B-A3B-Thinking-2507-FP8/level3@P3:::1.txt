

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

This paper extends the f-GAN framework introduced by Nowozin et al. (NIPS 2016) by establishing a variational information geometric identity (vig-f-GAN) that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors build upon the KL-exponential family connection (Amari, 2000; Csiszár, 1967) and extend it to the broader class of f-divergences, which is a significant theoretical contribution to the growing literature on GANs at NIPS. Previous work on GANs has largely focused on practical improvements (e.g., DCGAN, WGAN) or specific divergence measures (e.g., Jensen-Shannon), but this paper provides a unified information geometric framework that generalizes these approaches.

The main strengths of the paper are:

1. **Theoretical Contribution**: The vig-f-GAN identity (Corollary 8) is a novel and elegant connection between the f-GAN objective and information geometry. It provides a geometric interpretation of the GAN game where the generator converges to the natural parameters of the true distribution, which is a significant theoretical advance beyond the original f-GAN work.

2. **Sufficient Condition for Factorization**: The paper establishes a sufficient condition on activation functions (Lemma 11) that allows deep architectures to factorize deformed exponential family densities, which is a valuable theoretical insight for GAN design. The authors demonstrate that most popular activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfy this condition.

3. **Practical Implications**: The paper provides a principled approach to designing activation functions for generators and link functions for discriminators, with experimental validation showing improvements in GAN performance.

The paper's main weaknesses are:

1. **Clarity of Theoretical Results**: The main theorems are presented in very general forms, making them difficult to follow for readers unfamiliar with information geometry. The authors could have provided more concrete examples or a simpler special case (e.g., for KL divergence) to illustrate the general results.

2. **Experimental Section**: Section 6 presents experimental observations that are difficult to directly relate back to the theoretical results. For instance, the experiments comparing different activation functions (Figure 1) don't clearly demonstrate how the theoretical sufficient condition translates into practical improvements. The connection between the theoretical results and the experimental findings needs to be strengthened.

3. **Typographical Error**: A clear grammatical error appears on page 7, line 258: "For eq.(7), is means when C …" which should be corrected to "For eq. (7), it means when C …"

The paper is technically sound and makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field. The main theoretical result (vig-f-GAN identity) is interesting and has promising applicability for understanding and improving GAN architectures. The connection between deep architectures and deformed exponential families is particularly valuable for bridging the gap between theoretical understanding and practical implementation in GANs.

However, the paper would benefit from:
- More concrete examples illustrating the theoretical results
- A clearer exposition of how the theoretical conditions translate into practical improvements
- A stronger connection between the experimental results and the theoretical claims
- Correction of the grammatical error in Section 6

The paper's significance lies in providing a unified theoretical framework for understanding GANs through information geometry, which is increasingly important as GANs become more widely adopted in practice. The insights about activation functions could guide future architectural choices in GAN design, potentially leading to more stable and efficient training procedures.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a clear contribution beyond previous NIPS work on GANs. While Nowozin et al. (NIPS 2016) generalized GANs to f-divergences, this paper provides the crucial geometric interpretation missing from that work. The connection between deformed exponential families and deep architectures is novel and addresses an important gap in the literature.

The quality of the paper is high in terms of theoretical contribution, though the presentation of the main results could be improved. The experimental results support the theoretical claims but would be stronger with a clearer connection to the theory.

In summary, this is a high-quality theoretical contribution with significant potential impact on GAN research. The paper's main weakness is the difficulty in following the general theoretical results, which could be mitigated with clearer exposition and more concrete examples. The experimental section could also better demonstrate the practical implications of the theoretical results.

**Pro-acceptance arguments**: Strong theoretical contribution, novel connection between f-GANs and information geometry, practical implications for GAN architecture design, well-supported with theorems.

**Con-acceptance arguments**: Theoretical results are difficult to follow due to excessive generality, experimental section lacks clear connection to theoretical results, minor grammatical error.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs and would be of interest to the NIPS community. With minor revisions to improve clarity and strengthen the experimental-theoretical connection, it would be an excellent contribution to the conference.
