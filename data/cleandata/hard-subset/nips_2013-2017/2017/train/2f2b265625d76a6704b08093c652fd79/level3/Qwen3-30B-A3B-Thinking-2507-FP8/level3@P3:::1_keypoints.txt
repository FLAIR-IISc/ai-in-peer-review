- Introduces the vig-f-GAN identity linking f-divergences to Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families — a significant theoretical advance.  
- Extends the KL–exponential family connection to general f-divergences, giving a geometric interpretation where the generator converges to the true distribution’s natural parameters.  
- Proves a sufficient condition on activation functions (Lemma 11) that lets deep architectures factorize deformed exponential family densities; common activations (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) satisfy it.  
- Offers principled guidance for designing generator activations and discriminator link functions, supported by experiments showing improved GAN performance.  
- Main theorems are presented very generally and can be difficult to follow; more concrete examples or a simple special-case (e.g., KL) would aid readability.  
- Experimental section (Section 6) does not clearly demonstrate how empirical results (e.g., activation comparisons) follow from the theoretical sufficient condition; the link needs strengthening.  
- Minor typographical/grammatical error: page 7, line 258 ("is means" should be "it means").  
- Technically sound and high-quality theoretical contribution with promising applicability to understanding and improving GAN architectures.  
- Novelty: provides the missing information-geometric interpretation of Nowozin et al.’s f-GANs and a new connection between deformed exponential families and deep nets.  
- Recommended improvements: clearer exposition, more concrete illustrative examples, and a stronger connection between theory and experiments.  
- Overall verdict: valuable and original work likely suitable for NIPS pending minor revisions to improve clarity and empirical-theoretical linkage.
