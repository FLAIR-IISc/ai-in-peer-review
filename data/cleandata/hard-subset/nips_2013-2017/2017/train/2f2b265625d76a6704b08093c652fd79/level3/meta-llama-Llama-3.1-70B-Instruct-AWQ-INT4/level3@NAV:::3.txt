**Paper Review: "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"**

**Summary:**
This paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) principle in an information geometric context. The authors introduce a variational generalization of the KL divergence between regular exponential families and divergences between their natural parameters, which connects the f-divergence formulation to the Bregman divergence formulation. They show that deep architectures can factorize a large number of deformed exponential families, and provide a principled design of activation functions in the generator and an explicit integration of proper composite losses' link function in the discriminator.

**Strengths:**

* The paper provides a novel and interesting perspective on the GAN principle, connecting it to information geometry and deformed exponential families.
* The authors derive a variational information geometric identity for the f-GAN game, which provides a new characterization of the generators' convergence.
* The paper shows that deep architectures can be powerful at modeling complex escorts of deformed exponential families, and provides a sufficient condition on the activation function to guarantee the escort modeling.

**Weaknesses:**

* The paper assumes that data and model lie in the deformed exponential family, which may not always be the case in practice. The authors need to provide more justification for this assumption and explore whether the results extend beyond this assumption.
* The parameter-space f-GAN identity (Eq. 7) is not clearly explained in terms of its practical benefits, implementability, and experimental comparison to distribution-based optimization.
* The authors underuse the RHS of Eq. 7 and need to provide more intuition on why convergence in divergence might not imply parameter convergence with a realistic example.
* The experimental results are inconclusive, and the authors need to provide more comparison to prior heuristics (Ref. [34]) and discuss the reversibility in DCGAN and comparison to WGAN.
* The results in Table 2 appear within error bars, providing insufficient empirical support for theoretical claims.

**Originality:**
The paper provides a novel perspective on the GAN principle, connecting it to information geometry and deformed exponential families. The authors derive a variational information geometric identity for the f-GAN game, which is a new contribution to the field.

**Significance:**
The paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding the GAN principle in an information geometric context. The results have the potential to improve the design of GANs and provide a more principled approach to generative modeling.

**Quality:**
The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. However, the experimental results are inconclusive, and more work is needed to provide empirical support for the theoretical claims.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-written, but some sections are dense and require careful reading. The authors could provide more intuition on the deformed exponential families and the variational information geometric identity.

**Recommendation:**
I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions. The authors need to address the concerns mentioned above, provide more justification for the assumptions, and improve the experimental results. Additionally, the authors could provide more comparison to prior heuristics and discuss the reversibility in DCGAN and comparison to WGAN.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper provides a novel and interesting perspective on the GAN principle.
* The authors derive a variational information geometric identity for the f-GAN game, which is a new contribution to the field.
* The paper provides a theoretical framework for understanding the GAN principle in an information geometric context.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The paper assumes that data and model lie in the deformed exponential family, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The experimental results are inconclusive, and more work is needed to provide empirical support for the theoretical claims.
* The authors need to provide more justification for the assumptions and explore whether the results extend beyond this assumption.
