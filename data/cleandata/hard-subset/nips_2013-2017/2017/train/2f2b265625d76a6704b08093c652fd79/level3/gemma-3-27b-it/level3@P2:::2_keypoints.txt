- Presents an information-geometric perspective on f-GANs, connecting f-divergences to Bregman divergences and χ-divergences to analyze parameter-space convergence.
- Establishes Theorem 6 linking f-divergences and Bregman divergences in deformed exponential families, a key novel contribution.
- Shows how deep generators can factorize densities within a broad class of deformed exponential families and relates activation functions to these families.
- Mathematical rigor is strong and proofs appear sound overall (reviewer did not fully check Theorem 8).
- Theorem 6 is particularly impressive and forms a solid foundation for the analysis.
- Paper demonstrates deep understanding of information theory and differential geometry.
- Paper is dense and poorly organized, making it inaccessible to readers not already familiar with the math.
- Motivation for the IG viewpoint is introduced late and should be framed earlier with clear benefits (e.g., optimization/architecture guidance).
- Experiments feel disconnected from the theoretical contributions and often do not require the IG framework for interpretation.
- WGAN results and some experimental choices distract from the primary focus on f-GANs.
- Section 5 on representational power is isolated and does not clearly translate into practical design guidance (choice of f, architectures).
- The claim that φ_l are “deep sufficient statistics” is potentially imprecise and does not match the standard definition given equation (13).
- Suggested revisions: move Figure 1 into the main text, relocate the utility-theory discussion to the appendix, and more clearly articulate practical implications.
- Strengths summarized: strong theoretical foundation, novel IG–Bregman connection, and insightful generative capacity analysis.
- Weaknesses summarized: clarity/organization issues, disconnected experiments, lack of practical guidance, and potential imprecision in key claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, mathematically sound perspective with significant theoretical contributions.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to density, weak theory–experiment linkage, and insufficient concrete guidance for f-GAN design.
- Overall recommendation: accept with strong encouragement to revise for clarity, practical relevance, and tighter experimental/theoretical linkage.
