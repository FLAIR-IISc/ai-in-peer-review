- Proposes an information-geometric view of f-GANs, linking variational f-divergences to geometric optimization via deformed exponential families and Bregman divergences.
- Main contribution: a variational information-geometric identity that connects general f-divergence formulation to a geometric optimization problem.
- Main contribution: shows standard deep generator architectures can model complex escorts of deformed exponential families and factorize many such densities compactly.
- Main contribution: characterizes generator convergence in parameter space, guaranteed by Bregman divergence.
- Paper is well-organized with a clear, concise introduction; technical sections are dense but well-written and proofs are thorough.
- Strength: provides a novel and interesting information-geometric perspective on the f-GAN problem.
- Strength: delivers thorough and convincing proofs of the main results, including identity and convergence characterization.
- Strength: overall clarity and organization help present the technical contributions effectively.
- Weakness: paper density may hinder readers unfamiliar with information theory and geometry.
- Weakness: some sections (e.g., Section 5) appear disconnected from main claims and could be moved or removed.
- Weakness: experiments are disconnected from main claims and need improvement to demonstrate practical benefits.

- Recommendation: state early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior) instead of introducing it late.
