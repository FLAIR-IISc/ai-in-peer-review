- Introduces a novel information‑geometric (IG) perspective on f‑GANs, building on Nowozin et al.'s variational f‑divergence framework.  
- Establishes a key identity linking f‑divergences in the f‑GAN objective to Bregman divergences between parameters of deformed exponential families.  
- Provides a theoretical basis for analyzing generator convergence in parameter space.  
- Gives a factorization result for deep generative networks, connecting representational power and activation functions to the IG framework and offering implementation guidance.  
- Major strength: strong theoretical contributions, with Theorem 4 a standout result; most proofs appear sound (Theorem 8 not fully checked).  
- Bridges the information‑theoretic view of f‑GANs with geometric optimization perspectives from other GAN variants.  
- Major weakness: manuscript is dense and poorly organized, making it hard to follow for readers not expert in information geometry.  
- Suggestion: move Figure 1 (IT‑IG illustration) to the main text and state the motivation for the IG view up front to improve readability.  
- Narrative disconnect: Section 5 explains representational power but does not clearly guide choice of f or generator architecture in practice.  
- Skepticism about Theorem 6's claim that activations are "deep sufficient statistics"; eq. (13) may not strictly match deformed exponential family form.  
- Experiments feel disconnected from theory: activation results do not seem to require the IG framework, and including WGAN results distracts from the paper’s core focus.  
- Overall verdict: novel and potentially important theoretical work deserving acceptance, contingent on substantial reorganization for a clearer, more cohesive presentation.
