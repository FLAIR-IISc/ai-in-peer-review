- Proposes a novel information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs that extends to all f-divergences and offers a broader understanding of the optimization process.  
- Main technical contribution: a variational IG identity linking If-divergence and D (Bregman) divergence formulations and characterizing generator convergence.  
- Provides a factorization result connecting deep network activations to chi/f functions and claims standard deep generators can model escorts of deformed exponential families.  
- Offers implementation guidance for f-GANs and argues for compact network designs that realize the factorization.  
- Paper is technically sound; most proofs checked by the reviewer (except Theorem 8) and Theorem 4 is a particularly strong contribution.  
- Writing is clear but the paper is very dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with information theory/geometry.  
- Suggests moving Figure 1 from appendix to main text and stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior).  
- Section 5 (representational power of deformed exponential families) does not clearly connect to f-GANs and fails to guide how to choose f or design f-GANs.  
- Reviewer questions the claim in Theorem 6 that phi_l are "deep sufficient statistics."  
- Recommends relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix to focus the main text.  
- Experiments seem disconnected from the main claims; activation-function results do not require the IG view and WGAN results distract from the f-GAN focus.  
- Paper presents many useful results but needs reorganization and a more coherent, focused presentation.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept, conditional on reorganization and clarification to improve accessibility and coherence.
