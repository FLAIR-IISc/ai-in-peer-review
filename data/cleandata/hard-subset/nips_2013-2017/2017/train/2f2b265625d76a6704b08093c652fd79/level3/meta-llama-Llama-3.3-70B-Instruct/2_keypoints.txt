- Proposes a novel Fast-Slow RNN (FS-RNN) architecture combining multiscale and deep-transition RNN strengths.
- FS-RNN design: lower layer with multiple fast updates per time step and a higher slow layer updating once per step.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on Penn Treebank and Hutter Prize Wikipedia character-level language modeling.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains motivations, update rules, and provides a general framework for connecting RNN cells.
- Provides thorough analysis of network dynamics and comparisons to stacked and sequential LSTMs.
- Shows FS-RNN can learn long-term dependencies and adapt quickly to unexpected inputs.
- Theoretical analysis supports claims; experiments and presentation are careful and reproducible for experts.
- Originality and significance are high given the novel architecture and strong empirical results.
- Section 5’s connection to the rest of the paper is unclear and seems not directly derived from f-GAN results.
- More justification needed for viewing phi_l as “deep sufficient statistics” in Theorem 6; equation (13) doesn’t match a deformed exponential family form.
- Some paragraphs (e.g., utility theory) should be moved or removed to focus space on main results and improve coherence.
- Experiments should be more directly tied to the paper’s main claims and not rely on the IG view of f-GAN for presentation.
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to addressing the minor clarity and experimental-alignment issues above.
