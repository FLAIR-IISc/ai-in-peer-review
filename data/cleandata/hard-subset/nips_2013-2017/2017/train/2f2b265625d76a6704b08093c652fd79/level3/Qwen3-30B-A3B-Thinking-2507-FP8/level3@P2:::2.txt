

# Review of "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell"

## Summary and Relation to Previous Work
This paper extends Nowozin et al.'s f-GAN framework [30] by introducing an information-geometric perspective that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences. The authors establish a variational identity (Corollary 8) showing that the f-GAN game can be interpreted as a geometric optimization problem for deformed exponential families (a superset of exponential families). This builds upon the well-known identity for KL divergence between exponential families (Eq. 2), but generalizes it to all f-divergences. The work connects to information geometry literature [4, 7, 9] while extending it to the GAN framework, and provides a theoretical foundation for understanding generator convergence in the parameter space.

## Strengths

**Quality:** The paper presents technically sound theoretical contributions. The variational information-geometric identity (Corollary 8) is a significant theoretical advance, and the proofs (except Theorem 8) appear correct upon review. The factorization result (Theorem 9) provides valuable insights into how deep architectures can model complex distributions, linking activation functions to the chi-family. The paper carefully addresses both strengths and weaknesses of the information-geometric perspective.

**Originality:** The information-geometric interpretation of f-GANs is novel and addresses a gap in the literature. While Nowozin et al. [30] established the f-GAN framework, this work provides a deeper theoretical understanding of the generator's behavior and convergence properties. The connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families is particularly innovative.

**Significance:** The theoretical insights could guide future GAN architecture design, particularly regarding activation function choices and link functions in the discriminator. The paper demonstrates how the information-geometric perspective can lead to practical improvements (as shown in Section 6), though the significance would be stronger with more direct connections between theory and practice.

## Weaknesses

**Clarity:** The paper is very dense and assumes significant prior knowledge of information geometry. Key insights (like the importance of the IG perspective) are introduced late rather than early, making it hard for readers unfamiliar with the field to follow. The connection between Section 5 (proper loss picture) and the main f-GAN framework is unclear, as it explains representational power but doesn't show how to choose f or guide f-GAN design. The claim about "deep sufficient statistics" (lines 252-253) requires better justification.

**Clarity (continued):** The utility-theory paragraph (lines 257-269) appears unnecessary in the main text and should be moved to the supplement. The experiments (Section 6) seem disconnected from the main theoretical claims: activation-function results don't require the IG view, and WGAN results distract from the f-GAN focus.

**Organization:** The paper would benefit from a clearer presentation of why the information-geometric view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior) at the beginning rather than late in the paper. Figure 1 (IT-IG connections) should be moved from the appendix to the main text for better clarity.

## Arguments for Acceptance

- Strong theoretical contribution that extends the f-GAN framework with a novel information-geometric perspective
- Provides a new understanding of generator convergence in parameter space
- The factorization result (Theorem 9) offers valuable insights into deep architecture design
- The paper is technically sound with correct proofs
- Experiments provide some empirical validation of the theoretical insights

## Arguments Against Acceptance

- The paper is overly dense and would be difficult for most NIPS readers to follow
- The connection between theory and experiments is weak
- Key sections (Section 5) don't clearly connect to the main f-GAN framework
- The paper doesn't sufficiently explain why the information-geometric perspective is practically useful beyond the theoretical contribution

## Overall Assessment

This is a technically strong paper with significant theoretical contributions to the understanding of f-GANs. The variational information-geometric identity is a standout contribution that would be valuable to the community. However, the paper's density, organizational issues, and weak connection between theory and experiments prevent it from being a clear accept in its current form.

I recommend a **conditional acceptance** with the understanding that the authors address the clarity and organization issues raised. The paper would benefit from:
1. A clearer statement of why the information-geometric perspective is useful at the beginning
2. Moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text
3. Removing the utility-theory paragraph from the main text
4. Strengthening the connection between Section 5 and the f-GAN framework
5. Better aligning the experiments with the theoretical claims

With these revisions, this paper would make a significant contribution to the field of generative modeling and information geometry. As it stands, it's a strong theoretical paper that would be more impactful with improved presentation and organization.
