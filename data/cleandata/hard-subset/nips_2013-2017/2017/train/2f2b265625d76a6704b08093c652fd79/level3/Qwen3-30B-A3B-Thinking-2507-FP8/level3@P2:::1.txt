

# Review for NIPS 2017

This paper extends the f-GAN framework to an information geometric perspective, establishing a variational identity that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The work builds upon Nowozin et al.'s generalization of GANs to all f-divergences [30] by addressing a critical gap: the lack of characterization of the generator's convergence properties. The authors introduce the "vig-f-GAN" identity, which shows that solving the f-GAN game can guarantee convergence in parameter space (θ vs ϑ) for distributions in deformed exponential families—a superset of regular exponential families. This is a significant theoretical advancement over previous work that only provided distribution-level guarantees.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound theoretical contribution with well-supported claims. The main theorems (particularly Theorem 9 and Lemma 11) establish a sufficient condition on activation functions that enables deep architectures to model complex escorts of deformed exponential families. The proofs appear rigorous, though the general form of the theorems makes them somewhat challenging to follow. The experimental validation in Section 6 provides evidence for the practical relevance of the theoretical results, though the connection to the main theorems could be clearer. The authors demonstrate careful evaluation by considering both strengths (e.g., activation function design) and limitations (e.g., the need for reversible networks).

**Clarity:** The paper's main theoretical contributions are presented in highly general forms, which makes the key results difficult to follow. The paper would benefit from more concrete examples illustrating the main theorems. The experimental section (Section 6) does not adequately connect the observed results to the theoretical claims—readers cannot easily determine how the experimental findings validate the main theoretical contributions. There are also minor grammatical issues (e.g., "For eq.(7), is means when C..." on p.7, l.258).

**Originality:** This work makes a significant contribution to the literature by bridging the gap between f-GANs and information geometry. The variational information geometric identity (vig-f-GAN) is novel and provides a deeper understanding of the f-GAN framework. The connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families is particularly insightful, as it reveals a theoretical basis for why certain activations (like ReLU, Softplus) work well in practice. The paper clearly differentiates itself from related work on exponential families (e.g., [5, 13]) by extending to deformed exponential families and the f-divergence setting.

**Significance:** The results have important theoretical significance for understanding GANs, particularly regarding generator convergence and architecture design. The theoretical insights could guide future work on principled generator design, potentially leading to more stable and effective GAN training. The experimental results suggest practical benefits from applying the theoretical insights, though the connection between theory and experiments needs strengthening. This work advances the state of the art in understanding GANs beyond empirical observations.

**Strengths:**
- Theoretical contribution is novel and addresses a significant gap in the f-GAN literature
- The variational information geometric identity provides a deeper understanding of the f-GAN game
- The connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families is insightful
- The paper demonstrates careful evaluation of both strengths and limitations

**Weaknesses:**
- Main theorems are presented in highly general forms, making them difficult to follow
- Experimental section does not clearly connect to the theoretical results
- Minor grammatical/typographical errors that reduce readability
- The paper does not sufficiently address how the theoretical results scale to more complex real-world applications

**Arguments for acceptance:** This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of f-GANs through an information geometric lens. The connection between activation functions and deformed exponential families provides a principled basis for current empirical practices in GAN architecture design. The results are of interest to both theoretical and applied researchers in the field.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The main theorems are presented in an overly abstract form that makes the core contributions difficult to grasp. The experimental results do not clearly demonstrate the practical value of the theoretical insights, which weakens the paper's impact. The paper would benefit from more concrete examples and clearer connections between theory and practice.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper makes an important theoretical contribution to the understanding of f-GANs with clear potential for guiding future work on GAN architecture design. While the presentation could be improved to make the core ideas more accessible, the theoretical advances are significant and the paper addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. The authors should consider adding more concrete examples to illustrate the main theorems and strengthen the connection between the experimental results and the theoretical claims in the revised version.
