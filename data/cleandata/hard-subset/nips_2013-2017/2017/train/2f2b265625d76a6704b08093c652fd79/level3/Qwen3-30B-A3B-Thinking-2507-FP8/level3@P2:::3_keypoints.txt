- Paper links f-divergence minimization to generator parameter convergence via a variational information-geometric identity relating f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families.  
- Corollary 8 provides the main variational identity connecting f-divergences and Bregman divergences in the GAN setting.  
- Theorem 9 shows deep generators factorize escorts of deformed exponential families, suggesting a theoretical basis for certain activation choices.  
- Theorem 12 completes the supervised GAN game picture by relating proper composite losses to f-GANs and guiding discriminator output activations.  
- Theoretical results are novel, mathematically sound, and adapt information-geometric concepts to GANs.  
- Major weakness: the strong assumption that data and generator distributions lie in deformed exponential families lacks justification and realism.  
- Experiments are unconvincing and fail to demonstrate practical benefits of the theory.  
- The theoretically recommended μ-ReLU activation often underperforms standard ReLU and the paper does not adequately explain this contradiction.  
- The paper does not demonstrate clear practical utility of parameter-space convergence versus traditional divergence-based convergence.  
- Key equation (Eq.7) is underused; no concrete example shows parameter convergence is practically useful when divergence convergence is not.  
- Experimental differences are often within error bars, making empirical claims inconclusive.  
- Exposition is precise but concepts like deformed exponential families, escort distributions, and the signature function χ need more intuitive explanation.  
- Originality: novel in GAN context and builds on prior information-geometry work (e.g., Amari), providing new theoretical insight.  
- Significance: interesting theoretical contribution but currently lacks clear practical impact or generalization beyond restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: reject for now; authors should justify the deformed exponential family assumption, strengthen experiments, clarify practical significance of parameter convergence, and explain the μ-ReLU vs ReLU performance gap.
