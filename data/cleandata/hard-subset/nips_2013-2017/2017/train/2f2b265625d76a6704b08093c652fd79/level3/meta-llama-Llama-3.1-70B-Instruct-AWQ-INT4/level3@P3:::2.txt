This paper proposes a novel information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs, providing a significant advancement in the understanding of the f-GAN principle. The authors successfully establish a connection between the f-divergence formulation and the Bregman divergence formulation, showcasing parameter-space convergence via f-/chi-divergence mapping and Bregman divergence. This contribution is substantial, as it bridges the gap between information theory and information geometry, offering a more comprehensive understanding of the f-GAN game.

The paper's technical soundness is evident in the rigorous proofs and derivations presented throughout the manuscript. The authors provide a clear and well-organized exposition of their results, making it easier for readers to follow their line of reasoning. The reviewer has checked most of the proofs (except Theorem 8) and finds them likely correct. Theorem 4, which presents an IG exposition for f-GAN, is a standout contribution and impressive in its own right.

One of the paper's strengths is its ability to provide implementation guidance for f-GANs and a factorization result of deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions. This is particularly useful for practitioners seeking to apply the f-GAN principle in their work. The authors also provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, although the reviewer notes that the experiments seem disconnected from the main claims, and the activation-function results don't require the IG view.

The paper's clarity is generally good, although the reviewer suggests that the authors could improve the manuscript by moving Figure 1 (IT-IG connections) from the appendix to the main text for clarity. Additionally, the reviewer recommends stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior) instead of introducing it late. Some sections, such as Section 5, could be reorganized or removed to focus the main text and improve the overall flow of the paper.

The originality of the paper is evident in its novel combination of familiar techniques from information theory and geometry. The authors' use of deformed exponential families and chi-logarithms is a significant departure from previous work, and their results have the potential to advance the state of the art in the field. The reviewer notes that the paper's significance is substantial, as it provides a new framework for understanding the f-GAN principle and its applications.

In terms of related work, the authors provide an adequate reference to previous contributions in the field, including the work of Nowozin et al. and the generalized exponential families of Amari et al. The reviewer notes that the paper's results are significantly different from previous work in the field, and the authors' use of information geometry and deformed exponential families is a novel contribution.

Overall, the reviewer recommends accepting the paper, although it may benefit from some revisions to improve clarity and focus. The paper's many useful results and novel contributions make it a valuable addition to the field, and its potential impact on the development of f-GANs and related techniques is substantial.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a novel and significant advancement in the understanding of the f-GAN principle.
* The authors establish a connection between the f-divergence formulation and the Bregman divergence formulation, showcasing parameter-space convergence via f-/chi-divergence mapping and Bregman divergence.
* The paper provides implementation guidance for f-GANs and a factorization result of deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions.
* The authors' use of deformed exponential families and chi-logarithms is a significant departure from previous work.

Arguments for revision:

* The paper could benefit from improved clarity, particularly in the introduction and conclusion.
* Some sections, such as Section 5, could be reorganized or removed to focus the main text and improve the overall flow of the paper.
* The experiments seem disconnected from the main claims, and the activation-function results don't require the IG view.
* The reviewer suggests moving Figure 1 (IT-IG connections) from the appendix to the main text for clarity.
