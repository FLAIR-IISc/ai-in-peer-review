

This paper extends Nowozin et al.'s f-GAN framework by establishing a variational information geometric identity (vig-f-GAN) that connects f-divergences to Bregman divergences through deformed exponential families. The authors generalize the well-known KL-exponential family relationship to all f-divergences, showing that f-GAN(P, Q) can be expressed as a Bregman divergence plus a penalty term when P and Q lie in deformed exponential families. This provides a geometric interpretation of the GAN game, where generator convergence corresponds to parameter convergence in the Bregman geometry. The paper further shows that deep architectures can efficiently factorize deformed exponential family densities, providing a theoretical justification for the effectiveness of deep networks in GANs. The key contribution is a sufficient condition on activation functions (Lemma 11) that ensures the generator's density factors as escorts of deformed exponential families, which is satisfied by most popular activations (ReLU, ELU, Softplus).

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous proofs of the main theorems. The variational information geometric identity (Corollary 8) is a significant theoretical contribution that extends Nowozin et al.'s work (NIPS 2016) by providing a complete parameter-level interpretation of the f-GAN game. The connection to information geometry (Amari, 1990) and deformed exponential families (Amari & Ohara, 2011) is well-motivated and properly contextualized. The proof techniques are sound, though the theorems are presented in highly general forms that make them difficult to follow without substantial background.

The paper's clarity suffers from excessive abstraction. The introduction of deformed exponential families and chi-logarithms lacks intuitive explanations that would help readers grasp the key insights. The main theorems (Theorems 5-7) are dense with mathematical notation that obscures their practical implications. The experimental section (Section 6) fails to clearly demonstrate how the theoretical results translate to practical improvements. While the authors compare different activation functions and link functions, they don't explicitly connect these choices to the theoretical framework established in Sections 3-5. For instance, they don't show how the "factored generative distributions" property (Theorem 9) manifests in their experimental results, making it difficult to assess whether the theoretical insights actually guide better GAN performance.

The paper's originality is strong. It builds on Nowozin et al. (NIPS 2016) but makes a novel contribution by extending the geometric interpretation to all f-divergences, not just KL. The connection between deep architectures and deformed exponential families (Theorem 9) is a fresh perspective that has not appeared in previous NIPS papers on GANs. The sufficient condition on activation functions (Lemma 11) is particularly novel and could influence future GAN architecture design.

The significance of this work is substantial. It provides a theoretical foundation for understanding why deep architectures work well in GANs, addressing a key gap in the GAN literature. The information geometric perspective offers a new lens for analyzing and improving GANs, with potential applications in principled activation function design and discriminator architecture. However, the paper's significance would be stronger if the experimental results more directly demonstrated the practical utility of the theoretical insights. The authors note that "the tools we develop may help to improve further the state of the art" (Section 7), but the experiments only show marginal improvements (Figure 1) without clearly linking these to the theoretical framework.

The paper contains one typographical error (p.7, l.258: "For eq.(7), is means when C...") that should be corrected. The mathematical notation is generally consistent, though the highly abstract presentation may hinder accessibility for many readers.

In summary, this paper makes a technically sound and original contribution to the theoretical understanding of GANs through information geometry. The main theoretical result (vig-f-GAN identity) is significant and has promising applicability. However, the presentation is overly abstract, making the key insights difficult to follow, and the experimental section fails to clearly connect theoretical contributions to practical improvements. With improved clarity and more direct experimental validation of the theoretical claims, this work would be a strong candidate for NIPS.

Pro: Strong theoretical contribution, rigorous proofs, novel connection between deep learning and information geometry, sufficient condition on activation functions.
Con: Abstract presentation makes theorems difficult to follow, experimental results don't clearly relate to theoretical framework, marginal empirical improvements.
