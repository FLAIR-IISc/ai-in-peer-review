- Connects f-divergences and Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families and demonstrates parameter-space convergence for f-GANs.  
- Theorem 4 is a standout result, providing a valuable information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs.  
- Offers implementation guidance and a factorization result linking deep network representations to chi/f functions.  
- Most proofs appear sound on inspection, though Theorem 8 is excluded by the authors and remains a concern.  
- Ambitious effort to bridge information-theoretic foundations and practical generator design.  
- Paper is exceedingly dense and requires strong background in information theory and differential geometry to follow.  
- Motivation for adopting the IG view (analyzing optimization behavior) is introduced too late and weakens reader comprehension.  
- Section 5 on representational power feels disconnected from core f-GAN discussion and lacks concrete guidance for choosing f or improving design.  
- Theorem 6’s claim about “deep sufficient statistics” and matching eq. (13) to a deformed exponential family is questionable.  
- The utility-theory paragraph is extraneous and would be better placed in the appendix.  
- Experiments are somewhat disconnected from theoretical claims; activation-function findings don’t require the IG viewpoint and WGAN results distract from f-GAN focus.  
- Recommend relocating Fig. 1 into the main text and streamlining presentation to improve accessibility.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept, but requires substantial reorganization and clearer presentation to reach full potential.  
- Paper has strong theoretical foundation and potential to inform GAN architecture design despite presentation issues.
