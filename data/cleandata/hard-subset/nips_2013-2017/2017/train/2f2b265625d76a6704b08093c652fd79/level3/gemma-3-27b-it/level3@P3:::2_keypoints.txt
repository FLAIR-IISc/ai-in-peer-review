- Proposes a novel information-geometric (IG) framing of f-GANs connecting f-/chi-divergences and Bregman divergence in parameter space.
- Strong theoretical rigor overall, with most proofs appearing correct; Theorem 4 (IG exposition for f-GAN) is a standout contribution.
- Presents a factorization result linking deep network activations to chi/f functions, suggesting architectural implications for GANs.
- Provides practical implementation guidance for f-GANs useful to researchers.
- Most proofs seem correct except Theorem 8, which the reviewer flags as problematic.
- Paper is extremely dense and difficult to follow; motivation for the IG approach should be clearer up front.
- Section 5 (deformed exponential family representational power) feels disconnected from the central f-GAN narrative and doesn't guide how to choose f.
- Claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” (Theorem 6) needs stronger justification; Eq. (13) doesn't clearly match deformed exponential family form.
- Utility-theory paragraph appears tangential and could be moved to the appendix to improve focus.
- Experimental section is weak and partially confounding; activation results don't require the IG view and WGAN inclusion distracts from f-GAN focus.
- Experiments should directly test theoretical predictions (e.g., impact of activation functions on f-GAN convergence) and report hyperparameter tuning details.
- Figure 1 (IT–IG connections) belongs in the main text to aid comprehension.
- Notation in Section 3 is heavy and sometimes inconsistent (e.g., Bregman divergence argument order); needs cleanup.
- Theorem 9 assumes network reversibility, a strong limitation that should be acknowledged and discussed.
- Paper reads as a collection of interesting results that lack a cohesive synthesized narrative.
- Recommendation: clear accept contingent on addressing clarity, organization, stronger experimental validation, and clearer justification for key theoretical claims.
