- Paper studies GAN theory via f-divergences and deformed exponential families using information geometry.
- Key contributions: links f-divergences to Bregman divergences (Corollary 8), generator factorization result (Theorem 9), activation-function design guidance, and an expected-utility interpretation.
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and present a novel theoretical framework.
- Major concern: heavy reliance on the deformed exponential family assumption with little justification or discussion of generalization beyond it.
- Eq. 7 (parameter-space f-GAN identity) is unclear and its practical advantages over distribution-space optimization are not demonstrated.
- The right-hand side of Eq. 7 is underutilized; need a realistic example showing divergence convergence need not imply parameter convergence.
- Discriminator activation recommendation (g_f) is interesting but lacks comparison to existing heuristics (e.g., [34]).
- Experiments are inconclusive: μ-ReLU performing worse than ReLU is unexplained and reversibility assumption’s relevance to DCGAN is not discussed.
- Missing comparisons to WGAN and other baselines; Table 2 results fall within error bars and provide weak empirical support.
- Minor clarity issues: readers need more accessible intuition for deformations, signatures, and escorts.
- Need comparison of the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8 and clearer explanation of why escort normalization reduces the bound.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical insight but insufficient practical validation; score: Weak Reject.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical framework, clear mathematical results, potential for activation-function design impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, lack of practical demonstrations, unclear benefits of parameter-space optimization, and inconclusive experiments.
