- Main contribution: variational generalization linking f-GAN objectives to Bregman divergences for deformed exponential families, plus a penalty term (Eq. 11).  
- Result: deep generators can factorize escorts of deformed exponential families under a reversibility assumption, with activations determining the family (Theorem 9).  
- Result: discriminator output activation is reinterpreted as part of a proper composite loss, suggesting principled selection of final activation.  
- Strength: novel and significant theoretical link between divergence minimization and parameter-space convergence via information geometry.  
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions generalizing classical identity (Corollary 8) and connecting generator architectures to escorts.  
- Strength: clarifies role of discriminator activation and gives a framework tying architectural choices to theory.  
- Weakness: core results assume both data and model lie in the same deformed exponential family, a strong and likely unrealistic assumption for complex data.  
- Weakness: practical utility of the Bregman-parameter identity is unclear — paper lacks algorithms or guidance for optimizing the right-hand side.  
- Weakness: paper does not fully demonstrate or illustrate why divergence convergence fails to imply parameter convergence in practice.  
- Weakness: experiments are inconclusive and do not convincingly support theoretical claims.  
- Weakness (exp.): μ-ReLU underperforms or shows marginal gains vs ReLU; no analysis explaining the discrepancy or addressing reversibility in DCGANs.  
- Weakness (exp.): Matsushita link comparisons lack thorough benchmarking against known GAN stabilization heuristics.  
- Weakness (exp.): several reported improvements are within baseline error bars, giving insufficient empirical evidence.  
- Minor: paper assumes familiarity with information-geometry terms (deformations, signatures, escorts) and needs intuitive explanations for broader readership.  
- Minor: discussion of penalty term J(Q) and its bounds should be expanded, including practical implications for activation choice and escort normalization.  
- Conclusion: theoretically rich and original, but limited practical relevance due to strong assumptions and weak empirical validation; needs algorithms, broader generalization, and stronger experiments to bridge theory and practice.
