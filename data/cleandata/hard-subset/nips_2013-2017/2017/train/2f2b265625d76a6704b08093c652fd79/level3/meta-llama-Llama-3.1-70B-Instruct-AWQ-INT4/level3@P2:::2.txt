This paper proposes a novel information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs, which provides a more comprehensive understanding of the underlying optimization process. The authors show that the f-GAN principle can be extended to all f-divergences, and that this extension can be captured in an IG framework. The paper also provides implementation guidance for f-GANs and a factorization result of deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a variational information-geometric identity for the f-GAN game. This identity connects the general If-divergence formulation to the general D (Bregman) divergence formulation, and provides a new characterization of the generators' convergence. The authors also show that the standard deep generator architecture is powerful at modeling complex escorts of any deformed exponential family, and that this factorization happens for an especially compact design.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have clearly made an effort to make the content accessible to readers who may not be familiar with information theory and geometry. However, the reviewer notes that the paper is very dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with these topics to follow. The reviewer suggests moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to improve clarity, and recommends stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior) instead of introducing it late.

The reviewer also notes that Section 5, which explains the representational power of deformed exponential families, does not clearly connect to f-GANs. The section does not show how to choose f or guide f-GAN design, and the reviewer questions the claim in Theorem 6 that phi_l are "deep sufficient statistics". The reviewer suggests relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix to focus the main text.

The experiments presented in the paper seem disconnected from the main claims. The activation-function results do not require the IG view, and the WGAN results distract from the f-GAN focus. The reviewer notes that the paper presents many useful results, but that it needs reorganization and clearer, more coherent presentation.
