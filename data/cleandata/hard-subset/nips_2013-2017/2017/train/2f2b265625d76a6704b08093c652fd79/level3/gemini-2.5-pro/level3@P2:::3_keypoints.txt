- Introduces an information-geometric view of f-GANs: minimizing f-divergences equals minimizing a Bregman divergence between parameters for deformed exponential families.  
- Main theoretical contribution: variational identity (Corollary 8) linking f-GAN objective to a parameter-space Bregman divergence plus a penalty term.  
- Shows deep generative architectures (under a reversibility assumption) can factorize many escort distributions, making common activations (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) admissible.  
- Re-interprets discriminator objective via proper composite losses, giving a principled choice for the discriminator’s final activation (gf).  
- Experiments on MNIST and LSUN test varying generator activations and discriminator link functions to probe practical implications.  
- Strength: technically novel and original, connecting f-GANs to deformed exponential families and information geometry.  
- Strength: offers a principled explanation for discriminator output activations through proper composite loss theory.  
- Major concern: central assumption that P and Q lie in the same deformed exponential family is very strong and unrealistic for complex, high-dimensional data like images.  
- Major concern: paper lacks justification for that assumption and does not analyze robustness when the assumption is violated.  
- Major concern: practical benefit of Corollary 8 is not demonstrated—no new optimization method exploiting the RHS, nor counterexamples showing divergence vs parameter convergence mismatch.  
- Experimental weakness: empirical results are inconclusive; ReLU (weakly admissible) often matches or outperforms theoretically preferred activations like Softplus.  
- Experimental weakness: improvements from changing discriminator link functions are marginal and possibly not statistically significant.  
- Clarity issue: exposition is dense and targeted to information-geometry experts; concepts like deformed exponential families, signatures (χ), and escorts need more intuition for ML practitioners.  
- Significance concern: theory is interesting but practical impact on GAN training, stability, or performance is currently unproven.  
- Question for authors: clarify the reversibility assumption for deep generators and practical invertibility of DCGAN fractional-strided convolutions.  
- Question for authors: explain practical implications of the penalty term J(Q) and why its upper bound decreasing with escort normalization Z is desirable.  
- Question for authors: have you considered optimization algorithms that directly leverage the parameter-space Bregman divergence (RHS) and how they compare to standard f-GAN training?  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical framework, addresses foundational questions about GAN parameter convergence, and clarifies discriminator activation design.  
- Arguments for rejection: relies on strong, poorly justified assumptions; lacks demonstrated practical benefits; and has weak, sometimes contradictory empirical support.  
- Summary: interesting and original theoretical work bridging f-GANs and information geometry, but a large gap remains between theory and practical utility due to strong assumptions and inconclusive experiments.
