Summary and relation to prior work
The paper develops an information-geometric (IG) account of f-GANs. The central result shows that, under mild conditions, an f-divergence can be re-expressed as a χ-KL divergence, which in turn equals a Bregman divergence between the natural parameters of deformed exponential families (escorts included), up to a penalty that vanishes for standard exponential families. This yields a variational IG identity for f-GAN (your Theorem 4 in the camera-ready; Corollary 8 in the draft) that interprets the variational discriminator game as optimizing a Bregman divergence DC(θ‖ϑ) in parameter space, thus providing a parameter-space convergence guarantee when the model family matches. Building on this, the paper provides implementation guidance: the discriminator’s “output activation” in f-GAN should be understood as the link of a proper composite loss; the generator’s deep architecture can factorize escorts of deformed exponential families, with the activation function determining the χ signature. This connects deep-network activations to the choice of f/χ and suggests design knobs for both generator and discriminator.

The work builds on Nowozin et al. (NIPS 2016) for the variational f-GAN objective, on Amari’s information geometry and Bregman/KL equivalence within exponential families, and on deformed exponential families/escort distributions from statistical physics and IG. The mapping from general f-divergences to χ-KL and from χ-KL to parameter Bregman divergences extends familiar KL/Bregman identities to the f-GAN setting and, to my knowledge, is new and significant.

Quality
- Theory: I checked most proofs (except the theorem labeled 8 in the draft) and found them careful and likely correct. The constructive nature of the χ selection and the variational form are valuable. The standout is Theorem 4 (IG exposition of f-GAN): it crisply unifies the variational discriminator view with parameter-space IG.
- Scope/assumptions: The escort penalty J(Q) and the assumption that P,Q lie in deformed exponential families are well-motivated; still, practical situations where J(Q) is negligible deserve more guidance. The invertibility assumption for the generator to obtain densities is strong but standard in theoretical analyses; some discussion of approximate invertibility or flow-based architectures would strengthen the story.
- Implementation guidance: The proper composite loss perspective clarifies “gf” in Nowozin et al., showing how the discriminator link Ψ should be chosen and how the generator affects the supervised game.
- Proof clarity: Generally adequate; Theorem 4 is especially clean. I could not fully verify the long proof of Theorem 8 but found no red flags.

Clarity
The paper is dense and assumes considerable background in information theory/geometry. Several presentation changes would greatly improve accessibility:
- Move the “IT–IG connections” figure (currently in the appendix) to the main text early; it would anchor the reader.
- State upfront why the IG view is useful (e.g., the parameter-space convergence via Bregman divergence, links to optimization behavior and model design); currently this motivation arrives late.
- Section 5 reads disconnected from the f-GAN focus: it describes representational power of deformed exponential families but does not concretely guide choosing f or χ for practice. Please make the linkage explicit or trim.
- The paragraph on utility theory (lines 257–269) distracts; consider moving it to the appendix.
- Claim about “deep sufficient statistics”: Theorem 6/9 (numbering differs draft vs. camera-ready) asserts φl are “deep sufficient statistics.” However, equations (12–14) define φl through network recursions rather than as data statistics that render φl(X) sufficient for θ in a deformed exponential family density of X. Equation (13) in particular does not obviously match the canonical deformed exponential family form with φ as sufficient statistics of the data distribution. Please clarify the sense in which “sufficiency” is meant here (model of escort density of latent x vs. data x), and tighten the definition or adjust the terminology.

Originality
The mapping f-divergence → χ-KL → parameter Bregman divergence for deformed exponential families in a variational form is, to my knowledge, novel and important. The proper composite loss reinterpretation of the discriminator’s output link within f-GAN is a useful reframing. The factorization of escorts by deep networks linking activations v to χ is an interesting representational result with a constructive flavor.

Significance
The IG identity provides a principled way to analyze optimization behavior and generator convergence in parameter space for f-GANs, which has been missing. The proper loss guidance can influence how practitioners design discriminator heads. The activation–χ connection could inform generator design. With clearer exposition and tighter experimental validation, this line can impact both theory and practice.

Experiments
The experiments, while technically sound, feel disconnected from the main claims:
- Activation-function comparisons do not require the IG view; they could be framed as standard ablations. Tie them to the theory by showing that activations predicted to induce certain χ lead to measurable differences consistent with the IG identity (e.g., different discriminator links, convergence behavior, or measured Bregman distances).
- WGAN results distract from the f-GAN focus. If retained, motivate how they test your IG predictions; otherwise, concentrate on f-GAN variants and proper composite losses.
- Consider synthetic experiments where P,Q are known deformed exponential families. Track DC(θ‖ϑ) during training to empirically validate parameter-space convergence and illustrate the role of J(Q). Ablate invertibility.

Pros
- Elegant, general IG identity for f-GANs (Theorem 4) that yields parameter-space convergence via Bregman divergences.
- Clear implementation guidance via proper composite losses; clarifies the discriminator “output activation.”
- Interesting factorization result linking deep activations to χ/f choices and escorts, with constructive proofs.
- Proofs are careful; theory likely correct.

Cons
- Very dense; hard to parse without IG background.
- Several sections (e.g., Section 5, utility paragraph) are only loosely connected to the main thrust.
- Experiments do not strongly validate the IG-specific claims; inclusion of WGAN adds noise.
- Ambiguity about “deep sufficient statistics” and exact mapping of φl to deformed exponential family forms.

Suggestions for improvement
- Move the IT–IG connections figure to the main text and motivate the IG view early with a one-paragraph roadmap.
- Sharpen the connection between Section 5 and f-GAN design: provide explicit guidance for choosing f/χ given architecture/activations, or condense.
- Either remove or relocate the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix.
- Clarify the “deep sufficient statistics” claim and the precise sense of sufficiency in equations (12–14).
- Refocus experiments to test the IG predictions directly (parameter-space Bregman distances, role of Ψ, escort penalty), and drop or better motivate WGAN results.

Recommendation
Clear accept. The core theoretical contribution is strong and, with better organization and more targeted experiments, the paper can be influential. The manuscript would benefit from reorganization and clearer exposition to make it accessible to the broader NeurIPS audience.
