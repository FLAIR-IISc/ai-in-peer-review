- Provides an information-geometric (IG) interpretation of f-GANs linking f- and chi-divergences to Bregman divergences and showing parameter-space convergence.  
- Proves a factorization result for deep network representations and connects activation functions to chi/f functions for implementation guidance.  
- Mathematical exposition is rigorous; most proofs verified by reviewer except Theorem 8.  
- Theorem 6 is a strong contribution establishing the IG exposition for f-GANs.  
- Addresses limitations of Nowozin et al. by clarifying generator convergence and connecting it to generator design.  
- Shows convergence occurs within deformed exponential families, linking f-GANs to information-geometric optimization.  
- Appropriately acknowledges related work (e.g., WGANs) while positioning this work as broader theoretical grounding for GANs.  
- Strength: solid theoretical rigor with well-supported mathematical derivations.  
- Strength: novel IG perspective and the connection to deformed exponential families.  
- Strength: practical guidance for choosing activation functions in f-GANs.  
- Strength: Theorem 6’s identity between f-divergences and Bregman divergences is impactful.  
- Weakness: paper is dense and can be hard to follow for readers not versed in information theory/geometry.  
- Weakness: IG motivation appears late; benefits of the perspective should be stated earlier.  
- Weakness: Section 5 lacks clear, practical instructions on choosing f-divergences or guiding f-GAN architecture design.  
- Weakness: Claim that phi_l are “deep sufficient statistics” is questionable and needs more rigorous justification.  
- Weakness: experiments feel disconnected from theory; activation results don’t require IG view and WGAN results distract.  
- Weakness: utility-theory discussion is out of place and detracts from the main message.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—improve clarity, move Figure 1 into main text, state IG benefits early, strengthen Section 5 practical guidance, clarify phi_l justification, streamline experiments, and relocate/remove utility-theory content.
