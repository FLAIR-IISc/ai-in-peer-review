Summary and relation to prior work: This paper develops an information-geometric account of variational f-GANs. Building on Nowozin et al.’s variational formulation for arbitrary f-divergences, the authors introduce the chi-logarithm to generalize the classical KL–exponential family/Bregman link to deformed exponential families, yielding the variational information geometric f-GAN (vig-f-GAN) identity. This identity interprets the f-GAN objective as a Bregman divergence DC(θ‖ϑ) between natural parameters plus a penalty involving the escort distribution, thereby clarifying what the generator converges to. The paper further gives a sufficient condition on activation functions under which deep generators factorize escorts of deformed exponential families, and tightens the supervised-game side by identifying the proper composite loss/link governing the discriminator. These ideas connect information geometry (Amari) with GANs and complement geometry-aware objectives such as WGAN.

Quality: The main theoretical identity is interesting and, if the proofs in the supplement are correct, technically solid. The assumptions (e.g., invertibility of layers, existence/normalization of escorts) are clear but somewhat strong for standard architectures; discussion of how violations affect guarantees would help. Several theorems are presented in very general form, making them hard to parse; more guidance via special cases would improve rigor-by-clarity. The experiments illustrate trends but do not directly validate the geometric predictions (e.g., measured Bregman convergence, factorization diagnostics), making the empirical support limited.

Clarity: The exposition is ambitious but dense. Restating the main results first for exponential families/KL, then progressively generalizing, would help. Section 6 is difficult to tie back to the theory; add targeted tests of the identity and activation conditions. Typo/grammar: p.7, l.258: “For eq.(7), is means when C …” has incorrect clause structure.

Originality: High. The variational identity bridging f-divergences and parameter-space Bregman geometry for deformed exponential families appears novel, as does the sufficient condition connecting activations to factored escorts and the proper-loss view that fixes the “gf” ambiguity.

Significance: Promising. The identity can guide principled architecture/loss design and offers a theoretical lens on generator convergence. Broader impact will depend on demonstrating robustness beyond idealized assumptions and clearer empirical validation.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel, elegant vig-f-GAN identity with broad applicability.
- Clear design implications: activation conditions, proper composite losses.
- Bridges information theory and geometry in GANs.

Cons for acceptance:
- Theorems hard to follow; limited intuitive special cases.
- Experimental section weakly connected to theory; small-scale validation.
- Strong assumptions (invertibility) with limited discussion of practicality.

Suggestions: Add worked examples (KL/exponential family, specific χ and activations like ELU/ReLU), analyze non-invertible layers, measure DC(θ‖ϑ) empirically, test escort factorization, and expand the related-work positioning.
