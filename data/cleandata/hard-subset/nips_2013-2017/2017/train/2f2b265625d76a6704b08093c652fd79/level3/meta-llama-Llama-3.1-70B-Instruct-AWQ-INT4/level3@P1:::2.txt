This paper proposes an information-geometric (IG) view of f-GANs, which provides a novel perspective on the parameter-space convergence of the generator and discriminator. The authors show that the f-GAN objective can be mapped to a Bregman divergence, which enables the analysis of the convergence behavior in the parameter space. This is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a deeper understanding of the f-GAN framework.

The paper also provides implementation guidance for f-GANs and a factorization result of deep network representation linking activations to chi/f functions. The reviewer has checked most of the proofs and finds them likely correct, with Theorem 4 being a standout contribution.

However, the paper is very dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with information theory and geometry to follow. The reviewer suggests moving Figure 1 from the appendix to the main text to improve clarity and recommending stating early why the IG view is useful (e.g., for analyzing optimization behavior) instead of introducing it late.

The reviewer also questions the claim in Theorem 6 that phi_l are "deep sufficient statistics" and suggests relocating or removing the utility-theory paragraph to focus the main text. Additionally, the experiments seem disconnected from the main claims, and the reviewer suggests reorganizing the paper to improve coherence and presentation.

Overall, the reviewer recommends accepting the paper but suggests significant revisions to improve clarity, coherence, and presentation.
