- Paper derives a variational information-geometric identity connecting f-divergence in GANs to Bregman divergence between parameters.  
- Shows deep generators can factor as escorts of deformed exponential distributions under this framework.  
- Argues that careful choices of generator hidden activations and discriminator output activation may improve GANs.  
- Uses deformed exponential distributions as a novel theoretical lens for analyzing GANs.  
- Addresses questions about GAN solution vs. parameter convergence and what generator fits at convergence.  
- Investigates choosing discriminator output activation for compositeness.  
- Provides some experimental results to validate theoretical claims.  
- Many results assume data and models lie in the deformed exponential family, limiting generality and practical applicability.  
- The parameter-space f-GAN identity (Eq.7) is not clearly justified and its practical benefits are unclear.  
- Authors should clarify implementability of Eq.7 and compare experimentally to distribution-based optimization.  
- Experiments are inconclusive: μ-ReLU (theoretically preferred) performs worse than ReLU.  
- Results (e.g., Table 2) appear within error bars, giving insufficient empirical support for claims.  
- Reviewer asks for explanation of reversibility in DCGAN and comparison to WGAN.  
- Paper needs more intuition on deformations, signatures, and escorts for readers unfamiliar with deformed exponential densities.  
- Authors should compare the upper bounds for J(Q) in Theorem 8 and discuss implications for activation choice.  
- Need explanation of why decreasing the bound with escort normalization Z is beneficial.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by analysis, but clarity could be improved and some sections better connected.  
- Originality is strong (novel approach), but some results overlap prior work and contributions relative to prior art should be clarified.  
- Significance: provides interesting theoretical insights but practical relevance and usefulness are not fully established.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions; suggested scores Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
