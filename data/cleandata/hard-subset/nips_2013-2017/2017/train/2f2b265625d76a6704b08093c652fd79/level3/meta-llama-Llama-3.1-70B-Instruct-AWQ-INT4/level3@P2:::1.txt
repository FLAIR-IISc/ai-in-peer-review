The paper "f-GANs in an Information Geometric Nutshell" presents a novel approach to understanding the f-GAN principle, extending it to all f-divergences and providing a geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective. The authors introduce the concept of deformed exponential families, a superset of exponential families, and show that current deep architectures can factorize a large number of such densities using a compact design.

The paper begins by summarizing the main ideas of the f-GAN principle and its extension to f-divergences. The authors then present their main contribution, a variational information geometric identity for the f-GAN game, which connects the f-GAN objective to a geometric optimization problem. This identity, dubbed the vig-f-GAN identity, provides a new characterization of the generator's convergence and sheds light on the connection between the value of the optimal discriminator and the density ratio between the generator and discriminator distributions.

The authors also provide a sufficient condition on activation functions to obtain factored generative distributions, which is a key result in the paper. They show that popular activation functions such as ReLU, ELU, and Softplus satisfy this condition, making them suitable for use in the f-GAN framework.

The paper's main theoretical result, the vig-f-GAN identity, is interesting and has promising applicability. However, the presentation of the main theorems is often in very general forms, making them hard to follow. Additionally, the experimental observations in Section 6 are difficult to relate back to the theoretical results, and the connection between the two could be made clearer.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, but there are some typographical and grammatical issues, such as the incorrect clause structure on page 7, line 258. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the main theorems and a clearer connection between the theoretical results and the experimental observations.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to understanding the f-GAN principle and provides a new geometric interpretation of the f-GAN objective. The use of deformed exponential families and the vig-f-GAN identity are new contributions to the field.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact on the field of generative models and deep learning. The vig-f-GAN identity provides a new tool for understanding the f-GAN principle and could lead to new insights and applications in the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel approach to understanding the f-GAN principle. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper is a significant contribution to the field and has the potential to make a lasting impact.
