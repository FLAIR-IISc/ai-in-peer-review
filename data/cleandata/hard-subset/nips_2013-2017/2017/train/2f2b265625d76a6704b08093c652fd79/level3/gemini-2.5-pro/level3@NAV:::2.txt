This paper presents a novel information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs, building upon the variational framework for f-divergences introduced by Nowozin et al. The authors establish a key identity linking f-divergences in the f-GAN objective to Bregman divergences between the parameters of deformed exponential families. This connection provides a theoretical basis for analyzing the convergence of the generator in its parameter space. The paper further provides a factorization result for deep generative networks, linking their representational power and activation functions to this IG framework, and offers guidance on implementation.

The primary strength of this work lies in its theoretical contributions. The information-geometric exposition of f-GANs (Theorem 4) is a standout result, providing a significant new lens through which to analyze these models. The theoretical analysis appears sound; I have checked most proofs (except Theorem 8) and found them likely correct. This work successfully bridges the information-theoretic view of f-GANs with the geometric optimization perspective seen in other GAN variants.

However, the paper suffers from significant organizational and clarity issues. The manuscript is extremely dense, making it difficult to follow for readers not deeply versed in information geometry. To improve readability, I strongly recommend moving Figure 1, which illustrates the IT-IG connections, from the appendix to the main text. Additionally, the motivation for adopting an IG view should be stated upfront—explaining early why this perspective is useful for analyzing optimization would better frame the paper's contributions.

There are also disconnects in the narrative. The link between Section 5 and practical f-GAN design is unclear; it explains the representational power of deformed exponential families but fails to show how this guides the choice of the function *f* or generator architecture. I am also not fully convinced by the claim in Theorem 6 that activations are "deep sufficient statistics," as the form in eq. (13) may not strictly match that of a deformed exponential family. Finally, the experiments feel somewhat disconnected from the main theoretical claims. The results on activation functions do not seem to require the IG framework, and the inclusion of WGAN results distracts from the paper's core focus.

In summary, this paper contains several novel and potentially important theoretical results. However, its dense presentation and disjointed sections currently obscure the significance of these contributions. Despite these issues, the core theoretical work is strong enough to warrant a **clear accept**, contingent on a substantial reorganization to create a more cohesive narrative.
