- Introduces an information-geometric (IG) perspective on f-GANs linking f-divergences and Bregman divergences via deformed exponential families.
- Shows f-GAN optimization can be reformulated as a geometric optimization in parameter space.
- Theorem 4 (variational information-geometric identity) is a significant theoretical advance extending the KL–Bregman connection.
- Provides a factorization result demonstrating deep networks can efficiently represent escorts of deformed exponential families.
- Common activation functions (ReLU, ELU, Softplus) meet the conditions required by the factorization result.
- Theoretically rich contribution that clarifies generator convergence in parameter space.
- Proofs are generally sound, with the exception of concerns about Theorem 8.
- Factorization result offers useful insights for neural network design.
- Experiments validating activation choices and link-function tuning give practical guidance for f-GAN implementation.
- Significant clarity and organization issues: IG perspective is introduced too late and its utility should be stated early.
- Connection between Section 5 (representational power) and f-GAN is unclear and needs stronger justification.
- Theorem 6’s claim about “deep sufficient statistics” requires more rigorous support.
- Experimental sections feel disconnected from the main theory, and WGAN results distract from the f-GAN focus.
- Suggest moving the utility-theory paragraph to the appendix, placing Figure 1 in the main text, and reorganizing to better link theory and practice.
