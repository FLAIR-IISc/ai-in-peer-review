- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling while keeping theoretical guarantees.  
- Key insight: formulate the sampling distribution as a convex problem using safe upper/lower bounds on gradients to avoid full gradient computation each iteration.  
- Method provably outperforms Li-based sampling (including uniform) and is computationally efficient.  
- Theorem 3.2: proposed sampling always better than Li-based sampling; Theorem 3.4: O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution.  
- Shows efficient maintenance of safe gradient bounds for GLMs with manageable O(n log n) overhead per iteration.  
- Empirical results (rcv1, real-sim, news20) show large practical benefits for coordinate descent in iterations and total time.  
- Method improves over static sampling even with very loose bounds (e.g., from Cauchy–Schwarz).  
- Provides clear illustration of theoretical guarantees via evolution of the vk parameter controlling adaptive stepsize.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, well-structured, and connects adaptive sampling to optimal sampling (Example 2.3) and prior work.  
- Presentation is clear, reproducible, and well-contextualized in the literature.  
- Limitation: focus on GLMs; applicability and safe-bound maintenance for more complex, non-GLM models not fully explored.  
- SGD gains are less pronounced than CD; paper lacks sufficient explanation or guidance on when the method benefits SGD.  
- Suggests need for deeper discussion on practical behavior of competitive ratio ρk, since theoretical bounds appear conservative vs empirical results.  
- Overall significance: practical, theoretically grounded method that bridges optimal adaptive schemes and efficient fixed sampling, useful for large-scale optimization.
