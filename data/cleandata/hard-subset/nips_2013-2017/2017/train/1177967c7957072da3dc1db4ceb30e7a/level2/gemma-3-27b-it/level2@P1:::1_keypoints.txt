- Introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.  
- Aims to bridge the gap between theoretically strong but costly adaptive methods and simpler fixed sampling approaches.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical derivations and consistent notation, with clear connections to prior work on gradient- and duality gap-based sampling.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees showing the scheme outperforms uniform/fixed importance sampling and recovers gradient-based sampling in full-information settings.  
- Designed to be computationally efficient, with claimed overhead O(n log n) per iteration relative to fixed sampling.  
- The approach is general and can be integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, accessible to readers not deeply familiar with importance sampling.  
- Empirical evaluation is convincing on several datasets and losses but is somewhat limited in scope.  
- Reliance on “safe” upper and lower gradient bounds (`, u) may restrict applicability beyond GLMs and affects performance quality.  
- Competitive ratio analysis depends on non-overlapping bounds (γ), a potentially restrictive condition in practice.  
- Practical overhead of Algorithm 4’s sorting step could be significant for very high-dimensional problems; more analysis needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important scalability challenge, is theoretically sound, empirically effective, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on gradient bounds may limit use cases and empirical comparisons with state-of-the-art methods are insufficient.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to optimization for ML with some limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2017).
