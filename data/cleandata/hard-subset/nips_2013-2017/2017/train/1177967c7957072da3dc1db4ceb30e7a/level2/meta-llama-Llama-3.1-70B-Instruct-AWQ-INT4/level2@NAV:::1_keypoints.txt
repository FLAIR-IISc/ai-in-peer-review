- Proposes an adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe lower and upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling efficiently.
- Claims provable improvement over uniform and fixed importance sampling.
- Claims per-iteration computation cost of O(n log n) and easy integration into existing CD/SGD methods.
- Provides solid theoretical derivation of optimal sampling distributions and stepsizes, with proofs of superiority and supportive numerical experiments.
- Lacks detailed empirical comparisons with existing adaptive schemes (e.g., Zhao & Zhang [36]).
- Practical aspects under-discussed: how to compute safe gradient bounds efficiently and real-world computational/memory trade-offs.
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge but should discuss relations to other stochastic methods (e.g., variance-reduced SGD).
- Novel contribution but needs more on potential extensions (e.g., momentum, other optimizers).
- Paper is largely complete in theory and experiments but needs expanded implementation details.
- Acknowledges limitations (cost, memory, bound accuracy); reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions addressing comparisons, practicality, and bounds.
