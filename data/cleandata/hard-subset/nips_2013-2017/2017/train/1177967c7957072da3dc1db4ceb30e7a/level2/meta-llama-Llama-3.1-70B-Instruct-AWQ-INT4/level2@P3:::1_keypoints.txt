- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe lower and upper gradient bounds to approximate optimal gradient-based sampling.
- Claims optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically justified but computationally infeasible, motivating their approximation.
- Develops an efficient algorithm to compute the optimal sampling distribution with O(n log n) cost per iteration.
- Proves the adaptive sampling is provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling.
- Provides theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical evaluations on various ML tasks.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with concise presentation of results.
- Technical content is sound with thorough analysis and supporting experiments.
- Strength: novel and efficient adaptive importance sampling scheme with provable benefits.
- Strength: thorough theoretical and empirical analysis.
- Strength: clear exposition and introduction of the problem and solution.
- Weakness: assumes safe gradient bounds can be efficiently computed, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
- Originality: introduces a novel adaptive sampling scheme and an efficient O(n log n) algorithm.
