This paper introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent (CD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors propose a safe and efficient method to approximate the optimal gradient-based sampling distribution using safe bounds on the gradient. The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a practical and efficient adaptive importance sampling scheme that can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms. The proposed method is based on safe bounds on the gradient, which makes it computationally feasible. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the proposed method, showing that it is provably better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling.

The paper also presents extensive numerical experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed adaptive sampling scheme on various machine learning tasks. The results show that the proposed method significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in terms of iterations and computation time.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The proposed adaptive importance sampling scheme is novel and efficient, making it a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning.
2. The paper provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed method, which is essential for understanding its behavior and performance.
3. The numerical experiments are extensive and well-designed, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The paper assumes that safe bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The proposed method requires additional computations to update the safe bounds on the gradient, which may add overhead to the existing CD and SGD algorithms.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method and its comparison to existing methods.
