- Problem: Making adaptive importance sampling practical for large-scale methods (CD, SGD) where optimal gradient-based sampling is infeasible due to full-gradient costs.
- Core contribution: A "safe" adaptive sampling scheme using efficiently computable lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Novel formulation: Poses sampling choice as a min-max problem—choose distribution minimizing worst-case risk over gradients consistent with bounds.
- Theoretical guarantee: Provably improves over fixed Li-based sampling (Theorem 3.2), addressing limitations of previous approximations.
- Algorithmic contribution: Provides an efficient O(n log n) algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute the robust sampling distribution.
- Practicality: Generic scheme that can be integrated into existing solvers; clearly written and well-organized presentation.
- Empirical results: Extensive experiments on real datasets show significant iteration and wall-clock speed-ups over standard baselines.
- Main weakness: Method performance depends on gradient bound quality; current GLM bounds via Cauchy–Schwarz can be loose.
- Suggested exploration: Paper would benefit from deeper analysis of how bound tightness affects performance (e.g., competitive ratio ρk) and from exploring stronger bounding techniques.
- Additional note: Empirical gains are less pronounced for SGD than for CD and merit further investigation.
- Overall assessment: Strong, original, theoretically sound, and practically significant work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
