- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe upper/lower gradient bounds as a proxy for full gradients.  
- Four main contributions: theoretical justification for gradient-based sampling in CD; a generic, efficient adaptive sampling strategy; integration into CD and SGD for structured problems; extensive numerical validation.  
- Proven to be better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and near-optimal under limited gradient information.  
- Introduces minimal computational overhead (O(n log n) per iteration), making it practical for large-scale problems.  
- Generic method that can be readily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Empirical results show faster convergence and reduced computational cost versus fixed sampling on real-world datasets.  
- Innovative and constructive use of safe gradient bounds to compute an adaptive sampling distribution.  
- Limited empirical impact observed for SGD, with stronger benefits mainly for CD.  
- Relies on availability of safe gradient bounds, which may be difficult to compute for complex models.  
- Paper is dense and highly technical, reducing accessibility for a broader audience.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with other recent adaptive sampling methods, leaving relative performance unclear.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand comparisons, and discuss SGD limitations and extensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantees, practical efficiency, demonstrated CD improvements, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited SGD gains, dependence on gradient bounds, and dense presentation.
