- Proposes a novel "safe adaptive importance sampling" scheme for CD and SGD that avoids full gradient evaluations by using safe lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes.  
- Core contribution: a provably optimal sampling distribution derived from safe bounds, avoiding pitfalls of stale/naive approximations.  
- Algorithm 2 is proven always superior to fixed importance sampling and recovers optimal gradient-based sampling in the full-information limit.  
- Theoretical guarantees: strong results (Theorems 3.2, 3.4) and tight competitive ratio bounds (Lemma 3.5) fill gaps left by prior work.  
- Practical efficiency: per-iteration overhead O(n log n) (Theorem 3.4), which becomes negligible compared to O(d) gradient cost for large d.  
- GLM examples show safe bounds can be maintained at low cost, making the approach deployable in practice.  
- Empirical CD results on rcv1, real-sim, news20 show 2–5× faster convergence and dynamic improvement via adaptive stepsizes.  
- Safe bounds approach remains effective even with naive Cauchy–Schwarz estimates (Section 5.3).  
- SGD experiments show minimal gains over uniform sampling; authors should clarify whether this is inherent or implementation-specific.  
- Competitive ratio ρ_k (Eq. 10) is important but under-explained; a short numerical example would improve accessibility.  
- Minor clarity issues: better emphasize why prior bounds fail (transition to Sec. 3.1) and reference the proof sketch for Lemma 2.4 more clearly in the main text.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; bridges theory and practice and merits acceptance despite minor SGD-analysis and clarity issues.
