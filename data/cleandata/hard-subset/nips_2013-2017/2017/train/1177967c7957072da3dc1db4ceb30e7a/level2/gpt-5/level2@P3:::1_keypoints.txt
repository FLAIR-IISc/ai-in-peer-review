- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using lower/upper bounds on per-coordinate gradients to approximate optimal gradient-based sampling.  
- Formulates a minimax problem that yields the best sampling distribution under bounds, never worse than uniform or Li-based sampling, and recovers full-information optimal sampling.  
- Theoretically shows gradient-based sampling can provide up to an n-fold speedup in coordinate descent.  
- Introduces a safe adaptive stepsize αk = 1/vk where vk is the convex min-max solution, with worst-case guarantees and uniform improvement over Li-based sampling.  
- Provides an O(n log n) sorting-based algorithm to compute the optimal sampling distribution p̂ efficiently.  
- Gives practical O(n)-per-iteration recipes to maintain safe bounds (` , u) for GLMs in both CD and SGD.  
- Empirical results show marked iteration and wall-clock speedups for CD, and smaller but consistent gains for SGD; overhead is small enough to improve wall-clock time in tested regimes.  
- Connects to prior non-uniform/adaptive sampling and gradient/gap-based methods, but distinguishes itself by worst-case guarantees under bound sets.  
- Relates to Gauss–Southwell and approximate steepest coordinate descent and to adaptive SDCA literature; uses Sion/Komiya minimax theory in derivations.  
- Strength: technically sound derivations (lemmas/theorems) and interpretable competitive-ratio analysis.  
- Strength: general framework that recovers optimal full-information sampling and uniformly improves fixed Li-based sampling.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic instantiation and simple GLM bound updates with stepsize coupling motivated by expected-progress minimization.  
- Strength: empirical validation with diagnostic plots (vk/Tr[L]) that corroborate theory.  
- Weakness: per-iteration O(n log n) cost to compute p̂ may be prohibitive for very large n, small d, or highly sparse data; needs nuanced overhead analysis.  
- Weakness: Cauchy–Schwarz-based bounds for GLMs can be loose and conservative, causing near-fixed sampling behavior early on; limited exploration beyond GLMs.  
- Weakness: missing empirical comparisons to other adaptive strategies (duality-gap sampling, Gauss–Southwell, stale-gradient, adaptive SDCA).  
- Weakness: modest SGD improvements; lacks deeper analysis of conditions (variance structure, gradient-norm heterogeneity) where method helps and how to amortize p̂ updates.  
- Quality: careful theoretical development with standard assumptions and precise algorithmic details.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but has minor notational inconsistencies/typos and would benefit from a small inline example.  
- Originality: novel safe minimax formulation and its coupling with an adaptive stepsize compared to prior approximations.  
- Significance: valuable for widespread CD/SGD use; demonstrated CD improvements indicate practical impact and potential influence on future adaptive sampling designs.  
- Essential suggestion: add empirical comparisons to other adaptive CD methods (gap-based, Gauss–Southwell, adaptive SDCA).  
- Essential suggestion: analyze/report overhead vs. d, n, and sparsity; test periodic recomputation of p̂ to amortize O(n log n) cost.  
- Essential suggestion: include ablations on bound tightness (Cauchy–Schwarz vs tighter/data-driven bounds) and initialization strategies for (`0, u0).  
- Optional suggestion: discuss memory/implementation for large-scale bound maintenance, consider blockwise/streaming updates.  
- Optional suggestion: explore nonconvex objectives empirically and release code for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel provable guarantees, efficient algorithm, and convincing CD empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to competing adaptive methods, incomplete overhead analysis for unfavorable regimes, and modest SGD benefits.  
- Overall: solid theoretical and practical contribution—particularly impactful for CD—with acceptance recommended pending stronger empirical comparisons and overhead analysis.
