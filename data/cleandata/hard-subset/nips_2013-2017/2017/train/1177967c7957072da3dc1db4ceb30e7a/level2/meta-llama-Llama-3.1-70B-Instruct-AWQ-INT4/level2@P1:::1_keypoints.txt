Proposal: Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe bounds on gradients to approximate optimal gradient-based sampling.

Motivation: Optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically sound but often computationally infeasible; safe bounds offer an efficient approximation.

Technical soundness: Paper is well-written with sound technical content and thorough analysis.

Main contribution 1: Novel adaptive importance sampling scheme based on safe gradient bounds, efficient and easily integrated into existing CD/SGD.

Main contribution 2: Theoretical analysis including a proof of optimality and a competitive-ratio bound.

Main contribution 3: Comprehensive empirical evaluation on multiple datasets demonstrating practical effectiveness.

Strength 1: Novel and efficient method that integrates easily into existing algorithms.

Strength 2: Strong theoretical backing (optimality proof and competitive-ratio bound).

Strength 3: Comprehensive experiments showing effectiveness in practice.

Weakness 1: Assumes safe gradient bounds can be computed efficiently, which may not hold in all settings.

Weakness 2: Lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.

Overall assessment: High-quality, clear, original, and significant contribution (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance each rated 9/10).

Arguments for acceptance: Novel efficient scheme, solid theoretical analysis, and strong empirical results.

Arguments against acceptance: Practical concerns about computing safe bounds and missing detailed complexity analysis.
