- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds and convex optimization.
- Claims the scheme is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- Claims efficient per-iteration computation and recovery of gradient-based sampling in the full-information setting.
- Provides a geometric interpretation of the optimization problem and an efficient algorithm to compute the solution.
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and the paper thoroughly analyzes the scheme’s properties.
- Offers extensive numerical evaluation on real datasets showing practical effectiveness.
- Discusses computational complexity and the overhead introduced by the scheme in detail.
- Contribution is significant: a novel, efficient adaptive sampling method applicable to many ML problems.
- Novel approach compared to prior work, relying on safe gradient bounds and convex formulation rather than heuristics.
- Key limitation: assumes safe gradient bounds can be efficiently computed, which may not always be feasible.
- Performance may depend heavily on the quality of the bounds; more guidance is needed on how to choose or improve them.
- Suggests acceptance but recommends the authors provide more insight into bound selection and explore applications and limitations further.
