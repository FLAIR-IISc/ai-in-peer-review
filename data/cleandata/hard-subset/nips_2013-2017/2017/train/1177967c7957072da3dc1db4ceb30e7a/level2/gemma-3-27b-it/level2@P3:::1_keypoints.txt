- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for accelerating coordinate descent and SGD using bounds on gradients, provably better than uniform sampling and optimal given available information.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees and detailed proofs (in appendix) supporting the method’s benefits.
- Proposes a computationally efficient algorithm (O(n log n) per iteration) that is scalable to large problems.
- Method is generic and can be integrated into existing CD and SGD frameworks.
- Extensive experiments on real datasets show consistent improvements over fixed importance sampling and sometimes approach optimal gradient-based sampling.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the method and theory accessible.
- Performance depends on availability of accurate safe gradient bounds; obtaining these for complex models is not fully addressed.
- Experimental gains for SGD are modest, suggesting limited practical improvement in that setting and need for further investigation.
- Competitive ratio analysis is interesting but requires restrictive conditions (e.g., γ ≥ 1) that limit its applicability.
- Limited empirical comparison to other state-of-the-art adaptive sampling methods reduces strength of evaluation.
- Addresses an important practical problem in large-scale optimization.
- The solution is theoretically sound, computationally efficient, and empirically validated.
- Represents a novel contribution to importance sampling and adaptive optimization literature.
- Reliance on accurate gradient bounds could limit applicability in some real-world scenarios.
- Modest SGD improvements and restrictive competitive ratio conditions warrant further study.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable practical and theoretical contributions; weaknesses are relatively minor.
- Essential recommendation: discuss challenges of obtaining accurate gradient bounds for complex models and explore robust estimation methods.
- Essential recommendation: investigate causes of limited SGD improvement and consider scheme modifications.
- Optional recommendation: provide more detailed discussion of conditions for good competitive ratio.
- Optional recommendation: compare to other state-of-the-art adaptive sampling methods when feasible.
