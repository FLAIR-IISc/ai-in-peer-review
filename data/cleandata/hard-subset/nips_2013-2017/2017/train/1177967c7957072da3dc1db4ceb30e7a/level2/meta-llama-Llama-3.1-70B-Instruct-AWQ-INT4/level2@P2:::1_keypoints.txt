- Introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates the optimal gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.  
- Method is safe, efficient, and computationally feasible, making it easy to integrate into existing CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees showing the method is provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Extensive numerical experiments demonstrate significant improvements over fixed importance sampling in iterations and runtime.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the ideas.  
- Strength: Novel and efficient adaptive sampling approach with practical applicability.  
- Strength: Theoretical analysis that clarifies behavior and performance.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-designed empirical evaluation across tasks.  
- Weakness: Assumes safe gradient bounds can be computed efficiently, which may not hold in all settings.  
- Weakness: Additional computation to update bounds may introduce overhead to CD and SGD.  
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and direct comparisons to existing methods.  
- Pro-accept: Novelty, theoretical guarantees, and extensive experiments support acceptance.  
- Con-accept: Practical concerns about computing/updating safe bounds and added overhead temper enthusiasm.  
- Overall recommendation: Significant contribution and should be accepted, with suggested improvements addressing noted weaknesses.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
