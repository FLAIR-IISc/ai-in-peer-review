- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" for CD and SGD that uses safe gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling, guaranteeing improvements over uniform or fixed importance sampling while remaining computationally feasible.

- Proves the proposed sampling distribution is provably better than fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2) and gives an optimization formulation to compute it under safe bounds (Eq. 7).

- Complexity per iteration is O(n log n), which is often negligible compared to gradient computation costs (especially when d = Ω(n)).

- Provides a theoretical justification for adaptive sampling in CD and shows potential speedups up to n-fold versus fixed sampling (Section 2).

- Empirical results show consistent and substantial improvements for CD across multiple datasets (rcv1, real-sim, news20) with both L1 and L2 regularization, improving both iteration count and total time.

- Competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5 and 3.6) supplies additional performance guarantees and insights.

- Paper is well-written and well-organized, includes detailed algorithms (Algorithms 1–4), thorough proofs, and a helpful geometric interpretation (Appendix B).

- Method performs well even with very loose safe gradient bounds and requires no initialization of gradient estimates (Figures 3–6).

- SGD experiments show less dramatic improvements than CD, and the paper does not adequately explain this discrepancy.

- Scope is mainly GLMs for tracking safe gradient bounds; the paper lacks discussion of challenges for more complex models like deep neural networks.

- Omits citing a relevant recent work ("Faster Coordinate Descent via Adaptive Importance Sampling", Perekrestenko et al., AISTATS 2017), despite differences in guarantees.

- Computational overhead is minimal in practice and the method has clear practical value for CD methods.

- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and empirical contribution to adaptive importance sampling with recommendation for acceptance.

- Strengths: strong theoretical guarantees, clear theory–practice connection, comprehensive experiments, minimal overhead.

- Weaknesses: limited explanation of SGD results, missing related work citation, limited applicability beyond GLMs.
