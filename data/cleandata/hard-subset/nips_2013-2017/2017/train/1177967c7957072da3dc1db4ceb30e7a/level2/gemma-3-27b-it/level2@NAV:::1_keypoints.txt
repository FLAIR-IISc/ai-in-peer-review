- Addresses accelerating coordinate descent and SGD via adaptive importance sampling using safe gradient bounds.
- Core idea: approximate gradient-based sampling with safe bounds to get a computationally efficient scheme with provable guarantees.
- Identifies drawbacks of prior adaptive strategies: high computational cost and sometimes worse guarantees than uniform sampling.
- Claims the proposed sampling is optimal given available gradient bounds and always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- Strong theoretical analysis showing speed-up factors for CD and performance bounds.
- Well-connected to prior work on gradient-based and duality gap-based sampling.
- Practical contribution: efficient computation of the sampling distribution and clear algorithms for CD and SGD (Algorithms 2 & 3).
- Useful discussion on tracking safe gradient bounds for GLMs, giving a practical application.
- Experimental validation on several datasets and losses (squared hinge, logistic) supports theory and shows effectiveness.
- Plots of vk values illustrate benefits even with loose gradient bounds.
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and computational overhead, especially in very high-dimensional scenarios.
- Weakness: experiments focus mainly on GLMs; performance on complex models (e.g., deep nets) is not explored.
- Weakness: the performance gap between CD and SGD with the method could be better explained.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and overhead discussion.
