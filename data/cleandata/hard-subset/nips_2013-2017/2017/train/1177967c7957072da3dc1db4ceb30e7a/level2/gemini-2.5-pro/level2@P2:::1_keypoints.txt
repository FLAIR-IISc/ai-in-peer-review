- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using efficiently computable lower/upper bounds on gradient components.  
- Frames sampling selection as a minimax problem that minimizes the worst-case convergence bound over all gradients consistent with the bounds.  
- Provides an O(n log n) algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute the minimax sampling distribution.  
- The method is provably at least as good as L_i-based importance sampling (including uniform sampling) — Theorem 3.2.  
- Demonstrated generically and applied to GLMs where gradient bounds can be maintained with linear overhead per iteration.  
- Extensive experiments across losses, regularizers, and datasets show significant improvements over fixed sampling and near-optimal performance in iterations and wall-clock time.  
- Novel, principled minimax formulation moves beyond prior heuristic adaptive sampling methods.  
- Timing experiments support the claim that the computational overhead is negligible relative to coordinate gradient costs.  
- Performance depends on the quality/tightness of the safe bounds; GLM bounds from Cauchy–Schwarz can be loose and sensitivity is unclear.  
- Limited discussion on extending the bounding approach to other models (e.g., neural networks) where maintaining meaningful bounds may be challenging.  
- Suggest clarifying Algorithm 4 with a high-level walkthrough of how the worst-case vector is constructed by fixing components to bounds based on a threshold.  
- Suggest analyzing why SGD gains are smaller than CD gains (e.g., differences in optimal sampling structure or bound evolution).  
- Suggest commenting on the practical utility of the competitive-ratio analysis, since computing w_k is non-convex.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
