- Proposes a "safe adaptive importance sampling" scheme for CD and SGD using safe bounds on gradients to avoid full gradient computations.  
- Addresses infeasibility of gradient-based adaptive sampling by constructing a provably better sampling distribution from gradient bounds.  
- The sampling distribution is theoretically guaranteed to outperform uniform or fixed importance sampling and is derived via a convex optimization with worst-case guarantees.  
- Computational cost is O(n log n) per iteration, enabling practical computation of the sampling distribution.  
- Advances prior NIPS work by providing provable guarantees for approximate adaptive sampling that earlier methods lacked.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with clear proofs (e.g., Theorems 3.2 and 3.4).  
- Empirical results on rcv1, real-sim, and news20 show substantial speedups for CD and near-optimal performance with minimal overhead.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-structured algorithms, consistent notation, and logical exposition from theory to practice.  
- Originality is strong: novel convex formulation using gradient bounds and worst-case guarantees.  
- High significance: broadly applicable to CD and SGD, practical for large-scale optimization, effective even with loose gradient bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contributions, practical low-overhead implementation, extensive empirical validation, clear advancement over prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: O(n log n) complexity may be heavy for extremely large n; empirical gains for SGD are less pronounced than for CD.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with clear theoretical and practical value; recommend acceptance.
