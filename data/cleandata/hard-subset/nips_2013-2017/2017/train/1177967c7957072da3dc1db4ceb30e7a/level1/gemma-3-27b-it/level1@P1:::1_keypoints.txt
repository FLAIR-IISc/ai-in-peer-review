- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling using provable safe gradient bounds.  
- Addresses the scalability problem of fully gradient-based adaptive sampling by formulating sampling as a convex optimization problem.  
- The method is provably better than uniform sampling and recovers optimal gradient-based sampling in the full-information setting.  
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees, including a proof that the scheme is optimal given the bounds.  
- Presents an efficient implementation (Algorithms 1–4) and argues the sampling can often be computed at negligible extra cost.  
- The approach is general and can be integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Extensive empirical validation on real datasets and GLMs demonstrates iteration and runtime improvements.  
- Paper is clearly written and practically oriented, with helpful algorithms and explanations.  
- Relies on availability of safe lower/upper gradient bounds; applicability to complex models where bounds are hard to obtain is unclear.  
- Algorithm 4 has O(n log n) complexity per iteration, which could be costly for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Empirical comparisons to SGD variants are limited; missing comparisons to popular adaptive optimizers like Adam or RMSprop.  
- Discussion of the competitive ratio (Lemmas 3.5, 3.6) feels disconnected and needs clearer practical interpretation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important problem, strong theory, practical efficiency, convincing experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on gradient bounds, potential computational bottleneck, and incomplete empirical comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with minor limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance (Accept).
