This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent (CD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified, but often computationally infeasible. To address this, they introduce a novel way of using safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient to compute the adaptive sampling distribution.

The paper's main contributions are:

1.  A new adaptive importance sampling strategy that is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and can be efficiently computed.
2.  A generic and efficient algorithm for computing the safe sampling distribution, which can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.
3.  Theoretical guarantees that the proposed sampling scheme is never worse than static sampling and is the best possible with respect to the limited information at hand.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive numerical testing on real data, including generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization. The results show that the proposed adaptive sampling scheme significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time, and that the introduced computational overhead is small enough to outperform fixed importance sampling in terms of total computation time.

Strengths of the paper:

*   The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including proofs of the optimality of their sampling scheme and its computational efficiency.
*   The paper includes a detailed evaluation of the proposed method on real data, demonstrating its effectiveness in practice.
*   The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow and understand.

Weaknesses of the paper:

*   The paper assumes that safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case in practice.
*   The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of their approach with other existing methods, which would be useful for understanding its relative strengths and weaknesses.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of machine learning, providing a new and effective approach to adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD algorithms. The authors' thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation demonstrate the potential of their approach to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of these algorithms.
