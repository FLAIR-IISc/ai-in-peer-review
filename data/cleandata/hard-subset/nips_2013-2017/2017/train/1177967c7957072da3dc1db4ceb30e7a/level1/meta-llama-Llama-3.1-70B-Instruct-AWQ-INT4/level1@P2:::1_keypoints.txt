- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Approximates gradient-based sampling using safe lower and upper bounds on gradients to avoid infeasible computations.  
- Sampling distribution is provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Can be computed efficiently in many applications at negligible extra cost.  
- Builds on prior adaptive sampling work, including gradient-based and duality gap-based sampling, and variance reduction techniques.  
- Emphasis on safe bounds and efficient computation distinguishes it from prior approaches.  
- Technically sound with clear presentation and thorough theoretical analysis, including competitive ratio results.  
- Theoretical claims are supported by extensive numerical experiments on real datasets.  
- Well-written paper with clear notation and accessible mathematical derivations.
