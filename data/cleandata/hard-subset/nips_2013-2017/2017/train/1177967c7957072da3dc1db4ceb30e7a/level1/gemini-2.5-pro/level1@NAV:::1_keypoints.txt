- Proposes Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling: a min-max formulation that chooses sampling distributions robust to uncertainty in gradient magnitudes.  
- Uses efficiently computable safe lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes instead of full gradient information.  
- Provides a principled, theoretically-grounded alternative to heuristic upper-bound sampling (e.g., Zhao & Zhang 2015).  
- Theorem 3.2 guarantees the proposed sampling is always at least as good as fixed importance sampling (safety guarantee).  
- Presents an O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution with minimal overhead, suitable when d >> n.  
- Strong empirical results on real datasets show significant wall-clock speedups, especially for coordinate descent.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and supports claims with theory and extensive experiments.  
- Main limitation: safe gradient bounds are demonstrated primarily for Generalized Linear Models; effectiveness depends on tightness of bounds for more complex models.  
- Experimental gains for SGD are less pronounced than for CD; a brief discussion of this discrepancy would strengthen the paper.
