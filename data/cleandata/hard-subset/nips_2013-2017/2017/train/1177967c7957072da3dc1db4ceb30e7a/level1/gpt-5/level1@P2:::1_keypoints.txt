- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that solves a convex minimax problem over safe bounds to approximate full-information gradient sampling.  
- Resulting distribution p̂k is worst-case optimal over available bounds, provably dominates Li-based fixed sampling, and is computable in O(n log n) via a sorting/threshold algorithm.  
- Stepsize is tied to the minimax value (α̂k = 1/vk) and the method recovers full-information sampling when bounds are tight.  
- Extends prior work on fixed importance sampling, adaptive probabilities, gradient-norm sampling, and screening rules; novelty is the safe minimax formulation and efficient solver.  
- Strength: strong theoretical motivation showing explicit expected-progress improvements and potential n-fold speedups in the full-information case.  
- Strength: novel minimax formulation with best worst-case guarantee, dominance over Li-sampling, elegant geometric characterization, and efficient solver.  
- Strength: practical guidance for maintaining safe bounds in GLMs with simple updates and analyzed per-iteration cost.  
- Strength: empirical gains are large for CD (iterations and time); even loose bounds provide benefit and overhead appears manageable in tested settings.  
- Strength: principled stepsize-sampling coupling improves over conservative fixed stepsizes.  
- Weakness: per-iteration O(n log n) cost and O(n) bound maintenance may be nontrivial in high dimensions and for problems with cheap sparse coordinate updates.  
- Weakness: Cauchy–Schwarz-based safe bounds can be very loose; more study on bound-tightness effects and extensions beyond GLMs is needed.  
- Weakness: limited empirical gains for SGD; lacks deeper analysis of when adaptive sampling helps SGD (variance/heterogeneity conditions).  
- Weakness: related-work discussion and empirical comparisons should better cover dual-residue adaptive CD methods and approximate Gauss–Southwell rules.  
- Weakness: some key theoretical results and explicit convergence rates are deferred to the appendix rather than summarized in the main text.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations, minimax justification, and solver; competitive-ratio bounds are conservative but appropriate for worst-case analysis.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with intuitive explanations and helpful pseudocode; clarify roles of n vs d earlier and fix minor typos.  
- Originality: safe minimax construction and efficient solution are novel and distinct from prior adaptive sampling/screening approaches.  
- Significance: significant contribution for CD with broad applicability; more modest impact for SGD in tested setups but promising for extensions (distributed/asynchronous).  
- Suggestions: explore amortizing the O(n log n) step (heaps/partial resort), scale experiments to very large n, test sensitivity to sparsity and tighter screening bounds, provide ablations on p̂k cost vs epoch reduction, and compare to dual-residue/GS baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong theory, practical algorithm, fills gap for safe adaptive sampling without full gradients, solid CD experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: scalability/overhead analysis incomplete, limited SGD advantage, missing comparisons to some adaptive CD baselines, some proofs relegated to appendix.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
