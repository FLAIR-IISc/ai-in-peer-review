- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using gradient bounds.
- Core idea: approximate gradient-based sampling via safe upper/lower bounds to retain theoretical guarantees while being efficient.
- Shows the scheme is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and can be integrated with negligible overhead.
- Empirical results on real datasets validate theoretical improvements.
- Builds on prior adaptive importance sampling work and addresses computational challenges of gradient-based sampling.
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous analysis and provable guarantees are a major strength.
- Computational efficiency: claimed minimal overhead and practical implementability.
- Generality: applicable to both coordinate descent and SGD, increasing relevance.
- Clear presentation: method and theory are well-explained and well-cited.
- Safe approximation: ensures the method won’t perform worse than fixed sampling.
- Reliance on availability of safe gradient bounds may limit applicability beyond GLMs; more discussion needed.
- Algorithm 4’s O(n log n) claim needs more implementation details and clarity.
- SGD gains are modest compared to CD; deeper analysis of this gap is warranted.
- Needs more explicit comparison to very recent adaptive-sampling work and minor notation consistency fixes.
- Overall: valuable, theoretically solid, empirically supported contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
