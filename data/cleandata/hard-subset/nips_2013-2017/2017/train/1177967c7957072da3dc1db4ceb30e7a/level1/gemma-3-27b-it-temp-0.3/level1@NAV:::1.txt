## Review of "Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling"

This paper addresses the challenge of efficiently implementing adaptive importance sampling (AIS) for large-scale optimization, specifically within coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) frameworks. The core idea is to approximate gradient-based sampling using safe bounds on the gradient, ensuring theoretical guarantees while maintaining computational feasibility. The authors demonstrate that their proposed scheme is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and can be integrated into existing algorithms with minimal overhead.

The paper is well-motivated, building upon the established benefits of AIS while acknowledging its practical limitations due to full gradient computation. It clearly positions itself within the existing literature on importance sampling, referencing key works in the field (e.g., Nesterov, Richtárik, Zhao & Zhang). The theoretical contributions, particularly the analysis of the proposed sampling scheme and its comparison to optimal sampling, are strong. The authors convincingly argue for the advantages of their approach, providing bounds on its performance and demonstrating its optimality within the constraints of available information. 

A significant strength is the practical focus. The authors not only present a theoretical framework but also detail an efficient algorithm for computing the proposed sampling distribution and demonstrate its applicability to GLMs with clear instructions on how to obtain safe gradient bounds. The extensive numerical evaluation on several datasets with different loss functions and regularization techniques further strengthens the paper, showcasing the practical benefits of the proposed method. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the assumptions required for the safe gradient bounds. While the GLM example is illustrative, a broader exploration of scenarios where these bounds can be efficiently obtained would be valuable. Additionally, the performance gains in SGD appear more modest than those observed in CD. A deeper analysis of the reasons for this difference could provide further insights.  The writing is generally clear, but some sections (particularly those with heavy notation) could be made more accessible.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical foundation:** The paper provides rigorous analysis and guarantees for the proposed sampling scheme.
*   **Practical relevance:** The method is computationally efficient and can be readily integrated into existing algorithms.
*   **Empirical validation:** Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on real-world datasets.
*   **Novelty:** The proposed safe sampling scheme offers a practical solution to a challenging problem in large-scale optimization.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the applicability of safe gradient bounds beyond the GLM example.
*   Modest performance gains observed in the SGD experiments.
*   Some sections could benefit from improved clarity and accessibility.

**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. It presents a valuable contribution to the field of large-scale optimization, offering a practical and theoretically sound approach to adaptive importance sampling. The strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses, and the work is likely to be of interest to a broad audience within the NIPS community.
