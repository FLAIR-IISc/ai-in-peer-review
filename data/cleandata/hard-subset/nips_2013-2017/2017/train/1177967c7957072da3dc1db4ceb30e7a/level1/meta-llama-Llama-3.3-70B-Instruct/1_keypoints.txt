- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD using safe gradient lower/upper bounds.
- Casts picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem to avoid infeasible gradient computation.
- Main contributions: a generic adaptive sampling strategy, a proof it outperforms uniform/fixed importance sampling, and an efficient algorithm to compute the solution.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, including proofs of optimality and efficiency.
- Experimental results demonstrate the proposed scheme’s practical effectiveness.
- Assumes safe lower and upper bounds on gradients can be efficiently computed, which may not always hold.
- Computational cost is O(n log n) per iteration, potentially a bottleneck for very large datasets.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient method, strong theory, and supportive experiments addressing an important ML problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on efficiently computable gradient bounds and potentially prohibitive computational cost.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and discuss computational cost and gradient-bound assumptions.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
