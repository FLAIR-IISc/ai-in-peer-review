- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds and convex optimization to pick sampling distributions.  
- Argues optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically good but computationally infeasible, motivating the safe-bounds approach.  
- Method is novel and efficiently computes adaptive sampling distributions that are provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- The approach can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms, making it practical for large-scale optimization.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including properties of the method and competitive ratio guarantees.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and presents the problem, method, and related work concisely.  
- Main weakness: assumes safe lower and upper gradient bounds can be computed efficiently, which may not hold in practice.  
- Main weakness: lacks a detailed empirical comparison with other adaptive importance sampling methods.  
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and optimization problems, needing broader validation.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and a valuable addition to large-scale optimization with potential to improve CD and SGD.  
- Quality score: 8/10.  
- Clarity score: 9/10.  
- Originality score: 8/10, noting the safe-bounds idea builds on prior work.  
- Significance score: 8/10, impact may be limited by the computable-bounds assumption.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel efficient computation, easy integration, and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: reliance on computable safe bounds, missing comparisons to other adaptive methods, and limited experiments.
