This paper introduces a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) that uses per-iteration lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to compute, via a convex min–max formulation, the best sampling distribution consistent with the available information. The resulting distribution p̂ is proved (i) optimal in the worst case over the bound set, (ii) uniformly better than uniform and Li-based fixed importance sampling, and (iii) computable in O(n log n) time using a sorting-based procedure. The authors further show that with full information the method recovers gradient-based sampling, and they propose an adaptive stepsize α = 1/vk tied to the min–max value. For GLMs, they show how to maintain safe bounds cheaply using Cauchy–Schwarz updates. Experiments on rcv1, real-sim, and news20 demonstrate substantial speedups for CD (smaller for SGD). The work connects to importance sampling and non-uniform CD (Richtárik & Takáč; Qu et al.), gradient-based sampling in SGD (Zhao & Zhang; Zhu), gap-safe and screening ideas (Ndiaye et al.; Liu et al.), Gauss–Southwell/steepest CD (Nutini et al.), and adaptive SDCA/Ada sampling (Csiba et al.; Perekrestenko et al.). The min–max justification leverages classical minimax results (Sion; Komiya).

Quality: The technical development is sound and carefully argued. The min–max formulation, its geometric interpretation, and the O(n log n) algorithm are convincing. Proof obligations (e.g., exchanging min/max) are addressed. Assumptions on safe bounds are explicit and realizable for GLMs, though tightness may vary; the competitive-ratio analysis clarifies robustness.

Clarity: Generally clear and well structured, with concrete algorithms and helpful intuition. A few typos remain and some proofs relegated to the appendix could benefit from brief main-text sketches.

Originality: The safe-bounds min–max formulation yielding a distribution provably better than fixed sampling, and an efficient solver, appears novel relative to prior heuristic/stale/upper-bound adaptive schemes.

Significance: For large-scale CD this is practically relevant and likely to be adopted; the consistent gains and small overhead are compelling. Impact on SGD seems modest in the reported settings; broader evidence (e.g., deep models or variance-reduced methods) would strengthen significance.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and principled adaptive sampling with strong, informative guarantees.
- Efficient O(n log n) algorithm; easy to integrate in CD/SGD.
- Clear empirical gains for CD; careful analysis including competitive ratios.
- Bridges gap between full gradient-based sampling and practical constraints.

Cons against acceptance:
- Empirical scope limited: modest SGD gains; no comparison to Gauss–Southwell variants, AdaSDCA, or gap-safe sampling baselines in experiments.
- Dependence on quality of safe bounds; limited ablation on bound tightness and overhead.
- Some assumptions most natural for GLMs; less clear for complex non-linear models.
