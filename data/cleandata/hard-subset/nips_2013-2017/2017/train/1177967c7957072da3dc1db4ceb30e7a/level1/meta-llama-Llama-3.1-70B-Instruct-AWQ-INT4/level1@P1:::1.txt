This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified, but computationally infeasible. To address this, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient and formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, the proposed method, and the related work. The technical contributions are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its properties and the competitive ratio.

The main strengths of the paper are:

1. The authors provide a novel and efficient way to compute the adaptive sampling distribution, which is provably better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling.
2. The proposed method can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms, making it a practical solution for large-scale optimization problems.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its properties and the competitive ratio, which helps to understand the behavior of the algorithm.

The main weaknesses of the paper are:

1. The paper assumes that safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other adaptive importance sampling methods, which would help to understand the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed method.
3. The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and optimization problems, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive experiments to validate the proposed method.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the technical contributions are sound. The proposed method is a valuable addition to the field of large-scale optimization, and it has the potential to improve the performance of CD and SGD algorithms.
