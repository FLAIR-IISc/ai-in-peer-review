- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD that uses safe upper/lower gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling without full gradient computation.  
- Formulates the optimal sampling problem as a convex optimization over safe bounds and provides an efficient O(n log n) solver.  
- Shows the proposed sampling is provably better than fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2), unlike prior safe-bound approaches.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, with clear proofs and derived computational complexity per iteration.  
- Method is practical and easy to implement; demonstrates efficient safe gradient bounds computation for GLMs with negligible overhead.  
- Experimental results show significant speed-ups for coordinate descent (up to factor n in theory) and practical improvements even with loose bounds and no gradient initialization.  
- Improvement for SGD is modest compared to CD, and the paper does not sufficiently explain this difference.  
- Competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5–3.6) is somewhat abstract and doesn’t concretely link safe-bound quality to empirical gains.  
- Paper does not clearly differentiate from existing "safe screening" techniques, which use safe bounds in optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, practical benefits on real datasets, easy integration into existing algorithms, and minimal implementation overhead.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical gains for SGD, insufficient explanation of CD vs SGD performance, and no treatment of non-convex objectives.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper with provable and practical contributions primarily for CD; recommended for acceptance despite limited SGD impact.
