**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified and can lead to significant improvements over static strategies. However, computing the optimal adaptive sampling distribution is often infeasible. To address this, the authors propose a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme that relies on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient. They formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to compute the solution.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a clear and well-motivated introduction to the problem of adaptive importance sampling in CD and SGD algorithms.
2. The authors propose a novel and efficient algorithm for computing the safe adaptive importance sampling distribution.
3. The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including bounds on the expected progress and competitive ratios.
4. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm through extensive numerical experiments on real datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art adaptive importance sampling methods.
3. The paper focuses primarily on CD and SGD algorithms, and it is unclear whether the proposed method can be extended to other optimization algorithms.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-motivated presentation of their ideas. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the numerical experiments are well-designed and convincing.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas. The notation is well-defined, and the figures and tables are helpful in illustrating the key concepts.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme that relies on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient. While the idea of using bounds on the gradient is not new, the authors' formulation of the problem as a convex optimization problem and their efficient algorithm for computing the solution are original contributions.

**Significance**

The paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, and the proposed method has the potential to significantly improve the performance of CD and SGD algorithms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm through extensive numerical experiments, which suggests that the method can be widely applicable.
