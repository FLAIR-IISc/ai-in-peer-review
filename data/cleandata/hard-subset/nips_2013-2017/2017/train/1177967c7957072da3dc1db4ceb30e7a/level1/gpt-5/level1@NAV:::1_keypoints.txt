- Introduces a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration gradient magnitude bounds and a convex min–max to compute the best feasible sampling distribution.  
- Proves the computed distribution p̂ is worst-case optimal over the bound set, uniformly better than uniform and Li-based fixed importance sampling, and computable in O(n log n) via a sorting-based algorithm.  
- Shows the method recovers gradient-based sampling with full information and proposes an adaptive stepsize α = 1/vk tied to the min–max value.  
- Provides a cheap technique for maintaining safe bounds in GLMs using Cauchy–Schwarz updates.  
- Empirical results on rcv1, real-sim, and news20 show substantial speedups for CD and smaller gains for SGD.  
- Technical development is sound, with convincing min–max justification, geometric interpretation, and addressed proof obligations.  
- Presentation is generally clear with concrete algorithms and intuition, though a few typos remain and some appendix proofs could use brief main-text sketches.  
- Novelty: the safe-bounds min–max formulation and efficient solver appear new relative to prior heuristic/stale/adaptive schemes.  
- Significance: practically relevant and likely adoptable for large-scale CD; reported impact on SGD is modest and would benefit from broader evidence (e.g., deep models, variance-reduced methods).  
- Pros: principled adaptive sampling with strong guarantees, efficient O(n log n) algorithm, clear CD empirical gains, and useful conceptual bridge to full gradient sampling.  
- Cons: limited empirical comparisons (no Gauss–Southwell, AdaSDCA, or gap-safe baselines), dependence on bound quality with limited ablation, and assumptions most natural for GLMs so applicability to complex non-linear models is less clear.
