- Paper proposes a “safe” adaptive sampling for CD/SGD using efficiently maintainable gradient bounds and a convex min–max to choose sampling p that is worst-case optimal over the bounds.
- Method yields an O(n log n) algorithm to compute p, is provably at least as good as Li-based or uniform sampling, and integrates an adaptive stepsize αk for unbiased updates.
- Theoretical contributions include optimality of p given the bounds (Thm. 3.2), competitive-ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5–3.6), and constructive O(n log n) algorithm (Thm. 3.4).
- CD-specific analysis with αk shows potentially large speedups (up to factor n) over fixed sampling in favorable cases.
- Practical bound-maintenance strategies for GLMs are provided (Section 4) with O(n) updates per iteration and modest overhead when d = Ω(n).
- Empirical results show the method nearly matches infeasible gradient-based sampling for CD and outperforms uniform/fixed sampling on several text datasets, even with loose bounds.
- Relates to prior work on non-uniform/adaptive sampling, gradient-based sampling, gap-based CD/SDCA, and Gauss–Southwell rules, but fills a gap by providing worst-case guarantees under interval uncertainty.
- Strength: clear, principled optimization formulation capturing best distribution under uncertainty.
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees and insightful competitive-ratio discussion.
- Strength: elegant algorithmic solution with practical runtime and stepsize coupling.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to strong adaptive CD baselines (duality-gap sampling, Gauss–Southwell variants) and to non-uniform variance-reduced SGD methods.
- Weakness: empirical gains for SGD are modest; conditions where the method helps SGD need clearer discussion and study.
- Weakness: dependence on bound tightness; more sensitivity analysis on how bound quality affects vk and runtime is needed.
- Weakness: per-iteration O(n log n) cost and memory for bounds may be non-trivial when d ≪ n; incremental updates or sparsity exploitation are not explored.
- Weakness: guidance on setting/estimating coordinate-wise Li and Hessian bounds outside simple GLMs is limited; assumptions should be made explicit.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—timing experiments use different machines and datasets are limited to text; denser data and standardized timing would help.
- Quality: theoretical development appears sound and proofs/derivations are convincing.
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized but should collect assumptions early, clarify Li choices, and fix minor typos.
- Originality: the safe min–max formulation and O(n log n) constructive solution for sampling under interval uncertainty are novel.
- Significance: practically relevant and likely adoptable for CD; less clear impact for SGD under current experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, efficient adaptive sampling with worst-case guarantees that improves over fixed sampling and is easy to integrate.
- Cons against acceptance: missing key baseline comparisons, modest SGD benefits, and potential overhead in some regimes.
- Suggested improvements: add strong adaptive baselines, ablation on bound tightness vs. performance, report exact per-iteration overhead, explore incremental p̂k updates, broaden experiments, and clarify practical estimation of Li/Hessian bounds.
