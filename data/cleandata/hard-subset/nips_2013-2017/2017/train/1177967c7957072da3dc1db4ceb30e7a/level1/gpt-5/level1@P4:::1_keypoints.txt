- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD that uses safe lower/upper bounds on per-coordinate/component gradient magnitudes to compute a worst-case-optimal sampling p̂k and stepsize α̂k = 1/vk.
- Guarantees p̂k is worst-case optimal given the bounds, dominates fixed Li-based (including uniform) sampling in the same worst-case sense, and matches full-information sampling when bounds are tight.
- Key technical steps: one-step expected progress for unbiased CD updates; full-information optimal p ∝ √Li|∇if|; min-max formulation over a box of bounds reducing to max ||√L c||1/||c||2; closed-form thresholding worst-case c and O(n log n) Algorithm 4 to compute p̂k.
- Shows how to maintain safe gradient bounds cheaply for GLMs via inner-product updates (Cauchy–Schwarz), and reports strong empirical gains for CD and modest gains for SGD.

- Positions work relative to prior fixed nonuniform CD sampling, adaptive sampling by duality gaps, and gradient-norm-based SGD sampling; claims novelty in the principled min-max safe formulation with worst-case dominance and an efficient solver.

- Strength: clear optimization-based formulation and elegant reduction to a tractable worst-case problem.
- Strength: provable properties—best worst-case guarantee given bounds, dominance over fixed Li sampling, and recovery of full-information optimality with tight bounds.
- Strength: practical O(n log n) algorithm with implementable bound maintenance for GLMs and empirical CD speedups; overhead can be amortized.
- Strength: experimental evidence supports claims for CD; honest reporting of modest SGD gains.

- Weakness: computational overhead—O(n log n) for p̂k and O(n) bound updates may be prohibitive when n ≫ d; amortization and recomputation frequency need clarification.
- Weakness: guarantees are worst-case relative to bounds and may be loose when bounds are weak; need ablation on bound tightness vs performance.
- Weakness: limited empirical baselines—missing comparisons to Gauss–Southwell/steepest CD, adaptive duality-gap methods, and stale/heuristic gradient sampling.
- Weakness: SGD improvements are minor; lacks guidance on when the method benefits SGD.

- Theoretical derivations are sound and the minimax/thresholding reduction is elegant; one-step CD analysis is standard and correctly applied.
- Experimental support is strong for CD but mixed for SGD; complexity claims need more precise per-epoch/amortized quantification.

- Generally well-written and organized though notation is heavy; motivate the γk = αk/pik parameterization more explicitly.
- Practical bound-maintenance section is helpful; add pseudocode for SGD and clarify p̂k computation frequency to improve reproducibility.
- Minor typos present; recommend a proofreading pass.

- Original contribution: novel safe min-max formulation that guarantees dominance over fixed sampling and an efficient solver; full-information optimality is known but the limited-information safe approximation is new.

- Significance: likely impactful for CD on GLMs and problems with cheap bounds and heterogeneous gradients due to worst-case safety guarantee.
- Significance for SGD is limited unless tighter bounds or high gradient heterogeneity make the method more beneficial.

- Suggestion: provide detailed complexity analysis including recomputation frequency, warm-starting/sorting strategies, and amortized per-epoch costs for n ≫ d.
- Suggestion: add empirical comparisons to Gauss–Southwell and duality-gap adaptive CD methods and to stale/heuristic gradient sampling.
- Suggestion: include ablation studies on bound tightness, update frequency, and their effect on performance.
- Suggestion: discuss memory and implementation details for maintaining bounds in large-scale sparse settings.

- Arguments for acceptance: principled adaptive sampling under limited information with worst-case guarantees, efficient algorithm, and strong CD empirical gains aligning theory and practice.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential high per-iteration overhead in common n ≫ d regimes without amortization details, limited empirical comparisons to other adaptive CD baselines, and modest benefits for SGD as reported.
