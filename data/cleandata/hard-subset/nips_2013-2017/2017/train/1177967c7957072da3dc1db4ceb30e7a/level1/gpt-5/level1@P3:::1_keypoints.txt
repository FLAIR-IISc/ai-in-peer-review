- Problem: Adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD when full gradient information is unavailable.
- Approach: Formulate sampling as a minimax problem over a box of per-iteration lower/upper bounds on gradient components.
- Safe distribution: Derive a “safe” sampling p̂ that is optimal for the uncertainty set and computable efficiently.
- Theoretical guarantees: p̂ is always at least as good as fixed Li-based sampling and recovers full-information gradient sampling as bounds tighten.
- Objective function: Minimize worst-case ratio V(p,c)/||c||^2 with V(p,c)=∑i Li c_i^2 / p_i over c in the box C.
- Algorithm: Provide an O(n log n) thresholding/clamping algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute p̂ via a water-filling-like scheme.
- Stepsize choice: Use αk = 1/vk with γk = αk/pk[i] to obtain unbiased directional updates and safe expected decrease bounds.
- One-step improvement: For CD, expected one-step decrease improves over fixed sampling and can be up to an n-fold gain in equal-L cases.
- GLM bounds: Show practical, cheap safe bound constructions for GLMs (e.g., via Cauchy–Schwarz) and analogous SGD constructions.
- Experiments (CD): Substantial empirical gains for CD versus uniform/fixed importance sampling across GLM losses and penalties.
- Experiments (SGD): Only modest empirical gains for SGD, consistent with prior work.
- Theoretical soundness: Minimax formulation is convex/concave on the box, Sion/Komiya cited for min–max interchange, proofs in appendix.
- Practicality concern: Per-iteration overhead O(n) for bound maintenance and O(n log n) for computing p̂ may be prohibitive in very sparse, high-dimensional settings.
- Overhead justification: Paper argues overhead is negligible when d = Ω(n), but broader empirical evidence across sparsity regimes is lacking.
- Clarity issues: Paper is generally well-written but could clarify Algorithm 4 variable names, provide intuition for “directionally-unbiased” updates, and move a convergence theorem to the main text.
- Originality: The robust/minimax formulation and exact O(n log n) solution are novel relative to prior adaptive sampling methods.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for large-scale CD on GLMs; limited broader impact for SGD given small gains and added complexity.
- Suggested improvements: Add main-text convex/strongly-convex convergence rates, analyze amortized/periodic recomputation of p̂, and extend experiments to highly sparse regimes and varied preprocessing.
- Additional suggestions: Include ablations on recomputation frequency, tasks with highly varying gradient norms for SGD, and a brief main-text proof sketch for Theorem 3.4.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, original contribution with promising CD results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and computational-overhead concerns.
