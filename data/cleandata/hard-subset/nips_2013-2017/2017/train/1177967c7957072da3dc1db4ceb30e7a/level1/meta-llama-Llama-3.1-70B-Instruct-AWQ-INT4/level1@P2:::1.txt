**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified, but often computationally infeasible. To address this, they develop an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling using safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient. The proposed sampling distribution is provably better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed in many applications at negligible extra cost.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors build upon previous work on adaptive importance sampling, including gradient-based sampling and duality gap-based sampling for CD. They also draw inspiration from recent advances in stochastic optimization, such as variance reduction and non-uniform sampling. The paper's focus on safe bounds and efficient computation sets it apart from previous approaches.
