- Addresses efficient, scalable adaptive importance sampling (AIS) by approximating gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.  
- Proposes a novel, provably safe sampling scheme that is at least as good as uniform sampling and often better than fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides theoretical analysis (e.g., Lemma 2.1, Theorem 3.2) linking the method to steepest coordinate descent and existing importance sampling literature.  
- Shows efficient computation of the sampling distribution (Theorem 3.4) and easy integration into coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Includes practical algorithmic details (Algorithm 4) and a procedure for obtaining safe gradient bounds (Section 4).  
- Empirical validation on multiple datasets demonstrates consistent improvements over baseline methods, with analysis of vk for bound tightness.  
- Well-motivated and original contribution, appropriately situated within prior work.  
- Strengths: theoretical rigor, practical efficiency, and strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: dense presentation with heavy mathematical notation that may hinder accessibility.  
- Weakness: gradient-bound examples focus on GLMs; extending to other model classes (e.g., neural networks) would broaden impact.  
- Weakness: performance gains in SGD are modest compared to CD and need further investigation.  
- Weakness: figures could use clearer labeling and improved visual presentation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address noted points.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, provably effective, efficient, and empirically validated.  
- Rejection arguments: dense exposition, limited SGD gains, and figure presentation issues.
