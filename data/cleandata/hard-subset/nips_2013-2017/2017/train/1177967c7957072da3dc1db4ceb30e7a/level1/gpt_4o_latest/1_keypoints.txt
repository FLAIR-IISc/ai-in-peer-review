- Introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe gradient bounds to reduce computational cost of gradient-based sampling.
- Proposes a safe approximation using upper and lower gradient bounds that guarantees provable improvements over uniform and fixed importance sampling.
- Achieves computational efficiency with O(n log n) overhead per iteration and integrates into existing CD and SGD algorithms.
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees, showing the method is always better than fixed importance sampling and can approach optimal gradient-based sampling; derives competitive ratio bounds.
- Demonstrates how safe gradient bounds can be efficiently maintained, especially for generalized linear models (GLMs).
- Empirical results on real-world datasets show significant convergence speed-ups and robustness even with loose gradient bounds.
- Strength: Theoretical rigor with provable performance improvements and competitive-ratio analysis.
- Strength: Low computational overhead makes the method practical for large-scale applications.
- Strength: General applicability to both coordinate descent and stochastic gradient descent frameworks.
- Strength: Extensive empirical validation supports theoretical claims and shows practical speed-ups.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is largely limited to GLMs and does not explore complex models like deep neural networks or non-convex objectives.
- Weakness: Less pronounced performance gains for SGD compared to CD, with limited analysis explaining this gap.
- Weakness: Reliance on availability and maintenance of safe gradient bounds, which may be difficult for complex models.
- Weakness: Some mathematical derivations are dense and may be challenging for non-expert readers.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important optimization problem with a practical, theoretically sound, and computationally efficient solution backed by experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical scope and dependency on gradient bounds reduce generality and applicability to more complex or non-convex settings.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve presentation clarity and to better discuss limitations and broader applicability.
