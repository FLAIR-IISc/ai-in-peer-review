- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme to speed up coordinate descent (CD) and SGD using safe upper/lower bounds on gradients.  
- Approximates gradient-based sampling with bounds to ensure theoretical guarantees and computational efficiency.  
- Method is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and integrates into existing algorithms with minimal overhead.  
- Clear, well-structured paper with effective motivation and limitations of prior adaptive sampling methods.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: analysis of CD with adaptive sampling and derivation of the safe sampling scheme.  
- Practical algorithm: computationally efficient with well-analyzed complexity and easy integration.  
- Clear presentation: readable exposition, consistent notation, and informative figures.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments across datasets and loss functions that support the theory.  
- Novelty: framing sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization given gradient bounds is novel.  
- Weakness: reliance on safe gradient bounds (l, u) may limit applicability to complex or non-convex models.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the tightness of gradient bounds is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Weakness: SGD results show smaller improvements than CD without full explanation.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation scope is limited—needs more tasks, models, regularization and distribution variants.  
- Weakness: competitive ratio analysis depends on restrictive gradient-range assumptions and needs relaxation or practical guidance.  
- Weakness: discussion of computational cost (notably O(n log n) sampling) needs a more precise breakdown.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, theoretically sound, practical to implement, strong empirical evidence, and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on gradient bounds, weaker SGD results, and limited empirical breadth.  
- Overall: significant contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: elaborate on obtaining safe gradient bounds for complex models; investigate SGD shortcomings; expand empirical evaluation; provide detailed computational cost breakdown; clarify/relax competitive ratio assumptions.
