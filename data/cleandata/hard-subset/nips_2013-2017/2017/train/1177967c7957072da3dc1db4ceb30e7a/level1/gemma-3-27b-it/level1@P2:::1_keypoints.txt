- Core idea: Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme that approximates gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds to speed up CD and SGD.  
- Motivation: Importance sampling is valuable for large-scale optimization but adaptive variants are often computationally infeasible; the paper targets this gap.  
- Related work: Appropriately connects to prior work on importance sampling, coordinate descent, and stochastic gradient methods.  
- Theoretical soundness: Lemma 2.1 and the derivation of the optimal sampling distribution appear rigorous and persuasive.  
- Algorithm/theory presentation: Algorithm 2 and Theorem 3.2 are clearly presented and justify the proposed sampling properties.  
- Computational complexity: Discussion of complexity and links to gradient evaluation cost is well-justified.  
- Appendix reliance: Full technical assessment depends on proofs deferred to the appendix, which were not fully reviewed.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments support the theoretical claims and show performance gains on several datasets.  
- Clarity and writing: Paper is generally well-written and organized with standard notation, but some derivations are mathematically dense.  
- Need for intuition: More intuitive explanations of the optimization formulation in Section 3 would help readers.  
- Originality: The safe-bounds approximation and the convex optimization formulation for best sampling under limited information are novel.  
- Relation to prior methods: Properly acknowledges connections to duality gaps and existing gradient-based sampling approaches.  
- Significance: Potentially high impact if the method reliably speeds up CD and SGD with minimal overhead and easy integration.  
- Practical advantage: Ability to work with safe bounds rather than full gradients is especially important for real-world applications.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong theoretical foundation, computational efficiency, practical relevance, novelty, and empirical support favor acceptance.  
- Rejection concerns: Mathematical density may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Rejection concerns: Reliance on appendix proofs means technical soundness wasn’t exhaustively verified within review time.  
- Rejection concerns: SGD improvements are modest compared to CD, raising questions about broad applicability.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add more intuition for Section 3 and a clearer discussion of limitations, especially SGD performance.
