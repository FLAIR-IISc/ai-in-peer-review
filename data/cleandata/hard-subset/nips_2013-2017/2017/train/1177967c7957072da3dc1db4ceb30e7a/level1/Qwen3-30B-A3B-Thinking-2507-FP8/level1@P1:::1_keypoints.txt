- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" for CD and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate optimal but infeasible gradient-based sampling.
- Shows the sampling is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and recovers optimal sampling in full-information settings.
- Algorithm can be computed efficiently in O(n log n) time per iteration, often negligible compared to gradient computation.
- Formulates finding the best sampling distribution as a convex optimization over safe bounds, avoiding ad hoc approximations.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with clear proofs (Theorem 3.2) and strong competitive ratio bounds (Lemmas 3.5, 3.6).
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive on real datasets (rcv1, real-sim, news20) and multiple loss functions with L1/L2 regularization.
- Results show consistent and substantial speed-ups (often 2–5x) over fixed and uniform sampling.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation, clear explanations, and complete proofs in the appendices.
- The "safe" approach is an original and significant advance over prior adaptive sampling methods that lacked guarantees or were expensive.
- Method works well even with very loose safe gradient bounds, increasing applicability.
- Highly significant for large-scale ML by addressing a fundamental computational bottleneck in adaptive sampling.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees combined with a practical, low-overhead algorithm.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical validation across datasets and loss functions.
- Strength: clear exposition and well-organized presentation enabling replication.
- Weakness: empirical validation focuses on GLMs, limiting demonstrated generality.
- Weakness: paper does not fully explain why improvements are less dramatic for SGD versus CD.
- Weakness: the safe-bounds assumption is reasonable for GLMs but may not hold for all problem classes.
- Pro acceptance: first theoretically grounded adaptive sampling provably better than uniform with minimal overhead and strong empirical support.
- Con acceptance: limited validation beyond GLMs, incomplete SGD vs CD explanation, and potential applicability limits of safe bounds.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, significant contribution, reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
