- Paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that guarantees performance better than uniform or fixed sampling.
- Main contribution: a "safe" sampling strategy using gradient bounds to compute a provably superior sampling distribution.
- Formulates optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem solvable efficiently with given bounds.
- Integrates into CD and SGD with minimal overhead (O(n log n) per iteration, comparable to one gradient component).
- Theorem 3.2 provides theoretical guarantees that the adaptive distribution always improves over fixed importance sampling.
- Empirical validation across L1/L2-regularized GLMs (square, hinge, logistic losses) shows faster convergence in iterations and time.
- Adaptive stepsize behavior (vk) shown; even loose gradient bounds yield significant improvements.
- Strong theoretical development with clear proofs and principled use of bounds (advances prior work).
- Method is generic and applicable to multiple optimization algorithms.
- Practical implementation details and computational complexity are analyzed.
- Comprehensive experiments on real-world datasets support the claims.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and notation is consistent, but figures in Section 5 are hard to read.
- Originality: novel safe approach distinguishing it from prior, less-principled bound usage.
- Significance: practical, low-overhead method that yields substantial speedups for large-scale optimization.
- Weakness: unclear why SGD gains are less pronounced than CD; deeper analysis is missing.
- Weakness: figures in Section 5 need higher resolution/legibility.
- Weakness: could include more examples of deriving safe bounds for other problem classes beyond GLMs.
- Recommendation: technically sound and empirically strong; reviewer recommends acceptance after fixing figure readability.
