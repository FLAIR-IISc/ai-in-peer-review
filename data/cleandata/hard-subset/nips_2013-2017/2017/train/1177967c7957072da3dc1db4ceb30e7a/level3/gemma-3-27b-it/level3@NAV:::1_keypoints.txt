- Proposes a novel, efficient adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using gradient lower/upper bounds and solving an optimal convex sampling problem with negligible overhead.  
- Positions the method as a practical bridge between theoretical gradient-based sampling and computational feasibility.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis proving optimality relative to the provided bounds and potential speed-ups, especially for CD.  
- Demonstrates generic applicability to both CD and SGD.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes clear algorithms and explanations.  
- Extensive numerical experiments on real datasets empirically support the theoretical claims.  
- Example 3.1 is initially confusing and requires careful reading.  
- Algorithm 4 has issues: line 4’s condition (m = max(l^{sort})) may never be satisfied, and line 7 likely contains a typo (should be u^{sort} not c^{sort}).  
- Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2, 5, 21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- SGD experiments omit timing results, preventing assessment of computational cost.  
- Minor reference errors (incorrect title for [14]) and missing citation (Csiba & Richtarik, 2016).  
- Recommendation: accept after rebuttal addressing comparisons, timing, algorithm bugs/typos, and reference fixes.
