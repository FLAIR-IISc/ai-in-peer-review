- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that is generic and easily integrated with existing algorithms.  
- Addresses limitations of prior adaptive sampling that require full-gradient computation by using efficient safe gradient bounds.  
- Shows the proposed approximation is provably optimal w.r.t. the given bounds and always outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides a method that can be computed efficiently in many applications at negligible extra cost.  
- Applies the sampling scheme to CD and SGD and demonstrates efficiency through extensive numerical experiments.  
- Offers geometric interpretation and competitive ratio analysis of the optimization problem and solution properties.  
- Experimental results on generalized linear models (L1 and L2 regularization) show significant iteration- and time-wise improvements over fixed importance sampling.  
- Demonstrates that the computational overhead is small enough to yield lower total computation time than fixed importance sampling.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient detail for expert reproduction.  
- Theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation are thorough and support the claims.  
- Original combination of familiar techniques yields a novel solution that advances the state of the art.  
- Results are significant and likely to be used or built upon by practitioners and researchers.  
- Weakness: Figures in Section 5 are hard to read and should be enlarged for better readability.  
- Weakness: Some references are outdated (many from 2015 or earlier) and could be updated.
