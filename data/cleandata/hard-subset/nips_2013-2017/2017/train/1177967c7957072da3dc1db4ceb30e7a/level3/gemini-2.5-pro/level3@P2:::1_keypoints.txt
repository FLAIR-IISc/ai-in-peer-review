- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using maintained lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes/norms.  
- Formulates sampling as a minimax problem that minimizes worst-case variance over gradient vectors in the bounds.  
- Derives a provably optimal safe sampling distribution that outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling and is computable in O(n log n).  
- Shows how to maintain required gradient bounds for GLMs and reports empirical speedups over fixed strategies.  

- Strength: Novel minimax formulation provides a principled, non-heuristic approach to adaptive sampling.  
- Strength: Theoretical analysis is solid and supports claims; Algorithm 4 for computing the distribution is a valuable contribution.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly motivated, with coherent exposition from full-information sampler to safe approximation.  

- Major concern: Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art adaptive importance sampling methods (e.g., duality-gap-based methods).  
- Major concern: Possible logical flaw in Algorithm 4 initialization/loop (m = max(l_sort) and condition if [l_sort]_l > m appears impossible); needs clarification/correction.  

- Minor: Example 3.1’s explanation is confusing and should more clearly contrast robustness of p_hat vs. naive l/u-based distributions.  
- Minor: SGD experiments (Figures 7-8) lack timing results to assess computational overhead vs. convergence speedups.  
- Minor: Typo in Algorithm 4 line 7: [c_sort]_u likely should be [usort]_u.  
- Minor: Missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik (2016) and incorrect title for reference [14] in bibliography.  

- Conclusion: Strong, novel, and technically sound paper and a good candidate for acceptance, contingent on addressing the Algorithm 4 issue and expanding experimental comparisons.
