- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using per-iteration lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes via a convex min–max formulation.  
- Sampling probabilities are provably optimal relative to the provided bounds, strictly improve over uniform/fixed importance sampling, and recover exact gradient-based probabilities with full information.  
- Connects to prior fixed importance sampling, adaptive gradient-based sampling, and duality-gap based sampling, and adds a safety guarantee that sampling is never worse than fixed baselines.  
- Presents an efficient O(n log n) solver and practical integration into CD/SGD.  
- Theoretical development is largely sound and insightful, particularly the convex reformulation and worst-case optimality under bounds.  
- Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in α*, which propagates to the bound and needs correction.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing: maximizing in (7) seems to encourage setting c to bounds, which may contradict the claim that naive bound setting is suboptimal—requires clarification.  
- Algorithm 4 has logic issues: line 4 loop condition may never trigger if m = max(l^{sort}); line 7 likely should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}; algorithmic details need review.  
- Experiments show convincing CD gains with negligible overhead, but SGD experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive methods ([2],[5],[21]) and non-uniform fixed importance sampling, plus timing results rather than only iteration counts.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized, but contains minor typos and ambiguities that should be fixed.  
- Reference issues: [14] has an incorrect title; add Csiba & Richtárik “Importance Sampling for Minibatches” (2016, arXiv).  
- Originality: the safe min–max formulation and efficient solver are novel and well-motivated.  
- Significance: method is generic, easy to integrate, low-overhead, and its guarantee to always beat uniform/fixed sampling is valuable; stronger SGD evidence would increase impact.  
- Pros: strong theory and worst-case optimality, novel convex formulation with efficient solver, clear empirical CD gains, well written and broadly applicable.  
- Cons: minor correctness/clarity issues (Lemma 2.1, Example 3.1, Algorithm 4), missing SGD timing and key adaptive baselines, and reference errors.  
- Recommendation: after rebuttal addressing these points, the reviewer recommends acceptance.
