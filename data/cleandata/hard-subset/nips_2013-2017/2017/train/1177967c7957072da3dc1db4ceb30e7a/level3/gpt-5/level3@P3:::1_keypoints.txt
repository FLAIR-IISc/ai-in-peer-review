- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using per-iteration lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling at negligible extra cost.  
- Formulates sampling as a convex min–max problem over a box of admissible gradients, proves optimality relative to bounds, and gives an O(n log n) sorting-based algorithm to compute probabilities.  
- Instantiates the approach for GLMs with O(n) per-iteration bound updates and shows strong empirical gains for CD.  
- Situates work within prior non-uniform/adaptive sampling literature and contrasts provable safe bounds with prior stale/heuristic approaches.  

Strengths:
- Clear motivation: practical surrogate for impractical full-information adaptive sampling.  
- Solid theoretical contributions: min–max formulation dominates fixed/L_i/uniform sampling and recovers full-information when bounds are tight.  
- Novel convex formulation with an efficient O(n log n) solver and helpful geometric intuition.  
- Practicality: cheap bound updates for GLMs and convincing CD empirical results.  
- Well written with informative empirical diagnostics (evolving vk).  

Weaknesses / technical queries:
- Lemma 2.1 likely has a constant/factor-of-2 error in α*; proof and statement need correction.  
- Example 3.1 may confuse readers about choosing c = l or u; clarify min–max vs. naive plug-in distinctions.  
- Algorithm 4 line 4: initialization of m and the violation check can never trigger as written; consider initializing m differently or using ≥.  
- Algorithm 4 line 7: probable typo—should reference u^{sort} not c^{sort}.  
- Competitive ratio bound v_k/w_k uses w_k which is non-convex to compute; discuss how loose this can be or provide practical proxies.  

Experiments / reproducibility:
- Lacks head-to-head comparisons with other adaptive schemes (cited refs and stale-gradient baselines).  
- Fixed non-uniform (L_i-based) baselines should be included in all plots, not just uniform.  
- SGD experiments lack wall-clock timing to substantiate negligible overhead claims.  
- Hardware details for timing plots are missing and should be reported for reproducibility.  

References & presentation:
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title; fix it.  
- Add Csiba & Richtárik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches.  
- Minor typos and inconsistent variables/comments (especially in Algorithm 4); recommend careful proofreading.  

Evaluation:
- Quality: strong theoretical guarantees and efficient solver; minor technical clarifications needed.  
- Clarity: very good overall; suggested clarifications would further reduce confusion.  
- Originality: high—safe min–max formulation and always-better-than-fixed guarantee are novel.  
- Significance: high—practically useful drop-in adaptive sampling with negligible overhead.
