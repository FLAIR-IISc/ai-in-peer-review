- Paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using safe gradient upper/lower bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling efficiently.
- Method is provably better than fixed importance sampling, with strong theoretical guarantees (notably Theorem 3.2).
- Algorithm runs in O(n log n) per iteration, comparable to evaluating a single gradient component, making it practical for large-scale problems.
- Empirical results on GLMs with L1/L2 regularization validate the method, showing marked improvements for CD in iterations and runtime.
- Method is generic and easily integrable into existing CD and SGD frameworks with minimal changes.
- Figures are too small and should be enlarged or moved to the appendix for better readability.
- Notation inconsistencies: incorrect variable usage at Line 85 and unnecessary subscripts at Line 135 need correction.
- Impact on SGD is limited: optimal sampling yields only slight gains over uniform sampling in experiments.
- Missing explicit complexity analysis of the total cost for CD/SGD, including O(n) updates for gradient bounds.
- Suggest adding a comparison with uniform sampling analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2.
- Suggest including explicit remarks on how maintaining gradient bounds affects overall algorithmic complexity.
- Suggest improving figure clarity and addressing the noted notation issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix clarity and notation concerns.
