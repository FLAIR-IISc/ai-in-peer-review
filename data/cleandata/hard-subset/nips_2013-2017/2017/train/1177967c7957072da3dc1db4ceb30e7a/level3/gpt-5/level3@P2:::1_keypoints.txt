- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration bounds and a convex min–max to pick sampling probabilities and step sizes.  
- Recovers full-information gradient-based optimal sampling and can be up to n× faster than Li-based or uniform sampling.  
- With only bounds, the proposed safe sampling is the minimax-optimal choice over the uncertainty set and guaranteed no worse than fixed Li-based sampling.  
- The safe distribution p̂ can be computed in O(n log n) per iteration via a sorting-based algorithm.  
- Builds on fixed non-uniform sampling, gradient-based adaptive sampling, and duality-gap/adaptive probability literature; novelty is the min–max under uncertainty and efficient solver.  
- Theory: full-information derivation and safe min–max formulation are sound, with provable worst-case guarantees.  
- Algorithmic solution (Algorithm 4) and complexity claim are plausible and efficient.  
- Issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a 1/2 factor — check constants throughout Section 2.1 for consistency.  
- Issue: Provide precise convexity/compactness conditions (Sion/Komiya) or a short appendix proof to justify the min–max interchange in (7).  
- Issue: Clarify Example 3.1 and reconcile it with the min–max interpretation that worst-case c often lies at a bound.  
- Issue: Algorithm 4 contains logical/invariant errors (Line 4 initialization/condition) and a typo (Line 7 should reference usort); provide invariants and a brief proof sketch.  
- Competitive-ratio results (Lemmas 3.5/3.6) are informative but weak/practically hard due to a non-convex wk; add interpretable corollaries or empirical plots of ρk.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and separates regimes clearly; fix minor notation and pseudocode typos and restate final step-size near Algorithm 2.  
- Originality: the safe min–max formulation with an efficient solver is novel and goes beyond prior gradient-proxy or stale-gradient approaches.  
- Significance: provides a provably never-worse adaptive sampling that is practical to integrate and yields substantial CD speedups.  
- Empirics: strong CD gains across losses/datasets; SGD gains are modest.  
- Empirics request: add comparisons to other adaptive schemes (Csiba–Qu–Richtárik, Perekrestenko et al., Allen‑Zhu et al., prior adaptive SGD), additional non-uniform fixed baselines, and consistent timing results for SGD.  
- Suggestion: include a short discussion on why adaptive sampling helps CD more than SGD (variance vs. bias in model-based bound).  
- Minor: fix pseudocode typos, verify reference details ([14], Csiba & Richtárik), cite “Approximate Steepest Coordinate Descent” when discussing n-fold speedup, and note numerical stability when p̂i can be very small.  
- Pros: conceptually clean min–max formulation, strong theoretical guarantees, efficient solver, easy integration, solid CD empirical gains.  
- Cons: several theory/presentation gaps, limited adaptive-baseline comparisons, missing SGD timing, and competitive-ratio bounds need better context.  
- Questions: clarify min–max interchange conditions and Lemma 2.1 constant; provide ablation on bound tightness and sensitivity to skewed Li or sparse features.  
- Recommendation: initially borderline accept contingent on fixes and added baselines; post-rebuttal (after fixes and added comparisons/timings) recommends acceptance.  
- Confidence: 0.8 (reviewer familiar with adaptive sampling and CD/SGD literature).
