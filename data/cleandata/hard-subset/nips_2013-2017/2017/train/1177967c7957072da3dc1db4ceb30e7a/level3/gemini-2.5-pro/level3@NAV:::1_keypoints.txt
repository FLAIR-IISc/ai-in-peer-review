- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using maintained lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes instead of full gradients.  
- Main contribution: a convex maximin formulation (Eq. (7)) that finds the optimal sampling distribution relative to these bounds.  
- The method is a practical approximation that is provably superior to uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Novel and elegant formulation with strong theoretical guarantees, including the “safe” property that it always outperforms fixed sampling.  
- Presents an efficient O(n log n) solver (Algorithm 4) making the approach practical with low computational overhead.  
- Paper is well-written and makes a solid contribution to importance sampling for large-scale optimization.  
- Theoretical concern: proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in the derivation of α*.  
- Clarification needed for Example 3.1: the text’s claim that setting c to its bounds is suboptimal seems at odds with the maximization in (7).  
- Possible logic/typo issues in Algorithm 4: line 4’s condition may never hold if m initialized to max(l^{sort}); line 7 likely should reference u^{sort} not c^{sort}.  
- Empirical evaluation could be strengthened by comparing to other adaptive sampling methods ([2], [5], [21]) and non-uniform fixed sampling (e.g., Lipschitz-proportional).  
- Missing timing results for the SGD experiments to substantiate the low-overhead claim.  
- Minor reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and a missing citation to Csiba & Richtarik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant paper; recommend acceptance if authors address the listed concerns.
