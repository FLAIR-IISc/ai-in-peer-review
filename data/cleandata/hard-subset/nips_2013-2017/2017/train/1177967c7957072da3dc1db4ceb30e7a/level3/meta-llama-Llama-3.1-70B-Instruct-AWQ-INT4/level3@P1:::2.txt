This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent (CD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified, but often computationally infeasible. To address this issue, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient, which are used to formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem.

The paper presents an efficient algorithm to compute the solution, which provably performs better than any fixed importance sampling. The computational cost of the proposed scheme is of the order O(n log n) per iteration, which is comparable with the cost to evaluate a single component of the gradient.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through empirical evaluations on relevant machine learning tasks, including generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization. The results show that adaptive importance sampling significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the empirical evaluations are convincing.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to adaptive importance sampling, which is both theoretically well-founded and computationally efficient.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a proof of the optimality of the proposed sampling distribution.
* The empirical evaluations are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case.
* The computational cost of the proposed scheme is of the order O(n log n) per iteration, which may be prohibitive for very large-scale problems.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the impact on total complexity of RCD/SGD, including the cost of updating the bounds on the gradient.

Suggestions for improvement:
