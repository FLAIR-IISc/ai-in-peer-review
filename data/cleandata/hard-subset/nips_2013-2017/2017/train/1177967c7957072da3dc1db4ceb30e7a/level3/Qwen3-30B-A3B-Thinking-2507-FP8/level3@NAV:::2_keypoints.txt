- Proposes a "safe adaptive importance sampling" scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using safe upper/lower gradient bounds to make adaptive sampling practical.  
- Shows the method is computationally feasible compared to exact gradient-based sampling.  
- Theorem 3.2: the proposed sampling provably outperforms L-based sampling (which includes uniform sampling).  
- Provides clear theoretical guarantees underpinning the method.  
- Empirical results demonstrate significant speedups across multiple machine learning tasks.  
- Connection to generalized linear models is well-motivated.  
- Per-iteration computational complexity argued as O(n log n), convincing for many applications.  
- Minor errors: typo on line 85 (denominator p^*_i should be x_k, not x) and inconsistent subscript k on \hat p and \hat c (line 135).  
- Presentation issues: figures need improved ticks, labels, numbers, and some should be moved to the appendix for readability.  
- Suggestion: include an explicit comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n) in Conclusion 2 as a natural baseline.  
- Suggestion: discuss total complexity of RCD/SGD including the O(n) cost of maintaining gradient bounds to reach ε-optimality.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, original, and a strong contribution with practical impact; minor issues do not detract.
