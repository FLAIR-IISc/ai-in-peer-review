- Introduces a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using gradient bounds to compute sampling distributions efficiently.
- Frames sampling as a convex optimization problem and gives an efficient algorithm that is provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling with negligible overhead.
- Strength: Novel convex formulation and theoretically sound, computationally efficient adaptive sampling strategy.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical guarantees showing superiority over fixed importance sampling and always improving on static sampling.
- Strength: Practical algorithm with O(n log n) per-iteration overhead and straightforward integration into CD/SGD.
- Strength: Well-written paper with clear methodology, proofs, and reproducible experimental setup.
- Strength: Empirical results show significant speed-ups in CD and moderate improvements in SGD, consistent with theory.
- Weakness: Proof error — missing factor of 2 in the proof of Lemma 2.1 that must be corrected.
- Weakness: Example 3.1 is unclear and does not convincingly illustrate suboptimality of naïve approaches versus uniform sampling.
- Weakness: Algorithm 4 issues — Line 4’s condition may never be satisfied, risking incorrect behavior; Line 7 appears to contain a typo.
- Weakness: Experimental limitations — lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes and to fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions.
- Weakness: Missing timing results for SGD, leaving practical performance evaluation incomplete.
- Weakness: Reference issues — Reference [14] has an incorrect title and the paper omits an important related work (Csiba & Richtarik, 2016).
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on fixing the proof error, algorithmic issues, and experimental gaps.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded method; efficient algorithm with negligible overhead; strong empirical utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: Minor theoretical and algorithmic inconsistencies and limited experimental comparisons / missing SGD timings.
- Overall: Advances the state of the art in importance sampling and is a valuable conference contribution if the noted issues are addressed.
