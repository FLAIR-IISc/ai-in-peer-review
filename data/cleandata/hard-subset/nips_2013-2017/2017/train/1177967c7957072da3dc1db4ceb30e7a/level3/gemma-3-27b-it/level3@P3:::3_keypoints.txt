- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme to accelerate CD and SGD using provable gradient-bound approximations.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing improvements over uniform or fixed importance sampling, especially for coordinate descent.
- Method is computationally efficient with overhead similar to evaluating one gradient component.
- The approach is generic and easily integrated into existing algorithms (CD, SGD) and objective functions.
- Extensive numerical experiments across datasets, loss functions, and regularizers support the method's effectiveness.
- Clear connection and positioning relative to existing work on importance sampling and optimization methods.
- Figures in Section 5 are hard to read due to small fonts and cramped labels, hindering interpretation.
- SGD experiments show only marginal improvements over uniform sampling, requiring further analysis and explanation.
- Reliance on safe upper/lower gradient bounds may limit applicability beyond GLMs and needs discussion of challenges/alternatives.
- Comparisons are limited: more baselines (e.g., adaptive schemes based on duality gaps) would strengthen evaluation.
- For acceptance: the paper addresses a significant problem with a novel, practical, and theoretically justified solution, particularly effective for CD.
- Against acceptance: poor figure readability, limited SGD gains, and insufficient comparison to other adaptive sampling methods.
- Overall: strong contribution warranting acceptance if figures are improved and SGD/discussion issues are addressed.
