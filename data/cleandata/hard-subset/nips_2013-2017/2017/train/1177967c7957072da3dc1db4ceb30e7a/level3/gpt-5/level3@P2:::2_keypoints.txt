- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration safe lower/upper bounds and a convex min–max to get the best guaranteed sampling.
- Recovers true gradient-based sampling with full information and is never worse than fixed Li-based sampling; can improve uniform sampling in the equal-Li case.
- Provides an O(n log n) per-iteration solver (Algorithm 4) and O(n) bound updates for GLMs.
- Connects to prior importance-sampling, gap-based sampling, and steepest-coordinate work, but uses principled safe bounds and minimax guarantees rather than stale/heuristic approximations.
- Theorem 3.2(ii) and competitive-ratio lemmas give theoretical guarantees that the method improves over Li-based sampling and quantify performance.
- Algorithmic practicality is strong: sampling computation is efficient and bound maintenance is cheap for important model classes.
- Empirically shows substantial speedups for CD with small overhead; SGD improvements are modest in experiments.
- Lacks end-to-end complexity analysis (total time to ε) that incorporates per-iteration overheads and improved progress rates.
- Needs an explicit comparison or bound versus uniform sampling for general Li heterogeneity to contextualize typical gains.
- Should clarify when the method helps SGD (e.g., heteroscedastic gradients, structured curvature) and include experiments where SGD benefits more.
- Discuss amortization strategies for O(n) bound updates (e.g., updating bounds and p̂ every m>1 iterations) and their impact on accuracy/speed.
- More explicit contrast needed with gap-based adaptive sampling and approximate steepest variants, clarifying differences in guarantees and costs.
- Lemma 3.6’s γ-condition is somewhat opaque; provide simple corollaries for common bound constructions to improve interpretability.
- Presentation issues: figures need larger, standardized axis ticks/labels and some plots may be moved to the appendix for readability.
- Notation inconsistency: remove or clarify the subscript k in p̂ and ĉ when referring to the generic solution of (7).
- Minor typo: Example 2.3 denominator should depend on xk (||√L ∇f(xk)||1), not x.
- Recommend restating V(p,c) and the role of L early in Section 3 and adding a short proof sketch for the min–max interchange in (8).
- Theoretical and empirical claims are well-supported; convex analysis and minimax tools are appropriately used.
- The safe-bounds min–max formulation and O(n log n) exact solver are novel and nontrivial contributions.
- Significant practical impact expected for CD where gradient bounds are accessible; SGD impact is more conditional but framework is promising.
- Pros: principled, optimally safe adaptive sampling with provable improvement over Li-based sampling; efficient, generic, and empirically strong for CD.
- Cons: incomplete total-complexity accounting, modest SGD gains, presentation issues, and some missing comparative analyses.
- Questions for authors: provide iteration and total time complexity to reach ε accounting for overheads; add comparison bounds vs uniform sampling for general Li; report sensitivity to bound tightness and p̂ recomputation frequency.
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper leaning toward acceptance pending improved presentation and a more explicit total-complexity discussion.
