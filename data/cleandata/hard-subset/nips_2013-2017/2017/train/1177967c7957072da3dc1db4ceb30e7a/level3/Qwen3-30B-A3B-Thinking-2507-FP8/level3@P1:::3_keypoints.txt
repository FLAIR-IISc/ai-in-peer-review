- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" for CD and SGD that uses safe gradient bounds to get provably better sampling while avoiding full-gradient costs.  
- Identifies the limitation of existing adaptive sampling: full-gradient updates are computationally infeasible despite theoretical benefits.  
- Shows gradient-based sampling in CD can yield up to an n-fold speedup over fixed importance sampling (Section 2).  
- Formulates a minimax optimization to compute the best sampling distribution from safe gradient bounds and provides Algorithm 2 to solve it.  
- Proves the proposed solution is always better than fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2) and gives rigorous supporting lemmas (e.g., Lemma 2.4, Theorems 3.2, 3.4).  
- Demonstrates how to compute safe upper/lower gradient bounds efficiently for generalized linear models with O(n log n) overhead per iteration (Section 4).  
- Empirical results on rcv1, real-sim, news20 across losses and regularizers confirm practical effectiveness and up to ~4x speedups (Section 5).  
- Computational overhead is small and comparable to evaluating a single gradient component for many problems, making the method practical for large-scale applications.  
- Novelty: the minimax safe-sampling approach fills a gap versus prior adaptive sampling work by providing provable guarantees relative to fixed sampling.  
- Strong exposition and organization with clear motivation, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Strengths summarized: provable improvement over uniform/fixed sampling, rigorous theory, practical efficiency, extensive experiments, clear distinction from prior work.  
- Weaknesses: figures need better readability (larger labels, clearer presentation).  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion/analysis of why CD benefits more than SGD in experiments.  
- Weaknesses: some implementation details on tracking gradient bounds could be more thoroughly explained.  
- Overall assessment: significant, practically applicable contribution with strong theory and experiments; reviewer recommends acceptance.
