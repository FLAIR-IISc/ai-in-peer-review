- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates optimal gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.  
- Addresses computational infeasibility of exact adaptive sampling by providing an efficient approximation based on gradient bounds.  
- Technical content is sound, with thorough theoretical analysis and clear relation to existing methods.  
- Generic and efficient strategy applicable to both randomized coordinate descent and stochastic gradient descent.  
- Empirical evaluation on several ML tasks shows improved convergence rates and computational time versus fixed importance sampling.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written with consistent notation and clear definitions.  
- Lacks discussion of total computational complexity, notably the cost of updating gradient bounds.  
- Does not compare against uniform sampling, a common and important baseline.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, practical significance for large-scale optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing total-cost analysis and missing uniform-sampling comparison.  
- Minor revisions requested: add total-complexity discussion, include uniform baseline comparison, fix typo (Line 85) and consistency issue (Line 135), increase figure tick/label sizes, consider moving some figures to the appendix.
