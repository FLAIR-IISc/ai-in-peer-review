This paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The key idea is to use safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient to compute an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling, which is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds. The proposed method is shown to always outperform uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.

The paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the proposed method is well-motivated and explained. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. The proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*. Example 3.1 is confusing, as maximizing in (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal. Algorithm 4, line 4, may never be satisfied, and line 7 likely contains a typo.

Additionally, the numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2],[5],[21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling. No timing results are provided for the SGD experiments. There are also some reference issues, including an incorrect title for [14] and a missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik “Importance Sampling for Minibatches” (2016, arXiv).

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field. After addressing the minor issues mentioned above, I would recommend acceptance.

Quality: 9/10
The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and numerical results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

Clarity: 9/10
The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

Originality: 9/10
The paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme that is different from previous contributions. The authors provide a clear explanation of how the proposed method differs from previous work.

Significance: 9/10
The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed method has the potential to advance the state of the art in importance sampling and make a significant impact on the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds.
* The proposed method is shown to always outperform uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.
* The paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
