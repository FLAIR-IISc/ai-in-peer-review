- Proposes a novel, efficient safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD.  
- Core idea: approximate gradient-based sampling using safe lower/upper gradient bounds via a convex optimization problem.  
- Sampling distribution computed in O(n log n) time and has negligible extra computational cost.  
- Method is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling under the provided bounds.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides extensive numerical validation.  
- Effectively situates the contribution within the importance sampling literature and cites relevant prior work.  
- Correctly identifies computational cost limitations of existing adaptive strategies that require full gradients.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, showing optimality of the proposed method relative to the gradient bounds.  
- Argues the approach bridges the gap between computationally expensive optimal sampling and simpler fixed schemes.  
- Formulation as a convex optimization allows efficient computation of the sampling distribution.  
- Experiments on multiple datasets and losses show consistent improvements over fixed sampling.  
- Provides clear guidance for computing safe gradient bounds for generalized linear models, enhancing practicality.  
- Minor technical issues: missing factor of 2 in the proof of Lemma 2.1 that should be corrected.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing and needs clarification to better illustrate limitations of naive bound selection.  
- Algorithm 4 contains logical issues/typos (line 4 condition may never hold; similar typo in line 7) that require fixes.  
- Numerical results lack comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- SGD experiments are missing timing results, which is a notable omission for practical evaluation.  
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and omission of Csiba & Richtarik (“Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” 2016) which should be included.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of the safe adaptive scheme, strong theoretical guarantees, computational efficiency (O(n log n)), empirical validation, and practical relevance for GLMs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor technical and algorithmic issues, insufficient numerical comparisons to other adaptive methods, missing SGD timing results, and minor reference errors.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed concerns (corrected Lemma 2.1, clarified Example 3.1, fixed Algorithm 4, added comparisons and SGD timing); reviewer now strongly recommends acceptance.
