- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD that uses lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling.
- The sampling distribution is provably optimal w.r.t. the provided bounds and always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- The method can be computed at negligible extra computational cost in many applications.
- Shows theoretical advantages of adaptive importance sampling for CD, with possible speed-ups up to a factor of n.
- Casts finding the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and gives an O(n log n) algorithm to solve it.
- Demonstrates integration into CD and SGD for structured problems with empirical speed-ups on real-world regression, classification, and feature-selection tasks.
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with appropriate notation and consistent connection to prior work.
- Theoretical results (e.g., Theorems 3.2 and 3.4) are sound and well-justified; experiments are thorough and support the theory.
- Originality is significant: novel use of safe bounds guaranteeing superiority over fixed sampling and an elegant convex formulation.
- High practical significance: easy integration into existing CD/SGD implementations and substantial practical speed-ups.
- Minor technical issues: possible missing factor of 2 in proof of Lemma 2.1.
- Presentation issues: Example 3.1 is confusing and seems to contradict a preceding claim; Algorithm 4 has questionable conditions/typo.
- Experimental gaps: lacks comparisons to other adaptive methods (e.g., [2,5,21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling; SGD experiments lack timing results.
- Reference errors: incorrect title for [14] and omission of Csiba & Richtarik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches.
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality paper with novel theory and convincing experiments; recommend acceptance subject to minor fixes.
