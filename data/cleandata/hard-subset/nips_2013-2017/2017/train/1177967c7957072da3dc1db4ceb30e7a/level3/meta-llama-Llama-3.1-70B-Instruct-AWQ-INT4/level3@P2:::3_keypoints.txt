- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD.  
- Method relies on efficiently computable safe bounds on the gradient at each iteration.  
- Sampling distribution is provably optimal wrt the bounds and always better than uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Can be computed at negligible extra computational cost per iteration.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis and detailed comparisons with existing methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive across various machine learning tasks and datasets.  
- Paper is well-motivated, well-written, and clearly organized.  
- Figures in Section 5 are difficult to read and should be enlarged with clearer labels.  
- Paper lacks sufficient details on computational complexity and direct complexity comparison to prior methods.  
- Method is generic and easily integrated into existing algorithms, making it practically valuable.  
- Overall makes a significant contribution to optimization and machine learning with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address figure readability and complexity details; rating 8/10.
