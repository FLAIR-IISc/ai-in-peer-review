Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD that approximates optimal gradient-based sampling.

Approach uses safe upper and lower bounds on gradients to compute an efficient, provably optimal sampling distribution w.r.t. those bounds.

Claims the proposed sampling is always better than uniform and fixed importance sampling and can be computed at negligible extra cost in many applications.

Provides theoretical analysis and proofs supporting the scheme’s optimality.

Presents extensive numerical experiments on generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization showing better iteration and time performance than fixed importance sampling.

Strength: The method is generic and easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.

Strength: The authors supply rigorous theoretical guarantees, including optimality proofs under the given bounds.

Strength: Empirical validation on real-world datasets is extensive.

Weakness: Assumes safe gradient upper/lower bounds can be computed efficiently, which may not hold in all practical settings.

Weakness: The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed scheme.

Weakness: Needs a more thorough comparison with existing adaptive importance sampling methods.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

Requested revisions: add a detailed computational-complexity analysis, include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, and improve readability of the figures in Section 5.
