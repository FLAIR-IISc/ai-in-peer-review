- Paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds with an efficient, provably optimal approximation.  
- Method consistently outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling with negligible extra computational cost.  
- Novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver; scheme is generic and easily integrated.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound and grounded in convex optimization.  
- Thorough analysis of method properties and relation to existing importance sampling approaches.  
- Extensive numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Proof issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^*.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing: maximizing (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4, line 4: logic likely flawed because the condition may never be satisfied.  
- Algorithm 4, line 7: probable typo—should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Numerical results need comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- No timing/results reported for SGD experiments.  
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches."  
- Recommendation: after rebuttal, reviewer recommends acceptance but asks authors to address the listed weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, solid theory, and strong experimental evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor errors, need for broader numerical comparisons, and missing SGD timing results.
