- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using bounds on gradients.  
- Reformulates selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem using safe lower and upper gradient bounds.  
- Main contribution: a practical variant of the ideal algorithm based on solving that optimization problem.  
- Shows the proposed sampling is provably better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling.  
- Claims the sampling distribution can be computed efficiently in many applications at negligible extra cost.  
- Provides numerical experiments on real data demonstrating strong empirical performance.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible with a thorough introduction and literature review.  
- Theoretical results and proofs are well-presented, sound, and easy to follow.  
- Presentation strengths: clear intuition, simple effective notation, and well-designed figures.  
- Minor issues: typo in denominator of p^*_i (x_k vs x) and missing subscripts for \hat p and \hat c on line 135.  
- Figures need improved readability (larger ticks/labels/numbers) and some could be moved to the appendix.  
- Originality: introduces a novel convex-optimization-based approach to adaptive importance sampling.  
- Significance: potential to materially improve efficiency of widely used CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with well-supported claims; recommendation for acceptance to NIPS despite minor presentation issues.
