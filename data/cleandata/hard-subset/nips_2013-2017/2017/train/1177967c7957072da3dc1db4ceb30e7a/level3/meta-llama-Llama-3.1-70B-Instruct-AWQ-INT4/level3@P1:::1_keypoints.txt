- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe gradient lower/upper bounds.  
- Uses bounds to compute an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal relative to those bounds.  
- Method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and incurs negligible extra computational cost.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides a concise introduction to the problem.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and supports the claims.  
- Numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.  
- Proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^*.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing: maximizing (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the claimed suboptimality of naive bound-setting.  
- Algorithm 4 has issues: line 4 may never be satisfied and line 7 likely contains a typo.  
- Numerical experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2],[5],[21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- No timing results are provided for the SGD experiments.  
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik “Importance Sampling for Minibatches” (2016, arXiv).  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and recommend acceptance after addressing the minor issues.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
