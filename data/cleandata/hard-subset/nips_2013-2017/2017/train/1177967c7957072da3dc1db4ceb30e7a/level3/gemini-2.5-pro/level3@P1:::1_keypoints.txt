- Problem: optimal gradient-based sampling requires full gradients and is infeasible; stale/bound approximations can perform worse than uniform.
- Main contribution: cast sampling distribution selection as a minimax optimization over safe lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Safety guarantee: the derived sampling is provably never worse than fixed importance sampling (including uniform).
- Algorithm: provides an efficient O(n log n) solver (Algorithm 4) making sampling-overhead negligible in practice.
- Demonstration: applied to generalized linear models with efficient bound updates.
- Empirical result (CD): approaches ideal full-information sampling and significantly outperforms uniform in iterations and wall-clock time.
- Originality/significance: novel and elegant formulation that makes adaptive sampling robust and practical.
- Theoretical quality: technically sound with well-supported claims (e.g., Theorem 3.2) and a practical solver.
- Clarity: well-written, clearly motivated, with helpful illustrations (Figure 1).
- Weakness — Lemma 2.1: apparent missing factor of 2 in the optimal stepsize; needs correction.
- Weakness — Example 3.1: confusing; requires clearer explanation of why the minimax avoids naive bound-based pitfalls.
- Weakness — Algorithm 4: logical/typo issues (line 4 condition and line 7 likely incorrect) that hurt reproducibility.
- Weakness — experiments: comparisons only against uniform; should include other adaptive methods and L_i-based sampling.
- Weakness — timing: SGD wall-clock timing results are missing and necessary to validate practical efficiency.
- Referencing issues: incorrect title for ref [14] and missing citation for Csiba & Richtarik (Importance Sampling for Minibatches).
- Summary: valuable, well-executed paper with a strong safety guarantee; minor technical fixes and broader experiments would strengthen it.
