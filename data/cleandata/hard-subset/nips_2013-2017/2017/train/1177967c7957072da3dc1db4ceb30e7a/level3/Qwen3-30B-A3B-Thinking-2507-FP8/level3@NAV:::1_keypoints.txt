- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds to get provably optimal sampling with negligible overhead.  
- Key insight: cast optimal sampling as a convex optimization over provided gradient bounds instead of using risky approximations.  
- Fills a gap in adaptive importance sampling by offering a computationally cheap method that outperforms fixed importance sampling.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound; minor corrections were identified and addressed in the rebuttal.  
- Empirical results show significant performance gains across multiple datasets and problem types.  
- Strengths: elegant convex formulation (Theorem 3.2), efficient O(n log n) solver (Algorithm 4), strong empirical validation, and clear writing with good relation to prior work.  
- Initial weaknesses (factor of 2 in Lemma 2.1, confusing Example 3.1, Algorithm 4 details) have been satisfactorily resolved.  
- Suggested additional experiments: more comparisons to adaptive methods [2,5,21] and SGD timing results; authors provided clarifications.  
- High significance: theoretically grounded, easy to integrate into existing CD/SGD implementations, broadly applicable where gradient bounds can be maintained.  
- Overall: high-quality, original, technically sound contribution that meets NIPS criteria and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
