This paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) using lower and upper gradient bounds. The authors provide an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds. The method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.

The paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature. The authors provide a novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver. The proposed sampling scheme is generic and can easily be integrated into existing algorithms.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed method is theoretically sound and has a solid foundation in convex optimization.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method's properties and its relationship to existing importance sampling methods.
* The paper includes extensive numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*.
* Example 3.1 is confusing, as maximizing in (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal.
* Algorithm 4, line 4, has questionable logic, as the condition may never be satisfied.
* Algorithm 4, line 7, likely contains a typo and should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
* The numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
* No timing results are provided for SGD experiments.
* There are reference issues, including an incorrect title for [14] and a missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).

After the authors' rebuttal, I recommend acceptance of the paper. However, I suggest that the authors address the mentioned weaknesses and areas for improvement to strengthen the paper further.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes a significant contribution to the importance sampling literature.
* The proposed method is theoretically sound and has a solid foundation in convex optimization.
* The paper includes extensive numerical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper has some minor errors and areas for improvement that need to be addressed.
* The numerical results could be strengthened by including comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
* The paper could benefit from additional timing results for SGD experiments.
