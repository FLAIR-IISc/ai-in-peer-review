This paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that leverages per-iteration lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling. The key idea is to choose probabilities via a convex min–max formulation that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds, strictly improves over uniform or fixed importance sampling, and recovers the exact gradient-based probabilities when full information is available. The paper relates to prior work on fixed importance sampling for CD/SGD (e.g., Nesterov; Richtárik & Takáč), adaptive gradient-based sampling for SGD [22,36,37], and duality-gap based sampling for CD [5,23], while offering a new safety guarantee: with only bounds, the sampling is never worse than fixed baselines. The efficient solver (O(n log n)) and integration into CD/SGD make the scheme practical.

Quality: The theoretical development is largely sound and insightful, especially the reformulation as a convex problem and the worst-case optimality under bounds. However: (i) Lemma 2.1 seems to miss a factor of 2 in α*, which propagates to the bound; (ii) Example 3.1 is confusing—maximizing in (7) appears to encourage setting c to bounds, seemingly contradicting the claim that naive bound setting can be suboptimal; please reconcile or clarify. Algorithmic details need fixes: Algorithm 4, line 4, if m = max(l^{sort}) the loop condition may never trigger—logic should be reviewed; line 7 likely uses u^{sort} rather than c^{sort}. Experiments convincingly show CD gains; for SGD, please add baselines vs other adaptive methods ([2],[5],[21]) and non-uniform fixed importance sampling, and provide timing results (only iteration counts are shown).

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized. Minor typos and ambiguities (above) should be corrected. Reference issues: [14] has an incorrect title; please add Csiba & Richtárik “Importance Sampling for Minibatches” (2016, arXiv).

Originality: The safe min–max formulation and efficient solver for bound-based adaptive sampling are novel and well-motivated.

Significance: The method is generic, easy to integrate, and has negligible overhead; the guarantees that it always beats uniform/fixed sampling are valuable. The empirical CD speedups are substantial; broader SGD evidence would strengthen impact.

Pros:
- Strong theory; worst-case optimal under bounds; safety guarantee.
- Novel convex formulation with efficient solver.
- Clear empirical gains for CD with negligible overhead.
- Well written and broadly applicable.

Cons:
- Minor correctness/clarity issues (Lemma 2.1, Example 3.1, Algorithm 4).
- Missing SGD timing and key adaptive baselines; reference issues.

After rebuttal addressing these points, I recommend acceptance.
