- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for Coordinate Descent and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds to formulate optimal sampling as a convex problem.  
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) per-iteration algorithm that provably outperforms any fixed importance sampling.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, including a proof of optimality of the proposed sampling distribution.  
- Empirical results on generalized linear models (L1/L2) show adaptive sampling significantly outperforms fixed sampling in iterations and time.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and concise explanations.  
- Assumes safe gradient bounds can be efficiently computed, which may not hold in all settings.  
- Per-iteration cost O(n log n) may be prohibitive for very large-scale problems despite being comparable to evaluating one gradient component.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of total RCD/SGD complexity, notably the cost of updating gradient bounds.  
- Suggestion: provide an analogue of Conclusion 2 comparing the method to uniform sampling (p = 1/n).  
- Suggestion: discuss impact on total complexity including bound-updating costs.  
- Suggestion: give more details on how to efficiently compute safe lower and upper gradient bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, computationally efficient method with convincing empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practicality concerns about computing bounds and scalability of per-iteration cost.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommend acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses.
