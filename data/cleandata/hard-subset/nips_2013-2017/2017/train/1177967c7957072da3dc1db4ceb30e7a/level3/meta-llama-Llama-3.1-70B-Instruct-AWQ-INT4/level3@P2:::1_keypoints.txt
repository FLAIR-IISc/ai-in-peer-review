- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that formulates optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.  
- Argues gradient-based sampling is theoretically justified and provides an efficient approximation provably optimal relative to the provided bounds.  
- Method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling with negligible extra computational cost.  
- Generic approach that can be easily integrated into existing algorithms.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on several machine learning tasks supports claims.  
- Paper is well written, clearly organized, and technically sound with reproducible notation and explanations.  
- Novel contribution: new perspective on importance sampling and safe adaptive sampling provably optimal under given bounds.  
- Significant: addresses an important problem and provides practical tools to improve CD and SGD performance.  
- Recommendation: overall recommend acceptance; paper is a complete and valuable contribution.  
- Minor issue: proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*.  
- Minor issue: Example 3.1 is confusing and may contradict the claim that naive bound-setting is suboptimal.  
- Minor issue: Algorithm 4 contains questionable logic at line 4 and a likely typo at line 7.  
- Experimental concern: numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- Experimental concern: timing results for the SGD experiments are not provided.
