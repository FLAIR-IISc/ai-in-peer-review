This paper proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms, addressing a timely and popular topic in machine learning. The authors demonstrate that their method is generic and can be easily integrated with many existing algorithms, providing both theoretical results and practical experiments to support their strategy.

The paper begins by discussing the limitations of existing adaptive importance sampling strategies, which often require the computation of the full gradient in each iteration, making them computationally infeasible. The authors propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling, which is based on safe bounds on the gradient. They show that this approximation is provably the best sampling with respect to the given bounds, always better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed in many applications at negligible extra cost.

The proposed sampling scheme is then applied to CD and SGD methods, and the authors demonstrate its efficiency through extensive numerical testing. The paper also discusses the properties of the solution and how it can be efficiently computed, providing a geometric interpretation of the optimization problem and a competitive ratio analysis.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed adaptive sampling scheme on relevant machine learning tasks, including generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization, shows significant improvements over fixed importance sampling in iterations and time. The results also demonstrate that the introduced computational overhead is small enough to outperform fixed importance sampling in terms of total computation time.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method and its theoretical properties. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner.
