- Proposal: safe adaptive importance sampling for RCD/SGD using per-iteration lower/upper gradient bounds to compute a min–max optimal sampling via a convex program solvable in O(n log n).  
- Recovery/guarantees: with full information recovers gradient-based sampling; Theorem 3.2 guarantees at least as good as L-weighted sampling; Remark 3.3 suggests superiority to uniform under coordinate-wise L-smoothness.  
- Quality of analysis: technically solid—minimax formulation, optimality, and O(n log n) algorithm are well justified; CD/SGD experiments support claims (larger gains for CD).  
- Missing analysis: no end-to-end complexity bound to ε accuracy—paper reports per-iteration overhead but not total complexity when maintaining bounds.  
- Clarity issues: generally clear but presentation problems (tiny/missing figure ticks/labels; many figures could be moved to appendix); minor notation typos (Line 85: denominator x_k not x; Line 135: remove subscript k).  
- Originality: novel safe min–max formulation over gradient bounding box and an efficient algorithm (Theorem 3.4); elegant recovery of full-information optimal sampler.  
- Significance: practically useful for RCD/SGD where full gradients are infeasible; guarantees dominating L-based sampling are valuable, but explicit complexity trade-offs would increase impact.  
- Requested additions: (1) formalize comparison to uniform sampling beyond Li = L (promote Remark 3.3 to corollary with assumptions); (2) quantify total wall-clock complexity to reach ε including O(n) bound updates and O(n log n) sampling and identify regimes where method is net-faster.  
- Pros: theoretically principled, efficient, strong CD gains, broadly integrable.  
- Cons: lacks end-to-end complexity, figure readability problems, modest SGD gains, minor typos/notation.  
- Overall verdict: strong and interesting submission.
