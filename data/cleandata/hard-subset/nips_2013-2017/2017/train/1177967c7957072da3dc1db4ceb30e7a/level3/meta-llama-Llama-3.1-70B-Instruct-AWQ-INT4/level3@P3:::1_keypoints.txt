- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD.  
- Introduces a novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.  
- Method is provably optimal relative to provided bounds and consistently outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling with negligible extra cost.  
- Well-written paper that provides a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature.  
- Extensive numerical testing shows adaptive importance sampling significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time.  
- Lemma 2.1 proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing: maximizing (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4 has issues: line 4 may never be satisfied due to questionable logic, and line 7 likely contains a typo (should use u^{sort} not c^{sort}).  
- Numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- No timing/results reported for the SGD experiments.  
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance; strengths outweigh weaknesses and some concerns were addressed in rebuttal.  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims.  
- Originality and significance: original approach that advances the state of the art and is likely to be useful to researchers and practitioners.
