- Introduces a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for RCD and SGD using maintained lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Core contribution: formulates sampling distribution selection as a minimax optimization, producing an efficiently computable sampling strategy.  
- The proposed sampling is provably superior to standard fixed importance sampling.  
- Well-motivated and significant for large-scale optimization with solid theoretical guarantees.  
- Minimax formulation (Eq. 7) is novel and elegant.  
- Theorem 3.2 provides a key "safety" guarantee: always better than L-based sampling.  
- Extensive experiments show clear improvements in iteration complexity and wall-clock time over non-adaptive baselines.  
- Empirical presentation needs improvement: many figures have illegible axis ticks/labels—suggest moving plots to an appendix for larger, clearer figures.  
- Minor typos/inconsistencies to fix (e.g., Line 85 denominator of p*_i should use x_k; Line 135 p̂ subscripts).  
- Theoretical gap: provide an analogue of Conclusion 2 explicitly comparing to uniform sampling.  
- Complexity discussion should be expanded to account for total computational cost (including O(n) bound updates) to reach an ε-optimal solution, beyond the per-iteration O(n log n) overhead.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, valuable theoretical and practical contributions; weaknesses are addressable—recommend acceptance.
