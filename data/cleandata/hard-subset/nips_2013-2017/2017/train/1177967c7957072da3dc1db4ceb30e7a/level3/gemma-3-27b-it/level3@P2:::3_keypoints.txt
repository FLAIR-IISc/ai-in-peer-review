- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe upper/lower gradient bounds.
- Approximates gradient-based sampling with bounds to retain theoretical guarantees while being computationally efficient.
- Shows the proposed sampling is provably better than uniform/fixed importance sampling and yields practical speed-ups.
- Well-motivated and clearly positioned within adaptive importance sampling literature.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including optimal sampling under bounded gradients and connection to full-gradient optimal sampling.
- Method is practical and general: computationally efficient, integrable into CD/SGD, with techniques to track bounds in GLMs.
- Extensive empirical validation on rcv1, real-sim, news20 demonstrating improved convergence and plots of vk behavior.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation.
- Figures in Section 5 are hard to read; need larger size and bigger labels for clarity.
- SGD shows smaller performance gains compared to CD; requires deeper analysis of causes and hyperparameter effects.
- Dependence on safe gradient bounds adds assumptions and may limit applicability to complex models (e.g., deep nets).
- Limited empirical comparison to other recent adaptive sampling methods beyond the cited works.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, practical, empirically validated, and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor figure readability, unexplained CD vs SGD gap, and reliance on gradient bounds restricting scope.
- Overall recommendation from reviewer: strong accept.
