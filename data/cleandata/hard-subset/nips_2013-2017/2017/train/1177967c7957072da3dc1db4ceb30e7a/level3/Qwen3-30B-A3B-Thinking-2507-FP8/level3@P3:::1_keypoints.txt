- Proposes a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient components.  
- Formulates finding the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem (Eq. 7) solved efficiently (Alg. 4).  
- Method is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2).  
- Computational overhead is low (adds O(n log n) per iteration) and negligible in many ML settings.  
- Empirical results on rcv1, real-sim, and news20 show consistent speedups in iterations and wall-clock time for CD.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides thorough theoretical justification and implementation details.  
- Theoretical concerns: Lemma 2.1 may be missing a factor of 2 in α*.  
- Theoretical concerns: Example 3.1 is confusing—Eq. (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the claim about naive bound-setting.  
- Algorithmic concerns: Algorithm 4 contains questionable logic (line 4 possibly never satisfied; line 7 likely typo u^{sort} vs c^{sort}).  
- Experimental gaps: lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- Experimental gaps: no timing results reported for the SGD experiments.  
- Presentation errors: reference errors (incorrect title for [14]) and a missing Csiba & Richtarik 2016 citation.  
- Authors addressed the theoretical concerns in the rebuttal, and overall empirical/theoretical claims remain convincing.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—novel, practical, and theoretically solid contribution to importance sampling literature.
