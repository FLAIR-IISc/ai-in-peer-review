- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" for coordinate descent and SGD using maintainable safe lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Efficiently approximates gradient-based sampling while remaining computationally cheap for large-scale ML.  
- Provides a theoretical guarantee (Theorem 3.2) that the sampling is always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Strong theoretical analysis and extensive empirical validation on real-world datasets.  
- Original method: uses safe bounds in a novel optimization formulation, differing from prior ad-hoc approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: generic improvement applicable to many algorithms with measured speed-ups in iterations and total time.  
- Experiments show loose gradient bounds suffice and the adaptive stepsize is critical to performance.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Main weakness: figures in Section 5 are hard to read due to small text/labels and need enlargement.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve figure presentation.
