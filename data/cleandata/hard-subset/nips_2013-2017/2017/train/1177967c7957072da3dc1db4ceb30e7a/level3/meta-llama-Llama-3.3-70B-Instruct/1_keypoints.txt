- Proposes EigenPro, a preconditioned Richardson iteration using approximate second-order information to speed convergence for shallow models like kernel methods.  
- Identifies a fundamental limitation of gradient descent with smooth kernels causing slow convergence and over-regularization.  
- Provides careful motivation, a detailed algorithmic description, and analysis of EigenPro’s convergence properties.  
- Experimental results show EigenPro substantially improves convergence on several large-scale datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise introduction to the problem and proposed solution.  
- Strengths: thorough analysis of GD limitations, detailed EigenPro description, and strong empirical validation.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in ML and optimization, which may hinder non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers without strong linear algebra/optimization backgrounds.  
- Weakness: lacks a more detailed comparison to other preconditioning methods and related optimization work.  
- Minor revisions requested: correct the proof of Lemma 2.1 and add a missing related reference.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication pending minor revisions.
