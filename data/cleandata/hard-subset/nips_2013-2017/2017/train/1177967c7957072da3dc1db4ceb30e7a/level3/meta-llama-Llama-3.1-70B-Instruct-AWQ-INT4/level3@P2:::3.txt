This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical properties and empirical performance on various machine learning tasks.

The paper is well-motivated, as importance sampling has become a crucial strategy for speeding up optimization algorithms in large-scale applications. The authors identify the limitations of existing adaptive importance sampling strategies, which often rely on computationally infeasible calculations or approximations that can be worse than uniform sampling.

The proposed method is based on safe bounds on the gradient, which can be efficiently computed in every iteration. The authors show that the proposed sampling distribution is provably the best sampling with respect to the given bounds, always better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed at negligible extra cost.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing methods. The empirical evaluation is also comprehensive, covering various machine learning tasks and datasets.

The figures in Section 5 are difficult to read, and it would be helpful to enlarge them and make the labels more readable. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to existing methods.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning. The proposed method is generic and can be easily integrated into existing algorithms, making it a valuable tool for practitioners. The theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
