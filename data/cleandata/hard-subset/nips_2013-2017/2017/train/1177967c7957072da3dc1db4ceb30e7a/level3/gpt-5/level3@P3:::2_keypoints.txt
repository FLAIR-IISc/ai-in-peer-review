- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme for RCD/SGD using per-coordinate safe gradient bounds and a min–max optimization to get a worst-case-optimal sampling distribution.
- Method guarantees (i) optimal distribution given bounds, (ii) always at least as good as L-based importance sampling, and (iii) recovers full-gradient sampling when bounds are exact.
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) solver (Alg. 4) for computing the sampling distribution and O(n) bound updates for GLMs.
- Empirical results: substantial speedups for coordinate descent (CD) and modest but consistent gains for SGD.
- Positions the work relative to adaptive sampling literature and introduces a novel principled min–max formulation with worst-case guarantees.
- Theory is strong: Theorem 3.2 shows dominance over L-based sampling; geometric characterization and competitive-ratio bounds are insightful and practical.
- Algorithms are clearly specified for CD/SGD, including matched stepsize α̂k = 1/vk.
- Experiments corroborate theory for CD; SGD improvements are smaller, matching prior observations.
- Weakness: brief discussion of total complexity — lacks explicit end-to-end ε-complexity accounting.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, with clear optimization viewpoint and GLM safe-bounds construction.
- Presentation issues: figures have small/missing tick labels and legends; notation inconsistencies and minor typos need fixing.
- Originality: novel safe min–max formulation turning partial gradient information into a provably safe, efficiently computable sampler.
- Significance: high for RCD (likely become a default adaptive sampler); moderate for SGD given smaller empirical gains.
- Specific fixes requested: correct typo in Example 2.3 (Line 85) and remove redundant subscripts in Theorem 3.2 (Line 135).
- Suggestion to include an explicit analogue of domination over uniform sampling and empirical comparisons to uniform for nonuniform L.
- Ask to add end-to-end complexity: combine iteration counts (strongly convex and general convex) with per-iteration costs Titer = Θ(d) + O(n) + O(n log n), memory O(n), and discuss regimes and amortizations.
- Recommend reporting wall-clock timings or normalized times and ablations on update frequency to show time–accuracy trade-offs.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled formulation, strong theory, efficient algorithm, solid CD gains, easy integration.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited SGD gains, potential overhead when d ≪ n, and presentation/notation issues.
- Overall recommendation: reviewer leans positive pending minor fixes and a more explicit end-to-end complexity discussion.
