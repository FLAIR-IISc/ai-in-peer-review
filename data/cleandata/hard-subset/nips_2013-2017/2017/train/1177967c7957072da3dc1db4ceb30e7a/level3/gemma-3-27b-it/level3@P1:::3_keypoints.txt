- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme to accelerate coordinate descent (CD) and SGD in large-scale optimization.  
- Leverages upper and lower gradient bounds to define a sampling distribution that is optimal within those constraints.  
- Proposes an efficient approximation that is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling and computationally feasible.  
- Proves CD gradient-based sampling can yield up to an n-fold speed-up and presents a generic adaptive strategy for both CD and SGD.  
- Designed for easy integration into existing algorithms with minimal overhead.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with clear guarantees showing superiority over standard sampling strategies.  
- Method is practical and computationally efficient, applicable to a wide range of ML optimization problems.  
- Extensive numerical experiments validate theoretical findings on real-world datasets; Algorithms 1–3 are clearly presented.  
- Provides useful discussion of computational complexity and connections to prior work.  
- Weakness: practical implications of the competitive ratio bounds (Lemmas 3.5 and 3.6) need more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: Section 4’s description of how safe bounds are tracked is brief and needs concrete examples and discussion of challenges.  
- Weakness: Figures in Section 5 are hard to read due to small size and unclear labeling, impeding comparison of methods.  
- Weakness: Justification for chosen datasets and regularization parameters could be expanded.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on improving figure readability and elaborating on bounds tracking and competitive-ratio interpretation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, practical relevance, and strong empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor figure readability, limited elaboration on tracking safe bounds, and unclear practical meaning of competitive ratios.
