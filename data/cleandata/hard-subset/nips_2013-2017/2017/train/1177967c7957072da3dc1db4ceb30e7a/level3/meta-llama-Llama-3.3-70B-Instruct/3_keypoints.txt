- Identifies a fundamental algorithmic limitation of gradient descent with smooth kernels: after polynomial iterations only very smooth functions can be approximated, even with infinite data.  
- Proposes EigenPro iteration, a simple preconditioning scheme using a few approximate eigenvectors to modify the kernel matrix spectrum.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Provides a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art kernel methods showing improved convergence and reduced computational cost.  
- EigenPro is well-motivated, simple, and easy to understand as a practical solution.  
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate real-world potential.  
- Figures in Section 5 are too small and labels are illegible, hindering readability.  
- Some experimental results need more thorough explanation and better context on datasets and metrics.  
- Paper could use a more detailed comparison with related work.
