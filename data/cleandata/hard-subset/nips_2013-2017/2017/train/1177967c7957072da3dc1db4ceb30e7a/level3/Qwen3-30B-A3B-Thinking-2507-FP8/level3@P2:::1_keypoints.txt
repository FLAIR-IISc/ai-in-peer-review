- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds and casts finding the optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem.  
- Claims the method is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling with negligible overhead.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorem 3.2) and an efficient O(n log n) algorithm (Algorithm 4).  
- Builds on prior adaptive sampling work but addresses the computational infeasibility of gradient-based sampling via a novel convex formulation.  
- Quality: solid theoretical analysis and complexity results, but technical issues exist in Lemma 2.1 (missing factor of 2) and questionable logic/typo in Algorithm 4 (lines 4 and 7).  
- Empirics: compelling CD results showing substantial speedups and time improvements; SGD experiments are incomplete (no timing comparisons, missing relevant adaptive baselines [2,5,21]).  
- Clarity: paper is very well-written and organized, figures are effective, and reproducibility is adequate, though Example 3.1 is confusing and needs clarification.  
- Originality: novel convex optimization formulation with respect to gradient bounds that differs from previous approximation-based methods.  
- References: citation list has errors (incorrect title for [14]) and misses Csiba & Richtarik (2016) among other omissions.  
- Significance: method is practical and easy to integrate (O(n log n) per iteration) and provides a useful guarantee of outperforming uniform sampling.  
- Strengths: elegant formulation, strong theoretical guarantees, practical algorithm, convincing CD experiments, and broad applicability.  
- Weaknesses: fixes needed for Lemma 2.1 and Algorithm 4, additional comparisons to other adaptive methods and timing for SGD, and reference corrections.  
- Recommendation: strong paper with minor revisions required to address technical issues, missing comparisons, and reference errors; likely acceptable after fixes.
