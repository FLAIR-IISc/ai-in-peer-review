Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes “safe adaptive importance sampling” for RCD and SGD that uses per-iteration lower/upper bounds on coordinate/component gradients to compute a min–max optimal sampling distribution via a convex program solvable in O(n log n). With full information, the method recovers gradient-based sampling; with bounded information, Theorem 3.2 (Conclusion 2) guarantees it is always at least as good as L-based (Li-weighted) sampling, and Remark 3.3 indicates superiority to uniform in the coordinate-wise L-smooth case. This contributes to a line of work on non-uniform/adaptive sampling (e.g., Richtárik & Takáč; Zhao & Zhang; Csiba et al.; Allen-Zhu et al.; Jaggi et al.) by providing a principled, efficiently-computable rule with worst-case guarantees relative to the available bounds.

Quality: The analysis is technically solid: the minimax formulation, optimality of the bound-based distribution, and the O(n log n) algorithm are clearly justified; the CD/SGD integration and empirical results support the claims (notably larger gains for CD than SGD). That said, the paper stops short of end-to-end complexity bounds: it reports per-iteration overhead but not total complexity to ε accuracy when maintaining bounds.

Clarity: Generally clear and well organized, but several presentation issues hinder readability. Figures have tiny or missing ticks/labels; many could be moved to the appendix to enlarge the main ones. Minor corrections: Line 85, denominator of p*_i should be x_k, not x. Line 135, for consistency, remove the subscript k from p̂ and ĉ in the general statement.

Originality: The safe min–max formulation over a bounding box of gradients and the efficient algorithm (Theorem 3.4) are novel and neat; recovering the full-information optimal sampler is elegant.

Significance: Useful for practitioners of RCD/SGD where full gradients are infeasible; the guarantees that the new sampler dominates L-based sampling are valuable. Providing explicit complexity trade-offs would further strengthen impact.

Requests and suggestions:
- Provide an analogue of Conclusion 2 that directly compares the new sampling to uniform (p=1/n) beyond the Li = L case; or elevate Remark 3.3 to a formal corollary with assumptions spelled out.
- Discuss total complexity: quantify wall-clock cost to reach an ε-optimal solution including O(n) updates of ℓk, uk and O(n log n) sampling, and identify regimes (n, d, sparsity) where the method is net-faster than uniform/L-based baselines.

Pros: theoretically principled; efficient; strong CD gains; generic integration. Cons: missing end-to-end complexity; figure readability; modest SGD gains; minor typos/notation. Overall, a strong and interesting submission.
