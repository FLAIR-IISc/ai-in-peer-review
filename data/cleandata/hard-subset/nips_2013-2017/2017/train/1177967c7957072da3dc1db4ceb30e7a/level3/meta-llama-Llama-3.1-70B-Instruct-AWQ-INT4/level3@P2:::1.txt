This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified and provide an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds. The method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.

The paper is well written and a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solution. The technical contributions are well explained and easy to follow. The paper also includes a thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed method on several machine learning tasks.

The main idea of the paper is to formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to compute the solution. The authors show that the proposed sampling distribution is the best possible with respect to the limited information at hand and that it always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling.

The paper has several strengths. First, the proposed method is efficient and has negligible extra computational cost. Second, the method is generic and can easily be integrated into existing algorithms. Third, the paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed method on several machine learning tasks.

However, there are a few minor issues with the paper. First, the proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*. Second, Example 3.1 is confusing and seems to contradict the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal. Third, Algorithm 4 has a questionable logic in line 4 and a likely typo in line 7. Fourth, the numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling. Finally, the paper does not provide timing results for the SGD experiments.

In terms of the quality of the paper, I would say that it is technically sound and well written. The claims are well supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is a complete piece of work and not just a position paper. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well organized and easy to follow. The writing is clear and concise, and the notation is well defined. The paper adequately informs the reader and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver. The paper also provides a new perspective on the problem of importance sampling and proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in machine learning and provides a significant contribution to the field. The results are important and can be used to improve the performance of CD and SGD algorithms. The paper also provides a new tool for practitioners and researchers to use in their work.

Overall, I would recommend acceptance of the paper. The paper is well written, technically sound, and provides a significant contribution to the field. The minor issues with the paper can be easily fixed, and the paper is a valuable addition to the importance sampling literature.
