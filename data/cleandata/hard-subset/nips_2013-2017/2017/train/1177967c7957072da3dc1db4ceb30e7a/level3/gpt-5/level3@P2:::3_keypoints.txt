- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that uses only safe lower/upper bounds on per-coordinate gradients (CD) or per-component gradient norms (SGD).
- Sampling is chosen by solving a convex min–max over the uncertainty set; the resulting p̂k is worst-case optimal given the bounds and matches gradient-based sampling when bounds are exact.
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute p̂k and shows cheap bound maintenance for GLMs.
- Empirically yields strong improvements for CD across losses/regularizers and smaller but consistent gains for SGD.
- Connects to fixed importance sampling (Nesterov; Richtárik & Takáč), adaptive gradient-based sampling (Zhao & Zhang; Zhu), and gap/adaptive CD sampling (Perekrestenko et al.; Csiba et al.).
- Conceptual advance: principled min–max formulation with a tight analytic solution and efficient algorithm.
- Strong theoretical guarantees: worst-case optimality, dominance over uniform and Li-based sampling, and recovery of full-gradient sampling.
- Practicality: modest overhead overall and negligible in regimes with d = Ω(n); GLM bound updates are simple.
- Broad applicability: integrates into both CD and SGD and applies to many convex objectives.
- Experiments: consistent CD improvements and informative visualizations of adaptive stepsizes vk.
- Performance depends on the tightness of safe bounds; more ablation on bound quality effects is needed.
- O(n log n) per-iteration cost may be significant for very large n and small d; discuss partial/amortized updates.
- SGD benefits reported are modest; recommend experiments on datasets with larger gradient-norm variability or finite-sum/composite settings.
- Clarity issues: figures in Section 5 are hard to read and need larger labels/consistent axes.
- Algorithm 4 notation confusion: symbol “u” used for both an index and the upper-bound vector; initialization when u0 = ∞ needs explicit handling (finite surrogates for sorting).
- Provide an explicit SGD algorithm box analogous to Algorithm 2 (currently only described in text).
- Theory: explicitly state convexity/compactness/continuity conditions for the min–max interchange (Sion/Komiya) used in (7).
- Reportable metrics: provide a computable proxy or empirical reporting of the competitive ratio ρk (wk, vk) for diagnostics.
- Overall quality: careful theoretical development (progress bound, safety/dominance, constructive algorithm) and sound GLM arguments.
- Clarity: generally readable but notation overload and some figure/readability problems remain.
- Originality: novel safe min–max formulation and O(n log n) solution distinct from prior adaptive sampling methods.
- Significance: likely adoptable in coordinate-wise methods due to plug-in nature and safety guarantees; potential broader impact for minibatch/distributed selection.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, provably safe adaptive sampling with a practical algorithm and clear CD empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: limited SGD improvements, dependence on bound tightness, and some clarity/organization issues to fix.
- Key questions for authors: ablation on bound tightness effects; handling u0 = ∞ in Algorithm 4; prospects for amortized/partial p̂k updates; include an SGD algorithm box and experiments with greater gradient-norm skew.
- Minor comments: unify notation (avoid symbol reuse), fix typos, and ensure consistent wall-clock/epoch reporting across machines.
