This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors provide a novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver. The method is shown to be provably optimal relative to the provided bounds and always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling with negligible extra computational cost.

The paper is well-written and provides a valuable contribution to the importance sampling literature. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their proposed sampling scheme through extensive numerical testing. The results show that adaptive importance sampling significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time.

However, there are a few issues that need to be addressed. Lemma 2.1 proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*. Example 3.1 is confusing, as maximizing in (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal. Algorithm 4, line 4, may never be satisfied due to questionable logic, and line 7 likely contains a typo, which should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.

Additionally, the numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling. No timing results are provided for SGD experiments. There are also some reference issues, including an incorrect title for [14] and a missing reference to Csiba & Richtarik "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).

Despite these issues, the reviewer recommends acceptance of the paper. The paper's strengths, including its novel formulation of optimal sampling and efficient solver, outweigh its weaknesses. The authors' rebuttal has addressed some of the concerns, and the reviewer believes that the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field.
