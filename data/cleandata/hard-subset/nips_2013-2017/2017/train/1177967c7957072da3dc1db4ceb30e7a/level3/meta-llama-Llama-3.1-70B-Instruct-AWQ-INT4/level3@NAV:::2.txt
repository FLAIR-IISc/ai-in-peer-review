This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified, but its computation is often inadmissible. To address this issue, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient, which can be efficiently computed and updated during optimization.

The paper's main contributions are:

1. The proposal of a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme that is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.
2. The development of an efficient algorithm to compute the optimal sampling distribution, which can be implemented at no extra computational cost.
3. The demonstration of the scheme's effectiveness on generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization.

The paper's strengths include:

* The proposal of a novel and efficient importance sampling scheme that can be used in CD and SGD algorithms.
* The provision of theoretical guarantees for the scheme's performance, including a competitive ratio analysis.
* The demonstration of the scheme's effectiveness on real-world datasets.

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The assumption that safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed and updated during optimization, which may not always be the case.
* The lack of a comprehensive analysis of the scheme's computational complexity, including the cost of computing and updating the bounds.
* The limited evaluation of the scheme's performance, which is mostly focused on generalized linear models.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. However, the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive analysis of the scheme's computational complexity and a more extensive evaluation of its performance on different datasets and models.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel importance sampling scheme that is different from previous approaches. The use of safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient is a new idea, and the development of an efficient algorithm to compute the optimal sampling distribution is a significant contribution.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, namely the development of efficient and effective importance sampling schemes for CD and SGD algorithms. The proposed scheme has the potential to improve the performance of these algorithms in practice, and the paper's contributions are likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners in the field.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper for publication, subject to minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
