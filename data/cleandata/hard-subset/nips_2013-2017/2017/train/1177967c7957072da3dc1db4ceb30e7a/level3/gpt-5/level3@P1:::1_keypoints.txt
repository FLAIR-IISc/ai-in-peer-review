- Proposal: safe adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration bounds on gradient magnitudes and a convex min–max over sampling probabilities and admissible gradients.  
- Main technical contributions: worst-case optimal sampling p̂, O(n log n) solver via thresholding structure c_i = clamp(√Li m, ℓ_i, u_i), and cheap bound maintenance for GLMs.  
- CD results: theoretical up-to-n speedups, recovers full-gradient sampling when bounds collapse, and strong empirical gains in iterations and wall-clock.  
- SGD results: method applies but empirical gains are smaller in reported experiments.  
- Novelty: “safe” worst-case optimality relative to bounds plus practical O(n log n) algorithm appears new and differentiates from prior adaptive methods.  
- Theory quality: min–max formulation, geometric characterization, and thresholding solution are elegant and useful.  
- Critical theoretical bug: Lemma 2.1 has a factor-of-2 error — the expected decrease should be A^2/(2B) with α* = A/B; fix formulas and stepsize discussion accordingly.  
- Minimax justification: use of Sion’s theorem is appropriate but the paper should state the exact convexity/concavity and compactness conditions in the main text.  
- Example 3.1 clarity: clarify that naive choices of ℓ or u are suboptimal while the inner maximizer is a structured clamped vector determined by m.  
- Algorithm 4 issues: incorrect initialization/logic for m (line 4), typo csort/usort (line 7), and need to clarify index mapping after sorting; revise pseudocode and add a short correctness/termination proof.  
- Complexity/practicality: O(n log n) overhead is reasonable when gradient costs dominate but can be non-negligible for very sparse/low-dimensional problems — discuss regimes, amortization, or periodic updates.  
- Clarity suggestions: tighten exposition around (6)–(9), define vk and step-size role earlier, ensure consistent notation (bold vs scalar), and add pseudocode for bound updates with numerical safeguards.  
- Empirical suggestions: compare against gap-based CD, stale-gradient adaptive baselines, and Li-based sampling; report SGD wall-clock timings and analyze when SGD benefits are larger (e.g., high gradient-norm heterogeneity).  
- Minor fixes: correct reference title [14], add Csiba & Richtárik (2016) importance-sampling-for-minibatches, fix typos and notation inconsistencies in Section 4 and Algorithm 4.  
- Acceptance arguments: original, principled, efficient method with strong theory and practical CD gains; easy to integrate and likely impactful.  
- Rejection concerns: small but important theory fix, Algorithm 4 pseudocode issues, and need for more comprehensive SGD/ baseline experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept after addressing the factor-of-2 theory error, Algorithm 4 clarifications, and suggested empirical/completeness fixes.
