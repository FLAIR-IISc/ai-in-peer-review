- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using a minimax formulation.  
- Chooses sampling distributions via efficiently computable safe lower/upper bounds on gradients.  
- Guarantees the resulting sampling is provably never worse than fixed importance sampling.  
- Tackles the practical inefficiency of adaptive, gradient-based sampling and poor approximations.  
- Significant and original: principled worst-case performance guarantee bridging optimal sampling and heuristics.  
- High technical quality with theoretical proofs of optimality and superiority to fixed sampling.  
- Strong empirical validation on real datasets; method is generic and integrable with CD/SGD for GLMs.  
- Well-written and logically structured presentation of motivation, method, theory, and experiments.  
- Weakness: figures in Section 5 are hard to read due to small plots, labels, and legends.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/demonstration of maintaining gradient bounds for more complex models (e.g., deep networks).  
- Overall: strong, practical, theoretically grounded contribution with minor, fixable issues; recommend acceptance.
