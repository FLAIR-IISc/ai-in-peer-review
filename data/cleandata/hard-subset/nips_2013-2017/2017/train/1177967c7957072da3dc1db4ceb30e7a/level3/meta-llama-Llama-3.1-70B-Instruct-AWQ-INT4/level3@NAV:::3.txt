This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified and can lead to significant improvements over static strategies. However, they also acknowledge that computing the optimal adaptive sampling distribution is often computationally infeasible.

To address this issue, the authors propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling, which is based on safe bounds on the gradient. They show that the proposed sampling distribution is provably the best sampling with respect to the given bounds, always better than uniform sampling and fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed in many applications at negligible extra cost.

The paper provides both theoretical results and practical experiments supporting the proposed strategy. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed sampling scheme on generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization and show that it outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time.

The main strengths of the paper are:

* The proposed adaptive importance sampling scheme is generic and can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD algorithms.
* The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed scheme, including a proof of its optimality with respect to the given bounds.
* The paper includes extensive numerical experiments demonstrating the efficiency of the proposed scheme on real-world datasets.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that safe upper and lower bounds on the gradient can be efficiently computed, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed scheme, which could be an important consideration in practice.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing adaptive importance sampling schemes.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, and the proposed adaptive importance sampling scheme has the potential to be widely adopted in practice.
