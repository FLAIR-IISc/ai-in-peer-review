- Introduces safe adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using safe gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Provides computationally efficient scheme with provable theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical validation.  
- Builds on prior work in importance/adaptive sampling and coordinate descent, addressing the trade-off between guarantees and feasibility.  
- Technically sound: clear theoretical analysis (notably Theorem 3.2) with rigorous proofs in the appendix.  
- Authors acknowledge assumptions (safe gradient bounds) and discuss their implications.  
- Computational complexity analysis is thorough and realistic.  
- Generally well-written and organized, though notation can be dense for non-experts.  
- Algorithm and theory are clearly explained; derivation of optimal sampling could use more detail.  
- Novel approach: formulates adaptive sampling as a convex optimization using safe gradient bounds; novel use of Lemma 2.4.  
- Significant contribution for accelerating CD/SGD in large-scale ML where gradients are costly.  
- Demonstrated speed-ups and practical applicability make adoption likely.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation with clear guarantees.  
- Strength: minimal computational overhead and practical applicability.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments linking theory and implementation.  
- Weakness: Typo — Line 85 denominator of p^*_i should be x_k, not x.  
- Weakness: Consistency — remove subscript k for \hat p and \hat c at Line 135.  
- Weakness: Figures have small/missing ticks and labels; readability issues and some should be moved to appendix.  
- Weakness: Missing direct comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n).  
- Weakness: Need more detailed discussion of total complexity including cost of updating l_k and u_k to reach ε-optimality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, theoretically sound, computationally efficient, strong experiments, well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: figure readability, missing uniform baseline, and need for more detailed complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (fix typo/consistency, improve figures, add uniform comparison, expand complexity analysis).
