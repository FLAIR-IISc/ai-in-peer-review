- Problem: Tackles safe adaptive importance sampling by approximating gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds to accelerate CD and SGD.  
- Proposed method: Introduces a novel, generic sampling scheme that uses safe gradient bounds to compute adaptive sampling distributions efficiently and integrate into existing algorithms.  
- Theoretical guarantee: Shows the method is provably no worse than fixed importance sampling and outperforms uniform sampling while remaining computationally efficient.  
- Empirical validation: Provides extensive experiments on real datasets demonstrating significant speed-ups in iterations and computation time, particularly for coordinate descent.  
- Strength — Quality: Technically sound with clear math, theoretical proofs, and reproducible experiments; safe bounds add robustness to adaptive sampling.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel use of safe gradient bounds to compute adaptive sampling with a guarantee it never underperforms fixed importance sampling.  
- Strength — Significance: Broad practical implications for large-scale ML; compatible with CD and SGD and shows meaningful performance gains.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written and organized with sufficient algorithmic and dataset details for reproducibility.  
- Weakness — Figures: Section 5 figures are small and hard to read; need larger, clearer labels to improve result presentation.  
- Weakness — SGD results: Improvement for SGD is limited compared to CD and requires deeper analysis to explain the discrepancy.  
- Weakness — Gradient-bound initialization: Claim of no initialization needs more discussion on effects during early optimization iterations.  
- For acceptance: Addresses a relevant challenge with a novel, theoretically grounded, practical, and empirically validated solution that benefits optimization methods.  
- Against acceptance: Poorly readable figures, limited demonstrated gains for SGD (raising generalizability concerns), and insufficient analysis of gradient-bound initialization.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — fix figure clarity and provide more analysis on SGD performance and gradient-bound initialization.
