- Introduces a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that uses upper/lower gradient bounds to compute sampling distributions efficiently.  
- Key idea: optimize over bounded gradient information to produce a sampling distribution provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Avoids expensive full-gradient computations required by prior gradient-based sampling methods, using a computable safe approximation instead.  
- Theoretical guarantees are strong (Theorem 3.2) showing improvement over Li-based sampling and additional worst-case analysis via competitive ratio (Lemmas 3.5, 3.6).  
- Computational cost per iteration is O(n log n), comparable to evaluating one gradient component, making the method practically appealing.  
- Numerical experiments are comprehensive and show significant practical speedups across datasets and problem types, though benefits are smaller for SGD than for CD.  
- The approach is generic and demonstrated on generalized linear models; optimization formulation (7) is novel.  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported analysis and appropriate acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Clarity issues to fix: typo on line 85 (denominator of p*_i), inconsistent subscripts on line 135, and poor figure readability (small ticks/labels).  
- Suggested additions: include a theoretical comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n), discuss total complexity including O(n) cost to compute bounds l_k and u_k, and detail conditions for maintaining safe bounds beyond GLMs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, practical benefits, general applicability, and minimal overhead.  
- Arguments against acceptance: figure readability, lack of explicit uniform-sampling comparison, and incomplete total complexity analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address technical fixes, figure improvements, and suggested additional analyses.
