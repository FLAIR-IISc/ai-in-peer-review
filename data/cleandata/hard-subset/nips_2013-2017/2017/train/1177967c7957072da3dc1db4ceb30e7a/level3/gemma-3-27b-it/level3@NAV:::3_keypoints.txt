- Problem: addresses safe adaptive importance sampling for large-scale ML datasets.
- Proposal: novel, efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.
- Theoretical guarantees: provably better than uniform sampling with gains over fixed sampling; convex formulation and efficient Algorithm 4.
- Builds on prior work and addresses limitations of existing adaptive methods (gradient-based and duality-gap-based sampling).
- Balances theoretical guarantees with computational feasibility.
- Generality: method integrates into existing algorithms and applies to a wide range of problems.
- Empirical results: comprehensive experiments show practical benefits, particularly for coordinate descent (CD).
- SGD results: performance gains for SGD are more modest than for CD and need further analysis.
- Presentation issue: figures in Section 5 are small and unclear and should be enlarged/clarified.
- Arguments for acceptance: timely problem, novel theory with provable guarantees, practical improvements, and general applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: figure readability and limited SGD gains.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve figure clarity.
