- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme that approximates gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.  
- Targets acceleration of coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with provable guarantees.  
- Addresses practical limitations of full-gradient-based adaptive sampling by using computationally efficient bounds.  
- Theoretical strength: Theorem 3.2 shows the proposed sampling outperforms uniform sampling and is optimal given the safe bounds.  
- Provides algorithms for efficient computation (e.g., Algorithm 4) and discusses computational complexity.  
- Empirical validation on GLMs across datasets and regularizers shows reduction in iterations and total computation time.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents motivation, method, theory, and experiments.  
- Claim of practicality: method is computationally efficient and easy to integrate into existing optimizers.  
- Weakness: figures suffer from small fonts and missing labels; readability would improve by moving some figures to the appendix.  
- Weakness: lacks a direct empirical comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n) as suggested.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed discussion and quantification of the overhead for maintaining safe gradient bounds (updates of ` and `u) and total cost to reach ε-optimality.  
- Reviewer recommendation: overall strong paper with minor issues; recommends acceptance.
