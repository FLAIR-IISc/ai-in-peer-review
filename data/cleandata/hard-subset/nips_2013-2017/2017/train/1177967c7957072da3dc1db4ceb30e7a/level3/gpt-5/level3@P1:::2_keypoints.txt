- Problem and goal: Close the gap between ideal adaptive importance sampling and its computational infeasibility by using safe per-iteration bounds to compute a best-compatible sampling distribution.  
- Main idea: Formulate sampling with partial information as a convex min-max and solve it to get a provably safe adaptive sampling distribution.  
- Algorithmic result: Provides an exact O(n log n) per-iteration algorithm (Theorem 3.4) to compute the sampling distribution from bounds.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Never worse than fixed (Li-based) importance sampling and recovers optimal gradient-based sampling with full information (Theorem 3.2).  
- Progress bound: Theorem 3.2 shows improved expected progress over fixed distributions and yields an adaptive stepsize αk = 1/vk.  
- Empirical CD gains: Significant acceleration for coordinate descent on GLMs (square, squared hinge, logistic; L1/L2) in iterations and wall-clock time.  
- Empirical SGD gains: Modest improvements for SGD, consistent with prior observations that gradient-based sampling helps less for SGD.  
- Practicality: Simple to integrate in CD/SGD; safe bounds are cheap to maintain for GLMs and similar models.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and contrasts with existing importance sampling (Richtárik & Takáč, Zhao & Zhang, Csiba et al., Osokin et al.) by providing a computationally efficient, safe distribution.  
- Novelty: The safe-bounds min-max formulation and its exact efficient solution appear novel and distinctive.  
- Significance: High impact for CD, moderate for SGD; likely to influence practical implementations where partial gradient info is cheap.  
- Complexity critique: Paper focuses on per-iteration cost; reviewer asks for end-to-end ε-suboptimality complexity including O(n) updates of bounds and O(n log n) sampling cost, and relation to dimension d.  
- Uniform sampling comparison: Request for explicit corollary showing when and by how much safe sampling improves over uniform sampling.  
- Robustness to loose/violated bounds: Reviewer asks for discussion and ablation on how vk and competitive ratio behave when bounds are loose and how they tighten over time.  
- Amortization suggestion: When d ≪ n, recommend discussing recomputing p̂ every m iterations and quantifying the trade-off.  
- SGD guidance: Request identification of problem settings (e.g., heteroscedastic gradients, skewed Lipschitz constants) where the method yields larger SGD gains or combination with variance reduction.  
- Empirical suggestions: Ablation on bound tightness and frequency of recomputing p̂ would strengthen the practical story.  
- Clarity issues: Notational inconsistencies (use of c, ck, hats) and small/illegible figure axes; suggest unifying notation and improving figure readability.  
- Specific typos: Fix p* denominator to use xk (Line 85), remove inconsistent subscripts/hats (Line 135), and check norm types and signs/factors in Equation (3).  
- Acceptance arguments pro: Novel, principled, efficient, strong theory, clear empirical CD improvements, and easy to integrate.  
- Acceptance arguments con: Limited empirical SGD gains, incomplete end-to-end complexity analysis, and figure/notation issues.  
- Overall verdict: Technically solid and practically impactful for CD; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending improved clarity and explicit total-complexity and uniform-comparison discussions.
