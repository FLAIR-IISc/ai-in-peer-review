- Introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.
- Formulates optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm to solve it.
- Theoretically guaranteed to outperform uniform or fixed importance sampling given the gradient bounds.
- Computational overhead is negligible, making the method practical for large-scale problems.
- Applicable to both coordinate descent and stochastic gradient descent, and suitable for distributed or resource-limited settings.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and connects effectively to existing literature on importance sampling.
- Mathematical derivations are generally sound and the algorithm is presented clearly.
- Original contribution: framing adaptive sampling from gradient bounds as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.
- Significant potential impact: practical acceleration of optimization algorithms and wide applicability.
- Lemma 2.1 contains a suspected error: optimal stepsize missing a factor of 2.
- Example 3.1 is confusing and needs clarification about optimality relative to bounds.
- Algorithm 4 has logical errors in the conditions on lines 4 and 7 that require correction.
- Empirical evaluation is incomplete: missing comparisons to other adaptive methods ([2], [5], [21]), non-uniform fixed sampling, and timing results for SGD.
- Minor reference issues: incorrect title for [14] and missing Csiba & Richtarik paper ([1]).
- Rebuttal addressed concerns, and identified weaknesses are judged fixable.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing mathematical/algorithmic errors and augmenting empirical comparisons.
