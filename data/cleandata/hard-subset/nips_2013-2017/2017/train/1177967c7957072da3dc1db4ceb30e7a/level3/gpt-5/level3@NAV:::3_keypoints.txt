- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using per-coordinate/-sample safe bounds and a convex min–max to get a provably optimal sampling distribution.  
- Avoids full gradient recomputation by maintaining bounds and solves sampling in O(n log n) per iteration.  
- Method dominates uniform and fixed Li-based importance sampling and recovers gradient-based sampling in the full-information limit.  
- Theory is solid: worst-case optimality under bounds, guarantees vs. fixed sampling, and competitive-ratio analysis are carefully executed.  
- O(n log n) algorithm construction and correctness are convincing; practical bound maintenance for GLMs is clearly linked.  
- Experiments show strong speedups for CD on rcv1/real-sim/news20 and modest, theory-consistent gains for SGD.  
- Well written and organized overall, but figures in Section 5 are hard to read; request larger plots and axis labels.  
- Suggest adding clearer pseudocode with complexity, and a table of symbols for clarity.  
- Framing adaptive sampling under safe bounds as a min–max problem and deriving worst-case optimal distribution appears novel.  
- Approach is generic, easy to integrate, practically relevant for large-scale convex learning; “always at least as good as fixed sampling” is a strong selling point.  
- Suggested experiments/analyses: time-breakdowns to quantify O(n log n) overhead vs. n and d, ablation on bound tightness vs. speedup (including empirical ρk).  
- Suggested baselines/discussion: compare to stale-gradient and Gauss–Southwell/approximate steepest-CD; discuss minibatch SGD and non-convex models.  
- Clarify memory costs and initialization of bounds; release code for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: timely topic, generic method with clear theoretical advantages, strong analysis, efficient algorithm, and empirical gains (esp. CD).  
- Arguments against acceptance: bound construction currently limited mainly to GLMs, modest SGD benefits, missing strong baselines/ablations, and figure readability issues.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept.
