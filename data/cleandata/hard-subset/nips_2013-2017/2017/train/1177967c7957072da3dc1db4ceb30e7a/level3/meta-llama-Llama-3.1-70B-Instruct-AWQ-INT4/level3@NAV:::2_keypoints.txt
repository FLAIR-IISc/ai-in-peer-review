- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that uses safe lower and upper gradient bounds to approximate optimal sampling.  
- Claims the scheme is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides an efficient algorithm to compute the optimal sampling distribution, claimed to incur no extra computational cost.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness empirically on generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization.  
- Novel and efficient sampling method applicable to CD and SGD.  
- Offers theoretical guarantees, including a competitive ratio analysis.  
- Shows empirical gains on real-world datasets.  
- Relies on the assumption that safe gradient bounds can be efficiently computed and updated, which may not always hold.  
- Lacks a comprehensive computational complexity analysis, particularly the cost of computing/updating bounds.  
- Evaluation is limited, focusing mainly on generalized linear models.  
- Technically sound, clearly written, and well-organized, but would benefit from broader evaluation and complexity analysis.  
- Original idea with potential practical impact; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
