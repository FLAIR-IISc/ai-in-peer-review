- Proposes an adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD/SGD that uses safe per-coordinate gradient bounds and a minimax problem to compute a sampling distribution p̂ efficiently (O(n log n)).  
- p̂ is optimal given the bounds, guarantees expected surrogate progress never worse than Li-based or uniform sampling, and recovers true gradient-based sampling when bounds are exact; step size α̂k = 1/vk.  
- Connects to and extends prior gradient-based and duality-gap-based sampling methods by providing a principled safe-bound minimax instead of heuristic/stale-value approximations.  
- Provides an efficient algorithmic construction (thresholding/sorting) supported by a Sion-type minimax argument (Theorem 3.4).  
- Theoretical result: full-information gradient-based sampling can yield up to an n-fold speed-up for CD when Li = L.  
- Shows how to maintain safe bounds cheaply for GLMs using gradient tracking and Cauchy–Schwarz, keeping per-iteration overhead negligible when d = Ω(n).  
- Empirically demonstrates strong, practical speed-ups for CD on rcv1, real-sim, news20; only modest gains observed for SGD (authors acknowledge this).  
- Theory uses the standard one-step surrogate progress model; the “always better” guarantee applies to the surrogate, not directly to realized f decrease.  
- Algorithmic analysis (V(p,c) objective, minimax over [`,u]) is well-motivated; thresholding at √(Li m) is elegant and competitive ratio bounds are informative.  
- Presentation issues: figures in Section 5 are hard to read, main convergence rates are buried in the appendix, and an explicit SGD pseudocode is missing.  
- Originality: the safe minimax formulation with efficient computation and uniform-improvement guarantees appears novel compared to prior heuristic uses of bounds.  
- Significance: method is generic and easily pluggable, with clear practical impact for coordinate descent and convex GLMs; limited immediate impact for SGD without better bounds.  
- Strength: principled bridging of infeasible gradient-based sampling and practical implementability with provable guarantees.  
- Strength: strong theoretical properties (optimal under bounds, strictly better surrogate progress than fixed sampling) and efficient computation.  
- Strength: practical engineering for maintaining bounds in GLMs and solid CD empirical improvements.  
- Weakness: O(n log n) per-iteration overhead may be costly for extremely large n or when d ≪ n; need discussion of amortization and isolated timing.  
- Weakness: reliance on Cauchy–Schwarz can produce loose bounds; recommend ablations with tighter bound techniques to quantify trade-offs.  
- Weakness: limited SGD benefits and limited comparisons to adaptive baselines (e.g., Zhao & Zhang ’15, non-uniform SAG/SAGA, AdaSDCA); suggest more challenging SGD scenarios.  
- Weakness: scope restricted to convex problems; paper should discuss implications and feasibility for nonconvex settings (e.g., deep models).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled, provably safe and efficient approach with strong CD gains and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest SGD improvements, potential practical overheads, and guarantees only for a surrogate objective rather than tightened end-to-end rates.  
- Key questions for authors: can p̂ be updated less frequently (guidance on τ)? how sensitive are results to bound tightness and can bounds be adaptively refined? how to integrate with variance reduction or minibatching? any memory/sparsity concerns for very high-dimensional sparse data?
