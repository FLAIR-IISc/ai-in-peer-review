- Summary: Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD/SGD by formulating optimal sampling under gradient bounds as a convex problem and solving it in O(n log n), with provable improvements over uniform/static schemes and efficient GLM maintenance.

- Strength: Originality/technical quality — novel minimax formulation and a non-trivial, efficient O(n log n) solver (Algorithm 4).

- Strength: Theoretical guarantees — sampling is provably safe and always better than Li-based fixed importance (and uniform in the L-smooth case).

- Strength: Clarity/presentation — well-written, logically organized, and effectively motivates the approach from ideal to practical solutions.

- Strength: Practical significance — low computational overhead (O(n log n) per iteration), making it practical for high-dimensional problems.

- Weakness: Theoretical details — possible missing factor of 2 in Lemma 2.1's α* derivation; Example 3.1’s motivation for (7) is confusing and needs clearer explanation; Algorithm 4 loop/initialization logic appears questionable and should be clarified.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation — lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2],[5],[21]); timing results missing for SGD experiments; comparisons could include more non-uniform fixed-sampling heuristics.

- Weakness: Referencing — incorrect title for reference [14] and omission of Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" which should be cited and discussed.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with suggested revisions to clarify theory, broaden experimental baselines/timings (especially SGD), and fix references.
