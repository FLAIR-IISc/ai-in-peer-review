- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 3.2) shows the sampling always outperforms Li-based (and thus uniform when Li equal) importance sampling.  
- Sampling distribution is efficiently computable in O(n log n) time per iteration.  
- Connects to prior adaptive/gradient-based sampling work (Zhu 2013; Zhao & Zhang 2015) but adds provable worst-case guarantees.  
- Competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5, 3.6) further strengthens the theoretical foundation.  
- Practical to implement with minimal overhead; experiments on rcv1, real-sim, news20 validate benefits.  
- Approach is generic and applicable to many ML problems (generalized linear models, various losses/regularizers).  
- Notation issues and minor typos reduce clarity (e.g., Line 85 denominator typo).  
- Figures suffer from poor readability (small ticks/labels) and should be improved or moved to the appendix.  
- Paper lacks an explicit, direct comparison to uniform sampling in experiments.  
- Complexity analysis omits discussion of total cost to reach ε-optimality, particularly maintaining gradient bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel provable guarantees, low overhead, general applicability, supported by experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient emphasis on uniform baseline, presentation issues, and incomplete total complexity discussion.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contribution with minor fixable issues; recommended for acceptance.
