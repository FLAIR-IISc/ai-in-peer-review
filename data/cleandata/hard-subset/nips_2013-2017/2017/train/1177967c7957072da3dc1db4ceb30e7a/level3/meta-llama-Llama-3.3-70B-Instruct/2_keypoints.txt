- Analyzes limitations of gradient descent with smooth kernels and shows its computational reach is limited to very smooth functions.  
- Demonstrates that approximating less smooth functions requires a large number of GD iterations.  
- Proposes EigenPro, a novel preconditioning scheme that significantly improves GD convergence, especially on large datasets.  
- Experimental results are impressive and show EigenPro’s effectiveness across several large datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with a clear introduction and thorough related-work overview.  
- Strength: provides a clear, concise theoretical analysis of GD limitations and a novel, well-evaluated solution.  
- Weakness: figures are too small and lack visibility; recommend moving some to the appendix and enlarging key figures.  
- Weakness: needs a more detailed comparison of the new sampling method versus uniform sampling (relate to Theorem 3.2, Conclusion 2).  
- Weakness: should discuss the impact of the new sampling method on total complexity of RCD/SGD, including time to reach an ε-optimal solution.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution; minor revisions would make it an excellent paper.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
