- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds with provable guarantees.  
- Theoretical results (notably Theorem 3.2) show superiority over fixed importance sampling and are rigorously derived.  
- Algorithm is computationally tractable and designed to have minimal overhead compared to standard sampling methods.  
- Empirical validation on real datasets demonstrates practical benefits, especially for coordinate descent.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and effectively situates itself within prior work on adaptive importance sampling.  
- Strength: Novel, principled approach that balances efficiency and provable performance.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical soundness with compelling proofs of advantage over fixed sampling.  
- Strength: Practicality—algorithm implementation and complexity discussion are insightful.  
- Strength: Extensive experiments provide convincing empirical support.  
- Strength: Generally clear writing and explanations of concepts and algorithms.  
- Weakness: Figures are hard to read (small ticks/labels); suggest moving some to appendix for larger visuals.  
- Weakness: Lacks a direct comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n), which would clarify practical gains.  
- Weakness: Complexity analysis could be deeper, specifically total cost to update bounds l_k and u_k to reach ε-optimality.  
- Minor issues: Typo on Line 85 (denominator of p^*_i should be ‖x_k‖) and minor consistency on Line 135 (remove subscript k from \hat p and \hat c).  
- Recommendation: Accept—significant contributions, high technical quality, practical impact; addressing figure readability, uniform baseline, and complexity detail would further improve the paper.
