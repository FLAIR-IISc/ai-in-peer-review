## Review: Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling

This paper presents a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for accelerating coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The core idea is to approximate gradient-based sampling using safe bounds on the gradient, resulting in a computationally efficient method with provable guarantees. The paper builds upon existing work in adaptive importance sampling, addressing the practical limitations of full-gradient-based approaches. The contribution is significant, offering a theoretically sound and practically viable method to improve the speed of fundamental optimization algorithms.

The paper is well-structured, clearly outlining the motivation, proposed method, theoretical analysis, and experimental validation. The theoretical results, particularly Theorem 3.2 establishing the superiority of the proposed sampling over uniform sampling, are a key strength. The authors convincingly demonstrate that their approach is "the best possible" given the limited information available (safe bounds). The inclusion of algorithms for efficient computation (Algorithm 4) and discussion of computational complexity are commendable. The empirical evaluation on GLMs with various datasets and regularization techniques provides strong support for the theoretical findings, showcasing the benefits in both iteration count and total computation time. 

However, the paper could benefit from some improvements. The figures, as noted by the authors themselves, suffer from small font sizes and missing labels. Moving some figures to the appendix would improve readability. The connection between the proposed approach and existing uniform sampling is nicely established for L-based sampling, but a similar direct comparison with uniform sampling (p = 1/n) as suggested would strengthen the results. Finally, a more detailed discussion on the practical implications of maintaining the safe gradient bounds (e.g., the overhead of updating ` and `u`) and its impact on the overall complexity for achieving an ε-optimal solution would be valuable. While the authors mention the O(n) update cost, quantifying the total cost more explicitly would be useful.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed safe adaptive sampling scheme is a novel approach to address the computational limitations of existing gradient-based methods.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis, demonstrating the benefits of the proposed method.
*   **Practicality:** The proposed algorithm is computationally efficient and can be readily integrated into existing optimization algorithms.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental results confirm the effectiveness of the proposed method on real datasets.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Figure Readability:** The figures require improvement in terms of font size and labeling.
*   **Missing Comparison:** Direct comparison to uniform sampling is missing.
*   **Complexity Discussion:** A more thorough discussion of the total computational complexity, including the overhead of maintaining safe bounds, is needed.



Overall, this is a strong paper with significant potential impact. Addressing the minor weaknesses would further enhance its quality and make it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
