- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- The sampling distribution is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds and always improves over uniform/fixed importance sampling.  
- The method is formulated as a convex optimization problem solved efficiently (Algorithm 4) with O(n log n) cost for computing the distribution.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (notably Lemma 3.2) and empirical validation showing minimal overhead.  
- Approach is general and applicable to both coordinate descent and stochastic gradient descent.  
- Novel formulation reconciling theoretical optimality and practical feasibility for adaptive sampling.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically well-explained overall.  
- Significant contribution to importance sampling literature by bridging theory and practicality.  
- Error in Lemma 2.1: missing factor of 2 in α* that needs correction and affects guarantees.  
- Example 3.1 is confusing and may contradict claims; it requires clarification.  
- Algorithm 4, line 4: condition `if m = max(l^{sort})` may never hold, risking an infinite loop.  
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely typo using `c^{sort}` instead of `u^{sort}`.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- Missing timing results for the SGD experiments, limiting assessment of practical efficiency.  
- Minor reference errors: incorrect title for [14] and omission of Csiba & Richtarik (2016).  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on fixing the lemma and Algorithm 4 bugs, and ideally strengthening empirical comparisons and adding SGD timings.
