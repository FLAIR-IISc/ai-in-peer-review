- Paper proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling": an efficient approximation to optimal gradient-based sampling using safe per-coordinate gradient bounds.
- Shows adaptive sampling can give up to n× speedups over fixed sampling but optimal requires full-gradient computation, so authors approximate it safely.
- Formulates sampling as convex optimization with safe upper/lower gradient bounds and gives an O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution.
- Theoretical guarantee (Theorem 3.2): proposed sampling is always better than fixed importance sampling.
- Novel insight: safe convex formulation of adaptive sampling is more principled than prior heuristic approaches.
- Provides practical methods to maintain safe gradient bounds for generalized linear models and discusses integration into CD and SGD.
- Empirical results across datasets/losses show clear benefits for coordinate descent and comparisons to multiple baselines.
- Strong, rigorous theoretical analysis with sound proofs and clear linkage to implementation.
- Clear distinction from prior adaptive sampling work and original contribution to the field.
- Limited empirical gains for SGD—only marginal improvements over uniform sampling, undermining claims of full generality.
- Assumes safe gradient bounds can be efficiently computed, but examples are mainly limited to generalized linear models; unclear extension to complex models (e.g., deep nets).
- O(n log n) overhead may be significant when n is very large and can be comparable to gradient computation cost when n ≫ d; complexity claim needs clarification.
- No ablation study to show sensitivity to gradient bound quality or to isolate contributions of method components.
- Gap between theoretical guarantee and empirical improvements not fully explained—the worst-case competitive analysis doesn’t map cleanly to practical gains.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-written, and significant for large-scale optimization—particularly valuable for coordinate descent.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to (1) explain limited SGD improvements, (2) discuss generalizability of gradient bounds beyond GLMs, (3) clarify complexity when n ≫ d, and (4) add an ablation study on gradient bound quality.
- Final note: safe adaptive sampling is a meaningful, practically implementable advance for CD, though SGD benefits and broader applicability require further clarification.
