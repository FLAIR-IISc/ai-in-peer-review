- Proposes a “safe” adaptive sampling for CD/SGD: choose p̂ by a min–max over per-coordinate gradient magnitude (or norm) intervals to minimize worst-case quadratic upper-bound V(p,c)/‖c‖^2.  
- Guarantees: p̂ is worst-case optimal given the interval information, never worse than fixed importance sampling, and recovers the full-information gradient-based distribution when exact gradients are known.  
- Provides an O(n log n) exact algorithm to compute p̂ and a corresponding stepsize α̂ = 1/vk, with a thresholding/clipping structure for ĉ.  
- Shows up to an n-fold one-step improvement over fixed distributions in favorable cases, connecting to Gauss–Southwell/steepest coordinate behavior.  
- Develops O(n) safe bound maintenance for GLMs and describes efficient updates using Cauchy–Schwarz; demonstrates practical strategies for bound upkeep.  
- Theory is sound: derivations of full-information optimal p* ∝ √L |∇f| and the min–max guarantees are well-motivated and consistent with the quadratic model.  
- Practicality concern: per-iteration overhead (O(n) bound maintenance and O(n log n) p̂ computation) may be heavy when n ≫ d or for sparse updates that normally cost ~nnz(ai_k).  
- Experiments: substantial iteration/time gains for CD on GLMs and text datasets; only modest gains for SGD, aligning with theory.  
- Empirical caveats: timing measurements not always on the same hardware and missing comparisons to other adaptive CD baselines (gap/violation-based, stale-gradient methods).  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized; min–max formulation and thresholding rule are clearly presented.  
- Clarity gaps: need clearer guidance on computational trade-offs, update frequency for p̂, and more concrete examples for bounding ∇^2h in general GLMs.  
- Algorithm detail: Algorithm 4 pseudocode is terse; a short proof sketch for the optimal form of ĉ in the main text would improve understanding.  
- Originality: the robust min–max sampling formulation under interval uncertainty and the O(n log n) exact solver appear novel in the NeurIPS literature.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for CD (strong gains) and moderate for SGD (smaller gains); the “never worse than fixed” guarantee is compelling.  
- Pros: clear theoretical motivation, recovers full-information optimum, elegant exact algorithm, robust to loose bounds, and strong CD empirical performance.  
- Cons: per-iteration overhead may limit applicability in common large-n sparse settings, limited empirical baselines, modest SGD benefits, and heterogeneous timing comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: study less frequent recomputation of p̂ (every T iterations), provide examples for tight Hessian-based bounds beyond least squares, exploit sparsity for selective bound updates, and add comparisons to gap/violation/stale-gradient baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance if practical overhead and additional adaptive-baseline comparisons are addressed, since the safe min–max sampling idea is novel, well-justified, and empirically promising for CD.
