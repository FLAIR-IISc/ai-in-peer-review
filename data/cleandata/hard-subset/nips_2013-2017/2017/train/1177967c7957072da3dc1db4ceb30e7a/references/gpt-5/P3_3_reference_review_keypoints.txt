- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampler for CD/SGD that uses lower/upper gradient bounds and solves a robust min–max to choose sampling probabilities.  
- The sampler maximizes worst-case one-step improvement under a quadratic smoothness model and recovers the full-information gradient-based sampler as bounds tighten.  
- Key technical result: the min–max reduces to a thresholding problem with an O(n log n) algorithm computing the worst-case gradients and optimal p̂k ∝ √L ĉ.  
- Guarantees: p̂k is worst-case optimal over the uncertainty box Ck and is never worse (in the same model) than fixed importance sampling pL ∝ L.  
- Per-iteration step-size is α̂k = 1/vk with importance-sampling scaling γk = α̂k/p̂k[i], matching standard importance-sampled updates.  
- Provides methods to maintain safe gradient bounds in GLMs in O(n) per iteration using smoothness and simple inequalities.  
- Experiments show substantial iteration/time gains for CD (close to full-information), but only modest gains for SGD.  
- Novelty: principled robust optimization of sampling under uncertainty with a “never worse than fixed” guarantee, differing from stale-gradient or heuristic adaptive schemes.  
- Theoretical derivations and the thresholding reduction are solid, but the min–max interchange and convexity/compactness conditions need explicit verification.  
- Notational inconsistencies and typos (e.g., definition of V(p,x), Algorithm 4 variable reuse, absolute vs signed gradients) hurt clarity and should be fixed.  
- Complexity caveat: O(n) bound maintenance and O(n log n) sampling may dominate cost in very sparse/high-dimensional CD where single updates are much cheaper.  
- Practical suggestions: amortize sampling updates (every T steps), use lazy/event-triggered updates, exploit sparsity or blocks, and report wall-clock overhead on sparse datasets.  
- Empirical suggestions: compare against stronger baselines (Gauss–Southwell-Lipschitz, duality-gap adaptive CD, stale-gradient samplers) to contextualize gains.  
- SGD section is underdeveloped theoretically and empirically; either strengthen the section (variance reduction, minibatching, tighter bounds) or de-emphasize SGD results.  
- Edge cases need clarification: behavior when initial bounds have ℓ=0, u=∞, and how vk and p̂k are well-defined initially (scale invariance, fallback to pL).  
- Overall assessment: meaningful and novel contribution with elegant algorithmic and theoretical elements, strong CD results, but practicality and presentation issues remain.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept leaning accept, contingent on addressing practicality, stronger empirical baselines, and tightening technical/presentation details.
