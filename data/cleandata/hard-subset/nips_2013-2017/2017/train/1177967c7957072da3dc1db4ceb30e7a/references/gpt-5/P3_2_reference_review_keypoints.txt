- Studies adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD; full-information optima are √Li|∇i f(x)| (CD) and ‖∇fi(x)‖ (SGD).
- Main contribution: a “safe” adaptive sampling that uses per-coordinate lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to avoid needing full gradient.
- Formulates a robust min–max problem over uncertainty set Ck to choose pk and stepsize αk, minimizing worst-case V(p,c)/‖c‖2.
- Shows the resulting distribution p̂k is worst-case optimal, never worse than Li-based fixed sampling, and recovers full-information optimum as bounds tighten.
- Provides an O(n log n) sorting-based algorithm (via KKT structure) to compute p̂k, with practical pseudo-code.
- Describes efficient ways to maintain safe bounds for GLMs using simple updates (e.g., Cauchy–Schwarz) for CD and SGD.
- Experiments: strong practical improvements for CD; modest gains for SGD.
- Relates to prior work: fixed non-uniform CD and adaptive sampling (duality gaps/gradient norms) are known; safe screening is different; the robust min–max approach appears novel.
- Theoretical analysis of per-iteration decrease for CD is standard and sound; full-information optimal pk matches known forms.
- Claims about worst-case guarantees and comparisons to Li-based sampling follow from the min–max setup and valid min–max interchange.
- Geometric reformulation √vk = maxc∈Ck ⟨√l, c⟩/‖c‖2 is insightful.
- The O(n log n) algorithm leveraging thresholding/clip structure is plausible, but variable naming (u,l) reuse should be cleaned.
- Convergence rates are only sketched; a full global convergence theorem with explicit constants is missing from the main text.
- Significance: addresses computational cost of adaptive sampling and offers a principled trade-off between information and safety; broadly useful for CD.
- Clarity: generally well-written, but some notation confusions (e.g., Hessian reference) and minor typos/formatting issues.
- Complexity caveat: per-iteration overhead O(n log n)+O(n) can be significant relative to a single CD/SGD update, especially in sparse settings.
- Empirical concerns: SGD gains modest; maintaining bounds O(n) per iteration undermines SGD scalability; practical variants (refresh frequency) suggested.
- Missing baselines: gap-based/violation-based adaptive CD, Gauss–Southwell/approximate steepest CD, and other adaptive SGD samplings.
- Timing concerns: reported timings use different machines; need controlled hardware runs and overhead breakdowns.
- Ablations requested: effect of bound quality (exact vs Cauchy–Schwarz), refresh frequency, and amortization strategies.
- Originality: robust min–max safe adaptive sampling with tight O(n log n) algorithm and guarantee of outperforming fixed sampling appears new.
- Pros: principled robust formulation, worst-case optimality, efficient algorithm, practical CD improvements across losses/regularizers.
- Cons: per-iteration overhead may be prohibitive, missing explicit global rates, limited experimental baselines and controlled timings, clarity/notation issues.
- Reviewer questions for authors: explicit convex/strongly-convex convergence rates; practical recomputation frequency and amortization trade-offs; maintaining bounds for general smooth h without O(nd); extensions to block/proximal/composite settings; comparison to gap-based and Gauss–Southwell methods.
- Minor requests: fix Algorithm 4 variable naming, equation formatting, clarify absolute values/sign handling in CD bounds, and provide code/reproducibility details.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
