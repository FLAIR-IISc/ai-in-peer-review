Summary: Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD that uses upper/lower gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling, with theory and practical algorithm showing strong gains for CD.

Relation to prior work: Builds on adaptive importance sampling literature, addressing the computational cost of full-gradient methods by using safe bounds to approximate gradient information.

Strength — Theoretical soundness: Provides rigorous analysis, derives optimal sampling and strong bounds (Lemma 2.1, Theorem 3.2).

Strength — Computational efficiency: Sampling distribution computed in O(n log n) and gradient bounds can be updated incrementally.

Strength — Generality: Applicable to a broad class of problems (CD, SGD) and compatible with common regularizers; demonstrated on GLMs.

Strength — Practical relevance: Empirical results validate theory, especially for coordinate descent on real datasets.

Strength — Clear writing: Well-organized paper with clear notation and readable derivations.

Weakness — SGD performance: Empirical improvements for SGD are modest compared to theoretical promises and CD results.

Weakness — Assumptions on gradient bounds: Reliance on safe gradient bounds may be hard to satisfy or generalize beyond GLMs.

Weakness — Competitive ratio interpretation: Analysis is abstract and lacks clear practical meaning in terms of iteration complexity reduction.

Weakness — Limited SGD experiments: Empirical evaluation is comprehensive for CD but limited for SGD and broader datasets/models.

Argument for acceptance: Novel, theoretically solid, computationally efficient approach with strong CD results and practical applicability.

Argument against acceptance: Limited empirical gains for SGD, questionable generality of gradient-bound assumption, and unclear practical impact of competitive ratio.

Overall assessment: Strong contribution to adaptive importance sampling and optimization, meriting acceptance despite weaker SGD results.

Suggestion: Investigate and analyze the SGD discrepancy between theory and practice.

Suggestion: Discuss challenges and methods for obtaining safe gradient bounds in more complex models beyond GLMs.

Suggestion: Provide a more intuitive, practically interpretable explanation of the competitive ratio.

Suggestion: Expand experiments to more datasets and models for SGD and consider extensions to other optimization algorithms.
