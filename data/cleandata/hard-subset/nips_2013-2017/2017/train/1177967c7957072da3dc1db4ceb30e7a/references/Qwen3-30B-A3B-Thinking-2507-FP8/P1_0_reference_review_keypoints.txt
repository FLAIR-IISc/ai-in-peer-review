- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" using safe upper/lower gradient bounds to compute efficient adaptive sampling distributions for CD and SGD.  
- Formulates sampling as a convex optimization problem over safe bounds, yielding provable improvement over fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorem 3.2) and competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5-3.6).  
- Presents an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution per iteration.  
- Empirically validated on GLMs with L1 and L2 regularization, showing improvements over fixed sampling in CD and SGD.  
- Builds on prior adaptive sampling and safe screening work but targets the computational bottleneck of full-gradient requirements.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating provable advantage over fixed sampling.  
- Strength: strong computational complexity analysis; cost comparable to evaluating a single gradient component.  
- Strength: clear distinction between ideal full-information sampling and the practical safe adaptive approach.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation for the studied problem class (GLMs).  
- Weakness: safe gradient bounds and theoretical analysis limited to GLMs; "completely generic" claim unsupported.  
- Weakness: lacks convergence rate guarantees for the practical safe-adaptive algorithm (only for idealized case).  
- Weakness: main text does not clearly articulate relation to ideal gradient-based sampling (deferred to appendix).  
- Weakness: competitive ratio bound depends on a relative accuracy condition that may not hold in practice.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and algorithms are precisely described.  
- Clarity issues: connection between theory and practical implementation sometimes unclear; key derivations deferred to appendix.  
- Originality: novel use of safe bounds to compute adaptive sampling with performance guarantees; optimization formulation (7) is a key contribution.  
- Originality concern: insufficient differentiation from related adaptive-sampling-with-bounds work and limited experimental scope.  
- Significance: addresses an important optimization challenge with potential practical benefits for large-scale CD/SGD.  
- Significance caveat: broader impact limited by GLM-only experiments and unclear integration with other optimization techniques.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation 1: add at least one non-GLM empirical example to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Recommendation 2: provide convergence rate guarantees for the practical algorithm.  
- Recommendation 3: clarify relation to prior adaptive-sampling-with-bounds work and how this method improves on them.  
- Recommendation 4: better explain how theoretical guarantees recover ideal gradient sampling in practice and detail integration into existing frameworks.  
- Specific comments: clarify significance of beating Li-based sampling, prove "best possible" claim w.r.t. limited information, explain stepsize behavior in Fig.2, give concrete integration examples, and relate more clearly to safe screening literature.
