- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using upper/lower gradient bounds to enable efficient, guaranteed improvements.  
- Theoretical analysis shows improvements over fixed sampling and a competitive ratio to optimal gradient-based sampling (up to factor n in CD).  
- Presents a practical algorithm with O(n log n) per-iteration complexity that avoids full gradient computation.  
- Empirical evaluation on GLMs with real datasets supports effectiveness, particularly for Coordinate Descent.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized.  
- Empirical gains for SGD are marginal, leaving a significant gap between theory and practice in that setting.  
- Method performance depends on the tightness of gradient bounds; sensitivity to bound quality is not thoroughly explored.  
- Relies on the ability to track gradient changes, so generalizability beyond GLMs is not fully addressed.  
- Minor clarity issues in notation (e.g., definitions of 'l' and 'L') and in linking Lemma 3.5 to Lemma 3.6.  
- The convex formulation and efficient solver for safe sampling are original contributions.  
- Results are potentially significant for settings where full gradient computation is costly.  
- Main weaknesses: limited empirical SGD advantage, dependence on bound quality, and unclear applicability to non-GLM models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated problem, strong theory, practical algorithm, and solid CD results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak SGD empirical gains, reliance on bounds, and limited demonstrated generalizability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; address deeper analysis of SGD results and sensitivity to gradient bounds.
