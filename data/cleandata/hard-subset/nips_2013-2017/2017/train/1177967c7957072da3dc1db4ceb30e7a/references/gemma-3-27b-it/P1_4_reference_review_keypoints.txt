- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD, computing sampling distributions via a convex optimization using gradient bounds.  
- Shows theoretical guarantees that the proposed sampling improves over uniform or fixed importance sampling and relates to optimal gradient-based sampling.  
- Claims an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution using upper and lower bounds on gradients.  
- Applicable to both CD and SGD; empirical results on GLMs with various regularizers demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Positions work within prior adaptive sampling, duality-gap sampling, and safe screening literature.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis proving provable improvement over fixed sampling.  
- Strength: practical computational efficiency with manageable overhead in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: generality across CD and SGD and clear, well-written exposition.  
- Strength: safe approximation using gradient bounds and extensive empirical validation; provides practical guidance for GLMs.  
- Weakness: relies on availability of safe gradient upper/lower bounds; limited discussion for complex models (e.g., deep nets).  
- Weakness: improvements for SGD are less pronounced than for CD and need deeper investigation.  
- Weakness: O(n log n) complexity claim for Algorithm 4 needs more detailed justification; constant factors may be significant.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to initialization of gradient bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient, theoretically sound, empirically validated, and of broad interest.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on gradient bounds, limited SGD gains, and incomplete complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand discussion on obtaining gradient bounds for complex models, analyze SGD performance gap, justify Algorithm 4 complexity (including constants), and study sensitivity to initialization.
