- Proposes "safe adaptive importance sampling" for CD and SGD using only safe upper/lower gradient bounds instead of full gradients, with theoretical guarantees and practical algorithmic cost (O(n log n) per iteration).
- Derives the optimal adaptive sampling distribution (Example 2.3) and proves theoretical improvements over fixed sampling (Theorem 2.1).
- Introduces a safe sampling scheme with a provable guarantee that it is always better than fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2).
- Shows computational complexity of the method is O(n log n) per iteration, comparable to computing a single gradient component for many problems (Theorem 3.4).
- Demonstrates empirical gains across multiple losses, regularizers, and real-world datasets, with minimal reported computational overhead.
- Weakness: Method for obtaining safe gradient bounds is only detailed for GLMs and is not addressed for complex models like deep neural networks or non-GLM objectives.
- Weakness: Competitive-ratio analysis (Lemma 3.5) yields a weak bound (ρk ≤ vk/Lmin) that can be large in practice and lacks practical guidance on when it’s small.
- Weakness: Comparison to the infeasible optimal adaptive sampling is not fully convincing; vk values are empirically much smaller than worst-case but not explained.
- Weakness: Scalability to extremely high-dimensional problems (e.g., d >> n or d > 10^6) is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Clarity strengths: Well-organized paper, consistent notation, logical progression from theory to practice, and effective figures.
- Clarity weaknesses: Key proofs deferred to appendix, Algorithm 4 is cryptically described and needs a worked example, and wall-clock runtime comparisons are missing.
- Originality strengths: Novel safe-adaptive formulation and convex-optimization viewpoint; improves computational efficiency over prior adaptive-sampling work.
- Originality weaknesses: Not clearly distinguished from related work (e.g., Perekrestenko et al. 2016); some guarantees overlap with prior results.
- Significance strengths: Addresses an important problem for large-scale ML and has potential to speed up CD and SGD with low overhead.
- Significance weaknesses: Empirical improvements are modest in some cases; lacks comparisons to other adaptive methods (stale gradients, duality-gap sampling) and massive-scale evaluations.
- Overall assessment: Strong, theoretically sound, and practical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
- Suggested improvements: provide safe-bound methods beyond GLMs, compare to Perekrestenko et al. and other adaptive schemes, plot optimal-adaptive baseline, analyze extreme-scale scalability, clarify relationships to prior work, add an Algorithm 4 example, and report wall-clock times.
