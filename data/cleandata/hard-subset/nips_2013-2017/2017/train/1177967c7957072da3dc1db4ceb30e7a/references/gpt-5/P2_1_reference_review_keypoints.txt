- Paper studies adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD, proposing a “safe” minimax sampler using per-coordinate gradient bounds to optimize worst-case one-step progress.
- Core contribution: a provably optimal distribution and stepsize (p̂, α̂ = 1/v) from a convex min-max that dominate fixed Li-based sampling and match ideal sampling with full gradients.
- For CD, ideal gradient-based sampling can be up to n× faster than fixed Li-based importance sampling.
- Provides a constructive characterization and an O(n log n) sorting-based algorithm to compute p̂ and α̂.
- Shows how to maintain safe gradient bounds cheaply for GLMs and gives empirical results with substantial gains for CD and modest gains for SGD.
- Novelty: safe minimax formulation that returns provably optimal worst-case distribution under inexact gradient information, differing from stale/heuristic methods.
- Theory: one-step expected decrease bound is standard and sound; Lemma 2.4 and Theorem 3.2 are consistent and well-justified (Sion’s theorem usage plausible).
- Constructive result (Theorem 3.4) and competitive ratio analysis are clear and useful; convergence rates are relegated to appendix but ingredients are standard.
- Algorithm/complexity: computing p̂ is O(n log n); maintaining bounds claimed O(n) per iteration via Cauchy–Schwarz, which may dominate cheap CD updates.
- Practical caveat: O(n) per-iteration work can be prohibitive in sparse, high-dimensional settings where CD updates are O(nnz(a_i)); amortization assumptions may not hold.
- Empirics: CD experiments are convincing (p̂ close to ideal and faster than uniform/fixed); SGD gains are small, matching prior observations about importance sampling limits.
- Experimental limitations: lack of comparisons with other adaptive schemes (duality-gap sampling, AdaSDCA, stale-gradient approximations) and inconsistent timing hardware.
- Suggested empirical additions: overhead breakdowns (bound updates, sorting vs compute) and ablation on bound tightness and recomputation frequency.
- Clarity: paper is well organized with proofs in appendix; suggests explicitly stating min-max interchange conditions and clarifying data/cost models for CD.
- Requests: provide SGD variant of Algorithm 2 and fix minor typos/formatting in algorithms/references.
- Originality: safe minimax approach and efficient O(n log n) solution appear novel and nontrivial, with guarantees that beat fixed importance sampling.
- Significance: valuable for CD on convex smooth objectives; modular technique could influence practice, though impact for SGD is smaller.
- Main limitation to impact: scalability concerns due to O(n) bound maintenance; scalable variants or more efficient bound maintenance would strengthen the work.
- Pros: clear theory, safe dominance guarantee, efficient algorithm, strong CD empirical gains, generic framework for CD and SGD with GLM instantiation.
- Cons: O(n) bound maintenance may be heavy for large n/sparse updates; limited baselines; convergence rates not highlighted; modest SGD benefits.
- Questions for authors: can safe bounds be updated less frequently or on subsets to reduce overhead while retaining guarantees?
- Questions for authors: how do results change with tighter bounds or data-dependent precomputation (e.g., approximate inner products)?
- Questions for authors: can sorting be maintained incrementally to reduce O(n log n) cost, and any comparisons to Gauss–Southwell, approximate steepest CD, or AdaSDCA?
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — principled, implementable method with solid theory and promising CD results; address scalability of bound maintenance and expand empirical comparisons to strengthen contribution.
