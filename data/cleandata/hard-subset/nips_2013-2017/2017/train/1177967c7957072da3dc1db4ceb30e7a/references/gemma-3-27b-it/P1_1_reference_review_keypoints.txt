- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using convex optimization with safe gradient bounds.  
- Achieves provable improvements over fixed sampling while remaining computationally efficient (O(n log n) to compute the sampling distribution).  
- Theoretical contributions include convergence rates for CD and complexity estimates for the method.  
- Empirical validation on RCV1, real-sim, and news20 demonstrates effectiveness and insights on the stepsize parameter vk.  
- Method is general, applicable to both CD and SGD.  
- Paper is clearly written with accessible notation and derivations.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation showing trade-offs between optimal sampling and practical approximations.  
- Strength: practical efficiency by using safe bounds instead of full gradient information.  
- Weakness: discussion of tracking safe gradient bounds is limited to GLMs and lacks strategies for complex models (e.g., deep networks).  
- Weakness: only modest empirical improvements observed for SGD; causes not fully investigated.  
- Weakness: competitive ratio (ρk) analysis may rely on restrictive conditions; needs more nuanced discussion for real scenarios.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to the most recent adaptive sampling methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution that bridges optimal adaptive sampling and feasible approximations.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept (8/10) with suggested revisions to expand gradient-bound tracking discussion and comparisons to recent methods.
