- Introduces a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe upper/lower gradient bounds.  
- Claims theoretical improvement over fixed sampling and provides an efficient algorithm for computing the adaptive sampling distribution.  
- Builds on existing importance sampling and adaptive sampling literature, positioning itself as a more practical, theoretically grounded alternative.  
- Correctly cites relevant prior work and connects to accelerated coordinate descent and stochastic optimization results.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides rigorous analysis showing superiority over fixed sampling, with insightful speed-up results for CD.  
- Computational efficiency: avoids full gradient computations and offers an efficient method (Theorem 3.4) to compute the sampling distribution.  
- Generality: approach applies to both CD and SGD and can be integrated into various optimization problems.  
- Safe adaptation: using safe gradient bounds ensures the adaptive sampling is never worse than fixed sampling.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on real datasets show improved convergence and performance, especially for CD.  
- Clear presentation overall, with well-organized definitions and explanations.  
- Practicality concern: obtaining high-quality safe gradient bounds may be challenging outside GLMs and affects performance.  
- SGD benefits are marginal in experiments, weakening the impact in the SGD setting.  
- Notation is dense and could be made more accessible, particularly in theoretical sections.  
- Limited experimental scope: primarily evaluated on GLMs, lacking tests on broader problems like deep learning.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed work with convincing CD results.  
- Originality: novel combination of safe gradient bounds with convex optimization for adaptive sampling.  
- Significance: addresses a key optimization challenge and has potential to improve many algorithms.  
- For acceptance: strong theory, practical algorithm, generality, clear presentation, convincing CD experiments.  
- Against acceptance: reliance on available safe gradient bounds, limited SGD gains, and dense notation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify gradient-bound challenges, investigate SGD results, and simplify notation.
