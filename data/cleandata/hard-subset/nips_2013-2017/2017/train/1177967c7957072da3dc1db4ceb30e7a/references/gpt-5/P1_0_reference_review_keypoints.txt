- Paper addresses adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD, proposing a “safe” adaptive rule using minimax optimization with only interval bounds on gradient magnitudes.  
- With full information, gradient-based probabilities √Li|∇if(x)| (CD) or ‖∇fi(x)‖2 (SGD) are optimal and can be up to n× faster than uniform sampling.  
- Main contribution: choose sampling pk by solving minp∈Δ maxc∈Ck V(p,c)/‖c‖2 with V(p,c)=∑i Li c2i/pi using coordinate-wise safe bounds, and set step-size αk=1/vk.  
- The solution is never worse (under the model) than any fixed Li-based sampling and matches full-information optimal sampling when bounds are tight.  
- The algorithm admits an O(n log n) per-iteration procedure (Algorithm 4) via sorting and has a simple geometric interpretation.  
- Paper shows cheap maintenance of safe gradient bounds for generalized linear models (GLMs) and presents strong CD experiments on text datasets.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound under standard smooth convex assumptions; minimax is well-posed and constructive solution/theorem are convincing.  
- Competitive ratio analysis gives insight but relies on a somewhat artificial relative accuracy condition, limiting its generality.  
- Empirically, the method significantly improves CD in iterations and time and closely tracks full-information sampling; SGD gains are mild due to overhead.  
- Completeness issues: convergence rates for the adaptive CD algorithm are only sketched and should be stated as a concise theorem in the main text.  
- Comparisons omit other partial-information adaptive methods (e.g., gap-based CD, approximate Gauss-Southwell, Csiba et al.), weakening empirical positioning.  
- Clarity is generally good, but notation (L scalar vs. diag(Li)) should be clarified and Algorithm 4 needs more explicit pseudo-code and index-mapping details.  
- Section 4 should explicitly state assumptions and practical ways to bound Hessian-related terms without expensive computations.  
- Originality: framing adaptive sampling under partial information as a minimax problem with a provable “safe” guarantee and an O(n log n) solver appears novel.  
- Significance: practical and valuable for CD in convex GLMs; less compelling for SGD unless bound maintenance is cheap or asynchronous/distributed.  
- Pros: principled minimax formulation, efficient algorithm, practical GLM integration, recovers full-information optimum, clear step-size rule.  
- Cons: per-iteration O(n log n) sampling plus O(n) bound updates can be costly (especially for SGD), limited empirical comparisons, and somewhat weak competitive bounds.  
- Suggested improvements: add a main-text convergence theorem, provide wall-clock overhead breakdowns and amortization strategies, compare to more adaptive baselines, discuss block/minibatch and variance-reduction extensions, and release code.  
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline positive) for solid theoretical contributions and strong CD performance; SGD part less compelling but not fatal.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on technical convex/CD assessment; medium on breadth of empirical comparisons.
