This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The core idea is to approximate gradient-based sampling, which is often computationally expensive, with a more efficient scheme based on safe upper and lower bounds on the gradients. The authors provide theoretical guarantees demonstrating that their approach is superior to uniform or fixed importance sampling and recovers the gradient-based sampling in the full information setting.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. It builds upon existing work on importance sampling and adaptive sampling strategies, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, and the connections to existing results are clearly stated. The contribution of proving that the proposed method is *provably* better than uniform sampling, even with limited information, is significant. The presentation of Algorithm 2 (safe sampling) and Algorithm 4 (efficient computation of the sampling distribution) are particularly helpful. The empirical evaluation on GLMs is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.  The inclusion of timing results alongside accuracy results is a strong point.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the proposed method, including guarantees on its performance compared to uniform sampling.
* **Practicality:** The proposed algorithm is computationally efficient and can be easily integrated into existing CD and SGD methods.  The analysis of the computational overhead is sensible.
* **Novelty:** The idea of using safe bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling and formulating the sampling distribution computation as a convex optimization problem is novel.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the concepts and algorithms.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method on real datasets.
* **Connection to Prior Work:** The paper clearly relates its contributions to existing work in the field of importance sampling and adaptive optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the empirical evaluation is comprehensive for GLMs, it would be beneficial to see the performance of the method on other types of machine learning problems (e.g., deep learning). The experiments are heavily focused on CD.
* **Assumptions on Gradient Bounds:** The performance of the algorithm relies on the availability of accurate safe upper and lower bounds on the gradients.  The paper discusses how to obtain these bounds for GLMs, but it is not clear how well this approach would generalize to other models. The practical difficulty of maintaining tight bounds could be a limitation.
* **Competitive Ratio Analysis:** The competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5 and 3.6) is theoretically interesting but could be more clearly connected to practical performance.  The conditions in Lemma 3.6 seem quite restrictive.
* **SGD Results Less Pronounced:** The authors acknowledge that the improvements with SGD are less significant than with CD.  This discrepancy could be investigated further.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to adaptive importance sampling.
* The proposed method is computationally efficient and practical.
* The empirical evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the method on real datasets.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the concepts and algorithms.
* The work addresses a significant challenge in large-scale machine learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical evaluation could be broadened to include a wider range of machine learning problems.
* The reliance on accurate safe gradient bounds could be a limitation.
* The competitive ratio analysis could be strengthened and better connected to practical performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of adaptive optimization. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the proposed method is practical, and the empirical evaluation is comprehensive. While there are a few minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance.
