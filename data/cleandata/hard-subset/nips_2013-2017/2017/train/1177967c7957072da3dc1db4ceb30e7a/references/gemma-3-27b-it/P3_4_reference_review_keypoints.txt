- Summary: Proposes an efficient adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD by casting sampling selection as a convex optimization and using safe gradient bounds to avoid full gradients.  
- Summary (cont.): Provides theoretical analysis showing improvements over uniform/fixed sampling and empirical validation on real datasets.

- Relation to Prior Work: Positions work within importance sampling and CD/SGD literature, connecting to Nesterov, Richtárik, Zhao & Zhang, Csiba, and safe screening methods.

- Strength: Theoretical Soundness — rigorously establishes advantages over fixed sampling and derives the optimal full-information sampling baseline.
- Strength: Computational Efficiency — uses safe gradient bounds to avoid full gradients; sampling computation is O(n log n).
- Strength: Generality — applicable to both CD and SGD and potentially extensible to other optimizers.
- Strength: Empirical Validation — real-world experiments show iteration and timing improvements; visualizations (vk) aid understanding.
- Strength: Clear Writing — well-organized with consistent notation and appended proofs.

- Weakness: Gradient Bound Acquisition — limited discussion on how to obtain practical safe gradient bounds beyond GLM examples; Cauchy–Schwarz bounds may be overly conservative.
- Weakness: SGD Performance — empirical gains for SGD are weaker than for CD and lack thorough investigation into causes.
- Weakness: Competitive Ratio — tighter-bound conditions (Lemma 3.6) appear restrictive with limited practical discussion or mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: Memory Requirements — storing lower/upper gradient bounds (`l` and `u`) is not explicitly addressed and may be problematic in high dimensions.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, practically useful contribution with sound theory, efficiency, and supporting experiments; minor weaknesses do not undermine the main contributions.

- Arguments For Acceptance: strong theoretical guarantees, novel efficient algorithm, compelling empirical results, clear presentation, and relevance to stochastic optimization.
- Arguments Against Acceptance: insufficient general discussion on acquiring safe gradient bounds, modest SGD improvements, and restrictive conditions for tight competitive ratios.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions asking for more detail on gradient bound acquisition and deeper analysis of SGD performance.
