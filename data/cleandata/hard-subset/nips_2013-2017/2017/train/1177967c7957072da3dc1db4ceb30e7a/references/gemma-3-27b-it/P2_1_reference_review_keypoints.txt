- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling via a convex optimization formulation.  
- Provides an efficient algorithm to compute the sampling distribution (claimed O(n log n) complexity).  
- Theoretical guarantees show provable improvements over static sampling and connection to optimal gradient-based sampling.  
- Claims potential speedups up to dimension n for Coordinate Descent.  
- Method applies to both Coordinate Descent and Stochastic Gradient Descent.  
- Introduces the use of safe gradient bounds and a practical procedure for obtaining them in GLMs.  
- Empirical validation on real-world GLM datasets shows improved performance and includes timing results.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- SGD empirical improvements are weaker than for CD and not thoroughly investigated.  
- Empirical evaluation is focused on GLMs, limiting generalizability to other domains (e.g., deep learning, nonconvex problems).  
- Limited direct comparison to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., duality-gap-based or stale-gradient methods).  
- Reliance on safe gradient bounds—sensitivity to loose or inaccurate bounds is not fully analyzed.  
- Complexity claim depends on sorting; this could be a bottleneck in extremely high-dimensional settings.  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound, practical—recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- For acceptance: strong theory, practical efficient algorithm, demonstrated improvements, clear presentation, addresses important large-scale optimization issue.  
- Against acceptance: weaker SGD results, GLM-limited experiments, lack of comparisons to other adaptive methods.  
- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 8/10; Originality 8/10; Significance 7/10.  
- Recommendation and required revisions: investigate weaker SGD performance, compare to other adaptive techniques, analyze sensitivity to safe bounds, and acknowledge sorting bottleneck in complexity analysis.
