- Paper studies adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD, sharpening full-information optimal sampling and proposing a safe adaptive scheme using bounds on gradients.  
- Formulates a min–max over the probability simplex and a box of gradient bounds; proves the resulting pk is worst-case optimal given the bounds.  
- Shows the safe sampler is never worse than fixed importance sampling based on Li and recovers full-information sampling when bounds are exact.  
- Presents an O(n log n) "water-filling"-like algorithm to compute optimal pk and a safe stepsize αk = 1/vk, with plausible KKT-consistent construction.  
- Discusses maintenance of safe gradient bounds for GLMs and provides experiments on rcv1, real-sim, and news20 for CD and SGD.  
- Technical soundness: one-step progress analysis and derivations are standard, correct, and the min–max formulation is natural.  
- Claims vs evidence: main guarantee is well supported; experiments show substantial CD gains and modest SGD gains consistent with theory.  
- Scope/completeness: one-step descent and ingredients for rates are given, but a full end-to-end convergence theorem for the safe variant in the main text would strengthen the work.  
- Clarity: generally well written with clear algorithms, but notation is overloaded (n/d), a minor equation is ambiguous, and SGD bound assumptions should be stated explicitly.  
- Recommend adding a brief intuitive sketch of Algorithm 4 (water-filling/thresholding) in the main text for readability.  
- Originality: the safe min–max formulation under uncertainty sets and the efficient solver appear novel versus prior stale/upper-bounded gradient works.  
- Significance: yields substantial practical speedups for CD with strong guarantees; SGD gains are modest but framework is broadly applicable when bounds are tight.  
- Practical caveat: per-iteration overhead O(n log n) plus O(n) bound updates can be significant when d ≪ n or per-step costs are tiny; amortization/sparsification strategies would help.  
- Pros: principled formulation, optimal worst-case sampling, recovers full-information, efficient implementable algorithm, strong empirical CD results.  
- Cons: overhead may be large in some regimes, SGD safe bounds maintenance details deferred and possibly loose, no main-text global convergence rate, minor clarity issues.  
- Questions/suggestions: can pk be updated less frequently to reduce overhead; can tighter SGD bounds be obtained cheaply (e.g., Lipschitz Hessian); clarify Lemma 4.1 assumptions on hi; include a global convergence rate for μ-strongly convex f.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10) — novel, practically useful, with solid theory and experiments, needing some implementation and clarity improvements.
