- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling scheme that uses interval bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling for CD and SGD without full-gradient computation.  
- Maintains safe lower/upper bounds on per-coordinate gradients (CD) or per-component gradient norms (SGD) and solves a min-max problem to pick sampling p̂k and step-size α̂k.  
- p̂k is guaranteed to never underperform fixed importance sampling, recovers true gradient-based sampling when bounds are exact, and is optimal given the interval information.  
- Provides an O(n log n) algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute p̂k and a thresholding characterization for the solution.  
- Supplies a practical, simple (though loose) bound-maintenance strategy for GLMs using gradient-change tracking and Cauchy–Schwarz bounds.  
- Theoretical contributions include one-step expected progress analysis, min-max formulation, Theorems 3.2/3.4 on optimality under uncertainty, and competitive ratio bounds (Lemmas 3.5/3.6).  
- CD experiments show substantial iteration and time improvements; SGD experiments show only minor gains.  
- Novelty lies in the safe min-max formulation and provable guarantee to beat fixed importance sampling under interval uncertainty.  
- Assumes coordinate-wise L-smoothness; treatment of non-smooth separable regularizers (e.g., L1) is unclear and may require proximal/ composite analysis.  
- Presentation needs to clarify which parts extend to composite/proximal settings and what changes in assumptions and mappings.  
- Computational overhead: O(n log n) per iteration can be prohibitive for very large n or very sparse features; paper lacks amortization/incremental-update strategies.  
- Clarity issues: notational overloading (L), minor typos, incomplete references, and ambiguous treatment of non-smooth regularizers.  
- Practicality: GLM bound strategy is safe and works empirically for CD but is potentially loose; more ablations on bound tightness are needed.  
- Experiments miss ablations on bound tightness, update frequency of p̂k, sparsity effects, and clear wall-clock overhead measured on the same machine.  
- SGD section is less convincing; authors should tone claims or add experiments in settings with high gradient heterogeneity, mini-batching, or variance reduction.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify composite extension, detail Algorithm 4 implementation for large-scale/incremental updates, provide ablations on bounds, and report per-machine overhead and regime-specific experiments.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical and practical contribution for CD with strong guarantees and empirical gains; less compelling for SGD as presented.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance if composite-setting clarity and overhead/update-strategy issues are addressed.
