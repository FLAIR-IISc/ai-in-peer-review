- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that is computationally efficient and provably no worse than fixed importance sampling.  
- Frames sampling selection as a convex optimization solvable in O(n log n) given safe gradient lower/upper bounds.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and a proof of safety, connecting to existing CD/SGD literature.  
- Presents a practical O(n log n)-per-iteration algorithm with pseudocode suitable for integration into existing methods.  
- Empirical results on real-world GLM datasets show improvements in iteration count and runtime.  
- Leverages readily available or approximated gradient bounds within the framework for practical applicability.  
- Theoretical analysis (e.g., Section 2.1 and Lemma 2.4) is dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.  
- SGD empirical gains are less compelling than for CD; more analysis is needed to explain this discrepancy.  
- Experimental scope is limited (primarily GLMs); broader model and dataset evaluation would strengthen generality claims.  
- Performance depends on the quality of gradient bounds; sensitivity to looser bounds is not thoroughly explored.  
- Lacks comparison to other adaptive sampling methods beyond fixed sampling baselines.  
- Review scores: Quality 8.5/10, Clarity 7.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (improve explanations, analyze bound sensitivity, expand experiments, compare to other adaptive methods).
