- Proposes a novel safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that is provably better than uniform/fixed sampling and computationally efficient.
- Frames sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem using safe upper and lower gradient bounds to ensure robustness with imperfect gradient information.
- Claims theoretical convergence improvements, including a potential n-fold speedup for Coordinate Descent.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on real-world datasets for generalized linear models (GLMs).
- Builds on prior importance sampling and adaptive sampling literature, addressing theoretical and computational gaps in prior work.
- Distinguishes itself by providing a “safe” adaptive strategy with provable guarantees between costly optimal sampling and suboptimal fixed sampling.
- Strong theoretical rigor and clear analysis of convergence rate improvements.
- Computationally feasible design that leverages safe bounds for efficient sampling-distribution computation.
- General scheme applicable to both CD and SGD, indicating broad applicability.
- Practical relevance shown by deriving safe gradient bounds for GLMs and empirical validation.
- Well-written, organized presentation with clear notation and concise algorithms.
- Safety guarantee ensures performance is at least as good as fixed sampling.
- SGD empirical gains are limited compared to CD, needing further investigation or explanation.
- Performance depends on accurate and maintainable safe gradient bounds; generalizability to complex models and robustness to noise/ill-conditioning are unclear.
- Implementation complexity (sorting O(n log n) and bound updates) may add overhead not fully evaluated in timing experiments.
- Competitive ratio bounds are presented but their practical implications and dependence on bound quality are under-discussed.
- Experimental scope is limited mainly to GLMs; performance on non-convex problems or deep models is not evaluated.
- Recommends acceptance due to theoretical and practical contributions, with minor revisions requested.
- Suggested revisions: analyze limited SGD gains, discuss gradient-bound robustness, deepen competitive-ratio implications, and broaden experimental evaluation.
