- Tackles adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD, proposing a “safe” adaptive scheme using per-coordinate lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes.  
- Re-derives gradient-based sampling optimality under a quadratic upper-bound model and shows up to n-fold gain over fixed sampling in CD.  
- Core contribution: a robust min–max formulation that chooses p to minimize worst-case expected decrease over a box of unknown gradients, provably never worse than Li-based sampling.  
- Proposes a corresponding “safe” stepsize α̂k = 1/vk derived from the min–max value.  
- Provides an O(n log n) algorithm (water-filling style) to compute the worst-case-optimal distribution with a geometric interpretation.  
- Shows how to maintain safe gradient bounds efficiently for GLMs and gives simple Cauchy–Schwarz updates for bounds.  
- Experiments: notable gains for CD, modest gains for SGD, overall empirical support aligned with theory.  
- Novelty: robust-optimization view and efficient algorithm appear new relative to prior adaptive and gap-based schemes.  
- Strength: clear optimization-based formulation with a clean worst-case guarantee and intuitive algorithmic structure.  
- Weakness: per-iteration overhead (O(n) bound updates + O(n log n) recompute) may be prohibitive for large sparse problems with n ≫ 10^5.  
- Weakness: practical maintenance of tight safe bounds is under-specified (esp. general h and SGD), and proposed bounds may be loose, hurting adaptivity.  
- The “always better than fixed” guarantee applies to the per-step quadratic upper bound, not directly to uniform global convergence rates—connection to overall complexity is not fully clarified.  
- The min–max interchange used in the main theorem lacks detailed conditions/proof in the main text despite non convex-concave structure.  
- Clarity issues: typos, notational inconsistencies, and central proofs deferred; key assumptions and min–max conditions should be moved into main text.  
- Missing comparisons: need wall-clock and baseline comparisons to gap-based CD and Gauss–Southwell variants, and to practical SGD importance heuristics.  
- Suggestions: derive amortized or log-time updates for p̂ under sparse/local changes, clarify min–max conditions, provide global rates for strongly convex f with α̂k, tighten logistic/hessian bounds, and add larger-scale wall-clock experiments.  
- Overall judgment: conceptually appealing and technically sound but practicality and clarity need work; reviewer recommends weak accept.
