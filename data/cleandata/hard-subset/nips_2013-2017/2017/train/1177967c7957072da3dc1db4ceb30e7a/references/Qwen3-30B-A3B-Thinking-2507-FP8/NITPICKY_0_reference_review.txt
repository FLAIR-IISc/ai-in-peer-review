

# Review of "Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling"

## Summary
This paper proposes a "safe adaptive importance sampling" scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The key contribution is a computationally efficient approximation of gradient-based adaptive sampling that can be computed in every iteration while maintaining theoretical guarantees better than fixed importance sampling. The authors derive an optimization problem to find the best sampling distribution given safe lower and upper bounds on gradient information, and show how to solve it efficiently in O(n log n) time. The approach is validated on generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization.

## Strengths
The paper makes a theoretically sound contribution to the well-studied area of adaptive importance sampling. The key insight of formulating the sampling problem as an optimization over safe gradient bounds is novel and addresses a significant practical limitation of previous approaches. The theoretical guarantees in Theorem 3.2 are compelling, and the experimental results demonstrate practical benefits across multiple datasets and loss functions. The paper is well-structured, and the writing is generally clear.

## Weaknesses
Despite the promising theoretical contribution, the paper contains numerous technical inconsistencies and formatting errors that undermine its credibility and clarity:

1. **Notation inconsistencies**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout, including:
   - Inconsistent iterate notation (xk vs x^k)
   - Inconsistent norm notation (‖·‖22 vs ‖·‖_2^2)
   - Inconsistent index notation (i_k vs i_k)
   - In Example 2.3, "αk(p ? k) = ‖∇f(xk)‖22 ‖√L∇f(xk)‖2" should be "αk(p?k) = ‖∇f(x^k)‖_2^2 / ‖√L∇f(x^k)‖_2^2"

2. **Mathematical errors**:
   - Equation (1) contains a typo: "η 2Lu 2 ‖u‖ 2" should be "η^2 L/2 ‖u‖^2"
   - Example 2.2 states "[pL]i = LiTr[L]" which should be "[pL]i = Li / Tr[L]"
   - Theorem 3.2 states "Lmin ≤ vk ≤ Tr [L]" but Lmin is never properly defined
   - In Theorem 3.4, "m = ‖c‖22 · ‖ √ Lc‖−11" should be "m = ‖c‖_2^2 / ‖√Lc‖_1"

3. **Algorithmic description issues**:
   - Algorithm 4 contains confusing notation and inconsistent indexing
   - The description of "m = max(`sort)" is unclear and lacks proper context
   - The paper states "the introduced computational overhead reduces to O(log n) per iteration" without sufficient justification

4. **Formatting and typographical errors**:
   - Multiple inconsistent uses of apostrophes (e.g., "rcv1’" vs "rcv1'")
   - Inconsistent use of "i" as an index (sometimes as i, sometimes as i_k)
   - "the very naïve gradient information" is overly informal for a NIPS paper
   - "sum-structure" should be "sum-structured" or "sum-structured components"

5. **Theoretical justification gaps**:
   - The paper claims "our solution is the best possible with respect to the limited information at hand" but provides insufficient justification for this strong claim
   - The competitive ratio analysis (Lemma 3.5-3.6) lacks sufficient context about when the bounds are tight

## Recommendation
I recommend rejection. While the paper presents an interesting theoretical contribution to adaptive importance sampling, the numerous technical inconsistencies and formatting errors make it difficult to assess the validity of the theoretical claims. The paper would benefit from a thorough proofreading and notation standardization before being considered for publication. The minor flaws identified here are numerous enough to significantly undermine the paper's credibility as a scientific contribution, especially in the context of a top-tier venue like NIPS where precision and attention to detail are paramount.

The paper's theoretical insights are promising, but the current presentation contains too many preventable errors that would confuse readers and reviewers. The authors should address these issues before resubmission. The paper's core idea is valuable, but the current version does not meet the technical standards expected for NIPS.
