- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using interval bounds on coordinate gradients and a min-max formulation to choose sampling p̂ and stepsize α̂.  
- Shows full-information optimal sampling has p_i ∝ √Li |∇i f(x)| and can yield up to n× speedup over Li-based sampling.  
- Core contribution: a min-max problem over uncertainty box C = {ℓ ≤ c ≤ u} that guarantees worst-case improvement over fixed Li-based sampling and recovers full-information optimum when bounds are tight.  
- Provides an O(n log n) constructive algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute p̂ via thresholding/clipping (water-filling-like).  
- Gives methods to maintain safe gradient bounds cheaply for GLMs (updates via Cauchy–Schwarz) requiring only sampled-coordinate gradients.  
- Empirical results: substantial iteration and wall-clock speedups for CD on sparse text datasets; modest gains for SGD.  
- Novelty: robust, information-theoretic min-max formulation and “safe dominance” guarantee over fixed sampling not present in prior adaptive schemes.  
- Strength: conceptual clarity—casts adaptive sampling under uncertainty as a convex min-max problem with strong optimality and dominance properties.  
- Strength: methodological—efficient O(n log n) algorithm and geometric (Rayleigh-type quotient) characterization.  
- Strength: practical—cheap bound maintenance for GLMs and realistic experimental validation for CD.  
- Weakness: per-iteration O(n log n) overhead may be prohibitive in very large-n, cheap-coordinate regimes and needs runtime breakdowns and amortization/approximation strategies.  
- Weakness: minimax interchange/convexity justification for swapping min/max is deferred to appendix and should be clearly stated with assumptions in the main text.  
- Weakness: GLM bounds via Cauchy–Schwarz can be very loose initially (ℓ0=0, u0=∞), so practical benefits depend on bound tightness; more ablations and stronger cheap bounds are needed.  
- Weakness: scope overstated—theory/experiments focus on convex GLMs while title/intro suggest broader applicability (e.g., deep learning); either temper claims or add nonconvex study.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons omit key baselines (Gauss–Southwell, stale-gradient/duality-gap methods) and lack reporting of p̂ recomputation frequency/time and recompute-every-T variants.  
- Clarity issues: some equations/notation inconsistent (e.g., division by p_i in V(p,·)), reuse of symbols u, ℓ as indices, and missing explicit smoothness/minimax assumptions in main text.  
- Significance: promising and practical advance for CD on convex problems with safety guarantees; more modest and situational benefit for SGD.  
- Recommendation: lean accept conditional on clarifying minimax assumptions, expanding empirical comparisons, and discussing/practically addressing the O(n log n) overhead.
