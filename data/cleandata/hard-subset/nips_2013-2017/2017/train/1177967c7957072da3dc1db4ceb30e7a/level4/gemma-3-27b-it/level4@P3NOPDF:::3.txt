This paper addresses the important problem of safe adaptive importance sampling (AIS), a technique currently receiving significant attention within the machine learning community. The authors propose a novel method with compelling features, notably its generality – the approach is designed to be readily integrated with a wide range of existing algorithms – and a strong empirical and theoretical foundation. This combination is a significant strength, suggesting a potentially impactful contribution to the field.

The core idea behind the proposed method is sound and appears well-motivated. The theoretical analysis provides valuable guarantees regarding the safety and efficiency of the algorithm, which is crucial for practical application of AIS techniques. Demonstrating these theoretical results with well-designed experiments further strengthens the paper’s claims. The experimental results presented are promising and suggest that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches, particularly in challenging scenarios. 

However, there are areas where the presentation could be improved. My primary concern relates to the figures in Section 5. These figures are currently difficult to interpret due to their small size and the correspondingly small font used for labels. Improving the readability of these figures is crucial for the audience to fully appreciate the experimental results. Specifically, the figures should be enlarged, and the labels significantly increased in size. Clearer visualization will substantially enhance understanding and allow for a more accurate assessment of the method’s performance.

Beyond the figure readability, a few minor points could further strengthen the paper. While the authors highlight the generality of their approach, it would be beneficial to briefly discuss any limitations or potential difficulties in integrating the method with *specific* algorithm classes. Providing even a short discussion of these edge cases would demonstrate a thorough understanding of the method's scope.

Furthermore, while the theoretical results are presented, a more intuitive explanation of the key insights would be helpful. While the mathematical formulation is rigorous, connecting the theoretical findings to the practical implications for users of AIS would increase accessibility. For instance, a discussion of how the theoretical bounds translate into practical considerations for choosing hyperparameters or determining sample sizes would be valuable.

Finally, a comparison to more recent AIS variants (if any exist since the paper's likely submission date) would help position the work relative to the current state-of-the-art. It's possible the field has moved quickly, and acknowledging related work published since the initial draft would demonstrate the authors' awareness of the evolving landscape. 

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of safe adaptive importance sampling. The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated. Addressing the concerns regarding figure readability, clarifying the limitations of generality, adding intuitive explanations of the theoretical results, and acknowledging potentially relevant recent work would significantly improve the paper and strengthen its impact. I believe that with these revisions, this paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to NIPS.
