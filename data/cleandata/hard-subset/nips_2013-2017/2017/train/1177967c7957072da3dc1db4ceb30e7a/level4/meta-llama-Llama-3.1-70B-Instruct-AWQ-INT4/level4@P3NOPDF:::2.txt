This paper provides interesting insights into the Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The conclusion drawn in Theorem 3.2, particularly Conclusion 2, is noteworthy as it suggests that the proposed sampling method is superior to the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. To further enhance the paper, I offer the following suggestions:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of $p^*_i$ should be $x_k$, not $x$. This correction is necessary for the mathematical consistency of the expression.

2. In Line 135, it is advisable to remove the subscript $k$ from $\hat{p}$ and $\hat{c}$ to maintain consistency in notation.

3. The figures provided are too small, making it difficult to discern any labels, numbers, or text. To improve visibility, consider relocating some figures to the appendix and utilizing the gained space to display larger images.

4. If feasible, it would be beneficial to provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the proposed sampling method to uniform sampling ($p = 1/n$) instead of the L-based method. This additional analysis would offer a more comprehensive understanding of the new sampling method's efficacy.

5. The authors should remark on how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, it would be valuable to discuss the total complexity (including the $O(n)$ time required for updating $l_k$ and $u_k$) for RCD to find an $\epsilon$-optimal solution. This analysis would provide a clearer understanding of the method's practical implications and trade-offs.

By addressing these points, the authors can strengthen the paper and provide a more polished presentation of their research.
