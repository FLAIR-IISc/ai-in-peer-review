- Novel, theoretically grounded "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy with provable optimality given gradient bounds.  
- Efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling that incurs negligible computational overhead and is practical for large-scale problems.  
- Convex optimization formulation for selecting the sampling distribution is novel and accompanied by an efficient solution algorithm.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presents methodology and theoretical results.  

- Lemma 2.1: possible missing factor of 2 in the definition/proof of α*—needs verification.  
- Example 3.1: discussion is confusing—Eq. (7) suggests maximizing by setting c to bounds but the text claims this can be suboptimal; clarify the intended takeaway.  
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: condition likely never satisfied given m = max(l^sort); double-check this condition.  
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: probable typo—use u^sort instead of c^sort.  
- Empirical comparisons should include other established adaptive sampling methods ([2], [5], [21]) and fixed non-uniform priors chosen a priori.  
- Missing timing results for SGD; include to assess performance and computational cost in common optimization settings.  
- Reference issues: title of [14] is incorrect; consider adding Csiba & Richtarik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches."  

- Overall: significant, well-motivated contribution with strong theory and efficiency; addressing the minor concerns would further strengthen impact.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors’ responses satisfactorily addressed concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance.
