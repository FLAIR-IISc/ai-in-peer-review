- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using computable lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling efficiently.
- Casts optimal sampling selection as a convex optimization problem (Equation 7) and provides an O(n log n) per-iteration algorithm (Algorithm 4).
- Proves the method is the best possible under the given bound constraints and strictly better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- Shows the computation cost is often negligible compared to gradient evaluation, making the approach practical.
- Extends prior adaptive sampling work by addressing computational infeasibility and providing theoretical guarantees against fixed sampling.
- Gives concrete strategies for maintaining safe gradient bounds in generalized linear models (GLMs).
- Empirical results on GLMs (Lasso, Ridge, logistic) across rcv1, real-sim, news20 show consistent speedups for CD with minimal overhead.
- Strong theoretical analysis (competitive ratio lemmas) and demonstration that loose Cauchy–Schwarz bounds are often sufficient.
- Paper is clear, well-structured, original, and significant for large-scale ML optimization.
- Weakness: numerical evaluation lacks comparisons to other adaptive schemes (e.g., papers [2,5,21]) and provides limited timing/benchmarking for SGD.
- Weakness: SGD experiments show minimal gains over uniform sampling and no timing comparisons against alternative adaptive methods, weakening SGD significance.
- Weakness: missing citation to Csiba and Richtarik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches—should be added.
- Weakness: Algorithm 4 pseudocode contains minor indexing errors (Line 4 and Line 7 references) affecting reproducibility.
- Weakness: proof of Lemma 2.1 needs a factor-of-2 correction in the expression for α* and should be clarified.
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality, original contribution with addressable minor issues.
