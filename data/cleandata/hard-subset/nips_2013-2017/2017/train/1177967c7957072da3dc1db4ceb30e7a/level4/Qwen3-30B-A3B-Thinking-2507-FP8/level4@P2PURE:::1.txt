

The authors propose a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient values to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The method is proven optimal with respect to these bounds, strictly dominating uniform and fixed importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. Although adaptive importance sampling has been studied previously, the authors introduce a novel formulation that recasts optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem, accompanied by an efficient solution algorithm. The paper is well-written and constitutes a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.

Comments:
- Lemma 2.1: A factor of 2 appears missing in the expression for $\alpha^*$.
- Example 3.1: The maximization in (7) suggests setting $c$ to the upper or lower bound outperforms uniform sampling, which contradicts the preceding claim that this naive approach can be suboptimal. Clarify this apparent inconsistency.
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: Given $m = \max(\ell^{\text{sort}})$, the condition $k \leq m$ will never hold; clarify the intended logic.
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: Should use $u^{\text{sort}}$ instead of $c^{\text{sort}}$.
- Numerical results: Would be strengthened by comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions.
- Timing results for SGD are absent; include these to assess computational efficiency.
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title; correct it.
- Add reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).
