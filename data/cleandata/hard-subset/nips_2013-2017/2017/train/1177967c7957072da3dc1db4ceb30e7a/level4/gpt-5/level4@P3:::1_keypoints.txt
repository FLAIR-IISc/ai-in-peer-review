- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using computable lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes and solving a convex minimax each iteration with an O(n log n) algorithm.  
- With full gradient information, optimal CD sampling is p ∝ sqrt(Li)|∇i f|, yielding up to factor-n speed-up over fixed sampling.  
- Given only safe bounds, the proposed p̂ is worst-case optimal over all distributions consistent with the bounds and never worse than Li-based fixed sampling.  
- Method integrates with CD and SGD and uses an adaptive stepsize αk matched to the sampling.  
- Builds on prior importance-sampling, Gauss–Southwell, and screening-rule work; main novelty is the tractable minimax under partial information with worst-case dominance guarantees.  
- Theory: full-information analysis is clear; minimax objective V(p,c)/‖c‖² and geometric characterization are elegant; O(n log n) construction via sorting is correct and practical.  
- Assumptions: provides concrete safe-bound maintenance for GLMs with O(n) updates; competitive ratio analysis (ρk) explains effect of bound tightness.  
- Empirics: strong iteration/time gains for CD; smaller gains for SGD; vk/Tr[L] plots help interpret adaptive stepsize behavior.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized; explanation of αk and pk and transition from full-info to safe bounds is clear, but some notational/algorithmic details need fixes.  
- Originality: novel formulation and provably optimal distribution under partial information that dominates fixed sampling.  
- Significance: easy-to-plug method with modest overhead and worst-case improvements; practical impact expected, especially for CD and potentially distributed/asynchronous/minibatch settings.  
- Lemma 2.1: possible missing factor of 2 in optimal αk — check algebra in Eq. (3).  
- Example 3.1 vs Eq. (7): clarify how the example shows picking extreme c = ℓ or u is suboptimal compared to solving the minimax.  
- Algorithm 4 issues: clarify initialization/loop invariants (m and [ℓsort]ℓ > m condition) and replace csort with usort in line 7.  
- Empirical comparisons: add other adaptive baselines (gap/violation/gradient-based methods) to strengthen positioning.  
- SGD timing: provide wall-clock timings for SGD to evaluate O(n log n) overhead, especially when d ≪ n.  
- Claim wording: change “always better” to “never worse and often better” to reflect worst-case guarantee.  
- Overhead discussion: discuss regimes where O(n log n) is costly (tiny d, very sparse updates) and possible amortization/partial/minibatch variants.  
- Competitive ratio diagnostics: provide empirical estimates of ρk or proxies (vk, wk) over time to connect theory and practice.  
- Minor fixes: correct “deep leaning” typo, fix Reference [14] title, and add Csiba & Richtárik (2016) reference.  
- Pros: principled formulation with guarantees, efficient algorithm, solid CD empirical gains, and useful diagnostic plots.  
- Cons: limited empirical baselines, no SGD timing, some algorithmic/presentation glitches, and reliance on availability/tightness of safe bounds with only GLMs explored.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with clear theory and practical relevance; accept after addressing minor issues and expanding empirical comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with authors’ clarifications and recommends acceptance.
