- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradients.  
- Formulates selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem solvable efficiently.  
- Proves the sampling distribution is optimal w.r.t. given bounds (Theorem 3.2) and always outperforms fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and supports claims with empirical results.  
- Empirical experiments on GLMs (rcv1, real-sim, news20) show significant speedups, especially for CD.  
- Computational overhead is negligible: O(n log n) per iteration versus O(d) for gradient evaluations.  
- Implementation details and algorithms (1–4) are sufficiently described for reproduction.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and clear.  
- Original contribution: reframes adaptive importance sampling as convex optimization over gradient bounds, improving over prior ad hoc approaches.  
- Method is general and applicable whenever safe gradient bounds can be maintained.  
- Minor technical issue: proof of Lemma 2.1 may be missing a factor of 2 in α*, easily corrected.  
- Some empirical shortcomings: SGD gains are modest compared to CD and lack timing comparisons/explanation.  
- Comparative evaluation could be strengthened with experiments against other adaptive sampling methods ([2,5,21]).  
- References are mostly appropriate but one citation ("Importance Sampling for Minibatches") should be updated to the 2016 arXiv version.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, practical efficiency, strong empirical results, and high-quality writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker SGD results and insufficient comparisons to other adaptive schemes.
