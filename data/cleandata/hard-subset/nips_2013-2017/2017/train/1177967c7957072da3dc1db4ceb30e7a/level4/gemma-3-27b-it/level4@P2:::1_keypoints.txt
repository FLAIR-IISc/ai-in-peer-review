- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds and casts optimal sampling as a convex problem with an O(n log n) solver.  
- Novel approach with provable guarantees: optimal given the bounds, always better than uniform/fixed importance sampling, and incurs negligible overhead.  
- Addresses computational infeasibility of prior adaptive sampling methods by leveraging readily available gradient bounds.  
- Builds on prior importance/adaptive sampling literature but offers a unique, efficient framework and theoretical connection to optimal sampling.  
- Strong theoretical backing (e.g., Lemma 2.1, Theorem 3.2) supporting claims.  
- Computational efficiency demonstrated via convex formulation and Algorithm 4 with efficient complexity.  
- General and easily integrable into existing CD and SGD algorithms.  
- Clear, well-organized writing that makes complex ideas accessible.  
- Extensive empirical validation on real datasets; vk plots illustrate bound tightness and stepsize adaptation.  
- Performance depends on the quality of the gradient bounds; sensitivity to inaccurate bounds needs more exploration.  
- Limited empirical comparison to other adaptive sampling schemes beyond uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Missing timing results for SGD, limiting practical performance assessment in that setting.  
- Contains minor errors/typos (e.g., incorrect reference title [14], potential issues in Algorithm 4 lines 4 & 7).  
- Rebuttal clarifications (factor of 2 in Lemma 2.1, Example 3.1 intent, Algorithm 4 condition) were helpful and resolved concerns.  
- Authors’ explanation for lack of SGD timing results is reasonable and acknowledged as future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, practically useful, and empirically convincing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on bound quality and limited comparisons to other adaptive methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos and, if possible, add comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes.
