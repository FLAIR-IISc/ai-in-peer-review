This paper introduces a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) methods. The core idea is to use lower and upper bounds on the gradient magnitudes to efficiently approximate the optimal gradient-based sampling distribution. The authors demonstrate that their proposed method is (i) provably the best strategy with respect to the given bounds, (ii) guaranteed to be better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and (iii) computationally efficient, adding negligible overhead in many cases. While adaptive importance sampling is an active area of research, the main novelty here lies in formulating the selection of the sampling distribution as a tractable convex optimization problem and providing an efficient algorithm to solve it.

The paper is well-written and makes a solid contribution to the literature on importance sampling techniques. The theoretical guarantees are strong, and the proposed method is practical.

I have a few comments and questions for the authors:

*   In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears there might be a missing factor of 2 in the expression for the optimal stepsize `alpha^*`.
*   The intention of Example 3.1 is unclear. The text preceding the example suggests that naively choosing `c` based on the lower or upper bounds can be suboptimal. However, the results presented in the example seem to indicate that these choices are better than the optimal sampling for the given `c`. Could the authors clarify the message of this example?
*   Regarding Algorithm 4: In line 4, `m` is initialized to `max(l_sort)`. Given this initialization, it is not clear if the condition `[l_sort]_l > m` can ever be satisfied in the first iteration. Could you clarify the logic here?
*   In Algorithm 4, line 7: Is the use of `c^{sort}` a typo? It seems it should be `u^{sort}`.
*   The empirical evaluation is insightful but could be strengthened. It would be beneficial to include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]). Additionally, the current fixed sampling baseline is uniform sampling. A comparison against a non-uniform fixed importance sampling scheme would provide a more comprehensive benchmark.
*   The paper provides timing results for CD but not for SGD. To make the experimental section more complete, please consider adding timing results for the SGD experiments.
*   There appears to be a typo in the title of reference [14].
*   The authors may also consider citing the following related work: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv (2016).
