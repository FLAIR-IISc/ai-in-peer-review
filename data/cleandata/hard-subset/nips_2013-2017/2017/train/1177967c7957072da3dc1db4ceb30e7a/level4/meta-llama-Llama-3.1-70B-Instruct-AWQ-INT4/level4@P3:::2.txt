This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent (CD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The main idea is to use safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient to compute the optimal sampling distribution in each iteration. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical properties and empirical performance on several machine learning tasks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the technical details of their method. The organization of the paper is clear, and the notation is well-defined. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work in the field.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a theoretical guarantee that the proposed sampling method is better than any fixed importance sampling method. This is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a new perspective on how to design efficient sampling strategies for CD and SGD algorithms.

The empirical evaluation of the proposed method is also thorough and well-executed. The authors provide results on several machine learning tasks, including generalized linear models with L1 and L2 regularization. The results show that the proposed method can significantly outperform fixed importance sampling in terms of iteration complexity and computation time.

However, there are a few minor issues with the paper that need to be addressed. Specifically:

* In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be xk, not x.
* In Line 135, the subscript k for \hat p and \hat c should be removed for consistency.
* The figures are too small and lack labels, numbers, and words. Some of the figures should be moved to the appendix to gain space and show larger figures.
* It would be useful to provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 but comparing with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of L_i based method.
* The authors should remark on how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD, including the O(n) time for updating lk and uk.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. With a few minor revisions, it has the potential to be a top-tier paper in the field.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD algorithms.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical properties and empirical performance.
* The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning, providing a new perspective on how to design efficient sampling strategies.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper requires a few minor revisions to address some technical issues and improve the presentation.
* The figures are too small and lack labels, numbers, and words.
