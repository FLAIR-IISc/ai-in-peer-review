This paper introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling strategy designed for use with coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. The core idea is to leverage lower and upper bounds on gradient values to construct an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling. The authors demonstrate that this method achieves optimal performance given these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform and fixed importance sampling approaches, all while incurring a negligible computational overhead. While adaptive importance sampling is not new, this work provides a novel and compelling formulation, framing the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and presenting an efficient algorithm for its solution. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper and a valuable contribution to the literature on importance sampling techniques.

The example’s presentation is somewhat unclear in this regard, as it initially states that naively setting *c* to these bounds can be suboptimal. Clarification would be helpful.
