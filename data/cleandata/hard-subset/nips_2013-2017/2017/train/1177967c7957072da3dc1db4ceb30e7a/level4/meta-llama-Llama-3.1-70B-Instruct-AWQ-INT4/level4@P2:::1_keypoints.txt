- Presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD that uses safe gradient bounds.  
- Proposes an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling computable with negligible extra cost.  
- Formulates selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solver.  
- Proves the proposed sampling is optimal w.r.t. the given bounds and always improves over uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation demonstrating performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly presented.  
- Minor errors/typos noted (e.g., missing factor of 2 in alpha^* in Lemma 2.1).  
- Some examples and statements need clarification (notably Example 3.1).  
- Algorithm 4 has issues: Line 4’s condition may never hold and Line 7 should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling baselines.  
- Suggests adding timing results for SGD to evaluate computational overhead in practice.  
- Novel and significant contribution with potential application to GLMs, deep learning, and signal processing; reviewer recommends clear accept.
