This paper offers valuable insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. A particularly strong contribution is Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which demonstrates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the conventional L-based sampling strategy. To further improve the manuscript, I offer the following suggestions for the authors' consideration.

1.  Line 85: There appears to be a minor typo in the denominator of p^*_i; it should be x_k instead of x.
2.  Line 135: For notational consistency, the authors may wish to remove the subscript k from \hat{p} and \hat{c}.
3.  All figures are currently too small to be legible, with ticks, labels, and numbers being indiscernible. The authors are strongly encouraged to enlarge the figures, perhaps by moving some to an appendix to create additional space.
4.  The theoretical analysis could be strengthened by providing a result, analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, that compares the proposed method with uniform sampling (p=1/n).
5.  A discussion regarding the overall complexity is needed. How does the new sampling method affect the total complexity of RCD or SGD? For example, what is the total complexity for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution when the O(n) time required to update l_k and u_k is included in the analysis?
