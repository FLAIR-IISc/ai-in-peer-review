- Proposes an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is computationally feasible and backed by theoretical guarantees.
- Main contribution: proves gradient-based sampling in CD can yield up to n-fold speedups over fixed sampling.
- Main contribution: develops a generic, efficient adaptive sampling strategy applicable to both CD and SGD.

- Main contribution: provides comprehensive empirical validation across multiple datasets and loss functions.

- Strength: compelling computational complexity analysis (O(n log n) per iteration), small relative overhead compared to O(d) gradient costs.
- Strength: strong empirical results showing substantial speedups across datasets and loss types while preserving theoretical guarantees.
- Strength: clear presentation, well-structured sections, clear notation, and detailed proofs in the appendix.
- Strength: effectively contrasts with prior work, addressing limitations of previous adaptive sampling methods.
