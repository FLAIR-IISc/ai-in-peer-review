- Proposes a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling method using lower/upper gradient bounds, reducing adaptive sampling to a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.
- Method is proven optimal within the provided bounds and strictly dominates uniform and fixed importance sampling while adding negligible computational overhead.
- Paper is well-written and presents a significant theoretical and practical contribution to importance sampling techniques.
- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for α*; needs verification and correction.
- Example 3.1: inconsistency about whether setting c to the gradient bounds improves over uniform sampling; example must clarify objective values and when bounds help or are suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4: remove redundant condition checking m == max(l^{sort}); correct update from u^{sort} = c^{sort} to u^{sort} = u^{sort} (use correct variable).
- Experiments: should compare against additional adaptive schemes ([2], [5], [21]) and non-uniform fixed importance sampling; include timing results for SGD to support efficiency claims.
- References: fix incorrect title for [14] and add Csiba & Richtarik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv:1603.01407) to related work.
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed technical issues and updates; reviewer marks the paper as strongly acceptable.
