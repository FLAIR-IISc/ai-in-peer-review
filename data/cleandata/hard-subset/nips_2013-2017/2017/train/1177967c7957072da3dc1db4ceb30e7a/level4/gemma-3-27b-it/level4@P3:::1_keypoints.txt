- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD based on lower/upper bounds on gradients.  
- Introduces an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal relative to the given bounds.  
- Shows the method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.  
- Novel formulation: selecting the optimal sampling distribution cast as a convex optimization problem.  
- Provides an efficient algorithm to solve the convex formulation.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees with rigorous proofs of optimality and efficiency.  
- Computationally practical for large-scale problems; overhead clearly characterized and minimal.  
- Method is generic and easily integrated into existing coordinate descent and stochastic gradient frameworks.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation demonstrates effectiveness on real-world datasets.  
- Builds on and connects well to prior adaptive importance sampling literature.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to other adaptive sampling schemes (missing broader benchmark).  
- Weakness: missing timing results for SGD, limiting assessment for that context.  
- Minor technical issues (Lemma 2.1, Example 3.1, Algorithm 4) were raised but addressed satisfactorily in the rebuttal.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, well-motivated contribution with strengths outweighing minor flaws.
