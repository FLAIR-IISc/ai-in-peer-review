This paper provides interesting insights and useful analysis for Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. Theorem 3.2, in particular, offers valuable conclusions, with Conclusion 2 being especially noteworthy as it suggests that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. 

I have several comments that I would like the authors to consider in order to further improve the paper. 

Firstly, there appears to be a minor error in Line 85, where the denominator of p^*i should be xk instead of x. 

Secondly, in Line 135, the subscripts k for \hat p and \hat c may be removed to maintain consistency in notation.

Thirdly, the figures in the paper are too small, making it difficult to discern any ticks, labels, numbers, or text. I suggest relocating some figures to the appendix to create space for larger, more legible versions.

Fourthly, if feasible, it would be beneficial to provide a comparison between the proposed sampling method and uniform sampling (p=1/n), similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, which currently compares with the L_i-based method.

Lastly, I would appreciate it if the authors could discuss the impact of the new sampling method on the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For instance, what would be the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution?
