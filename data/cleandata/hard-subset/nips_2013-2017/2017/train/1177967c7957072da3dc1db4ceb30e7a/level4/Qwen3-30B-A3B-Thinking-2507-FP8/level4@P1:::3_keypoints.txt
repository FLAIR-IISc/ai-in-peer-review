- Proposes a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that approximates gradient-based sampling with theoretical guarantees.
- Core contribution: a convex optimization formulation that computes the optimal sampling distribution under safe gradient bounds.
- Theoretical guarantee: proposed sampling is always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2).
- Provides competitive-ratio bounds and geometric analysis of gradient bounds (Lemmas 3.5–3.6).
- Efficient implementation: O(n log n) time per iteration; O(n) maintenance of bounds for generalized linear models.
- Method is generic and easily integrated into existing algorithms with minimal computational overhead.
- Empirical results comprehensive: performance close to optimal gradient-based sampling while keeping low overhead.
- Even very loose gradient bounds (e.g., from Cauchy–Schwarz) yield significant improvements over fixed sampling.
- Experiments span multiple losses (squared hinge, logistic, square), regularizers (L1, L2), and datasets (rcv1, real-sim, news20) with attention to complexity.
- Analysis for both CD and SGD is thorough, with clear examples showing advantages over static strategies.
- Presentation is generally clear and precise, though Sections 3.2 and 4 could use more intuitive explanations.
- Main weakness: poor-quality figures in Section 5 (small fonts, low contrast) hinder assessment of results.
- SGD improvements are less pronounced in some experiments (Figures 7–9) and need more detailed explanation.
- Originality is strong: reframes adaptive sampling as a robust optimization problem, addressing lack of guarantees in prior work.
- Significance is high: practical for large-scale ML since overhead is negligible relative to gradient costs; notably strong speedups for CD.
- Overall recommendation: accept—technically sound, original, well-validated, and significant.
