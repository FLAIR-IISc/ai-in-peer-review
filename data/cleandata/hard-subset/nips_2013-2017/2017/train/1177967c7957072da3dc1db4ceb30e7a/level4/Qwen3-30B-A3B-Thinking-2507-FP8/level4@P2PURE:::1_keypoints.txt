- Proposal of a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy using lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling efficiently.
- Method proven optimal relative to the provided bounds and shown to strictly dominate uniform and fixed importance sampling with negligible overhead.
- Novel formulation recasting optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem.
- Accompanying efficient algorithm to solve the convex formulation.
- Paper is well-written and a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.

- Lemma 2.1: alleged missing factor of 2 in the expression for alpha*.
- Example 3.1: apparent contradiction where choosing c at a bound seems to outperform uniform sampling, needing clarification.
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: condition k ≤ m will never hold given m = max(l^{sort}); intended logic unclear.
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: should reference u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Numerical results: request comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes ([2],[5],[21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Missing timing results for SGD to evaluate computational efficiency.
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title and should be corrected.
- Suggested additional reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).
