The paper introduces a “safe” adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient algorithms. Using lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes, the authors construct an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. They prove that the resulting sampling distribution (i) is optimal with respect to the provided bounds (best worst-case guarantee), (ii) strictly improves over uniform and fixed importance sampling, and (iii) can be computed with negligible overhead in many applications. While adaptive importance sampling has been explored before, this work recasts the choice of sampling probabilities as a convex optimization (min–max) problem and provides an efficient solver for it.

The manuscript is clearly written and represents a valuable contribution to the literature on importance sampling.
