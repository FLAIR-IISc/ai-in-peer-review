- Paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent/SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds and an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling.  
- Sampling rule is provably optimal under the bounds, guaranteed to outperform uniform or any fixed importance sampling, with negligible overhead.  
- Novel formulation casts optimal sampling distribution choice as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solver.  
- Overall evaluation: well written and a solid contribution to importance sampling literature.  
- Issue: Proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in α^*.  
- Issue: Example 3.1 is confusing — objective in (7) is to maximize, and setting c to the upper/lower bound seems to outperform uniform, contradicting earlier claims that this naive strategy can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4, line 4: condition using m = max(l^{sort}) may never be satisfied — question of correctness.  
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely typo — should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Empirics: should compare to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling baselines.  
- Empirics: missing timing results for SGD.  
- Bibliography: title of reference [14] is incorrect.  
- Suggestion: add Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer appreciates responses and recommends acceptance.
