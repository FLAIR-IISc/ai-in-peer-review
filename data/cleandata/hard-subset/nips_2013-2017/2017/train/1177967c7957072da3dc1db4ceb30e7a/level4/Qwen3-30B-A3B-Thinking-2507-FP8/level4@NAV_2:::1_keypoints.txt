- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy using lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Formulates the optimal sampling selection as a convex optimization problem with rigorous worst-case guarantees.  
- Shows the sampling distribution is provably optimal w.r.t. the provided bounds and strictly dominates uniform/fixed importance sampling.  
- Claims negligible computational overhead and practical efficiency (O(n log n) per iteration).  
- Extends prior adaptive sampling approaches by providing a convex formulation that addresses limitations of previous approximations.  
- Provides thorough empirical validation across multiple datasets and loss functions, demonstrating consistent speedups in coordinate descent.  
- Lemma 2.1 has a proof error: α* should include a factor of 2 (missing currently).  
- Example 3.1 is unclear about why choosing c = u or c = ` is suboptimal and needs clarification.  
- Algorithm 4 contains errors: Line 4 ("m = max(`^{sort})") seems incorrect for sorted lower bounds, and Line 7 should reference `^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete; should include other adaptive schemes (e.g., Allen-Zhu et al. 2017; Perekrestenko et al. 2017) and fixed non-uniform sampling baselines.  
- Missing timing results for SGD experiments despite claims of computational efficiency.  
- Reference errors: title of [14] is incorrect and should be "Safe Screening with Variational Inequalities."  
- Bibliography omission: add Csiba & Richtárik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches."  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant contribution with theoretical and practical impact; recommend accept after addressing the technical corrections and experimental gaps.
