- Addresses adaptive importance sampling for large-scale optimization, proposing an efficient approximation using safe gradient bounds.  
- Provides provable guarantees and a novel convex formulation for sampling that ensures no worse performance than uniform sampling.  
- Avoids full gradient computation by leveraging safe bounds, offering a practical algorithm with theoretical support.  
- The method is generic and integrates easily into existing algorithms, showing significant speed-ups for CD and SGD.  
- Strong theoretical rigor with clear optimization formulation and key theorems (3.2, 3.4).  
- Extensive numerical validation and a concrete GLM example illustrate practical applicability.  
- Well-situated within prior work, appropriately citing related adaptive and approximation literature.  
- Presentation is occasionally dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations linking lemmas to the narrative.  
- Insufficient discussion on the assumptions and sensitivity of safe gradient bounds, and cases where bounds may be hard to obtain.  
- Competitive ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5, 3.6) feels disconnected and could be streamlined or better contextualized.  
- Experimental choices (datasets, regularization) and figure readability need clearer justification and improvement.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss safe bounds sensitivity, and enhance figure quality.
