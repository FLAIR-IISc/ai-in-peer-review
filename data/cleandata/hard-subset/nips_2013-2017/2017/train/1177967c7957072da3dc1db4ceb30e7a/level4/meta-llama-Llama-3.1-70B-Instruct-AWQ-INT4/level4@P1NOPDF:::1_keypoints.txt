1. Paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling.  
2. Authors show this strategy is optimal with respect to the provided bounds and outperforms uniform/fixed importance sampling while being computationally cheap.  
3. Novelty: they recast selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provide an efficient solver.  
4. Writing and overall contribution are strong; reviewer views the paper as a valuable contribution.  
5. Proof issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^* — clarification needed.  
6. Example 3.1: contradiction between Eq. (7) and the example about setting c to bounds — clarify the example's intent.  
7. Algorithm 4, line 4: condition using m = max(l^{sort}) seems suspicious — explain whether it can ever hold.  
8. Algorithm 4, line 7: likely bug — u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}; please verify.  
9. Experiments: add comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.  
10. Empirics: include timing results for SGD to strengthen evaluation.  
11. References: correct the title of reference [14] and consider adding Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv).
