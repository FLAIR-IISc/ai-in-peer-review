- Proposes an adaptive importance sampling strategy that ensures "safety" for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods using lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- The method is theoretically optimal with respect to those bounds and computable with negligible extra cost.  
- Introduces a novel convex optimization formulation for selecting the optimal sampling distribution and an efficient solver.  
- Manuscript is well-written and considered a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.  
- Lemma 2.1 proof likely misses a factor of 2 in the calculation of alpha*.  
- Example 3.1 and equation (7) suggest setting c to bounds may outperform uniform sampling, which appears to contradict the preceding claim that such naive choices can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4 issues: line 4’s condition using m = max(l^{sort}) may never be met; line 7 likely should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (refs [2], [5], [21]) and fixed non-uniform importance sampling.  
- Timing results for SGD are missing and should be provided.  
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title and should be corrected.  
- Add missing reference: "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" by Csiba and Richtarik (arXiv, 2016).  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.
