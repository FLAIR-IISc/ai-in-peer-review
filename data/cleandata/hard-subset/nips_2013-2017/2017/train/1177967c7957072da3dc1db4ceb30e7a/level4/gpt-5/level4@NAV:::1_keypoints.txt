- Introduces a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using lower and upper gradient bounds.
- Proposes an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling that is optimal with respect to the given bounds.
- Method strictly outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible additional computational cost.
- New formulation casts optimal sampling distribution choice as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.
- Paper is clearly written and a solid contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*.
- Example 3.1 is confusing: setting c to bounds seems to outperform uniform sampling, contradicting prior claim that naive bound choices can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: condition with m = max(l^{sort}) may never be met.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
- Missing timing results for SGD experiments.
- Reference [14] has an incorrect paper title.
- Add reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer thanks authors for addressing comments and recommends accept.
