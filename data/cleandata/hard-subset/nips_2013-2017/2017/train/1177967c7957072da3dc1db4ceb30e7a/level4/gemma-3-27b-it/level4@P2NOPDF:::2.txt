This paper presents interesting insights into Rao-Blackwellized Monte Carlo (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms, and offers a valuable contribution to the field. Specifically, Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 is a significant result, demonstrating the potential of the proposed sampling method to outperform the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have several comments that I believe would strengthen the paper.

First, there appears to be a typographical error in Line 85. The denominator of p^*i should be *x<sub>k</sub>*, not *x*. 

Second, for consistency in notation, I suggest removing the subscript *k* from  \hat{p} and \hat{c} in Line 135.

Third, the figures provided are currently difficult to interpret. They lack legible tick labels, axes labels, numbers, and accompanying text – and are generally too small. To improve clarity and utilize space effectively, I recommend increasing the figure size and moving supporting figures or detailed views to the appendix. Visual communication is crucial, and larger, clearly labeled figures will greatly enhance understanding of the results.

Fourth, while the comparison to L<sub>i</sub>-based sampling is valuable, it would be beneficial to see a similar result to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the performance of the proposed method against uniform sampling (p=1/n). This would provide a broader context for the proposed method’s advantages and disadvantages.

Finally, a discussion of the computational complexity implications of the new sampling method is needed. Specifically, it would be informative to analyze how this method impacts the overall complexity of RCD or SGD. For instance, could the authors provide an analysis of the total complexity—including the O(n) time required for updating l<sub>k</sub> and u<sub>k</sub>—for RCD to achieve an ε-optimal solution? Understanding the computational cost is essential for evaluating the practical applicability of the proposed method.
