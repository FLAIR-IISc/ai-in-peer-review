- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD using safe lower and upper bounds on gradients.  
- Notes optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically justified but often infeasible to compute directly.  
- Formulates selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem using gradient bounds.  
- Sampling scheme is generic and can be easily integrated into existing algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and strong theoretical results (notably Theorem 3.2).  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive across multiple ML tasks and datasets.  
- Minor correction: in Line 85 the denominator of p^*_i should be x_k, not x.  
- Minor suggestion: remove subscript k for \hat p and \hat c in Line 135 for consistency.  
- Presentation issue: figures are too small and lack labels/numbers/words; consider moving some to the appendix to enlarge others.  
- Suggestion: provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 comparing the method to uniform sampling (p = 1/n).  
- Request to analyze how the new sampling affects total complexity (e.g., include O(n) time for updating l_k and u_k when reporting RCD/SGD complexity to reach ε-optimality).  
- Quality rating 8/10, clarity 9/10, originality 8.5/10, significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient sampling scheme, solid theoretical insights, comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: small/poorly labeled figures and insufficient analysis of total computational complexity.
