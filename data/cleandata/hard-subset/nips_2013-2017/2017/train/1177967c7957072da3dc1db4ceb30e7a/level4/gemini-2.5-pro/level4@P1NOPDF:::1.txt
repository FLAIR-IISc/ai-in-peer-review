The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. The proposed method uses lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. A key contribution is the novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, for which the authors provide an efficient algorithm. The method is proven to be the optimal strategy with respect to these bounds, outperforming both uniform and fixed importance sampling, while being computationally efficient with negligible overhead.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that makes a solid contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

**Comments and Questions:**

1.  **Proof of Lemma 2.1:** In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears there may be a missing factor of 2 in the expression for α*.

2.  **Clarity of Example 3.1:** This example was slightly confusing. Given that the objective in (7) is a maximization, it seems to imply that setting *c* to its upper or lower bound would be superior to uniform sampling. This appears to contradict the preceding statement that such a naive approach can be suboptimal. Could the authors clarify the intended takeaway from this example?

3.  **Algorithm 4 Logic:** In Algorithm 4, line 4, the condition is `l_i^{sort} >= m`. Since `m = max(l^{sort})`, this condition appears to be satisfied only when `l_i^{sort} = m`. Is this interpretation correct? A brief clarification would be helpful.

4.  **Experimental Comparisons:** The numerical results would be strengthened by including comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]). Additionally, comparing against a well-chosen fixed, non-uniform importance sampling distribution would provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

5.  **SGD Timing Results:** The experiments provide timing results for coordinate descent but not for SGD. Could the authors include or comment on the timing results for the SGD experiments?
