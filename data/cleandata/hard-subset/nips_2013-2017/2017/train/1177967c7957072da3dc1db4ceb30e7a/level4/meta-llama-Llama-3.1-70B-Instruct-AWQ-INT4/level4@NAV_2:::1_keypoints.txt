- Proposes a novel adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradients.  
- Provides an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that incurs negligible extra computational cost.  
- Shows the method is optimal with respect to the provided bounds and always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Formulates the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and presents an efficient solver.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and supports the method’s claims.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- The approach is original and offers a new perspective compared to prior adaptive importance sampling methods.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons with other adaptive sampling schemes beyond uniform and fixed importance sampling.  
- Missing timing results for SGD (only CD timing provided).  
- Technically sound and complete work with significant potential applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept, with strengths outweighing the noted weaknesses.
