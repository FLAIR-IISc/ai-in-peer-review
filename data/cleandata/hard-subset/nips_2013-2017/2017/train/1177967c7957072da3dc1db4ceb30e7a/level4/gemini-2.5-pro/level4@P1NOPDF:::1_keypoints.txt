- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Formulates optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm.  
- Proves the method is optimal with respect to the provided bounds and outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling with negligible computational overhead.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper that makes a solid contribution to importance sampling techniques.  

Comments/questions:
- Lemma 2.1: possible missing factor of 2 in the expression for α*.  
- Example 3.1: confusing—maximization in (7) seems to favor setting c to its bounds, which appears to contradict the claim that that naive approach can be suboptimal; request clarification.  
- Algorithm 4: line 4 condition `l_i^{sort} >= m` seems satisfied only when `l_i^{sort} = m`; please clarify.  
- Experiments: recommend comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2],[5],[21]) and to a well-chosen fixed non-uniform importance sampling baseline.  
- SGD timing: provide or comment on timing results for the SGD experiments as done for coordinate descent.  

Minor points:
- Typo in Algorithm 4: line 7 `c_j^{sort}` likely should be `u_j^{sort}`.  
- Reference [14] appears to have an incorrect title.
