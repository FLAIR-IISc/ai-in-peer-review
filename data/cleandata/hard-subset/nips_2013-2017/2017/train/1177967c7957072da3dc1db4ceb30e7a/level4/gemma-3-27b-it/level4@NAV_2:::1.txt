The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Based on lower and upper bounds on the gradient values, an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling is proposed. The method is proven to be the best strategy with respect to the bounds, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently at negligible extra cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been previously proposed, the authors present a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it. 

This paper is well written and a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques. The problem is well-motivated, and the proposed approach offers both theoretical guarantees and practical efficiency. The connection to existing work is clearly established, and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. 

Specifically, the paper demonstrates that adaptive sampling can lead to significant speedups compared to static sampling strategies, particularly in the context of coordinate descent. The derivation of the optimization problem in Section 3, and the efficient algorithm presented in Algorithm 4, are particularly noteworthy. The careful analysis of the computational complexity and the discussion of practical considerations in Section 4 further strengthen the contribution.

Minor points:
* The proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in α*.
* Example 3.1 – The discussion around maximizing in (7) and the comparison to uniform sampling could be clarified to avoid potential confusion. It’s not immediately clear why setting c to the upper or lower bound is better than using uniform sampling, given the preceding statement about suboptimal choices.
* Line 4 of Algorithm 4 – Given that m = max(√L⁻¹`), will this condition ever be satisfied?
* Line 7 of Algorithm 4 – Should be u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}?
* The numerical results could benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5],[21]), and against fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution. 
* It would be valuable to see timing results for SGD to provide a complete picture of the method's performance.
* The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect. 
* Consider adding reference: Csiba and Richtarik. "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" 2016 (available on arXiv).

Arguments for acceptance:
* **Strong theoretical foundations:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating the advantages of the proposed sampling scheme.
* **Practical efficiency:** The algorithm is computationally efficient and can be integrated into existing optimization algorithms with minimal overhead.
* **Clear exposition:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the technical details accessible.
* **Empirical validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on real-world datasets.
* **Novelty:** The formulation of the sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and the efficient algorithm to solve it are novel contributions.

Arguments against acceptance:
* The comparison to other adaptive sampling schemes in the experimental section is limited.
* The lack of timing results for SGD is a minor drawback.
* Some minor typos/clarifications are needed (mentioned above).

Overall, the strengths of this paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
