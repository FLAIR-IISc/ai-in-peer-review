This paper presents interesting insights regarding Resampled Contrastive Divergence (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly valuable, as it indicates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have the following comments for the authors to address:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be x<sub>k</sub>, not x.
2. In Line 135, for consistency, consider removing the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c.
3. All figures currently lack legible ticks, labels, and numbers, and are generally too small. To improve readability and conserve space, please consider moving some figures to the appendix and increasing the size of those remaining in the main text.
4. It would be beneficial to present a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the proposed method to uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L<sub>i</sub>-based method.
5. Please discuss the impact of this new sampling method on the overall computational complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, please provide an analysis of the total complexity – including the O(n) time required for updating l<sub>k</sub> and u<sub>k</sub> – for RCD to achieve an epsilon-optimal solution.
