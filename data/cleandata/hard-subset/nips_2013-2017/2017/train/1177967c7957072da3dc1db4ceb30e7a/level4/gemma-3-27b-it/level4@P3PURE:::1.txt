The authors present a “safe” adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Based on lower and upper bounds on gradient values, they propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling. The method is proven to be optimal with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed with negligible additional cost. While adaptive importance sampling strategies are not new, the authors present a novel formulation, framing the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and providing an efficient algorithm for its solution.

This paper is well-written and represents a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.
