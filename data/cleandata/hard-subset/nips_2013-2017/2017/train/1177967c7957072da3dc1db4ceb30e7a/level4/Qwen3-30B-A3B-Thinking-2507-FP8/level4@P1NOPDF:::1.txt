

The authors propose a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes, they introduce an efficient approximation for gradient-based sampling. The method is proven optimal with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. Although adaptive importance sampling has been explored previously, the authors present a novel formulation that casts the optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem, accompanied by an efficient solution algorithm. The paper is well-structured and constitutes a valuable contribution to importance sampling research.

**Comments:**

*   **Lemma 2.1:** The proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for α*.
*   **Example 3.1:** The example claims maximizing (7) suggests setting c to the gradient bounds yields better results than uniform sampling. However, this directly contradicts the preceding statement that naively setting c to the bounds is suboptimal. Please clarify this apparent inconsistency.
*   **Algorithm 4, Line 4:** Given m = max(l^{sort}), will the condition `l_i > m` ever hold? If not, the check is redundant.
*   **Algorithm 4, Line 7:** Should the update use `u^{sort}` instead of `c^{sort}`?
*   **Numerical Results:** The evaluation would benefit significantly from comparisons against other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and against fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution (beyond uniform). Current comparisons lack this context.
*   **Timing Results:** Why are timing results for SGD omitted? This is a critical metric for practical adoption.
*   **Reference [14]:** The title of the cited paper is incorrect.
*   **Missing Reference:** Please add the reference to Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).
