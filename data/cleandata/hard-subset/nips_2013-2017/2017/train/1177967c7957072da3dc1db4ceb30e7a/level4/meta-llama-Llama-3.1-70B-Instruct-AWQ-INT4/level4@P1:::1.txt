The paper "Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling" presents a novel strategy for adaptive importance sampling in coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) methods. The authors propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling, which is based on safe bounds on the gradient. The method is proven to be the best strategy with respect to the bounds, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently for negligible extra cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed method. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The authors' main contribution is the formulation of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, which can be solved efficiently. This is a significant improvement over previous adaptive importance sampling strategies, which often rely on approximations or heuristics.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the related work in the field. The numerical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.
