I found this paper to be interesting, providing valuable insights into the RCD and SGD algorithms. The conclusion of Theorem 3.2 is particularly noteworthy, as it suggests that the proposed sampling method is superior to the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have several comments that I would like the authors to consider:

1. In Line 85, I believe there is a typographical error in the denominator of p^*i, which should be xk instead of x.
2. In Line 135, for consistency, the authors may want to remove the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c.
3. Unfortunately, the figures are too small, making it difficult to discern any ticks, labels, numbers, or words. To address this, I suggest relocating some figures to the appendix to free up space, allowing for the display of larger, more legible figures.
4. If feasible, I would appreciate it if the authors could provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the proposed sampling method to uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. I would like the authors to remark on the impact of this new sampling method on the total complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, it would be helpful to know the total complexity (including the O(n) time required for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution.
