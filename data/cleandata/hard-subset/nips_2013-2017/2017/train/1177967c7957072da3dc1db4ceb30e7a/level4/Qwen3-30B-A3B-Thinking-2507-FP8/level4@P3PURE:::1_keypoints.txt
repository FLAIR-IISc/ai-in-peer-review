- Paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling using lower/upper gradient bounds, proven optimal w.r.t. these bounds, dominating uniform and fixed importance sampling with negligible overhead; casts optimal sampling as a convex problem with an efficient solver — well written and a meaningful contribution.

- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for α*.

- Example 3.1: contradiction between claiming c at bounds outperforms uniform sampling and earlier statement that that naive approach can be suboptimal, causing confusion.

- Algorithm 4, Line 4: condition l_i ≤ m is redundant because m = max(l^sort).

- Algorithm 4, Line 7: update should use u^sort instead of c^sort.

- Numerical results: should include comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.

- Timing results: missing timing measurements for SGD and should be provided.

- Reference [14]: title is incorrect; should be "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (Csiba and Richtarik, 2016).

- Reference addition: include Csiba and Richtarik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv).

- Post-rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with authors' responses; identified issues resolved and paper recommended for acceptance.
