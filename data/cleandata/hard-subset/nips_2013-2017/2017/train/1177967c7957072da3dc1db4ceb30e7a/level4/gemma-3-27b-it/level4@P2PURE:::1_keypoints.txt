- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD.  
- Leverages lower and upper bounds on gradients to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Method is proven optimal under those bounds and consistently outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling with minimal overhead.  
- Novel formulation casting selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.  
- Paper is well-written and a valuable contribution to importance sampling literature.  
- Issue: Proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in the definition of α*.  
- Issue: Example 3.1/confusion in Eq. (7) — maximizing objective suggests setting c to bounds may be superior, contradicting the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: condition involving m = max(l^sort) may never be satisfied.  
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: likely should use u^sort instead of c^sort.  
- Experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.  
- Paper lacks timing results for SGD; please report computational/time overhead.  
- Citation issue: title of reference [14] is inaccurate.  
- Suggest adding reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer now recommends the paper for acceptance.
