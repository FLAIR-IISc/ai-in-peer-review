- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and SGD that approximates optimal gradient-based sampling using safe gradient lower/upper bounds.  
- Motivates the work by noting optimal adaptive sampling is theoretically good but often computationally infeasible.  
- Implements an efficient approximation relying on safe bounds to make adaptive sampling practical.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including properties and a competitive ratio for the proposed method.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and runtime across diverse ML tasks.  
- Balances theoretical foundation and practical evaluation; method is both well-justified and effective in practice.  
- Paper is well-organized, accessible, and technically sound.  
- Original contribution offering a new perspective on adaptive importance sampling; addresses an important problem.  
- Weakness: figures in Section 5 are hard to read and should be larger with bigger labels.  
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts and could use clarification.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on improving the visibility/labels of figures in Section 5.
