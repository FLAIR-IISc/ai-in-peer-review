

This paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, leveraging safe lower and upper bounds on gradient values to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The authors demonstrate that their sampling distribution is provably optimal with respect to the given bounds, strictly dominates uniform/fixed importance sampling, and incurs negligible computational overhead. This work extends prior adaptive sampling approaches (e.g., Csiba & Richtárik, 2016; Zhao & Zhang, 2015) by formulating the optimal sampling selection as a convex optimization problem with rigorous worst-case guarantees, addressing a key limitation in existing methods where approximations often fail to outperform static sampling.

The paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of adaptive sampling for large-scale optimization. Key strengths include: (1) the novel convex optimization formulation for adaptive sampling under bounded gradient information, (2) rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorem 3.2) showing dominance over fixed sampling, (3) practical computational efficiency (O(n log n) per iteration), and (4) thorough empirical validation across multiple datasets and loss functions.

Critical technical comments:  
- Lemma 2.1: The proof requires correction—α* should include a factor of 2 (currently missing).  
- Example 3.1: The example's implication regarding c = u or c = ` being suboptimal is unclear; the authors should clarify why the naive approach fails despite the subsequent claim.  
- Algorithm 4: Line 4 ("m = max(`^{sort})") is problematic since `^{sort} is sorted lower bounds; this condition likely never holds. Line 7 should reference `^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.  
- Experimental design: The numerical results would be strengthened by comparing against other adaptive schemes (e.g., Allen-Zhu et al., ICML 2017; Perekrestenko et al., AISTATS 2017) and fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions.  
- Missing data: No timing results for SGD experiments are provided, despite the claimed computational efficiency.  
- References: Title of [14] is incorrect; "Safe Screening with Variational Inequalities" should be corrected.  
- Additional reference: Add Csiba & Richtárik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," to the bibliography.

The paper addresses a well-motivated problem with clear theoretical and practical significance. The proposed method advances the state of the art in adaptive sampling by providing a computationally feasible scheme with provable guarantees, resolving a key gap in prior work. The empirical results support the theoretical claims, demonstrating consistent speedups over fixed sampling in CD while maintaining negligible overhead.

Strengths:  
✓ Novel convex optimization framework for adaptive sampling  
✓ Rigorous worst-case guarantees (Theorem 3.2)  
✓ Practical efficiency (O(n log n) per iteration)  
✓ Comprehensive empirical validation  

Weaknesses:  
✗ Technical corrections needed in Lemma 2.1 and Algorithm 4  
✗ Missing comparisons to key adaptive schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21])  
✗ Absence of SGD timing results  
✗ Ambiguity in Example 3.1  

After addressing these points in the rebuttal, the paper is a clear accept. The contribution is technically sound, well-motivated, and makes a meaningful advancement in adaptive sampling for large-scale optimization.
