This paper presents interesting insights regarding Resampled Control Variates (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly useful, as it suggests the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have the following comments for the authors to address:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*<sub>i</sub> should be x<sub>k</sub>, not x.
2. In Line 135, for consistency, I suggest removing the subscript *k* from both \hat{p} and \hat{c}.
3. All figures currently lack ticks, labels, and numerical annotations, and are quite small. To improve readability and utilize space effectively, please consider moving some figures to the appendix and increasing the size of those retained in the main text.
4. It would be valuable to provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing performance against uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L<sub>i</sub>-based method.
5. Please discuss the impact of this new sampling method on the overall computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, please provide an analysis of the total complexity – including the O(n) time required for updating l<sub>k</sub> and u<sub>k</sub> – for RCD to achieve an ε-optimal solution.
