This paper presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. The proposed method utilizes lower and upper bounds on gradient values to efficiently approximate an optimal, gradient-based sampling distribution. The authors prove that this strategy is optimal with respect to the given bounds, consistently outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently with negligible overhead. While adaptive importance sampling has been studied before, a key contribution of this work is the novel formulation of the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, for which an efficient solution algorithm is provided.

The paper is well-written and represents a solid contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

**Comments:**

*   In the proof of Lemma 2.1, there appears to be a missing factor of 2 in the expression for α*.
*   Regarding Example 3.1: The text preceding the example claims that naively setting the constants *c* to the upper or lower bounds can be suboptimal. However, the example itself, which aims to maximize the objective in (7), seems to suggest that setting *c* to one of the bounds is superior to uniform sampling. The intention of this example is confusing; please clarify.
*   In Algorithm 4, line 4: Given that *m* is defined as `max(l^{sort})`, it is unclear if the condition `l_i^{sort} == m` can ever be false. Please clarify the purpose of this check.
*   In Algorithm 4, line 7: Should `c^{sort}` be `u^{sort}`?
*   The numerical results would be more compelling with comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed, non-uniform importance sampling.
*   Why are there no timing results for the SGD experiments? Including these would be valuable for assessing the computational overhead in that setting.
*   The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect.
*   Please consider adding the following reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv 2016.
