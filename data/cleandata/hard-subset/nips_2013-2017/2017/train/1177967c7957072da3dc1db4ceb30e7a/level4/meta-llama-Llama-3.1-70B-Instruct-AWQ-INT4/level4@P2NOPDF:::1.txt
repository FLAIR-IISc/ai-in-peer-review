The authors present a novel, "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy tailored for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. This approach leverages lower and upper bounds on gradient values to devise an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling. The proposed method is proven to be optimal with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling. Notably, this strategy can be computed with negligible extra cost. Although adaptive importance sampling techniques have been previously explored, the authors introduce a fresh perspective by formulating the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and devising an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and constitutes a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:

1. In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears that a factor of 2 is missing in the expression for alpha^*. The authors should verify and correct this.

2. Example 3.1 is somewhat perplexing. Given that the goal is to maximize the expression in (7), it seems that setting c to either the upper or lower bound would yield better results than uniform sampling. This seems to contradict the preceding statement, which suggests that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal. The authors should clarify this example to ensure it accurately conveys the intended message.

3. In Algorithm 4, the condition on Line 4, given that m = max(l^{sort}), may never be satisfied. The authors should verify the correctness of this condition.

4. In Algorithm 4, Line 7 should likely read u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}. The authors should confirm this.

5. The numerical results would benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those presented in [2], [5], and [21], as well as fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

6. The authors should consider providing timing results for SGD to give a more complete picture of the method's efficiency.

7. The title of the paper in reference [14] appears to be incorrect. The authors should verify and correct this.

8. The authors may want to consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016), available on arXiv.
