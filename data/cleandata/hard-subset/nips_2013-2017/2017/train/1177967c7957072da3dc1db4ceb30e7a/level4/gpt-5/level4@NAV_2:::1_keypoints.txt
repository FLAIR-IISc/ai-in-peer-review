- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Proves the sampling distribution is optimal w.r.t. provided bounds (best worst-case), strictly improves over uniform and fixed importance sampling, and is efficiently computable with negligible overhead.  
- Recasts sampling probability choice as a convex min–max optimization and provides an efficient solver.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and is a valuable contribution to importance sampling literature.  
- Lemma 2.1: α* in the proof appears to be missing a factor of 2.  
- Example 3.1: Clarify whether choosing c at lower/upper bounds (endpoints) is intended to outperform uniform sampling, which seems to contradict earlier statement that endpoint choices can be suboptimal.  
- Algorithm 4, line 4: With m initialized as max(ℓsort), question whether the condition can ever be triggered.  
- Algorithm 4, line 7: Likely should reference usort rather than csort.  
- Experiments: add comparisons to additional adaptive schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.  
- Experiments: include wall-clock timing results for SGD.  
- Reference issues: title of [14] is incorrect; add Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer considers the paper a clear accept.
