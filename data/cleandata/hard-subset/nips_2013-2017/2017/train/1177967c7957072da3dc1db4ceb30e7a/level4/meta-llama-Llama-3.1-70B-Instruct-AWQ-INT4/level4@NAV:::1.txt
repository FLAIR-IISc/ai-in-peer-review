Here's a paraphrased version of the review while keeping the original structure, meaning, and technical content intact:

The authors propose an innovative, "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy tailored for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. This approach leverages lower and upper bounds on gradient values to devise an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling. Notably, the method is proven to be optimal with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, all while incurring negligible additional computational costs. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been explored previously, the authors introduce a novel formulation that frames the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, accompanied by an efficient algorithm for solving it.

This paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:
The proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*. 
In Example 3.1, equation (7) aims to maximize the expression. Therefore, it seems that setting c to the upper or lower bound would yield better results than uniform sampling. This intention is unclear, especially given the preceding statement suggesting that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal.
In Algorithm 4, line 4, considering that m = max(l^{sort}), it is uncertain whether this condition will ever be satisfied.
In Algorithm 4, line 7, it seems that u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}.
To further strengthen the numerical results, comparisons with other existing adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions would be beneficial.
The absence of timing results for SGD is noticeable.
The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect.
The authors may consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016) on arXiv.
