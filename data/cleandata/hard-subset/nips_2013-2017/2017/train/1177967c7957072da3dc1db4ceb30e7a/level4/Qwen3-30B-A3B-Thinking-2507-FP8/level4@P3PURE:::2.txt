

This paper presents interesting insights regarding RCD and SGD algorithms, with Theorem 3.2's Conclusion 2 offering particularly valuable evidence that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L_i-based sampling strategy. I recommend the authors address the following points before publication:  

1.  **Line 85:** The denominator in the expression for $p^*_i$ should be $x_k$ (not $x$).  
2.  **Line 135:** To ensure consistency, remove the subscript $k$ from $\hat{p}$ and $\hat{c}$.  
3.  **Figures:** All figures lack sufficient axis labels, legends, and annotations due to their small size. Please relocate some elements (e.g., detailed labels) to the appendix to free up space for larger, more legible figures.  
4.  **Comparison with Uniform Sampling:** It would be valuable to provide an analogous result to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, explicitly comparing the new sampling method against uniform sampling ($p_i = 1/n$).  
5.  **Complexity Analysis:** Please clarify how the proposed sampling method impacts the total computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, state the total complexity (including the $O(n)$ cost for updating $l_k$ and $u_k$) required to achieve an $\epsilon$-optimal solution.
