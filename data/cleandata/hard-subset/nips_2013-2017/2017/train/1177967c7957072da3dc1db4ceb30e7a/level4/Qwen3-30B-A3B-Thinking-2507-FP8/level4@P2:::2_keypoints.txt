- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds to avoid expensive full-gradient computations.  
- Constructs an efficient sampling distribution provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Shows gradient-based sampling for CD can theoretically outperform fixed sampling, with potential speedups up to factor n.  
- Proposes a generic, efficient adaptive sampling strategy applicable to both CD and SGD.  
- Demonstrates efficient implementation on structured optimization problems.  
- Provides extensive numerical evidence across datasets and loss functions showing practical benefits.  
- Theoretical claims are technically sound; key results (notably Theorem 3.2) give rigorous performance guarantees.  
- Notes per-iteration computational cost is O(n log n) and analyzes how this compares to standard implementations.  
- Clarity is generally good and writing is well-organized, but figures lack readable labels, tick marks, and numbers.  
- Originality: builds on prior adaptive sampling work but offers a novel, convex-optimization-based safe sampling approach.  
- Significance: method is practically useful for improving convergence in CD/SGD, even with loose gradient bounds.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, general applicability, careful cost analysis, and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: figures are poorly labeled; missing theoretical comparison to uniform sampling (p_i = 1/n); incomplete total complexity analysis for ε-optimality.  
- Specific requested fixes: correct denominator in Line 85 (x_k not x); remove subscript k from \hat{p} and \hat{c} in Line 135 for consistency.  
- Suggested revisions: improve figure readability (or move details to appendix), add theoretical comparison to uniform sampling, and provide total complexity including O(n) gradient-bound update costs.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to optimization for ML that, with the suggested revisions, is suitable for NIPS.
