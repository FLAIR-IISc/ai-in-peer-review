This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified, but its computation is often infeasible. To address this issue, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient, which are used to formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem. The proposed sampling scheme is generic and can be easily integrated into existing algorithms.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach. The theoretical results, including Theorem 3.2, are well-presented and provide valuable insights into the properties of the proposed sampling scheme. The empirical evaluation is also comprehensive, covering various machine learning tasks and datasets.

I have a few minor comments and suggestions for the authors to consider:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be xk, not x.
2. In Line 135, the subscript k for \hat p and \hat c can be removed for consistency.
3. The figures are too small and lack labels, numbers, and words. Consider moving some figures to the appendix to gain space and show larger figures.
4. It would be helpful to provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the new sampling method with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. Please remark on how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution?

Regarding the quality, clarity, originality, and significance of the paper, I would rate it as follows:
