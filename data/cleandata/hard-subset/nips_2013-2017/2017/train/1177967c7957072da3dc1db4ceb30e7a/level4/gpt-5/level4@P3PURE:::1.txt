This paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Using lower and upper bounds on gradient coordinates, the authors derive an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. They cast the choice of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provide an efficient algorithm to solve it. Within the bounds model, the method is proven optimal, strictly improving over uniform or fixed importance sampling, and incurring negligible additional computational cost. The paper is clearly written and makes a solid contribution to the study of adaptive importance sampling.

Comments and questions:

- Lemma 2.1: The proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in α*. Please double-check the derivation.
- Example 3.1: In equation (7) the objective is maximized. In the example, it seems that setting c to the upper or lower bound would outperform uniform sampling. Is this the intended takeaway? This appears to conflict with the preceding statement that the naive approach of setting c to a bound can be suboptimal. Please clarify the example’s purpose and reconcile these statements.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: Given that m = max(l^{sort}), will this condition ever be satisfied? As written, it seems impossible; please check the logic.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: Should this be u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}?
- Experimental evaluation:
  - Please include comparisons against other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and also against fixed non-uniform importance sampling baselines.
  - Why are there no timing results for SGD? Reporting wall-clock time for SGD would help substantiate the “negligible extra cost” claim.
- References:
  - The title of reference [14] is incorrect; please fix.
  - Please add: Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv:1602.02283, 2016.
