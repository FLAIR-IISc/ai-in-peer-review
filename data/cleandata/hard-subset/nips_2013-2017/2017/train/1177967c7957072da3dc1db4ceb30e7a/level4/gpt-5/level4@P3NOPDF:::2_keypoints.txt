- Paper proposes a new sampling for RCD/SGD with compelling theory and practical implications, notably Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 showing improvement over L-based sampling.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and a theory-driven, easy-to-implement proposal.  
- Strength: Theorem 3.2 (Conclusion 2) is practically relevant for choosing sampling distributions.  
- Strength: Analysis appears carefully developed.  

- Correctness/notation issue: denominator p_i^* should use x_k (current iterate) not x (Line 85).  
- Notation consistency: consider removing or clarifying subscript k on hat p and hat c (Line 135).  

- Compare to uniform sampling: provide an analogue of Conclusion 2 vs uniform sampling or state conditions/regimes where the new scheme outperforms uniform; if theoretical dominance is infeasible, add discussion and empirical comparison.  

- Implementation overhead: analyze total computational complexity including the cost to update probabilities (l_k, u_k); clarify amortized per-iteration cost and whether updates are O(n) or can be made O(log n) with data structures.  
- RCD complexity: give end-to-end complexity (or estimate) to reach ε-optimality including maintenance/update costs and whether updates can be periodic.  
- SGD impact: clarify whether probability updates depend on gradients or costly quantities and how that affects wall-clock performance.  
- Empirics: include wall-clock plots and an ablation isolating the overhead of probability maintenance versus convergence gains.  

- Figures/presentation: enlarge and clean figures, standardize fonts/line widths, move some to appendix, and ensure axis labels/legends are readable.  

- Add a short algorithm box/pseudocode showing the sampling update and any data structures used.  
- Discuss robustness/sensitivity to conditioning, Lipschitz heterogeneity, and tunable parameters.  
- Explicitly state key assumptions (strong convexity, smoothness, bounded variance) near main theorems and comment on extensions to nonconvex settings.  

- Minor: proofread for typographical issues and ensure consistent notation for iterates and per-iteration vs global quantities.  

- Overall: promising work with potential practical impact; strongly recommend adding comparison to uniform sampling, full complexity analysis including update overhead, and improved figures/notation to strengthen the paper.
