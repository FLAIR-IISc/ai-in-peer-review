The authors introduce a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient algorithms. The proposed approach leverages lower and upper bounds on gradient norms to construct an efficient approximation for gradient-based sampling. The authors provide theoretical proof that their strategy is optimal given these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform and fixed importance sampling schemes. Furthermore, the method is computationally efficient, adding only negligible overhead. While other adaptive importance sampling techniques exist, the key novelty of this work lies in formulating the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, for which the authors also provide an efficient solver.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and represents a valuable contribution to the field of importance sampling.

Comments:
*   In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears that a factor of 2 is missing from the definition of alpha^*.
*   Regarding Example 3.1: Given that the objective in equation (7) is a maximization, the example suggests that setting c to its upper or lower bound is preferable to uniform sampling. This seems to contradict the preceding statement that such a "naive" approach can be suboptimal. Could the authors clarify the intent of this example?
*   In Algorithm 4, line 4: The variable m is defined as max(l^{sort}). With this definition, it is unclear if the condition in this line can ever be met. Please clarify.

*   The experimental evaluation would be strengthened by including comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (such as those in [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed, non-uniform importance sampling baselines.
*   The experiments for SGD are missing runtime performance results. Could the authors include these or explain their omission?
