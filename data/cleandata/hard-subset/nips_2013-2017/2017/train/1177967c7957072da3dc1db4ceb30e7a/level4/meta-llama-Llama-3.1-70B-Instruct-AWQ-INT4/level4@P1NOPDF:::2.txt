I found this paper to be interesting and providing valuable insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly useful, as it suggests that the new sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have several comments that I would like the authors to consider.

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be xk instead of x.
2. In Line 135, it would be consistent to remove the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c.
3. Unfortunately, the figures are too small, making it difficult to discern any ticks, labels, numbers, or words. To address this issue, I suggest relocating some of the figures to the appendix, allowing for larger versions to be presented in the main text.
4. If feasible, it would be beneficial to provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the new sampling method to uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. The authors should discuss how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For instance, what would be the total complexity (including the O(n) time required for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution?
