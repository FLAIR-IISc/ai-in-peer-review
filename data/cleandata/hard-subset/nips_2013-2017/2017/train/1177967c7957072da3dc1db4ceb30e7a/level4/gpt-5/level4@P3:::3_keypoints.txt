- Paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using per-coordinate lower/upper gradient bounds and a min-max formulation to get worst-case-optimal sampling.
- Couples the min-max sampling with a stepsize α = 1/v (v from the min-max), yielding directionally unbiased updates and improved one-step progress bounds.
- With full gradient information the method recovers gradient-based sampling and can give up to n× speed-up for CD over uniform sampling.
- Under only bounds, the “safe” distribution provably dominates fixed Li-based importance sampling and uniform sampling.
- The safe sampling distribution can be computed exactly in O(n log n) time via a sorting-based routine with O(n) updates of bounds.
- The paper shows efficient maintenance of safe bounds for generalized linear models (GLMs).
- Experiments: substantial iteration and wall-clock gains for CD; more modest improvements for SGD on several text datasets.
- Builds on prior work (non-uniform sampling, adaptive sampling, duality-gap/Gauss-Southwell, safe screening) with novelty in the explicit min-max formulation and exact solver.
- Theory is careful and largely sound; use of Sion’s minimax theorem is appropriate but precise conditions should be stated in the main text.
- Competitive-ratio analysis is informative though the lower-bounding subproblem can be non-convex and mainly interpretative.
- Algorithmic complexity and practical overhead are argued convincingly when per-iteration gradient cost is Θ(d) with d = Ω(n), but overhead may matter in other regimes.
- Experiments are relevant but would benefit from additional ablations (bound tightness, overhead vs. d/n, better inner-product bounds).
- Clarity issues: figures in Section 5 are hard to read; fix typos and clarify notation (e.g., l.- and u.-bounds).
- Need to summarize minimax-interchange assumptions, sketch Algorithm 4’s correctness, and clarify role of m and decision set D in the main text.
- Originality: novel safe min-max sampling over gradient-uncertainty sets and distribution-dependent step-size coupling.
- Significance: broadly applicable and easy to integrate, with meaningful gains for CD and potential for SGD when gradient norms are skewed or bounds are tight.
- Pros: principled min-max formulation, exact efficient solver, provable dominance over Li/uniform, recovers full-information sampling, practical GLM bounds, solid theory+experiments.
- Cons: hard-to-read figures, limited ablations on overhead and bounds, modest SGD gains, minimax/stepsize assumptions not explicit, O(n log n) overhead may hurt when d << n.
- Suggestions: enlarge plots, provide time-per-iteration breakdowns across d/n and sparsity, ablate bound tightness (e.g., exact vs Cauchy–Schwarz), compare SGD to more adaptive baselines, state Sion assumptions and give a proof sketch, clarify memory needs.
- Overall assessment: technically solid and well-motivated with strong CD results and practical utility; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity (figures) and adding small ablations.
