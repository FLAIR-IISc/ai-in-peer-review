- Introduces a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate the optimal sampling distribution.
- Main novelty: formulates sampling-distribution selection as a tractable convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solver.
- Theoretical results: method is provably optimal w.r.t. the given bounds, guaranteed to outperform uniform or fixed importance sampling, and is computationally efficient.
- Paper is well-written, makes a solid, practical contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Possible missing factor of 2 in the proof of Lemma 2.1 for the optimal stepsize alpha^*; needs checking.
- Example 3.1’s intention is unclear—results appear to contradict the preceding claim that naively choosing c from bounds can be suboptimal; authors should clarify.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: initializing m to max(l_sort) may make the condition [l_sort]_l > m impossible in the first iteration; clarify the logic.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: use of c^{sort} looks like a typo and should likely be u^{sort}.
- Empirical evaluation should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and a non-uniform fixed importance sampling baseline.
- Provide timing results for the SGD experiments as well (currently only CD timing is reported).
- Typo in the title of reference [14] needs correction.
- Suggest adding citation: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv, 2016).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied and recommends acceptance.
