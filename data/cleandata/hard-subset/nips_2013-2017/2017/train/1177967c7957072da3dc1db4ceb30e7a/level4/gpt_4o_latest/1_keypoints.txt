- Paper proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method using gradient lower/upper bounds for coordinate descent and SGD, with an efficient approximation and negligible overhead.
- The method is formulated as a convex optimization problem and proved optimal within the provided bounds, outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- Paper is well-written and represents a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.
- Proof issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to be missing a factor of 2 in the expression for α*.
- Example 3.1 clarity: Equation (7) maximizes; it’s unclear whether setting c to the bounds can outperform uniform sampling, contradicting a preceding claim that this naive approach can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: Condition m = max(l^{sort}) seems unlikely to ever be satisfied—question about correctness.
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: Likely typo—should be u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Experiments: Recommend comparisons with other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Timing: Request timing results for SGD—currently absent.
- Reference errors: Reference [14] has an incorrect title.
- Missing citation: Add Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).
