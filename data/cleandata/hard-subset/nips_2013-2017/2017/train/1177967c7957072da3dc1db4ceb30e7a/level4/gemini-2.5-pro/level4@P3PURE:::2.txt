This paper presents interesting and valuable insights for Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly noteworthy contribution is Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which suggests that the proposed sampling method is superior to the commonly used L_i-based sampling strategy. I have the following comments and suggestions for the authors to consider.

1.  In Line 85, there appears to be a typo in the denominator of p*_i. It should be x_k instead of x.

2.  For notational consistency, in Line 135, the authors may want to remove the subscript k from \hat{p} and \hat{c}.

3.  All figures in their current form are too small, which renders axis ticks, labels, numbers, and other text illegible. Please enlarge the figures significantly. If space is a constraint, consider moving some figures to the appendix to allow for a clearer presentation in the main text.

4.  The comparison against L_i-based sampling in Theorem 3.2 is compelling. To further strengthen the paper's claims, it would be beneficial to provide a similar result that compares the proposed method with standard uniform sampling (p_i = 1/n).

5.  The paper would be improved by a more explicit discussion on how the new sampling method affects the total computational complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution when including the O(n) time required for updating l_k and u_k at each step?
