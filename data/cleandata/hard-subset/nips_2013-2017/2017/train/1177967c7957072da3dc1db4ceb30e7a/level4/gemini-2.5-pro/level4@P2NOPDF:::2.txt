This paper presents an interesting new sampling method and provides valuable insights for Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A key contribution is Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which compellingly suggests that the proposed sampling method is superior to the commonly used strategy of sampling based on Lipschitz constants.

I have the following comments and suggestions for the authors to consider for improving the manuscript:

1.  **Typo:** In Line 85, there appears to be a typo in the definition of p*_i. The denominator should contain x^k, not x.

2.  **Notational Consistency:** In Line 135, for notational consistency with the surrounding text, the authors may consider removing the subscript k from \hat{p} and \hat{c}.

3.  **Figure Legibility:** The figures are currently too small to be legible; axis ticks, labels, numbers, and other text are unreadable. The authors should significantly enlarge the figures in the main text to ensure clarity. To create the necessary space, some figures could potentially be moved to an appendix.

4.  **Comparison to Uniform Sampling:** The comparison in Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 against sampling based on Lipschitz constants is a strong result. It would further strengthen the paper's contribution to also include a similar theoretical comparison against the baseline of uniform sampling (i.e., p_i = 1/n).

5.  **Complexity Analysis:** The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the overall computational complexity. Please clarify how the proposed sampling method impacts the total complexity of the RCD or SGD algorithms. Specifically, what is the total complexity required for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution when accounting for the overhead of the sampling procedure (e.g., the O(n) time for updating l^k and u^k at each iteration)?
