This paper presents an interesting and valuable contribution to the area of adaptive importance sampling for optimization algorithms, particularly coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The core idea of approximating gradient-based sampling with safe bounds is well-motivated, addressing a key limitation of existing adaptive methods – their computational infeasibility. The theoretical guarantees provided, particularly Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 demonstrating improvement over L<sub>i</sub>-based sampling, are strong and practically relevant. The paper is clearly structured and the connection to prior work is well established.

The paper begins by outlining the challenges of large-scale optimization and the benefits of importance sampling. It then motivates the need for computationally feasible adaptive strategies, highlighting the drawbacks of existing gradient-based approaches. The authors propose a novel sampling scheme based on safe bounds on the gradient, demonstrating its theoretical advantages – being provably better than uniform and fixed importance sampling, and approximating gradient-based sampling in the full-information setting. This scheme is shown to be generic and easily integrated into both CD and SGD algorithms. Extensive numerical testing validates the proposed sampling strategy.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides strong theoretical guarantees for the proposed sampling scheme, demonstrating its advantages over existing methods. The proof of Theorem 3.2 and related lemmas is a significant contribution.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Addressing the computational cost of gradient-based sampling makes the proposed method applicable to a wider range of problems.
*   **Generality:** The method is shown to be applicable to both CD and SGD, increasing its potential impact.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The extensive numerical testing provides strong evidence supporting the theoretical results. The inclusion of results on different datasets and with various regularization parameters strengthens the findings.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Figure Clarity:** The figures are small and lack clear labels, tick marks, and numbers. This hinders the reader’s ability to fully interpret the results. Moving some figures to the appendix would allow for larger, more readable figures in the main body of the paper.
*   **Comparison to Uniform Sampling:** While the paper demonstrates improvement over L<sub>i</sub>-based sampling, a direct comparison to uniform sampling (p=1/n) would be valuable, mirroring the structure of Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2. This would provide a more intuitive understanding of the method's benefits.
*   **Complexity Analysis:** A more detailed discussion of the impact of the proposed sampling on the overall complexity of CD and SGD is needed. Specifically, quantifying the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating `l<sub>k</sub>` and `u<sub>k</sub>`) for achieving an ε-optimal solution would be beneficial.
*   **Minor Typo:** In Line 85, the denominator of p^*<sub>i</sub> should be ‖x<sub>k</sub>‖, not x.
*   **Notation Consistency:** In Line 135, consider removing the subscript k from  `p̂` and `ĉ` for consistency.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant problem in optimization – the computational cost of adaptive importance sampling.
*   The proposed method is theoretically sound and demonstrably outperforms existing approaches.
*   The method is general and applicable to widely used algorithms (CD and SGD).
*   The empirical results provide strong support for the theoretical findings.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents the authors’ contributions.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The small and unclear figures hinder the reader's ability to fully assess the results.
*   The lack of a direct comparison to uniform sampling leaves a gap in the analysis.
*   The absence of a detailed complexity analysis limits the practical implications of the work.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential impact. The theoretical contributions are substantial, and the empirical results are convincing. Addressing the minor weaknesses, particularly improving the figure clarity and providing a more detailed complexity analysis, would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
