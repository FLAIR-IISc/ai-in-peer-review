- Proposal: Safe adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration safe lower/upper bounds on component-wise gradient magnitudes.
- Formulation: Sampling distribution chosen via a convex min–max problem over these bounds with a closed-form, “water-filling”-like solution.
- Algorithm: Efficient O(n log n) algorithm based on sorting to compute the optimal sampling.
- Guarantees: Sampling is optimal w.r.t. available information (best worst-case), dominates Li-based fixed importance sampling, and recovers full gradient-based sampling when bounds are tight.
- Practical implementation: Shows how to maintain safe bounds efficiently for GLMs with negligible extra overhead in common regimes.
- Experiments: Substantial speed-ups observed for coordinate descent across losses/regularizers and datasets; smaller, problem-dependent gains for SGD.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on gradient-magnitude adaptive sampling and adaptive CD strategies but replaces heuristics/stale estimates with a principled min–max safe formulation and guarantees.
- Conceptual novelty: The min–max formulation and its efficient closed-form/algorithmic solution are new and elegant.
- Theoretical strengths: Best worst-case guarantee, dominance over Li-based sampling, recovery of full-information optimal sampling, and competitive-ratio bounds.
- Practical strengths: Simple integration into CD/SGD and concrete, low-cost recipes for GLMs.
- Empirical strengths: Consistent CD improvements and analysis of adaptive stepsize effects.
- Main weakness — SGD: Empirical gains for SGD are modest and guidance on when to expect benefits is limited.
- Main weakness — complexity accounting: Missing explicit end-to-end ε-complexity bounds that include O(n) bound updates and O(n log n) sampling computation.
- Presentation issues: Minor notational inconsistencies/typos, some proofs need explicit conditions, and figures are hard to read.
- Scope limitation: Safe-bounds maintenance is shown for GLMs; guidance for other models and tighter bounds is limited.
- Suggested technical fixes: Clarify iteration indices (e.g., xk), simplify notation in theorem statements, and state conditions for min–max interchange (Sion/Komiya).
- Suggested empirical/clarity fixes: Enlarge/move figures, add uniform-sampling comparison corollary, include more adaptive-sampling baselines, and add a “complexity to accuracy” subsection.
- Practical suggestions: Discuss initialization of bounds, memory/storage for bounds, and whether p̂k can be updated incrementally rather than recomputed.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, principled formulation with strong guarantees, efficient algorithm, and demonstrated practical benefits for CD.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited SGD benefits, missing explicit ε-complexity including adaptive overhead, and presentation issues.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid and practically useful for CD; would be stronger with complexity accounting and clearer presentation.
