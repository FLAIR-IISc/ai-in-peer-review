- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling.
- Frames optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm to solve it.
- Method achieves optimal performance given the bounds, outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling, and incurs negligible computational overhead.
- Novel and compelling formulation despite prior work on adaptive importance sampling.
- Paper is well-written and a valuable contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Lemma 2.1 proof: likely missing a factor of 2 in the derivation of α*.
- Example 3.1: Equation (7) is a maximization; setting c to upper/lower bound may outperform uniform sampling—example presentation is unclear and needs clarification.
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: the condition seems unlikely to be satisfied since m is defined as the maximum of l^sort.
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: typographical error—should use u^sort instead of c^sort.
- Experimental evaluation: should compare to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.
- SGD timing: paper lacks timing results for SGD; include to assess practical impact across algorithms.
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title.
- Missing reference suggestion: add Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.
