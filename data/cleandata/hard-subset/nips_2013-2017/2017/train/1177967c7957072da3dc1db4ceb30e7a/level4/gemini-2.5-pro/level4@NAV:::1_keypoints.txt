- Introduces a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient norms to approximate gradient-based sampling.
- Provides theoretical proof that the strategy is optimal given those bounds and empirically outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling.
- Method is computationally efficient, adding only negligible overhead.
- Novelty: casts selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solver.
- Paper is clearly written and is a valuable contribution.
- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to miss a factor of 2 in the definition of alpha^*.
- Example 3.1: apparent contradiction—maximization suggests setting c to bounds is preferable, which conflicts with claim that that naive approach can be suboptimal; needs clarification.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: definition m = max(l^{sort}) makes it unclear whether the condition on that line can ever be met; please clarify.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely typo — c^{sort} should perhaps be u^{sort}.
- Experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed non-uniform importance sampling baselines.
- SGD experiments are missing runtime performance results; authors should include these or explain omission.
- Reference [14] appears to have an incorrect title.
- Suggest adding Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv 2016, as a relevant reference.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer’s concerns were resolved and they now recommend acceptance.
