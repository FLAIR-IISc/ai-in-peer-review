- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds to compute per-iteration optimal sampling.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation across multiple ML tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and notation is well-defined.  
- Key theoretical contribution: guarantee that the proposed sampling outperforms any fixed importance sampling.  
- Empirical results: significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling on generalized linear models with L1/L2 regularization in iterations and runtime.  
- Minor correction: Line 85 — denominator of p^*_i should be x_k, not x.  
- Notation fix: Line 135 — remove subscript k for \hat p and \hat c for consistency.  
- Presentation issue: figures are too small, lack labels/numbers/words; move some to appendix to enlarge others.  
- Suggested addition: include a result like Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 comparing to uniform sampling (p=1/n).  
- Suggested discussion: remark on total complexity impact for RCD/SGD, including O(n) time to update l_k and u_k.  
- Overall verdict: strong, significant contribution with minor revisions needed; potential top-tier paper.
