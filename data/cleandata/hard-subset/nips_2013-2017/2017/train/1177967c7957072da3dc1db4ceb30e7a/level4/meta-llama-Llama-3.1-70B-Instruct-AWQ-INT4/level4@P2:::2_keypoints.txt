- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds and a convex program to pick the sampling distribution.  
- Argues optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically justified but often infeasible to compute, motivating the safe-bounds approach.  
- The method is provably better than L-based sampling (Theorem 3.2) and includes a competitive ratio analysis versus the optimal sampling.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and the paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Experiments show the proposed method outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and wall-clock time, with small computational overhead.  
- Main contribution: a new adaptive sampling method with theoretical guarantees and practical speedups.
