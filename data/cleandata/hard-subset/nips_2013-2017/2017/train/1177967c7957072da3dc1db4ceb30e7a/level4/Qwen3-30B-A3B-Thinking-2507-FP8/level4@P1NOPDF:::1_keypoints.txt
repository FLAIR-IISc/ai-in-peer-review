- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes.  
- Introduces an efficient approximation for gradient-based sampling that is optimal relative to those bounds and has negligible computational overhead.  
- Novel formulation casting optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.  
- Paper is well-structured and makes a valuable contribution to importance sampling research.  
- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for α*.  
- Example 3.1: apparent contradiction about setting c to gradient bounds—needs clarification.  
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: check `l_i > m` seems redundant if m = max(l^{sort}).  
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: update likely should use `u^{sort}` instead of `c^{sort}`.  
- Numerical results: should compare against other adaptive sampling schemes ([2], [5], [21]) and against non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- Timing results: SGD timing omitted and should be reported for practical adoption.  
- Reference [14]: cited paper title is incorrect.  
- Missing reference: add Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed concerns satisfactorily; additional comparisons and timing results included; recommend acceptance.
