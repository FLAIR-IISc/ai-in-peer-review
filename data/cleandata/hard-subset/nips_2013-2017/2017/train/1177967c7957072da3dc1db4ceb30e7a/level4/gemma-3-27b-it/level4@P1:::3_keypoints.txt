- Proposes safe adaptive importance sampling that approximates gradient-based sampling with safe gradient bounds, offering theoretical guarantees and practical speed-ups.
- Method is general and can be integrated into algorithms like coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
- Provides theoretical analysis showing provable improvement over uniform/fixed sampling and potential n-fold speed-up for CD.
- Presents a clear, implementable algorithm (Algorithm 2) and practical approach for obtaining safe gradient bounds for GLMs.
- Empirical evaluation on real datasets broadly validates the theoretical claims and shows performance gains.
- Strength: strong theoretical soundness with proofs of superiority over static sampling.
- Strength: high generality, applicable to both CD and SGD, increasing potential impact.
- Strength: addresses computational inefficiency of full gradient sampling with a practical efficient approximation.
- Strength: extensive empirical validation supporting theoretical results.
- Strength: well-structured and clearly presented paper.
- Weakness: figures in Section 5 are small with hard-to-read labels, reducing clarity of experimental results.
- Weakness: SGD experiments show only modest improvement over uniform sampling, needing deeper analysis of causes.
- Weakness: practicality of obtaining tight safe gradient bounds beyond GLMs is not fully explored.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses an important optimization problem with a novel, theoretically sound, and practical solution.
- Argument against acceptance: poor figure readability and limited SGD gains raise concerns about presentation and broad applicability.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with strong theory and empirical support; recommend acceptance subject to improving figure readability.
