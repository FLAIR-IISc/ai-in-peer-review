- Proposes a "safe adaptive importance sampling" method for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) using safe gradient bounds to avoid full-gradient computation.  
- Addresses the key challenge that gradient-based adaptive sampling is optimal but computationally infeasible per iteration.  
- Provides an efficient approximation that is provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Builds on and extends prior adaptive and non-uniform sampling literature, distinguishing itself by offering guaranteed improvements over previous approximations.  
- Theoretical contributions: shows adaptive sampling can yield speed-ups proportional to problem dimension and proves the proposed sampler outperforms fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2).  
- Algorithmic contribution: a safe, efficient adaptive sampling scheme applicable to both CD and SGD.  
- Computational claim: sampling distribution can be computed in O(n log n) time per iteration, often negligible versus computing a single gradient component.  
- Empirical contribution: practical implementation on structured problems with extensive experiments showing consistent improvements and convergence comparable to infeasible gradient-based sampling.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically rigorous with consistent notation.  
- Suggested improvement 1: include an explicit theorem comparing the method to uniform sampling.  
- Suggested improvement 2: provide a more detailed total complexity analysis for obtaining an ε-optimal solution, including O(n) cost for updating gradient bounds.  
- Suggested improvement 3: improve figure readability (larger labels/resolution or move some figures to appendix).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, original, significant, easily integrable into practice, and recommended for acceptance.
