- Summary: Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method using gradient norm bounds, cast as a convex optimization with an efficient solver.
- Main theoretical claim: Strategy is optimal given the bounds and provably outperforms uniform or any fixed importance sampling.
- Practical claim: Adaptive distribution can be computed with negligible overhead.
- Writing: Paper is well-written and clear.
- Novelty: Reformulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem is novel and elegant.
- Theory: Strong, rigorous proofs supporting optimality and superiority over baselines.
- Efficiency: Algorithm for computing the sampling distribution is highly efficient and practical.
- Proof issue: Potential missing factor of 2 in the final expression for α* in Lemma 2.1.
- Example clarification: Example 3.1 is confusing about whether setting c to bounds outperforms uniform sampling despite earlier saying such naive approaches can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4 clarifications: Line 4 — unclear if condition m >= u_k^{sort} can ever hold; Line 7 — likely typo using c^{sort} instead of u^{sort}.
- Experiments: Empirical evaluation should include comparisons to other adaptive importance sampling methods (refs [2],[5],[21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling baselines.
- Timing results: No timing data shown for SGD experiments to substantiate the negligible-cost claim.
- Minor fixes: Incorrect paper title in reference [14]; suggest citing Csiba & Richtarik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors addressed reviewer concerns; reviewer is satisfied and recommends accept.
