This paper presents an intriguing analysis that offers valuable insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. Notably, Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly useful, as it indicates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly employed L-based sampling strategy.

To further enhance the manuscript, I would like to bring the following points to the authors' attention:

1. A minor correction is needed in Line 85, where the denominator of p^*i should be xk instead of x.
2. For consistency, consider removing the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c in Line 135.
3. The figures are too small and lack clear ticks, labels, numbers, and text. To improve visibility, it may be beneficial to relocate some figures to the appendix, allowing for larger, more legible versions.
4. If feasible, it would be insightful to provide a comparison similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. A discussion on the impact of the new sampling method on the total complexity of RCD or SGD would be valuable. Specifically, the authors may want to analyze the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating lk and uk) required for RCD to attain an epsilon-optimal solution.
