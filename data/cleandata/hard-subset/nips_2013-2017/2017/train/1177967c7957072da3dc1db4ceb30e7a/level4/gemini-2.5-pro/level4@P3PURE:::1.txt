This paper presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. The proposed method utilizes lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The authors provide a theoretical analysis proving that their strategy is optimal with respect to these bounds and always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling. A key contribution is the novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem, for which an efficient solver is provided that adds negligible computational overhead. The paper is well-written and offers a solid contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

**Comments and Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Proof of Lemma 2.1:** In the proof, it appears there may be a missing factor of 2 in the expression for α*. Could the authors double-check this derivation?

2.  **Clarity of Example 3.1:** This example is currently confusing. It seems to suggest that setting the proxy `c` to either the upper or lower bound is superior to uniform sampling. This appears to contradict the preceding statement, which claims that such a naive approach can be suboptimal. Could the authors please clarify the intended takeaway from this example?

3.  **Algorithm 4, Line 4:** There is a potential issue in Line 4. Given that `m` is defined as `max(l^{sort})`, it seems the condition `m > u^{sort}_i` might never be satisfied, as `l_j <= u_j` for all `j` implies `max(l^{sort}) <= max(u^{sort})`. Please clarify if this is a typo or if there is a misunderstanding of the algorithm.

4.  **Algorithm 4, Line 7:** In Line 7, should the variable be `u^{sort}` instead of `c^{sort}`?

5.  **Experimental Comparisons:** The numerical results could be strengthened by including comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those proposed in references [2], [5], and [21]. Furthermore, a comparison against a strong baseline of fixed, non-uniform importance sampling would be highly informative.

6.  **SGD Timing Results:** The experiments provide timing results for coordinate descent but not for SGD. Could the authors include timing results for the SGD experiments to demonstrate the method's computational overhead in that setting as well?

**Minor Issues:**

*   The title of the paper in reference [14] appears to be incorrect.
*   The authors may consider citing the following relevant work: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv 2016.
