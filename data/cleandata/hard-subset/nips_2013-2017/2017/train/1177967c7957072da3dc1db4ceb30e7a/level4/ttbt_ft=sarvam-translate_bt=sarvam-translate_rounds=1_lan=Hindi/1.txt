The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Based on lower and upper bounds on gradient values, an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling is proposed. This method has proven to be the best strategy in terms of bounds, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed at negligible additional cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been proposed before, the authors present a novel formulation as a convex optimization problem to select the optimal sample distribution and a efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and a good contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.
