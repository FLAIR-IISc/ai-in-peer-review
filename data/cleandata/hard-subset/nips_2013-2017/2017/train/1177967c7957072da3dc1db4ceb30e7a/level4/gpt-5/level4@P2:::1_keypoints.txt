- Paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using per-iteration lower/upper bounds and a convex min–max problem to choose sampling probabilities.
- Method is provably optimal w.r.t. the bounds, always improves over uniform/fixed importance sampling, and reduces to true gradient sampling when full gradients are available.
- Computation is efficient (O(n log n) per iteration) and integrates with existing CD/SGD algorithms.
- Clear theoretical contribution: minimizes a tight upper bound on expected suboptimality decrease and guarantees at least as good as uniform/fixed sampling.
- Elegant optimization formulation with a constructive characterization and efficient algorithm.
- Broad applicability: works for CD and SGD, compatible with general GLMs; bound-updating strategies are simple and safe.
- Empirical results show meaningful speedups for CD with small overhead; method approaches ideal gradient-based sampling without full gradient cost.
- Well written and easy to follow overall.
- Lemma 2.1 likely missing a factor of 2 in the α* derivation — needs checking.
- Example 3.1 vs. Eq. (7) is unclear: clarify whether extreme bound choices can sometimes beat uniform but remain suboptimal relative to the min–max solution.
- Algorithm 4 line 4 may have an off-by-one/initialization issue with m = max(ℓsort) and the condition [ℓsort]ℓ > m; check update order.
- Algorithm 4 line 7 likely references the wrong variable (should be usort rather than csort).
- Empirical comparisons to other adaptive schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) are missing and should be included.
- Fixed importance sampling baselines should include non-uniform static distributions, not only uniform.
- No timing results for SGD are provided; include SGD timing or discuss overhead since iteration benefits seem small.
- Reference [14] title appears incorrect and should be fixed.
- Add citation: Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.
- Overall assessment: claims are well supported; minor derivation/algorithm fixes and example clarification needed.
- Clarity: generally good; add a short pseudocode summary for the SGD variant for completeness.
- Originality: novel min–max formulation and optimality guarantees improving on heuristic bound-based approaches.
- Significance: substantial practical gains for CD; smaller empirical benefits for SGD but method is principled and broadly applicable.
- Suggestions: fix Lemma 2.1 and Algorithm 4 issues, clarify Example 3.1, add recent adaptive baselines and non-uniform fixed baselines, include SGD timing, and correct/add references.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance after the addressed fixes and clarifications.
