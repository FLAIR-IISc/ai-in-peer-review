The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for adaptive importance sampling and stochastic gradient methods. Based on lower and upper bounds on gradient values, an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling is proposed. This method has proven to be the best strategy in terms of limits, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be efficiently computed at negligible additional cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been proposed before, the authors present a novel construction as a convex optimization problem to select the optimal sample distribution and an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and a good contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.
