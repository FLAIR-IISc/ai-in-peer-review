- Timely problem: tackles adaptive importance sampling for optimization, extending gradient-based sampling while fixing prior computational infeasibility.  
- Method: proposes a safe adaptive sampling scheme using provably tight gradient bounds.  
- Guarantee: theoretical superiority over fixed sampling (Theorem 3.2) and recovery of gradient-based sampling in the full-information limit.  
- Efficiency: computationally efficient with O(n log n) cost per iteration.  
- Validation: rigorous theoretical analysis (Sections 2–3) and empirical validation on generalized linear models (Section 5).  
- Empirical impact: demonstrates significant speedups in coordinate descent with minimal overhead.  
- Theoretical strength: technically sound guarantees and worst-case bounds absent from prior approximations.  
- Originality: novel optimization formulation for sampling under safe bounds and applicability to CD and SGD.  
- Significance: addresses a critical scalability bottleneck; negligible overhead makes it practical for large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: figure clarity—small fonts and low contrast in figures (esp. 2–9) hinder interpretation of convergence trends and error bars.  
- Pro: strong theory, practical implementation, significant empirical gains, and clear relevance to NIPS themes.  
- Con: presentation issues in figures that obscure key experimental findings.
