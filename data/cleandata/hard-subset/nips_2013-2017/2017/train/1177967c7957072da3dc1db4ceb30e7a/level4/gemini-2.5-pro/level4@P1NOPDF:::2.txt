This paper provides valuable insights into Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly noteworthy contribution is Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which suggests that the proposed sampling method is superior to the commonly used L_i-based sampling strategy.

I have the following comments for the authors to consider:

1.  On line 85, in the denominator of the expression for p*_i, there appears to be a typo. It should be a function of x^k, not x.

2.  On line 135, for notational consistency, the authors may wish to remove the subscript k from p̂ and ĉ.

3.  The figures are currently too small, making the axis ticks, labels, and numbers illegible. I strongly encourage the authors to enlarge them for clarity. To create additional space, it may be possible to move some of the figures to an appendix.

4.  The comparison against L_i-based sampling is compelling. To make the analysis more comprehensive, I would suggest including a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 that compares the proposed method with standard uniform sampling (p_i = 1/n).

5.  The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of how the new sampling method affects the total computational complexity of the RCD and SGD algorithms. For instance, what is the overall complexity for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution when including the O(n) time required for updating l^k and u^k in each iteration?
