This paper is interesting and provides useful insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly valuable, as it suggests that the proposed sampling method outperforms L-based sampling, a commonly used strategy. I have the following comments for the authors’ consideration.

1) Line 85: In the denominator of p_i^*, it should be x_k, not x.

2) Line 135: Consider removing the subscript k from hat p and hat c for consistency.

3) Figures: The axes ticks, labels, numbers, and text are too small to read. Please move some figures to the appendix to free space and enlarge the remaining ones.

4) If possible, provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 that compares the proposed sampling with uniform sampling (p = 1/n), not only with the L_i-based method.

5) Please discuss how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the overall complexity for RCD to reach an epsilon-optimal solution when accounting for the O(n) time needed to update l_k and u_k?
