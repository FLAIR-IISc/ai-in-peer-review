The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling technique for coordinate-based and stochastic gradient methods. Based on the lower and upper limits of the gradient values, an efficient approximate method of gradient-based sampling is proposed. The method is proven to be the best technique in terms of limits, which can always be computed efficiently at negligible additional cost, better than uniform or fixed importance sampling. Although adaptive importance sampling techniques have been proposed before, the authors present a new formula for selecting the best sample distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and a great contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.
