- Proposal: safe, adaptive importance sampling for CD and SGD using per-iteration safe lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes.  
- Key idea: solve a minimax problem over sampling distributions and compatible gradient vectors to approximate full-information gradient sampling.  
- Optimality: resulting sampling p̂ is worst-case optimal under the bounds, strictly better than fixed L-based sampling and never worse than uniform sampling.  
- Complexity: p̂ can be computed efficiently in O(n log n) via sorting and integrated without full-gradient computation.  
- Theoretical soundness: minimax formulation, solution (Theorem 3.4 / Algorithm 4), and guarantees (Theorem 3.2) are clear and compelling.  
- Empirics: strong empirical gains for CD (up to factor n in theory), smaller but positive gains for SGD; GLM experiments support practicality.  
- Practicality: provides guidance for maintaining safe gradient bounds in GLMs and is generic/easy to integrate into CD and SGD.  
- Originality: the “safe” minimax adaptive sampling and efficient solution appear novel relative to prior adaptive schemes.  
- Significance: guarantees of dominance over L-based sampling and non-inferiority to uniform sampling make the method practically useful for large-scale problems.  
- Pros summary: clear theoretical justification, novel safe adaptive p̂ with strong guarantees, generic applicability, and practical GLM guidance.  
- Cons summary: figures are too small to read, minor notation inconsistencies, lacks explicit end-to-end complexity bound to reach ε-optimality, and needs an explicit uniform-sampling comparison.  
- Specific fix 1: Line 85 denominator should use xk instead of x.  
- Specific fix 2: Line 135 — consider removing subscript k for p̂ and ĉ for notational consistency.  
- Specific fix 3: Enlarge figures and/or move some plots to appendix so labels, ticks, and legends are readable.  
- Specific fix 4: Add an explicit result comparing p̂ to uniform sampling (p = 1/n) in parallel to Theorem 3.2 Conclusion 2.  
- Specific fix 5: Provide overall complexity analysis translating per-iteration overhead (O(n) updates, O(n log n) p̂ computation) into total time to ε-suboptimality for both CD and SGD.  
- Overall recommendation: positive—interesting, well-executed work with valuable theoretical and practical contributions; address figures, minor notation, uniform comparison, and total complexity to strengthen the paper.
