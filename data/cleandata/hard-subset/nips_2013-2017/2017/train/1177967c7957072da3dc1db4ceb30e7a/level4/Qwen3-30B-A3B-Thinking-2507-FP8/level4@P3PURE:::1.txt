

The authors propose a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient values to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The method is proven optimal with respect to these bounds, strictly dominating uniform and fixed importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. Although adaptive importance sampling has been previously studied, the authors introduce a novel formulation that casts optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem, accompanied by an efficient solution algorithm. The paper is well-written and constitutes a meaningful contribution to importance sampling theory.

**Comments:**
- **Lemma 2.1**: The proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for $\alpha^*$.
- **Example 3.1**: The example claims that setting $c$ to the upper or lower bound outperforms uniform sampling (as per the maximization in equation (7)), yet the preceding statement asserts that such a naive approach can be suboptimal. This contradiction creates confusion regarding the example's intended message.
- **Algorithm 4, Line 4**: Given $m = \max(l^{\text{sort}})$, the condition $l_i \leq m$ is always satisfied, rendering this line redundant.
- **Algorithm 4, Line 7**: The update should use $u^{\text{sort}}$ instead of $c^{\text{sort}}$.
- **Numerical Results**: The evaluation would be strengthened by comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
- **Timing Results**: The absence of timing results for SGD is notable and should be addressed.
- **Reference [14]**: The title is incorrect; it should be "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (Csiba and Richtarik, 2016).
- **Reference Addition**: Include Csiba and Richtarik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv).
