- Introduces a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy using lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate the optimal sampling distribution.  
- Formulates choosing the best sampling distribution under bounds as a min-max optimization problem (Equation 7).  
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) algorithm (Algorithm 4) to compute the safe sampling distribution each iteration.  
- Proves a theoretical "safety" guarantee: the proposed sampling is provably better than uniform or any fixed importance sampling (Theorem 3.2).  
- Demonstrates practicality: overhead is negligible in many settings and timing experiments for CD show significant speedups.  
- Framework is general and easily integrable into many CD and SGD solvers; shows how to update bounds for GLMs.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents theory and connections to ideal sampling clearly.  
- Experimental baselines are limited; should compare to more adaptive schemes (e.g., duality gap-based sampling) and strong non-uniform fixed baselines (e.g., Li-based).  
- Missing wall-clock timing results for SGD; need SGD timing plots to confirm O(n log n) overhead doesn't negate gains.  
- Clarify proof of Lemma 2.1 — optimal step size αk in the appendix may be missing a factor of 2.  
- Clarify Example 3.1 — numbers seem inconsistent and the example's intent versus optimization (7) is unclear.  
- Fix ambiguities in Algorithm 4: Line 4's condition involving `m` and `sort` logic, and the undefined `c^{sort}` (likely should be `u^{sort}`).
