- Paper proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling method using gradient lower/upper bounds and an efficient convex optimization formulation to compute the optimal sampling distribution.
- Method is proven optimal w.r.t. the provided bounds, strictly improves over uniform or fixed importance sampling, and has negligible extra cost.
- Novelty: offers a new formulation selecting the optimal distribution via convex optimization plus an efficient solver, despite prior adaptive sampling work.
- Writing and presentation are clear; contribution is valuable.
- Proof issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^*.
- Example 3.1 is confusing: setting c to bounds seems to outperform uniform sampling, contradicting the earlier claim that that naive choice can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: with m = max(l^{sort}), the condition may never be satisfied — question about correctness.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely typo — should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Empirics missing comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- No timing results provided for SGD experiments.
- Reference issues: title of reference [14] is incorrect; add Csiba & Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with responses and recommends accept.
