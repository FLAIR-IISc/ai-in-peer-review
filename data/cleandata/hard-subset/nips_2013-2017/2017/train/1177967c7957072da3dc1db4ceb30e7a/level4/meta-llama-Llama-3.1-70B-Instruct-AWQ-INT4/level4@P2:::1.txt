The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. This paper is well written and a nice contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

The authors summarize the main ideas of the paper by arguing that adaptive sampling strategies are theoretically well justified, as they can lead to significant improvements over static strategies. They propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling, which is based on safe bounds on the gradient. The proposed sampling distribution is proven to be the best strategy with respect to the given bounds, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently for negligible extra cost.

The strengths of the paper include its novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and presenting an efficient algorithm to solve it. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical properties and empirical performance. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

The weaknesses of the paper include some minor errors and typos, such as the missing factor of 2 in alpha^* in the proof of Lemma 2.1. Additionally, some of the examples and statements could be clarified, such as Example 3.1, which may be confusing. The condition in Line 4 of Algorithm 4 may never be satisfied, and the variable in Line 7 of Algorithm 4 should be u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.

The paper could benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes and against fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution. The authors should also consider including timing results for SGD.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and an efficient algorithm to solve it. The proposed method is also proven to be the best strategy with respect to the given bounds, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to improve the efficiency of coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, which are widely used in machine learning and optimization. The proposed method could be applied to various applications, including generalized linear models, deep learning, and signal processing.

Overall, I think this paper is a clear accept. The authors have addressed my questions and comments, and the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of importance sampling techniques.
