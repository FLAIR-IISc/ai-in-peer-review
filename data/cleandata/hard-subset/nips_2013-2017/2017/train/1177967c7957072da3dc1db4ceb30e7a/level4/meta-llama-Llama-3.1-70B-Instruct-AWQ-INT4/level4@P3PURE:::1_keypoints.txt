- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Shows the method is optimal w.r.t. those bounds, outperforms uniform/fixed importance sampling, and is computationally efficient with negligible extra cost.
- Introduces a novel convex-optimization formulation for selecting the optimal sampling distribution and an efficient solver.
- Paper is well-written and makes a solid contribution.
- Comment 1: Possible missing factor of 2 in the proof of Lemma 2.1 for alpha^*.
- Comment 2: Example 3.1 is confusing—using c at bounds may appear better than uniform, contradicting preceding claim; needs clarification.
- Comment 3: Algorithm 4 line 4 checks if m > l^{sort}, but m is defined as max(l^{sort}) so the condition may never hold; revisit.
- Comment 4: Algorithm 4 line 7 likely should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Comment 5: Numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes ([2], [5], [21]) and non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
- Comment 6: Include timing results for SGD to assess efficiency.
- Comment 7: Reference [14] contains an incorrect paper title.
- Comment 8: Suggest adding Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv) to references.
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer finds authors' responses satisfactory and recommends clear accept.
