- Paper introduces a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for CD and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Formulates sampling as a minimax problem to maximize worst-case expected progress given gradient bounds.  
- Proposed sampling is provably optimal under the available information and at least as good as fixed importance sampling (including uniform).  
- Authors provide an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute the safe sampling distribution.  
- Derive and show how to update gradient bounds for GLMs, enabling practical implementation.  
- Experiments show substantial speedups over uniform sampling, with manageable computational overhead for CD.  
- Novelty: principled minimax formulation moves beyond heuristic adaptive sampling approaches.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees, notably the "safety" guarantee preventing slower convergence than fixed sampling.  
- Clear exposition linking theory to practical GLM implementation.  
- Practical relevance: method is generic and integrable into existing CD/SGD frameworks.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons lack other state-of-the-art adaptive sampling baselines (e.g., duality gap–based methods).  
- Weakness: no wall-clock timing results shown for SGD, making practical overhead unclear for that setting.  
- Minor issue: possible missing factor of 2 in Lemma 2.1 derivation for optimal step size αk.  
- Minor issue: Example 3.1 is confusing and may not illustrate the intended point clearly.  
- Minor issues/typos in Algorithm 4 (condition in Line 4 and probable variable typo in Line 7).  
- References: incorrect title for [14] and missing citation suggestion (Csiba & Richtarik, 2016).  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept; reviewer reaffirms acceptance after rebuttal.
