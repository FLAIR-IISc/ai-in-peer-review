- Addresses safe adaptive importance sampling (SAIS), filling a gap by focusing on safety during sampling.  
- Proposes a novel, theoretically grounded framework that integrates with existing IS algorithms.  
- Theoretical contribution: convergence guarantees showing SAIS maintains safety (bounded weights/risk) while asymptotically minimizing variance.  
- Method is generic/plug-and-play for diverse IS pipelines (e.g., SMC, adaptive MCMC) without algorithm-specific changes.  
- Empirical results across Bayesian inference and RL benchmarks report consistent variance reduction (up to ~40%) while meeting safety thresholds.  
- Ablation studies confirm necessity of each component; experiments use standard metrics and code is publicly available.  
- Critical flaw: Figures in Section 5 (esp. Figs. 3–5) are low resolution with illegible labels, error bars, and truncated legends, preventing result assessment.  
- Required figure fixes: regenerate at ≥1.5× resolution, increase label font to ≥12pt (14pt recommended), and use distinct line styles/markers for B/W clarity.  
- Minor: Better distinguish SAIS from prior constrained IS work and clarify handling of unknown/adaptive constraints.  
- Minor: Provide a formal definition of "stability" in Section 3 and relate it to safety constraints.  
- Minor: Demonstrate integration with a non-trivial existing adaptive IS algorithm rather than only a custom implementation.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision — core ideas are strong and timely, but empirical presentation must be fixed and minor clarifications addressed.
