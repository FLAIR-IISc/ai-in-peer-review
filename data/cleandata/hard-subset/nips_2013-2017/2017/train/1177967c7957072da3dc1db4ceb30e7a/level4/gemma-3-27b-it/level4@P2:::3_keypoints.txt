- Addresses adaptive importance sampling with an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling using safe gradient bounds.
- Proposed scheme is provably better than uniform sampling and can recover gradient-based sampling in ideal conditions.
- Method is general and easily integrated into existing algorithms like coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
- Theoretical guarantees include analysis of CD with adaptive sampling, showing possible speed-ups up to the dimension n.
- Provable advantages over uniform and fixed importance sampling are clearly stated and supported by analysis.
- Extensive numerical validation on real datasets demonstrates gains in iteration complexity and execution time.
- Strong connection to prior work on gradient-based and duality gap-based sampling and correct citation of relevant optimization literature.
- Well-motivated and clearly written, articulating limitations of existing adaptive strategies and positioning the contribution.
- Weakness: experimental figures (Section 5) are small and hard to interpret; need larger size and font for readability.
- Weakness: limited discussion of challenges in obtaining safe gradient bounds across different application domains.
- Weakness: reliance on knowledge of objective structure for efficient bound computation; applicability to less structured problems is unclear.
- Weakness: discussion of the competitive ratio (Lemmas 3.5 and 3.6) feels isolated and could be better integrated into the main narrative.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, practical relevance, addresses an important problem, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor figure quality and insufficient discussion of bound computation challenges.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and recommend acceptance contingent on improving figure quality and expanding discussion on bound computation.
