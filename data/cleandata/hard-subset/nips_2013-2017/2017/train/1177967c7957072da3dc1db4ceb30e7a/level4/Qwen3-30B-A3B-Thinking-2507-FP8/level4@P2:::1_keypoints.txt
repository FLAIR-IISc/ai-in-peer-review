- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD and SGD that uses safe lower/upper gradient bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling efficiently.  
- Provides three guarantees: optimal w.r.t. the given bounds, always better than uniform/fixed importance sampling, and computable with negligible extra cost.  
- Novel formulation: casts optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem using safe bounds, improving on prior stale-gradient/upper-bound approaches.  
- Demonstrated integration into CD and SGD on generalized linear models with experiments on rcv1, real-sim, and news20.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with rigorous proofs and O(n log n) per-iteration overhead comparable to evaluating a single gradient component.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written; algorithms and examples are easy to follow.  
- Originality: using safe bounds in a convex formulation with provable guarantees is new to the literature.  
- Empirical results show near-optimal performance close to infeasible gradient-based sampling and meaningful speedups over fixed strategies.  
- Technical issue: proof of Lemma 2.1 initially missed a factor of 2 in the optimal α*.  
- Algorithm clarity/bug: Algorithm 4 has issues (line 4 condition impossible given initialization; line 7 should reference "usort" not "csort").  
- Experimental comparisons are limited; need comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods and non-uniform fixed importance sampling.  
- SGD results omit timing measurements, limiting claims about computational efficiency.  
- Pros for acceptance: theoretically sound, practical, easy integration, and validated by experiments.  
- Cons against acceptance: requires minor technical/algorithm fixes, broader experimental comparisons, and inclusion of SGD timing results.  
- Authors addressed reviewer comments in rebuttal; overall assessment: strong contribution meeting NIPS standards and recommended for acceptance.
