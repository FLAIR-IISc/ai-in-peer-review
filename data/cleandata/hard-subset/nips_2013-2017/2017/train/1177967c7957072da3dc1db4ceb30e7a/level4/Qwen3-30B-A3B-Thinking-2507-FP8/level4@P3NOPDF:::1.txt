

The authors present a novel "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. By leveraging lower and upper gradient bounds, they propose an efficient approximation for gradient-based sampling, proven to be the optimal strategy within these bounds. The method strictly dominates uniform and fixed importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. Crucially, the authors formulate the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provide an efficient algorithm to solve it—addressing a key gap in prior adaptive sampling work. This represents a significant theoretical and practical contribution to importance sampling techniques.

The paper is well-written and makes a compelling case for the proposed method. However, several technical points require clarification or correction:

1. **Lemma 2.1**: The proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for $\alpha^*$. This should be verified and corrected.

2. **Example 3.1**: The example claims that setting $c$ to the gradient bounds yields better results than uniform sampling, yet the preceding text states that such a naive strategy is suboptimal. This inconsistency needs resolution. The example should explicitly clarify whether the bounds *do* yield improvement (e.g., by showing the objective value for $c = \ell$ or $c = u$ vs. uniform) or whether the suboptimality arises in specific cases.

3. **Algorithm 4**: 
   - Line 4: The condition `if m == max(l^{sort})` is redundant since $m$ is defined as $\max(l^{sort})$. This line should be removed.
   - Line 7: The update `u^{sort} = c^{sort}` should be `u^{sort} = u^{sort}` (using the correct variable name).

4. **Experiments**: The numerical results would be strengthened by comparing against additional adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions. The absence of timing results for SGD is notable, especially given the method’s efficiency claims.

5. **References**: 
   - Reference [14] lists an incorrect title; this must be fixed.
   - The authors should add Csiba and Richtarik (2016), "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv:1603.01407), to contextualize related work.

After addressing these points, the paper would be substantially strengthened. The core contribution—reducing adaptive sampling to a convex optimization problem with provable guarantees—is compelling and well-motivated. The theoretical foundation is sound, and the method’s efficiency makes it practically relevant.
