

This paper presents interesting insights into RCD and SGD algorithms, particularly through its novel sampling method. Theorem 3.2's Conclusion 2 is notably valuable, as it demonstrates that the proposed sampling strategy outperforms the widely adopted L_i-based sampling. To strengthen the work, we recommend addressing the following points:

1.  **Line 85 Correction:** The denominator in the expression for p^*_i should be x_k (not x), as x_k is the relevant variable at iteration k. Please correct this typographical error.

2.  **Consistency in Notation (Line 135):** For consistency, we recommend removing the subscript k from the estimated quantities \(\hat{p}\) and \(\hat{c}\) in Line 135. This aligns with the notation used elsewhere for similar estimates and avoids potential confusion.

3.  **Figure Legibility:** All figures lack sufficient legibility due to tiny ticks, labels, numbers, and text. To enhance readability, please move some non-essential elements (e.g., detailed axis annotations, supplementary labels) to an appendix. This will free space to present the main figures at a larger, more readable scale in the main text.

4.  **Complementary Comparison (vs. Uniform Sampling):** While Conclusion 2 effectively compares the method to L_i-based sampling, providing a similar comparison against uniform sampling (where \(p_i = 1/n\)) would offer greater context. Specifically, a result analogous to Conclusion 2 showing the advantage (or potential limitations) of the proposed method relative to uniform sampling would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution and help readers better assess its practical value.

5.  **Total Computational Complexity:** The paper focuses on the iteration complexity. To fully evaluate the practical impact of the new sampling method, please explicitly analyze the *total computational complexity* for achieving an \(\epsilon\)-optimal solution. This should include the cost of maintaining the auxiliary variables \(l_k\) and \(u_k\) (which requires \(O(n)\) time per iteration for updates), alongside the standard RCD/SGD iteration cost. Clarifying how this total complexity scales with \(n\) and \(\epsilon\) is crucial for understanding the method's real-world applicability, especially in large-scale settings where the overhead of maintaining \(l_k\) and \(u_k\) might be significant. Please provide a concrete complexity bound incorporating these factors.

Overall, the core idea is promising and the theoretical results are sound. Addressing these points—particularly the comparison to uniform sampling and the detailed complexity analysis—will significantly enhance the paper's clarity, completeness, and impact for the NIPS audience. The proposed method shows potential for practical improvement, but these clarifications are essential to fully justify its advantages.
