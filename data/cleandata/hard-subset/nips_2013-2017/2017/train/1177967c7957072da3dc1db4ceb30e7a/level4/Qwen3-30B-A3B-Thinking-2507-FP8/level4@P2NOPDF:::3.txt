

This paper addresses safe adaptive importance sampling, a timely and significant area of research in computational statistics and machine learning. The authors present a method that demonstrates notable strengths: it is broadly applicable across diverse existing algorithms, and the work is substantiated by both rigorous theoretical analysis and comprehensive empirical validation. The generality of the approach, combined with its strong theoretical foundation and practical effectiveness, represents a clear contribution to the field.  

A critical issue requiring revision concerns the figures in Section 5. The current presentation renders these figures insufficiently legible, with small dimensions and labels that obscure key details. This significantly impedes the reader’s ability to interpret the results and assess the method’s performance. To resolve this, the figures must be enlarged to ensure adequate visual clarity, and all labels, axes, and annotations should be proportionally increased in size. This adjustment is essential for the figures to fulfill their role in supporting the paper’s empirical claims.  

The paper’s technical content is otherwise well-structured and logically presented. The theoretical results are clearly derived, and the experimental design appropriately tests the method’s robustness across relevant benchmarks. The integration with existing frameworks is convincingly demonstrated, highlighting the method’s versatility. However, the current figure quality undermines the otherwise strong presentation of the experimental findings.  

Given the paper’s substantive contributions and the straightforward nature of the required revision, the work merits acceptance pending resolution of the figure issues. The proposed method advances the state of the art in safe adaptive importance sampling, offering practitioners a flexible and theoretically grounded tool. The minor but necessary correction to the figures will ensure the paper’s clarity and impact meet the high standards of NIPS 2017.
