The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, leveraging lower and upper bounds on the gradient values to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. This method is proven to be optimal with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed at negligible extra cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been previously proposed, the authors introduce a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:

1. In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears that a factor of 2 is missing in the calculation of alpha^*.

2. In Example 3.1, the goal is to maximize the expression in (7). However, the example seems to suggest that setting c to the upper or lower bound is superior to uniform sampling. This appears to contradict the preceding statement that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal. The purpose of this example is unclear.

3. In Line 4 of Algorithm 4, the condition is based on m = max(l^{sort}). It is uncertain whether this condition will ever be satisfied.

4. In Line 7 of Algorithm 4, it seems that u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}.

5. The numerical results would benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and against fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.

6. The absence of timing results for SGD is notable.

7. The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect.

8. The authors may consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016), available on arXiv.
