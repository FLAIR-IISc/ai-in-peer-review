- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to approximate gradient-based sampling.  
- Method is proven optimal under those bounds and consistently outperforms uniform and fixed-distribution importance sampling with negligible computational overhead.  
- Novel contribution: formulates optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solution algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and advances the theoretical understanding of importance sampling techniques.  
- Strength: core idea is compelling with clear theoretical justification; convex formulation provides a principled, efficient path to adaptive sampling and avoids suboptimal extreme-parameter choices.  
- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in α* and should be verified/corrected.  
- Example 3.1: contradiction about whether setting parameter c to gradient bounds is superior to uniform sampling—needs clarification if advantage holds only under specific conditions.  
- Algorithm 4: Line 4’s condition `m = max(l^{sort})` and `if m > 0` seems redundant given nonnegative lower bounds; clarify intent.  
- Algorithm 4: Line 7 likely has a typo `u^{sort} = c^{sort}` and should reference sorted upper bounds.  
- Experiments: should include comparisons to other adaptive schemes ([2],[5],[21]) and fixed-distribution non-uniform sampling, and add SGD timing results to demonstrate runtime efficiency.  
- References: Reference [14] has an incorrect title; add Csiba & Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv, 2016.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed technical issues, algorithmic fixes, and added comparisons/timing; reviewer recommends acceptance.
