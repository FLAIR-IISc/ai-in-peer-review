- Paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme to accelerate coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with solid theoretical guarantees.  
- Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 importantly shows the proposed sampling outperforms L_i-based sampling.  
- Typo: In Line 85 denominator of p*_i should be ||√L c||_1, not ||√L x||_1.  
- Consistency: remove subscript k from p̂ and ĉ in Line 135 for clarity.  
- Figures are too small and unlabeled; enlarge plots, add ticks/numbers, and move some to the appendix.  
- Add theoretical/empirical comparison to uniform sampling (p = 1/n) similar to Theorem 3.2’s Conclusion 2.  
- Discuss overall computational complexity impact, including O(n) updates for l_k and u_k and total cost to reach ε-optimality.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear theoretical analysis and supporting experiments.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs clearer explanations in parts and fixes noted above.  
- Originality: novel combination of adaptive importance sampling with safe gradient bounds, well-differentiated from prior work.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale ML optimization by providing a practical, theoretically grounded sampling scheme.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, provably better than L_i sampling, and effective on real datasets.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor figure readability, missing uniform-sampling comparison, and insufficient complexity analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the above issues.
