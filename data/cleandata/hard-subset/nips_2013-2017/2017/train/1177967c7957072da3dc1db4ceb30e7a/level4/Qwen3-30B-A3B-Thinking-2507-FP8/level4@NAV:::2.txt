

This paper presents interesting insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. The second conclusion in Theorem 3.2 is particularly valuable, as it demonstrates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L_i-based sampling strategy. The following points require attention from the authors:  
1. In Line 85, the denominator of \( p^*_i \) should read \( x_k \) instead of \( x \).  
2. In Line 135, consider removing the subscript \( k \) from \( \hat{p} \) and \( \hat{c} \) for consistency.  
3. All figures lack readable ticks, labels, numbers, and text due to insufficient size. Please relegate some elements to the appendix to accommodate larger, legible figures.  
4. If feasible, include a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 comparing the new method against uniform sampling (\( p = 1/n \)), rather than L_i-based sampling.  
5. Clarify the impact of the new sampling method on the total complexity of RCD or SGD, specifically addressing the overall complexity (including the \( O(n) \) cost for updating \( l_k \) and \( u_k \)) to achieve an \( \epsilon \)-optimal solution.
