

This paper presents an interesting contribution to the analysis of randomized coordinate descent (RCD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The insights provided are valuable, particularly Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which demonstrates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used $L_i$-based sampling strategy. The following points require attention to strengthen the presentation:

1.  **Line 85:** The denominator in the expression for $p^*_i$ should read $x_k$ (not $x$).
2.  **Line 135:** To ensure consistency, remove the subscript $k$ from $\hat{p}$ and $\hat{c}$.
3.  **Figure Readability:** All figures lack sufficiently large ticks, labels, numbers, and text, making them difficult to interpret. We recommend relocating some figure elements (e.g., detailed axis labels, legends) to the appendix to allow for larger, more readable main figures.
4.  **Comparison with Uniform Sampling:** It would be beneficial to provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, but comparing the new sampling method against uniform sampling ($p_i = 1/n$), rather than $L_i$-based sampling. This comparison would further clarify the method's relative advantages.
5.  **Complexity Analysis:** The authors should explicitly address how the new sampling method impacts the total computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, what is the total complexity (including the $O(n)$ cost for updating $l_k$ and $u_k$) required to achieve an $\epsilon$-optimal solution? This is crucial for assessing practical utility.
