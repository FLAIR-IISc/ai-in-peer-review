- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Uses bounds to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling with negligible extra cost.  
- Method is proven optimal with respect to the provided bounds and outperforms uniform/fixed importance sampling.  
- Novel formulation casts optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.  
- Paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to importance sampling research.  
- Proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^*.  
- In Example 3.1 (eq.7) it’s unclear why setting c to upper/lower bounds wouldn’t trivially outperform uniform sampling.  
- In Algorithm 4, line 4 (m = max(l^{sort})) the condition may never be satisfied.  
- In Algorithm 4, line 7 it seems u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}.  
- Recommend comparing against other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed non-uniform importance sampling.  
- Timing results for SGD are missing and should be reported.  
- Reference [14] has an incorrect paper title.  
- Suggest adding Csiba and Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv) to related work.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer is satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.
