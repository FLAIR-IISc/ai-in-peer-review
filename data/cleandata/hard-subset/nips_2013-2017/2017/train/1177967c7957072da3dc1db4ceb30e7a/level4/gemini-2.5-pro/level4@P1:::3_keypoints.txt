- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling method that adapts sampling distributions using maintainable lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to avoid full-gradient costs.  
- Formulates sampling selection as a min–max optimization to maximize expected progress under worst-case gradient realizations.  
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute the safe sampling distribution.  
- Proves the sampling scheme is guaranteed to outperform any fixed importance sampling strategy and recovers optimal gradient-based sampling with full information.  
- Demonstrates how to derive and update safe gradient bounds efficiently (O(n)) for generalized linear models (GLMs).  
- Validates practical performance with extensive experiments on CD and SGD across multiple losses, regularizers, and real datasets, showing iteration- and time-wise speedups.  
- Weakness: experimental figures are poorly rendered (too small/unreadable) and must be improved for clarity.  
- Weakness: applicability depends on efficiently computable safe gradient bounds; broader discussion/examples for other model classes are lacking.  
- Weakness: competitive-ratio analysis (Lemmas 3.5–3.6) is theoretically interesting but its practical implications and tightness are not clearly demonstrated.  
- Overall: a novel, theoretically solid, and practically validated contribution whose main issues are presentation and clearer discussion of applicability and some theoretical bounds.
