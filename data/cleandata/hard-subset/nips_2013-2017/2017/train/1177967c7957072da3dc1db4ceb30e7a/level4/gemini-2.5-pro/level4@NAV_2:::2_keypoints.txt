- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for CD/SGD using gradient lower/upper bounds to speed optimization.  
- Reframes sampling selection as a principled min-max optimization over all gradients consistent with the bounds.  
- Provides an efficient O(n log n) algorithm to compute the worst-case-optimal sampling distribution.  
- Proves a safety guarantee (Theorem 3.2(ii)) that the adaptive scheme is never worse than fixed Li-based importance sampling.  
- Shows how gradient bounds can be efficiently maintained for generalized linear models.  
- Empirical results demonstrate substantial speedups over uniform and fixed importance sampling, approaching full-information optimal sampling.  
- Figures are unreadable (too small, illegible labels/legends) and must be enlarged or moved to the appendix.  
- Lacks a full theoretical convergence/complexity analysis for CD/SGD equipped with the proposed sampling (overall cost to reach ε-optimality).  
- Needs a more general theoretical comparison to uniform sampling when Lipschitz constants are non-uniform.  
- Minor notation issues (e.g., inconsistent use of x vs xk in Example 2.3 and p̂/ĉ subscripts in Theorem 3.2) to be fixed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel paper with acceptance recommended pending fixes to presentation (figures) and completion of theoretical analysis.
