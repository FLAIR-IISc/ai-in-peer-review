- Presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD using gradient lower/upper bounds.
- Proposes an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling computable at negligible extra cost.
- Proves the method is optimal with respect to the bounds and always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.
- Novel formulation: selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem.
- Provides an efficient algorithm to solve the convex problem (Algorithm 4).
- Paper is well written, well-motivated, and a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.
- Strong theoretical guarantees alongside practical efficiency.
- Clear connection to existing work and accessible exposition of technical details.
- Empirical results show significant speedups over static sampling, especially for coordinate descent.
- Derivation in Section 3 and Algorithm 4 are particularly noteworthy.
- Careful analysis of computational complexity and practical considerations in Section 4.
- Minor: Lemma 2.1 proof appears to miss a factor of 2 in α*.
- Minor: Example 3.1 needs clarification on why setting c to bounds beats uniform sampling.
- Minor: Algorithm 4 line 4 — given m = max(√L⁻¹`), the condition may never be satisfied.
- Minor: Algorithm 4 line 7 — should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Experiments should compare to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2],[5],[21]) and non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
- Missing timing results for SGD; including them would give a fuller performance picture.
- Reference [14] has an incorrect title.
- Suggest adding reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to outweighed strengths over weaknesses.
