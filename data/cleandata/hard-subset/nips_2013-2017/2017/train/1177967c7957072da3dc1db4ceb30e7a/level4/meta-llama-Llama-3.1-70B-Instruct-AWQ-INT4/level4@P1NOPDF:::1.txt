The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. This strategy relies on lower and upper bounds on the gradient values to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The authors demonstrate that their method is the optimal strategy with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling. Moreover, this approach can be computed efficiently, incurring negligible extra cost.

Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been previously proposed, the authors introduce a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it. This paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:

1. Proof of Lemma 2.1: It appears that a factor of 2 is missing in alpha^*. Please clarify this in the proof.

2. Example 3.1: Equation (7) aims to maximize the expression. However, the subsequent example suggests that setting c to the upper or lower bound may be more effective than uniform sampling. This seems to contradict the preceding statement, which claims that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal. Please clarify the intention behind this example.

3. Algorithm 4: In line 4, given that m = max(l^{sort}), it is unclear whether this condition will ever be satisfied. Please provide additional insight into this step.

4. Algorithm 4: In line 7, it seems that u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}. Please verify this.

5. Numerical results: To further strengthen the paper, consider comparing the proposed method with other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those presented in references [2], [5], and [21]. Additionally, include comparisons with fixed importance sampling using a non-uniform distribution.

6. Timing results for SGD: The paper lacks timing results for stochastic gradient descent (SGD). Providing these results would be beneficial for a more comprehensive evaluation.

7. Reference [14]: The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect. Please correct this.

8. Additional reference: Consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016), available on arXiv.
