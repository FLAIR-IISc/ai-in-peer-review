- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD using minimax selection over safe gradient magnitude bounds.
- Motivated by the infeasibility of computing full gradients for optimal adaptive sampling and the poor performance of some approximations versus uniform sampling.
- Formulates sampling distribution selection as a minimax problem that minimizes worst-case objective upper bound over gradients consistent with given bounds.
- Shows the resulting distribution is provably better than Li-based (fixed) importance sampling and is optimal given the bounds.
- Provides an O(n log n) algorithm to compute the sampling distribution and O(n) updates for gradient bounds in GLMs.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over fixed sampling schemes and often approaches full-information optimal sampling in experiments.
- Strength: novel, principled minimax formulation to handle gradient uncertainty.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantee (safety net) that prevents worse performance than Li-based sampling.
- Strength: computational feasibility with efficient algorithms and manageable per-iteration overhead.
- Strength: thorough experiments across datasets, losses, and regularizers showing significant improvements.
- Weakness: lacks a full convergence rate/total complexity analysis relating bound tightness to ε-optimality.
- Weakness: experimental figures in the submission are low-resolution and illegible, hindering empirical assessment.
- Weakness: comparison to uniform sampling is indirect; a direct/general formal comparison or corollary would clarify guarantees.
- Minor: possible missing 1/2 factor in Equation (3) derivation.
- Minor: Line 85 likely has ∇f(xk) instead of ∇f(x) in the denominator of [p*k]_i.
- Minor: consider removing subscript k from (p̂, ĉ) in Theorem 3.2 for notational consistency.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses an important practical problem with a novel formulation, strong safety guarantee, efficient algorithm, and comprehensive experiments.
- Rejection/major-revision arguments: missing convergence analysis and illegible figures are significant issues to resolve.
