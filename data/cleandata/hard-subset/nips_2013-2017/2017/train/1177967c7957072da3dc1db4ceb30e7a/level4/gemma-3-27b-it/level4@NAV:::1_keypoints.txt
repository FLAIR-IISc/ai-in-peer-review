- Introduces a practical adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and SGD using gradient magnitude bounds to approximate gradient-based sampling, shown to be optimal within those bounds and low overhead.
- Uniquely formulates optimal distribution selection as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solution algorithm.
- Paper is well-presented and a valuable contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Lemma 2.1’s proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the definition of α*.
- Example 3.1 is unclear: given objective (7), choosing c as upper or lower bound seems preferable to uniform sampling, so the example’s intent needs clearer articulation.
- Algorithm 4, Line 4: the condition is unclear because m is defined as the maximum of l^sort, so the condition may never be met.
- Algorithm 4, Line 7: likely should reference u^sort instead of c^sort.
- Numerical evaluation should include comparisons to existing adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.
- Include timing results for the SGD experiments to better assess performance.
- Paper lists an incorrect title for reference [14] and should add the relevant work Csiba & Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016, arXiv).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer now recommends the paper for acceptance.
