The authors propose a “safe” adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Using lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes, they construct an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. The resulting sampling rule is provably optimal under these bounds, guaranteed to outperform uniform or any fixed importance sampling, and can be computed with negligible additional overhead. While adaptive importance sampling has been studied before, this work formulates the choice of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm to solve it. The paper is well written and makes a solid contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.
