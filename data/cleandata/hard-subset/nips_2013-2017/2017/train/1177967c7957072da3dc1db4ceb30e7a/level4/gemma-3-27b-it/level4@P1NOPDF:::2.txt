This paper presents interesting insights into Reservoir Computing Dynamics (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly useful result is Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, which suggests that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have the following comments for the authors to consider:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*<sub>i</sub> should be x<sub>k</sub>, not x.

2. In Line 135, removing the subscript *k* from  p̂ and ĉ would improve consistency.

3. All figures currently lack ticks, labels, and numbers, and are generally too small to easily interpret. To improve readability and conserve space, please consider moving some figures to the appendix and presenting the remaining ones at a larger size.

4. It would be valuable to see a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but comparing the proposed method to uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L<sub>i</sub>-based method. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the technique’s benefits.

5. Please discuss the impact of this new sampling method on the overall complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total computational complexity – including the O(n) time required to update l<sub>k</sub> and u<sub>k</sub> – for RCD to achieve an ε-optimal solution?
