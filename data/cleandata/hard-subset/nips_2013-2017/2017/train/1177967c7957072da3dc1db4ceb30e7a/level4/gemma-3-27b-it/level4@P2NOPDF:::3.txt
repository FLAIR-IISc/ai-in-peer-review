This paper addresses the important and currently active area of safe adaptive importance sampling. A key strength of this work lies in its generality; the proposed method is designed to be readily integrated with a variety of existing algorithms. This flexibility is further supported by a combination of rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling practical experiments demonstrating the efficacy of the approach.

However, a significant impediment to fully understanding the experimental results is the poor visual clarity of the figures presented in Section 5. The figures are currently too small, and the accompanying labels are insufficiently sized, making it difficult for the reader to discern key details and interpret the findings. To improve readability and impact, I strongly recommend substantially increasing the size of both the figures and their labels. This adjustment will allow for a clearer presentation of the experimental data and facilitate a more comprehensive understanding of the reported results.
