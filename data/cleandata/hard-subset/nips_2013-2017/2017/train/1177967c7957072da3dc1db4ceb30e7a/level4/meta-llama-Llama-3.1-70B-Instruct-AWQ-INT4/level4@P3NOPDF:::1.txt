This paper presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. The authors propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling using lower and upper bounds on the gradient values. The method is proven to be the optimal strategy with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently with negligible extra cost.

Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been previously proposed, the authors introduce a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it. This contribution is a valuable addition to the study of importance sampling techniques.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a commendable effort to clearly explain their methodology and provide supporting theoretical results. However, I have a few comments to further improve the paper.

Firstly, I noticed a minor error in the proof of Lemma 2.1, where a factor of 2 appears to be missing in the expression for α*. The authors should verify and correct this.

Regarding Example 3.1, the statement preceding equation (7) suggests that setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal. However, the example seems to indicate that this approach is actually better than uniform sampling. The authors should clarify this point to avoid confusion.

I also have some comments on Algorithm 4. In line 4, the condition m = max(l^{sort}) may never be satisfied. The authors should verify this and provide a correction if necessary. Additionally, in line 7, it appears that u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}.

In terms of numerical results, I believe that comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those proposed in references [2], [5], and [21], would be beneficial. Furthermore, a comparison to fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

I also noticed that there are no timing results for SGD. The authors should consider including these results to provide a more complete picture of their method's efficiency.

Finally, I pointed out a minor error in the reference list, where the title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect. The authors should correct this and also consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016) on arXiv.

After reading the author rebuttal, I am pleased to see that my questions and comments have been addressed. I believe that this paper is a clear accept and makes a valuable contribution to the field.
