This paper is compelling and offers useful insights for RCD and SGD methods. Item (ii) of Theorem 3.2 is especially valuable, as it shows the proposed sampling strictly improves over L-based importance sampling, a widely used baseline. I have the following comments for the authors to consider.

1. Line 85: in the denominator of p_i^*, it should be x_k rather than x.
2. Line 135: consider removing the subscript k on \hat p and \hat c for consistency.
3. Figures: axis ticks, labels, numbers, and text are too small to read. Please move some figures to the appendix to free space and enlarge the remaining ones.
4. If possible, include a counterpart to Theorem 3.2(ii) that compares against uniform sampling (p = 1/n) instead of L_i-based sampling.
5. Please discuss how the new sampling affects the overall complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity for RCD to reach an epsilon-optimal solution, including the O(n) time per iteration to update l_k and u_k?
