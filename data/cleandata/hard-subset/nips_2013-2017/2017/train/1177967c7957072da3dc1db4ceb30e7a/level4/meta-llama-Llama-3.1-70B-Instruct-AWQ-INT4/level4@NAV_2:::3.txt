This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified, but often computationally infeasible. To address this issue, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient and formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed method. The theoretical results, including the proof of the proposed sampling scheme's optimality, are sound and well-presented. The experimental evaluation is also thorough, covering various machine learning tasks and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to balance theoretical rigor with practical considerations. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their proposed method and demonstrate its efficiency through experiments. The paper also highlights the importance of adaptive stepsizes in achieving good performance.

The writing is clear, and the authors provide sufficient background information for readers unfamiliar with the topic. The notation is consistent, and the figures are well-designed, although they could be larger and with more prominent labels to improve readability.

In terms of originality, the paper builds upon existing work on adaptive importance sampling, but the authors' contribution is significant, as they provide a novel and efficient way to compute the optimal sampling distribution using safe bounds on the gradient.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to improve the efficiency of CD and SGD algorithms, which are widely used in machine learning. The proposed method is generic and can be integrated with many existing algorithms, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, this paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough and convincing presentation of their proposed method. With some minor adjustments to the figures, the paper is ready for publication.
