The paper introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes, it constructs an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. Within these bounds, the proposed distribution is proven optimal, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, while incurring negligible additional computational cost. Beyond prior adaptive schemes, the authors recast the choice of sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provide an efficient algorithm to solve it. The paper is clearly written and represents a solid contribution to importance sampling techniques.

Comments and questions:
- Lemma 2.1: The proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in α*.
- Example 3.1: In equation (7) the objective is maximization. In Example 3.1, it seems that setting c to either the upper or lower bound would outperform uniform sampling. Is this the intended message? This seems at odds with the immediately preceding statement that the naive approach of setting c to an endpoint can be suboptimal. Please clarify the intended takeaway.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: Since m = max(l^{sort}), can this condition ever be satisfied?
- Algorithm 4, line 7: Should this reference u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}?
- Experiments: The empirical section would be stronger with comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed importance sampling using a non-uniform distribution.
- Why are there no timing results for SGD?
- References: The title of reference [14] is incorrect. Please also add: Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.
