- Introduces a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper bounds on gradient magnitudes.
- Constructs an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling that is optimal within the provided bounds and outperforms uniform/fixed sampling with negligible extra cost.
- Recasts sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient solver, extending beyond prior adaptive schemes.
- Paper is clearly written and represents a solid contribution to importance sampling techniques.
- Lemma 2.1: proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in α*.
- Example 3.1: ambiguity—equation (7) is a maximization, and setting c to an endpoint seems to outperform uniform sampling, which conflicts with the claim that endpoint choices can be suboptimal; clarify intended takeaway.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: with m = max(l^{sort}), the stated condition may never be satisfied—question its validity.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely should reference u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Experiments: empirical section should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Experiments: missing timing results for SGD.
- References: title of reference [14] is incorrect; add Csiba and Richtárik, “Importance Sampling for Minibatches,” arXiv, 2016.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer read the authors’ responses and recommends acceptance.
