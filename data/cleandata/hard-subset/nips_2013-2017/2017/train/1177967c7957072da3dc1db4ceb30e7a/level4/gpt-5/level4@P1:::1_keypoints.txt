- Summary: Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for CD/SGD using per-iteration safe gradient bounds and a convex min–max to compute an optimal sampling distribution; O(n log n) per iteration, recovers gradient sampling with full information, shows strong CD speedups and modest SGD gains.

- Relation to prior work: Connects to fixed non-uniform and adaptive sampling literature but distinguishes itself by a principled min–max formulation with dominance guarantees under limited information.

- Strength: Principled min–max formulation that is optimal for given bounds and strictly dominates Li-based fixed sampling.

- Strength: Strong theoretical results—full-information recovery, worst-case optimality, and competitive ratio bounds.

- Strength: Efficient Algorithm 4 with O(n log n) overhead and practical bound-maintenance for GLMs; overhead often negligible relative to gradient work.

- Strength: Solid empirical CD results with diagnostics (vk trajectories) supporting theory; SGD shows smaller but present gains.

- Weakness: Possible typo in Lemma 2.1—α* may be missing a factor of 2.

- Weakness/clarity: Example 3.1’s message is unclear about whether fixing c to bounds can be suboptimal versus beneficial—needs clarification.

- Weakness/bug: Algorithm 4 indexing/logic issues—questions about line 4 (m = max(ℓsort) condition) and line 7 (csort vs usort).

- Weakness: Empirics lack comparisons to several adaptive baselines ([2], [5], [21]) and to additional non-uniform data-driven fixed baselines beyond Li-based.

- Weakness: No SGD wall-clock timing reported despite claiming negligible extra cost; such timings are needed because SGD per-iteration cost can be much lower.

- Weakness: Reference issues—title of [14] appears incorrect; missing citation suggestion Csiba & Richtárik (2016) on importance sampling for minibatches.

- Weakness: Practicality concern—O(n log n) per-iteration cost may be non-negligible when d ≪ n or very high sampling frequency; discuss amortization, partial updates, or subsampling.

- Quality: Technical development is sound with clear reductions and dominance guarantees; competitive ratio analysis is helpful but relies on a relative accuracy condition that should be discussed.

- Clarity: Overall well-written and organized; algorithms and GLM bound-tracking are clear, but several notational/typo issues should be fixed.

- Originality: The safe adaptive sampling via min–max optimization is novel and distinct from prior gradient-based or heuristic adaptive schemes.

- Significance: General, theoretically justified, and practically relevant approach with substantial CD improvements and meaningful SGD benefits; likely to be adopted and extended.

- Pros: Principled, provably superior under limited info; efficient and practical for GLMs; strong CD performance and useful diagnostics.

- Cons: Minor correctness/clarity bugs, missing adaptive baseline comparisons and SGD timing, and need for clearer discussion of overhead regimes.

- Minor recommendations: Fix reference [14] title, add Csiba & Richtárik (2016), include more non-uniform fixed baselines, report SGD wall-clock times, and discuss overhead mitigation strategies.
