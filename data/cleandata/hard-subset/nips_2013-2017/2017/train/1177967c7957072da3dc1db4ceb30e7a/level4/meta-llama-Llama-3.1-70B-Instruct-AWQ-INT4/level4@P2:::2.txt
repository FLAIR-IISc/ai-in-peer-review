This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for Coordinate Descent (CD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well justified, but its computation is often infeasible. To address this issue, they rely on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient and formulate the problem of picking the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed method. The theoretical analysis is sound, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

One of the main contributions of the paper is the development of a new sampling method that is provably better than the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. Specifically, Theorem 3.2 shows that the new method is always better than L-based sampling, and the authors provide a competitive ratio analysis to compare the new method with the optimal sampling solution.

The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms fixed importance sampling in terms of iterations and time. The authors also demonstrate that the introduced computational overhead is small enough to outperform fixed importance sampling in terms of total computation time.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be xk instead of x.
2. In Line 135, the subscript k for \hat p and \hat c should be removed for consistency.
3. The figures are too small and lack labels, numbers, and words. It would be better to move some of them to the appendix to gain space and show larger figures.
4. It would be interesting to provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 but comparing with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of L_i based method.
5. The authors should remark on how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution?

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is a significant contribution to the field of optimization. With some minor revisions, the paper is ready for publication.
