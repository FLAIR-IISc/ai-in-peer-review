

The paper presents interesting insights regarding RCD and SGD algorithms. Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2 is particularly valuable, as it demonstrates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used $L_i$-based sampling. The following comments should be addressed:  
1. Line 85: The denominator of $p^*_i$ should be $x_k$, not $x$.  
2. Line 135: Remove the subscript $k$ from $\hat{p}$ and $\hat{c}$ for consistency.  
3. All figures lack legible ticks, labels, text, and numerical annotations due to excessive smallness. Relegate some figures to the appendix to accommodate larger, readable versions in the main text.  
4. Provide an analogous result to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, comparing the proposed method against uniform sampling ($p = 1/n$) rather than $L_i$-based sampling.  
5. Clarify the impact of the new sampling method on the total computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, detail the total complexity (including the $O(n)$ cost for updating $l_k$ and $u_k$) required to achieve an $\epsilon$-optimal solution.
