The authors introduce a "safe" adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Using lower and upper bounds on gradient values, they propose an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. The approach is proven optimal with respect to these bounds, strictly outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and it can be computed with negligible additional cost. While adaptive importance sampling has been studied before, the paper offers a new formulation that casts the choice of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm to solve it.
This paper is clearly written and a solid contribution to the study of importance sampling methods.
