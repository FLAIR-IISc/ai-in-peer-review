- Introduces a "safe adaptive importance sampling" scheme for coordinate descent and SGD that avoids expensive full-gradient computations.  
- Uses safe bounds on gradient information to compute an efficient sampling distribution provably better than uniform or fixed importance sampling.  
- Builds on prior adaptive importance sampling and coordinate descent literature, positioning contribution clearly.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides worst-case guarantees showing the safe sampler outperforms static sampling (Theorem 3.2).  
- Algorithm is computationally practical, computable in O(n log n) time per iteration.  
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across real-world datasets and tasks (L1/L2 regularization, various losses).  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and gives sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Main weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of total computational complexity toward an ε-optimal solution.  
- Unclear how the O(n log n) overhead compares to typical gradient-evaluation cost O(d) in practice.  
- Comparison in Theorem 3.2 is limited to L-based sampling; direct comparison with uniform sampling (p=1/n) is missing.  
- Some figures lack clear labels, ticks, and legends, reducing interpretability—suggest moving elements to an appendix.  
- Meets NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) and recommended for acceptance.
