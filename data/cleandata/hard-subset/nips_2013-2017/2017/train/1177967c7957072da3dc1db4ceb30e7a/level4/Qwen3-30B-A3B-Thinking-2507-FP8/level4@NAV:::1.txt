

The authors introduce a "safe" adaptive importance sampling approach for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes, they propose an efficient gradient-based sampling approximation. The method is proven optimal under these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. Although adaptive importance sampling has been studied before, the authors offer a novel perspective by framing the optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and providing an efficient solution algorithm.  
This paper is well-written and represents a valuable contribution to importance sampling research.  

Comments:  
- Lemma 2.1: A factor of 2 appears to be missing in the expression for α*.  
- Example 3.1: Equation (7) involves maximization, suggesting that setting c to the upper or lower bound may dominate uniform sampling. However, the preceding statement claims that this naive c-bound approach can be suboptimal—this contradiction requires clarification.  
- Line 4 of Algorithm 4: Given m = max(l^{sort}), will the condition ever hold?  
- Line 7 of Algorithm 4: Should read u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}?  
- The numerical results would benefit from comparisons to additional adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions.  
- Why are timing results absent for SGD?  
- Reference [14] contains an incorrect paper title.  
- Add reference: Csiba and Richtarik. "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).
