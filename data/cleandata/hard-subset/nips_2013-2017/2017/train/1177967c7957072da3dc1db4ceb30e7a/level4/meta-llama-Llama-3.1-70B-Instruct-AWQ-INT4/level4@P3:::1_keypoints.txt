- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and SGD based on safe bounds on gradients.
- Develops an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal given the bounds.
- Proposed sampling always outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling and can be computed with negligible extra cost.
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting claims.
- Provides a clear optimization formulation for selecting the optimal sampling distribution and an efficient algorithm to solve it.
- Method is generic and can be applied to both CD and SGD and integrated into existing algorithms for structured problems.
- Experimental evaluation shows the adaptive scheme outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and total time, with small computational overhead.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Original contribution: novel convex formulation of optimal sampling and a new perspective on adaptive importance sampling.
- Significance is high due to applicability across a wide range of machine learning problems.
- Minor issue: proof of Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in alpha^*.
- Minor issue: Example 3.1 is confusing and its intent is unclear.
- Lacks comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes and to fixed importance sampling with non-uniform distributions.
- No timing results reported for SGD.
- Overall recommendation: clear accept despite minor issues.
