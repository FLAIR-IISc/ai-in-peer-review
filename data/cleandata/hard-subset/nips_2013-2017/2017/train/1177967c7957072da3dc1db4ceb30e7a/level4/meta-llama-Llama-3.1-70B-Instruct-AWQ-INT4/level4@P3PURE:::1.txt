The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. This strategy is based on lower and upper bounds on the gradient values, which enables an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling. The proposed method is proven to be the optimal strategy with respect to these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently with negligible extra cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been previously proposed, the authors introduce a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and makes a nice contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:

1. In the proof of Lemma 2.1, it appears that a factor of 2 is missing in the calculation of alpha^*.

2. Example 3.1 is somewhat confusing. In equation (7), the goal is to maximize the expression. However, in the example, it seems that setting c to the upper or lower bound would be a better choice than using uniform sampling. This contradicts the statement preceding the example, which claims that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal. It would be helpful to clarify the intention behind this example.

3. In Algorithm 4, line 4 checks if m > l^{sort}. However, since m is defined as max(l^{sort}), this condition may never be satisfied. It would be beneficial to revisit this condition.

4. In Algorithm 4, line 7, it seems that u^{sort} should be used instead of c^{sort}.

5. The numerical results would benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those presented in [2], [5], and [21], as well as fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

6. Timing results for SGD are not provided. Including these results would be helpful in evaluating the efficiency of the proposed method.

7. The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect.

8. The authors may want to consider adding a reference to Csiba and Richtarik's "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (2016), available on arXiv.
