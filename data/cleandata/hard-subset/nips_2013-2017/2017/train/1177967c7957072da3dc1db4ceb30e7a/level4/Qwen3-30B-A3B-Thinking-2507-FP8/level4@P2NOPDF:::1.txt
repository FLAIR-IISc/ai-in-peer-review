

The authors propose a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods, leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient magnitudes to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling. The method is proven to be optimal under these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform and fixed-distribution importance sampling while incurring negligible computational overhead. This work distinguishes itself by formulating the optimal sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem and providing an efficient solution algorithm—novel contributions within the literature on adaptive importance sampling. The paper is well-written and represents a meaningful advancement in the theoretical understanding of importance sampling techniques.

**Strengths:**  
The core idea is compelling, with clear theoretical justification for the proposed approach. The convex optimization formulation provides a principled and efficient path to adaptive sampling, addressing a key limitation in prior work. The authors effectively demonstrate that their method avoids suboptimal choices (e.g., setting sampling parameters to extreme bounds) that plague simpler heuristics.

**Specific Comments:**  
1. **Lemma 2.1:** The proof appears to omit a factor of 2 in the expression for α*. This should be verified and corrected.  
2. **Example 3.1:** The example claims that setting parameter *c* to the gradient bound (upper or lower) is superior to uniform sampling. However, the preceding statement asserts that such naive bound-based choices *can be suboptimal*. This contradiction requires clarification—does the example intend to show that *under specific conditions* bound-based *c* is beneficial, or is there an inconsistency in the argument?  
3. **Algorithm 4:**  
   - Line 4: The condition `m = max(l^{sort})` appears redundant since *m* is defined as this maximum. The condition `if m > 0` will always hold if *m* is derived from the sorted lower bounds (which are non-negative by context). Clarify the purpose of this check.  
   - Line 7: The update `u^{sort} = c^{sort}` likely contains a typo; it should reference the sorted upper bounds (`u^{sort}`), not *c*.  
4. **Experiments:** The numerical results would be strengthened by comparing against other adaptive schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and fixed-distribution sampling with non-uniform weights. Including timing results for SGD is also recommended, as runtime efficiency is critical for practical adoption.  
5. **References:**  
   - Reference [14] contains an incorrect title.  
   - Please add: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv, 2016.
