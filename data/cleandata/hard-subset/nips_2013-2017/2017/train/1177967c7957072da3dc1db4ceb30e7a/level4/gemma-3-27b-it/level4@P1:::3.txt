This paper focuses on safe adaptive importance sampling, a technique gaining popularity for accelerating optimization algorithms, particularly in large-scale applications. The core idea is to efficiently approximate gradient-based sampling using safe bounds on the gradient, offering theoretical guarantees and practical speed-ups. A key strength of the work is its generality – the proposed scheme can be readily integrated into existing algorithms like coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The authors support their method with both theoretical analysis and extensive numerical experiments.

The paper begins by motivating the need for adaptive importance sampling, highlighting the limitations of uniform and fixed importance sampling, as well as the computational challenges associated with full gradient-based adaptive methods. They then propose a novel sampling strategy that is provably better than uniform or fixed sampling and can be computed efficiently. The theoretical results demonstrate a potential speed-up factor of the dimension *n* for CD methods using this approach.  Importantly, the authors provide a clear algorithm (Algorithm 2) and demonstrate how to apply it in practice.  They also address the practical challenge of obtaining safe gradient bounds for GLMs (Generalized Linear Models), offering a feasible solution.  The empirical evaluation section (Section 5) demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed scheme on several real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides strong theoretical guarantees for the proposed sampling scheme, proving its superiority over static sampling methods.
*   **Generality:** The method is presented as a generic improvement applicable to both CD and SGD, increasing its potential impact.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors address the computational infeasibility of full gradient-based sampling by proposing a practical and efficient approximation.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  Extensive numerical experiments validate the theoretical results and demonstrate performance gains on real datasets.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-structured and presents the key ideas clearly.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Figure Readability:** The figures in Section 5 are small and the labels are difficult to read, hindering the reader's ability to fully assess the experimental results. Increasing the figure size and font sizes would significantly improve clarity.
*   **Limited SGD Performance Gain:** The experimental results for SGD show only a modest improvement compared to uniform sampling. The authors acknowledge this, but a more in-depth analysis of why the gains are less pronounced in SGD would be valuable.
*   **Assumptions on Gradient Bounds:** While the paper discusses obtaining safe gradient bounds, the practicality of this approach could be further explored for more complex models beyond GLMs.  A deeper discussion of scenarios where obtaining tight bounds is challenging would strengthen the work.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem in optimization, offering a novel and theoretically sound solution.
*   The proposed method is demonstrably better than existing approaches in many scenarios.
*   The work is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the key ideas.
*   The empirical validation is comprehensive and supports the theoretical claims.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The small figure size in Section 5 detracts from the overall presentation and makes it difficult to analyze the results.
*   The limited performance improvement in the SGD experiments raises questions about the applicability of the method to all optimization scenarios.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of optimization. The proposed safe adaptive importance sampling scheme is a valuable addition to the toolbox of techniques for accelerating large-scale machine learning algorithms. The theoretical guarantees, practical efficiency, and empirical validation make this work compelling. While the figure readability and SGD performance gain are minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the paper. I recommend **acceptance** with the request that the authors revise the figures in Section 5 to improve readability.
