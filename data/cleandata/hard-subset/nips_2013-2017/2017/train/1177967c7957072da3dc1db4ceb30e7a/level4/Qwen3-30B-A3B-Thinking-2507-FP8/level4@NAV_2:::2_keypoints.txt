- Introduces a novel safe adaptive importance sampling method for CD and SGD addressing feasibility and theoretical gaps in prior adaptive sampling.
- Efficiently computes sampling distributions via safe gradient bounds with O(n log n) per iteration.
- Theoretically provable improvement over uniform and L-based sampling, with Theorem 3.2 well-supported.
- Shows potential speed-ups up to n-fold compared to fixed (L-based) sampling.
- Proposes a generic adaptive sampling strategy applicable to both CD and SGD.
- Demonstrates practical integration into structured optimization problems.
- Presents practical gradient-bound tracking for generalized linear models (Section 4).
- Provides comprehensive empirical validation across losses, regularizers, and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible with appropriate notation.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees with clear proofs.
- Strength: practical implementation with minimal computational overhead.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical validation across multiple settings.
- Strength: clear exposition of the relationship between adaptive sampling and convergence rates.
- Strength: demonstrated superiority over common baselines (L-based and uniform).
- Weakness: typo in Line 85—denominator of p^*i should be xk rather than x.
- Weakness: inconsistent notation for \hat p and \hat c in Line 135; removing subscript k would improve clarity.
- Weakness: figures lack readable ticks/labels/numbers; some should be moved to the appendix for larger, legible figures.
- Weakness: Theorem 3.2 comparison should be extended to include uniform sampling (p = 1/n) for completeness.
- Weakness: missing analysis of total complexity impact (including O(n) gradient-bound updates) and iterations to ε-optimality.
- Overall: high-quality, original, and significant work with minor, easily addressable issues; strong grounds for acceptance.
