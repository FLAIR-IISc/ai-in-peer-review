- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling strategy using only lower/upper bounds on gradient coordinates; optimal sampling solved via a convex program and claimed to dominate uniform/fixed baselines with negligible overhead.

- Novel convex optimization formulation with clear theoretical guarantees.

- Safe design: performance improves over uniform/fixed sampling whenever bounds contain useful information.

- Emphasizes algorithmic efficiency and practical implementability.

- Clear presentation with well-structured proofs and motivation.

- Empirical evaluation needs stronger comparisons to existing adaptive schemes and more powerful fixed baselines.

- Multiple clarity and correctness issues in examples, proofs, and pseudocode require fixes.

- The “negligible extra cost” claim needs more detailed timing evidence, particularly for SGD.

- Lemma 2.1: α* expression appears to miss a factor of 2—check derivation and downstream constants.

- Example 3.1 / Eq. (7): intent unclear—clarify whether naive choices (c = l or c = u) can sometimes beat uniform yet remain suboptimal versus the proposed method; add complementary examples.

- Algorithm 4 errors: Line 4’s condition may be unreachable—verify m or inequality; Line 7 likely should reference u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.

- Experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., refs [2],[5],[21]) and to fixed non-uniform baselines derived from bounds (proportional to l, u, or smoothed).

- Report wall-clock time and per-iteration overhead for SGD, with a breakdown of sampling computation/updating costs.

- Clarify assumptions and scope: state when bounds are static vs. updated, how often sampling is recomputed, and analyze robustness/sensitivity to loose or violated bounds.

- Minor fixes: correct reference [14] title, add Csiba & Richtárik (2016), ensure notation consistency and define variables on first use, and optionally provide a per-iteration complexity summary.

- Overall: well-executed, meaningful contribution; acceptance recommended after improved experiments, corrections, and timing data.

- Post-rebuttal: authors’ responses addressed concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance.
