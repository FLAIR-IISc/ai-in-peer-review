I think this article is quite interesting and provides some good insights for RCD and SGD algorithms. Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 is very useful because it suggests that the new sampling method is better than L-based sampling, which is a widely used technique. I have some comments that I request the authors to consider.

1. In line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be written as xk, not x.
2. In line 135, you can remove the subscript k for \hat p and \hat c if you want, just to maintain consistency.
3. I don't see any ticks, labels, numbers, or words because they are all too small. Please move some of the details to the appendix to make room so you can show the larger figures.
4. If possible, provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, but comparing it with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L-based method.
5. Please comment on how this new sampling method will affect the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what would be the total complexity of RCD (with O(n) time for updating lk and uk) to find an epsilon-optimal solution?
