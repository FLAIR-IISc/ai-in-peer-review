This paper is interesting and provides useful insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. In particular, Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 is valuable, as it suggests the proposed sampling method outperforms L_i-based sampling, a commonly used strategy. I have the following comments for the authors to consider:

1. Line 85: In the denominator of p_i^*, it should be x_k, not x.

2. Line 135: Consider removing the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c for consistency with the surrounding notation.

3. Figures: I cannot read any ticks, labels, numbers, or text in the figures; they are too small. Please move some material to the appendix to free space so that the figures can be shown at a readable size.

4. If possible, provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 that compares the proposed sampling with uniform sampling (p = 1/n), not only with the L_i-based method.

5. Please comment on how the new sampling method affects the overall complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity to obtain an epsilon-optimal solution when accounting for the O(n) per-iteration time needed to update l_k and u_k?
