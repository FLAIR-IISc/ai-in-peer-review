- Proposes a "safe" adaptive importance sampling using lower/upper gradient bounds, provably optimal w.r.t. those bounds and efficiently computable with negligible overhead, outperforming uniform/fixed sampling.
- Novel formulation casting optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem and an efficient algorithm to solve it.
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology and solid supporting theoretical results.
- Minor proof error: missing factor of 2 in α* expression in Lemma 2.1; needs verification/correction.
- Clarify Example 3.1: surrounding text claims setting c to bounds can be suboptimal, but the example seems to show it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Algorithm 4 issues: line 4 condition m = max(l^{sort}) may never be satisfied—verify/correct; line 7 likely should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Experiments should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Add timing results for SGD to fully demonstrate method efficiency.
- Reference issues: incorrect title in ref [14]; suggest adding Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv).
- After the rebuttal, authors addressed concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance and views the paper as a valuable contribution.
