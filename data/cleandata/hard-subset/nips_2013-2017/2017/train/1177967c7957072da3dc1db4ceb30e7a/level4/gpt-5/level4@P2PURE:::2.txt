This paper is interesting and provides useful insights into RCD and SGD algorithms. Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 is particularly valuable, as it suggests that the proposed sampling method outperforms L-based sampling, a commonly used strategy. I have the following comments for the authors to consider:

1. Line 85: In the denominator of p^*i, it should be x_k, not x.
2. Line 135: Consider removing the subscript k from \hat p and \hat c for consistency.
3. The figures are too small; ticks, labels, numbers, and text are unreadable. Please move some material to the appendix to free space and enlarge the figures.
4. If possible, please provide a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, but comparing against uniform sampling (p = 1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. Please discuss how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. For example, what is the total complexity (including the O(n) time for updating l_k and u_k) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution?
