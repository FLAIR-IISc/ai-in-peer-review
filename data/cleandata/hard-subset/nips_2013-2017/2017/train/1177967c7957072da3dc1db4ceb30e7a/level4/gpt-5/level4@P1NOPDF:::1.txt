The authors present a “safe” adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Using lower and upper bounds on gradient values, they propose an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. The method is proven to be the best strategy with respect to these bounds, strictly better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and it can be computed with negligible extra cost. Although adaptive importance sampling strategies have been proposed before, the paper offers a novel formulation that selects the optimal sampling distribution via a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm to solve it. The paper is well written and makes a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:
- Proof of Lemma 2.1: It seems alpha^* is missing a factor of 2.
- Example 3.1: In (7) you maximize the objective. In Example 3.1, it appears that setting c to either the upper or lower bound yields better performance than uniform sampling. Is that the intended takeaway? This is confusing given the preceding statement that the naive approach of setting c to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: Given that m = max(l^{sort}), will this condition ever be satisfied?
- Algorithm 4, line 7: Should this be u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}?
- Empirics: The numerical results would be stronger with comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.
- Why are there no timing results for SGD?
- The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect.
