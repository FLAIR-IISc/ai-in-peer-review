- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent and SGD using safe lower/upper gradient bounds.  
- Frames selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem solvable with those bounds.  
- Method is generic and easily integrable into existing algorithms.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the adaptive sampling outperforms any fixed importance sampling.  
- Computational cost is O(n log n) per iteration, comparable to evaluating one gradient component.  
- Empirical results show adaptive sampling significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in iterations and time.  
- Adaptive sampling requires adaptive step sizes for best performance.  
- Very loose safe gradient bounds are sufficient for the method to work well.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, original, and significant per conference criteria.  
- Figures in Section 5 are low quality and need larger size and labels for readability.  
- Could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on improving Figure quality.
