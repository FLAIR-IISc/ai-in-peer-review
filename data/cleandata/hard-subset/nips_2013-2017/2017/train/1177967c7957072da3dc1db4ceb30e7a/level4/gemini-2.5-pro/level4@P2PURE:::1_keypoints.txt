- Presents a "safe" adaptive importance sampling method for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Proposes a novel convex optimization formulation to select the optimal sampling distribution and provides an efficient solver.
- Proves the strategy is optimal given the bounds and shows consistent improvement over uniform/fixed sampling with negligible overhead.
- Paper is well-written and a solid contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Lemma 2.1 proof likely missing a factor of 2 in the expression for α*.
- Example 3.1 is confusing: text claims setting c to bounds can be suboptimal, but the example suggests setting c to a bound outperforms uniform sampling—needs clarification.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: unclear if condition l_i^{sort} == m can be false given m = max(l^{sort}); purpose of the check should be clarified.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: probable typo—c^{sort} should be u^{sort}.
- Empirical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Missing timing results for the SGD experiments to assess computational overhead.
- Reference [14] contains an incorrect paper title.
- Suggest adding Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," arXiv 2016.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with authors' responses and recommends acceptance.
