- Proposes "Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling": use maintainable gradient bounds to approximate optimal sampling via a minimax formulation.  
- Provides an O(n log n) algorithm to solve the minimax problem efficiently.  
- Sampling strategy is provably optimal given the bounds and guaranteed to outperform uniform and standard fixed importance sampling.  
- Novel formulation (Equation 7) that principledly handles gradient uncertainty and moves beyond heuristic adaptive schemes.  
- Strong theoretical analysis, notably Theorem 3.2 establishing the safety guarantee.  
- Complete work coupling theoretical motivation, algorithm, and empirical validation.  
- Well-written and clearly organized, with a motivating introduction and good exposition.  
- Experiments should compare against other state-of-the-art adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., refs [5, 23]) to better situate practical performance.  
- Include Li-based fixed sampling in experimental plots since theory compares to it as a strong baseline.  
- Add timing results for the SGD experiments to verify negligible overhead in that setting.
