The authors introduce a safe adaptive importance sampling strategy applicable to coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Leveraging lower and upper bounds on gradient values, they propose an efficient approximation to gradient-based sampling. The method is proven to be optimal within the constraints of these bounds, consistently outperforming uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed with minimal additional overhead. While adaptive importance sampling has been explored previously, this work presents a novel formulation, framing the selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and providing an efficient algorithm for its solution. 

This paper is well-written and represents a valuable contribution to the field of importance sampling techniques.

Comments:
Proof of Lemma 2.1 – appears to be missing a factor of 2 in the definition of α*.
Example 3.1 – In equation (7), the objective is maximization. Therefore, in Example 3.1, setting *c* to the upper or lower bound appears superior to using uniform sampling. Is this the intended demonstration? The preceding statement suggesting the naive approach of setting *c* to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal creates some confusion.
Line 4 of Algorithm 4 – given that *m* = max(*l*<sup>sort</sup>), will this condition ever be met?
Line 7 of Algorithm 4 – should be *u*<sup>sort</sup> instead of *c*<sup>sort</sup>?
The numerical results would be strengthened by comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., [2], [5],[21]) and against fixed importance sampling employing a non-uniform distribution.
The paper lacks timing results for SGD.
The title of the paper cited as [14] is inaccurate.
