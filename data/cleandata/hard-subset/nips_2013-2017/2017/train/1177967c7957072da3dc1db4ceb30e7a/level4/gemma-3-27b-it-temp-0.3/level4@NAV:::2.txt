This paper presents interesting insights regarding Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly useful result is Conclusion 2 within Theorem 3.2, which demonstrates the potential superiority of the proposed sampling method compared to the commonly employed L-based sampling strategy. I offer the following comments for the authors’ consideration:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be corrected from ‘x’ to ‘xk’.
2. For consistency, I recommend removing the subscript ‘k’ from \hat p and \hat c in Line 135.
3. The figures are currently too small and lack visible ticks, labels, numbers, and text. To improve readability, please consider relocating some figures to the appendix to allow for larger, more detailed presentations of the remaining figures.
4. It would be valuable to include a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but with a comparison to uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L_i-based method.
5. Please discuss the impact of this new sampling method on the overall computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, providing an analysis of the total complexity – including the O(n) time required for updating lk and uk – to achieve an epsilon-optimal solution with RCD would be beneficial.
