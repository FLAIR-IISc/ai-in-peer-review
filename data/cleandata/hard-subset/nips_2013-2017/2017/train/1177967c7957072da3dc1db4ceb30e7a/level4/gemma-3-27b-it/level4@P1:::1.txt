The authors present a "safe" adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods. Based on lower and upper bounds on the gradient values, an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling is proposed. The method is proven to be the best strategy with respect to the given bounds, always better than uniform or fixed importance sampling, and can be computed efficiently with negligible extra cost. While adaptive importance sampling strategies have been proposed previously, the authors present a novel formulation of selecting the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and present an efficient algorithm to solve it.

This paper is well-written and represents a valuable contribution to the study of importance sampling techniques. The theoretical analysis appears sound, and the proposed method addresses a practical challenge in applying adaptive importance sampling – computational cost. The connection to existing work is clearly established, and the empirical evaluation provides convincing evidence of the method's effectiveness.

Here are some specific comments and suggestions:

*   **Lemma 2.1 Proof:** The authors should double-check the proof of Lemma 2.1. It appears there might be a missing factor of 2 in the expression for α*.
*   **Example 3.1:** This example is slightly confusing. The authors state that setting *c* to the upper or lower bound can be suboptimal, yet the example suggests the opposite. Clarifying this point would improve understanding.
*   **Algorithm 4, Line 4:** Given that *m* is initialized as the maximum of the sorted lower bounds, the condition in line 4 may never be satisfied. This should be checked and potentially revised.
*   **Algorithm 4, Line 7:**  It appears there is a typo in line 7; it should likely be *u<sup>sort</sup>* instead of *c<sup>sort</sup>*.
*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical results would benefit from comparisons to other adaptive sampling schemes, such as those presented in [2], [5], and [21], and against fixed importance sampling with a non-uniform distribution.
*   **SGD Timing Results:** It would be helpful to include timing results for the SGD experiments to provide a complete picture of the method’s performance.
*   **References:** The title of the paper in reference [14] is incorrect. Additionally, the authors should consider adding the following relevant reference: Csiba and Richtarik, "Importance Sampling for Minibatches," 2016 (arXiv).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for the proposed method.
*   **Novelty:** The formulation of the sampling distribution selection as a convex optimization problem is a novel and effective approach.
*   **Practicality:** The method is computationally efficient and can be readily integrated into existing algorithms.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness on real datasets.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Minor Typos/Clarifications:** The identified minor issues (typos in Algorithm 4, and clarity in Example 3.1) should be addressed.
*   **Limited Comparison:** A more comprehensive comparison to other adaptive sampling schemes would further strengthen the paper.
