- Proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling strategy for coordinate descent and stochastic gradient methods using lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Introduces an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is proven optimal with respect to those bounds.
- Empirically outperforms uniform and fixed importance sampling while adding negligible computation.
- Novel formulation: casts selection of the optimal sampling distribution as a convex optimization problem and gives an efficient solver.
- Paper is well-written and a valuable contribution to importance sampling techniques.
- Proof issue: Lemma 2.1 appears to miss a factor of 2 in the definition of α*.
- Example 3.1 ambiguity: confusion whether setting c to upper/lower bound is advantageous versus the claim that this can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: with m = max(l^sort), the stated condition may never be satisfied.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely should use u^sort instead of c^sort.
- Experiments should compare to other adaptive sampling schemes (e.g., refs [2], [5], [21]) and to fixed non-uniform importance sampling.
- Missing timing results for SGD; include timings for fairness.
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14]; add Csiba & Richtarik (2016) "Importance Sampling for Minibatches" (arXiv).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends clear acceptance.
