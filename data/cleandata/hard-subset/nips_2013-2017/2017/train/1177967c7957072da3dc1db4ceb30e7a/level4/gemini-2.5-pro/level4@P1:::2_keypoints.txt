- Paper introduces "Safe Adaptive Importance Sampling": uses maintainable lower/upper gradient bounds and a minimax formulation to approximate optimal gradient-based sampling; provides an O(n log n) algorithm and shows it outperforms fixed importance sampling in practice.
- Strength: Provable safety—Theorem 3.2(ii) guarantees the adaptive sampling is never worse than L_i-based importance sampling.
- Strength: The minimax formulation (Eq. 7) is well-motivated and principled for handling uncertainty from gradient bounds.
- Strength: Algorithm 4 is efficient and practical (O(n log n) sampling); empirical results show improvements in total computation time, not just iterations.
- Improvement: Provide a full convergence analysis and a theorem for total runtime to reach an ε-optimal solution to enable direct theoretical comparisons with uniform and L_i-based sampling.
- Improvement: Strengthen and formalize comparisons to uniform sampling (e.g., an analogue of Theorem 3.2(ii)) since uniform is a common baseline.
- Improvement: Improve figure clarity—remake Figures 2–9 at higher resolution/larger size and consider moving less critical plots to an appendix.
- Minor: Fix notation in Example 2.3—ensure gradient is consistently written as ∇f(xk) for the current iterate.
- Minor: Make notation consistent for the solution variables—remove or harmonize subscripts k and use consistent symbols (p̂, ĉ / α̂) in Theorem 3.2 and surrounding text.
