I think this article is quite interesting and provides some good insights into the RCD and SDG algorithms. Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 is very useful because it suggests that the new sampling method is better than the L-based sampling, which is a widely used technique. I have some comments that I would like the authors to consider.

1. In line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be written as xk, not as x.
2. In line 135, you can remove the subscript k for \hat p and \hat c if you want, just to maintain consistency.
3. I don't see any ticks, labels, numbers, or words because they are all too small. Please remove some of the details in the appendix so you can show the larger figures.
4. If possible, provide a result similar to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, but compare it with uniform sampling (p=1/n) instead of the L-based method.
5. Please comment on how this new sampling method will affect the total complexity of RCD or SDG. For example, what would be the total complexity of RCD (with O(n) time to update lk and uk) to find an epsilon-optimal solution?
