- Paper proposes a safe, efficiently computable adaptive importance sampling scheme approximating gradient-based sampling for CD and SGD.
- The method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, with Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 showing improvement over L_i-based sampling.
- Authors present a generic approach applicable to both coordinate descent and stochastic gradient descent.
- Extensive numerical experiments demonstrate empirical effectiveness and include comparisons across stepsize strategies.
- Novel contribution: practical solution to implement adaptive importance sampling efficiently.
- Strong theoretical guarantees linking the method to optimal gradient-based sampling.
- Clear motivation outlining limitations of existing (computationally infeasible) adaptive sampling methods.
- Writing is generally good and literature coverage is solid.
- Notation and some explanations lack clarity or consistency and should be improved.
- Figures are too small and missing ticks/labels/numbers, reducing interpretability.
- Paper compares primarily to L_i-based sampling but lacks direct comparison to uniform sampling (p=1/n).
- No clear analysis of total computational complexity including costs to update bounds l_k and u_k.
- Specific corrections: denominator of p*_i should use x_k (Line 85); consider removing subscript k for p̂ and ĉ (Line 135).
- Recommend moving some figures to the appendix to allow larger, clearer presentation.
- Suggest extending Theorem 3.2 to include comparison against uniform sampling to strengthen impact.
- Request to provide complexity analysis for achieving an epsilon-optimal solution with the new sampling scheme.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and provably better method, strong empirical results, and good writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate figures, missing uniform-sampling comparison, and lack of complexity analysis; plus minor notation issues.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially valuable contribution requiring minor revisions.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; authors must improve figures and add a discussion of overall computational complexity.
