This paper proposes a safe adaptive importance sampling scheme for coordinate descent (CD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithms. The authors argue that optimal gradient-based sampling is theoretically well-justified and can lead to significant improvements over static strategies. However, computing the optimal adaptive sampling distribution is often inadmissible due to its high computational cost. To address this issue, the authors propose an efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that relies on safe lower and upper bounds on the gradient.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including its properties and competitive ratio. The authors also demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed scheme through extensive numerical testing on various machine learning tasks. The results show that the proposed adaptive sampling scheme significantly outperforms fixed importance sampling in terms of iterations and time.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical and practical contributions. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their proposed method, while also demonstrating its effectiveness in practice. The paper is also well-organized and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

However, there are a few issues that need to be addressed. The figures in Section 5 are very difficult to see properly and should be made larger, with larger labels, so that the reader can see them properly.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method. The paper is also well-written and easy to follow. The proposed method is original and provides a new perspective on adaptive importance sampling. Finally, the paper is significant, as it addresses an important problem in the field and provides a practical solution.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method.
* The authors demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed scheme through extensive numerical testing.
* The paper balances theoretical and practical contributions.
* The proposed method is original and provides a new perspective on adaptive importance sampling.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The figures in Section 5 are difficult to see properly and need to be improved.
* Some of the notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts in the field.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but with the condition that the authors improve the figures in Section 5.
