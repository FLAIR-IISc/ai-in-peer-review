

This paper presents an interesting analysis of sampling strategies for randomized coordinate descent (RCD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD), offering valuable insights into the theoretical advantages of the proposed method. The key contribution lies in Theorem 3.2, particularly Conclusion 2, which establishes a concrete advantage of the new sampling method over the commonly used $L_i$-based sampling, providing a clear theoretical justification for its adoption. I recommend the authors address the following points to strengthen the paper:

1.  **Notational Consistency (Line 85):** The expression for $p^*_i$ contains $x_k$ in the denominator, but it should be $x$ (the vector, not the $k$-th iterate). This typographical error needs correction for clarity.

2.  **Notational Consistency (Line 135):** The subscripts $k$ on $\hat{p}$ and $\hat{c}$ appear inconsistent with their usage elsewhere. I recommend removing these subscripts to align with standard notation and improve readability.

3.  **Figure Legibility:** All figures lack adequately sized ticks, labels, and text, making them difficult to interpret. To maintain readability and focus on the core results, I strongly recommend moving some of the less critical figure elements (e.g., specific axis labels, minor tick marks) to the appendix. This will allow the main figures to be presented at a larger, more readable scale.

4.  **Comparison with Uniform Sampling:** A significant theoretical extension would be to derive a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, but comparing the proposed sampling method directly against uniform sampling ($p_i = 1/n$). This comparison is highly relevant, as uniform sampling is a fundamental baseline strategy. Providing such a comparison would further solidify the practical advantages of the proposed method and strengthen the paper's contribution.

5.  **Practical Complexity Analysis:** The paper establishes a strong theoretical advantage for the sampling method. To fully assess its practical impact, the authors should explicitly address how this method affects the *total computational complexity* of RCD or SGD. Specifically, they should provide the total complexity (including the $O(n)$ cost for updating $l_k$ and $u_k$ at each iteration) required to achieve an $\epsilon$-optimal solution. This analysis is crucial for practitioners to understand the real-world efficiency gains (or potential overheads) of adopting the new sampling scheme.

Overall, the paper makes a compelling theoretical contribution regarding sampling strategies. The core insight in Theorem 3.2 is valuable, and addressing the points above—especially the comparison with uniform sampling and the explicit total complexity analysis—would significantly enhance the paper's impact and clarity. The notational corrections and figure improvements are essential for readability and professionalism. I believe this work has merit and would be a strong contribution to NIPS upon addressing these points.
