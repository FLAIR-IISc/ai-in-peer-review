This paper presents interesting insights regarding Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly useful result is Conclusion 2 within Theorem 3.2, which demonstrates the potential advantages of the proposed sampling method over the commonly employed L-based sampling strategy. I have the following suggestions for the authors’ consideration:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be corrected from ‘x’ to ‘xk’.
2. For consistency, I recommend removing the subscript ‘k’ from \hat p and \hat c in Line 135.
3. The figures are currently too small and lack visible ticks, labels, numbers, and accompanying text. To improve readability, please consider relocating some figures to the appendix to allow for larger, more detailed visualizations in the main text.
4. It would be valuable to include a result analogous to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, but this time comparing the performance of the new sampling method against uniform sampling (p=1/n) as a baseline.
5. Please discuss the implications of this new sampling method on the overall computational complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, quantifying the total complexity – including the O(n) time required for updating lk and uk – for RCD to achieve an epsilon-optimal solution would be highly beneficial.
