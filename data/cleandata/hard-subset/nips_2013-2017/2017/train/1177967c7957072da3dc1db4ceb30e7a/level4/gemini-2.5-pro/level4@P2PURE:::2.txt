This paper presents interesting insights into Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. A particularly valuable contribution is Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2, which demonstrates that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used `L_i`-based sampling strategy. I offer the following comments and suggestions for the authors to consider.

1.  Line 85: In the denominator of the expression for `p_i^*`, the term `x` should be `x_k`.

2.  Line 135: For notational consistency, consider removing the subscript `k` from `\hat{p}` and `\hat{c}`.

3.  All figures are too small, rendering axis ticks, labels, and numbers illegible. Please enlarge the figures to improve readability. If space is a constraint, consider moving some figures to the appendix.

4.  The analysis would be strengthened by providing a result analogous to Conclusion 2 of Theorem 3.2 that compares the proposed method against uniform sampling (i.e., `p_i = 1/n`), in addition to the current comparison with the `L_i`-based method.

5.  Please include a discussion on how the new sampling method affects the total computational complexity of RCD and SGD. Specifically, what is the total complexity for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution when accounting for the `O(n)` time required to update `l_k` and `u_k` in each iteration?
