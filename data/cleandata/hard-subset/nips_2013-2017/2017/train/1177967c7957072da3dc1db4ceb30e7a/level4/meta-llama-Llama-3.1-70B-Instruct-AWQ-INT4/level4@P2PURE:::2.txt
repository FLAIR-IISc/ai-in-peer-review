This paper is interesting and provides valuable insights into the Randomized Coordinate Descent (RCD) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) algorithms. The conclusion in Theorem 3.2 is particularly noteworthy, as it suggests that the proposed sampling method outperforms the commonly used L-based sampling strategy. I have several comments that I would like the authors to consider:

1. In Line 85, the denominator of p^*i should be xk, not x, to ensure consistency.
2. In Line 135, the subscripts k for \hat p and \hat c could be removed for consistency.
3. The figures are too small, making it difficult to discern any ticks, labels, numbers, or words. To address this, some figures could be relocated to the appendix, allowing for larger, more legible versions to be presented in the main text.
4. If feasible, it would be beneficial to provide a comparison between the proposed sampling method and uniform sampling (p=1/n), similar to Conclusion 2 in Theorem 3.2, which currently compares it to the L-based method.
5. The authors should discuss how the new sampling method affects the total complexity of RCD or SGD. Specifically, they could analyze the total complexity (including the O(n) time required for updating lk and uk) for RCD to find an epsilon-optimal solution, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the method's efficiency.
