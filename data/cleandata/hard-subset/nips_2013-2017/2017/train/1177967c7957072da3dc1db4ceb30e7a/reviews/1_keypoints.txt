- Proposes a “safe” adaptive importance sampling for coordinate descent and SGD using lower/upper gradient bounds.
- Efficient approximation of gradient-based sampling that is provably optimal relative to the provided bounds.
- Method always outperforms uniform or fixed importance sampling and has negligible extra computational cost.
- Novel formulation of optimal sampling as a convex optimization problem with an efficient solver.
- Paper is well written and a valuable contribution to importance sampling literature.
- Lemma 2.1 proof appears to be missing a factor of 2 in alpha^*.
- Example 3.1 is confusing: maximizing in (7) seems to favor setting c to bounds, contradicting the preceding claim that naive bound-setting can be suboptimal.
- Algorithm 4, line 4: if m = max(l^{sort}), the condition may never be satisfied—questionable logic.
- Algorithm 4, line 7: likely a typo—should use u^{sort} instead of c^{sort}.
- Numerical results should include comparisons to other adaptive sampling methods (e.g., [2],[5],[21]) and to non-uniform fixed importance sampling.
- No timing results provided for SGD experiments.
- Reference issues: incorrect title for [14]; add Csiba & Richtarik “Importance Sampling for Minibatches” (2016, arXiv).
- After authors’ rebuttal, reviewer recommends acceptance.
