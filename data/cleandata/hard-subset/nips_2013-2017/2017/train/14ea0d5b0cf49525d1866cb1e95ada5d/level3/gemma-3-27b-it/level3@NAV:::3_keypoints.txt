- Proposes Aggressive Double Sampling to reduce extreme multi-class to pairwise binary classification, balancing class representation and lowering pairwise example cost.  
- Targets limitations of tree-based cascading errors and label-embedding low-rank assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis gives consistency guarantees despite induced dependencies and oversampling, leveraging Janson’s concentration inequalities.  
- Empirical results show superior accuracy and F1 on text datasets (notably >30k classes) and large gains in training time and memory.  
- Prediction is slower due to the candidate-selection heuristic, acknowledged as an important trade-off.  
- Strength: novel, creative sampling-based reduction for extreme classification.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor addressing dependencies from sampling.  
- Strength: compelling empirical performance and efficiency on large-scale datasets.  
- Strength: clear writing and explanation of core ideas.  
- Weakness: dense, technical theoretical exposition may be hard for non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: prediction-time bottleneck could limit real-time applicability.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to sampling parameters (class frequency and adversarial examples) not thoroughly explored.  
- Acceptance argument: significant, theoretically grounded contribution with strong empirical benefits.  
- Rejection argument: theoretical complexity and prediction slowdown are notable concerns.  
- Overall assessment: Strong accept — valuable addition likely to impact extreme classification.
