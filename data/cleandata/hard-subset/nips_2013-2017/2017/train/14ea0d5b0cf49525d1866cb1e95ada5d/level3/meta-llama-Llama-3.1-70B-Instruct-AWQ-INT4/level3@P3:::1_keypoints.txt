- Proposes a double sampling (π, κ)-DS strategy that transforms multiclass learning into binary classification over pairs to handle extreme classification.  
- Aims to reduce the number of constructed dyadic examples and balance class presence by oversampling small classes and subsampling large ones.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Shows consistency of empirical risk minimization despite label distribution shift and dependency between pairs.  
- Empirical evaluation on large datasets (including DMOZ and Wikipedia up to 100k classes).  
- Reports improvements over state-of-the-art in training time, memory consumption, and predictive performance.  
- Prediction time remains relatively long compared to some alternatives.  
- Strengths: novel sampling strategy, comprehensive experiments, addresses a practical extreme-classification problem.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty since approach builds largely on prior work [16].  
- Weaknesses: unclear interpretation and comparative advantage of the presented generalization bound.  
- Quality: technically sound with theoretical and empirical support, but evaluation could be more balanced.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written and organized; theoretical sections may be hard for non-experts.  
- Originality: new combination of familiar techniques—useful but not highly novel.  
- Significance: high practical impact and advancement of the state of the art; likely to be adopted or extended.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing novelty concerns, clarifying the generalization bound, and better contrasting with existing methods.
