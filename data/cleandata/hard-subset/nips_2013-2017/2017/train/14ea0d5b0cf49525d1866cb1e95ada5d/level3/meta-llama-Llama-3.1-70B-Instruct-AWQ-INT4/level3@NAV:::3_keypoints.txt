- Proposes a novel double-sampling strategy on a multi-class-to-binary reduction for extreme classification (up to 100k classes), forming binary tasks over pairs of examples.  
- Sampling strategy designed to mitigate long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise exposition.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, providing generalization bounds and a consistency proof.  
- Experiments on large-scale text classification datasets demonstrate the method's effectiveness.  
- Strength: Scalable to very large numbers of classes, suitable for extreme classification.  
- Strength: Double sampling effectively reduces pair count and improves classifier performance.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical grounding supports the approach.  
- Weakness: Requires careful, potentially time-consuming hyperparameter tuning.  
- Weakness: May be ill-suited for small-scale classification where sampling offers little reduction.  
- Overall assessment: Significant contribution, recommended for publication in a top-tier venue (NIPS).  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, scalability, and strong theoretical analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Hyperparameter tuning burden and limited applicability to small-scale problems.
