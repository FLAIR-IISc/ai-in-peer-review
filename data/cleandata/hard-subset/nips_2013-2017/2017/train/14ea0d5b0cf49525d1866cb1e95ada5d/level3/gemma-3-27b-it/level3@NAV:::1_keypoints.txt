- Tackles extreme multi-class classification using a double sampling strategy built on a multi-class-to-binary reduction.  
- Aims to mitigate long-tailed class distributions and reduce computational overhead via instance and pair sampling.  
- Provides theoretical generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexity tailored to the sampling scheme.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on large-scale text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia), including up to 100k classes.  
- Shows improvements in training time, memory consumption, and predictive accuracy over several baselines.  
- Well-motivated and practically relevant problem with scalable experimental validation.  
- Novelty is limited—primarily an extension of prior work [16] rather than a fundamentally new paradigm.  
- Prediction time is relatively long, which may be a critical drawback for real-world use.  
- Practical interpretation and advantage of the proposed generalization bound remain unclear and need elaboration.  
- Baseline comparisons may be skewed by mixing batch and online methods, making memory-efficiency claims potentially misleading.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance given empirical and theoretical contributions.  
- Acceptance suggested contingent on clarifying the generalization bound’s benefits and ensuring fair memory-usage comparisons.
