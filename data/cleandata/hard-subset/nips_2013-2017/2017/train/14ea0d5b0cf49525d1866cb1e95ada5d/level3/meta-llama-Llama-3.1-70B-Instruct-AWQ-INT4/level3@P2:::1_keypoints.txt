- Proposes an aggressive double sampling strategy to improve a multi-class-to-binary reduction method from [16], targeting extreme classification.  
- Method balances class presence by oversampling small classes and subsampling large classes, reducing dyadic examples.  
- Provides a new generalization bound based on local fractional Rademacher complexity, claiming faster convergence rates.  
- Presents an algorithm for the double sampling strategy and theoretical justification.  
- Empirical results on multiple text datasets (including LSHTC) show high performance and shorter training times.  
- Method scales to problems with a very large number of classes.  
- Novelty is limited because the work builds largely on [16], mainly adding double sampling and complexity bounds.  
- Prediction (inference) time remains long compared to other methods, which is a practical drawback.  
- Interpretation and comparative advantage of the new generalization bound are unclear and need more discussion.  
- Mixing batch and online baselines in experiments makes memory-usage comparisons uneven and potentially misleading.  
- Suggest separating batch vs. online comparisons or providing detailed memory-usage analysis.  
- Strengths: addresses an important practical problem, comprehensive experiments, and novel theoretical analysis.  
- Weaknesses: limited originality, unclear theoretical implications, and slower prediction time.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
