- Proposes PRUNE, a novel network embedding approach that preserves both global node ranking and community-aware proximity.  
- Uses a multi-task Siamese neural network trained with an objective combining proximity preservation and global ranking.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, with organized technical sections explaining model, objective, architecture, and training.  
- Novelty: combining proximity preservation with global node ranking is a unique contribution.  
- Provides extensive empirical evaluation on real datasets (Hep-Ph, Webspam, FB Wall Post).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across learning-to-rank, classification, regression, and link prediction tasks.  
- Key weakness: assumes global node ranking is available, which may not hold in many settings.  
- Key weakness: employs a simple neural network architecture that may not capture complex relations in large-scale networks.  
- Key weakness: lacks detailed analysis of time and space complexity, limiting assessment of practicality.  
- Overall assessment: novel and effective approach, suitable for publication in a top-tier conference (NIPS).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity, thorough technical exposition, and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on available rankings, simplistic architecture, and missing complexity analysis.
