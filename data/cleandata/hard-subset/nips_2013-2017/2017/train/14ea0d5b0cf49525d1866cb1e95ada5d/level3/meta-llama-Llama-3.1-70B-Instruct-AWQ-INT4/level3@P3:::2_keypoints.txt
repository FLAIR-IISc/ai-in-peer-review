- Proposes a novel double-sampling method to reduce large-scale multi-class classification to binary classification over pairs of examples.  
- Double sampling aggressively subsamples training data to mitigate long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs.  
- Methodology and reduction strategy are clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Experiments are well-designed and demonstrate effectiveness on large-scale text classification datasets.  
- Main contribution is the double sampling strategy, which reduces computational overhead and improves predictive performance.  
- Technical claims are well-supported by theory and empirical results.  
- Paper is well-organized, well-written, and sufficiently referenced for expert reproduction.  
- Originality: novel double sampling approach and new generalization-bound analysis.  
- Significance: potential impactful contribution to large-scale multi-class classification by improving efficiency and accuracy.  
- Weakness: prediction algorithm (Sec. 2.3) lacks sufficient detail, limiting comparisons to k nearest class centroids.  
- Weakness: needs more thorough comparison of sample complexity and generalization bounds with non-transformed multi-class formulations.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, valuable contribution with recommendation to accept (rating 8/10).
