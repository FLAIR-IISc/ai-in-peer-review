- Proposes a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification tailored for very large numbers of classes.  
- Introduces a double sampling strategy to rebalance long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs.  
- Provides generalization analysis using local fractional Rademacher complexity with improved bounds and an extension of Joshi et al. (2015).  
- Empirical results show competitiveness in time, memory, and performance against several strong baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, consistent mathematical notation.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with detailed descriptions of datasets, baselines, and evaluation measures.  
- Addresses correlated transformed datasets, improving robustness of the approach.  
- Main originality lies in the sampling/rebalancing technique; core reduction largely follows prior work (Joshi et al.).  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3, limiting comparisons to k nearest class centroids.  
- Missing discussion comparing sample complexity and generalization bounds to non-transformed multi-class formulations.  
- Technically sound and significant for large-scale multi-class classification problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel large-class reduction, double sampling, strong generalization analysis, and empirical competitiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absent theoretical analysis of prediction step and missing comparison of sample complexity/generalization to standard formulations.
