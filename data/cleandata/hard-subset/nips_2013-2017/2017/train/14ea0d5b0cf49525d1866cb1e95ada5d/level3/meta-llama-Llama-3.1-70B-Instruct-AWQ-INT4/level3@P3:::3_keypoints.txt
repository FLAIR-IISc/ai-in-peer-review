- Proposes a novel extreme classification method using a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class-to-binary reduction that creates binary problems over example pairs.  
- Aims to address long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of pairwise example combinations in the expanded data.  
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear explanation of the proposed solution and thorough related-work analysis.  
- Method is scalable and includes theoretical consistency guarantees relative to multiclass ERM.  
- Experimental results show superior accuracy and F1 for problems with >30,000 classes, faster training, and much lower memory usage.  
- Introduces a heuristic prediction-time candidate selection using class centroids to avoid enumerating all pairwise class features.  
- Theoretical analysis provides a generalization bound via local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Proof is nontrivial due to dependent dyadic examples and oversampling shifting the empirical distribution, but is clearly explained.  
- Strengths: novel scalable method, thorough related work discussion, strong empirical performance, and sound theoretical analysis.  
- Weaknesses: assumes long-tailed class distributions which may not always hold.  
- Weaknesses: lacks comparison with recent methods such as SLEEC and LEML.  
- Weaknesses: does not detail the computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging large-class problem with potential applications in text, image, and recommendation domains and strong empirical validation on five text datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS 2017; overall high-quality paper with a rating of 9/10.
