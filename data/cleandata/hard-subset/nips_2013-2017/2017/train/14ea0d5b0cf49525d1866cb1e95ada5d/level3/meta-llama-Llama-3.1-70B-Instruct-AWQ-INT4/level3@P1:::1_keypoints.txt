- Proposes a double sampling strategy to improve scalability for extreme multi-class classification, building on prior work.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexity.  
- Presents comprehensive experiments on text datasets showing good scalability, high performance, and shorter training times.  
- Double sampling targets long-tailed class distributions and reduces the number of expanded-data pairs while preserving ERM consistency.  
- Novelty is limited because the work largely extends the authors’ previous method by adding double sampling and new bounds.  
- Prediction time remains long relative to some competing methods, posing a practical drawback despite faster training.  
- The interpretation and comparative advantage of the presented generalization bound are unclear and need further explanation.  
- Mixing batch and online baselines creates uneven and potentially misleading memory-usage comparisons.  
- The paper tackles an important, practical problem with potential applications in text classification, recommendation, and ranking.  
- Recommends acceptance with revisions to clarify the bounds, provide careful prediction-time and memory comparisons, and explore broader applications.  
- Further research is needed to fully explore the approach’s potential and address the identified limitations.
