- Paper proposes a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification for very large label spaces, using fractional Rademacher complexity and a double sampling strategy; shows strong results on DMOZ and Wikipedia (up to 100k classes).

- Scalability and efficiency: double sampling balances classes and reduces training size, improving runtime and memory vs OVA and M-SVM.

- Theoretical contributions: provides generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity, arguing consistency despite sampling and dependencies.

- Empirical validation: extensive large-scale experiments show competitive accuracy and macro F1 while keeping computational costs low.

- Baseline comparisons: evaluated against tree-based, embedding-based, and traditional methods and demonstrates a superior trade-off among performance, runtime, and memory.

- Weakness — prediction theory: nearest-centroid-based candidate selection lacks detailed theoretical analysis of its performance impact.

- Weakness — sample complexity: paper needs more detailed discussion of sample complexity bounds and comparison to untransformed multi-class problems.

- Weakness — incremental novelty: core reduction builds on prior work (Joshi et al. 2015); novelty is mainly in the double sampling strategy.

- Pro arguments: addresses a critical large-scale classification challenge with practical, scalable method and solid theoretical/empirical support.

- Con arguments: incomplete theoretical treatment of the prediction algorithm and somewhat incremental methodological novelty.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add theoretical analysis of the prediction step and better emphasize unique contributions.
