- Experimental claim: comprehensive comparisons show high performance and shorter training times on several large-scale text datasets.  
- Strength: addresses the important, practical problem of extreme classification.  
- Strength: combines familiar techniques (double sampling, local Rademacher complexity) in a novel way.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness on large-scale text classification tasks.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—largely builds on [16] with added double sampling and local Rademacher bounds.  
- Weakness: prediction time remains long compared to other methods, which may matter more in practice.  
- Weakness: interpretation and comparative advantage of the presented generalization bound are unclear.  
- Weakness: mixing batch and online baselines makes memory-usage comparisons uneven and potentially misleading.  
- Quality: technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments, but should discuss limitations and prediction-time impact more.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, though some technical derivations (e.g., generalization bound) need clearer explanation.  
- Originality: offers a novel combination of known techniques, but overall originality is limited by reliance on prior work.  
- Significance: tackles an important area and demonstrates practical effectiveness on large-scale datasets.  
- For acceptance: important problem, strong empirical performance, and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, long prediction time, and unclear benefits of the generalization bound.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
