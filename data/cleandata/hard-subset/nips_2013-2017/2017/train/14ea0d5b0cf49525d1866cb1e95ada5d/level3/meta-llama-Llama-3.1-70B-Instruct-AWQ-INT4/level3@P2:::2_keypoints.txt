- Addresses large-scale multi-class classification with very many classes using a double sampling strategy on a multi-class-to-binary reduction (pairs of examples).
- Sampling strategy aims to mitigate long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs.
- Paper is well-motivated and relevant to the NIPS community.
- Related work overview is clear and concise, highlighting limitations of existing approaches.
- Main novelty is the sampling/rebalancing technique built on an existing reduction (Joshi et al., 2015).
- Provides a new generalization bound using local fractional Rademacher complexity, improving over existing bounds.
- Theoretical analysis of the sampling strategy’s effect on generalization bounds is provided.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, compares to strong baselines, and shows competitive performance.
- Results are presented clearly, making strengths and weaknesses easy to understand.
- Limitation: core reduction largely follows Joshi et al. (2015), so novelty is mainly in sampling.
- Limitation: no theoretical comparison of sample complexity/generalization bounds to non-transformed multi-class formulations.
- Limitation: lacks theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3 (k nearest class centroids), limiting comparisons.
- Writing and presentation are strong (Clarity 9/10).
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel sampling idea, solid theory, strong experiments, well-written.
- Arguments for rejection: reliance on prior reduction, missing theoretical analysis of prediction step, no comparison of sample complexity to non-transformed methods.
