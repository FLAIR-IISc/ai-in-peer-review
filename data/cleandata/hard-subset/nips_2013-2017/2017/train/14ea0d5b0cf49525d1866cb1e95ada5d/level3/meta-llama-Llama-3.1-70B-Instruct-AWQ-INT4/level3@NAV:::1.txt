This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass method in [16], with both theoretical and empirical analysis. The method scales to multiclass problems with many classes, as demonstrated by experiments on text datasets. The paper provides a comprehensive experimental comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, showing high performance and shorter training times on several datasets.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed method addresses the important and practical area of extreme classification.
* The paper provides a novel combination of familiar techniques, including double sampling and local Rademacher complexity bounds.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on large-scale text classification tasks.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The novelty of the paper is limited, as it largely builds on [16] and adds double sampling and local Rademacher complexity bounds.
* The prediction time remains long compared to other methods, which may be more important in practice despite faster training times.
* The interpretation and comparative advantage of the presented generalization bound are unclear.
* The mixing of batch and online baselines makes memory-usage comparisons uneven and potentially misleading.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and the potential impact of the long prediction times.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the organization is clear. However, some of the technical details, such as the derivation of the generalization bound, could be explained more clearly.

In terms of originality, the paper provides a novel combination of familiar techniques, but the novelty is limited by the fact that it builds on existing work.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important and practical area of extreme classification, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on large-scale text classification tasks.

* The paper provides a comprehensive experimental comparison with state-of-the-art approaches.

Arguments against acceptance:
