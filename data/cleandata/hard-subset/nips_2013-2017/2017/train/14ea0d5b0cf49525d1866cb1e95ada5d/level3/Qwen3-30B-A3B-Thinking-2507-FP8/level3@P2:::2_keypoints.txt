- Proposes (π, κ)-DS: an aggressive double sampling strategy for multi-class→binary reduction to scale to 10k–100k classes and long-tailed distributions.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015): oversamples small classes, subsamples large ones (inverse-proportional), then samples κ comparison classes per example.  
- Reduces O(mK) dyadic construction cost by randomly sampling κ negatives to shrink the transformed dataset.  
- Theoretical contribution: new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity, accounting for dyadic example dependencies and preserving ERM consistency.  
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive experiments on large text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) up to 100k classes, showing better trade-offs in training/prediction time, memory, and accuracy vs baselines.  
- Well-written with clear algorithmic and theoretical descriptions and careful comparisons to OVA, M-SVM, FastXML, PfastReXML, PD-Sparse.  
- Strength: effectively addresses practical scalability and long-tailed class distribution issues in extreme classification.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis that tightens prior bounds and handles inter-dependent dyadic samples.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—core reduction (φ(x,y) mapping) largely follows prior work; sampling is the main new element.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical comparison of sample complexity/generalization to standard multi-class formulations.  
- Weakness: prediction-stage candidate selection (nearest centroids, choice of q) is not theoretically analyzed.  
- Weakness: unclear how sensitive the sampling strategy is to different feature representations (experiments use a single φ).  
- Pro: practically significant problem, well-motivated method, strong theory and compelling empirical results.  
- Con: incremental novelty, missing theory for prediction algorithm, incomplete comparison to direct multi-class learning.  
- Overall: valuable and empirically strong contribution but with limited novelty and some theoretical gaps; reviewer recommends acceptance with suggested theoretical clarifications.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
