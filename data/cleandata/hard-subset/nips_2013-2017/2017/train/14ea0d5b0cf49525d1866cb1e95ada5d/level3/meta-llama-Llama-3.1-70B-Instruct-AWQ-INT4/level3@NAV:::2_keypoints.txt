- Proposes a double sampling strategy atop a multi-class-to-binary reduction that transforms the task into binary classification over pairs of examples for many-class problems.
- The sampling strategy is designed to mitigate long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs generated.
- Provides new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity, showing ERM on the transformed sampled data remains consistent.
- Empirical results on large-scale text classification datasets show improvements in training and prediction time, memory usage, and predictive performance.
- Novelty: introduces a previously unexplored double sampling technique for multi-class classification.
- Strength: the method effectively addresses long-tailed class issues and improves predictive performance.
- Strength: experiments demonstrate the approach’s scalability and resource efficiency on large datasets.
- Weakness: lacks a theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3, restricting comparisons to k nearest class centroids.
- Weakness: no discussion comparing the sample complexity or generalization bounds to standard (non-transformed) multi-class formulations.
- Significance: tackles an important problem (many-class classification) with broad applicability (text, image, recommendation systems).
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and explanations of the approach and theory are clear.
- Quality: technically sound with thorough analysis and well-designed experiments.
- For acceptance: novel approach, effective double sampling, and strong empirical evidence on large-scale problems.
- Against acceptance: missing theoretical treatment of the prediction step and absence of comparative sample-complexity analysis.
