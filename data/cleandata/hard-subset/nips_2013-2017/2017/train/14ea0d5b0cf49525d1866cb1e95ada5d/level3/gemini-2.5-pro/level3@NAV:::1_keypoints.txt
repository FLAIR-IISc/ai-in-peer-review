- Addresses extreme multi-class classification by proposing a double sampling strategy to scale a multi-class-to-binary reduction method.  
- Double sampling aims to balance long-tailed class distributions (over-sample rare, under-sample frequent) and reduce pair expansion to cut computational cost.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: a new generalization bound using local Rademacher complexity.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on large-scale text datasets, demonstrating scalability up to ~100,000 classes.  
- Shows strong predictive performance (Accuracy, Macro-F1) and substantial reductions in training time and memory versus baselines (OVA, M-SVM, PD-Sparse).  
- Sampling scheme is well-motivated and effective for extreme-class challenges.  
- Main novelty is limited—largely an incremental improvement over prior work [16], with sampling and a bound as primary contributions.  
- Prediction (inference) time remains long compared to tree-based methods like FastXML, which is important for low-latency applications.  
- Paper does not sufficiently address the trade-off between training efficiency and inference latency.  
- Theoretical contribution lacks clear interpretation or comparison to existing generalization bounds.  
- Memory-usage comparison may be misleading by mixing batch and online baselines with different memory profiles.  
- Overall: well-executed empirical study with modest novelty but meaningful performance and efficiency gains.
