- Tackles extreme multi-class classification by improving scalability of multi-class-to-binary reduction.  
- Proposes an aggressive double sampling strategy ((π, κ)-DS) to rebalance classes and reduce pairwise example explosion.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) and derives improved generalization bounds via local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Shows the sampling procedure is a logical way to mitigate class imbalance and dataset size issues.  
- Theoretical analysis demonstrates consistency of ERM under the proposed sampling strategy.  
- Empirical validation on large-scale text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) shows competitive accuracy with much better runtime and memory.  
- Clear problem statement and well-motivated solution with good positioning in related work.  
- Strong empirical evidence of benefits in scenarios with extremely many classes.  
- Lacks a dedicated theoretical analysis for the k-nearest class-centroid prediction algorithm.  
- Does not sufficiently compare sample complexity/generalization bounds to non-transformed multi-class formulations.  
- Ablation study exists but needs more detailed analysis of the effects of parameters π and κ.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; accept with minor revisions addressing the theoretical and ablation concerns.
