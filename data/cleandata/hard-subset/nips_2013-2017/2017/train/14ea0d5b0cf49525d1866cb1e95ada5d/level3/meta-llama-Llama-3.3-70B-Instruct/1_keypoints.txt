- Proposes PRUNE, a network embedding method that preserves global node ranking and community-aware proximity.  
- Introduces a multi-task Siamese neural network architecture to learn node embeddings.  
- Provides theoretical analysis linking the proximity objective to community detection.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on learning-to-rank, classification, regression, and link prediction tasks.  
- Strength: novel combination of ranking preservation and community-aware proximity in embeddings.  
- Strength: multi-task Siamese design is a noteworthy architectural contribution.  
- Strength: convincing experimental results across multiple downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: overall novelty is limited—main contributions are a double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds.  
- Weakness: prediction/runtime is slower than competing methods, which may limit practicality.  
- Weakness: presented generalization bound is unclear and not compared against existing bounds.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms with different memory profiles, complicating fairness.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should clarify the generalization bound and strengthen comparative analyses.  
- Quality: 8/10 — well-written with convincing experiments and sound theory.  
- Clarity: 9/10 — clear organization, figures, and explanations.  
- Originality: 7/10 — builds on prior work with some novel elements.  
- Significance: 8/10 — effective across tasks and applicable to multiple domains.
