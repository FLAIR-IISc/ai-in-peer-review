- Proposes a (π, κ)-double sampling strategy that oversamples small classes and subsamples large ones, followed by a dyadic transformation, building on [16].  
- Provides new generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity.  
- Experiments show scalability on DMOZ and Wikipedia with up to 100,000 classes; evaluates macro F1 and prediction time.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates significant training time and memory improvements versus OVA and M-SVM, which become intractable for large K.  
- Method is practically relevant to extreme classification and experiments are comprehensive across datasets and metrics.  
- Novelty is limited—approach mainly extends [16] via double sampling without a fundamental conceptual advance.  
- Prediction time remains substantial compared to tree-based methods, posing a critical practical limitation.  
- Interpretation of the generalization bounds is unclear, making the theoretical significance difficult to assess.  
- Mixing batch and online baselines produces uneven memory-usage comparisons that may mislead practical conclusions.  
- Overall technically sound with appropriate validation, but incremental contribution and practical constraints reduce its impact.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized, but concerns about novelty, theory interpretation, and prediction time warrant careful consideration for acceptance.
