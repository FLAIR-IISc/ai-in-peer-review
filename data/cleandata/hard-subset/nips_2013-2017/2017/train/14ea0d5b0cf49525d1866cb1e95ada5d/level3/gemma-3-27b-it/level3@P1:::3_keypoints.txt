- Tackles extreme multi-class classification with up to 100k classes using a novel double sampling strategy on a multi-class-to-binary reduction.
- Uses aggressive sampling of original examples and pairwise (dyadic) examples to reduce computation and improve generalization.
- Explicitly addresses long-tailed class imbalance via intelligent (π, κ)-DS sampling.
- Builds on prior work: avoids OVA infeasibility, tree-based error propagation, and low-rank assumptions of label-embedding methods.
- Distinguishes itself from previous binary reductions by reducing dyadic construction cost and providing theoretical sampling guarantees.
- Provides a complex but rigorous theoretical analysis proving consistency and offering concentration-based guarantees.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on large datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia, >30k classes) in accuracy, F1, training time, and memory.
- Introduces a candidate selection method at prediction time to mitigate pairwise comparison slowdown.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and positions contributions relative to prior work.
- Theoretical exposition is dense and may be hard for some readers to follow in detail.
- Needs more discussion of assumptions behind concentration inequalities and applicability to the problem setting.
- Sensitivity of performance to theoretical parameters (α, β) is unclear and not thoroughly explored.
- Performance improvements are less pronounced on smaller datasets, suggesting context-dependent effectiveness.
- Prediction latency and added complexity from candidate selection remain potential practical concerns.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (confidence 8/10), citing significant theoretical and empirical contributions.
