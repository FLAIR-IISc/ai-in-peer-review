- Proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass method in [16], with both theoretical and empirical analysis.  
- Experiments on text datasets indicate the method scales to multiclass problems with many classes.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show high performance and shorter training time on several datasets.  
- Addresses the important and practical area of extreme classification.  
- Novelty is limited—paper largely builds on [16], adding double sampling and local Rademacher complexity bounds.  
- Prediction time remains long compared to other methods, which may be more important in practice despite faster training.  
- Unclear interpretation and comparative advantage of the presented generalization bound.  
- Mixing batch and online baselines makes memory-usage comparisons uneven and potentially misleading.
