- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification for very large K, mapping (input,class) pairs via phi to learn a score function.  
- Addresses correlated transformed datasets (from creating K-1 comparisons per example) which invalidate many standard generalization bounds.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) by providing improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Introduces sampling procedures: per-class sampling (with replacement) to rebalance classes and random sampling of comparison classes to reduce transformed dataset size.  
- Shows theoretically that the two sampling procedures do not significantly worsen the generalization bounds.  
- Empirically competitive performance against several strong baselines.  
- Main novelty is the sampling/rebalancing technique; the core reduction idea largely follows Joshi et al.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3 that limits comparisons to the k nearest class centroids.  
- Lacks discussion comparing the sample complexity/generalization bounds to those for non-transformed multi-class formulations.
