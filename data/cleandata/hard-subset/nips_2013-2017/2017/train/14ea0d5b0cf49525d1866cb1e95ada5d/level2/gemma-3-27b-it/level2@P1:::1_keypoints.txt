- Tackles large-scale extreme multi-class classification via a double sampling strategy on a multi-class-to-binary reduction.  
- Core idea: aggressive instance and class sampling to address dimensionality and class imbalance.  
- Provides theoretical justification through generalization bounds linking to local Rademacher complexity and graph covering.  
- Empirical validation on large datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) up to 100k classes showing competitive accuracy, MaF1, training time, and memory.  
- Positions method well within related work (one-vs-all, tree-based, label embedding, binary reductions) with appropriate citations.  
- Strength: addresses an important real-world problem with a novel, well-motivated sampling combination.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis despite sampling-induced dependencies.  
- Strength: convincing large-scale experiments and useful visualizations.  
- Strength: clear problem setup, notation, and algorithm (π, κ-DS) description.  
- Weakness: joint feature mapping φ is under-explained; unclear rationale for the chosen 10 features.  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed hyperparameter tuning (values for π, κ, and tuning ranges for baselines).  
- Weakness: practicality of candidate selection (parameter q) and its trade-off with accuracy/time not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: theoretical bounds are complex and lack an intuitive, practical interpretation.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art extreme classification methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand feature mapping details, hyperparameter/tuning disclosure, analysis of candidate selection, and intuitive explanation of the bounds.
