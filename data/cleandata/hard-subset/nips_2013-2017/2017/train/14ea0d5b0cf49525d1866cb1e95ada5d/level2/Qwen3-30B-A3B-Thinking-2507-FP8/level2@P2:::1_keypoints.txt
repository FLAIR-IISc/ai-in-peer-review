- Proposes double sampling ((π,κ)-DS) to convert multi-class to binary via dyadic representations and balance classes by over/undersampling.  
- Novelty: (π,κ)-DS reduces constructed dyadic examples while correcting class imbalance.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides consistency of ERM under sampling using fractional Rademacher complexity (Theorems 1 & 2).  
- Empirical contribution: Experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia (up to 100k classes) show faster training, lower memory, and competitive accuracy.  
- Strength: Tackles an important, practical large-scale classification problem.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical analysis extending prior fractional Rademacher complexity results.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental comparison to many state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strength: Demonstrated clear practical benefits vs. OVA and M-SVM in speed and memory.  
- Weakness: Theoretical bounds include sampling-dependent terms (α, β) but the practical impact of these terms is not quantified.  
- Weakness: Excludes SLEEC and LEML without providing experimental evidence for their inferiority.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup omits important details (parameter tuning, whether baselines used memory optimizations like hashing).  
- Weakness: Lacks a baseline comparing class-balanced sampling without dyadic transformation to isolate the dyadic method’s benefit.  
- Recommendation: Quantify how (π,κ) affect generalization (relate α, β to empirical performance).  
- Recommendation: Include or provide evidence for comparisons with SLEEC and LEML.  
- Recommendation: Clarify experimental details, especially parameter tuning and optimization parity across methods.  
- Recommendation: Add a class-balanced sampling (no dyadic transform) baseline.  
- Overall: Well-written, theoretically grounded, and empirically strong paper; recommend acceptance.
