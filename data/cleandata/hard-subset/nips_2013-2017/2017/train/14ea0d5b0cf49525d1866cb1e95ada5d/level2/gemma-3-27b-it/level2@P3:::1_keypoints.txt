- Proposes a double-sampling strategy for reducing multi-class to binary classification to mitigate high dimensionality and class imbalance.  
- Provides generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains in training/prediction time, memory, and predictive performance on DMOZ and Wikipedia (up to 100k classes).  
- Practically relevant and targets key bottlenecks in extreme multi-class classification.  
- Technically sound with a strong theoretical foundation.  
- Thorough experimental validation, including comparisons that act as an implicit ablation study.  
- Experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning are described in detail, aiding reproducibility.  
- Theoretical exposition uses complex concepts (graph covering, fractional Rademacher) that need clearer intuition for broader accessibility.  
- The connection between the derived theoretical bounds and observed empirical performance is not made explicit.  
- The feature mapping φ is insufficiently detailed; the adaptation from learning-to-rank should be explained concretely.  
- Improvements should be contextualized better, e.g., with confidence intervals or statistical significance tests.  
- Related work should cover more recent extreme-classification methods, especially hashing/quantization approaches.  
- No sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (π and κ); an analysis of their impact is recommended.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with high potential impact and minor revisions suggested; recommended for acceptance.
