- Proposes a novel double-sampling strategy on top of a multi-class-to-binary reduction, transforming multi-class into binary over pairs of examples.  
- Sampling strategy aims to handle long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of the approach.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and provides generalization error bounds showing ERM consistency on the sampled transformed set.  
- Experimental evaluation compares to state-of-the-art on popular datasets and shows competitive predictive performance, runtime, and memory, especially at large scale.  
- Strength: novel and efficient approach for many-class classification.  
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees (generalization bounds).  
- Strength: well-conducted empirical comparisons and results.  
- Weakness: assumes classes are mutually exclusive, which may not hold in real-world tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and possible extensions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address complexity analysis and discuss limitations/extensions.
