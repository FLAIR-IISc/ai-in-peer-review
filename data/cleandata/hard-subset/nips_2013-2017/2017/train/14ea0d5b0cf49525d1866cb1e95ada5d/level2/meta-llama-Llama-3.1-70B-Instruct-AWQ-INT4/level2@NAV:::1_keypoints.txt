- Proposes double sampling to reduce large-scale multi-class to binary, backed by a new local fractional Rademacher complexity bound.  
- Claims double sampling mitigates long-tailed class distributions and improves multi-class performance.  
- Claims the local fractional Rademacher complexity yields a tighter generalization bound for their method.  
- Claims superior predictive performance, training time, and memory use versus state-of-the-art on large text datasets.  
- Provides theoretical derivation of the new bound and extensive experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia.  
- Demonstrates scalability to problems with up to 100,000 classes.  
- Situates work within existing multi-class literature and explains limitations of prior methods.  
- Introduces a novel prediction algorithm with candidate selection to boost efficiency.  
- Supplies sufficient reproducibility details including pseudo-code and experimental setup.  
- Acknowledges risks of over- and under-sampling and the trade-off between accuracy and computation.  
- Criticized for lacking comprehensive comparisons with methods like SLEEC and LEML.  
- Could have tested applicability beyond text (e.g., image classification or recommendation).  
- Reviewers note strong theoretical and empirical contributions, making the paper a significant contribution.  
- Concerns remain about broader benchmarking and deeper discussion of practical limitations.
