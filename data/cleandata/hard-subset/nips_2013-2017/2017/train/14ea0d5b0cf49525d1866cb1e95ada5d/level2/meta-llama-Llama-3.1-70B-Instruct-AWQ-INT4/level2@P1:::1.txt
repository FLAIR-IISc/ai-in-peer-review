This paper addresses the problem of multi-class classification with a large number of classes, proposing a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class to binary reduction strategy. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their approach in terms of training and prediction time, memory consumption, and predictive performance on large-scale text classification datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, including the mathematical formulations and theoretical analysis. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle the problem of long-tailed class distributions, which is common in large-scale multi-class classification problems. The proposed double sampling strategy effectively addresses this issue by oversampling the most small size classes and subsampling the few big size classes.

The theoretical analysis of the paper is also a significant contribution. The authors derive new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity, which implies faster convergence rates. The analysis relies on the notion of graph covering and provides a deeper understanding of the behavior of the proposed method.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the authors compare their method with several state-of-the-art approaches on popular datasets. The results demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed approach in terms of training and prediction time, memory consumption, and predictive performance.

However, there are some minor issues that could be improved. For example, the authors could provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process, as this is an essential aspect of the proposed method. Additionally, the authors could discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of large-scale multi-class classification. The proposed double sampling strategy is efficient and effective, and the theoretical analysis provides a deeper understanding of the behavior of the method. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed approach.
