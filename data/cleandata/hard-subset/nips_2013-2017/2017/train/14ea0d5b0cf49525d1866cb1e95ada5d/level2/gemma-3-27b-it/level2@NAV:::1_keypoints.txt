- Problem: Tackles large-scale multi-class classification by improving multi-class-to-binary reduction efficiency.  
- Method: Proposes double sampling (balance class distribution π, then reduce dyadic examples κ) to address long-tailed distributions and computational cost.  
- Motivation: Well-articulated, directly targets bottlenecks of existing reduction approaches.  
- Theory: Provides generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity and shows ERM consistency despite sampling-induced dependencies.  
- Experiments: Validated on DMOZ and Wikipedia datasets with up to 100k classes.  
- Empirical results: (π, κ)-DS matches competitive accuracy while substantially reducing training/prediction time and memory versus OVA, M-SVM, and tree-based methods.  
- Presentation: Results are clearly presented with numerical data and visualizations.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Limited analysis of parameter sensitivity, especially effects of π and κ.  
- Weakness — features: Feature mapping φ seems simplistic and its limitations across text scenarios are not discussed.  
- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks in-depth empirical comparison with recent embedding-based methods beyond citations.  
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated approach with strong theoretical grounding and compelling large-scale empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns about limited hyperparameter analysis, simplistic feature mapping, and insufficient embedding-method comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness to different feature representations.
