- Addresses extreme multi-class classification with very large class sets and long-tailed label distributions.  
- Builds on multi-class-to-binary reduction by classifying dyadic example-class pairs.  
- Proposes a novel double-sampling scheme (π, κ)-DS to make the reduction scalable.  
- π-step rebalances training data to counteract long-tailed distributions.  
- κ-step samples a small subset of negative classes per example to reduce computation.  
- Advances practicality over prior binary-reduction methods and offers an alternative to tree-based and embedding approaches.  
- Provides theoretical support: generalization error bounds (Theorems 1 and 2) justifying consistency under sampling.  
- Extensive experiments on large datasets (up to 100k classes) showing competitive Accuracy and Macro F1.  
- Demonstrates dramatic improvements in training time and memory efficiency.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and easy to follow.  
- Minor weaknesses: limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for κ and q.  
- Minor weaknesses: dyadic feature mapping φ is hand-crafted for text; adaptation to other domains (e.g., images) is not discussed.  
- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated, thoroughly validated, high impact; strong recommendation for acceptance.
