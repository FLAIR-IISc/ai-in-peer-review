- Proposes transforming multi-class classification into binary classification over example pairs with a novel double sampling strategy to handle long-tailed distributions.  
- Double sampling reduces the number of example pairs and mitigates class imbalance in expanded data.  
- Theoretical contributions include well-established generalization bounds that justify the approach.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, tested on several large-scale text classification datasets and compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Paper is technically sound, with thorough analysis and consistent, clear presentation (notation, figures, tables).  
- The approach is novel and provides new theoretical insights into multi-class-to-binary transformation and sampling.  
- Significant practical implications: competitive predictive performance with improvements in memory usage and computational complexity.  
- Pros: novel method, innovative double sampling, strong theoretical analysis, comprehensive experiments.  
- Cons: assumes a specific loss function that may limit applicability across tasks.  
- Cons: requires careful hyperparameter tuning, which can be time-consuming for very large datasets.  
- Cons: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for very large-scale settings.  
- Recommended revisions: add detailed computational complexity analysis, discuss limitations and future work, and provide additional experiments on other datasets/domains.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that, with revisions, could be a top-tier publication.
