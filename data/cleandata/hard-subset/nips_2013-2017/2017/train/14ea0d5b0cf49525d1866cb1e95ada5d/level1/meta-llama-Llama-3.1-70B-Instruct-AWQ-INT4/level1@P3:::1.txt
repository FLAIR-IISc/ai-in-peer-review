This paper proposes a novel approach to tackle the problem of multi-class classification in the presence of a large number of classes. The authors introduce a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class to binary reduction strategy, which transforms the original multi-class problem into a binary classification problem over pairs of examples. The aim of the sampling strategy is to overcome the curse of long-tailed class distributions exhibited in majority of large-scale multi-class classification problems and to reduce the number of pairs of examples in the expanded data.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The introduction provides a good overview of the problem and the existing approaches, highlighting the limitations of the current methods. The proposed approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a detailed description of their method, including the double sampling strategy and the binary reduction strategy.

The theoretical analysis of the proposed approach is thorough, and the authors provide new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity. The analysis is based on the notion of graph covering, and the authors derive a new generalization bound that implies second-order (i.e., variance) information inducing faster convergence rates.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed approach is extensive, and the authors compare their method with several state-of-the-art approaches on popular datasets. The results show that the proposed approach is highly competitive in terms of time, memory, and performance measures.
