- Proposes a novel double-sampling strategy on top of a multi-class-to-binary reduction to handle large-scale multi-class problems and reduce pair explosion.  
- Aims to overcome long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of example pairs in the expanded dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and a detailed description of the method (double sampling + binary reduction).  
- Provides new theoretical generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity and graph covering.  
- Theoretical results imply second-order (variance) information yielding faster convergence rates.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive and compares against several state-of-the-art methods on popular datasets.  
- Empirical results show the approach is competitive in time, memory, and performance.  
- Strength: novel approach tailored for many-class problems with thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments demonstrating competitive resource usage and accuracy.  
- Weakness: Assumes long-tailed class distributions, which may not hold in all applications.  
- Weakness: Lacks clear guidance on how to choose hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Does not provide a detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Originality: Double sampling on multi-class-to-binary reduction and the theoretical analysis are novel contributions.  
- Significance: Addresses an important, widely applicable problem (text, image, recommender systems) with potential practical impact.  
- Quality and clarity: Well-organized, clear notation, easy to follow, and rigorous in theory and experiments.  
- Decision suggestion: Accept, conditional on authors addressing weaknesses—add hyperparameter selection guidance and a detailed complexity analysis.
