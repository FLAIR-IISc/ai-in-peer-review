This paper proposes a novel method for reducing large-scale multi-class classification problems to binary classification. The approach, called aggressive double sampling, aims to overcome the curse of long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of pairs of examples in the expanded data. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method, including generalization error bounds and experimental results on several large-scale text classification datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its advantages. The introduction effectively sets the context for the problem and the proposed solution. The related work section is comprehensive, and the authors provide a detailed comparison of their approach with existing methods.

The technical content of the paper is sound, and the authors provide a rigorous analysis of their method. The generalization error bounds are derived using local fractional Rademacher complexity, which is a suitable tool for analyzing the proposed method. The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several large-scale text classification datasets.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The proposed method is novel and effective in reducing large-scale multi-class classification problems to binary classification.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method, including generalization error bounds.
* The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several large-scale text classification datasets.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in machine learning and text classification. While the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, some readers may find the technical content challenging to follow.
* The experimental results are limited to text classification datasets. It would be beneficial to see the results of the proposed method on other types of datasets, such as image or speech recognition.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its advantages. The technical content is sound, and the experimental results are impressive.
