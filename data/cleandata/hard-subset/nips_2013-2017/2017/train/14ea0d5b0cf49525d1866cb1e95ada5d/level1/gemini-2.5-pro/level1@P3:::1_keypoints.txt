- Addresses extreme multi-class classification by making multi-class-to-binary reduction practical via aggressive sampling.
- Proposes a doubly-sampled (π, κ)-DS: class-aware π-sampling (oversample rare, undersample frequent) then κ-sampling of a few adversarial negatives per example.
- Reduces binary training size from m(K-1) to κ|Sπ|, making training feasible for K up to 100k.
- Provides theoretical justification: generalization bounds (Theorem 2) using local Rademacher complexity and fractional graph covering to handle dyadic dependencies.
- Shows empirical competitiveness on large-scale text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) with up to 100k classes.
- Achieves strong Accuracy and Macro F1 while requiring significantly less time and memory than tree-based and optimization baselines.
- π-sampling effectively mitigates class imbalance, improving Macro F1 on long-tailed datasets.
- Method is simple, intuitive, well-motivated, and clearly presented.
- Relies on a heuristic 10-dimensional feature mapping φ; sensitivity or alternatives (learned φ) are not explored.
- Lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for κ and prediction-time candidate count q, limiting practical guidance.
- Theorem 2’s bound is complex and constants α, β depend on sampling vs true class distribution; more qualitative interpretation would help.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-supported contribution with minor empirical/theoretical clarifications needed.
- Final recommendation: Accept.
