- Addresses extreme multi-class classification (10k–100k classes) with a double sampling + dyadic binary reduction approach.  
- (π, κ)-double sampling oversamples small classes and subsamples large ones, then reduces to binary classification over example pairs.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: new generalization bounds (Theorems 1–2) using local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Analysis carefully handles class imbalance and inter-dependencies between dyadic examples.  
- Empirical validation on DMOZ and Wikipedia shows improved training time, memory usage, and predictive performance versus baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound with novel theoretical contribution and tighter convergence rates than prior fractional Rademacher work.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive and compares against relevant baselines (OVA, M-SVM, FastXML, PfastReXML, PD-Sparse).  
- Well-written and clearly organized with precise mathematical formulation and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Algorithm descriptions (including (π, κ)-DS) are clear and experiments are thoroughly described.  
- Novelty in aggressive sampling targeting long-tailed distributions and use of local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Distinguishes itself from tree-based and label-embedding methods and cites relevant prior work.  
- Significant practical impact: handles extreme class sizes efficiently without OVA intractability or tree error propagation.  
- Theoretical guarantees strengthen the practical contribution, offering a principled foundation.  
- Strength: effectively addresses long-tailed distributions with a novel sampling strategy.  
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution with new generalization bounds.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on large-scale text datasets.  
- Strength: clear demonstration of computational advantages over state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: well-organized and accessible presentation.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of parameter sensitivity for π and κ.  
- Weakness: could compare more thoroughly to recent NIPS extreme classification work (e.g., FastXML).  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis lacks intuitive explanation of advantages from local fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Weakness: unclear applicability to non-text classification domains.  
- Pro: novel, practical solution to a significant large-scale classification problem with solid theory and empirical gains.  
- Con: needs better contextualization with recent related work, more intuition in theory, and expanded parameter sensitivity/experimental comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS 2017 — technically sound, practically significant, and well-validated.
