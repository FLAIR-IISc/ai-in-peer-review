- Problem: Tackles extreme large-scale multi-class classification for text with very many classes and long-tailed distributions.  
- Method: Proposes a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class-to-binary reduction to balance classes and cut pairwise examples.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides generalization bounds (Theorems 1 & 2) and claims ERM consistency under the sampling scheme.  
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia (≤100k classes) show improved efficiency, memory use, and competitive accuracy versus baselines.  
- Motivation and positioning: Well-motivated problem with clear relation to OVA, tree-based, label embedding, and binary reduction literature.  
- Novelty: Aggressive double sampling is a novel, natural extension addressing imbalance and computational cost.  
- Strength of theory: Theoretical analysis distinguishes the work from purely empirical studies.  
- Experimental strengths: Demonstrates practical benefits in training/prediction time, memory, and overall performance.  
- Presentation weakness: Mathematical derivations in the main text lack clarity and key steps are under-explained.  
- Specific theoretical clarity issue: Connection between fractional Rademacher complexity and the sampling strategy is not fully elucidated; Definition 1 feels abrupt.  
- Hyperparameter reporting: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (e.g., values and selection methodology for π and κ).  
- Ablation missing: Lacks thorough ablation studies on sampling parameters and their impact.  
- Feature justification: Choice of features is only loosely justified (inspired by learning-to-rank) and feels arbitrary for extreme text classification.  
- Comparative evaluation gap: Excludes some recent methods (SLEEC, LEML) without detailed discussion of their limitations relative to this work.  
- Metric trade-offs: Comparison emphasizes runtime/memory but needs a deeper analysis of trade-offs with predictive performance and scenarios of accuracy loss.  
- Theoretical caveat: Bounds include α and β tied to class proportions, suggesting residual sensitivity to class imbalance that needs discussion.  
- Recommendation: Overall positive—accept with minor revisions to clarify theory, detail hyperparameter tuning, add ablations, and expand comparisons.
