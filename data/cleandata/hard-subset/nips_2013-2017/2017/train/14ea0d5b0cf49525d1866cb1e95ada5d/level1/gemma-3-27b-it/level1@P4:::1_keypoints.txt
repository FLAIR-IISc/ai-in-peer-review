- Problem addressed: large-scale multi-class classification with long-tailed class distributions and computational constraints.  
- Proposed method: aggressive double sampling combined with a multi-class-to-binary reduction to handle imbalance and reduce cost.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides consistency results for empirical risk minimization (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Empirical evaluation: demonstrates effectiveness on DMOZ and Wikipedia, with strong runtime and memory improvements.  
- Motivation: core idea is well-motivated and correctly frames limitations of one-vs-all and label-embedding methods.  
- Sampling rationale: class balancing plus reduction of dyadic examples sensibly targets both imbalance and computational burden.  
- Notation/clarity issues: dense notation (e.g., π, κ) is often unexplained, forcing frequent back-references and hurting readability.  
- Exposition: mathematical flow is disrupted by technicalities and lacks sufficient intuition.  
- Feature mapping description: φ is described too briefly; construction and specific features (beyond similarity-based mentions) are unclear.  
- Related work: relevant papers are cited but connections and direct comparisons are insufficiently developed.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details: experimental setup lacks specifics on search space and optimization procedure for reproducibility.  
- Results presentation: tables lack error bars/statistical tests; Fig.3 labels/scales could be clearer to improve interpretability.  
- Generalization bounds: Theorem 2’s constants (α, β) make practical interpretation difficult; scaling with dataset size/number of classes is not discussed.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important problem, proposes a novel approach, provides theoretical justification, and shows practical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: significant clarity problems, insufficient detail on key components, weak related-work comparison, and limited experimental reproducibility/interpretability.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising but needs clearer exposition and additional details; reviewer leans toward weak accept pending revision.
