- Proposes a novel double-sampling reduction from multi-class to binary classification to handle long-tailed class distributions.
- Demonstrates improved training/prediction time, lower memory use, and better predictive performance on large-scale text datasets.
- Provides clear organization and generally clear writing, with solid theoretical foundations and empirical evaluation.
- Main contributions: the (π, κ)-double subsampling strategy and new generalization bounds via local fractional Rademacher complexity.
- Thorough related-work analysis highlighting limitations of tree- and embedding-based methods and advantages of the proposed approach.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to multiple state-of-the-art methods across datasets.
- Some algorithmic sections (e.g., introduction to (π, κ)-double subsampling) need more detailed explanations.
- Paper would benefit from additional visualizations (plots/diagrams) to illustrate concepts and results.
- Lacks broader discussion of potential applications beyond text classification.
- Originality is high due to the novel reduction approach and new theoretical bounds.
- Significance is high because the method addresses computational and class-imbalance challenges in large-scale multi-class problems.
- Overall quality is strong: well-written, well-organized, and empirically supported.
- Clarity is good overall but impeded by a few under-explained sections and limited visual aids.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to expand explanations, add visualizations, and discuss wider applications.
