- Proposes an aggressive double sampling ((π, κ)-DS) strategy for multi-class-to-binary reduction in extreme classification (10k–100k classes).  
- Targets two challenges: long-tailed class distributions and computational cost of dyadic example construction.  
- Balances class representation by oversampling small classes and subsampling large classes (π sampling).  
- Reduces dyadic example count by sampling κ adversarial classes per example (κ sampling).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees using local fractional Rademacher complexity, showing ERM consistency under the sampling scheme.  
- Experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia show significant gains in training/prediction time, memory, and predictive performance versus state-of-the-art.  
- Advances prior binary-reduction work by addressing computational limitations and scaling to very large label sets.  
- Theoretical analysis is well-structured and supported by Theorems 1 and 2 giving meaningful generalization bounds.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to multiple relevant baselines (OVA, M-SVM, FastXML, PfastReXML, PD-Sparse).  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, solid theory, convincing empirical results, practical candidate selection strategy for prediction.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (π and κ) and omission of comparisons to recent methods like SLEEC and LEML.  
- Additional weakness: theoretical bounds could use more intuitive explanations of the local Rademacher complexity results.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, practically valuable contribution that meets quality criteria and is recommended for acceptance.
