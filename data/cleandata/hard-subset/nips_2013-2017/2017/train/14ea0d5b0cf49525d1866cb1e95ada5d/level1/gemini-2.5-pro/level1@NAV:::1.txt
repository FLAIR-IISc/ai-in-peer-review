This paper addresses the challenge of extreme multi-class classification, where the number of classes is exceptionally large. The authors propose a novel method based on the multi-class to binary reduction framework, which recasts the problem as a binary classification task over pairs of examples and classes. The core contribution is a "double sampling" strategy, named (π, κ)-DS, designed to overcome the computational cost of this reduction and the long-tailed class distributions common in this domain. This strategy involves first re-balancing the dataset by sampling from classes based on their frequency (π-sampling), and then creating a manageable number of pairs for the binary problem by sampling a small subset of negative classes for each example (κ-sampling). This work extends prior research on binary reductions by introducing a practical and aggressive sampling scheme to make them viable at a massive scale.

**Strengths:**
The paper’s main strength lies in its significant practical impact, supported by rigorous experimentation. The proposed method achieves a state-of-the-art trade-off between predictive performance, training time, and memory usage. On benchmarks with up to 100,000 classes, (π, κ)-DS is shown to be dramatically more efficient than other high-performing methods like OVA, M-SVM, and PD-Sparse, while maintaining or exceeding their accuracy and Macro-F1 scores. The originality of the double sampling approach is notable, providing an elegant solution to two critical bottlenecks simultaneously. Furthermore, the work is of high technical quality, providing generalization error bounds (Theorems 1 and 2) that theoretically justify the consistency of learning on the aggressively sampled and distribution-shifted data.

**Weaknesses:**
A primary weakness is the reliance on a hand-crafted, heuristic feature mapping for the joint example/class representation, which is specifically designed for text classification. While the authors suggest similar mappings could be developed for other domains, the paper would be stronger if it explored this or discussed the challenges of generalizing the feature engineering aspect. The theoretical section, while sound, is dense and could benefit from more intuition to improve readability.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Addresses a highly significant and challenging problem in modern machine learning.
*   Presents a novel, well-motivated sampling method with compelling empirical results that advance the state-of-the-art.
*   Provides solid theoretical grounding for the proposed algorithm.
*   The method offers an excellent and practical compromise between accuracy, speed, and memory consumption.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   The feature representation is task-specific, limiting the demonstrated generalizability of the approach.
*   The contribution, while effective, can be viewed as an incremental improvement on an existing framework.

Overall, the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. This is a complete and high-quality contribution with clear practical and theoretical value.
