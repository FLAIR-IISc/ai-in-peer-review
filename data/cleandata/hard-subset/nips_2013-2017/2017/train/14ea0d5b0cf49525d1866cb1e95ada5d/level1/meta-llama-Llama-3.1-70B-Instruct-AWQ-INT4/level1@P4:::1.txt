**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Aggressive Sampling for Multi-class to Binary Reduction with Applications to Text Classification" proposes a novel approach to tackle large-scale multi-class classification problems. The authors introduce a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class to binary reduction strategy, which transforms the original multi-class problem into a binary classification problem over pairs of examples. The sampling strategy aims to overcome the curse of long-tailed class distributions and reduce the number of pairs of examples in the expanded data. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the consistency of the empirical risk minimization principle defined over the double sample reduction and demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed approach through experiments on large-scale text classification datasets.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on multi-class to binary reduction strategies, such as the one-vs-all approach (OVA) and tree-based methods. However, the authors argue that these approaches suffer from limitations, including computational intractability and class imbalance problems. The proposed approach is related to label embedding methods, but it does not rely on the low-rank assumption of the label-matrix, which is often transgressed in extreme multi-class classification settings.
