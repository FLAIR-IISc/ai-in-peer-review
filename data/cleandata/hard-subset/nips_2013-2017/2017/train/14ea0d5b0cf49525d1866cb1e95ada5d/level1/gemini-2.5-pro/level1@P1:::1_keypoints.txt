- Problem: Tackles extreme multi-class classification by reducing it to a binary task on example/class pairs.  
- Contribution: Proposes an "aggressive double sampling" (π, κ)-DS scheme to make the pairwise reduction scalable.  
- π-sampling: Re-samples training data to balance long-tailed class distributions by over/undersampling classes.  
- κ-sampling: For each example, samples only κ adversarial negative classes to avoid quadratic pair explosion.  
- Theory: Provides generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity showing consistency under double sampling.  
- Prediction: Uses centroid-based candidate selection for efficient test-time prediction.  
- Empirics: Strong evaluation on large benchmarks (DMOZ, Wikipedia) showing competitive accuracy, runtime, and memory versus state-of-the-art.  
- Significance: Addresses a highly relevant large-scale problem with a simple, practical, and effective method.  
- Experimental rigor: Thorough comparisons to diverse baselines and multiple evaluation metrics.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of class-balancing and limited negative sampling targeting both imbalance and complexity.  
- Theoretical soundness: Provides formal justification (Theorem 2) rather than relying solely on heuristics.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness — φ mapping: Method depends heavily on domain-specific joint feature mapping φ(x,y) with little guidance for other modalities.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Lacks sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters κ and prediction candidate count q.  
- Weakness — related work: Needs discussion connecting κ-sampling to broader negative sampling techniques (e.g., word2vec).  
- Pro: Novel, practical, theoretically justified method with strong large-scale results.  
- Con: Limited discussion on feature mapping generality and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — high-quality, impactful contribution with minor weaknesses.
