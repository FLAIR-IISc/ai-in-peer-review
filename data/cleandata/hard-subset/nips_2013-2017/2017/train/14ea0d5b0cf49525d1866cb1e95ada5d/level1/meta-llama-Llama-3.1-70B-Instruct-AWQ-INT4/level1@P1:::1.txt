This paper proposes a novel approach for reducing a multi-class classification problem to a binary classification problem, leveraging a double sampling strategy to overcome the curse of long-tailed class distributions. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their approach through experiments on large-scale text classification datasets, showcasing improvements in training and prediction time, memory consumption, and predictive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the theoretical foundations and empirical evaluations. The writing is generally clear, although some sections, such as the introduction to the (π, κ)-double subsampling algorithm, could benefit from more detailed explanations.

The main contributions of this paper are the proposal of a novel double sampling strategy for reducing the dimensionality of the dyadic output prediction problem and the derivation of new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity. These contributions are significant, as they address the challenges of large-scale multi-class classification problems, where existing methods often suffer from computational intractability and class imbalance issues.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the related work, discussing the limitations of existing approaches, such as tree-based and embedding-based methods, and highlighting the advantages of their proposed method. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with comparisons to multiple state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.
