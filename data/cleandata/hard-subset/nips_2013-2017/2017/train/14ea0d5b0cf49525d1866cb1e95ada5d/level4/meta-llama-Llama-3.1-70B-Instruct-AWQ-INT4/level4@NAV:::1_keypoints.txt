- Introduces a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification, building on prior work [16].
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.
- Experiments on text datasets show effective handling of many-class multiclass problems.
- Extensive comparisons with multiple baselines demonstrate high performance and reduced training time.
- Addresses an important extreme classification problem with practical applications.
- Novelty is limited due to heavy reliance on [16]; main contributions are double sampling and new local Rademacher complexity bounds.
- Prediction time remains relatively high compared to some alternative methods.
- The presented generalization bound needs clearer explanation and comparison to other bounds.
- Mixing batch and online algorithms in experiments complicates fair comparisons because of differing memory characteristics.
