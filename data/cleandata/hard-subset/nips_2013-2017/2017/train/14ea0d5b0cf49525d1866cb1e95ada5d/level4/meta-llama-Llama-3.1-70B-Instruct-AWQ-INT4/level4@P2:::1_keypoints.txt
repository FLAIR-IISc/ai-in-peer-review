- Proposes a double sampling strategy (building on [16]) with theoretical and empirical analysis for multiclass/extreme classification.  
- Experiments on text datasets show the method handles many classes and achieves high performance with shorter training time.  
- Authors provide comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods.  
- The work is a meaningful contribution to extreme classification applications.  
- Novelty is relatively thin since the paper is largely based on [16], offering mainly the double sampling and a new generalization bound.  
- The paper lacks deeper discussion of the broader implications and how the improvements advance the state-of-the-art.  
- Prediction time is still long compared with other approaches; the training vs. prediction-time trade-off needs fuller discussion.  
- The presented generalization bound is unclear and should be contextualized and compared to other bounds.  
- Differences in memory usage between batch and online learning algorithms used in experiments are not clarified.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10).  
- Suggested revisions: clarify and compare the generalization bound, discuss training vs. prediction trade-offs, clarify memory usage differences, and expand discussion of implications.
