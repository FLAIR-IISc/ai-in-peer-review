- Proposes a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification, with theoretical analysis (local Rademacher complexities) and empirical validation on text datasets showing high accuracy and reduced training time.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: compares multiple baselines across datasets and consistently shows strong performance with significantly reduced training time.  
- Addresses a timely and practically relevant problem: extreme classification for large-scale real-world scenarios.  
- Novelty is limited: framework heavily relies on [16], with contributions mainly refining an existing approach rather than introducing a new paradigm.  
- Prediction latency is substantially higher than alternatives, creating a concerning trade-off since inference speed often matters more in deployments.  
- Interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and needs direct comparison to prior bounds (e.g., [16] and other extreme classification methods).  
- Experimental setup mixes batch and online algorithms, causing inconsistent memory usage profiles and complicating fair performance comparisons.  
- Overall: a modest incremental contribution—useful training efficiency gains but limited novelty and unresolved prediction-time and methodological issues that weaken impact.
