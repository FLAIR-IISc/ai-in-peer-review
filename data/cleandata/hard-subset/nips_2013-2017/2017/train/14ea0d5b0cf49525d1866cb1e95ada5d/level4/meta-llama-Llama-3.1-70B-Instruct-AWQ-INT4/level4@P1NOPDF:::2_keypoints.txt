- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification tailored for large-number-of-classes settings.  
- Model maps input-class pairs via a feature transform phi into R^p and learns a scoring function; loss counts fraction of incorrect classes scoring above the true class.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) which creates K-1 transformed points per example, leading to correlated training examples.  
- Identifies that correlated transformed examples make many standard generalization bounds inapplicable.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis using bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for the correlated setting.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling techniques to rebalance class representation and reduce transformed dataset size (sampling classes with replacement and sampling a subset of classes per example).  
- Shows sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results demonstrate competitive performance against strong baselines.  
- Novelty primarily resides in the sampling/rebalancing approach; the basic reduction follows Joshi et al.  
- Missing: theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3 that compares only the k nearest class centroids.  
- Missing: additional discussion comparing the sample complexity bounds to generalization bounds for non-transformed multi-class methods.
