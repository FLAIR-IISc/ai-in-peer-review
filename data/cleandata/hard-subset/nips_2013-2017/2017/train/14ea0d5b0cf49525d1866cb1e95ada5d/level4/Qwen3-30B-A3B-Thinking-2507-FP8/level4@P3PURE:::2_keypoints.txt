- Introduces a reduction from large-scale multiclass to binary classification using a hypothesis that scores (input, class) pairs and a loss counting incorrect classes ranked above the true class.  
- Proposes a feature transform φ mapping (input, class) to a p-dimensional space and learns a mapping from R^p to scores.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) by generating comparisons between the true label and other labels, which creates correlated transformed examples.  
- Identifies that correlated examples invalidate standard generalization bounds and addresses this theoretically.  
- Main theoretical contribution: tighter generalization analysis using fractional Rademacher complexity bounds for the correlated setting.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling procedures to rebalance class distributions and reduce the number of transformed comparisons (sampling classes with replacement; random sampling of comparisons).  
- Proves the proposed sampling techniques do not significantly degrade generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance against strong state-of-the-art baselines and a robust evaluation.  
- Novelty is primarily in the sampling strategy and the adapted generalization analysis; core reduction is an extension of prior work (Joshi et al.).  
- Critical gaps: no theoretical analysis of the prediction heuristic in Section 2.3 that restricts comparisons to the k nearest class centroids—needs rigorous justification or generalization impact analysis.  
- Suggests stronger theoretical context by comparing the sample complexity bounds to generalization bounds for non-transformed multiclass methods.
