This paper introduces a double sampling scheme to enhance the multiclass-to-binary reduction approach of [16]. The authors provide both theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation of the proposed method. Experiments on text datasets indicate that the approach scales to settings with a very large number of classes.

Pros:
- The experimental evaluation is broad, comparing against multiple strong baselines. The results suggest that the method attains competitive or superior performance with reduced training time on several datasets.
- Extreme classification is a timely and practically important problem with many real-world applications.

Cons:
- The work builds closely on [16]. Although it adds two main contributions—(1) a double sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the incremental novelty appears limited.
- While training is accelerated, prediction remains relatively slow compared to some alternatives; in many applications, inference time may be the more critical bottleneck.

Comments:
- The interpretation of the presented generalization bound is unclear. How does the bound compare (in rates, constants, or assumptions) to those of competing approaches?
- The experimental baselines mix batch and online methods, which can lead to substantial differences in memory footprints; a clearer normalization or discussion of this disparity would be helpful.
