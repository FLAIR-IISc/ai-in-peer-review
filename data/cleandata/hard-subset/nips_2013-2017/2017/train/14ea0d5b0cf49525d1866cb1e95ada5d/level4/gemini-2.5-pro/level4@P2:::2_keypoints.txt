- Addresses extreme multi-class classification via dyadic reduction to binary comparisons with a ranking loss, building on Joshi et al. (2015).
- Proposes aggressive double-sampling ((π, κ)-DS) to rebalance long-tailed class distributions and sample negatives, reducing transformed dataset size.
- Develops new theoretical analysis using local fractional Rademacher complexities to handle dependencies from dyadic pairs and show ERM consistency.
- Empirically competitive on large-scale text benchmarks (up to 100k classes), improving training time and memory while maintaining strong predictive performance.
- Practical significance: achieves a favorable accuracy vs. training-time/memory trade-off, enabling tasks infeasible for OVA or M-SVM.
- Theoretical contribution: provides generalization bounds for the proposed sampling scheme under dependent data (Theorem 2).
- Problem relevance: tackles an important, scalable problem with applications in tagging, recommendation, and document categorization.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of the prediction-time candidate selection heuristic (restricting to q nearest-centroid classes).
- Suggestion: analyze or discuss how the choice of q affects approximation error and its relation to the feature mapping φ.
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s bounds are presented without comparison to standard multi-class bounds, leaving sample-complexity trade-offs unclear.
- Originality: double-sampling scheme and the dependent-data theoretical analysis are non-trivial, novel extensions of prior work.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, with a clear algorithmic description, consistent notation, and thorough experimental setup.
