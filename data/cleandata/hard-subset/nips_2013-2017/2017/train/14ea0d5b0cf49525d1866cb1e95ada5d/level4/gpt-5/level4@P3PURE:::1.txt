Summary:
This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses. Experiments on text datasets show that the method can handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. The results indicate that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training time on several datasets.
- The extreme classification problem is an important research direction and has many practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper is largely based on [16]. Although there are two main improvements—(1) the proposed double sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the overall novelty is relatively limited.
- While the proposed approach shortens training time, it still requires long prediction time compared with other approaches. Arguably, prediction time is more important in practice.

Comments and questions:
- It is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper. How does this generalization bound compare with those of other approaches?
- Some methods evaluated in the experiments are batch learning algorithms, while others are online learning algorithms. Consequently, their memory usage characteristics differ substantially. Please clarify how memory usage was handled or normalized to ensure fair comparisons.
