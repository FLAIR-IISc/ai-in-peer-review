- Introduces a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification and a novel generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities, building heavily on [16].  
- Provides theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation on text datasets showing effectiveness for problems with many classes.  
- Strong experimental comparison against several baselines, showing good accuracy and reduced training time across multiple datasets.  
- Addresses an important, practically relevant problem area (extreme classification).  
- Novelty is limited because the work relies substantially on the foundations of [16] despite the two proposed improvements.  
- Method reduces training time but incurs longer prediction (inference) times, which is a practical drawback.  
- Practical interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear; a comparison to existing bounds is needed.  
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms, so differing memory usage characteristics are not fully addressed.
