- Reduces extreme multi-class classification to binary scoring of (input, class) pairs via dyadic comparisons between true class and all others.  
- Uses feature map φ and scoring function f to rank classes by score; loss measures fraction of incorrect classes outranking the true class.  
- Dyadic comparisons are dependent, invalidating classical generalization arguments.  
- First contribution: sharper generalization analysis than Joshi et al., using fractional/local Rademacher complexity to handle dependencies.  
- Second contribution: an aggressive double sampling scheme—sample classes with replacement to rebalance skew and randomly subsample negatives to shrink the expanded training set.  
- The sampling steps are shown to preserve consistency and yield comparable theoretical bounds.  
- Empirically competitive on large DMOZ and Wikipedia datasets while improving training/prediction time and memory.  
- Main novelty is the double sampling scheme; the dyadic reduction itself builds on Joshi et al. (2015).  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the candidate-selection prediction (scoring only q nearest-centroid classes) and its effect on consistency/error.  
- Suggestion: include more discussion comparing sample complexity bounds with those for direct (non-reduced) multi-class formulations.
