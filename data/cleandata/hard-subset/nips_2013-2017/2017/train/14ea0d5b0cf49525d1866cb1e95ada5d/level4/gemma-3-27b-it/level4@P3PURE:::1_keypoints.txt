- Paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification method of [16], supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Empirical results on text datasets show the method handles many-class problems effectively.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show high performance and reduced training times across datasets.  
- Extreme classification is a practically important research area motivating the work.  
- The method heavily builds on [16]; overall novelty is limited despite two innovations (double sampling and a new local Rademacher complexity bound).  
- Prediction times are longer than competing methods, which is a critical drawback for practical use.  
- The interpretation and contextualization of the presented generalization bound are unclear and need comparison to existing bounds.  
- Mixing batch and online algorithms in experimental comparisons may yield unfair memory-usage assessments and should be addressed.
