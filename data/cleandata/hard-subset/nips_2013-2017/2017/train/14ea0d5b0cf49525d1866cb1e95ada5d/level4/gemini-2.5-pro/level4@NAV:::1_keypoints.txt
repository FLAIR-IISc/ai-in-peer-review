- Proposal: introduces a double sampling strategy extending the multiclass framework of [16], backed by theoretical analysis and experiments on text extreme-classification tasks.  
- Experiments: extensive comparisons against many baselines show strong predictive performance and reduced training time across datasets.  
- Motivation: focus on extreme classification is well-motivated given its practical importance.  
- Novelty concern: heavy dependence on the framework of [16] makes the contribution feel somewhat incremental despite two new elements (double sampling and a local Rademacher-complexity bound).  
- Prediction latency: prediction time remains high compared to competing methods, which may be more critical than training time in practice.  
- Generalization bound: the practical interpretation of the new bound is unclear and needs comparison to bounds for other relevant approaches.  
- Experimental protocol: mixing batch and online learning algorithms complicates fair comparison because of differing memory-consumption profiles.
