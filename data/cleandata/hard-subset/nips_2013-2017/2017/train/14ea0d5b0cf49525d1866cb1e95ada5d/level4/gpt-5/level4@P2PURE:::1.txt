This paper proposes a double-sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification method in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses. Experiments on text datasets indicate that the proposed approach can handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on several datasets.
- The extreme classification problem is an important research direction and has many practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper relies heavily on [16]. Although there are two main improvements—(1) the double-sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the incremental novelty is limited.
- Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires long prediction time compared with other approaches. Arguably, prediction time is more important in practice.

Comments:
- It is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper. How does the generalization bound compare with those of other approaches?
- Some methods presented in the experiments are batch learning algorithms while others are online learning algorithms. Consequently, their memory usage differs substantially.
