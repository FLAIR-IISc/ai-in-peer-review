- Proposes a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification tailored for many classes, using a feature transform phi mapping input-class pairs to R^p.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) which creates K-1 transformed points per example, leading to correlated training examples.  
- First main contribution: improved generalization analysis via new bounds on the fractional Rademacher complexity for correlated transformed data.  
- Second main contribution: sampling techniques to rebalance class distributions and reduce the size of the transformed dataset.  
- Two sampling procedures: sampling classes with replacement for equal representation, and subsampling a random subset of classes instead of all K-1 comparisons.  
- Shows sampling procedures do not significantly worsen the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results are competitive with several strong baselines.  
- Main novelty is the sampling-based rebalancing and dataset reduction; the basic reduction originates from Joshi et al.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3, which compares only the k nearest class centroids.  
- Requests deeper discussion and comparison of sample complexity bounds relative to non-transformed multi-class generalization bounds.  
- Overall: solid theoretical and empirical contributions with potential impact, but needs additional analysis/discussion to strengthen the work.
