- Tackles extreme multi-class classification by proposing a double sampling strategy on top of a multi-class-to-binary reduction to handle many classes and class imbalance.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees via new generalization bounds accounting for sampling and pair dependencies.  
- Presents strong empirical results on DMOZ and Wikipedia, showing improved accuracy/F1 and notable speed and memory gains.  
- Focuses on practical relevance by addressing both predictive performance and resource consumption for large-scale applications.  
- Paper is clearly written and the algorithm is explained with sufficient detail.  
- Builds on prior binary-reduction framework [16], so the novelty feels somewhat incremental.  
- The practical interpretation and impact of Theorem 2 (and parameters α, β) are not clearly explained or compared to existing bounds.  
- Prediction time can be longer than some alternatives, and discussion of latency trade-offs and mitigation (e.g., candidate selection tuning) is cursory.  
- Comparisons of memory usage may be misleading without accounting for differences between batch and online methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, strengthening the empirical claims.  
- Theoretical analysis adds rigor and supports consistency of ERM under the sampling scheme.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, shows practical efficiency gains without accuracy loss, and supplies solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Arguments for rejection: incremental contribution, unclear practical usefulness of the bound, and insufficient treatment of prediction-time trade-offs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the generalization bound interpretation and to better discuss prediction-time trade-offs.
