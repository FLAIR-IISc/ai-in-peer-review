- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification using (input,class) feature mapping and a loss counting fraction of incorrect classes ranked above the true class.  
- Improves generalization analysis over Joshi et al. (2015) via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for correlated transformed data.  
- Introduces sampling procedures (per-class sampling and sampling of comparison classes) to rebalance class representation and reduce transformed dataset size.  
- Proves the sampling procedures do not significantly worsen the generalization bounds.  
- Empirically, the proposed method performs competitively against strong baselines.  
- Strength: Interesting reduction and appropriate generalization bounds for correlated reduced datasets.  
- Strength: Strong empirical comparisons to existing algorithms.  
- Strength: Thorough analysis of the sampling technique’s impact on generalization bounds.  
- Weakness: Core reduction largely follows Joshi et al.; main novelty is the sampling-based rebalancing/size reduction.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm that limits comparisons to k nearest centroids (section 2.3).  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion comparing sample complexity bounds to non-transformed multi-class generalization bounds.  
- Recommendation: Accept—novel and interesting reduction with solid analysis and strong empirical results.  
- Minor suggestion: Add theoretical analysis of the section 2.3 prediction algorithm.  
- Minor suggestion: Provide more discussion comparing sample complexity bounds to non-transformed results.  
- Minor suggestion: Include more experimental details (hyperparameter tuning, computational resources).
