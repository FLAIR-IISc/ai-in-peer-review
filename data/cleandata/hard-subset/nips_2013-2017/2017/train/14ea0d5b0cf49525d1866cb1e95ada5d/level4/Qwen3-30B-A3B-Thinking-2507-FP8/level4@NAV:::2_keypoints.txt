- Introduces a reduction from large-scale multi-class to binary classification via scoring functions on (input, class) pairs.  
- Proposes a feature transform φ mapping (input, class) to ℝ^p and a learned map from ℝ^p to scores.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) pairwise-comparison reduction, which creates K−1 comparisons per example and yields correlated transformed training data.  
- Notes that correlated transformed data invalidates standard generalization bounds.  
- Contribution 1: new fractional Rademacher complexity bounds to analyze generalization under correlated transformed data.  
- Contribution 2: sampling techniques to rebalance transformed-class distributions and reduce dataset size: (i) sampling classes with replacement for equal representation, (ii) using random subsets of classes instead of all K−1 comparisons.  
- Proves the sampling procedures negligibly affect generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical results show the method competes strongly with established baselines.  
- Main novelty is the sampling-based rebalancing/size-reduction and the improved generalization analysis; core reduction largely adapts prior work.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3 (restricting prediction to k nearest class centroids).  
- Lacks deeper discussion/comparison of sample complexity bounds versus non-transformed multi-class generalization results.
