- Proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach from [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.  
- Experiments on a text dataset show the method can handle multiclass problems with many classes.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental comparisons demonstrating high performance and reduced training times on several datasets.  
- Strength: addresses the important and practical problem of extreme classification.  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on [16]; main novelties are the double sampling and a new local Rademacher-based generalization bound, so overall novelty is limited.  
- Limitation: prediction time remains longer than other methods, which is critical in practical use.  
- Suggestion: the generalization bound is not clearly explained; compare it to bounds from other approaches to clarify its significance.  
- Suggestion: experiments mix batch and online algorithms with very different memory requirements; discuss these differences and their practical implications.
