- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification where hypotheses score input-class pairs and loss measures fraction of incorrect classes scored above the true class.  
- Uses a feature transform phi mapping input-class pairs to R^p and learns a mapping from R^p to scores.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015)’s reduction that creates K-1 transformed points per example (pairwise comparisons between true and incorrect labels).  
- The transformed dataset is correlated, making many standard generalization bounds inapplicable.  
- Primary theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for this correlated setting.  
- Secondary contribution: sampling techniques (class sampling with replacement and random subsampling of comparisons) to rebalance class distributions and reduce transformed dataset size.  
- Show that sampling yields roughly equal class representation and has negligible impact on the generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results demonstrate competitive performance against several strong baselines.  
- Novelty is mainly the sampling-based rebalancing and downsizing; the core reduction follows prior work (Joshi et al.).  
- Omission: no theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3, which only compares the k classes whose centroids are nearest to the input.  
- Omission: limited discussion comparing the derived sample complexity/generalization bounds to those for non-transformed multi-class problems.
