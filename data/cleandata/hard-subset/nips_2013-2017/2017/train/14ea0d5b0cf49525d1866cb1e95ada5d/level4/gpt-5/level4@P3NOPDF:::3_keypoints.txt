- Proposal: Aggressive Double Sampling (ADS) reduces extreme multiclass (~100k labels) to dyadic binary classification with a centroid-based candidate-generation heuristic for inference and theoretical consistency guarantees.
- Strength: Clear, principled reduction and sampling strategy that explicitly addresses long-tail label distributions.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis handling dependence among dyadic examples and distribution shift from oversampling using graph decompositions and Janson’s inequalities.
- Strength: Finite-sample risk bounds showing oversampling-induced bias shrinks linearly with training size and consistency to multiclass ERM.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on large text benchmarks—higher accuracy/F1 above ~30k classes, faster training, and much lower memory than alternatives (prediction slower).
- Method detail: ADS samples per-class inversely to empirical frequency and draws a fixed number of uniformly sampled “adversarial” negatives to form dyadic training examples.
- Inference detail: Uses class centroids to propose a candidate label pool per instance to avoid enumerating all class–instance pairs.
- Analysis detail: Dependency graph is partitioned to obtain sums of independent terms for concentration bounds; oversampling bias enters via class probability ratios.
- Weakness: Heuristic candidate generation—sensitivity to centroid choice and candidate pool size; single centroid may fail for multi-modal classes.
- Weakness: Prediction is slower than competitors; need exploration of accelerated candidate selection (ANN, multi-centroid) and reporting of end-to-end latency distributions.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity underexplored—need guidance on tuning, ablation studies (inverse-frequency sampling vs negatives), and robustness across datasets.
- Weakness: Theoretical bounds lack intuitive instantiated corollaries—recommend examples under simple class-frequency models and clarification of constants/magnitudes.
- Weakness: Insufficient accounting of training complexity—report dyadic example counts, feature-construction cost, per-epoch runtime, and scaling with negatives and label cardinality.
- Weakness: Ambiguity about negative sampling protocol—clarify per-instance vs global sampling and with/without replacement.
- Weakness: Empirics focused on very large-K; include smaller label-space results and micro vs macro metric breakdowns to locate crossover points and long-tail behavior.
- Weakness: Unclear how well ADS composes with deep feature extractors and whether that would worsen prediction-time costs.
- Clarity: Algorithmic description and test-time pipeline need concise pseudocode and a complexity summary.
- Clarity: Dependency graph partitioning and the role of Janson’s inequalities are dense—add a figure illustrating construction and argument flow.
- Clarity: Precisely state how candidate pool sizes are chosen and present the trade-off curve between candidate count, accuracy, and speed.
- Overall assessment: Strong, impactful paper with practical and theoretical contributions; primary concerns are prediction-time efficiency, reliance on a heuristic candidate generator, and limited hyperparameter/complexity analyses—addressing these would strengthen the work.
