- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification suited for very large K by learning score functions on (input, class) feature pairs.  
- Assumes a feature transform φ mapping (input, class) to R^p and measures loss by fraction of incorrect classes scoring above the true class.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) but addresses the correlated transformed examples that prevent standard generalization bounds.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for the correlated setting.  
- Introduces sampling strategies: resampling classes with replacement for balance and randomly subsampling the K−1 comparisons to shrink the transformed dataset.  
- Proves the sampling strategies do not significantly degrade the derived generalization guarantees.  
- Empirically competitive against strong baselines on large-class problems.  
- Novelty primarily in the sampling/rebalancing techniques; the core reduction follows prior work.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction approximation (restricting comparisons to k nearest centroids) and its effect on consistency and bias.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison of sample complexity bounds versus the original non-transformed multi-class problem, especially for extreme K regimes.
