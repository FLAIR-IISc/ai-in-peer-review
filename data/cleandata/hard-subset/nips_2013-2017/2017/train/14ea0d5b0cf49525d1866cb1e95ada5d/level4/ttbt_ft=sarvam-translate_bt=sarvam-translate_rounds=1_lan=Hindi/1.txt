This paper [16] presents a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their proposed approach. Experiments conducted on text datasets show that the proposed approach can handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors have conducted extensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. The results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with short training times on many datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important direction in research and has many applications in practice.

Cons:
- This paper is mostly based on [16]. Despite two main improvements: 1) a double sampling strategy was proposed, and 2) a new generalization threshold based on local Rademaker complexities, the novelty is relatively low.
- Despite reducing training time, the proposed approach still requires a long prediction time compared to other approaches. It is reasonable that prediction time is more important in practice.

Notes:
- It is not clear how to interact with the generalization threshold presented in the paper. How is the generation threshold compared to other approaches?
- Some of the methods presented in the experiments are batch learning algorithms, and some are online learning algorithms. Therefore, memory usage is very different.
