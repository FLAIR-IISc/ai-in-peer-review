- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary by learning a scoring function over (input, class) pairs via a p-dimensional feature transform and a linear model with a ranking loss.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) by creating K-1 pairwise comparisons per example, which induces highly correlated transformed training examples.  
- Main theoretical contribution: new generalization analysis using fractional Rademacher complexity that accounts for correlations and improves prior bounds.  
- Practical contribution: two sampling schemes — class rebalancing via sampling with replacement and subsampling incorrect classes per example — that make the reduction scalable while preserving bounds.  
- Empirical claim: thorough experiments show the method is competitive with or outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on large-scale classification.  
- Strength: addresses large-class scalability with practical, effective sampling techniques.  
- Strength: provides novel theoretical treatment of correlated reduced examples.  
- Strength: strong, convincing experimental validation.  
- Strength: clear exposition and well-motivated connection to prior work.  
- Weakness: missing formal analysis of the approximate prediction algorithm that restricts comparisons to k nearest class centroids and its effect on consistency/accuracy.  
- Weakness: needs clearer comparison of the derived sample complexity bounds to standard multi-class bounds to contextualize the theoretical trade-offs.
