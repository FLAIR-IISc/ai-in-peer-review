- Proposes a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification tailored for large numbers of classes, using a hypothesis that scores input-class pairs.  
- Uses a feature transform φ mapping input-class pairs to R^p and learns a mapping from R^p to scores.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) which creates K-1 transformed points per example, leading to correlated transformed datasets.  
- Standard generalization bounds fail due to correlations; paper provides improved generalization analysis.  
- Introduces new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity to handle the correlated transformed data, extending Joshi et al.  
- Proposes sampling techniques (class rebalancing via sampling with replacement and random sampling of comparison classes) to reduce and balance the transformed training set.  
- Shows sampling procedures do not significantly degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results are competitive against strong baselines, demonstrating efficacy of the reduction.  
- Omits a theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Sec. 2.3, which compares only the k nearest class centroids.  
- Requests more thorough discussion of sample complexity bounds and comparison to generalization bounds for non-transformed multi-class problems.  
- Main novelty highlighted as the sampling-based rebalancing and reduction of the transformed dataset size.
