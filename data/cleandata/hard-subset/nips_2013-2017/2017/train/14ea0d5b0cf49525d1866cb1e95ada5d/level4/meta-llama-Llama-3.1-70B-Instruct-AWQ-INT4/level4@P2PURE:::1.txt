This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach presented in [16], providing both theoretical and empirical analyses of the new method. Experiments conducted on a text dataset demonstrate the proposed approach's ability to handle multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors perform comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods, revealing that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on multiple datasets.
- The extreme classification problem is a significant research direction with numerous practical applications.

Cons:
- Although the paper builds upon [16] and introduces two key improvements – a double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher Complexities – the novelty of the work is relatively limited.
- Despite the proposed approach reducing training time, it still requires longer prediction times compared to other methods. In practice, prediction time is arguably more important.

Comments:
- The interpretation of the generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear. It would be beneficial to compare this bound with those of other approaches to assess its significance.
- The experimental comparison involves both batch and online learning algorithms, which have substantially different memory usage. This distinction should be taken into account when evaluating the results.
