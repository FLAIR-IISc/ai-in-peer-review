- Proposes a novel multi-class classification method using multi-class-to-binary reduction over example pairs plus a double sampling strategy.  
- Aims to mitigate long-tailed class distributions common in large-scale multi-class problems.  
- Transforms the problem into binary classification on dyadic pairs and uses double sampling to balance classes and reduce pair count.  
- Presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis including a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities.  
- Provides extensive experiments on datasets (including DMOZ and Wikipedia) comparing to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Shows high performance and shorter training time on several datasets.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness in time, memory, and performance for large-scale collections.  
- Limitation: methodological novelty is relatively thin and builds upon prior work ([16]).  
- Limitation: prediction/inference time remains relatively long compared to other methods.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, but some theoretical sections may be hard for non-experts.  
- Suggestion: add more explanations or examples to make theoretical parts more accessible.  
- Suggestion: provide a clearer comparison of the proposed generalization bound with existing bounds.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions; rating 8/10, reviewer confidence 8/10.
