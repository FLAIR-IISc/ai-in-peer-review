

This paper introduces a double sampling strategy to enhance multiclass classification in [16], supported by theoretical analysis and empirical validation. Experiments on text datasets demonstrate the approach's efficacy in handling large-scale multiclass problems.  

Pros:  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons against multiple baselines show the proposed method achieves superior performance with reduced training time across several datasets.  
- Extreme classification represents a significant research direction with substantial practical applications.  

Cons:  
- The work relies heavily on [16], and while two key contributions are introduced—(1) the double sampling strategy and (2) generalization bounds derived via local Rademacher complexities—the novelty remains somewhat limited.  
- Although training time is reduced, prediction time remains substantially longer than competing methods; prediction efficiency is often more critical for real-world deployment.  

Comments:  
- The interpretation and comparative significance of the proposed generalization bound relative to existing bounds are not clearly articulated.  
- The experimental comparison mixes batch and online learning algorithms, leading to inconsistent memory usage patterns that require explicit discussion.
