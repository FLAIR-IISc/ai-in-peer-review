- Paper proposes a double-sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass method in [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses showing scalability to many classes.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show the proposed method achieves high accuracy and shorter training times on several datasets.  
- Addresses extreme classification, an important and practical research direction.  
- Heavy reliance on [16]; novelty is limited despite the double-sampling strategy and new local Rademacher generalization bounds.  
- Prediction time remains long compared with other approaches, which is a practical drawback despite faster training.  
- The interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and it’s not compared to bounds from other methods.  
- Experiments mix batch and online algorithms, causing substantial and unaddressed differences in memory usage.
