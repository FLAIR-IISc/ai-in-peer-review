- Proposes a double sampling strategy to improve dyadic multi-class classification for extreme label spaces.
- Introduces a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities, but its practical significance is unclear.
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show competitive predictive performance and shorter training times on text datasets.
- Prediction (inference) time is longer than some baselines, which may hinder use in latency-sensitive settings.
- Heavily builds on prior work [16], so the novelty appears incremental.
- Lacks direct comparison of the new generalization bound to existing bounds and actionable guidance for practitioners.
- Mixing batch and online algorithms in experiments makes memory-usage comparisons difficult and potentially misleading.
- Experiments are well-presented, but the paper should report peak or per-example memory and provide a more nuanced prediction-time analysis.
- Overall assessment: solid but incremental contribution; reviewer leans weak accept if theoretical clarity and runtime/memory concerns are addressed.
