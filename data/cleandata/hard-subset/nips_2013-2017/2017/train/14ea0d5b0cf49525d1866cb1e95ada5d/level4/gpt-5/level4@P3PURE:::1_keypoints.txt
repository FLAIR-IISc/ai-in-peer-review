- Proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification method in [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.
- Experiments on text datasets show the method scales to problems with a large number of classes.
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons against several baselines; reports high performance and shorter training time on several datasets.
- Highlights extreme classification as an important and practical research direction.
- Paper is largely based on [16]; only two main contributions (double sampling and new local Rademacher-based bounds), so overall novelty is limited.
- Although training is faster, prediction time remains long compared to other approaches, which is a practical concern.
- Unclear how to interpret the presented generalization bound and how it compares to bounds from other methods.
- Experimental comparison mixes batch and online algorithms; need clarification on how memory usage was handled or normalized for fair comparison.
