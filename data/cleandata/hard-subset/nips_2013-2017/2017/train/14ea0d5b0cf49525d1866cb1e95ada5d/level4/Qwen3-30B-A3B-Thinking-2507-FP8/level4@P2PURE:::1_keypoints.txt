- Paper proposes a double sampling strategy built on [16] with theoretical and empirical analysis for multiclass/extreme classification.  
- Experiments on text datasets show the method handles large numbers of classes effectively.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons against multiple baselines; achieves high performance with reduced training time on several datasets.  
- Highlights extreme classification as an important, practically relevant research direction.  
- Heavy reliance on [16]; novelty is limited despite two contributions (double sampling and new local Rademacher-based bounds).  
- Method reduces training time but incurs substantially longer prediction times, which is problematic for deployment.  
- The interpretation and practical significance of the presented generalization bound need clarification and comparison to competing methods’ bounds.  
- Experimental setup mixes batch and online algorithms, leading to inconsistent memory requirements across comparisons.
