- Proposes a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification using a feature transform φ mapping (input,class) pairs to a p-dimensional space and scoring functions over R^p.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) by creating transformed points for class comparisons and addressing correlated training examples.  
- Provides improved generalization analysis via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for the correlated-data setting.  
- Introduces sampling procedures that rebalance class distributions and reduce the size of the transformed training set by sampling classes with replacement and subsampling comparisons.  
- Shows that the proposed sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results compare favorably to strong baselines, demonstrating competitiveness of the new reduction.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3, which limits understanding of its guarantees when only k nearest-centroid classes are considered.  
- Lacks detailed discussion and comparison of the sample complexity bounds to those for non-transformed multi-class methods.  
- Sampling techniques for rebalancing and dataset reduction are a novel and practically significant contribution.  
- Addresses an important problem for settings with very large numbers of classes and offers potentially efficient solutions.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation, aiding readability.
