- Introduces a reduction from multi-class to binary classification for large-class problems using a feature map phi and a score function over (input, class) pairs.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015)’s K-1 transformed-point reduction, noting the transformed dataset’s correlations break many standard generalization bounds.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for the correlated transformed data.  
- Second contribution: sampling techniques (per-class sampling with replacement and random sampling of classes for transformed points) to rebalance classes and shrink the transformed dataset.  
- Shows sampling procedures do not significantly degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results: the proposed procedure is highly competitive with several strong baselines.  
- Novelty primarily in the sampling/rebalancing strategy; the basic reduction itself was introduced earlier by Joshi et al.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3 (comparison limited to k nearest-centroid classes).  
- Would benefit from additional discussion/comparison of the sample complexity bounds versus generalization bounds for non-transformed multi-class methods.
