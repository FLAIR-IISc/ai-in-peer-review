- Proposes a learning reduction for extreme classification with up to ~100,000 classes.  
- Notes limitations of existing methods: tree-based approaches suffer suboptimal latent trees and cascading errors.  
- Notes limitations of label-embedding approaches when the label matrix is not low-rank.  
- Reformulates extreme classification as pairwise binary classification and proposes a scalable sampling-based reduction with consistency guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation on five text datasets: outperforms competitors in accuracy and F1 for >30,000 classes, trains faster, and uses much less memory, but predicts slightly slower.  
- Introduces Aggressive Double Sampling with two parameters: inverse-frequency sampling to upweight rare classes and uniform sampling of adversarial examples.  
- Uses centroid-based candidate class selection at inference to avoid generating pairwise features for all classes.  
- Theoretical challenges: dyadic example dependence and distribution shift from oversampling.  
- Addresses theory by partitioning dependent sums and applying concentration bounds for partially dependent variables (Janson); finite-sample risk bound has bias that decreases linearly with sample sizes.  
- Reviewer finds the analysis seemingly rigorous and concludes the method is innovative, practical, and resource-efficient.
