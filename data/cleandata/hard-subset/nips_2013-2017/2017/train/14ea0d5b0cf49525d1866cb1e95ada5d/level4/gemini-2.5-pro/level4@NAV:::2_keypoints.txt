- Proposes a reduction from large-scale multi-class to binary classification using a learned scoring function on phi(x, class) feature vectors.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) expansion approach, which creates K-1 pairwise transformed instances per example.  
- Identifies and addresses the challenge of correlated transformed examples that invalidate standard generalization bounds.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity, outperforming Joshi et al.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: two sampling strategies—class sampling (with replacement) and sampling a subset of incorrect classes—to rebalance classes and shrink training data.  
- Shows sampling procedures can be incorporated without significantly weakening the derived generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates highly competitive performance against several strong baselines.  
- Positive overall assessment: compelling reduction with supportive theory for correlated transformed data.  
- Noted omission: no theoretical analysis or formal justification for the prediction-time heuristic that restricts comparisons to k nearest-centroid classes.  
- Recommendation: include a more detailed discussion and comparison of the derived sample complexity bounds versus standard multi-class bounds to clarify trade-offs.
