This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multi-class classification method originally presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analysis to support their approach. Experiments conducted on text datasets demonstrate that the proposed method can effectively handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

**Pros:**
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on multiple datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important research direction with many practical applications.

**Cons:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited as it is primarily based on the work in [16]. Although the paper introduces two main improvements (the double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities), the contribution feels incremental.
- While the proposed approach shortens training time, it still requires a long prediction time compared to other methods. This is a significant drawback, as prediction time is arguably more important than training time in many practical applications.

**Comments:**
- It is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper. A comparison of this bound to those of competing approaches would help to contextualize the theoretical results.
- The experiments mix batch and online learning algorithms in their comparisons. Since these categories of algorithms have vastly different memory usage profiles, this aspect should be discussed to ensure a fair evaluation.
