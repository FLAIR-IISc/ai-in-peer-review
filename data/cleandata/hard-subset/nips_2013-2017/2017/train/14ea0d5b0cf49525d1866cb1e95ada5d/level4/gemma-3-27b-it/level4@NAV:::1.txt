This paper introduces a double sampling strategy designed to enhance multiclass classification, building upon the foundation laid in [16]. The authors support their method with both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation. Their experiments, focused on text datasets, demonstrate the approach’s capability to effectively address multiclass classification challenges, particularly those involving a large number of classes.

**Strengths:**

- The authors provide a thorough experimental comparison against a variety of baseline methods. The results indicate that the proposed strategy achieves competitive performance alongside reduced training time across multiple datasets.
- Research into extreme classification is a valuable and increasingly relevant area with numerous practical applications.

**Weaknesses:**

- The work is heavily reliant on [16]. While the paper presents two key contributions – a double sampling strategy and novel generalization bounds based on local Rademacher Complexities – the overall novelty appears somewhat limited.
- Although the approach reduces training time, prediction time remains comparatively long. Given that prediction speed is often a critical factor in practical deployment, this is a notable concern.

**Comments:**

- The practical implications and interpretability of the presented generalization bound are unclear. A discussion of how it compares to existing bounds would be beneficial.
- The experimental comparison includes both batch and online learning algorithms, which exhibit significantly different memory usage characteristics. This difference should be addressed when interpreting the results.
