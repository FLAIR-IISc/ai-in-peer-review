- Tackles extreme multi-class classification with a double sampling strategy sampling both classes and features to train weak learners more efficiently.  
- Provides a theoretical analysis including a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities.  
- Empirical results on large-scale text datasets show large training-time reductions while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Addresses a highly relevant, practical problem with applications like tagging, recommendation, and advertising.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against strong baselines on challenging datasets and demonstrating runtime benefits.  
- Novelty is limited—method is an incremental extension of the framework in [16] rather than a new conceptual contribution.  
- Prediction/inference time remains high relative to competing methods (e.g., tree-based), which may limit real-world applicability.  
- The practical interpretation and implications of the new generalization bound are not sufficiently explained or compared to existing bounds.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair without discussing memory usage—online methods often have much lower memory footprints.  
- Authors should discuss or evaluate strategies to accelerate inference (e.g., pruning, quantization, alternative data structures).
