- Introduces a reduction from multi-class to binary classification mapping (input, class) pairs to scores and a loss counting incorrectly higher-scored classes.
- Uses a p-dimensional feature transform φ for (input, class) pairs and learns a score mapping in that space.
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) by addressing the correlated nature of the K-1 transformed points per example.
- Provides improved generalization analysis via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity tailored to correlated transformed data.
- Proposes sampling techniques: sampling with replacement to rebalance class representation and sampling a random subset of classes to reduce transformed dataset size.
- Shows these sampling procedures preserve the derived generalization bounds.
- Empirical results demonstrate competitive and strong performance against several strong baselines.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology.
- Lacks detailed theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3 that uses nearest-centroid k-class comparisons.
- Needs more discussion on practical implications and scaling of the generalization bounds, especially comparison of sample complexity growth with K to direct multi-class methods.
- Strengths: novel/effective sampling for scalability, improved analysis for correlated data, strong empirical performance, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: limited theory on the nearest-neighbor approximation and missing direct comparison to non-transformed multi-class sample complexity bounds.
- Overall assessment: solid and substantial contribution; recommendation: Accept.
