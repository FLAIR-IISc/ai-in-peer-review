This paper presents a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach originally proposed in [16]. The authors provide both a theoretical analysis of their method, including a new generalization bound, and a comprehensive empirical evaluation. Experiments conducted on large-scale text datasets demonstrate that the proposed approach is effective for extreme classification problems involving a very large number of classes.

**Pros:**
- The paper includes a comprehensive experimental comparison against several strong baseline methods. The results demonstrate that the proposed approach achieves high performance with significantly shorter training times across multiple datasets.
- The work addresses the problem of extreme classification, which is an important research direction with numerous practical applications.

**Cons:**
- The work is largely an incremental extension of the method presented in [16]. While it introduces a double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher Complexities, the overall novelty is somewhat limited.
- Although the proposed method reduces training time, its prediction time is still significantly longer than that of competing approaches. In many real-world applications, prediction latency is a more critical factor than training time, which may limit the practical utility of this work.

**Comments:**
- The interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear. To better situate this theoretical contribution, the authors should provide context on how this bound compares to existing bounds for related methods.
- The experimental evaluation mixes batch and online learning algorithms. These paradigms have fundamentally different memory requirements, a factor that is not discussed. A direct comparison of training times can be misleading without also considering the significant differences in memory consumption between these methods.
