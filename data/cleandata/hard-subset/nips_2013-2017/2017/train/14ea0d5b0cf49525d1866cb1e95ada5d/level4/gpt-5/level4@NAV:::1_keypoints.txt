- Proposes a double-sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification method from [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.  
- Experiments on text datasets show the method can handle multi-class problems with very large numbers of classes.  
- Extensive experimental comparisons against several baselines show strong performance and reduced training time on several datasets.  
- Addresses the important and practically relevant problem of extreme classification.  
- Overall novelty is limited because the work is largely built on [16], despite adding the double-sampling strategy and new generalization bounds.  
- Prediction time is longer than competing methods, which may be critical in practice despite reduced training time.  
- Interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison to bounds from other approaches.  
- Experimental comparison mixes batch and online algorithms, producing inherently different memory-usage profiles.
