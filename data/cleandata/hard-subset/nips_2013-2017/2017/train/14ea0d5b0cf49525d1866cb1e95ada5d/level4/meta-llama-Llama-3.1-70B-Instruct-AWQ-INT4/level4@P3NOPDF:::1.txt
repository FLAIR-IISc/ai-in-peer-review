This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their proposed approach, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, as evidenced by experiments conducted on text datasets.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with multiple baseline methods, showcasing the proposed approach's high performance and shorter training times on several datasets. Secondly, the extreme classification problem addressed in this paper is a significant research direction with numerous practical applications.

However, there are also some limitations to the paper. Firstly, the work is heavily based on [16], and despite introducing two main improvements – a double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities – the novelty of the paper is relatively thin. Secondly, although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires longer prediction times compared to other approaches. In practice, prediction time is often more critical, making this a notable drawback.

Some additional comments and suggestions for improvement are as follows:

- The generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear and lacks context. It would be beneficial to compare this bound with those of other approaches to provide a more comprehensive understanding of its significance.
- The experimental evaluation includes both batch and online learning algorithms, which have vastly different memory usage profiles. To provide a more accurate comparison, it would be helpful to consider memory usage as an additional evaluation metric.

Overall, while the paper presents some promising results and addresses an important research problem, it could benefit from further clarification and more comprehensive evaluations to strengthen its contributions.
