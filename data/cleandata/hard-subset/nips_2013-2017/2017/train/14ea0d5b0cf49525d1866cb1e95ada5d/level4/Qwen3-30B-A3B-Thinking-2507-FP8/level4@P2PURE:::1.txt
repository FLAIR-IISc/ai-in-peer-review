

This paper introduces a double sampling strategy to enhance multiclass classification in [16], providing both theoretical and empirical analysis. Experiments on text datasets demonstrate that the proposed approach effectively handles multiclass classification with a large number of classes.  

Pros:  
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons against multiple baseline methods. Results indicate the proposed approach achieves high performance with reduced training time across several datasets.  
- Extreme classification represents a significant research direction with substantial practical applications.  

Cons:  
- The work is heavily reliant on [16]. Although two key improvements are introduced—(1) the double sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher Complexities—the novelty is limited.  
- While the proposed approach reduces training time, it incurs significantly longer prediction times compared to alternatives. Prediction time is arguably more critical in practical deployment.  

Comments:  
- The interpretation and significance of the generalization bound presented require clarification. How does this bound compare to those of competing methods?  
- The experimental evaluation mixes batch and online learning algorithms, resulting in substantially different memory requirements.
