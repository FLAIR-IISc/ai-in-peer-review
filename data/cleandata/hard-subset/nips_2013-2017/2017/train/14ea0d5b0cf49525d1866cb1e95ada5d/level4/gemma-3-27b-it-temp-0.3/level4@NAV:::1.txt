This paper introduces a double sampling strategy designed to enhance multiclass classification, building upon the work in [16]. The authors support their method with both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation. Experimental results on text datasets demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness in addressing multiclass classification problems, particularly those with a large number of classes.

**Strengths:**

- The authors provide a thorough experimental comparison against a range of baseline methods. The results indicate that the proposed approach achieves strong performance with reduced training time across multiple datasets.
- Research into extreme classification is a valuable and practically relevant area with numerous real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**

- The work is heavily reliant on the foundation laid in [16]. While the paper introduces two key advancements – a double sampling strategy and novel generalization bounds based on local Rademacher Complexities – the overall novelty appears somewhat limited.
- Although the proposed method reduces training time, prediction time remains comparatively long when benchmarked against other approaches. Given that prediction time is often a critical factor in practical applications, this is a significant consideration.

**Comments:**

- The practical interpretation of the presented generalization bound is unclear. A discussion of how this bound compares to existing bounds would be beneficial.
- The experimental comparison includes both batch learning and online learning algorithms, leading to potentially misleading comparisons regarding memory usage.
