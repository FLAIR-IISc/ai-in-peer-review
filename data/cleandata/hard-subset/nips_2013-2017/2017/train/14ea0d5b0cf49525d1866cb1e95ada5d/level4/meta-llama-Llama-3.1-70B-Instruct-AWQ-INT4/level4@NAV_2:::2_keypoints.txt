- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification that scores (input,class) pairs and measures loss by fraction of wrong classes ranked above the true class.  
- Uses a feature transform phi mapping (input,class) to R^p and learns a scorer; builds on Joshi et al. (2015) but addresses correlated transformed examples.  
- Contribution 1: improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Contribution 2: sampling techniques (per-class sampling with replacement and random sampling of comparison classes) to rebalance classes and reduce transformed dataset size.  
- Shows these sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the generalization bounds.  
- Empirically competitive: strong experimental performance against several strong baselines.  
- Missing: no theoretical analysis of the Sec. 2.3 prediction algorithm that compares only k nearest class centroids.  
- Missing: no discussion of sample complexity bounds or comparison to non-transformed multi-class sample complexity/generalization.  
- Novelty: main new element is the sampling-based rebalancing/reduction; core reduction idea largely from Joshi et al.  
- Overall: presents an interesting large-K reduction and corresponding generalization bounds for correlated reduced data.  
- Acceptance summary: pros—suitable large-K reduction, strong empirical results, solid generalization analysis; cons—lacks theoretical analysis of prediction heuristic and sample complexity discussion.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
