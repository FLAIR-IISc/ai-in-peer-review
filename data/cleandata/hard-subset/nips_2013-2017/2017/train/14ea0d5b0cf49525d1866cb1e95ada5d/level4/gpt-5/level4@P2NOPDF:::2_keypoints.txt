- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification for very large K using a scoring function over (x, c) and a loss equal to the fraction of incorrect classes scoring above the true class.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015), which creates K−1 pairwise transformed examples per training instance, and highlights the resulting correlation among transformed examples.  
- Gives improved generalization bounds for the correlated transformed examples via fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Introduces a sampling scheme that rebalances class frequencies and randomly subsamples pairwise comparisons to greatly reduce the transformed dataset size.  
- Proves the proposed sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation shows competitive or superior performance compared to strong baselines.  
- Lacks a theoretical analysis of the test-time candidate-pruning strategy (restricting comparisons to k nearest centroids); consistency and risk effects are not quantified.  
- Requests more discussion and comparison of the sample complexity bounds versus standard multiclass guarantees to clarify when the reduction with sampling is advantageous.
