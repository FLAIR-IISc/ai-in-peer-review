This paper [16] presents a double sampling technique for improving the multiclass classification method. The authors present both theoretical and empirical analyses of their proposed method. Experiments conducted on text datasets show that the proposed method can solve multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

Advantages:
- The authors conduct extensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. The results show that the proposed method achieves high performance in several datasets with short training times.
- Extreme classification is an important aspect of problem research and has many applications in practice.

Disadvantages:
- This paper is largely based on [16]. Despite two major improvements: 1) a double sampling technique has been proposed, and 2) a new generalization limit based on local રેডেমacher complexity has been created, the novelty is relatively low.
- Although the proposed method reduces training time, it still requires a long prediction time compared to other methods. In other words, prediction time is more important in practice.

Comments:
- It is unclear how to interconnect the generalization limit presented in the paper. How is the generation limit compared to other methods?
- Some of the methods presented in the experiments are batch learning algorithms and some are online learning algorithms. Therefore, memory usage is very different.
