Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification using a (input,class) feature mapping and a loss counting fraction of incorrect classes scoring higher than the true class.  
Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) but provides improved generalization analysis for the correlated transformed dataset via new fractional Rademacher complexity bounds.  
Introduces sampling procedures (class sampling with replacement and sub-sampling comparisons) to rebalance class representation and reduce transformed training set size.  
Shows that the proposed sampling methods do not substantially degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
Empirically competitive with several strong baselines on large-class problems.  
Primary novelty is the sampling-based reduction and improved generalization bounds; the basic reduction itself was previously presented by Joshi et al.  
Missing theoretical analysis of the Section 2.3 prediction algorithm that restricts comparisons to k nearest centroids, limiting evaluation of that heuristic.  
Asks for a more detailed discussion and comparison of the sample complexity/generalization bounds versus standard non-transformed multi-class results.
