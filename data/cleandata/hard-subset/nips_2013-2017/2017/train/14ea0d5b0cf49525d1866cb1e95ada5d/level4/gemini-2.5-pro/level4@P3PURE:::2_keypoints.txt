- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary via a scorer on (input, class) pairs and a ranking loss that predicts the argmax class.  
- Uses a phi feature transform mapping (x,y) to p-dimensional features and expands each (x,y) into K−1 pairwise comparisons (Joshi et al. 2015), producing highly correlated examples.  
- Identifies the challenge that standard generalization bounds fail due to the correlation in the transformed dataset.  
- Main theoretical contribution: new fractional Rademacher complexity bounds that account for the data correlation.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling techniques—sampling classes with replacement and sampling a random subset of negative classes per example—to rebalance classes and shrink the transformed training set.  
- Shows theoretically that the proposed sampling procedures can be used without significantly weakening generalization guarantees.  
- Empirically competitive with several strong baseline methods.  
- Novelty mainly lies in the sampling methods; the reduction framework itself builds on prior work (Joshi et al.).  
- Omission: no theoretical analysis of the approximate prediction algorithm that restricts comparisons to the k nearest class centroids.  
- Suggestion: paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion and comparison of the new sample complexity bounds with existing multiclass generalization bounds to clarify trade-offs.
