- Paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multi-class classification method from [16].
- Authors provide theoretical analysis and empirical validation for the proposed approach.
- Experiments on text datasets show the method handles large-scale multi-class problems effectively.
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show high performance and shorter training times versus baselines.
- Tackles the important and practical problem of extreme classification.
- Novelty is limited because the work is largely based on [16]; contributions feel incremental.
- Prediction time remains long compared to other methods, which is problematic for practical use.
- Interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison to competing bounds.
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms without accounting for their differing memory usage.
