This work proposes a double sampling strategy to extend the multiclass classification framework of [16]. The authors support their method with both theoretical analysis and empirical validation. The experiments, conducted on text datasets, demonstrate the proposed method's effectiveness in handling classification problems with a very large number of classes.

Pros:
- The paper includes extensive experimental comparisons against a variety of baseline methods. The results indicate that the proposed approach delivers strong performance while requiring less training time across multiple datasets.
- The focus on the extreme classification problem is well-motivated, as it is a significant research area with numerous practical applications.

Cons:
- The work is heavily based on the framework presented in [16]. While the paper introduces two main contributions (the double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound using local Rademacher Complexities), the overall novelty feels somewhat incremental.
- Although the proposed method reduces training time, its prediction time remains high compared to competing methods. In many practical applications, prediction latency is arguably a more critical performance metric.

Comments:
- The practical interpretation of the presented generalization bound is unclear. It would be valuable to see a comparison of this bound against those established for other relevant approaches.
- The experimental evaluation mixes batch and online learning algorithms. This makes direct comparison difficult, as these two classes of methods have significantly different memory consumption profiles.
