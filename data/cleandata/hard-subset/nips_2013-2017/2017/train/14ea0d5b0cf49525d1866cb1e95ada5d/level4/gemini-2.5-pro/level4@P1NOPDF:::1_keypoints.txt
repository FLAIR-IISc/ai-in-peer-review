- Paper proposes a double sampling strategy to extend the multiclass method from [16], with theoretical analysis and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities.
- Empirical evaluation on large-scale text datasets shows the method is effective for extreme classification with very large numbers of classes.
- Comprehensive experimental comparison against strong baselines: achieves high performance with significantly shorter training times across multiple datasets.
- Tackles the important and practical problem of extreme classification.
- Largely an incremental extension of [16]; overall novelty is limited despite the double sampling and new bound.
- Prediction latency remains significantly higher than competing approaches, which may limit practical usefulness despite faster training.
- Interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and should be compared to existing bounds for related methods.
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms without discussing differing memory requirements, making training-time comparisons potentially misleading.
