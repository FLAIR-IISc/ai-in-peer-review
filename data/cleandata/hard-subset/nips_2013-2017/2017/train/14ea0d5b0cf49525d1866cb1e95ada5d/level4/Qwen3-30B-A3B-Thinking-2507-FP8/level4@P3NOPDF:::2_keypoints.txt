- Introduces a reduction from large-scale multi-class to binary classification using a hypothesis mapping (input, class) pairs to scores via a feature transform φ and linear scoring.  
- Extends Joshi et al. (2015) by generating transformed comparison points but notes the resulting transformed dataset contains correlated examples.  
- Main theoretical contribution: novel generalization analysis using fractional Rademacher complexity tailored to correlated transformed data.  
- Practical contribution: two sampling techniques—(1) class sampling with replacement for balanced representation, (2) sampling a subset of incorrect labels per example—to reduce dataset size.  
- Proves the sampling strategies preserve the new generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical results show competitive accuracy and significant efficiency gains versus strong baselines.  
- Strengths: technically sound extension, theoretical rigor, practical scalability, and strong empirical validation.  
- Main novelty: the sampling strategy for rebalancing and size reduction; core reduction builds on prior work.  
- Missing theory: no analysis of the prediction step that restricts comparisons to the k nearest class centroids and its effect on error/bias.  
- Missing comparison: lacks sample complexity analysis relating the reduction’s required samples to standard multi-class methods (one-vs-all, softmax).  
- Presentation could improve: provide intuition for why fractional Rademacher complexity suits correlated data induced by (x,y)-pair transformations.  
- Empirical reporting could improve: quantify computational savings (training time and memory) from sampling with concrete metrics.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: solid contribution with minor addressable gaps; Accept pending minor revisions.
