- Introduces a double sampling strategy built on [16], with theoretical analysis and experiments on text datasets showing effectiveness for many-class problems.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons against multiple baselines, showing high performance and reduced training time on several datasets.  
- Tackles extreme classification, an important and practically relevant research direction.  
- Heavy reliance on [16]; main contributions are the double sampling method and a local Rademacher-based generalization bound, so novelty is limited.  
- Prediction time remains substantially longer than competing approaches despite reduced training time, which is problematic for practical deployment.  
- The interpretation of the proposed generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison to bounds for alternative methods.  
- Experimental comparison mixes batch and online learning algorithms, causing significant memory-usage differences that complicate fair evaluation.
