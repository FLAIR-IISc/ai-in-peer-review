- Proposes a double-sampling strategy: aggressively oversample small classes and subsample large classes, plus reduce dyadic examples in the binary reduction framework.  
- Provides new generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity, proving consistency of empirical risk minimization under the sampling scheme.  
- Empirical results on DMOZ and Wikipedia show improvements in training time, memory usage, accuracy, and macro-F1 versus OVA, M-SVM, and FastXML.  
- Demonstrates large memory savings (10–100×) and faster training on large-scale datasets.  
- Paper is well-structured and clear, with reproducible supplementary material and a detailed algorithm ((π, κ)-DS).  
- Strong experimental design: multiple large datasets (WIKI-Small, WIKI-50K, WIKI-100K), comprehensive metrics, and ablation study on sampling hyperparameters.  
- The double-sampling idea is conceptually straightforward and incremental over Joshi et al. (2017).  
- Theoretical use of local Rademacher complexity is a natural extension but lacks explicit quantitative comparison to prior bounds to show magnitude of improvement.  
- Prediction time is a weakness: comparable to OVA/M-SVM and significantly slower than tree-based methods, hurting latency-sensitive applications.  
- The paper’s statement on being “highly competitive in terms of Time” is misleading without distinguishing training vs. prediction time.  
- Theorem 2 is technically dense and should more clearly compare convergence rates and constants with prior work.  
- Overall, the method is practically significant for extreme classification despite moderate novelty.  
- Recommendation: Accept, provided authors clarify prediction-time tradeoffs and strengthen theoretical comparisons.
