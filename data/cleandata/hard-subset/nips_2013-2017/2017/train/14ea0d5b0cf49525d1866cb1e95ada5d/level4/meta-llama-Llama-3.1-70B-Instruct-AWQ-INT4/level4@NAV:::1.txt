This paper introduces an enhanced multiclass classification approach by proposing a double sampling strategy, building upon the work presented in [16]. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, encompassing both theoretical and empirical evaluations. Experimental results on text datasets demonstrate the proposed method's capability to effectively tackle multiclass classification problems involving a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct extensive experimental comparisons with multiple baseline methods, showcasing the proposed approach's high performance and reduced training time across several datasets.
- The extreme classification problem addressed in this research is a significant area of study with numerous practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited, as it heavily relies on [16], with the primary contributions being the double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher Complexities.
- Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires considerable prediction time compared to other methods, which may be more critical in practical scenarios.

Comments:
- The generalization bound presented in the paper requires further clarification. A comparison of this bound with other approaches would be beneficial.
- The experimental evaluation includes both batch and online learning algorithms, which have distinct memory usage characteristics, making direct comparisons challenging.
