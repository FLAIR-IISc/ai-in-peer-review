This paper presents a double sampling strategy to improve the multi-class classification approach in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analysis of their proposed approach. Experiments conducted on text datasets show that the proposed approach can handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training time on several datasets. 
- The extreme classification problem is an important direction of research and has many applications in practice.

Cons:
- The paper is mostly based on [16]. Despite two main improvements: 1) the proposed double sampling strategy and 2) a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities, the novelty is relatively thin. 
- Although the proposed approach shortens the training time, it still requires long prediction time compared with other approaches. In practice, prediction time is arguably more important.

Comments:
- It is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper. How does the generalization bound compare with other approaches?
- Some methods presented in the experiments are batch learning algorithms, while others are online learning algorithms. Therefore, the memory usages are very different.

Additional suggestions:
- The paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.
- The authors could provide more insights into the theoretical analysis, explaining the implications of the generalization bound and how it relates to the empirical results.
- Consider adding more details about the experimental setup, such as the specific hyperparameters used for each method and the computational resources employed.
- The paper could be improved by providing more visualizations, such as plots or figures, to help illustrate the results and facilitate comparison between methods.
- In the conclusion, the authors could provide more context about the significance of their contributions and the potential impact of their work on the field of extreme multi-class classification.
