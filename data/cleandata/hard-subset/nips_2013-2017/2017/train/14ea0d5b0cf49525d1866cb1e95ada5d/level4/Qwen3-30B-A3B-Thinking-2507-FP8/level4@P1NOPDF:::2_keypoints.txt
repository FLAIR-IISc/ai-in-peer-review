- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification tailored for very large K, mapping (input, class) pairs to scores and measuring loss by fraction of incorrect classes ranked above the true class.  
- Assumes a feature transform φ:(input,class)→ℝ^p and learns a mapping from ℝ^p to scores.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015): for each (x,y) create K−1 transformed comparisons (y vs. each y'), which produces correlated training examples.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis using new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity that handle the correlated transformed examples.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling strategies that rebalance class representation and reduce the transformed dataset by sampling classes with replacement and sampling a subset of comparisons per example.  
- Proves the sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance versus strong baselines on large-class tasks.  
- Gap: no theoretical analysis or justification for the Section 2.3 prediction heuristic that restricts comparisons to k nearest class centroids.  
- Gap: paper lacks analysis of how the centroid-based selection affects error rates or overall guarantees.  
- Gap: needs comparison of derived sample complexity bounds to direct multi-class generalization guarantees (without transformation) to assess tightness and practical trade-offs for large K.
