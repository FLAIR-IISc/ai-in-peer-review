- Introduces a reduction from multiclass to binary for very large K using a feature transform φ and a ranking-style loss over (input, class) pairs.
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) and addresses two challenges: correlated transformed examples and the cost of generating all K−1 comparisons.
- Contribution 1: improved generalization analysis that leverages fractional Rademacher complexity to handle dependence among transformed examples.
- Contribution 2: sampling strategies—class resampling to rebalance representation and random subsampling of negatives per example—to cut dataset size while preserving guarantees.
- Empirical results show competitive accuracy and improved scalability versus strong baselines.
- Strength: tackles an important, practically relevant large-K setting where standard approaches struggle statistically and computationally.
- Strength: the fractional Rademacher analysis is well-motivated and advances prior theoretical work on this reduction.
- Strength: simple, effective sampling schemes that reduce training cost and retain theoretical guarantees.
- Weakness: novelty is mainly in sampling and refined analysis; core reduction is not new and the paper should better quantify added value over Joshi et al.
- Weakness: prediction-time top-k centroid heuristic (restricting comparisons) lacks theoretical justification—no analysis of consistency, error rates, or approximation bounds.
- Weakness: missing explicit comparison of derived sample-complexity bounds to known bounds for direct multiclass methods (dependencies on K, p, imbalance, and sampled negatives).
- Weakness: limited practical guidance on choosing sampling rates and trade-offs (variance vs. bias, compute vs. accuracy) and lack of sensitivity analyses.
- Clarity: generally well written; reduction and induced correlations are clearly described.
- Presentation suggestions: state assumptions consistently and connect them to bound terms; summarize dependence of final bounds on K, negatives per example, and class imbalance.
- Recommended experiments/analysis: theoretical treatment of top-k filtering, comparisons of sample complexity with non-reduction methods, ablations for rebalancing and negative sampling, and practical defaults/heuristics for sampling and k.
- Overall assessment: solid and timely contribution with valuable analysis and sampling methods; main gaps are theory for the top-k heuristic and clearer comparisons to direct multiclass approaches, but the work is promising and practically relevant.
