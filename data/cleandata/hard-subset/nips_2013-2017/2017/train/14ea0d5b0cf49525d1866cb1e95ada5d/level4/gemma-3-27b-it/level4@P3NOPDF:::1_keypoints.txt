- Proposes a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification with theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation, effective for many classes and text data.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation against established baselines showing competitive accuracy and substantially reduced training time.  
- Addresses extreme classification, offering practical benefits for large-scale datasets via faster training.  
- Novelty is incremental: contributions (double sampling and a local Rademacher-based bound) are improvements rather than a new paradigm.  
- Prediction time is substantially longer than alternatives, which may limit real-world applicability where fast inference is critical.  
- The presented generalization bound is hard to interpret or use for model selection/hyperparameter tuning; its relation to prior bounds (e.g., [16]) is unclear.  
- Mixing batch and online algorithms in experiments confounds memory-usage comparisons; unclear if reported memory savings stem from the method or from algorithm differences.  
- Need clarification on sampling-rate sensitivity and guidance for choosing that hyperparameter.  
- Related-work discussion should better compare to approximate nearest-neighbor and tree-based methods (e.g., FAISS) commonly used in large-scale multiclass tasks.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-executed; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the bound, address prediction-time concerns, and better articulate limitations and favorable use cases.
