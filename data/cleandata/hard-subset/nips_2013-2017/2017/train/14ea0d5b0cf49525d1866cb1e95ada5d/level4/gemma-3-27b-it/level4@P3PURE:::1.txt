This paper presents a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach introduced in [16]. The authors support their proposed method with both theoretical and empirical analysis. Experiments conducted on text datasets demonstrate the approach’s ability to effectively address multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

**Pros:**

- The authors provide comprehensive experimental comparisons against several baseline methods. Results indicate that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times across multiple datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important and practically relevant research direction with numerous applications.

**Cons:**

- This work builds heavily on [16]. While the paper introduces two key improvements – a double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher Complexities – the overall novelty is relatively limited.
- Despite the reduction in training time, the proposed approach exhibits longer prediction times compared to other methods. Prediction time is arguably more critical in many practical applications.

**Comments:**

- The interpretation of the generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear. Further discussion is needed to contextualize this bound; how does it compare to existing generalization bounds in the literature?
- The experimental comparison includes both batch learning and online learning algorithms, leading to potentially unfair comparisons of memory usage. These differences should be addressed.
