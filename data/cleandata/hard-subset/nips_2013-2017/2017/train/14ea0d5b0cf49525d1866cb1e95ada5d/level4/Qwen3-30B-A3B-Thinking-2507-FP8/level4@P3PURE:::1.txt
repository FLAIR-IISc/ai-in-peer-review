

This paper introduces a double sampling strategy to enhance multiclass classification methods presented in [16], supported by theoretical analysis and empirical validation. Experiments on text datasets demonstrate the approach's effectiveness in handling multiclass problems with a large number of classes.  

Pros:  
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons against multiple baseline methods. Results indicate the proposed approach achieves high performance with reduced training time across several datasets.  
- Extreme classification represents a significant research direction with substantial practical applications.  

Cons:  
- The work relies heavily on [16], with two primary contributions: (1) the double sampling strategy and (2) a novel generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities. However, the novelty is limited.  
- While training time is reduced, prediction time remains substantially longer than competing approaches. Prediction time is arguably more critical in practical deployments.  

Comments:  
- The interpretation of the generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear. How does this bound compare to those of alternative methods?  
- The experimental comparison mixes batch learning algorithms with online learning algorithms, leading to significant differences in memory usage.
