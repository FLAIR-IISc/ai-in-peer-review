- Proposes a double-sampling strategy building on [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.  
- Experiments on text datasets demonstrate handling of extreme multiclass problems and strong performance.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods.  
- Achieves shorter training times on several datasets.  
- Notes the practical importance of extreme multiclass classification.  
- Novelty is limited since the work is largely based on [16], with two main additions (double-sampling and new local Rademacher bounds).  
- Prediction time remains long compared to other methods, which is a practical drawback.  
- The presented generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison to bounds from other approaches.  
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms, so differing memory usage should be accounted for.
