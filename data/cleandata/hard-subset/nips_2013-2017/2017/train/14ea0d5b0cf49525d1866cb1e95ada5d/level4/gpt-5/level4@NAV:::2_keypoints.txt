- Introduces a reduction from large-class multiclass to binary classification using a scoring function on (input,class) embeddings.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) by generating pairwise comparisons (K−1 per example), which induces correlations in the transformed dataset.  
- Main theoretical contribution: tighter generalization bounds via new fractional Rademacher complexity analysis that handles those correlations.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: a sampling strategy that rebalances class frequencies and subsamples negative classes to shrink the transformed dataset.  
- Proofs show the two sampling steps do not significantly degrade generalization guarantees.  
- Empirically achieves highly competitive performance against strong baselines.  
- Novelty primarily in the sampling strategy and the correlated-data generalization analysis; the core reduction follows prior work.  
- Missing: no theoretical treatment of the prediction procedure that restricts comparisons to the k nearest-class centroids (Section 2.3).  
- Missing: more discussion and comparison of the sample-complexity bounds to guarantees for the original, non-reduced multiclass formulation.
