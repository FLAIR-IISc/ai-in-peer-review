This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their proposed approach. Experiments conducted on a text dataset demonstrate that the proposed approach can effectively handle multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. The results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on several datasets.
- The extreme classification problem is an important research direction with many practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper is largely based on [16], with two primary improvements: 1) the proposed double sampling strategy, and 2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities. However, the novelty of the paper is relatively limited.
- Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires longer prediction times compared to other approaches. In practice, prediction time is often more important.

Comments:
- The interpretation of the generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear. How does this bound compare to those of other approaches?
- The methods presented in the experiments include both batch learning algorithms and online learning algorithms, which have significantly different memory usage.
