This paper proposes a double-sampling strategy to improve upon the multiclass classification method of [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of the proposed approach. Experiments on text datasets show that the method can handle multiclass classification problems with a very large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves strong performance with shorter training times on several datasets.
- Extreme multiclass classification is an important research direction with many practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper is largely based on [16]. Although there are two main improvements—(1) the double-sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the novelty feels relatively limited.
- Although the proposed approach shortens training time, it still requires long prediction time compared with other approaches. Arguably, prediction time is more important in practice.

Comments:
- It is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper. How does this bound compare with those of other approaches?
- Some methods included in the experiments are batch learning algorithms, while others are online learning algorithms. Consequently, their memory usage differs substantially, which should be taken into account when interpreting the comparisons.
