- Paper proposes a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification, building on prior work [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.  
- Experiments on text datasets show the method handles large numbers of classes effectively.  
- Strong experimental comparisons to multiple baselines show competitive performance and reduced training time.  
- Extreme classification is a relevant, practical research area.  
- Method heavily depends on [16], so overall novelty is limited despite two claimed improvements.  
- Although training is faster, prediction times are longer than competing methods, which is problematic for practical use.  
- The practical interpretation and comparative tightness/utility of the new generalization bound (local Rademacher complexities) is unclear.  
- Mixing batch and online algorithms in experiments complicates and undermines fair memory usage comparisons.
