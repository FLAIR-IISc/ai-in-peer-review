- Proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach in [16].
- Introduces new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities.
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.
- Experiments on a text dataset show effective handling of large-scale multiclass problems.
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baselines.
- Reports high performance and shorter training times on several datasets.
- Notes extreme classification is an important, practically relevant research direction.
- Novelty is relatively thin despite building on [16] with two main improvements.
- Prediction time remains long compared to other approaches, which is problematic in practice.
- Generalization bound interpretation is unclear and would benefit from comparison to other bounds.
- Experimental mix of batch and online algorithms omits discussion of their differing memory usage and implications.
