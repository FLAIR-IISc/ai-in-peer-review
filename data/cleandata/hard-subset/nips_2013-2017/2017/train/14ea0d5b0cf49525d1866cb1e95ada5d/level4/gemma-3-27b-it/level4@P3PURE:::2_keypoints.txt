- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification by mapping (input, class) pairs to scores via a p-dimensional feature transform φ.  
- Uses a loss that counts the fraction of incorrect classes that the model scores higher than the true class.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015), which creates K−1 pairwise comparison examples per data point, yielding correlated training examples.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis for the correlated reduced dataset via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Second contribution: sampling techniques to rebalance class distributions and reduce the size of the transformed training set (sampling classes with replacement and sampling a subset of comparisons).  
- Shows that the proposed sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirically competitive with several strong baselines.  
- Novelty is mainly in the sampling strategy and analysis; the fundamental reduction itself was previously proposed by Joshi et al.  
- Missing/weak points: no theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm that restricts comparisons to k nearest centroids (section 2.3), and limited discussion/comparison of sample complexity bounds relative to original multi-class generalization bounds.
