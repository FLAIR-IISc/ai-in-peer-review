This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification approach presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their approach. Experiments conducted on a text dataset demonstrate the proposed method's ability to handle multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

The paper has several strengths:

* The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with multiple baseline methods, showcasing the proposed approach's high performance and shorter training times on several datasets.
* The extreme classification problem addressed in this paper is a significant research direction with numerous practical applications.

However, there are also some limitations:

* The paper heavily relies on [16], with the primary contributions being the double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities. While these improvements are notable, the overall novelty of the paper is relatively limited.
* Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires longer prediction times compared to other methods. In practice, prediction time is often more critical.

Some additional comments and suggestions for improvement:

* The generalization bound presented in the paper is not clearly explained. It would be beneficial to compare this bound to those of other approaches to provide a better understanding of its significance.
* The experimental evaluation includes both batch and online learning algorithms, which have vastly different memory requirements. It would be helpful to discuss these differences and their implications for practical applications.
