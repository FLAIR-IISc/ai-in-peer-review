- Proposes a double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds (local Rademacher complexities) augmenting the multiclass approach of [16], with theoretical and empirical analysis on large-class text data.
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons show the method attains high performance and shorter training times across multiple datasets.
- Highlights the practical importance of extreme multiclass classification as a research direction.
- Limited novelty: method mainly builds on [16] with only two key improvements.
- Prediction time remains longer than competing methods, which is a significant practical drawback.
- The presented generalization bound is unclearly interpreted and should be compared with existing bounds to assess significance.
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms without accounting for substantially different memory usage, which should be addressed.
