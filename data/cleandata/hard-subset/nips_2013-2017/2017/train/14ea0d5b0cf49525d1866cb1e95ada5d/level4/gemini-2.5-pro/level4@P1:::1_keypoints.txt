- Problem: tackles extreme multi-class classification with long-tailed labels, building on a dyadic example–class binary reduction that suffers massive data expansion.  
- Contribution: proposes aggressive double sampling—rebalance classes by over/undersampling and sample few negative classes per example to curb computational costs.  
- Theory: provides new generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexities, arguing ERM consistency despite sampling-induced distribution shift and dependencies.  
- Experiments: large-scale text datasets (up to 100k classes) show strong accuracy, reduced memory use, and faster training compared to several baselines.  
- Strength: addresses an important, practical problem with a comprehensive empirical comparison against diverse strong baselines.  
- Strength: often achieves superior Macro-F1 and is significantly more efficient in training time and memory on largest datasets.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty—primarily an extension of [16] with engineering/theoretical refinements rather than a fundamentally new framework.  
- Weakness: inference/prediction time remains much slower than fast tree-based methods, limiting use in latency-sensitive settings.
