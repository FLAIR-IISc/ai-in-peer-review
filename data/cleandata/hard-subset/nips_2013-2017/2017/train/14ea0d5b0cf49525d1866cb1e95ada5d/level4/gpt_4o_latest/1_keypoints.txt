- Proposes a double sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass framework of [16], with theoretical analysis and empirical validation showing effectiveness on a text dataset with many classes.  
- Extensive experimental comparisons indicate competitive accuracy and reduced training time across datasets.  
- Tackles the important and practically relevant problem of extreme multiclass classification.  
- Heavy dependence on [16]; overall novelty is limited despite the two claimed contributions (double sampling and local Rademacher-based generalization bounds).  
- Method increases prediction time relative to alternatives, which may be problematic in real-world use despite faster training.  
- The interpretation of the new generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison to existing bounds.  
- Experimental setup mixes batch and online algorithms that have substantially different memory requirements, raising methodological concerns.
