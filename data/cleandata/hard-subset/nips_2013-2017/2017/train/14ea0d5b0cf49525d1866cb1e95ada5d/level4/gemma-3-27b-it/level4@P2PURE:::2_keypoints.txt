- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification aimed at problems with many classes.
- Uses a hypothesis h(x,y) scoring (input,class) pairs; loss measures fraction of incorrect classes ranked above the true class.
- Implements a feature transform φ((x,y)) to a p-dimensional space and learns a mapping R^p → scores.
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) by creating K-1 pairwise comparison examples per original data point.
- Notes that correlations among transformed examples invalidate many standard generalization bounds.
- Contribution 1: provides improved generalization analysis via new bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity for correlated examples.
- Contribution 2: proposes sampling techniques (class sampling with replacement and sampling a subset of comparisons) to rebalance classes and reduce training size.
- Claims the sampling procedures do not substantially degrade the derived generalization guarantees.
- Empirical results show competitive performance against strong baseline algorithms.
- Novelty primarily resides in the sampling/rebalancing technique; the basic reduction is from prior work (Joshi et al.).
- Paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3 that limits comparisons to k nearest class centroids.
- Would benefit from additional discussion/comparison of sample complexity bounds versus non-transformed multi-class generalization results.
