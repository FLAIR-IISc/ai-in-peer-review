Review- This paper proposes a double-sampling strategy to enhance the multiclass classification method in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their approach. Experiments on text datasets indicate that the method can handle multi-class problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors present extensive experimental comparisons against several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves strong performance with reduced training time on several datasets.
- The extreme classification problem is an important research direction with many practical applications.

Cons:
- The paper is largely built on [16]. Although it adds two main contributions—(1) the double-sampling strategy and (2) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the overall novelty is relatively limited.
- While the proposed approach reduces training time, its prediction time remains longer than that of other methods. In practice, prediction time may be more critical.

Comments:
- The interpretation of the generalization bound is unclear. How does this bound compare to those from other approaches?
- The experimental comparison mixes batch and online algorithms, which leads to inherently different memory usage profiles.
