- Proposes a reduction from multiclass to binary by learning a scorer on (input, class) pairs using a loss that counts incorrect classes ranked above the true class.
- Assumes a feature transform phi mapping (input, class) pairs into a p-dimensional feature space for learning the scoring function.
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) where each example yields K−1 pairwise transformed points, creating correlated training data.
- First contribution: improved generalization analysis for the correlated-data reduction, deriving tighter bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity than Joshi et al.
- Second contribution: introduces sampling techniques (class sampling with replacement and sampling a random subset of incorrect classes) to rebalance classes and reduce training size.
- Shows these sampling procedures have minimal effect on the derived generalization bounds.
- Empirical results demonstrate the proposed method is highly competitive with strong baselines.
- Main novelty is the sampling-based scalability improvements; the core reduction framework was previously introduced by Joshi et al.
- Weakness: no theoretical analysis of the Section 2.3 prediction algorithm that restricts comparisons to the k nearest-class centroids.
- Weakness: paper lacks a detailed discussion/comparison of the sample complexity bounds relative to standard (non-reduced) multiclass settings.
