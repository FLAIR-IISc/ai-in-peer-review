This paper introduces a double sampling strategy to enhance multiclass classification, building upon the approach presented in [16]. The authors support their method with both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation. Experiments on text datasets demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness in handling multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

Pros:
- The authors provide a thorough experimental comparison against several baseline methods. The results indicate that the proposed approach achieves strong performance with reduced training time across multiple datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important and practically relevant research area with numerous applications.

Cons:
- The paper is heavily reliant on the foundations laid in [16]. While the authors introduce two key improvements – a double sampling strategy and a novel generalization bound based on local Rademacher Complexities – the overall novelty is somewhat limited.
- Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it exhibits longer prediction times compared to other methods. Prediction time is often a more critical factor in practical applications.

Comments:
- The practical interpretation of the presented generalization bound is unclear. A comparison of this bound with existing bounds would be valuable.
- The experimental comparison includes both batch learning and online learning algorithms, leading to differing memory usage characteristics that are not fully addressed.
