- Proposes a double sampling strategy building on [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses showing effectiveness for large multiclass text classification.
- Comprehensive experiments against multiple baselines show high performance and shorter training times on several datasets.
- Addresses the important and practically relevant extreme multiclass classification problem.
- Work is heavily based on [16]; novelty is relatively thin despite the double sampling and new local Rademacher complexity bounds.
- Although training time is reduced, prediction time is longer than competing approaches, which is a significant practical drawback.
- The presented generalization bound is unclear and lacks comparison/context with bounds from other methods.
- Evaluation mixes batch and online algorithms with different memory profiles; memory usage should be reported as an additional metric.
- Overall, promising results but needs clearer exposition and more comprehensive evaluations to strengthen contributions.
