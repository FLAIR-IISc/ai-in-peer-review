This paper introduces a double sampling strategy to enhance multiclass classification, building upon the approach presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analysis to support their proposed method. Experiments on text datasets demonstrate the approach's effectiveness in handling multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes.

**Pros:**

- The authors perform comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods, demonstrating that the proposed approach achieves strong performance with reduced training time across multiple datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important and actively researched area with numerous practical applications.

**Cons:**

- The paper relies heavily on the foundation laid by [16]. While the authors present two key improvements – a double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher Complexities – the overall novelty is somewhat limited.
- Although the proposed approach reduces training time, it exhibits longer prediction times compared to other methods. Prediction time is often a more critical factor in practical applications.

**Comments:**

- The practical interpretation of the generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear. How does this bound compare to those of other approaches in terms of tightness or utility?
- The experiments include both batch learning and online learning algorithms, resulting in significantly different memory usage profiles. This makes direct comparisons of memory efficiency challenging and should be addressed.
