This paper presents a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach in [16]. The authors present both theoretical and empirical analysis of their proposed approach. Experiments conducted on text data sets show the proposed approach can handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods. Results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training time on several datasets. This is a significant contribution, as the extreme classification problem is an important direction of research and has many applications in practice.

However, the paper is mostly based on [16], and despite two main improvements - the proposed double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher Complexities - the novelty is relatively thin. The authors could have further discussed the implications of their improvements and how they advance the state-of-the-art.

Another limitation of the paper is that, despite the proposed approach shortening the training time, it still requires long prediction time compared with other approaches. In practice, prediction time is often more important, and the authors should have discussed this trade-off in more detail.

The generalization bound presented in the paper is unclear, and it would be helpful to provide more context on how it compares with other approaches. Additionally, the authors should have clarified the differences in memory usage between batch and online learning algorithms in their experiments.

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of extreme classification, but could benefit from further discussion on the implications of the proposed approach and its limitations.
