- Proposes a reduction from multiclass to binary for very large K using a feature map phi(x, class) and a loss counting fraction of incorrect classes scored higher than the true class.

- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) which creates K−1 transformed comparisons per example, leading to correlated training examples.

- First main contribution: improved generalization analysis via new bounds on the fractional Rademacher complexity for correlated transformed data.

- Second main contribution: sampling methods to rebalance class representation and reduce transformed dataset size by sampling classes with replacement and sub-sampling comparisons.

- Theoretical results show these sampling procedures do not drastically worsen generalization bounds.

- Empirical evaluation shows the proposed procedure is very competitive with several strong baselines.

- Novelty mainly lies in the sampling technique; the core reduction itself appeared in Joshi et al. (2015).

- Missing theoretical analysis of the prediction-time algorithm in Section 2.3 that restricts comparisons to the k nearest class centroids.

- Reviewer requests more discussion comparing the paper's sample complexity bounds to generalization bounds for the original (non-transformed) multiclass problem.
