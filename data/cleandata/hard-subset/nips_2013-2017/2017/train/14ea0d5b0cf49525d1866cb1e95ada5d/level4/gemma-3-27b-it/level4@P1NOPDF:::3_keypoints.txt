- Proposes a learning reduction for extreme classification (up to 100k classes).  
- Notes limitations of tree-based methods (suboptimal structures, error propagation) and label embeddings (fail when label matrix not low-rank).  
- Reduces multiclass to pairwise binary classification with a scalable sampling-based method and consistency guarantees for multiclass ERM.  
- Empirical results on five text datasets: outperforms competitors in accuracy/F1 for >30k classes, trains faster, uses much less memory, but predicts somewhat slower.  
- Introduces "Aggressive Double Sampling" with two parameters: class sampling frequency inversely proportional to empirical frequency (to cover rare classes) and number of uniformly drawn adversarial examples.  
- Uses a centroid-based heuristic at prediction time to limit pairwise evaluations by representing each class with its input-space average.  
- Theoretical analysis addresses dependencies and oversampling by decomposing dependent sums, using graph structure and Janson’s concentration inequalities; yields a biased finite-sample risk bound whose bias decreases linearly with training/resampling size.  
- Reviewer finds the analysis sound (not fully verified) and judges the method practical, memory- and compute-efficient, theoretically grounded, and valuable for practitioners.
