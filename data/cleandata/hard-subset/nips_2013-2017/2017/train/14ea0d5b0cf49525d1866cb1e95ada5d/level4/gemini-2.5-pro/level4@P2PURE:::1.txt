This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multi-class classification method presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical analysis and empirical validation for their approach. Experiments on text datasets show that the proposed method can effectively handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

**Pros:**
*   The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons against several baseline methods. The results demonstrate that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on multiple datasets.
*   The problem of extreme classification is an important research direction with many practical applications.

**Cons:**
*   The work is largely based on [16], and its novelty is therefore limited. While the paper introduces two main contributions—the double sampling strategy and a new generalization bound based on local Rademacher complexities—the improvements feel incremental.
*   Although the proposed approach shortens training time, it still requires a long prediction time compared to other methods. Prediction time is arguably more critical than training time in many practical applications.

**Comments:**
*   The interpretation of the generalization bound is unclear. How does this bound compare to those of competing approaches?
*   The experiments compare both batch and online learning algorithms. The authors should acknowledge that these methods have vastly different memory usage profiles, which should be considered when comparing their performance.
