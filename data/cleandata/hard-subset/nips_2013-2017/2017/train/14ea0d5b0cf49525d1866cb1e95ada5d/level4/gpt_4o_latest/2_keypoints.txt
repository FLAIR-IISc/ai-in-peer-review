- Proposes a reduction that converts large-scale multi-class classification into binary tasks via a score function on (input, class) pairs using a feature map phi and a scorer from R^p to scores.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015): for each (x,y) the reduction creates K−1 transformed comparison points, producing correlated training data.  
- Notes that the correlation in the transformed dataset invalidates many standard generalization bounds.  
- Main theoretical contribution: improved generalization analysis over Joshi et al. using novel bounds on fractional Rademacher complexity.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling strategies to rebalance class representation and reduce the number of transformed points (sampling classes with replacement and subsampling comparisons).  
- Proves (or argues) that the proposed sampling schemes do not significantly degrade the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance against several strong baselines.  
- Shortcoming: the paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the prediction strategy in Section 2.3, which limits comparisons to the k nearest class centroids.  
- Shortcoming: insufficient discussion of sample complexity bounds, especially relative to generalization bounds for the original non-transformed multi-class problem.
