- Proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach from [16], with theoretical and empirical analyses.  
- Experiments on text datasets show effectiveness for multiclass problems with many classes.  
- Thorough experimental comparisons with multiple baselines demonstrating high performance and shorter training times.  
- Tackles extreme classification, an important and practically relevant research direction.  
- Limited novelty since the work heavily builds on [16], with main additions being double sampling and new local Rademacher-based bounds.  
- Prediction time remains long despite reduced training time, which is critical in practical use.  
- Needs clarification and contextual comparison of the presented generalization bound with existing approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation should acknowledge and account for differing memory requirements of batch vs. online learning baselines.
