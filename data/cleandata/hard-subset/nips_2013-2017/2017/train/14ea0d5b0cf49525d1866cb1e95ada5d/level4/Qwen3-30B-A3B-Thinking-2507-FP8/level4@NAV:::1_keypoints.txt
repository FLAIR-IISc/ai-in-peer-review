- Introduces a double sampling strategy for multiclass classification built on [16], with theoretical analysis and empirical validation.  
- Experiments on text datasets demonstrate effectiveness for large-scale multiclass problems.  
- Strong experimental comparisons against multiple baselines show superior performance and reduced training time across datasets.  
- Positions work within the important, practically relevant area of extreme classification.  
- Heavy reliance on [16] means novelty is limited despite two contributions: the double sampling strategy and generalization bounds via local Rademacher complexities.  
- Prediction time is substantially longer than competing methods, which is problematic for real-world deployment where inference speed matters.  
- The paper does not clearly explain how the proposed generalization bound compares to existing bounds.  
- Experimental comparisons mix batch and online algorithms, producing inconsistent memory-usage patterns that need explicit discussion.
