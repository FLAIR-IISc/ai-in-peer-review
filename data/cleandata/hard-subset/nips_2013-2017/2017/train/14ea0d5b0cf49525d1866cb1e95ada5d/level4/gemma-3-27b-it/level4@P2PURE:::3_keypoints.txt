- Introduces a reduction for extreme multiclass classification (up to 100k classes) to pairwise binary classification.  
- Notes limitations of prior methods: inferred trees propagate errors, and label embeddings fail when label matrix isn’t low-rank.  
- Proposes a scalable sampling-based pairwise reduction with theoretical consistency to the original multiclass ERM.  
- Empirically outperforms competitors (accuracy/F1) on datasets with >30k classes, with faster training and much lower memory but slightly slower prediction.  
- “Aggressive Double Sampling” uses two parameters: inverse-frequency class sampling to preserve rare classes and uniform sampling of adversarial examples.  
- Prediction uses a centroid-based heuristic to select candidate classes and avoid generating all pairwise features.  
- Theoretical analysis handles dependent dyadic examples and oversampling via graph decomposition and Janson’s concentration inequalities.  
- Finite-sample risk bound is affected by oversampling ratios but biases decrease linearly with training/resampling size.  
- Reviewer finds the analysis plausible and rigorous in approach but did not verify detailed proofs.  
- Concludes the method is a valuable, practical, and theoretically grounded contribution to extreme classification.
