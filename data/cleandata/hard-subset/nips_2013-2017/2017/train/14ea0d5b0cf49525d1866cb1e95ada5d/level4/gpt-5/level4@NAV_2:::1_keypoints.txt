- Introduces a double sampling scheme to improve the multiclass-to-binary reduction of [16], with theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation.  
- Experiments on text datasets show the method scales to very large numbers of classes and often achieves competitive or superior performance with reduced training time.  
- Tackles the timely, practically important problem of extreme classification.  
- Novelty appears limited since the work builds closely on [16], adding mainly a double sampling strategy and new local Rademacher-based bounds.  
- Inference/prediction remains relatively slow compared to some alternatives, which may be the primary bottleneck in many applications.  
- The presented generalization bound is not clearly interpreted; unclear how it compares (rates, constants, assumptions) to competing bounds.  
- Experimental baselines mix batch and online methods, producing disparate memory footprints that need clearer normalization or discussion.
