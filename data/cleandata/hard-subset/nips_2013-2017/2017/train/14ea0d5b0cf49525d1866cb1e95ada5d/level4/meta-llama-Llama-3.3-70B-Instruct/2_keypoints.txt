- Proposes a reduction from multi-class to binary classification by mapping input-class pairs via a feature transform phi and learning a scoring function.
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) which generates K-1 transformed examples per input, leading to correlated training examples.
- Correlated transformed data invalidates many standard generalization bounds.
- Derives improved generalization bounds using new estimates of fractional Rademacher complexity to handle correlated examples.
- Introduces sampling techniques to rebalance class distributions and reduce the size of the transformed dataset.
- Samples each class with replacement to ensure roughly equal representation in the transformed data.
- Shows that comparing against a random subset of classes (rather than all K-1) does not substantially degrade generalization bounds.
- Empirical evaluation is competitive with several strong baselines.
- Novelty mainly in the sampling/rebalancing approach; the basic reduction mechanism is similar to prior work.
- Omits theoretical analysis of the prediction algorithm in Section 2.3 that restricts comparisons to the k nearest class centroids.
- Lacks deeper discussion and comparison of sample complexity bounds versus non-transformed multi-class generalization results.
