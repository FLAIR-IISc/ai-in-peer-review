This paper proposes a double sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification approach presented in [16]. The authors provide both theoretical and empirical analyses of their proposed approach. Experiments conducted on a text dataset demonstrate that the proposed approach can effectively handle multi-class classification problems with a large number of classes.

The paper has several strengths. The authors conduct comprehensive experimental comparisons with several baseline methods, and the results show that the proposed approach achieves high performance with shorter training times on several datasets. Furthermore, the extreme classification problem is an important direction of research with many practical applications.

However, there are also some limitations to the paper. While the authors build upon the work in [16] and introduce two main improvements – a double sampling strategy and new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities – the novelty of the paper is relatively thin. Additionally, although the proposed approach reduces training time, it still requires long prediction times compared to other approaches. In practice, prediction time is arguably more important.

Several comments and suggestions for improvement are also worth noting. Firstly, it is unclear how to interpret the generalization bound presented in the paper, and a comparison with other approaches would be helpful. Secondly, the methods presented in the experiments include both batch learning algorithms and online learning algorithms, which have significantly different memory usage profiles. A discussion of these differences and their implications would be beneficial.
