This paper proposes a double-sampling strategy to improve the multiclass classification method in [16], with both theoretical and empirical analyses. Experiments on text datasets suggest the approach scales to problems with a very large number of classes.

Pros:
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons against several baseline methods. The results indicate strong performance with reduced training time on multiple datasets.
- Extreme classification is an important research direction with many practical applications.

Cons:
- The work builds heavily on [16]. Although it introduces two main improvements—(i) the double-sampling strategy and (ii) new generalization bounds based on local Rademacher complexities—the overall novelty appears limited.
- While training time is reduced, prediction remains slower than competing approaches. In many practical settings, prediction latency is arguably more critical than training time.

Comments:
- The generalization bound is difficult to interpret. How should it be understood in practice, and how does it compare (both in form and tightness) to bounds from related methods?
- The experimental comparison mixes batch and online algorithms, which have inherently different memory requirements. A clearer discussion or normalization of memory usage would strengthen the empirical evaluation.
