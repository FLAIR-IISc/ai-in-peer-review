- Introduces a novel reduction from multi-class to binary classification for large K, scoring (input,class) pairs via a learned function over phi-transformed features.  
- Builds on Joshi et al. (2015) by generating comparisons between true and incorrect labels but addresses correlation among transformed examples.  
- Main theoretical contribution: new generalization bounds based on fractional Rademacher complexity tailored to the correlated transformed dataset.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: sampling strategies that rebalance class representation and reduce transformed dataset size (sampling classes with replacement and sampling a subset of K-1 comparisons).  
- Shows that the proposed sampling procedures preserve the derived generalization bounds.  
- Empirical results demonstrate competitive performance against established baselines.  
- Strength: compelling reduction plus theoretical analysis handling correlations in the reduction.  
- Suggestion: paper needs a theoretical analysis of the prediction procedure in Sec. 2.3 that restricts comparisons to k nearest class centroids.  
- Suggestion: provide more detailed sample complexity bounds and compare them to standard (non-transformed) multi-class generalization bounds.
