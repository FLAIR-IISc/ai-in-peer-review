- Introduces "Aggressive Double Sampling" to reduce extreme multiclass to binary classification using two sampling parameters addressing class imbalance and negative sampling.  
- First parameter re-balances training by sampling classes inversely to empirical frequency, protecting rare classes.  
- Second parameter uniformly samples adversarial negative classes to drastically cut the number of dyadic training pairs.  
- Provides a non-trivial theoretical analysis with finite-sample risk bounds handling dependent dyadic pairs and distribution shift from oversampling.  
- Shows consistency: bias from sampling decreases as training set size grows (proofs appear solid though not independently verified).  
- Uses centroid-based candidate selection at inference to avoid scoring all classes, making prediction tractable but slower than fastest tree methods.  
- Empirical results: superior accuracy and F1 on datasets with >30,000 classes, with much lower memory use and faster training than one-vs-all.  
- Overall: scalable, effective, and theoretically justified method; strong contribution recommended for acceptance.
