- Summary: Proposes dyadic reduction with (π, κ)-double sampling to convert multi-class to binary, with theoretical guarantees via local fractional Rademacher complexity and strong empirical results up to 100k classes.

- Strength: Tackles a meaningful, high-impact problem in large-scale classification with clear motivation versus OVA/tree/embedding methods.

- Strength: Provides strong theoretical foundations (Theorems 1–2) extending prior work using local fractional Rademacher complexity.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on LSHTC benchmarks (≤100k classes) showing large computational/memory gains and competitive accuracy/macro-F1 versus strong baselines.

- Strength: Includes practical implementation details, notably efficient prediction via candidate selection using class centroids.

- Weakness: Theoretical presentation is dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations and clearer links to implementation.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of sensitivity to sampling parameters (π, κ) and trade-offs between sampling rates and accuracy.

- Weakness: Baselines could be more comprehensive (missing some extreme classification methods like SLEEC, LEML).

- Weakness: Lacks discussion and examples of failure cases or settings where aggressive sampling may underperform.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel contribution that addresses computational complexity and class imbalance with sound theory and practical gains, though theory accessibility is a minor concern.

- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested additions of sampling-parameter sensitivity analysis and a brief discussion of potential failure cases.
