- Overall recommendation: promising paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: novel aggressive double sampling to reduce dyadic pair construction in extreme multi-class classification.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity and graph covering theory.  
- Scalability: demonstrates lower memory footprint and faster runtime on datasets up to 100,000 classes.  
- Presentation: well-written, clear notation and logical organization.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia showing consistent improvements over several baselines.  
- Missing baselines: lacks comparison to recent neural-embedding methods (e.g., FastText) and newer sparse extreme-classification techniques.  
- Ablation study needed: insufficient analysis of (π, κ)-DS—impact and sensitivity of sampling probabilities π and κ are unclear.  
- Feature mapping concern: joint example/class representation uses 10 hand-crafted features without strong justification or exploration of learned alternatives.  
- Candidate selection: introduced but not thoroughly evaluated for speed/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Practical use of theory: generalization bounds are strong but lack guidance on practical parameter choices and sample-size implications.  
- Experimental details: request repository link, clearer result visualization (e.g., error bars) and more exhaustive baseline comparisons.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., "even-though" → "even though") and clarify theorem notation (α and β).  
- Suggested additions: include an example applying the bounds to estimate required sample sizes and a deeper ablation of π/κ strategies.
