- Paper tackles large-scale multi-class classification by reducing to binary via a dyadic representation and proposes an "aggressive sampling" approach.  
- Core method: a (π, κ)-double sampling strategy that oversamples small classes, undersamples large ones, and limits dyadic examples per instance with κ.  
- Theoretical contribution: new generalization bounds using local fractional Rademacher complexity and proof that ERM remains consistent under the sampling scheme.  
- Empirical contribution: comprehensive experiments on WIKI-Small, WIKI-50K, and WIKI-100K showing strong runtime/memory efficiency and competitive predictive performance versus strong baselines.  
- Strength: problem is well-motivated with clear comparisons to tree-based, embedding-based, and dyadic methods.  
- Strength: theoretical results are technically solid and provide tighter guarantees than previous work.  
- Strength: experiments are thorough and demonstrate a good trade-off between efficiency and accuracy.  
- Weakness: theoretical exposition lacks intuitive explanation linking sampling strategy to practical gains, particularly the role of κ.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical sensitivity analysis of sampling parameters π and κ; no ablation study presented.  
- Weakness: related work does not clearly differentiate the proposed aggressive sampling from prior dyadic approaches (e.g., [16]).  
- Weakness: candidate selection using class centroids is under-explained and its impact/limitations are not analyzed.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant for large-scale classification, though clarity could be improved in some technical sections.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add a short explanation of aggressive sampling in the introduction.  
- Suggested revisions: include an ablation study varying κ and π to show sensitivity.  
- Suggested revisions: strengthen related work to clearly contrast with prior dyadic methods.  
- Suggested revisions: provide more intuition linking theoretical bounds to empirical performance.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify and analyze the candidate selection strategy and when it may fail.
