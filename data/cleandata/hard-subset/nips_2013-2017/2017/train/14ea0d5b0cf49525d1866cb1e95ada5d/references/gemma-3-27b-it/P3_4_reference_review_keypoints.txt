- Overall: Novel, practically motivated, and theoretically sound approach; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: Tackles computational intractability and class imbalance in extreme multi-class classification.  
- Positioning: Bridges label-embedding and dyadic approaches by adding a sampling strategy to dyadic reductions.  
- Core contribution: "Aggressive Double Sampling" combines class-balancing sampling with reduced dyadic example construction.  
- Theoretical basis: New generalization bounds using Local Rademacher complexity and graph covering arguments.  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on DMOZ and Wikipedia, competitive performance and improved runtime/memory.  
- Scalability: Demonstrated ability to handle datasets with ~100k classes — high practical relevance.  
- Novelty strength: Interplay between balancing and dependency-aware sampling is original and non-trivial.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous proof sketch and meaningful theorems on consistency.  
- Experimental strength: Well-conducted comparisons against OVA, M-SVM, FastXML, PD-Sparse.  
- Clarity weakness: Dense writing and abstract sections (fractional Rademacher, graph covering) need more intuition and examples.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lack of ablation/analysis for π and κ and missing justification for chosen values.  
- Feature mapping issue: φ is underspecified; more detail on features, alternatives, and optimality is needed.  
- Comparison gap: Exclusion of SLEEC and LEML is insufficiently justified; brief discussion of their limitations relative to this method is requested.  
- For acceptance: Addresses critical problem, provides novel and theoretically-backed solution, and shows practical scalability.  
- Against acceptance: Presentation clarity, hyperparameter analysis, and feature-mapping details require improvement.  
- Required revisions: Improve clarity (esp. Sections 2.1–2.2), add hyperparameter ablation, detail feature mapping φ, and justify exclusion of SLEEC/LEML.
