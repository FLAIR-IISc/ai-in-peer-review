- Proposes aggressive double sampling to reduce computational and statistical costs of dyadic multi-class-to-binary reduction.  
- Builds on prior sampling and dyadic representation work and situates contribution clearly in related literature.  
- Derives new generalization bounds for sampled dependent data using local Rademacher complexity.  
- Shows strong empirical performance: competitive or superior accuracy and much lower training time/memory versus multiple baselines, notably on WIKI-100K.  
- Addresses a practically important bottleneck in extreme multi-class classification.  
- Algorithm ((π, κ)-DS) is clearly described and code/datasets are provided for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical analysis (e.g., Theorem 2) is dense and relies on complex concepts, reducing accessibility.  
- Method performance depends on careful tuning of hyperparameters π and κ; sensitivity guidance is limited.  
- Feature mapping φ uses 10 features with limited justification and lacks ablation analysis.  
- Comparisons omit SLEEC and LEML; even a brief comparison would strengthen claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical relevance, novel effective solution, solid theoretical support, compelling experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex/inaccessible theory, hyperparameter tuning challenges, under-justified feature choices.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to clarify theory and expand discussion on hyperparameter selection and feature design.
