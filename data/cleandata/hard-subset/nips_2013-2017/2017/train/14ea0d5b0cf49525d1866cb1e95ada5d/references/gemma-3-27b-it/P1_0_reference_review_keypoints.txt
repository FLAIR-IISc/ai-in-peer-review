- Summary: Tackles large-scale multi-class classification by proposing "aggressive double sampling" to balance classes before dyadic example construction, with theoretical generalization bounds and experiments on DMOZ and Wikipedia.
- Relation to prior work: Clearly positions itself between label-embedding and binary-reduction approaches, distinguishing its sampling strategy and citing Rademacher complexity and graph covering connections.
- Novelty: The aggressive double sampling idea—balancing class distribution prior to dyadic construction—is a significant and effective contribution.
- Theoretical justification: Provides local Rademacher-based generalization bounds and handles inter-dependencies introduced by sampling.
- Scalability: Demonstrates substantially reduced memory and runtime versus OVA and M-SVM on large datasets.
- Empirical validation: Achieves competitive accuracy and MaF1 while improving speed and memory on large text corpora.
- Clear writing: Paper is generally well-written and organized with understandable technical detail.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance likely depends on (π, κ) choices; tuning details are relegated to supplementary material.
- Feature representation: Limited analysis of sensitivity to feature mappings and unclear rationale for using only 10 features.
- Limited comparisons: Lacks evaluation against more recent/deep learning-based extreme classification methods for a comprehensive benchmark.
- Candidate selection details: Description of candidate selection and choice of q is brief and its impact on performance is underexplored.
- Overly optimistic language: Claims like "highly competitive" should be qualified and quantified with clearer margins.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, proposes a novel theoretically sound scalable solution, and presents compelling empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: Concerns about hyperparameter sensitivity, limited feature analysis, and missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions in sampling strategy and theory; minor weaknesses do not substantially detract from quality.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: Expand hyperparameter tuning details in main text; analyze sensitivity to feature representations; add brief comparisons to recent state-of-the-art; elaborate on candidate selection and q choice; temper overly optimistic statements.
