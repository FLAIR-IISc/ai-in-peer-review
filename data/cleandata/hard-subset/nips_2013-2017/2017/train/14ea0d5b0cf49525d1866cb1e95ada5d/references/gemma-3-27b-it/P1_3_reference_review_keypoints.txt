- Tackles extreme multi-class classification, addressing computational cost and class imbalance in OVA approaches.  
- Proposes aggressive double sampling: oversample minority / subsample majority before constructing dyadic pairs, then reduce dyadic examples focusing on true-class pairs.  
- Provides theoretical generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity that account for induced distribution shift and pairwise dependency.  
- Empirically validated on large-scale text datasets (DMOZ, WIKI-100K), showing competitive accuracy, lower memory use, and faster runtime.  
- Positions work among label-space reduction and dyadic problem-space methods, improving scalability over prior dyadic approaches.  
- Novelty: combining pre-dyadic over/undersampling with targeted dyadic reduction to address imbalance and complexity.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis that supports the method despite sampling-induced dependencies.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably on WIKI-100K, and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: method likely sensitive to sampling hyperparameters (π, κ) with limited sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Weakness: feature mapping (φ) description is brief; more rationale and impact discussion needed.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent scalable multi-class methods beyond PD-Sparse.  
- Weakness: supplementary proofs are dense and could use more intuitive explanation for broader accessibility.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and practically useful contribution; minor issues do not outweigh merits.  
- Recommendation: Accept. Confidence: 8/10.
