- Method summary: Reduces extreme multiclass to binary via dyadic example–class pairs with double sampling (class-balanced π and κ negative classes) and joint feature map φ; optional q-centroid preselection at test time.

- Theoretical contribution: Derives generalization bounds for dependent dyads and for doubly-sampled setting using fractional/local Rademacher complexities (Theorems 1–2) with dependence on α, β, K, κ, M.

- Novelty claim: The combination of double sampling with dyadic reduction plus dependency-aware Rademacher analysis appears original.

- Theory critique: Bounds are plausible but lack nonasymptotic instantiated rates, interpretable scaling in terms of φ and κ, and empirical validation of tightness; no theory for candidate preselection.

- Method assessment: (π, κ)-DS is simple and practical and well-motivated for long-tailed labels; φ is only high-level described and a feature table is missing.

- Experimental strengths: Demonstrates scalability to 100K classes with favorable time/memory/accuracy trade-offs and provides released code.

- Experimental weaknesses: Evaluation limited to text, LSHTC conversion may bias results, missing ablations/sensitivity studies for π, κ, q and φ variants, and some strong baselines are absent.

- Clarity issues: Readable overall but suffers from typos, formatting glitches, incomplete references, missing φ details in main text, and unclear interplay between training sampling and test preselection.

- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem and advances theory for dependent reductions; impact would increase with broader modalities and deeper empirical analysis.

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles scalability/imbalance, provides nontrivial theoretical guarantees, offers a simple implementable method with code, and shows competitive large-scale results.

- Cons against acceptance: Narrow empirical scope and limited ablations, heuristic candidate selection without guarantees, underspecified implementation details, omitted comparisons, and uninstantiated theoretical guidance for hyperparameters.

- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to π and κ choices; computational implementation and misselection rates of q preselection; full φ feature definitions and portability beyond text; whether losses are reweighted in training or consistency is solely analytic.

- Overall assessment: Meets quality bar on theory and shows practical scalability; leans slightly positive due to theoretical novelty but is borderline if empirical breadth and ablation are heavily weighted.
