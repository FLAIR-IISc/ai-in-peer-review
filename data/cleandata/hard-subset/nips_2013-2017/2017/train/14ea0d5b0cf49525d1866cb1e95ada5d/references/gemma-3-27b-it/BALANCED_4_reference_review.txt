This paper addresses the challenging problem of large-scale multi-class classification, specifically focusing on the limitations of existing approaches like one-vs-all (OVA) and dyadic representations. The authors propose an aggressive double sampling strategy to reduce the computational burden and mitigate class imbalance issues. The core idea is to oversample minority classes and subsample majority classes before constructing dyadic examples, combined with theoretical analysis using local Rademacher complexity to guarantee consistency. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the drawbacks of existing methods. The proposed approach is a reasonable attempt to address the scalability and imbalance problems. The theoretical contribution, establishing generalization bounds for the sampled data, is valuable. The experimental evaluation on large datasets (DMOZ and Wikipedia) demonstrates competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, especially regarding runtime and memory usage.  The inclusion of detailed experimental setup and results, along with a clear presentation of the algorithm, strengthens the paper.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Large-scale multi-class classification is a significant challenge in machine learning.
* **Novel approach:** The aggressive double sampling strategy is a practical and intuitive way to tackle both scalability and imbalance issues.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The generalization bounds provided offer theoretical justification for the proposed method.
* **Strong experimental validation:** The results on large datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, particularly in terms of efficiency (runtime and memory).
* **Clear presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to understand the proposed method and the experimental results.
* **Comprehensive comparison:** The comparison with a variety of relevant baselines is thorough.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** The performance likely depends on the proper tuning of the sampling probabilities (π) and the number of candidate classes (κ). The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the sensitivity of the method to these hyperparameters and guidelines for their selection.  While Appendix B details hyperparameters, a more thorough analysis within the main body would be beneficial.
* **Feature mapping simplification:** The feature mapping (φ) described in Section 3.1 appears simplistic and potentially limiting. While justified for text data, it’s not clear how well it would generalize to other data types. The paper could acknowledge this limitation.
* **Limited novelty in theoretical analysis:**  The use of Rademacher complexity is a standard technique, and while the derivation for the sampled data is valuable, it doesn’t represent a significant breakthrough in theoretical understanding. A more in-depth discussion of the novel aspects of the theoretical contribution is needed.
* **Minor clarity issues:** Equation (6) is a restatement of (2) with the sampled data and doesn't provide new insight.  It could be removed or better contextualized. Similarly, the description of Algorithm 1's candidate selection could be more concise.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a practically important problem with a novel and well-motivated approach.
* The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed method.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the approach on large datasets.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The theoretical contribution, while sound, is not groundbreaking.
* The feature mapping used is potentially limiting, and the paper doesn’t fully address this.
* The sensitivity to hyperparameters could be a concern in practice.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of large-scale multi-class classification. The proposed aggressive double sampling strategy is a practical and effective approach, and the theoretical analysis provides a valuable justification. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Suggested revisions:**

* **Hyperparameter analysis:** Include a more detailed discussion on the sensitivity of the method to the sampling probabilities (π) and the number of candidate classes (κ), and provide guidelines for their selection.
* **Feature mapping discussion:** Acknowledge the limitations of the simplified feature mapping and discuss its potential impact on generalization to other data types.
* **Theoretical contribution clarification:** Elaborate on the novel aspects of the theoretical contribution and its significance.
* **Equation 6 removal/contextualization:** Either remove equation 6 or provide a clear explanation of its purpose beyond restating equation 2.
* **Algorithm 1 conciseness:**  Streamline the description of the candidate selection step in Algorithm 1.
