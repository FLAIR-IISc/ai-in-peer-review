- Tackles extreme multi-class classification inefficiencies (OVA) by proposing “aggressive double sampling” of the dyadic representation to balance classes and reduce computational cost.  
- Derives theoretical generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity and graph covering techniques.  
- Empirical results on large-scale text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) show improved performance and scalability.  
- Technically sound and novel combination of dyadic representations with aggressive sampling; strong theoretical foundation.  
- Proofs in the supplementary material are dense and require clearer, more detailed explanations.  
- Presentation suffers from dense notation and a convoluted algorithm description; links between theory and practical implications are not always clear.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: confined to text datasets, missing comparisons to some recent baselines, and lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (π and κ).  
- Significance: meaningful reductions in computational cost and memory for large-scale problems, but broader validation is needed to claim general applicability.  
- Strengths: addresses scalability, novel method, theoretical guarantees, demonstrated efficacy, clear motivation.  
- Weaknesses: clarity issues, limited experimental scope, and dense supplementary proofs.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: improve clarity and intuitive explanations, expand experimental comparisons and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and clarify proofs in the supplement.  
- Numerical ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10.
