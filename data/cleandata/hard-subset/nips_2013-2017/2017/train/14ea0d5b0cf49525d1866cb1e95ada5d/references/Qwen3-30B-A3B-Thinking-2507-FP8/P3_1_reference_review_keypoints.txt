- Paper proposes a dyadic multi-class-to-binary reduction with a (π, κ)-double-sampling that balances classes (πk ∝ 1/μk) and limits dyadic pairs per example (κ) to reduce imbalance and computation.  
- Theoretical guarantees: local fractional Rademacher complexity is used to derive generalization bounds and claim faster convergence despite sampling-induced distribution shift and dependent dyadic examples.  
- Empirical evaluation on large-scale text benchmarks (up to 100k classes) shows competitive accuracy with reduced memory and training time.  

- Strength: Introduces a novel theoretical analysis using local fractional Rademacher complexity to capture variance and tighter bounds.  
- Strength: Strong practical impact—good trade-off among accuracy, training time, and memory.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments across multiple large datasets and strong baselines.  
- Strength: Well-motivated candidate selection via class centroids to reduce prediction cost.  

- Weakness: Did not directly compare to recent strong baselines (e.g., SLEEC, LEML, PD-Sparse), weakening empirical claims.  
- Weakness: Sampling rule πk ∝ 1/μk is presented heuristically without solid theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters π and κ to show robustness or tuning guidance.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations (e.g., extremely imbalanced classes, classes with very few examples, train/test distribution shifts).  
- Weakness: The claimed “second-order” theoretical improvement may be overstated and appears a minor modification of prior bounds.  

- Suggestion: Add direct experimental comparisons including SLEEC and LEML (and PD-Sparse) for fairness.  
- Suggestion: Provide theoretical rationale for the πk ∝ 1/μk sampling choice (e.g., variance or risk minimization).  
- Suggestion: Include sensitivity analyses over κ and π to guide practitioners.  
- Suggestion: Clarify how the method avoids error propagation compared to tree-based methods.  
- Suggestion: Discuss implementation issues for classes with very few examples and extreme imbalance.  

- Overall: Strong, practically relevant paper with rigorous analysis and compelling results, but needs better comparisons and more justification/sensitivity study.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
