- Paper proposes aggressive double sampling (class balancing + dyadic reduction) for extreme multi-class classification.
- Main contributions: double sampling strategy and generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity.
- Empirical validation performed on large text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia).
- Method improves scalability (memory and runtime) and handles very large class counts where OVA/M-SVM fail.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and significant, using graph covering and local Rademacher complexity.
- Experimental results are competitive, though not a major accuracy leap; practical benefits are in scalability.
- Quality rated 8/10: strong technical foundations and reasonable experimental setup.
- Clarity rated 7/10: generally well-written but theoretical sections are dense and notation-heavy.
- Originality rated 8/10: novel combination of aggressive sampling, dyadic representation, and theoretical justification.
- Significance rated 8/10: practical value for extreme classification and likely applicability beyond text.
- Strength: clear problem definition and well-motivated approach.
- Strength: theoretical justification lends consistency to the method.
- Strength: creative and effective combination of techniques.
- Weakness: theoretical sections challenging for non-experts in Rademacher complexity.
- Weakness: limited ablation studies, especially on sampling parameters π and κ.
- Weakness: experimental setup and parameter choices should be clearer in the main paper.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on clarifying theoretical exposition and expanding ablation studies for camera-ready.
