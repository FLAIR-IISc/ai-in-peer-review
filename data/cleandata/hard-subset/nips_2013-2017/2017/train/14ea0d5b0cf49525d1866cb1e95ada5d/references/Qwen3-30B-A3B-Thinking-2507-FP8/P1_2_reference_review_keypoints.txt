- Proposes (π, κ)-double sampling to reduce large-scale multi-class to binary classification by oversampling small classes and subsampling large ones.
- Addresses OVA limitations: computational intractability with many classes and class imbalance in long-tailed distributions.
- Provides theoretical analysis using local fractional Rademacher complexity to obtain tighter, variance-aware consistency bounds.
- Evaluated on large text datasets (DMOZ, Wikipedia) with up to 100k categories, showing large computational gains while maintaining competitive performance.
- Strong practical motivation: tackles a real-world, large-scale problem relevant to text classification, recommendation, and tagging.
- Meaningful theoretical contribution: extends fractional Rademacher complexity analysis to a local (variance-sensitive) setting.
- Comprehensive empirical validation against multiple baselines (OVA, M-SVM, FastXML, PfastReXML, RecallTree, PD-Sparse).
- Clear explanation of why tree- and embedding-based methods struggle for extreme multi-class problems.
- Thoughtful candidate selection strategy for prediction that addresses a practical bottleneck.
- Lacks clear guidance and ablation studies for choosing hyperparameters π and κ; the optimality claim for πk ∝ 1/µk is under-validated.
- Incomplete empirical comparison: omission of SLEEC and LEML is justified but not demonstrated.
- Weak connection between theoretical bounds (Theorem 2) and observed empirical improvements; needs clearer explanation.
- Ambiguities in Algorithm 1 description (e.g., selection of κ classes) may hinder replication.
- Does not discuss behavior on extremely imbalanced datasets where a few classes dominate (e.g., 99%).
- Overall technical quality is high with rigorous theorems and thorough experiments, though some exposition could be clearer.
- Writing is generally clear and organized, but theoretical sections and notation could use more intuitive explanation.
- The approach is novel in applying aggressive sampling for long-tailed multi-class-to-binary reduction and in using local fractional Rademacher complexity.
- Significant practical impact: improves runtime and memory for problems with 100k+ classes while keeping competitive accuracy.
- Recommendation: accept — paper is theoretically sound, empirically effective, and practically relevant.
- Suggested improvements: add hyperparameter ablations for π and κ, include brief comparisons with SLEEC/LEML, clarify Algorithm 1, analyze extreme imbalance cases, and link Theorem 2 to experimental results.
