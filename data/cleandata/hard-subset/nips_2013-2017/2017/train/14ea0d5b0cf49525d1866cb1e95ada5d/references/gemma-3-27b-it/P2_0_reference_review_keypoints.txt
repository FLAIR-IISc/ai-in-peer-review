- Overall assessment: Novel, technically sound approach to extreme multi-class scaling; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: "Aggressive double sampling" within a dyadic representation to balance classes and reduce dyadic examples.  
- Theoretical result: New generalization bounds using local Rademacher complexity showing ERM consistency under the sampling bias.  
- Related work: Properly situated among tree-based, embedding-based, and dyadic-representation methods.  
- Strength — technical soundness: Rigorous analysis for sampled distributions and dependent variables, convincingly derived.  
- Strength — novelty: Clever combination of class balancing and dimensionality reduction via aggressive double sampling.  
- Strength — experiments: Large-scale text results (DMOZ, Wikipedia) show competitive accuracy and strong runtime/memory gains.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, clear notation, accessible explanations of complex theory.  
- Strength — significance: Meaningful scalability improvements for real-world extreme multi-class tasks.  
- Weakness — baselines: Lacks comparison to more recent methods, particularly deep-learning/embedding-based approaches.  
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis of how π (sampling probs) and κ (number of adversarial classes) affect performance.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Limited discussion on robustness when theoretical assumptions (e.g., i.i.d.) are violated in practice.  
- Weakness — candidate selection: Algorithm 1 (candidate selection) is under-explained; impact of choosing q nearest-centroid classes needs elaboration.  
- Decision rationale: Accept with minor revisions if authors add recent baselines, parameter sensitivity guidance, and more detail on candidate selection and assumptions.
