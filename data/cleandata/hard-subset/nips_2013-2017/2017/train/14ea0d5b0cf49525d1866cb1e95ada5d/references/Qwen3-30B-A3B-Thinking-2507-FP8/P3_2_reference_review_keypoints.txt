- Summary: Proposes (π, κ)-double sampling to reduce multi-class to binary by balancing classes and sampling κ negatives, improving efficiency while retaining accuracy up to 100K classes.

- Strength: Practical contribution — effectively addresses long-tailed distributions, reducing memory and training time with strong results on WIKI-100K.

- Strength: Theoretical novelty — uses local fractional Rademacher complexity and variance information for improved generalization bounds.

- Strength: Clear experimental design — comprehensive datasets and comparisons to multiple relevant baselines.

- Strength: Strong empirical results — best trade-off of time, memory, and predictive performance; only method feasible on largest dataset.

- Strength: Clear presentation — well-organized paper with consistent notation and logical flow.

- Weakness: Theoretical comparison lacking — does not clearly show how new bounds improve over [16] or provide direct rate comparisons.

- Weakness: Algorithmic ambiguity — Algorithm 1 contains unclear notation ("κ K"), hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness: Missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis — no study of how choices of π and κ affect performance.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent work — exclusion of SLEEC and LEML is asserted without supporting evidence.

- Weakness: Candidate selection details missing — unclear how class centroids, nearest-centroid, and q are computed.

- Weakness: Theoretical assumptions not justified — fractional chromatic number and proper fractional cover introduced without sufficient motivation.

- Weakness: Scalability beyond 100K unclear — no evidence on performance or limits past 100K classes.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound with rigorous analysis and well-designed experiments (4/5).

- Clarity assessment: Well-written and organized with clear contributions and supplementary details (5/5).

- Originality assessment: Novel aggressive double sampling and theoretical analysis distinguish the work (4/5).

- Significance assessment: Addresses an important real-world problem with practical benefits and likely adoption (5/5).

- Recommendations: Clarify Algorithm 1 notation, add π/κ sensitivity analysis, detail candidate selection (centroids, q), compare to SLEEC/LEML with evidence, directly compare theoretical bounds to [16], and discuss scaling beyond 100K.

- Overall assessment: Valuable and practical contribution with rigorous theory and convincing experiments; recommend acceptance despite minor clarity and comparison issues.

- Pros for acceptance: Practical significance, theoretical and empirical support, major computational efficiency gains, clear writing, competitive on challenging datasets.

- Cons for acceptance: Some unclear algorithmic details, missing hyperparameter sensitivity, unsubstantiated exclusions of prior methods, and limited recent-work comparisons.
