

This paper investigates query complexity in clustering with side information, where the side information is provided as a similarity matrix $W$ with entries $W_{ij}$ distributed as $f^+$ if $i,j$ belong to the same cluster and $f^-$ otherwise. The main result establishes that $\Theta(nk)$ queries are required without side information, whereas with side information, $O\left(k \log n / H^2(f^+ \| f^-)\right)$ queries suffice, where $H$ denotes the Hellinger distance. The authors further demonstrate that this bound is tight up to a $\log n$ factor.  

I find this a theoretically interesting contribution for several reasons. It closely relates to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), which has been extensively studied recently, yet it poses novel questions about how query capabilities alter the clustering threshold. The work demonstrates that incorporating side information can drastically reduce query complexity. While it is unsurprising that side information—potentially containing full cluster assignments—can significantly lower complexity, the paper's key insight lies in precisely quantifying the quality of the similarity matrix.
