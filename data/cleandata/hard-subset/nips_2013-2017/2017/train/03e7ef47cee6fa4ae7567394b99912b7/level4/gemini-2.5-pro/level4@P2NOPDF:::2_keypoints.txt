- Overall: Significant theoretical contributions but dense writing and presentation issues make correctness and novelty hard to verify, leading to an initial borderline recommendation.  
- Novelty/Prior Work: Claims of novelty are overstated due to omission of relevant literature (labeled/weighted SBMs and prior active learning in SBM).  
- Labeled/Weighted SBM: Generalizing SBM to arbitrary edge distributions is not new—related work on labeled/weighted SBMs was not cited.  
- Active Learning Prior Work: Active learning for community detection (e.g., Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) covers similar settings and should be acknowledged and compared.  
- Overstated Claims: Phrases like “first work” or “no systematic study” should be removed or rephrased given prior literature.  
- Ambiguous Formulation: Problem framework (e.g., minimax setup) is not clearly stated and must be made explicit.  
- Cluster Size Assumptions: It’s unclear whether results hold for arbitrary cluster size distributions, including highly imbalanced cases.  
- Theorem 2 Concerns: Theorem 2 appears to conflict with known SBM results (e.g., achievable clustering with Q=0 for small constant k); an unstated k→∞ assumption seems necessary and must be explicit.  
- Weak Experiments: Simulations only compare against trivial baselines and omit existing methods, limiting practical validation.  
- Poor Writing and Structure: Long, confusing phrasing, grammatical errors, and excessive length before stating main contributions impede readability.  
- Minor Issues: Clarify Monte Carlo vs. Las Vegas terminology and average query complexity, fix typos (Footnote 1), clarify adaptivity in “find Q subset,” avoid overstated claims in simulations, and fix small brackets in appendix math.  
- Post-Rebuttal: Recommendation raised to Accept after clarifying the relation to Gadde et al., but the paper still requires thorough revision—state all theorem assumptions explicitly (e.g., k large) and significantly improve proofreading and exposition.
