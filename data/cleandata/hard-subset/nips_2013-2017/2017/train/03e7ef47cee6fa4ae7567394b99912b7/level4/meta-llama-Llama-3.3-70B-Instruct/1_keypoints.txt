- Problem studied: query complexity of clustering when given side information in the form of a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).  
- Main theoretical result: without side information the query complexity is Θ(nk).  
- With side information the query complexity reduces to O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)), where H is the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-.  
- Tightness: the provided upper bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.  
- Connection to prior work: relates to the stochastic block model (SBM) and raises questions about how querying affects SBM-like threshold behavior.  
- Key insight: side information can substantially change query complexity, and the paper rigorously quantifies how the quality of the similarity matrix influences clustering efficiency.
