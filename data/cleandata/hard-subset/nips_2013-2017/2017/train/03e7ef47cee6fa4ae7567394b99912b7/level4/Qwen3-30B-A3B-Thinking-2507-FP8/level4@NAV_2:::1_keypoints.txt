- Studied query complexity of clustering given a similarity matrix as side information.  
- Showed that without side information, Θ(nk) queries are needed to recover the optimal clustering.  
- Proved that with side information (pairwise similarities from f+ for same-cluster and f- for different-cluster), query complexity becomes O(k log n / H²(f+||f-)).  
- Defined H as the Hellinger divergence and tied it directly to query complexity.  
- Demonstrated the upper bound is nearly tight (within an O(log n) factor).  
- Connects the problem to the stochastic block model (SBM) literature.  
- Analyzed how interactive queries change recovery thresholds in SBM-like settings.  
- Provided a precise characterization of how similarity matrix quality (via Hellinger divergence) impacts query efficiency, with implications for theory and practice.
