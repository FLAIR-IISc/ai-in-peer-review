

This paper establishes upper and lower bounds on the query complexity for clustering using noiseless pairwise comparisons, augmented by random side information per pair. While I recognize foundational aspects of the related literature, my incomplete familiarity makes full assessment of the contributions challenging. The paper's density further hindered thorough verification of the extended upper bound proof.

My overall assessment of both papers is that the technical contributions appear valuable if correct, yet the writing requires substantial improvement. Although content outweighs presentation, the numerous issues necessitate a borderline recommendation. A second review round to address suggestions would have been beneficial, though I understand this is prohibited for NIPS.

Regarding this paper, key relevant works are omitted:
- The generalization of the stochastic block model (SBM) beyond Bernoulli(p)/Bernoulli(q) to arbitrary distributions is not novel; searches for "labeled stochastic block model" or "weighted stochastic block model" yield multiple prior studies.
- Gadde et al. (ISIT 2016) study SBM with active learning via single-node queries. For fixed k and linear-size communities, this reduces to pairwise querying (via random sampling to identify cluster representatives). Their sublinear-sample upper bound appears to contradict our lower bound, likely due to our k→∞ assumption not stated in Theorem 2. Authors must remove/rephrase claims such as "initiate a rigorous theoretical study," "significantly generalizes," "first work," or "no systematic theoretical study."

Additional concerns:
- The minimax framework is implied but not explicitly stated. Clarify whether cluster size constraints exist (e.g., allowing k−1 singleton clusters).
- Theorem 2 is likely incorrect as stated. For k=O(1) (e.g., k=2), clustering may require zero queries even with Hellinger distance ~log(n)/n. The proof assumes k→∞—this must be declared in the theorem.
- The numerical section lacks meaningful comparison to existing methods, relying only on trivial baselines.
- Several unclear passages require revision (e.g., "assume, otherwise the clustering is done" and "theorem is already proved from the nk lower bound" without context).
- The manuscript contains pervasive grammatical errors, typos, and inconsistent punctuation (e.g., commas after "let," "we have," "since"; incorrect capitalization; misuse of "else," "And," "Or," "Plus"; footnote formatting).

Minor points:
- Abstract is overly verbose; the contributions are delayed.
- Terminology ("Monte Carlo," "Las Vegas") requires standardization; "average" query complexity must accompany "Las Vegas" on p.4.
- Footnote 1: Remove the extraneous "1-" after p=.
- Problem statement ("find Q ⊆ V×V") suggests non-adaptive algorithms.
- Disagree with "clearly exhibiting" in the Simulations section.
- Appendix contains undersized brackets.
