- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W, where Wij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and ~ f− otherwise.
- Main claim: without side information, Θ(nk) pairwise same-cluster queries are necessary.
- With side information, only O(k log n / H^2(f+‖f−)) queries suffice, with this bound tight up to a logarithmic factor.
- Relates to the stochastic block model (SBM) and raises natural questions about how allowing queries shifts recovery thresholds.
- Shows side information can dramatically reduce the number of required queries (extreme cases could fully reveal the clustering).
- Contribution: formalizes the usefulness of the similarity matrix via the Hellinger divergence.
