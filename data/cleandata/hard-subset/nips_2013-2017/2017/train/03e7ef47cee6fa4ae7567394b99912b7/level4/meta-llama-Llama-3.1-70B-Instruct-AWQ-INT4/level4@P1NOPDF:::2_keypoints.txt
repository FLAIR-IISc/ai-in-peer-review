- Two companion papers by same authors; this one studies noiseless pairwise queries with random side-information, the other studies noisy queries without side-information.  
- Paper provides upper and lower bounds on query complexity for clustering with side-information.  
- Reviewer somewhat unfamiliar with all related literature and found the paper very dense, making correctness hard to verify (especially long upper bound proof).  
- Contributions appear valuable if correct, but poor writing and many issues made the recommendation borderline.  
- Missing citations: prior work on labeled/weighted stochastic block models (generalizing Bernoulli(p)/Bernoulli(q)).  
- Missing citation: "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) and potential apparent contradiction with that paper’s sublinear upper bound.  
- Authors should remove/soften claims like “we initiate”, “first work”, “significantly generalizes”, etc., due to prior related work.  
- Model/problem presentation is unclear — minimax objective not explicitly stated and cluster size restrictions (e.g., very small clusters) are not clarified.  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect as stated; it likely assumes k → ∞ but this is not stated and may contradict known cases where Q=0 suffices.  
- Numerical experiments are weak: only trivial baselines, no comparisons to existing methods.  
- Many unclear or incomplete sentences (examples provided), reducing readability and correctness checking.  
- Paper contains numerous grammar, typo, and stylistic errors (capitalization, commas, articles, footnote punctuation) and needs thorough proofreading.  
- Abstract is too long and the paper spends ~3 pages before presenting contributions.  
- Terminology concerns: use of “Monte Carlo” and “Las Vegas” and missing mention of “average” query complexity when introducing Las Vegas.  
- Formatting/notation issues: e.g., Footnote 1’s “1-” after p=, and small brackets in the appendix.  
- Formal problem statement phrasing (“find Q subset of V x V”) suggests non-adaptive algorithms; clarify adaptivity.  
- Disagreement with phrasing “clearly exhibiting” in the Simulations section — wording should be revised.  
- Theorems and lemmas must state all conditions precisely (e.g., when results require sufficiently large k).  
- Post-author-feedback: reviewer updated to acceptance but insists on careful revision addressing all above points.  
- Specific grammar suggestions remain (avoid starting sentences with And/Or/Plus; revise “else/otherwise” usage and the sentence “assume, otherwise...”).
