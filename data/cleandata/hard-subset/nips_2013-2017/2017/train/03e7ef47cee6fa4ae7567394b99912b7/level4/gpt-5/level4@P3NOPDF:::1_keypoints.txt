- Paper studies query complexity for clustering with noisy similarity matrix W where entries follow f+ within clusters and f− across clusters, proving O(k log n / H^2(f+||f−)) queries suffice and Θ(nk) are needed without W, with a near-matching lower bound.  
- The problem is timely and well-motivated, bridging active clustering, statistical query complexity, and SBM-style inference.  
- Main results are clean and interpretable; dependence on Hellinger distance gives a natural measure of side-information quality and near-tight bounds are compelling.  
- Demonstrates how modestly informative side information can dramatically reduce query complexity, quantifying the tradeoff between W and queries.  
- Conceptual clarity: the f+/f− model is a natural, analytically tractable abstraction that generalizes common noise models.  
- Sharp results: striking contrast between Θ(nk) without side information and O(k log n / H^2) with it, with a nearly matching lower bound.  
- Metric choice: Hellinger distance is a principled, symmetric choice that yields tight information-theoretic bounds relating separation to queries needed.  
- Relation to SBM: closely related to stochastic block models, but novel in focusing on query complexity with side information.  
- Clarification needed on W assumptions: are W entries conditionally independent given clusters, and are f+ and f− known or must be estimated?  
- Oracle model question: what queries are allowed and are query responses noiseless; how do noisy queries affect dependence on H?  
- Cluster size conditions: do results assume balanced clusters or a minimum cluster size, and how do imbalanced clusters affect bounds?  
- Adaptivity: are the upper-bound queries adaptive or non-adaptive, and can non-adaptive schemes match the complexity (maybe with extra log factors)?  
- Computational complexity: are the algorithms polynomial-time with reasonable constants, or are some results purely information-theoretic?  
- Tightness detail: why is the lower bound loose by a log n factor—technical (e.g., union bound) or fundamental?  
- Suggestion: include concrete f+/f− examples (e.g., Gaussians, Betas) with explicit H^2 calculations to illustrate scaling.  
- Suggestion: discuss robustness to model misspecification and mild dependencies or contamination in W.  
- Suggestion: compare Hellinger-based bounds to other f-divergences (KL, TV) to show whether results are constants-robust or Hellinger-specific.  
- Suggestion: add simulations showing how query complexity changes with H^2, k, and n.  
- Suggestion: clarify extension to partially observed W (missing side information) and whether similar query savings hold.  
- Suggestion: more explicitly relate work to active learning and semi-supervised clustering literatures for broader context.  
- Clarity: state model assumptions, exact oracle, failure probability, and success criterion upfront.  
- Clarity: precisely list distributional assumptions (support, boundedness, moments, parametric vs nonparametric).  
- Clarity: separate information-theoretic guarantees from implementable algorithmic results.  
- Significance: the work meaningfully quantifies query savings from side information with near-tight bounds and clear dependence on Hellinger distance, making a relevant contribution to theory and practice.
