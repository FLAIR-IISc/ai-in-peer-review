- Analyzes query complexity for interactive clustering with a noisy similarity matrix W and pairwise oracle queries.
- Shows without side information Θ(nk) queries are needed; with W, gives an algorithm using O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries and a lower bound Ω(k^2 / H^2(f+||f-)), tight up to log n.
- Proposes a parameter-free Monte Carlo algorithm that adaptively estimates distributions and sample sizes, not needing k, f+, or f-.
- Identifies squared Hellinger divergence H^2(f+||f-) as the principal quantity measuring side-information quality.
- Frames the model as a generalization of the Stochastic Block Model, linking interactive queries to community-detection thresholds.
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental and practically motivated gap between heuristics and theoretical understanding.
- Strength: Provides strong, nearly-tight upper and lower bounds with rigorous, non-trivial proofs for adaptive queries.
- Strength: Algorithms are novel, efficient, practical, and parameter-free, with a reusable iterative-update technique.
- Strength: Elegant theoretical insight by using Hellinger distance to quantify information from side information.
- Weakness: Experimental validation is limited (only on Cora) and lacks comparisons to existing practical heuristics cited by the authors.
- Weakness: Lower bound requires H^2(f+||f-) ≤ 1/18; the paper should briefly justify this assumption and discuss other regimes.
- Weakness: A remaining log n gap between upper and lower bounds is left open.
- Quality: High technical quality; theoretical claims are well-supported and complete.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with helpful high-level descriptions despite dense proofs.
- Originality: Highly original—first tight characterization for this general side-information model.
- Significance: Results are significant for active learning, crowdsourcing, clustering, and SBM research communities.
- For acceptance: Fundamental question, near-optimal results, parameter-free algorithms, and high potential impact.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient experimental comparisons to established heuristics.
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
