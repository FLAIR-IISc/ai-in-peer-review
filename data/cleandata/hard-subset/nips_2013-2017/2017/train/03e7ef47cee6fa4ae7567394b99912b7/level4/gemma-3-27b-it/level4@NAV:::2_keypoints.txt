- Paper gives upper and lower bounds on query complexity for clustering with pairwise comparisons and random side-information.  
- Reviewer not fully familiar with all related work; paper density hindered thorough verification, especially the long upper-bound proof.  
- Contributions appear valuable if correct, but presentation and writing problems make the paper borderline; a revision cycle would help.  
- Missing citations: labeled/weighted stochastic block model literature and Gadde et al. (ISIT 2016) on SBM with active learning.  
- Authors should avoid overstating novelty (phrases like “we initiate,” “significantly generalizes,” “first work that…”).  
- Model and problem formulation need clearer presentation: state whether minimax bounds are targeted and whether cluster-size restrictions (e.g., imbalanced clusters) are allowed.  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect as stated; the proof assumes k → ∞ and this dependence should be explicit in the theorem.  
- For small k, the SBM may require no queries even with Hellinger distance scaling as log(n)/n, contradicting Theorem 2 as written.  
- Numerical experiments are weak: only trivial baselines used and no comparisons to existing methods.  
- Paper contains unclear sentences and phrasing that impede understanding (examples given).  
- Numerous grammatical errors, typos, and awkward wording throughout (capitalization, plurals, spacing, articles, commas); recommend thorough proofreading.  
- Style/terminology issues: abstract too long and delayed contributions; use “Monte Carlo”/“Las Vegas” terminology correctly and clarify “average” for Las Vegas; fix footnote and notation issues.  
- Formal statement “find Q subset of V x V” suggests a non-adaptive algorithm — clarify adaptivity.  
- Appendix formatting issues (e.g., bracket sizes) and other minor presentation flaws.  
- After author response, reviewer updated to accept but still urges careful revision, explicit k-dependence in results, and continued attention to writing and style comments.
