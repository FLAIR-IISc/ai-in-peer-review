- Problem setup: clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ for same-cluster pairs and f− for cross-cluster pairs.
- Main result: without side information Θ(nk) queries are necessary.
- With side information: only O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f−)) queries suffice.
- Tightness: the upper bound is shown to be tight up to a log n factor.
- Relevance: connects to the stochastic block model and asks the complementary question of how queries change statistical and computational thresholds.
- Impact: side information can dramatically reduce required queries.
- Quantification: the paper quantifies how the quality of W affects query complexity.
- Measure: the Hellinger distance between f+ and f− is used as a clean, interpretable signal-strength metric governing the query/sample trade-off.
