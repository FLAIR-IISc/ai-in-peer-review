This paper analyzes the query complexity of clustering with side information. The side information is given as a similarity matrix W, where each entry Wij is drawn from f+ if i and j are in the same cluster and from f− otherwise. The main result shows that, without side information, one needs Θ(nk) queries, whereas with side information only O((k log n)/H^2(f+ || f−)) queries are sufficient, where H denotes the Hellinger distance. They further establish that this bound is tight up to a log(n) factor.

I find this an interesting theoretical contribution for several reasons. It is closely connected to the stochastic block model literature, which has been very active in recent years, and it asks compelling questions about how permitting queries shifts the threshold. It demonstrates that side information can dramatically alter query complexity. While this is not entirely surprising—since the side information could, in the extreme, encode the full clustering—the paper identifies a principled way to assess the quality of the similarity matrix.
