- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information: n items, k unknown clusters, interactive same-cluster pairwise oracle, goal = recover optimal clustering with minimal queries.
- Main upper bound: query complexity reduced from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n · H^2(f+ || f−)), where H^2 is the squared Hellinger divergence between f+ and f−.
- Provides an information-theoretic lower bound that matches the upper bound up to a logarithmic factor and holds for randomized algorithms.
- Develops efficient algorithms that achieve the lower bound within an O(log n) factor.
- Technical arguments are sound and the paper gives a thorough analysis of query complexity.
- Paper is well written with clear, concise explanations of problem and results.
- Results are relevant to the NIPS community and have practical significance for clustering algorithms.
- Weakness: paper is dense and requires significant time to fully understand.
- Weakness: lacks sufficient context and background discussion of related work.
- Weakness: numerical experiments should compare the proposed methods to existing approaches.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to add more background, improve numerical comparisons, and increase accessibility.
