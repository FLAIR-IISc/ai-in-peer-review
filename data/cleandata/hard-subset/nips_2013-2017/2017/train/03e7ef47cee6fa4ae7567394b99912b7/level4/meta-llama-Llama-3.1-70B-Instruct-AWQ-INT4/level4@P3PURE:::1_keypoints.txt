- Studies query complexity for clustering when side information comes as a similarity matrix W with entries distributed as f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).
- Main theoretical result: without side information one needs Θ(nk) queries.
- With side information one can succeed with O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- The query upper bound is tight up to logarithmic factors.
- Work is closely related to the stochastic block model (SBM) literature.
- Analyzes how allowing queries changes the threshold for successful clustering.
- Demonstrates side information can drastically reduce query complexity (may contain near-complete cluster information).
- Contribution: provides a framework to quantify the quality/value of a similarity matrix for query-aided clustering.
