- Paper studies exact interactive clustering with noiseless same-cluster queries plus pairwise side information from unknown distributions f+ and f−, giving lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms.  
- Main theoretical result: side information can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to roughly Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) where H^2 is squared Hellinger divergence between f+ and f−.  
- Algorithms: a Monte Carlo method with ~O(min{nk, k log n/H^2}) queries and a Las Vegas method with O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n/H^2}) queries, achieving exact recovery w.h.p. or with probability 1.  
- Strength: tackles a timely, practical problem with clean information-theoretic analysis and near-matching upper/lower bounds.  
- Strength: parameter-free, computationally efficient algorithms that do not assume knowledge of k, f+, or f−.  
- Strength: lower-bound framework for interactive settings and conceptual link to SBM are potentially of independent interest.  
- Weakness: exposition is dense and theorem statements, assumptions, and asymptotic regimes need to be made precise.  
- Weakness: related work is incomplete—missing citations on labeled/weighted SBMs and active learning for SBM.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited, uses a single dataset, and lacks comparisons to existing baselines.  
- Weakness: some novelty claims are overplayed; language should be toned down.  
- Weakness: numerous grammatical and stylistic errors impede readability.  
- Missing citations: literature on labeled/weighted SBM and active learning in SBM (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016) should be cited and contrasted.  
- Modeling/clarity: explicitly state whether results are minimax, constraints on cluster sizes, and which asymptotic regimes (k fixed vs k→∞) are considered.  
- Comment on Theorem 2: as stated it may conflict with known zero-query SBM recovery for constant k in sparse regimes; clarify regime assumptions (e.g., k→∞) to avoid contradiction.  
- Empirical suggestions: add baselines (interactive/crowdsourcing heuristics, SBM-based methods) and evaluate sensitivity to k, imbalance, and H^2.  
- Writing fixes: many unclear sentences and grammar issues; tighten abstract and overall presentation, fix punctuation/capitalization/footnotes.  
- Minor technical/presentation notes: reconsider "Monte Carlo"/"Las Vegas" labels, check footnote typo, clarify adaptivity of queries, avoid overstated language in simulations, and fix appendix typesetting.  
- Pros for acceptance: important question, rigorous lower bounds, parameter-free practical algorithms, and nearly matching bounds.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete related work, imprecise theorem statements risking confusion with SBM literature, limited experiments, and major writing issues.  
- Post-author feedback required: thoroughly state conditions in every theorem/lemma, substantially revise prose and grammar, and expand/correct related work before final acceptance.
