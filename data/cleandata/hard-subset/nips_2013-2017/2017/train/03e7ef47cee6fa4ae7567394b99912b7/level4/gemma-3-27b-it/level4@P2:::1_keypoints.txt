- Main result: with side information query complexity falls from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f⁺ || f⁻)), nearly optimal up to a log factor.  
- Model: similarity matrix W whose entries follow f⁺ or f⁻ depending on same/different cluster, with Hellinger distance measuring similarity quality.  
- Connects to SBM and asks how active querying changes recovery thresholds in that model.  
- Provides parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k or the distributions f⁺, f⁻.  
- Develops a general framework for proving lower bounds in the interactive querying setting.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous, tight (up to log) guarantees on query complexity reduction.  
- Clear problem formulation focused on information-theoretic limits of interactive clustering.  
- Practical concern: limited discussion on how to estimate or use Hellinger distance in real-world data.  
- Experimental weakness: limited evaluation (single dataset) and insufficient comparisons to existing heuristics.  
- Proof presentation: some proof sketches in main text are high-level and require careful reading of the appendix; more intuition would help.  
- Related work discussion could be more nuanced, especially regarding recent results on clustering with noisy queries.  
- Overall assessment: paper is a strong theoretical contribution with significant strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
