- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information (pairwise oracle + noisy similarity matrix) and shows query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f⁺‖f⁻)).  
- Proves information-theoretic lower bounds and gives nearly matching, efficient, parameter-free algorithms for arbitrary discrete f⁺, f⁻.  
- Frames SBM as a special case (Bernoulli f⁺, f⁻) and extends analysis to general similarity distributions.  
- Overstates novelty: overlooks prior work on weighted/labeled SBMs and active learning in SBMs (e.g., Decelle et al., Gadde et al.).  
- Theorem 2’s lower bound is strong but requires k to be sufficiently large; this condition must be explicitly stated.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, only comparing to trivial baselines and missing comparisons to existing clustering-with-side-info methods.  
- Paper is technically dense but suffers from significant writing problems (grammatical errors, unclear phrasing, inconsistent capitalization).  
- The distinction between Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms is insufficiently clarified; “Las Vegas” results should state expected query complexity.  
- Formal problem statement misleadingly suggests non-adaptive queries; adaptivity of queries must be clarified.  
- Core technical contribution—dramatic reduction in queries using side information—is novel and well-justified.  
- Provides a valuable general framework for lower bounds in interactive settings, with connections to community detection thresholds.  
- Recommended revisions: correct novelty claims, state k-condition in Theorem 2, fix writing, clarify algorithm types, and expand experiments (compare to Mazumdar & Saha, Wang et al.).  
- Overall: valuable theoretical work but needs better attribution, clearer exposition, and stronger empirical validation before publication.
