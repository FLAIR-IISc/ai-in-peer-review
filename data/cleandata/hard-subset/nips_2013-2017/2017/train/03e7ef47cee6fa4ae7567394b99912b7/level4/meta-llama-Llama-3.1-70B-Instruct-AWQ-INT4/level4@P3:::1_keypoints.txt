- Problem: Studies query complexity for clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ or f- depending on same/different cluster.
- Main theoretical result: Without side information Θ(nk) queries are needed; with side information O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice, where H is Hellinger distance.
- Tightness: The query upper bound is nearly tight up to a log(n) factor.
- Relation to SBM: Model generalizes the planted partition / stochastic block model and connects to active questions in community detection.
- Lower bounds: Develops a generic information-theoretic framework for proving interactive clustering lower bounds with side information.
- Algorithms: Designs efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f- and proves near-optimality.
- Technique impact: Upper-bound techniques could enable parameter-free algorithms useful in practice.
- Experiments: Empirical results on the Cora dataset are encouraging and demonstrate algorithm effectiveness.
- Strengths summarized: Rigorous theory, generic lower-bound framework, efficient algorithms, SBM connection, and supportive experiments.
- Weaknesses: Assumes a perfect oracle; does not handle erroneous/noisy oracle answers.
- Weaknesses continued: Lacks detailed analysis of the time complexity of proposed algorithms.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance (Yes) with confidence level 5.
