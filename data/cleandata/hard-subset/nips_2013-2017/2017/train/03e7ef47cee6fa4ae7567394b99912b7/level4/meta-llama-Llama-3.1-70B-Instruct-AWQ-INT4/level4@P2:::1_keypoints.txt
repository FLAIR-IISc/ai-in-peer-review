- Problem setup: clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ if same-cluster and f- if different-cluster.
- Main theoretical result: without side information Θ(nk) queries are needed; with side information O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice, tight up to a log n factor.
- Key metric: Hellinger distance H(f+||f-) quantifies the quality of the similarity matrix and controls query complexity.
- Contribution: strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds for interactive clustering with side information.
- Algorithms: computationally efficient, parameter-free algorithms are proposed.
- Practical relevance: side information can drastically reduce query complexity; paper identifies how to assess similarity quality.
- Connection to prior work: model generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM) and links to community detection literature.
- Experiments: empirical results on a bibliographic dataset show the algorithms perform well; greedy strategy nearly matches the best strategy.
- Writing and organization: paper is well-written and provides a thorough theoretical analysis.
- Originality: new framework for clustering with side information and novel, practical algorithms.
- Significance: results impact community detection and SBM applications across domains.
- Weakness: paper could discuss implications for SBM and community detection in more detail.
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts and need clearer definitions.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed empirical/theoretical comparison to existing methods in terms of query complexity and accuracy.
