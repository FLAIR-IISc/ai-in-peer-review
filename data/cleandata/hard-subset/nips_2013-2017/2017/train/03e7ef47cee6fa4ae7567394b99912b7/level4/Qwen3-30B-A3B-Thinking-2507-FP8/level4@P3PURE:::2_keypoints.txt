- Paper addresses a relevant problem and claims valuable contributions, but writing is dense and needs substantial revision to verify technical claims.  
- Novelty is overstated: generalizing SBM to arbitrary edge distributions and labeled/weighted SBMs has prior work; remove/soften "first work" and similar claims.  
- Prior work (e.g., Gadde et al. 2016) overlaps and may contradict the paper’s lower bound; the authors must reconcile this and clarify implicit assumptions.  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect as stated—it implicitly requires k → ∞ and must state this (and be checked against regimes where Q=0 suffices).  
- Problem formulation lacks precision: minimax nature of bounds, cluster-size restrictions, and adaptivity vs. non-adaptivity (the "find Q subset" phrasing) must be clarified.  
- Several formal statements omit necessary asymptotic conditions (e.g., k → ∞); all theorems need precise, explicit hypotheses.  
- Exposition is unclear in places (missing/garbled phrases, incorrect references to bounds) and requires major rewriting for clarity.  
- Simulations are weak: they compare only to trivial baselines and omit existing methods; claims about uniqueness of implementable methods are unsupported.  
- Grammar and mechanics are poor throughout (typos, punctuation, misuse of words, sentence-starting conjunctions, footnote formatting), severely harming readability.  
- Minor fixes needed: shorten abstract, verify Monte Carlo/Las Vegas terminology and “average query complexity” definition, fix footnote errors and small appendix brackets.  
- Post-author update: authors addressed key technical concerns (e.g., Gadde contradiction, k → ∞), reviewer upgraded to accept, but insists on meticulous proofreading and precise theorem statements before publication.
