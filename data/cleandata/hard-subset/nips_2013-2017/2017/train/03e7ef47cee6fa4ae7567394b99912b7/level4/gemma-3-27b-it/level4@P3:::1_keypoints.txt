- Shows that adding a similarity matrix W reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), with H the Hellinger distance.  
- Proves this reduction is information-theoretically optimal up to a log(n) factor.  
- Frames interactive clustering with side information in the stochastic block model (SBM), leveraging known SBM results.  
- Bridges clustering and active learning, addressing scenarios with expensive labels.  
- Produces parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k, f+ or f-.  
- Provides rigorous information-theoretic lower and upper bounds on query complexity.  
- Novel contribution: first analysis of query complexity with side information in this setting.  
- Clear problem formulation motivated by practical tasks like entity resolution.  
- Algorithm description lacks a detailed walkthrough, small example, and clearer stopping criteria.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a single dataset and should be expanded across varied settings.  
- Needs more discussion of practical deployment issues (oracle cost, alternative query strategies).  
- Related work could better cover recent active-learning-for-graph-clustering papers (e.g., Gadde et al. 2016, Verroios et al. 2015).  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, rigorous proofs, parameter-free methods, and useful SBM connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation and insufficiently detailed algorithm exposition.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the algorithm and expand experiments.
