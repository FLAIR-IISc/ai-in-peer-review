

This paper investigates the query complexity of clustering when provided with side information in the form of a similarity matrix. The authors establish that without side information, Θ(nk) queries are required to recover the optimal clustering, whereas with side information (where pairwise similarity values follow distributions f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- for different-cluster pairs), the query complexity reduces to O(k log n / H²(f+||f-)), where H denotes the Hellinger divergence. They further demonstrate that this bound is nearly tight within an O(log n) factor.

I find this work to be theoretically significant for several reasons. It directly connects to the well-studied stochastic block model (SBM), which has been the focus of extensive research in recent years. The paper makes an important contribution by examining how the inclusion of interactive queries affects the recovery thresholds in such models. While the general idea that side information can reduce query complexity might seem intuitive, the paper provides a precise characterization of how the quality of the similarity matrix (quantified through the Hellinger divergence) impacts the query complexity. This precise characterization is valuable for both theoretical understanding and practical applications of interactive clustering methods.
