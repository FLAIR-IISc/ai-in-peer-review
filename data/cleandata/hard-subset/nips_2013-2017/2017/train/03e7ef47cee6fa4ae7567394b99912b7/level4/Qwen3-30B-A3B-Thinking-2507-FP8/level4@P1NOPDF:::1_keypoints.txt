- Studies query complexity in clustering with side information given by similarity matrix W sampled from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).
- Main theoretical result: without side information Θ(nk) queries are necessary.
- With similarity matrix, O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- Shows this upper bound is tight up to logarithmic factors in n.
- Quantifies how the quality of side information (via Hellinger distance) affects required queries, giving an information-theoretic metric.
- Builds on and is well-motivated by connections to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Technical approach is sound; Hellinger distance effectively captures intra- vs. inter-cluster distinguishability.
- Demonstrates that even modestly informative side information can yield substantial query savings.
- Tightness result enhances the practical relevance of the theoretical bound.
- Minor wording issue: phrase “can have the complete cluster information” is misleading — analysis applies to imperfect similarity matrices.
- Authors clarify the bound scales inversely with H^2, recovering O(k log n) for near-perfect side information.
- Overall assessment: strong, non-trivial theoretical contribution with rigorous analysis; recommended for acceptance.
