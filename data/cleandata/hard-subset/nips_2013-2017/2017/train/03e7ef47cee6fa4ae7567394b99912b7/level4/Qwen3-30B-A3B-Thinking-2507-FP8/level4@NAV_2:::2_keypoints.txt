- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of query complexity for clustering with side information, giving information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.
- Shows query complexity reduction from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f-)) when using a noisy similarity matrix, with H the Hellinger divergence.
- Formalizes interaction between side information and query complexity, a largely unexplored area.
- Model generalizes stochastic block model (SBM) by allowing arbitrary distributions f+ and f-.
- Novelty is overstated: related work on labeled/weighted SBM and active learning for community detection (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016) is not sufficiently cited.
- Technical results are strong and sound, but presentation is dense and writing quality is poor (grammar, punctuation, unclear phrasing).
- Theorem 2 needs clarification: lower-bound assumptions (particularly k → ∞) should be explicitly stated to avoid contradictions for fixed k.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and compares only to trivial baselines (e.g., Cora results lack comparison to existing methods).
- Strengths: strong theoretical contributions, clear query-complexity characterization, and parameter-free algorithms not needing k, f+, or f-.
- Weaknesses: overclaimed novelty, unclear model assumptions (minimax, cluster sizes), grammatical issues, and weak experiments.
- Recommended revisions: cite relevant generalized SBM literature, clarify theorem assumptions (dependence on k), improve writing, expand experiments, and explicitly state all assumptions in theorems.
