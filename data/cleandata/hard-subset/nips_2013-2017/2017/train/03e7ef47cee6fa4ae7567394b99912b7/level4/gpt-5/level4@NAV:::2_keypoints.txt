- Paper derives upper and lower query-complexity bounds for clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons plus random side information, but novelty is unclear given related literature.
- Exposition is very dense and long proofs (especially the upper-bound) are hard to verify within review time.
- Several closely related works are missing from the literature review, notably labeled/weighted stochastic block model papers.
- Missing citation and discussion of “Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models” (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016), which has a related query model and apparent implications for the results.
- Some novelty claims are overstated (phrases like “we initiate,” “first work,” “significantly generalizes”) and should be revised or removed.
- Problem formulation is not stated explicitly enough (is this a minimax analysis?) and constraints on cluster sizes are unclear.
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect or incompletely stated — it seems to implicitly require k → ∞ but this is not declared in the theorem statement.
- Empirical evaluation is weak: no comparisons to prior methods and only trivial baselines are used.
- Several sentences and phrases are unclear or confusing and need rewriting for clarity.
- Manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors, typos, punctuation and formatting issues and requires careful proofreading.
- Smaller issues: abstract is too long, unclear use of “Monte Carlo/Las Vegas,” ambiguous footnotes and formal statement suggesting non-adaptivity, small parentheses in appendix, etc.
- After author feedback the reviewer raised recommendation to accept but insists on precise theorem statements and thorough revision addressing the above points.
