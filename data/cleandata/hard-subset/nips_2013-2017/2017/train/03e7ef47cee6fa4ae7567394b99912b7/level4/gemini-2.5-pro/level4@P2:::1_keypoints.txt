- Presents a theoretical study of interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries augmented by noisy side-information given as a similarity matrix drawn from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).  
- Main upper bound: with side information, query complexity reduces to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)).  
- Complementary lower bound: nearly matching information-theoretic bound Ω(k² / H²(f+||f-)), showing near-optimality.  
- Algorithms are efficient and parameter-free, operating without knowledge of k, f+, or f- and estimating distributions on the fly.  
- Strong conceptual contribution explaining the gap between theoretical Ω(nk) bounds and practical success of similarity-based heuristics via Hellinger divergence.  
- Theoretical narrative is complete: provides both algorithmic upper bounds and hard lower bounds in an adaptive interactive setting.  
- Connects and generalizes the Stochastic Block Model to arbitrary distributions, raising new questions about active queries in community detection.  
- Iterative-update technique for distribution estimation is clever and practically valuable.  
- Remaining log(n) factor between upper and lower bounds is a gap worth discussing—unclear if artifact of analysis or fundamental.  
- Experimental validation is limited (single real-world dataset Cora); synthetic experiments varying n, k, H² would better corroborate theory.  
- Algorithm analysis assumes f+ and f- are discrete with finite support; extending to continuous or large/unbounded support needs discussion.  
- Paper is high quality, well-written, original, and significant; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses, making it a strong NIPS submission.
