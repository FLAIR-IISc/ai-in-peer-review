- Problem setup: studies query complexity of clustering with side information W, where W_ij ~ f+ if i,j same cluster and ~ f- otherwise.  
- Baseline: clustering without side information requires Theta(nk) queries.  
- Main upper bound: with side information, O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance.  
- Tightness: the upper bound is tight up to a log(n) factor.  
- Relation to prior work: connects to the Stochastic Block Model and contrasts active querying with passive observation for recovery thresholds.  
- Contribution: formalizes the notion of similarity-matrix quality and precisely characterizes its effect on query complexity.  
- Significance: demonstrates that side information can drastically reduce query complexity (though trivial extreme cases exist).
