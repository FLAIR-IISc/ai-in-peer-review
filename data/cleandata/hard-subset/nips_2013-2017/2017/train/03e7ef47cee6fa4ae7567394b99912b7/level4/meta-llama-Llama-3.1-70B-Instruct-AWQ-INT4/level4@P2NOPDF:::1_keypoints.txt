- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).  
- Main result: without side information Θ(nk) queries are required.  
- With side information, only O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice (H is Hellinger distance).  
- The upper bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.  
- The work links query-complexity clustering to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) literature.  
- Raises questions on how query availability affects clustering success thresholds.  
- Shows that side information can substantially reduce query complexity, emphasizing the need to assess similarity-matrix quality.  
- Provides a framework to evaluate the usefulness of side information beyond a theoretical observation.
