- Problem setup: Studies query complexity for clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f^+ for intra-cluster pairs and f^- for inter-cluster pairs.  
- Main upper/lower bounds: Shows Θ(nk) queries are necessary without side information and with side information O(k log n / H^2(f^+‖f^-)) queries suffice (H = Hellinger distance).  
- Tightness: The provided bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects closely to the Stochastic Block Model while asking new questions about how query access changes clustering thresholds.  
- Impact of side information: Demonstrates that good side information can drastically reduce required query complexity.  
- Key insight: Precisely quantifies the quality of the similarity matrix in terms of Hellinger distance to characterize query complexity.
