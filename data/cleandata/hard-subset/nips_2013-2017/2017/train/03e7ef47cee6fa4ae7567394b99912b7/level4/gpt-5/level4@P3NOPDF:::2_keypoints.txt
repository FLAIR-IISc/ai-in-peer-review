- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons plus random side information, giving upper/lower bounds and Monte Carlo/Las Vegas variants; reviewer found proofs dense and partly unverifiable but updated to accept after clarifications.
- Claimed novelty is overstated; related models like labeled/weighted SBMs exist and must be cited and positioned against these results.
- Active learning work (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016) is related; node-label and pairwise queries can simulate each other in some regimes and this relationship must be clarified.
- Avoid broad claims like “first work” or “we initiate”; state novelty precisely and conservatively.
- The paper should explicitly state whether it studies minimax query complexity and clearly define the adversarial class and any cluster-size constraints (e.g., balance or minimum size).
- Theorem 2 appears problematic for fixed k (e.g., k=2) and may implicitly require k → ∞; this dependency must be stated and checked against known Q = 0 regimes.
- The adaptivity of queries is unclear—state whether queries are chosen non-adaptively or adaptively based on prior answers and side information.
- Define Monte Carlo and Las Vegas variants precisely and consistently, explicitly connecting Las Vegas to average query complexity when introduced.
- Numerical experiments compare only to trivial baselines; either strengthen comparisons with existing spectral/likelihood methods or justify the chosen baselines and experimental insights.
- Notation and footnotes contain errors (e.g., Footnote 1, mismatched brackets); standardize and fix typos and formatting.
- The paper contains many grammatical and stylistic errors (articles, plurals, comma usage, sentence starts like “And/Or/Plus”); perform a thorough language edit.
- Revise unclear phrases and sentences that appear incomplete or misleading (examples given in the review).
- The abstract is too long and the main contributions are stated too late; tighten the abstract and present main results and assumptions earlier.
- Ensure all theorems and lemmas are self-contained with explicit conditions (e.g., whether k is fixed or growing, distributional and side-information assumptions).
- Soften overstated language in the Simulations section (e.g., “Clearly exhibiting”) or provide stronger justification.
- Fix visual issues in the appendix (small brackets/delimiters) for readability.
- After author feedback, reviewer updated recommendation to accept but insists final version must state assumptions precisely in theorem statements and not only in proofs.
- Overall: contributions are potentially significant, but acceptance is conditional on correcting novelty claims/citations, clarifying model and theorems, improving experiments, and substantially improving clarity and grammar.
