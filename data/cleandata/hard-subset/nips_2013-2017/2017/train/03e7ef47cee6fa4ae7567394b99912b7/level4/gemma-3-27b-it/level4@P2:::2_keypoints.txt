- Presents information-theoretic upper and lower bounds on query complexity for interactive clustering with pairwise comparisons and random side-information.  
- Proposes algorithms that purportedly achieve near-optimal query complexity close to the theoretical lower bounds.  
- Claims novelty by generalizing SBM to arbitrary pairwise-distribution models, but similar labeled/weighted SBM variants exist and aren’t cited.  
- Fails to cite key related work (e.g., generalized SBMs and Gadde et al. ISIT 2016 on active learning for community detection).  
- Paper statements asserting novelty or being the first theoretical study in this area are overstated and should be revised.  
- Model and problem formulation lack clarity: unclear whether results are minimax and whether arbitrary/unbalanced cluster sizes are allowed.  
- Theorem 2 appears potentially incorrect or at least underspecified—its dependence on k (number of clusters) needs explicit statement and justification.  
- For small fixed k (e.g., k=2) clustering may be possible without queries under some distributional separations, contradicting current theorem wording.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: uses trivial baselines and lacks comparisons with relevant existing methods.  
- Numerous unclear/awkward phrasings and pervasive grammatical, typographical, and stylistic errors hinder readability.  
- Reviewer’s overall assessment: borderline accept contingent on substantial revisions to clarity, correctness, literature engagement, and experiments.  
- Required revisions: thorough proofreading and rewriting, add missing related work citations, clarify/repair Theorem 2 assumptions and proofs, and strengthen empirical comparisons and baselines.  
- Authors’ response addressed some points, but major issues on writing quality and literature coverage remain unresolved.
