- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with noiseless pairwise oracle plus random side information and gives information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds showing large query reduction with side information.  
- Contributions potentially valuable if technical results are correct, but manuscript is dense and proofs (especially upper bound) are involved.  
- Major concern: poor positioning relative to prior work; several relevant papers (e.g., labeled/weighted SBM literature) are overlooked.  
- Major concern: active-learning variants of SBM have been studied (e.g., Gadde et al. 2016), so broad novelty claims should be rephrased to reflect specific contributions.  
- Model definition is imprecise: unclear if results are minimax, what cluster-size assumptions hold (e.g., highly imbalanced clusters), and the formal statement Q ⊆ V×V suggests non-adaptivity contradicting the interactive setting.  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect or imprecisely stated and may implicitly require k → ∞; all assumptions must be explicitly stated to avoid contradiction with known Q=0 recovery results.  
- Experiments are limited and compare only to simple baselines; stronger comparisons to existing methods are needed to demonstrate practical value.  
- Writing and presentation are poor: numerous grammatical errors, typos, awkward phrasing, misplaced commas, and overly long abstract/introduction impede readability.  
- Theorems and lemmas must state all necessary conditions (e.g., dependencies on k) directly in statements, not only in proofs.  
- Minor points: clarify "Monte Carlo" vs "Las Vegas" usage and that Las Vegas refers to average query complexity; fix Footnote 1 typo and small brackets in appendix.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed some concerns and reviewer updated recommendation to acceptance, but a careful revision for clarity, correctness, and presentation is strongly urged.
