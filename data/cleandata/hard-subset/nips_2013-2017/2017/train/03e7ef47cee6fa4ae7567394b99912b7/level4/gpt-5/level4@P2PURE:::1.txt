This paper investigates the query complexity of clustering when side information is available. The side information is given as a similarity matrix W, with entries Wij drawn from f+ if i and j are in the same cluster and from f− if they are in different clusters. The main result shows that Θ(nk) queries are required without side information, whereas with side information only O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f−)) queries are sufficient, where H denotes the Hellinger distance; this bound is tight up to a log n factor.

I find this to be an interesting theoretical contribution for several reasons. It is closely related to the stochastic block model (SBM), which has seen extensive recent work, yet it raises compelling questions about how allowing queries shifts the standard thresholds. The results demonstrate that adding side information can drastically reduce query complexity. While this is not entirely surprising—since the side information could, in principle, encode the full clustering—the paper provides a principled way to assess the quality of the similarity matrix.
