- Paper studies clustering with noiseless pairwise same-cluster queries plus side-information from distributions f+ / f−, gives information-theoretic lower bounds, nearly-matching upper bounds, and parameter-free algorithms; frames connection to SBM with arbitrary edge-label distributions.  
- Prior related work (e.g., labeled/weighted SBM, active learning for SBM such as Gadde et al. ISIT 2016) exists, so novelty and “first” claims should be toned down.  
- Strength: Tackles an important, timely problem quantifying how side information reduces query complexity.  
- Strength: Provides new lower bounds and efficient, parameter-free algorithms with near-matching upper bounds (up to log factors).  
- Strength: Highlights a meaningful connection to community detection/SBM and suggests directions for future work.  
- Weakness: Model/setup is unclear—minimax perspective, admissible instance class, and allowed cluster-size imbalance are not explicitly specified.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears problematic or misstated—may contradict known Q=0 exact recovery regimes for fixed small k; required parameter regimes (asymptotics in k, n, H2) must be explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: Numerical experiments compare only to trivial baselines; must compare to existing SBM/ER heuristics or justify why such comparisons are infeasible.  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense, proofs (especially upper bound) are long and hard to check; theorem statements and assumptions need greater precision.  
- Presentation/terminology issues: abstract too long; delayed statement of contributions; clarify Las Vegas/average query complexity; fix footnote, adaptivity wording, and bracket/typography problems.  
- Language/style issues: numerous grammatical, punctuation, capitalization, and phrasing errors—requires careful line-by-line proofreading.  
- Pros for acceptance: practically motivated problem, new theoretical results, and a general side-information model that could influence future work.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing citations/related work, over-strong novelty claims, unclear parameter regimes in theorems, weak empirical section, and poor writing.  
- Post-author feedback: reviewer updated to accept after clarifications but requests explicit regime statements in theorems, fixes to problematic phrasing (Else/And/Or/Plus, “assume, otherwise…”), and rewording to acknowledge prior labeled/weighted SBM and active learning work.
