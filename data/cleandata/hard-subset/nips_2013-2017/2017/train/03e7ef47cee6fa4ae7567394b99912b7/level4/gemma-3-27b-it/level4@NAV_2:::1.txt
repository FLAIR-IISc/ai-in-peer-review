This paper studies the query complexity of clustering with side information, specifically a similarity matrix *W* where *W<sub>ij</sub>* is distributed according to *f<sup>+</sup>* if *i* and *j* belong to the same cluster, and *f<sup>-</sup>* otherwise. The central contribution is demonstrating that, while Θ(*nk*) queries are required without side information, only O(*k<sup>2</sup> log *n* / H<sup>2</sup>(*f<sup>+</sup>*||*f<sup>-</sup>*)) queries suffice with side information, where *H* denotes the Hellinger distance. This bound is also shown to be information-theoretically optimal up to a factor of O(log *n*).

This is a compelling theoretical work with several noteworthy aspects. The problem is well-motivated by applications like entity resolution and active learning, and the connection to the stochastic block model (SBM) is clearly articulated. Recent work on SBM has been extensive, and this paper thoughtfully investigates how the ability to perform queries alters the recovery threshold. Demonstrating the substantial reduction in query complexity achievable with side information is a significant result; while the benefit of side information is intuitive, the paper provides a rigorous quantification of this improvement and characterizes the role of the similarity matrix quality via the Hellinger divergence.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Results:** The paper provides tight upper and lower bounds on query complexity, establishing a clear understanding of the benefits of side information.
*   **Clear Motivation and Connection to Existing Work:** The problem is well-motivated, and the connection to SBM and active learning is clearly explained. The discussion of related work is comprehensive.
*   **Novelty:** The interactive clustering setting with side information, combined with the rigorous theoretical analysis, constitutes a novel contribution.
*   **Parameter-Free Algorithms:** The algorithms developed are parameter-free, making them practical and appealing.
*   **Generalizability:**  The analysis extends to both discrete and continuous distributions *f<sup>+</sup>* and *f<sup>-</sup>*.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity of Proofs:** While the results are strong, the proofs appear dense and may be challenging to fully verify in a review setting. (Further verification would require detailed examination of the supplementary material).
*   **Practical Considerations:** The paper focuses on theoretical guarantees. A discussion of the practical implications of the Hellinger divergence threshold and the algorithm's performance on real-world datasets beyond the single example provided would be beneficial.
*   **Potential for Improvement in Lower Bounds:**  The authors acknowledge a gap between the upper and lower bounds of O(log *n*), and future work could focus on closing this gap.
* **Limited Exploration of Error Handling:** The paper assumes a perfect oracle. Exploring scenarios with noisy oracle responses, as mentioned briefly, would enhance the practical relevance.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in understanding the query complexity of clustering with side information.
*   The results are rigorously proven and are of high quality.
*   The connection to SBM and other related areas is well-established.
*   The parameter-free algorithms are a practical strength.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The density of the proofs may make it difficult for reviewers to fully assess the technical correctness.
*   The paper lacks a more extensive empirical evaluation.
*   The gap between the upper and lower bounds, while acknowledged, represents a potential area for improvement.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and is worthy of acceptance. The theoretical results are significant, the problem is well-motivated, and the connection to related work is clearly articulated. While the proofs are dense and a more extensive empirical evaluation would be beneficial, the strengths of the paper outweigh these minor weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, potentially focusing on providing more intuition behind the key theoretical arguments and expanding the experimental validation.
