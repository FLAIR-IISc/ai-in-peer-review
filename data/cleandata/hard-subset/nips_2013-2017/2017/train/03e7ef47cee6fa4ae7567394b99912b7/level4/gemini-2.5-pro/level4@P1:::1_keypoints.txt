- Problem setting: studies interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries plus a noisy similarity matrix W, where Wij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and ~ f- otherwise.
- Baseline: without side information, exact recovery requires Θ(nk) queries.
- Main upper bound: with W, query complexity reduced to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)).
- Main lower bound: information-theoretic Ω(k² / H²(f+||f-)), showing near-optimality up to log factors.
- Algorithmic contribution: a parameter-free, adaptive algorithm that needs no knowledge of k, f+, or f-.
- Key metric: identifies squared Hellinger distance H²(f+||f-) as the natural measure of side-information quality.
- Theoretical depth: nearly tight upper and lower bounds with a novel lower-bound technique for interactive settings.
- Connection to related work: frames the Stochastic Block Model as a special case, opening links to community-detection thresholds.
- Experiments: preliminary, tested only on a single dataset (Cora), limiting empirical validation.
- Model assumptions: assumes a perfect (noise-free) oracle and single f+/f- distributions for all pairs; extensions to oracle noise or cluster-specific similarities are not explored.
- Clarity and writing: paper is well-written, organized, and clearly motivates the results.
- Overall judgment: high quality, original, and significant theoretical contribution; strong recommendation for acceptance.
