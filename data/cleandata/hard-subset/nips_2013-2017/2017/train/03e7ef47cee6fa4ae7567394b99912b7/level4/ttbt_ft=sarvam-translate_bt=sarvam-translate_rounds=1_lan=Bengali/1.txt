This paper discusses the query complexity of clustering with "side information." "Side information" is given as a matrix W, where the Wij entries are distributed as f+ or f-, depending on whether i,j belong to the same cluster or different clusters. The main result of this paper is that the Theta(nk) query is required without side information, while they show that with side information, only O(k log n/H^2(f+||f-) ) query is sufficient, where H is the Helenger distance. They also show that up to the log(n) factor, it is very close.

I consider this an interesting theoretical result for several reasons. It is closely related to SBM, which has seen a lot of activity in recent years, but asks some interesting questions about how the threshold for allowing query changes affects it. It shows that allowing side information can drastically change the query complexity. Side information may contain complete cluster information, so it is probably not surprising, but this paper identifies how to evaluate the quality of the matrix.
