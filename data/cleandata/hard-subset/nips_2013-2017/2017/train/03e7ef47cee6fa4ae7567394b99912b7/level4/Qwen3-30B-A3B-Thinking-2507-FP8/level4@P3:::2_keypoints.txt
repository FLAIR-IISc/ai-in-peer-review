- Studies interactive clustering with side information and shows side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f⁺‖f⁻)).
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds for recovering the optimal clustering.
- Generalizes the stochastic block model to arbitrary within-/between-cluster distributions f⁺ and f⁻.
- Develops parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k, f⁺, or f⁻.
- Connects results to practical applications such as entity resolution where human queries are costly.
- Technical contributions are rigorous and well-proved (Theorem 1 and Theorem 2), but Theorem 2 needs explicit clarification of its asymptotic regime (sufficiently large k).
- Novelty is meaningful (characterizing impact of side information) but the paper overstates being the first; prior related work (e.g., Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) should be acknowledged.
- Experimental evaluation on Cora is reasonable but weak due to lack of comparisons against existing methods or heuristics.
- Major clarity and writing problems: dense prose, grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and inconsistent punctuation.
- Formal presentation flaws: the problem statement misleadingly suggests non-adaptive queries and fails to state that the Las Vegas algorithm’s complexity is in expectation.
- Presentation of main results needs clearer organization and more precise mathematical statements.
- Strengths: tight theoretical bounds, parameter-free algorithms, practical relevance, SBM generalization, and a useful lower-bound framework for interactive learning.
- Weaknesses: overstated novelty, poor writing quality, insufficient experimental comparisons, unclear asymptotic conditions for Theorem 2, and insufficient distinction between minimax and other models.
- Recommendation: accept pending substantial revisions to clarify claims, fix writing, acknowledge prior work, and strengthen experimental comparisons.
