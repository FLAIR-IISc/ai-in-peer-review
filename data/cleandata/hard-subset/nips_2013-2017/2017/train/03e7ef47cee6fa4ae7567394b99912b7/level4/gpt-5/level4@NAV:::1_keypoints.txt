- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ for same-cluster pairs and f− otherwise.
- Shows baseline query requirement without side information is Θ(nk).
- Proves that with side information O((k log n)/H^2(f+ || f−)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- Establishes the upper bound is tight up to a log(n) factor.
- Connects the problem to the stochastic block model literature and recent active research.
- Raises compelling questions about how allowing queries changes detection/identification thresholds.
- Demonstrates that side information can dramatically reduce query complexity.
- Notes the result isn’t entirely surprising in extreme cases but provides a principled measure of similarity-matrix quality.
