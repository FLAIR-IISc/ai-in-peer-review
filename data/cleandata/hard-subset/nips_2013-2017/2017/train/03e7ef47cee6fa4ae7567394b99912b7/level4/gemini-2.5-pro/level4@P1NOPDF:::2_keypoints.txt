- Paper studies query complexity bounds for clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons plus random side information; results seem valuable but dense and hard to verify.
- Omits relevant prior work: labeled/weighted stochastic block models and active querying literature (e.g., Gadde et al. 2016); novelty claims are overstated.
- Problem formulation is unclear: minimax objective not explicitly stated and assumptions on cluster sizes (including highly unbalanced cases) are unspecified.
- Theorem 2 may be incorrect as stated—likely requires an explicit k → ∞ condition, otherwise it seems to contradict known zero-query clustering results for fixed k.
- Numerical experiments are weak, comparing only to trivial baselines and not to existing relevant methods from the literature.
- Writing and presentation are poor with many grammatical errors, confusing sentences, incorrect punctuation, and formatting issues throughout.
- Many theorems and lemmas lack precise conditions (e.g., “sufficiently large k”) and should state all assumptions explicitly in the statements.
- Minor issues: overly long abstract and slow lead-in to contributions, questionable use of “Monte Carlo/Las Vegas,” typos (Footnote 1), ambiguous phrasing implying non-adaptivity, overstatements in the Simulations section, and mis-sized math delimiters in the appendix.
- After author response, reviewer updated recommendation to accept but insists on thorough revision to fix technical precision and language.
