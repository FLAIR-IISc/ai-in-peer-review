- Problem: Studies clustering query complexity augmented by a similarity matrix W with entries drawn from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).

- Main result (no side info): Without side information, Θ(nk) queries are necessary and sufficient for recovery.

- Main result (with side info): With side information, O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H is Hellinger distance.

- Tightness: The O(...) bound is tight up to a log n factor.

- Conceptual contribution: Quantifies how side information quality (via Hellinger distance) reduces query complexity, linking statistical distinguishability to query needs.

- Relation to SBM: Extends active SBM work and shows phase-transition shift (e.g., exact recovery threshold moves from Θ(n log n) to Θ(k log n) with side information).

- Technical approach: Upper bound via a designed adaptive algorithm; lower bound via information-theoretic arguments leveraging Hellinger distance.

- Minor limitation: Reliance on Hellinger distance—authors could briefly compare to alternatives like KL divergence.

- Impact and clarity: Clear, focused, timely work with practical implications for noisy side information scenarios.

- Recommendation: Strong theoretical advance; reviewer recommends acceptance.
