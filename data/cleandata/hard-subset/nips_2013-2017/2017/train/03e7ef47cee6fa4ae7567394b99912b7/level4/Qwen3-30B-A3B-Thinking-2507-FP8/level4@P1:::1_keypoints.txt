- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information provided as a similarity matrix and same-cluster oracle queries.  
- Shows prior query complexity Θ(nk) without side information and derives dramatic reduction with side information.  
- Proves upper bound O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) where H is Hellinger divergence between f+ and f-.  
- Establishes information-theoretic optimality of the bound up to logarithmic factors.  
- Develops parameter-free, computationally efficient algorithms that do not require knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Generalizes stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+ and f- distributions instead of Bernoulli.  
- Connects quality of similarity matrix (via Hellinger divergence) directly to query complexity threshold.  
- Introduces a novel lower-bound technique for interactive learning with potential broader applications.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with comprehensive proofs and clear exposition.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows greedy Hellinger-based strategy performs near-optimally.  
- Does not handle noisy or erroneous oracle answers in this paper (addressed in a companion paper).  
- Lower-bound proofs are technically complex and may be hard for some readers to follow.  
- Experimental section could better detail how f+ and f- distributions were estimated.  
- Single-dataset experiments limit empirical validation breadth.  
- Overall: strong, novel theoretical contribution with practical, parameter-free algorithms and meaningful connections to SBM.
