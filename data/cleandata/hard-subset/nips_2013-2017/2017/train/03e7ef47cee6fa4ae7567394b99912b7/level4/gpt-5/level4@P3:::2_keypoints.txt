- Paper studies query complexity of exact clustering with noiseless pairwise oracle plus side-information W drawn from f+ (within) and f− (cross), showing side information can greatly reduce queries versus Θ(nk) without it.
- Main lower bound: any algorithm needs Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) expected queries for constant success, and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for certainty, where H^2 is squared Hellinger divergence.
- Main upper bound: efficient, parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms use Õ(k^2/H^2) queries (Las Vegas adds O(n log n)), estimating f+, f− iteratively and using Hellinger-based stopping.
- Model connects to SBMs; zero-query case corresponds to community detection with general edge-weight distributions.
- Reviewer suggests citing and clarifying relation to labeled/weighted SBM literature and active querying work (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT’16).
- Technical contribution: an interesting, nontrivial lower-bound framework for adaptive interactive settings and plausible, well-designed upper-bound algorithms.
- Concern: Theorem 2 appears to implicitly require k sufficiently large; such regime assumptions must be made explicit.
- Concern: reconcile lower bounds with known Q=0 exact-recovery thresholds for constant k in SBMs to avoid contradictions.
- Inconsistency in complexity statements (O(k log n/H^2) vs O(k^2 log n/H^2)); unify and ensure upper bound matches lower bound scaling.
- Empirics are illustrative but lack comparisons to existing methods or interactive clustering/ER baselines; recommend adding modest baselines.
- Paper is dense and needs careful rewriting for clarity, structure, and flow.
- Problem statement should be framed in minimax terms and state cluster-size constraints (allowing imbalanced partitions must be clarified).
- Theorems should precisely state assumptions (ranges of H^2, regimes of k, whether “sufficiently large k” is required).
- Define “Monte Carlo” and “Las Vegas” precisely and state that Las Vegas bounds refer to average query complexity.
- Make adaptivity explicit where relevant (avoid language implying non-adaptivity like “find Q ⊆ V × V”).
- Numerous copy-editing issues: capitalization, articles, plurals, spacing, punctuation, awkward connectors, footnote formatting, bracket sizing around equations.
- Tone down novelty claims and properly position contributions relative to prior labeled/weighted SBM and active-query literature.
- Originality: the combination of interactive querying with unknown general f+, f−, adaptive lower bounds, and parameter-free near-matching algorithms appears novel and interesting.
- Significance: potential impact on theory (interactive learning, community detection) and practice (crowdsourced ER); Hellinger dependence is insightful.
- Pros: addresses important problem, provides adaptive information-theoretic lower bounds, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and sensible use of Hellinger divergence.
- Cons: clarity and writing issues, missing theorem regimes/assumptions, inconsistent complexity statements, weak empirical comparisons, and overstated novelty.
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending author response, with strong suggestions to fix assumptions, reconcile with SBM literature, unify complexity claims, rewrite for clarity, moderate novelty claims, add citations, and strengthen experiments.
