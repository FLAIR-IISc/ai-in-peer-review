This paper studies the query complexity of clustering with "side information." "Side information" is provided in the form of an similarity matrix W, where the Wij entry is distributed as f+ or f- depending on whether i,j belong to the same cluster or different clusters. The main result of this paper is that without side information, a theta (nk) query is required, while they show that with side information, only O(k log n/H^2 (f+||f-)) queries are sufficient, where H is the Hellinger distance. They also show that up to a log(n) factor, this is accurate.

I find this an interesting theoretical result for several reasons. It is closely related to SBM, which has seen a lot of activity in recent years, but it raises some interesting questions about how changing the query changes the bound. It shows that allowing side information can make a significant difference in query complexity. This is perhaps not surprising in itself because side information can contain complete cluster information, but the paper explains how to assess the quality of the similarity matrix.
