- Problem: Studies query complexity for clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ within clusters and f- across clusters.  
- Main result: Without side information, query complexity is Θ(nk).  
- Main result: With side information, query complexity is O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), where H is Hellinger distance.  
- Tightness: The side-information bound is information-theoretically tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.  
- Novelty: Connects to the Stochastic Block Model but addresses new questions about how queries change clustering thresholds.  
- Insight: Shows side information can drastically reduce query complexity and provides a framework to quantify similarity-matrix quality.  
- Presentation: Analysis is rigorous and results are clearly presented.  
- Minor issues: Some notation and terminology could be better explained for non-experts.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
