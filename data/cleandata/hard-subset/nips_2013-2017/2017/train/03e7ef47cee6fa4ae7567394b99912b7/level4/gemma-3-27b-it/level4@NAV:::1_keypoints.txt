- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information from a similarity matrix W, where W_ij ~ f+ for same-cluster and f- for different-cluster pairs.
- Shows leveraging side information drastically reduces required queries versus no side information.
- Proves Θ(nk) queries are necessary without side information.
- Proves O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice with side information, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- Establishes the upper bound is tight up to a log n factor.
- Connects the analysis to the Stochastic Block Model and investigates how query access affects clustering thresholds.
- Provides a rigorous framework quantifying how W encodes cluster structure and improves query efficiency.
- Reviewer regards the work as a compelling theoretical advancement.
