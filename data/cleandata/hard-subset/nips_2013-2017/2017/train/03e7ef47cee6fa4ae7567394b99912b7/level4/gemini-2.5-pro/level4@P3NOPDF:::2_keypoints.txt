- Paper studies query complexity bounds for clustering with noiseless pairwise queries plus random pairwise side-information; theoretical approach and algorithmic matching are valuable.

- Overall contribution promising but presentation, clarity, and literature positioning are poor; recommend major revision and tentatively lean toward acceptance.

- Problem formulation is unclear: need explicit statement of objectives (e.g., minimax bounds) and assumptions about cluster size balance, including extreme imbalances.

- Paper overstates novelty and omits relevant prior work (labeled/weighted SBMs and active learning for community detection); claims like "first" or "we initiate" should be removed or revised with proper citations.

- Theorem 2 is likely incorrect or incomplete: it seems to contradict known SBM regimes where zero queries suffice; any asymptotic assumptions (e.g., k → ∞) must be stated in the theorem.

- Empirical evaluation is weak: comparisons are only to trivial baselines and should include relevant existing methods or stronger justification for chosen baselines.

- Writing suffers from many grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing; comprehensive proofreading/editing is required.

- Introduction is too long and should present core contributions more concisely and earlier.

- Several sentences and justifications are unclear or incomplete and need rewriting (examples cited by reviewer).

- Terminology and notation issues: clarify use of "Monte Carlo"/"Las Vegas," specify whether query sets imply adaptivity, and fix small/bracketed mathematical expressions in the appendix.

- Clarify whether the algorithmic model is adaptive or non-adaptive, since notation like "find Q subset of V x V" suggests non-adaptivity but methods appear adaptive.

- Ensure theoretical claims, proofs, and bounds are precise, complete, and stated with all necessary conditions and regimes.
