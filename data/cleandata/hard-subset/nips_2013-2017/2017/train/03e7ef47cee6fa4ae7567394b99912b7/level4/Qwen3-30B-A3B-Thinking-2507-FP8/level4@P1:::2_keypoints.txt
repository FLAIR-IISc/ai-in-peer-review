- Main theoretical contribution: shows side-information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f⁺‖f⁻)).  
- Provides matching information-theoretic lower bounds for the query complexity.  
- Presents efficient, parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f⁺, or f⁻.  
- Generalizes stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary distributions f⁺ and f⁻ instead of Bernoulli parameters.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with a general framework for proving information-theoretic limits in interactive settings.  
- Algorithms are nearly optimal and experimentally the greedy strategy performs close to optimal on the Cora dataset.  
- Writing quality: numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology, and awkward phrasing impair readability.  
- Novelty claims are overstated; paper omits relevant prior work on labeled/weighted SBMs and related active-learning papers (e.g., Gadde et al.).  
- Theorem 2 is unclear about asymptotic regime (appears to assume k → ∞) and may conflict with known small-k results.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks meaningful comparisons to existing relevant methods, relying on trivial baselines.  
- Inconsistent use of technical terms (e.g., confusing "Monte Carlo" and "Las Vegas" algorithm labels).  
- Recommendation: accept only if authors fix presentation, correctly position novelty with citations, clarify Theorem 2’s regime, and strengthen experiments.
