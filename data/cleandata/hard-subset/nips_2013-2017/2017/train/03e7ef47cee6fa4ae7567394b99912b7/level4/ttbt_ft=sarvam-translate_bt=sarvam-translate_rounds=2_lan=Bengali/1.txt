This paper discusses the complexity of clustering queries with "side information." "Side information" is given as a matrix W, where Wij entries are distributed as f+ or f-, depending on whether i,j are in the same cluster or in different clusters. The main result of this paper is that the Theta(nk) query is required without side information, while they show that with side information, only O(k log n/H^2(f+||f-) ) query is sufficient, where H is the Hellenger distance. They also show that it is very close to the log(n) factor.

I consider this an interesting theoretical result for several reasons. It is closely related to SBM, which has seen a lot of activity in recent years, but it raises some interesting questions about how it affects the threshold for allowing query modification. It shows that allowing side information can dramatically change the query complexity. Side information may contain complete cluster information, so it is probably not surprising, but this paper identifies how to evaluate the quality of the matrix.
