I am reviewing two papers that appear to be by the same authors and on the same general topic. The other paper focuses on noisy queries without any side information.

This paper provides upper and lower bounds on the query complexity of clustering based on noiseless pairwise comparisons, along with random side-information associated with every pair. Although I am familiar with some of the related literature, my knowledge of it is far from complete, making it challenging to fully judge the value of the contributions. The paper's density and the lengthy upper bound proof also made it difficult for me to thoroughly check the correctness.

My overall impression of both papers is that the contributions seem valuable (if correct), but the writing requires significant improvement. While I acknowledge that the contributions are more important than the writing, the numerous issues raised concerns, making my recommendation borderline. An additional round of reviews to address the suggested changes would have been beneficial, but I understand this is not feasible for NIPS.

Regarding this paper, there are a few relevant works that were not cited. The idea of replacing Bernoulli(p) and Bernoulli(q) with a general distribution in the stochastic block model is not new. A Google search for "labeled stochastic block model" or "weighted stochastic block model" reveals several papers that have explored similar generalizations. Additionally, "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (Gadde et al, ISIT 2016) studies the SBM with an extra querying element, which, although using a different querying model, shares similarities with the pairwise querying model presented in this paper.

In light of these works, I suggest the authors remove or rephrase statements claiming to be the first to initiate a rigorous theoretical study or significantly generalizing existing models. These statements may not be accurate and could be perceived as misleading.

Further comments on the paper include:

- The model and problem statement should be presented more clearly, explicitly stating whether the minimax model has any restrictions on cluster sizes.
- Theorem 2 appears to be incorrect as stated. The SBM with k=2 (or any k=O(1)) may not require queries in certain cases, contradicting the theorem's claim. The assumption that k -> Infinity is not stated in the theorem and should be carefully examined to avoid potential contradictions.
- The numerical section lacks value, as it does not compare against existing works and only considers trivial baselines.
- Several sentences are confusing or unclear, such as "assume, otherwise the clustering is done" and "the theorem is already proved from the nk lower bound." These sentences should be revised for clarity.
- The paper is plagued by grammar mistakes, typos, and strange wording. Issues with capitalization, plurals, spacing, and articles are frequent, and commas are often misused. Sentences should not start with And/Or/Plus, and the word "else" is used incorrectly. Footnotes should be revised to start with a capital letter and end with a full stop.

Less significant comments include:

- The abstract is too long, and the contributions are not presented until page 3.
- The use of terminology "Monte Carlo" and "Las Vegas" should be verified as standard.
- Footnote 1 should be revised to remove the "1-" after p=.
- The formal problem statement should be rephrased to clarify that the algorithm is adaptive.
- The statement "clearly exhibiting" in the last sentence of the Simulations section is disputed.
- Many brackets in the appendix are too small.

Post-author feedback comments:

The authors' responses clarified some concerns, leading to an updated recommendation of acceptance. However, I strongly emphasize the need for a careful revision of the final version, addressing the comments mentioned above. While some reviewers may not have raised significant concerns about the writing, these issues may still affect the readability and understanding of the paper for many NIPS readers, particularly those from different academic backgrounds.

Specific comments include:

- The authors should not assume that minor issues like capitalization and misplaced commas were the primary factors in the review. These were mentioned as areas for revision, not as reasons for the borderline recommendation.
- The clarification on the (lack of) contradiction with Gadde's paper is appreciated.
- Theorems and lemmas must be stated precisely, including any assumptions or restrictions, such as k -> Infinity.
- The use of Else/And/Or/Plus, the sentence "assume, otherwise...", and other grammatical issues should be revised to conform to standard academic language.
