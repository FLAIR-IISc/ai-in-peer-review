- Paper analyzes query complexity for clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons plus random side information and gives upper/lower bounds, but proofs are dense and hard to verify.  
- Related work omitted: labeled/weighted stochastic block model literature generalizing Bernoulli(p)/Bernoulli(q).  
- Related work omitted: active querying in SBM (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) which may conflict with the paper’s lower bound for fixed k.  
- Authors should remove or soften sweeping claims like “we initiate,” “first work,” or “significantly generalizes.”  
- Model presentation is unclear: minimax formulation not stated explicitly and cluster-size constraints are unspecified (e.g., extreme singleton clusters).  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect as stated — it seems to assume k → ∞ and may contradict regimes where Q=0; this assumption must be explicit.  
- Numerical experiments are weak: comparisons are only to trivial baselines and not to existing applicable methods.  
- Several sentences are confusing or incomplete, making arguments and assumptions unclear.  
- Numerous grammatical errors, typos, punctuation mistakes, and awkward phrasing require careful proofreading.  
- Abstract is too long and main contributions are buried (about three pages to reach them).  
- Clarify terminology: use of “Monte Carlo” vs. “Las Vegas” should be standard and mention “average query complexity” when using Las Vegas.  
- Minor notation/format issues: footnote formatting, placement of “1−” after p, “find Q ⊆ V×V” reads non-adaptive, and small brackets in the appendix.  
- Post-author feedback: reviewer updated to accept but stresses a careful revision addressing all the above issues.  
- Theorems and lemmas must state precise conditions (e.g., “for sufficiently large k”) in the statements, not just in proofs.  
- Specific grammar fixes requested: avoid starting sentences with And/Or/Plus, replace “Else” with “otherwise,” and fix constructions like “assume, otherwise….”
