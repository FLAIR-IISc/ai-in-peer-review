- Problem setup: studies clustering query complexity with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ (same cluster) or f- (different cluster).
- Main theoretical result: without side information clustering needs Θ(nk) queries.
- With side information: clustering can be done with O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- Tightness: the above bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor.
- Relevance: connects to the stochastic block model (SBM) and raises questions about how queries affect clustering thresholds.
- Impact: demonstrates that side information can drastically reduce query complexity.
- Contribution: provides a way to assess the quality of the similarity matrix rather than just assuming it contains full cluster information.
