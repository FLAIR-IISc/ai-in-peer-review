- Problem setup: analyzes clustering query complexity with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ for same-cluster pairs and f− for different-cluster pairs.
- Main theorem: sharp separation — without side information Θ(nk) queries are needed, while with side information O(k log n / H^2(f+, f−)) queries suffice.
- Metric: the query complexity with side information depends on the Hellinger distance H(f+, f−).
- Tightness: the upper bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.
- Significance: theoretically compelling results showing side information can drastically reduce query complexity.
- Relation to prior work: problem is closely related to the stochastic block model but focuses on how query access plus side information change fundamental limits.
- Interpretation: using Hellinger distance gives a principled measure of similarity-matrix quality, though extreme cases (side information encoding full clustering) make the reduction unsurprising.
