- Studies query complexity for clustering n items into k groups using side information in a similarity matrix W with entries from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).
- Shows that without side information, Θ(nk) queries are necessary and sufficient for exact recovery.
- Proves that with W available, O(k log n / H²(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-.
- Provides a nearly matching lower bound, making the upper bound tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.
- Connects the problem to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and frames a novel query-based recovery setting.
- Highlights that allowing active queries raises new questions about information-theoretic thresholds for community recovery.
- Emphasizes the main contribution: precise quantification of how side information reduces query complexity.
- Uses Hellinger distance to give an interpretable measure of signal strength and its impact on algorithmic complexity.
- Demonstrates a dramatic reduction from linear dependence on n to logarithmic dependence when leveraging even weak statistical signals.
