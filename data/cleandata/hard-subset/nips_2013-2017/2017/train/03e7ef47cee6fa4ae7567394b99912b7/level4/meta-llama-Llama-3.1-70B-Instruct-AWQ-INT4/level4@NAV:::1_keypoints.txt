- Studies query complexity of clustering when given side information in form of a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters).  
- Shows baseline query complexity without side information is Θ(nk).  
- Proves with side information the complexity reduces to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), where H is the Hellinger distance.  
- Establishes this upper bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor.  
- Notes strong connection to the stochastic block model (SBM) and raises questions about how query allowance affects SBM thresholds.  
- Emphasizes the substantial reduction in queries achievable through side information.  
- Provides a systematic way to quantify the quality of the similarity matrix and its influence on query complexity.
