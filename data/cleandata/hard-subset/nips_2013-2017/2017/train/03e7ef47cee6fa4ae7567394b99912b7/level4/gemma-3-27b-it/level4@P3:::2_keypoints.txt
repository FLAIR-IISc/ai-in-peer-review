- Provides upper and lower bounds on query complexity for interactive clustering with noiseless pairwise queries and random pairwise side-information.  
- Demonstrates significant theoretical impact of side information in reducing query complexity.  
- Presents nearly matching information-theoretic lower and upper bounds and parameter-free algorithms.  
- Connects the problem to the stochastic block model (SBM), offering useful context.  
- Writing is dense, with numerous grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing that impede understanding.  
- Several relevant prior works (e.g., labeled/weighted SBMs and Gadde et al. ISIT 2016) are not cited, weakening novelty claims.  
- Claims of novelty should be toned down and language like “we initiate” or “significantly generalizes” should be revised.  
- The paper does not clearly state it focuses on minimax bounds and should clarify this.  
- The allowable cluster size distributions need clarification (e.g., whether highly imbalanced clusters are permitted).  
- Theorem 2 is potentially problematic or misstated; assumptions on k (e.g., k → ∞) must be explicit or the theorem revised.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak, relying on trivial baselines and lacking comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Proofs and theoretical claims need clearer assumptions and exposition to avoid contradictions with known SBM results.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical analysis, significant bounds, parameter-free algorithms, and SBM connection.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions—address writing, missing related work, theorem clarity, and stronger experiments.
