- Problem setup: clustering with side information given by similarity matrix W where W_ij ~ f+ if same cluster and ~ f- otherwise.
- Main result: with side information, query complexity drops to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), compared to Θ(nk) without side information.
- Tightness: the provided upper bound is information-theoretically tight up to a log(n) factor.
- Conceptual significance: connects to the Stochastic Block Model literature while asking how queries alter recovery thresholds.
- Practical insight: access to side information can drastically reduce required queries.
- Key technical contribution: identifies the Hellinger distance between f+ and f- as the fundamental parameter controlling problem difficulty.
