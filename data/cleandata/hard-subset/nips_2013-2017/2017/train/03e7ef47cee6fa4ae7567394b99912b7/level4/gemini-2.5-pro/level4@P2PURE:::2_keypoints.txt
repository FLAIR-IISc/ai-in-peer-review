- Paper gives upper and lower bounds for noiseless pairwise-query clustering with random side information; contributions seem valuable if correct but novelty is hard to assess fully.
- Writing quality (clarity, grammar, typos) is poor and is the main reason for a borderline recommendation; thorough proofreading and revision are required.
- The manuscript omits important prior work (e.g., labeled/weighted stochastic block models and active learning for SBM such as Gadde et al.); several claims of novelty are overstated and should be rephrased.
- Problem formulation is unclear (e.g., whether results are minimax, and whether cluster-size restrictions are imposed); this needs explicit clarification.
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect or incomplete as stated; crucial conditions (such as k → ∞) are not included in the theorem and may contradict known zero-query clustering regimes.
- Long and dense proofs (especially the upper bound) hinder verification of technical details within review time; statements must be more precise and assumptions explicit.
- Numerical experiments compare only to simple baselines and omit existing literature methods; justification or additional comparisons are needed.
- Several sentences are confusing, incomplete, or misleading, indicating sloppy presentation (examples provided); authors must fix ambiguous phrasing.
- Manuscript contains pervasive grammatical errors, capitalization/spacing/pluralization mistakes, misused commas, and awkward sentence starts; comprehensive copyediting is needed.
- Specific stylistic/terminological issues: clarify Monte Carlo vs. Las Vegas usage, fix Footnote 1 typo, and ensure notation like "find Q subset of V x V" correctly expresses adaptivity.
- Minor formatting/math issues: overly long abstract, overstated simulation claims, and too-small brackets in appendix.
- After author response, reviewer raised recommendation to accept but insists all the above points—especially precise theorem statements and major clarifications—must be addressed for the final version.
