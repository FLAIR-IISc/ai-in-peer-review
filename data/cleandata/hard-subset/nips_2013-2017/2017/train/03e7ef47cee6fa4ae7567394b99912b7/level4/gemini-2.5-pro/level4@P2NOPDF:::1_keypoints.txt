- Problem setup: clustering with side information modeled by similarity matrix W, where W_ij ~ f+ if i,j same cluster and ~ f- otherwise.  
- Main result: side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H²(f+ || f-)).  
- H²(f+ || f-) (squared Hellinger distance) quantifies the quality of the side information and controls the query complexity.  
- The upper bound is nearly tight, matching a corresponding lower bound up to a logarithmic factor in n.  
- Contextual significance: situated in SBM research and asks how active queries alter information-theoretic thresholds for community recovery.  
- Key contribution: provides a precise characterization linking statistical distinguishability of side information to reduced query complexity.
