- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ for same-cluster pairs and f− for different-cluster pairs.
- Main lower bound: Θ(nk) queries are required without any side information.
- Main upper bound with side information: O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f−)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance.
- The upper bound is tight up to a log n factor.
- Connects to stochastic block model (SBM) literature and examines how allowing queries shifts standard detection/recovery thresholds.
- Demonstrates that informative side information can drastically reduce query complexity and provides a principled metric (Hellinger distance) to assess similarity matrix quality.
