- Studies query complexity of interactive clustering with noisy side information W; shows side information can reduce queries from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)).
- Establishes information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching, parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms.
- Connects and generalizes the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) to more general similarity distributions.
- Addresses a fundamental, well-motivated problem with practical implications (e.g., entity resolution).
- Main theoretical result characterizes query complexity reduction as a function of Hellinger divergence between f+ and f-.
- Algorithms are parameter-free (do not require knowing k, f+, or f-), improving practical applicability.
- Introduces a novel information-theoretic lower bound technique for interactive, adaptive settings.
- Presentation suffers from grammar, typos, and awkward phrasing that hinder readability and understanding.
- Literature positioning is weak: fails to cite or adequately compare to related work (e.g., labeled/weighted SBMs and active learning in SBMs).
- Claims of novelty are overstated; statements like “first work” should be revised to reflect existing literature.
- Potential flaw/ambiguity in Theorem 2: lower bound may implicitly require large k and seems to conflict with known SBM exact recovery regimes for small constant k.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to existing baselines or heuristics from entity resolution/interactive clustering literature.
- Problem formulation and assumptions need clarification (minimax setting, imbalanced clusters, adaptivity of queries).
- Terminology and algorithm descriptions need clearer definition (e.g., Monte Carlo vs. Las Vegas guarantees).
- Recommendation: borderline but leaning accept if authors fix Theorem 2’s statement/proof, improve related-work positioning, add baseline experiments, and substantially revise writing for clarity.
