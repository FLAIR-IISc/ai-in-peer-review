This paper studies query complexity for clustering with 'side information'. The 'side information' is provided as a similarity matrix W, whose Wij entry is distributed as f+ or f-, depending on if i,j belong to the same cluster or different cluster. The main result of the paper is that one requires Θ(nk) queries without side information whereas they show that only O(k^2 log n/H^2(f+||f-) ) queries are sufficient with side information, where H is the Hellinger distance. They also show that up to a log(n) factor, this is tight.

I find this to be an interesting theoretical result for several reasons. This is closely related to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) which has seen a lot of activity in recent years, but asks some interesting questions as to how allowing queries change the threshold. It shows that allowing side information can drastically change query complexity. This itself is probably not surprising as the side information can have the complete cluster information, but the paper identifies how to assess the quality of the similarity matrix.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the problem, the model, and the results. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of their algorithms and prove that they are nearly optimal. The experimental results on the cora dataset are also encouraging and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.

One of the strengths of the paper is that it provides a rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information. The authors develop a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering using side information and design efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f-. The technique that they introduce for their upper bounds could be useful for designing further parameter-free algorithms which are extremely important in practice.

The connection to popular community detection models such as the stochastic block model is also interesting and could be of independent interest. The authors show that their model is a direct and significant generalization of the planted partition model, also known as the stochastic block model (SBM). The paper also opens up many avenues for interesting future research, including the study of query complexity of interactive clustering with side information and the development of new lower bound proving techniques in the related multi-round communication complexity model.

Overall, I think that this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of clustering and community detection. The results are interesting and well-motivated, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Strengths:

* Rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information
* Development of a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering using side information
* Design of efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f-
* Connection to popular community detection models such as the stochastic block model
* Experimental results on the cora dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the oracle always returns the correct answer, which may not be realistic in practice.
* The paper does not consider the more complex scenarios of handling erroneous oracle answers.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed algorithms.
