- Problem and model: Studies query complexity for clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ (same cluster) or f- (different cluster).
- Main theoretical result: Without side information Θ(nk) queries are needed; with side information O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice, and this is tight up to a log(n) factor.
- Connection to prior work: Closely related to the stochastic block model (SBM) and examines how allowing queries changes recovery thresholds.
- Insight about side information: Side information can drastically reduce query complexity; paper quantifies similarity quality via Hellinger distance.
- Presentation and rigor: Paper is well-written with clear arguments and rigorous theoretical analysis.
- Lower/upper bounds: Provides impressive and nearly tight information-theoretic lower and upper bounds for interactive clustering.
- General framework: Introduces a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering algorithms.
- Algorithmic contribution: Presents a parameter-free algorithm that achieves the optimal query complexity.
- Experiments: Empirical results show the algorithm attains high F1 with relatively few queries.
- Strengths (summary): Rigorous theory, significant and novel results, general lower-bound framework, parameter-free optimal algorithm, promising experiments.
- Weaknesses (summary): Assumes a perfect oracle; does not address noisy or incomplete similarity matrices; experiments are limited to a single dataset.
- Recommendation: Reviewer believes strengths outweigh weaknesses and recommends the paper as a good fit for NIPS.
