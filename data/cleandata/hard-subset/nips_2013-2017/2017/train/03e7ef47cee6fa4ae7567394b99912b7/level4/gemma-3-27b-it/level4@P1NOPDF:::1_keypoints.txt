- Study of clustering query complexity with side information: similarity matrix W with Wij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- otherwise.  
- Main theoretical result: without side information Θ(nk) queries required; with side information O( k log n / H²(f+ || f-) ) queries suffice.  
- Tightness: the upper bound is shown to be tight up to a log(n) factor.  
- Connection to Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and framing of how allowing queries affects the clustering threshold.  
- Demonstrates that side information can drastically reduce query complexity and quantifies this reduction via Hellinger distance.  
- Methodological strength: solid theoretical analysis and well-motivated results within existing clustering literature.  
- Thoughtful choice of Hellinger distance to characterize similarity matrix quality.  
- Minor concern: practical implications need more discussion, especially robustness to noise or imperfections in W.  
- Minor concern: should compare assumptions on f+ and f- distributions to typical SBM assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written theoretical contribution; recommends acceptance.
