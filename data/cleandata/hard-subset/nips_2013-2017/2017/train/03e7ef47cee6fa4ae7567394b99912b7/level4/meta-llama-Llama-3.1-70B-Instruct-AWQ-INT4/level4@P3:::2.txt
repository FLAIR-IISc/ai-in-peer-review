**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information" studies the problem of clustering with side information, where an oracle can interactively answer pairwise queries to determine if two elements belong to the same cluster. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical study of this problem, including strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds. They also propose efficient algorithms that can recover the clusters accurately with high probability.

The paper relates to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere, particularly in the areas of clustering, community detection, and active learning. The authors build upon existing models, such as the stochastic block model (SBM), and generalize them to allow for arbitrary probability distributions. They also draw connections to other related problems, such as entity resolution and crowdsourcing.
