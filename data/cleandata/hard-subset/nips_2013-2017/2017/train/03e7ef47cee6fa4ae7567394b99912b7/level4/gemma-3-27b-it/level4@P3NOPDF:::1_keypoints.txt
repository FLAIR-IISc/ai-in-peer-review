- Problem studied: query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ within clusters and f- across clusters.  
- Core contribution: shows leveraging W reduces queries from Θ(nk) (no side info) to O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)).  
- Metric identified: Hellinger distance between f+ and f- quantifies similarity matrix quality and governs query complexity.  
- Tightness: the O(k log n / H^2) bound is information-theoretically tight up to a log n factor.  
- Novelty: provides a new perspective on how query access changes fundamental limits compared to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Related work: connection to SBM is well articulated and situates the contribution within recent literature.  
- Rigor and clarity: mathematical derivations appear sound and notation is generally clear.  
- Practicality gap: paper lacks concrete discussion of realistic sources of W (e.g., embeddings, collaborative filtering, expert labels).  
- Distributional assumptions: reviewers ask for clearer specification of assumptions on f+ and f- and whether the result holds for specific distribution classes or may fail.  
- Implementation cost: paper should discuss per-query computational cost and tradeoffs, not just query counts.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address practical applicability and distributional/implementation clarifications.
