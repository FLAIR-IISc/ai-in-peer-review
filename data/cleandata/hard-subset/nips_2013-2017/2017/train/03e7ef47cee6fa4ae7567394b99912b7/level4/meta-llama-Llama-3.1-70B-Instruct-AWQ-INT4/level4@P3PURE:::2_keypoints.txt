- Two papers appear by same authors on same topic; this paper gives upper/lower bounds for clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons plus random pairwise side-information.  
- Contributions seem valuable if correct, but the paper is very dense and hard to verify (especially the long upper-bound proof).  
- Overall recommendation was borderline mainly due to poor writing and presentation, though after author response reviewer updated to accept pending careful revision.  
- Several relevant prior works are missing from citations, including “labeled/weighted stochastic block model” literature and Gadde et al. (ISIT 2016) on active learning for SBMs.  
- Authors should remove/soften claims like “we initiate,” “first work,” or “significantly generalizes,” given prior related work.  
- The model/problem statement is unclear: minimax setting isn’t explicitly stated and cluster-size constraints (e.g., tiny clusters) are not clarified.  
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect as stated; proof seems to implicitly assume k → ∞ but this is not stated, and the theorem may contradict known k=O(1) results.  
- The numerical experiments are weak: they compare only to trivial baselines and do not benchmark against existing methods.  
- Many sentences are confusing or incomplete (examples given like “assume, otherwise the clustering is done” and ambiguous lower-bound references).  
- The paper contains numerous grammar, typo, punctuation, and formatting errors that need thorough proofreading.  
- Specific presentation issues: abstract too long and three pages before contributions; small/incorrect brackets in appendix.  
- Terminology and phrasing concerns: unclear use of “Monte Carlo”/“Las Vegas” and missing “average” when mentioning Las Vegas; odd footnote formatting and possibly erroneous notation (e.g., “1-” after p=).  
- Formal problem statement wording (“find Q subset of V x V”) suggests non-adaptive algorithms and should be clarified.  
- Disagreement with some phrasing choices (e.g., “clearly exhibiting”) and multiple minor stylistic points (use of Else/And/Or/Plus, sentence starts).  
- Post-feedback: reviewer appreciates clarifications, stresses that theorems/lemmas must state conditions precisely (e.g., dependence on large k), and reiterates need for careful revision before final version.
