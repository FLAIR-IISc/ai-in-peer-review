- Studies query complexity for clustering with side information given by similarity matrix W where Wij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- otherwise.
- Main result: without side information Theta(nk) queries are required; with side information O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice (H = Hellinger distance).
- Shows this upper bound is tight up to a log(n) factor.
- Connects to the stochastic block model (SBM) literature and analyzes how queries change detection/learning thresholds.
- Demonstrates side information can drastically reduce query complexity and provides a framework to quantify similarity-matrix quality.
