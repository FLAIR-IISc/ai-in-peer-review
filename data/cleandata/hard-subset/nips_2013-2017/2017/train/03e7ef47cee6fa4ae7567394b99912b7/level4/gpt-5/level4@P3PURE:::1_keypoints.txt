- Problem setup: analyzes query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W, where Wij ~ f+ if i,j in same cluster and ~ f− otherwise.

- Main theoretical result: without side information Θ(nk) queries are required.

- With side information: only O(k log n / H^2(f+||f−)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance between f+ and f−.

- Tightness: the upper bound is shown to be tight up to a log n factor.

- Relevance: closely related to the stochastic block model (SBM) and studies how allowing queries shifts information-theoretic thresholds.

- Insight: demonstrates that side information can drastically reduce query complexity.

- Contribution: identifies the Hellinger distance between f+ and f− as an interpretable measure that quantifies the quality of the similarity matrix and governs required queries.
