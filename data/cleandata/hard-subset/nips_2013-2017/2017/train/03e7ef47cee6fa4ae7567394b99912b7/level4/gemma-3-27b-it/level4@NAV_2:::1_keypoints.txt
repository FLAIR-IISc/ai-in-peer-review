- Main contribution: Shows side information reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)), with this bound optimal up to O(log n).
- Problem motivation: Well-motivated by entity resolution and active learning, with clear connection to the stochastic block model (SBM).
- Theoretical significance: Provides tight upper and lower bounds on query complexity and quantifies the role of similarity quality via Hellinger divergence.
- Novelty: Introduces interactive clustering with side information and rigorous analysis not previously explored.
- Parameter-free algorithms: Algorithms require no hyperparameters, improving practicality.
- Generalizability: Analysis applies to both discrete and continuous f+ and f- distributions.
- Proof complexity: Proofs are dense and may be challenging to fully verify in review.
- Empirical evaluation: Limited real-world experiments; more extensive evaluation would strengthen practical claims.
- Lower-bound gap: Upper and lower bounds differ by a factor O(log n); closing this gap is left for future work.
- Oracle assumptions: Assumes a perfect oracle; limited discussion of noisy oracle scenarios.
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, rigorous results, clear connections to prior work, and practical parameter-free methods.
- Arguments against acceptance: Dense proofs hinder verification, limited experiments, and remaining gap between bounds.
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add intuition and expand experimental validation.
