- Paper studies query complexity for clustering when side information is available as a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ or f- depending on same-cluster membership.  
- Main result: without side information, Θ(nk) queries are required.  
- With side information, only O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries suffice, where H is the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-.  
- The provided upper bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor.  
- Result is theoretically interesting and related to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) literature.  
- The work raises questions about how allowing queries shifts the clustering success threshold.  
- Side information can drastically reduce query complexity since it may contain near-complete cluster information.  
- Authors contribute a method to assess the quality of the similarity matrix W.  
- Reviewer requests the remainder of the review text to continue polishing the evaluation.
