- Paper gives upper and lower bounds for clustering from noiseless pairwise comparisons with random side-information; contributions could be valuable but are hard to fully verify due to density and time constraints.
- Overall recommendation was borderline because pervasive writing issues plus unresolved technical questions made evaluation difficult.
- Missing relevant citations and overstated novelty — similar generalizations of SBM (e.g., labeled/weighted SBM) and prior active-learning SBMs are not acknowledged.
- Specific prior work (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) raises apparent contradictions with the paper’s lower bound that must be reconciled or clarified.
- Authors must remove or soften claims like “first work” or “we initiate a theoretical study” given existing literature.
- Problem formulation lacks clarity: minimax objective not stated explicitly and cluster-size restrictions (e.g., extreme imbalance) are unclear.
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect or incomplete as stated—likely requires an explicit condition such as k → ∞ to avoid contradicting known Q=0 regimes.
- Experimental section is weak: comparisons are only to trivial baselines and omit relevant existing methods.
- Manuscript has numerous clarity, grammar, and stylistic problems that significantly detract from readability and should be thoroughly proofread.
- Many specific sentence/construction issues cited (incomplete/confusing sentences, incorrect punctuation, misuse of “else,” starting sentences with conjunctions, malformed footnotes).
- Abstract is too long and the setup occupies excessive space before main contributions.
- Terminology needs clarification (e.g., meaning of “Monte Carlo” vs “Las Vegas” and that Las Vegas refers to average query complexity).
- Technical/notation issues: Footnote 1’s expression seems wrong; some formal phrasing (e.g., “find Q subset of V x V”) suggests non-adaptivity unintentionally; many brackets in appendix are mis-sized.
- The author response resolved several concerns and the reviewer updated recommendation to accept, but a careful revision is required before final publication.
- All theorems and lemmas must state precise conditions (e.g., dependence on k) in their statements, not only in proofs.
