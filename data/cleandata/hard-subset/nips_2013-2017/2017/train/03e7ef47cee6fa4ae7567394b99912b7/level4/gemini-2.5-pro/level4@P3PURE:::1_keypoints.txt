- Problem studied: query complexity of clustering with side information given by a similarity matrix W whose entries follow f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- otherwise.  
- Model detail: W_ij ~ f+ if items i and j are in the same cluster, and W_ij ~ f- otherwise.  
- Baseline: without side information, Θ(nk) queries are required for clustering.  
- Main upper bound: with side information, only O(k log n / H²(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H² is the squared Hellinger distance.  
- Tightness: the upper bound is tight up to a log n factor.  
- Novelty: connects active querying to information-theoretic recovery thresholds, a new angle relative to the Stochastic Block Model literature.  
- Key insight: side information can drastically reduce query complexity.  
- Core contribution: precisely quantifies the quality of side information via the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-.
