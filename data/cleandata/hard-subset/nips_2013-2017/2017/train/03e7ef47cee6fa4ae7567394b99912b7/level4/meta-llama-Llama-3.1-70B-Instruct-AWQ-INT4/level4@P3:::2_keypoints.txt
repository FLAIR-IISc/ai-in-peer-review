- Studies interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries, providing rigorous theoretical analysis and efficient recovery algorithms.  
- Derives strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity.  
- Generalizes stochastic block models to arbitrary probability distributions and connects to entity resolution and crowdsourcing.  

- Strength: Thorough, rigorous theoretical treatment of clustering with side information.  
- Strength: Strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Strength: Efficient algorithms that recover clusters accurately with high probability.  
- Strength: Builds on and relates to prior work in clustering, community detection, and active learning.  

- Weakness: Writing contains grammar mistakes, typos, and unclear sentences.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and needs more explanations and examples for accessibility.
