This paper explores the query complexity for clustering problems when "side information" is available. The side information is provided in the form of a similarity matrix W, where the entry Wij is distributed according to f+ if i and j belong to the same cluster, and f- if they belong to different clusters. The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of query complexity bounds with and without side information. Specifically, the authors show that without side information, the query complexity is Θ(nk), whereas with side information, it is O(k log n/H^2(f+||f-)), where H denotes the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-. Furthermore, they establish that this bound is tight up to a logarithmic factor in n.

I find this result to be theoretically interesting for several reasons. Firstly, it is closely related to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), which has been extensively studied in recent years. However, this work asks novel questions about how the availability of queries affects the threshold for clustering. The paper demonstrates that incorporating side information can significantly alter the query complexity, which is likely due to the fact that the side information can potentially contain complete cluster information. Nonetheless, the authors provide a framework for assessing the quality of the similarity matrix, which is a valuable contribution.

The analysis is rigorous, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their results. The writing is generally clear, although there are a few instances where the notation and terminology could be better explained for readers unfamiliar with the topic. Overall, I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of clustering problems with side information, and I recommend its acceptance.
