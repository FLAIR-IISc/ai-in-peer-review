- Core result: With side information, clustering needs O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) queries vs Θ(nk) without side information; bound is near-optimal up to log factors.  
- Model: Similarity matrix W entries drawn from f+ within clusters and f- across clusters; connects to and generalizes the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Significance: Rigorously quantifies how side information (measured by Hellinger divergence) can dramatically reduce query complexity.  
- Related work: Introduction situates the work among clustering, active learning, entity resolution, and community detection; ties to SBM are well-explained.  
- Strength — Theoretical results: Provides rigorous upper and lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Strength — Novelty: Investigates a novel, important problem with implications for expensive labeled-data scenarios.  
- Strength — Generality: Allows arbitrary f+ and f-, unknown k, and offers parameter-free algorithms.  
- Strength — Presentation: Generally well-written and main ideas are clearly articulated despite technicality.  
- Weakness — Experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited (only Cora); needs broader dataset variety and similarity-matrix regimes.  
- Weakness — Log factor: A logarithmic gap remains between upper and lower bounds; tightening constants/factors would help.  
- Weakness — Proof complexity: Proofs are dense; more intuition or intermediate steps in the main text would aid readability.  
- Weakness — Missing citations: Could expand connections to active learning and theoretical ML (e.g., PAC/transductive learning literature).  
- Accept arguments: Significant theoretical advance, novel/realistic model, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and potential broad impact.  
- Reject arguments: Limited experiments, complex proofs for some readers, and remaining gap between bounds.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions (expand experiments and clarify key proof steps).
