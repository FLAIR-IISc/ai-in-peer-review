- Problem setting: interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries plus a similarity matrix W where W_ij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- otherwise.  
- Main query-complexity result: without side information Θ(nk) queries; with side information O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f-)), tight up to a logarithmic factor.  
- Novel proof technique: new information-theoretic method for lower bounds in interactive settings.  
- SBM connection: generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+, f- and not assuming knowledge of k or cluster size distributions.  
- Algorithms: computationally efficient, parameter-free (do not require k, f+, f-), and implementable in practice.  
- Theoretical rigor: careful definitions and rigorous proofs for both upper and lower bounds.  
- Practical motivation: explains empirical success of heuristics in applications like entity resolution via the theoretical gap in query complexity.  
- Originality and significance: high—fills a literature gap with a first rigorous framework for interactive clustering with side information.  
- Tightness: bounds proven are tight (up to log factors), strengthening the theoretical contribution.  
- Experimental validation: limited to a single dataset (Cora); broader empirical evaluation would strengthen the paper.  
- Oracle assumption: assumes a noiseless oracle; discussion of noisy oracle scenarios is brief and could be expanded.  
- Relation to prior work: properly contextualizes and extends prior SBM and clustering-with-queries literature.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first rigorous analysis, tight bounds, novel lower-bound technique, meaningful SBM generalization, and practical, parameter-free algorithms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, heavy theoretical focus may limit practitioner accessibility, and limited treatment of noisy oracles.  
- Recommendation: accept—paper is a substantial, technically strong contribution with clear novelty and practical relevance.
