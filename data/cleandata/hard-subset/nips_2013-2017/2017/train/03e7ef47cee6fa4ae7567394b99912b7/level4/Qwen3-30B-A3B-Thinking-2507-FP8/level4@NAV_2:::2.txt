

This paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the query complexity for clustering with side information (a similarity matrix), establishing information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds. The authors show that with a similarity matrix representing noisy pair-wise relationships (where similarity values are drawn from f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- for different-cluster pairs), the query complexity reduces from Θ(nk) (without side information) to O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f-)), where H denotes the Hellinger divergence. This represents a significant theoretical contribution to a problem that has seen only heuristic approaches in practice.

The paper makes a valuable contribution by formalizing the interaction between side information and query complexity, which has been largely unexplored in the literature. The connection to the stochastic block model (SBM) is particularly relevant, as their model generalizes SBM by allowing arbitrary distributions f+ and f- rather than just Bernoulli(p) and Bernoulli(q). However, the authors' claims about novelty require careful re-evaluation, as several works have considered generalizations of SBM to arbitrary distributions (e.g., "labeled stochastic block model" or "weighted stochastic block model" literature). The paper also does not cite "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016), which studies a related query model (single-node queries vs. pairwise queries) that becomes equivalent for constant k when finding representative nodes from each cluster.

The quality of the paper is strong technically, with sound theoretical results, though the writing requires significant improvement. The presentation is dense and contains numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent capitalization, misplaced commas, and unclear phrasing (e.g., "assume, otherwise the clustering is done" appears to be missing text). The proof of Theorem 2 requires clarification: the lower bound appears to assume k → ∞, which should be explicitly stated in the theorem statement, as for fixed k (e.g., k=2), clustering can sometimes be achieved with Q=0 even when H²(f+‖f-) scales as log n / n, which would contradict the stated lower bound.

The significance of the work is high for the community, as it provides the first systematic theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information, though the paper overstates its novelty. The experimental results on the Cora dataset are limited in value, as they only compare against trivial baselines rather than existing methods. The authors should clarify whether their methods represent the only feasible approaches for this problem.

The paper's main strengths are its theoretical contributions, the clean characterization of the query complexity reduction, and the development of parameter-free algorithms that work without knowledge of k, f+, or f-. The weaknesses include the overstatement of novelty, the unclear presentation of the model (particularly regarding minimax assumptions and cluster size constraints), the grammatical issues, and the limited experimental evaluation.

The authors should revise the paper to:
1. Correct the overstatements about novelty by properly citing relevant works on generalized SBM
2. Clarify the assumptions for Theorem 2 (particularly the dependence on k)
3. Improve the writing quality throughout, addressing grammar, punctuation, and phrasing issues
4. Expand the experimental section to include comparisons with existing methods
5. Explicitly state all assumptions (e.g., k → ∞) in the theorems

Overall, the paper presents valuable theoretical insights into an important problem, but requires significant revision to clarify its contributions and improve its presentation before it would be suitable for publication at NIPS. The technical content is sound, but the writing and contextualization need substantial improvement.
