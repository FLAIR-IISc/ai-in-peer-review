- Problem setup: clustering with pairwise queries plus side information modeled as a similarity matrix W where Wij ~ f+ for same-cluster pairs and ~ f- otherwise.  
- Main upper bound: with side information, only O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice (H = Hellinger distance).  
- Comparison to no side information: this contrasts with Θ(nk) queries required without side information.  
- Lower bound: a nearly matching lower bound is proved, making the upper bound tight up to a log(n) factor.  
- Theoretical contribution: a compelling formal analysis showing how side information reduces query complexity.  
- Connection to literature: problem is closely related to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and active recovery thresholds.  
- Key insight: quantifies the quality of the similarity matrix via Hellinger distance and its direct impact on query complexity.
