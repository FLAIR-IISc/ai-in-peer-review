- Problem setup: clustering with side-information similarity matrix W where W_ij ~ f+ if i,j same cluster and ~ f- otherwise.  
- Main lower bound: Θ(nk) queries are necessary without side information.  
- Main upper bound: With side information, O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H is Hellinger distance.  
- Tightness: The upper bound is tight up to logarithmic factors in n.  
- Novelty/significance: Connects to stochastic block model literature and asks how queries change the computational/statistical threshold for clustering.  
- Conceptual contribution: Precisely quantifies how the quality of the similarity matrix (via Hellinger distance) controls query complexity, providing a fundamental framework for side-information utility.
