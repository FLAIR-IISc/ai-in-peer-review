The paper "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information" presents a rigorous theoretical study of the query complexity of clustering with side information, where the clustering algorithm has access to a similarity matrix. The authors provide strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds when side information is available, characterizing the effect of side information on query complexity precisely.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem is clearly presented. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work, including the stochastic block model and community detection models. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, including the development of a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering algorithms and the design of efficient algorithms for arbitrary distributions.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear. The authors provide a detailed analysis of their algorithms and prove their correctness. The experimental results are also presented, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.

However, there are a few areas that need improvement. The paper could benefit from a clearer presentation of the model and problem. The authors should explicitly state that they are looking at minimax bounds and clarify any restrictions on cluster sizes. Additionally, Theorem 2 should be revised to include the assumption that k -> Infinity, as this is crucial to the proof.

The numerical section could be improved by comparing the proposed methods to existing works and providing more insights into the results. Furthermore, the paper is full of grammar mistakes, typos, and strange wording, which should be carefully revised.

In terms of originality, the paper makes significant contributions to the field of clustering with side information. The authors' approach to using a similarity matrix to guide the clustering algorithm is novel, and the development of a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds is a major contribution.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS, as it presents a rigorous theoretical study of a problem relevant to the field of machine learning. The technical contributions are significant, and the paper is well-written and well-organized.

Strengths:

* Significant technical contributions to the field of clustering with side information
* Comprehensive review of related work
* Clear and well-organized writing
* Effective experimental results

Weaknesses:

* Needs clearer presentation of the model and problem
* Theorem 2 should be revised to include the assumption that k -> Infinity
* Numerical section could be improved
* Grammar mistakes, typos, and strange wording need to be revised

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, but with revisions to address the areas mentioned above. Additionally, the authors should carefully proof-read the paper to eliminate grammar mistakes, typos, and strange wording.
