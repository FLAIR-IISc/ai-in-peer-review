- Main theoretical result: without side information query complexity is Θ(nk); with side information it reduces to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)), tight up to O(log n).  
- Side information modeled via similarity matrix W with entries from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters); H is Hellinger divergence.  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+, f- and not requiring assumptions on cluster sizes or k.  
- Shows how adding queries changes the recovery threshold in SBM, linking interactive clustering to community detection.  
- Develops a novel framework for information-theoretic lower bounds in the interactive/adaptive query setting.  
- Designs efficient, parameter-free algorithms (no knowledge of k, f+, f-) with O(n²) runtime and near-optimal query complexity.  
- Practical motivation: relevant to entity resolution and crowdsourced clustering where human queries supplement similarity signals.  
- Experimental validation: tests on the Cora dataset show good performance even with approximate f+ and f-, but is limited to one dataset.  
- Writing and exposition are generally clear, though the lower bound proof (Section 2) is technically dense and may challenge some readers.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with matching upper and lower bounds.  
- Strength: novel and valuable connection between interactive clustering and SBM.  
- Strength: practical relevance and parameter-free algorithms suitable for real-world use.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is sparse and should include more datasets/examples.  
- Weakness: paper could give more intuition for why Hellinger distance is the right divergence to use.  
- Weakness: lower bound proof would benefit from a more intuitive explanation for accessibility.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant theoretical contribution, fundamental insights on side information, efficient algorithms, and clear organization.  
- Rejection concerns: limited experiments and weaker connection to practical application examples; need more intuition about Hellinger distance.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance, with suggestions to expand experiments and provide more intuition for technical choices.
