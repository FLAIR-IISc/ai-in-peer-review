This paper investigates the query complexity of clustering algorithms when supplemented with 'side information' in the form of a similarity matrix W. The entries of W, denoted as Wij, follow distinct distributions f+ or f- depending on whether the indices i and j belong to the same cluster or different clusters. The primary contribution of the paper is the demonstration that, in the absence of side information, the query complexity is Θ(nk), whereas the incorporation of side information reduces this complexity to O(k log n/H^2(f+||f-)), where H represents the Hellinger distance. Furthermore, the authors establish that, up to a logarithmic factor, this bound is tight.

I find this theoretical result compelling for several reasons. Its connection to the stochastic block model (SBM), a topic of significant recent interest, raises intriguing questions about the impact of query allowance on the threshold. Notably, the paper highlights the substantial reduction in query complexity achievable through the provision of side information. While it is intuitive that side information can significantly alter query complexity, this work provides a systematic approach to evaluating the quality of the similarity matrix, thereby offering a nuanced understanding of its influence.
