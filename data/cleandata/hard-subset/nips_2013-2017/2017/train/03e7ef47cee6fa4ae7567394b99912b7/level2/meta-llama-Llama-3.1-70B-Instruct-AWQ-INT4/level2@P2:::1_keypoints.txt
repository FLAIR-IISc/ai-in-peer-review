- Rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information using a similarity matrix.  
- Introduces a novel framework for analyzing query complexity of clustering algorithms that use side information.  
- Establishes strong information-theoretic lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Designs efficient algorithms that nearly match the derived lower bounds.  
- Demonstrates that side information can substantially reduce query complexity and analyzes query–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Uses Hellinger divergence to measure distributional distances as a technical innovation.  
- Proposes a novel iterative-update, parameter-free algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible.  
- Provides thorough algorithm analysis and comparisons to existing techniques (though reviewer notes some gaps).  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous proofs and analysis.  
- Assumes an oracle that always answers correctly, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms.  
- Reviewer finds the comparison with existing work insufficiently detailed in places.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, high technical quality, broad interest to researchers/practitioners.  
- Arguments for rejection: unrealistic oracle assumption, missing computational-complexity details, and incomplete comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
