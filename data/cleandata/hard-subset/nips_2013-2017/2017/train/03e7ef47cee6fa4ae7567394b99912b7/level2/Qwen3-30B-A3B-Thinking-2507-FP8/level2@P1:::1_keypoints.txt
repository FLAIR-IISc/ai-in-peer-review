- Significant theoretical contribution: analyzes query complexity for interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries and a similarity matrix as side information.  
- Main result: query complexity reduced from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) when side information distributions f+, f- differ.  
- Provides matching information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Presents efficient, parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.  
- Strong connection to the stochastic block model (SBM) and clear theoretical framing.  
- Novel lower bound technique that may be useful for other interactive learning lower bounds.  
- Empirical validation on the Cora dataset: greedy strategy performs near-optimal with far fewer queries than naive methods.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and situates the work within entity resolution and interactive clustering literature.  
- Weakness: connection to existing heuristic algorithms is not made explicit enough; more direct comparisons/explanations would strengthen the paper.  
- Weakness: lacks quantification of how well the theoretical bounds predict empirical performance in practice.  
- Weakness: needs more discussion on sensitivity to side-information quality, scalability to larger datasets, and practical limitations.  
- Weakness: would benefit from a clearer, intuitive explanation of why Hellinger divergence is the appropriate measure.  
- Weakness: does not directly address noisy oracle answers; discussion or extensions to noisy oracles would be valuable.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, practically relevant; recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions.
