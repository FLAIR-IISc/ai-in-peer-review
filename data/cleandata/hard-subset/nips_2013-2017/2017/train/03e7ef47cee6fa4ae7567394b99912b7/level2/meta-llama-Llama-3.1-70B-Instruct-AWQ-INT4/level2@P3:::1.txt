**Summary of the Paper**

This paper provides a rigorous theoretical study of the query complexity of clustering with side information. The authors consider a model where an oracle can interactively answer pair-wise queries about whether two elements belong to the same cluster. The goal is to recover the optimum clustering by asking the minimum number of queries. The paper shows that with side information, the query complexity can be drastically reduced from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n H^2(f+‖f−)), where H^2(f+‖f−) is the squared Hellinger divergence between the probability distributions f+ and f−. The authors also provide a lower bound of Ω(min{nk, k^2 H^2(f+‖f−)}) on the query complexity, which matches the upper bound within a logarithmic factor.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Rigorous theoretical analysis**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous analysis of the query complexity of clustering with side information, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Strong lower bounds**: The authors establish strong lower bounds on the query complexity, which provides a clear understanding of the limitations of clustering algorithms with side information.
3. **Efficient algorithms**: The paper presents two efficient algorithms, a Monte Carlo algorithm and a Las Vegas algorithm, that achieve the optimal query complexity.
4. **Generality of results**: The authors' results are general and can be applied to various clustering problems, including entity resolution and community detection.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Technical complexity**: The paper is highly technical and may be challenging for non-experts to follow.

3. **Assumptions on side information**: The paper assumes that the side information is generated from a specific probabilistic model, which may not always be the case in practice.
