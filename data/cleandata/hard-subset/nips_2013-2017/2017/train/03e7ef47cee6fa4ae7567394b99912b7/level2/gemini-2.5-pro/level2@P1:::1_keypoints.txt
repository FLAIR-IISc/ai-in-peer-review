- Problem: Rigorous study of query complexity for interactive clustering, explaining why practical heuristics outperform prior Θ(nk) theory by leveraging side information (similarity matrix W).  
- Model: Side information W with entries drawn from f+ if same cluster and f- otherwise.  
- Main lower bound: Any algorithm needs Ω(min{nk, k^2 / H^2(f+ || f-)}) queries, even if randomized and knowing k, f+, f-.  
- Main upper bounds: Nearly matching algorithms — a Monte Carlo algorithm with O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries and a Las Vegas algorithm.  
- Practical feature: Algorithms are parameter-free and do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.  
- Connection: Links the problem to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Significance/novelty: Provides a compelling theoretical explanation for exponential query reduction using noisy side information from unknown distributions.  
- Technical strength (lower bound): Elegant information-theoretic proof handling adaptive querying via a multi-hypothesis testing reduction.  
- Technical strength (upper bound): Constructive, practical algorithms with an on-the-fly iterative-update scheme to estimate distributions and set adaptive thresholds.  
- Clarity: Well-written, well-motivated (entity resolution), and precisely stated theorems.  
- Weakness (experiments): Empirical evaluation limited to Cora and lacks direct comparisons to prominent heuristics (e.g., refs [40,42,43]) and to diverse datasets.  
- Weakness (bounds gap): A remaining log n gap between upper and lower bounds; unclear whether this is fundamental or proof-artifact.  
- Weakness (appendix details): Crucial algorithmic and convergence details are deferred to the appendix, making full verification from the main text difficult.  
- Suggestions: Add empirical comparisons to heuristics and more datasets; discuss prospects/challenges for closing the log n gap; present algorithms more clearly (pseudocode/algorithm box).  
- Overall verdict: Technically deep, novel, and significant work; recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
