- Problem & model: Studies query complexity of interactive clustering with same-cluster queries plus noisy similarity matrix W drawn from unknown f+ (within cluster) and f− (across), with unknown k.

- Main lower bound: Shows Ω(min{nk, k^2/H2(f+‖f−)}) queries (expectation) and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H2(f+‖f−)}) for certainty.

- Main algorithms: Parameter-free polynomial-time Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms with query complexities stated as O(min{nk, k log n/H2}) and O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n/H2}) respectively, achieving exact recovery with high probability or w.p.1.

- Technical novelty (lower bound): New adaptive querying lower-bound technique using Hellinger divergence and reduction to multi-hypothesis testing handling adaptivity.

- Technical novelty (upper bound): Iterative, parameter-free algorithm that estimates p+/p− from side information and uses a Sanov-type Hellinger analysis to decide when to query.

- SBM connection: Relates to stochastic block models as a Bernoulli special case and discusses how queries can shift recovery thresholds.

- Relation to prior work: Recovers known Θ(nk) behavior without side information and extends/contrasts prior heuristics and active-SBM work; lower-bound framework appears novel.

- Strength: Addresses a timely, practical question showing how side information can dramatically reduce queries.

- Strength: Clear regime separation based on Hellinger divergence; dependence on H2 is intuitive and information-theoretic.

- Strength: Parameter-free algorithms with nontrivial analysis; lower bounds nearly match upper bounds up to logs.

- Weakness: Discrepancy in k-dependence — Monte Carlo bound listed as k log n/H2 versus lower bound k^2/H2; needs resolution/clarification.

- Weakness: Upper-bound analysis assumes discrete similarity support size q, while lower bounds allow continuous distributions — applicability to continuous/high-cardinality similarities is unclear.

- Weakness: Strong independence assumption on W given partition; real-world similarities often show correlations and robustness is not discussed.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation limited to one dataset, lacks comparisons to strong baselines, and relies on known/approximated f+, f− rather than fully parameter-free estimation.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — inconsistent notation, scattered definitions, missing pseudocode, and unclear roles for constants/thresholds and “grown/Waiting” logic.

- Quality assessment: Technical core is compelling; lower bound handles adaptivity well and upper bound analysis is reasonable for discrete alphabets.

- Main technical concern: The k vs. k^2 factor discrepancy must be resolved to assess tightness of results.

- Clarity: Generally well written but long/dense; centralizing definitions and providing algorithm blocks would help.

- Originality: High — novel adaptive lower-bound method and parameter-free algorithmic framework; use of Hellinger is thoughtful.

- Significance: High — formalizes empirical phenomenon, offers broadly applicable lower-bound ideas, and connects to SBM recovery.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical contributions, parameter-free efficient algorithms, novel lower-bound framework, and SBM connection.

- Cons for acceptance: Unresolved k-dependency discrepancy, restrictive upper-bound assumptions, limited empirical validation.

- Essential suggestions: Resolve k vs. k^2 dependence throughout; add discussion/techniques for continuous/high-cardinality similarities; provide clear pseudocode for the Monte Carlo algorithm and its thresholds/Waiting logic.

- Recommended suggestions: Add experimental baselines and sensitivity analyses, discuss robustness to mild dependence in W, and clean up notation/typos.
