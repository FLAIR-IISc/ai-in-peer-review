- Main theoretical claims: side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)), the bound is information-theoretically optimal up to O(log n), and the algorithms are efficient and parameter-free.  
- Strong theoretical rigor: provides tight information-theoretic lower bounds (Theorem 2) that match the upper bounds (Theorem 1) within logarithmic factors.  
- Novel lower-bound framework that handles the interactive nature of queries where standard techniques fail.  
- Well-connected to stochastic block model literature and correctly positioned within prior work.  
- Algorithms are efficiently implementable (O(n²) time) and operate without knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Iterative-update analysis offers a practical recipe for designing parameter-free algorithms.  
- Empirical validation: Cora experiments show the greedy strategy performs nearly as well as the optimal strategy with minimal extra queries.  
- Minor limitation: does not address erroneous oracle responses (noted as covered in companion work).  
- Open theoretical question: the remaining O(log n) gap between lower and upper bounds.  
- Clear presentation, well-structured proofs, and effective use of Hellinger divergence.  
- Overall significance: substantial contribution bridging theory and practice; strong candidate for acceptance.
