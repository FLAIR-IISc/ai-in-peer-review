- Presents a rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information via pairwise oracle queries.  
- Provides strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity.  
- Main contribution: shows side information (similarity matrix) can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n H^2(f+‖f−)).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement, related work, and contributions.  
- Technical sections give detailed, thorough analyses of lower and upper bounds.  
- Proposes a novel and efficient algorithm for interactive clustering with side information.  
- Experimental results on a real-world dataset demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Assumes an oracle that always answers correctly, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset and may not generalize.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to analyze computational complexity, handle noisy oracles, and include more datasets.
