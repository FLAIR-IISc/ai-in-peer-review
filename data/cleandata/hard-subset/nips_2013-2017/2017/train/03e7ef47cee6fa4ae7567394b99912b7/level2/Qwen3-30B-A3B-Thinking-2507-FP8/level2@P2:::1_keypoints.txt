- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of query complexity for clustering with side information (oracle + similarity matrix).  
- Main result: side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f-)) with matching lower bounds.  
- Novel lower bound technique for interactive settings where standard tools like Fano's inequality fail.  
- Develops a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive learning.  
- Algorithms are computationally efficient (O(n²)), parameter-free, and nearly match lower bounds up to a log n factor.  
- Connects the model to the stochastic block model (SBM), generalizing SBM and opening questions about query effects on recovery thresholds.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and effectively motivates the problem by contrasting theory and practical heuristics.  
- Technical contributions are detailed and the paper includes rigorous proofs for upper and lower bounds.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows the greedy strategy is near-optimal in practice.  
- The log n gap between upper and lower bounds is a noted limitation.  
- Assumes a perfect oracle (no errors); authors reference handling noisy oracles in a companion paper.  
- Experimental section is limited in scope and lacks evaluation on varied datasets (e.g., more clusters or noisier similarities).  
- Practical implications beyond Cora are under-discussed; robustness to different dataset characteristics is unclear.  
- Overall a strong, original contribution with clear theoretical and practical significance; recommends acceptance.
