## Review of "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information"

This paper investigates the query complexity of interactive clustering when an oracle provides pairwise similarity information. The authors establish a strong connection between the availability of a similarity matrix (side information) and a dramatic reduction in the number of queries needed to achieve optimal clustering, moving from Θ(nk) without side information to O(k² log n H(f+||f−)) with it, where H denotes the Hellinger divergence. This result is accompanied by nearly matching lower bounds, demonstrating the theoretical optimality of their approach.

The core strength of this work lies in its rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors provide a compelling argument for the power of side information and derive tight bounds on query complexity. The introduction clearly motivates the problem with the practical application of entity resolution and highlights the gap between existing heuristics and theoretical guarantees. The extension to the stochastic block model is insightful and establishes a connection to a well-studied area. The presented algorithms are efficient and, importantly, parameter-free, a significant advantage for real-world applications.

However, the paper's density and technical depth can be a drawback. The numerous lemmas and theorems, while rigorously presented, require significant effort to fully grasp. While the experimental results demonstrate the practical relevance, they are relatively limited and could be expanded to showcase performance across a wider range of datasets and similarity measures. A more detailed discussion of the assumptions underlying the model, particularly the independence of similarity values, would also be beneficial. The connection to SBM, while interesting, feels somewhat tangential and could be more fully explored. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical results with tight bounds on query complexity.
*   Addresses a practically relevant problem with significant implications for interactive learning.
*   Parameter-free algorithms with proven optimality.
*   Clear motivation and connection to related work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   High technical density and challenging to follow.
*   Limited experimental evaluation.
*   Connection to SBM could be further developed.


**Overall:** This is a valuable contribution to the field of clustering and interactive learning. The rigorous theoretical analysis and clear demonstration of the benefits of side information are compelling. Despite the paper's density and limited experimental evaluation, the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental results. The authors should also consider elaborating on the practical implications and limitations of the assumptions made in their model.
