- Problem and main claims: interactive clustering with pairwise same-cluster queries and noisy similarity matrix; information-theoretic lower bounds, parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms, and links to SBM.  
- Lower bounds: show query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+||f−)}) (up to constants) using Hellinger divergence.  
- Algorithms: Monte Carlo and Las Vegas procedures nearly match lower bounds up to logs, with complexities O(min{nk, k log n/H^2}) and O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n/H^2}).  
- Generalization: extends SBM-related results from Bernoulli to arbitrary unknown f+, f−, connecting to prior SBM and active community detection work.  
- Technical quality: strong development; nontrivial interactive lower-bound framework and constructive, parameter-free upper bounds using Hellinger-based tests.  
- Clarity issues: heavy notation; ambiguous assumptions about discrete vs continuous f+, f− and support size; Monte Carlo vs Las Vegas success probabilities unclear.  
- Inconsistency: dependence on k appears inconsistent across abstract, theorems, and algorithms (k vs k^2) and should be reconciled.  
- Reproducibility: pseudocode, end-to-end algorithms, and code release are missing and would aid verification.  
- Originality: high — novel interactive information-theoretic bounds and parameter-free algorithms for unknown f+, f−; insightful use of Hellinger divergence.  
- Significance: theoretically and practically important; explains reduction in crowd queries for ER pipelines and gives guidance via H^2(f+||f−).  
- Scalability concern: reliance on full O(n^2) similarity matrix may limit practical scalability despite being standard in ER/graph settings.  
- Empirical limitations: experiments limited to one dataset, lack comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., CORLEONE, transitivity heuristics), no ablations or runtime/memory reports.  
- Robustness suggestions: evaluate continuous f+/f−, noisy query oracles, and misspecified side information.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel theory, near-tight bounds, efficient parameter-free algorithms, timely connections to active clustering/ER and SBM.  
- Rejection arguments: limited empirical validation, scalability concerns, k-dependence clarity issues, and several proofs deferred to appendices.
