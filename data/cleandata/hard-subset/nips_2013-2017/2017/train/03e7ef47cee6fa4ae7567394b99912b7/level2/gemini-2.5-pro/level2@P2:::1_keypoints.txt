1. Rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with adaptive same-cluster queries.  
2. Models side information as similarity matrix W with f+ for same-cluster pairs and f- for different-cluster pairs; algorithms are agnostic to k, f+, f-.  
3. Information-theoretic lower bound: Ω(min{nk, k² / H²(f+||f-)}) queries.  
4. Monte Carlo algorithm: O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) queries, succeeds with high probability.  
5. Las Vegas algorithm: always succeeds with slightly higher query complexity.  
6. Demonstrates side information can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to logarithmic in n.  
7. Connects the model to the stochastic block model (SBM) as a special case, broadening impact.  
8. Strong technical novelty: a new lower-bound proof using a multi-hypothesis testing construction for interactive settings.  
9. Parameter-free, iterative-update algorithm that dynamically estimates distributions and sample needs is practical and elegant.  
10. Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and states theorems precisely.  
11. Key algorithmic details and some proofs are deferred to the appendix, reducing self-containedness.  
12. Experimental validation is limited (only Cora) and lacks comparisons to standard entity-resolution heuristics.  
13. Algorithms assume discrete, finite-support distributions; extensions to continuous or unknown support are not addressed.  
14. Overall: excellent theoretical contributions with minor presentation/experimental gaps; reviewer recommends accept.
