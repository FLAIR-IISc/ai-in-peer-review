This paper presents a rigorous theoretical study of the query complexity of clustering with side information, also known as similarity matrix. The authors provide strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds for the query complexity of clustering with side information. They show that the query complexity reduces drastically from Θ(nk) (no similarity matrix) to O(k^2 logn H^2(f+‖f−)) where H^2 denotes the squared Hellinger divergence.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, including the motivation and background. The technical sections are also well-organized and easy to follow.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1. The authors develop a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering using side information.
2. They design efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f− that nearly match the lower bound.
3. They show that the query complexity of clustering with side information is significantly reduced compared to the case without side information.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their results. The technical sections are well-organized, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of their algorithms and lower bounds.
