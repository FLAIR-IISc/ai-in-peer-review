- Rigorous theoretical study of query complexity for clustering with side information using pairwise oracle queries.  
- Main result: query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n · H^2(f+‖f−)) with side information (H^2 = squared Hellinger divergence).  
- General framework for information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering, applicable to randomized algorithms and even with full knowledge of f+, f−, and k.  
- Lower bound matches the upper bound up to a logarithmic factor.  
- Two proposed algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) are parameter-free and meet respective lower bounds within an O(log n) factor.  
- Algorithms are computationally efficient with query complexity O(min(nk, k^2 log n · H^2(f+‖f−))).  
- Empirical evaluation on a bibliographic dataset shows strong performance and robustness to distribution perturbations.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical characterization of query complexity with side information.  
- Strength: supplies a general, broadly applicable framework for lower bounds.  
- Strength: offers practical, parameter-free algorithms that are near-optimal in query complexity.  
- Weakness: assumes oracle answers are always correct (no noise model).  
- Weakness: does not address more complex scenarios with erroneous oracle answers.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear, detailed explanation of algorithm mechanics and how claimed query complexities are achieved.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting clearer algorithm explanations and consideration of erroneous oracle scenarios.
