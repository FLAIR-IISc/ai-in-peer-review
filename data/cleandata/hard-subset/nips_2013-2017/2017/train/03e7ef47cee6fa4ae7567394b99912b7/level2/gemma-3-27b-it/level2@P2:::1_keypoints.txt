- Main contribution: analyzes query complexity of clustering with a similarity matrix and interactive pairwise oracle, providing information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Key finding: side information dramatically reduces query complexity compared to no side information.  
- Connection: relates results to the stochastic block model (SBM), situating the work in a broader theoretical context.  
- Algorithms: proposes parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Strength — theoretical: strong, rigorous bounds with a noteworthy lower bound establishing fundamental limits.  
- Strength — practical relevance: addresses costly labeling in real-world tasks like entity resolution.  
- Strength — presentation: generally clear and well-organized with a concise introduction and defined contributions.  
- Weakness — experiments: limited empirical evaluation (only Cora); needs more datasets and comparisons to heuristics on accuracy and query count.  
- Weakness — proofs: proofs, especially the lower bound, are complex and may be hard to follow; more intuition and intermediate steps would help.  
- Weakness — lower-bound assumptions: lower bound assumes knowledge of k, f+, f-; impact when these are unknown is not fully discussed.  
- Weakness — practical challenges: lacks discussion of estimating similarity matrices and handling noisy oracle responses.  
- Weakness — minor clarity: heavy notation in Section 3; a summary of variables and more intuition for iterative updates would improve readability.  
- Accept arguments: novel theoretical advance, rigorous analysis, SBM connection, and useful parameter-free algorithms.  
- Reject arguments: limited experiments, proof complexity may hinder adoption, and restrictive assumptions in the lower bound.  
- Overall recommendation: positive—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and provide more intuitive explanations of key proofs.
