- Rigorous theoretical study of query complexity for clustering with side information (similarity matrix), providing strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Shows query complexity can drop drastically from Θ(nk) (no similarity matrix) to O(k^2 log n H^2(f+‖f−)) where H^2 is squared Hellinger divergence.  
- Main contribution 1: a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering with side information.  
- Main contribution 2: efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f− that nearly match the lower bound.  
- Main contribution 3: demonstrates substantial query-complexity reduction when using side information.  
- Paper quality: well-written with a clear, concise introduction and well-organized technical sections.  
- Technical analysis: detailed, rigorous, and easy to follow.  
- Relevance: important to ML/data-science (clustering/classification) with implications for efficient algorithm design.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, strong lower/upper bounds, clear writing, and organized presentation.  
- Weaknesses: assumes a perfectly correct oracle, does not consider noisy/incomplete similarity matrices, and lacks experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: suitable for top-tier publication; authors should address weaknesses and add experiments in future work.  
- Rating and outcome: 9/10; accept.
