- Rigorous theoretical analysis of query complexity for interactive clustering with side information (similarity matrix).  
- Main result: side information reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)), where H² is squared Hellinger divergence between same- and different-cluster similarity distributions.  
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Algorithms are computationally efficient and parameter-free (do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-).  
- Uses squared Hellinger divergence as the key discriminative measure between pairwise similarity distributions.  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary similarity distributions and no assumptions on cluster sizes or number of clusters.  
- Novel, technically sophisticated framework for proving interactive information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Upper bound achieved by a parameter-free Monte Carlo algorithm that matches the lower bound up to an O(log n) factor.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows near-optimal performance even with approximate similarity distributions.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and logical technical development.  
- Highly original: first rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information and novel link between side information and query complexity reduction.  
- Significant practical impact: substantial reduction in required human queries and useful parameter-free methods for applications like entity resolution and crowdsourcing.  
- Minor improvements suggested: evaluate on additional datasets, clarify connection to companion noisy-queries work, and provide more intuition for choosing Hellinger divergence and for the lower-bound proof.
