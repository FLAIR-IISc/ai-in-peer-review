- Paper studies query complexity for clustering with pairwise same-cluster oracle plus noisy side-information modeled by unknown f+ and f−, giving information-theoretic lower bounds and near-optimal, parameter-free algorithms.  
- Main results: lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2/H2}) (expectation) and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H2}) (high-probability), and efficient algorithms within polylog(n) of these bounds that achieve exact recovery w.h.p.  
- Algorithms iteratively estimate intra/inter distributions from few queries, use Hellinger divergence to decide when side information suffices, and update clusters greedily.  
- Work generalizes SBM recovery to arbitrary, unknown f+, f− and adversarial cluster sizes; connects to active community detection.  
- Positioned well relative to prior work in clustering with queries, ER/crowdsourcing, and SBM thresholds; lower bound technique (multi-hypothesis testing with adaptive queries) is novel.  
- Strength: provides sharp, intuitive characterization of how side information reduces queries via dependence on H2(f+‖f−).  
- Strength: general, adaptive lower bounds that hold even if k, f+, f− are known; nearly matching, parameter-free upper bounds.  
- Strength: supports arbitrary discrete f+, f− and unknown k; algorithmic novelty in Hellinger-based iterative updates.  
- Strength: empirical CORA study suggests large query savings and robust greedy performance.  
- Weakness: inconsistent statements of upper bounds (O(k log n/H2) vs O(k^2 log n/H2)) across the paper that must be reconciled.  
- Weakness: strong assumptions on side information (i.i.d., finite discrete support); upper bounds currently need discrete support and continuous-case handling is unclear.  
- Weakness: limited experiments—no comparisons to established heuristics, no synthetic tests varying k, imbalance, or H2.  
- Weakness: key technical details (e.g., Sanov adaptation to Hellinger) deferred to appendices, hurting accessibility; a sketch in main text is needed.  
- Weakness: model assumes a perfect oracle; robustness to noisy queries is not discussed here (though addressed in companion work).  
- Weakness: nontrivial constants and logarithmic gaps remain; tighter analysis for common parametric families would help.  
- Quality: technically solid with a highlight on the lower bound technique, but some derivations are intricate and rely on appendices.  
- Clarity: generally well written but contains typos, formatting glitches, and notation inconsistencies; algorithm pseudocode and clearer Phase 2 thresholds/stopping criteria are needed.  
- Originality: high—interactive lower bound framework and parameter-free Hellinger-based algorithms for unknown f+, f− appear novel.  
- Significance: high—provides principled understanding of when side information reduces query complexity, with implications for ER and active community detection.  
- Questions for authors: clarify correct upper-bound scaling (k vs k^2), sensitivity to non-i.i.d. side information, handling continuous f+/f− via discretization, and memory/time handling of O(n^2) side-information.  
- Suggestions: fix inconsistencies and unify notation, add a main-text proof sketch using Hellinger, expand experiments with baselines and synthetic tests, give discretization guidelines, include pseudocode and complexity summaries, and briefly discuss noisy oracle handling.  
- Pros summary: strong general lower bounds, near-optimal parameter-free algorithms, clear Hellinger-dependence, and practical iterative design.  
- Cons summary: inconsistencies in complexity claims, restrictive discrete i.i.d. assumptions, limited experiments, and many technical details deferred.
