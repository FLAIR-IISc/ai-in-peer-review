- Main theoretical result: side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n H(f+,‖f−‖)) (H = Hellinger divergence) with nearly matching lower bounds.  
- Core strength: rigorous, tight theoretical analysis demonstrating the power of side information.  
- Practical motivation: clearly motivated by entity resolution and the gap between heuristics and theory.  
- Algorithms: efficient, parameter-free algorithms with proven optimality.  
- Extension: connects the results to the stochastic block model (SBM).  
- Weakness: high technical density—many lemmas/theorems make the paper challenging to follow.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited; needs broader datasets and similarity measures.  
- Weakness: assumptions (e.g., independence of similarity values) require more detailed discussion.  
- Weakness: SBM connection feels somewhat tangential and could be more fully developed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and elaborate practical implications and model limitations.
