- Problem & setting: Studies interactive clustering with an oracle plus a similarity matrix, querying pairwise cluster membership.  
- Main theoretical contribution: Shows side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+,f−)) (Hellinger-based).  
- Lower bounds: Provides matching lower bounds up to a log n factor.  
- Algorithms: Presents algorithms achieving the bounds without knowing k or the underlying distributions.  
- Connection to SBM: Relates results to the stochastic block model, offering broader context for community detection.  
- Motivation & clarity: Well-motivated by real-world tasks (e.g., entity resolution) and clearly written.  
- Novelty: Novel rigorous analysis of how similarity matrices affect query complexity.  
- Practicality: Parameter-free algorithms increase practical applicability.  
- Experimental limitation: Empirical evaluation is limited (only Cora dataset).  
- Comparison gap: Lacks thorough comparisons against existing heuristics cited in the paper.  
- Sensitivity analysis missing: Experiments do not explore varying similarity quality (Hellinger divergence).  
- Presentation issue: Core algorithmic details are mostly in appendices, reducing accessibility.  
- Distributional assumption: Analysis assumes discrete distributions with fixed support size; extensions to continuous cases are not discussed.  
- Computational concern: No discussion of the cost of computing Hellinger divergence for high-dimensional distributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, tight bounds, parameter-free algorithms, and SBM linkage.  
- Arguments for rejection: Limited experiments, accessibility concerns due to appendix placement, and restrictive distributional assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: Expand experiments and comparisons, provide a high-level algorithm overview in the main text, and discuss relaxing the discrete-distribution assumption and Hellinger computation.
