- Problem: Studies query complexity of clustering with pairwise same-cluster oracle and side information W drawn from f+ (in-cluster) or f− (cross-cluster).  
- Main results: Information-theoretic adaptive lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms whose query costs match lower bounds up to log factors.  
- Quantitative bounds: Without W Θ(nk) queries; with W lower bound Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) and algorithms achieving O(min{nk, k log n/H^2}) (Monte Carlo) and O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n/H^2}) (Las Vegas).  
- Key technical tool: Use of squared Hellinger divergence H^2(f+||f−) to drive both lower and upper bounds.  
- Lower bounds: Novel framework handling adaptivity via reduction to k-hypothesis tests and product-distribution Hellinger arguments.  
- Algorithms: Parameter-free iterative-update method that learns empirical intra/inter distributions and uses Hellinger tests to decide when to trust W vs. query.  
- Soundness: Technical proofs appear careful; concentration via Sanov-type arguments adapted to Hellinger; bounds match up to O(log n).  
- Completeness: Core lower-bound ideas in main text; full algorithm proofs in appendices; experiments validate feasibility but are limited.  
- Clarity issues: Notational inconsistencies (e.g., O(k^2 log n/H^2) vs O(k log n/H^2)), ambiguous use of H vs H^2, and negative “Membership score” that causes sign confusion.  
- Presentation fixes: Undefined constants (C, B) scattered; proofreading and unified notation needed.  
- Originality: First rigorous treatment of interactive clustering with side information, handling adaptive queries and unknown f+, f−, k.  
- Significance: Explains query savings from W, quantifies role of H^2, connects to SBM and interactive recovery thresholds.  
- Strength: Tight near-optimal bounds, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and an adaptivity-aware lower-bound technique.  
- Model assumption concern: W assumed i.i.d. conditioned on cluster labels—real similarities often have dependencies or heteroskedasticity.  
- Discrete support assumption: Monte Carlo analysis assumes discrete fixed support; extension to continuous W (e.g., Gaussian) needs clarification and effect of discretization.  
- Lower-bound constant regime: Lower bound requires H^2 ≤ 1/18; need clarity on constants and behavior for larger H^2.  
- Algorithmic complexity: Computing intra-cluster histograms is O(n^2) in worst case; incremental/sparsity-aware alternatives not discussed.  
- Cluster imbalance: General lower-bound extension to arbitrary cluster sizes is sketched but needs explicit treatment beyond equal-size lemmas.  
- Robustness: Lack of analysis for noisy oracle responses; brief bridging to companion paper recommended.  
- Experiments weak: Single dataset, no synthetic scaling studies (varying k, H^2, cluster sizes), no baselines, and Las Vegas variant unevaluated.  
- Suggested improvements: Unify notation and complexities, add continuous-W discussion (adaptive binning), include synthetic experiments and baselines, discuss robustness and histogram resolution, and add complexity/memory analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong, near-tight theory, novel adaptive lower-bound framework, and parameter-free algorithms with practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental validation and clarity/notation issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept (leaning) contingent on improved clarity and stronger experiments.
