- Problem: Recover latent clustering of n items into k clusters with minimal same-cluster pairwise queries, leveraging side-information similarity matrix.  
- Model: Pairwise similarities drawn from f+ (same cluster) or f- (different clusters); distinguishability measured by squared Hellinger divergence H^2(f+||f-).  
- Lower bound: Proves an information-theoretic necessity of Ω(min{n k, k^2 / H^2(f+||f-)}) queries in expectation.  
- Algorithms: Presents parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that do not need k, f+, or f- and nearly match the lower bound up to a log n factor.  
- Relation to SBM: Model generalizes the Stochastic Block Model and clarifies the interplay between queries and side information in community detection.  
- Strength — Significance/Originality: Novel formal analysis linking side-information information content to query complexity; explains practical observations in applications like entity resolution.  
- Strength — Technical Quality (lower bound): Strong, technically challenging, and elegant lower bound proof identifying infrequently queried elements and a multi-hypothesis testing argument.  
- Strength — Technical Quality (algorithms): Parameter-free, iterative-update algorithms that dynamically estimate distributions are practically relevant and theoretically strong.  
- Strength — Clarity/Organization: Well-written, clearly structured, with good motivation and contextualization.  
- Weakness — Clarity of technical arguments: Some dense technical sections; would benefit from more intuition (e.g., roles of sets J' and J'' and summaries of key lemmas) in the main text.  
- Weakness — Assumptions: Upper-bound algorithms assume discrete distributions over fixed support; extension to continuous or varying support deserves discussion.  
- Weakness — Experimental evaluation: Limited experiments (Cora only) and no comparisons to established practical heuristics; more empirical validation recommended.  
- Weakness — Gap between bounds: Upper and lower bounds match up to log n; authors should comment whether this gap is a proof artifact or fundamental.  
- Summary — For acceptance: Provides rigorous, near-optimal, parameter-free algorithms and strong theoretical contributions with high potential impact.  
- Summary — Against acceptance: Main concerns are limited empirical comparison and discrete-distribution assumption, but these do not overturn the paper’s core theoretical merits.
