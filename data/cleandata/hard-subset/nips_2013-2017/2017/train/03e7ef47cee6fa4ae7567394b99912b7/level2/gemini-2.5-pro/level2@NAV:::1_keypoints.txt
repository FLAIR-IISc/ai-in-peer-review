- Problem: studies query complexity of interactive clustering with same-cluster queries and side information modeled by similarity values from two unknown distributions f+ and f−.
- Aim: explain the gap between worst-case Θ(nk) query complexity and better practical heuristics by formalizing the role of side information.
- Main lower bound: proves a nearly-tight information-theoretic lower bound Ω(k² / H²(f+‖f−)) queries are necessary.
- Algorithms: gives parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms achieving an upper bound O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f−)), matching the lower bound up to log factors.
- Novelty: develops a lower-bound framework for adaptive, interactive clustering and provides nearly-matching upper and lower bounds.
- Practical design: proposes a clever iterative-update, parameter-free scheme that enhances practical relevance.
- Literature connections: situates work well with links to the Stochastic Block Model and applications like entity resolution.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and states main theorems precisely.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset; synthetic studies varying k and Hellinger distance would better validate theoretical scaling.
- Weakness: the intriguing connection to changes in SBM recovery thresholds is noted but not explored in depth; more explicit discussion would strengthen impact.
- Overall: high-quality contribution that significantly advances understanding of interactive clustering by combining strong theory with practical algorithms.
