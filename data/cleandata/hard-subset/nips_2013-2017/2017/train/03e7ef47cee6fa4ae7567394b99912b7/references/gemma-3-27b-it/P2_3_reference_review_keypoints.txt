- Summary: Analyzes query complexity of clustering with an oracle plus precomputed side-information matrix, giving information-theoretic lower bounds and near-matching algorithms that reduce queries compared to O(nk) and generalize the SBM.

- Novelty & significance: Introduces and rigorously analyzes clustering with side information, explaining practical heuristic performance and generalizing SBM.

- Theoretical rigor: Provides strong interactive lower bounds and nearly-matching upper bounds, using Hellinger divergence effectively.

- Algorithms: Proposes parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms with iterative updates that appear practical.

- Connection to literature: Well-positioned with respect to active learning, entity resolution, community detection, and SBM work.

- Experimental validation: Empirical results on the CORA dataset show effectiveness and noise resilience but are limited in scope.

- Clarity (minor): Introduction is long/repetitive and some SBM-related explanations could be more concise.

- Experimental evaluation (major): Only one dataset tested; needs broader experiments across sizes, structures, and noise levels and comparisons to existing heuristics.

- Practical considerations: Lacks detailed discussion of implementation costs (e.g., estimating Hellinger divergence).

- Tightness of bounds: A remaining logarithmic factor separates lower and upper bounds and could be tightened.

- Overall assessment: Significant theoretical contribution with novel, near-optimal, parameter-free algorithms; weaknesses are mainly experimental scope and verbosity.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory and proofs, novel problem and solutions, parameter-free near-optimal algorithms, potential application impact.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental validation, somewhat verbose writing, and a logarithmic gap in bounds.

- Rating & recommendation: Strong Accept (7/10); accept with revisions to expand experiments and improve clarity.
