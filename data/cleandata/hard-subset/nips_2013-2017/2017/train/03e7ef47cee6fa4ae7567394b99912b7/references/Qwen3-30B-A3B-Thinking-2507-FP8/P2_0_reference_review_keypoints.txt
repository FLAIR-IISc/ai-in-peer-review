- Introduces side information (similarity matrix) to reduce clustering query complexity from Ω(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f₊‖f₋))).
- Main contributions: new information-theoretic lower-bound framework for interactive clustering and near-matching parameter-free algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas).
- Algorithms are iterative, do not require knowledge of f₊, f₋, or k, and have rigorously analyzed query complexity.
- Connects and generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f₊ and f₋ and no assumptions on k or cluster sizes.
- The lower bound (Theorem 2) is technically novel and challenging, addressing adaptivity where standard tools like Fano’s inequality fail.
- Empirical validation on the Cora dataset shows greedy strategies perform close to optimal, supporting theoretical claims.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and logically organized with clear problem motivation and rigorous proofs.
- Clarity could improve by briefly defining technical terms (e.g., Hellinger divergence) and simplifying the dense lower-bound proof for non-experts.
- Experimental evaluation is limited (single dataset, 124 clusters) and lacks deeper analysis of how Hellinger divergence impacts performance.
- Lacks a concrete example or guidance for computing H²(f₊‖f₋) in practice, which would aid application of results.
- Novelty is high relative to prior heuristic-focused work, offering the first rigorous theoretical bounds for clustering with side information.
- Contributions have practical significance for cost-sensitive applications (entity resolution, crowdsourcing) and theoretical impact on SBM recovery thresholds.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant theoretical work that warrants acceptance despite limited experiments.
- Recommendation: accept.
