- Paper shows a theoretical separation: with side information clustering query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f⁺‖f⁻))) with matching lower bounds.  
- Provides parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of f⁺, f⁻, or k.  
- Introduces a novel lower-bound framework for interactive queries using Hellinger divergence rather than KL divergence.  
- Connects the model to stochastic block models and practical applications like entity resolution to explain why heuristics need fewer queries.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows practical relevance and robustness to approximate distributions.  
- Strength: rigorous, information-theoretic analysis with tight bounds (within logarithmic factors).  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated proof techniques for adaptive query settings.  
- Strength: practical advantage from parameter-free algorithm design.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset (Cora), limiting empirical generality.  
- Weakness: assumes noiseless oracle responses; handling noisy/crowdsourced queries is not addressed here.  
- Weakness: theoretical constants (e.g., factor 4 in thresholds) may be large and affect practice.  
- Weakness: main results focus on discrete distributions, with only mentions of continuous extensions.  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs and complete theoretical contribution.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible for experts.  
- Originality: first rigorous analysis of query complexity for clustering with side information; novel connection to SBM.  
- Significance: high practical importance for reducing human/crowd queries and explaining empirical heuristics.  
- Recommendation: accept; strong theoretical contribution with practical relevance.  
- Final scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 10/10, Significance 9/10.
