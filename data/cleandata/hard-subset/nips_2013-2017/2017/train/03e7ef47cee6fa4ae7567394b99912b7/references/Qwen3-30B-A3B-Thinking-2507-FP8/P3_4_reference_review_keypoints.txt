- Problem & result: Studies query complexity of clustering with side information and shows reduction from Ω(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f+||f-))) using Hellinger divergence between f+ and f-.

- Contributions: Proves tight information-theoretic lower bounds and gives parameter-free algorithms that nearly match those bounds.

- Technical strength: Rigorous analysis; impressive lower-bound proof handling adaptive algorithms via generalized Fano and careful hypothesis construction.

- Algorithmic novelty: Develops parameter-free, near-optimal algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.

- Connection to SBM: Frames stochastic block model as a special case and generalizes to arbitrary f+, f- distributions.

- Experiments: Cora dataset validation shows greedy strategy performs close to optimal with few queries, supporting practical relevance.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized; technical content is dense but accessible with detailed supplementary proofs; lower-bound section could use more intuition.

- Originality: Novel precise characterization of side-information benefit, generalization of SBM, and new techniques for lower bounds in interactive settings.

- Significance: Substantial practical implications for crowdsourced entity resolution and theoretical impact on interactive learning and community detection.

- Limitation 1: A log n gap remains between lower and upper bounds without clear conditions for tightness.

- Limitation 2: Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Cora); broader experiments would strengthen claims.

- Limitation 3: Assumes a perfect oracle; noisy queries are deferred to companion work and not fully discussed in the main paper.

- Limitation 4: Connection to concrete entity-resolution errors (e.g., DBLP) is mentioned but not demonstrated in practice.

- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and technically sound work that meaningfully advances interactive clustering; minor issues are addressable.

- Recommendation: Accept.
