- Overall: Significant theoretical contribution showing clustering query complexity is reduced with side information; tight bounds and efficient algorithms; recommended for acceptance.

- Problem studied: Characterizes oracle query complexity for clustering with a full similarity matrix as side information versus without it.

- Main result: Side information can reduce query complexity drastically, potentially to sublinear, quantified via Hellinger divergence between within- and between-cluster similarity distributions.

- Technical approach: Derives information-theoretic lower bounds (using generalized Fano) and designs algorithms that nearly match those bounds.

- Connection: Generalizes and connects to the Stochastic Block Model, extending results to arbitrary unknown distributions.

- Generality: Handles unknown number of clusters, arbitrary similarity distributions, and no cluster-size assumptions.

- Algorithms: Proposes parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing distributions or cluster count.

- Tightness: Upper and lower bounds match up to logarithmic factors, indicating near-optimality.

- Practical relevance: Motivated by entity resolution; experiments on Cora support feasibility of methods.

- Strength — theoretical depth: Strong information-theoretic foundation and impressive treatment of adaptivity and continuous side information.

- Strength — applicability: Broad settings considered increase potential impact.

- Weakness — clarity: Paper is dense, heavy in notation, and some algorithmic explanations (e.g., iterative updates) need more intuition and intermediate steps.

- Weakness — experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset and lacks comparisons to existing heuristics.

- Weakness — constants: Potentially large constant factors (e.g., in Hellinger divergence) may limit practical performance; lacks discussion.

- Weakness — noisy oracle: Assumes a correct oracle; sensitivity to oracle noise is not explored in detail (companion work mentioned).

- Weakness — length: Long paper with many details deferred to appendix, making core ideas harder to digest.

- For acceptance: Offers rigorous, original, and significant theoretical analysis with strong positioning in literature.

- Against acceptance: Density/readability and limited empirical validation could make it less accessible and weaken practical claims.

- Final recommendation: Accept, with suggested improvements to clarity and expanded experimental evaluation.
