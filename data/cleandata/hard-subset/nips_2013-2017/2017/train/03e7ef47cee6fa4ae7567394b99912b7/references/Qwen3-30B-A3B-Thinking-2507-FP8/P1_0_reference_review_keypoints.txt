- Formalizes clustering with side information using arbitrary, unknown similarity distributions f+ and f- and characterizes query complexity via squared Hellinger divergence H²(f+||f-).  
- Shows side information reduces query complexity from O(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f+||f-))).  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+, f- and not assuming known k or cluster sizes.  
- Provides strong information-theoretic lower bounds (Theorem 2) and matching upper bounds (Theorem 1), giving near-optimal, parameter-free algorithms.  
- Develops a novel lower-bound proof technique extending Sanov’s theorem to Hellinger distance for the interactive setting.  
- Algorithms are parameter-free and empirically shown to work with approximate distributions.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset supports theoretical claims about practical effectiveness.  
- Limited empirical comparison: experiments do not benchmark against existing heuristic methods cited in the introduction.  
- Scalability concerns: time complexity reported as O(n²) but lacks detailed discussion of scaling and possible improvements.  
- Assumes availability of side-information matrix W without explaining practical generation or construction of W in detail.  
- Constants in theoretical bounds are not optimized and their practical impact is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Connection to SBM is noted but the implications for SBM recovery thresholds are conjectural and not proven.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, with well-supported claims and careful acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Writing and organization are generally clear, with a logical presentation of proofs and algorithm design.  
- Some technical explanations (e.g., “weak clusters” in the lower bound, Membership subroutine) are dense and would benefit from intuitive examples.  
- Experimental descriptions (best/worst/greedy strategies) need more detail for clarity.  
- The work is original in formalizing the general query-complexity-with-side-information framework and providing tight bounds.  
- Results are significant both theoretically and practically, explaining why side-information heuristics outperform O(nk) bounds.  
- The paper is likely to influence research in interactive clustering, active learning, and crowdsourcing due to its parameter-free algorithms.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution with adequate empirical validation despite some practical gaps.  
- Suggested improvements: add empirical comparisons to heuristics, clarify side-information generation, discuss constants’ effects, include illustrative examples for Membership/Hellinger usage, and expand on SBM implications.
