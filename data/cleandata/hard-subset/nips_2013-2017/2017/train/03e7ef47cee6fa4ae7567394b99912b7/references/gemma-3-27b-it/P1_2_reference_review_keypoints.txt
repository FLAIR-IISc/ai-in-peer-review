- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with an oracle plus side information (similarity matrix), deriving information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly-matching upper bounds.  
- Shows side information can greatly reduce query complexity versus classic O(nk) bounds.  
- Motivated by practical human-in-the-loop tasks like entity resolution where pairwise queries are costly.  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary unknown distributions for similarity entries and no assumptions on k or cluster sizes.  
- Connects to community detection and prior work on query/decision tree complexity and interactive clustering heuristics.  
- Provides parameter-free algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) that do not require knowing f+, f−, or k.  
- Theoretical contribution: novel framework to prove information-theoretic lower bounds in adaptive-query settings.  
- Rigorous proofs and extensive supplementary material supporting claims.  
- Main theoretical result: Theorem 2 gives a significant information-theoretic lower bound.  
- Practical concern: accurately estimating Hellinger divergence H2(f+‖f−) may be difficult with limited data.  
- Upper bounds include logarithmic factors that may hide large constant factors affecting practical performance.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to the Cora dataset and should be broadened across datasets and side-information qualities.  
- Clarity issue: Phase 2 iterative update algorithm and the link between cluster size and convergence need clearer explanation.  
- Quality, originality, and significance are rated highly (scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, near-optimal algorithms, SBM generalization, practical relevance, and originality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Hellinger estimation practicality, limited experiments, and some unclear algorithmic descriptions.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical impact; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance subject to minor clarifications and expanded experiments.
