- Main result: shows side information can reduce queries from O(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f⁺||f⁻))) with matching lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms; connects to SBM.  
- Technical strength: rigorous, matching upper and lower bounds and a novel framework for interactive lower bounds.  
- Algorithmic strength: efficient Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms (O(n²) time) that are parameter-free in practice.  
- Empirical support: experiments on Cora show greedy strategy nearly matches optimal with few queries.  
- Clarity: well-organized and accessible presentation with reproducible supplementary material; some proofs (Lemma 1) are challenging.  
- Originality: first information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering with side information and generalization beyond SBM.  
- Significance: important practical implications for entity resolution and crowdsourced clustering; narrows theory-practice gap.  
- Weakness: limited experimental validation—only Cora dataset used; needs more datasets/synthetic tests.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of the regime where H²(f⁺||f⁻) is large (poor side information).  
- Weakness: “parameter-free” claim depends on a constant C in thresholds with no guidance on choosing it.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparison to other existing heuristics or state-of-the-art algorithms for clustering with side information.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, technically sound contribution with clear practical relevance.  
- Suggestion: expand experiments to additional real and synthetic datasets to test robustness.  
- Suggestion: analyze behavior and trade-offs when H²(f⁺||f⁻) is large.  
- Suggestion: clarify/justify the choice of constant C and how to optimize it in practice.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons to existing algorithms and explain the choice of Hellinger divergence versus KL and its impact on guarantees.  
- Conclusion: high-quality theoretical paper that would be a strong NIPS contribution with minor empirical and clarification improvements.
