- Paper studies query complexity for clustering with noisy side information and interactive oracle queries, characterizing how side information reduces required queries.  
- Main contributions: framework for interactive lower bounds, parameter-free algorithms nearly matching lower bound, and query complexity reduction to O(k / (log n * H²(f+||f-))).  
- Generalizes SBM by allowing arbitrary intra-/inter-cluster distributions f+ and f- and not requiring knowledge of k or cluster sizes.  
- Clarifies why practical heuristics use far fewer queries than Ω(nk) by showing side information can explain the discrepancy.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides rigorous information-theoretic lower bound (Theorem 2) and matching upper bound (Theorem 1) with careful interactive analysis.  
- Practical relevance: parameter-free, efficient algorithms useful for entity resolution and crowdsourcing where human queries are costly.  
- Novel framework: new general method for proving information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive problems.  
- Experimental validation: results on Cora dataset closely match theoretical predictions and show robustness to distribution estimation errors.  
- Clear presentation: well-organized paper with precise problem statement and defined technical concepts.  
- Weakness: main lower bound gives only modest error probability guarantee (≥ 1/6 − O(1/√k)), lacking high-probability recovery.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition for choosing Hellinger divergence over other divergences like KL.  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks detail on side-information generation and algorithm tuning for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of connections to SBM, especially in the sparse regime.  
- Quality: technically sound, complete work with claims supported by proofs and experiments.  
- Originality: novel formalization of clustering with side information and new lower-bound framework distinguishing it from prior oracle-query and SBM work.  
- Significance: first rigorous guarantees for interactive clustering with side information and substantial query-complexity reduction potential for practical applications.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS — strong theoretical contribution, practical relevance, and solid validation.  
- Pro acceptance: first rigorous study, tight bounds, novel lower-bound framework, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and real-data validation.  
- Con acceptance: weak error-probability guarantee, need more rationale for Hellinger divergence, and more experimental detail.  
- Suggestions: cite TV–Hellinger relationship in lower-bound proof; clarify normalization choice for Hellinger; expand experimental details; detail SBM generalization in sparse regime; discuss extension to noisy oracle answers.
