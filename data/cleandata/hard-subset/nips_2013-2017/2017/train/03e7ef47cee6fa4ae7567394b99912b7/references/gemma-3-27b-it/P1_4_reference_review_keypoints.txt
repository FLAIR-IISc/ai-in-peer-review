- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with pairwise oracle queries plus a side-information similarity matrix, motivated by costly human labels.
- Main result: side information can significantly reduce queries from Ω(nk); lower bound Ω(min{nk, k² H²(f+||f−)}) and upper bound O(k log n H²(f+||f−)) nearly match.
- Uses Hellinger divergence H²(f+||f−) between within-/between-cluster similarity distributions to characterize query complexity.
- Algorithms are parameter-free, avoiding estimation of underlying distributions — a practical advantage.
- The model generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+ and f− and not assuming k or cluster size distribution.
- Proofs are rigorous and information-theoretic (Fano’s inequality, Sanov’s theorem); theoretical analysis is a core contribution.
- Experimental validation exists but is limited (single dataset: Cora), reducing empirical support across different settings.
- Algorithm description (especially Phase 2 iterative-update) is dense and would benefit from clearer exposition and pseudocode.
- The connection between Hellinger divergence and threshold τ1 is insufficiently explained in the text.
- Constant factors hidden in bounds and the extra logarithmic factor may matter in practice and deserve discussion or tightening.
- Paper assumes a perfect oracle; implications of noisy queries are only referenced via companion work and not discussed in the main text.
- Well-positioned relative to prior work on interactive clustering and recent SBM advances, and cites relevant literature.
- Practical relevance to applications like entity resolution is strong and motivates the study.
- Arguments for acceptance: important open problem, rigorous near-optimal bounds, parameter-free algorithms, and SBM connection.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear algorithmic description, limited experiments, and remaining logarithmic factor in bounds.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper worthy of acceptance despite weaknesses in clarity and empirical breadth.
- Recommendation: add clearer algorithm pseudocode, broaden experiments across datasets, discuss constant factors, and briefly address oracle errors.
