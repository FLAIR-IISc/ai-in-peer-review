- Establishes tight information-theoretic lower bound Ω(n + min{nk, k²/H²(f⁺||f⁻)}) and parameter-free algorithms achieving matching upper bounds (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas).  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary similarity distributions f⁺ and f⁻ instead of Bernoulli.  
- Uses squared Hellinger divergence H²(f⁺||f⁻) as the key separation metric for analysis and algorithm design.  
- Lower bound proof (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) is a strong theoretical contribution, overcoming challenges for adaptive algorithms.  
- Introduces a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive clustering, potentially applicable beyond this work.  
- Proposes a clever iterative-update, parameter-free algorithm whose design hinges on Hellinger divergence.  
- Problem formulation and main results are clearly stated and well-motivated by applications like entity resolution.  
- Experimental validation is weak: only one dataset (Cora) and a single perturbation tested.  
- Does not compare empirically against existing heuristics cited as motivation (e.g., [40, 42, 43]).  
- Lacks clear translation of theoretical query complexity into practical time/cost savings.  
- The “parameter-free” claim is ambiguous; thresholds/constants (e.g., the 4) are not justified or sensitivity-analyzed.  
- Connection to SBM is overstated and not fully detailed; Lemma 1 is not tight for sparse SBM without further analysis.  
- Lower bound proof is dense and would benefit from a high-level, intuitive sketch before formal details.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous proofs, though reliance on Sanov’s theorem and the Hellinger vs KL choice needs clearer justification.  
- Presentation is generally clear but has notational inconsistencies and could clarify algorithm implementation details.  
- The work is original and significant, offering fundamental insights into how side information reduces query complexity.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on expanding experiments, clarifying “parameter-free,” strengthening SBM connections, and adding a proof sketch.  
- Pro: first tight bounds, novel lower-bound technique, practical implications for entity resolution; Con: limited experiments, ambiguous parameter-free claims, and some proof and SBM-connection clarity issues.
