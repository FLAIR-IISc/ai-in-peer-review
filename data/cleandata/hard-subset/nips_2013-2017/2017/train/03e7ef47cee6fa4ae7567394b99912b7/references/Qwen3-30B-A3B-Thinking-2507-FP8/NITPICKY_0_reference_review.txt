

# Review of "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information"

This paper studies the query complexity of clustering with side information, where the algorithm has access to a similarity matrix and can query an oracle about whether two points belong to the same cluster. The authors claim to provide tight bounds on the query complexity that show a significant reduction when side information is available, and they develop parameter-free algorithms that match these bounds within logarithmic factors.

## Major Concerns

The paper contains numerous technical and notational inconsistencies that undermine its credibility as a rigorous theoretical contribution:

1. **Inconsistent Hellinger Divergence Notation**: The paper defines H²(f||g) as the squared Hellinger divergence, but then uses inconsistent notation throughout (e.g., H2(f+||f-), H(f||g), and "Hellinger square divergence"). The squared Hellinger divergence is defined as H²(f||g) = 1/2 ∑(√f(i) - √g(i))², but the paper writes "H2(f‖g) = 1 2 ∑ i (√ f(i)− √ g(i) )2" without proper spacing and formatting.

2. **Critical Mathematical Errors**: 
   - Theorem 1 states the bound as O(min(nk, k / (logn H2(f+‖f−)))), but this should be O(min(nk, k / (log n H²(f+||f-)))), with proper division and notation. The paper has multiple instances of missing division signs (e.g., "k 2 H2" instead of "k² / H²").
   - Theorem 2 is cut off: "that does not perform Ω ( min {nk, k 2 H2(f+‖f−)} ) expected" - it should be "that does not perform Ω(...) queries, will be unable to return the correct clustering with probability at least 1/6 - O(1/√k)".
   - In Lemma 3, the paper states "H(p+||p−) ∈ [H(f+||f−) ± 4H(p+||p−)² / (B √ log n)]", creating a circular definition where H(p+||p−) appears on both sides.

3. **Missing References**: The paper cites [13] as evidence for the Ω(nk) lower bound, but [13] is not included in the reference list. Similarly, [30] is cited in the introduction but not in the references.

4. **Formatting Errors**: 
   - "st Conference" should be "2017" or "NIPS 2017"
   - "k 2" should be "k²" throughout
   - "logn" should be "log n" with a space
   - Multiple missing parentheses in mathematical expressions
   - Inconsistent use of "‖" and "||" for divergence notation

5. **Confusing Statements**: The paper states "when H2(f+‖f−) is close to 1, e.g., when the side information is perfect, no queries are required" - this is misleading. When H²(f+||f-) is close to 1, the distributions are very different, so side information is good, and fewer queries would be needed. However, the paper's upper bound is O(k / (log n H²(f+||f-))), which would be small when H²(f+||f-) is large, so the statement is technically correct but poorly worded.

6. **Algorithm Description**: The algorithm states "We start by querying a few points so that there is at least one cluster with Θ(log n) points" but fails to specify how many queries are needed to achieve this, which is a critical detail for a query complexity paper.

## Minor Concerns

1. The paper states "the Hellinger square divergence" in the experimental results section, which is redundant. It should be "squared Hellinger divergence" or "Hellinger divergence squared" to be consistent with their definition.

2. The paper claims "the detailed proof of the Monte Carlo algorithm, and the Las Vegas algorithm and its proof are given in Appendix A and Appendix B respectively," but the supplementary material is not provided in the submission.

3. In the lower bound proof, the paper writes "P`(E) ≤ Pt(E) + √ H(P`||Pt)" which should be "P`(E) ≤ Pt(E) + √(H(P`||Pt))" or "P`(E) ≤ Pt(E) + H(P`||Pt)^{1/2}".

4. The paper states "H2(f‖g) = 1 2 ∑ i (√ f(i)− √ g(i) )2" but the 1/2 should be written as "1/2" for clarity.

## Strengths

The paper addresses an important problem in interactive clustering with side information, and the general approach of using Hellinger divergence to characterize the quality of side information is novel. The connection to the stochastic block model is insightful, and the experimental results provide some validation of the theoretical claims.

## Recommendation

While the paper addresses an interesting problem with potentially important theoretical contributions, the numerous technical and notational errors significantly undermine its credibility. As a rigorous theoretical contribution to NIPS, the paper should be free of such fundamental errors. The errors are not merely typographical but affect the mathematical correctness of the results, particularly the circular definition in Lemma 3 and the inconsistent handling of the Hellinger divergence.

Given the paper's claims of being a "rigorous theoretical study," these errors are particularly problematic. The paper would need substantial revision to correct these issues before it could be considered for publication at NIPS. 

I recommend rejection due to the numerous technical and notational errors that compromise the paper's mathematical integrity. The authors should carefully revise the mathematical notation and correct the fundamental errors before resubmission.
