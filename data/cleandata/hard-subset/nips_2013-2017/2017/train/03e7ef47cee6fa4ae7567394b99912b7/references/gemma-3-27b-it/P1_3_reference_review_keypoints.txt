- Problem: studies query complexity of clustering with an oracle plus side information (similarity matrix), filling a theoretical gap.  
- Main result: information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly-matching upper bounds showing significant query reduction with side information.  
- Framework: introduces a novel general framework for proving lower bounds in interactive clustering.  
- Algorithms: proposes parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of f+, f- or k.  
- Theoretical tightness: near-matching bounds with a reasonable logarithmic gap.  
- SBM connection: establishes relation to the stochastic block model while handling arbitrary f+/f- and unknown k.  
- Experiments: empirical validation on the Cora dataset demonstrating algorithm performance and resilience to approximate side information.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-organized, and proofs are appropriately referenced to supplementary material.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on clustering-with-oracle and query/decision-tree complexity literature and differentiates from prior SBM analyses.  
- Strength: significant theoretical contribution with practical-adaptable algorithms.  
- Strength: framework and bounds extend beyond the specific paper setting.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to one dataset and one similarity function.  
- Weakness: proposed algorithms (especially iterative updates) are complex to implement in practice.  
- Weakness: constants and logarithmic factors in bounds may materially affect practical performance and need deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: excludes noisy oracle responses, limiting applicability to realistic crowdsourced settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental, novel, theory-backed contributions with preliminary empirical support and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope, implementation complexity, and practical impact of constants/log factors.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper whose strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand experimental evaluation, discuss practical implementation/optimizations, and acknowledge implications of constants/log factors.
