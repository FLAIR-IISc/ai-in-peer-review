- Problem studied: query complexity of clustering with side-information (similarity matrix), oracle answers same-cluster, distributions f+ and f- unknown.

- Main contribution: tight query complexity bounds showing reduction to O(k / (log n · H²(f+||f-))) with matching upper and lower bounds.

- Novel theoretical tool: new framework for proving interactive lower bounds where standard Fano-type techniques fail.

- Algorithms: parameter-free, computationally efficient (O(n²)), and do not require knowing k, f+ or f-.

- Generalization: extends stochastic block model results to arbitrary unknown distributions and no cluster-size assumptions.

- Empirical validation: experiments on the Cora dataset show high accuracy with far fewer queries than naive methods.

- Strength — theoretical rigor: strong, clean analysis using Hellinger divergence and impressive lower bound proofs.

- Strength — practical relevance: directly applicable to entity resolution and crowdsourced clustering with query savings.

- Strength — parameter-free design: algorithms avoid needing hard-to-obtain parameters like k or distributional forms.

- Strength — comprehensive discussion: connects to SBM, sparse regimes, and outlines future research directions.

- Strength — clarity: contributions clearly distinguished from prior work.

- Weakness — limited experiments: only Cora dataset; needs more datasets and broader baseline comparisons.

- Weakness — perfect oracle assumption: unrealistic; noisy query handling is not addressed here.

- Weakness — technical density: lower bound proof is complex and could use more intuition or exposition.

- Weakness — weak SBM connection: conjectures about sparse SBM are interesting but not rigorously resolved.

- Weakness — scalability concerns: O(n²) time may be prohibitive for very large datasets.

- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with clear practical implications.

- Recommendation: accept. 

- Suggested improvements: expand experiments and baselines, discuss handling noisy oracles, add intuitive explanations and examples for algorithms, and broaden related-work coverage.
