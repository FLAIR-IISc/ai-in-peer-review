- Problem: Studies query complexity of exact clustering with adaptive same-cluster queries plus a noisy similarity matrix W from unknown distributions f+ (within) and f− (between), with unknown k and cluster sizes.  
- Lower bound: Any algorithm needs Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+‖f−)}) expected queries (and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for certainty) in regime H^2 ≤ 1/18; proof handles adaptivity via “weakly queried” clusters and a Hellinger-based change-of-measure.  
- Upper bound: Parameter-free polynomial-time Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms recover clustering using queries scaling polylogarithmically in n and inversely with H^2; Las Vegas variant uses O(n log n + ·) queries and algorithms assign points using estimated f+/f− and few queries for borderline cases.  
- Experiments: Cora experiments show a greedy side-information-guided strategy attains high F1 with far fewer queries than nk, but evaluation is limited.  
- Technical soundness: Lower-bound technique for adaptive querying is novel and careful; upper-bound analysis uses Sanov-type large deviations to justify log n/H^2 thresholds.  
- Rigor/support: Main proofs in appendices convey ideas, but inconsistencies in Monte Carlo query-complexity statements (k vs k^2 dependence) must be fixed to avoid contradicting the lower bound.  
- Empirical limitations: Single dataset, modest ablations, no stress tests across k or H^2, and no comparisons to strong external baselines.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and motivated, but needs pseudocode, notational consistency (H vs H^2), and fixes for typos like k/log n/H^2 vs k log n/H^2.  
- Originality: Novel adaptive-information lower bounds and parameter-free algorithmic design; model generalizes SBM to unknown parameters and arbitrary cluster sizes.  
- Significance: Sharpens when side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) toward log n/H^2 scalings and offers a potentially transferable lower-bound methodology; practical use limited by O(n^2) W and discrete-support assumptions.  
- Pros: Novel adaptive lower-bound technique; parameter-free near-optimal algorithms; general model beyond SBM; appropriate use of Hellinger distance; practical intuition supported by experiments.  
- Cons: Inconsistencies/typos in main theorem statements; limited empirical evaluation; discrete W assumption not fully addressed algorithmically; clarity and pseudocode missing.  
- Questions for authors: Reconcile Monte Carlo query complexity (is it O(min{nk, k log n/H^2}) or O(min{nk, k^2 log n/H^2})?); how essential is discrete-support assumption and can continuous W be quantized with provable effects on H^2?; provide pseudocode and justify |Waiting(C)| = O(1) w.h.p.; add experiments varying k and f+/f− separation.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance conditional on fixing theorem inconsistencies and clarity improvements; expanded experiments would strengthen the submission but are secondary to core theory.
