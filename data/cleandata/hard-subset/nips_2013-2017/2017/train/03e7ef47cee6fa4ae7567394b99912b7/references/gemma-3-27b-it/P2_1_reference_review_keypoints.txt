- Studies query complexity of clustering with a same-cluster oracle plus side-information similarity matrix.  
- Proves information-theoretic lower bounds and designs algorithms that nearly match them.  
- Shows side information can drastically reduce queries compared to O(nk) without side information.  
- Connects to and generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM) and handles arbitrary, unknown side-information distributions.  
- Provides strong theoretical foundation with rigorous proofs for lower and upper bounds.  
- Introduces parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of f+, f- or number of clusters k.  
- Establishes novelty in precisely characterizing the impact of side information on query complexity.  
- Argues practical relevance via entity resolution and notes heuristics often outperform theoretical bounds.  
- Situates work clearly within clustering, active learning, and community detection literature.  
- Includes experimental validation on the Cora dataset demonstrating practical performance.  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy; key concepts (e.g., Hellinger divergence) need more intuitive explanation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and narrow parameter settings; lacks broader comparisons to state-of-the-art heuristics.  
- Algorithmic upper bound contains a logarithmic factor whose practical impact is not discussed.  
- Assumption of fixed cardinality support for f+ and f- may be limiting; sensitivity to this assumption is unclear.  
- Ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—improve clarity, expand experiments, compare heuristics, and discuss the logarithmic factor and support assumption.
