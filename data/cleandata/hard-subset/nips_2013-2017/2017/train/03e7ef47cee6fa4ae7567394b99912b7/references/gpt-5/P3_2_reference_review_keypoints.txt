- Paper studies adaptive clustering with a perfect same-cluster oracle plus noisy side information W drawn from f+ / f−; asks minimal adaptive queries for exact recovery when k, f+, f− unknown.

- Main theoretical results: information-theoretic lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2/H2}) (and Ω(n+min{nk, k^2/H2}) for certainty) incorporating Hellinger divergence H2(f+||f−).

- Algorithms: parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas methods that grow a reliable cluster, estimate empirical p+, p−, and assign remaining points using Hellinger-based tests.

- Claimed algorithmic query complexities: Monte Carlo O(min{nk, (k log n)/H2}) and Las Vegas O(n log n + min{nk, (k^2 log n)/H2}).

- Connection to SBM: model generalizes SBM (unknown k, adversarial sizes, arbitrary f+, f−) and queries can shift SBM recovery thresholds.

- Empirical work: preliminary experiments on a CORA-based ER dataset showing greedy performs near-best.

- Lower-bound proof technique is insightful: reduction to k-ary hypothesis test, construction of weak clusters, and TV–Hellinger control.

- Lemma 1 has sign/formatting errors and mangled definitions (β, J′ thresholds); needs corrected constants and clear derivation.

- TV–Hellinger inequality is misused: authors mix H and H2 and incorrect constants; notation and constants must be standardized (||P−Q||TV ≤ sqrt(2) H).

- Lower bound restricted to H2 ≤ 1/18; paper lacks explicit discussion of behavior and statement validity beyond that regime.

- Algorithmic idea (grow a reliable cluster as anchor) is elegant and practical, using Hellinger distance and Sanov-style concentration.

- Major inconsistency: claimed Monte Carlo bound O((k log n)/H2) contradicts the lower bound Ω(k^2/H2) for large k; likely a typo that must be clarified and fixed across the paper.

- Phase-1 requirement that a cluster reaches size Ω((log n)/H2) may fail under adversarial small clusters; the fallback behavior and proof that queries stay O(nk) in worst case need to be spelled out.

- Analysis claims an extension of Sanov’s theorem to Hellinger via f-divergence inequalities but needs a clean lemma with assumptions, finite alphabet q, and explicit constants.

- Originality: formalizing side-information effect on query complexity, adaptive lower bounds, and parameter-free algorithms are novel contributions.

- Significance: timely and potentially impactful for explaining reduced query needs in practice and nearly tight information-theoretic limits.

- Clarity issues: inconsistent use of H vs H2, broken equations, inconsistent Monte Carlo query bounds, and missing pseudocode/termination logic.

- Assumptions on support size q and treatment of continuous similarities should be centralized and clarified.

- Experimental section is limited: single real dataset, no baselines or synthetic studies varying k, imbalance, and H2, and no error bars.

- Pros: important problem, rigorous formulation, novel adaptive lower-bound technique, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and SBM generalization.

- Cons: Monte Carlo vs lower-bound inconsistency, typos/notation errors in key proofs, unclear termination under tiny clusters, and weak experiments.

- Key questions for authors: resolve k vs k^2 dependence; guarantees if no cluster reaches Ω((log n)/H2); handling continuous similarities and effect of q; explicit constants in Sanov/Hellinger lemmas; robustness to early mis-specification of p+, p−.

- Minor requested fixes: correct broken equations, standardize H/H2 and TV–Hellinger inequality, provide pseudocode and per-phase complexity, and add comparisons and synthetic studies.

- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on fixing Monte Carlo inconsistency and clarifying theoretical and presentation issues; reviewer confidence high.
