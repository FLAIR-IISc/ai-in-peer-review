- Summary: Studies query complexity of interactive clustering with noisy pairwise side information W, giving an H2(f+||f−)-dependent lower bound and parameter-free algorithms nearly matching it up to logs, connecting to SBM and ER heuristics.

- Strength: Novel formalization bridging heuristic practice and worst-case theory for interactive clustering with side information.

- Strength: Clear quantification of side-information benefit via squared Hellinger divergence H2(f+||f−), matching intuition.

- Strength: Lower bound technique handles adaptivity using “lightly queried” elements and weak clusters; potentially reusable.

- Strength: Parameter-free, polynomial-time Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms; iterative threshold learning is practically attractive.

- Strength: Thought-provoking connection to SBM and how active queries might shift recovery thresholds.

- Weakness: Inconsistent/ambiguous bounds, especially k-dependence (various places state O(k/(log n H2)), O((k log n)/H2), O((k^2 log n)/H2), lower bound Ω(k^2/H2)); this undermines the “matches up to log” claim.

- Weakness: Lower bound proof sketch uses loose steps and ad hoc constants; needs precise statements, regimes, and extension from equal-size clusters to arbitrary partitions.

- Weakness: Unclear use of Sanov’s theorem (KL-based) to obtain uniform Hellinger deviation bounds; needs a precise KL→Hellinger lemma with constants for finite support.

- Weakness: Modeling assumption of independent W entries given clustering is strong; paper should discuss robustness to realistic pairwise correlation violations or provide counterexamples.

- Weakness: Experiments very limited (single dataset cora), few baselines, no ablations over k/H2/size, and unclear how f± are estimated without label leakage.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — typos, ambiguous O(·) notation, inconsistent use of H vs H2, dense algorithm description lacking pseudocode/figures/invariants.

- Quality: Technically strong and plausible contributions, but must resolve k vs k^2 discrepancy and improve clarity; experiments not commensurate with theory.

- Originality: First to give information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching parameter-free algorithms tying query complexity to Hellinger divergence and treating adaptivity — novel in this context.

- Significance: High theoretical significance; potential reference point for active clustering/SBM, with practical impact dependent on robustness and scalability.

- Questions for authors: Reconcile Theorems 1/2—correct k-dependence and regimes for Monte Carlo bound?

- Questions for authors: Provide exact lemma translating Sanov/KL guarantees to Hellinger confidence intervals in finite-support setting with constants.

- Questions for authors: Do algorithmic guarantees extend to continuous f±; what replaces histogram estimates p±?

- Questions for authors: How sensitive are guarantees to dependence in W (correlations among pairs sharing a node)?

- Questions for authors: How are f± estimated in experiments without leaking labels; add comparisons to prior active/crowd ER methods?

- Minor comments: Standardize notation (H2), clean up O(·) parentheses, complete references, add pseudocode and complexity table.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept — compelling theory but needs fixes to bounds/notation, clearer proofs, and stronger experiments.
