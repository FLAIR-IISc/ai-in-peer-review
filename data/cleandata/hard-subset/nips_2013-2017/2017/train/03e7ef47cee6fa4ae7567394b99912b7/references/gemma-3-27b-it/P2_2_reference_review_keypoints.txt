- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with an oracle plus side-information similarity matrix, proving side information can drastically reduce queries and providing Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that nearly match lower bounds while generalizing SBM.

- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous, novel information-theoretic lower bounds and adaptive-query proofs.

- Practical relevance: setting motivated by entity resolution and active learning with potential to cut labeling/query costs.

- Parameter-free algorithms: methods require no prior knowledge of cluster sizes or number of clusters.

- Experimental validation: initial experiments on the CORA dataset support effectiveness.

- Generally clear writing and well-motivated introduction, though technical sections are dense.

- Weakness: high technical depth and accessibility—some proofs need more explanation for broader audiences.

- Weakness: limited experimental scope—only one dataset (CORA); needs more diverse evaluations across cluster structures and noise levels.

- Weakness: insufficient detail on computational complexity, particularly the iterative update step in the Monte Carlo algorithm.

- Weakness: limited analysis of why existing heuristics perform well and how the proposed algorithms compare in practice.

- Weakness: discussion of noisy oracle responses is deferred to companion work and needs at least a brief treatment here.

- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, provides missing lower bounds, practical algorithms with guarantees, relevant connections to SBM/entity resolution.

- Arguments against acceptance: paper is technically dense, experiments are limited, and relation to heuristics needs deeper analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: expand experiments to additional datasets; provide a detailed comparison to existing heuristics; clarify runtime of iterative update in Monte Carlo algorithm; add a brief discussion on sensitivity to oracle noise.
