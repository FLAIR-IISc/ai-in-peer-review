- Summary: Characterizes minimum query complexity for exact clustering with side information using Hellinger divergence between within-/between-cluster similarity distributions.
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes SBM-style models while relaxing assumptions (no need to know k, cluster sizes, or distributions).
- Strength — Theoretical contributions: Provides tight upper and lower bounds (notably Theorem 2) for interactive query complexity.
- Strength — Novel methodology: Uses Hellinger divergence as a quality measure and introduces a parameter-free iterative-update algorithm.
- Strength — Practical relevance: Bridges theory and practice; experiments on Cora show near-oracle performance.
- Strength — Analysis depth: Thorough theoretical treatment, including an extension of Sanov’s theorem to Hellinger distance.
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Evaluation uses only the Cora dataset, limiting empirical validation across distributions and noise levels.
- Weakness — Unrealistic assumption: Assumes a perfect similarity matrix, reducing practical applicability.
- Weakness — Scalability concerns: Algorithm’s O(n^2) time may be prohibitive for large datasets; scalability/optimizations not discussed.
- Weakness — Comparison gap: Lacks rigorous empirical comparison to state-of-the-art heuristic methods that may use fewer queries.
- Weakness — Tightness question: The logarithmic gap between upper and lower bounds is not shown to be inherent or closable.
- Overall assessment: Strong, original theoretical contribution with clear significance; main limitations are empirical breadth and practical assumptions.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Suggested improvements: Add more datasets for experiments, discuss practical limitations of the side-information assumption, and include detailed comparisons with heuristic algorithms.
