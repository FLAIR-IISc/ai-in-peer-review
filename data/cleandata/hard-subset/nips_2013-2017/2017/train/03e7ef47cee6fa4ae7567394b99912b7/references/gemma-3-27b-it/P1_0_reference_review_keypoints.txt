- Studies query complexity of clustering with an oracle when side information (similarity matrix) is available.  
- Establishes information-theoretic lower bounds and designs algorithms that nearly match them.  
- Shows query complexity can be significantly reduced versus O(nk) without side information, potentially sublinear in n depending on side information quality and k.  
- Connects the model to the stochastic block model (SBM) while generalizing to arbitrary f+ and f- and making no cluster-size/k assumptions.  
- Provides both Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms with proofs.  
- Motivated by practical applications such as entity resolution and crowdsourcing.  
- Differentiates from prior work by focusing on information-theoretic lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms.  
- Correctly positions itself relative to literature on interactive clustering, SBMs, and active learning.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: rigorous information-theoretic analysis and handling of adaptive querying.  
- Presents parameter-free algorithms that do not require distributional or cluster-count knowledge.  
- Clear statement of theorems and algorithm descriptions linking theory to design.  
- Includes experiments (Cora) that validate practical performance and benefits of side information.  
- Provides detailed, extensive proofs in the appendix.  
- Proofs are dense and may be difficult to follow without substantial information-theory background.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Cora), limiting empirical generality.  
- Paper emphasizes asymptotic bounds without discussing constant factors affecting runtime and memory.  
- Heavy reliance on Hellinger divergence; other divergence measures are not explored.  
- Assumes a perfect oracle and lacks discussion of noisy oracle responses and practical mitigation strategies.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, original, and significant; recommended as Strong Accept.  
- Suggested minor revisions: discuss noisy oracles, clarify constant factors qualitatively, and consider additional datasets for experiments.
