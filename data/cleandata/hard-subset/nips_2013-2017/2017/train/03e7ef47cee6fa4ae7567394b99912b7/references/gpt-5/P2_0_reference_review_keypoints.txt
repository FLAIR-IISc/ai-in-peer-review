- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with side information given by similarity matrix entries drawn from f+ (same cluster) or f− (different clusters) plus a same-cluster oracle.
- Main theoretical contributions: information-theoretic lower bounds showing any algorithm needs Ω(min{n k, k^2 / H^2(f+‖f−)}) expected queries and Ω(n + min{n k, k^2 / H^2}) for certainty (H^2 ≤ 1/18).
- Algorithmic contributions: parameter-free polynomial-time Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms (unknown k, f+, f−) achieving O(min{n k, (k log n) / H^2(f+‖f−)}) queries up to logs and O(n^2) runtime.
- Methodological contributions: a general lower-bound technique for adaptive queries using Hellinger distance and an iterative-update method that estimates f+, f− and uses thresholds ~ log n / H^2 to decide querying.
- Conceptual connection drawn to stochastic block models (SBM) and how queries lower recovery thresholds; small experiments on Cora ER dataset illustrate algorithm behavior.
- Technical soundness: lower-bound framework and reduction to per-element multi-hypothesis testing are significant and well-motivated; upper-bound strategy is sensible and nearly matches lower bounds.
- Rigor/completeness concerns: some analyses deferred to appendices; main text has notational ambiguities and apparent sign/formatting errors that impede verification.
- Specific correctness issues: ambiguous typesetting of k^2 / H^2 vs k^2 · H^2, likely sign error in Lemma 1, and inconsistent statements about Las Vegas complexity that must be fixed.
- Empirics: experiments are minimal, compare only internal strategies, and lack baselines or comparisons to prior ER/crowdsourcing heuristics.
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation for complexity terms, distracting inconsistencies between lemmas/theorems, scattered algorithm definitions, and need for pseudocode/figure and a Las Vegas proof sketch.
- Writing strengths: overall organization, clear problem formulation, motivation, and justification for using Hellinger distance.
- Originality: high — first to provide interactive adaptive-query lower bounds in this general side-information model and parameter-free adaptive algorithms under unknown parameters.
- Significance: sharpens theoretical understanding of interactive clustering with side information and provides reusable lower-bound and algorithmic patterns; practical impact plausible but needs more empirical validation.
- Pros for acceptance: strong lower-bound method, parameter-free near-matching algorithms, clear positioning relative to ER/SBM, and promising future directions.
- Cons against acceptance: clarity/notation/typo issues in key theorems, limited experimental validation, and some assumptions (noiseless oracle, finite support) need broader discussion.
- Suggestions: fix all notation/typos (k^2 / H^2 vs k^2·H^2), correct Lemma 1 sign, reconcile Monte Carlo/Las Vegas complexity, add compact pseudocode and key Sanov-to-Hellinger lemma in main text, and expand experiments with baselines and sensitivity analyses.
- Overall recommendation: inclined to accept contingent on fixing clarity/notation issues; theoretical contributions are substantial and timely.
