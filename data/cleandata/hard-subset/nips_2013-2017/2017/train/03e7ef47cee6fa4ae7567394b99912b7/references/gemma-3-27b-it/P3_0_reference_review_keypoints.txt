- Studies query complexity of clustering with an oracle plus side-information similarity matrix, showing side information can drastically reduce queries versus O(nk).
- Proves lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k²H²(f+||f−)}) and provides matching upper bounds via Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms.
- Introduces Hellinger divergence H²(f+||f−) as the key quantity governing query complexity.
- Connects and generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM) to arbitrary distributions and cluster sizes.
- Presents parameter-free algorithms that don't require knowledge of distributions or number of clusters.
- Provides a clear, well-organized theoretical exposition with consistent notation.
- Includes experimental validation on the CORA dataset showing preliminary practical effectiveness.
- Situates the work within interactive clustering, crowdsourcing, entity resolution, and related literature comprehensively.
- Strength: strong, novel theoretical contribution with tight bounds and practical algorithm design.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to one dataset and one similarity function; lacks comparisons to existing ER heuristics.
- Weakness: constant and logarithmic factors' practical impact are not analyzed.
- Weakness: algorithm descriptions are complex and could use simplification and illustrative examples.
- Weakness: assumes a perfect oracle and does not analyze the effect of oracle errors/noise on query complexity.
- Weakness: paper does not deeply justify why Hellinger divergence is the most natural metric beyond theoretical convenience.
- Recommendation: accept (8/10) — significant theoretical advance; authors should expand experiments and clarify algorithm presentation.
