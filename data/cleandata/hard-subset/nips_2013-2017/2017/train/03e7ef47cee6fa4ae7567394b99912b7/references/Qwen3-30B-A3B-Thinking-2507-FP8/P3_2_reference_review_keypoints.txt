- Paper analyzes query complexity of clustering with side information and shows queries drop from Ω(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f⁺‖f⁻))) using Hellinger divergence.  
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds and parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that nearly match the bounds.  
- Connects results to the stochastic block model (SBM) and recovery thresholds when queries are allowed.  
- Theoretical rigor is strong with well-constructed proofs, especially for lower bounds in interactive settings.  
- Introduces a novel framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive problems.  
- Algorithms are parameter-free, not requiring knowledge of k, f⁺, or f⁻, enhancing practical applicability.  
- Clear statement of contribution resolving the gap between Ω(nk) bounds and heuristic low-query usage.  
- Experimental validation is limited: only one dataset (Cora) and few parameter variations.  
- Ambiguity in distribution assumptions: lower bound claimed for continuous distributions but proved for discrete ones without clear extension.  
- Overreliance on Hellinger distance without fully justifying its choice over KL divergence, and weakly explained link to Sanov’s theorem.  
- Algorithmic complexity claimed O(n²) but lacks clear explanation of handling the full n×n side-information matrix in practice.  
- Limited experimental baselines: only compares to “best” and “worst” strategies, missing comparisons to existing heuristics.  
- Lemma 1 to Theorem 2 transition is abrupt; need clarification why the two parameter cases exhaust regimes.  
- Iterative-update threshold τₓ = C log n / H(p⁺,ₓ‖p⁻,ₓ)² needs clearer justification and connection to lower bound.  
- Sanov’s theorem application requires better explanation why Hellinger distance is the appropriate metric.  
- Experimental details are lacking: how Hellinger squared divergence (0.6) was computed from similarity scores is unspecified.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical contribution with strong results and valuable parameter-free algorithms, but practical impact limited by sparse experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, rigorous theory merits publication despite need for stronger experimental support.  
- Revision request: expand experiments to multiple datasets and compare with literature heuristics.  
- Revision request: clarify extension of lower bound to continuous distributions and justify use of Hellinger over KL.  
- Revision request: explain how to implement algorithms efficiently without building the full n×n matrix for large n.  
- Revision request: analyze sensitivity to model deviations (e.g., poorly separated f⁺ and f⁻) and discuss computational scaling with n.
