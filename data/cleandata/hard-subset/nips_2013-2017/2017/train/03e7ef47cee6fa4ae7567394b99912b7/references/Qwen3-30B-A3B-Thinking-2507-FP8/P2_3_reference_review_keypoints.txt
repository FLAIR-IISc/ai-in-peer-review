- Studies query complexity of clustering with side information (similarity matrix), generalizing clustering with an oracle.
- Main theoretical result: query complexity reduced from O(nk) to O(k / (log n * H²(f₊||f₋))) where H is Hellinger divergence.
- Two major contributions: (1) framework for information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering with side information; (2) efficient parameter-free algorithms nearly matching the bounds.
- Lower bound (Theorem 2) is technically strong, adapting Sanov’s theorem to Hellinger divergence for interactive learning.
- Algorithmic contributions include elegant Monte Carlo and Las Vegas parameter-free algorithms that do not require f₊, f₋, or k.
- Experiments on the Cora dataset show the greedy strategy closely matches best performance with minimal queries, supporting practical relevance.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs for both lower and upper bounds.
- Writing and structure are clear and well-motivated, though the lower bound proof is complex and iterative updates could use more intuition.
- Originality: identifies Hellinger divergence as the right metric for side information’s effect and extends the oracle model meaningfully.
- Provides a meaningful theoretical bridge to the stochastic block model (SBM) and generalizes previous SBM results.
- Significant practical implications for entity resolution and crowdsourced labeling by substantially reducing required human queries.
- Strengths: first rigorous analysis with side information, matching lower/upper bounds up to logs, parameter-free algorithms, strong theory-experiment link.
- Weaknesses: experiments limited to a single dataset, reliance on a generative model for side information, and the SBM connection could be explored further.
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS — substantial theoretical advance with practical implications and strong technical quality.
