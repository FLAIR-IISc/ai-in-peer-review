- Problem: Studies query complexity of exact clustering with a noiseless same-cluster oracle plus pairwise similarity matrix W drawn from unknown within-/across-cluster distributions f+, f− and unknown k, allowing adversarial cluster sizes.

- Main lower bounds: Information-theoretic Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+‖f−)}) expected-query bound (for H^2 ≤ 1/18) and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for success probability 1.

- Algorithms: Two parameter-free algorithms — Monte Carlo using O(min{nk, k/(log n · H^2)}) queries with high probability, and Las Vegas using O(n log n + min{nk, k^2/(log n · H^2)}) queries with certainty; both run in O(n^2) time.

- Techniques: New interactive lower-bound method using Hellinger divergence and an iterative, parameter-free estimator design that learns f+, f− on the fly.

- Connections: Relates to SBM as a special case and discusses how queries shift exact-recovery thresholds.

- Experiments: Limited empirical validation on one entity-resolution dataset showing query/accuracy tradeoff and some robustness to approximate f+, f−.

- Relation to prior work: Generalizes SBM results to arbitrary unknown f+, f− and adversarial cluster sizes, and contrasts with query-only clustering and crowdsourced ER heuristics lacking guarantees.

- Technical soundness: High-level arguments for lower and upper bounds are plausible; lower bound reduces to multi-hypothesis testing and upper bounds use Sanov-type estimation and Hellinger-based affinity scores.

- Notational/clarity errors: Several typographical and notation mistakes (e.g., missing "1 −" in Lemma 1, incorrect expression "k 2 H2" vs intended k^2/H^2) obscure exact statements.

- Potential tension: Apparent discrepancy between the lower bound Ω(k^2/H^2) and the Monte Carlo upper bound O(k/(log n · H^2)) needs reconciliation (different success probabilities or model assumptions may explain gap).

- Clarity issues: Many typos (H2 vs H^2, on(1)/ok(1)), incomplete references, dangling footnotes, missing pseudocode, and undefined constants (C, B) impede readability.

- Theoretical details under-specified: Need explanation why Hellinger is chosen over KL, how Sanov is adapted for Hellinger, and what “fixed cardinality of support” means for f+, f− extensions to continuous supports.

- Originality: Formulation of query complexity with side information and unknown parameters is novel; lower-bound technique for adaptive interactive algorithms and parameter-free iterative estimation are notable contributions.

- Significance: Near-tight characterization (up to logs) of how side information reduces queries in a general setting that extends SBM; techniques are likely reusable.

- Pros: Important, well-motivated problem; general model; new interactive lower-bound methodology; parameter-free, efficient algorithms with provable guarantees; connections to SBM.

- Cons: Core theorem notations/typos need fixing; apparent LB vs Monte Carlo UB gap; thin experimental evaluation (single dataset, no baselines or variance reporting); some assumptions/steps under-specified.

- Requests for authors: Fix Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 expressions, provide a precise comparison table of assumptions/success rates for LBs vs UBs, supply pseudocode and definitions for thresholds/constants/pC, and discuss robustness to large/continuous supports.

- Empirical requests: Add comparisons to prior query-based clustering/ER heuristics, report variance over runs, and expand datasets.

- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical contribution with high impact potential contingent on correcting bound statements, reconciling the Monte Carlo vs LB discrepancy, and improving clarity and experiments; reviewer leans toward acceptance if addressed.
