- Studies query complexity of exact clustering with adaptive same-cluster queries plus noisy similarity matrix W under unknown k, f+, f−.
- Generative model: pairwise similarities drawn from f+ within clusters and f− across clusters; f+, f−, k unknown to algorithm.
- Information-theoretic lower bounds: Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+||f−)}) expected queries for constant-probability recovery, and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+||f−)}) for guaranteed recovery.
- Lower bounds hold for discrete or continuous f+, f− and use a novel adaptivity-aware argument based on Hellinger divergence.
- Presents parameter-free polynomial-time algorithms: a Monte Carlo procedure using empirical Hellinger thresholds and a Las Vegas variant.
- Algorithmic idea: grow clusters to size ≈ Θ((log n)/H^2), estimate f+, f− from W, classify remaining points using a Hellinger-based membership score and query when uncertain.
- Claimed query complexity of algorithms matches lower bounds up to logarithmic factors.
- Empirical evidence on the Cora dataset shows large query reductions with high F1 and robustness to imperfect f+ / f− estimates.
- Work generalizes SBM insights to arbitrary unknown f+, f−, unknown k, and adversarial cluster sizes, quantifying when queries help.
- Originality: first information-theoretic treatment of clustering with side information and adaptive same-cluster queries under very general assumptions.
- Novel lower-bound technique (Hellinger-based) for adaptive querying potentially useful beyond this work.
- Technical quality: careful adaptivity-aware lower bound and parameter-free, computationally efficient algorithm (O(n^2) to read W).
- Clarity: high-level ideas like membership function and iterative updates are well motivated; Hellinger role is explained.
- Inconsistency in Monte Carlo query bounds across the text; needs reconciliation with Theorem 2 and precise regime statements.
- Algorithm assumes discrete f+, f− for empirical histograms; unclear how to handle continuous W (e.g., Gaussian similarities) and impacts of discretization on complexity.
- Concentration analysis relies on Sanov-type bounds and anti-concentration; notation and constants (H vs H^2, TV relations) need tightening for rigor.
- Theorem 2 uses small-separation assumption H^2 ≤ 1/18 to get specific constants; clarify whether this is essential or cosmetic.
- Experiments limited: single dataset, no comparisons to prior heuristics, no synthetic sweeps over H^2 and k, and no runtime measurements.
- Presentation issues: typographical glitches, incomplete citations, inconsistent equation formatting, and notational slips need proofreading.
- Suggestions: unify and clearly state Monte Carlo guarantees, discuss continuous W and Hellinger estimation, add synthetic experiments varying H^2 and k, compare to heuristics, and clean up presentation.
- Pros: novel adaptive lower-bound methodology, parameter-free near-optimal algorithm, clear connection to SBM.
- Cons: inconsistent algorithmic bounds, limited empirical validation, notation/clarity issues and discrete vs continuous technical gaps.
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending clarifications and minor additions; theoretical contributions are strong and timely.
