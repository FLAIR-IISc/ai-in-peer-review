- Paper studies query complexity of exact clustering with same-cluster queries plus noisy similarity matrix W from unknown distributions f+ and f−, generalizing SBM.
- Main theoretical contributions: information-theoretic lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2/H2}) and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H2}) (for probability-1 success) in terms of Hellinger divergence H2(f+‖f−).
- Presents two parameter-free algorithms: a Monte Carlo algorithm (claimed O(min{nk,(k log n)/H2}) queries, success 1−o(1/n)) and a Las Vegas algorithm (O(n log n + min{nk,(k^2 log n)/H2}) queries, success 1).
- Introduces a proof framework for adaptive lower bounds using product-structure Hellinger divergence and an iterative-update algorithm that estimates distributions and adapts thresholds.
- Small empirical study on bibliographic entity-resolution showing a greedy query-ordering strategy performs near a best-case oracle.
- Model significantly generalizes SBM (beyond Bernoulli edges, unknown k, adversarial cluster sizes) and emphasizes interactive querying.
- Lower-bound technique handles adaptive queries and goes beyond non-interactive Fano-style arguments.
- Algorithms are parameter-free, contrasting with recent SBM work that assumes parameter knowledge.
- Novel technical ideas: reduction to k-ary hypothesis testing, identification of “weak” clusters, and use of Hellinger for product distributions.
- Practical motivation and empirical demonstration connect theory to entity resolution applications.
- Major weakness: apparent inconsistency between the Monte Carlo upper bound and the Ω(k^2/H2) lower bound that must be reconciled.
- Presentation issues: ambiguous Big-O expressions, inconsistent use of H vs H2, reliance on appendices for key lemmas, and missing/incomplete references and typos.
- Experimental validation is limited: single dataset, no comparisons to established heuristics, no stress tests over k, imbalance, or Hellinger, and unclear oracle/noise model in experiments.
- Technical assumptions need clarification: requirement H2 ≤ 1/18 and the necessity/sharpness of this regime.
- Important proof details (anti-concentration, Waiting(C) constant) need more intuition or brief proofs in main text.
- Quality: core ideas are sound and technically novel, but Monte Carlo vs lower-bound discrepancy and omitted derivations weaken the presentation.
- Clarity: generally readable but hampered by notation ambiguities and heavy dependence on appendix material.
- Originality: high — adaptive lower-bound framework, parameter-free iterative algorithm, and general side-information model are novel.
- Significance: high for theory community — quantifies side-information value in interactive clustering and could influence active learning and SBM with queries.
- Key suggested fixes: reconcile Monte Carlo bound, standardize Hellinger notation, outline Sanov-to-Hellinger conversions, explain Lemma 6 intuition, expand experiments, and discuss behavior under cluster-size imbalance.
- Pros: strong, nearly tight theory; new adaptive lower-bound technique; parameter-free efficient algorithms; meaningful SBM generalization.
- Cons: possible Monte Carlo vs lower-bound inconsistency, clarity/notation problems, and weak empirical validation.
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on clarifying the Monte Carlo bound and improving exposition.
- Reviewer confidence: high on lower-bound framework and significance; moderate on exact algorithmic query bounds due to the noted discrepancy.
