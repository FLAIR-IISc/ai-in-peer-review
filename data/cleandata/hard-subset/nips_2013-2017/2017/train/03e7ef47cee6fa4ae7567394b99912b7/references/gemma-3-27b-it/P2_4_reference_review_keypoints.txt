- Paper studies query complexity for clustering with an oracle plus side-information similarity matrix, giving lower bounds and matching algorithms.  
- Shows side information can substantially reduce queries versus classical Ω(nk) bounds.  
- Connects results to the stochastic block model (SBM) for theoretical grounding.  
- Provides parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms and an iterative-update strategy to handle unknown f+ and f-.  
- Proves rigorous information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly tight upper bounds (within a logarithmic factor).  
- Experiments on a bibliographic (Cora) dataset validate theory and show robustness to approximate side information.  
- Strong practical motivation via an entity resolution example.  
- Novel problem formulation at the intersection of clustering, active learning, and information theory.  
- Limited experimental scope — only a single dataset, needs more diverse evaluations.  
- Paper is dense and technically heavy; presentation/readability could be streamlined and redundancies removed.  
- Lacks deeper discussion on practical issues of estimating f+ and f- and algorithm sensitivity to estimation errors.  
- Upper bounds match lower bounds only up to a logarithmic factor; reducing that factor is an open improvement.  
- Overall technically sound, significant, and well-motivated work with recommended acceptance pending minor revisions.
