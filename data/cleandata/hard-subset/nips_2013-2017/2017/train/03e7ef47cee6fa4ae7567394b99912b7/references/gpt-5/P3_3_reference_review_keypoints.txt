- Paper studies query complexity of exact clustering with same-cluster oracle plus noisy pairwise similarity matrix W from unknown f+ (within) and f− (across).
- Main contributions: information-theoretic lower bound Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for probability-1 success using squared Hellinger H^2.
- Main contributions: two parameter-free algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) that do not need k, f+, f−, with claimed query bounds and differing success guarantees.
- Main contributions: a general lower-bound framework handling adaptive queries via under-queried-element arguments and product Hellinger bounds.
- Main contributions: an iterative parameter-free estimator for p+, p− using Hellinger-based tests to switch between side-information use and querying.
- Main contributions: a small Cora ER experiment showing greedy query strategies guided by side information achieve good F1 with far fewer than nk queries.
- Relates to and generalizes SBM by allowing arbitrary unknown f+, f− and adversarial cluster sizes; connects to query-based clustering and active learning for SBM.
- Strength: Identifies squared Hellinger H^2(f+||f−) as the key quantity governing query savings and explains why heuristics need far fewer than nk queries.
- Strength: Nontrivial lower bounds that handle adaptivity and unknown parameters; parameter-free algorithms are practically valuable.
- Strength: Iterative-update idea adapting thresholds empirically is elegant and potentially reusable; Las Vegas algorithm matches lower bound up to log n.
- Weakness: Inconsistency/confusion in stated Monte Carlo upper bounds (different placements of k and log n) that may contradict the lower bound by a factor of k.
- Weakness: Notational/confidence issues around Hellinger vs squared Hellinger and incorrect/unclear use of inequalities relating TV, Hellinger, and KL—needs standardization of constants.
- Weakness: Lower-bound proof has delicate steps needing clarification: construction of weak clusters (β, T), transition from TV to Hellinger for product distributions, and reliance on Markov’s inequality producing k^2 term.
- Weakness: Assumptions on f+, f− unclear—analysis is for small discrete support, extensions to larger alphabets/continuous distributions are sketched but lack detail.
- Weakness: Algorithmic thresholds and stopping criteria depend on Sanov/Hoeffding bounds; precise concentration statements for empirical Hellinger estimates are missing.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited (single dataset), lack baselines and synthetic sweeps over k, imbalance, and H^2; do not report exact recovery or how often side information suffices.
- Quality: Strong technical direction and compelling lower/upper bound program, but inconsistencies and notational ambiguities hinder verification; proofs need tightening.
- Clarity: Generally well-organized and motivated but marred by typographical and internal inconsistencies (k vs k^2, log placement, H vs H^2); assumptions should be stated prominently.
- Originality: High — first general interactive clustering analysis with arbitrary unknown f+, f− giving information-theoretic lower bounds and near-matching parameter-free algorithms.
- Significance: High if inconsistencies are resolved; quantifies when side information reduces query complexity with implications for active ER and SBM thresholds.
- Pros for acceptance: novel problem formulation, strong adaptive lower-bound framework, parameter-free efficient algorithms, bridges theory and practice.
- Cons for acceptance: contradictions between upper and lower bounds, notational/confidence issues in proofs, and weak empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on clarifying/correcting upper-bound statements (k vs k^2 and log n placement), tightening notation/proofs, and strengthening experiments.
