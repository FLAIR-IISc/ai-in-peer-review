This paper investigates the query complexity of clustering with side information, motivated by applications like entity resolution where obtaining full pairwise comparisons is expensive. The authors establish both upper and lower bounds on the number of queries needed to accurately cluster data when a similarity matrix (side information) is available. They generalize the stochastic block model (SBM) and derive information-theoretic lower bounds, demonstrating a reduction in query complexity compared to clustering without side information. They also present efficient algorithms that nearly match these lower bounds, and are parameter-free.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a practically relevant problem. The connection to entity resolution provides a concrete application and helps contextualize the work. The generalization of the SBM is a significant contribution, allowing for arbitrary distributions and cluster sizes. The rigorous theoretical analysis, including both lower and upper bounds, is a strength. The claim of parameter-free algorithms is compelling, as it simplifies practical application. The experimental results on a real dataset provide empirical support for the theoretical findings, though are somewhat limited in scope.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses in clarity and organization. The introduction is lengthy and contains considerable background information, making it difficult to quickly grasp the core contributions. The presentation of the theorems and proofs is dense and requires significant effort to follow. More intuitive explanations and examples would enhance understanding. The experimental section, while present, is not particularly comprehensive. The setup and evaluation metrics could be discussed in more detail. The analysis of the results is also brief. Furthermore, the jump between the theoretical discussion and the algorithm design could be smoother. The authors mention a companion work on noisy queries, and this connection could be made more explicit. There's also a missed opportunity to discuss the implications of these findings on existing heuristic algorithms used in entity resolution. While the authors acknowledge the limitations of their work, a more thorough discussion of future directions would be beneficial. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with well-established lower and upper bounds.
*   **Generalization of SBM:**  Extending the SBM to arbitrary distributions is a significant contribution.
*   **Parameter-Free Algorithms:** The development of algorithms that do not require knowledge of underlying distributions is practical and valuable.
*   **Motivating Application:** The connection to entity resolution provides a clear and relevant application.
*   **Novelty:** Addresses a gap in the theoretical understanding of clustering with side information.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity and Organization:**  The paper is dense and difficult to follow in places.
*   **Limited Experimentation:**  The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
*   **Algorithm Presentation:** The transition from theory to algorithm design could be smoother.
*   **Discussion:** Could benefit from a more thorough discussion of future directions.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of clustering with side information.
*   The generalization of the SBM is a valuable extension.
*   The parameter-free algorithms are a practical contribution.
*   The paper addresses an important problem with relevance to real-world applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper is dense and difficult to read, potentially limiting its impact.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited.
*   The presentation of the technical details could be improved for clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. While the clarity and organization could be improved, the strength of the theoretical results and the potential impact of the parameter-free algorithms make it a worthwhile submission. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the authors should consider adding more intuitive explanations, providing more details in the experimental section, and discussing the implications of their findings for existing methods.
