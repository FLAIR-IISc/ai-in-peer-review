- Main result: Side information (f+, f-) reduces query complexity from O(nk) to O(k / (log n · H²(f+‖f-))) using Hellinger divergence.  
- Algorithms: Novel Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms achieve near-optimal query complexity.  
- Lower bounds: New information-theoretic framework provides matching lower bounds for interactive learning settings.  
- Theoretical rigor: Formal theorems and rigorous proofs for both upper and lower bounds; lower-bound proof notably novel.  
- Parameter-free: Algorithms do not require knowledge of f+, f-, or k, increasing practical applicability.  
- Generality: Model generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary f+ and f- distributions.  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on the Cora dataset shows greedy strategy performs close to theoretical optimum.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and accessible to the NIPS audience.  
- Practical relevance: Results are important for real-world problems like entity resolution and crowdsourced labeling.  
- Broader impact: Framework could apply to other interactive learning problems and community detection.  
- Strength — rigorous contribution: Provides tight upper and lower bounds addressing a fundamental question.  
- Strength — novel techniques: Develops a new information-theoretic lower-bound framework for interactive settings.  
- Strength — strong experiments and connections: Good empirical support and clear ties to prior work and SBM.  
- Weakness — log n gap: Upper and lower bounds differ by a logarithmic factor, left as an open question.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: Validation is on a single dataset (Cora); more datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness — perfect side information: Assumes noise-free similarity matrix; noisy side information is deferred to companion work.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, original, well-executed theoretical and practical contribution.
