- Paper studies query complexity of interactive clustering with an oracle and side-information similarity matrix, proving information-theoretic lower bounds and giving near-matching Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms.  
- Algorithms operate parameter-free (unknown k, distributions, cluster count) and the work connects and generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM).  
- Fills a literature gap by providing theoretical guarantees for heuristics using side information and relates to recent community-detection/parameter-free SBM results.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: novel adaptation of Fano’s inequality and sophisticated lower-bound derivations for the interactive setting.  
- Novelty and generality: relaxes SBM assumptions, handles arbitrary similarity distributions, and requires no parameter knowledge.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated via an entity-resolution motivating example and experiments on the CORA dataset showing reduced query complexity.  
- Well-written, clearly organized paper with a comprehensive related-work discussion.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: needs more datasets, varying noise levels, comparisons with additional heuristics, and different similarity functions.  
- Algorithms match lower bounds only up to a logarithmic factor; implications and possibilities for removing this factor need clearer discussion.  
- Description of the iterative Phase 2 algorithm is high-level; convergence intuition, thresholds τ1/τ2, and constant C need clearer explanation.  
- Lower-bound proof is complex and may be hard for a broad audience; a more intuitive exposition of core ideas would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, nearly-matching algorithms, practical relevance, and strong presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental rigor, concerns about the logarithmic factor, and complexity of the lower-bound proof for accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept subject to minor revisions to expand experiments and provide more intuitive explanations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on experimental breadth and clearer algorithm/proof intuition.
