- Problem & contribution: Studies query complexity for clustering with an oracle and side-information similarity matrix; provides lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms that nearly match them.  
- Model & novelty: Generalizes SBM to arbitrary f+ and f- without knowing k or cluster sizes.  
- Theoretical quality: Strong technical results with a novel framework for interactive information-theoretic lower bounds (Theorem 2).  
- Algorithmic results: Efficient O(n²) parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of f+, f-, or k.  
- Information measure: Uses Hellinger divergence (Sanov extension) as a key analytical tool—novel and central to proofs.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on Cora support theory; greedy strategy with approximate distributions performs near-optimal.  
- Limitations acknowledged: Logarithmic gap between lower and upper bounds and specific assumptions on the side-information generative model.  
- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with detailed algorithms and proofs; lower bound proof is technical and may be hard to follow.  
- Suggested clarity improvement: Provide more intuition on why Hellinger divergence is the appropriate measure.  
- Originality: First rigorous theoretical analysis of clustering with side information and a novel Sanov-theorem extension using Hellinger distance.  
- Practical significance: Results reduce query complexity in practice (e.g., entity resolution, crowdsourcing) and make parameter-free methods applicable to real data.  
- Strengths summary: Novel theoretical framework, parameter-free efficient algorithms, tight bounds (up to log factor), SBM connection, and supportive experiments.  
- Weaknesses summary: Experiments limited to one dataset, restrictive generative model assumptions, technical difficulty of proofs, and a remaining logarithmic gap.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—high confidence due to significant, novel theoretical and practical contributions.
