- Problem: clustering with a same-cluster oracle plus noisy similarity matrix W with unknown within/across-cluster distributions f+, f− and unknown k; goal is exact recovery with adaptive queries.

- Lower bounds: information-theoretic limits showing Ω(min{nk, k^2/H2(f+‖f−)}) queries (and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H2}) for zero failure probability).

- Algorithms: parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that do not require k, f+, or f−; Monte Carlo is near-optimal within log n and Las Vegas achieves O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n / H2}).

- Lower-bound technique: new interactive/adaptive querying lower-bound framework leveraging Hellinger distance that goes beyond standard Fano arguments.

- Model generalization: conceptual extension of SBM to arbitrary unknown f+, f− and adversarial partitions, with discussion of how queries shift SBM recovery thresholds.

- Experiments: small empirical study on the Cora dataset showing query–accuracy tradeoffs and robustness to approximate f+, f−, but limited in scope.

- Relation to prior work: explains why ER/crowdsourcing heuristics can use far fewer queries, significantly generalizes SBM recovery results, and differs from prior interactive clustering assumptions.

- Theory quality: careful construction of adaptive lower bounds using “weak clusters” and product Hellinger distances; upper bounds supported by algorithmic design and Sanov-style concentration proofs (in appendix).

- Notational/consistency concern: serious ambiguity in big-O expressions for the Monte Carlo bound (written as O(k log n / H2) vs. expected O(k^2 log n / H2)) and inconsistent use of H vs H2; needs unification and parentheses fixes.

- Algorithmic clarity: the parameter-free iterative estimation is interesting but worst-case query complexity in Phases 1–2 under highly unbalanced/adversarial cluster sizes should be stated in the main text, not just appendix.

- Experimental limitations: no synthetic sweeps over H2, n, k, or imbalance, and no comparisons to established heuristics (e.g., Awasthi, Wang, Firmani, Vesdapunt); current experiments are suggestive but insufficient to validate theoretical scalings.

- Clarity fixes requested: fix big-O/parentheses, unify and define Hellinger notation at first use, state support assumptions for f+, f− in Theorem 1 and discuss continuous supports/discretization, clean references, and add concise pseudocode/flow diagram.

- Originality: novel adaptive lower-bound framework and a parameter-free algorithm matching information-theoretic limits up to log factors; insightful connection and conjecture for sparse regime.

- Significance: provides principled explanation for ER/crowdsourcing heuristics and may inform active learning and interactive testing, though practical uptake needs stronger experiments.

- Pros summary: strong, general adaptive lower bounds; parameter-free, computationally efficient algorithms near lower bounds; conceptual advance beyond SBM with relevant motivation.

- Cons summary: ambiguous/inconsistent query complexity statements and notation; key algorithmic proof/details deferred; limited empirical validation; current assumption of discrete finite support for f+, f−.

- Key suggestions: clarify Monte Carlo complexity and unify notation; bound queries for Phases 1–2 under adversarial cluster sizes; discuss handling continuous side information and discretization effects; add synthetic experiments sweeping H2, k, imbalance and compare baselines; provide concise pseudocode.

- Recommendation: Accept (leaning) — strong theoretical contributions, but address clarity and experimental expansions to strengthen the paper.
