- Problem: Studies adaptive clustering with a noiseless same-cluster oracle plus noisy pairwise side information from unknown distributions f+ and f−, asking the minimum oracle queries for exact recovery.
- Main lower bound: Information-theoretic lower bounds Ω(min{n k, k^2 / H^2(f+‖f−)}) in expectation and Ω(n + min{n k, k^2 / H^2}) for certainty, quantifying value of side information versus Ω(nk) without it.
- Algorithms: Parameter-free, efficient algorithms (no k, f+, f−) with query complexity O(min{n k, k log n / H^2}) Monte Carlo (whp) and O(n log n + min{n k, k^2 log n / H^2}) Las Vegas (probability 1).
- Tightness: Las Vegas algorithm matches the lower bound up to a log n factor.
- Model relation: Generalizes SBM (Bernoulli f+ / f−), informing how queries change SBM recovery thresholds and connecting to crowdsourced entity resolution.
- Technical soundness: Lower bound carefully handles adaptivity via k-hypothesis reduction and Hellinger/TV control; upper bounds use Sanov’s theorem and Hellinger concentration; iterative-update thresholding is well-motivated.
- Assumptions/scope: Lower bounds allow discrete/continuous f±; algorithms currently assume discrete finite support (authors claim extension possible); oracle is noiseless (noisy queries deferred).
- Adversarial setting: Results hold under adversarial cluster sizes, strengthening claims.
- Empirics: Experiments on Cora are limited—single dataset, no comparisons to prior interactive/crowdsourcing heuristics, and no ablations over k, H^2, or n.
- Clarity: Paper is generally readable with good intuition for Hellinger choice, but needs pseudocode, notation fixes, and clearer distinction between Monte Carlo and Las Vegas regimes earlier.
- Presentation issues: Typesetting glitches, ambiguous expressions (e.g., “k 2 H2”), misplaced footnotes, and incomplete references need cleaning.
- Originality: First info-theoretic adaptive clustering lower bound for arbitrary unknown f± and adversarial partitions; novel use of Hellinger and iterative-update online separation estimation.
- Significance: Quantifies tradeoff between separability and query budget, impacting theory (active community detection) and practice (entity resolution/crowdsourcing).
- Pros: Strong adaptive lower bounds, parameter-free efficient algorithms, near-tight Las Vegas result, unified Hellinger-based analysis, and SBM generalization.
- Cons/suggestions: Strengthen empirical section with baselines and multiple datasets, clarify notation, add pseudocode, discuss continuous similarities and discretization, address oracle noise briefly, and fix typos/references.
- Recommendation: Lean accept—technically strong, original, and significant; empirical and presentation improvements recommended.
- Reviewer confidence: High on theory; moderate on empirical assessment.
