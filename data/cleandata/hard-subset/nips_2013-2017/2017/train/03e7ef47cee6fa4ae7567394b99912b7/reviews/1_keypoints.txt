- Problem: clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W where Wij ~ f+ if same cluster and ~ f- if different cluster.
- Baseline: without side information, query complexity is Θ(nk).
- Main positive result: with side information, O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries suffice (H = Hellinger distance).
- Tightness: the upper bound is nearly tight—matching lower bound up to a log n factor.
- Relevance: closely related to the stochastic block model (SBM) literature but asks how queries change recovery thresholds.
- Insight: side information can drastically reduce query complexity and may even encode full clustering in extreme cases.
- Contribution: provides a principled way to assess similarity-matrix quality via the Hellinger distance between f+ and f-.
