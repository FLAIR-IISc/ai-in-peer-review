- Problem & contribution: Rigorous analysis of interactive clustering with side information (similarity matrix), showing query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+ || f-)).  
- Model: Side information modeled by distributions f+ (same-cluster) and f- (different-cluster); squared Hellinger divergence measures quality.  
- Theoretical results: Provides nearly matching information-theoretic lower and upper bounds on query complexity.  
- Connection to SBM: Relates and generalizes the stochastic block model, enabling use of SBM techniques.  
- Algorithms: Proposes parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Lower-bound framework: Introduces a novel general framework for proving lower bounds in interactive clustering.  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on the Cora dataset supports theoretical findings.  
- Strength — theory: Strong theoretical foundation with tight bounds.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Motivated by and applicable to entity resolution and real-world noisy similarity data.  
- Strength — parameter-free: Algorithms practical since they avoid needing prior parameter knowledge; iterative update promising.  
- Weakness — Hellinger interpretability: Hellinger divergence may be non-intuitive and needs guidance on estimation from real data.  
- Weakness — lower-bound assumptions: Lower bound relies on knowing k, f+, f-, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness — computational complexity: Algorithmic runtime, especially iterative updates, may be costly on large datasets.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: Empirical evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (Cora).  
- Originality: First rigorous study of side-information impact in interactive clustering; novel use of Hellinger divergence and lower-bound methods.  
- Significance: Advances fundamental understanding of query vs. side-information trade-offs and suggests new SBM-related research directions.  
- For acceptance: Tight theory, practical motivation, novelty, clear presentation, and SBM connection.  
- Against acceptance: Limited empirical validation, possible scalability issues, and interpretability concerns for Hellinger metric.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions on Hellinger interpretation, algorithm complexity, and expanded experiments.  
- Score: 8/10 (Strong Accept).
