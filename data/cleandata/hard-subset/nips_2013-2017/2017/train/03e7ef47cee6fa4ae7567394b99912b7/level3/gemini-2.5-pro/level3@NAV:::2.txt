This paper investigates the query complexity of interactive clustering when provided with noisy pairwise side-information. The authors model this side-information as a similarity matrix where values for same-cluster and different-cluster pairs are drawn from two distinct, unknown distributions, f+ and f-. This setup is a generalization of the well-studied Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The main contribution is a theoretical analysis providing information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on the number of queries needed for exact recovery. The results show a dramatic reduction in query complexity from Θ(nk) without side-information to nearly O(k^2 / H^2(f+||f-)), where H^2 is the squared Hellinger divergence.

**Strengths:**
The paper addresses a significant and well-motivated problem at the intersection of active learning and clustering. The theoretical contributions are potentially valuable; if correct, the tight bounds elegantly quantify the benefit of side-information, and the proposed parameter-free algorithms are of practical interest. The connection drawn between this problem and community detection in SBMs is insightful.

**Weaknesses:**
Despite its promising contributions, the paper is hindered by severe issues with clarity and technical precision that make it borderline for acceptance.

1.  **Quality of Writing:** The manuscript is poorly written, with numerous grammatical errors, confusing sentences, and formatting issues. This lack of clarity extends to the proofs, which are dense and extremely difficult to verify, raising concerns about their correctness.

2.  **Originality and Related Work:** The authors’ claims of novelty (e.g., "first work," "initiates study") are overstated. The paper fails to cite several relevant works, particularly from the literature on labeled or weighted SBMs. A critical omission is "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016), which is directly related to active querying in this context.

3.  **Technical Soundness and Clarity:** The formal model is not stated with sufficient precision, particularly regarding the minimax formulation and assumptions on cluster sizes (e.g., imbalanced clusters). Furthermore, Theorem 2 appears incomplete or incorrect as stated; it seems to implicitly assume k grows with n but fails to specify this condition, potentially contradicting known results for constant k where zero queries may suffice. The experimental evaluation is also weak, comparing only to trivial baselines rather than existing methods.

**Recommendation:**
While the core ideas are valuable, the paper in its current state is not ready for publication. However, the contributions seem significant enough to warrant a major revision. I would support acceptance on the condition that the authors undertake a thorough overhaul of the manuscript. This must include: (1) a complete proofreading to fix the pervasive language issues; (2) precise, unambiguous statements for all theorems, including all necessary conditions (e.g., any assumptions on k); (3) a revised introduction and related work section that accurately situates the contribution and discusses the missing literature; and (4) a strengthening of the experimental section.
