- Addresses query complexity of clustering with noiseless pairwise comparisons and random side information, providing upper and lower bounds.  
- Proposes parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that achieve query complexity near the theoretical lower bound.  
- Develops a theoretical interactive clustering framework showing side information (e.g., similarity matrix) reduces query complexity.  
- Uses Hellinger divergence as a central metric in deriving bounds.  
- Extends connections to the stochastic block model (SBM), generalizing SBM and exploring recovery thresholds under query-based settings.  
- Strength: novel and significant problem focus, bridging heuristics and rigorous theory; parameter-free algorithms have practical relevance.  
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions including information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Strength: iterative-update Monte Carlo algorithm is a promising parameter-free design framework.  
- Weakness: poor writing quality—dense text, grammar issues, typos, and an overly long abstract hindering readability.  
- Weakness: unclear model presentation—missing explicit cluster size restrictions and inconsistent use of terms like “Monte Carlo” and “Las Vegas.”  
- Weakness: omission of relevant prior work (e.g., SBM generalizations and active learning for community detection) weakens contextualization.  
- Weakness: some claims are overstated (e.g., “first work” or “initiate a rigorous study”) given existing related literature.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears incorrect for small k and relies on unclear assumptions (e.g., k → ∞), undermining the lower-bound rigor.  
- Weakness: experimental section is shallow, lacks comparisons to existing methods, and relies on trivial baselines.  
- Recommendations for authors: proofread and improve writing, clearly present model and assumptions, correct/clarify Theorem 2, revise overstated claims with citations, and strengthen experiments with meaningful baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novel parameter-free and efficient algorithms, and insightful SBM/Hellinger connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, omitted prior work, problematic theorem, and underdeveloped experiments.  
- Final recommendation: accept conditional on substantial revisions addressing the issues listed above.
