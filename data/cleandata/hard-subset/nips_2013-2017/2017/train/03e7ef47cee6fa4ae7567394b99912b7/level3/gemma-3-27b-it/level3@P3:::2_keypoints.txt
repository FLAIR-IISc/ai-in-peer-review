- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with pairwise oracle queries plus a noisy similarity matrix, aiming for upper and lower bounds.  
- Claims side information significantly reduces query complexity compared to no-side-information settings.  
- Core technical contribution: proposed upper bound O(k² log n H(f+||f−)) and lower bound Ω(min{nk, k² H(f+||f−)}).  
- Proofs are dense, hard to verify, and Theorem 2 appears problematic or implicitly assumes k → ∞ without stating it.  
- Formal model and minimax formulation are unclear; restrictions on cluster size and imbalanced clusters are not addressed.  
- Connection to stochastic block models (SBMs) and active-learning-in-SBM work is underexplored and under-cited (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016).  
- Claims of novelty (e.g., “first work”, “significantly generalize”) are overstated given prior related work like Ashtiani et al.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: compared only to trivial baselines, lacks benchmarking against existing clustering/active learning methods, and Cora results are unconvincing.  
- Writing quality is poor with confusing/incomplete sentences, grammar/formatting errors, and unclear terminology usage.  
- Minor presentation issues: overly long abstract, ambiguous Monte Carlo/Las Vegas phrasing, suspicious footnote notation, and appendix formatting problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, potentially significant theoretical results if correct, and a useful lower-bound framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unverifiable or flawed proofs, unclear model, poor writing, inadequate experiments, omitted prior work, and overstated novelty.  
- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical study but currently not ready for acceptance due to clarity, rigor, and polish issues.  
- Recommendation and required fixes: major revisions needed — improve clarity/writing, expand and clarify proofs (esp. Theorem 2), add missing related work, strengthen experiments with proper baselines, and explicitly address model/cluster-size assumptions.
