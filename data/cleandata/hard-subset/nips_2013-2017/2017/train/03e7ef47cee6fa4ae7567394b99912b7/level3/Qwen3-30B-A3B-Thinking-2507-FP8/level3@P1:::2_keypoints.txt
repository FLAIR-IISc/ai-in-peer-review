- Establishes query complexity for clustering with side information, showing reduction from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)).
- First rigorous study generalizing SBM to arbitrary distributions f+ and f- without requiring knowledge of k, f+, or f-.
- Main strengths: nearly tight upper and lower bounds, parameter-free algorithms, and strong connection to community detection literature.
- Writing quality is poor: numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent capitalization, and unclear/fragmented sentences that hinder comprehension.
- Novelty is overstated: relevant prior work (notably Gadde et al., ISIT 2016 on active learning for SBM) is omitted and claims of being "first" are inaccurate.
- Theorem 2 (lower bound) appears flawed for small k (e.g., k=2); paper must state conditions (e.g., k sufficiently large) under which the bound holds.
- Formal model is unclear: paper does not specify minimax vs. average-case setting and fails to address highly imbalanced cluster sizes.
- Experiments are weak: comparisons only to trivial baselines instead of benchmarking against state-of-the-art clustering methods that use side information.
- Numerous minor issues: inconsistent use of "Monte Carlo"/"Las Vegas", missing "average" qualifier in statements, odd footnote notation, ambiguous claims about adaptivity, and appendix formatting errors.
- Despite problems, the theoretical contribution and the lower-bound technique are valuable and could be of independent interest.
- Recommendation: accept pending major revisions to fix Theorem 2, improve writing, clarify model assumptions, add omitted references, and strengthen experiments.
