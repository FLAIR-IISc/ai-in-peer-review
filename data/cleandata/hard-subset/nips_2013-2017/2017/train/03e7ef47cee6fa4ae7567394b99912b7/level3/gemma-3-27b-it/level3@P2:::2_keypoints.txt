- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with noisy similarity side information and gives upper/lower bounds showing dramatic query reduction.  
- Core contribution: characterizing query reduction and proposing nearly optimal, parameter-free algorithms.  
- Builds on interactive clustering literature and connects to stochastic block models (SBM).  

- Strength: Addresses an important, practical problem bridging theory and heuristics.  
- Strength: Provides both upper and lower theoretical bounds (subject to verification).  
- Strength: Algorithms do not require prior knowledge of parameters (k, f+, f−).  
- Strength: Connection to SBM offers useful theoretical grounding.  

- Weakness: Paper suffers from poor writing, grammar, and unclear exposition hindering verification.  
- Weakness: Claims of novelty are overstated and some related work is omitted.  
- Weakness: Missing discussion of directly relevant prior work (e.g., active learning in SBMs, labeled/weighted SBMs).  
- Weakness: Formal model and Theorem 2 are unclear or possibly incorrect; assumptions (e.g., k → ∞, cluster-size restrictions) are not explicit.  
- Weakness: Experiments are weak, compare only to trivial baselines, and lack benchmarking against existing methods.  
- Weakness: Proofs are dense and difficult to verify due to heavy notation and limited explanation.  

- For acceptance: Theoretical results could be a substantial advance and parameter-free algorithms have practical appeal.  
- Against acceptance: Severe writing issues, overstated novelty, missing related work, potential flaws in Theorem 2, and weak experiments.  

- Overall: Potentially valuable work but requires major revisions; reviewer recommends acceptance with major revisions.  
- Required fixes: Revise writing thoroughly; clarify and correct Theorem 2 with explicit assumptions; expand related work; strengthen experiments with relevant baselines; clarify formal model and parameter restrictions; temper novelty claims.
