- Main contribution: Shows side-information (similarity matrix) reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), with H the Hellinger distance between within-/between-cluster similarity distributions.
- Tightness: The upper bound is nearly tight—matches a lower bound up to a log n factor.
- Novel perspective: Focuses on query complexity with side information, differing from standard SBM recovery-threshold analyses.
- Practical measure: Introduces Hellinger distance as a principled way to assess similarity-matrix quality.
- Application motivation: Motivated by interactive labeling tasks like entity resolution where queries are costly.
- Parameter-free algorithms: Proposes algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.
- Theoretical rigor: Results are well-motivated, clearly stated, and rigorously proven.
- Lower bound: Provides a matching lower bound that strengthens the theoretical claims.
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written and logically structured, though dense.
- Proof complexity: Proofs are technical and may be hard to follow without substantial background.
- Practical concerns: Computational cost of estimating Hellinger distance and iterative updates may limit scalability.
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments are limited in scope and lack extensive comparisons to heuristics across varied datasets.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant theoretical contribution despite noted weaknesses.
