This paper studies interactive clustering with pairwise same-cluster queries when side information is available as a similarity matrix W whose entries are drawn i.i.d. from f+ or f− depending on whether two items share a cluster. Without side information, Θ(nk) queries are necessary and sufficient. The paper shows that side information can reduce the query complexity to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f−)), where H is the Hellinger distance, via efficient parameter-free algorithms, and proves information-theoretic lower bounds that match up to a log n factor. The work generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM) by allowing arbitrary (unknown) f+, f− and adversarial partitions, and it raises the timely question of how active queries shift SBM recovery thresholds. The use of Hellinger distance provides a principled lens to quantify the quality of the similarity matrix.

Quality: The technical contributions are strong. The lower bound framework addresses adaptivity head-on, which is nontrivial beyond standard Fano-type arguments. The upper bounds are achieved by computationally efficient Monte Carlo and Las Vegas procedures that learn surrogates of f+, f− on the fly and use Hellinger-distance–based tests. The empirical section is modest but sensible and illustrates the practical benefit. One concern is a recurring discrepancy between O(k log n / H^2) and O(k^2 log n / H^2) in different places; the authors should reconcile this factor of k and clearly state the tightest bounds for both algorithms.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear and well-organized, with helpful connections to SBM and entity resolution. Some presentation issues remain: unify the use of “Hellinger distance/divergence,” state all success probabilities and constants in one place, and explicitly handle discrete vs. continuous f+, f−. A brief high-level intuition for the lower bound (beyond the technical steps) would aid accessibility.

Originality: High. The model, lower bound technique for adaptive queries, and parameter-free algorithms that scale with Hellinger distance are novel and go beyond prior NIPS work on same-cluster queries (e.g., Ashtiani et al.) and classical SBM thresholds.

Significance: High. The results offer a clean theory for the value of side information in interactive clustering, nearly tight bounds, and a metric (Hellinger) that practitioners can estimate to assess W. The connection to query-augmented SBM is likely to spur follow-up work.

Pros for acceptance:
- Nearly tight information-theoretic bounds; parameter-free, efficient algorithms
- Clear generalization of SBM and insightful use of Hellinger distance
- Addresses adaptive lower bounds, a technically challenging aspect
- Relevant to active learning and crowdsourced entity resolution

Cons against acceptance:
- Inconsistency on the k vs. k^2 factor and minor notation issues
- Limited experimental breadth; only one dataset and no baselines
- Assumes noiseless oracle; discussion is deferred to companion work
