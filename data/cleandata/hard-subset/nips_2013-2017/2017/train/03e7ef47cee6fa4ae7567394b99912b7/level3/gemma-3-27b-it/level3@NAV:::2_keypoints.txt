- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with side information and gives upper and lower bounds showing reduced queries vs. no side information.  
- Model uses pairwise queries and a noisy similarity matrix with distributions f+ and f− depending on cluster membership.  
- Frames work as a generalization of stochastic block models allowing arbitrary cluster sizes and unknown parameters.  
- Core contribution: characterizes impact of side information on query complexity and provides information-theoretic bounds.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem with potentially significant theoretical advances if proofs hold.  
- Strength: flexible problem formulation (arbitrary distributions, unknown k) that is practically relevant (e.g., entity resolution).  
- Weakness: proofs are dense, hard to follow, and difficult to verify.  
- Weakness: novelty claims are overstated and several relevant prior works are omitted (e.g., labeled/weighted SBMs and Gadde et al. ISIT 2016).  
- Weakness: formal model is unclear—minimax formulation and cluster size restrictions are not explicitly stated and may bias results.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears questionable and needs clarification about asymptotic behavior of k.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is weak, comparing only to trivial baselines and missing benchmarks against existing clustering/active learning methods.  
- Weakness: writing quality has numerous grammatical errors, incomplete sentences, and inconsistencies.  
- Decision rationale: paper has merit but current issues undermine credibility; reviewer recommends Accept with Major Revisions.  
- Required revisions: clarify and rigorously verify proofs (esp. Theorem 2 and asymptotics), expand related work, strengthen experiments, temper novelty claims, and thoroughly proofread.
