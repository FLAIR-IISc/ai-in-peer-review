**Paper Review: Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information**

**Summary**

The paper studies the query complexity of clustering with side information, where an oracle provides pairwise comparisons between elements. The authors provide upper and lower bounds on the query complexity, showing a significant reduction in queries when side information is available. They also propose two algorithms, Monte Carlo and Las Vegas, that achieve the lower bounds within a logarithmic factor.

**Strengths**

1. **Significant contribution**: The paper provides a rigorous theoretical study of clustering with side information, which is a fundamental problem in machine learning.
2. **Novel algorithms**: The proposed algorithms, Monte Carlo and Las Vegas, are efficient and parameter-free, making them practically useful.
3. **Upper and lower bounds**: The authors provide a tight analysis of the query complexity, showing a significant reduction in queries when side information is available.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dense proofs**: The proofs are dense and hard to verify, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
2. **Omitted prior works**: The authors omit relevant prior works, including papers on labeled/weighted stochastic block models.
3. **Missing citation**: The paper misses a relevant citation, "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" by Gadde et al.
4. **Overstated novelty claims**: The authors' claims of novelty are overstated, and some statements should be reworded.
5. **Unclear formal model**: The minimax formulation and restrictions on cluster sizes are not explicitly stated.
6. **Theorem 2 issues**: Theorem 2 is likely incorrect or incomplete, and may contradict known results for k = O(1) cases.
7. **Weak numerical experiments**: The numerical experiments are weak, comparing only to trivial baselines and not benchmarking against existing methods.
8. **Grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors**: The paper contains numerous errors that require thorough proofreading.

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses, I recommend accepting the paper with major revisions. The authors should:

1. **Clarify proofs**: Simplify and clarify the proofs to make them more accessible to non-experts.
2. **Address omitted prior works**: Include relevant prior works, including papers on labeled/weighted stochastic block models.
3. **Add missing citation**: Add the missing citation, "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" by Gadde et al.
4. **Reword novelty claims**: Reword the novelty claims to accurately reflect the contributions of the paper.
5. **Clarify formal model**: Explicitly state the minimax formulation and restrictions on cluster sizes.
6. **Revise Theorem 2**: Revise Theorem 2 to address the issues mentioned above.
7. **Strengthen numerical experiments**: Strengthen the numerical experiments by comparing to existing methods and providing more comprehensive results.
8. **Proofread thoroughly**: Thoroughly proofread the paper to eliminate grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors.

**Rating**

I rate the paper as follows:

* Quality: 7/10
* Clarity: 6/10
* Originality: 8/10
* Significance: 8/10

Overall, the paper makes significant contributions to the field of clustering with side information, but requires major revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
