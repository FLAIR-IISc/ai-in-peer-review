- Studies query complexity of interactive clustering with pairwise oracle answers plus noisy similarity side information.  
- Proves information-theoretic lower bounds and near-matching upper bounds, showing side information reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f₊‖f₋)).  
- Identifies Hellinger divergence as the key quantity governing query complexity.  
- Connects the model and results to community detection and stochastic block models (SBM).  
- Proposes parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that do not require k, f₊, or f₋.  
- Claims to be the first rigorous theoretical study of side-information-assisted interactive clustering, but this claim is overstated.  
- Omits several relevant theoretical works (e.g., Gadde et al., ISIT 2016; Abbe et al., FOCS/NIPS 2015).  
- Technical results appear promising and potentially practically useful if proofs are correct.  
- Experimental validation is limited (only Cora dataset) and compares against trivial baselines, not existing methods.  
- Writing quality is poor: grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, unclear explanations, and suspicious appendix formatting.  
- Formal model and theorem conditions are ambiguous (e.g., cluster size restrictions, whether Theorem 2 requires k → ∞).  
- Distinction between Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms is unclear and missing average-case qualifiers.  
- Novelty claims are weakened by relation to prior SBM recovery thresholds and missing citations.  
- Strengths: precise characterization of query reduction by side information, connection to SBM, Hellinger-based bounds, parameter-free algorithms.  
- Weaknesses: overstated novelty, omitted related work, potential theorem errors, poor clarity, and weak experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel characterization of side-information benefit, theoretically grounded bounds, parameter-free practical algorithms, SBM connection, and plausible correctness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: major related-work omissions, likely missing theorem conditions, poor writing hindering verification, weak experiments, and overstated claims.  
- Required revisions: correct novelty claims, clarify formal model and theorem conditions, fix grammar/formatting, strengthen experiments against real baselines, and add missing citations (e.g., Gadde et al.).  
- Recommendation: borderline — accept with major revisions if the authors address clarity, related work, theorem conditions, and experimental weaknesses.
