- Main claim: using a similarity matrix reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f₊‖f₋)), with information-theoretic lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms nearly matching them.
- Theoretical merit: connects to stochastic block models and targets an important interactive clustering problem.
- Theorem 2 is likely incorrect or incomplete — it implicitly assumes k → ∞ without stating it, conflicting with k = O(1) cases where Q = 0 suffices.
- Proofs are dense and hard to verify, making correctness unclear.
- Model assumptions (e.g., cluster sizes, minimax setup) are unspecified or unclear.
- Novelty claims are overstated (phrases like “first work,” “initiates study,” “significantly generalizes” are not justified).
- Missing citations to relevant prior work, notably Gadde et al., “Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models” (ISIT 2016).
- Writing quality is poor with numerous grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors.
- Abstract delays stating the main contributions, reducing clarity.
- Experimental evaluation is weak, comparing only to trivial baselines and not benchmarking against existing methods.
- Overall: idea has potential but current paper fails to demonstrate it due to theoretical flaws, poor writing, and missing citations.
- Required revisions: fix and clearly state theoretical claims (esp. Theorem 2), clarify model assumptions, include omitted literature, improve proofs and writing, and strengthen experiments.
- Recommendation: reject as submitted, but may be acceptable after careful revision addressing the above issues.
