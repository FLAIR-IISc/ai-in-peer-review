- Paper studies exact clustering with a noiseless same-cluster oracle plus a noisy similarity matrix W drawn i.i.d. from unknown f+ (intra) and f− (inter).  
- Main positive result: parameter-free algorithm achieves O(k log n / H^2(f+||f−)) queries; Las Vegas variant uses O(n log n + k log n / H^2) queries w.p.1.  
- Lower bound: information-theoretic interactive minimax lower bound Ω(min{nk, k / H^2(f+||f−)}) up to log n, even if k, f+, f− are known.  
- Model generalizes SBM (Bernoulli(p)/Bernoulli(q) is special case); H^2 captures side-information quality and reduces queries as it grows.  
- Upper-bound technique: Monte Carlo algorithm learns empirical intra/inter distributions from seed clusters, estimates H^2, grows clusters with threshold O(log n / H^2), and only queries ambiguous points.  
- Las Vegas variant adds O(n log n) overhead to guarantee correctness with probability 1.  
- Lower-bound technique: reduces low-queried assignment to a k-hypothesis test, uses Hellinger to control total variation on product distributions, and handles adaptivity.  
- Conceptual contribution: positions squared Hellinger distance as the right metric for side-information quality in this setting.  
- Practical relevance: motivated by entity resolution; small CORA experiments show greedy strategy guided by learned Hellinger gap attains good F1 with far fewer queries.  
- Theoretical quality: solid analysis, appropriate use of Hellinger for concentration and product-distribution comparisons; adaptivity handled carefully.  
- Algorithmic efficiency: plausibly efficient but processing W costs O(n^2) time; some constants/log factors not optimized.  
- Writing/clarity: generally clear with helpful SBM connections and intuitive algorithm, but contains notational/typographical inconsistencies and needs unified Hellinger notation.  
- Clarity requests: provide concise pseudo-code, state assumptions on support size of f+, f− and extensions to larger alphabets, and summarize Sanov-style derivations in the main text.  
- Originality: first principled analysis of query complexity with a generic similarity matrix and unknown f+, f−, plus a novel interactive lower-bound method.  
- Significance: high — gives a clean, interpretable dependence on H^2 and shows side information can drastically reduce query needs.  
- Key limitations: assumes noiseless oracle; upper bounds assume discrete f+, f− with fixed support; independence model for W may be unrealistic.  
- Empirical limitations: experiments limited to one dataset and small ablation; needs synthetic sweeps over H^2 and k and stronger baselines.  
- Practical limitations: runtime/memory O(n^2) for W processing; need discussion of streaming/large-n considerations.  
- Open theoretical gap: a remaining log n factor between upper and lower bounds; clarify whether fundamental.  
- Suggestions: fix k vs k^2 discrepancies and H vs H^2 notation, supply pseudo-code and complexity analysis, add synthetic experiments sweeping H^2 and k, and discuss robustness to model misspecification.  
- Pros summarized: clear important question, nearly tight bounds, parameter-free algorithms, novel lower-bound technique, strong SBM connection.  
- Cons summarized: presentation/notation issues, limited empirical validation, strong modeling assumptions (noiseless oracle, i.i.d. W), and a logarithmic gap remains.  
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial theoretical contribution with practical implications; improve clarity and experiments to increase impact.
