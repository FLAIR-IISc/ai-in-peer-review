- Rigorous theoretical study of query complexity for clustering with side information given as a similarity matrix W.
- Provides strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.
- Shows side information can drastically reduce query complexity, sometimes encoding the full clustering.
- Algorithms achieve query complexity O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) even with unknown parameters.
- Main contribution: use Hellinger distance between f+ and f- to quantify similarity-matrix quality and bound queries.
- Introduces a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering.
- Thorough theoretical analysis with proofs of correctness and efficiency.
- Experimental results on a real-world dataset demonstrate empirical effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.
- High originality: novel application of information-theoretic tools to clustering with side information.
- High relevance to the NIPS community and potential to influence future research.
- Argument for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, solid analysis, and supportive experiments.
- Argument against acceptance: assumes an oracle that always answers correctly, which may be unrealistic.
- Additional limitation: does not handle erroneous oracle answers and lacks a full characterization for arbitrary f+ and f-.
- Overall recommendation: accept despite limitations.
