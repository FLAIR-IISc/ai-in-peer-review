- Main result: Shows query complexity drops from Θ(nk) (no side info) to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) with a similarity matrix, nearly matching a lower bound up to log n.  
- Principal insight: Uses Hellinger distance to quantify similarity-matrix quality and its effect on query complexity.  
- Theoretical quality: Technically sound with rigorous upper and lower bounds and logically structured proofs (detailed in supplement).  
- Algorithms: Efficient, parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of distributions or cluster count.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and well-organized but dense in theoretical sections and assumes familiarity with information-theoretic concepts.  
- Experiments: Concise experimental validation but limited in scope and detail on algorithm/experimental setup.  
- Originality: Novel theoretical treatment of clustering with side information and a new SBM-perspective on query complexity; fills a gap beyond heuristic work.  
- Significance: High practical relevance (entity resolution, active learning, crowdsourcing) and a framework potentially extensible to other interactive learning problems.  
- Strengths (summary): Strong theoretical guarantees, parameter-free methods, principled similarity evaluation, SBM connection, and practical applicability.  
- Weaknesses (summary): Dense mathematics that may hinder accessibility, limited experiments, potentially large logarithmic constant, and restrictive assumption on support cardinality.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, sound results with practical implications, and well-motivated contributions.  
- Against acceptance: Accessibility concerns due to theoretical density, limited empirical evaluation, and the unresolved log-factor in the upper bound.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses; valuable contribution to interactive clustering and information-theoretic analysis.
