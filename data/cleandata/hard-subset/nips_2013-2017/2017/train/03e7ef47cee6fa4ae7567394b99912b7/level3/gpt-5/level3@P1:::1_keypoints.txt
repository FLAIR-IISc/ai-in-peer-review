- Problem: Studies query complexity of exact clustering with same-cluster queries plus a noisy similarity matrix W drawn i.i.d. from f+ / f− depending on cluster membership.  
- Key insight: Squared Hellinger divergence H^2(f+‖f−) summarizes side-information quality and governs extra queries needed beyond W.  
- Model relation: Generalizes SBM by allowing arbitrary/unknown f+, f− and adversarial cluster sizes; connects to exact/approximate recovery thresholds and active-query literature.  
- Lower bounds: Any adaptive algorithm needs Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) queries for constant success and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for certainty; proof uses a novel interactive lower-bound technique.  
- Upper bounds: Parameter-free Monte Carlo algorithm uses O(min{nk, (k^2 log n)/H^2}) queries w.h.p.; Las Vegas variant achieves O(n log n + min{nk, (k^2 log n)/H^2}) w.p.1.  
- Algorithmic technique: Iteratively learns empirical intra/inter distributions and applies Hellinger-based membership tests; analysis leverages Sanov’s theorem for Hellinger distance.  
- Experiments: On Cora, a greedy strategy guided by learned distributions approaches an ideal strategy with few queries and is robust to moderate f+/f− perturbations.  
- Strength: Novel, technically solid interactive lower-bound framework addressing adaptivity and filling a genuine gap.  
- Strength: Parameter-free algorithm that bootstraps from small clusters and matches lower bounds up to a log n factor.  
- Strength: Principled use of Hellinger divergence yielding clean dependence on side-information quality.  
- Weakness: Algorithm analysis assumes discrete, finite-alphabet f+, f−; extensions to continuous/large supports are undeveloped.  
- Weakness: Strong i.i.d. sampling assumption for W; real similarity matrices often violate independence.  
- Weakness: Perfect oracle assumption postponed to a companion paper; robustness to noisy oracles not fully treated.  
- Weakness: Limited experiments (single dataset, no synthetic scaling, no controlled H^2 comparisons or baselines).  
- Weakness: Inconsistent notation between H and H^2 and unclear unified statement of main bounds.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written but needs succinct pseudocode, clearer statement of assumptions (finite alphabet, independence), and unified Hellinger notation.  
- Originality: High — novel adaptive lower-bound technique and Hellinger-driven, parameter-free algorithmic framework.  
- Significance: High — provides an information-theoretic measure (H^2) for the value of similarity matrices and bridges theory/practice in interactive clustering and ER.  
- Empirical suggestions: Add synthetic SBM experiments varying H^2 and k, compare to established heuristics, report runtime/memory, and test robustness to dependence and oracle noise.  
- Questions for authors: Can algorithms extend to continuous/large alphabets, how robust are bounds to mild dependence in W, and can synthetic experiments validate theoretical scaling?  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical contributions but needs improved experiments, clarity, and discussion of modeling assumptions.
