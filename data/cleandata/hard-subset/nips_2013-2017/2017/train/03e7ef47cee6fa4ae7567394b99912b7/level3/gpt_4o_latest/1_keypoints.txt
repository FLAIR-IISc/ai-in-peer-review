- Rigorous theoretical study of query complexity for clustering with and without side information (similarity matrix W).
- Without side information, Θ(nk) queries are required.
- With side information, query complexity is O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), where H is the Hellinger distance; results tight up to a log n factor.
- Proposes efficient, parameter-free clustering algorithms and an iterative-update Monte Carlo method.
- Presents a general framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive clustering.
- Extends the Stochastic Block Model to arbitrary distributions f+ and f- and analyzes how side information affects clustering thresholds.
- Strength: strong information-theoretic lower bounds with nearly matching upper bounds, a significant theoretical advance.
- Strength: novel generalization of SBM and parameter-free algorithms increase applicability.
- Strength: highlights practical relevance by showing dramatic reduction in queries with side information.
- Strength: elegant algorithmic design (iterative-update) offering a general recipe for parameter-free methods.
- Strength: experimental results on real datasets show practical utility and robustness to approximate distributions.
- Weakness: exposition is dense and proofs/algorithms need clearer, more intuitive explanations.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; more diverse datasets and comparisons to heuristics are needed.
- Weakness: unresolved log n gap between lower and upper bounds without conjectures or insights to close it.
- Weakness: assumes an error-free oracle; lacks discussion or extensions to noisy settings.
- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem with significant theoretical and practical contributions.
- Against acceptance: clarity and limited empirical validation reduce accessibility and practical evidence.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on improving clarity and expanding discussion on practical implications and future directions.
