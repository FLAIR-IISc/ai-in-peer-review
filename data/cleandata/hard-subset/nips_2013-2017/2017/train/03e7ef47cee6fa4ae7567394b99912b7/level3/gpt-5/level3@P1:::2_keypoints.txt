- Main contribution: information-theoretic lower bounds and efficient parameter-free algorithms for exact clustering with pairwise queries plus noisy side information, characterizing query complexity in terms of n, k, and H^2 (squared Hellinger divergence).
- Lower bound: any algorithm needs on the order of min{nk, k^2/H^2} queries in the close f+/f− regime, proved via a novel interactive-information argument avoiding standard Fano barriers.
- Upper bounds: Monte Carlo algorithm with O(k log n/H^2) queries and a Las Vegas algorithm with O(n log n + min{nk, k^2 log n/H^2}) queries, nearly matching lower bounds up to log factors.
- Impact: side information can dramatically reduce worst-case Θ(nk) query complexity; model includes SBM as a Bernoulli special case.
- Technique: parameter-free iterative estimation of f+ and f− using Hellinger projections to decide when to trust side information instead of querying.
- Relation to prior work: builds on and should be contrasted with interactive clustering with constraints, SBM recovery/active querying literature (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT’16), and parameter-free SBM recovery results.
- Strength: clear formalization of query complexity with side information and a novel, reusable lower-bound technique for adaptive querying.
- Strength: parameter-free procedure leveraging Hellinger geometry and nearly matching upper/lower dependence on k, n, and H^2.
- Weakness: key assumptions and parameter regimes are not stated precisely; Theorem 2 lacks explicit asymptotic conditions and may contradict known SBM cases.
- Weakness: mismatch between upper-bound assumption of discrete fixed-support f+/f− and lower bound allowing continuous distributions is not discussed.
- Weakness: proofs are dense, contain missing steps and confusing phrasing; there are inconsistencies in query-complexity statements (e.g., O(k log n/H^2) vs O(k^2 log n/H^2)).
- Clarity issues: definitions (minimax vs average-case, cluster-size assumptions, k behavior) must appear up front; Monte Carlo vs Las Vegas guarantees need consistent clarification.
- Writing quality: numerous grammatical, punctuation, and formatting errors impede readability and verification.
- Originality: problem formulation and Hellinger-based dependence are interesting and novel in generality, but novelty claims are overstated given related active-querying and SBM literature.
- Significance: if corrected and clarified, the work could be an influential theoretical contribution with practical algorithms; current experiments are insufficient.
- Empirical evaluation: experiments are limited (single ER dataset); need comparisons to interactive clustering heuristics, SBM simulations across k,n,H^2, ablations of parameter-free estimation, and recovery-vs-query curves with error bars.
- Specific suggestions: precisely state the model (minimax vs sampled clusters, imbalance), add conditions for Theorem 2 (k, cluster sizes, H^2 regime), reconcile query-complexity claims, discuss discrete vs continuous f+/f−, and tone down “first”/“initiates” claims with more citations.
- Algorithmic clarity: improve pseudocode, define Membership properly, justify thresholds with explicit concentration bounds, and state exactly what “parameter-free” means.
- Acceptance pros: important question, novel lower-bound machinery, nearly matching bounds, parameter-free efficient algorithms—worthy if corrected.
- Acceptance cons: imprecise theorem statements, hard-to-follow proofs, weak experiments, and overstated novelty—major revisions required.
- Post-rebuttal stance: inclined to accept conditional on precise theorem conditions, reconciled complexity statements, expanded related work, clearer proofs, and stronger experiments.
