- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with noiseless same-cluster queries plus noisy pairwise side information drawn from distributions f+ / f−, without knowing k or f±.  
- Main theoretical results: information-theoretic lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) (and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2/H^2}) for certainty) and nearly matching parameter-free upper bounds O(min{nk, k^2 log n / H^2}).  
- Technique: learn empirical inter/intra distributions and use an iterative-update rule to decide when to avoid queries.  
- Framed as a generalization of SBM/community detection; connects to active learning and crowdsourced ER.  
- Strength: addresses practical question of how side information reduces query complexity, with clear dependence on Hellinger divergence.  
- Strength: parameter-free algorithms (no k or f± required) with iterative-update mechanism of potential broader use.  
- Strength: lower-bound framework for adaptive interactive queries is novel and potentially independent interest.  
- Weakness: several claims/proofs are hard to verify; Theorem 2 appears to omit key conditions (explicit dependence on k) and may be misstated for regimes where side information suffices.  
- Weakness: formal model not crisply specified — minimax setting, cluster size assumptions, and regularity/support requirements for continuous f± are unclear.  
- Weakness: misuse/ambiguity of “Las Vegas” terminology and inconsistent probability/query-bound statements for that algorithm.  
- Weakness: experiments are weak — single dataset, poor baselines, no robustness or ablation studies, and missing comparisons to prior methods.  
- Clarity issues: poor writing, notation inconsistencies, long abstract, confusing algorithm phases/thresholds, and unclear adaptive selection rules.  
- Originality: model and adaptive lower-bound framework are novel, but claims of being the “first” or “significantly generalizes SBM” are overstated without citing labeled/weighted SBM and related query-based community detection (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016).  
- Significance: potentially meaningful theoretical contribution if clarified, but practical impact limited by weak experiments and presentation.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear conceptual message, parameter-free efficient algorithms, and interesting lower-bound ideas for adaptive settings.  
- Cons for acceptance: theorem statements and conditions need correction, writing/organization must be improved, and experiments need strengthening.  
- Requests: clarify regimes and constants in Theorem 2, specify minimax/cluster-size assumptions, handle continuous/discrete f± cases, define Las Vegas query-expectation precisely, provide pseudocode and thresholds, and run stronger empirical comparisons.  
- Minor: temper novelty claims, add missing citations (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016; labeled/general SBM literature), and fix grammar/formatting throughout.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept conditional on careful revision addressing theorem statements, model clarity, writing, and stronger experiments.
