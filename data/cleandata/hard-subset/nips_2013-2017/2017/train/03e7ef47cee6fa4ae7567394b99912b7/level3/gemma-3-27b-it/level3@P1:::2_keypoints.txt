- Studies query complexity of clustering with noisy similarity matrix; gives information-theoretic lower and upper bounds for recovering optimal clustering via pairwise oracle queries.  
- Main contribution: shows side information can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n H(f+||f−)), where H is the Hellinger divergence.  
- Proposes algorithms that achieve the stated complexity and a general framework for proving interactive lower bounds.  
- Motivated by interactive clustering applications (e.g., entity resolution) and connects the problem to the stochastic block model (SBM).  
- Strength: novel rigorous theoretical analysis addressing a gap in interactive clustering literature.  
- Strength: attempts to provide information-theoretic lower/upper bounds, offering fundamental limits if proofs are correct.  
- Strength: practically relevant problem and valuable SBM connection.  
- Weakness: poor clarity and writing — convoluted sentences, grammatical/formatting errors hinder understanding.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 (and possibly others) lacks explicit assumptions (e.g., on k and cluster size distribution); formal model underspecified.  
- Weakness: omits relevant related work (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016 and labeled/weighted SBM literature) and overstates novelty.  
- Weakness: proofs are dense and difficult to verify within a typical review timeframe.  
- Weakness: weak experimental evaluation, comparing only to trivial baselines rather than established heuristics.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, strong motivation, and useful SBM framing.  
- Against acceptance: clarity issues, potentially flawed/incomplete theorem statements, missing related work, and weak experiments.  
- Overall: potentially valuable but currently unsuitable for acceptance without major revisions to clarity, completeness, related work, proofs, and experiments.  
- Recommendation: Reject with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the listed concerns.
