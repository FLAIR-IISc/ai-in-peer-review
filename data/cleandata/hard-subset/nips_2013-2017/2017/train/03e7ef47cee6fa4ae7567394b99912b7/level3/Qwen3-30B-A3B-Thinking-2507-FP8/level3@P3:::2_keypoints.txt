- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with side information, claiming query complexity reduces to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) with matching upper and lower bounds and efficient algorithms.  
- Connects model to stochastic block model (SBM) and allows arbitrary f+, f- without cluster-size or k assumptions.  
- Main strength: shows side information can drastically reduce query complexity; algorithms are efficient and parameter-free.  
- Lower bound framework may be independently useful for interactive classification problems.  
- Overlooks key prior work (notably Gadde et al., ISIT 2016) and several papers on labeled/weighted SBMs; claims of "first" or overly strong novelty are inaccurate.  
- Writing and presentation are poor: formal model, minimax formulation, and cluster-size assumptions are unclear; many grammar and formatting errors.  
- Theorem 2 is likely incorrect or incomplete (appears to implicitly require k → ∞, may contradict known small-k cases); proof contains technical gaps.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: compares only to trivial baselines, limited Cora results, and lacks benchmarking against existing methods.  
- Upper-bound analyses are dense and difficult to verify; some proofs require careful re-examination.  
- Minor issues: overly long abstract, unclear use of "Monte Carlo"/"Las Vegas" jargon, suspicious footnote notation, inconsistent appendix formatting, and confusing phrasing about adaptivity.  
- Quality: technically promising but undermined by unclear writing and potential proof gaps, especially Theorem 2.  
- Significance: could be important if theoretical claims are verified and presentation improved.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on correcting novelty claims, clarifying assumptions, fixing proofs, improving writing, expanding experiments, and correcting formatting.  
- Required author actions: cite relevant prior work (esp. Gadde et al. 2016); state assumptions precisely (e.g., k → ∞ if needed); rigorously verify Theorem 2; improve writing and proofreading; strengthen experimental comparisons; fix all grammatical/formatting issues.
