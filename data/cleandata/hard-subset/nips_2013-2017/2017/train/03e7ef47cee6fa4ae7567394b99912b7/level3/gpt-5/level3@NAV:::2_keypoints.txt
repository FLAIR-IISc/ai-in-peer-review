- Paper studies exact clustering with a noiseless same-cluster oracle plus random side-information matrix (entries i.i.d. from f+ within clusters and f− across clusters) and gives parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms.  
- Claims query complexity upper bounds roughly O(k log n / H^2(f+||f−)) (with O(n log n) overhead for Las Vegas) and an information-theoretic lower bound Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2(f+||f−)}), asserting near-tightness.  

Strengths:
- Tackles an ambitious, timely problem with clear practical motivation (entity resolution, interactive clustering).  
- If correct, the near-matching upper/lower bounds and adaptive lower-bound framework are valuable contributions.  
- Hellinger-based analysis and iterative thresholding ideas may have broader applicability.  

Weaknesses / required revisions (quality):
- Proofs are very dense and several arguments appear incomplete or hard to verify.  
- Theorem 2 as stated seems incorrect or incomplete (appears to implicitly assume k → ∞ and may contradict k = O(1) regimes where Q = 0).  
- Formal model is underspecified: need clarity on minimax setup, allowed cluster-size imbalance, independence assumptions, and whether “parameter-free” guarantees hold uniformly over f+, f−.  
- Numerical experiments are weak: only trivial baselines on one dataset and no comparisons to SBM/labeled-SBM likelihood, spectral methods, or active querying work (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016).  

Weaknesses / clarity:
- Writing quality is poor with confusing/incomplete sentences, inconsistent notation, formatting issues, and unclear use of “Monte Carlo/Las Vegas” (should add average/expected qualifiers).  
- Abstract is too long and delays main contributions.  

Weaknesses / originality:
- Novelty claims are overstated and several relevant works (labeled/weighted SBMs, active community detection literature) are omitted; cite Gadde et al. and related SBM literature.  

Significance:
- Potentially high, but contingent on corrected statements, clearer proofs, stronger experiments, and better positioning.  

Recommendation / required fixes:
- Revise to provide precise theorem statements with explicit conditions (e.g., on k and cluster sizes).  
- Strengthen and fully verify proofs; resolve apparent contradictions like Theorem 2.  
- Expand related work and temper novelty claims with proper citations.  
- Improve experiments with competitive baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Thorough proofreading and formatting fixes.
