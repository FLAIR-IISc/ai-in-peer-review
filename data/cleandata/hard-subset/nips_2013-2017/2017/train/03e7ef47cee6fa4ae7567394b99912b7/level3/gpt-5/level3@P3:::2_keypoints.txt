- Paper studies query complexity of exact clustering with a noiseless oracle plus random side-information W, giving lower and near-matching upper bounds in terms of Hellinger divergence H2(f+‖f−).
- Provides two parameter-free algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) and connects results to stochastic block models (SBMs).

- Strength: Information-theoretic lower-bound framework for interactive settings and Hellinger-based parameter-free upper bounds are valuable if correct.
- Strength: Iterative-update method to learn thresholds from side-information without known parameters is novel and broadly useful.
- Strength: Connection to SBMs and how queries shift recovery thresholds is appealing for follow-on work.

- Weakness: Proofs are dense, incomplete in places, and hard to verify.
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears to lack necessary conditions (e.g., large k) and may contradict known Q = 0 results for constant-k SBMs unless clarified.
- Weakness: Formal model is underspecified — unclear if analysis is minimax, what cluster-size constraints exist, and how extreme imbalance is handled.
- Weakness: Experiments are weak, compare only to trivial baselines on a single dataset, and omit established SBM/interactive-clustering baselines.
- Weakness: Writing quality, organization, and grammar issues impede understanding and verification.

- Clarity: Key technical arguments (Hellinger concentration, product-measure bounds, interactive lower-bound construction) need cleaner exposition and full conditions.
- Clarity: Precisely distinguish Monte Carlo vs Las Vegas guarantees (success probability, average vs worst-case query complexity).
- Clarity: Clearly state model assumptions: whether guarantees are high-probability over W or minimax over partitions, assumptions on k/cluster sizes, success criterion, and adaptivity.

- Originality: Interactive lower-bound framework is promising but claims of being first or significantly generalizing prior work are overstated given literature on labeled/weighted SBMs and active querying; rephrase novelty claims to be precise.

- Significance: Results could be influential if theorems are corrected and writing improved, but current empirical evaluation does not show practical impact.

- Requested change: Theorem 2 should state exact regimes (e.g., k scaling, H2 bounds, cluster-size conditions, error probability) and reconcile with known zero-query recovery results.
- Requested change: Clarify model/assumptions (minimax vs average-case, minimal cluster sizes like Ω(log n/H2), handling of imbalance).
- Requested change: Provide full proofs for Hellinger product-measure inequality, adaptation from Sanov to Hellinger concentration, and the anti-concentration lemma.
- Requested change: Strengthen experiments by comparing to interactive clustering/SBM baselines (Awasthi et al., Ashtiani et al., Gadde et al.), report accuracy vs query budget across datasets and regimes, and include ablations for imbalance and misspecified f±.
- Requested change: Add missing related work (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016 and labeled/weighted SBM literature) and correct overstated novelty language.

- Writing issues: Numerous grammar, formatting, and incomplete sentences; abstract and terminology need tightening; clarify Monte Carlo/Las Vegas and average vs worst-case language.

- Pros for acceptance: Potentially strong theoretical contributions with parameter-free algorithms and an interesting link to SBM thresholds under querying.
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete/unclear proofs (notably Theorem 2), underspecified model, weak empirical validation, and subpar writing.

- Post-rebuttal recommendation: Reviewer raises rating to accept conditional on thorough revision addressing writing, precise theorems and assumptions (including large-k regimes), tempered novelty claims, added related work, and stronger experiments.
