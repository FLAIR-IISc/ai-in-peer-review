- Problem: Studies query complexity of clustering with side information in a similarity matrix where similarities come from distributions f+ (same cluster) and f- (different clusters).  
- Main theoretical result: Side information reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)), where H is Hellinger divergence.  
- Optimality: Provides matching upper and lower bounds, establishing near-optimality of the query complexity.  
- Connection to SBM: Links the model and results to the stochastic block model literature, opening questions about querying vs. SBM thresholds.  
- Motivation: Addresses a fundamental and practically relevant problem given interactive clustering and crowd-sourcing trends.  
- Gap addressed: Highlights mismatch between heuristic algorithms and theoretical guarantees, positioning this work to fill that void.  
- Strength — theory: Strong, significant theoretical advancement with nearly tight bounds.  
- Strength — framework: Introduces a principled framework quantifying side-information quality via Hellinger divergence.  
- Strength — algorithms: Proposes parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.  
- Strength — SBM link: The SBM connection provides valuable context and future research directions.  
- Strength — presentation: Generally clear and well-organized with well-defined contributions.  
- Weakness — experiments: Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (primarily a single dataset) and lacks broader comparisons.  
- Weakness — constants: Logarithmic and constant factors in bounds are not fully optimized and may affect practical performance.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Fixed support cardinality assumption for f+ and f- may limit applicability; effect of relaxing it is not fully explored.  
- Weakness — proofs: Proofs are long and placed in the appendix; more intuition in the main text would improve accessibility.  
- Weakness — related work: Related-work discussion is incomplete, notably differences with [5] and contextualization of [19].  
- For acceptance: Technically sound, addresses an important problem, offers parameter-free algorithms, and provides valuable SBM insights.  
- Against acceptance: Limited empirical validation, potential practical issues from constants, and dense proofs reducing accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution with weaknesses that do not outweigh the strengths; recommendation to accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
