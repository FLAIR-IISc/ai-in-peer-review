- Problem: Clustering with pairwise "same cluster?" oracle queries augmented by a similarity matrix as side information.  
- Main theoretical result: Side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f₊‖f₋)).  
- Tightness: Provides matching upper and lower bounds, nearly tight up to a logarithmic factor.  
- Generality: Generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM) to arbitrary distributions without knowing k or cluster sizes.  
- Novel technique: Introduces an information-theoretic lower bound method applicable to other interactive learning problems.  
- Algorithms: Efficient, parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f₊, or f₋.  
- Empirical support: Experiments on the Cora dataset validate theoretical claims.  
- Quality metric: Proposes using Hellinger divergence to quantify similarity-matrix quality with practical implications.  
- Contribution to SBM: Connects and extends SBM literature, showing how query complexity affects recovery thresholds.  
- Writing and rigor: Well-written, clearly organized, with rigorous proofs and sufficient detail for experts.  
- Limitation: Lower bound proof is complex and could use a more intuitive explanation for non-experts.  
- Limitation: Experimental validation is limited to a single dataset and practical implementation details could be expanded.  
- Impact and significance: Substantial theoretical contribution with clear practical implications and new research directions.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—high confidence.
