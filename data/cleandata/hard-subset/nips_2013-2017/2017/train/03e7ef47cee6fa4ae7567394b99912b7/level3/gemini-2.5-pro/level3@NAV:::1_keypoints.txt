- Addresses query complexity of interactive clustering with noisy similarity matrix side information.
- Models side information with distributions f+ (same-cluster) and f- (different-cluster).
- Connects to stochastic block model but asks how active queries change recovery thresholds.
- Identifies squared Hellinger distance H²(f+ || f-) as the correct measure of side-information quality.
- Proves upper bound on queries: O(k log n / H²(f+ || f-)).
- Provides matching information-theoretic lower bound up to a log n factor.
- Offers computationally efficient Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms.
- Algorithms are parameter-free (do not require k, f+, or f-).
- Theoretical results are significant, nearly tight, and rigorously derived.
- Paper is technically sound and clearly written.
- Experimental evaluation limited to the Cora dataset.
- Suggests adding synthetic experiments controlling H²(f+ || f-) to validate scaling laws.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — strengths outweigh minor experimental limitation.
