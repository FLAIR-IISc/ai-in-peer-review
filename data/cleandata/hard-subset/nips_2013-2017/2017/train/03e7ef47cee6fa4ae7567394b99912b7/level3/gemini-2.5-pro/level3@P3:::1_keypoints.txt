- Studies interactive clustering to exactly recover a latent partition using pairwise oracle queries augmented by side information W.
- Models W with two unknown distributions f+ (within-cluster) and f- (between-cluster), generalizing the Stochastic Block Model.
- Main upper bound: O(k² log n / H²(f+ || f-)) queries suffice, where H² is squared Hellinger distance.
- Complementary lower bound: Ω(k² / H²(f+ || f-)), showing Hellinger distance precisely characterizes side-information quality.
- Algorithms are computationally efficient and parameter-free, operating without knowing k, f+, or f-.
- Monte Carlo algorithm uses iterative updates to estimate H(f+||f-) and adapt trust in side information.
- Connects active query complexity to SBM literature, reframing passive recovery thresholds in an active setting.
- Technical quality is high with rigorous proofs and near-tight upper/lower bounds (up to log factors).
- Lower bound proof technique is novel and notable for handling adaptive queries via seldom-queried element subsets and multi-hypothesis testing.
- Algorithmic and proof techniques appear original and potentially broadly applicable.
- Paper is well-written and organized; introduction and main results are clearly presented with intuitive proof sketches.
- A drawback is that detailed proofs are deferred to the appendix, complicating full verification from the main text alone.
- Experimental evaluation is limited (single CORA dataset) and lacks comparisons to practical heuristics from prior work.
- Significant both theoretically and practically: clarifies value of side information and motivates feature engineering via Hellinger distance.
- Opens avenues for future work (e.g., sparse SBM regimes, richer side-information models).
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — contributions outweigh the minor experimental weakness.
