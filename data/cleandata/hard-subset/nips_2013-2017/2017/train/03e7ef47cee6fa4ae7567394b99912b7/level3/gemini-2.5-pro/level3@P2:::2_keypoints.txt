- Problem: interactive clustering with oracle pairwise queries and noisy similarity matrix (side information) where same-cluster entries ~ f+ and different-cluster entries ~ f−.  
- Main theoretical result: information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity for exact recovery.  
- Query complexity improvement: side information reduces queries from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f−)) where H^2 is squared Hellinger divergence.  
- Connection to prior models: the side-information model generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM).  
- Algorithms: parameter-free, efficient Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms achieving the stated upper bounds.  
- Strength — significance: well-motivated, practically relevant problem that quantifies value of noisy side information for reducing annotation effort.  
- Strength — theoretical depth: comprehensive theoretical treatment with lower and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Strength — practicality: algorithms do not require knowing k or the distributions f+, f−.  
- Weakness — clarity/presentation: paper is difficult to read with dense writing, grammatical/formatting errors, and confusing sentences that impede verification.  
- Weakness — unclear formal model: problem statement lacks specification of whether bounds are worst-case (minimax) over partitions or assume a generative model; behavior on imbalanced clusters not discussed.  
- Weakness — incomplete/ambiguous lower bound (Theorem 2): statement appears incomplete or incorrect, not specifying assumptions (e.g., dependence on k) and may contradict known SBM regimes where Q=0 is possible.  
- Weakness — nonstandard jargon use: descriptions of "Monte Carlo" vs "Las Vegas" algorithms do not align with standard definitions.  
- Weakness — related work omissions: misses relevant literature on SBM with node labels/weighted edges and active learning for community detection (e.g., Gadde et al., ISIT 2016).  
- Weakness — overstated novelty: claims like "first rigorous theoretical study" should be toned down to reflect existing work.  
- Weakness — weak experiments: comparisons are only to trivial baselines rather than existing interactive clustering or entity-resolution heuristics that use similarity scores.  
- Recommendation: currently borderline for acceptance due to poor writing, lack of theoretical precision, and weak experiments, but potentially acceptable after substantial revision.  
- Required revisions: thorough proofreading; precise formal problem definition; corrected and assumption-explicit Theorem 2; expanded related work including missing citations; stronger experimental comparisons to relevant baselines.
