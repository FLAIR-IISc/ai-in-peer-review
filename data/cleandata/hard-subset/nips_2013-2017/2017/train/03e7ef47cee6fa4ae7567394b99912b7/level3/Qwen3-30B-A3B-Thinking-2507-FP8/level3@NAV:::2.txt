

This paper investigates query complexity for clustering with side information, claiming that a similarity matrix reduces the number of queries needed from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f₊‖f₋)). The authors present information-theoretic lower bounds and propose parameter-free algorithms that nearly match these bounds.

The work has theoretical merit in connecting to stochastic block models and addressing a practically important problem in interactive clustering. However, several critical issues undermine its contribution. Theorem 2's lower bound appears incorrect or incomplete, as it implicitly assumes k → ∞ without stating this condition, contradicting known k = O(1) cases where Q = 0 suffices. The proofs are dense and difficult to verify, with unclear model assumptions about cluster sizes and minimax formulation.

The novelty claims are overstated ("first work," "initiates study," "significantly generalizes"), overlooking relevant prior work such as Gadde et al.'s "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (ISIT 2016), which should be cited. The writing quality is poor, with numerous grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors, and the abstract unnecessarily delays stating contributions. The experimental section is weak, comparing only to trivial baselines without benchmarking against existing methods.

While the core idea has potential significance, the current paper fails to demonstrate this due to theoretical flaws, poor writing, and missing citations. The authors need to carefully revise the theoretical claims (particularly Theorem 2), clarify all model assumptions, address the omitted literature, and substantially improve the writing quality. With these revisions, the paper could make a valuable contribution to the field. As written, it does not meet NIPS standards for quality, clarity, or originality. I recommend rejection but would accept it after careful revision addressing these concerns.
