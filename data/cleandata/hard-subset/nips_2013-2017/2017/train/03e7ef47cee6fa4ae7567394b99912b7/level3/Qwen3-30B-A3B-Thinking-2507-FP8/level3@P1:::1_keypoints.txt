- Studies interactive clustering with side information where an algorithm can query same-cluster oracle and use a similarity matrix W with distributions f+ (same) and f- (different).
- Shows side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)), where H is Hellinger divergence.
- Proves this bound is tight up to an O(log n) factor via information-theoretic lower bounds.
- Develops parameter-free, computationally efficient algorithms (O(n²) time) that do not require knowing k, f+, or f-.
- Provides a Monte Carlo algorithm with high-probability bounds and a Las Vegas variant guaranteeing exact recovery.
- Novel lower-bound technique generalizes Fano’s inequality to the interactive setting and may be of independent interest.
- Connects results to stochastic block models (SBM), generalizing to unknown distributions and revealing effects of query complexity on recovery thresholds.
- First rigorous theoretical analysis quantifying how side information affects interactive clustering query complexity.
- Establishes a principled measure of similarity-matrix quality via Hellinger divergence.
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows practical efficacy (greedy strategy reaches 80% precision and 90% recall with 1136 queries).
- Limitations: experiments limited to a single dataset and similarity measure, needing broader empirical validation.
- Limitation: noisy oracle responses are not handled in this paper (addressed in companion work).
- The log n factor in the query bound might be improvable in specific settings, though likely optimal in the general framework.
- Well-written, clearly organized, and technically rigorous with significant theoretical contributions and practical relevance.
- Recommendation: acceptance, citing originality, tight bounds, parameter-free algorithms, and strong SBM connection.
