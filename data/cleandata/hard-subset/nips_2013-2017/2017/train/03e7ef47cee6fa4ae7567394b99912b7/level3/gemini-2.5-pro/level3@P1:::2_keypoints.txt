- Paper studies interactive clustering with a noiseless oracle plus random side information W, where w_uv ~ f+ for same-cluster and f- otherwise.  
- Main theoretical claims: without side info Θ(nk) queries; with side info O(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) upper bound and Ω(k² / H²(f+||f-)) lower bound.  
- Authors provide parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that nearly match the information-theoretic lower bound.  
- Paper connects the problem to the stochastic block model (SBM), treating SBM as a special case of their model.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, well-motivated gap between theory and empirical heuristics for clustering with similarities.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution—tight bounds that quantify dependence on side-information quality via squared Hellinger distance.  
- Strength: Parameter-free algorithms are practically valuable and a nontrivial technical advance.  
- Strength: Novel and useful connection to SBM literature that could open new directions.  
- Weakness: Writing quality is poor—confusing prose, grammatical errors, and dense, hard-to-follow proofs that obscure correctness.  
- Weakness: Formal model and theorem statements lack precision—unclear minimax vs average-case, cluster-size assumptions, and missing conditions (e.g., k → ∞) in Theorem 2.  
- Weakness: Inadequate citation of related work (e.g., labeled/weighted SBM literature and Gadde et al. 2016), leading to overstated novelty claims.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is weak—comparisons only to trivial baselines, lacking benchmarks against established heuristics or active learning methods.  
- Suggestions: Revise writing thoroughly, clarify Monte Carlo vs Las Vegas guarantees, and state whether bounds are in expectation.  
- Suggestions: Correct and tighten theorem statements to include necessary assumptions (e.g., on k and cluster balance).  
- Suggestions: Add missing related-work citations and temper novelty claims; expand experiments to compare against relevant methods.  
- Conclusion: Promising and potentially significant work, but currently borderline due to clarity, precision, missing citations, and weak experiments; reviewer would accept conditional on thorough revision addressing these issues.
