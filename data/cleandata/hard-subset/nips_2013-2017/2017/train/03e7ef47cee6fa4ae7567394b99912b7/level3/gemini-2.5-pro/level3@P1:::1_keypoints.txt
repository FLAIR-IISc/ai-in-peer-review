- Problem: interactive clustering with oracle queries and noisy similarity matrix side information for n items into k unknown clusters.  
- Baseline: without side information query complexity is Θ(nk).  
- Model: similarity entries drawn from f+ within clusters and f- across clusters; informativeness measured by H^2(f+ || f-).  
- Main positive result: parameter-free, efficient algorithm recovers clustering w.h.p. using O(k log n / H^2(f+ || f-)) queries.  
- Main negative result: information-theoretic lower bound of Ω(k^2 / H^2(f+ || f-)), showing near-optimality up to a log n factor.  
- Technical strength: complete theoretical characterization with both constructive upper bound and non-trivial adaptive lower-bound proof.  
- Novel proof technique: lower bound handles adaptivity where standard tools (e.g., Fano) do not directly apply.  
- Practical algorithmic feature: parameter-free Monte Carlo method that iteratively estimates distributions without knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Conceptual novelty: first rigorous study of the intersection between active clustering and generalized SBM-like noisy similarities.  
- Insight: links query complexity to Hellinger distance, providing a principled measure of similarity utility for clustering.  
- Significance: explains why similarity-based heuristics can massively reduce queries (logarithmic dependence on n vs Θ(nk)).  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, with main theorems upfront and intuitive proof/algorithm sketches; full proofs in appendix.  
- Weaknesses: remaining log n gap between bounds and limited empirical evaluation (single dataset).  
- Recommendation: strengths far outweigh weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
