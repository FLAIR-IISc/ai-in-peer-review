- Paper studies interactive clustering with pairwise queries and noisy side-information W; shows query complexity can drop from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n / H^2(f+||f-)) with matching information-theoretic lower bounds and parameter-free algorithms.  
- Algorithms are efficient and do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-; model framed as a generalization of the stochastic block model.  
- Strength: tackles an important practical/theoretical problem by formalizing side-information’s role in reducing human annotation cost.  
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions—upper and lower bounds tied to squared Hellinger divergence, offering a precise characterization if proofs hold.  
- Strength: practical advantage from parameter-free algorithms that avoid needing k or distributional parameters.  
- Weakness: paper suffers from poor writing, grammatical errors, and dense, hard-to-follow proofs requiring substantial proofreading and rewriting.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears incomplete/incorrect — missing assumptions (e.g., behavior for constant vs. large k) and may contradict known SBM regimes where Q=0 is possible.  
- Weakness: Formal model is ambiguous (not stated as minimax, unclear cluster balance assumptions), making interpretation of bounds uncertain for imbalanced clusters.  
- Weakness: Related work incomplete—omits relevant literature on labeled/weighted/semi-supervised SBMs and active querying (e.g., Gadde et al. ISIT 2016); novelty claims are overstated.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is weak—comparisons are only to variants of the authors’ method rather than existing baselines or practical heuristics.  
- Minor issues: overly long abstract, confusing Monte Carlo/Las Vegas wording, subjective phrasing, and formatting/readability problems in the appendix.  
- Recommendation: potentially valuable and worth acceptance only after a major revision addressing clarity, theorem statements/assumptions, literature placement, and stronger experiments.
