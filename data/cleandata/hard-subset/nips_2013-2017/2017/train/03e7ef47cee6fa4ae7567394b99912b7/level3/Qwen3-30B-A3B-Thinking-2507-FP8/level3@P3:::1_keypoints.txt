- Main contribution: reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H²(f+||f-)) with matching lower bounds up to a log n factor.  
- Model/setup: side-information given by similarity matrix W with entries from distributions f+ (same cluster) and f- (different clusters).  
- Metric: uses Hellinger distance H(f+||f-) to quantify similarity-matrix quality (preferred over KL).  
- Algorithms: efficient, parameter-free (do not require knowing k, f+, or f-).  
- Theoretical novelty: first rigorous analysis of clustering with side information for arbitrary f+, f-.  
- Connection to SBM: links results to stochastic block model and shows side information alters recovery thresholds.  
- Lower bounds: introduces a novel framework for information-theoretic lower bounds in interactive settings using a generalized Fano approach.  
- Technical quality: rigorous proofs for upper and lower bounds; lower bound proof particularly noteworthy.  
- Empirical support: experiments on Cora show greedy strategy near best-case performance with far fewer queries.  
- Originality: novel use of Hellinger distance and a general framework beyond previous heuristics/special cases.  
- Practical significance: substantial implications for entity resolution and reducing crowdsourcing cost; opens new research directions.  
- Minor weaknesses: algorithm description is somewhat complex and may hinder accessibility.  
- Experimental limitations: validation restricted to a single dataset (Cora).  
- Presentation issues: some technical proof details could be clarified.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
