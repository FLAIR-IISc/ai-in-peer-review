- Main result: With side information modeled by distributions f+ (same-cluster) and f- (different-cluster), query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H²(f+||f-)), where H is the Hellinger distance.
- Lower bounds: The authors give matching lower bounds up to a logarithmic factor, showing the bound is tight.
- Insight: Side information can drastically reduce queries needed for accurate clustering.
- Generalization: Connects to stochastic block model (SBM) literature by allowing arbitrary f+ and f-, generalizing recovery thresholds.
- Metric: Uses Hellinger distance as a principled measure of similarity-matrix quality.
- Algorithms: Provides parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-.
- Theory & presentation: Strong, rigorous theoretical treatment with clear writing and reproducible detail.
- Limitations: Experimental validation is limited (single dataset, Cora); noisy oracle responses are not handled and noted as future work.
- Impact: Novel theoretical framework with significant implications for entity resolution and active learning; strong contribution for the community.
