- Studies query complexity of interactive clustering with noisy pairwise side-information modeled by two unknown distributions f+ and f-, generalizing the SBM.  
- Main theoretical contribution: information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds showing query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to about O(k^2 / H^2(f+||f-)).  
- Addresses an important, well-motivated problem at the intersection of active learning and clustering.  
- Theoretical results, if correct, quantify the benefit of side-information and the proposed parameter-free algorithms are practically interesting.  
- Draws an insightful connection to community detection in stochastic block models.  
- Manuscript has poor writing quality, many grammatical/formatting errors, and dense proofs that are difficult to verify.  
- Novelty claims are overstated and related work is incomplete, notably missing "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" (Gadde et al., ISIT 2016).  
- Formal model lacks precision, with unclear minimax formulation and unspecified assumptions on cluster sizes/imbalanced clusters.  
- Theorem 2 appears incomplete or incorrect—it seems to implicitly assume k grows with n but does not state this, potentially conflicting with known constant-k results.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak, comparing only to trivial baselines rather than relevant existing methods.  
- Recommendation: not ready for publication; support only after major revision fixing language, precisely stating theorem conditions (e.g., assumptions on k), expanding related work, and strengthening experiments.
