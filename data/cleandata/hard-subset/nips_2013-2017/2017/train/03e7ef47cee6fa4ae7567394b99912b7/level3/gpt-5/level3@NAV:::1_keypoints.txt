- Paper studies interactive clustering with same-cluster queries plus a similarity matrix W whose entries come from f+ or f− depending on cluster membership.  
- Main positive result: side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H^2(f+||f−)) via parameter-free algorithms, where H is Hellinger distance.  
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds that match the upper bounds up to a log n factor.  
- Generalizes the stochastic block model by allowing arbitrary (unknown) f+, f− and adversarial partitions, raising questions about how active queries affect SBM thresholds.  
- The use of Hellinger distance is a principled way to quantify similarity-matrix quality and is estimable in practice.  
- Technical contributions are strong: novel adaptive lower-bound framework and efficient algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) that learn surrogates of f+, f− and use Hellinger-based tests.  
- Empirical section is modest but sensible and demonstrates practical benefit.  
- Major concern: recurring discrepancy between O(k log n / H^2) and O(k^2 log n / H^2) in different parts — authors must reconcile which is tight for each algorithm.  
- Presentation issues: unify “Hellinger distance/divergence” terminology, state success probabilities and constants in one place, and explicitly treat discrete vs. continuous f+, f−.  
- Accessibility could be improved by a brief high-level intuition for the lower bound beyond technical derivations.  
- Originality: high — novel model, adaptive lower-bound technique, and parameter-free Hellinger-based algorithms beyond prior work.  
- Significance: high — clean theory for value of side information, nearly tight bounds, and practical metric likely to spur follow-up work.  
- Pros for acceptance: nearly tight bounds, parameter-free efficient algorithms, principled Hellinger use, and treatment of adaptive queries relevant to active learning and entity resolution.  
- Cons against acceptance: inconsistency on k vs. k^2 factor, minor notation/presentation issues, limited experiments (one dataset, no baselines), and assumption of noiseless oracle deferred to companion work.
