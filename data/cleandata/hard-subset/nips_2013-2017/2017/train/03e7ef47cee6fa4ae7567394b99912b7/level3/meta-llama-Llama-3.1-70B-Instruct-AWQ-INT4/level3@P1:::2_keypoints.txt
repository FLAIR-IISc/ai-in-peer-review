- Paper studies query complexity of clustering with side information, giving information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds plus efficient algorithms for discrete distributions.
- Contribution: general framework proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering that holds for randomized algorithms and even with full knowledge of distributions and k.
- Contribution: Monte Carlo algorithm reduces queries from O(nk) to O(k^2 log n · H^2(f+||f-)) and recovers clusters accurately with high probability.
- Contribution: Las Vegas algorithm recovers clusters with probability 1 and query complexity O(n log n + k^2 log n · H^2(f+||f-)).
- Paper connects results to community detection models (e.g., stochastic block model) and suggests several future research directions.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical treatment of a fundamental clustering-with-side-information problem.
- Strength: efficient algorithms that nearly match the proved lower bound.
- Strength: exploration of connections to popular community-detection models.
- Weakness: poor writing quality makes arguments and proofs hard to follow.
- Weakness: omits several relevant prior works, including labeled/weighted stochastic block model literature.
- Weakness: novelty claims are overstated and should be reworded.
- Weakness: formal model is unclear; minimax formulation and cluster-size restrictions are not explicitly stated.
- Weakness: Theorem 2 appears incorrect or incomplete and may conflict with known k = O(1) cases where Q = 0 suffices.
- Weakness: numerical experiments are weak, comparing only to trivial baselines and not benchmarking against existing methods.
- Recommendation: revise writing, clarify and correct theorem statements, explicitly state the formal model and minimax/size restrictions, tone down novelty claims, and strengthen experimental comparisons.
- Rating: 6/10 initially; post-author-feedback rating 8/10 if careful revisions address writing, theorem precision, and grammar.
