Summary: The paper studies query complexity for clustering with pairwise side information, giving upper and lower bounds and two algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) that match bounds up to log factors.

Strengths:
- Rigorous theoretical contribution showing significant query reductions when side information is available.
- Novel, efficient, parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms with practical potential.
- Tight upper and lower bounds on query complexity.

Weaknesses:
- Proofs are dense and difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Relevant prior work (e.g., labeled/weighted stochastic block model papers) is omitted.
- Missing citation: "Active Learning for Community Detection in Stochastic Block Models" by Gadde et al.
- Novelty claims are overstated and some statements need rewording.
- Formal model (minimax formulation and cluster size restrictions) is not clearly stated.
- Theorem 2 appears incorrect or incomplete and may conflict with known k = O(1) results.
- Numerical experiments are weak, comparing only to trivial baselines rather than existing methods.
- Numerous grammar, punctuation, and formatting errors need proofreading.

Recommendation (accept with major revisions):
- Simplify and clarify proofs for accessibility.
- Add and discuss omitted related works, including labeled/weighted SBM literature.
- Include the missing Gadde et al. citation.
- Reword novelty claims to accurately reflect contributions.
- Explicitly state the minimax formulation and cluster size restrictions.
- Revise and correct Theorem 2 to resolve stated issues.
- Strengthen experiments by benchmarking against existing methods and expanding results.
- Thoroughly proofread to fix language and formatting errors.
