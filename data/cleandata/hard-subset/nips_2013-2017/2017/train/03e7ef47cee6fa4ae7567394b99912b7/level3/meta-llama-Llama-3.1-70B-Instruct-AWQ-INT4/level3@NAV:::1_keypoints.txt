- Problem & goal: Study query complexity of recovering k unknown clusters using pairwise same-cluster oracle plus similarity matrix W drawn from f+ (same) or f- (different).  
- Main result: With side information, query complexity reduces from Θ(nk) to O(k log n / H^2(f+||f-)), where H is the Hellinger distance.  
- Lower bound: Nearly tight lower bound Ω(min{nk, k^2 H^2(f+||f-)}) matching the upper bound up to logarithmic factors.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis with significant technical contributions.  
- Model & algorithm: Introduces a more general model and a new, nearly optimal clustering algorithm under that model.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on a real-world dataset support the theoretical findings.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and explains the problem and model clearly.  
- Strength — significance: Results are important for clustering/query complexity and applicable to tasks like entity resolution and community detection.  
- Weakness — oracle realism: Assumes an always-correct oracle, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness — data assumptions: Does not consider noisy or incomplete similarity matrix W.  
- Weakness — parameter choice: Lacks clear guidance on choosing algorithm parameters (e.g., threshold τ).  
- Overall recommendation: Recommend accept — significant, well-supported contribution.
