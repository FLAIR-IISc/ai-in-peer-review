- Provides the first tight information-theoretic bounds on query complexity for interactive clustering with side information: Θ(k² log n / H²(f+||f-)) vs Θ(nk) without side information.  
- Analyzes a model where side information is a similarity matrix from distributions f+ (intra-cluster) and f- (inter-cluster), plus an oracle for pairwise queries.  
- Extends prior lower bounds (which assumed no side information) to the general side-information case and connects the model to stochastic block models while relaxing SBM assumptions.  
- The lower bound technique handles adaptive queries and may generalize to other interactive learning problems.  
- Presents parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing k, f+, or f-, improving practical applicability.  
- Empirical results on the Cora dataset show the greedy strategy performs near the theoretical optimum.  
- Uses Hellinger divergence and Sanov’s theorem to tightly relate divergence to query complexity, demonstrating technical depth.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presented with a good discussion of implications for SBM.  
- Does not address how the side-information similarity matrix is generated from raw data, limiting practical applicability.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a single dataset (Cora), reducing evidence of generality.  
- Assumes a perfect oracle and does not integrate noisy/crowd-sourced query models into the main analysis.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the regime where H²(f+||f-) is extremely small (boundary cases where queries become costly).  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution with significant implications for interactive clustering and community detection.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first rigorous analysis with tight bounds, parameter-free algorithms, novel lower-bound technique, and SBM connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, omission of oracle error modeling, and no discussion of practical side-information construction.
