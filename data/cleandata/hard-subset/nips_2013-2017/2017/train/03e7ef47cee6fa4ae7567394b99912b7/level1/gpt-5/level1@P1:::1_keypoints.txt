- Problem and setup: Studies query complexity of exact clustering with noisy pairwise similarity matrix W and same-cluster queries, unknown f+, f− and k.  
- Main theoretical results: Information-theoretic lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) and algorithms nearly matching these bounds up to log n factors.  
- Algorithms: Parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that estimate intra/inter distributions and decide when to trust W; query bounds claimed O(k^2 log n/H^2) (clarity issue).  
- Lower-bound technique: New interactive-information framework using Hellinger distance and hypothesis-testing ideas, overcoming limitations of standard Fano bounds.  
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes SBM (Bernoulli case), complements active community detection, and advances over heuristic crowdsourced ER methods lacking guarantees.  
- Conceptual contribution: Shows rigorously how side information can reduce queries from Θ(nk) to near O(k^2/H^2).  
- Generality: Handles arbitrary discrete f+, f−, unknown k, adversarial cluster sizes; extensions to continuous settings are asserted but not developed.  
- Algorithmic novelty: Iterative-update approach learning p+ and p− on the fly and using Hellinger-based decisions.  
- Efficiency: Time O(n^2) (dominated by reading W); query complexity near-optimal in k and divergence up to logs.  
- Inconsistency issue: Monte Carlo query complexity stated as O(k log n/H^2) in one place but should likely be O(k^2 log n/H^2) to match lower bound — must be reconciled.  
- Distributional assumptions: Algorithms assume discrete fixed support for f+, f−; handling continuous or growing support (binning, sensitivity) is undeveloped.  
- Technical conditions: Lower bound assumes H^2 ≤ 1/18 and starts from equal-size clusters; intuition for k^2 factor and effect of imbalance is lacking.  
- Experimental limitations: Single real dataset (Cora), stylized evaluations, no comparisons to practical baselines, and no synthetic scaling study varying H^2, k, n.  
- Clarity and presentation: Notation, constants, and thresholds are dense; needs clearer pseudo-code, fixed typos, and clearer statement of Monte Carlo vs Las Vegas guarantees.  
- Modeling realism: W entries assumed i.i.d. conditional on labels; robustness to dependency or model misspecification is not discussed.  
- Noisy oracle: Real crowds may be noisy; extension deferred to companion work [34] but a brief discussion here would help.  
- Technical soundness: Proof strategy and analyses appear careful and appropriate, but the k vs k^2 inconsistency must be fixed.  
- Originality and significance: High — novel interactive lower-bound framework and parameter-free near-optimal algorithms with broad potential impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: Near-tight general bounds, innovative lower-bound method, and bridge between theory and observed query reductions using side information.  
- Cons against acceptance: Serious formula inconsistency, limited experiments, and some undeveloped assumptions and constants.  
- Suggested improvements: Reconcile k^2/H^2 dependence, add synthetic experiments varying H^2/k/n and compare baselines, provide discretization guidance for continuous similarities, and tighten presentation/pseudocode.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical contribution but needs corrected bounds and stronger empirical validation.
