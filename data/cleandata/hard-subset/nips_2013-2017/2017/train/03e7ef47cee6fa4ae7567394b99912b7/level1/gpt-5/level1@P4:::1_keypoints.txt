- Paper studies exact clustering query complexity with pairwise same-cluster oracle plus i.i.d. similarity side information, producing information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching parameter-free algorithms.  
- Without side information queries are Θ(nk); with side information algorithms achieve O(k log n / H2) (Monte Carlo) and O(n log n + k^2 log n / H2) (Las Vegas) where H2 is squared Hellinger divergence.  
- Lower bounds Ω(min{nk, k^2 / H2}) (and Ω(n + min{nk, k^2 / H2}) for certainty) hold even for adaptive/randomized algorithms with known k, f+, f−.  
- Strength: technically strong, general adaptive lower bounds using reduction to multi-hypothesis testing and Hellinger/TV control.  
- Strength: Nearly matching upper bounds with parameter-free, Sanov-based Monte Carlo algorithm and a Las Vegas variant; algorithms run in O(n^2).  
- Strength: Clear quantification of value of side information via explicit H2 dependence that drastically reduces query complexity.  
- Strength: Conceptual connection to SBM (special case Bernoulli(p), Bernoulli(q)) and to active recovery literature.  
- Strength: Practical relevance shown by simple membership scoring and a small experiment demonstrating large query savings.  
- Weakness: Algorithm analysis assumes discrete finite-support f+, f−; extension to continuous support not fully developed.  
- Weakness: i.i.d. independence assumption for pairwise similarities may be unrealistic and robustness to misspecification is unaddressed.  
- Weakness: Main results assume a noiseless oracle; noisy-query robustness is deferred to companion work.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation (single dataset) and no comparisons to established active/entity-resolution baselines.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—minor inconsistencies in stated bounds, typos, and reliance on appendices; pseudocode and constants are lacking.  
- Quality assessment: Theoretical arguments appear sound with careful probabilistic analysis; some details are in the supplement.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally well organized but notation inconsistencies and terse algorithm description reduce clarity.  
- Originality assessment: High — novel formulation, parameter-free strategy, and adaptive lower bounds.  
- Significance assessment: High — interpretable dependence on Hellinger divergence and broad applicability to active clustering regimes.  
- Suggested improvements: unify bound statements (k vs k^2), add concise pseudocode and explicit constants, discuss memory/time for large n, extend experiments and baselines, and outline extensions to continuous f+, f−.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong adaptive lower bounds, near-optimal parameter-free algorithms, links to SBM/active learning, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive algorithmic assumptions (discrete i.i.d., noiseless oracle), limited experiments and baseline comparisons, and minor presentation issues.
