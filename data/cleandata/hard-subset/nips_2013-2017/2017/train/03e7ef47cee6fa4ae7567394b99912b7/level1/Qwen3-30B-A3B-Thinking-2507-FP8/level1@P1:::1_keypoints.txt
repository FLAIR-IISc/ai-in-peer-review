- Paper establishes query complexity for clustering with side information, reducing queries from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n / H²(f+‖f-)).  
- Works for arbitrary unknown similarity distributions f+ (same-cluster) and f- (different-cluster), connecting to the stochastic block model as a special case.  
- Provides rigorous information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching, computationally efficient, parameter-free upper-bound algorithms.  
- Lower bound framework is novel and overcomes challenges for interactive settings where standard tools like Fano's inequality fail.  
- Upper bound algorithms are parameter-free (do not require k, f+, or f-) and are computationally efficient.  
- Theoretical guarantees hold even for randomized algorithms.  
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset supports theoretical claims but is limited to a single dataset and similarity function.  
- The paper assumes a noiseless oracle; noisy/crowdsourced oracles are not handled here (addressed in companion work [34] but not discussed in depth).  
- Assumes the generative similarity matrix model (f+ vs f-); robustness to model misspecification is not analyzed.  
- Lower bound proof is technically strong but may be hard to access for readers without information-theory background.  
- Contributions are original, significant for practical applications like entity resolution, and open new research directions.  
- Paper is well-written and organized with clear algorithms and pseudocode.  
- Recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to broaden experimental validation and more explicitly acknowledge the noiseless-oracle assumption.
