- Paper formalizes interactive clustering with side information modeled by two distributions f+ (intra-cluster) and f- (inter-cluster) over a similarity matrix.  
- Main theoretical result: query complexity reduces from Θ(nk) to Θ(k^2 / H^2(f+ || f-)) up to log factors, where H^2 is squared Hellinger divergence.  
- Provides nearly tight upper and lower bounds on query complexity under this model.  
- Develops computationally efficient, parameter-free algorithms that achieve the bound (within log n) without knowing k, f+, or f-.  
- Introduces an innovative lower-bound proof technique handling adaptive queries via "under-queried" elements and reduction to multi-hypothesis testing.  
- Proposes a clever Monte Carlo algorithm with an iterative-update mechanism that estimates required evidence dynamically.  
- Establishes a clear connection and generalization to the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Paper is technically strong, novel, and likely influential for active learning, crowdsourcing, and community detection.  
- Writing is generally clear and well-positioned within prior work; the introduction effectively motivates the problem (e.g., entity resolution).  
- Algorithm presentation (Section 3) is dense and would benefit from formal pseudocode and clearer stopping/phase descriptions.  
- Experimental validation is limited (only Cora); lacks synthetic scaling experiments and comparisons to practical heuristics.  
- Analysis assumes discrete distributions with fixed support and i.i.d. similarity entries; extensions are claimed but not fully discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, foundational theoretical contribution with minor presentation and empirical limitations.
