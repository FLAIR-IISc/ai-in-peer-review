- Develops a generic framework for proving lower bounds for interactive clustering and designs efficient algorithms for arbitrary distributions.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis that fills a gap in the literature.  
- Establishes strong information-theoretic lower bounds that are nearly matched by proposed algorithms.

- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and should be expanded.  
- Assumes an oracle that always answers correctly, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- The novel framework and use of information-theoretic techniques are original and impactful.  
- Results are significant for designing efficient clustering algorithms in domains like entity resolution, community detection, and crowdsourcing.
