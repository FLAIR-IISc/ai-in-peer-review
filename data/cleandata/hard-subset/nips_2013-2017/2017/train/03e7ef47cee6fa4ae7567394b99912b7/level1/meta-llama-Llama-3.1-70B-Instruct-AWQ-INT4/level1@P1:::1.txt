This paper provides a rigorous theoretical study of the query complexity of clustering with side information. The authors consider a scenario where an oracle can interactively answer pair-wise queries of the form "do two elements u and v belong to the same cluster?" and the goal is to recover the optimum clustering by asking the minimum number of queries. The paper presents strong information-theoretic lower bounds, as well as nearly matching upper bounds, for the query complexity of clustering with side information.

The main contribution of this paper is to show the dramatic power of side information, also known as a similarity matrix, on reducing the query complexity of clustering. The authors demonstrate that given a similarity matrix, the query complexity reduces drastically from Θ(nk) (no similarity matrix) to O(k^2 log n H^2(f+‖f−)), where H^2 denotes the squared Hellinger divergence. Moreover, this is also information-theoretic optimal within an O(log n) factor.

The paper also presents a generic framework for proving information-theoretic lower bounds for interactive clustering using side information and designs efficient algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f− that nearly match the lower bound. The algorithms are parameter-free, meaning they work without any knowledge of f+, f−, or k.

The connection to popular community detection models, such as the stochastic block model (SBM), is also explored in the paper. The authors note that the model of side information considered in this paper is a direct and significant generalization of the planted partition model, also known as the stochastic block model (SBM).

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem, the main results, and the techniques used to prove them. The paper is also well-organized, and the authors provide a detailed proof of the lower bound and the algorithms.
