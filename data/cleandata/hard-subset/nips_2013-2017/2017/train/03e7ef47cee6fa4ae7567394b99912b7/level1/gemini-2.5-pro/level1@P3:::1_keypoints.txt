- Formalizes interactive clustering with noisy similarity matrix where w_uv ~ f+ if same cluster and f- otherwise.
- Characterizes query complexity in terms of squared Hellinger divergence H^2(f+ || f-).
- Proves an information-theoretic lower bound Ω(min{nk, k^2 / H^2(f+||f-)}) for recovery with constant probability.
- Provides two efficient algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) achieving O(k^2 log n / H^2) queries (Las Vegas adds O(n log n)), nearly matching the lower bound.
- Algorithms are parameter-free and do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f-; Monte Carlo uses an iterative-update scheme.
- Connects the model to the Stochastic Block Model as a special Bernoulli case, generalizing SBM recovery interactively.
- Lower bound proof is technically strong and novel, using rarely-queried subsets and multi-hypothesis testing.
- Upper bound analysis is rigorous, leveraging large-deviation tools like Sanov’s theorem.
- Paper gives a complete near-tight theoretical answer, with both matching upper and lower bounds.
- Well-written and motivated, with precise main theorems and a clear introduction via entity resolution.
- Main technical details and algorithmic proofs are relegated to the appendix, making the paper less self-contained.
- Highly original as the first formal treatment of query complexity with general side information and parameter-free algorithms.
- Significant for theory and practice: explains gap between worst-case bounds and heuristics, and provides principled algorithms for crowdsourcing/entity resolution.
- Experimental evaluation is limited (single dataset) and lacks comparison to state-of-the-art heuristics.
- Model assumptions (i.i.d. f+/f-) simplify real-world similarity complexities.
- Overall assessment: excellent, foundational work with minor weaknesses; strong case for acceptance.
