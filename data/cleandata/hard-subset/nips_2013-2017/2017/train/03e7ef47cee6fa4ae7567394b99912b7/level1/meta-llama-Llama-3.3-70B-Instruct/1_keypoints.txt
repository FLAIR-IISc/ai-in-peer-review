- Rigorous theoretical study of interactive clustering with side information using oracle pairwise queries.
- Derives strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity.
- Main contribution: shows side information (similarity matrix) can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n H^2(f+‖f−)).
- Proposes two computationally efficient, parameter-free algorithms that match the lower bound within a log n factor.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough related-work review.
- Provides detailed analysis of query complexity and significant technical contributions.
- Includes experimental results on a real-world dataset demonstrating effectiveness of the algorithms.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of clustering with side information.
- Strength: two efficient, parameter-free algorithms matching theoretical limits up to log factors.
- Strength: empirical validation on real data.
- Weakness: assumes oracle always returns correct answers, which may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: does not consider noisy or incomplete similarity matrices.
- Weakness: lacks clear comparison with existing algorithms for clustering with side information.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-analyzed contribution despite noted weaknesses.
