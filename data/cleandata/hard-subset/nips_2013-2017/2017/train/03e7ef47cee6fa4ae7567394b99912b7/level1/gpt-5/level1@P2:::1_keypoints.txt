- Studies query complexity of exact clustering with same-cluster queries plus a similarity matrix W under a generative model with unknown f+ and f−; derives near-matching information-theoretic lower and upper bounds.  
- Main message: side information can reduce query complexity from Θ(nk) to roughly Õ(min{nk, k^2/H^2}), where H^2 is the squared Hellinger divergence between f+ and f−.  
- Introduces a new adaptive, interactive lower-bound framework since standard Fano arguments are insufficient.  
- Designs parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms that estimate intra/inter-cluster distributions from seeds and use Hellinger-based membership tests.  
- Iterative-update scheme adaptively improves estimates so most points can be placed without queries once side information suffices.  
- Generalizes and connects to SBM results while allowing arbitrary unknown f+, f−, unknown k, and adversarial cluster sizes.  
- Technical soundness: lower bound construction is careful and assumptions and steps are explicit.  
- Upper-bound analysis uses Sanov’s theorem and Hellinger metrics to derive sample thresholds and argues concentration of empirical estimates; Las Vegas adds O(n log n) overhead.  
- Caveat: inconsistent statements about k vs k^2 dependence across the paper (abstract vs theorem vs body) that must be reconciled.  
- Caveat: upper-bound algorithms assume discrete fixed-support f+, f−; extensions to continuous settings are not provided algorithmically.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well organized and explains high-level ideas before formalism.  
- Clarity issue: notational/typographical inconsistencies (k vs k^2, H vs H^2, missing parentheses) reduce precision and should be fixed.  
- Clarity issue: key constants and Phase 3 membership-test inequalities could be presented more cleanly and with intuition.  
- Proofs are deferred to appendix; acceptable but the main paper should highlight crucial lemmas and implications.  
- Originality: novel interactive lower-bound framework for side-information-driven clustering with adaptive queries.  
- Originality: parameter-free algorithm that calibrates the role of W via empirical Hellinger estimates is new and neat.  
- Originality: Hellinger-based characterization provides a distribution-agnostic measure that generalizes SBM results.  
- Significance: clarifies when and why similarity matrices can drastically reduce querying; likely to influence active learning and community detection research.  
- Practical significance limited by sparse empirical validation—experiments only on one ER dataset (Cora) and no comparisons to prior heuristics.  
- Pros: strong adaptive lower bounds, nearly matching parameter-free algorithms, clear dependence on H^2, generalization of SBM, and useful conceptual tools (Hellinger, Sanov thresholds).  
- Cons: inconsistent k vs k^2 factor, discrete-support assumption, unrealistic independence assumptions for W, limited experiments and scalability concerns, and a remaining log n gap between bounds.  
- Suggestions: reconcile complexity statements and Hellinger notation; prominently state support assumptions and discuss discretization/kernel options for continuous W.  
- Suggestions: add synthetic experiments varying H^2 and compare to existing query-selection heuristics; discuss robustness to dependent or biased W and oracle noise.  
- Suggestions: clarify constants/thresholds in Phase 3 and provide an algorithm flow schematic.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution supporting acceptance, contingent on fixing inconsistencies and improving presentation.
