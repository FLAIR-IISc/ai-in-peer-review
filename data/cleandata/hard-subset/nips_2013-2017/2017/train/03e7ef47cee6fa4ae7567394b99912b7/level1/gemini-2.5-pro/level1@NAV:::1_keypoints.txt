- Studies query complexity of interactive clustering and formalizes the empirical advantage of similarity matrices as “side information.”  
- Models pairwise similarities drawn from f+ or f− depending on same/different cluster, generalizing the Stochastic Block Model.  
- Main theoretical result: nearly-tight upper and lower bounds showing queries scale as ~ k² / H²(f+‖f−) (H² = squared Hellinger divergence).  
- Technically rigorous with strong information-theoretic lower bounds and matching efficient algorithmic upper bounds.  
- Introduces a novel lower-bound technique for the adaptive query setting.  
- Original contribution: first formal quantification of how side information reduces interactive clustering query complexity.  
- Proposes a creative, parameter-free iterative-update algorithm that is practical and efficient.  
- Significance: provides theoretical foundation for practical systems in entity resolution and crowdsourcing, bridging theory and practice.  
- Clarity: well-written paper with a clear introduction and upfront statement of main theorems.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and could be more comprehensive.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel near-tight characterization, bridges a long-standing gap, practical parameter-free algorithms, and an independently interesting lower-bound technique.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths substantially outweigh the minor weakness; strong accept.
