**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information" studies the problem of clustering with an oracle that can interactively answer pair-wise queries. The goal is to recover the optimal clustering by asking the minimum number of queries. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical study of this problem, including strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds. They show that the query complexity of clustering can be drastically reduced with the help of side information, represented as a similarity matrix.

The authors also connect their work to popular community detection models, such as the stochastic block model (SBM), and open up many avenues for interesting future research. They propose two algorithms, a Monte Carlo algorithm and a Las Vegas algorithm, both of which are completely parameter-free and match the query complexity lower bound within a log n factor.

**Strengths**

1. **Rigorous theoretical study**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous theoretical study of the problem of clustering with an oracle, including strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds.
2. **Connection to community detection models**: The authors connect their work to popular community detection models, such as SBM, and open up many avenues for interesting future research.
3. **Parameter-free algorithms**: The proposed algorithms are completely parameter-free, which is a significant advantage in practice.
4. **Experimental results**: The authors provide experimental results on a popular bibliographic dataset, which demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithms.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the algorithms**: The proposed algorithms, especially the Monte Carlo algorithm, are complex and may be difficult to implement in practice.
2. **Assumptions on the similarity matrix**: The authors assume that the similarity matrix is generated from a probability distribution f+ if two elements belong to the same cluster, and from a probability distribution f− otherwise. This assumption may not always hold in practice.
3. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and more extensive experiments are needed to fully assess the performance of the algorithms.

**Originality and Significance**
