- Core contribution: shows similarity matrix side information reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k² log n H(f+||f−)), with matching information-theoretic lower bounds up to log factors.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides upper and lower bounds and uses Hellinger divergence to quantify information from the similarity matrix.  
- Novelty: the dramatic reduction in queries with side information and the matching lower bound are significant contributions.  
- Parameter-free algorithms: presents algorithms that do not require knowledge of k, f+, or f−.  
- Connection to SBM: situates results within the stochastic block model framework, broadening theoretical relevance.  
- Motivation and context: well-motivated problem, connects to prior interactive clustering heuristics and query-complexity theory.  
- Experimental validation: includes experiments on a real-world dataset supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Dense and technical: paper is highly technical and may be hard for less mathematically inclined readers.  
- Distributional assumption: reliance on discrete f+ and f− may limit applicability; continuous cases are not fully addressed.  
- Limited experiments: empirical evaluation uses only one dataset and one similarity function, limiting empirical generality.  
- Appendix placement: crucial algorithmic details are in the appendix, impeding assessment of practicality and computational cost.  
- Notation readability: some lemmas and notation (e.g., Lemma 1) are dense and could be simplified.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution overall; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss continuous distributions, expand experiments, and move key algorithm details to the main text.
