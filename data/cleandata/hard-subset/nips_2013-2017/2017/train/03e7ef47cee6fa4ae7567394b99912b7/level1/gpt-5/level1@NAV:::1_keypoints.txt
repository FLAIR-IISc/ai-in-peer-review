- Main contribution 1: Information-theoretic lower bound showing side information reduces adaptive pairwise queries from Θ(nk) to roughly Ω(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) up to constants.  
- Main contribution 2: Efficient, parameter-free Monte Carlo and Las Vegas algorithms achieving Õ(min{nk, k^2/H^2}) or Õ(n + k^2/H^2) queries, nearly matching lower bounds.  
- Connects and generalizes SBMs, relating recovery thresholds with and without queries and tying to prior clustering/query/crowdsourcing work.  
- Novel lower-bound technique: casting adaptive assignment as a multi-hypothesis test and controlling total variation via Hellinger divergence for interactive settings.  
- Upper-bound technique: iterative, parameter-free estimator of H^2 using empirical inter/intra distributions with Sanov-type control and a membership test.  
- Technical assumptions: algorithms assume independent W entries and discrete support with fixed cardinality, while lower bounds handle continuous distributions.  
- Notational/statement inconsistencies (k vs k^2, log factors) across abstract/theorems need reconciliation.  
- Paper is well-organized but dense, with typos and equation formatting issues that hinder readability.  
- The parameter-free protocol could be summarized more concisely and assumptions (discrete vs continuous W, support size) should be centralized.  
- Originality: High — novel formulation, lower-bound framework for adaptive queries, and parameter-free algorithms that do not require k, f+, f−.  
- Significance: High — formalizes why similarity matrices reduce queries and provides near-optimal guarantees relevant to active clustering and ER.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong near-tight bounds and parameter-free algorithms; conceptual novelty; practical relevance to crowdsourced ER.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and notational inconsistencies; algorithmic guarantees depend on discrete fixed-support W; assumes perfect oracle; limited experimental evaluation and baselines.
