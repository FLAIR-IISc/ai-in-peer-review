- Rigorous theoretical study of query complexity for clustering with interactive pairwise oracle queries.  
- Provides strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity.  
- Main result: side information (similarity matrix) drastically reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n · H^2(f+, f−)).  
- The upper bound is information-theoretically optimal up to an O(log n) factor.  
- Presents a generic framework for proving interactive lower bounds with side information.  
- Designs efficient, parameter-free algorithms for arbitrary f+ and f− that nearly match the lower bound.  
- Connects the side-information model to community detection and generalizes the stochastic block model (SBM).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides detailed proofs.  
- Strength: demonstrates dramatic power of side information in reducing queries.  
- Strength: algorithms are parameter-free and broadly applicable.  
- Weakness: assumes an always-correct oracle, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: does not handle erroneous oracle answers or robustness to noise.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental evaluation on real-world datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication (high scores: 9/10 across quality, originality, significance, clarity, overall).
