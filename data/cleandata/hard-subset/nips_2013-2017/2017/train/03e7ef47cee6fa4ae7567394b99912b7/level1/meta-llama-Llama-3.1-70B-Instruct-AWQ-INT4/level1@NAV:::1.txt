**Summary of the Paper**

This paper studies the query complexity of clustering with side information, where an oracle can answer pair-wise queries about whether two elements belong to the same cluster. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical study of this problem and show that the query complexity can be drastically reduced with the help of side information. They develop strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds, and design efficient algorithms that are parameter-free, meaning they work without any knowledge of the number of clusters, the similarity matrix, or the probability distributions of the side information.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Rigorous theoretical study**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous analysis of the query complexity of clustering with side information, which is a fundamental problem in machine learning and data mining.
2. **Strong lower bounds**: The authors develop strong information-theoretic lower bounds that establish the minimum number of queries required to recover the clusters accurately.
3. **Efficient algorithms**: The paper designs efficient algorithms that are parameter-free and can recover the clusters accurately with high probability.
4. **Connection to stochastic block models**: The authors establish a connection between their model and stochastic block models, which is a well-studied model in community detection.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Technical complexity**: The paper is highly technical and may be challenging for non-experts to follow.
2. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The paper only provides experimental results on a single dataset, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical applicability of the algorithms.
3. **Assumptions about the oracle**: The paper assumes that the oracle always returns the correct answer, which may not be realistic in practice.

**Originality and Significance**
