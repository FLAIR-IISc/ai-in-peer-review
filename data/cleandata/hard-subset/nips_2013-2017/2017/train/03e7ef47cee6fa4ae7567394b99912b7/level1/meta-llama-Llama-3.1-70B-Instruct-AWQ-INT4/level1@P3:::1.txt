**Summary**

This paper studies the query complexity of clustering with side information, where an oracle can interactively answer pair-wise queries about whether two elements belong to the same cluster. The authors provide strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds, demonstrating the power of side information in reducing the query complexity of clustering. The paper also develops a generic framework for proving lower bounds for interactive clustering using side information and designs efficient algorithms for arbitrary distributions.

**Strengths**

1. **Rigorous theoretical study**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous analysis of the query complexity of clustering with side information, filling a gap in the existing literature.
2. **Strong lower bounds**: The authors establish strong information-theoretic lower bounds on the query complexity, which are nearly matched by their proposed algorithms.
3. **Efficient algorithms**: The paper presents efficient algorithms that are parameter-free, meaning they do not require knowledge of the underlying distributions or the number of clusters.
4. **Connection to community detection models**: The authors highlight the connection between their model and popular community detection models, such as the stochastic block model.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical complexity**: The paper assumes a high level of technical expertise in information theory, probability, and algorithms, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.
2. **Limited experimental evaluation**: While the paper includes some experimental results, they are limited to a single dataset, and more extensive evaluation would be beneficial.
3. **Assumptions about the oracle**: The paper assumes that the oracle always returns the correct answer, which may not be realistic in practice.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel framework for studying the query complexity of clustering with side information, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors' use of information-theoretic techniques to establish lower bounds and design efficient algorithms is also original and impactful.

**Significance**

The paper's results have important implications for the design of efficient clustering algorithms, particularly in scenarios where side information is available. The authors' framework and algorithms can be applied to various domains, such as entity resolution, community detection, and crowdsourcing.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide clear explanations of their technical contributions. However, the paper's technical complexity may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.

**Suggestions for improvement**

1. **Provide more intuitive explanations**: The authors could include more intuitive explanations of their technical contributions to make the paper more accessible to non-experts.
2. **Include more experimental evaluation**: The authors should consider including more extensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithms in various scenarios.
3. **Discuss limitations and future work**: The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their work and potential avenues for future research.

**Rating**

I would rate this paper 8.5/10. While it presents significant technical contributions and a thorough analysis, its technical complexity and limited experimental evaluation are drawbacks. With some revisions to address these issues, the paper has the potential to be even more impactful.
