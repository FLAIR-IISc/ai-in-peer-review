- Main theoretical contribution: tight information-theoretic lower bound Ω(n + min{nk, k²/H²(f⁺‖f⁻)}) and nearly matching upper bounds.  
- Query-complexity result: parameter-free algorithms achieve O(k² log n / H²(f⁺‖f⁻)) queries, a dramatic reduction from Θ(nk) without side information.  
- Parameter-free significance: algorithms work without knowledge of k, f⁺, or f⁻, improving practical applicability.  
- Theoretical rigor: proofs are technically sound and rigorous.  
- Connection to SBM: links to stochastic block models provide valuable context and grounding.  
- Novelty: first information-theoretic analysis of how side information affects interactive clustering query complexity.  
- Relation to prior work: appropriately builds on prior query-complexity and clustering-with-side-information literature while departing meaningfully.  
- Clarity and presentation: well-structured and clearly written, though the lower-bound proof is highly technical.  
- Experiments: Cora dataset results show practical relevance but empirical validation is limited and could be expanded.  
- Limitation: does not handle noisy oracle responses (noted as future work).  
- Overall assessment: scores highly on quality, clarity, originality, and significance; substantial advance and good fit for NIPS.  
- Acceptance vs. rejection points: strong theoretical and practical contributions argue for acceptance; limited experiments and omission of noisy-oracle analysis are secondary arguments against.
