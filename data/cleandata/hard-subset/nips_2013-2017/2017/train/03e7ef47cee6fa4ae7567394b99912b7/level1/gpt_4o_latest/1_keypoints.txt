- Addresses interactive clustering with an oracle and studies query complexity to recover true clustering.
- Provides information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching algorithmic upper bounds on query complexity.
- Shows that noisy similarity side information can drastically reduce required queries.
- Introduces efficient, parameter-free algorithms that do not require knowing cluster counts or similarity distributions.
- Establishes connections to the stochastic block model and generalizes SBM to arbitrary distributions.
- Proposes a general framework for proving lower bounds in the interactive setting.
- Uses Hellinger divergence as a novel, well-motivated separation measure between distributions.
- Develops an iterative-update method for designing parameter-free algorithms.
- Experimental validation on the Cora dataset shows high precision and recall with few queries.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with provable guarantees bridging prior heuristic work.
- Weakness: a remaining logarithmic gap between lower and upper bounds is unresolved.
- Weakness: assumes a perfect oracle and only briefly mentions noisy oracle handling.
- Weakness: proofs are dense and may be inaccessible to readers without an information-theory background.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, suggesting closure of the logarithmic gap and broader experiments.
