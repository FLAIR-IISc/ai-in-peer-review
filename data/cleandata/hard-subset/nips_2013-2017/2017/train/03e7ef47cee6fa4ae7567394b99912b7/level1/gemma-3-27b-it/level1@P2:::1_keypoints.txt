- Main contribution: Shows side information (similarity matrix) reduces query complexity from Θ(nk) to O(k^2 log n · Hellinger divergence), with matching information-theoretic lower and upper bounds.
- Algorithms: Presents algorithms achieving the stated query complexity, including parameter-free variants that do not require k, f+ or f−.
- Theoretical significance: Strong, rigorous analysis using Hellinger divergence and connections to the stochastic block model (SBM).
- Relation to prior work: Properly situates the paper within interactive clustering, query-complexity literature, and SBM-related work, citing relevant papers.
- Novelty: Introduces a new perspective on how side information affects interactive clustering query complexity.
- Experimental validation: Provides empirical results on the Cora dataset demonstrating practical performance.
- Strength: Thorough related work section and clear problem formulation motivated by real-world tasks like entity resolution.
- Weakness: Proofs (especially the lower bound) are intricate and could use more intuition for accessibility.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Cora); more diverse datasets would strengthen empirical claims.
- Weakness: Practical runtime concerns for very large datasets despite polynomial guarantees; discussion of complexity and optimizations is lacking.
- Minor issues: Some notation (e.g., consistent use of pmfs f+ and f−) needs clarification.
- Argument for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance with strong technical soundness and potential impact on interactive clustering design.
- Argument against acceptance: Accessibility hampered by complex proofs and limited empirical generalizability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve proof intuition, expand empirical evaluation, and discuss practical optimizations.
