- Tackles query complexity of interactive clustering with side information (similarity matrix) and an oracle, proving information-theoretic lower and upper bounds.  
- Shows side information can substantially reduce query complexity compared to no side information.  
- Well-motivated by practical applications like entity resolution where labeled data is costly.  
- Situates the work relative to prior interactive clustering, SBM, active learning, crowdsourcing, and community detection literature.  
- Identifies a gap in theoretical understanding of similarity matrices’ effect on query complexity and aims to fill it.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with nearly matching lower and upper bounds.  
- Novel contribution: analyzing impact of arbitrary similarity distributions (f+ and f-) and unknown number of clusters k.  
- Presents parameter-free algorithms, enhancing practical appeal.  
- Empirical support provided via experiments on the Cora dataset.  
- Clear presentation of contributions and technical roadmap.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and lacks comparisons to more heuristics and diverse benchmarks.  
- Proofs are complex and mostly in appendices, reducing accessibility for non-specialist readers.  
- Upper bounds include a logarithmic factor; constant factors and optimization opportunities are not fully explored.  
- Lacks discussion of practical challenges like computational cost of estimating f+ and f- for large datasets.  
- More detailed discussion of model limitations (e.g., oracle accuracy assumptions) would be valuable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, nearly matching bounds, parameter-free algorithms, strong motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, inaccessible proofs, and insufficient exploration of large-scale practical implications.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions—expand experiments, analyze runtime/scaling, and discuss practical limitations.
