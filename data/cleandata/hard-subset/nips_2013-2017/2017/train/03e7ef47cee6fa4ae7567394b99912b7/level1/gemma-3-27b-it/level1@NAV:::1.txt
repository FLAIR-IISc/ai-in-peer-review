## Review of "Query Complexity of Clustering with Side Information"

This paper investigates the query complexity of clustering when an oracle provides pairwise similarity information. The core contribution lies in demonstrating how incorporating a similarity matrix (side information) dramatically reduces the number of queries needed compared to the case without it, proving near-optimal bounds. The authors establish that the query complexity drops from Θ(nk) to O(k² logn H(f+||f−)), where H denotes the Hellinger divergence between distributions governing similar and dissimilar pairs. They provide both upper bounds (via algorithms) and matching lower bounds, achieving a significant theoretical advance in understanding interactive clustering.

This work is well-motivated by real-world applications like entity resolution where obtaining accurate similarity functions is challenging, and leveraging human input is common. The connection to the stochastic block model (SBM) is insightful, positioning the research within a well-studied framework while generalizing it to arbitrary distributions and unknown parameters. The paper builds upon previous work on interactive clustering ([7, 6, 40, 13]) by introducing a systematic theoretical analysis absent in prior heuristic approaches. They appropriately acknowledge and contrast their work with related areas like active learning and crowdsourcing, as well as the limitations of existing lower bounds ([5]).

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Results:** The paper delivers rigorous upper and lower bounds on query complexity, providing a solid theoretical foundation for interactive clustering with side information.
*   **Novelty:** The analysis of query complexity with side information is a novel contribution, filling a gap in the existing literature.
*   **Generality:** The framework is general, accommodating arbitrary distributions for similarity values and an unknown number of clusters.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The motivation from entity resolution and potential for improving existing heuristics make the results practically significant.
*   **Clear Connection to SBM:** Linking the problem to the well-known SBM provides context and allows for potential future extensions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity of Presentation:** The paper is dense and requires a strong background in information theory and clustering to fully grasp. While thorough, it could benefit from more intuitive explanations of key concepts.
*   **Experimental Evaluation Limited:** The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset and primarily focuses on illustrating the algorithm’s behavior. A more comprehensive evaluation with diverse datasets and comparison to existing heuristics would strengthen the claims.
* **Parameter Free Algorithm Complexity**: The algorithm proposed is parameter-free, however the detailed explanation is located only in the appendix. This could be better addressed in the main body.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution with clear and rigorous proofs.
*   The problem is well-motivated and relevant to current research in machine learning and data mining.
*   The results are likely to stimulate further research in interactive clustering and related areas.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The technical depth and density might limit the accessibility of the paper to a broader audience.
*   The limited experimental evaluation leaves some questions about the practical performance of the proposed algorithms unanswered.




**Overall:** I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest improving the clarity of the presentation by providing more intuitive explanations of key concepts and expanding the experimental evaluation with more diverse datasets and comparison to state-of-the-art heuristics.
