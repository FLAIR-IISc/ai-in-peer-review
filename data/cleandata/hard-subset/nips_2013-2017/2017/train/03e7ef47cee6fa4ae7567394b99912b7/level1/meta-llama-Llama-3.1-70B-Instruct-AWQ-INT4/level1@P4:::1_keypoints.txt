- Studies interactive clustering with pairwise oracle queries and aims to minimize query complexity using side information (similarity matrix).
- Provides strong information-theoretic lower bounds and nearly matching upper bounds on query complexity.
- Shows side information can drastically reduce queries and connects results to stochastic block model (SBM) and community detection.
- Proposes two parameter-free algorithms (Monte Carlo and Las Vegas) that match lower bounds up to a log n factor.
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with tight bounds.
- Links to popular community detection models and suggests directions for future research.
- Algorithms are fully parameter-free, improving practical usability.

- Algorithms (especially the Monte Carlo one) are complex and may be hard to implement.
- Assumes similarity matrix entries follow distributions f+ and f− by cluster membership, which may not hold in practice.
