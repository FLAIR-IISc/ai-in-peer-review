- Paper aims to find optimal personalized screening policies by modeling disease as a finite-state stochastic process to minimize screening costs and delays.
- Authors propose a computationally efficient approximate solution.
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced delays at similar cost compared to an annual-screening baseline.
- Personalized screening is an important research area.
- The proposed algorithm is reasonable, well-developed, and the paper is well written.
- Results are promising.
- Method relies on the unrealistic assumption that each patient’s disease model is known, whereas in practice model inference is the main challenge.
- Methodological novelty is limited.
- Experiments only compare to a trivial annual baseline and do not assess robustness to model misspecification.
- Need to evaluate sensitivity to uncertainties in the disease model to establish practical relevance.
- Overall: interesting and reasonable work but lacks novelty and practical validation to fully convince readers.
