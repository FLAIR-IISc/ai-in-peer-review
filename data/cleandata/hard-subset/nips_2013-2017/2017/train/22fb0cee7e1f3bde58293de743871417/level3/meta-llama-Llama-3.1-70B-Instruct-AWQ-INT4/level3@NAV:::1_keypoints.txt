- Introduces DPSCREEN, a method for personalized screening policies balancing screening cost vs. delay cost in disease detection.  
- Personalization uses both patient screening history and disease-relevant features.  
- Applicable to a wide range of disease models (within stated model class).  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise mathematical exposition.  
- Provides thorough analysis of computational complexity.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large real-world breast cancer dataset.  
- Results show DPSCREEN reduces screenings while maintaining detection delay, outperforming existing policies.  
- Significant practical potential and well-situated within related literature.  
- Strength: clear technical contribution with demonstrated efficiency gains.  
- Weakness: assumes a specific finite-state stochastic disease model that may not fit all diseases.  
- Weakness: relies on accurate estimation of model parameters, which can be challenging in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other personalized screening frameworks.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and should be accepted, with suggested future work addressing noted weaknesses.
