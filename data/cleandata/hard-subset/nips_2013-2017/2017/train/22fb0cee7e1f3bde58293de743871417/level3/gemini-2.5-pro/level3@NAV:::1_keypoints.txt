- Introduces DPSCREEN, a general framework for dynamic, personalized screening policies across many diseases.  
- Formulates screening as an optimization balancing screening frequency versus detection delay.  
- Personalizes recommendations using static patient features and dynamic screening history.  
- Generalizes prior work by supporting arbitrary finite-state stochastic disease models, not just Markovian processes.  
- Explicitly models external information processes (e.g., self-exams) that can trigger unscheduled visits.  
- Technically sound: provides Bellman formulation, PBVI approximation, and formal computational/approximation analysis.  
- Addresses robustness to model estimation errors theoretically.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written with strong literature positioning and exposition.  
- Demonstrates real-world significance with validation on 45,000 patients, achieving 28–68% fewer screenings without increasing detection delay.  
- Minor weakness: experimental validation could be strengthened with sensitivity analysis on learned disease model parameters.  
- Overall: strong, well-executed contribution with high practical impact; recommends acceptance.
