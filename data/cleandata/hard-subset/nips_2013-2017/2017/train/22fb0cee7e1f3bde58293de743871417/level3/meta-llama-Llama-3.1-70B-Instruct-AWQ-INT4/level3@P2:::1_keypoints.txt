- Introduces DPSCREEN, a novel method for personalized screening that balances screening cost against delay in disease detection.  
- Models disease as an arbitrary finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state, offering high flexibility.  
- Incorporates external information (e.g., self-exams, comorbidities) that can trigger earlier clinical arrival.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound with clear, concise mathematical exposition.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of computational complexity.  
- Validated on a large real-world dataset of 45,000 patients from Athena Health.  
- Results show personalization yields efficiency gains over existing policies without delaying disease detection across multiple disease models.  
- Strengths: handles varied disease models, integrates external signals, personalizes by patient features and history, and includes real-data validation.  
- Weaknesses: may be computationally intensive for large datasets or complex models.  
- Weaknesses: assumes model parameters are known in practice, though estimation-error effects and convergence of approximate policies are discussed.  
- Originality: significant and distinct contribution relative to more restrictive existing frameworks.  
- Significance: potential to improve healthcare efficiency, outcomes, and reduce costs.  
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS 2017 due to technical contribution, empirical validation, and practical impact despite noted limitations.
