- Proposes DPSCREEN: personalized screening using a disease model as a finite-state stochastic process to minimize screening costs and detection delays.
- DPSCREEN is computationally efficient and shows promising delay reductions at equal cost on simulated breast cancer data.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and addresses an important problem in personalized medicine.
- Method is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experiments supporting the claims.
- Strength: personalizes screening policies to individual patients, incorporating external information (self-exam, comorbidities).
- Experiments indicate the algorithm can reduce the number of screenings while maintaining the same detection delay.
- Major limitation: assumes a known disease model, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Lacks methodological novelty—incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- Experiments are limited in scope and only compare to a baseline method.
- Does not assess robustness to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model, weakening practical relevance.
- Recommendations: explore methods to infer the disease model from data and evaluate robustness to model misspecification.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address model inference, robustness, and more comprehensive experiments.
