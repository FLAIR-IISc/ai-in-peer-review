- Presents a framework for personalized screening policies modeled as a finite-state stochastic process to minimize screening costs and delays.  
- Core idea: adapt screening frequency using individual features and screening history, with a computationally tractable approximate solution (PBVI).  
- Demonstrated on simulated breast cancer data: reduced delays with comparable costs to annual screening.  
- Extends prior screening optimization work by accommodating non-Markovian disease dynamics and external information.  
- Links to controlled sensing literature, framing screening as optimal information gathering.  
- Strength: sound methodology using Bellman equations and a practical approximation to tackle intractability.  
- Strength: clear writing and organization, with helpful graphical model for system components.  
- Strength: promising empirical results showing personalized policies reduce screening frequency without increasing delay.  
- Strength: discussion on robustness and an approximation error bound adds credibility.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty; relies on established POMDP/decision-theory techniques.  
- Weakness: main baseline is trivial annual screening; lacks comparison to clinically relevant or advanced baselines.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on known patient disease models; sensitivity to misspecification insufficiently explored.  
- Weakness: experiments use a single breast cancer dataset, limiting generalizability to other diseases and cost structures.  
- Weakness: modeling of external information (self-exams, lump size) may be oversimplified and assumes conditional independence.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem with a clear mathematical framework, promising results, and good exposition.  
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, strong assumptions, inadequate baseline comparisons, and weak robustness analysis raise practical concerns.  
- Overall: technically sound and interesting but needs stronger validation on robustness, baselines, and generalizability; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
