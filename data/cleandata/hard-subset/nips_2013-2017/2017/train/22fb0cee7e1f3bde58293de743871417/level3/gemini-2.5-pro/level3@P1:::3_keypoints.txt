- Introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for dynamic, personalized screening policies balancing screening cost and delay cost.
- Models disease as a general finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state, relaxing strict Markovian assumptions.
- Incorporates an external information process to model patient-driven events that trigger unscheduled visits.
- Formulates policy optimization as mapping patient features and history to next screening date under uncertainty.
- Exact solution is intractable due to high-dimensional belief space; authors propose a PBVI-based approximation.
- Uses sampled representative belief points and value backups to construct an approximately optimal policy.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: a bound on approximation error (Proposition 1) and robustness to model estimation errors (Theorem 1).
- Empirical evaluation on a large real-world breast cancer dataset using a two-state model (Healthy/Diseased).
- Results show similar expected detection delays while reducing expected screenings by 28–68% versus annual/biennial guidelines.
- Strength: high practical significance with potential to reduce screening burden without sacrificing timeliness.
- Strength: original and general framework, explicitly modeling external information and allowing arbitrary finite-state disease models.
- Strength: technically sound formulation and use of PBVI with rigorous theoretical and experimental methodology.
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with effective illustrations and experimental reporting.
- Weakness: sparse details on model estimation (MCMC priors, convergence, goodness-of-fit) that affect reproducibility.
- Weakness: application uses a simplified two-state model and a single test type; scalability to multi-state models and test selection is not explored.
- Weakness: belief point sampling strategy (Algorithm 1) is heuristic and would benefit from more justification versus other POMDP sampling methods.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor methodological and implementation details to address; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
