- Proposes DPSCREEN: a method for personalized screening that balances screening cost against delay, modeling disease as a finite-state stochastic process with absorbing disease state and external triggers.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a concise summary and relation to prior work.  
- Strength: novel methodology addressing personalized screening.  
- Strength: provides a computationally efficient approximate solution.  
- Strength: simulated breast cancer experiments show reduced detection delays at similar cost versus annual screening.  
- Weakness: relies on the unrealistic assumption that each patient’s disease model is known.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited, building on existing POMDP and controlled-sensing frameworks.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison is limited to a trivial annual baseline.  
- Weakness: lacks robustness analysis and sensitivity evaluation to model misspecification and uncertainty.  
- Originality: a reasonable, well-developed contribution but with limited methodological novelty and unclear practical relevance.  
- Significance: high potential impact for public health and applicability to other diseases.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address known-model assumption, robustness/sensitivity analyses, and more details on computational efficiency and broader applicability.  
- Rating: 7/10; Confidence: 8/10.
