- Introduces DPSCREEN: a framework for personalized, dynamic screening policies modeling disease as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state.  
- Aims to balance screening frequency against detection delay and offers a computationally tractable approximation algorithm.  
- Demonstrated on a breast cancer dataset, reporting 28–68% reductions in screening frequency with comparable detection delays to clinical policies.  
- Builds on prior POMDP, Bayesian design, and controlled sensing work but claims greater generality and ability to incorporate external information.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, rigorous mathematical formulation, well-defined problem, and a feasible algorithm described in sufficient detail.  
- Strengths: model generality and a concrete empirical illustration increase the paper’s practical relevance.  
- Strengths: includes a limited robustness analysis and discussion of observational data and model estimation challenges.  
- Main weakness: limited methodological novelty; algorithm largely extends existing techniques (e.g., point-based value iteration).  
- Main weakness: overreliance on assuming known disease models, which is unrealistic in practice.  
- Main weakness: insufficient robustness and sensitivity analyses to model misspecification (e.g., transition probabilities, hazard rates).  
- Main weakness: inadequate empirical comparisons — only compared to annual screening baseline rather than more sophisticated personalized policies.  
- Concern that observed improvements may not justify added complexity without stronger validation or comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, is mathematically sound, shows potential screening reductions, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unrealistic model assumptions, insufficient robustness testing, and weak baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound but lacking novelty and practical validation; recommend rejection in current form with encouragement to add robustness analyses, better baselines, and model-estimation exploration before resubmission.
