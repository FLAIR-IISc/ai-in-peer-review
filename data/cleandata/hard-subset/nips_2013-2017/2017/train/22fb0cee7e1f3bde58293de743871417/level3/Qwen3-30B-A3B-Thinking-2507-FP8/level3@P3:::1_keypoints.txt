- Introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for constructing optimal personalized screening policies across multiple disease classes using patient features and dynamic screening history.  
- Extends prior work by handling general disease models (dynamic survival, first hitting time) beyond Markovian and stationary test-outcome assumptions.  
- Formulates the screening problem as a POMDP with a general disease progression model.  
- Proves the value function is convex and piecewise-linear, enabling tractable computation.  
- Develops Algorithms 1–2 with approximation error bounds and computational complexity analysis.  
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees and rigorous mathematical exposition.  
- Validates on a large real-world dataset (45k patients), achieving 28–68% reductions in screening frequency while maintaining equivalent detection delays.  
- Demonstrates applicability across multiple disease models and different delay cost structures (e.g., linear vs. quadratic).  
- Shows the method personalizes to patient features and screening history (illustrated in Figure 1).  
- Analyzes robustness to model estimation error and reports reasonable robustness.  
- Main limitation: primary real-data focus on breast cancer, but synthetic experiments support broader applicability.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, clarity, originality, and significance with strong arguments for acceptance and no substantial objections.
