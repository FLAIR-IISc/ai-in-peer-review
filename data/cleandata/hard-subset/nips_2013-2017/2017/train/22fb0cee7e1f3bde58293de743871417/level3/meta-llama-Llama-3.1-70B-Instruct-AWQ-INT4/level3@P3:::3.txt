This paper proposes a novel methodology for constructing personalized screening policies that balance the cost of screening against the cost of delay in detection of disease. The authors model the disease as an arbitrary finite state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state and incorporate the possibility of external information, such as self-examination or discovery of co-morbidities, that may trigger arrival of the patient to the clinician in advance of a scheduled screening appointment.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed methodology. The authors also provide a detailed description of the model and the problem formulation, which is helpful in understanding the complexity of the problem. The use of breast cancer data to illustrate the proposed methodology is also a strength of the paper.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a computationally tractable construction of an approximately optimal policy for personalized screening. The authors use a point-based value iteration (PBVI) algorithm to construct the approximately optimal policy, which is a significant improvement over existing methods. The paper also provides bounds for the approximation error, which is an important consideration in evaluating the performance of the proposed methodology.

The paper is also well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the importance of personalized screening policies in reducing the cost of screening while maintaining the same delay in detection of disease. The use of breast cancer data to illustrate the proposed methodology is also a strength of the paper.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive review of existing frameworks for personalized screening policies, including POMDP-based models and Bayesian design-based models. The authors also discuss the limitations of these existing frameworks and how their proposed methodology addresses these limitations.

The paper also provides a detailed evaluation of the proposed methodology using breast cancer data.
