- Proposes FS-RNN, a novel RNN architecture combining multiscale and deep transition RNN strengths.  
- Architecture: lower layer with multiple RNN cells updating multiple times per time step; higher layer with single cell updating once.  
- Enables learning of complex per-step transition functions while capturing long-term dependencies.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on character-level Penn Treebank and Hutter Prize Wikipedia tasks.  
- Provides empirical analysis of learning and network dynamics showing combined benefits of deep transition and multiscale designs.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and accessible writing.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation and comparison to related work.  
- Strength: detailed analysis of network dynamics that aids understanding of model behavior.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to specific related architectures (e.g., Clockwork RNN, Hierarchical Multiscale RNN).  
- Weakness: limited insight into hyperparameter tuning process and model sensitivity to settings.  
- Overall: significant contribution to RNNs and language modeling with strong potential impact.
