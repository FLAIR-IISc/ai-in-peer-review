- Proposes DPSCREEN, a novel personalized screening policy balancing screening cost against delay in disease detection.  
- Method is well-motivated and technically sound with a thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Handles arbitrary finite-state stochastic processes with an absorbing disease state.  
- Incorporates external information and yields a computationally tractable solution.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large breast cancer dataset, reducing screenings while maintaining detection delay.  
- Provides thorough analysis of approximation error and robustness.  
- Complexity of the algorithm may make practical implementation challenging.  
- Relies on accurate estimation of model parameters, which may limit real-world applicability.  
- Authors analyze computational complexity and robustness to address implementation concerns.  
- Lacks additional comparisons with existing methods from operations research and medical literature.  
- Overall contribution is significant and original, with strengths outweighing weaknesses pending revisions.
