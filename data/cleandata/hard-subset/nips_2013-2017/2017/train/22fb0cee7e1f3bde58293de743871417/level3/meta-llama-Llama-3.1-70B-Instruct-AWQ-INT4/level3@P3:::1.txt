This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a novel methodology for constructing personalized screening policies that balance the cost of screening against the cost of delay in detection of disease. The authors incorporate both patient screening history and disease-relevant patient features for personalization, resulting in efficiency gains over existing screening policies without delaying disease detection.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, with a clear and concise mathematical exposition. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, including a discussion of the challenges in developing personalized screening policies. They also provide a detailed description of their proposed approach, including the use of Bellman equations and point-based value iteration.

The authors validate their approach using a large real-world dataset of 45,000 patients from Athena Health, demonstrating improvements attributable to personalization. They also validate their approach across multiple disease model types, including breast cancer and pancreatic cancer.

The paper makes a clear technical contribution with potential practical impact. The authors' approach has the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of screening policies, leading to better health outcomes and reduced healthcare costs.
