This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for a broad class of diseases. In contrast to prior work, DPSCREEN incorporates both a patient's clinical (screening) history and features specific to the disease model. This enables the creation of personalized screening schedules, which leads to significant efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, this is a well-written and technically sound paper. The approach is well-motivated, thoroughly situated within the context of related work, and supported by a concise yet thorough discussion of its mathematical foundations, including computational complexity. The authors validate DPSCREEN on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, convincingly demonstrating the improvements afforded by personalization. The model's robustness is further confirmed through validation across different types of disease models. In conclusion, this paper makes a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical impact.
