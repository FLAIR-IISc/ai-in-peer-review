This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for identifying optimal screening policies applicable to a wide range of diseases. A key distinction from prior work is that DPSCREEN incorporates a patient's complete clinical screening history in conjunction with disease-specific patient features. This integration facilitates a personalized screening approach, which demonstrably improves efficiency over existing strategies without sacrificing the timeliness of disease detection.

In my assessment, the paper is of high quality, being both well-articulated and methodologically robust. The authors provide a strong motivation for their approach, situate it effectively within the context of related literature, and offer a clear and thorough treatment of the algorithm's mathematical foundations and computational complexity. DPSCREEN's performance is validated on a large-scale dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, which convincingly demonstrates the advantages of personalization. The model's robustness is further confirmed through validation across various disease model types. Consequently, this paper makes a notable technical contribution with significant potential for real-world clinical impact.
