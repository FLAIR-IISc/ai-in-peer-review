- Proposes a method to predict optimal personalized breast cancer screening times to minimize screening and delay costs.  
- Method incorporates individual medical history and external information for tailored recommendations.  
- Paper is generally well-written and shows significant improvements over existing methods.  
- Personalized screening approach is a notable strength versus one-size-fits-all strategies.  
- Use of ML/statistical modeling enables analysis of complex patterns in the data.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with comprehensive comparisons to baselines.  
- Results show significant reductions in screening costs and delay costs.  
- Sensitivity analysis of model parameters is detailed and adds rigor.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of clinical implications and how to integrate into existing protocols.  
- External information sources and how they’re used are insufficiently specified.  
- Could benefit from additional visualizations or data summaries to aid interpretation.  
- Writing could be improved for flow and readability; some paragraphs are overly long.
