This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for large classes of diseases. Unlike other work in this area, DPSCREEN takes into account both the patient's clinical (screening) history and patient features pertinent to the specific disease model, thereby enabling personalization in the screening process. This personalization leads to efficiency gains over existing screening policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors consider pertinent related work and provide a concise yet thorough discussion of the mathematical basis of the algorithm, including its computational complexity. The authors validate DPSCREEN on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the improvements provided by personalization. Furthermore, the model is validated across various disease model types, making the results more robust. The outcome is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with the potential for practical impact.
