- Introduces DPSCREEN, a method optimizing screening policies across many diseases by integrating clinical screening history and disease-specific individual features.  
- DPSCREEN enables personalized screening recommendations, improving efficiency without sacrificing timely disease detection.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear motivation and appropriate placement in related literature.  
- Provides a concise mathematical framework and useful discussion of computational complexity.  
- Validated on a large dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network, demonstrating robust benefits of personalization.  
- Performance validated across diverse disease models, indicating potential generalizability.  
- Incorporation of patient history alongside risk factors addresses over-screening of low-risk and under-screening of high-risk individuals.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear, facilitating understanding and potential real-world adoption.  
- Discussion of computational complexity is practical for deployment considerations.  
- Minor clarification requested: discuss limitations and potential biases in the Athena Health Network dataset and impact on generalizability.  
- Minor suggestion: quantify cost savings (e.g., reduced healthcare expenditure, decreased patient burden) from efficiency improvements.  
- Minor suggestion: investigate sensitivity to accuracy of underlying disease models and how model errors propagate through the screening policy.  
- Overall assessment: significant technical contribution with practical impact; recommendation for acceptance.
