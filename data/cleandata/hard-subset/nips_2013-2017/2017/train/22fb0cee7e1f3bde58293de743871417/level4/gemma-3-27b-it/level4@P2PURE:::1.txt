This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for identifying optimal screening policies across a broad range of diseases. In contrast to existing approaches, DPSCREEN incorporates both a patient’s clinical history—specifically, their screening record—and individual features relevant to the disease being modeled. This allows for personalized screening strategies, resulting in efficiency gains without compromising the speed of disease detection. 

Overall, I found this to be a well-written and technically rigorous paper. The approach is well-justified, appropriately contextualized within related work, and includes a concise yet comprehensive discussion of the algorithm’s mathematical foundations, including its computational complexity. The authors validate DPScreen using a large dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network, which effectively demonstrates the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model’s performance is evaluated across diverse disease models. The result is a paper that presents a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical application.
