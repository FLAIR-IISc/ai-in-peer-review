

This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for optimizing screening policies across large-scale disease screening scenarios. Unlike prior work, which typically considers only static patient features, DPSCREEN integrates both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and disease-specific patient characteristics. This dual consideration enables personalized screening strategies that improve efficiency without compromising detection timeliness.  

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, thoroughly contextualized within relevant literature, and includes a concise yet comprehensive mathematical foundation, including computational complexity analysis. Validation on a 45,000-patient dataset from the Athena Health Network robustly demonstrates the benefits of personalization. The method is further validated across multiple disease models, confirming its broad applicability.  

Overall, DPSCREEN offers a clear technical contribution with strong potential for real-world impact. The work effectively bridges theoretical rigor with practical screening optimization, addressing a meaningful gap in the literature. The evaluation is thorough, and the results convincingly support the claimed advantages over non-personalized approaches. The paper’s clarity and completeness make it a valuable addition to the field.
