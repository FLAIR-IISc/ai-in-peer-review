This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a novel method for optimizing screening policies across a wide range of diseases. A key strength of DPSCREEN is its incorporation of both a patient’s clinical history – specifically, prior screening results – and individual patient characteristics relevant to the disease being modeled. This allows for personalized screening recommendations, leading to efficiency gains over existing, non-personalized policies without compromising the speed of disease detection.

I found this paper to be technically strong and clearly presented. The motivation for the work is well-articulated, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of related research. The mathematical foundations of the algorithm are concisely explained, with a helpful discussion of its computational complexity. Importantly, the method is empirically validated using a substantial dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network, convincingly demonstrating the benefits of personalization. Further strengthening the evaluation, the model’s performance is assessed across different disease models, showcasing its generalizability. 

In summary, this paper presents a clear and valuable technical contribution with significant potential for real-world impact in healthcare.
