- Proposes DPSCREEN, a novel methodology for personalized screening balancing screening cost against delay cost in disease detection.  
- Models disease as an arbitrary finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state.  
- Incorporates external information (e.g., self-exam, comorbidities) that can trigger earlier clinician visits.  
- Evaluated on a large breast cancer dataset and compared to existing clinical policies.  
- Well-motivated problem with clear overview of related work and differentiation from prior methods.  
- Technical contributions are significant and the method is well-described.  
- Method handles wide range of disease models, including non-Markovian and non-stationary processes.  
- Provides robustness analysis showing insensitivity to estimation errors.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the approach and evaluation.  
- Lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in the field.  
- Could provide a more thorough discussion of limitations and directions for future research.  
- Originality: novel combination of decision theory and machine learning and use of finite-state stochastic disease modeling.  
- Recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Reviewer scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
