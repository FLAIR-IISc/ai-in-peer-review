- Paper aims to compute optimal patient-screening policies by modeling disease progression as a known finite-state stochastic process to minimize screening cost and delays.
- Authors propose a computationally efficient approximate solution.
- Evaluated on simulated breast cancer data, showing reduced delays at comparable cost versus an annual-screening baseline.
- Personalized screening is an important and timely research direction.
- The proposed algorithm is reasonable and technically well developed.
- Empirical results are promising and the paper is generally well written.
- Major limitation: assumes the patient’s disease model is known, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Methodological novelty is limited.
- Experimental evaluation is narrow (only compares to annual screening) and lacks robustness analysis to model misspecification.
- Overall: reasonable algorithm on an important topic, but limited novelty and practical relevance due to strong assumptions and missing robustness evaluations.
