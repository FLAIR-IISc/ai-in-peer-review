- Introduces DPSCREEN, a general framework for dynamic, personalized screening policies balancing screening cost and delayed detection cost.  
- Models disease progression as a general finite-state stochastic process, accommodating dynamic survival and non-Markovian models.  
- Incorporates an external information process (e.g., self-exams) that can trigger unscheduled clinical visits, reflecting real-world practice.  
- Frames the problem via Bellman equations but notes exact solution is intractable.  
- Proposes an approximate, tractable policy using a Point-Based Value Iteration (PBVI) adapted to the high-dimensional belief space by sampling reachable beliefs.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: Proposition 1 gives a performance bound and Theorem 1 shows robustness to small model estimation errors.  
- Validates the framework on a large real-world breast cancer dataset of 45,000 patients.  
- Empirical results show learned policies reduce screenings by 28–68% versus annual guidelines while maintaining the same expected detection delay.  
- Strength: high significance and originality due to generality and explicit modeling of unscheduled arrivals, with strong practical impact potential.  
- Strength: technically rigorous formulation and principled approximation method supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: thorough empirical validation demonstrating substantial efficiency gains from personalization.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and intuitive illustrations of personalization over time.  
- Weakness: main experiment uses a relatively simple two-state (Healthy/Diseased) Markov model, limiting demonstration of claimed generality.  
- Weakness: reliance on some externally sourced parameters (tumor growth, sensitivity) without sensitivity analysis to assess robustness.  
- Weakness: several tractability assumptions (e.g., conditional independence of test outcomes) are made without discussion of impacts if relaxed.  
- Conclusion: high-quality, impactful paper with strong theoretical and empirical support; recommend acceptance.
