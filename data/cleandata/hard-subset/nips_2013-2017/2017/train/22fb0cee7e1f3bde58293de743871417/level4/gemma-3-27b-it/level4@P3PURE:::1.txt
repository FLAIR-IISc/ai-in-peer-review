This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies across a large class of diseases. A key advantage of DPSCREEN over existing approaches is its incorporation of both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and patient features relevant to the specific disease model. This allows for personalized screening policies, resulting in efficiency gains without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, appropriately contextualized within related work, and provides a concise yet thorough discussion of the algorithm’s mathematical foundations, including its computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network, which convincingly demonstrates the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model’s performance is validated across different disease models. The result is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with substantial potential for practical impact.
