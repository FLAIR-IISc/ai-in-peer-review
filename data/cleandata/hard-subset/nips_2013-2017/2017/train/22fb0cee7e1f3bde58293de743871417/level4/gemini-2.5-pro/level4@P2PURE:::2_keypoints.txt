- Tackles personalized patient screening using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening cost and diagnostic delay.
- Proposes a computationally efficient algorithm to compute an approximate optimal screening policy.
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced diagnostic delays at comparable cost to annual screening.
- Problem choice is timely and important.
- Algorithmic approach is sound and well-developed.
- Empirical results are promising and the paper is clearly written.
- Major weakness: assumes patient-specific disease model is known a priori, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Limited methodological novelty relative to prior work.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient—only compared to an annual baseline and does not test robustness to model inaccuracies.
- Needs analysis of policy sensitivity to model uncertainty or mechanisms to infer patient models to demonstrate practical relevance.
