- Introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for personalized screening that balances screening cost against delay in disease detection.  
- Models disease as a general finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state and allows external information (e.g., self-exam) to trigger clinician visits.  
- Well-motivated and situates work within pertinent related literature.  
- Provides clear, concise mathematical exposition including computational complexity and approximation bounds.  
- Develops a computationally effective procedure to compute approximately optimal policies with provable error bounds.  
- Validated on a large breast cancer dataset, showing personalization improves outcomes.  
- Demonstrates large reductions in number of screens while maintaining the same expected detection delays, outperforming existing clinical policies.  
- Validated across different disease model types, highlighting the importance of cost choices.  
- Technically sound with clear potential for practical impact.  
- Weaknesses: assumes model parameters are known and lacks detailed robustness analysis to parameter/estimation errors.
