- Topic is highly relevant to personalized healthcare screening optimization.  
- Algorithm is logically developed, well-motivated, and computationally efficient.  
- Simulated results show reduced delays without increased costs compared to annual screening.  
- Paper is clearly written and technically sound.  
- Major unrealistic assumption: method requires the disease model to be known a priori, which is rare in practice.  
- Limited methodological novelty; builds on established stochastic control with incremental contribution.  
- Experimental validation is inadequate: compares only to annual screening and omits adaptive or learned-policy baselines.  
- No robustness analysis to model misspecification; fails to assess performance under model uncertainty.  
- Practical impact undermined by narrow focus on known-model scenario, making the work more academic than clinically actionable.  
- Necessary improvements: demonstrate model learning from data, validate against realistic baselines that account for model learning, and provide empirical robustness tests to model inaccuracies.
