This paper presents DPSCREEN, a novel method for determining optimal screening policies for a wide range of diseases. A key distinguishing feature of DPSCREEN is its consideration of both the patient's clinical history and disease-specific patient features, setting it apart from existing work in the field. By incorporating these factors, DPSCREEN achieves a personalized approach to screening, leading to improved efficiency without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, engage with relevant related work, and offer a concise yet thorough discussion of the algorithm's mathematical underpinnings, including its computational complexity. The validation of DPSCREEN on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network effectively demonstrates the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model's performance is validated across various disease models, underscoring its versatility. The result is a paper that makes a significant technical contribution with substantial potential for practical impact.
