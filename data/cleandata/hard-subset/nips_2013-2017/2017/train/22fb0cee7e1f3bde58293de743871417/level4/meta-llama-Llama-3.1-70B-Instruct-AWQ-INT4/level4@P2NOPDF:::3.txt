The paper proposes a novel method for predicting the optimal screening time for breast cancer patients, with the goal of minimizing both screening costs and delay costs. This personalized approach takes into account the individual's medical history, as well as external information, to provide tailored recommendations. The paper is well-written, and the proposed method demonstrates significant improvement over existing approaches.

The authors present a clear and concise introduction to the problem, highlighting the importance of balancing screening costs with the need for early detection. The proposed method is thoroughly explained, and the use of relevant mathematical notation and algorithms is appropriate. The experimental evaluation is also well-designed, with a suitable comparison to existing methods.

Overall, the paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The proposed method is innovative and has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of breast cancer screening. However, there are a few areas that could be improved upon. 

Firstly, while the paper provides a good overview of the proposed method, some of the technical details could be expanded upon. For example, the authors could provide more information on how the personal medical history and external information are incorporated into the model. 

Secondly, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more baseline methods for comparison. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance and its advantages over existing approaches.

Thirdly, the authors could provide more discussion on the practical implications of the proposed method. For example, how could the method be integrated into existing healthcare systems, and what are the potential challenges and limitations of implementing such a system?

In conclusion, the paper presents a promising approach to predicting optimal screening times for breast cancer patients. With some minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
