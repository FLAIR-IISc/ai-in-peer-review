Paper aims to find the optimal patient screening policy using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays.  
Authors propose a computationally efficient approximate solution and validate it on simulated breast cancer data.  
Results indicate reduced screening delays while maintaining equivalent costs compared to an annual-screening baseline.  
Personalized screening is a significant and important research area.  
The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, thoroughly developed, and the paper is clearly written.  
Methodological novelty is limited.  
Key limitation: the approach unrealistically assumes the patient-specific disease model is known rather than inferred from data.  
Experiments only compare to annual screening and fail to assess robustness to model misspecification or parameter uncertainty.  
Recommendation: strengthen practical relevance by addressing model uncertainty and demonstrating broader applicability beyond the idealized setting.
