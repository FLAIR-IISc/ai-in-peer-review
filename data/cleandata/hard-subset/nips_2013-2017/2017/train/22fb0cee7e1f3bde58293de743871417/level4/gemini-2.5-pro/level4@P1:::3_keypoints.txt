- Summary: DPSCREEN is a general framework for dynamic personalized screening that models disease as a finite-state stochastic process with an external information process and optimizes screening vs. delayed-detection costs via a belief-space dynamic program approximated by PBVI, demonstrating 28–68% fewer screenings on a breast-cancer dataset.  
- Strength — Originality & Generality: Handles non-Markovian disease trajectories and non-stationary tests, adding a novel external-information process for patient-initiated visits.  
- Strength — Technical Quality: Rigorous trajectory-belief formulation, PBVI approximation, and theoretical guarantees including approximation error bound and robustness analysis.  
- Strength — Significance & Impact: Empirical results suggest large real-world impact (substantial screening reductions for equivalent detection delay) and personalization to static/dynamic patient features.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, logically structured paper with effective illustrations (e.g., Figure 1) that clarify dynamic personalization.  
- Weakness — Validation Scope: Experiments use a two-state Markov model, not showcasing the framework’s advantage for more complex non-Markovian trajectories.  
- Weakness — Scalability & Cost: Key computational details (horizon T, sampled trajectories K, belief points B, wall-clock times) are missing, raising concerns about practical feasibility for larger models.  
- Weakness — Belief-Sampling Sensitivity: PBVI performance depends on sampled beliefs; no sensitivity analysis on B and K to assess robustness or guide users.  
- Weakness — Experimental Assumptions: Results rely on prior-model estimates (tumor growth, self-exam sensitivity); lack of sensitivity analysis to model misspecification limits understanding of real-world robustness.  
- Overall Assessment: Strong, original, and technically sound contribution with compelling empirical gains; main limitations are experimental scope and missing computational/sensitivity details, but the paper merits acceptance.
