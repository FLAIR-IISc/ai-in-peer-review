- Enables personalized, trajectory-adaptive screening strategies versus prior static-attribute or generic-rule approaches.  
- Achieves significant efficiency gains by reducing unnecessary screenings while preserving timely disease detection.  
- Maintains clinical timeliness without compromising detection performance.  
- Provides rigorous technical development with clear mathematical formulation and computational complexity analysis.  
- Effectively situates the work within existing literature and addresses key limitations of prior methods.  
- Validated on a large 45,000-patient Athena Health dataset, demonstrating robust performance gains.  
- Shows cross-disease generalizability with consistent improvements across multiple disease types.  
- Well-structured paper with concise, comprehensive presentation of methods, experiments, and results.  
- Empirical evaluation convincingly supports that longitudinal history plus disease features improve screening efficiency.
