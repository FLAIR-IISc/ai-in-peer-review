- Personalized screening is an important and impactful research area.  
- The proposed algorithm is reasonable, well-developed, and clearly presented.  
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced delays with comparable costs to annual screening.  
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- The method assumes known per-patient disease models, which is rarely realistic and shifts the main challenge to model inference.  
- Methodological novelty is relatively modest.  
- Experiments only compare to an annual screening baseline and lack stronger benchmarks.  
- The paper does not evaluate robustness or sensitivity of the policy to inaccuracies in the disease model.  
- Overall: interesting and reasonable approach but limited novelty and practical relevance; needs to address reliance on known models and demonstrate robustness to model errors.
