This manuscript introduces DPSCREEN, a novel approach to identifying optimal screening strategies for diverse disease categories. Notably, DPSCREEN differentiates itself from existing methods by incorporating both the patient's clinical screening history and disease-specific characteristics, thereby facilitating personalized screening processes. This tailored approach enables DPSCREEN to enhance the efficiency of screening policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, the paper is well-crafted and technically robust. The authors provide a clear motivation for their work, engage with relevant literature, and deliver a succinct yet comprehensive explanation of the algorithm's mathematical underpinnings, including its computational complexity. The validation of DPSCREEN on a substantial dataset (comprising 45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network effectively demonstrates the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model's performance is consistently strong across various disease models, underscoring its versatility. Ultimately, this paper makes a significant technical contribution with substantial potential for practical applications.
