

This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies across broad classes of diseases. Unlike prior approaches, DPSCREEN incorporates both the patient's clinical screening history and disease-specific patient characteristics, enabling personalized screening strategies. This personalization achieves greater efficiency compared to existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.  

The paper is well-written and technically robust. The approach is strongly motivated, thoroughly contextualizes relevant literature, and provides a concise yet comprehensive exposition of the algorithm's mathematical foundation, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a substantial 45,000-patient dataset from the Athena Health Network, adequately demonstrating the efficiency gains from personalization. Cross-validation across multiple disease models further strengthens the findings. Overall, this work presents a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical implementation.
