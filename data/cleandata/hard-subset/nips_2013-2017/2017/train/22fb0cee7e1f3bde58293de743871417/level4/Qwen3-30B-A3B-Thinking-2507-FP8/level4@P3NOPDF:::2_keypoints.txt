- Relevant and clearly formulated problem: personalized screening under a finite-state stochastic disease model.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate algorithm with logical, well-structured methodology.  
- Evaluation on simulated breast cancer data shows reduced delays while maintaining comparable costs to annual screening.  
- Writing is concise and accessible, improving readability.  
- Major concern: assumes perfect knowledge of each patient’s disease model, an unrealistic practice assumption.  
- Core missing element: does not address the practical challenge of inferring disease models from sparse/noisy clinical data.  
- Methodological novelty is limited and largely builds on established MDP techniques.  
- Baseline comparison (annual screening) is insufficient and not representative of clinical practice.  
- Lacks robustness analysis to model misspecification or uncertainty, undermining practical relevance.  
- Required improvements: incorporate model inference or joint learning-optimization, use meaningful clinical baselines, and test sensitivity to model errors.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but theoretical and lacking real-world grounding; recommendation — Weak Reject.
