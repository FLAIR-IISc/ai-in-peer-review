This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for deriving optimal screening policies across a broad class of disease models. In contrast to prior work, DPSCREEN conditions recommendations on both patient-specific features and the evolving clinical (screening/test) history. This enables genuine personalization of screening schedules, delivering efficiency gains over existing policies while maintaining the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found the manuscript clear and technically robust. The approach is well-justified, engages appropriately with related literature, and presents a concise yet thorough exposition of the algorithmic underpinnings, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is evaluated on a sizable dataset (~45k patients) from the Athena Health Network, convincingly demonstrating the benefits of personalization. The framework is also examined across different disease model types. The result is a clear technical contribution with credible potential for practical impact.
