- Aim: model disease as a finite-state stochastic process to derive an optimal screening policy that minimizes screening costs and delays, with a computationally efficient approximate solution.
- Results: Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced delays while maintaining similar costs compared to an annual-screening baseline.
