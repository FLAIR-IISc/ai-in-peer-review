This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a novel methodology for establishing effective screening strategies across a wide range of diseases. A key strength of DPSCREEN lies in its incorporation of both individual patient clinical histories and disease-specific patient characteristics, differentiating it from current approaches. This allows for personalized screening recommendations, resulting in improved efficiency compared to existing policies while maintaining prompt disease detection. 

I found this to be a strong, technically rigorous manuscript. The work is well-justified, appropriately contextualized within the existing literature, and presents a clear and concise explanation of the underlying mathematical framework, including a discussion of computational cost. The authors validate DPSCREEN using a substantial dataset (n=45,000) from the Athena Health Network, convincingly demonstrating the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model’s performance is evaluated across diverse disease models, highlighting its generalizability. Ultimately, this paper delivers a significant technical advancement with clear potential for real-world application.
