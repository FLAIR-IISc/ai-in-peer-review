This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for a wide range of diseases. A key distinction from prior work is that DPSCREEN incorporates both a patient's clinical history and individual features pertinent to the specific disease model. This approach enables personalized screening, which yields significant efficiency gains over existing policies without negatively impacting the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The proposed method is well-motivated, effectively contextualized with respect to related work, and supported by a concise yet thorough discussion of its mathematical foundations, including an analysis of computational complexity. The authors validate DPSCREEN on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the improvements afforded by personalization. Furthermore, the model's robustness is demonstrated across different types of disease models. The result is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical impact.
