- Addresses personalized patient screening by modeling disease progression as a finite-state stochastic process to minimize screening costs and delays.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate algorithm and demonstrates delay reduction with comparable costs to annual screening in breast cancer simulations.  
- Topic is important and relevant to personalized medicine.  
- The algorithm is well-developed, clearly presented, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Results are promising in simulation under the paper’s assumptions.  
- Major unrealistic assumption: the disease model is assumed known a priori, which is unlikely in real clinical settings.  
- The core practical challenge—inferring patient-specific disease models from data—is not addressed.  
- Methodological novelty is limited.  
- Experimental comparison is weak, only using an annual screening baseline.  
- Lacks evaluation of robustness/sensitivity to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model; such experiments are crucial.  
- To strengthen the contribution, the authors should demonstrate practical relevance by addressing model inference and robustness to model uncertainty.
