- Proposes DPSCREEN, a novel method for personalized screening that balances screening cost against delay cost using a finite-state stochastic disease model with an absorbing disease state.
- Incorporates external information that can trigger early patient arrival to clinicians.
- Develops a computationally tractable construction for an approximately optimal personalized screening policy, improving over existing methods.
- Uses Bayesian updating and point-based value iteration (PBVI) in a clear, well-explained algorithmic approach.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large breast cancer data set, reducing number of screenings while maintaining equal detection delay.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to a broad audience with appropriate use of technical notation.
- Strong balance of technical rigor and clinical/practical relevance; real-world evaluation increases credibility and impact.
- Weakness: assumes model parameters are known; lacks thorough analysis of parameter estimation error from observational data.
- Weakness: needs a more detailed and comprehensive comparison with existing screening approaches.
- Recommendation: significant contribution with minor revisions to address limitations and comparisons; overall positive assessment (rating 8/10, confidence 9/10).
