- Addresses optimal patient screening using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate solution.  
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced delays with comparable costs to annual screening.  
- Personalized screening is an important and valuable research direction.  
- The algorithm is well-reasoned, well-developed, and clearly written.  
- Methodological novelty is limited.  
- Assumes known, patient-specific disease models—a largely unrealistic premise for practical application.  
- Experimental comparison is limited to a fixed annual screening baseline.  
- Lacks evaluation of robustness to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model.  
- Overall: interesting and sound but incremental; needs work to demonstrate practical relevance and address model uncertainty.
