

The paper proposes a method for determining optimal breast cancer screening intervals that minimizes both screening costs and the costs associated with delayed detection. This personalized approach incorporates each patient's medical history alongside external clinical data. The paper is well-written and clearly structured, with a compelling technical contribution. The proposed method demonstrates substantial improvements over existing baselines, achieving consistent reductions in total costs across multiple evaluation scenarios. While the experimental results are promising, the authors should clarify how the method handles missing medical history data—a common real-world challenge. Additionally, expanding the sensitivity analysis to cover a broader range of cost parameters would further strengthen the practical applicability of the findings.
