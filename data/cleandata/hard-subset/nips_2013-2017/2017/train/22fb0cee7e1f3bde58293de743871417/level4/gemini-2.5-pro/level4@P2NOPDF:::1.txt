This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for a wide range of diseases. A key contribution of this work is its ability to integrate a patient's clinical and screening history with patient features relevant to the specific disease model. This approach enables the creation of personalized screening schedules, which yield significant efficiency gains over existing, more generalized policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors clearly motivate the need for personalized screening and effectively situate their approach within the context of related literature. The mathematical foundations of the DPSCREEN algorithm are presented with rigor and clarity, including a valuable discussion of its computational complexity.

A primary strength of the paper is its empirical validation. Using a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, the authors convincingly demonstrate the improvements achieved through personalization. The robustness of the approach is further strengthened by its validation across different types of disease models. In summary, this work makes a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical, real-world impact in clinical decision-making.
