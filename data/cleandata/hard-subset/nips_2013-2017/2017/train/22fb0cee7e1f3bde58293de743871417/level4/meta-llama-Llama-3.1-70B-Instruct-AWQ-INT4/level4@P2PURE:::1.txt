This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for large classes of diseases. Unlike existing approaches in this area, DPSCREEN incorporates both the patient's clinical (screening) history and patient-specific features relevant to the disease model, thereby enabling personalized screening. This personalization leads to efficiency gains over current screening policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found the paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, effectively considers pertinent related work, and provides a clear and comprehensive discussion of the algorithm's mathematical basis, including its computational complexity. The authors validate DPSCREEN using a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, demonstrating the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model's performance is evaluated across various disease model types, resulting in a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with potential practical impact.
