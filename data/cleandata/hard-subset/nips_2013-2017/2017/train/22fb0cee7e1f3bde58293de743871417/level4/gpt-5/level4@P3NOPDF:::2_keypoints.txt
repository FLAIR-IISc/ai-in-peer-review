- Paper studies optimal patient-specific screening policies using finite-state stochastic disease models to trade off screening costs and diagnostic delays.
- Proposed a computationally efficient approximate solution method and evaluated it on simulated breast cancer data, showing reduced delays at comparable costs versus annual screening.
- Strength: Personalized screening is timely and impactful.
- Strength: Sensible formulation with a technically sound, well-developed algorithm.
- Strength: Empirical results show meaningful improvements over a simple baseline.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Weakness: Assumes patient-specific disease model is known, which is unrealistic—real settings require learning under limited, noisy, biased data.
- Weakness: Methodological novelty is incremental—largely an application of known stochastic control/POMDP approximations.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited, comparing only to an annual baseline and lacking robustness checks to model misspecification.
- Weakness: Unclear performance relative to practical risk-stratified heuristics and stronger algorithmic baselines (e.g., exact DP, myopic, rollout).
- Suggestion: Incorporate and evaluate robustness to model misspecification via sensitivity analysis, distributional robustness, Bayesian averaging, or off-policy evaluation.
- Suggestion: Provide a pathway for learning patient-level parameters from data (two-stage or joint approaches), assess sample complexity, and quantify uncertainty/calibration.
- Suggestion: Expand baselines and ablations to include practical risk-stratified policies, optimal small-instance solutions, myopic/rollout baselines, and component-wise ablations.
- Suggestion: Improve external validity with semi-synthetic or real-world seeded data and report clinically relevant metrics (stage at detection, false positives, radiation exposure).
- Suggestion: Discuss practical integration with clinical workflows, constraints on screening frequency, adherence, guideline compliance, and fairness across subgroups.
- Suggestion: Enhance clarity and reproducibility with pseudocode, complexity/guarantee analysis, convergence/approximation evidence, and released code/parameters.
- Overall assessment: Promising and computationally efficient but limited by the known-model assumption, lack of robustness and stronger baselines, and modest novelty; needs work to demonstrate real-world applicability.
