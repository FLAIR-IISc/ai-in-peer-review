- Addresses personalized patient screening using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays.
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate algorithm and validates it on simulated breast cancer data.
- Experiments show reduced screening delays with equivalent costs compared to annual screening.
- Topic of personalized screening is important and timely.
- Algorithm is reasonable, well-developed, and rigorously implemented.
- Results are promising and the paper is clearly and effectively written.
- Assumes the per-patient disease model is known, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Main practical challenge—inferring patient disease models from observational data—is not addressed.
- Methodological novelty is limited.
- Experimental comparison is restricted to an annual-screening baseline.
- Lacks analysis of robustness/sensitivity to inaccuracies in disease-model parameters.
- Limited practical relevance unless the authors strengthen justification and evaluate model-uncertainty effects.
