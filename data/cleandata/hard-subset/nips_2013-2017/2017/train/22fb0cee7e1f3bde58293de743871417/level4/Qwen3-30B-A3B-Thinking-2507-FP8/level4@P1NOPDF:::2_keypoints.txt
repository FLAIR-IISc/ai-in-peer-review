- Topic of personalized screening is highly relevant and impactful.  
- Proposed algorithm is computationally efficient, well-constructed, and yields promising results.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Unrealistic assumption that each individual's disease model is known a priori.  
- Fails to address the central practical problem of inferring disease models from real patient data.  
- Treating the model as fixed/known makes the contribution largely academic rather than clinically actionable.  
- Methodological novelty is modest.  
- Experimental comparison is limited—only evaluates against a simplistic annual screening baseline.  
- No analysis of robustness to model inaccuracies or sensitivity to parameter misestimation (e.g., 10–20%).  
- Overall: needs explicit treatment of model inference and robustness testing to justify real-world relevance.
