- Introduces DPSCREEN, a novel method for finding optimal screening policies across many diseases.
- Personalizes screening by using both patient screening history and disease-relevant patient features.
- Achieves efficiency gains over existing policies without degrading timeliness of disease detection.
- Well-written and technically sound presentation.
- Provides clear mathematical formulation and discusses computational complexity.
- Validated on a large real-world dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network.
- Demonstrates versatility by validating across multiple disease model types.
- Balances technical rigor with practical relevance and accessibility to a broad audience.
- Comparison to existing screening policies offers a useful benchmark.
- Weakness: discussion of methodological limitations and practical implementation challenges is limited.
- Weakness: needs deeper analysis of results, specifically finer exploration of efficiency gains and impact on disease detection.
- Overall: significant contribution with practical impact, suitable for publication.
