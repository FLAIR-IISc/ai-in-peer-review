This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies across a wide range of diseases. Unlike previous work in this area, DPSCREEN incorporates both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and individual features relevant to the specific disease model. This allows DPSCREEN to personalize the screening process, resulting in efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, thoroughly considers related work, and provides a concise yet comprehensive discussion of the algorithm’s mathematical foundations, including its computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network, which convincingly demonstrates the benefits of personalization. Furthermore, the model’s performance is validated across different disease models, strengthening the results. This paper makes a clear technical contribution with the potential for significant practical impact.
