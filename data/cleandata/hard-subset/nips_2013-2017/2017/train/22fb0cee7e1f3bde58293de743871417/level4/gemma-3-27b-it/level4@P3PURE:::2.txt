The objective of this paper is to determine an optimal policy for patient screening, leveraging available patient information. The authors model disease progression as a finite-state stochastic process and aim to minimize both screening costs and associated delays. They propose an approximate solution designed for computational efficiency. Experiments conducted on simulated data related to breast cancer demonstrate that the proposed algorithm can reduce delays while maintaining comparable costs to a simple annual screening baseline.

**Positives:**

*   Personalized screening is an important and impactful research area.
*   The proposed algorithm is reasonable, well-developed, and clearly presented.
*   The experimental results are promising.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Negatives:**

*   The proposed algorithm currently appears to be primarily a theoretical exercise. A key limitation is the assumption of known disease models for individual patients, an assumption rarely valid in real-world clinical settings. The primary challenge in personalized screening lies not in policy optimization *given* a known model, but rather in accurately inferring the disease model from observed data.
*   The methodological novelty is relatively modest.
*   The experiments only compare the proposed algorithm against a baseline of annual screening. Considering the significant assumption regarding disease model knowledge, a crucial aspect to investigate experimentally is the robustness of the proposed method to inaccuracies in the disease model. Specifically, how sensitive is the optimal policy to uncertainties and errors within the disease model itself?

**Overall:**

The topic is interesting, and the proposed algorithm is reasonable in its approach. However, the methodological novelty is limited, and the paper needs to more convincingly demonstrate its practical relevance. Addressing the concerns regarding the reliance on known disease models and exploring the method’s sensitivity to model inaccuracies would significantly strengthen the work.
