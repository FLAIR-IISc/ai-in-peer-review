- Proposes a personalized method to determine optimal breast cancer screening intervals minimizing screening and delayed-detection costs.  
- Incorporates individual patient medical history together with external clinical data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and presents a compelling technical contribution.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over existing baselines.  
- Achieves consistent reductions in total costs across multiple evaluation scenarios.  
- Lacks clarification on handling missing medical history data, a common real-world issue.  
- Sensitivity analysis should be expanded to cover a broader range of cost parameters to improve practical applicability.
