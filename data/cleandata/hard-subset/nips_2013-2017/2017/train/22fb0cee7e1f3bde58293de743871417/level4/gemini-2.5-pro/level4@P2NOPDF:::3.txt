This paper proposes a novel method for personalizing breast cancer screening schedules. The core objective is to optimize the trade-off between the costs of screening and the risks associated with a delayed diagnosis. To achieve this, the model leverages a patient's individual medical history in conjunction with external data sources to predict an optimal screening interval.

The manuscript is well-written and the proposed approach is clearly articulated. The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating a significant improvement over existing, less-personalized screening strategies. This work represents a promising step towards data-driven preventive medicine and highlights the potential clinical utility of personalized risk assessment.
