- Objective: Learn optimal patient screening policy using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays; propose a computationally efficient approximate solution.
- Experiments: On simulated breast cancer data, the algorithm reduces delays while maintaining the same cost compared to an annual-screening baseline.
- Positive: Personalized screening is an important research topic.
- Positive: The proposed algorithm is reasonable and well-developed.
- Positive: The results are promising.
- Positive: The paper is well-written.
- Negative: The method assumes the disease model for each patient is known, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Negative: Main contribution has limited methodological novelty.
- Negative: Experiments only compare to a trivial annual baseline.
- Negative: Lacks robustness/sensitivity analysis to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model.
