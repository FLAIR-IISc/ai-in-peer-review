This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for optimizing screening policies across a wide range of diseases. Unlike prior approaches, DPSCREEN leverages both a patient’s screening history and disease-specific patient features. This enables personalized screening schedules that improve efficiency without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, the paper is well written and technically solid. The problem setting is clearly motivated, the related work is appropriately covered, and the algorithmic foundations—including computational complexity—are presented concisely yet thoroughly. The method is validated on a large dataset of approximately 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, convincingly demonstrating the benefits of personalization. The authors also evaluate the approach across multiple disease models, supporting its generality. In sum, the paper offers a clear technical contribution with strong potential for real-world impact.
