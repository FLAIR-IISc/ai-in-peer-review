

This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for diverse disease classes. Unlike prior work, DPSCREEN integrates both a patient's clinical (screening) history and disease-specific patient features, enabling personalized screening that achieves efficiency gains over existing approaches without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.  

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, thoroughly contextualizes relevant literature, and provides a concise yet comprehensive mathematical foundation for the algorithm, including computational complexity analysis. DPSCREEN is validated on a 45,000-patient dataset from the Athena Health Network, sufficiently demonstrating the efficacy of personalization. The model also generalizes across multiple disease models. Overall, the work presents a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical impact.
