- Proposes a novel personalized method to predict optimal breast cancer screening times that balances screening and delay costs.
- Incorporates individual medical history and external information to tailor screening recommendations.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly presented.
- Introduction clearly motivates the trade-off between screening costs and early detection.
- Method is thoroughly explained with appropriate mathematical notation and algorithms.
- Experimental evaluation shows significant improvement over existing approaches.
- Experiments are well-designed but could include more baseline methods for comparison.
- Technical details should be expanded on how personal history and external information are integrated into the model.
- Needs more discussion of practical implications, integration into healthcare systems, and implementation challenges.
- Overall, a promising and innovative approach that could make a significant contribution with minor revisions.
