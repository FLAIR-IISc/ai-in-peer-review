This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for optimizing screening policies across a wide range of diseases. Unlike prior work, DPSCREEN incorporates both a patient's clinical screening history and disease-specific patient features. This enables personalized screening, leading to efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

This paper is well-written, technically sound, and well-motivated. The authors effectively situate their work within the existing literature and provide a clear, concise discussion of the algorithm's mathematical underpinnings, including an analysis of its computational complexity. To validate their approach, the authors use a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network and also test the model across various disease types. These experiments successfully demonstrate the benefits of the proposed personalization. Consequently, the paper presents a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
