This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for a broad class of diseases. Compared to prior work, DPSCREEN accounts for both the patient’s clinical screening history and patient features pertinent to the specific disease model. As a result, it personalizes the screening process, yielding efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found the paper well written and technically sound. The approach is well motivated, engages appropriately with related work, and provides a concise yet thorough discussion of the algorithm’s mathematical foundations, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the benefits of personalization. The model is also validated across disease-model types. The result is a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
