This paper presents DPSCREEN, a novel method for determining optimal screening policies for large classes of diseases. Compared to existing work in the area, DPSCREEN takes into account both the patient's clinical (screening) history and patient features pertinent to the specific disease model. As a result, DPSCREEN achieves personalization in the screening process, yielding efficiency gains over existing screening policies without negatively impacting the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, considers pertinent related work, and provides a concise but thorough discussion of the mathematical basis of the algorithm, including computational complexity. The authors validate DPSCREEN on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, demonstrating the improvements provided by personalization. Furthermore, the model is validated across various disease model types, showcasing its versatility. The result is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with the potential for practical impact.

The strengths of the paper lie in its ability to balance technical rigor with practical relevance. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the algorithm, making it accessible to a broad audience. The use of a large and diverse dataset adds credibility to the results, and the comparison to existing screening policies provides a useful benchmark for evaluating the performance of DPSCREEN.

One potential area for improvement is the discussion of the limitations of the approach. While the authors mention the potential challenges of implementing DPSCREEN in practice, they could further explore the limitations of the method and potential avenues for future research. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including a deeper exploration of the efficiency gains and the impact on disease detection.

In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of disease screening, offering a personalized approach that has the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of screening policies. The technical soundness, practical relevance, and clarity of the presentation make this a strong paper that is well-suited for publication.
