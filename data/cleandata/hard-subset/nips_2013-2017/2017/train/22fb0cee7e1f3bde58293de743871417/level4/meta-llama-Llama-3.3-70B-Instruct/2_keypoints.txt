- Topic is important: personalized screening is a significant research area.  
- Proposed algorithm is logical and well-developed.  
- Results look promising: simulated breast cancer experiments show reduced delays without increased costs vs baseline.  
- Paper is well-written.  
- Major assumption: algorithm requires prior knowledge of a patient’s disease model, which is unlikely in practice.  
- Main practical challenge (inferring disease models from data) is not addressed.  
- Methodological innovation is limited.  
- Experiments only compare to an annual screening baseline and do not test robustness to disease-model inaccuracies or uncertainty.  
- Overall: reasonable algorithm on an intriguing topic but modest novelty and insufficient demonstration of practical relevance.
