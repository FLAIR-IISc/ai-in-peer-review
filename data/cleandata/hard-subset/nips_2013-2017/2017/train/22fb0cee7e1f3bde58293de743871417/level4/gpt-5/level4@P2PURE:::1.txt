This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for optimizing screening policies across a broad set of diseases. Unlike prior work, DPSCREEN conditions decisions on both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and disease-specific patient features. This enables personalized screening that delivers efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, the paper is well written and technically sound. The approach is well motivated, engages appropriately with related work, and provides a concise yet thorough mathematical treatment of the algorithm, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is evaluated on a large dataset (45k patients) from the Athena Health Network, which adequately demonstrates the benefits of personalization. The model is also validated across multiple disease model types. Altogether, the paper presents a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
