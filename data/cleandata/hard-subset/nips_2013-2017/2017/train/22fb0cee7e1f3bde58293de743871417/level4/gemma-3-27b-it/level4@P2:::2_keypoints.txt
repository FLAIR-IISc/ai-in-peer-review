- Addresses personalized screening to balance testing costs against risks of delayed disease detection.  
- Proposes a finite-state stochastic disease model and a computationally tractable algorithm for approximately optimal screening policies.  
- Algorithm accounts for patient-specific features and screening history.  
- Experiments on a breast cancer dataset show reduced screening frequency with comparable detection delays.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the algorithm.  
- Valuable contribution within a general stochastic framework, but methodological novelty is incremental.  
- Major limitation: strong assumption of known disease models; practical challenge is inferring models from data.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited—compares only to fixed annual screening baseline.  
- Lacks robustness/sensitivity analysis to model misspecification and uncertainty.  
- Insufficient discussion of how to integrate model estimation and practical implementation challenges.  
- Overall: solid technical work with practical limitations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address model estimation and sensitivity analysis.
