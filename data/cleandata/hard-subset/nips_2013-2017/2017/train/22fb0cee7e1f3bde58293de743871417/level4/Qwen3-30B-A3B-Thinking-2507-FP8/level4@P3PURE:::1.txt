

This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for large classes of diseases. Unlike prior work, DPSCREEN incorporates both the patient's clinical screening history and disease-specific patient features, enabling personalized screening that achieves efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising detection timeliness.  

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, thoroughly covers pertinent related work, and provides a concise yet thorough discussion of the algorithm's mathematical foundations, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large-scale (45,000-patient) dataset from the Athena Health Network, sufficiently demonstrating the improvements enabled by personalization. Additionally, validation across multiple disease model types strengthens the generality of the results. Overall, the paper makes a clear and meaningful technical contribution with demonstrable potential for practical impact.
