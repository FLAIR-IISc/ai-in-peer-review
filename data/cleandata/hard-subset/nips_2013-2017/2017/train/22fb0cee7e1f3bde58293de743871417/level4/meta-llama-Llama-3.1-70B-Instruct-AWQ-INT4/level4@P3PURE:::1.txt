This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies for large classes of diseases. Compared to other work in this area, DPSCREEN distinguishes itself by taking into account both the patient's clinical (screening) history and patient features pertinent to the specific disease model. As a result, DPSCREEN achieves personalization in the screening process, yielding efficiency gains over existing screening policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors consider pertinent related work while providing a concise yet thorough discussion of the mathematical basis of the algorithm, including its computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset of 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the improvements provided by personalization. Furthermore, the model is validated across various disease model types, underscoring its versatility. The result is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with the potential for practical impact.
