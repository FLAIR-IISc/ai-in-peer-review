This paper presents a method, DPSCREEN, for determining the optimal screening policy for major diseases. Compared to other works in this area, DPSCREEN considers both the patient's clinical (screening) history and the patient's characteristics relevant to the specific disease model. Consequently, DPSCREEN is able to achieve personalization in the screening process, which provides greater efficiency than existing screening policies without negatively affecting the time frame for disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The reasons behind this method are appropriate, relevant works are considered, and there is a concise but thorough discussion of the algorithm's mathematical basis and computational complexity. DPScreen was validated based on a large dataset (45,000 patients) obtained from the Athena Health network. It adequately demonstrates the improvements provided through personalization. The model was also validated for various disease models. Consequently, this paper makes a clear technical contribution that could have practical implications.
