- Goal: derive optimal patient screening policies in a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays.  
- Approach: propose a computationally efficient approximate solution.  
- Evaluation: simulated breast cancer data shows reduced delays with similar costs compared to annual screening.  

- Positive: Personalized screening is a timely and important research direction.  
- Positive: The proposed algorithm is logically sound and well-structured.  
- Positive: Results are promising for practical application.  
- Positive: Paper is clearly and professionally written.  

- Negative: Assumes the disease model for each patient is known, which is unrealistic and limits practical relevance.  
- Negative: Methodological novelty is limited.  
- Negative: Experiments only compare to annual screening and do not test robustness to model misspecification or uncertainty.  

- Overall: Interesting and reasonable work but lacks novelty and sufficient evidence for real-world applicability; needs stronger empirical validation and treatment of model inference/uncertainty.
