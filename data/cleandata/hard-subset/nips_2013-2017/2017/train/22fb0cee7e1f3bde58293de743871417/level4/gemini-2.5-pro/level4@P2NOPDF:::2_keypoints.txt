- Addresses an important practical problem: personalized screening policy design for individual patients.
- Proposes a technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly developed approximation algorithm.
- Simulation results (breast cancer) show reduced diagnostic delays versus annual screening without higher costs.
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation of problem, methods, and results.
- Major limitation: assumes a known patient-specific disease model a priori, ignoring the hard problem of model inference and uncertainty.
- Methodological novelty is limited—primarily an application of existing frameworks rather than a new technique.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: only compared to a single non-adaptive baseline and lacks robustness/sensitivity analysis to model misspecification.
- Overall: promising theoretical contribution but practical relevance is undermined by unrealistic assumptions and missing learning/robustness analyses; needs substantial additional work to be compelling.
