- Proposes DPSCREEN, a method for personalized screening balancing screening cost vs delay cost using finite-state stochastic disease models with an absorbing disease state.
- Incorporates external information (self-exams, comorbidities) that can trigger unscheduled clinician visits.
- Paper is well-written and the proposed algorithm is reasonable and well-developed.
- Personalized screening is an important and relevant research topic.
- Experimental results on simulated breast cancer data are promising, showing reduced delays at similar cost versus an annual baseline.
- Major limitation: method assumes the patient’s disease model is known, which is unrealistic in practice.
- Key practical challenge—inferring individual disease models from data—is not addressed.
- Methodological novelty is limited; core ideas are not substantially different from prior work.
- Experiments are limited: comparison only to annual screening baseline.
- Robustness to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model is not studied.
- Needs additional experiments and analysis to demonstrate practical relevance and robustness.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel personalized screening methodology, promising simulated results, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: impractical assumption of known models, limited novelty, insufficient experimental validation of robustness.
