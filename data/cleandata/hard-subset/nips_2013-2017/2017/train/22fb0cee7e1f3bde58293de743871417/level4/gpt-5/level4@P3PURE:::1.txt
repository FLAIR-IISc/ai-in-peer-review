This paper presents DPSCREEN, a method for determining optimal screening policies across large classes of diseases. Compared with prior work, DPSCREEN accounts for both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and patient features specific to the disease model. This enables personalized screening policies that improve efficiency without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

The paper is well written and technically sound. The approach is well motivated, positions itself appropriately with respect to related work, and provides a concise yet thorough mathematical treatment, including an analysis of computational complexity.

DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset (45k patients) from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the gains attributable to personalization. The model is also validated across different disease model types.

Overall, the paper makes a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
