- Tackles clinically relevant problem of personalized patient screening by modeling disease progression as a finite-state stochastic process and optimizing a policy to minimize screening costs and treatment delays.
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate algorithm that is technically sound and well-motivated.
- Experiments on a simulated breast cancer model show reduced treatment delays relative to an annual screening baseline while maintaining similar cost.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Major limitation: assumes patient-specific disease model is known a priori, an unrealistic and critical assumption in practice.
- Methodological novelty appears limited.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow—only compared to an annual baseline and lacks analysis of robustness to model misspecification.
- Overall: interesting problem and reasonable approach, but impact is reduced by limited novelty and the strong, unrealistic model assumptions; needs robustness analysis to be convincing.
