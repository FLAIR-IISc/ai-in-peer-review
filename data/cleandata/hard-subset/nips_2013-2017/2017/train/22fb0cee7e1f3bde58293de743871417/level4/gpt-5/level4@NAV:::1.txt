This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a method for identifying optimal screening policies across broad classes of diseases. Relative to prior work, DPSCREEN accounts for both a patient’s clinical (screening) history and patient-specific features relevant to the disease model. This enables personalized screening, yielding efficiency gains over existing policies without compromising the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found the paper clearly written and technically rigorous. The approach is well-motivated, engages with relevant related work, and provides a concise yet thorough treatment of the algorithm’s mathematical foundations, including computational complexity. DPSCREEN is validated on a large dataset (45k patients) from the Athena Health Network, which sufficiently demonstrates the benefits of personalization. The model is also validated across different disease model types. The result is a paper that offers a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
