- Proposal: Personalized recommendation of breast cancer screening intervals optimizing trade-off between screening costs and delayed-detection costs using patient history and external information; clear writing and promising empirical gains.
- Strength: Tackles an important, clinically and economically consequential problem with timely relevance.
- Strength: Conceptually sound to explicitly model trade-off between screening and delay costs aligned with health-economics decision-making.
- Strength: Personalization using individual histories and external covariates likely outperforms fixed-interval or one-size-fits-all rules.
- Strength: Empirical results show substantial improvements over baselines, suggesting practical utility.
- Strength: Exposition is generally clear with well-motivated problem statement and intuitive rationale.
- Weakness: Model specification and training objective are insufficiently detailed—unclear how time-to-event outcomes, censoring, and time-varying covariates are handled.
- Weakness: Cost modeling is unspecified—definitions, estimation, validation, patient-specificity, and sensitivity analysis over cost parameters are missing.
- Weakness: “External information” is undefined and needs concrete examples and an ablation to quantify its contribution.
- Weakness: Evaluation design unclear—retrospective vs counterfactual vs prospective simulation, handling of lead-time bias, and limited reported metrics beyond average cost.
- Weakness: Baselines are weak/incomplete—need comparisons to standard survival and dynamic prediction methods and simple risk-stratified heuristics.
- Weakness: Robustness and generalization not demonstrated—unclear performance under distribution shift, missing/sporadic covariates, and across demographic subgroups; no external validation.
- Weakness: Interpretability is lacking—need explanations for recommendations, feature attributions, and uncertainty quantification.
- Weakness: Practical deployment constraints (min/max intervals, recall policies, adherence, clinician overrides) are not addressed or encoded.
- Weakness: Statistical rigor needs improvement—report confidence intervals, tests vs baselines, and calibration assessments.
- Weakness: Ethical and fairness considerations are not analyzed—require subgroup performance, governance discussion, and potential unintended consequences.
- Question: How are delay costs operationalized, parameterized, and validated with clinical input?
- Question: How are right-censoring, competing risks, and incomplete follow-up handled?
- Question: Are covariates updated longitudinally and how is label leakage prevented when constructing features near events?
- Question: What safeguards prevent recommending overly long intervals for high-risk individuals?
- Question: Can the method provide uncertainty-aware recommendations or adjustable risk-tolerance levels?
- Question: What datasets and split strategies were used (site-level, temporal), and is there an external test set?
- Question: Will code and model checkpoints be released for reproducibility?
- Suggestion: Add a detailed problem formulation—decision space, objective, constraints, learning algorithm, and pseudocode.
- Suggestion: Include sensitivity analyses over cost parameters and visualize the cost–delay Pareto frontier.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations (without external info, varied history lengths, different feature subsets).
- Suggestion: Report comprehensive metrics including calibration, net benefit, time/stage at detection, and subgroup analyses.
- Suggestion: Compare against strong survival/dynamic models and simple scheduling heuristics.
- Suggestion: Demonstrate robustness to missing data and distribution shift and include external validation if possible.
- Suggestion: Add interpretability analyses and a clinician-oriented case study showing differences from current guidelines.
- Suggestion: Discuss deployment considerations—workflow integration, adherence, clinician oversight, and regulatory/ethical review.
- Clarity: Add a schematic figure of inputs, model, decision rule, and outcomes; move key implementation details from supplement to main text.
- Reproducibility: Specify datasets, inclusion/exclusion criteria, preprocessing, missing-data strategies, hyperparameter search, and release public code/minimal examples.
- Overall assessment: Relevant and promising contribution; leans toward acceptance conditional on addressing methodological detail, stronger baselines, robustness analyses, and clarity around costs and evaluation.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
