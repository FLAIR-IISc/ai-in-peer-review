This paper presents DPSCREEN, a novel method for determining optimal screening policies for a wide range of diseases. The key innovation of DPSCREEN is its integration of a patient's complete clinical history with disease-specific patient features. This holistic approach enables a high degree of personalization in the screening process, which, as the authors demonstrate, yields substantial efficiency gains over existing policies without negatively impacting the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, this is an exceptionally well-written and technically sound paper. The work possesses several significant strengths:

1.  **Motivation and Context:** The problem is clearly motivated and situated effectively within the context of related work. The authors make a compelling case for the need for personalized screening protocols.

2.  **Methodological Rigor:** The paper provides a concise yet thorough discussion of the mathematical basis for the DPSCREEN algorithm. The inclusion of a computational complexity analysis is a valuable addition that addresses the practical feasibility of the approach.

3.  **Empirical Validation:** The method is validated on a large and relevant real-world dataset, comprising 45,000 patients from the Athena Health Network. This extensive empirical evaluation sufficiently demonstrates the performance improvements afforded by personalization. Furthermore, the model's validation across different types of disease models strengthens the claims of its generalizability.

4.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the technical contributions accessible and easy to follow.

In conclusion, this is a strong submission that makes a clear technical contribution with significant potential for practical impact in computational healthcare. The methodology is sound, the evaluation is robust, and the results are compelling. The paper represents a valuable contribution to the field and is well-suited for the conference.
