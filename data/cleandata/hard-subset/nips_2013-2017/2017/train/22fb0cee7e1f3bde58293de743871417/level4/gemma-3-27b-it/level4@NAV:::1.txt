This paper introduces DPSCREEN, a novel method designed to identify effective screening strategies across a wide range of diseases. A key distinction from current approaches is that DPSCREEN incorporates not only individual patient characteristics relevant to the disease, but also their complete screening history. This allows for personalized screening recommendations, resulting in improved efficiency compared to standard policies, while maintaining appropriate disease detection timelines. 

The paper is generally well-written and demonstrates strong technical merit. The motivation is clear, related work is appropriately contextualized, and the mathematical foundation of the algorithm – including its computational complexity – is presented in a concise yet comprehensive manner. Validation using a substantial dataset (n=45,000) from the Athena Health Network convincingly illustrates the benefits of this personalization strategy. Furthermore, the model’s performance is robustly assessed across various disease models. Consequently, this work represents a significant technical advancement with promising potential for real-world application.
