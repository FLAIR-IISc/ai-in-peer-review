- Introduces DPSCREEN, a method to compute optimal personalized screening policies using clinical history and individual features.  
- Personalization yields efficiency gains without delaying disease detection.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-motivated.  
- Thorough mathematical exposition including computational complexity.  
- Validated on a large real-world dataset (45,000 patients from Athena Health).  
- Evaluated across multiple disease model types, demonstrating generalizability.  
- Effectively critiques existing methods that assume Markovian or stationary processes.  
- Proposes a flexible finite-state stochastic process framework applicable to many diseases.  
- Incorporates external information (e.g., self-examinations) to improve realism and policy effectiveness.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with approximation error bounds.  
- Needs more detail on the procedure for estimating model parameters from observational data.  
- Would benefit from sensitivity analysis showing dependence on parameter estimation accuracy.  
- The assumption of no false positives in test outcomes is potentially unrealistic and should be discussed.  
- Strong case for acceptance: technical soundness, novelty, practical impact, empirical validation, and generality.  
- No major negatives identified beyond requested clarifications on parameter estimation and false-positive handling.
