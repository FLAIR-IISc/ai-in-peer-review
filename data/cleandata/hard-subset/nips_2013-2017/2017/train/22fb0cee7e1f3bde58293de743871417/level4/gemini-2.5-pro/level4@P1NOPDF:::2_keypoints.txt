- Tackles personalized patient screening, an important and impactful problem.
- Proposes a well-motivated, reasonable, and clearly developed approximate algorithm.
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced diagnostic delays versus annual screening at similar cost.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Core unrealistic assumption: requires a known patient-specific disease model a priori, sidestepping the main challenge of inferring such models from data.
- Limited methodological novelty; techniques appear to be adaptations of existing methods.
- Experimental evaluation is narrow (single baseline) and lacks sensitivity analysis to model misspecification and robustness checks.
- Overall: interesting and promising but practicality and contribution are weakened by the strong modeling assumption and limited robustness/novelty evidence.
