- Introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for constructing screening policies across a broad family of disease models.
- Explicitly accounts for patient features and evolving clinical history (including past screening outcomes) to enable personalized screening schedules.
- Optimizes trade-off between testing frequency and delays in disease detection.
- Empirically achieves substantial efficiency gains over existing clinical policies without compromising timeliness.
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Clearly motivated and well-situated within literature on screening, decision theory, and POMDP approaches.
- Mathematical formulation is clear, including modeling assumptions, Bellman-equation solution strategy, and point-based value iteration for tractability.
- Discussion of computational complexity is concise and informative.
- Validated on a large real-world dataset (45,000 patients) from the Athena Health Network.
- Empirical results show personalization reduces number of screens while maintaining comparable expected detection delays.
- Evaluated across different disease model types, demonstrating method generality.
- Overall makes a clear technical contribution with strong potential for practical impact.
