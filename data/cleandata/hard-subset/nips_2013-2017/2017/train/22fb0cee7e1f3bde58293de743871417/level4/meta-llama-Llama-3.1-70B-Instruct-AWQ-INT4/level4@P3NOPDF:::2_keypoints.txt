- Topic is important: personalized screening is a worthwhile research area.  
- Algorithmic contribution: proposed algorithm is well-developed, reasonable, and computationally efficient.  
- Experimental result: simulated breast cancer experiments show reduced delays with similar costs versus the trivial baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Major limitation: method assumes the patient's disease model is known, which is unrealistic in practice.  
- Practical relevance concern: main challenge—inferring disease models from data—is not addressed.  
- Methodological novelty: contribution is limited and does not significantly build on existing work.  
- Weak empirical evaluation: comparison only to annual screening is insufficient and uninformative.  
- Robustness missing: need experiments testing sensitivity to inaccuracies or uncertainty in the disease model.  
- Recommendation: strengthen practical relevance by relaxing the known-model assumption and provide more comprehensive, robustness-focused experiments.
