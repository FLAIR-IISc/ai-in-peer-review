The paper proposes a novel methodology for constructing personalized screening policies that balance the cost of screening against the cost of delay in detection of disease. The disease is modeled as an arbitrary finite state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state. The authors incorporate the possibility of external information, such as self-examination or discovery of co-morbidities, that may trigger arrival of the patient to the clinician in advance of a scheduled screening appointment. The proposed method, DPSCREEN, is evaluated using a large breast cancer dataset and compared to existing clinical policies.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of personalized screening is relevant to the field of healthcare. The authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature and explain how their approach differs from previous works. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, and the proposed method is well-described.
