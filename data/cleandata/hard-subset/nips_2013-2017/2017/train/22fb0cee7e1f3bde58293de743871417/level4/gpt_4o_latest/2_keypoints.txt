- Paper proposes an optimal patient screening policy using a finite-state stochastic disease model to minimize screening costs and delays.  
- Authors provide a computationally efficient approximate solution.  
- Experiments on simulated breast cancer data show reduced delays at similar cost compared to an annual-screening baseline.  
- Strength: Personalized screening is an important and impactful problem.  
- Strength: The proposed algorithm is well-conceived and thoughtfully developed.  
- Strength: Experimental results are encouraging.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Method assumes a known per-patient disease model, which is unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: Methodological contribution is modest.  
- Weakness: Experiments only compare to an annual-screening baseline and do not test robustness to disease-model inaccuracies.  
- Recommendation: The paper is interesting but needs stronger evidence of practical relevance and robustness to model uncertainty.
