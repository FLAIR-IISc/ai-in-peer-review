This paper presents DeepScreen (DPSCREEN), a method for determining optimal screening policies for large categories of diseases. Compared to other work in this area, DeepScreen takes into account the patient's clinical (screening) history as well as patient characteristics relevant to specific disease models. Consequently, DeepScreen is able to achieve personalization in the screening process, which increases efficiency compared to existing screening policies without negatively impacting the timeliness of disease detection.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and technically sound. The approach is well-motivated, considers relevant related work, and provides a concise but thorough discussion of the algorithm's mathematical basis, including computational complexity. DeepScreen has been validated on a large (45k patient) dataset from the Athena Health network. It adequately demonstrates the improvements provided by personalization. The model has also been validated across disease model types. The result is a paper that makes a clear technical contribution with the potential for practical impact.
