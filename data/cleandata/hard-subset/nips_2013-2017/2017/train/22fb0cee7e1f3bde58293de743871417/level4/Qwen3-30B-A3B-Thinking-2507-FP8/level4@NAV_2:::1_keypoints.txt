- Introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for personalized screening that accounts for patient-specific features and dynamic screening history.  
- Models disease progression as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state and external triggers for early visits.  
- Generalizes prior stationary Markov-based approaches to non-stationary processes and provides tractable approximate optimality via point-based value iteration.  
- Validated on 45,000-patient breast cancer data, showing 28–68% reductions in screening frequency while preserving detection timeliness.  

- Technically sound with rigorous convexity derivation and approximation error bounds (Proposition 1).  
- Correctly handles non-Markovian delay costs and external information that invalidate standard POMDP methods.  
- Breast cancer validation includes robust model estimation (Gail scores) and comprehensive comparisons to annual/biennial policies.  
- Robustness to model estimation errors addressed theoretically (Theorem 1).  

- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with effective visualization of personalization (Figure 1).  
- Minor clarity improvement needed on belief space dimensionality reduction (Section 3.2).  

- Original contribution: first unified framework for personalized screening across diverse disease models, relaxing stationary Markov assumptions.  
- Clearly differentiates from prior NIPS-relevant works (e.g., Ayer et al. 2014; Balancing Suspense and Surprise 2016).  

- Demonstrates substantial practical impact and applicability across diseases with different delay-cost structures.  
- Potential to inform future clinical guidelines given large-scale empirical support.  

- Strength: First to handle general disease models with external information.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.  
- Strength: Clear demonstration of personalization via risk-group analysis.  
- Strength: Comprehensive real-world comparisons.  

- Weakness: Limited discussion of scalability to very high-dimensional state spaces beyond the studied case.  
- Weakness: Could more explicitly connect to recent disease-trajectory mapping work (Schulam et al., NIPS 2016).  

- Recommendation: Accept — significant technical contribution with solid theory and compelling large-scale empirical results.
