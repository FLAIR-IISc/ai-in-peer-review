- Introduces DPSCREEN: personalizes screening schedules by modeling patient screening history and disease-specific features to improve efficiency without sacrificing timeliness.  
- Mathematically grounded method with complexity analysis and evaluation on ~45k Athena Health patients across multiple disease models.  
- Strength: clear contribution—advances beyond one-size-fits-all screening by combining history and heterogeneity.  
- Strength: technical soundness—concise, reproducible mathematical exposition.  
- Strength: appropriate related work positioning and broad empirical validation demonstrating generality.  
- Strength: practical relevance with potential near-term clinical impact.  
- Concern: sensitivity to disease progression model and utility/cost parameter misspecification—needs systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Concern: evaluation metrics need clarification (false positives, screenings per patient, avg time to detection, cost per detected case) plus confidence intervals/statistical tests.  
- Concern: stronger personalized baselines and ablations required (e.g., risk-thresholding, learned hazard models, isolate history vs static features).  
- Concern: generalization/external validity—need external dataset validation or stratified results across demographics/comorbidities.  
- Concern: clarify handling of EHR issues—missingness, irregular intervals, censoring, delayed/noisy labels and effects of imputations.  
- Concern: report computational practicality—wall-clock training/inference time, memory usage, feasibility for real-time deployment.  
- Concern: incorporate policy constraints and safety guardrails (clinical guidelines, bounds, prevent sparse screening for high-risk).  
- Concern: fairness—perform subgroup analyses to ensure personalization does not widen disparities.  
- Concern: interpretability and clinician workflow—explain how policies are presented and which features/history drive recommendations.  
- Reproducibility request: provide code or pseudocode, hyperparameters, and model selection details.  
- Minor: ensure consistent naming (“DPSCREEN” vs “DPScreen”), add schematic/pseudocode, and clarify notation early.  
- Overall: well-written, technically solid, empirically compelling paper; recommend acceptance if sensitivity, baseline, and subgroup/robustness concerns are addressed.
