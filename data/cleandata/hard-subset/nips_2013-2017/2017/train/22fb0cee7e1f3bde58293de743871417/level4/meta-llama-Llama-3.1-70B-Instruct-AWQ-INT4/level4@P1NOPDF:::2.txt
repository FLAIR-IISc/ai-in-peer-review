The paper aims to determine the optimal policy for patient screening, given relevant information. To achieve this, the authors model a disease as a finite state stochastic process, with the goal of minimizing screening costs and delays. They propose an approximate solution that is computationally efficient. Experiments using simulated breast cancer data suggest that the proposed algorithm can reduce delays while maintaining the same cost as a trivial baseline.

Positives:
+ Personalized screening is a significant research topic, and this paper contributes to its development.
+ The proposed algorithm is reasonable and well-developed, indicating a clear understanding of the problem.
+ The results are promising, showing potential for practical application.
+ The paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.

Negatives:
- The proposed algorithm relies on an unrealistic assumption: that the disease model for a given patient is known. In practice, this information is rarely available, and the primary challenge in personalized screening is inferring the model from data. This limitation makes the proposed algorithm more of an academic exercise than a practical solution.
- The methodological novelty of the paper is not substantial, building on existing ideas rather than introducing significant new concepts.
- The experiments only compare the proposed algorithm to a baseline that performs annual screening. Given the assumption of a known disease model, a crucial question that should be experimentally investigated is the robustness of the proposed method to inaccuracies in the disease model. In other words, how sensitive is the policy to uncertainties in the disease model? This analysis would strengthen the paper's claims and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the algorithm's limitations.

Overall, the topic is interesting, and the proposed algorithm is reasonable. However, the lack of methodological novelty and the unrealistic assumption of a known disease model detract from the paper's impact. To convince readers of the practical relevance of this work, the authors need to address these limitations and provide more comprehensive experimental evaluations.
