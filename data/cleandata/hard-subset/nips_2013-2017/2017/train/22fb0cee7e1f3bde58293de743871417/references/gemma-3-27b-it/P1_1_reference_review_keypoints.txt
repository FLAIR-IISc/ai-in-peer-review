- Summary: Proposes DPSCREEN, a framework for dynamic personalized screening that optimizes screening cost vs detection delay under general stochastic disease progression and non-stationary information.
- Method: Provides an approximate solution with provable error bounds and demonstrates effectiveness on a breast cancer dataset.
- Positioning: Clearly differentiates from prior personalized screening and simulation approaches by relaxing Markov/stationarity assumptions and connecting to controlled sensing/POMDP work.
- Strength — Generality: Framework accommodates diverse disease models, non-stationary test outcomes, and external information.
- Strength — Theoretical Rigor: Presents a computational procedure for approximately optimal policies with a formal approximation error bound (Proposition 1).
- Strength — Empirical Results: Real-world breast cancer application shows 28–68% reductions in screening frequency with comparable detection delays.
- Strength — Personalization: Policies adapt to patient features and dynamic history, yielding nuanced screening decisions.
- Strength — Cost Sensitivity: Analysis shows how different delay cost functions materially affect policy regimes.
- Strength — Robustness: Theoretical analysis of model estimation error (Theorem 1) adds robustness guarantees.
- Weakness — Computational Complexity: The stated complexity (O(T(B)^2 T^2 K|Y||Z|)) may be prohibitive for large state spaces or long horizons; scalability mitigations are under-discussed.
- Weakness — Model Assumptions in Demo: Breast cancer demonstration relies on simplifying assumptions (two-state Markov, lump growth, self-exam rules) whose sensitivity needs exploration.
- Weakness — External Info Modeling: Lump-size/external information model is simplistic and ignores reporting bias, measurement error, and variable sensitivity.
- Weakness — Limited Comparison: Focuses on PBVI without discussion or comparison to other POMDP approximation methods (e.g., MCTS).
- Weakness — Generalizability Discussion: Paper could better discuss adaptation of the framework to other diseases with different characteristics.
- For Acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a novel, theoretically sound, and general approach backed by compelling real-data results.
- Against Acceptance: Computational demands and reliance on specific demo assumptions may limit practical applicability.
- Overall Assessment: Strong paper with significant impact potential in personalized medicine, meriting acceptance despite scalability and modeling caveats.
- Recommendation & Rating: Strong Accept; recommend presentation at NIPS 2017.
