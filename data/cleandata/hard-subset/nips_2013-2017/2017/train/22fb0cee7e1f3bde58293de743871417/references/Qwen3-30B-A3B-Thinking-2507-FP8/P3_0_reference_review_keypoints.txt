- Paper proposes DPSCREEN: a POMDP-based framework for personalized screening balancing screening cost vs detection delay, using general finite-state disease models and external information.

- Algorithmic contribution: a tractable PBVI-based algorithm (DPSCREEN) approximating the optimal policy.

- Theoretical insight: value function characterized as convex and piecewise-linear (Lemma 1).

- Error analysis: provides approximation error bounds (Proposition 1) and robustness to model estimation errors (Theorem 1).

- Empirical results: on breast cancer data, shows 28–68% reduction in screening frequency while maintaining expected detection delay.

- Builds on prior POMDP and personalized screening literature, claiming to relax Markovian and stationary-test assumptions.

- Strength: clear problem formulation and comprehensive related work situating the contribution.

- Strength: methodological rigor with detailed theoretical analysis and discussion of robustness.

- Weakness: novelty is overstated; prior works ([7], [9], [10]) already address similar personalized screening problems.

- Weakness: disease model used (two-state Healthy/Disease) is an oversimplification of real breast cancer progression.

- Weakness: unrealistic assumption of no false positives, which undermines practical relevance for mammography.

- Weakness: limited experimental validation — only breast cancer and a synthetic comparison; lacks broader disease benchmarks.

- Weakness: approximation error bound derivation (Ω(B̄)) is vague and inadequately justified, with proof relegated to supplementary material.

- Weakness: lacks comparison to other advanced personalized screening baselines beyond annual/biennial policies.

- Recommendation: clarify and precisely articulate novel contributions vs prior work, ideally with a comparative assumptions table.

- Recommendation: incorporate or analyze the impact of false positives in the model or discuss necessary modifications.

- Recommendation: expand experiments to multi-state disease models and additional disease types to demonstrate generalizability.

- Recommendation: strengthen and present the proof/details of Proposition 1 and discuss practical implications of the approximation bound.

- Recommendation: clarify the construction and necessity of the belief space (|ST × {0,1}|) and how it differs from standard POMDP beliefs.

- Overall assessment: promising and impactful work with strong theory and results but limited by overstated novelty, unrealistic assumptions, and insufficient theoretical and experimental rigor.

- Recommendation verdict: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing novelty claims, theoretical proofs, and modeling/experimental limitations.
