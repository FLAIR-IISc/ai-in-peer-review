- Proposes DPSCREEN: dynamic personalized screening that balances screening costs and detection delay using a general finite-state disease model and external triggers, solved via a modified point-based value iteration with approximation bounds.  
- Technical strength: rigorous mathematical formulation with Bellman analysis, error bounds (Proposition 1), and robustness results (Theorem 1).  
- Empirical strength: validated on 45k-patient breast cancer dataset showing 28–68% fewer screenings for equivalent detection delays.  
- Clarity: well-organized, clear notation, and thorough experimental descriptions linking to related work.  
- Originality: novel extension beyond Markov/stationary-test assumptions and novel adaptation of point-based value iteration for general disease models.  
- Significance: high potential clinical and economic impact with generalizability to other diseases and meaningful real-world improvements.  
- Weakness: needs deeper comparison to recent personalized healthcare/RL literature (e.g., Rizopoulos 2016, Schulam et al. 2017).  
- Weakness: assumption of no false positives is restrictive and unexplored in terms of method impact.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/examples on adapting the approach to diseases with different progression patterns (e.g., pancreatic cancer).  
- Weakness: computational complexity bounds lack concrete practical parameter estimates, leaving scalability unclear.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical and empirical contribution to ML and healthcare.  
- Final thought: paper is a solid NIPS candidate combining theory and practical impact; minor issues do not outweigh its contributions.
