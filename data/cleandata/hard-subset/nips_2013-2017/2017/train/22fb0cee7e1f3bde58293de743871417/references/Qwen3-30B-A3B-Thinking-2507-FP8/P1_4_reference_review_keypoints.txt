- Summary: Proposes DPSCREEN, a framework for personalized screening that balances screening cost and detection delay, showing 28–68% reduction in screening frequency on breast cancer data while maintaining expected delay.

- Technical contribution: Introduces a modified point-based value iteration for high-dimensional belief spaces with theoretical approximation-error guarantees.

- Model generalization: Extends prior work by allowing arbitrary finite-state disease processes and incorporating external information (e.g., self-examinations).

- Experimental validation: Thorough evaluation on a real breast cancer dataset with appropriate metrics, demonstrating meaningful improvements over current clinical (annual/biennial) policies.

- Theoretical rigor: Provides strong theoretical results (Lemma 1, Proposition 1) bounding approximation error by sampling density and includes sufficient supplementary detail for reproducibility.

- Limitation noted: Uses a simplified two-state Markov model for breast cancer progression, which may not capture full disease complexity (acknowledged by authors).

- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized with clear notation and effective figures; however, belief space reduction (Algorithm 1) and some technical supplement details need clearer integration into the main text.

- Originality: Novel relaxation of restrictive Markov/stationary assumptions and the incorporation of external triggers; presents a computationally tractable, general approach not addressed in prior POMDP screening work.

- Significance: High practical impact and generalizability to other diseases; addresses a key computational challenge in personalized screening with potential clinical relevance.

- Strengths: Addresses an important problem, provides theoretical guarantees, validates on real data, offers a general framework, clearly differentiates from prior work, and is well-structured.

- Weaknesses: Simplified disease model, unclear practical details of belief-space reduction, limited computational complexity analysis for large-scale use, and little discussion of scaling to multiple concurrent diseases.

- Recommendation: Strongly recommend acceptance based on technical contribution, experimental validation, and practical impact.

- Suggested improvements: Expand discussion on disease-model limitations and extensions; explain belief-space reduction (Algorithm 1) in main text; discuss handling multiple concurrent diseases; add brief comparisons to other approximate POMDP methods (e.g., MCTS).
