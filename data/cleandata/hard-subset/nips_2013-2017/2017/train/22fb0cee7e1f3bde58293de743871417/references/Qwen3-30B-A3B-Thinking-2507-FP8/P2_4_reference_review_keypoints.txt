- Proposes DPSCREEN, a dynamic personalized screening framework that handles non‑Markov disease progression, patient features, dynamic history, and external information.  
- Uses a belief‑based approach with point‑based value iteration to compute approximately optimal policies with bounded error.  
- Validated on breast cancer data, showing 28–68% reductions in screening frequency while maintaining comparable detection delays.  
- Strong technical foundation: generalizes beyond standard POMDP assumptions and rigorously formulates the problem.  
- Key theoretical contributions: Lemma 1 (convexity of the value function) enabling approximation and Proposition 1 providing approximation error bounds.  
- Experimental design is sound, comparing against clinical practices and stratifying by risk.  
- Main quality concern: assumes perfect test accuracy (no false positives/negatives), which limits realism though authors note extensibility.  
- Well‑structured and clearly written, with a strong problem formulation and useful graphical model in the appendix.  
- Clarity could be improved with a concrete example illustrating the belief‑space dimensionality reduction technique.  
- Original and novel: adapts point‑based value iteration and alpha vectors to high‑dimensional belief spaces in a new healthcare context.  
- Significant potential impact: substantial screening reductions, cost and burden savings, and generalizability to other diseases.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, practically relevant, and well‑presented; minor weakness is acknowledged.  
- Recommendation: accept — suitable for NIPS given the algorithmic contribution, theoretical guarantees, and real‑world application.
