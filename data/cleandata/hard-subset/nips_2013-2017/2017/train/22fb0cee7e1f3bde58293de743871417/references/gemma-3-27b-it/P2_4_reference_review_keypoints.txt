- Tackles personalized, dynamic screening with DPSCREEN that models patient features, disease progression, external info, and test outcomes to optimize schedules.  
- Handles non-Markovian disease processes and unknown delayed-detection costs with a computationally efficient approximation and provable error bounds.  
- Demonstrates reductions in screening frequency (28–68%) on a breast cancer dataset and with synthetic experiments.  
- Novel and significant: general framework extending beyond static approaches and accommodating external information.  
- Strong technical rigor: POMDP-based formulation, Bellman derivation, point-based value iteration, and theoretical error bounds.  
- Comprehensive treatment: model, algorithm, complexity analysis, performance evaluation, and robustness analysis included.  
- Empirical validation strengthened by real-world dataset application and synthetic studies.  
- Generally well-written with clear motivation; personalization discussion and figures are effective.  
- Dense and heavy notation; requires strong POMDP/stochastic modeling background, making accessibility an issue.  
- Algorithmic complexity (O(T(B)^2T^2K|Y||Z|)) may be high for large state spaces and long horizons; real-world feasibility needs more discussion.  
- Relies on assumptions (e.g., test-result independence, accurate hazard estimation); sensitivity to violations is underexplored.  
- Limited empirical scope: single disease/dataset (breast cancer); broader datasets would improve generality.  
- Some clarity issues: Theorem 1’s robustness discussion is abstract; “risk groups” explanation needs elaboration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel/theoretically sound approach, promising empirical impact, addresses key screening challenges.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical density limits audience, computational complexity could restrict practice, validation limited to one dataset.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-potential contribution offsetting weaknesses if clarity and computational concerns are addressed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and discuss computational considerations.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
