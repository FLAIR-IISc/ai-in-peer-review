- Paper proposes DPSCREEN, a framework for dynamic, personalized screening that handles arbitrary finite-state disease processes and incorporates external information to balance screening cost and detection delay.

- Key contributions: generalized formal model, point-based value iteration approximation, theoretical error bounds, and empirical improvements on breast cancer data (28–68% fewer screenings).

- Strength: Theoretical soundness with clear definitions and proofs, notably convexity of the value function (Lemma 1).

- Strength: Novel approach that removes restrictive Markov assumptions used in prior screening models.

- Strength: Compelling empirical results showing large reductions in screening frequency while maintaining detection delay and demonstrating personalization.

- Strength: Theoretical guarantees on approximation error (Proposition 1) support practical use of the approximate policy.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation and distinction from prior work.

- Strength: High potential real-world impact, particularly for breast cancer screening and healthcare resource allocation.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail in the main text about how the disease progression model is estimated from observational data.

- Weakness: Simplified test model assumes no false positives/negatives, which is unrealistic for real-world screening.

- Weakness: Simplified external-information model (e.g., self-examined lumps) may not capture real patient behavior complexity.

- Weakness: Computational complexity and scalability concerns for larger state spaces or more complex diseases are not fully addressed.

- Weakness: Limited empirical comparisons to other machine-learning or decision-theoretic approaches beyond clinical policies.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound, well-supported claims, with appropriate caveats about limitations.

- Clarity assessment: Well-organized and clearly written, but main text should include more model-estimation details rather than relegating them to supplementary material.

- Originality assessment: Significantly different from prior POMDP screening work by relaxing Markov and stationary outcome assumptions; novel use of point-based value iteration here.

- Significance assessment: Highly significant for healthcare with broad applicability and meaningful demonstrated improvements; cost-function choice impacts policy design.

- Recommendation: Accept—important real-world problem, strong theoretical and empirical contributions, and clear advancement over prior work.

- Specific suggestion: Add more main-text detail on model estimation from observational data.

- Specific suggestion: Discuss implications of assuming no false positives/negatives.

- Specific suggestion: Compare or discuss other machine-learning-based screening methods.

- Specific suggestion: Clarify scalability and computational complexity for larger applications.

- Specific suggestion: Discuss extensions to multiple diseases or competing risks.

- Arguments for acceptance: Significant impact, novel generalization, strong empirical gains, theoretical guarantees, clear writing.

- Arguments for rejection: Limited model-estimation detail, unrealistic test-assumption simplifications, and lack of ML baselines.
