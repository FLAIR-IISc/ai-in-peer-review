- Summary: DPSCREEN formulates personalized screening as an optimal-control POMDP-style problem with general disease models, belief states, convex piecewise-linear value function, point-based value iteration, theoretical error bounds, and strong breast-cancer case results (28–68% fewer screens).

- Strength 1: Tackles a high-impact healthcare problem with potential cost savings and maintained detection quality.

- Strength 2: Provides rigorous theory proving the value function is convex and piecewise linear, extending POMDP results.

- Strength 3: Novel application of point-based value iteration to non-standard disease models and personalization, with clear motivation.

- Strength 4: Compelling empirical results on breast cancer showing large reductions in screenings and a convincing ablation on self-examination impact.

- Strength 5: Supplies theoretical approximation-error guarantees (Proposition 1).

- Strength 6: Clear depiction of personalization mechanism linking patient features and dynamic history (Figure 1).

- Weakness 1: Experimental validation uses only a two-state Markov model, contradicting the claim of handling general/non-Markov disease processes.

- Weakness 2: The two-state breast cancer model is overly simplistic and omits realistic pre-cancerous stages, weakening the generality claim.

- Weakness 3: Error bound depends on sampling density Ω(B̄) but the paper gives no practical guidance on choosing it.

- Weakness 4: Lacks comparisons to recent deep RL methods applied to healthcare decision-making.

- Weakness 5: Complexity analysis is vague and unsupported by empirical runtime or scalability results.

- Weakness 6: Limited discussion of real-world clinical implementation barriers (acceptance, EHR integration, ethics).

- Quality Assessment: Technically sound with solid theory and experiments, but model simplifications weaken the contribution and transparency on limitations is needed.

- Clarity Assessment: Well-organized and clearly written overall, though some technical details (belief representation, trajectory-to-belief link) need more intuitive explanation and key appendix figures should be in the main text.

- Originality Assessment: Novel problem formulation and application of POMDP theory to personalized screening; core algorithm is standard PBVI but applied in a new setting.

- Significance Assessment: High potential impact given screening reductions, but significance tempered by simplistic experimental disease model.

- Recommendation 1: Extend experiments to non-Markov/multi-state disease models to validate generality.

- Recommendation 2: Provide practical guidance on choosing sampling density Ω(B̄).

- Recommendation 3: Compare with recent deep RL approaches for personalized healthcare decisions.

- Recommendation 4: Discuss barriers to clinical deployment and mitigation strategies.

- Recommendation 5: Clarify the relationship between the experimental two-state model and the paper’s claimed general disease-model capabilities.

- Final Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing limitations in the camera-ready version.

- Pro Acceptance Arguments: Addresses important problem, strong theory extending POMDPs, substantial empirical improvements, theoretical error bounds, and clear presentation.

- Con Acceptance Arguments: Limited disease-model complexity, simplistic breast-cancer model, insufficient comparison to deep RL, and approximation bound lacks practical guidance.
