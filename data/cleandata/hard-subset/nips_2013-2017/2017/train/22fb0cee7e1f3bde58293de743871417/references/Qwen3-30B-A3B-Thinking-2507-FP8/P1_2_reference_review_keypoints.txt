- Paper introduces DPSCREEN, a framework for personalized screening balancing screening costs and delay costs using a non-standard time-dependent POMDP.  
- Provides a point-based value iteration approximation with theoretical guarantees and validates on breast cancer data.  
- Technical quality: rigorous mathematical formulation with key result that the value function is convex and piecewise linear (Lemma 1).  
- Offers an approximation error bound (Proposition 1) that justifies the proposed algorithm.  
- Experimental design uses a real-world breast cancer dataset and compares to current clinical guidelines.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, consistent notation, and accessible algorithm explanation with helpful figures.  
- Originality: general framework that handles non-Markov disease processes, external information, and dynamic risk, extending prior disease-specific work.  
- Significance: demonstrates large reductions (28–68%) in screenings while maintaining comparable detection delays and supports different delay cost functions.  
- Limited experimental validation: evaluated only on breast cancer, with limited synthetic tests for other cost functions.  
- Practical scalability concerns: computational complexity discussed but lacks analysis for much larger populations or real-time deployment.  
- Unrealistic test-accuracy assumption: assumes no false positives, which limits clinical applicability.  
- Limited discussion of ethical implications, including potential impacts on health disparities.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with strong technical and practical value despite noted weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, significant practical impact, general applicability, clear comparison to prior work, and novel POMDP methodology.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited disease validation, unrealistic test assumptions, insufficient scalability discussion, and missing ethical analysis.  
- Recommendation: reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Suggested future work: validate on additional diseases, handle false positives, analyze large-scale computational performance, and include ethical considerations.
