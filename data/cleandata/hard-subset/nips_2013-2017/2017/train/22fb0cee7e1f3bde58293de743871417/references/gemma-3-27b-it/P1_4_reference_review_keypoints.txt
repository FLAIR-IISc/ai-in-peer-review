- Introduces DPSCREEN: a framework for dynamic personalized screening that handles non‑Markovian disease progression, external information, and computational intractability via point‑based value iteration with approximation bounds.

- Extends prior personalized screening, POMDP, and dynamic treatment regime work by relaxing Markov assumptions and incorporating external information.

- Connects to controlled sensing literature and positions DPSCREEN as a more general, flexible approach.

- Strength: high generality—applicable to many diseases and models due to relaxed assumptions.

- Strength: practical algorithm—point‑based value iteration provides a computationally feasible approximation.

- Strength: theoretical guarantees—provides bounds on approximation error to quantify accuracy vs. compute tradeoffs.

- Strength: empirical validation—breast cancer dataset shows large reductions in screening frequency with similar detection delays.

- Strength: demonstrates the importance of cost function choice and how it affects policy decisions.

- Strength: authors clearly discuss limitations, especially around model estimation challenges.

- Strength: paper is well written and organized, with clear presentation of methods and results.

- Weakness: implementation appears complex and may require significant POMDP/statistical expertise.

- Weakness: reliance on accurate model parameter estimation; sensitivity to estimation errors not fully explored.

- Weakness: experiments are limited to breast cancer; a second disease example would strengthen generality claims.

- Weakness: external information model (lump size) is simplistic and could be improved.

- Weakness: computational complexity analysis lacks detailed scaling behavior with states and horizon length.

- Qualitative: technically sound with solid theoretical analysis, though some math sections are dense and need more intuition.

- Qualitative: original and significant—combines several advances to address an important healthcare problem.

- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with promising empirical results and practical importance.

- Against acceptance: potential barriers include implementation difficulty, dependence on accurate models, and limited experimental scope.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: add practical implementation details and tool suggestions; perform sensitivity analysis on model parameters.

- Suggested revisions: apply DPSCREEN to a second (even simplified) disease to demonstrate generality.

- Suggested revisions: explore more sophisticated external information models and provide a more detailed scalability/computational complexity analysis.
