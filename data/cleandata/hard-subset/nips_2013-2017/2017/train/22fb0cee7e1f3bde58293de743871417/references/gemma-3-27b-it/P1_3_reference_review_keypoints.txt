- Paper proposes DPSCREEN, a framework for dynamic, personalized screening balancing screening costs and delayed detection.
- Framework handles non-Markovian disease dynamics, external information, and patient arrival patterns for individualized policies.
- Presents a computationally tractable approximation algorithm with provable error bounds and robustness to model estimation errors.
- Empirical evaluation on a breast cancer dataset shows 28–68% reductions in screening frequency with comparable delay times.
- Authors situate work well relative to prior screening, POMDP, and controlled sensing literature, highlighting key differences.
- Strength: high novelty and generalizability across disease models and patient-specific factors.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundations (Proposition 1 and Theorem 1) with approximation/error guarantees.
- Strength: practical relevance demonstrated via real-data application and substantial screening reductions.
- Strength: generally clear presentation and helpful graphical model.
- Strength: insightful analysis on how cost-function choices impact policies.
- Weakness: computational complexity remains high (O(T(B)^2T^2K|Y||Z|)); practical mitigation strategies and K’s impact need more exploration.
- Weakness: simplifying model assumptions (no false positives; independence of test outcomes and external info) may not hold and need discussion/extensions.
- Weakness: dataset limited to ages 60–65; broader demographics and disease stages would improve generalizability.
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis for key parameters (e.g., ι, σ) and their effect on optimal policies.
- Weakness: Algorithms 1 & 2 are in the appendix; a high-level description belongs in the main text.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, original, theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and practically relevant.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational scalability and restrictive model assumptions could limit applicability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing complexity discussion, false-positive assumptions, brief algorithm descriptions, and a sensitivity analysis.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
