- Presents DPSCREEN, a general framework for personalized, dynamic disease screening using a finite-state stochastic disease model and an external information process that triggers unscheduled visits.  
- Frames screening as a trade-off between screening cost and detection delay harm in a non-standard POMDP.  
- Proposes a tractable approximation using Point-Based Value Iteration (PBVI) over a sampled belief space.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: an approximation error bound (Proposition 1) and robustness to model estimation errors (Theorem 1).  
- Empirically strong: on a large breast cancer dataset, shows 28–68% fewer screenings without increasing detection delay.  
- Extends prior work by handling non-Markovian disease dynamics and integrating external information processes.  
- Originality: novel application and adaptation of PBVI to a new problem formulation with timing influenced by external observations.  
- Clarity concern: paper is dense and key algorithmic details are relegated to the appendix, hindering readability.  
- Practical concern: high computational complexity may limit real-world deployment despite parallelization potential.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant, and original work; strengths outweigh weaknesses and favor acceptance.
