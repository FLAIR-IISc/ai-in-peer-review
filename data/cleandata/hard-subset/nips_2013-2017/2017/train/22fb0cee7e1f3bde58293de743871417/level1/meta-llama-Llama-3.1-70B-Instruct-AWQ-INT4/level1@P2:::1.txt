This paper proposes a novel framework for constructing personalized screening policies for a wide range of diseases. The authors develop a dynamic programming approach, called DPSCREEN, to balance the cost of screening against the cost of delay in disease detection. The disease is modeled as a finite state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state, and the approach incorporates external information, such as self-examination or discovery of comorbidities, that may trigger patient arrival at the clinician.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed approach. The technical development is sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed approach, including a discussion of the computational complexity and the approximation error.

The paper also includes an illustrative experiment using a large breast cancer dataset, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach in reducing the number of screenings while maintaining the same delay in disease detection.

* The proposed approach is novel and addresses a significant problem in personalized medicine.
