- Summary: Proposes DPSCREEN, a personalized screening framework that trades off screening cost vs delay using a finite-state stochastic disease model and external information, validated on a large breast cancer dataset showing fewer screenings with similar detection delay.
- Strength: Novel approach to construct cost–delay–balanced personalized screening policies.
- Strength: Flexible framework applicable to multiple diseases and able to incorporate diverse external information.
- Strength: Personalizes policies using patient features and screening history.
- Strength: Empirically effective on a large breast cancer dataset.
- Weakness: Computing the alpha-vector sets is computationally intractable; authors rely on an approximate algorithm.
- Weakness: Model assumptions include convex delay-cost and perfect test accuracy, which may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison—only against current clinical policies, not other personalized screening frameworks.
- Originality: Original in incorporating external information and history-based personalization into screening policy design.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for personalized medicine and more efficient cancer screening across diseases.
- Clarity: Well-written and clearly explains model, algorithm, and problem formulation.
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough analysis and organized presentation.
- Argument for acceptance: Novel framework offering demonstrated effectiveness and significant practical implications.
- Argument against acceptance: Major concerns about computational tractability and limited benchmarking.
