- Introduces a novel framework for personalized screening that balances screening cost against detection delay, modeling disease as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing state and external triggers for early clinician arrival.
- Proposes DPSCREEN, a computationally tractable algorithm to construct approximately optimal personalized screening policies.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and detailed, well-organized technical development.
- Demonstrates practical applicability using a real-world breast cancer dataset.
- Strength: framework handles arbitrary finite-state processes and incorporates external information.
- Strength: empirical comparison to existing clinical policies shows fewer screenings while maintaining equal detection delay.
- Weakness: assumes the disease model is known; estimation error is discussed but not fully addressed.
- Weakness: computational complexity may hinder scalability to very large datasets or complex models.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons with other personalized screening methods to establish relative performance.
- Conclusion/recommendation: significant contribution with potential clinical impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and add comparisons.
