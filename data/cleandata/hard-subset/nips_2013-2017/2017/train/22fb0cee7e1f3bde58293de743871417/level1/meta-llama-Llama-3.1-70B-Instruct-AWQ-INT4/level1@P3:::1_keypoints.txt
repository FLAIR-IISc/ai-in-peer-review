- Proposes a dynamic-programming framework to construct personalized screening policies for disease modeled as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state.  
- Incorporates external information (self-exam, co-morbidity discovery) that can trigger unscheduled clinician visits.  
- Uses a point-based value iteration algorithm to compute approximately optimal, history- and feature-personalized policies.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large breast cancer dataset, reducing screenings while keeping detection delay unchanged.  
- Strength: personalization to patient features and dynamic history improves over existing clinical policies.  
- Strength: flexible framework applicable to dynamic survival, first-hitting-time, and Semi-Markov disease models.  
- Strength: provides bounds on approximation error and shows robustness to estimation errors.  
- Strength: empirically effective—fewer screenings for same detection delay in dataset evaluation.  
- Weakness: high worst-case computational complexity (can be computationally expensive for large instances).  
- Weakness: relies on assumptions such as perfect test accuracy and bounded maximum screening interval that may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison—only compared against existing clinical policies, not broader state-of-the-art methods.  
- Originality: novel methodology balancing screening cost against delay cost with a flexible, generalizable framework.  
- Significance: potential to significantly improve screening program effectiveness across diseases via personalization.  
- For acceptance: novel, flexible approach with theoretical error bounds and demonstrated empirical benefits.  
- Against acceptance: computational scalability concerns, possibly unrealistic assumptions, and limited comparative evaluation.  
- Recommendations: provide comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendations: investigate and propose methods to reduce computational complexity.  
- Recommendations: relax key assumptions and evaluate robustness under more realistic scenarios.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
