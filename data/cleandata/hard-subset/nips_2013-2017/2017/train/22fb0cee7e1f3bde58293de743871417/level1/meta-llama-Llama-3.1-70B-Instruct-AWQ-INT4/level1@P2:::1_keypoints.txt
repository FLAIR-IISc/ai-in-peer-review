- Proposes DPSCREEN, a novel dynamic-programming framework to personalize screening by trading off screening cost vs delay in detection.  
- Models disease as a finite-state stochastic process with an absorbing disease state and incorporates external triggers for clinician visits.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear, concise introduction and sound technical development.  
- Provides thorough analysis including computational complexity and approximation error bounds.  
- Illustrative experiment on a large breast cancer dataset shows fewer screenings while maintaining detection delay.  
- Strength: novel approach addressing an important problem in personalized medicine.  
- Strength: technically sound analysis and clear exposition.  
- Strength: empirical demonstration is well-designed and effective.  
- Weakness: assumes model parameters are known; estimation error handling is insufficient.  
- Weakness: evaluation focused on breast cancer, leaving generalizability to other diseases unclear.  
- Weakness: high computational complexity may limit practical applicability.  
- Review scores high on quality, clarity, and originality but lower on significance due to generalizability and complexity concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—authors should address parameter estimation, generalization, and computational complexity.
