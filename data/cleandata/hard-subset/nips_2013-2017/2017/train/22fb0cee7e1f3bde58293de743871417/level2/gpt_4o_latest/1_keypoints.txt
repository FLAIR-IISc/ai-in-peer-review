- Novelty & Significance: Presents a generalizable framework for personalized screening that incorporates external information and applies across disease models.  
- Technical Rigor: Provides detailed mathematical formulation, Bayesian belief updates, Bellman equations, and theoretical guarantees on approximation error.  
- Practical Impact: Demonstrates on a large breast cancer dataset a 28–68% reduction in screenings while maintaining similar detection delays and personalizing for risk groups.  
- Robustness: Shows the approach is resilient to model estimation errors, supporting real-world deployment.  
- Benchmark Comparison: Thoroughly compares to existing clinical policies and demonstrates clear efficiency and personalization advantages.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense and could be clearer, especially explanations of PBVI and POMDP/Bayesian concepts for non-experts.  
- Evaluation Scope: Empirical evaluation is limited to breast cancer; additional diseases would strengthen generalizability claims.  
- Assumptions: Assumes no false positives/negatives in tests; treatment of imperfect test accuracy is not addressed.  
- Computational Complexity: Scalability and computational requirements for large-scale or long-horizon implementations are not fully resolved.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Arguments for Acceptance: Novel, generalizable framework with strong experimental results and rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Arguments Against Acceptance: Limited evaluation scope and dense presentation that may hinder accessibility.
