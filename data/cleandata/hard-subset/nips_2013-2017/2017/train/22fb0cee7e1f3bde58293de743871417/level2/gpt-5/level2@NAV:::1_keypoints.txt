- Proposes DPSCREEN, a framework for dynamic personalized screening that trades testing frequency against detection delay using finite-state disease models and external patient-initiated information.  
- Frames screening as a nonstandard POMDP with trajectory-dependent costs and endogenous decision epochs and proves the value function is convex piecewise-linear.  
- Derives an exact but intractable alpha-vector solution and a tractable PBVI-style approximation that samples typical state trajectories to reduce belief dimensionality.  
- Provides an approximation-error bound under strong assumptions and a robustness result showing local constancy of optimal actions to small model-estimation errors.  
- Empirical application on a large breast-cancer dataset (Athena) reports 28–68% fewer tests versus annual/biennial screening while maintaining expected detection delay.  
- Ambitious and general modeling that explicitly accounts for external information and trajectory-dependent delay costs.  
- Clear formulation of a challenging decision problem outside standard POMDP templates and extension of piecewise-linear value structure.  
- Practical, parallelizable approximation with principled belief-set construction and demonstrated personalization across risk groups/histories.  
- Useful discussion of different delay-cost shapes and corresponding policy implications.  
- Empirical evaluation relies on a learned simulator from observational data with no counterfactual validation and limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Theoretical guarantees depend on unrealistic assumptions (no test errors, bounded intervals) and do not quantify constants, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Limited baselines: missing comparisons to constrained-POMDP solvers, myopic/threshold/index heuristics, and learning-based off-policy optimization.  
- Key estimation and implementation details are relegated to the supplement; reproducibility, code/data release, and computational/scale benchmarks are absent.  
- The “first framework” novelty claim is overstated given prior POMDP and controlled-sensing screening literature.  
- Suggest adding off-policy evaluation (IPS/DR), extensive sensitivity analyses, and comparisons to stronger baselines.  
- Recommend reporting runtime/scaling, ablations on belief-set construction, and incorporating/quantifying realistic test errors.  
- Suggest releasing code and parameter estimates and moderating novelty claims.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — important problem and thoughtful approach, but needs stronger validation and baselines.
