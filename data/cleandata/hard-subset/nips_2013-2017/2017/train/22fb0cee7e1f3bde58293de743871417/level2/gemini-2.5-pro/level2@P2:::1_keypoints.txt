- Proposes DPSCREEN, a general framework for personalized, dynamic disease screening balancing screening cost vs. delayed detection cost.  
- Models disease progression as a general finite-state stochastic process and adds an external information process for unscheduled patient arrivals.  
- Formulates the problem via Bellman equations similar to POMDPs but notes standard POMDP solvers are unsuitable due to trajectory-dependent costs and variable inter-decision times.  
- Introduces a PBVI-based approximation (sampling representative belief points) with a theoretical bound on approximation error and a robustness analysis to model estimation errors.  
- Demonstrates DPSCREEN on a large, real-world breast cancer dataset, achieving 28–68% fewer screenings at equivalent expected detection delay.  
- Shows policies are personalized to initial risk profiles and evolving test histories, with clear empirical illustrations.  
- Strength: tackles a societally important problem with potential to improve outcomes and reduce costs.  
- Strength: original and general model—relaxes Markov assumptions and models unscheduled visits via an external information process.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation, well-motivated PBVI approximation, and formal theoretical results (Proposition 1 and Theorem 1).  
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation using real data and clear comparisons to clinical guidelines.  
- Weakness: limited detail on model estimation (parameterization of pin/ptran and MCMC procedure) reduces reproducibility.  
- Weakness: practical computational costs not reported—missing values for K (trajectories), B (belief points), and runtimes.  
- Weakness: evaluation on a single learned model; lacks empirical sensitivity analysis to model parameter perturbations.  
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions (perfect test accuracy, convex delay cost) are strong; impact of relaxing them is not discussed.  
- Conclusion: significant, original, and technically rigorous work with strong empirical results; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
