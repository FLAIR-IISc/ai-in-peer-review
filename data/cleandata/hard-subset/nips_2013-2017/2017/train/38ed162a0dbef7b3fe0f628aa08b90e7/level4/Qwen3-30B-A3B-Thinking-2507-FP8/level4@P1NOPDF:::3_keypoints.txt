- Positive summary: Well-written, rigorous paper that characterizes CNN functional spaces with multilayer kernels and shows (near) group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.

- RKHS reformulation: Require explicit practical benefits of reframing CNNs in RKHS—implications for generalization bounds, computational efficiency via kernel approximations, and clear distinction from Mairal et al. (Convolutional Kernel Networks, 2014).

- Signal preservation: Clarify why preserving the full signal is necessary or beneficial, instead of focusing on preserving only task-relevant features for discriminative tasks (e.g., classification).

- Relation to invariant kernels: Connect the group invariance results to established invariant kernel methods (e.g., Burkhardt) or explain why they differ.

- Commuting operators: Clarify whether any operator commuting with group actions suffices for equivariance, or if specific structural constraints on patch, pooling, and kernel maps are required.
