

The paper presents a compelling analysis of stability properties for Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), establishing that appropriately chosen patch sizes yield stable representations under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (satisfying assumptions A1–A3). The work elegantly bridges deep learning and kernel methods, extending stability to locally compact groups in Section 4. The technical contribution is sound, the exposition is clear, and the paper is well-structured, making it a valuable addition to the literature.

However, the paper would benefit significantly from a stronger motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the sole justification (lines 56–57) merely notes that Lipschitz stability relates to adversarial robustness (citing [7]), without elaborating *why* stability under diffeomorphisms is specifically relevant to CKNs or how it addresses gaps in existing work. A brief discussion linking stability to practical concerns (e.g., invariance to small deformations in vision tasks, or theoretical guarantees for generalization) would greatly strengthen the contribution. For instance, the authors could clarify how their stability result differs from or complements existing work on Lipschitz continuity in neural networks.

Several minor technical points require attention for precision and clarity:  
1. **Notation conflict**: The symbol $\kappa$ is overloaded in Assumption (A3) (line 193), where it denotes a patch size parameter, while it also defines the kernel function $K$ in Equation (10). This risks confusion; the authors should use distinct symbols (e.g., $\kappa$ for patch size, $\kappa_K$ for the kernel parameter).  
2. **Redundant constraint**: The displayed equation between lines 384–385 includes a superfluous constraint ("$\forall v$...") due to the symmetry of kernel $k$; the first constraint ($\forall u$...) fully suffices.  
3. **Missing definition**: The definition of $\phi$ (line 416) is absent; it should be introduced explicitly in Equation (10) as $\langle \phi(z), \phi(z') \rangle_{\mathcal{H}(K)}$.  
4. **Typo in inequality**: Line 427–428 contains $|\|z\| - \|z\||^2$, which should be $|\|z\| - \|z'\||^2$ to reflect the correct comparison between distinct inputs.

Overall, this is a technically strong paper with a clear contribution to the theoretical understanding of CKNs. The stability result is novel and potentially impactful for robust model design. Addressing the minor issues above—particularly enhancing the motivation and resolving notational conflicts—would elevate the paper to a high standard. The authors have made a solid case for the relevance of stability analysis; amplifying this connection in the introduction would ensure the contribution resonates more broadly with the ML community. The paper is suitable for acceptance after these revisions.
