Summary and contributions:
The paper analyzes the stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C^1 diffeomorphisms, including translations, in the sense of Eq. (4). Under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernel assumptions (A1–A2) and an appropriate choice of patch sizes (A3), the authors prove Lipschitz stability of the multiscale CKN representation. They also sketch an extension from Euclidean domains (R^d, +) to general locally compact groups. The paper is clearly written, technically sound, and connects kernel methods with deep architectures. The main stability result is potentially valuable for understanding the robustness properties of CKNs.

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation, with stability defined precisely.
- Technically solid analysis under interpretable assumptions (A1–A3).
- Conceptual bridge between convolutional networks and kernel methods.
- Extension to locally compact groups is promising and broadens applicability.

Main suggestions for improvement:
- Motivation for stability: The current manuscript offers only a brief motivation (lines 56–57) via adversarial robustness. Please expand the discussion to articulate:
  - Why stability to C^1 diffeomorphisms is desirable for vision and signal tasks (e.g., deformation and translation variability).
  - How the result relates to and differs from prior stability analyses in scattering networks and other architectures.
  - Practical implications for design choices in CKNs, especially the trade-off governed by patch size (A3) between stability and discriminability.
  - Concrete examples of kernels satisfying (A1–A2), with explicit constants, to guide practitioners.

- Empirical or illustrative support: While the paper is theoretical, readers would benefit from a short illustrative example quantifying the stability bound for common kernels (e.g., Gaussian, arc-cosine) and showing how patch sizes meeting (A3) affect the Lipschitz constant across layers.

- Clarity of the group extension: The sketch in Section 4 is intriguing. Consider adding a brief worked example (e.g., the rotation group SO(2) or SE(2) for roto-translation) to clarify the assumptions and the form of the stability constants in non-commutative settings.

Specific comments and corrections:
- Notation overloading in (A3) [line 193]: Using κ both for the kernel-defining function and in (A3) is confusing. Please disambiguate (e.g., use κ_A3 or a different symbol h for the function defining K in Eq. (10)).

- Redundant constraint [between lines 384–385]: The second part (“and ∀v …”) is redundant given the symmetry of k and is equivalent to the first (“∀u …”). It can be safely removed.

- Missing definition of φ [line 416]: φ should be defined where Eq. (10) is introduced, e.g., K(z, z′) = ⟨φ(z), φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.

- Typographical error [lines 427–428]: In the inequality under “=”, | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z′|| |^2. As written, it evaluates to zero and does not justify the inequality.

- Assumptions (A1–A2): It would help to restate explicitly what “norm-preserving” and “non-expansive” mean in your setting (e.g., k(u, u) = 1 and Lipschitz constant ≤ 1 in each argument), and to verify these for a couple of canonical kernels used in CKNs.

- Constants and depth dependence: If possible, make explicit how the stability constant depends on depth, stride, and patch sizes, and whether the constant remains controlled as the number of layers increases under natural scaling choices.

- Relation to prior work: Briefly contrast the assumptions and resulting bounds with those in scattering transforms and other kernel-based hierarchical representations. Highlight what is new in the CKN setting (e.g., the role of learned or data-adapted filters via kernel approximations).

Minor style and clarity:
- Consider adding a brief intuitive paragraph after stating the main theorem to explain how (A3) interacts with (A1–A2) to yield stability across layers.
- Ensure consistency of symbols between the main text and the appendix.

Overall assessment:
This is a well-executed and timely theoretical contribution on the stability of CKNs. With stronger motivation, minor notational and typographical fixes, and a clearer presentation of practical implications (including simple kernel examples and a worked group case), the paper would be even more accessible and impactful. I recommend acceptance after addressing the above points.
