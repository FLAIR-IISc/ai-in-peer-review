This paper examines some of the good properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (DCNs), namely that DCNs are almost invariant to groups of transforms and stable for the operation of diffusionism, provided the appropriate patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, the good properties obtained from DCNs also apply to CNNs.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The only weakness is that the paper does not provide any empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. I would like to see some experimental results that show the conclusion that DCNs are invariant to transforms and diffusionism with appropriate settings.

Another question is whether explicit kernel mapping with a random projection or a finite number of Fourier series can also be a potential solution for kernel approximation. If so, what is the impact on stability?
