The paper studies desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing that CKNs are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, these properties extend to standard CNNs.

Overall, the paper is well written and technically sound. The main weakness is the absence of empirical validation. I would like to see experiments supporting the claim that CKNs, with appropriate settings, are invariant to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

A further question concerns kernel approximation: can random projections or explicit kernel mappings using a finite number of Fourier features serve as viable alternatives? If so, what is their impact on stability?
