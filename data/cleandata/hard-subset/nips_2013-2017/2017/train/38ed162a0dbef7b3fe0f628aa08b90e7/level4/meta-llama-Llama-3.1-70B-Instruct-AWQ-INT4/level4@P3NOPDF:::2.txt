The paper focuses on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14], analyzing their stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation) as per Equation (4). The authors demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] when the patch sizes are appropriately chosen [(A3)]. The manuscript also outlines an extension of the concept from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from deep networks and kernels. The stability result has the potential to be of interest to the machine learning community.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the submission would benefit from a more detailed motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only motivation provided is a brief sentence (line 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].') that motivates the main contribution of the paper. Providing additional context or explanations for the importance of stability analysis would strengthen the paper.

Furthermore, the overloading of the \kappa notation in (A3) [line 193] may cause confusion, as it also denotes a function defining kernel K in Equation (10). To avoid ambiguity, it would be beneficial to use distinct notations for these different concepts.

Additionally, the displayed equation between line 384 and 385 contains a superfluous part ('and \forall v...'). Given the symmetry of kernel k, this part is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...') and can be removed for clarity.

There are also a few minor issues that need to be addressed. For instance, the definition of \phi is missing and should be introduced in Equation (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}]. Moreover, in line 427-428, the inequality under '=' seems to also hold with equality, and | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
