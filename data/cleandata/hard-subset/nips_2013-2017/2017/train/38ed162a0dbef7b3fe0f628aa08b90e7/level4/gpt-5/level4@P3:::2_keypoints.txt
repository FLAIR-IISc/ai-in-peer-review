- Proposes multilayer convolutional kernel representation with feature maps near-translation-invariant, stable to C1 diffeomorphisms, and extendable to group-equivariant/invariant constructions.  
- Builds on CKNs via continuous-domain patch-extraction, kernel-mapping, and pooling, and analyzes the induced RKHS geometry.  
- Proves stability bounds ‖Φ(Lτx)−Φ(x)‖ ≤ (C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞/σn)‖x‖ linking stability to pooling scale and diffeomorphism Jacobian.  
- Discusses discretization, kernel-map approximations, and conditions for information preservation in practical implementations.  
- Shows a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS, transferring stability guarantees via norm control.  
- Extends construction to locally compact groups to achieve equivariance and global invariance through group patching and pooling.  
- Positions work relative to scattering transforms and group-equivariant CNNs, extending scattering-style analysis to kernels and certain CNNs.  
- Strength: technically solid theory using commutator bounds, separating roles of patch extraction and pooling, and matching intuitive depth/pooling scaling.  
- Strength: unifying kernel view encompassing CKNs and a subset of CNNs, extending stability beyond scattering networks.  
- Strength: clean group-theoretic treatment showing how subgroup pooling yields intermediate stability and final global invariance.  
- Strength: practical bridge clarifying how continuous theory maps to discrete, implementable models.  
- Weakness: motivation for diffeomorphism stability is weak—needs empirical justification (sample complexity, augmentation, viewpoint/nonrigid robustness).  
- Weakness: lack of discussion on tightness of (1+n) dependence and scaling of constants C1, C2 with dimension and κ.  
- Weakness: A3’s necessity/sufficiency and practical guidance for choosing σk and patch sizes are underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: Lemma 2’s deconvolution-based signal recovery relies on small pooling scales; numerical stability conditions should be noted.  
- Weakness: CNN inclusion relies on smooth homogeneous surrogates; an explicit smoothed ReLU example and approximation error discussion would help.  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully argued, with main proofs in appendix; minor clarity issues noted.  
- Clarity: well organized and readable, but some notational/editorial fixes needed.  
- Originality: extends scattering-style invariance/stability to broader kernel frameworks and a class of CNNs; group treatment is distinct from G-CNNs.  
- Significance: provides theoretical grounding for multilayer kernel representations and principled guidance on invariance/stability in CNN-like architectures.  
- Specific issues: expand motivation around diffeomorphism stability and cite empirical literature; fix κ overload; remove redundant constraint in equation; define ϕ explicitly.  
- Specific issues: correct typographical error in norm expression; state sampling density for Lemma 1 and robustness; note deconvolution stability conditions in Lemma 2.  
- Specific issues: quantify C1’s dependence on κ and dimension in Theorem 6; update several incomplete reference entries.  
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous stability guarantees, clear path to discretized architectures, unifying RKHS view linking stability and generalization, and clean group equivariance treatment.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical motivation for diffeomorphism stability, minor editorial/notation issues, limited guidance on constants/parameters, and gap between smooth surrogates and standard ReLU CNNs.  
- Overall: a well-executed theoretical contribution bridging scattering, kernels, and CNNs that would benefit from stronger motivation and minor clarifications.
