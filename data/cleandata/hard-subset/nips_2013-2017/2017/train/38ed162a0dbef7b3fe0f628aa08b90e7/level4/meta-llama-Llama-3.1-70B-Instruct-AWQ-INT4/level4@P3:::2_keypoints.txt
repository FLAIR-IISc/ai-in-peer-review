- Paper presents a theoretical analysis of stability and invariance for convolutional kernel network (CKN) representations.  
- Studies geometry of the functional space of CKNs, offering insights into their effectiveness and applications.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, accessible to readers familiar with the topic.  
- Combines deep network and kernel-method ideas, creating a novel contribution.  
- Main technical result: CKNs are stable to small deformations (Lipschitz-type stability).  
- Analysis is technically sound with clear presentation of assumptions, limitations, and mathematical tools.  
- Extension of the analysis from Euclidean space to locally compact groups broadens the framework.  
- Motivation for the stability analysis is underdeveloped and needs more context on its importance.  
- Minor notation/formatting issues (confusing use of kappa in (A3) vs. kernel K in eq. (10); redundant displayed constraint between lines 384–385).  
- Some references lack page information and reference [17] appears incorrect.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address motivation, notation/formatting, and reference fixes.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Originality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Impact 8/10.
