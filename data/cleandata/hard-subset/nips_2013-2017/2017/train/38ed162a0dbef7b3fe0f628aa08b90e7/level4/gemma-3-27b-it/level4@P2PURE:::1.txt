The paper investigates appealing properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they exhibit near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphic mappings when the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are appropriately designed. The authors further show that, under specific assumptions, these beneficial properties extend to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically rigorous. A key weakness, however, is the lack of empirical validation for the theoretical analysis. It would be valuable to see experimental results demonstrating the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms with suitable parameter settings. 

Additionally, it is worth considering whether alternative kernel approximation techniques, such as random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series terms, could also be viable solutions. If so, understanding their impact on stability would be of interest.
