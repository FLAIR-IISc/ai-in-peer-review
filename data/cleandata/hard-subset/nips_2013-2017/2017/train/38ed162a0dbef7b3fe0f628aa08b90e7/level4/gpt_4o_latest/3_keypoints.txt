- Overall: paper is well-written and rigorous, characterizes DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels, and demonstrates (near) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms; recommend acceptance pending revisions.
- Main contribution: clarify practical benefits of recasting DCNNs in RKHS (e.g., implications for generalization bounds, computational advantages via kernel techniques/approximations) and whether novelty extends beyond reformulation.
- Prior work: explicitly differentiate this work from "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.) and other related kernel-based convolutional approaches.
- "Not losing signal information": elaborate why preserving full signal information is critical given many DCNN applications only require task-relevant features (unless reconstruction is the goal).
- Group invariance: discuss any relationship between the presented approach and invariant-kernel methods such as those by Hans Burkhardt.
- Equivariance question: clarify whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would ensure the equivariance property of the DCNN components (patch, pooling, kernel maps) or if stronger/specific conditions are required.
