- Overall judgment: paper is clear and technically solid, characterizes DCNN function spaces via multilayer kernels, shows near-invariance and diffeomorphism stability; reviewer favors acceptance pending clarifications.  
- Main contribution / RKHS reformulation: require discussion of concrete practical/theoretical benefits (e.g., tighter generalization bounds, computational gains via kernel approximations) and whether novelty goes beyond a re-characterization.  
- Prior work differentiation: explicitly distinguish the paper from related methods, particularly "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.).  
- Signal-preservation motivation: justify why preserving full signal information is desirable given that CNNs typically aim to extract task-relevant features and discard others.  
- Group invariance context: situate and clarify relationships with existing invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Hans Burkhardt’s approach).  
- Equivariance generality: clarify whether equivariance holds for any operator commuting with the group action or is specific to the patch, pooling, and kernel-map operators used here.
