This paper presents compelling theoretical results regarding deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating their near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphic actions when utilizing carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. The authors further establish, under specific assumptions, that these beneficial properties extend to conventional convolutional neural networks (CNNs). 

Overall, the manuscript is well-written and technically rigorous. A key limitation, however, is the absence of empirical validation. Experimental results demonstrating the theoretical findings – specifically, the invariance of appropriately configured CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms – would significantly strengthen the work.

Furthermore, it would be valuable to consider alternative kernel approximation techniques. The potential of methods like random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite Fourier series, and their impact on the observed stability properties, warrants discussion.
