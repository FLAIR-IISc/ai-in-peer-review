Here's a paraphrased version of the review:

The paper's central theme revolves around Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors conduct an in-depth analysis of the stability of CKN representations with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (notably translation), as defined in Eq. (4). Their findings indicate that stability can be achieved for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193], provided that suitable patch sizes [(A3)] are chosen. Additionally, the authors outline an extension of their approach from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, seamlessly integrating concepts from deep networks and kernels. The stability result has the potential to be of significant interest to the machine learning community.

However, the submission could benefit from a more comprehensive motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only mention of motivation is a brief statement in lines 56-57 ('Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].'), which does not adequately justify the main contribution of the paper.

Furthermore, the use of the \kappa notation in (A3) [line 193] may cause confusion, as it also represents a function defining kernel K in Eq. (10).

The displayed equation between lines 384 and 385 contains a redundant second part ('and \forall v...'), which is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...') due to the symmetry of kernel k.

The definition of \phi is missing and should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}]. 

Additionally, the inequality in lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality as well, and should be rewritten as | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.

Lastly, several references ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25-27,30,31]) lack page information, and the citations for [9, 17, 19, 32] require correction to accurately reflect the original sources.
