- Paper studies deep signal representations that are near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to C^1 diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Authors generalize multilayer kernels from convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and analyze the geometry of the associated RKHS.  
- They prove stability of the representation and show models from this functional space (including many CNNs) may inherit the same stability.  
- Technical approach: stability analysis for C^1 diffeomorphisms (Eq. (4)) under assumptions A1–A3 (norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels, suitably chosen patch sizes).  
- Extension from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is sketched in Section 4.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and combines ideas from deep networks and kernels effectively.  
- Stability result is technically sound and potentially of interest to the ML community.  
- Main weakness: paper lacks sufficient motivation for why the stability analysis matters (only one short motivating sentence).  
- Minor technical/notational issues: overloading of κ in (A3) is confusing relative to Eq. (10).  
- Mathematical errors/omissions: displayed equation between lines 384–385 contains a superfluous part; φ is not defined in Eq. (10) notation [=<φ(z),φ(z')>_{H(K)}]; inequality in lines 427–428 appears to be equality; typo | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.  
- References issues: some references missing page information and a few incorrect citations.  
- Recommendation: accept (technically sound and significant), but request addressing the motivation and the minor technical/notation/reference fixes before publication.  
- Originality/Significance: provides a novel stability analysis for CKNs, relevant to researchers in deep networks and kernels.
