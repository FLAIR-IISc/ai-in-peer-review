- Positive evaluation: paper is well-structured and rigorous, shows DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels, highlights invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms, and recommends acceptance.  
- Main contribution / RKHS reformulation: must discuss why reformulating CNNs in RKHS is useful (e.g., for generalization bounds or computational gains via kernel techniques) and clarify novelty relative to prior work.  
- Distinguish from prior art: explicitly compare and contrast with "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.) to show what is new.  
- Signal preservation concern: justify emphasis on preserving signal information, since many tasks (e.g., classification) require only task-relevant features unless the goal is signal reconstruction.  
- Invariant-kernel connections: investigate links with Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach to situate and deepen the contributions.  
- Equivariance operators: clarify whether other operators commuting with the group would ensure equivariance or why the chosen patch, pooling, and kernel maps are particularly appropriate/unique.
