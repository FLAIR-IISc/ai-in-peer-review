- Shows CKNs can achieve near invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms with appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Extends these invariance and stability results to CNNs under specific assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, technically sound, and provides clear geometric insights into deep architectures.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation to support the theoretical claims, weakening practical relevance.  
- Recommends experiments visualizing CKN responses to controlled transformations (rotations, elastic deformations) on standard datasets.  
- Practical implementation concern: paper relies on implicit kernel representations without exploring finite-dimensional approximations.  
- Suggests investigating random Fourier features or finite Fourier expansions to reduce computational complexity.  
- Urges analysis of whether finite-dimensional kernel approximations preserve the derived invariance and stability properties, especially to diffeomorphisms.  
- Notes that for a venue like NIPS, lack of experiments (even small-scale on MNIST/CIFAR-10) reduces the paper’s impact.  
- Requests clarification on whether random Fourier features maintain theoretical guarantees or conditions under which stability breaks.  
- Overall: meaningful theoretical contribution but needs empirical grounding and a deeper discussion/analysis of kernel approximations to be fully convincing.
