This paper investigates the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14], specifically with respect to C<sup>1</sup> diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations, as formalized in Eq. (4)). The authors demonstrate that stability is maintained for kernels that preserve norms and are non-expansive [(A1-A2) in line 193], provided patch sizes are appropriately selected [(A3)]. A potential extension to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The manuscript is well-structured, clearly presented, and technically rigorous, effectively integrating concepts from deep learning and kernel methods. The core stability result is a valuable contribution to the machine learning field. 

However, the paper would be strengthened by a more substantial motivation for the stability analysis. The current motivation is limited to a single sentence (lines 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].').

Several minor points should be addressed: the notation \kappa in (A3) [line 193] is potentially confusing as it also represents a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10); the second constraint ('and \forall v...') in the equation between lines 384 and 385 is redundant due to the symmetry of the kernel k and is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...'); the definition of \phi is missing on line 416 and should be included in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>]; and the inequality on line 427-428 appears to hold with equality, and the expression should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |<sup>2</sup> rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |<sup>2</sup>.
