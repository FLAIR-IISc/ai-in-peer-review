This paper is well written and rigorous. It convincingly shows how certain functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) can be characterized through multilayer kernels, and it establishes appealing architectural properties: (near) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, subject to the clarifications below.

- Main contribution and usefulness of the RKHS reformulation: To my understanding, the primary contribution is a reformulation of CNN architectures within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) framework. Please elaborate on why this reformulation is (or will be) useful. For example:
  - Could the kernel formulation lead to new or sharper generalization bounds?
  - Does it point to computational advantages via standard kernel techniques or approximations (e.g., Nyström, random features), or is it mainly a conceptual tool?
  More broadly, does the novelty extend beyond a reformulation of DCNNs in an RKHS context?

- Relation to prior work: Please clarify the novelty relative to “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid. What are the precise conceptual and technical differences, and what does your analysis enable that was not addressed there?

- On “not losing the signal information”: The paper emphasizes the importance of preserving the signal. Why is this crucial in your framework? In many applications (e.g., image classification), only task-relevant information needs to be retained unless reconstruction is the goal. Please articulate whether your guarantees target task-agnostic information preservation, task-specific sufficiency, or both, and how this interacts with invariance and stability.

- Group invariance connections: Is there a link between your treatment of group invariance and the invariant kernel approach developed by Hans Burkhardt (invariant kernels)? If so, please describe the relationship and differences.

- Equivariance via commuting operators: You define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. Would any operator from the commutant (i.e., any operator that commutes with all group elements) ensure the equivariance property of the DCNN with respect to the group transformations? If not, what additional structural conditions are required?

Overall, the paper provides a clear and rigorous perspective on DCNNs through multilayer kernels and offers appealing stability and (approximate) invariance properties. Addressing the above points would further clarify the contribution, its novelty, and its practical and theoretical implications.
