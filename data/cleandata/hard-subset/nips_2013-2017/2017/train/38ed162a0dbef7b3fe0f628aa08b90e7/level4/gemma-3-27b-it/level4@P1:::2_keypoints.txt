- Paper analyzes stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) w.r.t. C¹ diffeomorphisms and proves stability under conditions (A1-A3), with an extension sketched to locally compact groups.
- Well organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining kernel methods with deep networks.
- Stability result is of potential interest to the machine learning community.
- Motivation is weak and should be strengthened beyond a brief link to adversarial robustness.
- Notation conflict: κ in (A3) may be confusing because κ also denotes the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10).
- Redundant constraint: the second part of the equation between lines 384–385 is superfluous due to kernel symmetry.
- Missing definition: ϕ is not defined at line 416 and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as the feature map for K.
- Typo/inequality error: lines 427–428 likely hold with equality and | ||z|| - ||z|| |² should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |².
- References [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31] lack page numbers and need completion.
- Specific reference corrections: [9], [17], [19], and [32] need corrected citation details as provided.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis, originality in kernel-based perspective, clarity of exposition, and potential significance for understanding generalization/regularization.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation and several minor technical/clarity issues to fix.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing the noted minor issues and strengthening motivation.
