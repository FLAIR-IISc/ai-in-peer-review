The paper investigates desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing that, when the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are properly designed, CKNs are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms. The authors also demonstrate that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties of CKNs extend to CNNs.

Overall, the manuscript is clearly written and technically sound. The primary weakness is the lack of empirical experiments to substantiate the theoretical analysis. I would like to see experimental results confirming that CKNs, with appropriate configurations, are invariant to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

Another question is whether random projections or explicit kernel mappings with a finite number of Fourier features could be used for kernel approximation. If so, what would be the effect on stability?
