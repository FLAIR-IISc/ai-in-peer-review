This paper presents a rigorous analysis of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors demonstrate how a multilayer convolutional kernel can characterize the functional spaces underlying CNNs, and establish properties like (near) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and offers a valuable theoretical perspective on deep learning. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points below.

**Summary:** The core contribution lies in reformulating CNN architectures within the RKHS framework. This allows for leveraging tools from kernel methods to analyze properties like stability and invariance. The paper extends previous work on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) by generalizing the kernel construction to a broader class of CNNs, including those with smooth homogeneous activation functions. The authors demonstrate that models within this functional space inherit stability guarantees similar to those established for scattering transforms, yet operate with learnable parameters unlike the fixed filters of scattering networks.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a mathematically sound analysis, building upon established results in kernel methods and harmonic analysis. Proofs are detailed and well-structured (though relegated to the appendix, as appropriate).
*   **Novelty:** While building on prior work (particularly CKNs and scattering transforms), the paper provides a significant generalization.  It extends the kernel framework to a wider range of CNN architectures and establishes a connection between RKHS norms, stability, and potential generalization bounds.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly explains complex mathematical concepts. The notation is consistent, and the introduction effectively motivates the research.
*   **Significance:** Understanding the geometry of the functional spaces inhabited by CNNs is a fundamental question. This work contributes to that understanding and could potentially inform the design of more robust and generalizable models. The link between stability and generalization is particularly promising.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation for Reformulation:** While the mathematical framework is compelling, the practical benefits of this reformulation aren’t fully articulated. The paper would be strengthened by explicitly discussing *why* representing CNNs as RKHSs is advantageous. For example, does this formulation lead to tighter generalization bounds? Does it suggest more efficient implementation strategies or approximations?
*   **Novelty Relative to CKNs:** The relationship to Mairal et al.’s "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (CKNs) should be clarified further. The authors mention generalizing the CKN construction, but a more detailed comparison of the key differences and advantages of the presented approach would be beneficial.
*   **“Signal Information” Preservation:** The authors emphasize preserving signal information, but the relevance of this in the context of typical CNN applications (e.g., image classification) isn't fully explained. It's important to acknowledge that for many tasks, only task-relevant information needs to be preserved, not the entire original signal. A discussion of this trade-off would be valuable.
*   **Connection to Invariant Kernels:** Exploring the connection to the invariant kernel approach developed by Hans Burkhardt would strengthen the related work section. This would demonstrate awareness of existing techniques for achieving invariance through kernel design.

**Specific Questions/Suggestions for Revision:**

*   **Elaborate on the benefits of the RKHS formulation:**  Add a paragraph discussing potential advantages, such as improved generalization analysis, computational efficiency, or connections to regularization techniques.
*   **Clarify novelty compared to CKNs:** Provide a more detailed comparison of the proposed framework with CKNs, highlighting the specific generalizations and contributions.
*   **Address the “signal information” concern:**  Discuss the trade-off between preserving all signal information and preserving only task-relevant information.
*   **Mention Burkhardt's invariant kernels:** Cite and briefly discuss the relevant work on invariant kernels.
*   **Operator Equivariance:** The final comment about other operators commuting with the group action is an interesting thought. While not essential, briefly acknowledging that the specific choice of patch extraction, pooling, and kernel maps isn't the *only* way to achieve equivariance could add nuance.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in understanding the properties of CNNs.
*   The mathematical analysis is rigorous and well-supported.
*   The potential for future work building upon this framework is high.
*   The identified weaknesses are relatively minor and can be addressed with revisions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The practical implications of the RKHS reformulation are not fully explored.
*   The connection to related work (particularly CKNs) could be strengthened.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with the potential to make a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep learning. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above will further enhance its impact. I recommend acceptance.
