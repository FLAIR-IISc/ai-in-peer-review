- Paper shows that carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling in CKNs yield near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.
- Authors argue these theoretical properties can extend to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.
- Manuscript is well-written and technically sound.
- Major weakness: no empirical experiments to validate the theoretical claims.
- Recommendation: include experiments to verify near-invariance and diffeomorphism stability for the proposed CKN designs.
- Open question: could alternative kernel approximations (random projections, finite Fourier features) be used, and how would they affect the analyzed stability properties?
