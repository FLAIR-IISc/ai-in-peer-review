**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations" presents a theoretical framework for understanding the properties of deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) in terms of multilayer kernels. The authors show that DCNNs can be represented as a sequence of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs), which allows them to study the invariance and stability properties of these networks. Specifically, they demonstrate that DCNNs are almost invariant to group transformations and stable to diffeomorphic transformations.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

The paper is well-written and rigorous, and the authors successfully show how DCNNs can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. The paper also highlights the nice properties of DCNNs, such as their invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations.

However, there are some limitations and potential areas for improvement. Firstly, the authors could provide more context on why this reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS is useful. For example, they could discuss how the kernel formulation may lead to interesting results on generalization bounds or whether it is computationally more efficient to implement using standard techniques/approximation on kernels.

Additionally, the authors could clarify the novelty of their approach beyond a simple reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS. They could also provide more discussion on the connection between their work and other related papers, such as "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal et al.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel framework for understanding the properties of DCNNs, and the authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to study the invariance and stability properties of these networks. However, the authors could provide more discussion on the significance of their results and how they contribute to the broader field of deep learning.
