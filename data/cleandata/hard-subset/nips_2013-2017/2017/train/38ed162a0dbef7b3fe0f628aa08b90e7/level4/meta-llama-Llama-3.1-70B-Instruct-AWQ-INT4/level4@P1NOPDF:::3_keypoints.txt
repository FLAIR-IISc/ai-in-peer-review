1. Paper is well-written and rigorous, showing DCNN functional spaces can be described using multilayer kernels.  
2. Paper highlights desirable properties of the architecture, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations.  
3. Reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on addressing the following concerns.  
4. Primary contribution looks like reformulating CNNs in an RKHS; authors should explain why this reformulation is useful (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational efficiency).  
5. Clarify novelty and distinctions from prior work, especially "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.  
6. Question the emphasis on preserving full signal information—ask authors to justify this given many DCNN tasks require only task-relevant information.  
7. Ask whether there is any connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel method.  
8. Curiosity whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would guarantee equivariance, beyond the defined patch/pooling/kernel maps.
