- Paper provides a rigorous, well-written analysis characterizing DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels and proves approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms; recommendation: accept pending revisions.

- Clarify concrete advantages of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., new generalization bounds, links to kernel approximation/computational methods) and clearly state what is novel beyond a re-expression.

- Explicitly discuss relationship and differences with prior work, especially “Convolutional Kernel Networks” (Mairal et al.), to situate novelty.

- Justify the emphasis on preserving “signal information,” noting that many DCNN applications need only task-relevant features (e.g., classification) and explaining why full signal preservation is prioritized.

- Explore connections to invariant-kernel frameworks (e.g., Hans Burkhardt’s work) and how those approaches relate to or differ from the paper’s group-invariance results.

- Address whether any operator commuting with the group action (i.e., in the commutant) would guarantee equivariance of the DCNN, and characterize which operators suffice.
