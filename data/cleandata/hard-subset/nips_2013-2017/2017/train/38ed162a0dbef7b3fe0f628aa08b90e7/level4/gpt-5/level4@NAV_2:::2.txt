The paper studies convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and analyzes the stability of the induced multilayer representation with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (including translations), in the sense of Eq. (4). The main result shows that when the kernels satisfy norm preservation and non-expansiveness (assumptions (A1–A2)) and patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3), the representation is stable. Section 4 outlines an extension from (R^d, +) to general locally compact groups.

The manuscript is well structured, clearly presented, and technically sound, synthesizing ideas from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability guarantees are potentially valuable to the ML community.

- The paper would benefit from a stronger motivation for conducting the stability analysis. At present, there is only a brief remark (lines 56–57: “Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”) linking the main contribution to robustness; expanding this discussion would help contextualize the results.
- The symbol κ in (A3) (line 193) is overloaded, as κ also denotes the function that defines the kernel K in Eq. (10); consider disambiguating notation.
- In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint (“and ∀ v ...”) is redundant; by symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first constraint (“∀ u ...”).
- Line 416: the definition of φ is missing and should be introduced in Eq. (10) via ⟨φ(z), φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.
- Lines 427–428: the inequality following “=” appears to hold with equality, and the term | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should read | ||z|| − ||z′|| |^2.
