

The paper is well written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how (specific) functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) can be characterized using multilayer kernels. It also establishes that the architecture possesses desirable properties: (nearly) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance pending the following clarifications:  
* The main contribution appears to be a reformulation of DCNN architecture within the framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors should explicitly discuss why this reformulation is useful (e.g., whether it enables novel generalization bounds or computational advantages via standard kernel techniques/approximations).  
* More broadly, does the novelty extend beyond this RKHS reformulation, or is it primarily conceptual? Clarify the distinction from the work "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid.  
* The authors emphasize "not losing signal information." Please justify this requirement: DCNNs are typically deployed for tasks like image classification, where only task-relevant information needs preservation, not full signal reconstruction.  
* Is there a connection between the group invariance properties discussed and Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach?  
* Regarding the commuting patch, pooling, and kernel maps: would *any* operator commuting with all group elements ensure the DCNN’s equivariance to group transformations, or are the specified operators uniquely necessary?
