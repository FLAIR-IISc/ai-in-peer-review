- Proves deep CKNs are near-invariant to transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms.
- Shows these properties rely on careful design of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators.
- Extends analysis to argue conventional CNNs can exhibit similar stability/invariance under certain assumptions.
- Paper is clearly written and the technical contributions appear solid.
- Main limitation: no empirical validation of the theoretical claims.
- Recommends experiments to demonstrate that CKNs with the proposed operators actually exhibit the claimed invariance and stability.
- Requests clarification on kernel approximation choices (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier features) and how alternative approximations would affect stability.
