This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms. The authors demonstrate that, under certain conditions on the kernel (norm-preserving and non-expansive), stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes. They extend their analysis to locally compact groups, outlining how to achieve group invariance. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining insights from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result is a potentially valuable contribution to the machine learning community, offering theoretical grounding for the robustness of these models.

The paper would be strengthened by providing more explicit motivation for the stability analysis. While the connection to adversarial robustness is mentioned (lines 56-57), expanding on the broader implications of stability – such as improved generalization or sample complexity – would increase the impact of the work. The notation \kappa in assumption (A3) (line 193) could be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining the kernel *K* in Eq. (10). The second part of the condition in the equation between lines 384 and 385 is redundant due to the symmetry of the kernel. Line 416 lacks the definition of \phi, which should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z),\phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>. Finally, there appears to be a typo in line 427-428; the inequality under the equals sign should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |², not | ||z|| - ||z|| |². 

Several references are missing page numbers: [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25-27, 30, 31]. Additionally, the following references require updating: [9] should cite Amit Daniely, Roy Frostig, and Yoram Singer, "Toward Deeper Understanding of Neural Networks: The Power of Initialization and a Dual View on Expressivity," *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS)*, 2016, pp. 2253-2261; [17] should cite Krikamol Muandet, Kenji Fukumizu, Bharath Sriperumbudur, and Bernhard Schölkopf, “Kernel Mean Embedding of Distributions: A Review and Beyond,” *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning*, 2013, 10(1-2): 1–141; [19] should cite Anant Raj, Abhishek Kumar, Youssef Mroueh, Tom Fletcher, and Bernhard Schölkopf, “Learning with group invariant features: A kernel perspective,” *International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Statistics (AISTATS)*, 2017, pp. 1225-1235; and [32] is a recent ICML acceptance (Yuchen Zhang, Percy Liang, and Martin Wainwright, “Convexified Convolutional Neural Networks,” *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)*, 2017).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous analysis of stability properties with clear mathematical proofs.
*   **Novelty:** The combination of kernel methods and deep learning, and the theoretical analysis of stability, are novel contributions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, despite the technical nature of the subject.
*   **Potential Impact:** The stability results could provide insights into the generalization capabilities of deep networks and inspire new regularization techniques.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Motivation:** The motivation for studying stability beyond adversarial robustness could be more thoroughly developed.
*   **Minor Technical Issues:** The minor errors and notational inconsistencies mentioned above detract slightly from the overall quality.
*   **Theoretical Focus:** The paper is primarily theoretical; empirical validation of the results would strengthen the contribution.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong submission with significant potential. The technical contributions are valuable, and the analysis is well-executed. While some minor improvements could be made, the paper currently sits at the border between accept and reject. Given the increasing interest in the theoretical foundations of deep learning, I lean towards **accept** with minor revisions, specifically addressing the motivational aspects and the noted technical details.
