The primary focus of this paper is Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors analyze the stability of the representation learned by CKNs – specifically, stability with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms (such as translation), as formalized in Eq. (4). They demonstrate that stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes (condition A3, line 193) when using norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (conditions A1-A2, line 193). The paper also sketches an extension of these results to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from the exciting fields of deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result is likely to be of interest to the machine learning community. 

However, the submission would benefit from expanded motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, only a single short sentence (lines 56-57: “Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”) connects the work to a broader motivating problem.

The notation of \kappa in condition A3 (line 193) may be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).

In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part (“and \forall v…”) is redundant; given the symmetry of the kernel *k*, it is identical to the first constraint (“\forall u…”).

Line 416 lacks a definition for \phi, which should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z), \phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>.

On line 427-428, the inequality under the '=' appears to hold with equality. The expression should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |², rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |².
