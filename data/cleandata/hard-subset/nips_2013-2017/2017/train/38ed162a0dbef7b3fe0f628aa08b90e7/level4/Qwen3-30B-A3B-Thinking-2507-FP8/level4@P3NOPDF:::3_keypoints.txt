- Presents a rigorous RKHS-based multilayer kernel formalization of CNNs, proving near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Technical execution is sound and the theoretical framework offers a compelling reinterpretation of CNN properties.  
- Must clarify novelty and utility beyond an RKHS reformulation: demonstrate practical benefits (generalization bounds, approximation or computational gains) and concrete uses of the kernel view.  
- Explicitly differentiate from Mairal et al. (Convolutional Kernel Networks 2014): specify whether novelty lies in kernel construction, theoretical guarantees, or invariance interpretation.  
- Question the emphasis on “preserving signal information”: explain why full signal preservation is necessary for discriminative tasks or how the framework prioritizes task-relevant features.  
- Connect group invariance to prior invariant-kernel work (e.g., Burkhardt 2000) and explain how pooling and multilayer composition extend or differ from those approaches.  
- Clarify the operator-commutation requirement: are specific operators (e.g., max pooling) required for equivariance, or do any operators commuting with the group suffice, and is commutation necessary and sufficient?  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on stronger contrasts with prior work (Mairal et al.), explicit discussion of practical utility, and clarification of the signal-preservation rationale.
