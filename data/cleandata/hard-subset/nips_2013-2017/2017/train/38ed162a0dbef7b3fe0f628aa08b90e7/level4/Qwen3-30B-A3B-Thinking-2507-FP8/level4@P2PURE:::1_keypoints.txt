- Investigates key properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Demonstrates CKNs exhibit near-invariance to groups of transformations.  
- Shows CKNs are stable under diffeomorphisms when using appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators.  
- Establishes that, under specific assumptions, these properties extend to conventional CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written and technically rigorous.  
- Primary limitation: absence of empirical validation demonstrating the claimed invariance and stability.  
- Unclear whether random projections or finite Fourier-feature kernel approximations are viable alternatives and what their impact on stability would be.
