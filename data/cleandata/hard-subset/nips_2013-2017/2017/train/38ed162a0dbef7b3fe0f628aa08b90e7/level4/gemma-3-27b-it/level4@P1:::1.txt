The paper studies desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they can be near-invariant to groups of transformations and stable to the action of diffeomorphisms when carefully designed – specifically, with appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, these properties extend to standard convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors clearly summarize related work, positioning their contribution within the context of scattering transforms, convolutional kernel networks, and group invariance studies. They provide a thorough theoretical analysis, building upon concepts from kernel methods and harmonic analysis. The connections drawn between the kernel framework and CNNs are particularly interesting, suggesting that the stability results may apply more broadly than initially considered.

The primary weakness of the paper is the absence of empirical validation. While the theoretical results are compelling, it would significantly strengthen the work to include experimental results demonstrating the invariance and stability properties of CKNs (and potentially CNNs) with the suggested settings. Demonstrating these properties on benchmark datasets would provide concrete evidence supporting the theoretical analysis and improve the paper's impact. 

A related question concerns alternative approaches to kernel approximation. The authors focus on subspace learning to approximate the kernel maps. It would be valuable to briefly discuss whether other methods, such as random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series terms, could also serve as viable approximation strategies. A discussion of the trade-offs between these approaches – particularly regarding their impact on stability – would further enrich the paper.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the invariance and stability properties of CKNs and their relation to CNNs.
*   **Novelty:** The work extends existing theories (e.g., scattering transforms) to a broader class of CNN architectures and establishes a connection to kernel methods.
*   **Significance:** Understanding the geometry of functional spaces underlying deep networks could lead to improved regularization techniques and better generalization performance.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, making the technical details accessible.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Lack of empirical validation:** The absence of experiments weakens the claims and limits the practical impact of the work.
*   **Limited discussion of alternative approximation methods:** Exploring alternative kernel approximation techniques would provide a more comprehensive analysis.
