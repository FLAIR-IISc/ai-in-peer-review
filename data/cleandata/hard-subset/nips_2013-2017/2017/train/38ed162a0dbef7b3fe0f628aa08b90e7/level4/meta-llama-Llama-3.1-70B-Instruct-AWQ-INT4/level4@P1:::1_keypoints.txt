- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their connection to CNNs.  
- Shows CKNs can be designed to be near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms.  
- Demonstrates these invariance/stability properties can extend to CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Strength: provides rigorous mathematical analysis that grounds the results.  
- Carefully defines multilayer convolutional kernel construction and analyzes stability to deformations and translation invariance.  
- Establishes a clear connection and applicability of CKN properties to CNNs.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical claims.  
- Suggests adding experiments to demonstrate CKNs’ practical invariance and stability.  
- Recommends exploring random projection or explicit kernel mapping (finite Fourier series) as alternative kernel approximations.  
- Overall: well-written, technically sound, and a valuable contribution.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis, demonstrated desirable properties, significant implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical validation and limited exploration of alternative kernel approximations.
