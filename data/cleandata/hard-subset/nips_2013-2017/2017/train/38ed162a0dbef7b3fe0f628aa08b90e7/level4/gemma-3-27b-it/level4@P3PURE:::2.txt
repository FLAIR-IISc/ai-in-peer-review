The primary focus of the paper is Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. In this manuscript, the authors analyze the stability—with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms (such as translation), in the sense of Eq. (4)—of the representation formed by CKNs. They demonstrate that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193], stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes [(A3)]. An extension from (Rᵈ,+) to locally compact groups is sketched in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from two exciting areas: deep networks and kernels. The stability result presented could be of interest to the machine learning community. 

However, the submission would benefit from additional motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, only a single short sentence (lines 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].') motivates the core contribution. Expanding on this connection would strengthen the paper.

The use of the notation κ in (A3) [line 193] may be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).

In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and ∀v...') is superfluous; given the symmetry of the kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ('∀u ...').

Line 416: the definition of φ is missing and should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=<φ(z),φ(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>].

Line 427-428: the inequality under '=' appears to also hold with equality; | ||z|| - ||z|| |² should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |².
