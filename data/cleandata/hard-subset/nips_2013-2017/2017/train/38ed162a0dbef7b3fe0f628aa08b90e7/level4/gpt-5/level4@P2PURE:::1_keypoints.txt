- Paper analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.
- Authors claim these invariance and stability properties extend to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.
- Presentation is clear and the theory appears technically sound.
- Main weakness: no empirical validation — experiments are needed to support the invariance and stability claims.
- Open question: can kernel approximations (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier feature mappings) serve as viable alternatives, and how would they affect stability?
