The paper is clearly written and technically rigorous. It convincingly shows that (a class of) functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional networks can be characterized via multilayer kernels, and that the resulting architectures enjoy desirable properties: approximate invariance to group actions and Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphic deformations. I recommend acceptance, subject to the comments below:

- To my reading, the main contribution is a reformulation of CNN architectures within an RKHS framework. Please expand on why this viewpoint is or will be valuable (e.g., does the kernel perspective enable sharper generalization bounds? can one leverage standard kernel approximations for computational benefits?). More broadly, does the novelty go beyond an RKHS restatement of DCNNs? In addition, please delineate what is new relative to “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Mairal, Koniusz, Harchaoui, and Schmid.

- The manuscript emphasizes “not losing signal information.” Why is this essential? In many practical settings (e.g., image classification), unless reconstruction is the target, preserving only task-relevant information may suffice. Please comment.

- Regarding group invariance: is there a relationship with the invariant kernel approach of Haasdonk and Burkhardt (invariant kernels)?

- A curiosity: you define patch extraction, pooling, and kernel mappings that commute with the group action. Would any operator from the commutant (i.e., any operator that commutes with all group elements) ensure equivariance of the DCNN with respect to the group transformations?
