

The primary contribution of the paper centers on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors investigate the stability [with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translation), as defined in Eq. (4)] of representations generated by CKNs. They establish that stability holds under appropriately selected patch sizes [(A3)] for kernels satisfying norm-preserving and non-expansive properties [(A1-A2) at line 193]. An extension to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.  
The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically rigorous, effectively bridging concepts from deep networks and kernel methods. The stability findings hold potential significance for the machine learning community.  
- The manuscript would benefit from enhanced motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, only a single sentence (lines 56–57: "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].") justifies the core contribution.  
- The notation \(\kappa\) is overloaded in (A3) [line 193]; it also represents the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10).  
- The second constraint ("and \(\forall v\)...") in the displayed equation between lines 384–385 is redundant due to kernel symmetry, making it identical to the first constraint ("\(\forall u\)...").  
- Line 416 omits the definition of \(\phi\); it should be introduced in Eq. (10) as \(=\langle\phi(z),\phi(z')\rangle_{H(K)}\).  
- Lines 427–428 contain an error: \(|\|z\| - \|z\||^2\) should be \(|\|z\| - \|z'\||^2\), as the inequality holds with equality.
