This paper investigates the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14], specifically with respect to C<sup>1</sup> diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) as defined in Eq. (4). The authors demonstrate that stability is maintained for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] when appropriate patch sizes are selected [(A3)]. A potential extension to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and technically rigorous, effectively bridging concepts from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result presented has the potential to be of significant interest to the machine learning community. 

However, the paper would be strengthened by expanding the motivation for this stability analysis. The current justification, found in lines 56-57 ("Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7]."), feels somewhat limited given the core contribution. 

Several minor points should be addressed: the use of the symbol \kappa in (A3) [line 193] could be confusing, as it also represents a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10); the second part of the equation between lines 384 and 385 ('and \forall v...') is redundant due to the symmetry of the kernel k; the definition of \phi is missing in line 416 and should be included in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>]; and the inequality under the '=' sign in lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality, and should be corrected from | ||z|| - ||z|| |<sup>2</sup> to | ||z|| - ||z'|| |<sup>2</sup>.
