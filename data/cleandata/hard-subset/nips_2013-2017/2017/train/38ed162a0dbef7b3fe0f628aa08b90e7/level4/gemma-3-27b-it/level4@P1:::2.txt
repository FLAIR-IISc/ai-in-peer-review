The primary focus of the paper is convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [15, 16]. In this manuscript, the authors analyze the stability (with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms, such as translation, in the sense of Eq. (4)) of the representation formed by CKNs. They show that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (conditions (A1-A2) in line 193), stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes (condition (A3)). An extension to locally compact groups is sketched in Section 4.

The paper is nicely organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from two exciting areas—deep networks and kernels. The stability result could be of interest to the machine learning community. 

However, the submission would benefit from additional motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, there is only a brief mention (lines 56-57: ‘Finally, we note that Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important for robustness to adversarial examples [8].’) which motivates the main contribution of the paper.

A few minor points could improve clarity: the use of κ in (A3) (line 193) might be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining kernel K in Eq. (10). In the equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and ∀v...') is superfluous; given the symmetry of kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ('∀u ...'). Line 416 lacks the definition of ϕ, which should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=⟨ϕ(z), ϕ(z')⟩<sub>H(K)</sub>]. Finally, in lines 427-428, the inequality under '=' seems to also hold with equality; | ||z|| - ||z|| |² should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |².

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous analysis of stability properties of CKNs and extends these results to a broader class of CNNs.
*   **Originality:** The work provides a novel kernel-based perspective on understanding the stability and invariance properties of deep convolutional architectures.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical concepts involved.
*   **Potential Significance:** The results could contribute to a better understanding of generalization in deep learning and potentially lead to improved regularization techniques.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Motivation:** The motivation for studying stability beyond its connection to adversarial examples could be strengthened.
*   **Minor Technical Points:** A few minor technical issues (as noted above) need to be addressed for clarity and correctness.



Overall, I believe this is a solid contribution with the potential to be valuable to the NIPS community, contingent on addressing the minor points raised above and strengthening the motivation. I would rate it as a **weak accept**.
