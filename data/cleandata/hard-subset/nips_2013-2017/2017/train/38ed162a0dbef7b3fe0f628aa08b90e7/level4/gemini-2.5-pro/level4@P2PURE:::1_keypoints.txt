- Paper shows deep Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) can be designed to be nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms.
- These properties result from careful design of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators.
- Under certain assumptions, the same invariance and stability properties extend to standard CNNs.
- The paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Main weakness: no empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis.
- Recommendation: include experiments demonstrating CKNs achieve claimed invariance and stability with appropriate settings.
- Reviewer asks whether alternative kernel approximations (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier series) could be used and how they would affect the presented stability guarantees.
