- Overall positive: paper is well-written and rigorous, characterizes DCNN-induced functional spaces via multilayer kernels, shows approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms, recommended for acceptance conditional on revisions.

- Primary contribution concern: authors should elaborate practical/theoretical benefits of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., generalization bounds, computational gains from kernel approximations) and clarify novelty beyond reformulation.

- Relation to prior work: explicitly discuss novelty relative to Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."

- "Not losing the signal information" claim needs justification: explain why preserving all signal information is desirable for tasks (like classification) where task-relevant compression is typical.

- Invariant-kernel connections: discuss potential links to the literature on invariant kernels (e.g., Hans Burkhardt).

- Equivariance question: clarify whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would suffice to ensure the DCNN architecture's equivariance to those transformations.
