This paper investigates appealing properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating their near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphic deformations when utilizing carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. The authors further establish that, under specific assumptions, these properties extend to standard convolutional neural networks (CNNs). 

Overall, the manuscript is clearly written and presents a technically rigorous analysis. A primary limitation is the absence of empirical validation. Demonstrating the theoretical findings – specifically, the invariance of appropriately configured CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms – through experimental results would significantly strengthen the work.

Additionally, the paper would benefit from a discussion of alternative kernel approximation techniques. Considering the potential of methods like random projections or explicit kernel mapping with a finite Fourier series basis, and analyzing their impact on stability, would be a valuable addition.
