- Presents a rigorous, well-written RKHS/multilayer-kernel formulation of DCNNs with elegant math, showing near-invariance to group transforms and stability to small diffeomorphisms.  
- The paper must better articulate the practical utility of the RKHS reformulation: what new insights, tighter generalization bounds, or computational benefits (e.g., kernel approximation) it enables.  
- Clarify novelty relative to prior work, especially a detailed comparison with Mairal et al.'s Convolutional Kernel Networks to delineate unique contributions.  
- Question the motivation for preserving full signal information—explain why this is desirable beyond reconstruction tasks and specify the intended problem classes.  
- Minor: discuss connections to the invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) to place the work in broader context.  
- Minor: comment on the generality of equivariance—would any operator commuting with the transformation group suffice to guarantee equivariance?  
- Recommendation: strong accept, contingent on addressing the above points to clarify practical implications, novelty, and motivation.
