The paper "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations" presents a theoretical analysis of the stability and invariance properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically those formed by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs). The authors' primary focus is on the stability of these representations with respect to C1 diffeomorphisms, such as translations, and they show that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels, stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from deep networks and kernels. The stability result is of interest to the machine learning community, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the topic.

One potential improvement is to add further motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, there is only a brief mention of the importance of Lipschitz stability in deep predictive models for robustness to adversarial examples. Providing more context and explanation for why stability analysis is crucial would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, there are a few minor issues with notation and formatting. The use of the symbol κ in assumption (A3) is overloaded, as it also denotes a function defining the kernel K in Equation (10). It would be clearer to use a different symbol for one of these uses. Furthermore, the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385 has a redundant part, and the definition of ϕ is missing in Equation (10). Lastly, the inequality on lines 427-428 can be written with equality, and the reference list is missing page information for several citations.

Overall, the paper provides a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional representations and their stability properties. With some minor revisions to address the mentioned issues, the paper can be even stronger.
