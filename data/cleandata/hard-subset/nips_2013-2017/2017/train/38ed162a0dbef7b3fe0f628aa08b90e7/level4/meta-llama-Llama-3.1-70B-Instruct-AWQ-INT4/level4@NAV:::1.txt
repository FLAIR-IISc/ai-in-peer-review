This paper investigates the desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), specifically their near invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphic actions, contingent upon the careful design of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. The authors further demonstrate that, under certain conditions, these favorable properties of CKNs can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper is well-structured and technically robust. However, a notable limitation is the absence of empirical experiments to corroborate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to include experimental results that substantiate the conclusion that CKNs, with suitable configurations, exhibit invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

An additional consideration is whether alternative approaches, such as random projection or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series, could also serve as viable solutions for kernel approximation. If so, it would be valuable to explore the impact of these methods on stability.
