This paper presents a theoretical analysis of the stability and invariance properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically those formed by convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). The authors study the geometry of the functional space associated with these representations, providing insights into their success and potential applications.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the subject matter. The authors combine ideas from deep networks and kernel methods, creating a unique and interesting contribution to the field. The stability result, which shows that CKNs can be stable to small deformations, is a valuable contribution to the machine learning community.

One of the strengths of the paper is its technical soundness. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the stability and invariance properties of CKNs, including a detailed discussion of the assumptions and limitations of their results. The paper also includes a clear and concise presentation of the mathematical tools and notation used throughout the analysis.

The authors' decision to extend their analysis from the Euclidean space to locally compact groups is a welcome addition, as it provides a more general framework for understanding the stability and invariance properties of CKNs.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One potential criticism is that the motivation for the stability analysis is not fully developed. While the authors mention the importance of Lipschitz stability in deep predictive models, they could provide more context and explanation for why this is a critical aspect of their research.

Additionally, there are a few minor issues with notation and formatting. For example, the use of the kappa notation in assumption (A3) is potentially confusing, as it is also used to define the kernel K in equation (10). Furthermore, the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385 contains a superfluous second part that is identical to the first constraint due to the symmetry of the kernel.

Finally, there are a few references that are missing page information, and one reference ([17]) appears to be incorrect.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning, and its technical soundness and clarity make it a worthwhile read for researchers interested in deep networks and kernel methods.
