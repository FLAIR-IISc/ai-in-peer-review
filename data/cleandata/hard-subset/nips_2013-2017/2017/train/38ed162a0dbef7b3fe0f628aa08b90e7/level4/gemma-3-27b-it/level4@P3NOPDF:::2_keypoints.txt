- Analyzes stability of Convolutional Kernel Network (CKN) representations to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) as formalized in Eq. (4).  
- Proves stability for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (conditions A1–A2) when appropriate patch sizes are chosen (condition A3).  
- Sketches extension of the stability results from Euclidean R^d to locally compact groups (Section 4).  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, bridging deep learning and kernel methods.  
- The stability result is potentially of significant interest to the machine learning community.  
- Motivation could be strengthened—expand the discussion of why Lipschitz stability is desirable, especially regarding adversarial robustness.  
- Notation issue: κ in condition (A3) conflicts with the kernel-defining function κ in Eq. (10); use a distinct symbol.  
- Redundant constraint between lines 384–385 due to kernel symmetry; remove the logically equivalent second part.  
- Missing definition of φ on line 416; introduce φ in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
