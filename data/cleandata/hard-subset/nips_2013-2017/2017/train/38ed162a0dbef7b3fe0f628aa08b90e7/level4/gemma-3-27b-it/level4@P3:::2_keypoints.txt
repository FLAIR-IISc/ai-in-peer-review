- Analyzes stability of Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) under C¹ diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows stability holds under kernel conditions: norm-preserving and non-expansive, with appropriate patch sizes.  
- Extends analysis to locally compact groups and outlines how to obtain group invariance.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining deep learning and kernel-methods insights.  
- Stability result offers valuable theoretical grounding for robustness of these models.  
- Recommendation to provide stronger motivation for stability beyond adversarial robustness (e.g., generalization, sample complexity).  
- Notation issue: κ in assumption (A3) conflicts with κ used for kernel K in Eq. (10).  
- Redundant condition: second part of equation between lines 384–385 unnecessary due to kernel symmetry.  
- Missing definition: φ not defined at line 416; should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.  
- Typo: inequality at lines 427–428 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |², not | ||z|| - ||z|| |².  
- Several references lack page numbers: [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25–27, 30, 31].  
- Specific references need updating/correction: [9], [17], [19], and [32] with the provided citations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous technical analysis, novelty in combining kernels and deep learning, clarity, and potential impact on generalization/regularization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited motivation beyond adversarial robustness, minor technical/notational issues, and lack of empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with significant potential; lean toward accept with minor revisions addressing motivation and technical details.
