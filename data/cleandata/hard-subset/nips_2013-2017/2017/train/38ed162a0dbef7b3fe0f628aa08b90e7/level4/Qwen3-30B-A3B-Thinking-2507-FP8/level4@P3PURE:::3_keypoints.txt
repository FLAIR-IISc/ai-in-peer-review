- Overall: well-written and rigorous demonstration that certain DCNN functional spaces can be described by multilayer kernels, showing (near) group invariance and diffeomorphism stability; recommend acceptance pending clarifications.  
- Main contribution: appears to be a reformulation of DCNNs in an RKHS framework; authors should state the practical/ theoretical utility of this reformulation.  
- Generalization: clarify whether the kernel formulation yields new insights or tighter generalization bounds for DCNNs.  
- Computational efficiency: clarify if implementing the architecture via standard kernel techniques or approximations is computationally advantageous.  
- Novelty: clarify how the work goes beyond prior art, especially Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (ICML 2014).  
- Signal preservation: explain why preserving "signal information" is critical, and contrast preserving all signal vs preserving only task-relevant information for tasks like classification.  
- Relation to prior invariant kernels: clarify connections (if any) to Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach (Invariant Kernel Methods, 2005).  
- Equivariance conditions: clarify whether any operator commuting with all group elements suffices for DCNN equivariance, or whether the specific patch/pooling/kernel maps defined are necessary.
