This paper studies convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14], analyzing the stability of their representations with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formalized in Eq. (4). The authors prove stability under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernel assumptions (A1–A2, line 193), provided patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3). They also sketch an extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and technically sound. It bridges deep learning and kernel methods, and the stability guarantees are of potential interest to the ML community.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Motivation: The paper would benefit from a stronger motivation for studying stability. Currently, the only explicit motivation appears in a brief remark on adversarial robustness (lines 56–57). Please expand on the practical and theoretical implications of Lipschitz stability for CKNs (e.g., invariance/robustness trade-offs, sample complexity, or links to generalization).
- Notation: The symbol kappa in (A3) (line 193) appears overloaded; it is also used as the function defining kernel K in Eq. (10). Consider disambiguating these uses to avoid confusion.
- Redundant constraint: In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second condition (“and ∀ v …”) is unnecessary; by symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first condition (“∀ u …”).
- Missing definition: Line 416 defines K via an inner product but does not introduce φ. Please define φ in Eq. (10), e.g., K(z, z′) = ⟨φ(z), φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.
- Typo/equality: Lines 427–428: the step labeled “=” appears to be an inequality that should actually be an equality. Also, there is a typo in the norm difference term: | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z′|| |^2.
