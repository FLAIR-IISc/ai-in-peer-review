- Paper analyzes translation-invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations built from Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) under diffeomorphisms.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, effectively combining deep network and kernel-method ideas.  
- Main technical contribution: a stability result showing CKNs can achieve stability to diffeomorphisms under certain conditions.  
- Provides a promising framework to extend results to locally compact groups.  
- Technically sound and of interest to the machine learning community.  
- Lacks sufficient motivation and context for why Lipschitz/stability analysis matters (e.g., robustness to adversarial examples).  
- Minor notation/formatting issues reduce clarity (e.g., κ used for both a kernel-defining function and a constant in assumption A3).  
- Some displayed equations could be formatted more clearly for readability.  
- Originality: presents a novel theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representation stability.  
- Significance: makes a valuable contribution with potential to be a strong paper after revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid analysis and promising extension framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs stronger motivation and minor fixes to notation/formatting.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address motivation and clarity issues.
