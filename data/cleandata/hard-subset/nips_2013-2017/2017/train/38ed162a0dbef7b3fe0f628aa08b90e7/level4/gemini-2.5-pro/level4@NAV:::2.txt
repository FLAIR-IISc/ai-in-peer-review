This paper centers on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14], providing a stability analysis for the representations they learn. Specifically, the authors investigate stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formally defined in Eq. (4). The key finding is that for kernels that are norm-preserving and non-expansive (conditions A1-A2, line 193), stability is guaranteed when patch sizes are selected appropriately (condition A3). Furthermore, an extension of this framework from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The manuscript is well-structured, lucid, and technically rigorous, effectively synthesizing concepts from the fields of deep learning and kernel methods. The theoretical result on stability is a valuable contribution that should be of significant interest to the machine learning community.

- The paper could be strengthened by expanding on the motivation for conducting the stability analysis. The current justification is confined to a single sentence (lines 56-57) linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness, and a more detailed discussion would better frame the main contribution.
- The reuse of the symbol \kappa in assumption (A3) (line 193) may cause confusion for the reader, as this notation is also used to denote the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).
- In the equation displayed between lines 384 and 385, the second part of the constraint ("and \forall v...") appears to be redundant. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, this condition is identical to the first constraint ("\forall u ...").
- The feature map \phi, used in line 416, is not formally defined. It would be beneficial to introduce it in the context of Eq. (10), for instance, as K(z,z') = <\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}.
- On lines 427-428, the inequality beneath the equality sign likely holds with equality. Additionally, there appears to be a typo in the expression | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2, which should read | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
