This paper presents a well-written and rigorous contribution, successfully demonstrating how certain functional spaces implemented by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. The authors also highlight the desirable properties of this architecture, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. Based on the comments below, I recommend acceptance of this paper.

The primary contribution of this paper, in my understanding, is the reformulation of CNN architecture in the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). To strengthen the manuscript, the authors should elaborate on why this reformulation is useful. For instance, they could discuss how the kernel formulation may lead to interesting results on generalization bounds or whether it is computationally more efficient to implement using standard techniques and approximations on kernels. Furthermore, it is essential to clarify whether the novelty of this approach extends beyond a simple reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS.

The authors should also compare their work to the paper "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid, and discuss the novelty of their approach with respect to this existing work.

The authors emphasize the importance of preserving "signal information." However, this is not entirely clear, as DCNNs are often used for tasks such as image classification, where only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. The authors should provide more context or justification for this requirement.

Regarding group invariance, it would be beneficial to explore any connections with the invariant kernel approach adopted by Hans Burkhardt. This could provide additional insights into the proposed method and its relationship to existing work in the field.

Finally, the authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, ensuring the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations. It would be interesting to know whether any other operator, within the set of operators that commute with all group elements, could also ensure this equivariance property.

Overall, this paper provides a valuable contribution to the field, and addressing these comments will further strengthen the manuscript.
