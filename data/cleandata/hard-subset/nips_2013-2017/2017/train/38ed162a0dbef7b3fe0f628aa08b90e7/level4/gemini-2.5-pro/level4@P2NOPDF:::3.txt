This paper presents a well-written and rigorous theoretical analysis of Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs). The authors successfully demonstrate that the functional spaces implemented by DCNNs can be characterized using multilayer kernels. A key contribution is showing that this architecture possesses desirable properties, such as near-invariance to group transformations and stability with respect to diffeomorphisms.

I recommend acceptance for this paper, contingent on the authors addressing the following points to further strengthen the manuscript:

*   **Clarification of Contribution and Novelty:** The central contribution appears to be the reformulation of the DCNN architecture within the framework of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). To strengthen the paper's impact, I encourage the authors to explicitly discuss the practical or theoretical benefits of this reformulation. For instance, could this kernel-based perspective lead to new insights on generalization bounds, or could it enable more computationally efficient implementations through standard kernel approximation techniques? This leads to a broader question about the novelty: does the contribution extend beyond a formal re-characterization of DCNNs? Clarifying the downstream implications would significantly enhance the paper. Additionally, the authors should clearly delineate the novelty of their work with respect to the "Convolutional Kernel Networks" paper by Mairal et al. A more detailed comparison in the related work section would be beneficial.

*   **The Role of Signal Information Preservation:** The paper emphasizes the importance of preserving signal information. While this is crucial for tasks like signal reconstruction, many common applications of DCNNs, such as image classification, primarily require the preservation of task-relevant discriminative information, not the entire signal. Could the authors comment on this distinction and clarify why complete signal preservation is highlighted as a key objective in the context of these applications?

*   **Connection to Invariant Kernels:** Concerning the discussion on group invariance, it would be valuable to connect the proposed framework to the established literature on invariant kernels, such as the work by Hans Burkhardt. A brief discussion on the relationship or differences between these approaches would provide helpful context for the reader.

*   **Generalizing the Equivariance Condition:** The paper establishes equivariance by defining patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. This raises a theoretical question: does any operator that commutes with all elements of the transformation group suffice to ensure the equivariance property of the DCNN architecture? A comment on the generality of this condition would be an interesting addition to the theoretical discussion.
