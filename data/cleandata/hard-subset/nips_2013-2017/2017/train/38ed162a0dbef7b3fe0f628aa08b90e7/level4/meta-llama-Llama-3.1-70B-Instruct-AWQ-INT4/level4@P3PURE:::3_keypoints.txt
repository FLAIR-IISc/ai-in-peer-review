- Paper is well-written and rigorous, demonstrates DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels, and shows near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms; recommend acceptance if comments addressed.

- Main contribution framed as a reformulation of CNNs in RKHS; authors should explain why this reformulation is useful (e.g., impact on generalization bounds or practical benefits).

- Clarify whether the approach yields novelty beyond a mere RKHS reformulation of DCNNs.

- Provide more context and comparison to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.) to clarify novelty/relations.

- Clarify why preserving “signal information” without loss is crucial, given many tasks only require task-relevant information (e.g., classification).

- Explore potential connections with invariant-kernel approaches such as Hans Burkhardt’s work.

- Investigate whether other operators that commute with all group elements (beyond their defined patch/pooling/kernel maps) could also ensure equivariance.
