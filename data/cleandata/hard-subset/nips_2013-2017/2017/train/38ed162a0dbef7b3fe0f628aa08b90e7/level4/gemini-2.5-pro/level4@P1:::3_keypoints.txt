- Presents an RKHS-based theoretical framework for multilayer convolutional kernels that encompasses deep CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Proves representation properties: near-invariance to group transformations, stability to small diffeomorphisms, and preservation of signal information.  
- Extends Convolutional Kernel Networks and scattering-transform stability analysis to a broader, more common class of CNN architectures.  
- Paper is well-written, technically rigorous, and results are well-supported.  
- Recommendation: accept, though several clarifications would strengthen the manuscript.  
- Clarify practical and theoretical benefits of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computationally efficient training).  
- Explicitly state novelties over prior work (especially Mairal et al.'s CKN) beyond extending stability and invariance results.  
- Justify the emphasis on preserving signal information when many DCNN tasks aim to discard task-irrelevant information.  
- Connect the group invariance discussion to the broader invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Haasdonk and Burkhardt).  
- Minor query: comment on whether any operator commuting with all group elements would suffice to guarantee equivariance of the resulting DCNN layers.
