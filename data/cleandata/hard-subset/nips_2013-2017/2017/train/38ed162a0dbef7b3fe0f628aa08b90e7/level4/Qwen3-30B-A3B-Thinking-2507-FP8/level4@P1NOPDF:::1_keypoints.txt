- Paper shows CKNs can achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.  
- Theoretical results are extended to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technically sound.  
- Major limitation: no empirical validation provided to support the theoretical claims.  
- Reviewer requests experiments measuring invariance/stability under controlled transformations to substantiate theory.  
- Reviewer asks whether kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier feature maps) could be used and how such choices would affect network stability, suggesting this be addressed to improve practical relevance.
