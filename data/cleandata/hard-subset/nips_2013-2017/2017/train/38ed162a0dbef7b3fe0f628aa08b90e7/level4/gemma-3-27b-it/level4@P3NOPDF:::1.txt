## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission

This paper investigates appealing theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that, under specific design choices for patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators, CKNs exhibit near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphic deformations. The authors further establish that, with certain assumptions, these beneficial properties extend to conventional convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper presents a well-written and technically rigorous analysis. The theoretical results are clearly stated and appear sound. The connection drawn between CKN properties and those of standard CNNs is a particularly interesting contribution. However, a significant weakness is the complete absence of empirical validation. 

While the theoretical framework is compelling, the claims of invariance and stability are difficult to assess without supporting experimental evidence. I strongly recommend that the authors include experiments demonstrating these properties in CKNs – and, by extension, CNNs – with carefully chosen parameter settings. Specifically, experiments quantifying invariance to known transformations (e.g., rotations, translations, scaling) and robustness to diffeomorphic deformations (e.g., elastic deformations) would greatly strengthen the paper. Visualizations of the learned kernels or feature maps under these transformations could also be insightful.

A related question concerns the potential for alternative kernel approximation methods. The paper focuses on a specific kernel mapping operator. It is worth considering whether other techniques, such as random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series terms, could also achieve similar stability and invariance. If these alternatives are viable, a discussion of their comparative advantages and disadvantages, particularly regarding computational cost and stability, would enrich the analysis. Specifically, how does the choice of kernel approximation method impact the observed stability properties?

In summary, this paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to understanding the properties of deep convolutional networks. Addressing the lack of empirical validation and considering alternative kernel approximation techniques would significantly enhance the paper's impact and persuasiveness. I believe that with these revisions, this work has the potential to be a strong contribution to the NIPS conference.
