

The paper investigates notable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that CKNs achieve near invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms when appropriately designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are employed. The authors further establish that these properties extend to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) under specific assumptions. Overall, the paper is well written and technically sound. However, a significant limitation is the absence of empirical validation for the theoretical claims. The authors should include experimental results demonstrating the claimed invariance to transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms for CKNs with the proposed operator designs. Additionally, it is unclear whether random projections or explicit kernel mappings using a finite number of Fourier series terms could serve as viable alternatives for kernel approximation. If applicable, the impact of such approximations on stability should be analyzed.
