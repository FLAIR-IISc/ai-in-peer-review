The research paper discusses some notable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks. That is, Ckns are almost invariant to transformations and stable for the operation of deforming transformations, provided the appropriate patch extraction operators, kernel mapping operators, and pooling operators are carefully designed. The authors further show that, under certain assumptions, the notable properties obtained from Ckns are also applicable to Cnn.

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The only weakness is that the paper does not provide any empirical tests to prove the validity of the theoretical analysis. I would like to see some experimental results that lead to the conclusion that Ckns are invariant with suitable settings for transformations and deforming transformations.

Another question is, can a random projection or a specific number of kernel mappings of a Fourier series be a possible solution for kernel approximation? If so, what is its impact on stability?
