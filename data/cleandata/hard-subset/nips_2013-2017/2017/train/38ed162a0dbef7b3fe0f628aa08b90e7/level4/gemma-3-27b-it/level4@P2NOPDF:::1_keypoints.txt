- Paper shows Deep Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) have near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphic deformations with specific patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling designs.  
- Authors prove, under certain assumptions, that these invariance and stability properties can extend to standard CNNs.  
- Manuscript is technically sound and well written.  
- Main weakness: lacks empirical validation demonstrating the theoretical invariance/stability claims.  
- Recommendation: include experiments showing CKNs (with proposed operators) are invariant/stable to transformations and diffeomorphisms.  
- Suggestion: explore alternative kernel approximation methods (random projections, finite Fourier series) and analyze/compare their effects on stability and trade-offs versus the CKN approach.
