- Analyzes theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel representations (CKNs).  
- Shows that with carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling, CKNs achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Proves these properties extend to a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations under appropriate assumptions.  
- Paper is clearly written and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: lack of empirical validation supporting the theoretical results.  
- Suggestion: include experiments demonstrating that CKNs configured as proposed exhibit the stated invariance and stability to deformations.  
- Open question: could random feature mappings (e.g., random Fourier features) or other finite-dimensional embeddings replace the proposed approximation, and what would be the impact on stability guarantees?
