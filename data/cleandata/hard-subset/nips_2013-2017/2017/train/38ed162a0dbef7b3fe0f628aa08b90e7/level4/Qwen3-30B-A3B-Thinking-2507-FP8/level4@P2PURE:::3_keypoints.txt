- Positive: Paper is well written, rigorous, and characterizes functional spaces of DCNNs via multilayer kernels, showing near invariance to group actions and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Clarify why reformulating DCNNs in an RKHS framework is useful—does it enable new generalization bounds or computational advantages via kernel techniques/approximations?  
- State whether the novelty goes beyond an RKHS reformulation or is primarily conceptual, and clearly distinguish the contribution from Mairal et al.’s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."  
- Justify the emphasis on “not losing signal information,” given that many DCNN applications (e.g., classification) require preserving only task-relevant information, not full signal reconstruction.  
- Explain any connection between the paper’s group invariance results and Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach.  
- On commuting operators: clarify whether any operator that commutes with the group elements suffices for equivariance, or if the specific patch, pooling, and kernel-map operators used are uniquely necessary.
