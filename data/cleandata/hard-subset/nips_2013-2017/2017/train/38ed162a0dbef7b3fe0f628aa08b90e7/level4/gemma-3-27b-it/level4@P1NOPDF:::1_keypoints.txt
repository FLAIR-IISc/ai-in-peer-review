- Shows deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.

- Proves that, under certain assumptions, these invariance and stability properties can extend to conventional CNNs.

- Paper is well-written and technically sound.

- Primary weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis; needs experiments demonstrating claimed invariance of configured CKNs.

- Raises question whether alternative kernel approximation methods (random projections or finite Fourier series) could be viable and how they would impact stability.
