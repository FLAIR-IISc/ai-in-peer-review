- Investigates theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations: invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Extends Mallat’s scattering transform to a general multilayer kernel-based framework that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Main contribution: proves stability of representations under diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information and shows applicability to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Bridges kernel methods and deep learning theory, providing a principled regularization perspective on CNNs.  
- Establishes a connection between the RKHS norm of the representation and both stability and generalization/sample-complexity.  
- Shows how to design patch extraction and pooling operators that commute with group actions, generalizing rotation/translation/reflection invariance.  
- Technically sound and well-organized with rigorous proofs and appropriate mathematical tools.  
- Framework encompasses both CKNs and a broad class of CNNs, extending stability results to standard networks.  
- Results link to practical concerns such as robustness to adversarial examples.  
- Acknowledges limitation that representations do not preserve norm (unlike scattering), mitigated for natural low-frequency signals.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear for a theoretical contribution, though could use more intuitive explanation of RKHS-generalization connection.  
- Original and significant: unifies prior invariance and kernel-based deep learning work into a comprehensive theoretical framework.  
- Main weakness: lack of empirical validation demonstrating claimed invariance and stability in practice.  
- Omits discussion of alternative kernel approximations (e.g., random Fourier features) and their impact on stability vs. computational trade-offs.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that would be strengthened by experimental results.
