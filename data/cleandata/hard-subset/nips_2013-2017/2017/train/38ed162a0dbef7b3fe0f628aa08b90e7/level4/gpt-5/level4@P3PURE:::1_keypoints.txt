- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Shows that with carefully designed patch-extraction, kernel-mapping, and pooling, CKNs are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms.  
- Argues that, under certain assumptions, these invariance and stability properties extend to conventional CNNs.  
- Paper is well written and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: absence of empirical validation to support the theoretical claims.  
- Recommends experiments demonstrating that CKNs (with appropriate settings) exhibit the claimed invariance and stability.  
- Questions whether kernel approximations (random projections or finite Fourier features) could be practical alternatives and how they would affect the stability guarantees.
