- Shows CKNs are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.
- Proves that, under certain assumptions, these invariance and stability properties can extend to CNNs.
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Primary weakness: lacks empirical validation of the theoretical analysis.
- Recommends experiments demonstrating CKN invariance/stability to transformations and diffeomorphisms with appropriate settings.
- Raises question about alternative kernel approximations (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier mappings) and how they would affect observed stability.
