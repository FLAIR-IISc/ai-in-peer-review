

The primary contribution of the paper is the analysis of stability [with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formalized in Equation (4)] for representations learned by convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors demonstrate that stability holds under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (satisfying conditions (A1)-(A2) at line 193) when patch sizes are appropriately chosen (satisfying condition (A3)). The extension to locally compact groups is briefly outlined in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively bridging two promising areas: deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result holds potential interest for the machine learning community.

Major concerns requiring revision:
- The motivation for the stability analysis is insufficient. Currently, only a single sentence (lines 56–57: "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].") justifies the main contribution.
- The \kappa notation is overloaded in condition (A3) (line 193), where it also denotes the function defining kernel K in Equation (10).
- The constraint "and \forall v..." in the displayed equation between lines 384–385 is redundant; due to kernel symmetry, it is identical to the preceding constraint "\forall u...".
- The definition of \phi is missing at line 416; it should be introduced in Equation (10) as \( \langle \phi(z), \phi(z') \rangle_{H(K)} \).
- Lines 427–428 contain an error: the expression \( | \|z\| - \|z\| |^2 \) should be \( | \|z\| - \|z'\| |^2 \), and the inequality under "=" likely holds with equality.
