- Demonstrates CKNs achieve near invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms with appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Shows these stability/invariance properties can extend to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Theoretical contributions are well-motivated, technically rigorous, and clearly written.  
- Primary limitation: no empirical validation of the theoretical claims (no experiments, visualizations, or quantitative metrics).  
- Recommends experiments demonstrating CKN stability under controlled deformations/transformations to bridge theory and practice for a NIPS submission.  
- Secondary concern: unclear whether implicit kernel mapping is necessary—need discussion of explicit approximations (e.g., random Fourier features) and conditions preserving guarantees.  
- Ask authors to address trade-offs in performance and computational efficiency for kernel approximations and provide experimental evidence in a revision.
