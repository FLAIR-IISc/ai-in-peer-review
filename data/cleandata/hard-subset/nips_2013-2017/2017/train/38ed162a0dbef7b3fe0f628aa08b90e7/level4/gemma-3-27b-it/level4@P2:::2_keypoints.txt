- Paper analyzes stability of Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to C^1 diffeomorphisms and provides a bound similar to Eq. (4).
- Stability is shown under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernel assumptions (A1–A2) and appropriate patch size selection (A3); extension to locally compact groups is sketched.
- Well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound exposition.
- Strong theoretical foundation drawing from kernel methods and harmonic analysis.
- Clear connection to prior work in kernel methods and deep learning.
- Results potentially generalize beyond CKNs to a broader class of CNNs.
- Novel insights into the geometry of functional spaces underlying CNNs.
- Motivation for the stability analysis is limited and could be expanded, especially beyond a brief adversarial robustness mention.
- Notational ambiguity: κ in (A3) conflicts with κ as the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10).
- Redundant equation part (lines 384–385) due to kernel symmetry.
- Missing definition of φ near line 416; it should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <φ(z), φ(z')>_{H(K)}.
- Typo in inequality around lines 427–428; second term should read | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
- Several references lack page numbers: [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25–27, 30, 31].
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical analysis, sound proofs, extension of scattering transform results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation, minor notational/errors, and insufficient elaboration of practical implications.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address motivation, notation, redundancy, missing definition, typo, and incomplete references.
