The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how certain functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. It also highlights the desirable properties of the architecture, including near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance of the paper, provided the authors address the following comments.

While the paper's main contribution is a reformulation of CNN architectures within the context of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), it would be beneficial to include additional comments explaining why this reformulation is useful. For instance, does the kernel formulation lead to interesting results on generalization bounds? Are there computational efficiency gains when implementing standard techniques or approximations on kernels? Furthermore, it is essential to clarify whether the novelty of the approach extends beyond a mere reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS.

Additionally, the authors should provide more context regarding the novelty of their approach compared to the work presented in "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid.

The authors emphasize the importance of preserving "signal information" without loss. However, it is not entirely clear why this is crucial. DCNNs are often employed in tasks such as image classification, where only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. The authors should comment on this and provide further clarification.

Regarding group invariance, it would be interesting to explore potential connections with the invariant kernel approach adopted by Hans Burkhardt.

Finally, as a point of curiosity, the authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, ensuring the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations. It would be worth investigating whether any other operators that commute with all group elements could also ensure this equivariance property.
