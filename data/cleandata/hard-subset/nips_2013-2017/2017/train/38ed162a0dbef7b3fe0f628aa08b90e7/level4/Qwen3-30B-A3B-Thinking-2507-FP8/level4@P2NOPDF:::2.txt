

The paper investigates the stability of representations formed by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14] with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formalized in Equation (4). The authors establish that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions (A1)-(A2)) when using appropriately chosen patch sizes (assumption (A3)). The extension of these results to locally compact groups is briefly sketched in Section 4. The work is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively bridging insights from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability analysis presents a potentially valuable contribution for the machine learning community.

However, the manuscript would benefit from stronger motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only justification (lines 56-57) states: "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7]." This single sentence inadequately contextualizes the significance of the paper's core contribution. A more substantial discussion of why stability under diffeomorphisms is relevant to practical ML tasks (e.g., robustness, generalization, or specific applications like image analysis) would significantly strengthen the paper.

Several technical points require clarification or correction:

1.  **Notation Overloading (Line 193):** The symbol $\kappa$ is overloaded in assumption (A3). It is used both to denote a kernel function (as in Equation (10)) and a constant parameter in (A3). This is likely confusing for readers. The constant parameter should be denoted with a distinct symbol (e.g., $\kappa_0$).

2.  **Redundant Constraint (Equation between Lines 384-385):** The constraint "and $\forall v \in \mathbb{R}^d$..." is superfluous. Due to the symmetry of kernel $k$, this condition is identical to the preceding constraint "$\forall u \in \mathbb{R}^d$...". It should be removed.

3.  **Missing Definition (Line 416):** The definition of $\phi$ is absent. The inner product $\langle \phi(z), \phi(z') \rangle_{H(K)}$ in Equation (10) requires $\phi$ to be defined. The authors should explicitly state the definition of $\phi$ (e.g., as the feature map associated with kernel $K$) within Equation (10) or an immediately preceding line.

4.  **Typographical Error (Lines 427-428):** The inequality contains a typo: $| \|\mathbf{z}\| - \|\mathbf{z}\| |^2$ should be $| \|\mathbf{z}\| - \|\mathbf{z}'\| |^2$. The expression as written is always zero, contradicting the context of the inequality.

Additionally, the reference list requires updates:

*   References [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25-27, 30, 31] are missing page numbers.
*   Reference [9] should be updated to: Amit Daniely, Roy Frostig, Yoram Singer. Toward Deeper Understanding of Neural Networks: The Power of Initialization and a Dual View on Expressivity. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS)*, pages 2253–2261, 2016.
*   Reference [17] should be updated to: Krikamol Muandet, Kenji Fukumizu, Bharath Sriperumbudur, Bernhard Schölkopf. Kernel Mean Embedding of Distributions: A Review and Beyond. *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning*, 10(1-2): 1–141, 2017.
*   Reference [19] should be updated to: Anant Raj, Abhishek Kumar, Youssef Mroueh, Tom Fletcher, Bernhard Schölkopf. *International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Statistics (AISTATS)*, PMLR 54: 1225–1235, 2017.
*   Reference [32] should be updated to reflect its acceptance: Yuchen Zhang, Percy Liang, Martin Wainwright. Convexified Convolutional Neural Networks. *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)*, 2017. (Accepted; available at https://2017.icml.cc/Conferences/2017/Schedule?type=Poster).

The paper presents a technically sound contribution to the understanding of CKN stability. Addressing the motivation gap, the notation issue, the redundant constraint, the missing definition, the typo, and the reference list will substantially improve the paper's clarity and impact. The core result is interesting and merits publication upon these revisions.
