- Shows CKNs can be near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms with appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Claims these invariance and stability properties can extend to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and situates contributions within scattering transforms, CKNs, and group invariance literature.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis using kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  
- Draws interesting connections between the kernel framework and CNNs, suggesting broader applicability of stability results.  
- Main weakness: no empirical validation to support theoretical claims.  
- Recommends experiments on benchmark datasets to demonstrate the proposed invariance and stability in practice.  
- Suggests discussing alternative kernel-approximation approaches (e.g., random projections, explicit Fourier-feature mappings).  
- Requests analysis of trade-offs between approximation methods, especially their impact on stability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novelty in extending scattering theory to CNNs via kernels, significance for understanding network geometry, and overall clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of experiments and limited discussion of alternative approximation techniques.
