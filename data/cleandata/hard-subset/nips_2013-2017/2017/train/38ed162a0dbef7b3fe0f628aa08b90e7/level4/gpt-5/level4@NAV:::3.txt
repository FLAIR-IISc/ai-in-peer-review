The manuscript is clearly written and rigorous, and it convincingly demonstrates how certain function spaces realized by DCNNs can be characterized through multilayer kernels. It further shows that the architecture enjoys desirable properties: (near) invariance to group actions and stability under diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance subject to the comments below:
* To my understanding, the main contribution is a reformulation of CNN architectures within the RKHS framework. Please add remarks explaining why this perspective is or will be useful (e.g., might the kernel viewpoint enable new generalization bounds? does it bring computational benefits via standard kernel techniques/approximations?). More broadly, does the novelty go beyond an RKHS-based re-expression of DCNNs? Also, please clarify the contribution relative to the paper “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid.
* The manuscript emphasizes “not losing the signal information.” Why is this essential? DCNNs are typically used for downstream tasks (e.g., image classification). Unless the objective is signal reconstruction, only task-relevant information needs to be retained. Please comment on this point.
* Regarding group invariance: is there any connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methodology?
* A curiosity: you define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. Would any other operator from the set of operators that commute with all group elements likewise ensure equivariance of the DCNN with respect to those group transformations?
