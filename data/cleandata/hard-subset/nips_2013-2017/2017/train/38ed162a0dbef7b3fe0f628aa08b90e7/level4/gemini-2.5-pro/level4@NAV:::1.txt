This paper establishes key properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating their near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms. The authors show that these properties are contingent upon the careful design of the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. Furthermore, the analysis is extended to show that under certain assumptions, these desirable stability and invariance characteristics also hold for conventional Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs).

Overall, the submission is clearly articulated and its technical contributions appear solid. The primary limitation, however, is the absence of empirical validation for its theoretical claims. The work would be significantly strengthened by experiments that demonstrate the central conclusion: that CKNs, when configured with the proposed operators, indeed exhibit the claimed invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.

A further point for clarification concerns the choice of kernel approximation. Could alternative methods, such as random projections or explicit kernel maps using a finite number of Fourier features, also serve as viable candidates? If these approaches are applicable, it would be insightful to understand how their use might impact the demonstrated stability properties of the network.
