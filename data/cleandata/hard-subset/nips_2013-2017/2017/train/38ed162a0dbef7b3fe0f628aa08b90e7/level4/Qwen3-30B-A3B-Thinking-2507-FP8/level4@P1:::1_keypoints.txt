- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations: near-invariance to groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernel framework from CKNs to define a functional space that includes CKNs and CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering transform to provide stability guarantees under diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Formulates a rigorous RKHS-based framework to analyze invariance properties of convolutional representations.  
- Shows stability of kernel representations can be transferred to predictive models by controlling the RKHS norm, connecting stability to generalization.  
- Demonstrates that many standard CNN architectures fall within the proposed functional space via homogeneous activation functions.  
- Strong technical development: precise multilayer kernel construction, signal-preserving layer maps, and explicit stability bounds involving layers and pooling.  
- Extends analysis to group invariance and provides operator design for equivariance to transformation groups.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with consistent notation and apparently sound proofs (appendices contain detailed proofs).  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical validation—no experiments, visualizations, or quantitative demonstrations of claimed invariance/stability.  
- Needs discussion of kernel approximation methods (e.g., random Fourier features) and whether they preserve theoretical guarantees or introduce errors.  
- Overall: significant theoretical advance connecting scattering theory and practical CNNs, meriting consideration despite missing experimental support.
