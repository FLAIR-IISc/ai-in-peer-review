1. Overall assessment: well-written, rigorous paper showing DCNN functional spaces described by multilayer kernels with demonstrated approximate group invariance and diffeomorphism stability; recommend acceptance if comments addressed.

2. Primary contribution/novelty: paper reformulates CNNs in an RKHS framework — authors should clarify why this is useful beyond a reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds, computational efficiency via kernel approximations).

3. Relation to prior work: clarify novelty relative to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.

4. Signal preservation claim: the emphasis on "not losing the signal information" needs justification since tasks like classification may require discarding irrelevant signal components.

5. Relation to invariant kernels: ask whether there is any connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approaches.

6. Equivariance clarification: ask if any operator that commutes with all group elements would suffice to ensure the DCNN’s equivariance to those group transformations.
