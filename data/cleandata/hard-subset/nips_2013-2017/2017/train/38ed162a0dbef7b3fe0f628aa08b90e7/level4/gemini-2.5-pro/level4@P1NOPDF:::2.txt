This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs). Specifically, the authors investigate stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translations), as defined in Eq. (4). They demonstrate that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1-A2), this stability is guaranteed when using appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3). The authors also sketch an extension of their analysis from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

This is a well-organized and clearly written paper that is technically sound. It effectively combines ideas from two exciting areas—deep networks and kernel methods—and the presented stability result should be of significant interest to the machine learning community.

The submission could be improved by addressing the following points:

*   **Motivation:** The paper would be strengthened by expanding the motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the primary justification is a single sentence (lines 56-57) referencing the importance of Lipschitz stability for robustness against adversarial examples [7]. Providing more context on why this specific form of stability is important for CKNs would make the contribution more compelling.

*   **Notation:** The overloading of the \kappa notation is potentially confusing. It is used in assumption (A3) on line 193, but it also denotes the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10). Using a different symbol for one of these would improve clarity.

*   **Redundant Constraint:** In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint ("and \forall v...") appears to be superfluous. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, this constraint is identical to the first ("\forall u ...").

*   **Missing Definition:** The feature map \phi is used on line 416 without a formal definition. It would be helpful to introduce it alongside the kernel definition in Eq. (10), for example, by specifying that K(z, z') = <\phi(z), \phi(z')>_{H(K)}.

*   **Typo:** There appears to be a minor typo in the inequality between lines 427-428. The term | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should likely be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2. Furthermore, the inequality under the equality sign seems to hold with equality.
