The paper is clearly written and rigorous. It convincingly shows that certain function spaces realized by DCNNs can be characterized via multilayer kernels, and that the architecture enjoys desirable properties: (near) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, subject to the clarifications below:

- My understanding is that the main contribution is a reformulation of DCNN architectures within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) framework. Please explain why this perspective is useful: for instance, can the kernel viewpoint lead to improved generalization bounds, new regularization insights, or sample complexity results? Are there computational advantages from leveraging standard kernel methods or approximations (e.g., Nyström, random features), making certain implementations more efficient? More broadly, does the novelty extend beyond the RKHS reformulation?

- Please clarify the relationship to and distinctions from Mairal, Koniusz, Harchaoui, and Schmid’s “Convolutional Kernel Networks.” In what ways does your approach differ theoretically or practically, and what new results are enabled here that were not covered there?

- The paper emphasizes “not losing signal information.” Why is this essential? In many applications (e.g., classification), preserving only task-relevant information may be sufficient unless the objective includes reconstruction or invertibility. Please discuss the motivation for information preservation in your framework and how it benefits downstream tasks or theoretical guarantees.

- Regarding group invariance: is there a connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methodology? If so, please outline the conceptual and technical links, and clarify what your framework adds beyond existing invariant kernel constructions.

- You define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. More generally, would any operator that commutes with all group elements ensure equivariance of the DCNN to those transformations? If not, what additional conditions (e.g., locality, stability/Lipschitz properties, or compatibility across layers) are required to guarantee equivariance and the reported stability results?
