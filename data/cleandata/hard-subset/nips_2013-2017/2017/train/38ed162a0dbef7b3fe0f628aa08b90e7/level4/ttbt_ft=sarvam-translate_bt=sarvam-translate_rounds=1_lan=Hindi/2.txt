This paper primarily focuses on CKN (Convolutional Kernel Network) [13, 14]. In this manuscript, the authors analyze the stability of the representations generated by CKN [w.r.t. C^1 deformation (e.g., translation), in the sense of equation (4)]. They demonstrate that for norm-preserving and non-expensive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193], stability is maintained for appropriately chosen patch sizes [(A3)]. Section 4 provides a sketch of the extension from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups.

This paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from two exciting fields (deep networks and kernels). The stability results may be of interest to the ML community.

- The presentation would benefit from adding more motivation to the stability analysis. Currently, there is only one short sentence (lines 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Dirichlet stability of deep predictive models was found to be important for achieving robustness to adversarial examples.' [7]), which motivates the main contribution of this paper.
- Overloading the \kappa notation in (A3) [line 193] can be misleading, as it also denotes the function defining the kernel K in equation (10).
- In the equation displayed between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is unnecessary; given the symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first constraint ('\forall u ...').
- Line 416: The definition of \phi is missing; it should be introduced in equation (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}]
- Lines 427-428: The inequality under '=' seems to apply even with equality, | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
