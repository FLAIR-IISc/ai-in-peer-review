- Paper analyzes multilayer convolutional kernel representations (generalizing CKNs), defines feature map Φn via patch extraction, non-expansive kernel maps, and Gaussian pooling.  
- Proves a signal preservation lemma showing information is retained and recoverable from discrete sampling, motivating a practical discretization.  
- Approximates kernel maps by finite-dimensional projections to obtain CKN architectures while preserving non-expansiveness.  
- Main stability theorem (Theorem 6): Φ is stable to C1 diffeomorphisms with bound ‖Φ(Lτx)−Φ(x)‖ ≤ (C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + (C2/σn)‖τ‖∞)‖x‖, so larger final pooling scale σn improves translation invariance.  
- Discusses norm loss due to pooling and shows conditions under which most energy is retained and how a final Gaussian kernel layer can restore unit norm while preserving stability.  
- Extends construction from (Rd,+) to general locally compact groups, achieving intermediate equivariance and global invariance via last pooling.  
- Shows the RKHS of the multilayer kernel contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations and provides RKHS norm bounds linking stability to generalization.  
- Builds on CKNs and scattering transform analyses, relating to group-equivariant CNNs and invariant kernels while separating representation from prediction.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized, technically sound work bridging kernels and deep networks with a general stability result applicable to CKNs and some CNNs.  
- Strength: Clean separation of representation/prediction and RKHS-based control of stability with implications for generalization/sample complexity.  
- Strength: Neat group-theoretic extension capturing equivariance and invariance.  
- Weakness: Motivation for Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms is underdeveloped; needs stronger empirical/architectural justification (e.g., robustness, domain shifts, sample complexity).  
- Weakness: Notation clash—κ is overloaded in assumption (A3) and as the kernel function in Eq. (10); rename one.  
- Weakness: Redundant constraint in displayed equation between lines 384–385; second condition is superfluous by symmetry.  
- Weakness: φ is undefined at line 416 and should be introduced via K(z,z′)=⟨φ(z),φ(z′)⟩H(K).  
- Weakness: Typo in inequality lines 427–428—equality issue and term |‖z‖−‖z‖|2 should be |‖z‖−‖z′‖|2.  
- Weakness: References need page numbers and updates for several citations (specific fixes listed).  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled, general stability result with solid theoretical contributions and useful RKHS-generalization link.  
- Cons for acceptance: Central motivation underdeveloped and multiple minor presentation issues (notation, definitions, typos, reference details) needing fixes.  
- Overall: Strong, timely theoretical paper that extends scattering guarantees to broader convolutional architectures; recommend accept after improved motivation and minor presentation corrections.
