This paper presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their stability to transformations and diffeomorphisms. The authors show that CKNs can be designed to be near-invariant to groups of transformations and stable to the action of diffeomorphisms, under certain assumptions on the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators. They also extend these results to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) under specific conditions.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, with a clear and thorough presentation of the theoretical framework and results. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the properties of CKNs and their relationship to CNNs, and they demonstrate the applicability of their results to a wide range of scenarios.

The main strength of the paper is its theoretical contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional networks. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the stability properties of CKNs and CNNs, which sheds light on the mechanisms underlying their success in various applications. The paper also highlights the importance of carefully designing the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators to achieve desired properties.

However, the paper lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to include experimental results that demonstrate the invariance and stability properties of CKNs and CNNs in practice. This would help to reinforce the theoretical findings and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the subject.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a discussion on the potential alternatives for kernel approximation, such as random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series. It would be interesting to explore the effect of these alternatives on the stability properties of CKNs and CNNs.

In terms of the NIPS reviewing criteria, the paper scores high on quality, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a thorough and well-organized presentation of their work, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The results are original and contribute significantly to the understanding of deep convolutional networks. However, the paper could benefit from additional experimental results to support the theoretical analysis.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a rigorous and original theoretical analysis of deep convolutional kernel networks and their stability properties.
* The authors demonstrate the applicability of their results to a wide range of scenarios, including convolutional neural networks.
* The paper is well-written and technically sound, with a clear and thorough presentation of the theoretical framework and results.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis.
* The authors could benefit from a discussion on potential alternatives for kernel approximation and their effect on stability properties.
