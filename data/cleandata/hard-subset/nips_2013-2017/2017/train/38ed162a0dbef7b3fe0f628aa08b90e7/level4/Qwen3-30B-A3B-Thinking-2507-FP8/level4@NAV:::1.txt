

The paper investigates notable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that CKNs exhibit near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms when appropriately designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are employed. The authors further establish that, under specific assumptions, these favorable properties extend to conventional CNNs. Overall, the paper is well-written and technically rigorous. The primary limitation is the absence of empirical validation for the theoretical claims; the authors should provide experimental results substantiating the claimed invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms under suitable CKN configurations. Additionally, it would be valuable to address whether random projection or explicit kernel approximation via finite-dimensional Fourier series constitutes a viable alternative for kernel approximation, and to analyze the resulting impact on stability properties.
