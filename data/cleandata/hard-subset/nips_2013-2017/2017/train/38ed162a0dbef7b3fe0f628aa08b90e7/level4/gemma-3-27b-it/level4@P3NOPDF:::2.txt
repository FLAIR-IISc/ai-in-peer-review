This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14] with respect to C<sup>1</sup> diffeomorphisms (such as translation, as formalized in Eq. (4)). The authors demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels – satisfying conditions (A1-A2) in line 193 – when appropriate patch sizes are chosen (condition (A3)). Furthermore, the paper sketches an extension of these results from Euclidean space (R<sup>d</sup>) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, successfully bridging concepts from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result presented has the potential to be of significant interest to the machine learning community. 

However, the motivation for this stability analysis could be strengthened. While the connection to robustness against adversarial examples is briefly mentioned (lines 56-57: "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7]."), a more detailed discussion of *why* stability is a desirable property in this context would be beneficial. Expanding on this point would help readers appreciate the broader impact of the work.

I have a few minor suggestions for improvement. First, the notation \kappa in condition (A3) (line 193) is potentially confusing, as it also represents a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10). Using a distinct symbol would enhance clarity. Second, in the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint ("and \forall v...") is redundant due to the symmetry of the kernel *k*; it is logically equivalent to the first constraint ("\forall u..."). Removing the redundant part would streamline the presentation. Third, on line 416, the definition of \phi is missing. It should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z),\phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>. Finally, on lines 427-428, the inequality under the '=' sign appears to hold with equality. Specifically, it should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |<sup>2</sup> rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |<sup>2</sup>.
