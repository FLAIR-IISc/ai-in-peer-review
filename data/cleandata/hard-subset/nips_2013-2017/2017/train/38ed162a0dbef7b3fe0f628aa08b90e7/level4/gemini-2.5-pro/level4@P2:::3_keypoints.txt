- Presents a theoretical multilayer RKHS framework that generalizes Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to continuous signals.  
- Analyzes deep representations for near-invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms.  
- Main theoretical result: the representation is Lipschitz stable to small deformations, with invariance improving with network depth.  
- Shows the constructed RKHS contains a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, extending stability guarantees to those CNNs.  
- Builds on prior CKN work and analytical techniques from the scattering transform.  
- Strength: technically rigorous proofs and solid mathematical analysis extending harmonic analysis and kernel methods to CNN-like architectures.  
- Strength: establishes a principled bridge between practical CNN architectures and RKHS theory, aiding conceptual understanding of stability and invariance.  
- Strength: provides important guarantees linking stability/invariance to the RKHS norm, connecting robustness with low-norm generalization.  
- Weakness: novelty relative to prior CKN papers is not clearly delineated—need explicit statement of new contributions beyond extending to continuous signals and stability analysis.  
- Weakness: practical implications of the RKHS reformulation are under-articulated (e.g., implications for regularization, generalization bounds, or alternative training algorithms).  
- Weakness: motivation for insisting on signal-preserving (lossless) representations is unclear given discriminative tasks often require discarding irrelevant variability.  
- Weakness: connection to existing group-invariant kernel literature (e.g., Haasdonk & Burkhardt) is missing and should be discussed.  
- Detailed question: would any operator from the commutant algebra that commutes with group actions suffice to build equivariant layers, or is the proposed sequence of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling special?  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with clear merits; recommend acceptance after clarifying novelty and practical significance.
