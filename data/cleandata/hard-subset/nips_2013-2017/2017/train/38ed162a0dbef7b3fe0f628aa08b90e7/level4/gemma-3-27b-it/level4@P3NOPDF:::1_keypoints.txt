- Paper proves that deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphic deformations under specific design choices.
- Authors show these invariance and stability properties can extend to standard CNNs under certain assumptions.
- Theoretical analysis is well-written, technically rigorous, and conclusions appear sound.
- Major weakness: complete absence of empirical validation to support theoretical claims.
- Reviewer requests experiments quantifying invariance to rotations, translations, scaling and robustness to elastic/diffeomorphic deformations, plus visualizations of kernels/feature maps.
- Suggests examining alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections, finite Fourier features) and discussing their computational costs and effects on stability.
- Recommends adding empirical results and discussion of approximation alternatives to strengthen impact and persuasiveness; with revisions the work could be a strong NIPS contribution.
