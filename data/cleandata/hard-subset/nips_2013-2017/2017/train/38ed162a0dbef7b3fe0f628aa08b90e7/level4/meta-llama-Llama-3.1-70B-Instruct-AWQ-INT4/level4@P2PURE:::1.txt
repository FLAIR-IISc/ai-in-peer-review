The paper explores desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. However, a notable weakness is the absence of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to include experimental results that demonstrate the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms under appropriate settings.

Additionally, a question arises regarding the potential of random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series as alternative solutions for kernel approximation. If such approaches are viable, it would be interesting to investigate their impact on stability.
