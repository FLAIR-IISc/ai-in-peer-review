- Paper proposes a theoretical framework representing DCNNs as sequences of RKHSs (multilayer kernels).  
- Shows DCNNs are nearly invariant to group transformations and stable to diffeomorphic transformations.  
- Paper is rigorous, well-written, and successfully connects DCNNs to multilayer kernel descriptions.  
- Highlights desirable DCNN properties (invariance and stability) through the kernel viewpoint.  
- Lacks discussion on why the RKHS reformulation is practically useful (e.g., generalization bounds or computational benefits).  
- Novelty is unclear beyond reformulating DCNNs in RKHS terms; needs clearer differentiation from prior work.  
- Should better connect to related literature (e.g., Convolutional Kernel Networks) and clarify contributions.  
- Needs more background/context on RKHS and multilayer kernels for broader readability.  
- Presents a novel framework but requires more discussion of the significance and impact on deep learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous framework; demonstrates invariance/stability; well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of significance, background, and novelty beyond reformulation.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the highlighted comments.
