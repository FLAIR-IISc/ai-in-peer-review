The paper presents a well-written and rigorous exploration of how DCNN-implemented functional spaces can be expressed in terms of multilayer kernels, showcasing the architecture's desirable properties, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, pending the authors' consideration of the following comments:

* The primary contribution of the paper appears to be the reformulation of CNN architectures within the context of RKHS. To further enhance the manuscript, the authors should elaborate on the benefits of this reformulation, such as its potential impact on generalization bounds or computational efficiency when utilizing standard kernel techniques and approximations.
* It is essential to clarify whether the novelty of this approach extends beyond a mere reformulation of DCNNs in the RKHS framework. Additionally, the authors should explicitly address the novelty of their work in relation to the paper "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid.
* The authors emphasize the importance of preserving "signal information." However, it is crucial to justify this emphasis, as DCNNs are often employed in tasks where only task-relevant information needs to be retained, such as image classification. The authors should provide context for why preserving signal information is vital in this context.
* Regarding group invariance, it would be beneficial to explore potential connections with the invariant kernel approach proposed by Hans Burkhardt.
* As a point of curiosity, the authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, ensuring the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations. It would be interesting to investigate whether other operators that commute with all group elements could also guarantee this equivariance property.
