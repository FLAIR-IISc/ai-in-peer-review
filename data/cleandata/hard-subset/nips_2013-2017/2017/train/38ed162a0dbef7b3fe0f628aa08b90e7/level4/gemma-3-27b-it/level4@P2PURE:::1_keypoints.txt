- Analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and shows they achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.  
- Proves that, under specific assumptions, these invariance and stability properties can extend to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).  
- Paper is well-written and technically rigorous.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical validation—no experiments demonstrating CKNs’ invariance/stability with appropriate parameter settings.  
- Suggestion: evaluate alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier-series explicit mappings) and assess their impact on stability.
