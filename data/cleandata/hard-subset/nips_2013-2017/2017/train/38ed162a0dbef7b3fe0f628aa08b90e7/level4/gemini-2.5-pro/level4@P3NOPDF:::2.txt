This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs). The central contribution is a theoretical analysis demonstrating that CKN representations are stable with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translations), as defined in Equation (4). The authors show that this stability property holds under specific conditions on the kernel—namely, that it is norm-preserving and non-expansive (Assumptions A1-A2)—and for appropriately chosen patch sizes (Assumption A3). The paper also sketches an extension of this framework from the group (R^d, +) to more general locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and the technical contributions appear to be sound. It successfully bridges two exciting and highly relevant areas of machine learning: deep networks and kernel methods. The main stability result is of significant interest to the machine learning community, particularly for understanding the theoretical properties of deep architectures.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Motivation for Stability Analysis:** The submission would be significantly strengthened by expanding on the motivation for studying stability. The paper currently provides only a single sentence (lines 56-57) linking stability to robustness against adversarial examples. The manuscript would be substantially improved by elaborating on this point. For instance, a more detailed discussion on why stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms is a desirable property, and how it relates to practical concerns like generalization, invariance, or adversarial robustness, would provide crucial context and better highlight the importance of the paper's contributions.

2.  **Clarity and Minor Issues:** The following points, if addressed, would improve the clarity and precision of the manuscript:
    *   **Overloaded Notation:** The symbol `\kappa` is used both in Assumption (A3) (line 193) and to define the kernel `K` in Equation (10). Using distinct notation would prevent potential confusion for the reader.
    *   **Redundant Constraint:** In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint (`and \forall v...`) is superfluous. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, this constraint is identical to the first (`\forall u ...`).
    *   **Missing Definition:** The feature map `\phi` is used on line 416 but is not explicitly introduced. It would be clearer to define it in the context of Equation (10), for example, by stating that K(z, z') = <\phi(z), \phi(z')>_{H(K)}.
    *   **Correction in Proof:** In lines 427-428, the inequality under the equality sign seems that it should also hold with equality. Additionally, there appears to be a typo: `| ||z|| - ||z|| |^2` should be `| ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2`.
