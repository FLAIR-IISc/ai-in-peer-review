The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how the functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) can be described using multilayer kernels. It also establishes that this architecture possesses desirable properties, including (approximate) invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the following comments:

*As I understand it, the primary contribution of this work is a reinterpretation of DCNN architecture within the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) framework. Please elaborate on the potential benefits of this reformulation – for example, could it yield insights into generalization bounds, or offer computational advantages through standard kernel techniques and approximations? More broadly, does the novelty of this approach extend beyond simply reformulating DCNNs in the context of RKHS? Furthermore, please clarify the novelty of this work with respect to the paper "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.

*The authors emphasize the importance of “not losing signal information.” However, this is not always necessary; DCNNs are frequently applied to tasks like image classification where only task-relevant information needs to be preserved, not necessarily the full signal. Please provide further discussion on this point.

*Concerning group invariance, is there any relationship to the invariant kernel approach developed by Hans Burkhardt?

*Finally, as a point of curiosity, you define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. Would any other operator (within the set of operators that commute with all group elements) also guarantee the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to these group transformations?
