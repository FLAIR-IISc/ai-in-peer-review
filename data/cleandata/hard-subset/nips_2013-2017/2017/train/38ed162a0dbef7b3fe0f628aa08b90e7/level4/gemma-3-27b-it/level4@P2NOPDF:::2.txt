This paper investigates the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14] with respect to C<sup>1</sup> diffeomorphisms (such as translations, as formalized in Eq. 4). The authors demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (conditions A1-A2, line 193) when appropriate patch sizes are chosen (condition A3). Furthermore, they outline a potential extension of their framework from Euclidean space (R<sup>d</sup>) to more general locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and technically sound, successfully bridging ideas from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result presented has the potential to be of significant interest to the machine learning community.

However, the paper would benefit from a more substantial motivation for the stability analysis. The current justification – a single sentence (lines 56-57) connecting stability to robustness against adversarial examples [7] – feels somewhat limited and doesn’t fully convey the importance of this investigation. Expanding on this motivation would strengthen the paper's impact.

There are a few minor points that could improve clarity. The notation \kappa in condition A3 (line 193) is potentially confusing as it also represents a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).  Additionally, the second part of the condition presented in the equation between lines 384 and 385 (“and ∀v…”) is redundant, given the symmetry of the kernel k; it is logically equivalent to the first constraint (“∀u…”). 

Finally, the definition of \phi is missing from line 416 and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z), \phi(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>. On line 427-428, the inequality under the '=' symbol appears to hold with equality; the expression should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |<sup>2</sup> rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |<sup>2</sup>.
