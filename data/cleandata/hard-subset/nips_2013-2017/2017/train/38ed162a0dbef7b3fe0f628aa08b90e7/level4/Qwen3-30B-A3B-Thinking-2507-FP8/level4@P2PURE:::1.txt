

The paper investigates several key properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that CKNs exhibit near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms when appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are employed. The authors further establish that, under specific assumptions, these favorable properties extend to conventional CNNs. Overall, the paper is well-written and technically rigorous. The primary limitation is the absence of empirical validation for the theoretical claims; I would have liked to see experimental results demonstrating the invariance to transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms for CKNs with appropriately designed operators. Additionally, it is unclear whether random projections or explicit kernel mappings using a finite number of Fourier features could serve as viable alternatives for kernel approximation, and if so, what impact this would have on the stability properties.
