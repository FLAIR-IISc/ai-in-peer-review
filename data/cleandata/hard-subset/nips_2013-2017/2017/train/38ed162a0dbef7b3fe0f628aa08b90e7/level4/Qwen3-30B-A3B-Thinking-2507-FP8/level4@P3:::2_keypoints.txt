- Studies stability to diffeomorphisms and invariance to group transformations for deep signal representations.  
- Extends convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes the scattering transform to standard convolutional architectures.  
- Proves representations preserve signal information while being stable to small deformations and shows how stability transfers to predictive models via RKHS norm control.  
- Bridges kernel methods and deep learning, connecting theoretical frameworks to practical CNN architectures.  
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering transform and CKNs to provide significant theoretical advances.  
- Extends stability guarantees from pre-defined wavelet filters to learnable CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- High technical quality: careful assumptions, well-justified proofs, and clear derivations linking stability and generalization.  
- Includes a thoughtful extension to group invariance, showing framework flexibility.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with concise mathematical notation overall.  
- Notational issues: κ is overloaded (Equation 10 and Assumption A3) and could confuse readers.  
- Minor technical errors: extraneous phrase between lines 384–385 and a typo in the inequality on lines 427–428.  
- Motivation for studying stability could be strengthened with clearer practical applications beyond adversarial robustness.  
- Missing page information for several references needs fixing.  
- Originality is strong; Proposition 7 linking the framework to CNNs is novel and valuable.  
- Significance: offers principled regularization (RKHS norm) and theoretical justification for CNN robustness and sample efficiency.  
- Pro-acceptance: meaningful theoretical extension, clear stability-generalization link, technically sound and well-written.  
- Con-acceptance: limited practical motivation and minor notational/typographical issues, but these are relatively minor.
