- Paper reformulates DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels/RKHS and is well-written and rigorous.  
- Demonstrates desirable properties: invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on addressing comments.  
- Ask authors to explain why the RKHS/kernel reformulation is useful (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational efficiency).  
- Request clarification whether the approach is novel beyond being a reformulation of DCNNs in RKHS.  
- Ask for a comparison to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.) and discussion of novelty relative to that work.  
- Challenge the emphasis on “preserving signal information,” asking for justification since tasks like classification only need task-relevant information.  
- Suggest exploring connections with Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach for additional insight.  
- Note that patch, pooling, and kernel maps are defined to commute with group action (ensuring equivariance).  
- Ask whether other operators that commute with all group elements could also ensure equivariance and to characterize them.
