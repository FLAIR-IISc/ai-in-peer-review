- Analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing careful design of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling yields near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphisms.
- Shows that, under certain assumptions, these invariance and stability properties extend to conventional CNNs.
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Primary weakness: no empirical experiments provided to validate the theoretical analysis.
- Experimental results demonstrating the proposed invariance and stability would significantly strengthen the claims.
- Asks whether other kernel approximation methods (random projections or finite Fourier series) could be used and how they would affect the network's stability results.
