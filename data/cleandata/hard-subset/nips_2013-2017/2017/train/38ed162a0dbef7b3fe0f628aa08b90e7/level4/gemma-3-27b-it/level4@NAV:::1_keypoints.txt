- Analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphic deformations with carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Proves that, under specific assumptions, these invariance and stability properties extend to standard convolutional neural networks (CNNs).  
- Manuscript is clearly written and presents a technically rigorous analysis.  
- Primary limitation: no empirical validation; experiments demonstrating the theoretical invariance and stability are needed.  
- Recommend discussing alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections, finite Fourier series) and their potential impact on stability.
