The primary focus of the paper is on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors analyze the stability of the representation formed by CKNs with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation), as defined in Eq. (4). They demonstrate that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193], stability holds when patch sizes are chosen appropriately [(A3)]. An extension of these results from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from two exciting areas: deep networks and kernels. The stability result is likely to be of interest to the machine learning community.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the submission would benefit from a more detailed motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only motivation provided is a single sentence (line 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].') that sets the stage for the main contribution of the paper.

Additionally, the use of the \kappa notation in (A3) [line 193] may be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining kernel K in Eq. (10). It would be helpful to use distinct notation for these two concepts.

In the displayed equation between line 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is unnecessary; given the symmetry of kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...'). Removing this redundant information would improve the clarity of the equation.

Furthermore, the definition of \phi is missing and should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}].

Finally, in line 427-428, the inequality under '=' appears to also hold with equality. Specifically, | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
