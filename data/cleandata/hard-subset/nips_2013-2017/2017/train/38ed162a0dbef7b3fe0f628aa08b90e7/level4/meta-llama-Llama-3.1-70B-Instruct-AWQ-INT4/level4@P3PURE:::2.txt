The primary focus of the paper is the convolutional kernel network (CKN) [13, 14]. In this manuscript, the authors analyze the stability of the representation formed by CKNs with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation), in the sense of Eq. (4). They demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] when the patch sizes are appropriately chosen [(A3)]. The authors also sketch an extension of their results from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from two exciting areas: deep networks and kernels. The stability result is likely to be of interest to the machine learning community.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the submission would benefit from additional motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the authors only provide a brief sentence (line 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].') to motivate the main contribution of the paper. Providing more context or explanations for the importance of stability analysis would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, the overloading of the \kappa notation in (A3) [line 193] might be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining kernel K in Eq. (10). To avoid confusion, the authors could consider using a different notation for the patch size.

In the displayed equation between line 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is superfluous, as it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...') due to the symmetry of kernel k. Removing this redundant part would improve the clarity of the equation.

Furthermore, the definition of \phi is missing in line 416. It should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}.

Finally, there appears to be a minor error in the inequality on line 427-428. The inequality under '=' seems to also hold with equality, and | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
