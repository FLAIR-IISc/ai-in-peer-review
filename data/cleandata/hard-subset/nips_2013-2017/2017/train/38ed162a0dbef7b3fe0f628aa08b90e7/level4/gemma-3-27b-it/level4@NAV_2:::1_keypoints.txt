- Investigates invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and connections to CNNs.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel construction and analyzes the geometry of the associated reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS).  
- Demonstrates near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms, providing guarantees comparable to the scattering transform while fitting CNN-like architectures.  
- Builds on prior work in convolutional kernel methods, scattering transforms, and group-invariant representations.  
- Extends analytical tools from scattering transforms to a broader class of CNNs via a kernel perspective.  
- Kernel framework enables separation of data representation and predictive modeling, facilitating focused analysis of stability and generalization.  
- Correctly situates the contribution within the deep learning with kernels literature.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clear, well-structured mathematical presentation and proofs.  
- Strength: establishes a significant connection between kernel representations and standard CNNs, suggesting practical implications for regularization and understanding.  
- Strength: thorough treatment of group invariance and stability grounded in harmonic analysis.  
- Weakness: complete lack of empirical validation to corroborate theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: does not explore alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections, finite Fourier series) or their effect on stability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clarity, novel extension to CNNs, and potential impact on understanding deep learning geometry and generalization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experiments and limited discussion of kernel approximation techniques.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requiring acknowledgment of the need for future empirical work.
