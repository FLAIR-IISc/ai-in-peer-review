- Paper analyzes stability of Convolutional Kernel Network (CKN) representations under C¹ diffeomorphisms and proves stability given norm-preserving/non-expansive kernels and appropriate patch sizes (assumptions A1–A3).

- Extends the stability framework from translations on R^d to more general locally compact groups and links the CKN functional space to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.

- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, providing a valuable theoretical bridge between deep networks and kernel methods.

- Main result generalizes prior scattering-transform stability to a broader class of learnable architectures, offering insight into geometric properties of convolutional representations.

- Suggest expanding motivation for stability beyond adversarial robustness to include implications for generalization, sample complexity, and invariances.

- Notation issue: κ is overloaded (used for patch size in A3 and for the kernel function K_κ); recommend renaming one usage to avoid confusion.

- Appendix: second constraint between lines 384–385 involving "∀v" appears redundant due to kernel symmetry and can be removed or clarified.

- Missing definition: feature map φ is used on line 416 but not defined in main text; explicitly introduce φ alongside Eq. (2) (K_κ(z,z') = ⟨φ_κ(z), φ_κ(z')⟩).

- Typo on lines 427–428: `| ||z|| - ||z|| |²` should be `| ||z|| - ||z'|| |²`.
