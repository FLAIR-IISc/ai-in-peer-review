- Frames deep convolutional representations in an RKHS, extending CKN to a multilayer kernel construction for principled analysis of invariance and stability.
- Generalizes Mallat’s scattering transform to standard CNNs via a kernel formalism that preserves signal while achieving near-invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.
- Main technical result: rigorous stability bounds under diffeomorphic deformations with decay as pooling scale increases (Theorem 6).
- Shows many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS defined by the kernel, linking kernel methods and CNNs.
- Technical development is sound with detailed proofs and clear assumptions (A1–A3).
- Addresses practical concerns via analysis of signal preservation (Lemma 1) and discretization (Lemma 2).
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with careful notation and helpful examples.
- Would benefit from more explicit discussion of translating theory into practical implementation challenges.
- Novelty: extends scattering and CKN work to a broader class of convolutional architectures and introduces group invariance extensions.
- Significance: provides a theoretical foundation relating RKHS norms to stability and generalization, with implications for sample complexity and model design.
- Strength: first comprehensive theoretical analysis of stability and invariance for a broad class of convolutional architectures.
- Strength: establishes a clear, useful connection between kernel methods and practical CNNs.
- Strength: demonstrates applicability to practical CNNs with smooth activations and addresses discretization concerns.
- Strength: offers a principled perspective on the relationship between stability and generalization.
- Weakness: does not clearly articulate practical utility of the RKHS formulation for training or model design.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of why preserving all signal information is beneficial for classification tasks.
- Weakness: lacks explicit comparison to prior invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Burkhardt [13]).
- Weakness: needs a concrete example showing how the theory could improve practical CNN design.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address practical motivation, signal-preservation implications, and comparisons to prior work.
- Overall: not merely a reformulation—demonstrates a broad class of CNNs reside in a function space with provable stability, bridging kernel methods and deep learning theory.
