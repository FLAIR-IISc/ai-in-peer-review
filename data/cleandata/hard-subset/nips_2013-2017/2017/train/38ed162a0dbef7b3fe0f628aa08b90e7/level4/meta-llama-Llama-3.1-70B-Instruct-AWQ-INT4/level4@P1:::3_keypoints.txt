- Presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations (invariance and stability) via a functional space from a kernel that generalizes Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) and contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Main contribution: rewrites CNN architecture in RKHS, showing the kernel representation commutes with translations at intermediate layers and final pooling on the translation group controls final invariance.  
- Shows how adapted patch extraction and pooling operators that commute with a transformation group G yield equivariance/invariance.  
- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and provides a thorough mathematical analysis.  
- Clarification needed on the claim about “not losing the signal information” and why signal preservation is important for task-specific uses like classification.  
- Relationship to the scattering transform and other invariant kernel methods (e.g., Hans Burkhardt’s work) is not fully discussed; novelty relative to these methods is unclear.  
- Question remains whether any operator commuting with all group elements would ensure equivariance, i.e., the generality of the commuting-operator requirement.  
- Novelty concern: the work appears to build on Julien Mairal et al.’s CKNs but needs clearer explanation of differences or improvements.  
- Strengths summarized: rigorous analysis, clear mathematical understanding, potential to significantly contribute new perspectives on CNN properties.  
- Weaknesses summarized: need more context on signal preservation, clearer relation to other invariant kernel methods, and explicit discussion of novelty vs prior CKN work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions requested: explain importance of preserving signal information; clarify relation to other invariant kernel methods; explicitly discuss novelty vs CKNs; add more detail in mathematical analysis.
