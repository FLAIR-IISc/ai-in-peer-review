

The paper investigates key properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they achieve near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms when appropriately designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are employed. The authors further establish that these properties extend to standard CNNs under specific assumptions. Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound.  

A notable limitation is the absence of empirical validation for the theoretical claims. The authors assert that CKNs with suitable operator design exhibit invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms, yet provide no experimental results to substantiate this. I would appreciate seeing empirical evidence—such as quantitative measures of invariance or stability under controlled transformations—to confirm these theoretical conclusions.  

Additionally, the paper raises an important question regarding kernel approximation: Could random projection or explicit kernel mapping via finite Fourier series serve as viable alternatives for approximating the kernel? If so, how would this choice impact the network’s stability properties? Addressing this would strengthen the practical relevance of the theoretical framework.
