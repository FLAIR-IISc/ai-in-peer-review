The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how certain functional spaces implemented by DCNNs can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. It also highlights the architecture's desirable properties, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, provided the authors address the following comments:

* In my understanding, the primary contribution of the paper is the reformulation of CNN architecture in the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The authors should elaborate on why this reformulation is useful. For instance, does the kernel formulation lead to interesting results on generalization bounds? Are there computational efficiency gains in implementing standard techniques or approximations using kernels? 

Furthermore, I would like the authors to clarify whether the novelty of their approach extends beyond a simple reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS. Specifically, how does their work differ from "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid?

* The authors emphasize the importance of preserving signal information. However, in many applications of DCNNs, such as image classification, only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. The authors should comment on this and explain why preserving signal information is crucial in their context.

* Regarding group invariance, I would like to know if there is any connection between the authors' approach and the invariant kernel method developed by Hans Burkhardt.

* As a point of curiosity, the authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, ensuring the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations. I wonder if any other operator, from the set of operators that commute with all group elements, would also ensure this equivariance property.
