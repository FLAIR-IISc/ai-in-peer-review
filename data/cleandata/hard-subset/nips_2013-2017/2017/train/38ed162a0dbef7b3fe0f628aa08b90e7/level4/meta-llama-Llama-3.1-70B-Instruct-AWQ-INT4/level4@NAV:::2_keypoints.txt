- Paper analyzes Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) and their stability to C^1 diffeomorphisms (notably translations) as defined in Eq. (4).
- Shows stability can be achieved for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (A1–A2) when appropriate patch sizes (A3) are chosen.
- Presents an extension of the approach from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.
- Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and effectively integrates ideas from deep networks and kernel methods.
- Stability result is potentially of significant interest to the machine learning community.
- Motivation for the stability analysis is weak and should be expanded beyond the brief mention of Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness (lines 56–57).
- Notation conflict: κ used in (A3) (line 193) may be confused with κ defining kernel K in Eq. (10).
- Redundant expression: the displayed equation between lines 384–385 repeats the same constraint due to kernel symmetry.
- Missing definition: φ is not introduced in Eq. (10) [⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}] and should be defined.
- Inequality issue: the inequality in lines 427–428 appears to hold with equality and should be written as |‖z‖ − ‖z'‖|^2.
- References problems: several citations ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]) lack page information and entries [9,17,19,32] need correction to match original sources.
