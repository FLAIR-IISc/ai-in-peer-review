The paper is clearly articulated and technically solid, successfully demonstrating how the function spaces associated with DCNNs can be characterized through the lens of multilayer kernels. The authors also establish that the resulting architecture possesses desirable properties, namely near-invariance to group transformations and stability with respect to diffeomorphisms. I am in favor of accepting this paper, provided the authors can clarify the issues outlined below:

*   My understanding is that the central contribution of this work is the reformulation of CNN architectures within the RKHS framework. The manuscript would be strengthened by a discussion of the practical and theoretical benefits of this perspective. For instance, could this kernel-based view facilitate the derivation of tighter generalization bounds, or does it offer computational advantages through standard kernel approximation techniques? More broadly, does the novelty extend beyond this re-characterization of DCNNs? A clear differentiation from the prior art, specifically the "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al., is also needed.

*   A key emphasis is placed on the preservation of signal information. The motivation for this design choice requires further justification. Since DCNNs are typically employed for tasks like classification, they are often designed to extract and preserve only task-relevant features while discarding other information. The authors should comment on why retaining the complete signal information is advantageous in this context.

*   Regarding the treatment of group invariance, it would be valuable to situate this work in the context of related literature. Is there a relationship between the proposed method and the established work on invariant kernels, such as the approach pioneered by Hans Burkhardt?

*   As a point of curiosity on the theoretical side: The construction relies on patch, pooling, and kernel map operators that are designed to commute with the group action. Could the authors comment on whether the equivariance property of the architecture would hold for *any* operator that commutes with all elements of the group, or is this property specific to the operators defined in this work?
