- Paper studies stability of Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) under C^1 diffeomorphisms and proves stability for norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes (assumptions (A1)-(A3)).  
- Extension to locally compact groups is briefly sketched (Section 4).  
- Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound; contributes useful insights bridging deep learning and kernel methods.  
- Motivation is weak: the authors need a stronger discussion of why stability under diffeomorphisms matters for practical ML (e.g., robustness, generalization, image analysis).  
- Notation overload: κ is used both as a kernel and as a constant in (A3); rename the constant (e.g., κ0).  
- Redundant constraint: the "∀ v ∈ R^d ..." condition is superfluous due to kernel symmetry and should be removed.  
- Missing definition: φ is not defined before Equation (10); explicitly state φ as the feature map of kernel K.  
- Typographical error: |‖z‖ − ‖z‖|^2 should read |‖z‖ − ‖z′‖|^2.  
- Reference list issues: several references ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]) lack page numbers.  
- Specific reference updates requested for [9], [17], [19], and [32] to the provided, corrected citations.  
- Overall recommendation: core result is interesting and merits publication after addressing motivation, notation, redundant constraint, missing definition, typo, and reference fixes.
