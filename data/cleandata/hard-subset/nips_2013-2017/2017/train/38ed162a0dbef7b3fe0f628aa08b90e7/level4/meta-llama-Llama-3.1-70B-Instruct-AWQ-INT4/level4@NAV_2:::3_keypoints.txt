- Presents a rigorous study of deep signal representations that are near-invariant to group transformations and stable to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel from convolutional kernel networks and analyzes the geometry of the resulting RKHS.  
- Rewrites CNN architectures in the RKHS framework, offering a new functional perspective on CNN properties.  
- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, enabling theoretical results for both kernels and CNNs.  
- Extends prior work on CKNs and scattering networks to more general CNN architectures within the kernel framework.  
- Emphasizes constructions that avoid losing signal information, focusing on theoretical understanding rather than new applied methods.  
- Defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with group actions to ensure equivariance of DCNNs.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written for readers with relevant background.  
- Strength: provides a thorough, rigorous theoretical analysis of invariance and stability for deep convolutional representations.  
- Strength: connects kernel representations and CNNs, elucidating the RKHS structure for continuous-domain CNNs.  
- Weakness: highly technical and may be hard to follow for readers lacking functional analysis or kernel-methods background.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implications and real-world applicability.  
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance for its significant theoretical contribution and clear presentation.  
- Suggested improvements: add more discussion of practical implications, include more examples/illustrations, and compare more extensively with related work (e.g., Mairal et al.).
