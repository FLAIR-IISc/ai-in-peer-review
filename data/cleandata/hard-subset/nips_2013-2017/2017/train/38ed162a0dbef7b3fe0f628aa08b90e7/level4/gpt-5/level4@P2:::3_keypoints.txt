- Develops a multilayer kernel representation mirroring CNNs (patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernel map, Gaussian pooling) and studies its RKHS geometry.
- Proves stability to C1-diffeomorphisms and near-translation invariance with bounds linear in ∥∇τ∥∞ and ∥τ∥∞/σn.
- Shows how to obtain global invariance to locally compact groups by designing layers that commute with the group action.
- Discusses discretization and kernel-approximation (CKNs), noting non-expansive projections preserve stability.
- Shows a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS, linking predictor stability and generalization to the RKHS norm.
- Generalizes scattering transforms by proving Lipschitz stability for learnable multilayer kernels instead of fixed wavelets.
- Extends CKNs from finite-dimensional approximations to a continuous-domain kernel construction with formal stability and group-invariance guarantees.
- Connects to invariant kernels and group-equivariant CNNs by providing an RKHS perspective plus local-deformation stability.
- Main stability proof uses commutator bounds and Schur’s test; assumptions A1–A3 (non-expansiveness, patch radius scaling) are clearly stated.
- Theory covers continuous signals, discretization, kernel approximations, and links to CNN predictors (Proposition 7); proofs deferred but roadmap convincing.
- Caveat: discrete “signal preservation” via deconvolution after Gaussian pooling can be ill-conditioned unless scales are small relative to subsampling.
- Norm non-preservation due to pooling is acknowledged; suggested fix (final normalized kernel map) is reasonable but needs empirical illustration.
- Activation restriction: requirement of smooth homogeneous activations excludes vanilla ReLU; smoothed variants should be instantiated with concrete choices and constants.
- Clarity: generally well organized; recommend stating Theorem 6 and its implications earlier with a brief proof sketch.
- Suggest adding explicit examples of admissible kernels and activations satisfying (A1–A2) and Lemma B.2, with typical C1, C2 values.
- Request clarification on conditioning of the deconvolution step and practical parameter ranges (ek, sk, σk).
- Minor issues: typos (“loosing”, “discusssed”) and formatting artifacts in references.
- Originality: new multilayer kernel stability and near-invariance results that subsume CKNs and a class of CNNs, extending scattering-style guarantees to learned features.
- Originality: introduces a general group-equivariant kernel layer construction achieving global invariance at the last layer.
- Originality: embeds CNN predictors with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS, linking RKHS norm to Lipschitz stability and potential generalization control.
- Significance: provides principled tools to analyze invariance, stability, and generalization; RKHS viewpoint separates representation from prediction and suggests norm-based regularization.
- Practical impact limited by theoretical focus; small-scale empirical illustration would strengthen the contribution.
- Pros: rigorous, interpretable stability bounds; unifying RKHS framework including CKNs and some CNNs; general group equivariance; links between representation stability and predictor generalization.
- Cons: potential fragility of deconvolution-based signal preservation; exclusion of vanilla ReLU; limited empirical validation; parameter/depth dependence discussed only qualitatively.
- Requests: expand on how RKHS norm yields concrete generalization bounds and whether kernel approximations offer computational/training benefits.
- Requests: clearly delineate novelty over Mairal et al. (CKN) regarding continuous-domain construction, diffeomorphism stability, group invariance, and inclusion of CNN predictors.
- Requests: clarify why information preservation is emphasized and its role for layerwise stability/equivariance and supervised tasks.
- Requests: relate last-layer group pooling to invariant-kernel constructions and compare multiscale/local pooling versus global invariance in expressivity and cost.
- Requests: discuss admissible commuting operators for group-equivariance and whether stability/norm-control require non-expansiveness or kernel smoothness.
- Requests: provide explicit smooth ReLU-like activations and concrete kernel choices (e.g., inverse polynomial, Gaussian) with numerical constants for C1, C2.
- Recommendation: Accept, pending the clarifications and empirical/illustrative fixes requested.
