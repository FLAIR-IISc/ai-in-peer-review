- Investigates desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Shows that with carefully designed patch-extraction, kernel-mapping, and pooling operators, CKNs are nearly invariant to certain transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphic deformations.  
- Argues that, under appropriate assumptions, these invariance and stability properties extend to standard CNNs.  
- Paper is clearly written and technically sound.  
- Main concern: lacks empirical validation—needs experiments showing CKNs configured as proposed actually exhibit the predicted invariance and stability.  
- Question about kernel approximation: could random projections or finite Fourier-component mappings be viable, and how would such approximations affect the stated stability guarantees?
