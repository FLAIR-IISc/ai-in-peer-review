The paper explores several desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), specifically their near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also demonstrate that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties of CKNs can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. However, its primary weakness lies in the absence of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to see experimental results that support the conclusion that CKNs, with appropriate settings, are indeed invariant to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

Another question arises regarding the potential of random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series as alternative solutions for kernel approximation. If viable, it would be interesting to investigate the impact of these approaches on stability.
