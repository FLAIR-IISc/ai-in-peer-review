- Paper studies theoretical invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations using a generalized multilayer kernel construction.
- Uses RKHS tools to prove near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.
- Shows these properties extend to a broad class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations.
- Builds on scattering transforms, kernel methods, and group invariance, offering a unified kernel-based perspective.
- Technical presentation is rigorous, clear, and logically organized with well-stated assumptions.
- Extends existing scattering-transform results to more general CNN settings and links stability to RKHS norms.
- Analysis of how kernel design influences invariance is a useful contribution.
- Major weakness: no empirical validation to demonstrate the theoretical predictions in practice.
- Recommends experiments on standard datasets (e.g., transformed image classification) to support claims.
- Paper focuses on subspace learning for kernel approximation and does not explore alternatives like random projections or explicit finite mappings.
- Suggests investigating trade-offs between approximation accuracy and computational efficiency in future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel unified framework, generalization of prior work, potential impact on robust architecture design.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of experiments and limited exploration of other approximation methods.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on adding preliminary experiments or a clear experimental plan to demonstrate practical relevance.
