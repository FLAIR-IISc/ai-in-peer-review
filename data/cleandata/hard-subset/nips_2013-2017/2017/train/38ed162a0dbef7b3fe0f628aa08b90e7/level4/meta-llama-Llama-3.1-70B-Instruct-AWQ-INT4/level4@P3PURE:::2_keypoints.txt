- Paper analyzes stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C^1 diffeomorphisms and proves stability under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels with appropriately chosen patch sizes.
- Authors sketch an extension of the stability results from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups (Section 4).
- Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from deep networks and kernels.
- The stability result is likely of interest and relevance to the machine learning community.
- The paper needs stronger motivation for the stability analysis; current justification is limited to a single sentence about Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness.
- Notation clash: the use of κ in (A3) conflicts with κ as the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10); rename the patch-size symbol to avoid confusion.
- The displayed equation between lines 384–385 contains a redundant constraint (the second part is identical by symmetry of k); remove it for clarity.
- Definition of φ is missing at line 416; φ should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.
- Minor algebra/typographical error on lines 427–428: the inequality under ‘=’ appears to hold with equality, and |‖z‖−‖z‖|^2 should be |‖z‖−‖z′‖|^2.
- Several references ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]) are missing page information and should be completed.
