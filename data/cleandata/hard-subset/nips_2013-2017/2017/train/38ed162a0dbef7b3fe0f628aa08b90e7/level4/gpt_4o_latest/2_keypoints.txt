- Paper studies convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and proves stability of their representations to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) under assumptions A1–A3.  
- Stability is preserved for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (A1–A2) when patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3).  
- Extension of the framework from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.  
- Manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, technically sound, and connects deep networks with kernel methods.  
- Results are likely of interest to the machine learning community.  
- Motivation is weak: the paper only briefly cites adversarial robustness and needs a more thorough justification for the stability analysis.  
- Notation issue: κ is overloaded in (A3) and also used for the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10), which may confuse readers.  
- Redundancy: the second part of the displayed equation between lines 384–385 is redundant due to kernel symmetry.  
- Missing definition: φ is not defined and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.  
- Typo/error: lines 427–428—equality likely holds and the term | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z′|| |^2.  
- References [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31] lack page numbers and need completion.  
- Specific reference corrections provided: [9] Daniely et al. NIPS 2016 citation; [17] Muandet et al. Foundations and Trends; [19] Raj et al. AISTATS 2017 citation; [32] Zhang et al. ICML 2017 accepted (link).
