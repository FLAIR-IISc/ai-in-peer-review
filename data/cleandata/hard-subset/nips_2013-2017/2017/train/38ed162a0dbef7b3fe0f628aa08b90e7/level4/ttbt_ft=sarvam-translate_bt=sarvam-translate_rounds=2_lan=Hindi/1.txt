This paper examines some of the good properties of Deep Convolutional Kernel Networks (DCNs), namely that DCNs are almost invariant for groups of transforms and stable for the operation of spreadability, provided that the appropriate patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, the good properties obtained from DCNs also apply to CNNs.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The only weakness is that the paper does not provide any empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. I would like to see some experimental results that show the conclusion that DCNs are invariant for transforms and spreadability with appropriate settings.

Another question is whether explicit kernel mapping with a limited number of random projections or Fourier series can also be a potential solution for kernel approximation. If so, what is the impact on stability?
