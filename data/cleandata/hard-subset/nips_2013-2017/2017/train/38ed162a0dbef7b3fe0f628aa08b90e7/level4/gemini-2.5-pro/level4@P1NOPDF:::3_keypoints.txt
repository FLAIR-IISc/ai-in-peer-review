- Positive evaluation: well-written, rigorous, describes DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels and shows near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Major contribution concern: clarify practical utility of the RKHS/kernel reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds, computational efficiency, or novel algorithms) and explicitly distinguish novelty from Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."  
- Clarify the motivation for "not losing the signal information": explain why preserving full signal is desirable vs. discarding task-irrelevant information in typical classification tasks and the implications for common DCNN applications.  
- Related work: discuss connections to prior invariant-kernel methods (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) and situate the method within that literature.  
- Technical clarification: confirm whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would similarly ensure equivariance of DCNN components (patch, pooling, kernel maps).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the above points.
