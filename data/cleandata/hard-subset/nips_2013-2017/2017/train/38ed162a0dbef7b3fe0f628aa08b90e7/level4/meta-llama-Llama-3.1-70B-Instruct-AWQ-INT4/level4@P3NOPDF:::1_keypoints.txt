- Analyzes desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing near invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphisms.  
- Demonstrates these properties hold when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are carefully designed.  
- Shows that, under certain assumptions, the invariance and stability results can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound with a clear, thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Main weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical claims.  
- Recommends adding experimental results demonstrating CKN invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms.  
- Suggests investigating alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projection, finite Fourier series) as potential viable solutions.  
- Advises analyzing how these approximation methods affect network stability.  
- Recommends comparing alternative approximations with the proposed method to clarify design trade-offs.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution that would be strengthened by empirical validation and exploration of approximation methods.
