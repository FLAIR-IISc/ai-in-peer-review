- Studies desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs): near invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows these properties hold when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are carefully designed.  
- Argues that, under certain assumptions, the CKN stability/invariance results can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: no empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis—experiments are needed to confirm invariance and stability claims.  
- Raises question about using random projection or finite Fourier-series explicit kernel mappings as kernel approximations and how these would affect stability.
