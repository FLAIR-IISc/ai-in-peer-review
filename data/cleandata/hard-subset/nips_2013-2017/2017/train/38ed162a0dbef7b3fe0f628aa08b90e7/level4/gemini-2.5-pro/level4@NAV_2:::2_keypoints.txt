- Defines a multilayer convolutional kernel representation and analyzes invariance/stability of deep convolutional representations.
- Main contribution: formal proof that the representation is stable to C1-diffeomorphisms, giving a Lipschitz-continuous bound on changes from small deformations.
- Stability result requires specific assumptions on kernels (norm-preserving, non-expansive) and architectural parameters.
- Sketches an extension to obtain global invariance to actions of locally compact groups.
- Establishes a formal link between the RKHS of the proposed kernel and a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, extending stability guarantees to those CNNs.
- Strength: technically rigorous, with well-constructed proofs using harmonic analysis and kernel theory.
- Strength: manuscript is clear and well-organized, moving from continuous-domain theory to practical/discrete implementations like CKNs.
- Strength: original synthesis bridging scattering-network stability analysis with learnable CKNs and certain CNN architectures.
- Strength: significant contribution to theoretical understanding of robustness to deformations and to connections between RKHS norm, stability, and generalization.
- Weakness: motivation could be strengthened—expand implications for sample complexity and learning invariances beyond adversarial robustness.
- Minor clarity issue: symbol κ is overloaded (kernel and patch-size constant); use distinct symbols to avoid confusion.
- Appendix suggestion: explicitly define the feature map φ corresponding to kernel K to make the presentation self-contained.
- Appendix technical correction: an inequality likely contains a typo and should involve the term | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
- Appendix technical note: the non-expansiveness condition appears redundant due to kernel symmetry—the constraints for u and v seem identical.
- Bibliography needs polishing: update final publication details for several cited works (e.g., [9],[17],[19],[32]) and add missing page numbers for multiple references.
