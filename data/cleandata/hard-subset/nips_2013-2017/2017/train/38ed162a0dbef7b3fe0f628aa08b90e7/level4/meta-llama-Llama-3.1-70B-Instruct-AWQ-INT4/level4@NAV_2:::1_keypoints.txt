- Presents theoretical analysis of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their stability to transformations and diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows CKNs can be designed to be near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms under assumptions on patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Extends the stability results to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) under specific conditions.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and presents a clear, rigorous theoretical framework.  
- Provides a detailed analysis of CKN properties and their relationship to CNNs, with broad applicability.  
- Main strength: rigorous theoretical contribution clarifying mechanisms behind convolutional network stability.  
- Emphasizes the importance of careful design of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators.  
- Lacks empirical experiments to validate and illustrate the theoretical findings in practice.  
- Missing discussion of alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections, finite Fourier features) and their impact on stability.  
- Rates highly on NIPS criteria: quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, rigor, broad applicability, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experimental validation and limited discussion of kernel-approximation alternatives.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that would be more impactful with experiments and expanded discussion on approximations.
