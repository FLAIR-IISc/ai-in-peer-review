- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and shows DCNN functional spaces can be characterized via multilayer kernels.  
- Shows the architecture is (nearly) invariant to group transformations and stable to diffeomorphic deformations.  
- Recommendation: accept pending several clarifications.  
- Clarify practical significance of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., new generalization bounds or computational gains from kernel approximations).  
- Explain whether the novelty goes beyond the RKHS viewpoint and distinguish the work from Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."  
- Justify the emphasis on “preserving signal information” given DCNNs often aim to retain task-relevant, not all, information.  
- State whether there is a connection to Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methodology.  
- Clarify whether any operator that commutes with group actions would suffice to guarantee equivariance in DCNNs, or if stronger conditions are needed.
