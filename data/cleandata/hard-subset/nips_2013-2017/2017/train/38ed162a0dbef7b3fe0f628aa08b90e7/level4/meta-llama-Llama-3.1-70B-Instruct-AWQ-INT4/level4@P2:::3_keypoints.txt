- Paper presents a rigorous, well-written theoretical framework describing DCNN functional space via multilayer kernels.  
- Main contribution: reformulation of DCNN architecture in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS).  
- Authors should explain why the RKHS reformulation is useful (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation benefits).  
- Approach appears to offer new insights (invariance and stability), but novelty beyond a reformulation should be clarified.  
- Unclear how this work differs from "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Julien Mairal et al.); authors should clarify novelty relative to that prior work.  
- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information”: authors should justify why full signal preservation is crucial given task-specific needs.  
- Suggest exploring connections to invariant kernel methods, specifically Hans Burkhardt’s approach.  
- Construction of patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with group action is elegant and noteworthy.  
- Unclear whether any operator commuting with all group elements would guarantee equivariance; authors should discuss this point.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, rigorous paper with potential impact; recommend acceptance pending responses to the above clarifications.
