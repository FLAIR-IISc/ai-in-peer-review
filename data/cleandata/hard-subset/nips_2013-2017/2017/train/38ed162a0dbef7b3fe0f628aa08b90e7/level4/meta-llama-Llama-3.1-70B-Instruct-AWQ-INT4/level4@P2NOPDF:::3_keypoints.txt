- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and demonstrates DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels.  
- Shows desirable architecture properties: invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance pending authors’ consideration of several comments.  
- Main contribution interpreted as reformulating CNNs within RKHS; authors should clarify why this reformulation is useful.  
- Suggest discussing potential benefits such as improved generalization bounds or computational advantages via kernel approximations.  
- Authors should clarify whether the novelty goes beyond a straightforward RKHS reformulation of DCNNs.  
- Provide a clear comparison/distinction with "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.) to establish novelty and impact.  
- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information” — request justification of its necessity outside reconstruction tasks.  
- Explore possible connections to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) to enrich discussion of group invariance.  
- Curiosity whether other operators that commute with all group elements could also guarantee equivariance; suggest addressing this.
