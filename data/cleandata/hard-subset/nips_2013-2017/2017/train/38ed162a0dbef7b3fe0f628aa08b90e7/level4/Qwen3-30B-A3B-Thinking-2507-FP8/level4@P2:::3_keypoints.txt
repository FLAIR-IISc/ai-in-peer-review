- Main contribution: Frames CNNs within a multilayer kernel framework, characterizing deep convolutional representations as elements of an RKHS with near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Novelty: Extends scattering transform and CKNs to continuous domains and shows theoretical guarantees that apply to standard CNNs, not only predefined filter networks.  
- Technical quality: Paper is rigorous with strong mathematical proofs demonstrating stability under diffeomorphisms and group invariance.  
- Significance: Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, linking stability properties to generalization performance.  
- Originality detail: Claims that the functional space contains CKNs and a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations; should clarify novelty relative to Mairal et al.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear mathematical exposition.  
- Practical usefulness: Needs more explicit discussion of why the kernel framework is practically useful beyond theory, e.g., for generalization bounds or computational/implementation advantages.  
- Signal-preservation claim: Authors should clarify why preserving full signal information is necessary in practice, given that classification often requires only task-relevant information.  
- Related work: Must explicitly discuss the connection to Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach and whether the frameworks are conceptually similar or distinct.  
- Generality of operators: Address whether operators beyond patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling can ensure equivariance, or if their specific operators are necessary for guarantees.  
- Limitations: Paper insufficiently argues how the kernel formulation leads to practical improvements in model design, regularization, or algorithms.  
- Requested clarifications: Explain (1) how the framework enables better generalization bounds, (2) whether theoretical insights can yield practical implementation advantages, and (3) computational implications versus standard CNN training.  
- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical contribution, technically sound and well-written; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify practical significance and novelty.
