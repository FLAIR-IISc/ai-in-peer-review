- Overall positive: paper is well written and rigorous, characterizes DCNN function spaces via multilayer kernels, and proves (approximate) group invariance and diffeomorphism stability; recommends acceptance subject to comments.

- Clarify practical usefulness of the RKHS reformulation: can it yield new generalization bounds, or computational benefits via kernel techniques/approximations?

- Explain whether the novelty goes beyond recasting DCNNs in RKHS and explicitly contrast contributions with Convolutional Kernel Networks (Mairal et al.).

- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information”: why is full signal preservation crucial for tasks (like classification) where only task-relevant information matters?

- Ask about connections to prior invariant-kernel approaches, specifically Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernels.

- Curious whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would guarantee equivariance of the DCNN to those group transformations.
