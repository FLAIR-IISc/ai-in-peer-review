This paper analyzes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14], focusing on the stability of their representations with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translations), in the sense of Eq. (4). The authors show that, under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2, line 193), stability holds when patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3). An extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The paper is nicely organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from deep networks and kernels. The stability result should be of interest to the ML community.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Motivation: The submission would benefit from more extensive motivation for studying stability. Currently, there is only one short sentence (lines 56–57: “Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”) that motivates the main contribution. Expanding this discussion would strengthen the paper’s positioning and impact.

Clarifications and minor issues:
- Notation: Overloading the κ notation in (A3) (line 193) may be confusing, since κ also denotes the function defining kernel K in Eq. (10).
- Redundant constraint: In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part (“and ∀ v ...”) is superfluous; given the symmetry of k, it is identical to the first constraint (“∀ u ...”).
- Missing definition: Line 416—The definition of φ is missing. It should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z), φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.
- Typographical issue: Lines 427–428—The inequality under “=” seems to also hold with equality. Moreover, | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z′|| |^2.
