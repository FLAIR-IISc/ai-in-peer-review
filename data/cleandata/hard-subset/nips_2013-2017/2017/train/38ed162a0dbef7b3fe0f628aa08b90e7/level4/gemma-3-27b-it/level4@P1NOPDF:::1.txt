The paper investigates appealing properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they are nearly invariant to groups of transformations and stable under the action of diffeomorphisms – provided that the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are carefully designed. The authors further show that, under certain assumptions, these beneficial properties extend to conventional convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The primary weakness is the lack of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be highly beneficial to see experimental results demonstrating the claimed invariance of appropriately configured CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

A further question arises regarding alternative approaches to kernel approximation. Could random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series terms also serve as viable solutions? If so, what would be the impact on stability?
