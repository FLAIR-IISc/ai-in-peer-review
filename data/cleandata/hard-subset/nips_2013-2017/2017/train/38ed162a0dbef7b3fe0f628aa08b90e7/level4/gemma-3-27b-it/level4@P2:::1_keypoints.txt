- Paper studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, focusing on invariance and stability.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel framework from convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and analyzes the RKHS geometry.  
- Shows near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Extends theoretical results to a broader class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activation functions.  
- Builds on scattering transform theory and connects to prior work on CKNs, group invariance, and kernels in deep learning.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clearly stated assumptions and careful derivations.  
- Strength: links kernel framework to stability and potential implications for generalization and regularization.  
- Strength: meaningful extension of CKN theory to more general CNN architectures.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and uses helpful notation.  
- Main weakness: complete lack of empirical validation to support theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections, finite Fourier series).  
- Recommendation: accept but strongly encourage inclusion of experimental validation in future revisions; reviewer score 6/10.
