- Paper analyzes desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs): near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are well-designed.
- Authors show these invariance and stability properties can, under certain conditions, be extended from CKNs to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
- The paper is well-structured and technically sound.
- Major limitation: no empirical experiments are provided to validate the theoretical results.
- Recommendation: include experiments demonstrating CKNs' invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms when properly configured.
- Suggestion: explore alternative kernel approximation methods (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier series) and study their effects on stability.
