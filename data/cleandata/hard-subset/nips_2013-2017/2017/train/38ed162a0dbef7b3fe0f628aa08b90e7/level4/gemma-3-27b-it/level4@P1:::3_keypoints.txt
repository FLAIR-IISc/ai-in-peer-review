- Positive overall: paper is well-written and rigorous, shows DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels, and demonstrates (near) group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing comments.

- Main contribution framed as RKHS reformulation of CNNs — ask authors to elaborate on the practical utility (e.g., implications for generalization bounds) and whether this yields computational advantages or leverages standard kernel approximations.

- Question the novelty: does the work go beyond reformulating DCNNs in RKHS, and how does it compare/contrast to Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks"?

- Challenge the emphasis on “not losing signal information”: why is preserving full signal important when many DCNN applications only require task-relevant information?

- Ask for connections to prior invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., work by Hans Burkhardt and colleagues).

- Technical curiosity: if patch, pooling, and kernel maps commute with the group action, would any operator commuting with all group elements guarantee equivariance of DCNN components?
