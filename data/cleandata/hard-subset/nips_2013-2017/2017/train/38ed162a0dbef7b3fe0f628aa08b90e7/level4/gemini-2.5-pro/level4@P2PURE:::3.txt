The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully characterizing the functional spaces induced by DCNNs in terms of multilayer kernels. It also demonstrates that the resulting architecture possesses desirable properties, such as approximate invariance to group transformations and stability with respect to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend this paper for acceptance, provided the authors address the following comments.

*   The primary contribution of this work appears to be the reformulation of the DCNN architecture within the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The authors should elaborate on the practical or theoretical benefits of this reformulation. For instance, could this kernel-based view lead to new insights on generalization bounds, or does it offer computational advantages through kernel approximation techniques? More generally, the authors should clarify whether the novelty of the approach extends beyond this reformulation. Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by a more explicit discussion of its novelty in relation to Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."

*   The authors emphasize the importance of "not losing the signal information." This assertion requires further justification. For many common DCNN applications, such as image classification, the goal is not to preserve all signal information but rather to extract and retain only task-relevant features. Please comment on why complete signal preservation is a desirable property, particularly for tasks other than signal reconstruction.

*   Regarding group invariance, the authors should discuss any potential connections between their approach and the work on invariant kernels, for example, by Hans Burkhardt.

*   On a more theoretical note, you define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action to establish equivariance. Could the authors comment on whether *any* operator that commutes with all elements of the group would suffice to ensure the equivariance property of the DCNN architecture with respect to these transformations?
