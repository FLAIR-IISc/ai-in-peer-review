### Summary

This paper analyzes the stability of representations generated by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs), building on prior work in [15, 16]. The authors study the stability of these representations with respect to C¹ diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as defined in Eq. (4). They demonstrate that this stability property holds when using norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1-A2) combined with appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3). The paper also sketches an extension of this framework from the translation group on Rᵈ to more general locally compact groups in Section 4, and establishes a connection between the CKN functional space and traditional CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.

### Strengths

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound. It provides a valuable theoretical contribution by bridging concepts from two important areas: deep networks and kernel methods. The main stability result, which extends prior work on scattering transforms to a broader class of learnable architectures, is of significant interest to the machine learning community and sheds new light on the geometric properties of deep convolutional representations.

### Suggestions for Improvement

*   The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed motivation for the stability analysis. While the introduction briefly mentions (lines 56-57) the connection between Lipschitz stability and robustness to adversarial examples [8], expanding on the broader implications of stability (e.g., for generalization, sample complexity, or understanding invariances) would better frame the main contribution.

*   The notation κ is overloaded, which could cause confusion for the reader. It is used for both the patch size constant in assumption (A3) on line 193 and the function defining the kernel Kₖ in Eq. (2). Using a different symbol for one of these would improve clarity.

### Minor Corrections and Typos

*   In the appendix (specifically, the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385), the second constraint involving '∀v' appears to be redundant. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, this constraint is identical to the first one involving '∀u'.

*   On line 416, the feature map φ is used, but its definition seems to be missing from the main text. It would be helpful to explicitly introduce it alongside the kernel definition in Eq. (2), for instance, by writing Kₖ(z, z') = <φₖ(z), φₖ(z')>_{Hₖ}.

*   On lines 427-428, there appears to be a typo. The term `| ||z|| - ||z|| |²` should likely be `| ||z|| - ||z'|| |²`.
