- Presents a theoretical study of invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations by generalizing the multilayer kernel framework of CKNs to continuous signals.  
- Shows a principled construction (patch extraction, kernel mapping, pooling) that is nearly invariant to group transformations and stable to small diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Extends scattering-transform formalism, especially Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms, to the multilayer kernel setting.  
- Proves stability bounds where translation-invariance is controlled by the scale of the final pooling layer.  
- Establishes that CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS of the constructed kernel, allowing transfer of stability guarantees to such CNNs.  
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis linking RKHS norm, deformation stability, and generalization capacity; significant theoretical contribution.  
- Originality/clarity: novel unified multilayer kernel approach, non-trivial extension of scattering proofs, well-written with clear multilayer construction.  
- Practical relevance: discusses discretization and kernel approximation to connect continuous theory to implementations.  
- Main weakness: complete absence of empirical validation to confirm theoretical bounds and pooling-scale effects.  
- Suggested experiment gap: measure stability of CKNs/CNNs under controlled diffeomorphisms and vary final pooling scale σn to verify Theorem 6.  
- Question on normalization: how critical is the specific normalization nk(u)=||Pk x_{k-1}(u)|| and can analysis extend to batch normalization or other practical schemes?  
- Question on kernel approximation: can alternative approximations (random Fourier features, Nyström) be used and how would they affect stability bounds, assuming non-expansive mappings?  
- Overall: high-quality, significant theoretical paper whose impact would be strengthened by empirical validation; recommendation leans toward acceptance.
