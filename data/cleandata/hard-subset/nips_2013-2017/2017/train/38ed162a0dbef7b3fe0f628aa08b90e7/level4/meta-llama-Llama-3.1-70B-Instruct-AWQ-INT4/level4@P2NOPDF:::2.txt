The primary focus of the paper is on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors analyze the stability of the representation formed by CKNs with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms, such as translation, in the sense of Eq. (4). They demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] when the patch sizes are appropriately chosen [(A3)]. The authors also sketch an extension of their results from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from deep networks and kernels. The stability result has the potential to be of interest to the machine learning community.

To further strengthen the submission, the authors could provide additional motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only motivation provided is a brief mention of the importance of Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models in achieving robustness to adversarial examples [7] (line 56-57).

A minor point of clarification is needed in the notation used in (A3) [line 193], as the symbol \kappa is overloaded and also denotes a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).

In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') appears to be redundant, as it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...') due to the symmetry of the kernel k.

Additionally, the definition of \phi is missing and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)} (line 416).

A small correction is also needed in the inequality on lines 427-428. The correct inequality should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2 instead of | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2, as the equality also holds.
