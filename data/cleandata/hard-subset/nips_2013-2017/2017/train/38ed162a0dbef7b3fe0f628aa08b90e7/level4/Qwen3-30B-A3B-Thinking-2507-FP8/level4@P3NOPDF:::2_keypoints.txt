- Presents a rigorous analysis showing CKNs are stable to C¹ diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) when patch sizes are chosen appropriately and kernels satisfy A1–A3.  
- Extends stability results to locally compact groups (Section 4), bridging deep learning and kernel methods.  
- Technical results are sound, exposition is clear, and the paper is well-structured and valuable to the literature.  
- Main weakness: motivation for studying stability under diffeomorphisms is weak—current justification (lines 56–57) merely cites a link to adversarial robustness without elaboration.  
- Recommend adding discussion connecting stability to practical concerns (invariance to small deformations in vision, generalization guarantees) and clarifying how this complements/contrasts with Lipschitz continuity results for neural networks.  
- Notation conflict: κ is overloaded in Assumption (A3) and Equation (10); use distinct symbols (e.g., κ for patch size, κ_K for kernel parameter).  
- Redundant constraint: the displayed equation between lines 384–385 contains a superfluous "∀v…" constraint due to kernel symmetry; the "∀u…" constraint suffices.  
- Missing definition: φ is not defined at line 416 and should be introduced explicitly in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z), φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.  
- Typo in inequality: lines 427–428 show |∥z∥ - ∥z∥|^2 but should read |∥z∥ - ∥z'∥|^2.  
- References need minor corrections: page numbers missing for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31].  
- Specific reference fixes required: update [9], [17], [19], and [32] to the corrected citation formats provided in the review.  
- Overall verdict: technically strong and potentially impactful; suitable for acceptance after addressing motivation, notation, minor technical fixes, and reference corrections.
