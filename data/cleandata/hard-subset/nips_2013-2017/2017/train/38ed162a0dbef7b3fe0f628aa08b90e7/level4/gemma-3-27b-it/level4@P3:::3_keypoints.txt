- Reformulates CNNs within an RKHS framework to analyze stability and invariance properties.  
- Extends convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Shows models in this functional space inherit stability guarantees similar to scattering transforms while remaining learnable.  
- Theoretical analysis is mathematically rigorous with detailed proofs.  
- Provides a significant generalization and links RKHS norms to stability and potential generalization bounds.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and uses consistent notation.  
- Advances understanding of the functional geometry of CNNs with implications for robustness and generalization.  
- Practical benefits of the RKHS reformulation are not clearly articulated (e.g., tighter bounds or computational gains).  
- Relationship and novelty relative to Mairal et al.’s CKNs need clearer, more detailed comparison.  
- Emphasis on preserving full signal information lacks discussion of task-relevant information trade-offs.  
- Connection to existing invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Burkhardt) is missing and should be addressed.  
- Suggest adding a paragraph on practical advantages of the RKHS view (generalization, computation, regularization).  
- Suggest clarifying specific generalizations over CKNs and their concrete benefits.  
- Suggest discussing the trade-off between preserving full signal vs. task-relevant information.  
- Suggest citing and discussing Burkhardt’s invariant-kernel work.  
- Suggest acknowledging that other operator choices (patch extraction, pooling, kernel maps) can also achieve equivariance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, rigorous analysis, high potential for future work, and only minor fixable weaknesses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical implications are under-explored and links to related work (CKNs) need strengthening.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
