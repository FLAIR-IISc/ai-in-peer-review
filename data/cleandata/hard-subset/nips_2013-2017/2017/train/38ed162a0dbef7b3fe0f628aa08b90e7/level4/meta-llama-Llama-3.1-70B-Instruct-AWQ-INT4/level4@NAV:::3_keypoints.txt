- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and demonstrates DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels with invariance and stability properties; recommendation: accept pending minor revisions.
- Main contribution framed as reformulating CNNs in RKHS; authors should expand on practical benefits (e.g., generalization bounds, computational efficiency, kernel approximations).
- Clarify whether the work is more than a reformulation and explicitly state novelty relative to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (Mairal et al.).
- Justify the emphasis on preserving "signal information" given many DCNN tasks only require task-relevant features (e.g., image classification).
- Explore connections between the paper’s group invariance results and Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach.
- Investigate whether other operators that commute with all group elements can also guarantee equivariance, beyond the defined patch, pooling, and kernel maps.
