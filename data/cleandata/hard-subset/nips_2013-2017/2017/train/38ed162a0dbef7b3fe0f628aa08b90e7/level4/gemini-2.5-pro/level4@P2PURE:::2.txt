This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. Specifically, the authors study stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translations), as defined in Eq. (4). They show that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1-A2, line 193), stability is achieved with appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3). A potential extension of this framework from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is also sketched in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, successfully integrating ideas from the exciting fields of deep networks and kernel methods. The main stability result is a valuable contribution that should be of interest to the machine learning community.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   The manuscript would be strengthened by providing a more thorough motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the main contribution is motivated by a single sentence (lines 56-57) that connects Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness. Expanding on why this particular form of stability is important would enhance the paper's impact.
*   The overloading of the notation \kappa in assumption (A3) (line 193) may cause confusion, as \kappa is also used to denote the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).
*   In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part of the constraint ('and ∀v...') appears to be superfluous; given the symmetry of kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ('∀u ...').
*   Line 416: The definition of φ is missing. It should be introduced in Eq. (10), for example, as K(z,z') = <φ(z),φ(z')>_{H(K)}.
*   Lines 427-428: There appears to be a typo in the inequality under the equality sign; it should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2 instead of | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2. Additionally, this inequality seems to hold with equality.
