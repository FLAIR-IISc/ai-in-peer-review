

The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how specific classes of functional spaces implemented by CNNs can be characterized using multilayer kernels. It also establishes notable architectural properties: (nearly) invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic deformations. I recommend acceptance pending the following clarifications:

1.  **Justification for RKHS Reformulation:** The primary contribution appears to be reframing CNN architecture within the RKHS framework. The authors should explicitly discuss the *practical benefits* of this reformulation. For instance, does the kernel formulation enable new insights into generalization bounds? Could it lead to computationally more efficient implementations via standard kernel approximation techniques? More broadly, how does the novelty extend beyond a mere reformulation of CNNs in RKHS terms? A clear comparison with Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (2014, *ICML*) is essential to establish distinctiveness.

2.  **Clarification on Signal Preservation:** The emphasis on "not losing signal information" requires justification. CNNs are typically deployed for discriminative tasks (e.g., image classification), where preserving *task-relevant* features—not the entire signal—is paramount. The authors should clarify why preserving the full signal is necessary or beneficial for the intended applications, rather than focusing solely on task-specific information retention.

3.  **Connection to Invariant Kernel Methods:** The group invariance property warrants a connection to established work. Specifically, is there a relationship between the proposed approach and invariant kernel methods, such as those explored by Burkhardt (e.g., *Invariant Kernel Methods*)? Highlighting this link (or explaining its absence) would strengthen the theoretical grounding.

4.  **Generality of Commuting Operators:** The paper defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with group actions to ensure equivariance. The authors should address whether *any* operator commuting with the group action would suffice to guarantee the equivariance property of CNNs under the given transformations, or if specific structural constraints are necessary. This would clarify the scope and necessity of the proposed definitions.
