This paper investigates desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). It shows that, with carefully designed patch-extraction, kernel-mapping, and pooling operators, CKNs are nearly invariant to certain transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphic deformations. Under appropriate assumptions, the authors further argue that these properties carry over to standard CNNs.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and technically sound. My main concern is the lack of empirical validation. I would like to see experiments that support the theoretical claims—specifically, demonstrations that CKNs, when configured as proposed, exhibit the predicted invariance to transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms.

I also have a question about kernel approximation within this framework. Could random projections or explicit kernel mappings using a finite number of Fourier components be viable alternatives? If so, how would such approximations impact the stated stability guarantees?
