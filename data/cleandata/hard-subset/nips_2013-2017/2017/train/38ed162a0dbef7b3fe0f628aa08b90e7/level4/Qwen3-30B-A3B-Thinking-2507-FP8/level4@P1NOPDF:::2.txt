

The paper focuses on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14], analyzing their representation stability under C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formalized in Equation (4). The authors establish that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (satisfying assumptions A1–A2) with appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3). An extension to locally compact groups is briefly sketched in Section 4.

The manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively bridging two active research areas: deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result holds potential interest for the machine learning community.

However, the paper would benefit from stronger motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, only a single sentence (lines 56–57) links stability to adversarial robustness via [7], which feels underdeveloped given the contribution’s significance.

Several technical points require clarification:
- The notation κ in assumption (A3) (line 193) is overloaded; it also defines the kernel function in Equation (10). This risks confusion and should be resolved.
- The displayed equation between lines 384 and 385 contains a redundant condition: the clause "and ∀v..." is identical to the first constraint "∀u..." due to kernel symmetry, making it superfluous.
- The definition of φ is missing at line 416. It should be explicitly introduced in Equation (10) as ⟨φ(z), φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
- In lines 427–428, the inequality under "=" likely holds with equality. The term | ||z|| − ||z|| |² should be | ||z|| − ||z'|| |².
