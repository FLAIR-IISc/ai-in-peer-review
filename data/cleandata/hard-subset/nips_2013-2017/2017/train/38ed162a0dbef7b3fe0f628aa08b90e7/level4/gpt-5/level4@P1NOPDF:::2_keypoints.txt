- Paper analyzes stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C^1 diffeomorphisms and proves stability under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2) when patch sizes satisfy A3.  
- Extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups is sketched in Section 4 but not fully developed.  
- Overall assessment: well organized, clearly written, technically sound, and of interest to the ML community.  
- Major suggestion: provide stronger motivation for studying stability specifically for CKNs, clarify which practical perturbations matter (small deformations, translations, general diffeomorphisms), and compare to existing stability bounds (scattering networks, Lipschitz-constrained CNNs).  
- Notation issue: Assumption (A3) overloads the symbol kappa, which conflicts with kappa used for kernel K in Eq. (10); use distinct symbols.  
- Redundancy: the second condition in the displayed equation between lines 384–385 is redundant by kernel symmetry; remove or note equivalence.  
- Missing definition: introduce phi as the feature map for K in Eq. (10) (i.e., <phi(z),phi(z')> = K(z,z')).  
- Typo/inequality: lines 427–428 the inequality appears to be equality, and | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z'|| |^2.  
- Scope/generalization: expand the group-extension discussion to address technical hurdles (measure-theoretic issues, invariance/equivariance, role of group representations in defining K).  
- References: add missing page ranges for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31] and correct entries for [9], [17], [19], and update [32] to the ICML 2017 poster citation.  
- Recommendation: strengthen motivation, fix notational/technical issues, and complete bibliographic details to improve the paper.
