- Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel generalizing Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to analyze deep convolutional representations.
- Proves representations are stable to C1-diffeomorphisms, with changes bounded by the deformation magnitude and gradient norm (Theorem 6).
- Shows the RKHS of the proposed kernel contains a broad class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, extending stability guarantees to these CNNs.
- Sketches extension of the framework to obtain equivariance and invariance to locally compact group actions.
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of stability and invariance for deep convolutional architectures.
- Strength: forges a novel bridge between kernel methods and deep learning, linking RKHS norms to CNN generalization.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and presents the multilayer kernel construction and proofs methodically.
- Strength: highly relevant to robustness and generalization concerns in modern deep learning.
- Weakness: introduction could better motivate the stability analysis and more clearly connect it to adversarial robustness and natural data variations.
- Argument for acceptance: delivers a novel, rigorous theoretical contribution on stability for deep convolutional representations.
- Argument for acceptance: establishes a formal connection between CKNs, a class of CNNs, and scattering network theory.
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound and well-written, addressing an important timely problem.
- Argument against acceptance: contribution is primarily theoretical unification/extension rather than a new architecture or empirical SOTA result (minor concern).
- Detail: ambiguous notation—κ used both as a constant and as the kernel function in assumption (A3).
- Detail: Appendix B, Lemma B.1 contains a seemingly redundant constraint due to kernel symmetry.
- Detail: Appendix B after Lemma B.2 should state the feature map φ alongside the kernel K = <φ(z), φ(z')> more explicitly.
- Detail: Appendix C.2 likely typo: term | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2, and the preceding inequality appears tight.
- Detail: several references need updated or complete publication details (e.g., [9], [17], [19], [36]) and others should include full citation information.
