- Paper studies Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) and analyzes stability of CKN representations to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) per Eq. (4).
- Shows stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2) when patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3).
- Sketches an extension of results from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups in Section 4.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining deep networks and kernel ideas.
- Stability result is potentially of interest to the machine learning community.
- The authors should provide stronger motivation for the stability analysis beyond a brief link to Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness.
- Notation issue: the symbol κ in (A3) is overloaded and conflicts with the kernel-defining function in Eq. (10).
- Redundancy: the displayed equation between lines 384–385 repeats the same constraint ('∀v...') due to kernel symmetry and can be simplified.
- Missing definition: φ is not introduced and should be defined in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
- Correction needed in inequality on lines 427–428: should be | ||z|| − ||z'|| |^2 (not | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2).
- References need page information for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31], and corrected citation details are provided for [9,17,19,32].
