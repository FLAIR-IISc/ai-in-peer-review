This paper investigates the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs). The authors' main contribution is a theoretical analysis demonstrating that CKNs are stable with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms, such as translations, as defined in Equation (4). They prove that this stability property holds under specific assumptions on the kernel—namely, that it is norm-preserving and non-expansive (A1-A2)—and for appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3). The paper concludes by sketching an extension of this analysis from the group (R^d,+) to more general locally compact groups.

Overall, this is a strong submission. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and the technical arguments appear sound. The work presents a compelling synthesis of ideas from deep learning and kernel methods, two highly relevant areas of machine learning. The central stability result is a valuable contribution and should be of significant interest to the community.

While the technical contribution is strong, the motivation for the stability analysis could be significantly expanded. The paper currently dedicates only a single sentence (lines 56-57) to this topic, linking Lipschitz stability to robustness against adversarial examples. A more detailed discussion on why this form of stability is a desirable property for CKNs and other deep representations would strengthen the paper's impact and better frame its contribution for a broader audience.

**Minor Comments:**

*   The overloaded notation for \kappa in (A3) [line 193] could be confusing, as it is also used for the function defining the kernel K in Equation (10). Using a different symbol would improve clarity.
*   In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint ("and \forall v...") appears to be redundant. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ("\forall u...").
*   Line 416: The definition of the feature map \phi is missing. It would be helpful to introduce it alongside Equation (10), for instance, by defining K(z, z') = <\phi(z), \phi(z')>_H.
*   Lines 427-428: There appears to be a typo in the inequality. The term | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should likely be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2. Furthermore, the inequality preceding the equality sign seems to hold with equality, as the expression is an identity.
