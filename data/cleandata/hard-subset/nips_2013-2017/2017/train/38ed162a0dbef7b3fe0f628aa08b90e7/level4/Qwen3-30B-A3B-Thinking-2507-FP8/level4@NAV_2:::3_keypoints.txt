- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations via multilayer kernel constructions in RKHS.  
- Shows near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations while preserving signal information.  
- Main contribution: formalizes CNN architectures within the RKHS framework, extending convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) with theoretical guarantees for stability and invariance.  
- Paper effectively demonstrates how standard CNNs fit into this functional space but needs clearer articulation of practical utility.  
- Authors should explain how the kernel perspective yields new insights on generalization bounds, computational efficiency, or alternative implementation strategies versus standard CNNs.  
- Request clarification on whether this formulation enables principled regularization or computational advantages via kernel approximation techniques.  
- Novelty relative to Mairal et al. (CKNs, NIPS 2014) is unclear and should be explicitly distinguished.  
- Connection to prior invariant-kernel work (e.g., Burkhardt 2007) should be explicitly addressed.  
- Clarify the practical importance of “not losing signal information” versus preserving only task-relevant information, and how this impacts performance.  
- Question whether any operator commuting with all group elements suffices for equivariance or if specific patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling choices are necessary.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound; recommends acceptance conditional on addressing the above points.
