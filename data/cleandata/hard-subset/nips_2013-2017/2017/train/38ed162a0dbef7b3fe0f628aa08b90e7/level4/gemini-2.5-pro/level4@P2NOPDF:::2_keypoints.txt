- Main contribution: theoretical proof that Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) are stable to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) as defined in Eq. (4).
- Stability shown under assumptions that the kernel is norm-preserving and non-expansive (A1–A2) and with appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3).
- Sketches an extension of the stability analysis from the group (R^d, +) to more general locally compact groups.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and the technical arguments appear sound.
- Presents a compelling synthesis of deep learning and kernel methods; the central stability result is a valuable contribution to the community.
- Motivation is weak: the paper gives only a single sentence linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness and should expand on why this stability is desirable for CKNs and deep representations.
- Minor: overloaded notation for κ in (A3) conflicts with κ used in Eq. (10); suggest using a different symbol.
- Minor: displayed equation between lines 384–385 contains a redundant constraint due to kernel symmetry.
- Minor: definition of the feature map φ is missing and should be introduced alongside Eq. (10), e.g., K(z,z') = ⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_H.
- Minor: typo on lines 427–428—| ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should likely be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2, and the preceding inequality appears to be an equality.
- Bibliography: many entries ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]) are missing page information.
- Bibliography corrections: [9] is published as Daniely et al., NIPS 2016, pp.2253–2261; [17] is Muandet et al., Foundations and Trends in ML, 10(1–2):1–141, 2017; [19] is Raj et al., AISTATS PMLR 54:1225–1235, 2017; [32] accepted to ICML 2017: Zhang, Liang, Wainwright, "Convexified Convolutional Neural Networks."
