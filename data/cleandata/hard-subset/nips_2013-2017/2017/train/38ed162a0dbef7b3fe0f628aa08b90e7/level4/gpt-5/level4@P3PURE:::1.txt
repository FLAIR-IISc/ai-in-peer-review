This paper studies important properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). It shows that, with carefully designed patch-extraction, kernel-mapping, and pooling operators, CKNs are nearly invariant to groups of transformations and are stable under the action of diffeomorphisms. The authors also argue that, under certain assumptions, these desirable properties extend to conventional CNNs.

Overall, the paper is well written and technically sound. The main weakness is the absence of empirical validation to support the theoretical analysis. It would be valuable to see experiments demonstrating that CKNs, with appropriate settings, indeed exhibit invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.

I also have a question regarding kernel approximation: could random projections or explicit kernel mappings using a finite number of Fourier features serve as practical alternatives? If so, what is their effect on the stability guarantees?
