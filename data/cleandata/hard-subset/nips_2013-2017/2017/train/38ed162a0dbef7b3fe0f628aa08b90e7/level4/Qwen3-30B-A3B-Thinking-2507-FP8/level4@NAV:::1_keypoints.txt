- Paper studies deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their theoretical properties.  
- Shows CKNs exhibit near invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.  
- Proves these favorable properties can extend to conventional CNNs under specific assumptions.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technically rigorous.  
- Major limitation: lacks empirical validation; experiments should substantiate claimed invariance and stability for appropriate CKN configurations.  
- Suggestion: evaluate whether random projection or finite-dimensional Fourier-series kernel approximations are viable and analyze their effect on stability.
