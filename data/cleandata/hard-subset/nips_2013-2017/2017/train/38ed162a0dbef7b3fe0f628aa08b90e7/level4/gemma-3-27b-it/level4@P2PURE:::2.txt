The paper focuses on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14] and analyzes the stability of the representations learned by these networks – specifically, stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation), as defined in Eq. (4). The authors demonstrate that stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes [(A3)] when using norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193]. A potential extension to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4. 

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining ideas from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result presented is likely to be of interest to the machine learning community.

However, the submission would benefit from more extensive motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the motivation is limited to a single sentence (lines 56-57: ‘Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].’).

The notation of \kappa in (A3) [line 193] is potentially confusing as it also represents a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).

In the equation displayed between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is redundant; due to the symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first constraint ('\forall u ...').

Line 416: The definition of \phi is missing and should be included in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}].

Line 427-428: The inequality under the '=' sign appears to hold as an equality. The term should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2 rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2.
