- Summary: Analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), proving near-invariance to certain group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms given suitable patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling, and argues analogous properties can extend to CNNs under assumptions.

- Strength: Provides a clear theoretical framework formalizing invariance and stability for CKNs.

- Strength: Offers a useful conceptual bridge connecting CKN behavior to CNN architectures.

- Strength: Mathematical development appears rigorous and well structured.

- Weakness: Lacks empirical validation, making practical relevance and tightness of bounds unclear.

- Weakness: “Near-invariance” and “stability” are not always quantified in an interpretable way; classes of transformations/diffeomorphisms need clearer delineation.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of whether finite-dimensional kernel approximations (e.g., random Fourier features) preserve stability guarantees and how constants degrade.

- Question: Which transformation groups (translations, rotations, scalings, etc.) are explicitly covered, and how does invariance depend on pooling scales and patch sizes?

- Question: How do stability constants depend on depth, patch stride, kernel bandwidth, and pooling; do effects accumulate across layers?

- Question: What precise assumptions are required for the CNN results, and how do filter approximation errors propagate through depth?

- Question: Can common kernel approximations (random Fourier, Nyström, structured projections) be accommodated, and how do approximation errors affect bounds and feature-count trade-offs?

- Question: What is the computational complexity of the proposed CKN constructions versus standard CNNs, and how does that affect scalability?

- Empirical suggestion: Test CKNs on controlled transformations (translations, rotations, scalings) varying patch and pooling configurations and report accuracy or feature-distance vs transformation magnitude.

- Empirical suggestion: Validate stability with synthetic diffeomorphic deformations (elastic warps) and plot representation distance versus deformation amplitude.

- Empirical suggestion: Compare CKNs to CNNs designed to mirror the architecture to substantiate transfer claims.

- Empirical suggestion: Evaluate exact kernel mappings vs random feature/Nyström approximations to study how number of features affects empirical stability and performance.

- Empirical suggestion: Run ablations over patch sizes, strides, kernel bandwidths, and pooling scales to illustrate invariance vs sensitivity trade-offs.

- Relation to prior work: Clarify differences and connections to scattering networks and their established diffeomorphism stability results.

- Clarity/presentation: Define “near-invariance” and “stability” early with precise norms/metrics and summarize main theorems intuitively, highlighting how constants scale with architectural parameters.

- Minor: Fix typos and spacing (e.g., “i.e. CKNs” and “diffeomorphisms”).

- Overall assessment: Technically solid and timely theoretical contribution linking CKNs and CNNs, but impact is limited by lack of experiments and discussion on approximations and constants; recommends adding targeted experiments and clarifications.
