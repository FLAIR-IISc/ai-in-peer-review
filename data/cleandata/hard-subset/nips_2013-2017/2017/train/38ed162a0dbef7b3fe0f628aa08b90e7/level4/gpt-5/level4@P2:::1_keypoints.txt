- Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel representation with near-invariance to transformation groups and Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to continuous domains using per-layer patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernel maps into an RKHS, and Gaussian pooling.
- Analyzes translation equivariance of intermediate layers and shows larger final-layer pooling increases global translation invariance.
- Extends construction to general groups via group-equivariant patch extraction and pooling for global invariance.
- Derives stability bounds to diffeomorphisms (scattering-style) with constants depending on deformation Jacobian norm and pooling scale.
- Treats discretization and kernel approximation, proving stability is preserved under non-expansive finite-dimensional projections.
- Shows a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS of the proposed kernel, allowing RKHS-norm control of CNN stability and Lipschitz constants.
- Builds on and extends scattering theory and CKNs, connecting to group-equivariant CNNs, invariant kernels, and kernel views of deep networks.
- Strength: provides a clear, principled RKHS framework unifying CKNs and certain CNNs.
- Strength: offers rigorous, intuitive stability bounds and shows translation invariance increases with pooling depth.
- Strength: careful analysis of discretization and kernel approximations preserving stability.
- Strength: group-equivariant formulation gives a clean path to global invariance across transformation groups.
- Strength: links RKHS norm to stability and generalization insights.
- Weakness: no empirical validation; theoretical claims need experiments to quantify constants and practical behavior.
- Weakness: restrictive assumptions (smoothed homogeneous activations, Gaussian pooling); effects of common nonsmooth activations and practical choices (stride, padding) unexplored.
- Weakness: constants may depend unfavorably on patch radius, dimension, and depth; limited practical parameter guidance.
- Weakness: boundary effects and initial anti-aliasing are noted but not empirically assessed.
- Quality: theoretical claims are well supported and proofs appear standard, but experimental corroboration is missing for finite-dimensional/discrete cases.
- Clarity: paper is well organized but notation is dense; requests for more intuition on constants, nk(u), and summarized assumptions.
- Originality: extending scattering-style stability to multilayer kernel representations and including CNNs in the RKHS is original and valuable.
- Significance: deepens theoretical understanding of invariance/stability and could influence architecture design and regularization if validated empirically.
- Key suggestions: discuss RFF vs Nyström for kernel approximation and conditions for non-expansiveness and stability degradation.
- Key suggestions: analyze sensitivity to nonsmoothed (leaky-)ReLU and bound approximation gaps in RKHS norm.
- Key suggestions: provide discretization experiments quantifying reconstruction error and numerical stability under diffeomorphisms.
- Key suggestions: give practical rules of thumb for choosing pooling scales and patch sizes to balance invariance, information preservation, and stability.
- Recommendation: accept if accompanied by minimal empirical validation demonstrating predicted stability/invariance and effects of finite-dimensional approximations.
