- Paper studies Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs), analyzing stability of their representation to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) as defined in Eq. (4).
- Authors prove stability for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2) when using appropriately chosen patch sizes (A3).
- Section 4 extends the stability concept from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups.
- Paper is well organized and clear, with strong technical soundness combining deep network and kernel ideas.
- The stability result is potentially of significant interest to the machine learning community.
- Motivation for the stability analysis is weak—currently limited to a single sentence linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness.
- Notation clash: the use of κ in (A3) may conflict with the kernel function K in Eq. (10).
- Redundant constraint: the second part of the equation between lines 384–385 ('and ∀v...') is redundant due to kernel symmetry.
- Missing definition: ϕ should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨ϕ(z),ϕ(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
- Possible typo in lines 427–428: an inequality appears to hold with equality, and |‖z‖−‖z‖|^2 should be |‖z‖−‖z'‖|^2.
- Several references lack page information: [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31].
- References [9], [17], and [19] have been updated with correct publication details.
- Reference [32] has been accepted and is available at the provided ICML 2017 link.
