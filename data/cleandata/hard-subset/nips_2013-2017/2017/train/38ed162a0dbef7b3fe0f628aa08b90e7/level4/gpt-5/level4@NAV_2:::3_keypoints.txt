- Paper is clearly written and technically rigorous.  
- Shows that a class of functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional networks can be characterized via multilayer kernels.  
- Resulting architectures exhibit approximate invariance to group actions and Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphic deformations.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance subject to addressing comments.  
- Main contribution appears to be an RKHS reformulation of CNN architectures; author should clarify why this viewpoint is valuable (e.g., sharper generalization bounds, computational benefits via kernel approximations).  
- Clarify whether the novelty goes beyond an RKHS restatement and explicitly delineate differences from “Convolutional Kernel Networks” (Mairal et al.).  
- Question the emphasis on “not losing signal information”: why is full signal preservation essential versus preserving only task-relevant information?  
- Ask about relationship to invariant-kernel approaches (Haasdonk and Burkhardt).  
- Curious whether any operator in the commutant (i.e., commuting with all group elements) would ensure equivariance of the DCNN to those group transformations.
