- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes CKNs to continuous-domain signals.  
- Demonstrates the representation is stable to small C1-diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows near-translation invariance, with invariance degree controlled by final pooling scale.  
- Extends framework to achieve global invariance to any locally compact group via adapted patch extraction and pooling.  
- Proves the RKHS of the kernel contains a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations.  
- Connects stability and invariance guarantees for the kernel directly to these CNNs through the RKHS norm.  
- Technical framework is rigorous, using operator theory in Hilbert spaces, with clear logical progression.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers versed in harmonic analysis and kernel methods.  
- Original synthesis of scattering transforms, kernel methods, and deep learning; extends scattering stability to learnable models.  
- Provides a theoretical basis linking RKHS norm, stability, and generalization, suggesting implications for regularization and architecture design.  
- Addresses discretization and practical approximations of the continuous framework.  
- Main weakness: no empirical validation or experiments.  
- Recommends experiments to confirm that pooling scale and model norm control stability and task performance under transformations.  
- Asks whether other kernel approximations (e.g., random Fourier features, Nyström) would preserve the stability guarantees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous framework; extends stability analysis to learned networks; strong RKHS-stability-generalization link; high technical quality.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of experiments leaves practical relevance and bound tightness unverified.
