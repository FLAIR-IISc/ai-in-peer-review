The primary focus of the paper is CKN (convolutional kernel network) [15, 16]. In this manuscript the authors analyse the stability [with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation), in the sense of Eq. (4)] of the representation formed by CKNs. They show that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] stability holds for appropriately chosen patch sizes [(A3)]. Extension from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is sketched in Section 4.

The paper is nicely organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from two exciting areas (deep networks and kernels). The stability result can be of interest to the ML community. 

Here are some suggestions for improvement:
-The submission would benefit from adding further motivation for the stability analysis. Currently there is only one short sentence (line 56-57: 'Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [8].') which motivates the main contribution of the paper. Expanding on this connection to robustness, or highlighting other benefits of stability (e.g., sample complexity), would strengthen the introduction.
-The notation \kappa in (A3) [line 193] might be confusing, as it also denotes a function defining kernel K in Eq. (2). Consider using a different symbol.
-In the displayed equation between line 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is superfluous; given the symmetry of kernel K, it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...').
-Line 416: the definition of \phi is missing; it should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}].
-Line 427-428: The inequality under '=' seems to also hold with equality, | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of stability and invariance properties of CKNs and related architectures.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and the technical concepts are explained clearly.
*   **Novelty:** The work extends existing theories of scattering transforms to a broader class of CNNs, providing new insights into their behavior.
*   **Significance:** The results contribute to a better understanding of deep network generalization and robustness.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Motivation:** The motivation for the stability analysis could be strengthened.
*   **Practical impact:** While the theoretical results are interesting, it is not immediately clear how they can be readily applied to improve practical CNN architectures.
*   **Complexity:** The mathematical formalism might be challenging for some readers.

**Overall:** I believe this is a solid theoretical contribution that is well-suited for NIPS. The minor issues can be easily addressed with revisions. I recommend acceptance, contingent on addressing the points raised above.
