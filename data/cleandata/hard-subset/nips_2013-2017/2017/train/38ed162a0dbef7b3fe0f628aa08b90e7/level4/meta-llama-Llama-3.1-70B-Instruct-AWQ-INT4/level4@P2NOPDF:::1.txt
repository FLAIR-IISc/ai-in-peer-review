This paper explores desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), specifically their near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also demonstrate that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties of CKNs can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. However, its primary weakness lies in the absence of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to include experimental results that demonstrate the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms under appropriate settings.

Additionally, it is worth investigating whether random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series could serve as alternative solutions for kernel approximation. If so, it would be interesting to examine the impact on stability.
