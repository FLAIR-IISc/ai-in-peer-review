- The paper rigorously shows DCNN functional spaces can be characterized by multilayer kernels, yielding near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Recommendation: accept if authors address the following concerns.  
- Clarify contribution/novelty: explicitly state practical or theoretical benefits of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., generalization bounds, kernel approximations) and clarify novelty beyond a formal re-characterization; compare to Mairal et al.’s Convolutional Kernel Networks.  
- Role of signal information preservation: justify emphasizing full signal preservation versus preserving only task-relevant discriminative information (e.g., for classification).  
- Connection to invariant kernels: relate and contrast the framework with established invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Hans Burkhardt).  
- Generalizing equivariance: discuss whether any operator that commutes with all group elements suffices for equivariance and comment on the generality of the commuting condition.
