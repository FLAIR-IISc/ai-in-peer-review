- Paper studies theoretical properties of deep signal representations that are near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms.  
- Generalizes multilayer convolutional kernel from convolutional kernel networks and analyzes the geometry of the corresponding RKHS.  
- Positions work relative to CNNs, scattering networks, and kernel methods, drawing on scattering results to extend to broader CNN architectures.  
- Main contribution: construction of a multilayer convolutional kernel for d-dimensional signals.  
- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions, enabling unified theoretical results.  
- Proves the kernel construction preserves signal information under discretization.  
- Establishes stability of the kernel representation to the action of diffeomorphisms.  
- Analyzes how geometry of the kernel representation relates to invariance and stability properties of CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, well-organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate theoretical claims about invariance and stability.  
- Raises open question whether random projection or explicit finite Fourier-series kernel approximations could approximate the kernel and how that affects stability.  
- Review scores high on quality, clarity, and originality; overall recommendation: accept with suggestion to add experiments and explore approximation methods.
