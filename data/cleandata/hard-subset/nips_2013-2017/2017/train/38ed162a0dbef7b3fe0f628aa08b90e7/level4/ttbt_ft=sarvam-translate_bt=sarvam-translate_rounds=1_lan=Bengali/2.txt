The main goal of this paper is CKN (Convolशनल Kernel Networks) [13, 14]. In this manuscript, the authors analyze the stability of the representation formed by CKN (in the sense of Eq. (4) with respect to C^1 deformations (such as translation)). They show that stability is maintained [(A3)] for a suitably chosen patch size for norm-preserving and non-spreading kernels [(A1-A2) line 193). The expansion from R^d, + to a locally compact group is illustrated in Figure 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, technically sound, and combines the concepts of two exciting fields (deep networks and kernels). The stability results may be of interest to the ML community.

- The submission would benefit from more inspiration on stability analysis. Currently, there is only one short sentence (lines 56-57: 'Finally, we note that deep predictive models have proven important for obtaining robustness for adversarial examples [7]') which inspires the main contribution of this paper.
- The excessive use of the symbol \kappa in (A3) [line 193] can be misleading, it also refers to a function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).
- In the equation shown between lines 384 and 385, the second part ('and \forall v...') is unnecessary; considering the symmetry of the kernel k, it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u ...').
- Line 416: The definition of \phi is missing, it should be introduced in Eq. (10) [=<\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}]
- Lines 427-428: The inequality under '=' seems to apply to the equality as well, | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2.
