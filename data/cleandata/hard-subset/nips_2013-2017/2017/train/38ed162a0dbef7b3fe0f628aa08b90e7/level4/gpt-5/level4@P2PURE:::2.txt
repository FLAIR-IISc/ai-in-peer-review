The paper centers on convolutional kernel networks (CKN) [13, 14]. It analyzes the stability, with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (including translations) as defined in Eq. (4), of the representations produced by CKNs. The authors show that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2, line 193) when patch sizes are appropriately chosen (A3). An extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result is likely to be of interest to the ML community.

- The submission would benefit from a stronger motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only motivation is a brief sentence (lines 56–57: “Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”).
- Overloading the kappa notation in (A3) (line 193) may be confusing, as kappa also denotes the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).
- In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second part (“and ∀ v ...”) is redundant; by symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first constraint (“∀ u ...”).
- Line 416: the definition of phi is missing; it should be introduced in Eq. (10) via ⟨phi(z), phi(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
- Lines 427–428: the inequality after “=” appears to hold with equality, and | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z'|| |^2.
