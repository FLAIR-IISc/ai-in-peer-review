This paper is well-written and rigorous. It successfully demonstrates how functional spaces (of some kind) implemented by DCNNs can be described in terms of a multilayer kernel. It also showcases some excellent architectural features: invariance for (approximately) group transformations and stability for deformed transformations. Considering the comments below, I recommend accepting it:

* The main contribution of the original paper to the reviewer's understanding is the rewriting of the CNNS architecture in the context of RKHS.
* Please add some comments on why/what makes this reconstruction useful (e.g., can the kernel formulation give interesting results on generalization bounds?, is it computationally more efficient using approximate kernels on the standard kernel?).
* More generally, what is new in the approach to going beyond the reconstruction of DCNNs in the context of RKHS?
* Also, clarify the novelty compared to Julien Mieral, Piotr Koniasz, Zeid Harchawi, and Cordelia Schmidt's paper "Convolशनल Kernel Networks".
* The authors emphasize the importance of "signal information loss".
* Why is this? DCNNs are often related to specific tasks (e.g., image classification). If the task is not just signal reconstruction, but only storing task-related information, please comment on this.
* Regarding group invariance: Is there any connection with the invariant kernel method (invariant kernel) adopted by Hans Burkhardt?
* More curiosity: You define patches, pooling, and kernel maps that interact with group operations. Would any other operator (within the set of operators that interact with all group elements) ensure the invariance property of DCNNs in the case of group transformations?
