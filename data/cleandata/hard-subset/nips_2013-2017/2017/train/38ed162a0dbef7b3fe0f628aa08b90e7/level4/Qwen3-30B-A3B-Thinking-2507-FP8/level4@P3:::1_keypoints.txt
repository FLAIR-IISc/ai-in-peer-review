- Paper develops a continuous-domain kernel framework generalizing multilayer CKNs to analyze convolutional representations.  
- Focuses on invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations.  
- Shows kernel-based representation preserves signal information at each layer while achieving stability to small deformations, with stability improving as pooling scale increases.  
- Establishes a formal connection between the kernel framework and standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, implying these CNNs inherit stability properties.  
- Extends the framework to handle group invariance (rotations, reflections, etc.) via group-covariant operators.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous with detailed proofs, especially for stability analysis.  
- Clearly distinguishes signal preservation from representation stability and discusses RKHS norm implications for generalization.  
- Well-motivated analysis that builds on scattering transforms and kernel methods, with appropriate citations to prior work.  
- Mathematical notation and exposition are clear and consistent; figures effectively illustrate the multilayer construction.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implications for practitioners and implementation considerations.  
- Originality: extends scattering transform results to standard convolutional architectures via a kernel approach, and generalizes to arbitrary transformation groups.  
- Significance: provides theoretical foundation for convolutional architectures’ robustness and links stability to improved sample complexity and regularization.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of stability and invariance.  
- Strength: clear connection between kernel representations and standard CNNs.  
- Strength: generalization to arbitrary transformation groups and relation to scattering literature.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation to demonstrate practical relevance of theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical kernel approximation/implementation (e.g., random features, Nyström).  
- Weakness: needs more detailed comparison to related stability/adversarial robustness work and to typical CNN architectures with non-homogeneous activations.  
- Main concern: absence of experiments to validate stability and generalization claims in practice.  
- Recommendation: accept for theoretical significance but encourage adding empirical results and more practical implementation discussion in future work.
