This paper studies convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14] and analyzes the stability of the resulting representations with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) in the sense of Eq. (4). The authors show that, under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1–A2, line 193), stability holds when patch sizes are chosen appropriately (assumption A3). They also sketch an extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

Overall, the paper is well organized, clearly written, and technically sound, drawing on ideas from both deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result should be of interest to the ML community.

Major suggestion
- The paper would benefit from a stronger motivation for studying stability. At present, the only explicit motivation is a brief note on lines 56–57 (“Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”). Please expand on why stability matters specifically for CKNs, what types of perturbations are most relevant in practice (e.g., small deformations, translations, or more general diffeomorphisms), and how your results compare with existing stability bounds in related architectures (e.g., scattering networks, CNNs with Lipschitz constraints).

Clarity and notation
- Assumption (A3), line 193: Overloading the notation kappa is potentially confusing, since kappa is also used to define the kernel K in Eq. (10). Please use distinct symbols.
- In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second condition (“and for all v …”) is redundant. By symmetry of the kernel k, it is equivalent to the first condition (“for all u …”). Consider removing it or noting the equivalence explicitly.
- Line 416: The definition of phi is missing. It should be introduced in Eq. (10) as the feature map associated with K, i.e., <phi(z), phi(z')>_{H(K)} = K(z, z').
- Lines 427–428: The inequality under the equals sign appears to hold with equality. Also, | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should be | ||z|| − ||z'|| |^2.

Scope and generality
- The extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups in Section 4 is promising but currently sketched at a high level. Including a brief discussion of the main technical hurdles (e.g., measure-theoretic aspects, invariance/equivariance properties, or the role of group representations in defining K) would improve clarity and help readers assess generality.
