

This paper primarily studies the stability of representations formed by convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) [13, 14] with respect to $C^1$ diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), as formalized in Equation (4). The authors establish that stability holds under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (Assumptions A1–A2, line 193) for appropriately chosen patch sizes (Assumption A3). The extension to locally compact groups is outlined in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively bridging two active research areas (deep networks and kernel methods). The stability result holds potential interest for the machine learning community.

However, the submission would benefit from enhanced motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only justification (lines 56–57: "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7]") is insufficiently developed to fully justify the main contribution.

Additional specific concerns:
- The notation $\kappa$ in Assumption (A3) (line 193) may cause confusion, as it is also used to denote the kernel-defining function in Equation (10).
- The displayed equation between lines 384 and 385 contains a superfluous condition ("and $\forall v$..."). Given the symmetry of kernel $k$, this is redundant with the first constraint ($\forall u$...).
- Line 416: The definition of $\phi$ is missing; it should be introduced in Equation (10) as $\langle \phi(z), \phi(z') \rangle_{H(K)}$.
- Lines 427–428: The inequality under "=" appears to hold with equality; the expression $| \|z\| - \|z\| |^2$ should be $| \|z\| - \|z'\| |^2$.
