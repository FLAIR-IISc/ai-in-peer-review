- Paper analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), showing near-invariance to transformation groups and stability under diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.
- Authors prove that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties of CKNs can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
- The paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Main weakness: absence of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis; experiments demonstrating invariance/stability are recommended.
- Suggest investigating alternative kernel approximations (random projection or explicit finite Fourier series) and assessing their impact on stability.
