The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how certain functional spaces implemented by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. Additionally, it showcases the architecture's desirable properties, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, pending the authors' consideration of the following comments.

The primary contribution of the paper, as understood by this reviewer, is the reformulation of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) architecture within the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). To further strengthen the manuscript, it would be beneficial to include comments on why this reformulation is useful. For instance, the kernel formulation may lead to interesting results on generalization bounds or be more computationally efficient to implement using standard techniques and approximations on kernels. The authors should clarify whether the novelty of their approach extends beyond a simple reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS.

Moreover, the authors should address the novelty of their approach with respect to the paper "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid. A clear comparison and distinction between the two works would enhance the manuscript's contribution and impact.

The authors emphasize the importance of "not losing the signal information." However, this is not entirely clear, as DCNNs are often employed in tasks such as image classification, where only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. Unless the task is solely signal reconstruction, it would be beneficial to comment on why preserving signal information is crucial.

Regarding group invariance, the authors may want to explore potential connections with the invariant kernel approach adopted by Hans Burkhardt. Investigating this relationship could provide additional insights and strengthen the manuscript.

Finally, as a point of curiosity, the authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, ensuring the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations. It would be interesting to know whether any other operator, within the set of operators that commutes with all group elements, could also ensure this equivariance property.
