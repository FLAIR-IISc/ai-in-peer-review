This paper analyzes the theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel representations. It demonstrates that, with carefully designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators, convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information. The authors also show that, under appropriate assumptions, these properties extend to a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and technically sound. The main weakness is the lack of empirical validation to support the theoretical results. It would be helpful to include experiments illustrating that CKNs configured as proposed indeed exhibit the stated invariance and stability to deformations.

A further question concerns kernel approximation: could random feature mappings (e.g., random Fourier features) or other explicit finite-dimensional embeddings be used in place of the proposed approximation? If so, what impact would these alternatives have on the stability guarantees?
