- Manuscript is clearly written, rigorous, and shows that function spaces realized by DCNNs can be characterized via multilayer kernels.
- Paper demonstrates desirable architecture properties: (near) invariance to group actions and stability under diffeomorphic transformations.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance subject to addressing the following comments.
- Clarify main contribution: justify usefulness of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., enabling new generalization bounds or computational benefits), and state if novelty goes beyond an RKHS re-expression; clarify relation to “Convolutional Kernel Networks” (Mairal et al.).
- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information”: explain why full signal preservation is essential for task-driven uses (e.g., classification) where only task-relevant information may matter.
- Ask whether there is any connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methodology.
- Curious whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would similarly ensure equivariance of the DCNN (beyond the defined patch, pooling, and kernel maps).
