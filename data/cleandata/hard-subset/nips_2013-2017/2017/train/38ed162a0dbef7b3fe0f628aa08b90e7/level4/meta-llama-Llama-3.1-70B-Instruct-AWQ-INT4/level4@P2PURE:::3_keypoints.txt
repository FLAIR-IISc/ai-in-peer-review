- Paper is well-written and rigorous, demonstrating DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels.  
- Shows desirable properties of the architecture, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, pending consideration of the listed comments.  
- Primary contribution appears to be reformulating CNNs in RKHS; authors should explain why this reformulation is useful (e.g., insights into generalization bounds or computational benefits) and whether novelty goes beyond a simple reformulation.  
- Question the necessity of preserving full signal information versus only task-relevant information (e.g., for image classification).  
- Suggest exploring connections to the invariant kernel approach of Hans Burkhardt and clarifying relations/differences.  
- Propose investigating whether other operators that commute with all group elements could also ensure DCNN equivariance.  
- Recommend comparing and clarifying novelty relative to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.
