- Paper provides a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations using multilayer kernels and RKHS.  
- Generalizes Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to signals on continuous domains via a multilayer patch–kernel–pooling architecture.  
- Shows representation is stable to small diffeomorphisms and nearly translation-invariant, with formal bounds like scattering transforms.  
- Demonstrates how to build representations equivariant and ultimately invariant to more general groups (e.g., rotations).  
- Proves the constructed RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, linking RKHS norm to stability and generalization.  
- Strength: technically rigorous, building on signal-processing and learning theory with elegant proofs.  
- Strength: significant contribution to understanding invariance and stability of learned deep models, unifying CKNs and CNNs.  
- Strength: clear exposition and well-organized presentation, including a motivated Section 5 linking theory to practical CNNs.  
- Strength: original synthesis extending stability analysis from scattering networks to learned CNN-like representations.  
- Weakness/question: practical implications unclear—could discuss RKHS-norm-based regularization, training/design algorithms for guaranteed stability, or tighter generalization bounds.  
- Weakness/question: clarify novel contributions relative to prior CKN work, emphasizing new stability/invariance analysis and link to general CNNs.  
- Weakness/question: question the universal desirability of signal-preservation—discuss trade-offs between preserving information and learning discriminative, low-dimensional features.  
- Weakness/question: connect Section 4 to classical invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Haasdonk & Burkhardt) and clarify extensions beyond single-kernel methods.  
- Weakness/question (minor): clarify whether any sequence of individually equivariant operators yields overall equivariance, i.e., generality of the equivariance construction.  
- Recommendation: high-quality paper with minor clarifications suggested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
