This paper analyzes key properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that with careful design of the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators, CKNs can achieve near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms. The authors also show that under certain assumptions, these desirable properties extend to conventional Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. Its primary weakness, however, is the lack of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. The claims would be significantly strengthened by experimental results that demonstrate the invariance and stability of CKNs when configured as proposed.

I also have a question for the authors: could other methods for kernel approximation, such as random projections or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series terms, be used? If so, what effect would these choices have on the network's stability properties as derived in the paper?
