- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and links to CNNs, showing near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making technical arguments easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of CKN properties and their connection to CNNs.  
- Bridges theoretical and practical aspects with accessible explanations of the underlying mathematics.  
- Main contribution: theoretical framework showing CKNs can be designed for near-invariance and diffeomorphism stability.  
- Demonstrates that CKN properties can be extended to CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis.  
- Recommends experiments comparing different patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators.  
- Suggests adding discussion on the effect of random projection or finite Fourier-series kernel mapping on CKN stability.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution and valuable resource despite missing empirical validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: thorough analysis, new theoretical framework, and connection between CKNs and CNNs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experiments and missing discussion on random projections/Fourier mapping effects.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add experiments and discussion.
