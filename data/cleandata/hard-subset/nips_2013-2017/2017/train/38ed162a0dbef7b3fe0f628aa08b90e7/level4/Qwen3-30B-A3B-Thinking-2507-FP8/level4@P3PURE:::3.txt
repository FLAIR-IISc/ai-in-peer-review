

The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how (certain) functional spaces implemented by deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) can be described using multilayer kernels. It also establishes notable properties of the architecture, including (almost) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance pending the following clarifications:  

*   The paper's main contribution appears to be a reformulation of DCNN architecture within the framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors should explicitly discuss the utility of this reformulation. Specifically: (1) Could the kernel formulation enable new insights into generalization bounds? (2) Is implementing the architecture via standard kernel techniques/approximations computationally more efficient?  
*   More broadly, does the novelty extend beyond a reformulation of DCNNs in RKHS? Please clarify the novelty relative to Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks" (ICML 2014).  
*   The paper emphasizes preserving "signal information." Why is this critical? DCNNs typically operate on task-specific objectives (e.g., image classification), where only task-relevant information needs preservation—not all signal. The authors should address this distinction.  
*   Regarding group invariance, is there a connection to Burkhardt's invariant kernel approach (e.g., *Invariant Kernel Methods*, 2005)?  
*   The paper defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with group actions. Would *any* operator commuting with all group elements ensure DCNN equivariance under group transformations, or are the specific operators defined necessary?
