This is a well-written and rigorous paper that successfully describes the functional spaces implemented by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) in terms of multilayer kernels. The authors further demonstrate that the resulting architecture possesses desirable properties, namely near-invariance to group transformations and stability with respect to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, provided the authors address the following points:

*   The paper's primary contribution appears to be the reformulation of the DCNN architecture within the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). To strengthen this contribution, the authors should elaborate on the practical utility of this reformulation. For instance, could this kernel-based view lead to new insights on generalization bounds? Does it offer a more computationally efficient implementation through standard kernel techniques or approximations? More broadly, the authors should clarify whether the novelty of their approach extends beyond this reformulation. It is also crucial to explicitly differentiate this work from the "Convolutional Kernel Networks" paper by Mairal et al. and clarify this paper's unique contributions.

*   The authors emphasize the importance of "not losing the signal information." The motivation for this is not entirely clear. DCNNs are typically applied to tasks like image classification, where the goal is to preserve only task-relevant information, not the entire signal. Unless the objective is signal reconstruction, discarding irrelevant information is often beneficial. The authors should comment on this design choice and its implications for common DCNN applications.

*   Regarding group invariance, is there a connection between the proposed method and prior work on invariant kernels, such as the approaches developed by Hans Burkhardt? A discussion of related work in this area would be beneficial.

*   The authors define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. As a point of clarification: would any operator that commutes with all elements of the group similarly ensure the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to these transformations?
