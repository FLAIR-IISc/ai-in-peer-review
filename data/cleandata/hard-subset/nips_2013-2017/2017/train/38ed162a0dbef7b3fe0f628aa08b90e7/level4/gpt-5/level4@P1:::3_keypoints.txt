- Summary: Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel for continuous signals (patch extraction → homogeneous dot-product RKHS map → Gaussian pooling) and proves signal preservation, stability to C1 diffeomorphisms (linear in deformation size, translation bound ∝ 1/σn), group invariance via covariant patching/pooling, inclusion of many CNNs in the induced RKHS, and preservation of non-expansiveness by CKN approximations.  
- Quality: Technically careful and largely rigorous with clear assumptions and precise operator-norm bounds; some constants and depth dependencies (e.g., (1+n) factor, dependence on κ) need more discussion.  
- Clarity: Exposition is clear and well organized; continuous-to-discrete and CKN/CNN connections are helpful, though a few points require explicit clarification.  
- Originality: Extends scattering-style analysis to a more general multilayer kernel framework that subsumes CKNs and captures a class of CNNs via RKHS inclusion; novelty should be more sharply delineated relative to prior CKN and group-invariant kernel work.  
- Significance: Provides a theoretical viewpoint separating representation and prediction, suggests RKHS-norm regularization to control generalization and stability, and could inform robust architecture design.  
- Strengths: Rigorous diffeomorphism stability bounds with clear dependence on pooling scale; unifying multilayer kernel construction subsuming CKNs and some CNNs; careful discretization and finite-dimensional approximation analysis; group-equivariant design yielding global invariance.  
- Main weakness/request: Need to justify practical value of RKHS reformulation (explicit generalization/sample-complexity bounds, computational benefits via kernel approximations, concrete design/regularization guidance).  
- Novelty clarification requested: Explicitly state what stability/invariance results go beyond original CKN work, whether Prop.7 (CNNs in RKHS) is the main bridge, and how this analysis differs from prior kernel analyses of deep nets.  
- Signal-preservation critique: Motivate importance of invertibility for task-driven learning and clarify whether stability/generalization arguments rely on it or if it is ancillary.  
- Relation to invariant kernels: Clarify connections and differences with orbit-integrated/invariant-kernel constructions (e.g., Haasdonk & Burkhardt).  
- Equivariance operators question: Comment on whether more general G-commuting operators (beyond patch/kernel/pooling) can be used and what extra properties (non-expansiveness, locality, smoothness) are needed to retain stability results.  
- Minor suggestions: Clarify dependence of constants C1/C2 on pooling kernel and κ, discuss boundary effects from finite support, and sketch a concrete margin-based sample-complexity bound if claiming improved sample complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Solid, well-presented theoretical unification of multilayer kernels, CKNs, and certain CNNs; meaningful stability/invariance guarantees and compatibility with practical approximations; potential to guide regularization and architecture design.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical benefits of the reformulation are under-explained, novelty over prior CKN/invariant-kernel work needs clearer articulation, limited empirical validation, and some abstract constants/depth dependencies.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing the requested clarifications.
