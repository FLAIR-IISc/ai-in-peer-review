The research paper discusses some remarkable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks. That is, CKNs are almost invariant to transformations and stable to the operation of diffeomorphic transformations, provided the appropriate patch extraction operators, kernel mapping operators, and pooling operators are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, the remarkable properties obtained from CKNs are also applicable to CNNs.

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The only weakness is that the paper does not provide any empirical tests to prove the validity of the theoretical analysis. I would like to see some experimental results that lead to the conclusion that CKNs are invariant to transformations and diffeomorphic transformations with appropriate settings.

Another question is whether a certain number of kernel mappings of a random projection or Fourier series can be a possible solution for kernel approximation. If so, what is its impact on stability?
