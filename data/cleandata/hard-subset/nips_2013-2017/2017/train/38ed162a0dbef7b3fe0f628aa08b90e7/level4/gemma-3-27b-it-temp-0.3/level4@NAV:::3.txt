This paper presents a rigorous and well-written analysis demonstrating how functional spaces implemented by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) can be effectively described using multilayer kernels. The authors further establish desirable architectural properties, including approximate invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance contingent upon addressing the following points:

*   My understanding is that the primary contribution of this work lies in reformulating the DCNN architecture within the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) framework. The manuscript would benefit from a discussion outlining the potential advantages of this reformulation – for example, could this kernel formulation yield insights into generalization bounds, or does it offer computational efficiencies through standard kernel techniques and approximations? Furthermore, it would be valuable to clarify the novelty of this approach beyond simply representing DCNNs in the context of RKHS. Specifically, please delineate its distinct contributions relative to the work presented in “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Mairal et al.

*   The authors emphasize the importance of preserving “signal information.” However, DCNNs are frequently applied to tasks beyond signal reconstruction, such as image classification, where only task-relevant information needs to be retained. Please elaborate on the rationale for prioritizing the preservation of all signal information in this context.

*   Concerning the demonstrated group invariance, it would be interesting to explore any connections to the invariant kernel approach developed by Hans Burkhardt.

*   Finally, as a point of curiosity, the paper defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. I wonder if any other operator (within the set of operators commuting with all group elements) would similarly guarantee the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to the specified group transformations?
