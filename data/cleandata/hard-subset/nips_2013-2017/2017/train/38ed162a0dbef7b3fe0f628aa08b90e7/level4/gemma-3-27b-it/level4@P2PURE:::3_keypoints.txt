- Paper is rigorous and clear, showing DCNN functional spaces via multilayer kernels and demonstrating (approximate) group invariance and stability; recommend acceptance pending responses to comments.  
- Clarify benefits of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., insights into generalization bounds or computational advantages from kernel techniques/approximations).  
- Explain whether the novelty extends beyond a reformulation and explicitly distinguish this work from Mairal et al.'s "Convolutional Kernel Networks."  
- Revisit the emphasis on “not losing signal information,” discussing that tasks like classification may only require task-relevant information, not full signal preservation.  
- Address whether there is a relationship to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach.  
- Clarify if any operator that commutes with all group elements would also guarantee the equivariance property of the DCNN with respect to those group transformations.
