

The paper is well-written and rigorous, effectively demonstrating how functional spaces implemented by DCNNs can be characterized through multilayer kernels. It further establishes that the architecture possesses desirable properties: (nearly) invariance under group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance pending the following clarifications:  
* As understood by the reviewer, the primary contribution reinterprets DCNN architecture within the RKHS framework. The authors should elaborate on the practical significance of this reformulation—e.g., whether it enables new generalization bounds or offers computational advantages via kernel approximation techniques.  
* More broadly, does the approach’s novelty extend beyond this RKHS reformulation? Clarify its distinction from the work "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.  
* The emphasis on "preserving signal information" requires justification. Given DCNNs typically serve task-specific goals (e.g., classification), why is retaining all signal information essential rather than retaining only task-relevant features?  
* Is there a connection between the group invariance approach and Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methodology?  
* Regarding the commutativity of patch, pooling, and kernel maps with group actions: could any operator in the set of group-commuting operators guarantee equivariance in DCNNs?
