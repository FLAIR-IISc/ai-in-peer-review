The paper explores some desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating their near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, these properties can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. However, its primary weakness lies in the absence of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to see experimental results demonstrating that CKNs with appropriate settings are indeed invariant to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

Additionally, it would be interesting to investigate whether random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series could also serve as viable solutions for kernel approximation. If so, what impact would this have on stability?
