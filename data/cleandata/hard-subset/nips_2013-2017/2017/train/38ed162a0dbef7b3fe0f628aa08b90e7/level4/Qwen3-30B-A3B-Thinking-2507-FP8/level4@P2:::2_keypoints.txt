- Investigates stability and invariance of deep convolutional representations using a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes CKNs to continuous domains.  
- Main contribution: proves representations are stable to C^1 diffeomorphisms with stability bounds that decay with pooling scale.  
- Extends stability results to group-invariant representations and links the kernel framework to standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with a logical flow from theoretical foundations to practical implications.  
- Technical content is strong and rigorous; proofs are sound and assumptions (A1–A3) are clearly stated and justified.  
- Provides a unified theoretical framework bridging kernel methods, scattering transforms, and deep learning, clarifying why CNNs work well.  
- Clarity is high: consistent notation and helpful figures, though the kernel parameter κ is overloaded and may confuse readers.  
- Originality: novel connection between CKNs and CNNs and extension of scattering-type stability guarantees to a broader class of models.  
- Significance: offers theoretical insight into CNN generalization and robustness, with implications for regularization and adversarial resilience.  
- Strengths summarized: unified framework for stability, CNN connection, applicability to practical models, rigorous mathematics, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses summarized: motivation for stability could be stronger, overloaded κ notation, minor typographical/math errors (lines 384–385, 427–428), missing φ definition in Eq.(10), and some references lack page numbers.  
- Recommendation: strong, NIPS-suitable paper that makes a significant theoretical contribution and merits acceptance.
