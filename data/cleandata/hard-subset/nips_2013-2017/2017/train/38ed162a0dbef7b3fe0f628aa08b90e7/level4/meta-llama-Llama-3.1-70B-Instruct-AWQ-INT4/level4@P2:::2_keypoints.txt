- The paper studies stability and invariance of deep convolutional representations built from Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs), focusing on C1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations).  
- It proves stability for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels with appropriately chosen patch sizes.  
- The manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound.  
- The work successfully blends ideas from deep networks and kernel methods.  
- The stability result is relevant and of interest to the machine learning community.  
- The analysis is thorough and provides valuable theoretical insight into deep convolutional representations.  
- The paper would benefit from stronger motivation for the stability analysis (e.g., expanded discussion of Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness).  
- Notation issues: the symbol κ is overloaded (used in assumption (A3) and to define kernel K in Eq. (10)).  
- A displayed equation (between lines 384–385) contains a redundant part and should be simplified.  
- Eq. (10) is missing the definition of ϕ.  
- The inequality on lines 427–428 can be written as an equality.  
- The reference list omits page information for several citations.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution that can be strengthened with minor revisions addressing motivation, notation, formatting, and references.
