

The paper investigates notable theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they achieve near invariance under groups of transformations and stability under diffeomorphisms when appropriately designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are employed. The authors further establish that these desirable properties extend to standard convolutional neural networks (CNNs) under specific assumptions. The theoretical contributions are well-motivated and technically rigorous, and the paper is clearly written with a logical structure.

The primary limitation is the absence of empirical validation for the theoretical claims. While the mathematical analysis is sound, the paper lacks experimental results to substantiate the asserted invariance properties (e.g., under transformations or diffeomorphisms) in practical settings. For instance, visualizations or quantitative metrics demonstrating CKNs' stability under controlled deformations or transformations would significantly strengthen the argument. Such experiments are essential to bridge the gap between theory and practice, particularly for a venue like NIPS where empirical relevance is highly valued. The authors should address this gap in a revision.

A secondary point concerns the kernel approximation strategy. The paper relies on implicit kernel mapping, but it remains unclear whether alternative approaches—such as random Fourier features or explicit finite-dimensional kernel approximations—could serve as feasible alternatives while preserving the stability guarantees. If applicable, the authors should discuss: (1) the conditions under which such approximations maintain the invariance/stability properties, and (2) any potential trade-offs in performance or computational efficiency. This question is particularly pertinent for scalable applications, as explicit approximations are often preferred over implicit methods in practice.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to understanding the geometric properties of deep kernel networks. The core arguments are compelling, but the lack of empirical support weakens the practical impact. Addressing these points would substantially enhance the paper's contribution and align it with NIPS standards. The suggested experiments and discussion on kernel approximation are critical for a complete evaluation of the framework's viability. The authors should prioritize providing these elements in a revision.
