The paper is well written and rigorous. It convincingly shows that certain function spaces realized by DCNNs can be characterized via multilayer kernels, and it establishes appealing architectural properties: (almost) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, subject to the comments below:

- To my understanding, the main contribution is a reformulation of CNN architectures within the RKHS framework. Please explain why this viewpoint is or will be useful (e.g., can a kernel formulation lead to new generalization bounds? Is it computationally more efficient when implemented via standard kernel techniques/approximations?). More generally, does the novelty extend beyond recasting DCNNs in an RKHS? Also clarify the novelty relative to “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid.

- The paper emphasizes “not losing the signal information.” Why is this crucial? DCNNs are typically used for tasks such as image classification; unless the task is signal reconstruction, only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. Please comment.

- Regarding group invariance: is there any connection to the invariant-kernel approach developed by Hans Burkhardt (invariant kernels)?

- A curiosity: you define patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. Would any operator from the set of operators that commute with all group elements ensure equivariance of the DCNN with respect to the group transformations?
