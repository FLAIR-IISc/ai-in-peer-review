- Request clearer articulation of why casting DCNNs in an RKHS/multilayer-kernel framework is useful (what new analysis, bounds, or algorithms it enables).  
- Ask whether the kernel view yields sharper generalization bounds (via RKHS norms, margins, Rademacher/covering numbers) that scale with depth/architecture.  
- Ask about computational implications: can kernel approximation methods (Nyström, random features, inducing points) be used for efficient implementation, training, or model selection?  
- Request identification of new insights or results made accessible by the RKHS view (e.g., principled pooling/nonlinearity design, architectural regularization, new stability guarantees).  
- Demand clarification of novelty relative to Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKN): assumptions, expressivity, handling of pooling/nonlinearity, and precise differences in results or hypotheses.  
- Ask whether the invariance and stability results are stronger, more general, or under different hypotheses than prior work, and to state this precisely.  
- Request explicit delineation of what is new in analysis, proofs, or architectural prescriptions if overlap with prior work exists.  
- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information”: why preserving full signal information is necessary, how it relates to sufficiency/invariance/selectivity or information bottleneck, and whether it’s required for stability/invariance results.  
- Ask for connections to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Burkhardt et al.): when do constructions coincide, generalize, or differ, and mapping to classical invariant kernel formulations.  
- Query whether any operator in the commutant of the group action ensures equivariance, and if not, what additional properties (linearity, boundedness, locality, Lipschitz) are required for stability through depth.  
- Request clarification of conditions under which pointwise nonlinearities preserve equivariance or controlled deviations from it.  
- Suggest formally defining “almost invariance” with quantitative bounds and how deviation scales with depth, layer parameters, and deformation magnitude.  
- Ask to state all regularity/curvature assumptions on diffeomorphisms and signals for stability results, and to indicate which constants depend on architecture and the invariance–sensitivity trade-offs.  
- Request an upfront concise theorem-level summary of main contributions: (i) RKHS representation, (ii) equivariance/invariance, (iii) stability to diffeomorphisms, (iv) implications for generalization/complexity.  
- Ask for discussion of computational tractability: how multilayer kernels are chosen/approximated, concrete algorithms or finite-dimensional feature maps, or explicit statement that the work is theoretical with suggested approximation paths.  
- Recommend adding a small illustrative (possibly synthetic) example showing how the RKHS perspective prescribes pooling/nonlinearity choices that improve invariance/stability while controlling information loss.  
- Suggest clarifying notation early (group actions on signals vs spatial domains, patch definitions, group measures) to make proofs self-contained and easier to follow.  
- Overall: paper is rigorous and compelling, but acceptance is recommended only after clarifying RKHS usefulness, comparisons to prior kernel-based models, and precise operator/equivariance conditions.
