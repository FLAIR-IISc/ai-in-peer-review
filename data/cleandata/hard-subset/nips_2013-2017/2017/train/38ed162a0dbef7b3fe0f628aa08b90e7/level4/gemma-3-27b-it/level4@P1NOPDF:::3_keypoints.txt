- Paper is well-written and rigorous, showing DCNN functional spaces can be described via multilayer kernels.  
- Highlights appealing properties: near-invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic transformations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing reviewer comments.  
- Main contribution questioned: clarify practical benefits of RKHS reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds, computational advantages, or use of kernel approximations).  
- Novelty scope: clarify whether approach offers more than a reinterpretation of DCNNs and explicitly contrast novelty with “Convolutional Kernel Networks” (Mairal et al.).  
- Clarify the claim about “not losing signal information”: explain why preserving full signal is critical given many tasks (e.g., classification) only require task-relevant information.  
- Ask whether there is any connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel approach.  
- Technical curiosity: would any operator that commutes with all group elements also guarantee DCNN equivariance to those group transformations?
