- Introduces a kernel-based theoretical framework to study invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, linking CKNs and scattering transforms.  
- Develops a multilayer convolutional kernel for continuous signals that yields representations stable to diffeomorphisms and near-invariant to group transformations.  
- Bridges kernel-method theory with practical convolutional architectures to analyze the functional spaces containing many CNNs.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with a logical progression from theory to implications.  
- Technical development is rigorous, with stability bounds that explicitly depend on the final-layer pooling scale.  
- Strength: Shows many standard CNNs reside in the proposed RKHS, extending Mallat’s stability results to a broader class of architectures.  
- Strength: Connects RKHS norm to generalization, implying stable representations (small RKHS norm) may need fewer samples.  
- Strength: Discretization and kernel approximation sections effectively connect theory to implementable CNNs.  
- Strength: Extension to group invariance demonstrates the framework’s versatility beyond translation invariance.  
- Weakness: Motivation for studying stability is underdeveloped; needs stronger discussion of practical impacts (generalization, robustness, natural deformations).  
- Weakness: Notation conflict—κ is overloaded in Assumption (A3) and Eq. (10) and should use distinct symbols.  
- Weakness: Redundant condition in displayed equation between lines 384–385 (due to kernel symmetry) should be removed.  
- Weakness: Eq. (10) should explicitly define the feature map φ with K(z,z') = ⟨φ(z),φ(z')⟩_H(K).  
- Weakness: Typo/error in inequality on lines 427–428: |‖z‖ - ‖z‖|² should be |‖z‖ - ‖z'‖|².  
- References: Multiple citations ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25-27,30,31]) lack publication details; several specific references need corrected/full citations ([9], [17], [19], [32]).  
- Overall: Novel, significant, and technically sound contribution that advances understanding of deep convolutional representations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
