The paper explores several desirable properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating their near invariance to groups of transformations and stability under the action of diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction operator, kernel mapping operator, and pooling operator are carefully designed. The authors also show that, under certain assumptions, these properties can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

In general, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors present a clear and thorough analysis of the theoretical foundations of CKNs, providing a valuable contribution to the understanding of these networks. However, the paper's main weakness is the lack of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to include experimental results that demonstrate the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms under appropriate settings.

One potential avenue for future research is the exploration of alternative kernel approximation methods. Specifically, it would be interesting to investigate whether random projection or explicit kernel mapping with a finite number of Fourier series could also be viable solutions. If so, it would be important to analyze the effect of these methods on the stability of the network. A comparison of these approaches with the proposed method would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the trade-offs involved in designing CKNs.

Overall, the paper provides a valuable theoretical analysis of CKNs, but would benefit from the inclusion of empirical results to support the claims made. With the addition of experimental validation and exploration of alternative kernel approximation methods, the paper could make an even more significant contribution to the field.
