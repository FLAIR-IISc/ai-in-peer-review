This paper analyzes the stability of representations learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14] with respect to C<sup>1</sup> diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations), quantified by a bound similar to Eq. (4) in the paper. The authors demonstrate that stability holds under certain conditions – norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) around line 193], and appropriate patch size selection [(A3)] – and sketch an extension to locally compact groups in Section 4. 

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining insights from deep learning and kernel methods. The achieved stability result is potentially valuable to the machine learning community. The core contribution lies in providing a theoretical framework to understand the robustness properties of CKNs, and by extension, a class of CNNs.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper builds upon solid mathematical foundations from kernel methods and harmonic analysis, providing rigorous analysis of stability.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The material is presented in a clear and logical manner, making the technical details accessible.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** The paper appropriately connects its work to relevant literature in both kernel methods and deep learning, acknowledging prior contributions.
*   **Potential for Generalization:** The result extends beyond CKNs to a broader class of CNNs, increasing its impact.
*   **Novelty:** The analysis provides new insights into the geometry of functional spaces underlying CNNs, which is a relatively unexplored area.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Motivation:** The paper could benefit from more extensive motivation for the stability analysis. The current justification (lines 56-57 referencing adversarial robustness [7]) feels somewhat isolated and doesn’t fully convey the broader significance of understanding stability.  Expanding on why stability is a desirable property for representations and its relation to generalization would strengthen the introduction.
*   **Notational Ambiguity:** The use of *κ* in assumption (A3) (around line 193) could be confusing, as it also represents a function defining the kernel *K* in Eq. (10). Choosing a distinct symbol would improve clarity.
*   **Redundancy in Equation:** The second part of the equation between lines 384-385 ("and ∀v...") is redundant due to the symmetry of the kernel *k*; the constraint is already implied by the first part ("∀u...").
*   **Missing Definition:** The definition of *φ* is missing in the vicinity of line 416; it should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <φ(z), φ(z')><sub>H(K)</sub>.
*   **Typo/Error:** The inequality around line 427-428 likely contains a typo; it should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |<sup>2</sup> rather than | ||z|| - ||z|| |<sup>2</sup>.
*   **Incomplete References:** Several references are missing page numbers: [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25-27, 30, 31]. Completing these would improve the review process.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of a relevant topic (stability of deep representations).
*   The technical contributions are sound and well-supported by mathematical proofs.
*   The work extends existing results on scattering transforms to a more general class of CNNs.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized, facilitating understanding of complex concepts.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The motivation for the stability analysis could be strengthened.
*   Minor notational ambiguities and errors detract slightly from the overall clarity. 
*   The practical implications of the theoretical results, while hinted at, could be further elaborated.

**Overall assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and the results are potentially impactful.  The weaknesses identified are relatively minor and can be addressed with some revisions. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest addressing the motivation, notational ambiguity, equation redundancy, missing definition, and typographical error.  Completing the references is also essential.
