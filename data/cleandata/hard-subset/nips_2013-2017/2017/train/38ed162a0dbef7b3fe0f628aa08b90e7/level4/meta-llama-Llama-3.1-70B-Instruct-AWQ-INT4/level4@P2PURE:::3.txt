The paper is well-written and rigorous, successfully demonstrating how certain functional spaces implemented by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. Additionally, it showcases the architecture's desirable properties, including invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, pending the authors' consideration of the following comments:

* In the reviewer's understanding, the primary contribution of the paper lies in reformulating the CNN architecture within the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). It would be beneficial to include comments in the text explaining why this reformulation is useful. For instance, does the kernel formulation offer interesting insights into generalization bounds, or is it more computationally efficient to implement using standard kernel techniques and approximations? Furthermore, it is essential to clarify whether the novelty of the approach extends beyond a simple reformulation of DCNNs in the context of RKHS.

* The authors emphasize the importance of preserving signal information. However, in many tasks, such as image classification, only task-relevant information needs to be preserved. It would be helpful to comment on the necessity of preserving signal information in these contexts.

* Regarding group invariance, it is worth exploring potential connections with the invariant kernel approach proposed by Hans Burkhardt. The authors may want to discuss how their work relates to or differs from this existing approach.

* As a point of curiosity, the paper defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action. It would be interesting to investigate whether other operators that commute with all group elements could also ensure the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to group transformations.

A comparison with the paper "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Julien Mairal, Piotr Koniusz, Zaid Harchaoui, and Cordelia Schmid would also be beneficial to clarify the novelty of the proposed approach.
