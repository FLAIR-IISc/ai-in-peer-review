The primary focus of the paper is on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. The authors analyze the stability of the representation formed by CKNs with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms, such as translation, in the sense of Eq. (4). They demonstrate that stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels [(A1-A2) in line 193] when the patch sizes are appropriately chosen [(A3)]. The paper also sketches an extension of these results from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining ideas from deep networks and kernels in a compelling manner. The stability result is of potential interest to the machine learning community.

However, there are a few areas that would benefit from improvement. Firstly, the authors could provide further motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the only motivation provided is a brief mention in line 56-57 of the importance of Lipschitz stability in deep predictive models for robustness to adversarial examples [7].

Additionally, the notation in (A3) [line 193] could be clarified, as the symbol \kappa is overloaded and also denotes a function defining kernel K in Eq. (10).

In the displayed equation between line 384 and 385, the second part of the equation ('and \forall v...') is redundant, as it is identical to the first constraint ('\forall u...') due to the symmetry of kernel k.

Furthermore, the definition of \phi is missing in line 416 and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as <\phi(z),\phi(z')>_{H(K)}.

Lastly, in line 427-428, the inequality under '=' appears to also hold with equality, and the expression should be | ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2 instead of | ||z|| - ||z|| |^2.
