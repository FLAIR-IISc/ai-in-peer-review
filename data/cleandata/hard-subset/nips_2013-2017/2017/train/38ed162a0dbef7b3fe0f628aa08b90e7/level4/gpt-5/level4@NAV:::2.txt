The central subject of the paper is the convolutional kernel network (CKN) [13, 14]. The manuscript analyzes the stability (with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms, e.g., translations, as formalized in Eq. (4)) of the representation produced by CKNs. It establishes that, under norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (A1–A2, line 193), stability holds when patch sizes are chosen appropriately (A3). Section 4 sketches an extension from (R^d, +) to locally compact groups.
The paper is well structured, clearly presented, and technically solid, synthesizing ideas from deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result is likely to be of interest to the ML community.
- The submission would benefit from a stronger motivation for the stability analysis. Currently there is only a brief sentence (lines 56–57: “Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7].”) that motivates the main contribution.
- Overloading the \kappa notation in (A3) (line 193) may be confusing, since \kappa also denotes the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10).
- In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second clause (“and ∀ v...”) is redundant; by symmetry of the kernel k, it duplicates the first constraint (“∀ u ...”).
- Line 416: the definition of \phi is missing; it should be introduced in Eq. (10) via ⟨\phi(z), \phi(z')⟩_{H(K)}.
- Lines 427–428: The inequality following “=” appears to hold with equality; moreover, | ||z|| − ||z|| |^2 should read | ||z|| − ||z'|| |^2.
