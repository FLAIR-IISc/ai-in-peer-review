1. Paper presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations (CKNs) on continuous signals, studying RKHS geometry and showing stability to C¹-diffeomorphisms and near-translation invariance.  
2. Main technical result: a Lipschitz-like bound (Eq. 4) on representation differences under deformations, extending scattering-transform stability to learned architectures.  
3. Connects CKNs/CNNs to RKHS theory, linking stability of representations to generalization via RKHS norms.  
4. Also sketches extending the framework to invariance under general locally compact group actions.  
5. Strength: technically rigorous, combining kernel methods, harmonic analysis, and deep learning with supporting propositions/lemmas (notably Theorem 6).  
6. Strength: original and significant—one of the first stability guarantees for learned deep architectures, advancing theoretical understanding of CNN robustness.  
7. Strength: clear, well-organized exposition with systematic introduction of assumptions (A1–A3) and connection to discrete implementations.  
8. Weakness: motivation for stability could be stronger—needs clearer links to adversarial robustness, sample complexity, generalization, and natural-signal properties.  
9. Weakness: minor clarity issues—overloading symbol κ, missing explicit definition of feature map φ_k in relation to K_k, and a likely typo in Appendix B.  
10. Weakness: bibliography contains incomplete or outdated references that should be updated with publication details.  
11. Argument for acceptance: provides novel, rigorous insights bridging scattering theory and learned convolutional models with implications for robustness and regularization.  
12. Argument against acceptance: motivation and the practical link from continuous theory to discrete implementations could be improved, especially regarding discretization effects on stability constants.  
13. Conclusion: strong, original, and technically sound paper with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
