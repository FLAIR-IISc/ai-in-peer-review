The paper presents a rigorous and well-written analysis demonstrating how functional spaces realized by Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) can be effectively characterized through multilayer kernels. Furthermore, it establishes appealing architectural properties, including approximate invariance to group transformations and stability under diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the following points:

*My primary understanding is that the core contribution lies in re-framing the DCNN architecture within the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) framework. The manuscript would benefit from a discussion outlining the potential advantages of this reformulation. For instance, does this kernel-based perspective open avenues for novel results regarding generalization bounds? Or, might it enable computationally efficient implementations leveraging established kernel methods and approximation techniques? Beyond this re-expression, it's important to clearly articulate the novelty of the approach. Does it extend beyond simply presenting DCNNs within an RKHS context? Specifically, clarification regarding the relationship to the work presented in “Convolutional Kernel Networks” by Mairal et al. would be valuable.

*The authors emphasize the importance of preserving “signal information.” While understandable in contexts like signal reconstruction, it is worth noting that DCNNs are frequently employed in tasks—such as image classification—where only task-relevant information needs to be retained. Please comment on this point, clarifying the motivation for prioritizing complete signal preservation.

*Concerning group invariance, it would be helpful to explore potential connections to the invariant kernel approaches pioneered by Hans Burkhardt.

*Finally, as a point of curiosity, given your definition of patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action, I wonder if any other operator – belonging to the set of operators commuting with all group elements – would similarly guarantee the equivariance property of DCNNs with respect to the considered group transformations?
