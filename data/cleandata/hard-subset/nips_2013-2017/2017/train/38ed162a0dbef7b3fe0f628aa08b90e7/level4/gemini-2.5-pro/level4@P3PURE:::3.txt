This paper is well-written, rigorous, and successfully demonstrates how the functional spaces implemented by DCNNs can be described in terms of multilayer kernels. The authors also show that the resulting architecture possesses desirable properties, namely (approximate) invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphic transformations. I recommend acceptance, provided the authors address the following comments.

*   The primary contribution of this work appears to be a reformulation of the CNN architecture within the context of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The authors should elaborate on why this reformulation is useful. For example, does the kernel formulation lead to new insights on generalization bounds? Does it enable more computationally efficient implementations using standard kernel approximation techniques? More generally, does the novelty of the approach extend beyond a reformulation of DCNNs? Furthermore, please clarify the novelty of this work with respect to "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.

*   The authors stress the importance of "not losing the signal information." This claim requires further justification. DCNNs are typically applied to tasks like image classification, where the objective is to preserve only task-relevant information while discarding irrelevant variability. Unless the goal is signal reconstruction, preserving the entire signal may not be necessary or even desirable. Please comment on this.

*   Regarding the discussion on group invariance, is there any connection between the proposed method and the invariant kernel approaches developed by Hans Burkhardt?

*   The paper defines patch, pooling, and kernel maps that commute with the group action to establish equivariance. As a point of clarification, would any operator that commutes with all elements of the group be sufficient to ensure the equivariance property of the DCNN with respect to the group transformations in this framework?
