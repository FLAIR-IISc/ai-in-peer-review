- Main theoretical claim: CKNs with designed patch extraction, kernel feature mapping, and pooling are nearly invariant to certain transformation groups and stable under diffeomorphisms; similar properties extend to CNNs under assumptions.

- Presentation and rigor: Paper is clearly written and technically sound; theoretical results are well motivated and appear rigorous.

- Primary weakness: Absence of empirical validation.

- Recommended experiments: Demonstrate CKN invariance to the considered transformation groups.

- Recommended experiments: Quantitatively assess stability to diffeomorphisms and how performance degrades with increasing deformation.

- Recommended experiments: Sensitivity analyses showing how different patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling choices affect invariance and stability.

- Recommended experiments: Compare to standard CNNs configured per the paper’s assumptions to support claimed property transfer.

- Question on approximations: Can random projections or finite-dimensional kernel mappings (e.g., random Fourier features) serve as practical approximations here?

- Follow-up on approximations: How do such approximations affect the stated stability and near-invariance guarantees?

- Follow-up on approximations: Are there bounds on the number of features required to preserve theoretical properties within a given tolerance?

- Follow-up on approximations: Do approximations introduce additional sources of instability or bias that interact with pooling and patch extraction?

- Overall suggestion: Addressing the above—especially providing empirical evidence—would strengthen impact and clarify practical implications.
