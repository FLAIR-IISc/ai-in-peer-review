- Demonstrates that deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) can achieve near invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.
- Shows that, under certain assumptions, these invariance and stability properties can be extended from CKNs to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.
- Major weakness: no empirical experiments—needs experiments to validate theoretical invariance and stability claims.
- Raises question whether random projection or explicit finite Fourier-series kernel approximations are viable and how they would affect stability.
