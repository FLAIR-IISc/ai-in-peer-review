This paper analyzes the stability of the representation learned by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) [13, 14]. Specifically, the authors investigate stability with respect to C^1 diffeomorphisms (such as translation), as defined in Eq. (4). They demonstrate that for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (assumptions A1-A2) and appropriately chosen patch sizes (assumption A3), this stability property holds. A potential extension of this framework from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is also outlined in Section 4.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, successfully combining ideas from two exciting areas: deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result presented is a valuable contribution that will likely be of interest to the machine learning community.

The following points are offered to help improve the manuscript:

*   The manuscript would be strengthened by a more thorough motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, the primary motivation is a single sentence (lines 56-57): "Finally, we note that the Lipschitz stability of deep predictive models was found to be important to get robustness to adversarial examples [7]." Expanding on this would better frame the importance of the paper's main contribution.
*   The use of \kappa in (A3) [line 193] is potentially confusing, as the same notation is used for the function defining the kernel K in Eq. (10). The authors may consider using a different symbol to improve clarity.
*   In the displayed equation between lines 384 and 385, the second constraint ("and \forall v...") appears to be redundant. Given the symmetry of the kernel k, this constraint is identical to the first ("\forall u ...").
*   The definition of \phi appears to be missing. It should likely be introduced in Eq. (10), for instance, as K(z, z') = <\phi(z), \phi(z')>_{H(K)}.
*   In the inequality on lines 427-428, the term `| ||z|| - ||z|| |^2` seems to contain a typo and should likely be `| ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2`. Furthermore, it appears this inequality might hold with equality.
