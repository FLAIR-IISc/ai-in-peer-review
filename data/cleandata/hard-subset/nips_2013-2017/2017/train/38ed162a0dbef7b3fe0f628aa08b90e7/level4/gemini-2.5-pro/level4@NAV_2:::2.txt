This paper presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations, focusing on their invariance and stability properties. Drawing from the frameworks of Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) and the stability analysis of Scattering Networks, the authors define a multilayer convolutional kernel representation. The primary contribution is a formal proof that this representation is stable with respect to the action of C1-diffeomorphisms. This result establishes that small deformations of an input signal lead to a controlled, Lipschitz-continuous change in the representation. This stability guarantee is shown to hold under specific assumptions on the kernels (norm-preserving and non-expansive) and architectural parameters. The paper also sketches an extension for achieving global invariance to locally compact group actions and, importantly, establishes a formal link between the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) of the proposed kernel and a class of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) with smooth homogeneous activations, thereby extending the stability guarantees to these models.

**Strengths:**
*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, presenting a rigorous mathematical analysis. The arguments are well-constructed, leveraging concepts from harmonic analysis and kernel theory to derive the main stability results.
*   **Clarity:** The manuscript is well-organized and clearly written. The authors effectively introduce the theoretical construction in a continuous domain before discussing its connection to practical, discrete implementations like CKNs.
*   **Originality:** The work's primary novelty is in bridging the theoretical stability analysis, previously applied to fixed scattering networks, with learnable architectures like CKNs and a specific class of CNNs. This synthesis of ideas from deep learning theory and kernel methods is a valuable and original contribution.
*   **Significance:** The results are significant as they contribute to a deeper theoretical understanding of the robustness of deep convolutional models to input deformations. The connection drawn between the RKHS norm, stability, and generalization provides a principled viewpoint on regularization and the properties that enable successful learning in these architectures.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   The motivation for the stability analysis could be strengthened. While the paper mentions robustness to adversarial examples, expanding on the broader implications of stability to diffeomorphisms—for instance, on sample complexity and the learning of invariances—would better frame the importance of the work for the wider machine learning community.
*   There are a few minor issues of clarity that could be addressed:
    *   A minor notational ambiguity exists where the symbol κ is used both in the definition of the kernel function in Equation (2) and as a constant related to patch size in assumption (A3). Employing distinct symbols would prevent potential confusion.
    *   In the appendix, the feature map `φ` corresponding to the kernel `K` should be explicitly defined alongside the kernel itself to ensure the presentation is self-contained.
*   The following minor technical points and corrections could be addressed in the appendix:
    *   One of the inequalities appears to contain a typo and should likely involve the term `| ||z|| - ||z'|| |^2`.
    *   The condition defining non-expansiveness seems to be stated with some redundancy; given the symmetry of the kernel, the constraint involving `v` appears to be identical to the one involving `u`.
*   The bibliography would benefit from an update to include the final publication details for several cited works, which would improve the manuscript's polish. For example:
    *   [9] should be updated to: Amit Daniely, Roy Frostig, Yoram Singer. Toward Deeper Understanding of Neural Networks: The Power of Initialization and a Dual View on Expressivity. NIPS 2016.
    *   [17] should be updated to: Krikamol Muandet, Kenji Fukumizu, Bharath Sriperumbudur, Bernhard Schölkopf. Kernel Mean Embedding of Distributions: A Review and Beyond. Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning, 2017.
    *   [19] should be updated to: Anant Raj, Abhishek Kumar, Youssef Mroueh, Tom Fletcher, Bernhard Schölkopf. Local Group Invariant Representations via Orbit Embeddings. AISTATS 2017.
    *   [32] should be updated to: Yuchen Zhang, Percy Liang, Martin Wainwright. Convexified Convolutional Neural Networks. ICML 2017.
    *   Additionally, page numbers are missing for several other references ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25-27,30,31]).
