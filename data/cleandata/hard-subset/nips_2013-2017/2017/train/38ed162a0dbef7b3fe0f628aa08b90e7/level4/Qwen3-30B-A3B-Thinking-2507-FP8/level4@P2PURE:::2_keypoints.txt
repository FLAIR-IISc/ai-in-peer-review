- Primary contribution: analyzes stability under C^1 diffeomorphisms (e.g., translations) for representations learned by convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), formalized in Eq. (4).  
- Main theoretical result: stability holds for norm-preserving and non-expansive kernels (conditions (A1)-(A2)) when patch sizes satisfy (A3); extension to locally compact groups sketched in Section 4.  
- Writing and organization: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, bridging deep networks and kernel methods.  
- Impact: the stability result is potentially interesting to the machine learning community.  
- Major concern: motivation for the stability analysis is weak—justified by a single sentence linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness.  
- Notation problem: the symbol κ is overloaded in condition (A3) (line 193) and also denotes the function defining kernel K in Eq. (10).  
- Redundancy: the constraint "and ∀v..." between lines 384–385 is redundant due to kernel symmetry and duplicates the preceding constraint "∀u...".  
- Missing definition: φ is not defined at line 416 and should be introduced in Eq. (10) as ⟨φ(z),φ(z′)⟩_{H(K)}.  
- Typo/error: lines 427–428 use |‖z‖−‖z‖|^2 instead of |‖z‖−‖z′‖|^2, and the displayed inequality under “=” likely holds with equality.  
- References: page numbers missing for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]; full citations provided/clarified for [9], [17], [19], and [32].
