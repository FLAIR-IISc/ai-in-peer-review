The paper "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations" presents a theoretical analysis of the stability of deep convolutional representations, specifically those formed by Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs). The authors study the translation-invariance and stability of these representations under the action of diffeomorphisms, and provide a framework for understanding the properties of these representations.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, combining ideas from deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result, which shows that CKNs can achieve stability to diffeomorphisms under certain conditions, is of interest to the machine learning community. The authors also provide a framework for extending their results to locally compact groups, which is a promising direction for future research.

However, the paper could benefit from additional motivation for the stability analysis. Currently, there is only a brief mention of the importance of Lipschitz stability in deep predictive models for robustness to adversarial examples. Providing more context and explanation for why stability analysis is important would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, there are a few minor issues with notation and formatting. For example, the use of κ to denote both a function defining a kernel and a constant in assumption (A3) may be confusing. Furthermore, some of the displayed equations could be improved for clarity and readability.

Overall, the paper is technically sound and makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional representations. With some revisions to address the minor issues mentioned above, it has the potential to be a strong contribution to the field.
