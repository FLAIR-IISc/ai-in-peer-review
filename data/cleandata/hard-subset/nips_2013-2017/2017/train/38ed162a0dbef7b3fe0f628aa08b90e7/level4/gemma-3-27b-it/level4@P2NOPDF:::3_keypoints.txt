- Presents a rigorous analysis that DCNN functional spaces can be described by multilayer kernels, yielding approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing listed concerns.  
- Core contribution framed as a reframing of DCNNs within an RKHS; this should be made explicit.  
- Paper needs a clearer discussion of the practical utility of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computation).  
- Authors should elaborate whether the kernel perspective yields tighter generalization bounds or computational benefits via kernel methods/approximations.  
- Clarify novelty beyond a perspective change and explicitly contrast with prior work, especially Mairal et al.'s Convolutional Kernel Networks.  
- The repeated claim of “not losing signal information” requires explanation and contextualization relative to tasks (e.g., reconstruction vs. classification).  
- Consider and discuss connections to invariant-kernel approaches such as Hans Burkhardt’s work.  
- Investigate whether any operator commuting with the group action would guarantee equivariance, and briefly analyze this general question.
