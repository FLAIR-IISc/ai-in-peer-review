- Analyzes deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), proving near invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are appropriately designed.  
- Argues these invariance and stability properties can be extended to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) under certain assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical claims.  
- Recommends experiments demonstrating that appropriately designed CKNs are indeed invariant to transformations and diffeomorphisms.  
- Suggests exploring whether random projection or explicit finite Fourier-series kernel approximations are viable and how they would affect stability.
