The paper studies appealing properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), demonstrating that they are nearly invariant to groups of transformations and stable under the action of diffeomorphisms, provided that the patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling operators are carefully designed. The authors further show that, under certain assumptions, these favorable properties extend to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The primary weakness is the lack of empirical validation for the theoretical analysis. It would be beneficial to see experimental results demonstrating the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms when employing appropriate settings. 

A further question arises regarding alternative kernel approximation techniques. Could random projections or explicit kernel mapping using a finite number of Fourier series also serve as viable solutions? If so, how would these alternatives affect the observed stability properties?
