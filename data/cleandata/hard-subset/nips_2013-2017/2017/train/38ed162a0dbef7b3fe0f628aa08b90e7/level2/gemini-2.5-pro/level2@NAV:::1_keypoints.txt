- Presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations via a multilayer convolutional kernel generalized from CKNs on continuous domains.  
- Main contribution 1: proves the representation is Lipschitz stable to diffeomorphisms (extending scattering network results).  
- Main contribution 2: shows near-translation invariance controlled by the final pooling scale.  
- Main contribution 3: proves the RKHS of the kernel contains a broad class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, extending stability guarantees to those models.  
- Technically rigorous and builds on Mallat’s scattering framework to adapt to a learnable CKN setting.  
- Novel unification of signal processing, kernel methods, and deep learning theory.  
- Clear, well-organized exposition guiding readers through kernel construction and stability analysis.  
- Theoretical results link RKHS norm, deformation stability, and implications for generalization.  
- Key weakness: gap between the theoretical model and practical CNNs due to assumptions like smooth homogeneous activations and non-standard normalization.  
- Key weakness: analysis is purely theoretical with no empirical validation of stability bounds or pooling–invariance trade-offs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous novel theory, conceptual unification, and important insights on regularization, robustness, and generalization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on assumptions misaligned with common practice and lack of experiments to corroborate claims.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution and strong candidate for acceptance despite minor practical gaps.
