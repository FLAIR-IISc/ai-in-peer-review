## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms. The authors build upon the framework of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and generalize it to a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions. They demonstrate that this functional space enjoys similar stability guarantees as the scattering transform, while offering the flexibility of learned filters.

The core contribution lies in establishing a kernel-based perspective on CNNs, allowing for a rigorous analysis of their invariance and stability. The authors show that the proposed kernel framework provides a way to separate data representation from predictive models, enabling a detailed study of each component. The theoretical results, particularly Theorem 6, provide bounds on the stability of the representation under diffeomorphic transformations, linking it to the network’s depth and the scale of pooling layers.  This work builds upon previous research in CKNs [15, 16] and scattering transforms [5, 17], extending the analysis to more general CNN architectures. The connection to related work, including group equivariant CNNs [9] and kernel methods for deep learning [2, 10], is well-established.

The strengths of the paper include a solid theoretical foundation, a clear presentation of the mathematical framework, and a compelling argument for the benefits of a kernel-based approach to understanding CNNs. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the assumption of smooth homogeneous activations and the potential loss of information due to pooling. The detailed proofs and appendices contribute to the reproducibility of the work.

However, the paper could benefit from further discussion of the practical implications of these theoretical results. While the authors mention the link to generalization, a more concrete exploration of how these stability guarantees translate into improved performance on real-world tasks would be valuable. Additionally, the connection between the kernel parameters and the resulting network architecture could be elaborated upon. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous analysis.
*   Novel kernel-based perspective on CNNs.
*   Clear connection to existing work in CKNs and scattering transforms.
*   Well-written and comprehensive presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of practical implications.
*   Potential for further elaboration on the link between kernel parameters and network architecture.

**Overall:** This is a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep convolutional networks. Despite some minor shortcomings, the paper presents a significant advancement in the field and is worthy of acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
