- Rigorous mathematical analysis of invariance and stability for deep convolutional representations.  
- Generalization of multilayer kernels from convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) using RKHS geometry.  
- Establishes links between stability, invariance, and generalization (e.g., Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms).  
- Extends scattering transform results to a broader class of CNN-like models, including CKNs.  
- Uses kernel methods as a powerful framework to study CNN functional spaces.  
- Analyzes effects of practical approximations (discretization, kernel approximation) on theoretical results.  
- Clear presentation with detailed derivations and comprehensive proofs in the appendix.  
- Argues that low RKHS norm correlates with improved sample complexity and generalization.  
- Discusses group invariance and how to achieve both invariance and stability.  
- Limited explicit connection to practical CNN architectures and design choices.  
- Lacks concrete examples showing which real CNNs fall within the analyzed functional space.  
- Relies on smoothness/homogeneity assumptions for activations, limiting applicability to non-smooth activations like ReLU.  
- Insufficient discussion of methods to control RKHS norm during training.  
- Minimal experimental validation to confirm theoretical claims on standard datasets.  
- Brief and underdeveloped treatment of adversarial robustness implications.  
- Potential accessibility issues due to dense mathematical notation for some readers.  
- Reference list (72 refs) is large and could be better organized for clarity.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution suitable for acceptance, with recommendations to strengthen practical connections and experimental support.
