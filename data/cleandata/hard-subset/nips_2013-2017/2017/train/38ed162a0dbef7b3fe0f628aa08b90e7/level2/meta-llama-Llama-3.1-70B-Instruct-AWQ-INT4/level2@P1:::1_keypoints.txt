- Paper proposes a multilayer kernel framework generalizing convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to study invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations to groups and diffeomorphisms, linking to RKHS and encompassing many CNNs.
- Manuscript is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and technically sound, thorough analysis of stability and invariance.

Strengths:
- Introduces a novel, rigorous theoretical framework for deep convolutional representations.
- Provides a thorough analysis of stability and invariance properties of the kernel representation.
- Clear exposition makes the paper accessible to many readers.

Weaknesses:
- Assumes substantial background in functional and harmonic analysis, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Lacks visualizations and illustrations to aid intuition and understanding.
- Many proofs and technical details are deferred to the appendix, making the main arguments harder to follow.

NIPS criteria evaluations:
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis.
- Clarity: Well-written and clearly introduced.
- Originality: Novel and rigorous contribution to theory of convolutional representations.
- Significance: Makes a meaningful contribution by analyzing stability and invariance of kernel representations.

Suggested improvements:
- Add more visualizations/illustrations to clarify concepts and results.
- Make technical details and proofs more accessible or integrate key proofs into the main text.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel and rigorous theoretical framework.
- Significant contribution on stability and invariance analysis.
- Clear writing and concise presentation.

Arguments for rejection:
- Heavy prerequisite knowledge required in functional/harmonic analysis.
- Insufficient visual/intuitive aids.
- Key technical details tucked in appendix, reducing readability.

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend accepting the paper subject to revisions to improve accessibility and add more illustrations.
