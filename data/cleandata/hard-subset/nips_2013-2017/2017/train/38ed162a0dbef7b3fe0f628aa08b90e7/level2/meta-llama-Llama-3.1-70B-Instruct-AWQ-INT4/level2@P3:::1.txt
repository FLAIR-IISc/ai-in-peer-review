**Summary of the Paper**

The paper studies the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations. The authors introduce a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes the construction of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and analyze its properties. They show that the signal representation is stable and that models from this functional space, such as a large class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs), may enjoy the same stability. The paper also discusses the link between the kernel representation and CNNs, and shows that the stability results apply to a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
