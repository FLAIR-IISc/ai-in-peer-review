- Presents a theoretical study of invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations.  
- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Analyzes the kernel’s geometry within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs).  
- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions.  
- Derives stability results showing the kernel representation is stable under diffeomorphisms.  
- Demonstrates stability results translate to predictive models by controlling their RKHS norm.  
- Well-written with a clear, concise introduction and adequate use of mathematical tools.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the kernel representation and impressive stability results.  
- Strength: new kernel construction offering a theoretical framework for invariance and stability.  
- Strength: solid link between the kernel representation and CNNs on continuous domains.  
- Weakness: requires strong background in mathematical analysis and RKHSs, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: lacks intuitive explanations and visualizations to illustrate concepts and results.  
- Weakness: needs more discussion on the practical implications and uses of the link to CNNs.  
- Overall evaluation: high quality, originality, and significance (scores: Quality 9, Clarity 8.5, Originality 9, Significance 9).  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS 2017, with suggestions to add intuition/visuals and discuss practical implications.  
- Pro acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, deep expertise, and clear exposition.  
- Con acceptance: accessibility issues due to heavy mathematical prerequisites and limited intuition/visual aids.
