- Investigates invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations via a kernel-based framework, extending scattering transform theory to general convolutional architectures including CNNs.  
- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and proves stability guarantees under diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Shows the functional space contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions, enabling translation of theoretical results to practical models.  
- Technically rigorous with detailed proofs in the appendix supporting stability and invariance claims.  
- Carefully distinguishes theory from practice, addressing discretization and kernel approximations through convolutional kernel networks.  
- Establishes a valuable connection between RKHS norm and stability/generalization, providing a principled way to control model complexity.  
- Builds effectively on prior work (Mallat’s scattering transform and Mairal’s CKNs) and situates contributions well in context.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, e.g., norm preservation lost due to pooling and mitigated via an additional non-linear prediction layer.  
- Main strength: elegant theoretical unification of scattering transforms and CNNs via a kernel framework with precise stability results (Theorem 6) that depend on pooling scales and depth.  
- Significant potential impact for understanding and designing more stable CNNs, linking stability to generalization and sample complexity.  
- Major weakness: lack of empirical validation or experiments demonstrating how theoretical stability properties manifest in actual CNN training/performance.  
- Restrictive assumption: requirement for smooth homogeneous activation functions excludes standard nonsmooth ReLU variants and weakens direct applicability to common CNNs.  
- Presentation is mathematically dense and could use more intuitive explanations or visualizations to improve accessibility.  
- Clarity is generally good (consistent notation and flow), but technical multilayer constructions would benefit from simple motivating examples.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of scattering transform to a broader class of convolutional architectures with theoretical guarantees.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add intuitive explanations, address empirical validation gap, and better connect results to standard CNN architectures.
