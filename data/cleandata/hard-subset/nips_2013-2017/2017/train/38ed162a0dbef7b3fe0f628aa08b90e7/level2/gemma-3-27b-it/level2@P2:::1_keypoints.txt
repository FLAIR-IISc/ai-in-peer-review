- Well-motivated study addressing theoretical properties of CNNs from a kernel perspective.  
- Positions clearly within literature, extending scattering transforms, CKNs, and kernel methods.  
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks to a broader class of convolutional architectures.  
- Uses RKHS theory to rigorously analyze geometry, providing detailed proofs.  
- Demonstrates stability to diffeomorphisms and near-invariance to group transformations.  
- Connects stability (via RKHS norm) to potential improvements in sample complexity and generalization.  
- Clear presentation and consistent notation despite mathematical depth.  
- Lacks empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact or illustrate theoretical bounds.  
- Mathematical complexity reduces accessibility for readers unfamiliar with RKHS and harmonic analysis.  
- Practicality concerns about kernel approximation and computational challenges are under-discussed.  
- Restriction to homogeneous activations (e.g., ReLU variants) may limit practical applicability.  
- Insufficient comparison between RKHS-norm-based regularization and standard techniques like weight decay or dropout.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous theoretical contributions that deepen understanding of CNN properties.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical support and potential accessibility issues for a broad audience.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept, recommending addition of experiments and discussion of practical implementation in revisions.
