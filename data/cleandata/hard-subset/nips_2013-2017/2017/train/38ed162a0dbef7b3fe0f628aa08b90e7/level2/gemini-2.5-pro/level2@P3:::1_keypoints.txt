- Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel in an RKHS to analyze invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, generalizing CKNs.
- Proves stability of the representation to small C1-diffeomorphisms with an explicit variation bound, analogous to scattering-transform results.
- Shows progressive translation invariance controlled by the scale of the final pooling layer.
- Extends the framework to equivariance/invariance for general group actions (e.g., rotations) via group-based patch extraction and pooling.
- Demonstrates the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, allowing transfer of stability guarantees to these CNNs.
- Significant: provides rigorous theoretical insight linking RKHS norm, stability, and generalization for CNN-like models.
- High technical quality: rigorous mathematical development, detailed proofs, and clearly stated assumptions (A1–A3).
- Original: novel synthesis of kernel methods, scattering transforms, and deep networks; elegant approach showing kernel-defined function space encompasses CNNs.
- Clear presentation: logical structure, consistent notation, and well-articulated motivation and relation to prior work.
- Weakness: purely theoretical with no empirical validation; experiments linking RKHS bounds to empirical robustness/generalization are suggested.
- Weakness: relies on smooth homogeneous activation functions (ReLU is not smooth); practical implications and comparisons to smoothed ReLU should be discussed.
- Weakness: stability constants (C1, C2) are abstract; their dependence on network parameters (patch size, pooling filter) needs clearer interpretation.
- Weakness: representation does not preserve signal norm due to pooling; trade-offs between stability/invariance and potential information loss (invertibility/conditioning of pooling) warrant more discussion.
