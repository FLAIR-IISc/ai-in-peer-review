This paper presents a theoretical study of the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations. The authors introduce a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes the construction of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and study its geometry in the context of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). They show that this functional space contains a large class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with smooth homogeneous activation functions, allowing them to obtain theoretical results for both classes of models.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the related work. The construction of the multilayer convolutional kernel is well-explained, and the use of mathematical tools is adequate. The stability results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the kernel representation and its properties.

The paper's main contributions are the introduction of the multilayer convolutional kernel and the study of its stability properties. The authors show that the kernel representation is stable under the action of diffeomorphisms and that the stability results can be translated to predictive models by controlling their norm in the functional space. They also provide a link between the kernel representation and CNNs, showing that the RKHS obtained from the kernel construction contains a set of CNNs on continuous domains with certain types of smooth homogeneous activations.

The paper's strengths include:

* The introduction of a new kernel construction that generalizes the CKNs and provides a theoretical framework for studying the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations.
* The thorough analysis of the kernel representation and its properties, including the stability results and the link to CNNs.
* The use of mathematical tools is adequate, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the construction and the analysis.

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The paper assumes a strong background in mathematical analysis and RKHSs, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations and visualizations to help illustrate the concepts and results.
* The link to CNNs is an important contribution, but the authors could provide more discussion on the implications of this result and how it can be used in practice.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep convolutional representations. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the subject matter, and the paper is a valuable addition to the field.
