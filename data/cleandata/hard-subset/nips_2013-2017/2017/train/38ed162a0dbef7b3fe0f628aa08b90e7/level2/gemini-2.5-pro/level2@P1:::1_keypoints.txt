- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel generalizing CKNs to continuous signals for analyzing deep convolutional representations.

- Proves the RKHS representation is stable to small C1-diffeomorphisms and nearly translation-invariant, extending scattering-transform guarantees to learnable architectures.

- Extends the framework to achieve equivariance and invariance to general locally compact group actions (e.g., rotations).

- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, allowing kernel-derived stability/invariance to apply to those CNNs.

- Significant theoretical contribution linking scattering-transform theory and practical CNN architectures, relating pooling scale and depth to geometric properties.

- Novel connection between CNNs and kernel methods: the RKHS norm controls stability and ties regularization, generalization, and deformation robustness.

- Paper is clear, rigorous, and mathematically precise, with well-structured proofs and links between continuous and discrete-domain constructions.

- Framework is general, applying to d-dimensional signals and enabling group-action-based invariances beyond 2D images.

- Practical gap: the CNN class in the RKHS uses a non-standard patch-norm-dependent activation, raising questions about relevance to standard ReLU CNNs.

- Lacks empirical validation; experiments demonstrating pooling scale vs. invariance or RKHS-based regularization effects would strengthen practical impact.

- Pooling is not norm-preserving, so progressive norm reduction may affect representation energy and the interpretation of stability bounds in very deep networks.

- Overall: original, technically strong theoretical work with important insights; recommended for acceptance despite the above practical concerns.
