- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations: invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel framework from CKNs to define a functional space containing CKNs and many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Main contribution: proves representations in this functional space are stable to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Shows stability properties transfer to predictive models via control of the RKHS norm.  
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering transform and prior CKN work, bridging stability guarantees with learnable CNN architectures.  
- Theorem 6 gives a clear bound on sensitivity to diffeomorphisms, highlighting the role of pooling and increased translation invariance with depth.  
- Carefully addresses continuous-to-discrete transition and connects theory to CKN implementation.  
- Proof techniques are rigorous and appropriately extend Mallat’s scattering analysis to the kernel setting.  
- Proposition 7 demonstrates a broad class of CNNs lies in the constructed RKHS, linking CNNs and kernel methods.  
- Argues RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, explaining why small-norm stable representations should generalize well.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and acknowledges limitations such as lack of norm preservation, proposing a nonlinear prediction layer fix.  
- Suggests practical implications for regularization and sample-efficient training but would benefit from a detailed computational complexity comparison to standard CNN training.  
- Would be strengthened by more explicit discussion of how theoretical stability guarantees translate to empirical performance on vision benchmarks.
