- Paper develops a multilayer convolutional kernel representation generalizing CKNs to continuous domains and analyzes invariance to group actions and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Using RKHS geometry, authors prove Lipschitz stability to C1-diffeomorphisms with explicit dependence on deformation magnitude and last pooling scale, extending scattering theory.  
- They design group-equivariant patch/pooling operators to obtain global invariance to compact groups.  
- Show a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the induced RKHS, transferring stability guarantees to these CNNs.  
- Positions work relative to prior CKN, kernel-deep-net, and group-equivariant CNN literature, and contrasts with scattering, kernel approximations, and robustness results.  
- Strength: Clear, rigorous theoretical framework unifying scattering-style stability with kernelized deep representations.  
- Strength: Thoughtful treatment of discretization and kernel approximation, including signal-preservation lemmas and finite-dimensional compatibility.  
- Strength: Valuable link showing CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations reside in the RKHS, suggesting ties between stability and generalization.  
- Strength: Principled, harmonic-analysis-aligned group-equivariant construction.  
- Weakness: No empirical validation or numerical evidence for bound tightness, constants, or practical robustness/generalization impact.  
- Weakness: Restrictive assumptions (smoothed ReLU-like activations, normalization nk(u), A3 coupling, small-deformation ‖∇τ‖∞ < 1/2) may limit practical scope.  
- Weakness: Stability bound scales with depth (1+n) and depends on κ; limited discussion of constant behavior for realistic architectures.  
- Weakness: Group-invariance analysis assumes signals on groups and ideal Haar-convolution; finite-grid, boundary, and non-compact group issues are briefly treated.  
- Weakness: No quantitative sample-complexity or finite-sample generalization bounds despite RKHS-norm narrative.  
- Usefulness: Provides conceptual clarity and tools for reasoning about invariance and stability in CNN-like architectures, likely to influence theory and design.  
- Suggestion: Add controlled experiments comparing deformation stability of CKNs/CNNs with and without proposed designs, and quantify C1/C2 behavior.  
- Suggestion: Discuss extensions to common components (ReLU, batch norm, strides, residuals) and how to approximate them in the RKHS.  
- Suggestion: Provide guidelines and sensitivity analyses for choosing σk, patch sizes, and projections under discretization.  
- Suggestion: Explore finite-sample generalization bounds using the induced RKHS norm for the CNN class.  
- Argument for acceptance: Solid, novel theoretical contribution extending scattering-like guarantees to broader deep architectures and bridging kernels and CNNs.  
- Argument against acceptance: Lack of empirical substantiation and practical limitations from assumptions and unquantified constants.
