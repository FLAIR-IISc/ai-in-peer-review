- Studies invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations, generalizing CKNs to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Proposes a kernel framework that yields stability guarantees similar to scattering transforms while retaining learning flexibility of CNNs.  
- Main contribution: establishes a theoretical connection between kernel methods and CNNs to explain stability and invariance properties.  
- Shows that controlling the RKHS norm can improve generalization, linking stability to sample complexity.  
- Extends prior scattering-transform results to a broader setting and characterizes the geometry of functional spaces for CNNs.  
- Theoretical treatment is rigorous with detailed proofs and strong engagement with related literature.  
- Strength: extends existing results to a wider class of networks and considers practical issues (discretization, kernel approximation).  
- Weakness: heavy use of dense mathematical notation may reduce accessibility for non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: assumption of smooth homogeneous activations limits direct applicability to common activations like ReLU (though smoothed variants are discussed).  
- Weakness: connection between the theoretical framework and practical CNN design lacks concrete, specific examples.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add concrete examples linking theory to practice.
