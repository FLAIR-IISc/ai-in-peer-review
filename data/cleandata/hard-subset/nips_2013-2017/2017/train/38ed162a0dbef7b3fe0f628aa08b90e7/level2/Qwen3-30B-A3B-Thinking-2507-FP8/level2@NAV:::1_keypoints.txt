- Paper studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations: invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Main claim: generalizing multilayer kernel framework from CKNs yields a functional space containing many CNNs with scattering-like stability while preserving signal information.  
- Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, implying potential improvements in sample complexity.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs for stability bounds under diffeomorphisms (Theorem 6).  
- Extends results to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations (Proposition 7).  
- Valuable connection between kernel framework and practical CNN architectures, bridging theory and practice.  
- Builds on and extends Mallat’s scattering transform work to more general CNNs.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with well-constructed proofs and appropriate mathematical tools.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: continuous framework requires discretization and kernel approximations for practical implementation.  
- Note that norm preservation differs from scattering transform, mitigated by using additional nonlinear prediction layers.  
- Significance: provides principled foundation for why CNNs work and links RKHS norm to robustness and generalization.  
- Limited experimental validation (acceptable given theoretical focus).  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and thoroughly referenced.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance.
