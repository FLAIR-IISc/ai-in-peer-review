- Paper shows deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms when patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling are properly designed.
- Authors prove that, under certain assumptions, the invariance and stability properties of CKNs carry over to convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
- The manuscript is well written and technically sound.
- Major weakness: no empirical experiments are provided to validate the theoretical analysis.
- Reviewer requests experimental results demonstrating CKNs’ invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms under the proposed settings.
- Open question: could random projection or explicit kernel mapping via a finite number of Fourier features serve as kernel approximations, and how would they affect stability?
