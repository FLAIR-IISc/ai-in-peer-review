- Paper is well written, rigorous, and demonstrates DCNN functional spaces can be described as multilayer kernels; shows (approx.) group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms — recommendation: accept.  
- Reviewer views main contribution as a reformulation of CNNs in the RKHS context and asks authors to state why this reformulation is useful (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation advantages).  
- Request to clarify whether the novelty goes beyond a pure RKHS reformulation of DCNNs.  
- Ask to clarify novelty relative to “Convolutional Kernel Networks” (Mairal et al.).  
- Question the emphasis on “not losing the signal information”: why preserve full signal info when many tasks only need task-relevant information?  
- Ask if there is a connection to invariant-kernel approaches such as Hans Burkhardt’s work.  
- Curiosity about whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would ensure DCNN equivariance, i.e., characterization of operators ensuring equivariance.
