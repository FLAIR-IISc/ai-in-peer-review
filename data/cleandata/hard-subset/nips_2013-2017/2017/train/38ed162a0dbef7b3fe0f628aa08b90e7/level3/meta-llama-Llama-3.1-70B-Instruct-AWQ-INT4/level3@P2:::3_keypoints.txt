- Proposes an RKHS reformulation of DCNNs to study their invariance and stability properties.
- Shows the functional space of a multilayer convolutional kernel contains a large class of DCNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
- Uses the RKHS view to analyze geometry of the functional space and derive stability bounds to deformations and translations.
- Paper is technically sound, rigorous, and well-written.
- Reviewer asks why the RKHS reformulation is useful in practice—implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation benefits are unclear.
- Requests clarification on whether the contribution goes beyond a pure RKHS recasting of DCNNs.
- Asks how this work relates to prior convolutional kernel networks (Mairal et al.) and similar literature.
- Questions the emphasis on “not losing the signal information” and why preserving full signal information is necessary for downstream tasks.
- Suggests exploring connections to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) and group-commuting operators for equivariance.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but require authors to address the above questions and provide more motivation and context.
- Strengths: originality, potential impact, clear exposition, and solid technical results.
- Weaknesses: uncertainty about novelty beyond RKHS framing, relation to prior work, and the necessity of preserving full signal information.
