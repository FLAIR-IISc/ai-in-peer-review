- Paper analyzes invariance and stability of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and relates these properties to CNNs.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.
- Shows conditions under which CKN properties carry over to CNNs.
- Well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented with proofs moved to the appendix.
- Thorough related-work review and consistent mathematical notation.
- Main strength: comprehensive geometric analysis of the RKHS associated with the kernel and its impact on stability and generalization.
- Main weakness: no empirical experiments to validate theoretical findings.
- Originality: novel combination of harmonic analysis and kernel methods, especially RKHS-based geometric view, but builds on prior work.
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of deep networks and offers a framework for analyzing other architectures.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis, novel methodological combination, and contribution to theory of image classification models.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of empirical validation and results are partly expected given prior work.
- Recommendations: add empirical experiments and expand discussion of practical implications for network design.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
