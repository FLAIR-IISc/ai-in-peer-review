- Presents a theoretical analysis of invariance and stability for deep convolutional representations via a multilayer convolutional kernel on continuous domains.  
- Generalizes the Convolutional Kernel Network (CKN) framework to continuous signals and designs patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling to be norm-preserving or non-expansive.  
- Main theoretical result: representation is stable to small C1-diffeomorphisms and nearly invariant to translations (Theorem 6).  
- Extends scattering-transform analysis to learned-filter architectures, broadening applicability beyond predefined wavelets.  
- Shows that under smooth, homogeneous activations, many standard CNNs lie in the RKHS of the proposed kernel, transferring stability/invariance guarantees to those CNNs (Proposition 7).  
- Strength: high technical quality and clarity with rigorous, well-structured mathematical arguments and linkage between continuous and discrete settings.  
- Strength: original and significant contribution connecting RKHS geometry to CNN representational properties and generalization via RKHS norms.  
- Strength: theoretical soundness with thorough analysis of how patching, mapping, and pooling affect stability and an extension to general group actions.  
- Weakness: complete lack of empirical validation—no experiments to assess practical impact of idealized assumptions or validate stability bounds.  
- Request for experiments: implement a CKN, apply controlled diffeomorphisms on synthetic signals, and empirically verify how ||Φ(Lτx)−Φ(x)|| scales with ||∇τ||∞, ||τ||∞, and pooling scale σn.  
- Question on kernel approximations: need discussion on how practical approximations (random Fourier features, Nyström) and their approximation errors affect stability guarantees.  
- Question on homogeneous activations: clarify practical implications of requiring smooth, homogeneous activations given common use of non-smooth ReLU and discuss sensitivity of results to smoothness.
