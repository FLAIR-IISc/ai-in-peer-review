- Studies theoretical properties of deep CNNs via positive definite multilayer convolutional kernels and their RKHS geometry.
- Shows signal representations are stable and that models in this RKHS (including many CNNs) inherit stability and approximate group invariance.
- Paper is well-written, rigorous, with clear notation and a logical construction of the multilayer convolutional kernel.
- Main contribution is a reformulation of CNNs in the RKHS framework.
- Reviewer asks authors to explicitly state why the RKHS reformulation is useful (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation benefits).
- Novelty relative to prior work (especially "Convolutional Kernel Networks" by Mairal et al.) should be clarified.
- Suggests discussing connections to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) and how this work differs.
- Questions the emphasis on "not losing signal information" since many tasks only need task-relevant features; authors should justify this stance.
- Requests investigation/characterization of operators that ensure equivariance (e.g., operators commuting with all group elements).
- Notes limitation of assumptions, such as smooth homogeneous activation functions and potential information loss from pooling layers.
- Strengths: rigorous mathematical treatment and providing stability/invariance guarantees for a broad class of CNNs.
- Significance: results could substantially impact deep learning and computer vision theory and design.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
- Pros: originality, rigor, and potential impact; Cons: need clearer differentiation from prior work and addressing stated limitations.
