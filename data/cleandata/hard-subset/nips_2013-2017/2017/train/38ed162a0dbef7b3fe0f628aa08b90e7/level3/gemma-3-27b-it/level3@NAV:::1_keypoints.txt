- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their relation to CNNs.
- Proves CKNs are near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms given suitable patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.
- Extends these theoretical invariance and stability results from CKNs to a broader class of CNNs.
- Frames the analysis using positive definite kernels and reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for rigorous mathematical treatment.
- Provides clear mathematical formulations and situates the work within prior research (scattering transforms and kernel methods).
- Demonstrates how kernel design and pooling choices affect stability and invariance.
- Major weakness: complete lack of empirical validation leaves practical implications unclear.
- Recommends experimental demonstration of robustness to image distortions and performance on transformed datasets.
- Raises an open question about approximating kernels (random projections, Fourier features) and how approximations affect stability guarantees and efficiency.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, technically sound theoretical contribution that fills a gap in understanding.
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experiments significantly weakens practical relevance.
- Overall score: 6/10, recommend acceptance contingent on adding experimental validation.
