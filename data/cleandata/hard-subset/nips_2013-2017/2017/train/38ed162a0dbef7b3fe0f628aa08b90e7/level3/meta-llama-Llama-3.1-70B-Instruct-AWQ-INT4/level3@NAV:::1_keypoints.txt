- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations using a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).
- Uses harmonic analysis to show the kernel representation is stable to small deformations and invariant to transformation groups.
- Proves that the derived stability and invariance properties extend to a broad class of CNNs under certain assumptions.
- Strength: provides rigorous mathematical foundations for invariance and stability in deep convolutional representations.
- Strength: results are applicable to a large class of CNN architectures, increasing relevance to the deep learning community.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Weakness: no empirical experiments are provided to validate the theoretical claims.
- Weakness: several assumptions and simplifications are used, which may limit practical applicability.
- Novelty: offers a new kernel-based perspective on CNN behavior.
- Contribution: delivers new theoretical insights into invariance and stability properties.
- Potential impact: could influence development of more robust and generalizable CNNs.
- Suggestions: include empirical validation to demonstrate practical relevance.
- Suggestions: relax or replace assumptions with more realistic ones to improve applicability.
- Suggestions: compare to existing methods for achieving invariance and stability in CNNs.
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
