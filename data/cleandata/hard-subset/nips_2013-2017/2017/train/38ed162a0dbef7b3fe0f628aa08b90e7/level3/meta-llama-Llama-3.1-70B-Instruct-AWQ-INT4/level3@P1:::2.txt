The paper "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations" presents a theoretical study of the stability properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically those obtained through convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). The authors aim to understand the geometric properties of these representations and how they relate to the stability and invariance of the models.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and mathematical derivations. The technical soundness of the paper is evident, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the underlying mathematical concepts, including reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and diffeomorphisms.

The main contribution of the paper is the stability analysis of CKNs to C^1 diffeomorphisms, which is an important step towards understanding the robustness of these models to transformations and deformations. The authors prove that under certain conditions (A1-A3), the CKN representation is stable to diffeomorphisms, and they provide bounds on the stability in terms of the norm of the diffeomorphism and the pooling size.

The extension of the stability analysis from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is an interesting direction, and the authors provide a sketch of how this can be done in Section 4. This extension has the potential to lead to new insights into the stability properties of CKNs and other deep neural networks.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the motivation for the stability analysis is not fully clear beyond a single sentence linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness. A stronger motivation for the analysis would help to contextualize the results and make them more compelling to the reader.

Secondly, there are a few notation issues that could be clarified. For example, the symbol \kappa is overloaded in (A3) and also used to denote the kernel function in Eq. (10), which may confuse readers.

Thirdly, there are a few minor errors and typos in the paper. For example, the second constraint in the displayed equation between lines 384-385 is redundant due to kernel symmetry. Additionally, the inequality around lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality, and ||z|| - ||z|| should be ||z|| - ||z'||.

Finally, the bibliography could be improved by adding page information for references [3,6,8,13,14,18,25-27,30,31] and updating the publication/acceptance details for references [9,17,19,32].

Overall, the paper is a solid contribution to the field of deep learning and kernel methods, and it has the potential to lead to new insights into the stability properties of CKNs and other deep neural networks.
