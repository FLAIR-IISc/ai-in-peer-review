- Paper studies invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernel framework from Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) and analyzes the corresponding RKHS.  
- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, enabling theoretical stability guarantees.  
- Extends Mallat’s scattering-transform analysis to standard CNN architectures via a kernel-based perspective.  
- Connects to Mairal et al.’s CKNs and claims a generalized framework with additional theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: exceptionally well-written, rigorous, with clear mathematical formulation and thorough proofs.  
- Strength: key theoretical contribution—DCNN functional spaces described as multilayer kernels with (approximate) group invariance and diffeomorphism stability.  
- Strength: insightful link between RKHS norm and generalization, suggesting stability can improve sample complexity.  
- Strength: solid discretization analysis and practical connection to CKN implementations.  
- Weakness: paper does not clearly articulate why the RKHS reformulation is useful—needs explicit new insights beyond prior work.  
- Weakness: unclear concrete implications for generalization bounds beyond the generic RKHS connection.  
- Weakness: unclear any computational or approximation advantages from the reformulation.  
- Weakness: novelty relative to Mairal et al.’s CKNs is insufficiently clarified—must state how the multilayer kernel differs and what new theoretical insights are gained.  
- Weakness: the claim about "not losing signal information" needs justification vs. selective, task-relevant representations for classification.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of connections to prior invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Burkhardt).  
- Weakness: would benefit from a more general characterization of operators that ensure equivariance beyond the specific patch extraction and pooling considered.  
- Recommendation: technically strong, clear, original, and significant—recommended for acceptance pending addressing the requested clarifications.
