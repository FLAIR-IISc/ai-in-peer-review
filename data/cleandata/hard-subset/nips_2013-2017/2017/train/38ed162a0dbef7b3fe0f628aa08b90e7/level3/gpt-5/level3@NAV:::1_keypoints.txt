- Multilayer convolutional kernel representation yielding near-invariance to transformation groups and Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms with bounds C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞/σn.  
- Equivariance/invariance achieved via group-covariant layers and final pooling; embeds CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS so stability transfers when RKHS norm is controlled.  
- Generalizes scattering theory from fixed wavelets to learned kernel mappings/CKNs and connects to group-equivariant architectures and kernel approximations.  
- Technical rigor: explicit assumptions (A1–A3), operator commutator analysis, and discretization/CKN projections preserve non-expansiveness.  
- Main limitation: absence of empirical validation to corroborate theoretical constants, trade-offs, and practical impact on modern CNNs.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; clear links between continuous analysis, discretization, and practical CKNs/CNNs; would benefit from quantifying how constants depend on kernel choices and patch/pooling schedules.  
- Originality: novel RKHS-based pathway transferring scattering-style stability to broader convolutional architectures; elegant inclusion of CNNs within RKHS via norm control.  
- Significance: potential to influence design and regularization of invariance/stability in deep models; impact would be substantial with empirical support.

- Cons: no empirical validation; practical implications of constants and approximations not quantified; smooth-activation assumptions may limit immediate applicability to standard ReLU CNNs without smoothing.
