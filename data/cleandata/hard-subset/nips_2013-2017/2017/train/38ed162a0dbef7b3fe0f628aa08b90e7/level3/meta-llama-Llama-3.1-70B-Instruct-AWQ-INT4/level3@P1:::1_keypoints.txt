- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and relates these properties to CNNs using harmonic analysis and RKHS tools.  
- Shows CKNs can be made nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms, and extends these guarantees to CNNs under certain assumptions.  
- Builds on prior work on convolutional kernel networks and the scattering transform, focusing on theoretical analysis rather than empirical evaluation.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous, clear, and accompanied by a detailed discussion of assumptions and limitations.  
- Paper is well-organized, with consistent notation and a helpful appendix for proofs.  
- Presents original and significant theoretical contributions, especially the connections between CKNs and CNNs.  
- Results are significant for machine learning and computer vision and could inform CNN design and training.  
- Major weakness: lack of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical findings.  
- Open question: impact of kernel approximations (e.g., random projections or finite Fourier features) on stability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous analysis with practical implications for CNNs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experiments and limited appeal to non-theoretical readers.  
- Conclusion: technically sound and well-written work that provides valuable theoretical insights despite missing empirical validation.
