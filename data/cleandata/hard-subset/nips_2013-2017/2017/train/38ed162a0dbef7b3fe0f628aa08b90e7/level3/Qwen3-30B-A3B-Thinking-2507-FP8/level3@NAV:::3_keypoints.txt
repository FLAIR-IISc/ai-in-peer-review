- Presents a rigorous theoretical framework linking DCNNs to RKHS, characterizing DCNN functional spaces as multilayer kernels with approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Builds on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and extends the framework to continuous domains, showing the functional space contains a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Theoretical results on deformation stability and translation invariance are compelling and connect to Mallat’s scattering transform while offering novel insights into DCNNs.  
- Technical quality is high: mathematical arguments are sound, notation is clear, proofs are thorough, and the writing is accessible.  
- Demonstrates that stability properties can be transferred to predictive models via RKHS norm control, suggesting potential implications for generalization bounds.  
- Needs clearer statement of novelty and utility of the RKHS reformulation relative to prior CKN work (Mairal et al.).  
- Should justify why preserving full signal information is advantageous when many tasks only require task-relevant features.  
- Should clarify the relationship to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Burkhardt’s work).  
- Main contribution appears to be a reformulation rather than a new technique; the paper needs stronger justification of practical benefits.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, valuable theoretical contribution; minor clarifications recommended; reviewer recommends acceptance.
