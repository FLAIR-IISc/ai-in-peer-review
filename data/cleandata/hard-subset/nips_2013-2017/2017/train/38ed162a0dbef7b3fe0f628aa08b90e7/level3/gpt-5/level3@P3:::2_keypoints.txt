- Paper develops a multilayer convolutional kernel representation that is translation-near-invariant via pooling, provably stable to C^1 diffeomorphisms under Lipschitz-type bounds, and preserves signal information with RKHS-compatible discretization and approximations.
- Central stability theorem: under A1–A3, the representation Φ satisfies a deformation stability bound of the form C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + (C2/σn)‖τ‖∞.
- Sketch provided to extend equivariance/invariance from translations to general locally compact groups using Haar integration and last-layer pooling.
- RKHS induced by the multilayer kernel contains a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, so stability transfers to these CNNs and their predictions are controlled by the RKHS norm.
- Generalizes scattering-transform stability results to CKNs and a subset of CNNs, linking deep networks and kernel methods.
- Builds on CKNs (Mairal et al.) by extending to continuous domains and providing deformation stability guarantees.
- Adapts harmonic-analysis tools from scattering transforms to a broader kernel/CNN framework.
- Complements group-equivariant CNN literature by offering a kernel/RKHS route to equivariance and invariance on locally compact groups.
- Strength: technically careful and sound analysis using operator-commutator bounds and explicit, reasonable assumptions (A1–A3).
- Strength: manuscript is well organized and clearly written, with clear discretization and approximation discussions.
- Strength: original extension of scattering-style stability to CKNs and some CNNs, plus a natural generalization to locally compact groups.
- Strength: significant theoretical foundation for stability/invariance in architectures close to practical CNNs and kernel networks.
- Weakness: practical motivation for deformation stability is brief and should be broadened beyond a single sentence on adversarial robustness.
- Weakness: dependence on κ and dimension (κ^(d+1) in Lemma 4) and linear depth scaling (1+n) in Theorem 6 need discussion on tightness and practical impact; (A3) may constrain architecture design.
- Weakness: approximation/discretization constants are not fully explicit, limiting practical guidance.
- Weakness: primarily theoretical with no empirical illustrations; toy experiments would strengthen impact.
- Request: expand motivation to include sample complexity, distribution shift, certified robustness, and links to generalization (covering-number/Rademacher bounds).
- Request: avoid overloading κ; rename one occurrence (e.g., ρ for patch-size factor or ψ for kernel profile).
- Request: remove a redundant constraint between lines 384–385; define ϕ before use at line 416; fix typos/inequalities and a ||z|| − ||z'|| replacement.
- Request: add full bibliographic details (pages, volume/issue) for multiple references and update publication details for specified entries.
- Request: state a formal proposition for group extension clarifying conditions for global invariance via last-layer pooling and comment on deformation stability analogues with Haar integration.
- Request: clarify how subsampling, boundary effects, and projection error affect the constants in Theorem 6 and provide practical guidance (ek, sk, σk).
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7.5/10, Significance 7.5/10.
- Pros for acceptance: solid, general theoretical contribution bridging kernels and CNNs; clear assumptions and elegant operator methods; extension to locally compact groups.
- Cons for acceptance: underdeveloped motivation, limited empirical illustration, some notational and bibliographic rough edges.
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to addressing motivation, notation, minor fixes, and (ideally) brief experiments.
