- Paper proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel representation stable to C1-diffeomorphisms and nearly invariant to translations/group actions via pooling.  
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to continuous domains and analyzes the induced RKHS geometry.  
- Theoretical contributions: signal-preservation lemmas in continuous/discrete settings (Lemmas 1–2).  
- Theoretical contributions: stability bound to diffeomorphisms that improves with depth via final pooling scale (Theorem 6).  
- Theoretical contributions: compatibility of stability with kernel approximation/projection used in practical CKNs.  
- Theoretical contributions: embedding result showing many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS, transferring stability via norm control (Proposition 7).  
- Connects and extends scattering transform literature by removing fixed-wavelet restriction and linking to CKNs and group-equivariant convolutions.  
- Technical soundness: assumptions A1–A3 clear and satisfied by many homogeneous dot-product kernels; proofs use nonexpansiveness and operator-norm bounds.  
- Stability bound is well-motivated, recovers translation equivariance at intermediate layers, and ties invariance to pooling scale; dependence on ‖∇τ‖∞ matches scattering analysis.  
- Discretization and kernel-approximation arguments show practical CKNs inherit nonexpansiveness and stability up to approximation error.  
- Proposition 7 constructively includes smooth-activation CNNs in the RKHS with explicit, composable norm bounds.  
- Weakness: motivation and empirical relevance are limited; need richer motivation (sample complexity, augmentation, robustness) and small empirical illustrations.  
- Weakness: constants and scope need clarification—how C1 scales with κ, d, h and interpretation of ‖∇τ‖∞ ≤ 1/2.  
- Weakness: linear-in-depth factor (1+n) requires discussion relative to modern deep nets and mitigation via larger σn.  
- Clarity issues: notation overload (κ used twice); recommend renaming to avoid confusion.  
- Clarity issues: redundant mathematical constraint between lines 384–385 can be removed.  
- Clarity issues: missing definition of feature map φ before its first use (line 416).  
- Clarity issues: typos/inequality equality note around lines 427–428 and replacement of “‖z‖ − ‖z‖” with “‖z‖ − ‖z′‖”.  
- Clarity issues: bibliography entries missing page info and some references need updated publication details.  
- Originality: unifies scattering-style stability with kernel/CKN viewpoints and provides a kernel/RKHS formulation of group invariance.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of invariance/stability beyond fixed-wavelet constructions with implications for generalization and robustness.  
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous multilayer stability analysis, clear continuous-to-discrete path, RKHS inclusion of CNNs, group-equivariant formulation, well-written.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited practical experiments and motivation, exclusion of nonsmooth activations like ReLU, (1+n) dependence and pooling trade-offs, minor notation/typo issues.  
- Suggestions: expand motivation (sample complexity, augmentation), give explicit kernel/activation examples and parameter guidance, add a small synthetic experiment varying σn and n.  
- Suggestions: clarify deconvolution stability in Lemma 2 (blur scale, boundary handling), discuss scaling of constants with dimension/patch size and potential sublinear-in-n bounds, relate RKHS norm to standard CNN weight-norm regularization.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (technically solid and substantive contribution); high confidence.
