- Paper studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and links to CNNs.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing CKNs and many CNNs can be nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms under proper design.  
- Builds on scattering transform and extends prior theoretical frameworks to a more general kernel-based setting.  
- Well-motivated: addresses fundamental geometry of function spaces, with ties to regularization and sample complexity.  
- Technical presentation is strong with clear definitions and careful mathematical derivations.  
- Comprehensive related-work discussion connecting kernel methods, scattering transforms, and group-invariant representations.  
- Uses harmonic analysis and kernel theory effectively, demonstrating strong mathematical grounding.  
- Extension from CKNs to a broader class of CNNs is a significant and practically relevant contribution.  
- Theoretical rigor is a key strength: clear assumptions and precise bounds on stability and invariance.  
- RKHS framework is used to analyze learned representations powerfully.  
- Treatment of diffeomorphic deformations and group invariance is thorough and well-justified.  
- Discussion of how kernel approximations (e.g., finite-dimensional subspaces) affect stability is insightful.  
- Major weakness: complete absence of empirical validation to support theoretical claims.  
- Authors acknowledge lack of experiments but provide no concrete experimental plan or results.  
- Suggested experiments: visualize CKN feature maps under deformations or measure performance with augmented transformations.  
- Future work idea: explore alternative kernel approximation schemes (random projections, Fourier features) and their effect on stability guarantees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novelty of kernel perspective, technical soundness, solid links to existing work, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of empirical validation and limited immediate practical guidance for CNN design/training.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but weakened by missing experiments.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, with strong encouragement to add empirical validation in future revisions.
