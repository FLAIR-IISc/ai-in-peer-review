- Proposes a theoretical framework describing deep convolutional representations as multilayer kernels.  
- Builds on Convolutional Kernel Networks and the scattering transform to construct an RKHS that contains a large class of CNNs.  
- Formally proves stability of these representations to small diffeomorphisms and near-invariance to transformation groups.  
- Connects the RKHS norm to stability, linking geometric properties of representations with generalization theory.  
- Provides a unified, mathematically rigorous and well-written lens for viewing CKNs and certain conventional CNNs.  
- Requests clearer articulation of practical and theoretical implications: what concrete benefits does the RKHS reformulation provide (e.g., new bounds, computational gains, architectures)?  
- Suggests sharpening novelty: clarify what the framework offers beyond a straightforward RKHS reformulation and how it extends Mairal et al.’s CKN work.  
- Questions the emphasis on “not losing the signal information,” noting discriminative tasks often require discarding task-irrelevant information.  
- Asks about connections to prior work on invariant kernels (e.g., Hans Burkhardt’s group).  
- Theoretically asks whether the authors can characterize the full class of operators that ensure equivariance (e.g., do all operators commuting with group elements suffice?).  
- Overall judgment: excellent, insightful theoretical contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance if the authors address the clarifications above.
