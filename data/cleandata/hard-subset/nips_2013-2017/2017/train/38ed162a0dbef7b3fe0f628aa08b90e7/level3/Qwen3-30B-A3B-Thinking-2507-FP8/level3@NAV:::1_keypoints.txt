- Presents a compelling theoretical framework for invariance and stability of convolutional representations.  
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to show near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms with appropriate patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Proves these invariance and stability properties extend to certain convolutional neural networks (CNNs), providing a rigorous foundation for CNN effectiveness.  
- Builds on and extends Mallat’s scattering transform and Mairal’s CKNs to a broader class of architectures.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and technically sound with clear mathematical exposition and careful assumptions.  
- Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, offering valuable theoretical insights for practitioners.  
- Novel connection between kernel methods and CNNs: many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations can be embedded in the proposed kernel-defined functional space.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation—no experiments demonstrating the claimed invariance and stability in practice.  
- Requests experiments showing CKN invariance to transformations and diffeomorphisms and assessment of whether theoretical assumptions hold on real CNNs.  
- Raises practical open question whether random projection or Fourier-feature kernel approximations are effective for implementation.  
- Overall: high-quality and original theoretical contribution, but acceptance should require major revision to include empirical results.
