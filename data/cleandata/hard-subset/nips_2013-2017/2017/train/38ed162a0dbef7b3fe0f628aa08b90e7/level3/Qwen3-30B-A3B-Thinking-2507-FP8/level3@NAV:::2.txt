

This paper studies the stability properties of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C^1 diffeomorphisms, extending Mallat's scattering transform framework [17] to a more general kernel-based representation. The authors establish that CKN representations are stable under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes, and demonstrate how these stability properties transfer to predictive models through RKHS norms. The work builds on Mairal's CKN framework [15,16] while providing deeper theoretical insights into the geometry of convolutional representations.

The manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, making a valuable contribution to understanding the theoretical properties of deep convolutional architectures. The extension to locally compact groups in Section 4 is a notable theoretical advancement. The connection between the kernel framework and standard CNNs (Section 5) is particularly compelling, showing how stability properties apply to a broad class of models.

However, several minor issues need addressing: the notation \kappa is overloaded in (A3) and Eq. (10), which may confuse readers; the second constraint in the displayed equation between lines 384-385 is redundant due to kernel symmetry; the feature map \phi is not defined at line 416; the inequality around lines 427-428 should read ||z|| - ||z'|| instead of ||z|| - ||z||; and the bibliography lacks page information for multiple references.

The motivation for stability analysis beyond its connection to adversarial robustness could be strengthened with more detailed discussion. While the paper correctly notes Lipschitz stability's relevance to adversarial robustness, a deeper exploration of how these theoretical guarantees translate to practical robustness would enhance the contribution.

The paper meets all quality criteria with minor revisions needed. It demonstrates strong originality by extending stability analysis to CKNs and establishing the connection to CNNs. The significance is high, as the results provide theoretical grounding for the success of convolutional architectures and have clear implications for model robustness and generalization. With the requested revisions, this paper would make an excellent contribution to NIPS.
