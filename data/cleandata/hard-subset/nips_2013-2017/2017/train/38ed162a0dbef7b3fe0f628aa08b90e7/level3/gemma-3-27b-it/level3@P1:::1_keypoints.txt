- Paper analyzes invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and links to CNNs.  
- Extends scattering transform theory to a broader class of models, showing near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Demonstrates that theoretical properties of CKNs also apply to CNNs that contain CKNs as a subset, offering theoretical grounding for observed robustness.  
- Thorough literature grounding: builds on scattering transforms, prior CKN work, group invariance, and kernel methods.  
- Rigorous mathematical analysis with careful treatment of continuous vs. discrete representations and clear notation.  
- Novel synthesis of kernel methods, harmonic analysis, and convolutional network theory.  
- Addresses a fundamental question about the geometric properties of functional spaces used by deep networks.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation to confirm theoretical claims on real-world data.  
- Practical implementation may be complex and computationally expensive; paper lacks discussion of such challenges.  
- Assumes “smooth homogeneous” activations, limiting applicability to common activations like ReLU without exploring smoothed variants’ performance impact.  
- Limited discussion of computational cost and trade-offs compared to standard CNNs.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound analysis extending to CNNs with clear presentation and important implications.  
- Argument for rejection: absence of experiments and insufficient treatment of practical/compute limitations weaken impact.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical contribution but practical relevance unclear without experiments.  
- Recommendation: weak reject; paper would be strengthened by empirical demonstrations of invariance/stability and discussion of computational approximations (e.g., random Fourier features).
