- Presents a rigorous, well-written analysis showing DCNN functional spaces can be described as multilayer kernels.  
- Main contribution: reformulates DCNNs in the RKHS framework to derive invariance and stability results.  
- Reviewer questions the practical usefulness of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation benefits).  
- Paper builds on Mairal et al.'s CKNs; novelty relative to that prior work is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Reviewer asks whether contributions go beyond a pure RKHS reinterpretation of DCNNs.  
- Emphasis on preserving full signal information is questioned, since many tasks only require task-relevant features.  
- Requests more context/justification for why preserving full signal information is necessary.  
- Asks about connections to invariant-kernel approaches (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) and potential implications.  
- Seeks characterization of operators that ensure DCNN equivariance (e.g., operators commuting with group elements).  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Strengths: rigorous analysis, significant contribution to understanding DCNNs, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: unclear RKHS implications, unclear novelty vs CKNs, questioned emphasis on full-signal preservation, limited discussion of invariant-kernel links, no operator characterization for equivariance.  
- Recommendation: Accept pending revisions addressing the reviewer's questions and comments.
