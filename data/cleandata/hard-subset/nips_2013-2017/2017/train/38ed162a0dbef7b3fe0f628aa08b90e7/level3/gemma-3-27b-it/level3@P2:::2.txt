This paper studies the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their connection to classical convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The authors generalize a multilayer kernel construction to achieve near-invariance to groups of transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms, while preserving signal information. They establish theoretical guarantees for both CKNs and a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, offering a valuable contribution to understanding the geometric properties of deep learning models.

The work builds upon prior research in scattering transforms [17], convolutional kernel networks [15, 16], and kernel methods for deep learning [2, 3, 7, 36]. It extends the theoretical framework of scattering transforms to more general CNN architectures, leveraging the power of positive definite kernels to separate data representation and predictive modeling. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature, highlighting the connections and novelties of their approach. The focus on stability to diffeomorphisms provides a rigorous framework for analyzing the robustness of these representations to small deformations, which is a critical aspect of generalization performance.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper presents a rigorous mathematical analysis of stability and invariance, building upon a solid foundation of kernel theory and harmonic analysis. The proof techniques appear sound and the results are clearly stated and justified.
*   **Generalizability:** The authors successfully demonstrate that their theoretical results extend beyond CKNs to a broader class of CNNs, increasing the practical relevance of their findings.
*   **Clear Exposition:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The notation is generally consistent, and the authors provide sufficient details to allow for reproducibility.
*   **Novel Contribution:** The work offers a novel perspective on understanding the geometric properties of deep networks, bridging the gap between kernel methods and CNNs. The characterization of stability and invariance provides valuable insights into the behavior of these models.
*   **Relevance to Current Research:** The topic of robustness and generalization in deep learning is highly relevant, and the authors’ work contributes to a deeper understanding of these critical issues.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation for Stability:** While the authors briefly mention the connection between Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness, the motivation for focusing on stability analysis could be strengthened. A more detailed discussion of the practical implications of stability, beyond a single sentence, would enhance the paper’s impact.
*   **Notation Overload:** The symbol *κ* is overloaded, appearing both in assumption (A3) and as a component of the kernel function definition in Eq. (10). This could potentially confuse readers.
*   **Minor Mathematical Simplifications:** The second constraint in the displayed equation between lines 384-385 appears redundant due to the inherent symmetry of kernels.
*   **Missing Definitions/Typos:** The feature map *ϕ* is not defined at line 416 before being used, and should be introduced using Eq. (10). Additionally, the inequality around lines 427–428 appears to hold with equality, and “||z|| - ||z||” should be “||z|| - ||z'||”.
*   **Bibliography Completeness:** Several references ([3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25–27, 30, 31]) lack page numbers. Several others ([9, 17, 19, 32]) would benefit from updated publication/acceptance details.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional representations.
*   The theoretical results are rigorous and well-supported, with clear connections to existing literature.
*   The extension of the stability analysis to a broader class of CNNs enhances the practical relevance of the work.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.
*   The topic is highly relevant to current research in deep learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The motivation for the stability analysis could be strengthened.
*   Minor notation issues and a couple of typos exist (easily fixable).
*   The bibliography is incomplete.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and should be accepted to NIPS 2017. The strengths of the paper—its rigorous theoretical foundation, clear exposition, and novel insights—outweigh the minor weaknesses. Addressing the minor issues regarding motivation, notation, and bibliography would further enhance the quality of the paper. The work has the potential to significantly impact the understanding of generalization and robustness in deep learning, and represents a valuable addition to the NIPS program.
