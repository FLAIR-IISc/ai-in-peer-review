- Multilayer convolutional kernel framework generalizing CKNs to continuous domains via patch extraction, RKHS mappings, and linear pooling.  
- Proves stability to small diffeomorphisms with a Lipschitz-style bound depending on the deformation τ and its gradient.  
- Shows near-translation invariance controlled by the final pooling scale and extends the framework to equivariance/invariance for arbitrary groups.  
- Establishes that the RKHS of the multilayer kernel contains a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, allowing transfer of stability/invariance guarantees to these CNNs via the RKHS norm.  
- Strength: Significant and impactful — provides a rigorous link between stability, generalization, and architecture.  
- Strength: Original and technically strong — novel generalization of CKNs and Proposition 7 embedding CNNs into the RKHS.  
- Strength: Clear and well-written — motivated, well-organized presentation and clear connections to prior work.  
- Weakness: No empirical validation — absence of experiments leaves practical relevance and tightness of bounds speculative.  
- Weakness: Feels incomplete for a venue valuing theory-practice interplay — unclear how assumptions (e.g., ‖∇τ‖∞ ≤ 1/2) hold in real settings.  
- Recommendation: Add synthetic experiments to validate the stability theorem, compare smooth activations vs ReLU, and illustrate the invariance vs signal-preservation trade-off.  
- Question: Discuss whether common kernel approximations (random Fourier features, Nyström) preserve stability guarantees and how their approximation errors propagate through layers.  
- Question: Discuss practical implications and empirical performance of the proposed smooth homogeneous activations compared to ReLU.  
- Conclusion: Strong theoretical contribution recommended for acceptance, with a strong suggestion to include experiments in the camera-ready version.
