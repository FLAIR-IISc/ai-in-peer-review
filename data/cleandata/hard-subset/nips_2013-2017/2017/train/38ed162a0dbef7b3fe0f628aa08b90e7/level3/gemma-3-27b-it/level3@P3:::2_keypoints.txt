- Strong theoretical foundation: rigorous mathematical analysis of stability with comprehensive, well-structured proofs.
- Novel extension: extends stability analysis from scattering transforms to CKNs and broader CNN architectures.
- Clear writing and organization: paper is well-written, logically organized, and notation is mostly consistent.
- High relevance: stability results relate to robustness and generalization, aligning with current research trends.
- Connection to practice: demonstrates how kernel-based theory applies to practical CNN architectures.

- Motivation underdeveloped: the link between Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness is mentioned but not sufficiently justified or exemplified.
- Notation overload/confusion: reuse of κ for both a kernel parameter and the kernel function is misleading.
- Redundant constraint: a displayed constraint (lines 384–385) is unnecessary due to kernel symmetry and should be removed.
- Missing definition: φ appears at line 416 without prior definition as the kernel feature map (Eq. 10).
- Minor technical errors: an inequality around lines 427–428 seems to be equality and “||z|| - ||z||” should read “||z|| - ||z'||”.
- Incomplete bibliography: many references lack page information and several need updated publication/acceptance details.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound contribution; significant extension to CKNs/CNNs; clear presentation; high relevance to robustness/generalization.
- Arguments against acceptance: motivation needs strengthening; minor technical and notation fixes required; bibliography must be completed and updated.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable theoretical results; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing motivation, notation, technical inaccuracies, and bibliography updates.
