- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on CKNs and their connection to CNNs.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of conditions under which CKNs are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel for CKNs and analyzes the geometry of the corresponding RKHS.  
- Proves signal representation stability and that many models in this RKHS (including a broad class of CNNs) can inherit stability.  
- Shows the RKHS contains CNNs on continuous domains with smooth homogeneous activations, bridging CKNs and CNNs.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and contains thorough, clearly explained proofs.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical claims about invariance and diffeomorphism stability.  
- Open question: does kernel approximation (random projections or finite Fourier features) preserve stability, and what are the implications of finite-dimensional layer representations?  
- Builds on prior work (Mallat et al.) but extends results to more general CNN architectures and the kernel framework; degree of novelty partly unclear.  
- Significance: provides a theoretical framework with practical implications for designing networks robust to transformations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should add empirical validation and explore kernel-approximation effects on stability.  
- Reviewer score: 7/10.
