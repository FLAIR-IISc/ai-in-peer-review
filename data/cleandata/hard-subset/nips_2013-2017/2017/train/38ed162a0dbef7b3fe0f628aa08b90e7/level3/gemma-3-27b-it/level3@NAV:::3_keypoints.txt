- Presents a compelling RKHS reformulation of DCNNs, showing multilayer kernels with approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Core contribution is the RKHS casting of CNN functional spaces; paper is technically sound with clear proofs.  
- Needs a clearer explanation of practical value: how the RKHS view yields better regularization, generalization bounds, or computational/approximation advantages.  
- Novelty relative to prior CKN work is underexplained; clarify specific advantages and cases where CKN approximations fail.  
- Questions the emphasis on “not losing signal information”: preserving full signal may be unnecessary or harmful if task-relevant features suffice.  
- Suggests exploring connections to earlier invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) to provide historical context.  
- Raises the theoretical question about operator commutativity/equivariance: characterize operators that commute with all group elements to guarantee DCNN equivariance.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and arguments for acceptance, but revisions needed to clarify practical impact, novelty, and connections to prior work.
