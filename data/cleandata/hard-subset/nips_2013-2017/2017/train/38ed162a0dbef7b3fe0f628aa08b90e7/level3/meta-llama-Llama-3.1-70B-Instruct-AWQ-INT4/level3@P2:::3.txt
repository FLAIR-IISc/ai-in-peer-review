This paper proposes a theoretical framework for understanding the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) by reformulating them in the context of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors show that the functional space associated with a multilayer convolutional kernel contains a large class of DCNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions, allowing them to obtain theoretical results for both classes of models.

The main contribution of this paper is the reformulation of DCNNs in the RKHS context, which provides a new perspective on the invariance and stability properties of these models. The authors demonstrate that this reformulation can be used to study the geometry of the functional space associated with DCNNs, and to derive bounds on the stability of these models to deformations and translations.

The paper is well-written and rigorous, and the authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their framework and results. The reviewer's main question is why this reformulation is useful, and what implications it has for generalization bounds or computational/approximation advantages. The authors should clarify whether the novelty of their work goes beyond a pure RKHS reformulation of DCNNs, and how their work relates to previous work on convolutional kernel networks (Mairal et al.).

The reviewer also questions the emphasis on "not losing the signal information" and asks why preserving full signal information is necessary when many tasks only require task-relevant information. Additionally, the reviewer is curious about whether there is a connection to invariant-kernel approaches such as Hans Burkhardt's work, and whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would ensure DCNN equivariance.

Overall, the reviewer recommends accepting the paper, but suggests that the authors address these questions and provide more context and motivation for their work.
