This paper investigates the stability of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), to C¹ diffeomorphisms. The authors prove stability under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes, extending previous work on scattering transforms to a broader class of architectures. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, effectively combining concepts from deep learning and kernel methods. The stability result is a valuable contribution to the machine learning community, potentially informing regularization strategies and improving sample complexity.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis of stability, building upon established results in harmonic analysis and kernel methods. The proofs appear comprehensive and well-structured (though detailed inspection requires reviewing the appendix).
*   **Novel Extension of Existing Work:** Extending the stability analysis from scattering transforms to CKNs and potentially broader CNN architectures is a significant step forward. This bridges the gap between theoretically well-understood models like scattering transforms and more widely used, learned architectures.
*   **Clear and Concise Writing:** The paper is remarkably well-written and easy to follow, given the technical complexity of the topic. The organization is logical, and the notation is mostly consistent.
*   **Relevance to Current Research:** The study of stability is directly related to robustness and generalization in deep learning, making this work highly relevant to current research trends, particularly in adversarial robustness.
*   **Connection to CNNs:** The authors demonstrate a clear link between the theoretical kernel framework and practical CNN architectures, showing that the established stability results can be applied to a wider range of models.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation:** While the paper mentions the connection between Lipschitz stability and adversarial robustness, the motivation for pursuing this specific line of inquiry feels somewhat underdeveloped. Expanding on this connection and providing more concrete examples of how stability properties could be leveraged for improved robustness would strengthen the paper. A single sentence feels insufficient to justify the depth of the theoretical work.
*   **Notation Overload:** The reuse of the symbol κ for both a kernel parameter (A3) and the kernel function itself (Eq. 10) is confusing. This could easily be resolved by using distinct symbols.
*   **Redundant Constraint:** The second constraint in the displayed equation between lines 384-385 is redundant due to the inherent symmetry of the kernel function. Removing this constraint would simplify the presentation.
*   **Missing Definition:** The symbol φ is used at line 416 without prior definition. It should be explicitly introduced as the feature map for the kernel K, referencing Eq. (10).
*   **Minor Technical Issues:** The inequality around lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality, suggesting a potential error. Additionally, “||z|| - ||z||” should be “||z|| - ||z'||”.
*   **Bibliography Completeness:** Several references are missing page information ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]). Furthermore, updated publication/acceptance details are needed for references [9],[17],[19],[32].

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound theoretical contribution to understanding the stability of deep convolutional representations.
*   The extension of stability analysis to CKNs and broader CNN architectures is significant.
*   The clarity of the writing and organization make the complex material accessible.
*   The topic is highly relevant to current research in robustness and generalization.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The motivation could be strengthened.
*   Minor technical issues and notation inconsistencies need addressing.
*   The bibliography requires completion and updating.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The theoretical results are significant, the writing is clear, and the connection to practical CNN architectures is well-established. I recommend **acceptance** contingent upon the authors addressing the issues related to motivation, notation, technical inaccuracies, and the bibliography. These are relatively minor revisions that would significantly enhance the quality and impact of the paper.
