The paper "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations" studies the theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their extension to more general convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The authors provide a framework for analyzing the stability and invariance of these models to diffeomorphisms, which is crucial for understanding their performance on tasks involving deformable objects.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound. The authors combine deep networks and kernel methods, providing a unique perspective on the field. The stability result is of interest to the machine learning community, as it sheds light on the links between generalization and stability.

One of the main contributions of the paper is the extension of the stability analysis from the Euclidean space to locally compact groups. This is a significant generalization, as it allows the authors to study the invariance and stability of models under more complex transformations.

The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the connection between the kernel representation and CNNs. They show that the RKHS obtained from their kernel construction contains a set of CNNs on continuous domains with certain types of smooth homogeneous activations. This result implies that the stability and invariance properties of the kernel representation apply to this class of CNNs.

The paper has several strengths, including its technical soundness, clarity of writing, and originality. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the subject matter, and their results have significant implications for the field of machine learning.

However, there are some minor issues with the paper. The notation is sometimes overloaded, which can cause confusion. For example, the symbol κ is used to denote both the kernel function and a constant in Assumption (A3). Additionally, some of the references are missing page information, and a few of the citations are not up-to-date.
