- Main contribution: Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel (generalizing CKNs) and proves stability of its representations to C^1 diffeomorphisms under norm-preservation and non-expansiveness assumptions.
- Stability specifics: Theorem 6 shows deformation stability controlled by the diffeomorphism Jacobian and near-translation invariance controlled by final pooling scale, analogous to scattering network guarantees.
- Extensions: Construction extends to equivariance with respect to general locally compact groups.
- Link to CNNs: Shows the RKHS contains a class of standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, allowing stability guarantees to apply to these CNNs.
- Technical soundness: High-quality, carefully constructed mathematical framework building on established kernel and signal-processing work; proofs and assumptions (A1–A3) are clearly stated and appear sound.
- Completeness: Theoretical contribution is comprehensive, from kernel construction and discretization to stability analysis and CNN implications.
- Clarity and organization: Manuscript is well-written and guides the reader through complex material; Section 5's connection to CNNs is a highlight.
- Motivation concern: Introduction’s motivation is weak; the link to adversarial robustness and modern deep-learning relevance should be strengthened.
- Notation issue: Overloading of the symbol κ (both a constant in A3 and the kernel function) is confusing and should be resolved.
- Kernel mapping notation: The definition and later use of φ (phi) are inconsistent or unclear in relation to K_n and Equation (2).
- Minor corrections: Appendix B contains a redundant constraint and a likely typo in an inequality and a variable mismatch (||z|| - ||z|| should be ||z|| - ||z'||).
- Originality: Novel adaptation of scattering stability techniques to the CKN framework and the new connection to CNNs is original and significant.
- Significance 1: Provides principled theoretical understanding of invariance and stability for a broad class of deep convolutional models.
- Significance 2: Embedding CNNs in an RKHS links stability to the RKHS norm, connecting smoothness, generalization, and robustness.
- Significance 3: Analysis could inspire new regularizers or architectures with provable stability guarantees.
- Strength — theoretical contribution: Rigorous proof of diffeomorphism stability for CKNs and certain CNNs.
- Strength — bridging fields: Effectively bridges kernel methods and deep learning theory.
- Strength — presentation: Clear exposition and logical structure make complex proofs accessible.
- Strength — impact: Results are important for researchers on theoretical foundations, regularization, and robustness.
- Weakness — motivation: Practical importance of diffeomorphism stability needs clearer articulation and links to current DL challenges.
- Weakness — minor clarity: Notational confusions and small typos should be fixed.
- Weakness — bibliography: Several references lack full publication details and page numbers and should be completed.
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-suited paper for NIPS; weaknesses are minor and addressable in revision.
