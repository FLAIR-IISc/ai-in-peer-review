- Paper proves deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) are nearly invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms under specific patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling designs.
- Results are extended to a broader class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) via connections through homogeneous activations.
- Technically sound, mathematically rigorous, and well-written with clear notation and development.
- Effectively connects CKN advances with scattering transform theory, providing a more general perspective on invariance and stability.
- Appropriately cites relevant literature on group-equivariant CNNs and kernelized deep learning.
- Key theoretical contribution: derivation of stability to diffeomorphic deformations (Theorem 6).
- Insightful discussion linking RKHS norms to generalization and sample complexity.
- Major weakness: complete lack of empirical validation to confirm practical relevance of theoretical claims.
- Theoretical assumptions (e.g., activation smoothness, specific kernels) may not hold in real-world settings, necessitating experiments.
- Open question: impact of kernel approximations (random projections or Fourier features) on stability and practical implementation.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novel synthesis of ideas, potential impact on understanding generalization, and clarity of presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of experiments and limited practical guidance for leveraging insights in CNN design.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on adding experiments demonstrating invariance/stability (even simplified) and addressing kernel-approximation concerns.
