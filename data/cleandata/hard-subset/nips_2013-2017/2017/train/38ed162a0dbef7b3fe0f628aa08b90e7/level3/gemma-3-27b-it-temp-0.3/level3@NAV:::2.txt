## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

This paper investigates the stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C¹ diffeomorphisms, proving stability under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes. The work extends stability analysis from Euclidean space to locally compact groups and provides a solid theoretical foundation for understanding the robustness of these models. The manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, successfully combining deep networks and kernel methods. The stability result is a valuable contribution to the ML community, potentially offering insights into generalization and adversarial robustness.

The paper builds upon prior work in scattering transforms [17] and CKNs [15, 16], extending the analysis to a broader class of CNNs. The authors effectively leverage concepts from positive definite kernels and reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces to establish their results. The connection drawn between stability and potential improvements in sample complexity is particularly interesting. The extension to locally compact groups, while sketched, hints at a promising direction for future work.

However, the motivation for focusing on stability beyond its link to adversarial robustness feels somewhat underdeveloped. While the connection is mentioned, a more detailed discussion of the broader implications of stability – for example, in the context of out-of-distribution generalization or robustness to sensor noise – would strengthen the paper. 

There are a few minor technical points to address. The notation uses ‘κ’ both for a parameter in the kernel definition (A3) and as the kernel function itself (Eq. 10), which could cause confusion. The second constraint in the equation between lines 384-385 appears redundant due to kernel symmetry. Additionally, the variable ‘ϕ’ is used at line 416 without prior definition; it should be introduced via Eq. (10) as the feature map for K. Finally, the inequality around lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality, and “||z|| - ||z||” should likely be “||z|| - ||z'||”.

The bibliography also requires attention. Page numbers are missing for references [3, 6, 8, 13, 14, 18, 25-27, 30, 31]. Several references ([9], [17], [19], [32]) need updated publication/acceptance details.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical results on stability of CKNs and related CNNs.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of complex concepts.
*   Novel combination of kernel methods and deep learning.
*   Potential for significant impact on understanding generalization and robustness.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Motivation for stability analysis beyond adversarial robustness could be stronger.
*   Minor notational and typographical issues.
*   Incomplete bibliography.



**Overall:** Despite these minor issues, I recommend acceptance. The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution with the potential to influence future research in deep learning and kernel methods. Addressing the noted issues would further strengthen the manuscript.
