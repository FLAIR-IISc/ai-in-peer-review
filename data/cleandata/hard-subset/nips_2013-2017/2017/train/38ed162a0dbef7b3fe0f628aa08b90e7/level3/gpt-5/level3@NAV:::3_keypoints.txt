- Paper develops a multilayer convolutional kernel whose RKHS contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations and proves near-invariance and Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Construction generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) from discrete to continuous domains and provides a principled discretization/approximation path.  
- Extends scattering-transform stability arguments to more general kernels and CNNs, and links to group-equivariant architectures and invariant kernels.  
- Technical quality is high: clear assumptions (A1–A3), careful proofs using harmonic analysis (commutator bounds, Schur’s test), consistent with scattering literature.  
- Proposition embedding CNNs into an RKHS is well motivated and ties norm control to stability and potential generalization.  
- Exposition is clean and self-contained with a useful continuous-to-discrete bridge.  
- Reviewer requests clearer summary of assumptions and stability constants, and explicit highlighting of which analysis parts go beyond scattering/CKNs.  
- Novelty: rigorous RKHS reformulation is valuable, but authors should clarify what new stability/invariance results go beyond prior CKN, scattering, or group-equivariant CNN work.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of invariance/stability in conv architectures and suggests norm-based generalization avenues; likely influential for theorists and designers.  
- Clarification requested: can the RKHS reformulation yield concrete generalization bounds (e.g., margin/Rademacher via ∥f∥H) or computational/approximation advantages over standard CNNs?  
- Clarification requested: what new guarantees are obtained relative to Mairal et al. (2014, 2016), especially under kernel approximation and finite-dimensional projections?  
- Clarification requested: can the group pooling be interpreted as constructing invariant kernels, and how do the stability constants compare to prior invariant-kernel/orbit-embedding work?  
- Clarification requested: why preserve full signal information if many tasks require only task-relevant sufficiency—discuss trade-offs with sufficiency and compression.  
- Clarification requested: beyond convolution/pooling, can the authors characterize all bounded linear intertwiners that commute with the group action, and does commuting with all group elements suffice for DCNN equivariance here?  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous, well-written theory; meaningful unification of kernels and CNNs; clear stability and invariance results with discretization guidance; RKHS link suggests norm–generalization connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: novelty relative to CKNs and invariant kernels needs sharpening and practical implications/empirical validation are limited.  
- Overall recommendation from reviewer: Accept.
