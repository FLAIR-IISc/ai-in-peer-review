## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

This paper investigates the stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C¹ diffeomorphisms, proving stability under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes. The work extends the stability analysis previously established for scattering transforms to a broader class of models, including CNNs, through a kernel framework. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound, combining concepts from deep learning and kernel methods – a valuable intersection for the ML community, and the stability result itself is a significant contribution. 

The core strength lies in the rigorous mathematical analysis. The authors effectively leverage kernel theory to provide a theoretical underpinning for the observed robustness of CNNs. The extension to locally compact groups in Section 4, though sketched, points toward potentially impactful future work. The detailed analysis of the discrete approximations and the connection to CKNs are also well-executed.

However, the motivation for this deep dive into stability, beyond its link to adversarial robustness, feels underdeveloped. While mentioned, the broader implications of stability – perhaps relating to generalization, sample complexity, or interpretability – could be more strongly emphasized. Expanding on *why* this stability is important beyond the adversarial context would elevate the work. 

There are a few minor technical points. The notation uses ‘κ’ both for patch size constraints (A3) and as the kernel function in Eq. (10), which is confusing and should be rectified. The second constraint in the equation between lines 384-385 appears redundant given kernel symmetry and should be checked.  Also, the variable ‘ϕ’ is used at line 416 without prior definition; it should be explicitly introduced referencing Eq. (10) as the feature map for K.  Finally, the inequality around lines 427-428 appears to hold with equality, and “||z|| - ||z||” should likely be “||z|| - ||z'||”. 

The bibliography also requires attention. Page numbers are missing for references [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31], and publication/acceptance details are needed for [9],[17],[19],[32]. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous mathematical analysis.
*   Novel application of kernel methods to analyze CNN stability.
*   Clear writing and organization.
*   Potentially significant implications for understanding CNN robustness and generalization.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Motivation beyond adversarial robustness could be stronger.
*   Minor notational and technical issues need addressing.
*   Bibliography requires completion.



**Overall:** This is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing the minor issues outlined above would further strengthen the work and solidify its impact. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
