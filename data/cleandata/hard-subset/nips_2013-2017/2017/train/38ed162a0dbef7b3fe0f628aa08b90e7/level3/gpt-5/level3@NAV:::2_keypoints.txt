- Develops a multilayer convolutional kernel framework and proves Lipschitz stability to C^1 diffeomorphisms under A1–A3, with bound C1||∇τ||∞ + C2||τ||∞/σn showing last-layer pooling controls translation invariance while intermediate layers ensure deformation stability.  
- Sketches extension from (R^d,+) to general locally compact groups via equivariant patch-extraction and pooling; group sketch sensible but less developed than Euclidean case.  
- Shows discretized/projected CKNs preserve non-expansiveness, so stability results transfer to discretized networks.  
- Embeds a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS induced by the multilayer kernel, yielding stability of predictions via RKHS norm control.  
- Positions the work relative to scattering networks, CKNs, group-equivariant CNNs, and robustness literature (e.g., Parseval networks).  
- Technical analysis is sound and carefully executed, featuring a clean commutator decomposition and operator-norm bounds.  
- Manuscript is well organized and readable but contains notational and editorial issues to fix.  
- Originality: extends scattering-style stability guarantees to a broader kernel class that includes CKNs and a subset of CNNs; the RKHS perspective and group sketch add novelty.  
- Significance: stability of learned convolutional representations is broadly relevant and should inform theory and practical choices (architecture, regularization).  
- Requested improvement: strengthen motivation beyond a single sentence on adversarial robustness and clarify practical implications (sample complexity, architectural design, depth vs. σn trade-offs, and how to choose κ, σk).  
- Notation issue: κ is overloaded (A3 vs. kernel κk in Eq. (10)); rename one to avoid confusion.  
- Math edits requested: remove redundant constraint between lines 384–385, define φ at line 416 as feature map of K, fix equality/inequality around lines 427–428 and correct ||z|| − ||z|| to ||z|| − ||z′||.  
- Bibliography fixes: add page ranges for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31] and update publication/acceptance details for [9],[17],[19],[32].  
- Pros for acceptance: clear, rigorous theory with meaningful takeaways; bridges kernels and CNNs; timely and well written.  
- Cons for acceptance: motivation could be deeper; group extension only sketched; numerous minor editorial issues; limited empirical illustration of design choices.
