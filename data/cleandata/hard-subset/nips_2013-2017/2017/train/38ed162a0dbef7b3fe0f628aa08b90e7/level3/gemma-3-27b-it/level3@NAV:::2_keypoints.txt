- Proves stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to C¹ diffeomorphisms for norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels with appropriate patch sizes.  
- Extends stability analysis from scattering transforms to a broader class of models, including CNNs, via a kernel framework.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound with rigorous mathematical analysis.  
- Effectively leverages kernel theory to provide theoretical underpinning for observed CNN robustness; stability result is a significant contribution.  
- Extension to locally compact groups (Section 4) is promising but only sketched.  
- Detailed analysis of discrete approximations and the connection to CKNs is well-executed.  
- Motivation beyond adversarial robustness is underdeveloped; broader implications (e.g., generalization, sample complexity, interpretability) should be emphasized.  
- Notational conflict: ‘κ’ is used both for patch size constraints (A3) and as the kernel function in Eq. (10), causing confusion.  
- The second constraint between lines 384–385 appears redundant given kernel symmetry and should be checked.  
- Variable ‘ϕ’ is used at line 416 without prior definition; it should be explicitly introduced referencing Eq. (10) as the feature map for K.  
- Inequality around lines 427–428 seems to hold with equality, and “||z|| - ||z||” is likely a typo for “||z|| - ||z'||”.  
- Bibliography needs completion: missing page numbers for refs [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31] and publication/acceptance details for [9],[17],[19],[32].  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novel kernel-based analysis of CNN stability, clear writing, and potential impact on understanding robustness/generalization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation beyond adversarial robustness, minor notational/technical issues, and incomplete bibliography.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the noted issues.
