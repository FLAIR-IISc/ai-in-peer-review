- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and extensions to CNNs, focusing on stability and invariance to diffeomorphisms.  
- Provides a framework for analyzing stability and invariance, important for deformable-object tasks.  
- Technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written.  
- Combines deep networks and kernel methods, offering a unique perspective.  
- Stability results link generalization and stability and are of interest to the ML community.  
- Extends stability analysis from Euclidean space to locally compact groups, a significant generalization.  
- Shows the RKHS from their kernel contains CNNs on continuous domains with smooth homogeneous activations, so results apply to that CNN class.  
- Strengths: technical rigor, clarity, originality, and thorough analysis with significant implications.  
- Minor issues: overloaded notation (e.g., κ used for multiple meanings).  
- Minor issues: some references lack page info and a few citations are not up-to-date.  
- Suggests stronger motivation needed for stability analysis beyond a single-line link to adversarial robustness.
