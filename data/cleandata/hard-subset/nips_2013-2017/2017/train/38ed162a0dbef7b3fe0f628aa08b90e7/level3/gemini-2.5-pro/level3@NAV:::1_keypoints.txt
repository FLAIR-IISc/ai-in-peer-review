- Paper presents a theoretical framework showing deep CKNs can achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms via designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Builds on prior work (CKNs, Mallat scattering) and extends stability/invariance analysis.  
- Key contribution: extends theoretical guarantees from the CKN RKHS to a class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations.  
- Strength: rigorous, high-quality theoretical analysis that gives geometric insights into deep representations.  
- Strength: establishes a significant connection between kernel frameworks and standard CNNs, explaining robustness to small input variations.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Major weakness: complete lack of empirical validation; theoretical claims are untested on real or synthetic data.  
- Suggested improvement: add experiments demonstrating CKN/CNN stability under controlled diffeomorphisms or invariance to transformations, measuring feature-space differences.  
- Suggested investigation: study how practical kernel approximations (random projections, finite Fourier features) affect the derived stability bounds.
