- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and links to CNNs.  
- Generalizes a multilayer kernel construction to achieve near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for CKNs and a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions.  
- Well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound presentation.  

Strengths:
- Strong theoretical foundation using kernel theory and harmonic analysis with sound proofs.  
- Results generalize beyond CKNs to a wider class of CNN architectures, increasing practical relevance.  
- Clear exposition, consistent notation overall, and sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Novel perspective bridging kernel methods and CNNs, offering insights into geometric properties of deep models.  
- Addresses an important topic—robustness and generalization in deep learning.  

Weaknesses:
- Motivation for focusing on stability (e.g., practical/adversarial implications) is thin and could be strengthened.  
- Notation overload: symbol κ is used ambiguously in multiple places.  
- Minor mathematical redundancy: a displayed constraint appears redundant due to kernel symmetry.  
- Missing definitions/typos: feature map ϕ not introduced before use; an inequality seems to be equality; a norm subtraction typo noted.  
- Bibliography incomplete or missing details (page numbers and updated publication/acceptance info for several refs).  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Technically rigorous and novel contribution with clear connections to existing literature.  
- Extension of stability analysis to broader CNNs enhances impact and relevance.  
- Paper is well-written and likely to influence understanding of generalization and robustness.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Motivation for stability needs elaboration.  
- Minor notation issues, typos, and incomplete bibliography (though easily fixable).  

Overall assessment:
- Strong contribution; recommend acceptance to NIPS 2017, with minor fixes to motivation, notation, and references.
