- Introduces hierarchical implicit models (HIMs) and likelihood-free variational inference (LFVI) as a scalable framework for hierarchical, likelihood-free Bayesian modeling.  
- Demonstrates the approach on several applications: large-scale predator–prey simulator, Bayesian GAN for supervised classification, and deep implicit model for symbol generation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides strong motivation and thorough related work.  
- Technical sections are detailed with clear explanations of key concepts and algorithms.  
- Strength: successfully merges implicit densities with hierarchical Bayesian modeling and approximate posterior inference.  
- Strength: expands Bayesian analysis to neural samplers, physical simulators, and interpretable latent structure.  
- Experiments are clearly presented and show effectiveness on the chosen applications.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited — experiments cover only a few applications and do not fully validate theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: discussion of potential limitations and challenges (e.g., stability of the ratio estimator) is brief and could be more detailed.  
- Weakness: could include more extensive comparisons to existing methods and deeper analysis of results.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel model class and scalable inference algorithm with demonstrated real-world applications.  
- Con-acceptance: insufficient extensive empirical validation and limited discussion/comparison of limitations and alternatives.
