- Paper presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations via multilayer convolutional kernels and RKHS, linking CKNs to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
- Shows the constructed deep representation is stable to small diffeomorphisms and nearly translation-invariant, extending scattering-transform analysis to a broader class.
- Generalizes the framework to achieve equivariance and invariance to arbitrary compact group actions.
- Establishes a formal connection between the kernel construction and standard CNNs, allowing transfer of stability/invariance results to those networks and relating stability to the RKHS norm.
- Strength: technically rigorous with detailed proofs grounded in harmonic analysis.
- Strength: exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition that makes complex theory accessible.
- Strength: significant theoretical contribution linking RKHS norm, stability to deformations, and generalization.
- Weakness: needs clearer articulation of practical/theoretical benefits of the RKHS reformulation (e.g., new regularizers, architecture insights, tighter generalization bounds).
- Weakness: novelty vs prior CKN work should be more sharply delineated—clarify what is new in kernel construction vs stability/invariance analysis.
- Weakness: relation to prior invariant-kernel methods (fixed invariant kernels) should be contrasted more explicitly with the proposed multilayer learned-feature approach.
- Weakness: motivation for preserving full signal information (Lemmas 1–2) is unclear for discriminative tasks that often require discarding nuisances.
- Pro-accept: provides a rigorous, elegant theoretical framework for CNN stability and invariance with clear writing.
- Con-accept: practical implications and novel insights from the RKHS perspective are not fully explicit; prior-art distinctions need sharpening.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to framing, motivation, and comparisons to prior work.
