- Main contribution: The paper studies stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) under C^1 diffeomorphisms, showing they can be stable while preserving signal information.  
- Methodology: Establishes stability for CKNs built with norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels and appropriate patch sizes, drawing parallels to the scattering transform.  
- Generalization: Extends the stability analysis from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups, broadening invariance and robustness perspectives.  
- Impact: Bridges kernel methods and deep learning by relating CKNs, RKHS geometry, and convolutional architectures.  
- Strength — theoretical: Significant theoretical advance connecting CKN stability to CNNs and group invariance.  
- Strength — presentation: Manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, making complex math accessible.  
- Strength — relevance: Results are relevant for robustness (including adversarial settings) and of interest to theoretical and applied researchers.  
- Strength — novelty: Novel extension of stability analysis to locally compact groups.  
- Weakness — motivation: Insufficient practical motivation and discussion of implications for real-world robustness.  
- Weakness — notation: Overloading of κ in (A3) conflicts with κ used for kernel K in Eq. (10), causing confusion.  
- Weakness — redundancy: A displayed equation (lines 384–385) is redundant due to kernel symmetry and should be simplified.  
- Weakness — missing definition: The function φ is not defined in Eq. (10), hindering comprehension.  
- Weakness — technical errors: Inequality in lines 427–428 seems to be an equality and a term should read ||z||−||z'|| instead of ||z||−||z||.  
- Weakness — references: Multiple references lack page information and several citations ([9],[17],[19],[32]) need updates/corrections.  
- Acceptance arguments: Rigorous theoretical framework, novel group-extension results, and clear exposition justify publication.  
- Rejection concerns: Weak practical motivation, technical errors, missing definitions, and reference issues reduce clarity and impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix motivation, notation, technical mistakes, missing definitions, and citation errors.
