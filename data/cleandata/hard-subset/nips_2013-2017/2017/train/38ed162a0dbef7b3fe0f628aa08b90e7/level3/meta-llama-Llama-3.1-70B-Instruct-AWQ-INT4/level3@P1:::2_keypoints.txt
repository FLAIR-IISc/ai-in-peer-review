- The paper studies stability properties of deep convolutional representations obtained via convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- It is well-organized and clearly written, with understandable mathematical derivations.  
- The work is technically sound and demonstrates solid understanding of RKHS and diffeomorphisms.  
- Main contribution: provable stability of CKNs to C^1 diffeomorphisms under assumptions (A1–A3), with explicit bounds depending on diffeomorphism norm and pooling size.  
- Extension sketch from (R^d,+) to locally compact groups is provided and is an interesting direction.  
- Motivation is weak: the link to adversarial robustness is mentioned only briefly and not fully developed.  
- Notation issues: symbol κ is overloaded (A3 vs Eq. (10)), which may confuse readers.  
- Minor errors/typos: redundant constraint between lines 384–385, an inequality that seems to hold with equality around 427–428, and a typo ||z|| - ||z|| should be ||z|| - ||z'||.  
- Bibliography needs improvements: add page information for many refs and update publication/acceptance details for several others.  
- Overall assessment: a solid, original contribution with potential to advance understanding of CKN stability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel analysis, technically rigorous, and potentially impactful for robustness understanding.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficiently motivated application context, notation/clarity issues, and bibliography/typo problems needing fixes.
