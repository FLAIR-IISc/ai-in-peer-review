**Paper Review: Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations**

**Summary**

This paper studies the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). The authors propose a multilayer kernel construction that generalizes CKNs and provides a theoretical framework for analyzing the stability of these representations under diffeomorphisms. They show that the representation is stable and that models from this functional space, such as a large class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs), may enjoy the same stability.

**Strengths**

1. **Theoretical contributions**: The paper provides a rigorous theoretical framework for analyzing the stability of deep convolutional representations, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Generalization of CKNs**: The authors propose a multilayer kernel construction that generalizes CKNs, making it a more comprehensive and flexible framework.
3. **Link to CNNs**: The paper establishes a connection between the kernel representation and CNNs, showing that the stability results apply to a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
4. **Well-organized and clearly written**: The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical framework and results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Motivation for stability analysis**: The paper could benefit from a more detailed motivation for the stability analysis, beyond the single sentence linking Lipschitz stability to adversarial robustness.
2. **Notation issues**: There are some notation issues, such as the overloading of the symbol \kappa, which may cause confusion for readers.
3. **Mathematical simplifications**: Some mathematical simplifications, such as the redundancy of the second constraint in the displayed equation, could be pointed out to improve the clarity of the paper.
4. **Bibliography issues**: There are some issues with the bibliography, including missing page information and outdated publication details.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel combination of deep networks and kernel methods, providing a new perspective on the stability of deep convolutional representations. The authors' approach to generalizing CKNs and linking the kernel representation to CNNs is original and contributes to the advancement of the field.

**Significance**

The paper's results have significant implications for the understanding of deep convolutional representations and their stability properties. The authors' work provides a theoretical framework for analyzing the stability of these representations, which can be applied to various applications, including image classification and object recognition.

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend accepting it for publication, pending minor revisions to address the notation issues, mathematical simplifications, and bibliography issues. The paper's originality, significance, and technical soundness make it a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning.
