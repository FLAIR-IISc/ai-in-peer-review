- Paper studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations, focusing on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and links to CNNs.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernel construction and analyzes the geometry of the associated RKHS.  
- Main theoretical result: stability to diffeomorphisms and near-invariance to transformation groups, extending scattering transform properties to broader CNN classes.  
- Work is mathematically rigorous, well-organized, and accessible.  
- Situates contributions within prior work on CNNs, scattering transforms, kernel methods, and group-invariant representations.  
- Strength: Technical soundness with clear definitions and proofs grounded in kernel theory and harmonic analysis.  
- Strength: Clear writing and logical progression from kernel construction to stability analysis and CNN connections.  
- Strength: Original perspective using RKHS geometry to analyze CNNs; significant extension beyond scattering transforms.  
- Strength: Results are significant for robustness, generalization, sample complexity, and potential adversarial robustness implications.  
- Weakness: Motivation for focusing on stability and its broader applications could be strengthened.  
- Weakness: Notational issues—κ is overloaded (assumption A3 and kernel definition).  
- Weakness: Mathematical simplification: a constraint appears redundant due to kernel symmetry.  
- Weakness: Missing definition of φ at line 416; should reference feature map from Eq. (10).  
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos (inequality around lines 427–428; ||z|| - ||z|| should be ||z|| - ||z'||).  
- Weakness: Bibliography missing page info and some publication/acceptance details need updating.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, extends theory to broader CNNs, well-written, and potentially impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: motivation could be stronger and several minor notational/typo/bibliography fixes are needed.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution worth acceptance pending minor clarifications and corrections; reviewer confidence 8/10.
