- Proposes an RKHS-based framework generalizing convolutional kernel networks to analyze CNN invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows the kernel space contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, enabling theoretical stability guarantees akin to the scattering transform.  
- Proves the representation preserves signal information and is stable to diffeomorphisms, with bounds depending on final-layer pooling scale.  
- Links RKHS norm to both stability and generalization, arguing stable representations can improve sample complexity.  
- Clear, rigorous mathematical exposition and thorough proofs; paper is well-organized from theory to discretization and CNN connections.  
- Distinguishes from prior work (CKNs and scattering), but novelty relative to Mairal's CKNs needs clearer clarification.  
- Practical implications for model design and training are not fully articulated; more guidance needed on how theory informs practice.  
- Questioned emphasis on preserving full signal information; authors should discuss trade-offs with task-relevant compression.  
- Utility of the RKHS reformulation needs stronger justification—clarify concrete advantages over direct CNN analysis (e.g., bounds or computational benefits).  
- Connection to invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Burkhardt) should be strengthened and contextualized.  
- Characterization of operators ensuring equivariance to group actions requires more complete elaboration.  
- Main strengths: rigorous theoretical contribution, clear link between kernel theory and CNNs, meaningful stability-generalization implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, rigorous proofs, valuable bridge between scattering/CKN and practical CNNs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical utility of RKHS reformulation, insufficiently differentiated from CKNs, questionable emphasis on full information preservation, weaker ties to invariant-kernel work.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify RKHS utility for practice and generalization; explicitly differentiate from CKNs; discuss signal-preservation trade-offs; strengthen connection to invariant-kernel literature.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions — high-quality theoretical contribution that would benefit from the suggested clarifications.
