- Paper presents a theoretical analysis of stability and invariance of deep convolutional representations using convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and C^1 diffeomorphism stability.  
- Main theoretical result: stability holds for CKNs with norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels (A1–A2) and constrained patch sizes (A3).  
- Connects reproducing kernel Hilbert space theory to modern deep learning and sketches generalization from translations to locally compact group actions.  
- Draws inspiration from scattering transform analysis to frame the stability proof.  
- Strength: rigorous, technically sound theoretical contribution grounding convolutional architectures in kernel theory.  
- Strength: manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, bridging kernel methods, signal processing, and deep learning.  
- Strength: links CKN framework to conventional CNNs with specific homogeneous activations, offering a unified theoretical perspective.  
- Strength: novel stability result of interest for geometric deep learning, invariance, and adversarial robustness research.  
- Weakness: motivation for studying diffeomorphism stability is underdeveloped; connection to adversarial robustness needs more elaboration.  
- Weakness: notational clarity issues—`\kappa` is overloaded and feature map `\phi` should be reintroduced for K_k.  
- Weakness: bibliography needs revision—missing page information for several references and updated final publication details for others.  
- Conclusion: high-quality paper whose strengths outweigh presentation issues; suitable for the conference pending minor revisions.
