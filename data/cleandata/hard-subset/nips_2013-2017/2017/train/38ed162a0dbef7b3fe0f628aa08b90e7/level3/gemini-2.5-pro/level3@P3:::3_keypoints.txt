- Paper frames deep convolutional representations in an RKHS, generalizes CKNs to multilayer convolutional kernels on continuous signals, and analyzes the induced functional space's geometry.  
- Main theoretical results: stability to small C1-diffeomorphisms with explicit bounds and approximate translation invariance that increases with depth.  
- Framework is extended to obtain near-invariance to more general transformation groups.  
- Shows the RKHS contains a large class of standard CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, transferring stability/invariance guarantees to these models.  
- Builds on and connects to scattering transform and prior CKN work for architecture and stability analysis.  
- Strength: technically rigorous, well-written, and uses harmonic analysis and kernel tools to formalize commonly stated properties of CNNs.  
- Strength: provides formal characterization of stability and invariance, offering theoretical justification for empirical CNN robustness.  
- Strength: unifying RKHS perspective links CNN capacity (RKHS norm) to robustness and provides a principled analysis framework.  
- Weakness: unclear practical utility of the RKHS reformulation—needs discussion of new insights, algorithmic advantages, or tighter generalization bounds enabled by this view.  
- Weakness: novelty vs. prior CKN papers is not fully delineated; authors should clarify which stability/invariance results are new beyond existing CKN work.  
- Weakness: representation “preserves all information” (Lemma 1) may conflict with desired task-specific selectivity; needs discussion of trade-offs between preservation and discriminative invariance.  
- Weakness: connection to broader invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Haasdonk & Burkhardt) is insufficient and should be better integrated.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the proposed construction of G-equivariant layers is exhaustive; request for characterization of all linear operators ensuring G-equivariance and relation to operators commuting with group actions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — high-quality theoretical contribution that deepens understanding of convolutional representations despite points needing clarification.
