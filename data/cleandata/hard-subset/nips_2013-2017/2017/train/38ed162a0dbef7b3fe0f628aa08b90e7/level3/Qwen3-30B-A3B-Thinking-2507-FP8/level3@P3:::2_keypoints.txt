- Main contribution: Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework proving C^1-diffeomorphism stability for convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) under norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels and appropriately sized patches.  
- Extension of prior work: Generalizes Mallat’s scattering transform to a broader class of (learnable) convolutional architectures and shows the functional space contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- RKHS connection: Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, linking stability guarantees to predictive performance and regularization.  
- Theoretical rigor: Paper is technically sound with clear, rigorous mathematical development and meaningful theoretical guarantees relevant to robustness.  
- Organization and clarity: Well-structured and clearly written overall, with accessible presentation of concepts.  
- Extension to groups: Sketches an extension to locally compact groups (Section 4), which is a valuable theoretical direction though not fully developed.  
- Practical relevance: Results are of significant interest to ML community and could guide design of more robust deep architectures.  
- Insufficient motivation: Motivation for studying stability is weak—connection to adversarial robustness is mentioned only briefly and needs expansion with concrete applications.  
- Notation conflict: κ is overloaded in (A3) and Eq. (10), creating potential confusion.  
- Redundant constraint: A displayed constraint (lines 384–385) is redundant due to kernel symmetry and should be removed or clarified.  
- Missing definition: Symbol φ is not defined at line 416 and should be introduced via Eq. (10) as the feature map for K.  
- Typographical/inequality error: Inequality around lines 427–428 seems to hold with equality and contains ||z|| - ||z|| instead of ||z|| - ||z'||.  
- Bibliography issues: Several references lack page information and publication details ([3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]; [9],[17],[19],[32] need updated details).  
- Originality: Novel kernel-based perspective linking CKNs and CNNs and extending stability theory beyond scattering transforms.  
- Significance: Offers theoretical insights into why CNNs perform well and potential implications for regularization and sample complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Provides rigorous stability guarantees, bridges kernel methods and deep learning, is well-written, and is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak motivation for stability, several correctable technical issues, some results only sketched, and incomplete bibliography.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor revisions to fix technical issues and strengthen motivation.
