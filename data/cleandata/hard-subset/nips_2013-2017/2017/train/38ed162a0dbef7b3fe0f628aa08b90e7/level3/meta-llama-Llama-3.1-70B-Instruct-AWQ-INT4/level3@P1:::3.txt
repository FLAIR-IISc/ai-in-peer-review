This paper, "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations," presents a rigorous and well-written analysis of the functional spaces associated with deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs). The authors demonstrate that these functional spaces can be described as multilayer kernels, which enables the study of their invariance and stability properties.

The main contribution of the paper is the reformulation of DCNNs in the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) context. This reformulation allows the authors to derive stability and invariance results for DCNNs, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of these models. However, the reviewer questions the usefulness of this reformulation, asking the authors to provide more insights into its implications for generalization bounds or computational/approximation advantages.

The paper builds upon the work of Mairal et al. on Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs), and the authors should clarify the novelty of their contribution relative to this previous work. The reviewer also asks whether the novelty goes beyond a pure RKHS reformulation of DCNNs.

The paper emphasizes the importance of preserving full signal information in the representation, but the reviewer questions this emphasis, pointing out that many tasks only require task-relevant information. The authors should address this point and provide more context on why preserving full signal information is necessary.

The reviewer also asks about the connection to invariant-kernel approaches, such as the work of Hans Burkhardt. This is an interesting question, as the authors' approach may have implications for these types of methods.

Finally, the reviewer asks about the characterization of operators that ensure DCNN equivariance, specifically whether any operator that commutes with all group elements would ensure equivariance. This is an important question, as it could provide a deeper understanding of the properties of DCNNs.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their results. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the functional spaces associated with DCNNs.

However, the reviewer's questions and comments highlight some areas where the paper could be improved. Addressing these points would strengthen the paper and provide more insights into the implications of the authors' results.

Recommendation: Accept, pending revisions that address the reviewer's comments and questions.

Strengths:

* Rigorous and well-written analysis of DCNN functional spaces
* Significant contribution to the understanding of DCNNs
* Clear and well-organized presentation of results

Weaknesses:

* Lack of clarity on the implications of the RKHS reformulation
* Unclear novelty relative to previous work on CKNs
* Emphasis on preserving full signal information may not be justified
* Limited discussion of connections to invariant-kernel approaches
* No characterization of operators that ensure DCNN equivariance

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a significant contribution to the understanding of DCNNs
* The analysis is rigorous and well-written
* The results have implications for the study of DCNNs and their applications

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks clarity on the implications of the RKHS reformulation
* The novelty of the contribution is unclear relative to previous work on CKNs
* The emphasis on preserving full signal information may not be justified

Overall, the paper is a strong contribution to the field, and with some revisions to address the reviewer's comments and questions, it has the potential to be an excellent paper.
