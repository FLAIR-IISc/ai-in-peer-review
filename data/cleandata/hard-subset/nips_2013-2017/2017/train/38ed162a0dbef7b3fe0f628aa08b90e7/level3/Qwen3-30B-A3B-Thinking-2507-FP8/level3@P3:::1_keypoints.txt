- Provides a rigorous theoretical framework for invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations.  
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to continuous domains for cleaner mathematical treatment.  
- Shows that properly designed patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling yield near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Proves these properties extend to standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, linking kernel methods and deep learning.  
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering transform while offering a more general kernel-based foundation.  
- Separates representation learning from predictive modeling to facilitate theoretical analysis.  
- Provides clear proofs, including a stability bound: ‖Φ(Lτx) − Φ(x)‖ ≤ (C1‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞)‖x‖ under assumptions.  
- Shows the RKHS norm of CNNs controls both stability and generalization, explaining aspects of CNN generalization.  
- Demonstrates how commuting patch extraction and pooling operators can achieve group invariance (rotations, reflections, etc.).  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, precise, and logically organized with effective lemmas and propositions.  
- Major weakness: complete absence of empirical validation to demonstrate practical manifestation of theoretical claims.  
- Raises but does not explore the open question of using random projections or Fourier-feature kernel approximations while preserving stability.  
- Lacks concrete implementation examples and does not address computational efficiency of the proposed framework.  
- High originality and theoretical significance, but practical impact is limited without experimental results; recommends acceptance contingent on added experiments.
