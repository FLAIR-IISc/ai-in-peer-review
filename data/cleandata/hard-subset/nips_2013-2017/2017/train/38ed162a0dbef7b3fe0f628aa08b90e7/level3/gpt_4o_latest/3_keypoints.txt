- Provides a rigorous RKHS-based framework for DCNNs showing near-invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernels, extends CKNs and scattering transforms to broader CNN architectures, and links theory to discretization, kernel approximations, and classical CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.

Strengths:
- Theoretical rigor: solid mathematical foundation with detailed proofs and derivations.  
- Novelty: RKHS reformulation bridges kernel methods and practical deep learning architectures.  
- Clear stability analysis: demonstrates control of translation invariance and diffeomorphic stability via kernel design and pooling.  
- Good connections to prior work: extends and situates results within scattering transforms and CKN literature.

Weaknesses:
- Practical utility is unclear: framework’s direct benefits for practitioners or architecture improvements are not well explained.  
- Preservation of signal information is insufficiently justified for task-specific settings (e.g., classification where some information loss is acceptable).  
- Lacks explicit connection to Hans Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methods.  
- Raises but does not explore whether other operators commuting with group actions could ensure equivariance.

Suggestions:
- Provide concrete examples or experiments demonstrating practical utility, especially where traditional CNNs struggle.  
- Clarify why preserving signal information matters for specific tasks or applications.  
- Discuss and compare to Burkhardt’s invariant kernel methods with citations.  
- Explore or hypothesize about other operators that might achieve equivariance.

Recommendation:
- Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve practical relevance and clarify connections.
