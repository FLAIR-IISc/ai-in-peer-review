- Paper proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel Φn built from patch extraction Pk, RKHS kernel mapping Mk, and Gaussian pooling Ak to obtain translation-equivariant-to-invariant feature maps.  
- Under assumptions (norm-preserving/non-expansive kernels and patch sizes matched to pooling scales) they prove a Lipschitz stability bound to C1 diffeomorphisms: ‖Φ(Lτx)−Φ(x)‖ ≤ (C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞/σn)‖x‖.  
- Stability improves toward translation invariance with larger final pooling scale σn, but scales linearly with depth as (1 + n).  
- They show signal preservation across layers under appropriate sampling and discuss discretization and finite-dimensional CKN approximations.  
- Sketches an extension to locally compact groups via group-covariant patch extraction and pooling.  
- They connect the multilayer RKHS to a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, so RKHS norms control prediction Lipschitzness and thus generalization/robustness.  
- Analysis generalizes scattering transforms by replacing fixed wavelets with a kernel framework that can encompass learned representations (CKNs) and certain CNNs.  
- Group-equivariant construction relates to prior group convolution networks; RKHS viewpoint connects to prior work on invariance/selectivity and kernel analyses of deep nets.  
- Discretization/approximation pipeline builds on CKN literature and classical kernel approximations (Nyström, low-rank).  
- Theoretical quality: technically sound proofs using operator-norm bounds, commutators, Schur’s test, with transparent and standard assumptions.  
- Two main limitations: stability requires ‖∇τ‖∞ < 1/2 and the bound grows with depth (1 + n); pooling is not norm-preserving, trading stability for energy loss unless a final kernel layer is used.  
- Discretization and deconvolution-based recovery rely on practically invertible filters and are sensitive to noise.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well organized and readable but needs stronger motivation linking RKHS Lipschitz control to sample complexity, regularization, augmentation, and domain-shift robustness.  
- Notation issue: κ is overloaded (used both for patch-size control in A3 and as the kernel function in Eq. (10)); rename one occurrence.  
- Minor math edits requested: remove redundant constraint around lines 384–385; define φ explicitly around line 416; fix inequality/equality and a typo at lines 427–428.  
- Bibliography edits: add page numbers for several refs and update publication/acceptance details for others.  
- Originality: novel extension of scattering-like deformation stability to multilayer convolutional kernels including CKNs/CNNs, and new RKHS inclusion with explicit norm bounds for smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of invariance/stability and links architecture/regularization choices to robustness and generalization; would be strengthened by empirical validation and practical guidance on choosing σk and Sk.  
- Additional suggestions: quantify constants for typical settings (e.g., d=2 Gaussian h), clarify scope/approximation error of “smooth homogeneous” activations versus ReLU, and formalize the group-stability sketch into a theorem with constants.  
- Acceptance arguments: solid, general theoretical results; clear RKHS–CNN linkage; careful discretization treatment; well written and relevant to the community.  
- Rejection/weakness arguments: limited empirical validation; depth-dependent bound and dependence on initial low-pass need clearer practical discussion; group-invariance section is a sketch; minor clarity/bibliographic issues.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity/motivation edits and, if possible, adding a brief empirical illustration.
