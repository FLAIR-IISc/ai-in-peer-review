This paper develops a multilayer convolutional kernel framework and proves Lipschitz stability of the resulting representations to C^1 diffeomorphisms, under assumptions A1–A2 (norm-preserving, non-expansive kernels) and A3 (patch sizes proportional to the pooling scale). The bound has the standard form C1||∇τ||∞ + C2||τ||∞/σn, clarifying how the last-layer pooling scale controls translation invariance while intermediate layers ensure deformation stability. The authors sketch an extension from (R^d,+) to general locally compact groups via equivariant patch-extraction and pooling, and they argue that discretized, projected CKNs preserve non-expansiveness so that the stability result transfers. Finally, they embed a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS induced by the multilayer kernel, yielding stability of predictions through norm control. The work is positioned relative to scattering networks (Bruna & Mallat; Mallat), CKNs (Mairal et al.), and group-equivariant CNNs (Cohen & Welling), and connects to robustness discussions (e.g., Parseval networks).

Quality: The analysis is technically sound and carefully executed, with a clean commutator decomposition and operator-norm bounds. The group sketch is sensible though less developed than the Euclidean case.

Clarity: The manuscript is well organized and readable. A few notational and editorial issues should be fixed (see below).

Originality: The paper extends scattering-style stability guarantees to a broader kernel class that includes CKNs and a subset of CNNs; the RKHS view and the group sketch add novelty beyond prior work.

Significance: Stability of learned convolutional representations is of broad interest; the results should inform both theory and practice, particularly around architectural choices and regularization.

Requested improvements:
- Strengthen the motivation beyond one sentence on adversarial robustness. Clarify practical implications (e.g., sample complexity, architectural design, trade-offs from depth and σn) and how to choose κ, σk in practice.
- Notation: κ is overloaded in A3 and as the kernel function κk in Eq. (10); please rename one to avoid confusion.
- Math edits: the second constraint in the displayed equation between lines 384–385 is redundant by kernel symmetry; remove it. Define φ at line 416 (via Eq. (10) as the feature map of K). Around lines 427–428, the inequality appears to hold with equality, and ||z|| − ||z|| should be ||z|| − ||z′||.
- Bibliography: add page ranges for [3,6,8,13,14,18,25–27,30,31]; update publication/acceptance details for [9], [17], [19], [32].

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, rigorous theory with meaningful takeaways; bridges kernels and CNNs; timely topic; well written.

Cons for acceptance:
- Motivation could be deeper; group extension is only sketched; minor but numerous editorial issues; limited empirical illustration of design choices.
