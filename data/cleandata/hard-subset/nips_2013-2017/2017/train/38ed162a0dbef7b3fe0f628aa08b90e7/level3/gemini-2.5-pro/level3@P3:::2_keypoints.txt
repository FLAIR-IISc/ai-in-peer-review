- Paper proves Lipschitz stability of Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to small C^1 diffeomorphisms under norm-preserving/non-expansive kernels and scaled patch/pooling sizes.  
- Extends scattering-network stability analysis to a more CNN-like multilayer kernel framework.  
- Sketches extension of stability analysis to actions of locally compact groups.  
- Shows the CKN RKHS contains a class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, transferring stability guarantees to those networks.  
- Main contribution is technically sound, significant, and advances understanding of deep representations’ geometry, robustness, and generalization.  
- Originality: adapts challenging scattering analysis to CKNs and connects kernel methods to practical CNN architectures.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-organized, with a logical progression from continuous theory to discrete implementation.  
- Motivation is weakly argued; the importance of diffeomorphism stability (e.g., for adversarial robustness or generalization) should be emphasized in the introduction.  
- Notation issue: `\kappa` is overloaded (used for both a constant and a kernel function) and should be disambiguated.  
- Redundant condition: one constraint stated around lines 384–385 is unnecessary given K’s positive-definiteness.  
- Undefined term: feature map `\phi` is used without formal definition; should be introduced as the canonical feature map for K.  
- Mathematical correction: inequality in the proof around lines 427–428 seems to be equality and `||z|| - ||z||` should be `||z|| - ||z'||`.  
- Bibliography needs revision: many entries lack page information and several preprints have since been published and should be updated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and publishable work with mostly minor presentation issues; recommendation to accept.
