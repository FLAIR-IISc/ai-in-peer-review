- Paper studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations (CKNs), proposing a multilayer kernel construction and proving stability under diffeomorphisms, with results that extend to a class of CNNs.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a rigorous framework for analyzing stability of deep convolutional representations.  
- Generalization: introduces a multilayer kernel construction that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Connection to CNNs: links the kernel representation to CNNs, showing stability for CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Motivation: lacks a more detailed motivation for studying stability beyond a brief link to adversarial robustness.  
- Notation: some notation issues (e.g., overloading of κ) that may confuse readers.  
- Mathematical clarity: certain simplifications/redundancies (e.g., a redundant constraint) are not pointed out and could clarify the exposition.  
- Bibliography: references have issues (missing page information, outdated details).  
- Originality: presents a novel combination of deep networks and kernel methods, advancing perspectives on stability.  
- Significance: results have meaningful implications for understanding and applying stable deep convolutional representations (e.g., image classification).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation, mathematical simplifications, and bibliography.
