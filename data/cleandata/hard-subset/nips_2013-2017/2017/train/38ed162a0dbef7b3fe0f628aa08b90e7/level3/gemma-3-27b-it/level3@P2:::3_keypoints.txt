- Frames deep CNNs within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) via a multilayer convolutional kernel, connecting CNNs to kernel theory.  
- Shows CNNs inherit stability to diffeomorphisms and approximate group invariance, paralleling results for scattering transforms.  
- Technical treatment is rigorous, with well-structured proofs and clear assumptions.  
- Offers a novel perspective that could enable applying kernel-method tools to analyze and improve CNNs.  
- Carefully situates the work relative to scattering transforms, convolutional kernel networks, and harmonic analysis.  
- Writing is clear and accessible, even for readers not expert in all mathematical details.  
- Demonstrated stability properties have important implications for generalization and sample complexity.  
- Main weakness: motivation for the reformulation is not fully articulated—unclear what practical advantages (new algorithms, tighter bounds, efficient approximations) it yields.  
- Needs clearer distinction from prior Convolutional Kernel Networks (Mairal et al.), explicitly outlining novel contributions beyond generalization and extension to standard CNNs.  
- Questionable emphasis on preserving “all signal information”; the trade-off between preserving information and discarding task-irrelevant details should be discussed.  
- Lacks discussion of connections to existing invariant-kernel literature (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) to better position the work.  
- Suggests exploring general conditions for operator equivariance (beyond commuting with group elements) to characterize operators suitable for equivariant DCNNs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides rigorous foundation for CNNs, links to RKHS theory, proves useful stability properties, clear presentation, and opens new research directions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: motivation could be stronger, novelty relative to prior kernel-based CNN work needs clarification, and the focus on preserving full signal may be problematic.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
