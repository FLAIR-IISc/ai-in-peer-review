- Core contribution: Frames a large class of CNNs in an RKHS/kernel framework, generalizing convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).
- Theoretical results: Proves group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms, extending scattering-transform–style properties to broader models.
- Technical quality: Analysis is mathematically rigorous and builds on solid related work in kernels, signal processing, and deep learning.
- Presentation: The paper is clear, well-written, and accessible despite technical depth.
- Significance: Provides a theoretical underpinning for CNN behavior and opens avenues for further analysis (e.g., generalization).
- Practical implications unclear: The authors should more explicitly explain why the RKHS reformulation is useful in practice (generalization bounds, regularization, computation).
- Novelty vs CKNs: Needs clearer articulation of advances beyond extending prior CKN work and what new insights the RKHS perspective affords.
- Signal preservation concern: Emphasis on preserving all signal information may be impractical; discuss trade-offs between preservation and task-specific feature extraction.
- Related work gap: Should explicitly connect to invariant kernel approaches (e.g., Hans Burkhardt) to situate contributions and potential synergies.
- Equivariance question: Ask whether any operator commuting with group elements guarantees DCNN equivariance and request at least a brief characterization.
- Strength — rigorous theoretical analysis: Strong mathematical treatment linking CNNs to RKHS tools.
- Strength — strong literature grounding: Thorough review and building on existing kernel/CKN literature.
- Strength — potential for future work: Framework can lead to research on generalization, regularization, and other theoretical questions.
- Recommendation: Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance despite minor weaknesses; technical rigor and clarity outweigh concerns.
