- Proposes an RKHS-based theoretical framework describing CNN functional space as a multilayer kernel with approximate group invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Reformulates CNNs in the RKHS context, showing the RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization.  
- Provides a detailed construction of the multilayer convolutional kernel that contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Demonstrates that stability results for the kernel representation can be translated to predictive models by controlling their functional-space norm.  
- Connects the theoretical framework to practical applications, offering a new perspective on the stability–generalization relationship in CNNs.  
- Compares and contrasts the approach with scattering transforms and convolutional kernel networks, situating the work within related literature.  
- Paper is well-written, rigorous, logically organized, and uses clear, well-defined notation.  
- Main contribution is a principled, theoretical analysis of CNN properties via the multilayer kernel / RKHS viewpoint.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and views the work as a significant contribution to machine learning.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Potential drawback: may be difficult for readers not familiar with the underlying mathematical concepts.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more examples or applications to better illustrate the framework’s usefulness and significance.
