This paper studies the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their connection to convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the conditions under which CKNs are nearly invariant to groups of transformations and stable to diffeomorphisms.

The main idea of the paper is to generalize the multilayer kernel introduced in the context of convolutional kernel networks and study the geometry of the corresponding reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors show that the signal representation is stable, and that models from this functional space, such as a large class of CNNs, may enjoy the same stability.

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the technical sections are well-organized and easy to follow. The manuscript is rich in mathematical details, and the proofs are thorough and well-explained.

One of the major strengths of the paper is its ability to bridge the gap between CKNs and CNNs. The authors show that the RKHS obtained from their kernel construction contains a set of CNNs on continuous domains with certain types of smooth homogeneous activations. This connection is important, as it allows the authors to transfer the stability results of CKNs to CNNs.

However, the paper has a major weakness: it lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. While the authors provide a thorough theoretical framework, they do not provide any experimental results to demonstrate the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms under the proposed settings. This is a significant omission, as it makes it difficult to assess the practical relevance of the theoretical results.

Another open question is whether random projection or explicit kernel mapping via a finite number of Fourier features could serve as kernel approximations, and how they would affect stability. The authors mention that the CKN construction provides a finite-dimensional representation at each layer, but they do not explore the implications of this for kernel approximation.

In terms of originality, the paper builds on previous work by Mallat [17] and others, but it provides a significant extension of these results to more general CNN architectures and to the kernel framework. The authors also provide a novel connection between CKNs and CNNs, which is an important contribution to the field.

The significance of the paper lies in its ability to provide a theoretical framework for understanding the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations. The authors show that these properties are not unique to CKNs, but can also be enjoyed by CNNs under certain conditions. This has important implications for the design of neural networks that are robust to transformations and diffeomorphisms.

In conclusion, the paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations. While it lacks empirical experiments, it provides a significant contribution to the field by bridging the gap between CKNs and CNNs. The authors' connection between CKNs and CNNs is an important result that has implications for the design of neural networks.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations.
* The authors bridge the gap between CKNs and CNNs, providing a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper is well-written and technically sound.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper lacks empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis.
* The authors do not explore the implications of kernel approximation for stability.
* The paper builds on previous work, and the extent to which it provides a novel contribution is unclear.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should provide empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis, and they should explore the implications of kernel approximation for stability.
