- Main contribution: Proposes hierarchical implicit models (HIMs) and likelihood-free variational inference (LFVI) for scalable inference in implicit probabilistic models.  
- LFVI performance: LFVI enables scalable, accurate inference and empirically outperforms rejection ABC, MCMC-ABC, and SMC-ABC in experiments.  
- Applications: Demonstrates flexibility and scalability on a large-scale physical simulator, Bayesian GANs, and a deep implicit sequence generator.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to a broad audience.  
- Technical quality: Technical sections are detailed, notation is consistent, and theoretical claims are supported.  
- Strength: Clear introduction to implicit models and limitations makes the work approachable.  
- Strength: Strong empirical showcase across diverse, large-scale applications highlighting practical impact.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion and comparison to existing work on implicit models and related methods.  
- Weakness: Experimental section lacks comprehensive comparisons and deeper analysis of results.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of potential limitations, challenges, and future research directions for the proposed method.  
- Conference fit: Meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and technical soundness.  
- Recommendation: Overall accept, but authors should expand related-work comparison, strengthen experiments, and discuss limitations/future work.
