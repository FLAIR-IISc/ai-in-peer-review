- Paper develops a multilayer convolutional kernel for continuous-domain signals, yielding RKHS feature maps via patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernels, and Gaussian pooling.  
- Proves stability to C1-diffeomorphisms with Lipschitz-type bounds and translation invariance scaling with final pooling.  
- Designs G-equivariant patch extraction and pooling to achieve near-invariance to general groups.  
- Shows a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the induced RKHS with explicit RKHS norm bounds, inheriting stability.  
- Discusses discretization and CKN-style finite-dimensional approximations that preserve non-expansiveness.  
- Technically rigorous with clear assumptions and proofs using harmonic analysis and operator bounds.  
- Clean separation between representation and predictor and a crisp operator-level stability decomposition.  
- Generalizes scattering stability beyond fixed wavelets to kernelized, learnable multilayer maps and applies to CNNs in the RKHS.  
- Group-equivariant construction is simple, general, and achieves near-invariance via final pooling.  
- Practical bridge: discretization/CKN approximations remain non-expansive and inform network design.
