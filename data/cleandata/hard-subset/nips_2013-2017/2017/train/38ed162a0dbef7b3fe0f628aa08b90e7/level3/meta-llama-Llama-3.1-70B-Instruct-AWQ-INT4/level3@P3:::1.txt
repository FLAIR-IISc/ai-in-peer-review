This paper, "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations," presents a theoretical study of the properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and their relationship to convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the invariance and stability of CKNs to transformation groups and diffeomorphisms, and they show that these properties carry over to CNNs under certain assumptions.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, with a clear and concise presentation of the main ideas and results. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the contributions of their paper. The use of mathematical notation and terminology is consistent throughout the paper, and the proofs of the main results are relegated to the appendix, making the main text more accessible to non-experts.

The main strength of the paper is its rigorous and comprehensive analysis of the invariance and stability properties of CKNs and CNNs. The authors provide a detailed study of the geometry of the functional space associated with the kernel, and they show how this geometry affects the stability and generalization properties of the models. The results are significant, as they provide a theoretical understanding of the success of deep neural networks in image classification tasks.

However, the paper has a major weakness: the lack of empirical experiments to validate the theoretical analysis. While the authors provide a detailed study of the theoretical properties of CKNs and CNNs, they do not provide any experimental results to demonstrate the practical relevance of their findings. This is a significant omission, as empirical validation is essential to demonstrate the effectiveness of any theoretical result.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of techniques from harmonic analysis and kernel methods to study the properties of deep neural networks. The authors' use of the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) framework to study the geometry of the functional space associated with the kernel is particularly innovative. However, the paper builds on previous work by the authors and others, and the results are not entirely unexpected.

The significance of the paper lies in its contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep neural networks. The authors' results provide a rigorous analysis of the invariance and stability properties of CKNs and CNNs, which is essential to understanding the success of these models in image classification tasks. The paper also provides a framework for studying the geometry of the functional space associated with the kernel, which can be used to analyze other deep neural network architectures.

In conclusion, this paper presents a rigorous and comprehensive analysis of the invariance and stability properties of CKNs and CNNs. While the lack of empirical experiments is a significant weakness, the paper's originality and significance make it a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning.
