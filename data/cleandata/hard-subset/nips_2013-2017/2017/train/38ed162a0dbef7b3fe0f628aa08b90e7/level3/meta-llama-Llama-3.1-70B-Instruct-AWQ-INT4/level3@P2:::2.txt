This paper studies the stability and invariance properties of deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), which are a type of neural network that combines the strengths of deep learning and kernel methods. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of CKNs, including their stability to diffeomorphisms and their invariance to translations and other group actions.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for readers with a background in machine learning and kernel methods. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work, including the scattering transform and other kernel-based methods, and they clearly explain the contributions of their paper.

The main contribution of the paper is the proof of stability of CKNs to C^1 diffeomorphisms, which is a significant result that has implications for the robustness of CKNs to adversarial attacks. The authors also extend their stability analysis to locally compact groups, which is an interesting direction for future research.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide detailed proofs of their results in the appendix. However, the reviewer notes that the motivation for the stability analysis could be stronger, and that the link to adversarial robustness is only mentioned briefly.

There are a few minor issues with notation and formatting, including the overloading of the symbol \kappa and a missing definition of \phi. Additionally, some references are missing page information or have outdated publication details.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional kernel networks. The results have implications for the design of robust neural networks, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
