This paper investigates the theoretical foundations of deep convolutional representations, demonstrating that deep convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) exhibit near-invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms under specific design conditions for patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling. The authors extend these properties to a broader class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs), providing valuable insights into the underlying geometry of these models. The work is technically sound, well-written, and builds upon previous research in kernel methods, scattering transforms, and group-invariant representations.

The paper effectively connects recent advances in convolutional kernel networks [15, 16] with the theoretical framework developed for scattering transforms [5, 17], offering a more general perspective on invariance and stability. The authors appropriately cite relevant literature, including work on group equivariant CNNs [9, 22] and kernelized deep learning [2, 10, 18]. The introduction clearly articulates the motivation for studying the geometry of the functional spaces inhabited by CNNs and highlights the potential benefits for regularization and sample complexity. The detailed mathematical development and clear notation contribute to the clarity of the manuscript. The connection made to CNNs via homogeneous activations is a valuable insight, further broadening the applicability of the theoretical results.

The primary strength of this work lies in its rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors provide a comprehensive mathematical framework for understanding invariance and stability, building upon existing tools from harmonic analysis and kernel methods. The derivation of stability results for diffeomorphic deformations (Theorem 6) is particularly noteworthy. The extension of these results to a wider class of CNNs is a significant contribution. The discussion relating RKHS norms to generalization is also insightful, connecting theoretical stability with practical learning outcomes.

However, the most significant weakness is the complete lack of empirical validation. While the theoretical results are compelling, their practical relevance remains unconfirmed. The authors acknowledge this limitation and request for experiments to demonstrate the invariance and stability properties of CKNs under the proposed settings. This is a critical omission, as the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis (e.g., smoothness of activations, specific kernel choices) may not hold in real-world scenarios. Without empirical evidence, it’s difficult to assess the true impact of these theoretical findings.  The paper's claims about the benefits for regularization and sample complexity also need experimental support.

An open question raised by the authors concerns the use of random projections or explicit kernel mapping via Fourier features as kernel approximations and their impact on stability. This is a relevant line of inquiry, as these techniques are often employed to improve the computational efficiency of kernel methods. Exploring these approximations could provide a bridge between the theoretical analysis and practical implementations.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical foundation:** The paper presents a rigorous and well-developed theoretical analysis of invariance and stability in deep convolutional representations.
*   **Novel combination of ideas:** The work successfully combines concepts from convolutional kernel networks, scattering transforms, and kernel methods, offering a novel perspective on the subject.
*   **Potential for impact:** The theoretical insights could have implications for understanding the generalization properties of CNNs and developing more robust learning algorithms.
*   **Clear and well-written:** The manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and technically sound.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Lack of empirical validation:** The absence of experiments is a major drawback, limiting the assessment of the practical relevance of the theoretical results.
*   **Limited practical guidance:**  While theoretically strong, the paper doesn’t offer concrete guidance on how to best leverage these insights in practical CNN design.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite its significant theoretical contributions, the lack of empirical validation raises concerns about the practical impact of this work.  I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, contingent upon the authors addressing the reviewer’s request for experimental results. Specifically, the authors should include experiments demonstrating the invariance of CKNs to transformations and diffeomorphisms, even if in a simplified setting.  Addressing the open question regarding kernel approximations would also strengthen the paper. Without empirical support, the manuscript remains a valuable theoretical contribution but falls short of demonstrating its real-world significance. The paper's strengths in clarity and rigor are commendable, but practical validation is crucial for a NIPS publication.
