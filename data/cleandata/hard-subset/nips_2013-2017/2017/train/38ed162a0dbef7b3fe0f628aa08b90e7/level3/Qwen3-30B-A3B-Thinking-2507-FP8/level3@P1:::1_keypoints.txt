- Formal connection established between convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), group invariance, and stability to diffeomorphisms, generalized to continuous domains.  
- Theorem 6: stability bound ||Φ(Lτx)-Φ(x)|| ≤ (C1(1+n)||∇τ||∞ + C2/σn ||τ||∞) ||x||, implying deeper networks and larger pooling scales increase invariance.  
- Proves stability properties extend to standard CNNs with homogeneous (smoothed) activations by embedding them in the RKHS defined by the kernel construction.  
- Extends Mallat’s scattering transform framework to a kernel-based approach applicable to standard CNN architectures rather than fixed filters.  
- High technical quality: rigorous mathematical development, well-motivated assumptions, precise definitions, and careful proofs (appendices included).  
- Exceptional clarity and exposition, with strong, well-contextualized connections to related work (CKNs, scattering, group invariance).  
- Original contribution: unifies scattering networks and CKNs and shows stability insights for CKNs carry over to practical CNNs.  
- High significance: provides a theoretical foundation for CNN invariance, links stability to generalization and sample complexity, informing model design and regularization.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation demonstrating the claimed invariance/stability on standard datasets.  
- Minor concern: practical requirement for homogeneous (smoothed) activations is unclear and lacks comparison to standard ReLU in practice.  
- Computational/implementation gaps: limited discussion of kernel approximations and how random projections or finite Fourier features would affect stability.  
- Recommendation: include experiments demonstrating stability/invariance to strengthen the paper and better connect theory to practice.
