- Presents theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations' invariance and stability under C1-diffeomorphisms.  
- Extends Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs) to continuous-domain signals and constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel.  
- Main contribution: proves L2-norm stability of the representation to diffeomorphisms, bounded by the diffeomorphism norm and its Jacobian norm.  
- Generalizes Mallat’s scattering-transform stability to a broader class including CKNs and certain CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations (Section 5).  
- Sketches extension to equivariance/invariance for actions of locally compact groups beyond translations.  
- Strength: technically rigorous and significant, linking translation-invariance to final-layer pooling scale.  
- Strength: embedding a class of CNNs into the constructed RKHS broadens applicability beyond kernel methods.  
- Strength: original synthesis of CKN framework, scattering stability, and RKHS theory.  
- Strength: manuscript is very clear, well organized, with detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Weakness: motivation for why this specific stability matters (practical benefits for architecture design, regularization, or generalization) needs strengthening.  
- Minor issue: overloaded symbol κ used for both kernel function and a constant in assumption (A3).  
- Minor issue: redundant constraint in Appendix (symmetry of kernel could simplify a displayed equation).  
- Minor issue: missing definition of φ in Appendix; should be introduced as the kernel feature map.  
- Minor issue: typographical/error in Appendix (||z|| - ||z|| likely should be ||z|| - ||z'|| and an inequality appears to be equality).  
- Minor issue: bibliography needs updating (missing page numbers, some preprints now published).  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension of stability guarantees to relevant models and clearly presented.  
- Argument against acceptance: motivation could be more compelling and minor notational/bibliographic issues should be fixed.
