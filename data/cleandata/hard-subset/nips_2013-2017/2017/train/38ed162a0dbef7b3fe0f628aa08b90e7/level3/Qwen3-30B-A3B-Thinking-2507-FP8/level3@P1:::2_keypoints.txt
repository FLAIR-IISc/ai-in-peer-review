- Studies stability of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) under C^1 diffeomorphisms, extending scattering transform theory to more general convolutional architectures.  
- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes CKNs and captures a broad class of convolutional models.  
- Shows the functional space contains both CKNs and many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, so stability results apply to standard CNNs.  
- Establishes rigorous conditions (A1–A3) guaranteeing stability to small deformations with a bound that decays with pooling scale σ_n (Theorem 6).  
- Connects RKHS norm to generalization (Proposition 7), offering a principled explanation for why stable representations generalize better.  
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering transform but extends results to kernel-based representations and more general CNN models, and links to group invariance and kernel-methods literature.  
- Manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, with thorough mathematical development and careful treatment of discretization and signal-preservation.  
- Extension to group invariance (Section 4) demonstrates framework versatility.  
- Motivation for focusing on diffeomorphism stability is underdeveloped; connection to adversarial robustness and practical necessity needs deeper exploration.  
- Several minor technical issues: κ overloaded (patch size vs. kernel), redundant constraint between lines 384–385, undefined feature map φ at line 416, and an apparent typo in the inequality around lines 427–428 (||z|| - ||z|| → ||z|| - ||z'||).  
- Bibliography problems: many references lack page information and several key citations need updated publication details (e.g., cite full PAMI paper for [17]).  
- Originality is clear: extends scattering theory to kernel representations and standard CNNs, broadening theoretical understanding of convolutional architectures.  
- Significance is substantial: provides a theoretical basis for robustness to small deformations and suggests RKHS-based regularization could improve sample complexity.  
- Quality is excellent with careful proofs and acknowledgment of framework limitations (trade-offs between norm preservation and pooling); recommendation: accept after fixing minor issues and strengthening practical motivation.
