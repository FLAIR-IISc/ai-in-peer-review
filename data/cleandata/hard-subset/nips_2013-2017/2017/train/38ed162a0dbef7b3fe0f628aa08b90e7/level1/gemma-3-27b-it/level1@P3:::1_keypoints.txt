- Paper studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, using a kernel framework to generalize CKNs and analyze the RKHS geometry.  
- Shows stability to diffeomorphisms and near-invariance to group transformations, connecting and extending scattering transform results to broader CNNs.  
- Well-motivated gap: provides geometric characterization of functional spaces for CNNs via kernels.  
- Strength: strong, rigorous theoretical foundation built on kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  
- Strength: generalizes scattering-transform theory to a wider class of CNN architectures beyond fixed architectures.  
- Strength: clear links to standard CNNs with practical relevance (notably Proposition 7).  
- Strength: nuanced discussion of how RKHS norm controls invariance, stability, and generalization.  
- Strength: comprehensive and thorough related work covering kernel methods, signal processing, and deep learning.  
- Weakness: mathematically dense and heavy notation, making accessibility difficult for non-experts.  
- Weakness: limited empirical validation — no experiments to show practical benefits or robustness improvements.  
- Weakness: stability results depend on strong assumptions (smooth activations, regular diffeomorphisms); impact of violations under-discussed.  
- Weakness: discretization section is brief; needs more detail on boundary effects and sampling choices.  
- Weakness: claim that framework “contains” many CNNs is vague; constraints on included architectures are not precisely defined.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, significant theoretical contributions, technically sound, and extends important prior work with substantial potential impact.  
- Rejection arguments: lack of empirical validation, high mathematical complexity limiting accessibility, and potentially restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with impressive theoretical results; concerns about empirical support and accessibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add discussion or preliminary experiments for empirical validation; simplify notation and add intuitive explanations.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify exact architectural constraints included in the framework and discuss effects of violating assumptions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand discretization discussion with boundary handling and sampling-rate guidance.
