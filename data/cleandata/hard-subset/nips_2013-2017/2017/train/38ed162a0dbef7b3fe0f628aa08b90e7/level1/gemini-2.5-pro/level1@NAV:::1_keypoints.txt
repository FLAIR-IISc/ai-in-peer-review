- Paper generalizes Convolutional Kernel Networks to a multilayer convolutional kernel and studies the geometry of its RKHS.  
- Adapts scattering-transform tools to prove Lipschitz stability of the representation to small diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows translation invariance is controlled by the scale of the final pooling layer.  
- Establishes a formal link between the kernel framework and standard CNNs by embedding CNNs with certain smooth, homogeneous activations in the RKHS.  
- Extends stability guarantees from the kernel representation to a class of learnable deep models, connecting RKHS norm to robustness and generalization.  
- Technical contribution is rigorous, significant, and could inspire new regularization and architectural insights.  
- Original synthesis of scattering theory, CKNs, and RKHS analysis extends prior fixed-filter results to learnable architectures.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with logical presentation of theory, discretization, and CNN connections.  
- Main weakness: the CNN connection relies on a nonstandard "homogenized" activation with patch-norm normalization, and implications of this deviation are under-discussed.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical paper whose strengths outweigh the minor weakness.
