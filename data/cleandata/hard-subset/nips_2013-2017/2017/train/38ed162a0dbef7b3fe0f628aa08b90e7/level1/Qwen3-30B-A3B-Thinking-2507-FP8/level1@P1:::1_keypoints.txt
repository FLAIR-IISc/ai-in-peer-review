- Extends multilayer kernel framework to continuous domains to study invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms in deep convolutional representations.  
- Proves signal representations are stable to diffeomorphisms and shows models in the corresponding RKHS (including many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations) inherit this stability.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with complete proofs; Theorem 6 generalizes scattering-network stability guarantees to more standard CNN architectures.  
- Establishes a connection between RKHS norm and stability/generalization (Proposition 7), offering a principled way to control model complexity.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with consistent notation and clear explanation of discretization and kernel-approximation implementations.  
- Novelly bridges Mallat’s scattering transform and standard CNN architectures via a kernel framework, accommodating smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Introduces a method for achieving global group invariance through designed patch extraction and pooling operators.  
- Results have significant theoretical and practical implications for explaining CNN performance and guiding design of robust, sample-efficient models.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete examples showing how popular architectures (e.g., ResNet, VGG) fit into the framework or how stability results guide architectural choices.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of the “norm preservation” issue and limited discussion of its practical impact or how common proposed workarounds are.  
- Weakness: ReLU is homogeneous but not smooth (outside the RKHS); more discussion is needed on smoothed ReLU variants and their practical effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous foundation for CNN invariance/stability, successful bridge between scattering transforms and CNNs, principled RKHS–generalization link, technically sound and significant.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs stronger connections to practical architectures, fuller discussion of norm-preservation workarounds, and lacks empirical validation.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality theoretical contribution with substantial impact; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
