- Paper develops a kernel-based theoretical framework analyzing invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations.  
- Extends scattering transform stability guarantees to a broader class of models including CKNs and (under smoothness assumptions) standard CNNs.  
- Proves stability to diffeomorphisms and near-invariance to group transformations, tied to RKHS geometry.  
- Strong grounding in RKHS and positive definite kernel theory providing rigorous mathematical analysis.  
- Demonstrates a clear connection between the kernel framework and practical CNN architectures under certain conditions.  
- Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel and provides detailed, mathematically rigorous stability analysis.  
- Positions work well within extensive related literature on scattering transforms, kernel methods, and group invariance.  
- Referencing is comprehensive and shows deep familiarity with recent relevant work.  
- Paper is highly technical and may be inaccessible to readers without background in kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  
- Lacks empirical validation; no experiments are provided to demonstrate practical benefits or robustness.  
- Requires smooth, homogeneous activation functions, limiting applicability to standard ReLU networks and relying on smoothed variants.  
- Brief treatment of kernel approximation and computational costs leaves practical scalability concerns unaddressed.  
- Contribution, while solid and valuable theoretically, may be viewed as incremental relative to prior scattering-transform results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theory, meaningful generalization of scattering stability, and strong mathematical depth addressing fundamental questions.  
- Arguments for rejection: high technical barrier, missing empirical support, restrictive activation assumptions, and potential computational impracticality.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with a lean toward accept, but authors should add experiments, computational discussion, and more intuitive explanations to improve impact and accessibility.
