- Studies invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms of deep convolutional representations.  
- Introduces a kernel framework generalizing multilayer constructions from convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).  
- Proves the signal representation is stable and that a broad class of models (including many CNNs) inherit this stability.  
- Connects the kernel framework to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, offering a new perspective on CNN generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents technical content concisely.  
- Technical analysis of stability and invariance properties is thorough and sound.  
- Well-motivated discussion of why understanding CNN functional geometry matters (regularization, robustness benefits).  
- Potential impact is high—provides significant theoretical insight into links between stability and generalization.  
- Main limitation: primarily theoretical work with limited experimental validation of practical impact.  
- Accessibility concern: assumes high mathematical sophistication (functional and harmonic analysis), limiting some readers.  
- Quality score: 8/10 (technically sound but mathematically demanding).  
- Clarity score: 9/10 (clear presentation but still challenging for non-experts).  
- Originality score: 9/10 (novel connection between kernel framework and CNN stability).  
- Significance score: 9/10 (potentially influential for deep learning theory).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical perspective, sound analysis, potential high impact.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited experiments and restricted accessibility due to mathematical depth.
