## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, specifically focusing on invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms. The authors generalize the multilayer kernel introduced in the context of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and leverage reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) geometry to analyze these properties. They aim to provide a theoretical foundation for understanding the success of CNNs, potentially aiding in regularization and improving sample complexity.

The work builds upon previous research in scattering transforms [17], CKNs [15, 16], and kernel methods [27]. It extends the analysis of invariance and stability, previously established for scattering transforms, to a broader class of CNN architectures via a kernel framework. The paper carefully connects its theoretical results to practical considerations like discretization and kernel approximation, demonstrating relevance to commonly used CNN implementations. The authors also explore how to achieve global invariance to transformation groups by strategically designing patch extraction and pooling operators.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis of invariance and stability, grounding these concepts in the well-defined framework of RKHS theory.
* **Generality:** The results are shown to apply not only to CKNs but also to a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions.
* **Connections to Practice:** The authors address practical concerns like discretization and kernel approximation, bridging the gap between theory and implementation.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow of ideas. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations appear sound.
* **Novelty:** Extending the stability analysis from scattering transforms to a wider range of CNN architectures is a significant contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The mathematical formalism is dense and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with RKHS theory and harmonic analysis.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** While the paper focuses on theoretical results, some empirical validation demonstrating the practical benefits of the derived stability properties would strengthen the work.
* **Assumptions:** The analysis relies on several assumptions (e.g., smoothness of activation functions, regularity of diffeomorphisms) that may not always hold in practice. The impact of relaxing these assumptions could be discussed.
* **Incremental Advance:** While the extension to broader CNN architectures is valuable, the core ideas build heavily on existing work in scattering transforms and kernel methods. The novelty, while present, might be considered incremental.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a rigorous and mathematically sound analysis of important theoretical properties of CNNs.
* The work extends existing theory to a broader class of models, increasing its applicability.
* The connections to practical considerations demonstrate the relevance of the research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The high level of mathematical complexity may limit the accessibility of the work.
* The lack of empirical validation weakens the impact of the theoretical results.
* The incremental nature of the advance may not fully justify publication at a top-tier conference like NIPS.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep convolutional networks. While the mathematical complexity and lack of empirical validation are drawbacks, the rigor, generality, and connections to practical considerations make it a worthwhile read for researchers interested in the foundations of deep learning. I recommend **acceptance**, with a suggestion to the authors to consider including some illustrative examples or experiments in a future version to demonstrate the practical implications of their findings.
