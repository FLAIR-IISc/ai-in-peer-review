- Presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations using RKHS, generalizing multilayer kernels and connecting to Mallat's scattering transform.
- Proves stability guarantees under diffeomorphisms for a functional space that includes CKNs and many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
- Extends Mallat’s scattering transform theory to more standard convolutional architectures and links it to practical CNN implementations.
- Uses well-executed harmonic analysis proof techniques that build logically on prior work.
- Establishes a principled connection between the RKHS norm and stability/generalization, offering a way to control model complexity.
- Shows that CNNs with homogeneous (smoothed ReLU-like) activations are contained in the constructed RKHS, a novel theoretical result.
- Formalizes how global invariance to group actions can be achieved via patch extraction and pooling design, extending group equivariance work.
- Technically sound with rigorous proofs and well-supported theoretical claims.
- Lack of empirical validation: no experiments demonstrating how stability properties affect real-world performance.
- Restrictive assumption: requires homogeneous smooth activations, excluding standard ReLU and limiting practical applicability.
- The connection to practical CNN training remains somewhat abstract; containment in RKHS isn't shown to yield improved empirical stability or generalization.
- Would benefit from discussion on how practitioners could use the theory for regularization or architecture design.
- Well-written and organized with clear notation, though some sections need more intuitive explanations.
- Original contribution by extending scattering theory to a broader class of CNNs and providing novel stability analysis.
- Potentially significant for understanding why CNNs generalize, but immediate practical impact is limited by missing experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical foundation, extension of Mallat’s theory, and RKHS–generalization link.
- Arguments against acceptance: no empirical validation, restrictive activation assumptions, and abstract practical connection.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add discussion on empirical testing and practical implications of activation assumptions.
