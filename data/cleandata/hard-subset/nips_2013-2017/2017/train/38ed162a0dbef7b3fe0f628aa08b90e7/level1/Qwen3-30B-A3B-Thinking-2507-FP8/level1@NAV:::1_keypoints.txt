- Studies invariance to group transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations.
- Generalizes multilayer kernel framework from CKNs to continuous domains and analyzes the corresponding RKHS geometry.
- Shows the representation preserves information while remaining stable to small deformations.
- Proves stability guarantees comparable to the scattering transform but applicable to a broader set of architectures including standard CNNs.
- Key contribution: many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS, so the stability results apply directly to them.
- Technically sound and well-structured with rigorous proofs provided in the appendix.
- Appropriately compares and generalizes prior work (scattering transform, CKNs), addressing prior limitations like lack of learning capacity.
- Insightful connection established between RKHS norms and stability/generalization properties.
- Strengths: novel theoretical bridge between kernel methods and CNNs, elegant stability proofs, clear analysis of pooling and translation invariance, and applicability via Proposition 7.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical validation and an abstract treatment of discretization from continuous theory to practice.
- Accessibility concern: mathematical notation and RKHS theory may be challenging for practitioners without prior exposure.
- Overall significance: advances theoretical understanding of CNNs with implications for regularization and sample complexity; recommended for acceptance.
