- Studies invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations using an RKHS/multilayer kernel framework.  
- Generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to analyze a broader class of CNNs and allows learned filters.  
- Extends scattering transform stability and invariance guarantees to more general architectures beyond predefined filters.  
- Establishes a connection between the RKHS norm and stability/generalization, suggesting regularization avenues.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical analysis with detailed proofs supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Addresses practical considerations such as discretization and kernel approximation in the analysis.  
- Heavy mathematical notation and RKHS theory may limit accessibility to non-expert readers.  
- Assumptions of activation smoothness and homogeneity reduce direct applicability to standard ReLU networks without smoothing.  
- Precise implications for typical CNN architectures used in practice are not fully elaborated.  
- Limited discussion on how theoretical guarantees can be translated into concrete network design or training strategies.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, extension of prior work (scattering), RKHS-generalization link, thorough proofs.  
- Weaknesses: high complexity, limited practical relevance for ReLU networks, insufficient guidance on leveraging results in practice.  
- Overall recommendation: significant theoretical contribution and recommend acceptance, with encouragement to clarify practical implications.
