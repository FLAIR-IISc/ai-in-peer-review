- Studies theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, focusing on invariance and stability to transformation groups and diffeomorphisms.  
- Generalizes the multilayer kernel from convolutional kernel networks and analyzes the geometry of the associated RKHS.  
- Proves the signal representation is stable and that models in this functional space, including many CNNs, can inherit that stability.  
- Shows the RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, linking these two properties.  
- Demonstrates stability is controlled by the norms of the linear operators defining the representation.  
- Shows Gaussian kernel mapping can produce a stable representation with unit norm.  
- Establishes that the RKHS contains CNNs on continuous domains with certain smooth homogeneous activations, connecting kernels and CNNs.  
- Provides a clear, thorough theoretical framework and detailed comparison with related work, including the scattering transform.  
- Paper is well-written and high quality, offering significant and interesting results and insights.
