This paper studies the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations, with a focus on understanding the geometry of the corresponding reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors propose a multilayer kernel framework that generalizes the construction of convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and scattering networks, and show that this framework contains a large class of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with smooth homogeneous activation functions.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, with a clear structure that makes it easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including the scattering transform and CKNs, and clearly explain the contributions of their paper. The technical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, with detailed proofs relegated to the appendix.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a multilayer kernel framework that provides a theoretical understanding of the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations. The authors show that this framework contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions, and provide a characterization of the stability and invariance properties of these representations.

The paper also provides a link between the kernel representation and CNNs, showing that the RKHS obtained from the kernel construction contains a set of CNNs on continuous domains with certain types of smooth homogeneous activations. This result provides a new perspective on the connection between kernel methods and deep learning.
