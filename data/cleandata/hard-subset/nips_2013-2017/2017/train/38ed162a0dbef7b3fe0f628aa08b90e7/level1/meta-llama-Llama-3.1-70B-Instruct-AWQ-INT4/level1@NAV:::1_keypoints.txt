- Studies deep convolutional representations that are near-invariant to transformation groups and stable to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information.
- Generalizes multilayer convolutional kernels and analyzes the geometry of the associated reproducing kernel Hilbert space.
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering networks and extends group-invariance and diffeomorphism-stability results to broader CNN architectures and kernel frameworks.
- Draws inspiration from prior empirical and methodological work on group-invariance in CNNs.
- Strength: technically rigorous and thorough theoretical analysis of stability and invariance properties.
- Strength: introduces a novel framework combining kernel methods and signal processing to study deep representations.
- Strength: results are significant for designing robust, invariant deep neural networks in vision and ML.
- Weakness: some sections are hard to follow for non-experts in kernel methods and signal processing.
- Weakness: scope is primarily theoretical with limited experimental validation or empirical comparisons.
- Pro: rigorous, original contribution with important implications and clear organization.
- Con: accessibility issues for non-experts and insufficient experiments or comparative evaluation.
- Recommendation: accept, with a suggestion to add more experimental results or comparisons to strengthen impact.
