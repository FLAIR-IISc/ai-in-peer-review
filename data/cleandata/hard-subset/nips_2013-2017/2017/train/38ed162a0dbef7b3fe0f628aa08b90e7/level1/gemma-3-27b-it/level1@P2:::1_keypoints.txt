- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations via positive definite kernels, generalizing CKNs and analyzing RKHS geometry.
- Well-motivated: tackles understanding geometry of deep nets and extends analysis beyond translations to other group actions.
- Strong theoretical foundation using kernel methods and harmonic analysis with rigorous derivations.
- Generalizes scattering-transform results to a broader class of CNNs, increasing theoretical applicability.
- Establishes a clear connection between the kernel analysis and practical CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
- Provides theoretical analysis of stability to diffeomorphisms and invariance to group actions.
- Paper is well-structured, comprehensive, and logically organized.
- Mathematical formalism is dense and may be challenging for readers without background in kernels/harmonic analysis.
- Results rely on assumptions (smooth activations, regular diffeomorphisms) with limited discussion of violations or robustness.
- Practical implications for CNN design and training are not fully developed; RKHS regularization link is underexplored.
- Limited empirical validation; lacks experiments to demonstrate practical relevance of theoretical claims.
- Kernel approximation and implementation details are treated superficially; effects on stability are unclear.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical contribution, generalization of prior work, and relevance to practical CNNs.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to complexity, insufficient empirical support, and reliance on assumptions.
- Overall assessment: strong accept recommendation with minor revisions to improve clarity and add empirical or design-focused discussion.
