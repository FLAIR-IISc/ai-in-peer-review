- Presents a theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations' invariance to transformations and stability to small deformations.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernel construction from CKNs to continuous-domain signals via patch extraction, kernel mapping, and linear pooling.  
- Main contribution: proof that the representation is stable to C1-diffeomorphisms, adapting techniques from Mallat's scattering transform.  
- Derives stability bounds showing near-translation invariance that improves with network depth and pooling scale.  
- Extends the framework to achieve global invariance to arbitrary compact groups.  
- Establishes a formal link between the kernel representation and a class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations, letting RKHS norm control stability.  
- High technical quality: rigorous mathematical framework and logically sound proofs provided in the appendix.  
- Assumptions for the main theorem (norm preservation, non-expansiveness, relation between patch size and pooling) are clearly stated.  
- Clear writing and organization, with consistent notation and helpful positioning relative to CKNs and scattering transforms.  
- Provides a step-by-step stability proof and discusses practical considerations like discretization and subsampling.  
- Original in extending Mallat-style stability from wavelets to a learnable kernel-based multilayer RKHS and embedding a family of CNNs in that RKHS.  
- Significant theoretical contribution linking RKHS norm, stability to deformations, and implications for regularization and generalization.  
- Strength: rigorous stability proof for diffeomorphisms and near-translation invariance across a broad class of convolutional representations.  
- Strength: bridges fixed-filter scattering models and learnable CNNs, showing similar stability properties can hold.  
- Strength: formal connection between RKHS norm and network stability, suggesting principled regularization insights.  
- Weakness: CNN class in RKHS is restrictive, requiring smooth, homogeneous activations and a nonstandard normalization term.  
- Weakness: entirely theoretical with no empirical validation measuring stability or correlating RKHS norm with generalization.  
- Weakness: pooling breaks norm preservation, potentially causing information loss with depth, which is not fully explored.  
- Overall: a strong, rigorous theoretical paper that generalizes stability analysis to a kernel framework including certain learnable architectures, despite limits on applicability and lack of experiments.
