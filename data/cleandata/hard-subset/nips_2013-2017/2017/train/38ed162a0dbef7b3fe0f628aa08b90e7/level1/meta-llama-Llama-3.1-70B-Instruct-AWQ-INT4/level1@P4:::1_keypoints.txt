- Studies invariance and stability of deep convolutional representations via RKHS geometry.
- Proposes a multilayer kernel framework generalizing CKNs and scattering networks.
- Shows the framework contains a large class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.
- Provides a theoretical characterization of invariance and stability properties.
- Establishes a link: the RKHS from the kernel construction contains continuous-domain CNNs.
- Well-written and well-organized with a clear structure and thorough related-work review.
- Technical results are clearly presented, with detailed proofs in the appendix.
- Offers a new perspective connecting kernel methods and deep learning; framework is novel.
- Results are applicable to a wide range of models, including CNNs and CKNs.
- Assumes high mathematical sophistication, limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Relies on a specific kernel construction that may not apply to all deep learning models.
- Does not provide empirical results to support the theoretical claims.
