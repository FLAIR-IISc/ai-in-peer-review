- Main contribution: extends stability and invariance guarantees from scattering transforms to a broader class of CNNs via a multilayer kernel / RKHS framework.  
- Key technical result: shows the RKHS contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, enabling provable stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Builds on prior work: unifies scattering transform and convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) with CNN theory.  
- Rigorous theory: provides careful proofs (e.g., Lemma 1, Proposition 3, Theorem 6) and separates theoretical guarantees from implementation details.  
- Clarity and exposition: well-structured, consistent notation, and clear explanations linking abstract theory to practical CNNs.  
- Originality: novel insight that many CNNs lie in the constructed RKHS and that RKHS norm controls stability.  
- Significance: connects stability to generalization, suggesting low RKHS norm representations may need fewer samples and informing regularization.  
- Practical limitation 1: results are developed for continuous signals while implementations are discrete; translation to discrete settings is not fully addressed.  
- Practical limitation 2: framework requires smooth homogeneous activations, excluding standard non-smooth ReLU and limiting immediate applicability.  
- Empirical gap: lacks experimental validation on real datasets to demonstrate practical relevance of theoretical results.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant theoretical advance, unifying framework, rigorous math, and implications for understanding CNN performance.  
- Rejection concerns: limited to smooth activations, incomplete treatment of discretization, and no empirical evidence.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with some practical limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance.
