- Paper generalizes multilayer kernel construction from CKNs and links it to RKHS geometry to study invariance and stability of deep convolutional architectures.  
- Extends scattering-transform stability and invariance results to a broader class of CNNs via a kernel framework.  
- Well-motivated connection to kernel methods offers a principled analytical framework.  
- Includes a practical discussion of discretization effects on theoretical results.  

Strengths:
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis grounded in RKHS and harmonic analysis.  
- Novel extension of scattering-transform-like results to more general CNNs.  
- Generally clear writing, notation, and logical structure aid comprehension.  
- Results are significant for understanding regularization, generalization, and robustness of CNNs.  

Weaknesses:
- Mathematical formalism is dense and may limit accessibility to non-experts.  
- Practical implications and concrete guidance for CNN design/training are underdeveloped.  
- Relies on assumptions (e.g., smoothness and homogeneity of activations) without extensive sensitivity analysis.  
- Lacks empirical validation to demonstrate practical relevance.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Significant theoretical advance extending known results to broader CNN classes.  
- Principled, rigorous kernel-based framework and clear presentation.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- High mathematical complexity may reduce accessibility and impact.  
- Absence of experiments makes practical impact uncertain; architectural connections are thin.  

Overall assessment / recommendation:
- Strong theoretical contribution worth accepting with minor revisions.  
- Recommend improving accessibility, adding discussion/avenues for empirical validation, and clarifying which CNN architectures directly benefit.
