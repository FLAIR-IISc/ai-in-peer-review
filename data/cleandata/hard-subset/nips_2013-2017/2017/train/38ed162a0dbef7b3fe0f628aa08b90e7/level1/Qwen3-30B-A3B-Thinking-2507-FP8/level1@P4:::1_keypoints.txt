- Paper analyzes invariance to transformation groups and stability to diffeomorphisms for deep convolutional representations.  
- Generalizes multilayer kernel framework from convolutional kernel networks and studies the RKHS geometry of the representations.  
- Proves stability guarantees showing representations preserve signal information while remaining stable like the scattering transform.  
- Bridges scattering transform stability analysis and standard CNNs via a kernel framework, extending stability to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous lemmas and theorems (notably Theorem 6) and proofs in appendices.  
- Clearly distinguishes continuous theoretical constructs from discrete/practical implementations and addresses discretization and kernel approximation.  
- Transparent about limitations, including lack of norm preservation compared to scattering transforms and how this might be mitigated.  
- Connects RKHS norms to generalization, offering a theoretical explanation for why some CNNs generalize well.  
- Well-structured and precise mathematically, with adequate background for ML readers though notation density may challenge non-specialists.  
- Original contribution by extending scattering ideas to a broader class of convolutional architectures and generalizing group invariance.  
- Novel link between homogeneous activation functions and RKHS membership not previously established.  
- Significant for advancing theoretical understanding of CNN geometry and informing stability-aware regularization and architecture design.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory for standard CNNs, kernel–CNN connection, careful continuous-to-discrete treatment, and comprehensive invariance/stability analysis.  
- Weaknesses: dense mathematics limits accessibility, excludes some activations (e.g., ReLU) due to smoothness/homogeneity assumptions, limited practical implementation guidance, and no empirical validation.
