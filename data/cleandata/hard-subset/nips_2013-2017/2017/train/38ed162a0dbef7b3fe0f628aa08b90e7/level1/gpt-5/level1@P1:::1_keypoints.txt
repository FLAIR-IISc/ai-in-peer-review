- Introduces a multilayer convolutional RKHS representation for continuous signals, generalizing CKNs with patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernels, and Gaussian pooling.  
- Proves recoverability of signals from samples under coverage assumptions; gives a discrete counterpart including subsampling and kernel approximation.  
- Main theoretical result: Lipschitz-type stability to diffeomorphisms with bound ≲ C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞/σn, yielding near-translation invariance as final pooling scale σn grows.  
- Analysis extends Mallat’s scattering techniques (commutator bounds, Schur’s test) to a broader class of multi-layer convolutional RKHS representations beyond wavelets.  
- Shows global invariance to general locally compact groups G via group-covariant patching and pooling.  
- Proves a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations and patch-norm normalization lie in the RKHS, so the RKHS norm controls their stability and generalization.  
- Situates work relative to scattering networks, CKNs, group-equivariant CNNs, invariant kernels, and kernel perspectives on deep nets; novelty is the unified RKHS stability/invariance framework.  
- Framework and assumptions are clear; Theorem 6 is technically sound under (A1–A3), initial anti-aliasing, and ‖∇τ‖∞<1/2.  
- Commutator analysis is carefully adapted to shifted pooling operators, extending prior scattering proofs.  
- Discretization lemmas (signal recovery with subsampling) are plausible but depend on stable deconvolution of Gaussian pooling and patch coverage; numerical stability conditions need more explicit statement.  
- Kernel approximation via finite-dimensional projections preserves non-expansiveness, so stability holds up to approximation error—practically relevant for CKNs.  
- Inclusion of CNNs into the RKHS is interesting but depends on smooth homogeneous activations and nonstandard patch-norm normalization; constants Cσ may be large and need concrete bounds.  
- No empirical validation is provided; even small experiments on stability to synthetic deformations would strengthen claims.  
- Paper is dense but well organized; layer operators (P, M, A) and continuous/discrete parameter relations are clearly presented.  
- Suggests adding explicit examples of admissible kernels and corresponding Cσ, clarifying scope of CNNs covered, and discussing magnitudes of constants C1, C2 and dependence on kernel/dimension.  
- Minor clarity fixes: explicitly state overloaded norm notation and include brief proof sketches from appendices in main text.  
- Originality: extends scattering stability to kernel-defined convolutional hierarchies, treats non-wavelet layers and shifted pooling, and novelly ties CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations to the RKHS.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of invariance/stability beyond scattering and links RKHS norm to generalization; practical impact would increase with experiments and clearer applicability to common CNNs.  
- Pros: rigorous stability theorem with interpretable dependence, unifying RKHS framework covering CKNs and some CNNs, and extension to general group actions.  
- Cons: requires smooth homogeneous activations and patch-norm normalization (limits applicability to standard ReLU CNNs), relies on deconvolution stability without detailed numerical conditions, lacks experiments, and bounds scale linearly with depth (1+n) with limited discussion on deep nets.  
- Questions for authors: provide explicit Cσ for inverse polynomial/Gaussian kernels with smoothed ReLU and their magnitudes for realistic filters.  
- Questions for authors: assess sensitivity of stability bounds to replacing nk(u)=‖Pkxk−1(u)‖ with batch/layer normalization or omitting normalization.  
- Questions for authors: clarify if stability extends qualitatively to ReLU via approximation and what is lost.  
- Questions for authors: report simple experiments comparing stability of CKNs/CNNs under controlled deformations and varying σn.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward accept for a solid theoretical contribution generalizing scattering stability to a broader convolutional RKHS framework and connecting to CNNs, but needs empirical illustrations and clearer constant quantification.
