This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional representations, focusing on invariance and stability, through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors generalize convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and study the geometry of the corresponding reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). They aim to provide theoretical guarantees for invariance to transformations and stability to diffeomorphisms, similar to those achieved by scattering transforms, but for a broader class of CNN architectures.

The paper is well-motivated. Understanding the underlying geometry of deep networks is a crucial open problem, and achieving invariance and stability are desirable properties for robust and generalizable models. The connection to CKNs and scattering transforms provides a solid foundation and allows the authors to build upon existing theoretical work. The exploration of group invariance beyond translations is also a valuable contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper leverages a solid mathematical framework based on kernel methods and harmonic analysis. The derivations appear rigorous and well-presented (though detailed proofs are relegated to the appendix, as is standard).
* **Generalization of Existing Work:** Extending the theoretical results from scattering transforms to a wider class of CNNs, including CKNs, is a significant achievement. This broadens the applicability of these theoretical insights.
* **Clear Connection to Practical CNNs:** The authors establish a link between their kernel-based analysis and standard CNN architectures with smooth homogeneous activation functions, making the results more relevant to practitioners.
* **Stability and Invariance Analysis:** The analysis of stability to diffeomorphisms and invariance to group actions provides a valuable theoretical understanding of these crucial properties of deep representations.
* **Well-structured and Comprehensive:** The paper is logically organized, covering the necessary background, kernel construction, theoretical analysis, and discussion of connections to CNNs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The mathematical formalism is quite dense and may be challenging for readers not familiar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis. While the authors do a good job of explaining the concepts, a higher-level overview or intuitive explanation might be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:** The results rely on several assumptions, such as smoothness of activation functions and regularity of diffeomorphisms. The impact of violating these assumptions is not fully discussed. While standard, justifying these choices more thoroughly would add to the paper’s strength.
* **Practical Implications:** While the paper provides theoretical guarantees, the practical implications for designing or training CNNs are not fully explored. It's unclear how these insights can be directly translated into improved algorithms or architectures. The link to regularization through RKHS norm control is mentioned, but could be expanded upon.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical analysis. Empirical validation, even on a small scale, would strengthen the claims and demonstrate the practical relevance of the results.
* **Kernel Approximation Details:** While the discussion of approximating the kernel is important for practical implementation, it's somewhat glossed over. More detail on the approximation schemes and their impact on stability would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of deep convolutional representations, building upon existing work in kernel methods and harmonic analysis.
* It generalizes important theoretical results to a broader class of CNNs, increasing their applicability.
* The connection to practical CNN architectures and the discussion of invariance and stability properties are valuable contributions to the field.
* The paper is well-written and logically organized, demonstrating a deep understanding of the underlying concepts.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The mathematical complexity of the paper may limit its accessibility to a wider audience.
* The practical implications of the results are not fully explored, and empirical validation is lacking.
* The reliance on several assumptions requires further justification.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. While the mathematical complexity and limited empirical validation are weaknesses, the theoretical contributions are significant and the paper addresses a fundamental open problem in deep learning. The rigorous analysis, generalization of existing work, and clear connection to practical CNNs make this a valuable contribution to the NIPS community. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the underlying concepts and present a compelling argument for the importance of their work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to potentially improve clarity and explore practical implications further. Specifically, I suggest adding a small-scale empirical test or a more detailed discussion of how the results could inform CNN design choices.
