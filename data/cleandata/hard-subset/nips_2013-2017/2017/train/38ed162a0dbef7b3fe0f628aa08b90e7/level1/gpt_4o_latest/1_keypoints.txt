- Paper proposes a rigorous RKHS-based theoretical framework for stability and invariance of deep signal representations, generalizing CKNs to continuous domains and linking to CNNs.

Strengths:
- Provides strong theoretical foundation and stability results for CNNs/CKNs, extending scattering-transform analyses to diffeomorphisms.
- Novel kernel-based perspective that unifies kernel methods and deep architectures, including RKHS generalization and links to smooth homogeneous activations.
- Mathematically rigorous with detailed proofs; uses RKHS norms to control stability and generalization.
- Addresses practical implementation via discretization and kernel approximations, connecting theory to CKNs.

Weaknesses:
- Dense, technical exposition may be inaccessible to readers lacking harmonic analysis or kernel-methods background.
- No empirical experiments to validate theoretical claims or demonstrate practical benefits.
- Limited exploration of practical implications of pooling-induced signal energy reduction.
- Results assume smooth activations, excluding widely used non-smooth functions like ReLU, limiting applicability.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Significant theoretical advance extending scattering frameworks to general convolutional architectures with stability guarantees.
- Kernel perspective is novel and likely to stimulate further research at the kernel–deep learning interface.
- High mathematical rigor and depth.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Lack of experimental validation undermines practical relevance.
- Dense presentation hurts accessibility for broader NIPS audience.
- Exclusion of non-smooth activations (e.g., ReLU) reduces generality.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions: add empirical results or clearer intuition/visuals to improve accessibility and demonstrate practical utility.
