- Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel whose RKHS contains a broad class of CNNs (including CKNs) and analyzes their properties.
- Proves Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms for the kernel representation, analogous to scattering transform stability.
- Links stability, RKHS norm, and generalization/sample complexity to provide a framework connecting representation stability and learning performance.
- Extends scattering-transform stability analysis to broader CNN architectures and builds on convolutional kernel networks.
- Positions work within kernel–deep learning literature and relates to group invariance studies in CNNs.
- Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation helping to explain why CNNs work.
- Uses a kernel framework to separate data representation from prediction and leverage kernel-theoretic tools.
- Demonstrates generality of stability results across many CNN architectures beyond CKNs.
- Establishes a potential connection between stable representations and improved sample complexity/generalization.
- Well-organized manuscript with detailed mathematical derivations, lemmas, and proofs.
- Offers a clear discussion on discretization and approximating the continuous model in practice.
- Mathematical formalism is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with kernels/harmonic analysis.
- Lacks concrete practical examples or guidance for improving CNN design/training based on the theory.
- Limited empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact of the stability results.
- Stability results depend on assumptions (diffeomorphism regularity, kernel properties); impact of violations is under-discussed.
- Representation does not preserve norm (unlike scattering transform), which may be a drawback in some applications.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, novel kernel perspective, potential to inform robust CNN design, and strong exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: primarily theoretical with limited immediate practical impact, sparse empirical validation, and potentially inaccessible math.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with a suggestion to add discussion of practical implications and future directions.
- Review rating: Strong Accept (7/10); reviewer confidence: 8/10.
