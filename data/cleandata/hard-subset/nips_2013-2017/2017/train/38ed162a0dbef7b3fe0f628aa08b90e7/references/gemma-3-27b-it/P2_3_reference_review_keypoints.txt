- Constructs a multilayer positive-definite convolutional kernel that encompasses a broad class of CNNs, including CKNs.  
- Demonstrates translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphic deformations comparable to the scattering transform while allowing learned filters.  
- Connects stability guarantees to potential improvements in generalization via RKHS norm control.  
- Builds on CKNs and scattering transform but generalizes results to standard, learned-filter CNN architectures.  
- Appropriately cites related work in kernel methods, group invariance, and adversarial robustness.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation using RKHS and harmonic analysis.  
- Strength: effectively bridges kernel-method guarantees and practical deep learning behavior.  
- Strength: framework is general and applicable to many CNN architectures.  
- Strength: clear structure and mathematical rigor, with proofs promised in the appendix.  
- Strength: novel extension of stability and invariance analysis to learned filters/CNNs.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation demonstrating theoretical claims on real datasets.  
- Weakness: dense mathematical formalism may be hard to access without more intuitive explanations or examples.  
- Weakness: assumptions (smoothness of activations, deformation regularity) may be restrictive and need deeper discussion.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of computational costs and scalability of discretization/approximation methods.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and complete within the defined framework (strong).  
- Clarity assessment: well-organized but notation and derivations are dense (good).  
- Originality assessment: novel combination of kernel techniques applied to learned CNN stability (strong).  
- Significance assessment: potentially impactful for understanding CNNs and robustness but hampered by lack of experiments (good).  
- Pro arguments: strong theory, extends prior work, offers generalization insights, well-written.  
- Con arguments: no empirical validation, mathematical complexity, possibly restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with suggestions for empirical validation and clearer, more intuitive exposition.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
