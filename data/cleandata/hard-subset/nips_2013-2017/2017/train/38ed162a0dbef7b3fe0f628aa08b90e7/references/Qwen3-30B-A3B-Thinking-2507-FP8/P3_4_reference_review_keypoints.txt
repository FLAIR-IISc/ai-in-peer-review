- Bridges scattering transforms and standard CNNs via a multilayer convolutional kernel, showing the RKHS contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Main theoretical result: establishes stability to diffeomorphisms and translation invariance for this class, with the RKHS norm controlling both stability and generalization.  
- Provides a rigorous, well-motivated theoretical framework with precise definitions of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling under justified assumptions (A1–A3).  
- Thoroughly situates the work relative to scattering transforms, convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), and group invariance literature.  
- Practical/discretization gap: results are proved for continuous signals with only brief and insufficient connection to discrete implementations used in practice.  
- Activation limitation: framework requires smooth homogeneous activations, excluding standard ReLU (only a smoothed variant is mentioned).  
- No empirical validation: entirely theoretical paper lacking experiments to demonstrate stability claims on real CNNs.  
- Strong deformation assumption: stability bound requires ||∇τ||∞ ≤ 1/2, which may be violated by natural image deformations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and valuable theoretical contribution that links RKHS norm, stability, and generalization, but with limited practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept—significant theoretical merit despite limitations common in theoretical deep learning work.  
- Pro arguments: first general stability guarantees for standard CNN architectures, principled kernel–CNN connection, theoretical link between stability and generalization, clear exposition.  
- Con arguments: limited to continuous domains, excludes ReLU, and lacks empirical validation.
