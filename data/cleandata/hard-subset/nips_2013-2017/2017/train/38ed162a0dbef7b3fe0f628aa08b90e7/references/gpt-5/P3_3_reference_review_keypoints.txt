- Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel framework mirroring CNNs with patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernel maps into RKHS, and Gaussian pooling.  
- Proves stability to C1-diffeomorphisms with a Mallat-style Lipschitz bound depending on ∥∇τ∥∞, ∥τ∥∞, and last pooling scale σn.  
- Shows discretized and sub-sampled versions preserve the signal under mild conditions.  
- Demonstrates finite-dimensional CKN approximations preserve non-expansiveness and thus stability.  
- Extends construction to global invariance via equivariant patch extraction and pooling on groups.  
- Embeds a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS and gives a recursive RKHS-norm bound linking stability and generalization.  
- Positions work between scattering networks and convolutional kernel networks, borrowing scattering harmonic-analysis techniques and CKN design.  
- Claims greater architectural generality than scattering (learnable filters, broader homogeneous activations) but loses strict energy conservation.  
- Provides an RKHS-centered derivation of equivariance/invariance related to group-equivariant CNNs.  
- Strength: clear, principled construction subsuming CKNs and containing a nontrivial CNN class with coherent use of norm-preserving/non-expansive properties.  
- Strength: strong diffeomorphism stability bound matching scattering-style guarantees while allowing more flexible architectures.  
- Strength: careful treatment of discretization and kernel approximation, noting projection preserves non-expansiveness.  
- Strength: conceptual link between invariance/stability and generalization via constructive norm bounds (Proposition 7).  
- Strength: extension to general transformation groups is conceptually clean.  
- Weakness: inclusion of CNNs requires smooth homogeneous activations and a nonstandard nk(u)=‖patch‖ normalization, diverging from typical ReLU/BatchNorm/bias/strided/MaxPool practice.  
- Weakness: constants C1, C2 and their dependence on dimension/κ are abstract; linear growth in depth n may be pessimistic and tightness is unclear.  
- Weakness: discretization/recovery relies on delicate stable deconvolution conditions (ek ≥ sk), which are sensitive to noise and boundaries and lack quantified conditioning.  
- Weakness: generalization connection remains qualitative; recursive norm bound can blow up with depth and no explicit Rademacher/covering bounds are given.  
- Weakness: group invariance section omits diffeomorphism-type stability for non-translation groups and lacks discussion of discretization and computational cost on noncommutative groups.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation of stability predictions, σn’s role, or practical relevance of RKHS norm bounds; small-scale experiments would help.  
- Presentation: generally clear but dense; suggest bringing concrete kernel examples and restating assumption (A3) in terms of discrete hyperparameters and a satisfying schedule.  
- Presentation: request to define nk(u) when zero, summarize deconvolution stability conditions, add a figure contrasting the architecture with scattering and standard CNNs, and fix minor typos.  
- Questions for authors: quantify C1, C2 numerically in 2D for Gaussian h/typical κ and required σn for ε-invariance; derive explicit Rademacher-style generalization bounds under norm constraints.  
- Questions for authors: explain adaptation to strided convolutions and max pooling, and provide empirical evidence that smoothed-ReLU with nk-normalization matches ReLU CNN behavior and predicted stability.  
- Recommendation: solid theoretical contribution extending scattering-style stability to a broader kernel/CNN framework; lean toward acceptance pending clarifications, quantification of constants, and better practical guidance.
