This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels, aiming to provide a more principled understanding of invariance and stability. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel and demonstrate connections to both convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and the scattering transform. They establish stability results under diffeomorphic deformations, relating the stability to the norm in the associated Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS).

The core idea – using a kernel framework to analyze CNNs – is interesting and potentially valuable. The paper is generally well-structured, with a clear progression from introducing the kernel construction to analyzing its properties. The authors make a substantial effort to connect their work to existing literature, citing relevant papers from the kernel methods, CNN, and scattering transform communities.

However, despite the interesting premise and technical depth, I find the paper ultimately falls short of NIPS acceptance due to a multitude of minor but persistent issues and a perceived disconnect between theoretical rigor and practical relevance. My concerns are detailed below, and can be broadly categorized into clarity, rigor, and significance.

**Clarity & Presentation:**

*   **Notation:** The paper suffers from inconsistent notation. For example, the meaning of “‖ · ‖” shifts frequently between Hilbert norms, L2 norms, and operator norms without sufficient clarification. This makes following the derivations challenging.  A clear table of notation would be beneficial.
*   **Assumptions:** Several key assumptions (e.g., A1-A3) are stated without sufficient motivation or explanation of their implications. Why are these specific assumptions necessary? Are there alternative assumptions that would yield similar results? The text simply states they *are* assumed, rather than *why* they are reasonable.
*   **Discretization:** The discussion of discretization (Section 2.1) feels rushed and somewhat hand-wavy. The connection between the continuous and discrete models is not entirely convincing, and the implications of the sampling process are not fully explored. Specifically, Lemma 2's requirement of `ek ≥ sk` feels arbitrary and deserves more justification.
*   **Kernel Examples:** While Appendix B mentions concrete examples of kernels, they are not thoroughly discussed in the main text. Providing more details about how these kernels would be applied in practice would increase clarity.
*   **Figure 1:** The figure is referenced frequently but provides limited information. A more informative diagram showing the flow of data and the operators at each layer would be valuable.

**Rigor & Technical Details:**

*   **Proof Sketches:**  Proofs are relegated to the appendix, which is standard. However, the proofs themselves often lack sufficient detail.  For example, Proposition 3 relies on Lemma 4 and 5, but the connection between the commutator norms and the stability results isn't explicitly stated.
*   **Lemma 4 & Schur’s Test:** The application of Schur’s test in the proof of Lemma 4 is not sufficiently justified. The authors state the result without explicitly demonstrating that the required conditions for applying the test are met.
*   **Homogeneous Activations:** The claim that "most successful activation function, namely rectified linear units, is homogeneous" is misleading. While ReLU *can* be expressed in a homogeneous form, it's not its standard representation and obscures its properties. The link between homogeneous activations and the RKHS is not fully clear.
*   **Proposition 7:** The conditions on σ in Proposition 7 are stated without elaboration.  What are the implications of violating these conditions?
*   **Lack of Empirical Validation:** A major weakness is the complete lack of empirical evaluation. The paper focuses entirely on theoretical results. Demonstrating the stability properties on a small set of CNN architectures and datasets would significantly strengthen the work.

**Significance & Originality:**

*   **Theoretical Focus:** While the theoretical analysis is rigorous, it feels somewhat detached from practical CNN design and training. The results are presented as applying to CNNs, but the connection is through the somewhat restrictive requirement of fitting into the constructed RKHS.
*   **Incremental Contribution:** While the paper extends previous work on kernel methods and scattering transforms, the contribution feels incremental. The results largely parallel those obtained for scattering transforms, with the primary novelty being the extension to a broader class of CNNs. The practical impact of this generalization is unclear.
*   **Limited Novelty:** The application of kernel methods to analyze CNNs is not entirely new (see references [2, 10, 18]), and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage over existing approaches.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of CNN stability.
*   The kernel framework provides a potentially useful tool for understanding CNN behavior.
*   The connection to scattering transforms and CKNs is well-established.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper suffers from numerous clarity and rigor issues.
*   The lack of empirical validation is a significant weakness.
*   The contribution feels incremental and lacks a clear practical impact.
*   The restrictive requirements for CNNs to fit within the RKHS limit the applicability of the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **rejection**. While the paper presents a technically sound analysis, the numerous minor flaws, lack of empirical validation, and limited practical significance outweigh the positive aspects. The paper would benefit significantly from a thorough revision addressing the clarity and rigor concerns, as well as the inclusion of empirical results demonstrating the applicability of the theoretical findings.  A more compelling narrative connecting the theoretical results to practical CNN design would also improve the paper's overall impact. The attention to detail, while commendable, ultimately overshadows the broader contributions of this work.
