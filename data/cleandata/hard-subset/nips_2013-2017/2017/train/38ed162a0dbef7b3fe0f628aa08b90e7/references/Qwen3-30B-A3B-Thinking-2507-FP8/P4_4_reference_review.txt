

The paper proposes a kernel-based framework to analyze invariance and stability properties of convolutional neural networks (CNNs). It generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to a continuous domain, demonstrating that this framework captures both CKNs and CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions. The authors prove stability to diffeomorphisms (small deformations) for their kernel representation, similar to the scattering transform but with a different theoretical foundation. They establish how stability properties translate to predictive models through the RKHS norm, which controls both stability and generalization. The paper also connects this framework to standard CNNs, showing their theoretical results apply to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.

Quality: The paper presents a technically sound theoretical framework with rigorous proofs. The mathematical arguments are well-developed, and the authors carefully address both strengths and weaknesses of their approach. The connection between the kernel framework and practical CNNs is well-established. However, the paper lacks empirical validation of the theoretical results, which would strengthen the claims about practical implications.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and logically organized, with clear notation and definitions. The mathematical content is presented at an appropriate level for the target audience, though some sections may be challenging for readers without a strong background in functional analysis. The connection to practical implementation (Sections 2.1 and 2.2) effectively bridges theoretical framework with real-world applications.

Originality: The paper builds on previous work on invariance (scattering transform) and CKNs, but provides a novel kernel-based framework that generalizes these approaches. The key novelty lies in using kernel methods to study invariance and stability properties of CNNs in a more general setting and demonstrating how these properties apply to standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations. The paper makes a clear contribution to the theoretical understanding of CNNs, which has been a gap in the literature.

Significance: The paper provides valuable theoretical insights that could inform better regularization techniques for CNNs, potentially improving sample complexity. The connection between stability, generalization, and RKHS norm is significant for understanding how CNNs generalize. The work bridges the gap between theoretical analysis and practical deep learning architectures, which is valuable for the field. The results could help practitioners design more robust and stable CNNs.

Strengths:
- Theoretical framework is rigorous and well-developed
- Clear connection between kernel framework and standard CNNs
- Establishes meaningful links between stability, generalization, and RKHS norm
- Provides a principled way to study invariance properties of CNNs

Weaknesses:
- Lacks empirical validation of theoretical claims
- Limited to smooth homogeneous activation functions, excluding widely used ReLU
- High technical barrier may limit accessibility to broader community
- Does not address how to practically implement stability properties during training

Pro arguments for acceptance: The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to understanding CNNs, which has been a topic of limited theoretical work. The framework generalizes and extends previous work on invariance and stability. The connection to practical CNNs is well-established and meaningful. The work has clear implications for improving regularization techniques in deep learning.

Con arguments against acceptance: The lack of empirical validation weakens the practical relevance of the theoretical results. The limitation to smooth activation functions excludes widely used architectures. The high technical complexity may make the paper less accessible to many readers in the NIPS community.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of CNNs. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and makes a clear contribution to the field. While the lack of empirical validation is a limitation, the theoretical insights are valuable and could guide future work in the area. The connection to practical CNNs is particularly strong and meaningful. The paper addresses an important gap in the theoretical understanding of CNNs and provides a principled framework for studying their invariance properties.
