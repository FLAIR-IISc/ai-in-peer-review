- Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that mirrors CNNs in RKHS using patch extraction, homogeneous dot-product kernel maps, and Gaussian pooling.
- Shows continuous-domain stability to small C1 diffeomorphisms with a Lipschitz-type bound and increased translation-invariance as final pooling scale grows.
- Extends scattering-transform analysis beyond wavelets to generic homogeneous kernels and connects to Convolutional Kernel Networks (CKNs).
- Constructs group-covariant patching and pooling to obtain global invariance to compact groups.
- Addresses discretization via sampling/subsampling with information-preservation lemmas under mild conditions.
- Proves that CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS with explicit recursive norm bounds, linking stability and generalization to RKHS norms.
- Argues that practical finite-dimensional kernel approximations (CKNs) preserve stability up to approximation error.
- Builds on Mallat’s scattering and CKN literature, and relates to group-equivariant CNN work (Cohen & Welling).
- Main stability bound (Theorem 6) is carefully derived using operator decomposition and novel commutator bounds for shifted pooling.
- Group invariance construction is correct and grounded in harmonic analysis on groups.
- Assumes an initial low-pass A0, smooth homogeneous activations (excludes ReLU), and patch sizes proportional to pooling scales (A3).
- Discrete signal preservation relies on deconvolution with a Gaussian and ek ≥ sk; conditioning and noise sensitivity of deconvolution are not analyzed.
- Paper is entirely theoretical with no experiments to validate constants, trade-offs, or RKHS norm implications for generalization.
- Proofs are in the appendix; main claims appear supported though constants and tightness are not fully explored.
- Paper is well organized and readable, with clear continuous/discrete correspondence and transparent role of pooling scale.
- Requests more intuition on deconvolution conditioning, concrete kernel examples/Cσ(a) table, and practical effect of homogenization nk(u).
- Recommends a concrete group example (e.g., SE(2)) with precise pooling kernel and sampling details.
- Originality: novel extension of scattering-style stability to broader kernels, shifted pooling commutator bounds, and a multilayer CNN–RKHS connection.
- Significance: provides principled conditions for stability and invariance, unifies CNNs/CKNs in an RKHS, and informs architectural/regularization choices.
- Pros: general stability bound, extension to shifted Gaussian pooling, discrete-continuous bridge, group-equivariant construction, CNN–RKHS inclusion with norm bounds.
- Cons: no empirical validation, restrictive smooth-homogeneous activation assumption (ReLU excluded), nonstandard homogenization factor, linear-in-depth stability growth and constant dependence, and potential ill-conditioning of deconvolution.
- Suggested improvements: add small experiments, quantify constants for typical settings, bound approximation errors, discuss RKHS-regularization for CNNs, provide SE(2) example, and analyze deconvolution robustness.
- Recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with novelty and rigor; acceptance warranted as a theory paper despite limitations.
