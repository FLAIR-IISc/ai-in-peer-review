- Presents a multilayer convolutional kernel Φn combining patch extraction, pointwise RKHS mappings, and Gaussian pooling, inducing a positive definite kernel whose RKHS contains linear predictors on final features.

- Shows signal preservation: under mild RKHS assumptions, previous layers can be recovered from sampled versions of current layers; discrete subsampling with patch size > stride still allows recovery.

- Proves stability to C1-diffeomorphisms and increasing translation invariance with final pooling scale σn, with explicit bound ‖Φ(Lτx)−Φ(x)‖ ≤ (C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + C2‖τ‖∞/σn)‖x‖.

- Establishes group equivariance/invariance by defining patch extraction and pooling over a locally compact group with Haar measure, enabling intermediate equivariance and global invariance via final pooling.

- Links the RKHS to a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations and gives recursive upper bounds on CNN RKHS norms (Proposition 7), relating norm control to Lipschitz stability and generalization.

- Treats discretization explicitly and shows finite-dimensional approximations (CKNs) via projections preserve non-expansiveness and stability bounds.

- Extends Mallat’s scattering analysis beyond fixed wavelet filters to learned kernel-induced multilayer maps while retaining similar Lipschitz stability properties.

- Builds on CKN literature by providing formal stability guarantees and a principled connection to CNNs; relates to group-equivariant CNNs and invariant kernel literature.

- Conceptual unification: clean RKHS framework capturing CKNs and a broad class of CNNs under clear assumptions (A1–A3).

- Rigorous stability analysis using operator commutators with transparent dependence on deformation magnitude, depth, and pooling scale.

- Group-theoretic generality offers a modular route to G-equivariance and global invariance via Haar-measure pooling.

- Practical relevance: addresses discretization and finite-dimensional approximations, preserving key stability properties.

- Provides an insightful theoretical link between stability and generalization via RKHS norms, suggesting design guidance for controlling stability.

- Limitation: requires smooth homogeneous activations, excluding standard nonsmooth ReLU and common components like biases and batch normalization without extra treatment.

- Limitation: assumes Gaussian linear pooling; does not analyze max-pooling or learned nonlinear pooling and only partially addresses strides/aliasing.

- Potential looseness: constants in stability bounds may be conservative (growth with κd+1 and linear in depth) with no empirical tightness evaluation.

- Continuous-domain emphasis leaves boundary conditions, finite-support effects, padding, dilations, and residual connections not fully addressed.

- Largely theoretical: lacks empirical validation comparing theoretical predictions (e.g., effects of σn or depth) in the main text.

- Technical quality: proofs are solid, assumptions explicit, and links to CNNs are carefully constructed with attention to normalization factors.

- Clarity: manuscript is generally clear and well organized; operator viewpoint and commutator analysis are easy to follow.

- Suggested clarifications: provide intuition and a concrete sampling scheme for Lemma 1’s condition, and clarify stability/conditioning of deconvolution in Lemma 2 relative to σk and stride.

- Suggested clarifications: summarize homogeneous kernels satisfying (A1–A2) and key properties of Cσ used in Proposition 7; discuss how biases or batch norm affect RKHS inclusion.

- Open questions: sensitivity of constants C1 and C2 to h and κ and scaling with depth/dimension; whether max or learned pooling can be incorporated while preserving stability.

- Open questions: whether Proposition 7 extends to biases and residual connections and guidance for choosing σk relative to stride for stable deconvolution.

- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution that unifies and extends invariance and stability analyses for deep convolutional representations, with minor clarifications needed.
