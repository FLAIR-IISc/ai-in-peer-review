- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes CKNs and can represent many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Proves translation-invariance and stability under diffeomorphisms, linking these properties to the RKHS norm.  
- Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, suggesting principled regularization strategies.  
- Builds on scattering transform theory but extends guarantees to more standard CNN architectures within a kernel framework.  
- Presents technically rigorous results with careful definitions, assumptions (A1–A3), and matching stability bounds.  
- Addresses discretization and kernel approximation to connect theory to practical implementations like CKNs.  
- Novel contribution by bridging kernel methods and CNNs, unifying previous approaches to invariance.  
- Theoretical significance: potential implications for understanding CNN performance and improving sample complexity.  
- Clarity issue: dense mathematical notation and limited exposition linking continuous and discrete representations.  
- Scope limitation: no empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact of stability results.  
- Assumptions concern: some strong/restrictive conditions (e.g., ‖∇τ‖∞ ≤ 1/2, specific kernel choices) and pooling’s effect on norm not fully explored.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revision: add brief empirical validation to demonstrate practical relevance.
