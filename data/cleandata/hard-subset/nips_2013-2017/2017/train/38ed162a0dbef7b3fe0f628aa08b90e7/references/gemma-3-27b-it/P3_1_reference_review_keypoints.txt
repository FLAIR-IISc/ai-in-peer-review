- Paper constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel (MCK) and analyzes translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms for CNNs.  
- Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, linking robustness to improved sample complexity.  
- Extends convolutional kernel networks and generalizes the scattering transform framework to learned parameters and broader activations.  
- References and positions the work well within CKNs, scattering transform, and kernel-methods literature.  
- Strong theoretical foundation using kernel theory and harmonic analysis; stability results are a significant contribution.  
- MCK framework is general and encompasses a wide range of CNN architectures, increasing impact.  
- Demonstrates a meaningful connection between stability (RKHS norm) and generalization useful for regularization.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and presents derivations clearly.  
- Careful treatment of discretization and its effect on theoretical results is a valuable practical consideration.  
- Lacks empirical validation to demonstrate theoretical claims or show RKHS-norm correlates with generalization.  
- Computational intractability of MCK is acknowledged; impact of CKN approximation on stability guarantees is underexplored.  
- Theory requires "smooth homogeneous" activations, limiting applicability to common activations like ReLU without smoothing.  
- Representation does not preserve norm; implications of this norm loss need further exploration.  
- Theoretical framework is complex and may be inaccessible; more intuitive explanations would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, rigorous justification for CNN robustness, general framework, and link to generalization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, unresolved computational challenges, and restrictive activation assumptions.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper recommended for acceptance despite empirical gaps.  
- Suggestions: add limited empirical validation, detail practical implications of CKN approximation, discuss extending beyond smooth activations, and provide more intuitive explanations.
