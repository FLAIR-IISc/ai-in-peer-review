- Summary: Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel generalizing CKNs and shows it contains a large class of CNNs, proving stability and invariance results and linking stability to improved sample complexity.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends scattering transform theory and connects to CKNs and recent kernel methods, group invariance, and Lipschitz stability literature.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical foundation with careful proofs grounded in kernel theory and harmonic analysis.  
- Strength: Generalizes stability properties from scattering transforms to a broader class of CNN architectures.  
- Strength: Connects representation stability to sample complexity and RKHS norm as a regulator for generalization.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation with consistent notation and motivating introduction.  
- Strength: Includes discretization/approximation discussion relevant for practical CKN implementation.  
- Weakness: Lacks empirical validation—no experiments demonstrating stability or improved sample complexity.  
- Weakness: Relies on strong assumptions (smooth/homogeneous activations, regular diffeomorphisms, kernel norm preservation) with limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Kernel construction may be computationally complex and hard to scale; practical cost not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion on how theoretical stability translates into concrete architectural or training guidelines.  
- Weakness: Group invariance discussion feels peripheral and could be more concise.  
- Pro-acceptance: Novel, rigorous analysis that bridges scattering transforms and general deep architectures with insights into stability-generalization links.  
- Anti-acceptance: Impact weakened by lack of experiments, strong assumptions, and unclear practical applicability due to computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution deserving acceptance but would be significantly strengthened by empirical validation.  
- Recommendation/rating: Weak Accept; encourage inclusion of empirical results in future versions.  
- Key suggestions/questions: Provide discussion/approximations for computational cost, sensitivity to non-smooth activations (e.g., ReLU), practical architectural guidance, and relation to adversarial robustness.
