## Peer Review for "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel, extending convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) to continuous domains, and analyze its translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms. They demonstrate that this kernel framework contains a large class of CNNs, allowing them to translate their theoretical results to these networks, specifically relating stability to generalization performance. The work builds on existing theories of scattering transforms and kernel methods, aiming to provide a more rigorous understanding of the geometric properties of deep learning models.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper effectively positions itself within a body of work attempting to theoretically understand CNNs. It draws heavily from the theory of scattering transforms (Bruna & Mallat, 2013), leveraging their techniques for analyzing invariance and stability. The authors also build upon the foundation laid by convolutional kernel networks (Mairal et al., 2014, 2016), extending their framework and exploring their connection to more standard CNN architectures. They acknowledge and differentiate their work from other approaches combining deep learning and kernels (e.g., Cho & Saul, 2009) and efforts to analyze group invariance in CNNs (e.g., Cohen & Welling, 2016).

**Strengths:**

* **Rigorous Theoretical Analysis:** The paper presents a substantial theoretical framework, utilizing kernel methods and harmonic analysis to analyze CNN properties. The proofs appear detailed and well-structured (although a complete assessment requires careful examination of the appendix).
* **Bridge Between Theory and Practice:**  The authors convincingly demonstrate the relevance of their kernel framework to practical CNN architectures, showing that a significant class of networks falls within their theoretical bounds. This is a crucial step towards translating theoretical insights into practical improvements.
* **Stability and Generalization Link:** Establishing a connection between stability (as measured by the RKHS norm) and generalization is a valuable contribution.  It provides a theoretical justification for the observed benefits of regularization and potentially guides the design of more robust models.
* **Extension of Existing Theories:**  The work successfully extends the theory of scattering transforms to a broader class of CNNs, offering a more comprehensive understanding of invariance and stability properties.
* **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the importance of understanding the geometry of CNN functional spaces, linking it to practical problems like regularization and sample complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity and Accessibility:** The mathematical formalism is dense and may be challenging for readers not already familiar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis. While the authors attempt to explain the key concepts, the paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations and examples.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical results. While the connection to CNNs is established, there’s a lack of empirical evidence demonstrating the practical benefits of the theoretical findings.  Experiments showing improved generalization or robustness based on the stability analysis would significantly strengthen the paper.
* **Assumptions:** The analysis relies on several assumptions, such as smoothness of activations and the specific form of the kernels. The impact of relaxing these assumptions on the theoretical results is not fully explored.
* **Computational Considerations:** While the CKN approximation is mentioned, the paper doesn't delve deeply into the computational challenges associated with implementing and scaling these kernel-based methods. This is a critical consideration for practical applications.
* **Norm Preservation Limitation:** While acknowledged, the loss of norm preservation in the proposed framework due to pooling layers seems potentially significant. The claim that this might be less relevant for signals with low-frequency energy doesn’t fully address the issue.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound analysis of CNNs, contributing to a deeper understanding of their properties.
* The connection to practical CNN architectures is well-established, making the theoretical results relevant to real-world applications.
* The link between stability and generalization is a valuable theoretical insight.
* The work builds upon and extends existing theories in a significant way.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper lacks empirical validation to support the theoretical findings.
* The mathematical complexity may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
* The assumptions made in the analysis may restrict the generality of the results.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of CNNs. The rigorous analysis and connection to existing theories are commendable. However, the lack of empirical validation and the complexity of the mathematical formalism are significant weaknesses.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. Specifically, I encourage the authors to:

1. **Add a section discussing potential empirical validation strategies.** Even a small-scale experiment demonstrating the link between stability and generalization would significantly strengthen the paper.
2. **Provide more intuitive explanations of key concepts.**  Consider adding examples or visual aids to make the paper more accessible to a wider audience.
3. **Discuss the sensitivity of the results to the assumptions made.**  Explore the potential impact of relaxing some of the constraints on activations or kernels.
4. **Address the concern about norm preservation more thoroughly.**  Explain how the loss of norm preservation affects the overall analysis and explore potential mitigation strategies.




**Confidence:** 7/10. The theoretical contributions are significant, but the lack of empirical validation introduces some uncertainty.
