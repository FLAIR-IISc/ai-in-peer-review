## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel and demonstrate that it contains a broad class of CNNs, including convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). They prove stability to diffeomorphisms and explore the relationship between stability, invariance, and generalization performance, aiming to provide a more principled understanding of why CNNs work. The work builds upon the scattering transform and kernel methods, extending their concepts to more general CNN architectures.

**Strengths:**

* **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The paper presents a rigorous mathematical analysis of CNNs, offering valuable insights into their invariance and stability properties. The use of kernel methods provides a powerful framework for studying these properties.
* **Extension of Existing Work:** The authors successfully extend the theory of scattering transforms and CKNs to a wider range of CNN architectures, making the results more broadly applicable.
* **Connection to Generalization:** The paper establishes a link between stability (measured via the RKHS norm) and generalization performance, potentially providing a theoretical basis for regularization strategies.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):**  The core ideas are well-motivated and explained, and the structure of the paper is logical. The notation, while dense, is consistent.
* **Potential Impact:**  Understanding the theoretical underpinnings of CNNs could lead to the development of more robust, sample-efficient, and interpretable models.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity and Accessibility:** The mathematical formalism is quite dense and may be inaccessible to readers without a strong background in kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  While the authors attempt to provide necessary background, it’s still a steep learning curve.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper is primarily theoretical.  While the authors allude to potential practical implications, they lack any empirical validation to demonstrate the benefits of their theoretical findings.  Demonstrating the link between RKHS norm and generalization, even on a small scale, would significantly strengthen the work.
* **Assumptions and Scope:** The work relies on several assumptions (e.g., smoothness of activations, specific kernel properties) that may not hold for all CNN architectures in practice. The authors acknowledge this but a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential impact of these assumptions would be helpful.
* **Incremental Nature:** While the extensions to existing work are valuable, the core results (stability to diffeomorphisms) are conceptually similar to those established for scattering transforms. The novelty lies in the broader class of CNNs considered, but the degree of advancement feels incremental rather than groundbreaking.
* **Discretization discussion feels incomplete:** The connection between the continuous theory and discrete implementation is somewhat hand-wavy, and the importance of the assumptions around the sampling rate and kernel approximation is not fully explored.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and the claims are generally well-supported by mathematical analysis. However, the lack of empirical validation detracts from the overall quality. (Score: 7/10)
* **Clarity:** While the authors make an effort to explain the complex concepts, the paper is dense and requires significant effort from the reader. (Score: 6/10)
* **Originality:**  The work builds on existing research but provides a valuable extension to a broader class of CNNs. The novelty is moderate. (Score: 7/10)
* **Significance:**  The theoretical insights are potentially significant, but the lack of empirical evidence makes it difficult to assess their practical impact. (Score: 6/10)

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of CNNs using a powerful kernel-based framework.
* Extends existing theoretical results on scattering transforms and CKNs to a wider range of architectures.
* Establishes a connection between stability and generalization.
* Contributes to a deeper understanding of the underlying principles of deep learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Primarily theoretical with limited empirical validation.
* High mathematical complexity may limit accessibility.
* Results are somewhat incremental to existing work.
* Assumptions and scope may restrict the applicability of the findings.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the theoretical understanding of CNNs. The mathematical analysis is rigorous and the extension of existing work is valuable. However, the lack of empirical validation is a significant weakness. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should be encouraged to include some preliminary empirical results to demonstrate the practical relevance of their findings, and to more thoroughly discuss the limitations of their assumptions.  A more detailed discussion regarding the discretization process would also be beneficial.
