- Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and covers a broad class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations.  
- Proves invariance and stability of the kernel-based representation to diffeomorphic deformations, similar to scattering transforms.  
- Links stability (measured by RKHS norm) to potential improvements in sample complexity and generalization.  
- Extends theoretical results from scattering transforms to more general, practical CNN architectures.  
- Properly situates the work within prior kernel and deep learning literature, noting connections to equivariant CNNs and group invariance.  
- Provides a strong mathematical foundation using kernel theory and harmonic analysis.  
- Demonstrates that a significant class of practical CNNs fits within the constructed kernel framework.  
- Thoughtfully addresses discretization, bridging continuous theory and discrete implementations.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear motivation and structure.  
- Mathematical notation and concepts are dense, reducing accessibility for non-expert readers.  
- Lacks empirical validation demonstrating practical benefits like improved robustness or sample efficiency.  
- Restriction to homogeneous activations limits applicability; implications for non-homogeneous activations are not fully explored.  
- Does not thoroughly address the impact of lack of norm preservation on stability guarantees.  
- For acceptance: offers novel, rigorous analysis and extends existing theoretical frameworks with potentially significant insights.  
- Against acceptance: overly theoretical, limited experiments, and restrictive activation assumptions.  
- Overall: a valuable theoretical contribution deserving publication with improvements.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—improve accessibility, discuss limitations (activations and norm preservation), add empirical illustration, and clarify RKHS norm implications.
