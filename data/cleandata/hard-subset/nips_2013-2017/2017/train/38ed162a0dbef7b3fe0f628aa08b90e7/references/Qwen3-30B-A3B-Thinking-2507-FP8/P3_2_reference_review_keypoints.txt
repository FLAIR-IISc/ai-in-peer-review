- Paper presents a multilayer convolutional kernel embedding CNNs into an RKHS and proves stability to diffeomorphisms (Theorem 6), extending Mallat's scattering framework.  
- Main theoretical novelty: connects kernel methods and standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, providing rigorous stability guarantees.  
- Mathematical framework is well-defined and rigorous, with clear operators (patch extraction, kernel mapping, pooling) and consistent notation.  
- Practical relevance: shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, suggesting principled regularization for CNNs.  
- Related work is comprehensively discussed, situating the paper among scattering transforms, CKNs, group-invariance, and kernel approaches.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation to demonstrate the theoretical stability in practice.  
- Scope limitation: results only apply to smooth homogeneous activations, leaving out common non-smooth activations like ReLU.  
- Discretization concerns: insufficient discussion on how continuous-signal theoretical guarantees translate to discrete image data.  
- Computational complexity of the kernel framework versus standard CNNs is not addressed.  
- Critical judgment: strong and rigorous theoretical contribution that meaningfully extends prior work and links RKHS norms to generalization.  
- Recommended improvements: add experiments on deformation stability, extend or justify applicability to ReLU networks, discuss computational feasibility, and provide more intuition on RKHS norm implications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on adding empirical evidence and addressing practical limitations.  
- Final view: valuable theoretical advance with moderate significance; addressing the noted practical gaps would substantially enhance impact.
