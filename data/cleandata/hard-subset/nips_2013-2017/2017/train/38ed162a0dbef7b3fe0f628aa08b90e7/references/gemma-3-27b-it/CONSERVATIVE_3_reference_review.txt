This paper explores the theoretical properties of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels, aiming to provide a more principled understanding of their invariance and stability characteristics. The authors build upon existing work in convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and scattering transforms, extending these concepts to a broader class of CNN architectures. While the topic is relevant to the NIPS community's interest in understanding deep learning, and the connection to established kernel methods is potentially valuable, I find the paper lacking in substantial novelty and practical impact, and ultimately falling short of the NIPS acceptance bar.

The core idea of framing CNNs within a kernel framework is not entirely new (see references [2, 4, 7, 26] cited in the paper, and many others). The authors do a reasonable job of extending kernel methods to a more general class of CNNs than previously considered, and the stability results are a welcome theoretical contribution. However, the paper primarily *states* results and sketches connections to existing theory; the actual technical contributions, beyond the extension of known results to a broader class of architectures, feel incremental. The numerous references to “Appendix C” and “Appendix B” suggest a large volume of technical detail is relegated to supplementary materials, making it difficult to fully assess the rigor of the proofs without significant effort. 

A major weakness is the limited connection to practical CNNs. While the authors demonstrate that a class of CNNs *falls within* their theoretical framework, they don't demonstrate how this framework provides new insights into *existing* CNN architectures commonly used in practice. The discussion of homogeneous activations feels somewhat contrived, and the claim that the results apply to CNNs with ReLU-like activations requires significant justification beyond the presented argument. The relevance of these theoretical guarantees to the empirical success of deep learning remains unclear. The authors mention regularization and sample complexity, but these connections are only superficially explored.

The paper's organization is generally clear, but the numerous definitions and lemmas (particularly in Section 2) are dense and difficult to follow without careful study. The notation is heavy, and while standard, it contributes to the overall complexity of the presentation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical grounding:** The paper attempts to provide a rigorous theoretical foundation for understanding CNNs.
*   **Connection to kernel methods:**  Linking CNNs to kernel methods is a potentially fruitful research direction.
*   **Stability analysis:** The stability results are mathematically sound (though detailed verification requires access to the appendix).

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited novelty:** The core ideas build heavily on existing work, and the extensions feel incremental.
*   **Lack of practical impact:** The connection to real-world CNNs is weak. The theoretical results don't immediately translate into improved architectures or training techniques.
*   **Heavy technical detail:** The paper is dense and requires significant mathematical background to fully comprehend. The reliance on appendices obscures critical details.
*   **Incremental contribution to understanding**: The paper does not substantially shift our understanding of CNN behaviour, and relies on existing results to provide its analysis.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical results are correct and potentially useful for researchers interested in the mathematical foundations of deep learning.
*   The paper provides a well-defined kernel framework for analyzing CNNs.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper lacks substantial novelty and practical impact.
*   The connection to commonly used CNN architectures is tenuous.
*   The technical details are dense and difficult to follow.
*   The contribution doesn't justify the complexity of the presented theoretical framework.



**Overall Assessment:**

I lean towards **Reject**. While the paper is technically sound and explores a relevant topic, it doesn't present a compelling enough contribution to justify acceptance to NIPS. The theoretical results feel abstract and disconnected from the practical realities of deep learning. The paper would benefit from a stronger focus on practical implications and a more detailed discussion of how the theoretical framework can inform the design and training of real-world CNNs. The extensive use of the appendices also detracts from the paper's accessibility and makes it difficult to fully assess the validity of the claims.
