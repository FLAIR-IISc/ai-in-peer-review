- Summary: Constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel (CKN) to analyze translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms, showing the framework covers CKNs and a broader class of CNNs and linking stability (via RKHS norm) to generalization.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on convolutional kernel networks, scattering transform theory, kernel methods for deep learning, and group invariance studies, situating the paper well in existing literature.

- Strength — strong theory: Provides a rigorous mathematical foundation connecting CNNs to positive definite kernels and RKHSs.

- Strength — generality: Extends results beyond CKNs to include many standard CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activations.

- Strength — stability–generalization link: Explicitly connects stability (RKHS norm) to generalization, offering a theoretical rationale for regularization.

- Strength — clarity: Well-written and organized with clear notation, lemmas, and proofs; helpful roadmap and appendices.

- Strength — discretization discussion: Thoughtful bridging of continuous and discrete models gives realistic perspective on applicability.

- Weakness — practical implications: Lacks clear guidance on translating theory into practical CNN design or training (e.g., how to estimate/use RKHS norm efficiently).

- Weakness — activation restriction: Reliance on homogeneous (or smoothed ReLU) activations limits applicability to common non-homogeneous activations without further analysis.

- Weakness — computational complexity: Does not sufficiently address scalability or computational challenges of kernel methods in high-dimensional settings.

- Weakness — limited empirical validation: No experiments demonstrating the proposed stability–generalization relationship on standard datasets.

- Weakness — occasional notation confusion: Dropping norm subscripts sometimes hinders clarity; more consistent notation would help.

- For acceptance: Novel, rigorous analysis that advances theoretical understanding of CNNs and extends beyond CKNs while linking stability to generalization.

- Against acceptance: Limited immediate applicability due to lack of practical guidance, restrictive activation assumptions, and unresolved computational concerns.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution recommended for acceptance, conditional on addressing practical/empirical limitations in future work.

- Confidence: 8/10.
