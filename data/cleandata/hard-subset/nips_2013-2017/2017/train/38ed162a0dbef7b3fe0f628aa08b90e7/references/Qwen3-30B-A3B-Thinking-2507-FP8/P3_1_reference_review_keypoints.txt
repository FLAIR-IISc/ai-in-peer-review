- Develops a theoretical continuous-domain kernel framework for CNNs, generalizing convolutional kernel networks (CKNs).
- Shows representations are stable to small diffeomorphisms with bounds depending on number of layers and pooling scales (Theorem 6).
- Extends Mallat’s scattering transform ideas to more standard CNN architectures via a kernel construction.
- Proves a novel embedding of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations into the RKHS of the proposed kernel (Proposition 7).
- Establishes a link between RKHS norm, stability to deformations, and potential improvements in sample complexity/generalization.
- Separates representation learning from prediction, suggesting RKHS-norm regularization can control stability and generalization.
- Extends the framework to handle group invariances (rotations, reflections) via adapted patch extraction and pooling operators.
- Provides rigorous mathematical analysis and detailed proofs, offering a solid theoretical foundation.
- Lacks any empirical validation or experiments to demonstrate the claimed stability or practical benefits.
- Discretization discussion is abstract and does not clearly map theoretical bounds to real discrete CNN implementations.
- Focuses on smooth homogeneous activations, excluding common non-smooth activations like ReLU and limiting practical applicability.
- Offers limited concrete guidance on designing CNN architectures to exploit the theoretical insights for improved performance.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — strong theoretical contribution but weakened by absence of empirical results.
- Suggested future work: extend framework to non-smooth activations and include empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact.
