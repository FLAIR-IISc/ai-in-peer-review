- Overall: Solid theoretical contribution framing deep CNNs in an RKHS, extending scattering results to broader CNN/CKN classes; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Summary: Construct a multilayer convolutional kernel that captures many CNNs, proving stability to diffeomorphic deformations and linking RKHS norm to generalization.
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Rigorous, well-motivated mathematical development using RKHS theory with apparently sound proofs.
- Strength — Extension of theory: Extends stability guarantees from scattering transforms to a wider class of CNNs that allow learning.
- Strength — Stability-generalization link: Shows RKHS norm relates to generalization, providing theory for why flatter minima generalize better.
- Strength — Related work: Comprehensive and thorough survey of CKNs, scattering transforms, and kernel methods.
- Strength — Presentation: Core ideas are clear and the paper is well-structured with generally consistent notation.
- Weakness — Practical implications: Limited discussion on how to estimate or regularize RKHS norms and translate theory to CNN training/design.
- Weakness — Modern architectures: Insufficient concrete discussion of how popular architectures (ResNets, DenseNets) fit into the framework.
- Weakness — Activation functions: Reliance on homogeneous/smooth activations limits direct applicability to non-smooth common choices like ReLU.
- Weakness — Computational cost: Does not adequately address computational challenges of RKHS norms and kernel matrices despite CKN approximations.
- Weakness — Clarity issues: Lemma 1 and discretization (Section 2.1) need clearer explanation of the continuous–discrete connection.
- Detailed comment — Theorem constants: Theorem 6’s constants (C1, C2) are not explicitly defined; their parameter dependencies should be clarified.
- Detailed comment — Group invariance: Group invariance section could benefit from concrete application examples (e.g., medical imaging, graphics).
- Detailed comment — Activations discussion: Treatment of ReLU and homogeneity is cursory; more detail on smoothness vs. homogeneity trade-offs is needed.
- Detailed comment — Appendices: Appendices are detailed but would be improved by better organization and readability.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound framework that generalizes scattering results and ties stability to generalization; well-researched and well-written.
- Against acceptance: Practical impact not fully explored, reliance on homogeneous activations, and unresolved computational complexity concerns.
