This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors aim to understand the invariance and stability characteristics of CNNs, building upon previous work on scattering transforms and convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). They construct a multilayer convolutional kernel and demonstrate that it encompasses a broad class of CNNs, allowing them to derive theoretical results regarding stability to diffeomorphisms and translation invariance. A key contribution is showing that the RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, potentially leading to improved sample complexity.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a fundamental question about the geometry of functional spaces underlying deep learning. The connection to kernel methods provides a valuable theoretical framework. The extension of existing results on scattering transforms to more general CNN architectures is a significant step. The thorough related work section demonstrates awareness of the existing literature. The paper is technically sound, with clear mathematical notation and logical flow. The inclusion of proofs in the appendix enhances reproducibility. The exploration of group invariance and its connection to pooling operators is a notable addition.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. While the theoretical results are strong, the link to practical implications for CNN training and architecture design feels somewhat tenuous. The conditions required for some of the theoretical results (e.g., smoothness of activations, regularity of diffeomorphisms) may be restrictive in practice. The discussion on the connection between the RKHS norm and generalization is suggestive but lacks concrete guidance on how to leverage this relationship in algorithm design. The continuous setting, while providing theoretical clarity, requires careful consideration of discretization effects, which are addressed but could be further elaborated. The notation can be dense and requires close attention to follow.  The paper would benefit from more intuitive explanations of the mathematical concepts for a broader audience. While the authors acknowledge the limitations of norm preservation, a more in-depth discussion of the implications of its absence would be helpful. Finally, while the paper establishes connections to CNNs, a comparative empirical analysis demonstrating the advantages of this kernel-based perspective—even on toy examples—would significantly strengthen the work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of CNNs using kernel methods.
*   **Novel Extension:** The extension of scattering transform theory to broader CNN architectures is a valuable contribution.
*   **Clear Mathematical Presentation:** The paper is technically sound and well-written from a mathematical perspective.
*   **Relevant Problem:** Understanding the geometry of CNN function spaces is a fundamental and important problem.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Practical Guidance:** The theoretical results lack clear guidance for improving CNN training or architecture design.
*   **Restrictive Assumptions:** Some theoretical assumptions may be difficult to satisfy in practice.
*   **Dense Notation:** The mathematical notation can be challenging for some readers.
*   **Lack of Empirical Validation:** The absence of empirical results limits the impact of the theoretical findings.



**Overall Assessment:**

I rate this paper as **weak reject**. While the paper presents solid theoretical work and addresses an important problem, the limited connection to practical applications and the restrictive assumptions weaken its impact. The lack of empirical validation further diminishes its contribution.  The authors have a strong grasp of the relevant theory, but the paper would benefit from a more focused discussion of practical implications and some empirical evidence to support their claims. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Practical Implications:** Elaborate on how the theoretical results (e.g., the connection between RKHS norm and stability) can be used to guide CNN architecture design or training strategies.
2.  **Relaxed Assumptions:** Investigate the robustness of the results to less restrictive assumptions.
3.  **Empirical Validation:** Include a small-scale empirical evaluation to demonstrate the benefits of the kernel-based perspective, even on toy datasets.
4.  **Intuitive Explanations:** Provide more intuitive explanations of the mathematical concepts to make the paper accessible to a broader audience.
5.  **Discretization Discussion:** Elaborate on the practical considerations and potential pitfalls of discretizing the continuous kernel framework.
6.  **Norm Preservation:** Provide a more thorough discussion on the implications of not preserving the norm during pooling. Could alternative pooling strategies alleviate this?
