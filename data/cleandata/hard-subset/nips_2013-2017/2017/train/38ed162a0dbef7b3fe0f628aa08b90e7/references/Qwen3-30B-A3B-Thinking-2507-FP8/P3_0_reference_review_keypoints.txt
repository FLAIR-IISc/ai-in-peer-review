- Introduces a kernel-based theoretical framework that analyzes invariance and stability of CNN representations, building on Mallat’s scattering transform and CKNs.  
- Establishes theoretical guarantees for translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms, with bounds (Theorem 6) matching a Lipschitz stability condition and explicit dependence on depth and pooling.  
- Proves a key link (Proposition 7) between the kernel framework and standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, extending applicability beyond scattering transforms.  
- Connects theory to practical design: shows how pooling scale and depth affect stability and relates RKHS norm to generalization and regularization.  
- Offers a comprehensive treatment covering continuous vs. discrete signals, discretization issues, kernel approximations, and group invariance.  
- Lacks empirical validation demonstrating that the theoretical stability leads to improved performance on real-world datasets with deformations.  
- Requires smooth homogeneous activation functions, excluding common nonsmooth ReLU; smoothed variants are mentioned but their practical relevance is unclear.  
- Representation does not preserve signal norm (unlike scattering); authors propose an extra nonlinear prediction layer but provide no empirical justification.  
- Stability results assume ‖∇τ‖∞ ≤ 1/2, limiting the class of deformations covered and leaving open extensions to larger deformations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and significant theoretical contribution that bridges scattering transforms and standard CNNs.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; suggest adding discussion of empirical validation and clarifying practical implications of the smooth activation requirement.
