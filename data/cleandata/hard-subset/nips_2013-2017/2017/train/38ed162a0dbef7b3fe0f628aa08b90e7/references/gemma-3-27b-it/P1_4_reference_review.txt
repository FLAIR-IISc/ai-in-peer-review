## Peer Review for "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and show that it encompasses a broad class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activation functions. They demonstrate that this kernel-based representation offers theoretical guarantees of invariance and stability to diffeomorphic deformations, akin to those found in the scattering transform, but within a more general framework applicable to standard CNN architectures. A key finding is the connection between stability (measured by the RKHS norm) and potential for improved sample complexity, suggesting a theoretical link between regularization and generalization performance.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The work builds upon the existing literature on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and scattering transforms. It distinguishes itself by extending the theoretical analysis of invariance and stability to a wider range of CNN architectures beyond the fixed-filter approach of scattering transforms, and connects these properties to the rich framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS).  The authors properly situate their work within the broader context of combining kernel methods and deep learning, citing relevant contributions like early multilayer kernel machines and more recent attempts to understand deep networks through kernel analysis.  They acknowledge connections to equivariant CNNs and group invariance, but focus on stability as a more general property.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical treatment of invariance and stability, using concepts from kernel theory and harmonic analysis. The connection to RKHS norms and their implications for generalization is a significant strength.
*   **Generalization Beyond Scattering Transforms:** Extending the theoretical results from the relatively restricted scattering transform framework to more general CNN architectures is a valuable contribution. This broadens the applicability of these theoretical insights.
*   **Connection to Practical CNNs:** The authors demonstrate that a significant class of practical CNNs falls within the framework of their constructed kernel, allowing the theoretical results to be directly applied.
*   **Clear Structure and Motivation:** The introduction clearly outlines the motivations and goals of the work, and the paper is generally well-organized.
* **Addresses Discretization:** The paper thoughtfully addresses the practical concern of discretization, bridging the gap between the continuous theoretical framework and discrete implementations.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity and Accessibility:** The mathematical notation and theoretical concepts are dense and may pose a challenge for readers not deeply familiar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis. While rigor is appreciated, a more intuitive explanation of the key ideas could broaden the paper’s impact.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper is primarily theoretical. While the connection to CNNs is established, there's a lack of empirical validation demonstrating the practical benefits of the theoretical results (e.g., showing improved sample complexity or robustness in real-world tasks). A small-scale experiment illustrating the benefits of the framework would strengthen the work.
*   **Homogeneity Restriction on Activations:** The requirement for homogeneous activations is a limitation. While the authors acknowledge the existence of smoothed ReLU variants that satisfy the conditions, it’s unclear how prevalent or practical such alternatives are.  The implications of non-homogeneous activations are not fully explored.
* **Norm Preservation Issues:** While acknowledging the lack of norm preservation, the paper doesn't thoroughly discuss the impact of this on the stability analysis. Further elaboration on how the loss of norm preservation affects the theoretical guarantees would be beneficial.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and mathematically rigorous analysis of invariance and stability in deep CNNs.
*   It extends existing theoretical frameworks (scattering transforms, CKNs) to a broader class of models.
*   The connection between stability and generalization is a potentially significant insight.
*   The paper addresses a fundamental question regarding the underlying principles governing the success of deep learning.

**Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper is highly theoretical and lacks empirical validation.
*   The assumption of homogeneous activations limits the applicability of the results.
*   The mathematical complexity may hinder accessibility for a broader audience.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep convolutional networks. The mathematical rigor and extension of existing frameworks are commendable. While the lack of empirical validation and the restriction to homogeneous activations are weaknesses, the theoretical insights are significant and potentially impactful.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend accepting the paper with the following revisions:

1.  **Improve Accessibility:** Add more intuitive explanations of key concepts, potentially including simplified examples or visual aids.
2.  **Discuss Limitations:** Provide a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the homogeneous activation requirement and the potential impact of norm preservation issues.
3.  **Consider Empirical Illustration:** Even a small-scale experiment demonstrating the benefits of the theoretical framework would significantly strengthen the paper.  This could involve showing improved robustness to perturbations or faster convergence with limited data.
4. **Clarify Implications:** Expand on the connection between RKHS norm and generalization, offering specific predictions on how manipulating the norm could lead to improved performance.



I am confident that addressing these minor revisions will further enhance the quality and impact of this work, making it a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
