- Paper proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes CKNs and links scattering transforms to standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, studying invariance and stability via positive-definite kernels.
- Main contributions: (1) kernel framework for translation-invariance and diffeomorphism stability, (2) showing CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the RKHS of the kernel, (3) RKHS norms control both stability and generalization supporting regularization.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with well-constructed proofs and elegant treatment of how pooling affects stability and invariance.
- Novelly connects scattering transform theory to standard CNNs, extending stability results to a broader class of models.
- Presents a clear theoretical framework linking stability, generalization, and RKHS norms, offering guidance for regularization.
- Discusses discretization and kernel approximation, relating the theory to practical convolutional kernel network implementations.
- Lacks empirical validation demonstrating how theoretical stability properties manifest in practical CNN training or predict generalization on datasets.
- Limited connection to modern architectures: focuses on smooth homogeneous activations while most state-of-the-art CNNs use non-homogeneous ReLU variants.
- Mathematical framework is complex and assumes strong functional analysis/kernel methods background, reducing accessibility for many ML researchers.
- Limited practical guidance on implementing stability-based regularization or measuring/controlling the RKHS norm in standard CNNs.
- Overall assessment: significant and well-executed theoretical contribution that extends scattering results to CNNs and links RKHS norms to generalization.
- Recommendation: accept—theoretical significance outweighs limitations despite lack of experiments and limited practical scope.
- Suggested revisions: add a brief empirical demonstration relating RKHS norm to generalization, discuss applicability to ReLU-based architectures, and include more intuitive explanations of the RKHS framework.
