This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels, aiming to provide insights into their invariance and stability. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and demonstrate that it encompasses a large class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activation functions. They establish stability results comparable to the scattering transform, guaranteeing robustness to diffeomorphisms and linking stability to generalization performance via the RKHS norm. 

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a fundamental question regarding the theoretical understanding of CNNs. It builds upon prior work in kernel methods, scattering transforms, and group invariance, and clearly positions itself within this existing landscape. The key contribution lies in extending the theoretical framework of scattering transforms to a broader class of CNN architectures via a kernel-based approach. The connection to CKNs provides a concrete instantiation of the theoretical framework.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper presents a mathematically sound analysis, leveraging concepts from kernel theory and harmonic analysis. Proofs are deferred to the appendix, indicating a commitment to rigorous justification.
* **Novelty:** Extending stability guarantees, previously established for scattering transforms, to more general CNN architectures is a significant contribution. The kernel-based framework provides a new perspective on analyzing CNNs.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is standard and clearly defined. The progression of ideas is logical.
* **Connection to Existing Work:** The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work, carefully citing relevant papers and contrasting their approach with existing methodologies.
* **Practical Implications:**  The link between stability and generalization via the RKHS norm offers potential insights into regularization strategies and sample complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The mathematical details, while rigorous, can be dense and challenging for readers not already familiar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis. While the appendix provides proofs, a more intuitive explanation of key concepts within the main text might improve accessibility.
* **Assumptions:** The analysis relies on several assumptions, such as smoothness of activation functions and the preservation of norms in kernel mappings. The impact of relaxing these assumptions is not fully explored.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical results. While a connection to CKNs is established, there is no empirical evaluation demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed framework on standard CNN architectures and datasets.
* **Homogeneous Activations:** The requirement of homogeneous activations is restrictive. Although the authors mention smoothed ReLU variants, the practical implications of this restriction warrant further discussion.
* **Discretization Details:** The discretization section, while present, feels somewhat rushed.  A more thorough discussion of the impact of discretization choices on the theoretical guarantees would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in understanding the properties of CNNs.
* The extension of stability guarantees to a broader class of architectures is a valuable contribution.
* The work is technically sound and well-motivated.
* The connection to kernel methods provides a novel perspective on CNN analysis.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The high level of mathematical complexity may limit accessibility.
* The lack of empirical validation weakens the practical impact of the work.
* The restrictive assumptions may limit the applicability of the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional neural networks. While the mathematical complexity and lack of empirical validation are weaknesses, the novelty and rigor of the work outweigh these concerns. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing clarity and providing a more nuanced discussion of the assumptions. Specifically, expanding the discussion on the impact of relaxing the homogeneity assumption on activations and adding a section discussing the practical considerations when applying this theory to architectures beyond CKNs would be helpful.
