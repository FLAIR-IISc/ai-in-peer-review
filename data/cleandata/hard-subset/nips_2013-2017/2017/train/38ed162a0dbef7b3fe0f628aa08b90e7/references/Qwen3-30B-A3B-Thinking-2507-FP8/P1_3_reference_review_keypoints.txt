- Paper develops a kernel framework generalizing convolutional kernel networks to analyze invariance and stability of convolutional representations, linking to Mallat's scattering transform.
- Shows stability to diffeomorphisms while preserving signal information and proves RKHS norms control stability and generalization.
- Demonstrates the functional space contains many CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, connecting theory to practical architectures.
- Provides rigorous mathematical definitions and proofs with precise stability bounds and sound technical analysis.
- Offers theoretical insight that stable representations (measured by RKHS norms) can improve sample complexity and generalization.
- Novelly bridges scattering transforms and standard CNNs, encompassing smoothed ReLU-like activations.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes helpful figures and appendices.
- Lacks empirical validation showing stability benefits on real-world tasks.
- Relies on smooth homogeneous activations, leaving applicability to standard (nonsmooth) ReLU networks unclear.
- Provides limited practical implementation guidance for discretization and kernel approximation.
- Focuses on stability to small deformations (diffeomorphisms) but does not address adversarial robustness or other stability types.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theoretical contribution and clarity despite empirical and practical limitations.
- Pro arguments: unified theoretical framework, link between stability and generalization, rigorous proofs, significant theoretical implications, excellent exposition.
- Con arguments: limited experiments, restrictive activation assumptions, sparse implementation details, omission of adversarial robustness issues.
