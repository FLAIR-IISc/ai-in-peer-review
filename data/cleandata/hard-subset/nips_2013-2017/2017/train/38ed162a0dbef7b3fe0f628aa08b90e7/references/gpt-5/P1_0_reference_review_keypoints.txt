- Develops a multilayer convolutional kernel framework mirroring CNNs, encompassing CKNs and a subset of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Each layer applies patch extraction, a norm-preserving/non-expansive RKHS map from a homogeneous dot-product kernel, and Gaussian pooling.  
- Proves stability to C1 diffeomorphisms and increasing translation invariance with depth; key Lipschitz bound is linear in the deformation Jacobian plus a translation term decaying as 1/σn.  
- Extends construction to group-equivariant layers, achieving global invariance via last-layer pooling and layerwise equivariance to subgroups.  
- Bridges theory and practice via discretization (signal recoverability/deconvolution) and finite-dimensional kernel approximation (CKN) that preserves non-expansiveness and stability.  
- Shows the RKHS contains a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations and provides a recursive RKHS norm bound linking stability/generalization to that norm.  
- Presents a clear unified operator view (patch → kernel map → pooling) that subsumes prior CKN formulations.  
- Provides novel theoretical advances: deformation stability beyond scattering and a new commutator bound for [Lc Aσ, Lτ].  
- Demonstrates layerwise group equivariance and how last-layer pooling yields global invariance.  
- Addresses practical considerations (discretization and finite-dimensional projection) and shows stability is retained after projection.  
- The CNN inclusion result conceptually links deep CNN regularization, stability, and generalization.  
- Situates work well relative to scattering transforms, group-equivariant CNNs, and prior CKN literature.  
- Relies on strong/idealized assumptions (norm-preserving, non-expansive maps; Gaussian pooling; patch size tied to pooling scale) that may not match typical CNNs.  
- Inclusion of CNNs requires homogenization and smooth activations; ReLU is excluded (only smoothed surrogates suggested), limiting direct applicability to real networks.  
- Discretization recovery depends on invertibility of Gaussian blurs and is not robust to noise; deconvolution stability is only qualitatively discussed.  
- Stability bound constants (C1, C2) and their dependence on κ and dimension can be large; linear depth dependence may be pessimistic and unevaluated empirically.  
- Limited empirical validation: theoretical claims need experiments showing predicted stability in CKNs/CNNs, effects of σn, and RKHS norm vs generalization.  
- Generalization link remains conceptual via RKHS norm, without explicit sample-complexity bounds for the class.  
- Technically sound with careful operator-theoretic reasoning and appropriate use of tools (e.g., Schur’s test), though some technical steps need further qualification.  
- Well organized and clearly written; recommends moving kernel examples to main text and clarifying choices of σk and κ and their effect on constants.  
- Original and valuable: extends scattering stability to a broader kernel-based multilayer class and contributes commutator analysis and group-equivariant kernel-layer construction.  
- Significant conceptual contribution to theory of invariance/stability and RKHS-based regularization; immediate practical impact limited by idealized assumptions and lack of experiments.  
- Key questions for authors: sensitivity of bounds to kernel choice, extension to non-smooth ReLU (or quantitative smoothing approximations), numerical illustrations of stability vs σn and n, robustness of discretization/deconvolution under noise, and accommodation of strided convolutions and max/avg pooling.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept; paper is meaningful and well executed but would be strengthened by small empirical illustrations and clearer practical implications.
