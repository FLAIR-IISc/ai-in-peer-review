This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a functional space derived from a kernel that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and demonstrate that this space contains a large class of CNNs. They then analyze the translation-invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms of this representation, achieving results comparable to the scattering transform while maintaining a more standard CNN architecture. A key motivation is to establish links between stability, generalization, and regularization in deep learning.

The paper is well-written and tackles a fundamentally important question in deep learning: understanding the geometric properties of the functional spaces learned by CNNs. The connection to kernel methods provides a rigorous framework for analysis, and the extension of results from scattering transforms to more general CNN architectures is a significant contribution. The detailed mathematical treatment, while demanding, is necessary for the type of theoretical results presented. The inclusion of related work is thorough, demonstrating awareness of existing research in kernel methods, scattering transforms, and group-invariant representations. The authors also appropriately address the practical implications of their work by discussing discretization and kernel approximation techniques (CKNs).

**Strengths:**

* **Rigorous Theoretical Foundation:** The use of kernel methods and harmonic analysis provides a strong mathematical foundation for studying CNN properties.
* **Generalization of Existing Results:** Extending results from scattering transforms to more general CNN architectures is a valuable contribution.
* **Clear Connection to Generalization:** The link between stability (RKHS norm) and generalization is a compelling argument for the significance of this work.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the relevant literature.
* **Practical Considerations:** Discussion of discretization and CKN approximations bridges the gap between theory and practice.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity and Accessibility:** The highly mathematical nature of the paper may limit its accessibility to a broader audience. The numerous lemmas and theorems, while technically sound, can make it difficult to grasp the key insights quickly.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper primarily focuses on theoretical results. While the authors mention the potential implications for practical CNNs, a more concrete demonstration on benchmark datasets could strengthen the claims. Specifically, demonstrating improved sample complexity or robustness based on the theoretical findings would be impactful.
* **Assumptions on Activation Functions:** The requirement for "smooth homogeneous activation functions" limits the applicability of the results to standard activations like ReLU without modification. While the authors acknowledge this and point to smoothed ReLU variants, the practical impact needs further consideration.
* **Computational Cost:** Implementing the proposed kernel-based analysis and approximations can be computationally expensive, particularly for large-scale CNNs. This aspect isn’t thoroughly discussed.
* **Incremental Contribution:** While rigorous and well-executed, the work feels somewhat incremental. Building on established theories from kernel methods and scattering transforms, the novelty lies more in the specific combination and extension rather than a groundbreaking new paradigm.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a fundamental question in deep learning with a rigorous mathematical approach.
* The results provide valuable theoretical insights into the properties of CNNs and their relationship to generalization.
* The extension of existing results to more general CNN architectures is a significant contribution.
* The paper is well-written and demonstrates a strong understanding of the relevant literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The highly mathematical nature of the paper may limit its accessibility and impact.
* The lack of extensive empirical validation weakens the claims.
* The assumptions on activation functions may limit the practical applicability of the results.
* The incremental nature of the contribution may not justify acceptance at a highly competitive conference like NeurIPS.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the theoretical understanding of deep learning. However, its complexity and limited empirical validation raise concerns. While the mathematical rigor is commendable, the lack of concrete experimental results makes it difficult to assess the practical significance of the findings.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should consider the following suggestions:

1. **Increase Empirical Validation:** Conduct experiments on benchmark datasets to demonstrate the practical implications of the theoretical results, such as improved sample complexity or robustness.
2. **Address Activation Function Limitations:** Explore the applicability of the theory to more commonly used activation functions (e.g., ReLU) and/or develop techniques to adapt the framework to handle non-smooth activations.
3. **Discuss Computational Cost:** Provide a more thorough discussion of the computational cost associated with the proposed kernel-based analysis and approximations.
4. **Simplify Presentation:** While maintaining technical correctness, strive for a more accessible presentation of the mathematical concepts to broaden the potential impact of the work. Consider adding intuitive explanations or examples to complement the formal proofs.
