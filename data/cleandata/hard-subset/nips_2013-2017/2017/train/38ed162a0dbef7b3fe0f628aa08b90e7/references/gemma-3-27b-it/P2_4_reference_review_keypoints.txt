- Builds a multilayer convolutional kernel that captures a large class of CNNs to study invariance and stability.  
- Shows kernel framework yields stability to diffeomorphisms similar to scattering transforms but in a more general setting.  
- Demonstrates the RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, linking stability to improved sample complexity.  
- Extends prior work on Convolutional Kernel Networks and scattering transforms to cover a broader class of CNN architectures.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with detailed proofs and sound use of kernel and harmonic analysis tools.  
- Connects abstract RKHS/kernel concepts to practical CNN architectures, including discussion of discretization and kernel approximation.  
- Generalizes stability guarantees of scattering transforms to standard CNNs.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, though dense.  
- Heavy mathematical notation and abstract concepts reduce accessibility for non-expert readers.  
- Paper is primarily theoretical with no empirical validation of claimed stability or sample-complexity benefits.  
- Analysis is restricted to homogeneous activations, excluding common nonlinearities like ReLU and limiting applicability.  
- Does not fully address consequences of lack of norm preservation or justify low-frequency bias assumption.  
- Potential overlap with existing kernel methods is acknowledged but not deeply explored; novel advantages vs. established techniques are unclear.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on future empirical validation, relaxation of activation constraints, and improved accessibility.
