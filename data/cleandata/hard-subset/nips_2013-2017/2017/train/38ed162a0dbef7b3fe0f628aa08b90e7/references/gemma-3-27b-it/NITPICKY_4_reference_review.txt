This paper investigates the invariance and stability properties of deep convolutional representations through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel, extending existing work on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and scattering transforms to a broader class of CNN architectures. They demonstrate theoretical guarantees for translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms, linking these properties to generalization bounds via the RKHS norm.

The paper is meticulously constructed and demonstrates a strong understanding of the relevant theoretical machinery – kernel methods, harmonic analysis, and the geometry of deep networks. The extension of scattering transform analysis to a more general kernel framework is a valuable contribution. The connection established between stability and generalization through the RKHS norm is also noteworthy. However, despite the technical soundness, the paper suffers from a number of issues that, cumulatively, lead me to recommend rejection.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper is built on a solid theoretical base, utilizing concepts from kernel methods and harmonic analysis to rigorously analyze CNN properties.
* **Generalization of Existing Work:** The authors successfully generalize previous results on scattering transforms and CKNs to a wider range of CNN architectures.
* **Clear Link to Generalization:** Establishing a connection between stability and generalization via the RKHS norm provides a theoretical justification for the importance of stable representations.
* **Detailed Mathematical Analysis:** The proofs appear rigorous and the derivations are presented with sufficient detail (though somewhat dispersed across the main text and appendix).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Excessive Technical Detail and Lack of Big Picture:** The paper is *overly* detailed, sometimes to the detriment of clarity.  The focus on minute technical aspects obscures the broader significance of the results. While rigor is important, the paper often dives into unnecessary complexity that doesn't substantially advance the core argument.
* **Incremental Contribution:** While theoretically sound, the contribution feels incremental. The results, while extending existing work, do not represent a paradigm shift in understanding deep networks.  The established connections (stability-generalization) are known in kernel methods and are applied here to a specific CNN setting.
* **Discretization Handling Feels Ad-hoc:** The treatment of discretization in Section 2.1 feels somewhat tacked on and lacks the same level of theoretical depth as the continuous analysis. The reliance on assumptions about initial pooling and patch sizes feels limiting.
* **Kernel Approximation Caveats Underplayed:** The discussion of approximating kernel maps with finite-dimensional subspaces (Section 2.2) glosses over the potential loss of information and the sensitivity of the results to the quality of the approximation. This feels like a crucial practical consideration that deserves more attention.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper lacks any empirical evaluation. While the focus is theoretical, even a small-scale experiment to demonstrate the practical implications of the stability results would significantly strengthen the paper. The connection to improved sample complexity is asserted without validation.
* **Formatting and Notation Inconsistencies:** Numerous minor formatting issues exist (e.g., inconsistent use of boldface, spacing issues). The notation, while mostly consistent, can be dense and requires careful attention. Several equations are spread across multiple columns.
* **Over-Citation:** The reference list is *extensive* but contains many citations that are only tangentially related to the core contribution.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Rigorous theoretical analysis of CNN properties.
* Extension of existing work on scattering transforms and CKNs.
* Potential insights into the role of stability in generalization.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* Incremental contribution and lack of novelty.
* Excessive technical detail and lack of a clear, overarching narrative.
* Limited empirical validation and practical relevance.
* Numerous minor flaws in presentation and formatting.
* Discretization and kernel approximation issues not sufficiently addressed.



**Overall:**

The paper presents a technically sound but ultimately incremental contribution. The excessive level of detail and lack of empirical validation detract from the significance of the results. The numerous minor flaws further diminish the paper's overall quality. While the authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the theoretical tools involved, the paper feels more like a detailed technical exercise than a compelling scientific contribution. Therefore, I recommend rejection. I encourage the authors to consider streamlining their presentation, focusing on the most significant results, and including some empirical validation to demonstrate the practical relevance of their work.
