- Proposes a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes CKNs and links to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Main contributions: kernel-based translation-invariance and diffeomorphism stability; translating stability to predictors via RKHS norm control; connecting framework to standard CNNs.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous mathematical foundations and well-structured proofs.  
- Defines key operators (patch extraction, kernel mapping, pooling) and proves stability bounds under diffeomorphisms.  
- Proofs grounded in harmonic analysis and RKHS theory; addresses pooling-induced norm issues and mitigation via nonlinear prediction layers.  
- Clear theoretical connection between stability and generalization through RKHS norm control.  
- Demonstrates that stability results apply to a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations (including smoothed ReLU).  
- Exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and accessible to the NIPS community with consistent notation.  
- Original: extends Mallat’s scattering transform and CKN theory to more practical CNN architectures via a kernel perspective.  
- Significant: advances understanding of why CNNs work and suggests RKHS-based regularization for improved stability and sample efficiency.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on smooth homogeneous activations limits direct applicability to standard ReLU networks.  
- Additional limitations: limited empirical validation and a weaker connection between continuous theory and discrete image implementations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and significant theoretical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
