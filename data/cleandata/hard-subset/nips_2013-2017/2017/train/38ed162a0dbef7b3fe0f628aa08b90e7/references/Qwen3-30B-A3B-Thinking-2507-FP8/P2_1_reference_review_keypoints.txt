- Introduces a kernel framework that analyzes CNN invariance and stability by generalizing convolutional kernel networks to continuous signals.  
- Develops multilayer convolutional kernels to study translation-invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Shows the kernel functional space contains a wide class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations, enabling transfer of stability results.  
- Establishes theoretical stability guarantees analogous to the scattering transform but in a more general framework.  
- Connects RKHS norms to control of stability and generalization, suggesting a principled regularization perspective.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical development with detailed proofs and careful handling of continuous-to-discrete transitions.  
- Unifies analysis of CKNs and standard CNNs, offering a new theoretical lens on why CNNs work.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and sufficient context for scattering-transform newcomers.  
- Lacks empirical validation demonstrating how theoretical insights affect practical performance.  
- Practical applicability limited by most CNNs using non-smooth ReLU activations; results rely on smooth activation assumptions.  
- Accessibility is limited by heavy use of functional and harmonic analysis, reducing appeal to broader audiences.  
- Computational trade-offs between the theoretical framework and practical implementations are not fully explored.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original, and rigorous theoretical contribution with clear implications for stability and generalization.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pro-accept: strong theory, rigorous proofs, unifying framework, and important implications for invariance and generalization.  
- Con-accept: limited empirical support, steep mathematical prerequisites, and indirect connection to non-smooth practical CNNs.
