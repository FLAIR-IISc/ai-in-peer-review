## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel (CKN) that generalizes existing CKNs and encompasses a broad class of standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations. They demonstrate that this kernel-derived functional space exhibits invariance and stability properties analogous to those of the scattering transform, specifically Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms. Importantly, they show that these stability results translate to predictive models, suggesting a connection between stability and generalization performance – potentially leading to improved sample complexity.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon several key areas. It draws heavily from the theory of scattering transforms [Bruna & Mallat, 2013] regarding invariance and stability, applying similar harmonic analysis techniques to a more general class of CNN architectures. It leverages the framework of convolutional kernel networks [Mairal et al., 2015, 2016], extending them to continuous domains. The paper also acknowledges connections to research on group invariance [Cohen & Welling, 2017], kernel methods for deep learning [Cho & Saul, 2009], and the interplay between stability and robustness to adversarial examples [Cisse et al., 2017].  The authors are thorough in situating their work within this existing landscape.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for understanding the invariance and stability properties of CNNs, moving beyond empirical observations. The use of kernel methods allows for a formal analysis that is often lacking in deep learning theory.
* **Generalization of Existing Frameworks:** The construction of the multilayer convolutional kernel generalizes both CKNs and a large class of standard CNNs, offering a unified framework for analysis.
* **Connection to Stability and Generalization:** The demonstration of a link between stability (measured through the RKHS norm) and generalization is a significant contribution. This suggests a principled way to regularize CNNs and potentially improve sample complexity.
* **Clear Presentation:**  Despite the mathematical complexity, the paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The notation is mostly clear and consistent.
* **Thorough Related Work:**  The authors demonstrate a strong awareness of related research and appropriately credit existing work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Implications & Computational Cost:** While the theoretical results are compelling, the paper lacks a concrete discussion of the practical implications. Implementing the full kernel framework as presented could be computationally prohibitive.  The connection between the theoretical RKHS norm and practical regularization techniques (e.g., weight decay) needs further elaboration.
* **Homogeneous Activations:** The requirement for smooth homogeneous activations is a limitation. While the authors acknowledge ReLU's homogeneity, its non-smoothness is a concern.  The extent to which real-world CNNs utilizing non-homogeneous activations benefit from this analysis is unclear.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper is primarily theoretical.  A small-scale empirical study demonstrating the practical benefits of the derived stability results (e.g., improved generalization with fewer samples) would significantly strengthen the contribution.
* **Assumptions (A1-A3):** The assumptions (A1-A3) regarding the kernel mapping operators (norm preservation, non-expansiveness) are somewhat strong and may not hold for all kernels. A discussion on the sensitivity of the results to deviations from these assumptions would be valuable.
* **Discretization Details:** While the discretization section is present, it feels somewhat rushed and relies on a lot of hand-waving regarding parameter choices (e.g., *e<sub>k</sub>*, *s<sub>k</sub>*). A more detailed analysis of the impact of discretization on the stability properties would be useful.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and mathematically rigorous analysis of CNNs using kernel methods.
* It offers valuable theoretical insights into the relationship between stability, invariance, and generalization.
* The generalization of existing CKN frameworks is a significant contribution.
* The work is well-written and situated within the relevant literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of practical validation and discussion of computational costs limits the immediate impact.
* The requirement for smooth homogeneous activations restricts the applicability of the results.
* The theoretical framework, while elegant, may be difficult to translate into practical improvements for standard CNN architectures.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep convolutional networks. The kernel-based approach provides a powerful framework for analyzing invariance and stability, and the connection to generalization is particularly promising. However, the lack of empirical validation and the limitations imposed by the requirement for homogeneous activations prevent it from being a truly outstanding paper. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, they should:

1.  Discuss the computational challenges associated with implementing the full kernel framework and potential approximations.
2.  Elaborate on the connection between the theoretical RKHS norm and practical regularization techniques.
3.  Include a small-scale empirical study demonstrating the benefits of the derived stability results.
4.  Discuss the sensitivity of the results to deviations from assumptions (A1-A3).
5. Provide more details and discussion on the discretization procedure.




**Confidence:** 7/10
