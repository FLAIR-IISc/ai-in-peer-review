- Presents a theoretical kernel-based framework generalizing the scattering transform to multilayer convolutional kernels that encompass CKNs and certain CNNs.  
- Main technical achievement: Theorem 6—rigorous stability bound to diffeomorphisms for the multilayer representation with per-layer commutator analysis.  
- Shows the RKHS norm of the representation controls both stability and generalization (Proposition 7).  
- Extends framework to group invariance (Section 4) and situates work relative to scattering, CKNs, and group invariance literature.  
- Paper is comprehensive and well-structured with clear definitions and careful analysis of patch extraction, kernel mapping, and pooling.  
- Strength: Rigorous, well-motivated theoretical contributions and proofs.  
- Major weakness: No empirical validation or experiments to demonstrate practical relevance on real datasets.  
- Weakness: Connection to standard CNNs is abstract; unclear how theory guides practical network design or training.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity and feasibility of the kernel-based approach are not analyzed; trade-offs of finite-dimensional approximations are omitted.  
- Weakness: Insufficiently distinguishes contributions from related kernel-based CNN and CKN work; unclear specific improvements over prior constructions.  
- Significance: Original and significant theoretical link between stability to diffeomorphisms and generalization via RKHS, advancing understanding of CNNs.  
- Limitation: Impact limited by lack of empirical results and tenuous practical applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and valuable theoretical paper but needs experiments and practical/computational discussion to strengthen impact.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—important theory but requires empirical validation and clearer practical/computational pathways for NIPS.
