- Proposes a multilayer convolutional positive-definite kernel on continuous domains that induces CNN-like feature maps (patch extract, kernel map, pooling) with stability to small diffeomorphisms and increasing translation invariance with depth.  
- Extends to global invariance via group-equivariant patch extraction and pooling and shows a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lie in the induced RKHS with explicit norm bounds.  
- Discusses discretization and finite-dimensional kernel approximations (CKNs), and how stability properties persist under these approximations.  

Relation to prior work:
- Generalizes scattering transform stability to homogeneous dot-product kernels and embeds a class of CNNs via RKHS, extending Mallat/Bruna’s framework.  
- Extends CKN construction to continuous domains and clarifies when discretized approximations preserve stability.  
- Provides a kernel/RKHS perspective on group-equivariant CNNs and group-pooling consistent with prior G-CNN and group-scattering work.  
- Connects RKHS norms to Lipschitz control of predictions, relating stability to generalization/robustness intuitively.  

Quality — technical soundness:
- Results are mathematically careful, using standard tools (Schur’s test, commutator bounds, Gaussian smoothing) and deliver a core stability bound C1(1+n)‖∇τ‖∞ + (C2/σn)‖τ‖∞ explaining depth and pooling scale effects.  
- The commutator bound for shifted kernels (Lemma 4) and the CNN-in-RKHS construction (Proposition 7) are nontrivial and technically substantial.  

Quality — assumptions and limitations:
- Stability requires norm-preserving, nonexpansive kernel maps and relations between patch size and pooling scale; boundary effects are ignored in the continuous analysis.  
- The covered CNN class needs smooth homogeneous activations (ReLU handled via smoothing/homogenization) and uses a nonstandard homogenization term; discretization assumes stable deconvolution and delicate filter/subsampling regimes.  
- Constant dependence on kernel/patch parameters is mostly qualitative, linear depth dependence may be pessimistic, and RKHS→generalization claims are conceptual without explicit bounds.  

Quality — empirical support:
- Entirely theoretical with no experiments to quantify constants, validate deformation stability, or demonstrate practical design benefits (e.g., σn growth).  

Clarity:
- Paper is dense but well organized; operator viewpoint and CNN connections are clearly explained with proofs deferred to appendices.  
- Suggests more explicit discussion of constant sizes and choices for σk, ek, sk; a concrete activation/kernel example for Lemma B.2; a figure for group pooling; and a small empirical/synthetic stability demonstration.  

Originality:
- Meaningfully extends scattering stability to general convolutional kernels, unifies scattering/CKN/CNN under an RKHS lens, and offers novel commutator analysis; group-invariance integration is appropriate though conceptually aligned with prior work.  

Significance:
- Advances theoretical understanding of stability and invariance in deep convolutional representations and ties prediction stability to RKHS norm control, with potential theoretical and practical influence despite limited immediate empirical impact.  

Pros:
- Provides rigorous multilayer stability bounds with architectural implications.  
- Unifies scattering, CKNs, and a class of CNNs in a single RKHS framework with explicit norm bounds for CNNs.  
- Treats discretization and kernel approximations thoughtfully, showing stability preservation under projection.  
- Clean extension to group equivariance and global invariance via group pooling.  

Cons:
- Lacks empirical validation and quantitative constants; tightness of bounds untested.  
- Assumptions (smooth homogeneous activations, homogenization, deconvolution stability) may limit applicability to common CNNs.  
- Linear depth dependence and pooling-related norm loss raise scalability concerns for the guarantees.  
- Group-invariance section is incremental relative to existing literature.  

Recommendation:
- Strong theoretical contribution worth acceptance despite practical gaps; small-scale experiments and explicit constant estimates would strengthen the paper.
