## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel, generalizing convolutional kernel networks (CKNs), and analyze its translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms. They demonstrate that this kernel framework captures a broad class of CNNs and that the resulting functional space allows for theoretical guarantees on stability and generalization, suggesting a link between stability and improved sample complexity. The work builds upon existing theoretical foundations in scattering transforms and kernel methods, aiming to bridge the gap between the empirical success of CNNs and a rigorous mathematical understanding of their behavior.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper presents a solid theoretical framework built upon kernel methods and harmonic analysis. The use of positive definite kernels provides a powerful tool for analyzing the functional space of CNNs and deriving guarantees on stability and invariance.
* **Generalization of Existing Work:** The authors successfully generalize the theory of scattering transforms to a broader class of CNNs, including those beyond the strictly wavelet-based architecture. This extension is significant as it allows for theoretical insights into more commonly used CNN architectures.
* **Connection to Sample Complexity:** The identified link between stability (RKHS norm) and generalization (sample complexity) is a compelling contribution. This suggests a principled way to regularize CNNs and potentially improve their performance with limited data.
* **Detailed Mathematical Analysis:** The paper provides a thorough mathematical analysis, including rigorous proofs for key results concerning stability and invariance. The inclusion of relevant mathematical tools in the appendix enhances readability and reproducibility.
* **Practical Considerations:** The authors address the practical aspects of discretization and kernel approximation, recognizing the need for finite-dimensional representations and providing insights into the implementation of the proposed framework using CKNs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions & Simplifications:**  The analysis relies on several assumptions, such as smoothness of activation functions and the regularity of diffeomorphisms (‖∇τ‖∞ < 1/2).  While common in similar theoretical work, these assumptions limit the direct applicability of the results to all CNN architectures and real-world scenarios. The homogenization process required to encompass standard CNNs introduces an artificial element which is not commonly found in real architectures. 
* **Computational Complexity:** While the authors acknowledge the computational challenges of working with infinite-dimensional kernels, the paper doesn’t offer concrete solutions or practical algorithms for efficiently implementing the proposed framework beyond leveraging CKN approximations. The scalability of this approach remains a significant concern.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper primarily focuses on theoretical analysis with limited empirical validation. While the theoretical results are valuable, demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed framework through experiments on benchmark datasets would strengthen the claims. 
* **Norm Preservation Concern:** The authors acknowledge that the proposed framework doesn’t inherently preserve the norm, and while they mitigate this by suggesting a final non-linear layer, the loss of norm information in intermediate layers could potentially impact the performance of the model.
* **Overlapping with Scattering Transforms:** A significant portion of the theory is built upon the existing work on scattering transforms. While extending these ideas is valuable, the paper needs to more clearly delineate its unique contributions and demonstrate how it advances beyond the scattering transform framework.

**Originality:**

The paper offers a novel combination of kernel methods, harmonic analysis, and CNN theory. While building upon existing work in scattering transforms and CKNs, the generalization to a wider class of CNNs and the explicit connection to sample complexity represent a significant contribution. The analysis provides a different, kernel-based perspective on the invariance and stability properties of deep networks.

**Significance:**

The results have the potential to impact our understanding of CNNs and to guide the development of more robust and generalizable models. The connection between stability and sample complexity offers a principled approach to regularization, and the kernel framework provides a theoretical foundation for analyzing and designing CNN architectures. The insights gained from this work could be valuable to both researchers and practitioners in the field of deep learning.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Provides a solid theoretical foundation for understanding the behavior of CNNs.
* Generalizes existing theory on scattering transforms to a broader class of models.
* Establishes a link between stability and sample complexity, offering potential benefits for regularization.
* Presents a rigorous mathematical analysis with detailed proofs.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Relies on several simplifying assumptions that limit the direct applicability of the results.
* Lacks substantial empirical validation.
* Faces computational challenges related to kernel approximations.
* Overlaps with existing work on scattering transforms.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of deep convolutional networks. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and presents a novel perspective on the invariance and stability properties of these models. While the limitations regarding assumptions, computational complexity, and empirical validation are noteworthy, the theoretical insights and potential impact of the work justify acceptance. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The authors should address the following points:

*   **Discuss limitations more thoroughly:**  Acknowledge the impact of the simplifying assumptions and discuss potential avenues for relaxing them in future work.
*   **Consider adding limited experiments:** Including a small-scale experiment to demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed framework, even on a simple task, would significantly strengthen the paper.
*   **More clearly articulate the novelty:** Provide a more detailed comparison with existing work on scattering transforms, highlighting the specific advancements made in this paper.
