This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors aim to understand the invariance and stability of CNN representations, building on previous work with scattering transforms and convolutional kernel networks (CKNs). They introduce a multilayer convolutional kernel and demonstrate that it encompasses a broad class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions. The core contributions lie in establishing stability guarantees under diffeomorphic transformations and relating these to generalization performance via the RKHS norm.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a fundamental question about the theoretical underpinnings of deep learning. The connection between kernel methods and CNNs is a fruitful direction, offering tools from harmonic analysis to analyze network behavior. The extension of existing theories (scattering transforms, CKNs) to more general CNN architectures is a valuable contribution. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, relate their work to a comprehensive set of prior research (extensive and relevant references), and provide a rigorous mathematical treatment. The notation is generally clear, and the organization is logical, progressing from kernel construction to stability analysis and finally, connecting back to standard CNNs.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The work is grounded in solid mathematical principles, leveraging concepts from kernel methods and harmonic analysis.
*   **Broad Applicability:** The results apply not only to CKNs but also to a wider class of CNNs with specific activation functions.
*   **Clear Connections to Generalization:** The link between stability (measured by the RKHS norm) and generalization is well-established and potentially insightful.
*   **Comprehensive Related Work:** The authors demonstrate a thorough understanding of the relevant literature.
*   **Well-Organized & Clearly Written:** Despite the mathematical complexity, the paper is generally well-structured and accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Practical Implications:** While the theoretical results are interesting, the paper lacks a clear discussion of how these insights can be leveraged to improve CNN design or training in practice. The conditions on activation functions (smooth, homogeneous) may be restrictive.
*   **Computational Complexity:** The kernel-based framework, while theoretically elegant, may not be computationally tractable for large-scale problems without efficient approximation techniques (already mentioned in the CKN context).
*   **Assumptions:** Several assumptions are made regarding signal regularity and kernel properties. While justified, the robustness of the results to violations of these assumptions is not explored.
*   **Incremental Contribution:** The extension of scattering transform stability results to a broader class of CNNs, while valuable, feels somewhat incremental. The core ideas build significantly on prior work, and the novelty lies more in the combination and generalization than in fundamentally new insights.
*   **Discretization Details:** The discussion on discretization is present, yet the complexities of bridging the gap between the continuous kernel framework and practical discrete implementations could be further elaborated.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a fundamental theoretical question in deep learning.
*   The mathematical analysis is rigorous and well-executed.
*   The results extend existing theoretical frameworks (scattering transforms, CKNs) to a broader class of models.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The practical implications of the results are limited.
*   The work is somewhat incremental, building heavily on prior research.
*   The assumptions made may limit the applicability of the results.
*   The computational complexity of the kernel-based framework may be a barrier to practical use.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of the properties of deep convolutional networks. While the practical implications are not immediately obvious, the rigorous analysis and extension of existing theoretical frameworks are commendable. The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to researchers in the field. However, the incremental nature of the contribution and the lack of concrete guidance for practical applications temper the overall impact. I would rate it as a **weak accept**.  More compelling discussion of potential avenues for leveraging these theoretical insights in practical CNN design would strengthen the paper significantly.
