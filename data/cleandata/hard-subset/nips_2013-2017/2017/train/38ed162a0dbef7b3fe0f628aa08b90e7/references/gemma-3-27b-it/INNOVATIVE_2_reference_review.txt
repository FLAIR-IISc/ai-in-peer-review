This paper explores the geometry of functional spaces induced by deep convolutional neural networks through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors build upon convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and extend existing theoretical results, notably those from the scattering transform, to a broader class of CNNs. The core idea – representing CNNs within a kernel framework to analyze invariance and stability – is compelling and potentially impactful. The work demonstrates that these architectures exhibit Lipschitz stability to diffeomorphisms, linking stability to potential improvements in sample complexity.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of kernel methods to analyze the functional spaces of CNNs is a fresh perspective. While kernel methods *within* neural networks are explored, framing CNNs *as* residing within a kernel-defined space, enabling theoretical analysis of stability and invariance, is a significant contribution.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper presents a substantial theoretical framework, building on established concepts from kernel methods and harmonic analysis (scattering transforms). The lemmas and theorems are clearly stated, and the proof sketches provided suggest a solid mathematical foundation (although detailed proofs are in the appendix).
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The authors thoroughly connect their work to relevant literature, including CKNs, scattering transforms, and classical kernel methods. This contextualization is valuable and helps position the contribution within the broader field.
* **Potential for Impact:** Establishing a link between stability, invariance, and generalization could have significant implications for understanding and improving CNN training and performance. The potential for improved sample complexity is particularly exciting.
* **Extension to CNNs:** Successfully demonstrating that the theoretical results extend beyond CKNs to a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations strengthens the impact and applicability of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper is heavily theoretical. While the authors discuss potential implications for practical CNNs, there's a lack of empirical validation. Demonstrating the stability properties on real-world datasets, or showing improvements in sample complexity, would significantly bolster the claims.
* **Assumptions and Constraints:** The theoretical results rely on several assumptions (e.g., smoothness of activations, kernel properties, regularity of diffeomorphisms). While these are standard in related fields, their impact on the applicability of the results is not fully discussed. Relaxing these assumptions or analyzing their sensitivity would be valuable.
* **Homogeneous Activations:** The restriction to smooth homogeneous activations, while allowing for theoretical tractability, limits the direct applicability to widely used activations like ReLU. The discussion acknowledges this but doesn’t fully address how to bridge the gap.
* **Complexity of the Framework:** The mathematical framework is dense and might be difficult for readers unfamiliar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis to fully grasp. More intuitive explanations or examples could improve accessibility.
* **Discretization Details:** While the discussion of discretization is present, it feels somewhat tacked on. A more in-depth treatment of the interplay between continuous theory and discrete implementations would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a fundamental and important question regarding the theoretical foundations of CNNs.
* The proposed kernel framework provides a novel and potentially powerful tool for analyzing CNNs.
* The theoretical results are rigorous and well-motivated, building upon established concepts.
* The extension to general CNNs enhances the practical relevance of the work.
* The paper, despite its theoretical nature, opens up new avenues for research in understanding and improving deep learning.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of empirical validation raises concerns about the practical significance of the theoretical results.
* The assumptions and constraints may limit the applicability of the framework.
* The complexity of the mathematical framework might hinder accessibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and potentially significant contribution. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-motivated, and the connection to broader concepts in kernel methods and harmonic analysis is clear. While the lack of empirical validation is a concern, I believe the novelty and theoretical depth outweigh this weakness.  I am willing to overlook the incomplete empirical assessment, as the core idea is sufficiently original and promising to warrant publication. The authors have taken a bold approach, and even if the full implications are not yet clear, this work provides a valuable new perspective on the fundamental properties of CNNs.

**Recommendation:** Accept. I encourage the authors to prioritize empirical validation and exploration of more practical activation functions in future work.
