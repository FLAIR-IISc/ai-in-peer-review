- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework that generalizes convolutional kernel networks to analyze CNN invariance and stability.  
- Shows stability guarantees comparable to Mallat’s scattering transform but applicable to a broader class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Connects the kernel framework to practical CNN architectures (Section 5) and links stability to predictive models via RKHS norm control.  
- Rigorous mathematical foundations with well-structured proofs for key stability results (Proposition 3, Lemma 4, Lemma 5, Theorem 6).  
- Carefully states conditions and limitations (e.g., regularity condition ‖∇τ‖∞ < 1/2) and discusses norm preservation issues (Section 3.1).  
- Lacks empirical validation—no experiments to show how theoretical stability manifests in real CNNs or vision tasks.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized for mathematically trained ML researchers but assumes strong background in functional/harmonic analysis and kernels.  
- Connection to practical CNN implementations is somewhat abstract and could use a concrete example to aid accessibility.  
- Original and significant: bridges scattering transform theory and standard CNNs, embedding CNNs in an RKHS framework.  
- Theoretical insights could inform robustness, regularization, and generalization but practical implications for model design are not fully articulated.  
- Does not address applicability to recent architectures (e.g., residual networks).  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel bridge to CNNs, clear proofs, addresses fundamental question of CNN invariance.  
- Weaknesses: no experiments, limited accessibility, abstract practical connection, tangential norm-preservation discussion, omission of newer architectures.  
- Recommendation: accept—strong theoretical contribution deserving NIPS publication if strengthened with clearer practical links.  
- Specific suggestions: emphasize Section 5 in the introduction, discuss how results inform layer/pooling design, add a small empirical study, condense the norm-preservation section, and compare to recent CNN stability work.
