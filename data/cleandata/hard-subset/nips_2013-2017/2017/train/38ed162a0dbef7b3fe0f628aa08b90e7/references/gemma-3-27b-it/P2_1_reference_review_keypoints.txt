- Paper constructs a multilayer convolutional kernel showing it contains a broad class of CNNs and proves stability to diffeomorphisms.  
- Uses kernel methods to provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of CNN invariance and stability.  
- Extends scattering transform and convolutional kernel network (CKN) theory to a wider range of CNN architectures.  
- Establishes a link between stability (RKHS norm) and generalization, suggesting theoretical grounding for regularization.  
- Presentation is mostly clear and well-structured, with consistent but dense notation.  
- Potentially high impact by improving theoretical understanding of CNN robustness and sample efficiency.  
- Mathematical formalism is dense and may be inaccessible to readers without strong background in kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  
- Lacks empirical validation demonstrating practical benefits or the RKHS–generalization link on real models/datasets.  
- Relies on assumptions (smooth activations, specific kernel properties) that may not hold for many practical CNN architectures.  
- Novelty is moderate: extends existing results but core stability results are similar to scattering transform theory.  
- Discretization and connection to discrete implementations are discussed insufficiently and feel hand-wavy.  
- Quality score: 7/10 (technically sound but weakened by lack of experiments).  
- Clarity score: 6/10 (dense and demanding for readers).  
- Originality score: 7/10 (valuable extension to broader CNNs).  
- Significance score: 6/10 (theoretical importance tempered by lack of empirical evidence).  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous kernel-based analysis, extends prior theory, links stability to generalization, deepens understanding of CNNs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: primarily theoretical, high mathematical complexity, incremental advances, and restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add preliminary empirical results, better discuss assumption limitations, and clarify discretization details.
