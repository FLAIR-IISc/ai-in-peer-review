- Summary: Develops a multilayer convolutional kernel framework for CNNs, analyzing translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms and linking stability to generalization.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on scattering transforms and convolutional kernel networks, and distinguishes itself from other kernel/deep-learning analyses.  
- Strength: Provides rigorous, detailed theoretical analysis using kernel methods and harmonic analysis.  
- Strength: Demonstrates the framework contains a large class of practical CNN architectures, bridging theory and practice.  
- Strength: Establishes a valuable link between stability (via RKHS norm) and generalization/regularization.  
- Strength: Successfully extends scattering-transform theory to a broader class of CNNs.  
- Strength: Clearly motivates the study by connecting geometric properties to practical issues like sample complexity.  
- Weakness: Mathematical formalism is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with kernels and harmonic analysis.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical validation — lacks experiments showing practical benefits of the theoretical findings.  
- Weakness: Analysis depends on assumptions (smooth activations, specific kernel forms) whose relaxation is not explored.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of computational and scaling challenges for implementing CKN-based methods.  
- Weakness: Loss of norm preservation due to pooling is acknowledged but not fully addressed in its impact or mitigation.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound contribution with practical relevance and an important stability–generalization insight.  
- Against acceptance: Lack of empirical support, high mathematical complexity, and possibly restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical paper that needs empirical validation and clearer exposition; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add empirical validation strategies or small experiments; provide more intuitive explanations/examples; discuss sensitivity to assumptions; more thoroughly address norm-preservation issues.  
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10.
