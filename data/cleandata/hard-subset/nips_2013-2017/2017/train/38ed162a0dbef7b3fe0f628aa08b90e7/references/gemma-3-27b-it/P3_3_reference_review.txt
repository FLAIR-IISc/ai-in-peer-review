## Review of "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the theoretical properties of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) through the lens of positive definite kernels. The authors construct a multilayer convolutional kernel (CKN) and analyze its translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms. They demonstrate that this kernel framework encompasses both CKNs and a broader class of CNNs with smooth, homogeneous activation functions. Crucially, they establish theoretical guarantees mirroring those of the scattering transform, but for learning-based architectures. The core argument is that stability (captured by the RKHS norm) relates to generalization performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon a rich body of research. It leverages the foundation laid by convolutional kernel networks [15, 16] and the theoretical framework for invariance and stability developed for scattering transforms [5, 17]. It also acknowledges connections to kernel methods for deep learning [2, 7, 10] and the exploration of group invariance in CNNs [6, 9, 22]. The authors do a commendable job situating their work within this landscape.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis of CNNs, grounding them in the well-established theory of positive definite kernels and RKHSs. This provides a level of theoretical understanding often missing in empirical deep learning research.
* **Generalization Beyond CKNs:**  The demonstration that the framework extends beyond CKNs to include a wider class of standard CNNs is a significant strength. This broadens the applicability of their findings.
* **Connection to Stability and Generalization:** The paper explicitly links stability, measured by the RKHS norm, to generalization performance, offering a theoretical rationale for regularization techniques. This is a crucial and impactful insight.
* **Clear Presentation:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is clearly defined, and the authors provide a helpful roadmap through the complex theoretical arguments. The inclusion of lemmas and proofs in the appendix enhances readability.
* **Discretization discussion:** The thoughtful discussion on the bridge between continuous and discrete models is well motivated and provides a realistic perspective to the theory.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Implications are Not Fully Explored:** While the theoretical results are strong, the paper doesn’t fully articulate *how* these insights can be directly translated into practical improvements in CNN architecture or training.  For example, how can the RKHS norm be efficiently estimated or used as a regularizer in practice? 
* **Homogeneous Activation Requirement:**  The reliance on homogeneous activation functions (or smoothed approximations of ReLU) is a limitation. While justified by the theoretical framework, it excludes commonly used activation functions without further analysis of the impact.  Exploring the sensitivity of the results to deviations from homogeneity would be valuable.
* **Computational Complexity:** The theoretical framework, while elegant, doesn't address the computational challenges associated with working with kernels in high-dimensional spaces. The connection to practical CKN approximations is mentioned, but a more detailed discussion of scalability would be beneficial.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper lacks empirical validation. Demonstrating the relationship between stability (estimated via proxy) and generalization on standard datasets would significantly strengthen the claims. While the focus is theoretical, some empirical evidence would be highly valuable.
* **Some notation is confusing:** While generally good, the frequent dropping of norm subscripts can sometimes make it difficult to follow the mathematical arguments.  A more consistent notation might improve clarity.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and mathematically rigorous analysis of CNNs, offering valuable theoretical insights into their properties.
* The extension beyond CKNs to a broader class of CNNs enhances the impact and relevance of the work.
* The connection between stability and generalization is a significant theoretical contribution.
* The work addresses a fundamental question in deep learning: understanding the geometry of the functional spaces in which CNNs operate.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of practical guidance and empirical validation limits the immediate applicability of the results.
* The restrictive assumption of homogeneous activation functions reduces the scope of the analysis.
* The computational complexity of the kernel framework remains a concern.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a significant contribution to our understanding of CNNs. While the lack of direct practical implications and empirical validation are weaknesses, the rigor and novelty of the analysis are compelling. The paper is well-written and clearly presented. I believe the strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I **recommend acceptance** with the caveat that the authors consider addressing the limitations outlined above in future work. Encouraging the authors to explore potential avenues for practical application and empirical validation would further enhance the impact of this valuable contribution.



**Confidence:** 8/10 (Strongly leaning towards acceptance, but acknowledging the limitations)
