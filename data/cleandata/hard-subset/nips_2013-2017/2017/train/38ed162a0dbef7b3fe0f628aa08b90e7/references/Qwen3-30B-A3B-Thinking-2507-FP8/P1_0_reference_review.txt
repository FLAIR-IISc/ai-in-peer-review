

# Peer Review: "Invariance and Stability of Deep Convolutional Representations"

## Summary

This paper addresses a fundamental theoretical question about convolutional neural networks (CNNs): the nature of their invariance properties and the geometry of the functional spaces they inhabit. The authors introduce a kernel framework that generalizes convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and provides a unified theoretical foundation for studying invariance and stability in CNNs. They show that their framework encompasses standard CNNs with smooth homogeneous activation functions, and prove stability guarantees to diffeomorphisms (small deformations) that match those of the scattering transform [17], but for more general CNN architectures. The paper also connects stability to generalization through RKHS norm control, demonstrating how stability may lead to improved sample complexity.

The work builds on Mallat's scattering transform [17] and Mairal's CKNs [15,16], extending them to provide theoretical guarantees for more standard CNN architectures. It makes significant connections between group invariance, stability to deformations, and the RKHS norm of predictive models.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Rigor**: The paper provides a mathematically sound framework with clear definitions and rigorous proofs. The stability analysis (Theorem 6) is particularly strong, establishing bounds for the representation's stability to diffeomorphisms that match the desired formalism from the scattering transform literature.

2. **Unifying Framework**: The paper successfully bridges the gap between the scattering transform (which provides strong theoretical guarantees but uses fixed filters) and standard CNNs (which learn filters but lack theoretical guarantees). The insight that CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations can be embedded in the RKHS defined by their kernel construction is significant.

3. **Practical Relevance**: The paper carefully addresses the discretization problem, connecting the continuous theory to practical implementation through the CKN model [15]. The analysis of signal preservation and recovery (Lemmas 1 and 2) is particularly valuable for understanding how to implement the theory in practice.

4. **Clear Connections to Generalization**: The paper effectively demonstrates how stability properties translate to predictive models through RKHS norm control, linking theoretical properties to practical concerns like generalization. The discussion of how "large margin" functions (low RKHS norm) generalize better provides a valuable theoretical perspective.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Experimental Validation**: While the theoretical contributions are strong, the paper lacks empirical validation. There are no experiments showing how these stability properties manifest in actual CNN training or how they might improve performance on standard vision tasks. The connection between the theory and practical deep learning outcomes remains largely conceptual.

2. **Accessibility**: The paper is quite technical and assumes familiarity with RKHS, harmonic analysis, and signal processing. The dense mathematical presentation may limit its accessibility to a broader audience of machine learning researchers, particularly those without strong backgrounds in these areas.

3. **Limited Discussion of Modern Architectures**: The paper focuses on traditional CNN architectures but doesn't address how their framework might extend to more recent innovations like residual connections, attention mechanisms, or transformer-based architectures. This limits the paper's relevance to current deep learning practice.

4. **Implementation Details**: While the paper discusses discretization, it doesn't provide concrete guidance on how to implement the kernel framework efficiently for large-scale problems. The practical computational considerations for using this framework in real-world applications are not sufficiently addressed.

## Recommendation

This is a high-quality theoretical contribution that makes a significant advancement in understanding the mathematical foundations of CNNs. The paper's main strength is its rigorous theoretical framework that unifies and extends previous work on scattering networks and convolutional kernel networks. The stability results (Theorem 6) are particularly compelling and provide a principled understanding of why CNNs might generalize well.

The main limitations are the lack of empirical validation and the technical accessibility of the paper. While the theoretical contribution is strong, the absence of experiments to demonstrate the practical implications of the theory is a notable gap. Additionally, the paper's technical depth may limit its appeal to a broader audience.

## Final Assessment

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The mathematical analysis is thorough and builds on previous work in a meaningful way. (4.5/5)

**Clarity**: The paper is well-structured but highly technical. The mathematical notation is consistent but may be challenging for readers without a strong background in functional analysis. (3.5/5)

**Originality**: The paper provides a novel theoretical framework that extends previous work on scattering networks to standard CNN architectures, demonstrating a significant theoretical advance. (4.5/5)

**Significance**: The results provide fundamental insights into why CNNs work well, with potential implications for designing more stable and generalizable architectures. The connection between stability, RKHS norm, and generalization is particularly significant. (4.0/5)

**Overall**: This is a strong theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of CNNs. While the lack of empirical validation is a limitation, the paper's theoretical rigor and novelty make it a valuable addition to the field. I recommend acceptance, though the authors should consider adding a brief discussion of potential practical applications or implementation considerations to address the accessibility issue.

**Pro**: Strong theoretical framework, rigorous stability analysis, clear connection to generalization, well-motivated extension of previous work.

**Con**: Limited empirical validation, highly technical presentation, lacks discussion of modern architectures, minimal discussion of practical implementation challenges.

This paper makes a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of convolutional neural networks, providing a unifying framework that connects stability properties to generalization. It represents an important step toward developing a more principled understanding of why CNNs work well in practice.
