- Presents a multilayer convolutional kernel framework mirroring CNNs (patch extraction, pointwise RKHS maps, Gaussian pooling) with stability to small diffeomorphisms and near-translation invariance.  
- Main stability result (Theorem 6, Eq. 4): representation is Lipschitz with constants scaling linearly in depth and inversely with the final pooling scale.  
- Analysis covers discretization (sampling/deconvolution recovery under subsampling) and finite-dimensional kernel approximations (CKNs).  
- Extends framework to global invariance via equivariant patch extraction and pooling on locally compact groups.  
- Proves a class of CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations lies in the multilayer RKHS with explicit norm bounds (Proposition 7), transferring stability and linking to generalization.  
- Positions work relative to prior art: generalizes Mallat’s scattering, connects to CKNs and group-equivariant nets, and provides layerwise commutator bounds beyond prior kernel viewpoints.  
- Assumptions are explicit and reasonable (norm-preserving/non-expansive kernel maps, bounded patch radii tied to pooling, small deformation ‖∇τ‖∞ < 1/2).  
- Technical contributions include a commutator bound for shifted pooling (Lemma 4) and control of non-commutation scaling as 1/σn (Lemma 5).  
- Theoretical path from continuous signals to discrete implementations is addressed and finite-dimensional projections preserve non-expansiveness.  
- Limitations are acknowledged: pooling-induced norm loss, border effects, small-deformation regime, and absence of experiments to test constants/tightness.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and self-contained, though notation is heavy.  
- Suggested clarifications: an early table of operators/norms, which activations satisfy Lemma B.2 with constants, example schedules for (Sk,σk) under discretization, and quantified border-effect conditions.  
- Novelty: multilayer RKHS that subsumes CKNs and admits a CNN class, new diffeomorphism stability with shifted-pooling commutator control, discrete recovery under subsampling, and group-invariant pooling in one framework.  
- Insightful conceptual bridge linking CNNs to RKHS with computable norms that imply stability beyond scattering.  
- Significance: advances theory of invariance/stability and suggests RKHS-based regularization choices, though immediate empirical impact is limited by differences from standard ReLU/BN pipelines and the small-deformation assumption.  
- Strengths: principled kernel–convolution–pooling–group construction, rigorous stability with explicit depth/scale dependence, discretization compatibility, and RKHS inclusion of CNNs.  
- Weaknesses: requires smooth homogeneous activations and local patch normalization (nonstandard vs ReLU), stability constants grow with depth, and guidance on σn trade-offs is limited.  
- Deconvolution-based recovery relies on small pooling scales; stability of deconvolution in realistic settings needs more analysis.  
- Lack of experiments to illustrate practical relevance or compare with scattering/CNN baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid, well-scoped theoretical advance with new tools and clear continuous-to-discrete path relevant to NeurIPS topics.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical validation, exclusion of common nonsmooth activations without smoothing/normalization, and applicability constrained to small diffeomorphisms.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful and rigorous theoretical contribution that would be strengthened by empirical evidence and broader activation coverage.
