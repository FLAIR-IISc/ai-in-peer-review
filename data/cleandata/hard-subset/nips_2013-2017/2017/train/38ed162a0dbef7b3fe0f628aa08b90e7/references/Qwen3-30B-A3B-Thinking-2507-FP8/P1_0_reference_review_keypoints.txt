- Summary: Introduces a kernel framework generalizing CKNs to study invariance and stability of CNNs, proving stability to diffeomorphisms and linking stability to generalization via RKHS norm control.

- Strength — Theoretical Rigor: Provides rigorous definitions and proofs, with a strong stability result (Theorem 6) matching scattering transform guarantees.

- Strength — Unifying Framework: Bridges scattering transforms and learnable CNNs, showing CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations can be embedded in the proposed RKHS.

- Strength — Practical Relevance: Addresses discretization and signal preservation/recovery (Lemmas 1–2), connecting continuous theory to implementable CKN models.

- Strength — Generalization Connection: Demonstrates how stability relates to generalization through RKHS norm control and large-margin reasoning.

- Weakness — Limited Experiments: No empirical validation showing how theoretical stability affects trained CNN performance on vision tasks.

- Weakness — Accessibility: Highly technical presentation assumes familiarity with RKHS, harmonic analysis, and signal processing, limiting broader accessibility.

- Weakness — Modern Architectures: Does not address extensions to residual connections, attention, or transformer-based models, reducing relevance to current practice.

- Weakness — Implementation Details: Lacks concrete guidance and computational considerations for efficient large-scale implementation of the framework.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance for its strong theoretical contribution, but request added discussion of practical applications and implementation considerations.

- Quality: 4.5/5 — technically sound with well-supported claims.

- Clarity: 3.5/5 — well-structured but mathematically dense and challenging.

- Originality: 4.5/5 — novel extension connecting scattering networks and standard CNNs.

- Significance: 4.0/5 — fundamental insights into CNN stability and generalization with potential practical implications.

- Pro: Strong theoretical framework, rigorous stability analysis, clear generalization links, well-motivated extension of prior work.

- Con: Lacks empirical validation, highly technical, omits modern architecture discussion, minimal practical implementation guidance.
