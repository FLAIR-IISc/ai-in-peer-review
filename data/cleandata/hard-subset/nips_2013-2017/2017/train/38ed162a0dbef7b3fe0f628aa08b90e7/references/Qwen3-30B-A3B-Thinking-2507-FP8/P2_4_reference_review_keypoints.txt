- Introduces a multilayer convolutional kernel framework generalizing CKNs and linking kernel representations to CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations.  
- Proves stability of the kernel representation to diffeomorphisms, analogous to the scattering transform.  
- Shows RKHS norm controls both stability and generalization, with Cauchy–Schwarz used to connect stability to predictive models.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical analysis with detailed proofs (Lemma 1, Proposition 3, Theorem 6) and addresses discretization.  
- Distinguishes carefully between continuous and discrete representations, important for practical application.  
- Derives how stability depends on pooling scale (σ_n) and number of layers (n), giving explicit constants.  
- Connects the theoretical framework to standard CNN architectures, embedding a broad class of smooth homogeneous-activation CNNs in the RKHS.  
- Advances theoretical understanding by unifying scattering transform theory and practical CNN architectures via kernels.  
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized writing and consistent notation; logical flow from kernel construction to stability to group invariance and CNN connections.  
- Acknowledges limitations: norm preservation not guaranteed and connection to CNNs excludes non-smooth activations like ReLU (only smoothed variants covered).  
- Lacks empirical validation and concrete examples showing how the theory guides model design, limiting practical demonstration.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with significant implications for understanding and designing stable convolutional architectures.
