- Presents a theoretical RKHS/kernel framework that generalizes convolutional kernel networks to continuous-domain CNN representations.  
- Rigorously analyzes translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms for CNNs, extending results previously limited to the scattering transform.  
- Shows CNNs with smooth homogeneous activations fit into the framework and that stability transfers to predictors via RKHS norm control.  
- Theorem 6 provides strong stability bounds showing dependence on layer depth and pooling scale.  
- Establishes a clear connection between RKHS norm, stability to deformation, and generalization (Lipschitz property).  
- Extends the theory to group invariance, showing how to achieve equivariance to other transformation groups via operator design.  
- Technically rigorous with well-justified assumptions (A1–A3) and full proofs in appendices.  
- Original contribution bridging scattering transforms and practical CNN architectures.  
- Significant implications for understanding why CNNs work and for guiding model design and regularization.  
- Mathematical density may limit accessibility to readers without functional analysis background.  
- Lacks empirical validation or concrete examples showing how theory informs specific CNN design choices.  
- Limited discussion of practical implementation details despite brief kernel-approximation remarks.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and significant theoretical contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
