- Constructs a multilayer convolutional positive definite kernel that captures a broad class of CNNs, including CKNs.  
- Proves translation invariance and stability to diffeomorphisms for representations in this kernel space, matching scattering-transform results for a wider set of CNNs.  
- Shows stability measured by the RKHS norm relates to generalization performance.  
- Builds on prior work in CKNs, scattering transforms, kernel methods, group invariance, and adversarial robustness.  
- Theoretical rigor: solid mathematical foundation using positive definite kernels and RKHS with detailed proofs.  
- Generality: framework covers a broader class of CNNs than prior scattering- or CKN-focused analyses.  
- Links stability (RKHS norm) to generalization, offering theoretical support for regularization benefits.  
- Provides a thorough discretization analysis to preserve signal information in practice.  
- Clear presentation and helpful notation make complex theory accessible.  
- Limited novelty: core ideas extend existing stability results rather than offering a breakthrough.  
- Practical implications are underexplored; lacks guidance on using insights to design CNNs or regularizers and on kernel choice impacts.  
- Relies on assumptions (smooth activations, homogeneous kernels) that may limit applicability and need clearer discussion.  
- Computational cost/scalability of RKHS-based approach acknowledged but not sufficiently addressed.
