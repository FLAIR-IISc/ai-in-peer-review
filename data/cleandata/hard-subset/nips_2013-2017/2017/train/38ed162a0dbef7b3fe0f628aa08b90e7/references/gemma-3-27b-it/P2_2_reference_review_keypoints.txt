- Paper develops a multi-layer convolutional kernel framework showing many CNNs lie in its RKHS, enabling theoretical analysis of invariance and stability.
- Authors prove invariance to diffeomorphisms and stability to deformations for a broad class of CNNs, extending scattering-transform-like results.
- Establishes a connection between stability (controlled by the RKHS norm) and generalization, motivating stability-promoting regularization.
- Builds on convolutional kernel networks (CKNs) and scattering transform theory, appropriately citing relevant prior work.
- Framework generalizes CKNs to encompass many standard CNN architectures, broadening applicability of theoretical guarantees.
- Theoretical treatment is rigorous with sound proofs (mostly in the appendix).
- Clear motivation and well-structured, generally readable exposition despite technical depth.
- Main results are conceptually similar to existing scattering transform results, limiting novelty.
- Practical implications are underdeveloped; paper lacks concrete guidance for using the theory to improve CNN design or training.
- Assumes smooth homogeneous activation functions, restricting applicability and needing clearer treatment for ReLU-like activations.
- Mathematical formalism is dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with kernel methods and harmonic analysis.
- Quality score: 8/10 (technically sound); Clarity: 7/10 (challenging mathematically); Originality: 6/10; Significance: 7/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis, broad applicability, stability–generalization link, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty vs. scattering transforms, weak practical guidance, potential accessibility issues due to complexity.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of practical implications, detail applicability to ReLU and common activations, and add a concise summary/table of key assumptions and their impacts.
