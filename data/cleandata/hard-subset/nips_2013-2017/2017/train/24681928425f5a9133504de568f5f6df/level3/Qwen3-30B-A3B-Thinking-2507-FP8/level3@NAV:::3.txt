

This paper proposes a cognitive model for human question asking by framing questions as programs that execute on possible world states to generate answers. The authors model question generation as a probabilistic process where the log-probability of a question is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity (length), answer type, and relevance features. They evaluate their approach in a Battleship domain where participants ask questions to identify ships on a grid, demonstrating the model's ability to predict human question patterns and generate novel, human-like questions.

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible. It makes an important contribution by showing how to generate compositional questions in a non-trivial domain, advancing both cognitive modeling and AI. However, the claim that the model reveals how people balance informativeness and complexity is not strongly supported. While the full model outperforms lesioned versions on log-likelihood, the correlation between predicted and human question frequencies is noisy due to sparse data (average Spearman's correlation 0.64), and bootstrap estimates indicate a 19% chance the EIG advantage is spurious. Surprisingly, models without EIG correlate as well with human judgments as the full model.

The paper lacks critical prior work, missing Hawkins (2015) on goal-based question models and Roberts (1996) on Question Under Discussion (QUD) framework, which are highly relevant to their approach. While the main contribution is valuable for demonstrating compositional question generation, the experimental evidence for EIG's role is weaker than claimed.

Quality: The methodology is sound with appropriate statistical evaluation, but the interpretation of results is somewhat overstated given the noisy correlation data.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

Originality: The program-based question representation is novel for modeling human question asking, though the core idea of question informativeness has precedents.

Significance: The approach enables more human-like question generation in AI systems, with potential applications in educational technology and cognitive modeling, making it a meaningful contribution to the field. The work demonstrates a promising path toward richer human-AI interaction through natural language questioning.
