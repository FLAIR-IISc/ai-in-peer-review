- Presents a computational model of human question asking as probabilistic program generation, composing conceptual building blocks into executable question-programs.
- Evaluated on a human question dataset from a "Battleship"-like game; model defines a log-linear distribution over program space.
- Model features: Expected Information Gain (EIG), question complexity (from a PCFG), and answer type (Boolean, Number).
- Fit to human data; predicts question frequencies in held-out contexts and generates novel, contextually relevant questions.
- Strength: Novel formulation framing goal-directed question asking as compositional program generation, offering a fresh perspective for active learning.
- Strength: Sound methodology with well-motivated log-linear features, thorough validation (leave-one-out CV, ablation studies) showing informativeness and complexity matter.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with insightful qualitative examples of generated questions.
- Weakness: Limited scope and scalability—evaluated only on a constrained toy domain with heavy reliance on a hand-crafted, domain-specific grammar.
- Weakness: Technical components (EIG, complexity penalties, grammar priors) are standard, so the paper isn’t a major technical advance.
- Weakness: Audience fit concerns—paper reads more like cognitive science modeling than broadly applicable ML research.
- Pro: Thought-provoking, complete work that could inspire research on compositional, goal-directed query generation.
- Con: Applicability to real-world ML is doubtful due to toy domain and domain engineering; venue relevance for NIPS is questionable.
