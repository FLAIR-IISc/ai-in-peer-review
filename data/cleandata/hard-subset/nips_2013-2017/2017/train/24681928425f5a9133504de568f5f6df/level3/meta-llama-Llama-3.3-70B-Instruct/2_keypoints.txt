- Proposes MPGM, a multiplicative update algorithm for quadratic-program graph matching with doubly stochastic constraints.
- MPGM offers theoretical guarantees of convergence and KKT optimality.
- Algorithm empirically produces sparse solutions and is simple and efficient to implement.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with thorough presentation of algorithm and theory.
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic, image-sequence, and real-world data shows MPGM outperforms state-of-the-art on objective and accuracy.
- Strengths: novel approach, strong theoretical analysis, comprehensive experiments, and clear discussion of limitations.
- Weakness: potential lack of scalability to larger domains limits practical applicability.
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison to related work, especially regarding theoretical guarantees and experiments.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis of MPGM-induced sparsity requires further study.
- Paper meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, need for deeper comparisons, and incomplete sparsity theory.
- Overall recommendation: accept, pending addressing scalability and comparative/theoretical gaps.
