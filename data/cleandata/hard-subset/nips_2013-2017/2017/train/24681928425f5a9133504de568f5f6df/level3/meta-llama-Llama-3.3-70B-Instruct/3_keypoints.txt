- Presents a cognitive model predicting when questions are asked by combining informativeness, complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Applied to the Battleship domain and successfully predicts held-out human data.
- Model’s cognitive-science impact is limited by inconsistent and noisy experimental results.
- Technically sound, well-written, and clearly presented.
- Fails to cite and contrast with relevant prior work (e.g., Hawkins 2015; Roberts 1996).
- Key contribution: ability to generate compositional questions in a non-trivial domain.
- Model has clear potential for future use in cognitive and AI systems.
- Reviewer is skeptical of the paper’s claims about how people balance informativeness and complexity.
- Strengths: technical rigor, clarity, reproducibility, and promising future applications.
- Weaknesses: missing related work, inconsistent results, and unsupported conclusions about human trade-offs.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel compositional-question capability, solid implementation, and future potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate literature review, noisy/inconsistent results, and limited empirical support for key claims.
- Overall: a useful scientific contribution that requires further development and stronger evaluation.
- Assessment details: quality—technically sound but incomplete; clarity—well-organized and reproducible; originality—approach not novel and poorly situated in prior work; significance—limited impact though provides unique dataset/conclusions.
