- Proposes a novel cognitive model that frames questions as formal programs with probabilities driven by informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Implements the model in the Battleship domain and shows the full model predicts human questions better than lesioned variants by log-likelihood.
- Extends active learning ideas to richer, natural-language-like question generation and links to program-based concept models and compositional semantics.
- Omits discussion of relevant prior work in goal-directed dialogue and goal-based question models (e.g., Hawkins 2015, Roberts 1996).
- Technical presentation is clear; grammar and probabilistic model are well-defined and code is provided for reproducibility.
- Demonstrates generation of novel, plausible questions, indicating potential for AI systems to formulate informative queries.
- The claim that the model explains how humans balance informativeness and complexity is weakly supported by the results.
- Including EIG does not substantially improve correlation with human judgments, and EIG’s significance shows a substantial bootstrap error (19%).
- Noisy, sparse data and possible feature interactions suggest additional factors are not captured by the current model.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add missing related work and temper claims about human cognitive insights.
