- Main idea: Recasts human question asking as program synthesis, treating questions as executable formal programs.  
- Method: Uses a simple LISP-/lambda-inspired programming language and grammar to compose question-programs.  
- Model: Probabilistic generative model with a log-linear scoring function over four features (expected information gain, answer type, complexity, relevance).  
- Evaluation setting: Tested on a large natural-language dataset from an information-search game similar to Battleship.  
- Empirical results: Model predicts the distribution of human questions and can generate novel, human-like questions not in training data.  
- Strength: Novel and creative approach to modeling question asking, capturing productivity and compositionality.  
- Strength: Thorough analysis of model performance and qualitative/quantitative evaluation.  
- Weakness: Task (Battleship-style game) is highly constrained and logically unambiguous, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: Reliance on a simple programming language and predefined grammar may restrict expressiveness and real-world applicability.  
- Originality: Innovative framing of question asking as program generation, though underlying probabilistic/log-linear methods are standard.  
- Significance: Valuable for understanding human question asking and informing active learning, but impact may be limited by scope and task simplicity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and broaden applicability.
