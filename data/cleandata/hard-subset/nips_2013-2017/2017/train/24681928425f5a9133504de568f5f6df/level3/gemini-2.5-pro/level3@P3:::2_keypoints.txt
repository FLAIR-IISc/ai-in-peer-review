- Novel framing: models human question-asking as probabilistic program generation using a lambda-calculus–style language.  
- Implementation: questions are generated by a hand-designed PCFG and scored by a log-linear model.  
- Goodness features: combines expected information gain (EIG), question complexity (negative log PCFG probability), and answer type.  
- Dataset/evaluation: tested on a Battleship-style grid; human natural-language questions were hand-coded into the formal language.  
- Evaluation method: leave-one-out cross-validation comparing full model to lesioned versions lacking informativeness, complexity, or answer-type features.  
- Main quantitative result: full model fits human data best; question complexity is the strongest predictor.  
- Generativity: model produces novel, plausible, contextually relevant questions not present in the human dataset.  
- Strength — originality/quality: elegant, principled synthesis of compositional semantics, program synthesis, and cognitive modeling.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, well-motivated, and provides helpful concrete examples of program representations.  
- Strength — evaluation: lesioned-model comparisons convincingly show humans balance informativeness and complexity.  
- Significance for cognitive science: offers a formal, predictive model of open-ended question-asking and explains complexity’s importance.  
- Weakness — scalability: demonstrated only in a small, structured domain with a hand-crafted grammar and computationally intensive inference.  
- Concern about relevance: unclear how the approach scales to high-dimensional, open-ended ML problems central to NIPS.  
- Suggested next step: either validate on a more complex domain or argue for specific application areas where knowledge engineering is justified.  
- Detailed suggestion: perform formal statistical model comparisons (e.g., likelihood-ratio tests) in addition to bootstrapping.  
- Theoretical suggestion: connect to discourse/inquiry work such as the Questions Under Discussion (QUD) framework to ground goal-directed question sequences.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, original, and promising work with compelling cognitive insights but reservations about scalability and broader ML impact.
