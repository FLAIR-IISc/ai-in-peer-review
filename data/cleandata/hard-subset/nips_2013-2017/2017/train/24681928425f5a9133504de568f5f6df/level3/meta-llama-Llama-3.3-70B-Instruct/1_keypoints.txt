Presents MPGM, a multiplicative update algorithm for QP graph matching with doubly stochastic and approximate discrete constraints.  
Main benefits: naturally handles doubly stochastic constraint, produces sparse (approximate discrete) solutions, and is simple and efficient to implement.  
Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and theoretical guarantees.  
Experiments on synthetic, image sequence, and real-world data show MPGM outperforms state-of-the-art in objective score and matching accuracy.  
Strengths: clear presentation, thorough theoretical analysis, comprehensive experimental evaluation, low computational complexity.  
Weaknesses: no theoretical proof of solution sparsity despite empirical sparsity.  
Weaknesses: comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is insufficiently detailed.  
Weaknesses: lacks discussion of advantages and disadvantages of competing methods.  
Meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, and significance; technically sound.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel effective algorithm, low complexity, easy implementation, strong empirical results, solid theoretical analysis.  
Arguments against acceptance: missing sparsity proof, incomplete comparative analysis and discussion.  
Overall recommendation: accept—strengths outweigh weaknesses and contributes significantly to the field.
