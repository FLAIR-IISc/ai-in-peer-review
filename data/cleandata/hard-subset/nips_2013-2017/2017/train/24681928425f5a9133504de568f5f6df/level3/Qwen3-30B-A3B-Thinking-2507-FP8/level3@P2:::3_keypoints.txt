- Proposal: model human question asking as program generation where questions are executable programs.
- Model: probabilistic combination of informativeness (EIG), program complexity (length), answer type, and relevance.
- Results: predicts human question frequencies on held-out contexts and generates novel, human-like questions.
- Technical quality: sound probabilistic model, well-defined features, reproducible implementation, and thorough ablation/evaluation.
- Weak evidence for informativeness: bootstrap shows a 19% chance the EIG advantage is not real; information-agnostic model performs similarly.
- Missing related work: omits Hawkins et al. (2015) and Roberts (1996), weakening literature positioning.
- Clarity: well-written, organized, clear grammar description and transparent evaluation; good supplementary materials.
- Originality: novel demonstration of compositional question generation in a non-trivial domain but builds on existing program synthesis work.
- Significance: promising for cognitive science and AI, but limited by narrow Battleship domain and unclear evidence for claimed cognitive mechanisms.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add missing citations and clarify evidence for informativeness.
- Overall: meaningful, compelling contribution that merits publication after addressing the noted issues.
