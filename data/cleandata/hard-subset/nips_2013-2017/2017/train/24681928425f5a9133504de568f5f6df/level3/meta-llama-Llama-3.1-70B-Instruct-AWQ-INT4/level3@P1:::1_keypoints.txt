- Paper proposes treating questions as executable programs and models question asking as program generation.
- Develops a formal language (lambda calculus / LISP-based) to represent questions as programs executed on world states.
- Introduces a probabilistic log-linear model over four question features (including expected information gain, answer type, and board-feature references).
- Evaluated on a large dataset of natural-language questions from an information-search game.
- Results show the model predicts human question frequencies and can generate novel, human-like questions.
- Main contributions: formal question language and probabilistic model for question generation.
- Strength: novel approach capturing compositionality and computability of human questions.
- Strength: technically sound, clearly written, well-organized, with thorough evaluation and comparisons to alternatives.
- Weakness: task is highly constrained and logically unambiguous, limiting generality.
- Weakness: model simplicity and limited scope reduce broader impact and significance.
- Criticism: core ideas may be obvious and not of strong interest to the NIPS community.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Significance 6/10, Impact 5/10.
- Recommendation: reject due to limited significance and impact.
- Suggestion: submit to a more specialized NLP or cognitive-science venue instead.
