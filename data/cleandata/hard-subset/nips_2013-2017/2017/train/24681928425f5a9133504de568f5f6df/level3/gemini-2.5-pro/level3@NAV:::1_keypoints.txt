- Presents a computational model of human question-asking as probabilistic program generation.  
- Represents questions as programs in a compositional artificial language that produce answers when executed on a world state.  
- Evaluated on a dataset of human questions in a constrained Battleship-like game.  
- Predicts human question choices with a log-linear model using four features: expected information gain (EIG), a zero-EIG penalty, answer type, and board-feature reference.  
- Motivation: extend active learning from simple label requests to richer, structured queries.  
- Strength: elegant framing—program-synthesis captures compositionality and generativity.  
- Strength: well-defined probabilistic model and clear manuscript with sufficient methodological detail.  
- Strength: ablation studies show both informativeness (EIG) and complexity matter for predicting human behavior.  
- Weakness: limited originality—uses well-established components (compositional semantics, EIG, log-linear models).  
- Weakness: task choice (Battleship) is a highly constrained toy domain that avoids ambiguity and common-sense reasoning.  
- Weakness: reliance on hand-crafted grammar and tractable EIG computation raises concerns about scalability and generalization.  
- Conclusion: solid contribution to computational cognitive modeling but limited significance for the broader ML/NIPS community seeking scalable, general-purpose methods.
