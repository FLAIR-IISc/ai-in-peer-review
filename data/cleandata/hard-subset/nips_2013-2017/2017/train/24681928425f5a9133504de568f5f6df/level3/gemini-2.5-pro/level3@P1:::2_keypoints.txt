- Models human question asking as probabilistic program generation in a Battleship-like grid world.  
- Represents questions as lambda-calculus programs produced by a PCFG and scored by a log-linear model.  
- Question score combines Expected Information Gain (EIG), program complexity, and answer type.  
- Trained and evaluated on a dataset of human questions hand-coded into the program representation.  
- Full model (informativeness + complexity + type) best predicts human question frequencies in leave-one-out CV.  
- Complexity is a particularly strong predictor, explaining why humans avoid purely most-informative queries.  
- Model qualitatively generates novel, plausible, and contextually relevant questions.  
- Strength: highly original framing linking program synthesis, cognitive science, and linguistics.  
- Strength: technically principled combination of generative grammar with utility and cognitive cost; solid ablation study.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with helpful examples.  
- Weakness: demonstrated in a small, discrete, hand-designed domain, raising scalability concerns.  
- Weakness: inference (importance sampling over program space) appears computationally intensive and may not scale.  
- Weakness: reliance on hand-engineered grammar; unclear how to learn grammar or handle high-dimensional real-world problems.  
- Recommendation notes: strong, influential idea vs. limited immediate ML applicability; suggested formal statistical tests (likelihood-ratio) and ties to QUD/discourse frameworks to model question sequences.
