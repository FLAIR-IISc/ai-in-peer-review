- Models question asking as program generation in a domain-specific lambda/LISP-like language with compositional semantics applied to Battleship boards.  
- Uses a PCFG over programs and a log-linear model with features (EIG, zero-EIG, complexity/length, answer-type, relevance) conditioned on context.  
- Parameters learned by maximizing likelihood of human questions via importance sampling; evaluated on 605 human questions across 18 contexts with leave-one-out prediction and ablations.  
- Connects to active learning, semantic/pragmatic theories of questions, program-induction cognitive models, and executable program representations.  

- Strength: Clear, interpretable formalism where the grammar covers the dataset’s questions.  
- Strength: Explicit trade-off between informativeness (EIG) and complexity mirrors human behavior.  
- Strength: Quantitative ablation studies showing complexity/length is critical; informativeness and answer-type help.  
- Strength: Qualitative samples show the model can generate syntactically novel, contextually relevant questions.  

- Weakness: Core ideas (questions-as-programs, utility–complexity trade-off, EIG) are straightforward and not novel to ML.  
- Weakness: Task is very simple and bespoke grammar/predicates make success unsurprising and limit external validity.  
- Weakness: Entirely sidesteps natural language—no parser or surface realization or end-to-end text→meaning mapping.  
- Weakness: Minimal, obvious feature set limits methodological contribution for a major ML venue.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—small dataset, two contexts dropped, reliance on uniform PCFG proposal, modest predictive correlations, and lacks stronger baselines.  
- Weakness: Unclear generalization beyond Battleship and high engineering cost for new domains.  

- Quality: Technically sound within scope; log-linear EIG framework reasonable but inference relies on a large fixed proposal and lacks sensitivity analyses.  
- Clarity: Well written and organized; formalism and features are clear though some implementation details need expansion.  
- Originality: Mildly novel combination but ideas are incremental relative to prior literature.  
- Significance: Limited impact for NIPS—does not advance scalable algorithms, datasets, or broadly applicable theory.  

- Pros for acceptance: Interpretable compositional model, supportive ablations, and qualitative samples that may interest a niche cognitive-ML audience.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited ML novelty, toy domain with hand-crafted grammar, no natural-language interface, modest empirical gains, and missing robustness analyses.  

- Suggested improvements: Demonstrate generalization to another domain or learn grammar pieces; add NL→LF parsing or surface realization and end-to-end evaluation.  
- Suggested improvements: Compare to stronger baselines (retrieval over logical forms, neural seq2seq with copy, RSA models).  
- Suggested improvements: Report sensitivity to proposal distribution, sample budgets, grammar limits, feature choices, and uncertainty on correlations.  
- Suggested improvements: Close the loop by showing active querying reduces steps to identify the board in simulation or with human oracles.  

- Overall recommendation: Careful, complete cognitive modeling paper but too narrow and incremental for NIPS; recommend rejection and targeting a cognitive science venue or broadening for ML audiences.
