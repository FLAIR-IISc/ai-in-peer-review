- Presents a probabilistic cognitive model that treats questions as executable formal programs.  
- Model defines a distribution over a compositional program space, favoring concise, context-useful programs.  
- Evaluated on a large dataset of human questions from an information-search game (Battleship-like).  
- Demonstrates ability to predict human question frequencies and to generate novel questions not in training data.  
- Captures question compositionality and computability in a principled way.  
- Provides thorough analysis of model performance.  
- Scope is limited to a specific, highly constrained domain and task.  
- Reliance on a grammar-based approach may limit generalization to other domains.  
- Focus on a simple logical task may not reflect complex, real-world question asking.  
- Likely of more interest to cognitive science/linguistics/HCI than to the broader NIPS community.  
- Paper is well-written and clear overall (Clarity 8/10).  
- Quality 7/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 5/10.
