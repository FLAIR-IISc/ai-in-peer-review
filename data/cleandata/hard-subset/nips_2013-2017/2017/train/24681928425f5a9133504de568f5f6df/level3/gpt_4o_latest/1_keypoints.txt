- Presents a probabilistic framework modeling human-like question generation in a Battleship-style game using compositional programs.  
- Uses a log-linear model with features for informativeness (EIG), complexity, and answer type to evaluate questions.  
- Employs a compositional grammar to generate novel, semantically coherent questions beyond the training data.  
- Evaluation is thorough, combining quantitative metrics (log-likelihood, correlation) and qualitative analysis of generated questions.  
- Alternative model variants are tested, showing the importance of complexity and informativeness features.  
- Grounded in active learning and informed by cognitive science and linguistics, framing question generation as program synthesis.  
- Core motivation and task lack novelty, being constrained and domain-specific (toy Battleship setting).  
- Heavy reliance on predefined domain knowledge and a hand-crafted grammar raises scalability concerns to other domains.  
- Does not address extension to natural language input, limiting practical applicability.  
- Generated questions favor syntactic coherence over deeper semantic creativity despite emphasis on informativeness.  
- Contribution is incremental and of limited relevance to the broader NeurIPS audience due to scope and novelty.  
- Recommendation: Reject; better suited to specialized venues in cognitive modeling or HCI.
