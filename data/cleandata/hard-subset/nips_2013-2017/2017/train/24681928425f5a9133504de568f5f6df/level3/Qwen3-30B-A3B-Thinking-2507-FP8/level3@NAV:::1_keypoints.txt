- Proposes modeling human question-asking as program generation: questions are programs executed on a world state.  
- Applied to a Battleship domain using a log-linear model with features for expected information gain, answer type, complexity, and board relevance.  
- Model predicts human question frequencies and generates novel, context-appropriate questions not in the training data.  
- Strength: clear presentation and reasonable motivation linking question-asking to active learning.  
- Strength: uses a compositional Montagovian semantic framework, giving a theoretically grounded representation.  
- Strength: demonstrates creativity by generating novel questions within the constrained domain.  
- Limitation: core idea overlaps prior work (e.g., Lake et al., 2015; Goodman et al., 2013).  
- Limitation: evaluation is confined to a simple, logically unambiguous Battleship task that limits realism.  
- Limitation: the four log-linear features are basic and not especially innovative.  
- Limitation: paper does not convincingly show generalization or scalability to open-ended, real-world question-asking.  
- Overall significance is limited and the contribution is incremental, making it unlikely to attract strong interest at NIPS.
