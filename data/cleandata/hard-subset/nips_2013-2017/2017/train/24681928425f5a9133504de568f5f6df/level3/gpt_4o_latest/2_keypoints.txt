- Presents a computational framework modeling human question-asking in Battleship, representing questions as lambda-calculus programs.  
- Uses a probabilistic context-free grammar (PCFG) to generate questions evaluated by informativeness, complexity, and answer type.  
- Empirical results show complexity, not informativeness alone, best predicts human-like questions.  
- Well-written paper bridging cognitive science and computational linguistics with a novel formal approach.  
- Creative, rigorous formalization using lambda calculus and PCFG enabling semantically coherent, context-relevant question generation.  
- Validated with human question data and robust leave-one-out cross-validation.  
- Model can synthesize novel, human-like questions, demonstrating generative capability.  
- Main limitation: scalability and generalizability due to reliance on domain-specific (Battleship) knowledge.  
- Computational efficiency concerns not thoroughly addressed given large hypothesis spaces.  
- Lacks nested model comparisons to statistically validate contributions of different features.  
- Suggestions: perform nested model comparisons, integrate formal semantics/pragmatics, test scalability across diverse domains, and cite Questions under Discussion literature.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations and suggestions.
