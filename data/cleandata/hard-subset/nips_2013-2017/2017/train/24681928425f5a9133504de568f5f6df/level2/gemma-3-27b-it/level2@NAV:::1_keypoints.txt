- Proposes a novel framework modeling questions as executable programs that capture compositionality and generate human-like queries in an information-seeking game.  
- Introduces a probabilistic model that predicts human question frequencies and can generate novel, plausible questions.  
- Strong experimental validation: model performance and generative capability are convincing.  
- Ablation study shows informativeness, complexity, and answer type matter, with complexity being especially important.  
- Grammar presentation is hard to grasp; needs a more intuitive explanation and clearer link to natural language.  
- Evaluation is confined to the Battleship game, limiting claims about generalizability to other domains.  
- Comparison to prior dialog systems is limited and should be broadened to discuss recent large language models capable of open-ended question generation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the grammar and expand related-work discussion.
