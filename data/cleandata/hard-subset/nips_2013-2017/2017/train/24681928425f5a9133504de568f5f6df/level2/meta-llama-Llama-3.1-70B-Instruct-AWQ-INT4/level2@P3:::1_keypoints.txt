- Main idea: Treats questions as executable programs with a probability distribution favoring concise, informative, compositional programs; evaluated on human question asking in an ambiguous Battleship game and can predict and generate novel questions.
- Related work: Positions itself against active learning and question-generation methods that lack rich query representation and goal-directed intentionality.
- Strength — Novel approach: Models question asking as program generation, offering a fresh perspective.
- Strength — Formalism: Provides a formal program-based representation enabling precise computational analysis.
- Strength — Evaluation: Thorough evaluation using human-generated questions and comparisons to alternative models.
- Strength — Insights: Highlights the roles of compositionality, computability, and informativeness in human question asking.
- Weakness — Domain specificity: Implemented in the Battleship domain, raising concerns about required knowledge engineering for new domains.
- Weakness — Limited generalizability: Unclear robustness and performance across other domains or tasks.
- Weakness — Complexity: Model complexity may hinder interpretability, especially for non-experts.
- Originality: Novel and innovative framing of question asking as program generation with a formal representation.
- Significance: Results have important implications for understanding human question asking and developing human-like question generation in applications (education, dialogue, HCI).
- Clarity: Well-written, logically structured, and accessible to non-experts.
- Quality: High-quality, rigorous computational approach and evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel idea, thorough evaluation, and significant implications for theory and applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: Domain restriction, interpretability/complexity concerns, and need for further generalization studies.
- Recommendations: Provide more details on model complexity and interpretability, discuss generalizability and extension to new domains, and give deeper performance comparisons to alternatives.
