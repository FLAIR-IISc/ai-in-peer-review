**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper "Question Asking as Program Generation" proposes a cognitive model that treats questions as formal programs that, when executed on the state of the world, output an answer. This approach is a significant departure from traditional active learning algorithms, which typically focus on simple information requests. The authors draw inspiration from linguistics and cognitive science, particularly the concept of compositionality, to model human question asking as a program synthesis problem.

The paper is related to previous work on active learning, goal-directed dialog systems, and visual question generation.
