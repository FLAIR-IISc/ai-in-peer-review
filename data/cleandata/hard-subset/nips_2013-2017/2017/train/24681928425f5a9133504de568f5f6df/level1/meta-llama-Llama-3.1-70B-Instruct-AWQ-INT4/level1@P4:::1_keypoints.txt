- Paper presents a cognitive model treating questions as executable programs that output answers given a world state, with a probabilistic preference for concise, context-informative programs.
- Builds on active learning, cognitive science, and linguistics, linking compositionality, computability, and formal question semantics (partitions over worlds).
- Strength: Novel computational framework modeling question asking as programs.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on a large human question dataset showing prediction of question frequencies and generation of human-like novel questions.
- Strength: Strong interdisciplinary grounding across cognitive science, linguistics, and computer science.
- Weakness: Model evaluated only in the Battleship domain, raising concerns about domain transfer and required knowledge engineering.
- Weakness: Operates on semantic representations rather than raw natural language, limiting real-world applicability.
- Weakness: Potential computational complexity issues, especially for large hypothesis spaces.
- Originality: Introduces a new probabilistic generative model for sampling context-sensitive, relevant program-like questions.
- Significance: Advances understanding of how people balance informativeness and complexity and offers the first predictive model of open-ended human question asking.
- Pro: Well-motivated, well-executed novel approach with comprehensive empirical support.
- Pro: Demonstrates ability to predict and generate human-like questions, with implications for active learning systems.
- Pro: Interdisciplinary approach could foster cross-field insights and applications.
- Con: Domain specificity may limit generality and require substantial re-engineering for new domains.
- Con: Reliance on semantic representations rather than NLP limits practical deployment on natural language input.
- Con: Computational cost may constrain scalability to larger or more complex hypothesis spaces.
- Recommendation: Explore extensions to additional domains and quantify required knowledge engineering.
- Recommendation: Integrate or interface the model with natural language processing to work directly from text.
- Recommendation: Investigate optimizations to reduce computational complexity for larger hypothesis spaces.
