- Proposes a probabilistic model that treats questions as executable programs over world states, favoring concise, informative programs.  
- Specifies a probability distribution over a compositional program space using a grammar and generative model.  
- Evaluated on human question data from an information-search (Battleship) game; model predicts human question distributions and generates novel, useful questions.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides thorough methodological detail.  
- Strengths: originality, clarity, significance; potential to advance active machine learning and cognitive science.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to the Battleship domain, raising generalization concerns.  
- Weakness: model operates on semantic representations rather than raw natural language, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Pro-accept: novel approach, strong empirical results, clear presentation, and significant potential impact.  
- Con-accept: limited domain generality, reliance on semantic inputs, and need for broader evaluation/extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommended for acceptance, with suggested future work to address limitations.
