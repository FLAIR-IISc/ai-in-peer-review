- Proposes a novel model framing goal-directed question asking as probabilistic program generation.  
- Represents questions as compositional programs that, when executed on a world state, yield answers.  
- Defines a probability over programs favoring informativeness (Expected Information Gain) and concision.  
- Extends active learning to rich, structured queries and connects to program induction in cognitive science.  
- Strength: Highly original and significant—principled, interpretable framework toward human-like active learning.  
- Strength: Technically sound with thorough experiments and ablations showing both informativeness and complexity are necessary.  
- Strength: Qualitative results demonstrate the model’s ability to generate creative, contextually relevant questions.  
- Strength: Exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: Relies on a hand-crafted, domain-specific grammar (Battleship), raising scalability and engineering concerns.  
- Weakness: Operates on semantic program representations, sidestepping natural language grounding and deployment challenges.  
- For acceptance: Powerful, elegant framework with rigorous evaluation and broad NIPS-community interest.  
- Against acceptance: Domain-specific grammar may limit immediate practical impact.  
- Overall: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; a high-quality, original, and thought-provoking contribution.
