This paper presents a novel approach to modeling human question asking as program generation, where questions are treated as formal programs that output an answer when executed on the state of the world. The model is evaluated on a data set of natural language questions asked by human participants in an information-search game, and the results show that the model can predict the frequencies of human questions and generate novel, human-like questions that were not present in the training set.

Finally, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the model in generating novel, human-like questions that are useful in a given context.
