- Proposal: Introduces a computational framework that models questions as formal programs which, when executed on a world state, yield answers.  
- Domain & model: Implements a probabilistic generative model in a Battleship-like game to predict human question patterns.  
- Key innovation: Represents questions as programs in a lambda-calculus-based language, enabling compositional question generation while trading off informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.  
- Theoretical contribution: Builds on cognitive science work (e.g., Lake et al., Goodman et al.) and addresses limits of prior question-generation approaches.  
- Empirical results: Thorough evaluation shows the full model outperforms variants lacking informativeness, complexity, or answer-type features and can generate novel, context-appropriate questions.  
- Strengths: Theoretically grounded, clear cognitive-science connections, strong empirical validation, interpretable model, and generates creative human-like questions beyond neural baselines.  
- Weaknesses: Grammar and model are domain-specific, no direct integration with NLP systems, narrow evaluation confined to a single game domain, and requires domain knowledge to design the grammar.  
- Overall appraisal: High-quality, significant contribution to modeling goal-directed question generation; recommended for acceptance and a promising foundation for future extensions.
