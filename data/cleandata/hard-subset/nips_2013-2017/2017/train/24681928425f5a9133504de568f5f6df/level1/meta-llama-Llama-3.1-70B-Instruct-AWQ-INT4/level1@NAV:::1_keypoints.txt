- Proposes a novel approach modeling human question asking as a program generation task.  
- Introduces a cognitive model treating questions as executable formal programs over world states.  
- Model defines a probability distribution over a compositional program space, favoring concise, informative programs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations.  
- Uses a probabilistic generative model to predict which questions people ask—significant for active learning.  
- Evaluated on a dataset of natural language questions from an information-search game.  
- Captures compositionality and computability of human questions, predicting question frequencies and synthesizing novel questions.  
- Results show the model can generate novel, clever, contextually useful human-like questions.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and argues greater flexibility/generalizability than prior methods.  
- Limitation: operates on semantic representations rather than raw natural language, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Limitation: parts of the grammar are Battleship-specific, requiring substantial modification for other domains.  
- Suggests more discussion of real-world applications and limitations; recommends accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
