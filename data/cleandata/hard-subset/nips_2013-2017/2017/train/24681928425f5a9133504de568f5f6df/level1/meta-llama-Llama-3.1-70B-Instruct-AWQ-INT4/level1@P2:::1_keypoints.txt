- Proposes modeling human question asking as program generation with questions as executable programs.
- Introduces a probabilistic cognitive model favoring concise, informative programs in context.
- Evaluated on human question data from an information-search game (Battleship).
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of model components.
- Novel use of a probabilistic program-generating approach to model questions.
- Model captures compositionality and computability of human questions.
- Can generate novel, creative, and useful questions not seen in training.
- Balances informativeness and complexity, providing nuanced insight into question asking.
- Thorough evaluation shows the model outperforms alternative models in log-likelihood.
- Uses leave-one-out cross-validation for robust performance estimation.
- Main weakness: evaluation confined to the Battleship domain, limiting generalizability.
- Unclear robustness to different features, parameters, or more complex/dynamic environments.
- Overall a significant contribution to AI and cognitive science, technically sound.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong evaluation, and impactful results.
- Arguments for rejection: domain-specific evaluation and potential sensitivity to modeling choices.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
