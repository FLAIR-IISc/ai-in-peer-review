- Models question asking as generation of executable lambda-like programs evaluated by Expected Information Gain (EIG) within a PCFG + log-linear feature model.
- Builds on formal semantics, pragmatic models, program induction, semantic parsing, and active learning literature.
- Clear formalization linking compositional question semantics to EIG under a Bayesian hypothesis space.
- Compact feature-based model capturing the trade-off between informativeness (EIG) and complexity.
- Demonstrates productivity: grammar covers human questions and generates novel, human-like context-relevant questions.
- Quantitative evaluation with leave-one-context-out and ablations showing importance of EIG and especially complexity.
- Careful semantic novelty criterion via program equivalence (mutual information over answer partitions).
- Heavy domain engineering: domain-specific predicates, hand-crafted relevance filter, and bespoke PCFG limit transferability.
- Complexity feature uses −log PCFG probability, conflating grammar idiosyncrasies with true program length; sensitivity unexplored.
- Learning/inference rely on fixed importance sampling and reuse of 150k samples, risking weight degeneration and biased estimates without diagnostics.
- Important sampling diagnostics (ESS, Ẑ variance) and robustness to proposal choice are not reported.
- Dataset limitations: several contexts and question types excluded; ambiguous questions omitted, possibly biasing evaluation.
- Reported Spearman correlation slightly favors the information-agnostic model, creating a metric discrepancy that needs analysis.
- No automated mapping from natural language to programs at test time; manual program annotation limits end-to-end applicability.
- EIG calculations assume specific priors and deterministic answers, ignoring plausible human priors and answerer noise.
- Technically sound formulation, but approximate inference/learning needs stronger justification and diagnostics.
- Generally clear and accessible writing, but critical implementation details are deferred or missing for reproducibility.
- Modeling question-as-program with an EIG/complexity trade-off is an original and timely synthesis.
- Conceptually significant for cognitive modeling of active querying; practical impact limited by single small domain and lack of NL interface.
- Suggested improvements: report IS diagnostics, compare alternative complexity priors, learn grammar probabilities, add pragmatic costs, provide NL semantic parser, and test transfer to another micro-domain.
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant formalism, empirical evidence for EIG+complexity, ability to generate novel questions, cross-disciplinary interest.
- Arguments against acceptance: domain-specific engineering, untested inference robustness, small/filtered dataset, no NL grounding, and mixed quantitative signals.
