- Proposes a novel framework that treats questions as executable programs generating answers when run on a world state.
- Develops an energy-based probabilistic model that samples from a compositional program space, favoring concise, informative, context-relevant questions.
- Evaluated on a dataset of 605 human questions from a Battleship-style game to test context-dependent question generation.
- Technically sound: clear formulation (Equations 1-2), well-defined features (EIG, complexity, answer type), and principled probabilistic treatment.
- Rigorous evaluation: model variants/lesions, log-likelihood comparisons with bootstrapping, and qualitative novel-question generation.
- Honest about limitations, noting domain-specific grammar and need for domain knowledge.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing; grammar and program examples make the approach accessible.
- Supplementary materials appear to support reproducibility.
- Original contribution: models questions as executable programs rather than surface strings, connecting program synthesis to human question asking.
- Distinguishes itself from neural question generators (no large paired datasets) and canned dialog systems (more creative output).
- Significant potential: advances human-like AI question asking with implications for HCI and active learning.
- Primary weakness: reliance on a domain-specific grammar requiring substantial knowledge engineering per new domain.
- Unclear how much domain knowledge or re-engineering is needed to generalize to other applications.
- Limited evaluation scope: tested only on the Battleship game, with no analysis of scaling to richer domains.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, strong theory, rigorous quantitative and qualitative evaluation, clear demonstration of informativeness vs. complexity, and high clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: domain-specific grammar, single-domain evaluation, limited discussion of generalization.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that meets reviewing criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
