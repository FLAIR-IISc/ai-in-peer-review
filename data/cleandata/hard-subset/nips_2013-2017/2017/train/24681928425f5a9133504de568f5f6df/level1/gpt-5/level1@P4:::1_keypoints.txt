- Models question asking as program generation in a small typed LISP-like DSL with a probabilistic grammar and log-linear features.  
- Key features: expected information gain (EIG) informativeness, a complexity prior (−log q(x)), answer-type indicators, and a relevance filter.  
- Trains by maximum likelihood on 605 human questions across 18 contexts using importance sampling to approximate partition functions.  
- Evaluation uses leave-one-context-out; full model outperforms lesions and achieves average Spearman correlation 0.64 with human frequencies.  
- Model can generate novel, contextually appropriate questions and enforces semantic novelty via mutual information over answers.  
- Connects to active learning/value of information, program induction/LoT, formal semantics of questions, and semantic parsing.  
- Strength: clear, principled executable-program representation enabling explicit answer distributions and EIG computation.  
- Strength: compositional grammar covers observed questions and supports generativity beyond the dataset.  
- Strength: probabilistic model integrates informativeness and complexity; complexity is empirically important.  
- Strength: sound MLE training with reasonable approximations and bootstrap uncertainty analyses.  
- Strength: demonstrates both predictive power (LL, rank correlations) and constructive generation.  
- Weakness: single-domain (Battleship) and small dataset limit generalization and ML impact.  
- Weakness: baselines are mainly lesion studies; lacks comparison to parametric or neural generative baselines and alternative priors.  
- Weakness: complexity feature is tied to the PCFG proposal (−log q(x)), potentially biasing results without sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: EIG computation required hypothesis enumeration and dropped two contexts/stochastic operator for tractability, raising scalability concerns.  
- Weakness: evaluation/reporting could be more detailed (absolute LL gains, per-context CIs, full uncertainty over training pipeline).  
- Weakness: some essential grammar/implementation details and references are deferred to the supplement or malformed.  
- Quality: technically sound methods and appropriate approximations; analyses support main claims about compositionality and informativeness.  
- Clarity: generally well written but should move core grammar/type tables into main text and clarify computational costs, equivalence criteria, and thresholds.  
- Originality: moderate—building on known program/question semantics but novel in combining PCFG-based generation with EIG to predict human question distributions.  
- Significance: meaningful for cognitive modeling and conceptual active learning advances, but limited NIPS/ML impact due to domain specificity and engineered grammar.  
- Pros summary: principled, interpretable model linking informativeness and complexity; strong evidence for complexity constraints and compositional generativity.  
- Cons summary: single domain, limited baselines, EIG scalability issues, and deferred methodological details.  
- Suggestions: evaluate an additional domain or transfer operators; compare to MDL/program-length and neural DSL models; report per-context metrics with full uncertainty; explore EIG approximations or amortization; provide minimal NL interface.  
- Recommendation: weak accept — technically sound and interesting but limited by domain and baseline comparisons.
