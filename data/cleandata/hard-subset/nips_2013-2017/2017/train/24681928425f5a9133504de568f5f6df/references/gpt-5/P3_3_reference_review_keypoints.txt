- Proposes modeling questions as typed, executable programs using a PCFG-like grammar and a log-linear model with features for informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Trains parameters by maximizing likelihood of 605 human questions across 18 Battleship contexts using importance sampling; full model achieves best held-out log-likelihood and samples novel human-like questions.
- Bridges active learning/Bayesian experimental design, probabilistic languages of thought, and program-based semantic parsing; contrasts with neural VQG and pragmatic RSA models.
- Strength: principled, compositional formulation that yields precise semantics and hypothesis partitions for EIG computation.
- Strength: integrates informativeness with a complexity cost, explaining why humans don’t simply maximize EIG.
- Strength: expressive yet constrained grammar (lambda/maps/sets) that covers the dataset and enables learning and search.
- Strength: empirical LOOCV and ablations show complexity is crucial and EIG and type features contribute.
- Strength: generated questions show genuine productivity beyond template reuse via equivalence checking by answer partitions.
- Strength: authors acknowledge limitations (domain specificity, NL interface) and position work as a step toward richer active learning.
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—EIG requires enumerating large hypothesis spaces; two contexts were dropped and stochastic rules removed.
- Weakness: Learning/inference rely on importance sampling from a fixed PCFG proposal pool (150k programs reused for 100k steps), risking bias/overfitting and lacking convergence diagnostics, variance estimates, and sensitivity analyses.
- Weakness: Limited computational cost and runtime/memory reporting.
- Weakness: Missing baselines—PCFG-only, EIG+length-only, nearest-neighbor/template reuse, and simple neural baselines are not evaluated.
- Weakness: Metrics ambiguous—full model wins LL but information-agnostic variant has slightly higher Spearman (0.65 vs 0.64); no CIs or analysis of this discrepancy.
- Weakness: Relevance feature f∅ seems ad hoc and possibly encodes domain knowledge that could be enforced via grammar constraints.
- Weakness: Novelty/equivalence procedure (treating argument variants as equivalent) is debatable; MI threshold and sensitivity unspecified.
- Clarity: Important implementation details are deferred (hypothesis priors, EIG computation for large H, proposal probabilities, resampling, runtime/memory); reference formatting inconsistent.
- Clarity: Natural language → program mapping is performed offline, limiting end-to-end reproducibility.
- Originality: Novel and well-motivated central idea—treat question generation as program synthesis evaluated by Bayesian design, applied to human question data.
- Significance: Provides a testable account of human question asking and a route to goal-directed agents, but evaluation is limited to a single engineered domain and lacks end-to-end NL demonstration.
- Suggestion: Report computational cost, convergence diagnostics, sensitivity to proposal pool size, and stability across random seeds; state if proposals are resampled during training.
- Suggestion: Add baselines (PCFG-only, EIG+length, nearest-neighbor, small neural) to contextualize gains and isolate feature contributions.
- Suggestion: Provide confidence intervals or bootstrap for rank correlations and analyze why information-agnostic model has slightly higher correlation.
- Suggestion: Detail MI threshold and equivalence procedure; analyze effects of argument-based equivalence or avoid conflating context-dependent variants.
- Suggestion: Include human evaluations of generated questions and, if possible, an online task showing model-suggested questions improve performance.
- Suggestion: Discuss or prototype approximate EIG methods for large hypothesis spaces (particle approximations, amortized inference) to demonstrate scalability.
- Suggestion: Clarify or replace the relevance feature, or constrain the grammar to board-referential constructs.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually elegant, bridges cognition and ML, solid ablation evidence for complexity, demonstrates compositional generalization and productive generation.
- Cons for acceptance: scalability and computational approximations insufficiently validated, limited domain and manual semantic mapping, missing baselines and mixed quantitative signals.
- Overall: Thoughtful, technically sound contribution likely to influence cognition–ML work; borderline accept contingent on clarifying computational details and strengthening baselines.
