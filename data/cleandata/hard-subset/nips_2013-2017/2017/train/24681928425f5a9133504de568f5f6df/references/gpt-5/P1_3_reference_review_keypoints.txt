- Summary: Models question asking as program generation in a LISP-like language, scoring programs by Expected Information Gain (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance, trained on human questions from a Battleship task.

- Relation to prior work: Connects active learning, information-theoretic query selection, compositional cognitive models, program induction, and visual question generation; novelty is generating questions (not answers) as programs.

- Strength: Clear formalization of questions as executable programs allowing direct EIG computation.

- Strength: Compositional PCFG grammar captures dataset diversity and generates novel, productive questions beyond templates.

- Strength: Probabilistic model combines informativeness with cognitive constraints (complexity prior, type preferences).

- Strength: Empirical leave-one-context-out evaluation shows full model outperforms lesioned variants and produces plausible novel questions.

- Strength: Bridges AI and cognitive modeling, offering a framework extensible by others.

- Weakness: Importance sampling procedure is under-specified (proposal q, resampling vs reuse of sample pool, and potential bias as θ changes).

- Weakness: Partition function estimator/formula appears incorrect or unclear and needs correction and clarification.

- Weakness: Dataset and evaluation limited—only 16 contexts used, 14 questions removed, and two complex contexts omitted—raising scalability/robustness concerns.

- Weakness: Natural-language → program mapping is undocumented (manual vs. automated, annotator agreement), affecting reproducibility and NL claims.

- Weakness: Missing stronger baselines—no EIG-only baseline with same grammar prior, no template or sequence-model comparisons.

- Weakness: Mixed metrics—full model higher likelihood but slightly lower average Spearman correlation than an information-agnostic model; needs explanation.

- Clarity: Full grammar (core and extended) should be in main text or summarized more fully for reproducibility.

- Clarity: Report annotation protocol and release NL→program mappings.

- Clarity: Describe construction of hypothesis space H per context and why some contexts are intractable.

- Clarity: Explain program equivalence detection (MI threshold, argument-renaming) and report learned feature weights/variability.

- Originality: Novel framing of question asking as program generation optimized for EIG with a grammar-based cognitive prior.

- Significance: Conceptually significant extension of active learning to rich, goal-directed queries; empirically promising but narrow in scope.

- Suggestion: Add an EIG-only baseline with same grammar prior and a "reuse human questions" baseline.

- Suggestion: Report per-context likelihoods/correlations with confidence intervals and statistical tests.

- Suggestion: Analyze complexity measures (length, depth, primitives) to see which predict human preferences.

- Suggestion: Release code, grammars, and program annotations.

- Suggestion: Discuss and experiment with scalable EIG approximations (amortized inference, MCMC, variational methods) and domain generalization strategies (grammar transfer, meta-learning).

- Suggestion: Explore lightweight NL interfaces/semantic parsers trained with weak supervision to bridge language gap.

- Pros for acceptance: Novel, principled formulation; solid ablation evidence; generates creative, context-appropriate questions.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited scope, handcrafted components, manual NL mapping, incomplete methodological details, and missing stronger baselines.

- Overall: Thought-provoking, well-executed first step with conceptual novelty; acceptance recommended pending clarifications and stronger baselines.
