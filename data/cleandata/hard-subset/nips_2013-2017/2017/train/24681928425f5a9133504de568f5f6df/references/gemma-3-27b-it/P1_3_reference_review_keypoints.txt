- Proposes modeling human question asking as program generation, treating questions as executable programs over world states.  
- Evaluated on a Battleship-like dataset; model predicts human question frequencies and generates novel, human-like questions.  
- Situates work in active learning, dialog systems, and visual question generation, claiming advantages in handling novel scenarios with less paired data.  
- Novel and insightful framework offering a formal, computationally tractable representation of question semantics.  
- Emphasizes compositionality and computability as alignment with cognitive principles of human question asking.  
- Empirical validation shows strong correlation between model energies and human question frequencies.  
- Clear presentation with a well-explained grammar, probabilistic model, and helpful examples.  
- Strong ablation study demonstrating the importance of informativeness, complexity, and answer type.  
- Model generates novel questions beyond the training set, illustrating generative capability.  
- Grammar is somewhat domain-specific (Battleship-tailored), raising concerns about scalability and adaptability to complex domains.  
- Computational complexity concerns due to importance sampling for normalization and lack of detailed cost analysis.  
- Limited evaluation: small dataset (605 questions) from a single game context limits generalizability.  
- EIG calculations depend on strong Bayesian prior assumptions; sensitivity to these assumptions is not thoroughly explored.  
- Lacks systematic qualitative evaluation of generated questions’ semantic coherence and practical usefulness.  
- Overall: strong, theoretically grounded contribution with acknowledged limitations but significant potential.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, convincing empirical results, informative ablation, and relevance to AI and cognitive science.  
- Arguments against acceptance: domain-specific grammar, potential computational barriers, and limited dataset diversity.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
