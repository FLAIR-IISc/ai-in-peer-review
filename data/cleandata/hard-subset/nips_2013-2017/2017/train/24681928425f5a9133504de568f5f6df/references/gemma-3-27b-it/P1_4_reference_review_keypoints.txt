- Presents a novel framework modeling human question asking as program generation, executed on a world state to yield answers.
- Implements a probabilistic model in a “Battleship”-like game and shows it predicts human question frequency and generates novel, human-like questions.

- Positions work within active learning, dialog systems, and VQA, arguing those focus on limited question types unlike this approach.
- Grounds the approach in cognitive science theories of compositionality and the “language of thought,” and uses an existing human question dataset.

- Novel framing: treats questions as executable programs, offering a tractable way to capture semantic richness.
- Strong theoretical foundation tying linguistics, cognitive science, and program synthesis.
- Empirical validation on a challenging human-generated dataset demonstrates predictive and generative capabilities.
- Detailed model description including grammar, features, and optimization.
- Clear and accessible writing explaining complex ideas.

- Strong domain specificity to the Battleship game; extending the grammar/model to broader domains may be difficult.
- Model complexity with many features/parameters makes component contributions unclear and raises overfitting risk.
- Importance sampling for the normalizing constant introduces approximation error and uncertainty.
- Limited qualitative analysis of generated questions; unclear if they truly match human naturalness and insight.
- Heavy feature engineering requires domain knowledge, reducing generality.

- For acceptance: introduces a transformative idea, shows compelling empirical results, and is theoretically well-grounded.
- Against acceptance: limited applicability beyond the domain, model complexity, reliance on importance sampling, and insufficient qualitative evaluation.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: discuss challenges and solutions for extending to new domains.
- Suggested revisions: explore more robust techniques for approximating the normalizing constant.
- Suggested revisions: include deeper qualitative or human-evaluation of generated questions.
- Suggested revisions: analyze how learned feature weights inform understanding of human questioning strategies.
