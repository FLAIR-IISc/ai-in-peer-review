- Core idea: Model human question asking as program generation where questions are programs executed on world states to produce answers; evaluated on a Battleship-like game.
- Relation to prior work: Positions itself at the intersection of active learning, dialog systems, and cognitive science, linking to compositional semantics and “language of thought.”
- Novelty: Framing question asking as program generation is original and theoretically insightful.
- Technical soundness: Probabilistic model using a context-free grammar and expected information gain is well-defined; importance sampling used for approximation.
- Empirical validation: Model predicts human question frequencies and generates plausible novel questions; ablations show importance of complexity and informativeness.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and uses examples effectively to explain concepts.
- Significance: Advances a fundamental AI capability with potential impact on active learning, HCI, and cognitive modeling.
- Domain specificity: Current grammar is tailored to Battleship, raising questions about generalizability to other domains.
- Grammar design: Grammar appears somewhat ad hoc; lacks systematic derivation from linguistic principles.
- Limited scalability: Importance sampling and model complexity may not scale to larger, more complex hypothesis spaces.
- Evaluation limitations: Small dataset and subjective qualitative judgments limit empirical strength.
- Computational cost: Computational expense of program execution and importance sampling is acknowledged but not fully analyzed.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach with supportive empirical results and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited immediate applicability due to domain specificity, scalability concerns, and small dataset.
- Overall assessment and recommendation: Strong paper; accept with minor revisions.
- Suggestions for improvement: Discuss grammar generalization, provide detailed computational cost analysis, explore alternative normalization approximations, and expand evaluation to more tasks and datasets.
