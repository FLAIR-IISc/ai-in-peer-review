- Proposes modeling human questions as executable programs capturing compositionality and computability.  
- Introduces a probabilistic program generation model with features for informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.  
- Evaluates model on 605 human questions from a Battleship-like game, showing it predicts question frequencies and generates novel human-like questions.  
- Strong theoretical foundation linking cognitive science (Language of Thought) and program semantics.  
- Comprehensive evaluation with quantitative log-likelihood comparisons and qualitative novel-question generation.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the importance of informativeness, complexity, answer type, and relevance.  
- Novel contribution by treating question generation as program synthesis rather than sequence modeling.  
- Rigorous Bayesian modeling and principled use of Expected Information Gain for informativeness.  
- Provides cognitive insight into trade-offs between informativeness and complexity in human question asking.  
- Evaluation limited to a single domain (Battleship) with a small dataset, limiting generalizability.  
- Lacks a natural language generation component to map programs to human language.  
- Relies on a domain-specific grammar without sufficient analysis of required domain knowledge or learnability.  
- Novelty metric based on mutual information of answer distributions is ad hoc and under-justified.  
- Insufficient comparison to neural question generation baselines and recent related work.  
- Recommend expanding evaluation to other domains to test generalizability.  
- Suggest adding discussion or methods for program-to-text (natural language) mapping.  
- Advise clarifying domain-knowledge requirements and whether components can be learned.  
- Recommend improving justification for the novelty metric and comparing alternative metrics.  
- Recommend adding neural sequence-to-sequence baselines for stronger comparative evaluation.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant contribution with strong theory and modeling, but limited by scope and NLG gap.  
- Conference recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
