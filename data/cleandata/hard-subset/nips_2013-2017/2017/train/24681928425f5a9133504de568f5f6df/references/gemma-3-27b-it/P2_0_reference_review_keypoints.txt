- Novel framework: models question asking as program generation/program synthesis.  
- Strong motivation: addresses limitations of active learning and canned dialog systems, grounded in cognitive science.  
- Well-defined compositional grammar for generating question-programs.  
- Empirical results: predicts human question frequencies and can generate novel, plausible questions, showing generalization.  
- Clear, well-written presentation.  
- Technical contributions are incremental: use of log-linear model and feature engineering is standard.  
- Limited baselines: lacks comparison to strong/modern language-model baselines.  
- Dataset limitations: Battleship dataset is small and domain-specific, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Evaluation of novel questions is subjective; needs more rigorous metrics.  
- Computational cost: inference via importance sampling may limit scalability.  
- NIPS criteria: quality solid but not groundbreaking; clarity high; originality strong; potential significance high.  
- Pro arguments: novel, insightful, cognitively grounded, and demonstrates plausible question generation.  
- Con arguments: incremental technical novelty, limited baselines and dataset, subjective evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept, with suggestion to expand evaluation to diverse datasets and explore advanced technical approaches.
