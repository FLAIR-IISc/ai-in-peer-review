- Proposes treating human questions as executable programs in a typed LISP-like language with a PCFG defining compositional question space.  
- Uses a log-linear model with features: expected information gain (EIG), program complexity (PCFG length), answer type priors, and a relevance filter.  
- Learns parameters by maximum likelihood with importance sampling to approximate partition function and gradients.  
- Evaluated on a Battleship-like dataset of 605 human questions across 18 contexts, leaving out 2 contexts and 14 questions for tractability.  
- Assessed via leave-one-context-out prediction (log-likelihood, rank correlation) and qualitative generation of novel, context-sensitive questions.  
- Ablation studies indicate removing complexity harms performance most; full model generally outperforms variants without EIG or type priors.  
- Integrates ideas from active learning (EIG), formal semantics (questions as partitions), language-of-thought, and program-based semantic parsing.  
- Advances over template- and data-driven approaches by enabling generative, goal-directed question synthesis with explicit semantics.  
- Modeling is sound: typed grammar supports executability, Bayesian EIG over hypothesis space, complexity tied to PCFG, and answer-type priors capture base rates.  
- Equivalence filtering via mutual information over answer distributions is a useful novelty measure.  
- Importance sampling is a reasonable inference approximation, but Z and gradient estimate quality depend on proposal q and diagnostics are limited.  
- Empirically, full model has best test log-likelihood and moderate alignment with human frequencies (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.64).  
- Major caveat: two contexts and 14 questions dropped for tractability, raising scalability and generality concerns.  
- Discrepancy: information-agnostic variant slightly outperforms full model on rank correlation (0.65 vs 0.64), needing reconciliation.  
- Baselines limited to ablations; lacking comparisons to simpler heuristics, PCFG-only priors, EIG-only enumerations, or template libraries.  
- Clarity is generally good, but key grammar fragments should be moved from supplement to main text or summarized; hypothesis-space sizes and EIG costs need detail.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of EIG with compositional generative question prior and program-generation approach to question asking.  
- Significance: important, under-explored problem with potential impact on interactive learning, dialog, and cognitive modeling, but currently domain-specific.  
- Strengths: principled combination of compositional semantics and active learning utility, executable questions, convincing complexity ablation, and creative qualitative outputs.  
- Weaknesses: scalability of EIG, limited baselines, dataset and grammar specificity, approximate inference without variance/error bars, and mixed metric results.  
- Requests for authors: provide LL variance estimates, hypothesis-space sizes and EIG runtimes, sensitivity to PCFG priors and complexity feature, and PCFG-only/EIG-only baselines.  
- Suggestions: add simple baselines with confidence intervals, explore alternative utilities and complexity priors, release code/grammar, and investigate scaling strategies (amortization, pruning).  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—principled and original but limited by scale, baselines, domain specificity, and some evaluation inconsistencies.
