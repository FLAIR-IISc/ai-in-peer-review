- Proposal: represent questions as executable programs generated by a CFG and predict human questions with a log-linear model using EIG, a complexity prior, answer-type biases, and relevance.

- Domain/data: Battleship-like single domain, 605 human questions across 18 contexts; two contexts dropped and one grammar rule removed.

- Evaluation: leave-one-context-out held-out log-likelihood, rank correlations with human frequencies, and qualitative assessment of sampled novel questions.

- Novelty: generating questions via program synthesis and jointly modeling informativeness and complexity is a novel combination in this setting.

- Strength: principled, compositional, and computable query space that is interpretable and domain-general in spirit.

- Strength: probabilistic integration of EIG and learned complexity captures trade-offs beyond pure information maximization.

- Strength: full model outperforms ablations on held-out LL; complexity prior shown as especially important.

- Strength: framework yields diverse, interpretable novel questions and uses equivalence classes via answer partitions.

- Strength: relevant to both ML and cognitive science audiences.

- Weakness: limited empirical scope—single hand-engineered domain and bespoke grammar raise scalability and generality concerns.

- Weakness: qualitative evaluation of novel questions lacks human judgments on usefulness or naturalness and no downstream utility metrics.

- Weakness: semantic parsing appears manual (NL→program mappings), undermining end-to-end realism and reproducibility.

- Weakness: potential circularity/sampling bias because the complexity feature (−log q) is also the IS proposal; no IS diagnostics (ESS, variance) or convergence reported.

- Weakness: mixed evidence on informativeness—rank correlation nearly identical for information-agnostic model vs full model, needing clarification.

- Weakness: missing absolute LL improvements, confidence intervals, and detailed ablation effect sizes; code/grammar not provided for reproducibility.

- Weakness: rudimentary cost model based solely on PCFG complexity; ignores richer human cost factors and lacks quantitative RSA comparisons.

- Quality: conceptually sound but inference is approximate and under-documented; dependence on handcrafted elements weakens generality and NeurIPS-level rigor.

- Clarity: generally clear with concrete examples, but critical experimental and implementation details are missing or relegated to SI; inconsistent references.

- Originality: moderately high—novel framing though built from known components (EIG, PCFG, program semantics).

- Significance: moderate—advances conceptual modeling of rich queries but limited ML impact due to domain specificity, scaling, and lack of NL integration.

- Suggestions: release code/grammars/mappings; report IS diagnostics and convergence; clarify LL vs correlation discrepancy with CIs; add stronger baselines (neural, heuristic EIG−λ·length, RSA); run human evaluations and task-utility analyses; port grammar to a second domain and test robustness; discuss integrating a semantic parser.

- Pros: elegant, interpretable framework; demonstrates importance of compositional program priors; clear qualitative novel-question demos.

- Cons: narrow domain and manual steps, computational limits (dropped contexts), limited quantitative evaluation, unclear IS reliability, and uncertain generalization to natural language.

- Recommendation: Weak reject.

- Reviewer confidence: High.
