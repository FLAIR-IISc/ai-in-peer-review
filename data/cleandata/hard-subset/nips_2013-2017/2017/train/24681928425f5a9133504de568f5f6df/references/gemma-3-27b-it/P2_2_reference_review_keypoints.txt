- Proposal: Model human question asking as program generation where questions are programs executed on possible world states to yield answers.
- Evaluation: Tested on a human question dataset from a Battleship-like spatial reasoning game; predicts question frequencies and generates novel questions.
- Relation to prior work: Positions between active learning, dialog systems, and computational linguistics; connects to formal linguistics and cognitive science.
- Novelty: The program-generation perspective is a significant, original contribution providing structured reasoning beyond standard NLP approaches.
- Technical soundness: Presents a well-defined probabilistic model and sensible optimization, using importance sampling for the normalization constant.
- Empirical validation: Model captures human questioning behavior, with encouraging results and ablation studies highlighting feature importance.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and explains technical details clearly.
- Cognitive grounding: Strong theoretical ties to compositionality and the "language of thought" strengthen the motivation.
- Domain specificity: Reliance on the Battleship-like game and a tailored grammar limits generalizability to other domains.
- Model complexity: Multiple features, probabilistic grammar, and sampling complexity make contributions hard to isolate and reproducibility difficult.
- Limited qualitative analysis: Generated-question examples are shown but lack thorough human-judgment evaluation of quality and relevance.
- Informativeness metric concerns: EIG may not fully capture human notions of question usefulness and assumes rational, goal-directed agents.
- Scaling limitations: Computational cost of evaluating the grammar poses significant scalability challenges for more complex domains.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel framework, substantial technical contribution, promising empirical results, and informative ablation studies.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited generalizability, high model complexity, and insufficient qualitative evaluation.
- Overall assessment: Reviewer scores it Strong Accept (8/10) and recommends minor revisions for more qualitative analysis and clearer scaling discussion.
