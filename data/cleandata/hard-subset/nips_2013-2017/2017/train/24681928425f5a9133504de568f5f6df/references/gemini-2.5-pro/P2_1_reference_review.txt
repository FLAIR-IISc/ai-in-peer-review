### NIPS Review

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel framework for modeling goal-directed, human-like question asking by framing it as a problem of program generation. The central idea is that a question can be represented as a program which, when executed on a possible world state, yields an answer. The authors develop a probabilistic model over a space of such programs, defined by a compositional grammar. The probability of a particular question (program) is determined by an energy-based model whose features include the question's expected information gain (EIG), its complexity, and its answer type. The model's parameters are fit to a dataset of natural language questions asked by humans in a "Battleship"-like information-seeking game. The evaluation shows that the model can predict the frequency of human questions in held-out contexts and, importantly, can generate novel, contextually relevant questions that were not observed in the training data.

---

### Detailed Review

This review evaluates the paper based on the NIPS criteria of Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance.

**Quality:**

The paper is of high technical quality. The proposed framework is well-conceived and rigorously executed.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   The probabilistic model is elegant, combining a generative grammar (to define the space of questions) with a log-linear model (to score them). This provides a principled way to balance multiple competing objectives that humans likely consider when asking questions, such as informativeness and simplicity.
    *   The choice of features is well-motivated. The inclusion of Expected Information Gain (EIG) grounds the model in rational agent theory, while the complexity penalty (`-log q(x)`) is a crucial and well-justified component that reflects cognitive constraints. The lesion studies convincingly demonstrate that both informativeness and complexity are essential for predicting human behavior, with the complexity-agnostic model performing particularly poorly.
    *   The experimental methodology is sound. Using leave-one-context-out cross-validation is an appropriate way to test the model's ability to generalize to new situations. The use of importance sampling to handle the intractable partition function is a standard and acceptable technique for this class of models.

*   **Weaknesses:**
    *   A critical detail that impacts reproducibility and the assessment of the work's completeness is the process of translating the 605 natural language questions into their program representations. The paper states this was done to abstract away from "the exact choice of words," but does not specify *how*. If this was done manually, it represents a significant, un-modeled step in the pipeline that required human intelligence and could introduce bias. A complete piece of work would either automate this step (e.g., with a semantic parser) or provide a thorough description and analysis of the manual annotation process.
    *   The computation of EIG requires iterating over a hypothesis space of possible game board configurations. The paper notes that two contexts were dropped "for computational reasons" due to large hypothesis spaces. This raises questions about the scalability of the EIG computation, which is a core component of the model. For more complex domains, this ideal observer analysis might become completely intractable.

**Clarity:**

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem, explaining their approach, and presenting their results.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   The introduction clearly situates the work relative to existing research in active learning and dialog systems, highlighting the gap this paper aims to fill.
    *   The core concepts of compositionality and computability are introduced and explained effectively, providing a strong conceptual foundation for the program generation approach.
    *   Figures and tables are used effectively to illustrate the game, the model's predictions (Figure 2), and its generative capabilities (Figure 3). The examples of generated novel questions are particularly compelling.

*   **Suggestions for Improvement:**
    *   As mentioned under Quality, the paper would be greatly improved by adding a section or appendix detailing the process of converting natural language questions into programs.
    *   A brief discussion of the computational complexity and runtime of the optimization procedure would provide a fuller picture of the method's practicality.

**Originality:**

The paper is highly original. The central idea of treating goal-directed question asking as probabilistic program generation is novel and powerful.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   This work represents a significant conceptual leap from traditional active learning, which is typically confined to querying labels. By defining a rich, compositional hypothesis space of queries, the authors open a path toward more flexible and human-like machine learners.
    *   It also differs substantially from dominant neural network approaches to question generation (e.g., VQG), which are data-hungry, less goal-directed, and struggle with the kind of systematic, zero-shot generalization demonstrated here. The synthesis of ideas from probabilistic modeling, program induction, and cognitive science is a novel and fruitful combination.
    *   The approach provides a formal, predictive model of open-ended human question asking, which is a novel contribution to computational cognitive science.

**Significance:**

The work is highly significant and likely to have a substantial impact on several fields.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   **For Active Learning/AI:** It provides a concrete and expandable framework for building machines that can learn more efficiently by asking richer, more targeted questions. This could be transformative for applications where data acquisition is expensive, such as robotics, scientific discovery, and medical diagnostics.
    *   **For Cognitive Science:** It offers a compelling computational account of a fundamental human cognitive ability. The finding that human questions are best explained by a trade-off between informativeness and complexity provides a quantitative validation of a long-standing intuition.
    *   **For NLP/Dialog Systems:** It pushes beyond template-based or purely data-driven dialog systems towards agents that can reason about their goals and information needs to formulate genuinely new and useful queries.

*   **Weaknesses (related to future impact):**
    *   The primary barrier to broader significance is the reliance on a domain-specific, hand-engineered grammar. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but its severity cannot be overstated. The success of this approach in new domains will depend heavily on the effort required to construct a new grammar and feature set. Future work demonstrating how parts of the grammar can be re-used or learned would dramatically increase the work's practical impact.

**Arguments Pro/Con Acceptance:**

*   **Pro:**
    *   Presents a highly original and elegant formal framework for a challenging problem.
    *   Strong empirical results that validate the model's components and demonstrate its ability to predict human data and generate creative, novel questions.
    *   High potential for significant impact on machine learning, AI, and cognitive science.
    *   The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written.

*   **Con:**
    *   The reliance on a hand-crafted, domain-specific grammar limits immediate generalizability.
    *   The crucial step of translating natural language to programs is not described, which is a significant omission affecting reproducibility.

**Conclusion:**

This is an excellent paper that introduces a novel and significant research direction. The framing of question asking as program generation is a powerful idea, and the authors' probabilistic model provides a compelling proof of concept. Despite the limitations regarding scalability and the manual components of the pipeline, the originality of the idea and the strength of the results make this a clear and enthusiastic accept. It is the kind of thought-provoking work that NIPS should showcase.
