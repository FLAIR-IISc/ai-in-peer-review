This paper investigates reproducing user queries in a Battleship game, framing the question-asking process not as individual square inquiries, but as queries expressed through an artificial language grounded in compositional (Montagovian) semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model utilizing four basic features of each question: expected information gain, answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and board-relatedness. 

While the high-level motivation connecting this work to active learning is sound, the presented ideas lack novelty and the task itself is quite straightforward, heavily constrained, and logically clear-cut. Consequently, I do not anticipate this paper resonating with the NIPS audience.
