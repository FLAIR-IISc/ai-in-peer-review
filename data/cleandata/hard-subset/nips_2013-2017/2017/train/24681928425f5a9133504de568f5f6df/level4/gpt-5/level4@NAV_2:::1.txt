This paper seeks to model the questions users ask in a Battleship-style game, substituting tile-specific inquiries with queries formulated in an engineered formal language exhibiting compositional, Montagovian-style semantics. A log-linear model is trained over a small set of four simple question features—namely expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numeric), and an indicator of whether the query references properties of the board at all.

While the active learning motivation is sensible, the core ideas are fairly straightforward and the task itself is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe this work will interest the NIPS community.
