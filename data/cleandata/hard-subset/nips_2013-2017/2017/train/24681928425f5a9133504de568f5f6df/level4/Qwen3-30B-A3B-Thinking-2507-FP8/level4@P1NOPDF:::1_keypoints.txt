- Models user questions in a Battleship-like game using an artificial, compositional Montagovian language.  
- Uses a log-linear model with four simple features: expected information gain, answer type (Boolean/numeric), and references to board features.  
- Motivation for active learning is reasonable.  
- Core contributions are unremarkable.  
- Task is trivially simple, highly constrained, and unambiguous—offering little challenge.  
- Approach relies on basic statistical feature modeling lacking novelty or depth.  
- Artificial language is technically precise but merely a straightforward application of existing active learning ideas.  
- Fails to justify relevance of this well-defined, fully observable setup to broader ML problems.  
- Lacks the theoretical or empirical substance expected at NIPS.  
- Likely has minimal potential for generalization to real-world problems.  
- Reviewer finds the work uninteresting for the NIPS community.
