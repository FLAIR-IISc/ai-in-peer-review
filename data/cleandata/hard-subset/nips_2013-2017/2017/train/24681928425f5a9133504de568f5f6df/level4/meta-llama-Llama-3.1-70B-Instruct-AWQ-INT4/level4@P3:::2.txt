The authors present a novel approach to modeling human question asking as program generation, where questions are treated as formal programs that output an answer when executed on the state of the world. The paper is well-written, and the model is evaluated from both quantitative and qualitative perspectives. The authors provide an innovative perspective, unifying modern linguistic and cognitive theory to examine how people ask questions, which has the potential to foster novel research in the active learning literature with models asking more sophisticated questions.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the cognitive science literature, advancing the field of self-directed learning. Previous work found that question informativeness was a poor predictor of human question asking, and this paper resolves part of why this is the case (it is secondary to complexity and may in part be due to the size of possible questions available to the model). The authors demonstrate that their model can predict the distribution of questions people ask in novel scenarios and generate genuinely novel, human-like questions that were not present in the data set.

The model is carefully evaluated, and the authors compare different models on their ability to quantitatively predict the distribution of human questions. The results show that the full model, which includes features for informativeness, complexity, answer type, and relevance, provides the best account of the data. The authors also demonstrate that the model can generate novel questions that are useful in their respective contexts.

However, there are some limitations to the approach. The method is computationally strained, and it is unclear how it could scale or extend to domains of interest to the broader community. The battleship domain is small, with only a few different features, and it is unclear how the approach could be applied to more complex domains. Additionally, the method relies on hand-coding human question text into a grammar, which may not be feasible for larger datasets or more complex domains.

Minor comments:

1. The authors may want to consider performing a nested model comparison with the log-likelihoods of the lesioned models to get tests of statistical significance.
2. For sequential question asking, there are other models within the linguistics literature of question-asking in formal semantics and pragmatics, such as the Questions under Discussion literature. The authors may want to consider exploring these models in future work.
