This research paper attempts to reproduce user queries in a battleship game, where questions are posed in an artificial language with constructive logical (Montagovian) semantics, rather than asking about individual classes. A log-linear model is used on four simple characteristics of the questions: expected information gain (informative), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question includes board features.

The motivation is appropriate in the context of highly active learning, but the ideas presented in this paper are all clear and the task is too simple, overly constrained, and logically ambiguous. I don't think this research paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
