This paper seeks to recreate user queries in a Battleship game by replacing questions about individual cells with queries posed in an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. A log-linear model is trained over four simple features of the questions—expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question involves features of the board at all.

At a high level, the active learning motivation is reasonable, but the ideas are fairly obvious and the task is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe this work will interest the NIPS community.
