- Studies how people ask K-ary categorical questions using a hand-specified PCFG for a Battleship-style grid world.
- Questions are lambda-calculus expressions with a PCFG prior; question quality modeled as informativeness, complexity, and answer type.
- Complexity (string length or PCFG negative log-prob) is the strongest predictor; informativeness is secondary.
- Human question data were annotated to fit the grammar; evaluation used leave-one-out cross-validation with component ablations.
- All three components contribute to prediction accuracy; model also generates novel, plausible questions.
- Paper is clearly written and combines quantitative analyses with informative qualitative examples.
- Advances understanding in computational cognitive science by showing question complexity and question-space size shape human queries.
- Limitation: unclear scalability and transferability beyond the small, low-dimensional Battleship domain; method appears computationally demanding.
- Recommendation: authors should articulate a concrete, higher-impact application where the framework is tractable and outperforms simpler methods.
- Minor suggestion: perform nested model comparisons (e.g., likelihood-ratio tests) for statistical significance.
- Minor suggestion: for sequential question asking, cite relevant formal semantics/pragmatics work (Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
