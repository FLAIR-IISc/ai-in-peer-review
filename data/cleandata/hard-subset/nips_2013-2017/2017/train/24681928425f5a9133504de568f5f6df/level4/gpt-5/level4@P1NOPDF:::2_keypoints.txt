- Studies human question asking with K-ary answers in a “battleship” domain using a hand-specified PCFG over lambda-calculus question representations.  
- Models question quality as a linear combination of informativeness (expected information gain), complexity (length or negative log PCFG probability), and answer type.  
- Human questions from prior work were hand-coded into the grammar for evaluation.  
- Compares lesioned model variants using leave-one-out cross-validation.  
- Finds all factors matter, with complexity the strongest predictor; generates several novel questions.  
- Paper is clear, well written, and unifies linguistic and cognitive theories, advancing self-directed learning and active question asking.  
- Explains prior null results by showing informativeness is secondary to complexity and sensitive to question-space size.  
- Major limitation: scalability and automation of mapping from text to formal semantics are unclear; method is computationally strained.  
- Domain limitation: small, simple battleship domain may limit broader NIPS audience appeal; authors should present a more general, applicable scenario.  
- Minor suggestions: perform nested model comparisons using log-likelihoods for statistical tests; consider literature on sequential questioning (Questions under Discussion—Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
