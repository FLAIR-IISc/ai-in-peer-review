- Models human question asking as probabilistic program generation in a compositional lambda-calculus language.
- Evaluated in a "Battleship"-like game where participants' natural language questions are mapped to formal programs.
- Core model: a PCFG defines the space of questions and a log-linear model scores programs by informativeness and simplicity.
- Features learned: Expected Information Gain (informativeness), complexity (program length penalty), answer type, and task relevance.
- Dataset required manual translation of human questions into the formal language for training and evaluation.
- Evaluation via leave-one-out cross-validation and lesion studies shows the full model best fits human data.
- Key finding: question complexity is the strongest predictor of human queries, outperforming EIG.
- Model can generatively produce novel, human-like questions in specific contexts.
- Strength: original framing linking active learning, cognitive science, and formal linguistics as program synthesis.
- Strength: technically rigorous with systematic quantitative and qualitative evaluation; clarifies prior puzzles about EIG.
- Strength: clear, well-written, and accessible presentation of a complex model.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on a hand-crafted, domain-specific grammar and manual annotation limits scalability and generalization.
- Weakness: computationally strained even in the toy domain; unclear path to complex, open-ended domains without substantial engineering.
- Weakness: limited immediate impact on mainstream ML due to bespoke semantic representation; needs integration with modern ML approaches.
- Suggestion: perform nested model comparisons (e.g., likelihood ratio tests) to assess statistical significance of model components.
- Suggestion: explore the Questions Under Discussion (QUD) literature to model sequential question asking and dialogue context.
- Overall: innovative and well-executed contribution to cognitive science, but practical applicability is limited until scalability and integration with modern ML are addressed.
