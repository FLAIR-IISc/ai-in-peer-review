- Models how users generate questions in a modified Battleship game using a formal compositional (Montagovian) question language.  
- Uses a log-linear model conditioned on a small set of simple features (e.g., expected information gain, answer type—Boolean vs numerical, and whether a question targets board features).  
- High-level active-learning motivation is reasonable.  
- The presented ideas are straightforward and lack novelty.  
- Experimental task is overly simplistic, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous.  
- Reviewer judges the paper unlikely to be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
