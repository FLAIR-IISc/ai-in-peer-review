- Studies human question-asking with K-ary answers using a hand-crafted PCFG in a "battleship" domain.
- Questions are represented in lambda calculus; question quality is a linear combination of informativeness, complexity, and answer type.
- Human question data were hand-coded into the grammar and evaluated with leave-one-out cross-validation.
- Ablation shows all factors matter, with complexity being the strongest predictor.
- The model generates novel, human-like questions and is evaluated both quantitatively and qualitatively.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and offers a unifying linguistic/cognitive perspective on question-asking.
- Key cognitive contribution: explains why informativeness alone is a poor predictor—complexity and question-space constraints matter.
- Major limitation: reliance on a hand-crafted grammar and unclear scalability to larger, real-world ML domains.
- The small battleship domain is already computationally strained, raising concerns about generalization.
- Paper’s impact would increase with a concrete application or scenario relevant to the broader ML community.
- Minor suggestion: perform nested model comparisons via log-likelihoods for formal significance tests.
- Minor suggestion: consider incorporating literature on Questions Under Discussion and formal semantics/pragmatics for sequential question-asking.
