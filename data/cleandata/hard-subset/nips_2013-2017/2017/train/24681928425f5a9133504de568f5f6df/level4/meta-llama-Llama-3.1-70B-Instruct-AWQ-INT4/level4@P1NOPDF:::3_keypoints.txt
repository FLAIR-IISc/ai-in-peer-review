- Proposes a cognitive model that scores questions by weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Models questions as programs executed on world states; applied to the Battleship grid task.
- Full model outperforms lesioned variants on held-out human data by log-likelihood.
- Model without EIG performs similarly to full model on correlation with human judgments.
- Evidence for people balancing informativeness and complexity is weak/noisy (19% bootstrap chance results could be wrong; sparse human data).
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible.
- Main contribution is as an AI demonstration: generating compositional questions in a non-trivial domain.
- Claims about cognitive insights (how people trade off factors) are not convincingly supported.
- Prior work is under-cited: similar ideas in Hawkins 2015 (goal-based questioner) and Roberts 1996 (QUD).
