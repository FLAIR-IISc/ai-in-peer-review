- Task: Models human question generation with single-word answers in a Battleship-like domain.  
- Method: Hand-specified PCFG induces a prior over lambda-calculus question programs.  
- Utility model: Log-linear energy combining expected information gain (EIG), program complexity (negative log-prob under uniform PCFG), and answer-type preferences.  
- Data mapping: Human questions from prior study mapped into the formal program language.  
- Evaluation: Leave-one-context-out cross-validation comparing full model to ablations removing informativeness, complexity, or type features.  
- Result: All feature classes contribute; complexity is especially critical for predicting human questions.  
- Qualitative: Shows sampled/generated questions not present in training data.  
- Contribution: Novel synthesis of linguistic and cognitive perspectives on question formation with implications for richer active learning queries.  
- Presentation: Clear, well-structured paper with quantitative and qualitative evaluation.  
- Cognitive-science insight: Advances understanding of self-directed learning and suggests complexity constraints outweigh informativeness alone, modulated by candidate space size.  
- Limitation: Scalability unclear—mapping NL to programs and computationally intensive inference in a constrained Battleship setting may not extend easily to larger ML domains.  
- Suggestion: Authors should articulate a concrete larger-scale application where the framework is practical and informative.  
- Minor: Recommend formal significance tests treating ablations as nested models using held-out log-likelihoods.  
- Minor: Cite relevant formal semantics/pragmatics work for sequential/discourse-sensitive questioning (Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
