- Proposes modeling human question asking as program generation using a probabilistic framework.  
- Treats questions as executable programs that map world states to answers.  
- Evaluated on a dataset of human questions from an information-search game.  
- Introduces a new computational framework explaining how people construct rich domain queries.  
- Provides a method to generate meaningful questions/programs from scratch.  
- Demonstrates the model predicts human questions and generates novel, human-like questions not in training.  
- Uses features like informativeness, complexity, answer type, and relevance to predict question usefulness.  
- Full model outperforms lesioned variants; lesioning key components lowers prediction quality.  
- Operates on semantic representations rather than raw natural language text.  
- Some grammar components are specific to the Battleship domain.  
- Relies on substantial domain knowledge and special-purpose structures, limiting generalizability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10); authors should address generalizability and consider additional features.
