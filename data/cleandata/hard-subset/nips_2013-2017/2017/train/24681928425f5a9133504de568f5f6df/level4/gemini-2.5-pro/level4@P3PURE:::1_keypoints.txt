- Models user question generation in a modified Battleship game using an artificial compositional language with Montagovian semantics.  
- Predicts questions with a log-linear model based on four simple features (including expected information gain, answer type—Boolean vs numerical—and whether the question references board features).  
- High-level motivation from active learning is reasonable.  
- Experimental setup is overly simple, tightly constrained, logically unambiguous, and artificial.  
- In this simplified setting the proposed solution is direct and unsurprising.  
- The work offers limited technical novelty.  
- Unlikely to be of broad interest to the NIPS community.
