- Proposes a cognitive model that predicts which questions people ask given belief states, scoring questions by a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Evaluated in the Battleship domain for identifying ship locations on a grid.
- Full model yields higher held-out log-likelihood than lesioned variants, but the EIG-less model correlates with human judgments about as well as the full model.
- Evidence for EIG’s influence is weak: log-likelihood improvement may be spurious (bootstrap ~19% chance) and correlation results show little/no EIG effect.
- Paper is technically sound, clear, and reproducible; main contribution is demonstrating compositional question generation in a non-trivial domain.
- More impactful for AI than cognitive science because results do not convincingly show how people balance informativeness and complexity.
- Omits important prior work (Hawkins et al. 2015; Roberts 1996 QUD) that models questions as goals/programs and trades off EIG and complexity in simpler domains.
