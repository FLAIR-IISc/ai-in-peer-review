- Proposes a cognitive model that scores questions by a weighted sum of factors (informativeness, complexity, answer type, relevance to belief/world facts).  
- Applies the model to the Battleship domain to predict questions aimed at locating ship clusters.  
- Full model outperformed lesioned variants in log-likelihood on human data.  
- Model without expected information gain achieved comparable correlation with human judgments.  
- Technically sound, but the paper's impact is stronger for AI than for cognitive science.  
- Results are inconclusive about how humans balance informativeness versus complexity; evidence for influence of expected information gain is weak.  
- Paper is well-written and reproducible.  
- Lacks citations to related prior work (e.g., Hawkins 2015 on "goals" and Roberts 1996 on question-under-discussion).  
- Main contribution: demonstrates feasibility of generating compositional, interesting questions in a non-trivial domain.
