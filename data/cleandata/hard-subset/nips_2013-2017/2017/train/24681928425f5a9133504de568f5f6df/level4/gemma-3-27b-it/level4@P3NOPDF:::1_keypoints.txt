- Paper studies reproducing user questions in a simplified Battleship game as an active learning task using an artificial compositional (Montagovian) language and a log-linear model with four features (expected information gain, answer type, board-related indicators).  
- Motivation is reasonable but the work is incremental and lacks sufficient novelty for NIPS.  
- Task formulation is overly simplified and highly constrained, reducing real-world complexity.  
- The artificial language’s explicit semantics remove much of the logical ambiguity present in natural language QA.  
- Compositional semantics are applied in a very restricted space, lessening the challenges of semantic parsing and question generation.  
- The four model features are intuitive but not a methodological advance, and the paper fails to explain why they work or explore richer features/models.  
- Evaluation is weak: no compelling baseline comparison, unclear how simple strategies would perform, and no ablation study to assess feature contributions.  
- Presentation and technical descriptions are clear.  
- Overall reads as a proof-of-concept with limited connection to broader active learning/NLP research and insufficient acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Recommendation: reject due to simplicity of the task, lack of novelty, and limited evaluation.
