- Proposes a cognitive model where unnormalized log-probability of a question is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.  
- Applies the model to the Battleship domain to predict which questions people ask to locate ship clusters in a grid.  
- Full model outperformed lesioned versions on held-out human data by log-likelihood.  
- However, a model without expected information gain (EIG) showed similar correlation with human judgments, questioning EIG’s contribution.  
- Log-likelihood advantage is uncertain (bootstrap indicates ~19% chance of being incorrect) and correlations are noisy due to sparse data, limiting cognitive conclusions.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and reproducible.  
- Claims about revealing how people balance informativeness and complexity are not convincingly supported by the results.  
- Missing citation/discussion of relevant prior work (e.g., Hawkins 2015 on “goals” and Roberts 1996 on QUDs) that similarly model questions and tradeoffs with EIG.  
- Main contribution is demonstrating generation of interesting compositional questions in a non-trivial domain, valuable for AI and future cognitive models.
