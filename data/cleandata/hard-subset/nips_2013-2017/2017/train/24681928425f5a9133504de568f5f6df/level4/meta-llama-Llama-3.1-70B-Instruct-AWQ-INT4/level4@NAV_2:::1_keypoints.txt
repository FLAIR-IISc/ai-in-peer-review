- Novel approach: models human question asking as program generation within active machine learning.  
- Method: probabilistic model treating questions as formal programs that execute on world state to produce answers.  
- Evaluation: tested on human natural-language questions from an information-search game; predicts human question frequencies and can generate novel, human-like questions.  
- Writing and positioning: paper is well-written with a thorough related-work review situating the contribution in active ML and cognitive science.  
- Model design: uses a log-linear model over four features (informativeness, answer type, complexity, relevance), a reasonable and effective choice.  
- Performance: demonstrates balanced trade-off between informativeness and complexity, producing concise informative questions.  
- Technical clarity: provides detailed explanations of grammar, probabilistic generative model, and optimization procedure.  
- Limitation — task scope: evaluation on a constrained Battleship-like game with unambiguous logical semantics raises generalizability concerns.  
- Limitation — model simplicity: reliance on a log-linear model over simple features may be seen as overly simplistic.  
- Overall: clear, well-motivated contribution with effective results but potentially limited impact due to task simplicity.
