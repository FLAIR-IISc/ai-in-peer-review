- Paper frames human question asking as program generation, modeling questions in a Battleship-like game as executable programs.
- Proposes a probabilistic model using a compositional grammar to predict and generate human questions.
- Evaluated on a dataset of natural language questions collected from human players in the game.
- Results: model predicts human question frequencies and generates novel, human-like questions.
- Finds both question informativeness (Expected Information Gain) and complexity are important for human-like question generation.
- Motivation is well-grounded: understanding question asking can improve active learning.
- Framing questions as programs with compositional semantics builds on cognitive-science views of reasoning as computation.
- Paper situates itself well relative to active learning, dialog systems, and visual question work.
- Strength: clear problem articulation and elegant formulation of questions as programs.
- Strength: probabilistic grammar allows prediction and creative generation; ablation study supports importance of informativeness and complexity.
- Strength: qualitative examples of generated questions aid interpretation.
- Weakness: the simple Battleship domain may limit generalizability to real-world question asking.
- Weakness: reliance on a pre-defined grammar introduces artificiality and raises questions about cognitive validity.
- Weakness: connections between chosen features (informativeness, complexity, answer type) and underlying cognitive mechanisms need more elaboration.
- Weakness: evaluation could be strengthened by comparing to stronger baselines (e.g., n-gram models) and discussing required vs. preserved domain knowledge.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framing, demonstrated predictive and generative ability, clear setup, insightful ablation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability, artificiality from grammar, insufficient exploration of cognitive links.
- Overall recommendation: accept with caveats — acknowledge limitations and pursue more complex, realistic future work.
