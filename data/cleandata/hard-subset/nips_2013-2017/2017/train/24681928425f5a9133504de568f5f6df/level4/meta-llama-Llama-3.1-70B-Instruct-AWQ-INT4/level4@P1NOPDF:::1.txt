This paper attempts to reproduce user questions in a game of battleship, where instead of asking about individual squares, users ask questions that essentially utilize an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model over four simple features of the questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question involves features of the board at all.

At a high level, the motivation in terms of active learning is reasonable. However, the ideas presented in this paper are rather straightforward and the task is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe that this paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
