This paper investigates replicating user questioning behavior in a Battleship game, reframing individual square queries as questions posed within an artificial language grounded in compositional, Montagovian semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model utilizing four features: expected information gain, answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and board-relevance of the question.

While the active learning motivation is sound in principle, the presented concepts are largely intuitive and the experimental task is notably simple, heavily constrained, and lacks logical ambiguity. Consequently, I do not anticipate this work will be of significant interest to the NeurIPS audience.
