This paper aims to reproduce user question-asking behavior in a Battleship game, replacing queries about individual squares with questions expressed in an artificial language endowed with compositional, Montagovian logical semantics. The approach employs a log-linear model over four simple features of the questions, including expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and whether the question involves features of the board at all.

While the high-level motivation—framed in terms of active learning—is reasonable, the ideas presented are fairly obvious, and the task itself is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Given these limitations, I do not believe the paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
