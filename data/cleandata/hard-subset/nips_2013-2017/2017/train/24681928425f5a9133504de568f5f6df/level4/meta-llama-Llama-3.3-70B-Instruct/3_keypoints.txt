- Proposes a cognitive model to predict the timing of specific questions within belief-state contexts.
- Model scores questions by a weighted sum of factors: informativeness, complexity, answer type, and relevance to the belief state.
- Applied in the Battleship domain; full model outperforms lesioned variants on log-likelihood of human data.
- But when measuring correlation with human judgments, the model without expected information gain (EIG) matches the full model.
- Technically sound overall, but the contribution reads as more significant for AI than for cognitive science.
- Claims about how people trade off informativeness and complexity are not conclusively supported; EIG’s influence is inconsistent or minor.
- Writing is clear and results appear reproducible.
- Missing references to relevant prior work (e.g., Hawkins 2015; Roberts 1996) that considered questions-as-programs and question-under-discussion and info-gain vs complexity.
- Main contribution is demonstrating generation of compositional questions for a non-trivial domain, showing promise for future cognitive and AI applications.
