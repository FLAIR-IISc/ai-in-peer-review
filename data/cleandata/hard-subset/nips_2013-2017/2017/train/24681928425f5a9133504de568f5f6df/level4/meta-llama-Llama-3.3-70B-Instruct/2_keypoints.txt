- Investigates human question-asking in a K-ary answer setting using a manually defined PCFG for a "battleship" domain.  
- Represents questions as lambda-calculus semantic statements generated by the PCFG.  
- Agent selects a single-word question to maximize information gain about a partially observable colored-shape grid.  
- Evaluates question quality with a linear model combining informativeness, complexity (length or negative log PCFG probability), and answer type.  
- Human questions from prior study were hand-coded into the grammar and evaluated via leave-one-out cross-validation.  
- Lesioned-model comparisons show all factors matter, with complexity having the largest effect.  
- Paper integrates modern linguistic and cognitive theories, offering a novel perspective relevant to active learning and richer question models.  
- Well-written, clear, and includes thorough quantitative and qualitative evaluation.  
- Advances self-directed learning research by explaining why informativeness alone poorly predicts human questions, emphasizing complexity and question-space size.  
- Major limitation: scalability and applicability to more complex domains are unclear; battleship domain is simple and the method is computationally demanding.  
- To increase appeal, authors should present a clear application scenario in a domain of broader interest.  
- Minor suggestions: perform nested model comparisons using lesioned-model log-likelihoods for significance testing and consider linguistics work on sequential questioning (e.g., Questions under Discussion, Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
