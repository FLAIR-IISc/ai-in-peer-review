

This paper seeks to model user queries in a Battleship game variant where square-by-square questions are replaced by queries posed over an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. A log-linear model is employed over four simple features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the query references board features. While the high-level motivation regarding active learning is reasonable, the ideas presented are largely obvious, the task is overly simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, this work does not offer sufficient novelty or technical depth to interest the NIPS community.
