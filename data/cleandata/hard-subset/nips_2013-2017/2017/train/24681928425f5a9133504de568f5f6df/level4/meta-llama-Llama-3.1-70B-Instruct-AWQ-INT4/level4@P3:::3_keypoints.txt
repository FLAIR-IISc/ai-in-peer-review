- Presents a cognitive model predicting question choice by summing informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance; applied to Battleship and full model beats lesioned versions on held-out log-likelihood.  
- Correlation with human judgments shows the model without EIG performs about as well as the full model, suggesting EIG’s role is unclear.  
- Paper is technically sound, clear, and likely reproducible.  
- Doubts about contribution to cognitive science: results are ambiguous about how people balance informativeness and complexity (bootstrap and sparse-data noise).  
- Paper needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and directions for future research.  
- Prior-work discussion is incomplete; missing relevant literature (e.g., questions-as-programs like Hawkins 2015 and Roberts’ QUD 1996) and should clarify differences.  
- Valuable contribution to AI/question generation: model generates interesting compositional, human-like questions for nontrivial domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically solid model, human-like outputs, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited cognitive-science insight, missing key references, shallow limitations/future-work discussion.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, requiring revisions to expand prior-work discussion and limitations/future-research sections.
