- Proposes a cognitive model predicting timing and context of questions relative to belief states.  
- Defines unnormalized log probability of a question as a weighted sum of four factors: informativeness (EIG), complexity (question length), answer type, and relevance (belief vs general facts).  
- Applies the model to the Battleship domain (locating ships in a grid).  
- Full model outperforms lesioned versions on human data by log-likelihood.  
- Model without EIG shows similar correlation with human judgments as the full model.  
- Results do not provide conclusive evidence on how people balance informativeness and complexity (19% chance log-likelihood result is incorrect; correlations noisy due to sparse data).  
- Paper is technically sound and successful as an AI project but less convincing in cognitive science contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and reproducible but lacks discussion of relevant prior work.  
- Approach parallels prior ideas (Hawkins 2015; Roberts 1996 QUD); Hawkins’ questioner model also trades EIG against complexity in a simpler domain.  
- Model family (compositional questions + EIG/complexity tradeoff) is promising with significant future application potential.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrates feasibility of generating interesting compositional questions for a non-trivial domain.
