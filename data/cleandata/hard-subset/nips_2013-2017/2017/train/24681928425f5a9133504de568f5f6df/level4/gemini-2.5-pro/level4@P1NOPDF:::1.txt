This paper models human question-asking in a modified version of the game Battleship. In this setting, simple queries about individual squares are replaced by more complex questions formulated in an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. The authors use a log-linear model to score potential questions based on four simple features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question pertains to features of the board.

While the high-level motivation, framed in the context of active learning, is reasonable, the ideas presented in this paper are rather straightforward. The experimental task itself is overly simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, the paper lacks the technical novelty and sophisticated problem formulation that would be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
