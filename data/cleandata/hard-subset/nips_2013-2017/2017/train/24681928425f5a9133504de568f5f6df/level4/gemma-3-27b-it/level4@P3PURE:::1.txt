This paper investigates reproducing user questions in a simplified Battleship game, replacing queries about individual squares with questions expressed through an artificial language possessing compositional, Montagovian semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model trained on four features of these questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question relates to board features. 

While the motivation connecting this work to active learning is reasonable at a high level, the core ideas presented feel largely obvious, and the task itself is quite simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, I do not believe this paper will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
