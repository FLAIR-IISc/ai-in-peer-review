This paper models how users generate questions in a modified version of the game Battleship. In this setting, instead of querying individual squares, users ask questions using a formal language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. The authors use a log-linear model conditioned on four simple features of the questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question pertains to board features.

While the high-level motivation from an active learning perspective is reasonable, the ideas presented are straightforward. The experimental task is overly simplistic, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, I do not believe this paper will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
