- Paper studies human single-word question-asking with K-ary answers in a "battleship" domain using a manually defined PCFG over lambda-calculus questions.  
- Agent selects a question to maximize information about a partially observable colored-shape grid.  
- PCFG acts as a prior; question quality is a linear combination of informativeness, complexity, and answer type.  
- Complexity is measured by question length or negative log probability under the PCFG.  
- Human question data were hand-coded into the authors’ grammar.  
- Evaluation uses lesioned model variants with leave-one-out cross-validation; all three factors matter, complexity is most influential.  
- Paper presents several novel questions generated by the model.  
- Writing and evaluation are clear, rigorous, and well-presented both quantitatively and qualitatively.  
- Contributions: novel synthesis of linguistic and cognitive theory; advances understanding of self-directed learning and active question formulation.  
- Resolves prior puzzle that informativeness poorly predicts human behavior by showing complexity plays a key role.  
- Major limitation: scalability and generality concerns—manual semantic encoding and computational cost may not extend to larger, real-world domains.  
- Recommendation: authors should clarify broader applicability and provide scenarios where the method yields novel insights.  
- Minor suggestions: perform nested model comparisons using log-likelihoods for statistical tests.  
- Minor suggestions: cite sequential question frameworks from linguistics (e.g., Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
