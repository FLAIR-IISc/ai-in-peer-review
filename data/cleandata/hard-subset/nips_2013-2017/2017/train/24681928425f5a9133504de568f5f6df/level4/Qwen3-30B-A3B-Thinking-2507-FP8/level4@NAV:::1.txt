

This paper seeks to reproduce user queries in a Battleship game by replacing square-specific questions with queries over an artificial language featuring compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. A log-linear model is applied to four simple features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question references board features. While the active learning motivation is reasonable at a high level, the paper's ideas are largely obvious, the task is overly simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not find this paper compelling for the NIPS community.
