- Investigates human question-asking with K-ary answers using a manually defined PCFG in a Battleship-like partially observable grid domain.  
- Represents questions as lambda-calculus statements and uses the PCFG as a prior over question forms.  
- Scores questions by a linear combination of expected information gain, complexity (length or negative log PCFG prob), and answer type.  
- Human question data from a prior study were manually encoded into the grammar.  
- Evaluated lesioned models with leave-one-out cross-validation; all factors contributed and complexity was the strongest predictor.  
- Introduced a set of novel questions generated by the model.  
- Positions the work at the intersection of linguistics and cognitive theory, with implications for active learning and question-generation models.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and rigorously evaluated both quantitatively and qualitatively.  
- Advances understanding of self-directed learning by showing informativeness alone poorly predicts human questions; complexity and question-space size matter.  
- Makes a substantive contribution to cognitive science and could merit acceptance at NIPS.  
- Major limitation: scalability and applicability to larger, more complex domains are unclear given the small Battleship domain and computational costs.  
- Recommendation: include a concrete, broader-interest application scenario to increase relevance.  
- Minor suggestion: run nested model comparisons using model log-likelihoods for statistical significance testing.  
- Suggested literature for sequential question-asking and formal semantics/pragmatics: Questions Under Discussion, Ginzburg (1995), Rojas-Esponda (2013).
