- Proposes modeling question asking as program generation: questions are formal programs executed on world state.
- Uses a log-linear model over features like expected information gain, answer type, complexity, and board relevance.
- Motivation connects to active learning and context-sensitive, goal-directed human question asking.
- Thorough evaluation including lesion analyses that remove informativeness, complexity, or answer-type features.
- Results show the full model (all features) best explains the human data; lesions reduce prediction quality.
- Task domain is simple (Battleship), highly constrained, and logically unambiguous.
- Simplicity and constraints limit generalizability to more complex, open-ended question-asking scenarios.
- Model operates on semantic representations rather than raw natural language, requiring domain-specific knowledge.
- Unclear how much knowledge engineering is needed to port the approach to other domains.
- Strengths: novel modeling approach, solid evaluation, and informative comparisons to alternative models.
- Weaknesses: limited task complexity, questionable generalizability, and reliance on domain-specific representations.
- Acceptance case: solid, novel, well-evaluated, and well-written contribution to active learning and question asking.
- Rejection case: task too simple, findings may not generalize, and limited appeal to the broader NIPS community.
