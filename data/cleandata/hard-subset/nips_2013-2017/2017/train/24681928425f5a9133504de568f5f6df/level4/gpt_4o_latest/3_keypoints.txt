- Introduces a cognitive model that predicts which questions are asked given belief states.  
- Question probability modeled as weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity (approx. length), answer type, and relevance.  
- Evaluated in the Battleship domain (locating ship clusters on a grid).  
- Full model outperformed simpler variants on held-out human data by log-likelihood.  
- A model without EIG performed comparably to the full model in correlation with human judgments.  
- Evidence for EIG’s role is weak/ambiguous (19% error probability from bootstrap; noisy sparse data).  
- Paper is technically sound and reproducible.  
- More successful as an AI/engineering contribution than as a strong cognitive-science account.  
- Claims about how people balance informativeness and complexity are not convincingly supported.  
- Overlooks relevant prior work (Hawkins 2015; QUD framework from Roberts 1996).  
- The compositional question-as-programs approach is promising and likely to be useful more broadly.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrating generation of meaningful compositional questions in a complex domain.
