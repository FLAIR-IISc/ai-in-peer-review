

This paper proposes a framework for generating user queries in a Battleship game, where direct square inquiries are replaced by questions formulated within an artificial language possessing compositional logical semantics (Montagovian). The approach employs a log-linear model over four simple features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question references board features. While the motivation in the context of active learning is reasonable, the core ideas presented are largely straightforward, and the task itself is highly constrained, simple, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe this work will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
