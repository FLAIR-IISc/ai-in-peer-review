This paper attempts to recreate user queries in a game of Battleship, where instead of asking about individual squares, questions are formulated using an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model that operates on four simple features of the questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question pertains to features of the board.

At a high level, the motivation behind this work, particularly in the context of active learning, is reasonable. However, the ideas presented in this paper are somewhat self-evident, and the task is extremely simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, I do not believe that this paper will resonate with or contribute meaningfully to the NIPS community.
