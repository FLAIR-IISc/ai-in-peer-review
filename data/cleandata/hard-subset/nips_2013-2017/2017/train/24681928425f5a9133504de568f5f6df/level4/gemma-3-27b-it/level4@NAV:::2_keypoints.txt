- Studies human question asking with K-ary answers in a “battleship” domain of hidden colored shapes on a partially observable grid.  
- Uses a Probabilistic Context-Free Grammar (PCFG) to model question structure and represents questions in lambda calculus.  
- Defines question quality as a composite of informativeness (expected information gain), complexity (length and PCFG probability), and answer type.  
- Encodes a human question dataset into the grammar for evaluation.  
- Leave-one-out cross-validation on lesioned models shows informativeness, complexity, and answer type each contribute to generation, with complexity most influential.  
- Produces a set of novel questions generated by the model.  
- Paper is compelling and innovative, integrating linguistic and cognitive theory, with exceptionally clear presentation and thorough quantitative and qualitative evaluation.  
- Makes a significant contribution to computational cognitive science/self-directed learning by explaining why informativeness alone poorly predicts human question choice.  
- Concerns about scalability and generalizability to more complex, real-world domains; current domain has limited feature space and high computational cost.  
- Recommends the authors articulate compelling real-world scenarios where the technique would be broadly applicable.  
- Minor: suggest nested model comparison using lesioned-model log-likelihoods to test significance.  
- Minor: for sequential question asking, consider linguistics literature on Questions under Discussion (e.g., Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
