- Paper models user question prediction in a modified Battleship game where users ask compositional logical queries instead of single-cell queries.  
- Uses a log-linear ranking model with simple hand-crafted features (expected information gain, answer type, grounding).  
- Motivation linking active learning and goal-oriented dialogue is sound and experiments are well-controlled.  
- Problem setup is clear and allows straightforward analysis.  
- Primary weakness: limited novelty — information-gain-based question modeling is well-established.  
- Modeling approach is simple and standard; no new technique, theory, or feature representation introduced.  
- Task/domain is overly simplistic and highly constrained, reducing broader impact.  
- Artificial, unambiguous language avoids real HCI/NLU challenges like parsing and semantic ambiguity.  
- Unclear how results would generalize to complex, realistic, unstructured query spaces.  
- Conclusion: contributions are incremental and not substantial enough for a top-tier conference; likely not of significant interest to NIPS readers.
