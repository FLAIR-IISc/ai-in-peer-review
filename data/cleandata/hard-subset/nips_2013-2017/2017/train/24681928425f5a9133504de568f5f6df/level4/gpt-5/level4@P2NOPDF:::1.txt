This paper models how users ask questions in a Battleship-style game, replacing queries about individual grid squares with questions expressed in an artificial language with compositional Montagovian semantics. The authors fit a log-linear model over a few simple features of the questions, including expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and whether the question references properties of the board.

While the motivation framed in terms of active learning is reasonable, the core ideas are fairly obvious and the task itself is very simple, tightly constrained, and logically unambiguous. As a result, the work feels limited in scope and novelty. I do not believe this paper will be of broad interest to the NIPS community.
