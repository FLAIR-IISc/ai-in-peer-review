- Paper models human question asking with K-ary answers using a hand-crafted PCFG and lambda-calculus semantics in a "battleship" grid domain.  
- Agent formulates single-word-answer questions to maximize information about the board; PCFG serves as a prior over questions.  
- Question quality is a linear combination of informativeness (expected information gain), complexity (length or -log PCFG probability), and answer type.  
- Human question data were hand-coded into the grammar and compared to model variants via leave-one-out cross-validation.  
- Lesioned-model comparisons show all factors contribute non-negligibly, with complexity being the dominant predictor; model can generate novel questions.  
- Strengths: novel synthesis of linguistic and cognitive theory, clear writing, and robust quantitative and qualitative evaluation.  
- Cognitive-science contribution: advances understanding of self-directed learning by showing complexity constraints explain prior failures of informativeness-based accounts.  
- Recommendation: contribution is sufficient for NIPS publication from a computational cognitive science perspective.  
- Limitation: scalability to larger or more complex domains is unclear—Battleship is small and the method is computationally intensive.  
- Suggestion: authors should clarify broader applicability and provide scenarios where the technique yields novel insights for larger communities.  
- Minor methodological comments: perform nested model comparisons/log-likelihood tests for significance.  
- Minor literature suggestion: consider formal semantics/pragmatics models for sequential question asking (e.g., Ginzburg 1995; Rojas-Esponda 2013).
