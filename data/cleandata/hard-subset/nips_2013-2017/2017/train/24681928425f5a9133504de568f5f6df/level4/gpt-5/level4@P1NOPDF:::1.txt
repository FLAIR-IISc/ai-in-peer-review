This paper attempts to reproduce user questions in a game of Battleship, replacing queries about individual squares with questions posed in an artificial language with compositional, Montagovian semantics. The authors fit a log-linear model over a small set of simple question features—expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and whether the question references features of the board at all.

While the high-level motivation in terms of active learning is reasonable, the ideas in the paper are fairly obvious, and the task is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe this paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
