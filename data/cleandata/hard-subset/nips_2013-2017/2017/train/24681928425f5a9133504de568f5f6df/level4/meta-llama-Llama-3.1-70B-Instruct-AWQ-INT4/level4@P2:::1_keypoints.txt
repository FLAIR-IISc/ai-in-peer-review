- Proposes modeling human question-asking in Battleship using a compositional logical language with Montagovian semantics and a log-linear probabilistic model over four features (EIG, answer type, complexity, relevance).
- Central hypothesis: active learning can be generalized to operate over compositional program spaces evaluated over possible worlds to enable richer question generation.
- Evaluated on 605 human questions; model predicts question distributions in novel scenarios and generates genuinely novel questions not in the dataset.
- Strength: novel approach to modeling question asking abilities.
- Strength: model predicts human question frequencies well.
- Strength: model can generate novel, plausible questions.
- Limitation: task is highly constrained and logically unambiguous, which may limit interest to the NIPS community.
- Originality: builds on existing active learning and compositional semantics; application to question asking is innovative but not entirely new.
- Clarity: generally good exposition; related work section needs more context and comparisons.
- Significance: moderate — contributes to understanding question asking but impact may be limited by task simplicity.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address noted limitations.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
