- Proposes modeling human question-asking as program generation, with questions as executable programs over world states.  
- Model combines informativeness (Expected Information Gain), complexity (program length), answer type, and contextual relevance.  
- Evaluated on 605 human-generated Battleship questions; full model outperforms ablations (bootstrap confidence 81% vs info-agnostic, 100% vs complexity-agnostic).  
- Model generates novel, contextually appropriate questions not seen in training.  
- Technical approach is sound with a probabilistic log-linear feature model and proper implementation.  
- Evaluation is rigorous (cross-validation, bootstrapped log-likelihoods, qualitative generation).  
- Major limitation: sparse human data (many questions asked only once), limiting cognitive inferences.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear with precise grammar, feature specs, and reproducible supplementary materials.  
- Core innovation advances open-ended compositional question generation beyond standard active learning frameworks.  
- Prior related work (Hawkins et al. 2015, Roberts 1996) overlaps and should be more explicitly addressed.  
- Significant AI contribution toward scalable, human-like question generation; useful for active learning systems.  
- Cognitive claims about how people balance informativeness and complexity are overstated given current data.  
- Recommendation: well-suited for NIPS as a methodological advance, with caveats about cognitive claims and prior-work engagement.
