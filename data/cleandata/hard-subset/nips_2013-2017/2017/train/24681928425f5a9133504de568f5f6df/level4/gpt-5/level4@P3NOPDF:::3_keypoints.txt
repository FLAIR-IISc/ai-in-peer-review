- Proposes a cognitive model predicting which questions people ask using a weighted sum of EIG, complexity, answer type, and a relevance term.  
- Instantiates and evaluates the model in a Battleship domain, showing full model outperforms lesioned variants on predictive log-likelihood.  
- Removing EIG has little effect on correlation with human judgments, suggesting mixed evidence for EIG’s role.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and a compositional, executable-program prior over questions.  
- Strength: technically sound modeling, well written, and reproducible in principle.  
- Strength: demonstrates generation and scoring of nontrivial compositional questions in a realistic domain.  
- Strength: ablation analysis shows multiple features contribute to log-likelihood performance.  
- Concern: cognitive claims overreach the evidence given mixed metrics and noisy, sparse human data.  
- Concern: inconsistency across evaluation metrics (likelihood vs. rank correlation) is not analyzed.  
- Concern: “relevance” and “answer type” features are under-specified and may correlate with complexity.  
- Concern: potential overfitting and identifiability due to partially collinear features and unclear regularization.  
- Concern: limited generality because all results come from a single domain/task.  
- Missing related work: Hawkins (2015) on EIG-complexity trade-offs and Roberts (1996) QUD framework.  
- Missing related work: links to optimal experimental design, active learning, and pragmatic question-asking literatures.  
- Suggestion: temper cognitive claims and frame results as tentative evidence for Battleship only.  
- Suggestion: provide qualitative and quantitative analyses of where EIG helps (which questions and belief states).  
- Suggestion: report uncertainty and identifiability (SEs/credible intervals, bootstrap CIs, sensitivity to regularization).  
- Suggestion: clarify and justify the operationalization of “relevance” with examples and independent contribution analyses.  
- Suggestion: reconcile metrics by reporting calibration and ranking metrics with CIs and analyzing EIG’s effect on probability sharpening.  
- Suggestion: add stronger baselines and generalization tests (template baselines, heuristic active-learning baselines, cross-validation across participants/boards).  
- Suggestion: expand related work discussion and explicitly state novel contributions versus prior work.  
- Suggestion: ensure reproducibility by releasing code/data, full grammar/prior, hyperparameters, and inference details.  
- Overall: solid AI/ML contribution demonstrating compositional question generation in Battleship; cognitive claims about EIG vs. complexity are suggestive but not conclusive.
