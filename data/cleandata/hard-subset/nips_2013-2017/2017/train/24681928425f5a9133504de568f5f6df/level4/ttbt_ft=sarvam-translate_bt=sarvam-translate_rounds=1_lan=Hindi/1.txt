This research paper attempts to reproduce user queries in a battleship game, where questions are posed in an artificial language with constructive logical (Montagovian) semantics, rather than asking about individual classes. A log-linear model is used on four simple characteristics of the questions: expected information gain (informative), answer type (Boolean or numeric), and whether the question includes board features.

Motivation is appropriate in the context of highly active learning, but the ideas presented in this paper are all obvious and the task is too simple, overly constrained, and logically unclear. I do not believe this research paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
