- Presents a cognitive model predicting question choice where unnormalized log-probability is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Applies the model to the Battleship domain and shows the full model outperforms lesioned versions by held-out log-likelihood.
- Finds that, for correlation with human judgments, the model without EIG performs about as well as the full model.
- Paper is technically sound and more successful as an AI/engineering project than as a cognitive-science explanation.
- Claims that the model reveals how people balance informativeness and complexity are unconvincing given noisy/sparse data and mixed statistical evidence (e.g., bootstrap ~19% uncertainty).
- Writing is clear and results appear reproducible.
- Paper lacks discussion of how to improve model performance, particularly given sparse human data, and could test other domains.
- Missing prior work: Hawkins 2015 (similar goal/program-based question model using EIG and complexity) and Roberts 1996 (QUD framework).
- Main contribution: demonstrates generation of interesting compositional questions in a non-trivial domain.
- Pro-acceptance: technically sound model, thorough quantitative and qualitative evaluation, and clear presentation.
- Con-acceptance: weak cognitive evidence about tradeoffs, omitted related prior work, and limited performance improvements due to sparse human data.
