- Models human question-asking in a battleship domain using a PCFG prior, lambda-calculus question forms, and a linear score combining EIG, complexity, and answer type; LOO CV and lesioned models show all components help, with complexity strongest.

- Timely, interesting problem connecting linguistics, cognition, and active learning.

- Clear modeling framework linking a generative prior over questions to decision-theoretic informativeness and pragmatic answer types.

- Well-written, transparent exposition that is easy to follow.

- Careful evaluation: quantitative ablations and cross-validation plus qualitative generated-question examples.

- Cognitive-science contribution: explains why EIG alone underpredicts human question-asking by showing complexity and hypothesis-space size matter.

- Scalability and generality concerns due to hand-engineered PCFG, manual mapping of human questions, and computational demands even in a small domain.

- Limited immediate relevance to broader ML/NeurIPS audiences unless adapted/automated for interactive ML, robotics, data labeling, or scientific discovery.

- Modeling choices unclear: dominance of complexity may reflect grammar/search constraints rather than true human preference.

- Data-handling risks: reliance on prior data and manual coding could introduce biases or ceiling effects; robustness to alternative codings unknown.

- Clarify the complexity term(s) used (length vs. PCFG surprisal) and report their relative roles and correlations.

- Use nested model comparisons or statistical tests on held-out log-likelihoods to formally assess each component’s contribution.

- Report how informativeness weight changes with grammar size and search budget to test whether limited question spaces suppress EIG’s utility.

- Provide computational profile: search/inference complexity, pruning strategies, runtimes, and effects of approximate search methods.

- Sketch automation paths: learned or induced grammars, semantic parsers mapping NL to logical forms, or neural priors respecting compositionality.

- Demonstrate broader application with a concrete scenario (e.g., interactive data collection, scientific hypothesis generation, robotics, tutoring) to improve ML relevance.
