This paper explores the reproduction of user questions in a game of battleship, where individual square inquiries are replaced by questions formulated in an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. A log-linear model is employed, leveraging four simple features of the questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question pertains to features of the board.

At a high level, the motivation is grounded in active learning, which is reasonable. However, the ideas presented in this paper are somewhat straightforward and lack novelty. The task itself is relatively simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. As a result, I do not believe that this paper will resonate with or contribute meaningfully to the NIPS community.
