- Presents a cognitive model where unnormalized log-probability of a question is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Models questions as executable programs mapping world states to answers, extending goal-directed dialog and compositional semantics.
- Applied and evaluated in the Battleship domain (inferring ship locations on a grid).
- Uses expected information gain and question complexity as principled factors for question probability.
- Technically sound with a thorough, reproducible evaluation using human data.
- Full model (all features) predicts held-out human data better by log-likelihood than lesioned variants.
- Correlation with human judgments is ambiguous: a model without EIG performs similarly to the full model.
- Evidence for how people balance informativeness and complexity is weak or inconclusive.
- Contribution to cognitive science is limited given unclear behavioral support for key claims.
- Missing citations/engagement with prior work: Hawkins 2015 (goals) and Roberts 1996 (QUD).
- Paper is well-written and clear, with reproducible details.
- Recommendation: valuable contribution to AI/active learning and compositional semantics, but needs stronger cognitive claims and incorporation of prior literature.
