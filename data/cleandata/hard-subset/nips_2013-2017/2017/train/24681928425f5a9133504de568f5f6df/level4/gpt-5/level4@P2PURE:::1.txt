This paper attempts to reproduce user questions in a game of Battleship, replacing queries about individual squares with questions expressed in an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. They train a log-linear model over four simple features of the questions, including expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and whether the question references features of the board at all.

At a high level, the active-learning motivation is reasonable, but the ideas are fairly obvious, and the task is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe this paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
