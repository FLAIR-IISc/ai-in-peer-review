- Presents a cognitive model predicting question selection using weighted features (informativeness/EIG, complexity/length, answer type, relevance).  
- Evaluated in the Battleship domain to infer ship locations from human question data.  
- Full model (all features) achieves higher held-out human question log-likelihood than reduced-feature variants.  
- Correlation analyses show the full model does not significantly outperform versions without the informativeness feature, possibly due to data sparsity.  
- Improvements from including EIG are not robust (bootstrap suggests ~19% chance results are due to chance).  
- Authors’ claim to offer a strong cognitive account is overstated given limited empirical support linking EIG to human choices.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-executed, clearly written, and appears reproducible.  
- Primary contribution is more to AI/active learning and question generation than to cognitive science.  
- Novel program-based approach enables generating compositional and informative questions in a non-trivial domain.  
- Would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work (e.g., Hawkins 2015; Roberts 1996/QUD).  
- Results are somewhat sensitive to estimation methods (bootstrap), indicating methodological fragility.  
- Overall a valuable contribution with potential to inspire future research, but some claims and positioning should be tempered.
