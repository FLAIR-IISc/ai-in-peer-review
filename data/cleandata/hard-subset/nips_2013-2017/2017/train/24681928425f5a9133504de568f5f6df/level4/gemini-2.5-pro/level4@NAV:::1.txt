This paper models human question-asking strategies in a modified Battleship game, where inquiries about specific squares are replaced by queries formulated in a formal language with compositional logical semantics. The approach utilizes a log-linear model based on four simple question features: expected information gain, the answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the query pertains to the board's features.

While the general motivation drawn from active learning is sound, the core ideas presented in this work are quite straightforward. The task is overly simplistic, operating within a highly constrained and logically unambiguous framework. Consequently, I am not convinced that this paper presents a contribution of sufficient novelty or impact for the NIPS community.
