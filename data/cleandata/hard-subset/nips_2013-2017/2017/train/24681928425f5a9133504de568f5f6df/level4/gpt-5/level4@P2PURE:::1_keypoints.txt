- Paper reproduces user questions in Battleship using an artificial language with compositional Montagovian semantics.  
- Queries about individual squares are replaced by compositional logical expressions.  
- They train a log-linear model over four simple features.  
- Features include expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs. numerical), and whether the question references board features.  
- Active-learning motivation is reasonable.  
- Methods and ideas are fairly obvious.  
- Task is very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous.  
- Reviewer doubts the paper will interest the NIPS community.
