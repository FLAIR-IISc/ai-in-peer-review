This paper attempts to replicate user queries in a game of battleship, where individual square inquiries are replaced by compositional logical questions, leveraging Montagovian semantics. A log-linear model is employed, incorporating four straightforward features of the questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and the involvement of board features.

At a high level, the motivation from an active learning perspective is reasonable. However, the ideas presented in this paper are somewhat intuitive, and the task is overly simplistic, highly constrained, and devoid of logical ambiguity. Consequently, I do not believe that this paper will resonate with the NIPS community.
