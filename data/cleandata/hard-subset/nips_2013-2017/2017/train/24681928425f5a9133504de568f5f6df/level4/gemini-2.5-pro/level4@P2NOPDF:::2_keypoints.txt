- Paper studies single-question asking in a "battleship" domain to maximally reduce uncertainty about hidden colored shapes on a partially observable grid.  
- Main contribution: a computational model using a hand-crafted PCFG as a prior over compositional questions expressed in lambda-calculus logical forms.  
- Question quality scored as a linear combination of three factors: expected information gain (informativeness), question complexity (length or negative log-prob under PCFG), and answer-type preference (e.g., binary vs. categorical).  
- Human data from a prior study were manually translated into the model's logical forms for evaluation.  
- Evaluation used leave-one-out cross-validation and compared the full model to lesioned versions with each factor removed.  
- Results: all three components improve predictive power, with question complexity being the dominant predictor of human behavior.  
- Paper presents qualitatively novel questions generated by the model to illustrate behavior.  
- Strength: novel integration of linguistic theory, cognitive modeling, and active learning to model human question selection.  
- Strength: clear writing, thoughtful model design, and convincing combination of quantitative and qualitative evaluation.  
- Cognitive-science contribution: explains why informativeness alone poorly predicts human question-asking by highlighting the stronger role of complexity.  
- Limitation: scalability and generalizability concerns—manual translation to logical forms and computational demands may not extend to high-dimensional, real-world domains.  
- Suggestion: provide a clearer roadmap and concrete large-scale application scenarios (e.g., interactive debugging, scientific discovery, complex dialogue systems) to broaden NIPS relevance.  
- Minor suggestion: perform nested model comparisons using lesioned-model log-likelihoods for formal statistical tests of component significance.  
- Minor suggestion: consider QUD (Questions under Discussion) frameworks for future sequential question-asking work; references: Ginzburg (1995) and Rojas-Esponda (2013).
