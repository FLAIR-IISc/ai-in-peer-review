- Paper studies reproducing user questions in Battleship by replacing square queries with an artificial compositional (Montagovian) language.
- Uses a log-linear model relying on four simple question features (e.g., expected information gain/informativeness, answer type—Boolean or numerical, and board-feature relevance).
- Motivation framed in active learning is reasonable.
- Core ideas and feature set are fairly straightforward.
- The task is overly simplistic, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous.
- Reviewer doubts the paper would be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
