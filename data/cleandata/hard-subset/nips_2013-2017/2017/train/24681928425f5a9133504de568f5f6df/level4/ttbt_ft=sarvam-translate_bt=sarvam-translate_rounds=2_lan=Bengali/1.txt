This paper attempts to reproduce the user questions of a battleship game, where instead of asking about individual squares, the questions are primarily asked with logical (Montagovian) semantics through an artificial language. A log-linear model is used on four common characteristics of the questions: expected information gain (informative), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question involves the properties of the board.

The purpose of this paper is to reproduce logical questions in the context of highly active learning, but the concepts in this paper are quite clear and the task is very simple, extremely limited, and logically unambiguous. I do not believe that this paper will be of interest to the NIPS community.
