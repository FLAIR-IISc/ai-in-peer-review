This paper explores the reproduction of user queries in a Battleship game scenario, where individual square inquiries are reformulated as compositional logical (Montagovian) semantic questions. The authors employ a log-linear model, leveraging four straightforward features of the questions: expected information gain, answer type (Boolean or numerical), and board feature involvement.

At a high level, the active learning motivation is sound, yet the ideas presented in this paper are relatively straightforward and lack novelty. The task is also overly simplistic, highly constrained, and devoid of logical ambiguity. Consequently, I question the relevance and appeal of this work to the NIPS community.
