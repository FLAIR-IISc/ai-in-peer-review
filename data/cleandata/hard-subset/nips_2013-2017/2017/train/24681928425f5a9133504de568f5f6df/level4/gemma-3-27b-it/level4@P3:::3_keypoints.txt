- Introduces a computational framework that models human questions as executable programs producing answers.
- Proposes people prefer concise programs that maximize information gain in context.
- Evaluates the model on a dataset of human questions from a “Battleship”-like game.
- Model incorporates informativeness (Expected Information Gain), complexity, answer type, and game-state relevance features.
- Full model using all features outperforms variants missing individual feature sets in experiments.
- Demonstrates the model can predict human question choices and generate novel, plausible questions.
- Builds on active learning, dialog systems, and visual question generation literature while emphasizing goal-directed, context-sensitive questioning.
- Connects conceptually to prior formalizations of question meaning and program-based question answering.
- Technically sound with a clear model specification and rigorous experimental evaluation.
- Uses leave-one-out cross-validation and includes ablation studies to strengthen analysis.
- Authors acknowledge dataset limitations and optimization approximations.
- Novel question generation is a particularly noteworthy result.
- Claims about revealing cognitive mechanisms of question asking are not strongly supported by the mixed results.
- Log-likelihood suggests some EIG influence, but correlation analysis shows similar performance without EIG, creating ambiguity.
- Sparse human data likely adds noise and weakens conclusions about factor balancing.
- Reliance on a predefined grammar and domain-specific knowledge raises concerns about generalizability.
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, and supplemented with helpful materials.
- Related work section misses prior program-based question-asking literature (e.g., Hawkins 2015; Roberts 1996).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel executable-program approach, technical soundness, positive predictive and generative results, potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited cognitive insight, domain specificity, and incomplete related work coverage.
- Overall assessment: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions; recommended score 7/10.
