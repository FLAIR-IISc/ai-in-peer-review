- Presents a cognitive model where question log-probability is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and relevance.
- Models questions as programs evaluated on belief states and applies the model to the Battleship grid domain.
- Full model (all features) outperforms lesioned versions on held-out human data by log-likelihood.
- In correlation with human judgments, the model without EIG performs about as well as the full model.
- Evidence for EIG’s role is ambiguous: log-likelihood advantage may be due to noise (bootstrap shows 19% chance of being wrong) and correlation results are noisy from sparse data.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and presents results clearly.
- Contribution to cognitive science is limited because results don’t strongly reveal how people balance informativeness and complexity.
- Missing prior work citations/relations: Hawkins (2015) on "goal"-based question models and Roberts (1996) on question-under-discussion (QUD).
- The approach is promising for generating compositional questions in non-trivial domains and useful for cognitive/AI models.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound model, clear writing, potential applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited cognitive contribution, missing prior work, noisy/sparse human data limiting conclusions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address missing prior work and clarify limitations.
