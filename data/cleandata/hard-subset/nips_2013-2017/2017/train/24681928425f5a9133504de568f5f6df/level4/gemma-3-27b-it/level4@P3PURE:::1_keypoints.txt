- Paper studies reproducing user questions in a simplified Battleship game using an artificial language with compositional Montagovian semantics.
- Replaces queries about individual board squares with questions expressed in that artificial language.
- Uses a log-linear model trained on four features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean vs numerical), and whether question concerns board features.
- Motivation ties the work to active learning at a high level.
- Reviewer finds the core ideas largely obvious.
- Reviewer criticizes the task as very simple, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous.
- Reviewer concludes the paper is unlikely to interest the NIPS community.
