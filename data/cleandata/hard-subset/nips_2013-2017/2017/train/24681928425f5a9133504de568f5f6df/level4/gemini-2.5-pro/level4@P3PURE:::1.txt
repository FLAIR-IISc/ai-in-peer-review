This paper models user question generation in a modified Battleship game where, instead of querying individual squares, players form questions using an artificial language with compositional logical (Montagovian) semantics. To predict which questions users will ask, the authors employ a log-linear model based on four simple features: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean or numerical), and whether the question involves features of the board.

While the high-level motivation from active learning is reasonable, the paper's contributions are limited by the simplicity of the experimental setup. The task is highly constrained, logically unambiguous, and artificial. In such a simplified setting, the proposed solution—using a log-linear model with a feature for expected information gain—is a rather direct and unsurprising approach. Given the limited technical novelty and the highly constrained nature of the problem, I do not believe this paper will be of broad interest to the NIPS community.
