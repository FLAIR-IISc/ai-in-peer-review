- Introduces a cognitive model that predicts which questions are asked using a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity, answer type, and a relevance term.  
- Represents questions as programs that, when executed on a world state, output an answer.  
- Evaluated in the Battleship domain (inferring ship locations on a grid).  
- Full model including all features outperformed ablated variants on held-out human data by log-likelihood.  
- The model omitting EIG performed about as well as the full model in correlation with human judgments.  
- Paper is generally technically sound, clearly written, and likely reproducible.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by strong cognitive claims about how people balance informativeness and complexity due to weak/noisy evidence (e.g., bootstrap error ~19% and sparse data).  
- Relevant prior work is missing, notably Hawkins (2015) and Roberts (1996) QUD framework.  
- Primary contribution is more AI-focused: demonstrating compositional question generation for a non-trivial domain, likely useful for future systems.
