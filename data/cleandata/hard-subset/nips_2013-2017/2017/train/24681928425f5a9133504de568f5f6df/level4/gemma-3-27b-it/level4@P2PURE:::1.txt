This paper investigates reproducing user questions in a Battleship game, reformulating the standard query of individual squares as questions expressed through an artificial language with compositional, Montagovian semantics. The authors employ a log-linear model utilizing four basic features of these questions: expected information gain (informativeness), answer type (Boolean versus numerical), and whether the question relates to the game board itself. 

While the motivation, framed within active learning, is sensible at a high level, the presented ideas are largely straightforward and the task itself is quite simple, heavily constrained, and logically unambiguous. Consequently, I do not believe this work will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
