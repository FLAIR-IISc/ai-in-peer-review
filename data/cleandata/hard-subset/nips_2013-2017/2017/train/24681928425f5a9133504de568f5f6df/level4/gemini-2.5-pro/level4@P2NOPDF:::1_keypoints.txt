- Paper models user question choice in a modified Battleship game using an artificial compositional language.
- Uses a log-linear model with simple features including expected information gain, answer type (Boolean vs numerical), and whether the question concerns board configuration.
- Frames human question-asking as active learning; this high-level motivation is sound and valuable.
- Features and modeling choices are straightforward and standard for this problem.
- Experimental task is a toy, fully specified, highly constrained, and logically unambiguous environment.
- The artificial setting removes real-world complexity and natural language ambiguity, weakening the challenge.
- Results lack novelty and are unlikely to generalize to more complex, real-world scenarios.
- Due to limited technical novelty and constrained experiments, the work is unlikely to interest the broader NIPS community.
