- Paper models user questions in Battleship using an artificial compositional (Montagovian) language and a log-linear model.
- Model uses four features: expected information gain, answer type (Boolean vs numerical), and board-related content indicators.
- Motivation via active learning is sensible.
- Methods feel largely intuitive and lack substantial novelty.
- Battleship task is highly constrained, simple, and logically unambiguous.
- Artificial language and formal semantics do not seem to necessitate the proposed modeling complexity.
- Findings have limited generalizability to more complex, real-world active learning problems.
- Experiments do not convincingly outperform simpler baselines or existing active learning strategies.
- Related work is insufficiently discussed and the contribution is not well positioned in the active learning literature.
- Overall scope and novelty are limited, making the work unlikely to interest the NeurIPS community.
