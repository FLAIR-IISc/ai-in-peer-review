- Proposes a cognitive model where unnormalized log-probability of a question is a weighted sum of informativeness (EIG), complexity (length), answer type, and relevance (belief-state vs world-independent).  
- Implements the model in the Battleship domain to predict which questions humans ask to locate ships on a grid.  
- Full model outperforms lesioned versions on held-out human data by log-likelihood, but models without EIG correlate with human judgments about as well as the full model.  
- Correlation results are noisy due to sparse data; bootstrap estimates give a 19% chance the log-likelihood result is wrong, so evidence for EIG’s role is uncertain.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible.  
- Claims about revealing how people balance informativeness and complexity are not strongly supported by the experimental results.  
- Missing related prior work: Hawkins 2015 (similar goal-based question model) and Roberts 1996 (QUD framework).  
- Main contribution is demonstrating generation of interesting compositional questions for a non-trivial domain, making the approach useful for AI and cognitive modeling.
