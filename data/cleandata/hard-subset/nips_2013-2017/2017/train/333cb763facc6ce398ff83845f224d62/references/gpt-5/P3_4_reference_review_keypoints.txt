- Proposal: Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs via low-rank spectral factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with per-channel complex Gaussian components.  
- Key benefit: Produces closed-form, real-valued stationary cross-covariances and generalizes scalar SM to multi-output settings.  
- Novelty: Strictly generalizes SM-LMC and CSM by allowing per-channel frequency means/variances and explicit phase and delay differences.  
- Theoretical foundation: Builds on Cramér/Bochner spectral theory and ensures PSD at each frequency via factorization.  
- Interpretability: Parameters map to intuitive spectral quantities (magnitudes, frequencies, bandwidths, phases, delays).  
- Conceptual clarity: Elegant, principled construction that guarantees PSD through Hermitian factorization (zH S z = ||R z||^2).  
- Modeling power: Explicit modeling of delay and out-of-phase relations that LMC/CSM cannot capture.  
- Practical form: Inverse Fourier transform yields tractable time-domain kernels combining SE and cosine with shifts.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic experiments show learning of derivative/delay relations; real data (climate, Jura) competitive or best on some tasks.  
- Real-valuedness: Needs explicit proof that Sij(−ω)=Sij(ω)̄ is ensured (not just evenness); suggest mirrored complex-conjugate pairs like scalar SM.  
- PD for Q>1: Must clarify that pairwise parameters are induced from per-channel parameters per component; otherwise PD is not guaranteed.  
- Constants/typos: Expressions for Σij and magnitude factors appear inconsistent (factors of 2, 1/4); derivations and constants should be double-checked and documented.  
- Identifiability: Global phase/shift and component label-switching induce non-identifiability; discuss initialization, priors, or constraints.  
- Scalability: Parameter count grows O(m Q (3n+2)), training is cubic; propose sparse/variational or inter-domain inducing methods.  
- Empirical rigor: Report initialization strategy (periodogram vs random), use appropriate paired statistical tests, and clarify KS-test usage.  
- Baselines and ablations: Recommend comparing to multivariate Matérn cross-covariances and ablations (no-delay/no-phase/tied spectra).  
- Parameter recovery: On synthetic data, report recovered θ, φ, μ, Σ against ground truth to validate interpretability.  
- Clarity issues: Notational inconsistencies (missing 1/2 factors, sign/positivity of αij) and imprecise language (PD vs cross-covariance) need tightening.  
- Quality assessment: Technically solid with a clean PSD proof, but some derivations/constants and PD statements require correction.  
- Clarity assessment: Well-written overall but needs fixes for minor typos and more implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Originality assessment: Meaningful, nontrivial extension of SM to multi-output with novel low-rank spectral factorization relative to CSM/SM-LMC.  
- Significance assessment: Potentially impactful for multi-task time-series/spatio-temporal modeling where phase/delay matter, though real-data gains are moderate.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled, interpretable, general construction with new capability to model phase and delay and closed-form kernels.  
- Cons for acceptance: Mathematical inconsistencies, lack of explicit PD statement for Q>1, limited scalability, and modest empirical rigor.  
- Recommendations: Provide full derivations (eqs. 8–11), state real-valuedness precisely, parameterize per-channel per-component in Def. 5, add ablations/parameter recovery, and present a scalable sparse MOSM with complexity/runtime.  
- Overall verdict: Solid, novel contribution recommended for acceptance pending mathematical clarifications and stronger empirical/scalability work.
