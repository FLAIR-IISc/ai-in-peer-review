- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture GPs to multi-output via complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem.  
- MOSM models delays and variable auto-covariances, offering a clear spectral interpretation of parameters.  
- Theoretical grounding is strong, leveraging Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems to link spectral densities and covariances.  
- Empirical validation on one synthetic and two real-world datasets shows competitive performance, illustrating delayed signals and derivatives.  
- Writing is generally clear and mathematical motivations are well explained.  
- Novelty: MOSM is a mathematically sound and original generalization of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs.  
- Interpretability: spectral parameters provide insight and guidance for modeling relationships.  
- Limited empirical advantage: performance gains over existing methods (especially CSM) are modest and not consistently significant.  
- Increased complexity: MOSM introduces many hyperparameters, and hyperparameter optimization challenges are not addressed.  
- Incomplete connection to CSM: constraints under which CSM is a special case are not thoroughly explored experimentally.  
- Scalability concerns: no sparse implementation or scalability results are presented, limiting practical applicability to large datasets.  
- Focused on stationary processes with no discussion on extending to non-stationary settings.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations and reasonable experiments, but stronger empirical evidence would help.  
- Clarity: generally clear but notation can be dense; more intuitive explanations of spectral densities would help.  
- Significance: offers a flexible, interpretable framework but limited current impact due to modest empirical gains and lack of scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, interpretable, mathematically sound, and competitive empirically.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical improvements, higher complexity, and missing scalable implementation and deeper CSM comparison.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept contingent on addressing scalability and providing more thorough comparisons with CSM in future work.
