- Proposes MOSM: extends the Spectral Mixture kernel to multi-output GPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase shifts.  
- Builds a rigorous theoretical foundation using Cramér's theorem and enforces positive definiteness via rank-1 factorization of the spectral density matrix.  
- MOSM generalizes CSM and can capture both delays and phase differences that previous approaches could not.  
- Demonstrates capability on synthetic tasks (derivatives, delayed signals) and two real datasets (climate signals, heavy metal concentrations).  
- Shows competitive or superior predictive performance, notably strong results for Cadmium prediction.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and clearly organized with effective figures and derivations.  
- Experimental design is strong and authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., similar performance to CSM on highly correlated climate data).  
- Computational cost/timing and scalability are not thoroughly analyzed, leaving concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Comparison to more recent MOGP methods (e.g., deep GPs or advanced kernels) is limited and could be expanded.  
- Sensitivity to the number of spectral components Q is not explored despite its importance to SM models.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and significant contribution to MOGPs with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS) — minor issues do not outweigh the paper’s contributions.
