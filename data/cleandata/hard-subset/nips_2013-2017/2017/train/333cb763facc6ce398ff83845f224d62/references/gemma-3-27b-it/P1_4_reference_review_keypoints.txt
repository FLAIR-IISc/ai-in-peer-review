- Summary: Proposes MOSM, a multivariate extension of the spectral mixture kernel using Cramér’s theorem and complex cross-spectral densities to model delays and varying auto-/cross-covariances; evaluated on synthetic and two real datasets against LMC, CONV, and CSM.

- Relation to previous work: Builds on scalar SM and CSM, leverages Bochner/Cramér, and aims to overcome interpretability and phase-shift limitations of prior MOGP methods.

- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Uses Cramér’s theorem to guarantee positive definiteness of the MOSM kernel.

- Strength — Interpretability: Parameters have clear spectral meanings (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay), aiding understanding of inter-output dependencies.

- Strength — Flexibility: Allows independent parameterization of auto- and cross-covariances and explicit delay components.

- Strength — Synthetic validation: Demonstrates ability to learn derivatives and delayed signals in controlled experiments.

- Strength — Competitive performance: Shows competitive or superior results on real data (notably the Jura dataset).

- Weakness — Complexity: Large number of parameters raises optimization and computational-cost concerns not thoroughly discussed.

- Weakness — Limited real-world advantage: Performance gains on some datasets (e.g., climate) are modest and not statistically significant.

- Weakness — Connection to CSM: Claim that CSM is a special case of MOSM lacks an explicit derivation or thorough comparison.

- Weakness — Scalability: Insufficient discussion of computational scalability to large datasets or high-dimensional outputs despite TF/GPflow implementation.

- Weakness — Rank (Q) treatment: Choice of Q=1 is simplistic; impact of larger Q on expressiveness and cost is unexplored.

- Overall assessment: Well-motivated, technically sound, and interpretable contribution with competitive results but limited demonstrated practical advantage and missing scalability/analysis details.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory, novel flexible kernel with clear interpretation, successful synthetic demo, competitive real-data performance, addresses positive definiteness and flexible cross-covariances.

- Arguments against acceptance: Modest empirical gains in some cases, inadequate complexity/scalability analysis, limited exploration of Q, and incomplete CSM comparison.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: add detailed computational-complexity analysis (including dependence on outputs and Q); provide explicit derivation showing CSM as a MOSM special case; explore impact of increasing Q on performance and cost; discuss strategies for scaling to larger datasets and higher-dimensional outputs.
