## Peer Review: Spectral Mixture Kernels for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel extension of the Spectral Mixture (SM) kernel to the multi-output Gaussian Process (MOGP) setting, termed the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel. The core idea – leveraging Cramér's theorem to construct a positive-definite kernel from complex-valued cross-spectral densities – is sound and potentially impactful. The paper is generally well-written, and the mathematical exposition is clear. While the experimental validation is promising, the results aren't overwhelmingly conclusive, and some comparisons seem limited.  I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, contingent on addressing the points raised below.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the challenging problem of designing covariance functions for MOGPs, balancing expressiveness with positive definiteness.  Existing approaches, like the Linear Model of Coregionalization (LMC) and Convolution models, often lack interpretability or impose restrictions on auto-covariances. The Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel improved upon these, but introduced an ad-hoc phase shift parameter. This work builds on the SM kernel framework, extending it to multiple outputs by defining complex-valued cross-spectral densities and using Cramér's theorem to guarantee positive definiteness. This avoids the manual parameter tuning of CSM and offers a more principled approach. The paper correctly positions its contribution relative to previous MOGP methods, highlighting both similarities and differences. The connection to Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems is clearly explained and motivates the proposed approach.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The MOSM kernel represents a genuine advancement in MOGP kernel design, offering a flexible and theoretically grounded approach.
* **Theoretical Foundation:**  The use of Cramér’s theorem is elegant and provides a strong justification for the proposed kernel’s positive definiteness.
* **Interpretability:**  The parameters of the MOSM kernel have clear spectral interpretations (frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay), enhancing understanding and potentially facilitating model selection.
* **Generality:** The MOSM framework encompasses existing approaches like CSM as special cases, demonstrating its broader applicability.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured. The mathematical derivations are presented clearly.
* **Synthetic Experiment:** The synthetic example successfully validates the ability of the MOSM kernel to capture known relationships (derivative and delay).

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Experimental Validation – Limited Impact:** While the experiments demonstrate competence, they don't showcase a substantial performance advantage over existing methods, especially on the real-world datasets. The MAE improvements are often marginal, and statistical significance is not consistently established.
* **Computational Complexity:** The paper doesn’t explicitly discuss the computational cost of the MOSM kernel, particularly regarding training and prediction. This is a crucial factor for practical applications.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The increased number of parameters in the MOSM kernel (compared to simpler models) raises concerns about sensitivity to initialization and optimization challenges.  This is not adequately addressed.
* **Comparison Scope:** The comparison to existing methods feels somewhat limited.  For instance, exploring the influence of the number of latent components (Q) in the MOSM and comparing its computational cost would be beneficial.
* **Complex-valued GPs:** The discussion briefly mentions complex-valued GPs. Expanding on the implications of this choice and potential benefits/drawbacks would be valuable.

**4. Detailed Comments & Questions:**

* **Section 3.1:**  The choice of Q=1 seems somewhat arbitrary. A discussion on the trade-off between rank (Q) and model complexity/expressiveness would be helpful.
* **Eq. (9):** The explanation of how `wij` ensures positive definiteness is slightly hand-wavy. A more rigorous justification would strengthen the argument.
* **Section 4.2 (Climate Data):**  The observation that the climate signals are very similar is crucial. This explanation significantly diminishes the impact of the results. A more challenging dataset, with stronger dependencies and less correlation between channels, would provide a more compelling evaluation.
* **Section 4.3 (Heavy Metal Data):** The results on the Cadmium data are encouraging, but the lack of statistical significance on the Copper data weakens the overall claim of superiority.
* **Future Work:** The suggestion of inducing variables for sparse implementation is excellent, but further elaboration on how spectral information would be incorporated into this process would be insightful.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The MOSM kernel offers a novel and theoretically sound approach to MOGP kernel design.
* The paper demonstrates a clear understanding of the relevant literature and positions its contribution effectively.
* The synthetic experiment provides strong validation of the kernel's ability to capture specific relationships.
* The clear parameter interpretation adds value and facilitates model understanding.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental results, while promising, lack a significant performance advantage over existing methods.
* The computational complexity and parameter sensitivity of the MOSM kernel are not adequately addressed.
* The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited in scope.



**6. Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. Specifically, the authors should:

*   **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include a more challenging dataset, explore the impact of the rank parameter (Q), and report statistical significance of the results.
*   **Address computational complexity:** Discuss the computational cost of the MOSM kernel and potential strategies for optimization.
*   **Investigate parameter sensitivity:** Analyze the model's sensitivity to different initialization strategies and hyperparameter optimization techniques.
*   **Elaborate on complex-valued GPs:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the implications of using a complex-valued kernel.




I believe that addressing these points will significantly strengthen the paper and solidify its contribution to the field of Gaussian process research.
