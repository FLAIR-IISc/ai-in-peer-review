- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM to multi-output GPs using complex-valued spectral densities.  
- Leverages Cramér's theorem to construct spectrally valid multi-output kernels that model delays, phase differences, and varying auto-covariances.  
- MOSM generalizes Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM); CSM is a special case under restricted equal-parameter settings.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and clearly addresses positive definiteness requirements.  
- Experiments include a synthetic example (showing learning of derivatives and delays) and two real-world datasets (climate and heavy metals) comparing SM-LMC, CONV, and CSM.  
- Synthetic results demonstrate clear advantages of MOSM where latent-process models fail.  
- Real-world gains are modest: MOSM similar to CSM on climate data (significant for one sensor) and only outperforms others for Cadmium on the heavy metal dataset.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear step-by-step mathematical derivations and effective figures.  
- Novel contribution: first extension of spectral mixture ideas to multi-output GPs with complex spectra enabling delays and phase modeling.  
- Significance is meaningful for temporal/sensor applications but somewhat limited by modest empirical improvements.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, interpretable spectral parameters, comprehensive validation, and thorough comparison to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability, modest real-world performance gains, and need for a clearer main-text explanation of the MOSM–CSM relationship.  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality technical contribution with minor suggested improvements on complexity discussion and clearer explanations of MOSM advantages over CSM.
