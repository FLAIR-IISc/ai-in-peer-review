- Proposes MOSM: a multi-output spectral mixture kernel built by factorizing a complex cross-spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) and using Cramér’s theorem to get closed-form cross-covariances.
- MOSM models channel-specific amplitude, mean frequency, bandwidth, delay, and phase via complex Gaussian spectral components, generalizing single-output SM.
- MOSM subsumes prior multi-output spectral approaches: recovers SM-LMC when channels share parameters and CSM when delays are tied but phases allowed.
- The spectral factorization guarantees S(ω) is PSD for every ω and symmetrization ensures real-valued processes.
- Key analytic form: cross-covariances equal exp(−½(τ+θij)TΣij(τ+θij)) cos((τ+θij)Tµij+φij) after inverse Fourier transform.
- Theory is well-motivated and leverages standard harmonic analysis, but several technical/notation issues need correction.
- Conjugation inconsistency: some equations omit the conjugate in Sij(ω)=conj(Ri(ω))Rj(ω); derived phase relations are consistent but notation must be fixed.
- Symmetrization justification: need brief proof or reference that averaging S(ω) and S(−ω) preserves PSD and yields real kernels when S(−ω)=S(ω)H.
- Parameter interpretation issue: αij is nonnegative by construction, so sign changes arise from the cosine term—not αij as stated in the text.
- Derivation details: explicitly give the Gaussian product identity or an appendix to reproduce Σij and µij computations.
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM captures derivative and delayed relations better than LMC/CONV; real-data results are competitive with statistically significant wins in a few cases.
- Initialization is crucial for SM-type models but the paper omits how channel-specific spectral parameters are initialized, harming reproducibility.
- Scalability is unaddressed: experiments use exact O(N^3) inference on small N; complexity analysis and runtimes are missing.
- Baselines are limited; should include standard multi-task Matérn/ICM and separate SM-LMC from CSM, plus report parameter counts.
- Notation and clarity issues: missing conjugations, dangling footnote marker, incorrect citation for [17], and minor dimensional typos (S(ω) is Hermitian complex-valued).
- Clarify whether Eq. (8) uses the 1/2 factor in the exponent and explicitly state the realness condition S(−ω)=S(ω)H.
- Originality: natural but nontrivial extension of SM to multi-output via complex spectral factorization, notable for explicit delays and per-channel O(m) parameterization.
- Significance: offers interpretability, linear per-output parameter scaling, and simultaneous modeling of phase and delay; empiric gains are moderate on real data but strong on targeted synthetic tasks.
- Pros: principled PSD construction, clear physical parameter interpretations, per-channel scaling, closed-form time-domain kernels, and competitive empirical performance.
- Cons/questions: notation/citation errors, missing initialization and scalability discussion, limited baselines, modest real-data gains, and potential identifiability issues between φ and θ when Σij is broad.
- Suggested improvements: fix conjugation and αij statements, correct citation/footnote, provide initialization procedure (e.g., periodogram+GMM, cross-periodograms), add complexity/runtime and sparse/inducing discussion, include ICM/Matérn baselines and parameter counts, and comment on identifiability or regularization.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and useful extension of spectral mixture ideas to multi-output with added flexibility; lean toward acceptance pending addressed issues.
