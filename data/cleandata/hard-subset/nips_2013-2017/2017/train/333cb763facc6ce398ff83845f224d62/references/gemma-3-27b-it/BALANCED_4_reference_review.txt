This paper introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a novel approach to constructing covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs). The core idea is to extend the spectral mixture concept from scalar GPs to the multivariate setting, leveraging Cramér’s theorem to ensure positive definiteness. The authors provide a clear theoretical foundation, a detailed explanation of the kernel’s parameters and properties, and experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The work builds upon existing MOGP methods like LMC, CONV, and CSM, addressing limitations in interpretability and flexibility.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed MOSM kernel provides a new way to construct MOGP covariance functions, offering increased flexibility compared to existing methods like LMC and CSM, particularly in modeling delays and phase differences between outputs.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper is well-grounded in mathematical theory, correctly applying Bochner's and Cramér's theorems to justify the construction of the kernel. The derivation is clear and easy to follow.
* **Interpretability:**  The spectral interpretation of the kernel parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) is a significant strength, providing insights into the learned relationships between outputs.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experiments are well-designed, including a synthetic example to validate the model’s ability to learn specific covariance structures and comparisons with established methods on real-world datasets.  The use of multiple datasets (climate and metal concentration) is appreciated.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the complex concepts accessible. The inclusion of equations and figures aids understanding.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While not explicitly discussed, the computational cost of the MOSM kernel (especially with a large number of spectral components 'Q') could be a concern for large datasets. The paper doesn’t address scalability or provide any insights into efficient implementation.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The MOSM kernel introduces several parameters (wi, µi, Σi, θi, φi), increasing the complexity of hyperparameter optimization.  The paper lacks discussion of practical strategies for efficiently tuning these parameters, beyond mentioning the use of TensorFlow and GPflow.
* **Limited Superiority on Real-World Datasets:** While competitive, the MOSM kernel doesn't consistently outperform other methods across all datasets and scenarios. The statistical significance tests indicate that improvements are only consistently observed for the Cadmium prediction task. More robust and consistent gains would strengthen the contribution.
* **Connection to Existing Work:** While the relationship to CSM is discussed, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with recent advances in MOGP kernels, particularly those leveraging deep learning (e.g., neural MOGPs).
* **Missing Implementation Details:**  More details about the experimental setup (e.g., specific hyperparameter settings used for each model, optimization algorithms, stopping criteria) would improve reproducibility.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and theoretically sound kernel for MOGPs, addressing limitations of existing approaches.
* The spectral interpretation of the kernel parameters is a significant contribution, providing insights into model behavior.
* The experimental validation demonstrates the model’s effectiveness, particularly in scenarios requiring modeling of delays and phase differences.
* The work is well-written, clear, and accessible.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The computational complexity of the proposed kernel is not addressed.
* The improvements over existing methods on real-world datasets are not consistently significant.
* The hyperparameter tuning complexity is not discussed.
* The paper doesn't thoroughly explore connections to more recent developments in the field.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of MOGPs. The MOSM kernel offers a flexible and interpretable approach to modeling complex relationships between multiple outputs. However, the lack of discussion regarding computational complexity, hyperparameter tuning, and more substantial performance gains on real-world datasets raises some concerns.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly focusing on:

1.  **Computational Complexity:** Investigate methods to improve the scalability of the MOSM kernel, possibly through sparse approximations or other techniques.
2.  **Hyperparameter Tuning:**  Provide practical guidelines and recommendations for efficient hyperparameter optimization.
3.  **More Comprehensive Experiments:**  Conduct experiments on a wider range of datasets and scenarios, including comparisons to more recent MOGP methods. Perform more rigorous statistical testing to demonstrate the significance of the improvements.
4.  **Implementation Details:**  Provide more detailed information about the experimental setup to ensure reproducibility.



Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential impact.
