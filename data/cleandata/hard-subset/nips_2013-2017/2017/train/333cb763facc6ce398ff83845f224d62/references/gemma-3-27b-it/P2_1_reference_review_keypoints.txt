- Proposal: Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM kernels to multi-output GPs via complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem to model delays and phase differences.  
- Novelty: Significant extension of SM kernels to multi-output settings, moving beyond LMC and ad-hoc CSM adjustments.  
- Theoretical soundness: Grounded in Cramér’s theorem with clear links to Bochner’s theorem, ensuring positive definiteness.  
- Interpretability: Parameters have spectral meaning (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) enabling insightful modeling of output dependencies.  
- Completeness: Paper covers theory, method, experiments, and related work in a comprehensive manner.  
- Experimental validation: Synthetic experiments recover known derivatives/delays; real-world tests show competitive performance and a significant improvement on one dataset.  
- Complexity: MOSM introduces many parameters, raising concerns about difficult hyperparameter tuning, especially with limited data.  
- Limited real-world gains: Performance improvements over simpler methods (e.g., CSM) are not consistently substantial, notably on the climate dataset.  
- Relation to CSM: The paper’s discussion of how MOSM generalizes CSM and the constraints to recover CSM is limited and not fully explored.  
- Scalability: No discussion of computational cost or mitigation strategies (e.g., sparse approximations) for matrix inversions and large datasets.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with correct derivations and clear results, but lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written, though some derivations (spectral densities) need more detail.  
- Originality assessment: Novel approach with appropriate positioning against related work.  
- Significance assessment: Valuable for problems needing phase/delay modeling, though practical benefits may be application-dependent.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically justified, interpretable, and empirically competitive with ability to recover known relationships.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Added complexity, modest real-world gains, limited scalability discussion, and unclear practical necessity of extra flexibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address scalability, hyperparameter tuning, stronger exposition on CSM relation, and justify when MOSM’s complexity is warranted.
