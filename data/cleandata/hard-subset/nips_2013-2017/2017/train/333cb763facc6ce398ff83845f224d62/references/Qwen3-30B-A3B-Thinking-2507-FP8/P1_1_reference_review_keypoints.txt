- Summary: Proposes MOSM kernel extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using complex-valued spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to model delays, phase shifts, and cross-covariances.

- Quality: Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous derivation; experiments (synthetic + two real datasets) validate capabilities with statistical tests.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with consistent notation, clear definitions, illustrative figures, and reproducible implementation details.

- Originality: Novel application of complex-valued spectral densities to MOGPs that generalizes CSM and naturally incorporates delays and phase differences.

- Significance: Addresses important limitation in modeling flexible, interpretable cross-covariances; shows clear advantages on synthetic data and significant gains on one real dataset.

- Strength 1: Proper and rigorous use of Cramér's theorem.

- Strength 2: Interpretable spectral parameters (delays, phases, spectra).

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experiments demonstrating learning of derivatives and delays.

- Strength 4: Competitive real-world performance with statistical significance in cases.

- Strength 5: Accessible presentation balancing theory and practice.

- Weakness 1: Limited discussion of computational complexity versus baselines, especially for many channels.

- Weakness 2: Marginal performance gains on climate dataset lack deeper analysis.

- Weakness 3: No experiments or analysis of scalability with increasing channel count.

- Weakness 4: Does not address handling of potentially asymmetric cross-covariances in practice.

- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical/methodological contribution with thorough validation, minor additions on complexity and scalability suggested.
