- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM kernels to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Uses Cramér's Theorem to design complex-valued spectral densities that ensure positive-definite MOGP covariance functions.  
- Models cross-spectral densities as complex-valued squared-exponential functions to represent delays and phase differences explicitly.  
- Generalizes prior approaches (e.g., CSM) by parameterizing delays and phases rather than using manual parameter shifts.  
- Demonstrates capability on a synthetic example (derivatives and delayed signals) and two real-world datasets (climate and heavy metal concentrations).  
- Technically sound and mathematically rigorous, correctly addressing positive-definiteness for MOGPs.  
- Properly positions the work relative to existing methods (CSM, SM-LMC, CONV) with clear theoretical explanations.  
- Experimental validation is thorough, with appropriate implementation (TensorFlow/GPflow) and metrics (MAE); synthetic example convincingly shows unique capabilities.  
- Shows competitive real-world performance and statistically significant improvement on the heavy metal dataset.  
- Exceptional clarity and organization, with strong background, step-by-step derivations, and helpful figures (e.g., spectral densities vs. kernels).  
- Provides clear interpretation of MOSM parameters mapping to frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Novel use of complex-valued spectral densities for multi-output GPs and principled delay modeling in this context.  
- Significant for applications with temporally or spatially offset signals (sensor networks, geospatial, biological signals).  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity, scalability, and training time/memory comparisons with other methods.  
- Does not explore trade-offs between model complexity (number of spectral components) and performance.  
- Incomplete statistical analysis for the climate data (only one sensor tested) and limited comparison with recent multi-output GP methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity and emphasize practical significance; overall score 7 (Strong Accept).
