- Overall: Novel extension of the SM kernel to MOGPs (MOSM) using Cramér’s theorem; sound theory and lean toward accept with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Construct multivariate kernel from complex-valued cross-spectral densities via Cramér’s theorem.  
- Theoretical soundness: Guarantees positive definiteness; clear derivation from spectral density.  
- Interpretability: Parameters correspond to frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay, aiding model understanding.  
- Generality: MOSM subsumes CSM and provides greater flexibility for cross-covariances, including delays and phase differences.  
- Presentation: Well-written, logically organized, consistent mathematical notation.  
- Synthetic experiment: Successfully recovers known auto- and cross-covariances, including delayed signals and derivatives.  
- Empirical gains limited: Competitive but not consistently better than strong baselines (CONV, CSM, SM-LMC).  
- Dataset choice: Climate and Jura datasets may be too correlated to showcase MOSM’s advantages.  
- Computational cost omitted: No discussion of MOSM’s computational complexity relative to other MOGP methods.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Large number of hyperparameters (especially with larger Q) may complicate optimization; guidance lacking.  
- Qualitative ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, clear spectral interpretation, validated on synthetic data, subsumes CSM.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited real-world improvement, dataset suitability concerns, computational and hyperparameter issues.  
- Recommendations: Discuss computational complexity and sparse approximations, test on less-correlated/complex datasets, provide hyperparameter tuning guidance.  
- Additional recommendations: Clarify trade-off with spectral rank Q and clarify the role of 'wij' in equation (9).
