- Introduces MOSM kernel for MOGPs using Cramér’s theorem and a low-rank complex spectral factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).
- Models per-output Ri(ω) as complex Gaussian bumps with linear phase and enforces evenness in ω to obtain real-valued processes.
- Inverse Fourier transform yields closed-form stationary cross-covariances that include output-pair-specific amplitudes, means/covariances, phase shifts, and explicit delays.
- Positive definiteness is guaranteed by the spectral factorization construction.
- MOSM is claimed to strictly generalize SM-LMC and CSM and provides interpretable spectral parameters per output pair (frequency, bandwidth, magnitude, phase, delay).
- Synthetic experiments (3-output) show MOSM best recovers known auto- and cross-covariances and extrapolates derivative and delay structure.
- Real-data results: climate sensor task shows MOSM comparable or slightly better than baselines; Jura dataset shows best performance for Cadmium and competitive for Copper.
- Implementations use GPflow/TensorFlow and report MAE as the evaluation metric.
- Technical soundness: spectral construction and closed-form inverse FT are principled; PD guaranteed by factorization.
- A short proof/lemma is needed to confirm S(ω) remains Hermitian PSD after symmetrization, especially with complex phases.
- Claims vs. evidence: synthetic results compelling; real-data gains are moderate and not uniformly superior.
- Missing empirical assessments: scalability, sensitivity to initialization, robustness, and runtime are not evaluated.
- Practical details lacking: initialization heuristics, choice of Q, computational complexity, and training runtime.
- Clarity: paper is well written with helpful spectral interpretations but has minor typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., Σ vs Σ−1).
- Should explicitly state and show Hermitian property Sij(ω)=Sji(ω) follows from Ri(ω) and symmetrization.
- Originality: novel and elegant use of complex cross-spectral densities with per-output spectral parameters and explicit delays, extending SM to MOGPs.
- Significance: flexible, interpretable MOGP family capable of modeling phase and delay; promising but needs scalability/initialization work for broader adoption.
- Strength: principled Cramér-based construction, interpretable parameters, closed-form kernels, and solid synthetic validation with competitive empirical performance.
- Weakness: cubic complexity with no sparse/inducing solution, potential sensitivity to initialization, and risk of over-parameterization/identifiability issues.
- Suggestions: add complexity discussion and sparse MOSM sketch, detail initialization and sensitivity analyses, discuss identifiability/regularization, and prove Hermitian PSD after symmetrization.
- Baselines: consider adding multivariate Matérn cross-covariances and advanced process convolution variants; temper claims about LMC’s inability to model derivatives.
- Evaluation improvements: report uncertainty calibration, hyperparameter counts, training time, learned delays/phases, and use paired tests or CIs for climate data.
- Minor fixes: clarify wij dependence, state input dimensionality and show 2D example if possible, and fix typographical/notation errors.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid methodological contribution and interpretability, but needs clearer theory details, broader experiments, and scalability/initialization treatment.
