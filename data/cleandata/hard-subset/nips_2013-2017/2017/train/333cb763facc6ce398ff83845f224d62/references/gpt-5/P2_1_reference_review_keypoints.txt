- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that designs matrix-valued cross-spectral density S(ω) via Cramér’s theorem, using a low-rank factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).

- Per-output complex SE components Ri(ω) include magnitude, mean frequency, bandwidth, phase, and delay, yielding closed-form cross-covariances (eq. 11).

- Construction enforces conjugate symmetry for real-valued processes and guarantees PSD via the factorized PSD, generalizing SM-LMC and CSM.

- Allows different auto-spectral parameters per output and explicitly models inter-output phase φij and delay θij.

- Theoretical derivation is sound; inverse Fourier transform is tractable and parameters are interpretable (αij, µij, Σij, θij, φij).

- Minor clarity issue: αij is nonnegative by construction, so negative cross-covariances arise from the cosine/phase term, not αij being negative.

- Rank-Q mixtures can approximate stationary matrix-valued kernels in principle; diagonal Σi is standard but somewhat restrictive.

- Model introduces many per-output-per-component parameters, raising identifiability, scaling/phase ambiguity, and overfitting risks.

- Optimization uses full marginal likelihood with O(N^3) cost; no sparse/variational scalability is implemented (noted as future work).

- Empirical synthetic experiment convincingly demonstrates MOSM’s ability to capture phase and delay not modeled by LMC/CONV.

- Real-data experiments (climate sensors, Jura) show competitive but modest gains over baselines; statistical testing is partial.

- Evaluation relies on MAE only; uncertainty calibration (NLL/coverage) and more comprehensive metrics are missing.

- Ablation studies (isolating θ and φ, sensitivity to Q/components) and initialization/prior strategies are lacking and would strengthen claims.

- Paper is generally well written with clear mapping from Ri to cross-parameters, but contains minor typos and sparse training/hyperparameter details.

- Implementation uses GPflow but code release, hyperparameter initialization, learning rates, restarts, and constraints are not provided, hurting reproducibility.

- Originality: an incremental but novel extension of SM to multi-output via complex factorized cross-spectra that model delays and phases; related work is adequately cited.

- Significance: offers flexible, interpretable MOGP kernel useful for temporal/spatiotemporal problems, but limited scalability and modest real-data gains temper impact.

- Pros: principled spectral construction, closed-form covariances, generalizes SM-LMC/CSM, and strong synthetic demonstration.

- Cons: large parameter count and identifiability concerns, lack of scalability, modest empirical improvements, limited statistical evaluation, and minor clarity issues.

- Suggestions: discuss identifiability/constraints or priors, provide initialization strategies, add ablations and uncertainty metrics, implement sparse/variational scaling, and release code.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and interpretable extension with demonstrated strengths on designed tasks but needs stronger empirical evidence and scalability work.
