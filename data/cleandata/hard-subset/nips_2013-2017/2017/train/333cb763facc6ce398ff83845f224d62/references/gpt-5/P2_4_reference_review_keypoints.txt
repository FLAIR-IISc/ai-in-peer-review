- Proposes MOSM kernel for MOGPs by factorizing cross-spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)^H R(ω) and parameterizing each column as a complex Gaussian with per-output µ, Σ, w, φ, θ.  
- Ensures positive definiteness via PSD factorization and symmetrization, yielding closed-form stationary cross-covariances that are Gaussian×cosine with channel-pair amplitude, frequency, phase, and delay.  
- Strictly generalizes SM-LMC and CSM by allowing different auto-covariance parameters per output, explicit inter-channel delays, and flexible phase differences.  
- Synthetic experiment (original, derivative, delayed copy) shows MOSM uniquely recovers derivative and delay structure.  
- Real-data experiments (climate sensor imputation, Jura metals) show MOSM is competitive and often best, though gains over CSM are modest.  
- Theoretical soundness: PSD factorization and inverse Fourier derivation are correct; symmetrization argument is plausible but not shown constructively.  
- Identifiability concerns: θij and φij have global-shift invariances and sign of wi can be absorbed into φi, suggesting a reference output and constraints (e.g., wi≥0) are needed.  
- Empirics: convincing synthetic results; real-data statistical testing choices (KS test for MAE) and multiple-comparisons control need clarification.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized with clear derivations and helpful figures, but has minor typos, occasional missing 1/2 terms, and could state rank-Q construction more explicitly.  
- Originality: meaningful and nontrivial generalization of spectral MOGP kernels by jointly modeling delay and phase in closed form.  
- Significance: provides a flexible, interpretable MOGP kernel useful for time series and sensor networks, but limited by O(N^3) scalability and modest real-world advantage.  
- Pros: guaranteed PSD, closed-form interpretable parameters, subsumes SM-LMC/CSM, can model derivative+delay, competitive baselines and some statistical testing.  
- Cons: lacks sparse/structured inference (scalability), parameter redundancy/identifiability issues, modest empirical improvement over CSM, symmetrization not proven explicitly, limited guidance on initialization/prior choice.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss identifiability and fix a reference channel; constrain wi≥0; provide explicit symmetrization construction; report computational cost and convergence; include ablations isolating delay/phase effects; prototype sparse/variational extensions; use paired tests (e.g., Wilcoxon) and multiple-comparison corrections.  
- Overall: quality and clarity good, originality moderate-to-high, significance moderate; recommendation: weak accept.
