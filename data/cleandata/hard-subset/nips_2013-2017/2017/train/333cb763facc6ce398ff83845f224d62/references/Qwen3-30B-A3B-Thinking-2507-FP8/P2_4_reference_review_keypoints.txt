- Introduces MOSM: a multi-output spectral mixture kernel using complex-valued spectral densities to model delays and phase differences between channels.
- Extends SM to multi-output GPs with a rigorous derivation based on Cramér's theorem to ensure positive definiteness.
- Claims MOSM can capture richer cross-channel relationships (delays, derivatives, phase shifts) than CSM and LMC.
- Strong theoretical foundation and careful mathematical treatment of multi-output positive definiteness.
- Experimental validation includes a synthetic example (derivatives/delays) and real-world datasets (climate, heavy metal concentration).
- Synthetic results show MOSM modeling capabilities that other methods fail to capture.
- Competitive real-world performance with statistically significant improvement on the Cadmium dataset.
- Reproducible implementation using GPflow/TensorFlow enhances practical credibility.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation, helpful definitions, and effective figures and tables.
- Novel contribution: use of complex-valued spectral densities generalizes CSM and models delays not handled by prior work.
- Significance for applications involving delayed or phase-shifted multi-channel data (e.g., sensors, climate analysis).
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability for practical MOGP use.
- Weakness: performance gains on the climate dataset were not statistically significant.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to more recent MOGP methods and limited discussion of concrete real-world application scenarios.
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, novel, well-validated, with minor weaknesses addressable at revision.
