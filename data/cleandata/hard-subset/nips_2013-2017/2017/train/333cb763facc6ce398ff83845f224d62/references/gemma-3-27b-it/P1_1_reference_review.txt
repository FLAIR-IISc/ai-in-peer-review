## Peer Review: Spectral Mixture Kernels for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel extension of the Spectral Mixture (SM) kernel to the multi-output Gaussian Process (MOGP) setting, dubbed MOSM. The core idea – leveraging Cramér’s theorem to construct a multivariate kernel from complex-valued cross-spectral densities – is sound and technically well-motivated. While the paper suffers from a lack of substantial empirical gains over strong baselines on the provided datasets, the theoretical contribution and clear spectral interpretation of the resulting kernel make it a worthwhile contribution to the field. I lean towards **accepting** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper addresses the challenging problem of designing covariance functions for MOGPs, specifically focusing on the trade-off between expressiveness and positive definiteness. Existing approaches like LMC and CONV lack interpretability and often impose constraints on auto-covariances. The Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel offers improvements but introduces a manual phase shift parameter. MOSM proposes a principled approach by extending the SM kernel concept to multiple outputs, using Cramér’s theorem to ensure positive definiteness. This allows for flexible modelling of cross-covariances, incorporating delays and variable parameters for auto-covariances, with a clear spectral interpretation.

The authors appropriately situate their work within the context of MOGPs and spectral methods. The connection to Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems is clearly explained. The related work section accurately positions MOSM relative to LMC, CONV, and CSM, highlighting its advantages and establishing that CSM is a special case of MOSM.  The mention of related work on complex-valued GPs ([14, 15]) and spectral estimation [16] is also relevant.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Soundness:** The foundation of MOSM on Cramér's theorem is solid and guarantees positive definiteness. The derivation of the kernel from the spectral density is clearly presented.
* **Interpretability:** The parameters of MOSM have a clear spectral interpretation (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay), which enhances understanding and potentially facilitates informed model design.
* **Generality:** MOSM subsumes the CSM kernel as a special case and provides greater flexibility in modeling cross-covariances, particularly regarding delays and phase differences.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and relatively easy to follow.
* **Synthetic Experiment:** The synthetic experiment successfully demonstrates the ability of MOSM to recover known auto- and cross-covariances, including delayed signals and derivatives. This is a valuable validation of the method.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Gains:** While MOSM performs competitively on the real-world datasets, it doesn’t consistently outperform strong baselines (CONV, CSM, SM-LMC) by a significant margin. The statistical significance analysis highlights this point.
* **Dataset Choice:** The datasets used (climate data and Jura metal concentrations) might be too correlated for MOSM to demonstrate its full potential. The authors acknowledge this for the climate data. Datasets exhibiting more complex and less correlated relationships between outputs would be more revealing.
* **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn’t discuss the computational cost of MOSM compared to other MOGP methods.  Given the increased complexity of the kernel, this is a potential concern.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The large number of hyperparameters in MOSM (especially with increasing Q) could make optimization challenging and require careful tuning. The paper does not provide detailed guidance on hyperparameter selection.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical justification is rigorous, and the synthetic experiment provides good evidence of the model's correctness. However, the limited empirical gains raise some concerns about the practical significance of the improvements.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are clear and the explanation of the spectral interpretation is insightful.
* **Originality (8/10):** The application of Cramér’s theorem to construct a flexible MOGP kernel is novel and represents a significant contribution.  While building on existing spectral methods, MOSM extends them in a meaningful way.
* **Significance (7/10):** While the theoretical contribution is valuable, the practical significance is somewhat limited by the lack of substantial empirical improvements. However, the potential for applications in areas requiring modeling of complex cross-dependencies between outputs (e.g., sensor networks, financial modeling) remains promising.

**5. Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically sound approach to MOGP kernel design.
* Clear spectral interpretation of the kernel parameters.
* Successful validation on a synthetic example.
* Subsumes existing CSM kernel.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited empirical gains on real-world datasets.
* Datasets may not be ideal for showcasing the benefits of MOSM.
* Computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity are not addressed.

**6. Recommendations:**

* **Address Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational complexity of MOSM and compare it to other MOGP methods. Consider exploring sparse approximations to improve scalability.
* **Explore Additional Datasets:** Evaluate MOSM on datasets with more complex and less correlated relationships between outputs to better demonstrate its benefits.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning Guidance:** Provide more guidance on hyperparameter selection and suggest strategies for efficient optimization.
* **Clarify the Role of Q:**  Discuss the trade-off between model complexity and performance as the rank Q of the spectral density matrix is increased.
* **Minor:** In equation (9), clarify the role of 'wij' in relation to the frequency components.



**Overall:** Despite the limited empirical gains, the theoretical contribution and clear interpretation of MOSM warrant acceptance. Addressing the recommendations above would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
