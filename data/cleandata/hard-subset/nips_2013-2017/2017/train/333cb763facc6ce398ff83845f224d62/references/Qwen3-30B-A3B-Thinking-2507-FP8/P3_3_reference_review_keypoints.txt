- Introduces MOSM kernel: extends single-output Spectral Mixture to multi-output GPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem to ensure positive definiteness.
- Key capability: models delays (θ_ij) and phase shifts (φ_ij) independently with clear spectral interpretation.
- Theoretical strength: sound extension with built-in positive definiteness via spectral construction.
- Interpretability strength: all parameters have clear spectral meanings; delay vs phase distinction emphasized.
- Synthetic validation strength: synthetic derivative/delayed signals show MOSM captures relationships LMC-style models cannot.
- Empirical strength: real-world experiments (climate, heavy metals) show competitive performance and significant improvement on Cadmium task.
- Presentation strength: well-written, logically organized, mathematically detailed with effective illustrative figures.
- Weakness: statistical significance testing is limited—Cadmium significance shown only on one sensor (Sotonmet).
- Weakness: CONV baseline taken from prior work rather than re-implemented, weakening fairness of comparison.
- Weakness: paper omits discussion of computational complexity and practical costs of added parameters.
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent MOGP methods beyond referencing Matérn cross-covariances.
- Weakness: novelty possibly overstated since prior work ([17]) also uses complex-valued cross-spectral densities—distinction needs clarification.
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound contribution with compelling synthetic validation and useful practical utility despite experimental limitations.
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS); authors should address computational complexity and broaden experimental comparisons in the final version.
- Pro acceptance points: theoretically sound SM extension, models delays/phase shifts not captured before, strong synthetic evidence, clear writing and foundation.
- Con acceptance points: limited statistical testing, unfair CONV comparison, missing complexity analysis, novelty claims need clearer differentiation from [17].
