- Proposes MOSM kernel extending the Spectral Mixture kernel to multi-output GPs using Cramér's Theorem.  
- Uses complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase differences across channels.  
- Generalizes CSM by introducing delay parameters and allowing channel-specific spectral parameters; CSM is a special case.  
- Provides a clear mathematical derivation ensuring the resulting kernel is positive-definite.  
- Synthetic example demonstrates recovery of derivatives and delayed signals that prior methods (CSM/LMC/CONV) cannot capture.  
- Experimental evaluation includes climate and Jura heavy-metal datasets with appropriate baselines and statistical validation (Kolmogorov–Smirnov tests).  
- Careful attention to positive definiteness requirements, addressing a key challenge in MOGP design.  
- Technically sound with a solid theoretical foundation and correct application of Cramér’s Theorem.  
- Exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible (implementation details in TensorFlow/GPflow provided).  
- Effective illustrations (Figure 1) and clear presentation of experimental results (Tables 1–3, Figures 2–3).  
- Novel contribution: spectral interpretation (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) offers meaningful physical interpretation.  
- Significant practical impact: enables modeling of delays/phase differences common in sensor networks and time series.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on real-world data and a statistically significant improvement for Cadmium prediction in Jura.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of when MOSM is preferred over CSM in real-world practice beyond the synthetic case.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity comparison to baseline methods.  
- Weakness: climate dataset’s high inter-channel correlation may not sufficiently showcase MOSM’s advantages.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of trade-offs between model complexity (number of spectral components) and performance.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong, novel contribution with sound theory and thorough experimental support.
