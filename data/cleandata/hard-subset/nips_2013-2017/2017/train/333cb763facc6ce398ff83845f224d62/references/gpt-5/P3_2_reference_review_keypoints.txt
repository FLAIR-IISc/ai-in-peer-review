- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs by designing matrix-valued PSD S(ω) and using Cramér’s theorem to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Parameterizes S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω) with complex Gaussian frequency factors having per-output magnitude, mean, covariance, delay, and phase.  
- Derives closed-form time-domain cross-covariances (Gaussian envelope × cosine with shift) that generalize SM to multiple outputs.  
- MOSM subsumes and extends SM-LMC and CSM, allowing per-output parameters and pair-specific cross-covariance means/bandwidths, delay and phase.  
- Synthetic experiment shows MOSM uniquely extrapolates a derivative and a delayed channel simultaneously, demonstrating richer cross-structure.  
- Empirically competitive on climate sensors and Jura benchmark, sometimes outperforming baselines; statistical testing reported where feasible.  
- Strength: principled spectral-domain construction that guarantees PD (modulo symmetrization caveat) and offers interpretable parameters.  
- Strength: closed-form kernels enable exact GP inference (subject to cubic cost).  
- Weakness: current symmetrization (entrywise averaging Sij(ω)+Sij(−ω)) may not preserve PSD; needs formal construction (augment R with mirrored factors).  
- Weakness: Hermitian/ conjugation notation issues—must ensure Sij(ω)=conj(Sji(ω)) is stated and enforced.  
- Weakness: parameter identifiability and redundancy (phase/delay trade-offs, sign absorbable into phase) require discussion, constraints, and regularization.  
- Weakness: some potentially misleading claims about negative αij and PSD compatibility need clarification.  
- Weakness: derivations of µij, Σij, wij and constant factors (e.g., 1/4) are omitted and should be included (appendix).  
- Weakness: scalability not addressed empirically; need runtimes/memory, relation to inter-domain/inducing Fourier features, and whether factorized S(ω) yields computational savings.  
- Weakness: experiments lack uncertainty metrics (test log-likelihoods, calibration), ablations (remove θ/φ or tie parameters), runtime sensitivity to Q, and datasets with known transport delays.  
- Weakness: comparability concerns for CONV results taken from literature.  
- Related work: should clarify precisely how CSM is a special case and discuss other cross-covariance constructions (e.g., multivariate Matérn with phase/delay).  
- Quality: technically sound and well-motivated overall, but needs theoretical fixes (symmetrization) and deeper experiments.  
- Clarity: generally well written, but key technical points (PSD proof after symmetrization, hermitian conjugation, derivations, identifiability, initialization) need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: moderately high—novel factorized complex Gaussian cross-spectra producing interpretable delays/phase and generalizing scalar SM.  
- Significance: useful for multi-channel signals with delays/phases; broader adoption requires scalable inference and robust initialization.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled PD-guaranteed construction, interpretable parameters, closed-form kernels, strong synthetic demo, competitive empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: symmetrization/PSD ambiguities, identifiability/initialization gaps, limited scalability evaluation, and shallower empirical evaluation.  
- Suggested improvements: provide rigorous PSD-preserving symmetrization (augment R), include derivations and identifiability discussion, specify initialization, report likelihoods/calibration and ablations, and propose/benchmark a scalable variant (e.g., variational inter-domain Fourier features).
