- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM to multi-output GPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities via Cramér’s theorem.  
- MOSM models flexible dependencies between outputs, including delays and phase shifts, with clear spectral parameter interpretation (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, correctly applying Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems with clear derivations and consistent notation.  
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic experiments (recovering derivatives and delays) and comparisons to LMC, CONV, and CSM on real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.  
- Novelty: genuine extension of SM to multi-output domain, more flexible than CSM by removing manual shift parameter.  
- Interpretability: parameters map directly to spectral properties, aiding understanding and constraints on learned relationships.  
- Empirical results: competitive performance, but not consistently significantly better than existing methods (CSM), with mixed gains across datasets.  
- Computational complexity is not discussed; increased parameter count may limit scalability to high-dimensional or many-output problems.  
- Initial focus on rank-one spectral decomposition; practical implications and costs of higher-rank extensions are not fully explored.  
- Overlap with CSM: core idea similar to prior spectral MOGP work; stronger comparison and differentiation from CSM is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, interpretable, and comprehensively evaluated; addresses an important MOGP modeling problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited and inconsistent performance gains, missing discussion of computational cost, and insufficient differentiation from CSM.  
- Requested revisions: discuss computational complexity and scalability, provide detailed comparative analysis with CSM, explore practical use/costs of higher-rank decompositions, and add limitations/future work.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept with minor revisions; quality 8/10, clarity 9/10, originality 7/10, significance 7/10.
