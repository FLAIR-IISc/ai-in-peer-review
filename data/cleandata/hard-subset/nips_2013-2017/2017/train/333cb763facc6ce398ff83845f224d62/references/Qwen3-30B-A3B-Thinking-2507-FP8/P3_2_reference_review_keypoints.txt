- Proposes MOSM: a multi-output spectral mixture kernel using Cramér's theorem and complex-valued spectral densities to model delays and phase shifts between channels.

- Theoretical soundness: derivation is rigorous and ensures positive-definite covariance matrices.

- Physical interpretability: parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) have clear spectral meaning, improving interpretability over LMC and CSM.

- Experimental validation: synthetic and real-world experiments demonstrate MOSM can capture complex dependencies; notably strong improvement on Cadmium prediction.

- Clear relation to prior work: MOSM generalizes CSM, with CSM recoverable as a constrained special case.

- Weakness — justification for complexity: paper lacks convincing argument that complex-valued formulation is necessary or superior, and does not explore higher-rank (Q>1) decompositions.

- Weakness — inconsistent performance claims: performance gains are dataset-dependent (strong for Cadmium, marginal/non-significant for climate), yet claims are sometimes overstated.

- Weakness — computational analysis missing: no discussion of training time, memory use, or scalability to larger numbers of outputs.

- Weakness — limited limitations analysis: insufficient exploration of why MOSM helps on some datasets but not others, and when delay/phase parameters are most beneficial.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, interpretable, and a valuable contribution to multi-output GPs, filling a gap in modeling inter-channel delays and phases.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: better justify complex-valued spectral densities and expressiveness, analyze computational complexity/scalability, temper performance claims, and provide deeper analysis of dataset-dependent behavior.
