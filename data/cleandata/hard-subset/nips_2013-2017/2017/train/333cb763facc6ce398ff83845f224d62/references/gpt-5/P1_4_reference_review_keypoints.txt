- Proposal: Introduces MOSM, a multi-output spectral mixture kernel built by factorizing a matrix-valued PSD S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with per-output complex Gaussian factors, symmetrized for real processes, yielding closed-form, PD kernels.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Wilson & Adams’ SM to multi-output, generalizes SM-LMC and CSM by allowing output-specific means/covariances, delays and phases, and asymmetric cross-covariances.  
- Theoretical validity: Use of Cramér’s theorem and the PSD factorization is appropriate and guarantees positive semidefiniteness; Fourier pairs give interpretable parameters (magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, delay, phase).  
- Notational/technical issue: Eq.9 incorrectly states Sij(ω)=Ri(ω)Rj(ω); it should reflect conjugation Sij(ω)=conj(Ri(ω))Rj(ω) and clarify Hermitian/conjugate-symmetry for real processes.  
- Synthetic experiments: Tri-variate synthetic task convincingly demonstrates MOSM can model delays and derivative relations that LMC-style models cannot.  
- Real-data performance: Competitive on climate and Jura datasets, best on some tasks (e.g., Cadmium) but gains are mixed across benchmarks.  
- Empirical shortcomings: Lacks details on initialization, selection of number of components Q, sensitivity analyses, runtimes, uncertainty calibration, and ablation studies.  
- Scalability: All inference is dense O(N^3); no scalable/variational sparse implementation is provided despite mentioning such approaches as future work.  
- Clarity: Paper is mostly clear with strong parameter interpretation, but contains typos and needs tighter terminology and explicit listing of per-output per-component parameters and identifiability/invariance discussion.  
- Originality: Moderately high—principled and nontrivial generalization of spectral-design approaches for multi-output kernels, building on but extending CSM and SM-LMC.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for multi-sensor/time-series/spatial modeling where delays and phases matter, though limited by current scalability and mixed empirical gains.  
- Pros: Principled PSD-guaranteed construction, interpretable parameters, strict generalization of prior models, expressive asymmetric cross-covariances, strong synthetic demonstration, good literature linkage.  
- Cons: Conjugation/notation error, no scalable inference, many hyperparameters raising identifiability/overfitting concerns, limited empirical analysis, no demonstrated high-dimensional input examples.  
- Suggested improvements: Fix conjugation/Hermitian notation; discuss identifiability, initialization, and invariances; provide sensitivity/ablation studies for Q and components; report computational costs; add sparse/variational experiments; show a 2D spatial example; evaluate predictive uncertainty.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel, interpretable, and useful generalization despite clarity issues and limited scalability/empirics.
