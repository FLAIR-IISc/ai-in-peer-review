The paper introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, an extension of the Spectral Mixture kernel to multi-output Gaussian processes. The core idea is to leverage Cramér’s theorem to construct a multivariate covariance function from complex-valued cross-spectral densities, offering flexibility in modeling dependencies between outputs, including delays and phase differences. The authors demonstrate the method on a synthetic example and two real-world datasets, comparing it to existing MOGP models like LMC, CONV, and CSM.

The paper is technically sound, building upon established theoretical foundations (Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems) and demonstrating a clear understanding of Gaussian process methodology. The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the connection to existing spectral methods is well-articulated. However, the presentation suffers from a number of minor, yet cumulatively significant, flaws. 

Firstly, the notation is inconsistent. For example, the definition of Sij(ω) in Section 3.1 is initially given with ‘wij’ as a coefficient, then redefined incorporating magnitude calculations. This makes tracking the parameters and their influence difficult. The use of superscripts (q) for parameters in Definition 5 feels unnecessarily complex and lacks clear motivation. Why is Q arbitrarily set to 1 before then allowing for an arbitrary Q? This feels disjointed. 

Secondly, several statements lack sufficient justification or clarity. The claim that the MOSM can “collapse” to simpler models when appropriate (near the end of Section 4.2) is not substantiated with experimental evidence or a theoretical explanation. The connection between the Jura dataset's characteristics (high correlation, non-Gaussianity) and the performance of different models is asserted without detailed analysis.

Thirdly, the experimental evaluation, while comprehensive in terms of datasets and baseline methods, is lacking in rigor. The parameter tuning process for each model is not described, and it is unclear whether a fair comparison was ensured. The statistical significance testing is mentioned but applied inconsistently. Saying that significance *cannot* be assured for some cases is less useful than performing the test and reporting the p-values. The choice of 5 latent components seems arbitrary and should be justified or explored with a sensitivity analysis. The presentation of results in Table 2 and 3 could be improved by reporting standard deviations for all metrics, providing a clearer indication of the variability of the results.

Fourthly, the writing quality could be improved. There are several instances of awkward phrasing and redundant information. For example, the repeated emphasis on the interpretability of parameters throughout the paper feels excessive. While this is a desirable feature, it should be highlighted concisely rather than reiterated multiple times. Minor grammatical errors and typos are present throughout the text, affecting readability (e.g., “Relying on Theorem 1” – the phrasing is clunky).

**Strengths:**

*   Novel extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs.
*   Sound theoretical foundation based on Cramér’s theorem.
*   Ability to model delays and phase differences between outputs.
*   Competitive performance on real-world datasets.
*   Clear and logical structure of the paper.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Inconsistent notation and presentation.
*   Lack of justification for certain design choices and claims.
*   Insufficiently rigorous experimental evaluation.
*   Suboptimal writing quality and minor errors.
*   The core innovation is incremental; it extends existing work in a logical, but not groundbreaking, fashion.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension to existing MOGP methods.
*   The proposed MOSM kernel offers increased flexibility in modeling dependencies between outputs.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on real-world datasets.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The presentation suffers from several minor flaws that detract from the overall clarity and readability.
*   The experimental evaluation lacks sufficient rigor and detail.
*   The significance of the contribution, while positive, is incremental rather than transformative.
*   The inconsistencies in notation and writing detract from the professional polish expected of a NIPS submission.



**Overall Assessment:**

I lean towards **rejection**. While the paper presents a valid contribution to the field, the cumulative effect of its minor flaws significantly diminishes its impact. The lack of rigor in the experimental evaluation and the inconsistencies in presentation raise concerns about the reliability and reproducibility of the results. A major revision addressing these issues would be necessary for reconsideration. The paper demonstrates competence, but not the exceptional quality expected at NIPS.
