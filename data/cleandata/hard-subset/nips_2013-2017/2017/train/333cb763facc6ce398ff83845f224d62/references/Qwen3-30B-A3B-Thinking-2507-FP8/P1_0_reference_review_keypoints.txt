- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending Spectral Mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér’s theorem.  
- MOSM uses complex-valued spectral densities to model frequency, magnitude, phase, and delays, generalizing Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) which cannot model delays.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic tasks (derivatives, delayed signals) and two real datasets (climate, heavy metals), showing competitive performance and a significant gain for Cadmium.  
- Technical strength: mathematically rigorous derivation with clear links to Bochner/Cramér and interpretable spectral parameters.  
- Clarity strength: exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible background and derivation; effective figures and appropriate metrics/statistics.  
- Originality strength: novel use of complex spectral densities to enable phase and delay modeling absent in prior MOGP kernels.  
- Significance strength: practically useful for applications with delays/phase shifts; maintains positive definiteness and shows real-world relevance.  
- Weakness: performance gains are marginal on some real datasets (climate), raising questions about when MOSM is preferable.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and training/inference costs relative to existing MOGP methods.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical discussion on how the number of spectral components (Q) affects expressiveness and generalization (bias–variance tradeoff).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; add discussion of scenarios where MOSM is especially advantageous and address computational complexity.
