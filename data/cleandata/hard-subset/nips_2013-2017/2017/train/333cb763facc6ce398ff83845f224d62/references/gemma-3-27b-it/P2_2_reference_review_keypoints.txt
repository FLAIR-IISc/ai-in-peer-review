- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel by defining cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain and using Cramér’s theorem to ensure positive definiteness.  
- MOSM models delays and phase shifts between outputs with interpretable spectral parameters.  
- Novel and principled extension of the Spectral Mixture kernel to multivariate GPs.  
- Solid theoretical grounding using Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems.  
- Spectral parameterization yields clear interpretability compared to black-box kernels.  
- Experimental comparison includes synthetic and real-world datasets against LMC, CONV, and CSM.  
- Synthetic example effectively demonstrates benefits of modeling delays.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with helpful background material.  
- Potential computational cost from Fourier transforms for high-dimensional inputs or large datasets.  
- Spectral methods can be sensitive to parameter choices; robustness and guidance on selecting number of components (Q) are lacking.  
- Performance gains over existing methods, especially CSM, are modest and not always statistically conclusive.  
- MOSM can collapse to simpler models, but this behavior and conditions are not clearly demonstrated in results.  
- Relationship to CSM acknowledged but needs clearer explanation of specific conditions where MOSM reduces to CSM.  
- Technically sound derivations and well-presented experiments, though some math sections could be simplified for broader readers.  
- Approach is original and potentially impactful for applications needing phase/delay modeling, but real-world significance needs further validation.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, interpretable, comprehensive evaluation, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: limited real-world gains, potential computational cost, and insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions addressing computational cost, parameter sensitivity, statistical testing, and clarity on MOSM–CSM connection.
