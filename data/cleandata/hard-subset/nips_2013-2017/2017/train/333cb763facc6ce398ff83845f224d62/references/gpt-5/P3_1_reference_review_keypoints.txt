- Paper proposes MOSM: a multi-output spectral mixture kernel using low-rank complex-valued spectral factors to encode per-output frequency magnitude, phase and delay with closed-form real-domain cross-covariances.

- Strength: principled frequency-domain construction grounded in Cramér’s theorem with positive-definiteness by design via S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).

- Strength: MOSM generalizes SM-LMC and CSM, allowing channel-specific spectral means/covariances and explicit delays, and can model non-symmetric, delayed cross-covariances and negative correlations.

- Strength: Parameters are interpretable (magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, delay) per output pair.

- Strength: Empirical results show clear gains on a synthetic derivative/delay task and improved Cadmium prediction on Jura; overall competitive on real datasets.

- Notational/error issue: Sij(ω) should be conj(Ri(ω)) Rj(ω); the paper sometimes omits the conjugate, contradicting Hermitian property and affecting θij/φij signs.

- Notational/error issue: Inconsistent Gaussian SE exponent—eq. (8) appears to miss a 1/2 factor present elsewhere.

- Clarity issue: Symmetrization discussion needs to state PSD preservation and the correct real-valuedness condition S(ω)=S(−ω)H, not just evenness.

- Identifiability concern: θ and φ can be confounded with μ (phase/delay shifts) and the Gaussian envelope is shifted by θ; priors or constraints should be discussed.

- Parameter explosion/optimization: Per-output, per-component parameters (wi, μi, Σi, θi, φi) create many degrees of freedom, multimodal likelihoods, and no guidance on initialization, priors, or regularization is provided.

- Experimental scope: Synthetic baseline unfairness—operator-aware MOGP baselines (e.g., process convolutions or known differential operators) are missing and should be compared.

- Experimental scope: Climate results differences are small; statistical testing should use paired tests (paired t-test or Wilcoxon) and report time/memory and convergence behavior.

- Experimental scope: Jura results mixed (Cadmium improves, Copper not); variability across seeds/splits and runtimes are not reported; analysis of when per-channel μ/Σ helps is missing.

- Expressivity limitation: With rank Q, PSD rank ≤ Q at each ω and per-output parameter combinations restrict admissible cross-spectra; approximation limits vs arbitrary Hermitian PSDs should be discussed.

- Relation to prior work: Good discussion of SM-LMC/CSM; should also cite multivariate Matérn cross-covariances and compare to process-convolution approaches that can encode delays.

- Quality assessment: Core construction is sound and inverse FT derivations plausible, aside from conjugation/symmetry notation issues.

- Clarity assessment: Generally well written, but needs fixes for conjugation, symmetry, exponent consistency, and more derivational detail in appendix for reproducibility.

- Originality: Moderately high—introduces frequency-dependent coregionalization via complex SE factors yielding closed-form cross-kernels with phase/delay.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for interpretable cross-channel modeling, though real-data gains are modest and scalability/identifiability issues remain.

- Pros for acceptance: Principled interpretable generalization of SM to multi-output, guaranteed PSD, closed-form kernels, solid synthetic demonstration.

- Cons for acceptance: Notational/technical clarity issues, limited empirical advantage on real data and missing stronger baselines, no scalability/identifiability solutions.

- Suggestions: Fix conjugation and symmetry notation, discuss identifiability and priors/constraints, provide initialization/optimization details and runtimes, add operator-aware baselines and ablations, and discuss approximation limits and sparse/variational extensions.
