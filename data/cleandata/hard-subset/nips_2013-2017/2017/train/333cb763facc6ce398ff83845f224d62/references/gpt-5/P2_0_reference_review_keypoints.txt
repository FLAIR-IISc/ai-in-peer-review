- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: builds cross-spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with per-output complex SE factors and symmetrization for real-valued processes.  
- Inverse Fourier transform yields closed-form time-domain cross-covariances allowing channel-specific auto-covariances, phase shifts, delays, and non-symmetric cross-covariances.  
- Shows CSM and SM-LMC are special cases of MOSM and demonstrates MOSM can uniquely recover derivative and delayed signals in a synthetic 3-output task.  
- Extends spectral mixture kernels (Wilson & Adams) to multi-output via Cramér’s theorem; differs from SM-LMC/CONV by directly designing cross-spectra and not forcing shared auto-structure.  
- Generalizes CSM by adding per-output spectral means/covariances/delays and an explicit delay term; related to multivariate Matérn but focuses on spectral interpretability.  
- Construction is principled: PSD guaranteed by spectral factorization and closed-form kernels; summing Q components preserves PSD.  
- Notational/technical issues: misstated Hermitian condition (should be conjugate symmetry Sij(ω)=Sji(ω)̄) and unclear/misapplied symmetrization step that needs justification or correction.  
- Recommend providing derivations or appendix for pairwise parameter formulas and inverse Fourier transform to support Eq. (10)/(11).  
- Experiments are reasonable but limited (1D inputs, small number of outputs); synthetic task compelling but real-data improvements are modest.  
- Evaluation underutilizes GP probabilistic outputs by reporting only MAE; predictive log-likelihood and calibration metrics are missing.  
- Clarity is generally good and parameters are interpretable, but notation and conjugate-symmetry discussion must be fixed.  
- Suggest adding details on optimization/initialization, parameter identifiability, and optimization stability to aid reproducibility.  
- Propose including an algorithmic box/pseudocode showing training with channel-wise parameterization and on-the-fly derivation of cross-pair parameters.  
- Original contribution: novel rank-Q spectral factorization with per-output complex SEs enabling interpretable phase-and-delay modeling not present in prior work.  
- Significance: conceptually important and likely influential, but practical impact limited by scalability (O(N^3)) and lack of sparse/inducing-point variant.  
- Pros: principled PSD-guaranteed Fourier-domain construction; interpretable per-output parameters; closed-form non-symmetric cross-covariances; convincing synthetic demonstration; some statistical tests.  
- Cons: notational inaccuracies around conjugate symmetry; limited empirical scope and modest real-data gains; no runtime/complexity or sparse approximation; missing likelihood-based metrics; unclear initialization/identifiability.  
- Concrete suggestions: clarify Hermitian/conjugate symmetry and symmetrization validity; add derivations in appendix; report log-likelihoods and ablations (remove delay/phase); provide complexity analysis and a sparse MOSM prototype; test more datasets and visualize learned phase/delay.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound, novel and interpretable, but needs clarification on conjugate symmetry and broader empirical/scalability work.
