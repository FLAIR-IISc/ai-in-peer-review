- Proposes MOSM kernel by extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér’s theorem.  
- MOSM models delays, phase differences, and varying auto-covariances while preserving positive definiteness.  
- Provides theoretical justification and connects to existing methods, generalizing CSM by removing manual phase parameters.  
- Properly situates work relative to LMC, CONV, and spectral GP methods using Bochner/Cramér frameworks.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets shows MOSM can learn derivative and delayed relationships.  
- The method is theoretically sound, with rigorous grounding ensuring kernel positive definiteness.  
- Offers flexibility and interpretability: spectral parameters have clear meanings and capture complex dependencies.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-written with accessible mathematical derivations.  
- Does not address computational complexity or scalability of training/inference with MOSM.  
- Lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., number of spectral components Q) and tuning strategies.  
- Real-world experiments are limited in complexity; need evaluation on higher-dimensional, more challenging datasets.  
- Insufficient analysis/visualization of the learned cross-covariance and cross-spectral structures in experiments.  
- Contains a few minor typographical errors that detract from polish.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically principled, empirically effective, and broadly applicable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing discussion on complexity, limited hyperparameter and experimental analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational, hyperparameter, and cross-covariance analysis concerns.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
