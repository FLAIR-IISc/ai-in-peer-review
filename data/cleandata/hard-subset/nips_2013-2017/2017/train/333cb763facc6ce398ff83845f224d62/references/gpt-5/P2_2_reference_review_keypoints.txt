- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for multi-output GPs by factorizing the cross-spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)^H R(ω) with complex Gaussian components.  
- Yields closed-form, real-valued cross-covariances with interpretable per-pair parameters: frequency mean, bandwidth, magnitude, delay, and phase.  
- Generalizes Wilson & Adams’ SM kernel to multi-output and subsumes SM-LMC and CSM while allowing per-channel spectral parameters and explicit delays.  
- Synthetic experiment shows MOSM recovers auto- and cross-covariances and extrapolates derivatives and delays better than CONV, SM-LMC, and CSM.  
- Real-world results: competitive with CSM on climate imputation and improves Cadmium prediction on Jura dataset, with modest overall gains.  
- The spectral factorization guarantees PSD in frequency domain and valid matrix-valued kernels after inverse Fourier transform.  
- Symmetrization S(ω)+S(−ω) ensures real-valued processes while preserving PSD at each ω.  
- Minor notational inconsistency: sometimes writes Sij=Ri Rj instead of Sij=overline{Ri} Rj; needs correction for conjugation/Hermitian structure.  
- Misleading statement about αij allowing negative correlations — αij>0 but cosine term produces sign changes; clarify wording.  
- Statistical testing is weak: KS test on single held-out segments is inappropriate for paired predictive comparisons; recommend paired t-test, Wilcoxon, or bootstrap on per-point errors and multiple splits.  
- Practical concerns: parameter count grows with m and Q, raising identifiability, initialization, and regularization issues not addressed.  
- Computational complexity remains O(N^3); no scalable (sparse/inducing) solution implemented or evaluated.  
- Clarity is generally good with helpful figures, but needs explicit statement of real-valuedness condition and symmetrization rationale, plus fixes to typos/notation.  
- Originality: incremental but meaningful — novel use of complex cross-spectra and delays in MOGPs is a notable contribution.  
- Significance: useful, interpretable, expressive kernel likely to be adopted and extended despite modest empirical gains on small datasets.  
- Recommendation: technically solid and interpretable work; lean accept with requests to strengthen empirical validation, clarify notation, and discuss initialization/regularization; suggest releasing code.
