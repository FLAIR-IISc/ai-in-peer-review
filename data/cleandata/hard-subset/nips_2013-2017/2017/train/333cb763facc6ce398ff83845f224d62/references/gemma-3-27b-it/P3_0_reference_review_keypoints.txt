- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output spectral mixture kernel using Cramér’s theorem to model delays, phase shifts, and varying auto-covariances.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical grounding that guarantees positive definiteness and links to harmonic analysis.  
- Maps kernel parameters to interpretable spectral properties (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Uses complex-valued spectral density for increased modeling flexibility and expressiveness.  
- Empirical validation includes a convincing synthetic example and two real-world datasets, showing competitive performance.  
- Builds on SM kernel and contrasts with LMC, convolutional models, and CSM, addressing their limitations.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear, consistent mathematical notation.  
- Potential computational complexity and scalability of MOSM (especially with large Q) are not addressed.  
- Model introduces many hyperparameters but lacks sensitivity analysis or tuning guidance.  
- Comparisons are limited; more recent or specialized MOGP baselines could strengthen the evaluation.  
- Real-world dataset improvements are modest and not always statistically conclusive.  
- Initial focus on rank-one (Q=1) decomposition is somewhat restrictive; trade-offs for larger Q are underexplored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound, interpretable, flexible, and empirically promising.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability and hyperparameter concerns, inconclusive real-world gains, and restrictive rank-one emphasis.  
- Overall: valuable contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend accept with minor revisions to address scalability and hyperparameter discussion.
