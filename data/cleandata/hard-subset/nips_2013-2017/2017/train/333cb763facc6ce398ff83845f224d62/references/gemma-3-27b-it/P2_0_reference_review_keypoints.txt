- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture kernels to MOGPs via Cramér’s theorem, modeling cross-covariances with spectral interpretations.  
- Validated on one synthetic and two real-world datasets (climate, heavy metal), compared against LMC, CONV, and CSM.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of spectral mixture methods to multivariate GPs, offering a different perspective from latent-variable approaches.  
- Theoretical foundation: rigorously grounded in Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems.  
- Interpretability: parameters map to spectral-domain concepts, enabling modeling of delays and phase shifts between outputs.  
- Empirical validation: results show competitive performance and synthetic example recovers complex dependencies convincingly.  
- Writing: paper is well-written and background material is accessible.  
- Limited performance gains: MOSM’s improvements over existing methods (notably CSM) on real data are modest and not always statistically significant.  
- Model complexity: MOSM introduces many hyperparameters, raising optimization difficulty and potential overfitting; this is under-discussed.  
- Computational cost: no analysis of training/prediction complexity is provided, limiting practical assessment.  
- Relation to CSM: relationship is described but lacks clear examples or scenarios showing MOSM’s distinct advantage over CSM.  
- Missing ablation studies: no experiments isolating the impact of components (e.g., delay/phase parameters).  
- Overall assessment: solid, theoretically sound contribution with useful interpretability and competitive performance, but needs stronger empirical/complexity analysis.  
- For acceptance: novelty, theoretical grounding, interpretability, competitiveness, and clarity.  
- Against acceptance: modest performance gains, increased complexity, and missing computational cost discussion.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (7/10); recommendation to add computational complexity analysis and ablation study to strengthen the paper.
