- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM to multivariate GPs via Cramér's theorem.  
- Models cross-spectral densities as complex-valued to represent delays and phase shifts between channels.  
- Builds on existing spectral GP kernel work and generalizes CSM by incorporating both delays and phase shifts.  
- Theoretical soundness: factorization S(ω)=R^H(ω)R(ω) ensures positive-definiteness with interpretable parameters.  
- Parameters are interpretable in terms of spectral properties (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay), aiding applied use.  
- Novelty: complex-valued cross-spectral density for cross-covariances is a genuine contribution over prior MOGP methods.  
- Synthetic experiment shows MOSM can learn derivative and delayed relationships without prior knowledge.  
- Real-world validation includes climate and Jura datasets, with MOSM significantly outperforming CSM on Cadmium prediction.  
- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art MOGP methods (omits recent relevant works like [12,13,17]).  
- No analysis of computational complexity scaling with number of outputs (m) and spectral components (Q).  
- Missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for Q; experiments fix Q=5 without justification or overfitting analysis.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments use small datasets and the paper doesn't discuss sparse approximations for large N.  
- Insufficient explanation of mixed experimental results: no analysis why MOSM fails to beat CSM on climate data or Copper in Jura.  
- Recommendations: expand related work, add computational complexity discussion, include sensitivity experiments for Q, analyze climate results, and address scalability via sparse methods.  
- Overall: technically solid, well-written, and novel; scores Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons, complexity, hyperparameter sensitivity, and scalability.
