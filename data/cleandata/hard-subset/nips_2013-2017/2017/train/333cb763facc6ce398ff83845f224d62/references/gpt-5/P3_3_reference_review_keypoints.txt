- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs using low-rank Hermitian spectral factorization S(ω)=∑q Rq(ω)H Rq(ω) and closed-form inverse Fourier cross-covariances.  
- Per-output complex Gaussian spectral factors have interpretable parameters: amplitude wi, mean μi, covariance Σi, delay θi, and phase φi, yielding per-pair parameters deterministically.  
- MOSM allows distinct spectral means/covariances per output, explicit time delays and phase offsets, and both positive and negative cross-correlations while guaranteeing PSD via spectral factorization.  
- Theoretical construction is principled and correctly uses Cramér’s theorem; closed-form inverse Fourier pairs are valid for Gaussian spectra with linear phase.  
- Ambiguity: Definition 5 could be misread as allowing free per-pair parameters; positivity is only guaranteed if per-pair parameters are induced from per-output factorization.  
- Symmetrization S(ω)=S(−ω) enforces real-valued GPs; a brief proof/construction that averaging preserves PSD would strengthen the exposition.  
- Synthetic experiment convincingly shows representation of antisymmetric cross-covariances (function–derivative) and delays that standard LMC struggles with.  
- Real-data experiments (UK climate sensors; Jura heavy metals) show MOSM is competitive or better in MAE versus CONV, SM-LMC, and CSM, but climate task is too easy and differences are small.  
- Evaluation limited to MAE; lacks predictive log-likelihood (NLPP), uncertainty calibration, and other GP-relevant metrics.  
- Novelty: parameterizing cross-spectra with complex Gaussian factors and deriving closed-form kernels with delay/phase is elegant and broadens flexibility beyond CSM/SM-LMC.  
- Significance: provides an interpretable, PSD-guaranteed family of MOGP kernels likely useful for multi-sensor time series and spatio-temporal tasks with lags.  
- Experimental evidence is promising but not definitive; stronger empirical validation on harder datasets, more baselines, and uncertainty metrics needed.  
- Strength: principled PSD guarantee via spectral factorization and interpretable per-output parameterization enabling exact GP inference.  
- Strength: MOSM generalizes and includes SM-LMC and CSM as special cases; synthetic example highlights representational power.  
- Weakness: Definition 5 ambiguity about free vs. induced per-pair parameters must be clarified to ensure PSD.  
- Weakness: Identifiability and optimization concerns—θ and φ can trade off with μ; no discussion of initialization, regularization, or constraints.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—no sparse/structured implementation and cubic complexity O((mN)^3); no practical guidance for larger m or N.  
- Weakness: Baseline fairness—claim that latent-process models “fail” may depend on operator choices; needs clarification or additional baselines (e.g., LMC with differential operators).  
- Questions for authors: confirm per-pair parameters are deterministic functions of per-output parameters and state the mapping explicitly.  
- Questions for authors: describe initialization strategy for μi(q), Σi(q), θi(q), φi(q) and sensitivity to initialization and Q.  
- Questions for authors: report NLPP/log-marginal likelihood and calibration metrics on test sets.  
- Questions for authors: in synthetic experiment, report recovered delays/phases and compare to ground truth.  
- Questions for authors: clarify whether symmetrization S(ω)→0.5(S(ω)+S(−ω)) always preserves PSD in their construction.  
- Questions for authors: test whether LMC with known differential operators or convolutions can recover derivative cross-covariance and report results.  
- Minor comments: fix typos (“multiouput”, “wrt”), missing footnote, use Cm×m for Hermitian S(ω), and add explicit parameter settings in figure captions.  
- Reproducibility: GPflow usage is good; release code, initialization scripts, seeds, and hyperparameter priors/constraints for full reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, elegant, and interpretable contribution with PSD guarantee; empirical validation should be expanded but paper merits acceptance (reviewer leans accept).
