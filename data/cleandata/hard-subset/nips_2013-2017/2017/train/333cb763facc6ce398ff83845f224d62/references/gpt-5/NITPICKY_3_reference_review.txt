Summary
This paper proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a stationary matrix-valued GP kernel constructed in the frequency domain via Cramér’s theorem. The authors factorize the cross-spectral density as S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω) with channel-wise complex-valued square-exponential factors Ri(ω) that include: (i) Gaussian spectral envelopes (mean and diagonal covariance), (ii) linear phase (delay), and (iii) constant phase offsets. They symmetrize to ensure real-valued processes and derive a closed-form time-domain kernel that generalizes SM-LMC and CSM, allowing pairwise delays and phases and channel-specific auto-covariances. Experiments on a synthetic 3-output signal (original, derivative, delayed) and two real datasets (climate and Jura metals) show competitive performance; MOSM is strongest on the synthetic task and one of the real tasks (Cadmium).

Relation to prior work: The construction follows Wilson & Adams’ SM via Bochner, extended here to multi-output via Cramér (as in some geostatistics and MOGP works). It generalizes SM-LMC [3,6,5] and CSM [5] by introducing a linear phase term (delays) and channel-specific spectral parameters, while retaining closed-form kernels.

Strengths
- Conceptually clean spectral-domain construction that directly ensures PSD at each frequency via factorization.
- Clear spectral interpretation: per-pair frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.
- Includes SM-LMC and CSM as special cases; the distinction from CSM (constant vs linear phase → true delays) is well-motivated.
- Derives closed-form time-domain cross-covariances; capable of modeling asymmetric, signed cross-covariances with delays.
- Synthetic experiment is well-chosen to demonstrate extrapolation and the need for pairwise delays.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
- Potentially serious derivational errors and notation inconsistencies:
  - Conjugation: With S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω), the (i,j)-entry must be Sij(ω) = R∗i(ω) Rj(ω). Eq. (9) (and surrounding text) repeatedly writes Sij(ω) = Ri(ω) Rj(ω) without conjugation, which breaks Hermitian PSD unless phases happen to cancel. Please fix and consistently propagate conjugation throughout.
  - Covariance of the product of Gaussians: The stated Σij = 2 Σi (Σi + Σj)−1 Σj is off by a factor of 2; in 1D this becomes 2σi^2 σj^2/(σi^2+σj^2), whereas the standard product gives (σi^−2 + σj^−2)−1 = σi^2 σj^2/(σi^2 + σj^2). This factor propagates to the time-domain kernel and amplitudes. Please re-derive carefully with the correct 1/2 factors in exponents.
  - Missing 1/2 factors: Eq. (8) and (9) occasionally drop the 1/2 in the SE exponent. This contributes to the above confusion.
  - Symmetrization for real-valued GPs: The text proposes Ssym(ω) = 0.5(S(ω) + S(−ω)). While the average of PSD matrices is PSD, the required reality condition is S(−ω) = S(ω)∗. Please prove that your construction enforces both Hermitian PSD and the real-valuedness condition (including off-diagonals) after symmetrization. The safer construction is to explicitly include mirrored components in R(ω) and keep S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω).
  - Q overloading: Q is first the rank of R(ω) (Section 3.1), then reused as the number of spectral mixture components in Def. 5. These are different concepts. Please distinguish rank (e.g., R) from number of mixture components (e.g., Q), and clarify positivity guarantees when summing multiple components.
  - Definition 5 suggests per-pair free parameters α(q)ij, μ(q)ij, Σ(q)ij, θ(q)ij, φ(q)ij; but positivity hinges on tying these to channel-specific parameters via R(ω). Please state explicitly that in training you optimize channel-wise parameters {wi(q), μi(q), Σi(q), θi(q), φi(q)}, from which per-pair quantities are induced; otherwise, the construction is not guaranteed PSD.

- Experimental limitations:
  - No ablations on the effect of delays/phases; no comparison to LMC/CONV models augmented with explicit delays via convolutional kernels (process convolutions can encode delays).
  - No uncertainty calibration or log-likelihood metrics; only MAE is reported.
  - No scalability/runtime analysis; exact GP training with all channels is O(N^3) in total points. Initialization for SM parameters (known to be critical) is not described.
  - Statistical testing: The Kolmogorov–Smirnov test on MAE distributions is not ideal for paired comparisons; consider paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank across repeated splits, or per-point paired tests.
  - Results are mixed: MOSM is best on the synthetic and on Cadmium, but not consistently superior on climate and Copper.

- Clarity/formatting issues:
  - Numerous typos and grammar: “infinitelymany”, “multiouput”, “south on England”, “derivate signal”, “rationale of standard GP”, etc.
  - Equation typography inconsistencies and line breaks mid-exponent; footnote marker “1” misplaced; “st Conference…” header fragment.
  - References have missing fields and inconsistent formatting (“ser”, “vol, , , ”).
  - Acknowledgements breach anonymity for a NeurIPS submission.

Questions for the authors
1. Please correct the conjugation in Sij and re-derive μij, Σij, and the amplitude term with consistent 1/2 factors. Provide a supplemental derivation.
2. Can you formalize the real-valuedness conditions after symmetrization and prove that S(−ω) = S(ω)∗ and S(ω) is PSD for all ω?
3. Clarify the distinction between spectral mixture components and rank. Did you use rank-1 with multiple spectral components, or higher rank? Show an ablation.
4. How did you initialize μi, Σi, θi, φi, and wi? How sensitive is MOSM to initialization?
5. Can you compare against a convolutional multi-output kernel with explicit delays in the impulse response?
6. Please report negative log marginal likelihood and calibration diagnostics, and add runtime/memory comparisons.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually appealing, principled extension of SM to MOGPs via Cramér’s theorem.
- Interpretable parameters (delay + phase) and closed-form kernels.
- Demonstrated advantage on a synthetic task tailored to delays/derivatives and on one real task.

Cons for acceptance
- Derivational inconsistencies (conjugation, Gaussian product) that affect correctness.
- Ambiguity around positivity guarantees when moving from factorized PSDs to per-pair parameters.
- Mixed empirical gains; limited evaluation breadth and statistical rigor.
- Significant clarity, notation, and formatting issues; anonymization violation.

Recommendation
Score: 5 (weak reject). The idea is promising and likely fixable, but the current manuscript has enough technical and presentation issues—including a potential error in the core derivation and ambiguity about PSD guarantees—that I cannot recommend acceptance as-is. A revised version addressing the derivations, clarifying the parameterization/positivity, strengthening experiments, and fixing formatting/anonymity would be much stronger.
