- Proposes MOSM: a multi-output spectral mixture kernel using Cramér's theorem with complex-valued spectral densities to model delays and phase differences.  
- Ensures positive definiteness via rank-1 factorization and validates on synthetic and two real-world datasets.  
- Strong theoretical foundation leveraging the multi-output extension of Bochner's theorem (Cramér's theorem).  
- Parameters have clear spectral interpretability (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly show modeling of derivatives and delayed signals that some baselines cannot handle.  
- Experimental design compares MOSM to relevant baselines (SM-LMC, CONV, CSM) using appropriate metrics.  
- Paper insufficiently differentiates MOSM from CSM; CSM already includes phase shifts and the distinction/expressiveness claim is unclear.  
- No analysis of computational complexity or scalability (timing, memory, scaling with outputs/data) is provided.  
- Real-world performance gains are marginal and often not statistically significant across datasets.  
- Missing discussion of scenarios where MOSM is not ideal or when simpler models (e.g., LMC) suffice.  
- Overall novelty is overstated given overlap with prior CSM work; theoretical contribution is valid but practical distinction is weak.  
- Experiments need ablations to isolate contributions of delays and phase components.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance conditional on clarifying MOSM vs CSM, adding complexity/scalability analysis, stronger statistical evaluation, and guidance on when MOSM is advantageous.
