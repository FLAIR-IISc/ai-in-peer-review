Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs). The idea is to construct a complex-valued matrix of cross-spectral densities S(ω) via a low-rank factorization and then obtain real-valued, stationary cross-covariances by inverse Fourier transform using Cramér’s theorem. Each output i has its own per-component spectral Gaussian Ri(ω) with mean µi, diagonal covariance Σi, complex phase φi, and linear phase (delay) θi. Cross-spectral terms are formed from products of these functions, yielding cross-covariances with interpretable parameters: amplitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, and delay. The authors argue this subsumes SM-LMC and CSM, adding delays and allowing different auto-covariances per output pair. Experiments include: (i) synthetic data with a base function, its derivative, and a delayed version; (ii) climate imputation; (iii) Jura heavy metals.

This builds directly on Wilson & Adams’ SM kernel (ICML’13) and the CSM kernel (Ulrich et al., NIPS’15). It also relates to multi-output kernels through LMC and convolution models (Álvarez & Lawrence, NIPS’08), and to cross-covariance constructions in geostatistics (Gneiting et al., JASA’10). The spectral-domain construction via Cramér’s theorem is a natural multivariate extension of Bochner-based SM designs.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: a clean spectral factorization that induces per-pair phase and delay in cross-covariances, offering interpretable parameters and including CSM/SM-LMC as special cases.
- Theoretical framing via Cramér’s theorem is appropriate and appealing; the factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) ensures PSD if implemented correctly.
- The synthetic example is well chosen to demonstrate simultaneous learning of phase and delay, and extrapolation capabilities.
- Implementation in GPflow/TensorFlow and comparison to relevant baselines (CSM, SM-LMC, CONV).

Major weaknesses and technical concerns
- Hermitian/complex conjugation inconsistency: The factorization needs Sij(ω)=R_:i(ω)H R_:j(ω)=conj(Ri(ω)) Rj(ω) for Q=1 to guarantee Hermitian PSD matrices and condition (6). The text then sets Sij(ω)=Ri(ω) Rj(ω) (no conjugate), which in general is not Hermitian and breaks the PSD guarantee. Several downstream sign relations (θij, φij) depend on whether conjugation is used; the paper currently uses θij=θi−θj and φij=φi−φj, whereas with the correct conjugation one gets θij=θj−θi and φij=φj−φi. This is not a minor nit: it affects correctness of the construction.
- Real-valued process condition: For real processes one needs conjugate symmetry S(−ω)=S(ω)H. The paper proposes making Sij symmetric via 0.5(Sij(ω)+Sij(−ω)), i.e., even symmetry without conjugation, which is not the correct condition and is unnecessary if the Hermitian factorization is used properly. This is a fundamental conceptual confusion that needs correction.
- Amplitude sign inconsistency: αij is defined as αij=wij(2π)n/2|Σij|1/2 with wij>0 by construction, so αij≥0. The text claims αij∈R allowing “negative correlation,” which is misleading. Negative cross-covariance comes from the cosine/phase, not from αij<0. Please fix the statement.
- Missing/incorrect constants and 1/2 factors: Multiple equations (e.g., (8), (9), (10)) appear to drop 1/2 factors in Gaussian exponents and/or normalization constants; (10) switches between Σij and Σ−1ij ambiguously across spectral/time domains. Later (11) includes a 1/2, earlier not. These inconsistencies hamper reproducibility and suggest careless derivation.
- Notation inaccuracies: In Theorem 2 discussion, S(ω) is said to be “Hermitian” but typed as S(ω)∈Rm×m; should be Cm×m. In (6) “z denotes the complex conjugate of z” uses the same symbol for a vector and its conjugate. δiris in Section 3.2 is unclear. Several stray superscripts/footnotes (“choose2 Q=1”), “multiouput,” “wrt,” etc.
- Identifiability and parameterization: Because only differences θi−θj and φi−φj matter, absolute phases/delays are non-identifiable; wi and wj scaling is also non-identifiable up to their product. The paper should discuss identifiability, constraints, and priors or reparameterizations to avoid degenerate solutions.
- Training/initialization and complexity: No details on initialization of spectral means/covariances/phases (SM is notoriously sensitive), step sizes, optimizers, number of restarts, or computational complexity. No discussion of scaling beyond O(N3); no sparse/structured approximations used despite claiming future interest.
- Experimental evaluation:
  - Metrics limited to MAE; no log-likelihood, calibration, or uncertainty assessment.
  - For the Jura dataset, CONV baselines are pulled from prior work, not re-run under the same protocol; this can bias comparisons.
  - Statistical tests: KS test on MAE with small n and multiple comparisons, without corrections, is weak evidence.
  - Some tables/figures are hard to interpret; columns/units are missing; standard errors sometimes absent.
- Relation to prior work: The statement about [17] “t-Student cross-spectral densities” seems mismatched to Gneiting et al. (2010), which is about Matérn cross-covariances. The description of CSM as “manual shift between cosine and exponential factors” is imprecise; please clarify the exact relation and constraints precisely, including the linear phase structure.

Clarity and presentation
- The paper is generally readable but marred by numerous typographical errors, inconsistent notation, and missing constants that undermine confidence in the derivations. The references list is incomplete and inconsistently formatted. Figures/tables appear truncated in places and lack sufficient captions/labels to be self-contained.

Questions for the authors
- Please correct the factorization and clarify whether Sij(ω)=conj(Ri(ω)) Rj(ω) is used. If so, revise all subsequent θij, φij, and the real-valuedness argument accordingly.
- Provide full, consistent derivations with correct constants and 1/2 factors, and explicit expressions for the inverse Fourier transforms yielding (10)–(11).
- How are parameters initialized? How many random restarts? Which optimizer/hyperparameters? Provide runtime and complexity versus alternatives.
- Discuss identifiability and any constraints imposed during training (e.g., fixing one output as a reference for θ, φ).
- Re-run all baselines under the same protocol, and report NLL/CRPS/coverage alongside MAE.

Pros
- Conceptually elegant spectral construction; interpretable cross-delays and phases.
- Potentially unifies/extends SM-LMC and CSM; promising synthetic results.

Cons
- Fundamental technical inconsistencies (Hermitian conjugation, real-valuedness condition).
- Derivations and notation contain numerous errors/omissions.
- Insufficient experimental rigor and reporting; limited metrics; baseline fairness concerns.
- Missing discussion of identifiability and practical training details.

Recommendation
Reject. While the core idea is promising and likely to be impactful if executed correctly, the current manuscript contains multiple technical and presentation issues that, in aggregate, undermine its soundness and reproducibility. I encourage the authors to correct the Hermitian/conjugation and real-valuedness conditions, clean up the derivations and notation, expand the experimental evaluation, and resubmit.
