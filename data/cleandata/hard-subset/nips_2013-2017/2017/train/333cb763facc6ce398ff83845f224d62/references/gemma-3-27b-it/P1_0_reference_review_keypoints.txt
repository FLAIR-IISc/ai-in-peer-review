- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing points below.  
- Contribution: proposes MOSM, a novel multi-output extension of the Spectral Mixture kernel using Cramér’s theorem.  
- Theoretical foundation: elegant, principled guarantee of positive-definiteness via complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Interpretability: MOSM parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) are clear and useful.  
- Generality: MOSM subsumes prior approaches (e.g., CSM) as special cases.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition.  
- Synthetic experiment: successfully demonstrates MOSM can capture known relationships (derivative, delay).  
- Experimental weakness: real-world results show only marginal MAE improvements and lack consistent statistical significance.  
- Computational complexity: paper omits discussion of training/prediction cost for MOSM.  
- Parameter sensitivity: increased parameter count raises concerns about initialization and optimization not addressed.  
- Comparison scope: limited comparisons (e.g., effect of number of components Q and computational cost not explored).  
- Complex-valued GPs: implications and trade-offs of using complex-valued formulations are under-discussed.  
- Section 3.1 comment: choice of Q=1 appears arbitrary; need discussion on rank vs. expressiveness.  
- Eq. (9) comment: justification that wij ensures positive-definiteness is hand-wavy and needs rigor.  
- Climate dataset critique: highly similar signals reduce the impact of results; need a more challenging dataset.  
- Heavy metal dataset critique: mixed outcomes—good on Cadmium but no significance on Copper.  
- Future work: suggestion to develop sparse/inducing-variable implementations is good but needs detail on incorporating spectral information.  
- Requested revisions: expand experiments (harder datasets, Q sensitivity, statistical tests), discuss computational cost, analyze parameter sensitivity and initialization, and elaborate on complex-valued GP implications.
