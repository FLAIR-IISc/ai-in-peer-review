- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs by extending spectral mixture idea via Cramér’s theorem using complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- MOSM models dependencies between outputs (delays, phase shifts) with parameters that have clear spectral interpretations (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Theoretical basis is sound, with clear derivations and links to Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems.  
- Positions well relative to prior work, addressing limitations of LMC, CONV, and CSM by offering more flexible cross-covariance modeling.  
- Evaluation includes a synthetic recovery experiment and two real-world datasets (climate, heavy metals), compared against LMC, CONV, and CSM.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with consistent and easy-to-follow mathematical notation.  
- Strength: MOSM provides greater flexibility and expressiveness for multi-output dependencies than existing methods.  
- Strength: Parameters are interpretable in the spectral domain, aiding understanding and application.  
- Strength: Empirical results show MOSM performs well on both synthetic and real-world tasks.  
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability of MOSM are not discussed and likely higher due to extra flexibility.  
- Weakness: MOSM has many hyperparameters (especially with larger Q), risking sensitivity to initialization and optimization; practical tuning is underexplored.  
- Weakness: Statistical significance of experimental results is limited, demonstrated definitively for only one dataset/case.  
- Weakness: Connection to the broader literature on complex-valued Gaussian Processes is acknowledged but not fully explored.  
- Weakness: Synthetic experiments are narrow (derivative and delayed signal); more varied synthetic tests would strengthen validation.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically justified approach with clear advantages in flexibility and interpretability, backed by empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: Need for discussion on computational/hyperparameter issues, stronger statistical analysis, and clearer positioning relative to complex GP literature.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution to MOGPs; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss MOSM computational complexity and optimizations, provide more rigorous statistical analysis, expand connection to complex-valued GP methods, and add broader synthetic experiments.
