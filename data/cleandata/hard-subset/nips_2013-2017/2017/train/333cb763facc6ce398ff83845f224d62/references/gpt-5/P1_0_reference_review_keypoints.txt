- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs by designing cross-spectral density in the Fourier domain via Cramér’s theorem, yielding closed-form time-domain kernels.
- Constructs S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with complex Gaussian components to guarantee PSD per frequency and uses symmetrization to obtain real-valued kernels.
- MOSM models output-specific frequencies, lengthscales, inter-output phase shifts, and delays, subsuming SM-LMC and CSM as special cases.
- Experiments: strong synthetic 3-output task result; competitive and sometimes best performance on climate imputation and Jura heavy-metal datasets.
- Builds on Wilson & Adams’ SM and generalizes SM-LMC/CSM, relating to LMC and convolution-process constructions; related work is appropriately cited.
- Technical soundness: PSD ensured by factorization and symmetrization; closed-form inverse Fourier transform yields interpretable parameters.
- Kernel symmetry kij(τ)=kji(−τ) appears satisfied by parameter tying, though an explicit proof is recommended.
- Empirical claims: synthetic results compelling; real-data gains modest and limited to some cases (e.g., one climate case, Cadmium).
- Missing details: no description of initialization/optimization strategy, sensitivity to number of components Q, or identifiability/multimodality issues.
- No runtime or scalability analysis and no sparse/variational approximation provided.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized but has minor notational/typo issues and ambiguous sign/normalization conventions.
- Originality: principled and elegant extension of SM to multi-output via complex spectral factorization with nontrivial additions (per-output parameters and delays).
- Significance: useful for interpretable cross-output structure (phase/delay); stronger empirical and scalability evidence would increase impact.
- Pros: principled PSD construction, interpretability, closed-form kernels, strong synthetic demonstration, competitive real-data performance.
- Cons: limited discussion of optimization/initialization, no scalability analysis, modest real-data gains, minor clarity issues, and unaddressed identifiability.
- Key questions: initialization strategy, explicit symmetry proof, φ vs θ identifiability/trade-offs, computational overhead vs CSM/SM-LMC, and ablation results (no delay/phase).
- Suggested improvements: add initialization strategy (e.g., periodogram-based), ablation and identifiability studies, runtime/scaling comparisons or sparse version, clarify sign/constraints, and visualize learned cross-spectra/delays.
- Overall: solid, principled, and interpretable generalization of SM to MOGPs with compelling synthetic evidence; needs more clarity on initialization/identifiability and scalability.
- Recommendation: Weak accept; reviewer confidence: high.
