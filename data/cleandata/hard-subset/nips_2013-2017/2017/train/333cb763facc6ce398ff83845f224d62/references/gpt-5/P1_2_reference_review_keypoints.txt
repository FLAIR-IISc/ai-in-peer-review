- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs via a matrix-valued spectral density S(ω) constructed using Cramér’s theorem.  
- Factorizes cross-spectral density as S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω) with per-output complex spectral factors Ri(ω) having magnitude, center, bandwidth, delay θi, and phase φi.  
- Derives closed-form stationary cross-covariances kij(τ) with pairwise parameters induced from per-output parameters, supporting phase shifts and delays.  
- Construction guarantees positive definiteness by factorization and produces real-valued kernels by symmetrizing the spectrum.  
- Recovers SM-LMC when spectral parameters are tied across outputs and no phase/delay is used.  
- CSM is a restricted special case; MOSM adds explicit inter-output delays and per-output spectral parameters for richer cross-structures.  
- Contrasts with CONV and classical LMC which produce symmetric, centered cross-covariances and tie behavior across outputs.  
- Experiments: synthetic 3-output demo shows MOSM learns derivative and delay relations and good extrapolation.  
- Experiments: climate sensor imputation (four stations) shows MOSM is competitive.  
- Experiments: Jura heavy metals dataset yields MOSM outperforming competitors on Cadmium and competitive on Copper.  
- Implementation uses GPflow/TF and maximum likelihood training.  
- Theoretical quality: PSD argument via S=R^H R is sound; per-pair parameter formulas follow Gaussian product identities.  
- Theoretical caveat: conjugation in Sij(ω)=R:i(ω)H R:j(ω) should be made explicit to ensure Hermitian symmetry and correct θij/φij expressions.  
- Real-valuedness step (symmetrizing Sij(ω)) is plausible and preserves PSD, but paper should explicitly state S(-ω)=S(ω)* and show how mirrored Ri(ω) construction satisfies it.  
- Empirics critique: synthetic task compelling; real-data results are competitive but not uniformly superior and only MAE is reported.  
- Empirics critique: missing likelihood-based metrics, calibration, and limited details on initialization and sensitivity to number of components Q.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized with clear derivation and parameter interpretations but has notational/typographical issues to fix (conjugates, factors of 1/2, mirrored components, typos).  
- Originality: meaningful and novel extension of SM kernels to multi-output with per-output spectral parameters and explicit delays/phases.  
- Significance: useful for multi-channel time series and spatial-temporal modeling where delays/phases are interpretable; could benefit from sparse/variational extensions.  
- Pros: principled spectral construction, closed-form kernels with phase/delay, includes SM-LMC/CSM as special cases, convincing synthetic demo.  
- Cons: modest empirical evaluation (only MAE, limited datasets), large parameter count with limited discussion of identifiability/regularization/initialization, some theoretical details need precision, missing comparisons to multivariate Matérn.  
- Key suggestions: clarify initialization strategy (spectral estimates?), address identifiability between θ and φ and between μ and φ, provide scalability/overfitting analysis, report log-likelihood/calibration metrics, include multivariate Matérn baselines, and report learned delays/phases for interpretability.  
- Recommendation: technically solid and interpretable contribution; lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying conjugation/symmetry issues and strengthening experimental details on metrics, initialization, and scalability.
