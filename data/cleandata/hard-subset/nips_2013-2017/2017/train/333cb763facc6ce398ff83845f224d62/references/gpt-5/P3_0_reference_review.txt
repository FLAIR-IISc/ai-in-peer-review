Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a stationary, matrix-valued covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) constructed by designing a complex cross-spectral density matrix S(ω) and invoking Cramér’s theorem. The key idea is to factor S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω), with R(ω) having m columns (one per output) whose entries are complex Gaussian (SE) functions with channel-specific parameters: amplitude, mean frequency, bandwidth, delay, and phase. After symmetrizing S(ω) to ensure real-valued processes (S ← (S(ω) + S(−ω))/2), the inverse Fourier transform yields closed-form cross-covariances of the form exp(−1/2(τ+θij)TΣij(τ+θij)) cos((τ+θij)Tµij + φij). The construction generalizes the single-output SM kernel and subsumes prior multi-output spectral approaches (SM-LMC and CSM): SM-LMC is recovered when all channels share identical spectral parameters and CSM is recovered when phases are allowed but delays are tied.

The paper relates to: (a) SM kernels (Wilson & Adams), (b) multi-output kernels via LMC/CONV (Álvarez & Lawrence), and (c) cross-spectral multi-output constructions (Ulrich et al.). Compared to CSM, MOSM explicitly accommodates both phase and delay differences and allows channel-specific auto-covariances while preserving positive definiteness via the spectral factorization.

Quality
- Theory: The approach is well-motivated and leverages standard harmonic analysis results. The PSD factorization ensures S(ω) is PSD for every ω; the symmetrization guarantees real-valued processes. However, there are a few technical and notational issues that merit correction/clarification:
  1) Conjugation: If S(ω) = R(ω)H R(ω), then Sij(ω) = conj(Ri(ω)) Rj(ω). Some equations (e.g., Eq. 9) appear to omit the conjugate, although the derived phase relation θij = θi − θj and φij = φi − φj are consistent with the conjugate product. Please fix this inconsistency.
  2) Symmetrization: Averaging S(ω) and S(−ω) preserves PSD and yields real-valued kernels provided S(−ω) = S(ω)H. A brief proof or reference would improve rigor.
  3) Parameter interpretation: In Eq. (10), αij is nonnegative by construction (product of amplitudes times a Gaussian overlap term). The text later states “αij ∈ R, allowing positive/negative correlations,” which is misleading—the sign changes arise from the cosine, not αij itself.
  4) The derivation of Σij and µij from products of Gaussians in frequency is standard; giving the explicit Gaussian product identity or an appendix would aid reproducibility.
- Empirics: The synthetic experiment is illustrative and shows MOSM can capture derivative and delayed relations that are challenging for LMC/CONV. Real-data results on climate and Jura are competitive; MOSM is best in some cases (statistically significant for one climate sensor and Cadmium in Jura). However:
  - Initialization is crucial for SM-type models; the paper does not describe how channel-specific spectral means/covariances/phases/delays are initialized. Without this, reproducibility and practical guidance suffer.
  - Scalability is not addressed beyond small N; exact O(N3) inference was used. A complexity analysis and practical runtimes would be helpful.
  - Baselines: It would strengthen the case to include standard multi-task Matérn/ICM baselines and to separate SM-LMC from CSM more clearly, including parameter counts.

Clarity
The manuscript is generally clear, with helpful spectral interpretations (µ: frequency, Σ: bandwidth, φ: phase, θ: delay). Notation is mostly consistent, but there are several typos/mismatches:
- The missing conjugations in Sij.
- A dangling footnote marker (“choose2 Q=1”).
- A mismatch in related work: the text claims [17] uses t-Student cross-spectral densities, but the reference [17] corresponds to Gneiting et al. on multivariate Matérn kernels.
- Minor dimensional typos (e.g., “S(ω) ∈ Rm×m” when it is Hermitian complex-valued).
Please also clarify whether Eq. (8) uses the 1/2 factor in the exponent, and state explicitly the realness condition S(−ω) = S(ω)H.

Originality
The construction is a natural but nontrivial extension of SM ideas to multi-output via spectral factorization with complex per-channel components. The inclusion of explicit delay parameters (in addition to phase) within a PD multi-output spectral framework and the per-channel parameterization that yields O(m) parameters per component (instead of O(m2)) are notable. While related ideas exist (CSM, LMC/CONV), the proposed factorized complex SM with delay offers additional flexibility and clear interpretability.

Significance
MOSM is a useful addition to the MOGP kernel toolkit. Its strengths are interpretability, controlled parameterization per output, and the ability to model phase and delay simultaneously. The empirical gains are moderate on real data but convincing on the synthetic task designed to stress cross-relations beyond LMC. The approach should be of interest to researchers building expressive multi-task kernels and to practitioners modeling multi-sensor time series.

Pros
- Principled spectral-domain construction guaranteeing PD via factorization.
- Clear physical interpretation of parameters; supports phase and delay.
- Per-channel parameterization scales linearly with m per component.
- Closed-form time-domain kernels enable standard GP inference.
- Competitive or superior performance on synthetic and some real tasks.

Cons and questions
- Notational inconsistencies (conjugation) and a mis-citation need correction.
- No discussion of initialization strategies; SM models are sensitive to this.
- No complexity/runtime analysis; scalability not addressed beyond small N.
- Limited baselines (no multivariate Matérn/ICM), and modest gains on real data.
- Potential identifiability issues between phase φ and delay θ when Σij is broad; any regularization or priors?

Suggestions for improvement
- Correct the conjugation in Sij and the αij sign statement; fix [17] citation and footnote.
- Provide an initialization procedure (e.g., per-channel periodogram + GMM fit; cross-periodograms for relative phase/delay).
- Include complexity and runtime, and a brief discussion of sparse/inducing extensions (beyond the concluding remarks).
- Add ICM/multivariate Matérn baselines and report parameter counts.
- Comment on identifiability and practical constraints (e.g., tying φ or θ when frequencies are near zero).

Overall assessment
A solid, technically sound contribution that cleanly extends spectral mixture ideas to multi-output with added flexibility (delay + phase) and interpretable parameters. While some empirical gains are modest and clarity issues need fixing, the method is well-motivated and likely to be useful. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted issues. Confidence: high.
