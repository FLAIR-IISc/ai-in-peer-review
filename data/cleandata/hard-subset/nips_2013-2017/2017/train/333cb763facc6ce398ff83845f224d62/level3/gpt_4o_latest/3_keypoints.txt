- Introduces Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel by generalizing spectral mixture kernels via Bochner's and Cramér's theorems.  
- Proposes a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities enabling modeling of delays and phase differences between channels.  
- The approach provides a theoretically sound framework for designing covariance structures in MOGPs.  
- Interpretable parameters (e.g., delay, phase) offer physical insight into inter-output relationships.  
- Demonstrates ability to recover known auto- and cross-covariances on synthetic data.  
- Shows competitive performance on multiple real-world datasets against established models.  
- Well-written paper with clear theoretical exposition and illustrative examples.  
- Potential for MOSM to become a standard in MOGPs if parameter learning remains manageable.  
- Does not evaluate performance with limited/sparse training data, a common real-world scenario.  
- Lacks explicit analysis of handling correlations across channels with differing length-scales.  
- Computational complexity and scalability for large datasets are not discussed.  
- Reproducibility issue: number of training points in the synthetic example is not specified.  
- Contains minor editorial issues (missing words on lines 74 and 140).  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial contribution to MOGPs, with suggested future work to address noted weaknesses.
