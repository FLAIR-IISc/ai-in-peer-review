- Proposes a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance in the spectral domain, extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output settings.
- Tackles modeling of cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.
- Parameters have clear spectral interpretation: frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay per channel pair.
- Builds on Wilson & Adams (2013); relationship to prior work is well-explained.
- Method is novel and sufficiently original for publication.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and GP fundamentals.
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is clear and thorough.
- Technically sound and well-motivated.
- Experiments are adequate and show competitive mean absolute error.
- Demonstrates ability to model rich relationships across multiple outputs.
- Experiments provide limited intuition and could use more insightful visualizations/explanations.
- Jura dataset results are hard to interpret and need better visualization or explanation.
- Paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of implications and potential applications.
- Strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
