- Proposes MOSM kernel: extends spectral mixture kernel to multi-output GPs using parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to build a multivariate covariance.  
- Provides clear interpretation of inter-channel relationships in terms of frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough background and related-work review.  
- Approach is well-motivated and demonstrated effective on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: novel, expressive MOGP kernel capable of modeling complex cross-channel relationships.  
- Strength: principled kernel construction via Cramér's theorem.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation shows competitiveness with existing methods.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical analysis (e.g., universal approximation properties, connections to other MOGP kernels).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive with additional datasets and state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Weakness: limited interpretability examples and visualizations to illustrate how the kernel reveals channel relationships.  
- Overall: significant and potentially widely adoptable contribution, but additional theoretical and empirical work needed.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
