- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for multi-output Gaussian processes using cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem.
- Extends Wilson & Adams (2013) and Tobar et al. (2015) spectral mixture ideas to model multi-output covariances, including delays and phase differences.
- Motivates working in the spectral domain to simplify ensuring positive-definiteness of covariance functions.
- Provides a parametric interpretation linking kernel parameters to spectral properties (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).
- Solid theoretical grounding and principled framework for constructing MOGP kernels.
- Demonstrated on synthetic data to recover known auto- and cross-covariances, including delays.
- Evaluated on real-world datasets (climate signals, Jura metal concentrations) to show practical applicability.
- Strength: flexible and interpretable multi-output kernels with the capability to model inter-channel delays/phase.
- Weakness: extrapolation benefits of spectral mixture kernels are not explicitly demonstrated in the multi-output experiments.
- Weakness: experimental results do not convincingly show substantial improvement over existing methods (e.g., CSM).
- Weakness: statistical significance of performance differences is unclear; reported std. devs often overlap across models.
- Suggests need for more rigorous statistical analysis and clearer reporting of significance.
- Concern: Jura dataset results may favor MOSM’s expressiveness; dataset selection could be biased.
- Recommends including datasets where existing methods perform well or MOSM struggles for a balanced assessment.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing statistical analysis, demonstrating multi-output extrapolation, and discussing limitations.
