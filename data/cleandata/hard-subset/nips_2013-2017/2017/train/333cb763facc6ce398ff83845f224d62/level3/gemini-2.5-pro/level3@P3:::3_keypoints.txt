- Summary: Proposes the MOSM kernel for MOGPs, derived via Cramér's theorem by designing cross-spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforming to obtain guaranteed positive-definite kernels; generalizes CSM with interpretable spectral parameters and strong empirical performance.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation—principled derivation from Cramér's theorem ensuring mathematical validity.

- Strength: High expressiveness and interpretability—models phase differences and time delays with physically meaningful hyperparameters.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem—expands the limited set of flexible, valid MOGP kernels.

- Strength: Clear presentation and motivation with effective illustrations.

- Strength: Experimental validation shows MOSM captures complex relationships and performs well on synthetic and real datasets.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter optimization—no discussion of initialization, multimodality, sensitivity to restarts, or training strategies.

- Weakness: Unclear scalability and computational complexity with respect to number of outputs m and spectral components Q.

- Weakness: No analysis of performance in low-data regimes or risk of overfitting given the large number of parameters.

- Question: What initialization procedures and optimization strategies were used, and how challenging was training in practice?

- Question: How does the model handle channels with very different characteristics (e.g., long vs short length-scales) given the combined Σij formulation?

- Question: What is the total number of training observations used in the synthetic experiment (sum of N1, N2, N3)?

- Minor: Line 74 appears to be missing a word (possibly "is given by the spectral mixture kernel").

- Minor: Line 140 appears to be missing a word (possibly "is the inverse Fourier transform of a spectral density Sij").

- Overall: High-quality, original contribution with strong theory and results; recommended for acceptance pending more practical details on training and low-data behavior.
