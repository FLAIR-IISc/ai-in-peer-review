- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs by designing cross-spectral density S(ω) in the Fourier domain with PSD factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) to guarantee positive definiteness.  
- Models each column of R(ω) as a complex Gaussian with channel-specific magnitude, frequency mean, spectral covariance, phase, and delay, producing closed-form cross-covariances after symmetrization and inverse Fourier transform.  
- MOSM generalizes spectral mixture (SM) kernels to multi-output settings and subsumes SM-LMC and CSM as special cases while adding delays and channel-specific spectra.  
- Validated on synthetic trivariate data, climate station sensor-failure simulations, and the Jura heavy metal dataset, showing competitive or superior MAE and strong performance on synthetic and Cadmium prediction.  
- Strength: principled theoretical derivation using Cramér’s theorem and constructive PSD factorization ensuring positive definiteness.  
- Strength: interpretable parameters for frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay; can model non-symmetric cross-covariances and negative correlations.  
- Strength: expressive model allowing different auto-spectra per channel and generalizing prior SM-based MOGP kernels.  
- Strength: clear writing, useful illustrations, and a mix of synthetic and real-world experiments.  
- Strength: high potential impact if optimization is tractable; could become a standard multi-output kernel family.  
- Weakness: optimization details (initialization, priors/constraints, identifiability among per-channel parameters) are sparse and not addressed.  
- Weakness: scalability and computational complexity with total observations and number of components Q are not discussed; sparse/inducing guidance deferred.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to MAE; lacks predictive log-likelihood, calibration metrics, and ablation studies isolating delays/phases.  
- Weakness: construction yields sum of rank-1 spectral components (Q terms) but discussion of effective rank, Q selection, and practical implications is limited.  
- Quality: technically sound derivation and correct use of theory, but empirical claims would be stronger with more metrics, ablations, and clarity on optimization.  
- Clarity suggestion: expand Section 3.2 with practical training details (parameterizations, constraints, initialization heuristics, early stopping/regularization).  
- Reproducibility suggestion: report number of training points per channel and total; clarify complexity in terms of channels, Q, and total data; note special cases for aligned inputs.  
- Minor: fix typos around lines ~74 and ~140.  
- Originality: novel and flexible extension of SM kernels to multi-output via factorized complex PSD construction enabling phase and delay with channel-specific spectra.  
- Significance: potentially high for sensor networks, spatiotemporal modeling, and multi-task regression if training is robust.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled, interpretable, general kernel family that subsumes prior SM-based MOGP kernels and shows solid empirical performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: practical hurdles due to limited optimization/scalability guidance, incomplete evaluation, and brief discussion of rank/Q selection and identifiability.  
- Key questions: behavior in low-data regimes, recommendation for priors/regularization, handling heterogeneous channel length-scales, and observed optimization pathologies.  
- Key questions: detailed initialization strategy (e.g., periodogram/CSD peaks), how Q is selected (BIC/AIC/validation), and training complexity/memory as a function of m, Q, and N.  
- Suggested additions: ablations (MOSM vs CSM vs MOSM without delays/phases), sensitivity to Q, low-data experiments, heterogeneous length-scale tests, and reporting of NLL/calibration metrics.  
- Practical suggestions: provide code, initialization recipes, and consider priors/reparameterizations (log-Cholesky for Σ, bounded phases) to stabilize training and discuss identifiability.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward accept, contingent on stronger optimization/scalability discussion and additional ablations/metrics.
