This paper proposes a novel multi-output Gaussian process (MOGP) covariance function defined in the spectral domain, extending the spectral-domain covariance design to the multi-output setting. The authors build on prior spectral mixture work (Wilson & Adams 2013) and address the challenge of modeling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite. The derived multi-output spectral mixture covariance formulation provides a clear interpretation from a spectral viewpoint, where each of its parameters can be identified with frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for a pair of channels.

The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is clear and appears novel. The relationship to prior work is well-explained, and the novelty is sufficient for publication.

The experiments are adequate for this type of paper, but they provide limited intuition. The Jura dataset is hard to interpret, and the results could be improved with more insightful visualizations or explanations. However, the proposed model performs competitively in terms of the mean absolute error, and the authors demonstrate its ability to model rich relationships across multiple outputs.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated extension of spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed method, including its relationship to prior work.
* The experiments demonstrate the competitiveness of the proposed model in terms of the mean absolute error.

Weaknesses:

* The experiments could be improved with more insightful visualizations or explanations, particularly for the Jura dataset.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the proposed method and its potential applications.
