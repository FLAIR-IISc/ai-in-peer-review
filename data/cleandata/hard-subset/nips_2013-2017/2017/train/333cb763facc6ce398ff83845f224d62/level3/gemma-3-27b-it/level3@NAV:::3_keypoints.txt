- Introduces a novel covariance structure for MOGPs by extending the spectral (Bochner) approach.
- Proposes a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and uses Cramér’s Theorem to construct interpretable covariances modeling delays and phase differences.
- Fills a gap in effective and interpretable covariance design for MOGPs, a field with limited existing methods.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, building intuition from single-output spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting.
- Illustrations and parameter choices are relevant and aid understanding of the method’s capabilities.
- Theoretical grounding via Cramér’s Theorem is solid.
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates effectiveness.
- Synthetic experiments highlight ability to learn derivatives and delayed signals, a strength over simpler models.
- Key weakness: limited discussion of parameter learning and potential optimization challenges for complex kernels.
- Question: how does the method perform with limited training data / scarce samples?
- Question: how does the model handle correlations between channels with drastically different length-scales?
- Minor comment: two missing words noted on lines 74 and 140.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses a challenging problem, clearly written, and empirically effective.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of parameter learning challenges and lack of analysis for limited-data or varying length-scale scenarios.
- Overall assessment: strong paper leaning toward acceptance, contingent on better discussion and investigation of parameter learning and behavior under different data conditions.
