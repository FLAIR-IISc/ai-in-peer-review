- Proposes a multi-output spectral mixture (MOSM) kernel extending Wilson & Adams (2013) to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Uses Cramér's theorem to design matrix-valued covariance functions via complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Models cross-spectral densities as complex-valued squared-exponential functions to capture delays and phase differences across channels.  
- Ensures positive definiteness through a rank-one decomposition of the spectral density matrix.  
- Paper is clear and well-motivated, with strong theoretical derivation and mathematical justification.  
- Thoroughly discusses related work (especially Cross-Spectral Mixture) and clearly states the novelty of incorporating delays and phase shifts.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound and derivations are technically correct.  
- Synthetic experiments demonstrate the model’s ability to capture derivative and delayed relationships.  
- Real-world experiments (climate data, Jura) offer limited interpretation and unclear reasons for performance differences.  
- Jura dataset shows only marginal improvement over CSM and lacks sufficient statistical significance testing.  
- Overall significance is moderate with useful potential applications; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve experiment interpretation and statistical validation.
