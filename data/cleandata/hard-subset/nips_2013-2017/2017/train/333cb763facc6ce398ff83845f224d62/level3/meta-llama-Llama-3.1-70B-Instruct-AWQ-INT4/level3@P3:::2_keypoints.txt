- Proposes a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that extends spectral mixture ideas to multi-output Gaussian processes via cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforms.  
- MOSM offers an interpretable spectral viewpoint where parameters map to frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay between channel pairs.  
- Unique capability to jointly model delays and phase differences across outputs.  
- Technical contribution is sound with a clear theoretical foundation and thorough methodological explanation.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides concise background and related work.  
- Experimental evaluation compares MOSM to existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets and reports competitive mean absolute error (MAE).  
- Evaluation appears thorough but lacks detailed statistical significance testing of performance differences.  
- Paper notes MOSM can be computationally expensive but does not analyze computational complexity in detail.  
- Paper could benefit from a more explicit discussion of limitations and practical constraints.  
- Originality: MOSM is a novel approach that adds expressive, interpretable modeling capabilities for multi-output correlations.  
- Significance: MOSM meaningfully advances MOGP modeling by enabling more expressive cross-output relationships.  
- Quality: Overall high quality—sound methodology, clear presentation, and adequate experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, interpretable contribution with competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omissions in complexity analysis, limitations discussion, and statistical significance evaluation.
