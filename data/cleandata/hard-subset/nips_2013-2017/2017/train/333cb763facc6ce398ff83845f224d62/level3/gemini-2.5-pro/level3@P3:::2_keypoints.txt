- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, generalizing spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs via Cramér's theorem.  
- Constructs a parametric cross-spectral density matrix from complex-valued spectral factors, guaranteeing positive-definiteness.  
- MOSM models frequency, lengthscale, interpretable delays (θij) and phase shifts (φij), offering more flexibility than LMC and CSM.  
- Theoretical contribution is original and elegant, providing a principled spectral-domain construction that avoids PD issues in the time domain.  
- Explicit delay and phase parameters enhance interpretability of inter-output relationships.  
- Synthetic experiment is well-designed and convincingly shows MOSM can learn relationships between a function, its derivative, and a delayed copy where simpler models fail.  
- Real-world evaluations (weather and Swiss Jura datasets) are less convincing: MOSM performs competitively but shows no clear, statistically significant advantage over CSM/SM-LMC.  
- Paper should demonstrate MOSM’s utility on datasets with complex, time-lagged dependencies (e.g., econometrics, neuroscience, multi-sensor systems).  
- Missing a multi-output long-range periodic extrapolation experiment analogous to the original SM kernel’s showcase.  
- Overall: solid, original, and theoretically sound contribution, but practical impact is weakened by limited experimental demonstrations.
