- Proposes STAR-T, a robust submodular streaming algorithm for monotone submodular maximization under cardinality constraints with element removals; uses partitioning and exponentially decreasing thresholds.  
- Introduces STAR-T-GREEDY, a simple greedy post-removal algorithm with a constant-factor approximation guarantee.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and theoretical guarantees.  
- Provides extensive experimental evaluation on two real-world data summarization tasks demonstrating practical effectiveness.  

Strengths:
- Novel algorithm addressing an important practical problem (robust submodular streaming).  
- Strong theoretical foundation including constant-factor approximation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on real datasets.  

Weaknesses:
- Algorithm depends on parameter τ, which requires knowledge of f(OPT(k, V \ E)).  
- Lacks comparisons to other robust submodular optimization algorithms in experiments.  
- Limited analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, practically important contribution.  
- Solid theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical results support effectiveness.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- τ-dependence may limit practical applicability.  
- Missing experimental comparisons reduce impact.  
- Scalability concerns due to limited complexity/memory analysis.  

Recommendation:
- Accept, but request addressing τ-dependence, include comparisons with other robust methods, and provide detailed complexity and memory analysis in revision.
