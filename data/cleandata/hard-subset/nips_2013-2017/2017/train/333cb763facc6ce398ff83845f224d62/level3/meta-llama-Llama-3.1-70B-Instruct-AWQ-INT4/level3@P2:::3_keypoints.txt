- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output spectral mixture kernel built from a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem.  
- The MOSM kernel can model inter-channel delays and phase differences while providing a full parametric interpretation of cross-channel relationships.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and an effective background review of GPs, spectral mixture kernels, and MOGPs.  
- Method presentation is thorough, with clear explanations of kernel design and properties, supported by illustrations and examples.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets show MOSM can learn complex multi-output relationships and often outperform LMC and CONV models.  
- Strengths include clarity, strong illustrations, comprehensive related-work positioning, and a theoretically principled approach.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on parameter learning and optimization (NLL minimization), missing discussion of training challenges and algorithms.  
- Concern about sensitivity to hyperparameter choices and the optimization procedure, which may affect practical performance.  
- Missing evaluation of robustness to small sample sizes—unclear how MOSM performs with few training points.  
- Unaddressed question of how MOSM handles correlations between channels with very different length-scales.  
- Overall assessment: significant and original contribution to MOGP modeling with solid empirical support.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.
