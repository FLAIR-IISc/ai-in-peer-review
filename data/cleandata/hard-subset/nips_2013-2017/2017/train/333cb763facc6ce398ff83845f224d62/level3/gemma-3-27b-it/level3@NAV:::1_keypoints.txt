Novel extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér’s Theorem.  
Constructs parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities for expressive, interpretable MOGP covariances.  
Effectively handles positive-definiteness while allowing modeling of non-positive-definite cross-covariances.  
Builds logically on Wilson & Adams (2013) and prior MOGP approaches (LMC, convolution), situating the contribution well.  
Enables modeling of delays and phase differences between channels not captured by simpler methods.  
Solid theoretical grounding with clear mathematical explanations.  
Excellent, well-motivated writing and presentation.  
Experiments demonstrate feasibility but have limited interpretability.  
Jura dataset results are hard to interpret; synthetic example shows delay/phase recovery but lacks real-world complexity.  
Experiments need more complex datasets/scenarios to fully showcase MOSM advantages.  
Overall valuable and novel contribution; recommendation for acceptance despite experimental limitations.
