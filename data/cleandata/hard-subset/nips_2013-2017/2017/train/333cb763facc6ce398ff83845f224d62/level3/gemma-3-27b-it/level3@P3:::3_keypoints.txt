- Introduces MOSM kernel: a parametric complex-valued cross-spectral density approach for MOGPs using Cramér’s Theorem.
- Extends spectral (Bochner) methods to model delays and phase differences between outputs.
- Claims increased expressiveness and interpretability compared to LMC and CSM.
- Well-motivated, addressing the challenge of designing informative, interpretable cross-covariances.
- Theoretical foundation is strong and grounded in Cramér’s Theorem.
- MOSM parameters (delay, phase, magnitudes) have clear spectral interpretations.
- Can capture delays and phase shifts not easily handled by prior methods.
- Paper is clearly written with useful illustrations that aid understanding.
- Experimental evaluation includes comparisons to CONV, SM-LMC, and CSM on synthetic and real datasets.
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance across datasets.
- Paper lacks detail on parameter learning and optimization challenges for the larger parameter set.
- Scalability and computational complexity for large datasets are not discussed despite TensorFlow/GPflow implementation.
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity; experiments rely on default or tuned settings without deeper sensitivity study.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses an important problem, clear exposition, and promising results.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient detail about optimization difficulties and computational scalability.
- Question: How does the method perform with few training points and can it estimate many parameters with limited data?
- Question: How well does the model handle channels with very different length-scales?
- Question: What optimization algorithms, initialization, regularization, and computational costs were used and how do they compare to other methods?
- Minor: state exact number of training points in the synthetic example.
- Minor: fix missing words in lines 74 and 140.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with potential to become a standard approach if parameter learning is manageable; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing optimization and scalability concerns.
