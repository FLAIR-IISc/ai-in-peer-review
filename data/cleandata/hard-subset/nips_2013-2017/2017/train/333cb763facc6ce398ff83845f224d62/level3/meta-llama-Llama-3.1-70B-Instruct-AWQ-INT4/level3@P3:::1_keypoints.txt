- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output Gaussian processes.
- Introduces a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and constructs a multivariate covariance via Cramér's Theorem.
- MOSM models delays and phase differences between channels, increasing expressiveness and interpretability.
- Provides a detailed derivation of the MOSM kernel with a clear spectral interpretation of parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).
- Uses a factor decomposition of the spectral density matrix to ensure positive definiteness while allowing non-positive-definite cross-covariances.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough explanations.
- Experimental results are encouraging: MOSM performs competitively on synthetic and two real-world datasets.
- Demonstrates MOSM’s ability to model complex inter-channel relationships, delays, and phase differences better than prior approaches.
- Jura dataset used is difficult to interpret, limiting generalizability of results.
- Lacks a more detailed comparison with other MOGP models and discussion of their relative advantages/disadvantages.
- Would benefit from additional experiments across a wider range of datasets for more comprehensive evaluation.
- Originality rated high: MOSM is a novel and significant extension of spectral mixture concepts to MOGPs.
- Significance rated high: potential impact on climate modeling, signal processing, geostatistics, and other multivariate applications.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, models delays/phase, clear parameter interpretation, encouraging experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: dataset interpretability concerns, insufficient comparative analysis, need for broader experiments.
