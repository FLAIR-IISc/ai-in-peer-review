- Proposes extending the spectral mixture (SM) kernel to multi-output GPs by defining cross-spectral densities and using Cramér’s theorem to derive multivariate covariances.  
- Enables modeling of delays and phase differences between outputs, addressing limitations of existing MOGP approaches like LMC.  
- Builds on prior SM kernel work and situates contribution clearly within MOGP literature.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong and principled, leveraging Cramér’s theorem to guarantee valid covariance functions.  
- Offers increased expressiveness and interpretability for modeling complex inter-output relationships.  
- Experimental evaluation covers synthetic, climate, and geological datasets, demonstrating method applicability.  
- Synthetic example shows learning of derivatives and delays but lacks a direct extrapolation comparison to original SM kernel results.  
- Empirical performance is largely comparable to existing methods, with only one notable improvement (Jura Cadmium) and limited statistical significance elsewhere.  
- Key weakness: no compelling demonstration of superior extrapolation capabilities relative to competing MOGP methods.  
- Scalability and practical impact could be improved via sparse implementations, as acknowledged by the authors.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance given the solid theoretical contribution, contingent on addressing extrapolation demonstrations and strengthening statistical analysis.  
- Suggests improving impact by evaluating on datasets where current MOGP models struggle or by providing more robust significance testing.
