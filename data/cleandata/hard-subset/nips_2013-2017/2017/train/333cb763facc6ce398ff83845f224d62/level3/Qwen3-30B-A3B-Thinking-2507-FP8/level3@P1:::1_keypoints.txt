- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture GPs to multi-output settings using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to ensure positive definiteness.  
- MOSM models delays, phase shifts, and distinct spectral content per channel, generalizing CSM by allowing distinct parameters for each channel pair.  
- Theoretical derivation is thorough and technically sound, showing how complex cross-spectra transform into valid covariance functions via inverse Fourier transforms.  
- Guarantees positive definiteness through factor decomposition of the spectral density matrix and a principled multivariate construction.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with a comprehensive background and clear relation to prior work and citations.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly demonstrate MOSM’s unique capabilities (modeling derivatives and delayed signals) that other models cannot capture.  
- Real-world results are mixed: only marginal improvement over CSM on climate data (signals highly correlated) and unclear interpretability on the Jura heavy-metal dataset (better on Cadmium but not Copper).  
- No computational complexity or scalability analysis provided, raising concerns for large datasets.  
- Limited discussion on practical guidelines for when MOSM is preferable to simpler alternatives.  
- Overall significance is high for the GP community due to interpretability and ability to model complex inter-channel temporal relationships.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — novel and theoretically solid contribution despite experimental weaknesses.
