- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- The construction is theoretically grounded in Cramér’s theorem, ensuring positive definiteness.  
- MOSM parameters are interpretable (frequency, magnitude, phase, and delays), enabling modeling of inter-output delays.  
- MOSM generalizes the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel and offers a more flexible cross-covariance framework.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows competitive or superior performance and recovers known dependencies in synthetic tests.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical derivations and informative figures.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical parameter learning and optimization strategies (initialization, computational cost).  
- Potential training difficulties and sensitivity with limited data due to increased parameter count are not sufficiently addressed.  
- Unclear how MOSM handles outputs with significantly different length-scales or heterogeneous temporal behavior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, interpretable, empirically validated, and clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient detail on parameter learning/complexity and untested behavior for limited data or varying length-scales.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address training/scalability concerns.  
- Minor comments: missing word at lines 74 and 140; synthetic example should state the number of training points.
