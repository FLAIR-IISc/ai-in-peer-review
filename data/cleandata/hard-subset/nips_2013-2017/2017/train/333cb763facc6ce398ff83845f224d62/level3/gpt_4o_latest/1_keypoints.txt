- Proposes a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for multi-output GPs designed in the spectral domain.
- Extends single-output spectral mixture kernels to multi-output settings using Cramér's theorem.
- Introduces a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays, phase differences, and varying auto-covariance across channels.
- Provides a principled, interpretable framework for constructing multivariate covariance functions and cross-covariances.
- Theoretical presentation and spectral interpretation of parameters are clear and rigorously motivated.
- Situates MOSM relative to prior methods (LMC, CONV, CSM) and demonstrates that MOSM generalizes existing approaches.
- Synthetic experiments effectively show MOSM can capture complex cross-covariances, including delays and phase shifts.
- Real-world experiments (climate and heavy metals) show competitive performance, notably for Cadmium prediction.
- Experiments lack deeper intuitive analysis explaining when and why MOSM outperforms other methods, e.g., on the Jura dataset.
- Paper does not discuss computational complexity compared to simpler models (SM-LMC, CONV).
- Absence of a sparse implementation limits scalability and applicability to large datasets.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an underexplored problem, offers a novel and interpretable kernel, and shows effective empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental insight and missing scalable (sparse) implementation.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve experimental analysis and address scalability concerns.
