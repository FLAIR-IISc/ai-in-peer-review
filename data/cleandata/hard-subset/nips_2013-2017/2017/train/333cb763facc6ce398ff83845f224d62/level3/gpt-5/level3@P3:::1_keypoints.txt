- Summary: Proposes a principled spectral-domain construction for multi-output GP kernels (MOSM) using Cramér’s theorem and parametric complex cross-spectral densities, yielding interpretable time-domain cross-covariances with closed-form inverse Fourier pairs.

- Strength: Elegant, principled extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs via spectral-domain reasoning.

- Strength: Expressive, interpretable cross-covariances that encode magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, and delay (allowing delayed/out-of-phase/negative correlations).

- Strength: Positive definiteness enforced correctly by ensuring S(ω) is Hermitian PSD via factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).

- Strength: Time-domain kernels remain closed-form (Gaussian times cosine), preserving SM tractability.

- Strength: Careful relation to prior work; SM-LMC and CSM arise as special cases under parameter tying.

- Strength: Clear writing, background, and spectral-parameter intuition.

- Weakness: Limited experimental gains on real datasets (modest improvements over strong baselines, especially CSM) and hard-to-interpret Jura results.

- Weakness: No ablation studies to isolate effects of delay, phase, per-output spectra, or rank Q.

- Weakness: Identifiability and symmetry issues (θij=θi−θj, φij=φi−φj) not discussed; multiple parameterizations yield identical cross-covariances.

- Weakness: Scalability and parameter efficiency concerns (parameter count grows with m×Q×(3n+2), training O(N^3)); little discussion of runtime, initialization, or regularization.

- Weakness: Exposition focuses on rank-one components; lacks empirical analysis of the need for higher rank and its stability effects.

- Technical: Construction and derivations are correct and sound; allowance for non-symmetric, non-PD cross-covariances is important.

- Technical: Experiments support claims on synthetic delay/derivative task; real-data support is competitive but limited; missing optimization/parameterization details and sensitivity analysis.

- Clarity: Very well written and organized; related work and spectral intuition are clear; minor suggestion to clarify K(τ) symmetry across outputs.

- Originality: Principled and more general spectral mixture extension to MOGPs with explicit delay/phase modeling and closed-form time-domain covariances.

- Significance: Conceptually important for multi-channel time series/spatio-temporal modeling where delays and phase shifts matter; practical impact promising but under-demonstrated.

- Suggestions: Add ablations (no delay/phase, tied μ/Σ, vary rank Q) and visualize learned delays/phases on real data.

- Suggestions: Report optimization/initialization details, parameter counts, runtimes, and discuss scalability or sparse/variational extensions.

- Suggestions: Address identifiability/invariances, regularization, and parameterizations for positivity/stability.

- Pros for acceptance: Principled, general, interpretable spectral-domain MOGP construction with clear relation to prior work.

- Cons against acceptance: Modest real-data gains, limited interpretability there, and missing ablations/scalability/identifiability discussion.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel, clean, and meaningful but should be strengthened with ablations, interpretability analyses, and scalability/identifiability discussion.

- Reviewer confidence: High (familiar with GP kernels, multi-output models, and spectral methods).
