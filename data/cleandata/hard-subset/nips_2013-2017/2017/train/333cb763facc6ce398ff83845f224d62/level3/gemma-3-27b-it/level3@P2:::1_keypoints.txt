- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel to design MOGP covariances directly in the spectral domain.  
- Uses Cramér’s Theorem to construct parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities ensuring valid cross-covariances.  
- Extends single-output spectral mixture ideas to multi-output settings, enabling modeling of delays and phase differences between channels.  
- Addresses positive definiteness while maintaining expressive cross-covariance modeling.  
- Clearly motivates limitations of prior approaches (LMC, CONV, CSM) and positions MOSM as a more principled, flexible alternative.  
- Theoretical derivation is meticulous, leveraging Bochner’s and Cramér’s Theorems with clear spectral-parameter interpretation.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and integrates theory with practical considerations.  
- Novelty and theoretical soundness represent a significant contribution to Gaussian process modeling.  
- Experiments show feasibility but are not fully convincing: synthetic example is contrived.  
- Real-world experiments (climate, heavy metals, Jura) provide limited or non-significant improvements and are hard to interpret.  
- No ablation study to isolate contributions of delay, phase, and other MOSM components.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for high-dimensional outputs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically robust, clear presentation, addresses an important MOGP challenge, and demonstrates feasibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental evidence not overwhelmingly convincing, limited insights from datasets, missing ablation and scalability analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to expand experimental evaluation, include ablation studies, and analyze computational characteristics.
