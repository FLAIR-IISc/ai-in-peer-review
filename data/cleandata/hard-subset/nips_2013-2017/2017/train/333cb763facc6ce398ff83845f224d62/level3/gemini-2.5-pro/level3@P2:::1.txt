**Summary**

This paper introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a novel and expressive covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs). The key idea is to extend the single-output Spectral Mixture (SM) kernel of Wilson & Adams (2013) to the multi-output setting. The authors take a principled approach by designing a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain. By invoking Cramér's Theorem (the multivariate generalization of Bochner's Theorem), they derive the corresponding matrix-valued covariance function in the time/space domain. This construction elegantly guarantees the required positive definiteness of the MOGP kernel. The resulting MOSM kernel is highly flexible, capable of modeling not only channel-specific frequencies and lengthscales but also delays and phase differences between output channels, which are properties not fully captured by previous MOGP methods like the Linear Model of Coregionalization (LMC) or the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel. The paper validates the model on a synthetic task designed to highlight its strengths and compares it against existing methods on two real-world datasets.

**Strengths (Arguments for acceptance)**

*   **Principled and Novel Formulation:** The paper's core contribution is its principled design of a multi-output kernel in the spectral domain. Using Cramér's Theorem and a rank-Q decomposition of the spectral density matrix is an elegant and theoretically sound way to ensure the positive definiteness of the resulting kernel. This approach is a significant and novel extension of the popular spectral mixture framework to the challenging multi-output domain.
*   **High Expressiveness and Interpretability:** The derived MOSM kernel is very expressive. Unlike many MOGP models that impose strong structural assumptions (e.g., forcing channels to share lengthscales), MOSM allows for channel-specific parameters. Crucially, it introduces dedicated parameters for modeling delays and phase shifts between channels, which is a key feature missing or handled in a more ad-hoc manner in prior work like CSM. The parameters all have a clear interpretation in the spectral domain, which is a major advantage of this family of kernels.
*   **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear background on single-output SM kernels and MOGPs before methodically developing their own model. The relationship to and improvements over prior work, particularly the CSM kernel (Ulrich et al., 2015), are explained very well, making the paper's contribution easy to appreciate.
*   **Effective Synthetic Experiment:** The synthetic experiment involving a function, its derivative, and a delayed version is perfectly chosen. It directly showcases the unique capabilities of the MOSM kernel to learn complex, non-trivial cross-covariances that models based on linear combinations of latent processes would fail to capture. The results in Figure 2 and Table 1 are a convincing demonstration of the model's power.

**Weaknesses (Arguments against acceptance)**

*   **Limited Intuition from Real-World Experiments:** While the real-world experiments show that MOSM is competitive with or better than state-of-the-art methods, they do not fully leverage the model's unique strengths. On the climate data, the signals are highly correlated, and the performance is similar to CSM. The Jura dataset is a standard benchmark, but it is unclear whether the complex relationships (delays, phase shifts) that MOSM is designed to capture are present in this spatial dataset. An application to a spatio-temporal or multi-sensor time series problem where such phenomena are known to exist would have been more compelling.
*   **Practical Challenges Not Discussed:** The MOSM kernel introduces a large number of hyperparameters, scaling with the number of outputs and spectral components. Optimizing these parameters can be a significant practical challenge, as the likelihood surface is often multimodal. A brief discussion of the optimization strategy, initialization, or potential difficulties would have made the work more complete for practitioners looking to apply this model.

**Evaluation**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical development based on Cramér's theorem is solid and the derivations appear correct. The experimental methodology is sound, and the authors are honest about the results, for example by using statistical tests to assess significance. This is a complete and well-executed piece of research.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is written with excellent clarity. The structure is logical, the notation is consistent, and the exposition of both background material and the proposed method is exemplary. It provides sufficient detail for an expert to understand and likely reproduce the work.
*   **Originality:** The work is highly original. While it builds on the SM kernel, its extension to the multi-output setting via the proposed spectral density matrix formulation is novel and non-trivial. It clearly distinguishes itself from related work like CSM by offering a more general and principled framework.
*   **Significance:** The contribution is significant. MOGPs are an important tool in machine learning, and the design of expressive and interpretable kernels is a key challenge. MOSM provides a powerful new tool for this purpose. Its ability to explicitly model delays and phase shifts from first principles is likely to be very useful for researchers and practitioners in fields like spatio-temporal modeling, econometrics, and multi-sensor fusion. This work is likely to be built upon in the future.

**Overall Recommendation**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel, theoretically principled, and highly expressive kernel for multi-output Gaussian processes. The clarity of the writing and the strength of the theoretical contribution are major highlights. While the real-world experiments could have been chosen to better showcase the model's unique capabilities, the synthetic experiment is very convincing, and the overall performance is competitive. The strengths of this work far outweigh its minor weaknesses. I strongly recommend acceptance.
