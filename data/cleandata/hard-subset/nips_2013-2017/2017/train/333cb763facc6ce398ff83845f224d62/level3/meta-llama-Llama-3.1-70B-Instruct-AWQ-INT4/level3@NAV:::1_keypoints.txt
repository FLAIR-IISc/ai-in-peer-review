- Proposes a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- MOSM can model delays, phase shifts, and inter-channel correlations, increasing expressiveness over existing MOGP approaches.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, background, and motivation.  
- The relationship to prior work is well-explained and the novelty is sufficient for publication.  
- Technical derivation of the MOSM kernel is thorough and theoretically sound.  
- Experiments include a synthetic example and two real-world datasets, showing competitive or superior performance in some cases.  
- Originality and significance are high, addressing a challenging problem with a broadly applicable solution.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.  
- Con: Experiments are limited to only two real-world datasets.  
- Con: More comparisons with existing models would strengthen validation.  
- Con: Results on the Jura dataset are hard to interpret and presentation could be clearer.  
- Suggestion: Add more comparisons with baseline models.  
- Suggestion: Provide more experimental details (hyperparameters, computational resources).  
- Suggestion: Validate on additional datasets and offer better interpretation of Jura results.
