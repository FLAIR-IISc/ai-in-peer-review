- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending single-output SM to multi-output GPs via a spectral-domain construction.
- Uses a principled parametric complex-valued cross-spectral density and Cramér's Theorem to guarantee positive-definite matrix-valued covariance functions.
- MOSM models channel-specific frequencies and lengthscales and explicitly captures delays and phase differences between outputs.
- Validated on a synthetic task designed to highlight MOSM’s strengths and compared to existing methods on two real-world datasets.
- Strength: Principled and novel formulation using rank-Q spectral density decomposition ensures theoretical soundness.
- Strength: High expressiveness and interpretability with clear spectral-domain parameters for delays and phase shifts.
- Strength: Paper is very clear and well-organized, with thorough background and relation to prior work (e.g., CSM).
- Strength: Synthetic experiment convincingly demonstrates MOSM’s ability to learn complex cross-covariances.
- Weakness: Real-world experiments do not fully showcase MOSM’s unique capabilities; datasets may not contain delays/phase effects.
- Weakness: Practical challenges—large number of hyperparameters and multimodal likelihood—are not sufficiently discussed.
- Evaluation: Technically sound derivations, honest and statistically tested experiments, and reproducible-level detail.
- Evaluation: Excellent clarity, consistent notation, and comprehensive exposition.
- Evaluation: Highly original extension of SM to multi-output setting, distinct from prior work like CSM.
- Evaluation: Significant contribution to MOGPs with potential impact in spatio-temporal and multi-sensor applications.
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with minor weaknesses; author recommends acceptance.
