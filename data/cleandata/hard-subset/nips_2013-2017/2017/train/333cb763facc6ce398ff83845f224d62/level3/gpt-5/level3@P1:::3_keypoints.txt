- Proposes MOSM kernel for MOGPs via complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem, factoring S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).
- Inverse Fourier transform yields closed-form cross-covariances capturing frequency, magnitude, per-channel lengthscales, phase shifts, and explicit delays.
- Strictly generalizes SM-LMC and CSM (SM-LMC by tying parameters; CSM as no-delay/shared-spectrum special case).
- Theoretical development is sound; positive definiteness follows from spectral factorization and Cramér’s theorem.
- Time-domain kernel is closed-form, enabling exact GP inference (subject to cubic scaling).
- Parameters (µ, Σ, φ, θ) are interpretable and link spectral factors explicitly to cross-covariances.
- Experiments: synthetic derivative/delay task effectively highlights phase/delay benefits.
- Real-data experiments (climate sensors, Jura) show competitive or improved accuracy with statistical testing where applicable.
- Paper lacks detail on optimization: initialization, parameter constraints, identifiability (phase vs delay), learning dynamics, and failure modes.
- Spectral mixtures’ sensitivity to initialization and local optima demands more guidance and diagnostics.
- No runtime or scaling analysis; sparse/variational approximations are not evaluated in experiments.
- Clarity: well written and organized; spectral construction and interpretability communicated effectively.
- Minor clarity improvements recommended: summarize training hyperparameters and make training sizes prominent in captions.
- Typos present: missing word(s) around lines 74 and 140.
- Originality: novel, nontrivial extension enabling channel-specific spectra and delays while preserving closed forms and PD guarantees.
- Significance: valuable for multi-sensor/time-series/spatio-temporal modeling; could become a standard tool if optimization proves robust.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled construction, interpretability, generality over prior methods, strong synthetic validation, competitive real-data results.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited optimization discussion, no runtime/scaling analysis, modest real-world gains in some cases, limited ablations on rank Q and parameter tying.
- Key question: how does MOSM behave with few training points per channel and does per-channel parameterization risk overfitting—recommend priors/regularization?
- Key question: does Σij = 2 Σi(Σi+Σj)−1Σj producing narrower cross-covariances limit modeling when channels have very different lengthscales?
- Key question: how sensitive is training to initialization of µ, Σ, φ, θ; are periodograms/cross-periodograms or annealing used to reduce local optima?
- Key question: impact of increasing rank Q on performance and optimization; when does tying parameters improve robustness?
- Key question: computational cost versus SM-LMC/CSM and prospects for sparse/variational MOSM (e.g., inter-domain Fourier features)?
- Suggestions: add an optimization section (parameterizations, constraints, priors, initialization heuristics), ablations on Q and parameter tying, and runtime/memory comparisons.
- Suggestions: include a small-sample experiment to assess robustness/overfitting and make training set sizes and standard deviations explicit in figures.
- Minor request: clarify the symmetrization step Sij(ω) ← 0.5[Sij(ω)+Sij(−ω)] and its effect on parameters.
