This paper proposes a novel approach to modeling multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) using spectral mixture kernels. The authors extend the spectral mixture concept to the multi-output setting, allowing for the modeling of complex relationships between different channels. The proposed Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel is able to capture delays, phase shifts, and correlations between channels, making it a more expressive and flexible model compared to existing approaches.

The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and background section that provides a thorough review of Gaussian processes and multi-output Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and the proposed extensions is clear and well-motivated. The relationship to prior work is well-explained, and the novelty of the approach is sufficient for publication.

The technical soundness of the paper is high, with a thorough derivation of the MOSM kernel and a clear explanation of the underlying theory. The experiments are adequate, with a synthetic example and two real-world datasets used to validate the proposed model. The results show that the MOSM kernel is competitive with existing models, and in some cases, outperforms them.

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a new approach to modeling MOGPs using spectral mixture kernels.
