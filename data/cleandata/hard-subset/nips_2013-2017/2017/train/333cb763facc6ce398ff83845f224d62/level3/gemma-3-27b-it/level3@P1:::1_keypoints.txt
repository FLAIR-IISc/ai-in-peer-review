- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that extends spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér’s Theorem.  
- MOSM models both phase differences and delays between channels, increasing expressiveness over LMC and variants.  
- Addresses challenge of designing cross-covariances that need not be positive definite in traditional MOGP formulations.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear introduction and accessible background on GPs and spectral mixture kernels.  
- Derivation of MOSM is thorough, mathematically clear, and well-connected to underlying theory.  
- Positions the work effectively within existing literature and argues novelty convincingly.  
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding and principled spectral approach with explicit parametric interpretation.  
- Strength: ability to model delays provides notable flexibility beyond prior methods like CSM.  
- Empirical: synthetic experiments convincingly show learning of delays and derivatives.  
- Empirical weakness: real-world experiments (climate, heavy metals, Jura) lack intuitive links between learned parameters and physical processes.  
- Hard to explain why MOSM outperforms or underperforms in specific real datasets; Jura results are particularly hard to interpret.  
- Suggests additional analyses (visualize learned cross-covariances, relate to domain knowledge) to strengthen validation.  
- Ablation study is insufficiently thorough; impact of individual MOSM components not fully explored.  
- Recommendation: overall strong, novel, and clear paper with potential significance; reviewer recommends acceptance despite experimental limitations.
