- Contribution: Proposes MOSM, a multi-output spectral mixture kernel using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem to model delays and phase shifts across channels.  
- Construction: Designs Hermitian spectral densities S_ij(ω)=R_i(ω)R_j(ω) and proves positive definiteness (Theorem 2), yielding closed-form cross-covariances.  
- Interpretability: Cross-covariance parameters have clear physical meaning: delays (θ_ij), phase shifts (φ_ij), spectral means (μ_ij) and covariances (Σ_ij).  
- Generalization: Extends Wilson et al. (2013) from single-output to multi-output, addressing limitations of CSM and LMC (no delay modeling or forced identical parameters).  
- Theoretical strength: Sound, well-motivated theoretical contribution that elegantly applies spectral mixture principles to MOGPs.  
- Synthetic validation: Convincingly recovers derivative and delayed signals where latent-process models (SM-LMC, CONV) fail, showing ability to model non-linear cross-covariances.  
- Reproducibility and implementation: Implemented in GPflow/TensorFlow, rank-Q generalization explained, code reproducible.  
- Literature positioning: Adequately cites and positions relative to CSM and LMC, framing MOSM as a natural generalization.  
- Missing extrapolation: Lacks experiments demonstrating extrapolation beyond training range, undermining a key claimed advantage of spectral mixture approaches.  
- Empirical performance: On real-world climate and Jura datasets MOSM is competitive but not decisively better than CSM or SM-LMC.  
- Statistical significance: Reported gains are marginal and not statistically significant (e.g., MAE differences with overlapping std devs; KS test α=0.05).  
- Dataset choice issue: Climate dataset’s high inter-signal correlation and Jura results may mask MOSM’s benefits; datasets chosen do not highlight where prior MOGP models fail.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel theoretical extension, clear parametric interpretation, strong synthetic evidence, solid implementation.  
- Con acceptance: Limited practical impact on benchmarks, no extrapolation demos, insufficient statistical validation, marginal and inconsistent real-world gains.  
- Overall: Technically sound and clear but of moderate significance; solid incremental contribution—borderline recommendation: Weak Accept pending stronger/extrapolation-focused experiments.
