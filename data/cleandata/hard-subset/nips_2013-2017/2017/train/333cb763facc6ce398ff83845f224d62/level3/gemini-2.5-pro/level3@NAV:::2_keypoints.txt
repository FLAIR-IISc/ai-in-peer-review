- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs, deriving cross-covariances from parametric cross-power spectral densities via Cramér’s theorem (multivariate Bochner) — a direct multi-output extension of the SM kernel.  
- Can explicitly model complex inter-channel dependencies such as delays and phase shifts, offering a flexible, interpretable kernel family.  
- Strength: original and theoretically elegant approach that increases expressiveness and interpretability compared to many existing methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient — results on common benchmarks (weather, Swiss Jura) are largely comparable to existing methods like CSM.  
- Reported standard deviations are large relative to mean differences, raising questions about the statistical significance of claimed improvements.  
- Misses demonstrating the SM kernel’s hallmark extrapolation ability in the multi-output setting, a critical omitted experiment.  
- Recommendation: include experiments on more challenging datasets where current MOGP models struggle to better showcase MOSM’s practical advantages.  
- Overall: theoretically promising but empirical evidence does not yet convincingly show a clear advantage over the state of the art.
