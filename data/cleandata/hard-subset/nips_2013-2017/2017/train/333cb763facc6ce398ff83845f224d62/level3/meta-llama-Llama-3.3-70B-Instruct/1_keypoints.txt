- Introduces a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel by extending spectral mixture ideas to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Proposes a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and uses Cramér's Theorem to construct a multivariate covariance function.  
- MOSM kernel offers interpretable cross-channel relationships in terms of frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction to GPs and a thorough explanation of the spectral mixture kernel.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on real-world datasets but may lack intuition, especially for the Jura dataset.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., [1]) and provides sufficient novelty and clear relation to existing methods.  
- Strength: clear and novel modeling of cross-covariance in the multi-output setting.  
- Strength: well-explained relationship to previous work and theoretical foundations.  
- Strength: competitive empirical performance on real-world datasets.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation may not be comprehensive or intuitive enough.  
- Weakness: paper could provide more discussion on broader implications and potential applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with interpretable kernel and supporting experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete/comprehensive experiments and limited discussion of implications/applications.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to expand experiments and discuss implications.
