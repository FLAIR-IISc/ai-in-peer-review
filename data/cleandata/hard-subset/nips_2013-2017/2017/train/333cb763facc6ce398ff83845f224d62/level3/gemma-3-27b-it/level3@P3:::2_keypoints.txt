- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output spectral mixture kernel using Cramér’s Theorem to obtain valid cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transforms.  
- Core idea: define parametric cross-spectral densities to model inter-output dependencies (delays, phase differences) interpretably.  
- Builds on LMC and CSM, extending Wilson & Adams (2013) SM kernel to multiple outputs for greater flexibility.  
- Well-motivated problem: designing interpretable, positive-definite cross-covariance functions for MOGPs.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: use of Cramér’s Theorem ensures positive definiteness.  
- High interpretability: kernel parameters correspond to frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Increased expressiveness: can capture delays and phase shifts not handled by existing kernels like CSM.  
- Clear and well-written presentation of mathematics and methodology.  
- Missing demonstration of extrapolation ability analogous to the original SM kernel — weakens claimed connection.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks rigorous statistical significance testing; reported differences may be marginal.  
- Dataset choices limit conclusions: high inter-signal correlation and common benchmarks may mask MOSM advantages.  
- Model complexity concern: more parameters do not consistently yield clear performance gains across datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, expressive approach with competitive experiments and field impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absent extrapolation evidence, insufficient statistical rigor, and not sufficiently challenging datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing statistical significance, discussion/demo of extrapolation, and analysis of computational cost.
