- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that generalizes the spectral (Bochner) approach for MOGPs and models delays, phase differences, and magnitudes across channels.  
- Constructs MOSM using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's Theorem, yielding closed-form, positive-definite, stationary covariance functions.  
- Autocovariances are spectral-mixture PD kernels; cross-covariances are spectral-mixture functions with pair-specific parameters.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with a detailed derivation and discussion of properties.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets (climate, metal concentrations) show competitive mean absolute error versus existing MOGP models.  
- Model parameters are interpretable, providing physical insight into delays, phases, and magnitudes across channels.  
- Solid theoretical foundation and principled approach to designing multivariate covariance functions.  
- Paper lacks detail on parameter learning (optimization procedure and convergence/complexity).  
- Insufficient analysis of computational complexity and implementation cost despite noting automatic differentiation.  
- Needs more evaluation of behavior with few training points and handling of channels with very different length-scales.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding details about parameter learning, computational complexity, and further analyses.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
