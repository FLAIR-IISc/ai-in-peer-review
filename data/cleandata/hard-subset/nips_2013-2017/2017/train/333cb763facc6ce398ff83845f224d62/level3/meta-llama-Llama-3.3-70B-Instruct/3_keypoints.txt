- Introduces a novel streaming approach (STAR-T) for robust submodular maximization under post-stream removals.
- STAR-T uses a partitioning structure and an exponentially decreasing threshold rule to retain a small, robust summary.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis with a detailed proof and a constant-factor approximation guarantee.
- Achieves compact summary size O((k + m log k) log k), improving over existing methods.
- Experimental results on two data-summarization tasks are strong and often outperform baselines, even when baselines know the removed subset.
- Paper is well-written and clearly describes the approach.
- Lacks discussion and analysis of parameter learning—assumes parameter τ is known in practice.
- Does not analyze algorithm behavior when the number of training points is small.
- Minor issues: missing words on lines 74 and 140, and synthetic example in Section 4.1 does not report the number of training points.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address parameter learning, small-sample analysis, and minor corrections.
