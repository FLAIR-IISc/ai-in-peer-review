- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending spectral mixture methods to multi-output GPs via Cramér’s theorem.  
- Builds on LMC and CSM, addressing their inability to model inter-channel delays while keeping parameters interpretable.  
- Constructs complex-valued cross-spectral densities to enable principled design of cross-covariance functions.  
- Provides interpretable parameterization for delays, phase differences, and other cross-channel spectral characteristics.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and clear, well-organized presentation with helpful illustrations.  
- Experimental validation shows MOSM learns complex relationships in synthetic data (derivative and delayed signals).  
- Competitive results reported on two real-world datasets (climate signals and heavy metal concentrations).  
- Novel theoretical contribution that generalizes spectral methods to multi-output GPs and improves expressiveness plus interpretability.  
- Lacks sufficient detail on parameter learning and optimization challenges given model complexity.  
- Does not analyze behavior with few training points, a critical practical concern.  
- Fails to discuss handling of channels with substantially different length-scales.  
- Synthetic experiment omits the number of training points, harming reproducibility.  
- Minor grammatical issues noted (lines 74 and 140).  
- High potential significance if parameter learning is tractable, but practical concerns about optimization and data scarcity remain.
