- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a principled spectral-domain extension of the SM kernel to multi-output GPs.  
- Builds on Cramér's Theorem to design a valid matrix-valued spectral density and derive the covariance via inverse Fourier transform.  
- Models cross-spectral densities as mixtures of complex-valued Gaussians, enabling interpretable parameters.  
- Explicitly models phase differences and time delays between outputs while guaranteeing positive definiteness.  
- Parameterization is clear and interpretable, using a rank-Q factor decomposition S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).  
- Validated on a synthetic task that shows recovery of derivative/delay relationships and on two real-world datasets with competitive results.  
- Original and significant contribution addressing a long-standing MOGP challenge; could become a standard tool if optimization is manageable.  
- Theoretical development and experimental evaluation are technically sound and well-presented; figures effectively build intuition.  
- Primary weakness: lack of discussion on hyperparameter optimization (many parameters, likely multimodal likelihood, no initialization/restart strategies or cost analysis).  
- Concern about robustness in low-data regimes and potential overfitting given the model’s large parameter count.  
- Requests for clarification: details on hyperparameter initialization, use of restarts, training time vs baselines, and handling of channels with very different length-scales/frequencies.  
- Ask to report exact training point counts in the synthetic experiment (N1, N2, N3) and fix minor wording/typo issues.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, theoretically elegant and expressive; recommend acceptance pending more practical training details.
