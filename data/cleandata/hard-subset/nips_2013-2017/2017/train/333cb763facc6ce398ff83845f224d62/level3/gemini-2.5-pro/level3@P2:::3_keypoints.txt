- Introduces MOSM kernel: a principled extension of the spectral mixture kernel to multi-output GPs using a parametric cross-spectral density and inverse Fourier transform (Cramér's Theorem).  
- MOSM models magnitude, frequency, phase differences, and explicit time delays between outputs, generalizing prior spectral MOGP methods like CSM.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on a synthetic task designed to expose delays/derivatives and on two real-world datasets, showing competitive or superior performance.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid: rank-one decomposition in the Fourier domain and clear derivation of a valid matrix-valued covariance.  
- Experimental validation is strong; the synthetic example convincingly shows expressiveness beyond LMC-based models.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes helpful visualizations (e.g., Figure 1) for intuition.  
- Original and significant contribution: provides flexible, interpretable MOGP kernels with physically meaningful delay and phase parameters.  
- Potentially high impact in domains needing delay/phase modeling (spatio-temporal analysis, econometrics, neuroscience) if training is tractable.  
- Strength: principled and novel spectral-domain formulation guaranteeing positive definiteness.  
- Strength: high expressiveness and interpretability of parameters for complex inter-output relationships.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: excellent clarity and presentation.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of optimization practicalities—large hyperparameter space, likely multimodal NLL, need details on initialization, stability, computational cost, and whether multiple restarts were used.  
- Weakness: risk of overfitting in low-data regimes due to many parameters; unclear performance degradation with fewer training points.  
- Weakness: possible restrictions in modeling cross-channel correlations when outputs have very different length scales, since cross-covariance parameters may be coupled to auto-covariances.  
- Detailed comment: explicitly report training set sizes in the synthetic experiment (N1=500, N2=400, N3=400).  
- Minor editorial: check phrasing/clarity at lines 74 and 140.
