## Review of "Spectral Mixture Kernels for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes"

This paper proposes a novel extension to spectral mixture kernels for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs). The authors address the challenge of designing cross-covariance functions that are not necessarily positive-definite, building upon prior work in spectral mixture kernels (Wilson & Adams 2013) and leveraging Cramér’s Theorem to ensure valid covariance construction. The core contribution lies in a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities, enabling the modeling of delays and phase differences between channels, offering increased expressiveness over existing MOGP methods.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, providing a clear introduction to MOGPs and a thorough background on spectral mixture kernels. The derivation of the multi-output spectral mixture kernel (MOSM) is presented logically and the explanation of the extensions is clear and appears genuinely novel. The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature, clearly articulating the limitations of prior approaches like the Linear Model of Coregionalization (LMC) and the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel, and demonstrating how MOSM addresses these shortcomings. The connection to Bochner’s and Cramér’s Theorems provides a strong theoretical foundation.

A strength of this work is its theoretical rigor and clear parameter interpretation within the spectral domain. The ability to model delays and phase shifts is a valuable addition to the MOGP toolkit. However, the experimental evaluation, while adequate, feels somewhat limited in its ability to provide strong intuition. The Jura dataset, in particular, is difficult to interpret without deeper domain knowledge, and the performance gains aren't dramatically highlighted. The synthetic example effectively demonstrates the ability to recover known relationships, but a more complex synthetic scenario could further showcase the benefits of the proposed method.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed MOSM kernel and its derivation are a significant contribution to the field of MOGPs.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The work is grounded in solid mathematical principles (Cramér’s Theorem).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Potential Impact:** The ability to model delays and phase differences could be valuable in various applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimental Insight:** The experiments, while valid, don’t fully showcase the advantages of MOSM.
*   **Jura Dataset Interpretation:** The results on the Jura dataset are hard to interpret without further context.



**Overall:** I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field and is worthy of acceptance. The novelty, theoretical soundness, and clarity of the presentation outweigh the limitations in the experimental evaluation. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to potentially expand the experimental analysis and provide more intuitive explanations of the results on the Jura dataset.
