- Proposes novel extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output Gaussian processes using complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Uses Cramér's Theorem to construct multivariate covariance functions that model delays and phase differences across channels.  
- Method is more expressive than prior MOGP approaches by explicitly modeling inter-channel delays.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear theoretical exposition and derivation.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic, weather, and Swiss Jura datasets shows competitive performance with existing MOGP models.  
- Presentation strengths: thorough theoretical background, clear explanation of spectral mixture approach, and well-described experiments and metrics.  
- Main weakness: lacks demonstration of multi-output extrapolation ability analogous to prior work [6].  
- Experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including datasets where competing models fail to show robustness.  
- Unclear whether reported results in Tables 1 and 2 are statistically significant; statistical tests are needed.  
- Originality: significant novel contribution but builds on existing spectral mixture and MOGP literature.  
- Impact could be increased by discussing practical applications (e.g., fault detection, data imputation, denoising).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and clearly written, with minor revisions needed; Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel modeling approach, solid experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing extrapolation demonstration, unclear statistical significance, limited discussion of practical impact.
