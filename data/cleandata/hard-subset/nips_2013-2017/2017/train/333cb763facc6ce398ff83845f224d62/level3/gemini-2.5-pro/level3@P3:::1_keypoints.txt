- Proposes MOSM: a new expressive, interpretable multi-output spectral mixture kernel for MOGPs built in the spectral domain using Cramér's Theorem.
- Constructs a valid positive-definite matrix-valued covariance by modeling spectral densities as sums of rank-one complex SE components.
- MOSM can model frequencies, lengthscales, delays, and phase shifts across outputs, offering richer cross-channel dependencies than prior kernels like CSM.
- Demonstrated on a synthetic task that highlights learning derivatives and delays, plus two real-world datasets for empirical comparison.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with a logical derivation and good background exposition.
- Theoretical contribution is principled and novel: an elegant, technically sound extension of SM kernels to the multi-output case.
- The kernel is highly expressive and interpretable, with parameters that have clear spectral-domain meaning.
- The authors situate their work well within prior literature and clearly explain advantages over related spectral MOGP methods.
- Real-world experiments give limited insight into practical benefits; performance is comparable to CSM on climate data and mixed on the Jura dataset.
- Lacks visualization of learned spectral densities or cross-covariance functions that would illustrate learned delays/phase relationships.
- Does not address computational scalability; complexity scales cubically with total observations and sparse approximations are only briefly mentioned.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance despite limited experimental demonstration.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, novel theory; elegant generalization of SM; expressive and interpretable kernel; very well-written.
- Argument against acceptance: real-world experiments are not fully convincing about when the added complexity is beneficial.
