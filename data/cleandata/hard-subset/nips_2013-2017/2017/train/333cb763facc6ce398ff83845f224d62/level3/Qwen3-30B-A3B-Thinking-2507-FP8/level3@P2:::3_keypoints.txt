- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM to multi-output GPs via a complex-valued spectral density matrix.  
- Derives MOSM from Cramér’s Theorem, guaranteeing positive definiteness and a sound theoretical foundation.  
- Key novelty: MOSM models delays between channels in addition to phase differences, which prior CSM kernels could not.  
- Provides clear spectral interpretation for parameters (frequencies, magnitudes, phase shifts, delays), improving interpretability.  
- Shows MOSM generalizes and relates to LMC and CONV while offering better interpretability than prior approaches.  
- Synthetic experiment demonstrates learning of delayed signals and derivatives without prior knowledge.  
- Empirical results: MOSM outperforms baselines on the heavy metal dataset; climate dataset shows no statistically significant gain over CSM.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear notation, figures, and literature positioning.  
- Original and significant contribution likely to become a standard tool for MOGP modeling where delays/phase matter.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on parameter learning, optimization challenges, and computational cost for training.  
- Weakness: synthetic example omits number of training points per channel and contains minor typos to fix.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor issues, recommended for acceptance (NIPS 2017).
