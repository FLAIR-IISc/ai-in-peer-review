- Paper introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a principled generalization of the single-output SM kernel to multi-output GPs via designed complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem.  
- MOSM models phase differences and explicit time delays between outputs and is validated on a synthetic task plus climate and Jura datasets.  
- Strength: original, principled derivation from first principles using a factorized Hermitian positive-definite spectral matrix (S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω)).  
- Strength: high expressiveness and interpretability—allows channel-specific spectral means/variances and explicit delay parameters (θij).  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with useful background on Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems and step-by-step kernel derivation.  
- Weakness: limited real-world impact—MOSM performs only comparably to CSM on the climate and Jura datasets, with often non-significant gains despite added complexity.  
- Weakness: missed opportunity to demonstrate multi-output long-range extrapolation capabilities analogous to single-output SM; experiments focus on imputation rather than forecasting.  
- Weakness: statistical-significance reporting is unclear; large standard deviations and lack of conventionally marked significant improvements reduce transparency.  
- Summary: novel and elegant theoretical contribution that enables modeling inter-channel delays, but practical benefits are not fully established; recommendation is acceptance.
