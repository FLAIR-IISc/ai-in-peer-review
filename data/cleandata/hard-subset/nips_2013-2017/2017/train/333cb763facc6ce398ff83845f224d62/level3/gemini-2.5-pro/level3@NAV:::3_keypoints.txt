- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a novel expressive covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Generalizes spectral mixture approach to multivariate case by modeling parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Uses Cramér’s Theorem to derive a valid matrix-valued covariance function, providing a principled multivariate extension.  
- More flexible and principled than existing MOGP methods like LMC and CSM.  
- Theoretical soundness is demonstrated and empirical results on synthetic and real data are competitive or better than state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivated with effective illustrations.  
- Original and significant contribution: offers interpretability of parameters (delays, phase differences, channel-specific frequencies).  
- Potential to become a standard kernel for MOGPs if optimization/learning challenges are addressed.  
- Main weakness: insufficient detail on parameter learning and optimization for the many introduced hyperparameters.  
- Open questions: model behavior in low-data regimes (risk of overfitting) and handling correlations between channels with very different length-scales.  
- Minor suggestions: explicitly state total training points in synthetic example and fix a few typos (e.g., lines 74, 140).  
- Overall assessment: strong, principled contribution to MOGP literature.
