- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending single-output spectral mixtures to multi-output GPs via principled cross-spectral density construction.
- Uses Cramér’s theorem (multivariate Bochner) to design complex-valued spectral densities that yield closed-form covariances with parameters for delays, phase shifts, and cross-correlations.
- Theoretical contribution is sound and well-motivated, providing a meaningful generalization of the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel by explicitly modeling inter-channel delays.
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM can learn derivative and delayed relationships that latent-process-based models cannot capture, demonstrating a clear modeling advantage in toy settings.
- Real-world experiments (weather and Swiss Jura) show competitive but not clearly superior performance compared to CSM, SM-LMC, and CONV.
- Reported performance differences in Tables 1 and 2 appear minimal and likely statistically insignificant given the standard deviations.
- Critically fails to demonstrate multi-output extrapolation ability, a key strength of single-output spectral mixtures and important for practical use.
- Paper lacks experiments on challenging or failure-case datasets where previous methods perform poorly, which would better showcase MOSM’s advantages.
- Missing discussion on specific scenarios where MOSM is preferable over existing techniques; the “more expressive” claim is not convincingly supported by empirical results.
- Overall quality and originality are strong theoretically, but experimental evaluation is insufficient, limiting the paper’s significance.
- Recommends additional statistical testing and experiments on datasets that challenge existing methods to substantiate claimed benefits.
