- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: defines cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain and uses Cramér’s Theorem to obtain multi-output covariance functions that model delays and phase differences.

- Motivation: argues existing MOGP methods lack interpretability/flexibility and that direct spectral design yields a more expressive, interpretable framework.

- Technical soundness: clear mathematical derivation using complex-valued cross-spectral densities, with positive-definiteness justified via Bochner’s and Cramér’s Theorems.

- Experimental evaluation: includes a synthetic validation and two real-world datasets (climate and heavy metals); synthetic results show ability to recover covariances/delays, real-world results are competitive but not dramatically better.

- Strength — Novelty: introducing direct cross-spectral design for MOGPs is a novel contribution.

- Strength — Interpretability: MOSM parameters give clear frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay interpretations between channels.

- Strength — Expressiveness: MOSM is more flexible than prior MOGP kernels for modeling cross-covariances.

- Strength — Theoretical foundation: the approach is grounded in solid spectral theory (Bochner/Cramér).

- Strength — Evaluation: experiments cover synthetic and real datasets to demonstrate capabilities.

- Weakness — Statistical significance: reported results lack rigorous statistical significance testing to support performance claims.

- Weakness — Extrapolation demo: no direct demonstration of multi-output extrapolation abilities that spectral mixtures can offer.

- Weakness — Limited impact on benchmarks: improvements on the tested real-world datasets are modest, reducing claimed impact.

- Weakness — Computational cost: the paper omits discussion/comparison of computational complexity versus other MOGP methods.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method that improves interpretability and expressiveness and shows competitive empirical performance.

- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient statistical evidence, missing extrapolation demonstration, and modest real-world gains weaken the case.

- Overall assessment: valuable contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: add statistical significance testing, include a multi-output extrapolation demonstration, and discuss computational complexity relative to other MOGP methods.
