- Introduces a novel MOSM covariance for multi-output GPs grounded in Cramér’s Theorem, extending spectral mixture kernels to multivariate settings.
- Proposes a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities that model delays and phase differences between channels.
- Provides a principled spectral framework ensuring positive definiteness and linking kernel parameters to spectral properties.
- Offers improved interpretability: parameters correspond directly to frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.
- Generalizes prior methods (e.g., CSM) by allowing more flexible cross-covariance modeling including delays and phases.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows competitive performance and recovery of known cross-covariances.
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and effective illustrations conveying key concepts.
- Lacks detailed discussion of parameter learning: optimization algorithm, computational cost, initialization sensitivity, and training challenges.
- Does not explore scalability or performance in low-data/few-shot regimes, which is important for practical use.
- Fails to address handling of vastly different length-scales across output channels and how SM components adapt.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, interpretable, flexible, and empirically validated approach to a hard MOGP problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient detail on parameter learning and unexplored scalability/limited-data behavior.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution meriting acceptance pending minor revisions to address learning, data-scarcity behavior, and length-scale handling.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request more detail on optimization, experiments on varying dataset sizes, discussion of length-scale differences, clarification of synthetic training points, and correction of minor typos.
