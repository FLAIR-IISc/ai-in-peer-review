- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a principled extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using cross-power spectral densities and Cramér's theorem.  
- Technical contribution: models complex-valued cross-spectra, explicit delays and phase shifts, addressing limitations of LMC and CSM.  
- Interpretability: provides clear spectral meaning for parameters, aiding transparency over many existing MOGP models.  
- Synthetic validation: successfully recovers auto- and cross-covariances (including delays and derivatives) and outperforms baselines in extrapolation on synthetic data.  
- Real-world evaluation: tested on climate and Swiss Jura datasets, showing competitive performance for imputation and prediction.  
- Missing real-world extrapolation experiments: paper does not demonstrate MOSM’s extrapolation capability on real datasets.  
- Limited dataset novelty: using standard datasets reduces the experimental novelty and limits demonstration of unique strengths.  
- Statistical significance concerns: reported performance differences fall within standard deviations and lack robust statistical testing.  
- Scalability not addressed: no discussion or experiments on computational scalability to large datasets.  
- Recommendation: meaningful, interpretable, and theoretically sound contribution but weakened by limited real-world extrapolation evidence and dataset novelty.  
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound kernel with strong interpretability and convincing synthetic experiments modeling complex relationships.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of real-world extrapolation evidence, limited dataset novelty, and inconclusive statistical superiority.  
- Overall conclusion: solid scientific contribution that would benefit from additional experiments, statistical analysis, and scalability discussion to strengthen impact.
