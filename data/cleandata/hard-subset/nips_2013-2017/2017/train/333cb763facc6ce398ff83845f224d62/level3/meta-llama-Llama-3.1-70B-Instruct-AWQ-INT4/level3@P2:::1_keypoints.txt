- Proposes a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that extends spectral-domain covariance design to multi-output GPs.  
- Builds on prior spectral mixture work (Wilson & Adams, 2013) and derives a multi-output spectral mixture covariance formulation.  
- Addresses the challenge of modeling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.  
- Clear, concise, and well-written exposition of GP fundamentals and spectral-domain methods.  
- Thorough explanation of relationship to prior work (LMC, CSM) demonstrating sufficient novelty.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound and clearly presented.  
- Experiments validate MOSM on a synthetic example and two real-world datasets (climate signals and metal concentrations).  
- Results show MOSM is competitive with existing MOGP methods.  
- Experiments are adequate but provide limited intuition about model behavior.  
- Jura dataset is hard to interpret, reducing clarity about practical significance.  
- Significance could be improved by demonstrating clearer practical applications and deeper experimental insight.  
- Scores well on quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept for publication despite experimental limitations.
