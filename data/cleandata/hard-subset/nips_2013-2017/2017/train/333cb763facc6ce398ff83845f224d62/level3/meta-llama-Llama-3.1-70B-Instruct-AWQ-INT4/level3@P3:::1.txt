This paper proposes a novel approach to multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) by extending the spectral mixture concept to the multi-output setting. The authors introduce a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities, which are then used to construct a multivariate covariance function via Cramér's Theorem. The resulting kernel, dubbed the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, is able to model delays and phase differences between channels, providing a more expressive and interpretable model than previous approaches.

The paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and a thorough explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and its proposed extensions. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the MOSM kernel and demonstrate its ability to model complex relationships between channels. The experimental results are adequate, with the MOSM kernel performing competitively against existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a clear interpretation of the kernel's parameters from a spectral viewpoint. The authors demonstrate how each parameter can be identified with frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for a pair of channels. This is a significant contribution, as it provides a more intuitive understanding of the relationships between channels.

The paper also addresses the challenge of modeling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite. The authors propose a factor decomposition of the spectral density matrix, which ensures positive definiteness and allows for non-positive-definite cross-covariances.
