- Proposes MOSM kernel: a novel, flexible covariance for MOGPs that extends the Spectral Mixture idea to multi-output GPs.  
- Leverages Cramér’s Theorem to design parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities, ensuring positive-definite matrix-valued covariances.  
- Factor decomposition of the cross-spectral density produces interpretable, valid cross-covariances that can model delays and phase shifts between outputs.  
- Provides an elegant, principled solution to a difficult multi-output kernel design problem, advancing beyond LMC and CSM approaches.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and technically sound, with clear derivations and strong positioning relative to prior work.  
- Synthetic experiments effectively demonstrate the model’s ability to capture derivative and delay relationships.  
- Real-world experiments (climate, Jura) show marginal or sometimes non-significant gains, and the Jura results are hard to interpret.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze learned spectral parameters on real data or apply to tasks with known inter-channel delays to increase experimental insight.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
