- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain.  
- Extends spectral-domain covariance design to the multi-output setting.  
- Builds on prior spectral mixture work (Wilson & Adams 2013).  
- Addresses the challenge of modeling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.  
- Derives a multi-output spectral mixture covariance formulation.  
- Paper is well written with a clear introduction and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is well explained and novelty is sufficient for publication.  
- Experiments are adequate for this type of paper but provide limited intuition; the Jura dataset is hard to interpret.
