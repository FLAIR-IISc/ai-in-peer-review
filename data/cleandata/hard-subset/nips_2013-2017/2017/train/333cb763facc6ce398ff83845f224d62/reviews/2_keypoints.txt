- Proposes a new multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using the inverse Fourier transform to obtain cross-covariances.
- Extends the spectral-mixture idea from [6] (Gaussian-mixture PSD → powerful kernel with extrapolation) to multiple outputs.
- Experiments conducted on a synthetic dataset, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal-concentration dataset.
- Extension is a clever and promising way to construct new multi-output covariance functions.
- Missing demonstration of the extrapolation ability for multi-output analogous to [6].
- Experimental evaluation uses common datasets and yields performance similar to existing multi-output covariance functions.
- Impact could be increased by including datasets where other models perform poorly.
- Unclear whether results in Tables 1 and 2 are statistically significant; reported standard deviations suggest similar performance across models.
