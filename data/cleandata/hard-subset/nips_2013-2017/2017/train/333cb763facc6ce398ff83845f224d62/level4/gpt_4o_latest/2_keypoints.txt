- Proposes a novel class of multi-output GP covariance functions by specifying cross-power spectral densities and applying inverse Fourier transforms to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Builds on the spectral mixture idea from [6] for single-output GPs, where PSD mixtures of Gaussians yield kernels with extrapolation capabilities.  
- Empirical evaluation on a synthetic dataset plus weather and Swiss Jura real-world datasets.  
- Extending [6] to multi-output settings is an innovative methodological contribution.  
- Unclear whether the proposed multi-output covariance inherits the extrapolation properties emphasized in [6]; this was not explored.  
- Experiments use common benchmark datasets and achieve results comparable to existing multi-output kernels, limiting demonstrated impact.  
- No statistical significance analysis reported for Tables 1 and 2; given the standard deviations, model performances appear nearly indistinguishable.
