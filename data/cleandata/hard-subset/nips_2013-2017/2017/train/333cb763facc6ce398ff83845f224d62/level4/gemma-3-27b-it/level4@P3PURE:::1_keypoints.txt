- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain.
- Argues spectral-domain covariance design is important and underexplored, and extends it to multi-output GPs.
- Builds on prior spectral mixture work but highlights the key challenge of modeling cross-covariances that must remain positive-definite.
- Proposes an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs to address that challenge.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and solid explanation of Gaussian process fundamentals.
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and the proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.
- Relates the method to prior work convincingly and demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a theory-focused paper, though some datasets (e.g., Jura) are hard to interpret and provide limited intuition.
