- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral-design methods to multi-output problems.  
- Builds on Wilson & Adams (2013) and adapts spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting.  
- Tackles the central challenge of modeling cross-covariance functions (which need not be positive definite) with a principled solution.  
- Introduces a novel spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and a lucid explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and extensions.  
- Relationship to prior work is thoroughly discussed; the contribution shows sufficient novelty for publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a methodological paper but gives limited intuition about model behavior.  
- Results on the Jura dataset are hard to interpret, weakening empirical clarity.
