- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that extends SM kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér's Theorem.
- Constructs a matrix of complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase differences between channels.
- MOSM generalizes prior methods (LMC, CONV, CSM) by incorporating delay parameters that CSM could not model.
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous with proper handling of positive definiteness via rank-one spectral decomposition.
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM can recover known relationships (derivatives, delays) where other methods fail.
- Real-world experiments (climate, Jura heavy metals) demonstrate competitive or superior performance, notably significant gains on Cadmium prediction.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized with clear mathematical exposition and effective figures.
- Original contribution: parametric family of complex cross-spectral densities enabling interpretable delay modeling across channels.
- Significance: addresses practical problems in sensor networks, fault detection, and data imputation by modeling inter-channel delays.
- Acknowledges limitations: MOSM and CSM perform similarly on highly correlated data, which is expected.
- Weakness: limited discussion of parameter learning complexity, convergence behavior, and behavior with limited training data.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
- Reproducibility issue: number of training points in the synthetic experiment should be specified.
- Minor edits needed: grammatical fixes at lines 74 and 140.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution meeting NIPS standards with recommendation for acceptance, tempered by concerns about scalability and limited-data performance.
