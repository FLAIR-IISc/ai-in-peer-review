- Proposes a novel multi-output GP formulation: specify cross-power spectral densities and use inverse Fourier transform to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Extends the spectral-mixture idea from [6] (single-output) to multiple outputs.  
- Applied the method to a synthetic example and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura).  
- Notes the approach is clever and raises the question whether it inherits the extrapolation capabilities of [6].  
- Experimental performance is comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions.  
- Recommends testing on datasets where existing models perform poorly to better demonstrate strengths and limitations.  
- Raises concern that statistical significance tests are not reported; results appear similar within reported standard deviations.
