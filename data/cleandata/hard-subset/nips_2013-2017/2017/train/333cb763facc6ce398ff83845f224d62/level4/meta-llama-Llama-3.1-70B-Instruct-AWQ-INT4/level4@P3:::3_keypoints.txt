- Introduces MOSM: a novel spectral-based covariance for multi-output Gaussian Processes grounded in Cramér's Theorem.  
- Uses complex-valued cross-spectral densities to interpret relationships between channels.  
- Models delays and phase differences across channels, making it more expressive than prior MOGP methods.  
- Method is clearly described with relevant, helpful illustrations.  
- Paper provides a thorough, self-contained background on GPs, MOGPs, and spectral mixtures.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show competitive performance versus LMC and CSM kernels.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated approach.  
- Original contribution with significant potential impact for MOGPs.  
- Could become a standard due to clear channel-relationship interpretation.  
- Missing detail on the parameter learning process, which is crucial for GP models.  
- Synthetic experiment (Section 4.1) omits the number of training points.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with spectral mixture approach and Cramér's Theorem; background may still be challenging for some.  
- Minor typographical omissions (missing words at lines 74 and 140).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity and parameter-learning details; caution that method may be unsuitable for very small datasets.
