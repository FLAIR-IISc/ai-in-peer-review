This paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes, effectively generalizing the spectral approach – building upon the Bochner Theorem and the work of A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams – to construct kernels. A key strength of this approach is the interpretability of its parameters, offering insights into relationships between outputs like time delays or phase differences.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a significant challenge, and current methodologies are limited. The authors’ proposed method is theoretically well-justified and empirically validated across multiple datasets. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the method, with thoughtfully chosen illustrative examples. Assuming parameter learning is computationally feasible (a point where the paper could benefit from further detail), this approach has the potential to become a standard technique in the field of multi-output GPs.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   How does the method perform with limited training data?
*   (As a point of interest) Given that channels with significantly different length-scales are unlikely to be highly correlated, how is this reflected within the model?
