- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a spectral-domain MOGP kernel built via complex-valued cross-spectral factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω).
- Chooses R columns as complex Gaussians and enforces spectral symmetry to obtain closed-form, real-valued cross-covariances with Gaussian envelopes and cosine terms.
- Provides explicit per-pair parameters: magnitude, frequency, lengthscale, phase, and delay, enabling interpretable cross-covariances.
- Preserves positive definiteness of the full matrix-valued kernel by construction (Cramér’s theorem / spectral factorization).
- Allows non-symmetric cross-covariances and models inter-channel delays, extending previous spectral MOGP approaches.
- Subsumes SM-LMC and relates closely to CSM (Ulrich et al. 2015) while relaxing parameter tying across outputs.
- Technical derivation is principled, yields closed-form kernels, and is compatible with standard GP inference (marginal likelihood, predictive posterior).
- Clearly maps per-output spectral parameters to pairwise cross-spectral parameters and shows special-case relationships to prior kernels.
- Empirically outperforms baselines on a synthetic task (signal, derivative, delayed version) and is competitive with some statistically significant gains on two real datasets.
- Does not empirically explore higher-rank spectral factors; identifiability and initialization for many parameters are not deeply analyzed.
- Lacks analysis of computational overhead and runtime comparisons versus SM-LMC/CSM; guidance on choosing Q and regularization is limited.
- Approach is stationary by construction; nonstationary extensions and varying spectra are not discussed or evaluated.
- Real-data improvements are modest in places; ablations isolating phase vs. delay and parameter-tying effects are missing.
- Paper is generally well written and organized; recommends summarizing notation early and adding intuition on how phase/delay appear in time domain.
- Novelty: direct complex cross-spectral parameterization with explicit delay/phase plus constructive PD guarantee is a clear advance over LMC/CSM.
- Significance: provides an interpretable, broadly applicable MOGP kernel useful for multi-sensor/time-series tasks where phase and delay matter.
- Pros: principled construction, interpretable parameters, generalizes prior kernels, strong synthetic results and competitive real-data performance.
- Cons: limited exploration of model rank/identifiability, no scalable approximations or runtime benchmarks, modest real-data gains, and stationarity constraint.
- Suggested improvements: add initialization heuristics (e.g., cross-periodograms), discuss identifiability/priors, include runtime/complexity comparisons, ablate phase/delay, explore higher-rank factors, and consider sparse/nonstationary variants.
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound, interpretable contribution to MOGP kernels; recommend acceptance.
