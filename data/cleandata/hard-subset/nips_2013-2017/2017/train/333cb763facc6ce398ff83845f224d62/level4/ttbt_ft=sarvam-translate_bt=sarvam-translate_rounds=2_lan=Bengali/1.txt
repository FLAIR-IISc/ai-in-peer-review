This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process co-variance function defined in the spectral domain. The method of designing co-variance in the spectral domain is a significant contribution to GP, which I personally believe has not received the attention it deserves. This paper extends this to multi-output situations. This paper is an extension of the work presented in [1]. However, considering multi-output cases, describing cross-variance is a challenge, which does not have to be positive. In this paper, the authors describe exactly such a method that leads to spectral mixing co-variance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixing kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are new to my knowledge. The connection to previous work is well explained, and I believe there is enough novelty in the work to justify the publication.

Testing is sufficient for such a paper, but they do not provide much insight, especially since it is difficult to actually support the data set of the Jura. However, I think experimental evaluation is sufficient for such a paper.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., and Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In, Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, June 16-21, 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). : JMLR.org.
