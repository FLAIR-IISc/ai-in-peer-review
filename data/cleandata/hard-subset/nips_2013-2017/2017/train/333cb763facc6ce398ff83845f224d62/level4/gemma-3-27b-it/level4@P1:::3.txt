The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach (based on Bochner’s Theorem) to kernel construction. A key advantage of the proposed method is the interpretability of its parameters, such as delays and phase differences between outputs.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem with relatively few existing solutions. The authors’ method is theoretically sound and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets. 

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, and the chosen illustrations appear relevant. If parameter learning doesn’t prove computationally burdensome (the paper provides limited information on this aspect), this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

I have a few questions:

*   How does the method perform with limited training data?
*   (More of a curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale might be expected to exhibit limited correlation with a channel having a small length-scale. How does the model handle such scenarios?
