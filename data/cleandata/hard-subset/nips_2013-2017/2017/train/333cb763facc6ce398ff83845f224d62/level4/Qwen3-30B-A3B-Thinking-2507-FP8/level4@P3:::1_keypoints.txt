- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture GPs to multi-output via Cramér's theorem, using complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase while preserving positive definiteness.  
- Generalizes prior methods (CSM, SM-LMC) by allowing explicit delays and more flexible parameter sharing across channels.  
- Provides a principled, theoretically grounded framework with clear parametric interpretation linking spectral parameters to covariance structure.  
- Rigorous derivation and clear connections to Wilson & Adams' SM and the CSM kernel.  
- Experimental validation includes synthetic recovery of derivatives/delays and two real-world datasets (climate and Jura heavy metals).  
- Demonstrates competitive performance and statistically significant improvement on Cadmium prediction in the Jura dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation, good background exposition, and figures that clarify spectral–covariance relationships.  
- Original contribution: first multivariate spectral approach to incorporate delays explicitly in the spectral domain.  
- Significance: addresses limitations of existing multi-output GP methods, useful for applications with temporal/spatial lags (e.g., sensor networks).  
- Strengths summarized: theoretical extension, interpretability of delays/phase, strong experiments, clarity, and competitive results.  
- Weaknesses: lack of intuitive explanation for why MOSM outperforms others on the Jura/Cadmium task.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability versus existing methods.  
- Weaknesses: paper does not explicitly discuss limitations (e.g., very large delays or highly non-stationary channel relationships).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance.
