- Proposes a novel multi-output covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforming to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Approach extends single-output spectral mixture ideas from [6] to multiple outputs.  
- Applied method to synthetic data, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.  
- Extension is a clever way to construct new multi-output covariance functions.  
- Paper misses demonstrating whether the multi-output kernel inherits extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6].  
- Experimental evaluation uses common datasets and yields results comparable to existing multi-output covariances.  
- Impact would be increased by testing on datasets where existing models fail, to highlight strengths/limitations.  
- Unclear if statistical significance was tested; reported means and standard deviations suggest most models perform similarly.
