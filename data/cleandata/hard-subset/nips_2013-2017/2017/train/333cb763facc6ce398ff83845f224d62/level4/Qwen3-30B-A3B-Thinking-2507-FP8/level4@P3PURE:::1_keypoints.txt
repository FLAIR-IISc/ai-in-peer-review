- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, addressing an underexplored area of GP methodology.  
- Extends Wilson & Adams (2013) to the multi-output setting and tackles the key challenge of describing cross-covariance, which need not be positive-definite.  
- Proposes a novel approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with a concise introduction and clear presentation of GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and the proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is thoroughly discussed and the novelty is sufficient to justify publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for this type of work but lacks intuitive insights, particularly for the Jura dataset.
