This paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral mixture kernel approach based on Bochner's theorem. A significant advantage of the proposed method is the interpretability of its parameters, which can represent physically meaningful quantities such as delays or phase differences between output channels.

The design of covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated on several datasets. The paper is well-written, the method is described clearly, and the illustrations are relevant.

The paper provides limited information regarding the practical aspects of parameter learning. Assuming the optimization procedure is not overly challenging, this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

**Questions**
*   How does the method behave when few training points are available?
*   Intuitively, an output channel with a large characteristic length-scale would not be highly correlated with a channel that has a small length-scale. How is this relationship captured within your model's parameters?
