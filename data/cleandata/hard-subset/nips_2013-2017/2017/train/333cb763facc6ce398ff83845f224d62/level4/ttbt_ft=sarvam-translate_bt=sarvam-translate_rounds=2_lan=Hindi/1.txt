This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process co-variance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach to designing co-variances in the spectral domain is a significant contribution to GP, which I personally feel has not received as much attention as it should have. This paper extends this to a multi-output scenario. It is an expansion of the work presented in [1]. However, the challenge in considering the multi-output case is how to describe the cross-variance, which need not be positive-definite. In this paper, the authors describe an approach that leads to spectral mixing co-variance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and the fundamentals of GPs. The explanation of the spectral mixing kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are new to my knowledge. The connection to previous work is well explained, and I believe there is enough novelty in the work to justify the publication.

The experiments are adequate for such a paper; they do not provide much intuition. However, it is indeed difficult to argue, especially about the Jura dataset. But, for such a paper, I think the experimental evaluation is sufficient.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., and Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, June 16-21, 2013 (PP. 1067-1075). : JMLR.org.
