This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is a valuable contribution to Gaussian processes that, in my opinion, has been historically underappreciated. This work extends this approach to the multi-output setting, building upon the foundation laid in [1]. A key challenge in the multi-output case lies in representing cross-covariance functions, which are not necessarily positive-definite. The authors address this challenge and present a method that results in a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, featuring a concise and clear introduction to the problem and the fundamentals of Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions appear novel to the best of my knowledge. The relationship to existing work is thoroughly discussed, and I believe the work demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication. 

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a paper of this nature. While the experiments do not provide substantial new intuition—particularly with the Jura dataset proving difficult to interpret—I find the evaluation sufficient given the theoretical focus of the work.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013*, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
