- Introduces a novel multi-output GP covariance function by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using the inverse Fourier transform.  
- Builds on [6]'s single-output spectral mixture idea; extending it to multiple outputs is innovative.  
- Paper demonstrates the method on synthetic data and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura).  
- The proposed multi-output kernel currently lacks the extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6]; adding this would strengthen the work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to common datasets and yields performance comparable to existing methods; testing on datasets where current models fail would increase impact.  
- It's unclear whether statistical significance tests were conducted for Tables 1 and 2; given overlapping standard deviations, significance testing is needed to validate reported differences.
