- Proposes a novel multi-output GP kernel construction by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms to obtain covariance functions.
- Approach is inspired by [6], which used Gaussian-mixture power spectral densities for single-output GPs to achieve strong extrapolation.
- Extending the spectral-density method to multi-output GPs is a potentially valuable contribution.
- Evaluated on a synthetic dataset and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Experimental results are comparable to existing covariance functions and do not show clear improvement.
- Open question: whether the proposed multi-output kernel inherits the strong extrapolation properties of the single-output method.
- Recommendation: include experiments on datasets where current multi-output GP models struggle or where the proposed kernel’s unique properties could provide an advantage.
- Concern: statistical significance of results in Tables 1 and 2 is unclear; observed differences are often within reported standard deviations and require formal significance testing.
