- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function formulated in the spectral domain.  
- Extends spectral-domain covariance construction to the multi-output setting, addressing an underappreciated area.  
- Identifies the unique challenge that cross-covariances in multi-output GPs may not be positive-definite.  
- Proposes a method to resolve this challenge, yielding a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Builds upon prior work (Wilson & Adams 2013) while extending it to multi-output cases.  
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction and solid exposition of GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is straightforward and the proposed extensions appear novel.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate but offers limited intuition, especially for the Jura dataset; results are nevertheless sufficient for the paper's scope.
