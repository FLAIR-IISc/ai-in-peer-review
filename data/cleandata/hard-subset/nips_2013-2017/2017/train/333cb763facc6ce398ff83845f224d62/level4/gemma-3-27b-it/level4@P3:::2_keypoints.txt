- Proposes MOSM kernel for MOGPs by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier (Cramér’s Theorem).

- Extends spectral mixture methods from single-output to multi-output for more flexible, interpretable cross-covariances.

- Validated on a synthetic trivariate signal and two real-world datasets (climate signals, heavy metal concentrations).

- Theoretically grounded: guarantees positive-definiteness and clearly connects to Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems.

- Enables modeling of delays and phase differences between outputs, beyond capabilities of LMC and Convolution models.

- Supports arbitrary rank mixtures (Q) to increase modeling flexibility.

- Well-motivated with strong literature connections and a convincing synthetic example showing derivatives and delays.

- Experimental results are competitive but do not consistently or significantly outperform existing methods.

- Benchmark datasets are standard and may not showcase advantages related to delays/phase differences.

- Statistical significance of improvements is weak; more rigorous analysis (p-values/confidence intervals) is needed.

- Mathematical notation is dense in places and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.

- Paper should better relate spectral parameters to practical/physical interpretations for the real datasets.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing statistical analysis, choice of datasets, clarity, and parameter interpretation.
