The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs), which corresponds to the generalization of the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to build kernels. One particular asset of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be interpreted, such as delay between outputs or phase difference.

The definition of covariance structures for MOGPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available to do so. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the choices for the illustrations appear relevant. The authors provide a clear description of the proposed method, including the theoretical background and the experimental results.

The proposed Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel is a significant contribution to the field of MOGPs. The kernel provides a clear interpretation from a spectral viewpoint, where each of its parameters can be identified with frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for a pair of channels. The ability to jointly model delays and phase differences is a unique feature of the proposed kernel, which is validated experimentally using a synthetic example.

The experimental results show that the proposed MOSM kernel performs competitively with existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets. The results also demonstrate that the MOSM kernel can capture complex relationships across multiple outputs, including delays and phase differences.

However, there are a few questions and minor remarks that need to be addressed. Firstly, it would be interesting to know how the method behaves when few training points are available. Secondly, the paper does not contain much information regarding parameter learning, and it would be helpful to know more about this aspect.

Additionally, a minor remark is that a word is missing in lines 74 and 140. Furthermore, in the synthetic example (Section 4.1), the number of training points should be specified.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of MOGPs, and the proposed MOSM kernel has the potential to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a new and theoretically sound method for defining covariance structures for MOGPs.
* The proposed MOSM kernel provides a clear interpretation from a spectral viewpoint, and its ability to jointly model delays and phase differences is a unique feature.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on several datasets.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper does not contain much information regarding parameter learning, which could be a potential drawback of the method.
* The behavior of the method when few training points are available is not well understood.
