- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms, extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output settings.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and two benchmarks (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentration).  
- Conceptually clever extension of the single-output spectral mixture idea from [6].  
- Fails to demonstrate the extrapolation capability that motivated [6]; no experiments showing improved extrapolation.  
- Experimental datasets are conventional and results are only comparable to existing multi-output covariances, limiting impact.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for Tables 1–2; overlapping standard deviations suggest differences may be meaningless.  
- Recommendation: include challenging scenarios where alternatives fail and add significance testing to strengthen the contribution.
