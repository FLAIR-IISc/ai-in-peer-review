The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), which generalizes the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to construct kernels. This work builds upon the research by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A significant advantage of the proposed method is that its parameters can be interpreted, such as the delay between outputs or phase difference.

The definition of covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging topic, with relatively few methods currently available. The authors' proposed method is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets. The paper is well-written, and the chosen illustrations are relevant.

If the learning of parameters is not too problematic (the paper does not provide extensive information on this aspect), I expect this method to become a standard for multi-output GPs. However, I have a few questions and comments to further improve the paper.

One concern is the behavior of the method when few training points are available. How does the proposed approach handle limited data? This is a crucial consideration, as many real-world applications often involve sparse data.

Additionally, I am curious about how the model handles channels with significantly different length-scales. Specifically, a channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel having a small length-scale. How does this discrepancy manifest in the proposed model?

Regarding the presentation, I suggest specifying the number of training points in the synthetic example (Section 4.1) for clarity.

Minor remarks: There appear to be missing words in lines 74 and 140. Addressing these minor issues will further improve the overall quality of the paper.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of multi-output GPs, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to become a standard reference in this area.
