This paper introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process (GP) covariance function defined via its spectral density. Designing covariance functions in the spectral domain is a powerful and promising approach for GPs, though it remains relatively under-explored. This work makes a valuable contribution by extending this paradigm to the multi-output setting.

Building upon the spectral mixture kernel of Wilson & Adams (2013), the authors address the central challenge of the multi-output case: modeling the cross-covariance structure. Unlike auto-covariances, cross-covariances are not required to be positive definite. The paper proposes an elegant solution to this problem, resulting in a principled and flexible spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written and accessible. The introduction clearly frames the problem and provides the necessary background on Gaussian processes. The authors offer a lucid explanation of the single-output spectral mixture kernel before detailing their own multi-output extension. To my knowledge, the proposed extensions are novel. The work is also well-contextualized, with a clear discussion of its relationship to previous literature. The methodological contribution possesses sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a primarily methodological contribution. While the experiments successfully demonstrate the model's effectiveness, they offer limited qualitative intuition. The results on the Jura dataset, for example, are challenging to interpret from a domain perspective. Despite this, the empirical results are sufficient to validate the proposed framework.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013)*.
