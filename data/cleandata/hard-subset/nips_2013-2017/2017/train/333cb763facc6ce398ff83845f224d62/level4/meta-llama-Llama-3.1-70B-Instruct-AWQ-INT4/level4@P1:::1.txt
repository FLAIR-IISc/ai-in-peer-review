This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, extending the approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain to the multi-output scenario. The authors build upon their previous work and tackle the challenge of describing cross-covariance, which does not have to be positive-definite. They propose a novel approach that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the extensions proposed are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the relationship to previous work, demonstrating sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments, while not providing a lot of intuition, are sufficient for a paper of this nature. The Jura dataset, in particular, is difficult to reason about, but the experimental evaluation is adequate. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method through a synthetic example and two real-world datasets, showcasing its competitive performance.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a clear interpretation of the kernel's parameters from a spectral viewpoint, allowing for the joint modeling of delays and phase differences. This is a unique feature of the proposed kernel, and the authors validate it experimentally using a synthetic example.

In terms of the four criteria for evaluating NIPS papers, I would rate the paper as follows:

* Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. (9/10)
* Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the extensions proposed are well-explained. (9/10)
* Originality: The authors propose a novel approach to designing covariance functions in the spectral domain for multi-output GPs. The extensions proposed are, to my knowledge, novel and provide a unique contribution to the field. (9/10)
* Significance: The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of Gaussian processes, extending the approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain to the multi-output scenario. The proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable in various fields. (9/10)
