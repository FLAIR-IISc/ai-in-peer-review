- Introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output GPs that generalizes Wilson & Adams' spectral mixture kernel.  
- Key advantage: parameters have clear physical interpretations (e.g., delays, phase differences between outputs).  
- Addresses a challenging problem—designing expressive covariance structures for multi-output GPs—with relatively few existing general methods.  
- Method is theoretically sound and demonstrated effectively on several datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and accompanied by relevant, insightful illustrations.  
- Caveat: limited details on parameter learning and its tractability in the paper.  
- Potential: could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs if parameter learning is tractable.  
- Question: How does the method perform in low-data regimes (few training points)?  
- Question: How does the model capture correlations between channels with very different length-scales?  
- Comment: Specify the number of training points used in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor remarks: A word appears to be missing at line 74 and line 140.
