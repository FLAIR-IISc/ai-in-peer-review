

This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. The focus on designing covariances within the spectral domain represents an important contribution to Gaussian processes, an area that I believe has not received sufficient attention. This work extends that approach to the multi-output setting. While building upon the framework established in [1], the paper addresses the key challenge of describing cross-covariances, which need not be positive-definite. The authors successfully propose a method that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  

The paper is well-written, featuring a concise and clear introduction to the problem and Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is thorough, and the proposed extensions appear novel to my knowledge. The relationship to prior work is well-articulated, and the novelty is sufficiently demonstrated to warrant publication.  

The experimental evaluation is adequate for the scope of this paper. While the results do not provide extensive intuitive insight—particularly regarding the Jura dataset—the evaluation is sufficient for validating the methodological contribution.
