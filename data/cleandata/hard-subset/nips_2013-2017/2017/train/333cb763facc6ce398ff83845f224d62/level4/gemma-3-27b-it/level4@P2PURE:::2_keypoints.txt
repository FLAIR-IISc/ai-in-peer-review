- Introduces a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using the inverse Fourier transform to get cross-covariances.  
- Extends the single-output spectral mixture approach from [6] (Gaussian-mixture PSDs) to the multi-output setting.  
- Demonstrated on a synthetic dataset, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.  
- Extension is a promising direction for building new multi-output covariance functions.  
- Important open question: does the new kernel retain the strong extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6]?  
- Experimental evaluation uses standard datasets and yields performance comparable to existing multi-output kernels.  
- Suggestion: strengthen impact by evaluating on datasets where current models struggle.  
- Concern: statistical significance of results in Tables 1 and 2 is unclear—reported standard deviations imply similar performance across models.
