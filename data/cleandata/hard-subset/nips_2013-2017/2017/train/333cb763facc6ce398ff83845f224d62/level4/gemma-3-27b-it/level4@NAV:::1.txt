This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function formulated within the spectral domain. The strategy of spectral-domain covariance design represents a valuable, though often underappreciated, contribution to Gaussian process methodology. This work builds upon that approach by extending it to the multi-output setting, where a key challenge lies in modeling cross-covariance functions that need not be positive-definite. The authors present a method that successfully addresses this issue, resulting in a spectral mixture covariance suitable for multi-output Gaussian processes.

The manuscript is clearly written, providing a concise introduction to the problem and a solid overview of Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions appear novel. The paper effectively situates itself within the existing literature, and I find the level of novelty sufficient to warrant publication. 

The experimental results are adequate for a theoretical contribution of this nature. While they don’t offer extensive intuitive insight – and the Jura dataset is particularly difficult to interpret – the evaluation is appropriate for the scope of the paper.
