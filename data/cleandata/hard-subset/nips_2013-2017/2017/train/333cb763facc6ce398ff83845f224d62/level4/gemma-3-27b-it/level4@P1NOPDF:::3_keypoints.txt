- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral (Bochner-based) approach of A.G. Adams & R.P. Adams.
- Key strength: parameters are interpretable and relate to meaningful output characteristics (e.g., delay, phase difference).
- Notes that defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is challenging and few effective methods exist.
- Method is theoretically well-founded and demonstrated to work across several datasets.
- Paper is clearly written with relevant and insightful illustrations.
- Limited detail on computational cost of parameter learning; scalability/feasibility is a concern.
- If parameter learning is not computationally prohibitive, the method could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?
- Question: How does the model capture low correlation between channels with very different length-scales?
- Comment: In the synthetic example (Section 4.1), specify the number of training points used.
- Minor remark: A word appears to be missing in lines 74 and 140.
