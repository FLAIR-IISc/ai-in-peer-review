- Introduces a multi-output GP covariance constructed in the spectral domain, extending spectral design to multi-output settings.
- Builds on Wilson & Adams (2013) spectral mixture idea and adapts it for multiple outputs.
- Addresses the central difficulty of specifying cross-covariances (which need not be PD individually) via a constructive spectral approach.
- Presents a construction that yields a valid multi-output spectral-mixture covariance.
- Novel extension with well-articulated connections to prior work; sufficient novelty to merit publication.
- Clearly written with a concise, accessible introduction and lucid presentation of the spectral mixture kernel.
- Experiments are adequate for a methods/kernel paper but offer limited intuition about results.
- Jura dataset evaluation is hard to interpret, which limits empirical insight.
- Overall empirical evaluation is sufficient given the paper’s focus on kernel design.
