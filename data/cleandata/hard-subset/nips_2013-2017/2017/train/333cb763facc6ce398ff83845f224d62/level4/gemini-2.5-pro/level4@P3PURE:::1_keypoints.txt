- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending Wilson & Adams (2013).
- Emphasizes that designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important but underexplored contribution to GP literature.
- Identifies the core challenge as modeling cross-covariance for multiple outputs, which need not be positive definite.
- Proposes a principled method to address this challenge, yielding a spectral mixture covariance for MOGPs.
- Paper is well-written with a concise introduction and clear explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and extensions.
- Novel multi-output extensions and their relation to prior work are well-explained; novelty justifies publication.
- Experimental evaluation is sufficient for a methodological paper but provides limited intuition.
- Results on the Jura dataset are particularly difficult to interpret, though overall experiments are adequate.
