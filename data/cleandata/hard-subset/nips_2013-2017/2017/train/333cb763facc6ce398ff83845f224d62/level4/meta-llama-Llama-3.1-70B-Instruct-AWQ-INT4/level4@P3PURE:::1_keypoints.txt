- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain.  
- Highlights importance of designing GP covariances in the spectral domain, a underexplored area.  
- Extends Wilson & Adams (2013) spectral mixture approach to the multi-output setting.  
- Identifies the key challenge of modeling cross-covariances, which need not be positive-definite.  
- Introduces an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is well-explained and the contributions have sufficient novelty for publication.  
- Experiments are adequate for the paper’s scope but offer limited intuition, particularly on the Jura dataset.
