- Introduces a new multi-output GP covariance structure generalizing spectral mixture kernels via Bochner’s theorem.  
- Parameters are interpretable and can encode physical quantities like delays or phase differences between outputs.  
- Covariance design for multi-output GPs is challenging and few methods exist; this fills an important gap.  
- Method is theoretically sound and empirically effective across several datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly described, and illustrated.  
- Provides limited information on practical parameter learning and optimization difficulty.  
- If optimization is tractable, the method could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with few training points?  
- Question: How does the model capture correlations when outputs have very different characteristic length-scales?  
- Comment: Specify the number of training points in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor remarks: Missing word at line 74 and line 140.
