This paper presents a new covariance structure for multi-output GSP, which is consistent with the generalization of the spectral approach (Buchner's theorem) for kernel construction by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams. A particular advantage of the proposed method is that the model parameters can be interpreted (for example, delays between outputs or phase differences).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GSP is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are available to do so. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound and its effectiveness has been demonstrated on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the selection of figures seems relevant to me. If parameter learning is not too difficult (the paper does not provide much information on this), I hope that this method will become a standard for multi-output GSP.

Questions:
* How does the method behave when there are few training points available?
* (More curious) A large length-scale channel may not be highly correlated with a small length-scale channel. How does this manifest in your model?
