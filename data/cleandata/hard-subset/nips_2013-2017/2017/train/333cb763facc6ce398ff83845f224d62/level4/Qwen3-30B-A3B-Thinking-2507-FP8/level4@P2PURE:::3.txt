

The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach (via Bochner's theorem) to construct kernels from Adams and Adams. A key advantage is the interpretability of model parameters, such as delays between outputs or phase differences.  

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a challenging problem with few existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is empirically demonstrated across multiple datasets. The paper is clearly written, with well-justified experimental choices. Provided parameter learning is feasible (the paper lacks detailed discussion on this aspect), this method has strong potential to become a standard for multi-output GPs.  

Questions:  
* How does the method perform with limited training data?  
* (As a point of interest) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one having a small length-scale. How does this constraint manifest in your model?
