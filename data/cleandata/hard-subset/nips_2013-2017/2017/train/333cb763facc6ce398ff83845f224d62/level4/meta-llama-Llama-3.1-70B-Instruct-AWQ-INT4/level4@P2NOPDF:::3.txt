The paper proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), extending the spectral approach based on the Bochner Theorem by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A significant advantage of this method is that its parameters can be interpreted meaningfully, such as the delay between outputs or phase differences.

The development of covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, with relatively few methods currently available. The authors' proposed method is theoretically sound, and its efficacy is demonstrated through experiments on multiple datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the illustrations are relevant and effective in conveying the authors' ideas. If parameter learning is not too problematic (the paper does not provide extensive information on this aspect), this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

Several questions arise from the paper:

* How does the method perform when only a few training points are available?
* (A question of curiosity) In the case where a channel has a large length-scale, it is unlikely to be highly correlated with a channel having a small length-scale. How does the proposed model capture this behavior?
