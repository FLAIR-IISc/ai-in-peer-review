- Proposes a new MOGP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and recovering cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform.
- Extends the spectral mixture (SM) approach of [6] from single-output to multi-output GPs.
- Demonstrated on a synthetic dataset and two real-world benchmarks (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Extension is clever and promising as a direction for new covariance constructions.
- Fails to evaluate or discuss whether the multi-output extension retains the original SM kernel’s key extrapolation capabilities.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to common benchmarks where the method only matches existing MOGP performance.
- Lacks experiments on problems where existing models are known to be unsuitable to show clear advantages.
- Unclear if statistical significance tests were performed for Tables 1–2; reported standard deviations suggest many results may be statistically indistinguishable.
