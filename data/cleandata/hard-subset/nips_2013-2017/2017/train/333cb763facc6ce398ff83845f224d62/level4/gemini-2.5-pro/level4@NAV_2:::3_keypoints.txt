- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a principled, expressive MOGP kernel derived via parametric complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér's Theorem.  
- MOSM generalizes single-output Spectral Mixture kernels and is more expressive than LMC and CSM, modeling delays, phase differences, and shared spectral content.  
- The approach is theoretically sound, technically robust, and builds on a solid spectral representation of covariance functions.  
- Experiments (synthetic and two real-world datasets) convincingly demonstrate MOSM’s ability to capture derivative/delay relationships and competitive or superior performance.  
- The paper is very clear, well-written, and uses effective illustrations and experiments to showcase model properties.  
- MOSM’s interpretability and flexibility give it strong potential impact as a standard tool for MOGP modeling with complex inter-channel dynamics.  
- Missing discussion on hyperparameter optimization: number of parameters, issues with local minima, initialization strategies, and sensitivity should be addressed.  
- Unclear how MOSM performs in low-data regimes—its data efficiency and parameter identifiability need analysis.  
- Request clarification on how cross-covariance S_ij models interactions between channels with very different spectral properties (e.g., smooth vs. high-frequency).  
- For reproducibility, state the number of training points used for each of the three signals in the synthetic experiment (Section 4.1).  
- Minor typo: line 140 likely “choose Q = 1” instead of “choose2 Q = 1.”  
- Intro readability issue: sentence describing the CSM kernel is interrupted by a footnote reference.
