- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance via cross-power spectral densities, extending single-output spectral mixture kernels.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and conceptually appealing, aligning with prior successful spectral methods.  
- Fails to demonstrate the key extrapolation capability for multi-output settings claimed as motivation.  
- Experiments use synthetic data, a weather dataset, and Swiss Jura but show no significant improvement over existing covariances (e.g., LMC).  
- Chosen datasets do not challenge current methods or illustrate scenarios where the new kernel would excel.  
- Reported results (Tables 1–2) show similar performance within standard deviations, offering no clear advantage.  
- No statistical significance tests are provided, so small RMSE differences (0.1–0.2%) may be noise.  
- Paper does not address computational scalability or hyperparameter sensitivity for multi-output cases.  
- Practical utility and empirical validation are underexplored despite a clear theoretical contribution.  
- Suggested fixes: test extrapolation on synthetic out-of-range data, compare to stronger baselines (deep kernels, hierarchical models), and report statistical significance.  
- Summary: technically coherent extension but lacks compelling empirical evidence and impactful novelty in practice.
