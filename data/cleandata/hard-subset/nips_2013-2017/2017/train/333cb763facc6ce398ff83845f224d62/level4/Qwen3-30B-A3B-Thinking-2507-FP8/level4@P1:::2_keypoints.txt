- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending Spectral Mixture kernels to multi-output Gaussian processes via the spectral domain.  
- Constructs complex-valued cross-power spectral densities and uses Cramér's theorem to derive cross-covariances, allowing parametric interpretation of inter-channel relationships.  
- MOSM can model delays and phase differences between channels, capabilities not captured by prior methods like Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM).  
- Theoretical foundation and mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-presented, logically building on prior work.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly show MOSM successfully learns derivative and delayed signal relationships and outperforms alternatives in extrapolation.  
- Real-world experiments (climate data, Swiss Jura) are well-designed with clear setups and proper statistical evaluation.  
- Improvements on real-world datasets are modest and not consistently statistically significant (e.g., limited KS-test confirmation).  
- The paper lacks experiments on datasets where delays and phase differences are clearly essential, weakening practical validation of MOSM’s unique strengths.  
- Writing and organization are clear, balancing mathematical detail with intuition.  
- Technical implementation (GPflow) and derivations are sound and standard for Gaussian process work.  
- Main weakness: practical advantages are not sufficiently demonstrated in real-world scenarios despite strong synthetic results.  
- Recommendation: accept for solid theoretical contribution, but suggest future work to better demonstrate practical impact with targeted datasets.
