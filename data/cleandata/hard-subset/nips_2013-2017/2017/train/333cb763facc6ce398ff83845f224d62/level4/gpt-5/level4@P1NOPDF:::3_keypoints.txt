- Proposes a new covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral approach via Bochner’s theorem (building on A. G. Adams & R. P. Adams).  
- Method parameters are interpretable (e.g., inter-output delays and phase differences).  
- Defining covariances for multi-output GPs is a hard problem with relatively few existing methods.  
- The proposed approach is theoretically sound.  
- Empirical effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with appropriate illustrations.  
- Could become a standard for multi-output GPs if parameter learning is not too troublesome (paper gives little detail on this).  
- Question: How does the method behave with few training points?  
- Question: How does the model handle correlation between channels with very different length-scales?  
- Comment: In the synthetic example (Section 4.1) the number of training points should be specified.  
- Minor remark: A word appears to be missing on lines 74 and 140.
