- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture GPs to multi-output settings via Cramér’s Theorem.  
- Proposes parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities that model delays (θ_ij) and phase shifts (φ_ij) between channels.  
- Derives MOSM by inverse Fourier transform with a rank-1 decomposition that guarantees positive definiteness and yields interpretable spectral parameters.  
- Validated on synthetic data (recovers derivatives/delays) and two real datasets: climate stations and Swiss Jura heavy metal concentrations.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical extension and clear derivation, with well-motivated parameter interpretations.  
- Strength: original capability to model delays, outperforming LMC-based models on synthetic tasks.  
- Strength: practical significance shown by statistically significant improvement for Cadmium prediction on the Jura dataset.  
- Strength: clear exposition, thorough background, definitions, experiments, and reproducible code (GPflow/TensorFlow).  
- Weakness: limited novelty over CSM since CSM already models phase shifts; need clearer contrast emphasizing MOSM’s delay modeling.  
- Weakness: climate dataset results not significantly better than CSM and may not require delay modeling due to high inter-station correlation.  
- Weakness: no sparse or scalable implementation provided and no benchmarks against sparse MOGP methods, limiting demonstrated scalability.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically strong, interpretable, and practically useful despite the noted limitations.
