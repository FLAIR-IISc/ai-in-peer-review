This paper presents a new covariance structure for multi-output GCs, which is consistent with the generalization of the spectral method (Bachner's theorem) for kernel construction by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams. A particular advantage of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be explained (e.g., delays or phase differences between the outputs).

Defining a covariance structure for multi-output GCs is a challenging task, and currently there are relatively few methods to do so. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound and has demonstrated its effectiveness on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choices for the figures seem relevant to me. If the parameters are not too difficult to learn (the paper does not provide much information on this), I would hope that this method becomes a standard for multi-output GCs.

Questions:
* How does the method behave if a small number of training points are available?
* (More curious) A channel of great length cannot be highly correlated with a channel of short length. How does this manifest in your model?
