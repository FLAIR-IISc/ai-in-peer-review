This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to Gaussian processes (GPs) that, in my opinion, has not received the attention it deserves. This paper extends this approach to the multi-output scenario. The paper builds upon the work presented in [1]. However, when considering the multi-output case, a key challenge is how to describe the cross-covariance, which does not have to be positive-definite. The authors address this challenge by proposing an approach that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to previous work is well-explained, and I believe that there is sufficient novelty in the work to justify publication.

The experiments are sufficient for a paper of this nature, although they do not provide a great deal of intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset, which is difficult to reason about. Nevertheless, I think the experimental evaluation is sufficient for a paper such as this.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
