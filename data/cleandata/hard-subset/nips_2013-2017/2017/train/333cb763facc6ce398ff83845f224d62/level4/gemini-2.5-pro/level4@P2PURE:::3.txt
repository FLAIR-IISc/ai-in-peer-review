This paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs) by generalizing the spectral mixture kernel approach based on Bochner's theorem. A key advantage of the proposed method is that its parameters are interpretable, corresponding to physically meaningful quantities such as delays and phase differences between output channels.

Defining expressive covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a significant challenge with relatively few existing solutions. The method proposed here is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated on several datasets. The paper is well-written and clearly describes the approach, with relevant illustrations that support the text. Provided that parameter learning is tractable—an aspect on which the paper provides limited information—this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

**Questions**
*   How does the method behave when only a small number of training points are available?
*   As a point of curiosity: a channel with a large length-scale is unlikely to be highly correlated with a channel that has a small length-scale. How is this relationship captured by your model's parameterization?
