This paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), which generalizes the spectral mixture kernel approach of Wilson and Adams. A key advantage of the proposed method is that its parameters have a clear physical interpretation, such as delays or phase differences between output channels.

Designing expressive covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, and relatively few general methods currently exist. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated on several datasets. The paper is well-written and clearly describes the method, and the accompanying illustrations are relevant and insightful. Assuming that parameter learning is tractable—the paper provides limited details on this aspect—this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

**Questions**
*   How does the method behave in a low-data regime, i.e., when only a small number of training points are available?
*   As a point of curiosity: it is often assumed that an output channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel that has a small length-scale. How is this relationship captured by your model's structure?
