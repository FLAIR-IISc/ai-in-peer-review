- Proposes a novel MOGP covariance construction by modeling cross-power spectral densities and recovering cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform.
- Extends the spectral mixture approach of [6] from single-output GPs to the multi-output setting.
- Evaluated on one synthetic dataset and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Extension is clever and promising as a direction for multi-output spectral kernels.
- Missed opportunity: does not investigate whether the multi-output kernel retains the extrapolation ability emphasized in [6].
- Experimental evaluation is limited to common benchmarks and achieves performance comparable to existing MOGP methods.
- Impact would be stronger if demonstrated on tasks where conventional models perform poorly.
- Unclear whether statistical significance tests were performed for Tables 1 and 2; reported standard deviations suggest most performance differences are marginal.
