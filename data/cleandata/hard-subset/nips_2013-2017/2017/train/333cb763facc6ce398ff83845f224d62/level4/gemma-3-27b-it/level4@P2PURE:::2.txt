This paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes. Rather than directly defining cross-covariance functions, the authors propose specifying cross-power spectral densities and then utilizing the inverse Fourier transform to derive the corresponding cross-covariances. This approach mirrors the work in [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, which represented the power spectral density as a mixture of Gaussians to yield a kernel with strong extrapolation capabilities. The proposed covariance function is demonstrated on a synthetic dataset, as well as two real-world datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.

Extending the approach from [6] to the multi-output setting is a promising idea for constructing new covariance functions. A key motivation in [6] was the development of a kernel capable of extrapolation; it would be valuable to investigate whether this new covariance function exhibits similar extrapolation properties. The experimental evaluation currently relies on standard datasets, and the observed performance is comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions. The paper’s impact could be strengthened by including results on datasets where current models struggle. 

Furthermore, the statistical significance of the results presented in Tables 1 and 2 is unclear. The performance of the various models appears similar, given the reported standard deviations.
