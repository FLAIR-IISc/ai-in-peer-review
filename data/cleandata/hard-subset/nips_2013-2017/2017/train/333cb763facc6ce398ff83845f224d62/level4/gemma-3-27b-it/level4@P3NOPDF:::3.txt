## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission: [Paper Title]

This paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), extending the spectral approach – building kernels via the Bochner Theorem, as originally proposed by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams – to the multi-output setting. A key strength of this approach is the interpretability of its parameters, which directly relate to meaningful quantities like delays or phase differences between outputs.

Defining appropriate covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a significant challenge, and currently, relatively few methods are available. This work addresses this gap with a theoretically sound contribution, demonstrating its effectiveness through empirical evaluation on several datasets. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the proposed methodology. The selection of illustrative examples appears thoughtfully chosen and effectively communicates the model’s capabilities. If parameter learning proves computationally tractable – a point requiring further clarification (discussed below) – this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GP modeling.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed covariance structure represents a valuable extension of existing spectral methods to the multi-output GP framework.
* **Interpretability:** The parameters of the model possess a clear physical interpretation, aiding understanding and potentially guiding model design.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The method is well-grounded in established mathematical principles (Bochner’s Theorem).
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, making the complex methodology accessible.
* **Effective Illustrations:** The chosen examples effectively demonstrate the model’s functionality and advantages.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost of Parameter Learning:** The paper lacks detailed discussion of the computational challenges associated with learning the model parameters. The scalability of the proposed method, particularly with a large number of outputs or complex datasets, is a significant concern that needs to be addressed.
* **Behavior with Limited Data:** The performance of the method when training data is scarce is not investigated. GPs, in general, can struggle with limited data, and the impact of data scarcity on this specific covariance structure warrants further examination.
* **Length-Scale Disparity:** The model’s behavior when dealing with outputs characterized by vastly different length-scales is not explicitly addressed. Intuitively, channels with significantly different length-scales may exhibit low correlation, and understanding how the model handles this scenario is important.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* How does the performance of this method degrade with decreasing numbers of training points? Are there any strategies to mitigate this issue, such as regularization or prior selection?
* The model constructs covariances based on spectral representations. Given that a channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one possessing a small length-scale, how does this constraint manifest within the model, and how does the model handle such discrepancies in length-scales between outputs?
* Could you provide more detail on the computational complexity of parameter estimation, particularly as the number of outputs increases? Are there any approximations or optimizations that can be employed to improve scalability?

**Minor Comments:**

* In the synthetic example (Section 4.1), please specify the number of training points used.
* There appears to be a missing word in lines 74 and 140. (Please review these lines for completeness.)

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong submission with the potential to significantly advance the field of multi-output GP modeling. The theoretical contribution is solid, the method is clearly presented, and the empirical results are promising. Addressing the questions raised above, particularly regarding computational cost and behavior in low-data regimes, will further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors providing satisfactory responses to these concerns.
