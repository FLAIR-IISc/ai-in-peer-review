- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner) as applied by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams.  
- Parameters are interpretable (e.g., represent delays or phase differences between outputs).  
- Addresses a challenging problem with few existing methods for defining multi-output GP covariances.  
- Method is theoretically sound and shown effective on several datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and uses judicious illustrations.  
- Limited discussion on computational cost of parameter learning; scalability remains unclear.  
- If parameter learning is not computationally prohibitive, the method could become a standard approach.  
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Question: How does the model handle channels with very different length-scales and their constrained correlation?  
- Comment: In the synthetic example (Section 4.1), specify the number of training points used.  
- Minor remark: Possible missing words on lines 74 and 140.
