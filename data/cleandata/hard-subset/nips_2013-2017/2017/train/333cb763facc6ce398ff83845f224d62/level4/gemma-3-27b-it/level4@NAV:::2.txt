This paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes, departing from direct cross-covariance specification by instead defining cross-power spectral densities and recovering cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform. This approach echoes the work in [6] for single-output GPs, which leveraged a Gaussian mixture representation of the power spectral density to generate a kernel with strong extrapolation properties. The proposed covariance function is demonstrated on a synthetic dataset, as well as applied to the weather dataset and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.

The extension of the technique from [6] to the multi-output setting represents a potentially valuable strategy for constructing new covariance functions. A key motivation in [6] was the development of kernels capable of extrapolation; investigating whether the proposed function possesses similar capabilities would be a worthwhile extension. The experimental evaluation currently focuses on established datasets, with performance largely comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions. Expanding the experimental analysis to datasets where current models struggle would strengthen the paper’s contribution. 

Furthermore, the statistical significance of the results presented in Tables 1 and 2 warrants further investigation. Given the overlap in performance within the reported standard deviations, assessing statistical significance is crucial to determine whether observed differences are meaningful.
