This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing kernels in the spectral domain is an important and, in my view, underappreciated direction for Gaussian processes. This work extends that perspective to the multi-output setting, building on the spectral mixture framework of Wilson and Adams [1].

In the multi-output case, the central challenge is specifying cross-covariances between outputs: while they need not be positive-definite on their own, the overall construction must yield a jointly positive semidefinite covariance. The authors propose a principled spectral-domain approach to this problem that leads to a valid multi-output spectral mixture kernel. To my knowledge, the resulting construction is novel.

The paper is well written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to relevant GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is accessible, and the extension to the multi-output case is presented clearly. The relationship to prior work is well articulated, and I believe the level of novelty justifies publication.

The experimental evaluation is appropriate for a paper of this type, though it offers limited intuition. In particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret and does not provide strong qualitative insight. Nevertheless, the experiments are adequate to demonstrate the method’s feasibility and benefits.

Reference:
[1] Wilson, A. G., and Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013), pp. 1067–1075. JMLR.org.
