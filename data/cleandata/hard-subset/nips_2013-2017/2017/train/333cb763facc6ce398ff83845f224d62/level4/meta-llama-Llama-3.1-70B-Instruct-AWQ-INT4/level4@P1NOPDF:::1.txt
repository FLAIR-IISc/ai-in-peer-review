This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, a valuable contribution to the Gaussian process (GP) community that, in my opinion, has not received the attention it deserves. The authors extend this approach to the multi-output scenario, building upon their previous work [1]. A key challenge in the multi-output case is describing the cross-covariance, which does not necessarily have to be positive-definite. The authors address this issue by proposing an approach that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is also clear, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the relationship to previous work, and I believe that the paper contains sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments are sufficient for a paper of this type, although they do not provide a great deal of intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset, which is difficult to reason about. However, for a paper focused on methodological contributions, I think the experimental evaluation is adequate.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
