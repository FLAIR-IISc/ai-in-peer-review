- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral-design of covariances to multi-output GPs.
- Addresses the multi-output challenge of specifying cross-covariances and their positive-definiteness.
- Proposes an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.
- Builds on and extends prior spectral mixture kernel work (Wilson & Adams 2013).
- Writing is clear with a concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.
- Explanations of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions are clear and appear novel.
- Links to prior work are well explained and the contribution is sufficiently novel to merit publication.
- Experiments are adequate for this paper type but provide limited intuition; the Jura dataset is hard to interpret.
