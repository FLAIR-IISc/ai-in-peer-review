This paper presents a new covariance structure for multi-output GSPs, which corresponds to the generalization of the spectral approach (Bochner's theorem) for kernel construction by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A particular advantage of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be interpreted (e.g., delays or phase differences between outputs).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GSPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available to do so. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound and its effectiveness has been demonstrated on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choice of figures seems relevant to me. If the parameter learning is not too difficult (the paper does not provide much information on this), I hope that this method will become a standard for multi-output GSPs.

Questions:
* How does the method behave when there are few training points available?
* (More curious) A large length-scale channel may not be highly correlated with a small length-scale channel. How does this manifest in your model?
