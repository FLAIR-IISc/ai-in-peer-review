This paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes, framed by specifying cross-power spectral densities and utilizing the inverse Fourier transform to derive the corresponding cross-covariances. This approach mirrors the work in [6] for single-output GPs, which leveraged a Gaussian mixture representation of the power spectral density to generate a kernel with strong extrapolation capabilities. The proposed covariance function is demonstrated on a synthetic dataset, as well as real-world datasets including weather data and metal concentrations in the Swiss Jura region.

The extension of the spectral density approach from [6] to the multi-output setting represents a potentially valuable contribution to covariance function design. A key motivation behind [6] was the development of kernels capable of extrapolation; exploring this property within the context of the presented multi-output covariance function would be of significant interest. The experimental evaluation currently relies on established datasets, and the reported performance is comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions. Broadening the experimental scope to include datasets where current models struggle could substantially strengthen the paper’s impact. 

Furthermore, the statistical significance of the results presented in Tables 1 and 2 warrants investigation. Given the overlap in performance within the reported standard deviations, assessing statistical significance is crucial for a robust comparison of the proposed method against existing approaches.
