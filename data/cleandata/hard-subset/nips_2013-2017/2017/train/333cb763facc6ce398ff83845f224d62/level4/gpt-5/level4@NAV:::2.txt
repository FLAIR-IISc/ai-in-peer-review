The paper introduces a novel class of covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes. Instead of specifying cross-covariances directly, the authors define cross-power spectral densities and recover the corresponding cross-covariances via the inverse Fourier transform. The method follows a similar idea to [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where the power spectral density is modeled as a mixture of Gaussians and then inverse-transformed to produce a powerful kernel with extrapolation capabilities. The proposed covariance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and two real-world datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.

Extending the idea from [6] is a clever way to construct new multi-output covariance functions. One of the motivations in [6] was to obtain a kernel with extrapolative ability; it would be interesting to demonstrate that the multi-output covariance inherits this property. The experiments focus on very common datasets, and the performance appears comparable to that of other multi-output covariance models. The impact of the work could be strengthened by including datasets where competing approaches do not perform well.
