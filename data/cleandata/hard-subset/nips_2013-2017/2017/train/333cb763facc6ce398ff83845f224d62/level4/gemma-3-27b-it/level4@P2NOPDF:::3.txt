This paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach outlined in the work of A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams, and leveraging Bochner’s Theorem for kernel construction. A significant strength of this approach lies in the interpretability of its parameters, which directly relate to characteristics such as delays or phase differences between outputs.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a challenging problem, and relatively few methods currently address it effectively. The authors’ proposed method is theoretically well-founded and demonstrates promising performance across a range of datasets. 

The paper is clearly written and the illustrative examples are well-chosen and relevant. Provided parameter learning is computationally feasible – an aspect not fully detailed within the paper – this method has the potential to become a standard approach for multi-output GP modeling.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   How does the performance of the method degrade with limited training data? Does it exhibit comparable robustness to single-output GPs in low-data regimes?
*   (As a point of curiosity) The model implicitly assumes relationships between channels based on their length-scales; specifically, a channel with a large length-scale is unlikely to be strongly correlated with one possessing a small length-scale. How is this constraint reflected in the model’s behavior and learned parameters?
