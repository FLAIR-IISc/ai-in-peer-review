- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain.
- Emphasizes that designing covariances in the spectral domain is important and understudied.
- Extends the spectral mixture kernel approach from single-output to multi-output GPs.
- Identifies the key challenge that cross-covariances in the multi-output case are not necessarily positive-definite.
- Proposes an approach that produces a valid spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs addressing that challenge.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and fundamentals of Gaussian processes.
- Explanations (especially of the spectral mixture kernel) are lucid, extensions appear novel, and relation to prior work is thorough.
- Experiments are adequate for the paper’s goals but provide limited intuition, particularly on the hard-to-interpret Jura dataset.
