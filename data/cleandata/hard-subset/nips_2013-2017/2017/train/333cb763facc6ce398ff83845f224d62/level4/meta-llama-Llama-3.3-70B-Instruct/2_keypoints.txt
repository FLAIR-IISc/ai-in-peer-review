- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Extends the single-output spectral mixture kernel idea from [6] to handle multiple outputs.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic example and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).  
- The extension is promising, especially given [6]’s focus on extrapolation, but it’s unclear if the multi-output version preserves extrapolation capabilities.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to commonly used datasets and yields results comparable to other multi-output kernels.  
- Recommends testing on datasets where current models fail to better demonstrate the method’s advantages.  
- Questions whether statistical significance was tested, noting that performances largely overlap within reported standard deviations and need deeper analysis.
