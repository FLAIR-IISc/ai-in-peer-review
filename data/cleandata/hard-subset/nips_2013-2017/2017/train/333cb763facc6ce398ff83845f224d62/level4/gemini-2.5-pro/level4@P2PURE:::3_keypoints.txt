- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs by generalizing the spectral mixture kernel via Bochner's theorem.  
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays and phase differences between channels).  
- Tackles a significant challenge—few existing expressive covariance solutions for multi-output GPs.  
- Method is theoretically sound and convincingly effective on multiple datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear descriptions and useful illustrations.  
- Limited information on tractability of parameter learning—needs clarification.  
- If parameter learning is tractable, method could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method behave with only a small number of training points?  
- Question: How does the parameterization capture low correlation when channels have very different length-scales?
