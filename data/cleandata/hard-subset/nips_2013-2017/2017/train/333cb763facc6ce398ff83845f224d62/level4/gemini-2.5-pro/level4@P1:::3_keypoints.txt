- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a principled generalization of the Spectral Mixture kernel to multi-output GPs using Cramér's Theorem.  
- Models cross-spectral densities directly, yielding flexible kernels with physically interpretable hyperparameters (delays, phase differences).  
- Addresses an important, under-served problem: designing valid and expressive covariance functions for MOGPs.  
- Theoretical construction is sound and grounded in spectral theory.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets convincingly demonstrates advantages over existing methods.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and illustrated effectively.  
- Potential to become a standard MOGP method provided hyperparameter learning is not overly complex.  
- Question: unclear how the model performs in low-data regimes given its rich parameterization.  
- Question: how does the model reflect or constrain correlations between channels with very different length-scales?  
- Reproducibility issue: number of training points per signal in the synthetic example (Section 4.1) should be specified.  
- Minor remarks: possible missing word in line 74 and another in line 140.
