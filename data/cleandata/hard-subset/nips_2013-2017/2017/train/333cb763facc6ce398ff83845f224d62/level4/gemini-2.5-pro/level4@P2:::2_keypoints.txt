- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs by designing cross-spectral densities and using Cramér's Theorem with inverse Fourier transform to obtain valid kernels.

- Models cross-spectral densities as a mixture of complex-valued rank-one components to guarantee positive-definiteness.

- MOSM can capture frequency, magnitude, inter-channel phase shifts, and time delays, making it more expressive than prior spectral methods like CSM.

- Validated on a synthetic task designed to highlight MOSM’s unique properties and on two real-world benchmarks (climate data and Swiss Jura).

- Novel and theoretically principled generalization of the SM kernel to multi-output settings; rank-one spectral construction is elegant and sound.

- Parameters are interpretable in the spectral domain (magnitude, mean frequency, spectral width, phase, delay), enabling modeling of explicit delays between channels.

- The synthetic experiment (signal, derivative, delayed version) compellingly demonstrates MOSM’s advantages where LMC and CONV fail.

- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with accessible derivations from spectral density to kernel.

- Empirical gains on real-world datasets are modest; MOSM is often statistically indistinguishable from the simpler CSM despite added complexity.

- Experimental evaluation is limited to interpolation tasks; lacks extrapolation or real-world cases where delays/phase shifts are critical to showcase MOSM’s practical benefits.

- The paper does not address potential optimization challenges from the large number of hyperparameters (sensitivity to initialization, local minima).

- Reviewer asks why MOSM’s added flexibility doesn’t help on climate data—possible high inter-signal correlation may make CSM sufficient.

- Notes high standard deviations in the synthetic experiment and recommends significance testing there to strengthen claims.

- Recommends adding an application (e.g., signal processing, geophysics, econometrics) with known delays/phase shifts to better highlight MOSM’s unique properties.

- Overall: technically sound and elegant contribution with limited empirical improvements on standard benchmarks but strong potential for modeling complex inter-channel relationships.
