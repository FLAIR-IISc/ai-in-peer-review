- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs generalizing the spectral (Bochner) approach.  
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs, phase differences).  
- Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is challenging and few methods exist.  
- Approach is theoretically sound.  
- Empirical results demonstrate effectiveness across multiple datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-justified experimental choices.  
- Insufficient information on parameter learning burden; practicality is unclear.  
- If parameter learning is not overly burdensome, method could become a standard for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with small numbers of training points?  
- Question: How does the model enforce that channels with very different length-scales cannot be highly correlated?
