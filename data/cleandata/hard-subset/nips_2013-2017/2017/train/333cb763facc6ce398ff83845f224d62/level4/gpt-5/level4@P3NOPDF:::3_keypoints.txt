- Proposes a new multi-output GP covariance extending Bochner’s spectral approach with interpretable parameters capturing delays and phase differences.  
- Addresses an important, underexplored problem: expressive, interpretable kernels for multi-output GPs.  
- Method appears theoretically sound and clearly derived.  
- Empirical results on several datasets demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Paper is well written with clear method description and illustrative examples.  
- Lacks details on parameter learning: optimization objective, initialization, constraints/priors for identifiability and positive definiteness, and regularization.  
- Unclear data efficiency: risk of overfitting with rich parameterization and need for mitigation strategies (priors, tying, sparsity).  
- Needs clarification on representing cross-scale correlations and whether long vs. short length-scale channels can spuriously correlate or cause identifiability issues.  
- Missing computational analysis: complexity vs. outputs, mixture components, training points; implementation choices (inducing points, structure, mini-batching) and runtimes.  
- Request experiments: learning curves for varying training set sizes and stability across random initializations.  
- Ask whether a large-length-scale channel can be strongly correlated with a small-length-scale channel, via which parameters and under what conditions.  
- Suggest specifying number of training points and noise level in synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Recommend expanding parameter learning section: optimization algorithm, initialization heuristics, regularization/constraints, and sensitivity analysis.  
- Advise adding a short comparison to other multi-output kernel constructions highlighting when this approach is preferable.  
- Minor editorial fixes: missing words around lines 74 and 140; “multioutputs GPs” → “multi-output GPs”; “informations” → “information”; small stylistic edits.  
- Overall: strong and timely contribution with interpretable spectral multi-output kernels; would be stronger with added learning, complexity, and low-data behavior details.
