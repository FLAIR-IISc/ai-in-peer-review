- Proposal: Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending Wilson & Adams' SM kernel to multi-output GPs by modeling cross-power spectral densities.  
- Method: Constructs a Hermitian positive-definite cross-spectral density matrix with a parametric form and uses inverse Fourier (Cramér’s Theorem) to obtain valid MOGP covariances.  
- Validation: Evaluated on a synthetic task (derivatives and time-delays) and two real-world benchmarks (climate and Jura heavy metal datasets).  
- Strength: Principled and clever extension of SM to multi-output, ensuring positive-definiteness while enabling expressive, interpretable interactions (delays, phase shifts).  
- Strength: Synthetic experiment convincingly shows MOSM captures complex relations (derivatives, delays) where simpler latent-process models fail.  
- Weakness: No dedicated extrapolation experiment to demonstrate whether MOSM retains the strong extrapolation capabilities of the original SM kernel.  
- Weakness: Real-world evaluation is limited; performance is largely comparable to existing methods (e.g., CSM), so practical advantage of increased model complexity is not clearly demonstrated.  
- Request: Clarify whether statistical significance tests were performed for the results in Tables 1 and 2, given small performance differences and reported standard deviations.
