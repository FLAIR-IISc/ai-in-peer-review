

The paper proposes a novel covariance structure for multioutput Gaussian processes (GPs), extending the spectral approach via Bochner's theorem to a general framework for constructing inter-output kernels. A key strength is the interpretability of model parameters, which explicitly capture meaningful physical quantities such as temporal delays between outputs and phase differences. This addresses a significant challenge in multioutput GP literature, where few methods offer both theoretical rigor and intuitive parameterization.

The theoretical foundation is sound, with the authors carefully deriving the covariance structure from first principles and establishing its connection to spectral representations. The method is well-motivated and clearly articulated, with the paper's exposition being both precise and accessible. Empirical validation across multiple datasets demonstrates the approach's effectiveness, particularly in scenarios where output relationships exhibit structured temporal or phase-based dependencies. The illustrative examples are relevant and effectively showcase the model's capabilities.

The primary limitation concerns the practical implementation of parameter learning. While the paper demonstrates successful optimization on the presented datasets, it provides minimal detail on computational complexity, convergence behavior, or sensitivity to initialization—particularly concerning the non-convex nature of the optimization problem. Without this information, it remains unclear whether the method scales efficiently to larger problems or is prone to local optima. If parameter learning proves computationally tractable and robust, this work could establish itself as a standard for multioutput GP modeling.

**Key Questions for Clarification:**
1. **Data Scarcity Performance:** How does the method behave when training data is limited (e.g., <50 points per output)? Given the reliance on spectral properties, does the model require more data than standard single-output GPs to reliably estimate parameters?
2. **Length Scale Discrepancies:** The model assumes a spectral representation where correlation is constrained by length scales. Specifically, a channel with a large length scale cannot exhibit strong correlation with one having a small length scale. How does this constraint manifest in the model's predictions when such disparate length scales are present in the data? Does the model inherently avoid such conflicts, or must they be explicitly handled during parameterization?

**Minor Corrections:**
- In Section 4.1 (Synthetic Example), the number of training points per output must be specified to enable reproducibility.
- A word appears missing in Line 74 and Line 140 (e.g., "a" or "the" likely omitted).

**Overall Assessment:**  
This work makes a valuable contribution to multioutput GP theory by bridging spectral methods with interpretable physical parameters. The method fills a critical gap in the literature, offering a principled alternative to ad-hoc multioutput covariance structures. The empirical results are convincing, and the clarity of presentation significantly enhances the paper's impact. The minor concerns regarding parameter learning details and data requirements are not fundamental to the core contribution but would strengthen the work if addressed. The approach is sufficiently novel and well-executed to warrant publication, provided the authors clarify the technical points raised above.  

**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions addressing the questions and minor corrections).
