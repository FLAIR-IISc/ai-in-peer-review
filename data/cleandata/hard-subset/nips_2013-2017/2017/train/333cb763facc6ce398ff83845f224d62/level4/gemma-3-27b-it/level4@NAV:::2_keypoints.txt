- Introduces a novel multi-output GP covariance defined via cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforms rather than direct cross-covariance specification.
- Extends the Gaussian-mixture power spectral density approach from [6] (single-output) to the multi-output setting.
- Demonstrated on a synthetic dataset and applied to the weather and Swiss Jura metal concentration datasets.
- Potentially valuable strategy for constructing new covariance functions; natural extension of prior work.
- Suggests evaluating whether the proposed kernel inherits the strong extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6].
- Experimental results are largely comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions; no clear win shown.
- Recommends expanding experiments to datasets where current models struggle to better demonstrate advantages.
- Raises concern about statistical significance in Tables 1 and 2, since reported means overlap within standard deviations and significance tests are needed.
