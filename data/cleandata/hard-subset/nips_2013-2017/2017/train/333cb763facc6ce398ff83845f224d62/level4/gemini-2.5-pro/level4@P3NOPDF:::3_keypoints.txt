- Proposes a cross-spectral mixture kernel for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral mixture approach via Bochner's theorem.
- Hyperparameters are interpretable, directly modeling delays and phase differences between outputs.
- Provides a theoretically sound framework and demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Significant contribution addressing the need for flexible, interpretable covariance structures beyond simple coregionalization models.
- Interpretability enables inference of channel-specific spectral content, inter-channel delays, and phase shifts.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented with effective illustrations.
- Method is flexible, general, and theoretically grounded, with potential for wide adoption if parameter learning is practical.
- Main weakness: many hyperparameters lead to a likely highly multi-modal marginal likelihood; optimization details, initialization, and convergence issues are under-discussed.
- Question: How does performance and hyperparameter reliability degrade with sparse training data and risk of overfitting?
- Question: Can the model learn strong correlations between processes with very different length-scales (smooth vs rapidly oscillatory)?
- Minor remarks: report number of training points in the synthetic example; missing words at Line 74 and Line 140.
