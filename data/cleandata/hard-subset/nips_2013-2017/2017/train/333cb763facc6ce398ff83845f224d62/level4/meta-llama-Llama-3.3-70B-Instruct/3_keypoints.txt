- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes extending the spectral (Bochner) approach.
- Inspired by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams, with parameters that are interpretable (e.g., inter-output delay and phase difference).
- Addresses a challenging problem with few existing methods for multi-output covariance design.
- Theoretical foundation is sound and empirical efficacy is shown on multiple datasets.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and uses relevant illustrations.
- Limited detail on parameter learning, but if tractable the method could become a standard for multi-output GPs.
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?
- Question: How does the model reflect correlations when channels have very different length-scales?
- Suggestion: Specify the number of training points in the synthetic experiment (Section 4.1).
- Minor editorial issues: possible missing words on lines 74 and 140.
