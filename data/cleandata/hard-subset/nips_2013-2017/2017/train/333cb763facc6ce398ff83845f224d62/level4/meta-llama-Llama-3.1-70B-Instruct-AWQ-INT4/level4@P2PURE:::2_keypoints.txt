- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforming to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Extends the single-output spectral mixture approach from [6] to multiple outputs, which is a clever and natural generalization.  
- Raises the question whether the proposed kernels inherit the extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6].  
- Experiments use common datasets and show performance comparable to existing multi-output covariances, limiting the paper's impact.  
- Unclear if statistical significance tests were performed; reported means largely overlap within standard deviations, casting doubt on result significance.
