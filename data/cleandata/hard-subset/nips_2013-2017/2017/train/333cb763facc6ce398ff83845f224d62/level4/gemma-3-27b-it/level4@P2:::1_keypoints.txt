- Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance constructed in the spectral domain.  
- Extends spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting, addressing cross-covariance construction.  
- Method is principled and grounded in Cramér’s Theorem for spectral-domain covariance design.  
- Frames a flexible way to model relationships between outputs (including delays and derivatives).  
- Well-written paper with clear exposition of GP fundamentals and spectral mixture extensions.  
- Novel contribution that builds on and clearly connects to prior work (e.g., Wilson & Adams 2013).  
- Synthetic experiments effectively demonstrate recovery of known covariance structure.  
- Real-world experiments show competitive performance against existing MOGP methods.  
- Experimental results (especially on the Jura dataset) lack strong qualitative/intuitive insights.  
- Mathematical framework may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with spectral analysis and Bochner/Cramér theory.  
- Experimental evaluation is sufficient but could be strengthened with deeper analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, novel, theoretically sound contribution warranting acceptance.
