- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, extending the spectral (Bochner) approach and prior Adams & Adams work.  
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., output delay, phase difference).  
- Tackles a challenging problem with limited existing methods for multi-output GP covariance design.  
- The approach is theoretically well-founded.  
- Empirical results on multiple datasets demonstrate efficacy.  
- Paper is clearly written with appropriate illustrations.  
- Paper gives limited detail on parameter learning, raising concerns about training/optimization.  
- If parameter learning is tractable, the method could become a standard for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with a limited number of training points?  
- Question: How does the model capture correlation between channels with very different length-scales?  
- Comment: Section 4.1 synthetic example should specify the number of training points.  
- Minor remark: A word appears to be missing in lines 74 and 140.
