- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Extends the single-output spectral mixture idea from [6] (mixture of Gaussians PSD → kernel) to multiple outputs.  
- Evaluated on one synthetic example and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).  
- Extension is clever and conceptually promising as a way to construct new multi-output kernels.  
- Paper does not demonstrate extrapolation capabilities for multi-output GPs analogous to the motivation in [6].  
- Experiments use common datasets and achieve results similar to existing multi-output covariance functions, limiting perceived impact.  
- Unclear whether statistical significance was tested; reported means and standard deviations largely overlap, questioning the significance of reported differences.
