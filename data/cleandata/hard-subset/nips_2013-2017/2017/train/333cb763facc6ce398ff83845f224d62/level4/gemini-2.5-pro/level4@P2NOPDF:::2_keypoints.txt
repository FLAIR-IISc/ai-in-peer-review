- Proposes a novel spectral approach for MOGP covariance construction by modeling cross-power spectral densities and recovering cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform, extending the spectral mixture kernel to multi-output.  
- Evaluated on one synthetic dataset and two real-world benchmarks (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).  
- The extension is clever and promising as a new direction for MOGP covariance design.  
- Major evaluation gap: paper does not test whether the extrapolation capabilities of the original spectral mixture kernel are retained in the multi-output formulation.  
- Experimental benchmarks are standard and show comparable but not clearly superior performance to existing MOGP methods; need datasets where traditional MOGPs fail to demonstrate unique value.  
- Unclear whether reported performance differences are statistically significant given overlapping standard deviations; significance tests or clarification are needed.
