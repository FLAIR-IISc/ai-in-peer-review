- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multioutput GPs, extending Bochner-style spectral methods to construct inter-output kernels.
- Model parameters are highly interpretable, explicitly representing physical quantities like temporal delays and phase differences.
- Theoretical derivation is sound, grounded in first principles, and clearly connected to spectral representations.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with precise and accessible exposition.
- Empirical results demonstrate effectiveness across multiple datasets, especially when outputs have temporal or phase-structured dependencies.
- Illustrative examples are relevant and effectively showcase the model’s capabilities.
- Primary limitation: sparse details on parameter learning—computational complexity, convergence behavior, sensitivity to initialization, and scalability are not addressed.
- Unclear whether optimization is robust to non-convexity or prone to local optima on larger problems.
- Key question: How does the method perform with limited training data (e.g., <50 points per output) given reliance on spectral estimation?
- Key question: How does the model handle disparate length scales across channels—does it inherently avoid conflicts or require explicit handling?
- Minor correction: specify number of training points per output in Section 4.1 for reproducibility.
- Minor correction: missing word(s) on Line 74 and Line 140.
- Overall assessment: valuable, principled contribution to multioutput GP modeling with convincing results.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing the technical questions and minor corrections.
