The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), which corresponds to the generalization of the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to build kernels, as proposed by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A particular asset of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be interpreted, such as the delay between outputs or phase difference.

The definition of covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available to address this issue. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choices for the illustrations appear relevant. If the parameter learning process is not too troublesome (the paper does not provide much information regarding this aspect), I would expect this method to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

However, I have a few questions and comments that I hope the authors will address. Firstly, how does the method behave when few training points are available? Secondly, as a matter of curiosity, a channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel with a small length-scale; how does this appear in the proposed model?

I also have a few minor comments. In the synthetic example presented in Section 4.1, the number of training points should be specified to provide a clearer understanding of the experiment. Additionally, it appears that a word is missing in lines 74 and 140.
