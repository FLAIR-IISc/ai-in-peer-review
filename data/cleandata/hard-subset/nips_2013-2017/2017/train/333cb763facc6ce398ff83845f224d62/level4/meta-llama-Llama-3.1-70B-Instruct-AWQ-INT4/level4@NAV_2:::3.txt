The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs), which corresponds to the generalisation of the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to build kernels from spectral densities. One particular asset of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be interpreted, such as delay between outputs or phase difference.

The definition of covariance structures for MOGPs is a challenging topic and relatively few methods are currently available to do so. The method proposed by the authors is theoretically sound and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets. The authors extend the spectral-mixture approach to MOGPs by designing a family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and then building on Cramér's Theorem to provide a principled approach to design multivariate covariance functions.

The paper is well-written, and the choices for the illustrations appear relevant. The proposed Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel is able to model delays among channels in addition to phase differences and is thus more expressive than previous methods, while also providing full parametric interpretation of the relationship across channels.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the MOSM kernel on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The experimental results show that the MOSM kernel is competitive with existing MOGP models in terms of the mean absolute error. The authors also discuss the relationship between the MOSM kernel and other existing MOGP models, such as the Linear Model of Coregionalization (LMC) and the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel.

Strengths of the paper include the clear and concise description of the proposed method, the thorough experimental evaluation, and the discussion of the relationship between the MOSM kernel and other existing MOGP models. The paper also highlights the potential of the MOSM kernel for modeling complex relationships between multiple outputs.

Weaknesses of the paper include the lack of discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method and the potential challenges of parameter learning. The paper does not provide much information on how the method behaves when few training points are available.
