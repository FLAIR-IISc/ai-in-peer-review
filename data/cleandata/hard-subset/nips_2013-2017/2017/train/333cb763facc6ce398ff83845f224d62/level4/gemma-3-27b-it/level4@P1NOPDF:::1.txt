This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to Gaussian processes that, in my opinion, hasn’t received the attention it deserves. This work extends this approach to the multi-output scenario. The paper builds upon the work presented in [1]; however, a key challenge in the multi-output case is representing cross-covariance, which isn’t necessarily positive-definite. The authors address this challenge by developing an approach that results in a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and the fundamentals of Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions appear novel to the best of my knowledge. The relationship to prior work is thoroughly explained, and I believe the work demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication. 

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this nature. While they don’t provide a great deal of intuition, particularly regarding the Jura dataset – which is difficult to interpret – I believe the experimental evaluation is sufficient for the goals of this work.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2013*, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
