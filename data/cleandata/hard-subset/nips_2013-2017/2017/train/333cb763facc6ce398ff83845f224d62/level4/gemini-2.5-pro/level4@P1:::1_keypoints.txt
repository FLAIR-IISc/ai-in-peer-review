- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel using parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to ensure positive-definite matrix-valued covariance.  
- Principled extension of the single-output spectral mixture kernel to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Addresses the challenge of constructing expressive cross-covariance functions that preserve overall positive definiteness.  
- Capable of modeling complex inter-output relationships, including delays and phase shifts, offering increased flexibility and interpretability.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and derivations are logical and easy to follow.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid, grounded in Cramér’s theorem (multivariate Bochner).  
- More flexible and interpretable than prior methods like CSM, notably via explicit modeling of delays between channels.  
- Experimental evaluation is the main weakness, offering limited intuition about learned model behavior on real data.  
- Synthetic experiments effectively validate delay and derivative capture, but real-world results (e.g., Jura) are presented mainly as MAE tables without deeper analysis.  
- Recommends visualizing learned parameters (delays, phases, spectral content) for climate/Jura datasets to demonstrate interpretability and justify complexity.  
- Overall assessment: novel, theoretically sound, and significant contribution suitable for publication, conditional on strengthening experimental insights.
