The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multioutput Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral approach (based on Bochner’s Theorem) to construct kernels. A key advantage of the proposed method is the interpretability of its parameters, such as delays between outputs or phase differences.

Defining covariance structures for multioutput GPs remains a challenging problem with relatively few established methods. The authors’ approach is theoretically sound and demonstrated effectively on several datasets. 

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the illustrative examples are relevant. Assuming parameter learning is not computationally prohibitive – the paper provides limited detail on this aspect – I anticipate this method becoming a standard technique for multioutput GPs.

However, some questions remain. How does the method perform with limited training data? Furthermore (more as a point of curiosity), a channel exhibiting a large lengthscale might be expected to have limited correlation with a channel possessing a small lengthscale. How is this reflected in the model’s behavior?

Specific comments: In the synthetic example (Section 4.1), the number of training points used should be explicitly stated. Minor revisions: I believe a word is missing in lines 74 and 140.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The method is built upon established mathematical principles (Cramér’s Theorem).
*   **Interpretability:** The parameters of the kernel have clear physical interpretations.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The method demonstrates competitive performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
*   **Novelty:** The approach offers a generalisation of existing spectral mixture techniques to the multioutput setting, addressing limitations of previous methods (like CSM).

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Computational Cost:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of the computational cost associated with parameter learning, which could be a practical limitation.
*   **Limited Detail on Parameter Sensitivity:** The impact of different parameter settings on model performance isn’t thoroughly explored.
*   **Minor Presentation Issues:** The paper contains a couple of minor grammatical errors.
