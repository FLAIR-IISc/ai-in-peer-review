This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to GPs that, in my opinion, hasn't received the attention it deserves. This paper extends this concept to the multi-output scenario. A key challenge in the multi-output case is describing cross-covariances, which are not necessarily positive-definite. The authors address this by proposing an approach that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions appear novel to me. The relationship to previous work is well-explained, and I believe the work demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this type. While they don’t provide substantial intuition—particularly as the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret—the experimental evaluation seems appropriate given the theoretical focus of the paper. 

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML-13)*, Atlanta, GA, USA (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
