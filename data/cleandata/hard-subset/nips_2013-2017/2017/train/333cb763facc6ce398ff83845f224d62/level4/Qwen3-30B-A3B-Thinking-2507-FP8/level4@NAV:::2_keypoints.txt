- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and applying inverse Fourier transforms to get cross-covariances.  
- Extends the mixture-of-Gaussians spectral-domain approach of [6] from single-output to multi-output processes.  
- Validated on synthetic data and two standard real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).  
- Clever extension of [6]'s spectral framework to multi-output settings.  
- Fails to demonstrate the extrapolation capability that motivated [6] for the multi-output case.  
- Experiments use only conventional datasets where performance is comparable to existing multi-output covariances, lacking scenarios that challenge current models.  
- Impact would be stronger with inclusion of challenging datasets where existing methods fail.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for Tables 1 and 2; reported differences lie within standard deviation margins, implying negligible distinctions among models.
