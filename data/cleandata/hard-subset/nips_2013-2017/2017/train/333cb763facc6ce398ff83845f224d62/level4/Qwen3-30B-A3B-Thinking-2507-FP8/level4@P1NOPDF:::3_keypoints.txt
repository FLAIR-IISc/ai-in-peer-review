- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multioutput GPs by generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner's theorem).  
- Key advantage: interpretable parameters such as inter-output delays and phase differences.  
- Addresses a challenging area with limited existing methods for defining multioutput GP covariances.  
- Approach is theoretically sound and empirically validated on multiple datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-chosen illustrations and sufficient methodological detail.  
- Concern: the paper lacks depth on parameter learning and whether learning is tractable in practice.  
- Potential impact: could become a standard method for multioutput GP modeling if parameter learning is tractable.  
- Question: how does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Technical question: how does the model reflect the constraint that channels with large length-scales cannot be highly correlated with those having small length-scales?  
- Comment: in Section 4.1 (synthetic example), specify the number of training points.  
- Minor edit: a word appears missing in lines 74 and 140.
