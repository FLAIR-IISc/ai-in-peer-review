- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, an underexplored but valuable contribution.  
- Extends prior spectral-domain GPs to the multi-output setting, building on the authors' earlier work.  
- Identifies cross-covariance modeling as a key challenge because cross-covariances are not necessarily positive-definite.  
- Proposes an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is transparent and accessible.  
- Proposed extensions appear novel to the reviewer.  
- Provides a thorough explanation of relationships to previous work.  
- Reviewer judges the paper to have sufficient novelty to warrant publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is satisfactory for a methodological paper but lacks substantial intuition, especially on the hard-to-interpret Jura dataset.
