This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important idea in GP modeling that, in my view, has not received the attention it deserves. This work extends that approach to the multi-output setting and builds on the ideas of [1]. In the multi-output case, a key challenge is how to specify cross-covariances, which need not be positive definite on their own. The authors propose exactly such a construction, which results in a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to GP fundamentals. The spectral mixture kernel is explained clearly, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to prior work is well articulated, and I believe there is sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this type, though they do not provide much intuition. In particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret. Nevertheless, for a methodological contribution like this, the experimental evaluation is sufficient.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013), Atlanta, GA, USA, 16–21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
