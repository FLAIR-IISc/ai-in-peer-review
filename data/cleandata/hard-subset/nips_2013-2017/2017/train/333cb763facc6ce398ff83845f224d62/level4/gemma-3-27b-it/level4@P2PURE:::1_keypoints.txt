- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain.
- Advocates spectral-domain covariance design as a valuable, historically underappreciated contribution to GPs.
- Extends the spectral approach to the multi-output setting, building on Wilson & Adams (2013).
- Identifies the core challenge: representing cross-covariance functions that are not necessarily positive-definite.
- Proposes a method that resolves this challenge and yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction and GP fundamentals.
- Provides a lucid explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and novel extensions.
- Thoroughly situates the work relative to existing literature and demonstrates sufficient novelty for publication.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a theoretical paper.
- Experiments do not yield substantial new intuition, and the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret.
