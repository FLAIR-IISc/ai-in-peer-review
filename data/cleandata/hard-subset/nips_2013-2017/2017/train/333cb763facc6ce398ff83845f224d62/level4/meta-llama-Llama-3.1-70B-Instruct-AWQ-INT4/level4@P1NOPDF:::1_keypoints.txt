- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function in the spectral domain, an underappreciated contribution to the GP community.  
- Extends previous spectral mixture work to the multi-output setting.  
- Identifies the key challenge that cross-covariances need not be positive-definite in the multi-output case.  
- Proposes an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs to address cross-covariance issues.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and the proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.  
- Provides a thorough discussion of related work and sufficient novelty to justify publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a methodological paper but lacks intuitive insight, particularly on the Jura dataset.
