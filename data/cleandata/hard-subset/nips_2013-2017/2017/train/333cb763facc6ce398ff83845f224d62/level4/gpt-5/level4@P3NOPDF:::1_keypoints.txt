- Proposes a multi-output GP kernel in the spectral domain, extending the spectral mixture (SM) kernel to vector-valued processes by constructing cross-spectral densities that ensure joint positive semidefiniteness.

- Manuscript is clearly written with a concise introduction to GPs and spectral methods and good placement in the literature.

- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and accessible overview of GP fundamentals and the SM kernel.

- Strength: principled and novel extension of spectral mixture ideas to the multi-output setting.

- Strength: construction of cross-spectral densities appears sound and addresses joint positive semidefiniteness.

- Strength: good discussion of related work and connections to existing approaches.

- Weakness: experimental section provides limited intuition; the Jura dataset is hard to interpret for cross-output behavior.

- Weakness: scalability and parameterization complexity not fully discussed; potential optimization, identifiability, and computational-cost issues as outputs or components increase.

- Weakness: unclear exactly what conditions ensure the cross-spectral density matrix is PSD at all frequencies and how these map to practical parameter constraints (weights, phases, bandwidths).

- Weakness: lacks systematic comparisons to strong multi-output baselines (ICM/LMC with Matérn/SE, separable kernels, other spectral variants).

- Suggestion: add qualitative analyses—visualize learned cross-spectral matrices/covariances and inferred frequencies, bandwidths, phases/delays.

- Suggestion: run simple synthetic experiments demonstrating recovery of inter-output delays, negative correlations, and shared/unique frequency structure.

- Suggestion: clarify theoretical guarantees—state sufficient PSD conditions per frequency, summarize proof idea or append appendix reference, and explain enforcement of Hermitian symmetry for real covariances.

- Suggestion: discuss identifiability issues (permutation/scaling of mixture components) and any priors or constraints used to mitigate them.

- Suggestion: expand on computation and scalability—provide complexity in N, P, Q, memory requirements, note exploitable structure (Kronecker/block Toeplitz) or the use of sparse/inducing approximations.

- Suggestion: report number of restarts, initialization strategies for frequencies/bandwidths, sensitivity analyses for Q, and ablation studies.

- Suggestion: strengthen baselines and reporting by including ICM/LMC (Matérn/SE), separable kernels, multi-output spectral kernels, and report uncertainty calibration (e.g., negative log predictive density) besides RMSE/MAE.

- Minor: ensure consistent Fourier transform conventions and clarify how conjugate symmetry is enforced.

- Minor: state whether construction is strictly stationary and discuss extensions to nonstationary multi-output kernels.

- Minor: include a schematic of the per-component parameterization (frequencies, bandwidths, coregionalization/phase parameters).

- Overall: principled, timely contribution that advances spectral methods for multi-output GPs; theory appears sound and exposition is clear, experiments adequate but could use more intuition and comparisons.

- Recommendation: Accept.
