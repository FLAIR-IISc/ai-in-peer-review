- Proposes a new class of multi-output GP covariance functions by specifying cross-power spectral densities and recovering cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform, extending [6].
- Uses Gaussian-mixture spectral modeling (as in [6]) to obtain flexible kernels with potential extrapolation capabilities.
- Evaluated on a synthetic example plus two real datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Extension from single-output to multi-output is a clever and natural idea.
- Important motivation is extrapolation; the paper should demonstrate that the multi-output covariance inherits comparable extrapolation ability.
- Experiments focus on common datasets and show results largely similar to existing multi-output covariances, weakening impact.
- Recommend testing on datasets where competing models struggle to highlight clear advantages.
- Question whether differences in Tables 1 and 2 are statistically significant, since reported standard deviations suggest performance is similar within error bars.
