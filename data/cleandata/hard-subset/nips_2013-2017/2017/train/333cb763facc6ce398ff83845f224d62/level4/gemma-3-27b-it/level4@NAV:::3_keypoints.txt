- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs that generalizes the spectral approach using Bochner’s theorem and prior work by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams.  
- Main strength is interpretable parameters that reveal relationships between outputs (e.g., time delays, phase differences).  
- Addresses a significant gap—defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs—with a theoretically justified and empirically validated method across multiple datasets.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, with well-chosen illustrative examples.  
- Concern about computational feasibility of parameter learning; paper should provide more detail on this point.  
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Question: How does the model capture/reflect that channels with very different length-scales are unlikely to be highly correlated?  
- Comment: Specify the number of training points used in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor editorial remarks: possible missing words at lines 74 and 140.
