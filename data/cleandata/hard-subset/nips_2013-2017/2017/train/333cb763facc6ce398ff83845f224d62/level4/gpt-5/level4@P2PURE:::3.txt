The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), extending the spectral approach based on Bochner’s theorem to construct kernels introduced by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A key advantage of the proposed method is that its parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs or phase differences).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available. The authors’ approach is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.

The paper is well written, the method is clearly described, and the chosen illustrations appear appropriate. If parameter learning is not too difficult (the paper provides little information on this), I would expect this method to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method behave when only a few training points are available?
- (More of a curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel with a small length-scale. How does this appear in your model?
