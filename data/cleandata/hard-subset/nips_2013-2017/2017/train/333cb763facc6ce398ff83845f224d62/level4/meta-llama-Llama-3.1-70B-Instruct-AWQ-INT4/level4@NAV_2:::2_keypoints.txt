- Proposes a new multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms.
- Extends Wilson et al. (2013) spectral mixture idea from single-output to multi-output GPs.
- Enables more flexible and interpretable modeling of inter-channel relationships.
- MOSM kernel can model delays and phase differences; parameters have clear spectral interpretation.
- Provides a clear, thorough theoretical explanation and kernel construction.
- Validated on a synthetic example and two real-world datasets (climate signals, metal concentrations).
- Shows competitive performance versus LMC, CONV, and CSM kernels.
- Experiments may not be challenging enough to showcase MOSM’s unique strengths.
- Results on the two real datasets are similar to other multi-output covariance functions, limiting impact.
- Statistical analysis is limited: MAE standard deviations reported but formal significance tests (e.g., KS or paired t-test) are missing.
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and easy to follow, though contains minor typos.
- Overall promising approach that would benefit from additional experiments and deeper statistical analysis.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 8/10, Impact 7/10, Clarity 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, models delays/phase, clear theory, competitive results.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently challenging experiments, similar empirical results to alternatives, lacks detailed significance testing.
