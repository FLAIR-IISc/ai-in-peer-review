The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), representing a generalization of the spectral approach (Bochner’s Theorem) as applied by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams to kernel construction. A key advantage of this proposed method is the interpretability of its parameters – for instance, as delays or phase differences between outputs.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, and relatively few methods currently exist to address it. The authors’ method is theoretically sound and its effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on several datasets. 

The paper is clearly written and well-structured, and the choice of illustrations appears judicious. Provided parameter learning is not computationally prohibitive (the paper contains limited discussion on this aspect), I anticipate this method could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

Questions:
* How does the method perform with limited training data?
* (As a point of curiosity) Given that a channel with a large length-scale cannot be strongly correlated with a channel possessing a small length-scale, how does this constraint manifest within your model?
