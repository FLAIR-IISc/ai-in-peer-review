- Presents a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral design to MOGPs.  
- Addresses the challenge of describing cross-covariances in the multi-output case and derives a spectral mixture covariance for MOGPs.  
- Builds on and extends prior spectral mixture work ([6], [1]) with a clear theoretical formulation.  
- Clear, well-written paper with a concise introduction to GPs and the spectral mixture kernel.  
- Novelty and relationship to prior models (LMC, CONV) are well explained and justified.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic and real-world data (climate signals, heavy metal concentrations) and shows competitive mean absolute error.  
- Jura dataset results are hard to interpret and the experiments provide limited intuition.  
- Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive (more datasets and comparisons to other MOGP models).  
- Paper is technically sound: claims supported by analysis and experiments (Quality).  
- Paper is clear and well-organized (Clarity).  
- Paper presents an original approach to MOGP covariance design in the spectral domain (Originality).  
- Paper addresses an important problem and makes a significant contribution to GPs (Significance).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept for publication.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, clarity, and sufficient experimental demonstration.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: limited experimental breadth and need for more intuition, especially for Jura results.
