- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a principled Fourier-domain construction for multi-output Gaussian processes that generalizes scalar SM and subsumes SM-LMC and CSM.
- Uses Cramér’s theorem to define complex-valued cross-spectral densities, symmetrize them to ensure real processes, and obtain closed-form cross-covariances via inverse Fourier transform.
- MOSM provides per-pair interpretable parameters (magnitude, mean frequency, bandwidth, phase, delay) enabling modeling of phase differences and explicit time delays.
- Places MOSM within MOGP literature, connecting to LMC, convolved GPs, multivariate Matérn, and other spectral constructions.
- Empirical results: strong synthetic performance (derivatives and delays) and competitive or superior results on two real datasets, especially when delays/phase matter.
- Strength: clear, principled factorization (S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω)) and symmetrization ensure PSD and interpretability.
- Strength: expressive yet interpretable per-pair parameters capture rich cross-channel structure with physical meaning.
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding using Hermitian PSDs and closed-form inverse Fourier transforms.
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrates MOSM’s advantages on tasks tailored to its inductive biases.
- Strength: clarifies relations to prior work and shows SM-LMC and CSM as constrained MOSM cases.
- Weakness: rich parameterization may cause optimization difficulties, identifiability issues, sensitivity to initialization, and component permutation/phase wrapping.
- Weakness: scalability concerns due to O(N^3) GP training cost; no empirical sparse/inducing approximation study provided.
- Weakness: statistical testing methodology (KS test) may be inappropriate; paired tests and multiple comparison corrections are not addressed.
- Weakness: limited ablation studies (e.g., removing delays/phases or sharing parameters) and sensitivity analysis to number of components Q.
- Weakness: insufficient practical details on hyperparameter constraints, initialization, and training times, hindering reproducibility.
- Quality assessment: derivation is sound and synthetic experiments are convincing, but empirical claims need stronger training/initialization details and significance analysis.
- Clarity assessment: paper is well written and organized, but some implementation/training details are sparse.
- Originality assessment: novel extension of spectral mixture ideas to multivariate settings capturing delays and phase.
- Significance assessment: broadly applicable and interpretable method likely useful for multi-sensor and spatiotemporal tasks.
- Questions for authors: how does MOSM perform with few training points per channel and are delays/phase robust in low-data regimes?
- Questions for authors: clarify effect of length-scale mismatch (Σij behavior) on cross-covariance width and practical implications.
- Questions for authors: describe initialization strategy and constraints for µi, Σi, θi, φi and weights to avoid poor optima and label switching.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to choice of Q and whether model selection or low-rank cross-spectra (Q < m) are used.
- Questions for authors: explain PSD guarantees after symmetrization and whether constraints are enforced during optimization.
- Questions for authors: report typical training times and memory vs CSM/SM-LMC, and any preliminary inducing-feature results.
- Suggestions: add practical training guidance (initialization, constraints, optimizer settings), training times, and sensitivity analyses for Q.
- Suggestions: include ablations isolating delays/phases and robustness to mis-specified Q.
- Suggestions: use paired statistical tests with multiple-comparison correction for per-instance errors.
- Suggestions: discuss identifiability and phase–delay trade-offs and any regularization used.
- Suggestions: clarify noise model (per-output noise, cross-noise terms) and release code for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, general, interpretable MOGP kernel capturing delays/phase and subsuming existing spectral MOGP kernels.
- Cons against acceptance: limited discussion on optimization, identifiability, scalability, modest real-data gains, and few ablations.
- Minor remarks: state number of training points and noise levels clearly, fix missing words at lines noted, and clarify KS test details (one-/two-sided, corrections).
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and well-motivated contribution; lean toward acceptance conditional on addressing training details and strengthening empirical analysis.
