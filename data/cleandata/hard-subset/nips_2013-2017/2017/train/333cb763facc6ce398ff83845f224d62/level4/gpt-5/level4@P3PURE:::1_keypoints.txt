- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral-design ideas to multi-output GPs.
- Addresses the key multi-output challenge of specifying cross-covariances that need not be positive definite.
- Proposes an approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.
- Builds on and extends prior single-output spectral mixture work (Wilson & Adams, 2013).
- Paper is well written with a concise, clear introduction and explanation of GP fundamentals.
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is clear and appears novel.
- Relationship to prior work is well articulated and shows sufficient novelty for publication.
- Experiments are adequate for a methodological paper but provide limited intuition.
- The Jura dataset results are difficult to interpret, though the overall experimental evaluation is deemed sufficient.
