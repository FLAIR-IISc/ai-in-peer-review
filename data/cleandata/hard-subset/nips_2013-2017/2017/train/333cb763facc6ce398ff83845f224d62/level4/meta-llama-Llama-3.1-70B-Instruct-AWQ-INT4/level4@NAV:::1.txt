This paper introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, a valuable contribution to the field of Gaussian processes that warrants further exploration. By extending the work presented in [1] to the multi-output scenario, the authors address the challenge of describing cross-covariance without the need for positive-definiteness. The proposed approach leads to the development of a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.

The paper is well-structured, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, original. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the relationship to previous work, demonstrating sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experimental evaluation, while not particularly insightful, especially with regards to the Jura dataset, is satisfactory for a paper of this nature. Overall, the authors present a well-written and coherent manuscript that contributes meaningfully to the field.
