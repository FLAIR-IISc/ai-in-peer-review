Review- The paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, extending the spectral kernel construction (via Bochner’s theorem) introduced by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A key strength of the approach is that its parameters are interpretable (e.g., inter-output delays and phase differences).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is challenging, and comparatively few methods exist. The proposed approach is theoretically grounded and its efficacy is demonstrated on multiple datasets.

The method is clearly explained in a well-written manuscript, and the illustrative examples appear well chosen. If parameter learning is not too difficult—an aspect for which the paper provides limited detail—I would expect this approach to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method perform when only a small number of training points is available?
- (Mostly out of curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel with a small length-scale. How is this represented in your model?
