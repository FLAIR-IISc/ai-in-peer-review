- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Extends the single-output spectral mixture approach from [6] (mixture of Gaussians PSDs) to the multi-output setting.  
- Experiments on a synthetic dataset, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset demonstrate the method.  
- Extension from [6] is a clever strategy for constructing new multi-output kernels.  
- The paper does not investigate whether the multi-output kernel inherits the strong extrapolation capabilities emphasized in [6].  
- Experimental evaluation uses common datasets and yields performance comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions.  
- Impact could be increased by testing on datasets where current models perform poorly or struggle.  
- Statistical significance of results in Tables 1 and 2 is unclear; a formal statistical test should be reported to confirm differences.
