- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral-design to multi-output GPs.  
- Builds on prior work and addresses the challenge of describing cross-covariance that need not be positive-definite.  
- Introduces a novel approach that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and explanation of GP fundamentals.  
- Clear explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and novel extensions, demonstrating sufficient novelty.  
- Provides a clear spectral interpretation of kernel parameters, enabling joint modeling of delays and phase differences.  
- Validates the interpretation experimentally on a synthetic example.  
- Empirical evaluation on a synthetic example and two real datasets (including Jura) shows competitive performance.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate but lacks intuitive insight, especially for the Jura dataset.  
- Quality: 9/10 — technically sound with well-supported claims.  
- Clarity: 9/10 — well-explained and readable.  
- Originality: 9/10 — novel approach for multi-output spectral covariances.  
- Significance: 9/10 — potential for broad applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept for publication at NIPS.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, thorough exposition, validated on synthetic and real data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments could offer more intuition and additional comparisons to strengthen empirical claims.
