This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function that is defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to GPs that has not received sufficient attention. This paper extends this approach to the multi-output scenario, addressing the challenge of describing cross-covariance, which does not have to be positive-definite. The authors propose a novel approach to tackle this issue, leading to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a clear and concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is thorough, and the proposed extensions are novel. The relationship to previous work is well-explained, and there is sufficient novelty in the work to justify publication.

The experiments are sufficient for a paper of this nature, although they may not provide a lot of intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset. However, for a paper that focuses on theoretical contributions, the experimental evaluation is adequate.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors have done an excellent job in providing a thorough explanation of the proposed approach and its relationship to previous work.

The originality of the paper lies in its extension of the spectral mixture concept to multi-output GPs, providing a principled approach to designing multivariate covariance functions. The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to advance the state of the art in GP research.

The significance of the paper is evident in its potential to impact various applications that involve multi-output GP regression. The proposed approach can be used to model complex relationships between multiple outputs, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to GP research, and its publication is justified. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides a novel approach to multi-output GP regression.
