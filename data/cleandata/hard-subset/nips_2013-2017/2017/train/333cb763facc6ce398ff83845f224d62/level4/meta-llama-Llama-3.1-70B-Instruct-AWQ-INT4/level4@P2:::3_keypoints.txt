- Introduces a new covariance structure (MOSM kernel) for multi-output Gaussian processes generalizing spectral approaches (Bochner Theorem).
- MOSM parameters are interpretable (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) enabling identification of delays and phase differences between outputs.
- Defining covariance structures for MOGPs is challenging and the proposed method fills a notable gap.
- The method is theoretically sound and backed by clear descriptions of the theoretical background.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate effectiveness and competitive performance with existing MOGP models.
- MOSM uniquely and jointly models delays and phase differences, validated in a synthetic example.
- Paper is well written with relevant illustrations and clear presentation of results.
- Unclear how the method performs with few training points; behavior in low-data regimes needs investigation.
- Paper lacks sufficient details about parameter learning and optimization procedures.
- Minor issues: missing words in lines 74 and 140, and the synthetic example omits the number of training points.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential to become a standard for multi-output GPs.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10 and reviewer confidence 8/10.
