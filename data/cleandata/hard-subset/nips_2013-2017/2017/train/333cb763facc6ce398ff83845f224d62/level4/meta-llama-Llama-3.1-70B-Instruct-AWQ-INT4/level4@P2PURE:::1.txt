This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, a valuable contribution to the field of Gaussian processes that has not received the attention it deserves. The authors extend this approach to the multi-output scenario, building upon their previous work [1]. A key challenge in this context is modeling cross-covariance, which is not necessarily positive-definite. The authors address this issue by proposing an approach that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and the fundamentals of Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is transparent, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the relationship to previous work, and I believe that the paper presents sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is satisfactory for a paper of this nature, although it does not provide substantial intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset, which is difficult to interpret. Nevertheless, I consider the experimental evaluation to be sufficient for a paper focused on methodological contributions.
