

This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain represents an important contribution to Gaussian processes that, in my view, has not received sufficient attention. The authors extend this approach to the multi-output setting, addressing the challenge of describing cross-covariance, which need not be positive-definite. The paper presents a solution that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is accessible, and the proposed extensions appear novel to my knowledge. The relationship to prior work is thoroughly explained, and I believe the work demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication.  

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a paper of this nature. While the experiments do not provide extensive interpretability—particularly regarding the Jura dataset—they are sufficient for validating the proposed methodology.
