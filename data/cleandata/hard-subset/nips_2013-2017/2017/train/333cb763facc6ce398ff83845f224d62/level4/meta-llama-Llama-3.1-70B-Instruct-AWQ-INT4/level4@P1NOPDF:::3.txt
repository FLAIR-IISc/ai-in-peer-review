The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to construct kernels, as presented by Adams and Adams. A key strength of the proposed method is that its parameters can be interpreted, such as the delay between outputs or phase difference.

The definition of covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, with relatively few existing methods addressing this issue. The authors' proposed method is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated through experiments on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choice of illustrations appears relevant. If parameter learning does not pose significant challenges (although the paper provides limited information on this aspect), I would expect this method to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

Questions:
* How does the method perform when only a few training points are available?
* (Primarily out of curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel having a small length-scale. How is this scenario represented in the proposed model?
