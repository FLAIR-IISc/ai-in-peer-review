- Introduces a novel multi-output GP covariance construction by parameterizing cross-power spectral densities in the spectral domain and recovering covariances via inverse Fourier transform.
- Extends prior single-output spectral approach [6] (Gaussian-mixture PSD → flexible kernel with strong extrapolation) to the multi-output setting.
- Empirical validation performed on a synthetic dataset and two real-world benchmarks (weather and Swiss Jura heavy metal concentrations).
- The generalization is conceptually elegant and promising for new covariance functions.
- The paper fails to investigate whether the multi-output extension retains the strong extrapolation capability emphasized in [6].
- Evaluation is limited to standard benchmarks, where the proposed model is comparable but not superior to existing multi-output kernels.
- Recommendation to test the method on more challenging problems where conventional models struggle to better demonstrate unique advantages.
- Raises concern about statistical significance: performance metrics in Tables 1–2 appear to overlap given reported standard deviations, so significance tests are needed.
