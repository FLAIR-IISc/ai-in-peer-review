The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes (GPs), corresponding to a generalization of the spectral approach (via Bochner’s theorem) for kernel construction from A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A notable strength of the proposed method is the interpretability of its parameters (e.g., inter-output delays or phase differences).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, and relatively few methods are currently available. The authors’ method is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the chosen illustrations are appropriate. If parameter learning is not too troublesome (the paper provides little information on this), I would expect this method to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method behave when few training points are available?
- (More out of curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel with a small length-scale. How does this manifest in your model?
