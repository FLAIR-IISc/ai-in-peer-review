- Novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral approach of A.G. and R.P. Adams via Bochner’s Theorem.
- Model parameters are interpretable, directly relating to delays or phase differences between outputs.
- Addresses the challenging problem of defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs, where few effective methods exist.
- The method is theoretically well-founded and demonstrates promising performance across multiple datasets.
- Paper is clearly written and includes well-chosen, relevant illustrative examples.
- Computational feasibility of parameter learning is not fully detailed and is a significant open concern.
- If parameter learning is feasible, the method could become a standard approach for multi-output GP modeling.
- Question: How does performance degrade with limited training data; is it robust in low-data regimes like single-output GPs?
- Question: How does the model’s implicit assumption relating channels by length-scales manifest in behavior and learned parameters?
- Comment: Specify the number of training points used in the synthetic experiment (Section 4.1).
