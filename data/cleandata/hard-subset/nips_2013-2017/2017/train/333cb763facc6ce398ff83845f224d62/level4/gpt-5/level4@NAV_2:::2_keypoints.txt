- Proposes a new multi-output GP covariance by specifying complex cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforming to cross-covariances.  
- Mirrors single-output method of [6], which uses Gaussian-mixture PSDs inverted to yield flexible kernels with strong extrapolation.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic example and two real datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura heavy metal concentrations.  
- Adapting [6] to multi-output is a compelling direction for deriving new cross-covariance functions.  
- Should demonstrate whether the proposed multi-output kernel retains the strong extrapolative behavior emphasized in [6].  
- Experiments use widely adopted datasets and show performance broadly comparable to existing multi-output kernels.  
- Impact could increase by including tasks where competing approaches are known to struggle or fail.  
- Questions whether statistical significance was assessed for Tables 1 and 2, since reported standard deviations suggest many comparisons may be indistinguishable.
