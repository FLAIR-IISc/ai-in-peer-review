This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to GPs that, in my view, has not received the attention it deserves. This work extends that idea to the multi-output setting. The paper builds on the work in [1]. In the multi-output case, the key challenge is how to specify cross-covariances, which do not have to be positive-definite. The authors present an approach that addresses this in a way that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.
The paper is well written, with a brief and clear introduction to the problem and to GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are, to the best of my knowledge, novel. The links to prior work are well explained, and I believe there is sufficient novelty to merit publication.
The experiments are adequate for a paper of this type, though they provide limited intuition; in particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret. Nonetheless, for a paper like this, I find the experimental evaluation sufficient.
