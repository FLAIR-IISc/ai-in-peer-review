- Proposes MOSM kernel by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transform to obtain MOGP cross-covariances.  
- Extends the spectral mixture idea from single-output GPs to multi-output GPs.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic example and two real datasets (weather and Swiss Jura for metal concentrations).  
- Experimental results show the MOSM kernel is competitive with existing MOGP models.  
- Theoretical background and method are presented clearly and concisely, accessible to GP readers.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Originality: novel application of spectral mixture ideas to MOGPs, though conceptually related to prior work.  
- Significance: method has potential to improve MOGP performance in applications where current models struggle.  
- Experimental limitations: needs more diverse and challenging datasets to demonstrate clear advantages.  
- Impact concern: results are similar to other covariance functions, which may limit the paper’s impact.  
- Missing statistical analysis: Tables lack standard deviations, so significance of performance differences is unclear.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, solid theory, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficiently diverse experiments, lack of statistical significance discussion, and unclear practical gains.
