

This paper proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner's theorem) to construct kernels. A key advantage is the interpretability of model parameters, such as delays between outputs or phase differences.  

The development of covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains challenging, with limited existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically rigorous and empirically validated across multiple datasets, demonstrating clear effectiveness. The paper is well-written, with a clear exposition of the method and relevant experimental choices. If parameter learning proves computationally tractable (a detail not elaborated in the paper), this method has strong potential to become a standard for multi-output GP modeling.  

**Questions:**  
1. How does the method perform under small training set sizes?  
2. (Curiosity) A channel with a large length scale cannot exhibit strong correlation with one having a small length scale. How does this constraint manifest in the model?  

**Comments:**  
- Section 4.1 (synthetic example): Specify the number of training points.  
- Minor: A word appears missing in lines 74 and 140.  

**Overall:** The contribution addresses a meaningful gap in multi-output GP modeling with sound theory and compelling results. The paper is well-structured and accessible. Addressing the parameter learning details and the two questions above would substantially strengthen the work. The method’s interpretability and theoretical foundation make it a promising advancement for the field.
