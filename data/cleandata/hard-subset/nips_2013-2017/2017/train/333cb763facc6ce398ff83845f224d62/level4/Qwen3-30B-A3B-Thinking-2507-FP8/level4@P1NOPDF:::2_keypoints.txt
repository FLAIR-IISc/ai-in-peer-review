- Introduces a novel multi-output GP covariance by specifying cross-power spectral densities, extending [6]’s spectral mixture approach.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic dataset, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.  
- Does not demonstrate whether the proposed kernel inherits the extrapolation capability emphasized in [6], a core motivation.  
- Without evidence of extrapolation, the contribution appears incremental rather than transformative.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: used standard benchmarks where results are only comparable to existing methods.  
- Missing experiments on challenging datasets (complex output dependencies or sparse multi-task settings) that could highlight advantages.  
- Statistical significance of reported differences is unclear; overlapping standard deviations require formal tests (e.g., paired t-tests or confidence intervals).  
- Overall contributions are underdeveloped; must address extrapolation, harder benchmarks, and rigorous statistical validation to strengthen impact.
