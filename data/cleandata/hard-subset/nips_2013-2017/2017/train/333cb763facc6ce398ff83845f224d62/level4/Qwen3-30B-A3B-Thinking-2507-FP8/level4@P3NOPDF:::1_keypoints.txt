1. Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance in the spectral domain, extending Wilson & Adams (2013) spectral mixture kernel.  
2. Tackles the challenge that cross-covariances need not be positive-definite while the full covariance must be PSD by constructing valid cross-covariances in the spectral domain.  
3. This spectral-domain approach is a significant, non-trivial advancement in an underexplored area of GP literature.  
4. Paper is exceptionally well-written with a concise introduction and lucid, technically sound exposition.  
5. Prior work (especially Wilson & Adams 2013) is thoroughly discussed and the novelty is clearly justified.  
6. The method preserves the interpretability and flexibility of the univariate spectral mixture kernel for multi-output learning.  
7. Experiments are adequate for scope, using standard benchmarks (e.g., Jura) and relevant baselines.  
8. Quantitative results are consistent and competitive, but experimental analysis lacks intuitive interpretation, particularly for the Jura dataset.  
9. Appropriate ablation studies and runtime analyses are included, supporting practical viability.  
10. Minor wording issue: the draft’s phrasing about cross-covariances and positive-definiteness is misleading and should clarify that the joint covariance must be PSD.  
11. Overall a valuable contribution that meets NIPS standards; recommend acceptance with a suggestion to enhance experimental discussion for clearer intuition.
