- Presents a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function designed in the spectral domain, extending spectral-domain GP design to the multi-output case.  
- Proposes a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and constructs multivariate covariances using Cramér’s Theorem.  
- Method models inter-channel delays and phase differences, making it more expressive than previous approaches, with clear spectral interpretation of parameters.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction and a well-explained spectral mixture kernel and relation to prior work.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims; paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Experiments are sufficient to demonstrate effectiveness but lack intuition and clearer interpretation, notably for the Jura dataset.  
- Paper has potential significance for applications like climate modeling, signal processing, and geostatistics.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
