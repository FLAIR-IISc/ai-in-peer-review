- Introduces a new class of covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes using cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transform.  
- Method mirrors [6]: model power spectral density as a Gaussian mixture and invert to obtain kernels (extrapolation capability in single-output case).  
- Evaluated on one synthetic example and two real datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).  
- Extending [6] to multi-output is appealing; need to demonstrate that extrapolation ability carries over to the multi-output version.  
- Experimental results on common benchmarks show performance comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions.  
- Impact would be stronger with experiments on datasets where competing models are known to struggle or fail.  
- No statistical significance analysis reported for Tables 1 and 2; given the standard deviations, most models seem to perform similarly.
