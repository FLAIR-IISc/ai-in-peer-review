

The paper presents a novel covariance structure for multioutput Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to construct kernels via A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A key advantage is the interpretability of model parameters, such as delays between outputs or phase differences.  

Defining covariance structures for multioutput GPs remains a challenging problem with limited existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically rigorous, and its efficacy is convincingly demonstrated across multiple datasets. The paper is clearly written with well-chosen illustrative examples. Provided parameter learning is feasible (the paper offers limited details on this aspect), this method has strong potential to become a standard for multioutput GPs.  

Questions  
* How does the method perform with limited training data?  
* (Curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one having a small length-scale. How does this constraint manifest in your model?
