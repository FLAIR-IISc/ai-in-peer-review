- Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined via its spectral density, extending spectral-domain design to multi-output GPs.  
- Builds on Wilson & Adams (2013) spectral mixture kernel and addresses the under-explored spectral design approach for GPs.  
- Central challenge: modeling cross-covariance structure for multi-output GPs; paper focuses on this problem.  
- Key insight: cross-covariances need not be positive definite, enabling an elegant, principled, and flexible spectral mixture solution.  
- Proposed multi-output spectral mixture covariance is novel and well-motivated.  
- Paper is well-written, provides clear background and lucid explanation of the single-output kernel and the multi-output extension.  
- Methodological contribution is sufficiently novel and well-contextualized relative to prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate to validate the framework but offers limited qualitative intuition.  
- Specific empirical results (e.g., Jura dataset) are hard to interpret from a domain perspective, though overall experiments support the method.
