

The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multioutput Gaussian processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner's theorem) to construct kernels. A key advantage is the interpretability of model parameters, such as delays between outputs and phase differences.  

Defining covariance structures for multioutput GPs remains challenging, with limited existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically sound and empirically validated across multiple datasets. The paper is clearly written, with well-chosen illustrations, and the method is presented with sufficient detail. Provided parameter learning is tractable (the paper lacks depth on this aspect), this method could become a standard for multioutput GP modeling.  

**Questions:**  
- How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- (A technical curiosity) Since channels with large length-scales cannot exhibit high correlation with those having small length-scales, how does this constraint manifest in your model?
