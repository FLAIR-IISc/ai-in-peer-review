The paper proposes a new covariance construction for multi-output Gaussian processes, extending the spectral methodology based on Bochner’s theorem used to define spectral mixture kernels. A key advantage is that the model’s parameters are interpretable, capturing quantities such as inter-channel delays and phase offsets.

Designing covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem with relatively few existing solutions. The authors’ approach is theoretically well-founded, and its effectiveness is demonstrated across multiple datasets.

The manuscript is clearly written, with an accessible presentation and appropriate experimental choices. Assuming hyperparameter learning is not onerous (the paper provides limited detail on this), I would expect this approach to become a standard tool for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method perform when only a small number of training points is available?
- (Curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one with a small length-scale. How does this manifest in your model?
