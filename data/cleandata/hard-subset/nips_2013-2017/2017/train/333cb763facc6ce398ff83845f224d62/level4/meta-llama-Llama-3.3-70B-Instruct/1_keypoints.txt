- Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, filling an overlooked gap.  
- Extends prior work ([1]) to multi-output GPs, tackling cross-covariance modeling without requiring positive-definiteness.  
- Proposes a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Paper is well-structured with a concise, clear introduction and GP fundamentals.  
- Provides a thorough explanation of the spectral mixture kernel.  
- Proposed extensions appear original and demonstrate sufficient novelty for publication.  
- Effectively situates the work within existing literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a theory-focused paper but lacks extensive intuitive insight (notably on the Jura dataset).  
- Empirical results support the validity of the proposed approach.  
- Overall assessment: significant advancement in GP research and publication is justified.
