- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain.  
- Emphasizes that designing covariance functions in the spectral domain is valuable and underappreciated.  
- Extends the spectral mixture approach of Wilson & Adams (2013) to the multi-output setting.  
- Identifies accurate modeling of cross-covariance (which may not be positive-definite) as a key challenge.  
- Proposes a method that yields a spectral mixture covariance suitable for multi-output GPs and addresses the cross-covariance issue.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear, concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions is lucid and appears novel.  
- Authors demonstrate strong understanding of related work and position their contribution effectively.  
- Contribution exhibits sufficient novelty to warrant publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for a theoretical paper but lacks deep insights, particularly on the Jura dataset.  
- Suggests additional simpler experiments or visualizations could improve intuitive understanding, though current evaluation is not a major weakness.
