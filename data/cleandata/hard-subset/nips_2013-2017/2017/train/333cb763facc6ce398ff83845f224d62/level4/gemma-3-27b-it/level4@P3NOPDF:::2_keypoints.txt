- Proposes defining multi-output GP cross-covariances via cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforms, rather than direct covariance specification.
- Builds on [6]’s spectral mixture idea for single-output GPs to extend PSD-based kernels to multi-output settings.
- Demonstrated on synthetic data and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- The extension is conceptually elegant and potentially valuable for multi-output GPs.
- The paper does not explicitly investigate or demonstrate extrapolation capabilities, a key motivation of [6].
- Experimental evaluation uses common benchmark datasets and shows only modest, largely comparable gains over existing methods.
- The work would be strengthened by testing on datasets where current multi-output GPs struggle to show distinct advantages or unique suitability.
- Reported results show overlapping standard deviations, leaving it unclear if differences are statistically significant.
- The paper should include formal statistical tests (e.g., t-test, ANOVA with post-hoc analysis) to substantiate performance claims.
- Overall technically sound but limited experimental scope and lack of statistical rigor weaken the paper’s impact.
- Addressing extrapolation experiments and adding statistical analysis would substantially improve the contribution.
