The paper proposes a novel type of covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes, deviating from the conventional approach of directly specifying cross-covariance functions. Instead, the authors suggest defining cross-power spectral densities and then utilizing the inverse Fourier transform to derive the corresponding cross-covariances. This concept is an extension of the idea presented in [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where the power spectral density was represented as a mixture of Gaussians and subsequently back-transformed to obtain a powerful kernel function with extrapolation capabilities.

Building upon the idea in [6] appears to be a clever approach to constructing new types of covariance functions for multiple outputs. One of the primary motivations in [6] was to develop a kernel function capable of extrapolation. It would be intriguing to see if the proposed covariance function for multiple outputs possesses similar extrapolation abilities. However, the experimental section only presents results using relatively common datasets, and the obtained results are comparable to those achieved with other covariance functions for multiple outputs. To increase the paper's impact, it would be beneficial to include experiments on datasets where existing models are not suitable.

It is unclear whether the authors performed tests for statistical significance of the results presented in Tables 1 and 2. Upon inspection, it appears that almost all models exhibit similar performance within the standard deviation, which raises questions about the significance of the reported results.
