This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process (MOGP) covariance function defined in the spectral domain, extending the work of Wilson & Adams (2013) [1]. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to the GP literature that has arguably not received the attention it deserves. In extending this approach to the multi-output scenario, the key challenge is modeling the cross-covariance function, which is not required to be positive definite. This paper describes a principled method to address this challenge, leading to the development of the spectral mixture covariance for MOGPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed multi-output extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to previous work is also well-explained. I believe the work contains sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments are sufficient for a methodological contribution of this nature. However, they provide limited intuition; the results on the Jura dataset, in particular, are difficult to reason about. Despite this, I find the experimental evaluation to be adequate for this paper.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013* (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
