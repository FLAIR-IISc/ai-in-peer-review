This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to Gaussian processes that, in my opinion, has not received the attention it deserves. This paper extends this approach to the multi-output scenario. The work builds upon the authors’ previous work [1]; however, a key challenge in the multi-output case is describing the cross-covariance, which is not necessarily positive-definite. This paper addresses this challenge by introducing an approach that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions appear novel to the best of my knowledge. The relationship to previous work is well explained, and I believe the work demonstrates sufficient novelty to justify publication. 

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this nature. While they do not provide substantial intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset – which is difficult to interpret – I believe the experimental evaluation is sufficient given the theoretical focus of the work.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013*, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
