- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain.  
- Emphasizes spectral-domain covariance design as an important, underappreciated GP contribution.  
- Extends spectral mixture approach to multi-output GPs, generalizing Wilson & Adams (2013).  
- Addresses the significant challenge of modeling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.  
- Proposes an elegant, well-executed solution for handling non-positive-definite cross-covariances via spectral modeling.  
- Writing is clear, concise, and well-structured with a lucid explanation of the spectral mixture kernel.  
- The proposed extensions appear novel and are justified relative to prior work.  
- Relationship to prior literature is thoroughly and accurately articulated.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate and demonstrates method efficacy.  
- Analysis of the Jura dataset lacks interpretability and stronger intuitive insights.  
- Given the paper’s theoretical focus, the empirical validation is sufficiently rigorous for publication.
