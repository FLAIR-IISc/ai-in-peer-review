

The paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes by specifying cross-power spectral densities rather than direct cross-covariance functions, leveraging the inverse Fourier transform to derive the corresponding cross-covariances. This approach extends the methodology of [6] for single-output processes, where a mixture-of-Gaussians representation of the power spectral density enabled extrapolation-capable kernel functions. The authors validate the proposed covariance using synthetic data and two standard real-world datasets: weather data and the Swiss Jura dataset for metal concentrations. While extending [6]'s spectral-domain framework to multi-output settings is a clever strategy, the paper fails to demonstrate the extrapolation capability that motivated [6] for the multi-output case. Furthermore, the experimental section relies solely on conventional datasets where results are comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions, without exploring scenarios where current models are inadequate. The impact would be strengthened by including such challenging datasets. Additionally, statistical significance testing for the results in Tables 1 and 2 is absent; performance differences fall within standard deviation margins, suggesting negligible distinctions among models.
