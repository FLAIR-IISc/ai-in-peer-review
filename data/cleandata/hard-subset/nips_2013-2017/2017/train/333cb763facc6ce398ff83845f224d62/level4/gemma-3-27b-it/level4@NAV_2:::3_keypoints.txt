- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multioutput GPs generalizing spectral approaches via Bochner/Cramér’s theorem.  
- Key advantage: kernel parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs, phase differences).  
- Theoretical foundation is strong and grounded in established mathematical principles.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real-world datasets with competitive performance.  
- Method is clearly described and well-written with relevant illustrative examples.  
- Potential to become a standard technique for multioutput GPs, assuming parameter learning is tractable.  
- Major concern: paper lacks detailed discussion of computational cost for parameter learning.  
- Unclear how the method performs with limited training data—needs evaluation.  
- Question about model behavior when channels have very different lengthscales (impact on inter-channel correlation).  
- Limited analysis of parameter sensitivity and the effect of different parameter settings on performance.  
- Specific request: state the number of training points used in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor presentation issues: likely missing words at lines 74 and 140; a couple of grammatical errors.
