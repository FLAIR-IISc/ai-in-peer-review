The paper introduces a new class of covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes. Rather than specifying cross-covariances directly, the authors define cross-power spectral densities and recover the corresponding cross-covariances via the inverse Fourier transform. This follows the approach of [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where the power spectral density is modeled as a mixture of Gaussians and then back-transformed to yield a flexible kernel with extrapolation capabilities. The proposed covariance is evaluated on a synthetic example and two real datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura dataset of metal concentrations.

Extending the idea from [6] to the multi-output setting is a clever and natural way to construct new covariance functions. One of the key motivations in [6] was to enable extrapolation. It would be valuable to demonstrate that the proposed multi-output covariance inherits comparable extrapolation ability. The experimental section, however, focuses on very common datasets, and the reported results are largely similar to those obtained with existing multi-output covariance functions. The paper’s impact would be strengthened by including experiments on datasets where other models are known to struggle or are not well suited, thereby highlighting a clear advantage of the proposed approach.
