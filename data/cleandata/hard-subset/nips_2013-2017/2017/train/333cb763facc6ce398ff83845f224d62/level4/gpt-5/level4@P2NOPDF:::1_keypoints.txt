- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending spectral kernels to multi-output settings.  
- Emphasizes that spectral-domain kernel design is important and underappreciated for GPs.  
- Builds on the spectral mixture framework of Wilson and Adams (2013).  
- Central challenge: specifying cross-covariances so the joint covariance remains positive semidefinite.  
- Proposes a principled spectral-domain construction that yields a valid multi-output spectral mixture kernel.  
- Construction appears novel to the reviewer.  
- Paper is well written with a clear, concise introduction and accessible explanation of the spectral mixture and its extension.  
- Relationship to prior work is well articulated and the level of novelty justifies publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate to show feasibility and benefits but offers limited intuition.  
- Jura dataset used in experiments is hard to interpret and provides weak qualitative insight.
