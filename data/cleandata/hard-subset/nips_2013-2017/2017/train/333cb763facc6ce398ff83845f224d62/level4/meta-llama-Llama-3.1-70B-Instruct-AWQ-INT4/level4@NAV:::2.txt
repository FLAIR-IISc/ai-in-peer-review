This paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes by specifying cross-power spectral densities and deriving the corresponding cross-covariances through the inverse Fourier transform. This approach builds upon the concept presented in [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where a mixture of Gaussians was used to represent the power spectral density, yielding a powerful kernel function with extrapolation capabilities. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their proposed covariance function using a synthetic data example and two real-world datasets, namely the weather dataset and the Swiss Jura dataset for metal concentrations.

Extending the idea from [6] to construct new covariance functions for multiple outputs is an innovative approach. However, it would be beneficial to equip this multi-output covariance function with extrapolation abilities, as was a primary motivation in [6]. The experimental evaluation is limited to commonly used datasets, resulting in performance comparable to other multi-output covariance functions. To amplify the paper's impact, it would be advantageous to include experiments on datasets where existing models are inadequate.

Furthermore, it is unclear whether the authors performed statistical significance tests for the results presented in Tables 1 and 2. Given the standard deviations, the performance of nearly all models appears similar, suggesting that statistical significance testing may provide valuable insights into the differences between the models.
