- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multioutput GPs by generalizing the spectral (Bochner) approach.  
- Key advantage: interpretable parameters (e.g., delays between outputs, phase differences).  
- Addresses a challenging, underexplored problem of defining multioutput GP covariances.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous.  
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate efficacy across multiple datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with good illustrative examples.  
- Limited details provided on parameter learning; feasibility of parameter estimation is a concern.  
- Potential to become a standard method for multioutput GPs if parameter learning is practical.  
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Question/curiosity: How does the model handle correlation constraints when channels have very different length-scales?  
- Comment: Specify the number of training points in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor remarks: A word appears to be missing in lines 74 and 140.
