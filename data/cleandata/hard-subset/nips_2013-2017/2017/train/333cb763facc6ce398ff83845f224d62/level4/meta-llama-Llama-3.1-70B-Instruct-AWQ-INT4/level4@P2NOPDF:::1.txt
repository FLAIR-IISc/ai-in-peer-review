This paper introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, a crucial contribution to Gaussian processes (GPs) that has been overlooked in the past. By extending the approach to the multi-output scenario, the authors build upon their previous work [1], addressing the challenge of describing cross-covariance in a manner that does not require positive-definiteness.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors effectively establish connections to previous work, demonstrating sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is satisfactory for a paper of this nature, although it may not provide substantial intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset, which can be difficult to interpret. Nevertheless, I believe the experiments are adequate for a paper focused on methodological contributions.

Reference:
[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
