This paper introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function formulated within the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain represents a valuable, yet often underappreciated, contribution to the field of Gaussian processes. This work builds upon that foundation by extending it to the multi-output setting, which introduces the significant challenge of modeling cross-covariance functions that may not be positive-definite. The authors successfully address this challenge, presenting a method that results in a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is clearly written, offering a concise and accessible introduction to the problem and relevant Gaussian process concepts. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is well-articulated, and the proposed extensions appear novel to the best of my knowledge. The authors provide a thorough discussion of related work, and I believe the presented work demonstrates sufficient originality to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a paper of this nature. While the results do not offer substantial intuitive insight – particularly regarding the Jura dataset which is difficult to interpret – the scope of experimentation is reasonable given the theoretical focus of the work.
