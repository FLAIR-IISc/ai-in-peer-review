- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that builds multi-output covariance functions via complex-valued cross-spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforms.  
- Key construction: factorize cross-spectrum S(ω) = R(ω) H R(ω) with complex SE components, symmetrize to obtain real-valued processes and ensure positive definiteness.  
- Time/space-domain kernel yields per-output-pair interpretable parameters: magnitude, frequency, length-scale, phase, and delay.  
- MOSM can be non-symmetric across outputs and models both phase shifts and explicit inter-channel delays, unlike LMC/CONV.  
- MOSM subsumes prior kernels: reduces to SM-LMC/CSM in restricted cases.  
- Theoretical contribution is principled: PD guaranteed frequency-wise, closed-form inverse FT, and straightforward rank-Q extension.  
- Empirical evaluation: strong synthetic results (derivative+delay task) where MOSM outperforms CONV/SM-LMC and improves on CSM.  
- Empirics on climate data: MOSM competitive with CSM and often better than SM-LMC/CONV; some statistically significant differences.  
- Jura dataset: MOSM achieves best MAE for Cadmium and competitive results for Copper.  
- Optimization concerns: objective is multimodal with many hyperparameters; initialization strategies and sensitivity to Q/rank are under-discussed.  
- Scalability concerns: training is O(N^3); no runtime/memory profiling or sparse/inducing-variable variants presented.  
- Evaluation metrics: primarily MAE used; lacks probabilistic assessments such as NLPD, calibration, and coverage.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with transparent derivations and explicit relation to prior work.  
- Minor clarity issues: need to clarify identifiability (phase vs delay, per-output vs per-pair parameters) and reconcile statements about αij sign.  
- Reproducibility gaps: provide initialization heuristics (FFT/periodogram), practical constraints on Σ, µ, θ, and report training time/memory.  
- Originality: novel extension of spectral mixture modeling to multi-output via complex spectral factorization that jointly models phase and delays.  
- Significance: valuable for multi-sensor, time-series, and spatio-temporal problems where lags and phases matter; advances interpretable multi-output kernels.  
- Pros: principled Cramér-based construction, interpretable parameters, generalizes prior models, closed-form kernel, strong synthetic demonstration of capabilities.  
- Cons: limited discussion of optimization/identifiability, scalability not addressed, evaluation lacks probabilistic metrics and ablations, real-world gains not consistently superior to CSM.  
- Suggested improvements: include ablations on Q/rank, initialization and regularization tips, report NLPD/calibration, runtime/memory comparisons, and outline sparse/inducing-variable MOSM variants.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, novel, and relevant; reviewer leans toward acceptance despite practical/empirical gaps.
