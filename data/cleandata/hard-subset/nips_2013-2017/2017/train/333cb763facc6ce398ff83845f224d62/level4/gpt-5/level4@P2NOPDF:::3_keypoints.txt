- Proposes a new covariance structure for multi-output GPs extending the spectral kernel construction (Bochner-based).
- Parameters are highly interpretable, capturing cross-output properties like delays and phase differences.
- Designing expressive, well-structured multi-output GP covariances is hard and few robust methods exist.
- The construction is theoretically well founded and shows strong empirical performance across several datasets.
- Paper is clearly written, with appropriate and informative experiments and visualizations.
- Concern: limited detail on parameter learning—could be difficult in practice and affects usability.
- Question: robustness/performance when training data is limited or observations per output are few.
- Question: how the model handles/expresses correlations between channels with very different length-scales.
- Request: specify number of training points in the synthetic experiment (Section 4.1).
- Minor remark: possible missing words around lines 74 and 140.
