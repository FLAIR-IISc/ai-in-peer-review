This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariances in the spectral domain is, in my view, an important contribution to GPs that has not received the attention it deserves. This work extends that perspective to the multi-output setting and builds on [1]. In the multi-output case, a key challenge is how to specify cross-covariances, which need not be positive definite. The authors describe an approach that addresses this challenge and leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to prior work is well articulated, and I believe there is sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this nature, though they do not provide much intuition; in particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret. Nonetheless, for this type of methodological contribution, the experimental evaluation is sufficient.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013), Atlanta, GA, USA, 16–21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
