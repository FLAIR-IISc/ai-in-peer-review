- Proposes a new MOGP covariance by designing cross-power spectral densities and using the inverse Fourier transform, extending the single-output spectral mixture idea; evaluated on a synthetic example, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura dataset.
- Strength: Spectral-domain construction is principled, ensures positive definiteness, and yields interpretable parameters.
- Strength: Clever extension to multiple outputs that can produce richer kernels with potential extrapolation power.
- Weakness: Paper does not explicitly demonstrate inherited extrapolation ability in challenging multi-output scenarios; experiments use common datasets and show largely comparable performance to existing kernels.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on tasks where existing models struggle (e.g., pronounced delays or phase relationships across channels) to highlight unique advantages.
- Suggestion: Clarify whether statistical significance tests were conducted for Tables 1 and 2, since reported means and SDs imply most models perform similarly.
