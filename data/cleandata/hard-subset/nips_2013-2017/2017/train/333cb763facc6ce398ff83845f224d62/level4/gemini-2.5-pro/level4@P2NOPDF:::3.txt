This paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs) by generalizing the spectral mixture kernel, which is based on Bochner's theorem. A key advantage of this formulation is that the model's hyperparameters are interpretable, directly corresponding to meaningful properties such as delays and phase differences between output channels.

The development of expressive and practical covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging and important problem, with relatively few general-purpose methods available. The approach proposed by the authors is theoretically sound and addresses a clear gap in the literature. The paper is well-written and the method is described with clarity. The chosen experiments and illustrations are relevant and effectively demonstrate the model's capabilities on several datasets.

A potential concern is the complexity of learning the model parameters. The paper provides limited information regarding the optimization procedure, its computational demands, and its sensitivity to initialization. Assuming the parameter learning is tractable and robust, I expect this method has the potential to become a standard tool for multi-output GP modeling.

**Questions:**

*   How does the method behave in low-data regimes? Specifically, how robust is the hyperparameter inference when only a limited number of training points are available?
*   The model allows for different length-scales (via the spectral densities) for each output channel. Intuitively, a channel with a very large length-scale might be expected to have low correlation with a channel exhibiting a very small length-scale. How is this relationship captured or constrained within your cross-spectral density formulation?
