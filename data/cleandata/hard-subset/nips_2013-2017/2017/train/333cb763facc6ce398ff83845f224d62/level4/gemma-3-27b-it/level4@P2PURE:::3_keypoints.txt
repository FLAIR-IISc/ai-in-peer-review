- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, extending the spectral/Bochner-based approach of A.G. and R.P. Adams.  
- Key strength: parameters are interpretable (e.g., inter-output delay, phase difference).  
- Multi-output GP covariance design is challenging with limited prior solutions; this method is theoretically well-founded.  
- Empirical results on several datasets convincingly demonstrate the method’s efficacy.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses appropriate illustrative examples.  
- Lacks detail on computational aspects of parameter learning — potential practical concern.  
- If parameter learning is tractable, the approach could become a standard technique for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Question: How does the model reflect low correlation when channels have significantly different length-scales?  
- Comment: Specify number of training points used in the synthetic example (Section 4.1).  
- Minor: Missing words at lines 74 and 140.
