This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process (GP) covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariance functions in the spectral domain represents a valuable, though often underappreciated, contribution to the field of GPs. This work builds upon the foundation laid in [1] by extending this approach to the multi-output setting. A key challenge in multi-output GPs is accurately modeling cross-covariance, which is not necessarily positive-definite. The authors address this challenge effectively, presenting a method that yields a spectral mixture covariance function suitable for multi-output GPs.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, featuring a concise and clear introduction to both the problem and the fundamentals of Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions appear novel to the best of my knowledge. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work and position their contribution effectively within the existing literature. Overall, the work exhibits sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a theoretical paper of this nature. While the experiments do not provide deep insights, particularly regarding the Jura dataset – which is difficult to interpret – the evaluation is sufficient to support the claims made in the paper. A more intuitive understanding of the kernel's behavior might be gained from additional, simpler experiments or visualizations, but the current evaluation is not a significant weakness. 

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013*, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
