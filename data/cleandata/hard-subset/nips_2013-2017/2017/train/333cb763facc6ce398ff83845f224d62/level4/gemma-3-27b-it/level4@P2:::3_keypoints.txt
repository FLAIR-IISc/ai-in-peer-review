- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs by extending spectral approaches (Bochner/Cramér) to kernel construction.  
- Parameters of the kernel are interpretable (delay, phase, lengthscale), aiding modeling and selection.  
- Method is theoretically grounded using Cramér’s Theorem to ensure positive-definiteness.  
- MOSM kernel generalizes existing approaches (e.g., CSM) by relaxing parameter-sharing constraints across outputs.  
- Empirical validation shows the method can recover known relationships on synthetic data and performs competitively on real-world datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with helpful illustrative examples.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational cost and scalability for parameter learning, raising practical applicability concerns.  
- Does not sufficiently address interactions when outputs have significantly different lengthscales.  
- Synthetic experiment omits the number of training points, harming reproducibility.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid, addresses a key MOGP challenge, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear computational feasibility for large-scale problems and incomplete analysis of lengthscale interactions.  
- Overall: strong, impactful contribution with minor but important issues around scalability and lengthscale handling.  
- Specific suggestions: fix wording at lines 74 and 140; report synthetic training set size; add discussion/experiments on disparate lengthscales; discuss computational cost and scalability strategies.
