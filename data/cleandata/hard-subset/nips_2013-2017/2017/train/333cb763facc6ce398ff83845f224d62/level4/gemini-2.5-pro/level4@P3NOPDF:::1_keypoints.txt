- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian Process covariance function designed in the spectral domain, extending spectral methods to multi-output GPs.
- Uses spectral density design (building on Wilson & Adams 2013) as a powerful approach that is underexplored for GPs.
- Addresses the key challenge of modeling cross-covariances by modeling the cross-spectral density, yielding a principled and flexible multi-output spectral mixture kernel.
- Theoretical development is sound and elegantly handles cross-covariance construction in the spectral domain.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of single-output spectral mixtures and a well-motivated extension to multi-output.
- The authors situate their contribution clearly with respect to prior work, demonstrating novelty and technical contribution worthy of publication.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: results (e.g., on the Jura dataset) are hard to interpret and give limited intuition about model behavior.
- A simple synthetic experiment explicitly demonstrating learned cross-covariance properties would strengthen the empirical section.
- Quantitative results support viability, but the lack of intuitive examples is the main empirical weakness.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance despite minor experimental shortcomings.
