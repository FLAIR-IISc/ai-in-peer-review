This paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes, effectively generalizing the spectral approach – building upon the Bochner Theorem and the work of A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams – to construct kernels. A key strength of this approach lies in the interpretability of its parameters, offering insights into relationships between outputs like time delays or phase differences.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a significant challenge, with relatively limited existing methodologies. The authors’ proposed method is theoretically well-founded and demonstrably effective across a range of datasets. 

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with illustrative examples that appear appropriately chosen. Assuming parameter learning is computationally feasible – an aspect not extensively detailed in the manuscript – this method has the potential to become a standard technique for multi-output GP modeling.

Questions for the Authors:
* How does the performance of the method degrade with limited training data?
* (As a point of interest) Given the inherent difficulty in establishing strong correlations between channels with vastly different length-scales, how is this phenomenon reflected within the model?
