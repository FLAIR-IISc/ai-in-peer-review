- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance by defining cross-power spectral densities and using inverse Fourier transforms to obtain cross-covariances.  
- Approach extends the single-output spectral mixture idea from [6], which used Gaussian mixtures of power spectral densities.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic dataset, a weather dataset, and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.  
- Extension to multi-output is clever and conceptually well-motivated.  
- Missing demonstration of the extrapolation capabilities that motivated [6] in the single-output case.  
- Experimental evaluation uses common datasets and yields results comparable to existing multi-output covariance functions.  
- Impact would be stronger with experiments on datasets where current models struggle or fail.  
- No statistical significance tests reported for Tables 1 and 2; reported standard deviations suggest performance differences may be insignificant.
