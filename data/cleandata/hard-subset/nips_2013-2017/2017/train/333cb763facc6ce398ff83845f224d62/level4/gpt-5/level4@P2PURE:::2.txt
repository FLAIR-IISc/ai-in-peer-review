This paper introduces a new class of covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes. Rather than specifying cross-covariance functions directly, the authors define cross-power spectral densities and use the inverse Fourier transform to obtain the corresponding cross-covariances. The approach mirrors [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where the power spectral density is modeled as a mixture of Gaussians and then inverted to produce a kernel with extrapolation ability. The proposed covariance is evaluated on one synthetic example and two real datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura metal concentration dataset.

Extending the idea from [6] to the multi-output setting is an appealing way to construct new covariance functions. One of the key motivations in [6] was to enable extrapolation; it would be valuable to demonstrate that the multi-output version retains this capability. The experimental section focuses on common benchmark datasets, and the reported performance appears comparable to that of existing multi-output covariance functions. The impact of the work could be strengthened by including experiments on datasets where competing models are known to struggle or fail.
