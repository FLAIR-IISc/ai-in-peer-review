- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes designed in the spectral domain.  
- Extends the single-output Spectral Mixture (SM) kernel using Cramér’s theorem (the multivariate generalization of Bochner’s theorem).  
- Key technical challenge: constructing valid cross-covariance functions that ensure the overall kernel matrix remains positive definite.  
- Solution: parameterize the matrix of cross-spectral densities via a factor decomposition that naturally guarantees positive definiteness.  
- MOSM is more flexible than prior work (e.g., CSM), as it can model both delays between channels and phase differences.  
- Model parameters (frequencies, magnitudes, phases, delays) have direct, interpretable meanings.  
- The method is technically sound and well-founded in stationary process theory; derivation is clear and claims are validated theoretically and empirically.  
- Paper is very well-written and organized, with a concise background and clear positioning relative to prior work.  
- Experiments: strong synthetic performance and competitive real-world results, but the real-data analysis misses deeper interpretation of learned spectral parameters.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original, and elegant contribution likely to influence MOGP research; main weakness is underutilized interpretability in real-world experiments.
