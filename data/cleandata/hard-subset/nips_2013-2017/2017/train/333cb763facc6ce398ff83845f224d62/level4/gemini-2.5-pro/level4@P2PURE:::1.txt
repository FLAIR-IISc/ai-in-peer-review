This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process (MOGP) covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariance functions in the spectral domain is a powerful and important approach for GPs that has not received the attention it deserves, and this paper effectively extends this methodology to the multi-output scenario.

The work is a direct extension of Wilson & Adams [1]. The primary challenge in the multi-output case is modeling the cross-covariance functions, which, unlike standard covariance functions, are not required to be positive definite. The authors propose a principled approach to address this challenge, resulting in a novel spectral mixture covariance for MOGPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to both the problem domain and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors clearly situate their work with respect to the existing literature, and I believe the contribution contains sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experiments are adequate for a methodological paper of this nature. However, they provide limited intuition, and the results on the Jura dataset, in particular, are difficult to reason about. Despite this, the experimental evaluation is sufficient to validate the proposed method.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013* (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
