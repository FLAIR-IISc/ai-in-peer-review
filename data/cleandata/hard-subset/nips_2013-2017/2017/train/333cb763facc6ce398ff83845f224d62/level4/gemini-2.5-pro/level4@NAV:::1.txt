This manuscript introduces a multi-output Gaussian Process (GP) covariance function that is formulated within the spectral domain. The approach of constructing covariances through their spectral densities represents a valuable contribution to the GP field, which, in my opinion, has not received the attention it warrants. This paper successfully generalizes this paradigm to the multi-output scenario. The work is a direct extension of the research in [1]. For the multi-output case, the central challenge is modeling the cross-covariance, which is not constrained to be positive-definite. The authors present a principled method for this task, resulting in a multi-output spectral mixture covariance.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the extensions proposed for the multi-output setting appear to be novel. The relationship to existing literature is thoroughly explained, and I find the work contains sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experimental validation is adequate for a methodological paper of this nature. While the results demonstrate the method's performance, they offer limited intuitive insight, particularly in the case of the Jura dataset, which is challenging to reason about qualitatively. Nevertheless, for a contribution of this type, the empirical evaluation is sufficient.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In , Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). : JMLR.org.
