- Proposes a novel MOGP covariance defined in the spectral domain by modeling cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier transforming to cross-covariances.
- Extends the spectral mixture kernel of [6] from single-output to multi-output GPs.
- Demonstrates the method on a synthetic dataset and two real-world benchmarks (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- The extension is clever and promising for constructing flexible MOGP covariance functions.
- Fails to evaluate whether the multi-output extension inherits the original spectral mixture kernel’s extrapolation capabilities.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to standard benchmarks and shows performance comparable to existing MOGP models.
- Paper impact could be increased by testing on tasks/datasets where traditional MOGP models perform poorly.
- Questions whether statistical significance tests were performed for Tables 1 and 2, since reported differences are small and unclear given the standard deviations.
