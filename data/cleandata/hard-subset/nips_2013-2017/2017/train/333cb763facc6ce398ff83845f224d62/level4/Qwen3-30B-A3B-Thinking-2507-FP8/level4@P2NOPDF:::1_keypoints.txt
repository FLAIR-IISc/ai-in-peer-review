- Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance in the spectral domain, extending Wilson & Adams (2013) to multi-output GPs.  
- Addresses a key gap: designing spectral-domain cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.  
- Proposes a multi-output spectral mixture kernel that is theoretically coherent and enables flexible modeling of complex dependencies.  
- Timely and well-motivated: spectral-domain approaches for multi-output GPs are underexplored.  
- Exceptionally well-written with a concise introduction and clear exposition of the spectral mixture kernel.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and the extension to multi-output settings is carefully presented.  
- Thorough discussion of related work; novelty is clear in avoiding unnecessary positive-definiteness constraints on cross-covariances.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate and shows competitive performance on benchmarks (including the Jura dataset).  
- Empirical analysis lacks deeper interpretability, particularly regarding physical/spatial intuition on the Jura results.  
- Technical contributions are sound and accessible, bridging spectral theory and practical implementation.  
- Provides a principled alternative to existing multi-output methods (e.g., linear model of coregionalization) and advances GP kernel design.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
