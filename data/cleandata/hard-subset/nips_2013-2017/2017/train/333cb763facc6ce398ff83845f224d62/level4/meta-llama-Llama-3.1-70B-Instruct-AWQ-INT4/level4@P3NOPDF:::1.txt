This paper presents a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, an approach that has not received the attention it deserves. The authors extend this concept to the multi-output scenario, building upon their previous work [1]. A key challenge in this context is describing the cross-covariance, which does not necessarily have to be positive-definite. The authors address this issue by proposing an approach that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and Gaussian process (GP) fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the relationship to previous work, demonstrating sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experimental evaluation is satisfactory for a paper of this nature, although it may not provide substantial intuition, particularly with the Jura dataset, which can be difficult to reason about. Nevertheless, I believe the experimental evaluation is sufficient for a paper focused on theoretical contributions.

One of the significant strengths of this paper is its ability to extend the spectral domain approach to multi-output GPs. This contribution has the potential to impact the field of Gaussian processes and machine learning. The authors' clear and concise writing style makes the paper accessible to a broad audience.

In conclusion, I recommend accepting this paper for publication. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field of Gaussian processes, and their work has the potential to influence future research in this area.

Reference:
[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
