- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a novel expressive covariance for multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Designs cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain and uses Cramér's Theorem to guarantee valid MOGP covariances.  
- Generalizes single-output Spectral Mixture ideas to multiple outputs, allowing modeling of magnitude, frequency, phase, and time delays.  
- MOSM is more general than the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel; CSM is a constrained special case of MOSM.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound, including rank-one spectral decomposition and ensuring Hermitian symmetry for real-valued processes.  
- Model parameters have clear interpretability (delay, phase, magnitude), enhancing understanding of inter-output relationships.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides necessary spectral and MOGP background.  
- Synthetic experiment convincingly demonstrates MOSM’s ability to learn relationships among a function, its derivative, and a delayed version.  
- Real-world experiments (climate, Jura) show modest, sometimes non-significant gains over CSM, not clearly showcasing delay modeling benefits.  
- Suggests adding a real-world dataset with known delays or deeper analysis of learned hyperparameters (e.g., delay θij) to strengthen empirical impact.  
- Does not discuss computational complexity or scalability; recommends acknowledging GP cubic scaling and proposing sparse/approximate extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel, and influential contribution with minor empirical and scalability considerations; recommend acceptance.
