The paper proposes a new covariance construction for multi-output Gaussian processes by specifying complex cross-power spectral densities and obtaining the corresponding cross-covariances via the inverse Fourier transform. This mirrors the single-output approach of [6], where a mixture of Gaussians models the power spectral density and is then inverted to yield a flexible kernel with strong extrapolation capabilities. The authors evaluate the resulting kernel on a synthetic example and two real datasets: a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura heavy metal concentrations.

Adapting the idea in [6] to the multi-output setting is a compelling way to derive new cross-covariance functions. Since a key motivation in [6] was extrapolation, it would be valuable to demonstrate that the proposed multi-output kernel offers similarly strong extrapolative behavior. The experimental section focuses on widely used datasets, and the reported performance is broadly comparable to that of existing multi-output kernels. The impact could be enhanced by including tasks where competing approaches are known to struggle or fail.
