- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a novel, expressive covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Generalizes single-output Spectral Mixture kernels to multi-output via Cramér's theorem (multivariate Bochner).  
- Constructs cross-spectral density matrix S(ω) by designing complex-valued functions and ensuring positive definiteness via factorization S(ω)=R(ω)ᴴR(ω).  
- Models R(ω) components as complex-valued squared-exponential functions to obtain a closed-form MOSM kernel by inverse Fourier transform.  
- MOSM parameters have clear physical interpretations (magnitude, frequency, phase, and inter-channel time delays).  
- Unique capability: explicitly models delays between output channels, distinguishing it from prior spectral MOGP methods (e.g., CSM).  
- Synthetic experiment (function, derivative, delayed version) compellingly demonstrates MOSM's advantage where delays/phase matter.  
- Real-world experiments show competitive or superior performance to existing MOGP models.  
- Technical quality is high: derivation is principled, clear, and mathematically sound.  
- Paper is well-written and organized with informative figures/tables and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Work is original: principled generalization of spectral kernel design to multi-output with a novel factorized cross-spectral parameterization.  
- Significant contribution: offers an elegant, interpretable, and potentially widely useful MOGP kernel, especially for multi-sensor/time-series applications.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical hyperparameter optimization challenges given the large number of parameters.  
- Concern: potential sensitivity to initialization and local minima in non-convex GP hyperparameter optimization not addressed.  
- Concern: no analysis of behavior in low-data regimes; model may be prone to overfitting due to high flexibility.  
- Concern: experiments use modest-sized datasets and cubic GP scalability is not discussed beyond future work.  
- Question: How difficult was MOSM hyperparameter optimization in practice, and how sensitive is performance to initialization/local minima?  
- Question: How does MOSM behave with small training sets compared to simpler MOGP models (risk of overfitting)?  
- Question: The cross-spectral magnitude wij depends on squared distance between µi and µj—does this limit capturing correlations between very different-frequency processes?  
- Question: Please specify the exact number of training points (N1, N2, N3) used in the synthetic experiment for reproducibility.  
- Minor remark: Fix awkward phrasing at Line 74 ("as is [10, 11]" → "as shown in [10, 11]") and review Line 140 for a possible missing word.
