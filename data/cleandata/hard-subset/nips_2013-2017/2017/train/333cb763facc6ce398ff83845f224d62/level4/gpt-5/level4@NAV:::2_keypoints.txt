- Introduces a novel class of multi-output GP covariance functions defined via cross-power spectral densities and recovered by inverse Fourier transform.
- Extends the approach of [6] (modeling power spectral density as a mixture of Gaussians) to produce expressive multi-output kernels.
- Evaluated on a synthetic dataset and two real-world datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Extension of [6] to multi-output is a clever idea.
- Reviewer recommends demonstrating that the multi-output covariance inherits the extrapolative ability of [6].
- Experiments use common datasets and show performance comparable to other multi-output covariance models.
- Impact could be strengthened by including datasets where competing methods perform poorly.
- Reviewer questions whether statistical significance was assessed for Tables 1 and 2, noting similar performance given the reported standard deviations.
