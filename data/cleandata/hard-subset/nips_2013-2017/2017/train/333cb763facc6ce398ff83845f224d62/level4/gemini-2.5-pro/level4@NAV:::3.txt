This paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes, which generalizes the spectral mixture kernel approach from A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A significant strength of the proposed method is that its parameters are interpretable, corresponding to quantities like delay or phase difference between the outputs.

Developing covariance functions for multi-output GPs is a well-known challenge, and the number of available methods is limited. The approach introduced here is theoretically well-grounded, and its practical utility is convincingly shown on several datasets.

The method is articulated clearly in a well-written manuscript, and the accompanying illustrations are effective and relevant. If the process for learning the model parameters is not overly burdensome (a topic on which the paper provides limited detail), I believe this method could become a benchmark for multi-output GPs.

**Questions**
*   What is the performance of the method in a low-data regime, i.e., with a small number of training points?
*   (A point of curiosity) How does the model capture the intuition that a channel with a very large length-scale should have low correlation with a channel possessing a small length-scale?
