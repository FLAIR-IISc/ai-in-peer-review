- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: extends single-output SM to MOGPs via Cramér's Theorem using parametric complex cross-spectral densities and inverse Fourier transform to yield valid matrix-valued covariances that model delays, phase shifts, and frequencies across outputs.

- Novelty/significance: designing MOGP kernels in the spectral domain is elegant and new, yielding a more expressive family than LMC, convolution processes, and the CSM kernel—explicitly models time delays between channels.

- Technical quality: derivation from a rank-Q decomposition ensures positive-definiteness by construction; claims are well-supported by experiments.

- Empirical validation: synthetic task (function, derivative, delayed version) convincingly demonstrates MOSM's unique capabilities; competitive or superior performance on two real datasets.

- Clarity: paper is well-written, provides necessary spectral GP background, and explains the link between spectral parameters and kernel properties clearly.

- Interpretability: hyperparameters have direct spectral meaning, making the model useful for analysis as well as prediction.

- Optimization concern: MOSM introduces many hyperparameters (scaling with outputs, input dims, Q) with little discussion of optimization difficulty, sensitivity to initialization, local minima, or computational cost.

- Low-data behavior: unclear how the highly-flexible MOSM performs or overfits in small-data regimes; experiments use reasonably large N and do not address sparsity.

- Model constraint question: parameterizing cross-spectra via shared components (Ri, Rj) may limit expressible relationships—unclear how interactions of Σi, µi and Σj, µj affect correlations between channels with very different length-scales/frequencies.

- Minor issues: typos noted ("Gaussian noised was added"; possible ω >τ vs ω^T τ in Eq. (1)).

- Overall conclusion: a strong, elegant, and interpretable contribution with convincing empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite minor practical questions.
