- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner/Adams & Adams).  
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs, phase differences).  
- The approach is theoretically sound and empirically effective across multiple datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-justified experimental choices.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of parameter learning feasibility—critical for practical use.  
- If parameter learning is feasible, the method could become a standard for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Question: How does the model reflect the constraint that a channel with large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one having a small length-scale?  
- Comment: Section 4.1 (synthetic example) should specify the number of training points.  
- Minor remark: A word appears missing in lines 74 and 140.
