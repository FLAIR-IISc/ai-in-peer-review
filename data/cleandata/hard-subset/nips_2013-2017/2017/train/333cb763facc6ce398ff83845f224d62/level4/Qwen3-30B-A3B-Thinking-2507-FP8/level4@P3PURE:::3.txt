

The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral approach (Bochner's theorem) for constructing kernels. A key advantage is the interpretability of model parameters, such as delays between outputs or phase differences.  

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem with few existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically sound, and its effectiveness is demonstrated through empirical results on multiple datasets. The paper is clearly written, with well-justified experimental choices. Provided parameter learning is not overly burdensome (the paper offers insufficient information on this aspect), this method has strong potential to become a standard for multi-output GPs.  

**Questions**  
* How does the method perform when only a small number of training points are available?  
* (A point of curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with one having a small length-scale. How is this constraint reflected in the model?
