- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain.  
- Emphasizes spectral-domain covariance design as an important, underexplored direction in GP research.  
- Extends spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting, building on Wilson & Adams (2013).  
- Addresses the key challenge of specifying cross-covariances (which need not be positive definite) and proposes a solution.  
- Paper is well written with a clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.  
- Spectral mixture kernel and the proposed extensions are clearly explained and appear novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is well articulated and the contribution has sufficient novelty for publication.  
- Experiments are adequate but offer limited intuition; the Jura dataset is hard to interpret, though empirical evaluation is sufficient.
