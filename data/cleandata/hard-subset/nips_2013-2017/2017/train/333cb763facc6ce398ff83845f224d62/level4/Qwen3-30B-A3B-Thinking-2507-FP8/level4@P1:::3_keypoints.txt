- Proposes MOSM kernel: extends spectral mixture to multi-output via Cramér's Theorem, modeling cross-covariances as complex-valued spectral densities to capture phase differences and time delays.  
- Provides direct parametric interpretation of cross-channel relationships (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay), unlike latent-process LMC approaches.  
- Theoretical development is sound, well-motivated, and connects clearly to prior spectral-kernel and MOGP literature.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive, including synthetic examples and two real-world datasets (climate and heavy metals).  
- Synthetic experiment shows MOSM can learn derivative and delayed relationships without prior knowledge, which SM-LMC and CONV fail to capture.  
- On real data, MOSM significantly outperforms alternatives on Cadmium prediction (α=0.05) and performs comparably to CSM on climate data.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear mathematical exposition and accessible theoretical-to-practical bridging.  
- Implementation details are sufficiently precise for reproducibility and use TensorFlow/GPflow with automatic differentiation.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability, particularly how the number of spectral components Q affects training time.  
- Synthetic experiment omits the number of training points per channel, leaving data-efficiency unclear.  
- Does not analyze model behavior when channels have very different length scales, a common multi-output scenario that may limit correlations.  
- Limited discussion of model limitations, failure cases, and the specific conditions under which MOSM outperforms existing methods.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, novel, technically sound, and practically useful contribution despite minor concerns.
