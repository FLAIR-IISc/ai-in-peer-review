- Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain.  
- Extends spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output setting, addressing cross-covariance challenges.  
- Provides a principled construction that ensures valid covariance functions even with potentially non–positive-definite cross-covariance via spectral representation.  
- Builds on prior spectral kernel work (Wilson & Adams 2013) and generalizes it to multiple outputs.  
- Mathematical derivations are clear, rigorous, and accessible to GP researchers.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with lucid explanations and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and well-motivated by limitations of existing methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is appropriate for a theoretical paper and demonstrates method functionality.  
- Experiments lack strong intuitive insights; more visualization/analysis of learned cross-covariance would help.  
- The Jura dataset is difficult to interpret and may not best showcase the method’s strengths.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution and sufficient novelty for acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
