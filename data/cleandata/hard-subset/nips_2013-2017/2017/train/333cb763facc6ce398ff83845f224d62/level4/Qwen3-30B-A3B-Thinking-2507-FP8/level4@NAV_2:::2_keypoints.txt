- Extends spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs by deriving the MOSM kernel from Cramér’s theorem.  
- Constructs principled complex-valued cross-spectral densities that model delays and phase shifts across channels.  
- Provides full parametric interpretation of cross-covariances with explicit delay (θ_ij) and phase (φ_ij) parameters.  
- Generalizes prior Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) work by adding delay modeling beyond phase differences.  
- Validated on synthetic data showing MOSM can learn derivative and delayed relationships without prior knowledge.  
- Evaluated on two real-world datasets (climate sensors and Jura heavy metals), matching or exceeding competitors.  
- Achieved statistically significant improvement on Cadmium prediction in the Jura dataset (α=0.05).  
- Strength: theoretically grounded extension with clear parametric meaning for delays and phases.  
- Strength: appropriate statistical testing (Kolmogorov–Smirnov) and successful synthetic validation.  
- Weakness: climate dataset shows only marginal gains over CSM, likely due to high inter-sensor correlation.  
- Weakness: real-world experiments do not explicitly test extrapolation capabilities demonstrated in synthetic case.  
- Weakness: paper could better emphasize how MOSM’s delay modeling enables derivative/delay learning in the synthetic example.
