- Proposes a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain, extending the spectral mixture (SM) kernel to MOGPs.  
- Addresses the core multi-output challenge of specifying cross-covariances by constructing complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Ensures positive definiteness via Cramér’s theorem, yielding a principled MOSM kernel.  
- MOSM retains interpretability in terms of lengthscales, frequencies, magnitudes, phases, and delays across channels.  
- Methodological development is clearly presented and appears to be novel relative to prior SM-based multi-output work.  
- Paper is well written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient GP and SM background.  
- Empirical evaluation is adequate for a methodological paper but provides limited intuition, especially on the Jura dataset.  
- Significance: bringing spectral-domain kernel design to MOGPs is valuable and likely to influence future work.  
- Empirical gains are not uniformly large, but the method’s expressiveness and interpretability justify the contribution.  
- Minor limitation: experiments could better illustrate when delays and phase shifts are critical.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
