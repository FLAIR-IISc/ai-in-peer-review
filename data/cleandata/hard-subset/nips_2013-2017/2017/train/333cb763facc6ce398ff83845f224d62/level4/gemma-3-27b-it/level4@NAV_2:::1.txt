This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is a valuable contribution to Gaussian processes that, in my opinion, has not received sufficient attention. This work extends this concept to the multi-output scenario, addressing the challenge of describing cross-covariances without requiring positive-definiteness. The authors propose a method leading to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, offering a concise and clear introduction to the problem and relevant Gaussian process fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions appear novel. The relationship to prior work is thoroughly explained, and I believe the work possesses sufficient novelty to warrant publication. 

The experimental evaluation is adequate for a paper of this nature. While the experiments may not offer extensive intuition—particularly, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret—the evaluation is sufficient to support the claims made.
