- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral (Bochner) approach.  
- Model parameters are interpretable, e.g., delays between outputs and phase differences.  
- Addresses a meaningful gap in covariance development for multi-output GPs where methods are limited.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous.  
- Empirically validated on multiple datasets, demonstrating clear effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and experimental choices.  
- Parameter learning computational tractability is not detailed and is critical for practical adoption.  
- Question: How does the method perform with small training set sizes?  
- Question: How does the model handle the constraint that channels with large length scales cannot strongly correlate with channels having small length scales?  
- Comment: Specify the number of training points in Section 4.1 (synthetic example).  
- Comment: Minor typos—missing words at lines 74 and 140.  
- Overall: Promising, interpretable approach with strong potential to become a standard if parameter learning and the above questions are addressed.
