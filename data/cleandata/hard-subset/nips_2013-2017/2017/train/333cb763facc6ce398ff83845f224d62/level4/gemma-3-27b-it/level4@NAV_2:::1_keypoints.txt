- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain.
- Highlights that designing covariances in the spectral domain is valuable and underexplored.
- Extends spectral-domain covariance design to multi-output GPs, addressing cross-covariances without requiring positive-definiteness.
- Introduces a method that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction and GP fundamentals.
- Provides a lucid explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and novel extensions.
- Thoroughly relates the work to prior literature, claiming sufficient novelty for publication.
- Experimental evaluation is adequate to support claims, though lacks deep intuition and the Jura dataset is hard to interpret.
- References relevant prior work (e.g., Wilson & Adams, 2013).
