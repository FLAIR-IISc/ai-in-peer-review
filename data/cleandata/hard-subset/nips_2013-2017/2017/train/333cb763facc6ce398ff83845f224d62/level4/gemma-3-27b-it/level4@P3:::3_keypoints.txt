- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs, using Cramér’s Theorem to model delays and phase differences between outputs.  
- Designs covariance functions in the Fourier domain; the theoretical foundation (Bochner/Cramér) is principled and well-motivated.  
- MOSM provides interpretable spectral parameters that clarify relationships between output channels.  
- Extends expressiveness over prior methods (e.g., LMC, CSM) by explicitly modeling inter-channel delays and phases.  
- Effectively situates the work within existing GP kernel and spectral-method literature.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world (climate, metal concentration) datasets demonstrates promising results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational challenges and scalability for high-dimensional outputs or large datasets.  
- Does not analyze how the number of spectral components (Q) impacts computational cost and model performance.  
- Experiments lack a systematic sensitivity analysis to parameter settings and noise levels.  
- Needs evaluation of performance in limited-training-data scenarios.  
- Should discuss how channels with vastly different lengthscales can still exhibit strong correlation and any related limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity, sensitivity analyses, and lengthscale interactions.  
- Minor edits needed: missing words on lines 74 and 140.
