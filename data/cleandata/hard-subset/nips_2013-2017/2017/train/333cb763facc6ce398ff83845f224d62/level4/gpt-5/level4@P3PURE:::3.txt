The paper introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, generalizing the spectral approach (via Bochner’s theorem) used to build kernels by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A notable asset of the proposed method is that its parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs or phase differences).

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging topic, and relatively few methods are currently available. The proposed method is theoretically sound and its effectiveness is demonstrated on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choices for the illustrations appear appropriate. If parameter learning is not too troublesome (the paper contains little information on this point), I would expect this method to become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method behave when few training points are available?
- (More a curiosity) A channel with a large length-scale cannot be highly correlated with a channel with a small length-scale. How does that appear in your model?
