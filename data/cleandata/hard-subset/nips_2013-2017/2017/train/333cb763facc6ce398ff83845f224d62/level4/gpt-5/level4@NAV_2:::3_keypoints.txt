- Proposes a new covariance construction for multi-output GPs extending spectral methods based on Bochner’s theorem and spectral mixture kernels.  
- Key advantage: model parameters are interpretable (e.g., inter-channel delays and phase offsets).  
- Designing covariance structures for multi-output GPs is challenging and existing solutions are few.  
- Approach is theoretically well-founded and shown effective on multiple datasets.  
- Manuscript is clearly written with accessible presentation and appropriate experiments.  
- Limited detail on hyperparameter learning; concern whether learning is onerous.  
- If hyperparameter learning is manageable, method could become a standard tool for multi-output GPs.  
- Question: How does the method perform with only a small number of training points?  
- Question: How does the model handle correlation between channels with very different length-scales?  
- Comment: In synthetic example (Section 4.1), the number of training points should be specified.
