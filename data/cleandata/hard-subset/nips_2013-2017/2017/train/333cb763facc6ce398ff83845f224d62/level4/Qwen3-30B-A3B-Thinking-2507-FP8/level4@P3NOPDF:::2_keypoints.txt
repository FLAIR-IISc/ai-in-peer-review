- Proposes a novel multi-output GP covariance via cross-power spectral densities and inverse Fourier, extending spectral mixtures from single to multi-output.
- Conceptually sound and technically competent extension of prior work [6].
- Experiments on synthetic, weather, and Swiss Jura datasets, but experimental justification is insufficient.
- Major omission: no demonstration of extrapolation capability, a core motivation of spectral mixture kernels in [6].
- No analysis of out-of-distribution or long-term extrapolation performance for the multi-output extension.
- Experimental results are underwhelming and comparable to standard baselines, with no clear consistent outperformance.
- Fails to identify a domain or dataset where conventional multi-output kernels fail but the CPSD approach succeeds.
- Statistical significance of reported differences is unclear; no statistical tests (e.g., paired t-tests) are provided.
- Computational complexity and scalability relative to existing multi-output kernels are not discussed.
- Synthetic experiment lacks details on how the ground-truth CPSD was constructed.
- Swiss Jura dataset description omits key details (number of outputs, spatial resolution), hindering reproducibility.
- Implementation appears correct and the spectral representation is well-motivated, despite empirical shortcomings.
- Suggested improvements: add extrapolation experiments, test on datasets with complex/non-stationary cross-correlations, and report statistical significance.
- Overall assessment: technically valid but incremental; experimental gaps and missing extrapolation analysis prevent a compelling claim of novelty or utility.
