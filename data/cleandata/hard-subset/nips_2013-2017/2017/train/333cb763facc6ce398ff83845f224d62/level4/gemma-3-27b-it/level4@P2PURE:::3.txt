This paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes, generalizing the spectral approach (based on the Bochner Theorem) as developed by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams for kernel construction. A key strength of this method is the interpretability of its parameters, representing quantities like inter-output delay or phase difference.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs remains a challenging problem with limited existing solutions. The authors’ proposed method is theoretically well-founded, and its efficacy is convincingly demonstrated through experiments on several datasets. 

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with illustrative examples that appear appropriately chosen. Provided that parameter learning does not present significant computational difficulties (a point where the paper lacks detail), this approach has the potential to become a standard technique for multi-output Gaussian Processes.

Questions:
* How does the method perform with limited training data?
* (As a point of curiosity) Given that channels with significantly different length-scales are unlikely to exhibit high correlation, how is this reflected in the model?
