This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function that is defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariances in the spectral domain is an important contribution to GPs that I personally feel have not gotten the attention that it deserves. This paper extends this to the multi-output scenario. The paper is an extension of the work presented in [6] and [1], however, considering the multi-output case the challenge is how to describe cross-covariance which is not required to be positive-definite. In this paper, the authors describe just such an approach in a manner that leads to the spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well written with a short and clear introduction to the problem and to GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear and the extensions proposed are to my knowledge novel. The relationship to previous work is well explained and I believe that there is sufficient novelty in the work to justify publication. Specifically, the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and its extension to the multi-output case. They also provide a detailed comparison with previous work, including the Linear Model of Coregionalization (LMC) and the Convolution Model (CONV).

The experiments are sufficient for a paper such as this, they do not provide a lot of intuition however, especially the Jura data-set is hard to actually reason about. But, for a paper such as this I think the experimental evaluation is sufficient. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both synthetic and real-world data, including climate signals and heavy metal concentrations. The results show that the proposed MOSM model is competitive with other MOGP models in terms of mean absolute error.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive. For example, the authors could have included more datasets or compared their approach to other MOGP models. However, I believe that the experimental evaluation is sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

In terms of the criteria outlined in the review guidelines, I believe that the paper meets the following:

* Quality: The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well-organized.
* Originality: The paper presents a novel approach to designing multi-output Gaussian process covariance functions in the spectral domain.
* Significance: The paper addresses an important problem in the field of Gaussian processes and provides a significant contribution to the field.

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of Gaussian processes. I would recommend acceptance for publication.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach and its relationship to previous work.
* The experimental evaluation is sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more intuition and explanation of the results, especially for the Jura data-set.
