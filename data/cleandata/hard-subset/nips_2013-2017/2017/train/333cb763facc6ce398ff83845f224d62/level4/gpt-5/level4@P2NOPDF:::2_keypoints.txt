- Proposes a new multi-output GP covariance by defining cross-power spectral densities and inverting them to obtain cross-covariances.
- Builds on the single-output spectral mixture idea (Gaussian-mixture PSD inverted to yield flexible kernels with extrapolation).
- Evaluated on one synthetic example and two real datasets (weather and Swiss Jura metal concentrations).
- Extension to multi-output is a natural and elegant way to get richer cross-task covariance functions.
- Key motivation in prior work was extrapolation; the paper should demonstrate whether the multi-output version retains extrapolation ability.
- Current experiments use standard benchmarks and show performance broadly comparable to existing multi-output kernels.
- Contribution would be strengthened by experiments on tasks where competing models fail—e.g., long-range input extrapolation or transfer across related outputs.
- Unclear whether reported differences in Tables 1–2 are statistically significant; please clarify significance testing or additional runs given overlapping standard deviations.
