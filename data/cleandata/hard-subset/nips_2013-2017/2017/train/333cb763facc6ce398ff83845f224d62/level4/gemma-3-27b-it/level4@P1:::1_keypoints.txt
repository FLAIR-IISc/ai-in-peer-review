- Proposes a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function designed in the spectral domain.  
- Highlights that designing covariances in the spectral domain is important and underexplored, and extends this to multi-output GPs.  
- Identifies cross-covariances as a key multi-output challenge (not necessarily positive-definite) and addresses it by deriving a multi-output spectral mixture covariance.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and explanation of Gaussian process fundamentals.  
- The spectral mixture kernel explanation and proposed extensions appear novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is well-explained and the contribution shows sufficient novelty for publication.  
- Experiments are adequate for a theory-focused paper but offer limited intuition; the Jura dataset is hard to interpret though the evaluation is appropriate.
