- Introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs by generalizing the spectral mixture kernel using Bochner’s theorem.  
- Hyperparameters are interpretable, mapping to meaningful properties like inter-output delays and phase differences.  
- Addresses an important, under-served problem; the approach is theoretically sound and fills a gap in the literature.  
- Paper is well-written and the method is clearly described.  
- Experiments and illustrations are relevant and effectively demonstrate the model’s capabilities across datasets.  
- Concern about the complexity of learning model parameters: limited details on optimization procedure, computational cost, and sensitivity to initialization.  
- If parameter learning is tractable and robust, the method could become a standard tool for multi-output GP modeling.  
- Question: How does the method perform in low-data regimes and how robust is hyperparameter inference with few training points?  
- Question: How does the cross-spectral density formulation capture or constrain correlations when output channels have very different length-scales?
