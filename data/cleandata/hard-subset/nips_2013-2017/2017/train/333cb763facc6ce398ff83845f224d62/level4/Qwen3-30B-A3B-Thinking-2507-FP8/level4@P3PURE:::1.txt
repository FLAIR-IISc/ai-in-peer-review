

This paper introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, making an important contribution to GP methodology that has not received sufficient attention. The extension to the multi-output setting builds upon the work of [1] and addresses the key challenge of describing cross-covariance, which need not be positive-definite. The authors propose a novel approach that leads to a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  

The paper is well-written, with a concise introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions appear novel to my knowledge. The relationship to prior work is thoroughly discussed, and the novelty is sufficient to justify publication.  

The experimental evaluation is adequate for this type of work, though it lacks intuitive insights, particularly regarding the Jura dataset. For a paper of this nature, however, the experiments are sufficient.  

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning* (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
