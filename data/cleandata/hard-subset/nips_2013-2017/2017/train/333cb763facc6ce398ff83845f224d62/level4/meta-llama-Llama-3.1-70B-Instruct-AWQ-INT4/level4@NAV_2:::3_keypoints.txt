- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, a new covariance structure for MOGPs generalising spectral approaches (Bochner/Cramér).
- MOSM yields interpretable parameters such as inter-output delays and phase differences.
- Extends spectral-mixture methods to multivariate settings via complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem.
- Addresses a challenging and underexplored problem—defining covariance structures for MOGPs.
- MOSM is more expressive than prior methods by modelling delays and phase relationships between channels.
- Theoretical development is sound and grounded in spectral theory.
- Paper is clearly written with consistent notation and relevant figures and illustrations.
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets is thorough and shows competitive mean absolute error performance.
- Authors compare MOSM to existing MOGP models (e.g., LMC, CSM) and discuss relationships and differences.
- Strengths: clear method description, strong experiments, and parametric interpretability of inter-output relationships.
- Weaknesses: lacks discussion of computational complexity and potential challenges in parameter learning.
- Weaknesses: insufficient analysis of MOSM performance when training data are scarce.
- Originality: proposes a novel, generalised spectral covariance for MOGPs capable of modelling delays and phases.
- Significance: has potential for wide use in modelling complex multivariate output relationships.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method, expressive kernel, clear writing, and strong experimental evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing analysis on few-shot behaviour and computational/learning scalability concerns.
