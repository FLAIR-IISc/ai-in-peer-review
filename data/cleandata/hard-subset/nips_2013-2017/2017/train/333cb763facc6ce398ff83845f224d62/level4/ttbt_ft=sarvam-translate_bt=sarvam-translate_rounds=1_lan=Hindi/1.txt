This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process co-variance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach to designing co-variances in the spectral domain is a significant contribution to GPs, which I personally feel has not received as much attention as it should have. This paper extends this to a multi-output scenario. It is an expansion of the work presented in [1]. However, the challenge in considering a multi-output case is how to describe the cross-covariance, which does not necessarily have to be positive-definite. In this paper, the authors describe an approach that leads to spectral mixture co-variance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and the basics of GPs. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is clear, and the proposed extensions are new to my knowledge. The connection to previous work is well explained, and I believe there is enough novelty in the work to justify the publication.

The experiments are sufficient for a paper like this; they do not provide too much intuition. However, it is indeed difficult to argue about the Jura data-set in particular. But, for a paper like this, I think the experimental evaluation is sufficient.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., and Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, June 16-21, 2013 (pp. 1067-1075). : JMLR.org.
