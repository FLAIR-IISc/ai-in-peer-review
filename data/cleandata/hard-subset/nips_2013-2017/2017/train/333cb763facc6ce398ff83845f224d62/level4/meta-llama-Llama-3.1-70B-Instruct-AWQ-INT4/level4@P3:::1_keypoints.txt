- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance defined in the spectral domain — an important, under-explored contribution to GPs.  
- Extends spectral-domain covariance design to multi-output GPs and addresses the challenge of modelling cross-covariances that need not be positive-definite.  
- Proposes a novel construction that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and thorough explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed extensions.  
- Relationship to prior work is well-explained and the approach shows sufficient novelty to justify publication.  
- Experiments are adequate for a theory-focused paper but may not provide strong intuition, particularly on the Jura dataset.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and experimental results support the claims.  
- Originality: provides a principled extension of spectral mixtures to multivariate covariance functions.  
- Significance: has potential to impact applications involving multi-output GP regression by modelling complex inter-output relationships.  
- Conclusion: paper is a significant contribution and its publication is justified.  
- Pro-accept: novel approach, strong theory and empirical support, clear writing, thorough contextualization.  
- Con-accept: experimental evaluation could be expanded to give more intuition and broader application evidence.  
- Overall rating/recommendation: 8/10 — Accept with minor revisions.
