This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain. Designing covariance functions in the spectral domain is, in my view, an important direction in GP research that has not received the attention it deserves. This work extends that idea to the multi-output setting and builds on [1]. In the multi-output case, the key challenge is how to specify cross-covariances, which do not themselves need to be positive definite. The authors propose such an approach, yielding a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well written, with a concise and clear introduction to the problem and to GP fundamentals. The spectral mixture kernel is explained clearly, and the proposed extensions are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to prior work is well articulated, and I believe there is sufficient novelty to justify publication.

The experiments are adequate for a paper of this type, though they provide limited intuition; in particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret. Nevertheless, for a paper of this nature, the empirical evaluation is sufficient.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013), Atlanta, GA, USA, 16–21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
