The paper presents a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, extending the spectral approach (Bochner Theorem) to construct kernels based on the work of A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A notable advantage of the proposed method is that its parameters can be interpreted, such as output delay or phase difference.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem with limited existing methods. The authors' approach is theoretically well-founded, and its efficacy is demonstrated through experiments on multiple datasets.

The paper clearly explains the method in a well-written manner, with relevant illustration choices. Assuming parameter learning is not overly problematic (the paper provides limited information on this aspect), this method has the potential to become a standard for multi-output GPs.

Questions:

* How does the method perform with a limited number of training points?
* (A point of interest) In the case of channels with significantly different length-scales, how does the model capture the correlation between a channel with a large length-scale and one with a small length-scale?
