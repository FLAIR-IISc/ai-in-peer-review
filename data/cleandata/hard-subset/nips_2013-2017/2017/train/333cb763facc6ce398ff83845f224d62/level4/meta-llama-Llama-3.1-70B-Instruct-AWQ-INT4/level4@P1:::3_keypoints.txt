- Introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output GPs generalizing the spectral (Bochner) approach.  
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., delays between outputs, phase differences).  
- Defining covariance for multi-output GPs is challenging and the paper fills a gap with a principled approach.  
- Method is theoretically sound and demonstrated effective on multiple datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written with relevant illustrations.  
- Could become a standard for multi-output GPs if parameter learning is manageable.  
- Strong literature review situating the contribution in context.  
- Evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing versatility.  
- Question: How does the method perform with few training points (data-scarce settings)?  
- Question: How does the model handle channels with very different length-scales and their limited correlation?  
- Minor remarks: missing words at lines 74 and 140; synthetic example should specify number of training points.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommended for publication, but concerns about parameter learning details and small-data performance.
