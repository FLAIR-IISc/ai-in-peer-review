- Proposes a novel covariance structure for multi-output GPs that generalizes the spectral mixture kernel.
- Model parameters are interpretable (e.g., delay or phase differences between outputs).
- Tackles an important, under-explored challenge—developing covariance functions for multi-output GPs.
- Approach is theoretically well-grounded and demonstrates practical utility on several datasets.
- Manuscript is well written and illustrations are effective and relevant.
- Paper gives limited detail on parameter learning; if learning is not burdensome, method could become a benchmark.
- Question: How does the method perform in low-data regimes (few training points)?
- Question: How does the model capture the intuition that a channel with very large length-scale should have low correlation with one having a small length-scale?
- Comment: Number of training points used in the synthetic experiment (Section 4.1) should be explicitly stated.
