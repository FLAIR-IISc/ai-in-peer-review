The paper introduces a novel covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian Processes (GPs), generalizing the spectral approach (based on Bochner’s Theorem) as developed by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams for kernel construction. A key strength of this method is the interpretability of its parameters, which relate to meaningful characteristics of the outputs, such as delay or phase difference.

Defining covariance structures for multi-output GPs is a challenging problem, and relatively few methods currently exist to address it effectively. The authors’ approach is theoretically well-founded and its efficacy is demonstrated across several datasets. 

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the chosen illustrations are relevant and insightful. Provided that parameter learning is not computationally prohibitive (the paper provides limited detail on this aspect), I anticipate this method becoming a standard approach for multi-output GPs.
