- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending Wilson & Adams' SM kernel to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Addresses a key limitation: models delays and phase differences between channels simultaneously while preserving interpretability.  
- Uses Cramér's theorem to build a principled multivariate spectral framework for multi-output covariance design.  
- Constructs MOSM via complex-valued spectral densities with clear parameter mapping to frequency, magnitude, delay, and phase.  
- Generalizes the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) by allowing channel-specific parameters while maintaining positive definiteness through a rank-1 spectral factorization.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, clearly presented, and mathematically accessible.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly show MOSM can extrapolate derivative and delayed signals where prior methods fail.  
- Real-world experiments (climate and heavy metals) demonstrate competitive performance and a statistically significant improvement on Jura Cadmium prediction.  
- Strong connection between model parameters and physical interpretation improves usability for practitioners.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and properly contextualizes related work.  
- Original contribution: novel ability to model delays together with phase shifts in a multi-output spectral framework.  
- Significant both theoretically (principled spectral MOGP construction) and practically (improved multi-output predictions with temporal relationships).  
- Weakness: Jura dataset analysis lacks deeper interpretation of why MOSM outperforms others for Cadmium.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability compared to existing methods.  
- Weakness: Broader connections to additional practical applications could be strengthened.  
- Overall recommendation: substantial, high-quality, and publishable contribution to multi-output Gaussian processes.
