The paper proposes a novel approach to designing covariance functions for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) by specifying cross-power spectral densities and using the inverse Fourier transform to obtain the corresponding cross-covariances. This method is an extension of the idea presented in [6] for single-output Gaussian processes, where the power spectral density was modeled as a mixture of Gaussians to create a powerful kernel function with extrapolation capabilities.

The authors successfully apply their newly established covariance function to a synthetic data example and two real-world datasets, including a weather dataset and the Swiss Jura dataset for metal concentrations. The experimental results demonstrate the competitiveness of the proposed method, known as the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, in comparison to existing MOGP models.

A key strength of the paper is its clear and concise presentation of the theoretical background and the proposed method. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the spectral mixture approach and its extension to MOGPs, making the paper accessible to readers familiar with Gaussian processes.

However, the experimental section could be improved by including more diverse and challenging datasets. The results presented are similar to those obtained with other covariance functions for MOGPs, which may limit the impact of the paper. To demonstrate the full potential of the MOSM kernel, it would be beneficial to include experiments on datasets where other models struggle to perform well.

Furthermore, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the statistical significance of the results presented in Tables 1 and 2. As the standard deviations are not provided, it is unclear whether the differences in performance between the proposed method and other models are statistically significant.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-organized.
