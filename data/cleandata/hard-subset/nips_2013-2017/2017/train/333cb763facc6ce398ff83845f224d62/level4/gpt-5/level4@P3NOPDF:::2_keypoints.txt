- Introduces multi-output covariance construction by specifying cross-PSDs and using inverse Fourier transform, extending spectral mixture ideas to multi-output GPs.
- Principled extension leveraging Bochner’s theorem for stationary processes.
- Potentially very expressive: can capture multiple frequencies, smoothness, scales, and encode cross-task phase/delay beyond LMC.
- Empirical results on synthetic, weather, and Swiss Jura data are competitive with standard multi-output kernels.
- Experiments are limited to common benchmarks and do not convincingly show clear advantages over existing methods.
- Does not demonstrate whether the multi-output extension inherits spectral mixture extrapolation (e.g., long-horizon forecasting).
- Reported means and standard deviations lack statistical-significance testing; many results overlap within one standard deviation.
- Missing clear parametrization and validity conditions to ensure cross-spectral matrix is Hermitian, PSD for all frequencies, and yields real-valued covariances.
- Mixture components raise identifiability and interpretability concerns (redundancy, label switching); guidance on initialization and regularization is absent.
- Computational scalability and complexity (training time, memory, scaling with outputs/components/data) are not sufficiently discussed.
- Question: How is cross-PSD validity enforced (parametrization/constraints per mixture component)?
- Question: Are phase offsets or time/space delays modeled explicitly, and how are they identified/learned?
- Question: Were extrapolation/long-horizon forecasting experiments performed or can they be added?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to number of spectral components and initialization; any heuristics used?
- Question: What is computational complexity in P (outputs), N (points), Q (components), and runtime vs. LMC/ICM/convolved baselines?
- Question: Were statistical significance tests done for the reported results across folds?
- Question: Is code and hyperparameter configuration available for reproducibility?
- Suggestion: Add experiments that stress spectral advantages (extrapolation, cross-task phase/delay, structured missingness).
- Suggestion: Compare against stronger/diversified baselines (ICM/LMC with multiple latents, semiparametric latent factor models, convolved processes, single-output spectral mixture).
- Suggestion: Provide ablations on Q, phase/delay terms, and initialization/regularization sensitivity, plus runtime/performance trade-offs.
- Suggestion: Clarify theoretical guarantees and implementation details ensuring kernel validity and discuss identifiability.
- Suggestion: Report training time, memory usage, and convergence behavior to contextualize practicality.
- Overall: Promising and natural extension but currently borderline due to insufficient empirical demonstrations, missing theoretical/implementation details, and absent significance/scalability analysis.
