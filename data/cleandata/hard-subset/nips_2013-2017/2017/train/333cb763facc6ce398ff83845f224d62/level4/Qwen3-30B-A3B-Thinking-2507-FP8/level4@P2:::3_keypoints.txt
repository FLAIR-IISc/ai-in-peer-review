- Proposes MOSM kernel extending Spectral Mixture to MOGPs via Cramér’s Theorem, using complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase differences.  
- MOSM provides interpretable parameters (frequency, magnitude, delay, phase) and guarantees positive definiteness via a rank-1 factorization.  
- Validated on synthetic derivative/delayed signals and two real datasets (climate sensors, Jura heavy metals); outperforms baselines on Cadmium prediction and synthetic extrapolation.  
- Originality: first MOGP kernel to jointly model delays and phase shifts with complex spectral densities, addressing limitations of CSM and SM-LMC.  
- Theoretical quality: technically sound with Theorems 1–2 linking spectral design to positive definiteness.  
- Experimental quality: synthetic example shows MOSM can learn cross-covariances without prior knowledge; competitive MAE and statistically significant result on Jura.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with precise definitions, clear derivation (Eq.11), and effective figures illustrating spectral parameter effects.  
- Significance: enables interpretable, physically meaningful cross-channel relationships (useful for fault detection, sensor fusion) and can reduce to CSM when parameters are shared.  
- Weakness — training complexity: hyperparameter optimization omitted; O(m²) spectral parameter space may hinder scalability for large numbers of channels.  
- Weakness — real-world impact: marginal MAE gains on climate data and statistical significance only for one sensor, limiting practical advantage in highly correlated datasets.  
- Minor omissions: synthetic experiment should state N per channel (500, 400, 400) and a missing word ("square-exponential") in Section 3.1.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel joint modeling of delays/phase, rigorous theoretical foundation, strong synthetic validation and Jura results, clear presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: scalability concerns, limited gains in correlated real data, and insufficient statistical testing for most real-world comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, technically rigorous, and novel contribution to MOGPs; recommendation: Accept.
