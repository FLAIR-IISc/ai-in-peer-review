- Proposes the MOSM kernel: a novel, flexible MOGP covariance built by designing complex-valued cross-spectral densities and inverting via Cramér’s Theorem.
- Principled derivation and originality: constructs a valid Hermitian positive-definite spectral density matrix, extending SM to multi-output and explicitly modeling delays and phase shifts.
- Related work: conceptually extends single-output Spectral Mixture (SM) and is related to the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel.
- Expressiveness and interpretability: MOSM parameters map to magnitudes, frequencies, phase shifts, and delays, enabling modeling of derivatives and delayed signals.
- Empirical evaluation: tested on a synthetic derivatives/delays task and two real-world datasets (climate and Swiss Jura heavy metals).
- Limited empirical advantage: on real datasets MOSM often performs similarly to existing methods (especially CSM), with overlapping error bars and modest improvements.
- Missed demonstration of extrapolation: paper does not show whether MOSM retains SM’s long-range extrapolation ability in multi-output settings.
- Statistical significance unclear: request to clarify whether improvements on synthetic and climate experiments are statistically significant like the Jura analysis.
- Hyperparameter concerns: ask for more intuition/strategy for initialization given many parameters and potential sensitivity to local optima.
