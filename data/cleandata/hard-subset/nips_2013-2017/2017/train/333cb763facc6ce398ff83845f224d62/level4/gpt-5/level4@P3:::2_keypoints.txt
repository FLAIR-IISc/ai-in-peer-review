- Proposal: Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for multi-output GPs built in the spectral domain via Cramér’s theorem.  
- Construction: Specifies complex-valued cross-spectral densities as Gaussian mixtures and obtains closed-form cross-covariances by inverse Fourier transform.  
- Interpretability: Parameters map to magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, and explicit time delay per output pair.  
- Generalization: MOSM generalizes SM-LMC and CSM by allowing per-pair spectral parameters and explicit delays/phase shifts.  
- Validation: Demonstrated on a synthetic three-output task (including derivatives/delays) and two small real datasets (climate stations and Jura).  
- Relation to prior work: Extends single-output SM (Wilson & Adams) to multi-output via spectral factorization and contrasts with latent-process LMC/CONV approaches.  
- PSD factorization: Ensures positive definiteness pointwise in frequency through S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) and enforces Hermitian symmetry for real-valued processes.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: Spectral-domain construction is principled and the factorization is transparent.  
- Strength — interpretability: Per-pair spectral parameters provide intuitive descriptions of cross-channel relationships.  
- Strength — expressivity: Complex cross-spectra capture asymmetric cross-covariances, negative correlations, and delays not handled by some prior models.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: Hermitian PSD construction and closed-form inverse transforms are well-motivated.  
- Strength — synthetic result: Synthetic derivative-and-delay example convincingly shows MOSM’s advantage over latent-process models.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Real-data experiments are small-scale and show only modest improvements; synthetic case is the clearest win.  
- Weakness — statistical testing: Significance testing is incomplete (KS test only on one sensor); paired tests across datasets and runs are needed.  
- Weakness — clarity/notation: Hermitian/conjugation notation and the symmetrization condition (Sij(−ω)=overline{Sji(ω)}) are sometimes unclear.  
- Weakness — practical guidance: Initialization, parameter constraints, and identifiability for Q>1 are not discussed in depth.  
- Weakness — scalability: All experiments use exact GP inference; parameter counts and complexity may grow quickly without sparse approximations.  
- Quality: Technical derivations appear sound but experimental metrics are limited (MAE only) and significance analyses are incomplete.  
- Clarity suggestions: Make conjugation explicit, state assumptions (stationarity, diagonal Σ), and summarize training details and parameter counts.  
- Originality: Using per-pair complex cross-spectral densities to model phase and delay is a novel and meaningful extension of prior spectral MOGPs.  
- Significance: Promising and interpretable approach, but needs stronger empirical demonstrations (known delays, larger m, extrapolation) to be compelling.  
- Author requests: Report paired statistical tests across datasets, provide ablations (CSM vs MOSM, with/without delay/phase), and show extrapolation examples.  
- Additional requests: Discuss identifiability/initialization strategies, visualize learned delays/phases, clarify Hermitian notation, and propose a sparse MOSM variant.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled, interpretable, expressive kernel with closed-form kernels and a clear synthetic advantage.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited real-data gains, incomplete statistical analyses, notation ambiguities, missing training details, and no scalable inference demonstrated.  
- Overall recommendation: Solid, well-motivated contribution recommended for acceptance conditional on stronger empirical evidence and clarifications in presentation and scalability.
