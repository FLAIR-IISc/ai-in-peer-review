Introduces a novel multi-output Gaussian process covariance function defined in the spectral domain, addressing an overlooked area.

Extends the spectral mixture approach to the multi-output scenario and proposes a way to describe cross-covariance that does not require positive-definiteness.

Builds on and generalizes prior work (Wilson & Adams 2013), representing a significant theoretical contribution.

Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction and solid coverage of GP fundamentals.

Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid and the proposed extensions appear novel.

Authors effectively connect to previous literature, showing sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

Experimental evaluation is satisfactory but may not provide strong intuition—results on the Jura dataset are hard to interpret—yet are adequate for a methodological paper.
