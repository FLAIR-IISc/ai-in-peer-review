- Introduces a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture approach to multi-output Gaussian processes.  
- Builds on CSM and LMC and provides a more principled construction via Cramér's Theorem.  
- MOSM offers clear parametric interpretation of inter-channel relationships, including delays and phase differences.  
- Kernel derived from complex-valued cross-spectral densities that ensure positive definiteness.  
- Validated with synthetic experiments and real-world climate and heavy metal concentration datasets.  
- Experimental results are competitive, with notably strong performance on Cadmium prediction.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides technical details for reproducibility.  
- Strength: principled theoretical extension of spectral mixture kernels to multi-output settings.  
- Strength: interpretable model parameters (delays, phases) and strong experimental validation against relevant baselines.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of parameter learning challenges and computational costs.  
- Weakness: insufficient information on model performance with small training datasets.  
- Weakness: synthetic example omits number of training points.  
- Questions for authors: performance when training data is scarce?  
- Questions for authors: how does the model handle channels with very different length scales and resulting correlation limits?  
- Minor: specify number of training points in Section 4.1 and fix missing words at lines 74 and 140.
