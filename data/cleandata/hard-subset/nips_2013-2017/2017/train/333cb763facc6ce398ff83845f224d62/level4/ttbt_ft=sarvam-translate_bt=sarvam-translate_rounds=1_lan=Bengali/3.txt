This paper presents a new covariance structure for multi-output GCs, consistent with the generalization of the spectral method (Bachner's theorem) for kernel construction by A.G. Adams and R.P. Adams. A particular advantage of the proposed method is that the parameters of the model can be interpreted (e.g., delays or phase differences between the outputs).

Defining a covariance structure for multi-output GCs is a difficult task, and currently, there are relatively few methods available to do so. The authors' proposed method is theoretically sound and has demonstrated its effectiveness on several datasets.

The method is clearly described in a well-written paper, and the choices for the figures seem relevant to me. If learning the parameters is not too difficult (the paper does not provide much information on this), I would hope that this method becomes a standard for multi-output GCs.

Questions:
* How does the method behave when a small number of training points are available?
* (More curious) A large length-scale channel cannot be highly correlated with a small length-scale channel. How does this manifest in your model?
