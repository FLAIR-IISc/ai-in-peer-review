This manuscript introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function constructed in the spectral domain. Designing covariances spectrally is, in my view, an important and underappreciated contribution to GP modeling, and this paper extends that idea to the multi-output setting. The work builds on [1]. In the multi-output case, the central difficulty is specifying cross-covariances, which individually need not be positive-definite. The authors present precisely such a construction in a way that yields a spectral-mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is clearly written, with a concise and accessible introduction to the problem and GP basics. The presentation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and to the best of my knowledge the proposed extensions are novel. The connections to prior work are well articulated, and I believe the paper contains sufficient novelty to merit publication.

The experiments are adequate for a methods paper of this kind, though they do not provide much intuition; in particular, the Jura dataset is difficult to interpret. Nevertheless, for a contribution focused on kernel design, the empirical evaluation is sufficient.

-[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In , Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2013, Atlanta, GA, USA, 16-21 June 2013 (pp. 1067–1075). : JMLR.org.
