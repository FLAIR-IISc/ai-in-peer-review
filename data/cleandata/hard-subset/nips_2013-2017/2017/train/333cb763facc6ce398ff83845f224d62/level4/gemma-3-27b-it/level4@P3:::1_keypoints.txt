- Novelty: Introduces MOSM, a novel spectral-domain MOGP kernel that models delays and phase differences across outputs.  
- Theoretical soundness: Kernel construction is principled and grounded in Cramér’s Theorem, ensuring validity.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well-written with lucid explanations, even for readers new to spectral methods.  
- Prior work connection: Builds convincingly on and distinguishes itself from prior spectral mixture work (e.g., Wilson & Adams 2013).  
- Parameter interpretability: Spectral formulation gives clear, intuitive interpretations of kernel parameters.  
- Experimental adequacy: Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate effectiveness but lack deep intuitive insight.  
- Dataset complexity: Jura dataset is complex, making definitive conclusions difficult from those results.  
- Comparison scope: Comparisons include SM-LMC, CONV, and CSM but are not exhaustive against state-of-the-art MOGP methods.  
- Computational concerns: Paper does not discuss computational cost or scalability of the proposed method.  
- Recommendation summary: Strong, theoretically sound contribution with clear presentation; reviewer recommends acceptance despite experimental and computational discussion shortcomings.
