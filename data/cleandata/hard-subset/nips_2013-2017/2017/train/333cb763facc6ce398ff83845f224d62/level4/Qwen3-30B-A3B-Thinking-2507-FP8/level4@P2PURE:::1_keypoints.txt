- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian process covariance function formulated in the spectral domain.  
- Highlights spectral-domain covariance design as an important, underexplored contribution to Gaussian processes.  
- Extends spectral methods to the multi-output setting and addresses the challenge of cross-covariance not being inherently positive-definite.  
- Presents a solution that yields a spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.  
- Paper is well-written with a concise, clear introduction to the problem and GP fundamentals.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is accessible and the proposed extensions appear novel.  
- Relationship to prior work is thoroughly explained.  
- The work demonstrates sufficient novelty to warrant publication.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate for validation but lacks extensive interpretability (notably on the Jura dataset).
