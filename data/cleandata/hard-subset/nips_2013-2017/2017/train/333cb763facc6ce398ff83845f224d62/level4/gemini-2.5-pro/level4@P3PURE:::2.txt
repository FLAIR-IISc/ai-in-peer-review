This paper introduces a novel covariance function for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs). The proposed approach defines the covariance through the spectral domain. Rather than specifying the cross-covariance functions directly, the authors model the cross-power spectral densities and then obtain the corresponding cross-covariances via the inverse Fourier transform. This method extends the spectral mixture approach of [6], which was originally developed for single-output GPs. In that work, the power spectral density was represented as a mixture of Gaussians, yielding a flexible kernel with strong extrapolation capabilities. The authors demonstrate their method on a synthetic dataset and two real-world benchmarks: the weather dataset and the Swiss Jura dataset for metal concentrations.

The extension of the spectral mixture kernel from [6] to the multi-output setting is a clever and promising direction for constructing flexible MOGP covariance functions. However, a key motivation for the original kernel in [6] was its ability to perform extrapolation. It is therefore surprising that this work does not investigate whether the proposed multi-output extension inherits this valuable property. The experimental evaluation is limited to standard benchmark datasets, where the proposed model achieves performance comparable to existing MOGP models. The paper's impact could be substantially increased by demonstrating its advantages on tasks or datasets where traditional MOGP models are known to be unsuitable.

Regarding the results in Tables 1 and 2, were statistical significance tests performed? The reported performance differences between the proposed method and the baselines appear to be small, and it is unclear if they are statistically significant, especially when considering the reported standard deviations.
