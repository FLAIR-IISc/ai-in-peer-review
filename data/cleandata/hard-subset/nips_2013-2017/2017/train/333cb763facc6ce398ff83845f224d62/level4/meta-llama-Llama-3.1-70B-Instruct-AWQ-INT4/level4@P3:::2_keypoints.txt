- Proposes MOSM kernel: specify cross-power spectral densities and inverse FT to obtain MOGP cross-covariances (extension of [6]).
- Well-motivated, a natural extension of the spectral mixture concept to multi-output GPs.
- MOSM offers clear spectral interpretation and can model delays and phase differences between channels.
- Uses Cramér’s theorem and spectral density factorization, providing a solid theoretical foundation.
- Experimental validation on a synthetic dataset and two real-world datasets (climate, metal concentrations) shows effectiveness.
- Experimental section is limited and results are similar to existing multi-output covariance functions.
- Lacks experiments on datasets where competing models fail, reducing demonstrated uniqueness and impact.
- No clear analysis of the MOSM kernel’s extrapolation capabilities compared to [6].
- Statistical significance is insufficiently detailed; unclear if improvements (e.g., Sotonmet) are significant.
- Paper is technically sound; theoretical claims are supported and presentation is well-structured.
- Writing, notation, and background/related-work coverage are clear and consistent.
- Contribution is original — novel approach to designing multivariate covariance functions for MOGPs.
- Potentially significant impact due to principled multivariate covariance design and spectral interpretability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, spectral viewpoint, modeling of delays/phases, thorough literature positioning.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, missing extrapolation discussion, incomplete statistical analysis.
