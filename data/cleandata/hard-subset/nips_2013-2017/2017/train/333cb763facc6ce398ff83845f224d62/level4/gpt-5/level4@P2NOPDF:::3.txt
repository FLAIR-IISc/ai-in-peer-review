This paper presents a new covariance structure for multi-output Gaussian processes, extending the spectral kernel construction (via Bochner’s theorem) introduced by A. G. Adams and R. P. Adams. A notable strength of the approach is the interpretability of its parameters, which capture meaningful cross-output properties such as delays and phase differences.

Designing expressive, well-structured covariances for multi-output GPs remains challenging, and relatively few robust methods exist. The proposed construction is theoretically well founded and demonstrates strong empirical performance across several datasets. The paper is clearly written, and the chosen experiments and visualizations are appropriate and informative. Provided that parameter learning is not prohibitively difficult—an aspect for which the paper offers limited detail—I expect this approach could become a standard tool for multi-output GPs.

Questions
- How does the method perform with limited training data? Is it robust when the number of observations per output is small?
- More of a curiosity: Under many kernel constructions, a channel with a large length-scale is typically not highly correlated with a channel having a small length-scale. How is this behavior expressed or constrained in your model?
