- Introduces a new covariance structure for multi-output GPs, generalizing the spectral (Bochner) approach.  
- Main advantage: interpretable parameters such as delays and phase differences between outputs.  
- Notes that defining covariance for multi-output GPs is challenging and existing solutions are few.  
- Method is theoretically sound and shown effective on several datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with relevant illustrations.  
- Limited information on computational cost of parameter learning; unclear if it’s burdensome.  
- If parameter learning is not too costly, the method could become a standard approach for multi-output GPs.  
- Reviewer questions: how does the method perform with limited training data?  
- Reviewer curiosity: how does the model handle channels with very different length-scales and thus expected low correlation?  
- Minor comments: specify number of training points in the synthetic example (Sec. 4.1); missing words likely at lines 74 and 140.
