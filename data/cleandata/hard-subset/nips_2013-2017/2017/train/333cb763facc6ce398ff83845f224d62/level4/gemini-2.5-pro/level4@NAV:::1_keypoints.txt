- Introduces a multi-output Gaussian Process covariance formulated in the spectral domain.  
- Constructing covariances via spectral densities is an underexplored and valuable approach.  
- Successfully generalizes the spectral-density paradigm to the multi-output setting.  
- Work is a direct extension of Wilson & Adams (2013).  
- Central challenge is modeling cross-covariance, which need not be positive-definite.  
- Authors present a principled method yielding a multi-output spectral mixture covariance.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with a concise GP introduction.  
- Explanation of the spectral mixture kernel and proposed multi-output extensions is lucid and appears novel.  
- Relationship to existing literature is thoroughly explained; novelty is sufficient for publication.  
- Experimental validation is adequate for a methodological paper but offers limited intuitive insight (notably on the Jura dataset).
