This paper presents a multi-output Gaussian process (GP) covariance function defined in the spectral domain. The approach of designing covariance functions spectrally is an important contribution to the GP literature that, in my opinion, has not received the attention it deserves. This paper makes a valuable contribution by extending this methodology to the multi-output scenario.

Building on the work in [1], this paper addresses the central challenge of the multi-output case: how to model the cross-covariance function, which, unlike a standard covariance, is not required to be positive definite. The authors propose a principled solution to this problem, resulting in a novel spectral mixture covariance for multi-output GPs.

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The authors provide a concise introduction to the problem domain and the fundamentals of Gaussian processes. The explanation of the spectral mixture kernel is lucid, and the proposed extensions for the multi-output scenario are, to my knowledge, novel. The relationship to previous work is also thoroughly explained. I believe the work contains sufficient novelty to warrant publication.

The experimental evaluation is sufficient for a paper of this nature. However, the experiments offer limited intuition into the model's behavior; the results on the Jura dataset, for instance, are difficult to interpret. Despite this, I find the overall experimental validation to be adequate for a primarily methodological contribution.

[1] Wilson, A. G., & Adams, R. P. (2013). Gaussian process kernels for pattern discovery and extrapolation. In *Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2013)* (pp. 1067–1075). JMLR.org.
