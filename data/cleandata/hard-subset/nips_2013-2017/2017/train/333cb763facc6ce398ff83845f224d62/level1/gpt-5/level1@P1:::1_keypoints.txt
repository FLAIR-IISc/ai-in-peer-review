- Proposes MOSM kernel using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and spectral factorization S(ω)=R(ω)^H R(ω) to guarantee positive definiteness and yield closed-form time-domain cross-covariances.  
- Models per-output amplitude, mean, covariance, phase, and delay in frequency domain; symmetrization and inverse FT give interpretable per-pair covariance formulas.  
- MOSM generalizes SM-LMC and CSM, adding inter-channel delays and channel-specific auto-spectra.  
- Experiments: synthetic three-output task shows MOSM uniquely recovers derivative and delay relations; climate imputation and Jura datasets show competitive or improved performance.  
- Theory is principled via Cramér’s theorem; symmetrization ensures real outputs and closed-form interpretable parameters (magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, delay).  
- Notational issue: Sij expression omits complex conjugation and should be \bar{Ri}Rj to maintain Hermitian structure.  
- Correctness: positive definiteness follows from spectral factorization; derived parameter formulas are standard and reduce correctly for i=j.  
- Empirics: synthetic study is strong and baselines are appropriate, but real-data sets are modest, and log predictive densities and computational costs are not reported.  
- Statistical testing is limited/incomplete for comparisons involving CONV (results borrowed).  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-organized with readable derivations; needs fixes for conjugation typo, minor typos, and more detail on initialization/optimization.  
- Originality: novel and elegant extension of SM kernels to multi-output via complex cross-spectra enabling joint phase-delay modeling distinct from prior LMC/CONV approaches.  
- Significance: useful, interpretable, and flexible addition to MOGP toolbox for lag/phase problems, but current scalability limits practical impact.  
- Strengths: principled PD construction, models phase+delay, closed-form covariances, generalizes prior kernels, strong synthetic demo, implemented in GPflow.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: lacks sparse/variational scalability; many per-output parameters need discussion of initialization and identifiability; recommend reporting log predictive density, runtimes, ablations, and parameter counts.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to Q and initialization of θ/φ; can θ capture long delays vs low-frequency phase; does increasing spectral rank Q meaningfully expand cross-output dependency?  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theoretical contribution, extends CSM by modeling delays, competitive results and compelling synthetic showcase, relevant to conference topics.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited scalability and empirical breadth, minor notation/theory issues, insufficient discussion of optimization/initialization and identifiability.  
- Overall recommendation: lean towards acceptance, contingent on addressing clarity, empirical evaluation, and scalability concerns.
