- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output GP kernel using complex-valued cross-spectral densities via Cramér’s Theorem.  
- Extends spectral mixture kernels to model both delays and phase differences between channels.  
- Claims greater expressiveness than LMC and CSM by explicitly parameterizing frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay.  
- Theoretical grounding is clear, with concise presentation of Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems.  
- MOSM kernel is clearly defined with interpretable parameters tied to spectral properties.  
- Experiments include synthetic validation (recovering delays/derivatives) and comparisons on climate and heavy-metal datasets.  
- Demonstrated improved performance and interpretability over existing MOGP methods in reported experiments.  
- Computational cost likely increases due to complex-valued parameters and multi-output extension; not adequately addressed.  
- Statistical significance of real-world improvements is weakly established; KS test only significant for one sensor.  
- The claimed generalization of CSM by MOSM needs clearer conditions and connections.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, practically relevant, and experimentally promising.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational challenges and inconsistent statistical evidence.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational implications and more rigorous statistical evaluation.
