- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel that constructs positive-definite cross-spectra via factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) using Cramér’s theorem.  
- Models each per-output factor Ri(ω) as a complex Gaussian with interpretable parameters (weight, mean frequency, spectral covariance, delay, phase).  
- Symmetrization yields closed-form real-valued cross-covariances: a SE envelope times a cosine with channel-pair-specific frequency, delay, and phase.  
- MOSM subsumes SM-LMC and CSM and adds joint modeling of delays and phase shifts while retaining interpretability.  
- Experiments: synthetic 3-output (signal, derivative, delayed) where MOSM uniquely extrapolates derivative and delay behavior.  
- Experiments: climate station imputation with similar performance to strong baselines.  
- Experiments: Jura geostatistics showing MOSM improves on Cadmium and is competitive on Copper.  
- Technical soundness: construction and positive-definiteness via spectral factorization and symmetrization are principled and derivations are consistent.  
- Empirical support: synthetic task convincingly demonstrates expressivity advantages; real-data results are competitive though modest.  
- Reproducibility: implementation uses GPflow/TensorFlow but lacks detailed initialization, constraints, optimization settings, runtime reporting, and code release.  
- Clarity: generally well written with helpful parameter interpretations distinguishing auto- and cross-covariances.  
- Clarity issues: minor notational inconsistencies (e.g., 1/2 factors), unclear explicit forms for Ri(ω) and symmetry operation, and missing discussion that summing Q components gives rank ≤ Q.  
- Identifiability: cross-parameters depend on differences (θi−θj, φi−φj) so global shifts are unidentifiable; recommend fixing a reference or using priors.  
- Originality: novel systematic complex cross-spectral factorization yielding phase and delay, with per-output parameterization that induces pairwise cross-parameters.  
- Significance: flexible, interpretable MOGP family that can express asymmetric cross-covariances—potentially impactful for multi-sensor and spatio-temporal tasks if made scalable.  
- Strength: principled PSD-guaranteed construction with clear spectral interpretation and closed-form time-domain kernel.  
- Strength: strictly generalizes SM-LMC and CSM and shows a convincing synthetic advantage.  
- Weakness: no scalability or sparse/approximate inference presented; cubic complexity limits practical applicability for large m, N, Q.  
- Weakness: limited optimization details—initialization heuristics, parameter constraints, phase wrap-around handling, regularization are not discussed.  
- Weakness: identifiability and potential redundancy between delay and phase (especially for narrow-band components) are not analyzed.  
- Weakness: coupling of cross-mean frequencies to per-output means may restrict expressivity when cross-spectra have frequencies absent in autospectra.  
- Weakness: limited empirical diversity and missing ablations on Q, rank, per-output vs per-pair parameterization, and sensitivity to initialization.  
- Suggestions: add scalability strategies (variational inducing, inter-domain/Fourier features, SKI/Kronecker/block-Toeplitz) for many outputs/long series.  
- Suggestions: provide initialization heuristics (periodogram peaks), constraints (fix reference θ1/φ1, bound phases), priors, and report runtime/memory; release code.  
- Suggestions: include ablations varying Q, rank, and parameterizations; test robustness to initialization and report optimization behavior.  
- Suggestions: discuss identifiability and possible reparameterizations to reduce degeneracy between delay and phase.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, interpretable extension of spectral GPs to multi-output with closed-form PSD-guaranteed kernels and novel modeling of delay+phase.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation breadth, no scalability story, missing ablations and optimization details, and only modest real-data gains over prior methods.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on addressing optimization/identifiability clarifications and adding a scalability/discussion component.
