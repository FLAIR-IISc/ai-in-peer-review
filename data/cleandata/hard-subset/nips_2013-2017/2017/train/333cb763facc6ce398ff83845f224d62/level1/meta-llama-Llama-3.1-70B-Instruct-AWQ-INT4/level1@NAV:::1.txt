**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a new kernel for multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) called the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel. This kernel is designed to model complex relationships between multiple outputs by constructing a positive-definite matrix of complex-valued spectral densities. The authors extend the spectral mixture approach to MOGPs using Cramér's theorem, which allows them to design the spectral densities in the Fourier domain. The resulting kernel provides a clear interpretation of its parameters from a spectral viewpoint, including frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for a pair of channels.

**Strengths**

1. **Expressiveness**: The MOSM kernel is more expressive than previous MOGP models, allowing for delays and phase differences between channels.
2. **Interpretability**: The kernel provides a clear interpretation of its parameters from a spectral viewpoint.
3. **Flexibility**: The MOSM kernel can model both positively and negatively correlated signals.
4. **Competitive performance**: The MOSM kernel performs competitively with other MOGP models on real-world datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The MOSM kernel may be computationally more expensive than other MOGP models due to the need to compute the inverse Fourier transform.
2. **Limited scalability**: The paper does not discuss how to scale the MOSM kernel to large datasets.
3. **Lack of sparse implementation**: The paper does not provide a sparse implementation of the MOSM kernel, which may limit its applicability to large datasets.

**Originality**

1. **Extension of spectral mixture approach**: The paper extends the spectral mixture approach to MOGPs using Cramér's theorem, which is a novel contribution.
2. **Design of complex-valued spectral densities**: The paper proposes a new way to design complex-valued spectral densities for MOGPs.

**Significance**

1. **Advances the state of the art**: The MOSM kernel advances the state of the art in MOGP modeling by providing a more expressive and interpretable kernel.
2. **Potential applications**: The MOSM kernel has potential applications in various fields, including climate modeling, geostatistics, and signal processing.
