- Proposes MOSM: a novel Multi-Output Spectral Mixture kernel for MOGPs built from parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities.
- Constructs MOSM using Cramér's Theorem to transform cross-spectral densities into a valid kernel.
- MOSM parameters have clear spectral interpretations: frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for channel pairs.
- Demonstrates MOSM on synthetic and real-world datasets (climate signals, heavy metal concentrations).
- Paper is well-written with a thorough background on MOGPs and spectral mixture kernels.
- MOSM is well-motivated and its construction and properties are clearly explained.
- Experimental results are convincing and include detailed analysis.
- Strength: novel approach to modeling multi-output GPs with spectral mixture kernels.
- Strength: provides interpretable spectral parameters for cross-channel relationships.
- Strength: empirical validation on synthetic and real-world data.
- Weakness: assumes high familiarity with MOGPs and spectral mixture kernels, limiting accessibility.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art MOGP models.
- Weakness: experiments cover only a few datasets, raising questions about generalizability.
- Originality: introducing MOSM and using complex-valued cross-spectra with Cramér's Theorem is unique.
- Significance: MOSM could be broadly applicable (climate, geostatistics, signal processing) and offers a new modeling perspective.
- Clarity: well-organized, consistent notation, and helpful figures/tables.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear spectral interpretation, and demonstrated effectiveness.
- Arguments for rejection: steep prerequisite knowledge, insufficient SOTA comparisons, and limited experimental breadth.
