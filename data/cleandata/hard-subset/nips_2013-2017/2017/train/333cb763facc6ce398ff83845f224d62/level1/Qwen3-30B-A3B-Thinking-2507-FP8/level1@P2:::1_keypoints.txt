- Introduces MOSM kernel that extends Spectral Mixture to multi-output GPs and models delays between channels.  
- Uses complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem for a principled multi-output covariance construction.  
- MOSM provides interpretable parameters for frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay, with positive definiteness via rank-one spectral decomposition.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound and clearly generalize previous approaches (subsumes SM-LMC and CSM).  
- Strong experimental validation on synthetic data showing MOSM can learn derivatives and delayed signals without prior knowledge.  
- Real-world experiments (climate and heavy metal datasets) show competitive performance, with statistically significant improvement for Cadmium prediction.  
- Experimental setup is well-designed with appropriate baselines (SM-LMC, CONV, CSM) and clear evaluation metrics (MAE).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible (implementation details in TensorFlow/GPflow provided).  
- Novel contribution: first principled way to model delays in MOGPs using complex cross-spectra, addressing a key limitation of CSM.  
- Significant practical impact for time-series tasks (fault detection, imputation, denoising) where inter-channel delays matter.  
- Strengths: theoretical rigor, clear exposition, compelling synthetic experiment, accessible implementation.  
- Weaknesses: modest and sometimes non-significant improvements on some real-world data (climate), limited discussion on when MOSM is most beneficial.  
- Weaknesses (cont.): computational complexity is not explicitly addressed; sparse/efficient implementations left to future work.  
- Overall conclusion: technically strong, novel, practically relevant work; recommended for acceptance.
