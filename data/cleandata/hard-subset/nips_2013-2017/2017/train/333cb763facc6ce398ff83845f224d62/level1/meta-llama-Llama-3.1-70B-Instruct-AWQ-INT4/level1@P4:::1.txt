The paper "Spectral Mixture Kernels for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes" proposes a novel approach to modeling multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) using spectral mixture kernels. The authors extend the spectral mixture concept to MOGPs by designing a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and then applying Cramér's Theorem to construct the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture kernel (MOSM).

The main contribution of the paper is the proposal of the MOSM kernel, which provides a clear interpretation from a spectral viewpoint, where each of its parameters can be identified with frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay for a pair of channels. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the MOSM kernel through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, including climate signals and heavy metal concentrations.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough background on MOGPs and spectral mixture kernels. The proposed MOSM kernel is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of its construction and properties.
