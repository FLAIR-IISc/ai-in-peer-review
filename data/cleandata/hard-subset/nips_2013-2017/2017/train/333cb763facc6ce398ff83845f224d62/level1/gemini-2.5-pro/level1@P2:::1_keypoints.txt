- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs that builds matrix-valued covariance functions in the frequency domain.  
- Extends single-output SM kernels using Cramér’s Theorem to construct valid cross-spectral density matrices.  
- Models the spectral density as a sum of rank-one complex-valued Gaussian mixture factors, yielding a closed-form matrix-valued kernel.  
- Uniquely models channel-specific frequencies/lengthscales, phase shifts, and time delays between outputs.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound, elegant, and guarantees positive-definiteness via the rank-Q decomposition.  
- Synthetic experiment convincingly demonstrates capabilities (learning function, its derivative, and delayed version) where simpler MOGP models fail.  
- Real-world experiments show competitive performance; authors report significance tests and candidly discuss modest gains.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation and effective figures linking spectral and time-domain interpretations.  
- Parameters (µ, Σ, θ, φ) are interpretable, aiding scientific insight beyond black-box models.  
- Method is highly original; CSM is a constrained special case of MOSM.  
- Contribution is significant with broad applicability (geostatistics, neuroscience, finance) and an open-source GPflow implementation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled derivation, increased expressiveness (notably delays), interpretability, strong empirical support, and high clarity.  
- Arguments against (minor): large number of hyperparameters leading to challenging non-convex optimization, and the model’s inherent stationarity limitation.  
- Overall verdict: strong accept — high-quality, novel, and practically significant work.
