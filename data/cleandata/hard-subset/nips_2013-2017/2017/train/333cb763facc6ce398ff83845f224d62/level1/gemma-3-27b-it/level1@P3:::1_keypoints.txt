- Proposes a novel MOSM kernel construction for MOGPs using spectral mixture kernels and Cramér’s Theorem to design cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain.  
- Enables flexible modeling of delays and phase differences between channels, offering greater interpretability than LMC and CSM.  
- Provides solid theoretical grounding and relates multivariate extension to Bochner’s Theorem for scalar GPs.  
- Introduces parametric kernel interpretation (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) that improves model interpretability.  
- Synthetic experiment shows the model can recover specific channel relationships (derivative and delay).  
- Real-world comparisons on two datasets show competitive performance but not consistent substantial improvements.  
- Ablation study gives some insight but is limited to comparisons with existing methods.  
- Implementation in TensorFlow and GPflow promotes reproducibility.  
- Synthetic validation is somewhat contrived and would benefit from more diverse scenarios.  
- Empirical results lack consistent performance gains and limited statistical significance reporting.  
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity or scalability versus alternatives.  
- Choice of Q=1 for decomposition rank is arbitrary and not sufficiently justified or explored.  
- Relationship to CSM is discussed but lacks a deeper comparison highlighting MOSM’s unique advantages.  
- Connection to complex-valued GPs is acknowledged but not fully explained in terms of practical handling or implications.  
- Reviewer’s arguments for acceptance: novel principled approach, strong theory, increased expressiveness and interpretability, synthetic success, competitive real-data results.  
- Reviewer’s arguments against acceptance: inconsistent empirical gains, contrived synthetic case, missing complexity analysis, unexplained Q=1 choice, unclear complex-GP treatment.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on broader synthetic experiments, deeper experimental analysis/statistics, complexity and scalability discussion, clarification of complex-GP implications, and justification/exploration of rank Q.
