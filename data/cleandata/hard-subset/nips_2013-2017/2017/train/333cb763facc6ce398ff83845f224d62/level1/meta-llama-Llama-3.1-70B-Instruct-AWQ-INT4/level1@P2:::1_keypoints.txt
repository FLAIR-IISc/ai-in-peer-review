- Proposes MOSM: a novel multi-output spectral mixture kernel for MOGPs built from a parametric family of complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem.
- MOSM models delays between channels, phase differences, and gives a full parametric interpretation of inter-channel relationships.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction to MOGPs and limitations of prior methods.
- Technical contributions are well-motivated, clearly explained, and positioned within related work.
- Strength: MOSM is expressive and captures complex multi-output relationships.
- Strength: Construction of MOSM is principled, leveraging Cramér's theorem.
- Strength: Evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows competitive performance versus existing MOGP models.
- Weakness: Assumes high familiarity with GPs and spectral mixture kernels, limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Weakness: Lacks intuitive discussion of MOSM properties and explicit differentiation from other MOGP models.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive with additional datasets and comparisons.
- Overall assessment: Significant, original contribution with broad potential applications (climate, signal processing, geostatistics).
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, novel expressive kernel, principled design.
- Arguments against acceptance: high assumed background, and insufficiently comprehensive experiments.
