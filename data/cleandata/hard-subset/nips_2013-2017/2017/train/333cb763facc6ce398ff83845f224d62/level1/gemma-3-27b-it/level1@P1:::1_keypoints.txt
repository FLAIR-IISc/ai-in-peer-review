- Proposes MOSM: a novel MOGP kernel extending the spectral mixture idea via Cramér’s theorem to design complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Key capability: models delays and phase differences between channels, increasing expressiveness over prior methods.  
- Theoretical grounding: derivation is mathematically sound and ties spectral density to covariance via inverse Fourier transform.  
- Builds on and correctly situates itself relative to LMC, CONV, CSM, SM and relevant spectral theorems.  
- Interpretability: parametric cross-spectral form yields clear spectral meaning for parameters versus black-box kernels.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiment shows expected covariance recovery; real-world tests (climate, metal concentration) show competitive performance.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well organized, making technical details accessible.  
- Computational concerns: paper does not analyze scaling or computational cost for many outputs or large datasets.  
- Limited comparisons: baseline parameter tuning and ablation studies are insufficiently detailed.  
- Synthetic evaluation is simplistic; more complex noise/correlation scenarios would strengthen validation.  
- Dataset choice is narrow; results on additional application domains would broaden empirical support.  
- Relation to other recent spectral GP advances and topics like kernel learning/ARD could be elaborated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, more expressive/ interpretable kernel with promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing complexity analysis, incomplete empirical evaluation, and contribution may be incremental.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution but needs minor revisions to address computational analysis and expand empirical details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add complexity discussion, baseline tuning details, expanded synthetic/real experiments, and note options for large-scale adaptation (e.g., sparse GPs).
