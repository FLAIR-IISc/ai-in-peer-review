- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs using Cramér's theorem to model delays and phase differences.
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous and well-explained, providing clear parametric interpretation of all kernel parameters.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows MOSM can learn derivatives and delayed signals and is competitive with state-of-the-art MOGP methods.
- Quality: technically sound approach with comprehensive validation and appropriate statistical analysis (e.g., KS tests).
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible, with helpful background and step-by-step derivations.
- Originality: introduces complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model inter-channel delays, extending prior CSM work.
- Significance: modeling delays between channels is valuable for time-series applications and shows practical gains (e.g., heavy metal dataset).
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity and how cost scales with outputs or spectral components.
- Weakness: could better distinguish between frequency-dependent phase shifts and global time delays in exposition.
- Weakness: comparisons omit more recent MOGP methods (e.g., deep GPs and advanced spectral approaches since 2015).
- Pro-accept: novel theoretical extension, models relationships previous methods cannot, strong experimental validation, and clear writing.
- Con-accept: limited scalability discussion, some ambiguity on phase vs. delay, and comparisons limited to older methods.
- Overall: strong, meaningful contribution to MOGPs with solid theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance.
