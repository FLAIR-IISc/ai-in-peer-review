- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending spectral mixture kernels to MOGPs via Cramér's Theorem.
- Models complex-valued cross-spectral densities to capture delays, phase differences, and per-channel auto-covariance variability.
- Validated on synthetic data and two real-world datasets, showing ability to model complex inter-output relationships and competitive performance.
- Builds on prior SM, LMC, and CSM work, adding delays and a more flexible parametric framework.
- Strength: novel technical contribution offering greater flexibility and interpretability than existing methods.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation ensuring positive definiteness through Cramér's Theorem.
- Strength: experiments (synthetic, climate, heavy metals) demonstrate modeling of delays/phase and practical utility.
- Strength: interpretable spectral parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay) are useful for practitioners.
- Weakness: not consistently superior on all real-world datasets (CSM comparable on some tasks).
- Weakness: paper lacks discussion of computational cost; MOSM likely more expensive than simpler models.
- Weakness: no sparse implementation provided, limiting scalability to large datasets.
- For acceptance: presents a novel, theoretically sound kernel addressing limitations of prior MOGP methods.
- Against acceptance: practical advantages are not consistently demonstrated and scalability is unaddressed.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address scalability and computational cost in future work.
