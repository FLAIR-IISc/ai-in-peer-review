This paper proposes a novel approach to modeling multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) using spectral mixture kernels. The authors extend the spectral mixture concept to MOGPs by designing a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and then applying Cramér's theorem to construct the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel. The MOSM kernel provides a clear interpretation of the relationships across multiple outputs from a spectral viewpoint, allowing for the joint modeling of delays and phase differences.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough introduction to the topic, including a clear explanation of the challenges in designing MOGP kernels. The background section is comprehensive, covering the necessary material on scalar-valued Gaussian processes, spectral mixture kernels, and multi-output Gaussian processes.

The proposed MOSM kernel is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a more expressive and flexible way to model MOGPs compared to existing approaches. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the MOSM kernel through a series of experiments on synthetic and real-world data, including climate signals and heavy metal concentrations.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel and expressive kernel for MOGPs, which allows for the joint modeling of delays and phase differences.
* A thorough introduction to the topic, including a clear explanation of the challenges in designing MOGP kernels.
* Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the MOSM kernel.
* A clear and well-organized presentation of the material.

However, there are some weaknesses and potential areas for improvement:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing MOGP methods, including a discussion of the advantages and limitations of the MOSM kernel.
* The experimental section could be expanded to include more datasets and a more comprehensive evaluation of the MOSM kernel's performance.
* Some of the notation and terminology used in the paper could be clarified or defined more explicitly, particularly for readers who are not familiar with the field.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity and scalability of the MOSM kernel, particularly for large datasets.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of MOGPs, and the proposed MOSM kernel has the potential to be a valuable tool for modeling complex relationships across multiple outputs.
