- Proposal: Introduces a novel parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and uses Cramér's Theorem to derive the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel with interpretable frequency, magnitude, phase, and delay relationships.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with thorough background on MOGPs, spectral mixture kernels, and Cramér's Theorem.  
- Derivation & analysis: MOSM kernel is carefully derived and its properties are thoroughly analyzed.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation (synthetic + two real-world datasets) shows MOSM is competitive with existing MOGP models.  
- Strength: Principled, novel approach that provides clear interpretation of inter-channel relationships.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical derivation and analysis of the kernel’s properties.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental validation demonstrating competitiveness.  
- Weakness: Method complexity may hinder implementation and scalability to large datasets.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to other MOGP models are limited, leaving performance picture incomplete.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of potential applications and broader implications.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel principled method, competitive empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Con acceptance: Computational/implementation complexity, limited baselines, and scarce application discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but authors should address scalability and broaden comparisons to strengthen the paper.
