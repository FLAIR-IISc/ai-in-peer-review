- Contribution: Proposes MOSM kernel for MOGPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model frequency, lengthscale, magnitude, phase, and explicit input delays.
- Construction: Enforces S(ω) ≽ 0 via factorization S(ω) = R(ω)^H R(ω) and symmetrizes S to ensure real-valued time-domain kernels.
- Closed-form kernel: Inverse FT yields a sum of Q components with envelopes times cosines on shifted lags, generalizing SM-LMC and CSM.
- Experiments: Evaluated on a synthetic 3-output task, climate sensor imputation, and Jura geostatistics; best on synthetic and Cadmium, competitive on others.
- Theoretical soundness: Construction is principled under Cramér’s theorem and PSD in frequency implies PSD in time domain.
- Notational issue: Eq. (9) appears to drop a conjugation—should be Sij(ω) = overline{Ri(ω)} Rj(ω); θij and φij definitions depend on this.
- Hermitian symmetry: Reviewer asks explicit verification that Sij(−ω) = overline{Sij(ω)} for real processes, not just at fixed ω.
- Empirical metrics: Only MAE reported; test log-likelihood and uncertainty calibration are missing and would strengthen probabilistic claims.
- Ablations missing: No experiments isolating the effects of delay (θ) and phase (φ) (e.g., MOSM without θ or without φ).
- Scalability concerns: No runtime, complexity, or parameter-count analysis; parameter count grows with number of outputs m and components Q.
- Initialization and identifiability: Trade-offs among μ, θ, φ and sensitivity to initialization are not discussed.
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well organized and interpretable but needs notational fixes, explicit Hermitian statements, and minor typo corrections.
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial extension of spectral mixture methods to model explicit delays in MOGPs with per-channel parameters.
- Significance: Valuable for multi-sensor/time-series and geostatistics where channel misalignment matters; wider impact needs scalability and more delay-known datasets.
- Pros: Principled PSD guarantee, interpretable parameters, strict generalization of SM-LMC/CSM, closed-form kernel, convincing synthetic validation.
- Cons: Notational/technical ambiguity, limited evaluation metrics, no ablations, potential identifiability issues, modest real-data improvements in places.
- Suggested fixes: Correct conjugation in eq. (9), explicitly verify Hermitian symmetry, report NLL/calibration, add ablations, provide parameter counts and runtimes, include a dataset with known delays, and discuss sparse/inducing extensions and multi-dimensional inputs.
