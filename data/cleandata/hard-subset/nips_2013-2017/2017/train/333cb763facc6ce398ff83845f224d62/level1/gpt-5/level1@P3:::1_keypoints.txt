- Proposes MOSM, a multi-output spectral mixture kernel with per-output spectral params, delays, and phases using spectral factorization and Cramér’s theorem.  
- Derives closed-form cross-covariances (exponentially windowed cosines) yielding output-pair-specific frequency, bandwidth, delay, and phase.  
- MOSM generalizes SM-LMC and CSM, subsuming them as special or more constrained cases.  
- Experiments: synthetic (derivative/delay), climate sensor imputation, and Jura metals — MOSM often best or competitive, especially on synthetic and Cadmium.  

- Theoretical correctness: spectral factorization via R(ω)H R(ω) guarantees Hermitian PSD cross-spectra and validity.  
- Notational/technical issue: inconsistent use of Hermitian transpose/conjugation (e.g., Sij vs. R:i(ω)H R:j(ω)) needs fixing.  
- Conjugate symmetry for real processes (Sij(−ω)=Sij(ω)*) is enforced informally; the mirrored construction and PSD preservation should be explicitly justified.  
- Identifiability concerns: per-output (θi, φi, μi, Σi) have symmetries that produce identical pairwise params; discuss tying/regularization.  
- Rank: model is rank-Q in spectral factorization, which constrains cross-spectral matrix rank per ω and should be discussed as a tradeoff.  

- Evaluation metrics: limited to MAE; should include calibrated log-likelihood or CRPS to assess uncertainty.  
- Initialization: critical for SM/MOSM but initialization details (μ, Σ, θ, φ) and restart strategy are missing.  
- Model capacity fairness: differing parameter counts across methods not reported; report parameter counts and sensitivity to Q.  
- Computational aspects: training time, scalability, and complexity analysis (and any practical tricks) are not reported.  

- Clarity: paper is readable and interpretations are strong, but fix conjugation inconsistencies and explicitly state Hermitian and conjugate-symmetry conditions.  
- Math clarity: show short derivation for Eq. (9–11) (e.g., how φij appears with conjugation) and ensure consistent use of superscripts and notation.  
- Minor issues: typos (“multiouput”, “wrt”) and notation inconsistencies to correct.  

- Originality: novel use of Cramér’s theorem for complex cross-spectra with per-output delays/phases; extends prior spectral MOGP work.  
- Significance: provides interpretable, flexible closed-form MOGP kernels useful for sensor networks and lagged systems; limited by stationarity and SM sensitivity to initialization.  

- Pros: principled PSD-guaranteed spectral construction, interpretable parameters, joint delay/phase modeling, closed-form kernels, generalizes prior models, implementable in GP toolkits.  
- Cons: notational/technical ambiguities, limited evaluation (metrics, init, params, runtime), assumes stationarity, rank-Q may be restrictive.  

- Suggested improvements: clarify Hermitian/conjugate symmetry and provide short proof; report initialization strategy, restarts, parameter counts, training time, and predictive log-likelihood.  
- Further suggestions: ablation studies varying Q and removing θ/φ, discuss identifiability and priors/constraints on θ/φ, and outline sparse/inducing-variable or scalable variants.
