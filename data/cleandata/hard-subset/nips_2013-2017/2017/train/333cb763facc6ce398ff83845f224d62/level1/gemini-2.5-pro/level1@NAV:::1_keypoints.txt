- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel: a principled, expressive kernel for multi-output GPs generalizing Wilson & Adams' SM kernel.
- Key theoretical contribution: constructs matrix-valued kernels in the frequency domain via a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem, ensuring positive definiteness.
- MOSM can explicitly model inter-channel delays and phase shifts, increasing flexibility over prior spectral MOGP methods.
- Shows Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel as a special case of MOSM.
- Originality/significance: elegant theoretical extension and principled design from spectral factorization, addressing limitations of existing methods.
- Empirical quality: derivations are sound and a synthetic experiment compellingly demonstrates learning of derivative and delay relationships that LMC-like latent models fail to capture.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written and provides necessary spectral and MOGP background.
- Experimental weakness: on two real-world datasets MOSM is competitive but does not consistently or significantly outperform the simpler CSM baseline.
- Practical significance limited by lack of a real-world task where inter-channel delays are known to be crucial.
- Scalability/optimization concern: paper omits discussion of optimizing the large number of MOSM hyperparameters and initialization/optimization strategies for many outputs or mixture components.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel theoretical advance with minor empirical/practical gaps that do not undermine the contribution.
