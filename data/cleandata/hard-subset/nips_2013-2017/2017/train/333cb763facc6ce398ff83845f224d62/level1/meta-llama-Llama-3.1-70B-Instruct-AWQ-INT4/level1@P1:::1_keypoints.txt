- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel using a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to design multivariate covariances.  
- MOSM models delays and phase differences across channels and offers full parametric interpretation of inter-channel relationships.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a clear motivation and concise background on GPs, spectral mixtures, and Cramér's theorem.  
- Main technical contribution: derivation of MOSM via factor decomposition of the spectral density matrix, yielding interpretable spectral parameters.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, showing MOSM can recover auto- and cross-covariances on synthetic data.  
- MOSM outperforms existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets in the experiments presented.  
- Strength: novel and expressive approach that extends spectral mixture kernels to multi-output settings.  
- Strength: clear and concise mathematical derivation that aids understanding and reproducibility.  
- Strength: well-designed experimental validation on both synthetic and real data.  
- Weakness: assumes high mathematical sophistication, potentially limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art MOGP models to contextualize performance.  
- Weakness: does not discuss computational complexity, an important practical consideration.  
- Originality: novel application of Cramér's theorem to construct multivariate covariance functions.  
- Significance: MOSM could substantially improve modeling of complex inter-channel relationships and offers interpretable spectral parameters.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to address accessibility, comparative baselines, and computational complexity in the final version.
