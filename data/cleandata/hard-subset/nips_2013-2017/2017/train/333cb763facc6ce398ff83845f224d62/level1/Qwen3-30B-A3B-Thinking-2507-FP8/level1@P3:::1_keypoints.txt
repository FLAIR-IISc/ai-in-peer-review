- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture kernels to MOGPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase differences.  
- Derivation is rigorous and technically sound, built from first principles via Cramér's theorem.  
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM can recover derivative relationships and delayed signals that prior methods cannot.  
- Real-world experiments (climate, heavy metals) are competitive; MOSM gives a significant improvement for Cadmium but only modest gains on climate data.  
- Paper lacks an ablation study isolating the contribution of the delay parameter versus phase shifts.  
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity or scalability for the more complex MOSM model.  
- Well-written and organized with clear mathematical derivations and effective figures.  
- Needs a more explicit parameter-level comparison between MOSM and CSM, particularly how θ_ij models delays.  
- Novelty: introduces complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model inter-channel delays, with CSM as a restricted special case.  
- Good positioning relative to prior SM, CSM, and LMC work, but could better contrast with Matérn cross-covariances that also use complex spectra.  
- Modeling delays is practically significant (e.g., sensor networks), though broader practitioner benefits are not fully demonstrated.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, novel delay modeling, interpretable parameters, and strong synthetic validation.  
- Weaknesses: limited ablation, modest real-world improvements vs CSM, potential redundancy on highly correlated data, and missing scalability discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with a suggested ablation study and further scalability analysis for the final version.
