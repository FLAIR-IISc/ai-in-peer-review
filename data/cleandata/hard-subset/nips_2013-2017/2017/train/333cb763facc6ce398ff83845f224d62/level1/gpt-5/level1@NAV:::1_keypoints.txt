- Proposes MOSM kernel for MOGPs via complex cross-spectral factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω), obtaining real-valued cross-covariances by symmetry and inverse Fourier transform.
- Positive definiteness ensured by spectral factorization and real-valuedness by spectral symmetry; inverse transforms yield closed-form kernels with interpretable parameters (magnitude, frequency, phase, delay).
- Strictly generalizes SM-LMC and CSM, providing per-pair parameters, phase shifts, and explicit modeling of inter-channel delays.
- Training performed by standard marginal likelihood optimization.
- Experiments show clear gains on synthetic derivative/delay tasks and the cadmium prediction task; competitive results on climate imputation and Jura geostatistics.
- Technical construction and derivations are sound, with precise statements of Bochner/Cramér and careful derivation.
- Paper should discuss identifiability concerns (phase vs delay, per-channel means vs cross-pair means), initialization strategies, and effects of restricting each spectral component to rank-one.
- Scalability is O(N^3) for full GPs; sparse/variational approaches are deferred, leaving feasibility for large N or many outputs untested.
- Clarity is generally good, but notation could be streamlined and a schematic linking per-channel parameters to induced cross-pair parameters would aid intuition.
- Originality: substantive and well-motivated extension beyond prior spectral MOGP kernels and multivariate Matérn families via complex factorization and explicit delays.
- Significance: jointly modeling phase and delay is valuable for multi-sensor/time-series and spatio-temporal fusion, but broader benchmarks and ablation studies (e.g., remove delay/phase) are needed.
- Pros: principled PSD-guaranteed construction, strict generalization of prior kernels, closed-form kernels for GP inference, and demonstrated empirical benefits on key tasks.
- Cons: limited empirical breadth and modest gains on some real tasks, only MAE reported, lack of scalable inference, unresolved identifiability/initialization issues, and incomplete comparisons to multivariate Matérn and other non-spectral baselines.
