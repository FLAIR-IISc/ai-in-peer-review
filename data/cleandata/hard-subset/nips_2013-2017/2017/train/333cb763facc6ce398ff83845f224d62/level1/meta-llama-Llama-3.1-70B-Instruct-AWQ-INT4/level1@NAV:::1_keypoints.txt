- Summary: Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs using Cramér's theorem to build positive-definite complex-valued spectral densities with interpretable parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).

- Strength: More expressive than prior MOGP models, modeling delays and phase differences.

- Strength: Parameters are spectrally interpretable (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).

- Strength: Flexible enough to model both positive and negative correlations between outputs.

- Strength: Demonstrates competitive performance on real-world datasets.

- Weakness: Potentially higher computational cost due to inverse Fourier transforms.

- Weakness: Paper does not address scalability to large datasets.

- Weakness: No sparse implementation provided, limiting applicability to large-scale problems.

- Originality: Novel extension of the spectral mixture approach to MOGPs via Cramér's theorem.

- Originality: New design of complex-valued spectral densities for multi-output settings.

- Significance: Advances MOGP modeling by offering a more expressive and interpretable kernel.

- Significance: Has potential applications in climate modeling, geostatistics, and signal processing.

- Pro acceptance: Novel, expressive, interpretable kernel with empirical competitiveness.

- Con acceptance: Lacks discussion/solutions for scalability, sparsity, and computational cost.

- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestion to address scalability and sparse implementations in future work.
