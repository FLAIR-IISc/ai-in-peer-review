- Proposes MOSM kernel: extends spectral mixture to multi-output GPs using Cramér’s Theorem and complex-valued cross-spectral densities with inverse Fourier transform to obtain covariances.  
- Core capability: models delays and phase differences between channels with a fully parametric spectral interpretation.  
- Builds on prior MOGP work: relates to LMC, CSM, and spectral mixture literature, positioning contribution clearly.  
- Theoretical foundation: grounded in harmonic analysis and probability, guaranteeing positive definiteness via Cramér’s Theorem.  
- Expressiveness: more flexible than existing MOGP kernels, able to represent delays, phase shifts, and derivatives.  
- Parametric clarity: kernel parameters have clear spectral meanings aiding interpretability and regularization.  
- Presentation quality: well-structured, clear mathematical derivations and logical organization.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic and real-world experiments demonstrate ability to recover delays and competitive performance.  
- Comparison: relevant empirical comparisons to CONV, SM-LMC, and CSM highlight contributions.  
- Complexity: resulting kernel is parameter-heavy and relatively complex, risking practical difficulties for high-dimensional outputs.  
- Limited real-world gains: advantages over simpler methods (e.g., CSM) are not consistently significant, especially on climate data.  
- Computational concerns: likely higher training and prediction costs not explicitly analyzed or reported.  
- Rank-one assumption: initial spectral density uses Q=1; impact of larger Q on performance and cost is underexplored.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks thorough analysis of robustness to hyperparameter changes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, expressive, and experimentally supported contribution to spectral GP methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability questioned due to complexity, unclear computational cost, and limited clear real-world superiority.  
- Overall verdict: solid contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revision — scalability: add discussion and strategies for computational complexity and scalability.  
- Suggested revision — rank Q: evaluate impact of different Q values on performance and cost.  
- Suggested revision — sensitivity: include a thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggested revision — stronger validation: test on more challenging real-world datasets to better demonstrate advantages.  
- Suggested revision — limitations: explicitly state practical limitations of the approach.
