This paper proposes a novel approach to modeling multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) using spectral mixture kernels. The authors extend the spectral mixture concept to MOGPs by designing a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and then applying Cramér's theorem to construct the multi-output spectral mixture kernel (MOSM). The MOSM kernel is able to model delays among channels in addition to phase differences and provides full parametric interpretation of the relationship across channels.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of MOGPs and the limitations of existing approaches. The technical contributions of the paper are well-motivated and clearly explained. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work and position their contribution within the context of existing MOGP models.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The MOSM kernel is a novel and expressive model that can capture complex relationships between multiple outputs.
* The authors provide a clear and principled approach to designing the MOSM kernel using Cramér's theorem.
* The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the MOSM kernel on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its competitive performance compared to existing MOGP models.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes a high level of familiarity with Gaussian processes and spectral mixture kernels, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more intuition about the properties of the MOSM kernel and how it differs from existing MOGP models.
* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, including more datasets and comparisons to other MOGP models.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of MOGPs and provides a novel and expressive model for capturing complex relationships between multiple outputs.
