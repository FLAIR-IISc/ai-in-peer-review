- Proposes MOSM kernel: matrix-valued stationary covariance via complex cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s theorem.  
- Claims MOSM generalizes SM and CSM, models per-pair phase and delay, and guarantees positive-definiteness via spectral factorization.  
- Claims MOSM is more expressive than LMC/CONV-based multi-output models.  
- Claims MOSM improves or matches state-of-the-art on synthetic and two real datasets.  
- Theoretical construction is sound; PD argument S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) is standard and inverse transforms are given in closed form.  
- Synthetic experiment convincingly shows MOSM uniquely recovers derivative and delay relationships.  
- Real-data imputation (climate and Jura) yields competitive MAE but only modest gains and limited statistical validation.  
- Paper is generally clear with correct spectral GP terminology and related work coverage.  
- Notational overload around Q (rank vs number of components) needs clarification.  
- Training/optimization details (initialization, constraints, hyperparameter settings) are under-specified; public code would help reproducibility.  
- The complex cross-spectral parameterization and Cramér-based derivation are novel and subsume SM-LMC/CSM as special cases.  
- Parameter interpretability (explicit pairwise phase and delay) is a key strength.  
- Practical significance: principled modeling of delays/phases is useful for multi-sensor time series.  
- Empirical impact is modest; broader benchmarks, ablations, and large-scale/sparse training experiments are needed.  
- Pros: principled, interpretable spectral construction that recovers prior methods; strong synthetic evidence; closed-form kernels.  
- Cons: limited empirical breadth, modest real-data improvements, and no large-scale/sparse training treatment.  
- Cons: statistical testing and reporting are weak; reproducibility details are light.  
- Cons: potential overparameterization and identifiability issues not analyzed; initialization guidance lacking.  
- Suggestion: add ablations isolating delay vs phase, rank>1 vs 1, and varying number of components.  
- Suggestion: use paired statistical tests (Wilcoxon/paired t) across test points and report confidence intervals.  
- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity, identifiability, practical initialization, and consider a sparse MOSM baseline.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on more challenging multi-output tasks (EEG/MEG, motion capture, audio, spatiotemporal climate).  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
