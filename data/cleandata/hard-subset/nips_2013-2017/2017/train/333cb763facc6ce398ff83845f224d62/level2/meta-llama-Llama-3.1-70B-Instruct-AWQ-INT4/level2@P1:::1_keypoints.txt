- Proposes a novel multi-output Gaussian process by extending spectral mixture kernels to the multi-output case using parametric complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Uses Cramér's theorem to construct multivariate covariance functions that model delays and phase differences between channels.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and thorough background review (spectral mixture kernel and Cramér's theorem).  
- The complex-valued cross-spectral design provides physical parametric interpretation and closed-form covariance functions.  
- Experimental results are strong: outperforms existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets.  
- Synthetic experiment demonstrates ability to learn cross-covariances and autocovariances without prior delay/derivative information.  
- Strength: method is novel, well-motivated, and clearly explained.  
- Strength: thorough background coverage aiding technical grounding.  
- Weakness: assumes high mathematical sophistication, potentially inaccessible to non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks practical guidance on hyperparameter selection and real-world implementation details.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
