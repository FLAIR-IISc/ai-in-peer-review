- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs using a factorized complex cross-spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω), guaranteeing PSD by Cramér’s theorem.  
- Per-channel complex Gaussian spectral factors Ri(ω) provide interpretable parameters: frequency µi, lengthscale Σi, magnitude wi, delay θi, and phase φi.  
- Closed-form inverse Fourier transform yields time-domain cross-covariances as sums of damped cosines with pair-specific amplitudes, frequencies, phases, and delays.  
- Extends single-output SM and prior multi-output approaches (LMC/SM-LMC, CSM) by allowing pair-dependent phase differences and explicit channel delays.  
- Validated on synthetic 3-output (derivative/delayed), climate sensor imputation, and Jura heavy metal datasets, matching or outperforming CONV, SM-LMC, and CSM.  
- Theory is principled: PSD factorization and spectrum symmetrization ensure positive semidefiniteness and real-valued GPs.  
- Exposition flaw: conjugation is sometimes elided—Sij should be explicitly stated as conj(Ri)Rj to ensure Hermitian S(ω).  
- Identifiability issue: only relative phases/delays are identifiable; absolute φi and θi should be fixed or constrained (e.g., reference channel).  
- Optimization/initialization concern: many per-component/channel parameters require discussion of initialization strategies, tying/regularization, and mitigation of local optima.  
- Scalability concern: dense GP training (O(N^3)) limits scale; climate experiment uses 1000-subsample, so runtime/memory and sparse/inducing implementations should be discussed.  
- Experiments: synthetic example strongly supports modeling phase/delay; real-data gains modest for climate, clearer for Cadmium in Jura.  
- Experimental details missing: selection of Q, number of restarts, initialization schemes, sensitivity analyses, and ablation studies (removing delay/phase).  
- Clarity: generally well written, but requires notational consistency (conjugation, 1/2 factors) and a compact summary mapping per-channel to pairwise parameters.  
- Recommend adding an algorithmic description (training loop, parameter constraints) and complexity/runtime discussion.  
- Originality: meaningful and notable extension of SM/CSM to expressive multi-output setting with factorized complex cross-spectral design and interpretable delays/phases.  
- Significance: moderate-to-strong potential, especially for tasks with physical delay/phase relationships; broader impact depends on scalable implementation.  
- Strengths: principled multivariate spectral foundation, interpretable parameters, closed-form kernels that generalize prior methods, strong synthetic results, competitive real-data performance.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: clarify conjugation/exponent factors, address identifiability via reference/fixation or priors, provide initialization/ablation details, report computational costs, and explore parameter tying/regularization.  
- Questions for authors: how are µi/Σi initialized and are parameters tied; have they fixed a reference channel; sensitivity to Q; runtime/memory footprints; can MOSM handle improper/complex outputs without symmetrization?  
- Overall evaluation: solid, well-motivated extension with clear theory and interpretability; with clarifications and more experimental details, reviewer leans toward acceptance.
