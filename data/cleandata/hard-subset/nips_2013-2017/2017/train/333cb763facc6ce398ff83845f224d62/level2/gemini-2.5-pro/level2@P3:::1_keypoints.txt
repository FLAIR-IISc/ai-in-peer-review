- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs that models cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain.
- Uses Cramér's theorem and a rank-Q factor decomposition to guarantee a valid, positive-definite matrix-valued covariance.
- MOSM explicitly models magnitude, frequency, phase differences, and time delays between outputs, increasing expressiveness over prior kernels.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on a synthetic task designed to showcase delays and non-symmetric cross-covariances where simpler models fail.
- Competitive or superior performance reported on two real-world datasets (climate and geological).
- Theoretical development is sound and logically derived from spectral theory.
- Rank-Q factorization is an elegant, correct construction for a Hermitian positive-definite spectral density matrix.
- Experiments support claims, but real-data analysis could be strengthened by inspecting learned hyperparameters (delays and phases).
- Paper is very clearly written, well-organized, with helpful figures and an accessible derivation.
- Suggestion to discuss model complexity, number of hyperparameters, computational cost, and optimization/initialization strategies.
- Highly original: extends spectral kernel design to multi-output via a principled parameterization and subsumes CSM as a special case.
- Significant contribution: interpretable parameters and a general framework likely useful across domains; scalability via sparse implementations is a clear future direction.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled novel theory, increased expressiveness (delays/phases), clarity, and high potential impact.
- Argument against acceptance: mixed real-world gains suggest added complexity may not always be necessary; more analysis of learned parameters needed to justify practical benefits.
