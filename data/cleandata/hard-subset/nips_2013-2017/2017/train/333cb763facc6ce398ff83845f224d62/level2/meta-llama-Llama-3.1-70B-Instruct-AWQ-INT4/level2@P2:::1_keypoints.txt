- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel to model MOGPs, extending spectral mixtures to capture delays and phase differences between channels.  
- MOSM is a positive-definite stationary kernel with parameters interpretable from a spectral viewpoint.  
- Theoretical contribution: principled multivariate covariance design using Cramér’s Theorem and detailed derivation of MOSM properties and PD conditions.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with consistent notation and clear mathematical explanations.  
- Experimental evaluation on two real-world datasets is thorough; MOSM is competitive and sometimes outperforms existing MOGP models.  
- Strength: significant theoretical advance in MOGPs and a novel kernel formulation.  
- Strength: comprehensive introduction and detailed derivation aid technical rigor.  
- Weakness: assumes high background in Gaussian processes and spectral analysis, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Weakness: insufficient insight into how MOSM parameters map to underlying physical processes (interpretability).  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of MOSM’s limitations and directions for future work.  
- Originality: novel approach for MOGPs using spectral mixture kernels; MOSM is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: potential broad impact across applications (e.g., climate modeling, signal processing, geostatistics).  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing accessibility, interpretability, and limitations in the final version.
