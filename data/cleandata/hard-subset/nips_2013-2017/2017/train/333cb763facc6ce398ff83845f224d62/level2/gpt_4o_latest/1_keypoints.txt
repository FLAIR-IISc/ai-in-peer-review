- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending spectral mixture kernels to MOGPs via Cramér's Theorem.  
- Uses complex-valued cross-spectral densities to model delays and phase differences across outputs.  
- Kernel parameters are interpretable in spectral terms (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Validated on synthetic data and two real-world datasets, showing ability to capture delays, phase differences, and derivative relationships.  
- Significant and well-justified theoretical contribution providing a principled foundation for multivariate spectral kernels.  
- Provides sufficient mathematical detail and a TensorFlow/GPflow implementation for reproducibility.  
- Real-world performance gains over existing methods (e.g., CSM) are not consistently statistically significant.  
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity and runtime compared to simpler baselines.  
- Lacks a sparse approximation, limiting scalability to large datasets or high-dimensional inputs.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze computational cost, study impact of number of spectral components (Q), and clarify practical scenarios where MOSM’s extra expressiveness matters.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on computational analysis and clearer practical advantages.
