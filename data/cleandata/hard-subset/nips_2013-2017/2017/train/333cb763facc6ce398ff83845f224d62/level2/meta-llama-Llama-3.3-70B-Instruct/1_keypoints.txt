- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel by designing a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities and using Cramér's Theorem to build multivariate covariances.  
- MOSM models delays and phase differences between channels, offering more expressive and interpretable multi-output relationships.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with a thorough background and motivation.  
- Provides a detailed, rigorous derivation of the MOSM kernel and explains underlying assumptions and math.  
- Demonstrates MOSM effectiveness through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: novel and expressive kernel for MOGPs that captures complex inter-output relationships.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical derivation and clear mathematical exposition.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation including comparisons to existing MOGP models.  
- Weakness: lacks clear discussion of computational complexity, parameter count, and optimization cost.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of robustness and sensitivity to hyperparameters and data noise.  
- Weakness: missing detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art MOGP approaches (e.g., deep learning or other kernels).  
- Overall: significant contribution with promising results but needs further exploration of limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theory, and convincing empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient complexity analysis, limited robustness study, and incomplete SOTA comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the identified weaknesses.
