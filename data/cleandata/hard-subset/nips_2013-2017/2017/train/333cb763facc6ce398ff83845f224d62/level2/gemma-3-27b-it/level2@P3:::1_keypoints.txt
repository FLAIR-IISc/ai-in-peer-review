- Proposes MOSM kernel extending spectral mixture to multi-output GPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér’s Theorem to model delays and phase differences.  
- Builds on LMC and CSM, aiming for greater expressiveness and parametric interpretability.  
- Strong theoretical foundation via Bochner’s and Cramér’s theorems with careful mathematical derivation.  
- Clear explanation of spectral-domain parameters, improving interpretability.  
- Validated on synthetic data (recovers known characteristics) and two real-world datasets, showing competitive performance.  
- Well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately positioned within related work.  
- Advantages over CSM beyond delay/phase modeling are not thoroughly articulated.  
- Climate-data experiments do not show a clear, consistent advantage over existing methods.  
- Statistical significance testing is limited (only performed for Sotonmet), leaving robustness uncertain.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of parameter sensitivity and computational cost.  
- Notation inconsistencies (e.g., Σ_ij vs Σ_i) reduce clarity and should be fixed.  
- Complex-valued Gaussian process treatment needs more explanation for readers unfamiliar with the approach.  
- Choice of PSD rank Q=1 is justified but higher ranks are unexplored and their impact is unknown.  
- Could strengthen connections to signal-processing formulations like Generalized Cross-Covariance (GCC).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions—add rigorous statistical tests, discuss limitations and computational cost, and clarify advantages over CSM.  
- Optional revisions: explore higher PSD ranks and expand discussion of complex-valued GPs.
