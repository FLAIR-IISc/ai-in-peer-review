- Summary: Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs, deriving closed-form, interpretable multi-output covariances with per-pair magnitude, frequency, bandwidth, phase, and delay via a complex SE factorization and Cramér’s theorem.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions MOSM as a strict generalization of SM-LMC and CSM, building on single-output SM, LMC/CONV, and CSM literature.  
- Theoretical construction: Principled and PSD by design (S = R H R and spectral symmetrization), yielding closed-form inverse Fourier transforms with delay and phase parameters.  
- Minor theoretical issues: Fix conjugation—Sij(ω) should read conj(Ri(ω)) Rj(ω)—and rephrase the statement about αij sign since αij≥0 and negative correlation comes from the cosine term.  
- Identifiability: φij and θij can interact (cos(μij τ + φij + μij θij)); identifiability may degrade with broad bandwidths or small |μij| and warrants discussion or constraints.  
- Parameterization & complexity: Per-channel, per-component parameters {w, μ, Σ, θ, φ} give high expressivity but O(m Q (3n+2)) parameters, risking overfitting and sensitivity to initialization in small-data/many-output settings.  
- Empirics — strengths: Synthetic experiments convincingly show MOSM captures derivatives and delays; real-data results are competitive and significantly better on one task (Cadmium).  
- Empirics — weaknesses: Needs reporting of runtime/optimization stability, sensitivity to Q and initialization, uncertainty calibration (NLL/predictive intervals), and clarity that baselines were tuned comparably.  
- Clarity: Well written overall, but fix conjugation/αij issues, clarify Q as rank vs mixture components, correct minor typos, and summarize parameter counts/complexity concisely.  
- Originality: Novel and meaningful extension by introducing explicit inter-channel delays within a general spectral mixture MOGP framework.  
- Significance: Offers a flexible, interpretable kernel family for encoding realistic cross-channel structure; practical impact could grow with better initialization and scalable methods.  
- Pros: Principled closed-form construction, interpretable per-pair parameters, strict generalization of prior methods, strong synthetic demonstration, competitive real-data performance.  
- Cons: Minor notation/theory inaccuracies, potential over-parameterization, limited analysis of optimization and runtime, modest real-world gains overall, and no demonstrated scalable/sparse variant.  
- Suggested fixes: Correct conjugation and αij wording and add appendix on symmetrization preserving PSD; discuss identifiability and parameter-tying or priors.  
- Suggested practical additions: Provide initialization heuristics (periodogram, spectral peaks), sensitivity studies for Q and initialization, runtime/stability reporting, uncertainty metrics (NLL/intervals), and explore sparse/variational scalability.  
- Additional evaluation: Compare against more structured multi-task kernels and ensure comparable hyperparameter tuning across baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: Solid, principled, and interpretable contribution that merits acceptance after minor corrections and expanded practical guidance.
