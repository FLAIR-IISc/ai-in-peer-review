- Introduces MOSM kernel: a novel, expressive covariance function for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes (MOGPs).
- Builds on the Spectral Mixture kernel and leverages Cramér’s Theorem to derive cross-covariances from first principles.
- Defines a parametric family of complex-valued cross-spectral densities enabling modeling of phase and time delays with interpretable parameters.
- Theoretical derivation is elegant and provides a principled approach to designing expressive multi-output kernels.
- MOSM can jointly model delays and phase shifts, representing a notable advance over prior methods like CSM.
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM recovers complex cross-covariance structures (function, derivative, delayed version) where competitors fail.
- Clear parametric interpretation is a practical advantage for practitioners.
- Empirical evaluation on real-world datasets does not strongly showcase MOSM’s advanced capabilities; gains over CSM are often not statistically significant.
- This may be due to chosen datasets lacking the complex inter-channel delays MOSM targets.
- Recommendation: evaluate on datasets where inter-channel delays are known to be critical to strengthen claims.
- Paper should more explicitly discuss limitations, e.g., optimizing many hyperparameters and susceptibility to local optima.
- Overall: clear, novel, and significant contribution with sound theory that will likely inspire further research.
