- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel for MOGPs using a Cramér-theorem PSD factorization S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with complex Gaussian columns, yielding closed-form cross-covariances with phase and delay.  
- Enforces conjugate symmetry for real processes and recovers stationary, spectral-mixture cross-covariances that include explicit inter-channel delays.  
- Subsumes SM-LMC and CSM by allowing channel-specific frequency means, bandwidths, phases, and delays.  
- Experiments: synthetic latent/derivative/delayed task, climate sensor imputation, and heavy-metal prediction; MOSM matches or improves baselines, with clear gains on synthetic and Cadmium tasks.  
- Builds on single-output spectral mixtures and multi-output LMC/ICM/process-convolution work; extends cross-spectral mixture (CSM) by deriving from Cramér’s theorem and adding delays and per-channel spectra.  
- Strength: Principled, PSD-guaranteed construction via spectral-domain factorization.  
- Strength: High interpretability—parameters map to magnitudes, frequencies, bandwidths, phases, and delays.  
- Strength: More expressive than CSM/SM-LMC by modeling both phase and delay and allowing per-channel spectral characteristics.  
- Strength: Closed-form kernels enable efficient evaluation and gradient-based training.  
- Empirical strength: Synthetic experiment convincingly recovers derivative and delayed cross-covariances; competitive on real data.  
- Weakness: Scalability—training is O(N^3) and hyperparameter count scales with m·Q·n; limited guidance on choosing Q and regularization.  
- Weakness: Initialization and identifiability—jointly learning phases, delays, and frequencies is multimodal; no heuristics, priors, or constraints described.  
- Weakness: Rank limitation—each component is rank-1 in output space so cross-spectral rank ≤ Q; discussion of sufficiency is brief.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MAE; lacks uncertainty-quality metrics like predictive log-likelihood or calibration/coverage.  
- Weakness: Statistical testing choices (KS on MAE) are suboptimal for paired comparisons; paired tests are preferable.  
- Clarity: Well written with clear derivations; recommend adding parameter-count/complexity summary, explicit rank discussion, initialization/training tips, and fix wording about αij sign.  
- Quality: Construction appears technically sound and experiments are carefully implemented; more optimization detail would improve reproducibility.  
- Originality: Meaningful incremental advance—principled multivariate spectral-mixture construction enabling phase and delay via Cramér’s theorem.  
- Significance: Flexible, interpretable MOGP kernel potentially useful for multi-sensor time series; scalability would boost practical impact.  
- Essential suggestions: provide parameter/complexity guidance, document initialization strategies and priors/constraints, and report predictive log-likelihood and calibration.  
- Nice-to-have suggestions: ablations (φ=0, θ=0, shared μ/Σ), include multivariate Matérn and ICM baselines, inspect learned delays/phases for interpretability, and discuss rank implications.  
- Pros summary: Principled derivation, interpretability, greater expressiveness, closed-form kernels, strong synthetic results.  
- Cons summary: Scalability and training details under-specified, identifiability challenges, limited uncertainty evaluation, and rank-1-per-component/stationarity limitations.
