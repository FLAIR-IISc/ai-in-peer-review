- Proposes MOSM kernel: extends Spectral Mixture to multi-output GPs using complex-valued cross-spectral densities.  
- Leverages Cramér’s Theorem (multivariate Bochner) to construct valid multivariate covariance functions.  
- MOSM can model delays between channels and phase differences via complex spectral parameters.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear parametric interpretation of cross-covariances.  
- Addresses key limitation of previous MOGP methods that could not model inter-channel delays.  
- MOSM generalizes CSM by incorporating delay parameters (θij) and phase parameters (φij).  
- Synthetic experiments compellingly show MOSM learns derivatives and delayed signals better than latent-process methods (SM-LMC, CONV).  
- Outperforms competitors on real datasets (notably statistically significant improvement on Cadmium prediction).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and properly cites relevant prior work.  
- Mathematical presentation is precise and explains the spectral-to-covariance connection clearly.  
- Lacks detailed discussion linking estimated delay/phase parameters to empirical performance in real-world examples.  
- Comparison with CSM is limited; needs a more nuanced analysis of when MOSM’s extra flexibility yields gains.  
- Computational complexity and scalability considerations are not discussed (implementation noted in GPflow).  
- Technically sound, acknowledges limitations (e.g., high correlation in climate data causing similar performance), and merits acceptance with minor revisions.
