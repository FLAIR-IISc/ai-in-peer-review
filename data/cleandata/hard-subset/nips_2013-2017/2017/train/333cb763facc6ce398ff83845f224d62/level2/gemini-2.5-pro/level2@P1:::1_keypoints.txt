Introduces MOSM kernel: extends the single-output Spectral Mixture to multi-output GPs by designing a parametric spectral-domain model using Cramér’s theorem.

Constructs spectral density S(ω)=R(ω)H R(ω) with complex-valued squared-exponential elements, guaranteeing positive-definiteness and allowing closed-form inverse Fourier transform.

MOSM hyperparameters map to magnitudes, frequencies, phase shifts, and time delays between outputs, yielding increased interpretability.

MOSM generalizes prior work and subsumes the Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) kernel as a special case.

Synthetic experiment demonstrates MOSM can recover non-trivial cross-covariances (e.g., function vs. derivative vs. delayed signal), highlighting its strengths.

Theoretical foundation is principled and elegant: using Cramér’s theorem and the factor decomposition effectively addresses MOGP kernel design.

MOSM is significantly more expressive than many existing MOGP kernels, notably by including delay parameters θij missing in related models.

High flexibility implies many hyperparameters (scaling with outputs m and components Q), making optimization challenging and prone to poor local minima; practical training details (initialization, optimizer, sensitivity) are needed.

Possible identifiability issues between phase (φij) and delay (θij) since both affect oscillatory behavior and may be confounded with limited data.

On the Jura dataset a simpler SM-LMC outperformed MOSM; the paper’s explanation is speculative and would benefit from deeper analysis (e.g., whether optimization difficulties caused MOSM’s poorer performance).

Minor clarity issue: the symmetrization step from complex spectral density to the real-valued kernel is brief—explicitly linking this to the cosine term would improve readability.

Overall assessment: original, clearly presented, theoretically sound, expressive and interpretable contribution to MOGPs; recommended for acceptance.
