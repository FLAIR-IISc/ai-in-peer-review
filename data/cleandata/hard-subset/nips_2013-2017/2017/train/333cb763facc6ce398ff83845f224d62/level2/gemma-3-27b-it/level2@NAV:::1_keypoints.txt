- Proposes Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending spectral mixture idea to MOGPs using Cramér’s Theorem to build cross-spectral densities.  
- Core idea of constructing kernels from spectral densities is principled and ensures positive-definiteness.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, clearly presented using Bochner’s and Cramér’s Theorems.  
- MOSM enables interpretable parameters controlling frequency, magnitude, phase, and inter-channel delays.  
- Experiments on synthetic data convincingly show MOSM captures known relationships (derivatives, delays).  
- Real-world comparisons (climate, metal concentrations) show MOSM is often competitive but not consistently superior.  
- Statistical significance testing on the climate dataset strengthens the empirical claims.  
- Paper lacks analysis of computational complexity, especially for high-dimensional output spaces.  
- Limited discussion of connections to prior work on complex-valued Gaussian Processes.  
- Insufficient treatment of hyperparameter sensitivity and guidance for choosing number of spectral components Q.  
- Recommendation: novel, well-executed contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions addressing complexity, hyperparameter issues, and scalability.
