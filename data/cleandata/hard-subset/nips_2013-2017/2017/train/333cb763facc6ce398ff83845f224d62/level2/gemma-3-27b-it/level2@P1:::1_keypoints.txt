- Proposal: Introduces MOSM, extending spectral mixture kernels to multi-output GPs by defining cross-spectral densities in the Fourier domain and using Cramér’s theorem for PD guarantees.  
- Expressiveness: Models delays and phase differences between channels with interpretable spectral parameters (frequency, magnitude, phase, delay).  
- Theoretical soundness: Cramér’s theorem provides a rigorous foundation ensuring positive definiteness.  
- Positioning: Builds clearly on scalar SM and existing MOGP work, correctly identifying limitations of LMC and CSM.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and explains motivation and principles effectively.  
- Synthetic results: Demonstrates ability to learn derivatives and delayed signals in controlled experiments.  
- Experimental weakness: Real-world gains are limited/inconclusive—climate data comparable to others and statistical significance shown for only one sensor.  
- Comparison to CSM: Presentation is somewhat dismissive; need stronger emphasis and empirical illustration of MOSM’s advantages despite CSM being a special case.  
- Mathematical presentation: Derivations are dense; more intuitive explanations needed—especially for complex-valued spectral densities and their link to delays/phase.  
- Unsubstantiated claim: Assertion that copper dataset non-Gaussianity harms SM-LMC performance lacks sufficient support.  
- Visualization suggestion: Include learned kernel or cross-spectral density visualizations to better interpret model behavior.  
- Writing and reproducibility: Writing is good with minor awkward phrases; TensorFlow/GPflow implementation details and comprehensive references aid reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Theoretical novelty and interpretability make the paper a valuable contribution despite limited empirical gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experimental discussion, and add visualizations.
