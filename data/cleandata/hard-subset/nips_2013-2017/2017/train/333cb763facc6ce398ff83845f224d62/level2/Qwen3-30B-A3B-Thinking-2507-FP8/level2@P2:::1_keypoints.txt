- Proposes a Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel extending SM to multi-output GPs via a complex-valued cross-spectral density to model phase differences and delays.  
- The construction is theoretically grounded using Cramér’s Theorem and a factor decomposition to ensure positive definiteness.  
- Demonstrates ability to learn derivatives and delayed signals in a synthetic experiment without prior relationship knowledge.  
- Shows competitive real-world performance on climate and heavy-metal datasets, with statistically significant improvement for Cadmium prediction.  
- Strength: provides a clear parametric, spectral-density-based approach to MOGP kernel construction.  
- Strength: novel capability to explicitly model delays between channels, beyond what CSM handled.  
- Strength: synthetic validation effectively supports the claimed capabilities.  
- Weakness: novelty claim is weak—delay parameter may be a natural extension of phase shifts and the paper doesn’t clearly argue why this is a significant generalization.  
- Weakness: climate dataset results are underwhelming and lack analysis of when MOSM outperforms CSM, especially in less-correlated settings.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability are not discussed; per-pair matrix operations may be costly compared to methods like LMC.  
- Weakness: limited statistical significance testing (KS tests used for climate but not for heavy-metal data), making some improvements hard to assess.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to recent MOGP/scalability work (e.g., Variational Fourier Features, NIPS 2016).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty, analyze when delays help, and discuss computational and comparative aspects.
