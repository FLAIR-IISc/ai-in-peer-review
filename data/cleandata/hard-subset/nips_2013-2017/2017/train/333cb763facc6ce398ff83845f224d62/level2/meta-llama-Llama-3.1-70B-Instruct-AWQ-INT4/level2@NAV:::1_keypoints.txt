- Introduces the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel to model complex relationships in multi-output Gaussian Processes.
- Extends spectral mixture concept using complex-valued cross-spectral densities and Cramér's theorem to build valid multivariate covariance functions.
- Paper is well-structured with clear problem setup, background, and related work.
- Technical contribution is clearly explained and theoretically grounded.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering synthetic and two real-world datasets.
- Strength — Novelty: MOSM is an expressive and flexible new approach for multi-output GPs.
- Strength — Theoretical foundations: Cramér's theorem ensures positive definiteness of the covariance functions.
- Strength — Empirical: Results demonstrate MOSM's effectiveness on multiple datasets.
- Weakness — Computational complexity: MOSM can be expensive, especially on large datasets.
- Weakness — Overfitting risk: Paper does not address potential overfitting from increased model flexibility.
- Suggestion — Develop a sparse implementation to reduce computational cost.
- Suggestion — Explore regularization (e.g., L1/L2) to mitigate overfitting.
- Suggestion — Include comparisons to other GP variants (e.g., deep GPs) and non-parametric approaches.
- Conclusion: MOSM is a valuable contribution with potential for wide use after addressing complexity and regularization.
- Rating/recommendation: 8/10, accept with minor revisions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, clear technical exposition, thorough experiments, potential significant impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks discussion on overfitting and has practical computational limitations.
