The paper "Spectral Mixture Kernels for Multi-Output Gaussian Processes" proposes a novel approach to model multi-output Gaussian processes (MOGPs) using spectral mixture kernels. The authors extend the spectral mixture concept to MOGPs, allowing for the modeling of delays and phase differences between channels. The proposed Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel is a positive-definite stationary kernel that provides a clear interpretation of its parameters from a spectral viewpoint.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough introduction to the topic, including a review of previous work on MOGPs. The theoretical contribution of the paper is significant, as it provides a principled approach to design multivariate covariance functions using Cramér's Theorem. The authors also provide a detailed derivation of the MOSM kernel, including its properties and the conditions under which it is positive-definite.

The experimental evaluation of the MOSM kernel is also thorough, with the authors comparing its performance to existing MOGP models on two real-world datasets. The results show that the MOSM kernel performs competitively with other models, and in some cases, it outperforms them.
