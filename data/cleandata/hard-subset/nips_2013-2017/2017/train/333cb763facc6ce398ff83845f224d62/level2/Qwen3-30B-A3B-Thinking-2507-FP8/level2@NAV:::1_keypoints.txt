- Proposes the Multi-Output Spectral Mixture (MOSM) kernel, extending Spectral Mixture to multi-output GPs via Cramér's Theorem.
- Claims MOSM models cross-covariances with interpretable parameters for delays, phase shifts, and spectral content across channels.
- Theoretical grounding is strong: MOSM is clearly derived from Cramér's Theorem and shown to be positive definite.
- MOSM generalizes previous methods and subsumes Cross-Spectral Mixture (CSM) as a special case.
- Synthetic experiments show MOSM successfully recovers derivative and delayed relationships where prior methods fail.
- Real-world experiments (climate data, heavy metal concentrations) demonstrate competitive performance and statistically significant improvement for Cadmium prediction.
- Strengths: clear theory, interpretable spectral parameters, and well-designed experimental validation.
- Weakness: unclear guidance on when to prefer MOSM over simpler alternatives like CSM, especially when performance is comparable.
- Questions remain about practical trade-offs between MOSM’s increased complexity and performance gains.
- Paper would benefit from more discussion of implementation considerations and scenarios with clear MOSM advantages.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, advances MOGP kernels, and recommended for acceptance.
