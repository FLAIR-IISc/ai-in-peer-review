This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between various data modalities, which is particularly useful for tasks like visual question answering (VQA). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The introduction effectively sets the stage for the problem and the proposed solution. The related work section is thorough and provides a good overview of existing attention mechanisms.

The proposed attention mechanism is based on a probabilistic model that uses high-order potentials to capture correlations between different data modalities. The authors provide a detailed explanation of how they compute the unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials, as well as how they use these potentials to obtain marginal probabilities. The use of mean-field inference to obtain marginal probabilities is a nice touch, as it allows the authors to model complex interactions between different modalities.

The experimental results are impressive, with the authors achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset. The comparison to existing methods, such as Lu et al. [15] and Fukui et al. [7], is also useful in demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach. While they mention that the number of parameters in their model is relatively small compared to existing methods, they do not provide a detailed breakdown of the computational cost of their approach.

Another potential weakness is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the interpretability of their approach. While they provide some visualizations of the attention weights, they do not provide a detailed analysis of what these visualizations mean or how they can be used to understand the behavior of the model.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of visual question answering. The proposed attention mechanism is novel and effective, and the experimental results are impressive.
