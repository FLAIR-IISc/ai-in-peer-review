**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that learns correlations between various data modalities. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the task of visual question answering (VQA), achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset. The proposed attention mechanism is based on a probabilistic model that computes "potentials" for each modality and uses mean-field inference to obtain marginal probabilities. The authors also introduce a tensor sketch algorithm to reduce the dimensionality of high-order correlation tensors, enabling efficient decision making.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors provide a thorough review of related work on attention mechanisms for image and textual data. They highlight the limitations of existing approaches, such as the lack of explicit consideration of multiple modalities and the use of hand-crafted features. The proposed high-order attention mechanism addresses these limitations by learning correlations between modalities and using a probabilistic model to compute attention probabilities.

**Weaknesses**
