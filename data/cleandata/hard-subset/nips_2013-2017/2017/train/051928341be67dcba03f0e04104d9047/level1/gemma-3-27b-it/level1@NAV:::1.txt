## Review of "High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering"

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism for multi-modal data, specifically focusing on Visual Question Answering (VQA). The core idea is to move beyond pairwise interactions and model high-order correlations between modalities (image, question, answer) using potentials and mean-field inference. The authors claim this allows for more effective attention focusing, leading to state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset. 

The work builds upon the growing body of research employing attention mechanisms in multi-modal tasks, citing seminal works like Bahdanau et al. (2014) for textual attention and Lu et al. (2016) & Fukui et al. (2016) for joint image-text attention.  It distinguishes itself by explicitly modeling higher-order interactions, addressing what the authors identify as a limitation of previous approaches that often treat modalities in an entangled or task-specific manner. The proposed model employs unary, pairwise and ternary potentials, solved using mean field inference, which is a probabilistic way to simplify the problem and obtain marginal probabilities. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The exploration of high-order attention, beyond simple pairwise interactions, is a significant contribution. Modeling interactions between *k* modalities is a natural extension and potentially powerful for complex tasks like VQA.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The ablation studies (comparing MCT and 2-layer MCB) are valuable in showing the impact of different design choices.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper provides a relatively clear explanation of the mathematical formulation, including the use of potentials and mean-field inference, and provides useful illustrations (Figs. 3, 4).
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a comprehensive review of relevant attention mechanisms for both image and text, and accurately position their work relative to existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The mathematical formulation, while clear, is complex. The benefits of this added complexity (over simpler pairwise attention) aren’t always intuitively explained. The connection to probabilistic modeling, while mentioned, could be further emphasized to justify the chosen approach.
* **Implementation Details:** The paper briefly mentions hyperparameter settings. Providing a more detailed account of the training process, including learning rate schedules and regularization techniques, would improve reproducibility.
* **Qualitative Analysis:**  While the visualization of attention maps (Figs. 5, 6, 7) is helpful, the analysis is somewhat limited. A more in-depth discussion of failure cases (like the umbrella example in Fig. 8), and the reasons for those failures, would be valuable.
* **Limited Ablation:** More ablation studies could be performed to better understand the contribution of each component (e.g., impact of different order potentials, different embedding dimensions).



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel approach to attention modeling.
* State-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark.
* Well-written and clearly explained.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Mathematical complexity might hinder adoption.
* Limited in-depth analysis of failure cases.




**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to attention modeling. While the complexity of the method may be a barrier to entry, the strong empirical results and clear presentation make it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
