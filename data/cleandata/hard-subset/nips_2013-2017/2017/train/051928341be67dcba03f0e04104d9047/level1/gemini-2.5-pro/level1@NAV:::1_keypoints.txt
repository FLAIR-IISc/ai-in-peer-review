- Proposes a novel multi-modal attention framed as a probabilistic model with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to model high-order correlations.  
- Combines potentials via a learned linear model interpreted as a single mean-field inference iteration.  
- Demonstrates joint attention over image, question, and candidate answers, achieving state-of-the-art results on VQA.  
- Builds on prior co-attention and compact bilinear pooling work but offers a more general, principled framework for >2 modalities.  
- Strength: original and significant formulation that principledly fuses multiple modalities and shows benefit from adding a third modality.  
- Strength: technically sound with rigorous experiments, useful ablations (2- vs 3-modality, decision units), and informative qualitative visualizations.  
- Weakness: some clarity issues—marginalization details and the learned weights (`w`) are not fully explicit.  
- Weakness: brief theoretical justification for treating the combination as a single mean-field iteration.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—k-th order correlation tensor grows exponentially and practical costs for k>3 are not addressed.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated, empirically effective approach that advances SOTA and could inspire other multi-modal work.  
- Against acceptance: light theoretical justification for choices and unexamined practical scalability beyond three modalities.  
- Overall: empirical and conceptual strengths outweigh the minor weaknesses; considered a solid contribution.
