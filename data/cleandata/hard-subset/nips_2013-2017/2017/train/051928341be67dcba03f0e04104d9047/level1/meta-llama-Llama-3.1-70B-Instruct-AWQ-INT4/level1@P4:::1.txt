**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between various data modalities, specifically designed for Visual Question Answering (VQA) tasks. The authors introduce a probabilistic model that captures unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to compute attention probabilities. The proposed model is evaluated on the VQA dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance. The paper also provides a qualitative evaluation of the attention mechanism, demonstrating its effectiveness in focusing on relevant regions of the image and question.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on attention mechanisms, particularly in the context of VQA. The authors mention several related papers, including [15], [25], and [7], which also propose attention mechanisms for VQA. However, the proposed model differs from these approaches in its ability to capture high-order correlations between multiple modalities. The authors also mention recent work on multimodal compact bilinear pooling (MCB) [7] and its extension to three modalities, Multimodal Compact Trilinear Pooling (MCT).

**Strengths**

1. **Novel attention mechanism**: The proposed model introduces a new way of computing attention probabilities by capturing high-order correlations between multiple modalities.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in VQA tasks.
3. **Qualitative evaluation**: The paper provides a qualitative evaluation of the attention mechanism, showing its ability to focus on relevant regions of the image and question.
4. **Simple and efficient**: The proposed model is relatively simple and efficient, with only 40 million parameters, compared to other models with over 70 million parameters.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the model on the VQA dataset, which may not be representative of other VQA tasks or datasets.
2. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper does not provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed model, which may limit its understanding and generalizability.
3. **Dependence on hyperparameters**: The model's performance may depend on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the embedding dimension and the number of LSTM layers.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that captures high-order correlations between multiple modalities, which is a new contribution to the field of VQA. The model's ability to focus on relevant regions of the image and question, as demonstrated in the qualitative evaluation, is also a significant contribution.

**Significance**

The paper's contribution is significant, as it proposes a new attention mechanism that achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset. The model's ability to capture high-order correlations between multiple modalities may have implications for other VQA tasks and datasets. Additionally, the paper's qualitative evaluation provides insights into the model's behavior and may inspire further research in this area.
