- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations across modalities, aimed at VQA tasks.
- Uses a probabilistic model with unary, pairwise, and ternary high-order potentials to capture multimodal interactions.
- Employs mean-field inference to obtain marginal probabilities and model complex modality interactions.
- Provides clear, well-written exposition with effective figures and tables.
- Related work section is thorough and situates the method among existing attention mechanisms.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, outperforming methods like Lu et al. and Fukui et al.
- Experimental comparisons to prior methods are useful and convincing.
- Claims relatively small parameter count compared to existing methods.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and runtime cost.
- Limited analysis of interpretability; attention visualizations are shown but not deeply analyzed.
- Recommends acceptance contingent on adding detailed computational complexity and interpretability analyses in the final version.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
