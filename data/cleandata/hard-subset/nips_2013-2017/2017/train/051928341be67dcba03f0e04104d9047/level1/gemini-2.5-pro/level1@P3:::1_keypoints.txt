- Proposes a novel multimodal attention framed as a probabilistic model using unary, pairwise, and third-order potentials to compute attention across modalities.  
- Combines potentials via a weighted linear mix and softmax (described as a single mean-field iteration) to produce modality-specific attention.  
- Applies the framework to VQA, modeling interactions between image regions, question words, and candidate answers, then fuses attended features with MCB/Trilinear pooling.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA, with a smaller parameter count and ablations showing ternary interactions outperform binary ones.  
- Principled and novel formulation that offers a structured way to model complex inter-modal dependencies beyond ad-hoc co-attention.  
- Framework is theoretically extensible to k-th order modalities, improving generalizability to more than two modalities.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective diagrams illustrating potential computations.  
- Claiming mean-field inference is misleading: the method is a single weighted combination plus softmax, not full iterative mean-field, and weights act like learned gating.  
- Third-order potential computation is computationally and memory expensive (O(nv * nq * na * d)), with no discussion of scalability or practical implications.  
- Using the answer modality tailors the model to classification/multiple-choice settings and may not extend to generative/open-ended VQA without clarification.  
- Correlation functions use simple inner products/three-way extensions; the paper lacks exploration of richer interaction functions (e.g., MLPs or kernel-based methods).  
- Qualitative visualizations are insightful but answer-attention plots are hard to interpret; some terminology (e.g., calling marginals “convolution”) is imprecise.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel contribution with compelling empirical results despite some methodological and practical concerns — recommend acceptance.
