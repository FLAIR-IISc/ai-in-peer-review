- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for multi-modal data, modeling ternary (and higher) interactions between image, question, and answer.  
- Uses unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials solved via mean-field inference to obtain attention marginals.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, supported by empirical results.  
- Ablation studies (e.g., MCT vs 2-layer MCB) demonstrate the impact of design choices.  
- Clear mathematical formulation and useful illustrations explaining the approach.  
- Thorough and well-positioned related work survey on attention mechanisms.  
- Added complexity from high-order modeling may be hard to intuitively justify and could hinder adoption.  
- Insufficient implementation details (hyperparameters, learning rate schedules, regularization) reduce reproducibility.  
- Qualitative analysis (attention visualizations) is helpful but lacks deeper failure-case analysis (e.g., umbrella example).  
- Limited ablation on individual components (order of potentials, embedding dimensions) leaves some contributions unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel attention modeling, strong benchmark results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: mathematical complexity and limited failure-case analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommended for acceptance despite complexity concerns.
