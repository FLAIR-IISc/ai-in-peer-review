- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA modeling correlations between image, question, and answer using potentials and mean-field inference.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Builds on multimodal attention literature and positions itself against joint/stacked/bilinear attention methods.  
- Novelty: explicitly models high-order (beyond pairwise) correlations via potentials and mean-field inference.  
- Generality: mechanism is presented as applicable to multiple modalities, not just two-input attention.  
- Theoretical grounding: solid probabilistic formulation connecting attention to mean-field inference.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and technical concepts (incl. Fig. 3) are clearly explained.  
- Uses tensor sketching to mitigate cost, but computational complexity remains a major concern for many modalities.  
- Lacks detailed runtime/complexity analysis (only reports 40 hours of training).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is unclear; tuning details for many hyperparameters are insufficient.  
- Ablation studies are limited; need component-wise evaluation (unary, pairwise, ternary) to justify complexity.  
- Failure-case analysis is weak (single example); needs broader analysis of common failure modes.  
- Comparisons omit some stronger, more recent VQA baselines (e.g., transformer-based or advanced fusion models).  
- Uses a lower embedding dimension than other SOTA models, which may confound the reported improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add computational analysis, comprehensive ablations, hyperparameter details, and stronger baseline comparisons.
