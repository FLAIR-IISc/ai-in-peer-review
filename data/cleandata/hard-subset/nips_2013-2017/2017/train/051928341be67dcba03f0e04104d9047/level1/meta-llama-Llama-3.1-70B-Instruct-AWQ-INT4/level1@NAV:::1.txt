This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between various data modalities, specifically designed for visual question answering (VQA) tasks. The authors introduce a probabilistic model that captures high-order attention correlations, which they found to improve performance significantly.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough review of related work in the field of attention mechanisms. The authors clearly explain their proposed approach, including the high-order attention models, data embedding, and decision making components. The experimental setup and results are also well-presented, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed attention mechanism is novel and well-motivated, addressing the limitations of existing approaches.
* The paper provides a thorough review of related work, demonstrating a strong understanding of the field.
* The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach, particularly in comparison to existing methods.
* While the experimental results are impressive, it would be useful to see more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
* Some of the figures and tables could be improved for clarity and readability.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated attention mechanism that addresses the limitations of existing approaches.
* The experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
* The paper is well-written and provides a thorough review of related work.

Arguments against acceptance:
