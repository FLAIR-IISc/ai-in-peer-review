- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary interactions among image, question, and answer using probabilistic potentials and mean-field inference.  
- Extends attention to arbitrary numbers of modalities, offering a generalizable framework beyond VQA.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, validating effectiveness.  
- Builds on prior multimodal attention work but explicitly models higher-order correlations missing in earlier approaches.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and shows awareness of existing literature.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, using probabilistic potentials and clear descriptions of potential types.  
- Provides detailed explanations of technical components, including data embedding and decision modules.  
- Model complexity is high, with multiple components (unary/pairwise/ternary potentials, tensor sketching) that complicate implementation and debugging.  
- Performance appears sensitive to hyperparameters; section 4.3 indicates tuning impact but lacks thorough analysis.  
- Ablation study is limited, insufficiently isolating contributions of ternary vs. pairwise potentials and other components.  
- Qualitative analysis is minimal; more examples and clearer attention visualizations are needed.  
- Failure case analysis is shallow and requires deeper investigation to understand model limitations.  
- Some implementation details are missing (e.g., exact pre-trained CNN used), hindering reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: model complexity, limited ablation and qualitative analyses, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worth accepting with minor revisions to expand ablations and implementation details.
