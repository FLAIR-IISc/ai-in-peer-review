- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA modeling multimodal correlations via potentials and mean-field inference, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Positions contribution within existing attention and multimodal literature and highlights novelty in explicit high-order correlation modeling.
- Strength: Novel probabilistic framework for high-order correlations that is more expressive than pairwise interactions.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance (SOTA) on the VQA dataset.
- Strength: General framework extensible to k modalities.
- Strength: Clear exposition and helpful figures explaining the method.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation with quantitative comparisons, qualitative visualizations, and some architectural analyses.
- Weakness: Introduces significant complexity that may be hard to grasp and may not be fully justified by gains.
- Weakness: Likely sensitive to hyperparameters; paper lacks details on hyperparameter search and tuning.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of which higher-order interactions (unary/pairwise/ternary) drive performance gains.
- Weakness: Failure case analysis is shallow and lacks investigation into causes.
- Weakness: Computational cost of high-order interactions is noted but not rigorously compared to alternatives.
- Weakness: Introduction is verbose and somewhat repetitive.
- For acceptance: Novel idea with strong results, generality, and clear presentation warrant publication.
- Against acceptance: Increased complexity, insufficient analysis of higher-order contributions, and incomplete computational-cost discussion argue for caution.
- Overall: Strong paper recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions—specifically more ablation on attention orders and a detailed computational-cost discussion.
