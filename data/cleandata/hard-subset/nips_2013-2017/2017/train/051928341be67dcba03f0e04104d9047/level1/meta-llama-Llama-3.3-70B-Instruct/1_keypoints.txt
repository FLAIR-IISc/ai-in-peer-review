- Proposes a novel probabilistic attention mechanism that learns unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture high-order correlations between data modalities.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on Visual Question Answering (VQA), achieving state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple model.  
- Paper includes a thorough review of related attention work and clearly highlights limitations of existing approaches.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with concise explanations of the method.  
- Experimental results are thorough with detailed performance analysis.  
- Strength: Generally applicable attention mechanism that models high-order cross-modal correlations.  
- Strength: Comprehensive literature review situating the contribution relative to prior attention mechanisms.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results on VQA, achieving SOTA with a simple model.  
- Weakness: Assumes data modalities are independent, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and practical scalability.  
- Weakness: Evaluation focused primarily on VQA, leaving generalization to other tasks/datasets unclear.  
- Pro-accept: Novel, effective mechanism with strong empirical validation and solid writing.  
- Con-accept: Independence assumption, missing complexity analysis, and limited task generalization raise concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address modality independence and computational complexity.
