- Proposes high-order attention modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture multi-way correlations for VQA.  
- Frames attention as a probabilistic model and uses mean-field inference to compute attention probabilities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance with ~40M parameters, fewer than competing models (70+M).  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with effective visualizations (Figs. 3–8).  
- Strength: technically sound approach with thorough experimental validation and consistent improvements.  
- Strength: genuinely novel explicit modeling of three-way correlations and proper contextualization against prior NIPS work.  
- Strength: framework has strong significance and could generalize to other multimodal tasks.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical grounding—minimal formal analysis of why high-order correlations improve performance.  
- Weakness: modest empirical gains over prior SOTA (64.57% vs. 63.82% MCT), leaving necessity vs. complexity unclear.  
- Weakness: failure-case analysis is good but could be expanded.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed contribution to multimodal learning; recommendation to accept.
