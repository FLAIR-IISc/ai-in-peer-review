- Proposes a multi-modal attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary learned potentials across image, question, and answer modalities.
- Computes marginal attentions via a softmax over a learned linear combination of marginalized potentials (framed as a single mean-field iteration).
- Fuses attended modality vectors for decision-making using compact bilinear/trilinear pooling (MCB/MCT) to approximate outer products.
- Novelty: explicitly formulates attention as a sum of learned higher-order potentials and extends co-attention to a three-way (V,Q,A) term.
- Empirically shows improved state-of-the-art performance on VQA multiple-choice with qualitative visualizations demonstrating sharper, conditioned attention.
- Modules are clearly defined and implementable (unary as 1×1 convs, pairwise via similarity matrices, ternary via triple inner products).
- The “mean-field”/probabilistic framing is informal and lacks a precise joint energy or iterative inference derivation.
- Ablation and empirical reporting are solid (training details, hyperparameters, parameter counts), but deeper ablations are missing.
- Completeness: code referenced and system complete, but evaluation is restricted to VQA multiple-choice only.
- Clarity: generally readable with helpful explanations and visualizations, but has notational inconsistencies, typos, and ambiguous tensor-shape descriptions.
- Originality: moderate—builds on established pairwise co-attention and bilinear pooling but adds a clean decomposition including ternary correlations.
- Significance: meaningful improvements within VQA MC and fewer parameters than some baselines, but broader applicability is unproven.
- Pros: clear modular attention formulation, empirical gains with fewer parameters, strong qualitative analyses, practical and reproducible decision fusion.
- Cons: heuristic probabilistic framing, limited ablations isolating unary/pairwise/ternary contributions, restricted evaluation, incomplete comparisons to some bilinear variants, and untested scalability to >3 modalities.
- Suggestion: provide a formal energy-based model or reframe probabilistic claims, or present iterative mean-field updates.
- Suggestion: run ablations for unary only, +pairwise, +ternary and analyze learned mixture weights α/β/γ.
- Suggestion: include open-ended VQA or other multimodal benchmarks to demonstrate generality.
- Suggestion: expand quantitative comparisons to contemporaneous bilinear/low-rank methods (e.g., MLB/MFB) under matched settings.
- Suggestion: fix notation/typos and explicitly state tensor dimensions and marginalization mechanics for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: empirically strong and practically useful for VQA MC, moderate theoretical novelty, leans toward acceptance if clarity and ablation concerns are addressed.
