- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across visual, textual, and answer modalities.  
- Uses a probabilistic mean-field framework to combine high-order potentials into attention distributions and compute attended features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance (64.57% test-dev with 2-layer MCB) and shows ablation evidence that third-order attention improves over 2-modality models.  
- Mathematical formulation and architecture are clearly presented with helpful visualizations (Fig. 3).  
- Qualitative results (Figs. 5–7) demonstrate improved attention focus and provide convincing comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Claim of novelty is overstated; insufficient distinction from Kim et al. (2017) "Structured Attention Networks," which also models high-order interactions.  
- Theoretical justification for why high-order attention is necessary is weak; unclear if gains stem from high-order modeling or greater model expressivity.  
- Computational complexity concerns (ternary O(n^3) operations) are not addressed, raising scalability questions for larger modalities or higher orders.  
- Limited comparison with other relevant recent work (e.g., dual attention networks by Nam et al., 2016).  
- Failure analysis is minimal—only a single example is shown and common failure modes or remedies are not discussed.  
- Contribution is valuable and generally applicable to multiple modalities, but novelty is incremental relative to recent structured-attention work.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, and empirically strong; recommend accept with minor revisions to better contextualize related work and strengthen theoretical/complexity discussion.
