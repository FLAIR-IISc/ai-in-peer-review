

This paper proposes "high-order attention" mechanisms that model correlations between multiple data modalities (image, question, answer) for visual question answering. The authors frame attention as a probabilistic model with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials, extending beyond typical pairwise attention approaches (Lu et al., NIPS 2016; Fukui et al., NIPS 2016). Their method uses mean-field inference to compute attention probabilities and demonstrates state-of-the-art VQA performance with 40 million parameters, significantly fewer than competing approaches (70+ million).

Strengths: The paper excels in clarity and organization, with well-structured explanations and helpful visualizations (Figs. 3-8) that effectively illustrate the high-order attention mechanism. The technical approach is sound, with thorough experimental validation showing consistent improvements over baselines. The method is genuinely novel in its explicit modeling of three-way correlations (beyond pairwise), and the authors properly contextualize their work against relevant NIPS papers. The significance is strong as the framework could generalize to other multimodal tasks beyond VQA.

Weaknesses: The theoretical grounding is somewhat limited—while the authors claim a "probabilistic model," they provide minimal formal analysis of why high-order correlations specifically improve performance beyond empirical results. The performance gains over prior state-of-the-art are modest (64.57% vs. 63.82% for MCT), and the paper doesn't sufficiently address whether high-order attention is fundamentally necessary or merely a more complex architecture. The failure case analysis (Fig. 8) is good but could be expanded.

Pro: Strong empirical results, clear writing, and a genuinely novel perspective on multimodal attention that addresses a meaningful gap in the literature.  
Con: Limited theoretical justification and modest performance gains relative to prior work.

The paper makes a solid contribution to multimodal learning with a technically sound and well-executed approach. While the theoretical depth could be strengthened, the empirical results and clear presentation make it a valuable addition to the field. I recommend acceptance.
