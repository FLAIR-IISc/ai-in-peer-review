- Novel attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for multimodal VQA.  
- Generic attention framework that generalizes to multiple modalities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA, with notable gains from ternary potentials in the 3-modality setup.  
- Competitive performance with fewer parameters than comparable models.  
- Comprehensive ablation study demonstrating the contribution of each component, especially ternary terms.  
- Clear writing and organization, with helpful visualizations of attention maps aligned with human attention.  
- Insufficient analysis and visual evidence for the effectiveness of "attention over answers," given answers are often single words.  
- Limited or negligible gains in the 2-modality setup, questioning applicability when fewer modalities are present.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines that include answers as inputs (e.g., Revisiting VQA Baselines).  
- Lack of discussion and analysis of failure cases.  
- Minor inconsistencies in reported evaluation details (validation vs. test) and some typographical errors.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors’ responses on 2-modality gains and baseline comparisons were unsatisfactory.  
- Despite rebuttal issues, core contributions and strong 3-modality results justify acceptance.  
- Concerns remain about unclear exact comparisons to state-of-the-art and possible inconsistencies in reported metrics.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address attention-over-answers clarity, add baseline comparisons, and resolve reporting inconsistencies.
