- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities for VQA.  
- Introduces a unified probabilistic attention framework that generalizes to any number of modalities.  
- Ablation study shows ternary (three-way) potentials significantly improve performance over pairwise-only models.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art VQA performance (69.4% on test-dev).  
- Paper is clearly written with well-structured explanations and helpful attention visualizations.  
- Main strength: generic multimodal formulation that could generalize beyond VQA.  
- Main weakness: unclear interpretation and lack of visualization for "attention over answers," especially since most answers are single tokens.  
- Primary performance gains appear driven almost entirely by the ternary potential component.  
- Lacks comparisons to models that explicitly use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al. [9]) despite ternary input being critical.  
- Missing analysis of failure cases and model failure modes.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 38 and a reporting discrepancy on line 237 (validation vs. test results).  
- Post-rebuttal concerns: authors claim better correlation with human attention but fail to clearly compare to SOTA attention methods like MCB, and reported MCB vs. MCT numbers appear inconsistent.  
- Review assessment: technically sound and original extension, clear presentation, significant empirical advance, but key improvements hinge on a single component that needs further explanation.  
- Recommendation: accept, with required revisions to clarify answer-attention interpretation and add clearer comparisons to relevant prior work.
