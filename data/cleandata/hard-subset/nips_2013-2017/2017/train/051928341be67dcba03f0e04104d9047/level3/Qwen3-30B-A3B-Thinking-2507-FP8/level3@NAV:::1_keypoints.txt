- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that incorporates unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to model multi-modal interactions.
- Positions the work as a probabilistic extension of MCB to higher-order interactions with a relatively simple, generally applicable architecture.
- Achieves strong empirical results and state-of-the-art performance on VQA datasets with thorough quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Attention maps convincingly show model shifts from unary to pairwise to ternary attention, suggesting gains come from better attention modeling rather than increased capacity.
- Architecture is potentially applicable to multi-modal tasks beyond VQA, increasing its practical value.
- Writing is confusing and disorganized, making the technical contributions hard to follow.
- Perceived novelty is limited because the method largely extends MCB ideas to higher orders without clearly differentiating from prior work.
- The probabilistic framing is motivational but lacks rigorous formalization or should be presented more cautiously.
- Figure 2 is presented at an inappropriate abstraction level and Figure 3 is never referenced, indicating presentation issues.
- Several key experimental/design questions are unanswered: learned coefficients for linear combinations of potentials are not discussed.
- Unclear why the Unary+Pairwise (2-modality) model underperforms MCB despite architectural similarities.
- Missing comparison between sequential MCB and single MCT decision heads, leaving an important baseline unexplored.
- Overall quality is supported by experiments, but unclear presentation and limited clear novelty reduce the paper’s impact and suitability for NIPS.
