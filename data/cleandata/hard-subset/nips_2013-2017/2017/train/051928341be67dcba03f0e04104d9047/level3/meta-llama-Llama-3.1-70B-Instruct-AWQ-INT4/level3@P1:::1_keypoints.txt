- Proposes a novel attention mechanism for VQA that learns high-order correlations and achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA.  
- Attention mechanism framed via a probabilistic model capturing high-order correlations between visual input, question, and answer.  
- Uses mean-field inference to compute marginal probabilities for each modality, which are used as attention weights.  
- Introduces a tensor sketch algorithm to reduce dimensionality of attention weights for efficient computation.  
- Provides thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation with comparisons to existing state-of-the-art, showing improved attention accuracy.  
- Strength: novel high-order attention mechanism with strong empirical performance and emphasis on high-order models.  
- Weakness: writing and presentation are jumbled and difficult to follow.  
- Weakness: novelty unclear—architecture appears to extend existing MCB ideas to different orders.  
- Weakness: probabilistic connection is only motivational and not formalized, risking reader confusion.  
- Weakness: some figures (e.g., Figure 2) are at an awkward abstraction level and should be simplified.  
- Request: show marginalized trinary attention maps to better illustrate the attention mechanism.  
- Request: provide results comparing sequential MCB vs single MCT decision heads to clarify trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: improve writing and presentation for clarity.  
- Recommendation: clarify and detail how the approach extends existing MCB methods.  
- Recommendation: formalize the probabilistic connection or soften the language presenting it.
