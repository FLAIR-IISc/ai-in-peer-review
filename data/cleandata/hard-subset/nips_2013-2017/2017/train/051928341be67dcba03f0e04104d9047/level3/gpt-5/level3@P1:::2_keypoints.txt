- Proposes a generic multimodal attention framework modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answer (A) with mean-field combination to produce attention distributions.  
- Pairwise potentials use learned similarity matrices (Corr2) and ternary potentials use learned triple-wise correlations (Corr3); fusion uses compact polynomial pooling (MCB/MCT).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA accuracy with the 3-modality model; ablations show clear gain from the ternary potential.  
- Conceptually extensible to k modalities and provides a principled probabilistic attention formulation.  
- Strong empirical results with a relatively compact parameter budget.  
- Ablation study supports the central claim that higher-order (ternary) correlations help.  
- Clear exposition and computationally efficient implementations of Corr2/Corr3 and compact pooling.  
- Qualitative visualizations demonstrate modality-specific attentions and interactions.  
- Role and utility of “answer attention” are under-specified; no visualizations and unclear practical benefit since many answers are single tokens.  
- The 2-modality variant does not outperform strong baselines (e.g., MCB) and appears weaker than SOTA except HieCoAtt, raising fairness concerns in comparisons.  
- Missing direct comparisons to prior answer-conditional models (e.g., Jabri et al.), which is crucial given the importance of the ternary path.  
- Limited failure analysis: only one failure case shown and little diagnosis of when high-order attention fails or misleads.  
- Minor reporting and editorial issues: typos, validation vs. test-reporting discrepancies, and inconsistent MCB vs. MCT numbers post-rebuttal.  
- Technically sound overall; probabilistic formulation and mean-field inference are reasonable and well-motivated.  
- Experimental setup and training details are standard and sufficient, but baseline fairness (dimensionality/architecture) is not fully controlled.  
- Paper is generally well written, but needs explicit definitions/examples and more visualizations of answer attention and clearer reporting.  
- High-order attention-as-potentials view combined with MCT is a novel explicit contribution, despite related prior work.  
- Demonstrated SOTA is meaningful and the framework could generalize to other multimodal tasks, though dependence on answers as inputs raises generality questions.  
- Key suggested actions: clarify/visualize answer attention, provide controlled 2-modality comparisons/sensitivity analyses, include answer-conditional baselines, expand failure analysis, and reconcile reported numeric inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: leaning accept — principled, extensible framework with SOTA 3-modality results, but needs improved comparisons, analysis, and reporting.  
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
