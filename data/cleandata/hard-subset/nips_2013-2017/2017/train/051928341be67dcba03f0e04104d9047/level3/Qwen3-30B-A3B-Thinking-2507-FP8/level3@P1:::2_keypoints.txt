- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism (unary, pairwise, ternary) for multimodal tasks, applied to VQA with mean-field inference to obtain marginal attention probabilities.  
- Positions work within recent attention/VQA literature and fills a gap in modeling correlations beyond pairwise interactions.  
- Method is generalizable beyond VQA to any number of modalities.  
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials provide notable performance gains (64.57% vs 63.82% on test-dev).  
- Paper is very well written with clear mathematics, illustrative figures, and logical organization.  
- Major concern: unclear concept and lack of visualizations for “attention over answers,” especially since answers are often single tokens.  
- Major concern: main performance gains appear driven almost entirely by the ternary component; without it the model doesn’t clearly beat two-modality baselines (except HieCoAtt).  
- Needs stronger comparison to prior work that uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al.), since ternary approach effectively treats answers as a third modality.  
- Minor technical issues: reporting discrepancy (Table 1 vs L237), a typo, and inconsistent MCB/MCT performance numbers in Table 1.  
- Lacks failure-case analysis and explanation for identical attention patterns across different questions (e.g., Fig. 8).  
- Significance is moderate-to-high: elegant probabilistic attention formulation with SOTA VQA results, but broader impact depends on successful generalization.  
- Recommendation: accept with caveats—authors should clarify answer attention, justify necessity over two-modality baselines, add comparisons/failure analysis, and fix reported inconsistencies.
