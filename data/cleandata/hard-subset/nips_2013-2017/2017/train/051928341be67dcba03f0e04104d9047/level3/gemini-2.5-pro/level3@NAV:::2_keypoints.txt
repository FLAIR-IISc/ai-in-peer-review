- Central idea: Proposes a high-order attention model for VQA that adds a ternary potential modeling joint image–question–answer correlations.  
- Novelty and clarity: The multimodal k-order attention formulation is conceptually novel and well-presented.  
- Broad applicability: Framework is generic and could extend beyond VQA.  
- Empirical evidence: Ablation study shows the ternary potential is crucial for the reported performance gains.  
- Ambiguity: “Attention over answers” is poorly defined and hard to interpret given mostly single-word answers.  
- Missing visualizations: No visualizations provided to help interpret answer attention or model behavior.  
- Over-reliance on ternary term: Performance gains seem almost entirely dependent on the ternary potential; the 2-modality model does not outperform existing methods.  
- Missing comparisons: Lacks comparison to prior work that also uses answers as input (e.g., Jabri et al., 2016).  
- Lack of error analysis: Needs failure-case analysis to clarify model limitations.  
- Minor issues: Typos (e.g., “mechanism” plural) and a discrepancy between claimed validation results (L237) and Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).  
- Overall verdict: 3-modality model and high-order attention are significant contributions, but clarifications and addressed comparisons are required for full conviction.
