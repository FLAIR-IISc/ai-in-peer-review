- Proposes a high-order attention model for multimodal tasks that combines unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials and reports state-of-the-art results on VQA.  
- Generalizes prior multimodal pooling work (e.g., MCB) by modeling interactions at multiple orders.  
- Strong empirical performance with thorough experiments and convincing quantitative comparisons.  
- Compelling qualitative visualizations showing attention shifts from unary/pairwise to the final combined attention.  
- Paper suffers from poor clarity and presentation; writing is jumbled and technical details are obscured.  
- Figures are problematic (Figure 2 too abstract; Figure 3 not referenced), reducing readability.  
- Novelty is limited—architecture reads as a direct extension of MCB-style ideas and needs clearer articulation of conceptual contribution.  
- Probabilistic terminology (potentials, mean-field inference) is used without formal justification and should be grounded or softened.  
- Authors should report learned linear coefficients for combining potentials and whether different modalities favor different orders.  
- Need explanation why the “2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise” model underperforms MCB despite architectural similarities.  
- Results comparing sequential MCB vs. a single MCT decision head are missing and should be provided.  
- Overall: effective model with impressive results but requires major revision to improve clarity, better position contributions, and temper claims.
