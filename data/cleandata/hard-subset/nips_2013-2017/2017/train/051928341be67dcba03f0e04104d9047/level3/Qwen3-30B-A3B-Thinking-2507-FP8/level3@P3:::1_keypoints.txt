- Proposes a high-order attention model for VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to learn multimodal correlations.  
- Uses a probabilistic framing with mean-field inference to combine potentials.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on VQA test-dev and test-std with fewer parameters than competitors.  
- Attention maps provide meaningful interpretability and qualitative evidence that improvements stem from better attention, not capacity.  
- Thorough qualitative analysis (Fig. 5-8) showing attention shifts between unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Demonstrates how attention combines different potential orders to focus on relevant regions.  
- Reviewer requested marginalized ternary attention maps for better understanding of modality integration.  
- Writing and presentation are confusing and poorly organized, making technical contributions hard to follow.  
- Probabilistic connection is only motivational and not formally derived or clearly interpreted.  
- Figure 2 is at an awkward abstraction level and should be simplified to emphasize potential generation.  
- Figure 3 (k-order attention) is not referenced in the text, which is a significant oversight.  
- Perceived novelty is limited—architecture largely extends MCB ideas to higher-order interactions without clear differentiation.  
- Claim of general applicability to any number of modalities is not demonstrated beyond VQA.  
- Critical experimental details missing: learned values of linear coefficients (αi, βi, γi) for combining potentials.  
- Unexplained counterintuitive result: 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise underperforms MCB despite similar architecture.  
- Missing comparison/results for sequential MCB vs single MCT decision heads.  
- Technically sound with strong empirical results, but poor presentation undermines the contribution and reduces perceived significance.  
- Recommendation: clarify probabilistic motivation, improve organization and figures, and provide deeper analysis of model behavior before acceptance.
