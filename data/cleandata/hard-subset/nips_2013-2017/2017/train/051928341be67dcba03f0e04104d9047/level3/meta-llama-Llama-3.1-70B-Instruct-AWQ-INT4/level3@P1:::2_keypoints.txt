- Proposes a novel, generic multimodal attention formulation for VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Key idea: model high-order (ternary) correlations between modalities to improve VQA performance.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed attention mechanism.  
- Thorough ablation study shows the ternary potential contributes substantially to performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset.  
- “Attention over answers” is unclear (most answers are single tokens) and lacks visualizations or detailed explanation.  
- Main performance gains appear driven by ternary potentials; without them, the model rarely outperforms 2-modality methods.  
- Needs comparison to prior methods that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al. Revisiting VQA Baselines).  
- Lacks analysis of failure cases and model failure modes to identify weaknesses.  
- Contains minor errors/typos and a reporting discrepancy (e.g., “mechanism(s)” and validation vs. test-dev/test-std claims).  
- Post-rebuttal claims that attention maps correlate better with humans are unclear relative to SOTA; reported MCB vs MCT numbers seem inconsistent/swapped and conflict with Table 1.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: generic formulation with potential generalization, clear writing, and SOTA results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear answer-attention, reliance on ternary potentials for improvement, and missing failure-mode analysis.
