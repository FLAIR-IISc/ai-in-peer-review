- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models ternary interactions between image, question, and answer.  
- Extends beyond pairwise attention by introducing ternary potentials to capture more complex multi-modal correlations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Situates work within prior attention literature and cites relevant methods (e.g., Lu et al., Xu et al., Fukui et al.).  
- Claims a generic, flexible framework potentially applicable to other multi-modal tasks.  
- Ablation study indicates ternary potentials (including answer modality) contribute to performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful visualizations (notably Figure 3).  
- The notion of “attention over answers” is unclear given VQA answers are typically single tokens and lacks attention visualizations.  
- Unclear whether improvements are solely due to including answers; requires stronger comparisons to two-modality baselines.  
- Needs comparison to prior work that explicitly uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al.).  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of failure cases and explanations of model weaknesses.  
- Contains minor errors and inconsistencies (wording and mismatched validation/test reporting).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/generic approach, SOTA results, clear presentation, demonstrated importance of ternary potentials.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear answer-attention component, insufficient comparative analysis and failure analysis, minor manuscript issues.  
- Reviewer recommendation: lean toward accept, contingent on clarifying answer modality’s role, stronger two-modality baselines, and fixing minor issues.
