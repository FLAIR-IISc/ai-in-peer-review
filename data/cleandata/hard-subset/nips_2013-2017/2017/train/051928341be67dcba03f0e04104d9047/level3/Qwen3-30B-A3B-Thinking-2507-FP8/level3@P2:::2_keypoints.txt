- Proposes a novel high-order (unary/pairwise/ternary) probabilistic attention framework for multimodal tasks, applied to VQA.  
- Extends prior pairwise attention methods by explicitly modeling ternary interactions across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA benchmark and provides clear theoretical formulation.  
- Strong ablation study showing ternary potentials significantly improve performance over 2-modality variants.  
- Framework is generalizable and could be applied beyond VQA to other multimodal problems.  
- Well-written and well-organized paper with clear mathematics and helpful illustrations.  
- Claim of “attention over answers” is unclear because most answers are single tokens and no answer-attention visualizations are provided.  
- Main improvement appears driven by ternary potentials; insufficient justification or comparison to existing 2-modality or answer-inclusion methods (e.g., Jabri et al.).  
- Lacks analysis of failure cases and model failure modes.  
- Reporting inconsistencies and minor errors (terminology, validation vs. test reporting, inconsistent MCB vs. MCT numbers).  
- Does not sufficiently differentiate from prior work that incorporates answer information, limiting perceived originality.  
- Overall technically sound with useful empirical results, but comparisons and reporting need improvement.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions to fix reporting issues and clarify the attention-over-answers concept.
