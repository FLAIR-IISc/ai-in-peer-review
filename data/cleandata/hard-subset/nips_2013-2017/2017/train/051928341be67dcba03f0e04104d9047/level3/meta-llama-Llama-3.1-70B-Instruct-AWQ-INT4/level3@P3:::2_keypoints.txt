- Proposes a novel multimodal attention mechanism combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer for VQA.  
- Introduces a generic attention formulation that models high-order interactions and could generalize beyond VQA.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains embedding, attention, and decision components, and reviews related work thoroughly.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset and reports a helpful ablation study.  
- Ablation shows the ternary potential is the main contributor to performance gains.  
- Without ternary potentials the model generally does not outperform existing two-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), raising robustness concerns.  
- The concept of “attention over answers” is unclear because most answers are single tokens and no answer-attention visualizations are provided.  
- Lacks comprehensive analysis of failure cases and model failure modes; only a single failure example is shown.  
- Does not compare against prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al.), despite ternary input being central.  
- Minor errors and typos present, including a reporting discrepancy in Table 1 (claims validation but shows test-dev/test-std).  
- Technically sound with well-supported theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Original contribution is significant as it departs from pairwise-only attention to include ternary interactions.  
- Paper addresses an important and difficult VQA problem and has potential to advance multimodal attention methods.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, generality, strong empirical results, and informative ablation; rejection arguments: unclear answer attention, reliance on ternary terms, insufficient failure analysis and missing comparisons.
