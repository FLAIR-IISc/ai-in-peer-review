- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials between image, question, and answer for multimodal tasks.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art VQA performance, with improvements largely attributed to the ternary potentials.  
- Well-written, modular design that is generally applicable and easy to extend to different modalities.  
- Ablation study convincingly shows the contribution of the ternary potential.  
- “Attention over answers” concept is unclear given single-token answers; visualizations are needed for clarification.  
- Model without ternary potentials does not substantially outperform existing 2-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), raising questions about baseline strength.  
- Needs to better justify whether gains are due to ternary potentials or a strong baseline implementation.  
- Lacks comparison to prior work that explicitly incorporates answers into attention (e.g., Jabri et al., 2017).  
- Missing detailed analysis of failure cases and common error types, limiting understanding of model limitations.  
- Contains minor errors and reporting inconsistencies (e.g., L38; discrepancy L237 vs. Table 1).  
- Arguments for acceptance: generic, widely applicable framework; quantitative 3-modality SOTA gains; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear answer-attention, limited gains without ternary potential, missing comparisons and failure analysis, minor reporting issues.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer leans to accept but flags unresolved concerns about attention-map comparisons to SOTA (MCB) and numeric inconsistencies between MCB and MCT.
