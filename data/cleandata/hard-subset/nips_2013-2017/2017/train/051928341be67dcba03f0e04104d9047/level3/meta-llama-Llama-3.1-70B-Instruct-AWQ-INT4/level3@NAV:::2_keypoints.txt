- Proposes a novel, generic multimodal attention combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer for VQA.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset using the high-order attention mechanism.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Proposed attention formulation is generic and may generalize beyond VQA to other multimodal tasks.  
- Ablation study shows the ternary potential contributes significantly to performance.  
- Results show a substantial improvement over existing methods overall.  
- "Attention over answers" concept is unclear, especially since most answers are single tokens.  
- No visualizations are provided to illustrate answer attention or interpretability.  
- Main performance gains appear driven primarily by the ternary potentials; without them the model doesn’t outperform 2-modality baselines.  
- Authors should clarify and analyze the specific contribution of the ternary potentials in depth.  
- Paper lacks comparison to prior work that uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al.).  
- Missing analysis of failure cases and model failure modes to understand limitations.  
- Technically sound; experimental claims are well-supported by results.  
- Originality lies in applying high-order (unary/pairwise/ternary) attention to VQA, though high-order potentials themselves are not new.  
- Significance: achieves 3-modality SOTA and could inspire new multimodal attention research.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance given the generic framework and quantitative gains, despite weaknesses in 2-modality comparisons and analysis.
