This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between various data modalities, which is demonstrated to be effective in visual question answering (VQA) tasks. The method is based on a probabilistic model that uses unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture correlations between different modalities. The authors show that their approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work on attention mechanisms. The proposed method is clearly explained, and the experimental results are presented in a clear and concise manner. The authors also provide qualitative evaluations of their approach, which help to illustrate the effectiveness of their method.

One of the strengths of the paper is the thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments that the authors conducted to evaluate their approach. The results show that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches on the VQA dataset, and the qualitative evaluations provide insight into how the method works.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The writing and presentation are sometimes jumbled and need improvement. For example, the authors could provide more context and explanations for the figures and tables, and the text could be more concise and clear.

Another weakness is that the paper does not clearly explain the novelty of the proposed method. The authors mention that their approach is an extension of the MCB ideas to higher/lower orders, but they do not provide a clear explanation of how their method differs from existing approaches.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from more formalization of the probabilistic connection between the attention mechanisms. The authors mention that the probabilistic connection is only motivational and not formalized, but they do not provide a clear explanation of how the probabilistic model is used in the method.

Finally, the paper could benefit from more analysis of the results. The authors provide some qualitative evaluations, but they do not provide a detailed analysis of the results, such as an analysis of the errors or a comparison of the results with other methods.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is interesting and effective. However, there are some weaknesses that need to be addressed, such as improving the writing and presentation, clarifying the novelty of the method, formalizing the probabilistic connection, and providing more analysis of the results.
