- Proposes a generic multimodal attention framework modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answer (A), combined and normalized via a single mean-field iteration to produce attentions PV, PQ, PA.  
- Uses tensor sketching for decision making: Multimodal Compact Trilinear pooling (MCT) or a two-layer Multimodal Compact Bilinear (MCB) stack to score answers.  
- Conceptually grounded in probabilistic graphical models and high-order potentials while remaining computationally feasible through sketching.  
- Subsumes prior unary and pairwise attention methods (e.g., SAN, HieCoAtt, MCB) and naturally extends to three-modality attention; cites relevant prior work including Lu et al. and Fukui et al.  
- Technical construction (potentials, learnable linear combination, single-step mean-field) is sound and clearly specified; decision modules are standard and justified.  
- Strong quantitative VQA results with the full three-modality model and convincing ablation showing the contribution of ternary potentials; useful qualitative visualizations for image/question attention.  
- Two-modality instantiation underperforms several published two-modality baselines, possibly confounded by reduced embedding dimensionality, weakening fairness of some comparisons.  
- Semantics and role of answer-attention (PA) are unclear and not visualized or analyzed in depth.  
- Failure modes and error analysis are limited and only briefly discussed.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and readable with helpful figures; minor notational issues and reporting inconsistencies remain.  
- Core originality is the unified, scalable high-order attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with a mean-field view.  
- Significance: advances SOTA in three-modality VQA and offers a reusable abstraction likely to influence multimodal tasks (grounded language, visual dialog, audiovisual QA).  
- Strength: generic multimodal attention formulation that generalizes beyond VQA.  
- Strength: clear exposition and intuitive probabilistic grounding with a tidy architecture.  
- Strength: solid ablation demonstrating gains from ternary potentials and three-modality SOTA results.  
- Strength: practical decision modules (MCT/MCB) with careful comparison.  
- Weakness: unclear meaning and utility of attention over answers in MC VQA; lacking visualizations/analysis of PA.  
- Weakness: two-modality model does not beat strong baselines (except HieCoAtt); attribution to lower embedding dim needs controlled verification.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to prior answer-conditioned baselines (e.g., Jabri et al., Revisiting VQA Baselines) under the same protocol.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and no breakdown by question type or error taxonomy.  
- Requests: clarify semantics and behavior of PA with visualizations/statistics (entropy, sensitivity to paraphrases, multi-word answers).  
- Requests: provide a fair two-modality baseline matching feature dim/backbone/classifier (e.g., 2048-d ResNet + identical MCB) and ablate α/β weights.  
- Requests: include comparisons to answer-as-input baselines (Jabri et al.) and discuss pros/cons versus ternary attention.  
- Requests: report exact human-attention correlation metrics (e.g., rank-correlation, KL) and compare to strong baselines controlling for resolution/normalization.  
- Requests: report experiments on multiple mean-field iterations and whether α/β/γ weights stabilize.  
- Requests: reconcile inconsistent post-rebuttal MCB/MCT numbers and specify backbone/split for each reported figure.  
- Minor comments: typos (e.g., aA vs aV), wording fixes (mechanism→mechanisms), reporting split inconsistencies (validation vs test-dev/test-std), cite dataset splits/preprocessing, clarify train+val usage.  
- Reproducibility: training details mostly specified and public implementation promised; ensure repo matches final configuration and includes scripts for all ablations.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-written with meaningful ternary potential gains; leans toward acceptance pending clearer two-modality comparisons, clarification of answer-attention, and reporting fixes.
