- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that models ternary potentials across image, question, and answer.  
- Generic probabilistic formulation using mean-field inference, applicable beyond VQA.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset, demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is generally well-written with helpful figures clarifying the model.  
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials materially contribute to performance.  
- "Attention over answers" concept is unclear given single-token answers and lacks visualizations/explanations.  
- Main gains seem driven by ternary potentials, raising concerns about whether improvements stem from higher-order modeling or increased capacity.  
- Missing comparisons to prior work that uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al. [9]).  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases and model failure modes.  
- Minor errors and inconsistencies in the manuscript (typos and mismatched validation/test claims).  
- Overall assessment: compelling and novel with strong results, but reliance on ternary potentials and missing comparisons are concerns.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generic formulation, state-of-the-art performance, clear presentation, demonstrated benefit of ternary potentials.  
- Arguments for rejection: heavy reliance on ternary potentials, lack of comparison to answer-inclusive methods, inadequate failure analysis, minor errors.  
- Reviewer confidence 7/10 and final recommendation: Accept.
