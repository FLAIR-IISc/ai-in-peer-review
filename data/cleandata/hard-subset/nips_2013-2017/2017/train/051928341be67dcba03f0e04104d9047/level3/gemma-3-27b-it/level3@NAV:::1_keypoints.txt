- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and uses mean-field inference to derive attention weights.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance and state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset.  
- Builds on prior attention work like MCB and MCT by extending interactions to higher-order correlations.  
- Provides thorough experiments, ablation studies, and qualitative attention visualizations supporting claims.  
- Qualitative results suggest performance gains come from improved attention targeting and hierarchical refinement from unary/pairwise to final attention.  
- Writing and presentation are jumbled and difficult to follow, hindering understanding.  
- The probabilistic motivation (high-order potentials) is informal and should be either formalized or toned down.  
- Figure 2 is overly abstract and needs simplification; Figure 3 is never referenced and is effectively useless.  
- Novelty is limited—primarily an extension of existing MCB ideas—so specific contributions need clearer articulation.  
- Several experimental details are missing or unanswered (learned linear coefficients, modality-specific favored potential orders).  
- Unexplained empirical issues: why the 2-modality Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB despite similar architecture.  
- Missing results/details on experiments comparing sequential MCB vs single MCT decision heads.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, novel application of high-order correlations, and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: confusing writing, limited novelty, lack of formal probabilistic treatment, unexplained figures and open questions.  
- Overall recommendation: reject now but encourage resubmission after substantial revision to address clarity and unanswered questions.
