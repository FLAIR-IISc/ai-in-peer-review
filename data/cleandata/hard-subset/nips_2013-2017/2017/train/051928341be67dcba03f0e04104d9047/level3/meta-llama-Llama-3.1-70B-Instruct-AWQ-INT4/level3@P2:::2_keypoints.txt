- Proposes a novel attention mechanism for VQA that models high-order correlations across modalities.  
- Introduces a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition, a useful ablation study, and thorough related-work analysis.  
- The generic formulation could generalize to other multimodal tasks beyond VQA.  
- Unclear explanation of what "attention over answers" means given most answers are single tokens.  
- No visualizations of answer attention to clarify how answer attention operates.  
- Main improvement seems driven primarily by ternary potentials; without them the model largely does not outperform 2-modality methods (except HieCoAtt).  
- Lacks comparison to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., Revisiting VQA Baselines), which is critical given ternary inputs.  
- No analysis of failure cases or model failure modes to better understand limitations.  
- Contains minor errors/typos and a reporting discrepancy (mechanism→mechanisms; validation vs. test reporting).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
