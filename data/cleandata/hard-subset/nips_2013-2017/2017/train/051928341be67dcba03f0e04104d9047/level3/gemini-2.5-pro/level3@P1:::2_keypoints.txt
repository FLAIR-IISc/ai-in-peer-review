- Proposes a novel probabilistic high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across modalities.  
- Applied to VQA by modeling attention over image, question, and candidate answers; potentials are linearly combined and softmaxed to produce attention.  
- Incorporating a ternary (image-question-answer) potential yields state-of-the-art results on VQA v1; ablation shows the ternary term is the main contributor.  
- Framework is theoretically generalizable to more than three modalities.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with helpful illustrations (notably Figure 3).  
- Technical quality is strong: competitive benchmark performance and ablation support the proposed idea.  
- The notion of "attention over answers" is poorly motivated and unexplained; visualizations of answer attention weights are needed.  
- The 2-modality (image-question) model does not outperform several prior 2-modality methods, suggesting gains are driven mainly by answer inclusion.  
- Insufficient comparison to prior work that uses answers as input (e.g., Jabri et al., 2016); more conceptual and empirical contrast required.  
- Failure analysis is limited — more systematic breakdown by question type and error modes is required.  
- Minor issues: typos and inconsistencies in reporting evaluation splits (val vs test-dev/test-standard).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, elegant, generalizable framework; SOTA results; clear writing; ablation validates ternary potential.  
- Cons for rejection: reliance on answer modality for gains, unclear answer-attention interpretation, missing comparisons, and superficial failure analysis.
