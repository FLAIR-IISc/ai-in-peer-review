- Proposes a multimodal attention mechanism that models unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Shows high-order correlations, especially ternary potentials, significantly improve VQA performance.  
- Extends prior attention methods (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) to a general framework applicable to any number of modalities.  
- Clear presentation and a generic framework that could generalize beyond VQA.  
- Thorough ablation study demonstrating the contribution of ternary potentials.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA benchmarks, demonstrating effectiveness.  
- “Attention over answers” is unclear given mostly single-token answers and lacks visualizations.  
- Main gains appear driven solely by ternary potentials; without them the model does not outperform most two-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), requiring stronger justification.  
- Needs comparison to Jabri et al. (2016), which uses answers as inputs and is relevant to the ternary approach.  
- Lacks failure-case analysis and examination of model failure modes.  
- Minor reporting issues: claim of validation reporting (L237) vs. Table 1 showing test-dev/test-std, and inconsistent MCB vs. MCT numbers.  
- Post-rebuttal claim about better correlation with human attention lacks comparison to SOTA methods like MCB.  
- Overall: technically strong and a significant contribution to multimodal attention; merits acceptance despite the noted concerns.
