- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between modalities, effective for VQA.
- Method is based on a probabilistic model using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture correlations.
- Shows state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
- Paper is well-organized with a thorough related-work review and clear method description.
- Experimental results are presented clearly and include useful qualitative evaluations.
- Strong quantitative and qualitative experiments demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.
- Writing and presentation are sometimes jumbled; figures/tables need more context and the text should be more concise.
- Novelty is not clearly explained—claimed as an extension of MCB but differentiation from existing approaches is unclear.
- Probabilistic connection is only motivational and not formally defined; usage of the probabilistic model is unclear.
- Lacks deeper analysis of results (e.g., error analysis and detailed comparisons with other methods).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, thorough experiments, superior VQA results.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation issues, unclear novelty, unformalized probabilistic link, insufficient result analysis.
- Rating 7/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
