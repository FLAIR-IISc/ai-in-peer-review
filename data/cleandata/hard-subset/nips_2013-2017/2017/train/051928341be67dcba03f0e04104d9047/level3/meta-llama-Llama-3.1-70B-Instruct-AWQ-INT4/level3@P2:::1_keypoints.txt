- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that models unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations for VQA.  
- Uses a probabilistic-style model with unary/pairwise/ternary potentials to compute attention probabilities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA real-image test-dev and test-std datasets.  
- Provides qualitative results showing attention focuses on reasonable image pixels and question locations.  
- Compares to prior methods (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) and reports superior performance.  
- Strength: novel attention formulation with strong empirical results.  
- Strength: thorough quantitative and qualitative experiments.  
- Strength: observed notable shifts between unary/pairwise attentions and final attention, indicating capture of complex correlations.  
- Weakness: writing and presentation are jumbled and need significant improvement.  
- Weakness: probabilistic connection is motivational only and not formally derived.  
- Weakness: contributions relative to extensions of MCB and prior work are not clearly clarified.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 is at an awkward abstraction level and should be simplified to highlight potential generation.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 is never referenced in the text.  
- Requests: provide details on learned linear coefficients for combining marginalized potentials and explain modality-specific favored potential orders.  
- Significance: important approach with potential applicability beyond VQA (e.g., translation, captioning).  
- Overall assessment: good contribution but needs clearer exposition and formal clarification of contributions.  
- Review rating: 7/10; reviewer confidence: 8/10.
