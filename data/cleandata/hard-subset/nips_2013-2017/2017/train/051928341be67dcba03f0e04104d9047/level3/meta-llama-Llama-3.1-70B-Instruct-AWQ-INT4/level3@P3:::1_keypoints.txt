- Proposes a novel high-order attention model that learns correlations across multiple data modalities and achieves state-of-the-art on VQA.  
- Model is extensible to any number of modalities, making it broadly applicable for multimodal learning.  
- Uses mean-field inference to obtain marginal probabilities for efficient computation of attention weights.  
- Thorough quantitative and qualitative evaluation demonstrating the model captures complex visual–textual relationships.  
- Attention maps qualitatively show reasonable pixel and question localization, outperforming existing approaches in some cases.  
- Main technical contribution is the high-order attention formulation and its application to multimodal fusion.  
- Writing and presentation are weak: text is jumbled and often confusing.  
- Notation is inconsistent (same quantities represented by different symbols).  
- Figures are poorly explained and some (e.g., Fig. 2) are at an awkward abstraction level.  
- Perceived novelty is limited—unclear how the approach departs from prior attention/multimodal work.  
- The probabilistic link between the attention model and the decision-making component is not formalized.  
- Results are significant and likely impactful for multimodal tasks if presentation and clarity are improved.  
- Authors should clarify contributions and provide detailed comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Questions for authors: what were the learned linear coefficients for marginalized potentials and do modalities favor different orders?  
- Questions for authors: why does the 2-modality Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB despite similar architecture?  
- Questions for authors: what were the results comparing sequential MCB vs single MCT decision heads?  
- Recommendation: either formalize the probabilistic connection to decision-making or soften the theoretical claims.
