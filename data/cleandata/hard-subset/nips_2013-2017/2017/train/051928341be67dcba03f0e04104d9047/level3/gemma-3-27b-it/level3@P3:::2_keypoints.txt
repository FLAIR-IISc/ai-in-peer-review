- Proposes a high-order attention model for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary interactions across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Key contribution: incorporating ternary potentials yields state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Presents a generic, probabilistic mean-field inference framework applicable to any number of modalities.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes an ablation study showing the impact of ternary potentials.  
- Major concern: unclear interpretation of “attention over answers,” especially since most VQA answers are single tokens.  
- Lack of visualizations for answer attention limits intuitive understanding of what the model attends to.  
- Performance gains appear heavily dependent on ternary potentials; the base 2-modality framework often does not outperform existing methods.  
- Needs clearer explanation of why this high-order potential formulation is effective compared to simpler approaches.  
- Missing comparison to models that directly incorporate answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., 2016), which could clarify the source of gains.  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases and attention map diagnostics to show when and why the model fails.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors and a reporting discrepancy between claimed validation results and Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal inclination toward acceptance due to generic framework and SOTA 3-modality results, but unresolved concerns remain.  
- Inconsistencies in reported SOTA comparisons (MCB vs. MCT numbers) need correction for clarity.
