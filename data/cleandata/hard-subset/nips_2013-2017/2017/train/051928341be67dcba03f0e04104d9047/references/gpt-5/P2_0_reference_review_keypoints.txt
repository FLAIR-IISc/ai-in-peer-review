- Proposal: High-order multimodal attention modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answer (A), combined and softmaxed (interpreted as one mean-field step), then fused with MCB/MCT; claims SOTA on VQA MC.

- Novelty claim: Explicit unary+pairwise+ternary potential decomposition with learned mixture weights and extensibility to k modalities, but probabilistic justification is weak.

- Technical soundness: Unary/pairwise/ternary feature construction is reasonable, but the mean-field inference claim is not substantiated and learned mixture weights decouple updates from variational inference.

- Computational complexity: C3 tensor scales O(nv·nq·na), feasible for MC (na≈18) but intractable for open-ended na≈3000; paper lacks memory/time quantification.

- Empirics: Key quantitative results and full comparison tables are missing; insufficient ablations (unary vs pairwise vs ternary), no analysis of learned weights or multiple MF iterations, and unclear fairness of baseline comparisons; runtime/memory not reported.

- Reproducibility: Many training details and promised code help reproducibility, but a non-anonymized code link conflicts with double-blind review.

- Clarity issues: Generally readable but has notational/editorial problems (ambiguous 1×1 conv description, variable reuse bug aV/aA), missing/unclear figures/tables, and unjustified MF terminology.

- Originality assessment: Moderate—conceptually clean extension of existing bilinear/trilinear attention and pooling methods; incremental step from pairwise to ternary and no demonstration beyond 3 modalities.

- Significance: Potentially useful for VQA if SOTA and parameter efficiency are confirmed, but broader impact limited by cubic scaling and lack of full quantitative support.

- Pros: Motivated modeling of higher-order interactions, simple modular architecture unifying unary/pairwise/ternary attentions, integration with compact pooling, promising qualitative visualizations, and training details provided.

- Cons: Missing key quantitative evidence and fair comparisons, weak MF derivation, unexamined computational/memory costs for ternary attention, limited ablations and sensitivity analyses, and notational errors.

- Questions for authors: Provide exact test-dev/test-std numbers and full matched comparisons; clarify whether ternary attention is MC-only and open-ended V–Q performance; give time/memory benchmarks and scalability discussion; supply a concrete graphical model/MF derivation or remove MF claim; include ablations (remove ternary, vary α/β/γ, multiple iterations, different d and backbones).

- Recommendation: Borderline reject — plausible idea but insufficient quantitative evidence, ablations, and clarity to merit acceptance as-is.
