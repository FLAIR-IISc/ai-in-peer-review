- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that models high-order correlations between multiple modalities.  
- Uses a probabilistic framework with mean-field inference to learn k-th order correlations.  
- Applies the method to VQA and achieves state-of-the-art performance.  
- Argues the approach is more general than pairwise attention and applicable beyond VQA.  
- Provides a clear mathematical formulation of the attention mechanism and inference.  
- Details each component (data embedding, attention, decision making) thoroughly.  
- Uses tensor sketching to mitigate parameter explosion for higher-order models.  
- Presents ablation studies (MCT vs 2-layer MCB) to justify design choices.  
- Includes qualitative visualizations that give intuition about attention behavior.  
- Novelty: explicitly modeling high-order inter-modal correlations is a significant contribution.  
- Strong empirical results support the method’s effectiveness on a challenging benchmark.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and generally well-organized.  
- Computational complexity of learning/inference for high-order correlations is a major concern.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (many tuning choices) is not explored in sufficient depth.  
- Limited and non-systematic error analysis; failure modes are not thoroughly characterized.  
- Comparisons to prior work lack depth and comprehensive hyperparameter matching.  
- Motivation could better explain scenarios where higher-order interactions are crucial, beyond VQA.  
- Some technical sections (e.g., tensor sketching) could be simplified for clarity.  
- For acceptance: novelty, strong results, clear description, and broad applicability argue in favor.  
- Against acceptance: unresolved compute concerns, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited error analysis, and comparison depth.  
- Overall score: 7/10 (Strong Accept); recommend acceptance with revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
