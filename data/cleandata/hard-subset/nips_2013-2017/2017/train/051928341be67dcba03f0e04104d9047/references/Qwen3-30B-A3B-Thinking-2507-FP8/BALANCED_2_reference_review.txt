

# Review of "High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering"

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that models high-order correlations between multiple data modalities (visual, textual, and answer) for visual question answering (VQA). The authors argue that existing attention mechanisms either handle single modalities or treat multiple modalities in a hand-crafted way, whereas their approach uses a probabilistic model with high-order potentials to capture correlations between any number of modalities through mean-field inference. They demonstrate their method on VQA, achieving state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-structured method with clear mathematical formulations. The authors provide thorough experimental results on standard VQA benchmarks, comparing against multiple strong baselines including Lu et al. (2016) and Fukui et al. (2016). The quantitative results (64.57% on test-dev with 2-layer MCB) are impressive and demonstrate the practical effectiveness of their approach.

2. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The figures (particularly Figures 3-7) effectively visualize the high-order attention mechanism and qualitative results. The mathematical formulations are presented clearly, with appropriate abstractions for the attention mechanism.

3. **Practical Implementation:** The authors provide sufficient implementation details (including architecture choices, hyperparameters, and training procedures) to allow reproducibility. Their use of tensor sketching (via MCB/MCT) to handle the exponential parameter growth of higher-order correlations is practical and well-motivated.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Originality Concerns:** While the paper claims novelty in "high-order attention," the approach appears to be a natural extension of existing bilinear models (e.g., [7, 13]) rather than a fundamentally new concept. The paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate itself from prior work that uses similar interaction mechanisms. For example, the bilinear models in [7, 13] already capture pairwise interactions between modalities, and the authors' ternary interaction is a straightforward extension. The paper would benefit from a more precise articulation of how their probabilistic mean-field inference approach differs from previous interaction mechanisms.

2. **Theoretical Justification:** The paper lacks strong theoretical justification for why high-order correlations (beyond pairwise) are beneficial for VQA. The authors state that "learning these correlations effectively directs the appropriate attention" but don't provide evidence that the high-order interactions are actually capturing meaningful semantic relationships. The failure case in Figure 8 suggests their model sometimes fails to capture question-specific attention, which undermines the claim that high-order attention is superior.

3. **Significance of Contribution:** While the paper achieves state-of-the-art results, the improvement over prior work is modest (e.g., 64.57% vs. 63.82% on val set with MCT vs. 2-layer MCB). The paper doesn't clearly establish whether the high-order attention is necessary for this improvement or if simpler modifications to existing approaches could achieve similar results. The authors claim "state-of-the-art performance" but don't sufficiently contextualize this within the broader VQA literature.

4. **Experiments:** The paper lacks ablation studies that isolate the contribution of high-order attention. For example, it's unclear how much of the improvement comes from the high-order attention versus other architectural choices (e.g., using ResNet features instead of VGG). The comparison to prior work doesn't include recent VQA approaches (e.g., from the 2017 VQA challenge winners), which would strengthen the significance claim.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

1. **Clarify the Novelty:** The authors should more precisely articulate how their probabilistic high-order attention differs from existing bilinear interaction models. A more detailed comparison with similar approaches (e.g., [13, 19]) would strengthen the originality claim.

2. **Add Theoretical Justification:** Provide evidence (either theoretical or empirical) that high-order interactions are beneficial for VQA. For example, they could analyze how often the high-order attention patterns correlate with the correct answer.

3. **Expand Experiments:** Include ablation studies to isolate the contribution of high-order attention. Compare against more recent VQA approaches (e.g., from the 2017 VQA challenge) to better establish the significance of their results.

4. **Address Limitations:** The paper should more thoroughly discuss the limitations of their approach, particularly the failure case in Figure 8. This would demonstrate a more balanced evaluation and help future work build on their method.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a technically sound approach that achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA. The high-order attention mechanism is well-motivated and implemented with care. However, the contribution appears to be an incremental extension of existing bilinear interaction models rather than a fundamentally new approach. The paper would benefit from stronger theoretical justification, clearer differentiation from prior work, and more rigorous ablation studies.

The significance of the work is somewhat limited by the modest performance gains over prior methods and the lack of clear evidence that high-order attention is necessary for these gains. While the results are promising, the paper doesn't sufficiently establish why high-order attention is a necessary component for VQA performance.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend rejection. While the paper is technically sound and achieves good results, the contribution appears to be incremental rather than transformative. The novelty is overstated relative to existing work, and the paper doesn't sufficiently demonstrate why high-order attention is fundamentally necessary for VQA performance. With more careful comparison to recent literature, stronger theoretical justification, and additional ablation studies, this work could be significantly strengthened for a NIPS audience.
