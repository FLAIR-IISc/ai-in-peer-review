- Introduces a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for multimodal VQA.
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on VQA with convincing quantitative and qualitative results.
- Extends prior pairwise attention work (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) to k-th order interactions.
- Strong empirical results and comprehensive evaluation with attention visualizations and baseline comparisons.
- Clear, well-organized methodology using a probabilistic framework and mean-field inference; reproducible implementation details.
- Demonstrates practical significance for multimodal tasks beyond VQA.
- Lacks theoretical justification for why high-order interactions improve performance in specific scenarios.
- Missing thorough ablation study to quantify contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.
- No discussion of computational complexity; k-th order attention may scale exponentially and be impractical.
- Claims generality to >3 modalities but only demonstrates three; practical limitations not discussed.
- Incomplete comparison with recent related work (e.g., Kim et al., Nam et al.).
- Technically sound and empirically well-supported overall.
- Well-written and clear; minor clarity issue in ternary potential explanation (Section 3.2.3).
- Novel extension of attention mechanisms to high-order multimodal correlations.
- Significant potential impact on multimodal learning and downstream tasks like image captioning and visual reasoning.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested fixes: add ablation study, analyze computational complexity, compare with recent high-order methods, provide theoretical motivation, clarify ternary computation, discuss scaling beyond three modalities.
