- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that models unary, pairwise, and higher-order correlations for VQA.  
- Formalizes attention as a probabilistic model and computes attention via mean-field inference.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on VQA with a smaller model (40M parameters) and ~0.7% absolute gain.  
- Claims the method is easily extendable to any number of modalities, unlike prior two-modality approaches.  
- Clearly differentiates from prior work (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) and situates contributions in related work.  
- Provides comprehensive quantitative results and qualitative visualizations of attention patterns.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and reproducible with detailed architecture and training information.  
- Mean-field inference formulation and probabilistic interpretation of attention are well-motivated and novel.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and helpful figures illustrate unary, pairwise, and ternary modules.  
- Does not thoroughly analyze the computational cost or training/inference time impact of high-order attention.  
- The necessity of high-order attention for VQA is not fully justified; pairwise often suffices in qualitative examples.  
- Evaluation is limited to VQA (three modalities) despite claims of generality to any number of modalities.  
- Comparisons to very recent VQA methods (e.g., 2017 challenge winners) are limited.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, significant, and practical contribution with minor issues (computational cost, stronger justification/evaluation) to address.
