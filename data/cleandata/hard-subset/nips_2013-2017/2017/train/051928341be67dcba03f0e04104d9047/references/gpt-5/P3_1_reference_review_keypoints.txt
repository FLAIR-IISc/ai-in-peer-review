- Proposes a high-order attention framework for multimodal VQA with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answers (A).  
- Attention computed by softmax over a learned linear combination of potentials, claimed as a single mean-field iteration.  
- Uses MCB/MCT pooling (tensor sketching) to combine attended vectors for decision making.  
- Strength: clear motivation to model explicit cross-modal interactions at the attention stage; framework is conceptually general and modular.  
- Strength: unary/pairwise computations are simple and compatible with standard CNN/LSTM embeddings; qualitative visualizations support pairwise/ternary influence.  
- Strength: empirical exploration of MCT vs two-layer MCB and three-modality variants shows some gains over two-modality baseline.  
- Major weakness: probabilistic/mean-field formulation is underspecified—no energy, variational objective, factorization, or derivation of update equations provided.  
- Major weakness: computational feasibility of ternary potentials unclear—C3 is nq×nv×na and potentially intractable; no algorithm, complexity, or memory analysis given.  
- Major weakness: evaluation incomplete and potentially unfair—mostly multiple-choice results while many baselines don’t condition on answers; open-ended results and standard VQA test-dev/test-std breakdowns missing.  
- Quantitative SOTA claims are unsubstantiated; comparisons against strong bilinear/multimodal baselines with identical backbones/features are missing.  
- Novelty is moderate/incremental: pairwise attention is standard; main new element is ternary attention, but prior answer-conditioned work exists.  
- Significance contingent on efficient ternary computation and clear empirical gains; current evidence is insufficient.  
- Clarity/presentation issues: missing/incorrect formulas, undefined pooling weights, inconsistent notation, missing references, and sparse training details (α,β,γ initialization, mean-field iterations).  
- Requests for authors: provide formal probabilistic model and derivation of Eq.(1); exact algorithm and complexity for computing θV,Q,A without materializing C3; full fair comparisons (open-ended/multiple-choice, per-type breakdowns) and detailed ablations removing ternary/pairwise terms and varying mean-field iterations.  
- Pros summary: interesting higher-order attention framing, simple modules, compatible with sketching, informative visualizations.  
- Cons summary: weak probabilistic grounding, likely prohibitive ternary complexity, incomplete/unfair evaluation, incremental novelty.  
- Recommendation: Reject (borderline); promising idea but needs rigorous formulation, efficient algorithms, and comprehensive fair validation.
