- Proposes "high-order attention" for multimodal VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials combined via a learned linear-weighted softmax (single mean-field-like update).
- Uses pairwise inner-product similarities and ternary triple correlations, then fuses attended representations with compact bilinear/trilinear pooling (MCB/MCT).
- Evaluated on multiple-choice VQA with claimed SOTA for the MC setting, qualitative visualizations, ablations, and public code.

Strengths
- Clear motivation to model higher-order cross-modal dependencies in a unified attention framework.
- Efficient use of tensor sketching (MCB/MCT) to avoid exponential growth in fusion parameters.
- Empirical improvements on VQA-MC with modest parameter/training budget and helpful visualizations.
- Simple, extensible architecture that can generalize to more modalities.

Weaknesses / Concerns
- The "probabilistic/mean-field" interpretation is mostly rhetorical: no formal energy model, derivation, iterative inference, or convergence analysis is provided.
- Ambiguity about na and ternary potential scale: unclear whether C3 is computed over global output space (3k) or per-question 18 choices, conflating settings and feasibility.
- Evaluation limited to multiple-choice VQA; no open-ended results, no extra datasets (e.g., Visual7W), and limited comparisons to strong open-ended baselines.
- Ablation analysis is thin: no quantitative breakdown of unary vs pairwise vs ternary contributions, no study of learned α/β/γ, and no comparison to simpler answer-aware fusion baselines.
- Notational/clarity issues and typos (e.g., aA mislabeled as aV, unclear 1×1 "convolution" on similarity matrices, undefined weight shapes for marginalizations).
- Decision fusion ("2-layer MCB") is under-specified and reported gains lack statistical significance and architectural detail.
- Fairness concerns: 2-modality baseline uses smaller embedding dimensions than some prior work, potentially confounding comparisons.

Quality / Originality / Significance
- Components are technically standard and sound; novelty is moderate—integrating ternary potentials and learned potential weighting into one framework.
- Probabilistic justification is weak and computational details for ternary attention need clarification.
- Moderate significance contingent on broader evaluation and stronger ablations to demonstrate robustness and practical benefit.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a formal energy-based model and derived mean-field updates, or drop the MF framing and present method as learned potential weighting.
- Explicitly clarify na and the ternary attention scope; include tensor shapes, compute/memory costs, and pseudo-code for C2/C3 construction and marginalization.
- Add targeted ablations: remove ternary/pairwise, vary α/β/γ, compare answer-aware attention vs answer-aware fusion only, and test multi-iteration updates.
- Evaluate on open-ended VQA and at least one additional dataset, compare to baselines that don't use answer choices, and report statistical significance across runs.
- Detail the "2-layer MCB" architecture and fix notation/typos.

Minor issues
- Eq. for attended modalities mislabels aA as aV; some references missing venue details.

Recommendation
- Weak accept, conditional on clarifying modeling/inference, computational details for ternary attention, and providing stronger ablations.
