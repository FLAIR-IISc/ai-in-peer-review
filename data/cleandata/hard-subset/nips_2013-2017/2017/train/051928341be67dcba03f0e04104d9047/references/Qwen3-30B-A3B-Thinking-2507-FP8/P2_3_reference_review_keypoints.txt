- Proposal: Introduces a novel attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary (high-order) correlations across image, question, and answer modalities using a probabilistic mean-field framework; reports state-of-the-art VQA results.
- Originality/Theoretical contribution: Identifies a gap in attention literature and presents a novel, well-motivated mathematical formulation for high-order multimodal interactions.
- Technical quality: Method is sound, clearly explained, and implemented elegantly with standard deep learning components and rigorous experimental design.
- Significance: Achieves strong VQA performance with fewer parameters, is generalizable to other multimodal tasks, and improves interpretability via qualitative analyses.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and detailed enough for reproducibility with illustrative figures.
- Weakness — justification for high-order: Insufficient evidence that ternary interactions are necessary—no ablation quantifying unary/pairwise/ternary contributions.
- Weakness — comparison to recent work: Fails to compare against other higher-order attention methods (e.g., Li et al. 2017), weakening the novelty claim.
- Weakness — ablation studies: Lacks component-wise ablations to show which parts drive the performance gains.
- Weakness — computational complexity: Limited discussion of scaling and training-time tradeoffs as modalities increase.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—novel, well-executed idea with substantial contribution; minor concerns can be addressed in revision.
- Final assessment: Compelling advance for modeling high-order attention in VQA, solid empirical and theoretical work; overall score 8/10 (strong accept).
