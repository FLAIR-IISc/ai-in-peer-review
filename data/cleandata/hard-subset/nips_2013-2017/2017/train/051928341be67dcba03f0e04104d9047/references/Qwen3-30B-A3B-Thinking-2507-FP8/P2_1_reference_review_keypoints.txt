- Proposes "High-Order Attention Models" that model high-order correlations between multiple modalities for VQA.  
- Formulates attention probabilistically with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and uses mean-field inference for marginals.  
- Applies the method to VQA and reports state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical derivations and a well-structured approach.  
- Experimental setup includes appropriate baselines and provides quantitative and qualitative evaluations with attention visualizations.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and gives sufficient implementation details for reproduction.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent VQA methods (post-2017), weakening the state-of-the-art claim.  
- Missing a thorough ablation study to quantify the contribution of unary, pairwise, and ternary components.  
- Does not discuss computational complexity or practical costs of the ternary (3D tensor) potential.  
- Provides limited analysis of why three-modality modeling outperforms two-modality alternatives.  
- Original contribution: meaningful extension of attention mechanisms to explicitly model high-order multimodal interactions.  
- Significant potential impact on multimodal learning and applicability beyond VQA (e.g., captioning, visual reasoning).  
- Strengths: well-motivated, probabilistic formulation, SOTA results, qualitative analysis, and theoretical generalizability.  
- Weaknesses: limited recent comparisons, lack of ablation and complexity analysis, and insufficient mechanistic explanation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add recent comparisons, detailed ablation studies, and a discussion of ternary complexity.
