- Summary: Proposes high-order attention (unary, pairwise, ternary potentials) with mean-field inference for VQA and claims state-of-the-art results with a ~40M-parameter model.

- Strength 1: Offers a clear probabilistic, high-order attention framework that structures multi-modal interactions beyond pairwise attention.

- Strength 2: Shows competitive empirical gains (≈0.75% over MCT) and provides qualitative analyses that improve interpretability.

- Strength 3: Presents a clear mathematical formulation and sufficiently detailed implementation for reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Novelty is questionable since prior work already explores multi-modal attention; ternary potential and mean-field use are incremental extensions.

- Weakness 2: Lacks ablation studies isolating unary/pairwise/ternary contributions, so the source of the modest improvement is unclear.

- Weakness 3: Claims of state-of-the-art are misleading due to missing comparisons with more recent VQA models and unremarkable parameter/training-time advantages.

- Weakness 4: Provides weak justification that ternary (answer-involving) attention is necessary; qualitative examples do not demonstrate a unique benefit over pairwise models.

- Weakness 5: Omits discussion of computational complexity; ternary potentials imply potentially prohibitive O(n_v × n_q × n_a) cost.

- Overall Assessment: Technically sound and interesting but incremental; limited significance due to modest gains and insufficient evidence that high-order attention drives improvements.

- Recommendation: Weak reject — accept only after stronger ablation studies and comparisons to recent SOTA work, otherwise suited to a less selective venue.
