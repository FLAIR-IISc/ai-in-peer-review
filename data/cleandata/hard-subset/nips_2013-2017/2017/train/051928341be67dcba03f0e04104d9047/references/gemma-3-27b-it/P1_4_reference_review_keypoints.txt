- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that models high-order (unary, pairwise, ternary) correlations between modalities using probabilistic potentials.
- Uses mean-field inference to compute attention probabilities for the high-order potentials.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, validating effectiveness.
- Extends prior multimodal attention and bilinear models by explicitly modeling higher-order interactions up to ternary.
- Framework is claimed to be generally applicable to any number of modalities, not just image–text pairs.
- Clear mathematical formulation and helpful illustrations (e.g., Fig. 3) aid understanding.
- Strong and thorough related-work discussion situates the contribution within existing attention and multimodal literature.
- Comparison between MCT and 2-layer MCB provides useful insight into decision-making unit configurations.
- Complexity and scalability are a concern: high-order potentials and mean-field inference may become costly as modalities increase.
- Parameter count (~40M) is high and ablation studies are not detailed enough to justify complexity.
- Visualizations focus mainly on two modalities; ternary attention effects and deeper failure analysis are lacking.
- Paper omits analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (embedding dimension, mean-field parameters).
- Heavy reliance on MCB/MCT tensor sketching units raises generalizability questions and calls for alternative approaches.
- Limited ablation isolating the contribution of unary vs. pairwise vs. ternary potentials weakens attribution of gains.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and well-written work with significant empirical results, recommended for acceptance despite noted scalability and analysis gaps.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
