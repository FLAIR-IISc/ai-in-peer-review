## Review of "High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism for multi-modal data, specifically targeting Visual Question Answering (VQA). The core idea is to move beyond pairwise attention and learn higher-order correlations between modalities (image, question, and answer). The authors formulate this as a probabilistic model and utilize mean-field inference to compute attention weights. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, showing the benefit of considering these higher-order interactions.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of explicitly modeling and learning high-order correlations between modalities in an attention mechanism is a valuable contribution.  While attention mechanisms are ubiquitous, moving beyond pairwise interactions is a logical and potentially powerful extension.
* **Generality:** The proposed approach isn’t limited to VQA and can, in principle, be applied to any task involving multiple data modalities. This broad applicability is a significant strength.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper reports achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, providing strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* **Clear Formulation:** The use of probabilistic modeling and mean-field inference provides a principled framework for the attention mechanism. The equations presented (Eqs. 1) are relatively clear and facilitate understanding.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing attention mechanisms for both image and text data, positioning the current work effectively within the existing literature.  The authors demonstrate awareness of related approaches (e.g., Lu et al. [15], Xu et al. [25], Fukui et al. [7]).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity & Scalability:** While theoretically elegant, the higher-order interactions introduce significant computational complexity, especially with increasing numbers of modalities. The discussion of tensor sketching (Sec. 3.3) attempts to address this, but its impact on performance and scalability could be further explored.  The parameter count (40M) is reasonable but potentially limiting for more complex scenarios.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The performance likely depends heavily on the learning rates, embedding dimensions, and the specific configuration of the MCB/MCT units. The paper does not provide extensive ablation studies examining the sensitivity of results to these hyperparameters.
* **Limited Ablation Study:**  The paper lacks a thorough ablation study demonstrating the individual contribution of the pairwise and ternary potentials. While they show a comparison between MCT and 2-layer MCB, a more granular breakdown of the impact of each order of correlation would be valuable.  For example, how much does the ternary potential improve over just pairwise?
* **Qualitative Results Could Be More Convincing:** While the qualitative examples (Figs. 5-8) are helpful, the improvement offered by the higher-order attention isn’t always dramatically evident. The failure case in Fig. 8 highlights a potential limitation in the model’s ability to focus on the relevant information.
* **Lack of Discussion on Potential for Overfitting:** Introducing higher-order correlations increases the model's capacity.  The paper mentions dropout, but a more detailed discussion of regularization strategies and their impact on generalization would be beneficial.



**Assessment based on NIPS criteria:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound, and the claims are generally well-supported by experimental results.  The use of a probabilistic framework is rigorous, and the performance improvements are significant.  However, the lack of in-depth ablation studies and discussion of scalability concerns slightly detract from the overall quality.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized, but some sections (e.g., the detailed explanation of the tensor sketching) could benefit from further simplification. The notation is clear, and the concepts are explained reasonably well.
* **Originality:** The idea of high-order attention is novel and offers a distinct contribution to the field.  The authors effectively differentiate their work from existing attention mechanisms and demonstrate its advantages.
* **Significance:**  The results are important and demonstrate a clear advancement in VQA performance. The potential for applying this approach to other multi-modal tasks is also significant.  This work could inspire further research into higher-order interactions in attention mechanisms.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark (VQA).
* Introduces a novel and theoretically sound approach to multi-modal attention.
* Offers a potentially generalizable framework applicable to various tasks.
*  Demonstrates a clear understanding of related work and positions the contribution effectively.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* Limited ablation studies to fully understand the contribution of each component.
* Concerns about scalability and computational complexity with more modalities.
*  Qualitative results are not consistently compelling.
* Lack of detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and overfitting.

**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper with minor revisions.  The novelty and strong empirical results outweigh the weaknesses. I would recommend the authors address the following points in a revised version:

1.  **Expand the ablation study:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the contribution of each order of potential (unary, pairwise, ternary) to performance.
2.  **Discuss scalability:** Provide a more thorough discussion of the computational complexity of the approach and potential strategies for scaling to larger datasets or more modalities.
3.  **Address hyperparameter sensitivity:** Include a brief analysis of the impact of key hyperparameters on performance.
4.  **Strengthen qualitative analysis:** Provide more compelling qualitative examples that clearly illustrate the benefits of higher-order attention.
