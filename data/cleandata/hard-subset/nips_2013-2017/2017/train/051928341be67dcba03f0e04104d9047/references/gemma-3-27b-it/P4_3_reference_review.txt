This paper proposes a high-order attention mechanism for visual question answering (VQA). The core idea is to model not just pairwise interactions between modalities (image and question) but also higher-order correlations, aiming for more expressive attention. The authors implement this using a probabilistic model and mean-field inference, presenting a technically detailed approach with experimental validation on VQA datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results.

The paper is well-structured and provides a comprehensive overview of related work, particularly focusing on attention mechanisms in both image and textual data. The authors carefully position their contribution with respect to existing approaches like Lu et al. [15] and Xu et al. [25], highlighting the limitations of those methods in handling multi-modal interactions and the advantages of their high-order attention model. The mathematical formulation of the attention mechanism is clear and the use of potentials and mean-field inference is well-explained. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating improved performance on the VQA dataset, and the ablation studies (comparing different decision units) are helpful in understanding the impact of different components. The inclusion of qualitative results visualizing the attention maps provides additional insight into the model’s behavior.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The concept of high-order attention, extending beyond pairwise interactions, is a significant contribution. While bilinear models [13] and dual attention mechanisms [19] exist, the explicit modeling of k-order correlations and the proposed mean-field inference approach are novel.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a technically sound approach, with a clear mathematical formulation and a well-defined inference procedure. The use of potentials and mean-field inference is justified and implemented effectively.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of the attention mechanism is clear, and the different components are well-defined. The illustrations (Figs. 3 & 4) are helpful in understanding the concepts.
*   **Significance:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset demonstrates the practical significance of the proposed approach. The method’s potential to extend to any number of modalities is a valuable advantage.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** The mathematical formulation and the detailed explanation of the mean-field inference process might be challenging for readers not familiar with probabilistic modeling. The paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the benefits of high-order attention without delving into the technical details immediately.
*   **Computational Cost:** High-order interactions inherently increase computational complexity. While the paper mentions using tensor sketching to mitigate this, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost and scalability of the approach would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Qualitative Analysis:** While the qualitative results are helpful, they primarily focus on two modalities (image and question). Visualizing the attention involving the answer modality (as proposed in the model) could provide further insight into the model’s behavior.
*   **Failure Case Analysis:** The presented failure case (Fig. 8) highlights a limitation of the model—attending to irrelevant features despite different questions. Further analysis of such failure cases and potential solutions could strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to attention modeling.
*   The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in VQA performance.
*   The concept of high-order attention has the potential to be applied to other multi-modal tasks.
*   The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive overview of related work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The technical complexity might make the paper difficult to understand for some readers.
*   The computational cost of high-order interactions could be a limiting factor.
*   The qualitative analysis could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution to the field of attention modeling. The proposed high-order attention mechanism is technically sound, well-explained, and demonstrates promising results on a challenging task. While the technical complexity and computational cost are potential concerns, the authors have addressed them to some extent. The strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend acceptance.
