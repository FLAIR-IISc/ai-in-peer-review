- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for multi-modal VQA that models beyond pairwise correlations using a probabilistic model and mean-field inference, reporting SOTA results.
- Novelty: explicitly models and learns high-order correlations between modalities, extending standard attention mechanisms.
- Generality: approach is applicable beyond VQA to other multi-modal tasks.
- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
- Clear formulation: principled probabilistic framework with mean-field inference and clear equations.
- Thorough related work: comprehensive review and proper positioning among prior attention methods.
- Complexity & scalability: higher-order interactions introduce significant computational cost; tensor sketching’s effectiveness and scalability need further exploration.
- Parameter count concern: ~40M parameters may limit applicability to more complex scenarios.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance likely depends on learning rates, embedding dims, and MCB/MCT configs, but analysis is lacking.
- Limited ablation study: insufficient breakdown of unary/pairwise/ternary contributions and impact of each order of correlation.
- Qualitative results: examples show some benefit but not consistently convincing; some failure cases highlight focus issues.
- Overfitting/regularization: limited discussion of regularization strategies and potential overfitting despite mention of dropout.
- Quality assessment: technically sound and empirically supported but weakened by lack of deep ablations and scalability discussion.
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written, though tensor sketching section could be simplified.
- Originality and significance: novel concept with significant potential impact for VQA and multi-modal research.
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA results, sound novel approach, generalizability, strong literature context.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablations, scalability/computational concerns, unimpressive qualitative evidence, missing hyperparameter/overfitting analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand ablation studies for unary/pairwise/ternary effects; discuss computational complexity and scaling strategies; analyze hyperparameter sensitivity; provide stronger qualitative examples.
