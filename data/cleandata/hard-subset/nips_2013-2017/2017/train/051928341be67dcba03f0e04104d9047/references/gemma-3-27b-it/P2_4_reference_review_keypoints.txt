- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that models high-order correlations between modalities, moving beyond common pairwise interactions.  
- Framework is general and extensible to any number of data modalities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA benchmark, showing strong empirical results.  
- Technical formulation is clearly explained, including potentials, mean-field inference, and tensor sketching.  
- Visual figures (e.g., Fig. 3) effectively illustrate the attention mechanisms.  
- Compares favorably to relevant baselines (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) and demonstrates improvements.  
- Computational complexity and scalability are concerning, especially for three+ modalities and long training times (40 hours on one GPU).  
- Reliance on tensor sketching raises questions about approximation effects that are not fully analyzed.  
- Ablation study is limited; lacking isolated evaluations of unary/pairwise/ternary contributions.  
- Attention visualizations provide limited insight into why the model attends to specific regions; more analysis of successes and failures is needed.  
- Lower embedding dimensionality (512 vs. common 2048) may limit performance; direct comparisons with larger embeddings are missing.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and logically organized, though some notation could be simplified.  
- The approach is original and significant, with promising applicability to other multimodal tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (strong accept) but urges addressing scalability and expanding ablation analyses in future work.
