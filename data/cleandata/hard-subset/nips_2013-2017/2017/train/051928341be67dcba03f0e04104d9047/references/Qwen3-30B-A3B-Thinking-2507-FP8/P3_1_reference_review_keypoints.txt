- Contribution: Proposes high-order attention modeling (unary, pairwise, ternary potentials) with mean-field inference for VQA, using tensor sketching to make it practical.  
- Claim: Reports state-of-the-art-like results on VQA, attributing gains to ternary (three-modality) attention.  

Strengths:
- Conceptual novelty: principled extension from pairwise to high-order attention in multimodal learning.  
- Presentation: well-structured paper with clear mathematical formulation and intuitive figures.  
- Experiments: thorough quantitative results on standard VQA benchmarks and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Practicality: uses tensor sketching (MCB/MCT) to handle high-order interaction complexity.  
- Related work: comprehensive survey distinguishing their approach from prior pairwise attention models.

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Marginal gains: performance improvements over strong baselines are small, raising questions about practical significance.  
- Ablation missing: lacks thorough ablation studies isolating contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Theoretical justification weak: limited explanation of when and why high-order interactions help or hurt.  
- Comparison gaps: does not compare against the most recent SOTA methods (e.g., Tan et al. [22]).  
- Complexity analysis lacking: insufficient evaluation of training/inference time and memory overhead versus alternatives.  
- Embedding choice unexplained: uses 512-d embeddings vs. common 2048-d without justification, complicating fair comparison.  
- Failure analysis limited: only a single failure case shown, insufficient to understand limitations.

Recommendations:
- Add comprehensive ablation studies to quantify each attention order's contribution.  
- Compare experiments to the latest SOTA VQA methods to support state-of-the-art claims.  
- Provide theoretical discussion on when high-order attention is beneficial and potential drawbacks.  
- Include empirical computational complexity (time/memory) comparisons with baselines.  
- Justify embedding dimensionality choice or run experiments with standard dimensions.  
- Expand failure-case analysis to illustrate limitations.

Overall assessment:
- Technically sound and conceptually meaningful extension of attention mechanisms, but practical significance is limited by marginal gains and insufficient analysis.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (marginal accept) contingent on addressing ablation, comparisons, and analyses.

Pros:
- Novel high-order attention concept, clear exposition, solid experiments, practical tensor-sketch implementation, good baseline comparisons.

Cons:
- Small performance improvements, insufficient ablations, missing recent SOTA comparisons, weak theoretical and computational analysis, unexplained implementation choices.

Final comment:
- Valuable conceptual contribution that could inspire future work, but needs stronger empirical and theoretical analysis to substantiate practical impact.
