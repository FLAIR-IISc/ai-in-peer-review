- Proposes multimodal attention using explicit unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across V, Q, A, combined linearly then softmaxed to produce per-modality attentions.  
- Pairwise potentials use learned similarity C2 = QWq(VWv)^T; ternary uses a 3-way correlation tensor C3; decision uses compact tensor sketching (MCB/MCT).  
- Novelty: explicit high-order attention across modalities and conditioning on answer candidates as a third modality; decision module uses known compact pooling.  
- Core idea is reasonable and well-motivated, but the “mean-field” interpretation is not formally derived or justified.  
- Paper lacks a defined probabilistic model (energy/partition function) to support the mean-field claim.  
- Implementation details are underspecified: shapes and mechanics of the “1×1 convolution” marginalizations and parameter sharing are unclear.  
- Computational/memory complexity of forming C3 is not analyzed; efficient computation strategy and na dependence should be explained.  
- Reliability weights α, β, γ are introduced but not analyzed or reported (no learned weight magnitudes shown).  
- Empirical claims of state-of-the-art on VQA multiple-choice lack full numbers, confidence intervals, and controlled baselines.  
- Ablations are weak: only a small MCT vs 2-layer MCB comparison shown; missing ablations isolating unary/pairwise/ternary contributions.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to VQA multiple-choice; open-ended VQA or other datasets are not reported or justified.  
- Paper is readable with useful figures and qualitative maps, but notation slips and key operations (convolutions/marginalizations) need clearer description.  
- Originality is moderate: extension to k-th order attention and including answers as modality is meaningful, but decision module and bilinear/trilinear pooling are not novel.  
- Potential significance if gains are robust, since the framework generalizes to more modalities, but current empirical evidence is limited.  
- Pros: clear intuitive high-order attention framework, scalable decision via compact pooling, compelling visualizations, and available code.  
- Cons: unsupported mean-field claim, missing implementation/complexity details, limited ablations, unclear contribution of ternary potentials, and narrow evaluation.  
- Suggestions: derive/formalize mean-field updates or remove the claim; detail tensor shapes, conv implementations, and complexity; report learned α/β/γ and stronger ablations; control for backbone and embedding size; evaluate on additional datasets.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—promising idea but needs clearer derivation and stronger, controlled experiments to substantiate impact.
