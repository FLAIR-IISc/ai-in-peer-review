- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling pairwise and ternary (and higher) correlations via probabilistic potentials and mean-field inference for VQA.  
- Claims general applicability to multiple modalities beyond two-modality attention models.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art empirical results on VQA benchmarks.  
- Provides a detailed mathematical formulation and clear explanation of model components.  
- Includes ablation studies comparing MCT and 2-layer MCB, showing 2-layer MCB performs better.  
- Introduces additional model complexity, mitigated partly by tensor sketching, but overall more intricate than simpler attention methods.  
- Lacks convincing justification that the extra complexity yields sufficient benefit over simpler bilinear models.  
- Hyperparameter tuning is important but sensitivity analysis is missing, raising concerns about robustness and generalizability.  
- Visualizations focus mainly on pairwise attention; ternary/higher-order attention visualizations are limited and unconvincing.  
- Comparisons to baselines omit some recent transformer-based and state-of-the-art VQA models.  
- Failure case analysis is superficial and does not explain causes or remedies.  
- Quality: technically sound with reasonable complexity management, but weakened by missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, though tensor sketching and many equations can be overwhelming.  
- Originality: novel extension of bilinear pooling to explicit high-order correlations using probabilistic potentials.  
- Significance: meaningful advance with strong VQA results and potential multimodal applications, but practical benefits vs. complexity need more study.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, state-of-the-art results, and potentially generalizable framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: model complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity, insufficient visualization/ablation for high-order benefits, and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: expand hyperparameter sensitivity analysis; provide detailed ternary attention visualizations; compare to recent transformer-based VQA baselines; deepen failure-case analysis.
