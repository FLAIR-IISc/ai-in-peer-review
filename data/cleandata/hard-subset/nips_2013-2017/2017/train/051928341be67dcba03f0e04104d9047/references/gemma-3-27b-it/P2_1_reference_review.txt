## Review of High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism based on high-order correlations between data modalities, aiming to address limitations of existing attention models in capturing complex relationships and being task-specific. The authors demonstrate its effectiveness on the Visual Question Answering (VQA) task, achieving state-of-the-art results. The core idea is to model pairwise and ternary (and potentially higher-order) interactions between visual, textual, and answer representations using probabilistic potentials and mean-field inference.  The paper provides a detailed mathematical formulation and experimental validation on VQA datasets.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of explicitly modeling high-order correlations between modalities using probabilistic potentials is a novel approach to attention mechanisms. While bilinear pooling (e.g., [13]) captures pairwise interactions, extending this to higher orders provides a more expressive capacity.
* **Generality:** The proposed framework is claimed to be generally applicable to multiple modalities, which is a significant advantage over task-specific or two-modality focused attention mechanisms.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the VQA task, validating the effectiveness of their approach.
* **Detailed Explanation:** The paper provides a thorough mathematical description of the proposed model, making it relatively easy to understand the key components and their interactions.
* **Ablation studies:** the authors compare the MCT and 2-layer MCB unit choices and show better results for 2-layer MCB.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The proposed model introduces additional complexity compared to simpler attention mechanisms. While the authors attempt to mitigate this with tensor sketching, the overall model remains relatively intricate.  The benefit of this complexity (vs. a simpler bilinear model, for example) isn’t entirely convincing.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The authors mention the importance of tuning hyperparameters, but don't provide a detailed analysis of sensitivity to these parameters. This raises concerns about the robustness and generalizability of the model.
* **Limited Visualization:** While visualizations are provided, they primarily focus on pairwise attention. More detailed visualization of the ternary (and potential higher-order) attention would provide a better understanding of the model's behavior.  The provided visualizations don't *strongly* demonstrate the advantages of the higher-order attention.
* **Comparison to Strong Baselines:** While comparisons are made to existing work, some of the baselines seem slightly dated. A more comprehensive comparison with recent, state-of-the-art VQA models, including those utilizing transformers, would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Failure Case Analysis:** The presented failure case, while acknowledged, doesn’t offer much insight into *why* the model failed and how it could be improved.

**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a clear mathematical formulation and strong empirical results. The use of tensor sketching is a reasonable approach to manage the complexity of higher-order interactions. However, the lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis weakens the claim of robustness.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, although some sections (e.g., the description of the tensor sketching units) could be further clarified. The notation is consistent, but the sheer number of equations can be overwhelming.
* **Originality:** The idea of explicitly modeling high-order correlations with probabilistic potentials is novel, although it builds on existing work in bilinear pooling and attention mechanisms. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from previous contributions.
* **Significance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on VQA is significant. The potential for applying this high-order attention mechanism to other multimodal tasks is promising. However, the practical benefits of the increased complexity over simpler models need further investigation.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to attention mechanisms.
* It achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark task (VQA).
* The framework is potentially generalizable to other multimodal tasks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The model is complex and potentially sensitive to hyperparameters.
* The benefits of high-order attention are not convincingly demonstrated through visualization or ablation studies.
* The comparison to current state-of-the-art VQA models could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** with minor revisions. The novelty and strong empirical results justify acceptance, despite the concerns about complexity and the need for more thorough analysis. I recommend the authors:

1.  **Expand the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.**
2.  **Provide more detailed visualizations of the ternary attention.**
3.  **Include comparisons to more recent and competitive VQA baselines (e.g., transformer-based models).**
4.  **Provide a more insightful analysis of the failure cases.**
