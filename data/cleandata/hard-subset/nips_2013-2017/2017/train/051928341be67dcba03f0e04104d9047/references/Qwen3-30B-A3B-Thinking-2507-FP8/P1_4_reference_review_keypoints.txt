- Paper proposes a high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, using mean-field inference.
- Applies the method to VQA and reports state-of-the-art results with marginal improvements over prior work.
- Method is technically sound with clear mathematical formalization and reproducible implementation details.
- Experimental setup is thorough with appropriate baselines (Lu et al., Fukui et al.), quantitative results, and qualitative attention visualizations.
- Model is parameter-efficient (≈40M parameters) compared to some baselines (70M+).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains key concepts and figures (e.g., Figure 3, Figures 5–8).
- Probabilistic interpretation of attention is a principled contribution that could generalize beyond VQA.
- Novelty is questionable because the ternary module closely resembles Fukui et al.’s MCT and other multimodal pooling methods.
- Improvement over prior work is marginal (e.g., 64.57% vs. 63.82%) and may be attributable to implementation/embedding-size differences rather than the attention mechanism itself.
- The relation and differentiation from MCB/MCT and other multimodal pooling techniques is not sufficiently clarified.
- Claim of trivial extension to more than three modalities is unsupported by experiments or evidence.
- Tensor sketch and some implementation details need clearer explanation for full reproducibility.
- Qualitative results do not convincingly show unique benefits of high-order attention over pairwise attention; failure cases highlight limitations.
- Recommendation is weak acceptance: solid, well-executed work but novelty overstated and empirical gains modest.
- Overall assessment: a modest, technically sound contribution that should better justify its novelty and demonstrate extensions or clearer advantages over existing methods.
