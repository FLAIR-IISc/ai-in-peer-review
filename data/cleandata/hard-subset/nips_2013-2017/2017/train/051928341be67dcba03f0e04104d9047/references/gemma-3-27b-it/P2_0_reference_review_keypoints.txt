- Proposes a novel attention mechanism that models k-th order correlations across modalities for VQA.  
- Models unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and uses mean-field inference to produce attention probabilities.  
- Claims general applicability to any number of modalities, not just VQA.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA benchmark, outperforming prior methods.  
- Provides clear, detailed technical exposition with helpful figures illustrating the architecture.  
- Ablation comparing MCT and 2-layer MCB shows the decision-making component matters.  
- Introducing higher-order potentials and mean-field inference substantially increases model complexity.  
- Computational cost and practical implications of higher-order correlations are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Ablation studies are limited—insufficient analysis of the specific contribution of ternary potentials versus pairwise terms.  
- Failure case analysis is minimal; the single example shows fixation on visual features irrespective of the question.  
- Embedding dimensionality is smaller (512) than some competitors, potentially limiting performance.  
- Notation becomes dense with multiple potential types; a concise notation table would help readability.  
- Qualitatively sound and well-written, with useful visualizations supporting findings.  
- The approach is original and significant, with potential impact beyond VQA for multimodal tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method, strong empirical results, broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: increased complexity, limited component-level ablation, and insufficient failure-mode analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add deeper ablations, extended failure analysis, and streamline notation.
