- Overall assessment: Novel high-order attention idea with strong VQA results, but needs major revisions due to insufficient analysis — weak accept.  
- Summary/prior work: Proposes unary/pairwise/ternary potentials with mean-field inference to model multimodal correlations for VQA; situates work among existing attention methods.  
- Strength — Novelty: Extends attention to higher-order correlations, offering a principled way to model complex multimodal interactions.  
- Strength — Generality: Presented as a flexible framework potentially applicable beyond VQA to other multimodal tasks.  
- Strength — Empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Strength — Problem framing: Clearly articulates limitations of prior attention methods and motivates the approach; well-written introduction.  
- Weakness — Ablation studies: Lacks thorough ablations to quantify contributions of unary/pairwise/ternary components.  
- Weakness — Baseline comparison: Comparisons are limited and somewhat superficial; more recent competitive VQA models should be included.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: No analysis of robustness to hyperparameter choices or selection methodology.  
- Weakness — Implementation complexity: Tensor sketching and mean-field inference details and computational/scalability costs are insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness — Qualitative analysis: Visualizations are hard to interpret and lack deeper insight or error-case analysis.  
- Weakness — Failure case discussion: The failure case analysis is brief and does not explain causes or guide improvements.  
- Weakness — Minor issues: Some awkward phrasing and formatting problems throughout the paper.  
- Originality: Combining high-order potentials with attention, mean-field inference, and tensor sketching is a novel and significant contribution.  
- Significance: If validated with more rigorous experiments, the method could substantially impact multimodal learning by capturing complex inter-modal relationships.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong empirical performance, novel attention formulation, and addresses known limitations of prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient ablations, limited comparisons, unclear implementation complexity, and missing hyperparameter analysis.  
- Key revision requests: Add extensive ablations, broader baseline comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, detailed implementation and complexity discussion, improved qualitative/error analysis, and deeper failure-case explanation.  
- Conclusion: Promising work that requires major experimental and presentation revisions before strong acceptance.
