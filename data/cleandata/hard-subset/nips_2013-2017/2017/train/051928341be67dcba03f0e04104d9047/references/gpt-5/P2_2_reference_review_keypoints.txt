- Proposes a unified high-order attention framework for multimodal tasks (unary/pairwise/ternary potentials) and applies it to VQA.  
- Potentials are computed via learned projections and bilinear/trilinear correlations; marginals formed by weighted sum + softmax (interpreted as one mean-field step).  
- Fuses attended vectors with compact bilinear/trilinear pooling (MCB/MCT) and reports state-of-the-art on VQA multiple-choice with released code.  
- Builds on co-attention and bilinear pooling literature; novelty is structuring attention as factorized unary/pairwise/ternary potentials and including answers as a modality.  
- Technical methods are standard and reasonable, but the “mean-field” probabilistic framing is heuristic and lacks formal derivation or iterative inference.  
- Empirical claims are promising but incomplete: state-of-the-art claim lacks exact numbers in the text and ablations isolating unary/pairwise/ternary contributions are missing.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to VQA multiple-choice; no open-ended results or tests on other datasets (e.g., VQA v2, Visual7W).  
- Ternary correlation C3 appears computationally heavy (nv × nq × na); paper does not clearly analyze complexity or practical implementation details.  
- Ambiguity about na (answer candidates = 18 vs full answer vocabulary = 3000) raises tractability and interpretation concerns.  
- Reproducibility is aided by released code and many training details, but some notation/specification gaps remain.  
- Clarity is generally good but plagued by inconsistent notation, typos, and unclear definitions (e.g., aV, wiv/wiq, α/β/γ dimensions and sharing).  
- Methodological novelty is moderate: clean unifying perspective but relies on existing bilinear/trilinear interactions and pooling techniques.  
- Potential significance is high if results hold, but limited evaluation, missing stronger baselines, and sparse ablations reduce broader impact.  
- Strengths: modular formulation, simple implementation with 1×1 convs and correlation tensors, strong reported performance with fewer parameters, qualitative visualizations, and code release.  
- Weaknesses: superficial probabilistic justification, unclear scalability of ternary terms, limited evaluation, insufficient ablations, and notational issues.  
- Key questions for authors: provide exact test-dev/test-std numbers and open-ended/VQA v2 results; clarify how C3 is computed and whether na=18; specify α/β/γ types and mean-field iterations; ablate unary/pairwise/ternary effects and report latency/memory; compare to MLB/MFB and stacked attention under same backbone.  
- Verdict: Weak accept conditional on clarifying quantitative results and scalability; idea is reasonable and empirically promising but needs clearer exposition and stronger ablations.
