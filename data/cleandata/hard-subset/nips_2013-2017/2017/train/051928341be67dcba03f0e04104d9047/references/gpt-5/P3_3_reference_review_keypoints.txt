- Proposes high-order attention for multimodal reasoning with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over (V,Q,A) and obtains attentions via a single learned mean-field–style update.  
- Builds pairwise and ternary interactions from inner-product correlations (C2, C3) between modality-specific embeddings.  
- Uses compact bilinear (MCB) and trilinear (MCT) pooling via tensor sketching for decision fusion to keep computation tractable.  
- Instantiated on VQA (multiple-choice), reports state-of-the-art MC performance with fewer parameters and provides attention visualizations and code.  
- Situates among co-attention, bilinear/trilinear fusion, structured attention, and iterative memory literature; novelty is explicit higher-order potentials and ternary V–Q–A attention.  
- Conceptual clarity in decomposing attention into unary/pairwise/ternary contributions and marginalizing them to form attentions.  
- Ternary attention (including answers-as-input) naturally extends co-attention and appears beneficial for multiple-choice VQA.  
- Sensible architectural choices: 1×1 learned linear maps, inner-product correlations, and tensor sketching to avoid blow-up in fusion.  
- Empirical gains achieved with smaller parameter budget and useful qualitative, question-dependent attention visualizations.  
- Reproducibility aided by code release.  
- Probabilistic/mean-field framing is underspecified and lacks a formal factor graph, variational objective, or derivation of fixed-point updates.  
- Attention-stage computational complexity for high-order potentials is not analyzed; Ck scales multiplicatively and becomes prohibitive beyond three modalities or large answer sets.  
- Experimental evidence is incomplete: missing per-type (Yes/No, Number, Other) breakdowns, open-ended results, and controlled comparisons with matched visual backbones.  
- Missing strong baseline comparisons (MLB/MUTAN/SAN/DAN) and controlled single-stream baselines under the same features and answer spaces.  
- Ablations are limited; need separate tests for unary/pairwise/ternary contributions, multiple mean-field iterations, normalization choices, effect of A-as-input, and MCT vs multi-layer MCB.  
- Notational inconsistencies, typos, unclear figure annotations, and ambiguous descriptions (e.g., 1×1 convs, form of α/β/γ).  
- Fairness concerns: parameter-count comparisons should control for feature extractors, answer space size, and augmentations.  
- Technical quality plausible but probabilistic justification weak and scalability to k>3 incomplete.  
- Originality moderate: higher-order/ternary attention is a useful perspective but partly incremental; “mean-field” feels like rebranding without rigorous grounding.  
- Significance conditional: could inform future multimodal models if gains hold under controlled settings, but practical impact limited by scalability concerns.  
- Key suggestions: provide a precise probabilistic/variational derivation or drop mean-field claim, add comprehensive ablations and per-type metrics, run apples-to-apples baselines, analyze C2/C3 complexity, clarify α/β/γ, and fix notation/figure issues.  
- Pros: clear modularization of potentials, effective ternary attention for MC VQA, efficient fusion via sketching, and code availability.  
- Cons: weak probabilistic justification, scalability and complexity concerns, missing strong baselines and detailed ablations, and clarity/reproducibility issues.  
- Overall assessment: promising idea with interesting empirical results but requires stronger experimental validation and clearer, more rigorous presentation; reviewer leans borderline/weak accept if fixes are made, otherwise weak reject.
