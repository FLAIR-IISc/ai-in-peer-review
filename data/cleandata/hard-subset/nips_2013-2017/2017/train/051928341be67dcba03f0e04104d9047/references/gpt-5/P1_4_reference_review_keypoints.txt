- Proposes a probabilistic formulation of multimodal attention using learned unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answer (A), with attention computed via a single mean-field–like linear combination + softmax.
- Computes pairwise potentials via bilinear (similarity) correlations and ternary potentials via trilinear correlations (3D tensor), marginalizing higher-order interactions to get per-modality attention.
- Uses Tensor Sketch–based pooling (MCB for pairwise, MCT for trilinear) to fuse attended vectors for classification.
- Evaluated on multiple-choice VQA, reporting state-of-the-art accuracy with fewer parameters than prior work and providing ablations and qualitative attention visualizations.
- Positions work relative to co-attention, bilinear pooling, stacked attention, and structured attention; novelty is explicit potential decomposition and including answer modality with trilinear attention.
- Strength: clear, general k-order multimodal attention framework with interpretable unary/pairwise/ternary decomposition and learnable mixture weights.
- Strength: jointly models correlations among V, Q, and A—natural for multiple-choice VQA and empirically helpful.
- Strength: solid engineering—detailed embeddings, training recipe, code release, ablations, and qualitative analyses.
- Strength: competitive performance and parameter efficiency; training time and hyperparameters provided.
- Weakness: “mean-field”/probabilistic claim is under-justified—no explicit energy/factor graph or iterative inference shown; method may be essentially a learned mixture of attention signals.
- Weakness: computational/memory complexity of ternary C3 (O(nv·nq·na)) not addressed; approach appears constrained to multiple-choice scenarios and doesn’t scale to large answer vocabularies.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation incomplete—missing results on open-ended VQA, VQA v2, Visual7W and comparisons to strong bilinear baselines (MLB/MFB/MUTAN); lacks ablations toggling unary/pairwise/ternary contributions.
- Weakness: two-layer MCB “more expressive” variant is ad hoc and needs clearer motivation and analysis.
- Weakness: clarity issues—heavy notation, typos (e.g., duplicated aV/aA), terse mean-field description, and insufficient complexity discussion.
- Quality: technically sound as a deep learning architecture using standard building blocks, but probabilistic framing and state-of-the-art claims require stronger baselines and rigor.
- Originality: moderate—useful consolidation/extension of prior ideas; core primitives (bilinear/trilinear, MCB/MCT) are known.
- Significance: potentially useful for multimodal tasks with candidate inputs (MC VQA), but tempered by limited evaluation and scalability restrictions.
- Reproducibility: good—implementation details, hyperparameters, and code link provided; additional ablations would further help.
- Suggested improvements: provide a concrete CRF/factor-graph derivation or tone down probabilistic claims; add complexity/memory analysis for C2/C3 and scalable computation details.
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments (open-ended VQA, VQA v2, Visual7W), compare to MLB/MFB/MUTAN under matched features, and include ablations isolating unary/pairwise/ternary and reporting learned mixture weights.
- Suggested improvements: clarify and motivate two-layer MCB or consider gated/residual fusion, and fix notational typos and figure captions.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (6); confidence 0.7.
