- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for multimodal tasks that models unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Uses a probabilistic framework with mean-field inference to compute marginal attention probabilities.  
- Applies the method to VQA and reports state-of-the-art quantitative results and meaningful qualitative attention visualizations.  
- Extends prior pairwise attention work by explicitly modeling interactions across three modalities (image, question, answer).  
- Technical formulation is sound, with clear math and implementation details, including tensor sketching (MCB/MCT) to handle computation.  
- Lacks a thorough ablation study isolating the contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Paper is generally well-written and reproducible, though it omits explicit clarification of how the answer modality is used during training vs. inference.  
- Novel contribution by addressing higher-order correlations in multimodal attention, filling a gap in prior VQA attention mechanisms.  
- Could better situate the work relative to broader higher-order/self-attention developments (e.g., transformers).  
- Significance demonstrated via SOTA VQA results and easy integration into existing frameworks, but evaluation is limited to VQA with three modalities.  
- Unclear whether high-order attention is strictly necessary for the observed performance gains without additional analyses.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, strong theoretical grounding, SOTA performance, interpretable visualizations, and practical simplicity.  
- Weaknesses: missing ablation, limited comparison to recent methods beyond 2017, and limited exploration of applications beyond VQA.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; add ablation experiments and discuss relation to recent higher-order attention work.
