- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across modalities and uses mean-field inference to compute attention.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset while using fewer parameters (~40M) than competing approaches.
- Formulates attention in a probabilistic framework that naturally extends to any number of modalities, unlike most prior two-modality methods.
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and mathematically clear with strong visualizations (Figures 3–8).
- Lacks ablation studies to quantify the contribution of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials individually.
- Does not compare against influential recent baselines such as Anderson et al. (2017) "Bottom-Up and Top-Down" attention.
- Choice of using only the top 3000 most frequent answers (covering 91% of training answers) is not well justified and may bias results.
- Explanation of ternary potential computation (Section 3.2.3) is confusing and needs clarification on how the C3 tensor produces modality attention.
- Missing implementation details: specific LSTM architecture for question embedding and hyperparameters for MCB/MCT units.
- Insufficient detail on the 2-modality baseline implementation and why it underperforms compared to the 3-modality model.
- Originality is strong: high-order attention modeling is a novel contribution compared to pairwise-focused prior work.
- Significance is high: method addresses an important limitation in multimodal attention and is potentially applicable beyond VQA.
- Provides qualitative evidence that attention focuses on relevant image regions and question words, aiding interpretability.
- Strengths: clear motivation, good mathematical development, compact model size, strong empirical results, and extensibility to multiple modalities.
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons, missing ablations, unclear explanations in parts, and limited justification for dataset preprocessing choices.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address evaluation and clarity shortcomings.
- Specific suggested revisions: add ablation studies, compare with Bottom-Up/Top-Down, justify top-3000 answer cutoff, detail 2-modality setup, clarify ternary computations (with an example), and discuss computational complexity and related recent work.
