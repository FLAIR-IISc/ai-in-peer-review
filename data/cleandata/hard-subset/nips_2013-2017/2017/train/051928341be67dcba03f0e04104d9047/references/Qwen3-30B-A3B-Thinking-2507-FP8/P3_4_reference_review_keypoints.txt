- Proposal: novel probabilistic high-order attention for VQA modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with mean-field inference and MCT/MCB for decision making.  
- Claimed result: modest SOTA improvement (64.57% vs. 63.82% on val).  
- Strength: clear, principled probabilistic formulation of attention beyond ad-hoc methods.  
- Strength: effective attention visualizations demonstrating interpretability.  
- Strength: theoretical grounding via unary/pairwise/ternary potentials.  
- Weakness: reported performance gains are marginal and may not justify added complexity.  
- Weakness: no ablation studies to show the contribution of higher-order (ternary) terms.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit recent transformer-based and more advanced VQA baselines.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why higher-order correlations help VQA.  
- Weakness: computational complexity not analyzed—ternary interactions scale cubically (e.g., 196^3 interactions).  
- Overall: interesting idea but insufficient empirical/theoretical evidence; extensibility claim unsupported.  
- Requested improvements: add ablation studies, compare to recent SOTA, provide theoretical motivation, and analyze computational cost/efficiency.  
- Recommendation: Weak Reject.
