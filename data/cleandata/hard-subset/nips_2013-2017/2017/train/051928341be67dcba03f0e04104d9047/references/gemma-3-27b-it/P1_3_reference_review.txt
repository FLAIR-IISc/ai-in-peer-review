## NIPS 2017 Review: High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel attention mechanism based on high-order correlations between multiple data modalities. The authors argue that existing attention mechanisms often lack the ability to effectively model interactions beyond pairwise relationships. They propose modeling these interactions using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials, and employing mean-field inference to calculate attention probabilities. The approach is demonstrated on the Visual Question Answering (VQA) task, achieving state-of-the-art results. The core idea is to move beyond simply *attending* to relevant features, but to learn *how* different modalities interact to determine relevance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the growing body of research on attention mechanisms for VQA. It acknowledges prior work on image and textual attention (e.g., [1, 15, 25]) and highlights limitations related to explicit modeling of multi-modal interactions. The authors distinguish their approach from recent bilinear models [13] and dual attention mechanisms [19] by emphasizing the generalizability to an arbitrary number of modalities and the use of a probabilistic framework based on potentials and mean-field inference. The comparison to Lu et al. [15] and Xu et al. [25] is particularly relevant, as these are well-known approaches in the VQA literature. The discussion of related work is thorough, though occasionally a bit dense.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of explicitly modeling higher-order correlations between modalities using potentials and mean-field inference is a novel contribution. This offers a principled way to capture complex relationships that are often ignored in simpler attention models.
* **Generality:** The framework is designed to be applicable to any number of modalities, which is a significant advantage over methods tailored to two specific inputs.
* **State-of-the-Art Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing attention mechanisms and motivates the need for a more expressive approach.
* **Detailed Explanation:** The mathematical formulation and explanation of the attention mechanism, including the use of convolutions and tensor sketching, are relatively detailed and well-presented.
* **Qualitative Results:** The visualizations of attention maps (Figs. 5, 6, 7, and 8) provide valuable insights into how the model is functioning and highlight the benefits of considering high-order correlations.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The model is quite complex, involving numerous parameters and operations. This complexity might make it difficult to implement and train, and could potentially lead to overfitting. While the authors address regularization (dropout), the high dimensionality of the learned representations could still pose a challenge.
* **Computational Cost:** The use of mean-field inference and tensor sketching, while addressing the scalability of high-order interactions, introduces additional computational overhead. The authors mention 40 hours of training on a single GPU, which is a substantial amount.
* **Ablation Studies:** While the paper compares against existing methods, the ablation studies exploring the contribution of each component (unary, pairwise, ternary potentials) could be more detailed. A clearer understanding of the individual impact of each potential would be valuable. The comparison of MCT and 2-layer MCB is a good start, but further dissection would be helpful.
* **Failure Case Analysis:** The provided failure case (Fig. 8) is illustrative, but a more comprehensive analysis of failure modes would strengthen the paper. Understanding *why* the model fails in certain scenarios is crucial for identifying areas for improvement.
* **Limited Discussion of Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions specific hyperparameters used in the experiments, but doesn’t discuss the sensitivity of the results to changes in these hyperparameters.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to multi-modal attention.
* It achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark dataset.
* The work has the potential to significantly impact research in VQA and other multi-modal learning tasks.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the model and the computational cost of training may limit its practical applicability.
* The ablation studies could be more thorough.
* The analysis of failure modes is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-modal attention. The proposed high-order attention mechanism is novel, well-motivated, and demonstrably effective. While the complexity and computational cost are concerns, the potential benefits of the approach outweigh these drawbacks. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept**. I believe this paper is worthy of publication at NIPS 2017. I would encourage the authors to address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing more detailed ablation studies and a more comprehensive analysis of failure cases, in a revised version of the manuscript.
