## NIPS 2017 Review: High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel, generally applicable attention mechanism based on high-order correlations between data modalities. The authors argue that existing attention mechanisms are often task-specific and lack explicit modeling of inter-modal relationships. Their approach learns k-th order correlations between k modalities (e.g., image, question, answer) using a probabilistic model and mean-field inference. The method is evaluated on the Visual Question Answering (VQA) task, achieving state-of-the-art performance. The core idea is to move beyond pairwise interactions to capture more complex dependencies between modalities, enhancing attention and ultimately improving VQA accuracy.

**Related Work:**

The paper provides a reasonable, though not exhaustive, review of relevant attention mechanisms in both image and text processing, specifically within the VQA context. It accurately positions its contribution against existing approaches like those of Lu et al. [15] and Xu et al. [25], highlighting their limitations in handling multiple modalities and explicitly modeling higher-order correlations.  The inclusion of more recent work like Kim et al. [13, 14] demonstrates awareness of the evolving landscape of attention models.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of explicitly modeling high-order correlations between modalities using a probabilistic framework is a significant contribution. This moves beyond the common pairwise attention approach and provides a more flexible and potentially powerful mechanism.
* **Generality:** The authors convincingly argue for the general applicability of their method beyond VQA, extending to any task involving multiple data modalities.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset is a strong indicator of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Clear Mathematical Formulation:** The paper provides a relatively clear and concise mathematical description of the attention mechanism, including the mean-field inference process.
* **Detailed Explanation:** The paper meticulously details each component of their method - data embedding, attention mechanisms, and decision making, offering good insight into the design choices.
* **Ablation Studies:** The comparison between MCT and 2-layer MCB demonstrates a thoughtful approach to refining the decision-making component.
* **Qualitative Analysis:** Visualizations in Figures 5-8 provide useful intuition into the attention mechanism’s behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the authors address parameter explosion with tensor sketching, the computational cost of learning and inferring with higher-order correlations is likely substantial. This isn’t fully addressed, and its practical limitations for very high-dimensional data or a large number of modalities aren’t discussed.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The method likely has many hyperparameters (learning rates, embedding dimensions, convolution kernel sizes, sketch parameters).  The sensitivity of performance to these parameters isn't explored in sufficient detail.  A more comprehensive ablation study would be beneficial.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** The qualitative examples (Fig. 8) reveal a failure case.  However, a more systematic error analysis identifying common failure modes and their causes would strengthen the paper.
* **Comparison Depth:** While the authors compare to Lu et al. [15] and Fukui et al. [7], a more direct comparison including implementation details and a more thorough hyperparameter sweep across all methods would be more convincing. The justification for using a lower embedding dimension than state-of-the-art in the comparison seems somewhat weak.
* **Clarity of Motivation:** While the introduction mentions the limitations of current attention mechanisms, the specific scenarios where higher-order interactions *truly* become critical aren’t always clear. More illustrative examples outside of VQA might strengthen the motivation.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** Technically sound and well-supported by experiments. The probabilistic framework seems carefully considered.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, some sections (e.g., the detailed description of the tensor sketching unit) could benefit from further simplification and explanation.
* **Originality:** The high-order attention mechanism with a probabilistic framework is novel and distinguishes this work from previous approaches.
* **Significance:**  The potential for improving performance in multi-modal tasks is significant. The state-of-the-art results on VQA demonstrate practical impact. The generality of the approach expands its potential applications.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and well-motivated approach to attention modeling.
* Strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark (VQA).
* Clear and detailed description of the method.
* Potential for broad applicability.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Computational complexity concerns are not fully addressed.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity is not thoroughly investigated.
* Limited error analysis.
* The depth of comparison with other methods could be improved.

**Overall Score:**

7/10 (Strong Accept)

**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2017.  While there are some minor weaknesses, the novelty, strong results, and potential significance of this work outweigh them. Addressing the concerns regarding computational complexity, hyperparameter sensitivity, and providing a more comprehensive comparison in a revision would further strengthen the paper.  The authors have presented a compelling and potentially impactful contribution to the field of attention mechanisms and multi-modal learning.
