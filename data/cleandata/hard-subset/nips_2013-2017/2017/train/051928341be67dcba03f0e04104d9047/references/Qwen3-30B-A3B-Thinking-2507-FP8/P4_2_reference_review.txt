

# Review of "High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering"

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism that models high-order correlations between multiple data modalities (visual, textual, and answer) for visual question answering (VQA). The authors extend existing attention mechanisms, which typically operate on pairwise interactions between modalities, to model unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials using a probabilistic framework. The attention mechanism employs mean-field inference to compute marginal probabilities, enabling the model to focus on relevant features across multiple modalities. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on VQA datasets, achieving 64.57% accuracy on the VQA test-standard dataset using a 3-modality model.

The paper builds upon recent NIPS work on attention mechanisms for VQA, particularly Lu et al. [15] (NIPS 2016) and Xu et al. [25] (ECCV 2016), which focus on pairwise attention. It also relates to Fukui et al. [7] (arXiv 2016) that introduced Multimodal Compact Bilinear Pooling (MCB) for pairwise interactions. The authors correctly identify that previous approaches primarily handle pairwise interactions and do not generalize to higher-order correlations across multiple modalities, which is the key innovation of their work.

Strengths:
1. Technical Soundness: The probabilistic formulation of attention with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials is well-motivated and theoretically grounded. The mean-field inference approach is clearly explained, and the mathematical details are sound. The authors provide sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.

2. Novelty: The paper successfully extends attention mechanisms beyond pairwise interactions to higher-order correlations, which represents a meaningful extension to the field. While MCB and MCT are used as components (following Fukui et al. [7]), the high-order attention modeling itself is novel and not present in previous NIPS work.

3. Significance: The approach achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA with a relatively simple model (40 million parameters), demonstrating the value of high-order attention. The qualitative analysis in Figures 5-7 clearly shows how the attention mechanism responds to different questions, providing interpretability.

4. Clarity: The paper is well-structured, with clear figures (particularly Figures 3-4) that effectively illustrate the proposed attention mechanism. The mathematical formulations are precise and well-explained.

Weaknesses:
1. The novelty claim could be strengthened by more thorough comparison to recent bilinear approaches (e.g., Kim et al. [13] which uses bilinear models). While the authors mention Kim et al. [13], they don't explicitly contrast their high-order approach with these bilinear methods.

2. The computational complexity of the ternary potential is not discussed in detail, though this is a minor point given the good results.

3. The failure case in Figure 8 is noted but not deeply analyzed. A brief discussion of why the attention mechanism fails in this specific case would strengthen the evaluation.

4. The paper claims the approach is "generally applicable" to any number of modalities, but the evaluation is limited to VQA with three modalities. More discussion of potential applications to other multimodal tasks would be valuable.

Arguments for acceptance:
- The high-order attention mechanism represents a meaningful extension beyond pairwise attention, which is the dominant approach in the literature.
- The approach achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA with a relatively simple model, demonstrating practical significance.
- The probabilistic formulation is theoretically sound and clearly presented.
- The paper provides both quantitative and qualitative evidence supporting the approach's effectiveness.

Arguments against acceptance:
- The novelty is incremental rather than revolutionary, as the core components (MCB/MCT) are adapted from existing work.
- The comparison to bilinear models (Kim et al. [13]) is not sufficiently detailed, which weakens the novelty claim.
- The paper could better demonstrate the generalizability of the approach beyond VQA.

Overall, this is a well-written, technically sound paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the attention mechanism literature. The high-order attention approach extends beyond the pairwise interactions that dominate current VQA systems, and the results demonstrate its practical value. While the novelty is incremental, the paper provides a clear and well-supported extension that advances the state of the art. The quality of the technical content, the thorough evaluation, and the clear presentation make this a strong candidate for NIPS. The paper meets all four criteria: it is technically sound, clearly written, novel in its extension of attention mechanisms, and significant for VQA and potentially other multimodal tasks.
