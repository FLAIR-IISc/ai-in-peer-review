- Overall: Novel, well-motivated extension of attention to high-order correlations; recommend Accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Proposes a general high-order attention model for k modalities framed as a probabilistic model solved with mean-field inference.
- Empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA, validating the approach.
- Novelty & generality: Extending attention to higher-order interactions is novel and scalable to arbitrary modalities.
- Theoretical foundation: Formulation as a probabilistic model with mean-field inference provides solid justification.
- Presentation: Clear explanations and helpful diagrams; thorough related-work review.
- Ablations: Includes comparison between MCT and 2-layer MCB decision units to justify design choices.
- Incremental gain: Performance improvement over prior work is modest; third-order attention gains appear marginal.
- Limited analysis: Paper lacks detailed analysis of when and why third-order attention helps and more qualitative examples.
- Computational cost: Scalability claim is not fully substantiated; tensor sketching and memory/computation overheads are not sufficiently discussed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Impact of key hyperparameters (embedding dimension d, learning rate, dropout) is insufficiently explored.
- Failure analysis: Only one simplistic failure case shown; more comprehensive error analysis is needed.
- Specific question — Eq.(1): Use of separate learnable parameters (αi, βi, γi) for each potential seems arbitrary and needs justification or alternatives.
- Specific question — Embedding dim: Choice of 512-d embeddings seems low for recent VQA models; rationale unclear.
- Fig.7 suggestion: Qualitative comparisons should include baseline attention maps side-by-side for clearer visual evidence.
- MCT vs MCB: Claim that MCB is more expressive needs clearer explanation beyond self-interaction intuition.
- Reviewer requests: Provide deeper analysis of third-order effects, discuss computational costs and scalability, explore hyperparameter sensitivity, expand failure cases, and add side-by-side visual comparisons.
