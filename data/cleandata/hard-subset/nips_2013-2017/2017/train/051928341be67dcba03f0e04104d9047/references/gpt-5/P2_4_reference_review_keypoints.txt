- Proposes multimodal attention that models explicit unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image (V), question (Q), and answer (A), combined linearly and softmaxed (described as a single mean-field iteration).
- Computes pairwise potentials via learned linear projections and inner products (C2) and ternary via a third-order correlation tensor (C3); final decision uses MCB/MCT pooling.
- Claims competitive/state-of-the-art results on VQA multiple-choice with fewer parameters and provides qualitative attention visualizations.
- Novelty: unified, explicitly high-order attention including answers, positioned between co-attention and bilinear pooling lines of work.
- Strength: clear, general conceptual decomposition into unary/pairwise/ternary potentials that is extensible.
- Strength: promising empirical performance on VQA-MC with compact model and sensible qualitative attention maps.
- Strength: practical engineering choice to use tensor sketching (MCB/MCT) to avoid explicit outer-product blow-up.
- Weakness: “mean-field” probabilistic justification is not formalized or rigorously derived from an energy/graphical model.
- Weakness: computational cost of full ternary C3 scales as O(d·nq·nv·na) and is infeasible for open-ended settings (na≈3000); complexity mitigation is not addressed.
- Weakness: comparisons may be unfair (2-modality baseline uses smaller embeddings) and SOTA claims lack complete in-text result tables.
- Weakness: ablations are limited—no systematic quantification of unary vs pairwise vs ternary contributions or learned weights α/β/γ.
- Weakness: narrow evaluation scope—only VQA (MC), no open-ended results, no cross-dataset tests, and no variance/multi-seed reporting.
- Weakness: clarity/notation issues, minor typos, and missing implementation details (nq, variable-length answers, when A is used).
- Quality assessment: technically sound as an attention variant but lacking rigorous theoretical grounding and complexity analysis; empirical validation is promising but incomplete.
- Originality: moderate—extends known pairwise/bi-linear ideas to explicit ternary potentials but is incremental.
- Significance: potentially useful to practitioners if high-order attention consistently helps, but impact limited by insufficient evaluation and analysis.
- Suggestions: provide formal probabilistic model or remove variational claims; add comprehensive ablations (remove terms, vary weights); match baseline embedding dims and show full quantitative tables.
- Suggestions: analyze computational/memory cost of C2/C3 and clarify when ternary is feasible or introduce low-rank/sketching for attention; evaluate on additional datasets and report variance across runs.
- Final recommendation: Weak reject—promising idea but requires stronger theoretical justification, fairer/ broader empirical evaluation, and clarity fixes before acceptance.
