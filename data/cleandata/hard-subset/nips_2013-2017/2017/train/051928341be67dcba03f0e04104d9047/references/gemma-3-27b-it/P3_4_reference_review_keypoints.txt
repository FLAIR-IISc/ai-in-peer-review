- Overall: Novel, well-motivated high-order attention model for VQA with strong empirical results; recommend accept with minor revisions for clarity and experiments.
- Contribution: Extends attention to model k-way (higher-order) correlations across modalities, framed as a probabilistic model solved via mean-field inference.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and generalizes existing attention/bilinear models (e.g., Lu et al., Xu et al., Kim et al.) by explicitly modeling higher-order interactions.
- Empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA benchmark, demonstrating effectiveness.
- Theoretical grounding: Probabilistic formulation and mean-field inference give a solid foundation and claim of generality to any number of modalities.
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written overall but mathematically dense and could use more intuitive explanations.
- System overview: Fig. 2 clearly illustrates embedding, attention, and decision-making components.
- Novelty strength: The extension to higher-order attention is genuinely novel and addresses a key limitation of pairwise-only approaches.
- Generality strength: Approach is argued to be broadly applicable to multiple modalities beyond image and text.
- Related work strength: Thorough and well-situated literature review.
- Complexity issue: Mathematical formulation and mean-field explanations are dense and hard to parse without simpler intuition or examples.
- Ablation gap: Lacks systematic ablation study isolating contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary (higher-order) terms.
- Hyperparameter gap: No discussion of sensitivity or robustness to choices like embedding dimensionality, learning rate, or dropout.
- Qualitative analysis gap: Attention visualizations are limited and not analyzed systematically across question types or failures.
- Decision unit clarity: MCB/MCT units are described cursorily; need more detail on how they mitigate computational costs of high-order correlations.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, strong benchmark results, potentially widely applicable, and well-researched.
- Against acceptance: Method complexity may hinder adoption; insufficient ablations and missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested revision — notation: Simplify mathematical notation and provide more intuitive explanations of mean-field inference.
- Suggested revision — ablations: Run thorough ablation experiments (unary; unary+pairwise; full model) to quantify gains from higher-order terms.
- Suggested revision — hyperparameters: Analyze robustness and sensitivity to key hyperparameters.
- Suggested revision — tensor sketching: Provide a detailed explanation of MCB and MCT and their impact on computational efficiency.
- Suggested revision — qualitative: Expand and systematize attention-map analysis, including comparisons across question types and failure cases.
