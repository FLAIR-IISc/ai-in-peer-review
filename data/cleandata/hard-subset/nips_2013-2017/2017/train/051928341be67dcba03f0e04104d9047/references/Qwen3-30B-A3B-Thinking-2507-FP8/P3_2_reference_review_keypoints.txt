- Paper proposes a high-order attention mechanism (unary, pairwise, ternary potentials) with mean-field inference for VQA, claiming simpler SOTA performance.
- Strength: clearly formulates the limitation of pairwise attention and motivates modeling higher-order multimodal correlations.
- Strength: well-structured mathematical formulation and helpful visualization (Figure 3) of the proposed attention modules.
- Strength: strong qualitative attention visualizations showing modality-dependent attention behavior (Figures 5–7).
- Strength: practical implementation using tensor sketching (MCB/MCT) to keep computation feasible.
- Major weakness: novelty overstated—insufficient distinction from related work such as Kim et al. (Structured Attention), Nam et al. (Dual Attention), and bilinear pooling papers.
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient—compares to only two baselines and omits many recent 2017 VQA methods.
- Major weakness: no ablation study isolating the contribution of ternary (3-modality) attention versus pairwise components.
- Major weakness: lacks comprehensive parameter count and computational cost comparisons with recent methods.
- Methodological concern: claim of mean-field inference is misleading since only a single iteration is used, not full iterative inference.
- Methodological concern: inconsistent/confusing notation for the ternary potential (should depend on all three modalities).
- Methodological concern: no theoretical justification provided for why high-order attention should outperform pairwise attention.
- Scope concern: evaluation limited to VQA despite claims of general applicability to other multimodal tasks.
- Scope concern: no discussion or experiments showing scalability to more than three modalities.
- Recommendation: expand and clarify related work comparisons, explicitly differentiating from Kim et al. and Nam et al.
- Recommendation: run comprehensive experiments—compare with at least 5–6 recent methods, include ablations, and report parameter and complexity analyses.
- Recommendation: fix notation, clarify the mean-field relationship, and provide theoretical motivation for high-order attention.
- Recommendation: demonstrate the approach on at least one additional multimodal task and discuss scaling beyond three modalities.
- Overall assessment: technically interesting and produces compelling visualizations, but novelty is limited and experimental support is inadequate.
- Final recommendation: Reject—encouraged to revise substantially to address novelty, experiments, and methodological clarity before reconsideration.
