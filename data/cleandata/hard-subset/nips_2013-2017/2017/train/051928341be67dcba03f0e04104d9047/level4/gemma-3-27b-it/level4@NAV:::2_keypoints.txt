- Introduces a generalized attention formulation for multimodal VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Core innovation: representing attention as a combination of unary/pairwise/ternary potentials across image, question, and answer.  
- Evaluation on VQA shows incorporating ternary potentials yields performance gains over prior models.  
- Strength: generic attention formulation potentially applicable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: clear, well-written paper with an ablation study that isolates the ternary potential’s contribution.  
- Weakness: notion of attention over the answer modality is unclear since answers are often single words; needs clarification and visualizations.  
- Weakness: main improvements appear driven by ternary potentials; model does not consistently beat existing methods in two-modality settings (except HieCoAtt); requires further discussion and quantitative support.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to models that explicitly use the answer as input (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).  
- Weakness: missing analysis of failure cases to inform future work.  
- Minor errors: line 38 “mechanism” should be “mechanisms”; line 237 claims validation evaluation while Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer still recommends acceptance despite the rebuttal not fully convincing on two-modality performance.  
- Post-rebuttal: rebuttal’s additional quantitative evaluation shows higher correlation of attention maps with human attention, but comparison to MCB is missing.
