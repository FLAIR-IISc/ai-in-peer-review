Overall I think the proposed method is interesting. The results are quite good and the attention maps seem to illustrate these gains might be due to improved attention mechanisms and not simply increased model capacity. I found the writing to be a bit weak and sometimes a bit confusing, though I imagine given more time the authors could improve the submission appropriately. 

**Strengths:**
- Interesting model with good results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
- The shift between unary and pairwise attentions, leading to the final attention mechanism, is compelling. The visualizations demonstrate the interplay between these attention levels.

**Weaknesses:**
- The writing and presentation could be improved for clarity. Certain sections feel jumbled, hindering a smooth understanding of the proposed method.
- While the architecture is more complex than and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [Fukui et al., 2016], the novelty feels incremental. Much of the added complexity stems from extending the intuition of [Fukui et al., 2016] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should more clearly articulate the specific contributions beyond this extension.
- The connection to a probabilistic model, while motivating, is not formally developed. Either a more rigorous formalization is needed, or the language should be adjusted to accurately reflect the motivational nature of this connection.
- Figure 2 is at an inappropriate level of abstraction. It lacks sufficient detail to understand the network’s functionality while also being too complex to easily grasp the overall approach. Simplifying this figure to emphasize the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation would be beneficial.
- Figure 3 is presented but not explicitly referenced in the text.

**Questions for the Authors:**
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equations (1)? Was there a discernible pattern in which modalities favored different potential orders?
- The 2-modalities unary+pairwise model underperforms MCB [Fukui et al., 2016] despite a similar architecture. This result deserves further discussion. Is this related to the switch to MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The comparison between sequential MCB and a single MCT layer for the decision head is intriguing, but no results are presented. Could the authors elaborate on their observations from this experimentation?

**Arguments For Acceptance:**
- The paper presents a novel attention mechanism that demonstrably improves performance on a challenging VQA task.
- The extensive experimental evaluation, including ablation studies and visualizations, provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- The approach is generally applicable and could potentially be extended to other multimodal tasks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- The writing quality needs improvement to enhance clarity and readability.
- The incremental nature of the novelty, relative to prior work [Fukui et al., 2016], might not fully justify acceptance at a competitive conference like NIPS.
- The probabilistic framing lacks a rigorous mathematical foundation.

**References:**
Fukui, Akira, et al. "Multimodal compact bilinear pooling for visual question answering and visual grounding." arXiv preprint arXiv:1606.01847 (2016).
