**General Impression**

The proposed method is promising, and the empirical results are compelling. The provided attention maps lend support to the claim that these performance gains stem from an improved attention mechanism rather than merely an increase in model capacity. However, the clarity of the manuscript could be significantly improved. While the presentation is sometimes confusing, I believe the authors could address these issues and strengthen the submission with additional time for revision.

**Strengths**

*   The model is novel and achieves strong performance on the given tasks.
*   The paper includes a comprehensive set of both quantitative and qualitative experiments.
*   The interplay between the unary, pairwise, and final attention mechanisms is a particularly compelling aspect of the work. It would be a valuable addition to visualize the marginalized trinary attention maps; it was unclear if these were included in the main text or supplementary material.

**Weaknesses**

*   As noted above, the manuscript's presentation can be disjointed, which detracts from the overall clarity and impact of the work.
*   The novelty of the contribution feels somewhat incremental. While the architecture is more intricate and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this complexity appears to extend the core intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders of interaction. I would encourage the authors to more explicitly define their novel contributions beyond this extension.
*   The connection to a probabilistic framework is not rigorously established. As presented, it serves more as a high-level motivation than a formal underpinning. The authors should either formalize this connection more concretely or adjust the language to clarify its role as an analogy.
*   Figure 2 is presented at an awkward level of abstraction; it lacks the detail needed to fully grasp the network's mechanics, yet it is not abstract enough to provide a clear high-level overview. A simplified figure that more directly emphasizes the generation of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials would be more effective.
*   I was unable to find a reference to Figure 3 within the text.

**Questions and Clarifications**

*   Regarding Equation (1), what were the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials? An analysis of these values could reveal whether different modalities learn to prioritize different potential orders.
*   It is surprising that the "2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise" model underperforms the MCB [7] baseline, given their architectural similarities. The paper would benefit from a discussion on this point. Could the authors offer any intuition for this result? Is it potentially related to the substitution of MCB with MCT modules for the decision computation?
*   The discussion on using sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was intriguing, but supporting results were not provided. Could the authors elaborate on the empirical observations from this comparison?
