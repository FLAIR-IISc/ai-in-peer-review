- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for multimodal VQA that combines learned unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials into attention via a softmax (framed as a single mean-field-like iteration).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on the VQA benchmark, showing benefits from higher-order interactions.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with clear quantitative comparisons and useful ablation between 2-modality and 3-modality models.  
- Strong qualitative visualizations (attention maps) that show how combined potentials better ground questions in relevant image regions.  
- Paper clarity is weak: model description, relationship between potentials, mean-field inference, and attention computation is jumbled and hard to follow.  
- Figure 2 is at an awkward abstraction level and should be simplified to better show generation and combination of unary/pairwise/ternary potentials.  
- Contribution positioning is unclear: need to more precisely differentiate the proposed framework from prior multimodal pooling/attention work.  
- Probabilistic/mean-field framing is underformalized; the energy function is not specified, making the connection feel like an analogy rather than a rigorous justification.  
- Suggest reporting learned potential coefficients (α, β, γ) to show how modalities rely on different orders of potentials.  
- Request clarification on why the 2-modality variant underperforms MCB (possible causes: lower embedding dimension or other architectural differences).  
- Ask for more analysis comparing decision heads (2-layer MCB vs. MCT) to explain why the former is more expressive for VQA.  
- Recommend visualizing the ternary potential’s influence or illustrating how the answer modality affects final image-question attention.
