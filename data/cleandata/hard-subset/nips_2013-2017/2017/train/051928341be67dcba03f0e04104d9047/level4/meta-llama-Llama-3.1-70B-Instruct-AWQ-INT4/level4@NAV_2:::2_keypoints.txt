- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for image, question, and answer.
- Evaluated on the VQA dataset and outperforms prior models when ternary potentials are included.
- Builds on hierarchical attention and MCB pooling but extends to model attention across three modalities.
- Strength: generic attention formulation that could generalize beyond a single task.
- Strength: ablation study demonstrates the contribution of ternary potentials.
- Strength: paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: unclear what "attention over answers" means given answers are mostly single tokens and no visualizations are provided.
- Weakness: main performance gain appears to come from the ternary potential; this needs further analysis.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- Weakness: no discussion or analysis of failure cases and model limitations.
- Weakness: minor errors/typos (e.g., L38 "mechanism"→"mechanisms") and inconsistency about evaluation splits (L237 vs. Table 1).
- Argument for acceptance: novel, generic multimodal attention framework.
- Argument for acceptance: empirical gains with ternary potentials and informative ablation study.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient clarity and evidence regarding answer-attention and the source of improvements.
- Argument against acceptance: missing comparisons to relevant baselines and lack of failure-mode analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept, provided authors address the noted clarifications, comparisons, visualizations, and error corrections.
