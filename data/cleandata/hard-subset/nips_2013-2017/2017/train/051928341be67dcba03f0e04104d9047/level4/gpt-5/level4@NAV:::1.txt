Review- General Impression:
Overall, the proposed approach is compelling. The results are strong, and the attention visualizations suggest the gains likely stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. The writing could be clearer and is occasionally confusing, though with more time the authors could refine the presentation appropriately.

Strengths:
- Engaging model with solid performance.
- Comprehensive qualitative and quantitative evaluation.
- I’m quite impressed by the progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention. That said, I would have liked to see marginalized trinary attention maps as well. Were these included in the main text or the supplement and I missed them?

Weaknesses:
- As noted, the writing/presentation feels somewhat disorganized at times.
- The novelty appears somewhat limited. While the architecture is more elaborate than—and outperforms—the MCB for VQA model [7], much of the added complexity seems to extend the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not necessarily a major issue, but I recommend clarifying these contributions (and any I may have overlooked).
- The probabilistic connection is not convincingly established. It reads more as motivation than a formal derivation, which is acceptable, but the authors should either make this link more rigorous or adjust the language accordingly.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of abstraction: it is neither detailed enough to convey the network’s operation nor abstract enough to clearly capture the overall idea. I suggest simplifying it to highlight the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 does not seem to be referenced, unless I missed it.

Some things I'm curious about:
- What were the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting to see whether different modalities leverage different potential orders.
- It is notable that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite the architectural similarity. There was little discussion of this; do the authors have intuition for this outcome? Could it be related to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion comparing sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was interesting, but no results were reported. Could the authors elaborate on what was observed?
