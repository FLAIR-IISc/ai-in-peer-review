- Introduces a generalizable multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for image, question, and answer in VQA.  
- Validated on the VQA dataset with improved performance, particularly due to the ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Proposes a generic, task-agnostic attention formulation potentially applicable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: Provides an ablation study showing the isolated impact of the ternary potential.  
- Strength: Manuscript is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: "Attention over answers" is not well-motivated or visualized, leaving its role unclear given mostly single-word answers.  
- Weakness: Without the ternary potential (two-modality setup), the model does not consistently outperform baselines, needing more insight.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparison should include methods that use answer information (e.g., Jabri et al. ECCV 2016).  
- Weakness: Paper lacks analysis of failure cases to show where the model fails and why.  
- Weakness: Minor issues/typos—"mechanism" should be plural, and mismatch between reported evaluation splits (validation vs. test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and strong empirical gains from three-modality attention.  
- Post-rebuttal: New analysis shows higher correlation of model attention maps with human gaze than HieCoAtt, strengthening the paper.  
- Follow-up question: Request clarification on reported MCT vs. MCB performance numbers, which appear swapped or inconsistent with Table 1.
