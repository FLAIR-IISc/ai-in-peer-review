- Paper proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, achieving SOTA when ternary potentials are used.  
- Strength: Generic attention framework is valuable and potentially generalizes beyond VQA if demonstrated across tasks.  
- Strength: Ablation study clearly isolates the impact of ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Weakness: Meaning and role of "attention over answers" is unclear given most answers are single words and no visualizations of answer attention are provided.  
- Weakness: Performance gains mainly come from ternary potentials; authors must explain why two-modality version does not outperform existing approaches.  
- Weakness: Should compare against models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) since ternary potentials drive improvements.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of failure cases to guide future work.  
- Weakness: Typos/errors—L38 "mechanism"→"mechanisms"; L237 claims validation evaluation though Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std.  
- Post-rebuttal: Despite unsatisfactory quantitative response on two-modality concern, reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework and strong 3-modality results.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reported correlation between proposed attention maps and human attention is promising and reportedly better than HieCoAtt, but correlation for other SOTA models (e.g., MCB) is not reported.  
- Clarification requested: Confusion over reported MCB vs. MCT percentages (paper/Table 1 reports MCB 68.6% but rebuttal cites MCB 68.4% and 2-layer MCB 69.4%)—are these numbers swapped or misstated?
