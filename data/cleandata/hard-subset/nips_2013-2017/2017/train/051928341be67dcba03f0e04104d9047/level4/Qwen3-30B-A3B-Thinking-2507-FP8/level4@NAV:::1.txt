

General Impression:  
The proposed method presents an interesting approach with strong empirical results. The attention maps effectively suggest that performance gains stem from refined attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the exposition requires strengthening in places, and with additional revision, the authors could significantly improve the submission.  

Strengths:  
- The model is compelling and yields high-quality results.  
- Comprehensive qualitative and quantitative evaluations are provided.  
- The transition between unary/pairwise attention and the final attention mechanism is particularly compelling; however, marginalized trinary attention maps would further strengthen the analysis (please confirm their presence in the main text or supplement).  

Weaknesses:  
- The writing and presentation occasionally lack clarity and flow.  
- The novelty is somewhat constrained; while the architecture is more complex and outperforms MCB [7], much of the added complexity recasts the core intuition from [7] at higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should explicitly clarify the scope of their contributions.  
- The probabilistic foundation lacks formal grounding, remaining largely motivational. The connection should either be formalized or the language adjusted to reflect this.  
- Figure 2 is overly detailed for its purpose, obscuring the high-level network structure while failing to convey sufficient technical nuance. Simplify to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation.  
- Figure 3 is referenced nowhere in the main text.  

Some Curiosities:  
- What are the learned linear coefficients for combining marginalized potentials in equation (1)? Do different modalities leverage distinct potential orders?  
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite architectural similarities. The authors should address this discrepancy and discuss potential causes (e.g., shifts in decision modules).  
- The comparison of sequential MCB versus single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing, but no results are presented. Please elaborate on observed trends.
