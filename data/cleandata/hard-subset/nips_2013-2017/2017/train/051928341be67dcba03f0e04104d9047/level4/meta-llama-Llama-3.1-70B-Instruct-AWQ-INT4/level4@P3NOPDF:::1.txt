**General Impression**

The proposed method is interesting, and the results are quite good. The attention maps provide valuable insights, suggesting that the gains achieved by the method may be attributed to improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. However, the writing could be improved, and some sections may benefit from clarification. With additional time and revisions, the authors can enhance the submission.

**Strengths**

* The proposed model is interesting, and the results are impressive.
* The authors conducted thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments, providing a comprehensive evaluation of their method.
* The transition between unary, pairwise, and trinary attentions, as well as the final attention, is impressive. However, it would be beneficial to include marginalized trinary attention maps for a more complete understanding. A review of the text and supplement did not reveal these maps; it is possible that they may have been overlooked.

**Weaknesses**

* The writing and presentation are sometimes jumbled, making it challenging to follow the authors' arguments.
* The novelty of the method feels limited. Although the architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of the added complexity is an extension of the intuition presented in [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should clarify their contributions and address any potential overlaps with existing work.
* The probabilistic connection is not well-established. The authors may want to consider either formalizing this connection or adjusting the language to avoid any confusion.
* Figure 2 is at an odd level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. The authors may want to simplify this figure to emphasize the unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation more clearly.
* Figure 3 is not referenced in the text; it is unclear whether this is an oversight or intentional.

**Questions and Suggestions for Future Work**

* The authors may want to provide the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equations (1). It would be interesting to investigate whether different modalities take advantage of different potential orders.
* The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms the MCB [7] despite similarities in architecture. A discussion of this result and potential explanations would be beneficial. Is this related to the swap from MCB to MCT decision computation modules?
* The discussion on using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head is intriguing. However, no results were provided. The authors may want to elaborate on their observations and findings.
