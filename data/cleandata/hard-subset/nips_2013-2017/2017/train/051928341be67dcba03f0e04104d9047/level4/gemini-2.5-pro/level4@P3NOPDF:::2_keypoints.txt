- Introduces a principled energy-based n-modal attention framework (unary, pairwise, ternary) applied to VQA; achieves state-of-the-art with ternary potentials driving gains.
- Generic, modality-agnostic attention framework with potential broad applicability.
- Strong ablation study demonstrating ternary potentials are the main contributor to performance.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presented.
- Role and motivation for "attention over answers" are unclear and lack visualizations.
- Performance improvements appear to come almost exclusively from the ternary potential; 2-modality (image+question) model does not consistently outperform existing methods.
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines that use answer information (e.g., Jabri et al. ECCV 2016).
- Paper lacks qualitative analysis and failure-case discussion.
- Minor typo: "mechanism" should be pluralized to "mechanisms".
- Inconsistency in reported evaluation split: text says validation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.
- Rebuttal added human-attention correlation results showing improvement over HieCoAtt, strengthening the paper.
- Concern about 2-modality performance remains somewhat unresolved but is mitigated by the paper's n-modal focus.
- Final recommendation: Accept.
- Camera-ready note: clarify and correct the reported MCB vs. MCT numbers (possible swap and discrepancy with Table 1).
