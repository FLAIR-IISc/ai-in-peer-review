

General Impression:  
The proposed method is interesting and demonstrates strong results. The attention maps convincingly suggest that performance gains stem from refined attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the writing contains clarity issues that could be addressed with revision.  

Strengths:  
- The model achieves compelling results with thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation.  
- The shift between unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention mechanism is particularly compelling. I note that trinary attention maps (marginalized) are not presented in the main text or supplementary materials—were these omitted intentionally?  

Weaknesses:  
- The presentation contains several clarity issues that distract from the technical contribution.  
- The novelty appears somewhat incremental. While the architecture is more complex than MCB [7] and outperforms it, much of this complexity merely extends [7]’s intuition to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should more clearly delineate the specific contributions beyond this extension.  
- The probabilistic connection lacks formal grounding and reads primarily as motivation. The authors should either formalize this link or adjust the narrative to avoid implying stronger theoretical claims.  
- Figure 2’s abstraction level is misaligned: it lacks sufficient detail to grasp network functionality yet remains too complex to intuitively summarize the approach. Simplify this figure to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation.  
- Figure 3 is referenced nowhere in the main text.  

Key Questions:  
1. What coefficients were learned for combining marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be insightful to know if different modalities leverage distinct potential orders.  
2. The 2-Modality Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite architectural similarities. This result merits discussion—was it related to the shift from MCB’s decision module to MCT?  
3. The comparison between sequential MCB and single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing but lacks results. Please elaborate on observed trade-offs.
