General Impression:
Overall, the proposed method is interesting. The results are strong, and the attention maps suggest that the gains may stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. The writing is somewhat weak and occasionally confusing, although with more time the authors could likely improve the submission.

Strengths:
- Interesting model with strong results.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- I am impressed by the shift between the unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention. However, I would also like to see marginalized trinary attention maps. Were these included in the main text or the supplement and I missed them?

Weaknesses:
- As noted above, the writing/presentation is occasionally jumbled.
- The novelty feels somewhat limited. The architecture is undoubtedly more complex than, and outperforms, the MCB for VQA model [7], but much of this additional complexity appears to extend the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not necessarily a major issue, but the contributions (and any I may have missed) should be clarified.
- The probabilistic connection is not well developed. It does not seem formal enough to serve as more than motivation. The authors should either formalize this connection more rigorously or adjust the language to make its role clear.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of abstraction: it is neither detailed enough to convey the network’s functionality nor abstract enough to succinctly capture the overall approach. I suggest simplifying it to emphasize the generation of unary/pairwise/trinary potentials more clearly.
- Figure 3 is never referenced, unless I missed it.

Some things I’m curious about:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients that combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting if different modalities relied on different potential orders.
- It is notable that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite the architectural similarity. I was disappointed that this was not discussed in more depth. Do the authors have intuition for this result? Is it related to switching to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion comparing sequential MCB layers to a single MCT layer in the decision head was interesting, but no results were presented. Could the authors elaborate on what was observed?
