

General Impression:  
The proposed method presents an interesting approach with strong empirical results. The attention maps effectively demonstrate that performance gains stem from refined attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the writing occasionally lacks clarity, and the presentation could be more polished; with additional revision, the authors could significantly strengthen the submission.

Strengths:  
- The model architecture is compelling, delivering robust quantitative results alongside thorough qualitative analysis.  
- The transition from unary to pairwise to final attention mechanisms is particularly well-executed and insightful. Notably, the omission of marginalized trinary attention maps (which might further validate the approach) is a minor gap; these were not found in the main text or supplement.  
- The experimental design comprehensively evaluates the model’s behavior across multiple dimensions.  

Weaknesses:  
- The manuscript’s presentation is occasionally disorganized, hindering clarity. Key contributions and methodological details require sharper articulation.  
- The novelty is somewhat incremental: while the architecture surpasses MCB [7] in performance, much of the complexity replicates [7]’s intuition at higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should explicitly clarify the core innovation beyond architectural extension.  
- The probabilistic motivation lacks formal grounding, remaining largely heuristic. To strengthen the argument, either formalize this connection or adjust the narrative to reflect its role as a conceptual guide rather than a rigorous foundation.  
- Figure 2’s abstraction level is problematic—it is overly detailed to grasp the network’s core functionality yet too vague to convey the approach’s structure. Simplifying this figure to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation would greatly improve readability.  
- Figure 3 is referenced only once (in the caption), leaving its relevance unexplained in the main text.  

Key Questions for Clarification:  
1. **Linear coefficients in Equation (1):** What values were learned for combining marginalized potentials? If modalities differentially leverage specific attention orders (e.g., visual vs. textual), this would deepen interpretation.  
2. **Underperformance of 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise vs. MCB [7]:** Despite architectural similarities, this model underperforms MCB. The authors should address this discrepancy—e.g., whether it stems from the shift to MCT decision modules or other factors.  
3. **Sequential MCB vs. single MCT layers:** The discussion of sequential MCB versus single MCT for the decision head is intriguing, yet no results are provided. A brief empirical observation on this trade-off would add value.  

Overall, the work demonstrates a meaningful step toward richer attention modeling in VQA. While the novelty is moderate and the writing requires refinement, the technical contributions are sound and well-supported by experiments. The authors should prioritize clarifying the core innovation, formalizing the probabilistic motivation, and improving figure clarity to elevate the submission. Addressing these points would make a strong contribution to the field.
