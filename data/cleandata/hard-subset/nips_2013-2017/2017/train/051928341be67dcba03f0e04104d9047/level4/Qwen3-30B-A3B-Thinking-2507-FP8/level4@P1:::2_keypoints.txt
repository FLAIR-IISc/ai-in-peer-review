- Introduces a novel high-order attention mechanism for multimodal tasks, applied to VQA.  
- Models attention with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture cross-modal correlations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA, with the ternary potential especially effective.  
- Proposes a generalizable attention framework that extends naturally to any number of modalities.  
- Ablation studies show the ternary potential provides the most substantial performance gains.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the contribution accessible.  
- Strengths: general multimodal framework, thorough ablations, clear presentation, and extension beyond pairwise interactions.  
- Concern: Attention over answers is not clearly explained or visualized; unclear how it functions given mostly single-word answers.  
- Concern: Main performance gains come from the ternary potential; more analysis is needed on why it is crucial and how it differs from prior approaches.  
- Concern: Missing comparison to methods that incorporate answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- Concern: Lacks discussion and analysis of failure cases to clarify limitations.  
- Minor issues: wording ("mechanism" → "mechanisms") and inconsistency about reported evaluation set (validation vs test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors’ reply on 2-modality performance is unsatisfactory quantitatively, but reviewer recommends acceptance due to the contribution.  
- Additional evidence showing better correlation with human attention maps supports that the attention captures meaningful relationships.  
- Confusion over MCB vs MCT results: reported numbers conflict and need explanation why 2-layer MCB can outperform the more general 3-MCT.
