- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for image, question, and answer in VQA.  
- Evaluated on VQA dataset and shows superior performance when ternary potentials are used.  
- Strength: Introduces a potentially generalizable attention framework for multimodal tasks.  
- Strength: Ablation study highlights the performance gain from the ternary potential.  
- Strength: Clear and well-structured writing.  
- Weakness: Attention over answers is unclear—need clarification and visualizations, especially for single-word answers.  
- Weakness: Table 1 implies gains mainly come from the ternary potential; without it the model largely does not beat prior two-modal models (except HieCoAtt).  
- Weakness: Should compare to prior methods that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of failure cases and model failure modes.  
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos and an inconsistency about which evaluation set was used (validation vs test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer leans to accept despite unsatisfactory rebuttal on two-modality performance due to the novel framework and quantitative three-modality gains.  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors’ attention-map evaluation reportedly shows higher correlation with human maps, though SOTA comparisons (e.g., MCB) are unclear.  
- Post-rebuttal: Noted discrepancy in reported numbers for 3-MCT vs 2-layer MCB and inconsistency in MCB scores—clarification needed.
