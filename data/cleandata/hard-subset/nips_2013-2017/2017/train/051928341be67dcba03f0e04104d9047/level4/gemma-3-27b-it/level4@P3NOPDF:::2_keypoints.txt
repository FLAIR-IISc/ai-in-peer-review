- Introduces a generic attention mechanism for multimodal problems using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, showing improved VQA performance, especially with ternary potentials.
- Strength: modality-agnostic framework with potential to generalize beyond VQA.
- Strength: clear ablation study isolating the impact of ternary potentials.
- Strength: paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Weakness: unclear rationale for including the answer modality given answers are often single words; no visualizations of attention over answers are provided.
- Weakness: performance gains appear to come mainly from ternary potentials; pairwise and unary terms alone do not consistently beat two-modality baselines.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to existing models that explicitly use answer information (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16) to contextualize novelty and effectiveness.
- Weakness: no analysis of failure cases or types of questions/images where the model fails.
- Minor issues: wording error ("mechanism" → "mechanisms") and discrepancy between claimed validation evaluation and Table 1 reporting test-dev/test-std results.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance but finds rebuttal on two-modality performance unconvincing due to limited quantitative evidence.
- Post-rebuttal: positive note that quantitative comparison of attention maps to human attention is encouraging; confusion remains over swapped/conflicting MCB vs MCT performance numbers.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions (score 7/10).
