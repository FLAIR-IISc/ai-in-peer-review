This research paper presents a new method for focusing on image and question in visual question answering (VQA) tasks. The main novelty lies in proposing a common thread of attention for multiple modalities – a combination of acre, pairwise, and ternary capability for the 3 modalities (image, question, and answer) in VQA. The proposed approach is evaluated on VQA datasets and performs better than existing models when ternary capability is used.

**Strengths:**
1. A common thread of attention for multimodal problems that is not specific to a particular task is useful, especially if it can be generalized for multiple tasks.
2. An ablation study showing performance without ternary capability is useful for understanding how much ternary capability helps.
3. The research paper is clearly written.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The approach mentions focusing on the 3 modalities – image, question, and answer. However, it is not clear what it means to focus on the answers because most answers are single words, and even if they are multi-word, they are considered single words. The research paper does not present any visualizations for focusing on the answers. Therefore, I would like the authors to clarify this.
2. From the results in Table 1, it seems that the main improvement in the proposed model is coming from the ternary capability. Without ternary capability, the proposed model is not performing better than the existing model (except for HieCoAtt) for the establishment of the 2 modalities. Therefore, I would like the authors to shed light on this.
3. Since ternary capability seems to be the main factor in the performance improvement of the proposed model, I would like the authors to compare the proposed model with the existing model where answers are also used as input, such as the Revisit Visual Question Answer baseline (Jabri et al., ECCV16).
4. The research paper does not discuss the failure cases of the proposed model. Focusing on the failure mode would be insightful so that future research can be guided accordingly.
5. Other errors/typos:
a. L38: तंत्र -> तंत्र (Tantran -> Tantran)
b. L237 mentions that the evaluation is on the validation set. However, Table 1 shows numbers on the test-dev and test-STD sets?

Post-rebuttal comments:
Although the authors' response to the concern that "the proposed model is not performing better than the existing model for the 2 modalities" does not seem satisfactory due to the lack of quantitative evidence, I would like to recommend acceptance because the research paper proposes a common attention framework for multiple modalities, and the 3-modality attention performs better than the SOTA. The quantitative evaluation of the proposed model's attention maps against human attention maps (reported in the rebuttal) also looks good and suggests that the attention maps are more correlated with human maps than the existing model. However, we do not know what this correlation value is for SOTA models like MCB, I think it is still quite better than HieCoAtt.
