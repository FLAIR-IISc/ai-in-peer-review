

General Impression:  
The paper proposes an attention-based VQA model extending the MCB framework to unary, pairwise, and trinary interactions. The results are compelling, with the attention maps suggesting gains stem from refined attention mechanisms rather than mere model capacity increases. However, the presentation is occasionally disorganized and unclear, though the authors could address these issues in revision. The core contribution is interesting but requires stronger justification of novelty and formalization.

Strengths:  
- The model design is novel in its systematic exploration of attention orders (unary, pairwise, trinary), yielding strong quantitative results.  
- Experiments are thorough, including detailed qualitative analysis (e.g., attention maps) and ablation studies.  
- The shift between unary/pairwise and final attention mechanisms is compelling, though the absence of marginalized trinary attention maps (in text or supplement) is noted as a minor gap.

Weaknesses:  
1. **Clarity and Presentation**: The writing is occasionally confusing, with disjointed explanations of the model’s architecture and contributions. Key concepts (e.g., the role of marginalization) require sharper articulation to avoid ambiguity.  
2. **Novelty Justification**: The contribution appears incremental. The architecture extends [7] by adding higher-order (trinary) and lower-order (unary) interactions but does not fundamentally depart from the MCB intuition. The authors must explicitly clarify how this differs from prior work—e.g., by emphasizing *why* trinary interactions are necessary beyond pairwise, or how the architecture avoids redundant complexity.  
3. **Probabilistic Motivation**: The connection to probabilistic modeling (Section 2.1) is underdeveloped. It serves as motivation but lacks formal grounding (e.g., no derivation linking the loss to a probabilistic objective). The authors should either strengthen this link or reframe the discussion to avoid overstating its significance.  
4. **Figure 2**: This diagram is overly abstract to convey functional details yet too detailed to convey the core idea. It should be simplified to emphasize the flow of unary → pairwise → trinary potential generation, removing extraneous elements.  
5. **Figure 3**: This figure is referenced in the text but never discussed or cited in results. Its omission is a critical oversight that weakens the paper’s narrative.  

Key Questions for Authors:  
1. **Equation (1) Coefficients**: What values were learned for the linear combination coefficients (e.g., λ₁, λ₂, λ₃)? Were modality-specific patterns observed (e.g., vision vs. language favoring different interaction orders)?  
2. **2-Modality Model Underperformance**: Why does the "Unary+Pairwise" model underperform MCB [7] despite architectural similarities? The paper notes this but lacks analysis. Could this relate to the MCB/MCT decision module swap or interaction order limitations?  
3. **Sequential MCB vs. Single MCT**: The discussion of sequential MCB vs. single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing but unsupported by results. Please clarify the observed trade-offs (e.g., accuracy, convergence) in the revision.  

Recommendation:  
**Weak Accept** (with major revisions). The technical content is strong, but the paper requires substantial rewriting to clarify novelty, fix presentation issues, and address the critical gaps (Figure 3, probabilistic motivation, and the underperformance of the 2-modality model). The authors must explicitly differentiate their contribution from [7] and justify the added complexity of trinary interactions. If these points are addressed rigorously, the paper could be a solid addition to the literature.  

*Specific Revision Requests*:  
- **Novelty Section**: Rewrite the contribution statement to explicitly state how the model *differs* from [7] (e.g., "While [7] uses pairwise interactions, our trinary layer captures higher-order context without requiring additional modules").  
- **Figure 2**: Redesign to focus solely on the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation pipeline.  
- **Figure 3**: Either integrate it into the results discussion or remove it.  
- **Equation (1)**: Report learned coefficients and discuss modality-specific trends.  
- **2-Modality Results**: Add a paragraph analyzing why Unary+Pairwise underperforms MCB.  

The work has merit but currently lacks the precision and clarity expected at NIPS. With the suggested revisions, it would significantly strengthen the argument for its contribution.
