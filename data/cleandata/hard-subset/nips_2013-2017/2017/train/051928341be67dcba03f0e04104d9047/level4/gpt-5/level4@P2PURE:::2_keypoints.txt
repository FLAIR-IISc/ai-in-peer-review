- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer; evaluated on VQA with gains when ternary potentials are used.
- Strength: A task-agnostic, generic attention formulation that could generalize beyond a single task.
- Strength: Ablation removing the ternary potential helps quantify its contribution.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: Unclear what “attention over answers” means given single-token answers and no visualizations of answer attention; needs clarification.
- Weakness: Main performance gain appears driven by the ternary potential; without it the model does not outperform existing two-modality baselines (except HieCoAtt).
- Weakness: Should compare to models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).
- Weakness: No discussion or analysis of failure cases to inform limitations and future work.
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos—L38 “mechanism”→“mechanisms”; L237 claims validation set while Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std.
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance despite remaining concerns because the three-modality results outperform state of the art.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors report higher quantitative correlation of their attention maps with human maps than existing models (encouraging), but comparisons to stronger SOTA like MCB are missing.
- Question: Reviewer asks whether the authors’ reported MCB vs. MCT numbers are swapped (69.4% vs. 68.4%) and which MCB value (Table 1 shows 68.6%) the authors referenced.
