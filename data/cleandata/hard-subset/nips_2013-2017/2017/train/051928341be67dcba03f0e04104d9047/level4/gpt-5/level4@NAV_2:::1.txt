General Impression:
Overall, I find the proposed approach compelling. The empirical results are strong, and the attention visualizations suggest the gains likely come from improved attention modeling rather than simply scaling model capacity. The exposition, however, is sometimes weak and occasionally confusing; with additional revision, the presentation could be clarified.

Strengths:
- Interesting model that delivers solid performance.
- Comprehensive qualitative and quantitative evaluation.
- The transition from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention is persuasive. I would have also liked to see marginalized ternary attention maps. If these exist in the main text or supplement and I missed them, please point them out.

Weaknesses:
- As noted, the writing/presentation can feel disorganized at times.
- The novelty seems somewhat incremental. While the architecture is more involved than, and outperforms, the MCB-for-VQA model [7], much of the added complexity appears to extend the intuition of [7] to higher (ternary) and lower (unary) orders. This need not be a major issue, but it would help to more crisply delineate the specific contributions (and any I may have overlooked).
- The probabilistic framing is not made rigorous enough to be more than motivational. That is acceptable, but the paper should either formalize the connection more carefully or temper the language to reflect its heuristic role.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of detail: it is neither sufficiently detailed to convey the mechanics, nor sufficiently abstract to neatly summarize the pipeline. I suggest simplifying it to emphasize the unary/pairwise/ternary potential construction more clearly.
- I do not see Figure 3 referenced in the text (unless I missed it).

Some things I'm curious about:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients that combine the marginalized potentials in equations (1)? It would be interesting to know whether different modalities favor different potential orders.
- The 2-modality Unary+Pairwise variant performs worse than MCB [7] despite the architectural similarity. The paper offers little discussion of this gap. Any intuition? Could this be related to the switch to MCB/MCT in the decision module?
- The comparison between sequential MCB layers and a single MCT layer in the decision head is intriguing, but no results are presented. Could the authors comment on what they observed?
