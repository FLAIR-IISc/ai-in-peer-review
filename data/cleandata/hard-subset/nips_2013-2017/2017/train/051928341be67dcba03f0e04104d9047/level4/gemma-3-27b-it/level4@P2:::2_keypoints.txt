- Proposes a novel, generic multimodal attention formulation using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials, applicable beyond VQA.  
- Handles an arbitrary number of modalities, which is a notable advantage.  
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials significantly contribute to performance.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains core concepts.  
- “Attention over answers” is unclear and under-justified given short answer lengths; lacks attention visualizations for answers.  
- Performance gains depend heavily on ternary potentials; paper lacks in-depth analysis explaining why.  
- Comparison to related work is incomplete; should include models that incorporate answers (e.g., Jabri et al. ECCV 2016).  
- No discussion or analysis of failure cases, limiting insight into model limitations.  
- Minor errors/inconsistencies: “mechanism”→“mechanisms” (Line 38) and mismatch between claimed validation evaluation (Line 237) and Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).  
- Overall assessment: novel and well-motivated approach that achieves SOTA with ternary interactions, but needs clarifications and deeper analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: generic attention framework, ternary potentials yield SOTA VQA results, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification of answer attention, heavy reliance on ternary potentials without explanation, insufficient comparisons, and lack of failure-case analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept (leaning strong accept), contingent on clarifying evaluation numbers in the rebuttal.
