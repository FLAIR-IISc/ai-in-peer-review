- General: Method is interesting with strong results; attention maps indicate gains from improved attention rather than capacity, but writing needs clarification and revision.  
- Strength: Proposed model is compelling and achieves impressive performance.  
- Strength: Authors provide thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluations.  
- Strength: Smooth transition among unary, pairwise, trinary, and final attention is notable; marginalized trinary attention maps appear missing and should be included.  
- Weakness: Writing and presentation are sometimes jumbled and hard to follow.  
- Weakness: Novelty is limited—architecture largely extends intuition from MCB [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders; clarify contributions and overlap with prior work.  
- Weakness: Probabilistic connection is weakly established; either formalize it or soften the language.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 is overly abstract and should be simplified to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, making its inclusion unclear.  
- Suggestion: Provide learned values for the linear coefficients combining marginalized potentials (eq. 1) to see modality-specific use of potential orders.  
- Question: Explain why the 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7]; investigate whether switching from MCB to MCT decision modules explains this.  
- Suggestion: Discuss and provide results comparing sequential MCB decision heads versus a single MCT layer, as the comparison is currently missing.
