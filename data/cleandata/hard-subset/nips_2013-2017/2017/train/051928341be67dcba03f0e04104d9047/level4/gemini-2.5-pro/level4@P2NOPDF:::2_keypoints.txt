- Proposes a generic, task-agnostic multimodal attention formulation (unary, pairwise, ternary potentials) with potential broad applicability.  
- Ablation study shows the ternary potential provides a significant performance boost.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- The motivation and implementation of attention over answers are unclear given mostly single-word answers; visualizations for answer attention are needed.  
- Performance gains appear almost entirely due to the ternary potential; the 2-modality model does not consistently beat baselines and requires further analysis.  
- A comparison to other methods that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) is missing but warranted.  
- The paper lacks analysis of failure cases and incorrect predictions to illuminate limitations.  
- Minor issues: typo ("mechanism" → "mechanisms") and a discrepancy between claimed validation-set evaluation (L237) and Table 1's test-dev/test-std results.  
- Post-rebuttal recommendation: accept, based on the novel framework and the strong 3-modality SOTA results.  
- The added evaluation against human attention maps is valuable and shows improved correlation over HieCoAtt, though direct comparison to MCB is missing.  
- Remaining question: clarify the apparent typo/confusion in the rebuttal comparing MCT vs. MCB scores (reported 68.4% vs. 69.4% and inconsistency with Table 1's 68.6%).
