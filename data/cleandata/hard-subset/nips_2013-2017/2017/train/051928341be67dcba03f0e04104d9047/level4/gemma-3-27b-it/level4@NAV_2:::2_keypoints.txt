- Novel generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for VQA, with potential to generalize beyond VQA.
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials provide clear performance gains and helps quantify each component's contribution.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making the approach easy to follow.
- Role and motivation for attention over the answer modality are unclear, especially since answers are short units and no attention visualizations are provided.
- Major performance improvement is largely driven by ternary potentials; without them the model is not consistently better than prior two-modality methods.
- Missing comparison to prior work that incorporates answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) to contextualize the contribution.
- No analysis of failure cases; lacks insight into when and why the model fails.
- Minor errors/typos (e.g., “mechanism”→“mechanisms”; mismatch between stated validation evaluation and Table 1 reporting test-dev/test-std).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel multimodal attention, state-of-the-art VQA results with ternary potentials, and informative ablation study.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification for answer attention, heavy reliance on ternary potentials for gains, and missing failure-case analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify answer-attention, report/clarify performance without ternary potentials, compare to relevant prior work, and include failure-case discussion.
