- General: High-order attention model for VQA is promising with impressive results, but writing and presentation need clearer organization and flow.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA and attention maps focus on relevant regions and words.
- Strength: Provides thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments that build confidence in the method.
- Strength: Demonstrates a notable shift between unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention, capturing high-order correlations.
- Weakness: Writing and presentation are unclear and sometimes jumbled, requiring more concise explanations.
- Weakness: Novelty is limited as the model extends prior work; contributions and differences from previous approaches are not clearly articulated.
- Weakness: The probabilistic connection between attention and decision-making is not formally established and is described imprecisely.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is overly abstract and hard to interpret; it should be simplified to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation.
- Weakness: Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, leaving its purpose unclear.
- Question: What values were learned for the linear coefficients combining marginalized potentials in Eq.(1), and do different modalities prefer different potential orders?
- Question: Why does the 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperform compared to MCB [7], and is this linked to swapping decision computation modules?
- Question: Were comparisons made between sequential MCB layers and a single MCT layer for the decision head, and what were the observations?
- Suggestion: Include marginalized trinary attention maps (in the main text or supplementary) to illustrate trinary interactions.
