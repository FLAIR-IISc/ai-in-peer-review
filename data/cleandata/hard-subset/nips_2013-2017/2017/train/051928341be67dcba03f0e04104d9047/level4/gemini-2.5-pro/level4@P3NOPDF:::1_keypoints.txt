- Introduces a novel multimodal attention factorization into unary, pairwise, and trinary interactions, evaluated on VQA with strong results.  
- Factorized attention components are combined to produce the final output, and visualizations suggest gains stem from the mechanism rather than model capacity.  
- Conceptually interesting model that achieves strong empirical performance on a challenging benchmark.  
- Comprehensive experiments including quantitative benchmarks and ablations that show each component’s contribution.  
- Qualitative attention-map visualizations effectively illustrate how the model refines attention through component combination.  
- Presentation and writing are unclear in places, hindering full comprehension of the architecture and technical details.  
- Novelty/positioning relative to prior work (e.g., MCB) is not sharply articulated; appears as a logical extension rather than a clearly distinct advance.  
- Probabilistic motivation is presented as an analogy and not formalized; needs either formal derivation or reframing as intuition.  
- Figures lack clarity: Figure 2 is ambiguously abstract and Figure 3 is unreferenced, reducing explanatory value.  
- Request to report learned linear coefficients in Equation (1) to see modality/task reliance on potential orders.  
- Asks for discussion on why the 2-modal unary+pairwise model underperforms MCB—possible impact of switching MCB to MCT or other factors.  
- Requests results/observations for using sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT decision head and inclusion of marginalized trinary attention maps in main text or supplement.
