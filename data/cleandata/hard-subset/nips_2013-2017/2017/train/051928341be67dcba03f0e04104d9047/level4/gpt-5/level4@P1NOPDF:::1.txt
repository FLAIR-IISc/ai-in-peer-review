General Impression:
Overall, the proposed method is interesting. The results are strong, and the attention maps suggest the gains may come from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. However, the writing is sometimes weak and confusing; with more time, I expect the authors could improve the presentation.

Strengths:
- Interesting model with solid results.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- I’m impressed by the contrast between the unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention. I would also have liked to see marginalized trinary attention maps. Did I miss these in the main text or the supplement?

Weaknesses:
- As noted above, the writing/presentation is occasionally jumbled and difficult to follow.
- The novelty feels somewhat limited. The architecture is undoubtedly more complex than, and outperforms, the MCB for VQA model [7], but much of this added complexity appears to reiterate the intuition of [7] at higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not necessarily a problem, but the contributions should be clarified and distinguished more explicitly (including any I may have missed).
- The probabilistic connection is not well developed. As written, it reads more as motivation than as a formal link. Either make the connection more rigorous or temper the language to reflect its motivational role.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of abstraction: it is neither detailed enough to convey the operational flow of the network nor abstract enough to cleanly summarize the approach. I recommend simplifying it to emphasize how unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials are generated and combined.
- Figure 3 is never referenced in the text (unless I missed it).

Questions and clarifications:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting to know whether different modalities rely on different potential orders.
- The two-modality Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite the similar architecture. The paper offers little discussion of this. Do you have intuition for this result? Is it related to the switch from MCB to MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion comparing sequential MCB layers to a single MCT layer in the decision head is interesting, but no results are reported. Could you share what you observed experimentally?
