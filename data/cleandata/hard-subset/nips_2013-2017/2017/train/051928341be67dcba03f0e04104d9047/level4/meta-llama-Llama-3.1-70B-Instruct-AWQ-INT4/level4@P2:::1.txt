**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel attention mechanism for visual question answering (VQA) tasks, which learns high-order correlations between different data modalities. The authors introduce a probabilistic model that uses unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture dependencies between modalities. The model is evaluated on the VQA dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The proposed attention mechanism is related to previous work on attention mechanisms for VQA tasks, such as the hierarchical attention mechanism proposed by Lu et al. [15] and the multi-modal compact bilinear (MCB) pooling approach proposed by Fukui et al. [7]. However, the authors claim that their approach is more general and can be easily extended to any number of modalities.

**Strengths**

* The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
* The authors provide thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments to evaluate their model.
* The attention maps generated by the model seem to illustrate the gains in performance due to improved attention mechanisms.

**Weaknesses**

* The writing and presentation of the paper could be improved for clarity and coherence.
* The novelty of the proposed approach feels limited, as it builds upon previous work on attention mechanisms for VQA tasks.
* The probabilistic connection between the proposed model and previous work is not clearly established.
* Figure 2 is not detailed enough to understand the network's functionality, and Figure 3 is not referenced in the text.

**Specific Comments**

* The authors should clarify the values learned for the linear coefficients in equations (1) and discuss how different modalities take advantage of different potential orders.
* The authors should provide more discussion on why the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model under-performs MCB [7] despite a similar architecture.
* The authors should provide more results and discussion on the comparison between sequential MCB and single MCT layers for the decision head.
