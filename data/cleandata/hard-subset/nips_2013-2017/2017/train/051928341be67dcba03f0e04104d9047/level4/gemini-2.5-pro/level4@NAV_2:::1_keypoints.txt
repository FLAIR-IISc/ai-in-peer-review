- Proposes a novel multimodal attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials combined via a single mean-field iteration and fused with tensor sketching, achieving SOTA on VQA.
- Performance & significance: achieves strong state-of-the-art results on standard VQA, showing benefit of higher-order interactions.
- Novelty: explicit probabilistic-style formulation of attention with unary/pairwise/ternary potentials generalizes co-attention to more than two modalities.
- Thorough evaluation: solid quantitative baselines and insightful qualitative visualizations; pairwise potentials visibly refine unary attention maps.
- Clarity and presentation issues: writing is occasionally jumbled, Figure 2 is presented at an awkward abstraction level, and Figure 3 is not referenced.
- Novelty positioning unclear: relationship to prior work (especially Fukui et al. [7]) needs clearer articulation since added complexity may largely extend existing multimodal interaction ideas.
- Probabilistic framing weak: connection to probabilistic models and mean-field inference is high-level and should be formalized or presented as architectural inspiration.
- Analysis gap: surprising underperformance of the 2-modality (Unary+Pairwise) model versus MCB [7] needs more analysis and explanation.
- Question: report learned linear coefficients (αi, βi, γi) to show modality reliance on different potential orders.
- Question: present formal table and discussion comparing 2-layer MCB decision module vs single MCT module (validation results cited).
- Question: provide visualizations of marginalized ternary attention maps to demonstrate the concrete contribution of third-order potentials.
- Question: offer intuition on why the 2-modality model underperforms MCB — e.g., embedding dimensions, hyperparameter tuning, or pairwise attention formulation differences.
