General Impression:
The proposed method presents an interesting approach, with notable results and attention maps that suggest the improvements may be attributed to enhanced attention mechanisms rather than increased model capacity. However, the writing could be improved for clarity, and with additional time, the authors may be able to refine the submission.

Strengths:
- The model is innovative, yielding good results and featuring comprehensive qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- The transition between unary, pairwise, and final attentions is impressive, although the inclusion of marginalized trinary attention maps would be beneficial for further insight.
- The authors demonstrate a thorough understanding of the subject matter, warranting praise for their experimental design.

Weaknesses:
- The writing style is sometimes confusing, which may hinder the reader's ability to fully grasp the content.
- While the architecture outperforms the MCB for VQA model, the novelty of the approach seems somewhat limited, as it builds upon and expands the intuition of existing work. Clarifying the contributions and their significance would strengthen the manuscript.
- The probabilistic connection, although motivational, is not formally established, and the authors may need to either solidify this connection or adjust their language to avoid confusion.
- Figure 2 requires revision to strike a balance between detail and abstraction, effectively conveying the network's functionality and approach.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text and could be either removed or incorporated into the discussion.

Points of Interest:
- The values learned for the linear coefficients in equations (1) would provide insight into how different modalities utilize potential orders.
- The underperformance of the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model compared to MCB warrants further discussion, particularly regarding any intuition behind this result and potential connections to the decision computation modules.
- The comparison between sequential MCB and single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing, and including results or observations would enhance the manuscript.
