General Impression:

Overall, the proposed method is interesting and yields strong results. The attention maps appear to demonstrate that these gains stem from improved attention mechanisms, rather than simply increased model capacity. While I found the writing somewhat weak and occasionally confusing, I believe the authors could address these issues with further refinement.

Strengths:

- The model is interesting and achieves good performance.
- The experiments are thorough, both qualitatively and quantitatively.
- I am impressed by the progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention mechanism. However, I would have appreciated seeing visualizations of the marginalized trinary attention maps as well. Did I overlook these in the text or supplementary materials?

Weaknesses:

- As mentioned above, the writing and presentation were at times a bit disjointed.
- The novelty feels somewhat limited. While the architecture is undeniably more complex than and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this added complexity appears to extend the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This isn’t a major issue, but I suggest the authors more clearly articulate the contributions of this work (and any that I may have missed).
- The probabilistic connection isn't presented convincingly. It doesn’t feel formalized enough to be considered more than motivational, which is acceptable, but I would recommend the authors either strengthen this connection with a more formal treatment or adjust the language to reflect its current status.
- Figure 2 is at an unhelpful level of abstraction. It lacks the detail needed to understand the network’s functionality, yet isn’t abstract enough to provide a clear overview of the approach. I suggest simplifying this figure to more clearly emphasize the generation of unary/pairwise/trinary potentials.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, as far as I can tell.

Some things I'm curious about:

- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be insightful to know if different modalities benefited from different potential orders.
- I found it interesting that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7], despite their architectural similarities. I was disappointed that this result wasn’t discussed further in the text. Do the authors have any intuition as to why this occurs? Is it related to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion of using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was intriguing, but no results were presented. Could the authors elaborate on their observations?
