### Summary

This paper presents an interesting multi-modal attention model that incorporates unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials, achieving strong results on the VQA task. The quantitative and qualitative results are thorough, and the attention map visualizations compellingly suggest that performance gains stem from the sophisticated attention mechanism rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the paper's primary weaknesses are in its presentation and the positioning of its novelty. The writing is often unclear, and the connection to probabilistic models feels more motivational than formal. With revisions to improve clarity and better contextualize its contributions, this could be a solid submission.

### Strengths

*   **Effective Model and Strong Results:** The proposed model, which extends attention mechanisms across multiple orders, is a compelling idea that demonstrates strong empirical performance.
*   **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors provide a comprehensive set of experiments, including both quantitative benchmarks and insightful qualitative analyses, which effectively highlight the model's capabilities.
*   **Insightful Visualizations:** The attention maps are particularly effective. The visualizations showing the shift between unary/pairwise attentions and the final combined attention provide convincing evidence for the model's reasoning process.

### Weaknesses

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The overall clarity of the paper needs improvement. The writing can be convoluted at times, making the core concepts more difficult to grasp than necessary.
*   **Positioning of Novelty:** The novelty of the work feels somewhat incremental. While the architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB model [7], much of this complexity arises from extending the core intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is a valid contribution, but the authors should more explicitly delineate their unique conceptual advances from what is a direct, multi-order extension of prior work.
*   **Underdeveloped Probabilistic Connection:** The connection to probabilistic models is not drawn with sufficient rigor. As presented, it serves as a high-level motivation rather than a formal framework. The paper would be stronger if the authors either cemented this connection mathematically or adjusted the language to clarify that it is intended as an analogy.
*   **Ineffective Figures:**
    *   Figure 2 is presented at an awkward level of abstraction; it is not detailed enough to fully understand the network's implementation, yet not high-level enough to provide a clear conceptual overview. A simplified diagram that more clearly emphasizes the generation and combination of the unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials would be more effective.
    *   Figure 3 does not appear to be referenced in the main text.

### Questions and Suggestions for Improvement

*   **Trinary Attention Maps:** The unary and pairwise attention maps are very insightful. Were marginalized trinary attention maps also generated? Including them in the paper or supplement would complete the qualitative analysis of the different potential orders.
*   **Learned Coefficients:** What were the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be fascinating to see if different modalities or task types learn to prioritize different potential orders.
*   **Underperformance of Sub-Model:** The observation that the "2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise" model underperforms the original MCB model [7] is surprising, given their architectural similarities. The paper would benefit from a discussion of the authors' intuition for this result. Could it be related to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules or other architectural choices?
*   **Ablation Study Results:** The discussion of an ablation study comparing sequential MCB layers to a single MCT layer for the decision head was interesting, but the corresponding results were not presented. Could the authors please elaborate on what was observed in this experiment?
