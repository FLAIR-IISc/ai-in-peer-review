- Proposes a higher-order attention model combining unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials for multimodal VQA with strong empirical and qualitative results.  
- Attention visualizations suggest gains come from improved reasoning rather than merely increased capacity.  
- Exposition and organization are sometimes unclear; terminology is inconsistent.  
- Novelty over prior work (e.g., MCB [7]) feels incremental—mainly extension to trinary interactions and added unary components.  
- Probabilistic framing is motivational but not formally derived or rigorous.  
- Figure 2 is awkwardly detailed and should more directly highlight the pipeline of computing, marginalizing, and combining potentials.  
- Figure 3 appears unreferenced in the main text, reducing its usefulness.  
- Request: include marginalized trinary attention maps to validate higher-order contributions.  
- Request: report learned linear coefficients from Eq. (1) and whether different modalities favor different orders.  
- Request: explain why 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise underperforms MCB—possible causes include decision head, optimization, or feature preprocessing.  
- Request: provide empirical ablation comparing sequential MCB layers vs a single MCT layer in the decision module.  
- Request: analyze attention robustness across question types (counting, attributes) and report failure cases.  
- Suggestion: explicitly list and ablate core contributions relative to MCB and related baselines.  
- Suggestion: either formalize the probabilistic connection (potentials, factorization, assumptions) or reframe it as heuristic motivation.  
- Suggestion: revise Figure 2 into a modular diagram keyed to equations showing the four-step pipeline.  
- Suggestion: reference and clarify Figure 3 in the text with precise captions.  
- Suggestion: include ablation removing trinary terms and visualizations of marginalized trinary attentions.  
- Suggestion: report combination coefficients stratified by modality or question type for interpretability.  
- Suggestion: provide controlled comparisons isolating the decision head to explain performance differences with MCB.  
- Overall: solid, promising work with strong results; main weaknesses are exposition, incremental novelty, and underdeveloped probabilistic framing—addressing clarity and requested analyses would substantially strengthen the paper.
