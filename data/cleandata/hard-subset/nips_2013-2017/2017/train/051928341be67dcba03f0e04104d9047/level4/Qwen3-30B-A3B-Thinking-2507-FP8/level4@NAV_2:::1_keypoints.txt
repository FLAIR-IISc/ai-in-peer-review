- Introduces a high-order attention mechanism modeling k-way correlations across modalities (visual, text, answer) for VQA.  
- Formalizes unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and uses mean-field inference to derive attention distributions.  
- Applies multimodal compact trilinear pooling (MCT) for decision making.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance (64.57% test-std) with fewer parameters (40M vs. >70M).  
- Qualitative analysis shows attention maps that align with question semantics and illustrate unary/pairwise integration.  
- Exposition is sometimes disjoint and equation-heavy, obscuring probabilistic intuition; key figures lack detail or explanation.  
- Contribution appears incremental—mainly extending existing pooling/attention (e.g., MCB) to ternary/unary terms without deep theoretical novelty.  
- Probabilistic claims are motivational rather than formally justified; mean-field/softmax connections need formalization or toned-down claims.  
- Lacks analysis explaining why the 2-modality unary+pairwise model underperforms MCB—possible causes include architectural differences or embedding dimensionality.  
- Missing ternary attention visualizations; qualitative results focus on 2-modality despite core ternary contribution.  
- Suggest revising figures (e.g., Fig. 2) to show how unary/pairwise/ternary potentials are generated.  
- Suggest clarifying relation to prior work (e.g., MCB) in the introduction and reporting learned coefficients (α, β, γ) to show modality reliance.  
- Suggest including ternary attention visualizations to validate interpretability of the 3-modality model.  
- Overall: technically sound and empirically strong with high significance, but novelty is modest and clarity/formalization/comparative analysis should be improved.
