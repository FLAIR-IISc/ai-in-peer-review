- Proposes a novel, task-agnostic generalized attention framework for multimodal problems, potentially applicable beyond VQA.  
- Ablation study shows the ternary potential is crucial and drives the performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- The notion and utility of "attention over answers" is unclear, especially since answers are single words and no visualizations are provided.  
- The 2-modality variant does not outperform prior methods (except HieCoAtt); authors need to explain why it offers no competitive advantage.  
- A direct comparison to other models that use answer information (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) is missing and warranted.  
- The paper lacks analysis of failure cases and error modes.  
- Minor errors/typos and a reporting discrepancy: "mechanism(s)" and validation vs. test-dev/test-std results need clarification.  
- Post-rebuttal recommendation: accept based on novelty and strong 3-modality results, despite remaining questions about the 2-modality explanation.  
- Rebuttal added a quantitative evaluation showing better correlation of model attention with human attention than HieCoAtt, but direct SOTA comparisons (e.g., MCB) are still missing.  
- Clarification requested about inconsistent MCB vs MCT performance figures (69.4% vs 68.4% and Table 1’s MCB 68.6%): authors should clarify these numbers.
