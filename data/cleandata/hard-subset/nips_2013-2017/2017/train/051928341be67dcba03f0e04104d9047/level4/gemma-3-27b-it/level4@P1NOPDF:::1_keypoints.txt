- Overall: Method is interesting and yields strong results; attention maps suggest gains come from improved attention rather than capacity, but writing is weak and needs refinement.
- Strength: Model is interesting and achieves good performance.
- Strength: Experiments are thorough, both qualitatively and quantitatively.
- Strength: Clear progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final mechanism; request for marginalized trinary attention visualizations.
- Weakness: Writing and presentation are sometimes disjointed and confusing.
- Weakness: Novelty feels limited—architecture largely extends prior MCB intuition to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders; contributions should be more clearly articulated.
- Weakness: Probabilistic connection is not convincingly formalized and reads more as motivation; either strengthen the formalism or tone down the language.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is at an unhelpful level of abstraction and should be simplified to emphasize generation of unary/pairwise/trinary potentials.
- Weakness: Figure 3 appears not to be referenced in the text.
- Question: What values were learned for the linear coefficients combining marginalized potentials in eq. (1), and do different modalities favor different potential orders?
- Question: Why does the 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB [7] despite architectural similarities—could this relate to the decision computation module change?
- Question: The comparison between sequential MCB and a single MCT decision head is intriguing but lacks results—authors should elaborate on observations and report outcomes.
