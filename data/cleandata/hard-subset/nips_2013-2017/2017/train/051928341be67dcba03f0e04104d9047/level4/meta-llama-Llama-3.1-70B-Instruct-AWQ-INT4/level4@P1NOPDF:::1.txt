General Impression:
The proposed method is interesting, and the results are quite good. The attention maps suggest that the gains may be due to improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. However, I found the writing to be somewhat weak and occasionally confusing. With more time, I imagine the authors could improve the submission.

Strengths:
- The model is interesting and achieves good results.
- The authors provide thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- I am impressed by the shift between unary, pairwise, and trinary attentions and the final attention. Although I would have liked to see marginalized trinary attention maps as well. It is unclear whether these were included in the text or supplement.

Weaknesses:
- As mentioned above, I found the writing and presentation to be somewhat jumbled at times.
- The novelty of the method feels a bit limited. While the architecture is more complex than and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this added complexity is essentially an extension of the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. I do not think this is a significant issue, but I suggest the authors clarify their contributions and any I may have missed.
- The probabilistic connection is not well-established. It does not seem to be formalized enough to be taken as more than motivational, which is acceptable. However, I suggest the authors either formalize this connection or adjust the language to clarify its role.
- Figure 2 is at an odd level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. However, it is not abstract enough to easily capture the outline of the approach. I suggest simplifying this figure to emphasize the unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, unless I missed it.

Some points of interest:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients for combining the marginalized potentials in equations (1)? It would be interesting to see if different modalities took advantage of different potential orders.
- I find it interesting that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite having a similar architecture. I was disappointed that this result was not discussed in the text. Do the authors have any intuition about this result? Is it related to the switch from MCB to MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion of using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was quite interesting, but no results were shown. Could the authors provide some insight into what was observed?
