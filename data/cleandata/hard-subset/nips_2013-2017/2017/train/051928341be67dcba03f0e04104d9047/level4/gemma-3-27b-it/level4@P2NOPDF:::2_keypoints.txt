- Proposal of a generic, task-agnostic multimodal attention formulation using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials are crucial and drive the model’s performance gains.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Unclear justification for treating the answer as an attention modality; lack of visualizations of answer-attention limits interpretability.
- Without ternary potentials the model generally does not outperform two-modality baselines (except HieCoAtt); more insight is needed.
- Missing comparison to prior models that explicitly incorporate the answer (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- No analysis of failure cases; discussion of where the model fails is absent.
- Minor errors/typos: “mechanism” → “mechanisms” (line 38) and discrepancy about evaluation split (line 237 vs. Table 1).
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer still recommends acceptance despite unsatisfactory quantitative rebuttal regarding performance without ternaries.
- Post-rebuttal: claim of state-of-the-art performance with three-modality attention and encouraging correlation with human attention maps (improvement over HieCoAtt), though MCB comparison remains unclear.
- Question for authors: Clarify the reported MCB vs. 3-MCT numbers—does 69.4% correspond to 3-MCT and which MCB value is 68.4% or 68.6%?
