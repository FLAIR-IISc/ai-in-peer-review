### General Impression

Overall, the proposed method is interesting and achieves strong results. The provided attention maps compellingly suggest that these performance gains are attributable to the improved attention mechanism rather than simply an increase in model capacity. However, the paper's presentation could be improved for clarity, as the writing is occasionally confusing. I am confident that these issues can be addressed in a revised version.

### Strengths

*   The paper proposes an interesting and effective model, supported by strong empirical results.
*   The experiments are thorough, with both qualitative and quantitative analyses.
*   The interplay between the unary, pairwise, and final attentions is particularly compelling. However, the qualitative analysis could be further strengthened by including visualizations of the marginalized trinary attention maps, which appear to be missing from the main paper and supplementary material.

### Weaknesses

*   As noted above, the paper's presentation is sometimes disjointed, which can make it difficult to follow the main arguments.
*   The novelty of the proposed method appears somewhat incremental. While the proposed architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB model [7], this added complexity seems to stem from extending the core intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not a major flaw, but the authors should more clearly delineate their specific contributions relative to prior work.
*   The connection to probabilistic models is not well-established. The link is presented more as a motivation than a formal framework. The authors should either formalize this connection or adjust the language to clarify that it is primarily an inspirational analogy.
*   Figure 2 is presented at an awkward level of abstraction. It lacks the detail needed to fully understand the network's implementation, yet it is not abstract enough to provide a clear, high-level overview of the approach. I suggest redesigning the figure to more clearly emphasize the generation of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials.
*   Figure 3 does not appear to be referenced in the text.

### Questions and Suggestions for Improvement

*   What were the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be interesting to see if different modalities rely on different potential orders.
*   The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms the MCB model [7], which is surprising given their architectural similarities. The paper lacks a discussion of this result. Could the authors provide some intuition? For instance, is this performance difference related to the switch between the MCB and MCT modules for the decision computation?
*   The discussion on using sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was interesting. Could the authors provide the empirical results or observations that led to their final design choice?
