General Impressions:

Overall, I think the proposed method is interesting. The results are quite good, and looking at the attention maps, it seems that these improvements may be due not only to the increased capacity of the model but also to the improved attention mechanism. I found the writing to be somewhat weak and occasionally confusing, although I believe that the authors could have improved the submitted work if they had more time.

Strengths:

- Interesting model with good results.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative testing.
- I was quite impressed by the changes between unitary/per pairwise attention and final attention. Although, it would have been nice to see the marginal ternary attention maps somewhere as well. Did I miss these in the text or supplement?

Weaknesses:

- As I mentioned above, I found the writing/presentation to be somewhat disorganized at times.
- The novelty here seems somewhat limited. Undoubtedly, the architecture is more complex and performs better than MCB [7] for the VQA model, but most of this added complexity repeats the concept of [7] in a high (ternary) and low (unitary) order. I don't think this is a major problem, but I would advise clarifying these contributions of the authors (and if I have missed anything).
- I don't think the potential connection is drawn very well. It doesn't seem formal enough to be considered something inspiring, which is fine, but I would advise the authors to either further formalize this connection or clarify it by changing the language.
- Figure 2 is at a strange level of abstraction, where it is neither detailed enough to understand the network's functionality nor abstract enough to easily grasp the outline of the method. I would suggest trying to simplify this figure to emphasize the unitary/pairwise/ternary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not mentioned, unless I missed it.

Some things I'm curious about:

- What values were learned for the linear coefficients for combining the marginal potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting if different modalities used different potential orders.
- I find it interesting that the 2-modality unitary+pairwise model performs worse than MCB [7], even though the architecture is similar. I was disappointed that this was not discussed more in the text. Is there any idea of this result? Is it related to swapping in MCB / MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion of sequential MCB versus using a single MCT layer for the decision head was quite interesting, but no results were shown. Could the authors say a little about what they observed?
