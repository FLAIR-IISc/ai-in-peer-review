- Introduces a probabilistic, generalizable attention framework combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with a single mean-field inference step.
- Applies the framework to VQA (image, question, answer), combining attended features with MCB/MCT pooling; ternary potentials yield state-of-the-art results.
- Original contribution: framing attention as learned high-order potentials in a unified, extensible model.
- Experiments are thorough and the ablation study demonstrates the ternary potential’s strong contribution.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; diagrams (e.g., Figure 3) effectively illustrate the modules.
- Significance: principled, extensible multimodal attention approach that could generalize beyond VQA and influence future designs.
- Strength: novel and generalizable framework applicable to any number of modalities.
- Strength: achieves strong empirical SOTA performance on a challenging VQA benchmark.
- Strength: effective ablation study showing the ternary potential drives performance gains.
- Strength: clear presentation makes the contribution accessible.
- Weakness: unclear how attention over the discrete answer vocabulary is computed or interpreted; needs clarification and visualizations for answer attention maps.
- Weakness: 2-modality model shows limited gains, suggesting improvements may stem mainly from including the answer modality rather than the potential-based mechanism itself.
- Weakness: missing direct comparisons to relevant baselines that also use answer inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).
- Weakness: lacks analysis of failure cases or qualitative examples where the model fails.
- Minor: wording in abstract (“attention mechanism” vs “attention mechanisms”) and dataset reporting (val vs test-dev/test-std) need clarification.
