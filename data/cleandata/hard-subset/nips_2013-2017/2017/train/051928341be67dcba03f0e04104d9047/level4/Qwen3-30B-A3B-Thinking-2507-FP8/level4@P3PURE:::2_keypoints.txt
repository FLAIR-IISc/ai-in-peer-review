- Proposes a generic multimodal attention framework with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer; achieves SOTA with ternary potentials.  
- Formulation is broadly applicable beyond VQA and has high potential if validated on additional tasks.  
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials are the primary driver of performance gains.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- "Attention over answers" is unclear given mostly single-word answers; answer-attention visualizations or clarification are missing.  
- Model without ternary potentials does not outperform existing two-modality methods; authors should explain why ternary potentials are indispensable.  
- Should compare against models that explicitly leverage answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- Lacks discussion of failure cases to identify limitations and guide future work.  
- Typos/errors: L38 "mechanism" → "mechanisms"; L237 claims validation set evaluation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors’ response on two-modality performance lacks quantitative support, but overall novelty and strong 3-modality results justify acceptance.  
- Rebuttal analysis showing higher correlation between model attention and human attention (vs. methods like HieCoAtt) is compelling.  
- There is a critical inconsistency in reported numbers for MCB vs. MCT (conflicting 68.6/69.4/68.4) that requires clarification.  
- Final recommendation: Accept.
