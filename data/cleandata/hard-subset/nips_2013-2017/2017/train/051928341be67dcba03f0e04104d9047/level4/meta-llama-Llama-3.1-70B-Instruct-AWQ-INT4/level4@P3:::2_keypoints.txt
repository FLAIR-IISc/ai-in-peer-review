- Main contribution: a novel generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for image, question, and answer in VQA.  
- Evaluation: tested on VQA and achieves state-of-the-art performance when ternary potentials are included.  
- Strength: formulation is general and applicable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: ablation study shows the specific impact of ternary potentials.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written with a clear explanation of approach and experiments.  
- Weakness: attention mechanism over answers is unclear (answers treated as single tokens); needs visualization and more insight.  
- Weakness: primary improvement appears driven by ternary potential; without it the model rarely outperforms 2-modality baselines—requires deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to existing models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).  
- Weakness: no discussion of failure cases or limitations of the proposed model.  
- Weakness: minor errors and typos throughout the paper.  
- Post-rebuttal positive: contributions remain significant; 3-modality attention outperforms SOTA and attention maps correlate better with human maps.  
- Post-rebuttal remaining concerns: still need clarification on answer attention, comparisons with answer-input models, and analysis of limitations/failures.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing the noted clarifications, comparisons, and analysis of limitations.
