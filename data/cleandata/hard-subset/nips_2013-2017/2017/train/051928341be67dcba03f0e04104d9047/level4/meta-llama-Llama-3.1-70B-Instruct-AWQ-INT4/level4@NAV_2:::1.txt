**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper proposes a novel attention mechanism for visual question answering (VQA) that learns high-order correlations between different data modalities. The authors argue that existing attention mechanisms are limited in their ability to capture complex interactions between multiple modalities. They introduce a probabilistic model that uses high-order potentials to compute attention probabilities over multiple modalities. The proposed approach is evaluated on the VQA dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

The paper relates to previous work on attention mechanisms, including the Multimodal Compact Bilinear Pooling (MCB) approach of Fukui et al. [7] and the hierarchical attention mechanism of Lu et al. [15]. The authors argue that their approach is more general and can be applied to any number of modalities, whereas existing approaches are limited to two or three modalities.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
* The attention maps provide insight into the decision-making process and suggest that the model is attending to relevant regions of the image and question.
* The paper provides a thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The writing and presentation could be improved for clarity and readability.
* The novelty of the approach is limited, as it builds on existing work on attention mechanisms and high-order correlations.
* The probabilistic connection is not formally established, and the language could be adjusted to clarify this point.
* Figure 2 is not detailed enough to understand the network's functionality, and Figure 3 is not referenced in the text.

**Qualitative Evaluation**

The paper provides a qualitative evaluation of the proposed approach, including attention maps and examples of correct and incorrect answers. The attention maps suggest that the model is attending to relevant regions of the image and question, and the examples demonstrate the model's ability to answer complex questions.

**Originality and Significance**

The proposed approach is original in its use of high-order correlations to compute attention probabilities over multiple modalities. The approach is significant because it achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset and provides insight into the decision-making process.

**Additional Comments**

* It would be interesting to see marginalized trinary attention maps, which could provide further insight into the decision-making process.
* The values learned for the linear coefficients for combining the marginalized potentials in equations (1) could provide insight into how different modalities are weighted.
