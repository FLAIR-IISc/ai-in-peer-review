- Proposed method is interesting and achieves state-of-the-art performance on VQA.
- Thorough quantitative and qualitative experiments support the approach’s effectiveness.
- The transition between unary, pairwise, and trinary attentions to the final mechanism is compelling and well-visualized.
- Attention visualizations suggest gains come from improved attention mechanisms rather than increased model capacity.
- Writing and presentation are weak and occasionally confusing, hindering understanding.
- Novelty feels incremental relative to Fukui et al. (2016); much complexity stems from extending their intuition to different orders.
- Authors should more clearly articulate specific contributions beyond extending prior work to unary/trinary orders.
- The probabilistic model connection is only motivational and lacks formal development or rigorous justification.
- Figure 2 is too abstract and either lacks detail or is overly complex; it should be simplified to show potential generation clearly.
- Figure 3 is included but not explicitly referenced in the text.
- Question: what values were learned for the linear coefficients combining marginalized potentials, and did modalities favor different orders?
- Question: why does the 2-modality unary+pairwise model underperform MCB despite similar architecture—could it be due to switching to MCB/MCT decision modules?
- Question: comparison between sequential MCB and a single MCT decision head is mentioned but results are not shown—authors should elaborate.
- Argument for acceptance: novel attention mechanism demonstrably improves performance on a challenging task.
- Argument for acceptance: extensive evaluations, ablations, and visualizations provide strong evidence.
- Argument for acceptance: approach is generally applicable and potentially extensible to other multimodal tasks.
- Argument against acceptance: writing quality needs improvement for clarity and readability.
- Argument against acceptance: incremental novelty versus prior work may not justify acceptance at a competitive venue.
- Argument against acceptance: probabilistic framing lacks rigorous mathematical foundation.
