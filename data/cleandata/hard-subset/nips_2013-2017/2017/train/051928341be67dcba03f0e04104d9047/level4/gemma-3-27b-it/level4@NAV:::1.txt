## Review

**General Impression:**

This work presents an interesting method achieving strong results. The attention maps provide compelling evidence that performance gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. While the writing could be strengthened for clarity and flow, I believe the authors could readily address these points with further refinement.

**Strengths:**

- The proposed model demonstrates both novelty and strong performance.
- The experiments are thorough, encompassing both qualitative and quantitative analyses.
- The transition from unary/pairwise to the final attention mechanism is particularly noteworthy. I would, however, have appreciated seeing visualizations of the marginalized trinary attention maps—perhaps these are present in the text or supplementary materials, but I did not locate them. 

**Weaknesses:**

- The presentation and writing occasionally lack clarity and could benefit from restructuring.
- The novelty of the approach, while present, feels somewhat incremental. The architecture builds upon and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], but a significant portion of the increased complexity appears to be a natural extension of the ideas presented in [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. While not a major concern, I recommend the authors more explicitly articulate the unique contributions of their work.
- The probabilistic motivation, while intuitively appealing, is not formally established within the manuscript.  The authors should either develop this connection more rigorously or refine the language to present it as motivational rather than a formal derivation.
- Figure 2 currently resides at an unsuitable level of abstraction. It lacks the detail necessary to fully understand the network’s operation while simultaneously being too complex to readily convey the overall approach. I suggest simplifying this figure to more clearly emphasize the generation of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials.
- Figure 3 appears to be unused and is not referenced in the main text.

**Questions/Curiosities:**

- What specific values were learned for the linear coefficients used in combining the marginalized potentials, as described in equation (1)? It would be insightful to know if different modalities preferentially utilize different potential orders.
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model’s underperformance compared to MCB [7] is surprising given the architectural similarities. I was disappointed to find limited discussion of this result in the text. Could the authors offer any insights into this observation, potentially relating it to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion surrounding the choice between sequential MCB layers and a single MCT layer for the decision head was interesting.  Could the authors elaborate on any observations made during experimentation regarding these different approaches?
