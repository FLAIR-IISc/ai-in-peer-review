- Proposal: Introduces a generic attention formulation for VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, showing improved performance especially with ternary potentials.
- Strength: Generic attention formulation is broadly applicable and potentially generalizable to other multimodal tasks.
- Strength: Ablation study highlights the significant impact of ternary potentials on performance.
- Strength: Writing is clear and well-organized.
- Weakness: Attention over answers is unclear (most answers are single words) and lacks visualizations or explanation.
- Weakness: Table 1 indicates ternary potentials drive gains; without them the 2-modality model generally does not outperform existing models (except HieCoAtt), requiring more insight.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- Weakness: No discussion of failure cases or model limitations.
