- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for image, question, and answer in VQA.  
- Contribution potentially valuable if generalizable across multiple tasks.  
- Ablation study highlights the contribution of the ternary potential.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Unclear purpose of attention over answers (answers typically single-token); no visualizations of answer attention—request clarification.  
- Table 1 suggests performance gains mainly come from the ternary potential; without it the model often does not outperform existing two-modality methods (except HieCoAtt)—needs more discussion.  
- Recommend comparison with other models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., “Revisiting VQA Baselines,” ECCV16).  
- Paper lacks analysis of failure cases; include error-type breakdowns to guide future work.  
- Minor issues: “mechanism” should be “mechanisms”; L237 claims validation evaluation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer leans to accept based on the generic framework and strong 3-modality results despite incomplete quantitative rebuttal.  
- Rebuttal’s human-attention correlation results look promising and outperform HieCoAtt, but comparison to MCB is unclear.  
- Reviewer questions authors’ MCB vs MCT numbers (possible reversal) and which reported 68.4% vs 68.6% the authors reference.
