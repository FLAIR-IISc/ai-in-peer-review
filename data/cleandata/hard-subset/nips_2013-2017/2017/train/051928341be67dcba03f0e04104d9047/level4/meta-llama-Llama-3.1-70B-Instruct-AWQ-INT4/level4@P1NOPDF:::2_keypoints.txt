- Paper proposes a novel generic multimodal attention formulation for VQA combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer, and reports improved results when ternary potentials are used.
- Strength: Generic multimodal attention formulation is useful and may generalize beyond VQA.
- Strength: Ablation study isolating the effect of ternary potentials helps quantify their contribution.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: Unclear what “attention over answers” means given most answers are single tokens and no attention visualizations over answers are provided.
- Weakness: Table 1 suggests main gains come from ternary potentials; without them the model often does not outperform existing two-modality models, needing further analysis.
- Weakness: Important comparison missing with prior work that uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16) to contextualize ternary-potential gains.
- Weakness: Paper lacks analysis of failure cases and model weaknesses.
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos—“mechanisms” (L38) and inconsistency about validation vs. test set reporting (L237 vs. Table 1).
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer leans to accept based on the generic framework and strong three-modality quantitative results.
- Post-rebuttal: Quantitative evaluation vs. human attention maps looks promising and shows higher correlation than HieCoAtt, though comparison to other SOTA (e.g., MCB) is unclear.
- Question: Authors’ MCB vs. MCT numbers appear inconsistent/swapped (reporting 68.4% vs. 69.4%), and it’s unclear which MCB score (e.g., 68.6% in Table 1) the authors referenced.
