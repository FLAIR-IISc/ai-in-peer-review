- Introduces a high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with mean-field inference for multimodal VQA.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art VQA performance and provides qualitative attention maps.  
- Ablation studies (2-modality vs 3-modality) support that higher-order attention is beneficial.  
- Probabilistic framing is largely motivational; equation (1) lacks rigorous justification for linear combination of potentials.  
- Authors omit analysis of learned coefficients (αᵢ, βᵢ, γᵢ), leaving modality reliance unexplained.  
- The 2-modality model performs worse than [7] despite architectural similarity and the discrepancy is not explained.  
- The MCT vs. 2-layer MCB claim in Section 4.2 is presented without supporting results.  
- Writing and presentation are sometimes jumbled; equations and marginalized potentials are not clearly linked to figures.  
- Figure 2 is overly abstract and confusing; Figure 3 is referenced but never explicitly discussed—figures need simplification and clearer linkage.  
- Terminology error: use "ternary" consistently instead of "trinary."  
- Novelty is incremental; the method largely reinterprets prior MCB-like frameworks at higher order.  
- The paper does not convincingly justify why higher-order correlations are uniquely beneficial beyond pairwise interactions.  
- Marginal performance gains and added model complexity undermine the claim that the model is "simple."  
- Recommendation: modest contribution with solid empirical work but technical gaps and limited generalization; recommend rejection unless probabilistic grounding or broader validation is added.
