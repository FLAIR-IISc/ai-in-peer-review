- Novelty: Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer; shows improved VQA performance when using ternary potentials.
- Strength: Generic attention formulation for multimodal problems is a valuable contribution if shown generalizable to other tasks.
- Strength: Ablation study clearly demonstrates the positive impact of ternary potentials.
- Strength: Paper is clearly and concisely written.
- Weakness: Role of attention over answers is unclear given answers are usually single tokens; paper lacks visualizations and clarification of answer-attention.
- Weakness: Main performance improvement appears driven by ternary potentials; without them the model generally does not outperform prior two-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), needing further discussion.
- Weakness: Should compare to prior methods that explicitly use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- Weakness: No analysis of failure cases or model limitations is provided.
- Weakness: Minor errors/inconsistencies (L38 “mechanism(s)”; L237 claims validation evaluation while Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std).
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework, quantitative gains with three-modality attention, and encouraging correlation with human attention.
- Post-rebuttal concern: Authors’ reported MCB vs. MCT numbers seem inconsistent/swapped (69.4% vs. 68.4%) and conflict with Table 1’s MCB value (68.6%); reviewer requests clarification.
