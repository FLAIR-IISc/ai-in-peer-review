

General Impression:  
The proposed method is interesting, with strong results that suggest the gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity, as evidenced by the attention maps. However, the writing is occasionally weak and confusing; with additional revision time, the authors could substantially improve the submission.  

Strengths:  
- The model is interesting and achieves good results.  
- The experimental evaluation is thorough, including both qualitative and quantitative analyses.  
- I am impressed by the shift between unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention mechanism. It would be valuable to see marginalized trinary attention maps included (I did not locate them in the main text or supplement).  

Weaknesses:  
- The writing and presentation are occasionally jumbled.  
- The novelty is limited; while the architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB model [7], much of this complexity simply extends the intuition from [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should clarify these contributions (and any others I may have overlooked).  
- The probabilistic connection is not sufficiently formalized, rendering it motivational rather than foundational. The authors should either strengthen this formal connection or adjust the language to clarify its role.  
- Figure 2 lacks appropriate abstraction: it is too detailed to understand the network’s functionality yet too abstract to capture the approach’s outline clearly. Simplifying this figure to emphasize unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation would improve clarity.  
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the main text (unless I missed it).  

Key Questions:  
- What linear coefficients were learned for combining marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be insightful to see if different modalities leverage different potential orders.  
- Why does the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB [7] despite architectural similarities? The text lacks discussion of this result—could it relate to the switch from MCB/MCT decision computation modules?  
- The comparison between sequential MCB and single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing, but no results are presented. Could the authors elaborate on observed differences?
