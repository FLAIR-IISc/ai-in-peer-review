- Introduces a generic multi-modal attention framework combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials, applied to image/question/answer for VQA, achieving state-of-the-art performance driven by ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Generic formulation is broadly applicable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: Ablation study clearly isolates and demonstrates the importance of ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: “Attention over answers” is not well-justified or visualized; unclear what attending over mostly single-token answers means.  
- Weakness: Two-modality version (without ternary) is not competitive with most existing methods; authors should explain why.  
- Weakness: Needs comparison to other methods that use answers as input (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of failure cases and common error types.  
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos — L38 “mechanism” → “mechanisms”; L237 claims validation evaluation while Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer now recommends acceptance based on the novel framework, strong 3-modality results, and compelling human-attention correlation improvements, though comparison to MCB is missing.  
- Outstanding question: Clarify the authors’ “MCB vs. MCT” numbers — they appear swapped and the reference for the 68.4% figure is unclear.
