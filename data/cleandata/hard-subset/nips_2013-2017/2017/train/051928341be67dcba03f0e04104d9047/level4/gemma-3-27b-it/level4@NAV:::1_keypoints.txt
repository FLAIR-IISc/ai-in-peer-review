- General impression: interesting method with strong results; attention maps suggest gains stem from improved attention rather than model capacity, but writing needs clearer presentation.
- Strength: proposed model is novel and achieves strong empirical performance.
- Strength: experiments are thorough and include both qualitative and quantitative analyses.
- Strength: the transition from unary/pairwise to the final attention mechanism is particularly noteworthy; missing visualization of marginalized trinary attention maps.
- Weakness: presentation and writing occasionally lack clarity and need restructuring for readability.
- Weakness: novelty feels somewhat incremental—largely an extension of MCB [7] to trinary and unary orders; authors should more explicitly state unique contributions.
- Weakness: probabilistic motivation is presented intuitively but not formally established; either formalize it or present it as motivation.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is at an unsuitable abstraction level—too detailed to convey the approach yet lacking operational detail; should be simplified to emphasize unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation.
- Weakness: Figure 3 appears unused and is not referenced in the text.
- Question: what specific values were learned for the linear coefficients combining the marginalized potentials (equation 1), and do modalities favor different potential orders?
- Question: why does the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperform compared to MCB [7], and can the authors relate this to switching to MCB/MCT decision modules?
- Question: could the authors elaborate on experimental observations comparing sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer for the decision head?
