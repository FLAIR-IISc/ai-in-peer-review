General Impression:
The proposed method is interesting, and the results are quite promising. The attention maps suggest that the performance gains may be attributed to improved attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the writing could be improved, as it is sometimes weak and confusing. With additional time, the authors may be able to refine the submission.

Strengths:
- The model is interesting, and the results are good.
- The qualitative and quantitative experiments are thorough.
- The transition between unary, pairwise, and final attentions is impressive, and I appreciate the comparison. However, I would have liked to see marginalized trinary attention maps as well. Were these included in the text or supplement, and I missed them?

Weaknesses:
- The writing and presentation are sometimes disorganized.
- The novelty of the method seems limited. Although the architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this added complexity is an extension of the intuition from [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. I suggest the authors clarify their contributions and any potential connections they may have missed.
- The probabilistic connection is not well-established. The authors should either formalize this connection or adjust the language to make it clear that it is primarily motivational.
- Figure 2 is at an intermediate level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. I recommend simplifying this figure to emphasize the unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, unless I missed it.

Some things I'm curious about:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients in equations (1) for combining the marginalized potentials? It would be interesting to see if different modalities utilize different potential orders.
- I find it intriguing that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite a similar architecture. Unfortunately, there is limited discussion about this result in the text. Could the authors provide some intuition behind this result, and is it related to the switch from MCB to MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion on using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was engaging, but no results were presented. Could the authors share their observations on this aspect?
