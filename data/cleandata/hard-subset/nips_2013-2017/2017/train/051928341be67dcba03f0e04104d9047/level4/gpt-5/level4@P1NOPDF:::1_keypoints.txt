- Proposed method is interesting with strong results; attention maps suggest gains come from improved attention mechanisms rather than increased model capacity.  
- Writing and presentation are sometimes weak and confusing and need improvement.  
- Strength: novel/interesting model with solid quantitative and qualitative experiments.  
- Strength: impressive contrast between unary/pairwise attentions and the final attention; reviewer would like to see marginalized trinary attention maps.  
- Weakness: novelty feels limited—added complexity appears to reiterate intuitions from MCB [7] at different orders; contributions should be clarified and distinguished.  
- Weakness: probabilistic connection is underdeveloped and reads more as motivation than a formal link; either formalize it or soften the language.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 is at an awkward abstraction level—neither operationally detailed nor cleanly summarizing the approach; simplify to show how unary/pairwise/trinary potentials are generated and combined.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 is not referenced in the text.  
- Question: What values were learned for the linear coefficients combining marginalized potentials in Eq.(1), and do different modalities rely on different potential orders?  
- Question: Why does the two-modality Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB [7] despite similar architecture—could it be related to switching from MCB to MCT decision modules?  
- Question: The discussion comparing sequential MCB layers to a single MCT layer in the decision head is interesting but lacks reported results—please share experimental observations.
