**General Impression:**
The proposed method is interesting, and the results are strong. The provided attention maps suggest that these performance gains are attributable to the improved attention mechanism itself, rather than simply an increase in model capacity. However, the writing is somewhat weak and occasionally confusing, which detracts from the overall quality of the submission. With revision, the authors could likely address these presentation issues.

**Strengths:**
- The paper introduces an interesting model that achieves strong empirical results.
- The evaluation is comprehensive, including both thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- The interplay between the unary, pairwise, and final attentions is a compelling aspect of the model. However, it would be beneficial to also visualize the marginalized trinary attention maps. It is unclear if these were included in the main text or the supplement.

**Weaknesses:**
- The primary weakness of the paper is its presentation, which is at times jumbled and difficult to follow.
- The novelty of the contribution could be better articulated. While the proposed architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this added complexity appears to stem from applying the core intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) order interactions. This is not a major flaw, but the authors should more clearly delineate their specific contributions relative to this prior work.
- The connection to probabilistic models is not drawn with sufficient rigor. As presented, it serves as a high-level motivation rather than a formal framework. The authors should either formalize this connection or adjust the language to clarify its motivational role.
- Figure 2 is presented at a challenging level of abstraction; it lacks the detail needed to fully understand the network's mechanics, yet is not abstract enough to provide a clear high-level overview of the approach. Simplifying this figure to more clearly emphasize the generation of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials would enhance the paper's clarity.
- Figure 3 does not appear to be referenced in the text.

**Questions for the Authors:**
- What are the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be insightful to see if different modalities rely on different orders of potentials.
- It is surprising that the "2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise" model underperforms the MCB model [7], given their architectural similarities. The paper would benefit from a discussion of the potential reasons for this result. Could this be related to the swap from MCB to MCT decision computation modules?
- The paper discusses the use of sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head, which is an interesting design choice. However, no corresponding results are presented. Could the authors elaborate on the empirical observations that informed this decision?
