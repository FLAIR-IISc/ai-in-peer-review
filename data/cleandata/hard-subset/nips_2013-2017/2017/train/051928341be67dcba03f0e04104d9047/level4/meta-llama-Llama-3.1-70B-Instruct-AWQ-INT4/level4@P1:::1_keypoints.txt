- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with mean-field inference, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Well-motivated framing that clearly explains limitations of existing attention mechanisms.
- Thorough evaluation with strong quantitative performance and impressive qualitative results.
- Unary potentials capture individual feature importance; pairwise/ternary capture feature correlations used to weight inputs.
- Presentation is sometimes unclear; model description could be more concise despite generally clear explanations and helpful figures.
- Novelty: builds on existing attention work — reviewer notes limited perceived novelty in places but ultimately considers it a clear advance.
- Related-work discussion is insufficiently detailed; needs more context on how this approach differs from prior methods.
- Paper should discuss limitations and potential future directions more explicitly.
- Experimental setup and evaluation metrics need more detailed description.
- Reviewer requests additional qualitative results to further demonstrate effectiveness.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address the above issues.
