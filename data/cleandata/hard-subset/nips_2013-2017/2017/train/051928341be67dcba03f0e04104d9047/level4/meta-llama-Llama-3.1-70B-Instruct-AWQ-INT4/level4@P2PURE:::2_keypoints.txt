- Proposes a versatile attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities, with potential generalizability to other multimodal tasks.
- Ablation study indicates ternary potentials substantially contribute to improved VQA performance.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- The notion of attention over answers is unclear for single-word answers and needs clarification and visualization.
- Primary performance gains appear to come from ternary potentials; the two-modality model generally does not outperform existing models (except HieCoAtt) and requires further explanation.
- Missing comparison to prior work that incorporates answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- No discussion of failure cases is provided.
- Minor errors/typos: "mechanism" should be plural, and there is an inconsistency between Line 237 (validation) and Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic multi-modal attention contribution and strong three-modality results, despite imperfect rebuttal on two-modality concerns.
- Attention-map evaluation against human maps in the rebuttal is promising but lacks comparison to state-of-the-art models like MCB.
- Possible numerical inconsistency in the authors' response regarding MCB vs. 3-MCT (3-MCT likely 69.4% not 68.4%; reported MCB 68.6% vs 68.4% mentioned) and needs clarification.
