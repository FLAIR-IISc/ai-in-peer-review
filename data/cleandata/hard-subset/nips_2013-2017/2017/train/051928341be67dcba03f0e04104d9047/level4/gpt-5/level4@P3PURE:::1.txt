General Impression:
Overall, the proposed method is interesting. The results are strong, and the attention maps suggest that the gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. The writing is at times weak and somewhat confusing, but with additional effort the presentation could be improved.

Strengths:
- Interesting model with good results.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- I am impressed by the progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention. I would also like to see marginalized trinary attention maps; if these are included in the text or supplement, please point them out.

Weaknesses:
- The writing/presentation is somewhat jumbled in places.
- The novelty feels limited. While the architecture is clearly more complex than, and outperforms, the MCB-for-VQA model [7], much of the added complexity appears to repeat the intuition of [7] at higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not necessarily a major issue, but the contributions should be clarified (including any I may have missed).
- The probabilistic connection is not drawn convincingly. As written, it does not seem formal enough to be more than motivational. Either make this connection more formal or adjust the language to reflect its primarily motivational role.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of abstraction: it is not detailed enough to convey the network’s functionality, yet not abstract enough to cleanly summarize the approach. Consider simplifying it to more clearly emphasize how unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials are generated.
- Figure 3 is never referenced (unless I missed it).

Questions and requests for clarification:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients that combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting to see whether different modalities rely on different potential orders.
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite the architectural similarity. There is little discussion of this result. Do you have intuition for why this occurs? Is it related to the swap between MCB and MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion comparing sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer for the decision head is intriguing, but no results are shown. Could you share what you observed?
