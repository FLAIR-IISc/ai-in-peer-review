- Proposes a generic attention formulation for multimodal problems (image, question, answer) using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.
- Ablation study isolates and shows the positive impact of ternary potentials on performance.
- Paper is clearly and concisely written.

- “Attention over answers” is unclear (answers often single-word); paper lacks visualizations and clarification of answer-attention.
- Table 1 implies gains mainly come from ternary potentials; without them the model rarely outperforms two-modality baselines, needing further discussion.
- Missing comparison to prior work that explicitly uses answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- No analysis of failure cases or model shortcomings to guide future work.
- Minor errors/typos: (a) L38 “mechanism” → “mechanisms”; (b) L237 says validation set but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.

- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and quantitative gains from three-modality attention, and cites rebuttal evidence that the attention maps correlate better with human attention.
- Remaining concern: authors’ rebuttal did not fully convince on relative performance versus two-modality models due to lack of quantitative evidence.
- Reviewer question: reported numbers for MCB vs. MCT appear inconsistent/swapped (confusion between 68.4%, 68.6%, and 69.4%).
