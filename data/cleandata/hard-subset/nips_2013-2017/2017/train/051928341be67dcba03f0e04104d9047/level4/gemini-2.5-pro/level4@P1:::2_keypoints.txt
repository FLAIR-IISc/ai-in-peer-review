- Generic, extensible multimodal attention framework that can apply beyond VQA.  
- Ablation study clearly shows ternary potentials provide significant performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- "Attention over answers" is not well-justified given mostly single-word answers; needs clearer explanation and answer-attention visualizations.  
- 2-modality (pairwise) model does not outperform many prior two-modality methods, raising questions about the unary/pairwise components' novelty and effectiveness.  
- Missing comparisons to other methods that use answers as input (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) despite the importance of the answer modality.  
- Only one failure example shown; a broader analysis of common failure modes is needed.  
- Minor issues: grammar/phrasing errors (intro sentence) and unclear description of which data splits are used for each reported result and qualitative example.
