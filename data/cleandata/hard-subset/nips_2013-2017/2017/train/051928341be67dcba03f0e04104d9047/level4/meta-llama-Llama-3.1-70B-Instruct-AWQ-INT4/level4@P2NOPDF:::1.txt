General Impression:
The proposed method is interesting, with impressive results and attention maps that suggest the gains are due to improved attention mechanisms rather than increased model capacity. However, the writing could be improved, as it is sometimes weak and confusing. With more time, the authors could refine the submission to better convey their ideas.

Strengths:
- The model is novel and achieves good results.
- The qualitative and quantitative experiments are thorough and well-executed.
- The transition between unary, pairwise, and final attention is impressive, and I appreciate the authors' efforts to visualize these mechanisms. I would have liked to see marginalized trinary attention maps included in the text or supplement, but it's possible I may have missed them.

Weaknesses:
- The writing and presentation are occasionally jumbled and could be clarified.
- The novelty of the approach feels somewhat limited, as it builds upon the intuition of [7] but at higher and lower orders. While this is not a major issue, the authors could better articulate their contributions and how they differ from existing work.
- The probabilistic connection is not well-established and feels more motivational than formal. The authors could either provide a more rigorous connection or adjust their language to reflect this.
- Figure 2 is at an awkward level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. I suggest simplifying the figure to emphasize the unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, which is confusing.

Some points of interest:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients in equations (1) for combining the marginalized potentials? It would be interesting to see if different modalities take advantage of different potential orders.
- I find it surprising that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite their similarities in architecture. The authors could provide more insight into this result and whether it is related to the swap from MCB to MCT decision computation modules.
- The discussion on using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was engaging, but unfortunately, no results were provided. The authors could elaborate on their observations and findings.
