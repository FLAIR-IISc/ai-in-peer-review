- Proposes an attention-based VQA model extending MCB to unary, pairwise, and trinary interactions; attention maps suggest gains come from refined attention, not just model capacity.  
- Presentation is disorganized and unclear; requires substantial rewriting and sharper formalization.  
- Core contribution is interesting but novelty needs stronger justification relative to prior work.

Strengths:
- Novel systematic exploration of attention orders (unary, pairwise, trinary) with strong quantitative results.  
- Thorough experiments with detailed qualitative analysis (attention maps) and ablation studies.  
- Compelling shift between unary/pairwise and final attention mechanisms; missing marginalized trinary maps noted.

Weaknesses:
- Clarity and presentation are confusing; key concepts like marginalization need clearer articulation.  
- Contribution seems incremental over [7]; authors must explain why trinary interactions are necessary and how redundancy/complexity is avoided.  
- Probabilistic motivation in Section 2.1 is underdeveloped and lacks formal derivation linking it to the loss.  
- Figure 2 is overly abstract and cluttered; should be simplified to show unary→pairwise→trinary potential generation.  
- Figure 3 is referenced but never discussed in results, a critical omission.

Key questions for authors:
- What values were learned for Equation (1) coefficients (λ₁, λ₂, λ₃)? Any modality-specific patterns?  
- Why does the Unary+Pairwise (2-modality) model underperform MCB [7]? Analyze architectural or decision-module causes.  
- Clarify trade-offs between sequential MCB and single MCT decision heads (accuracy, convergence), with supporting results.

Recommendation:
- Weak Accept with major revisions: fix presentation, clarify novelty, address Figure 3, strengthen or reframe probabilistic motivation, and explain 2-modality underperformance.

Specific revision requests:
- Rewrite novelty section to explicitly state differences from [7] and justify trinary layer.  
- Redesign Figure 2 to focus on unary/pairwise/trinary potential pipeline.  
- Integrate Figure 3 into results discussion or remove it.  
- Report learned coefficients from Equation (1) and discuss modality trends.  
- Add analysis explaining why Unary+Pairwise underperforms MCB.
