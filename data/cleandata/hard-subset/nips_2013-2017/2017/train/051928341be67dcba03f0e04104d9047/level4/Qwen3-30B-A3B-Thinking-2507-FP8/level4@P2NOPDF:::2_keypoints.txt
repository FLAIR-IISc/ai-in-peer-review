- Introduces a novel multimodal attention framework combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer; achieves SOTA on VQA with ternary potentials.  
- Strength: The attention mechanism is task-agnostic and generalizable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: Ablation study clearly isolates and demonstrates the impact of ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Weakness: "Attention over answers" is ambiguous for short answer tokens; mechanism needs clarification and visualizations.  
- Weakness: Major performance gains come from ternary potentials, but the paper lacks explanation for why ternary interactions outperform 2-modality approaches.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons are incomplete—should include models that take answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- Weakness: No failure analysis or error-case discussion is provided.  
- Weakness: Minor errors (L38 pluralization; L237 mismatch between claimed validation vs. table reporting test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors’ response on 2-modality performance lacked quantitative evidence, but reported attention-map correlation with human attention is a strong positive.  
- Post-rebuttal: Improvement over HieCoAtt is evident, though comparison with other SOTA baselines (e.g., MCB) remains incomplete.  
- Critical query: Rebuttal reports inconsistent numbers (claims 2-layer MCB 69.4% vs. Table 1 MCB 68.6%); likely misattribution between MCB and 3-MCT and needs clarification.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—framework is a meaningful advance, but final paper should address answer-attention clarity, 2-modality justification, baseline comparisons, and failure analysis.
