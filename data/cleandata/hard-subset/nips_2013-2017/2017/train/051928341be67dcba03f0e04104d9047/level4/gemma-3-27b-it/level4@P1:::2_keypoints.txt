- Generic multimodal attention formulation with potential to generalize beyond specific tasks.
- Ablation study shows ternary potentials substantially impact performance.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Unclear benefit of attending to answers; lacks visualizations of answer-focused attention.
- Primary gains appear driven by ternary potentials; without them the model often doesn't outperform two-modality baselines (except HieCoAtt).
- Needs comparison to prior models that incorporate answer information (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- Missing analysis of failure cases.
- Minor errors/typos: L38 “mechanism”→“mechanisms”; L237 claims validation set while Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer leans toward acceptance despite insufficient quantitative rebuttal on two-modality performance.
- Rebuttal reports better correlation with human attention maps, but lacks comparison to SOTA like MCB.
- Reviewer questions reported numbers for MCT vs MCB (possible swap: 69.4 vs 68.4) and discrepancy with Table 1 MCB 68.6.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generic approach, SOTA VQA with ternary potentials, promising human attention alignment.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited improvement without ternary potentials, missing related-work comparisons using answers, and absent failure-case analysis.
