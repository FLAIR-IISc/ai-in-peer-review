## Review

**General Impression:**

The proposed method presents an interesting approach, and the reported results are promising. The attention maps provide compelling evidence that the observed performance gains are likely attributable to the improved attention mechanisms, rather than simply increased model capacity. While the writing could benefit from further refinement for clarity and flow, I believe the authors could address these issues with additional revisions.

**Strengths:**

- The model is interesting and achieves strong results.
- The experiments are thorough, encompassing both qualitative and quantitative analyses.
- The transition from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention mechanism is particularly noteworthy. I would have appreciated seeing visualizations of the marginalized trinary attention maps; perhaps these are included in the text or supplementary materials, but I did not locate them.

**Weaknesses:**

- The presentation and writing occasionally lack clarity and can feel disjointed.
- The novelty of the approach, while present, feels somewhat incremental. The architecture demonstrably surpasses the MCB for VQA model [7] in performance, but a significant portion of this increased complexity appears to extend the intuitions of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. While not a critical flaw, I recommend the authors more explicitly articulate the specific contributions of their work.
- The connection to probabilistic reasoning is not fully developed. It currently reads as motivational rather than formally established. I suggest either strengthening this connection with a more rigorous formulation or adjusting the language to reflect its current status.
- Figure 2’s level of abstraction is problematic. It lacks the detail necessary to fully understand the network’s functionality, yet it is also not sufficiently abstract to provide a clear overview of the approach. Simplifying this figure to better highlight the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation would be beneficial.
- Figure 3 appears to be unused, as it is not referenced anywhere in the manuscript (to the best of my knowledge).

**Questions for the Authors:**

- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be insightful to know if different modalities leverage different potential orders.
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model’s underperformance compared to MCB [7] is surprising, given the architectural similarities. I was disappointed to find limited discussion of this result in the text. Do the authors have any intuition regarding this observation, and could it be related to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion regarding the use of sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was intriguing. Could the authors share any observations from their experiments comparing these approaches?
