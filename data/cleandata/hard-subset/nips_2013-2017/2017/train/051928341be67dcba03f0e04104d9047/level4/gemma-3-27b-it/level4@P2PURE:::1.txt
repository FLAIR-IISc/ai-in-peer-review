General Impression:
The proposed method is interesting and achieves strong results. The attention maps provide evidence that these gains are likely attributable to the improved attention mechanisms, rather than simply increased model capacity. While the writing could be improved for clarity and flow, I believe the authors could address these issues with further refinement.

Strengths:
- The model is compelling and delivers good performance.
- The experiments are thorough, both qualitatively and quantitatively.
- I am impressed by the progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention mechanism. However, I would have appreciated seeing visualizations of the marginalized trinary attention maps; I may have overlooked these in the text or supplementary material.

Weaknesses:
- The writing and presentation were occasionally disorganized and difficult to follow.
- The novelty of the approach feels somewhat limited. While the architecture outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7] and is more complex, much of this complexity appears to extend the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. This is not a critical issue, but I recommend the authors more clearly articulate the specific contributions of this work (and any that I may have missed).
- The probabilistic connection is not fully developed. It is presented more as motivation than a formal derivation. I suggest the authors either strengthen the formalization of this connection or adjust the language to reflect its motivational role.
- Figure 2 is at an unsuitable level of abstraction, lacking sufficient detail to understand the network’s functionality while also being too complex to easily grasp the overall approach. Simplifying this figure to more clearly emphasize the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation would be beneficial.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the main text (unless I missed it).

Some things I'm curious about:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equation (1)? It would be interesting to know if different modalities benefitted from different potential orders.
- I found it surprising that the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7], given their similar architectures. I was disappointed that this result was not discussed in more detail. Do the authors have any intuition regarding this outcome? Is it related to the switch to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
- The discussion of using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head was insightful, but no results were presented. Could the authors elaborate on their observations?
