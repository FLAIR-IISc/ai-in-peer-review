- Proposes high-order attention modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across modalities for VQA using probabilistic potentials and mean-field inference.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance (64.57% test-dev) with fewer parameters and lower embedding dimensionality than prior work.  
- Quantitative comparisons are thorough and well-motivated, showing gains beyond model capacity.  
- Attention visualizations compellingly show improved alignment between question semantics and image regions.  
- Clear experimental design with useful ablations (3-modality vs. 2-modality, MCT vs. 2-layer MCB) and analysis of embedding dimension effects.  
- Technical exposition is sometimes convoluted; key equations and figures (e.g., Eq. 3, Fig. 2, Fig. 3) lack clarity or proper referencing.  
- Novelty is incremental: ternary correlations are new but core mechanism resembles prior bilinear/MCB frameworks and needs clearer differentiation.  
- Probabilistic motivation is weakly formalized; Eq. (1) is not derived from a probabilistic model or log-likelihood and lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Missing analyses: insufficient exploration of why the 2-modality model underperforms prior work and lack of contextualization for MCB vs. MCT differences.  
- Suggest explicitly clarifying the probabilistic connection and how it differs from bilinear pooling approaches.  
- Suggest simplifying and properly referencing Figures 2 and 3 to emphasize unary/pairwise/ternary potential generation.  
- Suggest addressing the 2-modality performance gap by discussing embedding dimension vs. attention mechanism effects.  
- Suggest adding ternary attention visualizations to validate high-order interaction claims.  
- Significance: solid, broadly applicable contribution to multimodal attention with practical VQA improvements, but not revolutionary.  
- Verdict: Weak Accept with major revisions — technically sound and empirically strong but needs clearer exposition and stronger claim differentiation from prior work.
