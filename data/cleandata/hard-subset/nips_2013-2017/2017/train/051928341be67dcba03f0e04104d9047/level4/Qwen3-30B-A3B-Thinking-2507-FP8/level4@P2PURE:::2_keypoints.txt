- Paper proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities.
- Method achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA when ternary potentials are included.
- Strength: Generic, task-agnostic multimodal attention formulation that could generalize beyond VQA.
- Strength: Ablation study isolates the contribution of ternary potentials.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: Unclear what "attention over answers" means in practice; no visualizations of answer attention are provided.
- Weakness: Primary performance gain appears to come from ternary potentials; without them the model often does not outperform existing two-modality models (except HieCoAtt).
- Weakness: Paper should compare against prior work that treats answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).
- Weakness: No analysis of failure cases is included.
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos (e.g., "mechanism"→"mechanisms") and inconsistency about which splits were evaluated (line 237 vs Table 1).
- Post-rebuttal: Rebuttal did not adequately address the two-modality comparison quantitatively, but reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework and strong 3-modality results.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors’ attention maps show higher correlation with human attention than existing models, which is promising though SOTA comparisons (e.g., MCB) lack reported correlation values.
- Question for authors: Reported figures for MCB vs MCT (68.4% vs 69.4%) appear inconsistent and possibly swapped, and Table 1 lists MCB as 68.6%—please clarify which numbers correspond to which models and splits.
