General Impressions:

Overall, I think the proposed method is interesting. The results are quite good, and the attention maps suggest that these improvements may be due not only to the increased capacity of the model but also to an improved attention process. I found the text to be somewhat weak and occasionally confusing, although I believe that if the authors had more time, they could have improved the submitted work.

Strengths:

- Interesting model with good results.
- Thorough qualitative and quantitative testing.
- I was quite impressed by the changes between unitary/parzowাইজ attention and ultimate attention. However, it would have been nice to see the marginal ternary attention maps somewhere. Did I miss these in the text or supplement?

Weaknesses:

- As mentioned above, I found the text/presentation to be somewhat unstructured at times.
- The novelty here seems somewhat limited. Undoubtedly, the architecture is more complex and performs better than the MCB [7] for the PQ model, but most of this added complexity repeats the concept of high (ternary) and low (unitary) order [7]. I don't think this is a major problem, but I would advise the authors to clarify these contributions (and if I have missed anything).
- I don't think the potential connection is drawn very well. It doesn't seem formal enough to be considered something inspiring, which is fine, but I would advise the authors to either make this connection more formal or clarify it by changing the language.
- Figure 2 is at a strange level of abstraction, where it is neither detailed enough to understand the network's functionality nor abstract enough for easy understanding of the method's outline. I would suggest trying to simplify this figure to emphasize the unitary/parzowাইজ/ternary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not mentioned, unless I missed it.

Some things I'm curious about:

- What values were learned for the linear coefficients to integrate the marginal probabilities in equation (1)? It would be interesting if different methods used different potential sequences.
- I find it interesting that the 2-modality unitary + parzowাইজ model performs worse than the MCB [7], although the architecture is similar. I was disappointed that it was not discussed more in the text. Is there any idea of this result? Is it related to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules being swapped?
- The discussion about sequential MCB versus using a single MCT layer for the decision head was quite interesting, but no results were shown. Could the authors say a little about what they saw?
