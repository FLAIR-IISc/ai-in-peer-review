- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer for VQA, with improved performance when ternary potentials are used.  
- Strength: The generic attention formulation could generalize beyond VQA.  
- Strength: Ablation study isolates the contribution of ternary potentials.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Unclear what "attention over answers" means in practice, especially given single-token answers and no visualizations of answer attention.  
- Weakness: Main performance gains appear to stem from the ternary potential; without it the model fails to outperform prior two-modality methods (except HieCoAtt).  
- Weakness: Needs comparison to prior methods that incorporate answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).  
- Weakness: No analysis of failure cases or model limitations.  
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos and reporting inconsistency (line 38 wording; line 237 says validation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework, strong three-modality SOTA results, and compelling human-attention correlation evidence.  
- Post-rebuttal: Remaining concerns about insufficient quantitative evidence for two-modality performance and missing comparative numbers for SOTA models.  
- Post-rebuttal: Requests clarification on inconsistent MCB vs. MCT reported numbers (confusion between 68.4, 68.6, and 69.4%).
