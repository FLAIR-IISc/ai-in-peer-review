- Introduces a generic attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer modalities for VQA.
- Demonstrates improved VQA performance when ternary potentials are included.
- Strength: Generic multimodal attention formulation is versatile and potentially generalizes across tasks.
- Strength: Ablation study shows the impact of ternary potentials by evaluating performance without them.
- Strength: Paper is well-structured and clearly written, aiding comprehension.
- Weakness: Attention over answers (especially single- vs multiword handling) is unclear and lacks visualizations.
- Weakness: Table 1 suggests gains primarily come from ternary potentials; without them the model is not superior to existing two-modality methods except versus HieCoAtt.
- Weakness: Needs comparison to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al. ECCV16) given ternary potentials’ large effect.
- Weakness: Paper omits discussion of failure cases, which would guide future improvements.
- Weakness: Typos and inconsistencies—“mechanism” should be plural (line 38) and evaluation split reported (line 237) conflicts with Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).
- Post-rebuttal: Authors’ additional quantitative results and higher correlation of attention maps with human maps support acceptance, but lack of SOTA correlation values (e.g., MCB) limits comparison.
- Post-rebuttal/question: Reported accuracy numbers for MCB vs. MCT are inconsistent/confusing (68.4% vs. 69.4% vs. Table 1’s 68.6%), suggesting a possible mistake or unclear reference.
