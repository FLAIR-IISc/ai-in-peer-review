- Proposes a general probabilistic high-order attention framework modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for multimodal tasks, demonstrated on VQA.  
- Combines potentials via mean-field inference, differing from hierarchical co-attention and feature-fusion approaches.  
- The full three-modality (ternary) model achieves state-of-the-art results on the standard VQA dataset.  
- Strength: The framework is general, principled, and potentially applicable beyond VQA.  
- Strength: Ablation study isolates and quantifies the benefit of the ternary (high-order) component.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly explains the probabilistic formulation and inference.  
- Weakness: The concept of "attention over answers" is unclear and needs more justification and qualitative analysis.  
- Weakness: The two-modality (unary+pairwise) model is not competitive with existing two-modality methods, raising questions about its standalone novelty and effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to other models that incorporate answer information (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- Weakness: No qualitative analysis of failure cases to illuminate model limitations.  
- Minor: Typo ("mechanism" → "mechanisms") and unclear reporting of which datasets (val vs test-dev/test-std) correspond to results in Table 1.  
- Summary/Recommendation: Novel, empirically strong, and theoretically grounded work recommended for acceptance, contingent on addressing clarifications and additional comparisons.
