- Introduces a generic framework unifying unary/pairwise/ternary potentials across image, question, and answer for VQA; achieves SOTA when ternary potentials are used.

- Strength: The multimodal attention formulation is valuable and may generalize beyond VQA.

- Strength: Ablation study clearly isolates and quantifies the impact of ternary potentials.

- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly structured.

- Weakness: "Attention over answers" is unclear—answers are treated as atomic units and no visualizations or mechanism details are provided.

- Weakness: Table 1 indicates ternary potentials drive most gains; the two-modality variant fails to outperform baselines (except HieCoAtt), raising questions about fundamental limitations.

- Weakness: Should compare to models that explicitly incorporate answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016), not only two-modality baselines.

- Weakness: Missing analysis of failure cases where the model struggles.

- Weakness: Minor errors—L38 "mechanism"→"mechanisms"; L237 claims validation evaluation while Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std.

- Post-rebuttal: Despite insufficient rebuttal on Weakness 2, the reviewer maintains acceptance recommendation because the 3-modality results outperform SOTA and the framework is a meaningful contribution.

- Post-rebuttal: Quantitative comparison of attention maps against human annotations is compelling, showing stronger correlation than prior models (e.g., MCB vs HieCoAtt).

- Post-rebuttal question: Reported MCB/MCT numbers appear inconsistent (rebuttal claims 3-MCT 68.4% vs 2-layer MCB 69.4%, but Table 1 shows 2-layer MCB 68.6%); authors should clarify these discrepancies.
