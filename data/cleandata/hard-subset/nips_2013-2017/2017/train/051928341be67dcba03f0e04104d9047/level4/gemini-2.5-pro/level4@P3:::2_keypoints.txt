- Proposes a novel probabilistic high-order attention framework using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with a single mean-field inference step, evaluated on VQA.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA benchmark and ablation study shows ternary potentials significantly contribute to improvement.
- Presents a principled, extensible formulation for handling more than two modalities, distinguishing it from co-attention and bilinear pooling methods.
- Paper is well-written and organized; model decomposition and figures (e.g., Figure 3) aid understanding.
- The notion of "attention over answers" is unclear given most VQA answers are single tokens; needs detailed explanation and visualizations of answer attention.
- The 2-modality variant underperforms compared to strong 2-modality baselines (e.g., MCB), raising questions about whether gains stem mainly from adding the answer modality.
- Lacks direct comparison to other methods that use answer embeddings (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016) to contextualize the benefit of high-order attention.
- Qualitative analysis shows only successes; failure case analysis is missing and would clarify model limitations.
- Overall assessment: important, novel contribution with strong empirical results; recommended for acceptance pending clarifications and additional analyses.
- Minor issues: typo ("mechanism"→"mechanisms"), inconsistent reporting of val vs. test-dev/test-std splits in Table 1, and unclear explanation of reported MCB/2-layer baseline differences.
