- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation (unary, pairwise, ternary) for image, question, and answer in VQA with potential to generalize beyond a single task.
- Ablation study isolates the effect of the ternary potential, showing its contribution to performance.
- Paper is clearly written.

- Unclear what "attention over answers" means given most answers are single tokens and no attention visualizations over answers are provided; request clarification.
- Main performance gain appears to come from the ternary potential; without it the model generally does not outperform existing 2-modality models—needs explanation and quantitative evidence.
- Missing comparison to prior work that uses answers as inputs (e.g., Revisiting Visual Question Answering Baselines, Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- No analysis of failure cases or error modes of the proposed model.
- Minor errors/typos: L38 mechanism→mechanisms; L237 states evaluation on validation but Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std.

- Post-rebuttal: reviewer leans toward acceptance due to the generic framework and improved 3-modality results; reported human-attention correlation looks promising though not compared to SOTA like MCB.
- Asks for clarification on author-reported MCB vs MCT numbers (possible swapping/confusion between 68.4% and 69.4%, while Table 1 shows MCB 68.6%).
