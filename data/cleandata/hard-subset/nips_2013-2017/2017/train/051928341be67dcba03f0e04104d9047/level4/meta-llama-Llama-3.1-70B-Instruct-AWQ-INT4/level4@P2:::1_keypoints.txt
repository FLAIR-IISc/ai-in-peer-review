- Main idea: Proposes a novel attention mechanism for VQA that models high-order correlations via a probabilistic model with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials; reports state-of-the-art results on VQA.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on existing VQA attention methods (e.g., Lu et al., MCB by Fukui et al.) and claims greater generality and extensibility to more modalities.  
- Strength: Achieves SOTA performance on VQA.  
- Strength: Provides thorough quantitative and qualitative experiments.  
- Strength: Attention maps appear to demonstrate improved attention and performance gains.  
- Weakness: Writing and presentation need clearer, more coherent exposition.  
- Weakness: Perceived novelty is limited since it extends existing attention approaches.  
- Weakness: Probabilistic link between this model and prior work is not clearly established.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 lacks detail to understand the network; Figure 3 is not referenced.  
- Specific comment: Clarify learned values for linear coefficients in eq.(1) and how different modalities leverage different potential orders.  
- Specific comment: Explain why the 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB despite similar architecture.  
- Specific comment: Provide more results and discussion comparing sequential MCB and single MCT layers in the decision head.  
- For acceptance: Strong empirical results and comprehensive evaluations support acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Limited novelty and unclear presentation argue against acceptance.  
- Quality: Technically sound with clear model/experiment description, but presentation quality should improve.  
- Clarity: Paper organization is reasonable but explanations and prose require refinement.  
- Originality: Extends previous attention mechanisms and claims generality, but novelty is modest.  
- Significance: SOTA results and comprehensive experiments make the work significant for the field.
