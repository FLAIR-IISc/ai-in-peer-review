- Introduces a novel high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Core idea: learn multi-order potentials to guide attention to relevant elements within each modality.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset with qualitative visualizations showing improved attention focus.  
- Extends prior attention and bilinear/probabilistic models by handling arbitrary numbers of modalities, offering a more general and flexible approach.  
- Strong empirical contribution: comprehensive quantitative results, visual attention maps, and an ablation comparing MCT and MCB decision modules.  
- Clear problem motivation: articulates limitations of existing ad-hoc, task-specific attention mechanisms.  
- Writing is sometimes dense and confusing, hindering clarity of technical details.  
- The probabilistic framing is intuitive but lacks rigorous mathematical formalization.  
- Figure 2 is poorly pitched (neither detailed nor high-level enough) and should better emphasize unary/pairwise/ternary potential generation; Figure 3 is defined but not referenced.  
- The 2-modality model (unary+pairwise) underperforms the MCB baseline, which is puzzling and needs deeper analysis.  
- Need to investigate the influence of decision computation modules (MCB vs MCT) on observed performance differences.  
- Request to report learned linear coefficients (α, β, γ) from Equation (1) to reveal reliance on different potential orders.  
- Ask for visualizations of marginalized ternary attention maps to better understand ternary potential effects.  
- Request more details/results from the ablation comparing sequential MCB vs single MCT decision heads.  
- Ask to clarify the precise novelty relative to existing methods (e.g., is it higher-order extension or specific implementation details?).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical results and meaningful qualitative analyses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear writing, insufficient probabilistic formalization, and unexplained underperformance of the 2-modality model.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, formalize probabilistic connections, and address the 2-modality performance discussion.
