- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation for VQA combining unary, pairwise and ternary potentials over image, question and answer.
- Evaluated on VQA; ternary potentials yield improved performance and an ablation study quantifies the ternary contribution.
- Strength: Generic attention formulation could generalize beyond the specific task.
- Strength: Ablation study is useful to isolate the effect of ternary potentials.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: Unclear what attention over answers means given mostly single-word answers and no visualizations—authors should clarify and show examples.
- Weakness: Main improvements seem to come from ternary potentials; without them the model does not outperform existing 2-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), needing further explanation.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Revisiting VQA Baselines by Jabri et al.) despite ternary being central.
- Weakness: No discussion or analysis of failure cases and error modes.
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos (L38: mechanism→mechanisms; L237 mentions validation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std).
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer leans to accept based on the generic framework and strong 3-modality results plus improved correlation with human attention maps, despite lingering doubts about 2-modality performance.
- Open question: Confusion over authors' MCB vs MCT numbers (reported 68.4% vs 69.4% and Table 1’s MCB=68.6%)—reviewer asks for clarification which figures are correct.
