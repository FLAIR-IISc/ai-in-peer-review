

General Impression:  
The proposed method presents an interesting approach with strong empirical results. The attention maps effectively suggest that performance gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than merely increased model capacity. However, the presentation is somewhat unclear and occasionally confusing; with additional refinement, the authors could significantly strengthen the submission.

Strengths:  
- The model demonstrates compelling results alongside thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation.  
- The shift from unary to pairwise to final attention mechanisms is particularly insightful. I would have appreciated seeing marginalized trinary attention maps included in the text or supplement—please confirm whether these were omitted or I missed them.  

Weaknesses:  
- The presentation is inconsistent in clarity, with sections that are jumbled or difficult to follow.  
- The novelty is somewhat limited: while the architecture is more complex and outperforms MCB [7], much of the added complexity merely extends the intuition of [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should more explicitly clarify the specific contributions and address any additional distinctions.  
- The probabilistic connection lacks formal justification; it currently serves only as motivational text. The authors should either strengthen this connection with rigorous formalization or adjust the language to avoid implying stronger theoretical grounding.  
- Figure 2 is poorly balanced—it lacks sufficient detail to understand the network’s functionality yet is overly detailed to convey the core approach. Simplify this figure to emphasize the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation process.  
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the main text, creating a disconnect.  

Key Questions:  
- What learned coefficients were used for combining marginalized potentials in Equation (1)? It would be valuable to see if different modalities leverage distinct potential orders.  
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperforms MCB [7] despite architectural similarities. The authors should address this discrepancy—could it relate to the switch from MCB’s decision module to MCT?  
- The discussion of sequential MCB versus single MCT layers for the decision head is intriguing, but no results are presented. Please elaborate on observed differences.
