- Proposes a generic, task-agnostic multimodal attention framework that could generalize beyond VQA.  
- Includes an ablation study that isolates and highlights the importance of the ternary potential.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  

- The "attention over answers" concept is insufficiently explained and lacks visualizations to show its utility.  
- Performance gains appear driven mainly by the ternary potential; the 2-modality (no-answer) model does not beat most contemporaries, questioning unary/pairwise effectiveness.  
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete; missing comparisons to other models that use answers during training (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV 2016).  
- No qualitative analysis of failure cases is provided to reveal where the model struggles.  
- Minor issues: grammatical error ("mechanism"→"mechanisms") and inconsistency between stated evaluation set (validation) and Table 1 (test-dev/test-std).  

- Rebuttal added results but did not fully convince regarding 2-modality performance due to lack of quantitative evidence.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the framework’s novelty and strong 3-modality results that outperform prior work.  
- Rebuttal’s human-attention correlation is a positive interpretability signal, though comparisons to SOTA (e.g., MCB) are missing.  

- Final clarification requested: confirm whether the reported MCB vs MCT scores are swapped and reconcile the MCB score discrepancy (68.4% vs 68.6%).
