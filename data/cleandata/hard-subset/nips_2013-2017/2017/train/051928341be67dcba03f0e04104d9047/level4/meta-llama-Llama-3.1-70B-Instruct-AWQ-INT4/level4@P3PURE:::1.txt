General Impression:
The proposed method is interesting, and the results are quite good. The attention maps provide insight into the potential gains, suggesting that these improvements may be attributed to enhanced attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. However, the writing could be improved, as it sometimes appears weak and confusing. With additional time, the authors may be able to refine the submission.

Strengths:
- The model is interesting, and the results are promising.
- The paper presents thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments.
- The transition between unary, pairwise, and final attentions is impressive, although it would be beneficial to include marginalized trinary attention maps for further clarification. It is unclear if these were overlooked in the text or supplement.

Weaknesses:
- The writing and presentation are occasionally jumbled, which can hinder understanding.
- The novelty of the method seems somewhat limited. While the architecture is more complex and outperforms the MCB for VQA model [7], much of this added complexity is an extension of the intuition presented in [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders. The authors should clarify their contributions and address any potential oversights.
- The probabilistic connection is not clearly established. The current presentation appears more motivational than formal, which may undermine its significance. The authors should either formalize this connection or adjust the language to provide clarity.
- Figure 2 is at an intermediate level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. It would be beneficial to simplify this figure to emphasize the unary, pairwise, and trinary potential generation more clearly.
- Figure 3 is not referenced in the text, which may indicate an oversight.

Some points of interest:
- It would be interesting to know the learned values for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in equations (1). This information could provide insight into whether different modalities take advantage of different potential orders.
- The underperformance of the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model compared to MCB [7], despite their similar architectures, is noteworthy. A more in-depth discussion of this result in the text would be beneficial. Any intuition behind this outcome, potentially related to the switch from MCB/MCT decision computation modules, would be appreciated.
- The discussion on using sequential MCB versus a single MCT layer for the decision head is intriguing, but lacks supporting results. The authors should provide more information on their observations regarding this aspect.
