- Paper proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, improving VQA when ternary potentials are included.
- Strength: Task-agnostic multimodal attention that may generalize beyond VQA.
- Strength: Ablation removing ternary potentials helps quantify the ternary term’s contribution.
- Strength: The paper is clearly written.
- Weakness: Unclear what “attention over answers” means given mostly single-token answers and no answer-attention visualizations; clarification requested.
- Weakness: Table 1 suggests gains mainly come from ternary potentials; without ternary the two-modality model does not outperform prior work (except HieCoAtt)—please elaborate.
- Weakness: Should compare to methods that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16).
- Weakness: No analysis or discussion of failure cases and failure modes.
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos (L38: mechanism -> mechanisms) and reporting inconsistency (L237 says validation but Table 1 reports test-dev/test-std).
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework and strong three-modality SOTA results despite incomplete rebuttal evidence for two-modality performance.
- Post-rebuttal: Reported higher correlation with human attention maps is encouraging, though comparison to top models like MCB is missing.
- Post-rebuttal question: Authors’ “MCB vs. MCT” numbers appear confused—are 68.4% and 69.4% reversed, and how do these relate to Table 1’s 68.6% MCB?
