This research paper presents a new method for focusing on image and question in Visual Question Answering (VQA) tasks. The main novelty lies in proposing a common thread of attention for multiple modalities – a combination of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for the 3 modalities (image, question, and answer) in VQA. The proposed approach is evaluated on VQA datasets and performs better than existing models when ternary potentials are used.

**Strengths:**
1. A common thread of attention for multimodal problems that is not specific to a particular task is useful, especially if it can be generalized for multiple tasks.
2. An ablation study showing performance without ternary potentials is useful for understanding how much ternary potentials help.
3. The research paper is clearly written.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The approach mentions attention on 3 modalities – image, question, and answer. However, it is not clear what attention on answers means because most answers are single words, and even if they are multi-word, they are considered single words. The research paper does not present any visualizations for attention on answers. Therefore, I would like the authors to clarify this.
2. From the results in Table 1, it seems that the main improvement in the proposed model is coming from ternary potentials. Without ternary potentials, the proposed model is not performing better than existing models (except for HieCoAtt) for the establishment of 2 modalities. Therefore, I would like the authors to shed light on this.
3. Since ternary potentials seem to be the main factor in the performance improvement of the proposed model, I would like the authors to compare the proposed model with existing models where answers are also used as input, such as Revisit Visual Question Answering Baselines (Jabri et al., ECCV16).
4. The research paper does not discuss failure cases of the proposed model. It would be insightful to focus on failure modes so that future research can be guided accordingly.
5. Other errors/typos:
a. L38: mechanism -> mechanisms
b. L237 mentions that the evaluation is on the validation set. However, Table 1 shows numbers on the test-dev and test-STD sets?

Post-rebuttal comments:
Although the authors' response to the concern that "the proposed model is not performing better than existing models for 2 modalities" does not seem satisfactory due to the lack of quantitative evidence, I would like to recommend acceptance because the research paper proposes a common attention framework for multiple modalities, and the 3-modality attention performs better than SOTA. The quantitative evaluation of the proposed model's attention maps against human attention maps (reported in the rebuttal) also looks good and suggests that attention maps correlate more with human maps than existing models. However, we do not know what this correlation value is for SOTA models like MCB, I think it is still quite better than HieCoAtt.
