- Contribution: Proposes k-th order attention for multimodal tasks, explicitly modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials for VQA.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance with a smaller model (40M parameters) and strong quantitative benchmarks.  
- Qualitative: Attention maps align with task-relevant image regions; figures illustrate unary/pairwise contributions.  
- Method novelty: Generalizes prior attention work (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) to higher-order interactions and frames it probabilistically via mean-field-style marginalization.  
- Decision module: Uses tensor-sketch-based MCT/MCB decision module, well-motivated and consistently implemented.  
- Experimental rigor: Thorough evaluation and ablation (3-modality vs. 2-modality) validate the need for high-order modeling.  
- Practical significance: Simpler model yields SOTA results, indicating practical value and applicability to other multimodal tasks.  
- Writing/clarity: Method section is dense and sometimes unclear (e.g., role of coefficients α, β, γ); Fig. 2 is confusing and Fig. 3 is unreferenced.  
- Novelty concerns: Overstates novelty relative to [7]; extension to ternary is incremental without stronger theoretical justification.  
- Probabilistic framing: The mean-field/probabilistic connection is presented but underdeveloped and not formalized as a major theoretical contribution.  
- Missing analysis: Lacks explanation for why 2-modality unary+pairwise underperforms MCB and missing 2-layer MCB vs. MCT results to support expressiveness claims.  
- Qualitative gap: No ternary/answer-focused attention maps shown to validate ternary potential design.  
- Revision suggestions: Revise Figures 2–3, clarify probabilistic framework (mean-field status), add analysis for 2-modality gap and ternary maps, and better contrast with MCB/[7].  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — technically sound, significant for VQA, and appropriate for the venue.
