- Proposes a novel generic attention framework for multimodal input (image, question, answer) using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance when ternary potentials are included.  
- Extends prior attention work by modeling higher-order correlations and generalizing to any number of modalities.  

- Strength: Framework is general and applicable beyond VQA (e.g., image captioning, machine comprehension).  
- Strength: Incorporating ternary potentials demonstrably improves performance, highlighting the importance of three-way interactions.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with accessible mathematical formulation and clear system architecture.  
- Strength: Ablation study supports the contribution of ternary potentials.  

- Weakness: The notion of “attention” over answers is unclear given short answer texts and lacks visualization to demonstrate utility.  
- Weakness: Model heavily depends on ternary potentials; without them performance is not competitive, raising questions about the framework’s core benefit.  
- Weakness: Missing direct comparison to answer-as-input methods (e.g., Jabri et al.) to contextualize learning attention over answers.  
- Weakness: No analysis of failure cases to show where the model struggles.  
- Weakness: Minor textual errors (e.g., L38 pluralization, L237 reporting validation vs. test results).  

- For acceptance: Introduces a novel, broadly applicable attention framework with SOTA results and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: Overreliance on ternary potentials, unclear answer-attention utility, missing comparisons, and lack of failure analysis weaken the paper.  

- Overall: Valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify and visualize answer attention; analyze performance without ternary potentials and compare to two-modality baselines; include comparisons to answer-as-input methods; discuss failure cases and future work; fix minor errors.
