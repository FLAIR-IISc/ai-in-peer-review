**General Impression**

The proposed high-order attention model for visual question answering (VQA) is an interesting and promising approach. The results presented in the paper are impressive, and the attention maps provide valuable insights into the model's decision-making process. However, the writing and presentation could be improved for better clarity and flow.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting model with good results**: The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, and the attention maps demonstrate its ability to focus on relevant regions and words.
2. **Thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments**: The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the model, including both qualitative and quantitative results, which helps to build confidence in its effectiveness.
3. **Impressive shift between unary/pairwise attentions and final attention**: The model's ability to capture high-order correlations between different modalities is a significant strength, and the attention maps illustrate this capability effectively.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Writing and presentation**: The paper's writing and presentation could be improved for better clarity and flow. Some sections feel a bit jumbled, and the text could benefit from more concise and clear explanations.
2. **Novelty**: While the proposed model is an extension of previous work, its novelty feels a bit limited. The authors could clarify the contributions of their work and how it differs from previous approaches.
3. **Probabilistic connection**: The probabilistic connection between the attention mechanism and the decision-making process is not formally established, and the language used to describe this connection could be more precise.
4. **Figure 2**: The figure is at an odd level of abstraction, making it difficult to understand the network's functionality. Simplifying the figure to emphasize the unary/pairwise/trinary potential generation could improve its clarity.
5. **Figure 3**: The figure is not referenced in the text, which makes it unclear what its purpose is.

**Questions and Suggestions**

1. **Linear coefficients**: What values were learned for the linear coefficients for combining the marginalized potentials in equations (1)? It would be interesting to see if different modalities took advantage of different potential orders.
2. **2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model**: The underperformance of the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model compared to MCB [7] is not discussed in the text. The authors could provide some intuition into this result and whether it is related to the swap to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules.
3. **Sequential MCB vs single MCT layers**: The discussion of using sequential MCB vs a single MCT layers for the decision head is interesting, but no results are shown. The authors could provide some insights into what was observed during the experiments.
4. **Marginalized trinary attention maps**: The paper does not include marginalized trinary attention maps, which would be interesting to see. The authors could consider adding these to the supplementary material or the main text.

Overall, the proposed high-order attention model is a promising approach to VQA, and with some improvements to the writing and presentation, it has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
