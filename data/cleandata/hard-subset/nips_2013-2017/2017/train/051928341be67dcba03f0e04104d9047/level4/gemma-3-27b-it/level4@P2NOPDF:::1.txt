## Review for NIPS 2017 - Submission [Paper ID]

**Overall Impression:**

This paper presents an interesting and well-performing model for visual question answering. The reported results are strong, and the attention maps provide compelling evidence that the gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. While the technical contributions are promising, the presentation could benefit from greater clarity and refinement. With further revisions, I believe this submission has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Model and Results:** The proposed model demonstrates a compelling combination of performance and interpretability. The quantitative and qualitative results presented are encouraging.
*   **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The authors conducted a comprehensive set of experiments, providing both quantitative and qualitative analyses of their approach. 
*   **Novel Attention Mechanism:** The progression from unary and pairwise attentions to the final integrated attention mechanism is a particularly noteworthy aspect of the work. While a visualization of the marginalized trinary attention maps would be beneficial, I did not find evidence of their absence in the submitted materials.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity of Presentation:** The writing and organization of the paper occasionally lack clarity, making it difficult to follow the technical details. A more streamlined and focused presentation would significantly improve the reader's understanding.
*   **Incremental Novelty:** While the proposed architecture outperforms the baseline MCB model [7], the novelty feels somewhat incremental. The extension of the intuition from [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders, while effective, doesn’t represent a radical departure. The authors should more explicitly delineate the specific contributions of their work and how it advances the state-of-the-art. 
*   **Probabilistic Motivation:** The connection to probabilistic modeling, while presented as motivational, lacks formalization. The authors should either strengthen this connection with a more rigorous treatment or revise the language to reflect its primarily motivational role.
*   **Figure 2 – Level of Abstraction:** Figure 2 is not optimally designed. It’s insufficiently detailed to fully convey the network's functionality, yet not abstract enough to offer a clear overview of the approach. Simplifying this figure to focus on the generation of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials would greatly improve its clarity.
*   **Unreferenced Figure:** Figure 3 is included in the submission but is not referenced within the text.

**Questions and Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Potential Combination Coefficients:** The paper would benefit from disclosing the learned values of the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in Equation (1). It would be insightful to know if different modalities demonstrate a preference for different potential orders.
*   **Unary+Pairwise Performance:** The underperformance of the 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise model compared to the MCB baseline [7] is surprising, given their architectural similarities. I encourage the authors to provide further discussion and potential explanations for this result. Is this behavior related to the transition to the MCB/MCT decision computation modules?
*   **Sequential vs. Single MCT Layer:** The discussion regarding the use of sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer for the decision head is interesting. Sharing the observations from experiments comparing these two approaches would be valuable.
