- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation (unary, pairwise, ternary) for image, question, and answer in VQA, with improved results when using ternary potentials.
- Generic attention formulation is valuable and potentially generalizes to other tasks.
- Ablation study without ternary potentials helps quantify the ternary contribution.
- Paper is clearly written.
- Unclear what “attention over answers” means and paper lacks visualizations or clarification for answer attention.
- Table 1 indicates gains mainly come from the ternary potential; without it the model often does not beat existing two-modality models, needing further explanation.
- Lacks comparison to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., ECCV16) despite ternary being central to improvements.
- No discussion or analysis of failure cases and model error modes.
- Minor errors/typos: "mechanism"→"mechanisms"; inconsistency claiming evaluation on validation set while Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the generic framework and three-modality quantitative gains despite incomplete rebuttal on two-modality comparisons.
- Authors’ claim that attention maps correlate better with human maps is promising but lacks clear comparative numbers against SOTA (e.g., MCB).
- Confusion in authors’ “MCB vs. MCT” numbers: reported 68.4% vs 69.4% seem swapped and inconsistent with Table 1 (MCB 68.6%), leaving which number corresponds to which model unclear.
