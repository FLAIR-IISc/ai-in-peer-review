- Proposes a novel attention mechanism for VQA that models high-order correlations across multiple modalities via a probabilistic model with high-order potentials.  
- Claims improved generality over prior work (e.g., MCB, hierarchical attention) by handling any number of modalities rather than just two or three.  
- Evaluated on the VQA dataset and reports state-of-the-art performance.  
- Attention maps are presented and indicate the model focuses on relevant image regions and question components.  
- Provides thorough qualitative and quantitative evaluation, including examples of correct and incorrect answers.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA.  
- Strength: attention visualizations offer interpretability of the model’s decisions.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental analysis.  
- Weakness: writing and presentation need clearer exposition for readability.  
- Weakness: limited novelty since it builds on existing attention and high-order correlation work.  
- Weakness: the claimed probabilistic connection is not rigorously or formally established.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 lacks sufficient detail and Figure 3 is not referenced in the text.  
- Suggestion: include marginalized trinary attention maps to further interpret decision-making.  
- Suggestion: report learned linear coefficients for combining marginalized potentials to show modality weighting.  
- Suggestion: compare sequential MCB vs a single MCT layer in the decision head and report those results.
