- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer, combining them into attention via a single mean-field-like iteration and compact pooling for answer prediction.

- Strong empirical performance: achieves state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset, supporting the value of modeling multi-way modality interactions.

- Thorough experimental analysis: includes quantitative benchmarks and qualitative attention visualizations showing how unary and pairwise potentials lead to more focused final attention maps.

- Intuitive and generalizable framework: modeling attention via increasing-order potentials is principled and appears extendable to more than three modalities.

- Clarity concerns: the probabilistic framing (potentials and “mean-field inference”) is underdeveloped and not formally justified; either formalize the connection or reframe as a learned multi-component attention scorer.

- Figure 2 is ambiguous: too abstract to explain computations yet too detailed for a conceptual overview; a clearer diagram showing the flow from inputs to unary/pairwise/ternary modules and combination is needed.

- Novelty positioning: the paper should better delineate its conceptual advance over prior multimodal fusion work (e.g., multimodal compact bilinear pooling) to clarify its unique contribution.

- Analysis gaps: the 2-modality model underperforms MCB [7] with little explanation; possible causes like lower embedding dimension (512 vs 2048) or other architectural factors should be discussed.

- Decision head design: the finding that a 2-layer MCB outperforms a single MCT unit suggests richer self-interactions matter; this important observation requires deeper analysis.

- Request to report learned combination weights (αi, βi, γi) to show which potential orders most influence each modality’s attention.

- Ask whether the superior 2-layer MCB implies higher-order self-interactions matter more than direct three-way MCT interactions, and request elaboration.

- Suggest visualizing marginalized ternary attention maps to illustrate their unique contribution and ask whether such maps were generated during analysis.
