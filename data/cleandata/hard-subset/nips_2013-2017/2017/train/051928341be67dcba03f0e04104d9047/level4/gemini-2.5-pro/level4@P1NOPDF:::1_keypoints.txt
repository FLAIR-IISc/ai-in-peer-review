- Overall: interesting method with strong results; attention maps suggest gains come from improved attention rather than model capacity, but writing is sometimes unclear.
- Strength: presents a compelling model that achieves impressive task performance.
- Strength: thorough qualitative and quantitative experiments validate the approach.
- Strength: model can shift between unary, pairwise, and trinary attention orders, which appears effective.
- Weakness: clarity and presentation are sometimes convoluted; Figure 2 is neither detailed nor abstract enough and Figure 3 is not referenced.
- Weakness: novelty is limited—main contribution seems to extend prior MCB intuition to higher/lower orders and needs clearer differentiation from prior work.
- Weakness: probabilistic connection is underdeveloped and presented more as motivation than a formal framework; must be formalized or reworded.
- Question: Were marginalized trinary attention maps visualized in the paper or supplementary material?
- Question: What are the learned linear coefficients for combining marginalized potentials in Equation (1), and do modalities favor different orders?
- Question: Why does the 2-modalities Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB [7] despite similar architecture—could switching from MCB to MCT cause this?
- Question: Any empirical comparison or observations for sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer in the decision head?
