- Proposal: Introduces a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary-to-ternary modality interactions and uses mean-field inference; reports state-of-the-art VQA performance.  
- Strength — Strong performance: Achieves SOTA results on the VQA benchmark.  
- Strength — Novelty: Explicit modeling of high-order correlations and easy generalization to arbitrary numbers of modalities.  
- Strength — Thorough evaluation: Presents quantitative results and qualitative attention visualizations with comparisons to prior work.  
- Strength — Clear motivation: Well-motivated critique of existing attention mechanisms and rationale for the proposed approach.  
- Weakness — Writing clarity: Presentation is dense and sometimes confusing; abrupt transition from intuition to equations.  
- Weakness — Probabilistic formalization: The probabilistic connection is motivational rather than formally derived and should be strengthened or moderated.  
- Weakness — Figure 2: Architecture diagram is overly abstract and unclear; needs a simplified, focused version emphasizing unary/pairwise/ternary potential generation.  
- Weakness — Unreferenced Figure 3: Figure 3 is never explicitly cited in the text.  
- Weakness — 2-modality underperformance: The poorer performance versus MCB for the 2-modality model is unexplained and needs deeper analysis.  
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical evidence and good generalization potential.  
- For rejection: Issues with writing clarity, weak formal probabilistic linkage, and insufficient analysis of the 2-modality result.
