General Impression:
The proposed method is compelling, with strong empirical results. The attention maps suggest that the gains stem from improved attention mechanisms rather than simply increased model capacity. However, the writing is at times confusing and could be clarified with revisions.

Strengths:
- Interesting architecture that achieves strong performance.
- Extensive qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
- The progression from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention is particularly effective. I would also like to see marginalized trinary attention maps—if these were included in the paper or supplement and I missed them, please point me to the relevant section.

Weaknesses:
- The writing and exposition feel uneven and occasionally jumbled, which makes the method harder to follow.
- The novelty appears somewhat limited. While your model is more expressive than, and outperforms, the MCB for VQA [7], much of the added complexity applies the intuition of [7] at different orders (unary and trinary). This is not necessarily problematic, but the paper should more clearly articulate the specific contributions and what is genuinely new.
- The probabilistic interpretation is not sufficiently formal. As written, it reads more as motivation than a concrete theoretical link. Either strengthen the formal connection or revise the language to make its status explicit.
- Figure 2 sits at an awkward level of abstraction: it is too detailed to cleanly convey the high-level idea, yet not detailed enough to reconstruct the model. Consider simplifying it to spotlight the generation and roles of unary, pairwise, and trinary potentials.
- Figure 3 is never referenced in the text (unless I missed it). Please ensure it is integrated and discussed.

Questions and requests for clarification:
- What values were learned for the linear coefficients used to combine the marginalized potentials in Eq. (1)? It would be informative to know whether different modalities rely on different potential orders.
- The 2-Modalities Unary+Pairwise variant underperforms MCB [7] despite architectural similarities. Could you provide intuition or analysis for this result? Is it related to replacing the decision computation with MCB/MCT modules?
- The discussion contrasting sequential MCB layers versus a single MCT layer in the decision head was intriguing, but no quantitative results were reported. Could you share what you observed (e.g., performance differences, training stability, parameter efficiency)?
- If marginalized trinary attention maps exist, please include or reference them; they would help validate the contribution of higher-order interactions.
- More generally, could you disentangle where performance gains originate: improved attention, higher-order interactions, the decision module, or training/regularization choices? An ablation isolating each factor would strengthen the paper.

Summary:
This is a promising approach with solid results and insightful attention visualizations. The main issues are clarity of presentation, a clearer positioning of novelty relative to MCB [7], and a more rigorous treatment (or a reframing) of the probabilistic perspective. Improving Figure 2, integrating Figure 3, reporting omitted analyses (coefficients in Eq. (1), sequential MCB vs. single MCT), and adding or highlighting trinary attention maps would significantly strengthen the submission.
