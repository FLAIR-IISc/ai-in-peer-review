1. Proposes a novel high-order attention framework modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across image, question, and answer for multimodal VQA.  
2. Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on VQA benchmarks, with the ternary potential being particularly effective.  
3. Builds on prior pairwise attention work (Lu et al., Fukui et al.) and generalizes attention to arbitrary modalities while retaining efficiency via tensor sketching.  
4. Strength: framework is task-agnostic and principled, extending beyond existing two-modality attention models.  
5. Strength: ablation study isolates the impact of ternary potentials, showing their necessity for SOTA performance.  
6. Strength: paper is well-structured and clearly written with detailed technical implementation.  
7. Weakness: unclear interpretation of "attention over answers" and lack of visualizations for answer attention maps.  
8. Weakness: 2-modality variant does not consistently outperform existing 2-modality baselines (except HieCoAtt), raising questions about the ternary component’s necessity vs. additivity.  
9. Weakness: missing comparisons to models that use answers as input (e.g., Jabri et al.), which could contextualize the need for attention mechanisms.  
10. Weakness: no failure-case analysis despite visible failure modes in qualitative results (e.g., Fig. 8).  
11. Weakness: minor typos/clarifications needed (e.g., "mechanism"→"mechanisms"; clarify validation vs. test results in Table 1).  
12. Post-rebuttal: authors’ rebuttal lacks quantitative evidence on 2-modality concern but the novel multimodal framework and SOTA 3-modality results support acceptance.  
13. Post-rebuttal: correlation analysis with human attention maps is compelling and favors the proposed method over HieCoAtt.  
14. Post-rebuttal: reported MCT vs. MCB numbers are inconsistent with Table 1/test-dev results and should be confirmed (possible swapped or different configurations).  
15. Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance based on novelty and strong empirical results.
