- General impression: interesting method with strong results; attention maps indicate gains come from refined attention, but writing has clarity issues.

- Strength: the transition from unary/pairwise attentions to the final attention mechanism is convincing.
- Missing: trinary (marginalized) attention maps are not shown in main text or supplement—unclear if omitted intentionally.
- Weakness: presentation has multiple clarity issues that distract from the technical contribution.
- Weakness: novelty seems incremental—architecture mainly extends MCB [7] to higher (trinary) and lower (unary) orders.
- Weakness: probabilistic connection is presented as motivation but lacks formal grounding or theoretical support.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is misaligned in abstraction—too little detail to understand functionality yet too complex to summarize.
- Weakness: Figure 3 is not referenced anywhere in the main text.
- Question: what coefficients were learned for combining marginalized potentials in Equation (1), and do different modalities use different potential orders?
- Question: why does the 2-modality Unary+Pairwise model underperform MCB [7] despite architectural similarities—was this due to replacing MCB’s decision module with MCT?
