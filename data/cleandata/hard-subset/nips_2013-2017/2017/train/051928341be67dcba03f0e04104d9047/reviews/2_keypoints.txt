- Proposes a generic multimodal attention formulation for VQA combining unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials over image, question, and answer; ternary potentials yield improved results.  
- Strength: Generic multimodal attention formulation that could generalize beyond a single task.  
- Strength: Ablation study demonstrates the contribution of the ternary potential.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written.  
- Weakness: Unclear what “attention over answers” means given most answers are single tokens and no visualizations of answer attention are provided.  
- Weakness: Main improvement appears driven by ternary potentials; without them the model does not outperform existing 2-modality methods (except HieCoAtt), needing clarification.  
- Weakness: Should compare to prior models that use answers as inputs (e.g., Jabri et al., Revisiting VQA Baselines) since ternary input seems critical.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of failure cases and model failure modes.  
- Weakness: Minor errors/typos and reporting discrepancy (L38 “mechanism”→“mechanisms”; L237 claims validation but Table 1 shows test-dev/test-std).  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer leans to accept due to the generic framework and quantitative 3-modality SOTA gains despite unsatisfactory response on 2-modality comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors report attention maps correlate better with human maps, but comparisons to SOTA (e.g., MCB) are unclear.  
- Post-rebuttal question: Authors’ "MCB vs. MCT" numbers appear inconsistent/swapped (68.4% vs 69.4%) and conflict with the MCB value (68.6%) in Table 1.
