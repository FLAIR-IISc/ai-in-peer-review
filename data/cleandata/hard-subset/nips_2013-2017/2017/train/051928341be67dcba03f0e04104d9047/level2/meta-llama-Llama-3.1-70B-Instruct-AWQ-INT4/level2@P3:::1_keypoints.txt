- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism capturing unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Uses a probabilistic formulation to model high-order attention.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical formulation and experiments.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single VQA dataset, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- No ablation study to measure the contribution of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Method may be computationally expensive for large-scale datasets due to high-order correlation computations.  
- Suggests evaluating on additional VQA datasets to show broader applicability.  
- Recommends conducting ablation studies to isolate component effects.  
- Recommends exploring efficient implementations (approximations, parallelization) to reduce cost.  
- Potentially significant for improving VQA performance and impacting other attention-based NLP/CV tasks.  
- Overall: a significant, well-presented contribution with demonstrated effectiveness on VQA but with scalability and evaluation gaps.
