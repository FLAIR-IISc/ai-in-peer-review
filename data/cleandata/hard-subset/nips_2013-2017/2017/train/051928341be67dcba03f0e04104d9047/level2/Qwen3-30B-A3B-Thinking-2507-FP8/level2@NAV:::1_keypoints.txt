- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism modeling k-th order correlations across multiple modalities (visual, textual, answer) for VQA.  
- Claims the high-order approach generalizes beyond pairwise attention and achieves state-of-the-art performance.  
- Provides strong experimental support with thorough comparisons to baselines (Lu et al., Fukui et al.) on VQA test-dev and test-std.  
- Ablation studies show the 3-modality model significantly outperforms 2-modality variants.  
- Attention visualizations demonstrate meaningful correlations between image regions, question words, and answer candidates.  
- Unary, pairwise, and ternary potential modules are well-explained and implementation using tensor sketch (MCT/MCB) is sound.  
- Presents a clear probabilistic formulation of high-order attention with mean-field inference.  
- Well-motivated by limitations of existing attention mechanisms that treat modalities as a single combined input.  
- Strengths: comprehensive evaluation, clear theory, insightful visualizations, and generalizability beyond VQA.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion and explicit quantification of computational complexity and scalability to higher-order correlations.  
- Weakness: novelty claim could be stronger with a direct comparison to structured attention networks (Kim et al., 2017).  
- Overall: technically sound, well-executed, and a strong candidate for acceptance.
