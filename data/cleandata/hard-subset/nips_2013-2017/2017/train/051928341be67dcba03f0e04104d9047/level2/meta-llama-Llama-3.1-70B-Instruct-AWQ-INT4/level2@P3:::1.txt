This paper proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for visual question answering (VQA) tasks, which learns to direct attention to relevant elements in different data modalities (e.g., images, questions, and answers) to solve the joint task. The proposed attention mechanism is based on a probabilistic model that captures high-order correlations between data modalities using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the VQA dataset, achieving state-of-the-art performance.

Strengths:

1. **Novel attention mechanism**: The proposed high-order attention mechanism is a significant contribution to the field of VQA. It provides a new way of modeling attention that can capture complex correlations between different data modalities.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, outperforming existing methods.
3. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the mathematical formulation and experimental results.

Weaknesses:

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach on the VQA dataset, which may not be representative of other VQA tasks or datasets.
2. **Lack of ablation study**: The authors do not provide an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of each component of their approach, such as the unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.
3. **Computational complexity**: The proposed approach may be computationally expensive, especially for large-scale datasets, due to the need to compute high-order correlations between data modalities.

Suggestions for improvement:

1. **Evaluate on other datasets**: The authors should evaluate their approach on other VQA datasets to demonstrate its generalizability.
2. **Ablation study**: The authors should conduct an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of each component of their approach.
3. **Efficient implementation**: The authors should explore ways to efficiently implement their approach, such as using approximations or parallel processing, to reduce computational complexity.

Originality:

1. **High-order attention mechanism**: The proposed attention mechanism is a novel contribution to the field of VQA, and it provides a new way of modeling attention that can capture complex correlations between different data modalities.
2. **Probabilistic formulation**: The authors provide a probabilistic formulation of their approach, which is a significant contribution to the field of VQA.

Significance:

1. **Improving VQA performance**: The proposed approach has the potential to improve VQA performance by providing a more effective way of modeling attention.
2. **Broader impact**: The proposed approach may have a broader impact on other areas of research, such as natural language processing and computer vision, where attention mechanisms are widely used.

Overall, this paper is a significant contribution to the field of VQA, and it provides a new way of modeling attention that can capture complex correlations between different data modalities. While there are some limitations to the paper, the authors provide a well-written and well-organized presentation of their approach, and they demonstrate its effectiveness on the VQA dataset.
