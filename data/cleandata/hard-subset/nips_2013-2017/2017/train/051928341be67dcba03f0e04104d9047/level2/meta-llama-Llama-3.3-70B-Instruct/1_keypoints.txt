- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism using unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to learn cross-modal correlations.  
- Main claim: the high-order attention captures complex dependencies and yields state-of-the-art performance on VQA.  
- Method is supported by theoretical analysis and a clear, detailed explanation of the model.  
- Experimental results demonstrate SOTA performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and contains a thorough related-work review.  
- Uses a probabilistic framework that enables modeling of complex dependencies between modalities.  
- Provides detailed analysis of attention weights, improving interpretability of model decisions.  
- Limitation: relies on pre-trained CNNs for image embeddings, which may limit applicability to other domains.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity (parameter counts and compute requirements).  
- Limitation: needs additional experiments to demonstrate robustness across different datasets and tasks.  
- Overall: significant contribution to multimodal learning with strong pros (novelty, SOTA, clarity) but some practical evaluation gaps (compute, generality, robustness).
