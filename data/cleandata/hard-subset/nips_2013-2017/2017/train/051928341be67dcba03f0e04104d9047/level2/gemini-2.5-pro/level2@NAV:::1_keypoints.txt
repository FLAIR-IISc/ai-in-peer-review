- Introduces a novel attention mechanism modeling high-order (third-order) correlations among image, question, and answer for VQA.  
- Formalizes attention as a probabilistic model with unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and uses one-step mean-field inference to obtain attention distributions.  
- Extends prior co-attention work (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.) by providing a principled framework to incorporate an additional modality (answers).  
- Original and significant idea: potentials-based high-order attention is elegant, extensible, and moves beyond ad-hoc architectures.  
- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art on VQA and includes useful qualitative visualizations and a failure case.  
- Clarity is good overall, but the connection to mean-field inference is underexplained; need justification for using a single iteration.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study isolating the contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- The observation that a 2-layer MCB outperforms the conceptually cleaner MCT unit requires deeper analysis beyond “greater expressiveness.”  
- Overall assessment: a strong, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution likely to spur further multimodal research.
