- Proposes a novel, generally applicable high-order attention mechanism that learns multi-modal high-order correlations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and thorough explanation of the approach.  
- Introduction effectively motivates attention mechanisms and limitations of prior work.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and informative.  
- Model is well-motivated with detailed descriptions of data embedding, attention, and decision components.  
- Key technical contribution: use of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials to capture inter-modal correlations.  
- Clear explanation of mean-field inference used to obtain marginal probabilities from potentials.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, compares to baselines (e.g., Lu et al., Fukui et al.), and includes strong qualitative attention visualizations.  
- Missing a detailed analysis of computational complexity and component-wise cost despite reporting 40M parameters.  
- Does not provide a detailed comparison to some recent VQA attention work (e.g., Nam et al.).  
- Overall judged a strong contribution to VQA and attention mechanisms.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to include computational complexity analysis and additional comparisons.  
- Rating 8/10; reviewer confidence 8/10.
