- Introduces a high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models correlations among image, question, and answer using higher-order potentials, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of existing attention mechanisms (ad-hoc, task-specific feature extraction) and proposes a more generalizable correlation-learning approach.  
- Related work is reasonably comprehensive and comparisons to methods like Lu et al. and Fukui et al. are appropriate.  
- Key technical novelty: use of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials with mean-field inference to flexibly model inter-modal relationships and extend to more modalities.  
- Presentation strength: explanation of attention mechanism and potentials is fairly clear, and attention visualizations provide qualitative insight.  
- Empirical strength: significant performance improvement on VQA and an ablation comparing MCT vs. 2-layer MCB helps isolate component contributions.  
- Mathematical clarity is weak: Equation (1) is complex and roles of αi, βi, γi are not sufficiently explained.  
- Description of tensor sketching units (MCB, MCT) is abrupt and lacks implementation detail and justification.  
- Training details are sparse (e.g., learning rate schedule, optimizer not specified), despite mentioning dropout.  
- Failure case analysis shows the model can attend to irrelevant features even when questions specify otherwise, indicating robustness issues.  
- Ablation study is limited and should include separate analyses of unary, pairwise, and ternary contributions.  
- Baseline comparisons are narrow; inclusion of more recent and competitive VQA models is needed.  
- Parameter claim (40M vs >70M) lacks analysis of benefits or efficiency gains from fewer parameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel higher-order modeling, SOTA results, clear problem framing, and informative qualitative results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear mathematics, limited ablations and baselines, observed failure modes, and insufficient methodological detail.  
- Essential revisions: clarify mathematical notation (especially Eq. 1 and parameter roles), expand ablation to analyze each potential order, and broaden comparisons to recent VQA models.  
- Nice-to-have revisions: provide full training procedure (LR schedule, optimizer), detail MCB/MCT implementations, analyze efficiency from fewer parameters, and explore fixes for failure cases.
