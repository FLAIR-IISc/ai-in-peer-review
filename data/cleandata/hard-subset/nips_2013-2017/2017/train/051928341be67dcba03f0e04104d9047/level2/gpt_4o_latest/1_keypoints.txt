- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for multimodal tasks (VQA) that captures higher-order correlations between visual, textual, and answer data.
- Key contributions: a probabilistic attention model based on potentials, capability to model interactions across multiple modalities, and use of MCB/MCT pooling for efficient decision-making.
- Strength — Novelty: Introduces high-order attention and a fresh probabilistic framework beyond pairwise attention.
- Strength — Performance: Achieves state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset with thorough quantitative comparisons.
- Strength — Scalability: Designed to handle an arbitrary number of modalities, improving over approaches limited to two.
- Strength — Qualitative analysis: Provides visualizations showing the attention mechanism focuses on relevant image regions and text.
- Strength — Efficiency: Competitive performance with fewer parameters than some baselines, indicating computational efficiency.
- Weakness — Clarity: Paper organization and explanations (especially potentials and decision-making) are dense and could be clearer.
- Weakness — Reproducibility: Missing implementation details (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, training stability) that hinder replication.
- Weakness — Limitations: Insufficient discussion and analysis of failure cases and method limitations.
- Weakness — Generalization: Evaluation limited to VQA; applicability to other multimodal tasks is not demonstrated.
- Suggestion — Improve clarity: Expand intuitive explanations and simplify mathematical formulations for broader accessibility.
- Suggestion — Analyze failures: Provide deeper discussion of failure cases and limitations for balanced evaluation.
- Suggestion — Broaden evaluation: Test the model on additional multimodal tasks (e.g., image captioning, visual dialog) to show generality.
- Suggestion — Enhance reproducibility: Include more implementation details or open-source the code.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address clarity and reproducibility concerns.
