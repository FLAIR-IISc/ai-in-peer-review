**Review**

The paper "High-Order Attention Models for Visual Question Answering" proposes a novel attention mechanism that learns high-order correlations between various data modalities. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the task of visual question answering (VQA), achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed high-order attention mechanism is effective in capturing complex correlations between multiple data modalities.
2. The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide experimental results on the VQA dataset to support their claims. They compare their approach to several baselines, including the approach of Lu et al. [15] and the technique of Fukui et al. [7]. The results show that their approach outperforms the baselines in terms of accuracy.

**Usefulness**

The proposed approach is practically useful for VQA tasks, as it can effectively capture complex correlations between multiple data modalities. The authors also provide qualitative results, showing that their approach can attend to reasonable pixel and question locations.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of VQA and attention mechanisms. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including attention mechanisms for image and textual data.

**Novelty**

The work presents a significant improvement over existing approaches, as it proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that can capture complex correlations between multiple data modalities.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including the architecture of the proposed model, the experimental setup, and the results.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the fact that it may not be effective for all types of questions.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro:**

* The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.
* The approach is effective in capturing complex correlations between multiple data modalities.
* The paper provides a thorough review of related work and sufficient details for reproducibility.

**Con:**

* The approach may not be effective for all types of questions.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper, as it presents a novel and effective approach for VQA tasks. However, I suggest that the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach and discuss potential limitations and future work.
