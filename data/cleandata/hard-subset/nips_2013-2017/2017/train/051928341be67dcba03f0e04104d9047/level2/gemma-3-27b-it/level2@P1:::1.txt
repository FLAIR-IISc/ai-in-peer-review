This paper proposes a high-order attention mechanism for visual question answering (VQA). The core idea is to model interactions between different data modalities (image, question, answer) using higher-order correlations, going beyond pairwise interactions commonly used in existing attention mechanisms. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a clear limitation of existing attention mechanisms – their inability to effectively model high-order correlations. The introduction clearly articulates the problem and the proposed solution. The related work section is thorough, covering a wide range of attention mechanisms for both image and textual data, and accurately positions the proposed approach in relation to existing methods, highlighting the novelty of modeling higher-order correlations. The technical description of the high-order attention model is reasonably clear, although the notation can be a bit dense. The use of potentials and mean-field inference is a sound approach for dealing with the complexity of high-order interactions. The experimental evaluation on VQA is convincing, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and providing both quantitative and qualitative results. The ablation studies (comparing MCT and 2-layer MCB) are also valuable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed high-order attention mechanism is a novel contribution, extending existing attention mechanisms to model richer interactions between data modalities.
*   **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on the VQA dataset demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The use of potentials and mean-field inference provides a principled framework for modeling high-order correlations.
*   **Thorough Related Work:** The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing attention mechanisms.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the key concepts.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** The mathematical formulation of the high-order attention model can be complex and difficult to follow for readers unfamiliar with probabilistic graphical models. While the explanation is decent, further simplification or illustration could improve clarity.
*   **Computational Cost:** While not explicitly discussed in detail, modeling high-order correlations likely increases the computational cost compared to lower-order attention mechanisms. A more detailed analysis of the computational complexity would be helpful.
*   **Limited Qualitative Analysis:** While the qualitative results are helpful, they are somewhat limited. More in-depth analysis of the attention maps and their correlation with ground truth annotations could provide further insights into the behavior of the model. The failure case presented is a good starting point, but more such analyses would be valuable.
*   **Dependency on Dataset:** The strong performance is demonstrated on VQA, but it's not entirely clear how well the approach would generalize to other multimodal tasks. More experiments on different datasets would strengthen the claims of generality.
*   **Parameter Tuning:** The paper mentions hyperparameter settings (learning rate, batch size, dropout rate) but doesn’t delve into the sensitivity of the performance to these parameters, or the process of how those parameters were chosen.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to modeling attention in multimodal data.
*   The experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark dataset.
*   The paper is well-written and provides a comprehensive review of related work.
*   The approach has the potential to be applicable to a wide range of multimodal tasks.

**Arguments for rejection:**

*   The mathematical complexity of the model may limit its accessibility and adoption.
*   The computational cost of modeling high-order correlations is not fully addressed.
*   The generalization ability of the approach to other multimodal tasks is not thoroughly evaluated.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of multimodal learning. The proposed high-order attention mechanism is a novel and effective approach to modeling interactions between different data modalities. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2017.

**Minor suggestions:**

*   Consider adding a visual illustration of the high-order attention mechanism to aid understanding.
*   Discuss the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
*   Provide more detailed analysis of the attention maps and their correlation with ground truth annotations.
*   Explore the generalization ability of the approach to other multimodal tasks.
*   Include a discussion of parameter sensitivity and the parameter tuning process.
