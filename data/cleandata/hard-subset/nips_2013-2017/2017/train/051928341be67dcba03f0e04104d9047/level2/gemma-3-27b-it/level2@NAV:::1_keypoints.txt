- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism that models beyond pairwise interactions, applied to Visual Question Answering (VQA).  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the VQA dataset, supporting the approach empirically.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear motivation and comprehensive related work positioning the method within prior attention models.  
- Technical presentation details unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials and mean-field inference with reasonable clarity.  
- Uses tensor sketching to mitigate the computational complexity of high-order correlations.  
- Provides qualitative attention-map visualizations showing the model focuses on relevant image regions.  
- Theoretical grounding frames attention as a probabilistic model with high-order potentials, a key strength.  
- Performance gains over existing SOTA are marginal, raising questions about practical impact.  
- Contribution of ternary (higher-order) potentials is not convincingly isolated or thoroughly investigated.  
- Ablation studies are limited; missing experiments such as pairwise-only tests to better quantify component contributions.  
- Lacks discussion on potential VQA dataset biases and how they might affect results.  
- Figures are somewhat hard to interpret and should be clarified.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, encouraging more extensive ablations and discussion of dataset biases.
