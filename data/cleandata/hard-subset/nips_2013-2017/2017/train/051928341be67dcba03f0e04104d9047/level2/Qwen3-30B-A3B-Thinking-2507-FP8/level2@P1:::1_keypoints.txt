- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism for VQA that models unary, pairwise, and ternary correlations across image, question, and answer modalities.  
- Formalizes attention as a probabilistic model and uses mean-field inference to compute marginal attention probabilities.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on standard VQA benchmarks while using fewer parameters (40M vs. 70+M).  
- Clear technical contribution that extends existing attention mechanisms to higher-order correlations.  
- Well-structured mathematical formulation with precise, understandable derivations.  
- Strong empirical results with quantitative metrics on test-dev and test-std supporting claims.  
- Qualitative visualizations (Figures 5–7) effectively illustrate multimodal attention behavior.  
- Presentation is generally clear and professional; Fig. 3 helps clarify attention mechanisms.  
- Description of ternary potential computation (Section 3.2.3) could be more intuitive for readers unfamiliar with higher-order tensors.  
- Originality is strong: first systematic modeling and evaluation of higher-order (up to ternary) multimodal attention.  
- Could better distinguish the probabilistic approach from related "structured" and "dual" attention methods.  
- Needs a more detailed ablation study quantifying contributions of unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials.  
- Would benefit from deeper analysis explaining when and why high-order correlations are necessary for VQA.  
- Should expand comparisons to bilinear models and tensor-sketch approaches for completeness.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, significant improvement, and broadly applicable to multimodal tasks.
