- Proposes a high-order attention mechanism modeling interactions between image, question, and answer; achieves state-of-the-art on VQA.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limitation of pairwise attention and clearly articulates problem and solution.  
- Thorough related work that positions the novelty of modeling higher-order correlations.  
- Technical description is reasonably clear but uses dense notation that can be hard to follow.  
- Uses potentials and mean-field inference, giving a principled framework for high-order interactions.  
- Experimental evaluation is convincing with quantitative and qualitative results supporting SOTA claims.  
- Ablation studies (MCT vs 2-layer MCB) provide useful insight into model components.  
- Mathematical complexity may limit accessibility for readers unfamiliar with probabilistic graphical models.  
- Likely increased computational cost from modeling high-order correlations; detailed complexity analysis is missing.  
- Qualitative analysis is limited; more in-depth attention-map analysis and additional failure cases would help.  
- Generalization beyond the VQA dataset is unclear; more experiments on other multimodal tasks are needed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity and parameter-tuning methodology are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, well-written, potentially broadly applicable.  
- Rejection arguments: model complexity and accessibility concerns, computational cost not addressed, limited evidence of generalization.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept (NIPS 2017).  
- Minor suggestions: add visual illustration of the mechanism, discuss computational complexity, expand attention-map analysis, test on other multimodal tasks, and report parameter sensitivity.
