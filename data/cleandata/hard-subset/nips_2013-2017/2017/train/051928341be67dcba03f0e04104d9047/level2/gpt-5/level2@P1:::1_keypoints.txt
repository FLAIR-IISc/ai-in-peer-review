- Proposes a multimodal attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary interactions across image (V), question (Q), and answers (A) via learnable potentials and a mean-field-style update.  
- Uses bilinear pairwise and trilinear ternary correlations, marginalizes the ternary tensor for per-modality signals, and fuses attended features with compact bilinear/trilinear pooling (MCB/MCT).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on VQA multiple-choice (test-dev/test-std) with fewer parameters than prior methods and provides qualitative attention visualizations.  
- Construction of unary/pairwise/ternary potentials and bilinear/trilinear correlations is reasonable and aligns with prior multimodal attention literature.  
- The “mean-field” probabilistic interpretation is suggestive but not formally derived; an explicit energy/graphical model and derivation are missing.  
- Ternary tensor could be expensive; feasibility depends on using per-example answer candidates (na=18) and needs explicit complexity/memory analysis.  
- Empirically strong on MC accuracy and MCT vs. stacked MCB analysis, but ablations of attention components (unary/pairwise/ternary, weights α/β/γ, answer modality) are limited.  
- Evaluation is restricted to VQA v1 multiple-choice; open-ended VQA and VQA v2 results are not reported.  
- Implementation details, hyperparameters, and code link are provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Paper is generally readable and well-structured, though a few notational typos and ambiguities exist (e.g., A as global vs. per-instance answers).  
- Moderate originality: main novelty is treating attention as high-order potentials with per-modality marginalization and exploiting the answer modality for ternary attention.  
- Significant empirically for parameter-efficient SOTA on VQA MC and for providing interpretable multimodal attentions, but generality is limited by reliance on MC answer lists.  
- Pros: simple modular potential formulation, efficient fusion with MCB/MCT, clear visualizations, code available, extensible to more modalities.  
- Cons: weak probabilistic grounding, limited ablations on attention and ternary role, narrow evaluation scope, unclear computational costs for ternary potentials, minor clarity issues.  
- Suggestions: provide explicit probabilistic model and MF derivation; add ablations (remove ternary/pairwise/unary, vary α/β/γ, compare with/without answer inputs); clarify complexity and na=18 assumption; broaden evaluation (VQA v2, Visual7W, type-wise breakdowns); fix typos and unify answer embedding description.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, timely contribution with strong MC results and a clean framework; lean toward acceptance pending clearer modeling and expanded experiments.
