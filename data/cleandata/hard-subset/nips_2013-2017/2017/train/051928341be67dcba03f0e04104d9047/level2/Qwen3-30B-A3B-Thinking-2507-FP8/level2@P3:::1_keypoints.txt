- Proposes a novel high-order attention mechanism modeling unary, pairwise, and ternary potentials across visual, textual, and answer modalities within a probabilistic mean-field framework.  
- Framework trivially generalizes to any number of modalities beyond pairwise interactions.  
- Extends and differentiates from prior VQA attention methods (e.g., Lu et al., Xu et al.) that focus on pairwise modalities.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound with clear mathematical formulations for unary/pairwise/ternary potentials and principled mean-field inference.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art VQA performance (64.57%) with a relatively simple architecture (40M parameters vs 70M+ in prior work).  
- Experimental setup is thorough, including comprehensive baselines and quantitative results on test-dev and test-std.  
- Qualitative visualizations (Figures 5–7) convincingly show modality correlations and focused attention on relevant image regions.  
- Ablation studies (2-modality vs 3-modality) demonstrate the empirical benefit of high-order attention.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized with consistent notation; embeddings and decision sections are sufficiently detailed for replication.  
- Minor reproducibility concern: exposition and implementation details of the tensor sketch algorithm could be improved.  
- Original and novel formalization of attention as high-order correlations, representing a meaningful conceptual advancement.  
- Significant practical value and broader applicability beyond VQA, with potential for adoption in other multimodal tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis of performance and scaling when applied to more than three modalities.  
- Notably simple compared to complex architectures, suggesting easier adoption by other researchers.  
- Related work review is comprehensive and well-positioned; overall the paper is a strong, meaningful contribution.
