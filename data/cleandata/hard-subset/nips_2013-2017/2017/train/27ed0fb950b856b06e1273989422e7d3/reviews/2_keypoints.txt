- Proposes a novel method using RNNs to represent implicit distributions over count vectors via gamma distributions to capture complex long-time dependence.
- Uses neural networks to approximate the gamma shape parameters, combining information from prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer).
- Updates neural network parameters based on expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.
- Provides an interesting and smart solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models.
- Manuscript appears a bit rushed and needs polishing.
- Contains critical typos (e.g., equation 10) that must be fixed.
- Assumes fixed gamma scale parameters and updates only shape parameters—requires detailed discussion and analysis of this choice.
- Experiments are limited: only two-layer model shown; missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper models, and lacking analysis of other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).
- Recommends adding detailed graphical illustrations of the full model (e.g., similar to Fig.1 in Chung et al. 2015) for clarity.
- Suggests discussing recent related deep Poisson factor analysis work (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) in the Introduction.
