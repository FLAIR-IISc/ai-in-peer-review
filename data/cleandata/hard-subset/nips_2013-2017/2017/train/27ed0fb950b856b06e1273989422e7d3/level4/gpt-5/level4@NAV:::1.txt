Review- The paper presents a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model that extends PF to capture temporal dependencies. In contrast to prior dynamic PF work, it adopts a simplified recurrent neural network to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational methods, with an additional maximization step over the neural network parameters. The experiments cover five real-world datasets.
Overall, I find the idea potentially interesting, but the execution still needs improvement before it is suitable for a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.
+ Equation 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN: h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on the same layer’s previous time step, as an RNN usually would. What is the rationale for this simplified design?
+ In Section 2, some model definitions are missing (e.g., how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized, and what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3).
+ I disagree with the discussion on lines 110–118 about “implicit distributions.” Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit Gammas with specified shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) is available in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it is not implicit. The same applies to h in Eq. 10.
+ Lines 30–31: The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear; please elaborate.
+ The subsection on lines 133–155 is not clear. What exactly is the “loss function” in Eq. 15?
+ MSE/PMSE is generally not an appropriate metric for PF-like models. Please also report predictive log-likelihood at minimum.
+ The value of K used in the experiments (line 207) appears too small. I recommend trying larger values, such as K = 100 (at least).
+ I could not connect the discussion in lines 224–232 with Fig. 3; the linkage is unclear.
+ The variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) can be moved to the supplement.
+ The caption for Fig. 4 is not legible.
+ I strongly recommend following general guidance on minimizing passive voice, especially in the abstract.
+ The writing quality is weak, with many typos. Examples include:
 - Line 15, “high-dimensional”
 - Line 22, “factorize”
 - Line 24, “property of the”
 - Lines 29, 34, “Dirichlet”
 - Line 34, “nested”
 - Fig. 1, “visual representation”
 - Fig. 1, “transmission” (also consider whether this term is appropriate)
 - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 - Many missing articles (e.g., “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)
