- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using RNNs to capture long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Novel integration of deep neural transitions into Poisson factorization, moving beyond Markovian assumptions.  
- Addresses a relevant problem with applications in recommender systems, text analysis, and event modeling.  
- Major technical clarity issues: generative process not fully specified (e.g., p(θt|·) undefined).  
- Unclear how Gamma distribution parameters are produced by the neural network.  
- Questionable RNN formulation: dependencies in Eq.4 do not match standard recurrent structure and lack motivation.  
- Misuse of terminology: conditional posteriors presented as explicit Gamma distributions, not “implicit distributions.”  
- Confusing inference and “Probability AutoEncoder” section: objectives and loss derivations are ill-defined and unjustified.  
- Experimental metrics inappropriate: MSE and PMSE are not suitable for probabilistic count models; predictive log-likelihood or perplexity recommended.  
- Limited experimental exploration: too few latent factors tested (e.g., K=10); should evaluate larger K (e.g., 50–100).  
- Results discussion and qualitative connections (e.g., lines 224–232 vs. Fig.3) are unconvincing and unclear.  
- Poor writing and presentation: many grammatical errors, typos, and unclear sentences impede comprehension.  
- Organization issues: standard variational updates could be moved to appendix to improve readability.  
- Specific presentation problems: illegible figure captions, vague dataset descriptions (e.g., “fanatical data and disaster data”), and weak abstract voice.  
- Overall: promising idea but major technical, experimental, and presentation issues preclude acceptance in current form.
