- Main contribution: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) combining Poisson factor analysis with RNN-like recurrent structure and deep hierarchy to model long-range temporal dependence in sequential count data.
- Inference: Uses variational inference and gradient-based optimization to learn model and neural network parameters.
- Strength: Novel combination of PFA, recurrent networks, and deep models tailored for sequential count data.
- Strength: Strong experimental results on synthetic and real datasets with interpretable latent factors and helpful visualizations.
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-defined model formulation building on deep Bayesian literature.
- Key factual correction: The paper’s statement about modeling nonnegative numbers is incorrect — the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is used for nonnegative numbers.
- Presentation fix: Best results in Table 2 should be made bold for clarity.
- Evaluation suggestion: Include held-out perplexity as a stronger evaluation metric alongside MSE/PMSE.
- Comparative evaluation issue: Comparison with LSTM is useful but inconclusive because learning algorithms differ; a comparison with a single-layer dynamic PF would better justify multi-level hierarchy.
- Theoretical/algorithmic note: Discussion of "pruning" and limitations of mean-field variational inference is insightful and suggests future work.
- Major weakness: Numerous grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper hinder readability and must be corrected.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix factual inaccuracies, improve experiments (perplexity and single-layer baseline), and correct language issues.
