

The paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a novel model extending Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) to capture complex long-term temporal dependencies in sequential count data. DDPFA leverages recurrent neural networks to parameterize the shape of gamma-distributed latent variables, enabling deep hierarchical representations of time-dependent structures—addressing a key limitation in prior work like PGDS (which assumes independence between θ<sub>t-1</sub> and θ<sub>t+1</sub> given θ<sub>t</sub>) and shallow models such as LSTM. The approach combines variational inference for latent variables with neural network-based optimization of distribution parameters, validated on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, EBOLA, etc.). The work builds upon recent advances in deep Bayesian models (e.g., Deep Exponential Families [8], Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9–10]) but distinguishes itself through its explicit deep latent structure for long-range time dependence.

**Strengths:**  
1. **Originality (High):** The integration of RNNs to model implicit gamma distributions for time dependencies represents a meaningful extension beyond existing dynamic Poisson models (e.g., PGDS [2], Recurrent Poisson Factorization [25]). The deep structure specifically targets long-term patterns missed by shallow methods, as evidenced in ICEWS factor analysis (Fig. 3), where DDPFA captures sustained topic activations (e.g., Israel-Palestinian conflict) while PGDS fails.  
2. **Significance (Moderate-High):** The model demonstrates strong predictive and fitting performance across diverse datasets (Table 2), particularly for high-dimensional sparse data (e.g., NIPS corpus, EBOLA). The interpretable latent factors (Fig. 2) offer practical value for real-world applications like event analysis and epidemiology.  
3. **Quality (Moderate):** The variational inference framework is technically sound, and experimental results (synthetic and real-world) robustly support the core claim. The ablation study (Table 3) effectively identifies limitations like "pruning" and diminishing returns from deeper layers.  

**Weaknesses:**  
1. **Clarity (Moderate):** Critical typos and unclear derivations reduce reproducibility. Specifically, Equation (10) contains an error: it should be *h<sup>(n)</sup><sub>tk</sub> | Θ, h<sup>(n+1)</sup>, β, X* (not *h<sup>(n+1)</sup><sub>t</sub>*), and the fixed scale parameter β=1 for all gamma distributions (Section 3.2) lacks justification. Why not jointly optimize shape and rate parameters?  
2. **Quality (Moderate):** Ablation studies are incomplete. The paper compares DDPFA (2 layers) to single-layer PFA and PGDS but omits comparisons with:  
   - 1-layer DDPFA (to isolate the deep structure's contribution)  
   - Varying window sizes (c=2,4,6) or factor counts (K=5,10)  
   This limits understanding of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., Table 3 shows layer expansion yields marginal gains).  
3. **Originality (Moderate):** The introduction inadequately contextualizes recent deep Poisson factorization work (e.g., *Recurrent Poisson Factorization* [25], *Scalable Recommendation with Hierarchical Poisson Factorization* [26]). These should be contrasted with DDPFA’s neural network-based approach.  
4. **Significance (Moderate):** PGDS outperforms DDPFA on ICEWS prediction (Table 2), undermining the claimed advantage for certain datasets. The model’s "weakness" in ICEWS (due to PGDS’ smoothed transition) should be discussed as a trade-off.  

**Pro/Con Summary:**  
*Pro:* Novel deep latent structure for long-term time dependence; strong empirical results on sparse sequential count data; interpretable factors; efficient variational inference.  
*Con:* Missing ablation studies (layers/hyperparameters); critical typo in Equation (10); insufficient discussion of fixed scale parameter; inadequate comparison to recent DPF models [25,26].  

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The core innovation is significant and well-validated, but the paper requires clarification of technical details and expanded experimental analysis to fully demonstrate the necessity of the deep structure. The weaknesses are resolvable with targeted revisions.
