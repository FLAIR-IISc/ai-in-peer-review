### Review

This paper introduces a novel approach for modeling long-range temporal dependencies within a deep Poisson factorization (PF) framework. The core contribution is the integration of a recurrent neural network (RNN)-like structure to govern the temporal dynamics of the latent factors. This method stands in contrast to previous dynamic PF models that typically use simpler state-space assumptions. The model parameters are learned via variational inference, incorporating optimization techniques tailored for the neural network components.

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound and well-motivated model. However, its impact is diminished by some weaknesses in the experimental evaluation, a minor factual error, and a number of grammatical issues that affect readability.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novelty and Significance:** The paper effectively addresses a known limitation in existing dynamic Poisson factorization models, namely their difficulty in capturing complex, long-range temporal dependencies. The proposed RNN-based mechanism is an elegant and powerful solution to this problem.

2.  **Technical Soundness:** The development of the generative model and the corresponding variational inference procedure is solid. The integration of a deep architecture with a recurrent temporal structure is non-trivial, and the authors have presented a coherent inference scheme.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement**

1.  **Insufficient Experimental Justification for Deep Architecture:** The experimental evaluation, while demonstrating the model's effectiveness against some baselines, lacks a crucial comparison to fully justify the use of a multi-level hierarchy. To clearly establish the advantage of the proposed deep structure, the authors should compare their model against a single-level (i.e., not deep) version of the same dynamic PF model. The current comparison with an LSTM is valuable, but it is not an "apples-to-apples" comparison for validating the hierarchical aspect of the model, particularly given the differences in the underlying probabilistic assumptions and learning algorithms.

2.  **Choice of Evaluation Metrics:** The use of MSE/PMSE in Section 4.2 is acceptable, but the evaluation could be strengthened. For probabilistic models of count data, held-out perplexity is a standard and often more informative metric for assessing predictive quality. Including perplexity would provide a more robust evaluation and facilitate a more direct comparison with a broader range of literature on temporal point process and count sequence models.

3.  **Factual Inaccuracy:** There is a minor factual error on lines 86-87. The text states that the Poisson distribution is used for modeling non-negative real numbers. The Poisson distribution is defined for non-negative *integers* (counts). It is the underlying Gamma distribution in the Poisson-Gamma construction that models the non-negative real-valued rates. This should be corrected for precision.

**Minor Comments and Typos**

1.  **Table Readability:** In Table 2, please highlight the best-performing results in each column (e.g., by making them bold) to improve readability and make comparisons easier for the reader.

2.  **Grammar and Typos:** The paper would benefit significantly from a thorough proofreading to correct several grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrases that currently hinder readability. Below is a non-exhaustive list of examples:
    *   "unit the data"
    *   "Noticing the lack of capturing..." (Consider rephrasing, e.g., "Observing the inability to capture...")
    *   "PFA factorized a count matrix" (The tense is inconsistent with the present tense used in the surrounding sentences.)
    *   "other data with different pattern long-time dependence" (Should be "other data with different long-term dependency patterns")
    *   "Examples for this including" (Should be "Examples of this include")
    *   "shows excellent results on predict and" (Should be "on prediction and")
    *   "However, it is rough for them" (This phrase is unclear and should be rewritten.)
    *   "V-dimesnion sequentially" (Typo: "V-dimension")
    *   "K-dimension latent variable" (Should be "K-dimensional latent variable")
    *   "due to the including of feature-wise" (Should be "due to the inclusion of")
    *   "recommend systems" (Should be "recommender systems")

In summary, the paper presents a promising and technically interesting model. The core idea is strong, but the authors should strengthen their experimental section to better justify the architectural choices and thoroughly revise the manuscript to improve clarity and correctness.
