Review- The paper proposes integrating long-range temporal dependence into a deep Poisson factorization framework. Unlike prior dynamic PF works, it adopts an RNN-like architecture to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is conducted via variational methods, with optimization routines used to learn the neural network parameters. The paper contains strong technical content but is hampered by several grammatical and factual issues. These are listed below:
1. The statement regarding modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86–87 is incorrect. It is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that is used to model the nonnegative numbers.
2. Please bold the best results in Table 2.
3. In Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would have been a stronger indicator, though MSE/PMSE are acceptable.
4. The limitations in prior works for modeling long-range dependency (references 1, 2) are adequately addressed. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed multi-level hierarchy, a comparison with dynamic PF using a single-level deep architecture would have been helpful. The comparison with LSTM is useful, but the learning algorithms differ; therefore, it does not convincingly justify the necessity of multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed method.
5. There are numerous grammatical errors and typos that make the paper somewhat difficult to read, including:

-- "other data with different pattern long-time dependence .."
