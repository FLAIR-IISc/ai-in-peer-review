This paper proposes a novel method to model complex long-term dependencies in sequences of count vectors. The approach uses recurrent neural networks to parameterize gamma distributions that implicitly capture temporal structure. The time-varying shape parameters of these gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks that integrate information from both the prior (top-down, from higher layers) and the likelihood (bottom-up, from lower layers). The network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, which reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency. Overall, the method offers an interesting and elegant way to address long-range dependency in dynamic count models. I like the idea, but the manuscript feels rushed. I have the following comments:

1. There are critical typos, e.g., in Equation (10). Please proofread carefully and ensure all symbols and indices are consistently defined.

2. The model treats the gamma scale parameter as fixed and only updates the shape parameter. This design choice requires detailed discussion and analysis. Please justify why fixing the scale is appropriate, analyze the impact on model expressiveness and identifiability, and provide sensitivity studies to show how results change if the scale is learned or set to different values.

3. The experiments only report results for a two-layer model, without comparisons to a single-layer model or deeper variants. This is insufficient to understand how the number of layers affects performance. Please include ablations that vary the depth (one layer vs. two layers vs. more layers) and discuss the observed trade-offs. Other key hyperparameters also merit discussion and sensitivity analysis, such as the window size and the number of factors.

4. For clarity, I recommend adding a detailed graphical illustration of the full model, including the temporal dynamics and the interactions between layers. A schematic in the spirit of Figure 1 of [1] would greatly aid understanding.

5. The Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis models closely related to this work, such as [2] and [3], and clearly position the proposed method relative to them.
