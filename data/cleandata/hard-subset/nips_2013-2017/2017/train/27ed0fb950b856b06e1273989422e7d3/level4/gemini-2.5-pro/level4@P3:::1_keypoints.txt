- Paper contribution: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that integrates deep/recurrent structures into Poisson Factorization to model complex temporal dependencies in sequential count data, with variational inference and neural-network parameter learning.

Strengths:
- Addresses modeling long-term temporal dependencies in sequential count data, a relevant and challenging problem.
- Novel integration of deep, recurrent-like neural network structures into PFA to learn flexible temporal dynamics beyond first-order Markov assumptions.
- Demonstrates applicability on five diverse real-world datasets.

Weaknesses — Clarity & Technical Precision:
- Generative process is incompletely specified (e.g., p(θ_t|...) in Eq.3 undefined and parameterization of Gamma outputs from the RNN not given).
- Model description appears inconsistent with standard RNNs (hierarchical dependence on higher layers but not on previous time-step of same layer), requiring justification.
- Terminology misuse: conditional distributions parameterized by neural nets are treated as "implicit" despite being explicit, evaluable densities.
- Inference objective and loss are unclear: the "Probability AutoEncoder" objective and the jump to an L2-based loss (Eq.16) lack connection to the ELBO and the probabilistic model.

Weaknesses — Experimental Evaluation:
- Evaluation metrics (MSE, PMSE) are inappropriate for probabilistic count models; predictive log-likelihood on held-out data should be reported.
- Latent factor dimensionality (K=3 or 10) is too small for high-dimensional datasets (e.g., V=14036), potentially limiting model performance; larger K (e.g., 50–100) should be tested.

Weakness — Writing & Presentation:
- Paper has poor writing quality with grammatical errors and awkward phrasing that hinder readability.

Detailed comments / questions for authors:
- Clarify which long-range patterns in specific datasets prior models struggle with (e.g., "fanatical" and "disaster" data).
- Provide full explicit generative process for θ_t and h_t^{(n)}, including how Gamma parameters are produced from network outputs.
- Justify and derive the training objective: how Eq.16 follows from the probabilistic model and why ELBO optimization was not used.
- Consider moving standard variational update equations to appendix to improve flow.
- Better explain and label figures (e.g., Fig.3 connection to "long-time activation", replace "transmission" in Fig.1 caption).
- Strongly recommend thorough proofreading by a native English speaker.

Conclusion:
- Interesting and promising idea, but major shortcomings in model/inference clarity, experimental validity, and writing; not recommended for acceptance without major revision.
