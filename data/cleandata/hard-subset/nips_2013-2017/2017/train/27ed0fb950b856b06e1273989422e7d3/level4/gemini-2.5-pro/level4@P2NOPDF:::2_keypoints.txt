- Proposes a deep generative model for sequential count data using an RNN to drive shape parameters of a hierarchical gamma latent variable model.  
- Uses an expectation-based update scheme for latent variables that reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency.  
- Paper presents an elegant, well-motivated approach for dynamic topic modeling.  
- Manuscript clarity needs improvement: add a detailed graphical illustration of the full generative/inference model and fix typos (Equation 10 likely erroneous).  
- Require justification for fixing gamma scale parameters and only learning shape parameters, plus analysis of its effect on flexibility and performance.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: add ablation studies (single-layer baseline, deeper models), and sensitivity analyses for RNN window size and number of latent factors.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete; must compare and contrast with recent models for count data (e.g., Gamma Belief Networks, Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation).
