This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model, a model based on PF that allows for temporal dependence. Unlike previous work on Dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network that allows for long-term dependence. The prediction is made through a variance estimate, with an additional step to maximize on the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on 5 real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvement in its execution before it can be accepted at a conference like NIPS. Please see my comments below.
+ The formula given in equation 4 does not correspond to an RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vector, and it does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer as the RNN setup. Is there a reason for this simplified structure?
+ In section 2, some of the model equations are missing (for example, it is not clear how to parameterize gamma in equation 4, or what the distribution generating theta_t is in equation 3).
+ I disagree with the discussion about the "inherent distribution" in lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 are actually clear: they are gamma with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (equation 9) can be computed in closed form based on h^{(0)}, it cannot be called inherent. The same comment applies to h in equation 10.
+ Lines 30-31: The sentence "This method can be a weak point in analyzing other data with different pattern long-term dependence, such as extremist data and disaster data" was not clear to me; can you elaborate?
+ The sub-sections in lines 133-155 are not clear to me. What exactly is the "loss function" in equation 15?
+ MSE/PMSE is generally not a good metric for models like PF. The results should additionally include at least the predictive log-probability.
+ The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small to me. I suggest that the authors use larger values, such as K=100 (at least).
+ I could not connect the discussion in lines 224-232 to figure 3: it is not clear.
+ The update equations for the variance estimation process (equations 11-14) can be safely moved to the appendix.
+ The caption in figure 4 is not clearly readable.
+ I strongly recommend following the general writing advice about the use of passive voice (especially for the abstract).
+ The writing quality is poor, and the paper has many typos. Here are some examples:
 - Line 15, "high-dimensional"
 - Line 22, "factor"
 - Line 24, "of property"
 - Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet"
 - Line 34, "nested"
 - Figure 1, "visual representation"
 - Figure 1, "transfer" (I am not sure if this is even a suitable word)
 - Incomplete sentences (for example, line 49)
 - Several missing articles (for example, "although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution")
