The authors introduce a novel approach to modeling complex long-term dependencies in count data by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, specifically through the gamma distribution. By approximating the shape parameters of these gamma distributions using neural networks, the authors effectively capture temporal dependencies by integrating information from both higher and lower layers. The neural network parameters are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which helps mitigate variance and enhance computational efficiency. This model presents an innovative solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models, and its potential is undeniable.

However, I have some suggestions for improvement:

1. Several critical typos were found, including in Equation 10, which should be corrected.
2. The authors' decision to fix the scale parameter and only update the shape parameter in the gamma distributions warrants further discussion and analysis.
3. The experimental evaluation is limited to a two-layer model, and it would be beneficial to compare its performance with single-layer and multi-layer models to better understand the impact of layer depth on performance. Additionally, the effects of other parameters, such as window size and number of factors, should be explored.
4. To facilitate a deeper understanding of the model, I recommend including detailed graphical illustrations of the entire framework. The authors may find inspiration in Figure 1 of [1].
5. The introduction would benefit from a discussion of recent deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as those presented in [2] and [3], to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed approach.
