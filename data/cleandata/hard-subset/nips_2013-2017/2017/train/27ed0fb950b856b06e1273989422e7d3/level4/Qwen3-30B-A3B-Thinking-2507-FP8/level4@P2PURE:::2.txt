

The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-term temporal dependencies in count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to represent implicit distributions via the gamma distribution. Specifically, the shape parameters of these gamma distributions—which encode temporal dependencies—are approximated using neural networks that integrate information from prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) components. Parameters in the neural networks are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which reduces variance from sampling and enhances computational efficiency. The model offers a novel and effective approach to addressing long-term dependency challenges in dynamic models. While the work is promising, the manuscript appears somewhat hastily written, and I have the following specific comments:

1.  Critical typographical errors exist, including in Equation 10.
2.  The authors fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameters; this choice requires rigorous justification and detailed analysis.
3.  Experimental results only evaluate the proposed model with two layers, omitting comparisons with single-layer and multi-layer configurations. This omission limits understanding of how layer depth influences performance. Additionally, key hyperparameters such as window size and the number of factors require discussion.
4.  A detailed graphical illustration of the complete model architecture would significantly improve clarity. The authors may refer to Figure 1 in [1] for a suitable reference.
5.  The Introduction should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, including [2] and [3], to contextualize the contribution.  

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.  
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen. Augmentable gamma belief networks. Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1–44, 2016.  
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou. Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC. ICML, 2017.
