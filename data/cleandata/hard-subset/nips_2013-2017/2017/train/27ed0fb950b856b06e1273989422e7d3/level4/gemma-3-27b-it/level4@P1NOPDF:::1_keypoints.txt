- Overall: Idea is interesting but the paper needs significant refinement before being conference-ready (likely reject in current form).  
- Model/RNN issue: Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN—hidden state h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps within the same layer; request justification for this simplification.  
- Missing model details: Section 2 omits crucial parameterizations (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3).  
- Mischaracterization of distributions: Lines 110–118 incorrectly call distributions “implicit”—Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit Gamma distributions and θ_tk can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^(0).  
- Unclear limitation statement: Lines 30–31 about weaknesses on “fanatical data and disaster data” are unclear and need elaboration.  
- Inference/optimization clarity: Subsection lines 133–155 is hard to follow—specifically, clarify what the “loss function” in Eq. 15 is.  
- Evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE are not ideal for Poisson factorization models; report predictive log-likelihood (at minimum).  
- Latent dimension K: The experimental choice of K is too small; authors should test larger values (e.g., K ≥ 100).  
- Figure-text mismatch: Discussion in lines 224–232 is not clearly connected to Fig. 3.  
- Placement of derivations: Update equations for variational inference (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplementary material.  
- Figure caption/readability: Caption for Fig. 4 is difficult to read and needs reworking.  
- Writing style: Strong recommendation to reduce passive voice (particularly in the abstract) and improve overall writing quality.  
- Typos and grammar: Paper contains numerous typos, missing articles, inconsistent capitalization, incomplete sentences, and awkward phrasing—multiple specific examples provided (lines 15, 22, 24, 29, 34, Fig. 1, line 49, etc.).
