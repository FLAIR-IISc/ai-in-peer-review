- Paper proposes a deep, RNN-like Poisson factorization model with variational inference to capture long-range temporal dependencies; technically strong but marred by factual and grammatical issues.
- Incorrect factual claim: lines 86–87 about modeling nonnegative numbers are wrong — the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate model.
- Presentation request: bold the best results in Table 2 for clarity.
- Evaluation critique: MSE/PMSE are acceptable but held-out perplexity would be a stronger evaluation metric for Section 4.2.
- Comparative analysis: while existing long-range dependency limitations are addressed, the paper should compare to a single-level deep dynamic PF to isolate benefits of the multi-level hierarchy; current LSTM comparison is confounded by different learning algorithms.
- Readability issues: numerous grammatical errors and typos throughout (multiple quoted examples) that impede understanding.
