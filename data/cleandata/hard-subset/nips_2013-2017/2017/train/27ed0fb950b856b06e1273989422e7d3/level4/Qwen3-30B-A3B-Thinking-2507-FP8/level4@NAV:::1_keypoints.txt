- Paper proposes deep dynamic Poisson factorization using a simplified RNN and variational inference, evaluated on five datasets.  
- Concept is promising but requires major revisions to meet NIPS standards.  
- Eq. 4 is not a standard RNN—hidden state lacks within-layer temporal recurrence; authors must justify this simplification.  
- Section 2 omits crucial model details (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distributional form of θ_t in Eq. 3).  
- The claim about "implicit distributions" is incorrect: Eqs. 9–10 define explicit Gamma distributions and θ_tk is analytically tractable given h^(0).  
- Clarify the vague reference to "fanatical data and disaster data" as examples of "weak points."  
- The loss function in Eq. 15 is insufficiently explained and needs a clearer derivation/interpretation.  
- MSE/PMSE are inappropriate evaluation metrics for Poisson factorization; report predictive log-likelihood as a primary metric.  
- The chosen latent dimension K is unrealistically small (line 207); experiments should use K ≥ 100 at minimum.  
- The discussion (lines 224–232) does not adequately connect to or explain Figure 3.  
- Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are better placed in the supplement to streamline the main text.  
- Figure 4 caption is illegible and must be rewritten for clarity.  
- Use active voice throughout (especially the abstract) to match NIPS style.  
- Writing quality is poor with many typos and grammatical errors—specific issues: "high-dimensional," "factorize," missing words in "property of the," inconsistent capitalization of "Dirichlet," probable typo "nested," awkward terms in Figure 1, incomplete sentence at line 49, and missing articles.
