- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), adding deep neural hierarchies to model long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Extends Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent/deep structures to overcome shallow-model limits like PGDS.  
- Uses variational inference with neural-network parameter optimization and leverages Gamma–Poisson conjugacy for efficient updates.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) showing better prediction and fit than baselines including PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Demonstrates good interpretability of learned factors (e.g., factor activations in Figure 2).  
- Technical quality is strong: well-motivated model, proper implementation, and solid derivations.  
- Writing clarity is poor due to numerous grammatical errors and inconsistent phrasing, hindering readability.  
- Originality is good: clearly differentiates from prior dynamic Poisson models by introducing depth for long-term dependency modeling.  
- Significance is substantial: applicable across domains (recommendation, text, networks, time series) and valuable for capturing long-term patterns.  
- Lacks a direct comparison with a one-layer deep architecture to justify the necessity of multiple hierarchical layers.  
- Notes “pruning” as a limitation but provides insufficient analysis of its impact across datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation misses held-out perplexity, a standard metric for count models, relying instead on MSE/PMSE.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and valuable, accept with minor revisions to fix writing and strengthen experimental analyses.
