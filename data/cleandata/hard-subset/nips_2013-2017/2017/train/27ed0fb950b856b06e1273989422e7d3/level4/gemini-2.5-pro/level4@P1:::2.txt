This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model (DDPFA), a novel method for analyzing sequential count data. The model extends the framework of Poisson Factor Analysis to capture complex, long-range temporal dependencies. The core contribution is a deep hierarchical structure where latent variables at each layer follow a Gamma distribution. Crucially, the shape parameters of these Gamma distributions, which govern the temporal dynamics, are parameterized by recurrent neural networks (RNNs). This allows the model to learn flexible, non-linear transition dynamics. The inference procedure combines mean-field variational inference for the latent variables with gradient-based optimization for the neural network parameters, based on a loss function derived from a probabilistic autoencoder perspective. This approach elegantly avoids the high variance often associated with sampling-based methods.

**Strengths**

The primary strength of this work lies in its novel and compelling approach to modeling long-range dependencies in dynamic count data. Combining the interpretability of Bayesian matrix factorization models with the expressive power of deep recurrent neural networks is a powerful idea. The proposed method of using RNNs to parameterize the Gamma priors for the latent factors is an elegant way to learn complex dynamics beyond the typical first-order Markov assumptions seen in models like PGDS. The inference strategy, which updates neural network parameters based on the expectations of latent variables, is well-motivated and computationally efficient. The model provides an interesting and promising direction for dynamic topic modeling and temporal analysis of count matrices.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

While the core idea is promising, the manuscript in its current form could be significantly strengthened. My main concerns are related to clarity, model justification, and the depth of the experimental analysis.

1.  **Clarity and Presentation**: The model's formulation could be made clearer.
    *   A more detailed graphical model illustration would greatly aid reader comprehension. Figure 1 is quite abstract; a diagram that explicitly shows the dependencies between the latent variables (`h`, `θ`, `φ`, etc.) and the data `x`, similar to Figure 1 in Chung et al. (NIPS 2015), would be highly beneficial.
    *   Some key equations may contain typos or require further explanation. For instance, Equation 10 seems unclear in its current form and would benefit from a more detailed derivation or clarification.

2.  **Model Justification**: A key design choice appears to be modeling the temporal dynamics by having the RNNs output only the *shape* parameters of the Gamma distributions, while the *scale* (or rate) parameters seem to be treated as fixed or updated more simply. The rationale for this choice is not discussed. A detailed analysis or justification for why only the shape parameter is modeled with this complex machinery would strengthen the paper's technical contribution.

3.  **Experimental Evaluation**: The experimental section demonstrates promising results but could be more thorough to fully substantiate the claims about the benefits of the deep structure.
    *   While Table 3 provides a brief analysis of model depth, the finding that deeper layers do not yield significant improvements is a critical result that warrants a more in-depth discussion and investigation. Why does the model not benefit from added depth? Is this an issue of "pruning" as the authors suggest, an optimization challenge, or a fundamental property of the model/data?
    *   A more extensive ablation study analyzing the influence of key hyperparameters, such as the window size (`c`) and the number of factors (`K`), would provide valuable insights into the model's behavior and sensitivity.

4.  **Related Work**: The introduction could be updated to include discussion of other recent deep generative models for count data. In particular, the authors should consider discussing and comparing their approach to recent work such as Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks (Zhou et al., JMLR 2016) and Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation (Cong et al., ICML 2017), which also explore deep hierarchical structures for topic modeling.

In summary, this paper presents an innovative and potentially impactful model. However, its acceptance would be contingent on a significant revision that improves the clarity of the model's presentation, provides justification for key design choices, and offers a more comprehensive experimental analysis.
