- Novel method: uses recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions via gamma distributions to capture complex long-time dependence in count vectors.
- Shape parameters of gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks that combine prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.
- Parameter updates use expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.
- Well-motivated problem with a clear review of limitations of existing methods.
- DDPFA model is clearly described and the RNN-based implicit distribution approach is promising.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using four multi-dimensional synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets.
- Empirical results show DDPFA outperforms baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) in most cases.
- Manuscript contains typos (e.g., Equation 10) that need correction.
- The authors fix the gamma scale parameter and only update the shape parameter; this choice requires more detailed discussion and analysis.
- Experiments only consider a two-layer model; no comparisons to single-layer or deeper variants to assess layer influence.
- Other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) are not sufficiently discussed.
- The manuscript would benefit from more detailed graphical illustrations of the full DDPFA architecture.
- Recent deep Poisson factor analysis works (e.g., [2], [3]) are not discussed and should be cited in the Introduction.
- Overall assessment: strong and significant contribution but requires revisions and additional analyses before acceptance.
