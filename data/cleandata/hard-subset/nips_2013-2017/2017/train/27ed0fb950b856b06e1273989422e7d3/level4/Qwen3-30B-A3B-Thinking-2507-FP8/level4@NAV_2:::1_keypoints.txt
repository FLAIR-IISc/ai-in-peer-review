- Introduces DDPFA: a novel extension of Poisson Factor Analysis using deep latent structures and RNNs to model temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Sound technical basis using Gamma-Poisson conjugacy and variational inference, but model formulation has critical gaps.  
- Eq. (4) does not describe a standard RNN and lacks intra-layer temporal dependencies; Gamma parameterizations for latent variables are unspecified.  
- The paper claims “implicit distributions” but inference (Eqs. 9–14) depends on explicit Gamma distributions, creating a contradiction.  
- Inference algorithm description (Lines 133–155) is unclear; key elements like the loss in Eq. (15) are undefined.  
- MSE/PMSE are inadequate metrics for count models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Experimental choices limit impact: very small K (3–10) underrepresents model capacity; larger K (e.g., 100) should be tried.  
- Writing quality is poor with typos, missing articles, incomplete sentences, and an illegible Figure 1 caption, harming readability.  
- Discussion of Fig. 3 lacks a clear connection to the visualization; Eqs. (11)–(14) are ambiguously presented and could be moved to the supplement.  
- Conceptually original to integrate deep structures into dynamic Poisson factorization; ladder architecture addresses pruning in hierarchical models.  
- Neural network implementation (Eq. 4) is overly simplified and the relation to related work (e.g., Deep Exponential Families) is superficial.  
- Empirically strong on synthetic data and yields interpretable factors, especially for long-term patterns, but PGDS outperforms DDPFA on ICEWS.  
- LSTM baselines fail on high-dimensional datasets (EBOLA, ASP), and Table 3’s finding that added layers are “useless” undermines the claimed depth advantage.  
- Pros: novel integration of deep models with Poisson factorization, good synthetic results, interpretable real-data factors.  
- Cons: incomplete model specification, contradictory/incomplete inference claims, inadequate evaluation metrics, poor writing, and limited experimental settings.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions to fix technical gaps, clarify inference, improve evaluation, and substantially rewrite for clarity.
