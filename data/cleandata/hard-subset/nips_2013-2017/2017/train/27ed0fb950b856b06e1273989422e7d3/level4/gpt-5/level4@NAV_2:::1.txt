This paper presents a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPFA) model that extends Poisson factorization to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, it employs a simplified recurrent neural mechanism to model longer-range dependencies. Inference is performed via variational methods, with an additional optimization step for the neural network parameters. The paper provides results on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea is promising, but I believe the submission requires significant refinement before it is suitable for a venue like NIPS. My detailed comments follow.

+Equation (4) does not reflect a conventional RNN: h_t^(n) is conditioned only on top-layer states and does not incorporate same-layer temporal recursion (i.e., dependencies on h_{t−1}^{(n)}, etc.). What motivates this simplified architecture?

+Section 2 omits key specification details (e.g., how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized, and what exact distribution is used to generate θ_t in Eq. 3).

+I disagree with the characterization of “implicit distributions” in lines 110–118. Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit Gamma distributions with defined shape and rate parameters. Since p(θ_tk | h^(0)) in Eq. 9 is available in closed form, it is not implicit; the same applies to h in Eq. 10.

+Lines 30–31: The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear; please clarify what “fanatical data” means and elaborate on the claim.

+The subsection spanning lines 133–155 is difficult to follow. In particular, what precisely is being optimized in Eq. 15, and how should the “loss function” be interpreted?

+MSE/PMSE is not an ideal evaluation metric for PF-style models. Please also report predictive log-likelihood (or held-out perplexity) to better assess probabilistic fit.

+The choice of K in the experiments (line 207) appears too small. I recommend exploring substantially larger values (e.g., K=100 or more) to assess capacity and robustness.

+The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Figure 3; the mapping from text to the depicted factors is not evident.

+The variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the appendix/supplement without loss of clarity in the main text.

+The caption in Figure 4 is not legible.

+I strongly recommend revising the manuscript to reduce passive voice, especially in the abstract.

+The writing quality needs improvement, and there are numerous typos. Examples include:
 -Line 15, “high-dimensional”
 -Line 22, “factorize”
 -Line 24, “property of the”
 -Lines 29, 34, “Dirichlet”
 -Line 34, “nested”
 -Fig. 1, “visual representation”
 -Fig. 1, “transmission” (which may not be the appropriate term)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Frequent missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution”)
