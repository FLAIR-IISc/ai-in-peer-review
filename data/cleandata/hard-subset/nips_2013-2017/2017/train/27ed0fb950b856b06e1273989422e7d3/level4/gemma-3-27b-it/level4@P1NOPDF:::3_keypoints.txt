1. Proposes a deep, RNN-like Poisson factorization to model long-range temporal dependencies, using variational inference to learn network parameters.  
2. Paper has solid technical content but contains several factual inaccuracies and grammatical issues.  
3. The claim about modeling nonnegative numbers (lines 86–87) is incorrect; the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate method.  
4. Presentation: bold the best results in Table 2 for clarity.  
5. Evaluation: MSE/PMSE are acceptable but held-out perplexity would be a more compelling performance metric.  
6. Validation: compare against a single-layer dynamic Poisson factorization to justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels, since LSTM comparison alone is inconclusive.  
7. Readability: many grammatical errors and typos throughout (multiple example phrases provided) that must be corrected.
