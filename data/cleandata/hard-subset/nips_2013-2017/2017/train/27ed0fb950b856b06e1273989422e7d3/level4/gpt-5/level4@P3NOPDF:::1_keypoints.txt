1) Summary: Proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization with a simplified RNN-like top-down temporal pathway, variational inference plus NN parameter maximization, evaluated on five datasets.

2) Strength: Addresses an important problem of adding temporal structure to Poisson factorization.

3) Strength: Using a neural architecture to capture long-term dependencies is an interesting idea.

4) Strength: Variational inference enables scalability and the empirical evaluation spans multiple datasets.

5) Major concern 1 (model/RNN claim): Eq. 4 does not implement a standard RNN—hidden states depend only on top-layer memory, not previous same-layer states—please justify or clarify this architectural choice and its implications.

6) Major concern 2 (generative specification): The generative model is incomplete—missing Gamma parameterizations and the distribution for θ_t—provide full priors, shapes/rates, and notation.

7) Major concern 3 (implicit distributions): Calling p(θ_tk | h^(0)) “implicit” is misleading since it has a closed-form Gamma; revise the discussion.

8) Major concern 4 (unclear sentence): Clarify the unclear sentence about “fanatical/disaster” data—specify intended data types (e.g., financial) and what long-term dependency patterns challenge the method.

9) Major concern 5 (objective/optimization): The loss in Eq. 15 is unclear—explicitly state the ELBO (if used), which terms optimize variational vs. NN parameters, and give the optimization schedule/algorithm.

10) Major concern 6 (evaluation metrics): MSE/PMSE are inappropriate alone—report held-out Poisson log-likelihood, perplexity, ranking metrics, time-split evaluation, and calibration.

11) Major concern 7 (model capacity/sensitivity): K appears small—include experiments with larger K (e.g., K=100), sensitivity analysis over K and depth, plus runtime and convergence behavior.

12) Major concern 8 (results presentation): Fig. 3 and its text are unclear—specify axes, baselines, what is plotted, and make captions self-contained.

13) Major concern 9 (variational updates placement): Move detailed variational update algebra (Eqs. 11–14) to the appendix and keep intuition, complexity, and the algorithm in the main text.

14) Major concern 10 (figure quality): Improve readability of Fig. 4 (larger fonts, clearer labels).

15) Writing and presentation: Reduce passive voice, fix numerous typos and consistency issues (examples provided), and proofread for grammar and clarity.

16) Suggestions for improvement: Provide a complete generative model, clarify/justify the nonstandard RNN design or compare to true RNN baselines, make the objective explicit, expand evaluation and sensitivity analyses, and move algebraic details to the supplement.

17) Overall assessment: Important problem with a potentially interesting approach, but significant gaps in model specification, architectural justification, objective clarity, and evaluation mean the paper is not acceptable in current form.
