1) Contribution: Proposes using RNNs to parameterize gamma shape parameters for modeling long-range dependencies in count data, updating parameters via expectations to reduce variance and improve efficiency.  
2) Presentation/typos: Manuscript contains typos (e.g., Eq.10) and needs thorough proofreading.  
3) Model assumption: Fixing the gamma scale parameter while only learning the shape requires stronger justification and sensitivity analysis; implications for flexibility and performance are unclear.  
4) Experiments: Evaluation is limited (only two-layer model); need comparisons with single- and deeper-layer variants and sensitivity studies for hyperparameters (window size, latent factors).  
5) Visualization: Paper lacks a clear graphical model/architecture diagram showing interactions among RNN, gamma distributions, and latent variables.  
6) Related work: Introduction should discuss and contrast recent deep Poisson factor analysis work (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) to better position the contribution.  
7) Overall: Core idea is promising but the paper needs clearer presentation, stronger justification of design choices, more comprehensive experiments, and expanded related work to be convincing.
