- Paper proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model using a simplified RNN and variational inference, evaluated on five datasets.  
- Overall idea is intriguing but execution needs improvement to meet NIPS standards.  
- Eq. 4 does not follow a traditional RNN: hidden state h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer memory, not previous time steps—need justification.  
- Section 2 omits key model equations (Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating theta_t in Eq. 3).  
- The claim about "implicit distributions" is incorrect: Eqs. 9–10 define explicit Gamma distributions and theta_tk can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^(0).  
- The sentence about the method’s weaknesses on "fanatical" or "disaster" data (lines 30–31) is vague and needs clarification.  
- Subsection lines 133–155 is unclear, especially the definition and role of the "loss function" in Eq. 15.  
- MSE and PMSE are not appropriate metrics for PF-like models; predictive log-likelihood should be included.  
- Chosen latent dimension K appears too small; recommend larger values such as K = 100.  
- Discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to or explain Fig. 3.  
- Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to streamline the main text.  
- Caption for Fig. 4 is hard to read and should be improved.  
- Authors should follow general writing guidelines and avoid excessive passive voice, especially in the abstract.  
- Writing quality has many typos, grammatical errors, missing articles, and unclear phrases—multiple specific line-level corrections provided.
