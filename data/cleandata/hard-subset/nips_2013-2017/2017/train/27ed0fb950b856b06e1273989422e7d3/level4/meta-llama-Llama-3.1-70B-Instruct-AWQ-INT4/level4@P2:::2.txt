This paper proposes a novel method, called Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA), to capture the complex long-time dependence over the count vectors by exploiting recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions. The authors provide a thorough background on the existing methods for analyzing sequentially observed count vectors, highlighting their limitations and the need for a more sophisticated approach. The proposed DDPFA model is based on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and incorporates recurrent neural networks to learn complicated relationships between different factors among short time steps. The model also includes a deep structure to capture long-time dependence.

The authors provide a clear explanation of the model's components, including the variational inference algorithm used for inferring the latent variables and the gradient descent-based optimization method. The paper also includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation of the proposed model on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing complex time dependence and outperforming existing methods.

The strengths of the paper include its novelty, thoroughness, and clarity. The authors provide a well-motivated introduction, a clear explanation of the proposed model, and a comprehensive experimental evaluation. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. One of the main concerns is the lack of detailed discussion and analysis on the choice of fixing the scale parameter and only updating the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. Additionally, the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, including a comparison of the proposed model with different numbers of layers and a more detailed analysis of the influence of the window size and the number of factors on the performance.

Furthermore, the paper could benefit from more detailed graphical illustrations of the whole model, which would help readers better understand the proposed approach. Finally, the authors should discuss recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as [2] and [3], to provide a more comprehensive overview of the current state of the art.

In terms of the NIPS evaluation criteria, the paper scores well on novelty, clarity, and significance. However, it could be improved in terms of thoroughness, particularly in the experimental evaluation and the discussion of related work.
