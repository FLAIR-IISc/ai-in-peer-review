This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, extending PF to capture temporal dependencies. In contrast to prior dynamic PF work, it employs a simplified recurrent neural network to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional maximization step over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea is potentially interesting, but the paper requires further improvements before it can be accepted at a conference like NIPS. My detailed comments follow.

- The formulation in Eq. (4) does not correspond to an RNN structure: h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer, as in a standard RNN. Is there a reason for this simplified structure?
- In Section 2, some model equations are missing. For example, it is unclear how the Gamma distribution in Eq. (4) is parameterized, or what distribution generates θ_t in Eq. (3).
- I disagree with the discussion in lines 110–118 regarding “implicit distributions.” Eqs. (9) and (10) are explicit: they are Gamma distributions with specified shapes and rates. Since the distribution of θ_{t,k} (Eq. (9)) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it should not be called implicit. The same applies to h in Eq. (10).
- Lines 30–31: The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear; could you elaborate?
- The subsection in lines 133–155 is unclear. What exactly is the “loss function” in Eq. (15)?
- MSE/PMSE is typically not an appropriate metric for PF-like models. Please also report predictive log-likelihood.
- The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small. I suggest using larger values, such as K = 100 (at least).
- I could not connect the discussion in lines 224–232 with Fig. 3; the connection is unclear.
- The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11–14) can safely be moved to the supplement.
- The caption in Fig. 4 is hard to read.
- I strongly recommend following general writing advice regarding the use of the passive voice, especially in the abstract.
- The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Examples include:
  - Line 15: “high-dimensional”
  - Line 22: “factorize”
  - Line 24: “property of the”
  - Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
  - Line 34: “nested”
  - Fig. 1: “visual representation”
  - Fig. 1: “transmission” (not sure this is an appropriate word anyway)
  - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
  - Many missing articles (e.g., “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)
