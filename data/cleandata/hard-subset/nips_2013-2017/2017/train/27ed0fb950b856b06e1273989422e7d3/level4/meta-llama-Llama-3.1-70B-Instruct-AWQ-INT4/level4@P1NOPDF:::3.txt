The paper explores the incorporation of long-range temporal dependence in a Poisson factorization model with a deep structure. Unlike previous works on dynamic Poisson Factorization (PF), this paper employs a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies. Ultimately, variational inference with optimization routines is used to infer the parameters of the neural network. The paper has solid technical content, but it is also marred by grammatical and factual errors, which are listed below.

1. The statement on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers is incorrect. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is actually used for modeling nonnegative numbers.

2. The authors should bold the best results in Table 2 for clarity.

3. Held-out perplexity should have been a stronger indicator in the experiments in Section 4.2, although Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Predictive Mean Squared Error (PMSE) are acceptable.

4. The paper adequately addresses the limitations of existing works in modeling long-range dependencies (references 1 and 2). However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method with multiple levels of hierarchy, a comparison with dynamic PF using a one-level deep architecture would have been beneficial. While the comparison with Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) is useful, the learning algorithms differ. Therefore, this comparison does not fully justify the need for multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.

5. There are several grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper, which make it somewhat difficult to read. These include:
-- "unit the data" should be revised for clarity.
-- "Noticing the lack of capturing" is an awkward phrase and could be rephrased.
-- The tense of the sentence "PFA factorized a count matrix" is inconsistent with the tense of other sentences in the same paragraph.
-- "other data with different pattern long-time dependence" is unclear and could be rephrased.
-- "Examples for this including" is grammatically incorrect.
-- "shows excellent results on predict and" should be revised for clarity.
-- "However, it is rough for them" is an awkward phrase and could be rephrased.
-- "V-dimension sequentially" contains a typo.
-- "K-dimension latent variable" contains a typo.
-- "due to the including of feature-wise" is unclear and could be rephrased.
-- "recommend systems" should be revised to "recommendation systems."
