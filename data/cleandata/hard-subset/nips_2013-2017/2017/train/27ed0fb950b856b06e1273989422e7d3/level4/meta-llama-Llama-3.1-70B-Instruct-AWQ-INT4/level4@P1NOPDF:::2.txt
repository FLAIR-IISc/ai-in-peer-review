The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-time dependencies over count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, realized through the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which capture time dependence, are approximated by neural networks that attempt to incorporate information from both prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer). The parameters in the neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which helps reduce the variance caused by sampling and increase computational efficiency. The model provides an interesting and intelligent approach to handling the long dependency problem in dynamic models. Although I find the method compelling, the manuscript appears to have been written in haste, and I have several comments to address:

1. There are some critical typos, notably in Equation 10.
2. The authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. This decision requires more detailed discussion and analysis to justify the approach.
3. The experimental evaluation only presents the performance of the proposed model with two layers, without comparing it to models with a single layer or more layers. This limited comparison is insufficient to understand the influence of the number of layers on performance. Additionally, other parameters, such as the size of the window and the number of factors, need to be discussed.
4. To facilitate better understanding, I suggest the authors provide detailed graphical illustrations of the entire model. For reference, the authors can consider Figure 1 in [1].
5. Recently, new deep Poisson factor analysis models have been proposed, such as [2] and [3]. These models should be discussed in the Introduction to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed method.
