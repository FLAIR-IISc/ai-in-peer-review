This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, a model that builds on Poisson factorization (PF) to allow for temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous works on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to allow for long-term dependencies. Inference is carried out via variational inference, with an extra step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

1. The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure, because $h_t^{(n)}$ depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer, as in the RNN setup. Is there any specific reason for this simplified structure?

2. In Section 2, some of the model equations are missing. For instance, it is not clear how to parameterize the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 or what distribution generates $\theta_t$ in Eq. 3.

3. I disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding "implicit distributions". Eqs. 9 and 10 are actually explicit: they represent Gammas with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of $\theta_{tk}$ (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on $h^{(0)}$, it cannot be called implicit. The same comment applies to $h$ in Eq. 10.

4. The sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," was not clear to me. Could the authors provide more clarification or context?

5. The subsection in lines 133-155 is not clear to me. What exactly is the "loss function" in Eq. 15?

6. Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Predictive Mean Squared Error (PMSE) are typically not good metrics for PF-like models. The results should additionally include at least predictive log-likelihood.

7. The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small to me. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100 (at least).

8. I could not connect the discussion in lines 224-232 with Fig. 3. It is not clear how the two are related.

9. The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can safely be moved to the supplement.

10. The caption in Fig. 4 cannot be read clearly.

11. I strongly recommend following general writing advice about the use of the passive voice, especially in the abstract.

12. The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Here are some examples:
    - Line 15, "high-dimensional" (correct)
    - Line 22, "factorize" (correct)
    - Line 24, "property of the" (correct)
    - Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet" (correct)
    - Line 34, "nested" (correct)
    - Fig. 1, "visual representation" (correct)
    - Fig.1, "transmission" (not sure if that's an appropriate word anyway)
    - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
    - Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")
