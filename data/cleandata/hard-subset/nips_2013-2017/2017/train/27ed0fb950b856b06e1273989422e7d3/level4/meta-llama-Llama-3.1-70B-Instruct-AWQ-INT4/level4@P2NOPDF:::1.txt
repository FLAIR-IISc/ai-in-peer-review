This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, a model that extends Poisson factorization (PF) to account for temporal dependencies. Unlike previous works on dynamic PF, this paper employs a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed using variational inference, with an additional step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper presents experimental results on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I believe the paper requires some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

* The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps for the same layer, as in typical RNN setups. Is there any specific reason for this simplified structure?

* In Section 2, some model equations are missing. For instance, it is unclear how to parameterize the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 or what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3. Providing these details would enhance the clarity of the model.

* I disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding "implicit distributions." Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit, representing Gammas with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be considered implicit. The same comment applies to h in Eq. 10.

* The sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," is unclear to me. Could you elaborate on this point?

* The subsection in lines 133-155 is not clear, particularly the definition of the "loss function" in Eq. 15. A more detailed explanation would be helpful.

* Mean squared error (MSE) and predictive mean squared error (PMSE) are not typically suitable metrics for PF-like models. I recommend including additional metrics, such as predictive log-likelihood, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

* The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100, to better capture the underlying patterns.

* I found it challenging to connect the discussion in lines 224-232 with Fig. 3. Could you provide more context or clarify the relationship between the text and the figure?

* The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can be safely moved to the supplement to improve the paper's flow.

* The caption in Fig. 4 is difficult to read. I recommend revising it for better clarity.

* I strongly advise following general writing advice regarding the use of the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

* The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Here are some examples:
    - Line 15, "high-dimensional" should be "high-dimensional" (although this is correct, there are other typos)
    - Line 22, "factorize"
    - Line 24, "property of the"
    - Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet"
    - Line 34, "nested"
    - Fig. 1, "visual representation"
    - Fig. 1, "transmission" (consider rephrasing for clarity)
    - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
    - Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")

Addressing these issues will improve the overall quality and clarity of the paper.
