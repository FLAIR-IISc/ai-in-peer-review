- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): deep hierarchical Gamma latent variables with RNN-parameterized shape parameters for sequential count vectors.
- Observations modeled as Poisson with factors and scaling terms; temporal dependence captured by Gamma-distributed latents whose shapes are outputs of RNNs over sliding windows.
- Inference uses mean-field conjugate updates for Gamma–Poisson parts and a two-stage procedure alternating VI updates with RNN training via a “probability autoencoder” squared-error objective.
- Positions work relative to dynamic PFA/PGDS, Deep Exponential Families/GBN, VRNN, Recurrent Poisson Factorization, and neural autoregressive topic models.
- Strength: Addresses high-dimensional, sparse sequential count data common in text, events, and recommendation settings.
- Strength: Conceptually interesting integration of deep temporal structure with conjugate Poisson factor analysis to capture long-horizon dependence.
- Strength: Uses conditionally conjugate mean-field updates for many latents, which can be efficient and stable.
- Strength: Empirical results show improved fit/prediction over PFA and some gains versus PGDS/LSTM; ICEWS factors are qualitatively interpretable.
- Weakness: Neural network training objective is ad hoc (squared-error between expectations) rather than a principled ELBO; no guarantee of valid variational bound or convergence.
- Weakness: Gamma rate parameters are fixed rather than learned, raising concerns about posterior geometry, identifiability, and redundancy among λ, ψ, φ, and θ.
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on MSE/PMSE; predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and calibration on held-out data are missing.
- Weakness: Important related baselines (e.g., Recurrent Poisson Factorization, sequential GBN/DEF variants) are omitted from comparisons.
- Weakness: Limited ablations — depth, window size c, and number of factors K not systematically studied; pruning and ladder effects not quantified.
- Weakness: Complexity and scalability (time per iteration, memory vs V, T, K, layers) and code availability are not discussed.
- Clarity: Paper has many typos, inconsistent notation, and incomplete references; Eq. 10 and Gamma parameterization need correction and proofreading.
- Clarity: Generative model and inference flow need a plate diagram and compact algorithm box for clarity.
- Clarity: Roles and architectures of feedforward/feedback RNNs and ladder connections are not precisely described (inputs, outputs, sharing).
- Originality: Relationship to similar prior models is not clearly delineated; need to articulate methodological differences and include empirical comparisons.
- Significance: Approach could be impactful if inference is made principled and experiments expanded, but current empirical claims are not yet conclusive.
- Suggestion: Provide a principled ELBO and joint optimization (e.g., VRNN ELBO, implicit Gamma reparameterization, or black-box VI/SG-MCMC).
- Suggestion: Report predictive log-likelihood, calibration, and uncertainty (standard errors over runs); clarify data splits and hyperparameter tuning.
- Suggestion: Add systematic ablations for depth (1/2/3), K, window size, and present training curves and pruning analysis.
- Suggestion: Resolve identifiability by normalizing/constraining scaling components (e.g., fix Φ column sums or remove λ/ψ).
- Suggestion: Expand related work discussion (VRNN, GBN, deep LDA/SG-MCMC, Recurrent Poisson Factorization) and clarify differences.
- Suggestion: Include a plate diagram, algorithm box, detailed RNN settings, and release code and data splits.
- Pros for acceptance: Tackles an important problem with an appealing hybrid approach; promising empirical results and interpretable factors; partly conjugate inference helps efficiency.
- Cons for acceptance: Ad hoc neural training objective, incomplete/non-standard evaluation and baselines, insufficient ablations, and substantial clarity/presentation issues.
