- Proposes DDPFA: a novel RNN-based extension of Poisson Factor Analysis to capture complex, long-range temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Replaces Markovian Gamma transitions (e.g., PGDS) with RNNs to model implicit conditional distributions of latent temporal factors.  
- Uses a hybrid inference scheme combining coordinate-ascent variational updates for latent variables with gradient-based optimization for neural network parameters (VAE-like structure).  
- Technical contribution is non-trivial: integrates Bayesian probabilistic modeling with deep learning and a sound inference strategy.  
- Empirical evaluation spans synthetic and real-world datasets and shows favorable performance versus baselines (PFA, PGDS, LSTM).  
- Major experimental weakness: lacks a key ablation — a shallow (single-layer) version of the model — to validate that depth, not other factors, drives improvements.  
- Results (Table 3) do not consistently show benefits from added layers, weakening the central claim about the importance of depth.  
- Presentation is poor with numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing that hinder readability and technical clarity.  
- Evaluation metrics choice is suboptimal for text: held-out perplexity would be more standard than MSE for corpora like NIPS.  
- Minor inaccuracies/omissions: vague description of non-count modeling (compound Poisson) and lack of visual emphasis (bolding) for best results in tables.  
- Overall: promising and significant idea but currently undermined by insufficient ablation, weak empirical justification for depth, and poor writing; acceptance conditional on stronger experiments and thorough revision.
