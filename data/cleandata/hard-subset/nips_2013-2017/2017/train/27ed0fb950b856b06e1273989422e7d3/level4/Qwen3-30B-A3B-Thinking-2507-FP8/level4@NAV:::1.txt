

This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, extending Poisson factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, the authors employ a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to capture long-term dependencies. Inference utilizes variational inference with an additional step to optimize neural network parameters, and the model is evaluated on five real-world datasets.  

Overall, the concept holds potential, but the paper requires significant revisions before it meets NIPS standards. Specific concerns are:  
+ Equation 4 does not represent a standard RNN, as $h_t^{(n)}$ depends solely on top-layer memory vectors without temporal recurrence within the same layer. Justify this simplification.  
+ Section 2 omits key model details (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4, distributional form of $\theta_t$ in Eq. 3).  
+ The discussion of "implicit distributions" (lines 110–118) is incorrect; Eqs. 9–10 explicitly define Gamma distributions with known parameters, and $\theta_{tk}$ (Eq. 9) is analytically tractable given $h^{(0)}$.  
+ Lines 30–31: Clarify the unclear reference to "fanatical data and disaster data" as examples of "weak points."  
+ The loss function in Eq. 15 (lines 133–155) is not sufficiently explained.  
+ MSE/PMSE is inappropriate for PF models; include predictive log-likelihood as a primary metric.  
+ The value of $K$ (line 207) is unrealistically small; use $K \geq 100$ at minimum.  
+ The discussion (lines 224–232) fails to connect with Figure 3.  
+ Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) may be moved to the supplement.  
+ Figure 4 caption is illegible.  
+ Adopt active voice throughout (especially the abstract) to align with NIPS conventions.  
+ The writing is poor, riddled with typos and grammatical errors:  
  - Line 15: "high-dimensional" (typo)  
  - Line 22: "factorize" (typo)  
  - Line 24: "property of the" (missing word)  
  - Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet" (inconsistent capitalization)  
  - Line 34: "nested" (likely typo)  
  - Figure 1: "visual representation," "transmission" (inappropriate term)  
  - Line 49: Incomplete sentence  
  - Missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as *a* prior distribution").
