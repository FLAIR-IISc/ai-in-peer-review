- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA), a recurrent neural network–based extension of Poisson Factorization for sequential count vectors to capture long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Inference: Uses a hybrid inference scheme combining coordinate-ascent variational inference for latent variables with gradient-based optimization for neural network parameters.  
- Evaluation: Tested on synthetic and real-world datasets (e.g., NIPS, ICEWS).  
- Strength — Originality: Addresses limitations of Markovian dynamic PFA by using a deep recurrent architecture to model complex temporal dynamics.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Modeling implicit conditional distributions with neural networks within a hierarchical Bayesian framework is well motivated and fully specified.  
- Weakness — Clarity: Paper readability is poor due to numerous grammatical errors and typos, making technical details hard to follow.  
- Weakness — Experiments: Lacks a crucial baseline (shallow/single-layer DDPFA) to justify the benefit of multiple hierarchical layers; current LSTM comparison is not sufficient to isolate deep-architecture gains.  
- Weakness — Metrics: For text and event data, held-out perplexity would be a more standard and informative metric than MSE.  
- Weakness — Technical correctness: A potentially imprecise statement about modeling non-count real-valued matrices and the appropriate compound distribution (Poisson-Randomized Gamma may be more appropriate) needs clarification.  
- Minor suggestion: Emphasize best results in Table 2 (e.g., boldface) to improve readability.
