This paper proposes a method for modeling complex long-range temporal dependence in count vectors by using recurrent neural networks to parameterize implicit distributions realized via gamma distributions. The gamma shape parameters, which capture temporal dependence, are approximated by neural networks that integrate information from both the prior (higher layer) and the likelihood (lower layer). The neural network parameters are updated using expectations of the latent variables, which helps reduce sampling variance and improves computational efficiency. Overall, the model presents an interesting and clever approach to handling long-term dependencies in dynamic models. I like the idea, but the manuscript appears a bit rushed; I have the following comments:

1. There are critical typographical errors, e.g., in Equation (10).
2. The model treats the gamma scale parameter as fixed and updates only the shape parameter. This choice needs more detailed justification, discussion, and analysis.
3. The experiments only report results for a two-layer model. Please compare against single-layer and deeper variants to clarify how depth affects performance. Other hyperparameters also warrant discussion/ablation, such as the window size and the number of factors.
4. For clarity, include a detailed graphical illustration of the entire model. For guidance, see Figure 1 in [1].
5. The Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as [2] and [3].

Addressing the points above would substantially improve the clarity and empirical support of the paper.
