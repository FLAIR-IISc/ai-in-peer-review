

The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-term dependencies in count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to represent implicit distributions via gamma distributions. Specifically, the shape parameters of these gamma distributions—which encode temporal dependence—are approximated using neural networks that integrate information from higher-layer priors and lower-layer likelihoods. Parameters are updated via the expectation of latent variables, reducing variance from sampling and enhancing computational efficiency. The approach offers an interesting and effective solution to long-dependency challenges in dynamic models. However, the manuscript requires refinement to address several critical issues:  

1. **Typos and Notation**: Equation (10) contains critical typographical errors that require correction.  
2. **Scale Parameter Assumption**: The scale parameter is treated as fixed while only the shape parameter is updated. This choice warrants deeper justification and analysis, particularly regarding its impact on model flexibility and performance.  
3. **Layer Depth Evaluation**: Experiments only report results for a two-layer model without comparing against single-layer or deeper architectures. This omission prevents assessment of how layer depth influences performance. Additionally, the influence of hyperparameters—such as window size and the number of factors—requires discussion.  
4. **Model Visualization**: A comprehensive graphical illustration of the full model structure would significantly improve clarity. The authors should consider adopting a figure format similar to Figure 1 in [1].  
5. **Related Work**: The introduction should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, including [2] and [3], to contextualize the contribution within current literature.  

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.  
[2] Zhou M, Cong Y, Chen B. Augmentable gamma belief networks. Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1–44, 2016.
