This paper presents a Poisson factorization model enhanced with deep structure to capture long-range temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work in dynamic Poisson factorization, this approach utilizes a recurrent neural network-like architecture to model these long-term relationships, with parameter inference performed via variational inference and associated optimization techniques. The paper demonstrates strong technical merit, though it contains several grammatical and factual inaccuracies which are detailed below:

1. The description of modeling nonnegative numbers in lines 86-87 is inaccurate. The appropriate distribution for this purpose is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution.
2. For clarity, please highlight the best performing results in Table 2 using bold formatting.
3. While MSE/PMSE results are acceptable, the experiments in Section 4.2 would be strengthened by a more significant indicator of performance, such as held-out perplexity.
4. The paper effectively addresses limitations of existing methods for modeling long-range dependency (references 1 & 2). However, to further demonstrate the benefit of the proposed hierarchical approach, a comparison against a dynamic Poisson factorization model with a single-level deep architecture would be valuable. The comparison to LSTM is insightful, but differing learning algorithms limit its ability to definitively justify the necessity of multiple hierarchical levels.
