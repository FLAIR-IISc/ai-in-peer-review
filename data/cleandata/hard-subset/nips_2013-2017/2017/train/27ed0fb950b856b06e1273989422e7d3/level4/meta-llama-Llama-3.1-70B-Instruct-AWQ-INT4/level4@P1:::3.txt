The paper proposes a novel approach to modeling sequential count data by incorporating long-range temporal dependence into a Poisson factorization model using a deep structure. This work addresses the limitations of existing dynamic Poisson factorization models, which often fail to capture complex temporal dependencies. The authors employ a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies and use variational inference to estimate the latent variables and optimize the parameters of the neural network.

The paper has good technical content, but it is marred by several grammatical and factual errors. For instance, the statement about modeling nonnegative numbers is incorrect, as it is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that is used for this purpose. Additionally, there are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper, such as "unit the data," "Noticing the lack of capturing," and "recommend systems."

The experimental results presented in the paper are promising, with the proposed Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model outperforming existing methods on several datasets. However, the comparison with LSTM is not entirely fair, as the learning algorithms are different. A more meaningful comparison would be with dynamic Poisson factorization models that have a similar architecture but lack the deep structure proposed in this paper.

Furthermore, the paper could benefit from additional evaluation metrics, such as held-out perplexity, to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the model's performance. The current evaluation metrics, MSE and PMSE, are sufficient but do not provide a complete picture of the model's strengths and weaknesses.

Overall, the paper presents a novel approach to modeling sequential count data and demonstrates its effectiveness on several datasets. However, it requires further refinement to address the grammatical and factual errors and to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance.
