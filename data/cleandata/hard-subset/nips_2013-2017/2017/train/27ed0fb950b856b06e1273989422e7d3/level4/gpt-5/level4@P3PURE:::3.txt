Summary
The paper proposes a deep Poisson factorization model that captures long-range temporal dependence by using an RNN-like structure within a hierarchical framework. Inference is performed via variational inference together with optimization routines for the neural network parameters. The paper offers solid technical content and addresses key limitations of prior dynamic PF approaches (References 1 and 2), particularly with respect to modeling long-range temporal dependencies.

Main comments
- Technical correction: The statement on lines 86–87 regarding modeling nonnegative numbers is incorrect. It is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that is used for modeling nonnegative numbers.
- Experimental metrics: In Section 4.2, held-out perplexity should be emphasized as a primary indicator of performance. While MSE/PMSE are acceptable, perplexity would provide a stronger and more standard comparison for count data models.
- Justification of hierarchy depth: Although the proposed model addresses limitations of existing dynamic PF methods (References 1 and 2), the paper would more convincingly demonstrate the utility of multiple hierarchical levels by including a comparison to a dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture. The comparison with LSTM is useful, but because the learning algorithms differ, it does not by itself substantiate the need for multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.
- Presentation: Please make the best results bold in Table 2 to improve readability.

Overall assessment
The paper presents a promising and technically interesting approach to modeling long-range dependencies in Poisson factorization. Addressing the factual error about the distribution, emphasizing held-out perplexity in evaluation, adding a comparison to a one-level dynamic PF to justify hierarchy depth, and fixing the numerous writing issues would substantially strengthen the paper.
