- Proposes a novel Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) that incorporates long-range temporal dependence into Poisson factorization using a deep/RNN-like structure.  
- Uses variational inference to estimate latent variables and train the neural network parameters.  
- Addresses limitations of existing dynamic Poisson factorization models that fail to capture complex temporal dependencies.  
- Experimental results are promising: DDPFA outperforms existing methods on several datasets.  
- Comparison with LSTM is unfair because learning algorithms differ; results may not reflect architecture benefits.  
- A more meaningful baseline would be dynamic Poisson factorization models with similar architecture but without the proposed deep structure.  
- Evaluation relies on MSE and PMSE; additional metrics like held-out perplexity are needed for a more comprehensive assessment.  
- Paper has good technical content but contains several grammatical errors and typos (examples: "unit the data," "recommend systems").  
- Contains factual inaccuracies, e.g., incorrect statement about modeling nonnegative numbers versus use of the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution.  
- Suggests addressing grammatical/factual errors and typos throughout the manuscript.  
- Recommends expanding evaluation metrics and adding fairer baseline comparisons.  
- Suggests considering more robust optimization algorithms for neural network parameter training.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong experimental performance, addresses key limitations in prior work.  
- Arguments for rejection: grammatical/factual errors, unfair LSTM comparison, and incomplete evaluation.
