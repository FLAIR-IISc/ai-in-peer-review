1. Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPF): a temporal extension of Poisson Factorization using a simplified RNN and variational inference, evaluated on five datasets.

2. Core idea is potentially interesting but requires substantial improvements before NeurIPS-level acceptance.

3. Eq. 4 does not reflect a standard RNN—hidden state lacks recurrent within-layer connections; authors must justify this simplification.

4. Section 2 omits key equations/definitions: Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 is unclear and the distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3 is not specified.

5. The claim that certain distributions are “implicit” is incorrect—Eqs. 9 and 10 give explicit Gamma forms, so θ_t and h are not implicit.

6. The phrase about limitations with “fanatical data and disaster data” (lines 30–31) is vague and needs concrete explanation of problematic data characteristics.

7. Subsection lines 133–155 is hard to follow; the meaning and formulation of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 require clarification.

8. MSE and PMSE are inappropriate metrics for Poisson Factorization; results should include predictive log-likelihood for proper evaluation.

9. The chosen latent dimensionality K appears too small; experiments should explore larger K values (e.g., K ≥ 100).

10. The discussion in lines 224–232 is not clearly supported by Figure 3; the connection between text and figure needs clarification.

11. Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to streamline the main text.

12. Caption for Figure 4 is illegible and must be rewritten for clarity.

13. Heavy use of passive voice (notably in the abstract) should be reduced to improve readability.

14. Overall writing quality is poor with numerous typos and grammatical errors; many specific instances (lines and figure labels) need correction.
