1. Proposes a novel RNN-based method modeling count vectors via gamma distributions to capture complex long-time dependencies.  
2. Shape parameters of the gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks that integrate prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
3. Network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency.  
4. Presents an elegant solution to long-dependency modeling in dynamic systems and is overall compelling.  
5. Manuscript appears rushed and contains critical typographical errors, including in Equation 10.  
6. Treats the gamma scale parameter as fixed while only updating the shape parameter — this assumption needs deeper justification and analysis.  
7. Experiments are limited to a two-layer architecture with no comparisons to single-layer or multi-layer variants, nor exploration of hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
8. Lacks detailed schematic diagrams of the full model — adding such figures would improve clarity (e.g., see Figure 1 in [1]).  
9. Fails to discuss recent related work in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., [2], [3]) in the Introduction.
