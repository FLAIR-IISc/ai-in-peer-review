

The paper addresses the incorporation of long-range temporal dependencies within a Poisson factorization model enhanced by a deep structural component. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, this work utilizes a recurrent neural network-inspired architecture to model long-term dependencies, with inference performed via variational inference combined with optimization procedures for neural network parameter estimation. While the technical content is strong, the paper contains several grammatical and factual errors, as detailed below:  

1. The assertion regarding modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86–87 is inaccurate; the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is employed for this purpose.  
2. Bold the best results in Table 2.  
3. Held-out perplexity should have served as a stronger experimental metric in Section 4.2, though MSE/PMSE remain acceptable.  
4. The limitations of existing works on long-range dependency modeling (references 1, 2) are appropriately addressed. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method across multiple hierarchical levels, a comparison with a single-level dynamic PF architecture would have been beneficial. While the LSTM comparison is useful, the differing learning algorithms prevent it from substantiating the necessity of the multi-level hierarchy.
