The paper addresses incorporating long-range temporal dependencies into a Poisson factorization model by leveraging a deep, structured approach. Unlike prior work on dynamic Poisson factorization, this paper employs a recurrent neural network-like structure to model these long-term dependencies. Inference is performed using variational inference, with optimization routines used to determine the neural network parameters. The paper presents solid technical content but contains several grammatical and factual errors, detailed below.

1. The claim regarding modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86-87 is inaccurate. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate method for modeling nonnegative numbers.

2. For clarity, please bold the best results in Table 2.

3. While MSE/PMSE are acceptable metrics, held-out perplexity would have provided a more compelling indicator of performance in the experiments presented in Section 4.2.

4. The paper adequately addresses limitations in existing work concerning the modeling of long-range dependencies (references 1 & 2). However, to further illustrate the benefit of the proposed method's multiple levels of hierarchy, a comparison with a dynamic Poisson factorization model with a single-layer deep architecture would be valuable. The comparison to LSTM is useful, but the differing learning algorithms make it difficult to definitively justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed method.
