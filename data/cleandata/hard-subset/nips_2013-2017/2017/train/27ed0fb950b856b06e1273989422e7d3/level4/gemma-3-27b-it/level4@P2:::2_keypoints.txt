- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA): uses RNNs to model implicit gamma-distributed temporal factors in Poisson factor analysis for sequential count data.  
- Shape parameters of gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks that integrate prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers) information.  
- Variational inference is used, updating network parameters via expectations of latent variables to improve computational efficiency and reduce variance.  
- Strength: novel integration of PFA with recurrent neural nets to capture long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Strength: sensible application of variational inference and clear motivation addressing limitations of simpler transition models like PGDS.  
- Connection to prior work is noted (e.g., Deep Exponential Families, Deep Poisson Factor Analysis), situating the paper in Bayesian deep generative modeling.  
- Manuscript contains typographical errors (e.g., Equation 10) that detract from polish and clarity.  
- Key design choice—fixing the gamma scale parameter and only updating the shape—lacks sufficient justification and analysis of alternatives.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only two-layer results presented, with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper architectures to assess scaling with depth.  
- Missing sensitivity analyses for important hyperparameters (window size, number of latent factors), reducing robustness and reproducibility.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete and omits recent advances that should be discussed and compared.  
- Presentation would benefit from a full graphical model illustration to improve clarity and accessibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, potential significance for modeling long-term dependencies, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, incomplete empirical evaluation, omissions in related work, and weak presentation.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising contribution but requires significant revisions; reviewer recommends weak reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
