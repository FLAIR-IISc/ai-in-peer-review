- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNNs parameterize gamma latent factors to model long-term dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Integrates RNNs with Poisson factor analysis, building on PGDS and deep Poisson factor models.  
- Uses variational inference with neural networks and a loss balancing reconstruction and prior matching.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA) vs PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Latent factors are interpretable and show meaningful dynamics (e.g., Figure 2, Figure 3).  
- Technical approach is sound; gamma conjugacy and loss formulation are appropriate for count data.  
- Paper is generally well-structured with thorough experimental design.  
- Overlooks recent relevant work by Zhou et al. (augmentable gamma belief networks, deep LDA), weakening the novelty claim.  
- Fixing the gamma rate parameter β while only updating shape α is unjustified and may bias latent estimates.  
- Ablation study is insufficient: missing comparisons with single-layer and deeper (3+) architectures and limited hyperparameter analysis (window size, K).  
- Model graphical structure lacks a clear diagram, hindering understanding of layer connections.  
- Claims that the ladder architecture mitigates "pruning" are unsubstantiated without empirical layer-wise evidence.  
- Requested revisions: contrast with Zhou et al., expand ablation (single/deeper layers, hyperparameters), add model diagram, and provide empirical evidence on pruning.  
- Recommendation: technically competent but needs stronger novelty/experimental support — Weak Reject.
