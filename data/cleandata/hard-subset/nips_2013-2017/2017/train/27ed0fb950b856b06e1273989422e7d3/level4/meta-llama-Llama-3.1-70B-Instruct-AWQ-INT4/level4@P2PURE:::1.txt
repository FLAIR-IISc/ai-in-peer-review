This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, a model that builds on Poisson factorization (PF) to allow for temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous works on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to allow for long-term dependencies. Inference is carried out via variational inference, with an extra step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on 5 real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

+The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not incorporate information from previous time steps for the same layer, as is typical in RNNs. Can the authors provide a justification for this simplified structure?

+In Section 2, some of the model equations are missing or unclear (e.g., the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 and the distribution that generates theta_t in Eq. 3). Providing these details would improve the clarity of the model presentation.

+I disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding "implicit distributions". Specifically, Eqs. 9 and 10 define explicit distributions, namely Gammas with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it is misleading to refer to it as implicit. The same comment applies to h in Eq. 10.

+The sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," is unclear. Can the authors elaborate on this point and provide specific examples or explanations?

+The subsection in lines 133-155 is unclear, particularly with regards to the "loss function" in Eq. 15. Providing a clear definition and explanation of this loss function would improve the readability of the paper.

+Mean squared error (MSE) and predictive mean squared error (PMSE) are not typically suitable metrics for evaluating PF-like models. To provide a more comprehensive evaluation, the authors should consider including additional metrics, such as predictive log-likelihood.

+The value of K used in the experiments (line 207) appears too small. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100, to demonstrate the robustness of their approach.

+The discussion in lines 224-232 does not clearly connect to Fig. 3. Can the authors revise this section to improve its clarity and connection to the figure?

+The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) are not essential to the main flow of the paper and can be safely moved to the supplement.

+The caption in Fig. 4 is difficult to read. Can the authors revise the caption to improve its clarity and readability?

+I strongly recommend that the authors follow general writing advice and avoid using the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

+The writing quality of the paper is poor, and there are many typos throughout the text. Some examples include:
 -Line 15, "high-dimensional"
 -Line 22, "factorize"
 -Line 24, "property of the"
 -Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet"
 -Line 34, "nested"
 -Fig. 1, "visual representation"
 -Fig.1, "transmission" (which may not be the most suitable word choice)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")
