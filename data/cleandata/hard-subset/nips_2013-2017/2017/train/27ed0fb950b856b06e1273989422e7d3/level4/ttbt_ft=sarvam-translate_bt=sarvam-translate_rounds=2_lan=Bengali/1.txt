This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model, a model based on PF that allows for temporal dependence. Unlike previous work on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified recurrent neural network for long-term dependence. Neural network parameters are estimated through an additional step to maximize them. The paper presents the results of experiments conducted on 5 real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the concept potentially appealing, but I believe it needs some improvements in execution before it can be adopted at conferences like NIPS. Please see my comments below.

+The structure of equation 4 is not consistent with an RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-level memory vector and does not depend on the previous time step for the same layer as the RNN setup. Is there a reason for this simplified structure?
+In paragraph 2, some equations of the model are missing (e.g., it is not clear how gamma can be parameterized in equation 4 or what distribution theta_t creates in equation 3).
+I disagree with the discussion of "inherent distribution" on lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 are actually clear: they are gamma with specific shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (equation 9) can be computed in a closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be called inherent. The same comment applies to h in equation 10.
+Lines 30-31: "This method may have weak points for analyzing long-term dependencies with different patterns, such as fanatical data and disaster data, among other data" - this sentence was not clear to me; can you elaborate on it?
+The sub-paragraph on lines 133-155 is not clear to me. What exactly is the "loss function" in equation 15?
+MSE/PMSE is generally not a good metric for models like PF. The results should additionally include at least the predictive log-probability.
+The value of K in the experiment (line 207) seems too small to me. I suggest the authors use a larger value like K=100 (at least).
+I could not reconcile the discussion on lines 224-232 with Figure 3: it is not clear.
+The update equations for the variational inference method (equations 11-14) can be safely removed from the supplement.
+The caption of Figure 4 is not clearly readable.
+I strongly recommend following the general writing advice regarding the use of passive voice (especially for abstracts).
+The writing quality is poor, and the paper has many typos. Here are some examples:
 -Line 15, "high-dimensional"
 -Line 22, "factorize"
 -Line 24, "property"
 -Lines 29, 34, "Dirac"
 -Line 34, "nested"
 -Figure 1, "visual representation"
 -Figure 1, "transmission" (however, I am not sure if this is the appropriate term)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Many articles are missing (e.g., "although the Dirac distribution is often used as a prior distribution")
